👑 Two Thrones: Interpreting Daniel 7:9

Rabbi Yosei the Galilean
One throne is for judgment (דין) and one is for mercy (רחמים). The Holy One sits in strict judgment but tempers it with compassion on a second throne.
Rabbi Akiva
One throne is for judgment (דין) and one is for righteousness (צדק). The verse in Daniel supports two distinct thrones for God's attributes.
Rabbi Elazar ben Azarya
Akiva! What have you to do with Aggada? Go engage with Nega'im and Ohalot! Rather — one is a throne and one is a footstool: "The heavens are My throne and the earth My footstool" (Isaiah 66:1).
Why It Matters Rabbi Akiva — master of halakha — is rebuked for venturing into mystical aggada without sufficient grounding. The rebuff reflects the Gemara's caution: not every great scholar may expound the secrets of the Chariot freely.

🔮 Ma'aseh Merkavah — Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai and Rabbi Elazar

Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai rides a donkey on the road; Rabbi Elazar ben Arakh walks behind him as his student
↓
Elazar asks: "My teacher, teach me one chapter of the Design of the Divine Chariot (מעשה מרכבה)"
↓
Yoḥanan dismounts, wraps his head in his cloak in reverence, and sits on a stone beneath an olive tree — "It is not fitting that I should hear the words of the Shekhinah while mounted"
↓
Elazar Expounds
Elazar begins expounding the Chariot — fire descends from heaven and encircles them; ministering angels dance before them like guests at a wedding feast
Heavenly Confirmation
🔥 An angel responds from within the fire: "This is the very Design of the Divine Chariot, just as you have expounded it!"
↓
Yoḥanan kisses Elazar and declares: "Happy are you, Abraham our forefather, that Elazar ben Arakh came forth from your loins!"

🔥 River Dinur — The Ephemeral Angels

נהר די נור נגד ונפק מן קדמוהי — דניאל ז:י
נהר דינור Shmuel said to Ḥiyya bar Rav: "Son of great ones, come — let me tell you the great things your father would say."

Each and every day, ministering angels are newly created from the River Dinur (נהר דינור — the River of Fire). They recite song before the Holy One — and then immediately cease to exist.

This stands in striking contrast to Israel: while each angelic chorus is a one-time creation whose entire existence is a single moment of praise, the Jewish people endure eternally to sing before God across all generations.

The River Dinur also appears in Daniel 7:10 — "A river of fire flowed and streamed before Him" — the same cosmic source from which these transient angels emerge.
