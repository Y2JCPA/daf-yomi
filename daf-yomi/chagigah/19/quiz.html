Chagigah 19 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman, does immersion for non-sacred food (chulin) require specific intention (kavvanah)?
A. Yes — intention is always required for any valid immersion
B. No — non-sacred food requires no intention; immersion is valid regardless
C. Only if the person is a kohen eating priestly portions
D. Only when immersing in a constructed mikveh, not in natural water
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman argues against Rabba that chulin requires no kavvanah. He reinterprets the mishna: 'as if he did not immerse' refers only to tithes, not non-sacred food. The proof from Makhshirin confirms this — fruit becomes susceptible to impurity even through unintentional contact with water.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why is immersing in the arc (bow) of a wave invalid according to the baraita?
A. Seawater contains salt, which disqualifies it from use in a mikveh
B. The wave volume may fall below the required 40 se'a minimum
C. One cannot immerse 'in air' — airborne water is not a valid settled body of water
D. The arc of a wave is moving, and a valid mikveh must be stationary
Correct: C — The baraita explicitly states: one may immerse in the edges of waves but not in their arcs, because one may not immerse 'in air.' The arching water is still airborne and thus does not constitute a valid settled body of water.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Elazar, what may a person do after ascending from an unintentional immersion?
A. Nothing — he must immerse again with proper intention before eating any sacred food
B. Eat only non-sacred food until he immerses again with intention
C. Assume a presumptive status of purity (chezkat taharah) for any purpose he wishes
D. Consult a rabbi who can retroactively designate the immersion's purpose
Correct: C — Rabbi Elazar rules that once a person has ascended from immersion — even without any prior intention — he may assume chezkat taharah for whatever purpose he wishes, including eating tithes or terumah, without needing to immerse again.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Rabbi Yehuda hold about a person who is ascending from the mikveh with one foot still in the water?
A. His immersion is invalid — both feet must fully exit before it counts
B. He is still considered to be in the midst of immersing and may upgrade his intention to a higher level of purity
C. He may not change his intention at all once any part of him has left the water
D. He must call out his intended purpose aloud before lifting the second foot
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda holds that one foot in the water means the immersion is still 'in progress.' Therefore the person may still change or upgrade his intention — for example, from intending for a minor matter to intending for a major matter.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rav Mari derive about non-sacred produce (chulin) that was prepared according to the purity standards of sacrificial food (kodesh)?
A. It is treated as ordinary chulin with no additional stringency
B. It may only be eaten by kohanim
C. It is treated as sacrificial food itself — the highest purity standard applies
D. It is treated as terumah, one level below sacrificial food
Correct: C — Rav Mari derives from the mishna that chulin she-na'asu al taharas kodesh — non-sacred food prepared with the stringencies required for sacrificial food — is regarded as kodesh itself for purity purposes. This is the highest level discussed in the mishna.
