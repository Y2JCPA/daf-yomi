Chagigah 22 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, why did the Sages prohibit immersing small vessels inside a narrow-mouthed vessel?
A. It creates a bad appearance to onlookers, who might imitate improperly
B. Water cannot adequately reach the inner vessels through the narrow opening
C. The outer vessel becomes impure and contaminates what is inside
D. A narrow-mouthed vessel cannot contain the required forty se'a
Correct: B — Rava's concern is physical — water cannot flow through a narrow opening to properly immerse inner vessels. This contrasts with Rabbi Ila, who focuses on the problem of appearance (mar'it ha'ayin).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rava's ruling about a basket (גַּרְגּוּטְנִי) filled with vessels and immersed together?
A. It is prohibited for both teruma and sacrificial food due to appearance
B. It is pure for teruma but prohibited for sacrificial food
C. It is pure in all regards, even for sacrificial food
D. Each vessel must be separately immersed before going into the basket
Correct: C — Rava rules that a basket immersed with smaller vessels inside is pure even for sacrificial food, since water flows freely through the basket's open weave — his physical water-flow concern does not apply here.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Beit Shammai rule that metal and glass vessels inside a sealed earthenware vessel of an am ha'aretz are NOT shielded from impurity?
A. Metal and glass are inherently more susceptible to impurity than earthenware
B. A ḥaver might borrow these vessels and be misled into thinking they are pure
C. The am ha'aretz's earthenware vessel itself is always presumed impure
D. Metal vessels require a separate immersion and cannot be shielded by any vessel
Correct: B — Beit Shammai reasoned that ḥaverim might borrow metal or glass vessels (unlike food or earthenware, which ḥaverim would not use) and would be dangerously misled about their true purity status.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why are the Sages MORE concerned about antagonism (אֵיבָה) from an am ha'aretz regarding sacrificial food than regarding teruma?
A. An am ha'aretz is more emotionally invested in sacrificial offerings than in teruma
B. We accept sacrificial food from amei ha'aretz but not their teruma, so rejection stings more
C. Sacrificial food is more financially valuable and the am ha'aretz loses more money
D. The Torah explicitly commands avoiding antagonism only for sacrificial contexts
Correct: B — Since we DO accept sacrificial food from amei ha'aretz but do NOT accept their teruma, the am ha'aretz would feel the sting of rejection regarding sacrificial food specifically — causing the antagonism (eivah) the Sages sought to prevent.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Abaye resolve the apparent contradiction between the baraita stating amei ha'aretz ARE trusted regarding immersion and the halakha that ḥaverim must re-immerse borrowed vessels?
A. The baraita refers only to Galilean amei ha'aretz, who are more reliable
B. The baraita refers to immersion of their own bodies; vessels are a separate, less-trusted category
C. The baraita is an early ruling superseded by the later stringent position
D. Amei ha'aretz are trusted only when two witnesses observe their immersion
Correct: B — Abaye distinguishes: amei ha'aretz are trusted when they say they have immersed their own bodies, but they are NOT trusted regarding the immersion of vessels — two separate domains of reliability.
