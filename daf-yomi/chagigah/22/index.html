⚖️ Two Great Disputes of Daf 22

Rava
Physical concern: The prohibition on immersing vessels inside a narrow-mouthed vessel is because water cannot adequately reach the inner vessels. A basket (גַּרְגּוּטְנִי) is therefore permitted — water flows freely through its weave.
Rabbi Ila
Appearance concern: Even a basket is prohibited — it looks like improper immersion to observers. The Sages decreed against any immersion of vessels inside other vessels, regardless of water flow.
Beit Shammai
A sealed earthenware vessel of an am ha'aretz shields food, drink, and earthenware inside — but NOT metal or glass vessels that ḥaverim might later borrow and be misled about their purity.
Beit Hillel
The sealed earthenware vessel shields everything inside — both earthenware and metal vessels are pure. You cannot distinguish between them.
🙇 Rabbi Yehoshua's Remarkable Response After hearing Beit Shammai's reasoning from a student, Rabbi Yehoshua prostrated himself on the graves of Beit Shammai's students: 'I humble myself before you, bones of Beit Shammai! If your hidden reasoning is so compelling — how much more your revealed teachings!'

⚖️ Rava vs Rabbi Ila: Why Prohibit Vessels Inside Vessels?

Rava
Root concern = water flow. Needles immersed inside a narrow-mouthed vessel cannot be reached by water — the immersion is physically invalid. A basket poses no such problem since water penetrates its open weave. Therefore a basket filled with vessels and immersed is pure for all purposes, even sacrificial food.
Rabbi Ila
Root concern = appearance (מַרְאִית הָעַיִן). The Sages prohibited any immersion of one vessel inside another lest observers imitate without taking proper precautions. A basket is also prohibited — the visual problem is the same regardless of whether water physically flows through.
📌 The Practical Difference A basket or wicker bin (גַּרְגּוּטְנִי) filled with small vessels and immersed: Rava → PURE (water flows freely through weave) vs. Rabbi Ila → PROHIBITED (appearance problem unchanged). This dispute is also a tannaitic dispute in a baraita.

🤝 Am Ha'aretz Trustworthiness: By Category

❌ NOT Trusted

  • Immersion of vessels — assumed not done properly
  • That produce susceptibility to impurity was removed
  • Purity standards for sacrificial food
  • Ḥaver must re-immerse vessels borrowed from am ha'aretz

✅ Trusted

  • Immersion of his own body (Abaye's resolution)
  • That produce has not been made susceptible to impurity
  • Purity for everyday ḥullin purposes
  • Testimony accepted today — Rav Pappa rules per Rabbi Yosei

⚠️ Nuanced

  • Teruma: Not accepted from am ha'aretz → vessel purity less consequential
  • Sacrificial food: Accepted → rejection causes אֵיבָה (antagonism)
  • Am ha'aretz resents sacrificial rejection MORE than teruma rejection
  • Sages shaped law to avoid enmity — a social-halakhic balance

🏛️ Rabbi Yehoshua & the Shammai Student: Five Dramatic Moments

The Dispute: Beit Shammai rules that a sealed earthenware vessel of an am ha'aretz shields food, drink, and earthenware inside — but not metal or glass vessels that ḥaverim might borrow.
↓
Rabbi Yehoshua's Challenge: 'I am ashamed of your words, Beit Shammai! How can a corpse below render only some items in the vessel above impure? Either the vessel shields everything or nothing!'
↓
The Student Steps Forward: A Shammaite student approaches Rabbi Yehoshua and offers to explain their reasoning. Rabbi Yehoshua says: 'Speak.'
↓
Beit Shammai's Logic Revealed: An impure vessel does not shield its contents. Food and earthenware stay pure only because ḥaverim will never touch them. But metal vessels might be borrowed — a ḥaver could use them thinking they are pure, when in fact they absorbed impurity through the unsealed earthenware vessel.
↓
Rabbi Yehoshua's Prostration: He immediately went and prostrated on the graves of Beit Shammai's students: 'Bones of Beit Shammai — I humble myself before you! If your hidden reasoning is so compelling, how much more your revealed teachings must have been!'
