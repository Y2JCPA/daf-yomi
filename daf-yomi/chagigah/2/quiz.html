Chagigah 2 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What does the word 'kol' (all) in the mishna's obligation of re'iyah come to include, according to the Gemara?
A. A woman who accepted a Nazirite vow
B. A half-slave half-freeman
C. A blind person with sight in one eye
D. A deaf person who can read lips
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that 'kol' (all) includes the half-slave half-freeman, who occupies a partial legal status. This inclusion then drives the entire discussion about compelling the master to free him.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Beit Hillel, what is the minimum value of the Olah (burnt-offering) brought for re'iyah?
A. Two silver coins
B. One silver ma'a
C. One gold dinar
D. Half a shekel
Correct: B — Beit Hillel say the Olah must be worth at least one silver ma'a, and the Shlamim at least two silver coins — the inverse of Beit Shammai, who require two silver coins for the Olah.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Beit Hillel, which child is considered a minor exempt from re'iyah?
A. Any child under bar mitzvah age
B. Any child who cannot ride on his father's shoulders to the Temple Mount
C. Any child who cannot hold his father's hand and walk up from Jerusalem
D. Any child who has not yet grown two pubic hairs
Correct: C — Beit Hillel use a less physically demanding threshold — holding the father's hand while walking — compared to Beit Shammai who require the child be able to ride on the father's shoulders.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Who rules that a person blind in one eye is exempt from re'iyah, and what is the verse-based reason?
A. Rava, because such a person is considered like one who is sick
B. Yochanan ben Dehavai in the name of Rabbi Yehuda, derived from 'yir'eh' — appear/see symmetrically
C. Beit Shammai, because he cannot ascend safely
D. Ravina, from a textual redundancy in Deuteronomy 16:16
Correct: B — Yochanan ben Dehavai cites Rabbi Yehuda: the verse uses 'yir'eh' (he shall appear) — just as God 'sees' you with both eyes, you must appear with full sight in both eyes, excluding one blinded in one eye.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Ravina (or Rava) resolve the apparent contradiction between the Tosefta and the baraita regarding one who speaks but cannot hear?
A. The Tosefta refers to Rabbinic obligation and the baraita to Torah obligation
B. The baraita is a later authority and overrides the Tosefta entirely
C. The mishna is 'chaserah' (incomplete) — it implicitly includes them among those exempt from re'iyah
D. They are exempt in the Diaspora but obligated when living in Jerusalem
Correct: C — Ravina says the mishna is chaserah (incomplete). When read in full, it exempts both the one who speaks-but-can't-hear and the one who hears-but-can't-speak from re'iyah, even though they are obligated in other mitzvot.
