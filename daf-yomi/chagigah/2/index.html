⚖️ Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel: Defining Re'iyah Obligations

Beit Shammai
A minor is any child who cannot ride on his father's shoulders and ascend to the Temple Mount.
Beit Hillel
A minor is any child who cannot hold his father's hand and walk up from Jerusalem to the Temple Mount.
Beit Shammai
Burnt-offering (Olah): ≥ 2 silver coins.
Peace-offering (Shlamim): ≥ 1 ma'a.
Beit Hillel
Burnt-offering (Olah): ≥ 1 silver ma'a.
Peace-offering (Shlamim): ≥ 2 silver coins.
Key Reversal Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel invert the relative values — Hillel requires more for the peace-offering and less for the burnt-offering.

🔗 The Half-Slave Half-Freeman Dilemma

Gemara
If half-slave half-freeman is obligated in re'iyah, his master must free him — but Beit Shammai objects!
Beit Shammai
You have remedied the master's situation — he gets full service — but you have not remedied the slave's! He cannot marry a maidservant (half-free) nor a free woman (half-slave).
Beit Hillel
Therefore the master must free him! The slave writes a document for half his value. The world was created only for procreation — "He did not create it to be a waste; He formed it to be inhabited" (Isaiah 45:18).
Beit Shammai
(Retracted their position) — Beit Shammai ultimately conceded to Beit Hillel's ruling on this matter.
Ruling The master is compelled to free the half-slave; the slave writes a debt document for half his value to his former master.

📜 Chaserah Mishna — The Incomplete Mishna (Ravina/Rava)

Contradiction arises: Tosefta says one who speaks-but-can't-hear and one who hears-but-can't-speak are obligated in mitzvot — yet a baraita says they are exempt!
↓
Ravina (or Rava) resolves: The mishna is חֲסֵרָה — chaserah (incomplete). It must be read as listing additional exemptions beyond what is explicitly stated.
↓
Full reading of the mishna: All are obligated, except for the deaf-mute, imbecile, and minor — and also one who speaks-but-can't-hear and one who hears-but-can't-speak (exempt from re'iyah specifically).
↓
Biblical source for definitions: Psalm 38:14 — "I am as a deaf man [חֵרֵשׁ] who hears not, and as a mute [אִלֵּם] who does not open his mouth." — proves 'deaf' = doesn't hear; 'mute' = doesn't speak.
↓
Bottom line: Partial categories (speaks OR hears) are obligated in most mitzvot but exempt specifically from re'iyah and Festival rejoicing.
