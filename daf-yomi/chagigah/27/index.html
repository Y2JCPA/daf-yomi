⚖️ Why Are the Altars Exempt from Impurity?

Rabbi Eliezer
The altars are pure because they are like the ground — just as the ground cannot become impure, so too large fixed structures attached to the ground are not susceptible to impurity.
The Rabbis
The altars are pure because they are coated — the gold or bronze coating changes their legal status, rendering them not susceptible to impurity.
Gemara's Resolution The Rabbis may actually agree with Rabbi Eliezer. They are not offering an alternative reason — they are challenging him: Don't rely on the coating, which is nullified and subordinate. Instead, rely on the ground-analogy, which is the correct basis.

🪵 The Table Called 'The Altar of Wood'

הַמִּזְבֵּחַ עֵץ שָׁלֹשׁ אַמּוֹת גָּבֹהַּ — 'The altar of wood, three cubits high' (Yechezkel 41:22)
Biblical Source — Ezekiel 41:22 The verse describes the shewbread table yet calls it 'the altar of wood, three cubits high.' The Merciful One called the table an altar — teaching two things:

1. The table has an altar-like sacred status.
2. By calling it wood, the Torah teaches that a wooden vessel in a fixed place is not susceptible to impurity — like the ground itself.
4 / 9

🔄 One Verse — Two Names, One Lesson

Rabbi Yoḥanan
The verse (Ezek. 41:22) begins with the word altar and ends with the word table. This teaches: when a person eats at their table with proper purity and intent, their table atones for them like the altar.
Reish Lakish
I agree — while the Temple stood, the altar atoned for Israel. Now that the Temple is destroyed, a person's table atones. Hosting guests and eating in sanctity replaces the sacrificial service.
Post-Temple Teaching Both Amoraim derive the same lesson: the dining table is the altar of the post-Temple era. Eating with mindfulness and purity is a form of divine service and atonement — שׁוּלְחָן מְכַפֵּר.

🔥 The Fire That Cannot Harm — A Fortiori Arguments

Starting Point: The golden altar has only a dinar-thin gold coating — yet fire never damaged it
↓
Reish Lakish — Sinners of Israel
If a thin coating protects the altar from fire → Israel, who are full of mitzvot like a pomegranate, certainly cannot be consumed by Gehenna's fire
R. Abbahu / R. Elazar — Torah Scholars
A salamander is born from fire → anointing with its blood protects from fire. Torah scholars are immersed in the fire of Torah → certainly Gehenna cannot harm them
↓
Conclusion: אֵשׁ שֶׁל גֵּיהִנֹּם — The fire of Gehenna has no dominion over Torah scholars or over the sinners of Israel

🛡️ Two Protections from the Fire of Gehenna

Torah Scholars

  • Source: Rabbi Abbahu citing Rabbi Elazar
  • Analogy: The salamander (salamandra) — a creature born from fire
  • One who anoints with salamander blood is protected from fire
  • Torah scholars are 'immersed' in the fire of Torah study
  • Kal va-chomer: Certainly Gehenna's fire has no power over them
  • Protection is inherent — from the Torah itself

Sinners of Israel

  • Source: Reish Lakish
  • Analogy: The golden altar — thin dinar-thick coating
  • Fire burned for years but never damaged the altar's coating
  • Even Jewish sinners are 'coated' — full of mitzvot like a pomegranate
  • Kal va-chomer: Certainly Gehenna cannot destroy them
  • Protection is external — from accumulated merits
