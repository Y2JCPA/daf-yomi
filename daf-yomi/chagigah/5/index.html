⚖️ Rav Beivai bar Abaye vs. The Angel of Death

Rav Beivai bar Abaye
Do you have the right to take someone before her time? She was just sweeping the oven and burned her foot — is that a reason to end her life?
Angel of Death
Is it not written: 'There are those swept away without justice' (Proverbs 13:23)? Her luck ran out — the fire gave me the opening, and I took her.
Rav Beivai bar Abaye
Then what do you do with her extra years — the years she was supposed to live but didn't?
Angel of Death
I shepherd the souls and do not release them until the years of the generation are complete. Then I pass the soul to the angel Duma. The extra years are given to a scholar who overlooks an insult — he receives them as reward.
Key Principle Premature death can result from circumstance ('luck') intersecting with divine accounting. Unused life-years are redistributed — here to a Torah scholar who practices forbearance. Divine justice operates across the whole web of lives.

🔍 What Is 'Every Hidden Thing' God Judges? (Ecclesiastes 12:14)

Rav

  • One who kills a louse in front of a friend
  • The friend is disgusted by it
  • God judges him for causing unintentional shame

Rabbi Zeira

  • One who counts money from his own palm into another's palm
  • Even routine financial transfers done improperly
  • The manner of an act matters as much as the act itself

School of Rabbi Sheila

  • One who gives charity to a woman in private
  • She becomes suspected of prostitution
  • A good deed done improperly causes reputational harm

Rava

  • Sending certain meat to one's wife on Shabbat eve
  • Unless she is an expert in the relevant laws
  • Even domestic acts carry hidden halakhic dimensions

💧 God Weeps in a Secret Place Called Mistarim

מִסְתָּרִים תִּבְכֶּה נַפְשִׁי מִפְּנֵי גֵוָה (ירמיהו י״ג:י״ז)
Rav Shmuel bar Inya in the name of Rav The Holy One, Blessed be He, has a place where He cries — and its name is Mistarim (hidden places). Source: 'My soul shall weep in secret [bemistarim] for your pride' (Jeremiah 13:17).

But is there crying before God? Rav Pappa objects: 'Honor and majesty are before Him; strength and gladness are in His place' (I Chronicles 16:27)!

Resolution: There is no sadness in the outer chambers — those are filled with glory. But in the inner chambers, where no created being can enter, God weeps privately — for things too painful to express openly.

Three tears in Jeremiah 13:17: One for the First Temple · One for the Second Temple · One for Israel carried into exile

📚 Seeking God 'Daily' — Rav Idi's Journey & the Blind Scholar's Blessing

Rav Idi's Journey: He would travel three months on the road to reach Rabbi Yochanan's study hall — and spend only one day there before immediately traveling back to arrive home for Sukkot.
↓
The other Rabbis mocked him as 'one who causes harm to the Rabbis' — the journey seemed to exhaust resources without proportional benefit to the study hall.
↓
Rabbi Yochanan's defense: From Isaiah 58:2 — 'They seek Me daily' — even coming once a year constitutes 'daily' seeking. The sincerity and cost of the journey is what counts.
↓
The Blind Scholar's Blessing: Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi and Rabbi Ḥiyya traveled to greet a blind Torah scholar. He blessed them: 'You greeted one who sees and is not seen — may you be worthy to greet the One Who sees and is not seen.'
↓
The blessing was traced back to Rabbi Ya'akov of the village of Ḥitiyya, who greeted his teacher every day. His teacher replied with an a fortiori: if greeting a wise man who will die is so great, how much more so greeting the One Who lives forever.
