🏺 Empty vs. Full Vessels — Who Is Trusted from Modi'im Inward?

Reish Lakish
Amei ha'aretz are credible from Modi'im inward only for empty vessels. If the jugs are full of liquid, they are not deemed credible — the liquid may have contracted impurity.
Rabbi Yochanan
Even if the jugs are full of liquid — the am ha'aretz is deemed credible within the Modi'im boundary. The special trust granted there applies to all vessels, full or empty.
Practical Stakes This dispute affects whether a potter's full jugs purchased inside the Modi'im boundary may be used for sacrificial food (kodesh) without further purification.

🚔 Am Ha'aretz Tax Collectors Enter a House — Purity Outcome

Am ha'aretz tax collectors enter a house to collect
↓
No gentile with them
Limited impurity: only the area where their feet trod is impure. They are credible that they did not touch the remaining vessels.
Gentile (senior) is present
Entire house is impure. Fear of the gentile causes them to scatter throughout, touching all objects. (Rabbi Yochanan / Rabbi Elazar)

🕎 Why Is the Shewbread Table Susceptible to Ritual Impurity?

Question
The table is a wooden vessel designated to rest in a fixed place — such a vessel should NOT be susceptible to ritual impurity! Why do we warn am ha'aretz kohanim about it?
Reish Lakish
The verse says "upon the pure table" (Lev. 24:6) — this teaches the table was lifted and carried to show the pilgrims the miracle of the shewbread. As a portable vessel, it is susceptible to impurity.
New Question
Perhaps the table is susceptible via its golden coating? A mishna teaches: a table whose wood is nullified by its coating is susceptible through the coating.
Rabbi Yochanan
Whether the coating is fixed or not fixed, whether it covers the rim or not — this distinction makes no difference. The coating approach cannot fully resolve the question; Reish Lakish's portability answer stands.
Takeaway The shewbread table was lifted before pilgrims during the Festival to display the miracle — this act of display transformed it halakhically into a portable vessel subject to impurity.
