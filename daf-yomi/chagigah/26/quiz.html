Chagigah 26 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What does Abaye derive from the Mishna's ruling about the potter from Modi'im?
A. Potters are always trusted regarding their wares wherever they sell
B. Location inside the Modi'im boundary is the determining factor for credibility
C. Only new, unused earthenware can be trusted from any location
D. The potter must verbally declare his vessels are pure
Correct: B — Abaye argues that the Mishna's specific mention of Modi'im proves that location is key — the potter is trusted only because he is inside the boundary, not due to any general rule about potters.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Reish Lakish and Rabbi Yochanan about am ha'aretz credibility from Modi'im inward?
A. Whether wooden or earthenware vessels are included in the leniency
B. Whether large or small vessels are included
C. Whether the am ha'aretz is trusted for full vessels or only empty ones
D. Whether the leniency applies year-round or only during the Festival
Correct: C — Reish Lakish holds that only empty vessels are covered by the leniency; Rabbi Yochanan holds that even vessels full of liquid are included within the Modi'im credibility zone.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why does the presence of a gentile among tax collectors cause the entire house to become impure?
A. Gentiles always render a house impure by rabbinic decree regardless of actions
B. Fear of the gentile (their senior) causes the tax collectors to scatter and touch all objects
C. The gentile himself physically handles every vessel in the house
D. A gentile's legal testimony overrides the tax collectors' credible claim
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the collectors fear the gentile as their authority figure, causing them to move rapidly throughout the entire house — touching all the vessels — rather than staying in one area.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the contradiction between the two baraitot about a wine seller who opened his barrel during the Festival?
A. One baraita speaks of wine and the other of dough — different products have different rules
B. One baraita follows Rabbi Yehuda who is lenient; the other follows the Rabbis who are stringent
C. One baraita speaks of before the Festival and the other of after the Festival
D. The two baraitot actually agree — one is describing a large barrel, the other a small one
Correct: B — The Gemara resolves the contradiction by mapping each baraita to the tannaitic dispute in the Mishna: the lenient baraita follows Rabbi Yehuda, and the stringent baraita follows the Sages.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why is the shewbread table susceptible to ritual impurity, according to Reish Lakish?
A. Because it is coated with gold, which can independently contract impurity
B. Because it holds food, making it equivalent to a vessel used for eating
C. Because it was periodically carried and lifted to show the shewbread miracle to pilgrims
D. Because it was made from acacia wood, a special category of valuable wood
Correct: C — Reish Lakish derives from the phrase 'upon the pure table' that the table was lifted and carried before Festival pilgrims to display the miracle of the shewbread. This portability classifies it as a movable vessel susceptible to impurity.
