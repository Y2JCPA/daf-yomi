🧺 Does Mistaken Identity Cause Impurity?

Rabbi Elazar bar Tzadok
Two wives of ḥaverim had their garments switched in the bathhouse. Each wore the other's garment — and the case came before the Sages, who treated the garments as pure!
Rabbi Oshaya
Objects! If so — one who reached into a basket expecting wheat bread and pulled out barley bread, would that loaf also become impure due to mistaken identity? That is absurd!
Rabbi Yirmeya
The barrel case resolves it: the guardian said 'I guarded it from impurity but not from things that merely invalidate it.' Partial guarding — guarding from one type but not another — is valid!
Rabbi Oshaya
But is there such a thing as 'guarding by half measures'? Can one truly be said to guard if he does not guard against all possibilities?
Resolution Yes — partial guarding is valid! A baraita proves it: one who inserts his hand into a basket of pure food, the shovel (kaf) in his hand does not render the basket impure — because a vessel cannot render another vessel impure. Partial conditions can be satisfied independently.

📜 Rabbi Yishmael and the Distracted Weavers

כמה נאין דברי חכמים שאמרו: נתכוין לשמור — טהור; לא נתכוין לשמור — טמא
כמה נאים דברי חכמים Two women came before Rabbi Yishmael. Each said: 'I wove this garment in a ritually pure state, but my mind was not on it to guard it.' Under close questioning, each revealed a moment of distraction — one distracted by her daughter's crying, the other by an incident mid-weaving. Rabbi Yishmael declared both garments impure. He then exclaimed: 'How great are the words of the Sages — if one's mind is focused on guarding, it is pure; if one's mind is not focused on guarding, it is impure.' The Gemara notes: in any case, intent to preserve purity is the operative criterion.

🏺 Special Purity Rules for Kodesh Vessels

Stage 1 — Manufacturing: A vessel is fashioned and completed in a state of ritual purity — no impure contact occurred during its creation.
↓
Stage 2 — Teruma use: The vessel may be used immediately for teruma without any additional purification. Its manufacture in purity is sufficient.
↓
Stage 2 — Kodesh use: Even though fashioned in purity, the vessel still requires immersion (tevila) in a mikveh before it may be used with sacrificial food.
↓
Why the extra step? Sacrificial food operates at a higher purity threshold — even latent susceptibility introduced by the manufacturing process must be ritually cleansed before contact with kodesh.
↓
Further Kodesh rule — combining (tziruf): A vessel combines all food within it. If one piece inside becomes impure, all food in the vessel is rendered impure for kodesh purposes.
