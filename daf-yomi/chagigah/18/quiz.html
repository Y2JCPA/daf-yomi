Chagigah 18 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Elazar citing Rabbi Oshaya, how many days of redress (תשלומין) does Shavuot have?
A. Three days
B. Five days
C. Seven days
D. Fourteen days
Correct: C — Rabbi Elazar said in the name of Rabbi Oshaya that Shavuot has seven days of redress, paralleling the seven-day structure of Passover and Sukkot.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Yochanan reject Reish Lakish's linguistic proof from 'Festival of Harvest'?
A. Because the verse refers to Sukkot, not Shavuot
B. Because applying the same method to 'Festival of Gathering' would yield an impossible result for Sukkot
C. Because Rabbi Elazar's tradition from Rabbi Oshaya overrides it
D. Because linguistic proofs are never valid in halakhic derivation
Correct: B — Rabbi Yochanan pointed out that 'Festival of Gathering' (=Sukkot) by the same linguistic method would produce an impossible result, thereby undermining Reish Lakish's entire approach.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the contradiction between the mishna here (tithes require hand washing) and Bikkurim 2:1 (tithes do not)?
A. The Bikkurim mishna is later and supersedes this one
B. Our mishna follows Rabbi Meir; Bikkurim follows the Rabbis
C. Our mishna discusses eating; Bikkurim discusses touching only
D. There is no contradiction — they discuss different types of tithes
Correct: B — The Gemara resolves the contradiction by noting that the Bikkurim mishna follows Rabbi Meir's stringent view, while our mishna follows the Rabbis. The Gemara also offers a second resolution: eating vs. touching.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does the case of Yosef ben Yo'ezer illustrate about the purity hierarchy?
A. Even the most pious priest may eat non-sacred food without washing hands
B. Perfect purity at one level may still constitute impurity for the next higher level
C. Priests are exempt from the laws of midras impurity
D. The garments of all priests are automatically pure for kodashim
Correct: B — Yosef ben Yo'ezer was the most pious priest for teruma, yet his garment was considered impure (midras of a zav) for those eating sacrificial food — illustrating that each ascending level of holiness treats the level below as a source of contamination.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Gemara, what is required for hand washing to achieve ritual purity?
A. Using exactly a quarter-log of water, no more and no less
B. Washing before sunrise only
C. Intention (כוונה) — without it the hands remain impure despite the physical act
D. The presence of a rabbi or witness to validate the washing
Correct: C — The Gemara teaches that intention (כוונה) is essential: one who washes with intent to purify achieves purity; one who washes without intent does not — even if the physical act was performed correctly with 40 se'a of water.
