🌾 Shavuot Timing: Reish Lakish vs. Rabbi Yochanan

Reish Lakish
From 'Festival of Harvest' (Exodus 23:16) — on which Festival do you harvest? It must be Shavuot. Therefore Shavuot has 7 days of redress (תשלומין) — you can make up missed offerings throughout that week.
Rabbi Yochanan
By your method, consider 'Festival of Gathering' (Exodus 23:16) — on which Festival is there gathering? That must be Sukkot! When exactly? Your linguistic proof yields impossible conclusions for Sukkot, so the method itself is flawed.
Reish Lakish
I do not dispute Rabbi Yochanan's refutation. We can infer, however, that both of us agree that Chol HaMoed labor is biblically prohibited — that much is beyond question.
Key Inference Since Reish Lakish does not dispute Rabbi Yochanan, both Amora'im hold that labor on intermediate Festival days (חול המועד) carries a biblical prohibition.

⚒️ Tracing the Source: Chol HaMoed Labor Prohibition

Question: What is the biblical source prohibiting labor on Chol HaMoed (intermediate days)?
↓
Baraita: 'You shall observe the Festival of Passover seven days' (Exodus 23:15) — the full seven days carry sanctity restricting labor
↓
⚠️ Objection
The six weekdays are bracketed by Shabbat sanctity — yet labor on them is permitted!
✅ Rebuttal
Those days carry no intrinsic sanctity; the Festival seven days bear inherent holiness — a qualitative difference.
↓
Second Baraita (Deut. 16:8): 'On the seventh day there shall be a solemn assembly' — the seventh day alone is precluded from all labor; the other six are not fully precluded.
↓
Conclusion: Chol HaMoed labor is biblically restricted, but only the Yom Tov days themselves prohibit all forms of labor.

🏛️ Yosef ben Yo'ezer — Holiness Within Holiness

בגדי אוכלי תרומה מדרס לקדשים — The garments of those who eat teruma are considered midras-impurity for those who eat kodashim (Mishna, Chagigah 18b).
Case Study: The Pious Priest's Paradox Yosef ben Yo'ezer was deemed the most pious priest of his generation, painstakingly eating teruma in full ritual purity. Yet in the eyes of those who ate sacrificial food (קדשים), his very garment bore the impurity of midras — the tread-impurity of a zav — a primary level of ritual impurity.

This is the logic of graduated sanctity: each ascending level of holiness treats the level below it as a source of contamination. Perfect purity at one level is still impure at the next.

🎯 Intention (כוונה) in Ritual Hand Washing

Rule 1: One who washes hands with intention (כוונה) to purify — hands are pure.
↓
Rule 2: One who washes hands without intention — hands remain impure, despite the physical act.
↓
Parallel case: One who immerses hands in 40 se'a of water — with intention: pure; without intention: impure. Same principle applies.
↓
Implication: Physical performance alone is insufficient. Conscious direction of the act toward its purificatory purpose is required for halakhic validity.
↓
Broader principle: Kavanah (כוונה) threads through rabbinic purification — ritual acts without mental engagement do not achieve their halakhic effect.
