📋 Who Is Exempt from Temple Appearance (Re'iyah)?

Fully Exempt

  • 🧏 Deaf-mute (cheresh): cannot hear or speak
  • 🤪 Imbecile (shoteh): lacks mental capacity
  • 👶 Minor (katan): not yet obligated in mitzvot
  • All three are also exempt from re'iyah and hakhel

Textual Source

  • Gezera shava: 'appearance' at re'iyah = 'appearance' at hakhel
  • 'That they may hear and learn' — excludes those who cannot
  • 'In their ears' — must hear with both ears to be obligated
  • Exemption at hakhel implies exemption at re'iyah

Partially Exempt (R. Tanḥum)

  • 🦻 Deaf in one ear: exempt from re'iyah
  • 🦿 Lame in one leg: exempt from re'iyah
  • 'In their ears' → both ears required
  • 'Regalim' → both legs required

🗣️ Why Is a Mute Exempt from Hakhel?

Mar Zutra
Read the verse as 'that they may TEACH [yelamdu]' — a mute cannot teach others, so he cannot be obligated in the assembly.
Rav Ashi
No — the verse definitely means 'learn [yilmedu].' A mute cannot articulate his learning properly, and is therefore exempt.
Gemara Challenge
But two mute men in R. Yehuda HaNasi's neighborhood — after being miraculously healed — were found proficient in Mishna, Sifra, Sifrei, and all of Talmud! Mutes clearly CAN learn!
Gemara Resolution
Mar Zutra's reading ('teach') is superior — even if a mute absorbs Torah, he cannot transmit it to others. The assembly obligation requires the capacity to perpetuate Torah, not merely receive it.
Historical Note The miracle of R. Yehuda HaNasi's mute nephews demonstrates that muteness does not prevent comprehension — forcing the Gemara to locate the exemption in the inability to teach, not the inability to learn.

👧 Why Do Children Attend Hakhel?

הַקְהֵל אֶת הָעָם הָאֲנָשִׁים וְהַנָּשִׁים וְהַטַּף
Rabbi Elazar ben Azarya's Teaching Deuteronomy 31:12 — 'Assemble the people, the men and the women and the little ones.'

If men come to learn and women come to listen — why bring infants who understand nothing?

To give reward to those who bring them!

Parents who exert effort to bring young children to the assembly receive divine merit. The children's very presence generates spiritual benefit — even without their comprehension.

Rabbi Yehoshua was visibly pleased when his students reported this insight — he had asked them what novel teaching he had missed.

🗣️ Torah as Goads and Nails — From One Shepherd

"The words of the wise are as goads" (Ecclesiastes 12:11) — Torah prods and motivates, as a goad directs an ox to plow straight
↓
"As nails well fastened" — Torah rulings are firmly planted; they do not waver or fall away
↓
Challenge: A nail diminishes over time — does Torah shrink? No! 'Well fastened' means planted upward — Torah grows and expands, it does not diminish
↓
"Given from one shepherd" — All opinions, even contradictory ones (ritually pure / impure; permitted / forbidden), were given by One God, transmitted by Moses
↓
Practical lesson: Make your ear like a funnel — acquire a receptive heart to hear both those who rule impure and those who rule pure, without dismissing either
