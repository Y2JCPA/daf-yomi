QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, under what condition does an impure hand render another hand impure regarding sacrificial food?
A. Only if it is one's own other hand, not a stranger's hand
B. Whether one's own or another person's hand, provided the same impure hand made contact
C. Only if both hands are wet with liquid
D. Only if the second hand simultaneously touches the sacrificial food
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan rules that an impure hand defiles any hand it touches — one's own or another person's — as long as it is the same impure hand making contact. Reish Lakish initially disagreed but eventually retracted to agree with this ruling.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
From which verse does the Gemara derive that sacrificial food has a third degree of impurity from the Torah?
A. "You shall be holy, for I the Lord your God am holy" (Lev. 19:2)
B. "And the meat that touches any impure thing shall not be eaten" (Lev. 7:19)
C. "Speak to the priests, the sons of Aaron" (Lev. 21:1)
D. "Whatever touches them shall become holy" (Lev. 6:11)
Correct: B — The Gemara derives a Torah-level third degree of impurity for sacrificial food from Leviticus 7:19: 'And the meat that touches any impure thing shall not be eaten' — implying food that touched a second-degree impure item (which itself touched the primary impurity) is now forbidden.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rabbi Akiva's testimony primarily needed to establish, given that vessel-combining is already Torah law?
A. That incense and frankincense are combined by a vessel
B. That wet foods are more susceptible to impurity than dry ones
C. That even leftover meal-offering flour — which does not require a vessel — is combined by a vessel through Rabbinic decree
D. That the combining power of vessels applies only to liquids
Correct: C — The basic Torah law of vessel-combining is already known. Rabbi Akiva's testimony extends the rule by Rabbinic decree to the flour remainders of the meal-offering, which do not inherently require a vessel — teaching that the vessel still combines them for purposes of impurity.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did the Sages decree that an acute mourner (onen) must immerse before eating sacrificial food?
A. Because an onen's mourning creates corpse-impurity requiring full immersion
B. Because the Torah explicitly requires it in the laws of the peace-offering
C. Because until becoming an onen the person ate kodashim without immersion; the change in status required marking with a new immersion
D. Because an onen is classified as lacking atonement (mechusar kaparah)
Correct: C — The Sages' reasoning: until now the person freely ate sacrificial food without needing special immersion. The transition to onen status — even though not technically impure — warranted a Rabbinic immersion decree to mark the new status before resuming kodashim consumption.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the new mishna (end of Daf 24), during which period are amei ha'aretz in Judea trusted regarding the purity of teruma wine and oil?
A. Year-round without exception
B. Only during Passover, when they enter the Temple courtyards
C. During the winepress and olive-press harvest seasons only
D. Never — amei ha'aretz are never trusted for teruma
Correct: C — The mishna teaches that amei ha'aretz in Judea are trusted for teruma purity specifically during the winepress and olive-press seasons. The Sages enacted this leniency so they would not separate teruma while impure. Once the seasons end, the ḥaver must refuse barrels of teruma wine or oil from them.
