🌊 Ben Zoma's Vision of the Waters

וְרוּחַ אֱלֹהִים מְרַחֶפֶת עַל פְּנֵי הַמָּיִם
Too Deep in Thought to Rise Ben Zoma saw Rabbi Yehoshua on the Temple Mount steps but did not stand to honor him — he was absorbed in contemplating the verse: 'The spirit of God hovered over the face of the waters' (Bereishit 1:2).

He calculated: the upper and lower waters are separated by only a hairsbreadth — like a craftsman's tool between two planks. The hovering of God's spirit was like a dove hovering over its nest — touching but not touching.

Rabbi Yehoshua turned to his students and said sadly: Ben Zoma is gone — meaning, his mind had left him from excessive mystical contemplation.

👼 Metatron and the Error of Two Powers

Acher enters the Pardes and ascends to behold the heavenly realm — unlike Rabbi Akiva, he is spiritually unprepared
↓
He sees Metatron seated in heaven — sitting is normally forbidden in God's presence (an honor unique to God)
↓
Acher concludes there must be two powers [שתי רשויות] — Metatron as a second divine being
↓
The Heavenly Court removes Metatron and smites him with 60 rods [pulsei] of fire — to prevent others from making the same error
↓
Metatron had been granted special permission to sit while recording the merits of Israel — a temporary administrative function, not divinity
↓
Acher's heresy is now sealed — his misreading of a permitted angelic posture shattered his faith irreparably

📖 Acher Questions Rabbi Meir After Going Astray

Acher
What does the verse mean: 'God made even the one as well as the other' (Kohelet 7:14)?
Rabbi Meir
Everything God created has its paired opposite — mountains and hills, seas and rivers, all correspond in pairs.
Acher
Rabbi Akiva taught it differently: He created the righteous and the wicked; He created Gan Eden and Gehenna. Each person has two portions — one in each place.
Rabbi Meir
What does the verse mean: 'Gold and glass cannot equal it' (Iyov 28:17)? Torah is as precious as gold but as easily shattered as glass.
Acher
Just as glass vessels can be repaired when broken, a Torah scholar who sins can return! Why then can I not repent?
Rabbi Meir
Gold vessels can also be repaired — but who told you that you cannot repent? Return, Acher.
The Tragic Barrier A heavenly voice had declared Acher excluded from repentance — 'Return, O backsliding children — except for Acher.' He heard it and chose to believe it was final.

📚 May One Learn Torah from a Flawed Teacher?

Permit — Focus on the Content

  • Reish Lakish: 'Apply your heart to My knowledge' (Prov. 22:17) — not 'their knowledge'; the Torah belongs to God regardless of who teaches it
  • Rav Ḥanina: 'Forget your father's house' (Ps. 45:11) — one must receive wisdom even from an unworthy source
  • Rabbi Meir ate the date and discarded the peel — he extracted Torah wisdom from Acher without absorbing the heresy
  • In the West (Eretz Yisrael) they praised Rabbi Meir: 'He found a half-ripe date, ate the inside, and threw away the peel'

Prohibit — Teacher Must Be Worthy

  • Rabbi Yoḥanan: 'The priest's lips should keep knowledge, and they should seek Torah from his mouth — for he is an angel of the Lord' (Mal. 2:7)
  • If the teacher does not resemble an angel of God, do not seek Torah from his mouth
  • Rava's resolution: Only one as exceptional as Rabbi Meir may do this — ordinary students risk absorbing the teacher's flaws along with the Torah
  • Rabbi Yoḥanan himself refused to learn from Acher: 'Was there no one else to save him but by burning his teacher's grave?'
