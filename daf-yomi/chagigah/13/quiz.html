Chagigah 13 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What does the word 'hashmal' (חשמל) represent as an acrostic, according to Rav Yehuda?
A. Whispering holy flames of the throne
B. Speaking animals of fire (ḥayyot esh memallelot)
C. Hidden secrets of the Divine Chariot
D. Fiery wheels surrounding the divine presence
Correct: B — Rav Yehuda explains ḥashmal as an acrostic for ḥayyot esh memallelot — speaking animals of fire. A baraita adds: they are silent when God speaks, and speak when He does not.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rabbi Ami says esoteric Torah secrets may only be transmitted to someone possessing five specific traits. From which verse does he derive these traits?
A. Ezekiel 1:4 — the storm cloud vision
B. Isaiah 3:3 — the captain of fifty and the man of favor
C. Psalms 90:10 — the span of seventy years
D. Proverbs 27:26 — the lambs for your clothing
Correct: B — Rabbi Ami derives the five required characteristics from Isaiah 3:3: 'the captain of fifty, and the man of favor, and the counselor, and the cunning charmer, and the skillful enchanter.'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rava compares Ezekiel's detailed Chariot vision to a villager seeing the king. To whom does he compare Isaiah's briefer account?
A. A child who is frightened by the king's guards
B. A city-dweller accustomed to seeing the king
C. A servant who keeps his eyes downcast before royalty
D. A scholar who records only what is strictly necessary
Correct: B — Rava says Ezekiel is like a villager dazzled by royal grandeur, while Isaiah is like a city-dweller who sees the king regularly and feels no need to elaborate. Both saw the same vision.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Elazar decline when Rabbi Yochanan offered to teach him the Design of the Divine Chariot?
A. He had already learned it from a different master
B. He had not yet aged enough — these studies require a deeply settled mind
C. The Halacha forbids transmission of Merkavah in any form
D. He feared being consumed by fire as the youth had been
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar said he was not yet old enough, as Merkavah study demands extreme mental and spiritual composure. By the time he felt ready, Rabbi Yochanan had died — and the opportunity was lost.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Rabbis (as opposed to Rav's opinion), which two wings did the divine creatures lose between Isaiah's vision and Ezekiel's vision?
A. The wings used for flying through the firmaments
B. The wings used for covering their faces before God
C. The wings used for singing praises to God
D. The wings used for covering their feet
Correct: D — The Rabbis argue that the feet-covering wings were diminished — proof being that Ezekiel describes 'straight feet,' which would only be visible if those wings were gone. Rav held instead that the singing wings were lost.
