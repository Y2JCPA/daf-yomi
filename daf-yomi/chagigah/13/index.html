💬 Teaching the Merkavah — A Missed Opportunity

R. Yochanan
Come, and I will teach you the Design of the Divine Chariot.
R. Elazar
I have not yet aged sufficiently. One must be deeply settled in mind before approaching these studies.
Narrator
When Rabbi Elazar finally grew old, Rabbi Yochanan had already died — and the opportunity was lost.
Rav Yosef
I know the Merkavah well. But the Elders of Pumbedita urged me to teach them — and I replied: If a simple elder is as two, I am not yet qualified to judge.
R. Abbahu
Proverbs says 'kevasim' — read it as kevashim: things that are concealed [כְּבָשִׁים], they are for your clothing alone. Do not expose them to all.
Lesson Even great sages deferred studying Merkavah — and some lost the chance entirely. Esoteric wisdom demands not only intellect but spiritual maturity and the right moment.

👁️ Ezekiel and Isaiah: Same Vision, Different Response (Rava)

Ezekiel — The Villager

  • Saw the Divine Chariot in exile, far from Jerusalem
  • Like a villager who sees the king for the first time
  • Astonished by every detail of royal grandeur
  • Described Chayot, Ofanim, Hashmal, fire, and wings at length
  • Chapters 1 and 10 of Ezekiel are elaborate accounts

Isaiah — The City-Dweller

  • Saw the same Chariot vision (Isaiah 6)
  • Like a city-dweller accustomed to seeing the king
  • Did not feel the need to elaborate on familiar grandeur
  • Simply wrote: 'I saw the Lord seated on a high throne' (Isa. 6:1)
  • Brevity reflects familiarity, not a lesser vision

חַיּוֹת אֵשׁ מְמַלְּלוֹת
חַשְׁמַל — What Is Electrum? Rav Yehuda: It refers to speaking animals of fire. The word is an acrostic:
חayyot — שh (esh, fire) — מemalלelot

A baraita elaborates: At times they are silent; at times they speak. When divine speech emerges from the mouth of the Holy One, they are silent. When divine speech is not emerging, they speak.

Warning: A youth who expounded the Hashmal was consumed by fire that came out of it. The Sages sought to suppress Ezekiel entirely — until Ḥananya ben Ḥizkiya reconciled its contradictions with the Torah.

🪶 Resolving the Wings Contradiction: Six Wings (Isaiah) vs. Four Wings (Ezekiel)

Isaiah 6:2 — Six wings per creature
Two for face, two for feet, two for flying
↓
Ezekiel 1:6 — Four wings per creature
Two wings are missing — when and why?
↓
Between Isaiah and Ezekiel, two wings were diminished
↓
Rav Ḥananel (citing Rav)
Lost the singing wings.
Proof: Isaiah writes 'with two it flew' immediately before 'one called to another' — once singing ceased (Temple destroyed), those wings were taken.
The Rabbis
Lost the feet-covering wings.
Proof: Ezekiel describes 'straight feet' — visible only because the covering wings were already gone.
