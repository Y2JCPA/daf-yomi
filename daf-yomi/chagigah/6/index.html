⚖️ Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel: Values of the Festival Offerings

Beit Shammai
Burnt-offering of appearance: 2 silver coins.
Festival peace-offering: 1 silver ma'a.
The burnt-offering ascends entirely to God — it demands greater honor.
Beit Hillel
Burnt-offering of appearance: 1 silver ma'a.
Festival peace-offering: 2 silver coins.
The peace-offering predated Sinai (Exodus 24:5) and enables Festival rejoicing — it is primary.
Beit Shammai
The Sinai burnt-offering was an olat re'iya. The peace-offering of the princes cannot teach the value of the Festival peace-offering — a one-time matter cannot be derived from a recurring practice.
Beit Hillel
The Sinai burnt-offering was the daily tamid — a communal offering. No individual burnt-offerings existed before Sinai. Therefore it cannot prove the burnt-offering of appearance deserves greater value.
Core Disagreement Shammai: the wholly-ascending burnt-offering is primary. Hillel: the pre-Sinaitic peace-offering that enables human joy is primary. Each school inverts the other's hierarchy.

📊 Minimum Values of the Two Festival Offerings

Beit Shammai

  • 🔥 Olat Re'iya (burnt-offering): 2 silver coins
  • 🕊️ Shlamei Chagiga (peace-offering): 1 silver ma'a
  • Reason: The burnt-offering is entirely consumed by fire — it honors God above all
  • Proof: Derived from the burnt-offering sacrificed at Mount Sinai (an olat re'iya)

Beit Hillel

  • 🔥 Olat Re'iya (burnt-offering): 1 silver ma'a
  • 🕊️ Shlamei Chagiga (peace-offering): 2 silver coins
  • Reason: The peace-offering predates Sinai and enables the mitzva of rejoicing
  • Proof: Derived from the peace-offerings of the tribal princes (Nasi'im)

👶 Abaye's Chinuch Principle: When Must a Minor Be Trained?

Is the adult obligated in this mitzva by Torah law?
✅ Yes — Adult Obligated
Train the minor in this mitzva by rabbinic law (חינוך)
❌ No — Adult Exempt
Minor is also exempt — no chinuch obligation by rabbinic law either
↓
Application to our case: A minor who is permanently lame or blind — since an adult in that condition is exempt from Temple appearance by Torah law, there is no chinuch obligation for such a minor.
Rabbi Shimon's dilemma is resolved: the question only applies to temporary conditions.

⛰️ What Was the Burnt-Offering Sacrificed at Mount Sinai?

Rabbi Yishmael
General mitzvot were given at Sinai; specific details were given later at the Tent of Meeting.
→ The Sinai offering was an olat re'iya, given only in general terms — details came later.
Rabbi Akiva
Both general rules and specific details were given at Sinai, then repeated at the Tent of Meeting.
→ The Sinai offering was the daily tamid, which never ceased — the tribe of Levi funded it alone.
Rabbi Elazar
"It is a daily burnt-offering which was performed on Mount Sinai" (Numbers 28:6).
The details were stated at Sinai; the offering itself was the daily tamid, not an olat re'iya.
Rabbi Yosei HaGelili
The Sinai burnt-offering was the daily tamid — it did not require skinning and cutting into pieces at that time, since those details were transmitted only later at the Tent of Meeting.
Abaye's Alignment (6b) Beit Shammai + Rabbi Elazar + Rabbi Yishmael → Sinai offering = olat re'iya. Beit Hillel + Rabbi Akiva + Rabbi Yosei HaGelili → Sinai offering = daily tamid.

⚖️ Rabbi Yosei HaGelili: Each Offering Has a Unique Quality

🔥 Burnt-Offering of Appearance (עולת ראייה)
Unique quality: It is offered entirely to God — wholly consumed on the altar, nothing returned to the offerer. The other two offerings are not like this.
↓
🕊️ Festival Peace-Offering (שלמי חגיגה)
Unique quality: It predated the giving of the Torah at Sinai — "And they sacrificed peace-offerings of bulls to the Lord" (Exodus 24:5). The other two did not exist before Sinai.
↓
🎉 Peace-Offering of Rejoicing (שלמי שמחה)
Unique quality: It fulfills the mitzva of simchat yom tov — rejoicing on the Festival by eating meat. The other two offerings do not serve this function.
↓
Halakhic Conclusion: Because each offering possesses an exclusive quality absent in the others, no offering can be fully derived from another. Each stands on independent halakhic footing.
