QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥiyya bar Abba citing Rabbi Yoḥanan, which biblical passage did Rabbi Akiva expound to protect himself from Aḥer's error?
A. "Preeminent above a myriad" (Song of Songs 5:10)
B. "The Lord of Hosts is His name" (Isaiah 48:2)
C. "The Lord was not in the wind… not in the earthquake… not in the fire" (I Kings 19)
D. "And He came from the holy myriads" (Deuteronomy 33:2)
Correct: C — Rav Ḥiyya bar Abba citing Rabbi Yoḥanan taught that Rabbi Akiva expounded the Elijah passage (I Kings 19): God was absent from wind, earthquake, and fire — those are the angels themselves. This made clear that God and His angelic forces are categorically distinct.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In what way are demons described as similar to ministering angels, according to the baraita?
A. They eat and drink
B. They die like humans
C. They fly from one end of the world to the other
D. They multiply and procreate
Correct: C — The baraita lists three angel-like traits: demons have wings, fly across the entire world, and know the future (by hearing from behind the Divine curtain). Eating, dying, and multiplying are their three human-like traits.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Abba, what act constitutes 'lack of concern for the honor of one's Maker'?
A. Sinning in private where no witnesses are present
B. Looking at a rainbow
C. Failing to stand when a Torah scholar passes
D. Engaging in forbidden mystical speculation
Correct: B — Rabbi Abba teaches that looking at a rainbow is a lack of concern for the Creator's honor. The rainbow symbolizes divine glory — as in Ezekiel 1:28 where the rainbow is compared to the appearance of God's glory — and staring at it reflects insufficient reverence.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Shimon ben Shataḥ tell Yehuda ben Tabbai that he had shed innocent blood by executing the conspiring witness?
A. The Sanhedrin of 71 had not convened to confirm the verdict
B. Conspiring witnesses are only executed when both members of the pair are found to be conspirators
C. The witness had testified about a capital case and only the king could authorize execution
D. The conspiracy had not yet caused financial harm to the accused
Correct: B — The law of conspiring witnesses (עדים זוממים) requires that both witnesses be found liable together as a pair. Yehuda ben Tabbai executed only one of the two, meaning that witness had not been properly condemned — making the execution the killing of an innocent person.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rami bar Ḥama derive from the very existence of the semicha dispute among the Zugot?
A. Women are completely exempt from placing hands on offerings
B. The dispute is merely about timing — whether to do semicha before or after the Festival
C. The mitzva of semicha requires pressing with one's full strength, not merely resting hands
D. Semicha applies only to burnt-offerings, not peace-offerings
Correct: C — Rami bar Ḥama reasons: if semicha were just a casual gesture (hands resting lightly), it would not be significant enough to debate as a potential Festival labor violation. The debate's intensity proves semicha must involve pressing with full force — making it substantive enough to resemble forbidden labor.
