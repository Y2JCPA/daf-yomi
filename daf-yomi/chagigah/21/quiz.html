Chagigah 21 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the mishna, immersing one vessel inside another is permitted for which category?
A. Kodashim (sacrificial food)
B. Teruma (heave-offering)
C. Neither — it is always forbidden
D. Both teruma and kodashim
Correct: B — The mishna permits immersing one vessel inside another for teruma but forbids it for kodashim, which requires a stricter standard of purity with no interposition.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the primary concern that makes vessel-in-vessel immersion problematic for kodashim?
A. The vessels may break and become impure
B. Chatzitza — the outer vessel interposes and blocks water from the inner vessel
C. The onen status of the owner
D. The vessels were purchased from non-Jews
Correct: B — The concern is chatzitza (interposition) — the outer vessel blocks water from fully surrounding and reaching the inner vessel, which fails the stricter purity standard required for sacrificial food.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara note that knots tighten in water?
A. To explain why all knots must be untied before any immersion
B. To show that knotted handles create a real interposition in water, blocking purification of kodashim vessels
C. To prove rope vessels are categorically forbidden for kodashim
D. To illustrate why teruma vessels with knots are also prohibited
Correct: B — Knots tighten when submerged, creating a genuine chatzitza that prevents water from penetrating — this is why knotted vessel handles are specifically problematic for the higher purity standard of kodashim.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What did Rabbi Ila say in the name of Rabbi Ḥanina bar Pappa about the mishna's structure?
A. The mishna lists five leniencies unique to teruma
B. The mishna teaches ten stringencies of sacrificial food gathered in one place
C. The mishna's first clause is more lenient than the latter clause
D. The mishna discusses only cases of Torah-level impurity
Correct: B — Rabbi Ila said in the name of Rabbi Ḥanina bar Pappa that the mishna groups ten stringencies of sacrificial food together, with the first five connected to Torah impurity and the latter five being purely rabbinic safeguards.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How did Rava challenge Rabbi Ila's claim that both clauses share the reason of interposition?
A. Rava proved kodashim and teruma share identical purity rules throughout
B. Rava counted more than ten stringencies in the mishna
C. Rava argued that since the latter clause states interposition as its reason, the first clause must have a different, distinct reason
D. Rava held that the onen's restriction is entirely unrelated to immersion rules
Correct: C — Rava used the mishna's own structure as evidence: the fact that interposition is stated explicitly only as the reason for the latter clause implies the first clause must rest on a separate, distinct principle — otherwise why state the reason at all?
