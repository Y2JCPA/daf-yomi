🗺️ Why Is Galilee Excluded from Am Ha'aretz Wine Trust?

Reish Lakish
A strip of Samaritan [Kuti] land separates Galilee from Judea. The Sages decreed Samaritan lands impure as graveyards — any wine transported through it becomes impure.
Gemara (Challenge)
Let them place the wine in a closed box, chest, or closet — these have the status of separate tents and protect against transmitted impurity!
Gemara (2nd Challenge)
And further — let them use an earthenware vessel with a tightly bound cover, which is even spared from a corpse in the same tent (Num. 19:15)!
Gemara (Resolution via Ulla)
ḥaverim in the Galilee already purify their wine to sacrificial standards locally — there is no need to transport anything through Samaritan land at all.
Key Insight The Samaritan strip decree is the root problem. The sealed-vessel solutions are rejected, and ḥaverim solve the issue by local purification rather than transit.

⚖️ Judea vs. Galilee — Am Ha'aretz Trust for Wine & Oil

Judea ✅

  • Amei ha'aretz trusted for sacrificial wine & oil
  • Trusted during winepress & olive press season
  • Also trusted 70 days before the winepress begins
  • May bring teruma to ḥaver priest during the season
  • Direct land access — no Samaritan strip in the way

Galilee ❌

  • Amei ha'aretz not trusted for sacrificial wine & oil
  • Separated from Judea by impure Samaritan strip
  • Sealed vessels & covered earthenware rejected as solutions
  • ḥaverim in Galilee purify locally as a workaround
  • Water of purification also not spared by tightly bound cover

⚰️ Beit HaPeras — Who May Traverse It?

Beit HaPeras: field where a grave was plowed, scattering bones — Rabbinically decreed impure
↓
Paschal Offering Pilgrim
May blow at the ground while walking (Rav Yehuda / Shmuel) OR rely on a well-trodden path (R. Ḥiyya bar Abba / Ulla) — bones assumed cleared
Teruma Eater
Sages upheld the impurity decree — eating impure teruma risks karet; no leniency granted here
↓
Dilemma: If one examined the field for paschal purposes, may he also eat teruma after traversing it? Left unresolved — older sage defends Ulla's position from the mishna's quarter-log ruling

📅 The Seventy-Day Window & the Modi'im Boundary

Core season trust: During the winepress & olive press, amei ha'aretz are trusted even for teruma — everyone purifies vessels at this time, creating universal reliability.
↓
Seventy days before the winepress: Trust extends back 70 days as people begin purifying vessels in preparation. Abaye notes this incidentally teaches: the lulav is valid for 7 days in the Temple.
↓
After the season ends: A ḥaver priest may not accept an am ha'aretz's barrel of teruma wine — but the am ha'aretz may leave it over until the following winepress season when trust resumes.
↓
Modi'im boundary (new mishna): From Modi'im — 15 mil from Jerusalem — and inward, all potters including amei ha'aretz are trusted for the purity of their vessels for Temple use.
↓
Modi'im itself is sometimes inside, sometimes outside the boundary — it depends on where the potter is standing when conducting the sale of his vessels.
