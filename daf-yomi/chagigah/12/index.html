⚖️ Was Heaven or Earth Created First?

Beit Shammai
Heaven was created first, then earth — as it is written: 'In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth' (Gen. 1:1). Heaven is named first.
Beit Hillel
Earth was created first — as it is written: 'On the day the Lord God made earth and heaven' (Gen. 2:4). Does a builder construct the upper floor before the lower?
Beit Shammai
Your analogy fails! A king makes his throne first, then his footstool: 'Heaven is My throne and the earth is My footstool' (Isaiah 66:1).
Sages (Resolution)
Both were created simultaneously — 'My hand founded the earth and My right hand spread the heavens; I called to them together' (Isaiah 48:13). Neither precedes the other.
יַחְדָּו — Together The word 'together' (יַחְדָּו) means heaven and earth do not separate from one another — they were formed as one simultaneous act of creation

🕯️ The Journey of the Primordial Light (אוֹר הַגָּנוּז)

Day One: God creates a special primordial light — through it a person could see from one end of the world to the other
↓
God Foresees: He saw the corrupt deeds of the generation of the Flood and the generation of the Tower of Babel
↓
Concealment: God hid the Or HaGanuz so that the wicked could not exploit it — 'And God saw the light, that it was good' (Gen. 1:4)
↓
The Proof: 'Good' (טוֹב) always refers to the righteous — 'Say of the righteous, that it is good' (Isaiah 3:10)
↓
The Light Rejoices: When the light learned it was reserved for the righteous, it rejoiced — 'The light for the righteous shall rejoice' (Prov. 13:9)
↓
Future Revelation: The Or HaGanuz is stored in Aravot (highest firmament) and will illuminate the righteous in the World to Come

🏛️ The Seven Firmaments — Reish Lakish

1. Vilon (וִילוֹן) — Curtain

  • Contains nothing substantial
  • Enters at morning, departs at evening
  • Renews the work of Creation daily
  • "Who stretches out the heavens like a curtain" (Ps. 104:2)

2. Rakia (רָקִיעַ) — Firmament

  • Sun, moon, stars, constellations fixed here
  • "God set them in the firmament of the heaven" (Gen. 1:17)

3. Sheḥakim (שְׁחָקִים) — Skies

  • Millstones stand here grinding manna for the righteous
  • "He commanded the skies… He rained manna" (Ps. 78:23–24)

4. Zevul (זְבוּל) — Abode

  • Heavenly Jerusalem and Temple located here
  • Angel Michael stands here offering sacrifices
  • "I have surely built a house of Zevul for You" (I Kings 8:13)

5. Ma'on (מָעוֹן) — Habitation

  • Ministering angels sing here at night
  • Silent by day — to honor Israel's prayers
  • "By day the Lord commands His kindness; at night His song is with me" (Ps. 42:9)

6. Makhon (מָכוֹן) — Dwelling

  • Storehouses of snow, hail, and harmful dews
  • Chambers of tempests, storms, and mist
  • "The Lord will open His good treasury, the heavens" (Deut. 28:12)

7. Aravot (עֲרָבוֹת) — Highest Heaven

  • Righteousness, justice, charity, life, peace, blessing
  • Souls of the righteous and souls yet to be created
  • Dew of resurrection; ofanim, seraphim, Throne of Glory
  • God Himself dwells here: "Extol Him Who rides upon the Aravot" (Ps. 68:5)

👤 The Cosmic Dimensions of Adam HaRishon

לְמִן הַיּוֹם אֲשֶׁר בָּרָא אֱלֹהִים אָדָם עַל הָאָרֶץ וּלְמִקְצֵה הַשָּׁמַיִם וְעַד קְצֵה הַשָּׁמָיִם (דברים ד:לב)
How Great Was the First Man? Rabbi Elazar: Adam's height stretched from the ground to the skies — derived from: 'Since the day God created man upon the earth… and from one end of the heavens to the other' (Deut. 4:32).

Rav (cited by Rav Yehuda): Adam's breadth extended from one end of the world to the other — the second half of the same verse indicates his span across the earth.

Contradiction resolved: Both measures are one equal measure — the height of the world equals its width. This and that are the same.

After Adam sinned: God placed His hand upon him and diminished him — 'You have formed me behind and before, and laid Your hand upon me' (Ps. 139:5).
