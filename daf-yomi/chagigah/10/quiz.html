Chagigah 10 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Shmuel teaches that abandoning Torah study means leaving which discipline for which?
A. Leaving the synagogue to study at home
B. Leaving Talmud study to learn only Mishna
C. Leaving Aggadah to study halachic codes
D. Leaving prayer to study Torah
Correct: B — Shmuel taught that Talmud includes reasoning, while Mishna only states rulings. Abandoning Talmud for Mishna means losing the depth of Torah study — that is the 'adversary' of Zechariah 8:10.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Mishna say the halakhot of dissolution of vows 'fly in the air'?
A. They apply only in cases of extreme emotional distress
B. The Sages disagreed so sharply that no ruling was ever reached
C. They have no explicit scriptural support, yet have extensive detailed laws
D. They were transmitted only orally and are no longer practiced
Correct: C — The metaphor means these laws lack a clear anchor in the written Torah — yet Oral Torah developed rich, detailed rulings around them. The Gemara then has Sages compete to provide the best scriptural source.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rava say Shmuel's proof from Numbers 30:3 is superior to all the other sources for dissolving vows?
A. It is quoted by more Sages than any other verse
B. Every other proposed source can be refuted; only Shmuel's cannot
C. Numbers 30:3 explicitly mentions a rabbinic court
D. It was cited by Moses himself at Sinai
Correct: B — Rava shows that Rabbi Eliezer's, Rabbi Yehoshua's, Rabbi Yitzḥak's, and Rabbi Ḥananya's sources each admit an alternative reading that undermines the derivation. Only 'he shall not nullify' — implying others can — is airtight.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why are Shabbat laws 'mountains suspended by a hair' if the Shabbat prohibition is explicit in the Torah?
A. The 39 categories of forbidden labor appear only in the Oral Torah
B. The elaborate laws of unintended, destructive, and unnecessary-purpose labor far exceed what the Torah text states
C. Shabbat was revealed at Sinai without any written record
D. The Shabbat prohibition applies only in the Land of Israel according to one opinion
Correct: B — The Gemara focuses on edge cases: unintended acts, destructive labor, and labor not necessary for its own sake. These nuanced distinctions — debated between Rabbi Shimon and Rabbi Yehuda — are not derivable from the brief Torah text.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the laws of misuse of consecrated property, what is the surprising ruling when an owner sends an agent to use the consecrated item?
A. Neither is liable, since the owner lacked direct contact and the agent lacked intent
B. Only the agent is liable, since the agent performed the physical act
C. The owner is liable even though it was the agent who performed the act
D. Liability is split equally between owner and agent
Correct: C — This is counterintuitive: normally an agent's unauthorized act does not bind the principal in sin. Yet here the owner bears liability — the Gemara identifies this as a prime example of a law that 'hangs by a hair,' since it is derived from a verbal analogy rather than an explicit verse.
