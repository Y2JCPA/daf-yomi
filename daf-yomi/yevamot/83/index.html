The Hermaphrodite Debate & Following Rabbi Yosei

Mishna (R' Yosei & R' Shimon)
A hermaphrodite priest enables his wife to eat teruma.
Baraita (R' Yosei alone)
A hermaphrodite is a creature unto himself (beryah bifnei atzmah) — he does not enable her to eat teruma.
Rav
The baraita overrides the mishna — R' Yosei retracted. Halakha follows R' Yosei on hermaphrodite and grafting.
Shmuel
The mishna stands! Halakha follows R' Yosei on protracted labor and proscription only.
🔑 Core Issue Rav and Shmuel disagree on which of Rabbi Yosei's rulings to follow, creating a map of accepted halakhot across four domains.

Mishna vs. Baraita: Hermaphrodite & Teruma

Mishna
R' Yosei and R' Shimon say: A hermaphrodite priest enables his wife to eat teruma.
Baraita
R' Yosei says: A hermaphrodite is a creature unto himself. The Sages did not determine if he is male or female. He does not enable teruma.
Gemara
Since R' Yosei left R' Shimon (the baraita has only R' Yosei), he must have retracted his original position.
Shmuel
The baraita is not relied upon against the mishna. Shmuel only uses a baraita to add to a mishna, not to uproot it.
Principle Shmuel considers individual opinions in baraitot only when they supplement — not contradict — the mishna.

Sabbatical Year Planting Deadlines

When must one stop planting before Rosh HaShana of the Sabbatical Year?
↓
First Tanna
30 days to root + 30 days tosefet shevi'it = 60 days before R"H
R' Yehuda
3 days to root + 30 days tosefet = 33 days before R"H
R' Yosei & R' Shimon
14 days to root + 30 days tosefet = 44 days before R"H

Tumtum & Ḥalitza: R' Yehuda vs. R' Yosei b"R Yehuda

Rabbi Yehuda

  • A tumtum torn open and found male = definitely a eunuch
  • Cannot perform ḥalitza
  • If he did perform ḥalitza — does NOT disqualify other brothers
  • Certainty: his status is definitively impotent

Rabbi Yosei b"R Yehuda

  • A tumtum should not perform ḥalitza — perhaps found female, or if male — perhaps a eunuch
  • Status is doubtful, not certain
  • If he did perform ḥalitza — DOES disqualify other brothers
  • If no other brothers — must still perform ḥalitza (due to doubt)
Rava The practical difference: whether the tumtum's ḥalitza disqualifies other brothers from levirate marriage, and whether he must perform ḥalitza when he is the only brother.

The Tumtum of Biri

טומטום שהיה בבירי
🏘️ Real-World Challenge Rabbi Ami challenged Rabbi Yehuda: A tumtum in the town of Biri was torn open surgically and later fathered seven children! How can you say every torn-open tumtum is a eunuch?

Rabbi Yehuda's response: "Go inquire about his children — find out where they really came from." He questioned whether the children were truly fathered by that man.
