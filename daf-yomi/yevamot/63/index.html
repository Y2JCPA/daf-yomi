💍 Two Kinds of Wives

Good Wife (אשה טובה)

  • She is a good gift (Ben Sira)
  • Helps her husband in all ways
  • Transforms raw needs into refined outcomes
  • Her husband's iniquities crumble (Proverbs 18:22)
  • "Whoever finds a wife finds good" — the verse itself is her praise

Bad Wife (אשה רעה)

  • A plague upon her husband (Ben Sira)
  • Sets the table and abuses him simultaneously (Abaye)
  • It is a mitzva to divorce her (Rava)
  • "Cast out the scorner and contention will depart" (Proverbs 22:10)
  • Compared to evil that cannot be escaped (Jeremiah 11:11)

🤝 What Does 'Ezer Kenegdo' Really Mean?

Rabbi Elazar
If a man is worthy, his wife is his helper (ezer). If he is unworthy, she is against him (kenegdo). The word works both ways.
Elijah the Prophet
Rabbi Yosei asked me: in what way does a wife help? — When a man brings wheat, does he eat it raw? She grinds it, bakes it, and sets it before him. She refines everything he brings home.
Rabbi Yosei
So her help is practical transformation — she takes raw material and makes it usable. Without her, what does a man have?
Key Verse "I will make him a helpmate for him" (Genesis 2:18) — the same word contains both help and opposition, depending on the man's merit.

📜 Ben Sira on Women

כִּי אִשָּׁה טוֹבָה מַתָּנָה טוֹבָה הִיא
Ben Sira (ספר בן סירא) A good wife: a good gift for her husband — placed in the bosom of a God-fearing man.

A bad wife: a plague to her husband — she is like leprosy in his house. What is the remedy? He should divorce her and be cured of his plague.

On temptation: Turn your eyes from a graceful woman married to another. Do not turn to her husband to mix wine with him, lest you be ensnared by her beauty.

On worry: Do not suffer from tomorrow's trouble — you do not know what a day will bring. Perhaps tomorrow you will no longer exist, and you will have worried about a world that is not yours.

👶 Procreation — Why It Cannot Wait

Rabbi Elazar: Any man who does not have a wife is not a complete person — "I will make him a helpmate" implies incompleteness without one.
↓
Rabbi Eliezer: One who does not engage in pru u'rvu is as if he shed blood — "Whoever sheds blood of man" is followed immediately by "be fruitful and multiply."
↓
Rabbi Ya'akov: He is as though he diminished the Divine Image — for God made man in His image and immediately commanded: "Be fruitful and multiply."
↓
Ben Azzai's dilemma: He taught the importance of procreation but never married. The Sages challenged him: you expound well but do not practice! He replied: what can I do — my soul cleaves to Torah; let others maintain the world.
↓
Rabbi Asi: The Messiah will not come until all the souls of the guf (heavenly repository of souls) are exhausted — "For the spirit that enwraps itself is from Me" (Isaiah 57:16).
