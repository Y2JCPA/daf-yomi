QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is a woman NOT believed when she says her husband died in a fire but she survived?
A. Because women are generally not trusted as witnesses
B. Because if she survived by miracle, perhaps he did too
C. Because fire always leaves identifiable remains
D. Because she has motive to lie about his death
Correct: B — The Gemara's reasoning is: 'Just as a miracle occurred for you, perhaps a miracle occurred for him.' Both face equal danger in a fire, so her survival doesn't prove his death.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Idi, why IS a woman believed when she says bandits killed her husband but she survived?
A. Because bandits always leave witnesses alive
B. Because the migo argument applies — she could have claimed peace
C. Because 'a woman's weapons are upon her' — bandits would not typically kill a woman
D. Because one witness corroborates her testimony
Correct: C — Rav Idi explains that a woman is naturally protected from being killed by bandits (they would assault but not murder her), so her survival requires no miracle and her testimony about her husband's death is plausible.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the wedding fire story, why does Rava accept the bride's testimony while Rav Ḥiyya bar Avin does not?
A. Rava holds women are always believed; Rav Ḥiyya bar Avin does not
B. Rava notes she said 'Look at my husband!' and a body was found; Rav Ḥiyya bar Avin says the body might be someone else
C. Rava relies on the migo argument; Rav Ḥiyya bar Avin rejects migo entirely
D. Rava holds fire testimony is always accepted at weddings
Correct: B — Rava distinguishes this from the generic fire case: the bride actively directed people to look, and physical evidence (burnt body + severed palm) corroborated her claim. Rav Ḥiyya bar Avin counters that perhaps a rescuer burned, and the husband fled in shame.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rava reinterpret Neḥemya of Beit D'li's statement that 'this country is riddled with troops'?
A. As proof that one witness is believed even in wartime
B. As an explanation for why Neḥemya couldn't travel safely to testify in person
C. As evidence that wartime makes all testimony unreliable
D. As a reason to require two witnesses instead of one
Correct: B — Rava argues that 'riddled with troops' refers to travel danger, not a ruling about wartime testimony. If the point were about war, Neḥemya would have said 'any place with troops' rather than specifying 'this country.'
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the Abaye-Rava dispute about Yitzḥak the Exilarch, why does Rava say we need NOT worry about two people sharing the same name?
A. Because the Torah prohibits giving children identical names
B. Because R' Yehuda Nesia's instruction to search Neharde'a was only out of respect for Shmuel's father, not genuine concern
C. Because the Exilarch title is unique and cannot be shared
D. Because Abaye always rules leniently in marriage cases
Correct: B — Rava reasons that if R' Yehuda Nesia truly feared another 'Androlinai' existed, he would have required searching the entire world — not just Neharde'a. The limited search was merely a diplomatic response to avoid embarrassing Shmuel's father.
