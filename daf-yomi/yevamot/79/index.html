Three Distinguishing Marks of Israel

רחמנים — Merciful

  • Source: 'And He will give you mercy and have mercy upon you' (Deut. 13:18)
  • God bestows the attribute of mercy upon Israel
  • The Gibeonites showed no mercy — demanded death

ביישנים — Shamefaced

  • Source: 'That His fear shall be upon your faces' (Ex. 20:17)
  • The 'fear on one's face' is the capacity for shame
  • A moral conscience that restrains cruelty

גומלי חסדים — Kind

  • Source: 'That they may keep the way of the Lord, to practice righteousness' (Gen. 18:19)
  • Acts of kindness (gemilut ḥasadim)
  • Inherited from Avraham Avinu
David's Ruling Whoever has these three marks is fit to cleave to the nation; whoever lacks them is unfit to join Israel

Selecting Saul's Descendants via the Ark

Rav Huna: David passed Saul's descendants before the Ark of the Covenant
↓
Held Back by the Ark
Condemned to death — delivered to the Gibeonites
Not Held Back
Set apart for life — spared
↓
What about Mephibosheth son of Jonathan? David spared him due to his oath
↓
Initial Answer
David didn't pass him before the Ark at all
Problem
May favoritism be shown in matters of life and death?
↓
Resolution: David passed him, the Ark held him, David prayed for mercy, and the Ark released him
Final Answer David prayed that the Ark should not hold Mephibosheth back — a prayer, not an override of divine judgment

Uprooting Torah Law for Kiddush Hashem

Gemara
But the Torah says: 'Fathers shall not be put to death for children' (Deut. 24:16) — why were Saul's sons killed?
R' Yoḥanan
It is better that one letter be uprooted from the Torah than the name of Heaven be desecrated in public (parhesya)
Gemara
But leaving them unburied violates 'His body shall not remain all night upon the tree' (Deut. 21:23)!
R' Shimon b. Yehotzadak
Better that one letter be uprooted and the name of Heaven be sanctified — passersby saw how Israel protects even gerei toshav
Gemara
Result: 150,000 converts joined the Jewish people after witnessing this display of justice
Kiddush Hashem Public sanctification of God's name can override specific Torah commandments when necessary to repair a desecration
