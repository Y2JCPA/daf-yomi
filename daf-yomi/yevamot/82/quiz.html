5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Sheisha bar Rav Idi, why is the impure sin-offering nullified in the first clause of the baraita?
A. Because its impurity came from liquids — a rabbinic-level impurity
B. Because the meat was crushed into small pieces
C. Because the prohibition is only a standard lav, not karet
D. Because it is a davar she'yesh lo matirin
Correct: A — Rav Sheisha explains that the impurity in the first clause came from contact with liquids, which is only rabbinic in origin, and therefore the Sages allowed it to be nullified rather than let sacred food go to waste.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject Rav Ashi's explanation that sacred meat is a 'davar she'yesh lo matirin' (temporarily prohibited item)?
A. Because Rav Ashi contradicted a previous statement he made
B. Because for a priest it was never forbidden, and for an Israelite it is always forbidden — so the prohibition is never truly 'temporary'
C. Because the concept of davar she'yesh lo matirin does not apply to sacred foods
D. Because Reish Lakish already gave a better explanation
Correct: B — The Gemara calls this a 'beduta' (mistake) because a priest could always eat sacred meat (no prohibition to lift), while an Israelite can never eat it (the prohibition is permanent, not temporary).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the basis for Rabbi Yosei's view that teruma today applies by Torah law?
A. A gezeira shava from the laws of ma'aser
B. The double expression of 'inheritance' in Deuteronomy 30:5 teaches that the second sanctification of Eretz Yisrael was permanent
C. An explicit verse in Leviticus mandating eternal teruma obligations
D. A tradition from Moshe at Sinai (halakha l'Moshe mi'Sinai)
Correct: B — Rabbi Yosei, identified as the author of Seder Olam, derives from 'your fathers inherited… and you shall inherit it' that there were two sanctifications — and the second one is permanent, never requiring a third. Therefore teruma remains Torah law.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the mikveh case, what is the practical difference between the two readings of R' Yochanan's statement 'up to the greater part'?
A. Whether the mikveh needs 40 or 41 se'a initially
B. Whether a 50-50 mixture of water and other liquid is valid or invalid
C. Whether the mikveh must be refilled completely after adding non-water liquids
D. Whether rainwater and drawn water are treated differently
Correct: B — One reading requires the original water to remain the majority (so 50-50 would be invalid), while the alternative reading says only that the majority must not be removed (so 50-50 is still acceptable).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rabbi Eliezer's ruling about an androgynos challenge Rabbi Yochanan's position?
A. R' Eliezer says an androgynos cannot marry at all, contradicting the mishna
B. R' Eliezer's statement that stoning applies implies the first tanna is uncertain about the androgynos's male status — undermining R' Yochanan's claim that the androgynos is definitively male
C. R' Eliezer holds that an androgynos is female, directly opposing R' Yochanan
D. R' Eliezer forbids an androgynos from serving as a priest
Correct: B — R' Eliezer states that stoning applies to relations with an androgynos 'as with a male.' The fact that this needs to be stated as a separate opinion implies that the first tanna of the mishna views the androgynos's status as uncertain — which supports Reish Lakish's position over R' Yochanan's.
