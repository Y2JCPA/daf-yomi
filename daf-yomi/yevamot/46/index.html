✂️🌊 The Core Conversion Dispute

Rabbi Yehuda
Either circumcision alone or immersion alone is sufficient to effect conversion. If a convert claims 'I was circumcised but never immersed,' we accept him — circumcision already made him a convert.
Rabbi Yosei
A convert requires both circumcision and immersion. Neither act alone completes the conversion. We do not accept the claim of a convert who says he only did one.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
A man is never considered a convert until he is both circumcised and has immersed. The halakha follows Rabbi Yosei — I state this explicitly because otherwise you might follow the majority.
Why Explicit? Rabbi Yosei is only one Sage, while Rabbi Yehuda represents a broader position. Rabbi Yoḥanan must rule explicitly that the halakha follows Rabbi Yosei, overriding the usual principle of following the majority.

⛓️ What Does a Jew Actually Purchase When Buying a Slave?

Standard Rule (Rava)

  • A gentile owner had no ownership of the slave's body
  • He owned only the slave's labor
  • He could sell to a Jew only what he himself owned — labor rights
  • Rava: consecration, leavened bread prohibition, and emancipation abrogate any prior lien

Rav Avya's Extension

  • If a gentile sold himself directly to a Jew: Jew acquires full body
  • If a gentile sold another person: Jew acquires only labor
  • Source: 'Of them you may acquire' (Lev. 25:45) — from their slaves, not from free gentiles
  • Implication: gentile cannot acquire a Jew's body, only his labor (cf. Lev. 25:47)

Rav Aḥa's Refutation

  • The verse refers to acquisition by both money and immersion
  • Not a distinction between who originally sold the slave
  • Practical issue: slave must be held tightly during immersion (Shmuel)
  • Beloreya case: slaves who immersed first acquired freedom for themselves

🏔️ How Israel Converted at Sinai — The Original Model

Circumcision (מִילָה) — Moses circumcised Israel in Egypt before the Exodus, derived from the requirement that the Paschal lamb be eaten only by the circumcised
↓
Immersion (טְבִילָה) — 'Go to the people and sanctify them… let them wash their clothes' (Ex. 19:10). Tradition: there is no sanctification without immersion in a mikveh
↓
Sprinkling of Blood (הַזָּיַת דָּם) — 'Moses took the blood and sprinkled it upon the people' (Ex. 24:8). Principle: there is no covenant without blood
↓
Foremothers Also Immersed — By logical inference: if they did not immerse, a convert-daughter who married a priest would become retroactively forbidden — since her conversion was incomplete
↓
Debate: Rabbi Eliezer vs. Rabbi Yehoshua — Each derives conversion requirements from a different parent generation at Sinai, but both agree all three acts (circumcision, immersion, blood) were present

✂️ Circumcision Alone — Practical Consequences on Shabbat

Rabbi Yehuda
The court may immerse a convert who was already circumcised on Shabbat. Since circumcision alone effected conversion, the subsequent immersion is merely a rabbinic act — permitted on Shabbat.
Rabbi Yosei
The court may not immerse him on Shabbat. Both circumcision and immersion are biblical requirements. The immersion here is the principal conversion act — forbidden on Shabbat.
Gemara
Each Sage needed to state the Shabbat ruling explicitly — lest you invert it. Without it, you might think each Sage views the opposite act as principal, reversing the Shabbat ruling entirely.
Diagnostic Test The Shabbat ruling is a diagnostic: which act does each Sage treat as the principal biblical requirement? Rabbi Yehuda: circumcision is primary, immersion is secondary (rabbinic). Rabbi Yosei: both are equal and biblical.

⚖️ What Happens When a Convert Claims Incomplete Conversion?

Convert appears before court: 'I was circumcised for conversion but never immersed' — or — 'I immersed but was not circumcised'
↓
Rabbi Yehuda
Accept his claim — either act alone suffices.
Immerse him even on Shabbat (immersion = rabbinic here).
Rabbi Yosei / Halakha
Both acts required. Immerse him on a weekday only — the immersion is a full biblical conversion act.
↓
Rabba's Incident: A convert came before Rabbi Ḥiyya bar Rabbi + Rabbi Oshaya bar Rabbi + Rabbi Oshaya son of Rabbi Ḥiyya — three Sages. They immersed him.
Three lessons: (1) court of three required, (2) immersion required, (3) court must be present at immersion itself.
