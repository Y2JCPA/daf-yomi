QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, which three things abrogate any prior lien or ownership claim?
A. A bill of sale, a court ruling, and payment of the original price
B. Consecration to the Temple, the prohibition of leavened bread taking effect, and emancipation of a slave
C. Immersion, circumcision, and a declaration before three judges
D. Death of the owner, completion of the purchase, and a written deed
Correct: B — Rava states that consecration to the Temple, the prohibition of leavened bread taking effect on a leavened food, and emancipation of a slave all abrogate any prior lien — this is relevant to the question of what the gentile owner could transfer when selling a slave.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the Beloreya incident, why did her slaves acquire themselves and go free?
A. She verbally declared them free before immersing herself
B. They paid her double their market value
C. They immersed in the mikveh before she completed her own conversion immersion
D. A court of three ruled that they were already freeborn
Correct: C — Beloreya's slaves preempted her and immersed before her own conversion was complete. Rava rules that whether they immersed without any specified intention or with explicit intent to convert, the prior immersion freed them — she could not transfer ownership of people she no longer owned as a Jew.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rabbi Yoḥanan rule regarding conversion, and why must he state it explicitly?
A. Immersion alone suffices; stated explicitly because people forgot this
B. A man is never a convert until both circumcised and immersed; stated explicitly because Rabbi Yosei is only one Sage and one might otherwise follow the majority
C. A court of ten is required; stated explicitly to override local custom
D. Circumcision alone suffices for men; stated explicitly to distinguish from women
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan rules like Rabbi Yosei that both circumcision and immersion are required. He must state this explicitly because Rabbi Yosei is an individual Sage — without the explicit ruling, one might follow the majority opinion (Rabbi Yehuda) that either act alone suffices.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How many judges must be present for a conversion, and what is the Torah source?
A. Two, from 'a matter shall be established by two witnesses'
B. Ten, because conversion is a public act requiring a minyan
C. Three, from the word 'judgment' in 'one judgment shall be for you and the convert'
D. One, since conversion is a private agreement between the convert and God
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan derives from Numbers 15:16 — 'one judgment (mishpat) shall be both for you and for the convert' — that conversion requires a court of three, because 'judgment' implies a beit din.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Yoḥanan rule that the children of the converts in Gavla are mamzerim?
A. Because their mothers had not immersed either, making both parents gentiles
B. Because the conversions were performed under duress rather than voluntarily
C. Because circumcision without immersion leaves a man legally a gentile, and relations between a gentile man and a Jewish woman produce a mamzer
D. Because the local court in Gavla lacked the required three qualified judges
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan holds (following Rabbi Yosei) that circumcision without immersion does not complete conversion — the man remains a gentile. Relations between a gentile and a Jewish woman are forbidden, and the resulting children are mamzerim. This follows from Rabbi Yoḥanan's principle that one is not a convert until both acts are complete.
