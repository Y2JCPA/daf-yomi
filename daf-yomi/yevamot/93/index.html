Wife's Konam Vow on Her Earnings

Tanna Kamma
If she says Konam — whatever I prepare for your mouth is forbidden — he need not nullify the vow. She already owes him her earnings from the ketubah.
Rabbi Akiva
He should nullify the vow, lest she earn more than the required amount. The excess would be forbidden to him.
Rav Huna b. R' Yehoshua
This is not about forbidding future earnings. She says: Let my hands be consecrated to their Maker — her hands exist now, so this is not proof about future entities.
Key Distinction The refutation reframes the vow: she consecrates her existing hands, not her future earnings — so no proof about davar shelo ba la'olam.

Chain of Eight Sages: One CAN Acquire Future Entities

🔵 Rav Huna — Selling fruit of a palm tree before it grows: once fruit comes, buyer cannot retract
↓
🟡 Rav — 'This field I am about to buy — when I buy it, it is yours from now' — valid acquisition
↓
🟢 Rabbi Yannai → Rabbi Ḥiyya — Tithed fruit in his house for fruit his sharecropper had not yet delivered
↓
🟣 Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi — Buying a slave on condition to free him: 'When I acquire you, you are free from now'
↓
🔴 Rabbi Meir — Betrothal: 'You are betrothed to me after I convert / after you are freed' — she is betrothed
↓
🟠 R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov — Designating terumah from detached fruit for attached fruit before it ripens
↓
⭐ Rabbi Akiva — The Konam vow mishna: the vow applies even to earnings not yet in the world
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak All eight Sages agree: adam makneh davar shelo ba la'olam — a person can transfer ownership of an entity not yet in the world.

Rav Huna vs. Rav Naḥman: Selling Unripe Fruit

IssueRav HunaRav Naḥman
Before fruit growsSeller can retractSeller can retract
After fruit comes into worldCannot retract — sale takes effectCan still retract — acquisition was defective from the start
If buyer seizes fruitKeeps them — valid acquisitionCourt does not remove them — he was promised a sale
Underlying principleOne can acquire a future entityOne cannot acquire a future entity
Rav Naḥman's Concession Even though Rav Naḥman holds the acquisition is invalid, if the buyer seizes the fruit, the court does not reclaim them — because the seller made a promise.

The Bruised Reed Dream

קנה רצוץ — A bruised reed he shall not break
Aggadic Moment Rabbi Yannai tithed fruit for produce his sharecropper hadn't yet delivered. Rabbi Ḥiyya validated this from a baraita about tithing for Shabbat.

But Rabbi Yannai said: In a dream they read to me 'bruised reed' — perhaps meaning I rely on a flimsy basis! (II Kings 18:21)

Rabbi Ḥiyya responded: No — the dream refers to Isaiah 42:3: 'A bruised reed he shall not break' — a verse about the Messiah and truth. You acted correctly!

Proof Sources for Each Sage in the Chain

Monetary / Property

  • Rav Huna: Selling palm tree fruit before they grow
  • Rav: Transferring a field one hasn't yet purchased
  • Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi: Buying a slave to immediately free him

Ritual / Sanctity

  • R' Yannai / R' Ḥiyya: Tithing for fruit not yet received
  • R' Eliezer b. Ya'akov: Designating terumah for unripe produce
  • R' Akiva: Konam vow on future earnings

Personal Status

  • Rabbi Meir: Betrothal conditional on future conversion or freedom
  • The woman is not yet available for marriage — yet the betrothal takes effect
Breadth The chain spans monetary law, ritual law, and personal status — showing the principle of acquiring future entities is broadly applied.
