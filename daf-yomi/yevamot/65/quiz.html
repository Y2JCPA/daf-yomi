Yevamot 65 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Tanna Kamma (the accepted halakha), who is commanded in the mitzva of pru u'rvu?
A. Both men and women equally
B. Only men, not women
C. Only women, since childbirth is their domain
D. Neither — it is a rabbinic enactment applying to both
Correct: B — The Mishna states: 'A man is commanded regarding pru u'rvu, but not a woman.' Rabbi Yoḥanan ben Beroka disagrees, but the Gemara rules the halakha follows the Tanna Kamma.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What textual basis does the Gemara use to exclude women from pru u'rvu?
A. The word vekhivshuha ('conquer it') is written without a vav — read as masculine singular
B. Women are excluded by rabbinic decree due to the dangers of childbirth
C. The verse uses a verb form that only applies to men in biblical grammar
D. A tradition from Sinai (halakha l'Moshe miSinai) explicitly exempts women
Correct: A — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak explains: the word vekhivshuha is written deficiently (without vav) so it reads as singular masculine — 'he shall conquer it' — excluding women.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
For what reason does the Gemara explicitly permit departing from the truth?
A. To avoid personal embarrassment in public
B. To protect oneself from financial loss
C. For the sake of peace (shalom)
D. When the truth is impossible to verify
Correct: C — Rabbi Elazar son of Rabbi Shimon taught that it is permitted to deviate from truth for peace. This is proven from God's alteration of Sarah's words (Gen. 18:13), Joseph's brothers (Gen. 50:16), and Samuel's mission (I Sam. 16:2).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Yehudit disguise herself before asking Rabbi Ḥiyya whether women are commanded in pru u'rvu?
A. She wanted to test whether he would give inconsistent rulings
B. She feared he would give her a stricter ruling if he knew her identity
C. She was testing his knowledge of the law for academic purposes
D. She needed the ruling to apply universally, not just to her personally
Correct: B — Yehudit disguised herself so Rabbi Ḥiyya would not recognize her and give a different answer. She wanted an honest legal ruling she could then act upon — to take the sterilizing potion without his interference.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If a woman was married twice with no children, and wishes to marry a third time, what does the Baraita require?
A. She may never remarry under any circumstances
B. She must obtain approval from the rabbinic court before marrying
C. She may only marry a man who already has children
D. She must wait ten years and then may marry freely
Correct: C — The Baraita states: if she had no children with two husbands, she may not marry a third husband unless he already has children — so the mitzva of pru u'rvu can still be fulfilled through him.
