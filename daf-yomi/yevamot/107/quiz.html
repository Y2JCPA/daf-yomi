Yevamot 107 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Beit Shammai, why can a married minor girl NOT perform mi'un?
A. Because her marriage is Torah-level and irrevocable
B. Because allowing it might lead people to think adult marriages can be conditional
C. Because only a court can annul a marriage
D. Because the husband has already acquired her property permanently
Correct: B — Shmuel explains Beit Shammai's reason: 'There are no conditions with regard to marriage.' If a married minor could refuse, people might mistakenly believe that even adult marriages can be conditional.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Beit Hillel's response to the concern that mi'un would make the husband's intimacy retroactively licentious?
A. The Torah permits retroactive annulment of all marriages
B. Since there was kiddushin and a ketubbah, no one would consider the relationship licentious
C. The husband can simply remarry her to fix the problem
D. Licentious relations are only a concern for adult women, not minors
Correct: B — Beit Hillel argue that since there was both betrothal (kiddushin) and a marriage contract (ketubbah), no one would consider the husband's prior relations to have been licentious, even after mi'un.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Ulla, what does a minor girl's refusal of a yavam actually nullify?
A. The levirate bond directly
B. The original marriage, thereby eliminating the basis for the levirate bond
C. Only the yavam's betrothal, not the consummation
D. The ketubbah obligations but not the marriage itself
Correct: B — Ulla holds that mi'un directed at a yavam nullifies the original marriage retroactively. Since there was never a valid marriage, the levirate bond never existed — she doesn't dissolve the bond directly.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If a minor girl refuses one yavam, what does Shmuel say about her status with his brothers?
A. She is forbidden to all brothers, like a yevama who received a get
B. She is permitted to the other brothers, since she — not the yavam — initiated the refusal
C. She must perform chalitzah with each remaining brother
D. She is permitted only after reaching majority
Correct: B — Shmuel distinguishes mi'un from a get: with a get, the yavam acts on her (forbidding her to brothers). With mi'un, she acts — saying 'I don't want you,' implying she may still want his brother.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did the Sages rule harshly against Pishon the camel driver, allowing his wife's mi'un in his absence?
A. Because he was a known sinner who violated Shabbat
B. Because he was consuming the profits from her property irresponsibly
C. Because he refused to give her a get when she asked
D. Because he married her without her mother's consent
Correct: B — Beit Shammai explain that Pishon 'measured with a defective standard' — he was improperly consuming the profits from her property. So the Sages 'measured him with a defective standard' in return, allowing the refusal in his absence and despite the marriage.
