QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye, why may a woman betrothed to a priest with crushed testicles eat teruma?
A. Because she already ate teruma before the betrothal was contracted
B. Because his priestly status entitles his betrothed wife, derived from marriage-acquisition laws
C. Because his priestly status entitles his Canaanite slaves, which extends to his wife
D. Because Rabbi Yehuda holds that converts may eat teruma regardless
Correct: B — Abaye derives the halakha from the laws of acquisition of marriage: just as a priest's married wife may eat teruma, so too his betrothed. This is separate from Rava's argument based on the priest's power over his slaves.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the key practical case where Abaye's and Rava's positions diverge?
A. When the betrothal occurred on Shabbat vs. a weekday
B. When the priest's testicles were crushed after a valid marriage was already consummated
C. When the woman is the daughter of a Levite rather than an Israelite
D. There is no practical difference — both positions yield identical outcomes in all cases
Correct: B — If the wife was already eating teruma during a valid marriage and her husband's testicles were then crushed, Rava says she may continue (slave-entitlement persists). Abaye's 'prior entitlement of wife' reasoning would not add anything new for her — the Gemara uses this to show the positions are not identical.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Whose tannaitic opinion does Rabbi Yochanan's dilemma presuppose?
A. Rabbi Yehuda, who holds converts are called the congregation of the Lord
B. Rabbi Yosei, who holds converts are not called the congregation of the Lord
C. Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov, who requires a Jewish mother for a convert's daughter to marry a priest
D. Rabbi Meir, who disputes the ruling in the Mishna about the wedding canopy
Correct: C — Under R' Yehuda, she could never eat teruma (always forbidden). Under R' Yosei, she could always eat teruma (always permitted). Only under R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov — who creates an intermediate category — does a genuine dilemma arise.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rav's ruling about a priest's daughter entering the wedding canopy with a man unfit for the priesthood?
A. She is not disqualified from teruma unless actual intercourse occurs
B. She is disqualified from eating teruma by the canopy alone, without intercourse
C. She is disqualified only if two witnesses testify to the canopy entry
D. She is disqualified only if she is already twelve years old
Correct: B — Rav holds that entry into the wedding canopy has independent legal significance and disqualifies the priest's daughter from teruma, even in the absence of intercourse.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Below what age does the Gemara conclude that neither intercourse nor the wedding canopy can disqualify a girl from teruma?
A. Under one year old
B. Under two years old
C. Under three years and one day old
D. Under twelve years old
Correct: C — Rava infers from the baraita — 'a girl three years and one day old can be betrothed via intercourse... if one who renders women unfit had intercourse with her, he has disqualified her' — that below this threshold, intercourse lacks legal effect, and therefore so does the wedding canopy.
