Daf 101: Ḥalitza — Who Judges, What He Wears

Uncertain Father (Kohen)
Son must serve in both watches
↓
Why is he obligated to ascend?
↓
R' Yoḥanan: To avoid family flaw — people would think both families are unfit
↓
Mishna shifts to Ḥalitza:
Before 3 judges, even laymen
↓
Rabbis
3 judges suffice
(from 'Elders' = 2 + 1)
R' Yehuda
5 judges required
('Elders' = 2 + 3 base)
Key Ruling Rava said Rav Naḥman said: The halakha is ḥalitza before three judges, as this is the unattributed mishna.

Striking/Cursing Uncertain Fathers

Tanna Kamma
Whether he struck/cursed them one after the other or simultaneously — he is liable, since one is certainly his father.
R' Yehuda (Version 1)
Simultaneously — liable.
One after the other — exempt.
R' Yehuda (Version 2)
Even simultaneously — he is exempt.
Gemara
These are two tannaim each expressing their view in accordance with R' Yehuda.
Derivation R' Ḥanina: 'Blessing' (cursing) is stated regarding parents and regarding God — just as God involves no partnership, cursing a parent requires no doubt about identity.

Ḥalitza Footwear: Valid or Invalid?

Footwear TypeStatus
Soft leather shoe (covers whole foot)✅ Valid
Cloth shoe (anpileya)❌ Invalid
Sandal with heel (hard leather)✅ Valid
Sandal without heel❌ Invalid
Shoe on stump — amputation below knee✅ Valid
Shoe on stump — amputation above knee❌ Invalid
Sandal not his own✅ Valid
Wooden sandal✅ Valid
Left shoe on right foot✅ Valid
Too large (can still walk)✅ Valid
Too small (covers most of foot)✅ Valid
Principle The shoe must be real footwear he can functionally walk in — cloth shoes and heelless sandals fail this standard.

How Many Judges for Ḥalitza?

Chakhamim (Rabbis)

  • 3 judges required
  • 'Elders' (זקנים) = plural = 2, plus 1 more (court minimum)
  • Even laymen — if they can dictate the verses
  • Halakha follows this view (Rav Naḥman)

R' Yehuda

  • 5 judges required
  • 'Elders' = 2 additional beyond the base 3
  • Extra 2 for publicity (פרסומי מילתא)
  • Laymen eligible — derived from 'in Israel'
Halakha Rava said Rav Naḥman said: The halakha is 3 judges, since two unattributed mishnayot teach this (here and in Sanhedrin).

Verse Derivations: What Each Phrase Teaches

"Before the eyes of the Elders"
(Deut. 25:9)
Rabbis
Judges must see the spittle exit her mouth (Rava's teaching)
R' Yehuda
Excludes blind judges + also requires seeing spittle
↓
"In Israel"
(Deut. 25:10)
Rabbis
Court of born Jews — not converts
R' Yehuda
Even laymen may serve
(second 'in Israel' excludes converts)
↓
"Shall be called"
(Deut. 25:10)
Rabbis
All present respond: "Ḥalutz ha-na'al!" (per R' Tarfon)
R' Yehuda
Derives the same — those attending must respond aloud
Method Each sage accounts for all the key phrases in the verse — they differ on which phrase teaches which law.

Judges' Counsel to Ḥalitza Parties

📋 Judges call the yavam themselves — not through agents ('they shall call him')
↓
💬 Judges speak to him and give counsel appropriate to the situation ('they shall speak to him')
↓
👴👧 If he is old and she is young: 'What are you doing with a young girl?'
↓
👦👵 If he is young and she is old: 'What are you doing with an elderly woman?'
↓
🤝 'Go be with someone your own age — do not bring quarrel into your home'
Insight The judges are not mere procedural officials — they actively advise whether yibum or ḥalitza is more appropriate for the specific parties.
