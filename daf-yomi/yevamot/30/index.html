⚖️ Is the Levirate Bond (Zikah) Substantial?

Rav Ashi
The zikah IS substantial — it functions nearly like a full acquisition, giving it real legal weight that affects rival wife status even before yibbum is performed.
Rav Naḥman
The zikah is NOT substantial — it is a weak bond, so a rival wife who is a forbidden relative is fully exempt, even from ḥalitza.
Rav Ashi
Proof: Our mishna requires ḥalitza for the unrelated woman even after Shimon divorced first and then married her — showing the bond creates real obligations.
Rav Naḥman
Counter via Rabbi Yirmeya: The mishnayot are disjointed — they reflect different tannaim with different positions. No single ruling can be derived from comparing them.
Core Question Does the levirate bond (zikah) function like a full marital acquisition, generating real obligations — or is it a weak, conditional tie that yields to existing prohibitions?

📋 Three Mishna Cases on Daf 30a

Case 1: Levirate Betrothal Only

  • Reuven (Sister A) dies → Levi performs levirate betrothal (erusin only) with Sister A
  • Then Levi dies — both Sister A and Unrelated Woman fall before Shimon
  • Sister A: forbidden as sister of Shimon's wife (Sister B)
  • Ruling: Both do ḥalitza only — Unrelated Woman as rival of a forbidden relative; neither may do yibbum

Case 2: Unrelated Brother Dies First

  • Levi (Unrelated) dies → Shimon marries Levi's widow (Unrelated Woman)
  • Then Reuven dies → Sister A falls before Shimon
  • But Sister A is now the sister of Shimon's wife (Sister B) — forbidden
  • Ruling: Sister A is forbidden to Shimon forever; Unrelated Woman is already fully his wife

Case 3: Full Yibbum, Then Second Death

  • Reuven dies → Levi performs full yibbum (marries) Sister A
  • Then Levi dies → Sister A and Unrelated Woman both fall before Shimon
  • Sister A: forbidden (sister of Shimon's wife Sister B)
  • Ruling: Unrelated Woman also exempt — at Levi's death, her co-wife (Sister A) was forbidden; she cannot do yibbum either

📣 Rav Yehuda's Principle in the Name of Rav

כל יבמה שאין אני קורא בה 'יבמה יבא עליה' בשעה שנפלה לפניו — אינה יבמתו לעולם
רב יהודה אמר רב — כלל גדול ביבמות Any yevama to whom the verse 'Her brother-in-law will have intercourse with her' (Devarim 25:5) cannot be applied at the time she happens before him for levirate marriage — she is forever exempt from levirate marriage with that brother.

The moment of determination is when she first 'falls before' the surviving brothers. If she was forbidden at that instant, she remains permanently exempt — even if the original obstacle later disappears.

Key application: Even if she was eligible at an earlier point (e.g., when a different brother first died), what matters is her status at the moment the levirate obligation against this brother actually crystallizes.

🗣️ Rabba vs. Abaye: Uncertain Get and the Rival Wife

Rabba
When it is uncertain whether the thrown get landed closer to her (valid divorce) or closer to him (not divorced), the rival wife does NOT need ḥalitza. She has a chazakah of being permitted — we leave her in that presumptive status.
Abaye
If chazakah exempts her here, apply the same logic to uncertain kiddushin! The rival wife was presumptively permitted before the uncertain betrothal too — yet the mishna requires ḥalitza in that case. Your reasoning is inconsistent!
Gemara
Betrothal differs: the rival wife is certainly the yavam's wife and certainly requires levirate marriage. With uncertain kiddushin, doubt concerns only whether the forbidden relative was betrothed — we rule stringently and require ḥalitza.
Objection
But stringency breeds leniency! If we require ḥalitza and treat her as a yevama, people may later think ḥalitza permitted her completely — even to marry a man who definitely betrothed the forbidden relative's sister!
Recurring Tension in Yevamot A ruling that is 'stringent' in one dimension (requiring ḥalitza) can inadvertently create a 'leniency' in a related scenario — forcing the Gemara to refine its reasoning carefully.

❓ Forms of Uncertain Divorce and Betrothal (Mishna, 30b)

Uncertain Betrothal (קידושין בספק): Did he successfully betroth the forbidden relative? Outcome in doubt → Rival wife does ḥalitza only; yibbum prohibited
↓
Get in his handwriting, no witness signatures: Possibly valid, possibly not → Uncertain divorce → Rival wife does ḥalitza only
↓
Witness signatures present, but no date: Get may be invalid (undatable, possibly antedated) → Uncertain divorce → Rival wife does ḥalitza only
↓
Date written but no witness signatures: Get may be invalid (unsigned) → Uncertain divorce → Rival wife does ḥalitza only
↓
Uncertain proximity when thrown: Was the get closer to her (valid) or to him (not divorced)? Omitted from the mishna — Rabba: no ḥalitza required, since chazakah of permission applies before the uncertain throw
↓
Governing Rule: All cases in the mishna — if the forbidden relative may have been divorced or betrothed — rival wives do ḥalitza but cannot do levirate marriage (stringency under doubt)
