QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Rabbi Shimon and the Rabbis regarding the levirate bond (zikah)?
A. Whether levirate marriage is biblically required at all
B. Whether the zikah is strong enough to make the yevama 'like a married woman,' exempting her rival
C. Whether ḥalitza is preferable to yibbum in all cases
D. Whether a woman may refuse levirate marriage entirely
Correct: B — Rabbi Shimon holds the zikah grants the yevama the status of a married woman, automatically exempting her rival wife. The Rabbis hold the bond alone is insufficient — each yevama must independently perform ḥalitza or yibbum.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Abaye's challenge to Rav Oshaya's proof about Rabbi Shimon's position?
A. Rabbi Shimon explicitly stated his reason is a rabbinic decree, not Torah law
B. Perhaps Rabbi Shimon holds the zikah is strong only with one yavam, not two yevamin simultaneously
C. The baraita Rav Oshaya cited actually supports the Rabbis' position
D. Abaye argues there is no halakhic difference between one and two yevamin
Correct: B — Abaye says perhaps Rabbi Shimon holds the zikah is substantial only in the case of a single yavam — his ruling may differ when two yevamin face each other simultaneously. You cannot derive a general rule from the single-yavam case.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rav Ashi's resolution regarding yevamot who appear for levirate marriage simultaneously vs. sequentially?
A. Simultaneous: first exempts second; Sequential: neither exempts the other
B. In both cases neither woman exempts the other under any circumstance
C. Sequential: first is 'like a married woman' and exempts second; Simultaneous: neither exempts the other
D. In both cases one yevama always exempts the other
Correct: C — Rav Ashi resolves the apparent contradictions: if the yevamot appear for yibbum one after the other, the first bound is treated as a married woman and exempts the second. If they fall to yibbum simultaneously, neither exempts the other.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
On what principle do Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi and the Rabbis disagree regarding non-consensual levirate betrothal?
A. Whether levirate betrothal requires two valid witnesses
B. Whether halakhic rules of yibbum (intercourse) can be applied to derive rules of ma'amar (betrothal)
C. Whether a minor yavam can perform levirate betrothal
D. Whether levirate betrothal requires a monetary transaction
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi holds that yibbum laws and ma'amar laws form one halakhic category — so non-consensual ma'amar is valid, just like non-consensual yibbum. The Rabbis hold they are separate categories and ma'amar requires consent.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which Torah verse does Rabbi Shimon explicitly cite to exempt both rival wives from ḥalitza and yibbum?
A. "To build his brother's house" (Deuteronomy 25:9)
B. "Her brother-in-law will take her to him as wife" (Deuteronomy 25:5)
C. "You shall not take a woman to her sister, to be a rival" (Leviticus 18:18)
D. "He does not wish to take his sister-in-law" (Deuteronomy 25:7)
Correct: C — An explicit baraita states that Rabbi Shimon derives the exemption of both rival wives from Leviticus 18:18 — 'You shall not take a woman to her sister, to be a rival' — when two sisters are bound to the same yavam through levirate.
