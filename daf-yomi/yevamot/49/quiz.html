Yevamot 49 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Akiva, what type of forbidden union produces a mamzer?
A. Any union that violates any Torah prohibition
B. Any union punishable by karet
C. Only unions punishable by court-imposed death
D. Only unions with one's father's wife specifically
Correct: B — R. Akiva holds that mamzer applies to offspring of any karet-level union, derived from the proximity of 'a mamzer shall not enter the congregation' to the verse about his father's wife — showing that even karet-level forbidden relations produce a mamzer.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was Ben Azzai's evidence for his restrictive definition of mamzer?
A. A ruling by the Sanhedrin limiting mamzer to capital cases
B. A scroll of lineage records in Jerusalem recording a mamzer born of adultery
C. A baraita transmitted from Sinai listing only capital offenses
D. A letter from Ezra the Scribe defining mamzer narrowly
Correct: B — Ben Azzai found a מגילת יוחסין (scroll of lineage records) in Jerusalem explicitly recording someone as a mamzer from an adulterous union — a capital sin — supporting his view that only court-executable sins produce a mamzer.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Abaye rule that the offspring of a niddah is NOT a mamzer?
A. The niddah prohibition is only rabbinic, not biblical
B. There is no karet for relations with a niddah according to all opinions
C. Betrothal with a niddah takes effect, meaning a permanent bond is legally possible
D. Abaye follows Ben Azzai's restrictive view that only capital sins matter
Correct: C — Abaye derives from 'her impurity shall be upon him' (Lev. 15:24) that betrothal with a niddah takes effect. Since a legal marriage bond is possible, she does not fall into the category of unions producing a mamzer.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does the Gemara's concept of 'aspaklaria' explain about Isaiah's vision of God?
A. That Isaiah's prophecy was delivered in a dream state only
B. That Isaiah used a physical mirror in the Temple to receive his vision
C. That all prophets except Moses saw through an obscure glass — their visions were metaphorical, not literal
D. That Isaiah's vision was clearer than Moses' because he served in the Temple
Correct: C — The Gemara resolves the contradiction between Moses ('no man sees Me and lives') and Isaiah ('I saw the Lord') by teaching that Moses alone used a clear aspaklaria (lucid glass), while Isaiah and all other prophets used an obscure one — their visions were metaphorical, not literal claims of direct sight.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Isaiah remain silent when Manasseh accused him of contradicting the Torah?
A. Isaiah was bound by a prophetic oath not to speak before kings
B. Isaiah admitted privately that he had erred in his prophecies
C. He knew Manasseh would reject any explanation, and a response would transform Manasseh's sin from unwitting to deliberate
D. The law requires a prophet to remain silent during judicial proceedings
Correct: C — Isaiah calculated that since Manasseh would not accept any resolution, offering one would only change Manasseh's status from an unwitting sinner to a deliberate one — making his guilt greater. Silence was the only responsible option, even though it led to his death.
