👶 Who Counts as a Mamzer?

R. Akiva
Any offspring from a union punishable by karet — such as one's father's wife or other forbidden near-kinswomen.
Shimon HaTimni
Any offspring from a union that violates a lav (Torah prohibition), even without karet penalty.
R. Yehoshua
Only offspring from unions that can never become permitted — those with absolutely no remedy.
Ben Azzai
Only from unions punishable by court-imposed death — as I found recorded in a scroll in Jerusalem!
Mishna's Base Rule The mishna adopts R. Akiva's view: mamzer = offspring of any forbidden union with a near-kinswoman subject to karet. Ben Azzai's scroll evidence narrows this further.

📜 Ben Azzai's Scroll from Jerusalem

מגילת יוחסין — מצאתי בירושלים ספר שכתוב בו פלוני ממזר מאשת איש
Primary Evidence Ben Azzai testified: "I found a scroll of lineage records in Jerusalem, and it was written in it that so-and-so is a mamzer born of an adulterous union with a married woman" — a sin punishable by court-imposed death. This historical document supports his restrictive position: only capital-sin unions produce a mamzer, not every karet-level transgression as R. Akiva holds. The scroll implies the majority ruling was already being applied narrowly in practice.

👁️ Aspaklaria: How Prophets Received Vision

Moses

  • Prophesied through a clear aspaklaria — lucid, direct vision
  • "No man can see Me and live" — yet Moses uniquely could
  • His vision was unmediated and unclouded
  • Highest level of prophecy; no contradiction with literal sight

All Other Prophets (incl. Isaiah)

  • Prophesied through an obscure aspaklaria — metaphorical, indirect
  • Isaiah's 'I saw the Lord' (Isaiah 6:1) is visionary, not literal
  • This resolves the apparent contradiction with Moses' Torah
  • Manasseh wrongly treated Isaiah's vision as literal claim

⚔️ Isaiah's Death at Manasseh's Hands

Manasseh charged Isaiah with issuing prophecies that contradict Moses' Torah — treating him as a false prophet deserving death
↓
Three alleged contradictions: (1) Moses: 'no man sees God and lives' vs. Isaiah: 'I saw the Lord'; (2) Moses: God is near whenever we call vs. Isaiah: 'seek God while He may be found'; (3) Moses: I will fulfill your days vs. Isaiah: I add 15 years to Hezekiah's life
↓
Isaiah chose silence — he knew Manasseh would refuse any answer, and giving one would only make Manasseh's sin deliberate rather than unwitting
↓
Rava's ruling: Manasseh first formally condemned Isaiah as a false witness contradicting Torah, then had him executed — a quasi-judicial killing
↓
Gemara's resolutions: Aspaklaria explains 'I saw God'; Isaiah's 'seek God' refers only to the Ten Days of Repentance; the '15 years' verse is disputed between tannaim
