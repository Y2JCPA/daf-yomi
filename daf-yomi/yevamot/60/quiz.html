QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Sages (rejecting Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov), what type of prohibition must be violated to produce ḥalal offspring for a High Priest?
A. Any prohibition — positive or negative commandment alike
B. Only negative-commandment prohibitions (widow, divorcée, ḥalala, zona)
C. Only prohibitions punishable by karet
D. Only prohibitions involving a priestly family lineage
Correct: B — The Sages hold that the word אֵלֶּה ('these') concludes the list of negative prohibitions — only those specific violations produce a ḥalal. A raped/seduced woman is forbidden only by a positive commandment, so her child with a High Priest is not a ḥalal.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rabbi Shimon's general rule about when a priest must become impure for his sister?
A. Whenever she is biologically his full sister, regardless of her status
B. Whenever she is still living in his household
C. If and only if she is fit to be married to a High Priest
D. Only when she is a betrothed virgin who has never been touched
Correct: C — Rabbi Shimon rules explicitly: if the sister was fit for a High Priest, her brother must become impure for her; if she was not fit (e.g., she was divorced after betrothal), he may not become impure for her.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rabbi Shimon ben Yoḥai permits a female convert to marry a priest if she converted before what age?
A. Before she turns one year old
B. Before she turns three years and one day old
C. Before she reaches bat mitzva (12 years old)
D. Before she becomes a grown woman (bogeret)
Correct: B — Rabbi Shimon ben Yoḥai rules that a female convert who converted when she was less than three years and one day old may marry a priest. At that age she cannot halachically 'know' a man, so she is never disqualified.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How did the Israelites verify whether captured Midianite girls were virgins, according to Rav Huna bar Bizna citing Rabbi Shimon Ḥasida?
A. They asked the girls to testify under oath before a rabbinic court
B. They examined the girls physically before witnesses
C. They passed the girls before the golden frontplate (tzitz), which caused guilty girls to turn pale and innocent ones to shine
D. They consulted the Urim V'Tumim in the breastplate of the High Priest
Correct: C — Rabbi Shimon Ḥasida taught that the girls were passed before the frontplate (tzitz). Those who were disqualified turned pale-faced, while those who were fit shone. This miraculous sign was the method of verification.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does everyone — including Rabbi Yosei and Rabbi Shimon — agree that a priest must become impure for his sister who is a grown woman (bogeret)?
A. Because a grown woman is always considered a full virgin in priestly law
B. Because she is still biologically a virgin even if her hymen is diminished
C. Because she will eventually be a grown woman 'under' a High Priest who married her while she was young — she was always fit for him from the start
D. Because the Sages issued a special rabbinic decree to protect the honor of grown women
Correct: C — The logic is that a High Priest who married her when she was young will eventually have a grown woman as his wife — this situation was anticipated and permitted ab initio. Therefore she never loses her status as fit for a High Priest, and impurity applies to all.
