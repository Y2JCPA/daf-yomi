⚖️ Two Defining Disputes of Daf 60

Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov
The child of a High Priest's marriage to a raped or seduced woman is a ḥalal — the phrase 'a virgin of his own people shall he take' means all violations of his marriage law produce ḥalal status, including positive-commandment violations
The Sages
The word אֵלֶּה ('these shall he not take') ends the category — only negative-commandment violations (widow, divorcée, ḥalala, zona) produce ḥalal status; a positive-commandment violation does not
Rabbi Meir & Rabbi Yehuda
A priest must become impure for his betrothed sister even if divorced — 'his virgin sister' includes any woman once called his virgin sister; grown women and accidentally-torn-hymen women are also included
Rabbi Yosei & Rabbi Shimon
A priest may not become impure for his betrothed sister once divorced — Rabbi Shimon's rule: if she is fit for a High Priest, yes; if not fit, no. 'His virgin sister' excludes raped, seduced, and accidentally-torn-hymen women
Core Tension Does 'virgin' in priestly law mean biologically intact, or legally fit? The answer governs both marriage prohibitions and impurity obligations across the entire daf.

💍 High Priest Marriage: Permitted or Forbidden?

A woman seeks to marry the Kohen Gadol — what is her status?
↓
Young virgin (hymen intact)
Permitted — the ideal wife for a High Priest (Lev. 21:13–14)
Grown woman (בּוֹגֶרֶת)
Permitted — 'she will eventually be a grown woman under him' while already married
Hymen torn accidentally (not by a man)
Permitted — 'who has had no man' excludes only intercourse with a man, not accidents
Raped or seduced by another man
Forbidden — positive-commandment prohibition; marriage valid but must divorce (Lev. 21:14)

👶 Rav Ashi's Reframing: What Produces a Ḥalal?

Rav Ashi
The true dispute is not about Rabbi Elazar's view — it is whether a positive-commandment violation (e.g., High Priest marrying a raped woman) produces ḥalal offspring at all
Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov
'These shall he not take' — the word אֵלֶּה ends the negative-commandment list, but the verse continues: 'a virgin of his own people shall he take' — this positive command means violating it also produces a ḥalal
The Sages
The word אֵלֶּה concludes the matter entirely. Ḥalal status arises only from the listed categories: widow, divorcée, ḥalala, zona. A raped/seduced woman — forbidden only by a positive mitzva — does not produce a ḥalal
Gemara Note
Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov's teachings are described as קַב וְנָקִי — 'a kav but clean': few in number but all accepted as authoritative halakha — yet here the Sages disagree with him
Key Verse — Leviticus 21:14 אַלְמָנָה וּגְרוּשָׁה וַחֲלָלָה זֹנָה אֶת-אֵלֶּה לֹא יִקָּח — 'A widow, divorcée, ḥalala, zona — these shall he not take; but a virgin of his own people shall he take'

⚱️ Must a Priest Become Impure for His Sister?

Rabbi Meir & Rabbi Yehuda

  • Must become impure for betrothed sister ✓
  • Must become impure even if she was later divorced ✓
  • 'His virgin sister' = includes grown woman (partial virgin still counts)
  • 'Who has had no man' only excludes accidental tearing
  • A grown woman's status is derived from the word 'virgin' itself
  • Derivation: 'to him' includes woman whose hymen was torn accidentally

Rabbi Yosei & Rabbi Shimon

  • May NOT become impure for betrothed-then-divorced sister ✗
  • 'His virgin sister' excludes raped, seduced, accidentally torn ✗
  • 'Who has had no man' excludes even betrothal alone (R. Yosei)
  • Rabbi Shimon: 'to him' includes grown woman (one inclusion only)
  • Rabbi Shimon's rule: fit for High Priest → impurity; not fit → no impurity
  • A divorced woman is never fit for a High Priest, so no obligation

🔄 Rabbi Shimon ben Yoḥai: The Convert Under Three

כָּל אִשָּׁה יֹדַעַת אִישׁ לְמִשְׁכַּב זָכָר הֲרֹגוּ — כָּל הָרְאוּיָה לְבִיאַת אִישׁ (במדבר לא:יז)
A Foundational Ruling for Priestly Marriage Rabbi Shimon ben Yoḥai derives from Numbers 31:17–18 that a female convert who converted before age three years and one day may marry a priest.

The Verse: 'Kill every woman who has known man by lying with him; but every young woman that has not known man — keep alive for yourselves.' The phrase יֹדַעַת אִישׁ means fit for intercourse, not actual experience. A girl under three years is not halachically capable of such fitness — so she is never disqualified.

The Frontplate Miracle (Numbers 31): When daughters of Midian passed before the tzitz (the golden frontplate), girls who were fit shone brightly while those who were disqualified turned pale-faced. This miraculous verification was used in practice.

Halakha: Rabbi Ya'akov bar Idi said in the name of Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi: the halakha follows Rabbi Shimon ben Yoḥai — a convert under three may marry a priest.
