Yevamot 98 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, why does a gentile have no legal patrilineage?
A. Because gentiles don't know who their fathers are
B. Because it is a Torah decree regardless of actual paternity
C. Because gentile marriages are not recognized
D. Because the mother's lineage always takes precedence
Correct: B — Rava explicitly rejects the notion that it's about uncertainty. Even with identical twins who obviously share a father, the Torah still does not recognize the paternal bond — it is a gezerat ha-katuv.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What verse does the Gemara cite to prove the Torah 'dispossesses' a gentile father of his offspring?
A. "Be fruitful and multiply" (Genesis 1:28)
B. "Honor your father and mother" (Exodus 20:12)
C. "Whose flesh is the flesh of donkeys" (Ezekiel 23:20)
D. "A man shall leave his father and mother" (Genesis 2:24)
Correct: C — The Gemara cites Ezekiel 23:20 to demonstrate that the offspring of a male gentile is legally considered no more connected to him than the offspring of animals.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara question the reliability of the convert who cited Rabbi Akiva's ruling?
A. He was not a student of Rabbi Akiva
B. He personally benefited from the ruling he reported
C. He misquoted the verse about Jonah
D. He was not yet fully converted at the time
Correct: B — A scholar who teaches a halakha and then personally benefits from it is suspect. The Gemara cites Rav Huna in the name of Rav: if the ruling was stated before the incident, it's reliable; if after, it's not.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the dispute about a convert's mother-in-law after his wife dies, what is R' Akiva's position?
A. She is forbidden by Torah law
B. The prohibition is weakened (no death penalty), so the Sages did not decree it for a convert
C. She is permitted only if she also converted
D. The convert must divorce her immediately upon the wife's death
Correct: B — R' Akiva holds that the mother-in-law prohibition is weakened after the wife's death (no longer punishable by death). Since it's already weakened, the Sages did not extend a rabbinic decree to converts.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the mishna about mixed offspring, why can't one brother perform yibum with ALL the widows?
A. Because yibum with multiple women is biblically prohibited
B. Because each brother should have a chance to encounter his actual sister-in-law
C. Because the widows might be related to each other
D. Because ḥalitza is always preferred over yibum
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that each brother should perform yibum with one widow so that each has the chance to 'happen upon his own sister-in-law' and fulfill the genuine mitzvah of levirate marriage.
