📜 Rabbis vs. Rabbi Yosei b. R. Yehuda: Half-Sister Liability

Rabbis
One who unwittingly has relations with his half-sister (daughter of his father's wife) is liable for two sin-offerings: one because she is his sister, and one because she is his father's wife's daughter
R. Yosei b. R. Yehuda
He is liable for only one sin-offering. The phrase 'she is your sister' (היא אחותך) limits — you hold him liable as a sister, but not twice over for the same act
Rabbis
'She is your sister' teaches liability even when she is BOTH his father's daughter AND his mother's daughter simultaneously — extending coverage to the combined case
R. Yosei b. R. Yehuda
If so, the Torah should have written simply 'your sister.' The extra word 'she is' (היא) must serve as a limiter — for intentional violation only one punishment applies, and prohibitions from kal v'chomer incur no corporal punishment
Core Question Can a single forbidden act generate multiple sin-offerings when it simultaneously violates two distinct Torah prohibitions?

🔄 Converts: Do Secondary Forbidden Relationships Apply?

Western Sage (question)
In Eretz Yisrael they asked: Did the Sages issue a decree prohibiting secondary forbidden relationships for converts, or not? Conversion dissolves prior familial ties — so perhaps no rabbinic safeguard applies
Rav Naḥman
Were it not that the Torah itself permits a convert to marry his former relatives, we would have had to decree against it — lest converts say 'we went from a stricter sanctity to a lesser one'
Rav Naḥman
Ruling: Maternal half-brothers who both convert may NOT testify together — a rabbinic decree, even though their legal kinship is dissolved upon conversion
Gemara (distinction)
Why apply the decree to testimony but not forbidden relations? Forbidden relations are 'handed over to all' — each person chooses their own spouse, so the concern of appearances is heightened. Witnesses are court-assigned, same logic applies
Halakhic Principle Rabbinic decrees preserving the appearance of kinship norms can apply even after conversion legally dissolves the underlying family relationship

👨‍👦 Does a Mamzer Brother Create a Levirate Bond?

Mishna: 'Anyone who has a brother of any kind — that brother creates a levirate bond with his yevama'
↓
Gemara asks: What does 'any kind' (שהוא) come to add beyond an ordinary brother?
↓
Rav Yehuda: It adds a mamzer brother — who creates a levirate bond despite his flawed lineage
↓
Challenge
Maybe a mamzer brother does NOT obligate the yevama? His lineage is disqualified — perhaps he lacks full brotherly status for levirate?
Response
A mamzer child already exempts his father's wife from levirate (all agree). If a mamzer son has this power, a mamzer brother surely creates the bond — both flow from the same verse: 'his brother'
↓
Conclusion: A mamzer brother DOES create a levirate bond with his yevama ✓

✡️ Mamzer's Legal Standing: As Brother and As Son

Mamzer as Brother

  • Creates levirate bond (yibbum/chalitza required)
  • Inherits from his halakhic brother
  • A kohen becomes tamei for him at death
  • 'His kin that is near to him' (Lev. 21:2) includes all halakhic brothers
  • Flawed lineage does not remove these rights

Mamzer as Son

  • Exempts his father's wife from levirate obligation
  • Liable for striking or cursing his father
  • Father's repentance renders him 'of your people' (Ex. 22:27)
  • Counted as a full legal son for these purposes
  • The crooked can become straight through repentance

Exception: Non-Jewish Mother

  • Son of Canaanite maidservant: NOT a halakhic brother
  • Son of gentile woman: NOT a halakhic brother
  • Verse: 'His father's wife' (Deut. 25:5) — requires shared Jewish legal paternity
  • No levirate bond created whatsoever
  • Non-Jewish maternity severs the halakhic kinship entirely

📖 The Hermeneutical Battle Over 'She Is Your Sister' (היא אחותך)

Base verse (Lev. 18:9): 'The nakedness of your sister, the daughter of your father, or the daughter of your mother…you shall not uncover'
↓
Extra phrase: 'She is your sister' (היא אחותך) — the Torah appends this; why is it necessary?
↓
Rabbis: Creates liability when she is BOTH father's daughter AND mother's daughter — the combined case needs explicit coverage since both verses apply simultaneously
↓
R. Yosei b. R. Yehuda: 'She is' (היא) is a limiting particle — you hold him liable once as 'sister,' but the same act does not generate a second liability
↓
Deeper derivation: 'She is your sister' also teaches that prohibitions derived via kal v'chomer (a fortiori reasoning) do NOT incur corporal punishment — only explicit Torah verses create punishable violations (lashes/kareit)
↓
Practical boundary: 'Father's wife's daughter' (Lev. 18:9) covers only a daughter born of a woman his father married — excluding a daughter born of a woman his father raped, since she was never 'his father's wife'
