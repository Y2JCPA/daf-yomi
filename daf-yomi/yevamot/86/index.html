Who Gets the First Tithe?

R' Meir
The first tithe belongs exclusively to Levites. The Torah calls it teruma, so just as teruma is forbidden to non-kohanim, tithe is forbidden to non-Levites.
R' Elazar b. Azarya
One may give the first tithe even to a priest — a kohen is also from the tribe of Levi!
R' Akiva
The verse says 'you may eat it in any place' — this excludes a kohen, who cannot enter a cemetery. Tithe goes to Levites only.
R' Eliezer
In 24 places in Tanakh, priests are called 'Levites.' The verse can include kohanim too.
Core Dispute Does the Torah's language restrict the first tithe to Levites alone, or does the priestly connection to the tribe of Levi entitle kohanim to receive it as well?

R' Meir vs. R' Elazar ben Azarya: First Tithe Recipients

R' Meir
Teruma goes to a kohen; ma'aser rishon goes to a Levi. The verse in Numbers 18:24 calls tithe 'teruma' — just as teruma is forbidden to non-kohanim, tithe is forbidden to non-Levites.
R' Elazar b. Azarya
One may give the first tithe even to a kohen. A priest is also from shevet Levi — he is not a 'foreigner' (zar) to the tithe.
Gemara
If tithe is like teruma, should a non-Levite who eats it face death at Heaven's hand and pay the extra fifth?
Gemara
No — the Torah specifies: 'they will die through it' and 'add a fifth to it' — it refers to teruma specifically, not to tithe.
Key Limit Even R' Meir, who restricts tithe to Levites, agrees the death penalty and fifth-payment apply only to teruma, not tithe.

R' Akiva vs. R' Eliezer: Can Kohanim Receive Tithe?

R' Akiva — Levites Only

  • Numbers 18:26 says 'speak to the Levites' — not priests
  • 'You may eat it in any place' — excludes kohanim who cannot enter a cemetery
  • Redirected R' Elazar b. Azarya's garden entrance toward a cemetery to block him from claiming tithe

R' Eliezer — Priests Too

  • In 24 biblical passages, priests are called 'Levites'
  • Example: 'the priests the Levites, sons of Zadok' (Ezekiel 44:15)
  • 'Any place' simply means any city — unlike ma'aser sheni which requires Jerusalem
The Garden Incident R' Elazar ben Azarya (a kohen) would collect first tithe from a garden. R' Akiva moved its entrance to face a cemetery — kohanim cannot enter cemeteries, so R' Elazar was blocked from collecting.

An Israelite Woman's Eating Rights Through Successive Marriages

👰 Marries a Kohen → may eat teruma
↓
⚰️ Kohen dies, she has his child → still eats teruma (through the child)
↓
💍 Marries a Levi → loses teruma, gains ma'aser rishon
↓
⚰️ Levi dies, she has his child → still eats ma'aser (through the child)
↓
💍 Marries a Yisrael → loses both teruma and ma'aser
↓
⚰️ Yisrael dies, she has his child → still cannot eat teruma or ma'aser
Principle A woman's right to eat teruma or tithe is determined by her current husband or, if widowed, by the tribal status of her surviving children.
