QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Rava retract his lenient ruling from the evening by the following morning?
A. He received new testimony from witnesses overnight
B. He realized that regardless of which opinion we follow, there is no leniency available — Rabbi Meir helps nothing, and the Rabbis still require waiting
C. Rav Mesharshiyya brought a contradicting baraita
D. He found that Shmuel had already ruled on the matter definitively
Correct: B — Rava realized: if we follow Rabbi Meir the priest gains nothing, and if we follow the Rabbis she must wait regardless — so there is no basis for leniency in either direction.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rav Naḥman undercut Rava's argument that the majority of women give birth at nine months?
A. He cites a baraita proving seven-month births are equally common
B. He argues that since a nine-month fetus is always recognizable after a third of pregnancy, and this fetus was NOT recognized early, the birth must have been at seven months
C. He brings personal testimony from the women of his household
D. He rules that we disregard majority when family status is at stake
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman's logic: every 9-month fetus becomes recognizable at ⅓ of the days. Since this woman's pregnancy was not recognized early, the fetus was a 7-month one — meaning the child is the yavam's son.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Abaye, what is the halakhic status of the second child born in the uncertain paternity case?
A. Completely fit — even eligible for the High Priesthood
B. Treated as a definite mamzer, but may still marry another definite mamzer
C. Uncertain status only — may marry anyone freely
D. Forbidden to enter the congregation entirely, like a definite mamzer from the Torah
Correct: B — Abaye follows Shmuel/Hillel: the uncertain mamzer is treated stringently as a definite mamzer for some purposes, but — since uncertain mamzerim may marry definite mamzerim — the second child may still marry a definite mamzer.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why were Rav and Rav Naḥman permitted to take 'a wife for the day' in distant cities despite Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov's prohibition?
A. They obtained special rabbinic dispensation each time they traveled
B. Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov's ruling applies only to non-scholars
C. Their names were renowned everywhere, so their children would always be identifiable as theirs — eliminating the concern
D. They were traveling on official communal business, which creates a special exemption
Correct: C — Sages are different — their names are renowned throughout the world, so their children are always identified as their sons. The concern that half-siblings might unknowingly marry does not apply to them.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the core disagreement between Rabbi Abba and Rabbi Yirmeya in the inheritance case of the son of uncertain descent?
A. Whether a son of uncertain descent can inherit any portion at all
B. Whether an original verdict dividing an estate can be repealed in light of later developments, or must remain in force
C. Whether the yavam's sons are considered halakhic brothers of the son of uncertain descent
D. Whether the Admon ruling in Ketubot applies to levirate marriage estates
Correct: B — Rabbi Abba holds the original verdict stands even after new circumstances arise (like the Rabbis opposing Admon), while Rabbi Yirmeya holds the original verdict is repealed by the new situation (like Admon's position in the lost-path case).
