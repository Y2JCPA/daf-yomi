🌅 Rava's Morning Retraction & The Pregnancy Wait

Rav Ashi
According to Rava citing Rav — the Sages were lenient for a woman married to a priest, to allow the couple to remain together.
Rav Hoshaya
How can you compare those cases? There, the Rabbis disagree with Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel, giving a basis for leniency.
Rav Ashi
But here — a woman pregnant with another man's child — if we follow Rabbi Meir the priest gains nothing, and if we follow the Rabbis she must still wait!
Rav Mesharshiyya
Do you really permit her to marry without ḥalitza? Rava himself retracted this lenient ruling by morning!
Resulting Rule A woman may not remarry during the first 3 months after divorce or widowhood — even betrothal is forbidden — due to uncertainty about pregnancy from the previous husband.

⚖️ Abaye vs Rava: Status of Mamzer Uncertainty

Abaye — follows Shmuel/Hillel

  • Uncertain mamzer may marry a definite mamzer
  • First child: fit even to become High Priest
  • Second child: treated as definite mamzer — may still marry another mamzer
  • Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov adds stringency only for the second child's status
  • Shmuel: 'Hillel taught that 10 lineage categories descended to Babylonia…'

Rava — follows Rav/Rabbi Elazar

  • Uncertain mamzer may NOT marry even a definite mamzer
  • Both first and second children treated as definite mamzerim — forbidden to congregation
  • Verse: זִמָּה (lewdness) = acronym זוֹ מָה הִיא — 'What is this?'
  • Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov prohibits mixing uncertain with definite status entirely
  • Rav: 'Halakha follows Rabbi Elazar that marriage of certain and uncertain is forbidden'

⚠️ Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov's Chain of Warnings

Warning 1 — Wandering Fathers: A man who impregnated many unknown women (or vice versa) risks that his descendants will unknowingly marry each other, filling the world with mamzerim.
↓
Warning 2 — Insincere Marriage: Do not marry a woman while intending to divorce her — 'Do not devise evil against your fellow' (Proverbs 3:29).
↓
Warning 3 — Distant Cities: Do not marry in a city where you are unknown — your children may later unknowingly marry each other.
↓
Exception — Sages: Rav (in Dardeshir) and Rav Naḥman (in Shekuntziv) each announced: 'Who will be my wife for the day?' — permitted because Sages' names are renowned and their children always identifiable.
↓
Niddah Precaution: These Sages sent messengers seven days ahead of arrival, so the woman could count her seven clean days before the marriage and avoid ritual impurity issues.
