Yevamot 95 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
From which verse do we derive that a husband's relations with his wife's sister do not render the wife forbidden?
A. "Her" (אותה) — Numbers 5:12-13
B. "They shall be burned" — Leviticus 20:14
C. "After she has been defiled" — Deuteronomy 24:4
D. "A man shall leave his father" — Genesis 2:24
Correct: A — The word "אותה" (her) teaches that only her own intercourse with another man renders her forbidden, not her husband's intercourse with her sister.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Yosei, about which case did Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel disagree?
A. Relations with the wife's sister
B. Relations with the mother-in-law
C. Remarrying a divorcée
D. Relations with a married woman
Correct: B — R' Yosei says all agree the wife's sister does NOT disqualify. The dispute is about the mother-in-law — Beit Shammai say it disqualifies and Beit Hillel say it does not.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava and R' Yoḥanan, what is the 'light prohibition' in the kal va-ḥomer?
A. Remarrying one's divorcée after she married another
B. A married woman (eshet ish)
C. A wife's sister
D. A Kohen marrying a divorcée
Correct: B — Rava explains the 'light prohibition' is a married woman, because the husband can negate the prohibition by giving a get — unlike a wife's sister whose prohibition lasts a lifetime.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What did Shmuel rule regarding R' Yehuda's opinion that relations with one's mother-in-law disqualifies the wife?
A. The halakha follows R' Yehuda
B. The halakha does NOT follow R' Yehuda
C. It applies only to intentional relations
D. It applies only when done by court permission
Correct: B — Shmuel rules the halakha is NOT in accordance with R' Yehuda. Rav Yehuda flogged a transgressor but did not forbid his wife, citing Shmuel's ruling.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Ami, what does R' Yosei's principle 'whoever disqualifies others disqualifies himself' refer to?
A. The distinction between wife's sister and mother-in-law
B. The distinction between marrying by court permission vs. by witnesses
C. The distinction between unwitting and intentional sin
D. The distinction between betrothed and married women
Correct: B — R' Ami says R' Yosei refers to the first mishna of the chapter: one who married by court permission (which disqualifies the other wife) also disqualifies his own wife, whereas one who married by witnesses alone does not.
