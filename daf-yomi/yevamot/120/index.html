Rabbi Elazar's Reasoning: Why Permit the Rival Wife?

Gemara
What is the basis of Rabbi Elazar's ruling that the rival wife may remarry?
Option A
One rival wife may testify for another — the rival wife can marry even if the testifying wife hasn't married yet.
Option B
She would not cause herself injury — only if she has actually married can we permit the rival wife.
R' Elazar
"Since they were permitted to marry the brothers-in-law, they are now permitted to marry any man." — He spoke of a case where they already married.
Gemara
This proves his reason is self-injury — she wouldn't marry if her husband were alive.
Rejection
No — he argued to the Rabbis on their terms. On his own view, rival wife testimony suffices.
Rabbis
"Let me die with the Philistines" — A woman might harm herself just to harm her rival wife!
Unresolved The Gemara cannot conclusively determine R' Elazar's reasoning — both explanations remain viable.

A divorcee can marry a Yisrael — maybe she lied! ❌ Rival wife not permitted.

The Mishna: Standards for Identifying the Dead

פרצוף הפנים עם החוטם
Core Rule One may testify that a man died only if he saw the partzuf (countenance of the face) with the nose. Distinguishing marks (simanim) on the body or belongings are not sufficient.

One may not testify until the soul has actually departed — even if the person was cut open, crucified, or being eaten by an animal.

Testimony is valid only within three days of death.
R' Yehuda ben Bava: "Not every person, place, or time is the same" — decomposition varies.

Cut Open (Meguyyad): Can He Survive?

Tanna Kamma (Rabbis)

  • A person who is cut open — one may testify he is dead
  • A person who is crucified — one may not testify
  • Cut open = certainly fatal

R' Shimon ben Elazar

  • Even cut open — one may not testify
  • The wound can be scorched/cauterized
  • Cauterization may stop bleeding and allow survival
  • Our Mishna follows this stricter view
Resolution — Rava Our Mishna refers to being cut with a white-hot knife — the heat closes the wound, so all agree one cannot testify to death.
