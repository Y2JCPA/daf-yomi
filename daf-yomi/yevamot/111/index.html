Deaf-Mute vs. Minor: Which Acquisition Status?

Core Question: What is the nature of marriage to a deaf-mute vs. a minor?
↓
Rav Ḥisda's Reading of Rav
Deaf-mute = partially acquired (קנויה ואינה קנויה)
Minor = uncertain — either fully acquired or not at all
Rejected Alternative
Deaf-mute = uncertain (fully or not at all)
Minor = partially acquired
↓
Rav Sheshet confirms Rav Ḥisda from a baraita about two orphan sisters
Key Principle The deaf-mute's marriage creates a partial acquisition — real but incomplete. The minor's marriage is all-or-nothing uncertain.
3 / 9

How Does Rav's Ruling Work?

Rav Ḥisda
The deaf-mute is partially acquired (קנויה ואינה קנויה). The minor is either fully acquired or not acquired at all — we simply don't know.
Questioner
Why not say the reverse — the minor is partially acquired and the deaf-mute is uncertain?
Rav Ḥisda
Because if the minor were partial, there would be no path for the deaf-mute to be released — she can't do ḥalitza, and consummation is blocked by the minor's partial bond.
Rav Sheshet
Rav Ḥisda's reading is correct. A baraita about two orphan sisters proves it — only his framework allows the deaf-mute to be released via a get after transgressive intercourse.
Resolution Only Rav Ḥisda's reading allows both women a path to release from their levirate bond.

Baraita: Two Brothers, Two Orphan Sisters

Two brothers marry two orphan sisters: one a minor, one a deaf-mute
↓
Minor's Husband Dies
Deaf-mute gets a get (sister's bond)
Minor waits for majority → does ḥalitza
Deaf-Mute's Husband Dies
Minor gets a get (sister's bond)
Deaf-mute is forbidden forever — can't do ḥalitza, can't consummate (sister's bond blocks)
↓
If he transgresses and consummates with the deaf-mute → gives her a get and she is released
Why This Works Under Rav Ḥisda: if the minor is fully acquired, the deaf-mute is exempt as a forbidden relative's rival. If the minor is not acquired, consummation is valid. Either way, the get releases her.

Rabbi Neḥemya vs. The Rabbis: Invalid Intercourse

Rabbis (Majority)

  • An invalid sexual act (bi'ah pesulah) does NOT exempt from ḥalitza
  • If yavam has intercourse with minor first, then deaf-mute — the minor still needs ḥalitza at majority
  • Decree: perhaps he had intercourse with the deaf-mute first, making subsequent act invalid

Rabbi Neḥemya

  • An invalid sexual act ALSO exempts from ḥalitza
  • Cannot explain why the baraita requires the minor to perform ḥalitza at majority
  • Gemara concludes the baraita does NOT follow Rabbi Neḥemya
Conclusion The baraita accords with the Rabbis and with Rav Ḥisda's reading of Rav: deaf-mute = partial, minor = uncertain.

Rabbi Elazar: Instruct the Minor to Refuse (Mi'un)

Tanna Kamma
If the yavam consummated with the minor first and then with the adult, the minor is disqualified. Both need a get.
R' Elazar
The court instructs the minor to perform mi'un (refusal), annulling her marriage retroactively. She is then free to marry anyone.
Shmuel
The halakha follows R' Elazar — both in this case and in the earlier case of Yevamot 109a.
Gemara
Both rulings are necessary: one teaches mi'un even when the mitzva of yibum was fulfilled; the other teaches it even when the minor is already married to the yavam.
Halakha Mi'un is the preferred remedy — it retroactively erases the minor's marriage, cleanly resolving the levirate complications.
