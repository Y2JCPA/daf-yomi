QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, why does a forbidden relative (e.g., a wife's sister) not require a special verse to be exempt from yibum?
A. The Torah explicitly lists all forbidden relatives in Deuteronomy's yibum passage
B. A positive mitzva cannot override a prohibition that entails karet (excision)
C. A rabbinic decree exempts all forbidden relatives from yibum and chalitza
D. The yavam may waive his yibum obligation through a unilateral declaration
Correct: B — Rava's core principle: since marrying a forbidden relative involves karet, the positive commandment of yibum simply cannot override it. This follows from a general legal principle, making a specific verse unnecessary.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
For whose exemption is the phrase 'with her' (עמה) primarily required according to the Gemara's conclusion?
A. The forbidden relative herself
B. A woman the yavam previously divorced
C. The rival wife (tzara) of the forbidden relative
D. A convert who entered the yibum situation after conversion
Correct: C — The phrase 'with her' is needed for the rival wife (tzara). The forbidden relative herself is excluded by the karet principle. But the co-wife, whose own marriage was entirely permitted, requires a specific textual source for her exempt status.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What did Rav Huna bar Tahlifa say to resolve the difficulty raised by Rami bar Hama's challenge?
A. The two rulings apply to different categories of forbidden relatives
B. Two separate teachings are embedded in one verse — each governing a distinct scenario
C. One ruling is biblical in origin and the other is only rabbinic
D. The two rulings apply only when both brothers resided in the same city
Correct: B — Rav Huna bar Tahlifa resolved the tension by pointing out that two separate issues are written in the same verse. One phrase addresses the case where yibum is applicable; the other addresses where it is not — each independent of the other.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What halakha does Rabbi Yosei bar Hanina derive from the phrase 'and have intercourse with her' in the levirate verse?
A. The yavam must perform yibum within thirty days of the brother's death
B. Yibum requires two witnesses to be legally valid
C. A woman acquired through yibum is a full wife and may be divorced with a get
D. A yavam who refuses must perform chalitza before the court
Correct: C — Rabbi Yosei bar Hanina derives that yibum creates complete marital acquisition — she is fully his wife, meaning she is divorced with a get and is not subject to future yibum obligations. The phrase 'with her' signals a full, ongoing marital relationship.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the timing of the brothers' marriages matter for determining whether yibum applies to a rival wife?
A. Later marriages are treated as more stringent under rabbinic law
B. The prohibition established first determines which obligation is primary
C. Timing only matters when both brothers died without children simultaneously
D. The age difference between the wives determines which prohibition takes precedence
Correct: B — The first-established prohibition governs. If the deceased married first, his wife's 'brother's wife' status preceded the 'wife's sister' prohibition — creating the classic yibum scenario with a rival wife. If the living brother married first, his wife's sister prohibition predates any yibum obligation, blocking it entirely.
