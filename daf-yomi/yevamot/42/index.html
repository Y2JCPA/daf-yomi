⚖️ The Three-Month Waiting Period

Rav
For a bill of divorce, the three months are counted from the time the get is given.
Shmuel
For a bill of divorce, the three months are counted from the time the get is written.
Rava
For a yevama, all agree: the count begins from the husband's death — derived by an a fortiori argument from the karet prohibition.
Core Principle When does the 3-month waiting period (המתנת שלשה חדשים) begin? For a yevama it is unambiguous — from her husband's death, not from ḥalitza. For divorcées, Rav and Shmuel dispute the start point.

📏 Why Exactly Three Months — Not Less?

Why can't a woman wait fewer than 3 months and then remarry?
↓
❌ Wait only 1 month?
An 8-month baby is not viable. If she conceived immediately after remarrying, a birth at 8 months post-remarriage is ambiguous — attributed to neither husband safely.
❌ Wait only 2 months?
A birth at 7 months post-remarriage could still represent a 9-month pregnancy from the first husband — paternity still in doubt.
❌ Wait 2.5 months?
Mar Zutra: A 9-month pregnancy can run a few extra days beyond the ninth month (derived from Hannah's pregnancy with Samuel). So 2.5 months still leaves ambiguity.
↓
✅ Three full months are required to eliminate all possible ambiguity about paternity in every scenario.

🧬 Why Do All Women Wait? Two Reasons Debated

Rav Naḥman citing Shmuel

  • Source: "To be a God to you and your seed after you" (Gen. 17:7)
  • The Divine Presence rests only when offspring can be identified and attributed
  • Must distinguish: seed sown in sanctity (Jewish parents) vs. not in sanctity
  • Even converts who were married before conversion must wait 3 months after converting before remarrying each other

Rava

  • Rabbinic decree against misidentification of paternity
  • Risk 1: Child born to second husband but biologically first husband's → could lead to incest (marrying a paternal half-sister)
  • Risk 2: Misidentified child of deceased second husband exempts his mother from levirate — freeing her to marry others under false pretense
  • Rav Ḥananya objects from a baraita; Gemara defends Rava's view by reinterpreting the baraita

🔍 Why Can't We Just Examine Her? — The Examination Problem

Suggestion: Let her wait a minimal time, then be physically examined at the 3-month mark from the first marriage. If visibly pregnant → delay; if not → permit.
↓
Objection (Rav Safra): One does not examine the bodies of married women — it would shame them before their new husbands.
↓
Alternative: Examine how she walks — a 3-month pregnancy is visible in a woman's gait without direct body inspection.
↓
Objection (Rami bar Ḥama): A woman wishing her child to be attributed to her new husband will deliberately mask her pregnancy by altering her walk.
↓
Conclusion: No reliable non-invasive method exists. Therefore the Rabbis instituted a uniform 3-month waiting period for all women without exception.

🤰 Prohibition: Marrying a Pregnant Woman

Baraita
A man may not marry a woman pregnant with another man's child, nor a nursing mother within 24 months of birth.
Gemara Q
If she is known to be pregnant, why make her wait 3 months? Paternity is already clear — let her marry immediately!
Answer 1
Rabbinic decree: lest she become pregnant again and the original fetus be deformed into the shape of a sandal fish (sandal) by a double pregnancy.
Answer 2
Intercourse causes physical pressure and damage to the existing fetus in the womb.
Final Answer
A nursing mother must wait because pregnancy can dry up her milk, endangering the nursing infant who depends on it for survival.
Distinction The sandal-fish and pressure concerns do not prohibit relations with one's own pregnant wife, as those relations are permitted. The prohibition applies specifically to marrying another man's pregnant widow or divorcée.
