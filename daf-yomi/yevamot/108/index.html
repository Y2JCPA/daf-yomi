Mi'un (Refusal) of a Minor Wife — Core Framework

Minor girl married off by mother or brothers (rabbinic marriage)
↓
Does she refuse (mi'un) or receive a get?
Mi'un (Refusal)
Marriage treated as if it never existed. She may return to him. No restrictions.
Get (Divorce)
Marriage treated as real. She is prohibited from returning after remarrying.
KEY DISTINCTION Mi'un retroactively nullifies the marriage; a get treats it as valid — this difference drives the entire sugya.

What Constitutes a Valid Refusal (Mi'un)?

🗣️ She says: "I do not want so-and-so as my husband" — classic refusal
↓
💒 R' Yehuda: Even while sitting in the bridal chair (apiryon) traveling to husband's house
↓
🍷 R' Yehuda further: Even while serving drinks to guests at husband's feast
↓
🏪 R' Yosei bar Yehuda: Even when sent by husband to the shopkeeper — "no greater refusal than this"
PATTERN Each Sage expands the valid context for refusal further — even casual, ambiguous statements in domestic settings qualify.

Can Marrying Another Man Serve as Refusal?

R' Yehuda ben Beteira
A minor who didn't refuse but married another man — her new marriage constitutes her refusal.
Shmuel
The halakha follows R' Yehuda ben Beteira for both marriage and betrothal to another.
Gemara
But does this apply even if she was married (not just betrothed) to the first husband?
R' Yehuda HaNasi
The daughters-in-law of Abdan said "Let them be YOUR husbands!" — no greater refusal than this.
Gemara
Conclusion: The halakha follows R' Yehuda ben Beteira in all cases — even if she was fully married to the first man.
HALAKHA A minor's act of betrothal or marriage to another man is itself a valid refusal of the first husband.

Status of a Minor Wife — R' Eliezer vs R' Yehoshua

R' Eliezer (& R' Elazar)

  • The act of a minor is nothing
  • Husband has NO right to her finds or earnings
  • Cannot annul her vows
  • Does not inherit her
  • Priest husband need not become impure for her
  • She is not his wife in any sense
  • Only requirement: she must still perform mi'un to remarry

R' Yehoshua

  • Marriage by mother/brothers has rabbinic validity
  • Husband HAS rights to her finds and earnings
  • Can annul her vows
  • Inherits her assets
  • Priest must become impure for her
  • She IS his wife for all purposes
  • She needs mi'un or get to leave
R' ELAZAR'S IMAGE Shmuel praised R' Elazar's consistency: she walks with her husband, rises from intimacy, immerses, and eats teruma by evening — as if no marriage exists at all.

Does Refusal Nullify a Prior Get?

Husband divorces minor wife (get), then remarries her
↓
She then refuses him (mi'un) and marries another man
↓
Can she return to the first husband?
Yes — Mishna's ruling
Her refusal retroactively nullifies his get. Since the get is void, she was never truly divorced — no prohibition on returning.
But contradiction!
Later mishna says: one she left by get — she may not return. Refusal of another man doesn't nullify his get.
RESOLUTION Shmuel: The mishna is disjointed — different Tannaim taught different clauses. Rava: Her own refusal nullifies his get, but refusal of another man does not.
7 / 9

Resolving the Contradictory Mishna

Shmuel
The mishna is disjointed (meḥuseret) — he who taught one clause did not teach the other.
Rava
No contradiction! Her refusal of him nullifies his get. Her refusal of another man does not nullify his get.
Gemara
Why the difference? Because she is familiar with his intimations (keritzotav) — he could lead her astray to refuse the new husband.
Gemara
But she already refused him once — proof he cannot lead her astray! So her own refusal does nullify his get.
R' Elazar
I still say the mishna is disjointed — the two clauses come from different Tannaim.
Ulla
The final clause refers to a girl divorced three times — she appears adult, so refusal cannot cancel the divorce.
THREE APPROACHES Shmuel: different Tannaim. Rava: different scenarios (refusal of him vs. of another). Ulla: different frequency (divorced three times looks adult).
