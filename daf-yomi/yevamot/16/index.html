👴 The Great Delegation to Dosa ben Harkinas

Sages hear: the rival wife of a daughter was permitted in the time of Rabbi Dosa ben Harkinas — proof Beit Shammai acted on their rulings?
↓
Critical ambiguity: was it Dosa (revered sage) or ben Harkinas (his wayward brother Yonatan)?

Rabbi Yehoshua
Volunteers to visit Rabbi Dosa; seated on a golden bed
Rabbi Elazar ben Azarya
Selected as second delegate — praised by Dosa with Psalms 37:25
Rabbi Akiva
Third delegate — exits separately and encounters Yonatan ben Harkinas

🚶 The Sages' Diplomatic Visit to Rabbi Dosa

Rabbi Dosa greets Rabbi Yehoshua warmly and seats him on a golden bed — Dosa was extremely wealthy (עשיר גדול)
↓
Out of courtesy, they circle around different halakhot before raising the sensitive matter directly
↓
"What is the halakha with regard to the rival wife of a daughter?" — they finally ask
↓
Rabbi Dosa: "Did you hear this from Dosa ben Harkinas, or merely from ben Harkinas?" — Sages: we heard simply 'ben Harkinas'
↓
Rabbi Dosa reveals: it was his wayward brother Yonatan — who has 300 proofs permitting the rival wife of a daughter, but "beware lest he batter you with halakhot"
↓
Rabbi Dosa himself rules: the rival wife of a daughter is forbidden — aligning with accepted halakha

⚔️ Rabbi Akiva vs. Yonatan ben Harkinas

Yonatan ben Harkinas
Unleashes 300 halakhic proofs (שלש מאות ראיות) that the rival wife of a daughter is permitted — daring Akiva to refute them
Rabbi Akiva
Systematically refutes each and every proof; Yonatan cannot establish his position
Yonatan ben Harkinas
"You are Akiva ben Yosef, whose name has spread from one end of the world to the other! Yet you have not yet reached the level of cattle herders!"
Rabbi Akiva
Responds with calm humility — unmoved by the insult, the halakhic ruling stands
📜 Result Despite Yonatan's anger and 300 proofs, the צרת הבת (rival wife of a daughter) remains forbidden to the brothers — Beit Shammai's lenient ruling was Yonatan's alone, not authoritative

🚫 Why Reject Converts from Tarmod? Two Opinions

Opinion A: Servants of Solomon

  • Tarmodim descend from the servants of Solomon (עבדי שלמה)
  • When gentiles invaded the Sanctuary, they violated Jewish women
  • This opinion holds: offspring of a gentile/slave with a Jewish woman is a ממזר
  • ∴ Tarmodim lineage is irreparably tainted — converts not accepted

Opinion B: Intermingled with Ten Tribes

  • 12,000 Tarmodim (6,000 archers) invaded at the Temple's destruction
  • They intermingled with exiled Jewish women from the ten tribes
  • Descendants' status is uncertain — Jewish or gentile?
  • ∴ Lineage unverifiable — converts cannot be accepted
    Both opinions agree on the ruling; they disagree only on the reason

💍 Concern About Gentile Betrothal and the Ten Tribes

Rav Yehuda in the name of Rav Asi:
A gentile (נכרי) who betroths a Jewish woman today — we are concerned about the betrothal's validity
↓
Objection: A gentile's betrothal is normally legally meaningless — why be concerned?
↓
Answer: He may actually be from the עשרת השבטים (ten tribes) — exiled and assimilated among the nations, but halakhically still Jewish
↓
From permanent exile settlements
Concerned
Rabbi Abba bar Kahana: 'He put them in Halah and Habor' — fixed dwelling places, identity preserved
Scattered individually
Not concerned
Those who dispersed among the nations individually are assumed fully assimilated — no valid betrothal
