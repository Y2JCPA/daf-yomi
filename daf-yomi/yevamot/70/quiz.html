Yevamot 70 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, if a woman is rumored to have had intercourse with a priest and also with other men, what is the status of her child?
A. The child is a mamzer with no priestly rights
B. The child is attributed to the priest and is legitimate
C. The child's status remains permanently unresolved
D. The child may never eat teruma under any circumstances
Correct: B — Rava rules that when a priest is among those rumored, we 'cast the child after him' — the child is attributed to the priest and is therefore legitimate, enabling teruma if she is a priest's daughter.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rabbi Elazar derive that an uncircumcised priest may not eat teruma?
A. From the verse 'No foreigner shall eat holy things' (Lev. 22:10)
B. From the doubled word 'ish ish' in Leviticus 22:4
C. By a verbal analogy (gezera shava) from the phrase 'a sojourner and hired servant' linking Paschal lamb law to teruma
D. From the general rule that any bodily defect disqualifies a priest from all service
Correct: C — Rabbi Elazar establishes a gezera shava: 'a sojourner and hired servant' appears in the Paschal lamb prohibition (Exodus 12:45) and in the teruma context, linking the uncircumcised exclusion from one to the other.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara conclude that an acute mourner (onen) is NOT barred from eating teruma by the verbal analogy?
A. Because the Paschal lamb verse explicitly exempts mourners
B. Because acute mourning is a more severe status than uncircumcision
C. Because the disqualification must stem from 'foreignness' — a bodily personal status — and mourning is a temporary external circumstance
D. Because an acute mourner can immerse and immediately eat teruma before burial
Correct: C — Rava rules from Leviticus 22:10 that only 'foreignness' as a permanent personal status triggers the analogy's exclusion. Acute mourning is situational — it ends with burial — and therefore does not qualify.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The Gemara says a mamzer child can both disqualify AND enable a woman to eat teruma. What determines which outcome applies?
A. Whether the mamzer child is male or female
B. Whether the mamzer was conceived before or after her widowhood
C. Whether the mamzer's father was a priest (enabling) or a disqualifying non-priest (disqualifying)
D. A mamzer always disqualifies — enabling applies only to a fully fit child
Correct: C — The Gemara derives from 'ein la / ayyein ala' that even an unfit child's lineage is examined: a mamzer from a priest carries priestly enabling status; a mamzer from a disqualifying union blocks her return to teruma.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does the Mishna teach about a man with crushed testicles (petzua dakka) who is a priest?
A. He is permanently barred from all teruma and priestly duties
B. He is prohibited from marrying a Jewish woman, but he and his sons retain the right to eat teruma
C. He may eat teruma but his sons may not due to his disqualification
D. He must immerse daily to maintain his right to eat teruma
Correct: B — The Mishna rules that petzua dakka and kerut shofkha are prohibited from marrying Jewish women, but as priests they and their sons retain the right to eat teruma — the genital wound affects marital law, not their priestly eating rights.
