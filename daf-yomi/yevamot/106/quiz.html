QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yoḥanan, what constitutes a valid 'mistaken ḥalitza'?
A. The yavam is tricked into thinking shoe-removal is marriage
B. The yevama performs ḥalitza while asleep
C. Ḥalitza on condition of 200 dinars that are never paid
D. Ḥalitza performed without a Beit Din present
Correct: C — R' Yoḥanan rejects Reish Lakish's view that tricking the yavam works. For R' Yoḥanan, a valid 'mistake' is when ḥalitza is conditioned on payment that never materializes — the condition doesn't bind but the ḥalitza stands.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did R' Ḥiyya arrange a SECOND ḥalitza after tricking the yavam with the first one?
A. The first ḥalitza was performed on the wrong foot
B. To satisfy R' Yoḥanan's view that the first trick-ḥalitza was invalid
C. The yevama forgot to spit during the first ḥalitza
D. Two ḥalitzot are always required by Torah law
Correct: B — The first ḥalitza blocked levirate marriage but, per R' Yoḥanan, wouldn't free her to marry others since the yavam lacked proper intent. The second proper ḥalitza, performed with full knowledge, satisfied all opinions.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the principle of 'משטה אני בך' (I was fooling you) as applied to the ferryman case?
A. A promise made in jest is always fully binding
B. Agreements made under duress are not enforceable beyond fair value
C. One can never collect on verbal agreements
D. Prisoners are exempt from all financial obligations
Correct: B — The prisoner who promised the ferryman an inflated dinar only owes the standard fare. Similarly, the yevama who agreed to 200 dinars under pressure of needing ḥalitza can claim she never seriously intended to pay — the ḥalitza is valid but the debt is unenforceable.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Rava concede to Abaye that 'לא אבה יבמי' (he did not wish to consummate) must be recited without interruption?
A. Because it is the longest phrase in the passage
B. Because pausing could reverse the meaning to 'he DOES wish to consummate'
C. Because the Torah explicitly requires it to be said in one breath
D. Because the yevama must recite it while removing the shoe simultaneously
Correct: B — Rava generally holds that pauses are mere interruptions and not problematic. However, he concedes that splitting 'he did not wish to consummate' could make a listener hear only 'wish to consummate' — reversing the essential meaning of the declaration.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Gemara, what happens if the yevama spits but the wind carries her saliva away before it lands in front of the yavam?
A. The ḥalitza is valid since she performed the act of spitting
B. She must spit again but need not repeat any recitations
C. The ḥalitza has no halakhic validity at all
D. The judges may testify that she spat, making it valid
Correct: C — Abaye rules that if the wind seizes the spit so it never lands before the yavam's face, the action has no halakhic effect. The Torah requires 'she spit BEFORE him' — the spit must visibly land in front of him.
