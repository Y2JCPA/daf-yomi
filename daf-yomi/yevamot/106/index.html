Mistaken Ḥalitza (חליצה מוטעת)

↓
Reish Lakish
Yavam tricked into thinking shoe-removal = marriage → Valid

What Is a 'Mistaken Ḥalitza'?

Reish Lakish
If the yavam is told 'Let her remove your shoe and you'll marry her' — even though he intended marriage, the ḥalitza is valid.
R' Yoḥanan
Impossible! I teach that if either party lacks intent for proper ḥalitza, it's invalid. Both must intend to release the bond.
R' Yoḥanan
A valid 'mistaken ḥalitza' is when they say: 'Perform ḥalitza on condition she gives you 200 dinars' — even if she never pays, the ḥalitza stands because the condition is not binding.
Baraita
A baraita supports R' Yoḥanan: Mistaken ḥalitza = conditional on 200 dinars. R' Ḥiyya validated such a case.
Core Dispute Can ḥalitza work when the yavam thinks he's marrying her? R' Yoḥanan says no — both must intend actual ḥalitza.

R' Ḥiyya's Courtroom Strategy

🧑‍⚖️ A yevama appears before R' Ḥiyya bar Abba with her yavam
↓
R' Ḥiyya asks: 'Do you know why he wants levirate marriage?'
She replies: 'He wants my money!'
↓
She confirms: 'He is not good for me'
↓
R' Ḥiyya tells the yavam: 'Let her remove your shoe — this will marry her to you' (a deliberate deception!)
↓
After the shoe is removed, R' Ḥiyya declares:
'She is now permanently disqualified from marrying you'
↓
R' Ḥiyya then has him perform a second, proper ḥalitza — satisfying even R' Yoḥanan's stricter view
Judicial Creativity R' Ḥiyya used the first 'mistaken' ḥalitza to block levirate marriage, then arranged a proper second ḥalitza to release her completely.

Abaye, Rav Pappa & the Ferryman Analogy

Abaye
Tells the yavam (Rav Pappa's brother-in-law): 'Let her remove your shoe and you'll marry her.'
Rav Pappa
Don't you hold like R' Yoḥanan who says this type of ḥalitza doesn't work?
Abaye
Switches strategy: tells yavam to accept ḥalitza on condition of 200 dinars. Then tells the yevama: 'Go pay him.'
Rav Pappa
She can claim 'I was fooling you' (משטה אני בך)! Like the prisoner who promised the ferryman a dinar — he only owes the regular fare.
Abaye
Hears the argument, agrees, and exempts her from paying.
Principle משטה אני בך — 'I was fooling you': Agreements made under duress or desperation are not enforceable beyond the fair value.

Mishna: The Ḥalitza Ceremony Step by Step

🏛️ Yavam and yevama come before the Beit Din (court)
↓
💬 The Elders advise him — levirate marriage or ḥalitza?
↓
🗣️ She recites: 'My brother-in-law refused to establish a name...' (Deut. 25:7)
↓
🗣️ He recites: 'I do not wish to take her' (Deut. 25:8)
↓
👞 She removes his shoe
↓
💧 She spits before him — visible to the judges
↓
📢 She recites: 'So shall it be done to the man who does not build his brother's house' (Deut. 25:9)
↓
📣 Judges declare: 'The house of he who had his shoe removed' (Deut. 25:10)
R' Hyrkanus's Addition After prompting ḥalitza under the ela tree in Kfar Eitam, R' Hyrkanus established the custom of completing the entire Torah passage.

Prompting the Recitation: Abaye vs. Rava

Abaye

  • Do NOT prompt 'He did not' (pause) 'wish to consummate' — sounds like 'He DOES wish'
  • Do NOT prompt 'I do not' (pause) 'wish to take her' — sounds like 'I DO wish'
  • Must prompt entire phrase at once to preserve meaning
  • Even small pauses can reverse the meaning

Rava

  • A pause is merely an interruption — not a problem
  • It's just like taking a breath mid-sentence
  • The meaning is still clear from context
  • BUT: Rava concedes on 'He did not wish to consummate' — that one must be said together
Practical Halakha Rav Kahana followed Abaye's strict approach. Rava conceded that 'לא אבה יבמי' must be recited together without interruption.
