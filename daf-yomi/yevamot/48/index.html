💅 The Yefat Toar's Nails: Grow or Cut?

Rabbi Eliezer
She must cut her nails. Just as shaving the head removes beauty, so too cutting the nails removes beauty — both acts make her less desirable to the soldier. (Deut. 21:12)
Rabbi Akiva
She must let her nails grow. Growing long, unkempt nails diminishes her beauty. The verse uses the same language for nails as for mourning — implying neglect, not removal.
Shared Goal Both opinions agree the Torah intends to discourage the soldier from marrying the captive. The dispute is only whether 'doing her nails' means cutting or letting them grow.

📅 How Long Must the Captive Mourn Her Parents?

Tanna Kamma
Thirty days: The verse says 'a month of days' — a standard lunar month of 30 days. Simple and straightforward. (Deut. 21:13)
R. Shimon ben Elazar
Ninety days: 'A month' = 30 days; 'days' adds another 30; 'after that' adds yet another 30. Total: 3 × 30 = 90 days.
Ravina
Objects strongly: By that logic, 'after that' would require yet another 90 days — producing an infinite regression! The verse cannot be read that way.
Verse "A month of days and after that you may come to her" — Deuteronomy 21:13

✂️ Must a Master Circumcise His Slave?

Rabbi Yishmael — Permitted

  • One may maintain uncircumcised slaves under his control
  • Proof: 'The son of your maidservant will be refreshed' — implies an uncircumcised slave still rests on Shabbat
  • A slave cannot be forced to circumcise against his will, just like an adult son
  • The Torah tolerates delay when the slave refuses

Rabbi Akiva — Forbidden

  • One may not maintain an uncircumcised slave, even for a moment
  • The Torah's command to circumcise all slaves admits no exception
  • If the slave refuses after 12 months, sell him back to gentiles
  • Rabbi Yishmael's verse refers to a ger toshav, not a purchased slave

🕐 The 12-Month Rule for a Slave Who Refuses Circumcision

Master purchases a slave from a gentile
↓
The slave refuses circumcision — master may abide with him and wait
↓
Master may keep him up to 12 months, hoping the slave changes his mind
↓
After 12 months still uncircumcised — master must sell him back to gentiles
↓
Exception (Ravin citing Rabbi Ilai): A slave purchased on condition he would not be circumcised may be kept indefinitely
↓
Corroborated by a baraita: the city in Eretz Yisrael whose slaves refused — masters waited 12 months, then sold them

😢 Why Do Converts Suffer Hardships?

גֵּרִים בַּזְּמַן הַזֶּה — לָמָּה צָרוֹת בָּאוֹת עֲלֵיהֶם?
Three Opinions Rabbi Ḥananya son of Rabban Gamliel: Because as gentiles they did not observe the seven Noahide mitzvot — they are punished for prior transgressions.

Others say: Because they delayed in entering under the wings of the Divine Presence — they are punished for not converting sooner.

Rabbi Abbahu (or Rabbi Ḥanina): Cites a verse as proof for this position — the suffering is inherent to the delayed embrace of holiness.

All agree converts face hardship; the debate is whether it punishes past sins or present delay.
