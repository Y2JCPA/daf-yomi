Yevamot 52 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, what is the ideal procedure for levirate marriage?
A. Intercourse alone is sufficient and preferred
B. Levirate betrothal first, then intercourse
C. A written document must precede all other acts
D. Intercourse before betrothal fulfills the mitzva better
Correct: B — Rav Huna states that the mitzva is properly performed when the yavam first betroths the yevama and afterward has intercourse. Intercourse without prior betrothal still works but is not the ideal form.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why would Rav flog a man who betrothed a woman by intercourse?
A. Betrothal by intercourse is halachically invalid
B. He acted in a promiscuous, immodest manner
C. He failed to give a marriage contract first
D. Only money can be used for betrothal, not intercourse
Correct: B — Betrothal by intercourse is halachically effective, but Rav would flog the man because it is an immodest and promiscuous way to betroth. The act is valid; the method is improper.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rav Yehuda said in Rav's name that the mishna's ruling — nothing is effective after ḥalitza — follows which Tanna?
A. Rabban Gamliel
B. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
C. Rabbi Akiva
D. Rabbi Shimon
Correct: C — Rav Yehuda said in the name of Rav that the mishna follows Rabbi Akiva, who holds that a yevama who received a get is forbidden to the yavam forever — hence nothing (including betrothal) can take effect after ḥalitza.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If a man instructs a scribe to write a get for his betrothed 'so I can divorce her once I marry her,' and later gives it to her after marriage — what is the ruling according to Rami bar Ḥama?
A. The get is invalid; it must be written after marriage
B. The get is valid when given after their marriage
C. The get must be rewritten once the marriage takes place
D. The get is only valid for levirate divorce, not regular divorce
Correct: B — Rami bar Ḥama rules the get is valid because at the time of writing it was already in his power to divorce her (as his betrothed). Since the bond existed, the pre-written get takes effect when given after marriage.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rava, what precise scenario is the subject of dispute between Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi and the Rabbis regarding betrothal after ḥalitza?
A. He betroths her with money rather than intercourse after ḥalitza
B. He explicitly says 'Be betrothed with the levirate bond' after ḥalitza has dissolved it
C. He betroths her 'with levirate betrothal' without witnesses
D. He betroths her for regular marriage before ḥalitza is complete
Correct: B — Rava clarifies: when he says 'Be betrothed with levirate betrothal' all agree it works. The dispute is when he invokes 'the levirate bond' — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi says these words are meaningless since ḥalitza already severed that bond; the Rabbis say betrothal takes effect regardless.
