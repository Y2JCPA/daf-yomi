📋 How Levirate Betrothal Is Performed

With money: Yavam gives the yevama money or money's worth and declares: "You are hereby betrothed to me"
↓
With a document: He writes: "I, so-and-so, accept so-and-so my yevama upon me to feed and maintain her fitly... her marriage contract payable from property of her first husband"
↓
With intercourse: Technically valid but Rav would flog — acting in a promiscuous manner without prior betrothal
↓
Rav Huna's ruling: Betrothal before intercourse is the mitzva; intercourse before betrothal still fulfills the obligation but is not the ideal form
↓
Key distinction: The levirate bond already designates the yevama — levirate betrothal is therefore not identical to ordinary betrothal

📜 Restricted Get — Does It Disqualify the Yevama?

Abaye
If a yavam gave his yevama a get saying: 'You are divorced from me but not permitted to any other man' — is this get effective, and does it disqualify her?
Rabba
It disqualifies her. We fear people will confuse this restricted get with an ordinary get, thinking she is a regular divorcée.
Rabba bar Ḥanan
But if that is the concern, shouldn't a regular get also disqualify her? A regular get is the very thing causing the feared confusion!
Gemara
The baraita supports this: Scripture lists 'harlot,' 'profaned,' and 'divorcee' separately (Lev. 21:7), showing a yevama who receives a get is not automatically a 'divorcee' in the priestly-law sense.
Rami bar Ḥama
If one told a scribe: 'Write a get for my betrothed now so I can divorce her after marriage' — if he later married and gave her that get, it is valid, for it was already in his power to divorce her.
Key Distinction A get pre-written for a levirate bond is valid if given after levirate betrothal; but a get written for a complete stranger is never valid even if he later marries her — at writing time he had no power to divorce her.

🔓 Ḥalitza Followed by Betrothal — What Takes Effect?

Yavam performs ḥalitza with yevama
↓
She becomes a ḥalutza — levirate bond dissolved
↓
Yavam subsequently attempts to betroth the ḥalutza
↓
For levirate marriage (explicit)
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi: No get needed — betrothal ineffective
Rabbis: Betrothal takes effect; she requires a get
For regular marriage
Rav Yehuda (Rav): Nothing takes effect after ḥalitza — follows Rabbi Akiva, who forbids yevama to yavam forever once a get is given

⚖️ Abaye vs. Rava — Explaining the Post-Ḥalitza Betrothal Dispute

Abaye's Analysis

  • Rav Yosef: He is like one who hoes in another's field thinking it's his own — act has no legal effect
  • Abaye objects: Unlike hoeing, he does intend to acquire her — the analogy fails
  • Better analogy: Lifting an object to acquire it while believing he already owns it
  • Therefore the dispute requires a different explanation entirely

Rava's Analysis

  • If he says 'Be betrothed to me with levirate betrothal' after ḥalitza — all agree it is effective
  • The dispute arises when he says 'Be betrothed with the levirate bond'
  • Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi: Words are meaningless; ḥalitza already severed the bond
  • Rabbis: Betrothal still takes effect and requires a get to dissolve
