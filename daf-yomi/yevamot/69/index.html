⚖️ Who Disqualifies a Woman from Teruma?

First Tanna
Any man whose offspring are unfit disqualifies a woman he engaged in intercourse with — even if his offspring's offspring become fit.
Rabbi Yosei
Only a man whose offspring are also perpetually unfit like him disqualifies a woman. An Ammonite convert's children become fit → he does not disqualify.
Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel
Anyone whose daughter you may marry, you may marry his widow. Anyone whose daughter you may not marry, you may not marry his widow.
Ulla
The practical difference between Rabbi Yosei and Rabban Shimon is the case of an Ammonite or Moabite convert — Rabbi Yosei says he does not disqualify; Rabban Shimon says he does.
Shared Analogy All tannaʾim derive their opinions from the same case — a High Priest who engaged in intercourse with a widow — yet reach different conclusions about scope.

💍 Intercourse with Betrothed — Is the Child a Mamzer?

Rav
If a man engaged in intercourse with his betrothed in his father-in-law's house before the marriage was completed, the offspring is a mamzer.
Shmuel
The offspring is not a mamzer — the betrothal bond is meaningful enough that intercourse does not reach the level of full forbidden union.
Rava
Proof from the mishna: a priest who had extramarital intercourse with an Israelite woman — her child enables her to eat teruma. The child follows the father even in non-marital intercourse, showing lineage attaches.
Abaye
Rejects Rava's proof: the mishna speaks where she is rumored to have had intercourse with the priest specifically. That case does not resolve whether a betrothed man's illicit intercourse creates a mamzer.
Core Question Does betrothal (קידושין) create sufficient halakhic standing to prevent the child from being a mamzer, or does intercourse before completed marriage remain a full prohibition?

🔓 Four Types — How They Disqualify from Teruma

Gentile

  • Disqualifies via intercourse — yes
  • Disqualifies as offspring — no
  • Has no widowhood or divorce with a Jewish woman
  • Cannot create halakhic lineage at all

Slave

  • Disqualifies via intercourse — yes
  • Disqualifies as offspring — no
  • A slave son cannot disqualify his own mother
  • No halakhic paternity relationship

Mamzer

  • Disqualifies from teruma — yes
  • Also enables teruma — yes
  • Complex chains: can affect grandmother's status
  • Both disqualifying and enabling depending on chain

ḥalal (Priestly Disqualified)

  • Not excluded from the Israelite assembly
  • May marry a priest's daughter at the outset
  • Still disqualifies a woman through intercourse
  • Source: 'he profanes her' — verse explicitly includes ḥalal

🧑‍⚖️ The High Priest Analogy — Three Tannaitic Readings

Base case agreed upon by all: A High Priest who engages in intercourse with a widow — his children from her are unfit for the priesthood, and he disqualifies her from marrying a priest
↓
First Tanna: Just as the High Priest's children are unfit and he disqualifies the widow — so any man whose offspring are ever unfit disqualifies, even if a later generation becomes fit (e.g., 2nd-generation Egyptian)
↓
Rabbi Yosei: Just as the High Priest's children are unfit as he himself is priestly-unfit — the disqualifier must be someone whose offspring remain perpetually unfit. An Ammonite convert's children are fit → he does not disqualify
↓
Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel: Just as all the High Priest's children from the widow are unfit — only when all offspring are unfit does he disqualify. An Ammonite male's sons are still unfit → he does disqualify
↓
Practical result (Ulla): Ammonite/Moabite convert — Rabbi Yosei: does not disqualify (children fit). Rabban Shimon: does disqualify (sons still unfit). The identical analogy yields opposite conclusions.
