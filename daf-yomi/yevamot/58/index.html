🏛️ The Chuppah Question: Does Canopy Entry Disqualify from Teruma?

Rabbi Meir
Betrothal to a priest disqualifies an unfit woman from teruma — and entering the wedding canopy should likewise disqualify her.
Gemara's Challenge
We cannot assume Rabbi Meir extends betrothal rulings to chuppah — perhaps chuppah, being close to intercourse, operates under different rules entirely.
Rav Sheshet
There IS legal significance to chuppah with an unfit woman — it disqualifies her from teruma, as proven from the mishna about a betrothed sota.
Rava
Do not rely on that baraita — Rabbi Aḥa bar Ḥanina brought an alternate version from the South that undermines Rav Sheshet's proof entirely.
Core Issue Is chuppah (wedding canopy entry) equivalent to betrothal for disqualifying an unfit woman from teruma, or does its proximity to intercourse give it a categorically different halakhic status?

⚖️ Rabbi Yohanan vs. Reish Lakish: Yevama After Levirate Betrothal

Rabbi Yohanan
If a yavam performed levirate betrothal and has a priestly brother, she MAY partake of teruma. Even Rabbi Meir only restricts when she awaits Torah-level invalid intercourse — here the pending yibbum is only rabbinically problematic.
Reish Lakish
She may NOT partake of teruma. Even Rabbi Elazar and Rabbi Shimon only permit in betrothal cases where the priest himself can entitle her. Here the priestly brother cannot entitle her, so she may not eat.
Root of the Dispute Rabbi Yohanan focuses on the nature of the intercourse she awaits (Torah-level invalid vs. rabbinic). Reish Lakish focuses on whether the priest himself has the capacity to entitle her to teruma.

🔑 Betrothal vs. Chuppah vs. Intercourse: Teruma Effects

Betrothal (Kiddushin)

  • Creates formal legal acquisition (kinyan)
  • Rabbi Meir: disqualifies unfit woman from teruma
  • Rabbi Elazar & Shimon: does NOT disqualify
  • Not proximate to intercourse
  • Divorce returns her to father's house — verse applies

Chuppah (Canopy Entry)

  • Proximate to intercourse — different category?
  • Rav Sheshet: disqualifies like betrothal
  • Gemara: cannot assume same ruling as betrothal
  • Baraita dispute: some require actual intercourse
  • Independent legal significance debated

Intercourse (Biah)

  • Fully activates or deactivates teruma rights
  • Valid biah with priest: entitles to teruma
  • Torah-level invalid biah: permanently disqualifies
  • Licentious biah: may not constitute acquisition
  • Yibbum: acquires wife even without intent (Rav)

🔍 Rav Sheshet's Proof from the Mishna — and Its Unraveling

Rav Sheshet's Claim (via Rav Amram): There is legal significance to a priest entering the wedding canopy with a woman unfit to marry him — it disqualifies her from teruma. 'He illuminated our eyes from the mishna.'
↓
The Mishna's Implied Case: A betrothed sota is brought to drink the bitter waters — this requires that she be in a state with full teruma access, implying she entered chuppah (without intercourse), which gave her teruma rights that chuppah with an unfit woman would conversely revoke.
↓
Rava's Challenge: Do not rely on this baraita as accurate! Rabbi Aḥa bar Ḥanina brought an alternate version from the South reading the verse: 'And some man has lain with you' — implying a different case structure.
↓
Rami bar Ḥama's Alternative: The betrothed sota case works when her betrothed had licentious intercourse with her in her father's house — not for acquisition, so she remains 'betrothed' and the sota mechanism applies without needing chuppah.
↓
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak's Resolution: The question never truly arises — the sota oath is formulated expansively by extension (ribbui), covering future infractions as well, dissolving the apparent contradiction without relying on chuppah's independent status.
