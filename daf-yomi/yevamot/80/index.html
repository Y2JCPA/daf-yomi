Identifying & Classifying Sexual Incapacity

A man appears to be a saris chamah (natural eunuch) — born without sexual capacity
↓
How do we confirm he was never fit?
↓
Abaye's Test
Urine does not form an arch — indicates congenital defect
Cause
Mother baked bread at noon & drank shikhra marka (strong beer) during pregnancy
↓
Concern: Perhaps he was cured in the interim?
↓
Whole-Body Defect
Beginning & end impaired → no concern of interim cure
Single-Organ Defect
Must verify — blemish may have passed & returned (Bekhorot 38b)
Key Principle A whole-body defect present at birth and currently is presumed continuous, unlike a localized blemish that might fluctuate.

How Do We Know He Was Born Infertile?

Gemara
How do we know a saris chamah was never capable of having children?
Abaye
Anyone who urinates and does not form an arch — his urine dribbles downward — never had sexual capacity.
Gemara
What causes this defect?
Gemara
His mother baked bread at noon and drank shikhra marka (strong beer) while pregnant — excessive heat damaged the child's reproductive organs.
Rav Yosef
Now I understand what R' Ami meant by 'impaired from his mother's womb' — a man infertile from birth.
Medical Concept The Gemara attributes congenital infertility to prenatal conditions — the mother's excessive body heat during pregnancy.

Can We Presume Continuous Defect?

A person is impaired at birth and presently
↓
Concern: Was he perhaps cured in the interim?
↓
Whole-Body Defect (Saris)
We are NOT concerned about interim cure — presumption of continuity applies
Single-Organ (Eye Blemish)
We ARE concerned — must examine 3 times in 80 days (R' Ḥanina b. Antigonus)
Objection Source Rav Mari objected from Bekhorot 38b regarding a firstborn animal's eye blemish — but the Gemara distinguishes localized vs. systemic defects.

R' Eliezer's Retraction — Which View Did He Abandon?

Mishna Here (Yevamot)

  • Saris chamah PERFORMS ḥalitza
  • His brothers perform ḥalitza with his wife
  • Reasoning: Such men are cured in Alexandria of Egypt

Other Mishna (Niddah 47b)

  • 20-year-old male without pubic hairs = saris
  • Does NOT perform ḥalitza
  • R' Eliezer: Males follow Beit Hillel (age 20), females follow Beit Shammai (age 18)

Resolution

  • Shmuel: R' Eliezer retracted one view
  • Baraita supports: He kept the Yevamot view (saris performs ḥalitza)
  • R' Elazar: He never retracted — the Niddah ruling was about PUNISHMENTS, not ḥalitza
Resolution The baraita citing Alexandria cures supports that R' Eliezer maintained his Yevamot position. R' Elazar says no retraction occurred at all.

Signs of Saris (Natural Eunuch) — Amud B

AuthoritySign of a Saris
Tanna KammaNo beard, defective hair, smooth (hairless) skin
R' Shimon b. Gamliel / R' Yehuda b. Ya'irUrine does not raise foam
Some say (1)Urinates without forming an arch
Some say (2)Semen dissipates and fails to congeal
Some say (3)Urine does not ferment
Others sayBathes in rainy season — flesh does not give off steam
R' Shimon b. ElazarVoice is defective — not clearly male or female
Dispute Rav Huna: ALL signs must be present. R' Yoḥanan: Even ONE sign suffices. They agree: if he has two beard hairs, all signs are needed.

Eight-Month Baby — Viability & Delayed Birth

👶 A baby born in the 8th month is presumed non-viable — months of gestation incomplete
↓
🔍 R' Yehuda HaNasi: Check if hair and nails are fully developed
↓
✅ If developed → baby was fit at 7 months but DELAYED in the womb
↓
📖 Rava Tosfa'a's case: husband overseas, baby born 12 months later — ruled the child legitimate
↓
🤝 R' Yehuda HaNasi is joined by Rabban Shimon b. Gamliel — baby surviving 30 days = not stillborn (delayed 7-month birth)
↓
⚖️ Since two authorities agree, Rava Tosfa'a followed the MAJORITY, not a lone opinion
Practical Ruling Rabban Shimon b. Gamliel: Any baby surviving 30 days is presumed viable — treated as a 7-month baby delayed in the womb.
