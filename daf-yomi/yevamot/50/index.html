⏳ Can One Outlive Their Allotted Lifespan?

Rabbi Akiva
One cannot outlive the years allotted by God before birth. If deserving, God completes the span; if not, He reduces it — but no one ever exceeds the preordained limit.
The Rabbis
How can you say this? Isaiah told Hezekiah on his deathbed: 'I will add unto your days fifteen years' — proof that years beyond one's allotment are possible!
Rabbi Akiva
Those fifteen years came at a cost — they were deducted from Josiah, who was destined to descend from Hezekiah. The total allotment remained fixed; it was merely redistributed between them.
The Rabbis
But it is written only that Josiah would come from 'the House of David' — not specifically from Hezekiah! He could have descended from another Davidic line, so no years were necessarily transferred.
Unresolved Tension Does God ever grant years beyond a preordained lifespan, or is the cosmic total fixed and only redistributed? The debate ends without a clear resolution.

📜 Rabban Gamliel's Core Ruling

גט אחר גט — אינו כלום; מאמר אחר מאמר — אינו כלום
משנה — Rabban Gamliel Says: A get (bill of divorce) is not effective when given after a prior get to a yevama. Likewise, a second maamar (levirate betrothal) is ineffective after a first maamar.

Once either act has been performed once with a yevama, repeating it accomplishes nothing — the second act is legally void, and she still requires chalitza to be fully exempt.

👟 The Legal Finality of Chalitza

What is the first valid act the yavam performs with his yevama?
↓
👟 Chalitza
Levirate bond fully severed.

Nothing performed afterward — maamar, get, or biah — has any legal effect. She is completely free.
🔴 Biah (first act)
Full Torah-level acquisition.

She is his levirate wife. No subsequent act adds to or changes her status.
📜 Get or 💍 Maamar
Partial effect only.

Creates an obligation but does not fully resolve the levirate bond. Chalitza — and possibly a get — is still required.

🤔 Gemara: Why Are Get and Maamar Effective at All?

Question
A yevama is not the yavam's wife — so why should a get have any legal effect on her at all?
Gemara
Because a get is effective for married women generally. If it had no effect on the yevama, people would mistakenly treat her as a fully married woman, causing dangerous confusion about her status.
Question
And why is maamar (levirate betrothal) effective, since levirate marriage is only completed through intercourse?
Gemara
Because maamar resembles ordinary betrothal. Without its legal effect, people might mistake the yevama after maamar for a fully betrothed woman, subject to stoning for infidelity — a catastrophic error.
Question
Then why does invalid intercourse (biah in the middle) not fully acquire her, leaving subsequent acts effective?
Gemara
Rabbinic decree: intercourse after a get must remain 'invalid' to prevent confusion with intercourse after chalitza, which is a severe prohibition. The Sages kept consistent rules to avoid errors.

⚖️ Three Acts with a Yevama: Comparing Legal Weight

📜 Get (Bill of Divorce)

  • Effective even though she is not his wife
  • Reason: prevents confusion with married women
  • After get alone: chalitza still required
  • 2nd get after 1st: legally void (Rabban Gamliel)
  • After valid chalitza: get has no effect at all

💍 Maamar (Levirate Betrothal)

  • Rabbinic-level acquisition of the yevama
  • Reason: resembles ordinary betrothal
  • After maamar alone: chalitza still required
  • 2nd maamar after 1st: legally void (Rabban Gamliel)
  • After valid chalitza: maamar has no effect at all

🔴 Biah (Intercourse)

  • Torah-level acquisition — fully valid yibum
  • First act: fully acquires her as levirate wife
  • After valid biah: nothing changes her status
  • In the middle/after disqualifying act: invalid
  • Invalid biah: still requires get + chalitza
