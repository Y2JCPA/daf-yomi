⚖️ Which Disqualification Bars a Priest from Teruma?

Gemara
Perhaps only 'foreignness' — non-priestly lineage — disqualifies from teruma. An uncircumcised priest should still be permitted to eat!
Rabbi Akiva
No — the double expression 'ish ish' from the seed of Aaron includes an uncircumcised priest among those forbidden to eat teruma.
Gemara
Then why not also exclude an onein (acute mourner)? His state is involuntary, affects men and women, and applies at any time!
Rava
An acute mourner's state arises from the mitzva of honoring the dead. The Torah would not additionally penalize him by barring him from teruma.
Ruling Lack of circumcision disqualifies a priest from teruma; acute mourning does not — the mourner is in a mitzva-state beyond his control.

Still forbidden — their circumcision is not covenantal; they remain 'uncircumcised' halakhically

✂️ Was Peri'ah Commanded to Abraham?

Rabba bar Yitzchak (citing Rav)
The mitzva of peri'ah — uncovering the corona — was not given to Abraham. Only removal of the foreskin itself (milah) was commanded.
Gemara
Proof: Joshua 5:2 says 'circumcise again' — those born in the wilderness had milah but lacked peri'ah, and needed a second procedure.
Gemara (challenge)
Perhaps 'circumcise again' refers only to those never circumcised at all — not to those lacking peri'ah specifically?
Rava / Ravina
The verse equates the end of circumcision with its beginning: incomplete first circumcision must be redone. Ravina adds: peri'ah requires uncovering flesh covering most of the height and circumference of the corona.
Key Term פריעה (Peri'ah) — the second step of circumcision: pulling back the inner membrane to fully expose the corona. Not commanded until Sinai.

⚖️ Circumcision vs. Acute Mourning as Disqualifications

Lack of Circumcision

  • Applies only to males
  • Person can remedy it — yet has not
  • Disqualifies from Paschal lamb and teruma
  • Stronger: voluntary inaction is culpable
  • Verdict: excluded from teruma

Acute Mourning (אֲנִינוּת)

  • Applies to men and women equally
  • Occurs at any time; mourner cannot prevent it
  • Disqualifies from Paschal lamb only
  • Weaker: state arises from mitzva of honoring the dead
  • Verdict: not excluded from teruma (Rava)

👶 Edge Cases: Child Present at Eating, Absent at Preparation

The Rule: A father must circumcise his male children before eating the Paschal lamb. But what if a child was not present at the time of preparation?
↓
Rav Pappa: The infant's eye hurt him on the eve of Passover — could not be circumcised. He recovered by the time of eating the Paschal lamb.
↓
Rava: The infant's parents were imprisoned at the time of preparation; they slaughtered via an agent with no one available to circumcise the child.
↓
Rav Kahana (son of Rav Neḥemya): The infant was a tumtum (indeterminate sex). Surgery between preparation and eating revealed he was male.
↓
Rav Sherevya: The baby had extended his head from the womb seven days before Passover — considered born, but not yet fit for circumcision until that same day.
