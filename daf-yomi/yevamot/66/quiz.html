Yevamot 66 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna on Daf 66, what is the teruma status of melog (usufruct) slaves brought by a priest's forbidden wife?
A. They partake of teruma because they are 'his acquisition who acquired an acquisition'
B. They do not partake of teruma
C. Only tzon barzel slaves are restricted; melog slaves may partake
D. They partake only if the priest is the High Priest
Correct: B — The Mishna rules that melog slaves of a forbidden wife do NOT partake of teruma — since she herself cannot partake, she cannot enable them to do so.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Ravina's principle explaining why the forbidden wife's slaves cannot partake of teruma?
A. It is a rabbinic decree issued as a penalty against the priest
B. Only one who herself partakes of teruma can enable others to partake — the forbidden wife cannot, so neither can her slaves
C. Slaves of forbidden wives are legally equivalent to gentile slaves
D. The Torah verse 'his acquisition' grammatically excludes a forbidden wife's slaves
Correct: B — Ravina formulates the principle: whoever partakes of teruma can enable others; whoever does not partake cannot enable others. Since the forbidden wife cannot eat teruma, her slaves cannot either.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rav Ashi specifically refine the rabbinic decree about the forbidden wife's slaves?
A. The decree applies only to Israelite women married to priests, not to kohen daughters
B. It targets a widowed daughter of a priest who married the High Priest — lest she wrongly reason after his death that she may still enable her slaves to eat teruma
C. The decree applies uniformly to all cases with no distinction between types of wives
D. The decree is limited to tzon barzel slaves but not melog slaves
Correct: B — Rav Ashi refines the decree: it specifically addresses a widowed daughter of a priest (bat kohen) who married a High Priest. After the High Priest's death, she might incorrectly reason she retains priestly status — so the Sages preemptively prohibited it throughout the marriage.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the dispute between Rav Yehuda and Rabbi Ami about tzon barzel property, what does Rav Naḥman rule?
A. Halakha follows Rabbi Ami — the husband returns the monetary value only
B. Halakha follows Rav Yehuda — the wife reclaims the actual items
C. The dispute is left unresolved (teku)
D. It depends on whether the marriage ended through death or divorce
Correct: B — Rava cites Rav Naḥman as ruling that halakha follows Rav Yehuda: the wife may reclaim the actual guaranteed items, not merely a monetary equivalent, because they belong to her paternal family's estate.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the incident of the fine wool robe buried with the husband, why did Rava rule the wife could NOT reclaim it?
A. The orphans (heirs) had acquired a stronger ownership claim once the husband died
B. Tzon barzel property permanently transfers to the husband at death
C. The dead acquired it — items used for burial become forbidden from benefit, overriding her ownership claim
D. The robe had depreciated, so she could claim only monetary compensation under Rav Yehuda's ruling
Correct: C — Rava ruled 'the dead acquired it' (קנייה ליה מיתה) — once an item is consecrated for burial, benefit from it is forbidden. This principle (like hekdesh and chametz) takes effect immediately and overrides her prior ownership as tzon barzel property.
