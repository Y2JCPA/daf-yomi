QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the comb mishna in Kelim as an authoritative unattributed ruling?
A. The mishna was excluded from the official canon of the Mishnah by Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
B. The mishna's first clause contradicts its latter clause, and it is ultimately attributed to Rabbi Meir
C. Rabbi Naḥum demonstrated it reflected only a minority Galilean custom
D. The ruling was superseded by an explicit Talmudic debate in a later tractate
Correct: B — Rav Huna bar Manoaḥ explained that the comb mishna is internally contradictory (the first clause conflicts with the latter), and those exact in tradition append 'This is the statement of Rabbi Meir' — making it attributed, not truly anonymous.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Elazar in the name of Rabbi Ḥanina the Great, how is the three-month waiting period counted?
A. Exactly 90 calendar days from the date of death, including the day of death itself
B. Three complete calendar months beginning from the first day of the following month
C. Majority of the first month, all of the middle month, and majority of the third month
D. Only the middle month must be complete; the flanking months can each be as short as one day
Correct: C — Rabbi Elazar taught that one counts the majority of the first month, the entire middle month, and the majority of the third month — not necessarily 90 full days, and the endpoint days are excluded.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What happened when a man prepared a betrothal feast on the ninetieth day after his first wife's death?
A. Rava permitted the feast since 90 days clearly satisfies the three-month requirement
B. Ameimar allowed the feast but required a delay before cohabitation
C. Rava prohibited the betrothal, causing the man to forfeit his feast entirely
D. Rav Ashi ruled it was permitted since the 90th day begins the fourth month
Correct: C — Rava prohibited the betrothal on the 90th day itself, causing the man to forfeit his feast. The halakha follows that the 90th day still falls within the restricted period.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rava ultimately resolve the conflict between Rabbi Yosei's ruling and the prohibition before Tisha B'Av?
A. He rules that Tisha B'Av mourning is only rabbinic and therefore weaker than a widow's personal mourning
B. He emends Rabbi Yosei's ruling to refer to marriage rather than betrothal
C. He distinguishes Biblical mourning from communal mourning, limiting the a fortiori inference
D. He rules that Rabbi Yosei's opinion is rejected in favor of Rabbi Yehuda's stricter position
Correct: B — Rava emends Rabbi Yosei's statement: rather than 'all women may be betrothed within three months,' it should read 'all women may be married within three months' — a widow alone may not marry due to mourning.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the new Mishna (43b), if a man was married to two women and died childless, what is the rule for levirate marriage?
A. The yavam must perform levirate marriage or ḥalitza with both women individually
B. The yavam may only choose one wife; the other is permanently forbidden to all
C. Levirate marriage or ḥalitza performed with one wife automatically releases the rival wife
D. The eldest surviving brother takes the first wife and the next brother takes the second
Correct: C — The Mishna teaches that when the yavam performs either levirate marriage or ḥalitza with one of the two wives, the rival wife is automatically released from the levirate bond without requiring any further action on her part.
