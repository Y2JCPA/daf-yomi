Yevamot 87 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
When a priest's daughter returns to her father's house with no living children, what may she eat?
A. Teruma and breast & hind leg
B. Teruma only, not breast & hind leg
C. Breast & hind leg only, not teruma
D. Neither teruma nor breast & hind leg
Correct: B — The baraita teaches she returns to teruma but NOT to the breast and right hind leg of peace-offerings. Multiple Amoraim provide different scriptural sources for this distinction.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the source for the halakha that a priest's son grants his mother teruma rights even after she married a Levite?
A. The word 'bread' in Leviticus 22:13
B. The extra vav in 'But if a priest's daughter' (Leviticus 22:13)
C. The verse about widows' vows (Numbers 30:10)
D. The verse about levirate marriage (Deuteronomy 25:5)
Correct: B — Rabbi Abba said in Rav's name that the extra vav in 'But if (ve-) a priest's daughter' includes the case where she still has a son from a priest even after marrying a Levite.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why doesn't the Torah require a remarried woman to perform ḥalitza if her late husband's child (who exempted her) later dies?
A. Because dead children count the same as living for yibum
B. Because of the principle 'her ways are ways of pleasantness'
C. Because the verse says 'and he has no child'
D. Because her father cannot annul her vows
Correct: B — The Gemara invokes Proverbs 3:17 — 'Her ways are ways of pleasantness' — teaching that the Torah would not impose ḥalitza on a woman who has already remarried, as this would cause her distress.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, why is the breast and hind leg excluded when a priest's daughter returns home?
A. Because 'bread' means grain products only, not meat
B. Because her father's bread refers to property the priest owns, unlike sacrificial portions from God's table
C. Because she was married to a non-priest
D. Because the breast and hind leg are only for male priests
Correct: B — Rav Pappa distinguishes between teruma, which is the priest's own property ('her father's bread'), and the breast and hind leg, which priests receive from 'the table of the Most High' and do not truly own.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the Mishna's case of a woman who remarried after a false report of her husband's death, what is the status of children born from her second marriage?
A. Fully legitimate
B. Disqualified from priesthood only
C. Mamzerim by Torah law
D. Permitted but the marriage is retroactively annulled
Correct: C — Since she was never divorced from her first husband, her marriage to the second man is invalid and their children are mamzerim. The Mishna states the offspring is 'a mamzer from this one and from that one.'
