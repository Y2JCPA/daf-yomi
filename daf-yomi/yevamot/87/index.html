Mishna: The Priest's Daughter's Journey

👰 Priest's daughter marries an Israelite — cannot eat teruma
↓
⚰️ Israelite dies; she has a son from him — still cannot eat teruma
↓
💍 Marries a Levite — may eat ma'aser (tithe)
↓
💍 Marries a Priest — may eat teruma again
↓
⚰️ All sons die one by one — status changes with each death
↓
🔄 When no children remain — returns to father's house, eats teruma
Verse "And she is returned unto her father's house, as in her youth; she may eat of her father's bread" (Leviticus 22:13)

Why Can She Eat Teruma When Her Levite Son Dies?

Gemara
If she had a son from a priest, married a Levite, and the Levite died — she eats teruma due to the priest's son. Where is this derived?
R' Abba / Rav
The verse says "But if a priest's daughter" — the extra vav (ו) includes this case
Gemara
Is this only R' Akiva, who expounds the vav?
Gemara
No — even the Rabbis agree here, because the entire phrase "But if a priest's daughter" is superfluous, since the previous verse already established the subject
Key Point Everyone agrees the extra vav teaches that a priest's son still grants his mother teruma rights even after she was married to a Levite.

She Returns to Teruma — But Not Breast & Hind Leg

Teruma ✅

  • She may eat teruma upon returning
  • Called "her father's bread" — it belongs to the priest
  • Derived from Leviticus 22:13

Breast & Hind Leg ❌

  • She may NOT eat sacrificial portions
  • These come from "the table of the Most High"
  • Not the priest's own property
Distinction Teruma belongs to the priest; the breast and hind leg are portions given by God from offerings — a returned daughter does not partake of them.
6 / 9

Four Sources for the Breast & Hind Leg Exclusion

Rav Ḥisda / Ravina bar Sheila
"She may not eat of that which is set apart from the sacred things" (Lev. 22:12) — portions separated from offerings
Rav Naḥman / Rabba bar Avuh
"She may eat of her father's bread" — bread yes, but not all bread; this excludes breast and hind leg
Rav Safra
"From her father's bread" — she eats bread (teruma) but not meat (breast and hind leg)
Rav Pappa
"Her father's bread" = property owned by her father; breast and hind leg come from God's table, not the priest's own
Rava
"You, and your sons and your daughters with you" (Lev. 10:14) — daughters eat only when they are with you, not after leaving
Note Rami bar Ḥama challenged that "her father's bread" might exclude vow annulment instead, but Rava refuted this using the teaching of the school of R' Yishmael about widows and divorcées.
