Yevamot 97 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Shimon ben Yoḥai, what happens when a halakha is stated in a deceased Torah scholar's name?
A. His soul ascends higher in Gan Eden
B. His lips mouth the words in the grave
C. His merit protects his descendants
D. An angel repeats the teaching in Heaven
Correct: B — R' Shimon ben Yoḥai teaches that when a halakha is said in a scholar's name, his lips move (דובבות) in the grave, derived from Shir HaShirim 7:10.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What distinguishes a eunuch (סריס) from a late bloomer at age 20 without pubic hairs?
A. A eunuch is taller than average
B. A eunuch has developed other signs of being a eunuch
C. A eunuch cannot grow a beard
D. A eunuch was born by caesarean section
Correct: B — Rav Shmuel bar Yitzḥak said in Rav's name: the baraita about eunuchs applies only when he developed OTHER signs of a eunuch by age 20. Without those signs, he is merely a late bloomer.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara present the family riddles using cases of gentiles rather than Jews?
A. The halakha does not apply to Jews
B. Gentiles have different family structures
C. The Gemara does not wish to entertain the idea of Jews acting this way
D. Jewish lineage is traced differently
Correct: C — The Gemara explicitly states it uses gentile examples because it does not wish to entertain the idea that a Jew would engage in such incestuous acts.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the mishna permit a man to marry the sister of a woman he raped, while prohibiting marriage to the sister of his wife?
A. Rape creates a lesser bond than seduction
B. The Torah uses 'taking' (marriage) not 'lying' for the prohibition on wife's relatives
C. The raped woman's family can refuse the marriage
D. Only married women's relatives are considered family by blood
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that regarding a man's wife's relatives, the Torah uses 'taking' (לקיחה/marriage), not 'lying' (שכיבה/intercourse), teaching that only marriage — not non-marital relations — creates the prohibition.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the dispute about freed slaves who are full brothers, why does Rav Sheshet prohibit them from marrying each other's ex-wives?
A. Torah law requires levirate marriage for freed slaves
B. They are still considered brothers even after freedom
C. People also identify them by their mother, so onlookers may think a man is improperly marrying his brother's wife
D. Freed slaves retain their prior family obligations
Correct: C — Rav Sheshet holds that since people also call them by their mother's name, observers may not realize that converts are halakhically unrelated and may conclude that marrying a brother's ex-wife without yibum is permitted.
