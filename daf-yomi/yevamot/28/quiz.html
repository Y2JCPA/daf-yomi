QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the explanation that the mishna requires ḥalitza due to a rabbinic decree lest the second brother die?
A. Because the second brother's death is halakhically irrelevant
B. Because rabbinic decrees can never require ḥalitza
C. Because that reasoning would only justify ḥalitza retroactively, not as the first course of action ab initio
D. Because Rabbi Yoḥanan holds the decree was already annulled
Correct: C — The mishna implies ḥalitza is performed ab initio (from the outset as the primary ruling), but a decree based on the second brother possibly dying would only retroactively justify ḥalitza — it would not require it as the initial ruling.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, what specific sequence of events causes both sisters to be forbidden for levirate marriage?
A. Both sisters fell before the yevamin simultaneously, creating mutual prohibition
B. The non-mother-in-law fell first (permitted to both); when the mother-in-law later fell before them, she was forbidden — thereby also forbidding her rival wife (the first sister)
C. The mother-in-law fell first, immediately making her sister forbidden as well
D. One brother performed ḥalitza with one sister, automatically exempting the other
Correct: B — Rav Pappa: the non-mother-in-law fell first and was permitted to both brothers. When the mother-in-law subsequently fell before them, she was forbidden to both as a mother-in-law — and as a set-before-him forbidden woman, she now renders her rival wife (the sister) also forbidden.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What distinguishes Rav Yehuda's position from Abaye's regarding which women fall under the sister principle?
A. Rav Yehuda applies the principle to all 15 cases; Abaye applies it only to the mother-in-law
B. Rav Yehuda limits it to the first 6 cases; Abaye applies it to all remaining cases
C. Rav Yehuda excludes the first 6 cases and rape cases; Abaye extends the principle to include a daughter born from rape
D. There is no practical difference — they dispute only the biblical source
Correct: C — Rav Yehuda holds the sister principle applies from 'mother-in-law onward' and excludes rape cases, because the two-sisters-as-daughters scenario is impossible via marriage in the first 6. Abaye extends the principle to rape cases because the scenario is findable even there.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the new mishna's case of three brothers, if one of the two sisters is forbidden to the third brother due to a biblical prohibition against forbidden relatives, what is the halakha?
A. Both sisters must perform ḥalitza; neither may enter levirate marriage
B. The third brother is forbidden to the relative but permitted to marry her sister through levirate marriage
C. The third brother must perform ḥalitza with both, then may marry neither
D. The forbidden sister's status has no effect on the other sister
Correct: B — Because the relative is forbidden by a full biblical prohibition, she is not considered 'set before him' as a valid levirate candidate. She therefore does not render her sister forbidden, and the third brother may consummate levirate marriage with the other sister.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Rabbi Shimon's ruling in the three-brothers case where one sister is forbidden due to a mitzva-prohibition, and what is his source?
A. He permits levirate marriage with the non-forbidden sister; source: Deuteronomy 25
B. He requires ḥalitza from both sisters but permits neither levirate; source: Leviticus 18
C. He exempts both sisters from both ḥalitza and levirate entirely; source: 'You shall not take a woman to her sister' (Lev. 18:18)
D. He rules like Beit Shammai that one performs ḥalitza and one enters levirate
Correct: C — Rabbi Shimon derives from 'You shall not take a woman to her sister to be a rival to her' (Lev. 18:18) that where sisters are rival wives, even a mitzva-level prohibition on one makes the other like a full forbidden relative — thus both sisters are completely exempt from ḥalitza and levirate marriage.
