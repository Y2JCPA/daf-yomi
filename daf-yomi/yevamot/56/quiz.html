Yevamot 56 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, what is the fundamental limit of the yavam's power to grant teruma rights?
A. He can grant teruma rights after complete intercourse only, not the initial stage
B. He can only preserve teruma rights the original marriage already granted — he cannot elevate her status
C. He can always grant teruma rights regardless of the original marriage
D. He can grant teruma rights only if the yevama was from betrothal, not marriage
Correct: B — Shmuel's principle: the yavam establishes himself in the husband's place but cannot strengthen her beyond the husband's status. Where the original marriage gave teruma rights, yavam preserves them. Where it did not (betrothal only), yavam cannot create them.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the case of a yevama from betrothal who has initial intercourse with the yavam, what does Rav rule about teruma?
A. She may not eat teruma — the initial stage is insufficient
B. She may eat teruma only after complete intercourse with the yavam
C. She may eat teruma — the yavam's intercourse grants her full status in his household
D. She may eat teruma only by rabbinic enactment, not by Torah law
Correct: C — Rav holds that even initial intercourse with the yavam fully acquires the yevama and places her in his priestly household — entitling her to teruma just as any wife of a priest, regardless of whether she came from marriage or betrothal.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rabbi Natan's basis for allowing a woman to continue eating teruma even after her priest-husband became a deaf-mute?
A. A deaf-mute's marriage is fully valid under Torah law
B. Since she already ate teruma in a permitted manner, she may continue — we do not issue a new disqualifying decree
C. The Rabbis agreed with Rabbi Natan in practice even while disputing the principle
D. Her father's Israelite status provides an independent basis for teruma
Correct: B — Rav Yosef explains Rabbi Natan: marriage by one who was competent at the time of betrothal entitles her to teruma, and we do not add a new rabbinic decree against eating through such a marriage. Her valid start creates an ongoing entitlement.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why, according to Rava, is a priest flogged for having intercourse with his wife who was raped?
A. Because rape creates a sota prohibition that applies even to priests
B. Because she has become ritually impure and priests cannot have intercourse with impure women
C. Because she is now considered a zona, and the Torah forbids a priest from remaining with a zona
D. Because the positive commandment of priestly holiness overrides the permission given to Israelite husbands
Correct: C — Rava: all women who have extramarital intercourse — even through coercion — are included in the category of zona. The verse carving out an exception ('and neither was she taken') applies only to the wife of an Israelite. The priest's wife has no such verse, so she becomes a zona forbidden to her husband.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the mishna (56b), what is the teruma status of a widow who was merely betrothed (not married) to a High Priest?
A. She may eat teruma, since any priestly betrothal grants household status
B. She may eat teruma until the betrothal is dissolved by a rabbinic court
C. She may not eat teruma — the forbidden betrothal grants no teruma rights
D. She may eat teruma only if the High Priest's prior wife has died
Correct: C — The mishna rules clearly: a widow betrothed to a High Priest — a forbidden union violating a positive commandment — may not eat teruma even from the moment of betrothal. The invalid betrothal has no power to grant her priestly household privileges.
