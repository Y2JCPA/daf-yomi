🌾 Can the Yavam Elevate Her Status? Rav vs. Shmuel

Rav
A yevama from betrothal may eat teruma after even the initial stage of intercourse with the yavam — his act grants her full priestly household status.
Shmuel
The Torah includes inferior forms of intercourse only to establish the yavam in place of the husband — NOT to strengthen her beyond her original status with the deceased brother.
Rav
Therefore: yevama from marriage → continues eating teruma. Yevama from betrothal → may also begin to eat teruma after yavam intercourse.
Shmuel
Only where her original marriage entitled her to teruma does yavam intercourse preserve that right. Betrothal alone never granted teruma — the yavam cannot grant what the husband could not.
Shmuel's Principle (cited by Rav Naḥman) In any case where the woman's marriage to her original husband entitled her to eat teruma, intercourse with the yavam in any of the manners listed in the mishna also entitles her to teruma — but no more than that.

🔇 Deaf-Mute Yavam: Does She Retain Teruma Rights?

Rabbis
If a competent priest betrothed an Israelite woman but became deaf-mute before marriage, she may NOT eat teruma — intercourse with a deaf-mute yavam cannot elevate her to priestly teruma rights.
Rabbi Natan
If she already began eating teruma while he was competent (through valid marriage), she may continue to eat it even after he becomes deaf-mute — we do not retroactively disqualify her.
Abaye
Why does Rabbi Natan only disagree in the second clause? He should also disagree in the first clause — that she cannot eat teruma even when he was always deaf-mute!
Rav Yosef
Rabbi Natan let the Rabbis finish their statement, then disagreed with their entire ruling. His principle: marriage by one who was competent at betrothal entitles her — we issue no new decree against her.
Rabbi Natan's Principle Since she has already eaten teruma in a permitted manner (when he was competent), she may continue. Past valid eating establishes an ongoing right not subject to new disqualification.

⚠️ Raped Wife: Israelite vs. Priest's Wife

🏠 Wife of an Israelite — Raped

  • Remains permitted to her husband after rape
  • Verse: 'And neither was she taken' (Numbers 5:13) — if taken by force, she is permitted
  • She does NOT become a zona through coercion
  • No prohibition prevents her husband from continuing the marriage
  • Special verse carves out exception explicitly for her case

⚠️ Wife of a Priest — Raped

  • According to Rava: she becomes a zona through the rape
  • Her priest-husband is flogged for having intercourse with her
  • Priests are forbidden from marrying or remaining with a zona
  • The Israelite wife's exemption does NOT extend to the priest's wife
  • Rabba: all extramarital intercourse creates zona status — only the Israelite's wife is excepted by verse

📜 Mishna: Forbidden Priestly Unions and Teruma

אַלְמָנָה לְכֹהֵן גָּדוֹל, גְּרוּשָׁה וַחֲלוּצָה לְכֹהֵן הֶדְיוֹט — אֲפִלּוּ קִדֵּשׁ בִּלְבַד, לֹא תֹאכַל בַּתְּרוּמָה
משנה — Yevamot 56b Widow to a High Priest, divorcée or ḥalutza to a common priest — even if they only engaged in betrothal and never had intercourse, they may not partake of teruma. The forbidden betrothal (violating a positive commandment) grants no teruma rights.

If such women were later widowed or divorced from the priest after actual marriage:
• They are disqualified from the priesthood entirely
• A woman who is forbidden to a priest but has intercourse with him becomes a chalalah (חֲלָלָה)
• She loses all priestly privileges permanently
