🗣️ How Long Is a Persistent Rumor? Abaye said in the name of his mother: A rumor in the city lasts one and a half days (yom u'meḥatzah). The Gemara qualifies: This applies only if the rumor did not cease spreading during that period. If it ceased and then resumed, it is considered a new rumor — not the original persistent one.

⚔️ The Get Agent — Why Can He Not Marry?

Questioner
The mishna says the agent who brings a get from overseas may not marry the divorcée. Why is overseas significant? What about an agent who delivers locally?
Gemara
Specifically overseas — because the court relies entirely on his declaration 'written and signed in my presence' to validate the get. He personally enabled this divorce. Suspicion arises that he orchestrated it to marry her himself.
Questioner
But similarly, when a witness testifies that the husband died, we also rely on him alone. Why then can he marry the widow? He too 'enabled' her freedom!
Gemara
Not comparable. There, the woman herself is exacting in investigating her husband's death — she would not remarry on flimsy testimony. Here, the written get is tangible proof of the agent's involvement. The document itself is suspicious.
Halakhic Result The overseas get agent cannot marry the divorcée; an ordinary local witness may marry the widow.

🔓 Sage Who Ruled on a Vow: Two Outcomes

Rendered Her FORBIDDEN

  • Sage refused to annul the vow
  • Her vow made her forbidden to her husband
  • Husband was forced to divorce her
  • ❌ Sage MAY NOT marry her
  • Suspicion: he refused annulment in order to marry her himself

Rendered Her PERMITTED

  • Sage successfully annulled the vow
  • She was permitted to remain with her husband
  • She was later divorced or widowed for unrelated reasons
  • ✅ Sage MAY marry her
  • No suspicion — he acted to preserve her marriage, not dissolve it

⚖️ Judge at Ḥalitza or Refusal — May He Later Marry?

Judge participated in ḥalitza or mi'un (refusal) proceeding
↓
One of three judges on court
✅ May marry her
Functioned as part of a panel — no individual suspicion of ulterior motive
Sole expert judge (dayyан yachid)
⚠️ Analyzed separately
Rav Ḥisda: a single expert may dissolve vows — same expertise permits him to conduct ḥalitza alone, but suspicion is heightened
↓
Contrast with witnesses: Witnesses who merely signed a get or bill of divorce may marry the woman — signing is not the same as personally enabling the separation

📋 Yevamot 25 — Summary Map

Did this person enable her new freedom?
↓
✅ May Marry
• Annulled vow (preserved marriage)
• Acted as 1 of 3 judges
• Only signed the document
• Local get delivery (no validation role)
❌ Cannot Marry
• Brought get from overseas
• Testified husband died
• Refused to annul vow → divorce resulted
↓
Core Principle: Ḥashad (suspicion) disqualifies marriage when the person's action directly caused the woman's new availability — even if the act itself was entirely permissible
