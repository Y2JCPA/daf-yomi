📜 What Does 'The Firstborn She Bears' Mean?

Baraita
The verse (Deut. 25:6): 'The firstborn she bears shall be established in the name of his dead brother.' Does 'name' mean he inherits his estate?
Baraita
Or perhaps only that he carries his literal name? If the deceased was named Yosef, the levirate son must be called Yosef?
Rava
Even though in the entire Torah a verse never departs from its literal meaning, here the gezerah shavah on the word 'shem' (name) overrides: it teaches inheritance of estate, not literal naming.
Gemara
Without the analogy, the verse could mean: the court tells the yavam 'let your son carry the brother's name.' The gezerah shavah resolves the ambiguity — 'name' = inheritance.
⚖️ Halakhic Conclusion The gezerah shavah teaches: 'name' = succession to the estate. Literal naming of the child is not required by Torah law.

👑 Gemara's Logic: Why All Brothers May Perform Yibbum

Torah says 'the firstborn she bears' — seemingly only the firstborn brother may perform yibbum
↓
Challenge: If only the firstborn, why does 'brothers dwelling together' apply to non-firstborns at all?
↓
Answer
All brothers may perform yibbum; 'firstborn' language grants priority, not exclusivity
Further challenge
Then say: if there is no firstborn, a younger brother who acts first does not acquire her?
↓
Final ruling: Younger brother who acts first fully acquires the yevama — just as the firstborn's status takes precedence, so too the eldest's

🚫 Suspected Relations — Marriage Prohibitions

Maidservant → Freed

  • Man suspected of relations with a Canaanite maidservant
  • She is later freed (becomes a full Jewess)
  • Prohibition: He may NOT marry her
  • Even if priests — court does not remove them if already married
  • Reason: avoids 'strengthening the suspicion' — appears to legalize the sin

Gentile Woman → Converts

  • Man suspected of relations with a gentile woman
  • She later converts to Judaism
  • Her conversion IS valid (Rav) — she is a genuine convert
  • But Rav Asi: still prohibited ab initio
  • 'Who informed her husband?' — suspicion she engineered her freedom to marry him

Questionable Converts (R. Neḥemia)

  • Converts by lions (Samaritans — out of fear, II Kings 17)
  • Converts by dreams (supernatural motivation, not sincere)
  • Converts of Mordecai & Esther's era (converted from fear of Jews)
  • Converts in the days of the Messiah
  • All: not considered true converts; not accepted

🕊️ Converts in Special Eras Rabbi Neḥemia's four categories of suspect converts:

1. Converts by lions — Samaritans (Kutim) who converted under threat of divine punishment
2. Converts by dreams — motivated by supernatural experience, not genuine commitment
3. Converts of Mordecai and Esther's era — 'Many people of the land became Jews, for the fear of the Jews fell upon them' (Esther 8:17)
4. Converts in Messianic times — similarly opportunistic

Historical parallel: Converts were also not accepted in the days of David and Solomon, when joining Israel was politically advantageous.

All four types: not considered genuine converts
