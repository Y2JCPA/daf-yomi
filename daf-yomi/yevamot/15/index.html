⚖️ Did Beit Shammai Practice What They Preached?

Yes — They Acted
Beit Shammai did act on their ruling — rival wives of forbidden relatives were exempt from both chalitza and levirate marriage. This explains why Rabbi Yehoshua feared declaring certain children mamzerim.
No — They Did Not
Beit Shammai did not act on their ruling. The amendment for rival wives was enacted to protect the women themselves. Rabbi Yoḥanan the Ḥorani — a disciple of Shammai — always followed Beit Hillel in practice.
The Stakes If Beit Shammai acted on their ruling, children of rival wives who entered levirate marriage may be mamzerim — a catastrophic finding affecting real Jerusalem families like Beit Tzevo'im and Beit Kofai.

🏛️ Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel's Dilemma

The Problem
After the Sages ruled that rival wives should perform chalitza (not levirate marriage), some rival wives had already entered levirate marriage. Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel asked: what do we do about them now?
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
The amendment was enacted for the rival wife herself, not for her children. If she performs chalitza she seems repulsive to her husband. If she divorces, she loses her ketubbah. So she simply remains with him.
Pragmatic Resolution The Sages' amendment was a protective measure for the woman already in the marriage — not a theoretical ruling about mamzer status.

🔀 The Rival Wife Problem: All Options Are Problematic

Rival wife of a forbidden relative — her husband died childless. What must she do?
↓
Chalitza
Frees her to remarry — but makes her seem repulsive to a husband she has been living with
Levirate Marriage
Beit Shammai says forbidden — rival wife of a prohibited woman is exempt and may not enter yibbum
Divorce
She loses her ketubbah — an economically devastating outcome
↓
Rabbinic Solution: She remains with her husband — the existing marriage stands and she is not disturbed

🌿 Rabbi Tarfon vs. The Stringent View

Rabbi Tarfon

  • "I yearn for the day my daughter's rival wife comes before me — I will marry her!"
  • Would follow Beit Hillel: rival wife is permitted for levirate marriage
  • Novelty: excludes those advocating extra stringency beyond Beit Hillel
  • Even in this emotionally sensitive case (his own family), Beit Hillel's leniency applies
  • A Torah scholar confidently relying on the accepted halakhic ruling

The Stringent View

  • One might think: be stringent and require chalitza even for rival wives permitted by Beit Hillel
  • Worry that any levirate marriage in questionable cases could produce mamzerim
  • Rabbi Tarfon's statement explicitly rejects adding extra stringency
  • The Gemara: if Beit Hillel is accepted practice, why does Rabbi Tarfon need to yearn?
  • Answer: he excludes those who say one should go beyond Beit Hillel's leniency

📜 Rabbi Yehoshua's Testimony: The Great Jerusalem Families

שני משפחות גדולות היו בירושלים — בית צבועים מבן עכמאי ובית קופאי מבן מקושש
Historical Proof Question to Rabbi Yehoshua: What is the halakha for the rival wife of a daughter?

Rabbi Yehoshua: It is a dispute between Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel. But I testify that Beit Tzevo'im from Ben Akhmai and Beit Kofai from Ben Mekoshesh — two great Jerusalem families — are descendants of rival wives who married into the priesthood, and the High Priest would marry among them.

The inference: If Beit Shammai had acted on their ruling, these families would be mamzerim. Rabbi Yehoshua expresses only fear, not certainty of mamzer status — implying Beit Shammai did not act on their ruling.

Further nuance: Rabbi Yehoshua holds that mamzerut applies only to those liable to court-imposed capital punishment — not to those merely liable to karet. Even if Beit Shammai had acted, the children may not be mamzerim by his definition.

📋 Yevamot 15 — Summary Map

Core Question: Did Beit Shammai act on their ruling that rival wives of forbidden relatives are exempt from both chalitza and levirate marriage?
↓
If YES (acted):
• Children of rival wives who did yibbum = mamzerim
• Jerusalem families Beit Tzevo'im and Beit Kofai disqualified
• Rabbi Yehoshua's fear is fully justified
If NO (did not act):
• Amendment protected women already in marriages
• Children are fully permitted
• Rabbi Yoḥanan the Ḥorani confirms by his own practice
↓
Conclusion: Beit Shammai did not act on their ruling. The Sages' amendment was a pragmatic protection for women already in marriages. The Jerusalem families are permitted.
