Yevamot 20 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Sages (not Rabbi Yehuda), what does the term 'prohibition resulting from a mitzva' refer to?
A. A widow marrying a High Priest or a divorcée marrying a common priest
B. Secondary forbidden relationships prohibited by rabbinic law
C. Any woman forbidden by a Torah prohibition with the penalty of karet
D. A mamzeret or Gibeonite woman forbidden from the congregation
Correct: B — The Sages hold that 'prohibition from mitzva' refers to secondary (sheniyot) relationships — rabbinic fences around Torah law. Rabbi Yehuda reverses the categories and applies this term to priestly restrictions (widow to High Priest, etc.).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Abaye call the priestly restrictions a 'prohibition stemming from sanctity'?
A. Because the High Priest performs the holiest service in the Temple
B. Because they are derived from the verse 'He shall be holy' (Leviticus 21:6)
C. Because violating them carries the penalty of karet
D. Because they are more severe than all other prohibitions
Correct: B — Abaye derives the name 'sanctity' from the verse written about priests: 'He shall be holy to his God' (Leviticus 21:6). The priestly holiness requirement is the source of these prohibitions.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rava's final accepted reason that a High Priest may NOT take a widow in levirate marriage?
A. The verse 'his yevama shall ascend' excludes widows forbidden to the yavam
B. A widow is forbidden to the High Priest by both a positive mitzva and a negative prohibition simultaneously
C. The rabbinic decree against a second act of intercourse applies here
D. The High Priest's levirate obligation is nullified by his priestly status
Correct: B — Rava concludes that when a Torah prohibition is backed by both a positive mitzva ('take a wife in her virginity,' Lev. 21:13) AND a negative prohibition, the levirate bond is severed. Only ḥalitza remains available.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The Gemara challenges Rava's decree by invoking the case of the aylonit. What is the challenge?
A. An aylonit cannot perform ḥalitza, which creates an irresolvable contradiction
B. Since an aylonit may not do levirate, all yevamot should be prohibited by rabbinic decree due to possible confusion
C. An aylonit automatically nullifies the levirate bond of all co-wives
D. The Sages exempted the aylonit because she looks like a permitted yevama
Correct: B — The Gemara argues: by the logic used to restrict certain yevamot (due to confusion with forbidden cases), one should prohibit all women from levirate marriage lest they be confused with an aylonit, who cannot enter levirate. The Gemara answers that the Torah made the mitzva explicit.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the baraita cited near the end of the daf, what is the legal status if a prohibited yavam engages in intercourse with his yevama?
A. The act has no legal effect and she remains fully bound by the levirate obligation
B. He acquires her with the first act but is then prohibited from retaining her
C. He acquires her fully and the marriage stands, but he receives lashes
D. She is completely released from the levirate bond without needing ḥalitza
Correct: B — The baraita teaches that despite the rabbinic prohibition on the first act, it nonetheless legally acquires the yevama. However, he may not retain her — he must give a divorce. This ruling is cited as confirmation of Rava's framework.
