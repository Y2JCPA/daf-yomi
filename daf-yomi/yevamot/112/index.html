Core Question: Yevama claims no consummation — what procedures apply?
↓

Court forces ḥalitza whether or not yavam agrees
After 30 Days

Why Force Ḥalitza Instead of Consummation?

Gemara
If he hasn't consummated the levirate marriage, why not force him to consummate rather than perform ḥalitza?
Rav
The mishna refers to a case where her get is already in her hand — he gave her a bill of divorce, but she claims he never consummated.
R' Ami
When the baraita says she needs a get, it means she needs ḥalitza together with the get she already received.
Rav Ashi
Rav's case: a get for the levirate bond (before consummation). The baraita's case: a get for the consummation (after relations, she's his wife).
Distinction A get given for the zikah (levirate bond) still requires ḥalitza; a get given after consummation is a standard divorce.

He says: Yes, then retracts

Rava's Retraction Rava initially ruled that mutual admission of non-consummation requires only ḥalitza. When Rav Sherevya cited a baraita requiring both get and ḥalitza, Rava retracted.

Vows Against the Yavam

Wife vows during husband's lifetime: "No benefit from my yavam"
↓
Did she have children at the time of the vow?
Yes — Children
She didn't anticipate needing yibum → Court forces ḥalitza
No Children?
Gemara concludes: No difference — court forces ḥalitza either way (per Rav)
↓
Conclusion: Unless she explicitly states intent to avoid yibum, we don't suspect manipulation
Rav's Principle We do not assume a woman planned to avoid levirate marriage unless she says so explicitly. The court forces ḥalitza regardless.

Three Women Originally Divorced with Ketubah Payment

Original Ruling

  • "I am defiled to you" — priest's wife who was raped
  • "Heaven is between us" — husband cannot perform relations
  • "I am withdrawn from Jews" — vows against all relations

Revised Ruling

  • Defiled → must bring evidence of rape
  • Heaven between us → court deals with it by request, not force
  • Withdrawn → husband nullifies his part; she remains forbidden to all other Jews
Why the Change? The Sages feared a woman might cast her eyes on another man and deliberately ruin her marriage to leave with her ketubah.

Mishna: Deaf-Mute (Ḥeresh) Marriage & Divorce Rules

🤐 A deaf-mute man marries a competent woman → may divorce her by intimation (gestures)
↓
👂 A competent man marries a deaf-mute woman → may divorce her normally
↓
🔇 Wife becomes deaf-mute during marriage → husband may divorce her
↓
🧠 Wife becomes an imbecile → husband may not divorce her (no legal capacity)
↓
🚫 Husband becomes deaf-mute or imbecile → he can never divorce her
Core Principle Marriage by intimation → divorce by intimation. But a man must act willingly to divorce; a woman can be divorced even unwillingly.
