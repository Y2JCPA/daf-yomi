Yevamot 14 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What was Reish Lakish's position regarding Beit Shammai and their own rulings?
A. Beit Shammai always acted strictly according to their own rulings
B. Beit Shammai did not act according to their own rulings — the disputes were theoretical
C. Beit Shammai only followed their rulings after the Bat Kol
D. Beit Shammai followed their rulings only in matters of ritual purity
Correct: B — Reish Lakish argued that Beit Shammai did NOT in practice follow their own rulings — the halakhic disputes between the schools were theoretical debates, not lived practice.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How did Rava resolve the apparent violation of 'lo titgodedu' if Beit Shammai acted on their rulings?
A. He ruled that lo titgodedu only applies to matters of prayer, not marriage law
B. He said the Bat Kol retroactively validated all of Beit Shammai's practices
C. He argued that two schools present everywhere is like two courts in two different cities — which is permitted
D. He ruled that lo titgodedu only applies after the destruction of the Temple
Correct: C — Rava explained that lo titgodedu prohibits two courts in one city issuing conflicting rulings. Since both schools existed throughout Israel, it is analogous to two courts in separate cities — which is entirely permissible.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Gemara's conclusion, how were Beit Hillel able to marry into Beit Shammai despite the rival-wife dispute?
A. Beit Hillel held that all the children were fit and there was no concern
B. Beit Shammai notified Beit Hillel which women they had permitted, and Beit Hillel withdrew from those matches
C. A rabbinic enactment permitted intermarriage between the schools
D. Beit Hillel ruled leniently in cases of doubt regarding mamzer status
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that Beit Shammai would inform Beit Hillel of which women they had permitted under their rulings. Beit Hillel would then withdraw from those matches, avoiding any possibility of forbidden unions.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rabbi Abbahu's behavior regarding the oil lamp illustrates which principle?
A. A scholar must always follow the strictest available opinion
B. The Bat Kol's ruling overrides all local customs
C. A visiting scholar should follow local practice out of deference, even if his own ruling differs
D. Carrying objects on Shabbat after the flame burns out is universally prohibited
Correct: C — Rabbi Abbahu would carry the oil lamp (following Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi's leniency) in that locale, but would not carry it when visiting Rabbi Yoḥanan's city — demonstrating that a scholar defers to local rulings out of respect, even when his personal ruling differs.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Elazar — and against Rabbi Akiva's position — when does a child have the status of mamzer?
A. When born from any union that violates a Torah prohibition, including those punishable by lashes
B. Only when born from a union whose parties are liable to receive karet
C. Only when born from a union punishable by the death penalty administered by a court
D. When born from any union forbidden by rabbinic decree
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar taught that both Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel agree: mamzer status applies only to offspring of unions carrying karet liability (e.g., certain incestuous relationships). This explicitly rejects Rabbi Akiva's stricter view that even lash-level prohibitions produce mamzerim.
