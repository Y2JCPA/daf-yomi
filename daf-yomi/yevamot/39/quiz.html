QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye, why does the yavam have a weaker claim than the yevama to her property?
A. The Torah explicitly grants the yevama superior property rights over the yavam
B. Beit Hillel holds the husband's hand equals the wife's — so the yavam, further removed, has an even weaker hand
C. The yevama's property was all bequeathed before her first marriage began
D. Rava and Abaye agree on this point and it is the accepted ruling
Correct: B — Abaye reasons: Beit Hillel holds husband's hand = wife's hand (not stronger). The yavam is one step further removed than a husband, so his hand is necessarily weaker than the yevama's own claim to her property.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava, what status does Beit Shammai say levirate betrothal (zikah) confers on the yevama?
A. Full marriage status in every legal respect
B. No legal status whatsoever — she is entirely unacquired
C. Status of a doubly uncertain betrothed: like definitely betrothed in some ways, not betrothed in others
D. Status identical to a divorced woman awaiting remarriage
Correct: C — Rava explains that Beit Shammai views zikah as conferring a partial, hybrid status — certain aspects of definite betrothal apply, while other aspects treat her as if not betrothed at all.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the more limited version of the dispute between R. Yoḥanan and R. Yehoshua ben Levi?
A. Whether a minor brother can ever perform yibum validly
B. Whether the yevama's consent is required for ḥalitza
C. Only whether ḥalitza of the eldest brother is preferable to ḥalitza of a younger brother
D. Whether the court may force a yavam to perform ḥalitza against his will
Correct: C — The second version of the dispute holds that both sages agree yibum by any brother is preferable to ḥalitza. They only dispute whether, when only ḥalitza is being performed, the eldest brother's ḥalitza is superior to a younger brother's.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Abba Shaul, what is the status of yibum performed for the sake of the yevama's beauty or to inherit her husband's estate?
A. Fully valid, but the yavam must bring an atonement offering
B. Invalid — the yevama still requires ḥalitza from another brother
C. As though he has approached a forbidden relative (ervah)
D. Permitted but discouraged — considered a minor infraction only
Correct: C — Abba Shaul rules that yibum motivated by improper reasons (beauty, lust, or desire for inheritance) is like approaching an ervah. This strict ruling caused the later Sages to prefer ḥalitza over yibum during that period.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If the eldest brother refuses both yibum and ḥalitza, and all younger brothers also refuse, what does the mishna require?
A. The yevama is permanently forbidden to remarry anyone
B. The court forces the youngest brother to perform yibum
C. The court returns to the eldest brother and demands he perform ḥalitza at minimum
D. The yevama may petition a rabbinic court to release herself without any ḥalitza
Correct: C — The mishna states that if the younger brothers also refuse, the court returns to the eldest and demands he perform ḥalitza — even if he refuses yibum, ḥalitza is the minimum obligation that falls on him as the eldest.
