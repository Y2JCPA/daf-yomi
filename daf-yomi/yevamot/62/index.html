⚖️ Minimum Requirement for פרו ורבו

Beit Shammai

  • First baraita: two sons AND two daughters
  • Source: Cain and Abel each had sisters (Gen 4:2 — superfluous 'et')
  • Second baraita version: a son AND a daughter
  • Proof: 'Male and female He created them' (Gen 5:2)

Beit Hillel

  • First baraita: a son AND a daughter
  • Source: 'Male and female He created them' (Gen 5:2)
  • Second baraita version: EITHER a son OR a daughter
  • Proof: humanity itself was created one male and one female

🔄 Convert's Pre-Conversion Children — Does It Count?

Rabbi Yoḥanan
He HAS fulfilled פרו ורבו. Gentiles have genuine family lineage — proven by 'Berodach-baladan son of Baladan' (II Kings 20:12), identified by his father's name.
Reish Lakish
He has NOT fulfilled it. A convert is legally like a newborn — all prior relationships and children are annulled. Pre-conversion children carry no halakhic weight.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Consistently: the firstborn son born AFTER conversion is NOT a halakhic firstborn for inheritance — the earlier gentile son already established that role.
Reish Lakish
Consistently: the firstborn born AFTER conversion IS the halakhic firstborn — gentile children count for nothing in Jewish inheritance law.
Universal Exception: Canaanite Slave Rav: All agree a Canaanite slave has no family lineage — 'Remain here with the donkey' (Gen 22:5) implies they are likened to animals. Unlike gentiles, Bible never identifies slaves by their father's name.

💔 If Children Died — Was פרו ורבו Fulfilled?

Rav Huna
He HAS fulfilled it. Citing Rav Asi: the Messiah will not come until all souls in the Divine treasury are born into the world. Even children who died placed souls there — the mitzva's cosmic purpose was served.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
He has NOT fulfilled it. The mitzva's purpose is perpetuating humanity. If children died leaving no descendants, that continuity was broken. 'Be fruitful and multiply' demands living legacy.
Baraita
Grandchildren are like children — implying if children died WITHOUT leaving grandchildren, the father has NOT fulfilled the mitzva. This challenges Rav Huna's position.
Rav Huna (reply)
The baraita speaks of grandchildren fulfilling the mitzva FOR their grandfather — a separate principle. It does not contradict that souls were placed in the world by children who died without issue.
Rav Sheshet's Surprising Excuse Rav Sheshet cited the ruling that two grandchildren from one child don't suffice — but the Gemara reveals he was merely putting people off. The real reason: he became impotent from the strain of Rav Huna's lengthy discourses.

📚 Rabbi Akiva's Students — Tragedy and Renewal 12,000 pairs of Rabbi Akiva's students all died between Passover and Shavuot because they did not treat each other with proper respect (kavod).

The world was left desolate of Torah — until Rabbi Akiva traveled south and taught five new disciples:
Rabbi Meir · Rabbi Yehuda · Rabbi Yosei · Rabbi Shimon · Rabbi Elazar ben Shamua

These five upheld the Torah and it was not forgotten from Israel.

Rav Mattana rules: halakha follows Rabbi Yehoshua — a man should continue having children even after fulfilling the minimum of פרו ורבו, as stated: 'In the morning sow your seed, and in the evening do not withhold your hand' (Ecclesiastes 11:6).
