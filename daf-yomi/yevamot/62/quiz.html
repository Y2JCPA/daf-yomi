Yevamot 62 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Beit Hillel in the first baraita, what is the minimum to fulfill פרו ורבו?
A. Two sons and two daughters
B. A son and a daughter
C. Either a son or a daughter
D. Two sons
Correct: B — The first baraita records Beit Hillel as requiring one son and one daughter (mirroring Creation: 'male and female He created them'). The second baraita has Beit Hillel requiring either a son or a daughter.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
On what basis does Rav Huna rule that a man whose children died has still fulfilled פרו ורבו?
A. The Talmud equates the act of birth with fulfillment regardless of outcome
B. Grandchildren automatically substitute for deceased children
C. Rav Asi taught the Messiah waits for all stored souls to be born — even deceased children placed souls in the world
D. The mitzva is fulfilled at the moment of conception, not survival
Correct: C — Rav Huna draws on Rav Asi's statement that the Messiah will not come until the treasury of souls is emptied. Children who died still sent souls into the world, satisfying the mitzva's cosmic function.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Reish Lakish's position on a man who had children as a gentile and then converted?
A. He has fulfilled פרו ורבו since gentiles have family lineage
B. He has NOT fulfilled it — a convert is legally like a newborn and prior children are nullified
C. He has fulfilled it only if his children also converted
D. It depends on whether his children were born before or after age of majority
Correct: B — Reish Lakish holds consistently that a convert is like a newborn child — all pre-conversion legal statuses, relationships, and children have no halakhic standing after conversion.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Akiva's 12,000 pairs of students all die between Passover and Shavuot?
A. They were martyred during Roman persecutions
B. They failed to study the oral Torah diligently
C. They did not treat each other with proper respect
D. They violated the laws of ritual purity
Correct: C — The Gemara states explicitly they died because they did not conduct themselves with respect (kavod) toward one another — a failure of interpersonal ethics, not ritual law.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which amora teaches that a man without a wife is left without 'peace,' derived from Job 5:24?
A. Rabbi Tanḥum bar Rabbi Ḥanilai
B. Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi
C. Rava bar Ulla
D. Rav Yosef
Correct: C — Rava bar Ulla derives from 'you shall know that your tent is in peace' (Job 5:24) that domestic peace requires a wife. Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi uses the same verse for a different teaching about marital duty.
