Yevamot 122 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
How did the Sages determine that a disembodied voice on a mountaintop was human and not a demon?
A. The voice recited a prayer only humans would know
B. The speaker had a shadow of a shadow
C. The voice identified itself by name and lineage
D. They used a special incantation to test for demons
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes, based on Yonatan the demon expert, that demons have a shadow but not a 'shadow of a shadow' (tzel d'tzel). This was the definitive test used to confirm the voice was human.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why was the gentile innkeeper's testimony about a man's death accepted despite being questioned directly?
A. Gentile women are always believed about matters of death
B. She cried spontaneously before being questioned, establishing her account as offhand
C. She produced physical evidence — the staff, bag, and grave
D. Rabbi Tarfon cross-examined her and found her consistent
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that because she cried before anyone asked her anything, her initial spontaneous reaction established the entire account as 'mesiach lefi tumo' (speaking offhandedly), which is the basis for accepting gentile testimony.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the basis of the dispute between Rabbi Akiva and Rabbi Tarfon regarding examination of witnesses about a woman's husband's death?
A. Whether gentile testimony is ever acceptable
B. Whether the case is analogous to monetary law or capital law
C. Whether one witness suffices or two are needed
D. Whether the testimony must be given in a court or can be given anywhere
Correct: B — Rabbi Akiva compares it to monetary law (because of the ketubah payment) and doesn't require formal examination. Rabbi Tarfon compares it to capital law (because if the husband is alive, remarriage constitutes adultery) and requires full inquiry and interrogation.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How did Rabbi Tarfon test the witness who told the story of 'The Lion of Shiḥayya'?
A. He asked a second witness to corroborate the account
B. He deliberately altered details when repeating the story to see if the witness would correct him
C. He required the witness to take an oath
D. He sent investigators to the village of Shiḥayya to verify
Correct: B — Rabbi Tarfon subtly changed details — such as saying 'who is called Lion' instead of 'the lion from Shiḥayya' — to test whether the witness would passively agree or insist on accuracy. The witness corrected him each time, demonstrating consistent and reliable testimony.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara dismiss the concern that a 'rival wife' (tzara) might have faked the disembodied voice to trick the wife into remarrying?
A. Rival wives are always assumed to be trustworthy
B. The principle from the school of Rabbi Yishmael: in times of danger, we do not raise excessive suspicions
C. The voice was verified by three witnesses
D. The court investigated and confirmed no rival wife existed
Correct: B — The school of Rabbi Yishmael taught that during a period of danger — or analogous uncertain circumstances — we do not multiply suspicions. Just as a get may be written in dangerous times without full verification, here too the court accepted the voice without requiring proof it wasn't a rival wife.
