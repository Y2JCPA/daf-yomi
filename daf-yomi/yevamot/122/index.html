Yevamot 122 — Final Testimonies & Closing the Tractate

OVERVIEW The final daf of Yevamot addresses extraordinary forms of testimony that permit a woman to remarry: disembodied voices, candlelight identifications, gentile offhand remarks, and cross-examined travelers. The Gemara probes each for reliability — distinguishing demons from humans, rival wives from honest reporters, and casual speech from deliberate testimony. The tractate concludes with the debate between Rabbi Akiva and Rabbi Tarfon on whether witness examination is required, and closes with Rabbi Elazar's teaching that Torah scholars increase peace in the world.

↓

↓
⚔️ Sixty people walked toward the siege of Beitar — a gentile reported all died — wives permitted

📖 Key principle: Gentile testimony accepted when stated offhandedly (mesiach lefi tumo)

May a Woman Remarry Based on a Disembodied Voice?

Beit Shammai
The court may not allow a woman to remarry based on a disembodied voice — actual testimony is required.
Beit Hillel
The court may allow a woman to remarry based on a disembodied voice (bat kol).
Rabba bar Shmuel
Why teach this? The Mishna already rules like Beit Hillel!
Gemara
To clarify: any anonymous source that forbids relying on a voice is simply Beit Shammai and is not the accepted ruling.
RULING The halakha follows Beit Hillel: a disembodied voice proclaiming a man's death is sufficient to permit his wife to remarry.

Challenge
Demons also appear like people
Answer

But demons have shadows too!
Resolution
He had a shadow of a shadow (tzel d'tzel)
Source
Yonatan the demon expert confirmed: demons lack a shadow of a shadow

The Gentile Innkeeper — Why Was She Less Trustworthy?

ElementDetail
IdentityShe was a gentile innkeeper
Why credible?She was speaking offhandedly (mesiach lefi tumo)
What she said"This is his staff, his bag, and the grave where I buried him"
ObjectionThey asked her directly — isn't that deliberate, not offhand?
ResolutionShe cried first before being asked — the crying began the account spontaneously
PrincipleInitial spontaneous reaction establishes offhand character of entire statement
RAV KAHANA The innkeeper's credibility rested on her spontaneous crying before anyone questioned her — this qualified her entire account as offhand testimony.

Rabbi Akiva vs Rabbi Tarfon: Examining Witnesses

Rabbi Akiva

  • No inquiry and interrogation (drishah v'chakirah) needed
  • Testimony about a woman's status is like monetary law
  • Reason: There is a ketubah payment at stake
  • Rigorous procedures would prevent women from ever remarrying

Rabbi Tarfon

  • Full inquiry and interrogation required
  • Testimony about a woman's status is like capital law
  • Reason: If husband is alive, remarriage = adultery (capital offense)
  • Cross-examined 'The Lion' witness 2-3 times for consistency
R' ḤANINA'S PRINCIPLE By Torah law, both monetary and capital cases require inquiry and interrogation (Leviticus 24:22 — 'one law'). The Sages relaxed this for monetary law so as not to 'lock the door before borrowers.'
