QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye, why does Rabban Gamliel rule that a get is ineffective after a get?
A. He is uncertain whether a get actually precludes levirate marriage
B. The Sages enacted that each get applies only once to a given yevama, not once per brother
C. A second get requires additional witnesses that were not present
D. Two consecutive gets cancel each other out, leaving no legal trace
Correct: B — Abaye explicitly rejects the 'uncertainty' reading. Rabban Gamliel holds with certainty that a get precludes levirate marriage. The ruling is structural: the Sages enacted that each act applies once per yevama. The Rabbis disagree, allowing each brother to independently exercise levirate acts.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rabban Gamliel concedes that a get IS effective after a maamar. What does Abaye say this proves about Rabban Gamliel's position?
A. Rabban Gamliel is only uncertain about maamar, not about get
B. Rabban Gamliel holds both get and maamar are fully effective — the limit is acts per yevama, not doubt about their power
C. Rabban Gamliel holds that maamar is stronger than get and therefore overwrites it
D. Rabban Gamliel only requires ḥalitza when a get follows a maamar
Correct: B — This is the heart of Abaye's objection and answer. The fact that get works after maamar proves Rabban Gamliel is not uncertain — both acts are real. His restriction is a structural rule (once per yevama), not epistemic doubt.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rabbi Yoḥanan lists five authorities who hold maamar fully acquires the yevama. Which authority's source is the mishna about three brothers, two of whom were married to two sisters?
A. Rabbi Shimon
B. Ben Azzai
C. Beit Shammai
D. Rabbi Neḥemya
Correct: C — Beit Shammai's opinion that maamar is a full acquisition is derived from the mishna about three brothers — two married to two sisters, one single. Beit Shammai's ruling in that case logically requires maamar to function as a full betrothal.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is ben Azzai's unique asymmetric position on maamar after maamar?
A. Maamar works for all cases but only when performed in the correct order
B. Maamar is effective after maamar for two yevamin / one yevama, but NOT for one yavam / two yevamot
C. Maamar is only effective when the yevama has consented explicitly
D. Maamar after maamar is always effective regardless of the number of parties
Correct: B — Ben Azzai holds an asymmetric rule: when two yevamin compete over one yevama, the second yavam's maamar CAN take effect after the first's. But when one yavam has two yevamot, his second maamar does NOT work after the first. This reflects different structural dynamics in each scenario.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The Gemara debates whether zikah (the levirate bond) is substantial. What does Rabba bar Rav Huna ultimately clarify about this dispute?
A. Rabban Gamliel holds zikah is substantial; the Rabbis hold it is not
B. Both Rabban Gamliel and the Rabbis hold zikah IS substantial; they disagree only on ḥalitza procedures
C. Both Rabban Gamliel and the Rabbis hold zikah is NOT substantial; his earlier statement reflected only the minority opinion
D. The question of zikah's substantiality is left unresolved in the Gemara
Correct: C — Rabba bar Rav Huna clarifies that both Rabban Gamliel and the Rabbis hold zikah is NOT substantial (אין זיקה). His own earlier statement that seemed to imply zikah is substantial was merely presenting the position of those who hold it IS substantial — not his own or the mainstream view.
