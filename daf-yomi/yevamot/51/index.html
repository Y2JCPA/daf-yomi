⚖️ Rabban Gamliel vs. the Rabbis: Sequential Acts in Yibbum

Rabban Gamliel
A get is not effective after a get, and a maamar is not effective after a maamar. Each act applies only once to a given yevama — not once per brother.
The Rabbis
A get after a get IS effective! Every brother holds independent levirate rights over each yevama, so each brother's act takes full legal effect.
Abaye
Rabban Gamliel is NOT uncertain — he knows a get precludes and a maamar acquires. The dispute is structural: acts apply once per yevama (Rabban Gamliel) vs. once per brother (Rabbis).
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Rabban Gamliel, Beit Shammai, Rabbi Shimon, ben Azzai, and Rabbi Neḥemya all agree: maamar (מאמר) acquires the yevama as a full-fledged acquisition, like regular betrothal.
Core Question When one yavam performs sequential acts (get or maamar) with multiple yevamot — or multiple yevamin with one yevama — which acts are legally effective, and what obligations remain?

📋 Get After Get — Two Yevamot, One Yavam

Two yevamot fall before one yavam (שתי יבמות לפני יבם אחד)
↓
He gives a get (גט) to Yevama A
↓
He gives a get to Yevama B
↓
Rabban Gamliel
Ḥalitza with first only; forbidden to her relatives; permitted to marry relatives of Yevama B (second get = no effect)
The Rabbis
Forbidden to relatives of both; must perform ḥalitza with one of them (each get fully effective)

🔗 Abaye's Resolution: Why Does the Third Act Work?

The Problem
If Rabban Gamliel is uncertain about both get and maamar, why does he concede that a get IS effective after a maamar? The uncertainty should block all third acts!
Abaye's Answer
Rabban Gamliel holds with certainty: (1) a get definitively precludes levirate marriage, (2) a maamar definitively acquires. The restriction is Sages' enactment — each act applies once per yevama, not once per brother.
The Rabbis
The Sages enacted that each brother has independent acquisition and nullification rights over each yevama. Therefore every brother's get and every brother's maamar take full effect.
Abaye (continued)
Intercourse after a disqualifying act (invalid biah) creates a stronger obligation for ḥalitza than a mere maamar — this too is consistent with Rabban Gamliel's position.
Abaye's Principle The debate is not epistemic (uncertainty) but structural: how many times may levirate acts apply — once per yevama, or once per yavam-brother?

⚖️ Is Zikah (זיקה) — the Levirate Bond — Substantial?

Zikah IS Substantial (יש זיקה)

  • The levirate bond creates a real halakhic link requiring formal dissolution
  • Shmuel's ruling: ḥalitza is biblically required to sever the bond
  • Rabba bar Rav Huna's statement aligns with this view
  • Implies: two yevamot both need ḥalitza — bond is substantial for each
  • The Rabbis appear to hold this view (inferred from requiring ḥalitza with both)

Zikah Is NOT Substantial (אין זיקה)

  • The levirate bond is a weak, non-binding connection
  • Rabban Gamliel's ruling implies zikah is not substantial
  • ḥalitza is still required — as a rabbinic safeguard, not biblical law
  • Rabba bar Rav Huna clarifies: BOTH Rabban Gamliel AND Rabbis hold zikah is not substantial
  • His own prior statement was merely presenting the minority opinion

💍 Maamar After Maamar: Step-by-Step Ruling

Setup: Two yevamot fall before one yavam — both have levirate obligations to him
↓
Step 1: Yavam performs maamar (מאמר — levirate betrothal) with Yevama A, giving her money or document of betrothal
↓
Step 2: Yavam then performs maamar with Yevama B
↓
Rabban Gamliel rules: Second maamar has NO effect — give a get to Yevama A (acquired by first maamar), perform ḥalitza with her; forbidden to her relatives; permitted to marry relatives of Yevama B
↓
The Rabbis rule: Both maamarim are effective — give a get to both; must perform ḥalitza with one of them; forbidden to relatives of both
↓
Key principle: Subsequent invalid intercourse (ביאה פסולה) creates a stronger ḥalitza obligation than maamar alone — this remains consistent across opinions
