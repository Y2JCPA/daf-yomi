5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why was Amasa believed when he reported the tradition permitting Moabite women, even though it served his own interests?
A. Because he threatened violence and intimidated the court
B. Because Shmuel and his court were still alive to verify the tradition
C. Because he was a recognized Nasi of the Sanhedrin
D. Because Doeg admitted the tradition was correct
Correct: B — Normally a scholar who reports a self-serving tradition after it becomes relevant is not believed. But Amasa was believed because Shmuel's court was still alive, so the tradition could be independently verified.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the dispute between R' Yehuda and R' Shimon regarding the exemption of Ammonite/Moabite women?
A. Whether the exemption applies at all
B. Whether it applies to Moabite women or only Ammonite women
C. The SOURCE — R' Yehuda derives from masculine grammar, R' Shimon from the reason of the prohibition
D. Whether the exemption extends to their children as well
Correct: C — Both agree women are exempt. R' Yehuda derives it from the masculine form (עַמּוֹנִי/מוֹאָבִי), while R' Shimon derives it from the reason — 'they did not meet you' refers to men's behavior, not women's.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava, what are the 'two bands' (מוֹסֵרַי) that David says Hashem loosened?
A. The prohibition against Moabites and the prohibition against mamzerim
B. Ruth the Moabite and Na'ama the Ammonite
C. The sin of Judah and Tamar and the sin of Lot's daughters
D. David's poverty and his persecution by Saul
Correct: B — Rava teaches that the two 'bands' threatening David's lineage were Ruth the Moabite (his ancestor) and Na'ama the Ammonite (mother of his grandson Rehoboam). Both were permitted because the prohibition applies only to males.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does R' Yochanan derive from the phrase 'מֵעַמָּיו' (from his own people) regarding the Kohen Gadol's marriage?
A. A Kohen Gadol may only marry a woman from the tribe of Levi
B. A Kohen Gadol may marry a woman whose parents are from two different peoples, including an Ammonite convert's daughter
C. A Kohen Gadol may not marry any convert
D. A Kohen Gadol may marry only an established female convert
Correct: B — R' Yochanan expands the verse to include a virgin 'from two peoples' — e.g., the daughter of an Ammonite convert who married a Jewish woman — as fit even for a Kohen Gadol.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did R' Yochanan tell R' Zakkai to 'go teach his baraita outside'?
A. Because the baraita contained heretical content
B. Because R' Zakkai was not authorized to teach in the study hall
C. Because the baraita's teachings were either obvious or didn't reflect the accepted, broader halakha
D. Because the baraita contradicted an explicit verse in the Torah
Correct: C — R' Yochanan found R' Zakkai's baraita either stating the obvious (Ammonite woman is fit, her son is unfit) or too narrow — R' Yochanan's own derivation from 'from his own people' was broader and already covered the case, making the baraita unnecessary for the study hall curriculum.
