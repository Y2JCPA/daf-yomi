📜 The Ten-Year Rule: Sources and Qualifications

Biblical allusion: "And Sarai took Hagar…after Abram had dwelt ten years in Canaan" (Gen 16:3) — this verse supplies the basis for the ten-year waiting period before taking another wife
↓
Baraita ruling: After ten childless years, he must divorce her and pay the ketubah — perhaps he did not merit to be built through her. He may also simply take a second wife
↓
Rav's qualification: The ten years apply specifically to earlier generations who lived longer lives. For people of the current era, a proportionally shorter period may suffice
↓
Rabba's challenge: This qualification is not accepted as practical halakha — already in King David's era years were short, yet the mishna was composed much later by Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi without amendment
↓
Isaac's counterexample rejected: Isaac married at 40 and fathered children at 60 — a 20-year wait. But the Gemara explains that verse is needed for another teaching (twin birth), not as a precedent for waiting longer

🙏 Why Were the Forefathers Infertile?

Rabbi Yitzḥak
God desired the prayers of the righteous — so He made our forefathers and mothers infertile so that they would pray earnestly for children
Rabbi Yitzḥak
The prayers of the righteous are like a pitchfork: just as a pitchfork overturns grain from place to place, prayer can overturn a divine decree
Rav Naḥman
citing Rabba bar Avuh
Sarah was initially an aylonit — a sexually underdeveloped woman with no womb. The superfluous phrase 'she had no child' (Gen 11:30) beyond the word 'barren' indicates she lacked a womb entirely
Rabbi Yitzḥak
Isaac was himself infertile: 'Isaac entreated the Lord lenokhaḥ his wife' — the word lenokhaḥ (facing) implies they stood opposite each other, both barren, and he prayed for them both
Rava
to Rav Naḥman
We should derive from Isaac that one may wait longer than ten years — he married at 40 and had children at 60, a full twenty years of waiting without children!
Resolution The verse about Isaac's age at marriage is needed for Rabbi Ḥiyya bar Abba's teaching that Jacob and Esau were born simultaneously — proof relevant to levirate marriage laws. It cannot serve as a precedent for extending the ten-year rule.

⚖️ How Many Occurrences Establish a Ḥazaka?

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi

  • Ḥazaka established after two occurrences
  • Lethal wife: after 2 husbands die, she may not marry a 3rd
  • Circumcision: after 2 sons die, the 3rd is exempt
  • Holds that a pattern is proven with just two data points

Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel

  • Ḥazaka requires three occurrences
  • Lethal wife: only after 3 husbands die may she not marry a 4th
  • Circumcision: after 3 sons die, the 4th is exempt
  • More stringent threshold before overriding a mitzva obligation

Final Ruling (Rav Yosef)

  • Halakha follows Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi for marriage (lethal wife) — 2 deaths
  • Halakha follows Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi for lashings cases
  • Halakha follows Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel for circumcision — 3 deaths
  • Each opinion governs its own distinct domain

💍 Abaye's Dangerous Marriage: A Cautionary Tale

חומא בת איסי בר יצחק בר יהודה
The Story of Abaye and Ḥuma Rav Yitzḥak bar Yosef came from Eretz Yisrael and reported that Rabbi Yoḥanan ruled a ḥazaka is established after two circumcision deaths. Abaye relied on this report and married Ḥuma — a woman whose first husband, Raḥava of Pumbedita, had died, and whose second husband, Rav Yitzḥak son of Rabba bar bar Ḥana, had also died.

Rava's sharp rebuke: "Is there anyone who performs an action like this and endangers himself?" — especially since Abaye himself had previously declared that Rav Yitzḥak bar Yosef (called 'Yitzḥak the Red') is not reliable! He relied on an unreliable transmitter to risk his life.

The Gemara records that Rava himself subsequently died — making Ḥuma a widow three times. When she came before Rav Pappa seeking permission to remarry, he refused.
