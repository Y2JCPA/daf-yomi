Yevamot 64 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the mishna on Daf 64, how many years of childless marriage must pass before a man is obligated to marry another woman or divorce?
A. Five years
B. Seven years
C. Ten years
D. Twenty years
Correct: C — The mishna states explicitly: 'If a man married a woman and stayed with her for ten years and she did not give birth, he is no longer permitted to neglect the mitzva to be fruitful and multiply.' The number ten is derived from the verse about Sarai taking Hagar 'after Abram had dwelt ten years in Canaan' (Gen 16:3).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yitzḥak, why did God cause the matriarchs and patriarchs to be infertile?
A. To punish them for transgressions in their youth
B. Because He desired the prayers of the righteous
C. To demonstrate that children are a purely miraculous gift
D. So that they would adopt children and spread Torah
Correct: B — Rabbi Yitzḥak explicitly states: 'Why were our forefathers initially infertile? Because the Holy One, Blessed be He, desires the prayers of the righteous.' Their infertility was a divine strategy to draw out their heartfelt prayers.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does the Gemara mean when Rav Naḥman (citing Rabba bar Avuh) describes Sarah as an aylonit?
A. She was post-menopausal when she married Abraham
B. She was a sexually underdeveloped woman who lacked a womb
C. She had suffered damage to her reproductive organs from illness
D. She had vowed celibacy before meeting Abraham
Correct: B — The Gemara derives from the superfluous phrase 'she had no child' (Gen 11:30) — beyond the simple word 'barren' — that Sarah lacked a womb entirely, the defining characteristic of an aylonit. Her fertility was a miracle that gave her new anatomy, as hinted by the verse 'look to the rock from where you were hewn' (Isaiah 51:1-2).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, after how many of a woman's husbands die does a ḥazaka of danger arise, preventing her from remarrying?
A. After one husband dies
B. After two husbands die
C. After three husbands die
D. After four husbands die
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi holds that ḥazaka is established after two occurrences. If two husbands have died, she may not marry a third. This contrasts with Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel, who requires three occurrences before establishing the presumption of danger.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Rava criticize Abaye for marrying Ḥuma, whose previous two husbands had died?
A. Because Abaye had not consulted the Sanhedrin before marrying her
B. Because Abaye himself had previously said the report's transmitter was unreliable
C. Because Ḥuma had not received a proper get from her second husband
D. Because Abaye already had a living wife and could not take a second
Correct: B — Rava pointed out a glaring contradiction: Abaye had himself declared that 'Avin is reliable but Yitzḥak the Red (Rav Yitzḥak bar Yosef) is not reliable' — yet Abaye based his life-risking decision to marry a potentially lethal wife on a report transmitted by the very person he had deemed unreliable.
