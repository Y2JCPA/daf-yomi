⚖️ Reish Lakish vs. Rabbi Yoḥanan: Three Major Disputes

Rabbi Yoḥanan
Ḥalitza with a pregnant woman IS effective — the yevama remains bound until ḥalitza or yibbum regardless of the pregnancy.
Reish Lakish
Ḥalitza with a pregnant woman is NOT fully effective — if she miscarries afterward, she still requires another ḥalitza or yibbum.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
A father's usufruct rights over property ARE tantamount to ownership — so a son cannot validly sell what was written to him, and a purchaser acquires nothing.
Reish Lakish
Usufruct rights are NOT tantamount to ownership — the son held full title and his sale stands; the purchaser acquires the property.
Rava's Ruling Rava declares: The halakha is in accordance with Reish Lakish in all three of these disputes.

🤰 Yavam Consummates Yibbum with Pregnant Yevama

Yavam performs yibbum with his yevama while she is pregnant
↓
Offspring is NOT viable (stillbirth / miscarriage)
The yibbum is retroactively valid — she is his lawful wife. No penalty applies.
Offspring IS viable
He acquired a prohibited marriage. Rabbi Eliezer: he must send her out (divorce) as a penalty, even though the violation was only rabbinic.
↓
Key rule: Anyone eligible for yibbum is eligible for ḥalitza — and vice versa. A valid pregnancy may suspend both obligations.

📜 Rava's Three Rulings — Following Reish Lakish

Dispute 1 — Ḥalitza with a Pregnant Woman: Ḥalitza performed while the yevama is pregnant is not fully effective. If she miscarries afterward, she requires another ḥalitza or yibbum. A supporting baraita confirms this view.
↓
Dispute 2 — Verbal Division of Property: A father who divides property verbally and labels one portion as 'an inheritance' nullifies the entire division (contradicts halakha). Reish Lakish: stating 'as a gift' for one recipient is sufficient to validate the division for all. Rabbi Yoḥanan: must state it for each.
↓
Dispute 3 — Son Sells Father's Property: Father writes all possessions to son while retaining usufruct; son sells them, then dies before father. Reish Lakish: purchaser does acquire (usufruct ≠ title). Rabbi Yoḥanan: purchaser acquires nothing (usufruct = full ownership).
↓
Rava's Declaration: הלכה כריש לקיש — The halakha follows Reish Lakish in all three of these disputes.

💰 Does Usufruct Equal Ownership? — שיעבוד פירות

Rabbi Yoḥanan
Yes — שיעבוד פירות כשיעבוד הגוף. Rights to produce are tantamount to ownership of the item itself. Father retained usufruct → father retained title. The son had no full title to sell. Purchaser acquires nothing.
Reish Lakish
No — שיעבוד פירות לאו כשיעבוד הגוף. Rights to produce are NOT tantamount to ownership. The son held full title despite the father's usufruct. His sale is legally valid and the purchaser acquires the property.
Abaye (challenges Rava)
You say Rabbi Meir and Rabbi Eliezer expressed the same view — but perhaps Rabbi Eliezer only rules strictly in the yibbum case (due to its specific rabbinic prohibition), not as a general principle.
Rava
Despite Abaye's challenge, Rava maintains: halakha follows Reish Lakish. Usufruct is not equivalent to title.
Practical Outcome When the son sold the property and both son and father subsequently died, the purchaser retains the property — in accordance with Reish Lakish and Rava's ruling.

⚔️ Same Principle? Rabbi Meir vs. Rabbi Eliezer

Rabbi Meir

  • Case: One who marries a woman pregnant with another man's child, or nursing another's child
  • Prohibition type: Rabbinic
  • Ruling: Must divorce her immediately; may not remarry her
  • Penalty: Applies even though the prohibition is only rabbinic in origin
  • Source: A baraita teaching the laws of nursing and pregnant women

Rabbi Eliezer

  • Case: Yavam who performs yibbum with a pregnant yevama and the offspring turns out to be viable
  • Prohibition type: Rabbinic (premature yibbum before viability confirmed)
  • Ruling: Must send her out (divorce) even though she was his legitimate yevama
  • Penalty: Applies even for a rabbinic-level violation
  • Source: The Mishna on this daf
