QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, why does Rabbi Meir validate an amputee's wooden prosthetic but invalidate a cloth anpileya for ḥalitza?
A. The prosthetic is covered in leather, the anpileya is not
B. The prosthetic protects the foot, the anpileya does not
C. The prosthetic is worn daily, the anpileya is not
D. The prosthetic is mentioned in the Torah, the anpileya is not
Correct: B — Rava explains that R' Meir doesn't require leather but does require protective function. The wooden prosthetic protects the foot; the cloth anpileya without a hard sole does not.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Rav Yosef tell Abaye to give the yavam his sandal as a gift rather than just lending it?
A. A borrowed shoe is completely invalid for ḥalitza
B. Transferring ownership makes it 'his shoe' — fulfilling the verse l'khatchila
C. Only new shoes may be used for ḥalitza
D. The yavam must purchase the shoe himself
Correct: B — Using another's shoe is valid only bedi'avad. By giving it as a gift and transferring ownership, the shoe becomes 'his' — satisfying the Torah's requirement of 'his shoe' in the ideal manner (l'khatchila).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the key difference between Rav Pappi and Rav Pappa regarding a sandal with confirmed leprosy?
A. Rav Pappi permits it l'khatchila; Rav Pappa forbids it entirely
B. Rav Pappi says it's invalid even bedi'avad; Rav Pappa says it's valid bedi'avad
C. Both agree it's completely invalid
D. Both agree it's valid bedi'avad
Correct: B — Rav Pappi holds a confirmed leprous sandal is invalid even bedi'avad because it must be burned and is considered already destroyed. Rav Pappa holds it's still valid bedi'avad since it physically still exists.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Ameimar, why can a clubfooted person (who walks on the backs of his feet) not perform ḥalitza?
A. His feet are ritually impure
B. He cannot press his foot to the ground properly
C. The Torah excludes disabled people from ḥalitza
D. His shoe doesn't count as a shoe since it's worn upside down
Correct: B — Ameimar requires the yavam to press his foot to the ground during ḥalitza. A clubfooted person whose foot is twisted cannot do this, making ḥalitza impossible for him.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the contradiction between the mishna (prosthetic valid for ḥalitza) and the baraita (prosthetic excludes one from pilgrimage)?
A. The mishna is R' Meir and the baraita is R' Yosei
B. Ḥalitza says 'from ON his foot' — even what's on top of the foot counts; pilgrimage requires the actual foot itself
C. The prosthetic in the mishna has leather; the one in the baraita does not
D. Ḥalitza is rabbinic while pilgrimage is biblical
Correct: B — The verse for ḥalitza uses 'me'al raglo' — 'from ON his foot' — meaning even something placed on top of where the foot was counts. Pilgrimage requires 'regel' — the actual foot — which a prosthetic is not.
