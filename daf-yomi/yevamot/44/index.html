⚖️ Mamzer Status: Rabbi Akiva vs. The Rabbis

Rabbi Akiva
One who remarries his divorcée, marries his ḥalutza, or a relative of his ḥalutza — betrothal is not effective and the offspring is a mamzer in all these cases.
The Rabbis
Betrothal is effective and the offspring is not a mamzer in those cases. We agree only that marrying a relative of one's divorcée — a karet prohibition — produces a mamzer.
Rav Yosef / R. Shimon bar Rabbi
All agree (including Shimon HaTimni): offspring of a karet-level forbidden relation are disqualified from the priesthood — derived by kal va-ḥomer from a widow married to a Kohen Gadol.
Gemara
The kal va-ḥomer can be refuted: a widow causes her own priestly disqualification whereas a woman in a karet union does not. The a fortiori inference remains difficult (קשיא).
Bottom Line The final ruling on offspring disqualification from the priesthood rests on independent sources. The kal va-ḥomer from the widow's case is structurally challenged but the halakhic conclusion — disqualification for karet-level unions — is accepted.

👨‍👩‍👧 Can a Yavam Marry All His Yevamot?

Eldest surviving brother wishes to perform yibum with all of the deceased brothers' widows
↓
❌ Mishna's Surface Reading
All four brothers died — but then who performs yibum? The Gemara rejects a literal reading that all brothers perished.
✅ Corrected Reading
One brother survives. He has the legal right to marry all yevamot; the verse does not limit levirate marriage to a single wife.
↓
Practical condition: He may marry all of them only if he is able to provide for all four women. If not, the court offers עצה טובה (appropriate advice) to dissuade unsuitable matches — e.g., an old man taking a young woman.

🔄 Ḥalitza: Fit vs. Unfit for the Priesthood

יחלוץ לפסולה — שלא לפסול את הכשרה לכהונה
Halakhic Principle If two yevamot are available — one fit (כשרה) for the priesthood and one unfit (פסולה) — the yavam should perform ḥalitza with the unfit woman. Reason: Ḥalitza disqualifies a woman from marrying a kohen. Performing it with the fit woman would needlessly strip her of priestly eligibility. The unfit woman has no such privilege to lose. Rav Yosef confirmed this ruling applies even in contexts where the mishna seems to discuss ḥalitza as obligatory.

📜 Offspring of Remarrying a Divorcée and Ḥalutza

Rabbi Akiva
One who remarries his divorcée, marries his ḥalutza, or a relative of his ḥalutza — betrothal is ineffective (אין קידושין תופסין) and offspring is a mamzer.
The Rabbis
Offspring is not a mamzer in any of those cases. Only one who marries a relative of his divorcée — liable to karet — produces a mamzer. The others are lesser prohibitions.
Reish Lakish
Did Rabbi Akiva truly hold that a rabbinic prohibition (relative of ḥalutza) yields a mamzer? Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi may have reassigned that view to Rabbi Shimon HaTimni in the mishna.
Gemara (conclusion)
The mishna need not be emended. Rabbi Akiva's position is coherent: he holds that rabbinic decrees can carry Torah-level consequences when the Sages intend full stringency.
Key Verse "היא תועבה" — She is an abomination (Deut. 24:4). The emphasis on 'she' teaches that only she bears that status — her children are not rendered mamzerim by this verse alone.

⚖️ Categories of Forbidden Unions & Offspring Status

Karet Prohibition

  • Example: marrying relative of one's divorcée
  • Rabbis: offspring IS a mamzer
  • Rabbi Akiva: also a mamzer
  • Shimon HaTimni: agrees offspring disqualified from priesthood
  • Kal va-ḥomer from widow married to Kohen Gadol

Lav Prohibition

  • Example: remarrying one's divorcée after she remarried
  • Rabbis: offspring NOT a mamzer
  • Rabbi Akiva: offspring IS a mamzer (unique stringency)
  • Shimon HaTimni: agrees offspring has flawed lineage (פסולה לכהונה)
  • Verse: 'she is an abomination' — only she, not her children

Rabbinic Prohibition

  • Example: marrying relative of one's ḥalutza
  • Rabbis: offspring NOT a mamzer
  • Rabbi Akiva: debated — Gemara questions if he truly held mamzer here
  • Reish Lakish: Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi may have reassigned this view
  • Betrothal: Rabbi Akiva says ineffective; Rabbis say effective

Slave/Gentile with Jewish Woman

  • Rabbi Yoḥanan: all agree offspring follows the mother
  • 'All agree' includes even Shimon HaTimni
  • Child is Jewish — not categorized as a mamzer
  • Severity of the prohibition supports the ruling
  • Offspring has full Jewish status via the mother

🧬 A Fortiori Inference: From Widow to Karet-Union Offspring

Base case (ḥomer): A widow married to a Kohen Gadol — the prohibition applies only to the Kohen Gadol, not to ordinary kohanim. Yet her offspring are ḥalalim — disqualified from the priesthood.
↓
Proposed inference (kal): A woman in a karet-level forbidden relation — where the prohibition applies to all men equally — should a fortiori produce offspring disqualified from the priesthood.
↓
First objection: The widow's union itself disqualifies her personally from future priestly marriage — a unique feature not shared by women in karet unions. The inference may be structurally flawed.
↓
Resolution (Rav Yosef / R. Shimon bar Rabbi): The inference holds for karet-level relations; the severity of the prohibition more than compensates for the structural difference in how the woman herself is affected.
↓
Final conclusion: The objection regarding the widow's self-disqualification remains קשיא — difficult, unresolved. The halakhic ruling on offspring disqualification is affirmed through independent textual sources.
