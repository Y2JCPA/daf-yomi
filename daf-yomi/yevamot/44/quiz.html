QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why should ḥalitza be performed with the woman who is unfit for the priesthood rather than the one who is fit?
A. The unfit woman has halakhic priority as the elder yevama
B. Performing ḥalitza with the fit woman would needlessly disqualify her from marrying a kohen
C. The fit woman may still perform yibum afterward regardless
D. The Torah explicitly commands ḥalitza with unfit women first
Correct: B — Ḥalitza disqualifies a woman from marrying into the priesthood. Performing it with the fit woman causes unnecessary harm to her priestly eligibility. The unfit woman has no such privilege to lose, so she bears the ḥalitza without additional consequence.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What source does Mar Zutra bar Toviya cite to explain why ḥalitza may be performed with only one woman, not both?
A. The repeated phrase 'his yevama, his yevama' amplifying the mitzva
B. The verse calling the man 'the house of him who had his sandal removed' — implying one woman performs the rite
C. A baraita prohibiting partial fulfillment of levirate obligations
D. The verse 'If the man does not wish to take his yevama' implying a single yevama
Correct: B — Mar Zutra bar Toviya derives from the title 'the house of him who had his sandal removed' — the singular form indicates that only one woman performs ḥalitza with a given yavam.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Gemara's final resolution, who is included in the phrase 'all agree' in Rav Yosef's statement about offspring of forbidden unions?
A. All Tannaim and Amoraim without exception
B. Shimon HaTimni — who otherwise holds that lav-level offspring are not mamzerim but agrees here about karet
C. Only Rabbi Akiva and his disciples
D. Only those Tannaim who hold betrothal is ineffective for such unions
Correct: B — The 'all agree' is specifically inclusive of Shimon HaTimni. Although he generally does not render offspring of non-karet unions mamzerim, even he agrees that offspring of karet-level unions are disqualified from the priesthood.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the Gemara's conclusion regarding the a fortiori (kal va-ḥomer) inference from the widow married to a Kohen Gadol?
A. The inference is fully accepted and proves priesthood disqualification for all karet unions
B. The inference is completely refuted because a widow's case is entirely different
C. The inference remains difficult (קשיא) due to the widow's unique self-disqualification feature
D. The inference is accepted only for the specific case of remarrying one's divorcée
Correct: C — The Gemara explicitly states the objection remains 'קשיא' — difficult and unresolved. A widow married to a Kohen Gadol is herself subsequently disqualified from all priestly marriage, a feature not shared by women in karet unions, weakening the inference.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, what is the status of a child born from intercourse between a slave or gentile and a Jewish woman?
A. The child is a mamzer — the most severe forbidden union
B. The child follows the father's status (slave or gentile)
C. The child follows the mother's status and is Jewish
D. The child's status is disputed and the Gemara leaves it unresolved
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan states that all agree — including Shimon HaTimni — that when a slave or gentile has intercourse with a Jewish woman, the offspring follows the mother. The child is Jewish, not a mamzer in the technical sense.
