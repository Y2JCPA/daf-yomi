Yevamot 96 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Hamnuna, what happens to a widow awaiting her yavam who engaged in licentious relations?
A. She is permitted to her yavam
B. She is forbidden to her yavam
C. She must perform ḥalitza
D. She is exempt from yibum entirely
Correct: B — Rav Hamnuna rules that a shomeret yavam who had licentious sexual relations is forbidden to her yavam. This is one possible framework for the Rav-Shmuel dispute.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the mishna about false death reports, why is the husband permitted to the 1st, 3rd, and 5th wives?
A. Because they were married first
B. Because they are not sisters of each other or of his current wife
C. Because the death reports about them were true
D. Because they performed ḥalitza
Correct: B — The odd-numbered wives are permitted because none of them are sisters of the original wife. The 2nd and 4th are forbidden as sisters of his existing wife.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Shimon say a second nine-year-old's intercourse does NOT disqualify the first?
A. Because a minor cannot perform any legal act
B. Because only an adult's intercourse counts
C. Because if the first act counts, the second is superfluous; if it doesn't, neither does the second
D. Because the Torah exempts minors from all obligations
Correct: C — Rabbi Shimon presents a logical dilemma: either the first minor's act is a full act of intercourse (making the second meaningless) or it's not (in which case neither counts). The Rabbis resolve this by classifying it as ma'amar, where one can take effect after another.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the practical difference between Rav and Shmuel regarding a nine-year-old's intercourse equaling adult ma'amar?
A. Whether the boy can perform ḥalitza
B. Whether the rival wife's levirate obligations are affected
C. Whether the boy is considered an adult
D. Whether the yevama can remarry freely
Correct: B — If the minor's intercourse equals adult ma'amar (Shmuel/R' Yoḥanan), the rival wife may be exempt from yibum as the rival wife of a woman with bonds from two yevamin. Rav holds the equivalence is not complete in all regards.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rava, the rule that a yevama who had relations with a nine-year-old performs ḥalitza but not yibum applies:
A. Only when there is a rival wife
B. Only when the boy matured and married another woman
C. Even when there is no rival wife
D. Only according to Rabbi Shimon
Correct: C — Rava clarifies that this restriction is not merely a rabbinic decree due to the rival wife scenario — it applies even when there is no rival wife at all, as the mishna's first case demonstrates with a single yevama.
