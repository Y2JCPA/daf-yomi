Daf 96 — The Minor's Yibum & False Death Reports

Rav vs. Shmuel
Is a shomeret yavam like a married woman?
Rav
She is like a married woman — disqualified by illicit relations; betrothal by others invalid
Shmuel
She is not like a married woman — not disqualified; betrothal takes effect
↓
Mishna: False reports of wife's death → sequential marriages to sisters
↓
Mishna: Nine-year-old boy's intercourse with yevama — status and disqualification rules
Core Theme This daf explores the legal weight of a minor's sexual act in levirate marriage and the consequences of marrying sisters based on erroneous death reports.

Three Interpretations of the Rav-Shmuel Dispute

Rav Huna
Rav and Shmuel disagree about whether a shomeret yavam who was violated (ne'enesa) is disqualified from her yavam.
Gemara
Perhaps they disagree about Rav Hamnuna's ruling: a widow awaiting her yavam who had licentious relations is forbidden to her yavam.
Rav
She is like a married woman — licentious relations disqualify her.
Shmuel
She is not like a married woman — she is not disqualified by such relations.
Gemara
Or perhaps they disagree about whether betrothal (kiddushin) takes effect with a yevama from another man.
Gemara
But they already disputed this elsewhere! Answer: one ruling was stated by inference from the other.
Key Point The Gemara offers three possible frameworks for the Rav-Shmuel dispute, each centering on whether a shomeret yavam has the status of a married woman.

Pattern Odd-numbered wives (1st, 3rd, 5th) are permitted because they are not sisters of the original wife. Even-numbered wives are forbidden as sisters of an existing wife.

Nine-Year-Old Boy vs. Adult Yavam — Disqualification

Boy (9 yrs + 1 day)

  • Intercourse is valid as a ma'amar (levirate betrothal)
  • Disqualifies brothers only if he acts first
  • Brothers disqualify him whether they act first or last
  • Does not fully acquire the yevama

Adult Yavam

  • Intercourse fully acquires the yevama
  • Disqualifies brothers whether first or last
  • Full-fledged levirate marriage
  • Intercourse considered a complete kinyan
Rav Zevid bar Rav Oshaya A nine-year-old who has relations after his brother's levirate betrothal still disqualifies — but if the boy performed ma'amar (not intercourse) last, he does not disqualify.

Rabbi Shimon vs. the Rabbis — Two Minors

Rabbis
If a second nine-year-old has relations with the yevama after the first, he disqualifies her to the first boy.
R' Shimon
He does not disqualify. If the first act counts as intercourse, the second is meaningless. If the first doesn't count, neither does the second!
Rabbis
A minor's intercourse is like ma'amar — and one ma'amar can take effect after another ma'amar.
Ben Azzai
There is ma'amar after ma'amar with two yevamin / one yevama, but not with two yevamot / one yavam.
Gemara
Our mishna (which applies the rule to two yevamot) is not in accordance with Ben Azzai.
R' Shimon's Logic Rabbi Shimon presents a logical dilemma: the minor's intercourse either counts fully (making the second irrelevant) or doesn't count at all. The Rabbis find a middle ground — it's like ma'amar.
