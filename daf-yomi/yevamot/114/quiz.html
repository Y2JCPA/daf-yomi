5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Pedat's approach, why is it permitted to let children search for lost keys on Shabbat?
A. Because children are exempt from Shabbat entirely
B. Because the court is not commanded to prevent a minor from violating prohibitions on his own
C. Because carrying keys is only a rabbinic prohibition
D. Because the keys belong to the children
Correct: B — R' Pedat holds that קטן אוכל נבלות אין ב"ד מצווין להפרישו — the court is not obligated to stop a minor who independently violates a prohibition. The children carry the keys of their own accord.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Abaye reject the proof from a child detaching plants and throwing objects on Shabbat?
A. He says the baraita refers to a weekday, not Shabbat
B. He says detaching means from an unperforated flowerpot and throwing means in a karmelit — both rabbinic
C. He says the child was acting with his father's consent
D. He says the baraita is not authoritative
Correct: B — Abaye explains that both activities involve only rabbinic prohibitions — an unperforated flowerpot (עציץ שאינו נקוב) and a karmelit — so no proof can be drawn about Torah-level obligations.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does R' Yochanan say the ruling about a child extinguishing fire on Shabbat does NOT prove the court must always stop minors?
A. Because extinguishing is only a rabbinic prohibition
B. Because it refers to a child acting with his father's consent (da'at aviv)
C. Because fire poses a danger to life
D. Because the child is old enough to be obligated in mitzvot
Correct: B — R' Yochanan limits the obligation: one must stop a child only when he acts with da'at aviv (his father's consent/wishes). An independently acting child would be different.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the proof from a child suckling from a non-kosher animal?
A. Because non-kosher milk is only rabbinically prohibited
B. Because the child is too young to have intent
C. Because it is permitted due to danger — the child needs to eat
D. Because suckling is not considered 'eating' by Torah law
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that the leniency is due to the child's physical need (danger), not because there is no obligation to prevent minors from eating forbidden food in general.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In Rava's case of two deaf-mute spouses where no Torah prohibition applies to either, why must the deaf-mute husband still divorce his wife?
A. Because the Torah requires it for all levirate situations
B. Because a deaf-mute's marriage has no validity at all
C. Because of a rabbinic decree to prevent people from mistakenly thinking the yevama may marry someone else
D. Because R' Yochanan requires the court to stop deaf-mutes from sinning
Correct: C — Rav Shemaya explains it is a rabbinic decree: people might see the deaf-mute sister and think she is truly his wife's sister, concluding the yevama is free to marry others — when in fact she remains bound.
