Must the Court Prevent a Minor from Sinning?

R' Pedat
A minor who eats forbidden food — the court is not commanded to prevent him. We can let children walk where keys were lost on Shabbat without concern.
Gemara
But a baraita says if a Jewish child comes to extinguish a fire on Shabbat, we must say 'Do not extinguish!'
R' Yochanan
That case is different — it refers to a child acting with his father's consent (da'at aviv). Only then must the court intervene.
Gemara
R' Yochanan seems uncertain — he refutes proofs in both directions, treating each case on its own merits.
Core Dispute The Gemara brings multiple proofs attempting to resolve whether קטן אוכל נבלות בית דין מצווין להפרישו — whether the court must separate a minor from forbidden food. No definitive resolution is reached.

R' Pedat's Ruling: Children & Shabbat Keys

Keys lost in an area where carrying on Shabbat is forbidden
↓
Can adults carry them back?
No
Carrying in public domain is a Torah prohibition for adults
Solution
Let boys and girls walk there — if they find the keys, they'll bring them on their own
Implication R' Pedat holds: קטן אוכל נבלות — אין ב"ד מצווין להפרישו — the court need not prevent a minor from violating prohibitions on his own initiative.

Proof from Shabbat: Detaching Plants & Throwing

Gemara
A baraita says: One may not tell a child to carry on Shabbat, but one may allow a child to detach plants and throw objects in the public domain.
Gemara
This supports R' Pedat — you needn't stop a child who acts on his own!
Abaye
No proof. 'Detaching plants' means from an unperforated flowerpot (rabbinic only). 'Throwing' means in a karmelit (also rabbinic).
Abaye
Perhaps for a Torah prohibition, one IS obligated to stop the child. This proof only addresses rabbinic matters.
Key Distinction Unperforated flowerpot (עציץ שאינו נקוב) — detaching from it is only rabbinically prohibited. A karmelit (כרמלית) is an intermediate domain, also only rabbinic.

Gentile vs. Child: Extinguishing Fire on Shabbat

Gentile Extinguishes

  • Don't say 'Extinguish'
  • Don't say 'Don't extinguish'
  • His rest is NOT your responsibility
  • A gentile acts on his own wishes — he's an independent agent

Jewish Child Extinguishes

  • You MUST say 'Don't extinguish!'
  • His rest IS your responsibility
  • R' Yochanan limits this: only when child acts with father's consent (דעת אביו)
  • If acting independently — no obligation to stop him
R' Yochanan's Principle The obligation to stop a minor applies only when the child acts on his father's behalf — even without explicit instruction, if the child knows his father's wishes.

Why All Three Cases Were Needed (Amud B)

🦎 Creeping animals (שרצים) — prohibited by any amount (whole creature)
↓
🩸 Blood (דם) — entails karet, but only prohibited at a quarter-log
↓
⚱️ Ritual impurity (טומאה) — applies to priests only, not all Jews
↓
❓ Each case has a unique stringency AND leniency
↓
✅ Therefore all three must be stated — you cannot derive one from the others
Talmudic Logic The Gemara shows that each prohibition has a unique feature — you might have thought one case is more or less severe, so every case must be explicitly taught.
