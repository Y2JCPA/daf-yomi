Yevamot 88 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the 'uncertain meat' analogy for accepting one witness about a husband's death?
A. Because meat is a Torah prohibition while marriage is rabbinic
B. Because a married woman has an established presumption of prohibition (chezkat issur)
C. Because the witness might be lying about the meat too
D. Because meat requires two witnesses as well
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes: with uncertain meat, no prohibition was established, so one witness can tip the scale. But a married woman has a definite chezkat issur — she was certainly married — which normally requires two witnesses to override.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is R' Zeira's explanation for why one witness is accepted regarding a husband's death?
A. The Torah explicitly permits one witness in this case
B. The severe consequences if the testimony is wrong justify the initial leniency
C. One witness is always sufficient for matters of personal status
D. The woman's own testimony supplements the single witness
Correct: B — R' Zeira's principle: because the Sages imposed such harsh consequences at the end (if the husband returns), they could afford to be lenient at the beginning by accepting one witness. The severity deters false testimony.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Menaḥem son of R' Yosei, when must a woman leave her second husband after contradicting witnesses appear?
A. Always, regardless of timing
B. Only if the contradicting witnesses came AFTER she married
C. Only if the contradicting witnesses came BEFORE she married
D. Never — she may always remain
Correct: C — R' Menaḥem b. R' Yosei distinguishes by timing: if contradicting witnesses came before the marriage and she married anyway, she must leave. But if she married first and then contradicting witnesses arrived, she need not leave.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve Rava's challenge from the baraita about a kohen forced to divorce a woman of uncertain status?
A. The baraita is rejected as non-authoritative
B. Kohanim are subject to stricter rules — uncertainties are treated as definite prohibitions
C. The baraita refers only to a zonah, not a divorcee
D. Rav concedes his ruling is wrong
Correct: B — One answer given is that kehunah (priesthood) is different: uncertainties regarding priestly disqualifications are treated stringently, as though they were definite. This is a special rule for kohanim that doesn't apply to ordinary Israelites.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, what does Rav mean when he says the woman 'need not leave'?
A. She need not leave her second husband permanently
B. She need not leave her home
C. She need not leave her permitted status to return to her first husband
D. She need not leave the city where she remarried
Correct: C — Rav Ashi reinterprets: 'need not leave' means she retains her permitted status vis-à-vis her first husband. Since she remarried based on valid testimony, it is considered duress (ones), and like a woman violated against her will, she may return to her first husband.
