⚖️ The Core Dispute: Ḥalitza With a Pregnant Yevama

Rabbi Yoḥanan
Ḥalitza performed with a pregnant woman is valid — if she miscarries, it is retroactively revealed she needed ḥalitza. The verse 'he has no child' means it will be revealed he had none.
Reish Lakish
Ḥalitza performed with a pregnant woman is invalid — we do not apply retroactive validation to levirate acts. The bond must be established at the time of the act.
Stakes If valid: she is free to marry anyone immediately. If invalid: she needs ḥalitza again from the remaining brothers — and is disqualified from the priesthood.

⏳ The Three-Month Waiting Period: Who Must Wait?

Must Wait Three Months

  • Israelite adult woman (widow or divorcée)
  • Any woman whose marital status changed
  • Israelite female minor — by rabbinic decree (though she cannot conceive)
  • Decree extends to minor due to rule for adult

Exempt from Waiting

  • Adult female convert — guards herself before conversion
  • Freed maidservant (adult) — acts to prevent pregnancy
  • Shmuel: these two are categorically exempt
  • Mishna confirms: female minors returned to husbands immediately

🔄 Why Don't Converts and Maidservants Need to Wait?

Questioner
Since promiscuous intercourse was common for them as gentiles and slaves, we should decree that converts and freed maidservants must wait three months!
Abaye
A convert guards herself before conversion — she wishes to distinguish children born in sanctity from those conceived as a gentile. A maidservant turns over after intercourse to prevent absorption of semen. Neither is likely to be pregnant.
Conclusion No decree is warranted: converts proactively self-protect before conversion; maidservants take physical measures to prevent conception. Both exemptions stand on firm logical grounds.

📜 New Mishna: Ḥalitza and Yibbum With a Pregnant Yevama

חָלַץ לָהּ וְאַחַר כָּךְ הִפִּילָה — וְאִם אֵינוֹ בֶּן קַיָּמָא
משנה — יבמות לה: Case A (Ḥalitza): Ḥalitza was performed; she later miscarried — the ḥalitza was necessary. Both are forbidden to each other's relatives; she is disqualified from the priesthood.

Case B (Yibbum, non-viable child): Yavam had intercourse with pregnant yevama; child born was not viable — a levirate bond existed all along; the yibbum is valid and he may keep her.

Case C (Uncertain paternity): She gave birth 7 months after yibbum — uncertain if child is 9-month offspring of first husband (no bond) or 7-month offspring of yavam (bond existed).

⚔️ The Dispute: What Happens After Ḥalitza With a Pregnant Yevama?

Yavam performs ḥalitza with yevama who is pregnant
↓
She later miscarries — levirate bond existed all along
↓
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Ḥalitza is valid. Retroactively revealed she needed it. No further ḥalitza required. Disqualification from priesthood = rabbinic stringency only.
Reish Lakish
Ḥalitza is invalid. Bond was unknown at time of act. She needs ḥalitza again from brothers. Disqualification from priesthood = Torah-level ruling.
