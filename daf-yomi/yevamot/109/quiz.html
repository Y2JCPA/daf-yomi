QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why was Eifa's explanation for Rabbi Elazar's ruling rejected?
A. Because it contradicted a verse in the Torah
B. Because if she were truly a forbidden relative, she wouldn't need ḥalitza — but a baraita says R' Elazar requires it
C. Because Eifa was not considered a reliable authority
D. Because the Sages had already ruled on this matter differently
Correct: B — Eifa argued she was forbidden as the brother's ex-wife. But if that were so, she'd be a full ervah and exempt from even ḥalitza. Since a baraita teaches R' Elazar still requires ḥalitza, Eifa's reasoning doesn't hold.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Abaye, what is Rabbi Elazar uncertain about?
A. Whether the woman was truly divorced
B. Whether death or the original marriage establishes levirate candidacy
C. Whether a minor's marriage is valid by Torah law
D. Whether ḥalitza can substitute for yibum in all cases
Correct: B — Abaye says R' Elazar's doubt is whether levirate obligation is established at the brother's death (she'd be a valid candidate) or from the original marriage (she was once forbidden as an ex-wife). Due to this safek, she does ḥalitza but not yibum.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What concern does Rava raise about allowing levirate marriage in this case?
A. The woman might refuse the yavam
B. The yavam might not perform the ceremony correctly
C. People know about divorces but not always about remarriages — they'll think the yavam married his brother's ex-wife
D. The ketuba amount would be incorrect
Correct: C — Rava's concern is marit ayin (appearances). People are well-informed about divorces but not remarriages. If the brother remarried her in the evening and died in the morning, onlookers would think the yavam improperly took his brother's divorced wife.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What makes the case of an 'orphan in her father's lifetime' unique regarding levirate marriage?
A. Her father must give permission for the levirate marriage
B. She can never perform ḥalitza
C. Her divorce was Torah-level but her remarriage was only rabbinic, since she's still a minor without her father's authority
D. She is automatically exempt from all levirate obligations
Correct: C — Once a minor girl is divorced from a marriage her father arranged, her father can no longer marry her off. Any subsequent marriage while she's still a minor is only rabbinic. So the divorce 'outweighs' the remarriage, and everyone agrees she's forbidden to the yavam.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yosei, what happens to someone who says 'I have nothing other than Torah'?
A. He receives double reward for his dedication
B. He is exempt from other mitzvot
C. He does not even have Torah — because study and practice are inseparable
D. He must also take on additional stringencies
Correct: C — Rabbi Yosei derives from 'that you may learn them and perform them' (Deut. 5:1) that Torah study and mitzva observance are intertwined. One who studies without intending to practice doesn't even receive credit for the study itself.
