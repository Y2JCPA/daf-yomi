⚖️ The Great Offspring Dispute

Rav Dimi's tradition
Rav Yitzḥak bar Avudimi said in the name of Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi: the offspring of a gentile/slave + Jewish woman is a mamzer
Ravin's tradition
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi rules the offspring is kasher — lineage is fully unflawed. Rav, Shmuel, and Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi agree.
Rav Yosef
I rely on Rav Dimi. This is Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's opinion — the offspring is a mamzer.
Abaye
Rely instead on Ravin! Rav, Shmuel, Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi — all rule the offspring fit. Why list great men? Because the halakha follows them!
✅ Final Halakha The offspring of a gentile or slave who engaged in intercourse with a Jewish woman has unflawed lineage — whether the mother was unmarried or married.

📜 Two Conflicting Reports of Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's View

Rav Dimi's Report (strict)

  • Rav Yitzḥak bar Avudimi citing Rabbeinu
  • Offspring of gentile/slave + Jewish woman = mamzer
  • Logic: just as a gentile/slave cannot betroth a Jewish woman, violation of that boundary creates a mamzer
  • Rav Yosef adopts this position

Ravin's Report (lenient)

  • Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi rules offspring is kasher
  • Supported by: Rav, Shmuel, Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi in Eretz Yisrael
  • Logic: mamzer status requires betrothal that is potentially valid — gentile/slave betrothal is void entirely
  • Abaye champions this tradition

🐪 Rav and the Man Who Asked for His Daughter

Questioner
I am the child of a gentile who had relations with a Jewish woman. What is my halakhic status?
Rav
Your lineage is unflawed. You are fully fit to marry into Israel.
Questioner
If so — give me your daughter in marriage!
Rav
I will not give her to you.
Shimi bar Ḥiyya (Rav's grandson)
Like the proverb — 'a camel in Medes dances on a kav of space.' You rule one way but act another?
Rav
Even were he as great as Joshua son of Nun I would not give him my daughter. My ruling stands — my refusal is for other reasons.
⚡ Outcome The man refused to leave Rav's presence and kept pressing him. Rav placed his eyes upon him — and he died.

🔗 Offspring of Half-Slave Half-Freeman

Half-slave half-freeman engages in intercourse with a Jewish woman
↓
With an unmarried woman
Offspring is kasher — slave portion creates no mamzer status in a permitted union
With a married woman (adultery)
Offspring is a mamzer — the adultery itself generates mamzer status, regardless of father's origins
↓
Rabba's ruling: Now that we hold the offspring of a full slave is kasher, all the more so the offspring of a half-slave is kasher

🛁 The Slave Who Preempted His Master — Rav Asi's Ruling

Setup: A Jew purchases a gentile slave from a gentile owner
↓
Complication: Before the master could immerse the slave for slavery (which preserves slave status), the slave preemptively immersed for conversion
↓
Effect: Immersion for conversion renders him a freeman — the master loses his slave entirely
↓
Rav Asi's principle: A woman of uncertain conversion who lived as part of the Jewish community and immersed for niddah like all Jewish women — we treat her as a valid convert
↓
Applied ruling: Her daughter (born from a gentile/slave) has unflawed lineage and is fully fit to marry into Israel
