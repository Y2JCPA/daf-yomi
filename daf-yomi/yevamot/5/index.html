⚖️ Does a Positive Mitzva Override a Prohibition?

Gemara
We need to prove: an aseh (positive mitzva) overrides a lo ta'aseh (prohibition). Where is this derived in the Torah?
First Answer
From the leper's shaving! The leper must shave 'his head' on Day 7 — overriding the prohibition of rounding the corners of one's head.
Objection
Rounding corners (pe'ah) doesn't apply to women — it's not a universal prohibition. A non-universal case may be uniquely weak and can't teach a general rule.
Try Again
Then derive it from 'his beard' — the leper's shaving overrides the prohibition against shaving the beard corners.
Rejected Again
Beard prohibition also doesn't apply to women! And 'his beard' is needed to teach a novelty about priests — it's not superfluous.
Gemara (Amud B)
Actually, derive it from tzitzit: the Torah juxtaposes the sha'atnez prohibition with 'make twisted fringes' — permitting wool-linen in ritual fringes.
Core Principle עֲשֵׂה דּוֹחֶה לֹא תַּעֲשֶׂה — A positive commandment overrides a negative prohibition

🪒 Attempts to Derive Aseh Docheh from Leper's Shaving

Leper must shave on Day 7 (Lev. 14:9) — an unambiguous positive mitzva that forces shaving
↓
Attempt 1: 'His Head'
Overrides prohibition of rounding corners (pe'at ha-rosh)
❌ Rejected
Pe'ah doesn't apply to women — prohibition is not universal; may be inherently weaker
↓
Attempt 2: 'His Beard'
Overrides prohibition of shaving the beard corners
❌ Rejected
Beard prohibition also excludes women; 'his beard' is also needed to teach that priests' shaving requirement applies even to beard area
↓
Attempt 3: Leper overrides both the nazirite positive mitzva (grow hair) and nazirite prohibition (don't cut hair)
↓
❌ Rejected: A nazirite can dissolve his vow through a Sage — the nazirite prohibition is not absolute; we cannot derive from a dissolvable case

🧵 The Sha'atnez–Tzitzit Derivation (Amud B)

R' Yishmael's School
All Torah garments are wool or linen — so 'twisted fringes' must be wool-linen tzitzit, which overrides sha'atnez. QED: aseh overrides lo ta'aseh.
Rabbis
We don't accept that all garments are wool/linen. How do we derive the principle?
Gemara
From the verse itself: 'Do not wear sha'atnez… make yourself twisted fringes' — the juxtaposition permits wool-linen tzitzit.
Objection
'Twisted fringes' is not superfluous — it teaches the required number of threads for tzitzit, as it is taught: 'twisted' = entwined, totaling four doubled-over threads.
Response
Then let the verse just say 'sha'atnez.' Why add 'wool and linen together'? That phrase teaches: stitching two stitches of one kind with one of the other constitutes forbidden sha'atnez.
Further Objection
And 'sha'atnez' itself is needed! It is an acronym: שׁוּעַ (combed), טָווּי (spun), נוּז (woven) — defining exactly when sha'atnez applies.
Acronym שַׁעַטְנֵז = שׁוּעַ טָווּי וְנוּז — smooth-combed, spun, and woven

✂️ Aseh vs. Regular Prohibition vs. Karet Prohibition

Regular Lo Ta'aseh

  • ✅ Aseh overrides — established principle
  • Source: Tzitzit overrides sha'atnez
  • Source: Leper's shaving overrides beard prohibition
  • No karet punishment attached
  • General rule applies broadly

Lo Ta'aseh + Karet

  • ❓ Does aseh override karet prohibition?
  • Circumcision on Shabbat — pre-Sinai commandment
  • Paschal lamb on Shabbat — communal sacrifice
  • Daily offering on Shabbat — communal + pre-Sinai
  • ❌ Every single case has a unique feature
  • ❌ Combined cases also all fail

Yibbum & Parental Honor

  • 'With her' (עִמָּהּ) — yibbum applies only where permitted
  • Does NOT override wife's-sister prohibition
  • Co-wife of a forbidden relative is exempt
  • Father commands: 'Cook on Shabbat'
  • Son must refuse — God's Shabbat (karet) takes precedence
  • Lev. 19:3: 'Fear your parents AND keep My Shabbatot'

🔗 Combining Cases to Derive Override of Karet — and Why Each Fails

Circumcision alone: 8th-day brit milah overrides Shabbat (karet). Rejected: commanded to Avraham pre-Sinai — uniquely stringent or lenient; can't generalize.
↓
Paschal lamb alone: Korban Pesach slaughter overrides Shabbat (karet). Rejected: Communal sacrifice may carry its own override — not applicable to individual mitzvot.
↓
Daily offering alone: Tamid overrides Shabbat. Rejected: Also communal; also pre-Sinai. Two unique features — can't derive general rule.
↓
Combine Paschal lamb + circumcision: One communal, one individual — common feature is only 'overrides Shabbat.' But circumcision is pre-Sinai; Pesach is communal. Not a clean derivation.
↓
Combine circumcision + daily offering: Both were known before Sinai. The pre-Sinai factor is shared — perhaps only pre-Sinai commandments override karet. ❌ Rejected.
↓
Combine Paschal lamb + daily offering: Both are communal sacrifices. Perhaps only communal mitzvot override karet. ❌ Rejected. Conclusion: The principle cannot be established from any combination.
