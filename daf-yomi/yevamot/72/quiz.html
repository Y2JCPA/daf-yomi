QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, what conditions make circumcision and bloodletting dangerous and therefore prohibited?
A. On Shabbat and Yom Tov, when medical care is restricted
B. On cloudy days and when a south wind (shuta) is blowing
C. During the summer months when temperatures are highest
D. When the north wind is blowing strongly from the north
Correct: B — Rav Pappa derives from the wilderness experience that cloudy days and south winds (shuta) create dangerous conditions for circumcision and bloodletting — exactly the conditions that prevented circumcision for 40 years when the north wind did not blow.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the Gemara's final conclusion about re-circumcising a priest whose foreskin was drawn forward?
A. It is required by Torah law, derived from 'he shall surely be circumcised'
B. It is a rabbinic ordinance, enacted so that Torah observance not appear degraded
C. It is never required — he remains fully circumcised at all levels
D. It is required only if he drew the foreskin forward manually, not if it moved naturally
Correct: B — The Gemara resolves the apparent contradiction by clarifying that the baraita requiring re-circumcision speaks only at the rabbinic level. At the Torah level, Rav Huna holds that such a person remains circumcised and may eat teruma. The verse cited is merely an asmakhta supporting the rabbinic enactment.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Abaye explain the baraita's statement that a tumtum priest has 'nashav' (wives)?
A. Nashav refers to the tumtum's mother, who eats teruma through her son's household connection
B. The tumtum's male organ is hidden, but his testicles are externally visible, so his betrothal is valid
C. Nashav refers to female slaves who were acquired before the tumtum's status was in doubt
D. The baraita is speaking hypothetically about what would happen if he were found to be male
Correct: B — Abaye explains that this tumtum has externally visible testicles, making his male identity evident. Therefore his betrothal is upheld as a stringency — she cannot leave without a get — and he genuinely has 'wives' (nashav).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rabbi Elazar's ruling regarding an uncircumcised priest who sprinkles the red heifer purification waters?
A. His sprinkling is invalid — he is treated like a ritually impure person
B. His sprinkling is valid — he is comparable to a tevul yom who immersed that day
C. His sprinkling is valid only if countersigned by a fully circumcised priest
D. He is completely disqualified from all red heifer rites, including sprinkling and sanctifying
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar rules that an uncircumcised priest's sprinkling is valid, comparing him to a tevul yom (one who immersed that day). Both have partial disqualifications but remain fit for red heifer rites. The school of Rabbi Akiva disagrees, equating arel with tamei.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did the Jewish people not circumcise themselves during the 40 years in the wilderness, according to both explanations offered in the Gemara?
A. The obligation of circumcision only applied upon entering the Land of Israel
B. Moses instructed them to delay until the conquest was complete
C. Either because they were censured after the sin of the spies (undeserving of the north wind), or because the north wind simply did not blow, making post-surgical recovery medically dangerous
D. They lacked sharp flint implements required for the procedure in the wilderness
Correct: C — The Gemara offers two parallel explanations: (1) divine censure following the sin of the spies left them undeserving of the salutary north wind; (2) the north wind simply failed to blow during those years, making hot-weather recovery from circumcision medically dangerous. Both explanations agree that without the north wind, circumcision posed serious risk.
