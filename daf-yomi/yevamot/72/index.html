🌬️ Why the Wilderness Generation Went Uncircumcised

Problem: The entire 40-year wilderness generation was not circumcised despite the Torah obligation
↓
Reason 1 — Divine censure: After the sin of the spies, the people were under punishment and undeserving of the salutary north wind needed for safe recovery
↓
Reason 2 — Medical danger: The north wind simply did not blow those years; hot weather without it made post-circumcision complications likely
↓
Baraita confirms: Every night in the wilderness the north wind blew at midnight — enabling circumcision the next day had they not been censured
↓
Rav Pappa's halakhic derivation: Therefore, do not circumcise or let blood on a cloudy day or when a south wind (שוטא) blows
↓
Practical caveat: Nowadays many ignore these warnings without harm — the danger has lessened or constitutions are stronger

📜 Is Re-Circumcision After Foreskin Drawn Forward Torah or Rabbinic?

Rav Huna
By Torah law, if a priest's residual foreskin was drawn forward — whether by itself or manually — covering the corona, he may still partake of teruma. He is considered circumcised.
Baraita Objection
One whose residual foreskin was drawn forward so that it covers the corona requires a second circumcision — implying he is NOT considered circumcised!
Gemara Resolution
The baraita speaks only at the rabbinic level — the Sages enacted re-circumcision so that the words of Torah should not appear degraded (מעשה דרבנן).
Rabbi Yehuda
He should not be re-circumcised at all — it would be dangerous for him to undergo the procedure again.
Sages to R. Yehuda
Many have undergone re-circumcision and survived. The danger is not established as a certainty.
Amora's Error
He mistakenly thought: since the tanna cites a verse ('he shall surely be circumcised'), this must be a Torah law — but the verse serves merely as an asmakhta for the rabbinic ruling.
✅ Conclusion The obligation to re-circumcise one whose foreskin was drawn forward is rabbinic. At Torah level, Rav Huna holds he remains fully circumcised and may eat teruma.

🔍 Who Are the Tumtum's 'Nashav'? — Abaye vs. Rava

Abaye

  • The tumtum's male organ is hidden, but his testicles are externally visible
  • Since his maleness is evident, his betrothal (kiddushin) is valid
  • Betrothal upheld as a stringency — she cannot leave without a get
  • 'Nashav' = his actual wives (נשיו)
  • Support: The latter clause must repeat tumtum with a new case — one whose testicles are also hidden

Rava

  • 'Nashav' (נשיו) does not mean wives — it means 'his women'
  • Refers to the priest-father's mother, who after her husband dies continues eating teruma by virtue of her minor tumtum son
  • The son, as household member, keeps her entitled to priestly food
  • Support: The latter clause refers to a tumtum who was split open and found to be male — an entirely different case

💧 Uncircumcised Priest and Red Heifer Purification Waters

An uncircumcised (arel) priest sprinkles the purification waters of the red heifer (parah adumah)
↓
Rabbi Elazar & Rabbis
Valid sprinkling
Uncircumcised ≈ tevul yom (one who immersed that day); both permitted for red heifer rites
Tanna of R. Akiva's School
Invalid sprinkling
Uncircumcised = ritually impure (tamei); disqualified from sanctifying the waters
↓
Rav Yosef: The R. Akiva school treats uncircumcised like impure — deriving from a gezeirah shavah (shared verbal analogy)
↓
Rava's challenge: If arel = tamei, find a tanna who permits an impure person to sanctify! No such tanna exists — the equation is asymmetric
↓
Resolution: R. Akiva applies the equation only as a stringency for arel — not to import all leniencies of impurity status onto him
