QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Ravina, which type of tumah do gentile corpses convey?
A. Full tumah identical to Jewish corpses, including ohel
B. Only ohel (tent) tumah, not contact tumah
C. Tumah by contact and carrying, but NOT through ohel
D. No tumah whatsoever — they are entirely excluded
Correct: C — Ravina explains that Numbers 19:14 ('when a man [adam] dies in a tent') excludes gentiles specifically from ohel tumah, but they still convey tumah through direct contact or carrying. This resolves the verses in Numbers 31 that seem to include them.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the halakha if a priest betroths a widow and is subsequently appointed High Priest?
A. He must divorce her — High Priests can never marry widows under any circumstances
B. He may complete the marriage, since betrothal preceded his appointment
C. The betrothal is automatically void upon his appointment
D. He must perform chalitza and cannot marry her
Correct: B — The Gemara derives from 'shall he take for a wife' (Leviticus 21:14) that since valid kiddushin was established before his appointment as High Priest, he may proceed with the marriage. The case of Yehoshua ben Gamla and Marta bat Baitos illustrates this.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara rule that a High Priest may not perform yibum with his childless brother's widow?
A. A High Priest is entirely exempt from the yibum obligation
B. She is classified as a zona after his brother's death
C. A rabbinic decree forbids the first act because she would then become his widow, whom he cannot marry
D. The Torah explicitly forbids a High Priest from any levirate marriage
Correct: C — By Torah law the first act of yibum would technically be permitted. However, the Rabbis prohibited it because after that first intercourse she acquires the status of his wife — and when that union ends she becomes a widow, whom a High Priest cannot marry. The first act is thus rabbinically forbidden due to the prohibited second act.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Beit Hillel rule on fulfilling the mitzva of peru u'revu (be fruitful and multiply)?
A. Two males, following the precedent of Moses's sons
B. Two females, following the creation narrative
C. One male and one female, derived from 'male and female He created them' (Genesis 5:2)
D. Any three children regardless of gender
Correct: C — Beit Hillel derives from Genesis 5:2 — 'male and female He created them' — that one fulfills peru u'revu with one male and one female child, mirroring the original creation. Beit Shammai requires two males, deriving from Moses who had two sons.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Regarding the aylonit (sexually underdeveloped woman), what does Rabbi Yehuda rule for a priest who already has a wife and children?
A. He may marry her, since the peru u'revu obligation is already fulfilled
B. He may not marry her — she is classified as a zona forbidden to priests
C. He may marry her only with rabbinic approval
D. He may marry her since the Torah prohibition applies only to the first wife
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda rules that even if a priest already has a wife and children (fulfilling peru u'revu), he still may not marry an aylonit — because any intercourse that cannot lead to children constitutes harlotry (zona), and the Torah forbids priests from marrying a zona (Leviticus 21:7). The Rabbis disagree and permit it once peru u'revu is fulfilled.
