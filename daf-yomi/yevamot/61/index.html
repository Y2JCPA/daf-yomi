⚰️ Do Gentile Graves Convey Ohel Tumah?

R. Shimon
Gentile graves do NOT convey tumah through a tent. Ezekiel 34:31 says 'you My sheep…are men [adam]' — only Jews are called adam, so Numbers 19:14 ('when a man [adam] dies in a tent') excludes gentiles.
Objector
Numbers 31:40 says 'nefesh adam' about Midianite captives — gentiles ARE called adam! And Numbers 31:19 says 'whoever has killed anyone, purify yourselves' — implying gentile corpses do convey tumah!
R. Shimon
The Jonah verse (4:11) mentioning 120,000 'adam' in Nineveh also poses a problem. These verses require explanation.
Ravina
The real distinction is simpler: 'When a man [adam] dies in a tent' (Numbers 19:14) excludes gentiles from OHEL tumah specifically — but they still convey tumah by direct contact or carrying. No contradiction!
✅ Resolution Gentile corpses convey tumah by contact and carrying, but NOT through ohel (tent-based) transmission — Ravina

💍 High Priest's Pre-Appointment Betrothal

Rule: If a priest betroths a widow, then is appointed High Priest — he may complete the marriage
↓
Source: 'Shall he take for a wife' (Leviticus 21:14) — inclusive phrasing covers widows whose kiddushin preceded appointment
↓
Case of Yehoshua ben Gamla: Betrothed Marta bat Baitos (a widow), then the king appointed him High Priest — the marriage was permitted
↓
⚠️ Rav Yosef: 'I see a conspiracy here' — Marta reportedly paid a tarkav of gold dinars to secure his appointment; he was appointed but not truly worthy
↓
Legal principle: Once betrothal (kiddushin) is validly established, a subsequent elevation in status does not void the permitted marriage

🚫 High Priest Whose Brother Died Childless

High Priest's brother dies leaving no children — yibum obligation arises
↓
By Torah law: yibum would be technically permitted — the prohibition on widows has not yet attached
↓
The Problem
After the first act of intercourse, she becomes his widow — and a High Priest cannot marry a widow!
The Logic
Rabbis prohibited the first act because it inevitably leads to the prohibited second act
↓
Final ruling: He performs chalitza only — yibum is forbidden by rabbinic decree

🌱 Peru U'Revu: How Many Children Fulfill the Mitzva?

Beit Shammai

  • Fulfilled with two males
  • Source: Moses — had two sons, Gershom and Eliezer, both male
  • The male line continues the family name and inheritance
  • No female child required for fulfillment

Beit Hillel

  • Fulfilled with one male and one female
  • Source: 'Male and female He created them' (Genesis 5:2)
  • Mirrors the original creation of humanity
  • Both genders required to mirror God's act of creation
