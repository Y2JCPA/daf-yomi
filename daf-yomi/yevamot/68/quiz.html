QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, why does Abaye's interpretation of the mishna's 'nine-year-old' fail?
A. Because a nine-year-old cannot legally perform levirate intercourse at all
B. Because the mishna also discusses uncertainty about the boy's age, which would be irrelevant if he already acquired her
C. Because Abaye's interpretation contradicts the position of Rabbi Yosei
D. Because the case of a yavam is covered in a different tractate
Correct: B — Rava points to the latter clause of the mishna, which addresses uncertainty about whether the boy is nine years and one day old. If he had already had intercourse and acquired her, there would be no case of uncertainty — proving the mishna is discussing a different scenario entirely.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav (cited by Rav Yehuda), the primary proof-text for disqualification by unfit intercourse is derived from which phrase?
A. Lev. 22:13 — 'and returns to her father's house as in her youth'
B. Lev. 21:7 — 'they shall not take a woman who is a harlot'
C. Lev. 22:12 — 'that which is set apart from the sacred' (תְּרוּמַת הַקֳּדָשִׁים)
D. Deut. 23:3 — 'An Ammonite or Moabite shall not enter the assembly'
Correct: C — Rav Yehuda citing Rav derives the halakha from the apparently superfluous phrase 'that which is set apart from the sacred' (תְּרוּמַת הַקֳּדָשִׁים) in Lev. 22:12. Once Lev. 22:13 is established as the source for the basic prohibition, this phrase is freed to teach the disqualification by unfit intercourse.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rabbi Yosei son of Rabbi Ḥanina derive from the phrase 'no common man shall eat of the sacred'?
A. A non-priest husband permanently disqualifies his wife from ever eating teruma
B. God declared that common men cannot eat teruma, but a common-man husband enables his wife to eat by virtue of their marriage
C. Any non-priest who eats teruma by accident owes monetary restitution
D. The prohibition on non-priests eating teruma is only Rabbinic in origin
Correct: B — R. Yosei b. R. Ḥanina interprets the verse to teach a positive principle: while God prohibited common men from eating, a common man who becomes a priest's husband actually enables his wife (the priest's daughter) to eat teruma — his marriage creates the right, not just the absence of prohibition.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the a fortiori inference that a Levite or Israelite woman is also disqualified by unfit intercourse?
A. Because a fortiori reasoning cannot create new prohibitions in areas of personal status
B. Because the reverse logic may apply — non-sacred women could be seen as MORE susceptible to disqualification, not less
C. Because Levite and Israelite women have no personal connection to teruma
D. Because Rabbi Akiva explicitly rejected this extension
Correct: B — The Gemara rejects the inference by turning it around: one could argue that a non-sacred Levite or Israelite woman is actually MORE vulnerable to disqualification by unfit intercourse than a sacred priestess. Since the logic can go either way, a verse — not a fortiori reasoning — must be the source.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yosei in the baraita, which unfit males disqualify a woman by intercourse?
A. All non-Jews and all men who were born outside of Israel
B. Only those who are subject to karet (divine excision) for forbidden relationships
C. Only those whose offspring would also be unfit to enter the assembly of Israel
D. Any man who is prohibited from marrying into the general Jewish community
Correct: C — Rabbi Yosei's principle is that only an unfit male whose offspring would themselves be unfit to enter the assembly of Israel disqualifies a woman by intercourse. If the man's offspring would be fit (e.g., his children would have full Jewish status), his intercourse does not disqualify the woman.
