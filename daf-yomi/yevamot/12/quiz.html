QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, why is the rival wife of a me'un girl forbidden to the yavam?
A. Because me'un is equivalent to divorce by Torah law, creating a full prohibition
B. Due to a rabbinic decree based on the case of one's own daughter who performed refusal
C. Because the girl's first marriage was never valid from the outset
D. Because the yavam himself initiated the levirate betrothal
Correct: B — Shmuel's ruling stems from a rabbinic decree: if the girl who refused was the yavam's own daughter (a forbidden relative), her rival wife would be forbidden. To prevent confusion between these cases, the Rabbis forbade all rival wives of me'un girls.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the Gemara infer from the mishna's language 'were found to be an aylonit' rather than simply 'were an aylonit'?
A. That aylonit status can develop over time and change
B. That three witnesses are needed to legally establish aylonit status
C. That the husband discovered her condition only after the marriage, indicating a mistaken betrothal
D. That an aylonit is incapable of performing refusal (me'un)
Correct: C — The Gemara notes the precise language: 'were found to be' implies the condition was unknown at the time of marriage and only discovered later. This makes it a mistaken betrothal (kiddushei ta'ut), voiding the levirate bond and permitting her rival wife.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabba bar Livai, what occurs when a girl between the ages of 11 and 12 becomes pregnant?
A. She gives birth normally but the infant will not survive
B. She cannot become pregnant during this period at all
C. Both she and her fetus will die
D. She retains minor legal status even after giving birth
Correct: C — Rabba bar Livai's three-stage rule: under 11 — cannot conceive; ages 11–12 — can conceive but both mother and fetus die; over 12 — pregnancy proceeds normally. This is precisely why the minor in this age range uses a contraceptive resorbent.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the final halakhic conclusion about the rival wife of an aylonit whose condition was KNOWN to the husband at the time of marriage?
A. She is forbidden, since the aylonit's marriage was fully valid and binding
B. She must perform ḥalitza but is not eligible for levirate marriage
C. She is permitted, since the levirate obligation never applied to the aylonit in the first place
D. The matter remains unresolved and each case is judged individually
Correct: C — Even when the husband knew of her aylonit status, the levirate obligation simply never applied to her — she could not bear children and was not subject to the levirate bond. Therefore her rival wife is permitted, with no levirate obligation at all (12b).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which of the following women is NOT among the three who may use a contraceptive resorbent according to the baraita taught by Rav Beivai before Rav Naḥman?
A. A minor girl between the ages of eleven and twelve
B. A pregnant woman
C. A nursing mother
D. A woman whose husband is a kohen (priest)
Correct: D — The baraita specifies three women: a minor (pregnancy could be fatal), a pregnant woman (to protect the fetus from being compressed), and a nursing mother (to protect the infant from being weaned prematurely). A kohen's wife is not among them.
