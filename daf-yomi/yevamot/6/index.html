⚖️ Can a Positive Mitzva Override a Karet Prohibition?

Gemara
Perhaps we can derive that a positive mitzva overrides even a karet prohibition — from Temple building or honoring parents!
Gemara
No — the baraitot only discuss driving a laden animal (not a karet labor), so no such proof can be derived.
Gemara
But perhaps Shabbat is uniquely severe — that is why neither mitzva overrides it. Other prohibitions might still be overridden?
Gemara
The baraita about a nazirite defiling himself for a corpse shows Shabbat is not uniquely singled out — the same rule applies to all Torah prohibitions.
Conclusion No proof that a positive mitzva overrides karet prohibitions — every case involves mere preparation for the mitzva, not the mitzva act itself.

🏛️ Does Temple Building Override Shabbat?

Premise: A positive mitzva overrides a prohibition
(e.g., ציצית overrides שעטנז — tzitzit overrides mixed fibers)
↓
Claim: Temple building should override even karet-level Shabbat labors (building, demolishing)
↓
Refutation #1
Baraita only discusses driving a laden animal — a rabbinic-level prohibition, not a karet labor
Refutation #2
Even this minor act is preparation for the mitzva, not the mitzva itself — preparation never overrides

🔥 Why Was Kindling (הבערה) Singled Out?

Rabbi Yosei
הבערה ללאו יצאת — Kindling was singled out to teach it is merely a lav (regular prohibition), not punishable by karet. It is a leniency.
School of R. Yishmael
הבערה לחלק יצאת — Kindling was singled out to divide: each Shabbat labor is a separate prohibition with independent liability.
Rava
For the school of R. Yishmael, the word מושבות ('habitations') poses a difficulty — does it limit where Shabbat applies?
Gemara
Since Shabbat is an obligation of the body (not an object), and a bodily obligation has no spatial limit, מושבות cannot restrict the place of Shabbat observance.
Key Dispute Was הבערה singled out as a leniency (lav only, per R. Yosei) — or to divide liability for each labor (per school of R. Yishmael)?

🕍 Reverence for the Temple — ויראת ממקדשי

What Reverence Is NOT

  • You do not revere the Temple structure itself
  • You do not revere Shabbat itself as a day
  • Physical fear of buildings or times is not the intent

What Reverence IS

  • Revere God Who commanded about the Temple
  • No entering Temple Mount with staff, shoes, money belt, or dusty feet
  • Cannot use the Temple Mount as a shortcut
  • Obligation applies even when the Temple is not standing

⚔️ Capital Punishment on Shabbat — The Gezerah Shavah

Initial reasoning: 'If a man has committed a sin worthy of death, and he is put to death' (Deut. 21:22) — implies execution may happen any day, including Shabbat
↓
Verse intervenes: 'You shall kindle no fire throughout your habitations' — the word מושבות appears in both the kindling verse and in Numbers
↓
Gezerah shavah (verbal analogy): Just as מושבות in Numbers refers to 'in the court,' so too here — judgment/execution happens only in the court context
↓
Conclusion: Court-imposed capital punishments (including execution by burning — pouring molten lead) do NOT override Shabbat
↓
Rav Sheshet's parallel: Cooking lead for execution = cooking dye for Tabernacle curtains — if neither permitted on Shabbat, the comparison holds and confirms the ruling
