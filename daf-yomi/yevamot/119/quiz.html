QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why can't the widow simply marry a stranger based on the majority of women who give birth (meaning her rival wife likely had a child)?
A. Because the minority who miscarry combined with her chazaka of being bound to yibum neutralizes the majority
B. Because we never follow statistical majorities in halacha
C. Because the rival wife explicitly said she was not pregnant
D. Because R' Yehoshua forbids it
Correct: A — The Gemara explains that the chazaka (legal presumption that she is bound to yibum) combined with the minority of women who miscarry effectively counterbalances the majority, creating a state of doubt.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the difference between 'ruba d'ita kaman' and 'ruba d'leta kaman'?
A. One is biblical and one is rabbinic
B. One is a physically present, countable majority; the other is based on general statistics
C. One applies to women and the other to men
D. One requires witnesses and the other does not
Correct: B — Ruba d'ita kaman (evident majority) is like 9 kosher stores vs. 1 non-kosher — you can count them. Ruba d'leta kaman (non-evident majority) is a statistical generalization, like 'most women give birth.'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara prove that the Mishna follows R' Meir rather than the Rabbis?
A. R' Meir is explicitly named in the Mishna
B. The Rabbis follow non-evident majorities (proven from the case of minors and yibum), so they would not restrict the widow — only R' Meir who worries about minorities would
C. The Rabbis never discuss widows in the Talmud
D. R' Yehoshua's dissent proves the first opinion must be R' Meir
Correct: B — The baraita about minor boys/girls shows the Rabbis DO follow non-evident majorities (most minors develop normally). Since our Mishna does NOT follow the majority, it must be R' Meir who is concerned about the minority.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does R' Chanina require the widow to wait indefinitely rather than performing chalitza after 9 months?
A. Chalitza cannot be performed by a woman
B. It is a decree lest the rival wife have a viable child, making the chalitza superfluous, and people who saw the chalitza but missed the announcement would think a chalutza may marry a Kohen
C. R' Chanina holds chalitza is biblically invalid in cases of doubt
D. The yavam might not be available after 9 months
Correct: B — Abaye bar Avin and R' Chanina bar Avin explain: if the rival wife had a child, the chalitza was unnecessary. People who witnessed the chalitza but missed the later hakrazah (announcement) might mistakenly think a chalutza is permitted to a Kohen.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the case of two sisters-in-law who each testify 'my husband died,' why are both prohibited from remarrying?
A. Women cannot testify about their husband's death
B. Each is believed about her own status, but each may still be obligated in yibum with the other's possibly-living husband
C. The brothers may have married additional wives
D. They must both perform chalitza first
Correct: B — Each sister-in-law is credible about her own widowhood, but since the other's husband may still be alive, each may be bound to yibum with the surviving brother-in-law — creating a mutual restriction that prevents both from marrying.
