⚖️ Wife's Sister: From Which Case Do We Derive?

Gemara
Wife's sister is forbidden whether paternal or maternal — from where is this derived?
First Answer
Derive from his own sister: just as one's sister is forbidden whether paternal or maternal, so too his wife's sister is forbidden in both cases.
Challenge
Why not derive from his aunt instead? The aunt (wife of father's brother) is forbidden only paternally — derive wife's sister the same way!
Counter
Better to derive from sister — both wife's sister and one's own sister are prohibitions that take effect through betrothal (kiddushin). Derive from the analogous category.
Gemara
Final ruling: the like-from-like reasoning wins. Derive from 'sister' — wife's sister is forbidden whether paternal or maternal.
Key Principle Derive a prohibition from the most categorically analogous case — here, betrothal-category from betrothal-category — not merely the nearest grammatical parallel.

📖 Brother's Wife: The Double-Verse Derivation

Question: Does 'brother's wife' prohibition (Lev. 18:16) cover the wife of a maternal brother?
↓
Derive from sister (his own kin)
Both paternal & maternal — supports inclusion of maternal brother's wife
Derive from aunt (his kin's kin)
Paternal only — would exclude maternal brother's wife
↓
Neither analogy is decisive — logic alone cannot resolve the question
↓
Resolution: The Torah repeats: "It is your brother's nakedness" (Lev. 18:16) — the doubled phrase teaches the prohibition covers both paternal and maternal brothers
↓
A woman with children who would remarry her husband's brother incurs karet — confirmed by 'they shall be childless' (Lev. 20:21)

💋 Defining the Initial Stage of Intercourse (תחילת ביאה)

Shmuel

  • Initial stage = a kiss (נישוק)
  • Defined as external contact of the sexual organs
  • Compared to a finger inserted into a mouth
  • This external contact constitutes the legal beginning of intercourse

Rabba bar bar Chana citing R. Yochanan

  • Reported: 'completion of intercourse with maidservant' = insertion of corona
  • Ambiguity: was this the completion or the initial stage?
  • Created dispute among later transmitters
  • Rav Sheshet raised an objection to this formulation

Rav Dimi, Ravin & Rav Shmuel bar Yehuda citing R. Yochanan

  • Initial stage = insertion of the corona
  • Confirmed by three independent transmitters from Eretz Yisrael
  • Complete intercourse = the full act beyond the corona
  • Final ruling: corona-insertion defines the initial stage

📜 'Cohabitation With Seed' — Three Contexts, Three Functions

The general rule established: Initial stage of intercourse = full legal act for all forbidden relations. So why does the Torah still write 'cohabitation with seed' (שכבת זרע) in three places?
↓
1. Designated Maidservant (שפחה חרופה): The phrase requires a complete act for liability — one who penetrates with a dead organ, or performs only an initial-stage act, is exempt from lashes. The exception proves the rule.
↓
2. Sota (suspected adulteress): 'Cohabitation with seed' excludes intimate contact by other limbs — the sota waters are not administered unless actual intercourse occurred.
↓
Abaye's refinement: The sota exclusion specifically covers external contact of the sexual organs (kissing). She does not become a sota through this alone — only through penetrative intercourse.
↓
Final resolution: The identical phrase serves distinct functions: for the maidservant it requires full completion; for the sota it excludes non-penetrative intimacy. Both are necessary and neither is redundant.
