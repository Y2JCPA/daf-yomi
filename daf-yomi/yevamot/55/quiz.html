QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara prefer deriving the prohibition of one's wife's sister from the case of 'his own sister' rather than from the case of 'his aunt'?
A. Because the sister case appears earlier in the Torah than the aunt case
B. Because both wife's sister and one's own sister are prohibitions that take effect through betrothal (kiddushin)
C. Because the aunt case involves a different type of kinship entirely
D. Because the word 'sister' appears twice in the same verse
Correct: B — The Gemara reasons that one should derive a prohibition from the most categorically analogous case. Since both 'wife's sister' and 'one's own sister' are forbiddendue to betrothal, they share the same legal category — making the sister-derivation the correct model.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the purpose of the doubled phrase 'It is your brother's nakedness' in Leviticus 18:16?
A. To extend the prohibition to a brother who is married but childless
B. To teach that the prohibition applies to the wife of both a paternal and a maternal brother
C. To introduce karet as the punishment for this prohibition
D. To indicate the prohibition applies only after the brother's death
Correct: B — Since neither the sister-analogy nor the aunt-analogy could decisively resolve whether a maternal brother's wife is included, the Torah's repetition of the phrase serves to explicitly extend the prohibition to both paternal and maternal brothers.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the conclusion of the Gemara, what constitutes the 'initial stage of intercourse' (תחילת ביאה)?
A. Any external intimate contact between the parties
B. A kiss or external contact of the sexual organs, as defined by Shmuel
C. The insertion of the corona
D. The complete act of intercourse from beginning to end
Correct: C — Three independent transmitters — Rav Dimi, Ravin, and Rav Shmuel bar Yehuda — all reported Rabbi Yochanan as defining the initial stage as the insertion of the corona. This is the ruling the Gemara adopts over Shmuel's 'kiss' definition.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the context of a sota (suspected adulteress), what does the phrase 'cohabitation with seed' serve to exclude?
A. Cases where the husband failed to issue a formal warning
B. Cases where the act occurred in a public place
C. Non-penetrative intimate contact, such as intimacy by other limbs or external contact
D. Cases where the suspected man was a non-Jew
Correct: C — Rava and Abaye both conclude that 'cohabitation with seed' for sota excludes non-penetrative intimacy — whether contact by other limbs or external sexual contact. The sota waters are administered only when actual penetrative intercourse is suspected.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yochanan, if one violates all forbidden sexual relationships in a single lapse of awareness (העלם אחת), how many sin-offerings is he liable to bring?
A. One sin-offering covering all violations together
B. Two offerings — one for positive and one for negative prohibitions
C. He is liable for each and every violation separately
D. He is exempt because a single lapse cannot simultaneously cover multiple distinct acts
Correct: C — Rabbi Yochanan holds that since all forbidden relations are compared to one another via the verse 'for whoever shall do any of these abominations,' one is nonetheless liable separately for each individual violation, even when they occur within a single lapse of awareness.
