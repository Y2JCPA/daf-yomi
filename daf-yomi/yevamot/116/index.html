↓

Core Issue This daf explores when a wife's testimony about her husband's death is undermined — by quarrels, insufficient mourning signs, or limited circumstances of the original precedent.

The Found Get in Sura: Same Name, Different Man?

Setup
A get was found in Sura written by Anan bar Ḥiyya of Neharde'a. Another Anan bar Ḥiyya existed — from Ḥagra.
Witnesses
On that day, Anan bar Ḥiyya of Ḥagra was with us in Neharde'a — not in Sura.
Abaye
Even though I normally am concerned about same names — here we are not concerned. The witnesses place the other Anan in Neharde'a!
Rava
Even though I normally am not concerned — here we are concerned. Perhaps he traveled by 'flying camel', or by a miraculous shortcut, or gave verbal instructions to write the get remotely!
Reversal Abaye and Rava each reverse their usual positions when there is concrete evidence of another person with the same name.

What Constitutes a 'Quarrel' (Meriva)?

Wife says: "Divorce me!"
↓
❌ Not sufficient — all women say this when angry
↓
Wife says: "You divorced me before witnesses X and Y"
↓
Court asks X and Y — they say: "It never happened"
↓
✅ This is the meriva — she made a false claim of divorce
Rav Hamnuna Normally, a wife who says 'you divorced me' is believed (she wouldn't dare lie to her husband's face). But when named witnesses deny it — it's clearly a quarrel and a lie.

Why Does a Quarrel Disqualify Her Testimony?

Rav Ḥanina

  • She deliberately lies
  • The quarrel motivates her to testify falsely
  • She wants to be free of him
  • Applies when she initiated the quarrel

Rav Shimi bar Ashi

  • She reports what she imagines to be true
  • In peace — she investigates thoroughly
  • In quarrel — she is not meticulous
  • Applies even when he initiated the quarrel
Nafka Mina If he started the quarrel: Rav Ḥanina would accept her testimony (she still loves him, won't lie), but Rav Shimi bar Ashi would reject it (she's still not careful in her investigation).

R' Yehuda vs. the Rabbis: Must She Appear in Mourning?

R' Yehuda
A wife is never believed unless she comes crying, with torn clothing — signs of genuine mourning.
Rabbis
A crafty woman will know to fake tears and rip her clothes. A simple woman who tells the truth won't think to perform these acts. Is that fair?
Rabbis
Both this woman and that woman may remarry regardless of appearance.
Students of R' Yehuda
When a woman came to R' Yehuda's court, they whispered: "Lament, tear your clothes, unbind your hair" — so R' Yehuda would also permit her.
Halakha The Rabbis' view prevails — outward signs of mourning are not required for the wife's testimony to be accepted.

Beit Hillel vs. Beit Shammai: Scope of the Precedent

ConditionBeit Hillel (Initial)Beit Shammai
Type of HarvestOnly grain harvestAll harvests — olives, grapes, dates, figs
LocationOnly in the same countryEven from one country to another
ReasoningMust match the original incident exactlyThe incident was illustrative, not restrictive
CaravansBetween countries, she's not afraid to lieCaravans travel between countries — truth will emerge
Final RulingBeit Hillel retracted✅ Beit Shammai's view accepted
Rare Event This is one of the rare cases where Beit Hillel retracted and ruled in accordance with Beit Shammai.
