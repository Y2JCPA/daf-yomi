QUESTION 1 OF 5
What law does Rabbi Elazar derive from the juxtaposition of the shatnez and tzitzit verses in Deuteronomy 22:11–12?
A. That tzitzit may only be made from pure linen
B. That tzitzit (ritual fringes) override the shatnez (mixed-fiber) prohibition
C. That shatnez is permitted on all four-cornered garments without restriction
D. That wool and linen are the only permitted materials for any garment
Correct: B — The juxtaposition of 'You shall not wear shatnez' and 'You shall make tzitzit' in consecutive verses teaches that the tzitzit mitzvah is an exception to shatnez — a wool thread on a linen garment is permitted when fulfilling the obligation of ritual fringes.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is ben Azzai's argument that a sorceress should be executed by stoning specifically?
A. An explicit verse in Leviticus states that sorcerers are stoned
B. The Torah juxtaposed the sorceress verse with sexual immorality laws, which carry the penalty of stoning
C. The Oral Sinaitic tradition specifies stoning for sorcery
D. Sorcerers are compared to idolaters who are always stoned
Correct: B — Ben Azzai uses semichat parshiyot: the sorceress verse (Exod. 22:17) is adjacent to laws of sexual immorality, which are punished by stoning. Rabbi Yehuda rejects this method, demanding a more explicit source.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Yehuda accept juxtaposition of verses specifically in Deuteronomy, while rejecting it elsewhere?
A. Deuteronomy was composed by a different prophetic author who organized it carefully
B. Deuteronomy contains more commandments than any other book of the Torah
C. Deuteronomy is Mishneh Torah — Moses deliberately reviewed and arranged it, making every adjacency intentional
D. Deuteronomy is the final book and its laws override earlier contradictory verses
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that Deuteronomy is called Mishneh Torah (review/repetition of the Torah). Because Moses deliberately reorganized and reviewed it, every placement of verses is meaningful — unlike the rest of the Torah where adjacency may be incidental.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rava, what is the law when linen tzitzit threads are placed on a wool garment (or wool threads on a linen garment)?
A. It is completely forbidden as shatnez with no exception
B. It fulfills the mitzvah of tzitzit and the shatnez prohibition does not apply
C. It is permitted only for garments worn outside, not for sleeping
D. It fulfills the obligation only if the garment itself also contains both wool and linen
Correct: B — Rava rules that tzitzit threads of wool and linen fulfill the obligation regardless of whether the garment matches the thread material, because the tzitzit mitzvah overrides the shatnez prohibition — derived from the Deuteronomy juxtaposition.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The school of Rabbi Yishmael teaches that 'all garments mentioned in the Torah are of wool or linen.' What halakhic consequence does this have for tzitzit?
A. All garments require tzitzit since all are considered wool or linen by default
B. Garments made of other materials (not wool or linen) have no obligation of tzitzit at all
C. Tzitzit must always be made from a mixture of wool and linen
D. Sky-blue (tekhelet) dye may only be used on linen garments
Correct: B — The school of Rabbi Yishmael holds that since 'garments' in the Torah refers to wool or linen, the tzitzit obligation only applies to wool or linen garments. Garments made of other materials are exempt — limiting both the scope of the obligation and the shatnez override.
