⚖️ Can a Positive Mitzva Override a Prohibition?

Established rule: A positive mitzva (עשה) can override a regular prohibition (לאו)
↓
Regular Prohibition
✅ Positive mitzva overrides
e.g., tzitzit thread overrides shatnez
Prohibition + Karet (כרת)
❓ Does positive mitzva override?
e.g., yibum vs. forbidden relations
↓
Conclusion: No — a positive mitzva does not override a prohibition that carries karet. The word עמה limits yibum.

🏛️ Does Executing a Convict Override Shabbat?

Tanna (initial)
Temple service overrides Shabbat; murder overrides Temple service — therefore by kal va-chomer, execution must override Shabbat!
Tanna (retracts)
But wait — we said positive mitzvot override regular prohibitions. What about a prohibition derived from a positive commandment (לאו הבא מכלל עשה)?
Rav Shimi bar Ashi
The tanna was never claiming execution overrides Shabbat via the mitzva principle. He was only exploring a potential kal va-chomer that he then rejected.
Gemara (conclusion)
'Every one who profanes it shall surely be put to death' (Ex. 31:14) applies to all other labors — Shabbat is not overridden by the execution mitzva.
Key Term לאו הבא מכלל עשה — a prohibition derived implicitly from a positive commandment; treated as a lesser prohibition

📊 Two Levels of Prohibition — Can a Positive Mitzva Override?

Regular Prohibition (לאו)

  • ✅ Positive mitzva CAN override
  • No karet penalty attached
  • Example: tzitzit threads override shatnez prohibition
  • Lower level of severity

Prohibition Carrying Karet (כרת)

  • ❌ Positive mitzva CANNOT override
  • Extirpation (כרת) attached to violation
  • Example: forbidden relations in yibum context
  • Word עמה limits the mitzva of levirate marriage

🦯 Leper's Guilt-Offering: The Novel-Matter Principle

General rule: All guilt-offerings require blood and sacrificial parts placed on the altar
↓
Exception singled out: Leper's guilt-offering — blood placed on right thumb, right big toe, and right ear (entirely new ruling)
↓
Without restoration verse
❌ One might think altar placement is no longer required for leper's offering
With restoration verse (Lev. 7:7)
✅ 'As the sin-offering, so the guilt-offering' — altar placement still required
↓
Principle: When singled out for an entirely novel ruling (יצא לדון בדבר החדש), the case is removed from its generalization unless a verse explicitly restores it

✨ Ulla's Principle: הותרה הותרה

הואיל והותרה לצרעתו — הותרה (Since it was permitted for his leprosy — it is [fully] permitted)
Since It Was Permitted — It Is Fully Permitted A leper on his eighth day (day of purification) who also experienced a seminal emission may still enter the Temple to bring his offerings. Why? Because the Torah already permitted this leper to enter the Temple despite his leprous impurity. Since the door of permission was opened for one impurity, the additional impurity of the seminal emission does not close it. One established permission covers the additional complication — הואיל והותרה, הותרה. Rabbi Yoḥanan connects this to 'the new courtyard' (II Chron. 20:5) — he taught that Jehoshaphat innovated something new, indicating that novel rulings can be derived from such contextual clues.
