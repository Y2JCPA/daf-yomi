QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara on Daf 7, can a positive mitzva override a prohibition that carries karet?
A. Yes — a positive mitzva always supersedes any prohibition regardless of severity
B. No — a positive mitzva can override a regular prohibition but NOT one carrying karet
C. Only if the positive mitzva itself also carries karet for non-performance
D. It depends on whether the Sanhedrin explicitly rules so in each case
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that while positive mitzvot can override regular prohibitions (לאו), they cannot override prohibitions carrying the penalty of karet. This is why yibum does not apply to all women with whom relations are normally forbidden.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the seemingly superfluous word עמה ('with her') in the levirate marriage verse teach?
A. That the widow must consent to the levirate marriage
B. That levirate marriage applies only when the brother lives nearby
C. That the levirate marriage obligation does not override prohibitions on forbidden relations
D. That yibum may be fulfilled through a proxy representative
Correct: C — The Gemara derives from עמה that levirate marriage only applies in circumstances where the union is otherwise permitted — it does not override prohibitions, especially those carrying karet, on forbidden relations.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the legal consequence when a case is 'singled out from the general to discuss an entirely new matter' (יצא לדון בדבר החדש)?
A. The new ruling applies to every other item in the general category
B. The case is fully removed from its original generalization unless a verse explicitly restores it
C. The new ruling applies only on Shabbat and Yom Tov
D. The original generalization is abolished entirely
Correct: B — When a case is singled out for an entirely novel ruling (yatza lidon b'davar hachadash), it is removed from its generalization. Without a restoring verse, all prior rules of the general category would no longer apply to that specific case.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which verse restores the leper's guilt-offering to the generalization requiring altar placement of blood and sacrificial parts?
A. 'And he shall slaughter the sheep in the place where they slaughter the sin-offering' (Leviticus 14:13)
B. 'As the sin-offering is, so is the guilt-offering' (Leviticus 7:7)
C. 'Every one who profanes it shall surely be put to death' (Exodus 31:14)
D. 'But the soul that eats of the flesh of the peace-offerings' (Leviticus 7:20)
Correct: B — The verse 'As the sin-offering is, so is the guilt-offering' (Lev. 7:7) explicitly restores the leper's guilt-offering to its generalization, teaching that altar placement of blood and parts is still required despite the novel blood-on-thumb-and-toe ruling.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Ulla's principle (הותרה הותרה) as applied to the leper on his eighth day?
A. A leper must wait an additional seven days if he experiences a seminal emission on his eighth day
B. A leper's seminal emission on his eighth day exempts him from all purification offerings that year
C. Since the Torah permitted his Temple entry for leprosy, the additional impurity of seminal emission does not prevent him from bringing his offerings
D. A leper may enter only the outer courtyard, not the inner Temple, on his eighth day
Correct: C — Ulla teaches הותרה הותרה: since the Torah already permitted this leper to enter the Temple despite his leprous impurity, the additional impurity of a seminal emission does not revoke that permission. One established permission covers the complication.
