Rav Hamnuna's View: Widow Awaiting Yavam

Gemara
Rav Hamnuna says a shomeret yavam who had illicit relations is disqualified from levirate marriage, like a married woman who committed adultery.
Challenge
The mishna discusses disqualification only when the yavam himself had relations with her — implying relations with another man don't disqualify!
Gemara
No refutation. The same halakha applies even with another man. The tanna mentioned the yavam because the first clause discussed the yavam — for stylistic consistency.
Gemara
Regarding the aylonit (sexually underdeveloped woman): intercourse disqualifies her, but ḥalitza does not. This is not R' Yehuda's view, since he considers an aylonit a zona — already disqualified!
Key Point Rav Hamnuna's strict ruling survives the challenge — the mishna's focus on the yavam is merely stylistic, not exclusionary.

Why Must the Mishna Teach About the Saris?

A saris ḥama (naturally impotent priest) marries an Israelite woman
↓
Isn't it obvious he enables her to eat teruma?
↓
You might think: "born in his house" (Lev. 22:11) implies only one who can father children qualifies
↓
The mishna teaches: capacity for children is irrelevant — he is still a valid priest for teruma
Verse "And such as are born in his house, they eat of his bread" (Leviticus 22:11) — this refers to the priest's household, not his biological offspring.

Reish Lakish vs. R' Yoḥanan: Androginos Priest

Reish Lakish
A hermaphrodite priest enables his wife to eat teruma, but not the breast and thigh of shelamim (peace-offerings).
R' Yoḥanan
He even enables her to eat the breast and thigh of peace-offerings.
Gemara
What's different about breast & thigh? Both are Torah law! Answer: We're discussing teruma today — which is only rabbinic.
Reish Lakish
When the Temple stands, he doesn't enable even rabbinic teruma — lest she come to eat Torah-level teruma.
R' Yoḥanan
You hold teruma today is rabbinic? Reish Lakish: Yes! Proof: a cake of teruma figs is nullified in 100 — only possible if rabbinic.
Core Dispute Reish Lakish: teruma today is rabbinic, so an androginos priest has limited authority. R' Yoḥanan: even Torah-level teruma and offerings are permitted.

Nullification (Bitul): R' Meir vs. the Rabbis

R' Meir

  • Any item usually counted (sold by unit) renders a mixture forbidden
  • Cannot be nullified by any majority
  • All items in the mixture must be burned

The Rabbis

  • Only 6 specific items cannot be nullified
  • All others are nullified at 1:200 ratio
  • R' Akiva adds a 7th: homeowner's loaves
Dispute in Reading R' Yoḥanan reads R' Meir as: items sold exclusively by unit. Reish Lakish reads: items even sometimes sold by unit.
