⚖️ Rabbi Meir vs. the Rabbis: The Fifth Sin-Offering

Rabbi Meir
One impure person who eats forbidden fat (notar) on Yom Kippur incurs four sin-offerings. If he also carried the food on Shabbat, he incurs a fifth — Shabbat carrying is an independent prohibition!
The Rabbis
Shabbat carrying requires a dried fig's bulk as its minimum measure. An olive-bulk of food is insufficient to trigger that prohibition. No fifth offense here.
Rabbi Meir
Agreed — this specific case does not complete liability. But I illustrate the type of act that would: if a larger amount were carried, Shabbat's expanded prohibition would indeed attach.
Gemara
This confirms: Rabbi Meir holds a prohibition takes effect even where another already exists — a more encompassing prohibition (Shabbat) attaches atop prior violations.
Key Principle Rabbi Meir: an expanded prohibition (Shabbat) takes effect atop prior prohibitions — contra the view that an existing issur blocks a new one

👶 Can a Virgin Conceive from First Intercourse?

Mishna: Women separated for 3 months — concern they may be pregnant
↓
Gemara objects: A virgin does not become pregnant from her first act of intercourse!
↓
Rav Naḥman's Answer
The 3-month rule applies because multiple acts occurred — not only the first
Rava's Challenge
Tamar was a virgin and yet conceived from Judah! (Genesis 38)
↓
Rav Naḥman responds: Tamar broke her hymen with her finger beforehand — physiologically no longer a virgin in the relevant sense
↓
Conclusion: A true virgin does not conceive from first intercourse; Tamar's case is no counterexample

🔥 Er and Onan: Two Sins, One Woman (Tamar)

Onan's Sin — Explicit

  • Engaged in unnatural intercourse — withdrew before completion
  • Explicitly stated: "He spilled it on the ground" (Genesis 38:9)
  • Motive: Did not want offspring attributed to his deceased brother Er
  • Punishment: Died by divine judgment — וַיָּמֶת

Er's Sin — Derived

  • Also engaged in unnatural intercourse with Tamar
  • No explicit verse — derived via identical language: וַיָּמֶת ("and he died") for both brothers
  • Motive: Did not want Tamar's beauty diminished by pregnancy
  • Same sin, same death — verbal analogy seals the connection

⏳ Shmuel's Rule: The Three-Month Wait Before Remarriage

Shmuel's ruling: All women who had intercourse and may be pregnant must wait three months before marrying another man
↓
Purpose: To differentiate (להבחין) the child — establish whether born from the previous or the new husband
↓
Additional ruling (Rav Naḥman citing Rabbi Yoḥanan): A woman who waited ten years after divorce or widowhood can no longer conceive
↓
Story: A woman came before Rav Yosef claiming she waited ten full years yet still gave birth
↓
Rav Yosef: "My daughter, do not cast aspersions on the Sages' ruling" — you must have remarried before the ten years fully elapsed
↓
Scope of three-month rule: Applies to widows, divorcées, and minors released by refusal (mi'un)
