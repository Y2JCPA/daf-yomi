Yevamot 59 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the mishna and Gemara, a High Priest's prohibition against marrying a widow applies to:
A. Only a widow from full marriage (nissuin)
B. Only a widow from betrothal (erusin)
C. Both a widow from betrothal and from marriage
D. Only if she has children from the previous marriage
Correct: C — 'A widow…he shall not take' (Lev. 21:14) prohibits the High Priest from marrying any widow, whether she was widowed from betrothal or from full marriage. The mishna on teruma confirms both cases are treated equally.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rabbi Elazar and Rabbi Shimon permit the High Priest to marry a bogeret. What is their derivation?
A. The verse 'a virgin' implies even partial virginity qualifies
B. The possessive 'her virginity' indicates partial virginity suffices
C. A baraita explicitly permits a grown woman
D. She was betrothed to him before she became a bogeret
Correct: B — R. Elazar and R. Shimon derive from the word בְּבְתוּלֶיהָ ('her virginity') that even a woman with partial signs of virginity — such as a bogeret — is fit for the High Priest.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rabbi Zeira resolve the debate over Rav's statement that atypical intercourse disqualifies a woman?
A. Rav spoke of typical intercourse, not atypical
B. Rav spoke of a woman raped by the High Priest himself
C. Rav spoke of a woman who performed mi'un after only atypical intercourse
D. Rav agreed with R. Meir that atypical intercourse is irrelevant
Correct: C — Rabbi Zeira explains that Rav referred to a woman who dissolved her marriage via mi'un (a minor's refusal) after having only atypical intercourse. She is a non-virgin but not technically a zona.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What was Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's ruling in the Hitlu incident, and what principle does it illustrate?
A. She became a zona and was forbidden to the priesthood
B. She remained permitted — there is no harlotry designation for intercourse with an animal
C. She required a ritual bath and was then permitted
D. The case was left unresolved pending further discussion
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi ruled the girl remained permitted to a priest. Rav Ashi derives from the juxtaposition of 'harlot's hire' and 'price of a dog' in Deut. 23:19 that intercourse with an animal carries no zona designation.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If a High Priest raped a woman and subsequently married her, what is the halakha according to Rav Huna citing Rav?
A. The marriage is void and she is automatically free
B. The marriage is fully valid and he may remain with her
C. The marriage is valid but he must give her a bill of divorce
D. The ruling depends on whether she consented to the marriage
Correct: C — The baraita rules 'he is married' — meaning the marriage takes legal effect. But Rav Huna citing Rav adds that he must nonetheless divorce her with a get, since the marriage, though valid, is forbidden.
