🌸 May the High Priest Marry a Grown Woman (Bogeret)?

Tanna Kamma
Forbidden. The verse 'he shall take a wife in her virginity' (Lev. 21:13) excludes a bogeret — her hymen has worn away and is no longer as conspicuous as that of a na'arah. A High Priest may only marry a minor or a young woman.
R. Elazar & R. Shimon
Permitted. The possessive form בְּבְתוּלֶיהָ ('her virginity') indicates even partial virginity suffices. A bogeret retains some virginal signs and is therefore fit for the High Priest.
Key Verse "And he shall take a wife in her virginity" (Leviticus 21:13) — the entire daf orbits whether 'her virginity' expands or contracts eligibility at the margins.

👰 Widow: Betrothal vs. Marriage — High Priest Prohibitions

Widow from Betrothal

  • Husband died after kiddushin but before nissuin
  • Never had marital intercourse with him
  • Still forbidden to High Priest — she is an almanah
  • Disqualified from teruma as a priest's daughter
  • Source: mishna — 'widowed from betrothal' is explicit

Widow from Marriage

  • Husband died after full nissuin
  • Had complete marital relationship
  • Forbidden to High Priest — standard almanah case
  • Disqualified from teruma as priest's daughter
  • The paradigmatic case of Lev. 21:14

Betrothed Before Appointment

  • Betrothed a widow, then was appointed High Priest
  • Permitted to complete the marriage
  • We follow her status at time of betrothal
  • Source: mishna 61a — 'he may marry her'
  • Contrast bogeret: her body has changed

🔀 Atypical Intercourse — Rav's Ruling Resolved

Rav: A woman who had atypical (anal) intercourse is disqualified from marrying the High Priest
↓
Rava's objection: A baraita permits a rape victim — why would atypical intercourse disqualify her if typical rape does not?
↓
Rav Yosef's answer
Rav meant intercourse with an animal — she is a non-virgin but not a zona
Abaye refutes
If she is a non-virgin she is also a zona; if not a zona she is not a non-virgin — contradiction
↓
Rabbi Zeira's resolution: Rav spoke of a woman who performed mi'un (refusal) after only atypical intercourse — disqualified as a non-virgin, but not a zona

🐕 Intercourse with an Animal — Zona or Not?

R. Shimi bar Ḥiyya
Not a zona. A woman who had intercourse with an animal is like one whose hymen was torn accidentally. She is not a zona and remains fit for the priesthood. This is also taught in a baraita.
Rav Ashi (derivation)
From Deut. 23:19: 'the hire of a harlot or the price of a dog' — these are juxtaposed. Since a dog's price is not harlotry (Temura 30a), intercourse with an animal carries no harlotry designation at all.
📍 Historical Incident — Village of Hitlu A girl was sweeping her house when a hunting dog sodomized her from behind. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi ruled she remained fully permitted to a priest — no zona status was incurred.

⚖️ High Priest Who Raped or Seduced — The Halacha

Prohibition: A High Priest may not marry a woman he himself raped or seduced — she is commanded to be a virgin, and she is no longer one for him
↓
If he married her anyway: The baraita rules he is married — the marriage takes legal effect despite the prohibition
↓
Rav Huna citing Rav: He must give her a get (bill of divorce). The marriage is real but must be dissolved
↓
Contrast — another man's victim: A woman raped or seduced by a different man is also forbidden to the High Priest (she is a non-virgin)
↓
Key principle: It is intercourse that causes disqualification — not betrothal, not status, not intention. The physical act is decisive
