QUESTION 1 OF 5
When a son of uncertain descent (safek) and the yavam both claim the grandfather's estate, what is the halakhic ruling?
A. They divide the property equally since the safek may be a legitimate heir
B. The property is held in escrow until the uncertainty is resolved by a court
C. The yavam takes all because his claim is definite while the safek's is uncertain
D. The safek takes half since he has at least a 50% chance of being the true heir
Correct: C — The Gemara rules that an uncertain claim cannot extract property from a definite one (ספק אינו מוציא מידי ודאי). The yavam is definitively the grandfather's son; the safek's claim to being the grandson is uncertain.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Ulla, what legal status does a levirate bond (זיקה) formed with a previously betrothed woman create?
A. Equivalent to full marriage — the yavam gains all marital property rights
B. Equivalent to uncertain betrothal — the yavam's claim is therefore only uncertain
C. No legal status at all — zikah only applies when she was already married
D. Equivalent to full betrothal — Beit Shammai should restrict her property sales
Correct: B — Ulla holds that zikah with a betrothed woman creates a status equivalent to uncertain betrothal. Since the yavam's claim is only uncertain, Beit Shammai agree she may sell her property freely while alive.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rabba resolve the apparent inconsistency in Beit Shammai's rulings between the two clauses of the mishna?
A. The first clause involves a betrothed woman; the second involves a married woman
B. Both clauses involve married women; while alive her body's possession is decisive, on death no such factor applies
C. Beit Shammai actually agree with Beit Hillel in the first clause for a separate technical reason
D. The first clause is a later addition by a different tanna with a different view
Correct: B — Rabba argues both clauses concern married women. In the first clause, even though the yavam has a definite claim, the woman's living possession of her own body overrides it. On her death, that factor vanishes and both sets of heirs compete.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Beit Shammai's ruling regarding a 'bill of debt awaiting collection' (שטר העומד לגבות)?
A. It creates only an uncertain claim — the debt is not real until actually paid
B. It is invalid if the debtor died before collection could begin
C. It is considered as though already collected, making the creditor a definite claimant
D. It must be divided equally between the creditor and the debtor's heirs
Correct: C — Beit Shammai hold שטר העומד לגבות כגבוי דמי — a bill awaiting collection is as if already collected. This explains why they divide property in the Bava Batra case: the creditor's claim is actually definite, not uncertain.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When the grandfather and the yavam both come to divide the estate left by the deceased safek son, what does the Gemara rule?
A. The grandfather takes all as the paternal ancestor with priority
B. The yavam takes all since he may be the biological father
C. The property is divided equally because both parties have only uncertain claims
D. The property is given to the yavam's sons as the safek's possible half-brothers
Correct: C — Since neither the grandfather nor the yavam has a definite claim to the safek's estate — the uncertainty of paternity affects both sides equally — this is a case of uncertain ownership (ממון המוטל בספק) and the property is divided equally.
