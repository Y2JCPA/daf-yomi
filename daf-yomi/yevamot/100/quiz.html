QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why may teruma be sent to an uncircumcised man's home but NOT to a ritually impure man's home?
A. The uncircumcised man's state is entirely beyond his control, while the impure man could have protected himself
B. An uncircumcised man is still considered a priest, but an impure man is not
C. The uncircumcised man already owns teruma, while the impure man does not
D. Sending teruma to an impure man would render the teruma impure
Correct: A — The Gemara distinguishes that the uncircumcised man's condition (brothers died from circumcision) is entirely beyond his control, while the impure man bears some responsibility since he could have avoided impurity.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to one opinion, why is teruma not distributed to women at the granary?
A. Women are exempt from the mitzva of teruma
B. A divorced woman (who lost her right to teruma) might continue receiving it
C. Women cannot carry heavy loads of grain
D. Women are not permitted to own teruma independently
Correct: B — One opinion (Rav Pappa or Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua) holds that the concern is for an Israelite woman divorced from a priest who might continue collecting teruma she's no longer entitled to.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Rava change his practice from resolving men's cases first to resolving women's cases first?
A. He received a ruling from the Sanhedrin
B. He learned from a baraita that it is demeaning for a woman to wait among men
C. Women's cases were typically simpler and faster to resolve
D. The Torah requires prioritizing women in judgment
Correct: B — After hearing the baraita that women should receive poor man's tithe first because it is demeaning for them to wait among men, Rava applied the same principle to his courtroom.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How is the meal-offering of a 'mixed son' (uncertain if priest or Israelite) handled?
A. It is burned entirely like a priest's offering
B. A handful is taken and burned; the remainder is eaten by priests
C. A handful is taken and burned; the remainder is offered as wood (fuel) for the altar
D. The offering is not accepted at all due to the uncertainty
Correct: C — The handful is taken (Israelite stringency) and the remainder is not eaten (priestly stringency). To avoid the prohibition against burning non-designated parts, the remainder is placed on the altar as fuel, not as a sacrifice.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why is a child fathered by an unidentified priest (from a group of ten) denied priestly status, while a son born from levirate marriage within three months is fit even to be High Priest?
A. The verse 'and to his seed after him' only applies to sons of High Priests
B. The lineage requirement is rabbinic and was only applied to cases of licentious relations, not levirate marriage
C. Levirate marriage sons are attributed to the deceased brother, not the father
D. A son of ten priests has the status of a mamzer
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that the lineage attribution requirement is rabbinic (the verse is merely an asmakhta/support), and the Sages only applied it to cases of licentious relations, not to legitimate situations like levirate marriage.
