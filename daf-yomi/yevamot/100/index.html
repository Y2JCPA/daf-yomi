Yevamot 100: Teruma Distribution, Dignity, and Uncertain Lineage

Who receives teruma at the granary?
↓
Not Distributed To
Slaves, women, uncircumcised, impure, androginos, tumtum, or one who marries an unfit woman
May Be Sent Home
All of the above except the impure man and one who marries an unfit woman
↓
Uncertain lineage — sons of mixed-up mothers face dual priestly/Israelite stringencies
↓
Shetuki — even a definite priest's son is silenced from priesthood if father is unidentifiable
Core Theme This daf examines who may receive sacred gifts, the dignity owed to women in public settings, and how uncertain paternity affects priestly status.

Why Is Teruma Not Distributed to Women?

Rav Pappa
Because of a divorced woman — an Israelite woman married to a priest who got divorced might continue receiving teruma she's no longer entitled to.
Rav Huna b. R' Yehoshua
Because of yichud (seclusion) — the owner and the woman might be alone together in the granary.
Gemara
What's the practical difference (nafka mina)?
Gemara
A granary near town but not frequented — owner would know if she's divorced, but seclusion is still a concern.
Gemara
Or a granary far but frequented — owner might not know she's divorced, but seclusion is not a concern.
Nafka Mina The two opinions diverge in edge cases where one concern applies but not the other — showing how the Gemara tests competing rationales.

Rava's Courtroom Practice

גנאי הוא לאשה לעמוד בפני אנשים
Dignity of Women Rava said: Initially, when a man and woman came before me for judgment, I would resolve the man's case first, reasoning that since he is obligated in more mitzvot, I shouldn't waste his time.

But after hearing the baraita about giving women poor man's tithe first, I changed my practice — I now resolve the woman's case first, because it is demeaning for her to wait in the company of men.

The Mixed Sons: Freeing Each Other

Two sons mixed at birth — one is a priest's son, one is a slave's son
↓
Neither can marry a maidservant (might be a priest) nor a free woman (might be a slave)
↓
They cannot fulfill פרו ורבו (be fruitful and multiply)
↓
Mishna's Language
"They matured and freed each other" — implies voluntary
Rava's Correction
We coerce them to free each other — it is obligatory
Rava Since neither can marry in his current uncertain state, the court compels mutual emancipation to enable the mitzva of procreation.

Meal-Offering of the Mixed Sons

Israelite's Meal-Offering

  • Kohen takes a handful (קומץ)
  • Handful burned on altar
  • Remainder eaten by kohanim

Priest's Meal-Offering

  • No handful taken
  • Entire offering burned on altar
  • Nothing eaten

Mixed Son's Offering

  • Handful IS taken (Israelite stringency)
  • Remainder NOT eaten (priestly stringency)
  • Handful burned; remainder offered as 'wood' for the altar fire (R' Elazar)
R' Yehuda b. R' Shimon b. Pazi The remainder is brought to the altar only as fuel (wood), not as a sacrifice — avoiding the prohibition against burning parts not designated for the altar.
