Yevamot 54 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Yehuda, why does a sleeping man NOT acquire his yevama?
A. He must recite an acquisition formula
B. The verse 'her brother-in-law will have intercourse with her' requires deliberate intent
C. Sleeping is halakhically equivalent to coercion
D. A baraita explicitly exempts all sleeping men
Correct: B — Rav Yehuda derives from Deuteronomy 25:5 that the verb 'will have intercourse' demands intentional action — a sleeping man has no intent and therefore does not acquire.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rava rules that a man who intended intercourse with a wall but accidentally entered his yevama has NOT acquired her. What if he intended intercourse with another (permitted) woman?
A. Still no acquisition — wrong partner means invalid intent
B. He HAS acquired her — intent for any intercourse suffices
C. He must perform chalitza but need not do yibum
D. It is a dispute — Abaye says acquired, Rava says not
Correct: B — Rava distinguishes between types of intent: intending a wall = zero sexual intent → no acquisition. Intending another woman = sexual intent present → acquisition occurs even accidentally.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the Gemara's final accepted source that initial-stage intercourse (hava'at eivarim) counts as full intercourse for ALL forbidden relations?
A. The law of nidda — Leviticus 18:19
B. The comparison of brother's wife to nidda — Leviticus 20:21
C. The concluding verse of the arayot section — 'whoever does any of these abominations' (Lev. 18:29)
D. The word he'era in the law of father's sister — Leviticus 20:19
Correct: C — After rejecting each individual source (each has a unique stringency), the Gemara (Rabbi Yona / Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua) derives the rule from Lev. 18:29, which encompasses all the forbidden relations.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Abbahu, why does the Torah explicitly write BOTH 'father's sister' and 'mother's sister' when both could seemingly be learned from one verse?
A. One verse covers full sisters; the other covers half-sisters
B. Without both, each could be read as limited to one side of the family (paternal or maternal only)
C. The two verses assign different punishments to each prohibition
D. One is a Torah prohibition; the other is a rabbinic fence
Correct: B — Rabbi Abbahu: if only father's sister were written, one might limit it to paternal relationships; if only mother's sister, to maternal. Both verses confirm the prohibition applies regardless of which side of the family.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara derive that only the wife of a PATERNAL uncle (not a maternal uncle) is forbidden?
A. It is explicit in the verse 'you shall not uncover the nakedness of your uncle's wife'
B. A gezera shava: the word 'his uncle' appears in Lev. 20:20 and in the laws of redemption (Lev. 25:49), where only paternal relatives are meant
C. It is derived from the prohibition of a brother's wife by kal va-chomer
D. It is a halakha l'Moshe miSinai — Sinaitic oral tradition
Correct: B — Rava derives it via a gezera shava on the word 'dodo' (his uncle): in Lev. 20:20 and in the redemption laws of Lev. 25:49, the context of the latter restricts the term to paternal relatives.
