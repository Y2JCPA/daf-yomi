🌙 Does the Sleeping Man Acquire His Yevama?

Rav Yehuda
A sleeping man has NOT acquired his yevama. 'Her brother-in-law will have intercourse with her' — the verse requires intentional action.
Baraita (objection)
One acquires his yevama whether he was AWAKE or ASLEEP, and whether she was awake or asleep — intent is irrelevant!
Gemara Resolution
The baraita refers to a case where he intended intercourse with his wife, became erect, and his yevama forcibly grabbed him. His original sexual intent is sufficient.
Rava
If he intended to press into a wall but entered his yevama — NOT acquired. But if he intended intercourse with another woman and entered his yevama — IS acquired.
Ruling Principle Any intent for sexual intercourse — even with a different person — suffices. Zero intent for intercourse (e.g., pressing into a wall) never acquires.

⚡ Three Cases of Accidental Entry — Rava's Analysis

Fell from Roof

  • Man fell and was inserted by force of fall
  • Rabba: liable for 4 of 5 tort payments (unmarried woman)
  • Rava: does NOT acquire yevama
  • Reason: no intent for any intercourse whatsoever

Intended Wall → Got Yevama

  • Intended to press organ into wall
  • Accidentally entered his yevama
  • Rava: does NOT acquire
  • No intent for intercourse of any kind

Intended Other Woman → Got Yevama

  • Intended intercourse with another woman
  • Accidentally entered his yevama instead
  • Rava: DOES acquire yevama
  • Intent for intercourse (even wrong partner) is sufficient

📖 Seeking the Source: Initial Stage of Intercourse = Full Intercourse

Does hava'at eivarim (initial penetration) constitute full intercourse for forbidden relations?
↓
Try: Nidda (Lev. 18:19)
Objection: nidda is unique — prohibition cannot be permitted to others while it exists
Try: Brother's Wife (Lev. 20:21)
Objection: also cannot be permitted to others during husband's lifetime
Try: Father's/Mother's Sister (Lev. 20:19)
Objection: unique — prohibition applies to paternal relatives of father only
↓
Each proposed source has a unique stringency that prevents it from serving as a general model
↓
Final Answer — Rabbi Yona / Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua: Derived from the concluding verse of the entire arayot section — "whoever does any of these abominations" (Lev. 18:29)

👨‍👧 Why Write Both Father's Sister AND Mother's Sister?

עֶרְוַת אֲחוֹת אָבִיךָ לֹא תְגַלֵּה / עֶרְוַת אֲחוֹת אִמְּךָ לֹא תְגַלֵּה (ויקרא כ:יט)
Rabbi Abbahu Both verses are independently necessary:

If only father's sister were written → one might think: applies only through the paternal line, since paternal lineage determines tribal identity.

If only mother's sister were written → one might think: applies only to her, since her maternal connection is absolutely certain (birth is observable).

Therefore both are written — each prohibition covers the relative whether she comes from the father's father or the father's mother side.
