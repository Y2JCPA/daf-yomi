⚖️ Three Amoraim on Conditions in Ḥalitza

Rav Sherevya
The dispute concerns betrothal after valid ḥalitza. Once valid ḥalitza is performed the bond is gone — betrothal cannot rest on a nonexistent bond. The question is whether such betrothal could ever have been effective.
Rav Ashi
Invalid ḥalitza neither exempts nor fully nullifies the bond. The dispute is whether a condition can be attached to ḥalitza — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi holds yes; the Rabbis hold no condition is effective.
Ravina
Everyone agrees conditions work in ḥalitza. The dispute concerns the compound condition (תנאי כפול) — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi requires stating both 'if yes' and 'if no'; the Rabbis do not require the double formulation.
Core Issue When ḥalitza is performed conditionally and the condition is unmet — does the ḥalitza take effect, and can betrothal afterward acquire the ḥalutza?

🏠 Scope of Ḥalitza's Exemption: One Yavam, Two Yevamot

Rabbi Yoḥanan
Once ḥalitza is performed with one yevama, the levirate bond is entirely severed. The second yevama is automatically exempt. The mishna's case of two yevamot is not obvious — it genuinely teaches a novel law.
Reish Lakish
After ḥalitza, the entire household — except the ḥalutza herself — is liable to karet. The second yevama is an ervah. It is obvious betrothal cannot take effect on arayot — the mishna teaches nothing new here!
Resolution Reish Lakish: The mishna need not teach each clause independently. Since the tanna taught one yevama/one yavam, he also taught two yevamot/one yavam for formal completeness and structural symmetry.

🔢 Three Opinions on Intercourse as Middle or Final Act

First Tanna

  • Beginning: fully effective
  • Middle: something effective after it
  • End: something effective after it
  • Rationale: Decree — intercourse after get might resemble intercourse after betrothal, so we treat it leniently

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi

  • Beginning: fully effective
  • Middle: something effective after it
  • End: nothing effective after it
  • Rationale: End-intercourse resembles beginning-intercourse; no decree needed for either

Rabbi Neḥemya

  • Beginning: fully effective
  • Middle: nothing effective after it
  • End: nothing effective after it
  • Rationale: No decree needed at all — intercourse is always clearly distinct; no confusion with get

🤝 All Combinations of Intent Fulfill Levirate Marriage

בא עליה אנוס או מזיד, שוגג או מתכוין — הרי זו אשתו לכל דבר
New Mishna (53b) — Unwitting Intercourse Fulfills Yibbum Intercourse fulfills yibbum regardless of intent or awareness:

• He was unwitting, she was intentional ✓
• He was intentional, she was unwitting ✓
• He was coerced, she was willing ✓
• He was willing, she was coerced ✓
• Both unwitting or both coerced ✓

In all cases she is his wife in every respect.

Gemara: 'Coerced' cannot mean threatened with death (that is voluntary under Torah law). It must mean: he fell asleep and became erect, and she drew him onto herself.
