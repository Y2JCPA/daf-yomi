🔍 Central Question: May an Uncircumcised Man Eat Second Tithe?

Baraita
An uncircumcised man is prohibited from eating two different tithes — implying second tithe is one of them!
Gemara (Deflection)
The two tithes are first tithe (to Levites) and second tithe — but this follows Rabbi Meir, who alone forbids first tithe to non-priests. No general proof.
Another Baraita
An acute mourner may not eat second tithe but may eat teruma — proof that uncircumcised men likewise cannot eat second tithe?
Gemara (Rejects)
No proof! This baraita follows Rabbi Akiva's school, which uniquely equates uncircumcised men with the ritually impure — a minority position not accepted as halakha.
Resolution (Rabbi Yitzchak) The prohibition is established via gezerah shavah: the word 'mimenu' (of it) appears three times in both the Paschal lamb and second tithe contexts, linking their laws.

🔗 Rabbi Yitzchak's Gezerah Shavah — Tracing 'Mimenu'

Question: From where is it derived that an uncircumcised man cannot eat second tithe? (No explicit verse!)
↓
Paschal Lamb: 'Mimenu' written three times — 'do not eat of it raw… do not leave of it… do not break a bone of it'
↓
Second Tithe: 'Mimenu' written three times — 'I did not eat of it in mourning… impure… of it for the dead' (Deut. 26:14)
↓
Paschal Lamb 'Mimenu'
One = prohibition on leaving leftovers; one = not breaking bones; third is free → uncircumcised cannot eat
Second Tithe 'Mimenu'
One = mourner prohibited; one = impure prohibited; third (via gezerah shavah) → uncircumcised cannot eat

💬 Is the Gezerah Shavah Valid? The One-Sided Problem

Gemara (Difficulty)
The analogy works only from one side! The Paschal lamb 'mimenu' is superfluous, but each 'mimenu' in second tithe serves its own halakhic purpose — mourner, impurity, the dead.
Gemara (Resolves)
Rav Naḥman citing Rabba bar Avuh teaches: 'These are the statutes of the land' (Lev. 18:3) — impure teruma must be burned. This frees one 'mimenu' in second tithe for the analogy.
Mar Zutra (Objects)
But perhaps 'nor did I give of it for the dead' means it is forbidden to buy a coffin or shrouds with tithe money — not an analogy!
Reish Lakish / R. Samya
'For the dead' = burial use is forbidden, but anointing a living person with impure tithe is permitted — so that 'mimenu' remains available for the gezerah shavah.
Principle A gezerah shavah is strongest when both sides have a superfluous word (מופנה משני צדדין). Here, Rabba bar Avuh's teaching frees the necessary second-tithe verse.

🌅 Three Verses of Purity — Rava citing Rav Ḥisda (Lev. 22)

Verse 1 (Lev. 22:6): 'He shall not eat of holy things unless he has bathed his flesh in water' — immersion alone, with no mention of sunset
↓
Verse 2 (Lev. 22:7): 'When the sun sets he shall be pure, and then he shall eat of the holy things' — adds the requirement of waiting for sunset
↓
Verse 3 reading: 'Then he shall be pure' (וְטָהֵר) implies even one who lacks an atonement offering may eat after sunset — permitting teruma but not sacrificial food
↓
Rava's Three-Level Map: Verse 1 = second tithe (immersion only); Verse 2 = teruma (immersion + sunset); Verse 3 context = sacrificial food (full atonement required)
↓
Why does teruma get the stricter rule? Teruma already carries the stringency of death penalty for eating while impure — so it logically receives more stringencies, not second tithe

⚖️ Stringencies Compared: Which Food Is Strictest?

מַעֲשֵׂר שֵׁנִי Second Tithe

  • Immersion alone suffices (no sunset required)
  • Acronym ה-ד-ס-ט-ב: must be brought to Jerusalem, declared, redeemed if impure, paid in valid coin, eaten within walls
  • Uncircumcised man prohibited (per R. Yitzchak)
  • Mourner prohibited from eating
  • No death penalty — less severe than teruma

תְּרוּמָה Teruma

  • Requires immersion plus sunset (tevul yom may not eat)
  • Death penalty (by Heaven) for eating while impure
  • Forbidden to non-priests and ḥalala
  • Lacks atonement = may eat after sunset (unlike sacrificial food)
  • Impure teruma must be burned, not eaten

קָדָשִׁים Sacrificial Food

  • Requires immersion + sunset + atonement offering
  • Most stringencies overall (מ-ח-פ-ז mnemonic)
  • Stricter than both second tithe and teruma
  • Zav/metzora may not eat even after sunset until atonement
  • Woman after childbirth: days must be complete before eating
