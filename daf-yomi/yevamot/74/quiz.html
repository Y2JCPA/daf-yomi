QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yitzchak, from where is derived that an uncircumcised man is prohibited from eating second tithe?
A. From an explicit verse in Deuteronomy forbidding it directly
B. From a gezerah shavah using 'mimenu' (of it) linking the Paschal lamb to second tithe
C. From the ruling of Rabbi Akiva who equates the uncircumcised with the ritually impure
D. From the fact that second tithe has the same laws as the Paschal lamb in every respect
Correct: B — Rabbi Yitzchak derives this via gezerah shavah: 'mimenu' (of it) appears three times in the Paschal lamb context and three times in the second tithe confession (Deut. 26:14), one from each being free to establish the analogy.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava citing Rav Ḥisda, what does a tevul yom (one who immersed but has not yet waited for sunset) require before eating teruma?
A. Nothing — immersion alone suffices for teruma
B. Immersion plus bringing a full atonement offering
C. Immersion and waiting until nightfall (sunset)
D. Immersion, sunset, and an atonement offering
Correct: C — Rava derives from three verses that teruma requires immersion plus waiting for sunset. A tevul yom who has immersed but not yet waited for sunset may not eat teruma. Only sacrificial food requires the additional step of an atonement offering.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Reish Lakish (citing Rabbi Samya) derive from the verse 'nor did I give of it for the dead' (Deut. 26:14)?
A. That second tithe may never be used after the tithe-owner has died
B. That impure second tithe may be used for anointing a living person
C. That second tithe funds may be used to purchase a coffin for the deceased
D. That an acute mourner may not eat second tithe
Correct: B — Reish Lakish derives that impure second tithe may be used for anointing living persons. The verse prohibits using it for the dead (burial), but it is permitted for anointing. This also frees the verse to serve the gezerah shavah.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the proof from the baraita about acute mourners being forbidden second tithe?
A. Because the baraita discusses only the Paschal lamb, not second tithe
B. Because the baraita follows Rabbi Akiva's school, which uniquely equates uncircumcised men with the ritually impure — a minority view
C. Because acute mourners are a special category with no implications for uncircumcised men
D. Because the baraita was composed after the Temple's destruction and lacks authority
Correct: B — The Gemara explains this baraita was taught from the school of Rabbi Akiva, who groups the uncircumcised together with the ritually impure. Since Rabbi Akiva's position is a minority view, it cannot serve as a general halakhic proof.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rava argues that teruma — not second tithe — receives the stricter purity condition (requiring sunset). What is his primary reasoning?
A. Teruma is more commonly eaten, requiring extra care to prevent violations
B. Second tithe has more individual stringencies (the ה-ד-ס-ט-ב acronym) and needs no more
C. Teruma already carries the stringency of the death penalty for eating while impure, making additional stringency appropriate
D. Teruma is eaten outside Jerusalem, where oversight is weaker
Correct: C — Rava argues that since teruma is already subject to the stringency of death (by Heaven) for one who eats it impurely, it is logically appropriate to assign teruma the additional stringency of requiring sunset — not second tithe, which carries no such penalty.
