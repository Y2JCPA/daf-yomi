5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
In Shmuel's three-brothers case, if the yavam performs ḥalitza with the two sisters, what is the status of their rival wives?
A. The rival wives are fully exempt from levirate marriage
B. The rival wives are NOT exempt and must themselves perform ḥalitza
C. The rival wives may enter levirate marriage with the yavam
D. The rival wives are exempt only if the levirate bond is substantial
Correct: B — Shmuel rules that ḥalitza with the sisters does not exempt the rival wives. Because the sisters are in a forbidden category (rivals of a co-wife who is their sister), their ḥalitza is defective and cannot release their own rivals from the levirate bond.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, why does ḥalitza with the sisters fail to exempt their rival wives — even though Shmuel holds the levirate bond is substantial?
A. Because Shmuel secretly follows the view that the levirate bond is not substantial
B. Because the rival wives are considered primary yevamot with a stronger bond
C. Because the sisters, being in a forbidden category, cannot perform ḥalitza that releases their rivals
D. Because Beit Shammai's opinion requires a separate ḥalitza for each woman
Correct: C — Rav Ashi: Shmuel consistently holds the levirate bond IS substantial. The sisters' ḥalitza is ineffective for releasing rivals not because of the bond's strength, but because the sisters themselves are a forbidden category — rivals of their own sister who is the yavam's co-wife.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Regarding precedence between a get recipient and a ma'amar recipient for ḥalitza, what does Rav Ashi conclude?
A. The get recipient clearly has priority because the get is more powerful
B. The ma'amar recipient clearly has priority because the bond is stronger
C. Neither clearly precedes the other — both can be followed by the other without nullification
D. The earlier action always has priority over the later one
Correct: C — Rav Ashi proves from a disputed case that a get does not nullify a subsequent ma'amar, and a ma'amar does not nullify a subsequent get. This shows neither instrument has clear precedence — the dilemma remains unresolved.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, what is the ruling when the FIRST of two yevama-sisters dies before the yavam took any action?
A. The yavam is permitted to take the second sister in levirate marriage
B. The yavam is permanently prohibited from taking the second sister
C. The second sister must perform ḥalitza but may not enter levirate marriage
D. The ruling depends on whether the levirate bond is considered substantial
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan rules the second sister is permanently prohibited. When she originally fell before the yavam, the verse 'her brother-in-law will have intercourse with her' could not apply to her (she was a rival of her own sister). That initial prohibition at the moment of zikka is permanent — even though the first sister later died.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rabbi Yosei bar Ḥanina challenged Rabbi Yoḥanan from a mishna about four brothers and two sisters. How did Rabbi Yoḥanan respond?
A. He cited Beit Shammai's lenient opinion to resolve the contradiction
B. He said he does not know who taught 'sisters' in that mishna — he could not reconcile it
C. He reinterpreted the mishna to refer to sisters-in-law, not actual sisters
D. He accepted the challenge and withdrew his ruling
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan responded: 'I do not know who taught: sisters.' He was uncertain about the correct version of that mishna and could not find any reasonable explanation of it according to any known opinion — effectively acknowledging the difficulty without resolving it.
