Three-Day Identification Rule & Water's Effect

ScenarioCan Identify the Body?Reason
Body seen within 3 days on land✅ YesFacial features still recognizable
Body seen after 3 days on land❌ No (Rabbis)Face becomes bloated and unrecognizable
Drowned, pulled out after 3+ days✅ Yes — if viewed immediatelyWater contracts the body, preserving features
Drowned, pulled out but viewing delayed❌ NoBody bloats rapidly once removed from water
Body has a wound in water⚠️ ComplicatedWater aggravates wounds, causing swelling
R' Yehuda ben Bava "Not every person, nor every place, nor every hour is identical" — the three-day rule is not absolute. The Gemara concludes he rules leniently: sometimes identification is valid even after three days.

Water With a Visible End — May His Wife Remarry?

R' Meir
Whether the water has a visible end or not, his wife is prohibited from remarrying. A man once fell into the Great Cistern and emerged after three days alive!
Rabbis
If the water has a visible end (מים שיש להם סוף) — where one can see all shores — his wife is permitted. He would have been seen emerging.
Abaye
"Visible end" means: one stands in one place and can see the shore in all four directions.
Rabbis (to R' Meir)
The Great Cistern incident is a miracle (מעשה ניסים) — one does not derive halakha from miraculous acts!
Key Principle אין מזכירין מעשה ניסים — We do not cite miraculous events to establish general halakhic rules.

A man drowns in the lake of Samkei
↓
Rav Sheila permits the wife to remarry
↓
Rav to Shmuel: "Let us excommunicate him!"
↓
Shmuel: "Let us first send a message and clarify his reasoning"
↓
Rav Sheila confirms: Lake of Samkei has no visible end, wife should be forbidden
↓
Rav Sheila explains: witnesses testified the man had been attacked by a sea creature — special circumstances justified the ruling
Judicial Caution Shmuel's approach: investigate before punishing a scholar. Always seek to understand the reasoning behind a ruling before condemning it.

Fatal Falls — When Can We Testify He Died?

🔥 Blazing Furnace

  • Fell in → May testify he died
  • No dispute — certain death

🫒 Cauldron of Oil

  • Oil splashes into fire → ignites more
  • May testify he died
  • R' Aḥa and Rabbis agree

🍷 Cauldron of Wine

  • R' Aḥa: Wine extinguishes fire → cannot testify
  • Rabbis: At first it extinguishes, but eventually reignites
  • Conclusion: May testify he died

🐍 Pit of Snakes

  • May testify he died — they will bite
  • Concern: perhaps he is a snake charmer (חבר)?
  • Rejected: the pressure of falling on them provokes attack regardless
Principle In each case, the Sages assess whether survival is naturally possible or would require a miracle.

The Daughter of Neḥunya the Well Digger

סביביו נשערה מאד (תהלים נ:ג)
Aggadic Interlude Neḥunya's daughter fell into the Great Cistern. R' Ḥanina ben Dosa was notified and prayed. After the first hour: "She is at peace." After the second hour: "She is at peace." After the third hour: "She has ascended."

She reported that a ram led by an old man rescued her — understood as Avraham Avinu and the ram of the Akeida.

R' Ḥanina explained: "Could it be that the endeavor Neḥunya is engaged in — digging wells for the public — his offspring would stumble upon its fruits?"

Yet R' Abba noted: Neḥunya's son died of thirst. God is exacting with the righteous (מדקדק עם סביביו כחוט השערה) — even to a hairsbreadth.
