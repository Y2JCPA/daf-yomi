Yevamot 94 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject Rav Sheshet's proof that one witness is credible for a yevama?
A. Because the mishna is dealing with edei hazamah (conspiring witnesses), not a simple contradiction
B. Because the mishna follows Rabbi Akiva who requires stricter proof
C. Because the woman herself is not credible in that case
D. Because the mishna only applies to a married woman, not a yevama
Correct: A — The Gemara explains the mishna involves two sets of two witnesses, where the second pair are edei hazamah — they testified the first witnesses were elsewhere. This completely disqualifies the first pair, so the case doesn't prove anything about one-witness credibility.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rava's kal va-chomer (a fortiori) argument fail?
A. Because a yevama's prohibition is actually more severe than adultery
B. Because the woman herself disproves it — she's believed for karet but not for a regular prohibition, showing the severity logic doesn't hold
C. Because the Torah explicitly requires two witnesses for a yevama
D. Because Rava confused the prohibition of a yevama with that of a married woman
Correct: B — The woman herself breaks the kal va-chomer: she's trusted for the karet-level prohibition (married woman) but NOT for the lighter yevama prohibition. This is because hatred toward the yavam may lead her (and by extension, the witness she relies on) to be careless in verifying the facts.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If a man is told his wife died and marries her sister, what happens to his original marriage?
A. His wife becomes forbidden to him as the sister of his divorcée
B. His wife remains permitted to him — the erroneous marriage doesn't affect it
C. His wife requires a new kiddushin before they can resume relations
D. His wife becomes forbidden unless he gives the sister a get
Correct: B — The erroneous marriage to the sister is treated as mere licentious relations. The Gemara establishes that we do not say 'since the brother-in-law's wife is forbidden to her husband, his own wife should be forbidden to him.' His original marriage remains unaffected.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rav Giddel limit Rabbi Akiva's ruling that a wife's sister requires a get?
A. It only applies when the wife actually died, not when she was reported dead
B. It only applies to betrothal (kiddushin), not to full marriage (nisuin)
C. It only applies in Israel, not in the diaspora
D. It only applies when both sisters are present in the same city
Correct: B — Rav Giddel explains Rabbi Akiva's concern is about appearances: onlookers might think there was a condition in the betrothal that was unfulfilled, making the second marriage appear valid. But with full marriage (nisuin), no one assumes conditions — so no get is needed, and even Rabbi Akiva agrees.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why is a woman not believed when she says her yavam died, even though she IS believed about her husband's death?
A. Because the Torah requires two witnesses specifically for yibum
B. Because she may hate the yavam and therefore not investigate carefully before remarrying
C. Because a yavam's death must be confirmed by a beit din
D. Because a woman has no legal standing to testify about her yavam
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that sometimes a woman hates her yavam, so she won't be careful to verify the report of his death before remarrying. For her husband's death, she is more careful because the consequences of error (being forced to leave both husbands) directly affect her. But she is less concerned about the consequences for her children from a possibly invalid second marriage.
