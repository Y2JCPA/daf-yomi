QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye, what is the sharecropper's right regarding a sycamore tree growing in his field?
A. He may take both the beams and the full enhancement value
B. He may take the beams but not the enhancement value
C. He may not take the beams, but he receives the value of the tree's enhancement
D. He has no rights to either the beams or the enhancement value
Correct: C — Abaye distinguishes between the physical beams (which belong to the owner) and the monetary value of growth during tenancy. The sharecropper invested labor; he receives the value of that enhancement — not the beams themselves.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the interpretation that the Jubilee-year baraita appraises vegetables and beets for the departing sharecropper?
A. Because courts cannot appraise perishable produce
B. Because vegetables in the Jubilee Year become ownerless, making appraisal meaningless
C. Because the sharecropper already ate the vegetables
D. Because vegetables are not subject to Jubilee-year laws
Correct: B — The Jubilee Year, like the Sabbatical Year, renders produce ownerless (hefker). Appraising vegetables for the sharecropper when they already belong to everyone would be pointless.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When Rav Pappa bar Shmuel ruled that an owner owes a departing planter 'one-quarter,' what did Rav Ashi clarify this actually means?
A. One-quarter of the planter's own half share
B. One-quarter of the owner's two-thirds share, equaling one-sixth of total yield
C. One-quarter of the following year's projected harvest
D. One-quarter of the total value of the field itself
Correct: B — The owner's portion is two-thirds of the yield; one-quarter of that equals one-sixth of the whole. Rav Ashi demonstrates this interpretation keeps the accounting balanced — an 'actual quarter' would make the homeowner bear an unjust additional loss.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
A planter stipulated 'If I ruin the vineyard I will leave,' then caused damage while some enhancement remained. What is Rava's ruling?
A. He leaves but receives full enhancement value as compensation
B. He leaves and receives half the enhancement, split fairly
C. He forfeits enhancement rights entirely due to his stipulation
D. He must repair the damage before he may leave
Correct: C — Rava rules the stipulation forfeits enhancement. By violating his undertaking the planter loses any claim, analogous to the Mishna (104a) where one who lets the field lie fallow pays with best-quality land — a penalty clause in the original agreement governs.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When a mortgaged orchard naturally ages and stops producing fruit halfway through the mortgage term, what does Rava rule?
A. The creditor may extend the mortgage to compensate for lost profits
B. The debtor must replant the orchard at his own expense
C. Both parties share the loss and split the remaining wood
D. The creditor suffers the loss and the orchard returns to the debtor
Correct: D — Rava rules natural aging is the creditor's misfortune (ones). He accepted the risk when taking the mortgage. The baraita about chopped-down trees is distinguished — that refers to premature, unnatural loss, not the organic end of the orchard's productive life.
