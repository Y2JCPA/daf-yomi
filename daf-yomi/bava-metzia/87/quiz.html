QUESTION 1 OF 5
What principle does Rabbi Elazar derive from the angels' reluctance to enter Lot's home?
A. One may decline a lesser person's request but not a great person's
B. Hospitality to strangers is a biblical commandment
C. Angels are forbidden from accepting human invitations
D. One must always accept an invitation to a meal
Correct: A — Rabbi Elazar derives from 'and he urged them greatly' (Gen. 19:3) that one may decline a lesser person's request, but not that of a great man — the greatness of the host compels acceptance.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the School of Rabbi Yishmael learn from God's altered report of Sarah's laughter?
A. Women are more prone to doubt than men
B. Sarah sinned gravely by laughing at God's promise
C. Peace is so important that even God altered the truth to preserve it
D. Abraham was unaware of Sarah's private reaction
Correct: C — Sarah said 'my lord [Abraham] is old,' but God reported to Abraham only 'I am old' — omitting her reference to Abraham to preserve peace between husband and wife.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What historic benefit did Jacob's prayer bring into the world?
A. He was the first patriarch to pray for children
B. Illness before death, allowing people time to prepare
C. Visible aging to distinguish elders from the young
D. The ability to receive divine prophecy while ill
Correct: B — Before Jacob, people died suddenly with no warning. Jacob prayed for mercy and pre-death illness was introduced, allowing one to set affairs in order — first recorded in 'Behold, your father is ill' (Gen. 48:1).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Shmuel use the word 'sickle' (Deut. 23:26) to expand a laborer's eating rights?
A. It limits eating to grain crops harvested with a sickle
B. It teaches eating is only permitted after all harvesting is done
C. It extends the eating right to any produce requiring a sickle — i.e., all field crops
D. It prohibits using a sickle while eating from the produce
Correct: C — Shmuel derives that 'sickle' is not needed to teach the harvest-timing rule (that comes from 'vessel'), so it must extend the laborer's eating right to any produce harvested with a sickle.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does the term 'kenafshekha' (at your own pleasure, Deut. 23:25) teach about the laborer?
A. The laborer may eat as much as he desires without limit
B. The laborer's eating rights are identical to those of the vineyard owner
C. The laborer must eat during designated break times only
D. The laborer may take food home for his family as well
Correct: B — 'Kenafshekha' means 'as you are' — just as the vineyard owner eats his own produce freely, so too may the laborer. The laborer's right mirrors the owner's own relationship to the produce.
