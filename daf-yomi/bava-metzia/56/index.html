Bava Metzia 56: What Is Subject to Ona'ah?

Leviticus 25:14
"If you sell to your colleague an item that is sold…"
↓
What qualifies as an item sold from hand to hand?
INCLUDED
✅ Movable property
✅ Rentals (Abaye)
✅ Used documents sold as paper
EXCLUDED
❌ Land (not movable)
❌ Slaves (like land)
❌ Documents (no intrinsic value)
❌ Consecrated property ('his brother')
KEY VERSE The Gemara derives all ona'ah exclusions from precise readings of Leviticus 25:14, examining each word to determine which transactions are covered.

Second-Tithe Coin Redemption Under Duress

🪙 One holds second-tithe silver coins and needs to redeem them
↓
⚠️ Under duress, the Sages permit desacralizing silver onto copper coins
↓
🔄 One must then redesacralize the copper back onto silver coins
↓
📏 Under duress — yes; not under duress — no (Torah law)
↓
🌾 For demai (rabbinic obligation), Rabbi Meir permits this ab initio
INFERENCE Rabbi Meir is more lenient with rabbinic obligations than with Torah obligations — a key principle debated throughout the sugya.

Is Rabbi Meir Lenient or Stringent on Demai?

Shmuel
Rabbi Meir is lenient on rabbinic law — he doesn't equate it with Torah law.
Rav Yosef
Rabbi Meir is lenient on redemption of demai, but stringent on consumption — a proprietor must tithe demai before selling!
Ravina
But the Mishnah says one may tithe from a baker's bread across different molds! That's lenient on consumption too!
Abaye
The difficulties involve different severities — death at Heaven's hand vs. court-imposed capital punishment vs. mere prohibition. You can't compare them!
RESOLUTION The Gemara carefully distinguishes between different levels of severity when evaluating whether Rabbi Meir treats rabbinic law like Torah law.

Does 'His Hand' Mean Literal Hand or Possession?

Rabba bar Memel
"From his hand" just means possession! See Numbers 21:26 — 'took all his land from his hand' — he wasn't holding land in his hand!
Gemara
But the Torah about theft says 'found in his hand' — and needs a special double verb (himmatze timmatze) to expand beyond the literal hand!
Gemara
Same with get (divorce) — 'place in her hand' needs 'and place' to include roof, courtyard, enclosure.
Gemara
Conclusion: Every 'his hand' in Torah means literal hand, except where context makes that impossible (like land).
PRINCIPLE The default reading of יָדוֹ (his hand) in the Torah is literal. Only explicit textual expansions or logical impossibility extend it to mean general possession.

Rabbi Zeira's Dilemma: Is Rental Subject to Ona'ah?

One person rents property to another at an unfair price
↓
Is rental considered a 'sale' under Leviticus 25:14?
Perhaps Not
Torah says 'if you sell' — rental is not a permanent sale
Abaye: Yes!
Verse says 'sell an item that is sold' — not 'sold forever'.
Rental = sale for a limited period
HALAKHA Abaye establishes that שכירות — rental is a sale for its day (שכירות ליומיה מכירה היא). Therefore ona'ah applies to rentals.
