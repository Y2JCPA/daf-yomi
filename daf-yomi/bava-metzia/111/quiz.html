Bava Metzia 111 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav, when may a laborer who worked during the day collect his wages?
A. All day and all of the following night
B. All that day until sunset only
C. Within three days of completing work
D. Only at the exact moment of completing work
Correct: B — Rav reads the mishna disjunctively: a day laborer collects wages all that day (until sunset), while a night laborer collects wages all that night (until dawn). Each shift has its own distinct and limited window.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How many negative prohibitions does the baraita say an employer violates by withholding a laborer's wages?
A. Two
B. Three
C. Four
D. Five
Correct: D — The baraita states that withholding wages violates five negative prohibitions and one positive mitzva, drawn from verses in Leviticus 19:13 and Deuteronomy 24:14–15. Some apply by day and others by night.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rava's final ruling on the difference between 'oppression' (עֹשֶׁק) and 'stealing' (גָּזֵל) in the context of delayed wages?
A. Oppression is rabbinic; stealing is biblical
B. Oppression requires witnesses; stealing requires an oath
C. There is no practical difference — the Torah listed both to create double liability
D. Oppression applies to day laborers; stealing applies to night laborers
Correct: C — Rava rules that oppression and stealing are halakhically identical here. The Torah listed both categories so that an employer who withholds wages becomes liable for violating both prohibitions simultaneously — not because they describe distinct acts.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which tannaic school does Rava identify as the author of the mishna, and what verse is the basis for including animal and utensil rentals?
A. School of Rabbi Akiva, from 'Do not oppress your neighbor'
B. School of Rabbi Yishmael, from 'in your land' (Deut. 24:14)
C. Beit Shammai, from a verbal analogy of 'hired laborer'
D. Rabbi Yosei son of Rabbi Yehuda, from 'for he is poor'
Correct: B — Rava identifies the tanna as from the School of Rabbi Yishmael, who derives inclusion of animal and utensil rentals from the phrase 'in your land' in Deuteronomy 24:14, and includes the ger toshav from 'your stranger that is in your land.'
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why are the marketplace workers of Sura (per Rabba bar Rav Huna) exempt from the biblical prohibition of delaying wages?
A. They receive wages in advance and no payment is owed
B. Everyone knows they rely on the market day's proceeds — workers implicitly accept deferred payment
C. They work for a community fund to which different laws apply
D. Their employer is a ger toshav and the prohibition does not extend to him
Correct: B — Rabba bar Rav Huna taught that because it is universally understood that these employers rely on the market day's income to pay their workers, the workers implicitly consent to this arrangement, removing the biblical violation of delayed wages.
