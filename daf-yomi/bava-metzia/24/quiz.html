Bava Metzia 24 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Mar Zutra suspect a particular student of stealing the silver goblet?
A. The student was seen near the goblet
B. The student dried his hands on another person's cloak
C. The student had a history of theft
D. The student confessed immediately
Correct: B — Mar Zutra reasoned that someone who disrespects another's property (drying hands on someone else's garment) would also not hesitate to steal.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Shimon ben Elazar, why does a finder keep items found in a main thoroughfare?
A. The items are presumed ownerless (hefker)
B. The owner despairs (ye'ush) due to the multitudes
C. The Torah grants them to the finder
D. The items have no distinguishing marks by definition
Correct: B — R' Shimon ben Elazar's reasoning is that in crowded public areas, the owner gives up hope of recovery because so many people pass through.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Shmuel reconcile telling the finder both 'it belongs to you' and 'you must return it if a Jew gives a siman'?
A. The first ruling is for gentiles, the second for Jews
B. Strict law says keep it; returning it is beyond the letter of the law (lifnim mishurat hadin)
C. The first applies to coins, the second to vessels
D. The first is the Rabbanan's view, the second is R' Shimon ben Elazar's
Correct: B — Shmuel explains that by strict law the finder keeps it, but returning it when the Jewish owner identifies it is acting beyond the letter of the law — citing his own father's precedent.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Abaye permit keeping meat snatched by a kite from a Jewish-majority marketplace?
A. The marketplace was actually majority-gentile
B. A kite snatching an item is comparable to the tide of the sea — automatic ye'ush
C. The meat lost its kosher status and was worthless
D. Abaye follows R' Shimon ben Elazar even in Jewish-majority areas for all items
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that an item taken by a kite is analogous to being swept away by the sea — the owner automatically despairs regardless of the demographic majority.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Rav Yehuda require proclamation for coins found in the Biran River, despite its gentile-majority area?
A. The coins had a distinguishing mark (siman)
B. Jews dammed and dredged the river, so the owner doesn't despair
C. Rav Yehuda always rejects R' Shimon ben Elazar
D. River finds are never subject to ye'ush
Correct: B — The Biran River was uniquely maintained by Jews — they dammed it and dredged it — so the owner could reasonably expect recovery, preventing ye'ush despite the gentile majority.
