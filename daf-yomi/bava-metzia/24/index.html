Mar Zutra & the Silver Goblet

כסא דכספא אגניב מאושפיזא דמר זוטרא חסידא
Narrative A silver goblet was stolen from Mar Zutra Ḥasida's host. He noticed a Torah student who washed his hands and dried them on another person's cloak. Mar Zutra said: 'This one does not care about others' property' — he confronted the student, who confessed to the theft.

R' Shimon ben Elazar: Items the Finder Keeps

Rescued from Danger

  • 🦁 Taken from a lion, bear, or leopard
  • 🌊 Recovered from the tide of the sea
  • 🌊 Found in a river flood
  • Owner despairs — these belong to the finder

Found in Public Places

  • 🛣️ Main thoroughfare (seratya)
  • 🏛️ Large plaza (pelatya)
  • 📍 Any place where multitudes gather
  • Owner despairs due to crowd traffic
Principle In both categories, the legal basis is ye'ush (owner's despair) — the owner gives up hope of recovery.

Marketplace Encounters: Letter vs Spirit of the Law

Rav Yehuda
If one found a purse here in this gentile-majority market, what is the halakha?
Shmuel
It belongs to the finder.
Rav Yehuda
And if a Jew provides a siman?
Shmuel
He must return it.
Rav Yehuda
But those are contradictory rulings!
Shmuel
By strict law it's his. Returning it is lifnim mishurat hadin — beyond the letter of the law.
Precedent Shmuel cites his own father, who found donkeys in the desert and returned them after 12 months — acting beyond the strict requirement of the law.
