Bava Metzia 76: The Middleman, The Wage, and The Walkout

Mishna: "They deceived one another" — who deceived whom?
↓
A middleman (laborer) was sent to hire workers and misquoted the wage
↓
Employer said 4, middleman said 3
Laborers accepted — but Proverbs 3:27 creates a moral grievance
Employer said 3, middleman said 4
Laborers expected more — middleman is liable if he assumed personal obligation
↓
Separate question: What if laborers renege mid-job?
↓
No financial loss
Court appraises work done; employer has only a grievance
Financial loss
Employer may hire replacements at laborers' expense — even 40-50 dinars
KEY PRINCIPLE Whoever reneges or changes terms is at a disadvantage — but the Gemara carefully distinguishes moral grievance (ta'aromet) from enforceable monetary claims.
3 / 9

Who Deceived Whom?

Gemara
The Mishna says "they deceived one another" — not "reneged." This implies a middleman deceived the laborers about wages.
Gemara
Scenario: Employer told the middleman "Hire for 4 dinars" but the middleman told the laborers "3 dinars."
Challenge
They accepted the 3-dinar terms! What grievance do they have?
Answer
They can cite Proverbs 3:27: "Do not withhold good from him to whom it is due, when it is in the power of your hand to do it."
INSIGHT Even when workers knowingly accepted lower terms, they have a moral grievance if the employer was willing to pay more and the middleman pocketed the difference.

The Middleman's Liability

Employer says: "Hire laborers for 3 dinars"
Middleman tells laborers: "You're hired for 4"
↓
Did the middleman say "Your wage is on me" or "Your wage is on the employer"?
"On me" (personal guarantee)
Middleman pays the extra dinar from his own pocket
"On the employer"
Employer refuses to pay 4 — laborers have a grievance against the middleman only
↓
Proof from Baraita: One who hires a laborer and shows him the wrong field must pay full wages, then recover benefit from the field owner
RULE One who says "Your wage is incumbent upon me" creates a personal obligation and must pay the stated amount.

Why Do the Laborers Have a Grievance?

Answer 1: Lost Opportunity

  • Some employers pay 4, others pay 3
  • "Had you not said 4, we would have searched and found a 4-dinar employer"
  • The middleman caused them to lose a better-paying job

Answer 2: Homeowner Dignity

  • These are landowners (ba'alei batim) who occasionally hire out
  • "For only 3 dinars, it would have been beneath our dignity to work for another"
  • They only agreed because they thought it was 4

Answer 3: Extra Effort

  • "Because you said 4, we did higher-quality work"
  • Example: tilling land where quality isn't immediately visible
  • Ditch filled with water — employer can't verify the extra effort
NOTE The Gemara offers multiple explanations because the grievance must be substantial enough to warrant mention in the Mishna.

Appraising Partial Work: Tanna Kamma vs. Rabbi Dosa

Tanna Kamma
We appraise what they have done (ma she'asu). If the completed half is now worth 6 dinars (1.5 sela), the employer pays 1 sela (the original rate for that portion).
R' Dosa
We appraise what remains to be done (ma she'atid le'he'asot). If finishing costs 6 dinars (1.5 sela), the first workers get only half a sela (2 sela total minus 1.5 sela for completion).
Tanna Kamma
If the completed half is now worth only 1 sela — the workers still receive 1 sela.
R' Dosa
If the remaining work now costs only 1 sela — the first workers receive 1 sela.
DIFFERENCE Both methods protect the employer from overpaying, but R' Dosa's method focuses on replacement cost, while the Tanna Kamma focuses on the value of completed labor.
