Bava Metzia 76 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, who is the 'deceiver' referred to in the Mishna's phrase 'they deceived one another'?
A. The employer who changed the wage
B. A middleman laborer who misquoted the wage to other laborers
C. The laborers who demanded higher pay after agreeing
D. The court that miscalculated the appraisal
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the employer sent one laborer to hire others, and that laborer (the middleman) deceived them by misquoting the wage — either higher or lower than what the employer authorized.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
If a middleman told laborers 'Your wage is incumbent upon the employer' but the employer authorized a lower amount, who bears liability?
A. The employer must pay the higher amount
B. The middleman must pay the difference
C. The laborers split the loss with the employer
D. The laborers have only a grievance against the middleman
Correct: D — When the middleman attributes the obligation to the employer but the employer never agreed to that amount, the laborers have only a grievance (ta'aromet) against the middleman — not an enforceable monetary claim against the employer.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What verse does the Gemara cite to explain why laborers who accepted 3 dinars have a grievance when the employer authorized 4?
A. 'Love your neighbor as yourself' (Leviticus 19:18)
B. 'Do not withhold good from him to whom it is due' (Proverbs 3:27)
C. 'You shall not steal' (Exodus 20:13)
D. 'Justice, justice shall you pursue' (Deuteronomy 16:20)
Correct: B — The Gemara invokes Proverbs 3:27 — even though the laborers knowingly accepted 3 dinars, the middleman should not have withheld the extra dinar that the employer authorized.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When laborers renege mid-job and there IS financial loss (davar ha'aved), what may the employer do?
A. He can only file a grievance
B. He must accept whatever work was completed
C. He may hire replacements at the original laborers' expense, even up to 40-50 dinars
D. He may seize the laborers' personal property as collateral
Correct: C — In cases of financial loss, the employer has extraordinary power: he may hire replacement workers at any price — even 40-50 dinars — and charge the cost to the laborers who reneged, to prevent irreversible damage.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rabbi Dosa's appraisal method differ from the Tanna Kamma's when laborers quit mid-contract?
A. R' Dosa appraises what remains to be done; Tanna Kamma appraises what was already done
B. R' Dosa always pays more than the Tanna Kamma
C. R' Dosa uses market rate; Tanna Kamma uses the contract rate
D. R' Dosa requires the laborers to finish; Tanna Kamma lets them quit
Correct: A — The Tanna Kamma appraises the value of completed work (ma she'asu), while Rabbi Dosa appraises the cost of the remaining work (ma she'atid le'he'asot). Both methods protect the employer, but they calculate from opposite directions.
