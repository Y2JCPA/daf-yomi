Bava Metzia 31 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, why does the verse 'every lost item of your brother' use the word 'every'?
A. To include an obligation to protect another's land from damage
B. To include lost animals in addition to lost objects
C. To include non-Jews' lost property
D. To include items worth less than a perutah
Correct: A — Rava derives from 'every lost item' that the obligation extends beyond movable property to include protecting another's land — such as blocking floodwater heading toward a field.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rava resolve the apparent contradiction about an animal running on the path?
A. He distinguishes between large and small animals
B. He says the Mishna uses the literary style of 'its counterpart tells about it'
C. He distinguishes based on the direction the animal faces — toward the city vs. toward the field
D. He says one clause refers to daytime and the other to nighttime
Correct: C — Rava explains that an animal running toward the field (away from the city) is lost property, while one running toward the city is heading home and is not lost.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say the baraita about sheaves needing to dry on the land is NOT obvious?
A. Because sheaves are too heavy to move
B. Because you might think sheaves still needing the land have the legal status of land itself
C. Because the owner might not want them moved
D. Because they belong to the landowner, not the farmer
Correct: B — Since the sheaves still need the land for drying, one might think their status is like the land itself (and the return obligation wouldn't apply). The baraita teaches that they remain independent movable property.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Torah need TWO separate verses about returning collateral (hashev tashiv and ḥavol taḥbol)?
A. One applies to men and one to women
B. One applies to Israelites and one to converts
C. One refers to a day garment and one to a night garment
D. One applies to monetary loans and one to food loans
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that one verse teaches the obligation to return a night garment (for sleeping) and the other teaches returning a day garment (for wearing during the day).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rabbi Elazar ben Azarya hold regarding the doubled verb 'ha'aneik ta'anik' (furnishing a freed slave)?
A. It teaches you must furnish the slave twice
B. It means nothing extra — the Torah simply speaks in the language of people
C. It teaches the obligation applies even to a non-Hebrew slave
D. It teaches the obligation applies even if the slave ran away
Correct: B — R' Elazar ben Azarya holds that doubled verbs are normal emphatic speech (dibra Torah ki-leshon b'nei adam) and no additional halakha should be derived from the repetition.
