Must You Protect Another's Land from Flooding?

Rava
"Every lost item of your brother" includes land — you must build a barrier to stop water from flooding his field
Rav Ḥananya
A baraita supports you! It says one must block water heading toward another's field
Rava
Don't use that baraita to support me — it may refer to a field with sheaves, not the land itself
Gemara
If it's about sheaves, that's obvious! Why state it?
Gemara
The sheaves still need the land to dry — you might think they have the status of land. The baraita teaches: no, they are independent items
Principle Sheaves drying on a field are movable property, not part of the land — even though they still need the land

Rava Resolves the Mishna's Contradictions

The Mishna seems to contradict itself: is an animal running on the path lost or not?
↓
Running AWAY from city
Face toward the field → Lost property (fleeing)
Running TOWARD city
Face toward the city → Not lost (heading home)
↓
Grazing among vineyards — is it lost or not?
↓
Loss of Animal
Animal itself is not in danger while grazing → not lost
Loss = Land Damage
Animal damages the vineyard → obligation to prevent loss of the land
Rava vs. Abaye Abaye explains the Mishna uses a literary style (re'iyah magadato) — each clause teaches about the other. Rava rejects this and distinguishes by direction and type of loss

Abaye vs. Rava: Reading the Mishna

Abaye

  • Uses literary principle: "its counterpart tells about it" (re'iyah magadato)
  • Grazing on path = not lost → same for grazing in vineyards
  • Running in vineyards = lost → same for running on path
  • Simple, symmetric reading

Rava

  • Rejects Abaye: Mishna should teach the lenient case if using that style
  • Running on path: depends on direction — toward city (not lost) vs. toward field (lost)
  • Grazing in vineyards: depends on type of loss — animal safe, but land damaged
  • Nuanced, case-by-case reading
Rava's Challenge If Abaye is right, the Mishna could state it more efficiently with a kal va-ḥomer (a fortiori) — running on path is lost, all the more so in vineyards

Compound Verbs (Amud B): Expanding Torah Obligations

⚔️ Mot yumat — execute a murderer by any means if standard method unavailable
↓

↓

Dissenting View R' Elazar ben Azarya: The doubled verbs are simply how people talk — "the Torah speaks in the language of people" (dibra Torah ki-leshon b'nei adam) — no extra law is derived
