QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, why is the time of borrowing (rather than injury) the decisive moment for the owner-presence exemption?
A. Because the owner can only testify about what he witnessed at the borrowing
B. Because the verse 'when a man borrows' places liability at the act of borrowing, making that moment foundational
C. Because the Sages ruled that presence at injury is too difficult to verify
D. Because Rabbi Yoshiya and Rabbi Yonatan both agreed on this interpretation
Correct: B — Rav Ashi derives from the verse's structure: 'when a man borrows' — the borrowing is the act that creates liability, so the owner's presence at that moment is what determines exemption.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rami bar Hama asks about borrowing an animal to engage in bestiality. What is the core halakhic question?
A. Whether a prohibited act can ever create a valid legal agreement
B. Whether the borrower's liability requires that the purpose be monetarily valuable and permitted
C. Whether the owner is considered complicit and thus forfeits his rights
D. Whether the animal itself becomes prohibited for use and must be valued differently
Correct: B — The dilemma is whether liability requires a purpose with monetary value AND permissibility, or only monetary value. Bestiality has neither — but the question is which criterion matters.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rava resolves the question of a husband's status regarding his wife's usufruct property by saying he is like a:
A. Borrower — he uses it for free
B. Renter — he pays through ketubbah obligations
C. Guardian — he protects it on her behalf
D. Buyer — he fully acquires the usufruct rights
Correct: D — Rava rules that a husband is like a buyer (based on Rabbi Yosei b. Rabbi Hanina), fully acquiring the usufruct rights — he is therefore liable like an owner, not like a borrower or renter.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Regarding מְעִילָה (misuse of consecrated property) when a wife's consecrated usufruct property passes to the husband — when does Rava say the husband becomes liable?
A. At the moment of marriage, when he acquires the property
B. When the wife first designates the property as usufruct
C. Only when he actually takes and spends the money for himself
D. When the court's ordinance formally transfers ownership
Correct: C — Rava rules that the husband is liable for מְעִילָה only when he actually takes and spends the money, analogous to one who inadvertently spends consecrated coins. Passive acquisition alone is insufficient.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If a borrowed animal dies from ordinary labor it was doing for the borrower, what is the halakha according to Rava?
A. The borrower is fully liable, since all accidents during borrowing are his responsibility
B. The borrower is liable only for the depreciation in value, not the full animal
C. The borrower is exempt, because he can say 'I borrowed it for this work and it died doing that work'
D. The borrower is exempt only if he can prove the animal had a pre-existing weakness
Correct: C — Rava rules the borrower is exempt when an animal dies from normal labor, because he can legitimately say to the owner: 'I borrowed it to work with — it died while performing exactly that purpose.'
