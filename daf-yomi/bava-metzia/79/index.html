⚖️ Rav vs. Shmuel: The Donkey Carcass Dispute

Rav
If carcass proceeds are sufficient to purchase a replacement donkey, the owner MUST do so and continue the journey — the renter deserves completion of the service.
Shmuel
Even if proceeds are sufficient, the owner need NOT buy a replacement. The rental fee is simply deducted from the carcass proceeds — the contract was for THIS donkey, which has ended.
Rav
The carcass now constitutes the donkey's principal value. One may not fully deplete the principal — therefore its proceeds cannot be used to satisfy rental fees.
Shmuel
There is no prohibition here. The rental transaction is complete for the half already traveled — deduct the fee from proceeds and settle the account.
Common Ground Both Rav and Shmuel agree: the renter pays for the half-journey completed, but has only a ta'arumet (grievance) — no formal legal claim — against the owner for the disruption.

⛵ Boat Sinks Halfway — Who Bears the Loss?

Rabbi Natan
If the renter already PAID when the boat sank — no refund. If he has NOT yet paid — he does not pay now. Money stays where it is.
Gemara
How? If the owner said 'this boat' but renter said 'unspecified wine' — the owner can demand: 'Give me that wine and I will find another boat!' So the renter must pay!
Rav Pappa
Rabbi Natan's ruling applies ONLY when both parties specified: this specific boat AND that specific wine. Since that wine is lost and that boat is sunk — neither can perform, so money stays in place.
Gemara
This is elegant — mutual impossibility of performance means neither party has a claim. The loss is shared symmetrically.
Rav Pappa's Principle Mutual loss applies only with double specificity: THIS boat + THAT wine. Any lesser specification gives one party the right to demand alternative performance — breaking the symmetry.

🩺 Sick vs. Dead: Different Legal Outcomes

Donkey Falls Ill Mid-Journey

  • Owner may say: 'That which is yours is before you' — take it back
  • A sick donkey 'is no better than a dead one' for riding (Rabba bar Rav Huna)
  • Renter cannot demand a healthy replacement in this case
  • Follows Rabbi Shimon ben Elazar's ruling on seized animals
  • Illness mid-journey is treated like an oneis — unforeseen loss

Donkey Dies Mid-Journey

  • Renter pays proportional fee for completed portion of journey
  • Renter retains a ta'arumet (grievance) but no formal legal remedy
  • If contract was for unspecified donkey → owner must provide replacement
  • Rav: if carcass proceeds suffice → must purchase replacement donkey
  • Shmuel: deduct fee from carcass proceeds; no replacement obligation

👜 What a Renter May Load on a Donkey

Base entitlement (no extra fee): Renter may place — his garment, his water jug, and food for that specific journey
↓
Donkey driver's share: The driver accompanying the journey may also load his own garment, water jug, and personal food — this too is included in the rental price
↓
Food availability question: If food is purchasable along the route at every station, can the driver prohibit the renter from pre-loading full journey's food supply?
↓
Rav Pappa's resolution: Ruling applies where food is available only if one goes to the trouble to find it between stations (me'avna). Since a driver does not typically bother, renter may bring his own supply
↓
Extra cargo mid-journey: If renter unloads ADDITIONAL cargo into a boat halfway through, he pays only for the portion of the journey where extra cargo was carried
↓
Boat parallel — 'perakah': The term means the renter loaded MORE cargo mid-journey, not that he unloaded early. Fee is owed for the extra-cargo segment only
