Is Tza'ar Ba'alei Chayyim (Animal Suffering) Torah Law?

Baraita
The obligation to unload applies to a collapsed animal — not a habitual collapser, not standing, not with an excessive burden.
Gemara
If preventing animal suffering is Torah law, why should it matter if the animal is collapsed or standing? We should always be obligated!
Gemara
This baraita follows Rabbi Yosei HaGelili, who holds that tza'ar ba'alei chayyim is rabbinic — so the Sages limited where it applies.
Gemara
But the baraita also says 'not when unloaded' — meaning one loads for pay. That's the Rabbis' position, not R' Yosei HaGelili!
Gemara
The baraita is R' Yosei HaGelili, but on the matter of loading he agrees with the Rabbis.
RESOLUTION R' Yosei HaGelili holds tza'ar ba'alei chayyim is rabbinic, but adopts the Rabbis' rule that loading is for remuneration.

Who Is 'Your Teacher' (Rabbo Muvhak)?

Rabbi Meir

  • Only the teacher who taught you wisdom (chokhmah)
  • Meaning: deep Talmudic analysis
  • Not Bible or Mishna teachers

Rabbi Yehuda

  • Any teacher from whom you learned most of your knowledge
  • Could be Bible, Mishna, or Talmud
  • Quantity of learning is the criterion

Rabbi Yosei

  • Even one who enlightened you in a single mishna
  • Broadest definition of 'teacher'
  • Quality of insight matters most
CONTEXT This debate defines whose lost item, burden, or ransom takes precedence over your father's — the stakes of this definition are enormous.

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's Correction

לעולם הוי רץ למשנה יותר מן התלמוד
HISTORICAL TURNING POINT When the baraita praising Talmud study above all was taught, everyone abandoned Mishna to pursue Talmud. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi then taught: 'Always pursue Mishna study more than Talmud' — because without a firm grounding in the fundamental halakhot of the Mishna, talmudic discourse is futile.
