QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the final halakha, when a borrower breaks a tool, what must he do?
A. Pay the full original value of the tool in cash
B. Return the broken tool AND pay the monetary difference in value
C. Simply pay the difference between the tool's old and new value
D. Replace the tool with a new identical one
Correct: B — Rav Kahana and Rav Asi rule — and Rav was silenced — that the borrower returns the broken item AND supplements the difference monetarily. Merely paying the value difference is insufficient.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rava's clever tip: how can a borrower ensure he is exempt via she'ela b'ba'alim before borrowing an item?
A. Ask the lender to sign a contract waiving liability
B. Borrow the item on Shabbat when the lender is not working
C. Say 'pour me water' to the lender before taking the item
D. Borrow during the Kalla period in Elul or Adar
Correct: C — Rava teaches: if the borrower asks the lender to 'pour water' first, the lender is already working for the borrower at the moment of the loan — creating she'ela b'ba'alim and exempting the borrower.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the mishna's disputed cow case, if BOTH the renter and owner say 'I don't know' which cow died, what is the ruling?
A. The renter is fully liable since he possessed both cows
B. The owner bears the full loss as he trusted the renter
C. They divide the loss — the renter pays half the cow's value
D. The case is dismissed for lack of evidence
Correct: C — When both parties are equally uncertain, neither claim overcomes the other. The Mishna rules they split the disputed amount — the renter pays half the value of the cow.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why were Rava's students WRONG to claim exemption from liability when borrowing from Rava?
A. Students may only borrow from teachers during daylight hours
B. Rava is exempt from she'ela b'ba'alim as a Torah scholar
C. A teacher is not 'lent' to his students outside the Kalla gatherings
D. Torah teaching is unpaid so it cannot qualify as 'work'
Correct: C — She'ela b'ba'alim for a teacher applies only during the Kalla (Elul/Adar) when he is required to teach a fixed subject. Outside that, Rava is not 'lent' to his students — he teaches voluntarily, not as an employee.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Gemara's defense of Rav Naḥman, when does 'I don't know' create monetary liability?
A. Whenever the claim involves more than 100 dinars
B. Only when there is already a matter of an oath between the parties
C. Whenever two witnesses can confirm the original transaction
D. Only in cases involving real property, not movable goods
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman holds 'I don't know' generally does not compel payment. The mishna's case is distinguished because there is already an oath obligation between the parties — in that specific context, uncertainty shifts to liability.
