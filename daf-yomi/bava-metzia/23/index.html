What Counts as a Valid Distinguishing Mark (Siman)?

Rabba
A siman prone to trampling (סימן העשוי לידרס) is not a valid siman — it will be destroyed
Rava
A siman prone to trampling is a valid siman — it still identifies the owner
Gemara
From homeowner's loaves: they must be proclaimed even in public areas — conclusive refutation of Rabba!
Rabba
No — food is different. People don't pass by food without picking it up, so it won't be trampled
Gemara
But what about gentiles? They fear the food was placed for sorcery and won't touch it
Core Sugya This daf systematically examines what qualifies as a valid siman: trampled marks, self-forming marks, number, weight, location, and shape — building the halakhic framework for returning lost items.

Bundles in Public vs. Secluded Areas

Rabba
Bundles in public: keep them — they are trampled and any siman is destroyed
Rabba
Bundles in secluded area: proclaim — no traffic means siman remains intact
Rava
Bundles in public: keep them — they get kicked and roll away from their original location
Rava
Bundles in secluded area: proclaim — no kicking, so location is still a valid siman
Both
Sheaves (large bundles): always proclaim — too heavy to roll, too tall to trample
Key Distinction Rabba focuses on the siman being physically destroyed; Rava focuses on the item being displaced from its location.

Homeowner's Loaves — Why Food Is Different

Found homeowner's loaves in public area
↓
Siman exists: each person shapes loaves uniquely
↓
Challenge: Won't the siman be trampled?
↓
Rabba: People don't pass by food without picking it up — no trampling
↓
But gentiles?
They fear food left out is for sorcery (כשפים) — won't touch it
But animals?
Mishna refers to a place where beasts and dogs are not common
Rabba's Defense Food is uniquely protected from trampling because humans avoid stepping on it and gentiles fear sorcery — so the siman survives.

Two Types of Disputed Simanim

Siman Prone to Trampling (סימן העשוי לידרס)

  • A mark that may be destroyed by foot traffic
  • Rabba: NOT a valid siman
  • Rava: IS a valid siman
  • Example: Shape of loaves in a public area
  • Gemara's refutation supports Rava

Self-Forming Siman (סימן העשוי מאליו)

  • A mark that appears naturally, not placed intentionally
  • First Tanna: NOT a valid siman
  • R' Yehuda: IS a valid siman
  • Example: Shard in fig cake, coins in bread
  • Both Rabba and Rava agree this is the Tannaitic dispute
Resolution Rav Zevid in Rava's name: Both Tannaim agree a trampled siman IS valid. Their dispute is only about self-forming simanim.

Sealed Barrels — Before vs. After Storehouses Open

🛢️ One finds a sealed barrel of wine
↓
📅 Was it found before the storehouses opened?
↓
✅ If before: the seal identifies the owner — must proclaim (Mishna 25a)
↓
📅 Was it found after the storehouses opened?
↓
❌ If after: seals no longer identify owners — storekeepers sold barrels to public
↓
⚖️ Abaye to Rav Ya'akov bar Abba: Found a barrel after storehouses opened? Keep it!
Abaye's Ruling A distinguishing mark is only valid if it still uniquely connects the item to its owner. Once barrels are sold to the public, the original seal loses its identifying power.
