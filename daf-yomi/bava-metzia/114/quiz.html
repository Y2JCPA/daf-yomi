QUESTION 1 OF 5
What verbal analogy (גזירה שוה) did Elijah use to rule that arrangements are made for an ordinary debtor?
A. From 'garment' (בגד) — linking collateral and valuations
B. From 'poor' (עני) — connecting the debtor's case to valuations
C. From 'blessing' (ברכה) — the verse 'he shall bless you'
D. From 'return' (השב) — connecting all property-return cases
Correct: B — Elijah derived the rule via גזירה שוה from the word עני (poor), which appears in both the laws of an ordinary debtor and the laws of valuations — teaching that just as the court makes arrangements in valuations, so too for an ordinary debtor.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why was Elijah — a priest — permitted to stand in a gentile cemetery?
A. Gentile corpses do not impart priestly impurity (טומאה) to kohanim
B. Elijah had a special prophetic dispensation from Heaven
C. He was standing at the entrance only, not among the graves
D. The prohibition on priests entering cemeteries applies only in the Temple era
Correct: A — As Elijah told Rabba bar Avuh: the halakha taught in Seder Teharot is that gentile corpses do not transmit priestly impurity. Therefore a kohen may stand in a gentile graveyard without violating any prohibition.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What consequence did Rabba bar Avuh suffer for taking fragrant leaves from Gan Eden?
A. He was struck with poverty for taking what did not belong to him
B. A heavenly voice declared he had consumed his World-to-Come; he discarded the leaves
C. The leaves turned to ashes the moment he left the garden
D. He was told to return them immediately or forfeit his share in Olam HaBa
Correct: B — Upon exiting, Rabba bar Avuh heard a heavenly voice saying: 'Who else has consumed his World-to-Come like Rabba bar Avuh?' — meaning he had converted his next-world merit into this-worldly benefit. He cast the leaves away, yet his cloak retained their heavenly fragrance.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rava's interpretation of Rav Sheizevi's baraita, when must a 'day garment' taken as collateral be returned?
A. Before sunset, so the debtor can wear it at the end of the day
B. Before sunrise, so the debtor can wear it during the upcoming day
C. Within 24 hours of the creditor taking it
D. Only upon the debtor's explicit request
Correct: B — Rava explains that the Torah requires returning a night garment before nightfall and a day garment before sunrise — ensuring the debtor has each item when he actually needs it. The schedule of return is calibrated to the debtor's practical daily requirements.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R. Yoḥanan, if a creditor returned collateral and then the debtor died, what may the creditor do?
A. He may never reclaim the collateral once it passed to the heirs
B. He may reclaim the collateral from the children with no nightly return obligation
C. He must wait thirty days out of respect for the mourning period
D. He may only reclaim if the children are independently wealthy
Correct: B — R. Yoḥanan rules that the personal obligation to return collateral nightly — which stems from the living poor man's dignity and need — does not extend to his children. After the debtor dies the creditor may retake the collateral from the heirs without any obligation to leave it with them each night.
