When May You Mix Produce or Wine?

Mixing new produce with old produce
↓
Old is better quality
❌ Prohibited — lowers the value of what the buyer expects
New is more expensive
❌ Still prohibited — buyers want new produce for long-term storage
↓
Exception: Mixing strong wine with weak wine — permitted because it enhances the product
↓
R' Elazar: The phrase "Actually they said" (באמת אמרו) signals this is an established halakha beyond dispute
Key Insight Even when new produce costs more per se'a, mixing is still prohibited — the buyer values newness for its storage potential, not just its current taste.

When Is Wine Mixing Permitted?

Rav Naḥman
Mixing strong and weak wine is only permitted while wine is among the winepresses — during fermentation, blending enhances flavor. After fermentation, it harms the wine.
Rava's Practice
Today people mix old and new wine even after the winepress stage. How is this permitted?
Rav Pappa
Buyers are aware of the potential loss and waive their objection — it's an accepted market practice.
Rav Aḥa b. Rav Ika
This follows Rabbi Aḥa, who permits mixing whenever the product will be tasted before purchase — the buyer discovers the mixture himself.
Practical Rule Two justifications for mixing wine after fermentation: (1) buyers knowingly accept it, or (2) they can taste it first and decide.

Wine Sediment & Dilution Rules

ScenarioRulingReason
Mixing sediment from last night's wine into today's wine❌ ProhibitedForeign sediment degrades the wine
Giving buyer wine with its own natural sediment✅ PermittedSeller need not filter — it's the wine's own sediment (Rav Yehuda)
Selling watered wine in a store⚠️ Only if he informs the buyerBuyer can then decide for himself
Selling watered wine to a merchant❌ Prohibited even with disclosureMerchant will resell it without informing his buyers
Adding water where it is local custom✅ PermittedEveryone already knows and expects dilution
Rava's Story Rava bought wine, diluted it heavily, found it unpleasant, and returned it to the store. Abaye challenged him — but Rava said his dilution was obviously excessive, so no one would be deceived.

Adorning Merchandise: New vs. Old

New Merchandise ✅

  • Shmuel: May place fringes on a new cloak
  • Rav Yehuda: May clean and adorn ornamented garments
  • Rabba: May taper linen garments to appear fine
  • Rava: May draw arrows to ornament garments
  • Decoration enhances intrinsic beauty

Old Merchandise ❌

  • May not stiffen animal hair with bran water (Babylonian view)
  • May not scrub hair clean to inflate volume (Ze'eiri / Rav Kahana)
  • May not inflate innards to seem like more meat
  • May not soak meat in water to change its color
  • Adornment here conceals flaws = deception
Principle Enhancing real quality is permitted; concealing defects is prohibited. The line between presentation and deception depends on whether the item is new or old.

The Dyed Slave

צדיק מצרה נחלץ ויבא אחר תחתיו
Story An elderly slave dyed his hair and beard black to appear young. Rava wisely declined to buy him, saying: "Let the poor be members of your household."

Rav Pappa bar Shmuel bought him. When he asked the slave for water, the slave washed out the dye, revealed his white hair, and said: "I am older than your father!"

Rav Pappa applied the verse: "The righteous is delivered from trouble, and another comes in his stead" (Proverbs 11:8) — Rava was the righteous one spared; Rav Pappa was the "another."
