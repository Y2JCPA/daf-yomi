Finder's Liability & Care of Found Objects

Rabba
A finder of a lost item has the status of a שומר חינם (unpaid bailee) — liable only for negligence
Rav Yosef
A finder has the status of a שומר שכר (paid bailee) — liable even for theft or loss
Gemara
The Mishna seems to refute Rav Yosef — if the finder didn't use the money, everyone agrees he's exempt from loss
Rav Yosef
The dispute is not about theft/loss but about אונס (force majeure) — R' Tarfon treats the finder as a borrower
Core Question What level of liability does a finder bear for a lost item in his care?

Bailee Status of a Finder

Rabba
The finder is like a שומר חינם (unpaid bailee) — liable only for פשיעה (negligence)
Rav Yosef
The finder is like a שומר שכר (paid bailee) — liable for גניבה ואבידה (theft and loss)
Gemara
The Mishna says if the finder didn't use the money, all agree he's exempt — this refutes Rav Yosef!
Rav Yosef
Everyone agrees on theft/loss liability. The dispute is about אונס — R' Tarfon says he's a שואל (borrower), liable even for force majeure
Key Distinction Rav Yosef reframes the Mishna's dispute: not about theft/loss, but about whether permission to use money makes the finder a borrower

Rabbi Tarfon vs Rabbi Akiva on Found Money

Rabbi Tarfon

  • The finder MAY use found money
  • Permission to use = status of borrower (שואל)
  • Liable even for אונס (force majeure)
  • If money is lost, he IS liable to pay restitution

Rabbi Akiva

  • The finder may NOT use found money
  • Without permission to use = NOT a borrower
  • Not liable for circumstances beyond his control
  • If money is lost, he is NOT liable to pay restitution
Halakha Shmuel rules: The halakha follows Rabbi Tarfon — it is permitted for the finder to use the money

Rav Yosef's Reframing of the Dispute

Finder safeguards lost money
↓
All agree: finder liable as שומר שכר for theft/loss
↓
May the finder use the money?
R' Tarfon: Yes
He becomes a שואל (borrower) — liable even for אונס
R' Akiva: No
He remains a bailee — not liable for force majeure
Rabba's Clarification "Lost" in the Mishna means a ship sinking at sea — i.e., אונס (force majeure), not ordinary loss

Raḥava's Dinars: Limits on Using Found Money

ScenarioMay Finder Use the Money?
Money from selling a found item the finder tended to✅ Yes — he exerted effort caring for it
Found coins (no effort expended)❌ No — finder did not exert himself
Raḥava's orphan dinars❌ No — similar to found coins (Abaye's objection)
R' Ḥelbo / Rav Huna Permission to use found money applies only when the finder exerted himself by tending to the item before selling it — not to coins found as-is

Mishna: Care of Found Items

📜 Scrolls — Read once every 30 days to ventilate; if he can't read, roll and unroll them
↓
🚫 May NOT study new passages (leaves scroll exposed too long) or let another read with him (may tear it)
↓
👔 Garments — Shake once every 30 days; spread out for the garment's sake, not for personal prestige
↓
🥈 Silver/Copper vessels — Use for their sake (prevent tarnish), but not to the point of erosion
↓
🥇 Gold/Glass vessels — Do not touch them until Elijah comes (they don't deteriorate)
↓
💼 Items beneath his dignity to carry — he is not obligated to pick them up
Principle The finder must actively maintain items to prevent deterioration, but may not use them for personal benefit
