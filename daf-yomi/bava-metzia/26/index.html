Knife in the Wall: Which Way Does It Point?

Rav Ashi
For a knife found in a wall, follow the handle (nitzav). For a money pouch, follow the laces.
Gemara
But if so, why does the Mishna give a midpoint rule? Just check which way the handle faces!
Gemara
The Mishna refers to rags or metal strips — items with no handle or laces to indicate direction.
Baraita
If the hollow is filled with coins, homeowner and finder divide them — even if the hollow is inclined to one side.
Principle When an item has a directional indicator (handle, laces), that overrides the simple midpoint rule.

House was an inn for 3 Jews

House was an inn for 3 gentiles
Doesn't prove R' Shimon ben Elazar applies to Jewish majority
Rav Nahman / Rabba bar Avuh
Even with 3 Jews: owner asked them, they didn't return it → he despairs (intent to rob)
Key Dispute Does R' Shimon ben Elazar's rule (finder keeps in public areas) apply even in a Jewish-majority setting?

Rav Nahman: Sela Falling from a Group

ScenarioObligation to Return?Reason
Fell from one of 2 people✅ Must returnOwner thinks: 'Only one person was with me — I'll confront him'
Fell from one of 3 people❌ Finder keepsOwner thinks: 'Each will deny it — I have no claim'
Fell from 3, ≥1 peruta each (Rava)✅ Must returnPerhaps they are partners — no despair
Fell from 3, only 2 perutot total (Rava, alt.)✅ Must returnOne partner may relinquish his share to another
Rava's Addition If the losers might be partners (shutafin), they don't despair — each assumes a partner picked it up for the group.

Rava's Framework: Taking a Lost Item & Mitzvot Violated

📍 Scenario 1: Took it to STEAL before owner's despair
↓
⚠️ Violates: 'You shall not rob' + 'You shall return' + 'You may not disregard'
↓
📍 Scenario 2: Took it to RETURN, then decided to steal after despair
↓
⚠️ Violates only: 'You shall return' (positive mitzva)
↓
📍 Scenario 3: Waited until AFTER despair, then took it
↓
⚠️ Violates only: 'You may not disregard' (took no action to return)
Critical Timing Even returning the item after the owner despairs is merely a gift — the original transgression remains.

Store & Money Changer: Where Was the Item Found?

Before the Counter / Chair

  • In the store → belongs to finder
  • Before money changer → belongs to finder
  • Public area = owner despairs
  • R' Elazar: even on the table → finder keeps

Behind the Counter / Chair

  • Between counter & storekeeper → storekeeper
  • Between chair & money changer → money changer
  • Private area = no public access
  • Customers don't typically go there
Mishna Rule The dividing line is customer access — areas the public reaches are treated as public; behind the counter is private.
