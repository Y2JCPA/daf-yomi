Bava Metzia 71 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman in the name of Rav Huna, lending interest-free to a Jew takes precedence over lending to a gentile with interest. What is surprising about this ruling?
A. The Jew must forgo profit to prioritize a fellow Jew
B. The gentile is prohibited from paying interest
C. The Jew must also forgive the principal
D. It only applies to Torah scholars
Correct: A — The novelty (ḥiddush) is that the priority of lending to a Jew applies even when it means a financial loss — forgoing the interest income from the gentile loan.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does R' Yosei call lenders with interest 'blind'?
A. They can't see that their money will be lost
B. They voluntarily create documented proof of their own denial of God
C. They don't realize the borrower can't repay
D. They ignore the poor in favor of the rich
Correct: B — R' Yosei's vivid point: a person who is called 'wicked' is outraged, yet interest lenders bring witnesses and a scribe to formally document what amounts to a denial of God — they are blind to the severity of their own act.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why can't a Jew serve as guarantor for a gentile lending to a Jew with interest, according to the initial objection?
A. Because the guarantor would be lending to a gentile
B. Because under gentile law the guarantor pays first, making him effectively the lender charging interest to the Jewish borrower
C. Because guarantors are always prohibited in Jewish law
D. Because the gentile cannot be trusted to honor the agreement
Correct: B — Under gentile law, the lender collects from the guarantor first. This means the guarantor effectively borrows from the gentile and lends to the Jew — making the guarantor the one taking interest from a fellow Jew.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the middleman lending scenario: Jew₁ borrowed from a gentile, and Jew₂ wants the money. When is this permitted?
A. When Jew₂ pays Jew₁ directly with interest
B. When Jew₁ presents Jew₂ to the gentile, and the gentile agrees to lend directly to Jew₂
C. When Jew₂ signs a contract with Jew₁
D. It is always prohibited
Correct: B — The arrangement is only permitted when the gentile directly agrees to lend to Jew₂, so the interest flows between the gentile and Jew₂ — not between two Jews.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rav Ashi argued that 'no agency for a gentile' applies only to terumah. Why does the Gemara reject this?
A. Because terumah is less strict than other mitzvot
B. Because the derivation from 'you also' — requiring agents to be 'members of the covenant' — is a general principle, not limited to terumah
C. Because Rav Ashi was not a reliable authority
D. Because gentiles can never participate in any financial transactions
Correct: B — The verse 'you also' teaches that agents must be members of the covenant (Jews). Though derived in the context of terumah, the logic — 'just as you are Jews, your agents must be Jews' — applies as a general principle of halakhic agency.
