Lending, Interest & Priorities on Daf 71

May a Jew lend to a gentile with interest?
↓
Ravina
Torah scholars may — they won't be negatively influenced
General Rule
Restricted — concern the Jew may learn from gentile ways
↓
Even so: lending to a Jew interest-free takes precedence over lending to a gentile with interest
↓
Broader theme: those who lend with interest deny God and their wealth collapses permanently
Key Verse "If you lend money to any of My people, even to the poor person who is with you" (Exodus 22:24) — each phrase establishes a lending priority.

Priority in Lending (Exodus 22:24)

👥 "My people" — A Jew takes precedence over a gentile
↓
🪙 "The poor" — A poor person takes precedence over a rich person
↓
👨‍👩‍👧 "With you" — Your relative takes precedence over the poor of your city
↓
🏘️ Your city's poor take precedence over the poor of another city
↓
💰 Even lending interest-free to a Jew beats lending to a gentile with interest
Rav Naḥman / Rav Huna The precedence for a Jew applies even at a financial loss — forgoing interest income from a gentile to lend free to a Jew.

Why Restrict Lending to Gentiles with Interest?

Gemara
Why did the Sages decree against lending to a gentile with interest?
Reason
Continuous financial dealings may cause the Jew to learn from the gentile's ways.
Ravina
The mishna refers to Torah scholars — they will not be influenced.
Ger Toshav
One may lend to a ger toshav with interest — but only enough for the lender's livelihood, not as regular business.
Principle The restriction is protective, not absolute — it depends on the lender's susceptibility to negative influence.

R' Yosei: The Blindness of Lenders

בואו וראו סמיות עיניהם של מלווי בריבית
Powerful Imagery If someone calls another "wicked" in public, the person is outraged and harasses the offender.

Yet those who lend with interest bring witnesses, a scribe (lavlar), pen (kulmos), and ink — and write a document declaring:

"So-and-so denies the God of Israel."

They voluntarily document their own rebellion against the Torah!

Collapse: Lenders vs. Non-Lenders

Lends Without Interest

  • Psalm 15:5 — "He who does these shall never collapse"
  • If they do fall, they ultimately rise again
  • R' Elazar: Collapse is temporary

Lends With Interest

  • R' Shimon b. Elazar: Their property collapses
  • They collapse and do NOT rise again
  • R' Elazar: Permanent downfall
Habakkuk 1:13 "The wicked swallows one more righteous than he" — Rav Huna: he can swallow the more righteous, but not the completely righteous.

Guarantor for an Interest Loan

Can a Jew serve as a guarantor (arev) for an interest loan?
↓
Jew → Jew Loan
❌ Prohibited
Guarantor, witnesses all transgress (Mishna 75b)
Gentile → Jew Loan
⚠️ Problematic — gentile law lets him collect from guarantor first
↓
Rav Sheshet: Permitted when the gentile accepts Jewish collection procedure (collect from borrower first)
↓
He accepted Jewish law on collection method but not on the interest prohibition
Key Distinction The gentile can selectively adopt Jewish procedural law without adopting the substantive prohibition against interest.
