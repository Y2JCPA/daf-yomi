A sela coin erodes over time through use
↓
How much erosion = ona'ah (exploitation)?
R' Meir
4 issar
(1 issar/dinar)

4 pundeyon
(1 pundeyon/dinar)

KEY PRINCIPLE The Mishna's accepted ruling is 8 pundeyon (one-sixth) — matching the general ona'ah threshold, but the Tannaim dispute the exact measure for coins.

Why Is Ona'ah for Garments Different Than Coins?

Gemara
For garments, all agree ona'ah is one-sixth. For a sela, the Tannaim disagree. Why the difference?
Rava
The garment Mishna follows R' Shimon, who holds one-sixth for both coins and garments — no contradiction.
Abaye
People waive overpayment up to ⅙ on garments — as the saying goes: 'Overpay for your back, buy at cost for your stomach.'
Abaye
But a worn coin doesn't circulate well, so even a smaller disparity is not waived.
INSIGHT The psychology of purchase matters: people tolerate overpaying for clothing they desire, but not for receiving defective currency.

How Far Can a Coin Erode and Still Be Used?

A sela erodes over time

Is it still worth at least one shekel (half a sela)?
Yes
✅ Still valid as a sela
No
❌ Can only be sold for metal value
↓
Same for a dinar: valid until it reaches a rova (half-dinar)
↓
Once below that — must be destroyed or perforated

Returning Defective Coins: Cities vs Villages

In Cities (Kerakim)

  • Money changers are available
  • Must return within time to show a money changer
  • Everyone can quickly verify coin value
  • Short window for returns

In Villages

  • No money changer available
  • May return until Shabbat eves
  • People discover coin value at the market
  • Longer window for returns
RAVA'S DISTINCTION For garments, everyone can assess value, so there's no city/village distinction. For coins, only a money changer can tell — hence different rules by location.

What To Do With an Invalidated Coin

🪙 Coin erodes below minimum value threshold
↓
🚫 May NOT sell to a merchant, violent person, or murderer — they will deceive others
↓
✂️ Option 1: Grind, melt, or cut it into pieces
↓
🌊 Option 2: Cast it into the Dead Sea
↓
📿 Option 3: Perforate it in the middle and hang as ornament for child
↓
⚠️ But if perforated from the side — forbidden, lest it be trimmed and reused for fraud
R' ELAZAR'S KEY A hole in the center makes the coin obviously invalid. A hole on the edge could be cut off, leaving a smaller but deceptive coin.
