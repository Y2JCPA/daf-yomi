🌳 Planting Trees in Another's Field Without Permission

Shmuel
If someone plants trees in another's field without permission, he is paid only like a po'el batel — a worker whose work was halted — i.e., minimal compensation.
Rav
He receives the full value added to the field — the appraised improvement value, since the owner genuinely benefits.
Rav Pappa
They do not actually disagree! Shmuel speaks of a field not designated for planting; Rav speaks of a field designated for planting.
Rav (in practice)
When the field owner fenced in the field and safeguarded the trees, he demonstrated satisfaction. Therefore he must pay the planter the full improvement value.
Key Principle An owner's conduct — fencing, protecting the trees — signals acceptance of the improvement and triggers full payment obligations.

🌊 River-Swept Olive Trees: Who Owns the Produce?

River uproots olive trees and deposits them in another's field
↓
Trees take root and produce olives
↓
First 3 Years (Orlah)
All belongs to tree owner — field owner couldn't have consumed the fruit anyway due to the orlah prohibition
After 3 Years
Olives are divided between tree owner and field owner according to their respective contributions
↓
Reish Lakish (via Ravin): This ruling applies only when the trees were uprooted with their clods of earth, preserving the identity and vitality of the tree owner's property

🔑 Rental Notice Periods

Town House — Rainy Season

  • Cannot evict from Sukkot until Passover
  • No fixed days' notice required mid-winter
  • Presumed rented for the full rainy season
  • Rav Asi: even one day into the season = full protection
  • Landlord may raise rent without prior notice (Rav Huna)

Town House — Summer

  • 30 days' advance notice required
  • Applies to landlord AND renter equally
  • Baraita: notice requirement is mutual
  • Purpose: time to find alternative housing
  • Failure to give notice = must wait another 30 days

City House / Commercial Shop

  • City house: 12 months' notice required
  • Applies both summer and rainy season
  • Harder to find alternative housing in cities
  • Baker's or dyer's shop: 3 full years' notice!
  • Reason: lengthy investment in the location

📜 Field of Ancestors: Sharecropping from a Gentile

Situation: A Jew's ancestral field was seized by a gentile. The Jew now receives it back as a sharecropping arrangement — he works it and shares the produce with the gentile.
↓
Initial Reading of the Mishna: 'Field of his ancestors' = any field in Eretz Yisrael. The sharecropper must tithe even the gentile's share, since a gentile's acquisition abrogates the field's sanctity.
↓
Challenge (Rav Kahana to Rav Pappi/Rav Zevid): A baraita specifies the field was actively seized from him — so the mishna cannot refer to just any field. The penalty must target a specific wrongful situation.
↓
Revised Interpretation: 'Field of his ancestors' = his literal ancestral land seized by a gentile. The Sages penalized this specific Jew to pressure him to buy the field back.
↓
Rabbi Yoḥanan's Reason: כדי שיקנה — so that he will purchase it. The entire rabbinic penalty serves לישוב ארץ ישראל — encouraging redemption and resettlement of ancestral Jewish land.
