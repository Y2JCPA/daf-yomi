📜 Mortgage Duration: Who Is Believed?

Debtor
The mortgage was for only two years. You have already consumed enough produce — return my field!
Creditor
No — the agreement was for three years. I am entitled to one more year of produce.
Rav Kahana
The halakha follows my view: produce already consumed remains with whoever consumed it. We do not force retroactive redistribution.
Gemara
But why not follow Rav Yehuda here? Because this matter will be revealed — the witnesses who signed the document know the true duration and can testify.
Key Distinction A dispute 'that will be revealed' shifts the halakhic calculus. Unlike a truly unknowable matter, here witnesses can clarify — so we apply stricter standards to the party who consumed extra produce.

🌙 Day Laborer vs. Night Laborer — Wage Collection Windows

☀️ Day Laborer

  • Works during the daytime
  • Wage obligation arises at end of day
  • Collects wages all night following work
  • Source: 'shall not remain with you all night until the morning' (Lev. 19:13)
  • Employer violates Bal Talin if not paid by morning

🌙 Night Laborer

  • Works during the nighttime
  • Wage obligation arises at end of night
  • Collects wages all following day
  • Source: 'on his day you shall give him his wages' (Deut. 24:15)
  • Employer violates Bal Talin if not paid by evening

⏰ Hourly Laborer

  • Works a partial or fixed-hour shift
  • Collects wages all that day and night
  • Window closes at end of the full 24-hour period
  • Same Bal Talin applies once the window closes
  • Most flexible window; least common scenario

👶 Orphans' Claim of Enhancement on Mortgaged Land

Debtor dies; orphans inherit a field already mortgaged to creditor
↓
Orphans claim: 'We enhanced the land after inheriting it — creditor cannot collect that added value'
↓
Rabbi Ḥanina (initial view)
Orphans currently possess the land → creditor must bring proof against them
Rabbi Yoḥanan (final ruling)
Creditor's prior lien is established → orphans bear burden of proving enhancement
↓
Orphans prove enhancement → compensated in money (not land), per Rav Naḥman citing Shmuel

📖 Deriving Bal Talin — Step by Step from Torah

Leviticus 19:13: 'The wages of a hired laborer shall not remain with you all night until the morning' — primary source establishing the day laborer's overnight collection window
↓
Gemara challenge: Why not read the verse the other way — night laborer collects all night; day laborer collects all day?
↓
Resolution: The wage obligation arises only at the end of the work period. A day laborer finishes at day's end → obligation triggers at night → verse says 'do not hold it overnight' = pay before morning
↓
Deuteronomy 24:15: 'On his day you shall give him his wages' — extends the prohibition and adds source for night laborer collecting during the following day
↓
After the window closes: Rav — employer still violates the ongoing 'do not delay' prohibition. The Gemara clarifies this is a separate negative commandment (lo ta'aseh)
↓
Agency rule: Employer says 'go hire workers for me' → neither employer nor agent violates Bal Talin. Employer didn't hire directly; agent is not the wage-debtor.
