QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Ravina, why does a tenant farmer subtract from his share when the spring dries up?
A. Because the Rabbis decreed that all natural disasters reduce the tenant's obligation
B. Because the owner specified 'this irrigated field,' implying it was currently irrigated
C. Because the tenant farmer demanded an irrigated field and therefore bears no risk
D. Because Shmuel ruled that only the landowner bears the risk of natural disasters
Correct: B — Ravina holds that both the baraita and mishna involve the owner stating the terms. When the owner says 'this irrigated field,' it implies it is currently irrigated — so if the spring dries up, the owner effectively guaranteed that condition and the farmer may subtract.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How did Hillel the Elder resolve the question of the Alexandrian children's legitimacy?
A. He invalidated all betrothals made long before the wedding ceremony
B. He required the couples to perform a new betrothal before Rabbinic witnesses
C. He derived from the contract's wording that the marriage was already established
D. He ruled that Alexandrian customs were invalid under Biblical law
Correct: C — Hillel found the phrase 'when you come into my house, you are my wife' written in the marriage contract. Expounding this standard document language, he ruled the marriage was established by that clause — demonstrating that לשון בני אדם can resolve the most serious questions of personal status.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehoshua ben Korḥa, what is the legal effect of a creditor writing 'I will acquire the collateral' in the loan document?
A. The debtor forfeits all rights to the collateral at the moment of signing
B. The creditor acquires the collateral but is responsible if it depreciates in value
C. The clause is purely ceremonial and has no independent halakhic effect
D. The creditor may immediately sell the collateral without waiting for default
Correct: B — The Gemara resolves the apparent contradiction: writing this clause means the creditor acquires the collateral, but is then responsible for any depreciation — he can claim the balance of the debt from the debtor's other property, but cannot ignore the loss.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When a sharecropper plants wheat instead of the agreed sesame and the wheat unexpectedly yields MORE than sesame would have, what is the ruling?
A. The sharecropper receives a larger share to reward his superior agricultural judgment
B. The owner and sharecropper split the unexpected windfall profit equally
C. The owner keeps the full benefit; the sharecropper is not rewarded for unauthorized deviation
D. The contract is void and the sharecropper may keep all profits
Correct: C — Rav Ashi's proverb captures the principle: 'Let the land be weakened, but do not weaken its owner.' A sharecropper who deviates from the agreed crop cannot claim extra reward when the deviation happens to turn out well — the owner retains the full benefit.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In an Iska business partnership, how is the capital halakhically structured?
A. The entire capital is a loan that the manager must repay with interest
B. The entire capital is an investment and the manager bears no personal liability
C. Half is a loan the manager uses freely; half is an investment with shared profits and losses
D. The investor owns all profits while the manager receives only a fixed salary
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that in an Iska, half the capital is treated as a loan — the manager may use it as he chooses (even to drink wine) — while the other half is an investment in which he shares both profits and losses with the investor. This structure avoids the prohibition on interest.
