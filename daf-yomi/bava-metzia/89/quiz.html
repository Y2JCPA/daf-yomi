QUESTION 1 OF 5
How does the Gemara extend the prohibition of muzzling to all animals, not just oxen?
A. A general rabbinic decree covering all working animals
B. A verbal analogy (גְּזֵרָה שָׁוָה) between 'ox' in the muzzling verse and 'ox' in Shabbat laws
C. A logical extension ruled by Rav Pappa
D. A direct verse stating 'any animal that threshes'
Correct: B — The Gemara uses a verbal analogy (גְּזֵרָה שָׁוָה) linking the word 'ox' in the muzzling prohibition to 'ox' in the Shabbat context, thereby extending the law to all animals.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Ravina's resolution to the apparent contradiction between the 'tithes' and 'ḥalla' baraitot?
A. The ḥalla baraita applies only outside of Israel, where tithes are not required
B. The later baraita always overrides the earlier one in cases of contradiction
C. Combine both: most produce uses tithe-stage as the cutoff; dough uses ḥalla-stage as the cutoff
D. The contradiction stands — each Sage ruled according to the practice of his region
Correct: C — Ravina teaches that both baraitot should be merged into one unified rule: for most produce, the laborer eats until the tithe-completion stage; for dough, the cutoff is before ḥalla is separated. Both apply everywhere.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava, under what condition does dipping fruit in salt become forbidden for a laborer?
A. Always — adding any ingredient is forbidden
B. Only in Eretz Yisrael, where tithes apply to casual eating
C. When dipping a single fruit, since salt establishes casual eating
D. When dipping two fruits, since that constitutes a fixed meal obligating tithes
Correct: D — Rava rules that dipping one fruit in salt is still casual eating and is permitted, but dipping two fruits constitutes a fixed meal (קֶבַע), which obligates tithe separation and is therefore forbidden for the laborer.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the baraita-proof that singling in fire is forbidden for a laborer?
A. The baraita is considered a minority opinion and non-authoritative
B. The prohibition in that baraita is due to neglect of the owner's labor, not the use of fire itself
C. Rava explicitly permits singling in a later ruling
D. The baraita refers only to grain, not to fruit or vegetables
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the baraita forbids singling because the laborer is wasting the owner's work time — the issue is neglect of duty. This does not resolve whether the act of using fire to improve food is itself prohibited.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What scriptural source does Rav Mattana cite to prove that eating two fruits in a fixed manner establishes a tithe obligation?
A. 'You shall not put any in your vessel' (Deuteronomy 23:25)
B. 'He has gathered them as the sheaves of grain' (Isaiah 17:5)
C. 'You shall not muzzle the ox in its threshing' (Deuteronomy 25:4)
D. 'When you come into your neighbor's vineyard' (Deuteronomy 23:24)
Correct: B — Rav Mattana cites Isaiah 17:5 — 'He has gathered them as the sheaves' — deriving that just as two stalks form a harvested bundle (קמה), eating two fruits together constitutes a fixed meal that triggers the obligation to separate tithes.
