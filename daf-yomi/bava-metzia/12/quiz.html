QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why can a gift be placed in someone's courtyard in their absence, but a get (bill of divorce) cannot?
A. A get requires two witnesses but a gift does not
B. One may act in a person's interest in their absence, but not to their detriment
C. A courtyard only functions as agency for gifts, not for divorce
D. The Torah explicitly prohibits delivering a get to a courtyard
Correct: B — The principle of זכין לאדם שלא בפניו allows beneficial acts (gifts) in one's absence, but a get, which is detrimental to the wife, requires her presence.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Yochanan, what condition must be met for a field to acquire an ownerless running animal for its owner?
A. The field must be fenced on all four sides
B. The owner must be standing in the field at the time
C. The owner must be able to run after the animals and catch them
D. The owner must verbally declare acquisition three times
Correct: C — R' Yirmeyah in the name of R' Yochanan states the field acquires only if the owner could theoretically chase and catch the animals. For gifts, R' Abba bar Kahana removes this requirement.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rava reject the comparison between his flying purse dilemma and the mishna's running animals?
A. The purse has no owner while the animals do
B. A rolling item on the ground is considered at rest at each moment, unlike an item in airspace
C. Purses cannot be acquired by courtyard, only animals can
D. The mishna deals with a field, not a house
Correct: B — Rava distinguishes between מגלגל (rolling on the ground, considered as resting) and אויר (airspace, where the item never touches the ground). The cases are fundamentally different.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does R' Yochanan reinterpret the terms 'minor' and 'adult' in the mishna about found items?
A. Minor means under bar mitzvah; adult means over bar mitzvah
B. Minor means unable to speak; adult means able to speak
C. Adult dependent on father's table = 'minor'; minor not dependent = 'adult'
D. Minor means unmarried; adult means married
Correct: C — R' Ḥiyya bar Abba citing R' Yochanan redefines the terms: 'minor' means economically dependent on the father regardless of age, and 'adult' means economically independent regardless of age.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, when does a laborer's found item belong to his employer?
A. Whenever the laborer is on the employer's property
B. Only when the employer said 'work for me today' without specifying a task
C. Only when the laborer was hired specifically to collect found items
D. Whenever the laborer finds the item during working hours
Correct: C — Rav Pappa holds that a found item belongs to the employer only if the laborer was hired specifically to find items — such as gathering fish left on shore after a lake floods.
