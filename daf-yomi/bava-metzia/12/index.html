Daf 12: Who Owns What You Find?

An ownerless item is found by someone
↓
What is the finder's legal status?
Minor Child / Canaanite Slave / Wife
Found item belongs to the father / master / husband
Adult Child / Hebrew Slave / Divorced Wife
Found item belongs to the finder
Core Principle Ownership of found items depends on the finder's legal dependency — those under another's authority acquire for that authority figure.

Courtyard as Acquisition: Hand vs. Agency

Gemara
A courtyard (חצר) acquires for its owner because it acts as his hand — but it is no less effective than agency (שליחות).
Distinction
For a get (detrimental to wife) — cannot place in her courtyard in her absence.
Distinction
For a gift (beneficial) — can place in the recipient's courtyard in his absence.
Principle
זכין לאדם שלא בפניו — One may act in a person's interest without his presence, but not to his detriment.
Key Rule Courtyard combines two legal mechanisms: kinyan yad (hand) and shelichut (agency), each with different scope.

Field Acquiring Ownerless Animals

People are running after a found ownerless animal in your field
↓
You declare: "My field has acquired this for me!"
↓
Could you run after them and catch them?
Yes — can catch
✅ Your field acquires the animal for you (R' Yochanan)
No — cannot catch
❌ No acquisition for found items
✅ But for a gift — yes! (R' Abba bar Kahana)
R' Yirmeyah / R' Yochanan Field acquisition of found items requires the owner could theoretically chase and catch the animal — but gifts have no such requirement.

Rava's Dilemma: The Flying Purse

Rava
If one threw a purse through one entrance of a house and it exited through another — does the homeowner acquire it?
Rava
The question: Is an item in the airspace (אויר) of a courtyard that will not come to rest there considered as if it rested?
Rav Pappa
Isn't this resolved from the mishna about running animals and field acquisition?
Rava
No! A rolling item on the ground is different — it is considered as though it rests at each moment. A flying item through airspace is not comparable.
Unresolved Rava distinguishes rolling on the ground (מגלגל — considered resting) from flying through airspace (אויר — remains unresolved).

Shmuel vs. R' Yochanan: What Is a 'Minor'?

Shmuel
A minor's found item belongs to his father because the minor doesn't intend to acquire — he runs to give it to his father.
Gemara
But Shmuel rules like R' Yosei that a minor can acquire property — for gleaning (לקט)! Contradiction!
Resolution
Shmuel is explaining the Tanna's reasoning, not his own view. He himself holds a minor can acquire.
R' Yochanan
'Minor' in the mishna means an adult dependent on father's table. 'Adult' means a minor not dependent on father's table.
Two Approaches Shmuel: literal age, but minor lacks intent. R' Yochanan: 'minor/adult' = dependent/independent regardless of actual age.
