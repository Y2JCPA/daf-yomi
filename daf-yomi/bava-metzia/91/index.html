🐄 Muzzling the Ox: Flogging AND Payment?

Gemara
One who muzzles an ox while threshing is flogged AND must pay 4 kav for a cow, 3 kav for a donkey. But the rule is: once flogged, no monetary payment! How can both apply?
Rava
The flogging is for the transgression between him and God. The payment is for the loss suffered by the animal's owner. These are entirely separate obligations — קים ליה בדרבה מיניה does not apply here.
Rav Pappa
I offer a different answer: from the moment he pulled the cow to hire it for threshing, he became obligated to provide its sustenance. His payment obligation arose then. Flogging only comes at the actual moment he muzzles it during labor. The two obligations arise at different times — so they do not cancel each other.
Practical Result
Both Rava and Rav Pappa agree the owner is owed: 4 kav for a cow, 3 kav for a donkey — the typical amounts each consumes while threshing.
The Payment 4 kav for a cow • 3 kav for a donkey — amounts animals typically consume during threshing

🥛 Rav Pappa Resolves Two Dilemmas

לֹא תְבַשֵּׁל גְּדִי בַּחֲלֵב אִמּוֹ — שמות כג:יט
Rav Pappa's Rulings for the House of Rav Pappa bar Abba Dilemma 1 — Kneading dough with milk:
Is this prohibited?
Resolution: Yes, prohibited! A baraita derives from "You shall not boil a kid in its mother's milk" that even kneading dough with milk is forbidden under this prohibition.

Dilemma 2 — Bringing diverse species into the same pen:
Is there concern they will mate and violate kilayim?
Resolution: Yes, prohibited! Following Shmuel's principle — we assess from observed behavior, not only from witnessing the act itself. Concern for illicit mating is sufficient grounds to forbid sharing a pen.

🐑 Mating Assistance: Same Species vs. Diverse Kinds

Same Species — Permitted Assistance

  • Holding the female while male mounts: permitted (Rav Yehuda)
  • Inserting male organ manually: NOT permitted (Rav Aḥadvoi bar Ami)
  • The baraita implies: holding = yes; inserting = no
  • Rav Ashi's open question: one animal with both a species-mate AND a diverse-kind in same pen — unresolved

Diverse Kinds (כִּלְאַיִם) — All Assistance Forbidden

  • Torah prohibition: "You shall not let your cattle copulate with a diverse kind" (Leviticus 19:19)
  • Even holding the female during mating of different species is forbidden
  • Baraita: had Torah written only 'copulate' I would forbid all assistance; 'diverse kind' limits the prohibition — implying same-species holding is permitted
  • Shmuel: witnesses need only see adulterous-style behavior, not witness the act itself

🌿 May a Laborer Eat from a Different Vine?

Dilemma raised: A laborer working on this vine — may he reach over and eat from an adjacent vine? Or must the food source match the labor location?
↓
Attempt 1 — From mishna: "Working with figs may not eat grapes" — implies location matters. Refuted: This is about type of food, not location.
↓
Attempt 2 — From mishna: "He may hold back to eat better-quality fruit elsewhere" — implies he can move. Refuted: This still involves the same type of work-produce.
↓
Attempt 3 — From baraita: "Laborers may eat while walking" — implies location is irrelevant, so eating from another vine should be fine. Refuted: Walking is permitted only as a rabbinic enactment to prevent neglect of work (השבת אבידה), not because location is legally irrelevant.
↓
Counter-argument considered: Even if walking is only rabbinic, perhaps the same logic extends to eating from a nearby vine. But the Gemara finds no definitive proof.
↓
Result — תיקו: The Gemara leaves the question entirely unresolved. The rule for eating from a different vine than one's labor location remains open.
