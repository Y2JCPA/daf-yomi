Bava Metzia 118 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav, what is the threshold for the olive press roof collapse that triggers the owner's obligation?
A. A hole of four handbreadths
B. One quarter of the roof
C. Most of the roof
D. The entire roof
Correct: C — Rav requires that most of the roof collapse. Shmuel is more lenient, holding that even a hole of four handbreadths suffices to trigger the relevant obligation.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rav Naḥman's final resolution of the contradiction between the mishna and the baraita regarding paying a laborer with straw?
A. Mishna = employer's own property; Baraita = ownerless property
B. Mishna = laborer not yet finished; Baraita = laborer already completed the task
C. Mishna = Torah law; Baraita = rabbinic enactment
D. Mishna = daytime work; Baraita = nighttime work
Correct: B — After Rava's objection demolished the first resolution, Rav Naḥman concluded: both cases involve ownerless property, but the mishna speaks when the laborer has not yet finished (and may still refuse), while the baraita speaks when the task is already complete.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabba, the question of whether viewing ownerless property effects acquisition is a dispute between which parties?
A. Rav and Shmuel
B. Abaye and Rava
C. The First Tanna and Rabbi Yosei in the sefiḥim mishna (Shekalim)
D. Rabbi Meir and Rabbi Shimon
Correct: C — Rabba derives this as a tannaitic dispute from the mishna in Shekalim about watchmen of sefiḥim grain — the First Tanna implies viewing effects acquisition while Rabbi Yosei denies this.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the chain of liability for stones, once the donkey driver delivers the stones to the master builder, who is liable for any damage the stones cause?
A. The stonecutter
B. The chiseler
C. The donkey driver
D. The master builder
Correct: D — Liability transfers with each delivery. Once the donkey driver hands the stones to the master builder, the master builder bears responsibility for any damage from that point forward.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rava's clarification of the dispute about vegetables in the wall between two gardens, what do all opinions agree upon?
A. The leaves belong to the lower garden owner
B. The entire vegetable is split equally between both owners
C. The roots belong to the upper garden owner
D. Whoever first claims the vegetables acquires them
Correct: C — Rava clarifies that the roots indisputably belong to the upper garden owner, since the ground is his. The tannaitic dispute between Rabbi Meir and Rabbi Shimon concerns only the ownership of the leaves.
