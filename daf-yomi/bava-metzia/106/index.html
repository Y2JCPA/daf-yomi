🗓️ Does the Sabbatical Year Count as a Crop Year in a Field Sale?

Rav Ashi
If the Sabbatical Year is excluded because there is no produce in Israel, surely it should count — produce is available outside of Israel!
Rav Kahana
The Sabbatical Year is an abrogation of the King (God commanded it). We do not count it among the crop-years of the sale at all.
Mar Zutra (son of Rav Mari)
Then when one consecrates a field and redeems it, the Sabbatical Year should also not count toward the deduction of the redemption price!
Ravina
There is a difference: the verse says "according to the years remaining" (Lev. 25:16) — the Sabbatical Year is explicitly included in that count for redemption purposes.
Underlying Principle The Sabbatical Year is not a 'missing' crop year due to natural disaster — it is a divinely mandated cessation. Two different legal frameworks govern field sales versus consecration redemption.

🌪️ Who Bears the Loss? Three Wind-Blast Scenarios

Most of Valley Blasted

  • ✅ Tenant subtracts proportionally
  • Regional disaster = shared misfortune
  • Applies even if only tenant's fields were blasted — if valley majority also blasted
  • Loss attributed to communal bad fortune

Only Tenant's Fields Blasted

  • ❌ Tenant owes full amount
  • Owner argues: 'If it were my bad luck, a little would still remain' (Jer. 42:2)
  • Damage confined to tenant's fields = tenant's personal loss
  • No regional disaster = no reduction

All Owner's Fields Blasted (Valley Fine)

  • ⚠️ Tenant still owes full amount
  • Majority of valley was NOT blasted
  • Owner: 'My bad luck would still leave me something'
  • Shmuel: rule applies only when crops sprouted then were destroyed

🔄 How Many Times Must a Tenant Replant After Crop Failure?

First planting destroyed — tenant must replant (all opinions agree)
↓
Second planting destroyed — tenant must replant again (all opinions agree)
↓
Third planting destroyed — this is where the dispute begins
↓
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi: Presumptive status (חזקה) established after 2 occasions — tenant need NOT replant a 3rd time
↓
Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel: Presumptive status requires 3 occasions — tenant must replant a 3rd time, but not a 4th
↓
Reish Lakish adds: These limits apply only when crops sprouted and were then consumed. If they never sprouted, the landowner may always demand replanting — 'You have not yet fulfilled your obligation.'

🌿 Tenant Converts Crop Type — Is the Owner Compensated?

Owner specifies a crop type for the leased field
↓
Received for barley → planted wheat
Forbidden: wheat weakens soil more than barley — owner suffers real loss
Received for wheat → planted barley
Permitted: barley weakens soil less — owner suffers no loss
↓
Rav Ḥisda on Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel: 'The remnant of Israel shall not do iniquity' (Zeph. 3:13) — where no actual loss occurred, we do not penalize the tenant
