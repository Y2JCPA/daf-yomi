Intent to Misappropriate: Enough for Liability?

Beit Shammai
The verse says 'For every devar of trespass' (Exodus 22:8). Devar means 'word' — one is liable even for the thought of misappropriation!
Beit Hillel
But the verse also says 'Whether he has misappropriated his neighbor's goods' (Exodus 22:7) — liability requires actual misappropriation!
Beit Shammai
Then what does 'every matter of trespass' come to teach?
Beit Hillel
It teaches that if one instructed a slave or agent to misappropriate, he is liable — his speech creates liability for their actions.
Resolution Beit Hillel: Devar extends liability to one who orders misappropriation through others, not to mere intent.

Tilting vs. Lifting the Barrel

Tilted the Barrel

  • Bailee tilted barrel to take a quarter-log
  • If barrel broke: pays only for what he took
  • If wine fermented/spoiled: pays for entire barrel
  • Rabba: His actions were like 'arrows' — they caused the spoilage

Lifted the Barrel

  • Bailee lifted barrel to take wine
  • Shmuel: Even if he didn't yet take any wine
  • If it breaks, he is liable for the entire barrel
  • Reason: All the wine serves as a 'base' preserving the quarter-log he wants
Rav Ashi's Dilemma Does this apply to a purse of coins? A dinar can be preserved alone, unlike wine. Or perhaps a single coin is easily lost without the purse? Teku — unresolved.

Gold Coins: Currency or Commodity?

Rebbi (youth)
Gold coins are currency — they have higher value and are the standard of exchange.
Rebbi (old age)
Silver coins are currency — they circulate more widely, which is the true marker of money.
Rav Ashi
The mishna's own structure proves Rebbi's youth position: if silver were always currency, why state the obvious that silver is currency relative to copper?
Gemara
Even so — you might think copper perutot circulate more easily in certain places. The mishna teaches: silver is universally currency over copper.
Core Question Is 'currency' defined by higher value or by wider circulation?
