Promissory Note with Interest Written In

R' Meir
We penalize the lender — he may collect neither principal nor interest.
Rabbis
He may collect the principal but not the interest.
Gemara
R' Meir holds: We penalize regarding the permitted (principal) on account of the prohibited (interest).
Gemara
Rabbis hold: We do not penalize the permitted on account of the prohibited.
Core Principle קנסינן היתר אטו איסור — Do we extend a penalty beyond the actual violation? R' Meir says yes; the Rabbis say no.

Antedated vs. Postdated Documents

Antedated (מוקדמים)

  • Date written is EARLIER than the actual loan
  • Could create false liens on property sold between the two dates
  • Ruled INVALID entirely

Postdated (מאוחרים)

  • Date written is LATER than the actual loan
  • Disadvantages the lender (fewer years of lien priority)
  • Ruled VALID
Why Fully Invalid? Reish Lakish: Follows R' Meir — penalize the permitted on account of the prohibited.
R' Yochanan: Even Rabbis agree — a rabbinic decree lest the lender collect from the earlier (incorrect) date.

The Mortgaged Orchard Scheme

Lender holds mortgaged orchard for 3 years, consumes its produce
↓
Lender threatens: "Sell it to me — or I'll claim I bought it (חזקת ג' שנים)"
↓
Borrower's scheme: Transfers field to his minor son via gift deed, then "sells" to lender
↓
Sale is void — field belonged to the son, not the seller
↓
Can buyer collect from liened property?
Rava: No — this sale may not be written at all, so the document is completely invalid
Comparison to valid notes
When a debtor admits to a note, it can collect from liened property — but only if the document may be written
Rava's Distinction A document that may be written (records a real transaction) can collect from liened property. A document that may not be written (records a sham sale) is completely invalid.

Mishna: When May One Set Prices for Future Produce?

🚫 Cannot set a price BEFORE the market rate (שער) is publicized
↓
📢 Once market rate is set — may agree on a price even if seller has no produce yet
↓
🌾 If seller is 'first among reapers' — may set price for his own harvested stack
↓
🫒 Applies to: grain stacks, grape baskets, olive vats, potter's clay, baked plaster
↓
💩 Manure: Sages permit setting a price year-round; R' Yosei requires an existing pile
↓
💰 May set price at the 'highest rate' — largest quantity for the lowest price of the year
Why the Restriction? If produce is paid for in advance at a price below market value, the advance payment is treated as a loan, and the discount becomes interest (ריבית).
