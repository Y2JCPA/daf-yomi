Can Second Tithe Worth Less Than a Peruta Be Redeemed?

Mishna: Teruma has stringencies not so with second tithe
↓
Inference: Second tithe is negated in a simple majority?
↓
If Chizkiyya is correct (even sub-peruta tithe can be redeemed) → it's a davar she'yesh lo matirin
↓
Such items are never negated — even in 1:1000!
↓
Baraita resolves: Second tithe worth less than a peruta that entered and exited Jerusalem is negated in a majority
Core Tension The Gemara tests whether Chizkiyya's ruling (sub-peruta tithe is redeemable) can coexist with the rule that second tithe is negated in a majority — since redeemable items should never be negated.

Teruma vs. Second Tithe: Key Differences

Teruma / Bikkurim

  • Prohibited to non-priests (zarim)
  • Property of the kohen — can sell or betroth with it
  • Negated only at 1:100 ratio
  • Requires washing of hands
  • Tevul yom must wait for sunset

Ma'aser Sheni

  • Owner eats it himself in Jerusalem
  • Can be redeemed onto coins
  • Negated in a simple majority (1:1)
  • No special purity requirements for handling
  • These leniencies apply only when it cannot be redeemed
Key Point The Mishna lists teruma stringencies to highlight that second tithe is more lenient — including being negated more easily.

The Chizkiyya Problem: Davar She'Yesh Lo Matirin

Gemara
If Chizkiyya says even sub-peruta tithe can be redeemed, it's a davar she'yesh lo matirin — it has a permitting mechanism!
Gemara
Such items are never negated, even in 1:1000. But the baraita says second tithe is negated in a majority!
Answer
The case is where he did not redeem his tithe — he has no coins to redeem it upon. So it currently has no permitting factor.
Gemara
So let him bring other half-peruta produce and combine them to make a full peruta for redemption!
Answer
Second tithe by Torah law (the outside produce) and second tithe by rabbinic law (the mixture) do not combine.
דבר שיש לו מתירין An item whose prohibition can be permitted through a specific mechanism (like redemption) is never negated in a mixture — the Gemara tests whether this principle blocks the baraita.

Attempted Solutions to Redeem the Half-Peruta

❌ Bring other half-peruta produce to combine?
Torah-level and rabbinic-level tithe don't join
↓
❌ Bring half-peruta of demai?
Forbidden lest he bring from definite tevel
↓
❌ Bring 2 perutot, redeem 1½ perutot' worth?
Sanctity of 1 peruta goes on 1 peruta; half-peruta doesn't transfer
↓
❌ Bring an issar (8 perutot), redeem almost all?
Decree: lest he bring individual perutot instead
↓
✅ Conclusion: Sub-peruta second tithe that entered and exited Jerusalem is negated in a majority
Pattern The Gemara systematically proposes and rejects five creative workarounds, proving that this specific second tithe truly has no permitting factor and can be negated.

Jerusalem's Fallen Walls and Second Tithe

Gemara
Why can't the owner simply bring it back into Jerusalem and eat it there?
Answer
The case is where the walls of Jerusalem fell — the partitions (mechitzot) no longer stand.
Rava
Partitions enable eating by Torah law, but gathering produce into the city is rabbinic. Without walls, the decree shouldn't apply!
Gemara
Once the Sages decreed regarding partitions gathering produce, they did not distinguish between intact and fallen walls.
מחיצות Rava distinguishes two functions of Jerusalem's walls: enabling consumption (Torah law) vs. 'gathering' produce with sanctity (rabbinic). The Sages applied the gathering rule uniformly regardless of wall status.
