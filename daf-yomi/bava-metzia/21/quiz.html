Bava Metzia 21 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yitzchak, what is the dispersal ratio at which a finder may keep scattered produce?
A. 1 kav in 2×2 cubits
B. 1 kav in 4×4 cubits
C. 2 kav in 4×4 cubits
D. ½ kav in 4×4 cubits
Correct: B — R' Yitzchak establishes that 1 kav scattered over 4×4 cubits requires such great exertion to gather that the owner renounces ownership.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Abaye hold that ye'ush shelo mida'at (unconscious despair) is NOT valid despair?
A. Because the item might have a siman
B. Because the owner hasn't yet learned of the loss and cannot renounce what he doesn't know is missing
C. Because the Torah requires verbal renunciation
D. Because the finder has an obligation to search for the owner
Correct: B — Abaye's reasoning is that despair requires awareness — at the moment the finder picks up the item, the owner doesn't know it's lost, so he has not yet despaired.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara deflect the proof from 'scattered coins belong to the finder' against Abaye?
A. Coins have a siman (mint marks)
B. R' Yitzchak says a person constantly checks his money pouch, so the despair is conscious
C. Coins are too small to be worth returning
D. The Mishna refers only to damaged coins
Correct: B — R' Yitzchak's principle — a person feels his money pouch constantly — means the owner quickly discovers the loss, making the despair conscious, not unconscious.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Yehuda, when must a finder proclaim a round cake of pressed figs?
A. Always, because figs are valuable
B. When it contains an alteration like an earthenware shard inside it
C. Only if it weighs more than a kav
D. Only if found in a private domain
Correct: B — R' Yehuda says an alteration (shinui) such as a shard placed inside the fig cake serves as a distinguishing mark (siman) that the owner can use to reclaim it.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the Abaye-Rava dispute, what type of lost item do they both agree that unconscious despair does NOT apply?
A. Expensive items
B. Items found in a public domain
C. Items that have a distinguishing mark (siman)
D. Items swept away by a river
Correct: C — Both agree that for items with a siman, unconscious despair doesn't apply — the owner will say 'I have a siman' and expect to reclaim the item, so he never despairs.
