⚔️ The Dispute That Shattered Everything

Sages (Study Hall)
When does a sword, knife, dagger, spear, hand-saw, or scythe first become susceptible to ritual impurity — i.e., when is their manufacture complete?
Rabbi Yochanan
From when it is fired in the furnace — that firing is the completion of the manufacturing process.
Reish Lakish
No — from when it is scoured and polished in water after firing. Polishing is what truly completes the weapon.
Rabbi Yochanan
A bandit would certainly know the ways of his fellow bandits — you speak from your days as a robber!
Reish Lakish
How exactly have you benefited me by bringing me into Torah? Among bandits I was also called 'Rabbi'! What has truly changed?
Rabbi Yochanan
I brought you under the wings of the Divine Presence — that is how I benefited you!
💀 Tragic Outcome Rabbi Yochanan's mind was deeply offended. Reish Lakish fell gravely ill from distress and died. Rabbi Yochanan's sister (Reish Lakish's wife) begged Rabbi Yochanan to pray for him — for the sake of her children — but it was too late.

🪞 The Beauty of Rabbi Yochanan

בבא מציעא פד.
שיירי חולי ירושלים — Last Remnant of Jerusalem's Beauty Rabbi Yochanan declared: "I alone remain of the beautiful people of Jerusalem."

How to visualize his beauty: Take a silver goblet fresh from the smith — fill it with red pomegranate seeds — encircle its rim with a crown of red roses — place it between sunlight and shade. That radiance approximates Rabbi Yochanan's beauty.

The chain of beauty: Adam the First Man → Jacob our forefather → Rabbi Abbahu → Rav Kahana. (Rabbi Yochanan, lacking a beard, was not in this chain — yet his beauty was described as transcendent.)

He sat by the ritual bath entrance so that Jewish women emerging from their immersion would see him and conceive beautiful children. When questioned about the evil eye: "I descend from Joseph — the evil eye has no dominion over me."

🏛️ The Colossal Bodies of These Sages

Rabbi Yishmael b. Rabbi Yosei

  • So enormous that a pair of oxen could walk between him and Rabbi Elazar under their bellies
  • His organ was the size of a 9-kav jug
  • Roman noblewoman questioned how his children were conceived
  • Reply: our wives' bellies are even larger than ours
  • They answered her to prevent her from casting aspersions on Jewish children's lineage

Rabbi Elazar b. Rabbi Shimon

  • Equally enormous — oxen could pass under his belly without touching
  • His wife's belly was larger than his own
  • Despite extreme obesity, known for righteousness and Torah greatness
  • Body miraculously preserved without decay for ~18 years after death
  • Continued to adjudicate disputes posthumously from his attic

Rabbi Yochanan

  • His organ was the size of a 5-kav jug (some say 3-kav)
  • Described as the most beautiful person of his generation
  • Compared to: a silver goblet of pomegranates at sunrise
  • Declared himself last of Jerusalem's beautiful people
  • Beauty was so great it transcended the standard chain of comparison

⚰️ Rabbi Elazar's Body Preserved in the Attic

Dying request: Rabbi Elazar told his wife: "The Rabbis are angry at me for informing on their relatives to Rome. They won't properly bury me. When I die, lay me in my attic and do not be afraid of me."
↓
Sign of miraculous preservation: Every time a hair fell from his head, blood appeared in its place. His body showed no decomposition for approximately 18 years (some say 12).
↓
Posthumous jurisprudence: Litigants came to his doorway, each stated their case — and rulings emerged from the attic. The community did not know he had died.
↓
The worm: One day his wife saw a worm from his ear. She was distressed; Rabbi Elazar appeared in a dream: "This is not from sin — only from once hearing someone disgraced and not rebuking it."
↓
Final burial on Yom Kippur Eve: When townspeople were preoccupied with Festival preparations, the Rabbis summoned men from Biri who carried his bier to burial with weeping and great honor.
