Bava Metzia 84 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What was Reish Lakish doing when Rabbi Yochanan first encountered him at the Jordan River?
A. Studying Torah alone by the riverbank
B. Leading a band of marauders as their chief
C. Working as a traveling merchant
D. Serving as a Roman tax collector
Correct: B — Reish Lakish was the leader of a band of marauders. Rabbi Yochanan recognized his extraordinary strength and invited him to study Torah, offering his own sister in marriage as an inducement.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What legal question was being disputed when Rabbi Yochanan made his hurtful remark to Reish Lakish?
A. Whether stolen goods require double payment
B. When metal weapons become susceptible to ritual impurity
C. The definition of a valid sale in the marketplace
D. Who bears liability when a borrowed ox is injured
Correct: B — They were debating when a sword, knife, or similar weapon becomes susceptible to ritual impurity — Rabbi Yochanan said from when fired in the furnace; Reish Lakish said from when scoured in water afterward. Rabbi Yochanan then quipped that a bandit would know bandit ways, implying Reish Lakish still thought like a criminal.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Yochanan say the evil eye had no power over him when he sat by the ritual bath entrance?
A. Because he descended from Joseph, over whom the evil eye has no dominion
B. Because he recited a special protective prayer each morning
C. Because he was born with a protective amulet
D. Because Elijah the Prophet had personally blessed him
Correct: A — Rabbi Yochanan cited his descent from Joseph. The verse states Joseph's descendants shall multiply 'like fish in the sea' — just as fish in the sea are covered by water and thus beyond the evil eye's reach, so Joseph's descendants are protected.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How did Rabbi Elazar son of Rabbi Shimon's body serve the community after his death?
A. His tomb became a site of miraculous healings
B. Litigants came to his door, stated their cases, and rulings emerged from the attic
C. His Torah manuscripts were distributed to all the synagogues
D. He appeared in dreams to give legal guidance to the Rabbis
Correct: B — For approximately 18 years, his body lay in his attic per his dying request. Litigants would come to the doorway, each present their side, and a ruling would emerge — the community not realizing he had died.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi call the wife of Rabbi Elazar son of Rabbi Shimon an 'evil woman'?
A. She had informed on her husband to the Roman authorities
B. She squandered the family's Torah library after his death
C. She rejected Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's marriage proposal rudely
D. She prevented women from bringing menstrual blood questions to him, causing many fewer children to be born in Israel
Correct: D — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi lamented that by keeping Rabbi Elazar isolated from visitors for health reasons, his wife prevented women from bringing him their questions about menstrual blood. Had he ruled their blood pure — as he did when students brought him 60 samples and he declared all pure — those women could have had marital relations and borne children.
