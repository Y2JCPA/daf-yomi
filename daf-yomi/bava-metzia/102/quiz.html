Bava Metzia 102 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
When a renter vacates a rented house, what must he do with the mezuza he affixed?
A. Take it with him — he paid for it
B. Leave it affixed on the doorpost
C. Remove it and sell it back to the landlord
D. Give it to the next tenant only if that tenant is Jewish
Correct: B — The baraita explicitly rules that a renter may not take the mezuza when he leaves — he must leave it in place for the next occupant.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yosei b. Rabbi Ḥanina, under what condition does a courtyard effect acquisition for its owner?
A. Only when the owner is physically standing in the courtyard
B. Only when the owner explicitly declares his intent beforehand
C. Automatically, even without the owner's knowledge, if the courtyard is secured
D. Only for items the owner placed there himself
Correct: C — Rabbi Yosei b. Rabbi Ḥanina holds that a secured courtyard acts like the owner's hand and acquires for him automatically, even without awareness.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rava rules that an item traveling through a courtyard's airspace is not acquired by the courtyard owner. What is his reason?
A. Airspace is never legally part of the courtyard
B. An item that will not ultimately come to rest in the courtyard is not treated as having rested there
C. The owner must physically see the item for acquisition to occur
D. Only items touching the ground level of a courtyard can be acquired
Correct: B — Rava's principle: if an item passes through airspace but will not land in the courtyard, it is not deemed to have rested there — a prerequisite for courtyard acquisition.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When a house is rented simply 'for one year' and that year is intercalated, who benefits from the extra month?
A. The landlord, since he owns the property
B. They split the extra month equally
C. The renter, who pays nothing extra for the intercalated month
D. The court determines fair value and divides it
Correct: C — The mishna rules clearly: when rental is defined as 'for one year,' the renter benefits from an intercalated year — the extra month is included at no additional charge.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rava, from which moment does the shiluach haken (mother bird dispatching) obligation begin?
A. When the egg is fully laid and resting in the nest
B. When the majority of the egg has emerged from the mother's body
C. When the mother bird first sits upon the egg
D. When the courtyard owner becomes aware of the egg's presence
Correct: B — Rava teaches that the shiluach haken obligation commences once the majority of the egg has emerged — even before the egg fully lands — while courtyard acquisition only completes upon full emergence.
