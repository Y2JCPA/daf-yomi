⚖️ Contradictory Rental Terms: Year vs. Month

Landlord
The rent is twelve gold dinars per year — so the intercalated month is mine!
Renter
But we also agreed on one gold dinar per month — I simply pay monthly, extra month included!
Rabban Shimon b. Gamliel
The two expressions are contradictory — divide the disputed month between landlord and renter equally.
Rav
Follow the last expression stated: the monthly rate controls, so the renter pays for the extra month.
Shmuel
Rabban Shimon's ruling applies only when the landlord comes to court mid-month — then we split the remaining days.
Mishna Baseline When rental was defined simply as 'one year' and the year is intercalated, the extra month belongs to the renter at no extra charge.

🚪 Mezuza: Renter's Obligations

Renter moves in — the obligation to affix a mezuza rests entirely on the renter, not the landlord
↓
Renter affixes the mezuza at his own expense and keeps it for the duration of the tenancy
↓
Renter moves out — he may NOT remove the mezuza and take it with him
↓
He must leave it affixed on the doorpost for the benefit of the next occupant
↓
Exception discussed elsewhere: renting to a non-Jew permits removal to prevent possible desecration

🌿 Does a Courtyard Acquire Automatically?

Rabbi Yosei b. R. Ḥanina
A person's courtyard effects acquisition for him even without his knowledge — it acts as an extension of his hand.
Baraita Objection
If one declares 'let my courtyard acquire lost items for me today,' he acquires nothing — the declaration has no legal effect!
Gemara Resolution
That baraita speaks of an unsecured courtyard — only a secured courtyard can effect automatic acquisition.
Rava
An item passing through airspace that will not ultimately come to rest in the courtyard is never deemed to have rested there — no acquisition occurs.
Key Distinction Secured courtyard → acquires automatically. Unsecured courtyard → does not acquire at all.

💩 Refuse & Manure: Who Owns What?

Renter Owns

  • Ashes from the oven and stove (explicitly stated in mishna)
  • Refuse collected directly into renter's attached vessel (Abaye)
  • Manure entering renter's vessel fixed to the cow's rear — never touches courtyard (Abaye)
  • Refuse passing through courtyard airspace that never comes to rest (Rava)

Landlord Owns

  • Manure deposited in the rented courtyard — general rule (mishna)
  • Manure from stray oxen that passed through and stood in the courtyard
  • Refuse in the cowshed located within the rented courtyard (Abaye / Rav Ashi)
  • Any item that fully comes to rest in the landlord's secured courtyard

🐦 Shiluach HaKen & Courtyard Acquisition

Doves nest in an owner's dovecote or attic and lay eggs
↓
Has the majority of the egg emerged from the mother bird?
Majority emerged, not fully
Shiluach haken obligation begins (Rava) — but courtyard has not yet acquired the egg
Fully emerged and resting
Courtyard acquires the egg — but mother is still present, so shiluach haken still applies (Rav citing Rav Yehuda)
↓
Conclusion: In neither case may a stranger take the eggs without dispatching the mother — they are either not yet acquired or protected by rabbinic ordinance
