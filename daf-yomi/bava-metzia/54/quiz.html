Bava Metzia 54 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yoshiya, when the Torah says to add 'one-fifth,' what fraction of the original principal does this represent?
A. 1/5 of the principal
B. 1/4 of the principal
C. 1/6 of the principal
D. 1/3 of the principal
Correct: B — R' Yoshiya holds the fifth is calculated 'from without' (מלבר) — meaning the item plus the fifth total five parts. So the fifth is actually 1/4 of the original principal (e.g., 5 out of 20).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara prove that the one-fifth is calculated 'from without'?
A. From a verse in Leviticus directly
B. From the bidding scenario where 20 + one-fifth = 25
C. From a statement of R' Yochanan
D. From the laws of demai
Correct: B — The Gemara shows that when an owner offered 20 and the fifth is 5 (totaling 25), the fifth equals 1/4 of 20 — proving it is calculated from without, as 1/5 of the total (25) rather than 1/5 of the principal.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rav Pappa's reinterpretation of the R' Eliezer vs R' Yehoshua dispute?
A. They disagree about whether the fifth is 1/4 or 1/5
B. They both agree the fifth prevents consumption
C. They both agree it doesn't prevent consumption; they disagree about concern for negligence
D. They disagree about whether the law applies to hekdesh
Correct: C — Rav Pappa suggests both agree the fifth doesn't technically prevent consumption. R' Yehoshua is concerned the owner will be negligent and never pay the fifth, while R' Eliezer trusts the owner will pay later.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does R' Yochanan say that for hekdesh, even R' Yehoshua would agree the owner may use the property after paying only the principal?
A. Because hekdesh is less strict than ma'aser sheni
B. Because the Temple treasurers demand payment in the marketplace, preventing negligence
C. Because the Torah explicitly exempts hekdesh from the fifth
D. Because second consecration never requires a fifth
Correct: B — R' Yochanan explains that since the Temple treasurers actively demand payment, there is no concern for negligence — the owner will certainly pay the fifth, so he may use the property immediately.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Yehoshua ben Levi, when does the owner NOT add one-fifth upon redemption?
A. When redeeming ma'aser sheni
B. When redeeming first consecration
C. When redeeming second consecration (sanctity transferred via redemption)
D. When redeeming teruma that was eaten
Correct: C — R' Yehoshua ben Levi distinguishes: for first consecration (direct dedication) one adds a fifth, but for second consecration (where sanctity transferred to the redemption money by association), no fifth is added — 'he who consecrated it' refers only to active consecration.
