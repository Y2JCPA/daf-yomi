The One-Fifth Payment: Calculation, Scope & Consequences

Owner redeems ma'aser sheni or hekdesh
↓
Must add one-fifth (chomesh) — but calculated how?
R' Yoshiya
1/5 from without = 1/4 of principal
R' Yonatan
1/5 from within = 1/5 of principal
↓
Does failure to pay the fifth prevent consumption?
R' Eliezer
No — principal alone suffices for redemption
R' Yehoshua
Yes — fifth is essential to redemption
Key Theme This daf systematically examines the chomesh (one-fifth payment): how it's calculated, when it's indispensable, and whether it recursively generates further fifths.

Tannaitic Dispute: One-Fifth From Within or Without?

R' Yoshiya
"One-fifth from without (מלבר)" — the item plus its fifth total five parts. The chomesh is 1/4 of the original value.
R' Yonatan
"One-fifth from within (מלגו)" — the chomesh is literally 1/5 of the principal itself.
Gemara
The mathematical proof from the bidding scenario confirms: Learn from it that one-fifth is calculated from without — the halakha follows R' Yoshiya.
Leviticus 27:27 "And shall add unto it one-fifth part thereof" — the ambiguity of 'thereof' drives this dispute.

Does Failure to Pay the Fifth Prevent Consumption?

Owner redeems ma'aser sheni worth 4 dinars
↓
Pays the principal (4 dinars) but not the chomesh
↓
May he eat the produce outside Yerushalayim?
Position A
Produce worth 4 is redeemed with 4 — the fifth is a separate personal obligation → may eat
Position B
Produce worth 4 is redeemed only with 5 dinars — redemption incomplete → may not eat
Proof from Demai Ravina proves: for demai (doubtfully tithed), one pays principal but not the fifth — implying the fifth doesn't prevent consumption even by Torah law.

R' Eliezer vs R' Yehoshua: Eating Without the Fifth

R' Eliezer
One who paid the principal but not the chomesh — may eat the redeemed second tithe.
R' Yehoshua
May not eat — the redemption is incomplete without the one-fifth payment.
Rebbi
R' Eliezer is correct on Shabbat (leniency in deference to the day); R' Yehoshua is correct on weekdays.
Rav Pappa
Perhaps all agree the fifth doesn't prevent consumption — they only disagree about concern for negligence (שמא יפשע): will the owner eventually pay?
R' Yochanan
For hekdesh, all agree: the treasurers demand payment in the marketplace — no concern for negligence. The owner may use immediately.
Key Insight The debate may not be about the legal status of the fifth at all, but about practical enforcement — whether we trust the owner to pay later.

One-Fifth of the One-Fifth: Across Three Domains

Robbery (גזילה)

  • Pays principal + 1/5 to victim
  • If he swears falsely about the 1/5 — it becomes new principal
  • Adds 1/5 on top of that 1/5
  • Continues until amount < one peruta

Teruma (תרומה)

  • One who eats teruma unwittingly pays principal + 1/5
  • If he then eats the 1/5 payment (which is teruma) — pays 1/5 of the 1/5
  • Explicitly taught in Mishnah Terumot 6:1
  • Derived from 'veyasaf' + 'chamishitav' (plural)

Consecrated Property (הקדש)

  • Owner redeems hekdesh: adds 1/5
  • Does one-fifth of the one-fifth apply?
  • Rav Tavyumei/Abaye: YES — Torah juxtaposes 1/5 to valuation
  • Just as valuation requires 1/5, so does the 1/5 itself
Rava's Framework For robbery and teruma — one-fifth of the one-fifth is explicit. For second tithe — it is never written, taught, or even asked. For hekdesh — the Gemara must derive it.
