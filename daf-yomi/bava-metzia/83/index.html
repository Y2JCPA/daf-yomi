⚖️ Bailee Liability: Unpaid vs. Paid

Shomer Chinam (Unpaid Bailee)

  • Takes oath: 'I swear I was not negligent'
  • If negligent → pays
  • If lost without negligence → oath exempts
  • Must bring witnesses if evidence exists

Shomer Sachar (Paid Bailee)

  • Must pay even without negligence
  • Exception: theft or loss beyond control
  • Takes oath only in limited circumstances
  • Higher standard of care than unpaid bailee

⚖️ How Does the Bailee Phrase His Oath?

Rava
The oath is phrased: 'I take an oath that I broke it unintentionally.' The bailee must specify the accidental nature of the damage.
Rabbi Yehuda
An unpaid bailee takes an oath while a paid bailee pays — each in accordance with his own law and level of duty.
Gemara (Challenge)
But why does a paid bailee take an oath at all? Even if he was not negligent, he must still pay! And for an unpaid bailee — where there are witnesses, let him simply bring proof and be exempt!
Isi ben Yehuda
The verse 'and it died or was hurt or was driven away' teaches that an oath applies only where no witnesses are present — confirming the bailee's exemption requires his sworn testimony.
Key Principle The oath deters fraud and establishes the bailee's credibility about circumstances of loss when no independent witnesses exist.

🍷 Rava's Rulings on Broken Barrels

Case 1: Man transports barrel of wine through the beristeka (market) of Meḥoza — it breaks on a wall protrusion
↓
Rava examines: Is this market known for such protrusions?
↓
Known Hazard
Porter should have been aware → liable for damage
Unusual Obstacle
Not foreseeable → exempt from payment
↓
Case 2: Agent buys 400 pitchers of wine on another's behalf — the wine ferments
↓
Rava: If 400 pitchers fermented, this would generate publicity. Go and bring proof the wine was already fermenting when purchased — or you bear the loss.

👷 Rav Ḥiyya bar Yosef: The Pole Decree

בַּעֲגָרָא — a carrying pole shared by two workers
Decree in Sikhra Those who carry loads on a pole (be'agra) and the item breaks must pay half. Why? A pole is used by two carriers simultaneously — neither individual bears full control over the item. Since the liability is inherently split between the two, each pays only half of the damages.

🕵️ Rabbi Elazar's Method: Identifying Thieves

Background: A royal officer (parhagavna) tasked with arresting thieves asks Rabbi Elazar, son of Rabbi Shimon, for advice
↓
Step 1: Go to a tavern at the 4th hour of the day — when honest workers are busy
↓
Step 2: Observe who is drinking wine at that unusual daytime hour and sleeping
↓
Step 3 — Evaluate the suspect: Metal tools nearby → laborer who rose early to work. Dozing with books → Torah scholar who studied since dawn. Works at night → innocent.
↓
Step 4: If none of these apply and he has no legitimate excuse — he is likely a thief. Arrest him.
↓
Consequence: Rabbi Elazar is appointed chief officer, but later suffers profound guilt over condemning a laundryman he may have misjudged
