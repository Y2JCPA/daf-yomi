🕊️ Death of a Torah Giant

טָהוֹר טָהוֹר — Pure! Pure!
Dual Narrative Rabba bar Naḥmani (Pumbedita, 4th c.) fled Roman persecution, was summoned to heaven to resolve a nega dispute between God and the heavenly academy, and died declaring טָהוֹר — Pure! Notes fell from heaven announcing his passing. The daf also expounds Abraham's legendary hospitality and God's reciprocal care for Israel in the wilderness.

⚖️ The Heavenly Academy's Dispute on Nega

Holy One, Blessed be He
The person is pure — the spot (nega) appeared before the white hair grew
Heavenly Academy
The person is impure — the white hair appeared before the spot (טומאה קדמה)
Heavenly Academy
Who can arbitrate? Only Rabba bar Naḥmani — he once declared: 'I am expert in laws of nega'im and ohalot!'
Divine Voice (Bat Kol)
'Happy are you, Rabba bar Naḥmani — your body is pure and your soul left you with the word: Pure!'
Context In nega tzaraat (Leviticus 13), if the white hair appeared in the spot, the person is impure; if the spot came without white hair, he is pure. The order of appearance is legally determinative — and was genuinely disputed in heaven.

👑 Abraham's Feast vs. Solomon's Feast

Abraham (Genesis 18)

  • Three calves for only three guests
  • Served personally — ran to the herd himself
  • Three tongues with mustard (one per angel)
  • Butter, milk, and bread as well
  • Intimate, personal hospitality

King Solomon (1 Kings 5)

  • 30 kor fine flour + 60 kor meal daily
  • 10 fat oxen, 20 pasture oxen, 100 sheep
  • Deer, gazelles, roebucks, fatted fowl
  • 1,000 wives each prepared a full feast — for all Israel and Judah
  • Institutional, kingdom-scale provision

🥩 The Exegesis of Three Calves — Rabbinic Derivation

Rav Yehuda citing Rav: 'A calf' (1) + 'tender' (2nd) + 'and good' (3rd) — the conjunctive vav adds a third → Abraham prepared three calves
↓
Objection (Rabba bar Ulla / Rav Hoshaya): 'He gave it to the servant' — singular servant, implying one calf only
↓
Answer: One servant attended to all three calves; the singular refers to the attendant, not the number of calves — the earlier derivation stands
↓
Purpose (Rav Ḥanan bar Rava): Three calves were prepared so each of the three angels received one tongue with mustard — a special delicacy reserved for honored guests
↓
Who were the angels? Michael (announced Isaac's birth to Sarah), Raphael (healed Abraham's circumcision), Gabriel (overturned Sodom)
