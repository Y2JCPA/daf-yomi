QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Rabba bar Naḥmani's enemies inform on him to the Roman authorities?
A. He refused to pay his own taxes to Rome
B. His 12,000 disciples would abandon work twice yearly for lectures, evading royal taxes
C. He harbored fugitives in his academy
D. He publicly denounced the Roman emperor
Correct: B — The informers claimed that Rabba bar Naḥmani's students would leave their work during the Kallah months (Adar and Elul) to attend his lectures — thereby causing the king to lose tax revenue from those workers.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was the nature of the dispute in the heavenly academy that required Rabba bar Naḥmani?
A. Whether a sacrifice brought on an uncertain day was valid
B. Whether an oral bill of divorce required a scribe
C. Whether the nega spot preceded the white hair (pure) or the white hair preceded the spot (impure)
D. Whether a doubtful Shabbat violation required atonement
Correct: C — In the laws of nega tzaraat, if a white hair appeared within a spot, the person is impure; if the spot appeared without white hair, he is pure. The heavenly academy disputed the order of appearance — God ruled pure, the academy ruled impure.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥanan bar Rava, why did Abraham prepare three separate calves for three guests?
A. He did not know how many guests would arrive
B. Three calves were required by law for three guests
C. To serve each guest one tongue with mustard as a special delicacy
D. To honor each of the three angels' different dietary customs
Correct: C — Rav Ḥanan bar Rava teaches that Abraham prepared three calves — one per angel (Michael, Raphael, Gabriel) — in order to serve each guest a tongue with mustard, which was considered a particular delicacy.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why was the Angel of Death initially unable to take Rabba bar Naḥmani's soul?
A. Rabba bar Naḥmani had earned sufficient merit to defer death
B. God had not yet given the angel permission to approach
C. Rabba's lips never ceased from Torah study, so the angel could find no opening
D. The royal messenger's presence blocked the angel
Correct: C — The Angel of Death could not approach because Rabba bar Naḥmani's mouth did not cease from Torah study. Only when wind howled through the reeds and startled him into silence did the angel seize the opportunity to take his soul.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the principle Rav derives from comparing Abraham's hospitality actions to God's actions for Israel?
A. Hospitality guarantees long life and wealth for one's descendants
B. Whatever Abraham did personally, God did personally for Israel; whatever Abraham did through an agent, God repaid through an agent
C. God always surpasses human hospitality in scale and grandeur
D. Abraham's merit alone sustained Israel through forty years of wilderness
Correct: B — Rav teaches strict measure-for-measure reciprocity: Abraham ran to the herd himself → God sent quail Himself; Abraham stood by them → God stood at Horeb. But 'let water be fetched' (via agent Eliezer) → water came through Moses, God's agent.
