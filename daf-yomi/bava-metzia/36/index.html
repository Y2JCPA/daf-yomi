Transferring Bailments & The Limits of Liability

R' Yirmeya
Presents a systematic framework: false oaths by renters and borrowers yield sin-offerings (no monetary denial) or guilt-offerings (monetary denial) depending on what actually happened vs. what they claimed.
Rav
A bailee who transfers a deposit to another bailee is exempt — he entrusted it to a competent person (da'at).
R' Yochanan
He is liable even for ones — the owner can say: 'I don't want my property with someone else.'
Rava
The halakha follows R' Yochanan — the owner can also say: 'You are trustworthy to me under oath; that person is not.'
Core Theme This daf explores whether a bailee's duty is about outcome (keeping the item safe) or about relationship (the owner's trust in a specific person).

Why Does R' Yirmeya Teach This?

או נפש כי תשבע לבטא בשפתים
Excluding R' Ami R' Ami holds that an oath administered by the court does not incur a sin-offering for a false oath on an utterance — only self-initiated oaths do, based on "Or if a soul takes an oath clearly to utter with lips" (Leviticus 5:4).

R' Yirmeya's cases teach that the halakha is not like R' Ami — one is liable for a sin-offering even for court-administered oaths.

Bailee Who Transfers to Another Bailee

Rav
A bailee who transfers the deposit to another bailee is exempt in the same cases he would be exempt himself.
Abaye (for Rav)
Even a paid bailee → unpaid bailee (diminished safeguarding) is exempt. Why? He entrusted it to a mentally competent person (da'at).
R' Yochanan
He is liable — even for ones! The owner says: 'I don't want my deposit with another person.'
Abaye (for R' Yochanan)
Even an unpaid → paid bailee (enhanced safeguarding) is liable. The owner's trust is in the specific person, not the level of care.
Rav Ḥisda's Note Rav never stated this ruling explicitly — it was inferred from his ruling in the case of the gardeners' spades left with an old woman.

The Gardeners' Spades — Source of Rav's Ruling

Gardeners deposit spades daily with an old woman
↓
One day, they deposit with one of the gardeners instead
↓
He hears a wedding and transfers the spades to the old woman
↓
While he's away, the spades are stolen
↓
Rav's Ruling
Exempt — he transferred to a competent person
Implication
A bailee who transfers custody is not automatically liable
Rav Ḥisda This case is the real source of Rav's position — his general rule was inferred, not stated directly.

Rava's Final Ruling + The Oath Trustworthiness Factor

Paid → Unpaid Bailee

  • Diminished safeguarding level
  • Obviously liable — unpaid bailee exempt where paid is not
  • Owner clearly disadvantaged

Unpaid → Paid Bailee

  • Enhanced safeguarding level
  • Still liable! Owner says:
  • "YOU are trustworthy to me under oath"
  • "That person is NOT trustworthy to me"
  • Personal trust cannot be transferred
Rava's Halakha A bailee who transfers a deposit to another bailee is always liable — the owner's trust is personal and non-transferable.
