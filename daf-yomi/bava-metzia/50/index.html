The Dilemma: When Is Less Than 1/6 Waived?

The Sages
According to the Rabbis, one has only until he can show the item to a merchant to claim ona'ah. But for less than one-sixth — is the waiver immediate?
Questioner
Or does even a sub-1/6 discrepancy require the same waiting period before the transaction is final?
The Sages
If the waiting period applies to both, what's the difference between 1/6 and less than 1/6?
Gemara
At exactly 1/6, the exploited party can renege or demand the difference. At less than 1/6, the buyer keeps the item and gets the difference back — but cannot nullify the sale.
Core Question The Gemara probes whether sub-threshold discrepancies are forgiven instantly or only after the standard waiting period.

Rabbi Tarfon vs. The Rabbis: Ona'ah Thresholds

Rabbi Tarfon

  • Exploitation threshold: one-third
  • Buyer can renege for the entire day
  • A discrepancy of 1/6 is merely waived
  • More generous to merchants initially

The Rabbis (Chachamim)

  • Exploitation threshold: one-sixth
  • Buyer can claim only within time to show a merchant
  • A discrepancy of 1/6 = exploitation with refund
  • Shorter window but lower threshold
Historical Note The merchants of Lod initially rejoiced at Rabbi Tarfon's ruling (higher threshold = fewer claims), but reversed course when they learned the claim window was an entire day.

The Merchants of Lod: A Story of Changing Preferences

🏛️ Rabbi Tarfon announces: Ona'ah threshold is one-third, not one-sixth
↓
🎉 Merchants of Lod rejoice — fewer transactions qualify as exploitation!
↓
⏰ Rabbi Tarfon adds: The exploited party can renege all day
↓
😟 Merchants realize: a full-day return window is far worse for business
↓
🔄 Merchants say: 'Let Rabbi Tarfon leave us as we were!' — they revert to the Rabbis' ruling
Practical Lesson A higher threshold meant fewer claims, but a longer window meant more uncertainty — the merchants preferred the Rabbis' shorter, more predictable system.

If Position B: What's the difference between 1/6 and >1/6?
Answer: At 1/6 only the exploited party reneges; at >1/6, both parties can renege
↓
Rava's conclusion: Both nullification and exploitation claims are limited to the time to show a merchant
Resolution The Gemara argues that nullification is uncommon, so the merchants of Lod didn't factor it into their calculation — but Rava rules it too has a time limit.
