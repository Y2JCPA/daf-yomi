5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yitzchak, why is a bailee liable for binding coins in a cloth and slinging them behind him?
A. Because the Torah requires money to be 'in your hand' even when bound
B. Because cloth is not a secure container
C. Because carrying money in public invites theft
D. Because a bailee must always bury money in the ground
Correct: A — R' Yitzchak derives from Deuteronomy 14:25 ('bind up the money in your hand') that even bound money must remain in one's hand — not behind one's back.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say that praying for blessing AFTER measuring grain is a tefillat shav (prayer in vain)?
A. Because grain loses holiness once measured
B. Because blessing rests only on what is concealed from the eye, and measured items are known quantities
C. Because the prayer must be said while standing in the field
D. Because measured grain already belongs to the buyer
Correct: B — Blessing is found only in matters hidden from the eye. Once grain is weighed, measured, or counted, its quantity is known and blessing cannot rest upon it.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara rule that burying money requires only 1 handbreadth, unlike ḥametz which requires 3?
A. Money is smaller than bread
B. Ḥametz must be hidden from a dog's scent (3 tefaḥim), but money only from the eye (1 tefaḥ suffices)
C. The Rabbis were lenient about money since it's harder to bury
D. Three handbreadths would make the money too difficult to retrieve
Correct: B — Rav Ashi distinguishes: ḥametz burial depth is about defeating a dog's nose (scent), while money burial is about hiding from a thief's eyes — a much lower threshold.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the willow hut case, why is the established halakha that the bailee is liable?
A. Because a willow hut is never valid for safeguarding anything
B. Because the money was stolen, not burned, and theft was foreseeable
C. Because when negligence initiates the chain of events, the bailee is liable even if the final loss is accidental
D. Because the bailee should have insured the deposit
Correct: C — The halakha follows the principle: teḥilato b'peshia v'sofo b'ones — ḥayyav. The willow hut was negligent safeguarding (fire risk), and even though the actual loss was theft (accident), the initial negligence creates liability.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the deposited hops case, under what condition would the bailee clearly be liable?
A. If the depositor's hops were of higher quality
B. If the bailee's own hops were closer and the brewer delayed long enough that the bailee should have realized the brewer went to the wrong pile
C. If the brewer was a paid employee of the bailee
D. If the beer turned out well and the bailee profited
Correct: B — If the bailee's hops were closer and the brewer took longer than needed, the bailee should have realized the brewer was using the deposited hops. By not objecting, he revealed he was amenable — making him liable.
