Safeguarding Deposits: From Liability to Blessing

Mishna: A bailee who binds coins behind him, gives them to minors, or locks them inadequately is liable
↓
But if he safeguarded in the manner of bailees and it was stolen — exempt
↓
What is the proper manner of safeguarding money?
Shmuel
Burial in the ground is the only valid safeguarding for money
R' Yitzchak
Money must be in your hand — and divide wealth into thirds
Key Principle The standard of care for a bailee evolves with the sophistication of thieves — what counts as proper safeguarding changes with the times.

Action TakenVerdictReason
Bound coins in cloth, slung behind him❌ LiableNot in his hand (Deut. 14:25)
Gave to minor son or daughter❌ LiableMinors cannot properly safeguard

R' Yitzchak's Financial Wisdom

✋ Keep money accessible — always have funds available in your possession
↓
📊 Divide into thirds: ⅓ buried in ground, ⅓ in business (bifrakmatya), ⅓ in hand
↓
👁️ Blessing comes in concealment — only in matters hidden from the eye
↓
🙏 Pray before measuring grain — once counted, blessing cannot rest on it
↓
⚠️ Praying after measuring is a tefillat shav — a prayer in vain
Verse "The Lord will command blessing with you in your storehouses" (Deut. 28:8) — where grain is concealed from sight.

Evolution of Hiding Places for Money

Shmuel: Safeguarding money = burial in the ground
↓
🕵️ Rummagers became common (dig up buried items)
↓
New standard: Hide in roof beams
↓
🔨 Dismantlers became common (search beams)
↓
New standard: Hide between wall bricks
↓
🔊 Tappers became common (tap walls to find hollows)
↓
Final standard: Hide in the handbreadth nearest the ground or ceiling of a wall
Practical Halakha Rava adds: Shmuel concedes money can also be safeguarded in the wall or between corners of the house. The standard adapts to the prevailing threat.

How Deep Must Money Be Buried?

Rav Aḥa b. Rav Yosef
We learned that a dog can detect ḥametz through 3 handbreadths of rubble. Must money also be buried 3 handbreadths deep?
Rav Ashi
No! Bread must be 3 handbreadths deep because of scent. Money must be hidden from the eye — scent is irrelevant.
Rafram of Sikhera
One handbreadth is sufficient for burying money.
Distinction Ḥametz burial requires 3 tefaḥim to defeat a dog's nose. Money burial requires only 1 tefaḥ to defeat a thief's eyes.

Willow Hut Case: Teḥilato B'Peshia V'Sofo B'Ones

First Opinion

  • Placing money in a willow hut = negligence re: fire
  • But it is valid safeguarding against thieves
  • Negligence began the chain → LIABLE
  • Teḥilato b'peshia v'sofo b'ones = ḥayyav

Second Opinion (Yesh Omrim)

  • Placing money in a willow hut = negligence re: fire
  • But the actual theft = accident (ones)
  • Since the loss was from theft, not fire → EXEMPT
  • Teḥilato b'peshia v'sofo b'ones = patur
Halakha The law follows the first opinion: When negligence initiates the chain of events, even if the ultimate loss is accidental, the bailee is liable.
