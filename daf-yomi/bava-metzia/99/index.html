⚖️ Rav vs. Shmuel: Liability When Cow Is Sent via Slave

Shmuel
The mishna refers to a Hebrew slave — the master does not acquire his person. Therefore items in the slave's hand are not in the master's domain, and the borrower becomes liable upon the cow reaching the slave.
Rav
Even with a Canaanite slave, the borrower can be liable — if the borrower told the lender: 'Hit the cow with a stick and let it come on its own.' The cow's autonomous walk into the borrower's domain creates liability.
Baraita Challenge
A baraita states: if sent via son, agent, or slave, the borrower is liable; via the lender's own hand, the lender is liable. This implies slave = liability — complicating Rav's reading of the mishna.
Rav (Revised)
Rav refines: The mishna is the 'hit it, let it walk' case. The baraita's slave is Canaanite. These are different cases — not contradictory. Rav Naḥman confirms: 'hit and walk' = the borrower's courtyard acquired the cow.
Core Dispute When does a borrowed animal pass into the borrower's legal domain? The answer depends on the identity of the messenger and the nature of the borrower's instructions.

🧑‍🦱 Hebrew Slave vs. Canaanite Slave in Borrowing Law

Hebrew Slave (עבד עברי)

  • Master does not acquire his person
  • Items in his hand are not in master's possession
  • Functions like an independent agent
  • Shmuel: mishna's slave is Hebrew
  • Delivery to Hebrew slave → borrower liable

Canaanite Slave (עבד כנעני)

  • Master fully acquires his person
  • Items in his hand are in the master's possession
  • Cannot normally transfer liability to borrower
  • Baraita: uses Canaanite slave case
  • Liability requires special circumstances (e.g., 'hit and walk')

📜 Rabbi Elazar: Acquisition Parallels — Buyers, Bailees, and Land

Rabbinic enactment for buyers: Movable property is acquired via meshicha (pulling/lifting) — money alone no longer finalizes the sale.
↓
Same rule for bailees: Borrowers, renters, and paid custodians acquire their custodial responsibilities from the moment they pull the item.
↓
Land acquisition: Three valid methods — (1) giving money, (2) deed of sale (שטר), or (3) act of possession (חזקה).
↓
Rental of land is acquired by the same three methods — money, deed, or chazakah — mirroring the rules of purchase.
↓
Rav Huna vs. Rabbi Elazar: Rav Huna = liability begins at first use; Rabbi Elazar = liability begins at meshicha, even before any use.

🛢️ Rava: Porters Who Broke a Barrel of Wine

Rava
Porters break a barrel worth 4 dinars on regular days and 5 dinars on market day. If broken on market day — they pay 5 dinars, the actual loss suffered.
Qualification 1
The 5-dinar rate applies only if the shopkeeper had no other barrels to sell on that market day. If he had substitutes, he suffered no special loss — standard rate applies.
Qualification 2
From the 5-dinar payment, deduct the usual cost of effort in selling a barrel and the cost of tapping it — expenses the shopkeeper was spared by not having to sell it himself.
Principle
Damages = actual loss at time and place of harm. Market-day price governs when that value was genuinely lost — but saved costs are always netted out.
Damages Formula Compensation = (market-day price) − (costs the victim was spared), provided the victim had no substitute available.
