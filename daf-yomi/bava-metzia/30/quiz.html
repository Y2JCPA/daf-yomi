5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why can't the Gemara resolve from the mishna whether spreading a found garment for both one's sake and its sake is permitted?
A. The mishna explicitly prohibits it
B. The first clause and latter clause yield conflicting inferences
C. The mishna only discusses wooden vessels
D. Rav Pappa ruled it is forbidden
Correct: B — The first clause ('for its sake') implies 'not for both,' while the latter clause ('not for his prestige') implies 'for both is fine.' These conflicting inferences cancel out.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the proof from spreading a garment before guests?
A. Guests always steal items
B. The baraita is not authoritative
C. Spreading before guests is 'like burning it' — due to evil eye or thieves
D. The mishna permits spreading before guests
Correct: C — The Gemara says the case of guests is different because spreading a valuable item before them exposes it to risk from the evil eye or potential theft — making it uniquely harmful.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, when is a heifer disqualified from the eglah arufah ritual?
A. Any time it performs any labor at all, even accidentally
B. Only when the owner deliberately commands it to work
C. When the owner is amenable to the labor being performed
D. Only when it has pulled a yoke three times
Correct: C — Rav Pappa derives from the spelling of the verse (written as avad, read as ubbad) that the owner must be amenable to the labor — not requiring active intent but more than mere accident.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What happened when Abaye threw a clod of dirt at nearby goats?
A. The goats ran away permanently
B. Rabba told him he had obligated himself to return them
C. He was fined for damaging the goats
D. He was praised for protecting the crops
Correct: B — Rabba ruled that by striking the goats to move them, Abaye had begun the process of returning lost animals and was now obligated to complete the return.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Yishmael ben Rabbi Yosei buy the wood and declare it ownerless, if he was exempt from helping due to his dignity?
A. He was legally obligated despite his dignity
B. He acted lifnim mishurat hadin — beyond the letter of the law
C. He wanted the wood for himself
D. Beit Shammai required it
Correct: B — Though R' Yishmael was technically exempt due to his dignified status, he chose to act beyond the strict legal requirement (lifnim mishurat hadin), finding a creative way to help while preserving his dignity.
