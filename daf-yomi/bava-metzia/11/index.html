Core Principle A person's courtyard can acquire property on their behalf — but the scope depends on whether the courtyard is secured and whether the item is ownerless or a gift.

Mishna: Running Animals & Your Field

Field ACQUIRES ✅

  • Deer with a broken leg (slow-moving)
  • Young pigeons that cannot yet fly
  • Owner says: 'My field acquires this for me'
  • Animals effectively stay in the field

Field Does NOT Acquire ❌

  • Deer running at normal speed
  • Pigeons that can fly
  • Owner says: 'My field acquires this for me'
  • Animals will not remain in the field on their own
Key Rule A courtyard can only acquire items that would remain there — otherwise it is not considered a functional act of acquisition.

Must the Owner Stand Next to His Field?

Shmuel
The Mishna's acquisition works only when the owner is standing next to his field — making it a secured courtyard (חצר המשתמרת).
Gemara
But R' Yosei b. R' Chanina said a courtyard acquires even without the owner's knowledge!
Shmuel
That rule applies only to a secured courtyard. An unsecured courtyard (open field) requires the owner to stand nearby.
Gemara
What is the source for this distinction?
Shmuel
The law of the forgotten sheaf (שכחה) proves it — the owner's location determines whether his field functions as his courtyard.
Resolution R' Yosei b. R' Chanina's rule — that a courtyard acquires without the owner's knowledge — applies only to secured courtyards, not open fields.

Rabban Gamliel's Tithes on the Ship

R' Abba
Rabban Gamliel gave tithes to R' Yehoshua and R' Akiva by renting them the place where the produce sat — making it their courtyard. But they were all on a ship, not standing next to the field!
Ulla
Dismisses the question entirely — 'This one seems like one who has not studied halakha.'
Sage of Sura
No proof — the tithes were acquired as movable property together with land (אגב קרקע), not through courtyard acquisition.
R' Zeira
I accept this answer.
R' Abba
I do not accept it.
Rava's Support for R' Abba If Rabban Gamliel truly owned the tithes, they could have used a cloth (sudar) for symbolic exchange. The fact that he rented land proves he only had benefit of discretion (טובת הנאה), which cannot be transferred by exchange — so it must have been courtyard acquisition.
