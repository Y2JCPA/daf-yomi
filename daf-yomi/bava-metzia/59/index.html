Bava Metzia 59: Shame, Speech & the Oven of Akhnai

לא בשמים היא — It is not in Heaven (Devarim 30:12)
CORE THEME This daf explores the devastating power of words — from the prohibition of verbal mistreatment (ona'at devarim) to the most famous story in the Talmud: the Oven of Akhnai, where Heaven itself is overruled, and the ostracism of Rabbi Eliezer shakes the natural world. The thread connecting it all: how we treat one another matters more than how we relate to God.

Ona'at Devarim: Verbal Mistreatment

Rav Ḥinnana b. Rav Idi
"Lo tonu ish et amito" — amito = im ito, one who is with you in Torah and mitzvot. You shall not mistreat him.
Rav
A person must always be careful about mistreatment of his wife — her tear comes easily, and punishment is immediate.
R' Elazar
Since the Temple was destroyed, the gates of prayer were locked — but the gates of tears were never locked.
Rav Ḥisda
All the gates of Heaven may be locked, except for the gates of prayer for victims of verbal mistreatment.
R' Abbahu
Three sins whose perpetrators the pargod (curtain) does not block: ona'ah, robbery, and idol worship.
KEY PRINCIPLE R' Elazar: For all transgressions God punishes through an agent — except for ona'ah, which God punishes directly.

Following Your Wife's Counsel

Rav's Warning

  • Anyone who follows his wife's counsel descends into Gehenna
  • Proof: Ahab was incited by Jezebel (I Kings 21:25)
  • Applies to: general / divine matters

Popular Proverb

  • "If your wife is short, stoop and whisper to her"
  • Rav Pappa challenges Abaye with this saying
  • Applies to: household / domestic matters
RESOLUTION No contradiction: consult your wife on household matters, but exercise independent judgment on general or spiritual matters.

The Oven of Akhnai: R' Eliezer's Proofs

🐍 The Oven of Akhnai: Rabbis 'encircled' it with arguments like a snake (akhna) and ruled it impure
↓
🌳 R' Eliezer: If I'm right, let this carob tree prove it — tree uproots 100 (or 400) cubits!
↓
🌊 R' Eliezer: Let the stream prove it — water flows backward!
↓
🏛️ R' Eliezer: Let the walls prove it — study hall walls begin to collapse. R' Yehoshua rebukes them.
↓
🔊 R' Eliezer: Let Heaven prove it — a Bat Kol declares: the halakha is always with R' Eliezer!
↓
📜 R' Yehoshua stands and declares: "Lo bashamayim hi!" — It is not in Heaven! We follow the majority.
↓
😊 God's response: He smiled and said — "My children have triumphed over Me!"
PRINCIPLE Once the Torah was given at Sinai, halakha is determined by human majority ruling, not by miracles or Divine Voices.
