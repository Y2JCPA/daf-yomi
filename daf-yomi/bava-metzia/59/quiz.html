QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why is public humiliation considered worse than adultery with a married woman?
A. Because humiliation causes physical harm
B. Because the adulterer retains a share in Olam HaBa, but one who humiliates publicly does not
C. Because humiliation violates more commandments
D. Because adultery can be forgiven through teshuvah but humiliation cannot
Correct: B — David argues that one who commits adultery is punished by strangulation but retains a share in the World-to-Come, while one who humiliates another publicly has no share in the World-to-Come at all.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the meaning of R' Yehoshua's declaration 'Lo bashamayim hi' in the context of the Oven of Akhnai?
A. The Torah was given to angels, not humans
B. God no longer intervenes in human affairs
C. Since the Torah was already given at Sinai, halakha is determined by majority vote, not by Divine Voice
D. Heaven has no opinion on matters of ritual purity
Correct: C — R' Yirmeya explains: Since the Torah was given at Sinai, we follow the principle of majority rule ('after a majority to incline') and do not regard a Bat Kol as halakhic proof.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did R' Akiva volunteer to inform R' Eliezer of his ostracism?
A. Because he was the most senior sage present
B. Because he wanted to challenge R' Eliezer
C. Lest an unseemly person inform him offensively and thereby destroy the entire world
D. Because he was R' Eliezer's closest relative
Correct: C — R' Akiva feared that if someone insensitive delivered the news, R' Eliezer's reaction could be catastrophically destructive. Even so, R' Eliezer's tears destroyed one-third of the world's crops.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Elazar, what is unique about God's punishment for verbal mistreatment (ona'ah)?
A. It is delayed until after death
B. It is more severe than for any other sin
C. God punishes directly rather than through an agent
D. It affects the victim's family as well
Correct: C — R' Elazar teaches that for all other transgressions God punishes through an agent, but for ona'ah (verbal mistreatment), God Himself inflicts the retribution directly, as hinted by the word 'anakh' (plumb line) in Amos 7:7.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Imma Shalom prevent R' Eliezer from reciting taḥanun?
A. Because taḥanun is forbidden for an ostracized person
B. Because she feared his heartfelt supplication would cause God to punish her brother Rabban Gamliel
C. Because R' Eliezer was too weak from fasting to pray
D. Because the halakha exempts mourners from taḥanun
Correct: B — Imma Shalom knew that R' Eliezer's prayers of anguish over his ostracism could reach Heaven and bring divine retribution on Rabban Gamliel, who had led the decision to ostracize him. When she was momentarily distracted, he prayed — and Rabban Gamliel died.
