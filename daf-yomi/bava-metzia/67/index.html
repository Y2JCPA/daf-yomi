Mistaken Forgiveness (Meḥilah B'Ta'ut)

Rav Naḥman
Mistaken forgiveness is valid — even if granted in error, it stands.
Rava
But what about ona'ah (exploitation)? The buyer overpays by mistake — and yet the seller must return the excess! That's mistaken forgiveness that is not valid!
Rav Naḥman
Consider the ailonit: she forgave her husband's right to her property's profits while mistakenly thinking the marriage was valid — and it stands!
Gemara
Neither case actually proves or disproves Rav Naḥman. Ona'ah: the buyer didn't even know about the overcharge — so there's no forgiveness at all.
Gemara
Ailonit: She wanted to be called a married woman — so she willingly relinquished profits. It's not truly mistaken forgiveness.
DISTINCTION Forgiveness requires awareness of what is being forgiven. Without knowledge, there is no act of forgiveness to validate.

↓
Land

Produce
Is it ribbit ketzutzah or avak ribbit?
↓
Rabba bar Rav Huna & Rava: It is merely avak ribbit — cannot be collected in court
PRINCIPLE When no fixed sum was set as interest, produce consumed is only avak ribbit (rabbinic interest) — and courts cannot force its return.

Ribbit Ketzutzah vs. Avak Ribbit

Ribbit Ketzutzah (Fixed Interest)

  • Prohibited by Torah law (d'Oraita)
  • A fixed sum is stipulated as interest
  • Can be removed by court proceedings
  • Applies when a specific amount is agreed upon in advance

Avak Ribbit (Hint of Interest)

  • Prohibited by rabbinic law (d'Rabbanan)
  • No fixed sum was stipulated
  • Cannot be removed by court proceedings
  • Applies when benefit is incidental, not pre-set
KEY FACTOR Rava's reasoning: the seller/borrower did not fix a particular sum as interest — that is what makes it avak ribbit rather than Torah-level interest.

Fixed Payment (Mashkanta B'Kitzuta) — Permitted or Forbidden?

Rav Aḥa / Ravina
One says a fixed payment arrangement is permitted; the other says it is forbidden.
Gemara (Version 1)
Fixed payment = "For 5 years I consume produce without deduction; after that I appraise and deduct." Dispute: is the first period permitted?
Gemara (Version 2)
Fixed payment = "For 5 years I consume produce with a fixed deduction; after that I appraise and deduct." Any consumption without deduction is forbidden.
Gemara
The one who prohibits Version 1 may permit Version 2. But if both are prohibited — use the Sura model.
Sura Custom
Document states: "After these years, land returns without money — the loan is fully repaid through produce." This avoids interest entirely.
TORAH SCHOLAR STANDARD A tzurva miderabbanan may not consume produce even with a deduction — he must use a stricter fixed payment arrangement.
