Bava Metzia 47: How Transactions Work

A buyer says "Sell me your item for these coins"
↓
Rav Huna (Master)
Buyer acquires the item; seller has an ona'ah (exploitation) claim if underpaid by ⅙
Rav Abba / Rav Huna
Buyer acquires the item; no ona'ah claim — treated as pure chalifin (exchange)
↓
Core question: Can a coin function as a chalifin instrument?
↓
One Version
Rav Huna holds coin can effect chalifin
Mar Huna b. Rav Nachman
Rav Huna explicitly said coin cannot effect chalifin
KEY ISSUE This daf explores the boundaries between monetary purchase, barter exchange, and symbolic exchange — and whether coins can cross these categories.

Sale Language + Coins = What Kind of Transaction?

Rav Huna
If buyer says "Sell me your item for these coins" and underpays by ⅙ — buyer acquires the item, but seller has an ona'ah claim.
Gemara
Why does buyer acquire without meshikhah (pulling)? Because the seller isn't particular about the exact sum — so it's like chalifin.
Rav Abba / Rav Huna
If he said "Sell me for these coins" — buyer acquires and there is no ona'ah at all. It is a full chalifin transaction.
Gemara
The difference: Does using sale language ("sell me") imply a promise of fair price, or is it pure exchange?
PRACTICAL DIFFERENCE If it's chalifin — no ona'ah claim. If it retains sale characteristics — the seller can demand the price difference.

The Cow & Donkey Exchange — A Test Case

🐄 Man stands holding his cow, hoping to exchange it for a donkey
↓
🫏 Another man approaches: "I have a donkey! How much is your cow?"
↓
💰 They each state their prices — both are particular about value
↓
🤝 Donkey owner pulls the cow into his possession
↓
💀 Before cow owner can pull the donkey — the donkey dies
↓
❌ Donkey owner does NOT acquire the cow — transaction incomplete
RAVA'S CHALLENGE Rava objects: Are normal exchange parties fools who don't care about value? Rather, this case involved exchanging a donkey for a cow plus a lamb, and only the cow was pulled — the act of acquisition was simply incomplete.

Kinyan Sudar: Whose Vessel Effects the Exchange?

Rav

  • Exchange uses the acquirer's vessel
  • Acquirer gives his vessel to the transferor
  • When transferor pulls the vessel → transaction is complete
  • Logic: Acquirer wants the transferor to resolve to transfer ownership

Levi

  • Exchange uses the transferor's vessel
  • Acquirer pulls the transferor's vessel
  • The act of pulling the vessel effects the kinyan
  • Challenged by Rav Huna of Diskarta
HALAKHIC TERM Kinyan sudar (acquisition by cloth) — a symbolic exchange where one party hands over a vessel/cloth to finalize a transaction, commonly used to this day.

What Is an Asimon?

Rav
An asimon is a bathhouse token — a coin given for entry, redeemed later for payment.
Gemara
Objection! A baraita lists asimon and bathhouse tokens separately: "One does not desacralize tithe with an asimon nor with a bathhouse token."
R' Yochanan
An asimon is an unimprinted metal blank — shaped like a coin but not yet stamped.
R' Yochanan
R' Dosa and R' Yishmael agree: an asimon has monetary value. R' Yishmael derives from "vetzarta" — anything bound in your hand counts as money.
R' Akiva
I disagree — "vetzarta" means money with an imprint (tzura). A blank cannot desacralize tithe.
MNEMONIC Asimon (אסימון) — from the Greek asēmon, meaning 'without a mark/sign.' This supports R' Yochanan's definition of an unimprinted blank.
