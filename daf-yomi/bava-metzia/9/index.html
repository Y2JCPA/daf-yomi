Daf 9: Modes of Acquisition & Mobile Courtyards

How does one acquire a found animal?
↓
Pulling (Meshikha)
Effective for camels
Disputed for donkeys
Driving (Manhig)
Effective for donkeys
Disputed for camels
Riding (Rokhev)
Debated throughout the sugya
↓
Can pulling an animal acquire vessels on it?
Rava: No — chatzer mehalekhet (mobile courtyard) doesn't acquire
↓
✅ Exception: If the animal is bound (standing still), it functions as a courtyard
Key Principle A mobile courtyard (chatzer mehalekhet) does not effect acquisition — the container must be stationary to function as one's domain.

Pulling vs. Driving: Which Works for Which Animal?

First Tanna
Both pulling and driving are effective modes of acquisition for animals — even pulling a donkey or driving a camel.
R' Yehuda
One acquires a camel only by pulling and a donkey only by driving — each animal only in its natural manner.
Gemara
The First Tanna does concede on one opposite case — but which? Pulling a donkey (it won't move) or driving a camel (uncommon)?
Some Say
Pulling a donkey fails — donkeys tend not to move at all when pulled from the front.
Others Say
Driving a camel fails — it is not the common way to move a camel.
Takeaway Both Tannaim agree that each animal has a primary mode; they disagree on how far to extend the opposite mode.

Rider on Donkey + One Holding Reins: Who Acquires What?

OpinionRider AcquiresRein-Holder Acquires
Original BaraitaThe donkeyThe reins
First EmendationDonkey + half reinsHalf reins
Second EmendationDonkey + most reinsOnly the part in his hand
Rav AshiDonkey + halter on its headOnly part in his hand; middle section — unacquired
R' AbbahuThe donkeyFull reins — because he can detach & pull them to himself
Note The Gemara states R' Abbahu's position is an error — it would incorrectly allow acquisition of a garment merely because one could pull it toward oneself.

Chatzer Mehalekhet — Mobile Courtyard Debate

Rava's Rule

  • A mobile courtyard does NOT acquire
  • Pulling an animal cannot acquire vessels on it
  • Even standing still — if it could move, it doesn't acquire
  • Exception: animal is BOUND — then it functions as a courtyard

Challenges to Rava

  • Boat + fish: Boat sits idle, WATER moves it — not a mobile courtyard
  • Woman's basket + get: Basket sits idle, SHE walks beneath it — not mobile
  • Key distinction: the vessel itself must be self-propelled to be 'mobile'
Halakha The buyer acquires vessels on an animal's back only when the animal is bound — then it has the legal status of a stationary courtyard.

Mishna: Rider Asks Pedestrian to Pick Up a Found Item

🐴 A rider sees a found item (metzia) on the ground
↓
🗣️ He says to a pedestrian: "Give it to me"
↓
🤲 The pedestrian picks it up — performing an act of hagbaha (lifting)
↓
💬 If the pedestrian says: "I acquired it for myself" — he keeps it
↓
🔄 If he gives it to the rider, then later says: "I acquired it first" — he has said nothing
↓
⚖️ The physical act of lifting determines ownership, not who saw it first
Rule Seeing a found item does not create a legal claim — only a physical act of acquisition (ma'aseh kinyan) establishes ownership.
