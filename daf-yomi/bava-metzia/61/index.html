Why Does the Torah Need Separate Prohibitions?

The Torah writes separate prohibitions for interest, robbery, and exploitation
↓
Can't we derive one from the others? They all involve taking money illegitimately!
↓
Interest (Ribit)
Novel: even the borrower violates a prohibition
Robbery (Gezel)
Unique: taken against the victim's will
Exploitation (Ona'ah)
Unique: victim is unaware of the overcharge
Key Principle Each prohibition has a chiddush (novel element) that cannot be derived from the others — proving all three are independently necessary.

How Do We Derive Neshekh of Food & Ribit of Money?

Baraita
The verse explicitly links neshekh to money and marbit to food. We need a gezera shava (verbal analogy) to extend each to the other domain.
Ravina
No explicit derivation is needed! The verse interposes both neshekh and marbit between money and food — read it as: your money for neshekh AND marbit; for neshekh AND marbit your food.
Gemara
But the Tanna uses a gezera shava! Can an Amora override that?
Ravina
I'm not overriding it — I'm saying since the verse is written this way, the gezera shava is not needed for this purpose. It is needed instead to extend 'neshekh of anything lent for interest' from borrower to lender.
Resolution The gezera shava remains necessary — not for cross-applying neshekh/ribit to money/food, but for extending the borrower's prohibition to the lender.

Why Three Separate Prohibitions?

Interest (Ribit)

  • Both parties consent to the transaction
  • Novel: the BORROWER also violates a prohibition
  • Can't derive from robbery or exploitation alone

Robbery (Gezel)

  • Taken AGAINST the victim's will
  • No consent from either party
  • Can't derive from interest or exploitation

Exploitation (Ona'ah)

  • Occurs in normal buying and selling
  • Victim is UNAWARE of the overcharge
  • Can't derive from interest or robbery
Rava's Principle Each prohibition has a unique element (chiddush) preventing derivation from the others — all three are independently necessary.

Prohibition Against Deceptive Weights & Measures

Leviticus 19:35 — 'You shall do no unrighteousness in judgment, in measure, in weight, or in volume'
↓
Measure (Midah)
Land measurement:
Don't measure one share in summer and another in rainy season (cord length changes)
Weight (Mishkal)
Don't bury weights in salt to lighten them — violation occurs at the moment of burying
Volume (Mesura)
Don't froth liquids to create an impression of greater volume
Kal VaChomer If the Torah is fastidious about a mesura (1/33 of a log), how much more so for a hin (12 log) and larger measures!

The Exodus — God Distinguishes Hidden Truths

🇪🇬 In Egypt, God distinguished between the drop of seed that became a firstborn and the one that did not
↓
💰 God punishes one who attributes money to a gentile to lend to a Jew with interest
↓
⚖️ God punishes one who buries weights in salt — invisible to the human eye
↓
🔵 God punishes one who uses kala ilan (indigo) dye and claims it is genuine tekhelet
↓
🐟 God punishes one who mixes non-kosher fish innards with kosher ones for sale
Rava's Teaching The Torah mentions the Exodus in the context of interest, fringes, weights, and creeping animals because all involve hidden deceptions that only God can detect.
