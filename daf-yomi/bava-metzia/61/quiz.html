Bava Metzia 61 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Ravina, why is a gezera shava (verbal analogy) NOT needed to derive that neshekh applies to food and ribit applies to money?
A. Because the verse interposes both terms between money and food, implying both apply to both
B. Because an oral tradition from Sinai teaches this directly
C. Because neshekh and ribit are actually synonyms
D. Because food and money are legally equivalent in all Torah contexts
Correct: A — Ravina argues the verse's structure — placing neshekh and marbit between money and food — already implies both terms apply to both categories, making the gezera shava unnecessary for this specific derivation.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the 'novel element' (chiddush) of the interest prohibition that prevents it from being derived from robbery and exploitation?
A. Interest involves larger sums of money than robbery
B. The borrower also violates the prohibition, unlike the victim in robbery/exploitation
C. Interest is only prohibited between Jews
D. Interest requires a written contract
Correct: B — The Torah uniquely prohibits even the borrower in an interest transaction, whereas in robbery and exploitation only the perpetrator — not the victim — violates a prohibition.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, what does the separate prohibition regarding deceptive weights teach?
A. That deceptive weights carry a more severe punishment than robbery
B. That the prohibition is violated at the moment of burying weights in salt, even before deceiving anyone
C. That only merchants, not private individuals, are liable
D. That weights must be inspected by a court every thirty days
Correct: B — Rav Ashi explains the separate prohibition teaches that the violation occurs at the moment of preparing the deceptive weights (burying in salt), not when they are actually used to cheat a buyer.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Torah mention the Exodus from Egypt in connection with interest, fringes, weights, and creeping animals?
A. To remind Israel of their suffering as slaves
B. Because these mitzvot were first given during the Exodus
C. Because all involve hidden deceptions that only God — who distinguished firstborn in Egypt — can detect
D. Because these prohibitions only apply in the Land of Israel
Correct: C — Rava teaches that all these contexts involve hidden fraud (lending through a gentile, fake tekhelet, buried weights, mixed fish innards) — and the God who distinguished between firstborn and non-firstborn will detect these deceptions.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the significance of the Torah using 'Who brings you UP' (ha-ma'aleh) regarding creeping animals, instead of 'Who brought you OUT' (ha-motzi)?
A. It indicates that the prohibition applies even outside the Land of Israel
B. It teaches that abstaining from creeping animals ELEVATES Israel — and this alone would have justified the Exodus
C. It refers to a future redemption, not the historical Exodus
D. It distinguishes between land-based and water-based creeping animals
Correct: B — The School of R' Yishmael teaches that 'brings you up' indicates spiritual elevation — God says that even if He brought Israel from Egypt solely for them to abstain from creeping animals, dayenu — it would be sufficient, as it elevates them.
