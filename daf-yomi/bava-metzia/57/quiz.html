Bava Metzia 57 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Ami's resolution, what is the status of items exempt from ona'ah regarding bitul mekach?
A. They are exempt from both ona'ah and bitul mekach
B. They are exempt from ona'ah but subject to bitul mekach
C. They are subject to both ona'ah and bitul mekach
D. They are subject to ona'ah but exempt from bitul mekach
Correct: B — R' Ami resolved that items exempt from ona'ah (overcharging within 1/6) are still subject to bitul mekach — nullification of the transaction when the price disparity exceeds 1/6.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rav Ḥisda interpret the exemption of certain items from ona'ah?
A. No price protections apply at all
B. Only rabbinic protections apply
C. A stricter standard applies — even less than 1/6 overcharge allows reneging
D. The exemption applies only after the fact, not ab initio
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda's revolutionary interpretation: 'not subject to ona'ah' means a MORE stringent standard applies — the usual 1/6 tolerance is eliminated, and even a smaller disparity allows the exploited party to renege.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Shmuel, what happens when consecrated property worth 100 dinars is desacralized upon a coin worth one peruta?
A. The desacralization is invalid
B. The property is desacralized
C. The desacralization is valid only by rabbinic law
D. One must add the remaining value within 30 days
Correct: B — Shmuel rules that the consecrated property is desacralized, because ona'ah does not apply to consecrated property at all — any amount suffices for redemption.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does R' Yirmeya limit R' Ami's ruling (that bitul mekach applies) to land but NOT to consecrated property?
A. Consecrated property is more valuable than land
B. Because of Shmuel's rule that hekdesh can be desacralized on any amount
C. Because the Torah explicitly exempts consecrated property from all commercial laws
D. Because consecrated property cannot be sold at all
Correct: B — R' Yirmeya limits the ruling to land because Shmuel's principle — that hekdesh worth 100 dinars can be redeemed on a peruta — shows that consecrated property has a unique status where even bitul mekach may not apply.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara derive which items are excluded from double payment (kefel)?
A. From an explicit list in the Torah naming all excluded categories
B. From a generalization-detail-generalization hermeneutic applied to Exodus 22:8
C. From an oral tradition passed down from Sinai
D. From a gezeirah shavah linking kefel to ona'ah
Correct: B — The Gemara uses the hermeneutic rule of כלל ופרט וכלל (generalization-detail-generalization) on Exodus 22:8. The details — ox, donkey, sheep, garment — are all movable property with intrinsic value, so only similar items are subject to double payment, excluding land, slaves, and documents.
