Acquiring Found Items: Who Gets What?

A person lifts a found item on behalf of another
↓
Does the other person acquire it?
Rav Nachman & Rav Chisda
No — Like seizing assets for a creditor at others' expense
R' Yochanan
Yes — If he said "Acquire it for me" (not just "Give it to me")
Core Dispute Can one person's act of lifting (hagba'ah) transfer ownership to a third party who is not physically present?

"I Intended to Acquire It for Myself"

Ulla
The mishna where the lifter keeps the item refers to a case where he said: "I intended to acquire it for myself at the outset"
Rav Nachman
No — the lifter acquires it even without saying that. The phrase "at the outset" in the last clause merely sheds light on the first clause
Ulla
But the last clause says: "I acquired it for myself at the outset" — why mention "at the outset" if it's obvious? It must teach that the first clause also requires this declaration
Key Phrase The words מתחילה ("at the outset") — does this phrase in the seifa define or merely illuminate the reisha?

Lifting a Found Item for Another Person

Rav Nachman & Rav Chisda

  • One who lifts a found item for another — the other does NOT acquire it
  • Reason: Like seizing assets for a creditor at the expense of other creditors
  • Everyone has equal rights to ownerless property
  • One person cannot deprive others of that right on behalf of a third party

R' Chiyya bar Abba / R' Yochanan

  • One who lifts a found item for another — the other DOES acquire it
  • The mishna is only problematic if the rider said "Give it to me"
  • "Give it to me" = acquires only upon receipt
  • "Acquire it for me" = acquires immediately upon lifting
Practical Distinction "תנה לי" (Give it to me) vs. "זכה לי" (Acquire it for me) — the specific language determines when ownership transfers

The Laborer's Found Item

An employer hires a laborer for the day
↓
What instructions did the employer give?
Specific Task
"Weed for me" / "Till for me" → Found item belongs to the laborer
General Work
"Work for me today" → Found item belongs to the employer
↓
But doesn't this contradict Rav Nachman? A laborer acquires for another!
↓
Rav Nachman: A laborer is different — his hand is like the employer's hand
Rava's Challenge But Rav says a laborer can quit at midday! Rav Nachman: As long as he hasn't quit, his hand is the employer's. He can quit because "They are My slaves — not slaves of slaves" (Lev. 25:55)

The Four Cubits (ד' אמות) Rule — Where Does It Apply?

AuthorityScope of the RuleLimitation
Reish Lakish / Abba Kohen BardelaFour cubits around a person act as his courtyard everywhere"Everywhere" means even sides of the public domain
Rav PappaApplies on public landDoes NOT apply in a field with an owner (e.g., pe'a)
Rav SheshetApplies in alleyways (uncrowded)Does NOT apply in the public domain where multitudes crowd
Why Instituted? The Sages created the four-cubit rule to prevent quarrels — without it, people would constantly fight over found items at their feet

Can a Minor Girl Acquire via Her Courtyard?

Reish Lakish / Abba Kohen Bardela
A minor girl cannot acquire via courtyard or four cubits. Courtyard works through agency (שליחות), and a minor cannot appoint an agent
R' Yochanan / R' Yannai
A minor girl can acquire via courtyard or four cubits. Courtyard works as an extension of her hand (יד), and a minor can acquire with her hand
Core Mechanism Is a חצר (courtyard) an extension of one's hand or an extension of one's agency? This foundational question has far-reaching implications.
