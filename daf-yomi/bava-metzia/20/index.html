Can a Seller Forgive a Sold Debt?

Shmuel
One who sells a promissory note to another — the seller can still forgive the debt. The debtor's obligation is non-transferable; it runs to the original creditor alone.
Gemara
This explains why we don't suspect a wife sold her ketubah and then gave her husband a receipt — even if she did, she could forgive the debt.
Abaye
Even without Shmuel's rule, we can explain: the ketubah emerges from her possession, proving she didn't sell it.
Rava
That's insufficient — perhaps she has two marriage contracts and sold one while keeping the other!
Abaye
We don't worry about such remote possibilities. And even if she sold it — a receipt is valid from when written, not when delivered.
Abaye's Principle Once a monetary document is written, the witnesses' signatures acquire it on the recipient's behalf — eidav bachtumav zakhin lo.

What Are 'Documents of Beirurin'?

Babylonian Interpretation

  • Documents recording each litigant's clarification of claims (beirur)
  • Written statements submitted to the court
  • Focus: the substance of the arguments

R' Yirmeya (Eretz Yisrael)

  • Documents where litigants choose (borer) their judges
  • Each side selects one judge; together they pick a third
  • Focus: the composition of the court
Linguistic Root Both derive from the root ב-ר-ר — to clarify or to select — reflecting different legal procedures.

The Bill of Divorce from Sheviri City

Rav Huna
A get found in my court says it was written in Sheviri City on the Rakhis River. We must worry there may be two cities named Sheviri — we cannot return it.
Rav Ḥisda
To Rabba: Study this — Rav Huna will test you on it tonight!
Rabba
The Mishna says any court enactment must be returned. This get was found in court — it should be returned!
Rav Amram
You can't resolve a ritual matter (get) from a mishna about monetary matters!
Rabba
Fool! The mishna also lists ḥalitza and refusal documents — those are ritual matters too!
Dramatic Detail At that moment, a cedar beam dislodged from the study hall. Each scholar claimed it was a sign vindicating him against the other's offensive speech.

Rolls vs. Bundles: How Grouping Works

Found multiple documents together
↓
How are they arranged?
Roll (כרך)
Each document placed top-to-bottom of the next, then rolled together
Bundle (אגודה)
Documents stacked one on top of another and rolled together
↓
Finder proclaims: 'I found documents' (no number)
Owner identifies by exact count + arrangement
Why Three? Two documents wouldn't work — the word 'documents' (plural) already implies at least two. Three provides a meaningful distinguishing number beyond the plural.
