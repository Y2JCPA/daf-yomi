QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, what happens when someone sells a promissory note and then forgives the debtor's obligation?
A. The forgiveness is invalid since the note was sold
B. The debt is forgiven because the obligation is non-transferable
C. The buyer of the note can sue the seller for fraud
D. The debtor must pay both the buyer and the original creditor
Correct: B — Shmuel rules that a debtor's obligation runs to the original creditor alone and is non-transferable. Therefore, even after selling the note, the seller (or heir) can forgive the debt.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel rule that three promissory notes from one borrower to three different lenders should be returned to the borrower?
A. Because the borrower's name appears on all three
B. Because three separate creditors would not keep their notes together in one place
C. Because the court always gives documents to the debtor
D. Because the borrower paid the most money
Correct: B — The logic is that three different creditors would have no reason to store their separate promissory notes together. The most likely explanation is that the debtor collected them (e.g., after payment) and then lost them.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the difference between a 'roll' (כרך) and a 'bundle' (אגודה) of documents?
A. A roll has 2 documents; a bundle has 3
B. In a roll, documents are placed top-to-bottom sequentially; in a bundle, they are stacked on top of each other
C. A roll is tied with string; a bundle is in a container
D. There is no practical difference — both terms refer to the same thing
Correct: B — A roll (kerekh) means each document's top is placed at the bottom of the next one and then rolled. A bundle (agudah) means the documents are stacked directly on top of each other and then rolled together.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why is a simpon (debt cancellation) found in the creditor's own possession considered invalid, even if written in his handwriting?
A. Because only a court can cancel debts
B. Because we assume he was jesting — a serious cancellation would be given to the debtor
C. Because handwritten documents have no legal standing
D. Because the debtor must also sign the cancellation
Correct: B — Rav rules that a creditor who genuinely intended to cancel a debt would deliver the simpon to the debtor. Keeping it himself suggests he was not serious (mesaḥek) or was merely contemplating cancellation.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rabba refute Rav Amram's objection that a bill of divorce (ritual matter) cannot be resolved from a mishna about monetary documents?
A. He cites a separate mishna specifically about gittin
B. He points out the mishna also includes ḥalitza and refusal documents, which are ritual matters
C. He argues there is no distinction between ritual and monetary law
D. He concedes the point and offers a different proof
Correct: B — Rabba points out that the same mishna listing monetary documents also lists ḥalitza and refusal documents — both ritual matters — proving the mishna's rule about returning court documents applies to ritual matters as well.
