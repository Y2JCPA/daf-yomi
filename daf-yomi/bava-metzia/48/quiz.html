QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, what happens to someone who reneges on a verbal commitment WITHOUT having paid money?
A. He receives the Mi Shepara curse
B. The Sages are displeased with him
C. He must pay a fine
D. Nothing — verbal commitments have no standing
Correct: B — Rava distinguishes: verbal commitment alone means the Sages are displeased, but verbal commitment WITH money triggers the Mi Shepara curse.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What proof does Reish Lakish bring from Leviticus 5 that money alone doesn't acquire movable property?
A. The verse explicitly states meshikha is required
B. The verse about returning misappropriated items omits the case of 'pledge'
C. The verse mentions money cannot be used for consecrated items
D. The verse lists robbery as worse than monetary transactions
Correct: B — The second verse (Lev. 5:23) repeats deposit, robbery, and oppression but omits 'pledge' — because the lender never performed meshikha on the pledged item, showing money alone doesn't create acquisition.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the dispute between Abaye and Rava about applying Mi Shepara, what verse does Abaye use to argue we only INFORM (not curse)?
A. 'Love your neighbor as yourself'
B. 'Nor curse a ruler among your people'
C. 'Justice, justice shall you pursue'
D. 'You shall not bear a grudge'
Correct: B — Abaye cites 'Nor curse a ruler among your people' (Exodus 22:27) to argue that cursing a fellow Jew is prohibited, so Mi Shepara is merely informational.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the dispute between Rav and R' Yoḥanan regarding a down payment (arbon)?
A. Whether a down payment is legally binding at all
B. Whether it acquires merchandise proportional to its value or the entire transaction
C. Whether the seller can keep the down payment if the buyer reneges
D. Whether a down payment constitutes meshikha
Correct: B — Rav holds a down payment acquires only commensurate with its value; R' Yoḥanan holds it acquires the entire transaction amount.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say down payments work differently for land than for movable property?
A. Land requires witnesses while movable property does not
B. Money genuinely acquires land by law, but only creates a moral obligation for movable property
C. The Torah explicitly distinguishes the two
D. Land cannot be moved, so meshikha is impossible
Correct: B — Since money legally acquires land (kinyan kesef), a down payment can acquire the entire tract. For movable property, money only triggers Mi Shepara — not full legal acquisition — so the down payment's scope is limited.
