Bava Metzia 58 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, why do the agents transporting shekels take an oath?
A. To exempt themselves from paying for stolen shekels
B. To prove they performed properly and collect their wages
C. To fulfill a Torah obligation regarding consecrated property
D. To satisfy the residents that the shekels were actually consecrated
Correct: B — Shmuel explains these are paid bailees. The oath isn't about exemption from liability — it's to demonstrate they did their job properly so they can collect their wages.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does R' Yehuda say a Torah scroll is not subject to ona'ah (exploitation)?
A. Because it is consecrated property belonging to God
B. Because its value has no limit — it is priceless
C. Because one seeks to pair it with another scroll
D. Because the buyer always knows the true value
Correct: B — R' Yehuda exempts a Torah scroll from ona'ah because it is the Torah of God and therefore priceless — there is no fixed market value against which to measure exploitation.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara derive that Leviticus 25:17 refers to verbal mistreatment rather than monetary exploitation?
A. The verse mentions 'fear your God,' which implies matters of the heart
B. Monetary exploitation is already covered in verse 25:14, so verse 25:17 must mean something different
C. The verse uses the singular form of 'mistreat' instead of plural
D. A tradition from Sinai identifies it as verbal mistreatment
Correct: B — Since Leviticus 25:14 already explicitly addresses monetary ona'ah ('if you sell to your colleague...'), the second use of 'tonu' in 25:17 must refer to a different category — ona'at devarim (verbal mistreatment).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why is sending donkey drivers to someone who doesn't sell grain considered ona'at devarim?
A. It wastes the donkey drivers' money
B. It causes anguish to both the drivers and the supposed seller
C. It violates the prohibition of lying
D. It constitutes monetary exploitation of the seller
Correct: B — The Gemara explains this causes anguish to the donkey drivers (who travel for nothing) and to the supposed seller (who is embarrassed at having nothing to offer). This is verbal/behavioral mistreatment.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Elazar, why was a rabbinic oath instituted for agents carrying consecrated shekels?
A. Because the Torah requires oaths for all financial disputes
B. So that people will not treat consecrated property with contempt
C. To follow R' Shimon's view that consecrated items require oaths
D. Because paid bailees must always swear before collecting wages
Correct: B — R' Elazar concludes the oath is a rabbinic ordinance — if people knew there was no oath requirement, they would be careless with consecrated property. The oath ensures proper safeguarding.
