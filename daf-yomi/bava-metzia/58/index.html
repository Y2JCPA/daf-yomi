Bava Metzia 58 — From Monetary to Verbal Exploitation

וְלֹא תוֹנוּ אִישׁ אֶת עֲמִיתוֹ — You shall not mistreat one another
CORE THEME The daf transitions from the liability of bailees safeguarding consecrated property to a landmark teaching: Ona'at Devarim — verbal mistreatment is worse than monetary exploitation. Words that shame, mislead, or wound are prohibited by the Torah (Leviticus 25:17).
3 / 9

Do Bailees Take Oaths on Consecrated Shekels?

Shmuel
These are paid bailees — the oath is to prove they performed properly, in order to collect their wages
Rabba
They swear before the treasurers so the treasurers won't suspect the city didn't contribute at all
Rabba
'Stolen' means by armed bandits; 'lost' means the ship sank — these are ones (unavoidable circumstances), so a paid bailee is exempt
R' Yochanan
This follows R' Shimon: consecrated items one must replace are subject to ona'ah laws, and one swears concerning them
R' Elazar
The oath is a rabbinic ordinance — so people won't treat consecrated property with contempt
KEY ISSUE Can a bailee take a standard oath regarding hekdesh (consecrated property)? Multiple resolutions are proposed.

Items Exempt from Ona'ah (Exploitation Laws)

R' Yehuda's View

  • 📜 Torah scroll — priceless, no limit to its value
  • 💎 Pearl — one seeks to pair it with a matching pearl
  • 🐂 Animal — one seeks to pair it with equal-strength animal for yoking
  • Deviation allowed up to double the value (Ameimar)

R' Yehuda ben Beteira

  • 🐴 Horse in wartime
  • ⚔️ Sword in wartime
  • 🪖 Helmet (ḥatitom) in wartime
  • Reason: capacity to preserve life — any price is acceptable

Rabbis' Objection

  • Every item can need pairing under some circumstance
  • If pairing exempts ona'ah, the law would never apply!
  • R' Yehuda responds: animals and pearls are uniquely significant to match
PRINCIPLE When an item's value is inherently subjective or limitless, the fixed fraction rules of ona'ah do not apply.

The Mishna: Ona'at Devarim — Verbal Mistreatment

כְּשֵׁם שֶׁאוֹנָאָה בְּמִקָּח וּמִמְכָּר כָּךְ אוֹנָאָה בִּדְבָרִים
LANDMARK TEACHING Just as there is ona'ah in buying and selling (monetary exploitation), so there is ona'ah in statements — verbal mistreatment. The verse 'And you shall not mistreat one another; and you shall fear your God' (Leviticus 25:17) refers to ona'at devarim, while the earlier verse (25:14) addresses ona'at mamon.

Prohibited Forms of Verbal Mistreatment

🛒 Asking a seller 'How much is this?' when you have no intention to buy
↓
🔄 Telling a ba'al teshuvah (penitent): 'Remember your earlier deeds!'
↓
👨‍👩‍👧 Telling a child of converts: 'Remember the deeds of your ancestors!'
↓
📖 Telling a convert studying Torah: 'The mouth that ate tereifot now studies God's Torah?!'
↓
😢 Speaking to a suffering person like Job's friends: 'Surely you sinned — the innocent don't suffer!'
↓
🫏 Sending donkey drivers to buy grain from someone who never sold grain
↓
👀 R' Yehuda adds: Even casting your eyes on merchandise you won't buy
WHY 'FEAR YOUR GOD'? Verbal mistreatment is a matter of the heart — only the speaker knows their true intent. Therefore the Torah adds 'and you shall fear your God' — God knows what is in your heart.
