The Barrel Mishna & Misappropriation

Gemara
The first clause of the mishna (no designated place, bailee exempt) fits R' Yishmael — no need for owner's knowledge
Gemara
The latter clause (designated place, bailee liable) fits R' Akiva — owner's knowledge required
R' Yochanan
Anyone who can explain both clauses according to one tanna — I will carry his garments to the bathhouse!
Rav Sheshet
The bailee stood on the barrel to reach a bird's nest — borrowing without permission = robber. Both clauses follow R' Yishmael.
Core Issue Can the two clauses of the barrel mishna be reconciled under one tanna, and does shelichut yad (misappropriation) require actual monetary loss?

R' Yishmael vs R' Akiva: Da'at Ba'alim

R' Yishmael

  • We do NOT require da'at ba'alim (owner's knowledge) for return
  • Once the bailee replaces the barrel → he is exempt
  • Even if the owner did not designate a place
  • First clause of the mishna fits his view

R' Akiva

  • We DO require da'at ba'alim for valid return
  • Moving for his own purposes = status of a robber
  • Even if the barrel was returned to its place
  • Latter clause of the mishna fits his view
Key Term Da'at ba'alim (דעת בעלים) — 'Knowledge of the owners.' Whether the owner must be informed that a moved item has been returned for the bailee to be exempt from liability.

R' Yochanan's Challenge & Proposed Solutions

R' Yochanan
The first clause follows R' Yishmael and the latter follows R' Akiva — who can reconcile both under one tanna?
R' Yaakov bar Abba
The bailee took the barrel to rob (gazal) the owner — he must return it to its designated place
R' Natan bar Abba
The bailee took the barrel to misappropriate (shelichut yad) — making him liable for all subsequent damage
Rav Sheshet
He merely stood on the barrel to reach fledglings — borrowing without permission makes him a robber. Both clauses follow R' Yishmael!
Resolution Rav Sheshet reconciles both clauses: the bailee didn't intend to use the contents — he just climbed on it. A sho'el shelo mida'at (unauthorized borrower) is treated as a robber.

Amud B: Deriving Misappropriation for Different Bailees

📖 Torah states shelichut yad for unpaid bailee (shomer chinam)
↓
📖 Torah ALSO states shelichut yad for paid bailee (shomer sachar)
↓
❓ Why state it twice? Derive paid bailee via kal va-chomer from unpaid!
↓
💡 R' Yochanan: Extra verse teaches misappropriation does NOT require loss
↓
💡 R' Elazar: Both verses needed — the kal va-chomer can be refuted (double payment)
↓
💡 Rava: Derive BOTH bailees from borrower via kal va-chomer — two verses serve other purposes
Kal Va-Chomer If a borrower who uses with permission is liable for misappropriation, surely unpaid/paid bailees who may NOT use it are liable — so why state it explicitly?
