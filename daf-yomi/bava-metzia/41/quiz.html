QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does R' Yochanan say the two clauses of the barrel mishna cannot follow one tanna?
A. The first clause requires da'at ba'alim but the second doesn't
B. The first clause fits R' Yishmael (no da'at ba'alim needed) and the second fits R' Akiva (da'at ba'alim required)
C. Both clauses contradict the Torah's rule about bailees
D. The mishna was written by two different tannaim at different times
Correct: B — R' Yochanan identifies that the first clause's exemption aligns with R' Yishmael (no owner knowledge needed) while the second clause's liability aligns with R' Akiva (owner knowledge required), creating an internal contradiction.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rav Sheshet reconcile both clauses under one tanna?
A. The bailee intended to steal the barrel's contents
B. The bailee stood on the barrel to reach fledglings — an unauthorized borrower is treated as a robber
C. The bailee was a paid bailee in one clause and unpaid in the other
D. The barrel was ownerless property in both cases
Correct: B — Rav Sheshet explains the bailee merely stood on the barrel (not using its contents), making him a sho'el shelo mida'at (unauthorized borrower = robber). Both clauses then follow R' Yishmael, with the difference being whether a place was designated.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the practical difference between R' Yaakov bar Abba and R' Natan bar Abba's interpretations?
A. Whether the bailee must pay double
B. Whether misappropriation (shelichut yad) requires actual loss or mere intent suffices
C. Whether the barrel owner can demand the barrel back
D. Whether the bailee takes an oath
Correct: B — R' Yaakov says the bailee took the barrel to rob (requiring actual loss), while R' Natan says he merely misappropriated it (intent alone suffices). Their dispute maps onto the broader question of whether shelichut yad requires chisaron (depreciation).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rava, why does the Torah explicitly state misappropriation for both unpaid and paid bailees when it could be derived from the borrower?
A. To teach that bailees pay more than borrowers
B. One verse teaches shelichut yad doesn't require loss; the other prevents the dayo principle from exempting bailees working with the owner
C. Both verses teach about different types of oaths
D. To distinguish between movable and immovable property
Correct: B — Rava explains: one mention teaches that misappropriation doesn't require loss, and the other prevents applying the dayo principle — which would otherwise exempt bailees in partnership with the owner, just as a borrower working with the owner is exempt.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What refutation does R' Elazar offer against deriving the paid bailee's misappropriation law from the unpaid bailee via kal va-chomer?
A. A paid bailee is always more lenient than an unpaid one
B. The unpaid bailee has a unique stringency — paying double (kefel) for a false theft claim — so the paid bailee isn't consistently more stringent
C. The Torah never discusses misappropriation for unpaid bailees
D. Paid bailees cannot take oaths
Correct: B — R' Elazar notes that an unpaid bailee who falsely claims theft must pay kefel (double payment), while a paid bailee only pays the principal. Since the unpaid bailee has this unique stringency, we cannot assume the paid bailee is always more liable — breaking the kal va-chomer.
