🌾 Abaye vs. Rava: How Do We Read the Mishna?

Abaye
The mishna says the barley was difficult as the load — meaning barley of identical volume to the stipulated wheat, making it heavier overall than wheat would be at that volume.
Rava
The mishna says the barley was difficult for the load — meaning even the same weight of barley is harder on the animal because it occupies greater volume and shifts differently on the back.
Practical Difference Abaye: liability turns on total weight exceeding the agreed amount. Rava: liability also considers how the cargo's bulk and distribution affect the animal's body, independent of raw weight.

📢 Seller Discloses Multiple Defects — What Does the Buyer Know?

Baraita (Tosefta)
Seller says: "This cow gores, bites, kicks, and lies down under a load." Buyer pays full price. Seller has no further liability — all defects were disclosed.
Rav Aḥa b. Rava
What if the animal had all these defects simultaneously? Can the buyer argue he assumed the seller was exaggerating when listing so many problems at once?
Rav Ashi
The sale stands regardless. A buyer who hears full disclosure and pays assumes every stated risk. The parallel case of the maidservant — imbecile, epileptic, crazy — confirms this rule.
Principle Full disclosure of defects — even many at once — relieves the seller of all liability for those specific issues. The buyer cannot later claim the list seemed too long to be true.

⚓ Excessive Load Thresholds by Carrier Type

Porter (Human)

  • Excess threshold: 1 kav
  • Owner liable if porter is injured
  • Porter cannot throw it off once loaded
  • Applies mid-journey when removing load is impractical

Small Boat (אריבא)

  • Excess threshold: ½ kor (adriv)
  • Standard small vessel capacity implied in hire
  • Exceeding risks capsizing or damage
  • Owner of cargo bears liability

Regular Boat

  • Excess threshold: 1 kor
  • Standard capacity: 30 kor
  • Per Rav Pappa's inference from the baraita
  • Adding beyond 30 kor creates liability

Large Ship

  • Excess threshold: 3 kor
  • Higher baseline load capacity
  • 3 kor = proportional safety margin
  • Cargo owner liable for overload damage

🛠️ Artisans as Bailees: Shifting Status

Owner delivers raw materials to artisan's home for work — artisan immediately becomes a paid bailee (שומר שכר)
↓
As paid bailee, artisan is liable for theft and loss, but exempt for unavoidable accidents (אונס)
↓
Once artisan declares "I finished — come take your item", status shifts to unpaid bailee (שומר חינם)
↓
As unpaid bailee, artisan is liable only for negligence — exempt for theft, loss, or accident
↓
Why is the working artisan a paid bailee? The Gemara: the owner's act of giving him the work is itself the payment — even before wages are received
