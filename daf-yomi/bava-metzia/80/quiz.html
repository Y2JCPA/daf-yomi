5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
A renter hired a cow to plow a valley but instead plowed a mountain. The plowshare broke. What is his liability?
A. Exempt, since mountain plowing is actually easier and caused less strain
B. Liable, since mountain plowing is harder than the stipulated valley conditions
C. Exempt, since the plowshare would have broken on the valley too
D. Liable only if the owner explicitly warned him not to use mountains
Correct: B — When a renter uses equipment in harder conditions than agreed, he has diverged from the rental terms and bears liability for resulting damage — mountain plowing is harder than valley plowing.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the core practical difference between Abaye's and Rava's readings of the mishna on wheat vs. barley loads?
A. Abaye focuses on weight; Rava focuses on volume and how bulk affects the animal
B. Abaye says barley is always lighter by definition; Rava says it is always heavier
C. Abaye exempts the renter in all cases; Rava always holds him liable
D. They disagree only about whether the renter must pay for the donkey's veterinary costs
Correct: A — Abaye reads the mishna as measuring difficulty by total weight, while Rava holds that a load's volume and distribution also matter — even identical weight can be 'difficult for' the animal differently.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the baraita, what is the minimum excess weight that makes an owner liable if a porter is injured?
A. A kor
B. An adriv (half a kor)
C. A kav
D. Three se'a
Correct: C — The baraita teaches that a single kav is too large an addition for a porter. If the owner adds even a kav beyond the agreed load and the porter is injured, the owner bears liability.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When does an artisan's bailee status shift from paid bailee to unpaid bailee?
A. The moment the artisan physically finishes the work
B. When the artisan tells the owner 'take what is yours' and the owner has not yet collected it
C. When the owner pays the artisan's wages in full
D. When the finished item is physically moved to the owner's premises
Correct: B — Once the artisan declares 'I finished — come take your item,' the artisan becomes an unpaid bailee. Until that declaration, the artisan is a paid bailee liable for theft and loss.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabba bar Avuh's reversal of the baraita, what is Rabbi Meir's actual position on a renter's bailee status?
A. A renter is an unpaid bailee — liable for negligence only
B. A renter is a paid bailee — liable for theft and loss
C. A renter is like a borrower — fully liable even for unavoidable accidents
D. A renter has no bailee obligations at all; only the owner bears risk
Correct: B — Rabba bar Avuh reversed the standard attribution of the baraita, teaching that Rabbi Meir holds a renter is like a paid bailee — liable for theft and loss, but not for unavoidable accidents (ones).
