Bava Metzia 81 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda, what is the bailee status of an artisan once he has completed work on an item?
A. Unpaid bailee — his obligation ends when the work is done
B. Paid bailee — he retains responsibility for the item
C. He has no bailee status at all until collected
D. Borrower — since he benefited from using the item
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda rules that completing the work does not end responsibility. The artisan remains a paid bailee because he continues to hold and benefit from the item until the owner retrieves it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Ameimar rule about a borrower's status after the loan period ends?
A. He remains fully liable as a borrower until the owner explicitly releases him
B. He immediately becomes an unpaid bailee
C. He becomes a paid bailee — exempt from unforeseeable accidents
D. He bears no further liability whatsoever
Correct: C — Ameimar rules that after the loan period, the borrower's strict liability diminishes to that of a paid bailee. The baraita about vessels sent as a gift — where the carrier is exempt on the return trip — provides support.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the ice plant dealers story, why was Rav Pappa initially embarrassed by the Rabbis' objection?
A. Because the dealers had no formal agreement to guard the cloak
B. Because the owner was baking for the group at the time — making it bailment with the owner present
C. Because the cloak was stolen, not lost through negligence
D. Because the mutual safeguarding rule does not apply to food workers
Correct: B — The Rabbis pointed out that the cloak's owner was actively baking for the dealers when the theft occurred — a classic case of ba'al habayit imo (owner present), which exempts the bailee. Rav Pappa was embarrassed until it emerged the owner had already left before the theft.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rava's miggo argument in defense of the renter whose donkey drowned?
A. A renter who pays in full is always trusted over the owner
B. The renter is believed because he could have lied more effectively by claiming he used the permitted path
C. Since the donkey drowned, the accident proves it was the watery path's fault, not the renter's
D. The owner cannot restrict which path the renter uses
Correct: B — Rava applies miggo: the renter is credible because if he intended to deceive, he could have simply claimed he went via Neresh (the permitted path) — an unverifiable and stronger lie. Choosing the weaker claim suggests he is truthful.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the apparent contradiction between the mishna (lender = paid bailee) and the baraita reflecting Rabbi Eliezer and Rabbi Akiva's dispute over collateral?
A. The mishna follows only Rabbi Eliezer; Akiva's view is rejected entirely
B. The mishna refers to collateral taken at the time of the loan; the baraita refers to collateral seized afterward as distraint
C. There is no real contradiction — both sources say the lender is a paid bailee
D. The mishna applies to movable property; the baraita applies only to land
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes the two cases: the mishna discusses collateral voluntarily pledged at the time the loan was made (consensual security), while the baraita's dispute concerns collateral seized after the fact as distraint — a different legal relationship with different rules.
