Bava Metzia 65: Interest in Rent, Sales & Seized Property

Core Question:
When does a higher price for delayed payment constitute ribbit (interest)?
↓
Rental
Payment obligation arises at end of rental period → higher monthly price = permitted (discount for early payment)
Sale
Payment obligation arises immediately → higher price for delay = prohibited as ribbit
↓
Tarsha Exception: If price increase is not explicitly stated as being for the delay → Rav Naḥman permits
Key Verse "Like a hired laborer, year by year" (Leviticus 25:53) — rental obligation is incurred only in the following year

Seizing a Debtor's Slave — Appearance of Interest?

Rav Yosef bar Ḥama
I may seize my debtor's slaves and put them to work — this keeps the slave's work habits intact, per Rav Daniel bar Rav Ketina.
Rava
Rav Daniel's rule applies when the one seizing is not owed money. Since you are owed money, using the slave has the appearance of ribbit (interest).
Rava
As Rav Naḥman says: Even one who normally needn't pay rent must pay if he lent money to the owner — otherwise it looks like interest.
Rav Yosef bar Ḥama
I retract — I will no longer seize the slaves of my debtors.
Principle Benefiting from a debtor's property — even with no formal payment — creates marit ayin (appearance) of interest.

Abaye vs. Rava: Returning Interest Overpayments

ScenarioAbayeRava
Owed 1 dinar interest; wheat is 4 se'a/dinar; borrower gave 5 se'aReturn only 4 se'a (1 dinar); extra se'a is a gift/discountReturn all 5 se'a — it all came as interest
Owed 4 dinars interest; borrower gave a cloak insteadReturn 4 dinars (cash); cloak exchange = a saleReturn the cloak itself — so people don't say it was procured via ribbit
Owed 12 dinars interest; lender rented courtyard worth 10 for 12 dinars—Return 12 dinars — the full inflated rental price counts as interest collected
Rava's Logic Anything that entered the lender's possession through the mechanism of interest is fully classified as ribbit and must be returned in its entirety.

The Mishna: Rent vs. Sale — Why the Difference?

Rental — Permitted ✓

  • "Pay now: 10 sela/year; pay monthly: 1 sela/month (=12/year)"
  • Rent obligation arises at END of period
  • Early payment = discount, not interest
  • Source: "Year by year" (Lev. 25:53)

Sale — Prohibited ✗

  • "Pay now: 1,000 dinars; pay at harvest: 1,200 dinars"
  • Sale obligation arises IMMEDIATELY
  • Higher price for delay = payment for waiting
  • This is classic ribbit
Rabba & Rav Yosef The timing of when the payment obligation begins determines whether a surcharge is a discount (mutar) or interest (asur).

Tarsha — Rav Naḥman's Tacit Interest Ruling

Seller offers a field with delayed payment at a higher price
↓
Does the seller explicitly state the higher price is due to the delay?
Yes — Explicit
"If you pay now: 1,000; if at harvest: 1,200" → Prohibited as ribbit (Mishna)
No — Tacit (Tarsha)
Simply states: "The price is 1,200, payable at harvest" → Permitted per Rav Naḥman
Halakha Tarsha is permitted. The key distinction: the seller never explicitly links the higher price to the delayed payment.

Partial Payment for a Field: Who Gets the Profits?

🤝 Buyer makes partial payment for a field
↓
📜 Seller says: "Bring the rest whenever you wish and take your field" — this arrangement is prohibited (Mishna)
↓
💰 Rav Huna: Seller enjoys profits until full payment ("when you pay, you acquire")
↓
🏦 Rav Anan: Profits placed in escrow with a third party ("when you pay, you acquire from now")
↓
⚖️ Gemara: They don't disagree — different formulations by the seller create different rules
Rava's 4 Cases Depending on the seller's exact words, both, neither, or only one party may enjoy the field's profits during the interim.
