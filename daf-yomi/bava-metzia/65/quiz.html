5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Rava tell Rav Yosef bar Ḥama he should not seize his debtors' slaves?
A. Because the slaves might be injured during the work
B. Because benefiting from a debtor's property has the appearance of interest
C. Because the slaves legally belong to a third party
D. Because seizing property requires a court order first
Correct: B — Rava explained that while Rav Daniel's principle allows seizing slaves to maintain their work habits, when the seizer is owed money by the slave's owner, using the slave creates the appearance of ribbit (interest) — just as Rav Naḥman rules that a lender residing in a borrower's courtyard must pay rent.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
When a borrower owed 1 dinar of interest and gave 5 se'a of wheat (market rate: 4 se'a per dinar), how much does the court reclaim according to Rava?
A. 4 se'a (worth exactly 1 dinar)
B. 5 se'a (all the wheat given)
C. 1 se'a (only the excess)
D. Nothing, since the borrower gave voluntarily
Correct: B — Rava holds that since all 5 se'a initially came into the lender's possession as interest, the entire amount is classified as ribbit and must be returned. Abaye disagrees, viewing the extra se'a as merely a discount/favor.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why is a higher monthly rental rate permitted while a higher sale price for delayed payment is prohibited?
A. Rental amounts are always too small to constitute interest
B. Rental obligations arise at the end of the period, so the higher rate is the base price and early payment is a discount
C. The Torah only prohibits interest on sales, not rentals
D. Rental agreements cannot be enforced in court
Correct: B — Rabba and Rav Yosef explain that since rental payment is due at the end of the rental period (derived from Lev. 25:53), the higher monthly price is the true price, and paying upfront for less is a discount — not interest. In a sale, payment is due immediately, so a surcharge for delay is paying for waiting time.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is 'tarsha' (tacit interest) and what is its halakhic status?
A. Interest charged between family members — prohibited
B. A sale with a higher price for delayed payment where the link to the delay is not explicitly stated — permitted
C. Hidden interest disguised as a gift — prohibited
D. Interest on charitable loans — permitted
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman rules that tarsha is permitted. The key distinction from the Mishna's prohibition is that in tarsha, the seller simply states a price and payment date without explicitly saying the price is higher because of the delay. The Mishna prohibits only when the connection is made explicit.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In Rava's four scenarios for a partially-paid field, when are BOTH parties prohibited from enjoying the field's profits?
A. When the seller says "Acquire per measure of your money"
B. When the seller says "When you pay, you will acquire it"
C. When the seller says "When you pay, you will acquire it from now"
D. When the seller says "Acquire it from now; the balance is a loan"
Correct: C — When the seller says "When you pay, you will acquire it from now," both are prohibited: if the buyer doesn't complete payment, the field reverts to the seller and the partial payment becomes a loan — so either party's use of profits would constitute interest on someone's money. Rav Huna son of Rav Yehoshua notes this follows the view that uncertain interest is forbidden, unlike Rabbi Yehuda.
