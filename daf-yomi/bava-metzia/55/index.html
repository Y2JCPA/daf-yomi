Bava Metzia 55: Minimum Measures & the One-Fifth Addition

DAF OVERVIEW This daf enumerates minimum monetary thresholds in halakha — from the measure of ona'ah (exploitation) to the five cases involving a peruta and the five cases requiring addition of one-fifth. The Gemara debates whether exploitation applies to amounts less than an issar (8 perutot), and whether Rabbinic obligations like teruma of demai carry the same one-fifth penalty as Torah-level obligations.

Non-Kosher Animal: Intermediate vs. Ultimate Consecration

Rav Aḥa of Difti
A non-kosher animal is subject to intermediate consecration — so one should add one-fifth when redeeming it!
Ravina
We liken intermediate consecration to ultimate consecration: just as no one-fifth is added for ultimate consecration of a non-kosher animal (which doesn't exist), none is added for intermediate either.
Rav Zutra b. Rav Mari
Why liken it to ultimate consecration? Liken it to initial consecration instead — where one-fifth IS added!
Ravina
We derive the halakha of an item consecrated by association from another item consecrated by association — both intermediate and ultimate are secondary stages.
Gemara
As Rava says: "The burnt-offering" (Lev. 6:2) — the definite article indicates the first offering. So too, "the non-kosher animal" refers to initial consecration, not the intermediate stage.
PRINCIPLE The intermediate stage of consecration of a non-kosher animal follows the rules of ultimate consecration — no one-fifth is added.

↓

Five Cases of Adding One-Fifth (Ḥomesh)

Eating Sacred Produce

  • Non-priest who eats teruma
  • Non-priest who eats teruma of the tithe
  • Non-priest who eats teruma of tithe of demai
  • Non-priest who eats ḥalla or first fruits

Redeeming Sacred Property

  • Redeeming one's own fourth-year fruit or second tithe
  • Redeeming one's own consecrated property
  • Benefit from consecrated property (me'ila)

Robbery with False Oath

  • Robbing one peruta's value
  • Taking a false oath denying theft
  • Adds one-fifth upon repentance
MISHNA These five categories encompass all Torah-mandated cases of adding one-fifth (ḥomesh) to the principal.

Teruma of Demai: Rabbinic Law = Torah Law?

R' Elazar
Tithing demai is only a Rabbinic obligation — did the Sages really require adding one-fifth, equating it to Torah law?
Shmuel (via Rav Naḥman)
This Mishna follows Rabbi Meir, who holds the Sages reinforced their pronouncements to be parallel to Torah law.
Gemara
Proof: R' Meir says if an agent delivers a get from overseas without saying "it was written and signed in my presence," the offspring of a subsequent marriage is a mamzer.
Rav Hamnuna / Ulla
R' Meir's principle: anyone who deviates from the Sages' formula for a get — the subsequent marriage produces a mamzer. He treats Rabbinic requirements as Torah-level.
KEY DISPUTE R' Meir: Sages reinforced their decrees to match Torah law. The Rabbis disagree — Rabbinic violations carry lesser consequences.
