QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Ravina, why is one-fifth NOT added when redeeming a non-kosher animal at the intermediate stage of consecration?
A. Because intermediate consecration is likened to ultimate consecration, which doesn't exist for non-kosher animals
B. Because the Torah never mentions non-kosher animals in the context of consecration
C. Because one-fifth only applies to kosher animal offerings
D. Because Ravina holds non-kosher animals cannot be consecrated at all
Correct: A — Ravina argues that intermediate consecration should be likened to ultimate consecration. Since ultimate consecration doesn't exist for non-kosher animals (they are always redeemed), no one-fifth is added at any stage after the initial consecration.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the dispute between Rav Kahana and Levi regarding ona'ah (exploitation)?
A. Whether ona'ah applies to land transactions
B. Whether ona'ah applies to amounts less than an issar (8 perutot)
C. Whether ona'ah is one-sixth or one-fourth of the sale price
D. Whether ona'ah applies to consecrated property
Correct: B — Rav Kahana holds that the minimum amount for an ona'ah claim is one issar (8 perutot) — there is no ona'ah for individual perutot. Levi disagrees and holds that ona'ah applies even to amounts as small as one peruta.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Mishna, how many halakhic situations involve the value of one peruta?
A. Three
B. Four
C. Five
D. Six
Correct: C — The Mishna enumerates five halakhot involving perutot: partial admission to a claim, betrothal, misuse of consecrated property (me'ila), proclaiming a found object, and returning a stolen item after a false oath.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara attribute the Mishna's ruling on teruma of tithe of demai to Rabbi Meir?
A. Because Rabbi Meir authored the entire Mishna
B. Because Rabbi Meir holds the Sages reinforced Rabbinic decrees to be parallel to Torah law
C. Because Rabbi Meir is lenient about demai obligations
D. Because only Rabbi Meir discusses teruma of the tithe
Correct: B — Since demai is a Rabbinic obligation, requiring addition of one-fifth (a Torah-level penalty) seems excessive. Shmuel explains this follows Rabbi Meir, who holds that the Sages reinforced their pronouncements to be parallel to Torah law.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Rav Ketina's ruling about a court case where the disputed item depreciates below one peruta?
A. The case is immediately dismissed
B. The court must restart proceedings with a new claim
C. The court concludes the case even though the item is now worth less than a peruta
D. The defendant is automatically exempt from the oath
Correct: C — Rav Ketina rules that once a court begins proceedings over a claim worth one peruta, it concludes the case even if the item depreciates below a peruta. A peruta is required to begin proceedings, but not to issue a verdict.
