Can Coins Effect Kinyan Chalifin?

Central Question: Can coins be used to perform a kinyan chalifin (acquisition by exchange)?
↓
Gemara tests this through multiple scenarios: second tithe, money changers, and a mishna in Kiddushin
↓
Conclusion
Coins do NOT effect chalifin — Even Rav Pappa retracts, confirmed by Ulla, Rav Asi, and R' Yochanan
Sub-Debate
Does produce effect chalifin?
Rav Sheshet: Yes
Rav Nachman: Only vessels
KEY PRINCIPLE Money functions as a medium of payment, not as an object of exchange — it cannot serve as the instrument of kinyan chalifin.

The Second-Tithe Proof

Owner wants to desacralize ma'aser sheni (second tithe) without paying the extra one-fifth
↓
He gives produce as a gift to another person, who then desacralizes it on the owner's coins at home
↓
Why not just transfer the coins directly via chalifin?
↓
No Cloth
He has no cloth for kinyan sudar
No Land
He has no land to bundle coins with
Not His Floor
The threshing floor isn't his
↓
🔑 Conclusion: The baraita proves coins are NOT acquired by chalifin — otherwise the artifice would be unnecessary
OBJECTION Would the Tanna really go to all that trouble just to teach the case of a 'naked man' — one who owns absolutely nothing? This supports the conclusion that coins cannot effect exchange.

Rav Pappa's Retraction

Rav Pappa
Initially held: Coins CAN be acquired via chalifin
Gemara
But Rav Pappa had 12,000 dinars lent out in Bei Ḥozai
Rav Pappa
Transferred ownership to his agent Rav Shmuel bar Aḥa via granting the threshold of his house — land, not chalifin!
Gemara
If coins could be acquired by chalifin, why use land? Rav Pappa clearly retracted!
Rav Shmuel bar Aḥa
Successfully collected the debt and returned
Rav Pappa
Was so pleased he went out as far as Tevakh to greet him
PROOF Rav Pappa's own conduct — using land rather than chalifin to transfer coins — proves he conceded that coins cannot effect exchange.

The Money Changer Challenge (R' Abba vs. Ulla)

R' Abba
Objection: A baraita says if one asks a money changer for coins and promises to repay a dinar + tereisit — if he has money at home, it's permitted (not interest)
R' Abba
If coins can't effect chalifin, the money changer never acquires the home coins — so it's a loan and the extra is forbidden interest!
Ulla
(Was silent)
R' Abba
Perhaps both sets of coins are perotetot (unminted coins) — they have commodity status and CAN effect chalifin!
Ulla
Yes! The baraita says 'coins worth a dinar' — not 'a dinar coin.' This proves they are perotetot.
Rav Ashi
Alternative: No chalifin at all — it's like saying 'lend me until I find the key.' Having money at home means it's not really a loan.
KEY DISTINCTION Perotetot (unminted coins) have the status of commodities and CAN effect chalifin, unlike minted currency which cannot.

Does Produce Effect Kinyan Chalifin?

Rav Sheshet

  • Produce DOES effect chalifin
  • Mishna 'all items' includes produce
  • 'Ox for cow' = meat of ox for a cow
  • Broader definition of chalifin objects
  • Reish Lakish follows this view

Rav Nachman

  • Only VESSELS effect chalifin
  • Produce does NOT effect chalifin
  • Mishna = purchase with money, not chalifin
  • 'Ox for cow' = monetary value of an ox
  • Follows R' Yochanan: money acquires by Torah law
PRACTICAL IMPACT According to Rav Nachman, the mishna in Kiddushin describes a rare case where Torah-law money acquisition applies because the Sages only decreed against common transactions.
