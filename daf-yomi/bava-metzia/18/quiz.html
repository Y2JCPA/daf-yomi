5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Mishna say found gittin, wills, and receipts should NOT be returned?
A. The documents might be forged
B. The writer may have reconsidered and decided not to deliver them
C. The finder has a right to keep them
D. Lost documents are automatically void
Correct: B — The Mishna's concern is that the documents were written but the writer changed his mind (nimlakh) about delivering them. Returning them could cause unintended legal consequences.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabba, when is there a concern about returning a found get in a place where caravans are common?
A. Always — caravans common means never return
B. Only if it was found after a long delay
C. Only if it is established that two people with identical names live there
D. Only if the husband denies writing it
Correct: C — Rabba holds that even in a place where caravans are common, the concern about a mix-up only applies if it is established (uchzak) that two people with the same name (e.g., two 'Yosef ben Shimon') live in that city.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why can't a bill of divorce (get) serve as proof for collecting a ketubah?
A. A get is not a valid legal document
B. The get doesn't state the monetary obligation, and the husband can claim he already paid
C. Women are not permitted to hold a get after remarrying
D. The ketubah amount varies and cannot be standardized
Correct: B — The get contains no mention of the 100 or 200 dinar obligation. Even treating it as if it does, the husband can claim 'I already paid,' and the wife has a legitimate reason not to surrender the get (she needs it to prove she's divorced).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does R' Zeira's resolution of the contradiction differ from Rabba's approach?
A. R' Zeira says documents should never be returned
B. R' Zeira raises the contradiction from a baraita rather than from a Mishna
C. R' Zeira disagrees that caravans matter at all
D. R' Zeira requires the husband to appear in court
Correct: B — While both reach a similar resolution (distinguishing caravan-common vs. caravan-uncommon places), Rabba raises the contradiction between two Mishnayot, while R' Zeira uses a baraita — because he considers the Mishna's implication about 'long time' to be merely inferential, not explicit.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What was the significance of the get found in Rav Huna's court?
A. It proved that court documents are never valid
B. It showed that Rav Huna's court was corrupt
C. It demonstrated that even in a high-traffic locale (like caravans common), a get can be returned if no duplicate names are established
D. It proved that all found documents must be returned immediately
Correct: C — Rav Huna's court attracted people from many places (comparable to caravans being common), yet Rabba ruled the get should be returned — proving that the concern only arises when it is established that two people with identical names exist in that locale.
