Bava Metzia 18 — Found Documents & Identity Concerns

מצא גיטי נשים ושחרורי עבדים... הרי זה לא יחזיר
CORE QUESTION When someone finds a legal document — a get, deed of gift, will, or receipt — may they return it? The Mishna says no, because the writer may have reconsidered (nimlakh). But if the writer confirms it, the Gemara asks: does a concern about another person with the same name still block return?

Why a Get Cannot Serve as a Ketubah

Suppose a get (bill of divorce) doubles as proof to collect a ketubah
↓
Problem: The get doesn't state the husband owes 100 or 200 dinars
↓
Even if we treat it as if the amount is written — husband can claim: 'I already paid!'
↓
We'd say: 'You should have torn up the get after paying'
↓
He responds: 'She wouldn't let me — she needs it to prove she's divorced'
↓
Conclusion: A get cannot serve as a ketubah — the ketubah must be a court enactment (per R' Yochanan)
KEY POINT Abaye retracts his objection to R' Yochanan — the Mishna's case proves the ketubah is collected by rabbinic enactment, not a written document.

The Mishna: Found Documents — Do Not Return

Documents Listed

  • 📜 Bills of divorce (gittin)
  • 📜 Bills of manumission
  • 📜 Wills (matanot shkhiv me'ra)
  • 📜 Deeds of gift
  • 📜 Receipts

Why Not Return?

  • The writer may have reconsidered (nimlakh)
  • Documents were written but never delivered
  • Returning could cause unjust liability
  • The intended recipient may have no right to it
  • A get delivered wrongly could allow invalid remarriage
INFERENCE If the writer confirms — 'Give it to the recipient' — the finder must give it. But does this apply even after a long time?

Contradiction: Found Get — Return It or Not?

Mishna (here)
If the writer says 'give it,' the finder returns it — seemingly even after a long time
Mishna (Gittin 27a)
An agent who lost a get — if found immediately, it's valid; if not immediately, it's invalid
Rabba
No contradiction! Gittin discusses a place where caravans are common (שיירות מצויות) — concern another person has the same name
Rabba
Our Mishna discusses where caravans are uncommon — no concern about identical names, so return even after delay
QUALIFICATION Even where caravans are common, the concern applies only if it's established that two people named e.g. Yosef ben Shimon live in that city.

PRACTICAL RULING Rabba ruled to return a get found in a flax house in Pumbedita — the two versions disagree whether it was a marketplace (caravans common, but no duplicate names) or a soaking house (caravans uncommon).

R' Zeira's Parallel Resolution — Same or Different?

R' Zeira
Raises the same contradiction — but from a baraita: 'If husband admits he wrote it, return it to the wife'
R' Zeira
Resolves it the same way: baraita = caravans uncommon; Mishna Gittin = caravans common
Version 1
R' Zeira also requires two established identical names to withhold — same as Rabba
Version 2
R' Zeira says: caravans common = don't return even without established duplicates — stricter than Rabba
WHY DIFFERENT SOURCES? Rabba prefers contradictions from Mishna vs. Mishna (more precise language). R' Zeira uses a baraita because the Mishna's implication about 'long time' is only an inference, not explicit.
