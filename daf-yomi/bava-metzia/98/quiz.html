QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rami bar Ḥama, what must a bailee do to be obligated to take an oath?
A. Say 'I do not know' about the owner's entire claim
B. Make a partial monetary admission — admit to part while denying part of the same claim
C. Produce witnesses who saw the animal die
D. Swear that the owner's claim is entirely fabricated
Correct: B — Rami bar Ḥama requires classical הוֹדָאָה בְּמִקְצָת — a partial monetary admission where the bailee admits part of the specific claim while denying the rest. Mere uncertainty does not suffice for him.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
The owner says 'the borrowed cow died' and the renter says 'the rented cow died.' What is the ruling?
A. The owner is believed as the plaintiff and the bailee pays full borrower rates
B. The case is unresolvable and no payment is required
C. The renter takes an oath that the rented cow died and pays only the rental rate
D. They divide the difference between borrower and renter liability
Correct: C — When the renter (bailee) claims the rented cow died, he takes an oath confirming this and pays only at the lower rented rate — not full borrower liability. The owner may also demand an oath that the cow died naturally, not negligently.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When both the owner and the bailee say 'I do not know' which cow died, what is the ruling?
A. The bailee is fully exempt since the owner cannot prove the claim
B. The owner receives full compensation since the bailee cannot disprove it
C. They divide the disputed amount — each bears half the difference in liability
D. A rabbinic court must appoint an expert assessor to investigate
Correct: C — When both parties are genuinely uncertain, neither can take an oath to establish the higher or lower liability. The resolution is to divide the disputed amount — the difference between borrower and renter liability — equally between them.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In Rabbi Abba bar Memel's dilemmas, what is the final ruling on the series of mixed borrowing/renting sequences?
A. Each transaction stands independently; later transactions never inherit earlier rules
B. The earlier transaction always governs the entire relationship
C. All questions are resolved in favor of the stricter (borrower's) liability
D. All four dilemmas remain unresolved — תֵּיקוּ
Correct: D — The Gemara explicitly concludes 'these dilemmas shall stand unresolved' (תֵּיקוּ). Because each dilemma is conditioned on the resolution of the prior one, the entire chain of questions cannot be definitively answered.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the new Mishna, when does the borrower bear liability for the cow while it is being sent to him?
A. Always — from the moment the owner agrees to the loan
B. Only after the cow arrives and the borrower physically takes possession
C. When the borrower himself designated the messenger who will bring the cow
D. Only when the messenger is the borrower's own slave or son
Correct: C — The key principle is agency designation: if the borrower requested a specific messenger ('send it with your son' or 'my slave'), he accepts liability from the moment of dispatch. If the lender sends the cow via a messenger of his own choosing, the lender still bears the transit risk.
