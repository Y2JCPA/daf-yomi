Bava Metzia 27 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the baraita as interpreted by Rav Naḥman, why do coins found in produce from a single owner belong to the owner?
A. Because the owner threshed the grain himself
B. Because the grain was threshed by his Canaanite slave, whose possessions belong to the master
C. Because merchants always mix produce from different sources
D. Because the buyer is always responsible for checking purchased goods
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman objected that workers normally thresh grain, so the baraita must refer to the specific case of a Canaanite slave or maidservant, whose possessions belong to the master.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What TWO criteria does the garment (simlah) establish for the obligation to proclaim a found item?
A. The item is valuable and the owner is known
B. The item has distinguishing marks (simanim) and there are claimants
C. The item was found in a public place and is intact
D. The item belongs to a fellow Jew and was recently lost
Correct: B — The Mishna teaches: 'What is notable about a garment? It has distinguishing marks and it has claimants — its finder is obligated to proclaim.' Both criteria must be met.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the practical legal difference (nafka minah) between simanim being Torah law vs. rabbinic law?
A. Whether one must return lost animals
B. Whether a lost bill of divorce (get) can be returned to the agent based on distinguishing marks
C. Whether the finder receives a reward
D. Whether one can keep items found in a public marketplace
Correct: B — If simanim are only rabbinic, the Sages cannot use them for ritual matters like marital status — so a lost get cannot be returned based on marks alone. The Sages' authority to override applies only to monetary matters.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the concern that a donkey's saddle might have been borrowed (undermining saddle-based simanim)?
A. Because borrowing saddles is prohibited by Torah law
B. Because saddles are too expensive to lend
C. Because an ill-fitting saddle wounds the donkey, so people don't typically lend them
D. Because the owner must register the saddle with witnesses
Correct: C — Saddles must be custom-fit to the donkey. A borrowed saddle that doesn't fit properly will wound the animal, so people do not typically lend saddles — unlike garments, which are commonly borrowed.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Rava say the specific mention of 'donkey' in the lost-items verse is necessary?
A. To teach that one must return even the donkey's dung
B. To teach that a donkey is returned based on marks on the saddle (an accessory), not just the animal itself
C. To teach that donkeys are more valuable than other animals
D. To teach that one must feed the donkey while it is in the finder's care
Correct: B — Without 'donkey,' we would derive from 'garment' that one needs marks on the item itself. 'Donkey' teaches that marks on an accessory (the saddle) suffice to return the animal to its owner.
