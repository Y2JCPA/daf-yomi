What Is a Mashkanta (Mortgage)?

Rav Ashi
The elders of Mata Meḥasya told me: An unspecified mashkanta is for one year.
Gemara
What is the practical difference?
Rav Ashi
If the lender consumed produce for a year, the borrower can remove him. If not, the borrower cannot yet remove him.
Rav Ashi
Also: Mashkanta means it resides (shekhuna) with the lender — so he has neighbor's right of first purchase (bar metzra).
Key Point The mashkanta creates a quasi-ownership status — the mortgaged field 'resides' with the lender, granting certain property rights.

Rava's Three Prohibited Practices

Rav Pappa's Interest

  • Sold liquor on delayed payment at higher price
  • Believed it was permitted since he didn't 'gain'
  • Prohibited: the markup IS interest

Documents of Meḥoza

  • Lent money for joint venture
  • Added anticipated profits to principal in document
  • Prohibited: profits are uncertain — if loss occurs, borrower pays interest

Tenancies of Neresh

  • Borrower mortgaged land to lender
  • Then leased it back for additional fee
  • Prohibited: lender never truly acquired the field — lease fee = interest
Rava's Ruling The halakha rejects all three practices. Each disguises interest as a legitimate business arrangement.

Storekeeper Profit-Sharing (Mishna)

Owner gives produce or money
to a storekeeper to sell
↓
They agree to split the profits 50/50
↓
Does the owner pay wages
to the storekeeper?
Yes — wages paid
✅ Permitted
The storekeeper is compensated for his labor independently of the loan
No — no wages
❌ Prohibited
The storekeeper's free labor = interest on the half that functions as a loan
Why Both Cases? The Mishna teaches both produce and money cases: giving produce = less toil (just sell it); giving money = more toil (must find and buy produce). Both require wages.

How Much Wages Must Be Paid?

R' Meir
Whether a lot or a little — any agreed amount is sufficient.
R' Yehuda
Even if he only immersed his bread in brine with him, or ate one dried fig — that's enough.
R' Shimon b. Yoḥai
He must give him his full wage as a laborer.
Abaye
The wage standard is: pay him as a laborer idle from his typical work — the amount one would accept to switch to easier work.
Spectrum R' Meir and R' Yehuda require minimal payment; R' Shimon ben Yoḥai demands full market-rate wages. Abaye defines the standard as 'idle laborer' wages.

Animal TypeFirst TannaR' Yosei b. R' Yehuda
Goats❌ Prohibited — no revenue while eating✅ Permitted — they produce milk
Sheep❌ Prohibited — no revenue while eating✅ Permitted — they produce wool (shearing, washing, plucking)
Chickens❌ Prohibited — no revenue while eating✅ Permitted — they produce eggs
Other animals❌ Prohibited❌ Prohibited — no ongoing revenue
Root of Dispute They agree if the raiser keeps full milk/wool it's enough. They disagree about whey (benasyovi) and pluckings (toteri) — is that minimal benefit sufficient as wages?
