🏗️ Upper Story, Lower Story: Rights, Repairs, and Rebuilding

Rabbis
The plaster strengthens the ceiling — so it is the upper resident's burden (it serves his floor). The lower resident provides only the beams and mats.
Rabbi Yosei
The plaster is a weight pressing down on the ceiling — it weakens it. Therefore the lower resident must provide both the ceiling AND the plaster.
Rabbi Yehuda
The owner of the upper story who temporarily resides in the lower story must pay rent — one may not benefit from another's property even without causing loss.
Tanna Kamma
No rent is required — the upper resident did not cause the collapse, and the lower resident cannot demand rent while refusing to rebuild.
Core Tension When shared property collapses, who bears the duty to rebuild, who bears the cost of damages, and who may benefit from whom in the interim?

🪵 Who Provides What: Ceiling & Plaster (משנה)

Tanna Kamma
Lower resident provides the ceiling (beams & mats). Upper resident provides the plaster (חומר) — it is his floor, his obligation.
Rabbi Yosei
Lower resident provides ceiling AND plaster — the plaster presses down on the ceiling, so it is a burden on the lower resident's structure.
Rabbis (counterpoint)
Plaster actually strengthens the ceiling by filling gaps and sealing it — it benefits the lower story. So the upper resident should provide it.
Rav Ashi
Rabbi Yosei concedes in a case of 'his arrows' — if the water pours directly through, the upper resident caused it and is liable, regardless of the plaster debate.
Key Term מעזיבה (me'azivah) = plaster layer. Debate: does it strengthen the ceiling (Rabbis) or burden it (Rabbi Yosei)?

⚖️ Rebuilding: What Changes Are Allowed?

Lower Story Resident

  • ✅ Hewn stones → untrimmed (larger, stronger wall)
  • ✅ Bricks → girders (more stable)
  • ✅ Reduce windows (strengthens walls)
  • ❌ Girders → bricks (weakens wall)
  • ❌ Increase windows (weakens walls)
  • ❌ Heighten building (burdens upper story)

Upper Story Resident

  • ✅ Untrimmed → hewn stones (lighter, reduces load)
  • ✅ Bricks → girders (not applicable — court hears)
  • ✅ Heavy roof → light roof (reduces weight on walls)
  • ❌ Girders → bricks (heavier, burdens lower)
  • ❌ Increase windows
  • ❌ Heighten upper story (burdens lower structure)

If Neither Can Rebuild

  • Land is sold and divided
  • Baraita #1: Lower gets ½, Upper gets ½
  • Rabbi Natan: Lower gets ⅔, Upper gets ⅓
  • Others: Lower gets ¾, Upper gets ¼
  • Rationale: Lower story more fundamental to structure

📜 Three Places Rabbi Yehuda Teaches: No Benefit Without Consent

CaseSituationRabbi Yehuda's RulingWhy It's Rejected as Universal
1. Collapsed HouseUpper story owner lives temporarily in lower story while owner refuses to rebuildUpper resident must pay rent — he benefits from another's propertyPerhaps unique: he has no other shelter and is pressuring the lower owner
2. Dyer Changes ColorTold to dye wool red, dyed it black (or vice versa)Dyer acquires the wool and pays its value — he cannot impose unwanted benefitPerhaps unique: dye physically bonded to wool, ownership changed regardless
3. Partial Debt RepaymentDebtor repays half; note deposited with trustee. Creditor demands full note backTrustee may not hand over note — creditor cannot use it to collect twicePerhaps unique: protecting debtor from double-collection, not a general principle

🌿 Mishna: Olive Press Cave & Garden — Collapsed Roof

Setup: Olive press built inside a cave in a rock; a garden belonging to another person is planted on top of it
↓
Collapse: The roof of the olive press breaks, causing the garden above to sink inward — garden owner suffers loss
↓
Rule: Cave owner (olive press) must provide a roof strong enough to support the garden above — his obligation parallels the lower-story resident
↓
Wall/Tree falling into public domain: Owner is exempt from damages if it fell on its own — it was an accident (אונס)
↓
Exception: If the court warned the owner that the wall was shaky or tree was tilting and gave him time to remove it — and he did not act — he is now liable for resulting damages
↓
Adjacent wall & garden: If wall fell into neighbor's garden, and garden owner says 'clear your stones' — dispute about whether owner may say 'they are yours, I abandon them' or must remove them
