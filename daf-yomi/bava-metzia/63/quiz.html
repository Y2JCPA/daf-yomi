QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Oshaya's baraita, when may a borrower convert his debt into produce (wheat → wine → oil)?
A. Only if he actually has the commodity in his possession
B. Only if the market price has not changed since the loan
C. Only with the approval of a beit din
D. Only if both parties are merchants
Correct: A — The baraita states explicitly: if he has the items, it is permitted (a proper sale); if he does not, it is forbidden because price appreciation would constitute interest.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Rav and R' Yannai?
A. Whether loans can be made with interest at all
B. Whether a lender may accept the monetary value of produce instead of the produce itself
C. Whether a borrower needs witnesses to repay a debt
D. Whether market rate must be determined by a beit din
Correct: B — Rav permits arrangements for produce but not for money (it looks like interest). R' Yannai says 'What difference — produce or its value?' since the lender could sell the produce anyway.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does the phrase 'Your favor is thrown on the thorns' (טובתך שדיא אחיזרא) mean?
A. The seller is acting charitably toward the buyer
B. The buyer gains no benefit — he could buy produce elsewhere at the same price
C. The transaction is cursed and should be avoided
D. The produce has spoiled and is worthless
Correct: B — Rabba and Rav Yosef explain that the buyer derives no special benefit from the early payment arrangement because he could simply buy produce at the same price in nearby markets (Hini and Shili).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why must a buyer who pays in advance present himself at the seller's granary during threshing?
A. To perform meshikha (pulling) and acquire the produce
B. To invoke the Mi Shepara curse on the seller
C. So the seller relies on him and doesn't sell to someone else
D. To verify the quality of the produce before purchase
Correct: C — Since buyers often pay multiple sellers, if the buyer doesn't show up, the seller may assume the buyer found better produce elsewhere and feel free to sell to another. The buyer's presence signals his commitment.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Rav Nachman's principle regarding interest?
A. Interest is permitted if both parties agree to it in advance
B. Any additional payment made because the lender delayed claiming his money is forbidden
C. Interest is only forbidden between Jews, not between a Jew and a non-Jew
D. Uncertain interest is always permitted
Correct: B — Rav Nachman states: 'כל אגר נטר ליה אסור' — Any payment for waiting, i.e., any extra sum added in consideration of the lender's patience, is forbidden as interest.
