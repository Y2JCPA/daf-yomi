Pooled Funds for an Agent

Rava
If three people give money to one agent and he buys for one of them — he has purchased for all three.
Rava
But this is only when the money was mixed together in one bundle.
Gemara
If the agent wrapped each person's money separately and spent only one person's money?
Rava
Then he purchased only for that one person whose money he spent.
PRINCIPLE When funds are commingled, the agent's purchase is treated as a joint acquisition. Separation of funds preserves individual identity.

Situmta — Does Marking Effect Acquisition?

Rav Ḥaviva

  • Situmta effects actual acquisition
  • Merchandise belongs to buyer for all purposes
  • No further kinyan needed

The Rabbis (Halakha)

  • Situmta only triggers mi she-para curse
  • One who retracts accepts the curse upon himself
  • Court does not force completion of the sale

Exception: Local Custom

  • Where custom treats situmta as full acquisition — it is
  • Halakha recognizes legitimacy of local commercial practice
  • Custom can elevate situmta to full kinyan
HALAKHA Situmta alone = mi she-para only. But local custom (minhag ha-makom) can make it a full acquisition.

Applying Rav's Rule to Mishna Cases

Mishna: May set price on stack of grain
↓
Challenge: Drying + threshing + winnowing = 3 steps!
↓
Answer: Already placed in sun → only 2 steps remain
↓
Mishna: May set price on basket of grapes
↓
Challenge: Warming + pressing + treading + drawing = 4 steps!
↓
Answer: Already warmed + buyer draws wine → only 2 remain
METHOD The Gemara reconciles each mishna case with Rav's two-step limit by showing that some steps were already completed or are the buyer's responsibility.

The Dowry Jewelry Case — Setting Price at the Highest Rate

Case
A betrothed man paid sellers for jewelry (linduneya) as agent for his father-in-law. The price then dropped. He wants to retract.
Rav Pappa
If you set price at the highest rate — take it at the current lower price. If not — take it at the original price.
The Sages
If he didn't set at the highest rate, must he actually buy? Money alone doesn't effect acquisition!
Rav Pappa
Correct — I mean only regarding mi she-para. Whoever retracts receives the curse.
Rav Pappa
If he set highest rate → seller retracts → seller gets curse.
If he didn't → buyer retracts → buyer gets curse.
LOGIC Setting a 'highest rate' clause means the buyer always gets the best price. If the seller won't honor the lower price, the seller is the one retracting.
