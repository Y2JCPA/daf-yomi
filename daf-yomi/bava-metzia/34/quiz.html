Bava Metzia 34 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava's first formulation, when does ownership of the deposited animal transfer to the bailee?
A. At the moment the thief is caught
B. Retroactively from the time of the deposit
C. When the bailee actually pays the owner
D. At the moment the animal is stolen
Correct: B — Rava's first version states that ownership transfers retroactively from the time of the deposit, as though the owner conditionally transferred it when handing over the animal.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does R' Zeira distinguish between the double payment and the animal's fleece and offspring?
A. Fleece and offspring are worth less than the double payment
B. The Torah explicitly excludes fleece and offspring
C. Profit from elsewhere (kefel) is apt to be transferred; profit from the body (fleece/offspring) is not
D. Fleece and offspring belong to the thief, not the owner
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that a person is apt to transfer unanticipated profit from external sources (like the thief's double payment) but not anticipated profit that comes directly from the animal's body.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Yochanan, what is required for a bailee to acquire rights to the double payment?
A. Full payment to the owner
B. A declaration of intent to pay, even without actual payment
C. A formal kinyan (act of acquisition) before witnesses
D. Payment of at least half the value
Correct: B — R' Yochanan teaches that the mishna's 'if the bailee paid' means once he said 'I choose to pay' — even without actually paying — he acquires the kefel.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the first version of Rav Pappa hold that a borrower (sho'el) does NOT acquire the kefel by saying 'I choose to pay'?
A. A borrower is too poor to pay
B. A borrower's statement is ineffectual because he is liable to pay regardless
C. Borrowers are not trusted by the court
D. The owner never intended to give a borrower rights to the kefel
Correct: B — Since a borrower is liable for all losses (except death from ordinary use), his declaration to pay adds nothing — it is ineffectual and therefore cannot confer rights to the double payment.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Rav Huna require the court to administer an oath to a bailee who chooses to pay?
A. To verify the item's value before payment
B. Because all monetary claims require an oath
C. Out of concern the bailee coveted the item and is hiding it
D. To ensure the bailee is solvent enough to pay
Correct: C — Rav Huna is concerned that the bailee may be concealing the deposited item because he desires it (chomed). The oath ensures the item truly is not in his possession.
