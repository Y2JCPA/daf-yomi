QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, why is omitting a property guarantee from a promissory note considered a scribal error, but not from a deed of sale?
A. A lender would never give money without security, but a buyer may accept the risk of owning land even briefly
B. Promissory notes are more formal documents than deeds of sale
C. The Torah requires guarantees only for loans, not for sales
D. Scribes are more careful when writing deeds of sale
Correct: A — Shmuel distinguishes: no one lends money for nothing (so guarantee is assumed), but a person is apt to purchase land for a day — willing to accept the risk for potential short-term profit.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the story of Avuh bar Ihi, why did Shmuel refuse to help him recover from his sister?
A. Because she was family and exempt from guarantee obligations
B. Because the deed of sale did not include a property guarantee, and for sales this is not a scribal error
C. Because Avuh waited too long to bring his claim
D. Because the creditor had a stronger lien than Avuh's purchase
Correct: B — Shmuel's principle that omission of guarantee is a scribal error applies only to promissory notes. For land sales, if no guarantee was written, the buyer has no claim — a person is apt to purchase land for a day.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Shmuel hold that a buyer cannot receive compensation for enhancement even if the seller explicitly specified it in the deed?
A. Because enhancement is too difficult to calculate accurately
B. Because the buyer already benefited from using the land
C. Because since the seller doesn't get the land back, the transaction looks like a loan and enhancement payment resembles interest (ribbit)
D. Because the Rabbis enacted this for tikkun ha-olam
Correct: C — Shmuel's concern is structural: if the seller reimburses money but never receives the land, it appears the original payment was a loan. Paying enhancement on top then looks like interest (ribbit), which is prohibited.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Abaye, when Reuven sells a field to Shimon WITHOUT a guarantee and disputants emerge, when can Shimon still withdraw?
A. At any time before a court ruling
B. Only before he has taken possession by walking the field's borders
C. Within 30 days of the sale
D. He can never withdraw once the sale document is signed
Correct: B — Before taking possession (walking the borders), Shimon can withdraw. After possession, the purchase is like buying a tied sack — he accepted the risk and cannot back out.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara initially try to deflect Rava's objection from the Gittin mishna against Shmuel's position on enhancement?
A. By saying the mishna is referring to a different time period
B. By arguing that the enhancement clause refers to a creditor case (not a robber), where the sale was valid and payment doesn't look like interest
C. By saying Shmuel later retracted his position
D. By distinguishing between movable property and land
Correct: B — The Gemara suggests the enhancement clause refers to a creditor repossessing (where the sale was valid), not a robber. In a creditor case, the transaction doesn't look like a loan, so enhancement payment isn't interest. Rava then challenges this by showing the produce clause must refer to a robber.
