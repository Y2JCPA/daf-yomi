Bava Metzia 22 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Abaye and Rava on this daf?
A. Whether a thief must return stolen property
B. Whether unconscious despair (ye'ush shelo mida'at) is considered valid despair
C. Whether a river can transfer ownership of property
D. Whether teruma separated by a non-agent is valid
Correct: B — The central debate is whether ye'ush shelo mida'at — despair that occurs before the owner is aware of the loss — counts as halakhic despair. Abaye says no; Rava says yes.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rav Pappa resolve the difficulty of 'thief' in the baraita about items one need not return?
A. He says the thief later confessed to the owner
B. He reinterprets 'thief' (ganav) as referring to armed bandits (listim) who steal openly
C. He says the thief left a receipt
D. He says the item had no distinguishing mark
Correct: B — Rav Pappa explains that 'thief' (ganav) in the baraita refers to listim — armed bandits who take property openly, so the owner is aware and despairs. This avoids a case of unconscious despair.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the proof from 'ki yuttan' (susceptibility to ritual impurity) against Abaye?
A. Because the verse is not about lost items
B. Because there is a specific Torah derivation requiring the owner's knowledge for impurity, which cannot be generalized
C. Because Rav Pappa disagrees with the reading
D. Because impurity only applies to teruma
Correct: B — The requirement for the owner's awareness in ritual impurity comes from a specific scriptural derivation (the ketiv/keri of ki yitten/yuttan). Since it's a special Torah law, it cannot serve as proof for the general law of ye'ush.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What verse provides the conclusive refutation of Rava?
A. Numbers 18:28 — 'you also shall set apart a gift'
B. Leviticus 11:38 — 'when water is placed upon the seed'
C. Deuteronomy 22:3 — 'which shall be lost from him, and you have found it'
D. Exodus 22:8 — 'for every matter of trespass'
Correct: C — R' Yochanan in the name of R' Yishmael ben Yehotzadak derives from Deuteronomy 22:3 that one must return lost items — with or without a siman — until there is proven conscious despair, conclusively refuting Rava.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, why may one eat dates blown off a tree by the wind?
A. Because dates are ownerless by Torah law
B. Because the owner anticipates that insects and animals will eat them, so he despairs from the outset
C. Because wind damage is considered an act of God
D. Because the tree is in a public area
Correct: B — Rav Ashi explains that since repugnant creatures and creeping animals eat fallen dates, the owner despairs from the outset — this is conscious, advance despair, satisfying even Abaye's standard. But if the tree belongs to orphans or is fenced, the dates are forbidden.
