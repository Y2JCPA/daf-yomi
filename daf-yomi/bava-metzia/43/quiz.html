5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, when does a money changer bear responsibility for deposited money?
A. When the money is bound and sealed
B. When the money is unbound
C. Always, regardless of binding
D. Only if he explicitly agrees to liability
Correct: B — Unbound money signals that the money changer may use it, which creates liability. Bound and sealed money may not be used, so the money changer is not responsible.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Rav Huna and Rav Naḥman regarding a money changer with unbound deposits?
A. Whether the money changer may use the money at all
B. Whether the money changer is liable for ones (force majeure)
C. Whether the deposit constitutes a gift
D. Whether the money changer must return the exact coins
Correct: B — Rav Huna holds the money changer is liable even for ones (like a borrower), while Rav Naḥman holds he is a paid bailee — liable for theft and loss but exempt from ones.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In Rabba's wine barrel case, if the barrel's value rose from 1 to 4 dinars and broke by itself, how much does the robber pay?
A. 4 dinars
B. 1 dinar
C. 2.5 dinars (average)
D. Nothing — it was ones
Correct: B — When the barrel breaks on its own, the robber pays its value at the time of robbery (1 dinar). Only if HE broke or drank it does he pay the higher current value (4 dinars).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Gemara's final interpretation, what do 'decrease and increase' in the Beit Shammai/Beit Hillel dispute actually refer to?
A. Market price fluctuations
B. Inflation and deflation of currency
C. Shearing of fleece (decrease) and birth of offspring (increase)
D. Wear and tear vs. improvements made by the bailee
Correct: C — The Gemara reinterprets the dispute as being about physical changes — fleece (גיזה) and offspring (ולדות) — not market value. The core issue becomes: who owns the enhancements of stolen property?
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Rav Naḥman consider the money changer a PAID bailee rather than an unpaid one?
A. The depositor pays him a fee
B. He benefits from the potential to use the money for profitable purchases
C. Money changers always charge for safekeeping
D. The Torah classifies all professionals as paid bailees
Correct: B — Even without an explicit fee, the money changer derives benefit from having access to the funds — the possibility of making profitable purchases with the money serves as his 'payment,' elevating him to paid bailee status.
