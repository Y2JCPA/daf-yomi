↓
Is there any extra benefit to the lender?
Monetary
Direct financial interest — classic ribbit
Pre/Post Gifts
Gifts before or after the loan — ribbit mukdemet/me'uḥeret
Verbal
Sharing useful information as payment — ribbit devarim
None
No extra benefit — Permitted
Core Theme Daf 75 expands the definition of interest far beyond money — encompassing gifts, words, labor, and even a greeting.

Borrowing Commodities: Must You Have Some On Hand?

Rav Huna
One who has a se'a of an item at home may borrow one se'a of that item. If he has two, he may borrow two.
R' Yitzḥak
Even if he has only one se'a, he may borrow several kor in reliance upon it!
R' Ḥiyya
A baraita supports R' Yitzḥak: if one has not a drop of wine, he may not borrow — implying if he has a drop, he may borrow many.
Rav Sheshet
The Mishna's rule applies when no price was fixed: if wheat depreciates, the lender simply takes his wheat back; if it appreciates, the borrower pays the original value.
Key Principle Having even a small amount of the commodity on hand mitigates the concern of ribbit, since you could theoretically repay immediately.

Hillel vs. The Rabbis on Neighborly Loans

Hillel (Strict)

  • A woman may NOT lend a loaf of bread without establishing its monetary value
  • Even short-term loans of food are prohibited without price-fixing
  • Concern: wheat may appreciate before the loaf is returned
  • Shmuel rules halakha follows Hillel — but the Gemara rejects this

The Rabbis (Lenient)

  • One may borrow food items and repay them without specification
  • Neighbors are not particular about small fluctuations
  • Common practice is upheld — people lend bread, oil, etc. routinely
  • The halakha follows the Rabbis in practice
Halakha Despite Shmuel's ruling for Hillel, the Gemara concludes the halakha does not follow Shmuel — neighborly food loans are permitted.

Labor Exchanges: When Is Trading Work Permitted?

Two people agree to exchange labor
↓
Is the work of equal type and timing?
Yes — Same Season
✅ 'Weed with me today, I'll weed with you another day' — Permitted
All dry days count as one period; all rainy days as one period
No — Different Seasons
❌ 'Plow with me in the dry season, I'll plow with you in the rainy season' — Prohibited
Plowing in rainy season is harder = unequal exchange = interest
Distinction Minor differences within a season are ignored, but cross-season labor exchanges create unequal value — constituting ribbit.

Three Types of Non-Monetary Interest

🎁 Ribbit Mukdemet (Pre-Paid Interest) — Sending a gift before a loan, saying: 'So that you will lend to me'
↓
🎁 Ribbit Me'uḥeret (Post-Paid Interest) — Sending a gift after repayment, saying: 'For your money that was idle with me'
↓
🗣️ Ribbit Devarim (Verbal Interest) — Sharing valuable information with the lender as a form of payment
↓
👋 Even a Greeting — R' Shimon b. Yoḥai: If the borrower was not accustomed to greeting the lender, starting to do so after the loan is prohibited interest of davar/dibbur
↓
📖 Source: Deuteronomy 23:20 — 'Interest of any davar' — read as dibbur (speech)
Rabban Gamliel & R' Shimon Interest is not limited to money — any benefit given because of a loan, whether before, after, or in words, is prohibited.
