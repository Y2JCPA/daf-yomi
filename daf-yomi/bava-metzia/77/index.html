Task Completed Early: Assigning New Work

Rava
If the task finishes at midday, the employer may assign easier or equally difficult work.
Rava
But he may NOT assign harder work — he must pay full wages instead.
Gemara
Why full wages? Pay them as a po'el batel (idle laborer) for the remaining time!
Gemara
Rava was speaking of the workers of Meḥoza (akhlushei) — they grow weak if they sit idle.
Rava
For such workers, idleness is a burden, not a benefit — so full wages are owed.
Insight The concept of po'el batel (idle laborer wage) assumes idleness is enjoyable — but for laborers accustomed to hard work, sitting idle is itself a hardship.

Rabbis vs. Rabbi Dosa: Appraising Labor

Rabbis (Chachamim)

  • Appraise what was done
  • Laborer is at an advantage
  • If work done is worth 6 dinars (1.5 sela), laborer gets at least 1 sela (original rate)
  • Laborer does not forfeit stipulated wages even when reneging

Rabbi Dosa

  • Appraise what remains to be done
  • Laborer is at a disadvantage
  • If remaining work costs 6 dinars (1.5 sela), laborer receives only half a sela (shekel)
  • Whoever reneges bears the financial penalty
Dispute The core disagreement: Does reneging penalize the laborer (Rabbi Dosa), or does the laborer retain the upper hand regardless (Rabbis)?

Mid-Job Appeasement: Renegotiation Traps

📈 Price of labor increases midday
↓
😤 Laborers rebel and refuse to continue
↓
🤝 Employer appeases them — they resume work
↓
💰 Laborers claim: We were appeased because you'd raise our wages!
↓
🙅 Employer responds: I appeased you with better food and drink, not higher wages
↓
⚖️ Halakha: Original wage stands — appeasement ≠ renegotiation
Rule Ambiguous appeasement defaults to the original terms — the burden of proof falls on whoever claims the terms changed.
