QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, if an employer did NOT survey his field the night before and discovered no work was needed, who bears the loss?
A. The laborers, as it is their misfortune
B. The employer, and he pays full wages
C. The employer, and he pays wages of an idle laborer
D. They split the loss equally
Correct: C — Rava rules that failure to survey is the employer's negligence, so he bears the loss — but pays only the reduced rate of a po'el batel (idle laborer), not full wages.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
When a river that is 'prone to stopping' stops midday, and the laborers are NOT local residents, who bears the loss?
A. The laborers, because all river stoppages are their risk
B. The employer, because non-local workers could not have known
C. They split the cost evenly
D. The laborers, because they should have asked about the river
Correct: B — Rava distinguishes: if the river is prone to stopping but the workers are non-local and unaware, the employer bears the loss since the workers lacked the knowledge to assess the risk.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rava rule that the workers of Meḥoza receive FULL wages (not idle laborer wages) when harder work cannot be assigned?
A. Because they were promised a bonus
B. Because idle laborer wages are too low to be fair
C. Because they grow weak from sitting idle, so idleness is not enjoyable for them
D. Because the employer acted in bad faith
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that the workers of Meḥoza (akhlushei) were accustomed to constant strenuous labor, so sitting idle was itself a hardship — undermining the usual discount for 'enjoyment of rest.'
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the Rabbis' view, if a laborer reneges and the work already done is now worth more than originally agreed, what does the laborer receive?
A. Nothing — he forfeited his wages by reneging
B. The increased market value of the work done
C. At least the original stipulated wage rate for work completed
D. Half the increased value as a penalty
Correct: C — The Rabbis hold that the laborer is at an advantage — even when reneging, he does not forfeit his stipulated wages. He receives at least one sela (original rate) even if the work is now worth more.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel, how should a land sale with a partial down payment be documented to prevent reneging?
A. The buyer writes a promissory note for the full amount
B. The seller writes that the remaining balance is now a standard loan, transferring full ownership immediately
C. Both parties deposit the money with a third party
D. The sale is only finalized when full payment is made
Correct: B — Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel's method: the seller writes a document stating the full price, the amount paid, and the remaining balance — converting it into a written loan. The buyer acquires the entire field immediately, leaving no room for either party to back out.
