Deposited Produce: Who Bears the Loss?

One deposits produce with a bailee
↓
Bailee returns different produce in exchange
↓
Did bailee mix the deposit with his own?
Yes — Mixed
Bailee deducts standard shrinkage rates from what he returns
No — Designated Corner
Bailee says: "What is yours is before you" — no deduction
Core Issue The Mishna establishes fixed shrinkage rates for different grains when a bailee substitutes produce, balancing fairness to both depositor and bailee.

Mari bar Isak: Rav Ḥisda vs. Rabbi Ami

Rabbi Ami
Relatives who tend a captive's property are appraised as sharecroppers. Mari bar Isak should be reimbursed for his labor!
Rav Ḥisda
The captive case is different — the relative descended with court authorization. Mari acted without authorization.
Rav Ḥisda
Furthermore, Mari's brother was a minor — the court does not authorize managing a minor's property.
Rabbi Ami
They did not convey all the details to me. I was unaware the brother was a minor. Rav Ḥisda is correct.
Key Distinction Court authorization transforms a relative's labor from volunteer work into compensable sharecropping.

What Do the Mice Care?

R' Yoḥanan ben Nuri
What do the mice care how much produce there is? They eat the same from much or from little! Deduct only for one kor.
Sages
The reduction is not only from mice. Much produce is lost and much is scattered — losses scale with quantity.
R' Yehuda
A large measure (10 kor) actually increases! The increase offsets the decrease, so no deduction at all.
Three Views Tanna Kamma: proportional deduction. R' Yoḥanan ben Nuri: flat deduction. R' Yehuda: no deduction for large quantities.

When Does the Bailee Deduct Shrinkage?

Bailee receives produce deposit
↓
Did bailee designate a corner for the deposit?
Yes
"What is yours is before you" — no deduction
No — Mixed
Did bailee take supplies from the mixed produce?
↓
No supplies taken
Calculate actual loss and divide proportionally
Supplies taken — unknown amount
Apply Mishna's fixed rates for shrinkage
Gemara's Logic The Mishna's fixed rates are a fallback when actual loss cannot be calculated — i.e., when produce is mixed and the bailee took unknown amounts.

Rav Yehuda's Oil Profit Calculation

🛢️ Purchase barrel of oil for 6 dinars
↓
📏 Barrel yields 48 pitchersful of oil
↓
💰 Sell 36 pitchersful at 6 dinars each = recoups purchase price
↓
📉 Subtract 8 pitchersful (1/6) absorbed by barrels
↓
✅ 4 pitchersful remain as profit, plus barrel & sediment
↓
⚖️ After deducting labor & tapping costs, profit = exactly one-sixth
Shmuel's Rule One who profits from necessities of life (parnasa) may not profit more than one-sixth.
