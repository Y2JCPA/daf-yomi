Bava Metzia 92 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the central unresolved question of Daf 92?
A. Whether a laborer may eat while walking between vineyard rows
B. Whether a laborer eats from his own property or from Heaven's property
C. Whether Isi ben Yehuda's opinion is accepted as practical law
D. Whether a donkey may eat while being unloaded
Correct: B — The Gemara extensively debates — through a chain of attempted proofs — whether the laborer's eating right is a wage deduction (his own property) or a separate Torah gift (Heaven's property), ultimately leaving it unresolved.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was Rav's objection to Isi ben Yehuda's ruling that anyone passing a vineyard may eat?
A. It contradicts an explicit verse in Deuteronomy
B. It would allow laborers to eat more than a dinar's worth
C. It leaves no livelihood for vineyard owners since countless passers-by would eat all the fruit
D. Isi ben Yehuda was not a recognized halakhic authority
Correct: C — Rav said 'Isi has left no livelihood for any entity' — if all passers-by could freely eat, vineyard owners would have nothing left.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Rav Asi need to state BOTH of his rulings (hired for one cluster / harvested one cluster)?
A. They apply to different tractates and cannot be derived from each other
B. Each addresses a unique concern that would not cover the other case without being explicitly stated
C. One ruling is from the Torah and one is rabbinic
D. The first ruling was later retracted, making the second necessary
Correct: B — Without Ruling 1, we might say no other food is available so eating is forced. Without Ruling 2, we might say fulfilling the task fully is possible so eating is optional. Each case needs its own ruling.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the proof from the nazirite baraita (nazir cannot give grapes to wife) as evidence that eating rights are non-transferable?
A. Because a nazir's prohibition on grapes makes his case unique — it is not about ownership of the eating right
B. Because the baraita was authored anonymously and is unreliable
C. Because a nazir does not receive wages and so has no property claim
D. Because the verse cited applies only to figs, not grapes
Correct: A — A nazirite cannot give grapes to his wife because grapes are personally prohibited to him — not because the eating right in general is non-transferable. His case proves nothing about ordinary laborers.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the mishna on 4th-year produce (cited as a proof), why must the vineyard owner redeem the fruit before feeding his laborers?
A. Because laborers are not members of the owner's household
B. Because 4th-year produce must be redeemed regardless of who eats it
C. Because the Torah would not entitle laborers to transgress the 4th-year prohibition — so the owner must make it permissible first
D. Because laborers are considered like priests who require elevated sanctity
Correct: C — The Merciful One did not grant laborers the right to transgress a prohibition. Since 4th-year fruit is sacred and cannot be eaten without redemption, the owner must redeem it — proving the laborer's right comes from Heaven and requires the owner to act.
