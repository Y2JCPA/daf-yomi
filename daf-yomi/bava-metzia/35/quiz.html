QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the creditor swear first that the collateral is not in his possession?
A. Because the creditor is always suspected of dishonesty
B. Lest the debtor swear first and the creditor then produce the collateral
C. Because the Torah requires creditors to swear before debtors
D. Because the debtor already swore at the time of the loan
Correct: B — The Gemara explains the concern: if the debtor swears first about the value, the creditor might then 'find' the collateral, proving the debtor swore falsely. Having the creditor swear first prevents this.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Nachman, what constitutes negligence (peshia) for a bailee?
A. Leaving the deposit in an unlocked room
B. Saying 'I don't know where I placed the deposit'
C. Failing to insure the deposit
D. Lending the deposit to someone else
Correct: B — Rav Nachman rules that a bailee who says 'I don't know where I placed them' is automatically negligent — a responsible bailee must know where entrusted items are kept.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the mishna's case of a rented cow that was lent and then died, why can't the owner bypass the renter and collect directly from the borrower?
A. Because the borrower has no relationship with the owner
B. Because the renter acquires the cow from the moment of its death, not from the oath
C. Because the Torah prohibits owners from suing borrowers
D. Because the renter already paid the owner
Correct: B — Abaye explains that the renter's acquisition occurs at the moment of death — the oath merely alleviates suspicion. Since the renter already 'owns' the claim, the owner cannot bypass him.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the debtor trust the creditor that the collateral is gone, but the creditor doesn't trust the debtor about its value?
A. The creditor has more witnesses
B. The debtor sees the creditor as upright (Proverbs 11:3) while the creditor views the debtor with suspicion
C. The debtor signed a contract agreeing to trust the creditor
D. Rabbinic decree requires the debtor to trust the creditor
Correct: B — The Gemara cites Proverbs 11:3: the debtor sees the creditor's wealth as evidence of integrity, while the creditor sees the debtor's poverty as evidence of untrustworthiness.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In R' Zeira's chain-of-borrowing scenario, how many cows does Mar bar Rav Ashi say the owner owes?
A. One cow — it was always the same animal
B. Two cows — one for the borrowing category and one for the rental category
C. Four cows — one for each discrete transaction
D. Three cows — two for borrowing and one for rental
Correct: B — Mar bar Rav Ashi holds that while there were multiple transactions, the categories of borrowing and rental are each treated as one unit, so the owner owes only two cows total.
