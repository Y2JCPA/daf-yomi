Daf 64: The Many Faces of Ribbit (Interest)

💰 Pre-payment for future goods
↓
Goods Exist Now
✅ Permitted — Seller has them in possession
Goods Don't Exist
❌ Prohibited — Paying less now for more later = ribbit
↓
🏠 Benefit from a borrower — even indirect benefit can be ribbit
↓
⚖️ Key test: Is the arrangement close to gain, far from loss?
Core Theme This daf explores how ribbit (interest) extends beyond simple cash payments to include prepayment discounts, housing benefits, and even use of a debtor's slaves.

Extra Money: Mistake or Hidden Repayment?

Rav Aḥa b. Rava
If the giver is a harsh person who never gives gifts, should we assume the extra money was an error?
Rav Ashi
Perhaps he once robbed the recipient and is now secretly returning the stolen money within the calculation.
Rav Aḥa b. Rava
What if the giver is an outsider — someone who never did business with the recipient?
Rav Ashi
Perhaps a third party who robbed the recipient asked the giver: 'Include my debt when you pay him' — to avoid shame.
Principle We exhaust all possible innocent explanations before concluding someone made an error — even involving unknown third parties.

Rav's Gourd Ruling: Pre-Payment for Growing Produce

💰 Buyer pays now for 10 span-sized gourds per dinar
↓
🎃 Seller offers: Pay now, get cubit-sized gourds later
↓
❓ Does the seller currently have cubit-sized gourds?
Yes — In Possession
✅ Permitted
He is selling what he already has
No — Not In Possession
❌ Prohibited
Getting more for early payment = ribbit
Rav Kahana's Story Rav Kahana sat at the back of Rav's study hall and heard only 'Gourds! Gourds!' — he had to ask the other students what Rav meant.

Growing by Themselves: Gourds vs. Milk & Wool

🎃 Gourds

  • The same gourd continues to grow larger
  • Remove it — no replacement grows
  • Growth is an extension of the original item
  • Rava: Since they grow by themselves → permitted

🐐 Milk / Wool / Honey

  • New milk replaces extracted milk
  • New wool grows after shearing
  • The product is newly generated, not extended
  • Baraita: Specified amount → prohibited
Key Distinction The test is whether the product grows in continuity (gourds expand) or is replaced anew (milk is regenerated). This determines whether advance payment constitutes ribbit.

The Milk/Wool/Honey Baraita: Specified vs. Unspecified

ScenarioExampleRuling
Unspecified quantity"Whatever my goats produce is sold to you"✅ Permitted — could be more or less
Specified quantity"Such-and-such amount of milk is sold to you"❌ Prohibited — guaranteed extra value for early pay
Gourds — in possessionSeller already has cubit-sized gourds✅ Permitted
Gourds — not in possessionGourds haven't grown yet❌ Prohibited
Logic When the quantity is unspecified, the buyer takes a genuine risk — he may receive less than market value. A specified amount eliminates risk and creates guaranteed benefit for early payment.

Abaye's Wine Deal: Close to Gain or Close to Loss?

Abaye
A buyer may pay 4 dinars now for a future barrel of wine, where: if it sours — buyer's loss; if price changes — seller absorbs the difference.
Rav Sherevya
This is close to gain, far from loss for the seller! The buyer bears the souring risk. This is ribbit d'rabbanan!
Abaye
No — the buyer accepts depreciation risk too. Since there's potential for both gain and loss, it is permitted.
Principle — קרוב לשכר וקרוב להפסד An arrangement is permitted only when both parties face genuine risk. If one side is 'close to gain and far from loss,' it resembles ribbit.
