Bava Metzia 69 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, why is the 'surplus over one-third' arrangement for fattening a calf problematic?
A. The surplus always exceeds fair wages
B. The worker might end up working for free if there's no surplus
C. One-third is too small a share for the owner
D. The calf might die before reaching one-third profit
Correct: B — Shmuel holds that if there is no surplus beyond one-third, the worker goes home empty-handed, having worked for free — which constitutes ribbit. Therefore, the owner must set aside a guaranteed dinar.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Rav Ḥama's practice of 'renting' dinars at 1 peshita per day constitute prohibited interest?
A. The rental rate was too high
B. He didn't have a written contract
C. Unlike a hoe, the same coins don't return and depreciation is unknown — so it's a loan, not a rental
D. He rented to non-Jews, which is separately prohibited
Correct: C — A hoe returns as-is with known depreciation, making it a true rental. Dinars are fungible — different coins are returned — so this is really a loan, and the 'rental fee' is actually prohibited interest.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Rav Elazar reduce the sharecropper's share when the sharecropper became a co-purchaser of the calf?
A. The sharecropper contributed less money
B. As an employee, extra compensation was needed to avoid ribbit; as a partner, no such concern exists
C. Rav Elazar wanted to punish the sharecropper's wife for her advice
D. Partnership law requires equal division regardless of effort
Correct: B — When the sharecropper was only fattening Rav Elazar's calf, extra compensation (the head) was needed to ensure his labor wasn't 'free' — avoiding ribbit. As equal partners, both share risk and profit equally, so no extra compensation is required.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Under what condition may one 'inflate' (mafriz) the rent on a store, according to Rav Naḥman?
A. When the store is in a prime location
B. When the borrowed money is used to paint and improve the store walls
C. When both parties agree to the higher rent in writing
D. When the rental period exceeds one year
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman (citing Rabba bar Avuh) permits inflating store rent only when the loan improves the rented property itself — such as painting designs on the walls. Borrowing to buy merchandise to sell in the store is prohibited as disguised ribbit.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rava, why is it permitted for a borrower to pay someone 4 dinars to arrange a loan on his behalf?
A. Because 4 dinars is below the minimum threshold for ribbit
B. Because the payment is for the brokerage service (talking), not interest from borrower to lender
C. Because the broker assumes the risk of the loan
D. Because the lender never finds out about the payment
Correct: B — Rava teaches that the Torah only prohibits interest flowing directly from borrower to lender. Paying a third party for brokerage services — arranging or facilitating the loan — is permitted because it is compensation for the broker's effort, not interest.
