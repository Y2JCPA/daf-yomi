Structuring Deals to Avoid Ribbit (Interest)

Business Arrangement
One party provides capital, the other provides labor
↓
Worker Compensated
Wages or share of profit covers labor → Permitted
Worker Uncompensated
Risk of working for free → Looks like Ribbit
Core Principle Every business arrangement must ensure the laboring party is compensated for effort, or the capital party's profit resembles prohibited interest.

Surplus Over One-Third: Sufficient Wages?

Rav
If the owner says 'Fatten my calf and any profit beyond ⅓ of its value is your wages' — this is permitted. The surplus compensates his effort.
Shmuel
Not permitted! If there is no surplus beyond ⅓, the worker goes home empty-handed — he worked for free. That is ribbit.
Shmuel
The owner must set aside a dinar as guaranteed minimum compensation for the worker's effort.
Rav
Alternative: Give the worker the head of the calf as guaranteed wages, or — if he already has his own ox to feed — adding one more is minimal effort, so the surplus alone suffices.
Key Phrase כדאמרי אינשי: בלוש לתורא בלוש לתורי — 'Mix food for one ox, mix for two.' If he's already feeding his own animal, the extra effort is negligible.

Rav Elazar & the Sharecropper: A Lesson in Partnership

📋 Rav Elazar buys an animal and gives it to his sharecropper to fatten
↓
🐄 He gives the sharecropper the head as wages + ½ profits
↓
👩 Sharecropper's wife says: 'If you were a partner in the purchase, he'd give you the tail too!'
↓
🤝 Next time, sharecropper co-purchases the calf with Rav Elazar
↓
📉 Rav Elazar now gives him only ½ the tail and wants to split the head equally
↓
⚖️ Rav Elazar explains: Before, the extra was to avoid ribbit. Now as equal partners — no ribbit concern exists!
Adage אריסא אריסותיה גריעא — 'A sharecropper subjugates himself to the owner.' Partners share equally; employees need compensation above their share to avoid the appearance of interest.

Renting Dinars vs. Renting a Hoe

Renting a Hoe ✅

  • The exact same hoe returns to the owner
  • Depreciation is known and predictable
  • Rental fee is for use of the item itself
  • Permitted — genuine rental

Renting Dinars ❌

  • The same coins do NOT return — different coins are repaid
  • Depreciation is unknown and variable
  • This is really a loan, not a rental
  • Rav Ḥama lost all his money as divine punishment
Rav Ḥama's Error Rav Ḥama charged 1 peshita per day per dinar as 'rental.' But since dinars aren't returned as-is, this is a loan with interest (ribbit), not a rental. All his money was ultimately lost.

Third-Party Brokerage: When Is It Permitted?

Person A wants a loan from Person B
↓
A pays broker to approach B
A says: 'Take 4 dinars and tell B to lend me money'
→ Permitted — payment is for the brokerage service
B pays broker to lend for him
B says: 'Take 4 dinars and lend my money to A'
→ Permitted — ribbit only applies borrower → lender
↓
Abba Mar b. Rav Pappa: Took wax from manufacturers, then told Rav Pappa to lend them money. Rav Pappa ruled: Permitted — he takes payment for talking (שכר אמירה).
Rava's Principle The Torah prohibited only interest that comes directly from borrower to lender — not payments via a third party acting as broker.
