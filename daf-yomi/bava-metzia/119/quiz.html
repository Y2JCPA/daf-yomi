Bava Metzia 119 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the baraita (Tosefta Orla), when does a tree's new growth trigger a fresh orla count?
A. When growth sprouts from the trunk
B. When growth sprouts from the roots
C. Whenever the tree is pruned
D. Only when the tree is fully uprooted and replanted
Correct: B — Growth from the roots constitutes a new tree and restarts the three-year orla prohibition. Trunk growth is considered a continuation of the same tree.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rabbi Shimon's rule for vegetables at the border of an upper and lower garden?
A. Everything belongs to the lower garden owner
B. Everything is split equally
C. Whatever the upper owner can stretch and reach is his; the rest belongs below
D. Ownership follows whoever planted the seeds
Correct: C — Rabbi Shimon's reach test: the upper garden owner takes what he can physically reach; all remaining vegetables belong to the lower garden owner.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What dilemma does Rav Anan (or Rabbi Yirmeya) raise about Rabbi Shimon's rule?
A. Whether the rule applies on Shabbat
B. Whether reaching only the leaves (but not roots) or only the roots (but not leaves) is sufficient for ownership
C. Whether the rule applies to fruit trees or only vegetables
D. Whether the upper owner must actively harvest or mere proximity suffices
Correct: B — The dilemma is whether partial reach — either to leaves only or roots only — satisfies Rabbi Shimon's reach test. This question is left unresolved (Teiku).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Who transmits the ruling that the halakha follows Rabbi Shimon?
A. Rav Anan in the name of Rav
B. Efrayim the scribe in the name of Reish Lakish
C. The school of Rabbi Yannai
D. Rabbi Yirmeya in the name of Rabbi Yochanan
Correct: B — Efrayim the scribe, a student of Reish Lakish, transmits that the halakha follows Rabbi Shimon's position on the reach test.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is historically notable about the ruling being stated 'before King Shapur'?
A. King Shapur converted to Judaism afterward
B. It shows that Persian royalty enforced Jewish law in Babylonia
C. A non-Jewish ruler expressed interest in and engaged with this Jewish legal question
D. It establishes that non-Jews are bound by the reach-test rule
Correct: C — King Shapur (the Persian ruler) expressed interest in this halakhic question when it was stated before him, illustrating the respect Torah law commanded in the broader world.
