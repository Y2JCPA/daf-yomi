Bava Metzia 37 — Uncertainty in Deposits & Robbery

A person holds money/items and doesn't know who the rightful owner is
↓
Robbery Case
He admitted on his own — pays both claimants (penalty for transgression)
Deposit Case
No transgression — gives undisputed portions, remainder held until Elijah comes
Core Theme The Gemara distinguishes how we handle uncertain monetary claims based on whether the holder transgressed a prohibition or received deposits innocently.

Does Halakha Follow R' Yosei in the First Mishna?

Rav Yehuda
Rabbi Yosei disagrees even in the first mishna, and the halakha follows him even there.
R' Elazar
I agree — R' Yosei disputes even the first mishna, and halakha follows him.
R' Yochanan
R' Yosei concedes in the first mishna — because the bailee already paid and acquired the animal.
Gemara
But R' Yochanan himself says: 'I choose to pay' is equivalent to actual payment!
Resolution
Emend R' Yochanan: R' Yosei concedes because the bailee said 'I choose to pay.'
Key Point The double payment (kefel) goes to the bailee once he commits to pay — even before actual payment.

The Mishna's Two Cases of Uncertainty

Robbery — Unknown Victim

  • "I robbed one of you of 100 dinars"
  • He doesn't know which person
  • He gives 100 to EACH person
  • Penalty: Sages penalized him for transgression
  • Result: Pays double the stolen amount

Deposit — Disputed Amounts

  • Deposits of 100 and 200 dinars
  • Both claim the 200
  • Each gets 100 (undisputed)
  • No penalty: Bailee did nothing wrong
  • Remaining 100 held until Elijah comes
Distinction In robbery, the holder transgressed — so the Sages penalized him. In deposits, he is innocent — no penalty applied.

R' Yosei vs. Tanna Kamma — What Deters the Swindler?

Tanna Kamma
Give 100 to each depositor. The remaining 100 is held until Elijah comes.
R' Yosei
If so, what did the swindler lose? He risked nothing by lying!
R' Yosei
The entire deposit must be held until Elijah comes — so the fraud costs him even his own 100.
R' Yosei
Same for vessels worth 100 and 1,000 — both vessels are held until one admits the truth.
R' Yosei's Principle מה הפסיד הרמאי — A legal outcome must create a disincentive for fraud, or the swindler profits from dishonesty.

Do We Expropriate Property in Uncertainty?

Can we remove money from the holder's possession when ownership is uncertain?
↓
R' Akiva
One who robbed from one of five — pays each one. Yes, we expropriate.
R' Tarfon
Place the stolen item between them and withdraw. No, we don't expropriate.
↓
But R' Akiva in the collapsed house case says property retains its previous status (bechezkat ba'aleha)?
↓
Resolution (Rava): Collapsed house = uncertain vs. uncertain. Robbery = certain claim vs. uncertain — so we expropriate.
Key Distinction When one party has a certain claim (bari) and the other is uncertain (shema), we expropriate. When both are uncertain, we do not.
