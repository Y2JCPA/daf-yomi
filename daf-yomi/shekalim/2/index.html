🪙 Opening Mishnah: The Adar Proclamation

בְּאֶחָד בַּאֲדָר מַשְׁמִיעִין עַל הַשְּׁקָלִים וְעַל הַכִּלְאָיִם
Mishnah Shekalim 1:1 On the 1st of Adar, the court makes two public proclamations: regarding the annual half-shekel contribution (machatzit hashekel) for Temple service, and about kilaim (mixed seeds) requiring removal from fields. By the 15th of Adar, additional duties commence — the megillah is read in walled cities, roads are repaired for pilgrims, and mikvaot are inspected. The 30-day window ensures contributions reach the Temple treasury by 1 Nisan, when the new fiscal year of communal offerings begins.

📅 The Adar Calendar of Duties

1 Adar: Two proclamations issued — (1) shekalim collection opens, (2) kilaim inspectors sent to check fields for forbidden mixed plantings
↓
Why Adar specifically? Moshe Rabbeinu was shown a fiery coin and commanded the shekel on 1 Adar — the annual collection is timed so funds arrive by 1 Nisan when the new Temple year begins
↓
15 Adar: Megillah read in walled cities (Purim); collection tables set up in the provinces; roads, paths, and public mikvaot repaired after winter rains for pilgrims
↓
25 Adar: Collection tables moved to Jerusalem so remaining contributors can pay at the Temple mount itself
↓
1 Nisan: New year for communal offerings — all public sacrifices (tamid, mussaf, etc.) are purchased from the new year's freshly collected shekels

⚖️ Who Must Give the Half-Shekel?

Obligated

  • Adult Israelite men (age 20+)
  • Kohanim — though their obligation is debated
  • Levites
  • Converts (geirim)
  • Freed slaves (avadim meshuchrarim)

Exempt

  • Women — exempt but accepted if given
  • Minor children — exempt but accepted
  • Gentile residents — contribution rejected
  • Those under 20 in the wilderness count (debated for later generations)

The Fixed Amount

  • Exactly half a shekel
  • = 10 gerah by Torah weight (Exodus 30:13)
  • "The wealthy shall not give more and the poor shall not give less"
  • Radical equality — rich and poor contribute identically

🗣️ The 30-Day Principle — Derived from the Shekel Announcement

Mishnah
On the 1st of Adar they proclaim about the shekalim — exactly 30 days before 1 Nisan.
Yerushalmi
From this 30-day advance notice we derive a general principle: one asks and expounds the laws of Passover 30 days before Passover. Moshe taught Passover laws while still in Egypt before the first Passover.
R' Shimon b. Gamliel
Some say only two weeks before a festival is sufficient for public teaching — the 30 days of the shekel is unique to its collection needs.
Gemara conclusion
The accepted ruling: 30 days. A rabbi who teaches Passover laws from Purim onward has fulfilled this obligation — and Purim falls on 14-15 Adar, just after the shekel proclamation.
Halacha l'Ma'aseh The 30-day advance teaching requirement applies to all major festivals — Passover, Shavuot, and Sukkot — derived from the shekel's announcement timing

✨ Haman's Shekels vs. Israel's Shekels

יהיו שקליהם קודמין לשקליו — Their shekels shall precede his shekels
Midrashic Teaching — Megillah 13b The Holy One revealed to Moshe that one day Haman the wicked would weigh out 10,000 silver talents against Israel to buy their destruction from Achashverosh. Therefore, G-d preemptively commanded the mitzvah of the half-shekel — so that the merit of Israel's shekels would precede and neutralize Haman's shekels. This is why the mitzvah is placed in Adar: in the very month that Haman plotted, Israel's ancient merit stands as their defense. The Torah reading of Parashat Shekalim is read on the Shabbat before or on Rosh Chodesh Adar to recall this protection.

🔄 The Shekel Collection System

1 Adar: Court proclaims shekel obligation throughout all Jewish communities
↓
15 Adar: Collection tables (shulchanot) set up in each town and province
↓
Has the individual contributed their half-shekel?
Yes ✓
Receives a pledge token; obligation fulfilled for the year — no further action needed
No ✗
Assessors (gizbarin) pursue payment; a pledge (achinahta) may be seized from the delinquent contributor
↓
25 Adar: Collection tables relocated to the Temple Mount in Jerusalem
↓
1 Nisan: Treasury sealed — all communal offerings for the new year purchased from collected funds
