Shekalim 2 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
On which date does the court make proclamations about the shekalim?
A. 1st of Nisan
B. 15th of Adar
C. 1st of Adar
D. Rosh Chodesh Tishrei
Correct: C — The Mishnah opens: 'On the 1st of Adar they proclaim about the shekalim.' This 30-day window ensures contributions arrive before 1 Nisan when the new Temple fiscal year begins.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What broader Halachic principle does the Gemara derive from the 30-day advance shekel announcement?
A. Taxes must always be collected in Adar
B. One asks and expounds the laws of a festival 30 days before that festival
C. Road repairs must begin exactly 30 days before Passover
D. Kilaim must be removed within 30 days of being planted
Correct: B — From the shekel proclamation coming 30 days before Nisan, the Sages derive that a teacher should expound and answer festival law questions starting 30 days before each major festival.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does the Midrashic teaching about 'their shekels preceding his shekels' mean?
A. Israel's coins were minted before Persian currency existed
B. Mordechai collected shekels to bribe Achashverosh before Haman could act
C. G-d commanded the shekel mitzvah to Moshe so that Israel's merit would preemptively counter Haman's silver used to buy their destruction
D. The Temple treasury was richer than the Persian royal treasury
Correct: C — The Gemara teaches that G-d showed Moshe that Haman would one day weigh out silver against Israel, so He commanded the shekel mitzvah in advance — Israel's merit from fulfilling the commandment would 'precede and neutralize' Haman's silver.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which of the following is explicitly EXEMPT from contributing the half-shekel?
A. Kohanim
B. Converts (geirim)
C. Women
D. Freed slaves
Correct: C — Women are exempt from the half-shekel obligation. While their contributions may be accepted voluntarily, they are not required. Converts, Levites, and freed slaves are obligated; the Kohanim's obligation is debated.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What happened on 25 Adar in the shekel collection process?
A. The proclamation about kilaim was made
B. The Temple treasury was sealed for the year
C. Collection tables were moved from the provinces to the Temple Mount in Jerusalem
D. Defaulters had their property seized
Correct: C — After tables were set up in the provinces on 15 Adar, they were relocated to the Temple Mount on 25 Adar, giving remaining contributors a final opportunity to pay their shekel directly at the Temple.
