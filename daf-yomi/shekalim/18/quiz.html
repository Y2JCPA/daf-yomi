QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Meir, why is money found during a Jerusalem festival presumed to be second tithe?
A. Because the Temple treasurers drop coins during festivals
B. Because the majority of people in the city at that time are pilgrims carrying tithe money
C. Because all money in Jerusalem always has second-tithe status
D. Because festival money is marked with a special symbol
Correct: B — R' Meir applies the principle of רוב (majority) to the current circumstance: during a pilgrimage festival, pilgrims with second-tithe money predominate in Jerusalem, so found coins are presumed to have that status.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
An animal found wandering within the boundaries of Jerusalem is presumed by the Mishnah to be which type of offering?
A. A burnt-offering (עולה)
B. A sin-offering (חטאת)
C. A peace-offering (שלמים)
D. A guilt-offering (אשם)
Correct: C — The Mishnah rules that animals found near Jerusalem are presumed to be שלמים (peace-offerings) because most private animals brought by individuals to Jerusalem are peace-offerings, from which the owner may eat.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the primary difference between 'new shekalim' and 'old shekalim' in the Temple's administrative system?
A. New shekalim are gold; old shekalim are silver
B. New shekalim fund the current year's daily offerings; old shekalim go toward Temple upkeep
C. New shekalim are from Israel; old shekalim are from the diaspora
D. New shekalim are collected in Nissan; old shekalim in Tishrei
Correct: B — New shekalim (current year's donations) are the primary fund for the daily tamid and communal offerings. Old shekalim from prior uncollected years are directed toward בדק הבית — structural Temple maintenance and vessel procurement.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
R' Yose disagrees with R' Meir about found money in Jerusalem. What is the basis of R' Yose's ruling?
A. He holds that we never apply majority rules to monetary cases
B. He follows the majority of the year rather than the majority of people present at the moment
C. He believes all found money in Jerusalem must be brought to the Temple treasurer
D. He rules that finder's rights always override second-tithe concerns
Correct: B — R' Yose applies majority temporally: since most days of the year are non-festival, the baseline status of money in Jerusalem is ordinary (חולין). The brief festival period does not change the year-round presumption.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the final destination of Temple surplus funds after all sacrificial and vessel needs are met?
A. They are distributed to the Kohanim as additional wages
B. They are returned to the donors
C. They are used for בדק הבית — Temple structural repairs and maintenance
D. They are sent to the Sanhedrin's operating budget
Correct: C — After covering all prioritized Temple needs (offerings, vessels, incense materials), surplus shekalim funds are redirected to בדק הבית — the maintenance and repair of the Temple structure — ensuring the sacred building remains in perfect condition.
