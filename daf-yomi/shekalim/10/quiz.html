Shekalim 10 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the primary destination for leftover funds from the terumat halishkah (the annual half-shekel collection)?
A. They are returned to the donors who gave that year
B. They go toward voluntary communal burnt offerings (olot nedavah)
C. They are deposited into the Lishkat Chashaim for the poor
D. They are used to pay the Temple priests' wages
Correct: B — Surplus (motar) from the terumat halishkah goes toward olot nedavah — voluntary communal burnt offerings. This is the Mishnah's ruling that ensures all sacred funds serve a public purpose.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was the Lishkat Chashaim (Chamber of Secret Donations) used for?
A. Storing the half-shekel coins before they were counted
B. Secretly receiving and distributing funds to poor people from distinguished families
C. Hiding valuables during times of war or Roman occupation
D. Keeping the Temple vessels overnight for safekeeping
Correct: B — The Lishkat Chashaim allowed God-fearing donors to give anonymously. The funds were distributed three times a year (before Pesach, Shavuot, and Sukkot) to impoverished people from prominent families, preserving their dignity.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How many shofar-shaped collection chests (shofarot) were there in the Temple, according to the Mishnah?
A. Seven, corresponding to the days of the week
B. Ten, corresponding to the Ten Commandments
C. Thirteen, for various designated donation categories
D. Three, corresponding to the Shalosh Regalim (pilgrimage festivals)
Correct: C — There were thirteen shofar-shaped chests in the Temple, each designated for a specific type of donation — new shekalim, old shekalim, bird offerings, wood, frankincense, gold for the Kapporet, and others.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Regarding surplus incense funds (motar ketoret), what does the Mishnah rule?
A. It is given to the Kohen Gadol for personal use
B. It is used to repair cracks in the Temple walls
C. It is used to purchase incense for the following year
D. It is added to the general treasury with no restrictions
Correct: C — The Mishnah rules that motar ketoret — leftover incense funds — is used to purchase incense for the next year. This keeps the surplus within the same category before any excess flows to the general voluntary offering fund.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What happens to vessels donated to the Lishkat Kelim (Chamber of Vessels) that are not suitable for Temple use?
A. They are buried under the Temple Mount
B. They are given to the donating priests as compensation
C. They are held permanently in the chamber and never released
D. They are sold in the market and the proceeds go to the Temple treasury
Correct: D — Vessels that cannot serve a Temple function are sold in the marketplace after a 30-day holding period. The proceeds enter the Temple treasury, consistent with the principle that no Temple donation is ever wasted or lost.
