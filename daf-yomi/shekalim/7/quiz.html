Shekalim 7 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Coins are found in Jerusalem during Passover. How are they treated?
A. As Hullin — found money in the city is always unconsecrated
B. As Second Tithe — the majority of festival money in Jerusalem is ma'aser sheni
C. As Temple property — all Jerusalem money belongs to the Temple
D. As ownerless — the finder keeps them with no restrictions
Correct: B — During pilgrimage festivals, the majority of money circulating in Jerusalem is Second Tithe (ma'aser sheni) that pilgrims have brought to spend on food in the holy city. We therefore presume found coins share that status.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How are coins found on the Temple Mount (but not in the inner sanctum) treated?
A. As Second Tithe — the whole Temple precinct is sacred
B. As Temple property — they go directly to the kohanim
C. As Hullin — many non-priests pass through, so there is no sacred majority
D. They must be left in place until claimed by their owner
Correct: C — The Temple Mount was accessible to many people, including non-priests and ordinary visitors. Since there is no presumption that the majority of money there was consecrated, coins found on the Temple Mount are treated as ordinary Hullin.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
A Samaritan (Cuthean) arrives with his half-shekel. What happens?
A. It is accepted — all inhabitants of the Land of Israel contribute
B. It is accepted, but credited to a separate fund
C. It is declined — Samaritan status as full converts was doubted
D. It is accepted only if accompanied by a verbal declaration
Correct: C — The Rabbis ruled that Samaritan shekalim are not accepted because their status as genuine converts was questionable. The half-shekel is a specifically Israelite communal obligation binding only those who are fully part of Klal Yisrael.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How many times per year was the terumah (set-aside) taken from the shekalim chamber?
A. Once — before Passover only
B. Twice — before Passover and Yom Kippur
C. Three times — before Passover, Shavuot, and Sukkot
D. Monthly — a portion was withdrawn each Rosh Chodesh
Correct: C — The terumah was taken three times per year, before each of the three pilgrimage festivals — Passover, Shavuot, and Sukkot — so that communal sacrifices would be funded throughout the entire agricultural and sacred calendar.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Scattered coins are found on the ground next to an open money-changer's table. To whom do they belong?
A. The money-changer — proximity to his table establishes presumption of ownership
B. The Temple — all Temple Mount coins are sacred
C. The finder — scattered coins have no presumed owner
D. They must be held in escrow until the changer makes a claim
Correct: C — Scattered coins cannot be presumed to belong to the money-changer even if near his table, because the random arrangement indicates accidental loss with no identifiable owner. Only a bundled or tied pile near an open table creates a presumption in the changer's favor.
