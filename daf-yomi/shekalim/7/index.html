📍 The Location Principle

מָעוֹת שֶׁנִּמְצְאוּ לִפְנֵי סוֹחֲרֵי בְהֵמָה — לְחֻלִּין
Core Rule The Mishnah establishes that the sacred status of found coins in Jerusalem depends on where and when they are found. Coins near the altar area go to Temple needs; coins on the Temple Mount are Hullin; coins in the city during festivals are presumed Second Tithe; coins in the city at other times are Hullin. Context — not the coin itself — determines holiness.

🗣️ Coins Near the Money-Changer's Table

R' Yochanan
Coins found near a money-changer's table before it is open for business belong to the finder — we cannot presume ownership by the changer.
Resh Lakish
Even after the table is set up — if the coins are scattered on the ground, they belong to the finder. Scattered coins have no presumed owner.
R' Yochanan
Agreed on scattered. But coins found in a tied bundle or neat pile near an open table — the changer has a presumption of ownership. They must be returned.
Resh Lakish
The arrangement is decisive: bundle implies deliberate placement; scattered implies accidental loss. Form, not location, is the key.
Ruling Scattered coins near a money-changer → finder keeps. Bundled coins near an open table → return to changer. Arrangement determines presumption of ownership.

🏛️ Sacred Status by Zone

Between Altar & Woodpile

  • Goes to Temple needs
  • Innermost sacred area
  • Presumed consecrated funds
  • No individual may claim

Temple Mount (general)

  • Treated as Hullin
  • Non-priests pass through freely
  • No majority of sacred money
  • Finder may keep

Jerusalem — Festival Time

  • Treated as Second Tithe
  • Pilgrims flood city with ma'aser
  • Must be spent on food in Jerusalem
  • Sacred-but-redeemable status

Jerusalem — Non-Festival

  • Treated as Hullin
  • Ordinary commerce dominates
  • No majority of sacred coins
  • Finder keeps freely

📦 The Three Terumot of the Chamber

Collection phase: All Israel sends their half-shekels; the chamber fills throughout the year
↓
First terumah — before Passover: The priests enter the chamber and set aside funds for communal Pesach sacrifices
↓
Second terumah — before Shavuot: A second setting-aside replenishes Temple purchasing power for the summer
↓
Third terumah — before Sukkot: The final annual draw funds the autumn festival offerings
↓
Remainder: Leftover funds after all three terumot go to general Temple upkeep — nothing is wasted
