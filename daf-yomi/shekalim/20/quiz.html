Shekalim 20 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Money found in front of the animal dealers in Jerusalem is presumed to be what?
A. Chullin (non-sacred, ownerless)
B. Hekdesh (dedicated to the Temple)
C. Maaser Sheni (second tithe)
D. Shekalim (Temple half-shekel tax)
Correct: C — The animal market was where pilgrims spent their Maaser Sheni funds to buy animals for offerings and festive meals. Money found there is therefore presumed to be Maaser Sheni and must be redeemed before use.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why is money found on the Temple Mount paradoxically presumed to be chullin (non-sacred)?
A. The Temple Mount has no holiness for monetary purposes
B. Donations were only made at the outer gates, not inside
C. The majority of people passing through carry ordinary personal funds
D. A special rabbinic decree exempted Temple Mount findings
Correct: C — The legal principle of rov (majority) determines status. Most people traversing the Temple Mount are ordinary pilgrims and residents with personal spending money — not people making donations. The majority rules, yielding a chullin presumption despite the sacred location.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does proximity to a pilgrimage festival change the law for found money in Jerusalem?
A. It has no effect; only location matters
B. Near a festival, all found money must go to the Temple treasury
C. Near a festival, found money is presumed Maaser Sheni; otherwise chullin
D. Only money found near Pesach is affected, not other festivals
Correct: C — During festival seasons, masses of pilgrims stream into Jerusalem carrying their Maaser Sheni funds. This shifts the majority of circulating coins to second-tithe money, changing the presumption from chullin to Maaser Sheni.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How many times per year were formal withdrawals (terumot) made from the Temple shekel treasury?
A. Once — before Passover only
B. Twice — Passover and Yom Kippur
C. Three times — before each of the three pilgrimage festivals
D. Monthly, as communal offerings required funding
Correct: C — Three annual withdrawals were made from the shekel chamber — before Pesach, before Shavuot, and before Sukkot — providing funds for communal sacrifices throughout the year. This system is a central institution of Tractate Shekalim.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is done with surplus shekel funds remaining after all communal offerings have been funded?
A. Returned proportionally to all who contributed their half-shekels
B. Distributed to the Kohanim as a supplementary wage
C. Used to purchase golden Temple vessels and sacred implements
D. Carried over to the next year's treasury without special use
Correct: C — Surplus (motar) from the annual shekel collection is used to purchase golden vessels — menorah fittings, altar overlays, and other sacred implements — beautifying the Temple. This ensures every contributor, however humble, has a share in the Temple's splendor.
