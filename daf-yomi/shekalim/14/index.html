🏛️ Temple Families: Secrecy With Purpose vs. Without

Garmu Family

  • Responsible for the Lechem HaPanim (Showbread)
  • Never taught others their baking technique
  • Bread miraculously never became moldy
  • Dismissed; brought back when replacements failed
  • Praised: secrecy prevented suspicion of eating holy bread

Avtinas Family

  • Responsible for the Ketoret (Incense)
  • Never revealed the herb that made smoke rise straight
  • Smoke always ascended in a perfect column
  • Dismissed; brought back when replacements could not replicate
  • Praised: secrecy prevented suspicion of personal use of the herb

Ben Kamtzar

  • Could write all 4 letters of the Divine Name simultaneously
  • Held 4 quills between the fingers of one hand
  • Refused to teach this technique to anyone
  • Had no holy or protective justification for his secrecy
  • Criticized: his name was not 'remembered for good'

📋 Mishnah: The 15 Temple Officials The Mishnah (Shekalim 5:1) enumerates fifteen appointed officials who each oversaw a critical function in the Temple. Their roles spanned: the priestly seals, collection boxes, lots, bird-offerings, wicks, cymbals, sacred music, showbread, incense, curtains, and vestments. These appointments ensured the Temple service ran with precision, accountability, and spiritual integrity.

🗣️ Were the Garmu and Avtinas Families Acting Properly?

Defense
These families acted with complete propriety! They refused to teach their methods so that no one could falsely suspect them of using holy items for personal benefit. Their secrecy was entirely for the sake of Heaven — a form of ethical protection.
Critic
But they were hoarding sacred knowledge essential to the Temple service! Their sons reportedly never wore fine linen garments — proof even they felt uneasy. Should Temple knowledge not be preserved and transmitted beyond one family?
Gemara Resolution
The Sages ultimately praised both families. When Alexandrian craftsmen replaced them, the bread went moldy and the incense smoke scattered — proving divine assistance accompanied these families alone. Their secrecy was vindicated.
Core Distinction Secrecy with holy purpose (Garmu, Avtinas) = praiseworthy. Secrecy without justification (Ben Kamtzar) = blameworthy.

✍️ Ben Kamtzar — The Four-Quill Writer

The Skill: Ben Kamtzar could simultaneously write all four letters of the Divine Name (יהוה) using four quills held between the fingers of a single hand — an extraordinary and unique ability.
↓
The Refusal: Despite possessing this remarkable skill, Ben Kamtzar refused to teach it to any student or successor, offering no explanation or justification.
↓
The Contrast: Unlike the Garmu and Avtinas families whose secrecy shielded the sanctity of holy service, Ben Kamtzar's withholding served only himself — no accusation was being avoided, no holiness protected.
↓
The Verdict: The Sages declared his name was not remembered for good. Talents gifted in service of God must be transmitted; hoarding them diminishes the Temple's enduring legacy.
