Shekalim 13 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Which family was responsible for baking the showbread (לחם הפנים) in the Temple?
A. House of Avtinas
B. House of Garmu
C. Ben Kamtzar's family
D. House of Hugras
Correct: B — The House of Garmu held the hereditary responsibility for baking the showbread. Their secret technique kept the bread miraculously fresh throughout the week.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why is Ben Kamtzar specifically shamed while the House of Garmu is praised, even though both groups withheld their techniques?
A. Ben Kamtzar's skill was less important to the Temple than Garmu's
B. Garmu was from a priestly family but Ben Kamtzar was not
C. Garmu had a valid fear of idolatrous misuse; Ben Kamtzar had no such justification
D. Ben Kamtzar was paid by the Temple while Garmu worked voluntarily
Correct: C — The critical distinction is the reason for secrecy. Garmu feared their bread-baking method could be used for idolatrous worship. Ben Kamtzar's four-finger calligraphy posed no such risk — he withheld for personal prestige alone.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What happened when the Sages tried to replace the House of Garmu with Alexandrian bakers?
A. The Alexandrians succeeded and the family was permanently dismissed
B. The Alexandrians' bread hardened immediately, proving Garmu's technique was irreplaceable
C. The Alexandrian bakers also refused to share their methods
D. The new bread tasted the same but the smoke rose incorrectly
Correct: B — The Alexandrian bakers could not replicate the quality — their showbread hardened. The House of Garmu had to be rehired, which retroactively validated their claim that the secret was held to prevent misuse, not for selfish reasons.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What was Ben Kamtzar's unique calligraphic ability?
A. He could write the entire Torah from memory without a single error
B. He could hold four quills and write all four letters of God's Name simultaneously
C. He could inscribe microscopic text on the stones of the priestly breastplate
D. He invented the square Assyrian script used in Torah scrolls
Correct: B — Ben Kamtzar could hold four quills between his fingers and write יהוה — all four letters of the Divine Name — in a single simultaneous stroke. He refused to teach this remarkable skill to anyone.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which verse does the Mishnah invoke against those 'remembered for shame' like Ben Kamtzar?
A. "Love your neighbor as yourself" (Leviticus 19:18)
B. "You shall not steal" (Exodus 20:13)
C. "The memory of the righteous is for blessing, but the name of the wicked shall rot" (Proverbs 10:7)
D. "Do not stand idly by your neighbor's blood" (Leviticus 19:16)
Correct: C — Proverbs 10:7 is applied: those who contributed are 'remembered for blessing,' while those who withheld their gifts without cause — like Ben Kamtzar — have their names associated with rot and shame for generations.
