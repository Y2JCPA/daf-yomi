🤔 Why Did Garmu and Avtinas Keep Secrets?

Questioner
If Garmu and Avtinas are praised, why did they refuse to teach their techniques to others? Is not withholding Torah-knowledge always forbidden?
Sages
They feared their knowledge could be used for עבודה זרה — idolatrous worship. One who withholds to prevent desecration of sacred things acts righteously.
Questioner
Ben Kamtzar also refused to teach — why is he shamed while they are praised? Is the distinction not arbitrary?
Sages
Ben Kamtzar had no such justification. Writing four letters simultaneously poses no risk of idolatrous misuse. He withheld for personal glory alone — and that is unforgivable in sacred service.
Core Distinction Withholding to protect the sacred = praiseworthy restraint. Withholding for self-aggrandizement = spiritual stinginess that corrupts the Temple's communal mission.

🍞 The Showbread Secret of the House of Garmu

The Challenge: Twelve loaves of showbread (לחם הפנים) must remain fresh, warm, and whole on the Temple table for an entire week
↓
The Method: House of Garmu used a closely-guarded baking technique — exact details never taught outside the family across generations
↓
The Miracle: Priests eating the bread on Shabbat found it as fragrant and fresh as at the moment of baking — a weekly wonder
↓
Their Defense: They feared outsiders might replicate the method to bake consecrated-seeming bread for idolatrous shrines
↓
The Proof: When the Sages hired Alexandrian bakers to replace them, the substitute bread hardened immediately — the family had to be rehired, vindicating their irreplaceable craft

✍️ Ben Kamtzar's Four-Finger Technique

בֶּן קַמְצַר לֹא רָצָה לְלַמֵּד עַל מַעֲשֵׂה הַכְּתָב — וְעָלָיו נֶאֱמַר: שֵׁם רְשָׁעִים יִרְקָב
🖊️ The Shameful Secret Ben Kamtzar could hold four quills between his fingers and write all four letters of the Divine Name יהוה simultaneously in a single stroke. This virtuosity had no conceivable idolatrous application. By refusing to teach it he denied Israel the benefit of his gift for the sake of remaining uniquely indispensable. The Mishnah invokes Proverbs 10:7: "The memory of the righteous is for blessing — but the name of the wicked shall rot." His name endures, but as a byword for spiritual selfishness.

🔄 The Ethics of Specialized Temple Knowledge

A craftsman possesses a unique skill essential to Temple service
↓
Risk of Sacred Misuse?
Skill could enable idolatry or profanation → Permitted to guard the secret
Examples: Garmu (showbread), Avtinas (incense)
No Risk of Misuse?
Skill is purely beneficial with no profane application → Must be shared — withholding is selfish and brings lasting shame
Example: Ben Kamtzar
↓
Temple ideal: כָּל הָעוֹסְקִין בְּצָרְכֵי צִבּוּר יְהוּ עוֹסְקִין לְשֵׁם שָׁמַיִם
All who serve communal needs must do so for Heaven's sake alone
