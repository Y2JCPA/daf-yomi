📖 The Mishnah's Rule: Surplus of the Shekalim

מסכת שקלים פרק ד׳ משנה א׳
מִשְׁנָה מוֹתַר שְׁקָלִים מֶה הָיוּ עוֹשִׂין בָּהֶם — What did they do with the surplus of the shekalim? They used it for Kietz HaMizbe'ach (קִיץ הַמִּזְבֵּחַ) — compulsory burnt-offerings brought when the altar was idle so it would never stand empty. These offerings were funded from accumulated surplus to ensure the Temple service never ceased.
4 / 9

⚖️ What Counts as Kietz HaMizbe'ach?

R' Meir
Kietz HaMizbe'ach refers to olot nedavah — freewill burnt-offerings purchased from surplus funds whenever the altar would otherwise stand idle during the day.
R' Yehuda
The surplus funds are used to purchase animals in advance, stored nearby, so priests always have an offering ready. The altar must never be empty — even a momentary gap is avoided.
R' Shimon
I agree the altar must not stand empty, but the surplus terumah, not just surplus shekalim, may also be applied — and it further funds the gold plating of the Temple's inner walls and vessels.
Bottom Line All agree the altar must never be idle. Surplus funds ensure a continuous flow of offerings.

📦 Thirteen Shofar Chests of the Temple

New Shekel Shekalim

  • Current year's half-shekel obligation
  • Designated for Tamid daily offerings
  • Musafin and public sacrifices
  • Most urgent — used immediately

Old Shekel Shekalim

  • Previous year's shekalim not yet used
  • Rolled into general Temple treasury
  • Funds Kietz HaMizbe'ach offerings
  • Lower priority than current year

Voluntary Donations

  • Bird-offerings, sin-offerings
  • Wood contributions for altar
  • Frankincense (levona) donations
  • Gold plating donations

🔄 The Annual Shekalim Cycle — From Collection to Use

1 Adar: Announcement made — 'Give your shekalim!' Moneychangers set up tables in provinces
↓
15 Adar: Moneychangers move to the Temple Mount — exchange continues with surcharge (kalbon)
↓
Nisan: First terumah taken from chamber — funds Tamid, Musaf, and all public offerings through summer
↓
Atzeret (Shavuot): Second terumah taken — replenishes the chamber for the next season
↓
Rosh HaShanah: Third terumah taken — ensures all annual offerings are covered; any leftover becomes motar terumah
↓
Year End: Surplus calculated — remainder designated for Kietz HaMizbe'ach and Temple infrastructure
