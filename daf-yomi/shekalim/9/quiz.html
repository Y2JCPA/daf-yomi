Shekalim 9 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishnah in Shekalim, what were the surplus shekalim (motar shekalim) used for?
A. Gold plating of the Temple walls
B. Kietz HaMizbe'ach — burnt-offerings to keep the altar active
C. Paying the Levites' salaries
D. Returned to the donors at year's end
Correct: B — The Mishnah (Shekalim 4:1) states that motar shekalim was used for Kietz HaMizbe'ach — compulsory freewill burnt-offerings purchased to ensure the altar was never idle.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How many times per year was the terumah (taking) made from the Temple chamber?
A. Once — on Rosh HaShanah
B. Twice — Passover and Sukkot
C. Three times — Nisan, Atzeret, and Rosh HaShanah
D. Monthly, at each new moon
Correct: C — The terumah was taken three times annually: before Passover (Nisan), before Shavuot (Atzeret), and before Rosh HaShanah, ensuring sufficient funding throughout the year.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was done with the surplus of the terumah (motar terumah)?
A. It was locked in the chamber for the following year
B. Distributed among the priestly divisions
C. Used for Kietz HaMizbe'ach, city needs, and Temple courts
D. Donated to the poor of Jerusalem
Correct: C — Motar terumah had a broader range of uses than motar shekalim — it funded the altar's burnt-offerings, city infrastructure, and Temple court maintenance.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the principle behind Kietz HaMizbe'ach?
A. The altar must be cooled at the end of each day
B. The altar must never stand idle — offerings must be brought even when no private offerings are scheduled
C. Priests must take turns resting from sacrificial service
D. Only surplus from the previous year could fund public offerings
Correct: B — Kietz HaMizbe'ach (literally 'summer of the altar') refers to the practice of purchasing animals from surplus funds to ensure the altar always had an offering burning on it.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If money was set aside to purchase a sin-offering animal and the animal died, what happened to the leftover money?
A. It was returned to the donor
B. It fell to the Temple general treasury (motar chatat)
C. It was used to purchase a different type of offering
D. It was given to the kohen who performed the service
Correct: B — Surplus from sin-offering dedications (motar chatat) goes to the Temple's general treasury — the donor loses their claim since the original dedication was for atonement, already fulfilled.
