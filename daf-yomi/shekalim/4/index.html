🪙 Half-Shekel: Who Gives, Who's Exempt?

כָּל הַנּוֹתְנִין שֶׁקְלֵיהֶם — מִשֶּׁקֶל וּלְמַעְלָה, נוֹתְנִין קָלְבּוֹן
Core Question of Daf 4 Daf 4 maps the full landscape of half-shekel obligation: when money-changers set up tables across the land, who pays the kalbon surcharge, and the pivotal dispute about whether Kohanim must give at all — Ben Buchri argues they are exempt; the Sages respond that a priest who gives has committed no sin, implying he ought to give.

👨‍⚖️ Are Priests Obligated to Give the Half-Shekel?

Ben Buchri
Priests are exempt from the half-shekel. The Torah's command applies to the Israelites — Kohanim serve in the Temple itself; that is their contribution.
The Sages
The Mishnah says a priest who gives has not sinned — this phrasing implies he should give. All Israel, including Kohanim, are obligated.
Ben Buchri
The Kohanim argued: our blood and our service atone for Israel — we already sustain the Temple. We should not be compelled to fund communal sacrifices from our own pockets.
The Sages
The tamid offering bought with shekalim atones for all Israel — including priests. They benefit from it and therefore must contribute to it like everyone else.
Practical Compromise Priestly contributions were accepted, but set aside and not used to purchase communal sacrifices — lest it appear the priests are formally obligated. A nuanced middle ground.

📊 Categories of Half-Shekel Givers

Obligated (חַיָּב)

  • Adult Israelite men (age 20+)
  • Converts (גֵּרִים)
  • Freed slaves (עֲבָדִים מְשֻׁחְרָרִים)
  • Those living outside the Land of Israel

Exempt (פָּטוּר)

  • Women (נָשִׁים)
  • Slaves (עֲבָדִים)
  • Minors (קְטַנִּים)
  • Priests — per Ben Buchri's ruling

Voluntary — Accepted (נְדָבָה)

  • Women may give — accepted ✓
  • Slaves may give — accepted ✓
  • Minors: father may give on their behalf
  • Priests: accepted but set aside separately
