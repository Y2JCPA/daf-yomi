Shekalim 6 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
If someone pays his own shekel AND his friend's shekel using one sela coin, how many kalbonot does he owe?
A. None — paying for a friend exempts the surcharge
B. One kalbon only
C. Two kalbonot — one per person
D. Three kalbonot — a penalty for consolidating
Correct: B — The Mishnah rules that when paying for a willing adult friend with a single sela, only ONE kalbon is owed — the two obligations merge into a single exchange transaction.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai, what is the status of a priest who does NOT give the machatzit hashekel?
A. He is praiseworthy for asserting priestly privilege
B. He is exempt and commits no violation
C. He is sinning
D. He must bring a korban to atone
Correct: C — Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai strongly disagreed with Ben Bukhri — he held that priests ARE obligated, and failure to give constitutes a sin, since they eat from the communal offerings they did not fund.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
On which date were moneychanger tables set up INSIDE the Temple courtyard itself?
A. 1st of Adar
B. 15th of Adar
C. 25th of Adar
D. 1st of Nissan
Correct: C — Tables were set up throughout the land on Adar 15, but moved into the Temple precincts on Adar 25 — when active social pressure and pledging obligations began for those who had not yet paid.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does R' Meir rule that paying for a deaf-mute or minor requires TWO kalbonot (rather than one)?
A. They are more expensive to represent legally
B. They lack da'at and cannot join the payer's intent, so their payment stands separately
C. They are exempt from shekalim so the entire payment is a surcharge
D. The Sages imposed a penalty on those who pay for dependents
Correct: B — R' Meir holds that merging two payments into one kalbon requires that both parties have legal cognizance (da'at). A deaf-mute or minor lacks this, so their shekel cannot 'join' the payer's and each incurs a separate kalbon.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What happens to surplus funds (motar) left over from the communal chamber separations (terumah)?
A. Returned to those who gave shekalim proportionally
B. Donated to the Kohen Gadol's personal fund
C. Used for voluntary communal offerings (nedavah)
D. Stored until the following year's Nissan
Correct: C — Motar terumah — surplus from the chamber separations — is designated for nedavah (voluntary communal burnt-offerings), keeping the Temple altar continuously active and the community's atonement ongoing.
