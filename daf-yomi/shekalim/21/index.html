🗣️ Scattered or Stacked? Does Arrangement Matter?

R. Yochanan
Scattered coins on the Temple Mount — each coin stands alone. We rule each one to be Maaser Sheni based on its location, regardless of the others.
Reish Lakish
Stacked coins suggest intentional placement — they were left there by someone. This strengthens the presumption even more. The act of stacking indicates ownership and purpose.
R. Yochanan
Agreed on stacked coins. But scattered coins could have fallen accidentally — still, location governs. The Temple Mount is designated for Maaser Sheni, and that designation applies.
Reish Lakish
Then we agree: both scattered and stacked on the Temple Mount follow Maaser Sheni status. The difference only matters when found near the border zone between sacred and profane space.
Halacha Found coins on the Temple Mount — Maaser Sheni. Found in the Jerusalem market stalls — Ḥullin. Found between them — follow majority, or the location closer to which they were found.

🗺️ The Three Zones of Jerusalem for Found Objects

🏛️ Temple Mount (Har HaBayit)

  • Presumed: Maaser Sheni
  • Must redeem if coins
  • Meat found here: Shelamim offering
  • Rationale: Sacred space = sacred objects
  • Applies year-round

🛒 Market Streets (Shuk)

  • Presumed: Ḥullin (profane)
  • No special restrictions
  • Meat found here: Ḥullin
  • Rationale: Majority of city commerce is profane
  • Straightforward finders-keepers rules

⚠️ The In-Between Zone

  • Disputed — case-by-case analysis
  • Consider: majority population
  • Consider: proximity to each zone
  • Consider: time of year (Regel)
  • Strictest ruling may apply

🤫 The Chamber of Secrets (Lishkat HaChasha'im)

לִשְׁכַּת חֲשָׁאִים — יִרְאֵי חֵטְא נוֹתְנִין לְתוֹכָהּ בְּחָשַׁאי, וַעֲנִיִּים בְּנֵי טוֹבִים מִתְפַּרְנְסִין מִמֶּנָּה בְּחָשַׁאי
Temple Institution The Temple maintained a special chamber called Lishkat HaChasha'im (לִשְׁכַּת חֲשָׁאִים) — the Chamber of the Discreet. Wealthy people of good heart would secretly deposit money there, and poor people of noble families would secretly take from it. This preserved the dignity of the giver and the recipient alike. No one knew who gave, no one knew who took. This chamber embodied the highest form of tzedakah: giving without any expectation of honor.

💰 What Happens to Surplus Temple Funds (Motar)?

Motar HaNesachim — surplus from libation donations: goes to purchase communal burnt offerings (Olot Tzibur)
↓
Motar Ḥattat — surplus from sin offering collections: goes to Temple maintenance and repair (Bedek HaBayit)
↓
Motar Asham — surplus guilt offering funds: same as sin offering surplus → Bedek HaBayit
↓
Motar Olah — surplus burnt offering donations: also to Bedek HaBayit; the specific vessel used determines allocation
↓
General principle: surplus funds follow the original tzinor (conduit/channel) — the purpose of the donation determines the destination of any remainder
↓
If donor is silent about purpose and surplus arises: default to Bedek HaBayit (Temple upkeep) rather than personal return
