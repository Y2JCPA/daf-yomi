Shekalim 21 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
If you find a stack of coins on the Temple Mount in Jerusalem, what is their presumptive legal status?
A. Ḥullin — they belong to whoever finds them
B. Maaser Sheni — they must be redeemed and used in Jerusalem
C. Hekdesh — they must be donated to the Temple treasury
D. Unknown — no presumption can be established
Correct: B — Coins found on the Temple Mount are presumed to be Maaser Sheni (second tithe). The sacred location creates a legal presumption, and stacked coins (showing intentional placement) reinforce this status even more strongly than scattered coins.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was the Lishkat HaChasha'im (Chamber of the Discreet) used for?
A. Storing Temple vessels between services
B. Anonymous deposits from wealthy donors for poor families of noble lineage
C. Hiding the Temple's annual surplus from the Roman authorities
D. Safeguarding the High Priest's personal funds
Correct: B — The Lishkat HaChasha'im was a special chamber where God-fearing wealthy people secretly deposited charity, and poor people of noble families secretly withdrew from it — preserving everyone's dignity. This was considered the highest form of giving.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
You find slaughtered meat near the market stalls of Jerusalem (not near the Temple). What is its presumptive status?
A. Shelamim (peace offering) — must be eaten within Temple walls
B. Ḥullin (profane) — no special restrictions apply
C. Nevelah (improperly slaughtered) — forbidden to eat
D. Maaser Behemah (animal tithe) — must be brought to the Temple
Correct: B — Meat found near the market stalls is presumed Ḥullin (profane/non-sacred). The market area is not associated with Temple offerings, so the default assumption is that this is ordinary slaughtered meat with no special restrictions.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What happens to Motar HaNesachim — surplus funds collected for libation offerings (wine, oil, flour)?
A. Returned to the original donors
B. Added to next year's Shekalim collection
C. Used to purchase communal burnt offerings (Olot Tzibur)
D. Transferred to the Bedek HaBayit (Temple maintenance) fund
Correct: C — Surplus libation (nesachim) funds go toward purchasing communal burnt offerings (Olot Tzibur). Each type of Temple surplus follows its own designated channel (tzinor) — the original purpose of the donation determines where the remainder goes.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the time of year affect the presumption about found coins in the 'border zone' of Jerusalem?
A. Time of year is irrelevant — only location matters
B. Before a pilgrimage festival, more pilgrims bring Maaser Sheni coins, strengthening that presumption
C. During the winter months, all found coins are presumed Ḥullin
D. After Yom Kippur, all found coins revert to Temple treasury ownership
Correct: B — The Regel (pilgrimage festival) significantly increases the number of Maaser Sheni coins circulating in Jerusalem, since pilgrims bring second tithe to spend there. This strengthens the probability that found coins are Maaser Sheni. After the festival, pilgrims depart and this presumption weakens.
