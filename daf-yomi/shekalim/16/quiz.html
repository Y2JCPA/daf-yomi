Shekalim 16 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
How many shofarot (collection boxes) were maintained in the Temple courtyard?
A. Seven
B. Ten
C. Thirteen
D. Eighteen
Correct: C — There were thirteen shofarot in the Temple, each designated for a specific type of donation — from new shekalim to frankincense to gold for the Kapporet.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was the defining feature of the Lishkat Chassha'im (Chamber of Secrets)?
A. It stored the Temple treasury documents under seal
B. Wealthy gave and impoverished received in complete anonymity
C. It was reserved for Sanhedrin private deliberations
D. It held confiscated property awaiting judgment
Correct: B — The Chamber of Secrets enabled anonymous giving and receiving. Neither party knew the other, protecting the dignity of poor families of distinction — considered the highest form of tzedakah.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why were Nicanor's Gates left in plain bronze while all other Temple gates were covered in gold?
A. Bronze was more costly than gold at that period
B. Nicanor stipulated bronze in his donation contract
C. To commemorate the miracle of the gate that returned after being cast into the sea
D. The priests found the bronze easier to maintain
Correct: C — When one of Nicanor's bronze doors was thrown overboard to calm a storm and then miraculously reappeared beneath the ship's hull, the gates were left in natural bronze to memorialize the miracle.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What were Ben Kattin's two innovations for the Temple laver (kiyor)?
A. He replaced bronze with silver and added a drainage pipe
B. He added twelve spigots and a wheel mechanism to submerge it overnight
C. He moved it inside the Sanctuary and doubled its size
D. He inscribed the priestly blessings on its basin
Correct: B — Ben Kattin added 12 spigots so 12 priests could wash simultaneously, and invented a wheel to lower the laver into a water chamber each night so its water would not become invalid from remaining still.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the accepted halachah, what happens to surplus shekalim funds after the terumah collection?
A. They are returned proportionally to the donors
B. They are donated to the Chamber of Secrets
C. They are used for communal libations and meal-offerings
D. They are saved and rolled over to the following year
Correct: C — The halachah established that surplus shekalim are directed to public needs — specifically wine, oil, and flour for the nesachim (libations) and menachot (meal-offerings) accompanying communal sacrifices. Sacred communal funds remain communal.
