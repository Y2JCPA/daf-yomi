Shekalim 11 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
How many shofar-shaped collection chests were in the Temple?
A. Seven
B. Ten
C. Thirteen
D. Eighteen
Correct: C — The Mishnah in Shekalim states there were thirteen shofar-shaped chests (שלשה עשר שופרות), each designated for a different type of Temple donation.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
If coins are found near the Temple Mount, what is their presumed status?
A. Unconsecrated (chullin)
B. Maaser sheni money
C. Temple treasury funds
D. Priestly tithes
Correct: B — Coins found near the Temple Mount are presumed to be maaser sheni (second tithe) money, which must be spent in Jerusalem on food. The area near the Temple was where people redeemed their maaser sheni.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the dispute between R' Yochanan and Reish Lakish regarding surplus bird offering funds?
A. Whether birds can be offered at all after Yom Kippur
B. Whether surplus goes to the Temple treasury or reverts to the donor
C. Whether birds must be purchased with shekel funds only
D. Whether bird offerings require a Cohen to purchase them
Correct: B — R' Yochanan holds that surplus from bird offering funds goes to the Temple treasury (hekdesh), while Reish Lakish holds it reverts to the donor's estate. The resolution depends on the exact language of the donor's consecration.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Yerushalmi, what ultimately happens to surplus Temple funds (motar) at the end of the year?
A. Returned to donors who contributed that year
B. Saved for the following year's shekel collection
C. Used for communal burnt offerings and Temple infrastructure like water channels and city walls
D. Distributed to the Kohanim as an additional portion
Correct: C — Surplus Temple funds are first used for communal voluntary burnt offerings (olot nedavah), and any remaining surplus goes toward Temple and Jerusalem infrastructure — water channels, city walls, and towers.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the key distinction the Yerushalmi draws to resolve the motar dispute?
A. Whether the donation was made before or after Rosh Hashanah
B. Whether the donor was a Cohen or a Yisrael
C. Whether the donor consecrated 'these coins for my offering' vs. 'I will bring an offering from these coins'
D. Whether the coins were silver or gold
Correct: C — The Yerushalmi resolves the dispute by distinguishing language: 'these coins ARE for my offering' consecrates all of them to hekdesh, so surplus goes to the Temple. But 'I will BRING an offering from these' only consecrates what is actually used, leaving surplus unconsecrated.
