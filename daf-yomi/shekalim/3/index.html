🛐 Do Kohanim Pay the Half-Shekel?

R' Yehuda
Kohanim do not need to pay the half-shekel. They may keep the funds — it is permitted for them, and enforcing payment would cause unnecessary strife (eivah).
Tanna Kamma
Kohanim who did not pay — we do not protest against them. They derive their own teaching from Scripture that they are exempt, and we leave them be.
R' Shimon
Even the Kohanim Gedolim paid the shekel voluntarily, to avoid the appearance that the Tamid offering brought from public funds was somehow deficient.
Gemara
The Kohanim derive their exemption from the verse: "v'chiper al nafshoteichem" — one who needs atonement brings the shekel; Kohanim serving in the Temple do not require this atonement levy.
Bottom Line Kohanim are technically exempt from the half-shekel, but if they paid voluntarily, their contribution is accepted and they are not rebuked.

⚖️ Obligated vs. Exempt — Who Pays the Half-Shekel?

✅ Obligated

  • Adult Jewish males (20+)
  • Converts who have completed conversion
  • Freed slaves (once freed)
  • Those who became obligated mid-year

🔶 Exempt but Accepted

  • Women — exempt, but payment accepted
  • Slaves — exempt, but payment accepted
  • Minors — exempt, but payment accepted
  • Kohanim — traditionally exempt, payment tolerated

❌ Refused Entirely

  • Non-Jews (גויים) — not accepted
  • Kutim (Samaritans) — not accepted
  • Those who separated from the community

📜 Key Verse: The Source of Obligation

זֶה יִתְּנוּ כָּל הָעֹבֵר עַל הַפְּקֻדִים מַחֲצִית הַשֶּׁקֶל בְּשֶׁקֶל הַקֹּדֶשׁ
שמות ל׃יג "This shall they give — everyone who passes through the census — half a shekel of the holy shekel... a half-shekel as a donation to God." The phrase "כל העובר על הפקודים" — 'everyone who passes through the census' — is interpreted to mean adult Jewish males, establishing both the obligation and its limits.

👁️ Why We Refuse Shekalim from Non-Jews

Step 1 — The Mishnah's Rule: Non-Jews and Kutim who wish to donate a half-shekel — we do not accept it from them.
↓
Step 2 — The Concern: Accepting funds from non-Jews creates a stake for them in the Temple service. They might later claim ownership or rights over the offerings brought with their money.
↓
Step 3 — Scriptural Basis: The verse says "v'chiper al nafshoteichem" — the atonement is specifically for the souls of Israel, not outsiders.
↓
Step 4 — Communal Integrity: The public offerings (Tamid, Musaf) must be funded entirely by the Jewish people. Mixing in foreign funds compromises the collective ownership.
↓
Conclusion: Even well-intentioned donations from non-Jews are returned. The boundary around communal Temple funding is absolute.
