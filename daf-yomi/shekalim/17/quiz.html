Shekalim 17 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
How many shofar-shaped collection chests (shofarot) stood in the Temple?
A. Seven
B. Ten
C. Thirteen
D. Eighteen
Correct: C — The Mishnah (Shekalim 6:1) teaches there were thirteen shofarot, corresponding to the thirteen Temple gates and the thirteen prostration spots in the courtyard.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was the primary use of 'motar' — surplus funds — from the shofar chests?
A. Returned to the donors who contributed them
B. Used for nesachim — communal libations of wine, oil, and flour
C. Given to the Levites as a supplement to their tithes
D. Stored in reserve for the following Temple year
Correct: B — Surplus from the shofar chests was used to purchase nesachim (libations) accompanying communal burnt offerings. Any remaining surplus beyond that went toward bedek habayit — Temple structural maintenance.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What distinguishes the 'שקלים ישנים' (old shekalim) chest from the 'שקלים חדשים' (new shekalim) chest?
A. Old shekalim are silver; new shekalim are gold coins
B. Old shekalim cover prior year arrears; new shekalim fund the current Temple year
C. Old shekalim go to Temple repair; new shekalim buy tamid offerings
D. They are identical in purpose — the names are merely ceremonial
Correct: B — The new shekalim chest receives current-year half-shekel contributions. The old shekalim chest is for donors who missed paying in the prior year, ensuring proper annual accounting between Temple fiscal years.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Yehuda, what is the halakhic status of money placed into a Temple shofar chest?
A. It remains the donor's property until the Temple officer formally accepts it
B. It becomes consecrated (hekdesh) for its designated purpose the moment it is deposited
C. It requires a verbal declaration of consecration alongside the act of deposit
D. It has no sanctity until it is actually spent on the designated item
Correct: B — R' Yehuda holds that the act of placing money into the shofar chest is itself an act of consecration — the chest's label determines the holiness of its contents. R' Shimon disagrees, arguing donor intent governs the extent of consecration.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The thirteen prostration spots in the Temple courtyard were established to commemorate what event?
A. The thirteen tribes who donated to building the First Temple
B. The thirteen High Priests who served during the First Temple period
C. The thirteen breaches that the Greeks made in the Temple's inner wall, which the Hasmoneans repaired
D. The thirteen miracles that occurred during the Temple dedication
Correct: C — The Rabbis ordained prostration at the thirteen spots in the courtyard where the Greeks had breached the inner soreg wall. After the Hasmoneans repaired these breaches, the Sages decreed that worshippers bow there to honor the miracle of the Temple's rededication.
