🗃️ The Thirteen Shofarot — Two Categories

Seven Designated Chests

  • שְׁקָלִים חֲדָשִׁים — New shekalim (current year)
  • שְׁקָלִים יְשָׁנִים — Old shekalim (prior year arrears)
  • קִינִּין — Bird pairs (obligatory offerings)
  • קִינֵי זָב וְזָבָה — Birds for zav/zavah impurity
  • עֵצִים — Wood for the altar fire
  • לְבוֹנָה — Frankincense for the incense
  • זָהָב לַכַּפֹּרֶת — Gold for covering the Ark

Six Free-Will Chests (נְדָבָה)

  • Six identical chests each labeled נְדָבָה
  • Accept any voluntary monetary donation
  • Funds buy communal burnt offerings
  • Donor need not specify which offering
  • Surplus from these → nesachim libations
  • Further surplus → general Temple treasury

💰 Dispute: What Becomes of Surplus Funds (מוֹתָר)?

R' Yehuda
Surplus from the bird-offering chest (קינין) goes directly to nedavah — once deposited, the money is consecrated for Temple purposes and cannot revert to the donor
R' Shimon
The donor intended his deposit only for his specific obligation — whatever exceeds that remains his property; if identifiable, it should be returned
R' Yehuda
The act of placing money into a Temple shofar chest is itself an act of consecration — intent for a specific sum is irrelevant once deposited
R' Shimon
We follow donor intent — Temple consecration extends only as far as the donor's purpose; surplus is not automatically sanctified beyond that intent
Practical Ruling Surplus from all shofar chests is used first for nesachim (wine, oil, flour libations for communal burnt offerings); any remaining surplus goes toward bedek habayit — Temple structural maintenance

⚖️ Accounting for New and Old Shekalim

Adar 1st: Announcement made throughout the land — begin collecting the half-shekel for the new Temple year (שְׁקָלִים חֲדָשִׁים)
↓
Adar 15th: Money changers set up tables; those who have not yet paid are urged to do so before the annual terumah drawing
↓
Unpaid from prior year? Donor deposits into the 'old shekel' chest (שְׁקָלִים יְשָׁנִים) — tracked separately to ensure proper annual accounting
↓
Nisan 1st: New year for Temple offerings begins — the annual terumah drawing takes place; only new shekalim fund this year's communal offerings
↓
Old shekel funds are disbursed for the same communal purposes (tamid, musaf) but accounted as satisfying the prior year's obligation — full accountability maintained

📦 The Sanctity of Designated Temple Funds

כָּל הַשּׁוֹפָרוֹת שֶׁבַּמִּקְדָּשׁ הָיוּ שְׁלֹשָׁה עָשָׂר — All the shofar chests in the Temple were thirteen (Mishnah Shekalim 6:1)
Core Principle A central halakhic theme of this daf: once money is placed in a Temple shofar chest, it assumes the holiness of its designated purpose. Funds for wood may only purchase altar wood; funds for frankincense may only buy Temple incense. Even if the Temple already has a surplus of that item, the funds retain their designation. This prevents Temple officers from repurposing consecrated funds — a donor's intent, expressed through the choice of chest, is legally binding. The Rabbis saw this strict accounting as essential to maintaining public trust in Temple administration.
