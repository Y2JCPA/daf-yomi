👑 Does Manasseh Have a Share in the World-to-Come?

Anonymous Tanna
Manasseh has NO share in the World-to-Come — he led Israel into grievous sin for 55 years and filled Jerusalem with innocent blood.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Anyone who says Manasseh has no share DISCOURAGES penitents! He repented sincerely — God made an opening for him (II Chronicles 33:13).
Rabbi Shimon ben Yoḥai
The verse says 'He made an opening' rather than 'He received his entreaty' — God created a special tunnel through the heavens to accept Manasseh's repentance despite the angels blocking it.
Baraita
Manasseh studied 55 interpretations of Torat Kohanim — one per year of his reign. His Torah knowledge was vast; his repentance was profound.
🔑 Core Principle Repentance (תשובה) is never foreclosed — even the most wicked king can return. To deny this discourages all penitents.

🌍 God Nearly Destroys the World — Two Near-Calamities

God seeks to return the world to chaos and void (תֹּהוּ וָבֹהוּ)
As it was before Creation
↓
On account of Jehoiakim
His wickedness was so severe God nearly annihilated all creation — but He observed the merit of Jehoiakim's generation and relented
On account of Zedekiah's generation
The righteous had been exiled; only the wicked remained. God nearly annihilated all — but He observed the merit of Zedekiah himself and relented
↓
Result: In both cases, God withheld destruction — the merit of even one righteous individual or remnant protects the world

😈 The Wicked Kings — Escalating Depravity

Ahaz

  • Nullified Temple service entirely
  • Sealed the Torah (prohibited study)
  • Placed idol in the upper chamber of the Temple
  • Cited: Isaiah 8:16 — 'seal the Torah among my disciples'

Manasseh

  • Initially crafted one idol face; ultimately four faces
  • Placed the idol in the Sanctuary itself
  • Excised God's names from sacred books
  • Filled Jerusalem with innocent blood
  • BUT: repented — God made a tunnel through heaven for him

Amon

  • Committed sins more disgraceful than any predecessor
  • Engaged in sexual immorality with his own mother
  • Did NOT repent — unlike his father Manasseh
  • Cited: II Chronicles 33:23 — 'Amon increased his guilt'

Jehoiakim

  • Said: 'My predecessors did not know how to express insolence to God'
  • Declared: 'We don't need God's light — we have parvayim gold'
  • Engraved the Divine Name on his flesh in defiance
  • NOT listed among kings lacking World-to-Come — reason debated

🛡️ Psalm 91 — A Family Blessing

כִּי בִי חָשַׁק וַאֲפַלְּטֵהוּ — 'Because he has desired Me, therefore I will deliver him' (Psalms 91:14)
💡 Who Blessed Solomon with Psalm 91? The Gemara teaches a remarkable reading of Psalm 91: • Verses 1–8: David blessed his son Solomon — blessings a father gives a son (protection from evil, from bad dreams, from destructive students) • Verses 9–13: Bathsheba, Solomon's mother, continued the blessing — a mother's blessings for her child • Verses 14–16: God Himself concludes in first person — 'Because he has desired Me, I will deliver him… With long life I will satisfy him' The blessing 'No evil shall befall you' means: the evil inclination shall not dominate you. 'Nor shall any plague come near your tent' means: you will not have a child or student who overcooks his food in public (sins brazenly and brings shame).

🍞 Great is Eating — The Story of Micah's Idol

Micah crafts an idol and sets it up — a grave sin that persists for generations in Israel
↓
The ministering angels seek to have Micah dismissed from the world as punishment for his idolatry
↓
God says: 'Leave him — his bread is available for travelers.' Micah provided hospitality; this merit protected him.
↓
Consequence: The sin of Micah's idol caused those involved in the incident of the concubine of Gibeah (Judges 19–20) to fall — they crossed by Micah's idol without protesting it
↓
Rabbi Yoḥanan in name of Rabbi Yosei ben Kisma: 'Great is eating — it distanced two clans (Ammon and Moab) from Israel, as they met you not with bread and water' (Deuteronomy 23:4)
↓
Inverse principle: Bread/hospitality can protect even sinners (Micah); its withholding excludes entire nations. Eating connects to covenant and relationship.
