Sanhedrin 103 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Rabbi Yoḥanan objects to saying Manasseh has no share in the World-to-Come. What is his reason?
A. Manasseh was a great Torah scholar with 55 interpretations
B. Saying so discourages penitents, since Manasseh did in fact repent
C. The verse in Chronicles does not mention Manasseh being wicked
D. Manasseh's generation was too important to lose entirely
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan teaches that excluding Manasseh — who genuinely repented — sends the message that repentance is futile, thereby discouraging others from returning to God.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say God made a 'tunnel' (חֲתִירָה) through heaven for Manasseh's prayer?
A. To symbolize that Manasseh dug himself out of his own sin
B. Because the angels blocked his prayer and God created an alternate path to receive it
C. Because the verse uses the word 'opening' rather than a standard prayer term
D. Because Manasseh prayed underground in captivity in Babylonia
Correct: C — Rabbi Shimon ben Yoḥai notices the verse says 'He made an opening for him' rather than 'He received his entreaty' — suggesting God created a special passage, interpreted as a heavenly tunnel bypassing the angels who opposed accepting Manasseh's repentance.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda citing Rabbi Yirmeya bar Abba, which four groups do NOT receive the Divine Presence?
A. Idol worshippers, Sabbath violators, adulterers, and murderers
B. Cynics, liars, flatterers, and slanderers
C. The arrogant, the lustful, the greedy, and the envious
D. Those who eat forbidden foods, neglect Torah, dishonor parents, and quarrel
Correct: B — The Gemara lists: group of לֵצִים (cynics/scorners), שַׁקְרָנִים (liars), חַנְפָנִים (flatterers), and מְסַפְּרֵי לָשׁוֹן הָרַע (slanderers) — all of whom share the trait of distorting or corrupting truth and relationship.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did God spare Micah from punishment despite his creation of a famous idol?
A. Because Micah eventually repented and destroyed the idol himself
B. Because Micah's idol was not technically worshipped as a deity
C. Because Micah made his bread available for travelers — the merit of hospitality protected him
D. Because the era of judges was not held to the same standard as the monarchy
Correct: C — God told the angels who sought to punish Micah: 'Leave him, as his bread is available for travelers.' The merit of offering hospitality — even from a sinner — was sufficient to protect him from immediate punishment.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara explain the blessing structure of Psalm 91?
A. The entire Psalm was composed by Moses for Joshua before entering Canaan
B. David blessed Solomon in verses 1–8, Bathsheba continued in 9–13, and God concluded in 14–16
C. The Psalm alternates between prophetic voice and priestly blessing throughout
D. Solomon composed the entire Psalm as a blessing for his own son Rehoboam
Correct: B — The Gemara derives from context that: David blessed his son Solomon in the opening verses (a father's blessings), Bathsheba continued with a mother's blessings, and finally God Himself speaks in the first person in the concluding verses — 'Because he has desired Me, I will deliver him.'
