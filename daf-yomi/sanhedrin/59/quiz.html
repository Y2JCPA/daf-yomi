Sanhedrin 59 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yochanan, what is the legal consequence for a gentile who studies Torah beyond the seven Noahide mitzvot?
A. Lashes
B. Monetary fine
C. Death penalty
D. Exile from the community
Correct: C — Rabbi Yochanan derives from 'an inheritance of the congregation of Jacob' (Deut. 33:4) that Torah is exclusively Israel's domain. A gentile who oversteps this boundary is liable to death.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara reconcile Rabbi Meir's statement that a gentile studying Torah is 'like a High Priest' with Rabbi Yochanan's ruling?
A. Rabbi Meir's view is rejected outright as a minority opinion
B. Rabbi Meir refers to a gentile studying the seven Noahide mitzvot applicable to them
C. Rabbi Meir speaks only of a gentile studying in Hebrew, not translation
D. Rabbi Meir refers to a gentile in the process of converting
Correct: B — The Gemara resolves the apparent contradiction: Rabbi Meir's praise applies to a gentile who studies the halakhot of their own seven mitzvot — that is meritorious. Rabbi Yochanan forbids study beyond that domain.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R. Yosei bar R. Chanina's principle, what is the status of a Noahide mitzva that was NOT repeated at Sinai?
A. It applies equally to Jews and non-Jews, since it predates Sinai
B. It is entirely abolished for everyone at Sinai
C. It now applies to Jews only — descendants of Noah are paradoxically exempt
D. It applies only to non-Jews, since Jews received a different command at Sinai
Correct: C — Counterintuitively, R. Yosei bar R. Chanina rules that when a Noahide mitzva was repeated at Sinai specifically for Israel, the descendants of Noah are actually exempt from it — the Sinai version supersedes and restricts.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rav teaches that eating meat was forbidden to Adam. At what point was meat first permitted?
A. After Adam and Eve were expelled from the Garden of Eden
B. After the Flood, when God spoke to Noah (Gen. 9:3)
C. At the time of the Covenant between the Parts with Abraham
D. At Sinai, with the laws of permitted and forbidden animals
Correct: B — Rav derives from Gen. 1:29 that Adam was given only herbs and fruit. It was only after the Flood that God told Noah: 'Every moving thing that lives shall be food for you' (Gen. 9:3) — the first permission to eat meat.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why, according to the Gemara, are the descendants of Esau NOT obligated in circumcision, even though their grandfather Isaac was circumcised?
A. Because Esau was wicked and forfeited his covenant rights by selling the birthright
B. Because circumcision was not given to Isaac personally, only to Abraham
C. The phrase 'be-Yitzchak' is interpreted as 'part of Isaac' — only the Jacob branch of Isaac's offspring
D. Because the covenant of circumcision expired after one generation
Correct: C — The Gemara reads 'For through Isaac [be-Yitzchak] offspring shall be called yours' (Gen. 21:12) as 'part of Isaac' — not all of Isaac's descendants, but specifically Jacob's line. Esau's descendants are excluded.
