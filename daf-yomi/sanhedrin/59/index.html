⚖️ Gentile Torah Study: Death Penalty or High Priestly Honor?

Rabbi Yochanan
A gentile who engages in Torah study is liable for the death penalty. The verse says Torah is 'an inheritance of the congregation of Jacob' — it belongs exclusively to Israel.
Rabbi Meir
A gentile who engages in Torah study is like a High Priest! The verse 'which if a man do, he shall live by them' says man — not Kohen, Levi, or Israelite specifically.
Gemara (Resolution)
No contradiction: Rabbi Meir praises a gentile studying the seven Noahide mitzvot applicable to them. Rabbi Yochanan forbids studying Torah beyond that portion. Each has a domain.
Key Verse תּוֹרָה צִוָּה לָנוּ מֹשֶׁה מוֹרָשָׁה קְהִלַּת יַעֲקֹב — 'Moses commanded us a law, an inheritance of the congregation of Jacob' (Devarim 33:4)

🩸 Blood from a Living Animal: Noahide Prohibition?

R. Chanina ben Gamliel
Descendants of Noah are also prohibited from consuming blood from a living animal. The verse says: 'Flesh with its life you shall not eat; blood with its life you shall not eat' — two separate clauses, two prohibitions.
Rabbis (majority)
The phrase 'blood with its life' refers to blood that spurts at slaughter (dam ha-nefesh) — not a separate prohibition on blood from a living animal. There is only one Noahide blood prohibition.
Gemara
Even according to R. Chanina, blood from a living animal is also separately forbidden to Jews, derived from: 'Only be steadfast in not eating blood, as the blood is the life' (Devarim 12:23).
Disputed Verse בְּשָׂרוֹ בְנַפְשׁוֹ לֹא תֹאכֵלוּ — 'Flesh with its life you shall not eat' (Bereishit 9:4)

🔁 R. Yosei bar R. Chanina's Sinai Repetition Principle

A mitzva commanded to descendants of Noah
↓
✅ Repeated at Sinai
Stated for both groups — Jews AND non-Jews remain obligated
e.g., murder, theft, idol worship
❌ NOT repeated at Sinai
Stated at Sinai for Jews only — descendants of Noah are now exempt
e.g., Gid HaNasheh (R. Yehuda)
↓
Why repeat if already obligated? The Torah repeated circumcision for Shabbat override, and procreation to teach that a Sanhedrin decree requires a new vote to rescind — not for the mitzva itself

✂️ Circumcision: Which Descendants of Abraham Are Obligated?

Obligated

  • Jews — descendants of Jacob (Isaac's son)
  • Descendants of Keturah (Abraham's second wife) — per R. Yosei bar Avin / R. Yosei bar Chanina, since they are numerous and intermingled with obligated nations

Not Obligated

  • Descendants of Ishmael — 'through Isaac shall offspring be called yours' (Gen. 21:12) excludes Ishmael's line
  • Descendants of Esau — 'be-Yitzchak' interpreted as 'part of Isaac,' meaning only Jacob's line, not all of Isaac's children

🥩 The History of Permitted Meat: From Adam to Sinai

Adam in the Garden — Meat was forbidden. God said: 'Behold I have given you every herb that brings forth seed… and every tree' (Gen. 1:29). Only vegetation permitted.
↓
After the Flood — Noah's Generation — Meat first permitted: 'Every moving thing that lives shall be food for you; as the green herb I have given you all' (Gen. 9:3).
↓
Exception: Creeping animals (Sheratzim) — Still forbidden even after Noah. Derived from 'its blood' — only animals with blood that gushes in slaughter; sheratzim have no such blood.
↓
Aggadah: Adam dined in Eden — R. Yehuda ben Teima says ministering angels roasted meat and filtered wine for Adam. Resolved: miraculous heavenly meat descended, as in the story of R. Shimon ben Chalafta and the lions.
↓
At Sinai — Full system of kashrut established for Israel: permitted animals, slaughter requirements, and separation of milk and meat.
