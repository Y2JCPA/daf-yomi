Sanhedrin 19 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why are kings of Israel exempt from being judged by the Sanhedrin?
A. It is a biblical law derived from Deuteronomy
B. It arose from an incident: Yannai's slave killed someone and the court was paralyzed by fear
C. Kings are inherently above all law in Torah
D. Rav Yosef ruled that no human authority may judge a king
Correct: B — The Gemara traces the ruling to a specific incident: Yannai's slave killed a person, Shimon ben Shataḥ tried to compel Yannai to stand trial, but the judges were too afraid to act. Their death at Gabriel's hand prompted the enactment that kings of Israel are neither judged nor do they judge.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Yosef, to which kings does the halakha 'does not judge and is not judged' apply?
A. All kings of Israel including Davidic kings
B. Only the kings of the House of David
C. Only the kings of Israel (not of the Davidic dynasty)
D. Only kings who personally committed crimes
Correct: C — Rav Yosef explicitly limits the halakha to kings of Israel, who were violent and disobedient of Torah law. Kings of the House of David, who submitted to Torah authority, do judge and are judged.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did the Sages rabbinically prohibit even the first act of levirate marriage (yibum) for the High Priest?
A. A widow from betrothal is as forbidden as a divorcée — the mitzva cannot override it
B. The positive mitzva of yibum does not override a combined prohibition and positive mitzva
C. Concern that a first act will lead to a second act, which is not protected by the mitzva
D. The High Priest has no levirate obligations whatsoever under any circumstances
Correct: C — The Gemara explains: although the first act of yibum would fulfill the positive mitzva and technically override the prohibition, the Sages decreed it forbidden out of concern that he would proceed to a second act — which would not be covered by the mitzva and would be a full Torah violation.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What change did Rabbi Yosei institute in Tzippori regarding mourning consolation?
A. He ruled that 10 people minimum are needed before any consolation may be offered
B. He abolished public consolation lines entirely as undignified
C. He restored the original custom: mourners stand, all the people pass by to console them
D. He instituted that women may not speak during consolation processions
Correct: C — Rami bar Abba reports that Rabbi Yosei returned Tzippori's practice to the ancient original: mourners stand in place and the people pass by them one by one to offer consolation — reversing the later reversed custom.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does the Talmud derive from 'These are the generations of Aaron and Moses' (Num. 3:1) when only Aaron's sons are then listed?
A. That Moses had no biological children who survived
B. That Aaron's priestly line supersedes Moses's prophetic line
C. That whoever teaches Torah to another's child is credited as if he himself sired that child
D. That the genealogy of the Levites traces only through Aaron, not Moses
Correct: C — Rabbi Shmuel bar Naḥmani citing Rabbi Yonatan: the verse attributes 'generations' to Moses yet lists Aaron's sons — because Moses taught them Torah. This teaches that teaching another's child Torah earns the teacher scriptural credit as a parent.
