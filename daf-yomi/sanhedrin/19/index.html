⚖️ Yannai's Slave on Trial

Shimon ben Shataḥ
Yannai the king — stand on your feet! Witnesses will testify against you. You stand before God, not before us!
Yannai the King
I will not stand unless the entire Sanhedrin tells me to do so.
Fellow Judges
[They turned their faces to the ground and said nothing — paralyzed by fear of the king]
Shimon ben Shataḥ
You are masters of thought — but the Master of thought will punish you for this silence!
Consequence The Angel Gabriel struck the judges to the ground and they died. From then on it was instituted: a king of Israel does not judge and is not judged.

👑 Which Kings Are Subject to Judgment?

Kings of Israel

  • Do NOT judge others in court
  • Are NOT judged by the Sanhedrin
  • Reason: violent and disobedient of Torah law
  • Yannai's slave killed someone — court was powerless
  • Enactment arose from practical necessity, not principle

Kings of House of David

  • DO judge others
  • ARE subject to judgment by the court
  • Reason: they submitted to Torah authority
  • Rav Yosef: rule of non-judgment applies only to Israel's kings
  • Davidic kings remain within the halakhic system

💍 David, Merab & Michal — The Betrothal Saga

Saul promises Merab to David: "Slay Goliath and she is yours." David slays Goliath.
↓
David's words — "Who am I and what is my life?" — imply he did not fully accept the betrothal. Rabbi Yehoshua ben Korḥa: this was a mistaken betrothal.
↓
Saul gives Merab to Adriel. He tells David: bring me 100 Philistine foreskins as bride-price instead.
↓
Halakhic dispute: Saul says foreskins are worthless → betrothal via them is invalid. David says they have value to Saul → betrothal is valid!
↓
David marries Michal (Merab's sister). After Merab dies, David demands Michal back: "Deliver me my wife Michal" (II Sam. 3:14).
↓
Palti ben Laish — Michal's interim guardian — planted a sword between them and never touched her. His righteousness exceeded even Joseph's.

📚 Teaching Torah = Spiritual Parenthood

כָּל הַמְלַמֵּד בֶּן חֲבֵרוֹ תּוֹרָה — מַעֲלֶה עָלָיו הַכָּתוּב כְּאִלּוּ יְלָדוֹ
Principle Rabbi Shmuel bar Naḥmani citing Rabbi Yonatan: Anyone who teaches Torah to another's child is credited by Scripture as if he sired that child. Proof text: "These are the generations of Aaron and Moses" (Num. 3:1) — yet only Aaron's sons are listed. Because Moses taught them Torah, they are called his generations too. Corroborating cases: Boaz raised Oved (credited as parent), Miriam raised Moses (credited), Bithia daughter of Pharaoh saved Moses (credited). Whoever raises a child = as if they bore that child.
