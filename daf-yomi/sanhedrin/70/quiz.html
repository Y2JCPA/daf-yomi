Sanhedrin 70 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yosei, what is the minimum amount of meat a stubborn and rebellious son must eat to become liable?
A. Tarteimar
B. Half-log
C. Maneh
D. A se'ah
Correct: C — Rabbi Yosei doubles the unattributed mishna's amounts: maneh of meat (instead of tarteimar) and a full log of wine (instead of half-log). Rabbi Zeira infers this by noting Yosei doubled the wine measure.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why is a stubborn and rebellious son exempt if he eats heavily salted meat or fermenting press-wine?
A. These foods are forbidden by the Torah regardless
B. They are not eaten in a normal pleasurable way — the law targets typical gluttonous enjoyment
C. These foods belong to a priestly category and are always exempt
D. Salted meat and press-wine are considered mitzva items
Correct: B — Rabba and Rav Yosef teach that the law targets normal pleasurable gluttony. Heavily salted meat and unfermented wine are not consumed in the typical enjoyable manner, so they do not reflect the conduct the Torah penalizes.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
For what purpose does Rav Ḥanan say wine was created?
A. To gladden the hearts of the righteous in this world
B. To be used exclusively in Temple libation offerings
C. To comfort mourners and to reward the wicked in this world, so they have no reward left in the World-to-Come
D. To mark Shabbat and the holidays with joy
Correct: C — Rav Ḥanan teaches that wine's dual purpose is comforting mourners in their grief and serving as the reward for the wicked in this world — exhausting their share before the World-to-Come.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the opinion that Ham castrated Noah, why specifically was Canaan (Ham's fourth son) cursed?
A. Canaan was the eldest son and bore responsibility
B. Canaan personally participated in mocking Noah
C. Ham deprived Noah of a fourth son, so measure-for-measure Noah cursed Ham's fourth son
D. Canaan was cursed because he inherited Ham's worst traits
Correct: C — The measure-for-measure logic: Ham prevented Noah from fathering a fourth son. Therefore Noah's curse fell specifically on Ham's fourth son, Canaan — a precise recompense for the specific harm inflicted.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does R. Yoḥanan (in the name of R. Shimon b. Yoḥai) learn from the phrase 'son of my vows' in Proverbs 31:2?
A. Bat Sheva vowed to dedicate Solomon entirely to Temple service
B. Other women vowed for a son to be king; Bat Sheva vowed for a son fit to lead a great nation — a higher moral standard
C. Bat Sheva vowed never to drink wine while pregnant with Solomon
D. Bat Sheva vowed that Solomon would defeat all of Israel's enemies
Correct: B — R. Yoḥanan teaches that while other women merely prayed for sons worthy of kingship, Bat Sheva set a higher bar — she vowed for a son of extraordinary wisdom and character fit to guide an entire people.
