⚖️ Ben Sorer U'Moreh: Path to Liability

Son eats tarteimar of meat + drinks ½ log Italian wine
(R. Yosei: maneh meat + full log wine)
↓
⛔ Exempt
Mitzva meal, intercalation feast, second tithe in Jerusalem, non-meat food, non-wine drink, expensive or abnormal food
⚠️ Liable
Cheap (bezol) meat + cheap wine, bad company, normal gluttonous pleasure, outside all sacred contexts
↓
Core principle: 'He will not obey our voice' — must be pure willful rebellion, not sin in service of mitzva or transgression

🌿 What Was the Tree of Knowledge?

Rabbi Meir
The Tree was a grapevine 🍇 — even today, nothing but wine brings wailing and trouble upon a person. Most sins are caused by drunkenness.
Rabbi Yehuda
The Tree was wheat 🌾 — a child cannot say 'Mama' or 'Abba' until it has tasted grain. This proves grain unlocked Adam's knowledge.
Rabbi Nehemya
The Tree was a fig tree 🌿 — the proof is from the fruit itself: Adam and Eve immediately clothed themselves in fig leaves after they ate.
Shared Logic Each opinion proves itself from the fruit's ongoing effect in the world — each Sage sees the Tree of Knowledge in the thing that most defines human moral struggle in his own experience.

🚢 What Did Ham Do to Noah? Two Grave Opinions

Rav
Ham castrated Noah — Proof: Noah wanted a fourth son but was now denied that possibility. Therefore he cursed Ham's fourth son (Canaan) — measure for measure. Also: 'brother of Canaan' implies Canaan participated.
Shmuel
Ham sodomized Noah — Proof: 'He saw his father's nakedness' parallels the language used for Shechem's act against Dinah. The sexual language points to this violation.
Shared Conclusion Both opinions agree Ham committed an unspeakable violation. The curse on Canaan (Ham's fourth son) enacts divine measure-for-measure, whether for theft of a future son or for sexual desecration.

🍷 Wine in Rabbinic Thought: Peril and Promise

Wine as Peril ⚠️

  • Created only to comfort mourners and reward the wicked in this world (Rav Ḥanan)
  • 'Look not upon wine when it is red' — will whiten (shame) the faces of the wicked in the World-to-Come (R. Yitzḥak)
  • 13 vav-conversives in Noah's vineyard story = 13 warnings of wine's downward pull
  • Noah: planted → drank → uncovered → Ham sinned → curse on Canaan
  • R. Meir: Grapevine was the Tree of Knowledge — wine is the source of all sin

Wine as Promise ✨

  • 'Wine gladdens the heart of man' — yesamaḥ, not yeshamaḥ (Psalms 104:15)
  • Promised in Israel's redemption: 'flow to the bounty... for wheat, wine, and oil' (Jeremiah 31:12)
  • Given to the poor and downtrodden to forget their misery (Proverbs 31:6–7)
  • Sacred use: Shabbat kiddush, Temple libations, holiday celebration
  • Rava: The contradiction resolves — wine can bring joy OR destruction depending on the drinker

👑 Bat Sheva Rebukes Solomon

דִּבְרֵי לְמוּאֵל מֶלֶךְ מַשָּׂא אֲשֶׁר יִסְּרַתּוּ אִמּוֹ (משלי לא:א)
Proverbs 31:1–4 — R. Yoḥanan in the name of R. Shimon b. Yoḥai 'Son of my womb' — I pressed into the king's chamber while pregnant so that you might be born worthy of kingship; all other wives conceive and never see the king again.

'Son of my vows' — Other women vowed: Let me have a son who is king. But I vowed: Let me have a son fit for a great nation — a higher standard of wisdom and justice.

'It is not for kings to drink wine' (v. 4) — One who has the world's poor crying to him must not cloud his judgment with wine. But let wine be given to the bitter of soul to help them forget their misery (v. 6–7).

Solomon repented: 'I am more foolish than a man' (Proverbs 30:2) — an admission that he had failed to heed his mother's rebuke about wine and excess.
