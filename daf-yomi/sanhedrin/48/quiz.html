QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the mnemonic given in the Gemara for the series of proofs in the Rava-Abaye designation debate?
A. Scarf, Monument, Hewn grave, Pouch, Craftsman
B. Shroud, Coffin, Stone, Scroll, Talit
C. King, Court, Prophet, Sage, Elder
D. Fire, Water, Earth, Wind, Spirit
Correct: A — The Gemara explicitly gives a mnemonic: Scarf (סודר) · Monument (נצב) · That was hewed (חצב) · Pouch (תרמיל) · Craftsman (אומן) — to remember the five proofs.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Abaye, when does a cloth pouch designated for tefillin become forbidden for other uses?
A. Only after tefillin are actually placed inside it
B. Only after a formal verbal declaration before witnesses
C. From the moment of designation, even before any use
D. Never — pouches have no inherent sanctity
Correct: C — Abaye holds yichud (designation) is significant — the pouch is forbidden for money immediately upon designation, even before tefillin are ever placed inside.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which descendant of Solomon fulfilled David's curse that Joab's house would produce a 'leper'?
A. Rehoboam
B. Asa
C. Josiah
D. Uzziah
Correct: D — Uzziah was struck with leprosy as punishment for arrogantly entering the Temple to burn incense, a role reserved for priests (II Chronicles 26:19).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the majority opinion regarding the property of someone executed by the king?
A. Property goes to the Temple treasury
B. Property belongs to the king
C. Property belongs to the heirs of the executed
D. Property is divided between the king and the court
Correct: B — The majority holds that property of those executed by the king for crimes against him belongs to the king — evidenced by Ahab seizing Naboth's vineyard after Jezebel arranged his execution.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rav Ḥisda teach about a monument that was partially built before death and completed after?
A. The entire monument is forbidden once any portion is built in honor of the deceased
B. Only the row of stones added after death is forbidden; the original structure remains permitted
C. The monument must be dismantled within 30 days of burial
D. The monument is fully permitted as long as construction began during the person's lifetime
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda rules that if the corpse is removed, one may remove the row of stones added post-mortem and use them — only that added portion is forbidden, not the pre-existing structure.
