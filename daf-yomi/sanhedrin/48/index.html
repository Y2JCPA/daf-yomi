⚖️ Does Mere Designation Render Items Forbidden?

Abaye
Designation (yichud) IS significant — dedicating an item to the dead or sacred use renders it forbidden even without any subsequent action.
Rava
Designation alone is NOTHING — only an actual deed (ma'aseh) creates a prohibition. Mere intention or verbal declaration is insufficient.
Mnemonic for Proofs סודר • נצב • חצב • תרמיל • אומן — Scarf · Monument · Hewn Grave · Pouch · Craftsman

🧣 The Five Mnemonic Proofs

Central Question: Is yichud alone sufficient to forbid use of an object?
↓
Against Rava (supports Abaye)
Scarf: designated as shroud → forbidden before actual use

Pouch: designated for tefillin → forbidden to store money
For Rava (against Abaye)
Hewn Grave: father buried elsewhere → requires actual burial to forbid

Craftsman: Torah case not forbidden until scroll actually placed inside
↓
Monument proof is inconclusive — reinterpreted by both sides
↓
Resolution: Dispute between Tanna'im. Mareimar rules halakha follows Abaye.

🪦 Monument Built for the Living, Then the Dead

Baraita
A monument built as memorial for a living person — permitted. Once he dies and a new row of stones is added in his honor — those added stones are forbidden for benefit.
Rav Ḥisda
If the corpse is later removed from the grave, one may remove the added row of stones and use the rest — only the designated addition is forbidden, not the original structure.
Key Principle Only the portion added in the dead person's honor is forbidden — the pre-existing monument retains its permitted status.

👑 Property of Executed Persons

Executed by the KING

  • Property belongs to the king
  • Proof: Jezebel arranged false testimony — 'Naboth cursed the king' — so Ahab could execute and seize his vineyard
  • Rabbi Yehuda disagrees: Ahab was Naboth's cousin and inherited legitimately
  • Majority: King executes specifically to acquire the property

Executed by the COURT

  • Property belongs to the heirs
  • Proof: Witnesses also testified 'Naboth cursed God' — yet property was seized separately via king's portion
  • Court execution does not entail forfeiture of estate
  • Joab fled to the Sanctuary — property rights were also at stake in his case

📿 Phylactery Pouch — From Designation to Prohibition

Stage 1: Person designates (מייחד) a plain pouch to hold tefillin
↓
Abaye's view: Pouch is immediately forbidden for money — designation alone takes effect
↓
Rava's view: Pouch remains permitted for other use — until tefillin are actually placed inside, no prohibition applies
↓
Craftsman case supports Rava: A Torah scroll case commissioned from a craftsman is NOT forbidden until the scroll is actually placed inside it
↓
Halakhic ruling: Mareimar — halakha follows Abaye. The Rabbis disagree. Final word: halakha follows Abaye.
