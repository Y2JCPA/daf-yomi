📖 Interpreting 'His Father and His Mother' — Genesis 2:24

Rabbi Eliezer
'His father' = his father's sister. 'His mother' = his mother's sister. We interpret each term symmetrically — the common ground is sisterhood.
Rabbi Akiva
'His father' = his father's wife. She is literally called 'his father's nakedness' in the Torah — the most natural referent.
Rabbi Eliezer
But 'father's wife' is already derived from 'to his wife, not to the wife of another man.' The term 'his father' must teach something new — his father's sister!
Rabbi Akiva
And 'his mother' means his actual mother — that is itself a novel Noahide teaching. The verse must explicitly forbid intercourse with one's own mother for all mankind.
The Verse "Therefore a man shall leave his father and his mother, and shall cleave to his wife, and they shall be one flesh" (Genesis 2:24) — each phrase forbids a specific category of forbidden relations for descendants of Noah.

🔄 The Convert's Family Status

✅ Maternal Ties — Preserved by Rabbinic Decree

  • Must divorce his maternal half-sister
  • Must divorce his mother's maternal half-sister
  • R. Meir: must also divorce mother's paternal half-sister
  • Rabbis: may keep mother's paternal half-sister
  • Rationale: avoid treating conversion as license for incest

❌ Paternal Ties — Severed by Conversion (Rebirth)

  • May marry his brother's wife
  • May marry his father's brother's wife
  • May marry paternal half-sister converted before him
  • All paternal-line relations dissolved — he is legally reborn
  • Torah law treats him as a new person with no prior family

📜 Historical Proofs — Amram, Abraham, and Adam

Pro-Akiva (Proof 1)
Amram married Jochebed, 'his aunt' (Exodus 6:20). If maternal aunts were forbidden to Noahides, this was prohibited. So Akiva must be right — only paternal relations are the concern.
Rejection
No — Jochebed was Kohath's paternal half-sister only. A paternal-only aunt is not forbidden even according to R. Eliezer. No proof here.
Pro-Eliezer (Proof 2)
Abraham said of Sarah: 'She is my sister, the daughter of my father, but not of my mother' (Gen 20:12). A paternal-only half-sister was permitted — supporting Akiva's view.
Rejection
Sarah was actually Haran's daughter Iscah — Abraham's niece, not sister. Abraham spoke metaphorically. No proof can be drawn from here.
Stalemate Neither historical proof — from Amram, Abraham, nor Adam's arrangement for Cain — resolves the dispute. The debate between R. Eliezer and R. Akiva stands on its own logic.

Ze'eiri / R. Ḥanina

📅 The Gentile Who Keeps Shabbat

וְיוֹם וָלַיְלָה לֹא יִשְׁבֹּתוּ (בראשית ח:כג)
Reish Lakish — Counterintuitive Ruling A gentile who observed Shabbat is liable to receive the death penalty.

The verse states: "And day and night shall not cease" (Genesis 8:23) — literally: they shall not rest. This is the Noahide covenant: the world continues because work never fully stops.

Why isn't this listed among the Seven Noahide Laws? The Tanna counts only prohibitions requiring one to sit and refrain from a single act. Shabbat involves sustained, prolonged cessation — qualitatively different from the other six.

This ruling targets Shabbat as a Jewish institution, not rest per se. A gentile who rests without taking on Shabbat is unaffected.
