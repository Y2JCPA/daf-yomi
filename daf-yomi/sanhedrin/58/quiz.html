Sanhedrin 58 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Akiva, what does 'his father' in Genesis 2:24 prohibit for a Noahide?
A. Homosexual intercourse with one's father
B. Relations with one's father's wife
C. Relations with one's father's sister
D. Relations with one's father's brother's wife
Correct: B — Rabbi Akiva holds that 'his father' refers to his father's wife, who is elsewhere called 'his father's nakedness' — making her the most natural referent. Rabbi Eliezer (not Akiva) holds the term refers to the father's sister.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
A male convert has a maternal half-sister who converted before him. What is the halacha if he married her?
A. The marriage is fully valid — both are considered unrelated after conversion
B. He must divorce her, though by Torah law they are technically unrelated
C. He is forbidden by full Torah law and receives lashes
D. R. Meir permits it; the Rabbis forbid it
Correct: B — By Torah law, a convert is reborn and has no prior family relations, so they would be technically unrelated. However, the Rabbis decreed that a convert retains maternal kinship to prevent abuse of conversion, so he must divorce a maternal half-sister.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why isn't the prohibition against a gentile observing Shabbat counted among the Seven Noahide Laws?
A. It is a positive commandment, not a prohibition
B. It applies only in the Land of Israel
C. The Tanna counts only prohibitions requiring a single act of restraint; Shabbat involves prolonged cessation
D. The prohibition was added only after the Revelation at Sinai
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that the Tanna enumerates only mitzvot that require one to 'sit and refrain' from a discrete act. Shabbat involves a sustained, ongoing cessation of work — qualitatively different from the other six Noahide laws.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Rav Huna rule regarding one who habitually raises his hand to strike others?
A. He should be called wicked publicly and shamed
B. He should be imprisoned until he repents
C. His hand should be cut off
D. There is no remedy except burial
Correct: C — Rav Huna derives from Job 38:15 — 'the high arm shall be broken' — that the raised arm of a violent person should literally be cut off. The Gemara records that Rav Huna actually enforced this. Rabbi Elazar holds the even harsher view that burial is the only remedy.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rav Ḥisda rules that a Canaanite slave may marry his own mother. What is his reasoning?
A. Slaves have no halachic personhood, so no prohibition applies to them
B. He left the Noahide category through immersion for slavery, so Noahide prohibitions no longer bind him
C. The prohibition against marrying one's mother applies only to born Jews
D. Canaanite slaves were exempt from all Torah law as classified property
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda reasons that immersion in a mikveh for the purpose of becoming a Jewish-owned slave removes the person from the category of Noahide. Since he has not become fully Jewish either, the Noahide prohibition against marrying one's mother no longer applies to him.
