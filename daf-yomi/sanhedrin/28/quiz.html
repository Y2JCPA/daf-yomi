Sanhedrin 28 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
From what specific feature of Deuteronomy 24:16 does the Gemara derive that a brother's children cannot testify about the children of the other brother?
A. The word 'fathers' appears twice in the verse
B. The verse uses 'children' in the plural rather than singular
C. The verse appears in the context of capital punishment only
D. The verse is repeated identically in the book of Kings
Correct: B — If only the direct parent-child relationship were meant, the verse could have said 'child' (singular). The plural 'children' teaches that the children of one brother cannot testify about the children of the other brother.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rava reject Rami bar Ḥama's logical argument for disqualifying brothers' children from testifying together?
A. Logical reasoning can never override a biblical verse in testimony law
B. The three-brothers land-occupation case shows the logic produces an impossible result
C. The Sanhedrin requires 23 judges before such a ruling can be issued
D. Conspiring witnesses are governed by a separate tractate with different rules
Correct: B — Rava shows that if three brothers each occupied land one year in sequence, each year would count as separate ownership proof — yet they obviously cannot testify together. This contradicts Rami's logical framework, showing a verse-based derivation is necessary.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara extend testimony disqualification rules from capital law to monetary law?
A. From the repetition of 'fathers' in Deuteronomy 24:16
B. From a tradition passed down at Sinai (halakha l'Moshe miSinai)
C. From Leviticus 24:22: 'You shall have one manner of law'
D. From the principle that capital law is always stricter than monetary law
Correct: C — Leviticus 24:22 states 'one manner of law for you,' interpreted to mean that monetary and capital law follow the same guidelines regarding testimony disqualification.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rav Ḥisda's ruling regarding the father of the groom and the father of the bride testifying about each other?
A. They are disqualified because they are connected through their children's marriage
B. They are fit to testify — they are only like a 'lid on a barrel,' not truly related
C. They are disqualified only for monetary cases, permitted for capital cases
D. They may testify only if the married couple has already had children together
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda rules they are fit to testify. Their connection via the married couple makes them like a lid resting on a barrel — adjacent but not truly part of the family unit, so not halakhically 'relatives.'
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the final resolution in the case of the deed of gift signed by two brothers-in-law?
A. The deed is declared valid because Shmuel explicitly ruled like Rabbi Yosei of the mishna
B. The deed is torn up because both witnesses are definitively disqualified
C. The recipient is told to acquire the gift via witnesses who observed the physical act of transfer
D. The case is sent to the Exilarch for a final authoritative ruling
Correct: C — Because Abaye's challenge — whether Shmuel meant Rabbi Yosei of the mishna or the baraita — cannot be resolved, Rav Yosef cannot validate the deed. He instructs the recipient to rely instead on witnesses who personally observed the kinyan (act of acquisition).
