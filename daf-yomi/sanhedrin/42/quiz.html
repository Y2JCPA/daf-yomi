Sanhedrin 42 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan (as transmitted by Rabbi Asi via Rabbi Aḥa bar Ḥanina), what is the spiritual significance of blessing the new moon in its proper time?
A. It atones for the sins of the entire community for that month
B. It is as if one greets the Face of the Divine Presence
C. It fulfills the biblical obligation of sanctifying the new month
D. It is equivalent to bringing a peace-offering in the Temple
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan teaches that blessing the new month in its time is like greeting the Shechina, based on a verbal parallel (the root ל-ק-ר-א) between the verse about the moon and the verse about encountering God.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Ravina reject Rav Aḥa of Difti's suggestion to recite HaTov veHaMeitiv over the moon's light?
A. The moon's light is too faint to constitute a real benefit
B. HaTov veHaMeitiv is reserved for physical goods like wine and oil
C. HaTov veHaMeitiv requires two people to jointly receive a new shared benefit — the moon benefits everyone generally, not as a specific shared new good
D. The blessing for the moon was already fixed by the Men of the Great Assembly
Correct: C — HaTov veHaMeitiv applies when two parties jointly benefit from a specific new good together. The moon shines for all universally, so the condition of joint shared benefit is not met in the relevant sense.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In a capital case, what happens if the full 71-judge Sanhedrin HaGadol reaches a deadlock of 36 to 35?
A. The majority of 36 convicts the defendant
B. The Nasi casts the deciding vote to break the tie
C. The case is postponed until the next day when fresh deliberation may yield a majority
D. The defendant is released — no larger court exists to break the deadlock
Correct: D — Once the full Sanhedrin of 71 cannot reach a majority, the defendant must be released. There is no higher court to appeal to, and conviction requires an actual majority.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Through what legal mechanism does the Gemara prove that the place of stoning must be outside all three camps (Priestly, Levite, and Israelite)?
A. A direct biblical verse states 'outside all three camps' explicitly
B. A gezerah shavah links the blasphemer's execution (hotze) to the burning of sin-offering bulls, which required going outside all three camps
C. The Talmud derives it from the principle that disgrace must be maximized for capital offenders
D. Rav Pappa's calculation based on Moses's position is the sole source
Correct: B — The Gemara uses a gezerah shavah (verbal analogy) comparing the 'taking outside' language for the blasphemer to the bulls brought as sin-offerings, which were burned outside all three camps, thereby establishing the same rule for the place of stoning.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The phrase 'the judgment has grown aged' (nivla hadin) is used in monetary cases but NOT capital cases. What does the Gemara conclude this phrase actually signals?
A. The defendant has waited too long and must be released due to delay
B. The court announces it is unable to reach a verdict — a declaration of deadlock
C. The case has been resolved and the verdict has been finalized
D. The witnesses' testimony has become stale and must be re-examined
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that 'the judgment has grown aged' is an announcement by the greatest judge that the court cannot reach a verdict — a declaration of deadlock. In capital cases this phrase is not used; instead, judges are added progressively up to 71.
