✍️ The Ashurit Script Debate

R' Yehuda HaNasi
Initially the Torah was given in Ashurit. After Israel sinned it became ro'atz (impaired). When they repented under Ezra, Ashurit was restored.
R' Shimon ben Elazar
The script never changed at all! The Tabernacle's vavim (hooks) prove the letter-shapes remained identical from the very beginning.
R' Yehuda HaNasi
Then why is it called Ashurit? Because it is me'usheret — beautiful and straight in its form.
Baraita
It is called Ashurit because it ascended with Israel from Ashur (Babylonia) when they returned from exile with Ezra.
Key Term Hannishtevan (Ezra 4:7) — similar to nishtana (changed), hinting that the script had been altered during the exile period

🔮 Writing on the Wall — How Was It Unreadable?

Mysterious writing appears at Belshazzar's feast
↓
The wise men could not read the writing (Daniel 5:8)
↓
Rav's Explanation
Written in gimatriyya (numerical substitution code): aleph-tav-bet-shin cipher scrambled the letters beyond recognition
Shmuel's Explanation
Correct letters used, but written vertically (top to bottom) rather than horizontally — unreadable in normal order
↓
Daniel deciphers: MENE — God numbered your kingdom and ended it | TEKEL — You are weighed and found wanting | UFARSIN — Your kingdom is divided to Medes and Persians

👑 Abishag: Permitted to Solomon, Forbidden to Adonijah

R' Yoḥanan
Abishag was permitted to Solomon — he was king, and a king may use the privilege (scepter) of another king.
R' Yoḥanan
Abishag was forbidden to Adonijah — he was not king; claiming the king's attendant is tantamount to claiming the throne.
Abishag
'When the thief lacks what to steal, he makes himself a man of peace' — David cannot engage in relations, so he invents an excuse not to marry me.
King David
Responds: 'I am still king!' — immediately summons Solomon to demonstrate his royal authority is fully intact.
Rav Shemen bar Abba How severe is divorce — they permitted David to be secluded with Abishag unmarried, yet would NOT permit him to divorce one of his wives to marry her

💒 Teachings on Marriage, Divorce & Loss

Divorce

  • R' Eliezer: The altar sheds tears for one who divorces his first wife
  • God 'no longer shows regard' to such a man's offerings (Malachi 2:13)
  • So severe that David was permitted seclusion with Abishag rather than permit him to divorce

Death of a Wife

  • R' Yoḥanan: A wife dies when creditors press a man who cannot pay his debts
  • R' Yoḥanan: Loss of first wife = Temple being destroyed in his days
  • R' Alexandri: The world grows dark — 'his lamp shall be put out' (Job 18:6)

Finding a Match

  • Rabba bar bar Ḥana: Matching couples is as hard as splitting the Red Sea
  • Yet: 40 days before formation, a Divine Voice declares the destined match
  • R' Shmuel bar Naḥman: No substitute exists for the wife of one's youth

👑 Mishna: Laws of Royal Dignity

No riding the king's horse — his personal mount is reserved exclusively for him, even after death
↓
No sitting on the king's throne — the throne is a symbol of royal authority, forbidden to all non-royals
↓
No using the king's scepter — royal regalia may not be wielded by those who are not king; key to the Abishag ruling
↓
No seeing the king while having his hair cut, while naked, or in the bathhouse — based on 'You shall surely set a king over you' (Deut. 17:15): treat him with total awe
↓
Consequence: When Adonijah asked for Abishag, Solomon interpreted it as seizing the king's scepter — a capital offense — and had him executed
