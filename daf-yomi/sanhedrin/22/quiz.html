Sanhedrin 22 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, why is the Hebrew script called 'Ashurit'?
A. Because it ascended with Israel from Ashur (Babylonia) during the return from exile
B. Because it is me'usheret — beautiful and straight in its script
C. Because Ashur was the first nation to adopt this writing system
D. Because it was revealed to Moses at Mount Sinai in Assyria
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi holds the script never changed, so he cannot attribute the name to the Babylonian exile. Instead he derives Ashurit from me'usheret — meaning beautiful and upright. The other baraita (which he disputes) says the name comes from ascending from Ashur.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Shmuel, why could Belshazzar's wise men not read the writing on the wall?
A. The letters were encoded in a gimatriyya (numerical substitution) cipher
B. The four words were written vertically, top to bottom, not in normal horizontal order
C. The script used an unknown ancient Aramaic variant
D. The letters were written right-to-left instead of the standard direction
Correct: B — Shmuel explains that the correct letters for mene mene tekel ufarsin were used, but the words were arranged vertically rather than horizontally. Reading across rather than down made the text incomprehensible. Rav, by contrast, holds a gimatriyya code was used.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why was Abishag permitted to Solomon but forbidden to Adonijah?
A. Solomon was David's designated heir and therefore had special privileges
B. Solomon was a king, and a king may use the privilege (scepter) of another king
C. Abishag herself chose Solomon over Adonijah
D. Adonijah had already taken multiple wives and was disqualified
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan teaches that because Solomon was king, he could use the scepter — the royal prerogative — of a fellow king (David). Adonijah was not king; claiming the king's intimate attendant was tantamount to seizing the throne, a capital offense.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
To what catastrophe does Rabbi Yoḥanan compare the death of a man's first wife?
A. The splitting of the Red Sea — a world-altering upheaval
B. The destruction of the Holy Temple
C. The Babylonian exile and loss of the Land
D. The death of one's parents
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan says: 'For any man whose first wife dies, it is as if the Temple were destroyed in his days' — both the Temple and a first wife represent irreplaceable cornerstones of one's world, cited from Ezekiel 24.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How often must an ordinary (non-High) priest have his hair cut, and from where is this period derived?
A. Every day, from 'Your eyes shall see the king in his beauty' (Isaiah 33:17)
B. Every Shabbat eve (weekly), from the laws governing the turban
C. Every thirty days, derived from the nazirite's minimum term via the word yihye
D. Once every three months, by rabbinic decree after the Temple's destruction
Correct: C — The Gemara derives from Ezekiel 44:20 ('they shall only trim their heads') that priests cut their hair on a nazirite-like schedule. The minimum nazirite term of 30 days comes from the numerical value of yihye (יִהְיֶה = 30) in Numbers 6:5.
