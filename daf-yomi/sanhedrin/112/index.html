⚔️ Why Must Animal Offerings from the Idolatrous City Die?

Rabbi Yoḥanan
They die because "The offering of the wicked is an abomination" (Proverbs 21:27) — they are intrinsically unfit for the altar once owned by idol worshippers.
Reish Lakish
They die because the animal is the property of its owner — and all property of idolatrous city residents must be destroyed. The animal itself did nothing wrong.
Gemara
Reish Lakish cannot accept Rabbi Yoḥanan's verse: "offering of the wicked" applies after the owner sinned — the animal sinned nothing. Therefore: ownership alone condemns it.
Gemara
Rabbi Yoḥanan cannot accept Reish Lakish's reason: if ownership were the issue, a firstborn or animal tithe (which belong to Heaven) should also die — yet Rabbi Shimon exempts them!
Conclusion Each Amora's reasoning is internally consistent but mutually exclusive. Neither can adopt the other's rationale without contradicting their own position.

💰 Fate of the Righteous Person's Property

Righteous person resides in the idolatrous city
↓
Property inside city
Destroyed along with all other city property — no exception for the righteous
Property outside city
Spared — the verse condemns only what is within its borders
↓
Rabbi Shimon's moral logic: Who caused the righteous to dwell among the wicked? Their own property — attachment to possessions kept them there. Therefore their property in the city is destroyed as a consequence of that choice.
Baraita: property of righteous inside is destroyed; property outside is spared

🐄 Rav Ḥisda: Deposits and Mixed Ownership

Deposits from Another City

  • Outsider's property deposited in the city: permitted — it belongs to the outsider
  • City resident assumed liability for deposit: forbidden — now treated as his property
  • Outsider then re-assumed liability: permitted again — ownership reverted

Half-and-Half Animal

  • Animal co-owned by city resident and outsider: forbidden entirely
  • An animal cannot be physically divided — the condemned half taints the whole
  • No remedy: the animal must be destroyed

Half-and-Half Dough

  • Dough co-owned by city resident and outsider: permitted in part
  • Dough can be physically separated into two distinct portions
  • The outsider's permitted half is divided off and remains permitted

🏙️ Requirements and Procedure for Ir HaNidachat

Majority subverted: Most inhabitants must have worshipped idols — a minority of sinners does not turn the whole city into an ir nidachat.
↓
City square required: Deuteronomy 13:17 commands gathering spoils 'into the midst of its square.' A city with no square cannot become an ir nidachat — the mandated procedure is impossible.
↓
Subversion must be by locals: Dilemma — if inhabitants subverted themselves with no outside inciter, do city laws apply? The verse says 'and they subverted' — implying external subversion; case left unresolved.
↓
Women and children as inciters: If women or children subverted the city, the idolaters are judged as individuals (not as a collective city), since women and children cannot legally constitute valid inciters for ir nidachat.
↓
Sequential vs. simultaneous judgment: Rabbi Yoḥanan: try and stone each idolater one by one. Reish Lakish: multiply courts so all are tried simultaneously — both methods are halakhically valid.
↓
Who counts as a resident? Staying 12 months confers full resident status (charity and tax obligations). A caravan staying 30 days acquires partial obligations — relevant to determining who is counted among the city's inhabitants.
