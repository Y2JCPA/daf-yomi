📜 A Daf of Endings and Beginnings

בֵּן סוֹרֵר וּמוֹרֶה
Core Tension Sanhedrin 68 opens with Rabbi Eliezer's poignant deathbed scene — his vast Torah knowledge, the cucumber sorcery incident, and Rabbi Akiva's anguished mourning. The daf then pivots to a new Mishna introducing the Ben Sorer U'Moreh (stubborn and rebellious son), establishing age parameters and exemptions that define this rare and severe legal category.

🛏️ Rabbi Eliezer's Final Teachings

The Sages
Why have you come? (concealing that they came due to his illness)
Rabbi Eliezer
And why have you not come until now? You have neglected me!
Rabbi Eliezer
Woe to you, my two arms — like Torah scrolls being rolled up, never opened again. I learned much and taught much, yet took from my teachers no more than a dog laps from the sea!
The Sages
What is the halakha regarding a ball stuffed with rags? A last? A shoe on a last — susceptible to impurity?
Rabbi Eliezer
Tahor! Tahor! Tahor! — and with that word his soul departed in purity.
Setting Rabbi Eliezer was ostracized (Bava Metzia 59b); the Sages sat 4 cubits away. His son Hyrcanus tried to remove his tefillin for Shabbat and was berated.

🥒 The Cucumber Sorcery — Teaching vs. Performing

הָעוֹשֶׂה לְלַמֵּד — פָּטוּר
Gemara's Resolution Rabbi Akiva learned the cucumber-gathering sorcery from Rabbi Eliezer — but did not fully understand it, so he also learned it from Rabbi Yehoshua.

Objection: How could Rabbi Eliezer perform sorcery when the Mishna says one who performs sorcery is liable?

Answer: Performing sorcery in order to teach is permitted — only performing it for practical use is forbidden. Rabbi Eliezer said one statement and the entire field filled with cucumbers; then he said another and they gathered themselves together.

👦 Ben Sorer U'Moreh — The Liability Window

A boy steals from his parents to fund a gluttonous meal of meat and wine with lowly companions
↓
Too Young
Has not yet grown two pubic hairs — still a minor → Exempt
In the Window
From two pubic hairs until the lower beard surrounds corona → Liable
Too Old
Lower beard fully grown — treated as adult for other penalties → Exempt
↓
If liable: parents bring him to court → he is exhorted → if he repeats the offense → executed
He is killed for what he will become, not only what he did

😢 Two Modes of Loss — Teacher and Son

Rabbi Eliezer's Passing

  • 300 halakhot on snow-white leprous marks — no student ever asked
  • 300 (or 3,000) laws of the gourd — none studied with him
  • His Torah likened to a dog lapping from the sea
  • Died with the word tahor (pure) on his lips
  • Rabbi Akiva struck his flesh until blood flowed, crying: 'My father! My father! The chariots of Israel!'

Ben Sorer U'Moreh's Fate

  • Executed not for past sin but for anticipated future
  • Torah treats him as though he already became a murderer
  • Window closes at puberty's end — liability is brief
  • Father must be a full adult; a minor father breaks the chain
  • A rare case where Torah law is proactive, not reactive
