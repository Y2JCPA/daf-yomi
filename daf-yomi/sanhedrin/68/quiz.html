Sanhedrin 68 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did the Sages sit four cubits away from Rabbi Eliezer during their visit?
A. They feared contracting his illness
B. Halacha requires distance from a dying person
C. Rabbi Eliezer was ostracized, and one may not sit within four cubits of an excommunicated person
D. His room was too small to sit closer
Correct: C — Rabbi Eliezer had been excommunicated (see Bava Metzia 59b). Halacha prohibits sitting within four cubits of an ostracized person, so even his devoted students kept their distance.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
With what final word did Rabbi Eliezer's soul depart?
A. Shalom
B. Torah
C. Tamei (impure)
D. Tahor (pure)
Correct: D — Asked about the purity of a shoe on a last, Rabbi Eliezer declared 'tahor' (ritually pure) — and his soul departed at that very moment, a powerful image of a life of Torah.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the problem that Rabbi Eliezer performed sorcery, which the Mishna says is punishable?
A. Rabbi Eliezer was exempt as a rabbinic authority
B. Performing sorcery in order to teach is permitted; only practical use is forbidden
C. The cucumber incident was a parable, not a real act
D. Sorcery is only forbidden outside of Israel
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes between sorcery performed for practical benefit (forbidden and liable) and sorcery performed for the purpose of teaching students (permitted).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda, why can the son of a minor father not become a ben sorer u'moreh?
A. A minor's children are not legally recognized
B. The verse 'if a man has a son' implies the father must be a full adult man, not a minor
C. Minors cannot testify and thus cannot bring accusations
D. The son of a minor is always considered a minor himself
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda derives from 'if a man has a stubborn and rebellious son' that the law applies only when a full adult man has a son — not when a minor (who has fathered a child) has one.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the upper age limit for a boy to be liable as a stubborn and rebellious son?
A. Until his 13th Hebrew birthday
B. Until he grows the upper (facial) beard
C. Until the lower beard surrounds the corona
D. Until he is married
Correct: C — The Mishna refers to the lower beard surrounding the genitals — not the upper/facial beard. Rabbi Ḥiyya's baraita specifies 'until the hair surrounds the corona,' marking the end of the liability window.
