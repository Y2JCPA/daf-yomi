QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Kahana, under what condition is a rebellious elder NOT executed even if he deviates from the Sanhedrin?
A. When he rules on agricultural laws rather than Temple matters
B. When he claims his ruling is based on tradition received from his teachers
C. When he instructs others in private rather than in public
D. When he is older than the members of the Sanhedrin
Correct: B — Rav Kahana holds that an elder who says 'this is the tradition I received' is not executed even if the Sanhedrin rules differently. Only one who asserts personal interpretation ('in my eyes') against the Sanhedrin is liable — or one who instructs others to act on his deviant ruling.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the novel element in saying a rebellious elder who ACTS on his own ruling is liable, according to the Gemara's resolution?
A. Before being deemed a rebellious elder, acting on a deviant ruling was not forbidden at all
B. Before being deemed a rebellious elder, he required forewarning before execution; after, no forewarning is needed
C. Before, he could only receive lashes; after, he is executed
D. Before, the prohibition was rabbinic; after, it becomes Torah-level
Correct: B — The Gemara explains: initially, before he is officially deemed a rebellious elder, he requires forewarning (התראה) in order to be executed for acting on his deviant ruling. Once he has been ruled a rebellious elder, he no longer requires forewarning — that is the novel element.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the new mishna (88b), who is liable as a rebellious elder regarding tefillin?
A. One who says there is no mitzva of tefillin at all
B. One who says tefillin should be worn only on Shabbat
C. One who says tefillin requires only three compartments instead of four
D. One who says both men and women must wear tefillin
Correct: C — Greater stringency applies when one accepts the Torah's core obligation but disputes the rabbinic details that implement it. Denying tefillin entirely is denying the Torah itself — not a rebellious elder case. Ruling that tefillin needs only three compartments (not four) accepts the Torah basis but deviates from the rabbinic explanation, making one liable.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yosei's baraita, what caused 'the Torah to become like two Torahs'?
A. The translation of the Torah into Greek (Septuagint)
B. The Roman prohibition on Torah study
C. The proliferation of disciples of Shammai and Hillel who did not study sufficiently with their masters
D. The death of the last prophets and the closing of prophecy
Correct: C — Rabbi Yosei taught that when disciples of Shammai and Hillel grew in number but had not sufficiently attended to their masters, disputes proliferated and the Torah became like two Torahs. Previously, the unified Sanhedrin in the Chamber of Hewn Stone prevented this fragmentation.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the dispute about pe'a (corner of the field), what is Rabbi Yishmael's unique position?
A. Pe'a must be at least one-sixtieth of the field
B. Pe'a can be designated even from dough, not only from standing grain
C. Pe'a applies only to grain crops, not fruits
D. The owner may take back pe'a if no poor person claims it within three days
Correct: B — Rabbi Yishmael holds that pe'a can be designated from dough (after the grain has been processed), unlike the Rabbis who require it to come from standing grain or sheaves. The practical consequence: if a poor person uses dough-pe'a to betroth a woman, the betrothal is valid according to Rabbi Yishmael (since the dough legitimately belonged to the poor person) but invalid according to the Rabbis.
