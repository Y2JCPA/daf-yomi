📜 When Is the Rebellious Elder Executed?

Rav Kahana
If the elder says "This is the tradition I received from my teachers," and the court says "This is our considered view" — he is NOT executed. He followed received tradition, not personal defiance.
Rav Kahana
If he says "This is correct in my eyes" and the court says the same — all the more so he is not executed. No contradiction exists between the two positions.
Rabbi Elazar
Even if he claims tradition and the court says "This is our view" — he IS executed, so that discord will not proliferate among Israel and the Torah will not become like two Torahs.
Proof for Rav Kahana The mishna's language — 'this is what I interpreted' — implies the elder does NOT claim tradition, supporting Rav Kahana that tradition-based rulings are exempt from execution

🌾 Disputes in Agricultural Laws — "Your Gates"

Gleanings (Leket)

  • Two stalks fallen at harvest = poor person's (all agree)
  • Three stalks = disputed between Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel
  • Beit Shammai: three stalks → owner's property
  • Beit Hillel: three stalks → poor person's
  • Elder ruling against Sanhedrin on this is liable

Pe'a (Corner of Field)

  • Rabbis: pe'a only from standing grain or sheaves
  • Rabbi Yishmael: pe'a can be given even from dough
  • Practical stakes: poor person betroths woman with dough-pe'a
  • Rabbis: betrothal invalid; R. Yishmael: betrothal valid
  • Dispute applies whether pe'a applies before or after processing

Forgotten Sheaves (Shikheḥa)

  • Dispute between Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel
  • Beit Shammai: two sheaves forgotten = belongs to owner
  • Beit Hillel: two sheaves = belongs to the poor
  • Three sheaves = owner's (both agree)
  • Elder deviating from Sanhedrin on this is a rebellious elder

🏛️ Three Courts of Jerusalem — Escalating Procedure

1. Court in the Elder's Own City
The elder poses his question to local judges. If they have a clear ruling, they answer. If uncertain, they all travel together to Jerusalem.
↓
2. Court at the Entrance of the Temple Mount (23 judges)
The question is brought here. If they have a ruling, it is given. If uncertain, all proceed to the next level.
↓
3. Court at the Entrance of the Azara (23 judges)
The court at the entrance to the Temple courtyard rules if it can. If uncertain, all proceed to the highest authority.
↓
4. Great Sanhedrin — Chamber of Hewn Stone (71 judges)
The supreme halakhic body issues a definitive ruling. This ruling is binding. An elder who thereafter deviates is a rebellious elder.
↓
5. Elder Returns Home — Liability Threshold
He may teach his original view — he is exempt. He is only liable if he instructs others to act on his deviant ruling.

📉 How Discord Proliferated Among Israel

מאז שרבו תלמידי שמאי והלל שלא שמשו כל צרכן — רבו מחלוקות בישראל ונעשית התורה כשתי תורות
Historical Turning Point Rabbi Yosei taught: Initially, discord did not proliferate among Israel. The Great Sanhedrin sat in the Chamber of Hewn Stone and issued binding rulings for all Israel. Two courts of 23 sat at the Temple Mount and Temple entrance to handle escalating questions.

The turning point: When the disciples of Shammai and Hillel multiplied — disciples who did not attend to their masters sufficiently — disputes multiplied and the Torah became like two Torahs.

Restoring unity: The Sanhedrin dispatched letters to all places: "Whoever is wise, humble, and whose mind is at ease with people — let him be appointed judge in his city," from which judges could be elevated to ever-higher courts.
