⚔️ False Prophet Dispute: Abaye vs. Rava

Rav Ḥisda
The dispute (between Rabbi Shimon and the Sages) is specifically about idol worship — whether a prophet who says to fulfill part and nullify part of the prohibition is liable to strangulation.
Rav Hamnuna
The dispute applies to any mitzva — whether a prophet who prophesies to abolish the entire essence of any mitzva (not just part) is liable.
Abaye
I follow Rav Ḥisda. The baraita's dispute between Rabbi Shimon and Sages concerns idol worship specifically — partial fulfillment and nullification of that prohibition.
Rava
I follow Rav Hamnuna. The baraita's dispute is about abolishing the full essence of any mitzva — idol worship or otherwise — and whether that triggers strangulation.
Rabbi Yoḥanan's Ruling For all mitzvot: if a confirmed prophet instructs temporary violation of Torah — heed him. Except idol worship: even a bona fide prophet with signs and wonders cannot authorize any violation, even provisionally.

🌍 The Foundational Mishna (90a)

כָּל יִשְׂרָאֵל יֵשׁ לָהֶם חֵלֶק לָעוֹלָם הַבָּא — סַנְהֶדְרִין צ
Mishna · Sanhedrin 90a כָּל יִשְׂרָאֵל יֵשׁ לָהֶם חֵלֶק לָעוֹלָם הַבָּא
All of Israel has a share in the World-to-Come, as it is written: "And your people also shall be all righteous; they shall inherit the land forever" (Isaiah 60:21).

Three who forfeit their share:
① One who says resurrection of the dead is not derived from the Torah
② One who says the Torah is not from Heaven
③ An Apikoros (one who scorns Torah sages or denies core principles)

Rabbi Akiva adds: One who reads external books (sefarim ḥitzonim); one who whispers incantations over a wound using the verse about Egypt's plagues — treating Torah as a cure for the body.

The Measure-for-Measure Principle: He denied resurrection — therefore he will not share in the resurrection of the dead.

📖 Proofs for Resurrection of the Dead (תְּחִיַּת הַמֵּתִים)

Torah — Rabbi Yoḥanan

  • "You shall give the teruma of the Lord to Aaron the priest" (Num. 18:28)
  • Aaron had already died — yet verse commands giving him teruma
  • Implies Aaron will be resurrected to receive it
  • School of Rabbi Yishmael: teruma must go to a priest devoted to mitzvot like Aaron

Torah — Rabbi Simai

  • "I have established My covenant with them to give to them" (Ex. 6:4)
  • Said to the Patriarchs — who had already died
  • God's oath was to give the land to them, not merely to their children
  • Clinching proof: cannot be deflected by alternative reading

Prophets — Isaiah

  • "Your dead shall live; my corpse shall arise" (Isaiah 26:19)
  • "Awake and sing, you that dwell in the dust"
  • Heretics: perhaps refers to Ezekiel's vision of dry bones only
  • Rabban Gamliel refuted with further context of the verse

Writings — Song of Songs / Psalms

  • "Moving gently the lips of those that sleep" (Song 7:10) — the dead will speak
  • "May they blossom out of the city like grass" (Ps. 72:16)
  • Queen Cleopatra's question: Will they rise clothed or naked?
  • Rabbi Meir: Wheat is buried naked but sprouts with many garments — all the more so the righteous

👑 Debates with Heretics, Romans & Samaritans

Heretics challenge Rabban Gamliel using Torah, Prophets, and Writings — each time offering an alternative reading. He answers all three and ends with the verse from Deuteronomy 11:21 which they cannot deflect: "the land the Lord swore to give them" — to the dead Patriarchs themselves.
↓
Alternative final proof: "But you who cleave to the Lord your God are alive this day" (Deut. 4:4). Obvious they were alive — the verse implies: just as you are alive today, so too after death you will be alive.
↓
Romans ask Rabbi Yehoshua ben Ḥananya: Where are both resurrection AND divine foreknowledge in one verse? Answer: "Behold, you shall lie with your fathers and arise" — resurrection; "this people will arise and go astray" — God foresees the future.
↓
Rabbi Eliezer ben Rabbi Yosei vs. Samaritans: Refuted them using the doubled verb הִכָּרֵת תִּכָּרֵת (hikkaret tikkaret) — "shall be utterly cut off." First hikkaret = excised from this world; tikkaret = excised from the World-to-Come. Implies a soul that survives this world to be punished in the next.
↓
Roman Emperor to Rabban Gamliel: You claim the dead will live — but aren't they dust? Does dust come to life? (Daf ends on this challenge — to be continued on 91a)
