QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, what was the 'convincing response' the Jewish people gave Ezekiel when he urged them to repent?
A. We have already repented and God has not answered us
B. A slave sold by his master to another cannot return to the first master — God sold us to the nations
C. The evil inclination is too powerful and we lack free will to resist it
D. We are too far in exile and God's Presence has departed from us
Correct: B — Shmuel teaches that ten people sat before Ezekiel and argued: just as a slave sold to a new master has no standing to return to the first, since God 'sold' Israel to the nations they cannot return. This required a direct divine reassurance to answer.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was Balaam's unique knowledge referred to by the phrase 'he knows the knowledge of the Most High' (Numbers 24:16)?
A. He could hear divine speech directly without intermediary angels
B. He knew the thoughts and intentions of every king on earth
C. He knew the precise moment each day when God grows angry
D. He knew the exact date of the Messianic redemption
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that Balaam's claim to 'knowledge of the Most High' was his ability to identify the specific instant each day when God grows angry — imperceptible to anyone else. He planned to curse Israel during that moment, but God withheld His anger entirely during those days.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the baraita, Beor (Balaam's father), Cushan-Rishathaim, and Laban the Aramean are all the same person. Why was he called Cushan-Rishathaim?
A. Because he ruled over two kingdoms simultaneously
B. Because he performed two evil deeds (rishiyyot) against the Jewish people
C. Because he was doubly wicked — evil in thought and evil in deed
D. Because he oppressed Israel for twice as long as any other enemy
Correct: B — The name Cushan-Rishathaim is interpreted as referring to two evil deeds (rishiyyot — from rasha, wicked) that he committed against Israel. This is one of several names given to Balaam's father, each reflecting a different sin.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Abba bar Kahana, which of Balaam's blessings did NOT ultimately revert to become a curse for Israel?
A. The blessing of a king son of a king
B. The blessing of numerous descendants like water
C. The blessing of synagogues and study halls
D. The blessing of the Divine Presence resting among them
Correct: C — Rabbi Abba bar Kahana says all of Balaam's blessings eventually came to be fulfilled as curses — except for the destruction of synagogues and study halls, as it is written: 'I will not reject them... to destroy them utterly' (Leviticus 26:44).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say Ahijah the Shilonite's curse of Israel was better than Balaam's blessing of Israel?
A. Because Ahijah was a true prophet while Balaam was merely a diviner
B. Because Ahijah cursed like an olive harvest bringing renewal; Balaam blessed like a flowing river that never returns
C. Because Ahijah's curse was fulfilled while Balaam's blessing was never realized
D. Because Ahijah later repented of his curse but Balaam doubled down on his blessing
Correct: B — Rabbi Shmuel bar Naḥmani citing Rabbi Yonatan: Ahijah cursed Israel 'like a reed in the water' — bent but rooted, it regrows (olive analogy — beaten but the tree survives). Balaam blessed them like a river — waters flow away and do not return. A true friend's painful rebuke that leads to renewal is superior to an enemy's flattery.
