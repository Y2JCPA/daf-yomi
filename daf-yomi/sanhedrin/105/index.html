🗣️ Israel's Convincing Response to the Prophets

Prophet
Repent! Return to God — it is not too late.
Israel (Rav's version)
A person does not sin unless a spirit of folly enters him — the evil inclination dominates us and we cannot help ourselves!
Ezekiel (Shmuel's version)
Repent! God will accept your return.
Israel
A slave sold by his master to another — can he return to the first master? God has 'sold' us to the nations!
God via Ezekiel
"With a mighty hand and an outstretched arm I will be King over you" — I will redeem you despite yourselves! (Ezekiel 20:33)
Why 'Convincing'? The Gemara notes these responses were so cogent that the prophet could not immediately refute them — requiring a direct divine reassurance to overcome the argument.

🧙 Balaam — Many Names, Many Faces of Evil

Balaam the Prophet

  • Bilam = belo am — 'without a nation'
  • OR bila am — 'who swallowed the nation'
  • Greatest prophet of the gentile nations
  • Disabled in one leg: "walked limping [shefi]"
  • Practiced divination via his penis
  • Died young — no share in the World-to-Come

Beor / Cushan-Rishathaim / Laban

  • All the same person — Balaam's father
  • Beor — engaged in bestiality (בעיר)
  • Cushan-Rishathaim — two evil deeds against Israel
  • Laban the Aramean — sought to uproot everything
  • Father inferior to son in prophecy
  • Balaam prophesied; Beor merely divined

Balaam vs. Moses

  • Both greatest prophets — each to their people
  • Moses: humble, serving Israel's benefit
  • Balaam: arrogant, sought to destroy Israel
  • Moses knew God 'face to face'
  • Balaam knew only the moment of God's anger
  • Moses blessed Israel; Balaam tried to curse

🫏 Balaam and the Donkey — A Remarkable Exchange

Donkey
Am I not your donkey — merely for burdens? (Numbers 22:30)
Balaam
Merely for burdens — not for riding!
Donkey
"Upon which you have ridden" — you DO ride me regularly! (Numbers 22:30)
Balaam
That was a mere happenstance — not my normal practice.
Donkey
Moreover — hahasken hiskanti (Numbers 22:30) — I have also served you in conjugal acts at night! Your depravity is well known.
Talmudic Derivation The root סכן in hiskanti ('wont') is linked to Abishag's intimate service to David (I Kings 1:2) — the donkey alludes to Balaam's practice of bestiality, confirming the accusation against his father Beor as well.

⏱️ Balaam's Secret Power — The Moment of Divine Anger

Balaam boasts: "He knows the knowledge of the Most High" (Numbers 24:16)
↓
Objection: He didn't even understand his own donkey's mind — how could he know God's?
↓
Resolution: His real skill was identifying the precise instant each day when God grows angry — imperceptible to all others
↓
The Moment
Lasts only an instant — no rooster crowing, no sunrise redness at that point
Balaam's Plan
Curse Israel during that instant so God would not protect them
God's Response
God withheld His anger entirely during Balaam's attempt — "God was not angry those days" (Numbers 23:21)

❤️ Love, Hatred, and Torah — Three Life Principles

Love Negates Prominence — Rabbi Shimon ben Elazar
Abraham saddled his own donkey for the Akeidah (Genesis 22:3) — his love for God caused him to bypass his servants and act himself
↓
Hatred Also Negates Prominence — Balaam
Balaam saddled his own donkey (Numbers 22:21) — his hatred of Israel drove him to act personally, without waiting for servants
↓
Study Torah Even Not for Its Own Sake — Rav (citing Rav Yehuda)
"A person should always engage in Torah and mitzvot even shelo lishmah — through this, he will come to lishmah"
↓
Proof from Balak: He offered 42 sacrifices — more than the patriarchs combined
His shelo lishmah merit caused Ruth to descend from him → Ruth bore Obed → Obed bore Jesse → Jesse bore David → Messiah
↓
Ahijah's Curse Beats Balaam's Blessing — Rabbi Shmuel bar Naḥmani
Ahijah cursed Israel like olive harvest — beaten, but the tree regrows. Balaam blessed like a river — flowing away, never returning. A friend's painful correction outweighs an enemy's flattery.
