🔢 How Many Survived the Angelic Destruction of Sennacherib's Army?

Rav
Ten soldiers survived. Proof: "The rest of the trees of his forest shall be few, that a child may write them" (Isaiah 10:19). The smallest number a child can write is ten.
R. Yehoshua ben Levi
Fourteen survived. Proof: the same verse lists "two or three… four or five" — summing all the numbers yields fourteen.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Five survived: Sennacherib himself, his two sons, Nebuchadnezzar, and Nebuzaradan the captain of the guard — the very men who would later threaten Israel.
Background The angel slew 185,000 in a single night (Isaiah 37:36). The debate concerns survivors from an army of millions — the contrast underscores total divine annihilation.

🌍 Three Who Experienced Kefitzat HaDerech (Land Contraction)

Eliezer

  • Servant of Abraham
  • Traveled to find a wife for Isaac
  • Arrived in Aram-Naharaim with miraculous speed
  • Source: Genesis 24

Jacob

  • Fled Beersheba; land contracted to Mount Moriah
  • God caused the sun to set early so Jacob would sleep there
  • Vayyifga = prayer at the sacred site
  • Source: Genesis 28:10–11

Abishai ben Zeruiah

  • Racing to rescue David from Ishbibenob
  • Land contracted beneath him instantly
  • Arrived in Philistia before nightfall
  • Source: II Samuel 21:17

👴 The Hired Razor — God Disguised as an Old Man

בְּיוֹם הַהוּא יְגַלַּח ה' בְּתַעַר הַשְּׂכִירָה (ישעיה ז:כ)
Rabbi Abbahu: "Were this verse not written, it would be impossible to say it" God appeared to Sennacherib as an old man and asked: "When you return to the kings of the east and west whose children you killed — what will you tell them?"

Sennacherib: "I too was afraid and fled."

God: "Then I will make you a razor — you will shave them." Sennacherib becomes the excuse for the nations: "Even Sennacherib fled — how could we have withstood them?"

This fulfills: "On that day the Lord shall shave with a hired razor in the parts beyond the river" (Isaiah 7:20). The razor = Sennacherib; hired = deployed by God as an instrument of humiliation.
