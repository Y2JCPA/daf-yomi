QUESTION 1 OF 5
How does the baraita derive that one who lies with his father's wife is executed by stoning?
A. From an explicit statement 'he shall be stoned' in Leviticus 20:11
B. From a gezerah shavah: 'their blood shall be upon them' appears also in the blasphemy case, which is punished by stoning
C. From a kal v'chomer: adultery is punished by stoning, so this certainly is too
D. From the phrase 'he has uncovered his father's nakedness' — nakedness implies stoning
Correct: B — The baraita uses a verbal analogy (gezerah shavah): the phrase 'their blood shall be upon them' appears both in Lev. 20:11 and in the case of the blasphemer (Lev. 20:27), which is executed by stoning.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Rabbi Yehuda and the Rabbis regarding Leviticus 18:7?
A. Rabbi Yehuda says it refers literally to one's father; Rabbis say it refers to one's father's wife
B. Rabbi Yehuda says it refers to one's father's wife; Rabbis say it refers literally to homosexual intercourse with one's father
C. Both interpret it as the father's wife but dispute whether punishment applies after the father's death
D. Rabbi Yehuda limits it to when the father is alive; Rabbis extend it to after his death
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda reads 'nakedness of your father' as referring to his wife (honoring the father). The Rabbis read it literally as prohibiting intercourse with one's father, deriving the father's wife prohibition separately from Lev. 18:8.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Akiva, how is the prohibition against passive homosexual intercourse derived?
A. From Deuteronomy 23:18 — 'there shall not be a kadesh among the children of Israel'
B. From a kal v'chomer: if active intercourse is forbidden, passive certainly is
C. From Leviticus 18:22 re-read: 'tishkav' → 'tishakev' = you shall not enable your being lain with
D. From the punishment verse Leviticus 20:13 which implies both roles are liable
Correct: C — Rabbi Akiva derives the passive prohibition directly from Lev. 18:22 by re-reading 'tishkav' (you shall lie) as 'tishakev' (you shall be lain with), making both roles covered by the same verse.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Abaye hold that Deuteronomy 23:18 ('there shall not be a kadesh') cannot serve as the source for prohibiting passive bestiality?
A. Because Deuteronomy 23:18 only applies to priests, not ordinary Israelites
B. Because Deuteronomy 23:18 applies only to intercourse with humans, not with animals
C. Because Deuteronomy 23:18 is overridden by the more specific verse in Exodus
D. Because Deuteronomy 23:18 only prohibits the active role, not the passive role
Correct: B — Abaye holds that the term 'kadesh' in Deut. 23:18 refers specifically to one who makes himself available for intercourse with men — not for intercourse with animals. For passive bestiality, the source must be Exodus 22:18.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the dispute between Rav and Shmuel regarding age thresholds for halakhically significant intercourse with a male?
A. Rav: under 12 years old; Shmuel: under 9 years old
B. Rav: under 9 years old (for passive role); Shmuel: under 3 years old
C. Rav: under 3 years old; Shmuel: under 9 years old
D. Both agree on 9 years old but disagree on whether it applies to the active or passive partner
Correct: B — Rav holds the threshold is 9 years and one day (below which the passive role has no halakhic significance). Shmuel holds it is 3 years old, because Lev. 18:22 equates the passive male to a woman, and a girl's virginity threshold is age 3.
