🔍 Rabbi Yehuda vs. Rabbis — 'Nakedness of Your Father'

Rabbi Yehuda
"The nakedness of your father" (Lev. 18:7) refers to your father's wife — not literally to your father. There is therefore no separate explicit verse prohibiting intercourse with one's father.
Rabbis
"The nakedness of your father" means literally your father, prohibiting homosexual intercourse with him. We derive the prohibition of father's wife from a different verse entirely.
Rabbi Yehuda
I derive the prohibition of intercourse with one's father via kal v'chomer: if one's father's wife is forbidden, how much more so one's father himself! And this depends on the Abaye/Rava dispute about punishing for inferences.
Rabbis
The phrase "she is your mother" (Lev. 18:7) teaches: you are liable for intercourse with one's mother, but not doubly liable for both 'father's wife' and 'mother' simultaneously — they are one prohibition here.
Key Dispute Does Lev. 18:7 prohibit intercourse with one's father literally, or is it a reference to one's father's wife? This determines the source of multiple prohibitions and whether double liability applies.

⛓️ Deriving Punishment for Intercourse with Father's Wife

Lev. 20:11: "The man who lies with his father's wife… both of them shall be put to death, their blood shall be upon them"
↓
Gezerah shavah: "their blood shall be upon them" appears also in the blasphemer case → blasphemer = stoning → here also stoning
↓
During Father's Lifetime
Punishment: stoning
Prohibition: Lev. 18:8 — "nakedness of your father's wife"
After Father's Death
Rabbi Yehuda: derived via verbal analogy
Rabbis: derived from Lev. 20:11 — "he has uncovered his father's nakedness"
↓
Mother who is also father's wife → double liability (mishna). Mother who is NOT father's wife → Rav Sheisha: Lev. 18:7 "she is your mother" = still liable

🗣️ Abaye vs. Rava — Punishment for Derived (Kal V'chomer) Prohibition

Rava
One does administer punishment for a derived prohibition (kal v'chomer). Therefore Rabbi Yehuda holds one is liable for intercourse with one's father even without an explicit verse — the kal v'chomer suffices.
Abaye
One does not administer punishment for a derived prohibition. Therefore Rabbi Yehuda must have an explicit verse for intercourse with one's father. He re-reads Lev. 18:7 to include it explicitly.
Rava
Under my view, Rabbi Yehuda holds: one who lies with his father is liable for two transgressions — the general prohibition against intercourse with a male, and the specific prohibition against intercourse with one's father.
Abaye
Under my view, even Rabbi Yehuda acknowledges only one prohibition for intercourse with one's father via kal v'chomer, since we don't punish for inferences. A second explicit verse would be required for double liability.
Underlying Principle This Abaye-Rava dispute about punishing for kal v'chomer recurs throughout the Talmud and here determines whether Rabbi Yehuda's position yields one or two liabilities for intercourse with one's father.

🔄 Active vs. Passive Roles — Step-by-Step Derivation

Step 1 — Homosexual intercourse, active: Prohibition from Lev. 18:22 — "you shall not lie with a male as with a woman." Punishment from Lev. 20:13 via gezerah shavah — stoning.
↓
Step 2 — Homosexual intercourse, passive: Rabbi Yishmael: from Deut. 23:18 — "there shall not be a kadesh [sodomite] among Israel." Rabbi Akiva: re-reads Lev. 18:22 — "tishkav" → "tishakev" = you shall not enable your being lain with.
↓
Step 3 — Bestiality, active: Prohibition from Lev. 18:23. Punishment from Lev. 20:15 — stoning. Both the person and the animal are stoned.
↓
Step 4 — Bestiality, passive: Rabbi Yishmael: from Deut. 23:18 OR Ex. 22:18. Abaye objects: Deut. 23:18 applies only to intercourse with humans, not animals. Source must be Ex. 22:18 alone.
↓
Step 5 — Abaye's ruling: Ex. 22:18 ("whoever lies with an animal") equates passive to active. Just as the active role carries both prohibition and punishment, so does the passive role — derived by analogy.
↓
Step 6 — Combined cases (Rabbi Abbahu): One who both actively and passively engages in homosexual intercourse in one lapse of awareness: R. Yishmael = two prohibitions; R. Akiva = one prohibition (Lev. 18:22 only).

📏 Rav vs. Shmuel — Age Threshold for Male Intercourse

Rav

  • Threshold: 9 years and 1 day for passive intercourse to be halakhically significant
  • Below 9: intercourse with him does not render the active partner liable
  • At 9+: full liability applies to the active partner
  • Principle: halakhot of active intercourse apply equally to passive intercourse
  • The Torah does not deem a boy under 9 to be like one who is 9 or older

Shmuel

  • Threshold: 3 years old (paralleling the standard for a girl's virginity)
  • Below 3: no halakhic significance — like intercourse with a girl under 3
  • At 3+: full liability applies to the active partner
  • Principle: Lev. 18:22 — 'as with a woman' — passive male is like a woman
  • Just as a girl under 3 has no legally significant virginity, so too a boy under 3
