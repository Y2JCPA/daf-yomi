🏙️ The Idolatrous City (Ir HaNidachat)

Wicked men (benei veliyya'al — those who cast off Heaven's yoke) subvert an entire city to worship idols
↓
City must be within the Land of Israel and wholly within one tribal territory (per Reish Lakish)
↓
All inhabitants executed by sword; animals destroyed; property burned
↓
Even property of the righteous inside the city is destroyed — unlike their property outside the city
↓
Consecrated items (property of Heaven) are redeemed; tithes eaten; sacred scrolls hidden away
↓
Spoils gathered in the square and burned — the city becomes an eternal ruin, never to be rebuilt

אמן — אל מלך נאמן
🔤 What Does Amen Mean? Rabbi Ḥanina teaches: Amen is an acronym —

אל מלך נאמן

El Melekh Ne'eman — "God, faithful King."

When we answer Amen to a blessing, we affirm that the Holy One, Blessed be He, is the faithful King who stands behind every word of prayer.

God
Moses — didn't YOU tell Me that 'slow to anger' is an attribute reserved for the righteous alone? The people who sinned with the spies are not worthy of atonement!
Moses
Master of the Universe — is this not what YOU said to me? You declared 'slow to anger' without restriction. As it is written: 'Let the power of my Lord be great, as You have spoken, saying: The Lord is slow to anger…' (Numbers 14:17)
God
Moses rightly reminded Me of My own word. Therefore: 'I have forgiven according to your word' (Numbers 14:20) — the attribute applies even to the wicked.
What Did Moses See at Sinai? Rabbi Ḥanina ben Gamla: Moses saw the attribute of slow to anger (ארך אפיים).
The Rabbis: Moses saw the attribute of truth (אמת).
A baraita supports Rabbi Ḥanina ben Gamla's view.

🔥 Destroyed in the Ir HaNidachat

  • All inhabitants — killed by sword
  • All animals within the city
  • Property of the righteous inside the city
  • The city itself — burned entirely
  • The site — forever an eternal ruin (tel olam)

✅ Preserved or Handled Differently

  • Passing caravans (donkeys/camels) — they escape
  • Consecrated items (hekdesh) — redeemed before destruction
  • Tithes (terumah/maaser) — eaten or handled per their rules
  • Sacred scrolls — hidden away (genizah)
  • Property of the righteous outside the city — fully preserved

👑 Who Receives the Ultimate Reward?

One who subdues his evil inclination in battle against sin
↓
Also engages in Torah study
Receives spirit of justice — the attribute of justice has no claim against him
Torah study without self-mastery
The attribute of justice asks: How are these different from those? — the quality of restraint is essential
↓
Rabbi Elazar citing Rabbi Ḥanina: The Holy One, Blessed be He, will Himself be a crown of glory and diadem of beauty on the head of each righteous person (Isaiah 28:5)
