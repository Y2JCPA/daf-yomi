Sanhedrin 111 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ḥanina, the word 'Amen' is an acronym. What are the three words it represents?
A. Emet, Mishpat, Nifla — Truth, Justice, Wonder
B. El Melekh Ne'eman — God, faithful King
C. Adonai, Magen, Notzer — Lord, Shield, Guardian
D. Ein Melekh Notzer — There is no king but the Guardian
Correct: B — Rabbi Ḥanina teaches that Amen (אמן) stands for El (א-ל) Melekh (מ-לך) Ne'eman (נ-אמן) — 'God, faithful King.' This gives theological depth to the universal practice of answering Amen.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How did Moses successfully argue that the attribute of 'slow to anger' applies even to the wicked?
A. He quoted the Akeidah as proof that God shows mercy to the undeserving
B. He argued that even wicked people have a spark of righteousness
C. He pointed to God's own words in Numbers 14:17 — 'as You have spoken, saying: The Lord is slow to anger'
D. He reminded God of the merit accumulated by the Patriarchs
Correct: C — Moses cited Numbers 14:17 — the phrase 'as You have spoken' indicates God previously declared this attribute applies universally, not only to the righteous. God accepted the argument and forgave Israel.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What happens to the property of righteous people who live INSIDE an idolatrous city but did not worship idols?
A. It is returned to their heirs after the city is destroyed
B. It is consecrated to the Temple treasury
C. It is destroyed along with the rest of the city's property
D. It is distributed to the poor of neighboring cities
Correct: C — The mishna explicitly states that property of the righteous inside the idolatrous city is destroyed — in contrast to their property located outside the city, which is preserved. This is one of the stringencies of Ir HaNidachat.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Rabbi Yoḥanan and Reish Lakish regarding the Ir HaNidachat?
A. Whether idol worship must involve a majority or all residents of the city
B. Whether the condemned city may ever be rebuilt as an orchard or garden
C. Whether a single city can be divided between the territories of two different tribes
D. Whether animals inside the city are killed by sword or left to die naturally
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan holds that one city can span two tribal territories; Reish Lakish disagrees. The practical implication concerns whether a man owning property in a city of a different tribe triggers the Ir HaNidachat laws.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Elazar citing Rabbi Ḥanina, what will the Holy One, Blessed be He, be for each righteous person in the future?
A. A pillar of fire lighting their path in the World-to-Come
B. A crown of glory and diadem of beauty on their head
C. A wellspring of wisdom flowing from their hearts
D. A shield of protection against all enemies
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar citing Rabbi Ḥanina derives from Isaiah 28:5 that God Himself will be 'a crown of glory and a diadem of beauty' placed on the head of each righteous person — the ultimate personal reward in the World-to-Come.
