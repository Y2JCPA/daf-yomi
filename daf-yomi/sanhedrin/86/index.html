🏛️ Rebellious Elder: Court Escalation Process

Local city court — elder disputes a ruling; both sides record their positions
↓
Court at entrance to Temple Mount — elder restates both views; ruling sought
↓
Court at entrance to Temple Courtyard — third escalation; same procedure repeated
↓
Great Sanhedrin in Chamber of Hewn Stone (71 judges) — final authoritative ruling issued
↓
Elder returns home. If he rules contrary to the Sanhedrin → executed by strangulation (Deut. 17:12)

📌 Who Is Exempt from Abduction Liability?

Verse: "If a man shall be found abducting a person of his brethren" (Deut. 24:7)
↓
Abaye's Inference
Excludes one's own son — son is already "found" in the father's possession before any abduction occurs
Rava's Extension
Teachers of children (dardekei) and mishnah reciters — their students are considered 'found in their possession,' so teachers are exempt
↓
Exodus 21:16: "If he is found in his possession, he shall be put to death" — confirms the prior-possession exemption (Abaye's anchor verse)

🗣️ Abaye vs. Rav Pappa — The 'If Found' Derivation

Abaye
The Rabbis derive the father's exemption from Deut. 24:7 — 'if a man shall be found abducting' excludes one who abducts a person already found in his possession, like a son in his father's home.
Rav Pappa
Challenge: Deut. 22:22 also says 'if a man shall be found lying with a married woman' — should we similarly exempt adultery when the couple was already together beforehand?
Abaye
No — I anchor the exemption in Exodus 21:16: 'if he is found in his possession, he shall be put to death.' The adultery verse has no parallel limiting phrase. The sources are distinct.
Key Distinction Abaye's derivation rests on Exodus 21:16, not Deuteronomy 24:7 — neutralizing Rav Pappa's adultery challenge entirely.

📜 Two 'Shall Not Steal' Verses — Two Different Laws

Exodus 20:13 — Ten Commandments

  • Adjacent to 'shall not murder' and 'shall not commit adultery'
  • Neighbors are capital offenses
  • Therefore: this verse refers to kidnapping a person — also a capital crime
  • Source: R. Yoshiya (primary prohibition on abduction)
  • R. Yoḥanan derives from "They shall not be sold as slaves" (Lev. 25:42)

Leviticus 19:11 — Holiness Code

  • Adjacent to 'shall not deal falsely' and 'shall not lie to one another'
  • Neighbors are monetary/property offenses
  • Therefore: this verse refers to stealing property
  • Confirmed by a second baraita explicitly teaching this
  • Demonstrates: Torah uses same words for different sins — context determines meaning

🕵️ Conspiring Witnesses in Abduction — Executed?

Ḥizkiyya
Conspiring witnesses to abduction and sale are NOT executed. Following Rabbi Akiva: testimony is only valid when it attests to an entire matter. Neither set of witnesses alone can cause execution — so neither is punished as conspiring witnesses.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Conspiring witnesses to abduction and sale ARE executed. Their testimony combines to create capital liability. Conspiring witnesses receive the punishment they intended for the accused — and these witnesses intended his death.
Rav Pappa
Objection to Ḥizkiyya: If you concede that final witnesses to a stubborn son ARE executed, shouldn't witnesses to the sale of an abductee also be executed by the same logic?
Rav Pappa
Resolution: Reframe the dispute — witnesses to the sale are clearly executed by all opinions. The actual disagreement concerns witnesses to the abduction when rendered conspiring before the sale witnesses testify.
Abaye's Summary Formula All agree: initial stubborn-son witnesses → NOT executed. All agree: final stubborn-son witnesses → ARE executed. Dispute: intermediate cases only.

🏛️ Rebellious Elder (זָקֵן מַמְרֵא) — Mishna Details

Trigger: An ordained elder issues a ruling contrary to his colleagues on a matter of Jewish law
↓
Court at Temple Mount entrance: Elder declares both positions — 'this is what I interpreted; this is what my colleagues interpreted'
↓
Court at Temple Courtyard entrance: Same declaration repeated — escalation continues if unresolved
↓
Great Sanhedrin (Chamber of Hewn Stone): Issues the definitive ruling — "from this place Torah goes forth to all Israel"
↓
If elder complies: Goes free — even praiseworthy for using the process
↓
If elder defies and rules contrary at home: Executed by strangulation — the crime is defying the court, not the original ruling itself
