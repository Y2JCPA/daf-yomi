Sanhedrin 50 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Rabbis, which category of priest's daughter receives burning for adultery?
A. Only the betrothed daughter
B. Only the married daughter
C. Both betrothed and married daughters
D. Neither — both are stoned like any adulteress
Correct: B — The Rabbis hold that only the married daughter of a priest is singled out for burning. A betrothed daughter is executed by stoning, like any other betrothed adulteress.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara prove that stoning is more severe than decapitation?
A. Stoning is explicitly listed first in the Torah
B. Stoning causes more pain and public shame
C. The subverter of an idolatrous city receives stoning, while the subverted city receives only decapitation
D. The blasphemer receives stoning, and blasphemy is worse than idolatry
Correct: C — The meisit (subverter) who incited the idolatrous city receives stoning, while the people of the city receive decapitation. Since the instigator always gets the harsher punishment, stoning must be more severe than decapitation.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What verbal analogy (gezerah shavah) links the priest's daughter's punishment to the betrothed adulteress?
A. The word 'fire' (אֵש) appears in both Leviticus 21 and Deuteronomy 22
B. The phrase 'her father' (אָבִיהָ) appears in both Leviticus 21:9 and Deuteronomy 22:21
C. The term 'harlot' (זוֹנָה) connects the two passages
D. The phrase 'evil in Israel' links both verses
Correct: B — The term 'her father' (אָבִיהָ) appears in Leviticus 21:9 regarding the priest's daughter and in Deuteronomy 22:21 regarding a betrothed adulteress. This verbal analogy links the two cases.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rabbi Shimon holds that the betrothed daughter of a priest who commits adultery is executed by burning. What is the basis for this view?
A. A special biblical verse explicitly mandates burning for betrothed priest's daughters
B. Since the Torah singles out the betrothed priest's daughter from ordinary betrothed women, it must be to make her punishment more severe — elevating stoning to burning
C. Rabbi Shimon holds that burning is actually less severe than stoning
D. The betrothed priest's daughter is compared to the married non-priest's daughter, who is stoned
Correct: B — Rabbi Shimon reasons that the Torah's singling out always elevates punishment. Since the betrothed priest's daughter is distinguished from ordinary betrothed women (who are stoned), she must receive the more severe burning.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the practical legal difference between the Rabbis' and Rabbi Shimon's positions?
A. There is no practical difference — both agree she dies
B. Rabbi Shimon exempts conspiring witnesses from punishment in these cases
C. If a betrothed priest's daughter is convicted of two capital crimes, Rabbis say she is stoned; Rabbi Shimon says she is burned
D. The Rabbis require two witnesses while Rabbi Shimon requires only one
Correct: C — When convicted of two capital crimes, one receives the more severe punishment. If the betrothed priest's daughter committed adultery (stoning per Rabbis, burning per Rabbi Shimon) plus another crime punishable by stoning, the Rabbis would stone her while Rabbi Shimon would burn her.
