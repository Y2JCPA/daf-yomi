Sanhedrin 12 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Talmud forbid intercalating the year during a famine?
A. The Torah explicitly prohibits it during times of scarcity
B. It would delay the omer offering and prolong the prohibition on the new grain crop
C. The Sanhedrin cannot convene properly when food is scarce
D. People cannot afford the extra month's expenses during a famine
Correct: B — Adding a month before Nisan delays the omer offering. Until the omer is brought, the new grain crop is prohibited. Intercalating during a famine would thus extend the food shortage — the opposite of relief.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was the significance of Ba'al Shalisha in Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's proof?
A. It was where the Sanhedrin convened to declare leap years
B. It was the slowest-ripening land in Eretz Yisrael, yet crops had fully ripened
C. It was the fastest-ripening land in Eretz Yisrael, yet only barley had ripened
D. Elisha performed a miracle there that caused crops to ripen out of season
Correct: C — Ba'al Shalisha had the fastest-ripening produce in Eretz Yisrael. That even there only barley (the omer crop) had ripened proves the year's spring produce was marginal — the year should have been intercalated, but famine prevented it.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did the Sages of Eretz Yisrael send a coded message to Rava about intercalation?
A. They were embarrassed to admit they had made a calendrical error
B. Rava was hard of hearing and could not receive spoken messages
C. Roman persecution forbade open communication about calendar decisions
D. The message was in Aramaic, which Rava did not understand
Correct: C — Under Roman rule, the Sages could not openly discuss or announce intercalation. They encoded the message using biblical allusions — 'a pair from Rakkath' (scholars from Tiberias) and 'netziv' (pillar = added month, from the twelve officers of Solomon).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Gemara's primary answer, why did Hezekiah seek divine forgiveness after intercalating the year?
A. He intercalated without convening the full Sanhedrin
B. He intercalated Nisan during Nisan itself, rather than intercalating Adar
C. He intercalated during a famine year in violation of the law
D. He intercalated three years in a row without authorization
Correct: B — Only Adar may be intercalated. Hezekiah mistakenly thought it was still Adar and added a month, but in fact it was already Nisan. The principle 'since it is fit' does not apply retroactively, making his intercalation improper.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Shmuel rule that the court may not intercalate the year on the 30th of Adar?
A. The 30th of Adar is Rosh Chodesh and legal proceedings are suspended
B. Since the 30th of Adar is fit to be Nisan, it is halakhically treated as Nisan
C. Purim festivities on the 30th render the court unfit to deliberate
D. There is insufficient time left before Passover to notify the public
Correct: B — Shmuel holds that since the 30th of Adar could legally be declared Rosh Chodesh Nisan, it is treated as Nisan for purposes of intercalation — and you cannot intercalate 'Adar' once the day is already considered Nisan.
