Sanhedrin 41 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara require three separate scriptural sources for the forewarning requirement — one each from the schools of Ḥizkiyya, Yishmael, and Yehuda HaNasi?
A. Three witnesses are required for capital cases, so three sources are needed
B. Each source covers a distinct penalty type (lashes/sword, stoning, burning) that cannot be derived by inference from the others
C. The three sages disputed whether forewarning was required at all
D. Each source applies to a different category of sinner: murderer, Shabbat violator, and adulterer
Correct: B — Each source is indispensable because different penalty types cannot be derived from one another. 'His sister' (lashes) doesn't cover capital cases; 'intentionally' covers only the lenient sword death; stoning needs two sources due to the dispute over whether stoning or burning is more severe.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥanan (following R' Yosei b. R' Yehuda), why are conspiring witnesses NOT executed when they falsely testify against a betrothed ḥavera?
A. A ḥavera's own testimony overrides that of ordinary witnesses
B. The witnesses can claim they intended only to forbid her to her husband — their testimony cannot be rendered conspiratory to impose death
C. Conspiring witnesses are exempt whenever the accused is a Torah scholar
D. The ḥavera herself cannot be executed, so the witnesses face no reciprocal punishment
Correct: B — A ḥavera requires no forewarning (R' Yosei b. R' Yehuda). The witnesses therefore never declared a capital penalty. Since they can claim 'we came only to forbid her to her husband,' their testimony cannot be made conspiratory — and testimony that cannot be rendered conspiratory is entirely invalid.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
One witness says the defendant killed the victim with a sword; the other says he used an ariran (club). What is Rav Ḥisda's ruling?
A. Testimony stands — the weapon type is a minor examination detail
B. The court investigates further before deciding
C. Testimony is void — this is a fundamental contradiction, not a peripheral discrepancy
D. Testimony stands only if both witnesses agree on time and place
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda rules this is not congruent testimony. Unlike minor details (sandal color, fig stems), the type of weapon describes the very act of killing — a fundamental contradiction that voids the testimony entirely.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did the Gemara initially question whether 'ben Zakkai' who examined witnesses about fig stems was Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai?
A. Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai was known to oppose the use of fine examinations
B. The Sanhedrin departed the Chamber of Hewn Stone (required for capital cases) 40 years before the Temple's destruction, before Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai's active years
C. Ben Zakkai was too old to serve on the Sanhedrin during capital trials
D. The baraita explicitly names a different person entirely
Correct: B — The Sanhedrin left the Chamber of Hewn Stone 40 years before the Temple's destruction, ending its authority over capital cases. Since Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai's prominence came only after the Temple's fall, he seemingly couldn't have served on a capital court. The Gemara ultimately resolves that the incident occurred during his student years, when he was still called 'ben Zakkai.'
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the dispute between Rav Sheshet and Rava, what is the correct ruling when two witnesses say 'we know' the interrogation answer but one says 'I do not know'?
A. Testimony is void — even one ignorant witness among three invalidates interrogation testimony
B. Testimony stands — a majority of knowing witnesses is sufficient for interrogations
C. The court may proceed but must discount that witness entirely
D. Testimony stands for examinations; for interrogations the ruling is disputed and unresolved
Correct: A — Rava corrects Rav Sheshet's reading: for interrogations (time, place), if even one witness says 'I do not know,' the testimony is void — because it cannot be rendered conspiratory without that foundational information. For examinations (peripheral details), the testimony stands even if one witness is ignorant.
