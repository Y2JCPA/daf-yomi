🌴 Palm vs. 🌲 Cedar: Moses Blessed, Balaam 'Blessed'

🌴 Palm Tree (Moses's Blessing)

  • Grows near water — sustained by source
  • Roots replenish: cut one, another grows
  • Numerous roots — firmly anchored
  • Bends with the wind but is not uprooted
  • Symbol of resilience, renewal, and abundance

🌲 Cedar Tree (Balaam's 'Blessing')

  • Does not stand near water — no lasting source
  • Cannot regenerate when cut — permanent loss
  • Few roots relative to its great height
  • Tall and impressive, but ultimately topples
  • Curse hidden inside a blessing

💃 Balaam's Six-Step Trap at Shittim

Step 1 — The Insight: Balaam tells Balak: 'The God of Israel despises lewdness; they crave linen garments and have none — use this.'
↓
Step 2 — The Setup: Balak erects enclosures with wall hangings from Mount Hermon to Beit HaYeshimot along Israel's path.
↓
Step 3 — The Lure: Old woman sits outside (non-threatening); young woman inside. Israelites are invited in after eating and drinking.
↓
Step 4 — The Wine: A jug of Ammonite wine is offered — Ammonite wine and gentile wine had not yet been prohibited at this time.
↓
Step 5 — The Demand: Once inhibitions lowered, the woman produces an idol from her lap and says: 'Worship this — it is nothing to you.'
↓
Step 6 — The Plague: Israel sins with Baal Peor; 24,000 die in the divine plague that follows.

⚔️ Balaam's Death — What Did He Seek There?

Gemara
Numbers 31:8 says Balaam was slain alongside the Midianite kings. But what was he doing there — why did he come to the battlefield?
Answer
He had come to collect his reward from Balak — and to witness the destruction of Israel through the plague he engineered with his counsel of immorality.
Gemara
Joshua 13:22 calls Balaam 'the diviner' (ha-kosem) — but earlier he appeared to be a prophet. Which was he?
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Initially he was a prophet. But after sinning, the spirit of prophecy was withdrawn from him, and he was degraded to the status of mere diviner.
Rav
At his execution, Israel applied all four court-mandated methods of death upon Balaam — stoning, burning, decapitation, and strangulation.
🗓️ Balaam's Age at Death A heretic asked Rabbi Ḥanina: How old was Balaam? From Psalms 55:24 — 'Bloody and deceitful men shall not live half their days' — Balaam died at approximately 33–34 years old.
