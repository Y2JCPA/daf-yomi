⚖️ Solomon Judges Joab

Solomon
Why did you kill Abner ben Ner?
Joab
I was the blood redeemer (גואל הדם) for my brother Asahel — Abner killed him!
Solomon
Abner was fleeing and could not aim with precision — it was self-defense. Set Abner aside. Why did you kill Amasa?
Joab
Amasa rebelled against the king — he delayed mustering Judah past the three-day deadline!
Solomon
Amasa found men beginning a new tractate. The words akh and rak exempt Torah study. YOU are the rebel — you followed Adonijah!
Verdict Both of Joab's defenses fail. He is executed for murder on Solomon's order.

⚔️ Did Abner Murder Asahel? The Blood Redeemer Claim

Joab
Abner murdered Asahel — I was justified as blood redeemer. And if Abner couldn't aim while fleeing, how did he strike the fifth rib so precisely? (II Sam 3:27)
Solomon
Abner was running away from Asahel who pursued him. He struck him only to save his own life — the Torah permits this. Asahel's own momentum drove him onto the spear.
Joab
"And Abner smote him in the fifth rib" (II Sam 2:23) — this was a deliberate killing, not an accident of momentum!
Solomon
Abner even warned Asahel to stop chasing him. He bears no guilt. Your blood-redeemer defense is rejected.
Halakhic Point A killer acting in self-defense is exempt from the blood redeemer — Joab therefore had no halakhic right to kill Abner.

📜 Amasa's Defense: Akh and Rak as Limiting Terms

"אַךְ חֲזַק וֶאֱמָץ" — Joshua 1:7; אַךְ and רַק restrict the scope of a command
Why Torah Study Overrides a Royal Command Joab accused Amasa of rebellion (Joshua 1:18): "Any man who rebels against your commandment…shall be put to death."

But Solomon pointed out that Amasa found the men of Judah beginning a new tractate of Torah study. He applied the rule that akh (אַךְ) and rak (רַק) are restrictive particles — they exclude cases from a general rule. Therefore, the verse's death penalty for disobeying the king does not apply when the delay is caused by Torah study.

Amasa was not guilty of rebellion. Joab, however, had openly followed Adonijah's rebellion — making Joab himself the true rebel.

☠️ The Four Death Penalties — Order of Severity

1 · Stoning · סְקִילָה

  • Most severe (Tanna Kamma)
  • Idolatry, blasphemy, Shabbat violation
  • Thrown from height, then stone dropped
  • R. Shimon: ranks this 2nd (below burning)

2 · Burning · שְׂרֵפָה

  • Second in severity (Tanna Kamma)
  • A kohen's daughter who commits adultery
  • Molten lead poured into the mouth
  • R. Shimon: ranks this 1st — greatest shame

3 · Decapitation · הֶרֶג

  • Third in severity
  • Murder; a city that becomes idolatrous (עיר הנדחת)
  • Death by the sword
  • Swift — no visible prolonged suffering

4 · Strangulation · חֶנֶק

  • Least severe (Tanna Kamma)
  • False prophet, rebellious elder, adulterous wife
  • Cloth wound around the neck
  • No wound left on the body

🔢 Rav Huna's Principle: When Does Mishna Order Matter?

Rava → Rav Seḥora → Rav Huna:
Wherever the Sages list items in a mishna, the order is generally not significant
↓
✅ Order IS Essential
1. Seven laundering substances (Nidda 61b) — doing them out of order or all at once is invalid
2. Four death penalties — Rabbi Shimon's dispute proves the order is deliberate
3. Yom Kippur service (Yoma 60a) — any change of order invalidates
4. Daily offering in Tamid — "This is the order" signals sequence
❌ Order NOT Essential
Ḥalitza ceremony: Rav Yehuda — it is a mitzva to follow the proper order, but switching steps does not invalidate the ḥalitza

Priestly garments: Trousers must come first (verse-based), but the listing order of the other garments is not halakhically binding
↓
Key test: Does a competing opinion dispute the order? If yes → the order is deliberate and significant
