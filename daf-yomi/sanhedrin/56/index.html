🗣️ The Blasphemy Trial Procedure

During trial: Witnesses testify using an appellation — 'May Yose strike Yose' — not the actual divine name
↓
At sentencing: One witness repeats the actual curse using the ineffable name, so judges can vote based on the real words
↓
Subsequent witnesses say only: 'I too heard as he did' — avoiding further utterance of the curse
↓
Liability requires 'blessing the Name with the Name' — the defendant must curse God explicitly using a divine name
↓
Source (Shmuel): Derived from nokev (Lev 24:16) — the doubling implies Name-with-Name requirement

📖 What Does נָקַב (Nokev) Mean?

Suggestion A
Nokev = making a hole, like 'vayyikkov in its lid' (II Kings 12:10) — perhaps blasphemy means desecrating God's name physically?
Gemara Refutes
Impossible: 'Name with Name' requires two names — you can't make a hole 'with' a name. Besides, the verse singles out a person who does it with his mouth.
Suggestion B
Nokev = uttering the ineffable name, like 'nikkevu by name' (Num 1:17) — perhaps mere utterance triggers liability?
Gemara Refutes
Still impossible: 'Name with Name' requires a second name. Uttering one name alone cannot satisfy the doubling.
Proof (Alt.)
'The son of the Israelite woman blasphemed [vayyikkov] the name AND cursed' (Lev 24:11) — nokev and cursing are equated, proving nokev = cursing.
Conclusion Nokev means to curse. Blasphemy liability requires cursing God's name using another divine name — 'Name with Name.'

🌍 Who Is Liable for Cursing God?

Rabbi Meir

  • Ish ish (anyone) includes gentiles
  • Gentiles liable even for cursing appellations
  • 'Convert and homeborn' → stoning (not beheading)
  • Derived from: Lev 24:15 broadens liability universally

Rabbis

  • Death only for cursing the ineffable name
  • Cursing appellations → lashes only
  • 'Convert and homeborn' → stoning for Jews
  • Gentiles liable for ineffable name only

Rav Yitzḥak Nappaḥa

  • Lev 24:15 (ish ish) needed only to include gentiles cursing appellations
  • Per Rabbis: gentiles not liable for appellations
  • Rav Meyasha disagrees: Ben Noah liable for any divine name
  • 'Convert/homeborn' limits stoning to Jews alone

🔍 Genesis 2:16 — Word-by-Word Noahide Source

וַיְצַו ה' אֱלֹהִים עַל הָאָדָם לֵאמֹר
'And the Lord God commanded the man, saying' (Gen 2:16)
↓
וַיְצַו (commanded)
Courts of Judgment — 'For I have known him, to command his household' (Gen 18:19)
ה' (the Lord)
Prohibition of Blasphemy — 'Who blasphemes the name of the Lord shall die' (Lev 24:16)
אֱלֹהִים (God)
Prohibition of Idol Worship — 'You shall have no other gods' (Ex 20:3)
↓
עַל הָאָדָם (upon the man)
Prohibition of Bloodshed — 'Whoever sheds the blood of man' (Gen 9:6)
לֵאמֹר (saying)
Forbidden Sexual Relations — 'Saying, if a man sends his wife…' (Jer 3:1)
מִכֹּל עֵץ תֹּאכַל (you may eat)
Prohibition of Robbery — implies only what is permitted; stealing is excluded
↓
Limb from Living Animal added explicitly in Gen 9:4 — 'But flesh with its life, its blood, you shall not eat'
