📚 Teaching Torah vs. Withholding It — Proverbs 11:26

Withholds Torah from a Student

  • Source: 'He who withholds bar (grain/Torah), the people yikkevuhu' (Prov. 11:26)
  • Ulla bar Yishmael: Such a teacher is 'perforated like a sieve'
  • Proof: vayyikkov (boring a hole) in 2 Kings 12:10 uses the same root
  • 'The people' who curse = fetuses in the womb, from Gen. 25:23

Teaches Torah to a Student

  • Source: 'Blessing upon the head of one who provides' (Prov. 11:26)
  • Rava citing Rav Sheshet: He receives blessings like Joseph
  • Rav Sheshet: Teaches Torah in This World → merits to teach in World-to-Come
  • Proof: 'He who satisfies [umarveh] shall be satisfied himself [yoreh]' (Prov. 11:25)

🪦 Who Were the Dead Ezekiel Revived? (Ezekiel 37)

Rav
Descendants of Ephraim who miscalculated the end of Egyptian slavery, left 30 years early, and were killed by Philistines (Ps. 78:9 — 'armed Ephraimites turned back on the day of battle')
Shmuel
Those who denied resurrection of the dead — their own words prove it: 'Our bones are dried and our hope is lost' (Ezek. 37:11)
R. Yirmeya bar Abba
People who lacked even the moist residue of a mitzva — 'Dry bones, hear the word of the Lord' (Ezek. 37:4). Even while alive they were like dry bones.
R. Yitzchak Nappacha
Those who filled the Sanctuary with repugnant creatures and creeping animals — 'every form of creeping animal and repugnant animal, and all the idols of the house of Israel' (Ezek. 8:10)
Rabbi Yochanan
The Dura Valley dead — corpses filled the land from the Eshel River to Rabat, slain when Nebuchadnezzar demanded they bow to his idol. God sent Ezekiel to revive them at the moment Hananiah, Mishael & Azariah were cast into the furnace.
Point of Agreement Rabbi Eliezer: The revived dead stood, recited song — 'The Lord kills with justice and gives life with mercy' — and then died again. This was a miraculous sign, not the permanent eschatological resurrection.

🔥 Six Miracles on the Day of Hananiah, Mishael & Azariah

Miracle 1: The furnace rose from its sunken position up to ground level
↓
Miracle 2: The furnace was breached — its walls burst open supernaturally
↓
Miracle 3: Its bottom fell out — fire had nowhere to contain the three men
↓
Miracle 4: The officials who cast Hananiah, Mishael & Azariah into the furnace were themselves burned by the flames
↓
Miracle 5: Four ranks of officials from enemy monarchies were burned (Dan. 3:2–3) — they had come to witness the punishment
↓
Miracle 6: God commanded Ezekiel to simultaneously revive the Dura Valley dead — when they rose, Nebuchadnezzar's idol fell on its face

✨ Will the Resurrected Righteous Die Again?

School of Eliyahu: The righteous God will resurrect will not return to dust
Proof: 'He who remains in Zion shall be called holy' (Isa. 4:3)
↓
Challenge: When God renews His world — 'The Lord alone shall be exalted on that day' (Isa. 2:11) — what will the righteous do? Water will cover the world.
↓
Answer
God gives them wings like eagles — they soar above the waters (Ps. 93:4)
Objection from Ezekiel
The dead Ezekiel revived did die afterward — Rabbi Eliezer: they recited song, then died
↓
Resolution: Ezekiel's revival was a temporary miraculous sign, not the final resurrection. The school of Eliyahu speaks of the permanent eschatological resurrection of the righteous.
