Sanhedrin 92 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Ulla bar Yishmael, what punishment awaits a teacher who withholds Torah from a student?
A. He is blessed like Joseph in the World-to-Come
B. He is perforated like a sieve
C. He is exiled from his community
D. His students curse him openly in the marketplace
Correct: B — Ulla bar Yishmael derives from the parallel root נקב (nakav) — 'yikkevuhu' in Prov. 11:26 and 'vayyikkov' (boring a hole) in 2 Kings 12:10 — that withholding Torah causes the teacher to be perforated like a sieve.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rabbi Elazar teaches that 'great is knowledge' because of how its verse in Samuel is structured. What is the parallel he draws?
A. Knowledge and the Torah both appear 613 times in Scripture
B. Both knowledge and the Temple are 'placed between two names of God' in their verses
C. Both knowledge and the Temple were created before the world
D. Both are compared to water in Prophets
Correct: B — 'A God of knowledge is the Lord' (1 Sam. 2:3) flanks da'at with two divine names — just as Exodus 15:17 flanks the Temple with two divine names. Rabbi Elazar concludes: as Temple-building is great, so too is da'at.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Shmuel, who were the dead that Ezekiel revived in his vision of the dry bones?
A. Descendants of Ephraim who left Egypt 30 years early
B. People who filled the Temple with creeping animals
C. Those with not even a moist residue of a mitzva
D. People who denied the resurrection of the dead
Correct: D — Shmuel identifies them as deniers of resurrection — their own quoted words prove it: 'Our bones are dried and our hope is lost' (Ezek. 37:11). Their revival was measure-for-measure: those who denied resurrection were themselves resurrected.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does the School of Eliyahu teach will happen to the righteous during the period when God renews the world?
A. They will enter a special chamber in Gan Eden until the waters recede
B. They will be temporarily returned to dust and re-resurrected afterward
C. God will give them wings like eagles so they can soar above the floodwaters
D. They will be transported to the Land of Israel which will remain dry
Correct: C — The School of Eliyahu teaches that the righteous who are permanently resurrected will not return to dust. When God's renewal covers the world with water, God will give them wings like eagles to soar above — derived from Psalms 93:4.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What song did the dead revived by Ezekiel recite, according to Rabbi Eliezer, before they died again?
A. 'Hear O Israel, the Lord our God, the Lord is One'
B. 'The Lord kills with justice and gives life with mercy'
C. 'Who is like You among the mighty, O Lord'
D. 'Give thanks to the Lord, for He is good, His kindness endures forever'
Correct: B — Rabbi Eliezer teaches that the dead revived by Ezekiel stood on their feet, recited the song 'The Lord kills with justice and gives life with mercy,' and then died. This demonstrates that their revival was temporary — a miraculous sign, not the final resurrection.
