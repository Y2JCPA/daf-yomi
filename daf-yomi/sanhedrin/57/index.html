📖 Overview

כָּל שׁוֹפֵךְ דַּם הָאָדָם, בָּאָדָם דָּמוֹ יִשָּׁפֵךְ — בראשית ט:ו
⚖️ Core Principle of Daf 57 The Noahide laws carry the death penalty — but who is executed, for what, and under what procedure? One judge, one witness, no forewarning. The Gemara debates which violations actually trigger execution, then explores edge cases: killing a fetus, women as judges, and a gentile who violates a Jewish woman.

🔢 How Many Noahide Violations Carry the Death Penalty?

Rav Yosef
A Noahide is executed for three violations — the mnemonic ג׳ ש׳ ר׳: forbidden sexual relations, bloodshed, and blasphemy. This is what they say in the study hall.
Rav Sheshet
That tradition is flawed. Only bloodshed has an explicit execution verse. The others cannot be derived by simple analogy without becoming a contradiction. Reject that version — the correct count is four: add idol worship.
Rav Huna & Rav Yehuda
(Students of Rav)
We say all seven Noahide laws carry capital punishment. The Torah revealed the penalty explicitly for one — bloodshed — and we extend it to all seven by analogy. This is the full ruling.
Ruling The Students of Rav prevail: capital punishment applies to all seven Noahide laws, derived by analogy from the explicit bloodshed verse (Genesis 9:6).

👨‍⚖️ Jewish Court Procedure vs. Noahide Court Procedure

Jewish Capital Law

  • Requires 23 judges for capital cases
  • Requires two witnesses minimum
  • Forewarning (hasra'ah) required
  • Defendant must acknowledge the warning
  • Women may not serve as judges or witnesses
  • Relatives may not testify against defendant

Noahide Court

  • Only one judge required
  • Only one witness required
  • No forewarning needed (derived: an animal cannot warn)
  • No acknowledgment required
  • Women may not judge or testify (derived from 'man')
  • Relatives may testify against defendant

👶 Is a Noahide Executed for Killing a Fetus?

Book of Aggadot in Rav's study hall: Rabbi Yishmael teaches that a Noahide is executed for killing a fetus
↓
What is the reason for Rabbi Yishmael's ruling?
↓
Genesis 9:6: "One who sheds the blood of man, within man (ba-adam), his blood shall be shed" — the fetus inside is also 'man'
↓
School of Menashe (First Tanna)
Disagrees. The verse refers only to full murder (decapitation). Fetus not included — Noahide not executed.
Rabbi Yishmael
"Blood of man within man" includes the fetus. A Noahide who kills a fetus is executed.

💑 Gentile Who Engages with a Jewish Woman — Which Law Governs?

The Baraita: Leviticus 18:6 uses the double form "ish ish" (no one) — the doubling extends the prohibition to gentiles who engage with Jewish women.
↓
But gentiles already have this from Genesis 2:16! The Gemara resolves: Genesis covers their own women; Leviticus 18:6 covers Jewish women specifically.
↓
Rav Naḥman bar Avuh: A gentile who violates a forbidden Jewish woman must be judged by a Sanhedrin, with two witnesses and forewarning — Jewish procedural standards apply.
↓
Rabbi Yoḥanan (preferred ruling): The verse is needed only for a betrothed young Jewish woman (na'arah me'orasah) — a legal status that does not exist for gentile women.
↓
Key distinction (Rabbi Ḥanina): Gentile women can attain the status of a married woman (after intercourse) but NOT a betrothed one. Hence this unique case requires Jewish law.
