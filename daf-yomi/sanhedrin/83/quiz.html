Sanhedrin 83 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
From which halakhic category does the Gemara derive the punishment of death at the hand of Heaven for eating tevel (untithed produce)?
A. Notar (leftover offerings)
B. Teruma (priestly portion)
C. Nevelah (improperly slaughtered meat)
D. Kodshim (consecrated items)
Correct: B — The Gemara derives the punishment for eating tevel from teruma using a gezerah shavah (verbal analogy) on the word חִלּוּל (desecration). Teruma is preferred over notar because tevel and teruma share more common features: both are produce-based, both involve separation obligations, and both can become permitted through corrective action.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rav disagrees with the baraita about a non-priest who eats teruma. What is Rav's ruling?
A. Death at the hand of Heaven, agreeing with the baraita
B. Exempt entirely from punishment
C. Flogged (lashes), not liable for death
D. Must bring a guilt-offering
Correct: C — Rav holds that a non-priest who eats teruma is flogged but does NOT receive death at the hand of Heaven. Rav argues that the phrase 'I am the Lord Who sanctifies them' concludes the death penalty clause before the non-priest teruma case, placing it outside that scope.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
A tevul yom is a priest who has immersed in a mikveh but not yet waited for nightfall to complete his purification. If he performs the Temple service, what is his punishment?
A. No punishment — immersion is sufficient for service
B. Prohibition only; no death penalty
C. Death at the hand of Heaven
D. Lashes and an atonement offering
Correct: C — The Gemara derives from a verse in Leviticus — re-applied since it is not needed for the impure priest case (derived separately) — that a tevul yom who performs Temple service is liable for death at the hand of Heaven.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which of the following priests is explicitly NOT punished with death at the hand of Heaven for performing the Temple service?
A. A priest lacking priestly vestments
B. A priest drunk with wine
C. An acute mourner (onen) on the day of a relative's death
D. A priest with unwashed hands and feet
Correct: C — The baraita explicitly distinguishes: an uncircumcised priest, an acute mourner (onen), and a priest performing service while seated all receive prohibition only — not death at the hand of Heaven. No explicit death-penalty verse is applied to these cases.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara establish that a priest lacking priestly vestments who performs Temple service is liable for death at the hand of Heaven?
A. A direct verse in Numbers states 'they shall die without their vestments'
B. From the verse 'you shall bind them [the vestments] on Aaron' — priesthood is active only when vestments are worn
C. By analogy to a non-priest, since without vestments he is like a non-priest
D. From Ezekiel's rebuke of the priests in the Temple
Correct: C — Rabbi Abbahu derives from Exodus 29:9 ('and you shall bind the priestly sash on them… and their priesthood shall be theirs') that priesthood is operative only when the vestments are upon them. Without vestments, the priest's status is like a non-priest — who is explicitly liable for death — making him liable for death at the hand of Heaven.
