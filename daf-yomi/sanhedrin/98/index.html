⚖️ Will Redemption Come Through Repentance or Divine Decree?

Rabbi Eliezer
Israel must repent before redemption can come — 'If you return, Israel, says the Lord, return to Me' (Jeremiah 4:1). Teshuvah is the prerequisite.
Rabbi Yehoshua
God has set an appointed time — 'In its time I will hasten it' (Isaiah 60:22). Even if Israel does not repent, the end will come regardless.
Rabbi Eliezer
The verse speaks of a conditional promise: repentance unlocks redemption. Without return to God, the appointed time remains sealed.
Rabbi Yehoshua
The 'hasten it' clause overrides: if Israel merits, He hastens; if not, still 'in its time' — the end is guaranteed by divine decree alone.
Resolution Both paths exist: if Israel repents, God hastens redemption; if not, it still arrives at its appointed time — 'in its time I will hasten it' contains both possibilities.

📋 Signs That Precede the Messiah's Coming

Social Collapse

  • The arrogant will cease from Israel (Ze'eiri/R. Ḥanina)
  • All judges and officers gone — no autonomous rule (R. Simlai b. R. Elazar)
  • Torah wisdom steadily diminishing generation to generation (R. Yoḥanan)
  • A fish cannot be found for the sick (R. Ḥanina)

Political Signs

  • Rome's contemptuous kingdom ceases from Israel (R. Ḥama bar Ḥanina)
  • Evil Roman kingdom spreads through Eretz Yisrael for nine months (Rav)
  • Rome's gate falls three times — rebuilt twice, not a third (R. Yosei ben Kisma)
  • Persian sorcerers cease when arrogant cease (Rav Pappa)

Spiritual Signs

  • Entire generation innocent OR entirely guilty (R. Yoḥanan)
  • Mountains of Israel give fruit — the clearest sign (R. Abba)
  • Jerusalem redeemed only through righteousness (Ulla)
  • Waters of Cave of Pamyas turn to blood (R. Yosei ben Kisma's sign)

↓

😰 'Let the Messiah Come — But After My Death!'

Ulla
Let the Messiah come, but let me not see him — the suffering preceding his arrival is too great to bear.
Rabba
Same — let him come after my death. I fear lest my sins forfeit the protection Torah study would otherwise grant me.
Rav Yosef
Let the Messiah come, and may I merit to sit in the shadow of his donkey's dung! (I embrace even the hardships to witness the redemption.)
Abaye → Rabba
Why fear? Torah and good deeds protect from the birth-pangs of the Messiah — as R. Elazar taught his students: 'Engage in Torah and charity!'
Rabba
I fear lest sin cause that protection to be forfeited — as R. Ya'akov bar Idi taught: God's promises can be suspended by one's own transgressions.
R. Yoḥanan's Fear It is written: 'Ask now whether a man gives birth' — even the Holy One, as it were, will agonize like a woman in labor over the suffering of Israel in that era.

🌳 R. Abba's Sign: The Most Explicit Harbinger of Redemption

וְאַתֶּם הָרֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל עַנְפְּכֶם תִּתֵּנוּ וּפֶרְיְכֶם תִּשְׂאוּ לְעַמִּי יִשְׂרָאֵל — יחזקאל לו:ח
Rabbi Abba Teaches You have no more explicit sign of the end of days than this: when the mountains of Israel give forth their branches and yield their fruit to the Jewish people returning to the land — as it is stated: 'But you, mountains of Israel, you shall give your branches and yield your fruit to My people Israel, for they are close to coming.' (Ezekiel 36:8). When the Land of Israel produces abundantly for its returning people, the end is truly near.
