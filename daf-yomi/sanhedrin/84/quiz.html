Sanhedrin 84 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
From where does the Gemara derive that an acute mourner (onen) may not perform Temple service?
A. A verbal analogy from the term 'desecration' applied to a blemished priest
B. From the verse about the High Priest commanded to remain in the Temple despite his parents' death
C. From the verse 'stand and minister' which excludes those in a state of acute mourning
D. From the juxtaposition of a mourner and an impure priest in Leviticus
Correct: B — The Gemara derives the prohibition for an acute mourner from the verse about the High Priest, who was commanded to stay in the Temple when his parents died — implying that ordinarily, an onen must not serve.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the key disagreement between Rabbi Yishmael and Rabbi Akiva regarding verbal analogies (gezerah shavah) in this context?
A. Rabbi Akiva allows deriving yumat from yamut; Rabbi Yishmael does not
B. Rabbi Akiva derives yumat from yumat only; Rabbi Yishmael also derives yumat from yamut
C. Both agree on the method of analogy but disagree on the resulting punishment
D. Rabbi Yishmael rejects all gezerah shavah; Rabbi Akiva accepts any form
Correct: B — Rabbi Akiva holds one may only derive yumat (passive) from yumat (passive). Rabbi Yishmael holds one may also derive yumat from yamut (active form), allowing a broader application of verbal analogies.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the new Mishna on 84b, which of the following is NOT listed among those executed by strangulation?
A. One who strikes his father or mother
B. One who abducts a Jewish person
C. A false witness (ed zomem) who plotted murder
D. A rebellious elder who defies the Great Sanhedrin
Correct: C — A false witness who plotted murder is executed by the same method that would have befallen the victim — not automatically strangulation. The Mishna lists: one who strikes parents, kidnapper, rebellious elder, and false prophet.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The Gemara concludes that one is liable for striking his parent only if he:
A. Causes the parent permanent injury
B. Acts with clear intent to harm the parent
C. Draws blood — causes a wound
D. Strikes in the presence of witnesses who issued a formal warning
Correct: C — From the juxtaposition of striking a person with striking an animal (Lev 24:21), the Gemara derives that just as animal-striking requires actual damage for liability, striking a parent requires drawing blood — an actual wound.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Rav not allow his son to extract a thorn from him, and what does the Gemara say distinguishes this from a stranger treating him?
A. A son is categorically forbidden from any medical contact with a parent; strangers are always permitted
B. Rav held that extracting thorns is inherently forbidden; strangers may do so because they are paid professionals
C. Concern that the son might accidentally wound him, which is more severe than a stranger accidentally wounding him
D. Rav's son was not a physician, so the concern was incompetence rather than religious prohibition
Correct: C — If a stranger accidentally wounds while treating, it is merely a general prohibition. If a son accidentally wounds his father, he has struck his parent — a far more severe prohibition. This asymmetry explains Rav's caution.
