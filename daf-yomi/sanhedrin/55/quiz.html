Sanhedrin 55 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the baraita, who is liable for bestiality in both typical and atypical manners?
A. Men only — women are liable for typical manner alone
B. Both men and women for both manners
C. Women only — men are liable for typical manner alone
D. Neither — the baraita restricts liability to typical manner for both
Correct: B — The baraita rejects both Rav Naḥman and Rav Pappa and rules that both manners are punishable for both sexes, derived from 'as with a woman.'
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rava's reason that an animal used in bestiality is killed, while an animal that was merely worshipped is not?
A. Bestiality causes greater public shame than idol worship
B. The animal enjoyed the transgression of bestiality
C. Bestiality always involves two witnesses; worship may not
D. The animal in bestiality is considered an active participant under Torah law
Correct: B — Rava derives that the animal enjoyed the transgression, warranting its death. Trees (which do not enjoy) are destroyed for other reasons, but living animals require this 'enjoyment' criterion.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rav Hamnuna's unresolved dilemma regarding a Jew who unwittingly commits bestiality?
A. Whether he is required to bring a sin-offering for the animal's benefit
B. Whether the animal is stoned when there is shame but no calamity (no execution of perpetrator)
C. Whether unwitting bestiality is treated more leniently than intentional idol worship
D. Whether partial intercourse qualifies as bestiality in an unwitting case
Correct: B — Since the Jew acted unwittingly, he is not executed — there is no calamity. Rav Hamnuna asks whether shame alone, without the calamity of the perpetrator's execution, is sufficient to stone the animal.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why is a minor girl (3 years + 1 day) who commits bestiality exempt from execution, yet the animal is still stoned?
A. Her act is not considered intercourse at all, but the animal is punished for proximity
B. She acted unwittingly, and unwitting acts do not generate personal liability
C. She acted intentionally, but the Merciful One exempts her due to her age — this compassion does not extend to the animal
D. The animal is only stoned if a second witness confirms the act, which always occurs in such cases
Correct: C — The Gemara explains: since she acted intentionally, there is calamity. The Merciful One has compassion on her due to her youth, but that pity does not spare the animal.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Mishna introducing the law of blasphemy at the end of Daf 55, when is one liable?
A. Whenever one speaks disrespectfully about Torah scholars or the Temple
B. Only when one explicitly pronounces the Divine Name and curses it
C. When one denies divine providence in front of two witnesses
D. When one curses Israel, the king, or the High Priest
Correct: B — The Mishna states: one who blasphemes is not liable unless he utters (pronounces) the Divine Name and curses it — a strict standard requiring explicit pronunciation of the Name.
