⚖️ Two Ways of Lying: The Liability Dispute

Rav Naḥman bar Rav Ḥisda
A woman has two manners of lying — vaginal and anal. She is liable for both when committing bestiality. A man with a human male has only one typical manner, so only liable for that.
Rav Pappa
On the contrary! A woman's typical manner is vaginal — she is only liable for that, not anal. A man is active in both manners and is therefore liable for both.
Baraita
Neither view is sustained. The baraita states: one who commits bestiality — whether in a typical or atypical manner — is fully liable. Both man and woman are liable for both manners.
Outcome The baraita rejects both amoraic positions and treats all manners of bestiality as equally liable for both sexes.

♀️ Man vs. Woman: Bestiality Liability — Three Views

Rav Naḥman bar Rav Ḥisda

  • Woman: liable for BOTH vaginal and anal (she has two manners)
  • Man (with male): only ONE typical manner
  • Therefore man is liable for typical manner only

Rav Pappa (Objects)

  • Woman: typical = vaginal; liable for THAT manner only
  • Man: active in both ways; liable for BOTH
  • "On the contrary" — her typical manner limits her liability

Baraita (Final Ruling)

  • Both man and woman: liable for typical AND atypical
  • Neither amoraic view prevails
  • "As with a woman" — any act applicable to a woman counts

🔪 Partial Intercourse: Does the Initial Stage (הערה) Count?

Ravina
If one performs only the initial stage of homosexual intercourse — insertion without completion — what is the halakha? Is he liable?
Rava
The verse says 'as with a woman' — any act considered intercourse with a woman renders him liable. The initial stage transmits impurity with a woman, so it counts equally here.
Rava (re: animals)
The verse about uncovering a sister's nakedness uses he'era (הערה) — alluding to initial-stage intercourse. This superfluous verse exists solely to teach: initial-stage bestiality is also punishable.
Rav Ashi (re: self)
Performing initial-stage intercourse on oneself is impossible with an erect member — only conceivable with a flaccid one, which is subject to a separate halakhic dispute.
Key Term — הערה The initial stage of prohibited intercourse (he'era) is halakhically significant. Liability attaches even without completion of the act.

🌍 Gentile Commits Bestiality — Must the Animal Be Stoned?

Gentile commits bestiality
Must the animal be killed? Two reasons needed?
↓
Possible Rule
Both calamity (perpetrator's execution) AND shame are required → gentile has no court death → no killing?
Rav Sheshet's Answer
Baraita: Trees used for idolatry are destroyed — how much more a living animal that enabled sin!
↓
Challenge: Jew who bows to his own animal — is that animal also killed?
↓
Abaye
Worship: Jew has no execution → no shame attached to animal
Bestiality: both calamity + shame → animal killed
Rava
Bestiality: animal enjoyed the transgression → must die
Worship: animal felt no enjoyment → not killed

🚫 New Mishna: The Law of Blasphemy

המגדף אינו חייב עד שיפרש את השם
Mishna — New Chapter Begins One who blasphemes (מגדף) is not liable unless he explicitly pronounces the Divine Name and curses it. Rabbi Yehoshua ben Korḥa adds: during court testimony, witnesses use a substitute name so the actual Name is not uttered in the proceeding. Only in the final verdict is the true name-curse assessed.
