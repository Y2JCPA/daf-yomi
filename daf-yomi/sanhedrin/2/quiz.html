Sanhedrin 2 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the mishna, what is the minimum court size for cases of robbery and personal injury?
A. One judge
B. Three judges
C. Twenty-three judges
D. Seventy-one judges
Correct: B — Robbery and personal injury require three judges — the same number as monetary law — but they must be ordained expert judges (mumḥin), unlike admissions and loans.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What key distinction does the Gemara establish between cases of admissions/loans and cases of robbery/injury?
A. Admissions require more judges than robbery
B. Robbery cases can be decided by a single judge after the fact
C. Admissions and loans may be judged by any three laypeople; robbery requires ordained expert judges
D. Loans require twenty-three judges while robbery needs only three
Correct: C — The Gemara explains the tanna specified robbery separately to teach that it requires mumḥin — ordained experts — derived from the word 'Elohim' in Exodus 22:8. Admissions and loans have no such requirement.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the mishna's derivation of twenty-three judges, what role does the narrative of the spies play?
A. It provides the model for how judges should deliberate
B. It establishes that a minimum 'congregation' consists of ten men
C. It teaches that only members of a tribe may judge their own
D. It shows that capital cases require a unanimous verdict
Correct: B — God said of the ten faithless spies, 'How long shall I bear with this evil congregation?' — proving that ten men constitute a minimum legal 'congregation,' the basis for counting 2 × 10 = 20 in the derivation of 23.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Neḥemya, what is the minimum population a city needs to support a lesser Sanhedrin?
A. 120 men
B. 230 men
C. 71 men
D. 180 men
Correct: B — Rabbi Neḥemya requires 230 men — corresponding to Moses' ministers of tens, so that each of the 23 judges represents ten citizens. The Tanna Kamma requires only 120.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which of the following cases requires a court of seventy-one judges according to the mishna?
A. An ox sentenced to stoning for killing a person
B. A case of personal injury and damages
C. Declaring an entire city as an idolatrous city
D. Intercalation of the year
Correct: C — An idolatrous city (ir hanidaḥat) requires the Great Sanhedrin of 71 judges, along with cases involving an entire sinning tribe, a false prophet, or a High Priest in a capital case. Ox stoning requires 23; injury requires 3; intercalation requires 3.
