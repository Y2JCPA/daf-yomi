🔥 Core Dispute: Which Bat Kohen Is Executed by Burning?

Rabbi Yishmael
Only the betrothed daughter of a priest is executed by burning. A married bat kohen who commits adultery is executed by strangulation — like any other married adulteress. The verbal analogy limits the ruling to a young betrothed woman.
Rabbi Akiva
Both the betrothed and the married daughter of a priest are executed by burning. The extra vav ('and the daughter') in Lev. 21:9 includes married daughters. The verbal analogy only excludes the unmarried bat kohen.
The Verse (Lev. 21:9) "וּבַת אִישׁ כֹּהֵן כִּי תֵחֵל לִזְנוֹת — And the daughter of a priest, when she profanes herself by playing the harlot, she profanes her father; she shall be burned with fire."

📜 Interpreting Rabbi Eliezer: 'With Her Father' vs. 'With Her Father-in-Law'

Ravin (citing R. Yosei b. R. Ḥanina)
'With her father' = under her father's authority (betrothed). 'With her father-in-law' = under her father-in-law's authority (married). Where her sin's inherent punishment is more severe than burning, she gets the bat kohen penalty (burning); where it is less severe, she gets the standard penalty.
Ravina
Per the Rabbis (married = burning, betrothed = stoning): reverse the wording of Rabbi Eliezer. 'With her father' in the text actually refers to the married state (burning); 'with her father-in-law' refers to the betrothed state (stoning). The labels are switched.
Rava's Objection Why interpret the two clauses differently? Either read both literally (intercourse with her father / father-in-law directly) or both figuratively (authority). You cannot mix methods — and literal reading makes this irrelevant to bat kohen specifically!

👨‍⚖️ Rabbi Yishmael vs. Rabbi Akiva — Full Position Comparison

Rabbi Yishmael

  • Betrothed bat kohen → 🔥 Burning
  • Married bat kohen → 🪢 Strangulation
  • Verbal analogy limits ruling: only young betrothed woman
  • Paramour: 'to his brother, not his sister' — no reversal
  • Extra vav ('and the daughter') teaches a separate law (via Shmuel's father's baraita)

Rabbi Akiva

  • Betrothed bat kohen → 🔥 Burning
  • Married bat kohen → 🔥 Burning
  • Extra vav in 'and the daughter' includes married women
  • Verbal analogy only excludes unmarried bat kohen
  • Priests must be holy — stringency extends to married daughters

🔗 How the Gezerah Shavah Derives Father–Daughter Intercourse

Step 1 — Base verse (Lev. 20:14): 'If a man takes a wife and her mother… it is zimmah (lewdness); they shall be burned with fire.' Burning established for man-wife-mother case.
↓
Step 2 — Second verse (Lev. 18:17): The identical word zimmah appears regarding intercourse with a woman and her daughter. Links the two cases verbally.
↓
Step 3 — Gezerah Shavah applied: Via verbal analogy (Rava citing Rav Yitzḥak bar Avudimi), the burning penalty is extended to intercourse with one's own daughter — though never stated explicitly in the Torah.
↓
Step 4 — Consequence: Since father–daughter prohibition is derived only through analogy (not written explicitly), the verse in Lev. 21:9 is needed to confirm that the bat kohen's burning applies even in that scenario.
↓
Step 5 — Result: Lev. 21:9 is therefore not redundant — it covers edge cases (e.g., she is 'with her father') that would otherwise not clearly fall under the burning penalty.
