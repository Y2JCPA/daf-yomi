Sanhedrin 67 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why does the mishna specify that the inciter is 'an ordinary person'?
A. To indicate that a prophet who incites receives a different penalty (strangulation per R. Shimon)
B. To exclude women from the inciter category
C. Because only ordinary people are tempted by idol worship
D. To contrast with the subverter who must be a community leader
Correct: A — The Gemara infers that the mishna's phrase 'ordinary person' implies that a prophet-inciter is treated differently — Rabbi Shimon holds a prophet who subverts gets strangulation, not stoning. The mishna follows Rabbi Shimon's view.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the acronym כְּשָׁפִים (keshafim) stand for according to Rabbi Yoḥanan?
A. כּוֹחֵשׁ פַּמַלְיָא שֶׁל מַעְלָה — contradicts the heavenly entourage
B. כּוֹבֵשׁ פַּחַד שֶׁל מַעְלָה — suppresses fear of Heaven
C. כּוֹנֵס פְּרִי שֶׁל מַעְלָה — gathers heavenly fruit
D. כּוֹפֵר פְּנֵי שֶׁל מַעְלָה — denies the Divine face
Correct: A — Rabbi Yoḥanan explains keshafim as a notarikon for כּוֹחֵשׁ פַּמַלְיָא שֶׁל מַעְלָה — sorcery 'contradicts' or 'mocks' the heavenly entourage, appearing to override the natural laws God established.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Abaye, what is the legal status of one who merely 'deceives the eyes' (makes sorcery appear to happen without doing it)?
A. Liable to stoning — equivalent to real sorcery
B. Fully permitted — same as Rav Ḥanina and Rav Oshaya
C. Exempt from execution but still prohibited
D. Liable to lashes but not death
Correct: C — Abaye establishes three tiers: real sorcery → stoning; eye-deception → exempt from execution but prohibited; and academic/permitted use (like Rav Ḥanina and Rav Oshaya creating a calf via Sefer Yetzirah) → fully permitted.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why was Rabbi Ḥanina unafraid when a woman tried to gather dust from under his feet for a curse?
A. He held a charm that nullified sorcery
B. He trusted divine providence — 'there is none else besides Him' — protects the righteous from harm
C. He did not believe she was a genuine sorceress
D. He planned to reverse the curse using the same dust
Correct: B — Rabbi Ḥanina cited Deuteronomy 4:35 — 'there is none else besides Him' — applying it even to sorcery. The Gemara notes this was specifically true for Rabbi Ḥanina due to his extraordinary merit; others cannot be so complacent.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What did Rabbi Elazar ben Azarya say when Rabbi Akiva expounded on the frog plague?
A. 'Your verbal analogy contradicts the plain meaning of the verse'
B. 'Akiva, why do you involve yourself in aggadah? Return to laws of nega'im and tent-impurity!'
C. 'The verse clearly speaks of many frogs from the beginning'
D. 'You have misread the vowels — the word is plural throughout'
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar ben Azarya rebuked Rabbi Akiva for straying into aggadic homiletics rather than focusing on his areas of halakhic mastery (nega'im and ohalot). This reflects the Talmudic value of scholars staying within their expertise.
