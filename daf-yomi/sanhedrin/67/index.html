⚖️ Three Capital Transgressors on Daf 67

Meisit — Inciter

  • Ordinary person seducing an individual
  • Hidden witnesses uniquely permitted
  • No prior warning required
  • Executed by stoning
  • Ben Setada hanged on Passover eve

Madiaḥ — Subverter

  • Seduces an entire city to idol worship
  • Says: 'Let us go worship idols'
  • Halakha same as inciter at root (Rav)
  • Debate: ordinary vs. prophet penalty
  • Executed by stoning

Mekhashef — Sorcerer

  • Real sorcery → liable (stoning)
  • Eye-deception → exempt but prohibited
  • Academic use → permitted (Rav Ḥanina)
  • Keshafim = contradicts heavenly entourage
  • Cannot harm the truly righteous

🕵️ Entrapping the Inciter — The Hidden Witnesses Procedure

The inciter privately tells one man: 'Let us go worship idols'
↓
That man replies: 'I have friends who want to hear this — tell them too' — luring the inciter to repeat himself
↓
The court lights a candle in the inner room where the inciter will speak
↓
Witnesses are placed in the outer dark room — they can see and hear him, but he cannot see them
↓
The inciter repeats his words of incitement, unaware he is being observed
↓
The man challenges him: 'How can we abandon our God in Heaven and worship idols?'
↓
If inciter retracts → released. If he persists → brought to court and stoned

🔥 How Is the Sorceress/Witch Executed? — Three Views

Rabbi Yosei HaGelili
Verbal analogy: 'You shall not allow a witch to live' (Ex 22:17) ↔ 'You shall allow nothing that breathes to live' (Deut 20:16). Just as Canaanites die by the sword, so too witches → decapitation.
Rabbi Akiva
Verbal analogy: 'You shall not allow a witch to live' ↔ 'whether animal or man, it shall not live' (Ex 19:13). Those who violated Sinai were stoned, so witches → stoning.
Rabbi Yosei HaGelili
I derive from a verse about Canaanites which has only one death type. Your Sinai verse covers many death types for Jews — so my analogy is cleaner and more reliable.
Rabbi Akiva
It is better to derive a law about Jews from a verse about Jews (Sinai) than from a verse about gentiles — even if the Sinai verse includes multiple death types.
Ben Azzai
The Torah juxtaposes 'witch' (Ex 22:17) directly with 'one who lies with an animal' (Ex 22:18). The juxtaposition teaches: just as bestiality = stoning, so too sorcery = stoning.
📌 Ruling The halakha follows stoning for real sorcery. One who merely deceives the eyes is exempt from execution but still prohibited.

👁️ Keshafim: Sorcery vs. Divine Providence

כּוֹחֵשׁ פַּמַלְיָא שֶׁל מַעְלָה
כְּשָׁפִים — The Acronym of Sorcery Rabbi Yoḥanan asks: Why is sorcery called keshafim (כְּשָׁפִים)? Answer: It is a notarikon (acronym) for כּוֹחֵשׁ פַּמַלְיָא שֶׁל מַעְלָה — "contradicts the heavenly entourage" — because sorcery appears to override the natural laws established by God.

Rabbi Ḥanina: This is true even regarding sorcery — 'there is none else besides Him' (Deut 4:35) means sorcery cannot harm a righteous person.

Story: A woman tried to take dust from under Rabbi Ḥanina's feet to curse him. He said: 'If you succeed, go ahead — nothing harms one whose time has not yet come.'

Gemara's resolution: Rabbi Ḥanina was uniquely meritorious. Most people should not be complacent — sorcery is a real concern for ordinary individuals.
