⚖️ Do We Follow the Majority (Rov) in Capital Cases?

Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua
We do NOT blindly follow the majority in capital cases! Each case must be judged on its own merits. The Torah says 'the congregation shall rescue' — we must investigate every individual fully.
Ravina
We DO follow majority in capital cases! Proof from the mishna: if two witnesses disagree whether the crime was Monday or Tuesday, we do not acquit — we rely on the majority of the month's days.
Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua
That case is different — the witnesses personally observed the event and know the day. Following majority there is not the same as presuming facts about the defendant's status.
Rabbi Yirmeya of Difti
Further proof: a man who has intercourse with a 3-year-old girl married to another is executed. We follow the majority that her betrothal was valid — this is rov applied in a capital case!
Tension Unresolved The Gemara tests both positions against multiple sources. The weight of proofs favors applying rov even in capital cases, but Rav Huna insists the Torah demands individualized scrutiny when life is at stake.

⏱️ The Three-Month Window for Judging a Rebellious Son

Boy grows two pubic hairs
Onset of halachic adulthood — can now theoretically father a child
↓
His wife conceives
Pregnancy begins; full-term = 9 months; 7-month birth also possible
↓
Fetus becomes detectable at ⅓ of pregnancy
~3 months for full-term; ~2⅓ months for 7-month birth
↓
Rabbi Kruspedai / Rabbi Shabbtai
Only 3 months total to judge and sentence the rebellious son
The constraint
Boy must not yet have grown a beard — upper limit of rebellious son status. The 3-month window exhausts itself before the beard grows.

👦 Nine-Year-Old's Intercourse: Legal Effect vs. Biological Capacity

Beit Shammai
If a woman acted lewdly with her minor son (initial stage of intercourse), she is disqualified from marrying a kohen.
Beit Hillel
She is not disqualified. A minor's intercourse has no legal standing whatsoever.
Rav Ḥisda
All agree: a boy nine years and one day old — his intercourse IS legally recognized. Both Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel concede this point without dispute.
Rabba (to Abaye)
Even though a nine-year-old has sperm, he cannot father a child. His sperm is like grain cut before reaching one-third of its growth — it exists but cannot produce offspring.
Key Distinction Legal act (intercourse) ≠ biological result (fathering). A nine-year-old's intercourse creates legal consequences (disqualification, impurity transmission), but his sperm cannot actually generate a pregnancy.

⚖️ Two Views: Majority Rule (Rov) in Capital Cases

Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua

  • We do NOT follow majority blindly in capital cases
  • Torah requires 'the congregation shall rescue' — maximize defendant's chances
  • Each case must stand on directly verified facts
  • Presuming a person's status from majority endangers the innocent
  • Source: Numbers 35 — extra scrutiny in capital matters

Ravina + Rabbi Yirmeya of Difti

  • We DO follow majority (rov) even in capital cases
  • Proof 1 (Ravina): Witnesses disagree Mon/Tue — we use majority of days, not acquit
  • Proof 2 (Rabbi Yirmeya): Man executed for intercourse with 3-year-old bride — based on rov that betrothal was valid
  • Rov is a universal Torah principle with no carve-out for capital law
  • Restricting rov to non-capital cases would paralyze the entire system

👧 Why Only a Son — Not a Daughter?

כִּי יִהְיֶה לְאִישׁ בֵּן סוֹרֵר וּמוֹרֶה — "בֵּן" וְלֹא בַּת
Rabbi Shimon's Reasoning (Baraita) Rabbi Shimon reasons: Logic would suggest a daughter should qualify as a stubborn and rebellious child — she too can act immorally and endanger society.

Yet the verse states: "If a man has a son [בֵּן]" (Devarim 21:18) — the Torah explicitly specifies son, not daughter.

The deeper rationale:
• The rebellious son is executed for his future crimes — he will grow up to rob and murder
• A daughter's path of wrongdoing differs from this trajectory
• Nevertheless, the exclusion is ultimately a gezeirat hakatuv — a scriptural decree

Implication: Even when logic points one way, the Torah's text is determinative.
