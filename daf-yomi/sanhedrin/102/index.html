👑 Three Kings: No Share in World-to-Come

Jeroboam

  • Made two golden calves — added to wilderness debt
  • Caused Israel to sin — חֵטְא יָרָבְעָם
  • Left the destiny of Jerusalem forever
  • God grabbed his garment — still refused repentance
  • Divine Voice offered return — he refused

Ahab

  • אָח to Heaven, אָב to idol worship
  • Minor sins = other kings' major sins
  • Merit: added Samaria; honored Torah (22 letters)
  • Perfectly balanced — mitzvot and sins equal
  • Wrote 'Ahab denies God of Israel' on Samaria's gates

Manasseh

  • Name = נָשָׁה יָהּ — he forgot God
  • Mastered Torah yet fell to evil inclination
  • Quizzed Rav Ashi on HaMotzi in a dream
  • R. Yehuda: does have share — he repented
  • Excised God's name; wrote Ahab's name instead

🚶 Jeroboam Leaves Jerusalem — The Unraveling

Jeroboam leaves Jerusalem (I Kings 11:29)
Ahijah the Shilonite finds him wearing a new garment, alone in the field
↓
R. Ḥanina bar Pappa
He left the destiny of Jerusalem — removed himself from its merit-category forever
Rav Naḥman
'New garment' = laws invented with no tradition, like a new garment with no prior patches
Rav (via Rav Yehuda)
'Alone in the field' = all Torah scholars of the generation were like grass compared to them — they were wiser than all
↓
Divine Voice at Coronation (R. Ḥanina bar Pappa):
'Return, Jeroboam! I, you, and David will stroll together in Gan Eden.'
Micah 1:14 — the houses of Achzib are a deceit for the kings of Israel
↓
Jeroboam asked: 'Who will be first?' — Told: 'David.' He refused.
God grabbed his garment; still Jeroboam would not repent → Sealed: No share in World-to-Come

⚖️ Ahab — Merit vs. Sin

Rabbi Yoḥanan
Omri earned the monarchy for adding one city to Eretz Yisrael — Samaria (שֹׁמְרוֹן). His son Ahab inherited from this merit.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Ahab reigned 22 years because he honored the Torah, which was given in 22 letters. He refused to give Ben-Hadad 'the delight of his eyes' — identified as a Torah scroll.
Gemara Challenge
Perhaps 'the delight of his eyes' was an idol — he was protecting idolatry, not Torah?
Gemara Response
'Cannot enter your mind!' — It says 'all the elders AND all the people' objected. The righteous among them would never have agreed to protect an idol.
Rav Naḥman
Ahab was perfectly balanced — mitzvot and transgressions equal. God needed to search for a volunteer spirit to tip the scales and cause his fall at Ramoth Gilead.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Despite all merits, Ahab has no share in the World-to-Come: 'I will excise from Ahab every man' (I Kings 21:21) — even in the next world.
His Ultimate Sin Ahab wrote on the gates of Samaria: 'Ahab has denied the God of Israel.' He also caused Ahaziah his son to excise God's name from Torah scrolls and replace it with Ahab's name.

😴 Rav Ashi's Dream — Manasseh Humbles Him

Rav Ashi announces to his students: 'Tomorrow we will begin with our colleagues — the three kings.' He implied that as Torah scholars, the kings were his peers.
↓
That night, Manasseh appears in a dream and confronts Rav Ashi: 'You called me your colleague and the colleague of your father?! You are not fit to carry my shoes!'
↓
Manasseh tests him: 'Tell me — from where must one cut the loaf of bread when reciting HaMotzi leḥem min ha-aretz?'
↓
Rav Ashi admits: 'I do not know.' Manasseh: 'You don't know this basic law and yet call us colleagues?!' — Then he teaches: cut from the place where the crust bakes most crisply.
↓
Rav Ashi asks: 'Since you were so wise, why did you worship idols?' Manasseh: 'Had you been there at that time, you would have lifted your cloak and run after the idols — the evil inclination for idolatry was overpowering in that generation.'

🍞 Benefiting Without Blessing = Theft

כָּל הַנֶּהֱנֶה מֵהָעוֹלָם הַזֶּה בְּלֹא בְרָכָה — כְּאִלּוּ גָּזַל לְהַקָּדוֹשׁ בָּרוּךְ הוּא וּכְנֶסֶת יִשְׂרָאֵל
Rabbi Ḥanina bar Pappa Anyone who derives benefit from this world without reciting a blessing — it is as though he stole from the Holy One, Blessed be He, and from the congregation of Israel. As it is written: 'Whoever robs his father and his mother and says it is no transgression — he is the companion of a corrupter' (Proverbs 28:24). The 'corrupter' is Jeroboam son of Nebat, who corrupted Israel before their Father in Heaven — sinning and causing others to sin.
