QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye, why did the righteous Jehu ultimately sin, and what principle does this teach?
A. He was corrupted by Ahab's example and gradually adopted his ways
B. A covenant made with the lips is binding — his verbal declaration 'Jehu will serve Baal much' bound him even though he intended it as a trap
C. He inherited spiritual damage from Ahijah the Shilonite's original error
D. He was overwhelmed by the evil inclination, just like Manasseh explained
Correct: B — Abaye teaches that 'a covenant is made with the lips' (בְּרִית כְּרוּתָה בַּשְּׂפָתַיִם). Even though Jehu declared 'Jehu will serve Baal much' as a cunning trap for the Baal priests, his spoken words created a binding commitment that led to sin in later generations.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, why did Ahab merit to reign for 22 years?
A. He added the city of Samaria to Eretz Yisrael, which was his father Omri's merit
B. He consulted the elders and the people before making major decisions
C. He respected the Torah, given in 22 letters, refusing to surrender 'the delight of his eyes' — a Torah scroll — to Ben-Hadad
D. He repented on the battlefield before his death at Ramoth Gilead
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan teaches that Ahab's 22-year reign corresponded to his honor for Torah, which was given in 22 Hebrew letters. When Ben-Hadad demanded all his possessions, Ahab surrendered silver, gold, and wives — but refused to give 'the delight of his eyes,' which the Gemara identifies as a Torah scroll.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What did Manasseh teach Rav Ashi in the dream, and what deeper point did the encounter make?
A. He revealed the precise laws of idol worship that had originally tempted him
B. He taught that from where to cut the loaf for HaMotzi — from the crispest crust — proving his Torah mastery despite his sins
C. He demonstrated that sincere repentance can earn a share in World-to-Come for anyone
D. He warned Rav Ashi never to publicly shame wicked kings, as they might haunt him
Correct: B — Manasseh asked Rav Ashi where to cut bread for the HaMotzi blessing, and when Rav Ashi didn't know, Manasseh answered: from the crispest-baked part. The encounter humbled Rav Ashi, who had called the three kings his 'colleagues,' showing that profound Torah knowledge and catastrophic sin can coexist in one person.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ḥanina bar Pappa, who is 'the companion of a corrupter' in Proverbs 28:24, and what sin does this describe?
A. Ahab, who wrote blasphemy on the gates of Samaria — his sin was public desecration
B. Manasseh, who erased God's name from Torah scrolls — his sin was textual corruption
C. Jeroboam son of Nebat — one who benefits from the world without a blessing is his companion, having robbed God and Israel
D. The generation of the wilderness who made the Golden Calf — their sin triggered all later calf worship
Correct: C — Rabbi Ḥanina bar Pappa derives from Proverbs 28:24 that failing to recite a blessing before benefiting from the world is like robbing God and the congregation of Israel. The 'companion of a corrupter' (חָבֵר לְאִישׁ מַשְׁחִית) refers to Jeroboam son of Nebat, who both sinned and caused all of Israel to sin — the ultimate corrupter.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What did Ravina say was the meaning of God telling the lying spirit to 'go out' (צֵא)?
A. Go out immediately and begin the mission without further deliberation
B. Go out among the prophets of Israel and find which ones are susceptible to deception
C. Go out from within My inner partition and do not return — permanently expelled because falsehood cannot stand before God
D. Go out to the battlefield of Ramoth Gilead and cause confusion among the soldiers
Correct: C — Ravina explains that God's command 'go out' (צֵא) meant the lying spirit was banished from God's inner domain permanently, as it is written in Psalms 101:7: 'He that speaks falsehoods shall not be established before My eyes.' The spirit's willingness to lie excluded it forever from God's presence.
