Sanhedrin 104 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why was Ahaz not enumerated among the kings with no share in the World-to-Come?
A. He sincerely repented on his deathbed
B. He is sandwiched between two righteous kings — his father Jotham and his son Hezekiah
C. His wickedness was considered less severe than the others on the list
D. The Great Assembly simply forgot to include him
Correct: B — Rabbi Yirmeya bar Abba explains that Ahaz is 'cast between two righteous men' — his father Jotham and his son Hezekiah were both righteous, and he is omitted out of deference to them.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How did the captives on Mount Carmel determine the camel was blind in one eye?
A. They recognized the camel from the Jerusalem marketplace
B. The captor had mentioned the camel's condition to them earlier
C. The camel ate grass only from the side it could see, leaving the other side untouched
D. They heard the camel favoring one side as it walked
Correct: C — The captives deduced the camel's one-eyed blindness from its selective grazing — it ate grass only from one side of the road and ignored the grass on the other side, which it could not see.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rav Yehuda say in the name of Rav about Jerusalem being described as 'like a widow'?
A. Jerusalem is like a widow who will never remarry — God has permanently abandoned her
B. Jerusalem mourns like a widow mourns for lost children, not a lost husband
C. Jerusalem is like a woman whose husband went overseas but intends to return — not a true widow
D. Jerusalem is compared to a widow to show she has no legal protections
Correct: C — Rav teaches that Jerusalem is 'like a widow, but not an actual widow' — she is like a woman whose husband has gone overseas but intends to return. God's departure is temporary, not permanent.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabba citing Rabbi Yoḥanan, the compound verb 'bakho tivke' (she cries, crying) alludes to what two events?
A. Crying over the death of Josiah and crying over the Babylonian exile
B. One cry for the destruction of the First Temple and one for the Second Temple
C. Crying at night and crying during the day — two separate mourning periods
D. Crying over the loss of the Davidic dynasty and the loss of the priesthood
Correct: B — Rabba says in the name of Rabbi Yoḥanan: The two expressions of crying correspond to two destructions — one cry for the First Temple and one cry for the Second Temple.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, why is the book of Lamentations written as an alphabetical acrostic?
A. To make the book accessible and memorable for mourners reciting it annually
B. To honor the full range of Jerusalem's grief, from beginning to end
C. Because Israel violated the Torah, which was given through all the letters of the alef-beit
D. To mirror the poetic structure used in Psalms and Proverbs
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan explains: Israel violated the Torah, which was given through the full alef-beit. Therefore their punishment is articulated through those same letters — the acrostic reflects the completeness of their transgression.
