Sanhedrin 21 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Tanna of the Mishna, how many wives may a king marry, and from where is the number derived?
A. 12 — from the twelve tribes of Israel
B. 18 — from Nathan's prophecy comparing David's wives with "like these and like those"
C. 24 — from Ravina's reading of the doubled phrase in Nathan's rebuke
D. Unlimited — provided none turn his heart away from God
Correct: B — The Mishna says 18. Rav Kahana explains: Nathan said 'like these and like those' — both terms kahenna are equal, each = 6 added to David's existing 6 in Hebron, yielding 18 total.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rav identify Eglah (David's 6th wife in Hebron) with Michal?
A. Both were daughters of King Saul
B. Both were from the tribe of Benjamin
C. Michal was beloved to David like a calf (egel), hence the name Eglah
D. The verse explicitly states they are the same person
Correct: C — Rav explains that Eglah was so named because she was dear to David 'like a calf' (egel). Since Michal was David's beloved first wife, Rav identifies her as Eglah — resolving why David had six wives yet Michal seems missing from the list.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How many Torah scrolls must a king write, and what becomes of the second one?
A. One scroll — same obligation as every other Jew
B. Two scrolls — one for the treasury, one written small like an amulet worn on his arm
C. Three scrolls — one for each of his major obligations as king, priest-liaison, and judge
D. Two scrolls — both deposited in the treasury for safekeeping
Correct: B — The king writes two scrolls. The first fulfills the general Jewish obligation (like all Jews per Rava). The second, specifically for the king, is made very small like an amulet and hung on his arm so Torah accompanies him always.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rabbi Yitzḥak teaches that Torah rationales were deliberately hidden. What evidence does he bring?
A. Moses asked that the reasons be hidden to prevent misuse
B. The Sanhedrin voted at Sinai to conceal the reasons
C. Two rationales were revealed — wives and horses — and Solomon failed in both, citing the very reasons to justify himself
D. Ezra removed the rationales when rewriting the Torah in Assyrian script
Correct: C — Solomon said regarding wives: 'I will multiply but my heart won't turn' — and it did. Regarding horses: 'I will accumulate but not send to Egypt' — and he did. Knowing the reason led him to rationalize violations. Hence reasons are better hidden.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Mar Zutra / Mar Ukva teach about the history of Hebrew script?
A. Torah was always written in Assyrian (square) script from the time of Moses
B. Torah was originally given in Ivrit (Libona'a) script, then re-given in Assyrian script in Ezra's time; Samaritans retained the original
C. Ezra invented a new script at Babylon and the original Torah script was lost
D. Both scripts were used simultaneously from Sinai — Assyrian for sacred texts, Ivrit for everyday writing
Correct: B — Torah was originally given in Ivrit (Libona'a) script. In Ezra's time it was re-given in Assyrian script (Ktav Ashuri). The Samaritans (Kutim) retained the original Ivrit script. Rav Ḥisda identifies 'Ivrit script' as Libona'a script.
