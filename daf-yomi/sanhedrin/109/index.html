🗼 Three Factions of the Tower of Babel

R. Yirmeya bar Elazar
They split into three factions: one said 'Let us dwell atop the tower,' one said 'Let us worship idols atop it,' and one said 'Let us wage war against God in heaven.'
School of R. Sheila
They intended to wage war against God directly — the tower was a military project aimed at heaven itself.
R. Natan (Baraita)
All three factions intended idol worship. 'Let us make a name' (Gen 11:4) parallels 'make no mention of the name of other gods' (Ex 23:13) — 'name' here means idolatry.
R. Yoḥanan
The top third of the tower was burned, the bottom third sank into the earth, and only the middle third remained standing — visible to this day.
Rav adds The atmosphere of the tower causes forgetfulness — anyone who stands there forgets their learning.

🔥 Sodom's Sins: Competing Interpretations of 'Wicked and Sinners'

Rav Yehuda

  • "Wicked" (רְשָׁעִים) = bodily sins — derived from Joseph's rejection of Potiphar's wife: 'this wicked deed'
  • "Sinners" (חַטָּאִים) = monetary sins — derived from the sin-offering verse
  • Bodily sins listed first as the more severe category

Baraita (opposing view)

  • "Wicked" = monetary sins — 'your hand shall not be wicked toward your poor brother'
  • "Sinners" = bodily sins — 'the sinners against their own souls'
  • Monetary exploitation is root cause — listed first

Rava's Root Cause

  • Sodom sinned due to excessive Divine blessing and abundance
  • 'Out of the earth comes bread… its stones are sapphires' (Job 28)
  • Wealth bred arrogance: 'We have everything — why allow travelers who only take our property?'
  • Goal: abolish proper treatment of guests (הַכְנָסַת אוֹרְחִים) entirely

🛏️ The Bed of Sodom — Cruelty Systematized

The Procrustean Bed: Sodom had beds for guests — if a guest was longer than the bed, they cut off his legs; if shorter, they stretched him to fit.
↓
Eliezer's Response: When told to lie down, Eliezer said: 'I made a vow at my mother's grave never to sleep in a bed.' He escaped the trap through wit.
↓
The Dinar Scheme: Each Sodomite gave a poor visitor a dinar with their name written on it — but no one would sell him bread.
↓
After the visitor died of hunger: Each Sodomite reclaimed only the dinar bearing their own name. The visitor got nothing — and died having been publicly 'helped.'
↓
Wedding Cloak Law: Whoever invited a guest to a wedding had his cloak seized. Eliezer, arriving uninvited, sat at the end and cleverly transferred the liability to another guest.
↓
The Young Woman (עֲלָמָה): She secretly brought bread to the poor hidden in a pitcher. When discovered, they smeared her with honey, placed her on the city wall, and hornets consumed her. This was the 'outcry' (צְעָקָה) that God heard before destroying Sodom (Gen 18:20–21).

🌍 Korah's Rebellion — Interpreting the Names

"And Korah… took [וַיִּקַּח]" (Num 16:1)
Reish Lakish: He purchased a bad acquisition for himself — through his own foolishness he lost his share in the World-to-Come
↓
Dathan דָּתָן
Violated the dat (precepts/law דָּת) of God — name encodes his sin
Abiram אֲבִירָם
Braced [אִיבֵּר] himself against repentance — refused to return
On ben Peleth אוֹן בֶּן פֶּלֶת
Saved by the wisdom of his wife!
↓
On's Wife's Argument: 'What difference does it make to you? If Moses is the master, you are the student. If Korah is the master, you are still the student — you gain nothing!'
She made him drink wine until he slept, then sat unveiled at the entrance. Korah's men, careful about modesty, refused to approach and left.
