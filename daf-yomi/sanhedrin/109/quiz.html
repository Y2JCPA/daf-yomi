Sanhedrin 109 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Yehuda, to which type of sin does the word 'wicked' (רְשָׁעִים) refer regarding the people of Sodom?
A. Monetary sins against the poor
B. Sins committed with their bodies
C. Idol worship in the city square
D. Murder of travelers
Correct: B — Rav Yehuda derives 'wicked' from the bodily sin context — Potiphar's wife called Joseph's refusal 'this wicked deed' — so 'wicked' refers to bodily/sexual sins, while 'sinners' refers to monetary wrongs.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, what happened to the Tower of Babel?
A. It was entirely burned by heavenly fire
B. The top third burned, the bottom third sank into earth, the middle remained
C. It was swallowed entirely into the earth
D. It collapsed and its stones were scattered across the world
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan teaches a three-part fate: the uppermost third burned, the lowermost third sank into the earth, and only the middle third remained standing and visible.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How did On ben Peleth's wife save him from Korah's rebellion?
A. She secretly informed Moses of Korah's plan
B. She made him drunk so he slept, then sat unveiled at the entrance deterring Korah's modest followers
C. She disguised him as a Levite so Korah did not recognize him
D. She bribed Dathan and Abiram to remove On from the list of rebels
Correct: B — On's wife reasoned that he had nothing to gain from the rebellion. She made him drink wine until he slept deeply, then sat unveiled at the tent entrance. Korah's men, who were careful about modesty, refused to approach a tent where a woman sat with uncovered hair, and eventually gave up.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What made Sodom's 'dinar scheme' against poor visitors so cruel?
A. The dinars were counterfeit and worthless
B. Each person gave a dinar with their name on it but refused to sell bread — the visitor died of hunger and each person reclaimed their own dinar
C. The dinars were taken back by force as soon as given
D. The visitor was required to work a full day before receiving a single dinar
Correct: B — The scheme was a cruel charade of charity: each Sodomite gave a labeled dinar but no one sold food. When the visitor died of starvation, each person retrieved only their own marked coin, leaving the victim with nothing — not even dignity.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rava, what was the root cause of Sodom's wickedness?
A. Sodom was founded by Cain's descendants who inherited his violent nature
B. They suffered poverty and competition for scarce resources drove them to cruelty
C. They became arrogant and sinful due to the extraordinary wealth and goodness God bestowed upon their land
D. They were corrupted by trade with pagan nations surrounding them
Correct: C — Rava cites Job 28: 'Out of the earth comes bread… its stones are sapphires, it has dust of gold.' This extraordinary abundance bred arrogance — 'We have everything, why do we need travelers?' — and led them to actively abolish hospitality and kindness.
