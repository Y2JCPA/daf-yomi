⚔️ The Zealot's License — and Its Limits

קנאים פוגעים בו — Sanhedrin 82a
Core Principle of Daf 82 A zealot who witnesses a Jew engaging in intercourse with a gentile woman may strike — but only in the act. If the transgressor separates first, the zealot is liable for murder. The court does not authorize this; it lives outside normal legal channels. Pinehas acted on remembered tradition while Moses stood silent. This daf probes those limits, the miracles that protected Pinehas, Zimri's many names, and whether extra-judicial striking extends to an impure priest who performed Temple service.

🔍 What Did Pinehas 'See' That Moved Him to Act?

Rav
Pinehas saw the event unfolding before him and remembered the forgotten halakha: zealots may strike one engaged in intercourse with a gentile woman. He said to Moses: 'I remember this tradition — stand aside!' Moses had momentarily forgotten it.
Shmuel
Pinehas saw and applied the verse: 'There is neither wisdom nor understanding nor counsel against the Lord' (Proverbs 21:30). Wherever God's Name is desecrated, one does not stand on honor for any teacher — not even Moses. He deduced permission from this principle.
Both Agree Moses was rendered speechless and could not recall the ruling. Pinehas acted entirely on his own initiative — the court plays no role in this law.

📜 Hasmonean Decree: Rav Dimi vs. Ravin

Rav Dimi's Report

  • נ — Niddah (menstruating woman)
  • ש — Shifcha (Canaanite maidservant)
  • ג — Goya (gentile woman)
  • ז — Zona (prostitute)
  • Four rabbinic prohibitions violated when engaging with a gentile woman. The Hasmoneans issued a formal decree encoding this liability.

Ravin's Report

  • נ — Niddah
  • ש — Shifcha
  • ג — Goya
  • ז — Zona
  • Ravin agrees with Rav Dimi on the Hasmonean decree's existence, but their reports of exactly which prohibitions are invoked differ slightly in the Talmudic discussion.

✨ Six Miracles Performed for Pinehas (Rabbi Yoḥanan)

1. Zimri did not separate from Cozbi during the act — had he done so, Pinehas would have been liable for murder
↓
2. Pinehas aimed precisely and pierced both simultaneously with a single thrust, male through female, proving the act was in progress
↓
3. The spear held both bodies aloft — visibly demonstrating the transgression and preventing a murder accusation
↓
4. An angel sealed their mouths so they could not cry out and rouse the tribe of Simeon against Pinehas
↓
5. An angel swept away the members of the tribe of Simeon who had surrounded Pinehas to protect Zimri
↓
6. Pinehas's strength was supernaturally sustained to carry the two bodies on the spear before the congregation
