Sanhedrin 82 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda, what happens if someone comes to the court to ask whether he may kill a man he sees engaging in intercourse with a gentile woman?
A. The court confirms it is permitted and provides written authorization
B. The court does not instruct him — he may not act on court authority
C. The court refers the case to the full Sanhedrin of 71
D. The court punishes the questioner for contemplating extrajudicial killing
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda rules that the court does not instruct zealots to act. The permission is an extra-legal tradition, not court-sanctioned. Asking forfeits the right.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What would have been the legal consequence if Zimri had separated from Cozbi before Pinehas killed him?
A. Pinehas would have received lashes
B. Pinehas would have been executed for murder
C. Zimri would still be liable to court-imposed death penalty
D. Pinehas would have been exiled to a city of refuge
Correct: B — The zealot's license applies only while the transgression is actively occurring. Once Zimri separated, killing him would make Pinehas fully liable for murder — one of the miracles was precisely that Zimri did not separate.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Shmuel, what was the verse that moved Pinehas to act without waiting for Moses?
A. "You shall be holy for I the Lord your God am holy" (Leviticus 19:2)
B. "There is neither wisdom nor understanding nor counsel against the Lord" (Proverbs 21:30)
C. "The Lord your God is a consuming fire, a jealous God" (Deuteronomy 4:24)
D. "Pinehas arose and prayed and the plague was stopped" (Psalms 106:30)
Correct: B — Shmuel derives from Proverbs 21:30 that wherever God's Name is desecrated, one does not defer to the honor of any teacher — not even Moses. This gave Pinehas license to act immediately.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How many miracles does Rabbi Yoḥanan enumerate as having been performed for Pinehas when he killed Zimri and Cozbi?
A. Three
B. Four
C. Five
D. Six
Correct: D — Rabbi Yoḥanan lists six miracles: Zimri did not separate; both were pierced simultaneously; the spear held them aloft; they could not cry out; the tribe of Simeon was swept away; and Pinehas's strength was divinely sustained.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the debate between Rav Aḥa bar Huna and Rav Sheshet about a priest who performed Temple service in a state of ritual impurity, what does the mishna establish about his punishment?
A. He is brought before the court and executed by lashes
B. He is not brought to court, but his priestly brethren strike him extra-judicially
C. He is entirely exempt since his service is valid after the fact
D. Only the Sanhedrin of 71 may adjudicate his case
Correct: B — Rav Sheshet proves from the mishna itself: the impure priest is not brought to court for formal judgment, but his fellow priests — the 'young of the priesthood' — strike him as a zealot act, outside normal judicial process.
