⚖️ Single Lapse, Multiple Idol Acts — One Offering or Many?

Rabbi Ami
If one sacrificed, burned incense, and poured a libation to an idol — all in one lapse of awareness — he owes only one sin-offering.
Gemara (challenge)
But Abaye derived from three separate bowing verses that idol worship acts are divided — implying separate liability for each distinct act!
Resolution
Abaye only articulated that reasoning in accordance with Rabbi Ami's view — Abaye himself does not hold that position personally.
Talmudic Principle A scholar may explain the internal logic of a ruling without endorsing it — exposition and personal opinion are distinct.

📜 Abaye's Three Bowing Verses Decoded

Why does the Torah mention the prohibition against bowing to idols three times?
(Exodus 20:5, 34:14; Deuteronomy 5:9)
↓
Verse 1
Prohibition against bowing to an idol for which bowing is its standard worship
Verse 2
Prohibition against bowing even when bowing is NOT its standard worship
Verse 3
Divides worship acts — separate liability for each distinct act
↓
Revised reading (Gemara): Verse 1 actually covers idols worshipped honorably (like bowing) but whose specific rite is not bowing — extending liability beyond exact mimicry of standard worship

🗣️ 'You Are My God' — Does Verbal Acceptance Create Liability?

Rav
One who says to an idol 'You are my god' is obligated to bring a sin-offering, even though it is a purely verbal act.
Rabbis (objection)
A sin-offering is owed only for transgressions that involve an action — verbal declaration alone cannot trigger this obligation!
Rav (aligned with Rabbi Akiva)
I rule according to Rabbi Akiva, who obligates a sin-offering even for non-action transgressions — as established for the blasphemer.
Gemara
Rav adds a novel point: Lest you think Rabbi Akiva limits this to blasphemy (explicit verse), the golden calf verse equates verbal acceptance to active idol worship.
Source Verse "They have made a molten calf, and have bowed to it, and have sacrificed to it" (Exodus 32:8) — acceptance, bowing, and sacrifice are linguistically equated.

✡️ The Letter That Saved the Jewish People

אֵלֶּה אֱלֹהֶיךָ יִשְׂרָאֵל אֲשֶׁר הֶעֱלוּךָ מֵאֶרֶץ מִצְרָיִם — שמות לב:ד
Rabbi Yoḥanan — A Letter With Cosmic Stakes Were it not for the extra vav (ו) in the word he'elukha — giving it a plural form — the Jewish people would have been sentenced to destruction at the sin of the golden calf.

The plural implies multiple beings brought Israel from Egypt, not the calf alone replacing God. One letter made the difference between apostasy and ambiguity.

This view is corroborated by a dispute among Tannaim cited immediately after.

🚫 Idol Worship Acts: Severity of Liability

⚔️ Capital Punishment

  • Sacrificing to an idol
  • Burning incense to an idol
  • Pouring a libation to an idol
  • Bowing to an idol
  • Saying 'You are my god' (Rabbi Akiva)

⚠️ Prohibition — No Lashes

  • Hugging an idol
  • Kissing an idol
  • Cleaning or polishing an idol
  • Spraying water before an idol
  • Vowing in an idol's name
  • Taking an oath in an idol's name

✅ Mockery — Permitted

  • All mockery of idols is permitted (Rav Naḥman)
  • Calling Baal Peor by scatological names
  • Saying Bel 'crouches' and 'stoops' (Isaiah 46:1)
  • Renaming Markulis derogatorily
