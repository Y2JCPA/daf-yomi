Sanhedrin 63 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ami, if one performs three separate idol worship acts (sacrifice, incense, libation) within a single lapse of awareness, how many sin-offerings are owed?
A. Three — one for each distinct prohibited act
B. One — a single lapse of awareness creates a single liability
C. Two — sacrifice is categorically distinct from the others
D. None — only capital punishment applies, not sin-offerings
Correct: B — Rabbi Ami rules that all acts committed within one lapse of awareness merge into a single liability, requiring only one sin-offering.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Abaye ultimately derive from the third Torah mention of the prohibition against bowing to idols?
A. Bowing is only prohibited in the Beit HaMikdash precinct
B. It prohibits bowing to any object, even non-idols
C. It divides idol worship acts, creating separate liability for each
D. It creates a blanket prohibition against all forms of honoring idols
Correct: C — The third mention of bowing serves to divide idol worship acts from one another, so that each act performed in a single lapse creates its own separate liability.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rav specifically invoke Rabbi Akiva when ruling that saying 'You are my god' to an idol requires a sin-offering?
A. Because Rabbi Akiva authored the verse connecting acceptance to sacrifice
B. Because the Rabbis hold sin-offerings require an action — only Rabbi Akiva obligates offerings for non-action transgressions
C. Because Rabbi Akiva is the only authority who discusses idol worship liability
D. Because Rav must cite a Tannaic source to override a Mishnaic ruling
Correct: B — The standard view requires an action to trigger a sin-offering. Rabbi Akiva — who obligated an offering even for blasphemy (a non-action) — provides the basis for Rav's ruling about verbal idol acceptance.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What made the word הֶעֱלוּךָ (he'elukha) in Exodus 32:4 so important to Rabbi Yoḥanan's analysis of the golden calf sin?
A. It indicates the calf was fashioned from pure gold rather than molten silver
B. Its plural form implies multiple beings brought Israel out of Egypt, not the calf alone replacing God
C. It shifts blame from Aaron to the people by using an impersonal construction
D. It links the calf to the Exodus narrative rather than Sinai, changing the legal category
Correct: B — The plural 'he'elukha' ('who brought you up') implies multiple entities were credited — not the calf alone. Without this vav, the verse would condemn Israel for wholesale replacement of God with the calf.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Yehuda citing Rav, the idol Succoth-Benoth worshipped by the men of Babylonia took the form of which animal?
A. A golden calf
B. A rooster
C. A hen
D. A mule
Correct: C — Rav teaches that Succoth-Benoth was fashioned in the image of a hen (תַּרְנְגֹלֶת), symbolizing a mother brooding over her young. The men of Cuth's idol Nergal, by contrast, was a rooster.
