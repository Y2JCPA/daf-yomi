5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Reish Lakish object to Rabbi Hiyya bar Zarnokei and Rabbi Shimon ben Yehotzadak intercalating the year in Asya?
A. They lacked the minimum quorum of ten Sages required for intercalation
B. He believed they were suspected of desecrating the Sabbatical Year
C. They performed the intercalation outside the borders of the Land of Israel
D. They failed to first consult the Nasi of the Sanhedrin
Correct: B — Reish Lakish observed them plowing and pruning during the Sabbatical Year. Though they offered innocent explanations, he concluded they were shvi'it violators — and people suspected of desecrating the Sabbatical Year are unfit to intercalate the year.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the Talmudic source for the principle that a 'conspiracy of wicked people' (agudah shel reshaim) is not counted as part of a group?
A. Korach's rebellion, where his followers were swallowed by the earth
B. Shebna's secret attempt to surrender Jerusalem to Sennacherib
C. The spies' negative report about the Land of Canaan
D. Doeg and Ahitofel's counsel that turned against David
Correct: B — Shebna secretly conspired with a large group to surrender to Sennacherib, but an angel scattered his followers before the plan materialized — proving that a wicked conspiracy never truly constitutes a valid majority or group.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava, when is a robber disqualified from bearing witness WITHOUT requiring a formal court proclamation?
A. When he is also a shepherd who grazes on others' property
B. When he was caught stealing in front of two valid witnesses
C. When he is a robber by Torah law, as distinct from rabbinic law
D. When he steals only during the harvest seasons of Nisan and Tishrei
Correct: C — Rava distinguishes between levels of robbery: a rabbinic-level robber needs a court proclamation to be formally disqualified, but a Torah-level robber is disqualified automatically and immediately, without any proclamation.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Rav Huna son of Rav Yehoshua reinstate the gravediggers who buried on the first day of Shavuot?
A. Because burying the dead on a Festival is explicitly permitted by rabbinic enactment
B. Because Rav Pappa's excommunication was procedurally invalid
C. Because they believed they were performing a mitzvah and acted in sincere good faith
D. Because the prohibition applies only on the second day of Shavuot in the Diaspora
Correct: C — Rav Huna reasoned that since burying the dead is a great mitzvah, the gravediggers sincerely believed they were obligated to act. Mistaken performance of what one genuinely believes is a mitzvah does not constitute the wickedness required to disqualify a witness.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Under what precise conditions does Rav Nahman rule that a 'thief of Nisan and Tishrei' is NOT disqualified from bearing witness?
A. He repaid double the stolen amount before the court was notified
B. He is a tenant farmer who took a small amount of a fully processed item
C. The theft occurred at night and was not witnessed by two people
D. The field owner verbally forgave him before any legal proceedings began
Correct: B — Rav Nahman's leniency requires all three conditions: (1) the thief is a tenant farmer, (2) he took only a small amount, and (3) the item's work was already complete. In such a case, he likely views it as fair compensation for his labor, and is not formally called a 'thief.'
