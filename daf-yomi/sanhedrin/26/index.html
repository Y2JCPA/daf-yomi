📅 The Asya Intercalation Controversy

Reish Lakish
Look at this priest plowing during the Sabbatical Year! Are those suspected of desecrating shvi'it fit to intercalate the year?
Rabbi Hiyya bar Zarnokei
He can claim: I am a hired worker [agiston] in a gentile-owned field. The pruner needs the branches to make a bale [ekel] for his cow!
Reish Lakish
This is a conspiracy of wicked people! Just as Shebna's camp was not counted with Hezekiah, these wicked ones cannot constitute a valid court.
Rabbi Yohanan
The year was once intercalated by three cattle herders — permitted! We depend not on their expertise but on a majority agreeing. Why is this different?
Reish Lakish
Not similar! There, the Sages themselves then voted and intercalated the year. Here it is a conspiracy of wicked — they cannot be counted at all.
Conclusion Rabbi Yohanan conceded to Reish Lakish: an agudah shel reshaim (conspiracy of wicked people) is never counted as part of a valid group or majority.

🏰 Shebna: The Talmudic Source for 'Wicked Conspiracy'

Shebna — royal steward over Hezekiah's house; held grand Torah lectures attracting large crowds
↓
Sennacherib besieges Jerusalem — Shebna secretly sends a note over the wall: "We have appeased Sennacherib; Hezekiah has not."
↓
Hezekiah fears: the majority have submitted — will God follow the majority against the righteous?
↓
Isaiah's Prophecy (22:17–18)
"He will hurl you away… there you shall die" — Shebna exiled, developed leprosy, hung by heels
God's Response
An angel scattered Shebna's followers before he could surrender — the conspiracy never truly stood behind him
↓
Lesson: Shebna's majority was illusory — a conspiracy of wicked people is not counted. Hezekiah's righteousness was vindicated.

👁️ Witness Disqualification: Proclamation Rules

Requires Court Proclamation

  • Robber by rabbinic law — court must formally announce disqualification
  • Shepherd — one opinion: needs specific proclamation of disqualification
  • Gambler / usurer — only when it is their sole occupation
  • Merchants of Sabbatical Year produce (per Rabbi Yehuda)

No Proclamation Needed

  • Robber by Torah law (Rava) — disqualified immediately upon act
  • Shepherd — other opinion: 'ordinary shepherd' implies no proclamation needed
  • Signatories on a deed proved to be robbers: invalid without announcement

Not Disqualified At All

  • Seasonal thief (Nisan / Tishrei) — tenant farmer, small amount, completed item
  • Gravediggers on Shavuot — acted in good faith believing it was a mitzvah
  • One who takes charity from gentiles privately (not befarhesya)

⛏️ The Gravediggers Who Buried on Shavuot

Gravediggers buried a corpse on the first day of Shavuot, violating the Torah prohibition against labor on a Festival.
↓
Rav Pappa excommunicated them and then disqualified them from bearing witness — reasoning that they committed a deliberate Torah violation.
↓
Rav Huna son of Rav Yehoshua reinstated them: "They assumed they were performing a mitzvah" — burial of the dead is a great obligation that could override other laws.
↓
Rav Pappa presses: But I excommunicated them, and they continued burying on the Festival afterward — doesn't that prove willful wickedness?
↓
Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua answers: They assumed the excommunication was a punishment for performing the mitzva (being zealous), not a declaration that they acted wrongly — so they continued.
↓
Principle established: Sincere but mistaken performance of what one believes is a mitzvah does not constitute the wickedness required to disqualify a witness.
