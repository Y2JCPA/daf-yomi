⚖️ The Rebellious Elder (Zaken Mamre) — Conditions for Liability

A case arises 'too hard' for the local court — brought before the Great Sanhedrin in Jerusalem
↓
The mufla — the most distinguished, ordained expert judge — rules on the matter
↓
The rebellious elder returns home and instructs others contrary to the Sanhedrin's ruling
↓
Liability requires: the matter must involve potential karet (intentional) and a chatas (unwitting) — not just any Torah ruling
↓
Three Tannaitic positions: R. Meir (karet domain), R. Yehuda (full Torah ruling), R. Shimon (even without appearing before the court)

🗣️ Three Opinions on Rebellious Elder Liability

Rabbi Meir

  • Liable only for matters involving karet (intentional) and a chatas (unwitting)
  • Source: verbal analogy (gezeira shava) on the word davar (matter) linking Deut. 17 to another context
  • Reason: only serious violations with severe penalty warrant this extreme punishment

Rabbi Yehuda

  • Liable only when the matter is a full Torah ruling subject to karet
  • Source: 'According to the Torah they shall instruct you' (Deut. 17:11) — must be a complete Torah matter
  • Narrower: the ruling must stem from the full body of Torah law

Rabbi Shimon

  • Liable even if he did not personally appear before the Sanhedrin
  • Source: 'from that place' (Deut. 17:10) — even ruling from a distance suffices
  • Broader reach: physical presence before the court is not required for liability

📅 Status of the 10th Day — Between Niddah and Ziva

Rabbi Yochanan
The 10th day is like the 9th: just as the 9th day requires examination and a full 'clean day' before immersion, so too the 10th. A discharge on day 10 triggers the need for a clean day — treated stringently, adjacent to the niddah zone.
Reish Lakish
The 10th day is like the 11th: just as the 11th day requires no clean day before immersion, so too the 10th. A discharge on day 10 does not require the full ziva process — she may immerse directly without a prior clean day.
Background — The 11-Day Cycle A Sinaitic tradition: days 1–7 are niddah days; days 8–18 are the 11 'ziva' days. The 10th day is legally ambiguous — adjacent to both realms — triggering this dispute.

🔬 'Between Mark and Mark' — Leprous Mark Disputes

Marks on a Person

  • Dispute: R. Yehoshua vs. Rabbis (Nega'im 4:11)
  • Snow-white (baheret) mark — if it spreads into otherwise healthy skin, does that render impure?
  • A rebellious ruling here involves misclassifying bodily tum'ah

Marks on Houses

  • Dispute: R. Elazar son of R. Shimon vs. Rabbis (Nega'im 12:3)
  • R. Elazar: 'wall' (singular) + 'walls' (plural) in same verse → even one stone on one wall suffices
  • Rabbis: mark must spread across the full wall

Marks on Garments

  • Dispute: R. Yonatan ben Avtolemos vs. Rabbis
  • Disagreement on what constitutes a spreading mark on woven fabric
  • Any ruling contrary to the Rabbis by the elder on this creates liability
