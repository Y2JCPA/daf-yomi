5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Meir, what type of matter makes a rebellious elder liable?
A. Any Torah violation, even Rabbinic in origin
B. A matter involving karet for intentional violation and a chatas for unwitting violation
C. Only monetary disputes adjudicated by a local court
D. Matters pertaining exclusively to Temple service
Correct: B — R. Meir derives via a gezeira shava on the word davar (matter) that the rebellious elder is liable only for karet-level violations — matters serious enough to carry karet intentionally and a chatas unwittingly.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the phrase 'Then you shall arise and ascend' (Deut. 17:8) teach about the Land of Israel?
A. That one must travel only by foot, never by donkey, to Jerusalem
B. That the Sanhedrin convened only during the day, never at night
C. That the Temple is higher than Eretz Yisrael, and Eretz Yisrael is higher than all other lands
D. That cases could be submitted in writing without physically traveling
Correct: C — The consistent use of ascent language teaches two geographic-spiritual lessons: the Temple is elevated above the rest of Israel, and Israel itself is elevated above all other nations' lands.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rabbi Yochanan's position on the status of the 10th day of the ziva cycle?
A. The 10th day is like the 11th — no clean day is needed before immersion
B. The 10th day is like the 9th — she must observe a clean day before immersion
C. The 10th day is entirely neutral and carries no special halakhic status
D. A discharge on the 10th day triggers a full 7-day ziva count
Correct: B — R. Yochanan holds the 10th day resembles the 9th: just as a discharge on day 9 requires observing a clean day before immersion, so too the 10th — treating it stringently as adjacent to the niddah zone.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Rav and Levi regarding blood discharged after childbirth?
A. Rav holds it is always pure; Levi holds it is always impure
B. Rav holds there is one source that alternates pure/impure; Levi holds there are two separate sources
C. Rav holds immersion is required immediately; Levi holds she must wait 40 days
D. Rav holds this blood follows niddah rules; Levi holds it follows ziva rules entirely
Correct: B — Rav: one source alternates between impure and pure states. Levi: two distinct sources — the impure source seals after 1 week (male birth) or 2 weeks (female birth), after which the pure source opens.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does R. Elazar son of R. Shimon derive from the alternating use of 'wall' and 'walls' in the verse about leprous marks on houses?
A. That only exterior-facing walls can become impure, not interior walls
B. That the entire house must be demolished even if only one room is affected
C. That even a single stone on one wall suffices to constitute a leprous mark
D. That the mark must be visible simultaneously on all four walls to be declared impure
Correct: C — R. Elazar derives from the tension between singular 'wall' and plural 'walls' in the same verse that even one stone on a single wall can constitute a leprous mark — contra the Rabbis who require the mark to span a full wall.
