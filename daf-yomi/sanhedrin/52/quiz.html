Sanhedrin 52 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Mattana, how is the mitzva of execution by burning carried out?
A. The condemned is wrapped in bundles of branches and set aflame
B. A wick of molten lead is poured down into the condemned's intestines
C. The condemned is thrown into a furnace of fire
D. Hot coals are placed around the condemned until death
Correct: B — Rav Mattana teaches that 'burning' means a wick (פתילה) of lead — a thin strip melted and poured down the throat, burning the soul while the body remains externally intact.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara prefer to derive the burning method from the deaths of Korah's assembly or Nadav & Avihu rather than from the bull offerings?
A. Because the bull offerings use a different Hebrew word for fire
B. Because it is more logical to derive a person's death from another person's death than from animals
C. Because the bull offerings were not considered a form of divine punishment
D. Because the verse about the bulls does not mention burning explicitly
Correct: B — The Gemara reasons that capital punishment and the deaths of Korah's assembly / Nadav & Avihu share more common elements (both involve persons, both are divine punishment) than capital punishment and burnt offerings share.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What two errors did Rav Ḥama bar Toviyya commit when he executed Imrata bat Talei?
A. He performed strangulation instead of burning, and failed to submerge her in dung
B. He used actual fire rather than molten lead, and acted without proper communal authority
C. He used the wrong type of scarf and did not have two witnesses present
D. He executed her before the verdict was finalized and did not announce the crime publicly
Correct: B — Rav Yosef says Rav Ḥama erred regarding Rav Mattana's ruling (burning requires molten lead, not actual fire) and regarding the baraita requiring communal assembly authorization before carrying out an execution.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What halakhic lesson does the Gemara derive from 'And you shall love your neighbor as yourself' (Lev. 19:18) regarding capital punishment?
A. The condemned may not be executed in the presence of his family
B. Two witnesses must affirm that the condemned has repented before execution
C. Even a condemned prisoner must be given the most compassionate death available
D. Execution may only be carried out by a court of 23 judges, never fewer
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman citing Rabba bar Avuh: 'Love your neighbor as yourself' mandates that even when executing a condemned person, the court selects the least painful method — this is why molten lead is used over actual fire.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yonatan, what is the default method of execution when the Torah prescribes death without specifying the method?
A. Burning, as it is the penalty most often mentioned in the Torah
B. Stoning, as it is the most severe and thus most deterring
C. Decapitation, as it is the swiftest method
D. Strangulation, because it leaves no external mark on the body
Correct: D — Rabbi Yonatan teaches that every unspecified death penalty in the Torah defaults to strangulation, since it leaves no external mark — mirroring death at the hand of Heaven, which also leaves no external sign.
