🔥 Three Execution Methods Defined

Burning: Submerge in dung to knees → open mouth with scarf → pour molten lead down throat. Soul burns; body remains intact. This IS halakhic burning.
↓
Source for method: Verbal analogy from deaths of Korah's assembly or Nadav & Avihu — in both cases only the soul was consumed, not the body.
↓
Decapitation: Cut off head with sword, as the monarchy does. Permitted despite resemblance to gentile custom — it is written in Torah, not learned from them.
↓
Strangulation: Submerge in dung → rough scarf inside soft one → pull until death. Default penalty when Torah specifies death without naming the method.

🔥 How Execution by Burning Is Performed

Condemned is submerged in dung up to his knees so he cannot move
↓
Executioner places a rough scarf within a soft one, wraps it around the neck, and pulls — forcing the mouth open
↓
Rav Mattana: A wick [פתילה] of lead — a thin molten strip — is poured down into the intestines
↓
The soul burns while the body remains externally intact. This constitutes halakhic 'burning.'
↓
⚠️ Rav Ḥama bar Toviyya erred: he wrapped Imrata bat Talei in branches and burned her with actual fire — violating both the correct method and the rule requiring communal authority

📜 What Is the Source That Burning = Molten Lead?

Rabbi Elazar
Verbal analogy from Nadav and Avihu: 'Fire came out and devoured them' — yet their garments were not singed. Only the soul burned; bodies were intact.
Other Sage
Verbal analogy from Korah's assembly: the firepans were salvaged to plate the altar — proving the bodies were not destroyed, only the souls consumed.
Gemara Challenge
Why not derive from bull offerings that are burned? If burning of bulls means actual fire, perhaps capital burning should too?
Gemara Answer
It is more logical to derive a person's death from another person's death (Korah / Nadav) than from the burning of animals. Shared elements outweigh the bull analogy.
Conclusion Both sages agree the method is molten lead — they dispute only which scriptural source teaches it. The verbal analogy is still needed: without it, we would not know this method fulfills the mitzva.

❤️ Love Your Neighbor — Even in Execution

וְאָהַבְתָּ לְרֵעֲךָ כָּמוֹךָ — ויקרא יט:יח
Core Halakhic Principle Rav Naḥman citing Rabba bar Avuh: 'And you shall love your neighbor as yourself' (Lev. 19:18) teaches that even when executing a condemned person, the court must select the most compassionate and least painful death available. Without the verbal analogy, this principle alone would have led us to use molten lead over actual fire. The verbal analogy is still required — to teach that this method actually fulfills the mitzva of burning and is not a legal fiction. This same principle also prohibits cutting a condemned person in half (גִּסְטֵרָא) during decapitation — even technically, one must cause minimal suffering.

⚖️ The Four Death Penalties at a Glance

🪨 Stoning

  • Push off ledge twice body height
  • If not dead: witnesses drop stone on chest
  • Most severe of all four penalties
  • Idol worship, blasphemy, Shabbat violation

🔥 Burning

  • Submerge in dung to knees
  • Scarf forces mouth open
  • Molten lead poured into throat
  • Soul burns; body externally intact
  • Priest's daughter who committed adultery

⚔️ Decapitation

  • Cut off head with sword
  • Like monarchic execution
  • Permitted despite gentile custom — it is Torah law
  • Murderers; people of an idolatrous city

🔗 Strangulation

  • Submerge in dung to knees
  • Rough scarf inside soft one — pull until death
  • Leaves no external mark
  • Default when Torah unspecified
  • Adultery (married woman), various other sins

🔗 Why Strangulation Is the Default Death Penalty

Torah prescribes death for a sin but does not name the method
↓
Rabbi Yonatan: Every unspecified death in Torah = strangulation by default
↓
Why strangulation?
It leaves no external mark on the body — analogous to death at the hand of Heaven
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi adds
Death at Heaven's hand leaves no mark → court execution should likewise leave no external mark → strangulation
↓
⚠️ Objection: Burning (molten lead) also leaves no external mark — should it be the default?
↓
Answer: The Torah explicitly specifies burning for a priest's daughter (Lev. 21:9). If burning were the default, this specification would be redundant. Therefore strangulation remains the default.
