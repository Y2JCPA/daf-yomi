5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Yoḥanan tell Rabbi Zakkai to 'go out and teach it outside'?
A. The baraita was too advanced for the students present
B. The baraita ruled that multiple idol worship rites in one lapse require only one sin-offering, contradicting the accepted halakha
C. Rabbi Zakkai was not yet ordained and lacked authority to teach
D. The baraita contained a scribal error in the verse citation
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan rejected the baraita because the accepted halakha holds that each distinct act of idol worship — even in one lapse of awareness — requires its own sin-offering. The baraita's ruling was incorrect and unfit for the study hall.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Abaye, one who worships an idol out of love or fear of a person is:
A. Exempt, because he never intended to accept the idol as a deity
B. Liable, because this constitutes genuine idol worship and he was unaware it is forbidden
C. Subject to lashes but not a sin-offering
D. Exempt only if he is a scholar who should have known better
Correct: B — Abaye holds that worshipping out of love or fear is a real transgression. The person is unaware that even such worship is forbidden — making him an unwitting transgressor obligated to bring a sin-offering.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rabbi Natan say about why kindling fire was singled out from the labors of Shabbat?
A. To teach that kindling is a lesser prohibition carrying flogging rather than death
B. To exclude it from Shabbat prohibitions entirely on certain occasions
C. To divide the labors — each labor performed in one lapse incurs its own sin-offering
D. To indicate that kindling was already prohibited before Sinai
Correct: C — Rabbi Natan says kindling was singled out to teach division: each category of Shabbat labor performed in a single lapse of awareness is treated as a separate transgression requiring its own sin-offering.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the 'stringency of other mitzvot over Shabbat' described in the baraita (Amud B)?
A. Violating other mitzvot carries more severe punishments than violating Shabbat
B. Other mitzvot apply to non-Jews, whereas Shabbat applies only to Jews
C. With other mitzvot, even a fully unintentional act (without awareness) incurs a sin-offering; Shabbat requires awareness
D. Other mitzvot have two witnesses required for all violations, while Shabbat requires three
Correct: C — The stringency of other mitzvot is that an unintentional act (e.g., swallowing forbidden fat mistaken for spittle) still obligates a sin-offering. Shabbat, by contrast, requires at minimum awareness that it is Shabbat for liability to attach.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Rav Yosef reject Rabbi Abba's comparison between kindling on Shabbat and bowing in idol worship?
A. Bowing is a positive act, not a prohibition, so the categories are incomparable
B. Rabbi Yosei already derives the division of Shabbat labors from Lev. 4:2 independently of kindling, so the parallel to idol worship breaks down since that verse cannot apply there
C. There is no verse in the Torah that mentions bowing to idols as a distinct prohibition
D. Rav Yosef holds that Rabbi Yosei agrees with Rabbi Natan on the question of division
Correct: B — Rav Yosef argues that Rabbi Yosei does not need kindling to derive the division of labors — he derives it from 'from one of them' (Lev. 4:2). Since idol worship cannot be derived from that verse (it applies only to priestly sin), Rabbi Abba's bridge collapses entirely.
