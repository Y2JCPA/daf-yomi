Sanhedrin 93 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav, what happened to Hananiah, Mishael, and Azariah after they emerged from the furnace?
A. They ascended to Eretz Yisrael and had children
B. They drowned in the spittle of the crowds
C. They died as a result of the evil eye
D. They went to dig the great river in Tiberias
Correct: C — Rav says they died from the evil eye — the intense jealousy of all who witnessed their miraculous deliverance. This mirrors the tannaitic opinion of Rabbi Eliezer, one of three positions debated across two generations.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Daniel arrange to be absent when HMA were cast into the furnace?
A. He was afraid of being burned
B. God commanded him to leave Babylonia permanently
C. He feared the verse 'graven images shall you burn' — since Nebuchadnezzar had worshipped him
D. He was sent to bring figs for the exiled elders of Judah
Correct: C — Daniel feared that because Nebuchadnezzar had prostrated himself and worshipped Daniel (Daniel 2:46), Daniel might be burned as an idol. The Gemara identifies three partners — God, Daniel, and Nebuchadnezzar — each with independent reasons for sending Daniel away.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How did Nebuchadnezzar expose Ahab and Zedekiah as false prophets?
A. He checked the prophecy against Scripture and found contradictions
B. He asked Hananiah, Mishael, and Azariah, who confirmed no such divine command existed
C. He consulted Daniel, who denounced them
D. An angel appeared and testified against them
Correct: B — Nebuchadnezzar said: 'Haven't I asked Hananiah, Mishael, and Azariah about this and they told me no such command was given?' — the trustworthy survivors of the furnace served as his standard of truth, immediately discrediting the false prophets' claim.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rava, what unique ability will the Messiah possess, as derived from Isaiah 11:3?
A. He will perform miracles greater than those at the Exodus
B. He will be burdened with mitzvot heavy as millstones
C. He will smell and judge — perceiving guilt or innocence without sight or hearing
D. He will rule by military might over all nations
Correct: C — Rava derives from hariḥo ('and he shall smell') that the Messiah judges by spiritual perception alone — he can sense righteousness and wickedness without needing to see or hear testimony. Bar Kokheva was tested on this ability and failed, proving he was not the Messiah.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why were Joshua the High Priest's garments singed in the furnace when Hananiah, Mishael, and Azariah had emerged completely unharmed?
A. He had aided Ahab and Zedekiah in their false prophecy
B. He had previously bowed to Nebuchadnezzar's image under duress
C. His sons married women unfit for priestly marriage and he failed to rebuke them
D. He entered the furnace voluntarily rather than being thrown in
Correct: C — Rav Pappa explains that Joshua's sons married women who were not fit for marriage into the priesthood, and Joshua did not rebuke them. This sin diminished his protection — the fire was partially effective against him as divine punishment, while his ultimate salvation showed his basic righteousness.
