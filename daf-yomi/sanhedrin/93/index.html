🏁 Where Did HMA Go After the Furnace?

Rav
They died as a result of the evil eye — everyone was jealous of their miraculous deliverance and that envy destroyed them.
Rabbi Yehoshua
They drowned in the spittle of the awestruck crowds who gathered to see them emerge from the fire.
The Rabbis
They ascended to Eretz Yisrael, married wives there, and had children — they lived full lives in the land.
Parallel Tannaitic Dispute This amoraic debate mirrors an earlier tannaitic dispute: Rabbi Eliezer (evil eye), Rabbi Yehoshua (spittle), and the Rabbis (Eretz Yisrael) — the same three positions across two generations.

🏃 Three Partners — Why Daniel Was Absent

How did Daniel avoid being present when the decree against idol-refusers was enforced?
↓
☁️ God
Let Daniel go — so that idol worship would not be attributed to him; his presence would imply divine endorsement of Nebuchadnezzar's image
🧍 Daniel
I will leave — so the verse won't apply to me: 'The graven images of their gods shall you burn with fire' (Deut. 7:25). He feared being burned as an idol
👑 Nebuchadnezzar
Send Daniel away — so the people won't say I burned my own god. Nebuchadnezzar had literally prostrated himself and worshipped Daniel (Daniel 2:46)
↓
Result: Daniel sent away — to dig the great river in Tiberias (Rav) or to bring figs for the elders of Judah (Shmuel)

⚖️ True Servants vs. False Prophets in the Furnace

Hananiah, Mishael & Azariah

  • Refused to bow to Nebuchadnezzar's graven image
  • Emerged from furnace completely unharmed — not a hair singed
  • Angel of God stood with them in the fire
  • Nations of the world acknowledged the God of Israel
  • Their deliverance: proof that the righteous are greater than ministering angels

Ahab ben Kolaiah & Zedekiah ben Maaseiah

  • Falsely claimed God commanded them to commit adultery with Nebuchadnezzar's daughter
  • Exposed: Nebuchadnezzar confirmed that HMA denied any such divine command
  • Chose Joshua the High Priest as a third to be tested with them — hoping his merit would save them
  • Cast into furnace — burned completely; only Joshua survived (singed)
  • Became a byword of cursing: 'God make you like Zedekiah and Ahab' (Jer. 29:22)

👑 The Messiah's Attribute — Smell and Judge

Rabbi Alexandri
hariḥo (Isaiah 11:3) means God burdened the Messiah with mitzvot and afflictions like millstones [reiḥayim] — heavy obligations weigh upon him.
Rava
hariḥo means the Messiah will smell and judge — he will perceive righteousness and guilt by spiritual smell alone, without sight or hearing.
The Sages (re: Bar Kokheva)
Bar Kokheva ruled 2½ years and claimed to be Messiah. The Sages tested him: 'Can you smell and judge?' He could not — so they killed him.
Isaiah 11:2–3 — ישעיהו י״א 'And the spirit of the Lord shall rest upon him… and he shall smell with the fear of the Lord' — six virtues rest on the Messiah; the seventh is supernatural discernment without ordinary perception.
