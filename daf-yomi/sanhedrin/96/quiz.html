Sanhedrin 96 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Abraham's strength weaken when he pursued the kings as far as Dan?
A. He was physically exhausted from the long chase
B. He saw through the Divine Spirit that his descendants would worship idols at Dan
C. He encountered a massive enemy force stationed at Dan
D. Dan was beyond the border of the Promised Land
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan teaches that Abraham foresaw via ruach hakodesh that his descendants (Jeroboam's golden calves) would worship idols at Dan, which weakened him spiritually mid-pursuit.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What specific act earned Nebuchadnezzar his enormous worldly dominion?
A. His military genius in conquering Assyria and Egypt
B. His construction of the great hanging gardens of Babylon
C. Running four paces to correct a letter so that God's name would appear first
D. His relatively merciful treatment of Jewish captives in Babylon
Correct: C — Nebuchadnezzar ran four paces after the royal messenger to retrieve a letter and correct the order of greetings so that God's name would precede Hezekiah's — four steps of reverence that earned him vast reward.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How was the miraculous boiling of Zechariah's blood finally stopped?
A. Nebuzaradan poured water and wine over it as an offering
B. The priests performed a special purification ritual at the altar
C. Nebuzaradan addressed the blood directly: 'The worthy I killed for you — shall I kill them all?' and it ceased
D. It stopped naturally when the Temple walls collapsed in the fire
Correct: C — After massive killings failed to pacify the blood, Nebuzaradan spoke to Zechariah directly. When he offered to kill everyone, acknowledging his accountability, the blood immediately ceased its miraculous boiling.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the baraita distinguish between Naaman's and Nebuzaradan's relationship to Judaism?
A. Both were full converts (gerei tzedek) who observed all commandments
B. Naaman was a ger toshav (Noahide), while Nebuzaradan was a ger tzedek (full convert)
C. Naaman converted fully, while Nebuzaradan only accepted the Noahide laws
D. Neither converted — both merely acknowledged God's power without changing their practice
Correct: B — The baraita explicitly distinguishes them: Naaman was a ger toshav who accepted the seven Noahide laws but did not fully convert, while Nebuzaradan became a ger tzedek — a completely righteous full proselyte.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Rav Naḥman call the Messiah 'bar niflei' (son of the fallen ones)?
A. Because the Messiah will arise from the poorest and most downtrodden of Israel
B. Because the Messiah's father will fall in battle before his birth
C. Based on 'I will raise up the fallen (hanofelet) tabernacle of David' (Amos 9:11)
D. Because the Messiah comes after the fall of the Roman Empire
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman derives the title from Amos 9:11 — 'On that day I will raise up the fallen (הַנֹּפֶלֶת) tabernacle of David.' The Messiah is the one destined to restore what has fallen (נפל) — the Davidic kingdom and Jewish sovereignty.
