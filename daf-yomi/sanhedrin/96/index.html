🏃 Four Paces That Earned an Empire

Royal Scribes
We wrote the letter: 'Greetings to King Hezekiah, greetings to Jerusalem, greetings to the great God.' That is how all royal correspondence is ordered.
Nebuchadnezzar
This is an outrage! God's name must appear first! I must catch the messenger and correct the letter before it reaches Hezekiah!
Gabriel
[Stops Nebuchadnezzar after four paces] His pursuit ends here. If I had not intervened, there would have been no remedy for Israel's enemies.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Those four paces of honor earned Nebuchadnezzar dominion over the entire world. If that is the reward for a wicked man's four steps — how much more will God reward Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob?
Jeremiah's Question Answered 'Why does the path of the wicked prosper?' (Jer. 12:1) — Even four paces of reverence yield enormous reward. This is not injustice; it is the precision of divine accounting.

300 mules laden with iron axes that can cut iron — to breach the Temple gates
↓

Zechariah's Blood Discovered
The prophet's blood still boils in the Temple courtyard — priests claim it is ordinary sacrificial blood
↓
Nebuzaradan kills thousands
Sacrifices, Sanhedrin members, young priests — but the blood will not stop boiling

🕊️ Former Enemies Who Embraced Torah

Naaman the Aramean

  • Commander of Aram's army
  • Healed of tzara'at by Elisha (II Kings 5)
  • Became a ger toshav
  • Accepted the 7 Noahide laws
  • Did not fully convert
  • Refrained from idol worship

Nebuzaradan

  • Babylon's chief executioner
  • Personally destroyed the Temple
  • Witnessed Zechariah's miraculous blood
  • Repented upon reflection on divine justice
  • Became a ger tzedek — full convert
  • Abandoned rank and wealth to do so

Descendants of Haman

  • Haman sought to annihilate all Jews
  • His descendants studied Torah
  • Taught in Bnei Brak
  • Even Nebuchadnezzar's descendants sought God
  • God wished to bring them under His wings
  • Josiah's era prevented this from occurring

🌟 Bar Niflei — The Messiah

בַּיּוֹם הַהוּא אָקִים אֶת סֻכַּת דָּוִיד הַנֹּפֶלֶת — עָמוֹס ט:יא
Who Is Bar Niflei? Rav Naḥman asked Rabbi Yitzḥak: 'Have you heard when bar niflei will come?' Rabbi Yitzḥak asked who this is. Rav Naḥman replied: 'He is the Messiah.' Rabbi Yitzḥak challenged: 'Do you call the Messiah bar niflei?' Rav Naḥman cited Amos 9:11 — 'On that day I will raise up the fallen tabernacle of David (הַנֹּפֶלֶת).' The Messiah is called 'son of the fallen' because he comes to restore what has collapsed — the Davidic kingdom, the Temple, and Israel's glory.
