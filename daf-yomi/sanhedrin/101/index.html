👑 Sacred vs. Profane, Healing, and Lost Kings

שִׁיר הַשִּׁירִים אֵינוֹ נִקְרָא כְּשִׁיר חוּלִין — Song of Songs may not be read as a secular tune
Core Theme of Daf 101 Sanhedrin 101 spans three major threads: (1) the sanctity of Torah texts — especially Song of Songs — and how misuse destroys the world; (2) the boundaries of permitted healing and incantation on Shabbat; and (3) two royal tragedies — Rabbi Eliezer's illness revealing that afflictions carry divine love, and Jeroboam's fatal pride that cost him the World to Come despite his worthy rise to kingship.

📜 Song of Songs — Sacred Text or Drinking Song?

The Sages
One who reads a verse from Song of Songs at a banquet house as a secular song — merely for entertainment — introduces evil to the world and forfeits his share in the World to Come.
Holy One, Blessed be He
My daughter, while people are eating and drinking — in what should they be engaged? Even at the table, Torah must remain sacred. Let them engage in Bible, Mishna, or Talmud according to their level.
The Torah
Before Him she says: If they are masters of Bible — Bible. If masters of Mishna — Mishna. If masters of Talmud — Talmud. Torah belongs at every table, spoken with reverence.
Rabbi Akiva
"All the writings are holy, but the Song of Songs is the Holy of Holies." — It may never be reduced to a drinking tune. Its sanctity is absolute.
Ruling Treating שיר השירים as secular song desecrates holiness and introduces spiritual harm to the world — among the gravest misuses of sacred text.

😔 Four Sages Visit the Ailing Rabbi Eliezer

Rabbi Eliezer falls gravely ill. He weeps and says: "There is intense wrath in the world" — implying his suffering is not random but divinely sent.
↓
Rabbi Tarfon: "You are better for Israel than a drop of rain" — rain helps only in this world; my teacher provides benefit in this world and the World to Come.
↓
Rabbi Yehoshua: "You are better than the sphere of the sun" — the sun illuminates only this world; my teacher illuminates both worlds.
↓
Rabbi Elazar ben Azarya: "You are better than a father and mother" — parents provide only for this world; my teacher provides for this world and the next.
↓
Rabbi Akiva: "חַבִּיבִין יִסּוּרִין — Afflictions are cherished!" Rabbi Eliezer immediately asked to be supported upright to hear this statement — it pierced through all the other consolations.

⚡ Jeroboam — Rise to Kingship and Total Fall

Why He Rose

  • He rebuked King Solomon for his sins — an act of great moral courage
  • God rewarded his boldness by granting him the kingdom of the Ten Tribes
  • Yorovam (ירבעם) = one who was exalted among the nation, or who contended on behalf of the people against the king

Why He Lost Everything

  • He rebuked Solomon publicly, shaming him — forbidden even when rebuke is deserved
  • He removed his phylacteries before Solomon — demonstrating contempt for royal authority
  • He set up golden calves to prevent pilgrimage to Jerusalem — a calculating act of idolatry
  • Feared: "They will see Rehoboam sitting and me standing and abandon me" — pure pride
  • Result: No share in the World to Come — כִּי הָיְתָה הַזֹּאת חַטָּאת לְבֵית יָרָבְעָם

🔮 Three Who Looked but Were Mistaken

Three figures received prophetic visions but misidentified their subject
Each saw something true — but about someone else
↓
Nebat (Jeroboam's father)
Saw fire emerging from his penis → believed he would rule.

Truth: it foretold Jeroboam his son would emerge from him and rule.
Ahithophel
Saw leprosy glowing on his penis → believed he would rule.

Truth: it foretold Solomon would descend from his granddaughter Bathsheba.
Pharaoh's Astrologers
Saw a sign and thought they identified an Egyptian-born threat.

Truth: the sign pointed to Moses at Meribah — waters of contention — not what they feared.
