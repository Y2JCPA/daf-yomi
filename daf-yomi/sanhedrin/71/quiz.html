5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is a boy NOT liable as a stubborn and rebellious son if he steals from his father and eats on his father's property?
A. He did not steal enough meat and wine
B. He is afraid of his father, so this theft is not a sign of dangerous boldness
C. The father did not bring him before the elders
D. He must first be flogged before this applies
Correct: B — The Gemara explains: even though it is accessible, he is afraid of his father. The liability requires easy access AND absence of fear — both are achieved only when he steals from his father but eats on others' property.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Shimon, why was the passage about the stubborn and rebellious son written in the Torah if no such case ever occurred?
A. To serve as a deterrent against bad behavior
B. To provide a legal precedent for future courts
C. לדרוש וקבל שכר — to study the passage and receive reward for Torah learning
D. Because it did occur once in the time of the Sanhedrin
Correct: C — Rabbi Shimon holds the case is entirely theoretical. The Torah wrote it so scholars would analyze it and be rewarded for their Torah study — דרוש וקבל שכר.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why can a city never become an idolatrous city (עיר הנדחת) if even one mezuza is present?
A. The mezuza spiritually protects the city from idolatry
B. A mezuza cannot be burned, but the Torah requires burning all property — so the condemnation is impossible
C. The owner of the mezuza automatically becomes a witness
D. A city with a mezuza is considered under priestly jurisdiction
Correct: B — The Torah requires burning all the city's property. A mezuza contains God's name and cannot be burned. Therefore, any city with a mezuza cannot fulfill the verse's requirement — making the verdict impossible.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ḥanina, if a descendant of Noah cursed God's name and then converted to Judaism, what is his legal status?
A. He is liable to the death penalty as a Jew for the same act
B. He is exempt, because his trial procedure and death penalty both changed
C. He is exempt only if he did not know it was forbidden
D. He must bring a sacrifice but is not liable to death
Correct: B — Rabbi Ḥanina rules he is exempt because both conditions for exemption are met: (1) his trial procedure changed (Noahide court → Jewish court), and (2) the specific death penalty changed.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the fundamental reason the Torah sentences a stubborn and rebellious son to death?
A. Because eating stolen meat and wine is an intrinsically severe sin
B. Because he dishonored his parents, which carries a capital penalty
C. Because he is judged based on his ultimate end — he will grow up to commit murder
D. Because he violated both a positive and negative commandment simultaneously
Correct: C — The mishna states explicitly: he is sentenced על שם סופו — on account of his ultimate end. The Torah foresees he will become a murderer, so it is better he die innocent now than guilty later: מוטב שימות זכאי ואל ימות חייב.
