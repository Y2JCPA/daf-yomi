🏛️ Was There Ever a Stubborn and Rebellious Son?

Tanna Kamma
The Torah sets out the law of the stubborn son — it must apply in practice. We derive real halakhot from it.
Rabbi Shimon
A stubborn son was never executed and never will be. The requirements are so precise they can never all be met simultaneously. The passage exists only לדרוש וקבל שכר — to study and receive reward.
Rabbi Yehuda
His mother must match his father in voice, appearance, and height — an impossible standard. I agree the case is purely theoretical.
Baraita
Similarly: there was never an idolatrous city (עיר הנדחת) and never a house afflicted with leprosy (נגע הבית) — these too exist only for theoretical study.
Principle Three 'never-was' laws: Rebellious Son, Idolatrous City, Leprous House — studied for intellectual reward, not practical execution.

🏙️ Why Idolatrous City & Leprous House Could Never Exist

עיר הנדחת — Idolatrous City

  • Requires majority of city to sin
  • Cannot contain even one mezuza
  • A mezuza cannot be burned (it contains God's name)
  • Every Jewish home has a mezuza → impossible to condemn entire city
  • Rabbi Akiva: Even one Torah scholar blocks condemnation

נגע הבית — Leprous House

  • Mark must appear precisely in the corner (Rabbi Elazar b. Rabbi Shimon)
  • Requires two stones, two beams, two handfuls of mortar
  • Technically nearly impossible to fulfill
  • Yet: Rabbi Eliezer b. Rabbi Tzadok testifies a leprous ruin existed near Gaza
  • Proof that the law can apply — it is not purely theoretical

🔄 Descendant of Noah Who Converts — Still Liable?

Rabbi Ḥanina
A ben Noach who cursed God's name and then converted is exempt — his trial procedure changed, and the death penalty changed.
Gemara Challenge
What about murder or adultery? A gentile who killed or had relations with a married woman, then converted — he is also exempt per the baraita. Why?
Resolution
For exemption we need both: (1) change in trial procedure, AND (2) change in the specific death penalty. Murder: gentile dies by sword, Jew dies by sword — same penalty, so still liable?
Rav Shimon (School of Menashe)
All Noahide capital punishment is by strangulation (חנק). So even murder shifts from strangulation → sword. The lenient is absorbed in the severe (קל בחמור).
Rule קל בחמור — if the convert now faces a more severe penalty, the prior lenient sentence is subsumed. If now less severe, exempt — the new law replaces the old.

🔮 Rebellious Son — Punished for the Future

The Principle: בן סורר ומורה נידון על שם סופו — A stubborn and rebellious son is judged based on his ultimate end, not his current act (eating meat and wine).
↓
The Torah foresees: this boy will exhaust his father's wealth, then seek to satisfy his cravings, and ultimately commit murder to fund his appetites.
↓
Therefore: it is better for him to die while innocent (before sinning gravely) than to die after becoming wicked — מוטב שימות זכאי ואל ימות חייב.
↓
Wine & Sleep of the Wicked — beneficial to them AND to the world: when drunk or asleep, they cannot sin against others.
↓
Wine & Sleep of the Righteous — detrimental to them AND to the world: every moment they sleep or drink, the world loses their Torah and good deeds.
↓
Dispersal of the Wicked — good for them (less provocation to sin) and good for the world. Dispersal of the Righteous — bad for both.
