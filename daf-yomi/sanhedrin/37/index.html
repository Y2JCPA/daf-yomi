🌍 Whoever Saves One Soul

כָּל הַמְאַבֵּד נֶפֶשׁ אַחַת מִיִּשְׂרָאֵל מַעֲלֶה עָלָיו הַכָּתוּב כְּאִלּוּ אִבֵּד עוֹלָם מָלֵא — וְכָל הַמְקַיֵּם נֶפֶשׁ אַחַת מִיִּשְׂרָאֵל מַעֲלֶה עָלָיו הַכָּתוּב כְּאִלּוּ קִיֵּם עוֹלָם מָלֵא
The Great Principle of Daf 37 Adam was created alone to teach that whoever destroys one soul of Israel, the Torah considers it as if he destroyed an entire world — and whoever saves one soul of Israel, it is as if he saved an entire world. Every person is stamped with the same divine seal, yet each emerges utterly unique.

🔵 The Sanhedrin Sits in a Semicircle

שָׁרְרֵךְ אַגַּן הַסַּהַר — זוֹ סַנְהֶדְרִין, שֶׁיּוֹשֶׁבֶת בְּטַבּוּרוֹ שֶׁל עוֹלָם
Source: Song of Songs 7:2–3 Rabbi Aḥa bar Ḥanina derives the semicircle from the verse "Your navel is like a round goblet". "Navel" = the Sanhedrin, sitting at the navel of the world. "Round goblet" = the semicircular formation, so judges can see each other. "Heap of wheat set about with lilies" = all benefit from their Torah, and even the seemingly empty among Israel are full of mitzvot as a pomegranate — the source for trusting Jews not to sin even in private.

⚠️ Warning Witnesses: What Is Conjecture?

Court
Perhaps you saw the accused pursuing a man into a ruin, then found him with a sword dripping blood and a corpse at his feet — that is conjecture, not testimony. You did not witness the killing itself.
R. Shimon ben Shatah
I swear by oath: I once saw exactly this scene — yet I could not convict him, for the Torah requires two witnesses to the act itself. I said: "He who knows thoughts will exact punishment." A snake came and bit the murderer before he left the ruin.
Rav Yosef
Since the Temple's destruction the Sanhedrin no longer executes — yet Heaven still punishes in kind. One liable to stoning falls from a roof; liable to burning falls into fire; liable to strangling drowns or suffocates.
Ruling Principle Circumstantial evidence, however compelling, is never sufficient for a capital conviction in Jewish law. The court demands direct witness to the act.

🍷 Monetary Law vs. Capital Law

Monetary Law

  • False testimony → pay money → atonement possible
  • Error can be corrected through repayment
  • Conjecture has relative leniency in practice
  • Hearsay warning less urgently stressed
  • Witnesses bear financial, not mortal, responsibility

Capital Law

  • False testimony → cannot restore a life — no atonement
  • Error is permanent and irreversible
  • Conjecture absolutely forbidden
  • Hearsay explicitly ruled out
  • Witness bears guilt for victim AND all future descendants

🌍 Why Adam Was Created Alone — Three Lessons

God created Adam alone — a single human being, one origin for all humanity
↓
Lesson 1: Human Equality
No person may say "my ancestor is greater than yours" — all descend from a single progenitor
Lesson 2: Infinite Value
Destroying one soul = destroying a world
Saving one soul = saving a world
Each person contains multitudes of potential descendants
Lesson 3: Divine Uniqueness
A king stamps coins — all identical. God stamps all humanity with one seal — each person emerges utterly unique
↓
Therefore every person must say: בִּשְׁבִילִי נִבְרָא הָעוֹלָם — the world was created for my sake
