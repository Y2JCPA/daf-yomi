🗡️ Rodef: Must One Use Minimal Force?

Rav Pappa
The mishna (listing his sister) discusses seduction — in seduction, all agree one may not kill the pursuer to save the pursued, since she is not being coerced.
Abaye
The mishna discusses rape, in a case where one could have saved her by injuring — not killing — the pursuer. He killed anyway, so he is liable.
R' Yonatan b. Shaul
If one could save the pursued by injuring a limb of the pursuer, he may not kill the pursuer. If he did kill, he is liable — as derived from the case of fighting men who strike a pregnant woman.
📜 Scriptural Source "If men strive and strike a pregnant woman…" (Shemot 21:22) — the bystander who caused death is liable despite saving life, proving that unnecessary lethal force is forbidden.

🔥 The Three Cardinal Sins

כל עבירות שבתורה יעבור ואל יהרג, חוץ מעבודה זרה, וגילוי עריות, ושפיכות דמים
יהרג ואל יעבור Rabbi Yoḥanan taught in the name of Rabbi Shimon ben Yehotzadak: The Sages convened in the upper story of the house of Nitza in Lydda and ruled — for ALL prohibitions in the Torah, if one is threatened with death, transgress and live (ya'avor v'al yehareg). Exceptions — one must die rather than transgress: • Avodah Zarah — Idol worship • Giluy Arayot — Forbidden sexual relations • Shefichat Damim — Murder Source for forbidden relations and murder: derived via gezeirah shavah from the case of a betrothed maiden — just as one must die to prevent murder of another, so too for sexual immorality.

📢 Public vs. Private — The Persecution Factor

Not a Time of Persecution

  • Threatened privately: transgress rather than die (except the three)
  • Threatened publicly (10+ Jews present): die rather than transgress
  • Applies even to a minor mitzva — even changing sandal straps!

Time of Religious Persecution (Shemad)

  • Enemies seek to uproot Jewish practice specifically
  • Must die rather than transgress even a minor custom
  • Applies even privately — no distinction
  • Source: 'You shall not profane My holy name' (Vayikra 22:32)

👑 The Case of Esther — A Public Sin?

Question
Esther's cohabitation with Ahasuerus was a public act of forbidden relations. Why did she not surrender her life rather than transgress?
Abaye
Esther was passive — she did not act but was acted upon (karka olam, like the ground). The prohibition applies only to one who actively commits the sin.
Rava
When gentiles order transgression for their own pleasure — not to persecute Israel or make them abandon their religion — it is different. One need not die in such a case.
Rava (proof)
We allow giving coal shovels to Persian priests for their fire-temples on festival days — this is for their pleasure, not persecution. So too, Esther's situation was for Ahasuerus's pleasure.
⚖️ Rava's Rule If a gentile orders a Jew to cut grass on Shabbat under pain of death — he should cut it and not be killed, since the gentile wants the grass, not the Jew's apostasy.
