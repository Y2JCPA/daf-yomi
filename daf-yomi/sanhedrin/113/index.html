🏗️ Ahab and Elijah at Hiel's House of Mourning

Ahab
Perhaps Joshua only cursed rebuilding Jericho under its own name, or naming another city Jericho. Hiel did neither — so the curse should not have applied!
Elijah
Abiram died at the foundation and Segub died at the gates — the curse was fulfilled letter for letter. There is no dismissing divine prophecy!
Ahab
But if Joshua's curse about a single city was so literally fulfilled, then Moses wrote that Israel would serve other gods — yet that too must surely be void by now...
Elijah
As the Lord God of Israel lives — there shall be no dew or rain these years, except according to my word! (I Kings 17:1)
Talmudic Context Ahab was Hiel's close friend and groomsman. His dismissal of Moses' warnings at a moment of grief provoked Elijah's most dramatic oath, initiating the three-year drought (I Kings 17–18).

🏗️ Joshua's Curse on Jericho — Fulfilled Across Generations

Joshua curses (Joshua 6:26): Whoever rebuilds Jericho shall lose his firstborn at the foundation and his youngest at the setting of the gates
↓
Hiel the Bethelite rebuilds Jericho during the reign of King Ahab
↓
Foundation laid
Abiram his firstborn dies — attributed to chance by Hiel
Gates erected
Segub his youngest son dies — inexcusable to continue
↓
Baraita: Hiel should have learned from Abiram's death not to continue. Segub's death was therefore entirely his fault.

🔑 The Three Keys God Keeps and Does Not Delegate

🌧️ Key of Rain

  • Elijah seized this key with his drought oath
  • Normally held exclusively by God
  • Rain withheld for three-plus years over Israel
  • Returned by Elijah when he needed the resurrection key
  • Basis: a servant cannot hold two keys when master's home is not empty

👶 Key of Childbirth

  • God alone grants or withholds birth
  • Never delegated to prophet or angel
  • Rachel's barrenness — 'Am I in the place of God?' (Gen 30:2)
  • Elisha and the Shunamite woman parallel
  • Symbolizes God's direct sovereignty over life's origin

☀️ Key of Resurrection

  • Elijah requested this to revive the widow's son
  • God granted it — replacing Elijah's rain key
  • One messenger, one key: divine economy of miracles
  • Rain returned only after the resurrection
  • Prefigures ultimate resurrection in messianic era

⚡ Rabbi Yosei of Tzippori and Father Elijah

כל זמן שרשעים בעולם — חרון אף בעולם; אבדו רשעים מן העולם — נסתלק חרון אף מן העולם
Aggadic Teaching (113b) Elijah was accustomed to revealing himself to Rabbi Yosei of Tzippori each day. Once, he was absent for three days. When he finally returned, Rabbi Yosei asked: 'Why did the Master not come?' Elijah replied that he had been occupied — he had to rouse Rabbi Yosei's colleagues who were debating. This aggadah illustrates that even heavenly messengers operate within the constraints of divine mission. The Gemara then connects to the principle: when the wicked exist in the world, there is wrath in the world — and when they perish, wrath departs.
