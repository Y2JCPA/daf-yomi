⚖️ The Great Equinox Debate

Rabbi Yehuda
The equinox day concludes the prior season. Intercalate if the Tammuz season lacks completion for 20 days into Tishrei — two-thirds of the month.
Rabbi Yosei
The equinox day begins the new season. Intercalate only if the Tammuz season lacks completion for 21 days into Tishrei — one day more.
The Core Issue Both expound the same verse: "Festival of the Ingathering at the turn of the year." One day's difference in how the equinox is counted shifts the entire intercalation threshold.

⚔️ Four Opinions on Intercalation Thresholds

Rabbi Yehuda

  • Equinox concludes the prior season
  • Intercalate if Tammuz season lacks completion for 20 days into Tishrei
  • Two-thirds of the month is the threshold
  • Passover must fall in the season of Aviv

Rabbi Yosei

  • Equinox begins the new season
  • Intercalate if season lacks completion for 21 days into Tishrei
  • One day stricter due to equinox belonging to next season
  • Winter season before Passover: 16 days into Nisan

Rabbi Shimon

  • Even if season lacks 16 days into Tishrei — intercalate
  • Stricter than Rabbi Yosei for Sukkot
  • Ensures all of Sukkot falls after the equinox
  • Disputes whether Yosei's silence implies non-intercalation

Aḥerim (Others)

  • Intercalate for even a minority of the month
  • Threshold: 14 days into Nisan or Tishrei
  • Cannot simply add a day to Adar — must add full month
  • All of Sukkot must occur after the equinox

🤲 Laying of Hands: How Many Judges Required?

Rabbi Shimon
Three judges. The verse says "congregation shall lay" — a congregation requires minimum three. The word 'elders' is needed for the act itself, not the count.
Rabbi Yehuda
Five judges. "Elders" (plural = at least 2) + "congregation" (minimum 3) = five. Each word contributes to the number.
Gemara's Analysis Rabbi Yehuda: if the verse wrote only "on the head of the bull," one might think adjacent placement suffices. The verse specifies laying hands on the head to require actual contact — proving physical action matters.

👨‍⚖️ Rabbinic Ordination: Principles & Heroism

Biblical Source: Moses laid hands on Joshua (Numbers 27:23) — "He laid his hands on him and commanded him before Elazar." This establishes the precedent for ordination.
↓
Three judges required — not one alone. Derived from the communal context of Joshua's appointment before the entire congregation of Israel.
↓
Ordination is by name, not hands. Rav Ashi: the recipient is called "Rabbi" publicly and granted formal permission to adjudicate cases involving dinei kenasot (laws of fines).
↓
Geographic requirement: Both the ordainer and the ordained must be in Eretz Yisrael. Ordination cannot be granted or received outside the Land of Israel.
↓
Rabbi Yehuda ben Bava: During Roman persecutions that forbade ordination on pain of death, he ordained five students in the valley between Usha and Shefaram. Roman soldiers pierced him with lances — his martyrdom saved the laws of fines from extinction.
