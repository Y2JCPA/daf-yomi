Sanhedrin 13 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the fundamental dispute between Rabbi Yehuda and Rabbi Yosei about the equinox?
A. Whether the equinox is calculated astronomically or by observation
B. Whether the equinox day concludes the old season or begins the new one
C. Whether Sukkot or Passover is the primary reference festival
D. Whether the court may intercalate without a full Sanhedrin
Correct: B — The Gemara explicitly states: the difference between them concerns whether the equinox day concludes the prior season (Rabbi Yehuda, threshold 20 days) or begins the new season (Rabbi Yosei, threshold 21 days).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda, how many days into Tishrei must the Tammuz season be lacking before the court intercalates?
A. 16 days
B. 14 days
C. 20 days
D. 21 days
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehuda says two-thirds of Tishrei — which equals 20 days. He holds the equinox concludes the prior season, so the threshold is one day less than Rabbi Yosei's 21.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How many judges does Rabbi Yehuda require for the laying of hands by the Sages?
A. Three
B. Five
C. Seven
D. Twenty-three
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda derives five: 'elders' (plural = minimum 2) + 'congregation' (minimum 3) = 5. Rabbi Shimon requires only three, using 'elders' for the act itself rather than the count.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, how is rabbinic ordination actually performed?
A. By physically laying hands on the candidate's head before the Ark
B. By name — calling the person 'Rabbi' and granting permission to judge fines
C. By a written document signed and sealed by three ordained judges
D. By the candidate successfully ruling on three disputed cases
Correct: B — Rav Ashi clarifies that ordination is done by name: the candidate is publicly called 'Rabbi' and granted permission to adjudicate dinei kenasot (laws of fines) — not through physical hand-laying.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why is Rabbi Yehuda ben Bava remembered with special praise in the Gemara?
A. He authored the rules of calendar intercalation still used today
B. He ordained five students between Usha and Shefaram despite a Roman death decree, preserving laws of fines
C. He was the last Sage to receive ordination directly tracing back to Moses
D. He ruled that ordination could be performed outside Eretz Yisrael in emergencies
Correct: B — During Roman persecution that outlawed ordination on pain of death, Rabbi Yehuda ben Bava ordained five students in a valley between Usha and Shefaram. He was killed by Roman soldiers but his act saved dinei kenasot from being lost to Israel.
