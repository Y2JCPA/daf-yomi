🧱 Why Was Adam Created Alone?

Adam was created alone
Baraita, Sanhedrin 38a — but for what reason?
↓
Theological
So heretics cannot say "many powers exist — each created a different human"
Social Peace
So families won't quarrel, boasting "our ancestor is greater than yours"
Human Dignity
To teach: one who saves a single soul is as if he saved an entire world

👼 Should Man Be Created? Angels vs. God

God
I wish to create a human being upon the earth.
First Angels
"What is man that You are mindful of him, and the son of man that You think of him?" (Ps. 8:5) — such a creature is not worth creating!
God
[Outstretches His small finger among them — they are consumed by fire]
Second Angels
Master of the Universe! The first group erred — but we ask the same: What is man?
God
[Burns the second group as well]
Third Angels
Master of the Universe, the first two groups erred before You. Do what is best in Your eyes — we will not oppose.
God
Man is created. Even seeing the generation of the Flood and the Tower of Babel — I do not regret it. As Scripture states: the first angel groups were correct in warning Me.
Rav Yehuda citing Rav Sanhedrin 38b — the third group's submission is what allowed human creation to proceed

🌍 How Great Was Adam? Two Tannaitic Views

Rav Yehuda citing Rav

  • Adam spanned from one end of the world to the other
  • Source: "from one end of heaven unto the other" (Deut. 4:32)
  • Interpretation: horizontal cosmic breadth
  • When he sinned, God placed His hand on him and diminished his size

Rabbi Elazar

  • Adam's height was from the ground to the firmament
  • Same verse: Adam stood 'upon the earth' and rose to 'the end of heaven'
  • Interpretation: vertical cosmic height
  • After sin, God placed His hand and reduced his stature

🗣️ Adam Spoke Aramaic and Saw All Generations

מַה־יָּקְרוּ רֵעֶיךָ אֵל — תהילים קלט:יז
Rav Yehuda citing Rav / Reish Lakish Adam spoke Aramaic — derived from Psalms 139:17 read as Adam's voice: "How weighty are Your thoughts to me, O God." Furthermore, Reish Lakish teaches: The verse "This is the book of the generations of Adam" (Gen. 5:1) teaches that God showed Adam every future generation — its scholars, its scribes, its leaders — before the world was created. Adam perceived all of them inscribed in that primordial book.
