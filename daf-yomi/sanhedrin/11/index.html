🤫 Shmuel HaKatan Protects the Unknown Intruder

Rabban Gamliel
I said: Bring me seven Sages — no more! Whoever entered this loft without being summoned must leave at once!
Shmuel HaKatan
I am the one who ascended without permission. I did not come to participate in intercalating the year — I came only to observe and learn the practical halakha.
Rabban Gamliel
Sit, sit! All the years are worthy of blessing only because of you. But henceforth, no one may enter without being summoned.
The Hidden Truth Shmuel HaKatan had not actually entered uninvited. He willingly accepted humiliation to shield the true offender from shame — embodying the very trait he learned from the chain of tradition stretching back to Joshua.

⛓️ The Chain of Learned Self-Sacrifice

Joshua (Yehoshua 7:10)
When Israel sinned at Ai, God said: 'Get up — why do you fall on your face? Israel has sinned.' Joshua implicated the nation rather than expose the individual sinner.
↓
Shecaniah ben Jehiel (Ezra 10:2)
Though he himself had not sinned, he declared: 'We have betrayed our God' — taking collective blame to protect others from shame.
↓
Shmuel HaKatan
Falsely claimed to be the uninvited intruder at Rabban Gamliel's loft, saving the true offender from disgrace.
↓
Rabbi Meir → Rabbi Ḥiyya
Each generation passed down this noble characteristic: willingly bearing embarrassment to spare another person's dignity.

🕊️ The Departure of the Divine Spirit

משמת חגי זכריה ומלאכי נסתלקה רוח הקודש מישראל — ואף על פי כן היו משתמשין בבת קול
Talmudic Teaching After the deaths of Haggai, Zechariah, and Malachi — the last three prophets — the Ruach HaKodesh (Divine Spirit) departed from Israel. Yet Heaven did not go entirely silent: the Sages could still receive communication through a Bat Kol (Heavenly Echo). When Sages reclined in the loft at Yavne, a Bat Kol declared: There is one among you fit for the Divine Presence to rest upon him — but his generation is not worthy. All eyes turned to Shmuel HaKatan. The generation's unworthiness, not his own, withheld the gift. When Shmuel HaKatan died, the Sages mourned: Alas, the pious man! Alas, the humble man! They wished to say the same of Yehuda ben Bava, but he was martyred by the Roman government and those executed by the state may not be publicly eulogized.

📋 Rules Governing Intercalation of the Year

CategoryRuleIf Violated
Who decidesOnly with the Nasi's agreementCannot intercalate without him
LocationMust be in Judea; Galilee is valid ex post factoIf outside Eretz Yisrael — invalid
Time of dayOnly during the dayIf done at night — invalid
Valid agricultural reasonsUnripe grain, unripe fruit, late equinox (equinox alone is insufficient)Not intercalated for animals alone
Invalid reasonsYoung goats, lambs, or fledgling dovesMay not intercalate for these
Regional requirementTwo of three regions (Judea, Transjordan, Galilee) must need itOne region alone is insufficient

📬 Rabban Gamliel's Three Letters Announcing Intercalation

Letter 1 — Galilee

  • To the Upper Galilee and Lower Galilee
  • May your peace increase
  • Time has come to separate tithes from olive oil
  • The year has been intercalated — an extra Adar is added

Letter 2 — Judea & South

  • To the people of the South (Darom)
  • May your peace increase
  • Time has come to separate tithes from grain
  • The year has been intercalated

Letter 3 — Diaspora

  • To Babylonia, Media, and all Jewish Diaspora
  • May your peace greatly increase
  • We inform you: the doves are tender and the lambs are thin
  • The year has been intercalated — observe 30 extra days of Adar
