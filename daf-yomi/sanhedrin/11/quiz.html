Sanhedrin 11 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Shmuel HaKatan claim to be the uninvited intruder at Rabban Gamliel's loft?
A. He had genuinely entered without being summoned and needed to confess
B. He wanted to demonstrate his eagerness to learn the laws of intercalation
C. To protect the true uninvited person from public embarrassment
D. He hoped Rabban Gamliel would allow him to stay and participate
Correct: C — Shmuel HaKatan willingly took blame upon himself to shield whoever had actually entered without permission — a noble act of self-sacrifice to spare another person's dignity, a trait he learned from the chain of tradition going back to Joshua.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
From whom did Shmuel HaKatan learn the characteristic of implicating himself to spare others embarrassment?
A. Rabbi Meir
B. Rabbi Ḥiyya
C. Joshua bin Nun
D. Shecaniah ben Jehiel
Correct: D — The Gemara traces the chain directly: Shmuel HaKatan learned from Shecaniah ben Jehiel, who said 'we have betrayed our God' (Ezra 10:2) even though he himself had not sinned, in order to protect others from shame.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why was Shmuel HaKatan not granted the Ruach HaKodesh, even though a Bat Kol declared he was worthy?
A. He had not yet mastered all areas of Torah
B. His generation was not worthy of such a gift
C. The Divine Spirit had permanently and irrevocably left Israel
D. He himself declined the gift out of humility
Correct: B — The Bat Kol announced that Shmuel HaKatan was personally fit for the Divine Presence to rest upon him — but his generation was unworthy. Prophetic gifts depend on the worthiness of the generation, not solely the individual.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which of the following is explicitly stated as NOT a valid reason to intercalate the year?
A. The roads to Jerusalem are too damaged for pilgrims to travel
B. The equinox will fall on the 16th of Nisan or later
C. Young goats and lambs are still too small for Passover sacrifice
D. The barley grain has not yet ripened in time for the Omer offering
Correct: C — The Baraita explicitly rules that the year may NOT be intercalated because of young goats, lambs, or fledgling doves. While valid reasons include unripe grain, unripe fruit, damaged roads, and a late equinox, the condition of sacrificial animals is not among them.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If the court intercalates the year at night, what is the halakhic result?
A. It is valid but must be ratified by the Nasi the following day
B. It is valid according to Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel but disputed by the Rabbis
C. It is invalid — the year is not intercalated
D. It is valid only if done in Judea with the Nasi present
Correct: C — The Baraita states clearly: the court may intercalate the year only during the day. If intercalation was performed at night, it is halakhically invalid and the year is not considered intercalated.
