🦅 Rabbi Yehuda ben Bava: Martyr for Ordination

Rome decreed death for anyone who ordained or received ordination — the chain of semikha was on the verge of extinction
↓
Rabbi Yehuda ben Bava chose a spot between Usha and Shefaram, between two Shabbat boundaries — desolate, unaffiliated with any city, beyond Roman jurisdiction's easy reach
↓
He ordained five students: Rabbi Meir, Rabbi Yehuda bar Ilai, Rabbi Shimon bar Yoḥai, Rabbi Yosi bar Ḥalafta, Rabbi Elazar ben Shamua
↓
Roman troops discovered them. He urged his students: "My sons, run! I am as immovable as a bed of nails — I cannot flee."
↓
The Romans pierced him with 300 iron spears. He died so the chain of ordination — and the power to impose fines — would survive for generations.

⚖️ Ordination Outside Eretz Yisrael: Is It Valid?

Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi
There is no ordination outside Eretz Yisrael — the ceremony itself cannot be performed outside the Land.
Gemara Challenge
But we see ordained judges adjudicating fine cases outside Israel — if ordination is impossible outside the Land, how does this work?
Resolution
One ordained inside Israel may travel and adjudicate fines abroad. The ban is on performing the ordination ceremony outside the Land.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
If those ordaining are inside Israel and those being ordained are outside — permitted. If ordainers are outside — forbidden.
Gemara Proof
Rabbi Yoḥanan was distressed that Rav Shemen bar Abba was absent when the Nasi gave consent. This shows Nasi's consent is a formal requirement for valid ordination.
Three Requirements for Valid Ordination (1) Three ordained judges present; (2) Nasi's consent obtained; (3) Ceremony performed within Eretz Yisrael

🐄 Egla Arufa: How Many Judges Are Required?

Rabbis (Tanna Kamma)

  • "Your elders" (plural) = 2 judges
  • "Your judges" (plural) = 2 more judges
  • Total of 4 — even number, so add 1
  • Result: 5 judges
  • Must be members of the Great Sanhedrin in Jerusalem

Rabbi Shimon

  • "Your elders" (plural) = 2 judges
  • "Your judges" = denotes quality, not quantity
  • No even-number problem arises
  • Result: 3 judges
  • Still must be Sanhedrin members — verbal analogy from Leviticus

Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov

  • "Your elders and your judges" = Sanhedrin required
  • Agrees the panel must measure the distance
  • Holds: 5 judges total
  • Proven via rebellious elder (zaken mamre) baraita
  • Even if Sanhedrin went out to Beit Pagei — all went

🙈 Rabbi Zeira, Ordination Songs & the House of Eli

לָא כָּחַל וְלָא שָׂרַק וְלָא פִּירְכּוּס — וְנָאָה חֵן עוֹלָמִים
Humility, Honor & Lineage Rabbi Zeira hid himself repeatedly to avoid being ordained, following Rabbi Elazar's maxim: "Always be obscure and remain alive" — humility and anonymity protect a person.

When finally ordained, the Sages sang to him: "She wears no blue eye shadow, no rouge on her face, no hair dye — yet she radiates grace." Meaning: Rabbi Zeira needed no external credentials; his inner greatness spoke for itself.

When Rabbi Abbahu visited the emperor's court, the ladies sang: "O great one among his people, leader among his brothers, a candelabrum of pure gold in the sacred place."

House of Eli excluded: Rabbi Shmuel bar Naḥman in the name of Rabbi Yonatan: No sage from the house of Eli may ever be ordained — "There shall not be an elder in your house forever" (I Samuel 2:32).
