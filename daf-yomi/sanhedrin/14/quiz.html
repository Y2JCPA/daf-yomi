Sanhedrin 14 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Where exactly did Rabbi Yehuda ben Bava perform the secret ordination, and why was that location chosen?
A. In the city of Usha, because it had strong walls for protection
B. Between Usha and Shefaram, between two Shabbat boundaries — a desolate area not belonging to any city
C. In a cave near Jerusalem, to be close to the Temple site
D. In Babylon, because Roman law did not extend there
Correct: B — He chose a desolate area between the two cities' Shabbat boundaries so Roman authorities could not punish any specific community. The location was deliberately unaffiliated and obscure.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi, what exactly is prohibited regarding ordination outside Eretz Yisrael?
A. Ordained judges may never travel or adjudicate outside Israel
B. The ordination ceremony itself cannot be performed outside Israel, though those already ordained may adjudicate abroad
C. All rabbinic functions including teaching Torah are banned outside Israel
D. Only the Nasi himself may perform ordination anywhere
Correct: B — The prohibition is on performing the ordination ceremony outside the Land. A judge already ordained in Israel retains that status and may adjudicate fine cases even when outside Israel.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How do the Rabbis derive that five judges are needed for egla arufa, and how does Rabbi Shimon arrive at three?
A. Rabbis count the letters in the verse; Rabbi Shimon uses a verbal analogy
B. Rabbis read 'elders' and 'judges' as each adding two, then add one to avoid an even number; Rabbi Shimon reads 'judges' as denoting quality, not quantity
C. Rabbis require 5 because egla arufa is a capital-adjacent case; Rabbi Shimon holds it is civil
D. Both derive from the same verbal analogy but disagree about which verse is primary
Correct: B — The plural 'elders' = 2 and plural 'judges' = 2 more, totaling 4. Since courts need odd numbers, the Rabbis add one = 5. Rabbi Shimon reads 'judges' as a qualitative descriptor (Sanhedrin members), not an additional count, yielding 3.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Zeira repeatedly hide from the Sages to avoid being ordained?
A. He believed he was not yet sufficiently learned
B. He came from the House of Eli and was therefore ineligible
C. He followed Rabbi Elazar's principle that obscurity and humility prolong life
D. He feared Roman retribution for accepting ordination
Correct: C — Rabbi Elazar taught: 'Always be obscure and remain alive.' Rabbi Zeira internalized this teaching about the virtue of humility and the protective value of anonymity.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the unresolved dilemma raised by Rabbi Yirmeya about three partners sharing one purse?
A. Whether their partnership agreement requires witnesses or written documentation
B. Whether for purposes of valuation panels they are counted as three separate individuals or as one entity
C. Whether each partner must separately tithe their share of the profits
D. Whether they may jointly serve as judges in a monetary dispute
Correct: B — If they count as three individuals, each contributes independently and three separate panels might be needed. If they count as one, different rules apply. The Gemara does not resolve this question.
