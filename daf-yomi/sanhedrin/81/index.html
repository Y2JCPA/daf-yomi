📜 Interpreting Ezekiel 18: Deriving Dual-Penalty Halakha

Rabba bar Natan
From Ezekiel 18: 'shedder of blood' = liable to beheading; 'defiled neighbor's wife' = liable to stoning. Both sins listed yet only one verdict issued — the more severe (stoning). This is the source for the ruling!
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
Objection! Perhaps all sins in Ezekiel 18 are punishable by stoning: 'shedder of blood' = the wayward and rebellious son (stoning); 'defiled neighbor's wife' = stoning too. If so, no new halakha is derived at all!
Gemara (Response)
If all the transgressions are stoning anyway, what new teaching does Ezekiel add? Nothing. But under the first interpretation, a genuine halakha is derived: when penalties differ, the more severe applies. Therefore the first reading stands.
KEY VERSE Ezekiel 18:10–13 lists multiple capital sins under a single verdict — implying the court selects the most severe applicable penalty

⚖️ Sages vs. Rabbi Yosei: One Act, Two Penalties

Sages / Mishna

  • Two transgressions, different penalties → more severe applies
  • One act that violates two prohibitions → still apply the more severe
  • Example: intercourse with married daughter-in-law → burning (for in-law) overrides stoning (for married woman)

Rabbi Yosei

  • One act, two penalties → sentenced for the penalty of the first prohibition
  • Principle: a new prohibition cannot take effect where one already exists
  • Exception: Rabbi Yosei concedes when an 'expanded prohibition' later adds a person to the forbidden category

↓
2nd violation → court administers lashes again
↓
3rd violation (repeated pattern established) → defendant placed in the kippah
↓

↓
Dimensions (Rav Yehuda): exactly the prisoner's full height — so tight he cannot move
↓
Rationale: court cannot formally execute him, but God will 'avenge his blood' — confinement brings death
