Sanhedrin 81 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, when a defendant is liable for two different court-imposed death penalties, what is the halakha?
A. He receives both penalties carried out in sequence
B. He is sentenced to the more severe form of execution
C. He is sentenced to the less severe penalty as a form of leniency
D. The court chooses based on whichever transgression was committed first
Correct: B — The Mishna rules that when liable for two different death penalties, the defendant is sentenced to the more severe — the court always applies the stricter applicable punishment.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak objects to Rabba bar Natan's reading of Ezekiel 18. What is his objection?
A. Ezekiel is speaking about the afterlife, not earthly courts
B. The verse refers only to intentional sins, not accidental ones
C. All transgressions in the verse could be punishable by stoning, making it derive nothing new
D. The verse contradicts an explicit ruling in the Torah
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman argues that all the sins in Ezekiel 18 might be punishable by stoning (e.g., 'shedder of blood' = the wayward son), so the verse would teach no new halakha. The Gemara rejects this because Rabba's reading at least derives something new.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What are the dimensions of the kippah according to Rav Yehuda?
A. A large dungeon shared with other prisoners
B. Small enough that the prisoner must always crouch
C. A cell exactly the full height of the prisoner, leaving no room to move
D. The same size as a standard prison cell in that era
Correct: C — Rav Yehuda teaches that the kippah is constructed to be exactly the height of the transgressor — so tight that he cannot move within it, making it an instrument of slow death.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What diet is prescribed for the murderer placed in the kippah (Mishna, 81b)?
A. Meat and wine to hasten natural death
B. Complete fasting with water only
C. Sparing bread and scant water (Isaiah 30:20)
D. Barley bread until the stomach ruptures
Correct: C — The Mishna for the murderer (81b) specifies 'sparing bread and scant water' from Isaiah 30:20. Note: the recidivist transgressor receives barley bread until the stomach ruptures — the diets differ between the two kippah cases.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Regarding the zealot who kills someone engaging in intercourse with an Aramean woman — what limitation does the Gemara imply?
A. The zealot must first obtain permission from the Nasi
B. The killing is only permitted if done during the act itself, not after
C. The zealot must issue a verbal warning before striking
D. Only a kohen may act as a zealot in this case
Correct: B — The zealot may only act during the act itself (כְּדֶרֶךְ שֶׁפִּינְחָס עָשָׂה — following the Pinchas precedent). If he comes to the court to ask permission, or strikes after the fact, it is not permitted.
