⚖️ Grama: Indirect Causation of Death

Did perpetrator directly cause death?
Yes — direct action
Liable for execution
No — indirect (grama)
Examine further: was death inevitable from his action?
↓
Was there an intervening cause that could have prevented death?
Yes — e.g., lion may not attack
Exempt from execution (Rava)
No — death certain from his act
Liable (e.g., mosquitoes will certainly bite)

💨 Who Is Liable for Suffocation Deaths?

Rava
One who overturns a vat upon another causing suffocation — I hold he is exempt, since death came not from his direct act but from the consumed air over time.
Rabbi Zeira
One who takes another into a sealed marble house and kindles a lamp, consuming all breathable air — he is liable, since his action directly caused the depletion.
The Sages
In the marble house without a lamp, the room is large enough — suffocation does not begin at the moment of action. But with the vat, even without a lamp, suffocation begins immediately.
Key Distinction Does suffocation begin at the moment of the perpetrator's action, or only later? Immediate suffocation = liable; delayed / uncertain = debated or exempt.

↓

Exempt

Exempt

⚖️ Liable vs. Exempt — Indirect Causation

Liable — חַיָּב

  • 🦟 Bound before mosquitoes — death is certain
  • 💨 Overturned vat — suffocation begins immediately at time of act
  • 🧱 Stone/arrow caroms off wall — his force caused the death (kocho)
  • 💧 Diverted water onto bound victim — water = his arrows (Rav Pappa)
  • ⬆️ Threw stone upward, went sideways and killed — liable (Rav Pappa)

Exempt — פָּטוּר

  • 🦁 Bound before lion — lion may not attack
  • 🌞 Bound victim died of starvation — not direct action
  • 🪜 Another removed the ladder from the pit
  • 🛡️ Shield taken before arrow arrived — death was avoidable
  • 🌿 Herbs scattered or available in market — recovery was possible

🎯 Kocho — The Principle of Indirect Force

Kocho k'gufo (כֹּחוֹ כְּגוּפוֹ): One's indirect force is treated legally like one's own direct action in certain circumstances
↓
🧱 Stone caroms off wall and kills a third party — liable (Rava): the rebound was still generated by his throw
↓
⬆️ Stone thrown upward, travels sideways and kills — liable (Rav Pappa): the lateral motion originated from his force
↓
⚠️ Exception — ashes trickling into purification water: Ashes that slowly trickle from the side of a vessel are not his direct force — movement too slow and passive
↓
🪡 Needle found wet: Rav emends the Mishnah — not sprayed (mitza) but found (matza) wet; we cannot attribute uncertain wetness to his force
↓
Rule: Kocho applies when motion is vigorous and directly traceable to the act. Slow trickle, uncertain causation, or intervening independent factors breaks the chain.
