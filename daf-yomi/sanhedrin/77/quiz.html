QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, if one binds another and leaves him before a lion, what is the ruling and why?
A. Liable — confining someone near a predator is inherently lethal
B. Exempt — the lion may choose not to prey on the victim
C. Exempt only if the lion did not actually kill the person
D. Liable only if the perpetrator owned the lion
Correct: B — Rava exempts him because there is a possibility the lion will not attack — death was not inevitable from the perpetrator's action. Since an independent will (the lion's) could intervene, his act is not the direct cause.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What critical distinction makes leaving someone before mosquitoes different from leaving someone before a lion?
A. Mosquitoes are too small to qualify as a separate intervening cause
B. Lions are considered a form of indirect grama but mosquitoes are not
C. Mosquitoes will certainly and inevitably bite a bound person to death; a lion may choose not to attack
D. The victim should have protected himself from mosquitoes but not from a lion
Correct: C — Certainty of death is the deciding factor. Mosquitoes will inevitably bite a bound, defenseless person to death, making the outcome certain from the perpetrator's act. A lion retains its own will and may not attack, breaking the causal chain.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rav Ashi rule the archer exempt even when no one actually scattered the victim's healing herbs?
A. Because the archer did not know about the herbs
B. Because the victim was obligated to use the herbs himself
C. Since herbs capable of healing arrow wounds are available in the marketplace, it was never established that the shot would be lethal
D. Because herbs can only create exemption if they were in the victim's hand, not nearby
Correct: C — Rav Ashi reasons that since effective healing herbs exist in the marketplace, the arrow wound was never certainly fatal from the outset. The attacker is therefore exempt from execution even without anyone scattering the herbs.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the key distinction between a sealed marble house WITH a kindled lamp vs. WITHOUT one?
A. A lamp allows the perpetrator to witness the victim's suffering, increasing culpability
B. With a lamp, oxygen is consumed immediately at the time of action; without a lamp in a large room, there is initially sufficient air
C. Without a lamp the victim cannot find the exit and is therefore responsible for his own death
D. The lamp heats the room to a lethal temperature, changing the cause of death
Correct: B — A kindled lamp immediately consumes available oxygen, so suffocation begins at the moment of the perpetrator's act. Without a lamp, a large marble room has enough air that suffocation does not begin immediately — the act's lethality is delayed.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, what is the ruling when someone binds another and then diverts a flow of water onto him, causing death?
A. Exempt — water is a natural force independent of the perpetrator's action
B. Liable — the diverted water is tantamount to his own arrows; his force caused the death
C. Exempt only if the water was diverted before the victim was bound
D. Liable only if the victim was unable to free himself before the water arrived
Correct: B — Rav Pappa rules him liable because diverting water onto a bound person is legally equivalent to shooting arrows at him — the diverted water becomes the direct instrument of his lethal force (kocho), and applies only where the water would kill before the victim could escape.
