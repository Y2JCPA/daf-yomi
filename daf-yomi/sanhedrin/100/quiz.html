Sanhedrin 100 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, what makes someone an epikoros?
A. Denying the Oral Torah entirely
B. Speaking of Sages with contempt — saying 'those Sages' dismissively
C. Refusing to appoint a rabbi or teacher
D. Reading the books of Ben Sira
Correct: B — Rav Pappa defines an epikoros as one who uses contemptuous language about Sages — saying 'those Sages' with dismissive overtones. Rav Pappa himself once slipped and said it unintentionally and felt stricken, illustrating how seriously this is taken.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why was Gehazi punished, according to Rabbi Yoḥanan?
A. He stole silver and garments from Naaman
B. He called his teacher Elisha by name without saying 'Rabbi'
C. He refused to perform a healing miracle
D. He read heretical books in public
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan teaches: Gehazi was punished because he said 'My master sent me' (II Kings 5:22) — referring to Elisha by name without the title Rabbi. Calling one's teacher by name is the very definition of an epikoros according to Rav Naḥman.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How many worlds will each righteous person receive in the World-to-Come, according to Rava bar Mari?
A. 70 worlds, corresponding to the nations
B. 100 worlds, corresponding to the 100 blessings
C. 310 worlds, derived from gematria of יֵשׁ
D. An infinite number beyond calculation
Correct: C — Rava bar Mari derives from Proverbs 8:21 — 'To bequeath to those who love me yesh (יֵשׁ)' — that each righteous person receives 310 worlds, since the numerical value (gematria) of יֵשׁ equals 310.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rav Yosef permits teaching certain parts of Ben Sira publicly. Which teaching is explicitly cited as an example?
A. A sparse-bearded man is clever; a thick-bearded man is a fool
B. Prevent the multitudes from inside your house
C. A good wife is a gift given into the bosom of a God-fearing man
D. Do not introduce anxiety into your heart
Correct: C — Rav Yosef says we teach the superior matters of Ben Sira in public, and the Gemara cites Ben Sira 26:3 — 'A good wife is a good gift; she will be given into the bosom of a God-fearing man' — as an example of what is permitted and valuable.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rabbi Ḥanina interprets 'all the days of the poor are terrible' (Proverbs 15:15). What does 'the poor' refer to in his reading?
A. Masters of Talmud burdened by unanswered questions
B. One who starves himself while studying Torah
C. One who has a bad wife
D. One who is physically ill or temperamentally anxious
Correct: C — Rabbi Ḥanina reads 'the poor' as one with a bad wife — whose difficult temperament makes every single day terrible — while 'the good-hearted feast' refers to one blessed with a good wife, making every day a joy.
