🚫 Defining the Epikoros

Rav Pappa
An epikoros is one who speaks of Sages dismissively — saying 'those Sages' with contemptuous overtones, as if they deserve no respect. I myself once slipped and said it — I felt as though arrows had pierced me.
Rav Naḥman
An epikoros is one who calls his teacher by name without the title 'Rabbi.' As Rabbi Yoḥanan taught: Gehazi was punished precisely for this — he said 'My master sent me' (II Kings 5:22), calling Elisha by name alone.
Levi bar Shmuel
[A practical illustration:] When we reached the Scroll of Esther while mending mantles, we said it needs no mantle — it is not as significant. Rav Yehuda rebuked us: even this sounds like the speech of epikorsim!
Gehazi's Sin He said 'My master sent me' — calling Elisha by name, not Rabbi. Disrespect in speech, even once, can forfeit one's portion in Olam HaBa.

🌊 The Future River from the Holy of Holies

וְעָלֵהוּ לִתְרוּפָה (יחזקאל מז:יב)
Eschatological Vision — Ezekiel 47 Rabbi Yirmeya taught before Rabbi Zeira: The Holy One will cause a river to emerge from the Holy of Holies, and alongside it will grow all sorts of delicacies.

"Its leaf for medicine [לִתְרוּפָה]" — three opinions:
• One sage: abbreviation for riv u'feh (רִיב וּפֶה) — to unlock the mouths of mutes
• Bar Kappara: to unlock the mouths of barren women
• Rabbi Yoḥanan: literally medicine for the sick

The gates of Jerusalem will be 20 cubits high — but this refers only to windows; the actual gates will be far higher, as implied by komemiyyut (upright stature = 200 cubits × 2).

📖 Ben Sira: What May We Teach?

❌ Forbidden / Problematic

  • Reading Ben Sira as sacred scripture (loses Olam HaBa)
  • A sparse-bearded man is clever; thick-bearded is a fool
  • Do not introduce anxiety to your heart (already in Proverbs)
  • Prevent multitudes from your house (already taught by Rabbi)
  • Folk sayings about bread and thirst — crass and beneath Torah dignity

✅ Permitted — Even in Public

  • A good wife is a gift given to a God-fearing man (Ben Sira 26:3)
  • A bad wife is like leprosy — remedy: divorce her with a get
  • Avert your eyes from a graceful woman lest you be trapped
  • The poor suffer both day and night — nights of cold and worry
  • Many have been corrupted by the beauty of a beautiful woman

😔 Four Readings of 'All the Days of the Poor Are Terrible' (Proverbs 15:15)

Rabbi Zeira (citing Rav): 'The poor' = masters of Talmud who struggle painfully with unresolved questions. 'The good-hearted feast' = masters of Mishnah, for whom tradition is clear and settled.
↓
Rava / Rav Mesharshiyya: Opposite! Masters of Mishnah are like stone-quarriers — hard labor, sorrowful results. Masters of Talmud are like wood-choppers — their analytical work warms them with insight and satisfaction.
↓
Rabbi Ḥanina: 'The poor' = one who has a bad wife. 'The good-hearted feast' = one blessed with a good wife. Home life colors every single day.
↓
Rabbi Yannai: 'The poor' = one who is physically delicate or temperamentally anxious — their constitution makes every day feel terrible.
↓
Ben Sira adds: Not only are the poor's days terrible — their nights are too. The lowest roof in the city: freezing in rain, scorching in heat, with worry filling the dark hours.
