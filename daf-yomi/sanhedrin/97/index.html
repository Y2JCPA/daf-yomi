Year 1: Rain withheld from one city but falls on another — "I will cause it to rain upon one city" (Amos 4:7)
↓
Year 2: Arrows of famine sent to selected places — partial famine begins across the land
↓
Year 3: Great famine — men, women, children, and pious ones die; Torah is forgotten
↓
Year 4: Partial plenty — neither full abundance nor famine; some relief
↓

↓

⚖️ Is Repentance Required for Redemption?

Rabbi Eliezer
If Israel repents they are redeemed — "Return, wayward children, I will heal your iniquities" (Jeremiah 3:22). Repentance is the condition.
Rabbi Yehoshua
But it says: "You were sold for nothing, and you shall be redeemed without money" (Isaiah 52:3) — redemption comes regardless of repentance!
Rabbi Eliezer
"Return to Me and I will return to you" (Malachi 3:7) — God's return is conditional on our return first.
Rabbi Yehoshua
"I will take you one from a city and two from a family" (Jeremiah 3:14) — God redeems even a remnant who did not repent.
Rav
The matter depends only upon repentance and good deeds. When Israel repents, they will be redeemed — no other condition.
Shmuel
It is sufficient for the mourner to endure in his mourning — redemption will come in its appointed time even without prior repentance.
Core Tension Rav and Rabbi Eliezer make redemption conditional on repentance; Shmuel and Rabbi Yehoshua say God's promise is unconditional — He will redeem Israel even without full national repentance, in His own time.

✨ The Thirty-Six Hidden Righteous & Warning Against End-Calculations

"אַשְׁרֵי כָּל חוֹכֵי לוֹ" — ישעיהו ל:יח | "תִּפַּח עַצְמוֹתָן שֶׁל מְחַשְּׁבֵי קִצִּין"
לו צדיקים — Thirty-Six Righteous in Every Generation Abaye: The world has no fewer than 36 righteous people in each generation who greet the Divine Presence, as it states: "Happy are all they who wait for lo" (Isaiah 30:18) — לו has the gematria of 36.

Challenge (Ḥizkiyya citing Rabbi Shimon bar Yoḥai): I have seen bnei aliyah — the spiritually elite — and they are few, even as few as one or two!

Resolution: Abaye speaks of those who must request permission from the angels to view the Shekhinah; Rabbi Shimon speaks of those who may enter without asking — the truly luminous ones.

Warning (Rabbi Zeira citing Rabbi Yonatan): "May those who calculate the end of days be cursed [tippaḥ]" — once a predicted date passes without redemption, people despair and abandon hope, thereby actively delaying the Messiah's coming.
