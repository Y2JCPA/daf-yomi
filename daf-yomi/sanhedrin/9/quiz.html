Sanhedrin 9 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
If witnesses warned a woman she would receive lashes but did NOT warn her about the death penalty, what punishment can the court impose?
A. Execution, since adultery inherently carries the death penalty
B. Lashes only, since the warning defined and capped her liability
C. She is fully exempt because the warning was incomplete
D. The court chooses based on severity of the act
Correct: B — Hatra'ah (warning) defines the scope of liability. Witnesses who warned only about lashes limit the court to imposing lashes — not death — even though adultery ordinarily carries capital punishment.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Meir, what happens if one of three witnesses in a capital case is revealed to be a close relative?
A. Only the relative's testimony is excluded; the other two proceed normally
B. The two valid witnesses may testify independently and the case continues
C. The entire testimony is nullified, even though two valid witnesses remain
D. The court reduces the charge from capital to monetary
Correct: C — Rabbi Meir holds that a disqualified witness contaminates the entire group. Even with two perfectly valid witnesses remaining, the whole testimony is invalid.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rabbi Yosei limits the rule nullifying testimony when a disqualified witness is present. To which type of case does this rule apply according to him?
A. All monetary cases (dinei mamonot) only
B. Capital cases (dinei nefashot) only
C. All cases involving more than two witnesses
D. Only cases involving marital disputes
Correct: B — Rabbi Yosei clarifies that the group-nullification rule applies specifically to capital cases. In monetary disputes, the remaining valid witnesses can still testify.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What logical problem does the 'two brothers' scenario expose about an overly broad nullification rule?
A. Brothers who witness a crime must recuse themselves from the Sanhedrin entirely
B. If relatives invalidated all co-witnesses, public crimes could never be prosecuted — an absurd outcome
C. Two brothers may combine as a single valid witness in capital cases
D. Brothers must issue a formal warning before their testimony is accepted
Correct: B — If any group containing relatives was wholly nullified, two brothers witnessing a murder alongside a stranger would make the stranger's testimony invalid too — meaning publicly-witnessed crimes could never be prosecuted. Rabbi Yosei rules this out.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Yosef, may a man who testifies 'I was sodomized with my own consent' combine with another witness to testify against the perpetrator?
A. Yes — as a victim he always retains standing to testify
B. Yes — only if the second witness is a scholar
C. No — he has self-incriminated as wicked and his testimony is barred by Torah law
D. No — only a disinterested third party may testify in such cases
Correct: C — A person who admits consensual participation in a prohibited act brands himself as wicked. The verse 'Do not put your hand with a wicked person to be an unrighteous witness' bars such self-incriminating testimony entirely.
