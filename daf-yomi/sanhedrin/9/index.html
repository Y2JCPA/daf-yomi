⚖️ Rabbi Meir vs. The Rabbis: What Nullifies Testimony?

Rabbi Meir
If one witness in a group is disqualified — as a close relative or otherwise invalid — the entire testimony is nullified, even if two perfectly valid witnesses remain.
The Rabbis
If two valid witnesses remain after removing the disqualified one, their testimony stands independently. The invalid witness does not contaminate the others.
Rabbi Yosei
This nullification rule applies only in capital cases (dinei nefashot), not in monetary disputes — and cannot lead to logically impossible outcomes.
The Practical Stakes This dispute determines whether a woman warned only about lashes (not death) escapes execution when part of the testimony against her is disqualified.

⚠️ The Warning (Hatra'ah) and Its Consequences

Witnesses observe a woman committing adultery
↓
They warn her: "You will receive lashes" — but do not warn her of the death penalty
↓
She commits the act despite the warning
↓
She is brought to trial; testimony is examined by the court
↓
Result: She can receive lashes only — the warning defined and capped her liability
↓
Ravina's alternative reading: Same result if one witness is later found disqualified and only two valid witnesses remain

🚫 Does a Disqualified Witness Nullify All Testimony?

A group of witnesses testifies in a capital case
↓
Is one witness found to be a close relative or otherwise disqualified?
Rabbi Meir — YES, nullified
Entire testimony is invalidated — even the two valid witnesses cannot proceed alone
Rabbis — NO, partial
If 2+ valid witnesses remain, their testimony stands independently and trial proceeds
↓
Rabbi Yosei's limit: Nullification applies only in capital cases — not monetary disputes

📜 Rabbi Akiva's Principle: Accessories and Witnesses

Rabbi Akiva
The Torah punishes an accessory to a transgressor identically to the primary transgressor. Kal v'chomer: God rewards one who helps another perform a mitzvah even more generously!
Derived Rule
Just as with two witnesses — if one is disqualified the testimony is nullified — so too with three witnesses: if one is disqualified, the entire group's testimony falls.
Rabbi Yosei
This nullification only applies in capital cases. In monetary cases, removing the invalid witness still leaves the valid pair able to testify.
Key Verse (Exodus 23:1) "Do not put your hand with a wicked person to be an unrighteous witness" — the source for rejecting self-incriminating testimony

👥 The Brothers Paradox

ואם כן שני אחים שראו באחד שהרג את הנפש — מה יעשו?
Logical Impossibility If the rule were that any group containing a relative is wholly nullified, what happens when two brothers and one unrelated person all witness a murder?

The brothers cannot testify against each other (relatives). But if their presence also invalidates the third witness, no one can ever testify about a publicly-witnessed crime — a logical absurdity that would destroy justice entirely.

Therefore: Rabbi Yosei limits nullification to cases where removing the disqualified witness leaves fewer than two valid witnesses — not when two valid witnesses remain independently.
