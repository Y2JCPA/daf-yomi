⚖️ The Core Contradiction

Mishna (Sanhedrin):
Court retries monetary cases for either acquittal or liability
↓
Capital Cases
Court retries only to acquit — never to convict
Mishna (Bekhorot)
Judge who errs need not retry the case at all
↓
❓ Contradiction: When does a court retry a case?
↓
Three Answers: Rav Yosef · Rav Naḥman · Rav Sheshet
3 / 9

⚡ Three Sages Resolve the Contradiction

Rav Yosef
No contradiction. Sanhedrin speaks of an expert judge (מומחה) — his errors are retried. Bekhorot speaks of a non-expert who pays from his own pocket — no retry required.
Rav Naḥman
No contradiction. Sanhedrin speaks where a court greater in wisdom and number exists to overturn the ruling. Bekhorot speaks where no such superior court is available.
Rav Sheshet
No contradiction. Sanhedrin speaks of error in a matter explicit in the Mishna — ruling revoked. Bekhorot speaks of error in deliberation (disputed law) — ruling stands.
Shared Principle All three agree: the nature of the judicial error and the structure of the court system determine whether a verdict must be revisited.

🐄 The Cow of Menahem: A Case Study in Error Types

The Situation: A cow of Menahem's household had its womb removed. Rabbi Tarfon ruled it a tereifa (fatally injured, forbidden). The owner fed it to dogs.
↓
New Evidence: The case came before the Sages at Yavne. Todos the doctor testified: No cow or pig leaves Alexandria of Egypt without its womb removed — yet they survive. Therefore removal alone does not render it a tereifa.
↓
Yavne Rules: The animal is permitted — not a tereifa. Rabbi Tarfon erred in a matter that appears in the Mishna.
↓
Rabbi Akiva's Double Argument: (1) You erred in a Mishna matter — the ruling should be revoked. (2) Even if you did not, you are a mumḥeh approved by the community and are therefore exempt from personal liability.
↓
Rav Naḥman's Challenge: The cow was already eaten by dogs — retrial is impossible! Why does the error category matter at all?
↓
Rava Answers: The distinction matters for future rulings — and critically, to determine whether Rabbi Tarfon owes monetary indemnification from his personal funds.

🔄 Who May Argue in Capital Cases?

May Argue to Acquit ✅

  • Any judge on the court
  • A student sitting before the court
  • Even one who previously argued to convict
  • Source: "the righteous, do not slay" (Exodus 23:7)
  • Acquittal can always be raised — it saves life

May NOT Argue to Convict ❌

  • A witness — restricted to testimony alone
  • A student before the court
  • One who previously argued to acquit
  • Source: Numbers 35:30 — witness limited to testimony
  • Source: "the wicked, do not make innocent" — extra caution required

Exception: The Enticer 🚫

  • One who entices others to idol worship (מסית)
  • Rules are reversed — court actively seeks conviction
  • "Neither spare nor conceal him" (Deuteronomy 13:9)
  • Even a witness may argue to find him liable
  • Rav Shimi bar Ashi: this is the explicit Torah exception
