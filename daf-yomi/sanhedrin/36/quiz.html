Sanhedrin 36 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
In capital law cases, who speaks first when judges deliberate?
A. The Nasi (president of the court), as the most authoritative
B. The most senior and greatest judge present
C. The judges on the side benches — the least significant judges
D. The two court scribes, followed by the judges in order
Correct: C — The mishna teaches that in capital cases, opinions begin from the side benches — the junior judges — specifically so they are not swayed by the positions of senior judges on a life-or-death matter.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Adda bar Ahava, after Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, who next combined unparalleled Torah greatness with unparalleled secular greatness?
A. Abaye, who was both a leading scholar and a wealthy landowner
B. Rava, who led his academy and was close to the Exilarch
C. Rav Ashi, in whose generation this combination reappeared
D. Rav Kahana, one of Rav's greatest students
Correct: C — Rav Adda bar Ahava states: from Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi until Rav Ashi, no one combined unparalleled Torah scholarship with unparalleled secular (wealth and political) greatness — Rav Ashi is the next such figure.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which three types of individuals cannot sit on the Sanhedrin?
A. Kohanim, Levites, and converts to Judaism
B. Very old men, castrated men, and men without children
C. Scholars, merchants, and craftsmen
D. Debtors, foreigners, and the physically ill
Correct: B — A baraita (Tosefta 7:5) teaches that the Sanhedrin may not include a very old person, one who is castrated, or one without children — each lacks the life experience and empathy needed for compassionate capital judgment.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When the Gemara qualifies Rav's ruling that a teacher may judge capital cases with his student, it limits this to students like Rav Kahana and Rav Asi. Why are they exceptional?
A. They were already ordained as full judges in their own right
B. They needed Rav's halakhic traditions but not his reasoning — they were his near-peers
C. They had studied under Rav for more than ten years
D. They were explicitly appointed by Rav to serve on his court
Correct: B — The Gemara limits Rav's ruling to students like Rav Kahana and Rav Asi, who had mastered Rav's analytical reasoning but still needed to learn his specific halakhic traditions — making them near-peers rather than true novices.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What shape does the mishna prescribe for the seating arrangement of a Sanhedrin?
A. A straight row, all judges facing the witnesses
B. Two parallel rows facing each other across a central aisle
C. A full circle surrounding the defendant and witnesses
D. Half of a circular threshing floor — a semicircle
Correct: D — The mishna states the Sanhedrin is arranged 'like half of a circular threshing floor' (כמחצית הגורן העגולה) — a semicircle — so that all judges can see one another and also observe the witnesses directly.
