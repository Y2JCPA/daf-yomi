🗣️ How Judges Examine Witnesses (Mishna)

Bring witnesses into a private room in the courthouse
↓
Intimidate them to speak only truth — cite verses about the fate of false witnesses
↓
Remove all others except the judges, so no one can coach the witnesses
↓
Examine each witness separately: 'How do you know the defendant owes money?'
↓
If statements are congruent, judges deliberate; 2-to-1 majority decides the verdict
↓
Announce verdict publicly — a judge may not reveal how he personally voted

⚖️ Mar Ukva and His Brothers-in-Law

Brothers-in-law
Why do you disqualify yourself? If you follow Rabbi Yehuda (relatives persist through children), we can bring a letter from Eretz Yisrael proving that opinion is wrong — so you have no reason to recuse yourself!
Mar Ukva
Am I stuck to you with kira (wax)? I agree we are no longer relatives. But I disqualify myself because you will not accept my verdict — and I refuse to issue a futile ruling.
Key Insight A judge may recuse himself not only for formal disqualification, but also when his authority will not be respected — preserving the dignity of the court.

👰 Disqualification: Enemy and Lover

Who may not serve as witness or judge?
↓
One Who HATES
Did not speak with litigant for 3 days out of enmity
Source: 'He was not his enemy' — Numbers 35:23
One Who LOVES
Not written in the verse — derived by kal va-chomer: same bias applies in reverse
Example: the groomsman during 7 days of feasting
↓
Valid testimony: 'Not his enemy and not one who loves him' — a fully neutral party

🗣️ Which Verse to Cite When Intimidating Witnesses?

Rav Yehuda
Cite: 'As clouds and wind without rain, so is he who boasts of a false gift' (Proverbs 25:14) — false witnesses gain nothing from their lies.
Rava
Cite instead: 'As a hammer, a sword, and a sharp arrow — so is a man who bears false witness' (Proverbs 25:18). False witnesses die prematurely — a sharper deterrent!
Rav Ashi
Both can be dismissed by folk logic. Better to warn them: even those who hire false witnesses despise them — as Jezebel herself mocked Naboth's perjurers after using them.
Folk Saying (countering Rav Yehuda and Rava) 'Seven years there was a famine, but over the craftsman's door it did not pass' — and 'Seven years there was a pestilence, but a man who has not reached his years did not die.' Witnesses can always rationalize away threatened punishment.

📜 When Is an Oral Admission of Debt Legally Binding?

✅ Binding Admission

  • Debtor explicitly says: 'You are my witnesses'
  • Admission made before a properly convened court
  • Made seriously, not in jest
  • Witnesses performed a kinyan (formal act of acquisition)

❌ Disputed / Insufficient

  • Admission without designating witnesses
  • Debtor later claims: 'I was only teasing'
  • Admission overheard by hidden witnesses — debtor unaware
  • Statement made publicly to appear less wealthy (Rav Naḥman)

⚖️ Borderline Cases

  • Hidden witnesses in canopy or grave — Rava: still binding
  • Deathbed statement: Rabbi Yishmael b. Rabbi Yosei — binding
  • 'A kav of debts' man: only named creditors are valid
  • Rabbi Ḥiyya: principle of 'not wanting to appear sated' applies even after death
