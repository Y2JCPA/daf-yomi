⚖️ Why Is the Sorcerer Missing from Karetot?

R. Yochanan
Necromancer and sorcerer share ONE prohibition in the Torah — so one unwitting act yields only one sin-offering. The sorcerer is simply subsumed under necromancy.
Reish Lakish
Sorcery involves only SPEECH, not a physical action. Since no sin-offering is brought for a prohibition lacking an action, the sorcerer is excluded from Karetot's list.
R. Yochanan
But capital punishment and sin-offerings are independent categories! A shared prohibition in karet does not mean a shared sin-offering.
Reish Lakish
Even R. Akiva, who permits minor actions, still requires SOME action. Sorcery is pure speech — no motion at all.
Resolution (Ulla / Rava) The necromancer in Karetot refers to one who burns incense to demons in order to raise the dead — a full physical action — so the Rabbis' list is consistent.

🧟 Necromancer vs. Sorcerer — Definitions

אוֹב — Necromancer

  • A 'pitom' — causes the voice of the dead to speak
  • Voice appears to emerge from armpit or between joints
  • Method: strikes arms together to produce sound
  • OR: inquires of a skull (גּוּלְגּוֹלֶת)
  • Skull answers in its normal voice — even on Shabbat
  • Stoning is the penalty

יִדְּעוֹנִי — Sorcerer

  • Places a bone of the yadua animal in his mouth
  • The bone speaks on its own
  • Transgression is through speech alone
  • No physical act required
  • This is why Reish Lakish excludes him from Karetot
  • Also punishable by stoning

🗣️ Is Speech an 'Action'? The Liability Chain

Question: Does a speech-only prohibition trigger sin-offering liability?
↓
R. Akiva
ANY action suffices — even minor movement. Bowing counts. Striking arms counts.
The Rabbis
Requires a SIGNIFICANT action. Bending height alone is not enough.
↓
Blasphemer: only speaks — yet R. Akiva obligates sin-offering
↓
Rava's answer: Blasphemy is also in the heart — intention is constitutive, making it unique. Pure speech prohibitions without intent-dependency remain excluded.

🤖 Rava's Golem

אִם יִרְצוּ צַדִּיקִים בּוֹרְאִין עוֹלָם — רָבָא בְּרָא גַּבְרָא
The Righteous Can Create Worlds Rava said: If the righteous wished, they could create a world — for it says 'your iniquities separate you from God' (Isaiah 59:2), implying nothing separates a sinless person from divine power. Indeed, Rava created a man (golem) and sent it to Rabbi Zeira. Rabbi Zeira spoke to it — it did not reply. Rabbi Zeira said: 'You were made by the chavrayya (group of sages) — return to your dust.' Rav Ḥanina and Rav Oshaya studied Sefer Yetzira every Shabbat eve and created a third-born calf (עֵגְלָא תִּילְתָּא) which they ate.

🌊 The Sabbatyon River — Shabbat Debate with Turnus Rufus

Turnus Rufus
What makes THIS day Shabbat? Who decreed it? And furthermore — how do you know which day of the week it actually is? Perhaps another day is the real Shabbat.
R. Akiva
The Sabbatyon River proves it: the river churns and flows all week but rests only on Shabbat. A medium who raises the dead also cannot do so on Shabbat — the dead do not rise on Shabbat.
Turnus Rufus
You contradict yourself — you cite the medium to support Shabbat, yet sorcery is forbidden!
R. Akiva
I cite your own words against you — according to those who practice it (not that it is permitted). The phenomenon itself testifies that Shabbat has cosmic reality.
Context The Rabbis hold that the dead do not rise on Shabbat, unlike on weekdays. This itself is used as proof of Shabbat's sanctity.
