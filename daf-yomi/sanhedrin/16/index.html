📜 What Does 'Every Great Matter' Mean?

First Baraita

  • "Great matter" = difficult matter
  • Hard legal questions escalate to Great Sanhedrin
  • Based on Exodus 18:22 (Yitro's advice to Moshe)
  • Two verses needed: one for general law, one for the king

Rav Adda bar Ahava

  • "Great matter" = matter of a great person
  • High Priest on trial requires Great Sanhedrin
  • Aligns with the second baraita's interpretation
  • The verse's meaning shifts from difficulty → dignity of defendant

🗡️ Source for False Prophet Requiring 71 Judges

Rabbi Yosei b. Ḥanina
Verbal analogy (גזירה שוה): 'presumptuousness' by the false prophet links to 'presumptuousness' by the rebellious elder — just as the elder requires 71 judges, so does the false prophet.
Gemara Objects
But when 'presumptuousness' appears by the rebellious elder, it is in the context of his death penalty — which requires only 23 judges! The analogy would prove too little.
Reish Lakish
Reframe the analogy: the false prophet derives his requirement for 71 from the rebellious elder's standing before the Great Sanhedrin — not from the death-penalty clause.
Gemara Challenges
Why not let the analogy run in reverse — deriving the rebellious elder's rule from the false prophet — and conclude the elder also needs 71? That would be circular!
Resolution The verbal analogy runs in one direction only: the false prophet's requirement for 71 judges is derived from the rebellious elder, but the rebellious elder's law is independently stated and cannot be 'returned' to derive from the false prophet.

🏙️ Extending Jerusalem and Temple Courts

Can the city of Jerusalem or Temple courtyards be expanded?
↓
Yes — but requires Great Sanhedrin (71 judges)
Source (Rav Shimi bar Ḥiyya): "According to all that I command you" — as Moses sanctified the Tabernacle under Divine directive with all Israel present
↓
Rava Objects
Future Temple vessels are sanctified by service, not anointment — so why use Moses's anointment as a model?
Rav Pappa Resolves
"And they shall take" (אֹתָם) is emphatic: sanctification is by anointment alone, not by service — for all generations
