📜 'Shall Condemn' — Singular or Plural?

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
The verse states yarshi'un — vocalized as a plural verb. This proves that 'the court shall condemn' refers to multiple judges, requiring three judges even for capital cases, parallel to monetary cases.
Rabbis
The word is written in a form that could be read as singular! You cannot derive a ruling from the vocalization alone. The written consonantal text is what counts, and it permits a single judge reading.
Rabbi Yitzḥak bar Yosei citing Rabbi Yoḥanan
This dispute is a specific instance of a general principle: five Tannaim — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, Rabbi Yehuda ben Roetz, Beit Shammai, Rabbi Shimon, and Rabbi Akiva — all hold that the kerei (vocalization) is halakhically authoritative.
Key Terms כְּתִיב (ketiv) = what is written in the scroll | קְרִי (kerei) = how it is traditionally vocalized/read — when they differ, a fundamental dispute arises

🩸 How Many Altar Horns for Sin-Offering Blood?

Beit Shammai

  • Blood must be applied to all four horns of the external altar
  • Proof: 'horns' written plene (with vav) once → plural = at least four
  • Failure to apply to all four horns invalidates the offering
  • Position: holds kerei / plene spelling is authoritative
  • Derives from Leviticus 4:25, 4:30, 4:34 — full application required

Beit Hillel

  • A single presentation of blood is sufficient for atonement
  • Proof: 'horns' written deficient (without vav) twice → singular suffices
  • Supported by a fortiori: bird sin-offering (lesser) needs only one application
  • Clinched by verse repeating 'the priest shall make atonement' three times
  • Position: one application atones; four applications are the ideal (l'khatchila)

👶 Rabbi Yehuda ben Roetz: Two Proofs on Birth Impurity

Students' Question: Leviticus 12:5 says a woman giving birth to a female is impure 'two weeks, as in her menstrual impurity' — does this extend her total impurity to 80 days?
↓
First Proof (by comparison): For a male birth, the Torah grants extra days as days of purity. By analogy, the extra days for a female birth are also purity days — proving the 80-day structure is not simply doubled impurity.
↓
Change of heart: After his students left, Rabbi Yehuda ben Roetz followed them outside to give a better, more direct proof.
↓
Second Proof (kerei): Read the verse as shvu'ayim — 'two weeks' — the vocalization itself directly indicates two distinct and separate weeks of impurity, not a combined extension.
↓
Principle demonstrated: The kerei (vocalized text) provides a cleaner, authoritative ruling — the comparison-based inference was valid but unnecessary once the vocalization is examined directly.

📦 Rabbi Akiva: Tefillin Compartments from Totafot

טַ״ט בְּכַתְפִי — שְׁתַּיִם, פַּ״ת בְּאַפְרִיקִי — שְׁתַּיִם (סַנְהֶדְרִין ד׳)
Linguistic Derivation The Torah writes totafot (טוֹטָפֹת) — the head-phylactery straps — three times. An earlier baraita derives four compartments from the three occurrences. But Rabbi Akiva says: there is no need for that proof! The word totafot itself encodes the number four: tat (טַ״ט) in the Katfei language means two, and pat (פַ״ת) in the Afriki language means two — thus totafot = 2 + 2 = four compartments, each housing a different Torah portion.
