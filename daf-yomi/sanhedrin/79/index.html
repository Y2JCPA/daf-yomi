⚔️ Two Great Disputes: Intent & Intermingling

Rabbis
If one intended to kill this person and killed that person, he is liable — the intent to kill was present, and someone died.
Rabbi Shimon
He is exempt. The verse says 'lies in wait for him and rises up against him' — the specific victim intended must be the one killed.
Rabbis
When condemned criminals with different death penalties are intermingled, all receive the most lenient penalty.
Rabbi Shimon
Burning is more severe than stoning — proof: a priest's daughter who commits adultery is burned, while a betrothed Israelite's daughter is only stoned.
Core Tension Does criminal intent transfer when the wrong victim is struck? And when condemned criminals are mixed together, how do we assign penalties we can no longer track?

🎯 Intent, Force, and the Wrong Body Part

Assailant strikes with lethal intent toward a specific location
↓
Aimed at loins (lethal) → hit chest → died
Force sufficient for intended spot. Victim died from misdirected blow → LIABLE
Aimed at chest (lethal) → hit loins → died
Force not sufficient for loins. Victim died anyway → EXEMPT
↓
Rule: Liability requires that the blow was lethal for the actual location hit — not just the intended one

⚖️ Intended Adult, Killed Minor — The Core Dispute

Mishna
Intended to kill an adult; blow insufficient for adult. Hit a minor; blow sufficient for minor — minor died. Assailant is exempt.
Rabbis
If one intended to kill this person and killed that person — he is liable. The intent to kill was present; a person died.
Rabbi Shimon
He is exempt. Verse: 'He lies in wait for him and rises up against him' — requires that the intended victim and the killed victim are the same person.
Gemara
Rabbi Shimon disagrees with the first clause: intended to kill an animal → killed a person; intended a gentile → killed a Jew; intended non-viable → killed viable. All exempt per R. Shimon.
School of Chizkiya Diverges from both Rabbis and Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi: one who intended to kill person A and killed person B is exempt from execution AND from monetary payment — derived from the laws of killing animals.

🔀 Intermingled Death Penalties — Which Is More Severe?

Burning vs. Stoning

  • When intermingled → all receive most lenient
  • Rabbis: stoning is more lenient → all stoned
  • Rabbi Shimon: burning is more severe than stoning
  • Proof: priest's daughter (adultery) → burning
  • Betrothed Israelite's daughter (adultery) → stoning
  • More severe crime gets more severe penalty → burning > stoning

Beheading vs. Strangulation

  • Parallel dispute to the above
  • Rabbi Shimon: beheading is more lenient
  • When intermingled → all beheaded
  • Rabbis: strangulation is more lenient
  • When intermingled → all strangled
  • Mirrors the burning/stoning disagreement on severity ranking

🏛️ Who Are the 'Others'? — Three Amoraic Answers

The Question: A murderer was intermingled with 'others' — if those others are innocent, it's obvious they're exempt! Who are these others that requires a ruling?
↓
Mnemonic: Beit-Shin-Kuf-Reish — marks the sequence: Rabbi Abbahu citing Shmuel, Reish Lakish, and Rava
↓
Rabbi Abbahu citing Shmuel: The murderer's verdict was not yet finalized. He was jailed pending conclusion of trial and became intermingled with other pre-verdict prisoners — none can be executed.
↓
Reish Lakish: Pre-verdict cases are obviously exempt. Rather: a post-verdict condemned murderer was intermingled with other condemned criminals (for different crimes) — identities are now indistinguishable.
↓
Rava: Offers a further analysis — the sugya continues exploring whether these positions can be reconciled and what the halakhic implications are for each scenario.
