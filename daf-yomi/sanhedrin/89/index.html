🔮 Six Categories of Prophetic Sin

Executed by Human Court

  • Prophesies what he did not hear (e.g., Zedekiah son of Chenaanah)
  • Prophesies what was not said to him (e.g., Hananiah son of Azzur)
  • Prophesies in the name of an idol (e.g., prophets of the Baal)

Executed by Heaven

  • Suppresses his prophecy (e.g., Jonah son of Amittai)
  • Contemptuously forgoes the statement of a prophet
  • A prophet who violates his own prophetic statement (e.g., Iddo)

🏛️ The Rebellious Elder's Path to Execution

Local city court — lacks jurisdiction; cannot execute the rebellious elder
↓
Court at Yavne — seat of the Sanhedrin after the Temple's destruction — still cannot execute him
↓
Taken up to Jerusalem — brought before the High Court of 71 judges on the Temple Mount
↓
Detained until the next festival — Passover, Shavuot, or Sukkot — so word spreads to the entire nation
↓
Executed publicly on the festival — fulfilling "And all the nation shall hear and fear" (Deut. 17:13)

📢 Four Transgressors Requiring Public Proclamation

The Four Who Must Be Proclaimed

  • One who instigates others to idol worship (mesit)
  • The stubborn and rebellious son (ben sorer u'moreh)
  • The rebellious elder (zaken mamre)
  • The false prophet (navi sheker)

The Debate: Hear vs. See

  • Rabbi Akiva: execution timed to the festival so multitudes witness it directly
  • Rabbi Yehuda: verse says 'hear and fear' — the entire nation need not see; hearing the report suffices
  • Practical outcome: execution is still delayed to the festival to maximize public awareness
  • Common ground: the proclamation deters future prophetic sin

⚔️ Was Hananiah's Sin 'Not Said to Him' or Something Else?

Rav Pappa
The mishna's category 'one who prophesies what was not said to him' is imprecise for Hananiah ben Azzur. Not only was this prophecy not said to Hananiah — it was not stated to any prophet at all about Israel!
Abaye
Since Jeremiah was standing in the upper market saying words of doom for foreign nations, Hananiah could have heard it. He took what was said to Jeremiah about others and falsely reapplied it as Israel's redemption — that IS the category of 'not said to him.'
Background Jeremiah prophesied disaster for Elam and Babylon; Hananiah flipped this into a false promise that within two years the Temple vessels and exiles would return (Jeremiah 28)

🪢 The Akeidah: Satan's Escalating Challenges to Abraham

God commands Abraham: "Take, please, your son, your only, whom you love, Isaac" — the word na (please) is an expression of entreaty (R. Shimon bar Abba)
↓
Challenge 1 — The Old Man
Satan appears as an elder: "Where are you going?"
Abraham: "To pray."
Satan: "To slaughter your son!"
Abraham: "So I was commanded."
Challenge 2 — Job's Words
Satan quotes Job 4:2–5: "You have instructed many… but now trouble comes upon you and you are weary."
Abraham responds: "I will walk in my integrity" (Job 2:9)
↓
Satan transforms into a deep river blocking the road — Abraham wades in until water reaches his neck, then cries out to God
↓
God rebukes the waters; Abraham and Isaac cross safely and ascend to Mount Moriah — Abraham's faith vindicated at every stage
