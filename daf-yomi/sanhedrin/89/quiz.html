Sanhedrin 89 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Where must the rebellious elder (zaken mamre) be executed, and when?
A. In the court of his own city, immediately upon conviction
B. In the court at Yavne, during the regular session of the Sanhedrin
C. In the High Court in Jerusalem, on a festival so all the nation hears
D. In any Sanhedrin of 23 judges in the nearest major city
Correct: C — The mishna (89a) requires taking him up to the High Court (71 judges) in Jerusalem and executing him on a festival — fulfilling 'And all the nation shall hear and fear' (Deut. 17:13).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Which prophet is cited as the paradigm of 'one who suppresses his prophecy'?
A. Zedekiah son of Chenaanah
B. Hananiah son of Azzur
C. Jonah son of Amittai
D. Iddo the prophet
Correct: C — Jonah fled to Tarshish to avoid prophesying to Nineveh, making him the classic example of suppressing a prophecy. His punishment was at the hand of Heaven (89b).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yitzḥak's principle, how can one distinguish a true prophecy from a false one?
A. A true prophecy must be accompanied by a public miracle as verification
B. The same prophetic content is conveyed to multiple prophets, though their individual styles differ
C. A true prophet must receive written confirmation from the Jerusalem Sanhedrin
D. Only prophecies that were fulfilled within one year are considered authentic
Correct: B — Rabbi Yitzḥak teaches (89a) that a true prophecy on a given subject is revealed to multiple prophets in essentially the same content — as seen with Obadiah and Jeremiah both prophesying about Edom's pride, in different styles.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did God use the word 'please' (na) when commanding Abraham to take Isaac to the Akeidah?
A. Because Abraham was elderly and God wished to show him special respect
B. To signal that the command was a suggestion, not a true obligation
C. As an expression of entreaty — God beseeched Abraham to endure this trial so his earlier trials would not be doubted
D. Because Isaac had not yet given his consent to be brought as an offering
Correct: C — Rabbi Shimon bar Abba explains (89b) that 'na' signals entreaty — God asked Abraham so that those who witnessed it would not say his earlier trials had not been genuine tests of faith.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the dispute between the Rabbis and Rabbi Shimon regarding a prophet who incites others to idol worship?
A. Rabbis: burning by fire; Rabbi Shimon: stoning
B. Rabbis: strangulation; Rabbi Shimon: burning
C. Rabbis: stoning; Rabbi Shimon: strangulation
D. Rabbis: strangulation; Rabbi Shimon: stoning
Correct: C — The Rabbis derive stoning via a gezerah shavah (verbal analogy) linking the prophet-inciter to the lay mesit (instigator); Rabbi Shimon holds that any unspecified 'death' in the Torah means strangulation (89b).
