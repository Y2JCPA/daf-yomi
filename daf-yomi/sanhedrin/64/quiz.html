Sanhedrin 64 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why was the captured evil inclination for idol worship placed in a lead container?
A. Lead is fireproof and can contain a fiery lion cub
B. Lead absorbs sound, silencing its cries
C. Lead was the symbol of truth sent from heaven
D. Lead prevents the inclination from burrowing underground
Correct: B — Zechariah instructed: cast it into lead, for lead absorbs sound. When caught, one of its hairs fell out and it cried out for 400 parasangs — lead was needed to contain its voice.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What happened when the Sages imprisoned the evil inclination for sexual matters?
A. All idol worship immediately ceased throughout the land
B. The Temple was miraculously restored
C. A fresh one-day-old egg could not be found anywhere
D. All the chickens fled to gentile territories
Correct: C — With the sexual inclination imprisoned, reproduction stopped entirely. Even chickens stopped laying eggs — they could not find a fresh egg even for the sick. The Sages concluded they could only diminish, not eliminate it.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the mishna and Gemara, what is required for liability for Molekh worship?
A. Passing the child through fire alone is sufficient
B. Handing the child to Molekh priests alone is sufficient
C. Both handing over to priests AND passing through fire are required
D. Intent to worship Molekh is the sole determining factor
Correct: C — The mishna and its Gemara establish that both actions are independently required: handing the child to the priests of Molekh (from 'you shall not give') AND passing the child through fire (from gezera shava on 'to pass').
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Rava say is the 'typical manner of passing' through fire to Molekh?
A. Walking slowly between two bonfires on foot
B. Leaping over a bonfire, like children do on Purim
C. Being carried aloft over flames by the Molekh priests
D. Circling the fire three times before crossing through
Correct: B — Rava says the typical manner resembles children leaping over bonfires in a pit on Purim. The baraita explicitly supports him: 'If he passed him by foot, he is exempt' — leaping is required.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yosei son of Rabbi Ḥanina, for what three cases are the three mentions of karet regarding idol worship needed?
A. Worshipping idols of gold, silver, and wood respectively
B. Typical idol worship, atypical idol worship, and the Molekh ritual
C. Worshipping in the Temple, outside, and in a foreign land
D. For priests, Levites, and Israelites who worship idols
Correct: B — The three mentions cover: (1) worshipping an idol in its typical manner, (2) worshipping an idol not in its typical manner (e.g., bowing to Mercury), and (3) the Molekh ritual specifically — which might otherwise not be classified as standard idol worship.
