Sanhedrin 110 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the baraita (as opposed to Rabbi Yoḥanan), what was Korah's fate?
A. He died in a plague, separate from both punishments
B. He was only swallowed by the earth
C. He was both swallowed by the earth AND consumed by fire
D. He escaped and died in exile
Correct: C — The baraita states Korah experienced both fates: he was among the swallowed (Numbers 26:10: 'the earth swallowed them with Korah') and among the burned (same verse: 'when the fire devoured'). Rabbi Yoḥanan disagreed, arguing he died in a plague.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How did On ben Peleth's wife save him from being destroyed with Korah's assembly?
A. She appealed directly to Moses to spare her husband
B. She hid him in a pit until the punishment passed
C. She exposed her hair at the tent entrance, causing men to turn back out of modesty until it was too late
D. She convinced On to publicly denounce Korah before the assembly gathered
Correct: C — On ben Peleth's wife reasoned that regardless of who won (Moses or Korah), On's status wouldn't change. She got him drunk so he wouldn't leave, then sat uncovered at the tent entrance — men who came to fetch On turned back due to her immodest appearance, and by then the assembly had been swallowed.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rav say about one who perpetuates a dispute?
A. He violates a rabbinic enactment
B. He violates a Torah prohibition
C. He merely acts against the spirit of peace but commits no formal violation
D. He is obligated to bring a guilt-offering
Correct: B — Rav derives from 'And let him not be like Korah and his company' (Numbers 17:5) that perpetuating a dispute is a Torah prohibition. Rav Ashi adds a further consequence: such a person is fit to be afflicted with leprosy.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Of Joseph's three treasures buried in Egypt, where did the one revealed to Korah end up?
A. Swallowed into the earth along with Korah
B. Redistributed to the poor of Israel
C. Taken by Pharaoh after Korah's death
D. Recovered by Moses and used for the Tabernacle
Correct: A — Korah's treasury was swallowed into the earth together with him — fulfilling Ecclesiastes 5:12: 'wealth kept for the owner to his detriment.' The treasure was so vast that 300 white mules were needed merely to carry the keys to the storehouses.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Ravina, from when does a minor child of the wicked merit the World-to-Come?
A. From when the child first says Amen
B. From the moment the child begins to speak
C. From the moment of conception
D. From the moment of birth
Correct: C — Ravina derives from 'Their seed shall serve Him' (Psalms 22:31) that even before birth — from conception — the child can merit the World-to-Come. Rabbi Meir says from when the child says Amen; other amoraim say from birth or from when they begin to speak.
