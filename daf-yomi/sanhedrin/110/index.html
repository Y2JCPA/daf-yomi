🔥 What Was Korah's Exact Fate?

Rabbi Yoḥanan
Korah was neither among the swallowed nor among the burned — he died in a plague. 'The men who belonged to Korah' were swallowed; his sons were burned. Korah himself died separately in the plague.
Baraita
Korah was BOTH among the swallowed (Numbers 26:10: 'the earth swallowed them with Korah') AND among the burned (same verse: 'when the fire devoured 250 men'). He experienced both punishments!
Cosmic Sign The sun and moon ascended to zevul (the 4th firmament) in protest, refusing to shine during Korah's punishment, until God threatened them with arrows — as Habakkuk writes: 'at the light of Your arrows as they go' (Habakkuk 3:11).

🏜️ Did the Generation of the Wilderness Earn the World-to-Come?

No Share — Default Ruling

  • 'In this wilderness they shall be consumed and there they shall die' (Numbers 14:35)
  • Died in the desert without entering the Land
  • Sages' initial position in the baraita

Rabbi Eliezer — They Come

  • 'Gather My pious together to Me, those who entered My covenant by offering' (Psalms 50:5)
  • They entered an eternal covenant with God at Sinai
  • That covenant guarantees their future reward

Rabbi Shimon ben Menasya

  • 'The redeemed of the Lord shall return and come singing into Zion' (Isaiah 35:10)
  • Those whom God redeemed will yet come to Zion — this is the generation He redeemed from Egypt
  • They are destined to return

Rabbi Akiva — They Come

  • 'Go, tell them to return to their tents' (Deuteronomy 5:27) — God directed them to return to normal life
  • Implies future reward and continuity
  • Characteristic piety: saving doubtful cases

⚠️ The Prohibition of Perpetuating a Dispute

Core Prohibition (Rav): Anyone who perpetuates a dispute violates a Torah prohibition — derived from 'And let him not be like Korah and his company' (Numbers 17:5)
↓
Rav Ashi: One who perpetuates a dispute is fit to be afflicted with leprosy — gezeira shava: 'by the hand of Moses' here parallels Moses's leprous hand at the burning bush
↓
Rabbi Yosei: Disputing the reign of the House of David — fitting for a snake to bite him, paralleling Adonijah's challenge to Solomon (I Kings 1:9)
↓
Rav Ḥisda: Disagreeing with one's teacher = disagreeing with the Divine Presence — Dathan and Abiram's dispute with Moses is called striving 'against the Lord' (Numbers 26:9)
↓
Rabbi Ḥanina bar Pappa: Expressing resentment against one's teacher = resentment against God — 'Your murmurings are not against us, but against the Lord' (Exodus 16:8)

👶 Minor Children of the Wicked — World-to-Come?

Rabbi Ḥiyya / R' Shimon bar Rabbi
One says: from the moment he is born. The other says: from when he begins to speak. Both agree a child has a share in the World-to-Come from these early stages.
Ravina
Even from the moment of conception! As it is written: 'Their seed shall serve Him' (Psalms 22:31) — before birth, the child can already merit the World-to-Come.
Rabbi Meir (baraita)
From when the child first says Amen — 'Open the gates so that the righteous nation that keeps emunim (faithfulness) may enter' — do not read emunim but Amen-im.
Universal Agreement All agree: minor children of the wicked of the NATIONS OF THE WORLD do not come to the World-to-Come — they have no virtue at all. Rabban Gamliel derives this from 'You have smitten all my enemies on the cheek' (Psalms 3:8).
