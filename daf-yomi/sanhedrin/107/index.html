⚠️ David Seeks — and Fails — His Ordeal

David
Master of the Universe! Why do they say 'God of Abraham, God of Isaac, God of Jacob' — but not God of David? Test me as You tested them!
God
They were not informed of the nature of their ordeal. I will test you — and I will tell you: it involves sexual temptation.
David
I accept the test. I will even strengthen myself — I will engage with my wives by day rather than night to quench desire.
Rav (via Rav Yehuda)
A person should NEVER bring himself to undergo an ordeal — for David brought himself to one and failed, despite knowing in advance!
⚠️ Core Rule לעולם אל יביא אדם עצמו לידי נסיון — Even forewarned is not forearmed when it comes to temptation.

✋ Right Hand Draws Near, Left Hand Pushes Away

The Correct Approach

  • Left (weaker) hand gently pushes the sinner away
  • Right (stronger) hand draws them near simultaneously
  • Sinner never fully despairs of return
  • Applies to: evil inclination, children, women
  • The door of teshuvah remains always open

Elisha's Error with Gehazi

  • Elisha pushed Gehazi away with BOTH hands
  • Gehazi despaired and sinned further
  • Lost his share in the World-to-Come permanently
  • Yehoshua ben Peraḥya made the same error with his student
  • Overly harsh rejection causes irreversible spiritual ruin

📜 Yehoshua ben Peraḥya and His Student

King Yannai kills the Sages — Yehoshua ben Peraḥya flees with his student to Alexandria of Egypt
↓
Shimon ben Shetaḥ sends word: "From me, Jerusalem the holy city, to you Alexandria of Egypt: My husband dwells within you and I sit desolate"
↓
At an inn on the return journey, Yehoshua praises the inn; his student comments negatively on the innkeeper's physical appearance
↓
Yehoshua rebukes him sharply and excommunicates him — he intended it temporarily, but his student did not know this
↓
Student returns repeatedly seeking readmission; one day Yehoshua was reciting Shema and waved his hand meaning 'wait' — student misread it as final rejection
↓
Lesson applied: Always let left push away and right draw near — Yehoshua's double-handed rejection caused his student to go permanently astray
