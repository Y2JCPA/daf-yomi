⚖️ Defending God Against Skeptics

Emperor / Heretic
Challenges: Is God a thief? Are there two gods? Does God rejoice at destruction? Is He a priest or a jester?
Rabban Gamliel / Rabbi Abbahu
Defends God's unity, wisdom, and mercy — using analogy, wordplay, and Scripture — proving that apparent contradictions reflect deeper truth
Core Theme When confronted with theological paradoxes, the Sages respond with sharp wit and precise reasoning, affirming God's unity, compassion, and infinite wisdom

🏛️ The Emperor Calls God a Thief — Slide 1 of 5

Emperor
Your God is a thief! He put Adam to sleep and secretly stole his rib (Genesis 2:21) — like a surgeon who anesthetizes a patient before operating!
Rabban Gamliel's Daughter
Last night thieves entered our house — they took a silver vessel but left a golden one in its place!
Emperor
Would that such a thief came to me every night!
Daughter
Exactly! God took one rib from Adam and gave him a wife in return. Was this not a gift?
Emperor (follow-up)
If so, why did God do it secretly, during sleep, like a thief hides his actions?
Daughter
She placed raw meat before him overnight — covered and hidden, it became edible. Some benefits require concealment to reach perfection.
🧠 Note Rabban Gamliel's daughter answered before her father could respond — her analogy reframes 'theft' as divine generosity

😢 Does God Rejoice at the Downfall of the Wicked? — Slide 2 of 5

Contradiction: 'The Lord is good to all' (Psalms 145:9) — universal compassion
vs.
'The Lord is good to those who wait for Him' (Lamentations 3:25) — selective kindness
↓
Key Question: Did God rejoice when Egypt drowned at the Red Sea?
↓
Angels wanted to sing
God said: 'My creations are drowning and you wish to recite songs?!' — God Himself does not rejoice
But Israel did sing
Rabbi Yosei bar Ḥanina: God does not rejoice — but He causes others to rejoice. The verse is precise: 'it shall rejoice' (not 'I will rejoice')

🕊️ Obadiah vs. Abraham — Who Feared God More? — Slide 3 of 5

Abraham

  • Verse: 'For now I know that you fear God' (Genesis 22:12)
  • Word 'greatly' is NOT used
  • Lived apart from openly wicked rulers
  • Merited founding of the Jewish people

Obadiah

  • Verse: 'Obadiah feared the Lord greatly' (I Kings 18:3)
  • Word 'greatly' IS used — a higher level of fear
  • Lived in the household of BOTH Ahab AND Jezebel — two wicked people — yet remained righteous
  • Hid 100 prophets in a cave; merited prophecy concerning Edom

🔥 Heretic Challenges to Rabbi Abbahu — Slide 5 of 5

Challenge 1 — 'God is a Jester': God told Ezekiel to lie on his left side (Ezekiel 4:4), then on his right side (Ezekiel 4:6) — as if toying with him
↓
Response: This was not mockery — each side symbolized a different period of national punishment: the exile of Israel and the exile of Judah respectively
↓
Challenge 2 — 'God is a Priest': God commanded teruma (Exodus 25:2), which belongs to priests. When God buried Moses, in what mikveh did He purify Himself?
↓
Response: God immersed in fire — 'For, behold, the Lord will come in fire' (Isaiah 66:15)
↓
Counter: But is immersion in fire halachically valid?
↓
Final Response: Fire is in fact the primary form of purification — 'Everything that can withstand fire, you shall pass through fire' (Numbers 31:23)

📋 Sanhedrin 39 — Summary Map

Theological Challenges
Emperors and heretics attack God's unity, justice, and ritual consistency
↓
Unity Defended
One God made mountains and wind; one Creator made eyes and ears in the same face
Mercy Defended
God does not rejoice at the wicked's downfall — angels forbidden to sing at the Red Sea
Righteousness Defended
Obadiah, living among the most wicked, reached a higher level of fear of God than Abraham
↓
Conclusion: Israel's uniqueness lies not in isolation from humanity, but in its covenant with the One God who created all — and whose compassion extends even to His enemies
