Sanhedrin 8 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Rav declare himself disqualified to judge his host's case?
A. His host was a Torah scholar whom he respected
B. He might be partial due to hospitality and benefit received from his host
C. He was related to the host by marriage
D. The case involved more money than his court could adjudicate
Correct: B — Rav explicitly stated: 'I am disqualified to judge for you because I may be partial due to what you have done for me.' The benefit of hospitality creates a conflict of interest that disqualifies the judge.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Reish Lakish, what is the practical teaching of 'hear the small and great alike'?
A. Children and adults must receive equal treatment in court
B. A case involving one peruta must be treated with as much care as a case of 100 maneh
C. All judges on a panel must speak equal amounts
D. Small courts and large courts have equal halakhic authority
Correct: B — Reish Lakish interprets 'small and great' as referring to the monetary value at stake — even a claim worth one peruta deserves the same judicial diligence as a claim worth 100 maneh.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ḥanina (or Rabbi Yoshiya), what did Moses do wrong, and what was his punishment?
A. He judged while angry; his ruling was overturned
B. He failed to appoint enough judges; he had to judge alone
C. He said 'bring it to me' presumptuously; the women's inheritance portion was not taught through him
D. He refused to hear the daughters of Zelophehad; they had to petition God directly
Correct: C — The verse 'bring the hard case to me and I will hear it' displayed presumptuousness. As punishment, the Torah portion about women's inheritance — which was fitting to be written through Moses — was instead occasioned by the daughters of Zelophehad bringing it themselves.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does 'zimmun' mean in the baraita that requires three people?
A. A quorum of three for reciting Grace After Meals
B. A formal summons issued by the court requiring a litigant to appear
C. A committee of three elders who certify rabbinic rulings
D. The public announcement of a new legal precedent
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes zimmun for Grace After Meals from zimmun in this baraita's context, concluding that here it refers to a formal court summons (via the agent of the court) issued by a panel of three judges.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rava, what is the actual dispute between Rabbi Meir and the Rabbis regarding the defamer case?
A. Whether the husband's false witnesses are flogged
B. Whether we must be concerned for the honor of the first (three-judge) court
C. Whether rumors of adultery must be formally investigated
D. Whether the woman must testify before the court
Correct: B — Rava holds that no one is concerned about rumors escalating the case. The dispute is whether the honor of the original three-judge court is at stake — i.e., if the case later turns capital, does that require 23 judges from the very beginning to avoid undermining the first court's ruling?
