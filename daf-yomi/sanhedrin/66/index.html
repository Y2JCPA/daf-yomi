🔮 The Divination Prohibition

ויקרא יט:כו — לֹא תְנַחֲשׁוּ וְלֹא תְעוֹנֵנוּ
לֹא תְנַחֲשׁוּ וְלֹא תְעוֹנֵנוּ Leviticus 19:26: 'Nor shall you practice divination nor soothsaying' — this prohibits taking omens from weasels, birds, or fish.

Also included: telling a charity collector 'Don't collect from me first — it is a bad sign for me' or 'Don't collect from me now because it is morning.'

These verbal behaviors constitute a form of divination by treating coincidental circumstances as meaningful omens.

🗣️ Cursing a Parent: God's Name or Appellations?

Rabbi Meir
One who curses a parent using an appellation (כינוי) of God's name — e.g., 'the Merciful,' 'the Gracious' — is liable to execution by stoning.
Rabbis (R. Menaḥem b. R. Yosei)
Liability attaches only when one curses using the explicit four-letter Name (the Tetragrammaton). Appellations do not trigger capital liability.
Baraita Proof
Lev. 24:16: 'He who blasphemes the name [shem] of the Lord shall be put to death.' The word shem specifies the explicit Name — excluding appellations.
Gemara — Scope
One is liable whether he curses his father alone, his mother alone, or both together. Derived from ish ish (double language: 'a man a man') in Lev. 20:9.
Mode of Execution Stoning — derived by analogy to blasphemy: 'all the congregation shall stone him' (Lev. 24:16). Established via a binyan av (paradigm) from the blasphemer.

🔥 Betrothed Woman vs. Priest's Daughter: Differing Executions

Betrothed Young Woman (Adultery)

  • Execution: Stoning (סקילה)
  • Applies to: any Jewish betrothed na'arah
  • 'Then they shall both die' — both man and woman stoned
  • R. Yoshiya: both must be equally subject to punishment
  • R. Yonatan: punishment applies even if only one party is liable
  • If 10 men engaged in anal intercourse: all are executed by stoning

Daughter of a Priest (Adultery)

  • Execution: Burning (שריפה)
  • R. Yehuda HaNasi / R. Yishmael: she must first be betrothed (teḥel = first profanation)
  • R. Meir: applies even if she married someone who disqualified her (e.g., a mamzer)
  • The man who lies with her: stoning, not burning
  • 'She profanes her father' — her disgrace reflects on the priestly family
