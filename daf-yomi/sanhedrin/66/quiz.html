Sanhedrin 66 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Rabbis (identified as Rabbi Menaḥem b. Rabbi Yosei), when is one liable for execution for cursing a parent?
A. When using any appellation of God, such as 'the Merciful'
B. When using the explicit four-letter Name of God (the Tetragrammaton)
C. When cursing both parents at the same time
D. When the parent is present and hears the curse
Correct: B — The Rabbis derive from Lev. 24:16 — 'he who blasphemes the name [shem] of the Lord' — that the explicit divine Name is required. Rabbi Meir disagrees, holding appellations also trigger liability.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What distinguishes a Shabbat violation that carries stoning from one that does not?
A. Stoning applies only if the work was done in front of witnesses in the marketplace
B. Stoning applies only on Yom Kippur, not regular Shabbat
C. Stoning requires that intentional performance carry karet and unwitting performance obligate a sin-offering
D. Stoning applies whenever any Torah-prohibited labor is performed
Correct: C — The mishna limits stoning to violations where intentional performance incurs karet and unwitting performance requires a sin-offering. Violating Shabbat boundaries (techum) incurs neither, so it is excluded.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What are the four conditions required for the law of the betrothed young woman to apply?
A. Adult, betrothed, Jewish, residing in the city
B. Na'arah (young woman ~12–12.5), virgin, betrothed, residing in her father's house
C. Minor, virgin, married, residing in the city
D. Na'arah, divorced, betrothed, residing in her mother's house
Correct: B — The mishna (66b) specifies all four: she must be a na'arah (neither minor nor full adult), a virgin, betrothed but not yet living with her husband, and still in her father's house.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the baraita ultimately derive the prohibition against cursing any member of the Jewish people, including one's parents?
A. Directly from 'Honor your father and mother' in the Ten Commandments
B. From a halakha l'Moshe mi'Sinai (oral tradition from Sinai)
C. Through a three-case paradigm combining judges, kings, and a deaf person — their common element 'of your people' extends to all Jews
D. From a verbal analogy (gezeira shava) between two unrelated verses
Correct: C — No single case suffices: judges have authority, kings have prominence, the deaf are wretched — each is unique. Their shared element — belonging to the Jewish people — creates the prohibition for all, including parents.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Huna's explanation of Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, what is required for the daughter of a priest to be executed by burning?
A. She must be fully married and living in her husband's house
B. She must have committed adultery publicly in the city square
C. She must first have been betrothed — in line with Rabbi Yishmael's view
D. She must be under twelve years of age
Correct: C — Rav Huna explains that Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi follows Rabbi Yishmael: the word teḥel ('if she profanes herself') indicates she must first acquire a disqualifying status through betrothal before the burning penalty applies.
