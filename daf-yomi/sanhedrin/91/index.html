🏺 Can Dust Come Alive? — Heretics vs. Rabbis

Heretic
You say the dead will live — but aren't they dust? Does dust come to life?
Rabbi Ami
Parable: A king ordered servants to build a palace where there was no water or earth — and they did! Now that water and earth are available, how much more so can he build again. So too with man, made from water and earth.
Heretic
Woe unto you wicked ones — those who are alive die. How can you say the dead will come to life?
Geviha ben Pesisa
Woe unto you! If those who previously had no life now live (the newborn), then surely those who once lived will live again — a fortiori!
Core Logic kal v'chomer: Creation ex nihilo (birth) is harder than resurrection — if God can do the former, surely He can do the latter

⚖️ Three Nations Before Alexander of Macedon

Canaanites

  • Claim: The land of Canaan belongs to us — proven from Torah
  • Geviha's counter: Torah itself cursed Canaan — 'Servant of servants shall he be to his brothers'
  • A servant cannot own property against his masters
  • Result: Canaanites fled — no response found in three days

Egyptians

  • Claim: Israel borrowed silver and gold at the Exodus — repay it
  • Geviha's counter: Pay wages first — 600,000 workers enslaved for 430 years
  • The debt owed to Israel dwarfs anything borrowed
  • Result: Egyptians fled — no response found in three days

Ishmaelites & Keturah

  • Claim: Canaan belongs to all Abraham's sons — Ishmael and sons of Keturah too
  • Geviha's counter: Torah says Abraham gave gifts to the sons of the concubines and sent them away — Isaac alone inherited
  • Sons sent away have no land claim
  • Result: Ishmaelites fled — no response found in three days

👁️ Antoninos Questions Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi

Q1: Can body and soul each escape judgment by blaming the other?
Body: 'The soul sinned — I'm inert clay.' Soul: 'The body sinned — I'm blameless spirit.'
↓
Answer — Parable of the Lame & Blind Guards:
A king placed a lame man and a blind man to guard his orchard. They conspired to steal figs. At judgment he placed the lame on the blind and judged them together as one. So too, God reunites body and soul on judgment day.
↓
Q2: Why does the sun rise in the east and set in the west?
Rabbi: If it were reversed you would ask the same. The sun sets westward to greet its Creator — 'the hosts of heaven worship You' (Nehemiah 9:6). Setting is an act of prostration.
↓
Q3: When does the soul enter the body?
Rabbi initially said: at formation (40 days). Antoninos argued: meat left three days without salt spoils — the soul must enter earlier. Rabbi accepted: from conception (פקידה).
↓
Q4: When does the evil inclination begin?
Rabbi: From birth — if the yetzer hara were active in the womb, the fetus would kick and try to leave. The inclination joins only at emergence.

☀️ Three Messianic Contradictions Resolved

אֲנִי אָמִית וַאֲחַיֶּה מָחַצְתִּי וַאֲנִי אֶרְפָּא (דברים לב:לט)
Contradictions in End-Times Prophecy Ulla's Contradiction: Isaiah 25:8 — 'death will be swallowed forever' vs. Isaiah 65:20 — 'a youth may die at 100.' Resolved: Isaiah 65 describes the messianic era; Isaiah 25 describes the World to Come (עולם הבא).

Rav Hisda's Contradiction: Isaiah 24:23 — sun and moon will be ashamed (dimmed) vs. Isaiah 30:26 — sun will shine sevenfold brighter. Resolved: The sevenfold brightness is for healing the sick; the sun appears dim only relative to the brilliant radiance of the Shechina.

Rava's Contradiction: Deut. 32:39 — 'I will kill and bring to life' (two different people?) vs. 'I wounded and I will heal' (same person). Resolved: 'Kill and bring to life' refers to the same individual — God kills and then resurrects the very same person.
