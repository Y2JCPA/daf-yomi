Sanhedrin 91 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What was Geviha ben Pesisa's decisive counter-argument against the Canaanites' land claim?
A. The Torah explicitly forbids Canaanites from owning land
B. Canaan was cursed to be a servant, and a servant cannot own property against his masters
C. The Canaanites voluntarily abandoned the land when Israel arrived
D. Abraham purchased the land from the Canaanites
Correct: B — Geviha argued directly from Torah: Canaan was cursed — 'servant of servants shall he be.' A slave has no property rights against his masters, so Canaan can make no claim on the land.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the parable of the lame and blind guards, what theological point does Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi illustrate to Antoninos?
A. The soul alone bears responsibility for sins committed in life
B. The body and soul will each be judged separately in the afterlife
C. God reunites body and soul to judge them together, since they sinned together
D. Physical infirmity exempts a person from divine punishment
Correct: C — Just as the king placed the lame guard on the blind guard and judged them as one unit, God will reunite the soul with the body on Judgment Day and judge them together as they sinned together.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Gemara's resolution, what is the difference between the 'messianic era' and the 'World to Come'?
A. There is no difference — they are the same period
B. The messianic era still has some death; the World to Come abolishes death entirely
C. The World to Come has death but the messianic era does not
D. The messianic era is spiritual while the World to Come is physical
Correct: B — Ulla's contradiction is resolved by distinguishing eras: Isaiah 65 (youth dying at 100) describes the messianic era, while Isaiah 25 ('death swallowed forever') describes the ultimate World to Come.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How did Rabbi Meir derive resurrection of the dead from Exodus 15:1?
A. The verse explicitly mentions God reviving the dead
B. The future tense 'yashir' (will sing) implies Moses will sing again after resurrection
C. The Song of the Sea was sung prophetically about the future
D. The verse uses a word that also means 'return to life'
Correct: B — Rabbi Meir noted the verse says 'az yashir Moshe' — 'then Moses will sing' in future tense. It does not say 'sang' (past), implying Moses will sing this song again after resurrection.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When does Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi conclude the soul enters the body, and what convinced him?
A. At 40 days of gestation, when the embryo's features form
B. At birth, when the infant takes its first breath
C. From the moment of conception — Antoninos persuaded him
D. At the moment of viability, approximately six months gestation
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi initially thought the soul enters at formation (40 days), but Antoninos convinced him: meat spoils after three days without salt — the soul must preserve the body from conception onward.
