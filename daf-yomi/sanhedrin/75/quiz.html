Sanhedrin 75 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did the Sages rule that an unmarried woman could not be brought before the lovesick man, according to Rav Pappa?
A. She was actually married, making contact absolutely forbidden
B. Because of the family flaw — one may not disgrace her entire family to save him
C. Because she herself refused to cooperate with the doctors
D. Because pikuach nefesh never overrides any sexual prohibition
Correct: B — Rav Pappa says it is forbidden due to the family flaw (פגם המשפחה) — bringing disgrace upon an entire family to save one lovesick man is not permitted.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rabbi Yitzḥak's statement about the Temple's destruction add to this discussion?
A. That sexual prohibitions are stricter in the post-Temple era
B. That even marrying the woman would not cure the lovesick man, so no leniency applies
C. That the woman's family suffers greater disgrace after the Temple's destruction
D. That the Sages were more lenient about forbidden desire after the Temple fell
Correct: B — Rabbi Yitzḥak says that since the Temple's destruction, sexual pleasure was taken from the permitted and given to sinners — so even marriage would not satisfy the lovesick man's mind, eliminating any justification for leniency.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava, whose tannaitic opinion does the Mishna reflect when it lists the mother-in-law among those executed by burning?
A. Rabbi Yishmael alone
B. Both Rabbi Yishmael and Rabbi Akiva
C. Rabbi Akiva alone
D. An anonymous tanna opposed by both named sages
Correct: C — Rava holds that the dispute between R. Yishmael and R. Akiva concerns whether the mother-in-law prohibition persists after the wife's death. Since the Mishna includes the mother-in-law in the burning list, it reflects only R. Akiva's permanent prohibition.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What Hebrew word forms the basis of the gezeira shava (verbal analogy) equating 'his relatives' with 'her relatives' for the burning penalty?
A. זִמָּה (zimmah — lewdness)
B. עֶרְוָה (ervah — nakedness)
C. הֵנָּה (henna — they/them)
D. חֻקָּה (chukah — statute)
Correct: C — Rava says in the name of Rav Yitzḥak bar Avudimi: the equation of his relatives to her relatives is derived via a verbal analogy on the word הֵנָּה (henna), which appears in both the verse about his son's/daughter's descendants and the verse about a woman and her daughter.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why is it impossible to apply the burning penalty to one who has relations with the mother of his own mother?
A. Relations with one's grandmother are not forbidden at all by Torah law
B. An explicit verse limits the prohibition to 'she is your mother,' and it is absurd that a more distant relative would receive a more severe penalty than the mother herself
C. The gezeira shava applies only to in-law relatives, never to blood relatives, by rabbinic decree
D. The penalty for the mother's mother was left intentionally unresolved in the Gemara
Correct: B — Abaye cites 'she is your mother, do not uncover her' as limiting the specific liability. Additionally, since his own mother is punished by the less severe stoning, it would be illogical for the more distant mother's mother to receive the more severe burning — a principle confirmed by multiple refutations.
