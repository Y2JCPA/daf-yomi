Sanhedrin 43 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Huna's dilemma, what is left unresolved regarding the cloth and horse used during an execution?
A. Whether the Beit Din is required to provide them at all
B. Whether they come from the condemned man's property or the community's property
C. Whether the horse must outrun the agents on foot to be valid
D. Whether the cloth must be a specific color to be recognized
Correct: B — Rav Huna raises a teiku (unresolved dilemma) about whether the cloth used to signal a halt and the horse that races after the agents are funded by the condemned's estate or by the community.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say rabbis are NOT sent with the condemned the very first time he claims a new argument?
A. His first claim is presumed to be a delay tactic and is automatically dismissed
B. A man facing imminent execution is too frightened to articulate his argument clearly
C. The judges themselves accompany him on the first return only
D. The first return requires a full rehearing regardless of substance
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that fear of impending death prevents a man from speaking clearly, so the court grants the first return automatically without requiring advance substance — benefit of the doubt is given once.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Jesus the Nazarene receive an unusual 40-day public search for defenders before his execution?
A. Because the witnesses against him were suspected of bearing false testimony
B. Because he was considered a talmid chacham whose execution required extra scrutiny
C. Because he was close to the Roman government, compelling extraordinary procedure
D. Because his crime of incitement had never before been tried in Jerusalem
Correct: C — Ulla explains that Jesus was not truly deserving of such a search (being an inciter to idolatry), but his proximity to the Roman government made the court conduct a 40-day public appeal. No defenders emerged.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How did the court rebut Nakai's argument that 'the innocent [naki] you shall not slay' (Ex 23:7)?
A. 'There is no man who is fully innocent before God'
B. 'In secret places he slays the innocent [naki]' (Ps 10:8) — the same word convicts him
C. 'The innocent will be rewarded but the wicked will be punished'
D. 'You shall not take a bribe, for a bribe blinds the judges'
Correct: B — The court responded with Psalms 10:8, using the same Hebrew root naki to show that the wicked man himself 'slays the innocent in secret' — turning his word-play argument against him.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What theological benefit does the Mishna (43b) say a condemned person gains by reciting Vidui (confession) before execution?
A. His sentence is commuted to exile rather than death
B. His death serves as atonement and he retains his share in the World to Come
C. The court is obligated to delay one additional day to search for acquittal
D. His family is exempt from any financial penalties associated with his crime
Correct: B — The Mishna teaches that whoever confesses has a share in the World to Come — death becomes an act of atonement. Achan's confession to Joshua is the proof-text: despite his sin and execution, he secured Olam HaBa through sincere confession.
