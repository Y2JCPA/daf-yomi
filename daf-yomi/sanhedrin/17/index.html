🔮 What Did Eldad and Medad Prophesy?

First Opinion
Moses will die and Joshua will lead the Jewish people into Eretz Yisrael — hence Joshua's fierce jealousy
Abba Ḥanin (R. Eliezer)
They prophesied about the quail: 'Rise and eat meat' (Numbers 11:18) — hence the people's excitement
Rav Naḥman
They prophesied about Gog and Magog — the end-of-days war, foretold in Ezekiel 38
What all agree on All other prophets prophesied and stopped — Eldad and Medad never stopped. Numbers 11:27 uses present tense: מִתְנַבְּאִים — they are still prophesying.

⚖️ Unanimous Conviction Acquits

סַנְהֶדְרִין שֶׁכּוּלָּן מְחַיְּבִין — יִזָּכֶה
Rav Kahana's Rule In a capital case, if all judges vote to convict — the defendant is acquitted.

Why? The tradition requires overnight deliberation (לינה). If every judge immediately seeks conviction, no one has searched for merit on behalf of the accused. A court without a single voice of doubt has failed its duty.

🏙️ Minimum City Population for a Sanhedrin

First Tanna: 120 Men

  • 23 judges on the court
  • 3 rows × 23 students = 69
  • 2 scribes, 2 bailiffs
  • 2 litigants + 2 witnesses per side
  • 2 witnesses to render first pair zomemim
  • 2 more to render those zomemim
  • = ~120 total participants

Rabbi Neḥemya: 230 Men

  • Based on Moses appointing ministers of tens
  • 23 judges × 10 constituents each = 230
  • Reflects Jethro's model (Exodus 18)
  • Each judge must oversee 10 people
  • Higher standard — more community depth
