Sanhedrin 17 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Moses prepare 72 slips for the lottery when only 70 Elders were needed?
A. He wanted extras in case some slips were damaged
B. Six were selected from each of 12 tribes, totaling 72 candidates
C. The Torah requires two witnesses for any appointment
D. Two tribes had no eligible candidates so extras filled in
Correct: B — Moses selected 6 from each of 12 tribes = 72. Since only 70 were needed, 2 slips were left blank. Whoever drew blank was told: 'Heaven did not will it for you — accept it.'
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the first opinion in the Gemara, what did Eldad and Medad prophesy?
A. The war of Gog and Magog at the end of days
B. That the quail would rise up to be eaten by Israel
C. That Moses would die and Joshua would lead Israel into Eretz Yisrael
D. That the Tabernacle would be completed by Bezalel
Correct: C — The first opinion: Eldad and Medad prophesied 'Moses will die and Joshua will lead Israel into Eretz Yisrael.' This explains why Joshua urged Moses to 'imprison them' — he was jealous on Moses's behalf.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the halakhic consequence when all judges unanimously vote to convict in a capital case?
A. The defendant is executed immediately without delay
B. The case is automatically appealed to the Great Sanhedrin
C. The defendant is acquitted
D. The judges must deliberate for an additional three days
Correct: C — Rav Kahana: unanimous conviction in capital law = acquittal. The tradition of overnight deliberation (לינה) requires judges to search for merit. If no one found any merit, the process failed — so the defendant goes free.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why must Sanhedrin members be 'masters of sorcery' according to Rabbi Yoḥanan?
A. To protect the court chambers from witchcraft during sessions
B. So they are qualified to adjudicate capital cases involving sorcery
C. Because sorcery was practiced by most criminals of the era
D. To perform miraculous signs that validate their rulings
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan requires Sanhedrin members to understand the nature of sorcery so they can competently judge cases involving it. They must know what they are adjudicating — ignorance of sorcery would make fair judgment impossible.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the first Tanna, what is the minimum city population needed to establish a lesser Sanhedrin, and what drives that number?
A. 70 men — corresponding to the 70 Elders appointed by Moses
B. 100 men — corresponding to the ministers of hundreds
C. 120 men — enough to staff the court and all its required personnel
D. 230 men — corresponding to 23 judges overseeing 10 people each
Correct: C — The first Tanna requires 120 men: 23 judges + 3 rows of 23 students + 2 scribes + 2 bailiffs + litigants + witnesses + witnesses to render them zomemim, etc. Rabbi Neḥemya requires 230 (23 × 10), based on ministers of tens.
