🤔 What Does 'Such as I' Mean?

Rav Naḥman
One such as I may judge monetary cases alone — without a panel of three.
Rabbi Ḥiyya
One such as I may also judge monetary cases alone.
Gemara
Dilemma: Did they mean expert in deriving new rulings? Or: publicly accepted as an authority?
Resolution
From Mar Zutra's case: he erred and Rav Yosef exempted him from paying — proving 'such as I' means publicly accepted as an authority.
Key Rule A judge publicly accepted as an expert is exempt from restitution if he errs — even judging alone.

📜 The Scepter of Judah

לֹא-יָסוּר שֵׁבֶט מִיהוּדָה וּמְחֹקֵק מִבֵּין רַגְלָיו
בראשית מט:י "The scepter shall not depart from Judah, nor the ruler's staff from between his feet, until Shiloh comes."

The Gemara interprets: The Exilarch in Babylonia descends from the tribe of Judah — fulfilling the scepter. The Nasi in Eretz Yisrael fulfills the ruler's staff. Both hold legitimate judicial authority, each in their own domain, until the Messianic era.

🐑 Why Couldn't Rav Permit Firstborns?

Answer 1
Rav lacked sufficient practical expertise in identifying blemishes.
Objection
But Rav said: I apprenticed with a shepherd for 18 months to learn permanent vs. temporary blemishes — he clearly had deep expertise!
Answer 2
Precisely because he was so expert, he permitted blemishes laypeople don't recognize — causing them to permit non-blemished animals by erroneous analogy.
Lesson
A scholar who is too expert may mislead others unintentionally — sometimes restriction protects the community.
Principle Greater expertise can require greater caution in teaching, not less. The scholar's leniency becomes the layperson's error.
