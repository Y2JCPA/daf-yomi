🪨 Two Great Disputes of Daf 45

Man vs. Woman at Stoning

  • Man: covered with ONE cloth in front only
  • Woman: covered with TWO cloths — front and back
  • Verse 'stone him' derives man is exposed, not woman
  • Rabbis: concerned arousal from seeing naked woman
  • Resolution via 'Love your neighbor' — dignity in death

Hanging Corpses After Stoning

  • Rabbi Eliezer: ALL who are stoned are hung afterward
  • Rabbis: ONLY blasphemer and idol worshipper are hung
  • Man hung facing the people; woman faces the tree (R. Eliezer)
  • Rabbis: women are not hung at all
  • Dispute reflects two methods of Torah interpretation

🧍 Stoning Naked? Rabbis vs. Rabbi Yehuda — Resolving the Contradiction

Rabbis
A man is stoned naked; a woman is not. The verse says 'stone him' (Lev. 24:14) — the man is exposed. We are concerned that the sight of a naked woman will arouse improper thoughts among the witnesses.
Rabbi Yehuda
But this contradicts my own position on the sota! I hold the priest does NOT expose the sota's chest — meaning I am concerned about arousal. If so, why would I allow a woman to be stoned naked? The Rabbis' contradiction is the harder one!
Rabba
For Rabbi Yehuda — the sota case is different. She might leave the court innocent. Young priests would then remember her exposed, violating her dignity for no purpose. But at an execution, that concern doesn't arise.
Rava
For the Rabbis — the sota's humiliation deters other women (Ezek. 23:48: 'I will cause lewdness to cease'). Her disgrace serves a purpose. But a condemned woman already receives death — adding humiliation would be two punishments. 'Love your neighbor' (Lev. 19:18) forbids this.
Key Principle Rava's resolution: humiliation in punishment is only permitted when it serves a distinct Torah purpose (deterrence for the sota). For the condemned, 'love your neighbor as yourself' demands that death alone suffice — without added degradation.

📐 The Stoning Procedure — Step by Step

Step 1 — The Platform: The condemned stands on a platform twice the height of an ordinary person. Combined with his own height, the total drop is three times a person's height. This ensures death is swift.
↓
Step 2 — The Push: The first witness who testified against him pushes him off the edge. The condemned falls on his chest (or back). Derived from: "He shall be stoned or shall be thrown down" (Exodus 19:13).
↓
Step 3 — First Stone: If he did not die from the fall, the second witness (together with the first) lifts the large prepared stone. The second witness alone casts it onto his chest so it falls with full force.
↓
Step 4 — All Israel: If he still does not die, the obligation passes to all of Israel. Source: "The hand of the witnesses shall be first upon him… and afterward the hands of all the people" (Deut. 17:7).
↓
Why such height? Rav Naḥman citing Rabba bar Avuh: "You shall love your neighbor as yourself" (Lev. 19:18) — even for a condemned man, choose a compassionate death. The height ensures the fall alone is lethal.

🤲 Shmuel's Ruling: Witnesses' Hands Severed After Sentencing

Transgressor is sentenced to stoning by the Sanhedrin
↓
Witnesses' hands INTACT
Execution proceeds — witnesses stone him first, then all Israel. Sentence is carried out.
Witnesses' hands SEVERED after sentencing
Shmuel: Transgressor is EXEMPT. The verse requires 'the hand of the witnesses shall be first upon him' (Deut. 17:7) — they personally must cast the first stone.
↓
Challenge: What if witnesses had no hands from the outset of their testimony?
↓
Gemara: Disqualified from the start — testimony about a capital offense requires witnesses capable of casting the first stone. Exception: A murderer is different — 'he that smote him shall be put to death' (Num. 35:21) includes all modes of execution.

ואהבת לרעך כמוך — ברור לו מיתה יפה (ויקרא י״ט:י״ח)
ואהבת לרעך כמוך — Love Your Neighbor As Yourself Rav Naḥman citing Rabba bar Avuh derives from Leviticus 19:18 that even a condemned criminal deserves a compassionate death (מיתה יפה — a 'beautiful death'). This principle shapes three rulings on this daf: • The stoning platform is built high (two body-heights) — the fall alone should be lethal, avoiding prolonged suffering • A condemned woman is not stoned naked — death is the punishment; humiliation must not be added • These derive from the same verse, showing the Torah's vision: even capital punishment must reflect the sanctity of human life The condemned is still your neighbor. Execute justly — but with dignity.
