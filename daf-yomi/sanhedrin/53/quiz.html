Sanhedrin 53 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, what is NOT in question regarding the four death penalties?
A. Which sins receive which type of execution
B. Whether all four types exist in halakha
C. Whether strangulation is the most lenient
D. Whether the Rabbis or Rabbi Yehuda is correct
Correct: B — Rava states the four types are 'learned as tradition' — their existence is settled. The dispute concerns only which penalty applies to which transgression.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What verbal analogy does Abaye use to derive stoning for transgressors not explicitly mentioned?
A. 'They shall be put to death' and 'he shall surely die'
B. 'Their blood shall be upon them' (דמיהם בם) appearing in both passages
C. 'He is a murderer' linking all capital cases
D. 'You shall not uncover her nakedness' repeated twice
Correct: B — Abaye derives stoning via the verbal analogy (gezera shava) of the phrase דמיהם בם — 'their blood shall be upon them' — linking the derived cases to necromancers where stoning is explicit.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why doesn't the case of a married woman who commits adultery pose a difficulty for Rabbi Zeira's derivation of stoning?
A. Because adultery is punishable by burning, not stoning
B. Because a betrothed woman is explicitly stoned, implying a married woman is NOT stoned
C. Because the verbal analogy doesn't apply to sexual sins
D. Because Rava rules adultery is punished by strangulation as the default
Correct: B — The Torah explicitly specifies stoning for a betrothed woman — from which we infer a married woman is NOT stoned. Therefore, 'put to death' in the adultery verse cannot serve as the stoning source.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rabbi Yehuda holds that one who has intercourse with his mother who is also his father's wife is liable for how many sin-offerings?
A. Three — mother, father's wife, and violation of family structure
B. Two — one for each distinct prohibition
C. One — the prohibitions merge into a single liability
D. None — the verse excludes liability entirely
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehuda holds only one sin-offering is brought. Rava explains: 'the nakedness of your father' itself refers to the father's wife, causing the two prohibitions to merge into one.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Rava's final explanation for Rabbi Yehuda's view that only one sin-offering is brought for intercourse with one's mother who is also one's father's wife?
A. The singular 'ervatah' (her nakedness) limits liability to one offering
B. The verse 'She is your mother' cancels the father's wife prohibition
C. 'Nakedness of your father' (Lev. 18:8) itself means father's wife, so the same prohibition appears in both verses — merging into one
D. Rabbi Yehuda uses a tradition that any double prohibition yields only one offering
Correct: C — Rava argues Rabbi Yehuda interprets 'the nakedness of your father' as referring to his father's wife (derived from Deut. 23:1). Since both verses address the same prohibition, engaging in intercourse with one's mother who is father's wife triggers only one prohibition — yielding one sin-offering.
