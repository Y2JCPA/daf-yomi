Sanhedrin 44 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What does Rabbi Abba bar Zavda derive from the verse 'Israel has sinned' (Joshua 7:11)?
A. The entire nation must fast whenever any individual transgresses
B. Even when Israel sins, they are still called Israel
C. Israel forfeits its covenant when any member worships idols
D. Collective punishment is appropriate for a hidden communal sin
Correct: B — Rabbi Abba bar Zavda teaches: 'Even though he sinned, he is still called Israel' — a foundational principle that sin does not sever one's identity as part of the Jewish people.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, why were Achan's sons and daughters brought to the Valley of Achor?
A. They were stoned alongside Achan as knowing accomplices
B. They carried the stolen goods and needed to be interrogated
C. They were brought as witnesses so the lesson would be seared into their hearts
D. They testified against their father before the court
Correct: C — Rav Huna explains that the family came as observers — to witness the terrible consequences of sin — so they would be morally chastened and never repeat Achan's deeds. Only Achan was executed.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Shmuel bar Unya say in the name of Rav about Torah study compared to the daily offerings?
A. The daily offering atones even for neglect of Torah study
B. Torah study is greater than the daily offerings
C. Both are equally obligatory and neither may ever be deferred
D. The daily offering is greater because it sustains the entire world
Correct: B — From the angel's words 'I have now come' — appearing specifically for the neglect of Torah study rather than the missed offering — Shmuel bar Unya derives that Torah study is a greater obligation than the daily sacrifices.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What are the three names of Angel Gabriel, according to Rabbi Yosei son of Rabbi Ḥanina?
A. Michael, Raphael, and Piskon
B. Paskonit, Gabriel, and Metatron
C. Piskon, Itmon, and Sigron
D. Sigron, Azriel, and Chamiel
Correct: C — Rabbi Yosei son of Rabbi Ḥanina teaches that Gabriel is known as Piskon (he who reproves/argues), Itmon (he who seals/protects), and Sigron (he who locks the gates). Each name reflects a different aspect of his advocacy role.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Gemara, what formula does the condemned man use if he was wrongfully convicted and believes he is innocent of the specific charge?
A. He is forbidden from confessing at all, lest he falsely implicate himself
B. He must say the standard formula: 'Let my death atone for all my sins'
C. He may say: 'Let my death be atonement for all my sins except this one'
D. He should remain silent, as only guilt can be atoned through death
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehuda rules that a man who knows he was wrongfully convicted may say: 'Let my death be an atonement for all my sins except this one' — his death still achieves atonement for other sins, while not constituting a false admission.
