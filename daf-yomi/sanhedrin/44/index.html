⚖️ Sin, Confession, and Collective Identity

אַף עַל פִּי שֶׁחָטָא יִשְׂרָאֵל — עַדַיִן יִשְׂרָאֵל הוּא
Core Teaching Daf 44 weaves together the story of Achan's catastrophic sin with profound principles: Israel retains its identity even in sin, Torah study surpasses the daily offerings, and a condemned person's sincere confession brings atonement. Angel Gabriel's bold intercession before God frames the theme of advocacy and Divine judgment.

⚔️ Were Achan's Family Punished Alongside Him?

Exilarch
Joshua took Achan's sons, daughters, oxen, asses, and sheep to the valley — this implies his entire family was executed alongside him!
Rav Huna
By your logic, if Achan sinned, did all Israel sin too? Yet the verse says 'all Israel with him' came to the valley — they were witnesses, not the condemned.
Exilarch
Then why bring the sons and daughters at all? The verse explicitly enumerates them!
Rav Huna
They came to witness their father's punishment — so the lesson would be seared into their hearts and they would not repeat his deeds. Only Achan was executed.
Resolution Achan alone was stoned. His family and all Israel witnessed. The phrase 'all Israel with him' confirms collective presence for a moral lesson, not collective punishment.

📜 Achan's Transgressions — Layers of Sin

Theft of Dedicated Property

  • Took a fine mantle of Shinar (choice wool cloak)
  • Took 200 shekels of silver
  • Took a wedge of gold (50 shekels weight)
  • Concealed all items beneath his tent

Sexual Immorality

  • Engaged in relations with a betrothed young woman
  • Alluded in: 'he committed a wanton deed in Israel'
  • Rabbi Abba bar Zavda derives this from Joshua 7:15
  • Transgressed even this severe Biblical prohibition

Concealing Circumcision

  • Stretched remaining foreskin to hide that he was circumcised
  • Rabbi Ile'a: Achan violated all five books of Torah
  • Each book alluded to in Joshua's account of the sin
  • Rabbi Yehuda bar Masparta's teaching (44a)

🙏 The Confession Procedure for the Condemned

4 cubits from execution: The condemned man's garments are removed. A man is covered in front; a woman both front and back (Rabbi Yehuda's ruling).
↓
Invitation to confess: Court officials tell him: 'Confess — for all who confess have a share in the World to Come. So Achan confessed and Joshua said to him: "Give glory to God"'
↓
Simple formula (if needed): If he doesn't know how to confess, they instruct: 'Say simply — Let my death be an atonement for all my sins'
↓
Wrongful conviction (Rabbi Yehuda): If he knows he is innocent of this specific charge, he may say: 'Let my death be atonement for all my sins except this one'
↓
Achan as the paradigm: His confession achieved atonement — his name 'Zimri' in Chronicles shows restoration. He has a portion in the World to Come, despite his grave sins.
