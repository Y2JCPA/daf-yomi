Sanhedrin 85 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Sheshet, why may a court agent flog or curse a condemned person even though these acts are normally prohibited?
A. The condemned person has lost all legal rights
B. The honor of Heaven overrides the normal prohibition
C. The court's authority automatically exempts any agent
D. Only Rabbinically ordained judges may do so
Correct: B — Rav Sheshet teaches that the Torah's prohibition against flogging/cursing applies universally — the agent is only permitted because the honor of Heaven (carrying out divine law) supersedes normal interpersonal prohibitions.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the Mishna (85b), what distinguishes the halakha of striking a parent from cursing a parent?
A. Striking is more severe because it causes physical harm
B. Cursing is more severe because it applies even after the parent's death
C. Striking requires two witnesses; cursing requires only one
D. Both are equally treated with no distinctions
Correct: B — The Mishna notes a stringency of cursing over striking: one who curses a parent is liable even after the parent's death, whereas striking requires an actual wound during the parent's lifetime.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yoshiya interpret 'his father AND his mother' in Leviticus 20:9?
A. One is liable for cursing each parent individually
B. One is liable only if both parents are cursed together simultaneously
C. The verse refers only to the father, the mother being included by inference
D. The verse applies only to a son, not a daughter
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoshiya holds that conjunctions (vav) in the Torah mean 'both together' — one must curse both father AND mother together to be liable under this verse. Rabbi Yonatan disagrees, holding the vav includes each one individually.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What condition does Rabbi Yehuda add — beyond the first tanna — for capital liability in a kidnapping case?
A. The abductor must have acted with premeditation
B. The victim must be a Torah scholar
C. The abductor must exploit the victim before selling him
D. The abduction must occur in a public domain
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehuda derives from Deuteronomy 24:7 ('and he exploited him and sold him') that exploitation must precede the sale for capital liability to apply. The first tanna also requires exploitation but does not insist on this order.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Rav Sheshet say a son who strikes his condemned father (while the father proceeds voluntarily to execution) is liable?
A. Because a son can never be exempt from the prohibition against striking his father
B. Because in this case the son was not acting as an official court agent
C. Because the father had not yet been formally convicted
D. Because striking is always worse than cursing
Correct: B — Rav Sheshet interprets the baraita as describing a case where the court is not formally imploring the father to proceed — meaning the son is not acting in an official capacity. Without that official role, the normal prohibition against striking one's father applies.
