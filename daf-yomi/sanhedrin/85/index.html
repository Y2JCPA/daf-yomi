⚖️ Can a Son Serve as Court Agent Against His Father?

Questioners
What is the halakha if the court appoints a son as its agent to flog or curse his condemned father?
Rav Sheshet
Did the Torah permit flogging or cursing another who is NOT one's father? The Torah prohibits such acts! The agent is permitted only because the honor of Heaven supersedes the honor of the father — and this applies to any agent, including a son.
Baraita Objection
One commanded to strike another has a mitzva not to exceed — so one NOT commanded surely should not strike at all?
Rav Sheshet (Response)
The baraita's two clauses refer to different scenarios: one where striking is a mitzva (court-ordered), and one where it is not. A son acting as court agent falls under the mitzva category — he is permitted and even obligated.
Gemara Challenge
A baraita states: if a son strikes or curses his condemned father while being led to execution, the son IS liable — proof that a son cannot be agent!
Rav Sheshet (Reinterpretation)
The baraita refers to a case where the court is NOT formally imploring him to proceed — the son acts outside his official capacity. When acting as a duly appointed court agent, the son is exempt.
Conclusion No conclusive refutation of Rav Sheshet's view is found — a son may serve as a court agent to punish his father.

📊 Striking vs. Cursing a Parent — Mishna Comparison

✋ Striking a Parent

  • Capital offense only if a wound (חַבּוּרָה) is inflicted
  • No wound = no capital liability
  • Stringency: requires physical damage
  • Exempt if blow causes no lasting mark

🗣️ Cursing a Parent

  • Liable even without physical contact
  • More severe in one respect: applies even after parent's death
  • Applies to each parent individually (or together)
  • Source: Leviticus 20:9 — 'He has cursed his father and his mother'

📖 Rabbi Yoshiya vs. Rabbi Yonatan — 'Ish Ish' and Conjunctions

Rabbi Yoshiya
The doubled 'ish ish' (אִישׁ אִישׁ) expands liability — a woman who curses her parents is also liable. The phrase 'his father AND his mother' means BOTH together — one is only liable if both are cursed simultaneously.
Rabbi Yonatan
The 'vav' conjunction ('and') in 'his father AND his mother' means BOTH together AND EACH individually — one is liable for cursing either parent alone. The doubled 'ish ish' is merely rhetorical (Torah speaks in human language).
Gemara
Since Rabbi Yonatan uses 'his father and his mother' for the each-one-separately rule, how does he derive liability for cursing a parent AFTER DEATH?
Rabbi Yonatan (Answer)
From the parallel verse: 'And one who curses his father or his mother shall be put to death' (Exodus 21:17) — this includes post-mortem cursing. The redundant phrase instead derives: daughters, tumtum, and hermaphrodites are also obligated.
Key Verse Leviticus 20:9: מְקַלֵּל אָבִיו וְאִמּוֹ מוֹת יוּמָת — 'He has cursed his father and his mother; his blood shall be upon him'

🌙 Rabbi Yirmeya's Dilemmas on Kidnapping

דברים כד:ז — וְנִמְצָא בְיָדוֹ וְהִתְעַמֶּר בּוֹ וּמְכָרוֹ
Unresolved Questions (תֵּיקוּ) Dilemma 1: If one abducts a person and sells him while he is asleep — is that considered 'exploitation' sufficient for capital liability?

Dilemma 2: If one abducts a pregnant woman for the purpose of benefiting from her fetus alone — is that valid exploitation of a 'person'?

Why are these genuine dilemmas? One might think there is obviously no exploitation of an unaware/unborn person — but the Gemara explains: the question is whether intent-based exploitation (using a person as a means) qualifies even if the person is unaware.
