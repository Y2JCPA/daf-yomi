🚪 Two Central Disputes of Daf 15

Abaye
A lechi she'omeid me'elav (self-standing side post) is valid — even if not placed there for alleyway purposes, provided residents relied on it before Shabbat
Rava
A self-standing side post is not valid — it must have been designated for alleyway use from the outset
Rav Pappa
When breach equals the standing partition, carrying is permitted — the Torah only requires that the majority not be breached
Rav Huna b. Yehoshua
When breach equals the standing partition, carrying is prohibited — the standing portion must be greater than the breach
Key Resolution (Amud A) All agree: a full mechitza standing by itself IS valid without intent. The Abaye-Rava dispute is narrowed to the lechi (side post) alone.

🐄 Living Creatures as Halachic Objects

Rabbis (Majority View)

  • Living creature valid as sukka wall
  • Living creature valid as lechi (side post)
  • Living creature valid as lavud board around a well
  • Get may be written on a living creature
  • Verse: vekhatav lah sefer — 'a scroll' includes any surface capable of bearing writing

Rabbi Meir

  • Living creature invalid as sukka wall
  • Living creature invalid as lechi
  • Living creature invalid as lavud board
  • Living creature invalid as grave covering
  • Reason: may die, flee, or become ritually impure — fundamentally unreliable

Rabbi Yosei HaGelili

  • Agrees with Rabbis: living creatures valid as partitions
  • But: get on living creature is invalid
  • Source: sefer keritut — a 'scroll of severance' implies permanence
  • A living creature may run away — no true keritut (severance)
  • The expanded form keritut vs. karet teaches an unconditional severance

⚖️ Breach in a Partition — The Caravan Case (Amud B)

Setup (Mishna): Caravan camped in open valley, surrounded themselves with saddles and equipment as partition walls — carrying is permitted inside
↓
Mishna rule on breaches: Any breach ≤ 10 cubits is treated as an entrance — permitted. Any single breach > 10 cubits — prohibited to carry anywhere in the enclosure
↓
New dispute: What if total breach area equals the total standing portion — neither majority?
↓
Rav Pappa: Permitted — the Merciful One commanded only 'do not breach the majority.' Equal portions means the majority is not breached → valid partition
↓
Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua: Prohibited — 'breaches shall not exceed the built segment' means the standing must be greater, not merely equal
↓
Sukka proof (attempted): Metal skewers (invalid s'chach) interspersed with valid roofing — Rav Pappa: equal amounts = valid. Gemara responds: Rabbi Ami says add more valid roofing; Rava says place roofing crosswise to ensure visual dominance

📜 Deriving the Get Laws from the Verse

Rabbis
Vekhatav lah — 'that he write for her' teaches she is divorced only by writing, not by money. The word sefer merely means any surface capable of bearing writing
R. Yosei HaGelili
Sefer keritut — 'a scroll of severance' means a static document. He derives 'not by money' from keritut alone, freeing sefer to exclude living creatures
Rabbis
We derive from keritut: the get must sever all connection between them. If he says 'you are divorced, but you may not marry so-and-so' — it is invalid
R. Yosei HaGelili
I derive unconditional severance from the expanded form keritut rather than karet — both terms teach different aspects of complete divorce
Practical Result All agree a conditional get that restricts whom she may remarry is invalid — the document must effect complete, unrestricted severance (keritut).
