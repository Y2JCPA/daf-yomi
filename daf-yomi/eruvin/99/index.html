🫦 Is Spitting from Private to Public a Torah Violation?

Rav Yosef
One who urinates or spits from a private domain into a public domain on Shabbat is liable to bring a sin-offering — this constitutes a complete act of carrying.
Gemara Challenge
Liability requires lifting and placing from/onto a space of 4×4 handbreadths. The mouth is far smaller — how can there be Torah-level liability?
Resolution
Intent renders an area significant. One who throws food into a dog's mouth — his intent makes that small target count. Likewise, a person considers his own mouth important, so it is treated as 4×4.
Rava
New dilemma: if one stands in private domain but his member protrudes into public domain and he urinates — do we follow where the urine originates or where it exits? — Unresolved.
Key Principle Intent (כַּוָּנָה) can elevate a sub-minimal area to full halakhic significance for Shabbat carrying liability.

🍶 Drinking Across Domains — Head and Body Rule

Person wants to drink from a different domain than where he stands
↓
Does he bring his head and the majority of his body into the domain of the drink?
↓
✓ Yes — body follows
Permitted: He is legally present in the drinking domain; no cross-domain transfer occurs
✗ No — only extends head or hand
Prohibited: Body remains in origin domain; he risks absent-mindedly dragging the vessel back
↓
Rav Yosef: Even the lenient Rabbis agree here — concern for mishkach shakhach (forgetting and pulling vessel into wrong domain)

🏛️ Does the Drinking Rule Apply to a Karmelit?

Abaye — Karmelit = Same as Torah Domain

  • Proof from mishna: 'and the same applies in a winepress'
  • First clause already covered private domain — winepress must be karmelit
  • Therefore: prohibited to drink from karmelit without bringing most of body
  • Karmelit governed by identical drinking rules as Torah domains

Rava (+ Rav Sheshet) — No Proof from Winepress

  • 'Same applies in winepress' = topic of tithes (maasrot), not Shabbat
  • One may drink grape juice in winepress without tithing — that is the parallel
  • Rabbis: diluted with hot water → must tithe; cold water → need not tithe
  • No Shabbat ruling for karmelit can be derived from this mishna

🌳 Phlegm Before One's Master & Tree Canopy on Shabbat

כָּל מְשַׂנְאַי אָהֲבוּ מָוֶת — משלי ח:לו
Reish Lakish — Two Sharp Rulings Ruling 1 — Phlegm before one's Torah master:
Expelling phlegm in front of one's rav is liable for death at the hand of Heaven, as the verse states: 'All who hate Me love death' (Proverbs 8:36) — such contempt for Torah is akin to hatred of God.

Isn't this involuntary? No — we speak of one who felt phlegm accumulating and could have swallowed it, but chose to spit. That constitutes intentional disrespect.

Ruling 2 — Tree canopy on Shabbat:
A tree whose branches hang like a tent, tips within 3 tefaḥim of the ground, forms a private domain below. One may carry beneath it. But if the area is greater than 2 beit se'a — Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua: carrying is prohibited, as a roofed area beyond 2 beit se'a loses its domain status.
