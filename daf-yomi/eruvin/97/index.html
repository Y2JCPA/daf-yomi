🪢 New vs. Old Tefillin — The Carrying Dispute

Rabbi Meir
Whether tefillin are new or old, one carries them in pair by pair — both are fully permitted on Shabbat.
Rabbi Yehuda
New tefillin are forbidden to carry into town — a person exerts himself to fashion a permanent knot, and until tied, the object appears unfinished and lacking purpose.
Shmuel's Father
What defines 'old' tefillin? Any whose straps are permanently and firmly knotted — that is the operative criterion for fitness.
Gemara
But why tie a permanent knot at all? Why not simply use a bow, which is not a prohibited knot on Shabbat? — This question drives the next dispute.
Core Principle Rabbi Yehuda holds that tefillin without a permanent knot resemble an unfinished object — carrying them constitutes carrying an item that does not yet serve its purpose.

🎀 Is a Bow Knot a Valid Workaround on Shabbat?

Question: Why not tie tefillin straps with a bow? A bow is not prohibited on Shabbat!
↓
Abaye answers: Rabbi Yehuda holds that a bow IS a full-fledged knot — prohibited by Torah law on Shabbat
↓
Challenge from Rav Yehuda bar Rav Shmuel: The form of tefillin straps was given to Moses at Sinai — one cannot alter it regardless!
↓
Resolution
Case involves a bow shaped like a permanent knot — not a standard bow — hence the Sinaitic law applies
Implication
Even if bows were permitted, the divinely mandated form of the tefillin knot is binding and non-negotiable

⚠️ Carrying Tefillin in a Time of Danger on Shabbat

First Tanna

  • Cover the tefillin and proceed on your way
  • Applied to danger posed by gentiles
  • One person carries them continuously
  • Risk: may forget and carry 4 cubits unwittingly
  • Mishna's baseline ruling

Gemara — Baraita

  • In time of danger: carry less than 4 cubits at a time
  • Applied to danger of Shabbat violation
  • Stop and rest, then continue less than 4 cubits
  • Mishna is incomplete — both rulings apply in different cases
  • Gentile danger vs. Shabbat violation danger are distinct

Rabbi Shimon

  • Pass tefillin from person to person
  • Each carrier holds less than 4 cubits
  • Prevents unwitting full-distance carrying
  • Even an infant: pass to 100 people if necessary
  • Preferred: active passing beats passive resting

📖 New Mishna: Scroll Rolling Off a Roof — Retrieval Rules

Case 1 — Threshold: Reading on an elevated threshold; scroll rolls into public domain — one end remains in hand → permitted to roll it back
↓
Case 2 — Roof: Reading on a full private-domain roof; scroll rolls toward public domain
↓
If scroll is still above 10 tefachim from the ground → still in its own domain → roll it back
↓
If scroll falls below 10 tefachim → has entered public domain → he averts his eyes and does not retrieve it
↓
Rabbi Yehuda: Even a needle's breadth above the ground counts — roll it back
↓
Rabbi Shimon: Even if on the ground itself, roll it back — no rabbinic decree was enacted in a way that causes neglect of Torah
