QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Rabba permit sitting on roots that rise above 3 handbreadths but then bend back down close to the ground?
A. Bent roots are not usable and therefore pose no concern
B. Anything within 3 handbreadths of the ground is halakhically considered as the ground
C. The roots came from below, so they never acquired tree status
D. Rabba rules leniently in all tree-related disputes
Correct: B — Rabba applies the general principle that anything within 3 handbreadths of the ground is treated as the ground itself — so roots bending back into that zone revert to 'ground' status and may be used.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the Gemara's final ruling on walking on grass on Shabbat, and why?
A. Always prohibited, derived from 'he who hastens with his feet sins'
B. Permitted only in winter and only when wearing shoes
C. Generally permitted, since we rule like Rabbi Shimon that there is no liability for an unintentional act during a permitted act
D. Prohibited whenever the grass is green
Correct: C — The Gemara concludes: now that halakha follows Rabbi Shimon — who holds there is no liability for an unintentional prohibited act performed in the course of a permitted act — walking on grass is generally permitted.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does the Gemara say is the proper way for a wife to 'demand' the conjugal mitzva, and why?
A. She should explicitly state her desire to fulfill the mitzva
B. She should ask a rabbi to instruct her husband
C. She should make herself pleasing to him so he understands her desire without her stating it explicitly
D. She should withhold household duties until he initiates
Correct: C — The Gemara distinguishes praise-worthy 'demand' from immodesty: a wife should not voice her desire explicitly (due to Eve's curse of shyness and tzniut norms), but should make herself attractive and welcoming — and he will understand.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, what does the rooster teach us about interpersonal conduct?
A. Fidelity — staying with one partner for life
B. Modesty — concealing intimate behavior from others
C. Appeasement — promising before intimacy and apologizing afterward
D. Industriousness — providing for others before oneself
Correct: C — The rooster spreads its wings before mating as if to promise 'I will buy you a coat reaching to your feet,' and bends its head afterward as if to say 'I have no money to pay.' This models appeasing one's spouse before and after intimacy.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Dimi, what is one of the additional curses given to Eve beyond the seven derived from the verse in Genesis?
A. She is wrapped like a mourner — must cover her hair
B. She is forbidden to speak in the presence of men
C. She must fast on the day of her wedding
D. She may not inherit property from her parents
Correct: A — Rav Dimi adds three curses not found in the verse: (1) wrapped like a mourner — must cover her hair; (2) ostracized — cannot seclude herself with men; (3) demands her husband in her heart but is too shy to voice it. The baraita adds three more: grows hair like Lilit, urinates seated, and serves as a pillow.
