Eruvin 92 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, when one courtyard established an eiruv and the other did not, who may carry in the ruin between them?
A. Both courtyards may carry freely in the ruin
B. Only the courtyard that established an eiruv may carry in the ruin
C. Neither courtyard may carry in the ruin at all
D. Only the courtyard without an eiruv may carry in the ruin
Correct: B — Rav Huna rules that the ruin is conferred to the courtyard that established an eiruv — only its residents have rights to carry there.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the Mishna, if a large roof and small roof are adjacent with no partition, what is the halakha?
A. Both roofs are fully permitted for carrying
B. Both roofs are fully forbidden for carrying
C. The large roof is permitted; the small roof is forbidden
D. The small roof is permitted; the large roof is forbidden
Correct: C — The Mishna rules that the large domain is permitted and the small is forbidden, as the small is subsumed within the large — not the reverse.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Shmuel say the Mishna repeats the ruling for both roofs and courtyards?
A. To teach that the walls of a house below serve as partitions for the roof above
B. To teach that a courtyard, like a roof, is not a place where multitudes regularly tread
C. To emphasize that both domains require their own independent eiruv
D. To distinguish between reshut ha-rabbim and reshut ha-yachid
Correct: B — Shmuel says the repetition teaches that a courtyard is like a roof — since multitudes do not regularly tread in a courtyard, it is treated leniently like a private roof domain.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If nine men are in the large courtyard and one man is in the adjacent small courtyard, can they form a valid minyan?
A. No — all ten must be in the same physical space
B. Yes — but only if there is an open doorway between them
C. Yes — the small courtyard is subsumed within the large, so all ten are considered together
D. No — a minyan requires all men to hear the ḥazan clearly
Correct: C — Since the large courtyard extends into the small, the single man in the small courtyard is legally considered part of the group in the large, completing the quorum of ten.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Abaye's novel concern about the large/small courtyard principle?
A. A partition between two domains can itself cause a prohibition that would not exist without it
B. Prayer quorums should never be split across two separate domains
C. Kilayim laws do not apply within enclosed private courtyards
D. An eiruv in the large courtyard automatically validates the small courtyard's eiruv
Correct: A — Abaye identifies מחיצה האוסרת: the partition between large and small courtyard creates the 'extension' relationship that forbids kilayim — without the partition, both would be one domain and no prohibition would arise.
