QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi need to state BOTH the general leniency rule for eiruv AND that the halakha follows Rabbi Yoḥanan ben Nuri specifically?
A. The two rulings address different tractates of the Talmud
B. Each ruling prevents a mistaken inference the other alone would permit
C. One ruling is biblical in origin and the other is rabbinic
D. Rabbi Yoḥanan ben Nuri always rules stringently in eiruv matters
Correct: B — Without the general leniency rule, one might follow R' Yoḥanan ben Nuri even when he is strict. Without the specific rule, one might think leniency only applies in individual-vs-individual disputes, not when one opposes many.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What proof does Rav Pappa bring that even in rabbinic law, a single authority vs. multiple authorities is treated differently?
A. The laws of nolad on Festivals
B. The laws of eiruv techumin for one who was asleep at Shabbat onset
C. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi retracted his ruling when he remembered multiple colleagues disagreed with Rabbi Elazar
D. Rava's explicit ruling that eiruv is always lenient
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi ruled like Rabbi Elazar but retracted upon remembering that Rabbi Elazar's colleagues — a majority — disagreed, demonstrating that majority matters even in rabbinic halakha.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the Tannaitic hierarchy, whose opinion prevails in a dispute between Rabbi Yehuda and Rabbi Yosei?
A. Rabbi Yehuda
B. Rabbi Meir
C. Rabbi Yosei
D. Left as an unresolved dilemma (תיקו)
Correct: C — Rabbi Ya'akov bar Idi quotes Rabbi Yoḥanan: in disputes between Rabbi Yehuda and Rabbi Yosei, the halakha follows Rabbi Yosei.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What defines a 'distant report' (shmu'ah rechokah) with regard to mourning laws, and what is its practical consequence?
A. The relative died in a foreign country; the mourner must still observe full shiva
B. The mourner was traveling when they heard the news; they mourn for three days
C. The news arrived more than 30 days after the death; only one day of mourning is required
D. The mourner did not personally know the deceased; no mourning is required
Correct: C — A proximate report arrives within 30 days of death and requires full shiva. A distant report arrives after 30 days and requires only one day of mourning — the lenient majority opinion prevails.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Rav Mesharshiya's position on using the Tannaitic decision-making principles (e.g., 'halakha follows Yehuda over Meir')?
A. They are biblical in origin and absolutely binding in all cases
B. They apply only to eiruv disputes, not to other areas of halakha
C. They are not to be relied upon, since explicit contrary rulings exist in the Talmud
D. They apply only when both sages debated each other directly
Correct: C — Rav Mesharshiya warns these principles are unreliable, citing that Rav explicitly ruled like Rabbi Shimon against Rabbi Yehuda — contrary to the standard rule. The Gemara responds that explicit rulings are exceptions, but the caveat remains.
