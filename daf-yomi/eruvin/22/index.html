🐦 Who Is 'Like a Raven' in Torah Study?

Rabba
One who blackens his face — who fasts and deprives himself physically for the sake of Torah
Rava
One who is cruel to his household — like Rav Adda bar Mattana, who left for the study hall despite his wife's pleas about their children's needs
Source The raven feeds its young sparingly and cries out — a symbol of harsh, single-minded dedication. Both interpretations draw from the verse connecting dwelling in wisdom to self-denial.

⚖️ God's Long-Suffering: Two Directions

אֶרֶךְ אַפַּיִם — שְׁתֵּי אַפַּיִם
אֶרֶךְ אַפַּיִם — Erekh Appayim (Deuteronomy 7:10) Rabbi Ḥaggai (some say Rabbi Shmuel bar Naḥmani): The word erech appayim is written in plural — teaching two forms of divine patience:

1. Toward the righteous: God delays their reward — it is stored for the World to Come
2. Toward the wicked: God delays their punishment — granting time for repentance

Rabbi Ila adds: 'He will not be slack' means punishment comes swiftly; only reward for the absolutely righteous is deferred. Today is the time to do them; tomorrow to receive their reward. (Deut. 7:11)

🛤️ Does a Public Path Invalidate Partitions?

Rabbi Yehuda

  • Public path through boards: must divert the path to the sides
  • Two houses on either side of public domain: use two beams + two boards
  • A proper wall enclosure: permitted even without diverting
  • Boards alone ≠ sufficient when public freely traverses them

The Rabbis

  • Public path through boards: no diversion needed
  • Four nominal partitions (boards) suffice despite public use
  • Public thoroughfare does not invalidate a nominal partition
  • The mishna demonstrates the strength of partitions even under pressure

🗺️ Eretz Yisrael & Public Domain Liability

Rabbi Yoḥanan: One is not liable for carrying in the public domain of Eretz Yisrael
↓
Abaye challenges
Babylonia is also surrounded by rivers — why is it different?
Rav Dimi answers
The Ladder of Tyre and Slope of Gader form valid partitions (10+ handbreadths) around Eretz Yisrael specifically
↓
Ravin's version: The ascents and descents of Eretz Yisrael create private domain status throughout — Rava confirms even steep rope-climbed slopes qualify
↓
Beit Gilgul exception: Joshua loved Israel — he established roads wherever terrain was convenient, handing those areas to the public domain
