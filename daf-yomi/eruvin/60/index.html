No — no partition

Yes — 10+ high

No — under 10 high

🪜 Do Ladders Negate a Wall? Rav Beruna Challenges Rav Yehuda

Rav Yehuda (Shmuel)
A wall lined with ladders — even spanning more than 10 cubits — still has the status of a valid partition. The ladders do not create a prohibited opening wider than 10 cubits.
Rav Beruna (challenge)
But Shmuel himself said: residents of a balcony and a courtyard who did not make a joint eiruv restrict each other! If the ladder-lined wall is valid, what is the problem?
Rav Yehuda (reply)
That balcony case involves one that is under 10 handbreadths high — so it is considered part of the courtyard. The wall's validity is not in question; the balcony's domain status is.
Gemara (follow-up)
If it is already part of the courtyard, why does placing a partition matter at all? Answer: the partition creates a lavud closure — filling a gap of less than 3 handbreadths and making the area a distinct sub-section.
הלכה A wall lined with ladders retains partition status (Shmuel); height of 10 tefachim determines independent domain.

🔄 Abaye's Five Retractions — The Kakunya Case

Starting point: Rav Yosef dispatches Abaye to establish an eiruv for Kakunya, a formerly-public city now enclosed as private
↓
1st retraction: The mishna states one may not establish an eiruv for all of it — so an excluded section is required; Abaye cannot simply make one eiruv for the whole city
↓
2nd retraction: Rabba bar Avuh made an eiruv for all of Meḥoza (also formerly public) neighborhood by neighborhood — perhaps Kakunya can use the same approach
↓
3rd retraction: Meḥoza worked because rooftops connected its neighborhoods as one domain; Kakunya's houses cannot connect via roofs the same way
↓
4th retraction: Windows between houses can substitute for roof connections and link the neighborhoods sufficiently
↓
5th retraction: Windows aren't even necessary — the excluded storehouse of Mar bar Pofidata in Pumbedita shows an isolated, separated section can serve as the required exclusion zone

🧭 Eiruv Placement: Inside City Outskirts vs. Outside City Boundary

Eiruv Placed in City Outskirts

  • Area within ~70 cubits surrounding the city (עיבורה של עיר)
  • Treated halakhically as though placed inside the city
  • 2000-cubit Shabbat limit still begins from the city edge
  • Person gains no extension of their Shabbat range
  • Mishna: כאילו לא עירב — as if he did not make an eiruv at all

Eiruv Placed Beyond City Boundary

  • Even 1 cubit beyond the city's legal boundary (techum)
  • 2000 cubits measured from the eiruv location — not city edge
  • Gain distance on the side toward the eiruv
  • Lose equal distance on the opposite side of the city
  • Strategically valuable when traveling in a specific direction on Shabbat
