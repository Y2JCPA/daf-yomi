🌊 Central Disputes of Daf 87

Mishna
A water channel through a courtyard requires a 10-handbreadth partition at both the entrance AND the exit — one side alone is insufficient.
Rabbi Yehuda
The existing courtyard wall can serve as the required partition. Proof: the Elders of Avel permitted this very practice!
Mishna
A balcony over water requires a purpose-built 10-handbreadth partition around the hole to draw water on Shabbat.
Rabbi Ḥananya ben Akavya
For a 4×4 cubit balcony over the Sea of Tiberias — carve a 4×4 handbreadth hole; the floor edges bend down and form the partitions themselves!
Core Question Both disputes turn on whether existing structures — walls, floor edges, natural banks — can substitute for purpose-built partitions in Shabbat domain law.

🔀 Two Traditions of Rabbi Yoḥanan: Rav Dimi vs. Ze'eiri

Rav Dimi
Rabbi Yoḥanan said: A place smaller than 4×4 handbreadths is an exempt domain. Transferring objects from a private domain through such a place into a karmelit is forbidden even by rabbinic standards.
Ze'eiri
Rabbi Yoḥanan said: Where a wall between two courtyards is less than 4×4 handbreadths thick, each side may carry food up to the top and eat there — treating it as an exempt domain permitting independent use.
Abaye
These are not contradictory — they address different legal contexts and were transmitted as separate rulings.
Gemara's Resolution Rav Dimi's tradition concerns Torah-level domains (private ↔ public) where the Sages were strict. Ze'eiri's tradition concerns rabbinic domains (karmelit) where they were lenient. Two rulings, two domains.

📐 Do Cavities Extend a Domain? Private vs. Karmelit

Private Domain (Torah Law)

  • Cavities in a private-domain wall ARE part of the domain
  • An object placed in a hole is treated as if in the main domain
  • Torah-level rule — strictly enforced by Sages
  • Logical: the wall belongs to the domain it borders

Karmelit (Rabbinic Domain)

  • Abaye bar Avin & Rav Ḥanina bar Avin: NO cavities for a karmelit
  • Since karmelit is only rabbinic, Sages did not extend the prohibition into adjacent holes
  • A water channel does not extend its domain to the windows above it
  • Rav Ashi adds: adjacent holes ARE nullified by the karmelit — but only those touching it, not distant ones

Ravina's Final View

  • The baraita dispute (3 vs. 4 handbreadths) concerns the channel's banks, not its width
  • Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel: lavud applies at <4 handbreadths
  • Sages: lavud applies only at <3 handbreadths
  • Resolves the entire baraita without invoking karmelit-cavity rules

🐟 Rabbi Ḥananya ben Akavya's Three Leniencies for Tiberias

חנניה בן עקביא התיר לאנשי טבריה שלשה דברים — Ḥananya ben Akavya permitted the people of Tiberias three things
תקנות טבריה — Special Rulings for Tiberias 1. Drawing water through a balcony hole on Shabbat
Permitted over the Sea of Galilee because its clearly defined banks are visible from the balcony, and the surrounding towns and enclosures give it the status of an enclosed area — the natural geography substitutes for constructed partitions.

2. Insulating food in the pods of legumes
One may insulate warm food in legume pods even though dew moistens them overnight. Since the dew contact was unintentional (אינו מתכוין), the produce was never rendered susceptible to tumah — and insulating with those pods does not violate Shabbat rules.

3. A third unnamed leniency
Specific to Tiberias and its unique circumstances as a lakeside city.
