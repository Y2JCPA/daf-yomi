Eruvin 87 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, a water channel passing through a courtyard requires partitions at how many points to permit drawing on Shabbat?
A. Only at the entrance to the courtyard
B. Only at the exit from the courtyard
C. At both the entrance and the exit
D. No partitions are needed if the courtyard is enclosed
Correct: C — The Mishna requires a 10-handbreadth partition at both the entrance AND the exit. A partition at only one end is explicitly insufficient — the baraita confirms that either case alone is forbidden.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did the Sages dismiss Rabbi Yehuda's proof from the water channel that ran from Avel to Tzippori?
A. The Elders of Avel had no authority to issue such a ruling
B. The channel was located in a public domain
C. The channel likely lacked the depth or width to qualify as a halachic domain
D. The ruling applied only to that specific town and could not be generalized
Correct: C — The Sages responded that drawing from the Avel-Tzippori channel was permitted simply because it was either not ten handbreadths deep or not four handbreadths wide — making it an exempt domain to begin with, not a proof about partition leniency.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How did the Gemara resolve the apparent contradiction between Rav Dimi's and Ze'eiri's traditions in the name of Rabbi Yoḥanan?
A. One of them misremembered the ruling
B. Rav Dimi later retracted his position in favor of Ze'eiri
C. Rav Dimi addresses Torah-level domains; Ze'eiri addresses rabbinic domains (karmelit)
D. Ze'eiri's ruling was limited to the specific case of a wall between two courtyards
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that both transmissions are accurate but apply to different legal contexts. Rav Dimi's teaching (exempt domain forbids transfer) concerns Torah-level domains where the Sages were strict; Ze'eiri's (each side may carry up and eat) concerns rabbinic domains where they were lenient.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Abaye bar Avin and Rav Ḥanina bar Avin ruled that regarding a karmelit:
A. Its cavities follow the same rules as private-domain cavities
B. It has no cavities — adjacent holes are not included in its domain
C. Cavities apply only if adjacent to the karmelit, not to distant holes
D. Cavities are forbidden only when the hole is larger than 4×4 handbreadths
Correct: B — Because the karmelit is only a rabbinic prohibition, the Sages did not apply the Torah-level cavity rule. A water channel does not extend its karmelit status into holes in adjacent walls. (Rav Ashi later adds a nuance: adjacent holes that touch the karmelit are nullified by it, but distant ones are not.)
QUESTION 5 OF 5
For what geographic reason did Rabbi Yoḥanan (citing Rabbi Yosei ben Zimra) limit Rabbi Ḥananya ben Akavya's balcony leniency to the Sea of Tiberias?
A. The Sea of Tiberias is smaller than four cubits wide
B. The city of Tiberias had a special rabbinic dispensation from the Sanhedrin
C. The Sea of Galilee has clearly visible banks and is surrounded by towns and enclosures that form a natural enclosure
D. The balconies in Tiberias were always positioned within three handbreadths of a wall
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan explained that Rabbi Ḥananya permitted the practice specifically for the Sea of Tiberias because it has clearly defined, visible banks, and the surrounding towns and enclosures create a recognizable enclosed area — circumstances that cannot be assumed for an arbitrary body of water.
