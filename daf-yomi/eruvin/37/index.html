⚖️ Is Retroactive Designation (ברירה) Valid?

Rabbi Meir / Rabbi Yehuda
Breira is valid — one may designate teruma or an eiruv to apply retroactively. Rabbi Yehuda at least allows it for rabbinic enactments.
Rabbi Yosei / Rabbi Shimon
No breira — retroactive designation is legally ineffective. Produce consumed before designation cannot be covered by a later act of separation.
The Core Question When you say 'the two log I will separate later shall be teruma for what I already drank' — does the future act sanctify the past? The entire daf turns on this dispute.

🦅 Ulla Resolves the Contradiction in Rabbi Yehuda

Ayo (disciple)
Rabbi Yehuda permits breira even for Torah-level obligations — you can retroactively designate teruma for wine already consumed. The Tosefta supports this.
Ulla
Ayo's ruling directly contradicts the Mishna — reject it. Rabbi Yehuda allows breira only for rabbinic decrees. For Torah obligations like teruma, even he agrees there is no breira.
Ulla's Pairing of Authorities Ulla teaches the Tosefta names in pairs: Rabbi Meir & Rabbi Yehuda permit retroactive designation; Rabbi Yosei & Rabbi Shimon prohibit it.

🐦 Does Rabbi Yosei Accept Breira? — The Bird Offering Test

Two women jointly bring birds — one needs a burnt-offering, one a sin-offering. The priest decides at sacrifice which bird is which.
↓
If this is valid, it seems Rabbi Yosei accepts breira — the bird's status is determined only retroactively at the moment of offering.
↓
Rabba's Answer
The women stipulated from the outset that the priest would decide. This is not breira — it is fulfillment of a pre-existing condition.
Gemara's Follow-up
Even without stipulation, birds are fully designated at purchase (burnt-offering) or at the moment the priest offers them. No retroactive designation is needed.

📜 Breira: Torah Law vs. Rabbinic Decrees

Torah Obligations

  • Rabbi Yehuda: NO breira (per Ulla's resolution)
  • Rabbi Yosei: NO breira — proven from tithe cases
  • Rabbi Shimon: NO breira (wine of Kutim ruling)
  • Example: Separating teruma for consumed wine
  • Example: Second tithe redeemed on uncertain coin

Rabbinic Enactments

  • Rabbi Yehuda: YES breira (his leniency applies here)
  • Rabbi Meir: YES breira in all cases
  • Rabbi Shimon: possibly YES (Rav Yosef disputes this)
  • Example: Eiruv for whichever direction I travel
  • Rav Yosef: no distinction — whoever rejects breira rejects it everywhere

🍇 Rava's Distinction — The Wine of Kutim Is Not About Breira

רֵאשִׁית דְּגָנְךָ — 'The first of your grain' (Devarim 18:4)
Rava's Key Insight Rabbi Shimon's prohibition on wine of a Kuti is not evidence against breira at all. The Torah requires teruma to be 'the first of your grain' — meaning the teruma must be separated while recognizable remnants of the produce still exist. Once the wine is consumed, there are no remnants left, so the separation is invalid on independent grounds. Abaye challenges this with cases of two pomegranates and a pile of produce, but Rava distinguishes: a heap always has visible remnants around it.
