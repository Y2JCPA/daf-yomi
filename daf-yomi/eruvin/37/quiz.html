5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Ulla, in what domain does Rabbi Yehuda permit retroactive designation (breira)?
A. Torah-level obligations only
B. Both Torah and rabbinic obligations equally
C. Rabbinic enactments only, not Torah law
D. Only when both parties explicitly stipulate at the outset
Correct: C — Ulla resolves the apparent contradiction in Rabbi Yehuda's rulings by limiting his leniency: breira is valid only for rabbinic enactments (like eiruv), not for Torah obligations such as teruma separation.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rabba explain the Mishna about two women and their birds without invoking breira?
A. The birds automatically become designated at the moment of purchase
B. Rabbi Yosei makes a special exception for Temple sacrifices
C. The women stipulated from the outset that the priest would determine which bird is which
D. The priest's intention alone is sufficient to classify the offerings
Correct: C — Rabba explains that the women made an explicit stipulation before bringing the birds that the priest would decide their roles. This is fulfillment of a pre-established condition, not retroactive designation.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reverse the attributions in the Tosefta about second tithe redemption?
A. Rav Naḥman ruled that the scribe made a copying error
B. The latter clause shows Rabbi Yosei conceding a case that proves he requires certainty at the moment of the act
C. Rabbi Yosei is always quoted first in Tosefta disputes by convention
D. The Rabbis always take the lenient position, so the original must be wrong
Correct: B — The latter clause of the same Tosefta shows Rabbi Yosei conceding that a 'new sela' (single certain coin) does work for redemption — demonstrating he requires certainty upfront, locking in the reversed attribution.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rava's reason that wine of a Kuti cannot be covered by retroactive designation?
A. Kutim are presumed to separate tithes incorrectly, requiring extra stringency
B. Wine has a special Torah prohibition against mixing consecrated and unconsecrated portions
C. The Torah requires teruma to be 'the first,' so recognizable remnants must exist at the time of separation
D. Retroactive designation is always invalid for liquids, which have no fixed form
Correct: C — Rava argues this has nothing to do with breira. The Torah's requirement of 'reishit' (firstfruits) means teruma must be separated while the produce still has identifiable remnants. Once drunk, the wine leaves nothing to designate.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What was Rav Naḥman's response when Rava challenged him with the baraita about an eiruv set for an uncertain direction?
A. He ruled the baraita follows Ayo and Rabbi Yehuda's lenient school
B. He said breira is always valid for eiruvim since they are rabbinic
C. He was silent and gave no answer
D. He rejected the baraita as a minority opinion with no practical weight
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman was silent. The Gemara notes he could have deflected by citing Ayo's school (Rabbi Yehuda's leniency), but did not — leaving Rava's challenge open as a pointed difficulty.
