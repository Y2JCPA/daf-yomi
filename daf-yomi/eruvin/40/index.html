🌙 Two Days of Doubt

יום טוב שני של גלויות
Core Theme Eruvin 40 orbits the ambiguous status of Yom Tov Sheni — the second Festival day observed in the Diaspora due to calendar uncertainty. Are the two days one extended sanctity or two distinct sanctities? This question ripples through debates about forbidden food, Rosh HaShana prayer formulas, conditional blessings, and whether Shehecheyanu requires a cup of wine on Yom Kippur.

🦌 Who Ate at the Exilarch's Table?

Rav Naḥman & Rav Ḥisda
We ate the animal brought to the Exilarch on the Festival. Something carried from outside the Shabbat limit is forbidden only to the person it was brought for — the Exilarch. Others present may freely eat it.
Rav Sheshet
I did not eat it. Anything brought to the Exilarch's house is effectively brought with all the Sages in mind — he always invites them on Festivals. Therefore it is forbidden to all of us, not just to him.
Ameimar (resolving)
Rav Sheshet's objection concerned a separate question — whether the two Festival days are distinct sanctities. The Exilarch invitation argument and the techum debate should not be conflated; Ameimar's version clarifies the positions.
Key Rule Food carried from outside the techum (Shabbat limit) for a specific person is forbidden only to that person — others may eat it freely.

🥬 Rava and the Turnips of Meḥoza

Gentile merchants bring turnips from outside the Shabbat limit into Meḥoza on a Festival day
↓
Rava examines the turnips — they are withered, clearly harvested days earlier, not on the Festival itself
↓
✅ Rava permits purchase
"Something brought for one Jew is permitted to another — and these came for many Jews at once"
↓
Later development
Merchants begin bringing large quantities specifically for Jewish Festival customers — a new pattern emerges
Rava's new ruling
❌ Rava prohibits purchase — evident that produce is now being deliberately harvested on the Festival for Jews

📜 Rabbi Dosa vs. The Rabbis on Rosh HaShana Prayer

Rabbi Dosa

  • The prayer leader mentions the New Moon in the Rosh HaShana Amida on both days
  • Adds a conditional formula: 'If today is the first day, this prayer stands; if tomorrow is the true day, tomorrow's prayer stands'
  • The condition addresses genuine calendar uncertainty about which day is authentic Rosh HaShana
  • Also applied his conditional approach to ordinary New Moons throughout the year

The Rabbis

  • Did not accept the use of conditional prayers (tenai) in this context
  • A baraita confirms: Rabbis rejected Rabbi Dosa's condition for regular New Moons throughout the year as well
  • The Gemara concludes: their dispute is about the condition itself, not about whether to mention the New Moon
  • Both sides agree New Moon should be mentioned — they differ only on adding a tenai

⏰ Shehecheyanu on Rosh HaShana and Yom Kippur

The Dilemma (raised in Rav Huna's academy): Must one recite Shehecheyanu — 'Who has given us life, sustained us, and brought us to this time' — on Rosh HaShana and Yom Kippur?
↓
Rav Yehuda's ruling: "I recite Shehecheyanu even over a new gourd — I certainly recite it on Rosh HaShana and Yom Kippur!" (Optional on mundane occasions; obligatory on holy days.)
↓
The Yom Kippur problem: Normally recited over wine — but one cannot drink wine before Yom Kippur (lest one accept the fast early) nor leave wine tasted-after (must drink immediately upon blessing).
↓
Precedent from Rav: Rav accepted early Shabbat via Kiddush, proving one can accept the sanctity of a day early. But Yom Kippur's wine prohibition complicates this solution.
↓
The Conclusion (sent via Rav Yeimar the Elder to Rav Ḥisda): Shehecheyanu is recited on both Rosh HaShana and Yom Kippur. It does not require a cup of wine — it may be recited even in the open marketplace.

📋 Eruvin 40 — Summary Map

Yom Tov Sheni Uncertainty
Two Festival days in Diaspora due to calendar doubt — one long sanctity or two distinct ones?
↓
Food Law
Techum produce → forbidden only to intended recipient; brought for many = permitted to all. Rava stops buying when merchants exploit the leniency.
Prayer Formula
Rabbi Dosa: add tenai (condition) + mention New Moon. Rabbis: reject the condition. Both agree New Moon belongs in Rosh HaShana Amida.
Shehecheyanu
Required on Rosh HaShana & Yom Kippur. No wine cup needed. May be recited in the marketplace. Rav Yehuda: obligatory on holy days.
