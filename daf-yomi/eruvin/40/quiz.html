QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Rav Sheshet refuse to eat the animal brought to the Exilarch's house on the Festival?
A. The animal had been slaughtered improperly on the Festival
B. Anything brought to the Exilarch's house is considered brought for all the Sages, making it forbidden to everyone
C. It was unclear whether the animal had been caught before the Festival
D. Rav Sheshet held both Festival days share one sanctity, making all Festival food forbidden
Correct: B — Rav Sheshet held that since the Exilarch always invites the Sages to dine with him on Festivals, food brought for the Exilarch is implicitly brought for all of them — rendering it forbidden to everyone present.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Rava first permit the turnips, and why did he later prohibit them?
A. He permitted fresh turnips; later prohibited withered ones as unfit
B. He permitted them since they were brought for many Jews; later prohibited when merchants visibly began harvesting on Festival days for Jewish customers
C. He permitted them because gentiles are exempt from techum; prohibited them when Jews began transporting turnips themselves
D. He permitted them on day one of the Festival and prohibited them on day two
Correct: B — The withered turnips were clearly pre-cut (not harvested on the Festival), and brought for many Jews — so they were permitted. Once merchants began bringing large quantities specifically on Festival days for Jewish buyers, it was evident they were being harvested on the Festival itself, so Rava prohibited them.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does the Gemara conclude was the actual dispute between Rabbi Dosa and the Rabbis about Rosh HaShana prayer?
A. Whether the New Moon should be mentioned at all on Rosh HaShana
B. Whether ten or seven blessings should be recited in the Amida
C. Whether to add a conditional formula (tenai) to the prayer
D. Whether the prayer leader should repeat the Amida aloud
Correct: C — The Gemara concludes that both Rabbi Dosa and the Rabbis agree the New Moon should be mentioned on Rosh HaShana. Their dispute was specifically about the conditional formula (tenai) — Rabbi Dosa required it to address calendar uncertainty; the Rabbis rejected conditional prayer as a category.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the final halakhic ruling regarding Shehecheyanu on Rosh HaShana and Yom Kippur?
A. Recited only on Rosh HaShana; omitted on Yom Kippur due to the fast
B. Recited over a cup of wine before Yom Kippur begins
C. Recited on both days; no cup of wine required — may even be said in the marketplace
D. Recited only if a cup of wine is available on both occasions
Correct: C — The Gemara's conclusion, sent via Rav Yeimar to Rav Ḥisda, is that Shehecheyanu is recited on both Rosh HaShana and Yom Kippur, and — unlike Kiddush — does not require a cup of wine. It may be recited even in public.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Beit Shammai, how many Amida blessings are recited when Rosh HaShana falls on Shabbat?
A. Seven — the standard Shabbat Amida
B. Nine — the standard Rosh HaShana Amida
C. Ten — nine Rosh HaShana blessings plus one Shabbat blessing
D. Eighteen — the standard weekday Amida
Correct: C — Beit Shammai require ten blessings: the nine standard Rosh HaShana blessings plus one additional blessing for Shabbat. Beit Hillel, by contrast, require only seven blessings on this occasion.
