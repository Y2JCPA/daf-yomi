⚖️ Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel: Why Hillel Won

Beit Shammai
The halakha is in accordance with our opinion!
Beit Hillel
The halakha is in accordance with our opinion!
Divine Voice
Elu v'elu divrei Elohim chayyim — Both these and those are the words of the living God. But the halakha follows Beit Hillel.
Gemara
Why Beit Hillel? Because they were agreeable and forbearing — they always taught Beit Shammai's opinion before their own.
Key Principle Anyone who humbles himself — the Holy One exalts him. Anyone who exalts himself — the Holy One humbles him. Greatness flees those who pursue it.

Yet practically, the halakha follows Beit Hillel, because they were humble, forbearing, and always cited their opponents' views first — even before their own.

🎓 Rabbi Meir's Two Teachers

Rabbi Yishmael

  • Rabbi Meir's first teacher
  • Meir placed iron sulfate in ink — Yishmael initially said nothing
  • Later warned: fly landing on a letter crown could cause unintended erasure of God's Name
  • Concern rooted in the sota passage: 'shall be erased' must be intentional

Rabbi Akiva

  • Rabbi Meir's second teacher (went to Akiva after struggling with Yishmael's depth)
  • Also prohibited iron sulfate in sota scroll ink
  • Praised a disciple's novel teaching only to sharpen students' minds — not as ruling
  • Meir eventually returned to Akiva as his primary master

🔍 Identifying 'That Disciple' Before Rabbi Akiva

A disciple stated a halakha before Rabbi Akiva in the name of Rabbi Yishmael — who is he?
↓
Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi: Whenever you see this formula — 'a disciple said before Rabbi Akiva in the name of Rabbi Yishmael' — it is always...
↓
...Rabbi Meir, the only student who served both Rabbi Yishmael and Rabbi Akiva
↓
Difficulty #1
One source says Meir first studied with Akiva; another says he first studied with Yishmael
Resolution
Meir first came to Akiva, couldn't grasp his depth, then went to Yishmael — and later returned to Akiva

🌱 Was It Better for Man to Have Been Created?

Beit Shammai
It would have been preferable had man NOT been created than to have been created — the burden of Torah obligations makes existence treacherous.
Beit Hillel
It is preferable for man to have been created than not — existence brings the opportunity for Torah and mitzvot.
Final Vote
They voted and concluded: It would have been preferable had man not been created — but since he has been created, let him carefully examine his past deeds and scrutinize his future actions.
Duration of This Dispute Two and a half years — shorter than the three-year halakhic debates, yet its conclusion cuts to the heart of human responsibility.
