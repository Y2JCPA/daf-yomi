QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why do upright boards surrounding wells NOT apply in Babylonia?
A. The boards would be swept away by Babylonian floods
B. Water is plentiful from rivers and canals, making wells non-essential
C. Babylonian wells are too deep for the boards to function
D. Babylonian Jews follow a different Shabbat tradition for wells
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the leniency was created because wells are essential for water. In Babylonia, however, rivers and canals provide abundant water — wells are not essential, so the leniency has no purpose there.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was the correct sequence of who revealed the full breadth of Torah?
A. Solomon stated it, Ezekiel explained it, David confirmed it
B. Moses hinted at it, David stated it, Zechariah explained it
C. David stated it without explaining; Ezekiel hinted further; Zechariah fully explained it
D. Zechariah stated it, David elaborated, Ezekiel finalized it
Correct: C — Mari bar Mar taught: David stated Torah is 'exceedingly broad' without explanation; Ezekiel hinted through the scroll's three contents; Zechariah came and fully explained it via the flying scroll.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava, why were the words of the Sages not written in the Torah itself?
A. They were added centuries later and could not be retrofitted into the text
B. The Sages themselves preferred oral transmission over written text
C. It is impossible to fully commit the Oral Law to writing — 'of making many books there is no end'
D. They were considered a lower category of law not worthy of the Torah scroll
Correct: C — Rava interprets Ecclesiastes 12:12: 'of making many books there is no end' means the Oral Torah's infinite nature makes full written recording impossible — it must remain oral.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What did Rabbi Akiva insist on doing even when the water brought to prison was barely enough to drink?
A. Reciting the full Grace after Meals with all blessings
B. Performing the complete morning prayer service while standing
C. Making an eruv to permit his prison cell for carrying
D. Washing his hands ritually before eating bread
Correct: D — Rabbi Akiva refused to eat without washing his hands, saying: 'I would rather die of thirst than transgress the words of my colleagues' — showing supreme dedication to rabbinic enactment.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What happened when King Solomon instituted the ordinances of eruv of courtyards and ritual handwashing?
A. The Sanhedrin formally ratified the enactments by majority vote
B. A Divine Voice emerged declaring: 'My son, if your heart is wise, my heart also rejoices'
C. The other Sages disputed his authority to enact new laws for three years
D. God appeared to Solomon in a prophetic vision to confirm the laws
Correct: B — Rav Yehuda said in Shmuel's name: when Solomon instituted eruv and handwashing, a Divine Voice emerged quoting Proverbs 23:15 — 'My son, if your heart is wise, my heart also rejoices' — divine validation of his rabbinic enactments.
