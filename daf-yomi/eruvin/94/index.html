🏚️ Collapsed Walls & Carrying Rights: Rav vs. Shmuel

Shmuel
If a wall between two courtyards collapsed on Shabbat, each side may carry to the base of the former partition — since it was permitted at the start of Shabbat, it remains permitted throughout.
Rav
I hold it is prohibited to carry there — but since it is Shmuel's jurisdiction, I will not say so explicitly. I will merely turn my face away to show my displeasure.
Shmuel
If Abba (Rav) is particular about this, take his belt and tie it to the cloak and hang it in the breach! — though only for privacy, not to create a halachic partition.
Rav
I turned my face so that people would not say I hold in accordance with Shmuel's lenient ruling. One must distinguish between respecting local authority and appearing to agree.
Inferred Ruling Rav's opinion was never stated explicitly — it was inferred by his students from his behavior. הֶיתֵּר בִּתְחִלַּת שַׁבָּת — permission existing at Shabbat's start persists throughout.

⚖️ Mishna Dispute: Courtyard Breached into Reshut Harabim (>10 Cubits)

Rabbi Eliezer
The breached courtyard becomes public domain. One who carries from inside to the public domain is liable. The sides of reshut harabim share its status!
Rabbis
The courtyard becomes a karmelit — neither public nor private. One who carries there is exempt (though rabbinically forbidden). Sides of reshut harabim are NOT reshut harabim.
Rabbi Eliezer
Proof from the baraita: when the multitudes select a path in a field, they acquire it as public domain. So too, a breach into which the public enters becomes public.
Rabbis
That only applies when the public misplaced an existing path. And even then — the courtyard wall adjacent to the breach is not the same as the open road!
Gemara's Resolution The Tanna'im disagree about tzidei reshut harabim — whether the sides/edges of a public domain share its full legal status. Rabbi Eliezer: yes. Rabbis: no.

🏗️ Rav vs. Shmuel: Why Is a Two-Sided Breach Different?

Rav's Explanation

  • Mishna: breach is ≤10 cubits but at a corner
  • A corner cannot serve as an entrance — ein osin pischei zaviyot
  • Therefore prohibited even though each side is small
  • For a house: roof was slanted/inclined at the corner — cannot apply pi tikra yored v'sotem
  • Pi tikra does work for Rav when the roof is level and straight

Shmuel's Explanation

  • Mishna: breach is wider than 10 cubits — clearly a breach, not an entrance
  • Requires two sides because it is a house: one side alone would be offset by pi tikra
  • For a courtyard, even one breached side >10 cubits would be prohibited
  • Pi tikra does not apply when all four walls are formed by the roof edge
  • But pi tikra does apply when only some sides rely on it

🏛️ Pi Tikra Yored v'Sotem

עירובין צד: — פִּי תִּקְרָה יוֹרֵד וְסוֹתֵם
פִּי תִּקְרָה יוֹרֵד וְסוֹתֵם "The edge of the roof descends and seals" — when a roofed structure is open on one or more sides, we imagine the roof-edge extending downward as a virtual wall, sealing the opening and preserving the private domain.

Rav: Applies this principle → one may carry in the entire portico as if it were fully enclosed.

Shmuel: Does NOT apply when all four sides are open → may only carry within 4 amot (karmelit rules).

Both agree: if openings are <3 handbreadths → sealed (lavud). If openings are >10 cubits → definitely a breach. They dispute the middle range of 3–10 cubits.
