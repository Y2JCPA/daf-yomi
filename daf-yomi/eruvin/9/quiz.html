Eruvin 9 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
If a cross beam is less than 3 handbreadths from the alleyway wall, what is the ruling?
A. It is invalid and must be replaced with a properly placed beam
B. It is valid due to the principle of lavud — treated as if attached
C. It depends on whether it is drawn away or suspended in air
D. Rav Ashi rules it invalid because both sides must be attached
Correct: B — The principle of lavud treats any gap under 3 handbreadths as if it were closed. A cross beam within 3 handbreadths of the wall is therefore considered attached and halakhically valid.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Abaye, what is the halakhic status of the area between the side posts of an alleyway?
A. Reshut hayachid — a full private domain
B. Reshut harabbim — a public domain
C. Karmelit — carrying is forbidden there
D. Mekom petur — exempt from all domain laws
Correct: C — Abaye holds that the space between side posts is a karmelit. He derives this from Rav's ruling that a doorway between a courtyard and a karmelit requires a side post, implying the entrance inherits karmelit status.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rava explain why an alleyway entrance inherits karmelit status (but not public domain status) from the domain it faces?
A. Public domain is already more stringent and overrides the entrance
B. The area between posts is always a karmelit regardless of what it faces
C. The entrance 'finds its own type' — undefined spaces join with karmelit; public domain has strict formal criteria and does not spread
D. Rava rejects this entirely — neither domain spreads to the entrance
Correct: C — Rava explains that an undefined entrance space 'finds its type' when adjacent to a karmelit — both lack formal domain criteria, so the status spreads. A public domain has strict size requirements and does not spread to adjacent undefined areas.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, what is the status of a side post that is visible (protruding) from the outside but flush with the inside of the alleyway wall?
A. Valid — any protrusion counts as a side post
B. Valid only if it is at least one handbreadth wide
C. Not a valid side post
D. Valid according to Rabbi Yoḥanan, invalid according to Rabbi Ḥiyya
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan rules explicitly that a side post visible only from the outside (protruding outward, flush inside) is NOT a valid side post. Rabbi Ḥiyya disagrees, making this an amoraic dispute. The Gemara concludes they simply hold different views on this principle.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In Ravina's resolution of the breached small courtyard problem, why does lavud not apply to close the breach?
A. The total length of the breach exceeds 10 cubits, overriding lavud
B. Lavud never applies to walls, only to beams and posts
C. The small courtyard walls are 4 handbreadths from one side of the large courtyard — exceeding the 3-handbreadth limit for lavud
D. Rabbi Zeira rules that protruding walls can never be joined by lavud
Correct: C — Ravina specifies that the small courtyard walls are 2 handbreadths from one wall of the large courtyard but 4 handbreadths from the other. Since 4 handbreadths exceeds the 3-handbreadth threshold for lavud, lavud cannot apply from both sides — leaving an open question about applying it from only one side.
