📏 Central Question: Does Techum Apply Above Ten Handbreadths?

One travels above 10 handbreadths from the ground — e.g., boat far above the riverbed, or leaping through the air
↓
Techum APPLIES above 10 tefachim
Must have been within 2000 cubits of destination before Shabbat; boat passengers remain restricted by their pre-Shabbat position
Techum does NOT apply above 10 tefachim
Elijah could travel freely through the air; nazirite also restricted on Sundays; boat passengers unrestricted by techum
↓
Gemara leaves question unresolved — rules stringently in practice; Elijah's travel is rejected as proof (miracles differ from halakha)

🚢 Do Tanna'im Disagree About a Moving Boat?

Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
The tanna'im do NOT disagree about a moving boat. When Rabbi Yehoshua and Rabbi Akiva 'sought to be stringent,' they went beyond the letter of the law voluntarily — proving all agree one is permitted on a moving boat.
Rav Aḥa son of Rava
But Rav and Shmuel explicitly ruled the halakha follows Rabban Gamliel regarding a boat — if they had to decide, it implies the tanna'im DID in fact disagree about the boat!
Rav Ashi
Correct — they disagree, but only about a STATIONARY boat. The phrase 'sought to be stringent' applies to a moving boat, where all agree. A stationary boat is disputed between Rabban Gamliel and Rabbi Yehoshua and Rabbi Akiva.
Resolution Moving boat: all agree one may walk 4 cubits freely within it. Stationary boat: disputed — Rabban Gamliel permits, Rabbi Yehoshua and Rabbi Akiva restrict.

⚗️ Nazirite Vow: 'On the Day the Messiah Comes' — by Day of Week

Shabbat & Yom Tov

  • ✅ Permitted to drink wine
  • Elijah promised not to come on Shabbat eve — too much trouble for preparations
  • If Elijah cannot come on Shabbat eve, Messiah cannot arrive on Shabbat
  • Nazirite vow cannot take effect → wine permitted

Weekdays

  • ❌ Prohibited to drink wine
  • Elijah may already have come unannounced the previous day
  • Messiah could arrive at any moment on a weekday
  • Stringent: treat vow as potentially active

Sunday (the dilemma case)

  • ⚠️ Should be permitted — Elijah cannot come Shabbat, so Messiah cannot come Sunday
  • Yet tanna ruled stringently on Sunday too
  • Reason: uncertain if techum applies above 10 tefachim
  • If techum does NOT apply, Elijah could travel on Shabbat → Messiah on Sunday is possible

🧍 Nehemya Beyond the Techum — Human Partition Rescue

The problem: Nehemya son of Rav Ḥanilai was so absorbed in his Torah learning that he walked beyond his Shabbat limit without noticing.
↓
Rav Ḥisda alerts Rav Naḥman: 'Your student Nehemya is in distress — he is outside the Shabbat limit and cannot return!'
↓
Rav Naḥman's ruling: 'Establish a human partition (mechitza) for him' — people who established an eiruv covering that area line up in two parallel rows.
↓
The mechanism: The two rows of people form valid halachic walls on either side, creating a corridor through which Nehemya may walk — retracing his steps back within the Shabbat limit.
↓
Key requirement: Those forming the partition must themselves be halachically permitted to stand at that location — they must have an eiruv valid for that spot, or be within their own 2000-cubit limit.

📋 Eruvin 43 — Summary Map

Core dispute on this daf: Does techum apply above 10 handbreadths from the ground?
↓
If yes: Elijah cannot cross from outside on Shabbat → Messiah won't come on Shabbat → nazirite may drink; boats are restricted by pre-Shabbat position
If no: Elijah travels freely → Messiah could come any day → nazirite must be cautious even on Sunday; boats face fewer techum concerns
↓
Practical rulings: (1) Rav & Shmuel rule like Rabban Gamliel on boats; (2) Nehemya rescued via human partition; (3) Rabban Gamliel's tube verified techum at sea; (4) Elijah's miraculous travel rejected as proof — miracles differ from halakha
