QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is Rav Mesharshiya's conclusion about halakhic rules of thumb (e.g., 'halakha follows Rabbi Yehuda over Rabbi Meir')?
A. They are universally binding and always determine the halakha without explicit rulings
B. They were never universally accepted — explicit Amoraic rulings are always required
C. They apply only to Babylonian traditions, not to rulings from Eretz Yisrael
D. They apply only when the dispute involves exactly two Tannaim, not three or more
Correct: B — Rav Mesharshiya argues that these principles were not universally accepted by all Sages — some accepted them, others did not. Every case therefore requires an explicit ruling.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda, who may establish an eiruv with their feet (by walking to a spot and declaring it their Shabbat residence)?
A. Only a pauper who genuinely cannot afford bread
B. Only a Torah scholar as an honor
C. Both a pauper and a wealthy person
D. Neither — eiruv must always involve a physical deposit of food
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehuda holds that both pauper and wealthy may use their feet. The bread eiruv was only instituted as a convenience for the wealthy — it is not obligatory.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Yosei permit a priest to leave Eretz Yisrael for Torah study even when a teacher is available in Israel?
A. Torah study categorically overrides all ritual purity prohibitions
B. Rabbi Yosei holds the earth of Eretz Yisrael is no longer considered impure
C. A person cannot always learn effectively from just any teacher — the right match matters
D. The prohibition applies only to priests in the Temple service, not ordinary priests
Correct: C — Rabbi Yosei reasons that a person does not merit to learn from everyone — the specific teacher-student relationship is spiritually and intellectually irreplaceable, and the right teacher may only be found outside Israel.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Rava reverse his initial ruling permitting Meḥoza residents to buy the rams brought to Mavrakhta on Shabbat?
A. The rams turned out not to be owned by gentiles after all
B. Ravina demonstrated that halakha follows Rabbi Yoḥanan, who holds gentile objects DO acquire Shabbat residence
C. The residents of Mavrakhta objected and Rava deferred to local custom
D. Shmuel's ruling was later found to be the authoritative one over Rabbi Yoḥanan
Correct: B — Ravina invoked the principle that halakha follows Rabbi Yoḥanan over Shmuel in disputes. Rabbi Yoḥanan holds gentile objects acquire Shabbat residence, meaning the rams were limited to Mavrakhta and could not be moved to Meḥoza.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the debate about women's waiting periods, what is Rabbi Yosei's unique ruling?
A. All women must wait a full three months before any new legal connection with a man
B. Widows are exempt from waiting; only divorcées must wait three months
C. All women may be betrothed immediately — the decree applies only to marriage; widows additionally wait due to mourning
D. Women betrothed in Judea are fully exempt because relations are presumed to have occurred
Correct: C — Rabbi Yosei holds all categories of women may be betrothed immediately because the three-month decree targets marriage (to identify a potential pregnancy), not betrothal. Widows have an additional wait due to the separate obligation of mourning their deceased husbands.
