Eruvin 23 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Akiva, what is required to permit carrying in a garden of exactly two beit se'a?
A. A watch-house, storehouse, or shed must be present
B. A water source such as a cistern or ditch must be present
C. No special feature — area within two beit se'a is sufficient
D. It must have been originally enclosed for residential purposes
Correct: C — Rabbi Akiva holds that no dwelling feature is required. The sole criterion is that the area not exceed two beit se'a — he is the most lenient of the disputants.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
From which biblical source does the Gemara derive that two beit se'a is the limit for carrying in an outdoor enclosure?
A. The dimensions of the Tabernacle structure itself (30×10 cubits)
B. The courtyard of the Tabernacle — 50×100 cubits (Exodus 27:18)
C. The Temple Mount outer courtyard dimensions
D. The standard field measurement in the book of Leviticus
Correct: B — Rav Yehuda derives from the verse describing the Tabernacle courtyard (50×100 = 5,000 square cubits) that this equals two beit se'a — the outer limit for carrying in an un-residential enclosure.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yosei (whose opinion is the halakha), when is carrying prohibited due to the shape of a garden?
A. When the length exceeds the breadth by even a single cubit
B. When the length is more than double the breadth
C. When the enclosure is not perfectly square
D. Shape is irrelevant to Rabbi Yosei — only area matters
Correct: B — Rabbi Yosei permits carrying even when length exactly equals double the breadth. Only when length exceeds that 2:1 ratio is carrying prohibited — making him more lenient than Rabbi Eliezer.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In a karpef larger than two beit se'a, what is the effect of having the greater part planted with trees?
A. Carrying is prohibited — trees indicate non-residential use
B. Carrying is permitted — trees do not nullify dwelling status
C. Carrying is permitted only if there is also a structure present
D. Carrying is prohibited unless the karpef was originally enclosed for dwelling
Correct: B — The Gemara rules that trees (unlike sown crops) preserve the dwelling character of the karpef — an orchard is considered analogous to a courtyard, so carrying remains permitted.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Elai report his three rulings from Rabbi Eliezer as uncertain or uncorroborated?
A. He could not locate a written Mishnaic source for any of them
B. He sought confirmation from other disciples of Rabbi Eliezer but found none
C. Rabbi Eliezer later publicly retracted all three positions
D. The rulings were rejected by the majority of Sages and deemed non-normative
Correct: B — Rabbi Elai personally circulated among all of Rabbi Eliezer's disciples seeking corroboration for these three unusual rulings, but not a single disciple had heard them from Rabbi Eliezer — leaving them without support.
