💬 What Is Required to Carry in a Garden? — The Core Dispute

R. Yehuda ben Bava
Carrying is permitted in a garden ≤ two beit se'a — but only if it contains a dwelling feature: a watch-house, storehouse, or shed.
R. Yehuda bar Ilai
Even a water cistern, elongated ditch, or cave suffices — water bestows upon the space the status of a dwelling.
R. Akiva
No dwelling feature is required at all. Carrying is permitted so long as the area does not exceed two beit se'a — size alone determines permissibility.
R. Eliezer
Shape matters too: if the length exceeds the breadth by even one cubit beyond the ratio of 2:1, carrying is forbidden — even if the total area is within two beit se'a.
Halakha (Shmuel via Rav Yehuda) The halakha follows both R. Akiva (no dwelling feature required) and R. Yosei (length up to double the breadth is permitted). Both rulings needed to be stated separately.

📏 Three Opinions on Shape — Length vs. Breadth

Rabbi Eliezer (Strictest)

  • Length > breadth by even 1 cubit → prohibited
  • Any elongation beyond a square disqualifies
  • Calculates using the diagonal (squaring method)
  • Baraita clarifies: means length > double breadth

Rabbi Yosei (Middle)

  • Length = double breadth → still permitted
  • Only prohibited when length exceeds double breadth
  • Halakha follows R. Yosei (per Shmuel)
  • Difference with R. Eliezer: the diagonal calculation

Rabbi Akiva (Most Lenient)

  • No explicit shape restriction stated
  • Only restriction: total area ≤ two beit se'a
  • Halakha also follows R. Akiva (per Shmuel)
  • Practical difference with first tanna: a tiny fraction

🏛️ Deriving Two Beit Se'a from the Tabernacle Courtyard

Question: From where is the two beit se'a limit derived? What connects Shabbat carrying to the Tabernacle?
↓
Verse (Exodus 27:18): "The length of the courtyard shall be one hundred cubits, and the breadth fifty by fifty, and the height five cubits."
↓
Rav Yehuda: The phrase 'fifty by fifty' teaches us to square the courtyard — 50×100 = 5,000 square cubits = two beit se'a.
↓
Abaye explains the plain meaning: The Tabernacle itself was 30×10 cubits. The verse directs us to complete a 50×50 square around it — deriving the standard for outdoor spaces.
↓
Principle: The Tabernacle courtyard is the paradigmatic functional outdoor enclosure. Carrying is forbidden in any similar space that exceeds its area.

📜 Rabbi Elai's Three Lone Traditions from Rabbi Eliezer

גִּלְגַּלְתִּי עַל כָּל תַּלְמִידָיו וְלֹא מָצָאתִי לוֹ חָבֵר — I circulated among all his disciples and found no corroboration
שָׁמַעְתִּי מֵרַבִּי אֱלִיעֶזֶר — I heard from Rabbi Eliezer Rabbi Elai reported three rulings he heard only from Rabbi Eliezer himself, which no other disciple could corroborate: 1. 🌿 Karpef size: One may carry in a garden or karpef even if it is a beit kor — thirty times larger than two beit se'a. 2. 🏠 Eiruv cession on Shabbat: A resident who forgot to join the eiruv and then ceded his share in the courtyard on Shabbat itself — the cession is legally effective. 3. 🌱 Maror on Passover: One may fulfill the obligation to eat bitter herbs with arkablin (a specific plant). Rabbi Elai circulated among all of Rabbi Eliezer's disciples and found no one who had heard these rulings — leaving them uncorroborated.
