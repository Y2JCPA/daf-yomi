⚖️ Abaye vs. Rav Yosef: Minimum Alleyway Length

Abaye
An alleyway must be at least 4 cubits long to be rendered permitted by a cross beam or side post. Proof: a baraita requires houses opening into courtyards opening into the alleyway — impossible in less than 4 cubits.
Rav Yosef
An alleyway of even 4 handbreadths is sufficient. The baraita can be satisfied when courtyards open into the alleyway at its corners, allowing minimal length while still meeting the structural requirement.
Stakes This dispute determines whether extremely short alleyways can be rendered permitted domains for carrying on Shabbat via cross beam (קורה) or side post (לחי).

🪵 Three Understandings of the Cross Beam (קורה)

Position 1: Seals Below

  • Cross beam prohibits use of area beneath it
  • Treated as if alleyway is sealed from its inner edge
  • Area below beam = forbidden zone
  • Requires 4 handbreadths minimum width to function

Position 2: Conspicuous Marker

  • Cross beam serves as a siman — a visible boundary marker
  • Marks the entrance threshold of reshut hayachid
  • Area beneath beam is permitted for use
  • Debate: does a marker below equal one above?

Position 3: Lower = Upper

  • Halakha of lower marker follows upper marker
  • Ground-level and elevated markers unified in law
  • No distinction between siman above vs. below
  • Fully supports permitting the area beneath the beam

⛏️ Hollowing Ground Under a Low Cross Beam

Cross beam is positioned less than 10 handbreadths above the ground — too low to function halachically
↓
One hollows out the ground beneath the beam to achieve the required 10-handbreadth clearance
↓
Is Hollowing Valid?
Yes — artificially completing the 10-tefach height is valid; beam now functions as a proper korah
Analogous Case (Rav Ami/Asi)
Breach in alleyway side wall: if < 3 tefachim, principle of lavud applies — treated as closed
When It Fails
Breach 3–10 cubits: needs upright board (צורת הפתח). Breach >10 cubits: alleyway is invalidated entirely

📏 Abaye's Two Proofs for the 4-Cubit Minimum

Proof 1 — From a Baraita: An alleyway is only permitted by side post or cross beam if it has houses opening into courtyards opening into the alleyway. This nested structure physically requires at least 4 cubits of length.
↓
Rav Yosef's Rebuttal: The baraita works even with 4 handbreadths — if the courtyards open into the alleyway at its corners, two 4-handbreadth openings can coexist in a minimal-length alleyway.
↓
Proof 2 — From Rami bar Ḥama: A side post (לחי) protruding 4 cubits into an alleyway is itself treated as a new alleyway requiring its own side post. This shows 4 cubits is a significant threshold — the minimum length constituting a full alleyway.
↓
Rav Yosef's Rebuttal: The thresholds are asymmetric. Removing a lechi's status requires 4 cubits of protrusion, but establishing a new alleyway requires only 4 handbreadths. Different functions, different measures.
↓
Standing Dispute: Abaye requires 4 cubits (אמות); Rav Yosef holds 4 handbreadths (טפחים) suffice — the proofs have been answered and the disagreement remains unresolved.

📐 Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua vs. Rav Ashi: The 8-Cubit Threshold

Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua
The 4-cubit protrusion rule applies only when the alleyway is at least 8 cubits wide. Derived via kal vachomer: just as a courtyard (which requires full partitions, not merely a lechi) still needs 4 cubits of valid space, an alleyway must have 4 cubits on each side of the protrusion — totaling 8 cubits minimum width.
Rav Ashi
Even an alleyway exactly 8 cubits wide is subject to the rule — and yet still valid without an extra lechi. When the protrusion leaves exactly 4 cubits open (standing = breach), this is a rabbinic doubt. Since carrying in an alleyway is only a rabbinic restriction, we rule leniently.
Core Principle — ספק דרבנן לקולא In matters of rabbinic law, doubt is resolved leniently. When the 'standing' portion exactly equals the 'breach,' we do not require an additional correction.
