QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara's conclusion, what is the primary function of a cross beam (קורה) spanning an alleyway entrance?
A. It physically seals the alleyway, prohibiting use of the area directly beneath it
B. It serves as a conspicuous marker (סימן) indicating the boundary of the domain
C. It acts as an actual partition (מחיצה) equivalent to a full wall
D. It elevates the alleyway to the full status of a Torah-level reshut hayachid
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that a cross beam functions as a conspicuous marker (סימן), not a physical seal. The area beneath it is permitted; it simply marks the boundary of the private domain so that residents are mindful not to carry beyond it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Abaye's minimum required length for an alleyway to be rendered permitted by a side post or cross beam?
A. 4 handbreadths (טפחים)
B. 10 handbreadths (טפחים)
C. 4 cubits (אמות)
D. 10 cubits (אמות)
Correct: C — Abaye requires a minimum of 4 cubits, proven from a baraita that requires the alleyway to have houses opening into courtyards opening into it — a structure that demands at least 4 cubits — and from Rami bar Ḥama's ruling about the 4-cubit side post.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If a side post protrudes exactly 4 cubits into an alleyway, what is the halachic consequence according to Rami bar Ḥama (citing Rav Huna)?
A. The side post is entirely invalid and the alleyway is forbidden for carrying
B. The side post remains valid and no additional post is needed
C. The protruding section becomes a new alleyway requiring its own additional side post
D. The alleyway must be sealed with a full tzurat hapetach on both ends
Correct: C — Rami bar Ḥama rules that a 4-cubit protrusion transforms that section into a new interior alleyway. This new alleyway requires its own lechi to be permitted for carrying on Shabbat.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rav Yosef respond to Abaye's proof from the baraita requiring 'houses opening into courtyards opening into the alleyway'?
A. He rejects the baraita as non-authoritative for practical halacha
B. He agrees the baraita proves 4 cubits but limits it to Torah-level alleyways
C. He explains the baraita works even with 4 handbreadths if courtyards open at the alleyway's corners
D. He rules that only one courtyard is needed, not two, so 4 handbreadths suffice
Correct: C — Rav Yosef answers that if two courtyards open into the alleyway at its corners, two 4-handbreadth openings can fit within a minimal-length alleyway, satisfying the baraita's requirement without needing 4 full cubits.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rav Ashi rules that even when a 4-cubit protruding lechi leaves exactly 4 cubits of space on the other side (standing = breach), the alleyway is still valid without an extra lechi. What principle underlies this?
A. The principle of lavud — gaps under 3 tefachim are treated as closed
B. Doubt in rabbinic law is resolved leniently (ספק דרבנן לקולא)
C. A cross beam alone always suffices to render an alleyway permitted
D. Rav Ashi holds the 4-cubit protrusion rule never applies in practice
Correct: B — Rav Ashi applies the rule that in matters of rabbinic law (d'rabbanan), uncertainty is resolved leniently. Since carrying in an alleyway is only rabbinically restricted, a doubtful case where standing equals breach defaults to permitting without requiring an additional side post.
