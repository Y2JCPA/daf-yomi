🏗️ Rabba vs. Rava: What Validly Reduces an Oversized Karpef?

Rabba
A column 10 handbreadths high and 4 wide IS an effective reduction — it is large enough to be excluded from lavud (the principle merging gaps under 3 handbreadths), and thus stands as its own partition.
Rava
A partition erected less than 4 handbreadths from the karpef wall is NOT effective — it lacks the minimum size for its own domain and is subsumed into the existing wall via lavud.
Rabba
Even wet plaster that stands for now is valid — כיון דקאי השתא קאי (since it currently stands, it legally stands).
Rava
Wet plaster that cannot stand independently is לאו כלום הוא — it is nothing. A partition must be self-supporting to carry legal weight.
Core Question Which structural additions genuinely reduce a karpef to ≤2 beit se'a? The debates pivot on permanence, independence, and the boundary principles of lavud.

🧱 Partition on Top of Partition — Rav Ḥisda vs. Rav Sheshet

Rav Ḥisda
A partition erected atop existing walls IS effective for Shabbat. One who dwells between the upper partitions actively uses them — they serve a real purpose and are legally recognized.
Rav Sheshet
Even for Shabbat, partition-on-partition is NOT effective. The area between the outer lower walls and the inner upper partition remains prohibited for carrying.
Rav Ḥisda
Even Rav Sheshet concedes: if the partition is erected on the mound itself — not merely adjacent — carrying is permitted on the mound, since the upper partition directly serves the person dwelling there.
Gemara
Conclude: It was Rav Ḥisda who holds partition-on-partition is effective — demonstrated from the case of acquiring a convert's ownerless property through such an action.
Principle Does a partition built atop existing walls function independently, or is it halachically swallowed by the lower structure below?

🌿 Acquiring a Deceased Convert's Ownerless Property

✅ Valid Acquisition

  • Erect a partition on the mound → acquires enclosed area
  • Plow the ground, even slightly → acquires that plot
  • Upper partitions still standing after lower walls sank → valid acquisition (per Rav Ḥisda)
  • Any act that improves the land at the moment of action confers ownership

❌ Invalid Acquisition

  • Sowing seeds = NOT valid — land is unimproved at moment of sowing
  • Turnips improve land only when they grow — improvement is independent, not caused by the person
  • Partition-on-partition (per Rav Sheshet) = ineffective, no acquisition
  • Act that does not demonstrably improve the land at the moment of performance = no ownership

🚪 Karpef Fully Breached into a Courtyard

חצר מותר לטלטל בה וכרפף אסור לטלטל בו
Rav Ḥisda's Ruling When a karpef (larger than 2 beit se'a, not enclosed for residence) is fully breached into a courtyard, the space of the fallen walls is counted as part of the karpef's area — pushing it further over the 2 beit se'a limit.

Result: Carrying in the karpef is prohibited; carrying in the courtyard remains permitted (due to the remnants of its own walls forming a valid entrance).

Rabbi Zeira agreed with Rava: a space already permitted (the courtyard) does not render the karpef permitted — rather the karpef's excess area nullifies its own permissibility.
