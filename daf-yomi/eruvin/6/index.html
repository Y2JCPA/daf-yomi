🧱 Core Dispute: Breach Rules in Alleyway Walls

Rav Ḥanin bar Rava
Side breach: forbidden only if ≥10 cubits (smaller counts as an entrance). Front breach: any breach wider than 4 handbreadths forbids carrying.
Rav Huna
No distinction between side and front — in both cases only a breach of up to 4 handbreadths is permitted. Do not dispute me on this ruling!
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak's Support Rav Huna's view is supported by the L-shaped alleyway dispute between Rav and Shmuel — Rav's ruling proves that even a small opening can annul partitions.

⚖️ Can a Public Domain Be Rendered Fit by Eiruv?

Tanna Kamma (Baraita)
A public thoroughfare is rendered fit for carrying by constructing a form of a doorway on one side and a side post or cross beam on the other.
Rabbi Yehuda
A public domain cannot be rendered fit by side posts or cross beams alone — full doors that can be locked are required on both sides.
Ulla's Testimony from Meḥoza The city entrances of Meḥoza qualify as a public domain. Were it not for their doors being locked at night, residents would be liable for carrying in them on Shabbat.

🏗️ Three Positions on Rendering a Thoroughfare Fit

Tanna Kamma / Rav

  • Form of a doorway (צורת הפתח) on one side
  • Side post or cross beam on the other
  • Sufficient for alleyways open on both ends
  • Rav rules this is the operative halakha

Ḥananya / Beit Hillel / Shmuel

  • Form of a doorway required on both sides
  • Door must be functional (able to be locked)
  • Whether locking is required is debated
  • Shmuel rules the halakha follows Ḥananya

Rabbi Yehuda / Beit Shammai

  • Full doors required on both sides
  • Doors must actually be locked
  • True public domain cannot be fixed with mere posts
  • Most stringent position — not followed in practice

📢 The Divine Voice and the Ruling of Beit Hillel

Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel disputed laws of Shabbat, purity, and eiruv for many years — both schools considered their views authoritative
↓
A baraita states: "The halakha is always in accordance with Beit Hillel"
↓
The same baraita also says: "One who wishes to act like Beit Shammai may do so; one who wishes to act like Beit Hillel may do so"
↓
Contradiction! If halakha always follows Beit Hillel, why may one choose either school?
↓
Resolution 1: The permissive clause preceded the Divine Voice (בת קול) that declared the halakha follows Beit Hillel. After the Voice, one must follow Beit Hillel.
↓
Resolution 2: Both clauses came after the Voice — adopting Beit Shammai's leniencies makes one wicked; adopting Beit Hillel's leniencies makes one foolish (taking the easy path in each case).
