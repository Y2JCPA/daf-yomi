QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Berurya, what does 'ordered in all things and secure' (II Samuel 23:5) teach about Torah?
A. One must study Torah in a fixed sequential order from beginning to end
B. If Torah is ordered in all 248 limbs — studied with one's whole body — it will be retained securely
C. Torah study is only secure when performed in a group of at least 248 scholars
D. The 248 positive commandments ensure the security of the Jewish people
Correct: B — Berurya rebuked her student by explaining that the verse means Torah must be internalized through the entire body — studying aloud with full physical engagement — in order to be retained securely.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In Rava's allegory on Numbers 21:18, what does the stage of 'Nahaliel' represent?
A. The moment of haughtiness when one is cast into the valley
B. The desert wandering before Torah was given
C. God grants the student an inheritance after Torah is received as a gift
D. The teacher (El) who delivers the Torah to the student
Correct: C — Rava explained: after Torah is given as a gift (Mattanah), one inherits it — neḥalo (his inheritance) + El (God) — meaning God grants him the inheritance. This follows naturally from humble receptivity.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How many times in total did Aaron hear Moses' Torah lesson in the transmission chain?
A. Once — he entered alone and Moses taught him
B. Twice — once alone, once when his sons entered
C. Three times — alone, with sons, and with elders
D. Four times — alone, with sons, with elders, and when all the people entered
Correct: D — Aaron heard Moses' lesson four times: when he entered alone, when his sons entered, when the elders entered, and when all the people entered. This is the source for Rabbi Eliezer's ruling that a teacher must repeat lessons four times.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Ḥiyya bar Abba in the name of Rabbi Yoḥanan compare Torah to a fig tree?
A. Both are sweet and were both given in the wilderness
B. Figs must be preserved carefully just as Torah knowledge must be guarded
C. Figs do not all ripen at once, so one always finds fresh fruit — similarly Torah always yields new insight
D. The fig tree is the tallest of trees, representing Torah's supreme status
Correct: C — Just as figs on a tree don't ripen simultaneously, so a person searching the tree always finds a fresh fig. Similarly, Torah always offers fresh, new insight whenever one meditates upon it — the devoted student is never disappointed.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov derive by reading ḥarut (engraved) as ḥeirut (freedom)?
A. Torah study liberates one from the obligation of physical labor
B. Had the first tablets not been broken, no nation would ever have ruled over Israel
C. Moses was personally freed from his limitations when he received the Torah
D. The written Torah was given freely without any preconditions
Correct: B — Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov taught that the word ḥarut (engraved) on the tablets contains the hidden word ḥeirut (freedom) — signaling that had those first tablets remained intact, Israel would have enjoyed eternal freedom from foreign domination.
