Eruvin 63 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Rav Hamnuna ruled in Ḥarta De'argez during Rav Ḥisda's lifetime. Why was this ultimately deemed acceptable?
A. Rav Ḥisda had given him explicit permission to rule there
B. They were located in different towns, which makes it permissible
C. Rav Hamnuna was ruling to prevent a prohibition
D. Rav Hamnuna was Rav Ḥisda's equal, not his student
Correct: B — The Gemara ultimately resolves that during the lifetime of Rav Huna (Rav Hamnuna's principal teacher), he did not rule; during Rav Ḥisda's years, being in a different town made it permissible.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava, in what circumstance may a disciple issue a ruling even in his teacher's actual presence?
A. When the teacher is elderly and cannot respond quickly
B. When the disciple is clearly more knowledgeable on that topic
C. In order to separate someone from a prohibition they are about to commit
D. When the ruling concerns a rabbinic law rather than a Torah law
Correct: C — Rava rules that preventing another person from violating a prohibition permits the disciple to rule even in the teacher's presence — 'there is no wisdom or understanding against the Lord' (Proverbs 21:30).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Abba bar Pappa, why specifically was Joshua punished with childlessness?
A. For issuing a halakhic ruling in Moses's presence
B. For preventing the Jewish people from fulfilling the commandment of procreation
C. For neglecting the evening Tamid offering before the battle of Jericho
D. For not consulting the Urim V'Tumim before the military campaign
Correct: B — Rabbi Abba bar Pappa attributes Joshua's childlessness to preventing procreation — because the Ark was not at rest, the nation was forbidden marital relations, and Joshua's conduct prolonged this.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What solution did Abaye offer when many Jews lived alongside a gentile who refused to rent his domain for an eiruv?
A. They were forbidden to carry at all until the gentile moved out
B. They should appoint an agent to acquire the gentile's domain by subterfuge
C. All residents should renounce their domains in favor of one individual
D. The eiruv was valid without any participation from the gentile
Correct: C — Abaye ruled that mass renunciation in favor of one individual is itself uncommon, and the Sages did not issue preventive decrees for uncommon situations — so that one person may carry legally.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does the angel's rebuke of Joshua ('I am now come') teach, according to Rav as cited by Rabbi Shmuel bar Inya?
A. A military commander must ensure all offerings are brought before battle
B. Torah study is greater than the offering of the daily sacrifices
C. The Divine Presence departs when the Ark is displaced from its resting place
D. A leader bears collective responsibility for his nation's transgressions
Correct: B — The angel came 'now' — on account of the neglect of Torah study specifically. This teaches that Torah study takes precedence over the Tamid offerings; the angel came for the Torah sin, not the sacrificial one.
