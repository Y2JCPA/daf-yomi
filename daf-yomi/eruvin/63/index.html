⚖️ The Scholar's Dilemma: Ruling in Teacher's Territory

Rav Ashi
Why did you examine that knife here in Babylonia while I, your teacher, also live in Babylonia?
Ravina
Did not Rav Hamnuna rule in Ḥarta De'argez during Rav Ḥisda's lifetime? Different towns permit it — as are we.
Ravina
Moreover, I acted to prevent a prohibition — even in one's teacher's actual presence, this is permitted!
Rav Ashi
'There is no wisdom or understanding against the Lord' (Proverbs 21:30) — preventing sin permits even this.
Core Rule A disciple may NOT rule in his teacher's jurisdiction — but preventing another's sin overrides this, even in the teacher's actual presence.

🔪 When May a Scholar Examine a Slaughtering Knife?

✅ Permitted

  • A Torah scholar may examine a knife for himself (Rava)
  • Examining on behalf of a host who slaughters for you — you are the beneficiary
  • Ruling in a different town from your teacher (Rav Hamnuna / Ḥarta De'argez)
  • Ruling in order to prevent someone from violating a prohibition (Rava)

❌ Prohibited

  • Ruling in teacher's actual presence without justification
  • Ruling in teacher's town even without teacher being present
  • Examining knife 'as a passerby' — Ravina said this does not count as acting for himself
  • Issuing rulings without intent to prevent a sin, in teacher's domain

📖 Joshua's Three Sins and His Punishment

Sin 1 — Neglected the Evening Tamid: Due to war preparations before Jericho, the daily evening offering was not brought
↓
Sin 2 — Neglected Torah Study: That same night Joshua failed to learn Torah; the angel appeared with drawn sword specifically for this sin
↓
Sin 3 — Prevented Procreation: While the Ark was not at rest, the entire nation was forbidden marital relations; Joshua prolonged this state through the conquest
↓
Torah > Sacrifices: 'I am now come' — the angel came on account of the neglect of Torah study, teaching that Torah study is greater than offering the daily sacrifices (Rav, via R. Shmuel bar Inya)
↓
Punishment — Childlessness: 'Non his son, Joshua his son' (1 Chronicles 7:27) — implying Joshua himself had no children of his own

🏠 Eiruv Problem: Gentile Neighbor Refuses to Rent His Domain

Jewish Neighbors
Laḥman bar Ristak the gentile lives in our alleyway and refuses to rent us his domain. How can we carry on Shabbat?
Abaye
All of you renounce your rights to the alleyway in favor of one person — that one person lives 'alone' with the gentile and may carry.
Jewish Neighbors
But the rule allowing one Jew with a gentile applies because it's uncommon. Here many of us live with him — the decree should apply!
Abaye
Renunciation of many domains in favor of one individual is itself uncommon — for uncommon occurrences, the Sages never issued a decree.
Principle For uncommon occurrences (מילתא דלא שכיחא), the Sages did not issue preventive decrees (גזרה).
