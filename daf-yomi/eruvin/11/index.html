🚪 Central Dispute: Does Tzurat HaPetach Override a Wide Opening?

Rav Yehuda (baraita)
If an alleyway entrance wider than ten cubits has a tzurat hapetach, one need not diminish its width — the doorway form suffices.
Rav
He must still diminish! A tzurat hapetach does not permit carrying in an alleyway whose entrance exceeds ten cubits.
Reish Lakish
Just as vines on poles create a valid partition for kilayim, so too they permit carrying on Shabbat — I permit it regardless of the width.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
I agree with Rav. As proven by the incident with Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi: exactly ten cubits is permitted, but more than ten cubits is not.
Resolution The Gemara concludes that Rabbi Yoḥanan agrees with Rav: tzurat hapetach does not help when the alleyway opening exceeds ten cubits in width.

⚖️ Three Opinions on Alleyway Requirements

Beit Shammai

  • Requires both a side post (lechi) and a cross beam (korah)
  • Two separate structural markers needed
  • More stringent — alleyway needs stronger demarcation

Beit Hillel

  • Requires either a side post or a cross beam
  • One marker alone suffices to permit carrying
  • Standard halacha follows Beit Hillel

Rabbi Eliezer

  • Requires two side posts — one on each side
  • Gemara debates: does he also require a cross beam?
  • May be more stringent than Beit Shammai, or simply a distinct opinion

🔧 Requirements for a Valid Tzurat HaPetach

Two upright posts — one reed/pole on each side of the opening (even minimal thickness suffices)
↓
Horizontal bar resting on top — even a thin reed placed across the tops of both posts is valid
↓
Bar need not physically touch the posts — lavud principle applies within 3 handbreadths (Rav Naḥman's practice at the Exilarch's house)
↓
Strong enough to hold a door — even a flimsy straw door qualifies; structural capacity is the standard (Rav Ḥisda)
↓
Mark for hinges required in the doorpost — loops (avkata) into which a hinge can be inserted (Reish Lakish in name of Rabbi Yannai)
↓
Placement from the side (horizontal beam beside rather than atop the posts) is invalid for Shabbat — must be truly atop (Rav Ḥisda)

🏛️ Arched Gateway: Rabbi Meir vs. Rabbis

Abaye (framing)
Everyone agrees: if supports are less than 3 handbreadths high, or total arch height is less than 10 handbreadths, the arch is invalid as a tzurat hapetach.
Rabbi Meir
When supports ≥3 handbreadths and arch ≥10 handbreadths but narrows to <4 handbreadths wide at top — we carve it out conceptually. It is valid!
Rabbis (Majority)
We do not apply 'carve it out' here. If the arch is actually narrower than 4 handbreadths at the 10-handbreadth mark, it is invalid — no conceptual carving.
Rav Sheshet
Tell the Exilarch's household nothing about this baraita of the arched gateway — it is proof against the ruling I already gave them!
Key Dimensions Both sides require: supports ≥ 3 handbreadths high, total arch ≥ 10 handbreadths high, and width ≥ 4 handbreadths at the 10-handbreadth height. They dispute only the 'carve it out' principle.
