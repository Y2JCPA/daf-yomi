🔲 Cross Beam: Marker or Partition?

Rav / R. Ḥiyya
The area beneath the cross beam is permitted — the קורה functions as a conspicuous marker signaling separation from the public domain. Residents know not to carry beyond it.
Levi / Stringent View
The area beneath the cross beam is prohibited — the קורה functions as a partition, legally as though it descends to the ground. One may only carry up to its inner edge.
The Core Question Does a קורה (cross beam) serve as a מחיצה (partition) or an היכר (conspicuous marker)? This determines whether the space directly beneath it is permitted or forbidden for carrying on Shabbat.

🏠 Does Backyard Ownership Affect Carrying?

Rav Mesharshiya
When two breaches in the alleyway are not opposite one another, carrying is permitted — but only if the backyard belongs to many people. They are unlikely to agree on adding buildings that would change the configuration.
Single-Owner Case
If the backyard belongs to a single individual, he might decide to add buildings, invalidating the existing arrangement. Carrying in the alleyway is prohibited as a precaution against future changes.
Source (Amud A §6) Derived from Ravin bar Rav Adda citing Rabbi Yitzḥak — the incident of Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's alleyway opening to the sea, where the distinction between communal and private domains was applied.

🐛 The Centipede Alleyway — Abaye vs. Rava

Abaye's Lenient View

  • A main alleyway with side branches = like a crooked (L-shaped) alleyway
  • Follows Shmuel: a crooked alleyway has the status of a closed alleyway
  • A single lechi (side post) at the main entrance suffices for all branches
  • All branching alleyways are covered by the main permit

Rava's Stringent View

  • Each small alleyway must receive its own tzurat hapetaḥ (doorway-shaped opening)
  • One side of the branches gets doorway openings; the other side is permitted by a lechi or cross beam
  • Cannot rely on the main alleyway's permit alone — each branch is independent
  • More stringent: the centipede structure creates distinct, separate domains

📐 The Chain of Rav Kahanas — Diagonal Cross Beam

Question: If a cross beam is placed diagonally across an alleyway entrance (not perpendicular), does it still render carrying within permitted?
↓
Transmission chain: Rav Kahana (teacher of Rav) → Rav Kahana bar Malkiyu → Rav Kahana bar Minyumi → Rav Kahana bar Taḥalifa
↓
Ruling: A diagonal cross beam is valid — it still functions to mark and close the alleyway entrance for purposes of Shabbat.
↓
Rav Kahana's limit: This is only valid when the diagonal span does not exceed the width of the alleyway itself.
↓
Rava explains both views: For the marker opinion — outer edge counts. For the partition opinion — inner edge counts. Either way, the beam must sit within the plane of the alleyway walls.
