QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, why is Torah 'not in heaven' (Deuteronomy 30:12)?
A. Because Torah was given on earth at Sinai, not in heaven
B. Because one who is haughty like the heavens — thinking he needs no teacher — cannot acquire Torah
C. Because even if it were in heaven, God would lower it down for us
D. Because the angels in heaven do not study Torah
Correct: B — Rava interprets the verse homiletically: Torah is not found in one who elevates himself arrogantly like the heavens, believing he is above needing instruction. Humility is the prerequisite for Torah.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
For a bow-shaped city, what is the maximum gap between its two sections that still allows them to be treated as a single city?
A. 141⅓ cubits (two karpefot)
B. 2,000 cubits (one Shabbat limit)
C. 4,000 cubits (two Shabbat limits)
D. 70 cubits (one karpef)
Correct: C — Rav Huna rules that if the gap is less than 4,000 cubits, the imaginary interior is filled in and the city is treated as one unit. At 4,000 cubits or more, they are two separate cities.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What minimum condition converts a hut settlement into a permanent city for Shabbat limit purposes?
A. Ten adult men forming a minyan dwelling there permanently
B. A synagogue with a live-in caretaker at its center
C. Three courtyards each containing at least two properly built houses
D. A surrounding fence or wall of at least ten handbreadths
Correct: C — Rav Ḥinnana bar Rav Kahana citing Rav Ashi: three courtyards with two properly built houses (six houses total) among the huts establish the settlement as permanent, granting the full 2,000-cubit Shabbat limit.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What reason does Ulla give for the harsh statement about marrying daughters of hut-dwellers?
A. They are untrained in Torah and mitzvot and cannot raise a Jewish family
B. They lack bathhouses, so men must walk far from camp — creating opportunity for prohibited conduct
C. They move constantly and cannot establish a stable, permanent household
D. Their poverty makes them unable to fulfill the obligations of a marriage contract
Correct: B — Ulla explains that hut settlements have no bathhouses, forcing men to walk a considerable distance from camp for bodily needs. This physical separation creates opportunity for premarital intimacy — hence the concern about their daughters.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Rav Huna rule that a Torah scholar may not dwell in a city without vegetables?
A. Vegetables are required for the Shabbat table according to halakha
B. A city without vegetables signals poverty that cannot support a scholar's needs
C. Vegetables benefit physical health, and a scholar must maintain his body to sustain learning
D. Lack of vegetables indicates the city has no proper market, making Torah commerce impossible
Correct: C — Rav Huna's ruling is grounded in health: vegetables support physical well-being, which is a prerequisite for sustained Torah study. The body must be cared for in order for the mind to learn.
