📏 Above vs. Below Ten Handbreadths

Above 10 Handbreadths

  • Tree (if 4×4 wide) = private domain (רשות היחיד)
  • Eiruv is not valid — separated from the person
  • Masses rest burdens on horizontal section → forbidden to use
  • Cannot be retrieved without violating Shabbat restrictions

Below 10 Handbreadths

  • Eiruv remains within accessible reach
  • Eiruv is valid — Shabbat residence established
  • Can be retrieved at bein hashmashot (twilight)
  • Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi: rabbinic bans lifted at twilight

🏛️ Ravina vs. Rabbi Yirmeya: Why Is the Basket Valid?

Gemara
This tree — what are its circumstances? If not 4×4 handbreadths, it is an exempt domain and carrying is unrestricted. No proof about public domain can be drawn!
Ravina
The Tosefta deals with a tree that is 4×4 handbreadths. Above 10 = private domain → invalid. Below 10: the basket completes the 4×4 width, yet Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi still permits it.
Gemara
Whose opinion does Ravina follow? If Rabbi Yosei son of Rabbi Yehuda — he holds a basket does complete dimensions to create a private domain. So why is the eiruv below 10 valid?
Rabbi Yirmeya
A basket is different! One can tilt it and bring it to within 10 handbreadths without any carrying violation. That is why Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi permits it — no need for Ravina's reasoning.
Core Question Does the basket's ability to be tilted render it accessible (Rabbi Yirmeya), or is Ravina correct that the basket's position relative to the tree's 4×4 dimensions is the key factor?

🌙 Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's Twilight Principle

Base rule: Anything prohibited only by rabbinic decree (גזירת שבות) is NOT prohibited during twilight (בין השמשות)
↓
Using a tree on Shabbat is only a rabbinic prohibition — the Torah does not explicitly forbid it
↓
Twilight (bein hashmashot) is a period of uncertainty: is it still Shabbat, or already the next day?
↓
Rabbinic stringencies are not imposed upon a time period that may not even be Shabbat — safek protects from rabbinic extension
↓
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi follows Rabbi Meir: curved arch top completes 10 handbreadths, and Rabbi Yehuda: requires 4×4 platform
↓
Conclusion: Basket eiruv below 10 handbreadths is valid — retrievable at twilight when the rabbinic tree-ban does not apply

🎉 Rav Pappa: Festival Falling on Friday

כשחל יום טוב בערב שבת — עירוב תחומין לשניהם
New Challenge Rav bar Shabba raises an objection to Rav Pappa from a mishna: when a Festival (Yom Tov) falls on Friday, one needs an eiruv valid for both the Festival and Shabbat.

The challenge: the eiruv must be accessible at the time it takes effect. If the tree is forbidden on Yom Tov as well, can one ever retrieve the eiruv in time?

This presses against Rabbi Yirmeya's lenient explanation — if tilting alone makes the eiruv valid, what happens when even twilight access is blocked by a Festival restriction?
