Eruvin 105 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Rabbi Yochanan says that R' Shimon ben Nannas and R' Akiva both derive their rulings from the same verse. What is the core of their disagreement?
A. Whether Levites or priests are more sanctified for Temple service
B. Whether the handoff to Levites shows the obligation ended or simply reflects where Levites can serve
C. Whether impurity must be removed before or after Shabbat ends
D. Whether the verse refers to the First or Second Temple
Correct: B — Nannas reads the handoff as proof the obligation is complete; Akiva reads it as logistical — Levites took over in the courtyard only because they couldn't enter the Sanctuary.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
The baraita rules that ideally, Temple repairs should be done by priests. What happens if no priests are available?
A. The repair must be postponed until a priest arrives
B. Only the High Priest (Kohen Gadol) may authorize a non-priest to enter
C. Levites may enter; if no Levites, Israelites may enter
D. Non-priests may enter only if the Sanhedrin approves each case
Correct: C — The baraita establishes a descending hierarchy: priests (unblemished first, then blemished), then Levites, then Israelites — the mitzva is to use whoever is closest to the ideal.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rav Kahana use the word אַךְ ('only') in Leviticus 21:23?
A. To prove blemished priests are completely banned from all Temple areas
B. To show that the prohibition on blemished priests is absolute and has no exceptions
C. As a limiting term — signaling blemished priests may enter when unblemished are unavailable
D. To restrict the prohibition to the Holy of Holies only, not the Sanctuary
Correct: C — Rav Kahana reads אַךְ as an exclusion — it limits the prohibition's scope. While unblemished priests are the ideal, the word signals blemished priests retain the capacity to enter when necessary.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav, when both a ritually impure priest and a blemished priest are available for Temple repairs, who should enter?
A. The blemished priest, since his condition is physical and permanent
B. The impure priest, since impurity has a communal exception (tumah hutrah b'tzibur)
C. Neither — the task must wait for a fully qualified priest
D. Both together, since their combined status equals one valid priest
Correct: B — Rav rules the impure priest enters because ritual impurity carries a public/communal exception — making his disqualification situationally less severe than a permanent physical blemish.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rabbi Shimon's principle that 'the Sages permitted only rabbinic prohibitions' is demonstrated by the laws of knots on Shabbat. What is the ruling?
A. All knots are permitted on Shabbat in an eruv context
B. Forming a bow is permitted (rabbinic only); a full knot is not (Torah violation)
C. A full knot is permitted but only if tied before Shabbat begins
D. Levites may tie any knot in the Temple regardless of Shabbat
Correct: B — A bow cannot constitute Torah-prohibited 'tying,' so the Sages may permit it. A full knot is a Torah violation — and Rabbi Shimon's principle holds that rabbinic leniency cannot extend there.
