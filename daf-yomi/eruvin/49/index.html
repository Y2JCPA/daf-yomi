💰 Eiruv: Acquisition or Residence?

Shmuel
Acquisition theory: Depositing food creates a legal kinyan in the house — each contributor acquires a share of the residence where the eiruv is placed.
Rabba
Residence theory: Depositing food makes each contributor a symbolic resident of that house for Shabbat — no property transfer required.
Three Practical Differences These views diverge on: (1) eiruv via utensil alone, (2) food worth less than a peruta, and (3) whether a minor may collect and deposit the eiruv.

🏛️ Courtyard Between Two Alleyways (Shmuel)

A courtyard sits between two alleyways
Its residents habitually use one or both
↓
Eiruv with BOTH
❌ Prohibited in both alleyways — courtyard renders each forbidden
Eiruv with NEITHER
❌ Prohibited in both — residents are accustomed to both, no eiruv established
Eiruv with ONE (accustomed)
❌ Prohibited in the one they joined
✅ Permitted in the other
↓
Sodom Principle: If the alleyway they joined established its own eiruv without them — we compel them to permit it, as refusing benefits no one

💰 How Does an Eiruv Work? Shmuel vs. Rabba

Shmuel
An eiruv works through acquisition. Each person's food contribution creates a legal stake in the house — they are considered co-owners of that space.
Rabba
An eiruv works through residence. Depositing food makes each contributor symbolically dwell in that house, merging all residents into one domain.
Shmuel
Why bread and not a coin? A ma'a is often unavailable on Shabbat eve — people spend their money on Shabbat needs. Bread is always accessible. Money is forbidden by rabbinic decree lest people rely on it and fail to prepare.
Rabba
Even a utensil or food worth less than a peruta suffices — symbolic residence has no monetary threshold. One can 'reside' in a house that has only a utensil in it.
Abaye's Challenge (resolved) Abaye challenged both views: who collects the eiruv acts as an agent for all contributors, effecting acquisition or determining residence on everyone's behalf.

⚖️ Three Practical Differences: Acquisition vs. Residence

Shmuel — Acquisition (קנין)

  • ❌ Utensil alone cannot establish eiruv — a utensil cannot be 'acquired' as food for residence rights
  • ❌ Food worth less than a peruta is invalid — acquisition requires minimum legal value
  • ❌ A minor cannot collect the eiruv — minors cannot effect legal acquisition on behalf of others

Rabba — Residence (דירה)

  • ✅ A utensil alone can establish eiruv — one can reside in a house containing only a utensil
  • ✅ Food under a peruta is valid — symbolic residence needs no monetary threshold
  • ✅ A minor can collect the eiruv — depositing food establishes residence; no legal transfer needed

🍞 When Is an Eiruv Invalid? (Rav Yehuda / Shmuel)

Case 1 — Being particular: A person contributes food but insists others may not eat from his portion
↓
❌ Invalid — Eiruv means joining (ערוב). One who excludes others undermines the very concept of communal merger
↓
Rabbi Ḥanina dissents: The eiruv is technically valid, but that person is labeled a man of Vardina — a notorious miser
↓
Case 2 — Dividing the eiruv: A person splits his food portion across two separate utensils
↓
❌ Invalid — physical division signals separation, not unity. An eiruv must be a single unified deposit
↓
Why state both rulings? Particularity = social separation (others can't eat it); Division = physical separation (food is split) — each covers what the other does not

🌳 Foot Eiruv — Declaring Residence at a Landmark

Shabbat overtakes a traveler on the road
A tree/fence stands 2,000 cubits ahead and 2,000 cubits from his home
↓
"My residence is at the trunk"
✅ Valid — walks 2,000 to tree + 2,000 more toward home
"My residence is beneath the tree"
Rav: ❌ Said nothing — imprecise, no residence established
Shmuel: ✅ Valid under tree only; cannot go further
"My residence is here"
✅ Valid — acquires current location, but loses extended range to home
↓
The Pauper's Eiruv: One who lacks bread may walk anywhere within his Shabbat limit and verbally declare it his residence — eiruv b'raglav (establishes eiruv with his feet)
