🪜 Minimum Ladder Height to Permit a 10-Handbreadth Wall

Shmuel

  • Requires 14 handbreadths
  • Ladder placed diagonally against wall
  • Must physically reach the top at an angle
  • Most stringent opinion

Abaye

  • Requires 11 handbreadths + a bit
  • Diagonal ladder reaches within 3 handbreadths of top
  • Principle of lavud bridges the remaining gap
  • More lenient than Shmuel

Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua

  • Requires only 7 handbreadths + a bit
  • Ladder stood upright against the wall
  • Reaches within 3 handbreadths of top — lavud completes it
  • Most lenient opinion

🏛️ Does a Stake Driven Into a Pillar Diminish Its Private Domain?

Rav Adda bar Ahava
Only a stake 3+ handbreadths high diminishes the pillar. Less than 3 is governed by lavud — treated as part of the flat top, so the full 4×4 area remains.
Abaye & Rava
Even a tiny stake diminishes the pillar! A 4×4 surface is usable only when level. Any projection — however small — renders it unfit for placing objects.
Rav Ashi
Even a 3-handbreadth stake does NOT diminish the pillar. One can hang items on the stake — the pillar retains functional utility and remains a private domain.
Rav Aḥa b. Rava (challenge)
If one filled the entire top with stakes — what then? Rav Ashi replied: Like a pit + its stone embankment — the rim is usable, so items can rest there, joining the combined measure.
Underlying Dispute The debate turns on whether functionality (can objects rest there?) or alternative utility (can one hang items?) defines a valid private domain of 4×4 handbreadths.

🌴 Babylonian Ladders & Palm Trunks — Heavy Enough to Stand Alone

Rabba citing Rabbi Ḥiyya: Palm tree trunks placed next to a wall between two Babylonian courtyards do not need to be permanently attached — they are already considered established passage.
↓
Rav Yosef citing Rabbi Oshaya: Babylonian ladders likewise require no permanent fixing. Their great heaviness establishes them in place — they will not be moved.
↓
Logical inference: Whoever permits ladders without attachment would certainly permit palm trunks, which are placed there from the outset explicitly for climbing.
↓
Woven-straw rungs problem: A ladder with straw rungs in the middle is invalid — the sole of the foot cannot grip the straw. Even if the combined width is 4 handbreadths, it is not a functional passage.
↓
Rule: A valid ladder passage requires a structurally stable surface, at least 4 handbreadths wide, upon which the foot can rest securely.

🏞️ Mishna: Ditch Between Two Courtyards

Ditch between two courtyards
10 handbreadths deep × 4 handbreadths wide
↓
Full-fledged partition → two separate eiruvin required; residents may not combine
↓
Filled with hay
❌ Not considered sealed — hay is temporary and removable
(Abaye: hay is a partition but not a fill)
Filled with dirt or pebbles
✅ Considered sealed — treated as solid ground; one eiruv suffices
Board (≥4 hb) placed across
✅ Valid passage — one eiruv for both courtyards
↓
Key distinction (Abaye): Hay divides space (valid partition) but does not annul the ditch — only solid fill truly eliminates the ditch as a halakhic barrier.
