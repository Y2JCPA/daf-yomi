QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara's explanation of the dispute, what does Rabbi Meir hold regarding the prohibition of bal tosif?
A. One violates bal tosif even without intent whenever performing a mitzva not at its proper time
B. One only violates bal tosif if one has explicit intent to add to the mitzva
C. Bal tosif does not apply to rabbinic enactments, only Torah commandments
D. One violates bal tosif only when performing a mitzva at its exact prescribed time
Correct: B — Rabbi Meir holds that intent is required to violate bal tosif — without intent to add to the mitzva, the act itself is not a violation, even if one wears two pairs of tefillin.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject Rabbi Akiva as the tanna who holds Shabbat is a fit time for tefillin?
A. Rabbi Akiva never discussed the laws of tefillin in any baraita
B. A baraita shows Rabbi Akiva explicitly says Shabbat is not a time for tefillin — Shabbat is itself a sign
C. Rabbi Akiva holds that tefillin are only worn at night, not during the day
D. Rabbi Akiva's rulings about tefillin apply only in the Land of Israel
Correct: B — A baraita quotes Rabbi Akiva: 'I might have thought one dons tefillin on Shabbat… therefore it says: it is a sign — Shabbat is itself a sign.' This directly contradicts the claim that Rabbi Akiva permits tefillin on Shabbat.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara prove that neither Rabbi Meir nor Rabbi Yehuda follows Rabbi Yosei's position?
A. A direct statement by both sages explicitly rejecting Rabbi Yosei
B. Rabbi Meir's ruling on shofar on Rosh HaShana and Rabbi Yehuda's ruling in the Sifra on hand-laying both contradict Rabbi Yosei
C. Both sages authored responsa specifically refuting Rabbi Yosei's leniency for women
D. Their rulings on tefillin in the Tosefta mention women are exempt
Correct: B — Rabbi Meir's position on children sounding the shofar implies women are fully obligated (contra Rabbi Yosei), and the unattributed Sifra — which is authored by Rabbi Yehuda — rules women may not lay hands on sacrifices, directly rejecting Rabbi Yosei.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rabbi Elazar rules on found sky-blue material. Why are strips of tekhelet unfit for tzitzit?
A. Strips are too wide to be woven into fringes and must first be cut down
B. The strips were dyed with intent for use as a cloak, not for tzitzit — lacking the required sacred purpose
C. Strips may not be used because they were found in a public marketplace
D. Strips are unfit because they have already been worn and become impure
Correct: B — The strips' form indicates they were dyed for a cloak rather than for tzitzit. Since the work of dyeing was not performed with the intent (lishmah) required for tzitzit, the strips are unfit for sacred use regardless of their color.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What challenge does Rava raise against the reasoning that 'people do not exert themselves without purpose'?
A. Rava argues that all marketplace items are presumed made for secular use
B. Rava notes that people do exert themselves to make amulets fashioned in the form of tefillin — showing exertion alone does not prove sacred intent
C. Rava rules that only items made by a Torah scholar carry presumed sacred intent
D. Rava agrees with Rabbi Zeira that all found tekhelet is automatically fit for tzitzit
Correct: B — Rava challenges the logic by pointing out that people do exert themselves crafting amulets shaped like tefillin for non-sacred purposes. This shows that the assumption 'exertion implies sacred intent' is not absolute, and the matter is subject to a tannaitic dispute.
