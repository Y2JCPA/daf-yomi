Eruvin 67 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
If the resident of the INNER courtyard forgot to establish an eiruv, what is the halakhic result?
A. Only the inner courtyard is prohibited; the outer courtyard may still carry
B. Carrying is prohibited in both courtyards
C. He may renounce his rights and all carrying becomes permitted
D. Residents can close the connecting door and carry freely in the outer courtyard
Correct: B — An inner resident who forgot prohibits carrying in both courtyards. Closing the door only helps when the outer resident forgot — the inner resident's status cannot be remedied by a physical barrier.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rav Huna son of Rav Yehoshua suggested the inner resident could renounce rights. Rava rejected this by saying it only follows whose opinion?
A. Rabbi Akiva, who requires individual consent
B. Beit Shammai, who takes the stricter view
C. Rabbi Eliezer, who holds one renunciation covers all residents
D. Rabbi Yehuda, who permits renunciation mid-Shabbat
Correct: C — Rava clarified that the suggestion only works according to Rabbi Eliezer, who holds that a single blanket renunciation suffices. The majority Sages require each resident to renounce individually, making Rav Huna's solution impractical.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was Rav Sheshet's ruling when gentiles built an enclosure around two Jewish houses on Shabbat?
A. Jews may carry freely since they did not build the enclosure
B. Jews may carry after renouncing rights to one another
C. Jews may not carry — the prior inability to have made an eiruv is decisive
D. Jews must wait for a rabbi to assess the dimensions of the enclosure
Correct: C — Rav Sheshet ruled that renunciation of rights is not possible here. Since before Shabbat there was no shared enclosure and no eiruv could have been established, the current enclosure cannot unlock the leniency of renunciation.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabba and Rav Yosef, why does a Jewish-owned courtyard with an enclosure LARGER than two beit se'a render the alleyway prohibited?
A. A large private enclosure is treated like a public domain
B. The Jew cannot carry in the large enclosure (rabbinic decree), so he must use the alleyway — becoming a resident there
C. An enclosure larger than two beit se'a invalidates any prior eiruv
D. The Sages decreed that Jews with large enclosures must rent rights from their neighbors
Correct: B — Because a large enclosure (>2 beit se'a) is forbidden for carrying by rabbinic decree, the Jew has nowhere to carry except through the alleyway. This makes him an active user of the alleyway, and without joining the eiruv he restricts his neighbors there.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When a rock in the sea is SMALLER than two beit se'a (fully permitted for carrying), what did the Sages decree about carrying from the sea to the rock?
A. It is fully permitted, since the rock is a private domain
B. It is permitted only with a special eiruv arrangement
C. It is prohibited by rabbinic decree
D. It depends on whether the rock is natural or man-made
Correct: C — The Sages prohibited carrying from the sea to a small, fully-usable rock. Since carrying on the rock itself is common, the Sages protected that common case by prohibiting the less common act (sea↔rock) to prevent blurring of domain boundaries.
