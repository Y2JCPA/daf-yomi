📜 Rabba vs. Abaye: What Kind of Threshold?

Rabba
The mishna deals with a threshold trodden by the public (a true public domain). To prevent degradation of sacred writings, the Sages permitted overriding the rabbinic decree entirely.
Abaye
Objection! The baraita allows rolling the scroll back within 4 cubits — but if this is a true public domain, any leniency would be forbidden. Your explanation cannot work.
Abaye
Rather: the threshold is a karmelit — 4 handbreadths wide but less than 10 high — flanked by private domain on one side and public domain on the other.
Gemara
Within 4 cubits: no Torah violation possible → Sages permitted rolling back. Beyond 4 cubits: carrying 4 cubits in public = Torah prohibition → no leniency granted, turn scroll face-down.
Key Resolution Rav Aḥa bar Ahava: one does not throw sacred writings — it is demeaning — so there is no concern that one might hurl the scroll across a domain boundary.

🚪 The Karmelit Threshold — Abaye's Full Analysis

Abaye
The threshold is a karmelit: 4 hb wide, less than 10 hb high, private domain on one side, public on the other.
Gemara
But shouldn't we decree against rolling back within 4 cubits, lest he carry the scroll from karmelit into the private domain?
Abaye
If the threshold is extended, while walking its length he will remember not to bring the scroll inside.
Gemara
And if the threshold is NOT extended — how then? He might walk straight inside!
Ben Azzai
Walking is like standing. Passing through the karmelit counts as pausing there — the scroll is considered 'at rest' in the karmelit, breaking any through-transfer.
Also Valid Ordinarily one finishes reading a scroll and then places it away — so there is no realistic concern of walking straight from karmelit into the private domain.

⚖️ Within 4 Cubits vs. Beyond 4 Cubits on a Karmelit

Within 4 Cubits

  • Even if whole scroll leaves hand
  • Rolling it back = no Torah sin-offering liability
  • Sages permitted rolling back
  • Concern for degradation of sacred writings outweighs rabbinic decree
  • No domain-crossing at Torah level

Beyond 4 Cubits

  • Rolling back = carrying 4 cubits in a public domain
  • Torah-level prohibition applies
  • Sages did NOT permit rolling back
  • Must turn scroll face-down to protect the writing
  • Degradation concern does not override Torah law

Ben Azzai's Rule

  • הילוך כעמידה דמי
  • Walking = standing
  • Passing through karmelit = pausing there
  • Object considered 'at rest' in karmelit
  • Prevents Torah liability for through-transfer
