⚖️ Rabbis vs. Rabbi Yehuda on Entrance Height

חכמים — The Rabbis

  • Max valid height: 20 cubits
  • Source: Doorway of the Heikhal (Sanctuary)
  • Heikhal doorway: 20 cubits high × 10 cubits wide
  • Above 20 cubits: eye does not register the beam
  • A korah higher than 20 cubits renders alleyway unfit

רבי יהודה — Rabbi Yehuda

  • Max valid height: 40 cubits
  • Source: Doorway of the Ulam (Entrance Hall)
  • Ulam doorway: 40 cubits high × 20 cubits wide
  • Sanctuary and Entrance Hall share equal sanctity
  • Permits korah even 100 cubits high (per bar Kappara)

enclosed 3 sides, 4th side opens to public domain

📜 What Does 'Doorway of the Tent of Meeting' Refer To?

Rabbis
The verse 'at the doorway of the Tent of Meeting' refers only to the Heikhal (Sanctuary). The Entrance Hall has a separate, discrete sanctity. Therefore the maximum doorway height is 20 cubits.
Rabbi Yehuda
The Sanctuary and Entrance Hall share equal sanctity — they are one. 'Doorway of the Tent of Meeting' encompasses both, so a valid doorway can reach 40 cubits.
Rabbis
The verse says 'doorway of the Entrance Hall of the House' — meaning the doorway leads into the House (Sanctuary). The Entrance Hall is subordinate, not equal in sanctity.
Rabbi Yehuda
Had the verse said 'doorway of the Entrance Hall' I would agree. But 'of the House' means the Entrance Hall belongs to the House — one unified complex, one sanctity.
Rav's Teaching (Rav Yehuda citing Rav) Both the sukka height law and the alleyway entrance law are derived from the same source: the doorway of the Sanctuary — 20 cubits high, 10 cubits wide. Rabbi Yehuda derives from the Entrance Hall: 40 cubits high, 20 cubits wide.

🔗 Establishing that Tabernacle = Temple Legally

Problem: The verse 'at the doorway of the Tent of Meeting' (Lev. 17:4) describes the Tabernacle in the wilderness — how can we derive permanent Temple dimensions from it?
↓
Principle: The Tabernacle is called 'Temple' and the Temple is called 'Tabernacle' — they share legal identity and halakhic derivations flow freely between them.
↓
Temple called Tabernacle: 'I will set My Tabernacle among you' (Lev. 26:11) — context of exile and return shows this refers to the permanent Temple.
↓
Tabernacle called Temple: 'He abandoned the Tabernacle of Shiloh, the tent He had pitched among men' (Ps. 78:60) — 'tent' here refers to the Tabernacle, demonstrating the terms interchange.
↓
Conclusion: The legal equivalence is established. Temple doorway dimensions govern the rules for alleyway entrances, even though the source verse concerns the Tabernacle.

⚡ Abaye's Resolution — Why No Mishna Dispute on Width?

אָמַר אַבַּיֵּי: לְעוֹלָם פְּלִיג רַבִּי יְהוּדָה אַמַּתְנִיתִין — Abaye said: In fact Rabbi Yehuda disagrees with the baraita [on width] (Eruvin 2b)
Abaye (אַבַּיֵּי) Explains Rabbi Yehuda does dispute the 10-cubit width limit — but only in a baraita, not in the Mishna. Why? In the Mishna, Rabbi Yehuda already voices his disagreement about height (permitting above 20 cubits). A tanna does not record two separate disputes in the same Mishna. His position on width — that even a wider entrance can be fixed with a korah alone — is preserved in a baraita where it stands alone.
