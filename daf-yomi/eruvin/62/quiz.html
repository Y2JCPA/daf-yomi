QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye's resolution, what do Rabbi Meir and Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov actually agree upon regarding a gentile's residence?
A. A gentile's residence is a significant residence that intrinsically prohibits carrying
B. A gentile's residence is NOT significant — their dispute concerns only the scope of a rabbinic decree
C. An eiruv can never be established in any courtyard shared with a gentile
D. Two Jews are always required to establish any valid eiruv
Correct: B — Abaye overturns the initial assumption: both Tannaim agree the gentile's courtyard is like a pen — not a significant residence. Their entire dispute is about how broad the rabbinic decree against learning from gentile ways should be.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov hold that a single Jew with a gentile does NOT trigger the rabbinic decree?
A. Because a gentile's residence is never halakhically significant
B. Because one Jew can always establish an eiruv independently
C. Because a lone Jew rarely shares a courtyard with a gentile, fearing bloodshed — making it too uncommon for a decree
D. Because the Sages explicitly exempted single-occupant situations
Correct: C — Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov reasons that a single Jew would normally avoid sharing a courtyard with a gentile out of fear of bloodshed. Since the scenario is uncommon, there is no need for a preventive rabbinic decree.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What distinguishes a 'full-fledged rental' from a 'flawed rental' when a Jew rents from a gentile for eiruv purposes?
A. Full-fledged requires payment of at least a perutah; flawed requires less
B. Full-fledged is confirmed by legal documents and officials; flawed is symbolic with no formal documentation
C. Full-fledged is valid on Shabbat; flawed is valid only before Shabbat begins
D. Full-fledged requires two witnesses; flawed requires only one
Correct: B — A full-fledged rental (שכירות גמורה) is formalized through legal documents (moharkei) and officials (aburganei). A flawed rental is a symbolic, token gesture without documentation — both have different concerns about why the gentile might refuse.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does the phrase קב ונקי (kav v'naki) mean when applied to Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov?
A. He only ruled on matters involving precise measurements
B. His rulings are large in quantity but sometimes disputed
C. His body of teachings is small in quantity but uniformly pristine and authoritative — the halakha follows him in all his rulings
D. He disagreed with the majority in exactly a kav's worth of cases
Correct: C — קב ונקי ('a kav, but clean') means Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov had relatively few rulings, but they are complete, clear, and universally accepted as authoritative halakha — a small but flawless corpus.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why was Rav Ḥisda permitted to issue halakhic rulings in Kafri during Rav Huna's lifetime, despite the rule against a disciple ruling in his teacher's jurisdiction?
A. Rav Huna gave him explicit written permission
B. Kafri was outside Rav Huna's actual place of jurisdiction
C. Rav Ḥisda had formally been promoted to the rank of his teacher
D. The rulings in Kafri only concerned Megillat Ta'anit matters
Correct: B — The prohibition against ruling in a teacher's jurisdiction is geographic. Since Kafri was not Rav Huna's domain, Rav Ḥisda could issue rulings there freely — the restriction applies only within the teacher's actual locality.
