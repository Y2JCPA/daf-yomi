Eruvin 71 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman, what happens when a Jew acquires a convert's property on Shabbat?
A. Carrying is permanently forbidden for all neighbors
B. Carrying is forbidden but renunciation of rights can restore permission
C. The acquisition is invalid and carrying remains permitted
D. Carrying is forbidden only if the convert died before nightfall
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman rules that the acquisition renders carrying prohibited, but bitul reshut (renunciation) is effective per Beit Hillel to restore permission.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the core disagreement between Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel about bitul reshut on Shabbat?
A. Whether renunciation requires a formal declaration before a court
B. Whether renunciation applies only to converts' property
C. Whether renunciation of domain constitutes a forbidden act of acquisition
D. Whether renunciation is effective only if performed before Shabbat
Correct: C — Beit Shammai holds renunciation equals acquisition (forbidden on Shabbat); Beit Hillel holds they are not equivalent and renunciation is permitted.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav, under what condition is a homeowner's wine partnership with both neighbors sufficient to exempt them from an eiruv?
A. Only if all three reside in the same courtyard
B. Only if the wine is stored together in one shared vessel
C. Only if the partnership was established before Shabbat began
D. Under all circumstances regardless of how the wine is stored
Correct: B — Rav rules the exemption applies only when the wine is in one vessel, making them truly a single household. Abaye disagrees, allowing even separate vessels when the same item is shared.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rabbi Shimon's ruling regarding wine-and-oil partnerships between a homeowner and neighbors?
A. An eiruv is required because wine and oil cannot be combined
B. An eiruv is required only if they live in different courtyards
C. No eiruv is needed — any genuine shared partnership with the homeowner suffices
D. No eiruv is needed only if the oil and wine together equal a minimum measure
Correct: C — Rabbi Shimon takes the lenient position: in both cases (wine+wine and wine+oil), any authentic partnership with the homeowner exempts from establishing an eiruv.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the baraita require both an eiruv between courtyards AND a shittuf for the alleyway, when either might suffice alone?
A. Because each covers a different halakhic domain that the other cannot reach
B. So that the laws of eiruv will not be forgotten by future generations
C. Because Rabbi Meir holds shittuf is invalid without a backing eiruv
D. Because the Rabbis required double protection against Shabbat violations
Correct: B — The baraita explains: if only shittuf were used, children would never learn about eiruv and the institution would be forgotten. Both are required to preserve the practice.
