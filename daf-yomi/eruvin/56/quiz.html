5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
When Rabbi Yosei says to use the 'seasons' to find cardinal directions, what specifically does he mean?
A. Plant a tree and observe which side faces the most sun
B. Observe where the sun rises in Nisan or sets in Tishrei to locate true east and west
C. Count the days of summer and winter to estimate direction
D. Use the direction from which rain falls in autumn
Correct: B — At the equinoxes (Nisan in spring, Tishrei in autumn), the sun rises due east and sets due west. Rabbi Yosei teaches that observing this gives a reliable — if less precise — method for orientation when stars are unavailable.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Shmuel, how many days and hours separate one tekufah (season) from the next?
A. 90 days exactly
B. 91 days and 6 hours
C. 91 days and 7.5 hours
D. 92 days and 3 hours
Correct: C — Shmuel's system assumes a solar year of exactly 365¼ days. Divided by 4 seasons: 365.25 ÷ 4 = 91 days and 7.5 hours between each tekufah.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why is it wrong to measure the 2000-cubit Shabbat limit diagonally from the corners of a squared city?
A. Diagonals are rabbinically forbidden for eruv measurement
B. The diagonal of a square is longer than its side, so you get more than 2000 cubits
C. The corners would be cut off, and residents there would receive less than the full 2000-cubit range
D. Diagonal measurement requires a surveyor and is impractical
Correct: C — Measuring diagonally from the corners causes the boundary to curve inward — the corners of the eruv would fall short of 2000 cubits in the straight-line directions. Squaring the outer boundary fills in the corners, giving all residents the full limit.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What geometric principle underlies Rava's proof that the Levite city open space (migrash) equals one-quarter of the total squared boundary?
A. The perimeter of a circle is π times its diameter
B. A circle's area is three-quarters of the area of its enclosing square
C. The diagonal of a square equals √2 times its side length
D. Equal areas have equal perimeters
Correct: B — Using the Talmudic approximation that π ≈ 3, a circle's area = ¾ of its surrounding square. The open space ring (circular) is ¾ of its square equivalent — after this adjustment, the migrash works out to exactly 9 million of 36 million total square cubits = ¼.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What did Shmuel warn happens when the vernal equinox (Tekufat Nisan) falls during the planetary hour of Jupiter (Tzedek)?
A. It will bring unusually heavy rainfall
B. It signals a year of famine
C. It will break trees with powerful winds
D. It indicates a coming solar eclipse
Correct: C — Shmuel taught: whenever Tekufat Nisan falls in the planetary hour of Jupiter, that season invariably brings strong winds that break trees — a practical meteorological pattern connected to his precise astronomical calendar.
