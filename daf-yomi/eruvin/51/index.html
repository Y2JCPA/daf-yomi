⚖️ How Is an Eruv Teḥumin Established?

Rabbi Meir
The primary ordinance of eruv is with bread. A verbal declaration alone only suffices for a pauper who lacks food for two meals.
Rabbi Yehuda
The primary ordinance is by foot — going to the location and declaring residence there. This applies to both pauper and wealthy person.
Rav Naḥman
Their dispute is when one says 'My residence is at my current location.' Rabbi Meir requires bread even then; Rabbi Yehuda permits verbal declaration alone.
Rav Ḥisda
No — their dispute is when one says 'My residence is in such-and-such distant place.' Neither foot nor bread alone suffices without actually going there.
Ruling A baraita supports Rav Naḥman's interpretation. The halakha follows Rabbi Yehuda: foot-declaration is primary for both pauper and wealthy person.

🌳 The Tree-Trunk Residence Rule

Shabbat approaches while a traveler is on the road
He cannot return home before nightfall
↓
He declares: 'My residence is beneath that palm tree'
↓
Rava: Valid only if…
He could reach the trunk before Shabbat — even if he does not actually go there
Abaye objects:
The mishna says 'it grew dark while traveling' — implying he cannot reach the tree in time!
↓
Resolution: 'Grew dark while traveling' means he cannot return home — but he can still reach the tree before Shabbat

📏 Deriving the 2000-Cubit Shabbat Limit

Step 1 — Four Cubits

  • Exodus 16:29 — 'Remain every man in his place'
  • A person's 'place' = 4 cubits (basic personal space)
  • Minimum Shabbat zone for a stationary person with no eruv

Step 2 — Gezera Shava

  • Rav Ḥisda: Verbal analogy between 'place' (Exodus 16:29) and 'place' in Levite cities (Numbers 35:5)
  • Levite open space = 1000 cubits per side
  • Therefore Shabbat limit = 2000 cubits total

Step 3 — Why Not Another Analogy?

  • Challenge: use 'outside' (city of refuge) instead?
  • Answer: prefer identical term (place/place) over similar term (outside/outward)
  • School of Rabbi Yishmael: always use the identical term when available

🦶 Pauper vs. Wealthy — Who Establishes Eruv by Foot?

Rabbi Meir
Only a pauper (lacking food for two meals) may establish eruv with his feet — walking to the spot and declaring 'My residence is here.' The primary ordinance requires bread.
Rabbi Yehuda
Both pauper and wealthy establish eruv by foot. Going and declaring is the primary ordinance. Depositing bread is only how the Sages enhanced it for those who can afford it.
Rav Ḥiyya bar Ashi
Teaching before Rav: this leniency applies to both pauper and wealthy.
Rav
Add to your teaching: 'And the halakha follows Rabbi Yehuda' — foot-declaration is primary for everyone.
Historical Proof The families of Memel and Guryon in the village of Aroma distributed dried figs and raisins to paupers so they could walk and establish eruv by foot — confirming Rabbi Yehuda's position in practice.

🤝 Rabba & Rav Yosef — Establishing Eruv Together on the Road

Setting: Rabba and Rav Yosef are traveling together as Shabbat approaches and cannot return home
↓
Rabba declares: 'Our residence will be beneath the palm that carries its brother' — a tree with another leaning on it, used as a distinctive landmark
↓
Rav Yosef: 'I do not know that tree.' — He cannot personally identify the landmark
↓
Rabba invokes Rabbi Yosei: If two travelers go together, one knowing the landmark and one not, the knowledgeable one establishes residence for both — the other may rely on him
↓
Gemara's caveat: Rabba cited Rabbi Yosei because Rav Yosef would accept that authority — Rabba may have derived the rule differently himself
↓
Principle established: Shared knowledge of a landmark enables joint eruv teḥumin when traveling together
