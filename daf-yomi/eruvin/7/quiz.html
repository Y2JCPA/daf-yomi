Eruvin 7 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Sheizvi, when is it forbidden to adopt the stringencies of two different halakhic authorities?
A. Always — one must consistently follow a single authority
B. Only when the two stringencies directly contradict one another
C. Only when the matter involves a Biblical prohibition
D. Never — one may always choose to be stringent
Correct: B — Rav Sheizvi rules that adopting two stringencies is only problematic when they contradict each other (like the spine/skull case). When they can coexist without contradiction, following both is permitted.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Akiva observe Tu BiShvat on both the 1st and 15th of Shevat?
A. He held that both dates are independently valid by Torah law
B. He wished to be maximally stringent by following both Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel
C. He was uncertain about his own tradition and observed both dates out of genuine doubt
D. He was demonstrating that the dispute between Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel was unresolved
Correct: C — The Gemara resolves that Rabbi Akiva acted from safek (doubt) about which date his tradition taught — not from a desire to pile on stringencies. Doubt-based practice is categorically different from deliberate adoption of two authorities' rulings.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If an alleyway has a main street (public domain) on one side and a valley (karmelit) on the other, how is it treated halakhically?
A. Like a public domain on both sides — requires both a door and a tzurat hapetach
B. Like a valley on both sides — a tzurat hapetach on each end suffices
C. The public-domain side needs a door; the valley side needs a tzurat hapetach
D. It is prohibited to carry in such an alleyway in any configuration
Correct: B — The Gemara rules that main street + valley is treated like valley on both sides, since the karmelit carries only a rabbinic prohibition. A tzurat hapetach on each side suffices and no full door is required.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara use the spine-and-skull dispute between Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel to illustrate contradictory stringencies?
A. Because Beit Shammai permits corpse impurity while Beit Hillel prohibits it
B. Because Shammai requires more vertebrae (strict spine) but a smaller skull hole (lenient skull) — being strict on both simultaneously is incoherent
C. Because neither Beit Shammai nor Beit Hillel's position can be verified from the Torah
D. Because the dispute involves a Biblical law where contradictions are especially problematic
Correct: B — Shammai is stricter about the spine (2 vertebrae) but more lenient about the skull (smaller hole). Hillel is stricter about the skull. Adopting both stringencies means requiring 2 vertebrae AND a larger skull hole — an internally inconsistent hybrid that belongs to neither system.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What was Abaye's argument about the ruling that an alleyway terminating in a backyard requires only a tzurat hapetach?
A. The ruling is invalid because a backyard is not a recognized halakhic category
B. This must be Shmuel's ruling, not Rav's, because attributing it to Rav creates a contradiction
C. Rav Yehuda issued this as his own independent ruling, not from either teacher
D. The ruling applies only when the backyard is owned by a single individual
Correct: B — Abaye argued that attributing this ruling to Rav would create two separate contradictions with other positions of Rav — therefore it must be Shmuel's ruling. Rav Yosef replied that he didn't know the source, but Rav himself had permitted it in practice in a specific incident.
