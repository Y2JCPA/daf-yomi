⚖️ May We Adopt Two Authorities' Stringencies?

Problem: Neharde'a adopted both Rav's and Shmuel's stringencies for the same alleyway — is this permitted?
↓
Rav Sheizvi's Rule
Forbidden only when the two stringencies contradict each other
Rav Mesharshiya Objects
Rabbi Akiva observed Tu BiShvat on both the 1st and 15th of Shevat!
↓
Resolution: Rabbi Akiva acted from doubt about his tradition — not to pile on stringencies. Doubt-based practice is categorically different.

⚖️ The Two Stringencies Dispute

Rav Sheizvi
We forbid adopting both stringencies only when they contradict each other — where following one would undermine the other's logic.
Rav Sheizvi
When stringencies do not contradict, we may follow both simultaneously. Therefore Neharde'a's practice was valid — Rav's door requirement and Shmuel's tzurat hapetach coexist peacefully.
Rav Mesharshiya
Objection from a baraita! Rabbi Akiva fasted on both the 1st of Shevat (Beit Shammai's Tu BiShvat) and the 15th of Shevat (Beit Hillel's Tu BiShvat) — adopting both stringent opinions at once!
Gemara
Answer: Rabbi Akiva was not deliberately stringent — he was in genuine doubt (safek) about his tradition. He did not know which date his teachers taught, so he observed both from uncertainty, not stringency.
Core Distinction Acting from safek (doubt) is fundamentally different from deliberately adopting two authorities' stringencies

💀 Spine & Skull: Illustrating Contradictory Stringencies

שדרה וגולגולת שאינן שלמות — אינן מטמאות באוהל
משנה: טומאת אוהל An incomplete spine or skull from a corpse does not convey ritual impurity via a tent (ohel) unless a threshold is met. Beit Shammai: Spine → 2 vertebrae; Skull → hole the size of a small drill Beit Hillel: Spine → 1 vertebra; Skull → hole the size of a large drill Why these are contradictory stringencies: Shammai requires more vertebrae (stricter spine) but a smaller skull hole (more lenient skull). Hillel is stricter on the skull. Adopting both would mean requiring 2 vertebrae AND a larger skull hole — mixing the stricter side of each authority, which is internally incoherent.

🏘️ Alleyway Open on Two Opposite Sides

Public Domain — Both Sides

  • Most restrictive configuration
  • Requires: full door (delet) on one end
  • AND: tzurat hapetach on the other
  • Both Rav's and Shmuel's requirements apply
  • Neharde'a practice: adopted both

Main Street + Valley Opposite

  • Treated as if valley on both sides
  • Valley = karmelit (rabbinic prohibition only)
  • Tzurat hapetach on each side suffices
  • No full door required
  • Intermediate configuration

Valley (Karmelit) — Both Sides

  • Most lenient configuration
  • Only rabbinic prohibition involved
  • Tzurat hapetach on each end suffices
  • No door required at all
  • Baseline for comparison

🔚 Alleyway Terminating in a Backyard

Scenario: Alleyway opens to public domain on one end, but terminates in a closed backyard (chatzer) on the other
↓
Rav Yehuda's ruling: A tzurat hapetach (opening-in-form-of-doorway) suffices — no full door needed on the backyard side
↓
Abaye's Position
This must be Shmuel's ruling — attributing it to Rav creates two contradictions with Rav's other statements
Rav Yosef's Response
I don't know which teacher it follows — but Rav himself permitted it in an incident in a shepherds' village
↓
Rav Sheshet resolves: Explains Rav's position regarding a back wall breached into a public domain — harmonizing Rav's rulings and validating Rav Yehuda's source

📋 Eruvin 7 — Summary Map

Core Question: May one simultaneously adopt the stringencies of two halakhic authorities?
↓
Contradictory Stringencies
e.g., spine/skull: each authority is strict on a different element
→ Forbidden: results in incoherent hybrid standard
Non-Contradictory Stringencies
e.g., Neharde'a alleyway: door + tzurat hapetach can both be built
→ Permitted: both can be fulfilled together
↓
Caveat — Doubt vs. Stringency: Rabbi Akiva observed Tu BiShvat twice not from stringency but from safek — a separate, permitted category
↓
Alleyway Principle: Public domain (reshut harabim) → strict requirements; Karmelit/valley → tzurat hapetach alone suffices on each side
