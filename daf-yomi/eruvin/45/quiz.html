Eruvin 45 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R. Eliezer, if someone walked 3 cubits beyond their Shabbat limit, what is the rule?
A. They may reenter using their 4-cubit Shabbat grant
B. They may not reenter their original limit
C. They receive 2,000 cubits from where they now stand
D. They must remain in the center of their 4 cubits
Correct: B — R. Eliezer: 2 cubits beyond the limit → may reenter (the 4 cubits cover it); 3 cubits beyond → may not reenter. The 4 cubits are subsumed within the original limit, not added beyond it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Rav stress that warriors may return specifically with their weapons?
A. Weapons are too valuable to abandon in the field
B. A historical incident showed that unarmed defenders were pursued back inside, causing deadly chaos
C. The Torah commands that warriors always remain armed
D. Rabban Gamliel issued a special decree for armed soldiers
Correct: B — An actual incident: enemies saw the defenders had left their weapons behind; they pursued them back inside; the defenders grabbed their weapons and the enemies entered after them — causing great confusion and danger.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Under what condition may a border town fight on Shabbat according to the baraita?
A. Only when lives are directly and immediately threatened
B. Even if the gentiles came only for hay, straw, or financial plunder
C. Only with explicit permission from a beit din each time
D. Only if the Jewish defenders outnumber the enemy
Correct: B — A border town (עיר הסמוכה לספר) may desecrate Shabbat even if the enemy came only for hay and straw — because a border breach threatens all of Israel, not just local residents.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which Tanna holds that a person who falls asleep before Shabbat without knowing Shabbat began still receives 2,000 cubits?
A. Rabbi Eliezer
B. The Rabbis
C. Rabbi Yoḥanan ben Nuri
D. Rabbi Yehuda
Correct: C — R. Yoḥanan ben Nuri holds that a person acquires a Shabbat residence even without awareness — therefore he receives the full 2,000-cubit limit. The Rabbis disagree and give only 4 cubits.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does the baraita about rain that fell on the eve of a Festival prove in the Gemara's discussion?
A. That liquid carries no Shabbat limit
B. That ownerless objects acquire Shabbat residence, supporting R. Yoḥanan ben Nuri
C. That Festival laws are always stricter than Shabbat laws
D. That rain belongs to whoever has intent to collect it
Correct: B — Rain is ownerless, yet the baraita gives it 2,000 cubits — proving ownerless objects acquire Shabbat residence. This supports R. Yoḥanan ben Nuri. The baraita says 'rain has 2,000 cubits,' not 'like the feet of the townspeople,' confirming it is treated as ownerless.
