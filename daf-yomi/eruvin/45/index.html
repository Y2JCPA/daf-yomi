🤔 Does R. Eliezer Contradict Rabba?

Abaye
R. Eliezer rules: left limit by 2 cubits → may reenter; left by 3 cubits → may not. This implies the 4-cubit grant is subsumed within the original limit!
Rabba bar bar Ḥana
You raise an objection against our Master Rabba from R. Eliezer? But the halakha follows R. Eliezer — Rabba himself said so!
Abaye
Exactly — I heard from our Master that the halakha IS R. Eliezer. So the 4 cubits are counted within the original limit, not beyond it.
Resolution The 4 cubits granted to someone who left their Shabbat limit are treated as subsumed within the original limit — not as additional distance beyond it.

⚔️ War on Shabbat: Regular Town vs. Border Town

Regular Town

  • Gentiles came for monetary matters (robbery)
  • Do NOT go out to fight with weapons
  • Do NOT desecrate Shabbat in any other way
  • Life not immediately at risk for all of Israel
  • Standard Shabbat protections apply

Border Town

  • Even if gentiles came only for hay and straw
  • GO OUT with weapons — desecrate Shabbat
  • Reason: breach of border endangers all of Israel
  • Babylonia / Neharde'a treated as a border town (Rav Naḥman)
  • Proof: David fought Philistines at Ke'ila for threshing floors

🏃 Warriors Returning on Shabbat — The Weapons Question

Mishna: Those who go out to save lives may return to their places on Shabbat
↓
Question: May they return even if they went beyond 2,000 cubits from their limit?
↓
Rav Yehuda (citing Rav)
They may return with their weapons within 2,000 cubits — not more
Why stress weapons?
Historical incident: enemies saw unarmed defenders, pursued them back inside — caused great chaos and danger
↓
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak: Unlimited return (mishna) = Jews defeated the enemy; 2,000-cubit rule (Rosh HaShana) = standoff, enemies still present

🌧️ Do Ownerless Objects Acquire Shabbat Residence?

Rava
Dilemma: Does R. Yoḥanan ben Nuri hold that ownerless objects acquire Shabbat residence? Or does he give sleeping people special status because they still have potential awareness?
Rav Yosef
Come hear: Rain that fell on the eve of a Festival has 2,000 cubits in each direction — rain is ownerless, yet it acquires residence. This must be R. Yoḥanan ben Nuri!
Abaye
A tribe's cistern (ownerless) — its water has 2,000 cubits. An individual's cistern — water follows the owner's feet. The tribe's case = ownerless = R. Yoḥanan ben Nuri.
Rav Yosef
The baraita says 'rain has 2,000 cubits' — not 'like the feet of the townspeople.' This proves it's treated as ownerless, not as belonging to the city's residents!
Final Ruling on Festival Rain Rain that fell on the Festival itself is 'like the feet of all people' — it was still absorbed in clouds when Yom Tov began, so it acquired no independent Shabbat residence; and the uncertainty (same clouds?) is rabbinic, resolved leniently.
