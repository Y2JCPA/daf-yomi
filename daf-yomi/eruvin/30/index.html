⚖️ Beit Hillel vs. Beit Shammai: Eruv with Restricted Foods

Beit Hillel
One may establish an eruv for a nazirite with wine and for an Israelite with teruma — even though they personally cannot eat it.
Beit Shammai
The food must have been fit for that person to eat. A nazirite may never drink wine — the eruv is invalid!
Beit Hillel
But you yourself permit an eruv for an adult on Yom Kippur, even though an adult cannot eat on that day — why is that different?
Beit Shammai
On Yom Kippur, the food was fit to eat while still day (erev Yom Kippur). But wine is never fit for a nazirite — it is permanently prohibited to him.
Conclusion The mishna follows Beit Hillel: eruv validity depends on whether the food is generally suitable, not whether this specific person can eat it.

📜 Oath vs. Vow: Can the Loaf Be Used for Eruv?

Rav (via Rav Huna)
If one says 'I swear I shall not eat this loaf' (שבועה — oath), one may still establish an eruv for him with it. Eruv is not personal eating.
Baraita objection
But the baraita says: if one declares 'this loaf shall be forbidden to me' (נדר — vow), one may NOT use it for an eruv!
Rav Huna
I follow Rabbi Eliezer: even a vow of 'this loaf shall be forbidden to me' does not invalidate the eruv — such a vow cannot block fulfillment of a mitzva.
Gemara resolution
Two tanna'im transmitted Rabbi Eliezer's opinion differently — hence the apparent contradiction between sources.
Key Distinction Oath (שבועה): sworn not to eat this loaf — eruv valid per Rav Huna, since eruv is communal, not personal consumption.
Vow (נדר): loaf declared forbidden — disputed; the baraita and Rav Huna's ruling reflect two transmissions of Rabbi Eliezer.

👥 Eruv Quantity: Whose Appetite Sets the Measure?

Standard Person

  • Two meals' worth of food
  • Based on average adult appetite
  • Default measure for eruv calculations

Ill or Elderly Person

  • Smaller amount suffices
  • Rabbi Shimon ben Elazar: calibrate to their reduced appetite
  • Mishna: 'measures are per the individual'

Glutton

  • We do NOT follow the glutton's outsized appetite
  • Standard two-meal measure still applies
  • Excess appetite is not legally recognized

Og King of Bashan

  • Requires 4-handbreadth door opening (per Rabbi Yoḥanan)
  • Illustrates: measure fits the person
  • Rabbi Shimon ben Elazar vs. Rabbis: does his standard generalize?

📌 Summakhos vs. Rabbis: Teruma for Eruv

סומכוס אומר: אין מערבין לישראל בתרומה — אלא בחולין
Core Dispute Rabbis: One may establish an eruv for an Israelite with teruma — it is food, even if the Israelite personally cannot eat it.

Summakhos: Teruma may NOT be used as eruv for an Israelite — only regular non-sacred food qualifies.

Why not annul the teruma or separate from elsewhere? (1) Ḥaverim are not suspected of separating teruma improperly from other produce; (2) removing teruma from the eruv pile itself may leave insufficient quantity; (3) asking a Sage to annul it is technically available but the Gemara finds each workaround blocked — ultimately Summakhos's view is reframed as a principled dispute, not a practical one.
