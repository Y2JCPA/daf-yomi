QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav, may one establish an eruv using raw meat?
A. No — raw meat is not considered food for eruv purposes
B. Yes — it can be eaten when necessary, so it qualifies as food
C. Only if the meat has been salted for the altar
D. Only for Kohanim who are permitted to eat certain raw meats
Correct: B — Rav Ḥiyya bar Rav Ashi citing Rav explicitly states that raw meat may be used for an eruv because it can be eaten when necessary, even though it is not ordinarily eaten that way.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Beit Shammai's reason for permitting an eruv for an adult on Yom Kippur, but forbidding one for a nazirite with wine?
A. Wine is a liquid and liquids cannot legally serve as eruv food
B. On Yom Kippur the food was fit to eat while still day (erev Yom Kippur); wine is never fit for a nazirite
C. A nazirite's prohibition is biblical, while Yom Kippur eating is only rabbinic
D. Yom Kippur eruv only applies to children, not adults
Correct: B — Beit Shammai distinguishes: on erev Yom Kippur, the food was fit when set; wine was never fit for the nazirite since his prohibition is permanent — it never had a moment of fitness.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rav Huna resolve the tension between his ruling about oaths and the baraita about vows?
A. He limits his ruling to eruv techumin, not eruv chatzeirot
B. He holds that all vows and oaths equally invalidate an eruv
C. He rules in accordance with Rabbi Eliezer, and two tanna'im transmitted Rabbi Eliezer's opinion differently
D. He concedes his ruling was a mistake and retracts it
Correct: C — The Gemara concludes that Rav Huna follows Rabbi Eliezer's lenient view; the contradiction with the baraita reflects two tanna'im who disagreed in how they transmitted Rabbi Eliezer's position.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why, according to the Gemara, can't one simply ask a Sage to annul the teruma used for an eruv and thereby permit the Israelite to eat it?
A. Teruma can never be annulled under any circumstances
B. Annulment of teruma requires the consent of the original owner's heirs
C. Ḥaverim are not suspected of separating teruma improperly, so certain technical annulment routes are blocked
D. A Sage may only annul vows, not consecrated items like teruma
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that ḥaverim (scrupulous observers) are not suspected of separating teruma from other produce improperly — this closes the workaround of annulling from elsewhere.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda in the mishna, why may a priest establish an eruv placed in a cemetery?
A. The prohibition for a priest to enter a cemetery is only rabbinic, not biblical
B. The priest receives a special Shabbat dispensation for eruv purposes
C. The priest can walk between the gravestones using interposition, which acts as a tent shielding against impurity
D. An eruv food placement does not require the person to physically retrieve the food
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehuda permits it because the priest can navigate between gravestones using a form of ohel (tent) interposition, which halakhically shields against the impurity of the graves beneath.
