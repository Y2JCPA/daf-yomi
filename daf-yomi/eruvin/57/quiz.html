5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, where is the 1000-cubit migrash (open space) of a Levite city located?
A. Along the four sides of the city only
B. At the four corners of the city only
C. Equally on all sides and corners combined
D. Only on the eastern and western sides
Correct: B — Rav Ashi holds the migrash applies only at the corners. Ravina disagrees, saying it applies to the sides. Rav Ashi defends his view by arguing 'around' in Numbers 35:4 can refer to corners, just as in the burnt-offering context.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
The Gemara notes that 8 million sq. cubits of migrash appears to be one-third (not one-quarter) of the 24 million sq. cubit boundary. How is this resolved?
A. The boundary is actually 32 million sq. cubits
B. The corners are excluded from the migrash calculation entirely
C. 'One quarter' refers to the linear dimension of the boundary, not the area
D. The migrash is calculated only for three sides, not four
Correct: C — The resolution is that 'one quarter' describes the linear ratio: the 1000-cubit migrash strip is one quarter of the 4000-cubit side of the full extended boundary square — not one quarter by area.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What condition in the mishna allows three villages arranged in a triangle to combine into one Shabbat unit?
A. All three villages must be within 70 cubits of the middle one
B. The two outer villages must be within 141⅓ cubits of each other
C. Each village must have at least 600 residents
D. The middle village must be larger than both outer ones
Correct: B — The mishna rules that if the two outer villages are within 141⅓ cubits of each other, the middle village combines all three into a single Shabbat city unit from which the 2000-cubit limit is measured.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How did Rava prove to Rav Safra that Akistefon and Ardeshir legitimately measure from each other's far end?
A. He produced a rabbinic responsum (teshuvah) authorizing it
B. He measured the distance himself with a 50-cubit rope
C. He cited Rav Huna's explicit ruling about those two cities
D. He pointed to submerged wall foundations in the Tigris River connecting them within 70⅔ cubits
Correct: D — Rava physically showed Rav Safra foundation stones submerged in the Tigris River, demonstrating a wall had once connected the two cities within the halakhically required distance, validating their status as a single combined unit.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the new mishna at the end of the daf, what happens when a measurer reaches a canyon while measuring the Shabbat limit?
A. The measurement stops; the canyon is an absolute boundary
B. The depth of the canyon is added to the 2000-cubit count
C. The depth of the canyon is not counted; the measurer spans it and resumes
D. The measurer must go around the canyon, adding the detour distance
Correct: C — The mishna rules that neither the depth of a canyon nor the height of a fence counts toward the 2000-cubit Shabbat limit. The measurer spans the obstacle and resumes on the other side, as if it were not there.
