📏 How to Measure the Shabbat Limit

Begin measuring with a 50-cubit rope of approved material (not shorter, not longer)
↓
Flat terrain
Measure straight along the ground
Canyon encountered
Span it — measure as if flat; if >50 cubits wide, use 'piercing'
Hill encountered
Span it if incline ≥10 tefachim in 4 amot; otherwise measure over it
↓
Do not go beyond the city's Shabbat limit while measuring — decree against misleading bystanders
↓
Measurement accepted only from an expert surveyor; even a gentile slave/maidservant is believed for limit testimony

🌿 The Three Halakhic Ropes (Rav Yosef)

Rope of Magag

  • Material: bulrush reed fiber
  • Used for: burning of the Red Heifer (פרה אדומה)
  • The heifer was bound with it before being placed on the woodpile

Rope of Netzer

  • Material: fibrous palm vine
  • Used for: measuring the Shabbat limit (תחום שבת)
  • Must not stretch — accuracy is critical

Rope of Flax

  • Material: flax fibers
  • Used for: the rite of the beheaded heifer (עגלה ערופה)
  • Each halakhic rite demands its specific material

🏚️ How Deep May a Canyon Be and Still Be Spanned?

Rav Yosef
A canyon may be spanned as long as it is not more than two thousand cubits deep. Beyond that, the slope itself must be measured.
Abaye (objection)
A baraita teaches: if a canyon is up to 100 cubits deep and up to 50 cubits wide, one may span it — otherwise, not. How can the limit be 2,000 cubits?
Rav Yosef (alt. version)
Even if the canyon is more than 2,000 cubits deep, it may be spanned — this follows the view that spanning is always permitted when the wall is vertical (plumb line drops straight down).
Resolution
The tanna'im's dispute applies where the plumb line does not drop straight (sloped walls). Where the wall is vertical, spanning is permitted regardless of depth.
Key Distinction Vertical canyon wall (חוט המשקולת נופל) → span it. Sloped wall → measure the slope; depth matters.

🏔️ The 'Piercing' Method — How to Measure Through a Hill

Step 1: Two people hold the two ends of the 50-cubit measuring rope.
↓
Step 2: One person stands at the base of the hill; the other ascends to a point directly above, holding the rope taut and level.
↓
Step 3: Using a plumb line or sighting rod, the measurer determines the horizontal distance — as if the hill were 'pierced through' (מנקב).
↓
Step 4: The vertical height is ignored; only the horizontal projection is counted toward the 2,000-cubit limit.
↓
Limitation: Rav Naḥman: piercing is not used for measuring distances in the rite of the beheaded heifer (עגלה ערופה) — that rite follows different rules.

⚖️ Trusted Testimony — The Surveyor & Shabbat Limits The mishna rules: only an expert surveyor (מומחה) may measure the Shabbat limit. If he extended the limit in one place and reduced it in another (a jagged line), we follow the more lenient result. Remarkably, even a gentile slave or maidservant is believed when saying 'the Shabbat limit reached until here' — the Rabbis were lenient about Shabbat limit testimony because violations carry only a rabbinic prohibition in this context, and the information is easily verifiable.
