QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye, why does extending a roof over existing grapevines NOT prove that a partition causes prohibition?
A. Because a roof is not a valid halachic partition
B. Because the vineyard prohibition already existed before the roof was extended
C. Because Abaye holds agricultural laws don't apply inside roofed structures
D. Because the roof must be permanent to create prohibition
Correct: B — Abaye argues the prohibition pre-existed the roof — the vines were already there before the extension. The roof only reveals an existing prohibition; it does not create a new one. This is the core distinction.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In Rav Yehuda's three-enclosure case, when is each resident entitled to a full 2 beit se'a allocation?
A. When all three enclosures are equal in width
B. When the middle enclosure is the widest and protrudes
C. When the two outer enclosures protrude, flanking the breach with walls on both sides
D. Only when there is exactly one person in each enclosure
Correct: C — When the outer enclosures are wider and protrude, their walls extend on both sides of the gap between them and the middle enclosure, creating valid partitions. Each resident then receives the full 2 beit se'a they are entitled to.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rav Ḥisda's ruling on an embankment of 5 handbreadths combined with a partition of 5 handbreadths?
A. They always combine to form a valid 10-handbreadth partition
B. They do not combine to form a valid 10-handbreadth partition
C. They combine only when the embankment is at least 6 handbreadths
D. They combine for Shabbat law but not for eiruv purposes
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda rules that an embankment of 5 handbreadths and a partition of 5 handbreadths above it do NOT join together to form a partition of ten handbreadths — the minimum required to enclose a private domain.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Abaye reject Rav Ḥisda's attempt to resolve Rav Hoshaya's dilemma from the mishna about large/small courtyards?
A. Because the mishna only addresses Torah-level domains, not rabbinic eiruv
B. Because the mishna certainly refers to a breach that occurred before Shabbat, not on Shabbat
C. Because Rav Hoshaya's case involves people moving, not walls collapsing
D. Because the mishna's ruling was overturned by a later baraita
Correct: B — Abaye insists the mishna must be discussing a breach that happened erev Shabbat (while still day). Since it doesn't address mid-Shabbat collapses, Rav Ḥisda cannot use it to resolve Rav Hoshaya's dilemma about walls collapsing on Shabbat itself.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When a wall between two courtyards collapses on Shabbat, what does Shmuel rule?
A. Carrying is forbidden throughout the combined area until Shabbat ends
B. Each resident may carry only within 4 cubits of their original position
C. The two courtyards merge into one domain and residents may carry throughout
D. The ruling depends on which courtyard's wall it was before collapsing
Correct: C — Shmuel rules that the physical reality governs: when the wall collapses, the two areas merge into one domain, and residents may carry throughout the combined space. Rav disagrees, limiting each person to 4 cubits based on the domains as they stood at Shabbat's start.
