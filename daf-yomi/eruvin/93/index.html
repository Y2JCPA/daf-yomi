🔥 Does a Partition Cause Prohibition?

Rabba bar Rav Ḥanan
A partition causes prohibition! If half a house is roofed with vines underneath — extending the roof over the whole house would prohibit sowing there. The roof itself created the prohibition.
Abaye
There the prohibition existed before the roof — the vines were already growing. The roof merely reveals what was already prohibited. The partition did not create a new prohibition.
Rava (via Rav Shemaya)
In a vineyard 11 cubits from a wall, one may not sow seeds between them. The wall itself creates this stringency — a clear case of partition causing prohibition!
Abaye
If that's your objection, raise it from the mishna on vineyard clearings. But even there — the 4-cubit work area of a vineyard is part of the vineyard itself, not a partition effect.
Conclusion The 4-cubit work area alongside a vineyard is inherently vineyard-space — the wall doesn't create the prohibition, it merely borders a zone already defined as forbidden. Dilemmas left unresolved are ruled leniently.

🏘️ Three Enclosures Side by Side — Rav Yehuda

Three adjacent enclosures
Middle + two outer ones, walls breached between them
↓
✅ Outer ones PROTRUDE (wider than middle)
Outer walls extend past the breach on both sides → valid partitions flank the gap. One person each outer, one in middle: each gets full 2 beit se'a
❌ Middle one PROTRUDES (wider than outers)
Entire width of each outer enclosure is breached into middle. One person each outer, one in middle: prohibited — middle swallows the outers
↓
Unresolved dilemma: 1 each in outers + 2 in middle, or 2 each in outers + 1 in middle — how to count beit se'a per person?
Halakha: rule leniently in both cases

📐 Embankment + Partition — Do They Combine?

Rav Ḥisda
An embankment of 5 handbreadths plus a partition of 5 handbreadths above it do NOT combine to form a valid 10-handbreadth partition. Each element must independently reach 10 tefachim.
Baraita
Two courtyards — upper and lower: if the upper is 10 handbreadths higher, OR has an embankment of 5 + partition of 5, residents establish two separate eiruvin. This implies they DO combine!
Rav (clarifying Rav Ḥisda)
Rav Ḥisda concedes that embankment + partition combine for the lower courtyard — it faces a full 10-handbreadth wall. The dispute is about whether the upper courtyard can use this combination.
Mareimar
An embankment of 5 handbreadths with a partition of 5 handbreadths built on top of it DO combine to form a valid partition of ten handbreadths. Ravina confirmed this ruling.
Rabba bar Ulla's Resolution The baraita refers to a case where full 10-handbreadth walls protruded on both sides of the 5-handbreadth section, extending at least 4 cubits — functioning as corner posts (לבוד) to validate the shorter middle section.

🔄 Upper & Lower Courtyards — Eiruv Scenarios

Height Disparity ≥ 10 Tefachim

  • Fully separate halachic domains
  • Each courtyard establishes its own eiruv
  • Two independent eiruvin are valid
  • Residents carry only within their own area
  • Standard rule — no ambiguity

Height Disparity < 10 Tefachim

  • Treated as a SINGLE merged domain
  • Residents establish ONE joint eiruv
  • Cannot establish two separate eiruvin
  • Applies unless lower is fully breached into upper
  • Per Rabba son of Rava's interpretation

Lower Fully Breached into Upper

  • Lower establishes eiruv together WITH upper
  • Lower courtyard CANNOT have independent eiruv
  • Upper retains its own independent eiruv
  • Special case — full breach = absorption
  • Baraita's 'one eiruv not two' refers to this case

🔍 Rav Hoshaya's Dilemma — Step by Step

Setup: A wall between two courtyards collapses on Shabbat. Residents of one courtyard now face the other's open space.
↓
The dilemma: Do these "new arrivals" (residents now exposed to the other courtyard) count as residents for eiruv purposes? May they carry in the combined area?
↓
Rav Ḥisda's attempt: Cite the mishna — large courtyard breached into small; large may carry freely, small is prohibited. This implies mid-Shabbat breach restricts the smaller side.
↓
Abaye's rebuttal: The Master should not say 'one could explain it that way.' The mishna certainly refers to a breach before Shabbat (while still day) — you cannot derive Shabbat-collapse rules from it.
↓
Final ruling: Rav — each person limited to 4 cubits. Shmuel — merged domain, carry throughout. Practical halakha follows the lenient approach.
