Eruvin 27 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What did Rabbi Yoḥanan teach about general statements using the word 'all' (כּוֹל) in a mishna?
A. They are absolutely inclusive with no further exceptions
B. One may not derive that they are all-inclusive, even where the mishna says 'except'
C. They apply only to rabbinic laws, not Torah commandments
D. They are binding only when stated anonymously (stam)
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan's principle: general statements in mishnayot are never absolute. Even an explicit 'except' clause doesn't exhaust all exceptions — there are always additional cases not listed.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Which two items are explicitly excluded from both eiruv use and second-tithe purchasing?
A. Meat and fish
B. Olive oil and vinegar
C. Water and salt
D. Wine and bread
Correct: C — The mishna states all foods may establish an eiruv and be bought with second-tithe money, 'except for water and salt.' This parallel exclusion in both contexts is the focus of much of Daf 27.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Yosef, when did Rabbi Yitzḥak permit buying a water-and-salt mixture with second-tithe money?
A. When the mixture is used for preserving holy meat
B. Only when approved by a rabbinic court
C. When oil was added to the water-and-salt mixture
D. When the mixture costs at least a perutah
Correct: C — Rav Yosef clarifies that Rabbi Yitzḥak's permissive ruling applied only when oil was added. Since oil itself may be purchased with second-tithe money, the water and salt are included in that purchase.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What did Rabbi Yoḥanan offer to do for anyone who could explain why 'on oxen' (Deut. 14:26) is not superfluous according to ben Bag Bag?
A. Study with them in private for a full year
B. Carry their clothes after them into the bathhouse
C. Exempt them from all communal obligations
D. Grant them rabbinic ordination
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan offered to carry the person's garments into the bathhouse — an act of great honor and deference. He found 'on oxen' entirely redundant since ox+hide is an obvious a fortiori from sheep+fleece.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What practical dispute does Abaye identify as the difference between the two hermeneutical methods (ribbui/miyut vs. klal/prat) for second-tithe purchases?
A. Whether wine jugs may be purchased alongside wine
B. Whether ox hides may be purchased alongside oxen
C. Whether fish brine may be purchased (fish don't grow from the ground)
D. Whether salt may be purchased together with meat
Correct: C — Abaye identifies fish as the test case: under ribbui/miyut (broad re-inclusion), fish brine is permitted; under klal/prat (restricted to 'offspring of offspring of the earth'), fish — which do not grow from the ground — are excluded.
