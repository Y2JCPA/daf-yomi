QUESTION 1 OF 5
When Tisha B'Av falls on Shabbat, the fast is deferred to Sunday. But if Tisha B'Av falls on Friday (Shabbat eve), what is the ruling according to Rabbi Yosei and the final halakha?
A. One eats and drinks fully on Friday; there is no fast at all
B. One must fast and complete the fast on Friday
C. One stops eating at midday on Friday
D. One observes a half-fast — eating in the morning, fasting in the afternoon
Correct: B — Rabbi Yosei holds — and the final halakha (as stated by Ulla and Mar Zutra citing Rav Huna) follows — that one must fast and complete the fast on Friday (Shabbat eve), just as one stops eating before Shabbat ends when Tisha B'Av falls on Sunday.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
When Rabbi Akiva's students brought him a slightly cooked egg on Tisha B'Av that fell on Shabbat eve, what did he do — and why?
A. He refused to eat it and fasted the entire day
B. He ate it with salt to demonstrate that full Shabbat eating is required
C. He swallowed it without salt as a subtle personal sign of mourning
D. He set it aside and instructed students not to eat until nightfall
Correct: C — Rabbi Akiva swallowed the egg without salt — not because this was halakhically required, but as a personal act of subtle mourning. He did so to show students that eating is permitted on Shabbat while personally honoring the day's somber character.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the mishna, if gentiles forcibly took a person outside the Shabbat limit and then forcibly returned him, what is his status?
A. He may walk only 4 cubits from his current position
B. He may walk only within the nearest enclosed city
C. It is as if he never left — his full original Shabbat limit is restored
D. He must wait until Shabbat ends before resuming normal movement
Correct: C — The mishna rules that when the gentiles both took him out and returned him (all involuntarily), it is as if he never left, and he may move about within his original Shabbat limit as before.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What principle does Rabba invoke to permit a person restricted to 4 cubits to move beyond them when he urgently needs to relieve himself?
A. Pikuach nefesh — danger to life overrides all
B. Kavod HaBriot — great is human dignity
C. Lo plug — the Rabbis made no exceptions to their decrees
D. Shev v'al ta'aseh — passivity is preferable to action
Correct: B — Rabba states that כבוד הבריות (kavod habriot — great is human dignity) is a powerful principle that overrides rabbinic restrictions, permitting the person to move beyond the 4-cubit limit to find a private place to relieve himself.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rav Pappa rules that produce intentionally taken beyond the Shabbat limit and then returned has not lost its place. Who disputes this view?
A. Rav Naḥman and Shmuel
B. Rabba and Abaye
C. Rabbi Neḥemya and Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov
D. Rabban Gamliel and Rabbi Elazar ben Azarya
Correct: C — Rabbi Neḥemya and Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov disagree, holding that produce taken beyond the Shabbat limit intentionally is prohibited to carry beyond 4 cubits, and must be returned unwittingly to restore its full status. The Gemara concludes this is a tannaitic dispute.
