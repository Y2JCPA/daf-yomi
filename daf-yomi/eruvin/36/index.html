⚖️ Rabbi Yosei: Lenient on Eiruv, Stringent on Impurity?

Rabbi Yosei (on Eiruv)
A doubtful eiruv is valid. Keep the teruma in its presumptive state of purity — it was pure before Shabbat.
Rabbi Yosei (on Impurity)
One who immersed in a doubtful ritual bath remains impure. Keep the person in their presumptive state of impurity.
Rava
The difference: impurity has a Torah basis — we are stringent. Eiruv is rabbinic, so we follow the lenient chazakah.
Rav Huna bar Ḥinnana
Correct — Torah-based impurity commands a different standard. Even rabbinic-origin impurity follows Torah rigor when immersion is its cure.
Resolution — חזקה Rabbi Yosei applies presumptive status (chazakah) asymmetrically: stringent for Torah-based ritual impurity, lenient for rabbinic eiruv — because the domains have different legal weight.

🔴🔵 Rabbi Meir vs. Rabbi Yosei: Doubtful Eiruv

Rabbi Meir — Stringent

  • Doubtful eiruv is invalid
  • All uncertainty treated strictly
  • Eiruv must be certainly fit at twilight
  • Does not accept retroactive designation (no breira)
  • Two loaves — one pure, one impure — neither may serve

Rabbi Yosei — Lenient

  • Doubtful eiruv is valid
  • Follow presumptive status (chazakah)
  • Teruma previously pure keeps its presumption
  • Lenient on rabbinic matters like eiruv
  • Stringent only where Torah-law impurity is at stake

⏰ When Does Eiruv Take Effect? The Tevul Yom Flask

Flask filled from untithed barrel
by a tevul yom — immersed but awaiting nightfall for full purification
↓
Person declares: 'Establish an eiruv for me with the contents of this flask'
Flask is still tevel (untithed) — forbidden to eat
↓
Rava's Proof
Eiruv takes effect at end of bein hashmashot. At that moment flask was tevel → eiruv invalid. Proves: eiruv is acquired at twilight's close.
Rav Pappa's Rebuttal
Even if eiruv takes effect at start of Shabbat day, we require the food be fit while still day — tevel is never fit, so no proof about timing.

🗺️ The Conditional Eiruv

עירובין לו — עירוב על תנאי: אם חכם בא ממזרח — עירובי למזרח
📜 Mishna — עירוב על תנאי A person may place two eiruvin — one to the east, one to the west — and declare:

'If a Sage comes from the east, my eiruv is in the east so I may greet him; if from the west, my eiruv is in the west.'

The eiruv in the Sage's direction takes effect. But if both a Sage and a tax collector come from opposite sides simultaneously — go toward the Sage, away from the tax collector.

Rabbi Yehuda: If one of them is his teacher, he must go to his teacher — we assume that was always his intention.

🔀 Breira — Does Retroactive Designation Work?

Rabbi Yoḥanan
This is NOT true breira — the Sage had already arrived by twilight. The person simply didn't know which direction. At twilight, only one Sage had come, so the eiruv was already determined.
Rabbi Yehuda (via Ayo's baraita)
One cannot make conditions about two things at once. If both Sages could come from either direction, the eiruv is split and ineffective — no retroactive fixing.
Rav
The mishna allowing free choice should be rejected based on Ayo's baraita. Rabbi Yehuda consistently denies breira — the baraita reflects his true view.
The Gemara
The Tosefta also confirms Rabbi Yehuda rejects breira. Since multiple sources align, we uphold the baraita over the mishna in this case.
Core Dispute — ברירה Breira: can an ambiguous act be legally defined retroactively by a later event? Rabbis say yes in rabbinic domains. Rabbi Yehuda says no — intent must be fixed at the moment of action.
