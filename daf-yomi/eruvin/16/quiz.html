Eruvin 16 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, what is the halakha when a partition's breached segments equal its standing segments?
A. Carrying is prohibited — standing must strictly exceed breached
B. Carrying is permitted — equality is sufficient for validity
C. The partition must be rebuilt before Shabbat
D. One may only carry within four cubits of the standing portions
Correct: B — Rav Pappa holds that equal standing and breached segments still permit carrying. Though the Gemara delivers a conclusive refutation (תיובתא), it rules the halakha follows Rav Pappa based on the Mishna's precise language.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the Tosefta of Kilayim, what rule applies to boards that are between four handbreadths and ten cubits wide?
A. The gap between boards may be up to three handbreadths wide
B. The gap between boards must not equal the full width of one board
C. The entire partition is automatically invalidated
D. Lavud joins all boards within four handbreadths automatically
Correct: B — For boards from 4 handbreadths to 10 cubits wide, the gap must be less than the full width of a board. A gap equal to the board width invalidates the partition. Rav Pappa interprets 'full-width gap' as slightly wider (for easy insertion), not exact equality.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
The Mishna states that a caravan partition using ropes requires how many ropes, and to what minimum combined thickness?
A. Two ropes, each at least half a handbreadth thick
B. Three ropes, with a combined thickness greater than one handbreadth
C. Three ropes, each individually one handbreadth thick
D. Four ropes spanning the full ten-handbreadth height
Correct: B — The Mishna requires three ropes, one above another. Their combined thickness (not each individually) must exceed one handbreadth, and the full structure must reach ten handbreadths in height.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Rabbi Yosei son of Rabbi Yehuda require for a partition to be halakhically valid?
A. The partition must stand for at least one full Shabbat
B. It must contain both vertical (warp) and horizontal (woof) elements
C. It must enclose no more than two beit se'a of space
D. It must be built from natural materials, not manufactured boards
Correct: B — Rabbi Yosei son of Rabbi Yehuda requires both warp (vertical elements like boards) and woof (horizontal elements like ropes). Either alone is insufficient. Rav Naḥman explains that his position in the Mishna concerns providing adequate space, not rejecting rope partitions entirely.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman citing Rabbeinu Shmuel, what is the maximum space an individual (or two individuals) may enclose for carrying on Shabbat?
A. One beit se'a
B. Two beit se'a
C. Four beit se'a
D. Unlimited, provided the partition is halakhically valid
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman in the name of Rabbeinu Shmuel rules that both an individual and two individuals may enclose up to two beit se'a. Three or more people receive space proportional to their needs. Rav Naḥman initially erred and later corrected himself, teaching that the majority Rabbinic opinion provides two beit se'a as the baseline.
