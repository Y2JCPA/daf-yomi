⚖️ The Central Dispute: When Breached Equals Standing

Rav Pappa
If the breached segment equals the standing segment, carrying is still permitted. Only when the breach exceeds the standing portion is the partition invalid.
Rav Huna bar Yehoshua
If the breached segment equals the standing segment, carrying is prohibited. The standing portion must be strictly greater than the breached portion.
🏁 Resolution The Gemara delivers a conclusive refutation of Rav Pappa — yet rules the halakha follows him, supported by the precise language of the Mishna (פשיטא מתניתין כוותיה דרב פפא).

🔍 What Does 'A Gap the Full Width of a Board' Mean?

Rav Huna bar Yehoshua
A gap equal to the board width means literally equal in measurement. If the gap equals the board, the partition is invalid — standing cannot merely equal breached.
Rav Pappa
A 'full-width gap' means slightly wider — enough to easily insert and extract the board. A gap exactly equal to the board is still valid!
📜 Stakes This technical dispute over the Tosefta's language in tractate Kilayim determines whether geometric equality or functional ease-of-passage defines an invalidating gap.

🪢 Building a Caravan Partition on Shabbat (Mishna)

Step 1: Surround the encampment with three ropes (חֲבָלִים), one above another
↓
Step 2: Ensure the combined thickness of all three ropes together exceeds one handbreadth
↓
Step 3: The full structure — ropes plus gaps between them — must reach ten handbreadths high
↓
Alternative: Use upright boards, but the gap between any two boards must be less than three handbreadths
↓
Dispute: Rabbi Yehuda limits this leniency to caravans only; the Sages extend it to any individual as well

🧵 Three Positions on Partition Requirements

Rabbi Yehuda

  • Rope-only (horizontal) partitions are valid
  • Board-only (vertical) partitions are valid
  • Either element alone suffices
  • Leniency is for caravans only — exigent circumstances

Rabbi Yosei son of Rabbi Yehuda

  • A partition must have BOTH warp (vertical) and woof (horizontal)
  • Ropes alone — without vertical stakes — are invalid
  • Boards alone — without a horizontal rope — are invalid
  • Applies this stringency even to caravan situations

The Rabbis (majority)

  • Rope partitions valid for both individuals and caravans
  • No warp-and-woof requirement
  • Space enclosed may not exceed two beit se'a (if unoccupied)
  • Halakha follows this majority opinion
