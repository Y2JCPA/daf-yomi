⚖️ The Great Eiruv Dispute: Rav vs. Shmuel

Rabbi Shimon
Roofs, courtyards, and enclosures are all one domain for vessels that were inside when Shabbat began — carry freely between them.
Rav
Halakha follows Rabbi Shimon only if residents did not establish a separate eiruv. If they did establish an eiruv, they may not move objects from houses to the courtyard.
Shmuel
Halakha follows Rabbi Shimon whether or not an eiruv was established — the leniency applies in all cases.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Who whispered to you that there is a difference? Rabbi Shimon's ruling applies unconditionally — eiruv or no eiruv.
Core Dispute Does establishing a separate eiruv forfeit Rabbi Shimon's leniency of treating all elevated/open areas as one domain?

🏗️ Rabbi Shimon's Domain Rules

✅ One Domain — Permitted

  • Roofs of multiple properties
  • Courtyards opening to each other
  • Enclosures adjoining courtyards
  • Porticos and balconies
  • Vessels already outside at start of Shabbat
  • Carrying between all these areas freely

❌ Not One Domain — Prohibited

  • Vessels that began Shabbat inside a house
  • Bringing house-vessels into courtyard then to roof
  • House interiors remain separate private domains
  • The leniency applies only to the outer/shared spaces
  • A house retains its distinct domain status

🚫 Rav Ḥisda's Objection & Resolution

Rav Ḥisda
Strong objection! If Shmuel is right, people will see two vessels in the same courtyard — one that was there at Shabbat's start, one that came from the house — and think both may be carried to the roof. This causes dangerous confusion!
Gemara
Rabbi Shimon follows his consistent reasoning: he does not issue decrees for such concerns. As in the mishna of three courtyards — he permitted carrying from the middle to the outer without decreeing against confusion with the other outer.
Principle
Rabbi Shimon's entire halakhic approach: אין גוזרין גזירה לגזירה — we do not make decrees to prevent violations of other decrees. He consistently refuses to restrict valid actions due to fear of confusion.
Rabbi Shimon's Method Unlike the Rabbis, Rabbi Shimon does not enact protective fences around his leniencies — he trusts people to distinguish the permitted from the forbidden.

📜 The Time of Danger — Carrying the Torah Scroll

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi recalls: When studying with Rabbi Shimon in Tekoa, they carried oil and a towel from courtyard to roof and from roof to courtyard across domains
↓
Rav Yehuda cites another incident: During a time of danger (religious persecution), they carried a Torah scroll from courtyard → roof → courtyard → enclosure
↓
The Sages object: "An incident during a time of danger is no proof!" — emergency conditions permit carrying even where rabbinic law ordinarily forbids it
↓
Principle established: שעת הסכנה שאני — A time of danger is different. Actions taken under duress to preserve Torah or life cannot serve as precedent for normal Shabbat practice
↓
Conclusion: These dramatic incidents support Rabbi Shimon's view conceptually, but the halakhic source must come from his actual ruling, not from emergency behavior

🔀 When Can You Carry Between Courtyards & Roofs?

Did residents establish a separate eiruv within their courtyard?
YES — Eiruv established
Rav: Cannot bring vessels from house to courtyard (eiruv creates binding — domain rules apply)
Shmuel: Still permitted under Rabbi Shimon
NO — No eiruv at all
All agree: roof, courtyard, enclosure = one domain per Rabbi Shimon
Carry vessels freely between them
↓
In either case: vessels that began Shabbat inside a house may NOT be taken out to the shared areas
