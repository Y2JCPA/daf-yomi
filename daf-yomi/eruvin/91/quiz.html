Eruvin 91 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Shimon, which vessels may be carried freely between roofs, courtyards, and enclosures on Shabbat?
A. Any vessel, regardless of where it was at the start of Shabbat
B. Only vessels that were already in those outdoor areas when Shabbat began
C. Only vessels belonging to the majority of residents
D. Vessels carried during a time of danger only
Correct: B — Rabbi Shimon permits carrying only vessels that were already in the roofs, courtyards, or enclosures at the start of Shabbat. Vessels that began Shabbat inside a house remain bound by the house's domain and may not be brought out.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rav's condition for the halakha to follow Rabbi Shimon's leniency?
A. The areas must be enclosed by a wall of at least ten tefachim
B. There must be a minimum of three residents in each courtyard
C. The residents must not have established a separate eiruv for themselves
D. The Shabbat must be a regular weekday Shabbat, not Yom Tov
Correct: C — Rav rules that the halakha follows Rabbi Shimon only when the residents did NOT establish a separate eiruv. If they established an eiruv, it creates distinct domains and they may not move objects from their houses into the courtyard.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why do the Sages reject the incident of carrying a Torah scroll during a time of danger as halakhic proof?
A. The incident occurred before Rabbi Shimon's ruling was known
B. Carrying a Torah scroll is always permitted regardless of Shabbat laws
C. A time of danger permits what is ordinarily rabbinically prohibited, so it cannot serve as precedent
D. The Torah scroll was being carried for the purpose of a mitzvah, which is always allowed
Correct: C — שעת הסכנה שאני — a time of danger is categorically different. Emergency conditions suspend rabbinic prohibitions to protect life and Torah. Actions taken in such circumstances cannot establish precedent for normal Shabbat conduct.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara answer Rav Ḥisda's objection about people confusing courtyard-vessels with house-vessels?
A. People are educated enough to know the difference in most communities
B. Rabbi Shimon consistently does not issue protective decrees — this is his standard approach
C. A sign can be posted in the courtyard to indicate which vessels came from the house
D. The confusion only applies to vessels that look similar, which is uncommon
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that Rabbi Shimon consistently refuses to enact preventive decrees (גזירות) to forestall confusion. This is demonstrated by the mishna of three courtyards, where Rabbi Shimon permitted carrying without worrying about people confusing the outer courtyards with each other.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What inference do the baraitot (91b) support regarding the eiruv dispute between Rav and Shmuel?
A. They support Shmuel's view that eiruv status is irrelevant to Rabbi Shimon's leniency
B. They are inconclusive and support neither position
C. They support Rav's view that the leniency applies only when no eiruv was established
D. They show that Rabbi Shimon and the Rabbis agree in all cases
Correct: C — The baraitot phrase the permitted case as when residents "forgot and did not establish an eiruv." The Gemara infers: the reason it is permitted is that they did NOT establish an eiruv — implying that if they HAD, it would be prohibited. This supports Rav's position.
