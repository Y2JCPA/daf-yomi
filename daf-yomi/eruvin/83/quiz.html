QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Tosefta, what is the minimum amount of food that contracts ritual impurity?
A. One full egg-bulk
B. Half a peras — approximately two egg-bulks
C. One-quarter of a standard loaf
D. One full omer
Correct: B — The Tosefta teaches that one-eighth of the standard loaf equals half a peras, roughly two egg-bulks — the minimum measure for contracting the impurity of foods.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How many egg-bulks are in a Tzippori se'a, as measured by Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi?
A. 144
B. 173
C. 207
D. 432
Correct: C — The Tzippori se'a is 207 egg-bulks. It is larger than the Jerusalem se'a (173) by one-sixth, and larger than the wilderness se'a (144) by approximately one-third.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rav's ruling about a shared area that one courtyard can reach only by lowering and the other only by throwing?
A. It belongs to the courtyard that lowers — lowering is easier
B. It belongs to the courtyard that throws — throwing shows more effort
C. It is permitted for both courtyards to use freely
D. It is prohibited for both sets of residents
Correct: D — Rav rules it is prohibited for both. Since neither courtyard has convenient access, they hold equal — and competing — claims over the area, rendering it forbidden to each.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The Gemara derives the ḥalla minimum from the manna. What fraction of an eifa is one omer?
A. One-sixth
B. One-fifth
C. One-tenth
D. One-third
Correct: C — The verse states explicitly: 'an omer is the tenth part of an eifa' (Exodus 16:36). This gives the omer as 43⅕ egg-bulks, establishing the minimum dough quantity for ḥalla separation.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Mishna, under what two conditions does an embankment near a courtyard belong to the balcony residents?
A. It must be at least four handbreadths high and dry
B. It must be at least ten handbreadths high and located near the balcony
C. It must be below ten handbreadths and distant from the balcony
D. It always belongs to the balcony regardless of height
Correct: B — The Mishna requires both height (≥10 handbreadths) and proximity (near the balcony). If the embankment is distant from the balcony, it belongs to the courtyard even if it exceeds ten handbreadths.
