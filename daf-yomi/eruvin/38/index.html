⚖️ Can One Make Two Eiruvin for Festival Adjacent to Shabbat?

Rabbi Eliezer
Shabbat and Festival are שתי קדושות — two distinct sanctities. One may establish two eiruvin and say: 'My eiruv on the first day shall be to the east, on the second day to the west.'
Rabbis
Impossible! He must either establish an eiruv in one direction for both days, or establish no eiruv at all. The two days cannot be split.
Rabbi Eliezer
Proof: If the eiruv is eaten on day one, it works for day one but fails for day two — showing the days are legally distinct!
Rabbis
That merely shows the food ran out. Also: you cannot establish an eiruv on Yom Tov for Shabbat — that itself proves nothing about sanctity.
Key Concept שתי קדושות (Sh'tei Kedushot) — Two Sanctities: the question whether Shabbat and Yom Tov, when consecutive, share one legal status or constitute two independent domains

📜 How to Establish Eiruv for Both Days

Eve of the first day (e.g., Yom Tov eve): Bring eiruv bread to the spot you wish to establish as your residence — 2,000 cubits in the desired direction
↓
Remain there until nightfall — the eiruv acquires residence automatically at the onset of bein hashmashot (twilight), before the holy day begins
↓
If eiruv is intact on Day 2: Valid for both days — you may walk the full 2,000 cubits in that direction on each day
↓
If eiruv bread is eaten on Day 1: Effective for Day 1 only — on Day 2 his residence reverts to his home, and he is limited to 2,000 cubits from there
↓
Rabbis: He may only choose one direction for both days, or no eiruv at all. Rabbi Eliezer permits splitting the two days since they are two sanctities.

⚔️ The Proofs: Are the Two Days Distinct?

Rabbi Eliezer
Surely you agree one cannot establish an eiruv for half-a-day north and half-a-day south? Because one day = one sanctity — so by the same logic, two days = two sanctities!
Rabbis
Agreed on one day — but two consecutive holy days are also one sanctity together. The eiruv that took effect at the first twilight should carry through both days.
Rabbi Eliezer
Don't you agree that one who established an eiruv with his feet on Day 1 must nonetheless establish a new eiruv for Day 2? This proves they are separate!
Rabbis
Yes — but that is because one may not establish an eiruv on Yom Tov for Shabbat due to the prohibition of hakhanah (preparation), not because the days are legally distinct.
Rabbi Eliezer's Reply The hakhanah prohibition is real — but both reasons coexist: the days are two sanctities AND preparing on one for the other is forbidden. The two principles are independent.

🥚 Contradiction: Rav's Egg vs. Rav's Two-Sanctities Ruling

Rav Ḥisda
Contradiction! Rav ruled the halakha follows two-sanctities — yet Rav himself said: an egg laid on a Festival is prohibited on the adjacent Shabbat. That implies one sanctity!
Rabba
No contradiction. The egg is prohibited not because of single sanctity — but because of הכנה (hakhanah): the Festival day 'prepared' the egg for Shabbat, which is forbidden by the Torah.
Abaye
But then how can the Mishna permit establishing an eiruv on Yom Tov for Shabbat? Wouldn't placing the eiruv bread on Yom Tov also be forbidden hakhanah?
Rabba
The eiruv acquires residence at the very beginning of twilight — before Shabbat. It is not an act of preparation during Yom Tov for Shabbat; it takes effect automatically and simultaneously with the onset of the holy day.
Baraita: Source of Hakhanah Prohibition "On the sixth day they shall prepare what they bring in" (Exodus 16:5) — one may prepare on a weekday for Shabbat, but NOT on Shabbat or Festival for the other holy day

🔀 Two Sanctities vs. One Sanctity: Final Comparison

Rabbi Eliezer / Four Elders (Rav's Ruling)

  • Shabbat + Festival = שתי קדושות — two distinct legal domains
  • Two separate eiruvin may be established, one per day
  • Eiruv with feet on Day 1 must be renewed for Day 2
  • Hakhanah still forbidden — egg laid on Festival prohibited on Shabbat
  • Eiruv placement: acquires at twilight onset, not 'preparation'

The Rabbis

  • Shabbat + Festival = קדושה אחת — one unified sanctity
  • Only one eiruv direction valid for both days, or no eiruv at all
  • Eiruv with feet on Day 1 carries over automatically to Day 2
  • Cannot split the two days into separate legal domains
  • Egg prohibition: flows from the shared single-sanctity status
