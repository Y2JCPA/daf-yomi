Eruvin 38 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Eliezer, what is the legal status of Shabbat and an adjacent Festival?
A. They share a single sanctity; only one eiruv is permitted for both
B. They are two distinct sanctities; two separate eiruvin may be established
C. They are treated identically to two regular weekdays
D. No eiruv may be established at all when they are adjacent
Correct: B — Rabbi Eliezer holds that Shabbat and Yom Tov are שתי קדושות — two distinct sanctities — and therefore one may establish two eiruvin, designating a different direction for each day.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the Mishna, what happens if the eiruv bread is eaten on the first day (Yom Tov)?
A. The eiruv is entirely invalid — both days are lost
B. The eiruv remains valid for both days since it worked at twilight
C. The eiruv is valid for the first day but not the second day
D. He must immediately replace the eiruv before Shabbat begins
Correct: C — The Mishna rules: if the eiruv was eaten on the first day, it is effective for that day only. On the second day he has no eiruv and reverts to his home location as his Shabbat base.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How did Rabba resolve the apparent contradiction between Rav's two-sanctities ruling and Rav's ruling that an egg laid on Yom Tov is forbidden on Shabbat?
A. Rav actually held the two days were one sanctity and later retracted
B. The egg is prohibited due to hakhanah (preparation), not because of single sanctity
C. Eggs are a special case of muktzeh unrelated to sanctity at all
D. The egg ruling applies only when Yom Tov falls on Friday, not Sunday
Correct: B — Rabba distinguishes the two rulings: the egg is forbidden not because the days share one sanctity, but due to the independent prohibition of hakhanah — the Festival 'prepared' the egg for Shabbat, which the Torah forbids.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does establishing an eiruv teḥumin on Yom Tov for Shabbat NOT violate the hakhanah prohibition?
A. Because eiruv teḥumin is Rabbinic while hakhanah is Biblical — Rabbinic overrides here
B. Because the eiruv acquires one's residence at the very onset of twilight, not during the day
C. Because the person merely walks there and performs no physical act of preparation
D. Because Rabbi Eliezer issued an explicit exemption for eiruv teḥumin
Correct: B — Rabba explains: the eiruv acquires residence at the very beginning of bein hashmashot — it takes effect simultaneously with the onset of the holy day, not as an act performed during one holy day to prepare for another.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rav ruled that halakha follows four Elders who agreed with Rabbi Eliezer. Which of the following was NOT among those four?
A. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
B. Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel
C. Rabbi Yishmael son of Rabbi Yoḥanan ben Beroka
D. Rabbi Akiva
Correct: D — The four Elders cited are Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel, Rabbi Yishmael son of Rabbi Yoḥanan ben Beroka, and (implicitly) Rabbi Eliezer himself. Rabbi Akiva does not appear in this sugya at all.
