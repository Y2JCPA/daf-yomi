QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ḥanina, at what level of intoxication is a person NOT legally responsible for their actions?
A. Any level after drinking two cups of wine
B. When they reach the level of intoxication of Lot
C. When they cannot walk in a straight line
D. When a physician certifies them as fully incapacitated
Correct: B — Rabbi Ḥanina taught that a drunk is legally responsible for all actions unless he reaches 'the level of Lot' — completely unaware of what he is doing, as Lot was with his daughters (Genesis 19). This sets an extremely high bar for exemption.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the dispute between Rabbi Ḥanina and Rabbi Yoḥanan about 'a shield at a time of arrogance'?
A. Whether one should carry a physical shield in the marketplace
B. Whether the shield metaphor refers to Torah study or prayer
C. Whether one should cover/suppress (afik) or draw out/reveal (mapik) the shield
D. Whether arrogance is ever permitted for Torah scholars
Correct: C — Rabbi Ḥanina holds that one who 'covers' himself (afik) — suppressing arrogance — is protected from trouble. Rabbi Yoḥanan holds one must 'draw out' (mapik) the shield — actively deploying it. Both agree the result is that troubles are sealed away.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak says halakhic study requires clarity 'as on a day when a north wind blows.' What does Abaye add in the same spirit?
A. That one should study only at sunrise when the mind is freshest
B. That drinking a small amount of wine sharpens halakhic memory
C. That if his stepmother asked him to bring kutaḥ, he could no longer study normally
D. That the Shema must be recited before any serious study session
Correct: C — Abaye illustrated how even minor domestic disruptions — his stepmother asking him to fetch kutaḥ (a pungent condiment) — shattered his concentration. This supports Rav Naḥman's principle that a perfectly clear, undisturbed mind is needed for sharp halakhic analysis.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the two-courtyard eruvin case, whose combined opinions does Rav Huna say Rav's ruling reflects?
A. Rabbi Yehuda and Rabbi Shimon
B. Rabbi Meir and Rabbi Eliezer ben Yaakov
C. Rabbi Eliezer ben Yaakov (for the gentile) and Rabbi Akiva (for the Jew)
D. Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel
Correct: C — Rav Huna resolves the debate by showing Rav follows Rabbi Eliezer ben Yaakov regarding the gentile's disqualifying presence (he must actually reside there) and Rabbi Akiva regarding the inner Jew's foot — which renders carrying in the outer courtyard prohibited even though he is permitted in his own domain.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rabbi Ḥiyya teaches that one who stays clear-minded after drinking wine has 'the mindset of 70 Elders.' What is his numerical allusion?
A. The Sanhedrin had exactly 70 members who were known wine drinkers
B. Wine must be aged 70 days before it has full halakhic status
C. The word יין (wine) has a gematria value of 70 (yod+yod+nun = 10+10+50)
D. There were 70 grape clusters in the Temple's golden vine decoration
Correct: C — Rabbi Ḥiyya's allusion is linguistic: the Hebrew word יין (wine) equals 70 in gematria (yod=10, yod=10, nun=50). One who can drink wine and remain mentally sharp has, in a sense, absorbed the full 70 — the measure of Elder-wisdom.
