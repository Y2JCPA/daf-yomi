🍷 A Daf of Wine, Mind & Law

כָּל שֶׁדַּעְתּוֹ מְיוּשֶּׁבֶת עָלָיו בְּיֵינוֹ — יֵשׁ בּוֹ דַּעַת כְּשִׁבְעִים זְקֵנִים
Central Theme Eruvin 65 weaves two major threads: intoxication and mental clarity — when is a drunk legally and spiritually responsible? — and eruvin law — how does a gentile resident affect carrying rights in linked courtyards? The daf opens with Rabbi Elazar ben Azarya's bold claim that the world deserves acquittal since the Temple's destruction, because Israel is perpetually 'drunk' from suffering, and closes with tannaitic debates over renting from gentile innkeepers on Shabbat.

🛡️ Cover or Draw Out? The Shield Debate

Rabbi Ḥanina
Whoever covers [afik] himself with a shield at a time of arrogance — troubles will be closed and sealed from him. Suppress the evil inclination when intoxicated or proud.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
That is not the correct reading. Whoever draws out [mapik] a shield at a time of arrogance — troubles will be closed and sealed from him. One must actively reveal and deploy the shield — not merely hide it away.
Rabbi Ḥanina
Proof from Job 6:15 — 'like the channel [afik] of wadis' — afik denotes channeling/passing, i.e., directing the inclination away from harm.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Proof from Psalms 18:16 — 'the channels [afikei] of waters were seen' — the word means revealing: actively bringing the shield into view against the evil inclination.
Practical Difference The dispute turns on whether one who was angry and suppressed himself did well (Rabbi Ḥanina) versus one who actively expressed and deployed restraint (Rabbi Yoḥanan). Both agree: mastering oneself during arrogance shields a person from trouble.

🍺 Degrees of Intoxication & Legal Responsibility

Ordinary Intoxication

  • Acquisition is binding
  • Sale is valid and cannot be retracted
  • Legally responsible for all actions
  • Punishable for sins committed
  • Standard drunk: impaired but still aware of actions

Lot-Level Intoxication

  • Completely unaware of what he is doing
  • Not legally responsible for his acts
  • Named after Lot who 'did not know' (Gen. 19:33)
  • Rabbi Ḥanina: only at this extreme is one truly exempt
  • A very high — practically unreachable — evidentiary bar

Wine as Spiritual Barometer

  • Appeased by wine → shares the Creator's mindset (Gen. 8:21)
  • Clear-minded after wine → mind of 70 Elders
  • Wine [יין] = numerical value 70 (yod+yod+nun)
  • Wine created for mourners and to reward the wicked in this world
  • Wine flowing like water in the home → house is blessed

🧠 Factors That Disrupt Prayer & Torah Study

Anger or Distress — Rav: 'One whose mind is unsettled should not pray.' Rabbi Ḥanina did not pray on days he was angry, because distress disqualifies proper intention.
↓
Road Fatigue — Rabbi Elazar: one returning from a journey must not pray for three days to recover, citing Ezra 8:15. Shmuel's father observed this scrupulously.
↓
Scent of Wine in the Room — Shmuel would not pray in a house containing alcoholic beverage; even the smell disrupted his concentration.
↓
Personal Irritants — Abaye: if his stepmother asked him to bring kutaḥ (a pungent condiment), he could no longer study normally. Rava: even a single louse bite ended his learning session.
↓
Overcast Skies vs. North Wind — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak: 'The study of halakha requires clarity, as on a day when a north wind blows and clears the skies.'
↓
Night vs. Day — Rav Yehuda: night was made only for sleep. Rav Naḥman: 'We Torah scholars are day workers.' Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov 'borrowed and repaid' any missed daytime study from the night.

🏠 Eruvin: Two Courtyards, a Jew & a Gentile

Setup: Inner courtyard — one Jew + one Gentile. Outer courtyard — one Jew alone. All rent from the same gentile landlord.
↓
Rav's Ruling: The outer Jew cannot carry freely. The inner Jew must first rent rights from the inner gentile — otherwise carrying is forbidden throughout.
↓
Rabbi Meir?
Rejected. Rabbi Meir holds a single Jew suffices to prohibit carrying — no need to posit a gentile at all.
Rabbi Akiva?
Partially fits. A Jew's 'foot' in his own domain renders another domain prohibited. But then why mention the gentile?
Rav Huna's Resolution ✓
Accepted. Rav follows R. Eliezer b. Yaakov for the gentile (must reside there to prohibit) + R. Akiva for the inner Jew (foot renders outer domain prohibited).
↓
Guiding Principle: 'Give to a wise man and he will be yet wiser' (Prov. 9:9) — when in doubt about a gentile's presence, be stringent and require rental.

📋 Eruvin 65 — Summary Map

Intoxication & Responsibility
Ordinary drunk: legally liable. Lot-level drunk: exempt. The world has been 'drunk' from suffering since the Temple's destruction — a basis for divine leniency.
↓
Wine Reveals Character
Cup, pocket, and anger are the three tests. Wine given numerical value 70 = the wisdom of the Elders. Appeased by wine → shares the Creator's disposition.
↓
Clarity for Study
North wind, daytime, no lice, no pungent smells — only then is halakhic reasoning at its sharpest.
Fitness for Prayer
No prayer when angry, road-weary, or distracted by wine's scent. Mind must be settled before standing before God.
↓
Eruvin: Courtyards & Gentiles
Rav (via Rav Huna): follow R. Eliezer b. Yaakov for the gentile + R. Akiva for the Jew's foot. Rent from the gentile when in doubt. Leniency is allowed for doubtful rabbinic prohibitions at an inn.
