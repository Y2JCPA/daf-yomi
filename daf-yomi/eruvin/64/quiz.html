QUESTION 1 OF 5
Rava rejected making an announcement to explain a symbolic eiruv. What was his specific objection?
A. Announcements during Shabbat are rabbinically prohibited
B. Children who don't hear or understand the announcement will be confused about the law later
C. The gentile would overhear the announcement and be insulted
D. Rava held that no eiruv at all should be made in an alleyway with a gentile
Correct: B — Rava pointed out that even a public announcement cannot fix the problem — children won't hear or comprehend it, and they will grow up having seen a functioning eiruv with a gentile, which will mislead them.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the key halakhic distinction between one who 'drank wine' (שָׁתָה) and one who is 'intoxicated' (שִׁיכּוֹר) regarding prayer?
A. Both are forbidden to pray; if either prayed, the prayer is invalid
B. The intoxicated person may pray; the one who merely drank wine may not
C. One who drank wine should not pray but if he did it counts; the intoxicated person's prayer is a תוֹעֵבָה and must be repeated
D. There is no practical distinction — both must wait until fully sober
Correct: C — Rabba bar Rav Huna teaches: one who drank wine should ideally not pray, but his prayer is valid b'dieved. An intoxicated person's prayer, however, is called a toevah (abomination) and he is obligated to repeat it when sober.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Rava challenge Rav Naḥman for selectively approving only some of Rav Yehuda's rulings in Shmuel's name?
A. Rav Naḥman was a student of Rav Yehuda and was obligated to accept all his rulings
B. The verse teaches that one cannot praise one teaching from a sage while disparaging another from the same source
C. Both rulings had been approved by the majority of Amoraim before Rav Naḥman's time
D. Rav Naḥman himself was intoxicated when he made the selective approval
Correct: B — Rava cited Rabbi Aḥa bar Ḥanina's teaching derived from Proverbs 29:3 — it is inconsistent to praise the wisdom of one ruling from a sage while disparaging another ruling from the same sage in the same tradition.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How did Rabban Gamliel know to call out the name 'Mavgai' to a gentile stranger he had never met?
A. Rabbi Elai had previously introduced Mavgai to Gamliel in another city
B. Mavgai wore a name tag identifying him as a guardian of the nearby towns
C. Rabban Gamliel possessed divine inspiration (ruach hakodesh)
D. Mavgai was the generic term for a Gentile road-guardian in that region
Correct: C — The Gemara states explicitly: 'At that time we learned that Rabban Gamliel divined the gentile's name by way of divine inspiration (ruach hakodesh) that rested upon him.'
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The Gemara asks why Rabban Gamliel needed to walk three mil to sober up, when earlier it said one mil suffices. How does the Gemara resolve this?
A. Gamliel had drunk more than a quarter-log, which requires more distance
B. Italian wine is uniquely potent and intoxicating, requiring three mil rather than the usual one mil
C. Gamliel was elderly and his body processed wine more slowly
D. He was riding a donkey rather than walking, so the same distance had less effect
Correct: B — The Gemara resolves the apparent contradiction: ordinary wine requires only one mil to dispel, but Italian wine (יין איטלקי) is stronger and more intoxicating, requiring the greater distance of three mil to fully clear one's head.
