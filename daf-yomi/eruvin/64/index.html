🏠 Solving the Gentile Neighbor Problem

A gentile lives in the alleyway — Jewish residents cannot carry freely without addressing his presence
↓
Rav Huna's suggestion
Make a reminder eiruv anyway — symbolic, no legal function
Rava rejects
Observers will mistake it for a valid eiruv with a gentile — causes greater error
↓
Rava's ruling: One neighbor befriends the gentile and obtains permission to use part of his domain — the gentile becomes legally like a householder who ceded his rights
3 / 9

🏠 The Gentile Neighbor Debate

Rav Huna b. R. Yehoshua
They should establish an eiruv anyway as a reminder of eiruvin laws, even though it serves no halakhic purpose in this case.
Rava
That creates a worse problem — observers will mistakenly conclude that an eiruv with a gentile is halakhically valid!
Rav Huna b. R. Yehoshua
Then make an announcement to the community explaining the situation.
Rava
Can we make announcements for the children? They won't understand, and they'll grow up confused about the law.
Rava (final ruling)
One neighbor should befriend the gentile, receive permission to use a space in his domain, and thus legally neutralize his presence entirely.
Bottom Line Rava's solution is the only approach that prevents both the halakhic error and the communal misunderstanding.

🍷 שָׁתָה vs. שִׁיכּוֹר — Drank Wine vs. Intoxicated

שָׁתָה — Drank Wine

  • Should not pray l'chatchila
  • If he prayed, his prayer is valid (b'dieved)
  • Can still speak coherently before a king
  • Walking one mil or brief sleep dispels effect
  • May not issue halakhic rulings

שִׁיכּוֹר — Intoxicated

  • Must absolutely not pray
  • If he prayed, his prayer is a תוֹעֵבָה (abomination) — must repeat it
  • Cannot speak coherently before a king
  • Italian wine requires three mil to dispel
  • Absolutely forbidden to rule or dissolve vows

🍷 Rav Naḥman and Rav Yehuda's Rulings — The Selective Approval Problem

Rav Yehuda citing Shmuel: (1) A gentile's hired laborer may contribute to the eiruv in his master's place. (2) One who drank a quarter-log of wine may not issue a halakhic ruling.
↓
Rav Naḥman: Approves ruling #1 (laborer and eiruv) but does not approve ruling #2 (wine and ruling)
↓
Rava challenges: Rabbi Aḥa bar Ḥanina cites Proverbs 29:3 — "He who keeps company with prostitutes destroys wealth" — one cannot praise one teaching from a sage while disparaging another
↓
Accept both rulings
The laborer eiruv ruling AND the wine-ruling prohibition are both valid from Shmuel
Reject selectively
Inconsistent — Rava implies Rav Naḥman must either accept or reject both teachings together

🛤️ Rabban Gamliel's Journey: Akko to Keziv

Rabban Gamliel rides from Akko toward Keziv; his student Rabbi Elai accompanies him
↓
Gamliel spots a loaf on the road and calls out: "Mavgai! Do not touch that loaf!" — to a stranger he had never met
↓
Rabbi Elai stays behind and confirms: the gentile's name really is Mavgai, from the towns of the guardsmen — Gamliel never knew him
↓
Lesson: Rabban Gamliel possessed רוּחַ הַקֹּדֶשׁ (divine inspiration) — he divined the stranger's name supernaturally
↓
They arrive in Keziv; a man asks Gamliel to dissolve his vow — Gamliel asks Rabbi Elai: "Did I drink a quarter-log of Italian wine earlier?"
↓
The petitioner must follow Gamliel for three mil until he arrives at the Ladder of Tyre and sobers up
↓
Gamliel dismounts, wraps himself in his shawl in judicial fashion, and dissolves the vow using the verse: "There is one who speaks like the piercings of a sword" (Proverbs 12:18)
