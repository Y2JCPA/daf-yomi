🥬 Raw Beets: May They Be Used for Eiruv?

Rav Ḥisda
Raw beet kills a healthy person — one may not establish an eiruv with raw beets.
Questioner
But we see people eating raw beets and they are not dying!
Gemara
Those are people who are already ill — raw beet merely hastens death for them; it is still dangerous for the healthy.
Rav Ḥisda
Cooked beets, however, are beneficial: good for the heart, good for the eyes, and all the more so for the intestines.
Abaye
That benefit applies only when the pot makes a tukh tukh sound — it must be fully cooked.
Ruling Raw and partially-cooked beets are forbidden for eiruv — they are physically dangerous. Fully cooked beets are permitted and even healthful.

🧅 Onions for Eiruv: Leaves vs. Bulb

Forbidden — Onion Leaves

  • Leaves contain dangerous toxins
  • Cannot be used to establish an eiruv
  • R. Shimon ben Elazar: forbidden
  • Especially toxic when bulb has not yet grown to a span
  • Incident: R. Ḥanina ate half an onion with its toxins and became deathly ill
  • Remedy: drinking beer afterward neutralizes toxin (Rav Pappa)

Permitted — Onion Bulb

  • The bulb of the onion is fit for eating
  • May be used to establish an eiruv
  • Permitted once the bulb has grown to a span (thumb to little finger)
  • Mishna permitting onions for eiruv refers to bulbs
  • The two rulings are reconciled: different parts of the onion
  • Measure: enough to eat with food of two meals

🍺 Beer for Eiruv — Rav Aḥa's Rejected Proof

Question: How much beer is needed to establish an eiruv?
↓
Rav Aḥa's reasoning: Shabbat carrying law requires 4× as much beer as wine (¼-log wine vs. 1-log beer) → apply 4:1 ratio to eiruv → need half a log of beer
↓
Rav Aḥa's Claim
Beer = 4× wine for eiruv → half-log required
Gemara Rejects
Shabbat ratio inflated because less than ¼-log beer is insignificant for carrying. For eiruv, beer remains a significant drink — no 4× multiplier
↓
The eiruv measure for beer is not derived from Shabbat carrying ratios — it stands independently

💊 Food & Healing: Abaye's Mother's Remedies

Roasted grains (kisanei) — good for the heart and drive away worrisome thoughts. Two Pumbeditan bunei measures needed for eiruv.
↓
Cooked beets (Rav Ḥisda) — beneficial for the heart, eyes, and intestines, but only when fully cooked (tukh tukh sound).
↓
Weakness of the heart: Bring meat of the right thigh of a ram and dung of grazing cattle from the month of Nisan.
↓
Onion toxin antidote (Rav Pappa): Drinking beer after eating onion leaves neutralizes the harmful toxins.
↓
Persians eat roasted meat (tabahakki) without bread — Rav Yosef cites this as proof that meat alone constitutes food; Abaye objects: Persians are not the majority of the world.
