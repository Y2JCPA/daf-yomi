🏠 How Much of a Rooftop May One Carry On?

Rabbi Shimon
Rooftops, courtyards, and enclosures are all one domain for vessels present when Shabbat began — carry freely between all of them.
Rabbis
Each roof is its own separate domain. Rooftops rarely frequented — Sages did not prohibit carrying between them, but each is distinct.
Rav
Per the Rabbis: one may carry on each roof only within four cubits. Walls do not extend upward to create virtual partitions.
Shmuel
Per the Rabbis: one may carry throughout the entire roof — גוד אסיק מחיצתא, the walls below extend upward creating complete virtual domains.
Key Principle גוד אסיק מחיצתא — 'Extend and raise the partitions' — house walls are treated as rising upward to the sky, creating virtual partitions between adjacent rooftops. Rav rejects this principle here; Shmuel applies it.

📜 Mishna: Three Opinions on the Rooftop Domain

Rabbi Shimon

  • Rooftops, courtyards, and enclosures = one unified domain
  • Vessels present at the start of Shabbat may be carried freely between all
  • NOT one domain for vessels brought from inside a house — those are restricted

Rabbis (baseline)

  • Each roof is its own discrete domain
  • Rooftops infrequently used — Sages did not add a fence-law prohibiting between-roof carrying
  • Disagreement (Rav vs. Shmuel) on how much of each roof one may use

Rabbi Meir

  • All rooftops form a single domain — carry freely across all
  • Two areas that are halakhically equivalent merge into one domain
  • Exception: fixed objects (pillars, millstone bases) — treated like a mound in public domain

🔲 What Is Rabbi Meir's Rationale? Abaye Resolves the Puzzle

Abaye bar Avin & R. Ḥanina bar Avin
The Rabbis say: divided below (houses) → divided above (rooftops). But Rabbi Meir permits carrying on all rooftops! If he also holds 'divided above,' why the leniency?
Abaye
Rav Yitzchak bar Avdimi quoted Rabbi Meir: Wherever two areas constitute two separate domains yet are halakhically treated as one, carrying is permitted throughout both.
Abaye bar Avin & R. Ḥanina bar Avin
Then Rabbi Meir would prohibit adjusting a burden on an overturned mortar or vat in a private domain — they form a 'second domain within a domain'!
Abaye (quoting Rabba)
Rabbi Meir spoke only of a fixed pillar or millstone base. People assign these a permanent place — like a public-domain mound, they are rarely moved and thus create a legally distinct zone.
Bottom Line Fixed, permanent objects (pillars, millstone bases) → carrying restricted between them and the surrounding area. Moveable objects (mortar, overturned vat) → no restriction, since they have no designated fixed place.

🧠 Rav Yosef Forgets His Learning — and Recovers It

After his illness, Rav Yosef says: 'I have not heard this halakha of Shmuel regarding rooftops.'
↓
Abaye reminds him: 'You taught us yourself — Rav Yehuda said in Shmuel's name: This applies only when there are residents on both rooftops. The virtual partition of the small roof is treated as a trampled pathway — carrying on it is prohibited.'
↓
The recalled principle: If there are no residents on either rooftop, carrying on both is fully permitted (virtual walls extend upward unimpeded).
↓
Rav Yosef corrects the tradition: 'I remember now — I spoke not of residents but of actual physical partitions: the prohibition applies only when there is a real partition on all four sides of both the large and small roof.'
↓
Final ruling: If the large roof has a full enclosing partition but the small roof does not, residents of the small roof may carry on all the rooftops — the small roof merges into the larger domain.

🪜 Ladders and Dakka: When Can You Carry on All Rooftops?

Does the rooftop owner have a permanent ladder affixed to his roof, while neighbors do not?
↓
✅ Yes — ladder affixed (neighbors have none)
Permitted to carry on all neighboring rooftops. The failure to erect ladders shows neighbors have no claim of access (Rav Naḥman).
❓ No ladder — check for a dakka
Owner built an upper story and erected a dakka (small partition, 4 tefachim high) at the roof entrance. Which way does it face?
↓
Dakka opens toward other rooftops
Permitted — the dakka opening toward neighboring rooftops acts as a symbolic enclosure embracing them. Owner may carry on all rooftops. (Abaye)
Dakka faces the garden; sides toward rooftops are sealed
Prohibited — the sealed sides create a closed private domain. The owner is shut off from neighboring rooftops and may NOT carry on them. (Rava)
