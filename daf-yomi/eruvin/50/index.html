📖 Overview

כָּל שֶׁאֵינוֹ יָכוֹל לַעֲשׂוֹתוֹ בְּזֶה אַחַר זֶה — אֲפִילּוּ בְּבַת אַחַת אֵינוֹ יָכוֹל לַעֲשׂוֹתוֹ
⚡ Daf Theme Rabba formulates a sweeping principle: "Anything that cannot be accomplished sequentially — even simultaneously it cannot be accomplished." The Gemara then stress-tests this rule against grain tithes, animal tithes, and thanks-offering loaves — finding exceptions in each case that illuminate the principle's precise limits.
3 / 9

🌳 Two Versions of Rabba's Explanation for Rav

Rabba (v.1)
Rav's ruling is because the place is not precisely defined. When one says 'beneath the tree,' he has not specified which four cubits under its canopy he intends — so no residence is acquired.
Rabba (v.2)
Rav's ruling is because of the sequential principle: one cannot simultaneously designate a tree AND a spot beneath it, since you cannot perform these two acts one after the other — the tree itself cannot be one's eruv location.
Gemara
Nafka mina (practical difference): He says 'my eruv is in four cubits of so-and-so's place.' Version 1 → still invalid (location undefined among the four cubits). Version 2 → valid, since the sequential principle does not block this designation.
📌 Key Distinction Only version 2 (sequential principle) would permit "four cubits of so-and-so's place" — because here the precise location IS derivable, removing the sequential impossibility objection.

⚡ Objections to Rabba's Sequential Principle

Rabba's Rule: What cannot be done one-after-another cannot be done all-at-once
↓
🌾 Grain Tithe
Cannot tithe each kernel sequentially — yet designating an entire pile works simultaneously!
🐄 Animal Tithe
Two beasts exit together as the tenth — both consecrated at once, not one after the other.
🍞 Todah Loaves
Eighty loaves brought when forty are required — consecration dispute between Ḥizkiya and Rabbi Yoḥanan.
↓
Each objection resolved: Grain tithe acts partially per kernel (sequential possible in theory); animal tithe permits sequential error-designation; todah — Rabbi Zeira limits the dispute to undeclared amounts.

🐄 How Each Tithe Case Escapes Rabba's Principle

🌾 Grain Tithe (Ma'aser)

  • Objection: one cannot tithe kernel by kernel across a pile
  • Yet the whole pile is tithed simultaneously — contradiction?
  • Answer: Tithe takes effect partially
  • Even half a kernel can be designated as tithe
  • Therefore sequential designation is theoretically possible → simultaneous is valid

🐄 Animal Tithe (Ma'aser Behemah)

  • Objection: two animals exit as the tenth together
  • Cannot designate tenth and eleventh sequentially ab initio
  • Answer: Sequential designation IS possible — in error
  • If the 9th is called 10th, and 10th called 10th again, all three are holy
  • Error-case sequential possibility validates simultaneous

🍞 Thanks-Offering Loaves (Todah)

  • Objection: 80 loaves brought when 40 required
  • Ḥizkiya: only 40 consecrated; Rabbi Yoḥanan: none consecrated
  • Rabbi Zeira resolves: Both agree — if he explicitly said 'forty of these eighty,' forty ARE consecrated
  • Dispute only when he designated 80 without stating how many
  • No challenge to Rabba remains

⚖️ Two Baraitot — Rav vs. Shmuel

Baraita #1 supports Rav: One who designates residence near a tree or fence must select a well-defined spot — a cave, a pit, or a plain sunken 10 handbreadths deep (4 cubits to 2 beit se'a in area).
↓
Two travelers: one knows a distant location, one does not — the unknowing one may entrust his eruv to the knowing one. If he did NOT specify near vs. far side of the tree, he may not walk toward it.
↓
Baraita #2 supports Shmuel: One who erroneously established eruv in two opposite directions simultaneously — both eruvin are void, and he may not move from his spot.
↓
Implication of Baraita #2: eruv CAN take effect without precise location specification (otherwise the 'both are void' statement is redundant — one would already be invalid per Rav).
↓
Resolution: Baraita #2 genuinely contradicts Rav — but Rav has tanna status (תַּנָּא הוּא וּפָלֵיג) and may dispute a baraita. The contradiction stands as a legitimate tannaitic dispute.
