⚓ Boundaries and Partitions: When Does an Enclosure Help?

עירובין מ״ב — תחומין, מחיצות, וספינה
Core Theme Eruvin 42 examines the interplay of Shabbat limits (techum) and enclosures (mechitzot). When gentiles build a partition on Shabbat, does it enable carrying inside? When produce is carried beyond its techum and returned, may it be eaten? The daf culminates in the boat exception — where Rabba and Rabbi Zeira debate why one may move freely aboard a boat on Shabbat, and the practical case that separates their views.

🌾 Produce Carried Beyond the Shabbat Limit

Produce carried beyond the Shabbat limit (techum Shabbat)
↓
Taken out unwittingly
May be eaten — even if not yet returned to its original place
Taken out intentionally
May not be eaten — even if returned to its place
↓
Rabbi Neḥemya: If returned to its original place — may be eaten regardless of how it was taken out
↓
Gemara clarifies: "returned to its place" means returned unwittingly — intentional return does not override the prohibition

🏕️ Valley Surrounded by a Gentile Partition on Shabbat

Shmuel
One may walk 2,000 cubits in each direction from his acquired place of residence — this is his Shabbat limit. The gentile partition benefits him for walking.
Rav Huna
Agreed on walking — but he may carry only 4 cubits, as in a karmelit. The partition built on Shabbat does not permit full carrying inside.
Rav Huna
Carrying throughout the 2,000 cubits is prohibited — the open side beyond his limit makes it like a partition breached entirely, open to a forbidden area.
Ḥiyya bar Rav
He may walk 2,000 cubits and carry freely throughout those 2,000 cubits. The partition is effective for both walking and carrying.
Rav Naḥman
Rav Huna — do not argue with Shmuel! A baraita was taught in explicit accordance with his view.
Key Reason for Restriction Rav Huna: Carrying beyond 4 cubits is prohibited lest one be drawn after the object past the 2,000-cubit limit — a rabbinic preventive measure (גזירה).

⚖️ Pen and Stable on Shabbat: The Tanna'im Dispute

Rabban Gamliel & R. Elazar ben Azarya

  • He may walk the entire pen or stable
  • The enclosure is its own Shabbat domain
  • No decree: pen walking is not limited by open-field rules
  • And certainly: may carry throughout the pen freely
  • Applies to: pen, stable, and boat alike

Rabbi Yehoshua & Rabbi Akiva

  • He has only four cubits — no more
  • The open-field restriction applies even inside the pen
  • Decree: pen walking is limited because of field rules
  • Carrying within the pen: also restricted to 4 cubits
  • Consistent ruling for pen, stable, and boat

🚢 Why Is the Boat Different? Rabba vs. Rabbi Zeira

The Question: Rav and Shmuel both rule that the halakha follows Rabban Gamliel for a boat — one may move freely. But why is a boat different from a pen or stable?
↓
Rabba's Answer: Because the person acquired his place of residence within the boat's partitions while it was still day (before Shabbat began). Since he was enclosed within the boat at the onset of Shabbat, the entire boat counts as his 4-cubit domain.
↓
Rabbi Zeira's Answer: Because the boat constantly moves him — lifting him from the start of 4 cubits and placing him at the end. Since he physically cannot remain in any fixed 4 cubits, the entire boat becomes his permitted space.
↓
Practical Difference — Breached Walls: If the boat's walls are breached after Shabbat begins: Rabba restricts him (no longer within valid partitions); Rabbi Zeira still permits movement (the motion argument holds regardless of walls).
↓
Why doesn't Rabbi Zeira use Rabba's reasoning? Because the sides of a boat are not regarded as proper mechitzot — they exist to keep out water, not to create a valid halachic enclosure.
