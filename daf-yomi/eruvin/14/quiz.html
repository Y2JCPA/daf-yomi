QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is a one-handbreadth cross beam valid even though a standard brick is 1½ handbreadths wide?
A. The Sages reduced the required brick size to one handbreadth
B. Plaster fills the remaining ½ handbreadth on each side to secure the brick
C. Any beam wider than one finger is automatically valid by rabbinic decree
D. Rav Sheshet ruled that fractional differences in width are halakhically ignored
Correct: B — The Gemara answers that the beam receives the brick for one handbreadth, and the remaining ½ handbreadth is filled with plaster applied to each side, allowing the brick to stand in place.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
From which biblical source does the Talmud derive the ratio π ≈ 3?
A. The dimensions of the Ark of the Covenant (Exodus 25)
B. The measurements of the Tabernacle courtyard (Exodus 27)
C. Solomon's Molten Sea — 10 cubits diameter and 30 cubits circumference (I Kings 7:23)
D. The description of the round bases of the Temple pillars (I Kings 7:31)
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan derives from I Kings 7:23 that the sea was 10 cubits from brim to brim and 30 cubits in circumference — giving circumference = 3 × diameter, i.e., π ≈ 3.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rami bar Yeḥezkel, how was Solomon's Sea structured to contain exactly 150 ritual baths?
A. It was entirely round, 5 cubits tall, with a wide brim adding extra volume
B. The bottom 2 cubits were round and the top 3 cubits were square
C. The bottom 3 cubits were square (100 baths) and the top 2 cubits were round (50 baths)
D. It was entirely square but the brim held an additional 50 baths of overflow
Correct: C — Rami bar Yeḥezkel: the lower 3 cubits were square, yielding 300 cubic cubits = 100 ritual baths; the upper 2 cubits were round, yielding 150 cubic cubits = 50 ritual baths — totaling 150.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Rava rule about a side post raised three handbreadths above the ground?
A. It is valid because the principle of lavud fills the gap beneath it
B. It is valid only if the post itself is taller than 13 handbreadths total
C. It is entirely invalid — not a valid side post by any opinion
D. It depends on whether the alleyway's walls also extend to the ground
Correct: C — Rava rules that a side post raised 3 handbreadths from the ground — or distanced 3 handbreadths from the wall — accomplishes nothing and is not a valid side post.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When asked the practical halakha regarding the minimum width of a side post, what does Abaye advise?
A. Rule strictly like Rabbi Yosei — require 3 handbreadths of width
B. Rule like Shmuel — require at least one full handbreadth of width
C. Go out and observe what the people do — common practice is to rely on minimal width
D. Defer to the local rabbinic court, as no single ruling was established
Correct: C — Abaye tells Rava bar Rav Ḥanan: 'Go out and see what the people do.' Common practice is to rely on a side post of minimal width, rejecting Rabbi Yosei's requirement of 3 handbreadths.
