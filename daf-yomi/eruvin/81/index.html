💰 Two Disputes: Bread Fitness & Money Acquisition for Eiruv

Rabbi Yehoshua
Only a whole loaf is valid for an eiruv. A broken loaf may not be used — it causes enmity among neighbors.
Rabba bar bar Ḥana
The mishna repeats the eiruv foods ruling precisely to exclude this view — broken loaves and varied foods may also be used.
Rabbi Eliezer
One who gives a coin to a grocer or baker acquires the eiruv food — money effects a valid acquisition here.
The Rabbis
Money alone does not confer possession on movable goods. One must physically pull or lift the item to acquire it.
Core Theme Eruvin 81 examines what makes bread fit for an eiruv (wholeness, composition, size) and what legal act transfers ownership of eiruv food — along with whether an eiruv requires the beneficiary's consent.

🍞 Whole vs Broken Loaf — the Enmity Problem

Rabbi Yehoshua
A whole loaf: yes — valid for eiruv. A broken loaf: no — it is unfit.
Rabbi Yosei ben Shaul
citing Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
The reason is enmity (איבה). If your neighbor deposits a broken piece while others bring whole loaves, hard feelings arise.
Rav Aḥa son of Rava
If all participants contributed broken loaves, there is no enmity — so is it permitted in that case?
Rav Ashi
Still forbidden — we worry the problem will recur in the future, when one later brings a whole loaf and the others broken ones.
Exception — Rav Ḥisda If two broken pieces are joined with a wood chip so that the loaf appears whole, it may be used for an eiruv — visual wholeness suffices.

📏 Ḥalla Removal and Loaf Validity for Eiruv

A loaf is proposed for use as an eiruv — but some bread was already removed
↓
Missing baker's ḥalla (1/48)
Only a small fraction removed — loaf remains nearly complete
Missing householder's ḥalla (1/24)
A larger fraction removed — loaf is noticeably diminished
↓
Baker's loaf (1/48 removed): ✅ Valid for eiruv
Householder's loaf (1/24 removed): ❌ Not valid for eiruv
↓
Source — Mishna Challah: One who bakes for personal use sets aside 1/24; a professional baker sets aside 1/48. The baker's portion is smaller, so less is missing from the loaf.

🌾 Exotic Breads — Valid for Eiruv?

Rice Bread

  • ✅ Valid — Rabbi Zeira citing Shmuel
  • ❌ Not valid — Mar Ukva citing Shmuel
  • Dispute over which bread Shmuel permitted
  • Rice bread is generally edible and common

Millet Bread

  • ❌ Not valid — Mar Ukva citing Shmuel
  • Less palatable than rice bread
  • Lower status as a food item
  • Shmuel's ruling appears to exclude it

Lentil Bread

  • ✅ Valid — Rav Ḥiyya bar Avin citing Rav
  • Must be pure lentil — not mixed grains
  • Mixed lentil bread was rejected even by a dog!
  • Like Ezekiel's bread: mixed grains = inedible disgrace

🤝 Knowledge & Consent — Eiruv Teḥumin vs Eiruv Ḥatzeirot

General Mishna rule: One may establish an eiruv for another person only with their knowledge and consent.
↓
Rabbi Yehuda's distinction: The consent requirement applies specifically to eiruv teḥumin — joining Shabbat travel boundaries — because it restricts where a person may walk. Restriction requires consent.
↓
Eiruv ḥatzeirot (joining courtyards to permit carrying) only grants benefit — it never limits movement. Therefore, one may establish it for another even without their knowledge.
↓
Rav Yehuda citing Shmuel: The halakha follows Rabbi Yehuda in this distinction between the two types of eiruv.
↓
Principle: זכין לאדם שלא בפניו — We may acquire benefit for a person in their absence, but we may not restrict their rights without consent.
