Eruvin 86 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav's application of Rabbi Shimon's ruling, when does an absent Jew NOT render a courtyard prohibited?
A. When he travels to a different town for Shabbat
B. When he goes to his daughter's house in the same town
C. When he goes to his son's house in the same town
D. When he is present but stays indoors all Shabbat
Correct: B — Rav rules like Rabbi Shimon, but limits it to one who went to his daughter's house — a father is at ease there and will not return. Going to a son's house does not qualify, since a father may feel unwelcome and return.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Where did Beit Shammai say the partition dividing a shared cistern must be placed?
A. Above the water, near the rim of the cistern
B. Touching the water's surface exactly
C. Below — near the water, within the cistern's airspace
D. On the exterior wall of the courtyard above the cistern
Correct: C — Beit Shammai said the partition should be placed below, down near the water in the cistern's airspace. Beit Hillel said it goes above, near the rim. Both rely on the principle of lavud for any remaining gap.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the homeowner's stored muktzeh object (e.g., a plough pin) in a tenant's quarters affect the courtyard eruv?
A. Because muktzeh creates an independent halachic domain
B. Because heavy objects physically block shared pathways
C. Because it establishes the homeowner's right of usage in the tenant's space
D. Because the tenant cannot legally possess the owner's tools
Correct: C — Rabba bar bar Chana explains: storing an object — even a muktzeh one that cannot be moved — establishes the homeowner's reshut (right of usage) in the tenant's quarters. This means the homeowner is still legally present and renders the courtyard prohibited.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What actually happened in Tzippori when a Torah scroll could not be carried to the synagogue on Shabbat?
A. They spread sheets between buildings on Shabbat to form partitions
B. They relied on Rabbi Yosei's explicit permission for suspended walls
C. They found sheets already spread on pillars and used them as existing partitions
D. They left the scroll in place and postponed the Torah reading
Correct: C — The Gemara corrects an initial reading: one may not erect a partition (ohel arat'i) on Shabbat. What actually occurred is that sheets were already spread on the pillars — they simply made use of existing suspended partitions.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rav Yehuda require regarding reed partitions placed below the waterline in a cistern?
A. The reeds must extend from the cistern floor all the way to the rim
B. The tops of the reeds must be visible one handbreadth above the water's surface
C. The reeds must form a solid wall with no gaps whatsoever
D. The partition must be exactly 10 handbreadths tall regardless of water level
Correct: B — Rav Yehuda (citing Rav / Rabbi Chiyya) rules that the partition's reed tops must protrude one handbreadth above the water's surface. This makes the division visible and recognizable as a legal partition.
