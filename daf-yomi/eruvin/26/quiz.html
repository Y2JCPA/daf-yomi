Eruvin 26 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What defines a 'new town' (עיר חדשה) for the purpose of measuring the Shabbat limit?
A. A town founded within the last generation
B. A town where the wall was built before residents arrived
C. A town where residents arrived before the wall was built
D. Any town with fewer than 50 permanent residents
Correct: B — A 'new town' is one where the wall was erected first and residents came afterward. The Shabbat limit is measured from the wall, since the wall — not settlement — defines the boundary.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Eliezer, if someone forgot to join the eiruv and wishes to renounce his rights on Shabbat, to whom must he renounce?
A. To the rabbi or community leader
B. To each resident of the courtyard individually
C. To one resident — which covers all residents
D. He cannot renounce on Shabbat at all
Correct: C — Rabbi Eliezer holds that one who renounces does so generously and expansively. Renouncing to one resident is treated as renouncing to all, since a generous-spirited renunciation is presumed.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
The Exilarch quoted Jeremiah 4:22 ('wise to do evil, but to do good they have no knowledge') in response to which incident?
A. Rabbis who issued an invalid ruling on a complex eiruv question
B. Rabbis who dismantled Rav Huna bar Ḥinnana's valid eiruv arrangement on Friday
C. Rabbis who forgot to establish their own eiruv before Shabbat
D. Rabbis who overruled the Exilarch's personal halakhic decision
Correct: B — Rav Pappa and Rav Huna son of Rav Yehoshua cleverly found legal grounds to remove the reeds — but in doing so, they destroyed a valid eiruv on Friday. The Exilarch criticized their misuse of legal acumen.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why is it permitted to establish an eiruv teḥumin for a nazirite using wine?
A. A nazirite may drink wine after his term of nezirut ends
B. Wine is not considered a real food for eiruv purposes
C. Wine is permitted to other people, even if the nazirite personally cannot drink it
D. The Mishna permits any food for eiruv regardless of personal restrictions
Correct: C — The Mishna rules that eiruv may use food permitted to others. Wine is permitted to non-nazirites; therefore it qualifies as valid eiruv food even when placed on behalf of a nazirite who cannot drink it.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Abaye's ruling regarding explicit statements about ceding eiruv rights?
A. Explicit statements are overridden by the halakhic presumption in each case
B. Only statements made before Shabbat with two witnesses are binding
C. Once wishes are explicitly stated — broad or narrow — we follow them regardless of presumptions
D. Explicit statements only apply according to Rabbi Eliezer, not the Rabbis
Correct: C — Abaye resolves Rav Pappa's question by ruling that once a person explicitly reveals his wishes, we follow that statement — both R. Eliezer and the Rabbis agree that express intent overrides any default presumption.
