⚖️ Three Challenges to the Mishna — All Rejected

Gemara
Mishna is NOT per Ḥananya — he only allows boards for cisterns, ropes for caravans (not boards for wells)
Rejection
A cistern has a distinct law: its water is consumed and the boards become a permanent enclosure — Ḥananya would agree
Gemara
Mishna is NOT per Rabbi Akiva — he permits boards for both wells AND cisterns, yet the mishna only mentions wells
Rejection
The tanna teaches well (spring water) distinctly because there is no comparable case — cistern simply wasn't mentioned
Gemara
Mishna is NOT per Rabbi Yehuda ben Bava — he only permits boards for a public well, yet mishna says 'wells' broadly
Rejection
'Wells' means to include only public wells — the tanna follows Rabbi Yehuda ben Bava perfectly
Pattern Each challenge follows the same structure: suggest incompatibility → reject with a distinction. The mishna is fully reconcilable with all three opinions.

🦴 Tzela: Face/Side or Tail?

Eve Was a Face (Side of Adam)

  • Adam had two faces back-to-back: one male, one female (Ps. 139:5: 'behind and before')
  • Vayyitzer double-yod = two formations, two distinct beings within one
  • 'Male and female He created them' — both existed simultaneously
  • 'One of his sides' = one of two faces, then separated
  • Challenge: 'And He built the tzela' — how do you build something already existing?
  • Response (R. Shimon ben Menasya): God braided/adorned Eve's hair like a noble woman

Eve Was a Tail (Appendage)

  • Tzela = tail, a literal appendage of Adam removed to form Eve
  • 'And He built the tzela' — building from scratch suits a tail perfectly
  • Challenge: Vayyitzer double-yod if only one being?
  • Response (R. Shimon ben Pazi): Two yods = two inclinations, good and evil, within each person
  • Challenge: 'Male and female He created them' = plural from the start?
  • Response (R. Abbahu): Created as androgyne then split — 'He created him' singular first, plural after split

👤 Adam's Two Faces — The Core Teaching

דּוּ פַּרְצוּפִין נִבְרָא אָדָם הָרִאשׁוֹן — 'Adam was created with two faces' (cf. Berakhot 61a)
Rabbi Yirmeya ben Elazar Adam the first man was created with two faces — one male and one female — joined back to back as a single being. God then separated them. This explains Psalms 139:5: 'You formed me behind and before.'

'Behind' = Adam was last in the order of creation (not formed until the sixth day, Shabbat eve).
'Before' = Adam was first for punishment — in the Flood, humans were blotted out before animals (Gen. 7:23).

🚶 Modesty: Walking Order and Looking at Women

Core principle: A man should not walk behind a woman — even his own wife — due to modesty concerns
↓
If you encounter her on a bridge, let her pass first so she is not behind you as you cross
↓
Manoah was an ignoramus: He literally walked behind his wife (Judges 13:11) — shows unfamiliarity with Torah
↓
Objection by Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak: Elkana also 'walked after his wife' — surely he was not an ignoramus!
↓
Resolution: Both verses mean 'followed her counsel and advice' — not literally walked behind her
↓
Rav Ashi adds: Manoah hadn't even learned basic Torah stories children know — Rebecca rode in front of her servant (Gen. 24:61)
↓
Rabbi Yoḥanan's hierarchy: Better to walk behind a lion than a woman • Better behind a woman than behind idolatry
↓
Counting money hand-to-hand with a woman in order to gaze at her — even if otherwise like Moses — loses his share in the World to Come
