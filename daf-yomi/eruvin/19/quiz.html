QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yirmeya ben Elazar, where are the three entrances to Gehenna?
A. The sea, Jerusalem, and Babylon
B. The wilderness, the sea, and Jerusalem
C. The wilderness, Jerusalem, and Rome
D. The sea, the desert, and the Valley of Ben Hinnom
Correct: B — Rabbi Yirmeya ben Elazar taught three entrances: the wilderness (Korah swallowed alive, Numbers 16:33), the sea (Jonah's cry from She'ol, Jonah 2:3), and Jerusalem ('Whose furnace is in Jerusalem,' Isaiah 31:9).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the contradiction between Reish Lakish's two statements about repentance at Gehenna?
A. One statement applies only before Rosh Hashanah, the other after
B. Reish Lakish retracted his first opinion in a later discussion
C. Sinners of Israel repent and are redeemed; complete rebels and nations do not repent
D. Repentance is possible at the wilderness entrance but not at the Jerusalem entrance
Correct: C — The Gemara distinguishes: חַטָּאֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל (sinners of Israel), who are filled with mitzvot, accept divine judgment and are ultimately redeemed. פּוֹשְׁעֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל / nations (complete rebels) do not repent even standing at Gehenna's gate.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi, how many names does Gehenna have, and what is the seventh?
A. Five names; the fifth is Tzalmavet
B. Six names; the sixth is Avadon
C. Seven names; the seventh is Eretz HaTaḥtit
D. Seven names; the seventh is Tofte
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi lists seven names: She'ol, Avadon, Be'er Shaḥat, Bor Shaon, Tit HaYaven, Tzalmavet, and Eretz HaTaḥtit — each sourced from a biblical verse. 'Tofte' and 'Gehenna' are descriptions, not formal names.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, for a cistern of what width does everyone agree that upright boards between the double posts are NOT needed?
A. Four cubits wide
B. Six cubits wide
C. Eight cubits wide
D. Ten cubits wide
Correct: C — Rav Pappa rules that for a cistern of 8 cubits, even adding two cubits on each side for cows yields only 12 cubits — within Rabbi Meir's 10-cubit gap standard (after subtracting the cistern itself), so both opinions agree no extra posts are needed.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say 'Gehenna' is NOT counted among the seven names of Gehenna?
A. It is an Aramaic word, not a Hebrew name
B. It is a description (valley of Ben Hinnom / everyone tosses sins there), not a formal name
C. It was only used after the destruction of the Temple
D. Gehenna is actually the name of an angel, not a place
Correct: B — The Gemara explains 'Gehenna' is descriptive: either 'a valley (גַּיְא) as deep as the valley of Ben Hinnom,' or a place into which everyone 'tosses' (גֶּה) their sins for nothing (חִנָּם). Since it describes rather than names, it is not counted among the seven.
