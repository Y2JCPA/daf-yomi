⚖️ Do Sinners Repent at Gehenna's Gate?

Reish Lakish (I)
The wicked do not repent even at Gehenna's entrance — 'they shall go forth and look upon the carcasses of the men who rebel against Me' (Isaiah 66:24)
Reish Lakish (II)
'Those who pass through the valley of weeping turn it into a spring' (Psalms 84:7) — sinners accept God's judgment and are redeemed from Gehenna
Gemara (Resolution)
No contradiction: Rebels who don't repent are nations / complete apostates. Those redeemed are sinners of Israel — filled with mitzvot like a pomegranate with seeds
Proof — Golden Altar
The golden altar — covered only a dinar's thickness of gold — was never consumed by Temple fire. So too Jewish sinners filled with good deeds are not destroyed by Gehenna's flames
Key Distinction חַטָּאֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל (sinners of Israel) repent and are redeemed. פּוֹשְׁעֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל (complete rebels) do not repent even at the gate.

🐄 Abaye's Four Dilemmas Before Rabba

Dilemma 1 — Gap Too Wide: If gaps between double posts exceed the limit, do you extend the boards in width or add more boards? Rabba resolves: mishna says 'increases the boards' = extends their width. Abaye pushes back; two versions of this debate exist.
↓
Dilemma 2 — Rabbi Yehuda's Limit Exceeded: If gaps exceed 13⅓ cubits under Rabbi Yehuda, what then? Rabba resolves from baraita: Rabbi Yehuda only permits up to two beit se'a — beyond that, even double posts cannot help.
↓
Dilemma 3 — Mound as Double Post: Can a mound rising 10 handbreadths within 4 cubits serve as a double post? Rabba resolves from Rabbi Shimon ben Elazar: 'we see the stone as if it were altered' — we may visualize it as a valid post.
↓
Dilemma 4 — Reed Barrier: Can a barrier of reeds (each < 3 handbreadths apart, joined by lavud) serve as a double post? Rabba: Yes — baraita proves: 'a tree, fence, or barrier of reeds serves as a double post.' Gemara debates whether this means reeds-by-lavud or an actual thicket.
