📜 Mishna — Haystack as Partition A haystack between two courtyards that is ten handbreadths high has the status of a partition. The residents may establish two separate eiruvim but not one joint eiruv. Each side may feed its animals from the stack on Shabbat — each from its own side. If the hay drops below ten handbreadths, carrying is prohibited in both courtyards.

Abaye
The baraita deals with a house slightly less than 13 handbreadths high. The hay is 10 handbreadths high — within 3 handbreadths of the ceiling. By lavud, the hay counts as reaching the ceiling and forms a full partition.
Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua
Even if the house is exactly 10 handbreadths high, hay at slightly more than 7 handbreadths suffices — lavud closes the remaining gap of under 3 handbreadths. Once the hay drops below that threshold, the partition fails entirely.
📐 Principle of Lavud — לָבוּד Any gap of less than 3 handbreadths between two surfaces is treated as if they are joined. This principle governs how partition height is calculated relative to ceilings and walls.

🐄 May One Feed Animals from the Haystack on Shabbat?

Animal owner wishes to feed from communal haystack on Shabbat
↓
Indirect — Permitted
Stand in front of the animal so it cannot go elsewhere; it walks to the haystack and eats on its own
Direct — Prohibited
Put hay into a basket and carry it to the animal — handling muktzeh hay directly is forbidden
↓
Rav Huna: One may not handle the hay; the mishna permits only the animal eating by itself from alongside the stack

🛢️ Merging an Alleyway — Procedure

One resident selects a barrel of his own food to serve as the communal eiruv for all alleyway residents
↓
He lifts the barrel at least one handbreadth from the ground — performing a valid act of acquisition on their behalf
↓
He declares: "This is for all the residents of the alleyway" — conferring a share in the food to each household
↓
He may confer possession through his adult son or daughter, or through a Hebrew slave or maidservant
↓
He may NOT use his minor child or a Canaanite slave — whatever they acquire belongs automatically to their master
