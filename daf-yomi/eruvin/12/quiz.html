Eruvin 12 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Aḥlei, what width makes an alleyway require NO marker whatsoever?
A. Less than 4 cubits
B. Less than 4 handbreadths
C. Less than 10 handbreadths
D. Less than 2 cubits
Correct: B — Rav Aḥlei teaches that up to 4 handbreadths wide, the alleyway is too narrow to require any marker. Only from 4 handbreadths onward does a side post or cross beam become relevant.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the halachic status of an alleyway whose length equals its width?
A. It requires a side post like any alleyway
B. It requires a cross beam only
C. It is treated like a courtyard, requiring an upright board of 4 handbreadths
D. It requires nothing at all since it is enclosed on all sides
Correct: C — Rav (cited by Rav Yehuda) rules that a square alleyway — length equal to width — is treated like a courtyard, and therefore requires an upright board of 4 handbreadths rather than a minimal side post.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Yehuda and Rabba, how does a lechi (side post) differ from a korah (cross beam)?
A. A lechi is decorative; a korah is functional
B. A lechi functions as a true partition; a korah functions only as a conspicuous marker
C. Both function equally as full partitions
D. A korah creates a partition; a lechi is merely symbolic
Correct: B — Both Rav Yehuda and Rabba hold that a lechi constitutes a true partition — creating a private domain and generating liability — while a korah serves only as a marker, which is why throwing into a korah-only alleyway does not create liability.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did the Sages apply more lenient rules to a sea inlet that breached a courtyard wall?
A. Water cannot be physically carried and is therefore irrelevant to Shabbat
B. The Sages issued a blanket exemption for all waterfront property owners
C. It is a known principle that the Sages lessened requirements in water-related cases
D. A sea inlet is automatically considered a private domain
Correct: C — Abaye explicitly invokes the principle that the Sages were more lenient in cases involving water. This is a deliberate halachic policy, not a casual exception — the sea inlet case therefore requires less stringent conditions than an ordinary wall breach.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Shmuel initially thought an alleyway needed its length to be double its width to qualify. How did Rav correct him?
A. Rav agreed — double the width is indeed required
B. Rav said the length need only be greater than the width, not necessarily double
C. Rav said even a square alleyway qualifies
D. Rav said the length is irrelevant; only the width matters
Correct: B — Rav corrected Shmuel's initial view: an alleyway need not be double in length — it only needs its length to exceed its width. Rav Naḥman's tradition confirms this: any alleyway where length is greater than width qualifies as a true alleyway.
