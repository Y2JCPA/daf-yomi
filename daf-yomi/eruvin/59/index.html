⚖️ Shabbat Limits: Lenient or Stringent?

Baraita
The Sages enacted Shabbat limits to be stringent — not to be more lenient than Torah law.
Mishna
The Sages did not state the laws of Shabbat limits to be stringent, but rather to be lenient.
Ravina
Both are correct! The very institution of Shabbat limits is a rabbinic stringency beyond Torah law — but within that framework, when rulings conflict, we rule leniently.
Resolution Shabbat limits are a rabbinic stringency — but disputes within that system are resolved in favor of leniency.

📐 Abaye: When Is the Surveyor's Extended Measurement Accepted?

Surveyor extends the Shabbat limit beyond the standard measure
↓
Within the diagonal allowance
A square city's diagonal is longer than its sides — the surveyor may use that difference as extra allowance
Beyond the diagonal allowance
Extension exceeds the diagonal-to-side difference — measurement is not accepted
↓
Abaye's Rule: Accept the extended limit only up to the amount the city's diagonal exceeds its straight measure (ריבוע העיר)

🏙️ Private City vs. Public City — Eiruv Rules

Private → Public

  • Small village gains many residents and becomes a public city
  • A public domain now passes through it
  • Place side posts (לחיים) on each side of the public domain
  • Establish eiruv for each alleyway separately

Public → Private

  • Public city loses residents and becomes private
  • Cannot establish eiruv for all of it without exclusion
  • Must leave out an area the size of Ḥadasha in Judea — a city of 50 residents
  • Ḥadasha is the halakhic benchmark for 'private city'

↔️ Rav Pappa: Two Versions on Dividing a City

Rav Pappa (Version 1)
One may not establish an eiruv for half a city divided lengthwise — this cuts the public domain so each half has only one entrance to the other half.
Rav Pappa (Version 2)
Do not say only lengthwise is forbidden — even dividing widthwise is forbidden. Rabbi Akiva holds a foot permitted in its own place is permitted elsewhere too.
Rabbis (rebuttal)
Even according to the Rabbis who reject Rabbi Akiva, dividing a city may be forbidden — residents of each half can access the other half's alleyways and share a domain.
Core Tension Is the prohibition on half-city eiruv about the public domain being cut, or about residents sharing each other's space? Both versions have halakhic implications.

🗑️ Rabbi Zeira's Eiruv and the Garbage Dump

Rav Ḥiyya bar Asi had established an eiruv for the entire city without excluding any section
↓
Rabbi Zeira learned this from the city Elders and initially followed suit — then reconsidered and established eiruv for only part of the city
↓
Abaye explained: In Rav Ḥiyya's time, a garbage dump blocked one entrance to the public domain — leaving effectively only one entrance, permitting a full city eiruv
↓
The garbage dump was later removed — two entrances now open, restoring full public domain status
↓
Rabbi Zeira's partial eiruv correctly reflects current reality: one entrance is required for a whole-city eiruv to be permitted
