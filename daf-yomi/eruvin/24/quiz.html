Eruvin 24 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Huna son of Rav Yehoshua, what is the ruling when the majority of a karpef is sown?
A. Permitted — the sown portion is batel to the enclosed area
B. Prohibited to carry within the karpef
C. It becomes a full private domain with no restrictions
D. One must uproot the sown area before carrying
Correct: B — Rav Huna son of Rav Yehoshua rules that when the greater part is sown, the minor unsown portion is nullified — the entire karpef is treated as sown and carrying is prohibited.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rav Yehuda citing Avimi taught that trees permit carrying in a karpef only if they are planted in what manner?
A. Around the perimeter forming a natural fence
B. In rows (itztablaot), the customary orchard arrangement
C. In clusters of at least three trees together
D. Alternating with cultivated soil
Correct: B — Avimi required trees to be planted in rows (itztablaot) — the normal orchard style — for the karpef to acquire courtyard status. Mar Yehuda's case added that trees large enough to shelter people also qualify.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What method did Rav Naḥman (citing Shmuel) prescribe to permit carrying in a large karpef not enclosed for residence?
A. Plant trees over the majority of the area
B. Construct a doorframe at the main entrance
C. Breach the wall to more than 10 cubits wide, then fence off the breach
D. Fill the area with water fit for drinking
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman said one should breach the wall wider than 10 cubits and fence off that section, thereby establishing the enclosure as now serving residential purpose — making carrying permitted throughout.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What was the core principle Rabbi Yoḥanan applied to resolve Ḥizkiya's dilemma about incremental perforations?
A. A majority of the original vessel must remain intact
B. Sealed perforations combine in size like open ones
C. A new entity has arrived — the repaired object is legally a new object
D. Impurity is suspended when the vessel is in use
Correct: C — From Ḥizkiya's own teaching about the sandal with two repaired straps, Rabbi Yoḥanan derived the 'new entity' principle: the repaired object is legally new, and therefore each breach-and-fence creates a fresh enclosure.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Rava reject Abaye's suggestion to place a side post at the vineyard path opening in Pum Nahara?
A. A single side post cannot serve as a valid partition
B. It would mislead people into thinking a side post permits any vineyard path
C. The vineyard path was too wide for the side post to be effective
D. Camels walking the path would knock over the side post
Correct: B — Rava objected that people would incorrectly generalize and think a side post permits carrying in vineyard paths generally — but most such paths are open at both ends, making a single side post insufficient. Rava instead recommended placing the side post at the yard's opening to the town.
