🌱 Three Scenarios: Karpef Character and Carrying

Majority Sown

  • Minor sown portion nullified by the majority
  • Entire karpef treated as if fully sown
  • Prohibited to carry
  • Source: Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua
  • Rav Yirmeya of Difti teaches same ruling as leniency (a fortiori)

Minority Sown

  • Small sown area does not override the whole
  • Karpef retains its enclosed character
  • Permitted to carry
  • The minor part is batel to the majority
  • Per Rabbis (not Rabbi Shimon)

Majority Trees (in Rows)

  • Trees planted in rows (itztablaot)
  • Gives karpef status of a courtyard
  • Permitted to carry
  • Rav Yehuda citing Avimi
  • Trees large enough to shelter also qualify (Mar Yehuda's case)

🌳 Must Trees Be Planted in Rows? Mar Yehuda's Challenge

Rav Yehuda (Avimi)
Trees must be planted in rows (itztablaot) — the customary orchard arrangement — for the karpef to acquire courtyard status and permit carrying.
Mar Yehuda
I visited Rav Huna bar Yehuda's house and saw trees not planted in rows, yet people carried freely among them. Does the Master not hold by this ruling?
Rav Huna bar Yehuda
Those trees are large enough to provide human shelter. Trees that shelter people make the karpef dwelling-suitable regardless of whether they are planted in rows.
Two Valid Conditions Trees qualify a karpef as courtyard-like either (a) when planted in rows (standard orchard style), or (b) when individually large enough to provide shade/shelter — both render it suitable for dwelling.

🔨 Permitting a Large Karpef: The Breach-and-Fence Method

Problem: Karpef > 2 beit se'a was enclosed not for residence — carrying is prohibited as a karmelit
↓
Solution (Rav Naḥman / Shmuel): Breach the wall to a width greater than 10 cubits
↓
Immediately fence off the breached section as a new dedicated enclosure
↓
This act establishes the karpef as now enclosed for the purpose of residence
↓
Result: Carrying is permitted throughout the entire karpef
↓
Incremental approach also valid: Breach one cubit, fence it, breach another cubit, fence it — repeating until 10+ cubits total — the "new entity" principle applies at each stage

👼 Ḥizkiya & Rabbi Yoḥanan: A New Entity Has Arrived

לאו בר אינשי הוא אלא מלאך הוא
The New Entity Principle — חידוש Ḥizkiya's dilemma: If a vessel is perforated olive-size, sealed, perforated again, sealed again — repeatedly — does it ever accumulate to pomegranate-size and become pure through breakage?

Rabbi Yoḥanan answered from Ḥizkiya's own sandal teaching: when a sandal's first strap breaks and is repaired, it remains impure (second strap still intact). When the second strap breaks and is repaired, a new entity has arrived — legally a new sandal.

Application: Each cycle of breach-and-fence creates a legal new entity. The incremental method works because at completion, a wholly new enclosure has been established.

Ḥizkiya's stunned reaction: "This is not a human being — he is an angel who resolved my problem from my own teaching!"

🏘️ Pum Nahara: How to Permit the Problematic Yard

Abaye
Place a side post (lechi) at the opening of the vineyard path into the yard. Since the side post is effective for the path (permitting carrying on it), it is also effective for the yard.
Rava
Rejected! People will wrongly conclude that a side post permits any vineyard path for carrying — but most paths are open at both ends and a single side post does not suffice. This causes public error.
Rava
Instead: place the side post at the opening of the yard to the town. Since it is effective for the town (a valid side post there), it is also effective for the yard — no misleading inference arises.
Rav Aḥa / Ravina
Unresolved dispute: even with Rava's solution, may one carry between the town and the yard? One authority permits; the other prohibits.
Setting Pum Nahara: a yard > 2 beit se'a not enclosed for residence. One wall opens to the town alleyway; another opens to a walled vineyard path leading to the river (where camels drink).
