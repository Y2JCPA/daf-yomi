⚖️ One Sanctity or Two? The Heart of Eruvin 39

Rabbi Yehuda
The two days of Rosh HaShana can be split: establish an eiruv with feet toward the east on Day 1 and toward the west on Day 2. A tenai (condition) can separate them — one day is certainly a weekday!
The Rabbis
The two days of Rosh HaShana are a single extended sanctity (kedusha achat) — they cannot be split by different eiruvim, by conditions, or by muktze rulings. Both days must be treated as one.
Rav (Amora)
The 'Rabbis' who disagree with Rabbi Yehuda in our Mishna are those who follow Rabbi Yosei's opinion — not a different school entirely.
Rabbi Yosei
Even when witnesses arrived after minḥa and Day 1 is observed only by rabbinic decree — the two days are still one sanctity. You cannot use one day's uncertainty to permit something on the other day.
Core Dispute Are the two days of Rosh HaShana one long sanctity (so they must be treated identically in all respects) or two separate sanctities (so conditions, split eiruvim, and cross-day leniencies can apply)?

📍 Eiruv with Feet on Two Festival Days — Rav Yehuda's Rules

Day 1 Eve: Establishes eiruv with feet (walks to a spot to extend boundary)
↓
Day 2: Feet Again
✅ Permitted — may re-establish eiruv with feet in any direction
Day 2: Switch to Bread
❌ Forbidden — cannot switch from feet eiruv to bread eiruv
↓
But what if Day 1 used bread and the bread was eaten?
↓
Day 2: Switch to Feet
✅ Permitted — bread eiruv lapsed, feet method now available
Day 1: Feet → Day 2: Bread
❌ Forbidden — once locked into feet, cannot switch to bread

🍞 Same Bread or Different? Shmuel vs. Rav Ashi vs. The Rabbis

Shmuel
When the Mishna says one may use bread on both days, it means the same physical loaf — the identical bread must survive to Day 2. A different loaf does not work.
Rav Ashi
The Mishna's wording itself proves Shmuel correct — it says 'he may use bread on Day 2,' implying the same bread from Day 1 is still present and being reused.
The Rabbis
This is no proof! The Mishna gives only good practical advice — use one loaf to avoid preparing a second. But a different loaf on Day 2 would also be valid.
At Stake Shmuel requires the same physical loaf to span both days. The Rabbis argue the Mishna is advisory, not restrictive — any bread suffices for Day 2.

🧺 Making a Tenai (Condition) on Tevel — Rabbi Yehuda's Method

Setup: Two baskets of tevel (untithed produce) — one large, one small. Day 1 of Rosh HaShana begins.
↓
Day 1 declaration: "If today is sanctified, let the small basket be teruma for the large basket. If today is a weekday, my words have no significance."
↓
Bestow the name of teruma on the small basket — but do not eat yet, as today may be the Festival.
↓
Day 2 declaration: "If today is a weekday, let this basket be teruma for that basket. If today is sanctified, my words have no significance."
↓
Bestow the name of teruma again and eat the produce — one of the two days was certainly an ordinary weekday, so tithing is complete.
↓
Result (Rabbi Yehuda): By making opposing conditions each day, the tevel is properly tithed without violating either day's potential sanctity. The Rabbis reject this — days cannot be split.

🦌 The Deer at the Exilarch — Trapped Day 1, Slaughtered Day 2

Rav Naḥman & Rav Ḥisda — Ate From It

  • Deer was trapped by a gentile on Day 1 of the Diaspora Festival
  • Slaughtered on Day 2 of the Festival
  • Gentile's trapping does not impose muktzeh on a Jew
  • Two Diaspora Festival days = two separate sanctities
  • Day 2 slaughter therefore permitted
  • Rav Naḥman (sarcastically): "What can I do for Rav Sheshet who doesn't eat deer?"

Rav Sheshet — Refused to Eat

  • Cited teaching of Isi (some say: a baraita):
  • "Just as the two days of Rosh HaShana are one sanctity, so too the two Festival days of the Diaspora are one sanctity"
  • Trapping on Day 1 = muktzeh for all of Day 2
  • Cannot be slaughtered or eaten on Day 2
  • Rava and Rav Asi each propose alternative readings of Isi's teaching
  • Rav Ashi: Ameimar later reported the deer was not actually trapped on the Festival
