🏪 Rabbi Yoḥanan's Apparent Contradiction — Renting vs. Eiruv

R. Yoḥanan
You acted well when you rented the gentile's property on Shabbat — this resolves the carrying prohibition.
Sages of Neharde'a
But didn't Rabbi Yoḥanan himself say that renting from a gentile is like establishing an eiruv? These teachings seem to contradict each other!
Gemara
No contradiction — his comparison was a leniency: just as eiruv may be established for less than a perutah, so too rental may be paid for less than a perutah.
Gemara
And just as one person may establish an eiruv on behalf of five courtyard residents, so too one person may rent the gentile's rights on behalf of all residents.
Principle Renting from a gentile follows the same leniencies as eiruv — minimal payment suffices, and agency is valid for the whole courtyard.

📋 Shmuel's Three Categories of Shared Spaces

Both Conditions Met ✓

  • Render each other prohibited: YES
  • Can establish eiruv together: YES
  • → Bitul reshut IS valid
  • Example: Two courtyards sharing one alleyway opening

Only Eiruv-Capable ✗

  • Render each other prohibited: NO
  • Can establish eiruv together: YES
  • → Bitul reshut NOT valid
  • Example: Two courtyards with shared wall but separate alleyways

Only Prohibition ✗

  • Render each other prohibited: YES
  • Can establish eiruv together: NO
  • → Bitul reshut NOT valid
  • Example: Gentile who arrived on Shabbat — eiruv impossible retroactively

💭 Rav Yosef's Forgotten Halakhot — Abaye Reconstructs His Own Teaching

Rav Yosef states: "I have not heard Shmuel's halakha regarding two courtyards, one within the other — I don't know if renunciation applies there."
↓
Rav Yosef also states: "There is no bitul reshut in a ruin — the Sages instituted renunciation only for a courtyard, which is a proper residential space used by people."
↓
Abaye reminds him: "You yourself taught us in the name of Shmuel that we may only be lenient with eiruvin in accordance with the wording of the Mishnah!"
↓
Rav Yosef is astonished: "Did I say that in the name of Shmuel?" — He had forgotten his own teaching due to serious illness that impaired his memory.
↓
Abaye clarifies the context: You said Shmuel's ruling about following the Mishnah's wording specifically regarding the alleyway Mishnah — it applied to a particular case, not as a blanket restriction against leniency.

⚖️ Why Most Inner/Outer Scenarios Cannot Use Renunciation

ביטול רשות — מחצר לחצר פנימית וחיצונה
Rava's Reasoning When the eiruv is in the outer courtyard and an outer resident forgot: the inner resident cannot renounce outward — doing so makes the inner courtyard like a house opening to the outer, which still harbors a non-participating resident. The outer resident who forgot cannot renounce to the inner — other outer residents still render the outer prohibited. In every such case, there is no party who can receive a renunciation that would actually fix the problem.

Only when the eiruv is in the outer courtyard and an inner resident forgot can renunciation work — the inner renounces outward, and the outer eiruv covers all.
