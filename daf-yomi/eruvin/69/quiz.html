Eruvin 69 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, who is considered an apostate Jew (mumar)?
A. One who violates any Torah prohibition three times intentionally
B. One who desecrates Shabbat in public
C. One who eats non-kosher food regularly
D. One who has not observed family purity laws for a year
Correct: B — Rav Huna defines a mumar as one who desecrates Shabbat b'farhesia — in public — placing him outside the legal community of Israel. This is debated as either a specific eiruvin rule (Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak) or a general status (Rav Ashi).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak limit Rav Huna's statement about a public Shabbat desecrator?
A. He limits it to matters of marriage and divorce only
B. He says it applies only in cities without a Jewish majority
C. He says it is specific to eiruvin — such a person may not renounce domain rights
D. He says it applies only during the Shabbat of Passover
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak clarifies that Rav Huna addressed only eiruvin: since a Jew can renounce rights but a gentile cannot, a public Shabbat desecrator — treated like a gentile — cannot perform bitul reshut.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Beit Hillel, when may one renounce domain rights to permit carrying?
A. Only before Shabbat begins while it is still fully daytime
B. Only at the Friday mincha prayer time before sunset
C. Even after nightfall, once Shabbat has already begun
D. Only through a written document prepared before Shabbat
Correct: C — Beit Hillel rules that bitul reshut (renunciation) is valid even after nightfall — i.e., on Shabbat itself — since the practical need typically arises on Shabbat when one discovers the eiruv was not made. Halakha follows Beit Hillel.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the mishna: one resident forgot the eiruv and renounces his rights to the others. What is now the status of his house?
A. Permitted to him and to all other residents
B. Prohibited to him, but permitted to the other residents
C. Permitted to him, but prohibited to the other residents
D. Prohibited to everyone until a new formal eiruv is made
Correct: B — After renouncing his rights, his house is prohibited to him (he surrendered his domain) but permitted to the other residents, who now hold full rights over the entire courtyard including his house.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The baraita on Leviticus 1:2 accepts voluntary sacrifices from Jewish sinners but not apostates. Which phrase excludes apostates?
A. "Of the herd or of the flock"
B. "Brings an offering to the Lord"
C. "Of you" — but not all of you
D. "Any man" — excluding women and communities
Correct: C — The baraita expounds "of you" to mean 'but not all of you' — excluding apostates from bringing sacrifices. Ordinary transgressors remain included via the phrase "of the cattle," compared to animals who also bring offerings despite their nature.
