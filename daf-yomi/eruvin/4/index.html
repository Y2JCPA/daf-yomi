📜 Halakha l'Moshe m'Sinai — Three Foundational Categories

שיעורין וחציצין ומחיצות — הלכה למשה מסיני
Core Theme Daf 4 covers two threads. First, it resolves the Abaye–Rava dispute about cubit types by showing that Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel's ruling excludes only the altar's special cubit. Then Rav declares three categories as halakha transmitted to Moses from Sinai: measures (shi'urin), interpositions (chatzitzot), and partitions (mechitzot). The Gemara challenges each — showing they seem written in Torah — and resolves that the Sinai tradition supplies precise operative details the text leaves unstated.

📏 Abaye vs. Rava: Which Cubits Are Larger?

Rava
The baraita's meaning: kilayim cubits use expansive handbreadths (thumb extended), and sukka cubits use depressed handbreadths (thumb inward). Both are 6-handbreadth cubits — just measured differently, making kilayim slightly larger.
Abaye
Emend the baraita: the cubit for kilayim is an enlarged cubit of six handbreadths, while other halakhic cubits are the standard five-handbreadth cubit. These are two completely different measuring units.
Gemara
Abaye is forced to align with Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel alone — proving this is a tannaitic dispute! But must Rava also concede it is disputed among tannaim?
Rava
No — Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel's emphasis on 'six handbreadths' comes only to exclude the altar's cubit (Ezekiel's cubit = 6 + 1 handbreadths), not to dispute the first tanna. So the issue need not be a tannaitic controversy.
Key Verse (Ezekiel 43:13) "The cubit is a cubit and a handbreadth" — the altar used a special 7-handbreadth cubit, which Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel explicitly excludes from kilayim measurement.

🌾 The Seven Species — Each Alludes to a Halakhic Measure

Wheat 🌾

  • Tzara'at of a house: one becomes tamei by remaining inside the time it takes to eat a half-loaf of wheat bread
  • Sets the time-unit for tumah through a house afflicted with leprosy

Barley 🌿

  • A bone from a corpse the size of a barley-grain imparts tumah through contact and carrying
  • Does NOT impart tumah through ohel (tent/overhang) — smaller threshold applies

Vine 🍇

  • Nazirite prohibition: liable to lashes for drinking a quarter-log (revi'it) of wine
  • Minimum measure for nazirite wine liability — derived from the vine

Fig 🍋

  • Shabbat carrying: liable only if carrying food equal to a dried fig-bulk (k'grogeret)
  • Standard minimum measure for food-carrying on Shabbat

Pomegranate 🔴

  • Wooden vessels of householders become tahor when broken below pomegranate-bulk capacity
  • A vessel holding less than a pomegranate cannot contract or maintain tumah

Olive & Date 🫒🌴

  • Olive-bulk (k'zayit): most common eating measure for violations (e.g., forbidden fats)
  • Date-bulk (kotevet): eating on Yom Kippur — liable only if consuming a large date's worth

🚪 Fixing the Too-High Korah — Raising vs. Lowering

The Problem: The korah (cross-beam) marking the alleyway entrance sits higher than 20 cubits above the ground — too high to be noticed by those passing under it, and therefore invalid as an eruv marker.
↓
Obvious Fix — Lower the Beam: Simply lower the korah until it is at or below 20 cubits. The Gemara notes this solution is too obvious to require discussion.
↓
The Real Question — Raise the Ground: If one builds up a platform beneath the korah so the beam is now within 20 cubits of the raised floor, how wide must the platform be?
↓
Opinion 1 — 4 × 4 Handbreadths: The raised platform must constitute a meaningful space (4 × 4 tefachim). This view holds that the area beneath the korah is usable, so the korah functions as the top of a partition-wall — requiring a proper base.
↓
Opinion 2 — 1 Handbreadth: Only a single handbreadth of width is required. This view holds the area beneath the korah is NOT utilized — the korah functions purely as a symbolic marker, not as part of a wall.
↓
Underlying Dispute: Do we 'use' the space under the korah (treating it as a wall-top) or not? This connects to broader debates about how the korah creates a halachic partition for the alleyway.
