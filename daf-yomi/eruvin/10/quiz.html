5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the final halakha regarding a lechi that is visible from outside the alleyway but appears flush with the wall from inside?
A. It is not a valid lechi under any circumstances
B. It is a valid lechi, per Rabbi Ḥiyya's baraita
C. It is valid only if a second lechi is placed opposite it
D. Its validity depends on the width of the lechi itself
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that such a lechi IS valid. Despite deductive arguments pointing the other way, Rabbi Ḥiyya's explicit baraita is authoritative and the halakha follows it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Shmuel citing Levi, how does one render a 20-cubit wide alleyway fit for carrying?
A. Stick a single reed at the entrance
B. Build two solid walls narrowing the opening to exactly 10 cubits
C. Stand a board 10 handbreadths high × 4 cubits long lengthwise down the middle
D. Create a tzurat hapetach spanning the full 20 cubits
Correct: C — Shmuel citing Levi prescribes standing a board 10 handbreadths high with a length of 4 cubits down the middle of the alleyway, thereby creating two sub-alleyways each narrower than 10 cubits.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Rav Yosef not remember the halakha about a lechi visible from the outside?
A. He had never learned it — it was a new ruling
B. He disagreed with it and deliberately did not teach it
C. He had become ill and forgotten his learning
D. The ruling was transmitted secretly and not publicized
Correct: C — The Gemara explicitly states that Rav Yosef had become ill and as a result forgot his learning. Abaye then reminded him of the very teaching Rav Yosef himself had transmitted to his students.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Abaye, what is the core point of dispute between Rav Dimi and Ravin about the bathroom seat leather?
A. Whether leather of a bathroom seat is subject to tumah at all
B. The exact number of handbreadths the seat must measure
C. Whether 'standing > breached' applies by combining standing segments from both sides of a breach
D. Which material counts as leather for these purposes
Correct: C — Abaye proves through finger arithmetic that they disagree about principle: Rav Dimi requires each side to independently have standing > breached; Ravin permits combining both sides, and their dispute focuses on the case where breached exactly equals standing per side.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In what case is an alleyway entrance wider than ten cubits valid without any reduction?
A. When the alleyway is used exclusively by one family
B. When it is less than twenty cubits wide
C. When upright boards are placed within three handbreadths of the side walls
D. When the entrance is built in the form of a doorway (tzurat hapetach)
Correct: D — The mishna rules that if the entrance is shaped as a doorway — two upright posts with a horizontal beam across the top (tzurat hapetach) — it is valid even if wider than ten cubits, since its halachic form itself constitutes a complete entrance.
