⚖️ The Great Domains Debate: Rav vs. Shmuel

Rav
One may carry throughout the entire roof (or ship, or portico) — it has valid partitions and constitutes one unified domain.
Shmuel
One may carry only within four cubits — these areas lack proper residential walls and do not form a true private domain.
Shmuel
Yet even Shmuel concedes: the halakha follows Rav for a ship, as his rationale is more convincing.
Scope of Dispute This debate spans roofs, large ships, and open porticos — any elevated or enclosed space whose walls may not qualify as residential partitions.

🤔 Rami bar Hama's Roof Dilemma

One carries 2 cubits on Roof A, then 2 more cubits on an adjacent non-residential roof
(According to Rav, the 4-cubit limit applies per roof)
↓
Unified Domain?
Neither roof is fit for residence → they merge into one undivided space → carrying across both is permitted
Separate Domains?
Each roof is its own distinct space → 4-cubit limit resets independently on each → carrying across is forbidden
↓
Parallel case (Rav Beivai): House roof + open ruin roof with one side exposed to public domain
Ruin is fit for residence → does the analysis change?

🚢 Carrying on a Large Ship

Rav
Permitted throughout the entire ship — it has real walls that form valid partitions, rendering the ship one private domain.
Shmuel
Only within four cubits — the ship's walls are built to keep out water, not to create a residence; they are not full-fledged partitions.
Shmuel
Halakha follows Rav — I concede his reasoning is more compelling.
Rav (concession)
If the ship is overturned (e.g., to tar its underside) and stands >10 handbreadths high — only 4 cubits permitted, as it no longer serves as a residence.
ספינה גדולה Large ship: Rav permits carrying throughout; Shmuel limits to four cubits; final ruling follows Rav. Exception: overturned ship = four cubits only.

🏛️ The Portico (אכסדרה): Two Approaches

Rav

  • Carrying permitted throughout entire portico
  • Principle: פי תקרה יורד וסותם
  • Edge of roof descends conceptually to the ground
  • Open sides are 'sealed' → valid private domain
  • Aligns with Rabbi Meir's view on level roofs

Shmuel

  • Only four cubits permitted in a portico
  • Principle: פי תקרה אינו יורד וסותם
  • Edge of roof does NOT descend and seal
  • Open sides remain open → karmelit status
  • Aligns with Rabbis' view on isolated roofs

📐 Rav Yehuda's Resolution: Separate Domain Hierarchies

Roofs form their own domain group — one may carry from roof to roof (per Rabbi Meir), but not from roof to courtyard
↓
Courtyards form their own domain group — one may carry between courtyards (with eruv), but not from courtyard to roof
↓
Enclosures (קרפיפות) form their own domain group — same principle applies; carry within the group only
↓
Why not mix groups? If one area shrinks below 2 beit se'a, it becomes a private domain — cross-carrying would then be a Torah-level violation
↓
Shmuel's parallel (Rabbis): Karmelit roofs may transfer only to other karmelit roofs — not to karmelit courtyards — for the same reason
