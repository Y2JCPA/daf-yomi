QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rami bar Hama's dilemma, why might two adjacent non-residential roofs be treated as one unified domain?
A. Since neither roof is fit for residence, they lose individual identity and merge into one space
B. Because a wall between them is less than ten handbreadths high
C. Because an eruv was established connecting both roofs
D. Because both roofs belong to the same owner
Correct: A — Rami bar Hama raises the possibility that since neither roof qualifies as a residence, they lose individual domain identity and merge, permitting carrying between them without the four-cubit restriction resetting.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rav rule that one may carry throughout an entire ship on Shabbat?
A. A ship at sea is exempt from Shabbat carrying restrictions
B. The ship's walls constitute valid partitions, rendering the entire ship one private domain
C. The ship's interior is considered a public domain where all carrying is permitted
D. Shmuel explicitly agreed there are no carrying restrictions aboard ships
Correct: B — Rav holds that the ship's walls are genuine, valid partitions that unify the entire ship into a single private domain, permitting carrying throughout — not merely within four cubits.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In what circumstance does Rav concede that only four cubits may be carried on a ship?
A. When the ship is traveling at sea rather than docked
B. When the ship belongs to a non-Jewish owner
C. When the ship is overturned upside-down (e.g., for tarring its underside) and stands over ten handbreadths high
D. When the ship carries more than ten passengers
Correct: C — Rav concedes that an overturned ship set up for tarring its hull does not function as a residence; its sides therefore lack the status of residential partitions, limiting carrying to four cubits.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does the principle פי תקרה יורד וסותם (pi tikra yored v'sotem) mean, and who invokes it?
A. A beam across a gap legally closes it; invoked by Shmuel to permit portico carrying
B. The edge of a roof descends conceptually to the ground, sealing open sides; invoked by Rav to permit portico carrying
C. A roof must physically extend ten handbreadths to count as a partition; invoked by Rav
D. Partitions must reach the ceiling to be valid; invoked by both Rav and Shmuel
Correct: B — Pi tikra yored v'sotem — 'the edge of the roof descends and seals' — is Rav's principle: the roof overhang is treated as extending down to the ground, sealing the open sides of a portico and creating a valid private domain throughout which one may carry.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Yehuda's analysis, why is one permitted to carry between two roofs but not from a roof to a courtyard?
A. A courtyard always requires a full eruv regardless of its domain status
B. Roofs and courtyards form separate domain hierarchies; mixing them risks a violation if one space later changes its status
C. The Sages issued a special decree prohibiting roof-to-courtyard carrying
D. A courtyard is always a private domain while a roof is always a karmelit
Correct: B — Rav Yehuda teaches that roofs form their own unified domain group and courtyards form theirs — carrying may flow freely within each group but not between them. The concern: if one space shrinks below 2 beit se'a it becomes a private domain, and transferring between a karmelit and a private domain would be a Torah violation.
