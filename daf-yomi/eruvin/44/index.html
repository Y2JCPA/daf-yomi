🚧 Human Partitions: Rava vs the Baraitot

Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
I raise an objection from a baraita: if a sukka wall fell on Shabbat, one may NOT use people to replace it — this proves human partitions are prohibited on Shabbat!
Rava
But another baraita explicitly permits positioning a person as a sukka wall! I can cite that baraita just as you cite yours. These two must reflect a machlokes.
Gemara
Perhaps they reflect Rabbi Meir vs Rabbi Yehuda? — But Rabbi Yoḥanan said ALL agree one may not erect a temporary tent for the first time on a Festival. Neither can permit it!
Gemara (Final Resolution)
Rather, BOTH baraitot follow the Rabbis. The distinction: knowing participants = prohibited; unknowing participants = permitted.
Practical Ruling Rava himself permitted flasks carried through unknowing human partitions in the marketplace of Meḥoza — the crowd did not know they were being used as walls

🌿 Living Creatures as Partitions — Two Tannaitic Views

Rabbi Meir

  • Sukka wall of living creature → UNFIT (sukka invalid)
  • Holds: living creature is NOT a valid halakhic partition
  • ∴ Using people as Shabbat walls = PERMITTED
  • Logic: since it's not a real partition, erecting it doesn't violate 'making a tent'
  • Concern: animal might wander away, leaving sukka exposed

Rabbi Yehuda

  • Sukka wall of living creature → FIT (sukka valid)
  • Holds: living creature IS a proper halakhic partition
  • ∴ Using people as Shabbat walls = PROHIBITED
  • Logic: since it IS a real partition, constructing it = making a tent on Shabbat
  • No concern about movement — the wall is valid regardless

📜 One Who Leaves Shabbat Limit for a Permitted Purpose

כָּל הַיּוֹצְאִין לְהַצִּיל חוֹזְרִין לִמְקוֹמָן
מִשְׁנָה — Eruvin 44b Scenario: A person left their Shabbat limit to testify about the new moon or to save a life. Along the way they are told: the action has already been performed. Rule 1: If still within his original Shabbat limit → treated as if he never left; retains his full 2000-cubit range. Rule 2: If outside his original limit → receives only 4 cubits. General Principle: All who go out to battle or save lives may return to their original locations on Shabbat — they are not penalized for their permitted departure.

📏 Subsuming of Shabbat Limits (בִּלּוּעַ תְּחוּמִין)

Rabba
The mishna means: if he is still within his original limit, he has his full 2000 cubits from wherever he is. The concept of 'subsuming limits' is not at issue here.
Rav Shimi bar Ḥiyya
The mishna means something deeper: even if he left his original limit, if his newly granted 2000-cubit zone is entirely subsumed within his original limit, he may move freely throughout his original territory.
Abaye
Rabba, don't you hold subsuming of limits IS significant? Consider: a cave 4000 cubits wide — one who establishes Shabbat residence there can walk its full interior! That's subsuming in action.
Rabba
The cave is different: the person established residence within its partitions before Shabbat. But here — the traveler did not establish residence within those partitions before Shabbat began. The cases are incomparable.
Core Dispute Does 'subsuming of Shabbat limits' apply to someone who acquired their Shabbat residence outside of partitions? Rabba: No. Rav Shimi bar Ḥiyya: Yes.
