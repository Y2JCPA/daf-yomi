Borrowed Ox: Who Pays for Damage?

Baraita
If one borrowed a cow and it caused damage — the lender is liable, not the borrower.
Gemara
But the latter clause says if the wall broke at night he's exempt, implying by day he's liable. How can both be true?
Gemara (Answer)
The two clauses address different cases: the first — he did not accept safeguarding duty; the latter — he did accept it.
Resolution
If he accepted responsibility: liable for damage, unless the wall broke at night or bandits opened it — then exempt.
Principle Liability for a borrowed animal's damage depends on whether the borrower explicitly accepted responsibility to prevent it from causing harm.

Shared Courtyard: Contradicting Baraitot

Rav Yosef's Baraita

  • In a shared courtyard or inn (פונדק)
  • One IS LIABLE for Eating and Trampling
  • Courtyard designated for produce only
  • Ox shouldn't be there → like injured party's property

Tosefta (R' Shimon ben Elazar)

  • In a shared courtyard or valley field
  • One is EXEMPT for Eating and Trampling
  • Courtyard designated for both produce AND oxen
  • Both have right to keep oxen → neither fully liable
Resolution No contradiction: Rav Yosef's baraita = courtyard for produce only (ox shouldn't be there). Tosefta = courtyard for both produce and oxen (both partners accept the risk).

Mishna's Rules for Damage Payment (14b)

💰 Monetary Appraisal — Damages assessed by monetary value, not offset
↓
🏞️ Items Worth Money — Court collects from land (קרקע), not movables
↓
🏛️ Before a Court — Requires formal judicial proceeding
↓
👥 Free Jewish Witnesses — Not slaves, must be members of the covenant
↓
👩 Women Included — Both as injurers and injured parties
↓
🤝 Both Parties Involved — Injured party and damager participate in payment
Key Rule If a cow damages a cloak and the cloak trips the cow — we don't simply offset. Each side's damage is appraised separately in monetary terms.

Why Does 'Items Worth Money' Mean Land?

Rabba bar Ulla (v.1)
It means something worth any amount — i.e., land, which has no price fraud (ona'ah).
Gemara
But slaves and documents also have no price fraud, yet we don't collect damages from them!
Rabba bar Ulla (v.2)
It means something acquired with money — land is acquired with money.
Gemara
Slaves and documents are also acquired with money!
Rav Ashi
It means something worth money but not itself money. Movables, slaves, and documents are treated like money (portable + valuable) — only land is different.
Rav Ashi's Logic Land is uniquely 'worth money' without being money itself. Movable property functions like currency — portable and inherently valuable — so it's excluded.
