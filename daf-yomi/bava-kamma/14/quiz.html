Bava Kamma 14 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
If someone borrows an ox but does NOT accept responsibility to prevent it from causing damage, who is liable if the ox damages property?
A. The borrower, because he has possession
B. The lender (owner), as in any case where his ox causes damage
C. Both share liability equally
D. Neither — it is considered an accident
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that borrowing an ox for work does not automatically transfer responsibility for preventing damage. Without explicit acceptance, the lender/owner remains liable.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the contradiction between the two baraitot about liability in a shared courtyard?
A. One baraita is rejected as unreliable
B. One refers to a courtyard designated for both produce and oxen; the other to one designated for produce only
C. One discusses Eating damage and the other Goring damage
D. One refers to day and the other to night
Correct: B — When the courtyard is designated for both produce and oxen, partners accept the risk (exempt). When designated for produce only, bringing an ox there creates liability — like damaging in the injured party's exclusive space.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, why does 'items worth money' (שווה כסף) refer specifically to land?
A. Because land is the most valuable asset
B. Because land is worth money but is not itself money — unlike movables which function like currency
C. Because land cannot be stolen
D. Because the Torah explicitly mentions land for damage payments
Correct: B — Rav Ashi explains that movable property, slaves, and documents all share the characteristics of money (portable and valuable). Land alone is 'worth money' without being money itself.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the case of a cow damaging a cloak while the cloak simultaneously injures the cow, what does the Mishna require?
A. The damages automatically cancel each other out
B. Each side's damage is appraised separately in monetary terms
C. The cow owner always pays because animals are presumed at fault
D. A compromise of half-damages to each side
Correct: B — The Mishna's principle of שׁוּם הַזֶּק בְּכֶסֶף (monetary appraisal) requires independent assessment of each party's damage. Only after separate appraisal does the court calculate offsets.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When collecting damages from orphans who inherited their father's liability, the court may collect from:
A. Any property the orphans own, including their own earnings
B. Only the father's inherited land, as it is liened to his debts
C. Only movable property, as land passes to heirs free of liens
D. Nothing — liability does not transfer to heirs
Correct: B — Inherited land is liened to the father's debts and can be collected by the court. Inherited movable property is not liened — unless the injured party seized it during the father's lifetime.
