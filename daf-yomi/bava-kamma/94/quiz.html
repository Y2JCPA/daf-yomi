Bava Kamma 94 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Beit Hillel, what does the word "these" (eileh) in Deuteronomy 23:19 exclude?
A. Both the changed form and the offspring of the harlot's hire
B. Only the offspring of the animal
C. Only the changed form of the item
D. Items given voluntarily rather than as payment
Correct: A — Beit Hillel derives two halakhot from 'these': the prohibition applies only to the original form (not after change), and only to the items themselves (not their offspring).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov say that separating ḥalla from stolen wheat that was baked into bread is 'blasphemy'?
A. Because bread cannot be used for ḥalla
B. Because the physical change doesn't remove the stolen status — the 'blessing' over a mitzva with stolen goods is blasphemy
C. Because only a kohen may separate ḥalla
D. Because the robber already acquired the wheat through the change
Correct: B — R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov holds that shinui (change) does not transfer ownership. The wheat, though radically transformed, remains stolen — so performing a mitzva with it is blasphemy, not a blessing (Psalms 10:3).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the purpose of Takanat HaShavim (the ordinance for the penitent)?
A. To punish robbers more severely
B. To allow victims to demand higher compensation
C. To ease the burden on those who wish to repent, so they won't refrain from returning stolen items
D. To transfer ownership of all stolen items to the robber automatically
Correct: C — The Sages were concerned that if repentance were too burdensome — requiring return of every changed item — robbers would simply never repent. The ordinance eases this process.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Ya'akov's clarification of R' Yoḥanan, what type of change was R' Yoḥanan discussing when he said changed stolen items are returned by Torah law?
A. Any physical change whatsoever
B. Changes that increase the item's value
C. A reversible change — like sanded wood fashioned into vessels
D. Changes caused by natural forces rather than the robber
Correct: C — R' Ya'akov clarified that R' Yoḥanan referred specifically to reversible changes (shinui ha-chozer), like wood shaped into vessels that could be broken back into wood. This reconciles his view with the mishna about dyeing wool.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why are the children of a deceased robber obligated to return a specific stolen item (like a cow or garment) he left them?
A. Because children automatically inherit all their father's legal obligations
B. Because the stolen item still legally belongs to the victim and was never acquired
C. Due to the honor of their father who repented but died before returning it
D. Because the Sages imposed a penalty on the heirs of robbers
Correct: C — The Gemara clarifies this applies only where the father repented (otherwise, honoring a wicked father wouldn't apply per Exodus 22:27). Since he repented but didn't manage to return the items before death, his children complete the act for his honor.
