5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara's conclusion, why doesn't consecrating a stolen animal count as a 'sale' for fourfold/fivefold payment?
A. Because consecration is reversible
B. Because the animal remains in the owner's domain — it was his ox and is still his ox
C. Because the Temple cannot legally 'buy' property
D. Because only monetary transactions count as sales
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes: selling to a person creates a new owner, but consecrating means the animal is still considered 'your ox.' No transfer of possession occurs.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is R' Shimon's position on sacrificial animals for which the owner bears financial responsibility?
A. The thief is exempt because the animal belongs to the Temple
B. The thief pays only double payment
C. The thief pays fourfold/fivefold because the animal is still in the owner's possession
D. The thief must return the animal itself rather than pay
Correct: C — R' Shimon holds that when the owner must replace a lost or damaged offering, the animal is still considered in the owner's possession — so the thief is liable for fourfold/fivefold payment upon slaughter.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was R' Elazar's challenge to the explanations of R' Yochanan and Reish Lakish?
A. The animal was already dead so there is no liability
B. Slaughter alone doesn't render the meat permitted — sprinkling or redemption does
C. The thief has no standing to consecrate a stolen animal
D. Fourfold/fivefold payment applies only to non-sacred animals
Correct: B — R' Elazar pointed out that according to R' Yochanan, sprinkling (not slaughter) permits the meat, and according to Reish Lakish, redemption (not slaughter) permits it — so how can slaughter trigger the payment?
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara prove that R' Shimon considers blood 'ready to be sprinkled' as if already sprinkled?
A. From a verse in Leviticus about sacrificial procedures
B. From a case of notar: meat left overnight after the blood was fit for sprinkling is susceptible to tum'ah
C. From the laws of Pesach sacrifice
D. From the requirement to sprinkle blood before sunset
Correct: B — The baraita teaches that notar is susceptible to tum'at okhalin only if the blood was fit to be sprinkled (enough time before sunset). This shows R' Shimon treats fitness for sprinkling as equivalent to actual sprinkling.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why was the Gemara's initial attempt to explain the Mishna according to R' Shimon rejected?
A. Because R' Shimon's opinion is never followed in practice
B. Because the latter clause explicitly states R' Shimon's view, implying the first clause follows a different opinion
C. Because R' Shimon only discusses offerings of lesser sanctity
D. Because financial responsibility doesn't affect ownership according to the Mishna
Correct: B — Since the latter clause of the Mishna introduces R' Shimon's position separately, the first clause must represent a different Tanna's view — otherwise R' Shimon's opinion would be stated redundantly.
