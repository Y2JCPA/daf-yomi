Consecration, Slaughter & the Fourfold/Fivefold Payment

Mishna
One who stole and consecrated an animal, then slaughtered or sold it — pays double but not fourfold/fivefold.
Sages
Granted he doesn't pay for slaughter (he slaughtered Temple property). But shouldn't consecration itself count as a sale? What difference — sold to a person or sold to Heaven?
Gemara
Selling to a person: it was Reuven's ox, now it's Shimon's. Consecrating: it was Reuven's ox and it's still Reuven's ox. No transfer of possession.
R' Shimon
Sacrificial animals for which the owner bears financial responsibility — thief pays fourfold/fivefold. No responsibility — exempt.
Core Question Is consecrating an animal equivalent to selling it to another party?

Why Isn't Consecration a Sale?

Sages
מה לי מכרו להדיוט מה לי מכרו לשמים — What difference: sold to a person or sold to Heaven?
Gemara (Attempt 1)
The Mishna follows R' Shimon: animals with financial responsibility remain in the owner's possession.
Gemara (Rejection)
But the latter clause is R' Shimon's — so the first clause must be someone else!
Gemara (Attempt 2)
It follows R' Yosei HaGelili: offerings of lesser sanctity (kodashim kalim) are the owner's property.
Gemara (Rejection)
If so, for most sacred offerings he'd pay — but the Mishna doesn't distinguish!
Gemara (Final)
No difference: lesser or most sacred. Sold to a person = new owner. Consecrated = still your ox. No sale occurred.
Resolution Unlike a sale that transfers ownership entirely, consecration leaves the animal in the domain of its owner — it was his ox before and remains his ox after.

R' Shimon: Financial Responsibility & Payment

Bears Financial Responsibility

  • Owner must replace the animal if lost or damaged
  • Animal considered in owner's possession
  • Consecration ≠ sale (no transfer)
  • Thief pays fourfold/fivefold for slaughtering

No Financial Responsibility

  • Owner has no obligation to replace
  • Animal fully belongs to the Temple
  • Consecration = complete transfer
  • Thief is exempt from fourfold/fivefold
R' Shimon's Logic The key factor is whether the animal remains economically 'yours' — if you must replace it, you still have a stake in it.

R' Shimon's Principle: Ready = Done

כל דם הראוי לזריקה כזרוק דמי
כל הראוי — Whatever Is Fit כל הראוי לזריקה — כזרוק דמי
Any blood that is ready to be sprinkled is considered as though it has been sprinkled.

כל הראוי לפדות — כפדוי דמי
Any animal that is ready to be redeemed is considered as though it has been redeemed.

This resolves R' Elazar's challenge: since slaughter makes the blood fit for sprinkling, the meat is treated as already permitted.
