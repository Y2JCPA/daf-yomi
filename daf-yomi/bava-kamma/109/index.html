Can Robbery Obligations Be Forgiven?

Gemara
Why can't the son-heir simply forgive the debt to himself? We learned that an owner can forgive robbery principal!
R' Yochanan
Two opinions: The mishna allowing forgiveness follows R' Yosei HaGelili. Our mishna (son cannot forgive) follows R' Akiva.
R' Yosei HaGelili
Forgiveness works — both to oneself and to others. If a convert established the debt as a loan and then died, the robber acquires it.
R' Akiva
No forgiveness ever works. The robber has no remedy until he physically removes the stolen item from his possession.
R' Yochanan
The baraita teaches the loan case to show R' Akiva's stringency — even restructured as a loan, the obligation remains.
Stakes R' Akiva: Robbery creates an indelible obligation that cannot be waived by any party
5 / 9

R' Yosei HaGelili vs R' Akiva

R' Yosei HaGelili

  • Forgiveness works for robbery obligations
  • Owner can forgive to himself or others
  • If convert dies — robber can forgive to himself
  • Loan restructuring fully converts the obligation
  • Mishna on 103a reflects this lenient view

R' Akiva

  • Forgiveness never works for robbery
  • Cannot forgive to self or others
  • Must physically remove stolen item from possession
  • Even loan restructuring does not help
  • Our mishna (son-heir) reflects this strict view
Rav Sheshet's Challenge Both mishnayot can follow R' Yosei HaGelili — the son's case is different because the heir must still pay to fulfill his father's atonement

Robbery of a Convert Who Dies (Rava's Resolution)

Robber steals from a convert (גר) and swears falsely
↓
Does the convert die before or after the robber admits?
Convert dies THEN robber admits
God (HaShem) acquires it → goes to priests. Robber cannot forgive.
Robber admits THEN convert dies
Convert already owned the claim → he could have forgiven it. Robber keeps it.
Rava's Principle The timing of admission determines whether the obligation transfers to the priests or remains forgivable

Female Convert — Does the Restitution Law Apply?

QuestionResolution
Does Numbers 5:8 ('if the man has no kinsman') exclude women?No — 'man' (אִישׁ) is the verse's normal style
Source for including women?Double phrase 'that is made' (מושב) includes female converts
Then why say 'man' at all?For a man — investigate if he has heirs
What about a minor convert?No investigation needed — a minor convert has no possible heirs
Ruling The restitution-to-priests law applies equally to male and female converts who die without heirs
