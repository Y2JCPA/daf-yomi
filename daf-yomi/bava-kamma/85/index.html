Keviyya and Ḥabura: Generalization & Detail

Torah
The verse states: "Keviyya for keviyya, a wound for a wound, ḥabura for ḥabura" (Exodus 21:25). Keviyya is the generalization (all burns); ḥabura is the detail (burn with bruise).
R' Yehuda HaNasi
Since "a wound for a wound" intervenes between the generalization and the detail, we do not apply klal u'prat. One pays for pain in all burns, even without a bruise.
Ben Azzai
We do apply klal u'prat even when they are distant. Therefore, one pays for pain only if the burn is accompanied by a bruise.
Gemara
According to R' Yehuda HaNasi, why mention ḥabura at all? To teach that a burn with a bruise requires additional compensation beyond the burn alone.
KEY DISPUTE Can a klal u'prat be applied when the generalization and detail are separated by intervening text? This affects whether pain compensation covers all burns or only those with bruises.

How to Assess Pain Compensation

❌ Evaluate how much to sever his hand? — No, that includes all five indemnities, not just pain
↓
❌ Sever his already-severed hand? — No, that still includes humiliation (flesh thrown to dogs)
↓
❌ How much to accept switching drug → sword? — No one would accept money for extra pain
↓
✅ How much would he give to switch sword → drug? — This isolates pure pain value
↓
📐 Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua: Court evaluates what the injured party would pay the government to have his condemned hand severed by drug instead of sword
FINAL ANSWER Pain is assessed by how much a person whose hand is already condemned to be severed would PAY to have it done painlessly (by drug) rather than painfully (by sword).

RABBIS' REASONING The Torah links medical costs and loss of livelihood together — whoever is liable for one is liable for the other, and whoever is exempt from one is exempt from both.

Permission Granted to Heal

וְרַפֹּא יְרַפֵּא — מִכָּאן שֶׁנִּתְּנָה רְשׁוּת לָרוֹפֵא לְרַפֹּאות
DERASHA The verse says "verappo yerappe" (Exodus 21:19) — the doubled language of healing teaches two things:

1️⃣ School of R' Yishmael: Permission is granted to a doctor to heal (it is not considered interfering with divine decree)

2️⃣ Rav Pappa in Rava's name: The injurer must pay medical costs even where he already pays damage compensation

The Gemara proves both are derived: if only one lesson, the Torah could have written verofeh yerappe or repeated the same word. The change in form — verappo yerappe — teaches both halakhot.

Each Indemnity Can Apply Independently

Pain Alone

  • Burned with a skewer or hot nail
  • Burned on the fingernail — no bruise
  • Court evaluates the monetary value of pain

Medical Costs Alone

  • Body part was already hurting
  • Another applied a strong drug that whitened his flesh
  • Must provide medicine to restore skin color

Livelihood Alone

  • Locked someone in a room (inderona)
  • Forced idleness, no physical injury
  • Must compensate for lost wages

Humiliation Alone

  • Spit in another's face
  • No physical injury caused
  • Must pay for embarrassment
PRINCIPLE Each of the five indemnities has independent standing — a person can be liable for any one even without causing permanent damage.
