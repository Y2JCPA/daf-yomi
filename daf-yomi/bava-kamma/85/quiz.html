Bava Kamma 85 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yehuda HaNasi, why does the Torah mention 'ḥabura' if 'keviyya' already includes burns with bruises?
A. To teach that additional compensation is paid for a bruise beyond the burn
B. To limit pain compensation to burns with bruises only
C. To exclude burns without bruises from all compensation
D. To teach that ḥabura is a separate category of injury
Correct: A — R' Yehuda HaNasi holds that keviyya covers all burns. The mention of ḥabura teaches that when a burn includes a bruise, the injurer pays ADDITIONAL compensation for the bruise beyond the burn compensation.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara ultimately define the assessment of pain compensation?
A. How much a person would accept to have his hand severed
B. How much a person whose hand is condemned to be severed would PAY to switch from sword to drug
C. How much a person would accept to switch from drug to sword
D. The market price for pain-relieving medication
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that pain is assessed by how much a person whose hand is already condemned to be severed would PAY to have it done painlessly (by drug) rather than painfully (by sword). This isolates the pure value of avoiding pain.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What TWO halakhot does the school of R' Yishmael and Rav Pappa derive from 'verappo yerappe'?
A. Permission to heal, and medical costs are paid alongside damage compensation
B. Doctors must be paid, and patients must follow medical advice
C. Medical costs are always the injurer's responsibility, and doctors cannot refuse treatment
D. Permission to heal, and the injured party chooses his own doctor
Correct: A — The doubled language 'verappo yerappe' teaches both that permission is granted to a doctor to heal (R' Yishmael) and that the injurer must pay medical costs even when already paying damage compensation (Rav Pappa in Rava's name).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rava, how is loss of livelihood assessed for someone whose leg was broken?
A. As a watchman of cucumbers
B. As a watchman at the opening of a courtyard
C. As one who grinds at a mill
D. His entire value, as he is unfit for employment
Correct: B — Rava teaches that a broken leg prevents even cucumber-field watching (which requires walking). So the court views him as a courtyard watchman — a sedentary job he can still perform.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which case illustrates liability for medical costs ALONE, without any damage?
A. Locking someone in a room and forcing idleness
B. Spitting in another person's face
C. Applying a drug that whitened another's flesh without causing loss of function
D. Burning someone on the fingernail without causing a bruise
Correct: C — When someone applies a strong drug that whitens another's flesh — there is no damage (loss of function), but the person must pay medical costs to restore the skin color. This is the Gemara's example of medical costs applying independently.
