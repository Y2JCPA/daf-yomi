5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, why are heirs exempt from returning interest money their father collected?
A. Because the heir's domain is like a purchaser's domain
B. Because the Torah's command to return interest applies only to the lender, not his children
C. Because interest money cannot be identified after the father's death
D. Because minors are always exempt from monetary obligations
Correct: B — Rava derives from Leviticus 25:36 ('that your brother may live with you') that the obligation to return interest is personal to the lender — the Torah cautioned 'him,' not his son.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
If heirs slaughter and eat a borrowed cow thinking it belonged to their father, what must they pay?
A. The full market value of the cow
B. Nothing — they acted in good faith
C. The value of inexpensive meat
D. Double the value as a penalty
Correct: C — Rava rules that heirs who unknowingly slaughter a borrowed cow pay only the value of inexpensive meat (בשר בזול), not full market value.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why is someone who slaughters a BORROWED cow on Shabbat exempt from payment, while someone who slaughters a STOLEN cow on Shabbat must pay?
A. Borrowing is a lesser offense than stealing
B. The thief's monetary liability preceded the Shabbat violation, but the borrower's liability arose simultaneously with it
C. The borrower had permission from the owner
D. Shabbat violations always override monetary penalties
Correct: B — Rav Pappa explains: the thief was already liable for theft before Shabbat, so the payment obligation pre-exists. The borrower's liability only arises at the moment of slaughter — simultaneously with the Shabbat violation — so kim leh bid'rabbah mineh applies.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Under what condition does Shmuel allow testimony to be accepted without the defendant present?
A. Whenever the plaintiff requests it
B. Only when the court has already opened the case and summoned the defendant, but he didn't come
C. Only when the disputed amount exceeds a certain threshold
D. Only when both parties are from the same city
Correct: B — Mar Ukva clarifies Shmuel's view: testimony in absentia is permitted only when the court already opened the case, sent for the defendant, and he failed to appear.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In Rava's collection procedure, what happens during the 90-day ostracism period?
A. The court immediately seizes the debtor's land
B. The court waits, assuming in each 30-day period that the debtor is trying to obtain funds
C. The debtor is imprisoned until he pays
D. The creditor may take any movable property
Correct: B — The court divides 90 days into three charitable phases: perhaps he's borrowing money (days 1–30), perhaps he's selling property (31–60), perhaps his buyer is obtaining funds (61–90). Only after this full period does the court issue an adrakhta.
