QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the order of tooth and eye injuries to a Canaanite slave matter financially?
A. The first injury triggers emancipation, so the second injury's damages go to a free person (not the master)
B. Only the more severe injury counts for emancipation purposes
C. The master can choose which injury to compensate
D. Both injuries must be compensated regardless of order
Correct: A — The first injury frees the slave. After emancipation, the second injury is inflicted on a free person, so damages for it are not owed to the master — changing the master's total liability based on which injury came first.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rava's principle regarding hakchashah (contradiction) and hazamah (conspiring testimony)?
A. Contradiction completely replaces hazamah
B. Contradiction is the start of determining testimony is conspiring
C. Contradiction prevents hazamah from ever applying
D. Contradiction and hazamah are identical legal categories
Correct: B — Rava holds that hakchashah techilat hazamah — contradiction of testimony is the beginning of the hazamah process. This means testimony that was contradicted can still be subject to the full conspiring witness penalty.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara prove that R' Elazar (not R' Yochanan) holds contradicted-then-conspiring capital witnesses are NOT killed?
A. R' Elazar says such witnesses pay monetary damages instead
B. R' Elazar rules contradicted capital witnesses are flogged — which wouldn't apply if they faced potential death penalty
C. R' Elazar explicitly states his position in a separate teaching
D. R' Elazar exempts all conspiring witnesses from capital punishment
Correct: B — R' Elazar rules contradicted capital witnesses are flogged. If he held they could later be killed via hazamah, flogging couldn't apply — because a prohibition carrying a death penalty (lav she-nitten l'azharas misat beit din) does not carry flogging.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Abaye require that the master had already stood trial before the conspiring witnesses testified?
A. Otherwise the master would be exempt from all liability
B. Otherwise the conspiring witnesses would owe the entire slave value, not just the eye value
C. The court cannot hear the case without a prior judgment
D. Jewish law requires two trials for slave injury cases
Correct: B — If the master hadn't already been found liable, the conspiring witnesses would have been imposing liability from scratch — making them responsible for the entire slave's value, not just the difference between eye and tooth values.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How do the Sages refute Rav Idi bar Avin's proof for Rava from the mishna about theft and slaughter witnesses?
A. They argue the mishna discusses a different type of witness entirely
B. They say the witnesses were rendered conspiring on slaughter first, then theft — so slaughter testimony was never merely contradicted
C. They claim the fourfold/fivefold payment is only rabbinic, not biblical
D. They argue that theft and slaughter are considered a single testimony
Correct: B — The Sages argue the mishna refers to a case where hazamah on slaughter occurred before hazamah on theft. This way, the slaughter testimony was rendered conspiring directly — never passing through a stage of being merely contradicted.
