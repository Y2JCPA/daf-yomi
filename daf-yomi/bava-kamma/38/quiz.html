5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara cite TWO verses (Habakkuk 3:6 and Deuteronomy 33:2) as sources for the asymmetric ox-damage rule?
A. Each verse covers a different type of damage
B. In case Habakkuk is used for Rav Mattana/Rav Yosef's expositions, Deuteronomy provides an independent source
C. One verse applies to innocuous oxen and the other to forewarned
D. The two verses contradict each other and must be reconciled
Correct: B — The baraita provides a backup verse because if Habakkuk 3:6 is needed for Rav Mattana's exposition (exile) or Rav Yosef's (releasing prohibitions), then Deuteronomy 33:2 independently teaches the monetary ruling.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Chanina, what is the status of a gentile who voluntarily performs a mitzva?
A. They receive no reward whatsoever
B. They receive the same reward as a Jew who performs the same mitzva
C. They receive reward, but less than one who is commanded and performs
D. They are punished for performing mitzvot they were not commanded
Correct: C — R' Chanina teaches that one who is commanded and fulfills (metzuveh ve'oseh) is greater than one who is not commanded and fulfills (eino metzuveh ve'oseh). Gentiles still receive reward — just at a lesser level.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did God spare Moab and Ammon from destruction despite their hostility to Israel?
A. They had not yet committed enough sins to warrant destruction
B. God had two righteous women — Ruth and Naamah — to bring forth from those nations
C. They were protected by their ancestor Abraham's merit
D. God made an unconditional covenant with Lot
Correct: B — God told Moses He had 'two virtuous fledglings (feridot)' to extract: Ruth the Moabite (foremother of David) and Naamah the Ammonite (wife of Solomon, mother of Rehoboam).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did the younger daughter of Lot's descendants (Ammon) receive GREATER protection than the elder's (Moab)?
A. Ammon was a smaller nation and needed more protection
B. The younger daughter used a euphemism ('Ben Ami') instead of explicitly referencing her father
C. Ammon accepted the Torah before Moab did
D. The younger daughter repented while the elder did not
Correct: B — R' Ḥiyya bar Abba in the name of R' Yoḥanan: God rewards even pleasant speech. The younger daughter's euphemistic naming ('son of my nation' vs. 'from my father') earned Ammon full protection — even forced labor was forbidden.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does R' Abbahu resolve R' Meir's seemingly contradictory treatments of Samaritans (full damages like gentiles, but impure bloodstains like Jews)?
A. R' Meir changed his opinion over time
B. Samaritans are true converts by Torah law, but R' Meir imposed a monetary fine to prevent assimilation
C. Different types of Samaritans have different statuses
D. The bloodstain ruling is only rabbinic while the damages ruling is biblical
Correct: B — R' Abbahu explains that Samaritans are genuine converts — their blood is impure like any Jew's. However, R' Meir imposed a punitive fine giving them gentile-level liability for damages, specifically to discourage Jews from assimilating with them.
