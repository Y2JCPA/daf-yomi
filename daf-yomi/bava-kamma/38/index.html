Cross-Community Ox Damages & the Status of Non-Jews

Mishna
A Jew's ox that gores a gentile's ox — exempt. A gentile's ox that gores a Jew's ox — pays full damages.
The Sages
This is difficult either way! If "of another" is precise — gentile's ox goring Jew's should also be exempt. If imprecise — Jew's ox goring gentile's should be liable!
R' Abbahu
From Habakkuk 3:6 — "vayyatter": God saw the nations didn't keep the 7 Noahide laws, so He permitted (hittir) their money to Israel.
R' Yochanan
From Deuteronomy 33:2 — "He appeared from Mount Paran": when the Torah was given, gentile property was revealed/granted to Israel.
Core Issue The asymmetric rule is derived from the nations' failure to uphold the Noahide covenant — explored through multiple scriptural expositions.

The Mishna's Asymmetric Rule

ScenarioInnocuous OxForewarned Ox
Jew's ox → Gentile's oxExemptExempt
Gentile's ox → Jew's oxFull damagesFull damages
Jew's ox → Jew's oxHalf damagesFull damages
The Difficulty The phrase "ox of another" (Exodus 21:35) creates a contradiction: if it limits liability to fellow Jews, then gentiles goring Jews should also be exempt. If it doesn't limit, Jews should pay for goring gentile oxen too.

Three Expositions of "Vayyatter" (Habakkuk 3:6)

R' Abbahu
Vayyatter = hittir (permitted): God saw the 7 Noahide laws were not fulfilled, so He permitted their money to Israel — creating the asymmetric liability.
Rav Mattana
Vayyatter = exile: God saw they didn't keep the 7 mitzvot and exiled them from their land. Proven from Leviticus 11:21 — lenatter means 'to leap' from one place to another.
Rav Yosef
Vayyatter = released their prohibitions: God permitted the nations to violate their own commandments — they are no longer held to the same standard.
Gemara
But if transgressions became permitted, a sinner profits! Rather: even if they fulfill mitzvot, they receive a lesser reward — like one who is not commanded.
Key Distinction The baraita cites both verses (Habakkuk and Deuteronomy) because if Habakkuk is used for Rav Mattana or Rav Yosef's readings, Deuteronomy 33:2 provides an independent source.

Commanded vs. Not Commanded — Reward Structure

Rav Yosef: Nations' prohibitions were released
↓
Gemara objects: A transgressor profits?!
↓
Mar b. Rabbana: Not that sin is permitted — they just get lesser reward
↓
Objection
R' Meir: A gentile who studies Torah is like a Kohen Gadol (Lev. 18:5)
Resolution
R' Chanina: Metzuveh ve'oseh (commanded & performs) is greater than eino metzuveh ve'oseh (voluntary)
R' Chanina's Principle One who is commanded and fulfills a mitzva receives greater reward than one who fulfills it voluntarily. Gentiles still receive reward — just less.

Ruth & Naamah — Two Fledglings from Moab & Ammon

🏔️ God commands: Do not attack Moab or Ammon
↓
❓ Why spare nations that harmed Israel?
↓
🕊️ God: I have two virtuous fledglings (feridot) to extract
↓
👑 Ruth the Moabite — foremother of King David's dynasty
↓
👑 Naamah the Ammonite — wife of Solomon, mother of Rehoboam
↓
📖 Lot's elder daughter preceded younger by 4 generations: Obed → Yishai → David → Solomon
Lesson A person should always hasten to perform a mitzva — the elder daughter's initiative resulted in her descendants entering Israel four generations before the younger daughter's.

Moab vs. Ammon — Reward for Pleasant Speech

Elder Daughter → Moab

  • Named her son Moab — openly: "from my father"
  • God's command: "Do not be at enmity with Moab"
  • Prohibition: Full-fledged battle only
  • Permitted: Imposing forced labor (angarya)

Younger Daughter → Ben Ami

  • Named her son Ben Ami — euphemism: "son of my nation"
  • God's command: "Do not harass them nor contend"
  • Prohibition: All hostility forbidden
  • Even forced labor prohibited
R' Ḥiyya bar Abba / R' Yoḥanan God does not deprive any creature of its reward. The younger daughter's euphemistic speech earned her descendants greater protection — even modesty in language is rewarded.
