Eye for an Eye: Literal or Monetary?

R' Dostai ben Yehuda
A small person injures a large person — how can you inflict an equal wound? It must mean money.
Sages
Just as with murder — a small person kills a large person, we still execute him. The punishment need not be physically identical.
R' Shimon ben Yochai
A blind person who blinds another — how can you take his eye? It must mean money.
Sages
Where possible, apply the literal punishment; where not, exempt him. A tereifa who kills proves this principle.
Abaye
The School of Ḥizkiyya proves it: we cannot take an eye AND a life for an eye — so it must be monetary.
KEY CONCLUSION Through five independent derivations, the Gemara establishes that "an eye for an eye" means monetary compensation, not literal physical retaliation.

R' Shimon ben Yochai vs. The Sages

R' Shimon ben Yochai
A blind person who blinds another, a person with a severed limb who severs another's — how do you fulfill 'eye for an eye' literally?
R' Shimon ben Yochai
The Torah says 'one manner of law' (mishpat echad) — the law must be equal for all. So it must mean money!
Sages
Where possible, apply the literal punishment. Where not possible, exempt him. This doesn't violate 'one manner of law.'
Sages
Proof: A tereifa (terminally ill person) who kills a healthy person — we exempt him from execution. Same principle applies.
REJECTED The Sages show that unequal application of punishment does not violate the principle of equal law — some cases are simply exempt.

School of R' Yishmael: The Superfluous Verse

Leviticus 24:19 already states:
'As he has done, so shall it be done to him'
↓
Why does v. 20 repeat:
'So shall it be given unto him' (ken yinaten bo)?
↓
'Giving' (נתינה) = a term for monetary transfer
↓
The superfluous verse teaches:
monetary restitution, not literal injury
METHOD R' Yishmael's school derives law from redundant language — if a verse is unnecessary, it must teach something new.

School of R' Ḥiyya vs. School of Ḥizkiyya

School of R' Ḥiyya

  • Source: Deuteronomy 19:21 (conspiring witnesses)
  • 'Hand for hand' = compensation given hand to hand
  • Superfluous phrase: v. 19 already says 'as he purposed to do'
  • Extra 'hand for hand' must teach: MONETARY payment

School of Ḥizkiyya (via Abaye)

  • Source: Exodus 21:23-24
  • 'Eye for eye' AND 'life for life'
  • If literal: injuring an eye could accidentally KILL the person
  • Torah can't demand eye AND life for just an eye — must be MONEY
ACCEPTED Both derivations stand — the Sages do not refute them. Together with R' Yishmael's school, three independent proofs establish monetary compensation.
