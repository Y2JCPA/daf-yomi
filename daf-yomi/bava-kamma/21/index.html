Must a Squatter Pay Rent?

R' Abba bar Zavda
Sends a question to Rav Huna: Does one who resides in another's courtyard without his knowledge need to pay rent?
Rav Huna (via Rabba)
He does not need to pay rent.
Gemara
But Rav Huna also said: If you rent from Reuven and it turns out to be Shimon's house — you must pay Shimon!
Gemara Resolution
No contradiction: A courtyard that stands to be rented — pay. One that does not stand to be rented — exempt.
KEY DISTINCTION The entire sugya hinges on whether the courtyard is one that the owner would normally rent out (עומדת להשכרה) or not.

PRINCIPLE This is the classic case of זה נהנה וזה לא חסר — one who benefits while causing no loss to the other.

Why Is the Squatter Exempt?

Rav
Because of the verse: "Desolation remains in the city, and the gate is stricken unto ruin" (Isaiah 24:12) — an empty house collapses.
Rav Yosef
A home that is lived in is settled and safeguarded; a home that is not lived in has no one to maintain it.
Gemara
What is the practical difference between Rav and Rav Yosef?
Gemara
A house used to store wood and straw — not empty, but no one lives there. Per Rav Yosef, the squatter is still exempt.
MAR BAR RAV ASHI "I saw this ruin and it gores like an ox" — an uninhabited house deteriorates devastatingly fast.

Rav Naḥman & the Mansion on Orphans' Land

EventDetail
A man builds a mansion (apadna)On a garbage heap belonging to orphans
PreviouslyCarmanians (nomads) lived there and paid the orphans a small rent
The builder's actionHe removed the Carmanians, costing the orphans their income
Rav Naḥman's ruling"Go appease the orphans" — he refused
Final resultRav Naḥman confiscated his mansion
NOTE This does NOT prove a squatter must always pay. Here the orphans had actual lost income from the displaced Carmanians — a special case.

Animal Eating at the Side of the Road: Rav vs. Shmuel

Rav

  • Animal turns its head from the public domain to eat from the side of the road
  • Owner pays only for benefit derived (מה שנהנית)
  • Analogous to one who digs a pit in his own property — it's the produce owner's fault for placing items near the road

Shmuel

  • Even if the animal turns its head from the public domain
  • Owner pays for full damage (מה שהזיקה)
  • The animal sees the produce — unlike a hidden pit, one cannot claim unawareness
MISHNA RULE Eating in the public square → pays for benefit. Eating from the side of the square → pays for damage.

Tannaitic Dispute: Eating in the Public Domain

R' Meir & R' Yehuda
Ate in the public square → pays for benefit. Ate from the sides → pays for damage. Liability for Eating (שן) applies only in private property.
R' Yosei & R' Elazar
It is not typical for an animal to eat in the public domain — only to walk there. Therefore the owner is liable even in the public domain.
Gemara
R' Meir: "It consumed in the field of another" — not the public domain.
Gemara
R' Yosei: The verse excludes only when an animal eats on its own owner's property — not the public domain.
ILFA & R' OSHAYA If an animal ate produce off the back of another animal in the public domain — is this like eating on private property? R' Yosei says yes.
