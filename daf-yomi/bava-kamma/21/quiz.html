QUESTION 1 OF 5
When is a squatter exempt from paying rent for living in another's courtyard without permission?
A. When the courtyard does not stand to be rented out
B. When the squatter improves the property
C. When the owner is out of town
D. Always — a squatter never pays
Correct: A — The Gemara resolves Rav Huna's seemingly contradictory rulings by distinguishing: a courtyard that does not stand to be rented — the squatter is exempt (זה נהנה וזה לא חסר). A courtyard that stands to be rented — the squatter must pay.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the practical difference between Rav's reasoning (Isaiah verse about desolation) and Rav Yosef's reasoning (a lived-in home is maintained)?
A. A house used to store wood and straw — not desolate, but not lived in
B. A house in a city vs. a rural house
C. A new house vs. an old house
D. A large house vs. a small house
Correct: A — Per Rav, only a truly desolate house benefits from a squatter. Per Rav Yosef, even a house storing wood and straw (not desolate but not lived in) would benefit from habitation, so the squatter is still exempt.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Rav Naḥman confiscate the mansion built on the orphans' garbage heap?
A. Because the builder displaced Carmanians who had been paying rent to the orphans
B. Because a squatter must always pay rent
C. Because building on a garbage heap is forbidden
D. Because the orphans needed the land for farming
Correct: A — The Carmanians had been paying the orphans rent. When the builder removed them and refused Rav Naḥman's instruction to compensate the orphans for lost income, Rav Naḥman confiscated the mansion.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Mishna, if a dog takes a cake with a coal and ignites a grain stack, what does the owner pay?
A. Full damage for the cake, half damage for the grain stack
B. Full damage for both
C. Half damage for both
D. Full damage for the grain stack, half damage for the cake
Correct: A — The dog is forewarned for eating (שן), so the owner pays full damage for the cake. But igniting grain with a coal is unusual behavior, making the dog tam (innocuous) regarding fire — so the owner pays only half damage for the grain stack.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If a goat fell (rather than jumped) from a rooftop and broke vessels, what is the owner's liability according to the Mishna's view?
A. Exempt — negligence that ends in accident exempts the owner
B. Full damage — the owner should not have let it on the roof
C. Half damage — the animal is tam for falling
D. Pays for benefit derived only
Correct: A — The Mishna implies that since the animal fell accidentally (even though the owner was negligent in letting it climb up), the principle of 'begins with negligence, ends in accident' (תחילתו בפשיעה וסופו באונס) applies, and the owner is exempt.
