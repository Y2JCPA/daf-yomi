Bava Kamma 75 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav, what does the doubled expression 'himmatze timmatze' teach?
A. A thief pays double and a bailee pays double
B. One who admits to a fine is exempt even if witnesses later testify
C. Witnesses must testify within a fixed time period
D. The court must have a minimum of three judges
Correct: B — Rav derives from the redundancy of two verses excluding self-incrimination that admission exempts even retroactively — the second verse adds the novelty that subsequent witness testimony cannot override the admission.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Shmuel interpret the doubled expression 'himmatze timmatze'?
A. It excludes self-incrimination from fines
B. It teaches that a thief himself pays double payment, not just a bailee
C. It requires two sets of witnesses for every fine
D. It means the stolen item must be physically found
Correct: B — Shmuel uses the double expression to teach that the thief himself pays kefel (double payment), not only the bailee who falsely swore — as derived by the school of Chizkiyya.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rav Hamnuna's key distinction about when admission exempts from a fine?
A. Admission only exempts if made before three judges
B. Admission only exempts when it simultaneously creates an obligation to pay the principal
C. Admission exempts only for theft but not for injury
D. Admission only exempts if made voluntarily, not under duress
Correct: B — Rav Hamnuna holds that if the theft was already established by witnesses, admitting to slaughter doesn't obligate the principal — so it's not a 'real' admission. Only when the admission itself creates a principal payment obligation does it exempt from the fine.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does admitting to theft exempt the thief from the entire fourfold/fivefold payment (not just the double)?
A. Because the Torah only mandates 4x or 5x — removing the double portion leaves 3x or 4x, which are not Torah-prescribed fines
B. Because once you admit theft, slaughter cannot be proven
C. Because the court has discretion to reduce all penalties upon confession
D. Because the fourfold/fivefold payment requires a separate admission for each component
Correct: A — The fourfold/fivefold payment is an indivisible unit that includes the principal and double payment. Once the double-payment component is removed through admission, the remaining 3x (sheep) or 4x (ox) is not a recognized Torah fine.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the case of conspiring witnesses, what does Sumakhos hold when the slaughter-witnesses are found to be conspiring?
A. The thief is completely exempt from all payment
B. The conspiring witnesses pay the entire fourfold/fivefold payment
C. The conspiring witnesses pay double and the thief pays the remaining portion (3x for ox, 2x for sheep)
D. The thief pays double and the conspiring witnesses pay the rest
Correct: C — Sumakhos splits the total fine: the conspiring witnesses pay the double payment (the portion they tried to falsely impose), and the thief — who actually stole — pays the remaining threefold (ox) or twofold (sheep) portion.
