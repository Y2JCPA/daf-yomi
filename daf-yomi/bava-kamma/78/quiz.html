Bava Kamma 78 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, where does Rava's principle that 'seh' excludes kilayim actually apply?
A. Sacrificial animals on the altar
B. Animal tithe (ma'aser behema)
C. Redemption of a firstborn donkey
D. Firstborn animals
Correct: C — The Gemara systematically eliminates sacrifices, tithe, and firstborn — all derivable from other sources — leaving only pidyon peter chamor (firstborn donkey redemption) as the context for Rava's principle.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
If someone vowed a burnt-offering, set aside a bull, and it was stolen, what does Rava conclude the thief must repay?
A. Only a bull of equal value
B. A sheep suffices (according to the Rabbis)
C. Cash value of the bull
D. Nothing, since it was consecrated property
Correct: B — Rava resolves that the thief can repay with a sheep (Rabbis) or even a bird (R' Elazar ben Azarya), since the vow-obligation defines what is owed, not the specific animal set aside.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Mishna, which scenario WOULD require fourfold/fivefold payment?
A. Thief sold the animal except for 1/100th
B. Thief ripped open the animal instead of proper shechita
C. Thief performed valid halakhic slaughter on the stolen animal
D. Thief tore the windpipe during slaughter
Correct: C — The Mishna states that fourfold/fivefold payment applies only when the animal is entirely sold or slaughtered with valid halakhic shechita. All other listed scenarios result in only double payment.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the dispute between Rav and Levi regarding the partial sale exemption?
A. Whether partial sale exempts from double payment
B. Whether fleece counts as a retained portion that exempts from 4x/5x payment
C. Whether the animal must be alive at time of sale
D. Whether partnership counts as partial ownership
Correct: B — Rav holds the thief must retain a part permitted through shechita (not fleece or horns). Levi holds even retaining just the fleece makes the sale incomplete enough to exempt from the enhanced payment.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara argue that two EXCLUSIONS from 'bull or sheep...or goat' make more sense than two INCLUSIONS?
A. Because exclusions are always preferred in halakhic exegesis
B. Because if kilayim were included, including an animal resembling another species would be obvious and redundant
C. Because the Torah never uses inclusive language
D. Because diverse kinds are always forbidden in all contexts
Correct: B — If kilayim (diverse kinds) were included by the first phrase, there would be no need for a second inclusion for animals merely resembling another species — they are less problematic than kilayim. Two exclusions are needed because even after excluding kilayim, one might still think animals resembling another species are permitted.
