Bava Kamma 90 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, which three events abrogate an existing lien on property?
A. Consecration, chametz prohibition, and emancipation
B. Sale, inheritance, and emancipation
C. Consecration, Shabbat, and marriage
D. Theft, loss, and destruction
Correct: A — Rava states that hekdesh (consecration), the advent of the chametz prohibition, and emancipation (shichrur) all abrogate any existing lien.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the case of a slave sold with 30 days of service retained by the seller, R' Yosei exempts both owners from capital punishment. Why?
A. He holds neither has any ownership status
B. He is uncertain whether kinyan peirot equals kinyan ha-guf, and we rule leniently in capital cases
C. He holds both are full owners simultaneously
D. He believes the slave is ownerless during the transition period
Correct: B — R' Yosei is uncertain about the status of usage rights, and since this is a capital case, the ruling follows the principle of leniency in cases of doubt (safek nefashot le-hakel).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was R' Akiva's response when the man showed that the woman uncovered her own head for an issar's worth of oil?
A. He reduced the payment to match the value of an issar
B. He ruled the woman forfeited her claim by her behavior
C. He said it was irrelevant — one who injures oneself is exempt, but others who injure are liable
D. He increased the penalty as punishment for the man's trickery
Correct: C — R' Akiva's principle: self-harm carries no penalty, but that has no bearing on another person's liability. The man still owed the full 400 dinars.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The Gemara resolves that R' Yosei HaGelili's penalty of 100 dinars refers to which type of coinage?
A. State coinage (worth 12.5 Tyrian dinars)
B. Tyrian coinage (full silver value)
C. Half-Tyrian coinage
D. The Gemara leaves the question unresolved (teiku)
Correct: B — The incident with R' Yehuda Nesia, who explicitly ordered payment of 100 dinars of Tyrian coinage, proves this is the correct reading of the mishna.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Eliezer, what is required for a master to receive the 'day or two' exemption from capital punishment when his slave dies?
A. Only that the slave is under his authority (usage rights)
B. Only that the slave is his property (ownership)
C. Both authority over the slave AND ownership of the slave
D. Neither — R' Eliezer abolishes the exemption entirely
Correct: C — R' Eliezer reads 'for he is his money' (Exodus 21:21) as requiring the slave to be uniquely his — meaning the master must have both full ownership (kinyan ha-guf) and usage authority (kinyan peirot).
