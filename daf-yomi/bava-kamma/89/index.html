Who Receives the Financial Advantage of the Ketubah?

Rav Idi bar Avin
Conspiring witnesses who testified a woman received her ketubah pay only the tovat hana'ah (financial advantage), not the full value.
Abaye
The tovat hana'ah money is given only to the wife — if it went to the husband, the witnesses could say they caused her no loss.
Rav Shalman
Even if the husband receives it, there is a gain to the household that benefits the wife too.
Rava
Halakha: The financial advantage goes only to the wife, and the husband does not even garner profits from that money.
KEY PRINCIPLE The Sages instituted profits of her property for the husband, but not profits of the profits.

How Is Tovat Hana'ah Calculated?

Witnesses testify woman already received her ketubah
↓
Witnesses are found to be conspiring witnesses (eidim zomemim)
↓
Do they pay the full ketubah value?
No
They pay only the tovat hana'ah — the price someone would pay to purchase the rights to the ketubah
Calculation
Factor in: if she is widowed/divorced → purchaser collects full value. If she dies first → husband inherits, purchaser gets nothing.
DEFINITION Tovat Hana'ah = the discounted present value of a future contingent right to collect the ketubah.

Does the Ordinance of Usha Exist?

Rav Idi bar Avin
If there were no ordinance of Usha, a woman could sell her usufruct property outright — so why would her husband inherit from her? The tovat hana'ah concept proves the ordinance exists.
Abaye
Even without the ordinance, she cannot sell guaranteed property (nikhsei tzon barzel) — so the ketubah still couldn't be sold for full value.
Rav Pappa
The mishna (87a) says a married woman who injures others is exempt because she has no money. If she could sell usufruct property, she would have money — proving the ordinance!
Gemara
Even with the ordinance, she could sell the financial advantage of usufruct property. The mishna must discuss a case where she has no usufruct property at all.
ORDINANCE OF USHA The Sages at Usha enacted that a married woman cannot sell her usufruct property (nikhsei melog) during marriage.

Types of Marital Property

Nikhsei Melog (Usufruct Property)

  • Wife retains ownership of the principal
  • Husband enjoys the profits during marriage
  • Under Ordinance of Usha: wife cannot sell
  • If she dies, husband inherits

Nikhsei Tzon Barzel (Guaranteed Property)

  • Listed in the ketubah at fixed value
  • Entirely under husband's authority
  • Wife cannot sell even without Usha ordinance
  • Husband bears risk of loss and gain
NOTE Even Abaye concedes the wife cannot sell nikhsei tzon barzel — the dispute is only about nikhsei melog.

Why Can't a Married Woman Pay Damages?

👩 A married woman injures someone and is liable for damages
↓
🚫 She cannot sell usufruct property outright (Ordinance of Usha)
↓
❓ Can she sell the tovat hana'ah of usufruct property?
↓
❓ Can she sell the tovat hana'ah of her ketubah?
↓
🔒 R' Meir: A man may not live with his wife even one hour without a ketubah
↓
⚖️ If she sells the ketubah, she might forgive the debt in her husband's favor — courts don't supervise this without cause
↓
📋 Result: She has no money with which to pay — she is exempt
RABBI MEIR'S CONCERN Without a ketubah, the wife is demeaned in his eyes and the husband will easily divorce her.
