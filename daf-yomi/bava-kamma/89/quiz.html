Bava Kamma 89 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is 'tovat hana'ah' of a marriage contract?
A. The full face value of the ketubah
B. The amount someone would pay for the contingent right to collect the ketubah
C. The husband's share of the wife's property
D. The dowry the wife brought into the marriage
Correct: B — Tovat hana'ah is the discounted price someone would pay for the right to collect the ketubah if the woman is widowed or divorced, accounting for the risk that she dies first and the purchaser gets nothing.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava, why doesn't the husband receive profits from the tovat hana'ah money?
A. Because the husband forfeited his rights by mistreating his wife
B. Because the Sages gave the husband profits of her property, but not profits of the profits
C. Because the money belongs to the conspiring witnesses
D. Because the husband already received the full ketubah value
Correct: B — Rava distinguishes between profits of the wife's property (which the husband receives) and profits of those profits (which he does not). The tovat hana'ah represents a secondary derivative, not primary property.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Abaye reject the proof for the Ordinance of Usha from the tovat hana'ah concept?
A. Because the ordinance was already proven from another source
B. Because even without the ordinance, the wife cannot sell guaranteed property (nikhsei tzon barzel)
C. Because the tovat hana'ah is too small to matter
D. Because conspiring witnesses are not reliable sources of law
Correct: B — Abaye argues that even without the Ordinance of Usha, a woman cannot sell her guaranteed property (nikhsei tzon barzel), so the ketubah's financial advantage would still be less than full value. No proof for the ordinance can be drawn.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why can't a married woman sell the tovat hana'ah of her ketubah to pay damages, according to the Gemara's conclusion?
A. Because the husband forbids the sale
B. Because she is likely to forgive the ketubah debt in her husband's favor, and courts don't supervise without cause
C. Because the Torah prohibits women from engaging in commerce
D. Because the injured party must collect directly from the husband
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that in any matter benefiting her husband, the wife will likely forgive the debt, making the sale meaningless. The courts do not trouble themselves to supervise such transactions without cause.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rava, what happens to the 'sons' inheritance' (b'nin dikhrin) stipulation when a woman sells her ketubah?
A. The stipulation is permanently voided
B. The stipulation transfers to the purchaser's sons
C. The stipulation remains in force because she sold under duress of financial need
D. The stipulation is suspended until the husband dies
Correct: C — Rava teaches that since the woman sold under financial duress, she did not intend to abrogate her sons' future inheritance rights. The b'nin dikhrin stipulation survives the sale of the ketubah.
