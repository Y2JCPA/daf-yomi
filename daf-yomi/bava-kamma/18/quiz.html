Bava Kamma 18 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Rabbis (who disagree with Sumakhos), how much does the owner pay for damage caused by pebbles propelled by his animal?
A. Full damage
B. Half damage
C. Quarter damage
D. No liability
Correct: B — The Rabbis hold that for tzrorot (pebbles), the owner pays only half the cost of the damage (chatzi nezek), while Sumakhos holds the owner pays full damage.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the case of chickens pecking a dough-covered rope causing a bucket to fall, why is pecking the rope classified as Eating (Shen) rather than Goring (Keren)?
A. Because chickens always eat rope
B. Because the rope was filthy with kneaded dough, making pecking normal behavior
C. Because Eating always applies to chickens
D. Because the damage occurred in private property
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that eating a clean rope would be atypical (Goring), but since the rope was covered in dough, pecking it is normal chicken behavior and classified as Eating (Shen), which carries full liability.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is 'koach kocho' (force of a force) in this sugya?
A. A chicken pecking a rope directly
B. A shard from the broken bucket hitting another vessel
C. Pebbles propelled by a walking animal
D. A dog carrying a coal
Correct: B — Koach kocho refers to damage that is two steps removed from the animal's action. The chicken pecks → bucket breaks → shard flies and damages another vessel. The shard damage is 'force generated by a force.'
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why can't Rava resolve his dilemma about forewarning for pebbles from the baraita about the dog and the grain stack?
A. The baraita is too old to be reliable
B. Both opinions in that baraita follow Sumakhos, not the Rabbis whose position Rava questions
C. The baraita discusses fire, not pebbles
D. Rava wasn't aware of that baraita
Correct: B — Rava's dilemma is specifically according to the Rabbis who hold half-damage for pebbles. The Gemara explains that both the first tanna and R' Elazar in the dog baraita follow Sumakhos, so it provides no evidence for the Rabbis' position.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Gemara's explanation of Rabbi Tarfon, when does he accept the 'dayyo' (sufficiency) principle?
A. He never accepts it
B. He always accepts it
C. Only when applying it limits but does not refute the a fortiori inference
D. Only in cases involving monetary damages
Correct: C — Rabbi Tarfon rejects dayyo when it would completely refute the kal va-chomer inference, but accepts it when it merely limits the inference's scope — for example, requiring payment from the animal's body rather than from land, without eliminating the full-damage liability.
