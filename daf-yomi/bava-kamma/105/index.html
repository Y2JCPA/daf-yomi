Why Is the Heir Exempt from the One-Fifth?

Gemara
If the father already stood trial, the debt is like a promissory note — the heir should pay everything, including the one-fifth!
Rav Huna b"R Yehoshua
The heir is exempt because one does not pay the one-fifth for denying a debt secured by a lien on land (shibuda d'karka).
Rava
Different explanation: the stolen item is still extant but was in the father's disakaya (sack) deposited with others. The heir swore truthfully — he didn't know his father stole it.
Gemara
So the heir pays the principal (the item exists) but not the one-fifth (his oath was honest).
TWO APPROACHES Rav Huna bar Rav Yehoshua: structural exemption (land-lien debts). Rava: factual exemption (the heir didn't know).

Must the Robber Pursue the Victim?

First Version of Rav Pappa

  • Item NOT extant → no need to pursue
  • Item IS extant → must pursue the victim
  • Reason: item may appreciate back above a peruta
  • We ARE concerned about future appreciation

Second Version of Rav Pappa

  • Item NOT extant → no need to pursue
  • Item IS extant → ALSO no need to pursue
  • We are NOT concerned about appreciation
  • Less than a peruta = forgiven either way
CONTEXT The Mishna taught that if the victim forgave all but less than a peruta, the robber need not pursue him. Rav Pappa's two versions disagree about whether an extant item changes this rule.

Rava's Depreciated Bundles

Robber stole 3 bundles worth 3 perutot total
↓
Bundles depreciate → now worth only 2 perutot total
↓
Robber returns 2 bundles
Must he return the 3rd?
↓
Yes — Obligated
Even though the 3rd bundle is now worth less than a peruta, it was originally worth a peruta
Proof: Chametz
Leavened bread after Passover is worthless but if not in its original form, robber must pay original value
RAVA'S PRINCIPLE An item that was worth a peruta at the time of robbery retains its obligation even if it later depreciates below a peruta.

Robber stole 2 bundles worth 1 peruta totalEach bundle < 1 peruta individually—
Returns 1 bundleRemaining bundle < 1 perutaIs he exempt?
Argument for exemptNo 'stolen item' exists — remainder < perutaExempt from returning
Argument for obligatedHe never returned the full stolen itemMust return the 2nd
Rava's resolutionNo stolen item here (< peruta) but also no fulfillment of the mitzva of returningExempt, but gets no credit
SUBTLE POINT Rava rules the robber is exempt — but clarifies he gets no mitzva credit for 'returning stolen property' either, since the returned portion was also less than a peruta.
