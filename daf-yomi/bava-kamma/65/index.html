When Does a Thief Pay — At Theft-Time or Trial-Time?

A thief must pay principal + double payment (+ fourfold/fivefold for ox/sheep)
↓
Rav's Rule: Principal = value at time of theft; Penalty = value at time of trial
↓
Source
Exodus 22:3 — 'ḥayyim' (alive) teaches: revive the principal to theft-time value
Challenges
Baraitot about lean→fat and fat→lean animals seem to contradict Rav
↓
Gemara resolves: Rav speaks only of market price changes, not physical changes to the animal
Key Principle The principal restores the victim to their original loss; the penalty is assessed at trial to reflect current judicial reality.

Himmatze Timmatze: Doubling the Language

אם הִמָּצֵא תִמָּצֵא בְיָדוֹ הַגְּנֵבָה
Textual Derivation The Torah uses the repetitive phrase הִמָּצֵא תִמָּצֵא (himmatze timmatze) in Exodus 22:3.

Why not simply repeat the same word twice?

The variation in wording teaches two halakhot:
1️⃣ Double payment applies wherever the stolen item is found — roof, yard, or enclosure (karpef)
2️⃣ A thief pays double even without having taken an oath

Rav vs. Rav Sheshet: Valuation at Theft or Trial?

Rav
The principal is paid at theft-time value. The double/fourfold/fivefold payment is at trial-time value.
Rav
Source: The word 'ḥayyim' (alive) in Exodus 22:3 teaches — revive (aḥayah) the principal to its theft-time value.
Rav Sheshet
Rav must have said this while half-asleep! A baraita teaches: if an animal was lean at theft and the thief fattened it, ALL payments follow theft-time value!
Sages
That's no refutation — the thief can say: 'I fattened it! Why should you benefit from my labor?'
Resolution Rav's rule applies only to market price fluctuations, not to physical changes the thief caused to the animal.

The Bailee's False Oath — R' Ḥanina's Support for Rav

A bailee (shomer) falsely claims a deposit was stolen and takes an oath
↓
Admits BEFORE witnesses come
Pays principal + one-fifth and brings a guilt-offering (asham)
Admits AFTER witnesses testify
Pays double payment + guilt-offering; one-fifth is covered by double payment
↓
R' Ḥanina asks: When does 'one-fifth = penalty portion of double'?
Only if item was worth 4 dinars at theft and 1 dinar at trial!
↓
This supports Rav: principal + one-fifth at theft-time; double payment at trial-time
The Math Item worth 4 at theft, 1 at trial: one-fifth of principal (¼ × 4) = 1 dinar. Penalty portion of double payment = 1 dinar. They match!

Rava's Alternative: Four Oaths on the Same Item

R' Ḥanina's Reading

  • Item worth 4 dinars at theft, 1 at trial
  • One-fifth = 1 dinar (¼ of 4)
  • Penalty of double = 1 dinar (at trial value)
  • Supports Rav's split valuation

Rava's Reading

  • Item worth 4 dinars at theft AND at trial
  • Bailee swore falsely FOUR times
  • Each oath adds one-fifth (¼ of principal)
  • 4 × 1 dinar = 4 dinars = penalty of double
  • ḥamishitav (plural) allows multiple fifths
Key Hebrew The plural form חֲמִישִׁתָיו (ḥamishitav) — 'its fifths' — indicates multiple one-fifth payments can accrue on a single principal.
