Bava Kamma 79 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is there 'agency for transgression' specifically in the case of theft and slaughter?
A. Because the Torah explicitly exempts theft from normal agency rules via the juxtaposition of 'slaughters' and 'sells'
B. Because the agent did not know the animal was stolen
C. Because bailees always bear the sender's liability
D. Because slaughtering is a mitzvah act that overrides normal agency
Correct: A — The Torah juxtaposes 'slaughters it or sells it' — just as selling requires another party (the buyer), slaughtering creates liability even through another party. This is a specific exception to the general rule of ein shaliach l'dvar aveirah.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does consecrating a stolen animal (hekdesh) count as a 'sale' for fourfold/fivefold payment?
A. Because hekdesh always generates monetary obligations
B. Because the ownership transfers — 'what difference if he sold to a person or sold to Heaven?'
C. Because the Kohanim are considered buyers
D. Because consecration requires a formal kinyan like a sale
Correct: B — The Gemara reasons that since the animal changes ownership — whether to an ordinary person or to the Temple treasury — it qualifies as a sale.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai, why is the thief (ganav) punished more severely than the robber (gazlan)?
A. Because the thief causes more financial damage
B. Because the thief is harder to catch
C. Because the thief showed more respect for people than for God, dishonoring the Divine
D. Because the robber acts under duress
Correct: C — The thief hides from people but not from God — treating human eyes as more powerful than Divine sight. The robber fears neither, which paradoxically shows equal treatment of God and humans.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If a thief stole AND slaughtered an animal entirely within the owner's domain, what does he pay?
A. Fourfold or fivefold payment
B. Double payment only
C. Nothing — he is completely exempt
D. The principal value only
Correct: C — The Mishna teaches that theft is not legally completed until the stolen item is removed from the owner's premises. If everything occurred within the owner's domain, there is no theft and no liability for fines.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does R' Meir say the ox carries a fivefold penalty while the sheep carries only fourfold?
A. Because oxen are more valuable than sheep
B. Because the ox performs labor, so the thief caused it to cease working — a greater loss
C. Because oxen are used for sacrifices
D. Because sheep are easier to steal and thus less culpable
Correct: B — R' Meir emphasizes the power of labor (melakhah): an ox works the field, so stealing it disrupts productivity. A sheep performs no labor, so the economic disruption is less, warranting a lower penalty.
