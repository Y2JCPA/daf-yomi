QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why can't the honey owner claim 'I was merely fooling' when he agreed to pay for the spilled wine?
A. Because the wine owner suffered a genuine financial loss to save the honey
B. Because agreements made near water are always binding
C. Because the honey owner initiated the arrangement
D. Because Beit Din witnessed the agreement
Correct: A — The Gemara distinguishes between inflated pricing under duress (where 'I was fooling' works, like the prisoner and ferryman) and cases where the other party suffers real loss — making the agreement binding.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav, if Reuven's donkey emerges from the river on its own after he rescued Shimon's donkey, what happens?
A. Shimon pays nothing since Reuven lost nothing
B. Shimon pays half the stipulated amount
C. Shimon still pays the full stipulated amount
D. They split ownership of both donkeys
Correct: C — Rav rules that it was 'from Heaven that mercy was bestowed upon him' — Reuven's good fortune does not retroactively void the valid stipulation. Shimon must still pay.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How should a caravan calculate each traveler's share of ransom paid to bandits?
A. Equally per person (number of souls)
B. According to the value of money each traveler carries
C. According to the weight of each traveler's cargo
D. The wealthiest traveler pays everything
Correct: B — The baraita teaches that ransom is calculated according to money carried, because greater wealth makes one a bigger target. Scout wages, by contrast, are divided per person.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When a boat must be lightened in a storm, how is each sailor's share calculated?
A. According to the monetary value of their cargo
B. Equally per sailor
C. According to the weight of their cargo
D. The captain decides unilaterally
Correct: C — The baraita states that the calculation is done by weight, not monetary value — because it is the weight of cargo that threatens to sink the boat, not its price.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the case of rescuing a caravan's property from bandits, when does the rescuer acquire all the property for himself?
A. Whenever he single-handedly defeats the bandits
B. When the other members explicitly renounce their property
C. When partners jointly own the goods and the rescuer declares he is saving the property for himself
D. When the rescuer is the caravan leader
Correct: C — Rami bar Ḥama explains the case involves partners: by declaring 'I rescue for myself,' the rescuer effectively dissolves the partnership and divides the property — a right any partner has unilaterally.
