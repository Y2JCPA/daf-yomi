Bava Kamma 36 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, when the injured party 'seizes' the damaging ox, what is the court's role?
A. The court orders the liable party to hand over the ox
B. The court allows the injured party to keep it but cannot compel payment initially
C. The court splits the ox's value equally between the parties
D. The court returns the ox to its owner immediately
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that seizure (תפיסה) creates a de facto right — the court won't remove the ox from the injured party's possession, but cannot compel payment ab initio.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rav Aḥa the Elder argue the mishna's first clause must refer to innocuous oxen?
A. Because the mishna mentions half-damages
B. Because the mishna uses the plural 'they are liable' and the word 'both'
C. Because R' Akiva is explicitly named in the mishna
D. Because forewarned oxen cannot cause serial damage
Correct: B — Rav Aḥa argues that 'they are liable' (plural) and 'both' only make sense for innocuous oxen, where liability attaches to each specific ox. For forewarned oxen, the man — not the oxen — would be liable.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In R' Shimon's division, after a 200-dinar ox gores three oxen, how much does the first victim receive?
A. 100 dinars
B. 50 dinars
C. 25 dinars
D. 75 dinars
Correct: C — After three gorings: last victim gets 100, second victim gets 50, and the first victim and the ox's owner split the remaining 50 — each getting 25 dinars (one gold dinar).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rava's explanation of R' Meir, why does the last victim collect first?
A. The last injury is always the most severe
B. The Torah prioritizes recent claims over older ones
C. Each prior victim who seized the ox became a paid bailee responsible for subsequent gorings
D. R' Meir holds that all creditors collect simultaneously
Correct: C — Rava explains that when the first injured party seizes the ox, he becomes a paid bailee. If the ox gores again under his watch, the new victim collects from him — creating a chain where the last victim has priority.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the practical legal difference between R' Yishmael's creditor model and R' Akiva's partnership model?
A. Only under R' Akiva can the owner sell the ox
B. Only under R' Yishmael can multiple victims collect
C. Under R' Akiva, the injured party can consecrate his share of the ox; under R' Yishmael he cannot
D. Under R' Yishmael, the injured party receives full damages
Correct: C — R' Yoḥanan states that the practical difference is consecration (הקדש): a partner (R' Akiva) can consecrate his share of the ox, but a creditor (R' Yishmael) has no ownership stake to consecrate.
