5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman's initial explanation, why is the owner of a water channel exempt when an ox falls in and dies?
A. The channel is not deep enough to have lethal fumes
B. The channel belongs to the public domain
C. Water cushions the fall and prevents injury
D. The ox was trespassing and therefore the owner is exempt
Correct: A — Rav Naḥman explains the exemption is because the channel lacks sufficient fumes to cause death (following Rav's opinion), though the impact from hitting the ground can still cause fatal damage.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why is the stoning structure built to the height of two people rather than just ten handbreadths?
A. To ensure witnesses can observe clearly
B. To fulfill 'love your fellow as yourself' — select a compassionate death
C. To match the minimum height required by Torah law for execution
D. To prevent the condemned from surviving the fall
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman citing Rabba bar Avuh derives from 'Ve'ahavta le-rei'akha kamokha' (Vayikra 19:18) that even for the condemned, we must select a compassionate death — ensuring it is quick without disfiguring the body.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the Gemara's final resolution, how does an ox falling into a 6-tefach water channel meet the 10-tefachim threshold?
A. Water in the channel counts double
B. The ox's stomach is 4 tefachim above ground, so 4 + 6 = 10
C. The surrounding embankment adds height to the channel
D. The splash creates additional fumes equivalent to 4 extra tefachim
Correct: B — The Gemara calculates: the ox's belly is 4 tefachim above the ground, and the channel is 6 tefachim deep. Since it lands on its stomach, the total fall distance is 10 tefachim.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabba bar bar Ḥana citing Shmuel bar Marta, a pit of 8 dry tefachim with 2 tefachim of water is liable. Why?
A. Water makes the pit slippery so animals cannot escape
B. Each tefach of water equals two tefachim of dry space, making it effectively 10
C. Water absorbs the fumes and makes them more concentrated
D. The water adds weight that crushes the animal upon impact
Correct: B — The principle is that 1 tefach of water is equivalent to 2 tefachim of dry space. So 8 dry + (2 water × 2) = effectively 12 tefachim, exceeding the 10-tefachim threshold with lethal fumes.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the practical difference between the Rabbis and R' Yehuda HaNasi regarding a pit dug by multiple people?
A. The Rabbis say no one is liable; R' Yehuda HaNasi says the first is liable
B. The Rabbis hold only the last person liable; R' Yehuda HaNasi holds all contributors liable
C. R' Yehuda HaNasi exempts all partners; the Rabbis hold them jointly liable
D. The Rabbis require intent; R' Yehuda HaNasi does not
Correct: B — The Rabbis hold that only the last person who brought the pit to its lethal depth is liable, while R' Yehuda HaNasi holds that all who contributed to creating the hazard share in the liability.
