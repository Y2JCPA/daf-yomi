The Ten-Handbreadth Threshold: Impact, Fumes & Shared Liability

Rav Naḥman
A pit less than 10 tefachim lacks sufficient fumes to kill — but impact from falling can kill. That's why the fallen ox is a suspected tereifah.
Rava
If impact alone can kill at less than 10, why does the stoning structure need to be the height of two people? A minimal 10 tefachim should suffice!
Rav Naḥman
"Love your fellow as yourself" (Vayikra 19:18) — even for a condemned prisoner, select a compassionate death. The extra height ensures a quick death.
Gemara
Revised answer: The ox's stomach is 4 tefachim above ground + 6 tefachim deep channel = 10 total. The standard holds.
Key Principle The 10-tefachim threshold for lethal pits remains intact — but the calculation may include the animal's own height above the ground.

Can a Shallow Pit Kill? Rav Naḥman vs. Rava

Rav Naḥman
A pit under 10 tefachim has insufficient fumes to kill (per Rav), but the impact of hitting the ground can still cause fatal organ damage.
Rava
If fumes aren't enough to kill but can injure, how do you explain the Mishnah: 'If injured by it, he is liable'?
Rav Naḥman
There are not sufficient fumes to kill, but there are sufficient fumes to cause injury — the distinction is one of degree.
Rava
But the stoning structure from Sanhedrin is the height of two people — if impact kills at less than 10, why so tall?
Rav's Principle Fumes and impact are distinct mechanisms of harm. A pit can be dangerous from impact even if its fumes alone are not lethal.

Two-Partner Pit: Who Is Liable?

Rabbis' View

  • Only the last person who failed to cover the pit is liable
  • The first partner 'transferred' responsibility by passing it
  • Exception: If the first dug a full 10 tefachim and others deepened it — all are liable

R' Yehuda HaNasi

  • Both partners bear liability for damage
  • Both had the obligation to cover the pit
  • Applies even when the second one was last to pass by
Rav Zevid's Reconciliation Where the first dug a full 10 tefachim and others deepened it further, even the Rabbis agree all are liable. The dispute is only where the first dug less than 10.

Water Equivalence in Pit Depth

📏 Rabba bar bar Ḥana: 1 tefach of water = 2 tefachim of dry space
↓
🕳️ Pit of 8 tefachim + 2 tefachim water → effectively 10 tefachim + fumes → liable
↓
❓ Pit of 9 dry + 1 water — deeper pit but less water. Sufficient fumes? Teku
↓
❓ Pit of 7 dry + 3 water — shallower pit but more water. Sufficient fumes? Teku
↓
🔓 Widening a pit's opening — reduces fumes but increases access. Rav Ashi: depends on cause of death
Unresolved Multiple dilemmas about water-depth equivalence remain teku — demonstrating the complexity of calibrating the lethal-fumes threshold.
