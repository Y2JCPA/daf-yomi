Shevi'it Produce: Soaking & Laundering

Rabbis
The verse says "For food" (Lev. 25:6) — but not for soaking flax, and not for laundering. These uses are forbidden because benefit follows consumption.
R' Yosei
The verse also says "For you" — meaning for all your needs, including soaking and laundering.
Rabbis
"For you" means: similar to "for food" — only items whose benefit and consumption coincide.
R' Yosei
I need "for food" to exclude a poultice (melogema) — a medical use that doesn't apply equally to all people.
Principle R' Yosei includes laundering (universal need) but excludes poultices (only for the sick) — the test is whether the use applies equally to every person.

Rav Huna's Two Rulings

📜 Rav Huna states: Halakha follows R' Yehoshua ben Korḥa
↓
💰 Loan by promissory note: may not collect from gentiles near their holidays
↓
🗣️ Loan by oral agreement: may collect — considered salvaging money from their possession
↓
📜 Rav Huna also states: Halakha follows R' Yehuda (on the dyer)
↓
🤔 Rav Yosef objects: R' Yehuda's view is already established as an unattributed Mishna — why restate it?
Rav Yosef's Point When the Mishna records a dispute and then restates one side as an unattributed opinion, the halakha automatically follows that opinion — no need for Rav Huna's ruling.

Agent Deviates: Two Baraitot on Wrong Grain

Baraita #1 (R' Meir)

  • Sent to buy wheat → bought barley instead
  • Loss → borne by the agent
  • Profit → kept by the agent
  • Deviation = agent acquires the item for himself
  • Agent has abrogated his agency

Baraita #2 (R' Yehuda)

  • Sent to buy wheat → bought barley instead
  • Loss → borne by the agent
  • Profit → split between agent and investor
  • Deviation ≠ agent acquiring the item
  • Original agreement still holds; agent bears risk of deviation
R' Yoḥanan The two baraitot reflect the same dispute as in the dyer case: R' Meir says deviation = acquisition; R' Yehuda says the original arrangement holds.

R' Elazar's Challenge to R' Yoḥanan

R' Elazar objects:
R' Meir's rule may be limited!
↓
Personal Use Item
Wool dyed wrong color — not usable for intended purpose → agent acquires it (R' Meir)
Merchandise
Grain bought for resale — still sellable for profit → original deal holds
↓
R' Elazar's resolution:
Both baraitot follow R' Meir!
↓
For Eating
Buyer wanted wheat to eat → barley is unwanted → agent acquires
For Resale
Bought as merchandise → deal holds → split profits, agent bears losses
Key Distinction R' Elazar distinguishes personal-use purchases (deviation = acquisition) from merchandise purchases (original deal holds).
