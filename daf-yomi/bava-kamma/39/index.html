Liability for Oxen of Incompetent Owners

Ox of deaf-mute, imbecile, or minor gores another ox
↓
Innocuous Ox (First Offense)
Owner is exempt — no steward appointed for single incident
Habitual Gorer
Court appoints steward (apotropos) and warns in steward's presence
↓
Ox rendered forewarned (mu'ad) → full damages from superior-quality property
Key Principle The court protects the public by appointing stewards for incompetent owners of dangerous animals, while balancing the rights of those who cannot legally represent themselves.

Does Forewarned Status Survive a Change of Custody?

R' Meir
When the deaf-mute regains hearing, the imbecile becomes competent, or the minor reaches majority — the ox reverts to innocuous status.
R' Yosei
The ox retains its forewarned status. The ox's nature doesn't change just because custody changes.
Sumakhos
A change of custody changes the ox's status. It must be re-warned in the new owner's presence.
R' Yosei (Baraita)
Change of custody does not change status — it's determined by the ox's own actions, not who holds custody.
Core Dispute Is forewarned status a property of the ox (R' Yosei) or of the owner-ox relationship (R' Meir/Sumakhos)?

Rava's Resolution: When Is a Steward Appointed?

Ox of deaf-mute/imbecile/minor gores once
↓
❌ No steward appointed — owner exempt as innocuous ox
↓
Ox develops reputation as habitual gorer
↓
✅ Court appoints apotropos (steward)
↓
Steward is warned → ox rendered mu'ad (forewarned)
↓
If ox gores again → full damages from superior-quality property (idit)
Rava's Insight The mishna's two clauses don't contradict: the first discusses a single incident (no steward), the second discusses a habitual gorer (steward appointed).

Collecting Damages from Orphans' Property

R' Yochanan
Damages from a forewarned ox are collected from the orphans' superior-quality property.
R' Yosei bar Chanina
Damages are collected from the steward's property. The steward is later repaid by the orphans when they grow up.
Rav Asi (via Rav Yehuda)
But the court only collects from orphans' property when interest is eroding their estate!
Rava
Don't reverse the opinions! R' Yosei bar Chanina was a judge who delved into halakha — we shouldn't attribute errors to him.
Practical Concern If stewards must pay from their own pocket, people will refuse to serve as stewards. R' Yosei bar Chanina answers: they are repaid when the orphans mature.

Sumakhos vs. The Rabbis: Steward for Innocuous Ox

Sumakhos

  • Ox retains its 'completeness' (tmimut) — no steward for innocuous ox
  • Injured party must WAIT until owner becomes competent
  • Cannot collect from body of innocuous ox without owner present
  • Change of custody RESETS forewarned status

The Rabbis

  • Court DOES appoint steward for innocuous ox
  • Ox can be rendered forewarned in steward's presence
  • Injured party can collect damages immediately
  • Status follows the OX, not the custody arrangement
Word Play Sumakhos says betammuto (in its innocuousness) — reread as bitmimuto (in its completeness): the owner's share is not reduced.
