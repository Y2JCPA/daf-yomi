↓
Was the door locked appropriately?
Yes — Proper Lock
Animals escape anyway → Owner EXEMPT
No — Improper Lock
Animals escape and damage → Owner LIABLE
Key Principle The Torah limits the required standard of safeguarding for Pit, Fire, Eating, and Trampling — reasonable precaution suffices.

Why Is 'Good' Missing from the First Tablets?

R' Ḥanina ben Agil
Why does the first version of the Ten Commandments omit the word טוב (good) from honoring parents?
R' Ḥiyya bar Abba
Before you ask why it's omitted, ask me if it's omitted — I'm not sure of the exact wording! Go ask R' Tanḥum bar Ḥanilai.
Shmuel bar Naḥum
The first tablets were destined to be broken after the Golden Calf. Had 'good' appeared in them, all good would have ceased from Israel.
Rav Ashi
If טוב had been written on those broken tablets, God forbid, all goodness would have departed from Israel when they shattered.
Insight The word טוב was reserved for the second tablets — ensuring goodness would endure permanently for the Jewish people.

The Letter Tet (ט) in Dreams

R' Yehoshua: Seeing ט in a dream is a good sign
↓
Why? Maybe ט appears in destructive verses too?
↓
The first ט in the entire Torah appears in טוֹב — 'And God saw that the light was good' (Gen. 1:4)
Principle From בראשית until Genesis 1:4, the letter ט does not appear anywhere. Its Torah debut in a context of goodness makes it an auspicious omen.

Diverse Kinds (Kilayim) in Birds & Animals

Birds as Kilayim

  • Cock, peacock (טַוָּס), and pheasant — all kilayim with each other
  • Even though they are reared together, they are separate species (Rav Ḥaviva)
  • Reish Lakish: Crossbreeding sea creatures also violates kilayim

Domestic vs. Wild Goose

  • Shmuel: They are kilayim with each other
  • Not because of beak length (Rava bar Rav Ḥanan's objection — camels differ in neck size but aren't kilayim)
  • Abaye: Wild goose — testicles external; Domestic — internal
  • Rav Pappa: Wild goose lays one egg at a time; Domestic lays several at once
Key Distinction Superficial physical differences (beak/neck size) don't determine species — reproductive anatomy does.

Mishna: Safeguarding Animals in a Pen

ScenarioLiability
Locked door appropriately, sheep escapeExempt — proper safeguarding
Locked door inappropriately, sheep escapeLiable — negligence
Locked properly, wall breached at nightExempt — unforeseeable
Bandits breach wall, sheep escapeExempt (owner); bandits may be liable
Bandits take sheep out, sheep damageBandits liable
Left animal in the sun or with minor/deaf-muteLiable — negligence
Conveyed to a shepherdShepherd liable — assumes responsibility
Definition Appropriate locking = door can withstand a typical wind (רוח מצויה) without collapsing or opening.

Animal Falls into a Garden — Damage Appraisal

🐑 Animal falls into garden (not its usual manner)
↓
💰 Owner pays only for the benefit derived (hana'ah), not full damage
↓
🐑 Animal descends normally into garden
↓
💰 Owner pays for full damage caused
↓
📐 Court appraises a beit se'a (large area) — damage measured in context of surrounding field
↓
📉 Damage appears less significant in larger context → smaller payment
R' Shimon's Exception If the animal ate ripe produce, the owner pays full value per se'a consumed — no contextual reduction.
