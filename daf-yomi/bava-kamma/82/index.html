Three Sets of Ten: Joshua, Ezra & Jerusalem

📜 Joshua stipulated 10 conditions for land apportionment (attributed to R' Yehoshua ben Levi)
↓
✡️ Ezra the Scribe instituted 10 ordinances for communal Jewish life
↓
📖 Torah reading, court schedules, laundry, garlic, baking, modesty, immersion
↓
🏛️ 10 special laws of Jerusalem — due to its unique status as shared tribal territory
↓
🐔 Restrictions: no chickens, kilns, gardens, balconies, garbage dumps, or overnight corpses
Key Theme Three great legislators — Joshua, Ezra, and the Sages — each established sets of ten enactments shaping communal life in Eretz Yisrael.

Who Taught Joshua's Ten Stipulations?

R' Avin / R' Yochanan
Joshua also stipulated that one brings bikkurim from a tree leaning into another's field — that's more than ten conditions!
Gemara
The baraita listing exactly ten was taught by R' Yehoshua ben Levi, an amora. R' Yochanan, another amora, may disagree.
Rav Geviha of Bei Katil
I have an explicit version: R' Tanchum and R' Berayes say in the name of R' Yehoshua ben Levi — Joshua stipulated ten conditions.
Resolution Since both are amoraim, R' Yochanan can add stipulations beyond the ten listed by R' Yehoshua ben Levi.

Torah Reading: From Moshe to Ezra

After the Red Sea, Israel traveled 3 days without Torah and grew weary
↓
The prophets instituted Torah reading on Shabbat, Monday & Thursday
↓
Original format: 1 reader, 3 verses — or 3 readers, 3 verses total
↓
Ezra expanded: always 3 readers, 10 verses total
↓
10 verses correspond to the 10 batlanim (communal officers) of each city
Principle "Water" in Exodus 15:22 is interpreted as Torah — Israel must never go 3 days without it.

Garlic on Shabbat Eve: Five Benefits

Physical Benefits

  • 🧄 It satisfies hunger
  • 🔥 It warms the body
  • ✨ It makes the countenance shine

Intimate Benefits

  • 💧 It increases sperm production
  • 🪱 It kills intestinal parasites
  • ❤️ Some say: instills love, removes jealousy
Proof Text "Like a tree planted by streams of water, bringing forth fruit in its season" (Psalms 1:3) — R' Yehuda: this refers to one who has relations every Shabbat eve.

Ten Special Laws of Jerusalem

LawReasoning
Houses never become permanently soldJerusalem was not apportioned to any tribe
No eglah arufah (broken-necked heifer)Not included in "the land… to possess it"
Cannot become an ir ha-nidachat (idolatrous city)Not "one of your cities… to dwell there"
No house-leprosy (tzara'at)Not "a house of the land of your possession"
No projections or balconiesDanger of corpse-impurity / pilgrim injury
No garbage dumpsAttract impure creatures (sheratzim)
No kilnsUnsightly smoke mars Jerusalem's beauty
No gardens or orchards (except ancient rose gardens)Bad odor from weeds and fertilizer
No raising chickensProtect sacrificial meat from contamination
No leaving a corpse overnightSanctity of the city
Core Principle Jerusalem was not apportioned to any single tribe — it belongs to all Israel. This shared status exempts it from laws applying to tribal possessions.
