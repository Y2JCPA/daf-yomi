5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Akiva, from whose land is the quality of damage payment assessed?
A. The injured party's land
B. The one liable for damages (the damager)
C. The court's assessment of average land
D. Whichever party has superior-quality land
Correct: B — R' Akiva holds that 'the best of his field' refers to the damager's field — the injured party collects from the damager's superior-quality land.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Shimon ben Menasya, what happens when a common person's ox gores a consecrated ox?
A. The owner is exempt because it is Temple property
B. The owner pays half-damages only
C. The owner pays full damages whether the ox was innocuous or forewarned
D. The Temple treasury pays for its own loss
Correct: C — R' Shimon ben Menasya holds that the owner pays full cost of damage to consecrated property regardless of whether the ox was tam (innocuous) or mu'ad (forewarned).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov reject applying the seasonal valuation adjustment to damage collection?
A. Because the Torah doesn't discuss seasons
B. Because it would weaken the rights of injured parties that the Torah intended to enhance
C. Because damaged land cannot be sold
D. Because Abaye had no source for his ruling
Correct: B — The Torah grants injured parties the right to collect from superior-quality land, intending to enhance their position. Applying the seasonal adjustment to lower-quality land would undermine this Torah-given advantage.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does the principle 'locking the door before borrowers' (na'alta delet bifnei lovim) mean?
A. Borrowers should not be allowed to take multiple loans
B. If creditors are disadvantaged in collection, people will stop lending
C. Courts should prevent excessive borrowing
D. Lenders must secure their loans with property liens
Correct: B — If the collection process penalizes creditors (e.g., forcing unfavorable seasonal valuations), people will stop lending money, which harms society. This is why the seasonal adjustment is rejected for creditor collection.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
To which type of collection is the seasonal land valuation distinction ultimately applied?
A. Damage payments (nizakin)
B. Loan repayment to creditors
C. Ketubah (marriage contract) collection
D. Temple treasury collection
Correct: C — After rejecting application to damages (weakens injured parties) and loans (discourages lending), the Gemara settles on ketubah collection as the appropriate context for the seasonal valuation trade-off.
