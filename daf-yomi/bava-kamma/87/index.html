R' Yehuda: Blind Person's Exemption

R' Yehuda
A blind person is exempt from all civil laws (dinei mamonot) in the Torah.
Gemara
Source: "The congregation shall judge between the smiter and the avenger of blood according to these laws" (Numbers 35:24).
Gemara
One who is not subject to the law of smiter/avenger — like a blind person — is not subject to civil laws.
R' Yehuda
A blind person also does not receive boshet (humiliation compensation) and is exempt from all mitzvot.
Rav Sheisha b. Rav Idi
Source: "This is the commandment, statutes, and laws" (Deut 6:1) — whoever is not subject to civil laws is not subject to mitzvot.
DERIVATION R' Yehuda links civil law liability to mitzvah obligation through a chain of biblical verses.

Rav Yosef's Reversal

גדול המצווה ועושה ממי שאינו מצווה ועושה
PERSONAL TESTIMONY Rav Yosef (who was blind) said:

At first: I wanted to celebrate if the halakha followed R' Yehuda — because I would be not commanded yet still performing mitzvot voluntarily!

But then I heard R' Ḥanina say: "One who is commanded and performs is greater than one who is not commanded and performs."

Now: I want to celebrate if the halakha does not follow R' Yehuda — because being commanded means greater reward!

Mishna: Disadvantageous Encounters

Deaf-Mute, Imbecile, Minor

  • If you injure them → you are liable
  • If they injure you → they are exempt
  • Reason: they lack legal awareness
  • No future liability

Slave & Married Woman

  • If you injure them → you are liable
  • If they injure you → they are exempt now
  • Reason: they have no money of their own
  • Pay later — when freed or divorced
DISTINCTION The exemption for slaves and married women is temporary — once they gain financial independence, they must pay. The exemption for minors and the incompetent is permanent.

Gemara: Who Gets Compensation for Injury to a Minor Daughter?

R' Elazar asks Rav:
One injures another's minor daughter — who receives payment?
Maybe Father?
Torah gave him profits of her youth (betrothal money) — injury lowers her value
Maybe Daughter?
Father can't injure her — so he has no ownership of her body
↓
Rav rules: Torah gave the father only profits of her youth — compensation goes to the daughter
↓
Abaye qualifies: Rav concedes that loss of livelihood (shevet) goes to the father, since her earnings belong to him
PRINCIPLE The father's rights over his daughter are limited to economic benefits (earnings, betrothal). Bodily injury compensation belongs to her.
