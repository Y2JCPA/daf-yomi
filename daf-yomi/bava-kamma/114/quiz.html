Bava Kamma 114 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is a single Jewish witness excommunicated for testifying in a gentile village court?
A. Because gentile courts expropriate money based on one witness, causing unjustified loss
B. Because any contact with gentile courts is forbidden
C. Because he violated the prohibition against gossip (lashon hara)
D. Because one witness is always considered a liar
Correct: A — The problem is specifically that gentile village courts will expropriate money based on a single witness — something Jewish law would not do. In an official courthouse (bei davar) that only imposes an oath based on one witness, there is no excommunication.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why is someone who sells land to a gentile bordering a Jew's property excommunicated?
A. Because of the right of first refusal (bar metzra)
B. Because he has 'placed a lion on the border' — exposing his neighbor to potential harm
C. Because selling land to gentiles is always forbidden
D. Because the land price decreases for the neighboring property
Correct: B — Bar metzra (right of first refusal) does not apply to gentile transactions. The reason is the danger: a gentile neighbor may cause harm to the adjacent Jewish landowner — described as 'placing a lion on my border.'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, why does the Mishna's rule of presumed ye'ush apply only to a gentile bandit?
A. Because gentile bandits are more violent
B. Because Jews cannot sue gentiles in any court
C. Because the victim thinks: 'I can take a Jewish bandit to court and recover my property'
D. Because gentiles are legally exempt from returning stolen property
Correct: C — Rav Ashi distinguishes: with a Jewish bandit, the owner holds out hope of recovery through beit din, so he doesn't despair. With a gentile bandit, there's no such recourse. (Rav Yosef actually argues the opposite!)
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the core conclusion about R' Yehuda HaNasi's position on thief vs. robber?
A. He follows the Rabbis — neither thief nor robber acquires through ye'ush
B. He follows R' Shimon — ye'ush applies to robbers, and he extends it to thieves as well
C. He creates a third category between thief and robber
D. He holds that only thieves acquire through ye'ush, not robbers
Correct: B — The Gemara proves that R' Yehuda HaNasi equates thief and robber according to R' Shimon's view that owners despair when robbed. R' Yehuda HaNasi extends this to theft, so both acquire stolen items through ye'ush.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Yochanan ben Beroka, who is believed to testify about where a swarm of bees originated?
A. Only two adult male witnesses
B. Only the owner of the field where the bees landed
C. Even a woman or a minor
D. Only a Torah scholar who is familiar with beekeeping
Correct: C — R' Yochanan ben Beroka rules that even a woman or minor — whose testimony is generally not accepted in court — is deemed credible to identify the origin of a swarm of bees.
