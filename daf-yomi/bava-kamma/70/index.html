Can One Write an Orakhta for Movable Property?

Sages of Neharde'a (v.1)
One cannot write an orakhta for movable property at all.
Ameimar
This follows R' Yochanan's principle: a stolen item before despair — the owner can't consecrate it because it's not in his possession. Similarly, he can't transfer ownership.
Sages of Neharde'a (v.2)
One cannot write an orakhta only when the bailee denied owing it — because it appears as falsehood.
Gemara
This implies: if the bailee did not deny, one can write an authorization document.
KEY CONCEPT Orakhta (אורכתא) — a legal document transferring the right to collect a debt or recover property on someone's behalf.

Owner wants someone to collect a debt or recover property on his behalf

No
❌ Invalid — debtor can say: "You have no claim against me"
Yes
✅ Valid — collector claims as owner
↓
Abaye: Even transferring half, a third, or a quarter is sufficient — once he must litigate over part, he litigates over all
PRACTICAL RULE The authorization must transfer at least partial ownership so the collector has legal standing.

Can the Collector Keep What He Collects?

Ameimar
If the collector seizes the item for himself, the court does not take it away — ownership was transferred to him in the document.
Rav Ashi
Since the owner wrote "I accept upon myself anything that develops" — he made the collector a mere agent (שליח), not an owner.
Third Opinion
The collector is a partner — he owns half, and can seize up to half.
Gemara
Halakha: The collector is an agent — he may not seize any portion for himself.
HALAKHA The collector is an agent (שליח), not a partner or owner — he must return everything collected.

Mishna: Cases of Fourfold/Fivefold Payment

ScenarioPays 4x/5x?Reason
Stole & slaughtered/sold — same witnesses testify to both✅ YesStandard case
Stole & slaughtered/sold — different witnesses for each✅ YesTestimonies combine
Stole & sold on Shabbat✅ YesSale itself not a capital offense
Stole & sold for idol worship✅ YesSale act not capital
Stole & slaughtered on Yom Kippur✅ YesNot liable to death for the slaughter
Stole father's animal, slaughtered, then father died✅ YesTheft preceded inheritance
Stole, slaughtered, then consecrated the animal✅ YesConsecration follows slaughter
PRINCIPLE Even when the slaughter/sale involves a sin, if it is not a capital offense directly through the sale act, the monetary penalty applies.

Combining Witness Testimonies: R' Akiva vs. Rabbis

R' Akiva

  • Torah requires testimony about a complete matter (דבר שלם)
  • If each pair only testifies to part, they don't combine
  • BUT concedes: if first testimony stands on its own (e.g., betrothal), it's a complete matter
  • Theft testimony + slaughter testimony = each is complete

Rabbis

  • "Matter" (דבר) excludes only truly partial testimony
  • Example: one witness sees one hair, another sees another hair
  • Each testimony is half a matter — cannot combine
  • But separate witness pairs for theft & slaughter can combine
ABAYE'S INSIGHT Just as betrothal witnesses + intercourse witnesses combine (because betrothal stands alone), theft witnesses + slaughter witnesses combine (because theft alone establishes liability for double payment).
