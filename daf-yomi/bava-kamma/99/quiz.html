QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel's first explanation, why doesn't the mishna about burned wool prove Rav Asi's principle?
A. The wool burned before the dye took hold, so there was no enhancement
B. The dyer was not considered a craftsman
C. The wool belonged to the dyer, not the homeowner
D. The mishna discusses a case where the dyer acted intentionally
Correct: A — Shmuel explains the wool burned at the moment of falling into the cauldron, before the dye took hold, so there was no enhancement to speak of — eliminating the proof for Rav Asi.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara distinguish between hired labor (po'el) and contractual labor (kablan) in the case of laundering a garment?
A. To determine which type of worker can acquire through enhancement
B. Because hired labor is paid per action (no outcome-based enhancement), while a contractor is paid for the finished result
C. Because hired laborers are always exempt from damages
D. To establish that only contractors can violate Shabbat for their work
Correct: B — A worker paid per tread (hired labor) has no claim to enhancement of the garment, so wage-delay rules apply straightforwardly. A contractor paid for the completed softened garment could potentially acquire through enhancement.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava's resolution of the goldsmith betrothal dispute, what do ALL opinions agree upon?
A. A craftsman acquires through enhancement
B. Betrothal with a loan is valid
C. A craftsman does NOT acquire through enhancement, and betrothal with a loan is invalid
D. The goldsmith's labor has no monetary value
Correct: C — Rava states that everyone agrees a craftsman does not acquire through enhancement AND that betrothal with a loan is invalid. The real dispute is about betrothal with a loan plus one peruta (the added jewel).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the core disagreement between R' Natan and R' Yehuda HaNasi regarding betrothal through labor?
A. Whether a craftsman can perform betrothal at all
B. Whether wage obligation accrues continuously or at completion
C. Whether betrothal with a loan plus one peruta is valid — does her mind focus on the loan or the peruta?
D. Whether gold jewelry counts as valid betrothal money
Correct: C — Both R' Natan and R' Yehuda HaNasi agree wages become a loan. They disagree about whether adding a jewel (peruta) alongside the loan effects betrothal — R' Natan says her mind is on the loan (invalid), R' Yehuda HaNasi says her mind is on the peruta (valid).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Shmuel describe the expert butcher as BOTH 'one who causes damage' AND 'negligent'?
A. To make the ruling apply in both paid and unpaid scenarios
B. Because the butcher violated two separate prohibitions
C. To distinguish between intentional and accidental damage
D. Because 'causes damage' applies to the animal and 'negligent' applies to the owner
Correct: A — 'Causes damage' alone would apply only when the butcher is paid. Adding 'negligent' teaches that even an unpaid expert butcher is liable — analogous to an unpaid bailee who is liable for negligence.
