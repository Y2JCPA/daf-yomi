Daf 32: Collisions, Permissions & Liability

Core Question: When two people act with permission, who is liable for resulting damage?
↓
Public Domain Collisions
Cross beam/barrel & cow cases — rules depend on who stopped, who was first
Intimate Injury
Husband injuring wife — Rava rules liable despite permission
Chopping Wood
Chips flying between domains — liable in all combinations
↓
Principle: Permission to act does not automatically exempt from liability — the active party bears responsibility
KEY THEME This daf explores the boundaries of liability when people act within their rights but cause harm to others.

Is a Husband Liable for Injury During Intercourse?

Rabba bar Natan
If a husband injures his wife during intercourse, what is the halakha? He acts with permission — but should he be more careful?
Rav Huna
He is exempt — just like the cross beam and barrel case. Both had permission to walk; here too, both have permission.
Rava
He is liable! An a fortiori from the forest case: even in a public forest, the killer is exiled. Here the husband enters her domain — all the more so he's liable!
Gemara
But the mishna exempts the beam owner when both walk with permission?
Gemara Resolution
Different! In the mishna, both are equally active. In intercourse, only the husband is the active participant — so he is liable.
DISTINCTION When both parties are equally active (walking), permission exempts. When only one party performs the action, that party is liable even with permission.

Reish Lakish: Prone Cow vs. Walking Cow

Two cows in the public domain — one prone (lying down), one walking
↓
Walking cow kicks prone cow
Walking cow's owner is exempt — the prone cow shouldn't be lying in the road
Prone cow kicks walking cow
Prone cow's owner is liable — the cow took active harmful action
↓
Attempted proof from mishna: Cross beam stopped = prone cow?
↓
Rejected: The mishna deals with a beam blocking the entire road like a carcass — the barrel had no escape route. Reish Lakish's cow left room to pass.
KEY DIFFERENCE A stationary obstacle blocking the entire road is liable. One lying to the side, where the other could avoid it, is exempt if the collision was passive.

Chopping Wood: Three Domain Combinations

Private → Public

  • Chip flies from his property to public domain
  • Acting with permission (his property)
  • Common for people to be in public domain
  • Liable — multitudes are expected there

Public → Private

  • Chip flies from public domain to another's property
  • Acting without permission (public domain)
  • Not common for people in private property
  • Liable — he shouldn't chop in public domain

Private → Private

  • Chip flies from his property to another's property
  • Acting with permission, victims are few
  • Neither reason from other cases applies
  • Still liable — all three cases are needed!
WHY ALL THREE? Each case has a unique combination of permission and likelihood of victims. Without all three, we might have exempted one scenario. The mishna teaches: liability applies in every combination.

Carpenter's Workshop: Permission and Exile

Tosefta
If one enters a carpenter's workshop without permission and a wood chip kills him — carpenter is exempt. With permission — carpenter is liable.
R' Yosei bar Ḥanina
'Liable' means: pays four types of indemnity (damage, pain, medical costs, lost livelihood). But if the visitor dies, the carpenter is exempt from exile.
Rava
On the contrary! A fortiori from the forest: if a stranger in a public forest triggers exile, a welcomed guest should certainly trigger exile!
Rava (revised)
Actually, the carpenter is exempt from exile because exile is insufficient — this is shogeg karov le-meizid (negligence approaching intentional). He should have checked before swinging!
PRINCIPLE Exile atones for truly unintentional killing. When negligence approaches intentional harm, exile is too lenient — the carpenter should have been more careful with a guest present.
