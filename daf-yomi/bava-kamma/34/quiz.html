Bava Kamma 34 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the injured party's claim on the belligerent ox take priority over a creditor who seized it, even if the debt preceded the goring?
A. Because the injured party can say: 'If the ox were in your possession, I would collect from you anyway'
B. Because damage claims always precede monetary debts by Torah law
C. Because the creditor's lien is automatically voided when the ox causes damage
D. Because a later creditor's collection is never valid
Correct: A — The injured party's claim is specifically tied to the ox that caused damage. He argues: even if you held this ox, I would collect from you, since my claim attaches to this particular animal.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Akiva, why does the injured party not share in the belligerent ox's appreciation when the owner fattened it?
A. Because the injured party is merely a creditor, not a partner
B. Because the owner's labor created the appreciation, not the partnership
C. Because appreciation is always assessed at time of injury
D. Because fattening an ox is considered a prohibited benefit
Correct: B — Even though R' Akiva considers the injured party a partner in the ox, the appreciation that resulted from the owner's effort (fattening) belongs to the owner alone, since the partner contributed nothing to that increase.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When the injured ox depreciates in value, Rav Ashi specifies that the liable party must pay at the time of trial. What type of depreciation does this refer to?
A. Depreciation due to the owner working the ox too hard
B. Depreciation due to market fluctuations
C. Depreciation due to the continuing effect of the wound
D. Depreciation due to the ox aging naturally
Correct: C — Rav Ashi explains the depreciation must be from the wound's ongoing effect — 'the horn of your ox is still buried in it.' If the owner caused the depreciation through labor, the liable party would not owe extra.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the practical difference between R' Meir and R' Yehuda according to R' Yochanan's explanation?
A. Whether the carcass depreciation is assessed at time of injury or trial
B. Whether the injured party collects from the body of the ox or from the owner's assets
C. Who receives the appreciation in value of the carcass after the ox's death
D. Whether an innocuous ox pays half or full damage
Correct: C — R' Yochanan explains that R' Meir holds the entire carcass appreciation goes to the injured party, while R' Yehuda holds it is split — half to the injured party and half to the liable party.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does R' Yehuda cite the verse 'He shall pay' (יְשַׁלֵּם) in addition to his logical argument that the liable party cannot profit?
A. To establish that payment must be in cash, not in kind
B. To cover the case where the injured party suffers no net financial loss, yet the liable party still cannot profit
C. To prove that an innocuous ox pays only half damage
D. To show that the verse overrides the logical argument
Correct: B — The logical argument only proves the liable party cannot profit when the injured party would sustain a loss. The verse 'He shall pay' extends the rule: even when the injured party has no net loss (e.g., the carcass appreciated), the liable party still cannot gain.
