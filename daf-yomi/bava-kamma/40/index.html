Safeguarding, Ransom & Change of Custody

R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov
Both innocuous and forewarned oxen — reduced safeguarding exempts the owner. Liability only if no safeguarding at all.
R' Yehuda
A forewarned ox retains its element of innocuousness, and reduced safeguarding suffices for the forewarned component.
Ravina
The real dispute: does change of custody reset the ox's status? R' Yehuda says no; R' Ya'akov says yes.
Rav Ḥisda
Stewards pay damages from superior property but never pay ransom — orphans have no obligation of atonement (kaparah).
Key Theme Daf 40 explores who bears liability when the ox's owner is incapacitated or absent — stewards, borrowers, and partners — and whether ransom serves as compensation or spiritual atonement.

Safeguarding Standards: Three Views

R' Yehuda

  • Forewarned ox retains innocuous element
  • Reduced safeguarding (shemirah pechutah) suffices for forewarned ox
  • Superior safeguarding needed only for full forewarned liability

R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov

  • Both innocuous AND forewarned: reduced guarding = exempt
  • Owner liable ONLY if no safeguarding at all
  • No distinction between ox categories for guarding

R' Ya'akov

  • Court appoints stewards for innocuous ox owners
  • Stewards enable injured party to collect from ox's body
  • Change of custody resets forewarned status
Practical Impact These views determine when a negligent owner crosses the line from exempt to liable for damages.

What Is the Purpose of Ransom (Kofer)?

Rabbanan
Ransom = monetary restitution. Amount is the value of the victim (nizak). Derived from verbal analogy: 'imposing' (yushat) links to compensation for miscarriage.
R' Yishmael b"r Yoḥanan
Ransom = atonement. Amount is the value of the liable party (mazik). 'Redemption of his life' — the owner redeems himself.
Rav Pappa
Perhaps all agree ransom is atonement! They only disagree on how to calculate the amount — by the victim's value or the owner's value.
Nafka Mina If ransom is atonement, stewards of orphans are exempt. If it's restitution, they might be liable even for minors.
