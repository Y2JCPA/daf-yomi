QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye, why does R' Eliezer require slaughtering a forewarned ox?
A. Because the Torah says 'the owner has not secured it' means no safeguarding works
B. Because of the principle 'you shall not bring blood into your house' — one may not keep a dangerous animal
C. Because forewarned oxen cannot physically be restrained
D. Because the owner has already been warned three times
Correct: B — Abaye rejected Rabba's reading of the verse and instead cited R' Natan's baraita based on Deut. 22:8 — the prohibition of maintaining a hazardous situation in one's home.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the case of an ox goring a cow whose fetus is found dead, why does the owner pay only ¼ damages for the fetus?
A. Because the fetus is worth half the cow
B. Because there is uncertainty whether the goring caused the fetus's death, so half of the half-damages is paid
C. Because the fetus was not yet born and has no legal standing
D. Because the Rabbis limit liability for fetuses to one-quarter
Correct: B — Since it's uncertain whether the cow gave birth before or after the goring, and an innocuous ox pays half-damages, the owner pays half of the half-damages (= ¼) for the fetus. This follows Sumakhos' principle of dividing uncertain property.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Shmuel, why can't the buyer void the sale of a goring ox as a mistaken transaction?
A. Because the buyer should have inspected the ox beforehand
B. Because we don't follow the majority in monetary matters — the burden of proof is on the buyer
C. Because all oxen are potentially dangerous
D. Because the sale price was fair
Correct: B — Shmuel holds that we follow the majority (rov) only for matters of prohibited/permitted status (issur v'heter), not for monetary disputes. Since the burden of proof is on the claimant (buyer), the seller can claim 'I sold it for slaughter.'
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When does the court hear the defendant's arguments before the claimant's?
A. When the defendant is a Torah scholar
B. When the defendant's assets are depreciating because of the pending claim
C. When the claimant has no witnesses
D. When the case involves a forewarned animal
Correct: B — The Sages of Neharde'a taught that when the defendant's assets are losing value due to the unresolved claim, the court lets him present his defense first so he can sell his assets at their true price.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In Abaye's calculation of the cow-gores-ox case, why does the total payment come to only ⅜ of the damage?
A. Because the cow is innocuous and pays only half, minus a penalty
B. Because ¼ comes from the cow (half of half-damages) and ⅛ from the offspring (half of ¼, due to uncertainty about when it was born)
C. Because three-eighths is the standard payment for an innocuous animal
D. Because the offspring was alive and therefore has reduced liability
Correct: B — Abaye explains: the cow pays ¼ (half of the half-damages, since the offspring may share liability), and the offspring pays ⅛ (half of ¼, since it's uncertain whether it was part of the cow at the time of goring). This only applies when cow and offspring have different owners.
