How Must Damages Be Paid?

Rav Huna
Damages are paid with money or with one's best-quality land (idit).
Rav Naḥman
But a baraita on Exodus 21:34 includes even bran — a low-quality commodity — as valid payment!
Rav Huna
That case is when he has no money or land at all. He pays with what he has.
Gemara
Isn't that obvious? If he has nothing else, of course he pays with what he has!
Rav Huna
You might think we'd require him to sell his commodities and bring money. The baraita teaches: no need to sell.
Principle A damager pays with money or best-quality land; only when he lacks both may he pay with inferior commodities directly.

AuthorityRuling When Creditor Seizes One Brother's Land
RavThe original division is voided; remaining land is divided equally anew
ShmuelEach brother relinquished (viter) rights to reimbursement upon taking his share

↓
🤷 The dilemma stands unresolved (teiku)
↓
🌍 Eretz Yisrael tradition: Up to 1/3 extra comes from your own pocket
↓
✨ Beyond 1/3 — God reimburses the additional expense
Limit One is NOT required to spend one-third of his entire estate per mitzvah — that would bankrupt someone facing three simultaneous obligations.

The Mishna: Conditions for Damage Liability

Liable When

  • Responsible to safeguard and damage occurs
  • Facilitated even part of the damage
  • Damaged property belongs to Jews (bnei brit)
  • Property is non-consecrated (no me'ilah)
  • Property is owned/assigned
  • Damage in shared domain or public space

Exempt When

  • Not responsible for safeguarding
  • Damage to consecrated property
  • Damage to ownerless property
  • Damage in domain exclusive to the liable party
Key Rule The one liable for damage caused by insufficient safeguarding must pay restitution with his best-quality land (idit).
