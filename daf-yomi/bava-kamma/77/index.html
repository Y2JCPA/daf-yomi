Fourfold & Fivefold Payment: When Slaughter ≠ Sale

R' Yochanan
The mishna about sacrificial animals applies to all sacrifices, even unblemished — the slaughter itself is unfit, so no liability
Reish Lakish
The Torah compares slaughter to sale — if sale doesn't apply (as with kodashim), slaughter doesn't either
R' Yochanan
For a stolen tereifa that is sold: the thief is liable for fourfold/fivefold — sale and slaughter are independent
Reish Lakish
The thief is exempt — since slaughter of a tereifa doesn't incur liability per R' Shimon, neither does sale
Core Issue Does the Torah's linkage of slaughter and sale mean liability must apply to both or neither, or can they operate independently?

Red Heifer: Fit for Consumption?

הואיל והיתה לה שעת הכושר
Amud A — Opening Principle The meat of the parah adumah (red heifer) is susceptible to tumat ochlin (food impurity) despite being assur b'hana'ah (prohibited for benefit) — because it had a time when it was fit for consumption.
4 / 9

When Was the Red Heifer 'Fit'?

R' Yochanan
It was fit before designation as a red heifer — at that earlier time it could have been eaten
Reish Lakish
Per R' Shimon: The red heifer can be redeemed even after slaughter on its pyre — so it always retains potential fitness
R' Shimon's Principle כל העומד ליפדות כפדוי דמי — Anything ready to be redeemed is considered as though already redeemed

Reish Lakish: Slaughter = Sale Linkage

Exodus 21:37 — "If a man steals an ox or sheep and slaughters it or sells it"
↓
Torah compares slaughter to sale
↓
Sale valid → Slaughter liable
Fourfold/fivefold applies to both
Sale invalid → Slaughter exempt
Fourfold/fivefold applies to neither
Application Sacrificial animals (kodashim) cannot be validly sold — so Reish Lakish says slaughter also incurs no fourfold/fivefold payment

Tereifa: Selling a Fatally Wounded Stolen Animal

R' Yochanan

  • Thief is liable for selling a stolen tereifa
  • Sale and slaughter operate independently
  • Even if slaughter doesn't create liability (R' Shimon), sale still can

Reish Lakish

  • Thief is exempt from selling a stolen tereifa
  • Sale and slaughter are linked by the verse
  • If slaughter doesn't create liability, sale cannot either
Per R' Shimon Slaughtering a tereifa is not a valid shechitah (its meat cannot be eaten) — so no fourfold/fivefold for slaughter. The dispute is whether sale follows.
