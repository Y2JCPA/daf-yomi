↓

Intended the woman

Core Issue Who receives compensation for miscarried offspring, how is it calculated, and what happens when the husband dies?
3 / 9

Does the Ox's Intent Matter?

Mishna (inference)
If the ox intended to gore the woman, the owner pays dmei vladot — the exemption applies only when the ox intended another ox.
Rav Adda bar Ahava
Even if the ox intended to gore the woman, the owner is exempt from dmei vladot.
Gemara (defending Rav Adda)
The Mishna says 'intending another ox' only to parallel the later clause about a person intending to injure another person — not to exclude intent toward the woman.
Verse (Exodus 21:22)
"If men struggle and hurt a pregnant woman" — the Torah's case involves a person who intended to hit another person, not the woman directly.
Key Point Rav Adda bar Ahava survives the challenge by arguing the Mishna's phrasing is stylistic, not exclusionary.

Rav Pappa: Ox Goring a Canaanite Maidservant

שפחה כנענית שנגחה — חמור מעוברת נגח
Novel Ruling If an ox gores a Canaanite maidservant (שפחה כנענית) causing miscarriage, the owner pays dmei vladot.

Why? The ox merely injured "a pregnant donkey" — the offspring are the master's property, not independent persons. This falls under property damage, not the Torah's exemption for dmei vladot.

Source: "You remain here with (עם) the donkey" (Genesis 22:5) — expounded to mean a people (עם) similar to a donkey.

How to Calculate Dmei Vladot: Rabba vs. Rava on R' Shimon ben Gamliel

Rabba's Reading

  • R' Shimon ben Gamliel challenges: a woman's value is HIGHER after birth (less risk of death)
  • So subtracting post-birth value from pre-birth value yields a negative number!
  • Solution: appraise the value of the fetuses directly
  • Give that amount to the husband

Rava's Reading

  • R' Shimon ben Gamliel challenges: does only the HUSBAND benefit from the pregnancy increase?
  • The woman herself also gains value from being pregnant
  • Solution: appraise the offspring value → give to husband
  • PLUS: husband and wife DIVIDE the increase in her value due to pregnancy
Practical Difference Under Rava's reading, the woman receives a share of the compensation — the increase in her own value due to pregnancy.

Resolution The two baraitot (one saying liable, one exempt after convert's death) refer to different payments: the 'liable' one refers to the woman's share of her increased value; the 'exempt' one refers to dmei vladot.

Another Jew seizes it

Convert's collateral held by a Jew
