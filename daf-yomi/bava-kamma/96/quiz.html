Bava Kamma 96 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, if a robber enhanced a stolen item and then sold it, what must the robbery victim do to recover the item?
A. Pay the purchaser for the enhanced value
B. Simply take the item back with no payment
C. Pay the original value to the purchaser
D. Sue the robber, not the purchaser
Correct: A — Rava rules that the robber 'sold his enhancement' — the victim must compensate the purchaser for the enhanced value to recover the stolen item.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rav Pappa rule that turning a broom into a rope is NOT a valid shinui?
A. A rope is worth less than a broom
B. The rope can be unraveled back into a broom
C. Both are made of the same material
D. The Torah considers them the same category
Correct: B — The key factor is reversibility — since the rope can be unraveled and become a broom again, the change is not considered permanent enough to constitute a valid shinui.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, if someone steals a silver bar and mints it into coins, has the robber acquired the item?
A. Yes, because coins have a new name
B. Yes, because coins are more valuable
C. No, because the coins can be melted back into a bar
D. No, because minting requires royal authority
Correct: C — The coins can be melted back into a silver bar, making the change reversible. However, the reverse (coins→bar) IS a valid shinui because re-minting would produce NEW coins.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Rav Nachman order the robber to pay half the enhanced value of the land plowed with stolen oxen?
A. It was the strict halachic requirement
B. He was penalizing a habitual robber beyond the law
C. The Sages always split enhancement value equally
D. The robbery victim and robber agreed to this settlement
Correct: B — Rav Nachman acknowledged to Rava that strict law would not require this payment, but he exercised judicial discretion to penalize an experienced, habitual robber.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ela, if one steals a lamb that grows into a ram while in the thief's possession, what happens if the thief then slaughters it?
A. He pays four-fold penalty plus the ram's value
B. He must return the meat to the original owner
C. He slaughters his own animal and owes no four/five-fold penalty
D. He pays the original lamb's value plus a fine
Correct: C — The biological maturation (lamb→ram) constitutes a shinui — the robber acquired the animal. Therefore he slaughters his own animal and is not liable for the four or five-fold penalty.
