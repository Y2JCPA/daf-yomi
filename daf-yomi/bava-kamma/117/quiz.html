QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Huna bar Ḥiyya, what is the practical difference between a ruling being halakha versus a fine (knas)?
A. A fine requires witnesses but halakha does not
B. If halakha, we can extrapolate to other cases; if a fine, we cannot
C. A fine can only be imposed by a court of 23, while halakha needs only 3
D. Halakha applies only in Eretz Yisrael while fines apply everywhere
Correct: B — Rav Huna bar Ḥiyya taught that if a ruling is halakha, we learn from it and apply it to other cases. If it is a fine, we do not extrapolate, as the fine may have been imposed for case-specific reasons.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does R' Yirmeya hold that one who pours another's wine as a libation is liable to pay, despite also incurring the death penalty?
A. The death penalty is not actually enforced in practice
B. Monetary liability arises at the moment of lifting (theft), before the death penalty arises at the moment of pouring
C. Payment for libation is a fine, not standard damages
D. The wine owner forgave the death penalty, leaving only monetary liability
Correct: B — R' Yirmeya distinguishes the timing: monetary liability for theft arises when the thief lifts the wine, while the death penalty for idol worship arises only when he pours it. Since they don't arise simultaneously, kim lei bidrabba minei does not apply.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did the serpent at Rav Kahana's cave only open when R' Yochanan called himself the 'disciple'?
A. The serpent only responded to words of humility
B. R' Yochanan needed to acknowledge Rav Kahana as his teacher (superior in learning)
C. The serpent was testing whether R' Yochanan knew the correct password
D. Rav Kahana had instructed the serpent to only admit students
Correct: B — The serpent only opened when R' Yochanan referred to himself as the disciple and Rav Kahana as the teacher, reflecting the Gemara's conclusion that Babylonian scholars were more advanced than those of Eretz Yisrael.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, when is a bailee who hands a deposited silver cup to thieves liable to pay?
A. Always, because he violated his duty as bailee
B. Never, because he acted under duress
C. If the bailee is wealthy (thieves came for his money, so he saved himself with another's property)
D. Only if the bailee failed to resist the thieves physically
Correct: C — Rav Ashi distinguishes based on the bailee's wealth: if wealthy, the thieves likely came for his money, and handing over the cup means saving himself with another's property (liable). If poor, the thieves came specifically for the cup (exempt).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
On what basis did Rabba exempt the man who pushed another's donkey off the ferry into the river?
A. The donkey owner consented by boarding first
B. The man who pushed had no other option available
C. The donkey owner was classified as a rodef (pursuer) because his donkey endangered the passengers
D. Maritime law permits jettisoning cargo to save a vessel
Correct: C — Rabba ruled that the donkey owner was a rodef (pursuer) from the outset because his donkey endangered the other passengers. It is permitted to stop a pursuer by any means, including destroying his property, and one who does so is exempt from payment.
