Joshua's Ten Conditions for the Land

🐑 Graze small animals in large-tree forests
↓
🪵 Gather thorny twigs from others' fields
↓
🌿 Collect wild vegetation (except from fenugreek fields)
↓
🌱 Pluck shoots for planting (except olive shoots)
↓
💧 Draw water from any spring, even on private land
↓
🐟 Fish in the Sea of Tiberias (no underwater fences)
↓
🧱 Relieve oneself behind any fence & take a cleaning stone

KEY CONCEPT These ten conditions represent communal easements established by Joshua bin Nun when Israel entered the land — balancing private property rights with public necessity.

Why Exclude Fenugreek Fields from Gathering?

Gemara
Is wild vegetation good for fenugreek? But the Mishna in Kilayim says vegetation is harmful to it!
R' Yirmeya
Answer #1: For seeds — vegetation is harmful (weakens yield). For stalks — vegetation is beneficial (fenugreek climbs on it and grows larger).
R' Yirmeya
Answer #2: For human consumption — vegetation is bad. For animal consumption — the owner wants the vegetation too as additional fodder.
Rav Pappa
How do we know the purpose? Planted in rows (mesharei) = human consumption. Planted haphazardly = animal consumption.
RESOLUTION The exclusion of fenugreek applies only when planted for stalks or for animal fodder — where the wild vegetation serves the owner's purpose.

Rules for Plucking Shoots (Yichur)

Olive Trees 🫒

  • ❌ Shoots may NOT be plucked
  • Exception: Must leave egg-sized piece on trunk
  • R' Tanchum & R' Berayes in name of an elder
  • Olive trees are especially vulnerable to damage

Reeds & Grapevines 🍇

  • ✅ May take shoots
  • Only from the first knot and above
  • Not from lower parts of the plant

All Other Trees 🌳

  • ✅ May take shoots
  • Only from the thick part with many branches
  • NOT from the sun-facing side where fruit grows
  • Based on: 'precious fruits of the sun' (Deut. 33:14)
PRINCIPLE Shoots may be taken for propagation, but only in ways that do not harm the tree's productivity.

Sea of Tiberias — Naphtali's Portion

ים של טבריה בחלקו של נפתלי נפל
TRIBAL ALLOCATION The Sea of Tiberias (Sea of Galilee) was in the portion of Naphtali. The tribe also received a strip of land to the south of the sea — the full length of a fish-trap rope — so they could spread their nets. This fulfills: 'O Naphtali, satisfied with favor… possess the sea and the south' (Deut. 33:23).

R' Shimon ben Elazar: Detached items found in the mountains at the time of conquest belonged to all tribes equally (as spoils), but attached items (trees) belonged to the tribe whose land they were on.

Every single tribe received a portion of mountains, lowland, countryside, and valley — as stated in Deuteronomy 1:7.
