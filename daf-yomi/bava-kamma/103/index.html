↓

↓

Cascading One-Fifth Payments (חומש על חומש)

🔴 Robber steals an item and takes a false oath denying it
↓
💰 Owes principal + one-fifth payment
↓
🤥 Takes another false oath on the one-fifth itself
↓
💰 That one-fifth becomes a new principal — owes another fifth on top
↓
🔁 Cycle repeats until the amount is less than a peruta
↓
🐑 Brings a guilt-offering (אשם) to achieve atonement
Source "He shall restore it in full, and shall add the fifth part more to it" (Leviticus 5:24) — applies to robbery, deposits, pledges, and lost items alike.

Robbed One of Five — Who Must Be Paid?

R' Tarfon
He places the stolen item between the five claimants and withdraws. He has fulfilled his obligation.
R' Akiva
This is not the way to spare him from transgression! He must pay each and every one of the five.
Gemara
Our Mishna requires oath-taking for the travel obligation. It fits neither opinion perfectly!
Gemara
Actually it follows R' Akiva — his strict ruling applies only when a false oath was taken, as the verse specifies "on the day of his being guilty."
Resolution R' Akiva's requirement to pay all five claimants is triggered specifically by the false oath, aligning with the Mishna's condition.

The Great Ordinance (תקנה גדולה)

Robber took a false oath and must return stolen item
↓
Is the expense of returning greater than the principal?
No
Must return item directly to victim, travel as far as needed
Yes
May pay principal + one-fifth to the court (בית דין), then bring guilt-offering
↓
Does R' Akiva accept this ordinance?
Known Victim
Yes — court conveys to the known owner
Unknown Victim
No — money won't reach the real owner, so no shortcut
R' Elazar b. R' Tzadok The Sages instituted this ordinance to encourage robbers to repent by easing the practical burden of repayment.

R' Tarfon vs. R' Akiva: When Does the Ordinance Apply?

R' Tarfon

  • Accepts the Sages' ordinance broadly
  • Even with an oath, robber can place item between 5 claimants
  • The ordinance overrides the verse's strict requirement
  • Practical leniency to encourage repentance

R' Akiva

  • Ordinance applies only when victim is known
  • Unknown victim = money won't reach rightful owner
  • Must pay each of the five when oath was taken
  • Strict standard: no remedy without certainty of return
Core Dispute Can the Sages' ordinance function when there is doubt about who the true victim is? R' Tarfon says yes; R' Akiva says no.
