Are Slaves Classified as Land or Movable Property?

Ulla / R' Elazar
Slaves have the legal status of land — a creditor can collect slaves from orphans just like land
Rav Naḥman
Slaves have the legal status of movable property — they cannot be collected from orphans
Rava
Ulla, R' Elazar, the judges of Neharde'a, and Rav Ḥana bar Bizna all say slaves = land. Whose opinion do you follow?
Rav Naḥman
I have a baraita from Avimi: prosbol doesn't apply to one who owns only slaves, and movable property can't be acquired together with slaves — proving slaves ≠ land
Core Issue This classification affects debt collection from orphans, prosbol applicability, and methods of property acquisition

Ulla's Hidden Opinion and Rav Naḥman's Reaction

Ulla
(After Rav Naḥman left the study hall)
R' Elazar actually said: A slave can be collected from orphans, because slaves = land
Rav Naḥman
Ulla evaded me! Had he told me R' Elazar's full opinion to my face, I would have refuted it
Judges of Neharde'a
We collected slaves from orphans to pay a creditor
Rav Ḥana bar Bizna
I too collected slaves from orphans in Pumbedita
Rav Naḥman
Return those slaves! If you don't, I will collect from your own mansions — this is an elementary error!
Key Point Rav Naḥman considered seizing slaves from orphans a reversible judicial error, requiring the judges themselves to compensate

Avimi's Baraita: Two Proofs That Slaves ≠ Land

HalakhaLandSlaves
Prosbol takes effect?✅ Yes — prosbol works for a debtor who owns land❌ No — prosbol does not work for a debtor who owns only slaves
Movable property acquired together?✅ Yes — movables are acquired with land via kinyan agav❌ No — movables cannot be acquired together with slaves
Rav Naḥman's Source Both halakhot from Avimi's baraita demonstrate that slaves lack the legal properties of land

Two Resolutions of Rav Ika bar Rav Ami

Version 1: Slaves = Land

  • Everyone agrees slaves are legally like land
  • Baraita #2 follows this logically
  • Baraita #1 requires land like 'fortified cities of Judea' — immovable
  • Slaves move around, so they don't qualify for kinyan agav

Version 2: Slaves = Movable Property

  • Everyone agrees slaves are legally like movable property
  • Baraita #1 follows this logically
  • Baraita #2 applies only where movables were piled upon the slave at the moment of acquisition
  • A special case, not a general classification
Source Verse II Chronicles 21:3 — 'Their father gave them silver, gold, and precious things, with fortified cities in Judea' — the basis for acquiring movables together with land

Mishnah's Rule: Damage to Consecrated Property

📜 Mishnah: One is liable for damage only to property that would not trigger me'ilah (misuse of sacred property)
↓
🤔 Gemara infers: The property IS consecrated, but has no me'ilah — who holds this?
↓
🧑‍⚖️ R' Yoḥanan: This refers to offerings of lesser sanctity (kodashim kalim)
↓
📖 Follows R' Yosei HaGelili: such offerings are the owner's property while the animal is alive
↓
🔪 After slaughter, even R' Yosei HaGelili agrees — the offering becomes property of Heaven
Verse Leviticus 5:21 — 'Commits a trespass against the Lord and deals falsely with his neighbor' — includes denying possession of offerings of lesser sanctity
