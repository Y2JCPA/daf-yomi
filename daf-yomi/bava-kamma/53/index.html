What Causes Pit Damage — Fumes or Impact?

Rav
Pit liability is only for lethal fumes (הבלא). Fell backward = exempt, because death was from impact, not fumes.
Shmuel
The Torah holds one liable for fumes AND impact (חבטא). Forward or backward — liable either way.
Baraita
Whether it fell forward or backward, he is liable — a conclusive refutation (תיובתא) of Rav!
Rav Ḥisda
Rav concedes when the pit is on private property: 'Either way you're liable — your fumes or your land's impact.'
Rabba
The baraita describes where the animal tumbled — started falling forward, then rolled onto its back. Fumes initiated the death.
Rav Yosef
The baraita discusses damage to the pit by the ox — it contaminated the water. Direction doesn't matter there.
Core Dispute Rav limits pit liability to its unique hazard (הבלא — lethal fumes). Shmuel says impact (חבטא) is included a fortiori. Three Amoraim rescue Rav from refutation.

Rav vs. Shmuel: What Makes a Pit Dangerous?

Rav — Fumes Only (הבלא)

  • Pit liability = lethal fumes (suffocation)
  • Impact damage (חבטא) is NOT pit liability
  • Fell backward on back → exempt
  • Death from impact, not from pit's unique hazard
  • Based on: 'fall therein' = normal manner of falling

Shmuel — Fumes AND Impact

  • Liable for fumes (הבלא) — certainly
  • AND all the more so for impact (חבטא)
  • Forward or backward → liable either way
  • Backward + exempt = fell OUTSIDE the pit
  • Kal va-ḥomer: if fumes obligate, surely impact does
Key Term הבלא (hevla) = lethal fumes/bad air inside a pit. חבטא (ḥavta) = impact from hitting the ground. Rav says only the unique pit hazard creates liability.

Rabbi Natan: Shared Liability When Ox Pushes Ox into Pit

First Tanna
Ox pushed another ox into a pit → ox owner is liable, pit owner is exempt.
R' Natan
Both share liability! Ox owner pays half, pit owner pays half — each bears full responsibility.
Gemara
But another baraita says R' Natan rules: pit owner pays 3/4, ox owner pays 1/4?
Gemara
No contradiction! 1/4 + 3/4 = innocuous ox (תם). 1/2 + 1/2 = forewarned ox (מועד).
R' Natan
Each party is responsible for all the damage. When one party can't pay full share, collect from the other.
R' Natan's Principle When two parties jointly cause damage, each bears full responsibility. If one can't pay (e.g., innocuous ox only pays half), the shortfall shifts to the other party.

Rava's Comprehensive Liability Matrix

🕳️ Scenario: A person and an ox together push a victim into a pit
↓
💰 Damage (נזק): Person, ox owner, AND pit owner — all liable
↓
🩹 Four Payments (pain, medical, livelihood, humiliation): Only the person is liable
↓
⚰️ Ransom & 30 Shekels (if victim killed): Only the ox owner is liable
↓
🏺 Vessels in pit: Person & ox owner liable; pit owner exempt (Torah excludes vessels from pit)
↓
🐂 Consecrated ox in pit: Pit owner exempt — 'the carcass shall be for him' doesn't apply when carcass has no owner
Verse "And the carcass shall be for him" (Exodus 21:34) — Pit liability only applies when the victim's owner can take back the carcass. A disqualified consecrated animal has no true owner, so pit owner is exempt.
