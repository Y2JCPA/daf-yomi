Bava Kamma 53 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav, why is the pit owner exempt when an ox falls backward into the pit?
A. Because the ox was not startled by the pit
B. Because backward falls are caused by impact, not the pit's lethal fumes
C. Because the pit was too shallow to cause harm
D. Because the ox's owner was negligent
Correct: B — Rav holds that pit liability covers only death by lethal fumes (הבלא), not death by impact (חבטא). An ox falling backward dies from impact with the ground, not from the pit's fumes.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rav Ḥisda reconcile Rav's opinion with the baraita that says both forward and backward falls create liability?
A. The baraita refers to a pit on private property, where the owner is liable for either fumes or impact of his own land
B. The baraita is discussing a very deep pit where fumes always kill
C. The baraita refers only to damage caused to the pit by the ox
D. The baraita is discussing a case where a person, not an animal, fell in
Correct: A — Rav Ḥisda explains that Rav concedes liability for a pit on private property: the animal's owner can argue 'your fumes or your land's impact — either way you're liable.' The baraita discusses this private-land case.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Natan, when an innocuous ox pushes another ox into a pit, why does the pit owner pay 3/4 and the ox owner only 1/4?
A. The pit is more dangerous than the ox
B. Each party is fully responsible, but the innocuous ox owner can only pay half damages, so the shortfall shifts to the pit owner
C. The pit owner dug the pit intentionally while the ox acted instinctively
D. The Torah assigns greater liability to passive hazards than active ones
Correct: B — R' Natan holds each party fully responsible for ALL the damage. The innocuous ox owner's liability is capped at half (the Torah's rule for tam), so the pit owner absorbs the other quarter — paying 3/4 total.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In Rava's matrix, who is liable for ransom (kofer) when a person and an ox together push someone into a pit and the victim dies?
A. All three parties — the person, ox owner, and pit owner
B. Only the person who pushed
C. Only the ox owner
D. The ox owner and the pit owner, but not the person
Correct: C — Ransom (kofer) and the 30 shekels for a Canaanite slave are obligations unique to ox damage. The person who pushed and the pit owner are exempt from these specific payments.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why is the pit owner exempt from liability when a disqualified consecrated ox falls into his pit?
A. Consecrated animals cannot be owned, so they cannot be damaged
B. The verse 'the carcass shall be for him' limits liability to cases where the claimant can take the carcass, which doesn't apply to consecrated animals
C. The priest who has the animal is considered the owner of the pit as well
D. Consecrated animals are immune to lethal fumes
Correct: B — Rava resolves that 'the carcass shall be for him' (Exodus 21:34) means pit liability only applies when the carcass can belong to the claimant. A disqualified consecrated animal's carcass cannot be sold or used, so this condition is unmet.
