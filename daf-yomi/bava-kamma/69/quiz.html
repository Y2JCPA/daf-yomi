Bava Kamma 69 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, what happens when a court issues a definitive ruling ordering a thief to pay, and he then slaughters the stolen animal?
A. He pays fourfold or fivefold because he is still a thief
B. He does not pay fourfold or fivefold because he is now classified as a robber
C. He pays double payment only
D. He is exempt from all payments
Correct: B — A definitive court ruling transforms the thief (ganav) into a robber (gazlan). The fourfold/fivefold payment applies only to a ganav, not a gazlan.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does R' Yochanan rule that an owner cannot consecrate a stolen item (before ye'ush)?
A. Because the owner has renounced ownership
B. Because the item no longer exists in its original form
C. Because the item is not in the owner's possession
D. Because only a Kohen can consecrate stolen property
Correct: C — R' Yochanan derives from 'When a man shall sanctify his house' (Lev. 27:14) that consecration requires the item to be in one's possession, just as one's house is in one's domain.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was the purpose of the pious ones setting aside coins near their fourth-year vineyard?
A. To pay for damages caused by passersby
B. To desacralize grapes that might be picked by thieves, preventing them from eating unredeemed produce
C. To purchase the vineyard from its previous owner
D. To fulfill the obligation of pe'ah (corner of the field)
Correct: B — The pious ones went beyond the letter of the law to protect even thieves from the sin of eating unredeemed fourth-year grapes, by declaring that anything picked would be desacralized onto the coins.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara retract its suggestion that R' Yochanan reformulated the pious ones' declaration into future tense?
A. Because the pious ones explicitly used past tense in their declaration
B. Because R' Yochanan rejects the principle of retroactive designation (bereirah)
C. Because Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel disagreed with the reformulation
D. Because future-tense declarations have no legal standing
Correct: B — The future-tense reformulation would rely on bereirah (retroactive designation). Since R' Yochanan holds brothers dividing inheritance are like purchasers — demonstrating he rejects bereirah — the reformulation is untenable.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When two unattributed mishnayot conflict, what principle did R' Yochanan use to choose between them?
A. He followed whichever mishna was taught earlier chronologically
B. He followed the stricter opinion (l'chumra)
C. He followed the mishna supported by a biblical verse
D. He followed the majority opinion among the Tannaim
Correct: C — R' Yochanan chose the mishna about double payment over the pious ones' practice because it is supported by the verse 'When a man shall sanctify his house' (Lev. 27:14), teaching that consecration requires possession.
