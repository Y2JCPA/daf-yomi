Bava Kamma 19 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi's first dilemma, if shinui (deviation) applies to tzeroros and an animal propels pebbles atypically, how much does the owner pay?
A. Full damage
B. Half damage
C. One-quarter damage
D. Exempt entirely
Correct: C — If shinui applies to tzeroros, the owner pays half of the standard half-damage for pebbles, resulting in one-quarter of the total damage — half of half.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say a rooster inserting its head into a glass vessel is NOT atypical behavior (shinui)?
A. Roosters naturally explore vessels
B. There were seeds inside the vessel
C. The vessel was in the rooster's pen
D. The owner trained the rooster
Correct: B — The Gemara answers that there were seeds in the vessel, making it typical behavior for the rooster to extend its head inside to eat, rather than atypical behavior classified as Goring.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Sumakhos, why might a koach kocho (force of a force of a force) case reduce liability to half damage?
A. It falls under Goring instead of Trampling
B. The halakha l'Moshe mi-Sinai of half-damage may apply specifically to such indirect causation
C. The owner could not have prevented it
D. Public domain rules exempt it
Correct: B — Sumakhos may hold that the halakha l'Moshe mi-Sinai of half-damage for tzeroros was transmitted specifically for indirect force (koach kocho), while standard pebble damage (koach) receives full damage as a subcategory of Trampling.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why is pebble (tzeroros) damage exempt in the public domain?
A. It is too minor to warrant liability
B. Tzeroros is a subcategory of Regel (Trampling), which is exempt in the public domain
C. The owner cannot control pebbles in public spaces
D. R' Yochanan rules that all indirect damage is exempt publicly
Correct: B — Since tzeroros is classified as a subcategory of Regel (Trampling), it follows Regel's exemption in the public domain. R' Zeira clarifies that R' Yochanan's rule about half-damage applying everywhere refers to Goring, not tzeroros.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, if a person ties a string to a chicken and the string breaks a vessel, why does the owner pay full damage rather than half?
A. The chicken acted intentionally
B. The string is classified as Bor (Pit), created by a person
C. Tying the string makes the chicken mu'ad (forewarned)
D. The owner is directly negligent for any resulting damage
Correct: B — When a person ties the string, it is classified as Bor (Pit) — a hazard created by human action. The Torah assigns full liability for Pit damage. By contrast, a self-entangled string is treated like tzeroros (pebbles) at half damage.
