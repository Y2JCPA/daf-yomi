Is There Forewarning (Ha'ada'ah) for Pebbles?

First Tanna (Baraita)
There is forewarning for pebbles — after 3 incidents of pebble damage, the owner pays full cost.
Rav Yosef
There is no forewarning for pebbles — the owner always pays only half the damage.
Gemara
Perhaps both agree, and the baraita discusses a single incident. The dispute is really Sumakhos vs. Rabbis on the base payment for tzeroros.
Rava
I raise my own dilemma: Is there forewarning for pebbles or not?
KEY QUESTION If tzeroros is a halakha l'Moshe mi-Sinai capping liability at half, can forewarning override that cap?

Sumakhos vs. Rabbis: Koach Kocho (Force of a Force)

Sumakhos

  • Pebbles (tzeroros) → owner pays full damage
  • Tzeroros is a subcategory of Regel (Trampling)
  • The halakha l'Moshe mi-Sinai of half-damage applies only to koach kocho (force of a force)
  • Dilemma: What about a force generated by a force of a force? — Unresolved

Rabbis (Majority)

  • Pebbles (tzeroros) → owner pays half damage
  • The halakha l'Moshe mi-Sinai caps payment at half
  • Applies even to standard koach (direct pebble propulsion)
  • No distinction between koach and koach kocho needed
CORE DISPUTE According to Sumakhos, the half-damage tradition from Sinai applies only to indirect force of a force, not to standard pebble damage.

Tail Swinging & Atypical Body Movements

R' Yehuda Nesia / R' Oshaya
If an animal swung its tail and caused damage, what is the halakha?
The Other Sage
Must the owner grasp its tail while walking? Tail-swinging is typical behavior — he should be exempt!
Gemara
Unlike Goring (atypical), tail-swinging is normal. But the dilemma is about excessive tail-swinging — is that still typical?
Rav Eina
What if the animal swung its penis and caused damage? Is that like Goring (urge overcame it) or not (no intent to damage)?
Gemara
Teiku — Rav Eina's dilemma also remains unresolved.
DISTINCTION Goring = atypical behavior, owner must prevent it. Tail/body movements = typical behavior — but excessive versions create uncertainty.
