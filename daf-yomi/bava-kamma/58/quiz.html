Bava Kamma 58 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is the garden owner NOT treated like one who 'repels a lion' (and thus uncompensated)?
A. Because the garden owner acted intentionally
B. Because the garden owner suffered a loss and did not act voluntarily
C. Because the Rabbis enacted a special decree for gardens
D. Because the animal's owner explicitly asked for help
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes: one who repels a lion acts with intent and suffers no loss, whereas the garden owner did not choose this situation and his produce was damaged.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Kahana, if another animal of the same owner pushed the first animal into the garden, what does the owner pay?
A. Nothing — it was an accident
B. Only for the benefit derived
C. Full damages — the owner was negligent
D. Half damages as an unusual occurrence
Correct: C — Rav Kahana holds that if another animal pushed it, the owner should have led his animals one by one, making this negligence — so he pays full damages.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Yochanan, when does the owner become liable for full damages after the animal fell into the garden?
A. As soon as the animal moves to a second garden bed
B. After the animal eats for more than one hour
C. Once the animal leaves the garden and returns with the owner's knowledge
D. Only if the animal causes damage to the fence on re-entry
Correct: C — R' Yochanan holds that even moving bed to bed all day only incurs benefit-payment. Full damages apply only once the animal leaves and returns with the owner's knowledge, as the animal has now 'learned the path.'
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does the phrase 'bisde acher' (Exodus 22:4) teach about damage appraisal?
A. Damage is appraised only by the injured party
B. Damage is appraised at market price on the day of the incident
C. Damage is appraised relative to the surrounding field, not the damaged patch alone
D. Damage is appraised by three independent experts
Correct: C — The Gemara derives from the placement and wording of 'bisde acher' that damage is appraised relative to the broader field — using a 1-in-60 ratio — yielding a fairer valuation.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject appraising damage based on a beit kav (small area) by itself?
A. It would make the calculation too difficult
B. Small isolated patches have inflated value, unfairly enhancing the injured party's position
C. The Torah explicitly prohibits small-area appraisals
D. A beit kav is too small to have any meaningful market value
Correct: B — Appraising a small area in isolation inflates its value because small patches sell at a premium per unit. This would unfairly enhance the injured party's position at the liable party's expense.
