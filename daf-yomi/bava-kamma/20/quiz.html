QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Rava obligate the goat's owner to pay full damage for the broken barrel?
A. Because climbing to eat is typical behavior, making the breakage part of Shen
B. Because the barrel was on private property
C. Because the goat acted with intent to destroy
D. Because breaking barrels is typical goat behavior
Correct: A — Since eating the turnip was typical and climbing was necessary to reach it, the resulting breakage is classified as Shen (Eating), not Keren (Goring), and full damage applies.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Ilfa, why is the owner liable when his animal stretches its neck to eat from a sack on another animal's back in public?
A. Because stretching the neck is atypical behavior
B. Because the back of the other animal is like the injured party's private property
C. Because eating from sacks is always classified as Keren
D. Because the public domain has stricter rules for Shen
Correct: B — Ilfa considers the back of the other animal equivalent to the injured party's courtyard (reshut ha-nizak), so Shen rules apply and the owner pays full damage.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rav's principle regarding an animal that ate garments left in the public domain?
A. The owner always pays half damage for atypical eating
B. The owner pays full damage because garments are valuable
C. The owner is exempt because the injured party deviated first by leaving items in public
D. The owner is exempt because eating garments is too atypical to classify
Correct: C — Rav's principle: When the injured party deviated from normal behavior (leaving garments in public), and then the animal also deviated (eating them), the animal's owner is exempt.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the 'zeh neheneh v'zeh lo chaser' debate, why does Rava criticize Rami bar Ḥama's proof from the mishna?
A. Because the mishna discusses Keren, not Shen
B. Because the mishna's case involves benefit WITH loss, while the squatter case involves benefit WITHOUT loss
C. Because the mishna is about animals, not people
D. Because Rami bar Ḥama quoted the wrong mishna
Correct: B — Rava points out that the mishna deals with an animal eating produce (benefit with loss), while the courtyard squatter case involves benefit without any loss to the owner — these are fundamentally different categories.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does the collapsed house case (upper and lower story) suggest about 'zeh neheneh v'zeh lo chaser'?
A. The beneficiary must always pay
B. The beneficiary is exempt — rent is not deducted from the upper story owner's expenses
C. The beneficiary pays half the market rent
D. Both parties split the costs equally
Correct: B — The upper story owner who rebuilt and lived in the lower story does not have rent deducted from expenses owed to him, suggesting that when one benefits and the other suffers no loss, the beneficiary is exempt from paying.
