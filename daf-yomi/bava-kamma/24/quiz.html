Bava Kamma 24 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Meir, how many gorings are needed to make an ox forewarned, and must they occur on separate days?
A. 3 gorings on 3 separate days
B. 3 gorings, even on the same day
C. 2 gorings on 2 days
D. 1 goring is sufficient with witnesses
Correct: B — R' Meir holds that three gorings suffice to make an ox forewarned, even if all three occur on a single day. He derives this through a kal va-chomer (a fortiori) argument.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did the Sages bring the case of the zava (woman with uterine discharge) against R' Meir?
A. To show that frequency doesn't always create stricter status — the zava needs separate days, not more sightings on one day
B. To prove that women and men have identical purity laws
C. To show that animal law follows purity law exactly
D. To demonstrate that goring requires physical examination
Correct: A — The Sages argued that the zava disproves R' Meir's kal va-chomer: a zava who sees blood 3 times on one day is pure, but on 3 separate days she is impure — showing that more frequent occurrences don't always lead to a stricter outcome.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the practical difference between saying the 3 days of testimony are 'to render the ox forewarned' vs. 'to forewarn the owner'?
A. Whether half or full damages are paid
B. Whether 3 groups of witnesses can all testify on the same day
C. Whether the ox must be destroyed
D. Whether the owner must sell the ox
Correct: B — If the testimony is to render the ox forewarned, witnesses can come on one day. If it is to forewarn the owner, they must come on separate days so the owner receives repeated warnings.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rava, if someone incites another person's dog and the dog bites the inciter himself, what is the dog owner's liability?
A. Full damages
B. Half damages
C. Exempt — the inciter deviated from normative behavior
D. Liable only if he knew the dog was dangerous
Correct: C — Rava rules that when someone deviates from normative behavior (inciting a dog against himself), and the dog responds by biting, the owner is exempt — because the victim's own deviation triggered the harm.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Rav Naḥman follow R' Yosei's combined opinion rather than R' Shimon's?
A. R' Yosei is chronologically earlier
B. R' Yosei's reasoning (nimmuko) is with him — it is sound
C. R' Shimon's view was rejected by the Sanhedrin
D. R' Yosei had more students supporting him
Correct: B — When Rava challenged Rav Naḥman that R' Shimon's combination is equally valid, Rav Naḥman replied that he follows R' Yosei because 'R' Yosei nimmuko imo' — his analysis/reasoning is sound and accompanies his rulings.
