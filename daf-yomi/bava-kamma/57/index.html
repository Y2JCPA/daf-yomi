Is a Finder of Lost Property a Paid or Unpaid Bailee?

Rabba
A finder of a lost item is like a shomer chinam (unpaid bailee) — liable only for negligence.
Rav Yosef
A finder is like a shomer sachar (paid bailee) — liable even for theft and loss.
Rabba
I concede regarding lost animals — since they wander, they need extra guarding. But not for other items.
Abaye
Rav Yosef, don't you agree a finder is unpaid? R' Yochanan says a finder who falsely claims theft pays double — only unpaid bailees pay double!
Rav Yosef
An unpaid bailee pays double for any false theft claim. A paid bailee only pays double for a false listim mezuyan claim.
Core Issue The Gemara never reaches a definitive conclusion — the question of a finder's bailee status remains unresolved (teiku-like).

Owner not typically present → Invalid return

Rabba's Reading The baraita teaches two separate halakhot: proper return (morning) frees the finder; improper return (midday) leaves him liable even as an unpaid bailee, due to negligence.

Unpaid Bailee vs Paid Bailee: Key Differences

Shomer Chinam (Unpaid)

  • Liable only for negligence (peshia)
  • Exempt for theft, loss, or accident
  • False theft claim → pays double payment
  • Rabba's view of a finder's status

Shomer Sachar (Paid)

  • Liable for theft and loss
  • Exempt only for accident (ones)
  • False claim of armed bandit → double payment
  • Rav Yosef's view of a finder's status
Why Would a Finder Be Paid? Rav Yosef reasons: performing the mitzvah of hashavat aveidah itself constitutes 'payment' — the spiritual reward elevates the finder's duty of care.

Where Can You Return a Lost Item?

Rabba
The Torah says "hashev teshivem" — the doubled verb teaches that returning to the owner's garden or ruin (even unsecured) fulfills the mitzvah.
Rabba
If returning to an unsecured location suffices, the finder must be like an unpaid bailee — a paid bailee would need higher security!
Rav Yosef
No — the baraita means a secured garden or ruin. The novelty is that you don't need the owner's knowledge of the return.
R' Elazar
All who must return items require the owner's knowledge — except for returning lost items, as "hashev teshivem" permits return without notification.
Deuteronomy 22:1 "Hashev teshivem" — the double expression expands the permissible methods of returning lost items beyond what is required for other returns.

The Renter's Cow: Testing the Armed Bandit Theory

🐄 Renter hires a cow from its owner
↓
🦹 The cow is stolen
↓
💬 Renter says: "I'll pay — I don't want to swear an oath"
↓
🔎 The thief is later found
↓
💰 Thief pays double payment to the renter
↓
❓ Does this prove armed bandit = thief? Gemara: No — multiple alternative explanations exist
Rejected Proof The Gemara offers three refutations: (1) the baraita may follow R' Meir (renter = unpaid bailee), (2) it may involve an unarmed bandit per Rabba bar Avuh's reversal, or (3) per R' Zeira, the claim was of an armed bandit but the actual theft was by an unarmed one.
