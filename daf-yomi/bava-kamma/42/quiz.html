QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye and Rava's final explanation, why does R' Yosei HaGelili need the verse 'the owner shall be clear' for miscarried offspring?
A. Because Exodus 21:22 doesn't mention oxen at all
B. Because when the woman dies, men face capital punishment and are exempt from payment, but ox owners would still owe payment without this verse
C. Because innocuous oxen are more dangerous than forewarned ones
D. Because the Torah never discusses animal-caused miscarriage elsewhere
Correct: B — Abaye and Rava explain that without the verse, since ox owners don't receive capital punishment when the woman dies, they would remain financially liable — unlike men, who are exempt from payment when facing execution.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rava mean by the expression 'the native is on the ground and the stranger is in the heavens'?
A. Foreign law should not apply to Torah cases
B. The proposed interpretation inverts the expected stringency hierarchy between innocuous and forewarned oxen
C. Converts have different legal obligations than native Jews
D. Heavenly law overrides earthly law
Correct: B — Rava uses this idiom to reject Rav Ulla's suggestion, which would make an innocuous ox MORE liable than a forewarned ox — contradicting the basic principle that forewarned carries greater liability.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Akiva (in Rava's final explanation on Amud B), why is the verse needed for the Canaanite slave case?
A. Because slaves have no legal standing
B. Because the Torah imposes a fixed thirty-shekel payment for a slave — more stringent than a freeman — so we might extend this stringency to innocuous oxen
C. Because innocuous oxen cannot be stoned
D. Because the slave's owner cannot bring the case to court
Correct: B — Rava explains that since the Torah is stricter regarding slaves (fixed 30 shekel even if the slave was worth less), we might think this stringency extends to innocuous ox liability too. The verse teaches otherwise.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the 'slaughtered ox' discussion, what scenario does Rav Shmuel bar Rav Yitzḥak describe?
A. The ox escapes before trial
B. The owner sells the ox to a third party
C. The owner slaughters the ox before it is sentenced to stoning
D. The court delays the ox's execution indefinitely
Correct: C — Rav Shmuel explains that R' Akiva's interpretation applies where the owner slaughtered the ox before the court verdict — the verse teaches that the victim's family still cannot collect from the slaughtered ox's flesh.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Abaye reject Rava's second attempt to justify R' Yosei HaGelili's reading?
A. Because the logic would equally apply to compensation for humiliation, but R' Yosei HaGelili only mentions miscarried offspring
B. Because Rava misquoted the verse
C. Because Abaye holds R' Yosei HaGelili's position is entirely wrong
D. Because the a fortiori inference is mathematically incorrect
Correct: A — Abaye argues that if Rava's logic works for miscarried offspring, it should equally apply to humiliation — yet R' Yosei HaGelili's baraita only mentions offspring, not humiliation, proving the explanation doesn't fit.
