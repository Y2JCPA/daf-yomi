Deriving Double Payment from Exodus 22: Step by Step

Torah states: "If the theft shall be found (himmatze timmatze)... ox, donkey, sheep, alive (chayyim)"
↓
The Gemara asks: How do we derive that all items (not just animals) require double payment?
↓
Baraita's Approach
Treats verse as klal u'prat u'klal (generalization, detail, generalization)
Final Resolution
School of R' Yishmael: ribbui u'mi'ut u'ribbui (amplification, restriction, amplification)
Core Issue The Torah could have simply said 'theft' to cover everything — why list specific animals? Each detail teaches an exclusion from double payment.

The Baraita's Step-by-Step Analysis of the Verse

📖 Start with 'ox' + 'theft' — should include all items via klal u'prat
↓
🐑 But 'sheep' is stated — excludes limiting to altar-worthy items
↓
🫏 'Donkey' is stated — excludes limiting to firstborn-sanctity items
↓
🐾 'Alive' is stated — excludes limiting to animals only
↓
❓ So why are all these words needed if 'theft' covers everything?
Method Each new detail in the verse prevents limiting double payment to a narrow category, progressively expanding scope.

How to Read the Verse's Structure

Gemara
The baraita says: Let the verse state 'ox' then 'theft.' But isn't it written 'theft' then 'ox'? That's a klal u'prat — only ox would be included!
Rava
The tanna relies on the word 'alive' (chayyim) at the end as a second generalization. So it's really klal u'prat u'klal — generalization, detail, generalization.
Gemara
But the two generalizations aren't similar! 'Theft' includes everything; 'alive' includes only animals!
Rava
This tanna is from the school of Rabbi Yishmael, who applies klal u'prat u'klal even when the generalizations are dissimilar.
Key Point Rava resolves the baraita's structure by identifying 'chayyim' as the closing generalization, making the verse a proper klal u'prat u'klal.

The Problem with Klal u'Prat u'Klal Here

Himmatze timmatze — two generalizations adjacent to each other
↓
Details (ox, sheep, donkey) follow after both generalizations
↓
This is klal, klal, prat — not klal, prat, klal!
↓
Ravina cites Eretz Yisrael principle: Place the detail between the two generalizations
↓
Insert 'ox' between himmatze and timmatze → now it's klal, prat, klal
Ravina's Rule Wherever two generalizations are adjacent, insert the detail between them and expound as klal u'prat u'klal.

Two Hermeneutical Approaches

Klal u'Prat u'Klal

  • Generalization → Detail → Generalization
  • Include items SIMILAR to the detail
  • Problem: 'sheep' has nothing left to include
  • Approach ultimately rejected here

Ribbui u'Mi'ut u'Ribbui

  • Amplification → Restriction → Amplification
  • Include EVERYTHING except specific exclusions
  • School of Rabbi Yishmael's method
  • Final accepted approach on this daf
Difference Klal u'prat u'klal includes only what resembles the detail; ribbui u'mi'ut u'ribbui includes everything except what's specifically excluded.

What the Three Details Exclude from Double Payment

Detail in VerseWhat It ExcludesReason
🐂 OxLand (karka)Land is immovable property — unlike an ox
🫏 DonkeyCanaanite Slaves (avadim)Slaves are compared to land in halakha
🐑 SheepFinancial Documents (shtarot)Documents lack intrinsic monetary value
Result Double payment applies to all movable property with intrinsic monetary value — excluding land, slaves, and documents.
