QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara's resolution, when does mere denial (without an oath) make a bailee liable for accidents?
A. When the deposit is in the bailee's house
B. When the deposit is in a neutral location like a swamp
C. Only when the denial is made before witnesses
D. Never — an oath is always required
Correct: A — The Gemara distinguishes: if the deposit is in the bailee's possession (his house), denial alone creates liability. Ilfa's requirement of an oath applies only when the deposit is in a neutral place like a swamp.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rav's ruling when witnesses prove a court oath was false?
A. The oath-taker pays double
B. The oath-taker is exempt from monetary restitution
C. The oath-taker must bring a guilt-offering but also pay
D. The case is retried from the beginning
Correct: B — Rav derives from Exodus 22:10 ('the owner accepted it, and he shall not make restitution') that once the claimant accepted the oath, the oath-taker is exempt from paying even if witnesses later prove the oath false.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rav Nahman explain why the mishna requires payment after an oath, seemingly contradicting Rav?
A. The mishna refers to a non-Jewish court
B. The mishna's oath was taken by 'leaping' before the court required it — not a full-fledged oath
C. The mishna deals with a case where no witnesses exist
D. Rav Nahman rejects Rav's position entirely
Correct: B — Rav Nahman distinguishes between one who 'leaped' to swear voluntarily (not a valid court oath — so Rav's exemption doesn't apply) and one who swore when the court required it (where Rav's exemption applies).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the source for R' Yochanan's ruling that a false-claiming bailee pays fourfold/fivefold if he slaughtered or sold the animal?
A. A kal va-chomer from ordinary theft
B. A juxtaposition of verses in Exodus 22:6-7, or the expanded term 'הגנב'
C. An explicit verse stating the bailee's liability
D. A rabbinic enactment to deter dishonest bailees
Correct: B — R' Yochanan's ruling is based on the juxtaposition (סמיכות) of verses about thieves and false-claiming bailees, or alternatively from the expanded term 'הגנב' (the thief) which includes the bailee. Logical objections cannot refute textual derivations.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does R' Yochanan say the baraita's case of 'eating the ox' involves a neveilah (unslaughtered carcass)?
A. Because eating a neveilah is a worse sin requiring harsher punishment
B. Because if it was properly slaughtered, he would owe fourfold/fivefold — and the baraita only mentions double payment
C. Because the baraita explicitly states it was a neveilah
D. Because R' Yochanan holds proper slaughter is impossible for stolen animals
Correct: B — The baraita rules only double payment. If the animal was properly slaughtered, R' Yochanan would require fourfold/fivefold. So the case must involve eating without valid slaughter (neveilah), where no fourfold/fivefold applies.
