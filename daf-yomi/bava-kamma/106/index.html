Does Denial Alone Create Robber Status?

Rav Sheshet
One who denies a deposit becomes liable for accidents (אונסין) — treated as a robber from the moment of denial.
Ilfa
An oath effects acquisition (שבועה קונה) — only the oath, not mere denial, transforms liability.
Gemara
If only an oath effects acquisition, then denial alone should NOT create robber-status — contradicting Rav Sheshet!
Resolution
Ilfa refers to a deposit in a swamp (not bailee's house). If in his house — denial alone suffices to acquire it.
DISTINCTION Location matters: if the deposit is in the bailee's possession, denial alone creates liability. If in a neutral place, only an oath does.

Rav's Rule: Oath Exempts from Payment

Claimant says: 'You owe me 100 dinars'
↓
Defendant denies & takes an oath
↓
Witnesses arrive proving the oath was false
↓
Rav: He is exempt from payment
'The owner accepted the oath — he shall not make restitution' (Exodus 22:10)
RAV'S PRINCIPLE Once the claimant accepted the oath, the Torah releases the oath-taker from monetary restitution — even if later proved false.

Rava Analyzes Rav's Ruling: Loan vs. Deposit

Denying a Loan (הלוואה)

  • Loan money is meant to be spent
  • No longer in the borrower's possession
  • Torah reasonably exempts after oath
  • Rava: Rav's ruling makes sense here

Denying a Deposit (פקדון)

  • Deposit remains in owner's possession
  • Must be returned intact
  • Not really 'monetary restitution'
  • Yet Rav applies exemption here too — because the verse IS about deposits!
RAVA'S CONCLUSION Despite the logical distinction, Rav's exemption applies even to deposits — because the source verse (Exodus 22:10) explicitly discusses a bailee.

R' Yochanan: False Bailee Pays Like a Thief

R' Yochanan
A bailee who falsely claims theft pays double. If he slaughtered or sold the animal — fourfold or fivefold (ד' וה').
Objection
A thief pays double even without an oath — the bailee only pays double after swearing falsely. They're not comparable!
Sages
R' Yochanan's source is a juxtaposition (סמיכות) of verses — logical objections cannot refute textual derivations.
Alternative
Or from the expanded term 'הגנב' (the thief) instead of 'גנב' — the ה"א includes the false-claiming bailee.
BIBLICAL SOURCE Exodus 22:6-7 — the juxtaposition of 'if the thief shall be found' and 'if the thief shall not be found' links actual thieves to bailees who falsely claim theft.
