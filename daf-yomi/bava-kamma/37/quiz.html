QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why do orphans not require a prosbol?
A. Because orphans are exempt from all debt laws
B. Because the court automatically serves as their steward
C. Because the Sabbatical Year does not apply to minors
D. Because Rabban Gamliel personally guaranteed their debts
Correct: B — The court is legally considered the steward (apotropos) of orphans, so their debts are automatically transferred to the court — achieving the same effect as a prosbol without needing one.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the core disagreement between Rav Zevid and Rav Pappa?
A. Whether an ox can ever become forewarned
B. Whether forewarning for one species extends by default to all species
C. Whether the mishna is authoritative
D. Whether half-damage applies to innocuous oxen
Correct: B — Rav Zevid holds that by default, forewarning extends to all species (the mishna discusses the special case of proven innocuousness). Rav Pappa holds forewarning is species-specific by default.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Yehuda, when does an ox forewarned for Shabbat revert to innocuous status?
A. After 30 days without goring
B. After three consecutive Shabbatot without goring
C. After children can safely pet it
D. After it is sold to a new owner
Correct: B — R' Yehuda rules that an ox forewarned for Shabbat reverts to innocuous status when it refrains from goring for three consecutive Shabbatot — matching the three-incident pattern that established the forewarning.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Mishna exempt liability when a Jew's ox gores a consecrated ox?
A. Because consecrated animals cannot be damaged
B. Because the Torah says 'the ox of another' — excluding Temple property
C. Because the Temple has its own insurance system
D. Because only Kohanim can assess damage to sacred animals
Correct: B — Exodus 21:35 uses the phrase 'the ox of another' (שור רעהו), which the Sages interpret as excluding consecrated property — it belongs to the Temple treasury, not to 'another' person.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Rava need to teach that an ox repeatedly goring upon hearing a shofar becomes forewarned?
A. Because shofar sounds are sacred and cannot cause sin
B. Because one might think the first goring was merely a startle response, not a pattern
C. Because forewarning only applies to visual stimuli
D. Because the ox might be responding to a different sound each time
Correct: B — Rava's teaching addresses the concern that the first shofar blast merely startled (siyyuta) the ox. Since the pattern repeated three times, it proves the shofar is a consistent trigger — not a random startle.
