QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava's resolution of Rami bar Ḥama's dilemma, what determines whether the one-fifth payment is exempted?
A. The monetary obligation that creates double payment liability
B. The oath that creates double payment liability
C. Whether the bailee admitted voluntarily or was caught by witnesses
D. Whether the bailee is paid or unpaid
Correct: A — Rava proves from a Mishnah in Shevuot that it is the monetary obligation — not the oath — that exempts from the one-fifth payment. This is demonstrated by the case of a non-bailee whose oath cannot create double payment liability.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In Rava's proof from Shevuot 49b, why is the case of a non-bailee accused of theft significant?
A. A non-bailee cannot take an oath at all
B. A non-bailee's oath does not create double payment liability, yet he still doesn't pay one-fifth after witnesses testify
C. A non-bailee always pays both double and one-fifth
D. A non-bailee is exempt from all penalties
Correct: B — The significance is that a non-bailee's oath cannot trigger double payment (that rule applies only to bailees). Yet after witnesses testify creating double payment liability, he doesn't owe one-fifth — proving the monetary obligation, not the oath, is the determining factor.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava, when does a bailee retain standing to act as the owner's agent in pursuing a thief?
A. Whenever the bailee took any oath
B. Only when the bailee paid the owner without taking an oath
C. When the bailee's oath turns out to have been truthful
D. Only when the bailee is a paid bailee (shomer sachar)
Correct: C — Rava rules that a bailee who swore truthfully — meaning the deposit really was stolen — retains the owner's trust and standing. A false oath severs the relationship, and the owner would not rely on such a bailee to collect on their behalf.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How do Abaye and Rava disagree regarding an unpaid bailee when a deposit is stolen through circumstances beyond his control?
A. Abaye says the bailee is liable; Rava says he is exempt
B. Abaye says the bailee can choose to swear and step away or pursue the thief; Rava says he must pursue the thief
C. Abaye says only a paid bailee can pursue the thief; Rava says both can
D. They agree that both types of bailee must pursue the thief
Correct: B — Abaye gives the unpaid bailee a choice: pay the owner and pursue the thief, or take an oath and let the owner handle it. Rava holds that both unpaid and paid bailees must enter into judgment with the thief — neither can simply swear and walk away.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Ravina's dilemma about splitting the one-fifth and double payment among two bailees asks which fundamental question?
A. Whether two bailees can share custody of one deposit
B. Whether the Torah's rule against combining penalties applies per person or per monetary obligation
C. Whether witnesses can testify against only one of two bailees
D. Whether an oath by one bailee exempts the other from oath
Correct: B — Ravina asks: Does the Torah bar one person from paying both penalties, or does it bar both penalties from being paid on one obligation regardless of how many people are involved? If one bailee admits (owing one-fifth) and the other is caught by witnesses (owing double), are these still barred? The question remains unresolved (teyku).
