Why Are Conspiring Witnesses Excluded?

Gemara
Conspiring witnesses pay monetary restitution — the exact sum they conspired to cause the defendant to lose. Why doesn't Rabbi Oshaya list this?
Gemara (Answer)
Rabbi Oshaya holds like Rabbi Akiva: conspiring witnesses do not pay on their own admission.
Gemara
If they don't pay on admission, it must be a fine (kenas), not restitution — and Rabbi Oshaya doesn't list fines.
Gemara (Challenge)
But if he holds like R' Akiva, he should list two types of Ox: ox damages property (half damages) vs. ox injures a person (full damages)!
Gemara (Answer)
R' Akiva 'broke the force of his fist' (gezizeih) — even for ox injuring a person, payment comes only from the body of the ox, not superior property. So it doesn't belong on the list.
Exodus 21:31 "According to this judgment shall it be done to him" — limits payment to proceeds from the ox's body, not the owner's superior-quality property.

Damage Evident vs. Damage Not Evident

Damage Evident (Hezek Nikar)

  • Physical change to the object
  • E.g., ox gores another ox
  • Universally classified as damage
  • Already covered in Rabbi Oshaya's list

Damage Not Evident (Hezek She'eino Nikar)

  • Object physically unchanged but halakhic status changed
  • E.g., making teruma impure, mixing teruma with non-sacred food, pouring wine as idol libation
  • Disputed: is it damage or a fine?
  • Either way — excluded from Rabbi Oshaya's list
R' Ḥiyya's View R' Ḥiyya lists these as distinct categories. Even if he holds damage not evident is real damage, he distinguishes between evident and non-evident types.

Rava: Deriving Categories via Common Factor

Rava
If you add Pit to any other category, all remaining categories can be derived through a common factor (tzad ha-shaveh).
Rava
The one exception is Goring — because all other categories are forewarned from the outset (mu'ad mi-techilatan), but Goring starts as innocuous (tam).
Dissenter
On the contrary — Goring is superior because the ox's objective is to cause damage. So even Goring can be derived from a common factor!
Gemara
If all categories can be derived from others, why did the Torah write them all explicitly?
Gemara (Answer)
Each category has unique halakhot that can only be derived from its own verse.
Tzad Ha-Shaveh The 'common factor' method: if two sources share a common element, a third case sharing that element can be derived — unless it has a distinguishing feature that breaks the analogy.
