Fanning Flames + Wind: Why Is He Exempt?

Gemara
If one fanned a fire and the wind also fanned it, and his fanning alone was not sufficient — he is exempt. But why? One who winnows on Shabbat with wind assistance is liable!
Abaye
He fanned from one side, the wind from the other. The fire went the wind's direction — his fanning clearly didn't help.
Rava
He fanned along with a normal wind (insufficient), then an atypical wind came and spread the fire. He couldn't have anticipated it.
R' Zeira
He merely heated (tzamurei) the fire by breathing on it — not proper fanning at all.
Rav Ashi
On Shabbat, Torah prohibits melekhet maḥshevet — planned labor where the objective is met. In damages, this is merely indirect causation (grama) — and grama be-nizakin patur.
Key Principle Grama be-nizakin patur — One who causes damage through indirect action is exempt in tort law, even though in Shabbat law the same act would be prohibited.

Dispute Rabbis: 'Standing grain' = only exposed items are covered (concealed items exempt).

R' Yehuda vs. Rabbis: Concealed Articles in a Fire

Rabbis (Majority)

  • "Standing grain" = exposed items only
  • Concealed articles (tamun) — exempt
  • "Or standing grain" — the word "or" includes items of stature (trees, animals)
  • The word "or" also divides the categories (liable for each individually)

Rabbi Yehuda

  • "Standing grain" = items of stature
  • Concealed articles (tamun) — liable
  • The word "or" is needed to divide the categories
  • Liability applies to all items damaged by fire, whether hidden or exposed
Practical Impact If someone's fire burns a haystack and hidden jewelry was inside — the Rabbis exempt the fire-starter from the jewelry; R' Yehuda holds him liable for everything.

Conduct During Plague & Famine

🏠 Plague in the city? Gather your feet — stay indoors! (Exodus 12:22)
↓
🪟 Rava would close the windows during plague (Jeremiah 9:20: "Death comes through our windows")
↓
🌙 Even during the day, remain inside — "Enter your chambers, shut your doors" (Isaiah 26:20)
↓
⚔️ Even if there's fear inside — "Outside the sword bereaves" — still safer indoors (Deut. 32:25)
↓
🍞 Famine in the city? Leave! Avram went to Egypt during famine (Genesis 12:10)
↓
🚶 Even with uncertain danger in leaving — better than certain starvation (II Kings 7:4)
Opposite Rules Plague → stay put. The danger is everywhere outside.
Famine → leave. Staying guarantees suffering; leaving offers hope.
