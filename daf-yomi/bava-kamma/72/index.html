Daf 72: Slaughter Timing & Conspiring Witnesses

Core Question: When does shechitah take halakhic effect?
Reish Lakish
Only at the end of the slaughter process
R' Yochanan
Throughout — from beginning to end
↓
Mishna: Rules for edim zomemim — conspiring witnesses pay what they conspired to impose
↓
Gemara: When is a conspiring witness disqualified from future testimony?

Retroactively — from when he testified

KEY THEME This daf examines how the precise moment of a legal act — slaughter or testimony — determines liability and disqualification.

When Does Shechitah Take Effect?

Rav Chavivi of Mechoza
Slaughter must be considered performed only at the end. If it were throughout, then slaughtering a non-sacred animal in the azarah — the first cut would prohibit the animal, and the rest of the slaughter wouldn't be on an owned animal!
Rav Huna b. Rava
Even if slaughter is throughout, the obligation for the 4x/5x payment attaches at the first bit of slaughter — before the animal becomes forbidden.
Rav Ashi
Don't dismiss Rav Chavivi so quickly! The verse says 'and slaughter it' — we need complete slaughter for the 4x/5x payment. A partial cut is not yet slaughter.
Rav Gamda / Rava
Resolution: The mishna discusses where the thief cut part of the simanim outside the Temple and finished inside. The prohibition and liability arise together only at the end.
PRINCIPLE The Torah requires complete slaughter ('and slaughter it') to trigger the fourfold or fivefold payment — partial cutting is insufficient.

The Temple Slaughter Problem

Thief steals an animal and slaughters it in the Temple courtyard (azarah)
↓
Does the prohibition on non-sacred slaughter in the azarah take effect during or after the cut?
If During
First cut → animal is forbidden → no longer 'owned' → no 4x/5x?
If At End
Prohibition and obligation arise simultaneously → 4x/5x payment applies
↓
Rav Gamda's solution: Simanim cut partly outside, finished inside — works even if slaughter is throughout
RESOLUTION By splitting the act of slaughter between outside and inside the Temple, the prohibition and the liability become concomitant.

Splitting the 4x/5x Between Witness Sets

First Set (Theft)

  • Testified that the defendant stole the animal
  • If conspiring: pay the double payment (kefel)
  • Their testimony is the foundation — without it, no slaughter liability exists
  • Even one invalid witness collapses everything

Second Set (Slaughter/Sale)

  • Testified that the defendant slaughtered or sold the animal
  • If conspiring: pay only the additional amount (2x sheep / 3x ox)
  • Their testimony is dependent on the first set's validity
  • One invalid witness voids only their testimony
VERSE "You shall do to him as he had conspired to do to his brother" (Deuteronomy 19:19) — each set of witnesses bears the exact penalty they sought to impose.

When Is a Conspiring Witness Disqualified?

Abaye
Retroactively — from the time he testified. He was already a rasha (wicked person) at that moment. The Torah says: 'Do not put your hand with the wicked to be a witness' (Exodus 23:1).
Rava
From this point forward only. The entire law of edim zomemim is a chiddush (novelty) — logically, it's 2 vs. 2! Why believe the second set over the first? Since the whole concept is anomalous, we limit its reach.
Abaye
The wickedness is a fact about the person — it existed when he lied, not just when he was caught. Any testimony given after his false testimony is tainted.
Rava
We only know he is wicked once the second set testifies. Until then, he has chezkat kashrut — a presumption of validity. Don't extend the novelty beyond its bounds.
PRACTICAL IMPACT If Abaye is right, all testimony this witness gave after his false testimony is retroactively invalidated — potentially unraveling other court cases.
