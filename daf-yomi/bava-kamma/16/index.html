Who Does the Mishna Follow? And How Do Lions Cause Damage?

R' Elazar
The entire mishna follows R' Tarfon — a jointly owned courtyard can be treated as the injured party's domain for Eating and as public domain for Goring
Rav Zevid
That can't work! The mishna says liability for Eating only when the animal eats something fitting for it. R' Tarfon would require full payment even for unfitting items!
Rav Kahana
Fine — the mishna follows the Rabbis. It's incomplete: the five acts of Goring are initially innocuous but can become forewarned. Eating and Trampling are always forewarned.
Ravina
But the later mishna (24b) asks 'How so?' about Goring in the injured party's courtyard — proving the mishna does discuss Goring there. The mishna lists both the consensus cases and the R' Tarfon/Rabbis dispute.
Key Issue The Gemara must reconcile the mishna's structure with whether liability for Goring in the injured party's courtyard is disputed or universally accepted.

Rav Zevid's Challenge & Rav Kahana's Revision

Rav Zevid
The mishna says Eating liability is only for food fitting for the animal. If R' Tarfon, even unfitting food (= atypical = Goring) should get full payment in the injured party's courtyard!
Rav Kahana
You're right. The mishna follows the Rabbis. It's incomplete (chaserah): the five acts start innocuous but can become forewarned. Eating/Trampling are always forewarned in the injured party's courtyard.
Ravina
But the later mishna (24b) explicitly elaborates on Goring in the injured party's courtyard with 'How so?' — this proves the mishna here must mention that case!
Ravina
My reading: The mishna has three categories — (1) five acts of Goring, (2) Eating/Trampling always forewarned, and (3) the disputed case of Goring in the injured party's courtyard.
Resolution A supporting baraita confirms Ravina's reading, distinguishing consensus cases from the R' Tarfon/Rabbis dispute.

Forewarned Animals: The Mishna's List

Always Forewarned

  • 🐺 Wolf
  • 🦁 Lion
  • 🐻 Bear
  • 🐆 Leopard
  • 🔮 Bardelas (hyena/polecat)
  • 🐍 Snake

R' Elazar's View

  • If domesticated → NOT forewarned
  • Exception: Snake is ALWAYS forewarned
  • Even domesticated snakes remain dangerous
  • Baraita corrected: 'A snake' not 'Also a snake'

Practical Effect

  • Forewarned = full damage payment
  • Innocuous = half damage payment
  • Domestication can change status
  • Snake: no domestication defense
Textual Note The baraita originally said R' Elazar says 'Also the snake' — the Gemara emends this to just 'A snake' to match his position in the mishna.

The Verse from Nahum (2:13)

אַרְיֵה טֹרֵף בְּדֵי גֹרוֹתָיו וּמְחַנֵּק לְלִבְאֹתָיו
Scriptural Challenge "The lion tears apart for its young, and strangles for its lionesses…"

The Gemara asks: Doesn't this prove tearing is typical lion behavior? Answer: Every phrase refers to providing food for others — young cubs, lionesses, lair, den. We cannot derive from this that a lion typically tears prey for its own immediate use.
