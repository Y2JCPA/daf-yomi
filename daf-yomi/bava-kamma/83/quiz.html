Bava Kamma 83 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why was the household of Rabban Gamliel permitted to study Greek wisdom despite the general prohibition?
A. They were kohanim and needed it for Temple service
B. They had close ties with the Roman government
C. They studied it only for Torah commentary purposes
D. Rabban Gamliel issued a special decree for his family
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the household of Rabban Gamliel was different because they were 'kerovim l'malkhut' — close to the government — and needed Greek wisdom for diplomatic purposes.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the mishna, how is lost livelihood (shevet) assessed for an injured skilled worker?
A. Based on his actual professional wages
B. As though he were a cucumber watchman
C. Based on average wages in his city
D. Half his normal wages for the recovery period
Correct: B — Lost livelihood is assessed as a cucumber watchman (low-skill work) because the injured party's professional skill-based earning capacity was already compensated under the 'damage' (nezek) category through the slave-market valuation.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the Gemara's primary proof that 'eye for an eye' means monetary compensation?
A. A tradition from Moses at Sinai
B. Juxtaposition of striking a person with striking an animal — just as animal-striking requires monetary payment, so does person-striking
C. The word 'eye' is a metaphor in Hebrew
D. A verse explicitly states 'pay money for an eye'
Correct: B — The baraita derives it from the Torah's juxtaposition: just as striking an animal (Leviticus 24:18) obligates monetary compensation, so too striking a person (Leviticus 24:19) obligates monetary compensation rather than physical retaliation.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why must pigeon traps in areas with vineyards be placed 100 mil away rather than the standard 30 ris?
A. Vineyards attract more pigeons overall
B. Pigeons rest in vineyards, allowing them to travel much farther
C. Vineyard owners have special property rights over pigeons
D. The 100 mil rule applies to all settled areas, not just vineyards
Correct: B — According to Rav Yosef, since pigeons rest in vineyards along the way, they can traverse much greater distances than usual, requiring a greater distance for traps to avoid catching privately owned pigeons.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does R' Eliezer the Great compare raising dogs to raising pigs?
A. Both are ritually impure animals
B. Both cause agricultural damage
C. The same curse applies to one who raises either
D. Both are prohibited by Torah law explicitly
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that the practical significance of R' Eliezer's comparison is regarding the curse: the same curse that applies to one who raises pigs also applies to one who raises unchained dogs.
