Greek Wisdom vs. Greek Language

Challenge
R' Yehuda HaNasi praised Greek — so how can it be prohibited?
Sages
The Greek language is permitted — only Greek wisdom (chochmat Yevanit) is prohibited. They are two separate matters.
Challenge
But Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel said 500 of his father's household studied Greek wisdom!
Sages
The household of Rabban Gamliel was different — they had close ties with the government (kerovim l'malkhut).
Proof
Similarly, Avtolmos bar Reuven was permitted the Roman komi haircut due to government ties — same principle.
Principle Greek wisdom was banned after the Hasmonean civil war, but exceptions were made for those who needed it for diplomatic relations with Rome.

Laws of Raising Dogs

A person wants to raise a dog
↓
Interior City
Must keep it tied with a chain at all times
Border City
Tie during the day; may release at night
↓
R' Eliezer the Great: Raising unchained dogs is like raising pigs — subject to the same curse
↓
Rav Naḥman: Babylonia (specifically Neharde'a) is treated as a border city
Story A dog barked at a pregnant woman. The owner said: 'Don't worry — its teeth were removed.' She replied: 'Too late! The baby has already moved from its place.' Unchained dogs can cause the Divine Presence to depart from Israel.

Abaye's Distinction Pigeons fly much farther than 50 cubits, but their stomachs become full within 50 cubits — so they won't damage crops beyond that range. The 30 ris rule addresses ownership, not crop damage.

The Five Indemnities for Personal Injury

Value-Based

  • Nezek (Damage) — Difference in slave-market value before and after injury
  • Shevet (Lost Livelihood) — Compensated as a cucumber watchman, not prior profession

Subjective

  • Tza'ar (Pain) — What would a similar person accept to endure this pain?
  • Boshet (Humiliation) — Depends on stature of both parties

Ongoing

  • Ripui (Medical Costs) — Liable until fully healed, including recurring wounds from the blow
  • If wound fully heals, no longer liable for subsequent conditions
Key Detail Lost livelihood is assessed as a cucumber watchman — a low-skill job — because the injured party's professional capacity was already compensated under damage (the slave-market differential).

'Eye for an Eye' — Literal or Monetary?

Torah
"An eye for an eye, a tooth for a tooth, a hand for a hand" (Exodus 21:24)
Possible Reading
Perhaps one who blinds another should literally lose his own eye?
Baraita
The Torah juxtaposes striking a person with striking an animal. Just as striking an animal requires monetary payment — so too for a person.
Second Proof
"You shall not take ransom for the life of a murderer" — only for a murderer is ransom forbidden. For severed limbs, you may take ransom (= monetary compensation).
Foundation This passage is one of the Talmud's most famous derivations: proving that 'eye for an eye' was always understood as monetary compensation, never literal retaliation.
