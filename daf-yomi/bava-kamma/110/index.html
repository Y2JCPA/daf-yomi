The Old or Sick Priest: What Can He Delegate?

Baraita
An old or sick priest gives his offering to any priest he wishes, but the service and hide go to the priestly watch.
Gemara
If he can serve — it should all be his! If he can't — how can he appoint an agent?
Rav Pappa
He can serve with difficulty (בדוחק). For service — difficulty still counts, so he can appoint an agent.
Rav Pappa
For eating — eating with difficulty is excessive eating (אכילה גסה), which is nothing. So he cannot appoint an agent to eat.
⚡ Key Distinction Difficult service is still valid service. Difficult eating is not eating at all — it is achilah gassah.

Three Cases of Delegated Sacrifice

Rav Pappa: Old/Sick Priest

  • Can serve with difficulty
  • Service: can appoint agent
  • Eating: cannot appoint agent
  • Hide → priestly watch

Rav Sheshet: Impure Priest

  • Communal offering — impure priests sacrifice
  • Blemished but pure priests eat
  • Service performed despite impurity
  • Hide → priestly watch

Rav Ashi: High Priest as Onen

  • Acute mourner may sacrifice
  • May NOT eat during mourning
  • Can appoint an agent (chiddush!)
  • Hide → priestly watch
🔗 Common Thread In all three cases, the priest has partial capacity — enough to delegate service, but the eating and hide go to the watch.

Rava's Dilemmas: Dividing Restitution

Rava
Can priests trade shares — one takes more from this robbery, another takes more from the next?
Rava
The Torah calls the stolen item "asham" (guilt). Just as guilt-offering portions cannot be traded...
Rava
...so too restitution for robbery of a convert may not be divided with unequal trades.
Rav Aḥa b. Rava
I teach this explicitly as a ruling, not as a dilemma: priests may not trade shares of convert restitution.
💡 Principle Because the Torah uses the word "asham" for the stolen property, it is treated like a sacrifice — all priests of the watch share equally.
