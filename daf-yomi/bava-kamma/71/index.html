Fourfold/Fivefold Payment When Sin Accompanies Slaughter

Mishna
A thief who stole and slaughtered on Yom Kippur pays the fourfold or fivefold payment
The Sages
But slaughtering on Yom Kippur incurs lashes — one cannot be flogged and pay for the same act!
Resolution
The Mishna follows Rabbi Meir, who holds one can be flogged and obligated to pay
Gemara
If so, why is the thief exempt when he slaughters on Shabbat (a capital offense)? R' Meir should say he pays even then!
Resolution
The baraita speaks of a thief who appoints an agent to slaughter — the thief himself commits no capital sin
Core Issue Can a person receive two punishments (lashes + payment, or death + payment) for a single act? The daf resolves conflicting sources through the mechanism of agency.

Yom Kippur vs. Shabbat: The Punishment Gap

Slaughter on Yom Kippur

  • Prohibition: Melacha on Yom Kippur
  • Penalty: Lashes (no death penalty)
  • R' Meir: Thief pays fourfold/fivefold
  • Principle: Flogged AND pays

Slaughter on Shabbat

  • Prohibition: Melacha on Shabbat
  • Penalty: Death (capital offense)
  • Mishna: Thief is exempt from payment
  • Principle: Greater punishment absorbs lesser
Key Distinction The severity of the sin determines whether the financial penalty still applies — lashes don't cancel payment, but death does.

R' Meir vs. The Rabbis: The Baraita's Three Cases

R' Meir
A thief who slaughters on Shabbat, for idol worship, or an ox sentenced to stoning — he pays fourfold/fivefold in all cases
Rabbis
He is exempt from the fourfold/fivefold payment in all these cases
Gemara
This proves R' Meir holds one can receive death AND payment — contradicting our initial resolution!
R' Yochanan
The baraita speaks of a thief who appointed an agent to do the slaughter — the thief himself didn't violate Shabbat
Resolution By reinterpreting the baraita as involving agency, R' Meir's position becomes consistent: the thief pays because he committed no capital offense.

Can One Sin Make Another Person Liable?

Thief appoints agent to slaughter stolen animal on Shabbat
↓
Objection: Can one person sin and another become liable?
הוא חוטא וזה מתחייב?
↓
Rava
The verse juxtaposes slaughter and sale — just as selling requires another party, so too slaughter can be through an agent
School of R' Yishmael
The word "or" (או) in "slaughter it or sell it" includes agency
School of Chizkiyya
The word "tachat" (תחת — "for") includes agency in slaughter
Three Derivations Three Amoraic traditions independently derive the same principle: a thief is liable for fourfold/fivefold payment even when an agent performs the slaughter.

Rava's Resolution: The Ox Sentenced to Stoning

📋 Owner entrusts ox to a bailee (שומר)
↓
🐂 Ox kills a person while in bailee's custody and is sentenced to stoning
↓
🔓 Thief steals the condemned ox from the bailee's house
↓
🔪 Thief slaughters the ox (now worthless — benefit is prohibited)
↓
💸 Bailee can no longer return intact ox to owner — must pay owner its original value
↓
⚖️ R' Meir: Thief pays 4x/5x — the ox had effective value to the bailee
Key Principle דבר הגורם לממון כממון דמי — An item whose elimination causes financial loss is considered to have monetary value, even if the item itself is worthless.
