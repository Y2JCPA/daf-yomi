Bava Kamma 71 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Mishna rule that a thief who slaughters on Yom Kippur pays the fourfold/fivefold payment?
A. Because Yom Kippur violations carry no punishment at all
B. Because R' Meir holds one can be flogged AND obligated to pay
C. Because the slaughter is considered invalid on Yom Kippur
D. Because the payment is considered a sacrifice, not a penalty
Correct: B — The Mishna follows R' Meir, who maintains that a person can receive both lashes (for Yom Kippur violation) and a monetary obligation (fourfold/fivefold payment) for the same act.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the baraita where R' Meir seems to hold that one pays even when slaughtering on Shabbat (a capital offense)?
A. R' Meir changed his opinion over time
B. The baraita is speaking of a thief who appointed an agent to slaughter
C. Shabbat slaughter is only punished by lashes, not death
D. The baraita is a forgery and should be disregarded
Correct: B — R' Yochanan (and others) explain that the baraita discusses a case where the thief appointed an agent to slaughter on Shabbat. The thief pays because he himself committed no capital offense — only the agent did.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is R' Shimon's basis for exempting the thief from fourfold/fivefold payment when slaughtering for idol worship?
A. Idol worship cancels all monetary obligations
B. Slaughter that doesn't render meat fit for consumption is not considered slaughter
C. The animal never belonged to the thief in the first place
D. Payment for idol worship goes to the Temple treasury instead
Correct: B — R' Shimon holds that שחיטה שאינה ראויה (slaughter that is not fit for its full ritual purpose) is not legally considered an act of slaughter. Since meat slaughtered for idolatry is forbidden for benefit, the slaughter is 'unfit.'
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rav Ashi answer Mar Zutra's objection that an agent shouldn't have 'more power' than the thief himself?
A. He concedes that the law is inconsistent and needs reform
B. He explains that the thief IS liable but is excused by the greater punishment of death; with an agent, that excuse disappears
C. He argues that agents always have more legal power than principals
D. He says Mar Zutra's objection only applies to selling, not slaughtering
Correct: B — Rav Ashi distinguishes between not being liable and being excused from paying. The thief who slaughters on Shabbat is always liable for the payment — the death penalty merely overrides it. When an agent slaughters, the thief faces no death penalty, so the payment obligation takes full effect.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rava, why does R' Meir obligate fourfold/fivefold payment for slaughtering a stolen ox that was sentenced to stoning?
A. Because stoning sentences can be appealed and reversed
B. Because the ox was entrusted to a bailee, and its elimination causes the bailee financial loss — making it effectively valuable
C. Because the ox's meat can still be eaten after stoning
D. Because R' Meir ignores the stoning sentence entirely
Correct: B — Rava constructs a case where the ox was with a bailee. By slaughtering it, the thief prevents the bailee from returning it intact, obligating the bailee to pay the owner. This follows R' Shimon's principle: דבר הגורם לממון כממון דמי — an item whose elimination causes financial loss has monetary value.
