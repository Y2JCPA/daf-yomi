Fire: Arrows or Property?

R' Yochanan
One's liability for fire is due to its similarity to his arrows (isho mishum chitzav) — it is a direct act of damage.
Reish Lakish
One's liability for fire is due to its similarity to his property (isho mishum mamono) — fire is like an ox that escapes.
Abaye
Difficulty with 'arrows' view: If fire = arrows, one should be liable for concealed items too! Arrows damage everything in their path.
Abaye
Resolution: The exemption applies when a fence collapsed (not from fire) and fire then spread — his 'arrows were depleted.'
Gemara
But if arrows are depleted, he should be exempt for exposed items too! There should be no distinction.
Gemara
Therefore: Even R' Yochanan must hold fire is also like property — he could have repaired the breach but didn't.
Resolution Both views converge: the 'arrows' proponent must also accept the 'property' element to explain why concealed items are exempt but exposed items are not.

Concealed Items: How the Exemption Works

🔥 Fire starts in Reuven's courtyard
↓
Fence between courtyards collapses on its own (not from fire)
↓
Fire spreads to Shimon's courtyard and causes damage
↓
Could Reuven have repaired the breach?
Yes — didn't repair
Liable for exposed items (property negligence)
Exempt for concealed items (arrows depleted)
No — couldn't repair
Exempt for both exposed and concealed items
Analogy Not repairing the breach is like having an ox about to escape and not striking it in the face to hold it back — negligence creates liability.

R' Yochanan vs. Reish Lakish: The Practical Difference

R' Yochanan — Fire = Arrows + Property

  • Fire is fundamentally like shooting an arrow
  • Also has a property component (for concealed items)
  • If fire injures a person: liable for all 5 payments
  • Damage + Pain + Medical + Lost Livelihood + Humiliation

Reish Lakish — Fire = Property Only

  • Fire is solely like one's property causing damage
  • Similar to an ox that escapes and gores
  • If fire injures a person: liable for damage only
  • No additional 4 indemnities (pain, medical, etc.)
Key Nafka Mina The practical difference: When fire injures a person, R' Yochanan requires the four additional indemnity payments because fire = arrows = direct bodily harm.

The Dog, the Cake & the Coal

🐕 A dog burrows under a fence into another's property
↓
🍞 It grabs a cake (charrara) with a burning coal attached
↓
🏠 Dog brings coal + cake to a grain stack
↓
🍞 Dog owner pays full damage for the cake (Eating — Shen)
↓
🔥 Dog owner pays half damage for the burned grain (unusual Fire)
↓
🚪 Rav Mari b. Rav Kahana: This proves ordinary doors are vulnerable to dog burrowing
Why Half? The dog carrying fire to a grain stack is unusual behavior (meshuneh), so the owner pays only half-damage for the fire — but full for the cake, which is normal dog behavior.

Is the Mouth of an Animal the Owner's Domain?

Gemara
Dilemma: Is the mouth of a cow like the courtyard of the injured party, or the courtyard of the one responsible for damage?
Gemara
If it's the owner's domain, how can Shen (Eating) ever apply? The Torah requires damage to occur on the injured party's property!
Rav Mari b. Rav Kahana
Eating liability could still apply: e.g., a cow rubbing against a wall for pleasure, or soiling produce — nothing enters its mouth.
Mar Zutra
Objection! Eating requires total destruction — 'as consumes the tooth until it be all gone' (I Kings 14:10). Rubbing a wall doesn't destroy it!
Ravina
It can — if the cow completely erases decorative images on the wall.
Rav Ashi
And soiling can destroy — if the cow treads on produce and crushes it completely.
Unresolved The dilemma about the animal's mouth remains open — each attempted proof is deflected.
