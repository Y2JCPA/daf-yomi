Bava Kamma 92: Forgiveness, Consent & Folk Wisdom

Core Theme This daf teaches that monetary payment alone does not achieve atonement — the wrongdoer must seek forgiveness from the victim. It then explores consent to bodily injury vs. property damage, and concludes with a remarkable series of folk proverbs traced to biblical verses by Rava and Rabba bar Mari.

When May One Cut Down a Fruit Tree?

A fruit tree stands in the way of construction or agriculture
↓
Barren tree is cheaper
Cut down the barren tree first
Fruit tree worth more as lumber
Permitted to cut the fruit tree
↓
Shmuel: Date palms weakening grapevines → cut them down
Rav Ḥisda: Grapevines more valuable than date palms → uproot palms
Principle The verse says "Only" — economic value can override the prohibition of cutting fruit trees

The Obligation to Seek Forgiveness

בראשית כ:ז
Mishna Even if the assailant pays all required damages, his transgression is not forgiven in the heavenly court until he requests forgiveness from the victim.

Source: "Restore the wife of the man; for he is a prophet, and he shall pray for you" (Genesis 20:7)

And if the victim refuses to forgive, he is considered cruel — as Abraham prayed for Abimelech (Genesis 20:17).

Consent to Damage: Body vs. Property

Bodily Injury

  • "Blind my eye / Cut my hand / Break my leg"
  • Even with instruction → LIABLE
  • Even with explicit exemption condition → LIABLE
  • A person cannot waive rights to bodily integrity

Property Damage

  • "Tear my garment / Break my jug"
  • Without exemption condition → LIABLE
  • With explicit exemption condition → EXEMPT
  • Owner can waive monetary rights to property
Key Distinction One cannot consent to bodily harm in a way that exempts the assailant, but one CAN consent to property damage with an explicit exemption clause

Abimelech's Defense — Was He Innocent?

Abimelech
"Will You slay a righteous nation? Abraham himself told me: She is my sister!"
R' Shmuel bar Naḥmani / R' Yonatan
"Restore the wife" — she must be returned regardless, she is his wife
R' Shmuel bar Naḥmani / R' Yonatan
Abraham is a prophet — he read your behavior. Does one ask a guest about his wife? You intended to take her!
Gemara
From here: a gentile is executed even for unknowing transgression — he should have learned the law
Lesson Ignorance of moral law is not an excuse — one bears responsibility to learn proper conduct
