Brotherhood, Humiliation & Property Rights

R' Yehuda
A Canaanite slave has no humiliation indemnity — the Torah says 'his brother,' excluding slaves
Sages
A slave is a brother regarding mitzvot — he is obligated in most commandments
Gemara
If slaves aren't 'brothers,' conspiring witnesses against a slave shouldn't be executed!
Rav Sheshet
'Put away the evil from your midst' — conspiring witnesses are punished regardless
R' Yochanan
Ownership of usufruct = ownership of the item itself — purchaser loses
Reish Lakish
Usufruct is not ownership — the son validly sold the property
Key Theme This daf explores legal personhood: who counts as a 'brother' under Torah law, and whether holding usage rights equals true ownership.

Injuring a Canaanite Slave: Five Indemnities

R' Yehuda

  • Slave receives only 4 types of indemnity
  • No boshet (humiliation) payment
  • 'His brother' (Deut. 25:11) excludes slaves
  • Slaves lack 'brotherhood' with Jews

Sages (Chakhamim)

  • Slave receives all 5 types of indemnity
  • Boshet applies to slaves too
  • Slaves are 'brothers' regarding mitzvot
  • Obligated in most commandments = brotherhood
Five Indemnities Nezek (damage), Tza'ar (pain), Ripui (medical costs), Shevet (lost wages), Boshet (humiliation)

Why Can't a Slave Bear Witness?

Ulla
Derive from a woman: she may marry a Jew but can't testify — kal va'chomer, a slave who can't marry a Jew surely can't testify!
Gemara
A woman can't undergo circumcision — that's why she's different! A slave can be circumcised.
Ulla
A minor proves circumcision is irrelevant — minors are circumcised yet can't testify!
Gemara
A minor isn't obligated in mitzvot — but a slave is!
Ulla
A woman proves again — she's obligated in mitzvot yet can't testify! The derivation revolves back.
Gemara
Common denominator rejected: women and minors aren't men — but a slave is a man!
Talmudic Method This passage demonstrates mah ha-tzad (common denominator) reasoning and its refutation through pirka (objection).
