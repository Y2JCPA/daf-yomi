QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why are BOTH exclusions needed — Canaanite slaves AND gentiles — for witness disqualification?
A. Each has a quality the other lacks (lineage vs. mitzvot connection)
B. They are excluded for the same reason but from different verses
C. Slaves are excluded by rabbinic law while gentiles by Torah law
D. One exclusion is for civil cases and the other for criminal cases
Correct: A — A slave has mitzvot connection (like a woman) but no lineage; a gentile has lineage but no covenant connection. Without both teachings, we might qualify one based on the quality the other lacks.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, why does the Torah require only HALF-damages for an innocuous ox rather than full damages?
A. Because the ox is presumed safe and shouldn't cause damage at all
B. Because the Torah had pity — the owner wasn't yet aware his ox might gore
C. Because half-damages is a penalty to encourage better guarding
D. Because the injured party shares blame for not protecting his property
Correct: B — Rav Pappa holds ordinary oxen are NOT presumed safe, so full damages should theoretically apply. The Torah reduced liability to half because the owner hadn't yet been forewarned about his ox's behavior.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the practical difference between chatzi nezek being mamona vs. kenas?
A. Mamona is collected from the body of the ox; kenas from the owner's property
B. Kenas requires two witnesses; mamona requires only one
C. Mamona is payable on self-admission; kenas is not
D. Mamona applies only in Eretz Yisrael; kenas applies everywhere
Correct: C — The key principle is 'modeh b'kenas patur' — one who admits to liability for a fine is exempt from paying. If chatzi nezek is monetary restitution (mamona), it's payable even on self-admission; if it's a fine (kenas), it is not.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the apparent 'conclusive refutation' of the kenas position?
A. It shows the mishna in Ketubot is not authoritative
B. The mishna couldn't make a blanket rule about 'less than damage' because tzerorot (pebble damage) is half-damages that IS mamona
C. Rav Huna b"R Yehoshua retracted his position
D. The refutation only applies in Eretz Yisrael, not Babylonia
Correct: B — Tzerorot (pebbles propelled by a walking animal) carries half-damage liability that is known by tradition to be mamona. So the mishna couldn't say 'anyone paying less than damage doesn't pay on admission' — tzerorot would be the exception.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara need THREE separate sources to establish that women are equal to men in Torah law?
A. Each source was given by a different rabbi and all three must be honored
B. Each covers a domain where a unique logical argument might have excluded women
C. One is from the Torah, one from the Prophets, and one from the Writings
D. Women's equality was disputed and required overwhelming proof
Correct: B — Each source covers a scenario where women might have been excluded: punishments (women have fewer mitzvot), civil law (women less involved in business), and killings (might only apply to men who have full obligations). Each source closes a distinct logical gap.
