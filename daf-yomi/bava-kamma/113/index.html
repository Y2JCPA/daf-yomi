Must Heirs Return a Stolen Bed or Table?

Mishna
If a robber left stolen property to his children — if the item can serve as loan guarantee (land), heirs must return it.
R' Yehuda HaNasi
Not only land — even a cow he plows with or donkey he drives, because of the father's honor. People will point out the stolen inheritance.
Rav Kahana
What about a bed or table? These are used privately, not publicly visible like animals.
Rav
"Give to a wise man and he will be yet wiser" (Proverbs 9:9) — if cow and donkey must be returned, all the more so bed and table!
LOGIC Rav implies the obligation is not limited to conspicuous items. If heirs must return publicly visible stolen property for their father's honor, private items must also be returned — the stolen nature of the item, not its visibility, is what matters.

Robbery vs. Lost Property of Gentiles

Robbery — PROHIBITED

  • R' Shimon Ḥasida: It is prohibited to rob a gentile
  • Source (Rav Huna): "You shall consume all the peoples... that God shall deliver unto you" (Deut. 7:16)
  • Only permitted in wartime — "when delivered into your hand"
  • Applies to both gentile and ger toshav

Lost Items — NO OBLIGATION

  • R' Shimon Ḥasida: Permitted to retain a gentile's lost item
  • Source (Rav Ḥama bar Gurya): "Every lost thing of your brother's" (Deut. 22:3)
  • "Brother" = fellow Jew; excludes gentile
  • Ravina: Even if already in your hand — still no obligation to return
CRITICAL EXCEPTION R' Pineḥas ben Ya'ir: Where retaining the lost item would cause chillul Hashem (desecration of God's name), it is prohibited to keep it — one must return it.
