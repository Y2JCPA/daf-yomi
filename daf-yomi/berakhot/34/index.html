🏛️ Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel: Gentile Bodily Emissions

Beit Shammai
Blood of a gentile niddah or zava is ritually pure. The Torah's laws of menstrual impurity were stated only for Jewish women: 'Speak to the children of Israel.'
Beit Hillel
Blood of a gentile woman imparts impurity when moist only, not when dry. This acts as a conspicuous marker (היכר) distinguishing it from Jewish blood which is always impure.
Gemara (objection)
If Beit Shammai deem her blood pure, why do they agree that her saliva and urine impart impurity when moist? They should be fully pure too!
Beit Shammai (reply)
The saliva/urine of a gentile zava are impure when moist (by rabbinic law). Her blood being fully pure serves as the conspicuous marker — everyone will know the moist-only rule is only rabbinic.
📌 Core Principle Both schools require a 'conspicuous marker' (היכר) to distinguish gentile from Jewish impurity. They disagree only on which substance serves as the marker.

🧪 Rava: Gentile Man's Ziva vs. Semen

What is the status of a gentile man's bodily emissions?
↓
Ziva (discharge)
Impure — even per Beit Shammai
His semen is pure, so ziva can be marked as distinct. No conspicuous marker problem.
Semen (זרע)
Pure — even per Beit Hillel
Semen's purity signals to all that his ziva is only rabbinically impure. Serves as the required marker.
↓
Proof from Mishna Mikvaot 8:4: Semen of a Jew found in a gentile woman is impure (follows its Jewish origin). Semen of a gentile is pure everywhere — supports Rava.

❓ Rav Pappa's Dilemma — Semen of a Jew in a Gentile Womb After 3 Days

Rav Pappa
A Jewish man had relations with a gentile woman. After 3 days, his semen remains in her womb. Is it still impure (as Jewish semen), or has it become pure?
Possibility A
Jewish women are careful about mitzvot — their bodies are warm, so semen spoils and becomes foul within 3 days. Gentile women are not careful, their bodies are cooler, semen remains viable. Therefore: still impure after 3 days.
Possibility B
We follow the current location, not the origin of the semen. Once it is in a gentile womb beyond 3 days, treat it as pure — like all gentile semen.
Gemara
The baraita only addresses semen within 3 days — it cannot resolve the dilemma. Question remains open (תיקו).
⚖️ Unresolved Rav Pappa's dilemma about semen after 3 days in a gentile womb is left unresolved by the Gemara.

👶 Does a Minor's First Ziva Sighting Impart Impurity?

The Dilemma (Reish Lakish / Rav Yosef)

  • An adult zav: first sighting always imparts impurity
  • A minor's semen does NOT impart impurity
  • Are ziva-impurity and semen-impurity linked?
  • Possibility: No semen-impurity → no first-sighting ziva impurity either
  • Counter: They may be fully independent tracks of law

Rava's Resolution (from Baraita)

  • Verse: 'This is the law of the zav — whether adult or minor'
  • Torah explicitly equates adult and minor for all ziva laws
  • Just as adult's first sighting is impure...
  • ...so too a minor's first sighting is impure
  • Semen-impurity and ziva-impurity are independent categories

🪲 Beit Hillel's Derivation: Female Leper's Blood Imparts Impurity

Verse (Lev. 15:33): 'whether it be a male or female' — this phrase comes to teach us something about a female leper's emissions.
↓
If the verse only teaches her saliva/urine are impure — we'd already know that by analogy to the male leper. The verse would be superfluous.
↓
Beit Hillel conclude: The verse must teach that a female leper's blood also imparts impurity — the one thing we cannot derive from the male leper.
↓
Beit Shammai object: We cannot derive male leper's laws from female leper — she is more stringent (she also has niddah/zava blood). The a fortiori inference is flawed.
↓
Beit Hillel respond: We compare male leper to female leper within leper laws, not to zava! That comparison is valid and the inference stands.
↓
Beit Shammai alternative: The phrase 'whether it be a male' serves to include even a minor male leper in the laws of ziva — not to teach about her blood.
