Berakhot 23 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Elazar (as taught by Rav Aḥa son of Rava), a gezerah shavah that is 'not free at all' — meaning neither verse needs its shared word — has what status?
A. It is completely invalid and cannot be used
B. It can be used to derive halakhot but can also be refuted
C. It is as strong as a gezerah shavah free on both sides
D. It can only be used when both sides agree on the derivation
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar's lenient position is that even an analogy not free at all can derive halakhot — but unlike one free on both sides, it can be challenged by a logical refutation (teshuvah).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rabbi Yannai explains Rabbi Meir's position by saying animal eyes face forward like a human's. Which of the following is therefore impure according to Rabbi Yannai's logic?
A. All birds equally, since all have two eyes
B. Snakes, because their pupils are round like a human's
C. Owls, because their eyes face forward like those of a human
D. No animals at all — only humans can have forward-facing eyes
Correct: C — Rabbi Yannai's criterion is forward-facing eyes. Owls (little owl and great owl) meet this criterion and are therefore included in Rabbi Meir's ruling of impurity, unlike other birds whose eyes are on the sides.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rabbi Yoḥanan requires which set of features for a fetus to be considered to have a 'human face' that renders the mother impure?
A. Eyes and nose only, since these are most distinctively human
B. Any single human facial feature in the correct position
C. Forehead, eyebrows, eyes, cheeks, and chin — all five required
D. Half the face, including at least one eye and the chin
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan's stringent standard requires the complete set of human facial features: forehead, eyebrows, eyes, cheeks, and chin. Ḥasa takes a more lenient position, requiring only half a face.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rava rules on a fetus with a punctured esophagus (tereifa) vs. a sealed esophagus. Which statement correctly describes his rulings?
A. Both render the mother impure, since both have a face
B. Punctured esophagus → impure; sealed esophagus → not impure
C. Sealed esophagus → impure; punctured esophagus → not impure
D. Neither renders the mother impure, as both are non-viable
Correct: B — A punctured esophagus means the fetus had the structure of a living being (even if a tereifa) — mother is impure. A sealed esophagus means it was never formed as a viable creature — mother is not impure.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The unresolved question (left as a dilemma) that Rav Adda bar Ahava asks Abaye concerns which scenario?
A. Whether a woman who discharges an owl-shaped fetus is impure according to the Rabbis
B. According to Rabbi Meir, whether a human-form fetus found in an animal's womb renders the animal forbidden
C. How much of the lower body must be removed to cause death
D. Whether Rabbi Meir requires both eyes to face forward or just one
Correct: B — Rav Adda's question is: Rabbi Meir holds an animal-form fetus in a woman is a full-fledged offspring — so what about a human-form fetus in an animal's womb? The Gemara suggests Rabbi Yoḥanan's ruling about a pigeon fetus may resolve it, but this is rejected, leaving the question open.
