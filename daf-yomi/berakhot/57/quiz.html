Berakhot 57 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Yehuda bar Ami, when is a beit haperas considered pure?
A. When a Samaritan walks through it and testifies to its purity
B. When it has been trodden underfoot by many people
C. When a kohen examines it and finds no bones
D. When rain has washed over the field three consecutive times
Correct: B — Rav Yehuda bar Ami rules that a beit haperas trodden by many people is pure, since any bone fragments of a barley-grain's size or larger would have been broken into smaller pieces or swept to the sides.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the mishna accept a Samaritan priest's testimony that a burial area is pure?
A. Samaritans are always trusted in matters involving priestly purity
B. He is holding teruma in his hand, showing he actively avoids impurity
C. He swore an oath before a Jewish rabbinic court
D. A second Jewish witness corroborated his testimony
Correct: B — The mishna refers specifically to a Samaritan priest holding teruma. Since he would not risk rendering the teruma impure, his willingness to stand there proves the area is pure — this is behavioral evidence, not mere testimony.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
A blood stain found on which part of a woman's body renders her impure according to the mishna (57b)?
A. The outer side of her foot
B. Her robe from the belt and above
C. Her robe from the belt and below
D. A robe worn only during the day, regardless of location
Correct: C — The mishna rules that a stain on the robe from the belt and below renders her impure, since uterine blood could reach that area. From the belt and above is pure. A robe used as a nighttime covering is impure wherever the stain appears.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Shmuel's novel ruling about a blood stain found on a woman?
A. A stain smaller than a gris is always pure regardless of location
B. If she examined the ground beneath her first and found nothing, she is pure
C. A stain found at night is treated more leniently than one found by day
D. Stains on white garments always render her impure regardless of location
Correct: B — Shmuel rules that if a woman examined the ground beneath her, found it clean, and subsequently found a stain on herself, she is pure. The clean ground examination indicates the blood likely came from an external source, not the uterus.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yoḥanan reconcile the baraita (allowing Samaritan testimony about a field) with the mishna (which rejects it for beit haperas)?
A. The baraita refers to a Samaritan who converted to Judaism
B. The baraita involves a case where two Samaritans corroborate each other
C. The Samaritan in the baraita walked to and fro over the entire area, so he would have become impure had a grave been there
D. The baraita only applies to fields outside of Israel where beit haperas laws are less stringent
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan explains that the baraita refers to a Samaritan who traversed the entire area repeatedly. Since he would certainly have encountered any grave and become impure, his continued entry is behavioral proof of the field's purity — he is not merely testifying about uncertainty.
