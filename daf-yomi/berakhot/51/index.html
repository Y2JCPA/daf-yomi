🐦 Bird Carcass in the Throat: Does Food Impurity Require Intent?

Abaye
The bird carcass in the throat is a severe impurity — and yet it still doesn't render another person impure. If even severe impurity requires no intent to become food-impure, all the more so regular food impurity should require no intent!
Rava
Wrong direction! The bird carcass is actually lenient in one respect — it doesn't transfer impurity onward. We cannot derive leniencies for regular food from something that is lenient in a different way.
Rav Sheshet
The Rabbis' real point: bird carcass needs no liquid for susceptibility because it already transmits severe impurity. But regular food does need liquid (הכשר) first — and only then does intent matter.
Rav Pappa
Actually the Rabbis' rejection of Rabbi Yoḥanan ben Nuri was not about bird carcass at all — they were referring to a different halakha entirely as their counter-example.
Key Distinction נבלת עוף טהור (bird carcass): inherently impure, no liquid contact needed for susceptibility. Regular food (אוכלין): requires הכשר — contact with one of the seven liquids — before intent to use it as food is halakhically relevant.

💧 Why Doesn't Bird Carcass Require Liquid for Susceptibility?

Baraita — Three Rulings on נבלת עוף טהור:
1. Defiles garments while in the throat (unique severe impurity)
2. Does not require slaughter to be permitted for eating
3. Does not require prior liquid contact to be susceptible to food impurity
↓
Question: Why is no liquid contact required? Doesn't the verse (Leviticus 11:38) require seeds to first touch water?
↓
School of Rabbi Yishmael's Rule
Seeds need liquid first because seeds will never transmit impurity severe enough to defile a person — only items that cannot cause severe human-level impurity need liquid as a prerequisite
Bird Carcass Exception
Bird carcass can defile a person at the severe level (garments in throat) — therefore it is exempt from the liquid prerequisite and is susceptible to food impurity by default
↓
Rule: Only items that will never cause severe (human-level) impurity require prior liquid contact to become susceptible to impurity as food

🌾 Intent for Tithes While Produce Is in the Ground

Rava's Position

  • Intent expressed while produce is still attached to the ground counts halakhically for tithing
  • Proof: Mishna (Ma'asrot) — hyssop planted for human use is tithe-obligated even if later eaten by animals
  • Intent at planting time = effective halakhic intent

Rav Ashi's Challenge

  • That hyssop grew on its own in a courtyard — unspecified plants default to human use
  • No actual proof that intent at planting overrides later circumstances
  • Mishna (50a) means 'types of food obligated in tithes' — not all situations involving such food

Rav Shimi bar Ashi's Proof

  • Case: One declares vineyard hefker (ownerless), then harvests the next day
  • Pe'a applies (per Pe'a 3:2, Rabbi Akiva) — yet tithes do NOT apply (hefker = tithe-exempt)
  • Proves the mishna speaks of categories/types only — pe'a does not always drag along tithes

🐟 Fish Signs: Why Torah Writes Both Fins and Scales

Mishna Rule: Any fish with קשקשת (scales) also has סנפיר (fins). Therefore scales alone are sufficient to identify a kosher fish. Fish with fins but no scales are non-kosher.
↓
Gemara Question: Since every scaled fish has fins, why does the Torah (Leviticus 11:9) write both signs — fins AND scales?
↓
Answer 1: If the Torah only wrote one word, I might think kaskeset means fins (not scales) — meaning even a finless fish could be kosher. Writing both disambiguates.
↓
How do we know kaskeset = scales, not fins? From I Samuel 17:5 — Goliath wore a coat of kaskeset (overlapping metal plates = armor). Kaskeset = overlapping coverings = scales.
↓
Question: Now that we know kaskeset = scales, why still write fins?
↓
Answer 2 (Rabbi Abbahu / School of R. Yishmael): If you find a fish in the sea with scales, you may eat it even if you cannot see its fins. Scales alone in the field suffice — fins need not be verified.

Does severe impurity require intent? Does it need liquid susceptibility (הכשר)?

No liquid needed — transmits severe impurity → exempt from הכשר requirement
