Berakhot 11 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, how does a woman establish a veset kavua through jumping?
A. One instance of jumping followed by blood is sufficient
B. Two instances establish the cycle, a third confirms it
C. Three instances of jumping followed by blood establish the fixed cycle
D. She must jump on the same day of the week three times
Correct: C — Rav Huna rules that three consistent occurrences of jumping followed by blood establish a veset kavua triggered by jumping. The day of the week is incidental — the jump itself is the operative cause.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the practical halakhic difference between Rav's and Levi's positions on post-birth blood?
A. Rav says all post-birth blood is impure; Levi says all is pure
B. Rav's one-source view easily explains why no examination is needed in the purity period; Levi's two-source view creates difficulty requiring extra reasoning
C. Rav requires two daily examinations; Levi requires only one
D. Rav follows Beit Hillel on this issue; Levi follows Beit Shammai
Correct: B — Rav's one-source theory explains the mishna's exemption naturally: the Torah defined the status of each phase. Levi's two-source theory raises the question of why no examination is needed at the end of the impure phase — lest that source has not ceased.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Yehuda citing Shmuel, to what activity does the twice-daily examination requirement apply?
A. Engaging in intercourse with her husband
B. Handling ritually pure items (taharot)
C. Both intercourse and handling pure items equally
D. Entering the Temple courtyard
Correct: B — Rav Yehuda citing Shmuel qualifies the mishna: the twice-daily exams pertain to handling ritually pure items. For her husband, a woman retains presumptive purity — no examination is required before intercourse.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Beit Hillel, when does a virgin's hymen blood lose its exemption from impurity?
A. Immediately after the first act of intercourse
B. After three acts of intercourse
C. If she stopped seeing blood during intercourse and then sees blood again, or if a night passed without intercourse and she sees blood
D. Only after a physician certifies the wound is healed
Correct: C — Beit Hillel attributes blood to the torn hymen until it heals. Two signs indicate healing: (1) she stopped bleeding during intercourse but later saw blood again, or (2) an entire night passed without intercourse and she subsequently saw blood.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The mishna exempts certain women from using examination cloths during intercourse. Which of the following is NOT one of those exemptions?
A. A menstruating woman (niddah)
B. A woman during the blood-of-purity period after birth
C. A virgin whose hymen wound is still active (per Beit Hillel)
D. A woman who has never established a fixed menstrual cycle
Correct: D — The three mishna-stated exemptions from examination cloths during intercourse are: a menstruating woman (forbidden anyway), a woman in her purity period, and a virgin (per Beit Hillel). A woman without a fixed cycle is not listed as exempt — she retains presumptive purity for her husband but must examine before handling pure items.
