Berakhot 26 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Sages in the Mishna, why does an afterbirth (שליה) found in a house cause ritual impurity?
A. The afterbirth itself has the halakhic status of a corpse
B. There is no afterbirth without an offspring — implying an offspring once existed there
C. The afterbirth contains blood that defiles like a niddah
D. Rabbi Shimon issued a special decree extending tent-impurity to afterbirths
Correct: B — The Sages reason that every afterbirth implies a prior offspring. Even if the offspring dissolved, its prior existence in the womb is sufficient to cause the house to be a tent over what was effectively a corpse-like entity.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rav Pappa explains that the sandal fetus and its twin do not lie side by side, but rather the full-fledged offspring's head presses into the middle of the sandal. What is the halakhic consequence for the law of firstborns according to Rav Pappa?
A. The full-fledged offspring exits first and is the firstborn for inheritance
B. The sandal exits first, so the twin born after it inherits as a firstborn
C. Neither qualifies as a firstborn because the birth is abnormal
D. The sandal itself is counted as the firstborn for pidyon purposes
Correct: B — According to Rav Pappa, the sandal (pressing from above) exits the womb first, making the twin who follows it the firstborn for inheritance — though not for the obligation of pidyon haben.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rav (as transmitted by Rav Yosef, son of Rav Menashya of D'vil) states a condition for afterbirth attribution. What is it?
A. The afterbirth must be discharged within seven days of the birth
B. The afterbirth must measure at least one handbreadth
C. Afterbirth is attributed only to a viable offspring, not to a non-viable discharge
D. The woman must have been examined by a midwife within 24 hours
Correct: C — Rav explicitly says that afterbirth is attributed only to a viable item. A non-viable newborn or ambiguous discharge cannot draw afterbirth attribution, even if the afterbirth is discharged within the three-day window.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The Gemara challenges Rabbi Oshaya's list of five items with a one-handbreadth minimum by raising the case of a cross beam (קורה) over an alleyway entrance. How does the Gemara resolve this challenge?
A. The cross beam is added as a sixth item to Rabbi Oshaya's list
B. The cross beam requires two handbreadths, not one, so it is excluded
C. The cross beam halakha is subject to a rabbinic dispute, so Rabbi Oshaya excluded disputed cases
D. Rabbi Oshaya's list only covers items related to Festival observance
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that Rabbi Oshaya only catalogued undisputed halakhot. Since the cross beam's one-handbreadth minimum is disputed among the Sages, Rabbi Oshaya did not include it — just as he excluded the protruding stone (also disputed).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rabbi Yosei ben Shaul asked Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi about a woman who discharged something in the form of a crow with an afterbirth tied to it. What did Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi respond?
A. She is impure as after a normal childbirth and must bring a korban
B. She is impure with niddah-impurity only, not post-birth impurity
C. The afterbirth is not attributed to the crow-form discharge
D. "You asked about a matter that does not exist" — the scenario is impossible
Correct: D — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi dismissed the second question as anatomically impossible — a crow-form discharge cannot be physically tied to an afterbirth. Rabbi Yosei ben Shaul disagreed by gesture, insisting the case is real, leaving the question unresolved.
