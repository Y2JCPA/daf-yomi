QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav (as explained by Rav Adda bar Ahava), which days of bleeding can be attributed to labor pains, exempting a woman from becoming a zava?
A. Only bleeding during the eleven days of ziva
B. Bleeding during both the seven menstrual days and the eleven ziva days
C. Only bleeding during the final week before the due date
D. Only bleeding if she experienced no other emissions that month
Correct: B — Rav holds that labor-pain blood during both the seven menstruation days and the eleven ziva days is attributed to labor pains — unlike Levi who requires the blood to specifically fall during the ziva days.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did the early generations of pious men (chasidim rishonim) engage in marital relations specifically on Wednesday nights?
A. Wednesday is considered an auspicious day in Kabbalistic tradition
B. To ensure their wives would not give birth on Shabbat, which would require its desecration
C. Because Wednesday follows the two days of niddah-separation after menstruation
D. To align with the Temple priestly rotation schedule
Correct: B — The concern was preventing Shabbat desecration: since full-term birth takes at least 271 days, conception on Wednesday ensures birth falls on Thursday or later — never Shabbat. Sunday, Monday, or Tuesday conceptions risk Shabbat births.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the דַּיּוֹ (dayyo) principle as invoked by Rabbi Eliezer in this daf?
A. A doubt about impurity in the public domain is ruled pure
B. The conclusion of an a fortiori inference cannot be more stringent than its source
C. A woman's status during the eleven days of ziva is presumptively pure
D. A greater zava must count seven clean days before immersing in a mikveh
Correct: B — Dayyo limits kal vachomer inferences: even when logic pushes toward a stricter ruling, the conclusion is 'sufficient' to merely equal — not exceed — the stringency of its source. Rabbi Eliezer uses this to block extending ziva-impurity to labor-pain blood.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In Rabbi Meir's 150-day calculation, what prevents the woman from ever becoming a greater zava despite prolonged bleeding?
A. She immerses in a mikveh every three days, resetting her status
B. Cycling through labor-pain blood, menstrual days, and post-birth purity (lochia) prevents three consecutive ziva days
C. Rabbi Meir holds that all blood during pregnancy is automatically pure
D. She maintains a presumption of purity that overrides the ziva count
Correct: B — Rabbi Meir's scenario cycles: labor-pain blood (exempt from ziva) → menstrual days (niddah, not ziva) → post-birth lochia (40 or 80 days of purity) → back to menstruation — never accumulating three consecutive days of ziva bleeding.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the mishna at the end of Daf 38b, what is the presumptive status of a woman during the eleven days of ziva?
A. She is presumed to have immersed in a mikveh during this period
B. She has a presumptive status of ritual purity, as bleeding during these days is unusual
C. She may eat terumah and enter the Temple without additional examination
D. Her husband is exempt from niddah-separation laws during this window
Correct: B — The eleven days of ziva are an atypical time for bleeding, so the Rabbis established a presumptive status (chazakah) of purity. Practically, this means she is treated as pure unless she actually experiences and reports bleeding during these days.
