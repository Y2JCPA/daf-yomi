🩸 Five Impure Blood Colors — Niddah 20

שחור — Black

  • Dark as ḥeret (ink)
  • Like garments of Siva'a inhabitants
  • Like bath attendants' cloths from overseas
  • Examined on white linen cloth

אדום — Red

  • Standard impure red
  • Baseline reference color
  • Benchmark for all shade comparisons

כרתום — Saffron

  • Middle leaf of middle crocus layer
  • 3 layers × 3 leaves per layer
  • Flower must still be in its clump
  • Detached flowers change color

מי אדמה — Earth Water

  • Water inundating red earth
  • Fertile soil from Beit Kerem Valley
  • Water one thumb's thickness above dirt
  • Mixed with vessel, not by hand

יין מזוג — Diluted Wine

  • 2 parts water : 1 part wine
  • Specifically Sharon region wine
  • Deeper shade disputed by Bar Kappara
  • Lighter shades generally pure

🔍 Deeper or Lighter Than the Standard — What Is the Ruling?

Ulla
For all five colors: if the shade is deeper than described → impure; if lighter → pure. The mishna singles out black only to counter assuming that black faded to lighter = automatically pure.
Rabbi Ami bar Abba
Agrees on deeper = impure, but holds: if lighter than the standard → also impure. A shade still within the general family of that color remains halakhically problematic.
Rami bar Abba
Reversal: if deeper → pure; if lighter → impure. The standard is an upper boundary — a color that exceeds it has changed character entirely and is no longer the same type.
Bar Kappara
General rule: deeper → impure, lighter → pure. Exception — diluted wine: if deeper, it becomes like undiluted wine, which is itself an impure category (red), so deeper diluted-wine blood is also impure.
Key Dispute The five standard colors are benchmarks, not boundaries. Whether shades beyond them are impure or pure is a fundamental four-way disagreement among Tannaim and Amoraim. Ulla's view (deeper=impure, lighter=pure) is the most widely cited baseline.

🌸 How to Identify Saffron-Colored Blood (כרתום) — Abaye's Method

Step 1: Obtain a crocus flower (karkom) still attached to its clump of earth — do not detach it, as detached flowers change color
↓
Step 2: Identify the three layers of brightly colored stigmas/styles, stacked one above the other
↓
Step 3: Within each layer, locate the three individual leaves (styles or stigmas)
↓
Step 4: Grasp the middle leaf of the middle layer — this is the precise halakhic standard color
↓
Step 5: Compare the blood sample to this leaf. If the colors match → טמא (impure)
↓
Result: Abaye reconciles four contradictory baraitot (upper/lower/middle) — they each reference different layers of the same flower, and only the middle leaf of the middle layer is authoritative

🌊 Testing Blood Like Water Over Red Earth

Bring fertile earth from the Beit Kerem Valley
↓
Place earth in a vessel (not in your bare hand)
↓
Pour water until it rises one thumb's thickness above the surface of the earth
↓
Do NOT muddy the mixture by hand — stir only with a vessel. Examine the resulting color.
↓
Blood matches this water color
טמא — Impure
Blood does not match
טהור — Pure

⚖️ Yalta and the Second Opinion — Can One Authority Reverse Another?

נידה כ׳ עמוד ב — דם יַלְתָּא / הלכה שניה ועדות אישה
Case Study: Yalta bat Rav Naḥman Facts: Yalta brought blood to Rabba bar bar Ḥana → ruled impure. She brought the same blood to Rav Yitzḥak son of Rav Yehuda → he initially also ruled impure. Turn: Yalta said: 'Every day I bring blood this color to Rabba bar bar Ḥana and he deems me pure — only today did he rule against me.' Rav Yitzḥak then ruled pure. Gemara's Resolution: Rav Yitzḥak relied on his own independent scholarly judgment, not merely on Yalta's testimony. Her claim only overcame his reluctance to contradict Rabba bar bar Ḥana — the ruling itself was his own. Baraita Principle: A woman is believed when she says 'My blood looked like this color but I lost it.' She may similarly report prior rulings — but the second authority must still rule independently.
