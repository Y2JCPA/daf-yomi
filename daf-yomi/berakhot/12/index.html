🔍 When Must a Woman Examine Herself?

A woman and her husband wish to have intercourse
↓
Veset Kavua — Fixed Cycle
Examination required only when she handles tahoros (ritually pure items). Otherwise, husband's return itself may serve as implicit examination.
Ein Lah Veset — No Fixed Cycle
Forbidden to have intercourse without prior examination. She loses marital rights if she does not comply.
↓
Post-intercourse timing: The halakhic minimum period (שיעור) = time for an external wipe only — a full internal examination cannot be completed within this window (Rav Huna from baraita)
↓
Rav Yehuda: She should NOT examine herself before intercourse — examining may generate prohibition rather than prevent it
3 / 9

🗣️ Slide 1: Must She Examine Before Intercourse?

Rabbi Zeira
What is the halakha? Should a woman examine herself before engaging in intercourse with her husband?
Rav Yehuda
She should not examine herself before intercourse.
Rabbi Zeira
But why not let her examine herself and ensure purity? Would that not be more careful?
Rav Yehuda
Examination may cause her to find blood and create an unnecessary prohibition — we do not require her to generate potential liability through her own examination.
📌 Principle Pre-intercourse bedikah is not required — proactive examination may create prohibition rather than prevent transgression.
4 / 9

⏱️ Slide 2: Can She Examine Immediately After Intercourse?

Rabbi Abba
What is the halakha? Should she examine immediately after intercourse — within the minimum period for niddah onset — to render her husband liable for a provisional guilt offering (asham taluy)?
Rav Huna
Can you find a case where a full examination is physically possible within the minimum period for onset of menstruation?
Baraita
What is the minimum time for niddah onset? The time needed for her to remove her undergarment and extend her hand to examine herself.
Rav Huna
That baraita speaks only of an external wipe — not a full internal examination. A full examination takes longer than this minimum period.
🔑 Key Distinction The halakhic minimum period (שיעור וסת) = time for an external wipe only. A full internal bedikah cannot be completed within that window.

📅 Slide 3: Fixed Cycle vs. No Fixed Cycle

יש לה וסת — Fixed Cycle

  • Husband may rely on her presumed purity (chezkat tahara)
  • Examination required only if she handles tahoros (pure items)
  • Wives of donkey drivers and laborers: remain in presumptive purity when husband returns from travel
  • Husband's return and request for intercourse itself serves as an implicit 'examination' (Shmuel)
  • If sleeping: need not be awakened — would demean her (Rava to Rav Pappa)

אין לה וסת — No Fixed Cycle

  • Forbidden to engage in intercourse without prior examination
  • No right to collect marriage contract (ketubah) if divorced or widowed
  • Not entitled to provisions or sustenance from husband's estate
  • Does not receive worn clothing back
  • Husband must divorce her and may never remarry her
  • Rabbi Ḥanina ben Antigonus: TWO examination cloths — one before, one after intercourse

🏠 Slide 4: The Returning Traveler and Presumptive Purity

Donkey drivers, laborers, and those returning from mourning or wedding feasts arrive home after travels
↓
Their wives remain in a presumptive status of purity (חזקת טהרה)
↓
When husband requests intercourse upon return, this serves as an implicit examination — 'there is no greater examination than this'
↓
Shmuel's ruling (per Rabbi Abba bar Yirmeya): A woman with a fixed cycle who is awake need not perform a formal bedikah; the husband's request suffices
↓
If she is sleeping — she is not required to be awakened for examination before intercourse, as this would cause her shame and degradation
↓
Rav Kahana asked the women of the household directly, confirming the halakhic concern for a wife's dignity and self-respect

📋 Berakhot 12 — Summary Map

Does she have a fixed menstrual cycle (וסת קבוע)?
↓
YES — Veset Kavua
No examination required before intercourse unless she handles tahoros.
Husband's return = implicit examination.
She may not be awakened from sleep.
NO — No Fixed Cycle
Forbidden from intercourse without bedikah.
Loses ketubah, provisions, and worn clothing.
Husband must divorce and cannot remarry her.
↓
Remedy (Shmuel — halakha follows Rabbi Ḥanina ben Antigonus):
Two examination cloths — one before intercourse, one after — permit a woman without a fixed cycle to have intercourse. The cloths may serve either for her benefit or detriment.
↓
Post-intercourse examination: The halakhic minimum period (שיעור) = time for an external wipe only. A full internal examination takes longer — so a post-coital bedikah cannot make the husband retroactively liable.
