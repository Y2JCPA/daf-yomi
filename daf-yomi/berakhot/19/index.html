🩸 Five Colors of Impure Blood (Mishna)

אָדֹם Red

  • Like blood from a wound
  • Standard impure red
  • Not slaughter-base (too dark) — fresh wound blood

שָּׁחוֹר Black

  • Black as ḥeret ink
  • Deeper = still impure
  • Lighter = impure (like red)
  • R. Ḥanina: actually darkened red

כַּרְכּוֹם Crocus

  • Bright saffron-yellow
  • Color of the karkom flower
  • Third mishna color

מַיִם אֲדָמָה Earth-Water

  • Water flooding red earth
  • Specifically Beit Kerem Valley
  • Water must pool on the surface

יַיִן מָזוּג Diluted Wine

  • Wine mixed with water
  • Two parts water, one part wine
  • Fifth and final mishna color

⚖️ Beit Shammai vs Beit Hillel: Additional Colors

Beit Shammai
Even blood the color of fenugreek-soaking water (מֵי תִּלְתָּן) and blood the color of meat-dripping liquid are ritually impure — we add these to the five mishna colors.
First Tanna
These two colors are left in abeyance (תְּלֵי) — uncertain, neither ruled pure nor impure outright.
Beit Hillel
Blood the color of fenugreek water or meat drippings is ritually pure — they fall outside the five impure categories.
Gemara
Beit Shammai may hold there are more than four types (rejecting Rabbi Abbahu), OR they accept four types but read the derivation differently to include these shades.
Three-Way Split Beit Shammai = impure | First Tanna = abeyance | Beit Hillel = pure. Practical difference between First Tanna and Beit Hillel: First Tanna creates doubt-status; Beit Hillel grants full purity.

📜 Biblical Source: Pure Blood Exists in a Woman

בֵּין דָּם לְדָם — יֵשׁ דָּם טָהוֹר וְדָם טָמֵא בָּאִשָּׁה
Rabbi Ḥama bar Yosef citing Rabbi Oshaya Devarim 17:8 — 'If a matter is too hard for you… between blood and blood…' The rebellious elder (זָקֵן מַמְרֵא) can only be liable for contradicting the Sanhedrin if there is genuine room for dispute — this requires some blood to be pure and some impure. Rabbi Abbahu's derivation (Vayikra 12:7 + 20:18): The word דָּמֶיהָ (her blood) appears twice in plural form, yielding 4 types. The mishna lists 5 — Rabbi Ḥanina resolves: black is just darkened red, so only 4 truly distinct types exist.

🟢 Green Blood — Four Positions

Woman sees green blood — what is its halakhic status?
Akavya ben Mahalalel
Tamei as niddah blood — green blood is ritually impure like the five listed colors
The Rabbis
Tahor — not among the five mishna colors; fully pure
Rabbi Meir
Even if not impure as niddah blood, green blood renders food susceptible to impurity — it is one of the seven liquids
Rabbi Yosei
Neither — not impure as niddah blood, nor does it render food susceptible
↓
Rabbi Yoḥanan clarifies Rabbi Meir: Rabbi Meir agreed with Akavya — green blood is niddah-impure. His mishna statement addressed the Rabbis' view on red-then-green sequences, arguing green blood should at minimum be a susceptibility liquid.

🔴 What Shade Exactly Is Impure Red?

Shmuel
Like blood of slaughter — not the blackened pool at the neck's base, but the fresh, bright red that flows freely.
Ulla
Like blood from a living wounded bird. (Dilemma left unresolved: small bird or large bird?)
Ze'eiri / Rabbi Ḥanina
Like blood from a crushed head louse. (Objection raised: but a woman may attribute such a stain to a louse — implying louse blood looks different!)
Ami of Vardina / R. Abbahu
Like blood from the smallest finger of the hand when pressed and squeezed.
Rav Naḥman
Like blood from bloodletting — the standard medical phlebotomy shade of red.
Why So Many Definitions? Each Amora tries to capture the precise mid-range red — not too dark (louse blood objection), not too bright. Practical stakes: a woman's purity status hangs on identifying the exact hue.
