⚖️ Legal Status Under Anatomical Uncertainty

Mishna
A woman who gives birth to a tumtum or hermaphrodite must observe the strictures of both a male and female birth — full stringency in both directions.
Gemara
This seems obvious — their sex is unknown! Why state it explicitly?
Resolution
Lest one say: since Rabbi Yitzḥak teaches sex is determined at conception, we can determine it retroactively — the mishna teaches we cannot. Observable uncertainty governs.
Core Principle of Daf 28 When sex or birth status is anatomically indeterminate, halakha applies the maximum stringency from every relevant category — not a theoretical resolution.

🔥 Does a Burned Corpse Impart Roof Impurity?

Rabbi Yoḥanan
A deformed or burned corpse imparts tumah to all items under the same roof (tumah b'ohel). He follows Rabbi Eliezer: any corpse — even missing limbs — imparts roof impurity.
Ravina to Rav Ashi
Rabbi Yoḥanan's ruling is consistent with Rabbi Eliezer (Oholot 2:3): a corpse still has its form even if deformed. But the Rabbis disagree — items under small openings (less than 4 handbreadths) are deemed pure.
Abaye
How can a burned corpse retain its צורה (form)? It was burned atop a katavla — a hard leather spread — which prevents the body from sinking in, thereby preserving its shape.
Dispute: What Makes a Corpse Impart Ohel Tumah? Rabbi Eliezer: the corpse itself suffices. The Rabbis: form (tzurah) must be intact. The case of the katavla shows form can survive even burning.

✋ Woman Who Discharges a Shaped Hand or Foot

Childbirth Impurity — YES

  • Shaped hand/foot proves a full fetus existed inside
  • Mother is טמאה as a יולדת (woman after childbirth)
  • Observes impurity days: 7 (male) or 14 (female)
  • Baraita: she is impure with the impurity of a woman after childbirth

Days of Purity — NO

  • Rav Ḥisda & Rabba bar Rav Huna: no ימי טהרה granted
  • Cannot confirm a complete birth occurred — only a limb emerged
  • Downside certainty applies; upside benefit does not
  • Rav Yosef objects from mishna (29a) — Abaye resolves: she observes menstrual strictures regardless

👶 Fetus Extends Hand — Steps of the Sugya

Rav Huna rules: If a fetus extends its hand out of the womb and retracts it, the mother is impure as a יולדת (woman after childbirth). Proof: Zeraḥ extended his hand before Peretz was born (Genesis 38:28–29) — the hand-emergence was legally recognized as birth.
↓
Rav Yehuda objects from a baraita: 'If a fetus extends its hand, its mother need not be concerned about yoledet status with regard to any matter.' This seems to directly contradict Rav Huna!
↓
Rav Naḥman resolves: The baraita means she need not be concerned about 'any matter that comes later' — i.e., if the fetus is subsequently born dead, the prior hand-extension already established birth. The birth itself is what matters.
↓
Abaye clarifies further: The baraita means she need not be concerned with matters of a niddah — because she is fully a yoledet (the higher status), not merely a menstruant. The hand-extension elevated her status entirely.

🏛️ Tumtum/Hermaphrodite: Temple Entry vs. Teruma

Tumtum or Hermaphrodite emits white ziva or red menstrual blood — definitely impure by at least one category
↓
🏛️ Entering the Temple
NOT liable for lashes or korban
Numbers 5:3: 'Both male and female you shall send out' — Rav derives: only definite male or female is liable; tumtum/hermaphrodite excluded
🍇 Touching Teruma
Teruma IS burned
Impurity stringency applies even under sex uncertainty — rabbinic concern requires burning the teruma they contact
↓
Gemara resolves the asymmetry: The verse 'both male and female' excludes uncertain-sex persons from the Torah prohibition of Temple entry. Teruma-burning follows a separate rabbinic stringency applying impurity even under doubt.
