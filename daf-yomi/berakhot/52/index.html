⚖️ Mi'un (Refusal): Rabbis vs. Rabbi Yehuda

Rabbis

  • Mi'un ends once two pubic hairs appear
  • Two hairs = adulthood = Torah-level marriage
  • No refusal possible after that point
  • Concede: intercourse after hair growth also ends mi'un
  • Supported by the ruling after R. Yishmael's daughter's case

Rabbi Yehuda

  • Can still refuse even after two hairs appear
  • …unless black hair is preponderant in pubic area
  • Mar Ukva: means two curled black hairs suffice
  • Concedes: intercourse after hair growth → cannot refuse
  • R. Yitzḥak and disciples of R. Ḥanina ruled this way in practice

💬 Why Does the Mishna Specify Obligations for Both?

Gemara
Since we learned that a girl with two hairs is an adult obligated in mitzvot, why does the mishna also specify the same for a boy — isn't it obvious?
Answer
For the girl, it was needed to exclude Rabbi Yosei, who requires 'man' (Deut. 25:7) for ḥalitza — meaning only an adult male. The mishna teaches that a girl with two hairs qualifies too.
Gemara
But then why specify the boy is 'obligated in all mitzvot'? Was it to teach the stubborn-and-rebellious son rule?
Answer
No — that rule was already taught elsewhere. The boy's clause is unnecessary on its own. It was added for stylistic symmetry, since the girl's clause was needed.
Talmudic Principle The Talmud often includes parallel statements for rhetorical balance (אגב), even when only one side requires the legal clarification.

📖 Must Two Hairs Each Have Their Own Follicle?

אִיּוֹב ט׃יז — שַׁעֲרָה יְשׁוּפֵנִי
🔬 The Follicle Requirement — Resolved via Job Rabbi Ḥelbo citing Rav Huna: The two hairs must have follicles (שׁוֹרָשִׁים) to be valid signs of adulthood. Problem: Can two hairs share one follicle? God rebuked Job (38:1): 'Greatest imbecile in the world! I created many hairs on a person's head, and for each I created its own distinct follicle so that two hairs should not draw sustenance from one follicle.' Resolution (Rav Ashi): No contradiction — on the head, two hairs cannot share a follicle. But on the rest of the body (including pubic area) they can. So two pubic hairs in one follicle are still a valid sign of adulthood.
