QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Rabbis, at what point can a girl no longer perform mi'un (refusal)?
A. When she turns twelve years and one day
B. When she grows two pubic hairs
C. When black hair becomes preponderant in the pubic area
D. Only after she has had intercourse with her husband
Correct: B — The Rabbis hold that two pubic hairs mark legal adulthood, ending the minor status required for mi'un. Rabbi Yehuda disagrees, requiring preponderant black hair — but the practical ruling follows the Rabbis.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the mishna state that a boy with two hairs is 'obligated in all mitzvot,' even though this seems obvious?
A. To specifically teach the law of the stubborn and rebellious son
B. To clarify that facial hair does not count
C. For stylistic symmetry with the girl's clause, which was needed to counter Rabbi Yosei
D. To include Rabbi Yosei's opinion about adult males in ḥalitza
Correct: C — The mishna needed to state this for the girl to counter Rabbi Yosei (who required 'man' for ḥalitza). Since it stated it for the girl, it added it for the boy as well for parallel structure — even though the boy's clause was technically unnecessary.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What did God say to Job that the Gemara uses to address the follicle question?
A. Two hairs sharing a follicle are a sign of divine blessing
B. Each hair on a person's head has its own distinct follicle, so two hairs never share one
C. Follicles are irrelevant to the halakhic sign of adulthood
D. The number of follicles on the head equals the number of mitzvot
Correct: B — God tells Job He created a distinct follicle for every hair on the head. The Gemara resolves the apparent contradiction by limiting this to head hair — pubic hairs can share a follicle and still serve as a valid sign of adulthood.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rav Pappa distinguish rulings of Rav Malkiyya from those of Rav Malkiyyu?
A. Rav Malkiyya taught in Babylon; Rav Malkiyyu taught in the Land of Israel
B. Rav Malkiyya lived earlier; Rav Malkiyyu was his student
C. Rulings linked to a mishna or baraita are Rav Malkiyya's; independent amoraic statements are Rav Malkiyyu's
D. Rav Malkiyya dealt with purity laws; Rav Malkiyyu dealt with monetary law
Correct: C — Rav Pappa's practical rule: if the ruling is anchored to a mishna or baraita, attribute it to Rav Malkiyya. If it is a free-standing amoraic statement, it belongs to Rav Malkiyyu.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ḥanina ben Antigonus, how can a stain lead to ziva concern?
A. Any stain larger than the size of a split bean triggers ziva status automatically
B. A stain on a garment worn for three days always creates ziva concern
C. Finding stains on three separate examined robes, or actual bleeding on two days plus a stain on the third day
D. Only a stain seen during the seven clean days causes ziva concern
Correct: C — Rabbi Ḥanina ben Antigonus rules that stains can create ziva concern in two scenarios: (1) stains found on three separately examined robes, or (2) she saw actual bleeding on two days and then found a stain on the third day.
