⚖️ Rabbi Eliezer vs. The Rabbis: Holding the Penis

Rabbi Eliezer
Anyone who holds his penis and urinates is like one who brings a flood to the world!
The Rabbis
But if one does not hold his penis, won't he spray urine on his legs — rendering himself unfit for Torah and prayer?
Rabbi Eliezer
Better that people cast aspersions about his children as mamzerim than that he render himself wicked even one moment before the Omnipresent!
Rabbi Eliezer
Practical solution: stand on an elevated place and urinate downward, or urinate into loose earth that absorbs the urine.
The Rabbis
What if he has neither an elevated place nor loose earth? Then what is he to do?
Rabbi Eliezer
It is preferable to be suspected of fathering mamzerim than to sin even a moment before God.
✅ Resolution — Rav Naḥman A married man is permitted to hold his penis while urinating, as his sexual urges are satisfied by his wife and there is no concern of illicit arousal.

✋ The Examining Hand: Women vs. Men

Women — Praiseworthy

  • Diligent self-examination is praised
  • Women cannot be aroused by self-examination
  • Examination determines ritual purity status
  • Even occasional examination is encouraged
  • Deaf or incapacitated women may have friends assist them

Men — Hand Should Be Severed

  • Diligent examination risks seminal emission (שכבת זרע לבטלה)
  • Even occasional examination is problematic
  • Rock or earthenware: permitted (hard, won't warm the body)
  • Cloth below the corona: permitted (Abaye)
  • Cloth above the corona: prohibited — warms and may cause emission

Liable to death — as severe as the punishment of the wicked

🤲 Rabbi Tarfon: Literal or Hyperbole?

Rabbi Tarfon
If one's hand goes to his penis, his hand should be severed upon his navel!
The Rabbis
If a thorn was stuck in one's belly, should he not remove it? Severing the hand at the navel would split the belly open!
Rabbi Tarfon
Yes! Let his belly be split open rather than let him descend into Gehinnom!
Gemara
Rabbi Tarfon cannot mean this literally — he must mean: anyone who inserts his hand below his navel. The statement is rhetorical, expressing severity, not a practical court ruling.
Conclusion The court does not literally sever one's hand. The mishna and Rabbi Tarfon speak hyperbolically to convey the gravity of the prohibition.

🏺 Enabling an Imbecile Priest to Eat Teruma

Problem: An imbecile priest may emit semen unknowingly, rendering him impure and unfit to eat teruma (תרומה). How can he reliably eat it?
↓
Method 1 — Leather Pouch (Rabbi Eliezer b. Tzadok): Wrap a leather pouch around his penis before giving him teruma; check afterward for emission.
↓
Objection: A leather pouch warms the penis, potentially causing the very seminal emission we seek to detect — defeating its purpose!
↓
Method 2 — Copper Pouch (Sages in name of Rabbi Elazar): Use a metal (copper) pouch instead. Copper does not absorb liquid, so any drop of semen remains visible on its surface.
↓
Abaye's Note: This discussion teaches that tight-fitting trousers are also prohibited — they warm the penis in the same way as a pouch, potentially causing emission. The priestly trousers were designed differently to avoid this.
