QUESTION 1 OF 5
From which verse does the Gemara derive that niddah (menstruation) laws apply to a one-day-old baby girl?
A. "And if a woman has an issue" (Lev. 15:19) — broad language includes any female from birth
B. "Whether it be a male or a female" (Lev. 15:33) — this covers all ages
C. "When a person shall have in the skin of his flesh" (Lev. 13:2) — includes infants
D. "And he has no child" (Deut. 25:5) — covers all stages of life
Correct: A — The baraita derives niddah law for baby girls from the broad phrasing of Leviticus 15:19. The word 'woman' is not restricted to adults; inclusive language brings in females from their very first day.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What additional ruling does Rav Sheshet derive from the mishna's statement that a one-day-old baby disqualifies his mother from teruma?
A. Even a fetus can disqualify its mother from teruma
B. Only a male baby, not a female baby, has this disqualifying power
C. The baby also disqualifies his father's Canaanite slaves from eating teruma
D. The disqualification extends to the baby's maternal grandparents
Correct: C — Rav Sheshet explains the mishna specifies a one-day-old (rather than just a fetus) to teach the additional ruling that this child disqualifies his father's Canaanite slaves from teruma, since as an heir the child now holds a share of those slaves.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Mar son of Rav Yosef in the name of Rava, what is the primary reason the mishna discusses a one-day-old baby's inheritance?
A. To teach that the baby can inherit his mother's estate and bequeath to paternal brothers
B. To show that a one-day-old counts as a 'child' who reduces the firstborn's double portion
C. To establish that a posthumous firstborn always receives a double portion
D. To prove that only property acquired after birth enters the estate
Correct: B — Mar son of Rav Yosef in Rava's name teaches that the mishna discusses a one-day-old's inheritance specifically to establish that such a baby reduces the firstborn's double portion of the estate — he is a full 'child' for this purpose.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What happens halakhically when a man engages in intercourse with a girl who is less than three years and one day old?
A. She is fully betrothed and requires a get to dissolve the relationship
B. She becomes impure with niddah impurity and imparts it to her partner
C. The act has no halakhic effect — like placing a finger in the eye, the hymen regrows
D. She is disqualified from marrying a priest but not fully betrothed
Correct: C — The mishna teaches that for a girl under three years and one day, intercourse is 'like placing a finger in the eye' — just as the eye tears and then recovers, the hymen regrows and the act creates no legal bond or impurity status.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yannai and Rabbi Yoḥanan, what is the root of the dispute between Rabbi Meir and the Rabbis regarding the age of betrothal through intercourse?
A. Rabbi Meir requires witnesses; the Rabbis do not
B. Rabbi Meir holds 30 days in a year count as a full year; the Rabbis require a complete 12-month calendar year
C. Rabbi Meir limits this rule to betrothal with a Torah scholar; the Rabbis apply it universally
D. Rabbi Meir counts from conception; the Rabbis count from birth
Correct: B — The dispute turns on the principle of '30 days in a year.' Rabbi Meir holds that 30 days equals a year, so two years and 30 days constitutes 'three years.' The Rabbis require three complete 12-month calendar years, yielding their higher threshold of three years and one day.
