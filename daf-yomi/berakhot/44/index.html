🩸 Deriving Niddah & Ziva Laws for Infants

Question: From where do we derive that niddah laws apply to a one-day-old baby girl?
↓
Baraita cites Leviticus 15:19 — "If a woman has an issue, and her issue in her flesh is blood"
↓
The word "woman" is broad; the Sages derive it includes any female from birth onward
↓
Ziva for males: Leviticus 15:2 — "When any man has an issue" — includes any male from birth
↓
Ziva for females: Leviticus 15:33 — "Whether it be a male or a female" — Rabbi Yishmael b. Rabbi Yoḥanan ben Beroka's derivation covers both sexes at any age
↓
Conclusion: All niddah and ziva laws apply from the very first day of life — inclusive biblical language is the key

⚖️ Why Does the Mishna Discuss a One-Day-Old's Inheritance?

First Explanation
The baby inherits his mother's estate when she dies on his birth day, so he can bequeath it to his paternal brothers — heirs who are not her heirs.
Objection
Is it really presumed the mother dies before the fetus? There was an incident where a fetus made three spasmodic motions after the mother died!
Mar b. Rav Ashi
Those motions are merely involuntary spasms — like the tail of a lizard that moves after being severed. The fetus was already dead.
Mar b. Rav Yosef (Rava)
The mishna's real teaching: a one-day-old baby reduces the firstborn's double portion. Even one day of life makes him a full 'child' for inheritance purposes. A firstborn born after his father's death gets no double portion.
Halakha (All Versions Accepted) A one-day-old reduces the firstborn's double portion; a posthumous firstborn receives no double portion; only property existing before the father's death counts for the double portion.

🍞 Teruma — Baby Enables vs. Disqualifies His Mother

Baby Enables Teruma

  • Mother: Israelite woman who married a priest
  • Now divorced or widowed from the priest
  • But has a baby from the priest
  • → Baby keeps her eligible to eat teruma
  • Source: "Born of his house, they may eat of his bread" (Lev. 22:11)
  • Also teaches: Baby enables his father's Canaanite slaves to eat teruma

Baby Disqualifies from Teruma

  • Mother: Daughter of a priest who married an Israelite
  • Now divorced or widowed from the Israelite
  • But has a baby from the Israelite
  • → Baby bars her from eating teruma
  • Source: "Has no child" + "As in her youth" (Lev. 22:13)
  • Also teaches: Baby disqualifies his father's Canaanite slaves from teruma (Rav Sheshet)

💍 At What Age Can a Girl Be Betrothed Through Intercourse?

Rabbi Meir
A girl who is three years old — the baraita says even two years and one day — can be fully betrothed through intercourse.
The Rabbis
She must be three years and one day old — a full three-year period including complete months must elapse.
Rabbi Yannai / Rabbi Yoḥanan
The dispute: Rabbi Meir holds 30 days in a year count as a full year. So 2 years + 30 days = "3 years." The Rabbis require a full 12-month calendar year — three complete years.
Mishna Rule
Under three years: intercourse is like "putting a finger in the eye" — the eye tears and recovers; so too the hymen regrows and there is no halakhic effect.
At 3 Years + 1 Day Full betrothal through intercourse · She receives a ketuva of 200 zuz · She imparts ziva impurity to her partner · If she marries a priest she may eat teruma · Intercourse with an unfit man disqualifies her from the priesthood.
