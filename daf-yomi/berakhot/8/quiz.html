Berakhot 8 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Rabbi Elazar states halakha follows Rabbi Eliezer in four cases. The Gemara concludes these four cases are all within which Talmudic order?
A. Seder Nashim — laws of women and marriage
B. Seder Teharot — laws of ritual purity
C. Seder Moed — laws of festivals
D. Seder Nezikin — laws of damages
Correct: B — The Gemara establishes that Rabbi Pedat (son of Rabbi Elazar) meant the four cases are all within Seder Teharot. This is the resolution that allows the balsam challenge (from Seder Zera'im) to be deflected.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the apparent contradiction between Rabbi Elazar's 'four rulings' and the mishna about instructing a minor wife to refuse (mi'un) for levirate purposes?
A. That ruling is rabbinic, not biblical, so it doesn't count among the four
B. The ruling about mi'un belongs to Rabbi Elazar (amora), not Rabbi Eliezer (tanna)
C. Mi'un is already implied within one of the four cases and is not a separate ruling
D. Rabbi Elazar retracted that ruling later in the Gemara
Correct: B — The Gemara points out that the mi'un ruling in levirate context is attributed to Rabbi Elazar the amora, not to Rabbi Eliezer the tanna. Since Rabbi Elazar was listing cases of Rabbi Eliezer's rulings, this is not an additional case.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ḥanina ben Gamliel, how many blessings should one recite in the Amida on Shabbat or Yom Tov?
A. Seven — the standard abbreviated Shabbat Amida
B. Eight — seven plus a special havdala blessing
C. Fourteen — double the Shabbat count
D. Eighteen — the full weekday Amida including havdala
Correct: D — Rabbi Ḥanina ben Gamliel rules one prays all eighteen blessings, inserting havdala in the blessing 'Who graciously grants knowledge.' His reasoning: havdala cannot be compressed into a different blessing — it requires the full prayer structure.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rav Kahana's baraita lists three types of virgins. According to Rabbi Ḥanina son of Rav Ika, why is 'a woman who has seen menstrual blood' excluded from the classic three-item list?
A. Because her status is determined by age, not by physical condition
B. Because the list only covers cases relevant to monetary law
C. Because a woman can revert to a virgin-like status once her regular menses return, while the three listed items never revert
D. Because women are covered under a separate category in Seder Nashim
Correct: C — Rabbi Ḥanina son of Rav Ika explains that the list of three includes only items that permanently remain in their non-virgin state once changed. A woman, however, may see menstrual blood and then have it stop — reverting to a non-menstruating state — so she does not fit the list's criterion.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Sumakhos citing Rabbi Meir, after how many months does a pregnant woman's fetus become 'known to all,' establishing her presumptive pregnant status?
A. One month
B. Two months
C. Three months
D. Five months
Correct: C — Sumakhos says in the name of Rabbi Meir that the fetus is publicly visible after three months. Rabbi Meir himself calls the proof-verse from Isaiah 26:18 only an 'allusion,' not definitive proof, because some pregnancies run only seven months — meaning three months isn't a universal marker.
