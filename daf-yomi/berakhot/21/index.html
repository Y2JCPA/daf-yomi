⚔️ Can the Womb Open Without Blood?

Rabbis
A woman who discharges tissue without blood is pure. The womb can open without blood — tissue alone does not render her impure.
Rabbi Yehuda
A woman who discharges tissue without blood is impure. The womb cannot open without blood — blood must have preceded the tissue even if unseen.
Shmuel (via Rav Yehuda)
Rabbi Yehuda deems impure only when the tissue appears as one of the four impure blood colors. Otherwise even Rabbi Yehuda agrees she is pure.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
If tissue appears as a blood color, all agree she is impure. Dispute is only when she does not know the color (e.g., lost tissue). Rabbi Yehuda: impure by default; Rabbis: pure.
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
True dispute: Whether פתיחת הקבר (womb opening) is possible without blood. Rabbi Yehuda: impossible — blood must have been shed. Rabbis: possible — no blood means no impurity.
📜 Baraita Challenge Rav Hoshaya's baraita lists red, black, green, and white tissue — Rabbis hold pure in all cases. This challenges both Shmuel's and Rabbi Yoḥanan's explanations of the dispute, and supports Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak's resolution.

⚔️ Three Interpretations of the Rabbi Yehuda–Rabbis Dispute

Shmuel
Rabbi Yehuda deems impure only when the tissue itself has one of the four impure blood colors (red, black, saffron, muddy water). Outside these colors, all agree she is pure.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
If tissue has a blood color → all agree impure. Dispute is only when she doesn't know the color of the lost tissue. Rabbi Yehuda rules stringently impure; Rabbis rule pure.
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
The real dispute: Can the womb open without blood? Rabbis: yes — pure. Rabbi Yehuda: no — some blood was shed even if unseen — impure. This alone explains the baraita listing all four colors with Rabbis ruling pure in every case.
🔑 Why the Baraita Challenges Shmuel and Rabbi Yoḥanan The baraita includes green and white tissue (not among the four blood colors) alongside red and black. In all cases Rabbis hold pure. This makes Shmuel's color-based limit and Rabbi Yoḥanan's lost-tissue theory unworkable — only the womb-opening dispute explains every case uniformly.

🔍 Four Opinions on Examining Discharged Tissue

Sumakhos / R. Shimon ben Menasya

  • A Sage presented with the tissue must examine it
  • If it appears to contain blood → woman is impure
  • More stringent than Rabbis of the Mishna
  • Focuses on external appearance suggesting blood

Rabbi Aḥa

  • Tear the tissue open and examine the interior
  • Interior looks red → impure, even without actual blood
  • Aligned with Sumakhos but focuses on interior color
  • Appearance of redness inside is sufficient for impurity

Rabbi Binyamin

  • Tear it open to examine its contents
  • Contains a bone → classified as a fetus
  • Mother is impure with post-birth impurity
  • A bone is definitive evidence of fetal development

R. Shimon ben Yoḥai (via R. Yoḥanan)

  • Tear it open to examine its contents
  • Contains accumulated blood → impure
  • No accumulated blood → pure
  • Requires actual blood content, not mere appearance

📜 Blood in a Tube — The 'In Her Flesh' Rule (Lev. 15:19)

Question (Rabbi Yirmeya → Rabbi Zeira):
A woman inserts a tube into her vagina and finds blood inside the tube.
Is she rendered impure as a niddah?
↓
Rabbi Zeira: She is pure.
The Torah states בִּבְשָׂרָהּ — "in her flesh" (Lev. 15:19).
Blood must emerge through direct contact with her flesh, not via a tube interposition.
↓
🩸 Blood inside discharged tissue
Impure
It is the manner of women (דרך ראייה) to see blood within discharged tissue. This satisfies "in her flesh" — normal physiological discharge.
🧪 Blood inside a tube
Pure
Not the normal manner of discharge. The tube is an interposition — does not satisfy "in her flesh." All opinions agree she is pure.
↓
Baraita Resolution: First tanna, Rabbi Eliezer, and the Rabbis all agree on the tube case — pure. Their dispute concerns only whether blood inside cracked tissue is menstrual blood or the tissue's own blood.

🍷 Key Rules and Standards of Daf 21

Sharon Wine Standard (21a): Wine of the Sharon region equals undiluted (not diluted) and new (not old) Carmelite wine for blood examination comparison. Stronger, redder standard applies.
↓
Tiberian Cup (Abaye, 21a): For blood resembling diluted wine, examination uses only a simple Tiberian cup. Reason: cups are made to a universal standard — the vessel's standardization ensures consistent comparison.
↓
Four Impure Blood Colors: Red (אדום), Black (שחור), Saffron/bright (כרוכמא), Muddy water (מי אדמה). These are the halachic benchmark throughout this daf for determining ritual impurity from blood discharge.
↓
"In Her Flesh" — בִּבְשָׂרָהּ (Lev. 15:19): Blood must discharge through the woman's own flesh. Excludes: blood in a tube (all agree — pure), blood in a gestational sac, and blood in tissue (per the first tanna's view).
↓
Practical Outcome of the Womb Dispute: Rabbi Yehoshua holds she brings a certain offering (womb cannot open without blood — she definitely gave birth or is a zava). Rabbi Eliezer holds she brings an uncertain offering (womb may open without blood — her status is doubtful).
