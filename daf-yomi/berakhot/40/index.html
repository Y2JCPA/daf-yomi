⚖️ Does Caesarean Birth Count as Halakhic Birth?

Rabbis
Caesarean birth does NOT trigger postpartum impurity or a birth offering. Source: "וְזָרְעָה — emitted seed" (Lev. 12:2) — only a birth through the natural passage where seed was originally emitted qualifies.
Rabbi Shimon
Caesarean birth DOES have full halakhic birth status. Source: "וְאִם נְקֵבָה תֵלֵד — But if she gives birth to a female" (Lev. 12:5) — the doubled תֵּלֵד expression includes all forms of birth.
📜 Mishna Context The Mishna (40a) states: after a caesarean birth the mother does not observe 7 or 14 days of impurity, 33 or 66 days of purity, and is not obligated to bring a birth offering. The Gemara explores the scriptural basis for both views.

🍼 Rabbi Mani bar Patish: The Core Dispute Explained

Rabbi Mani bar Patish
Rabbis' reasoning: Lev. 12:2 says "כִּי תַזְרִיעַ וְיָלְדָה" — the birth must result from emitted seed passing through the natural opening. Caesarean bypasses this, so no laws are triggered.
Gemara (counter)
Rabbi Shimon's use of that verse: He reads "emitted seed" to include a decomposed fetus — even if the fetus entirely dissolved into fluid resembling the original seed, full birth laws still apply.
Key Insight Both sides agree the verse is doing work — they simply disagree on what it teaches. The Rabbis use it to limit birth laws; Rabbi Shimon uses it to expand them to dissolved fetuses.

🔀 Inclusion of Tumtum and Hermaphrodite in Birth Laws

Question: Does a mother who delivers a tumtum (indeterminate sex) or androgynos (hermaphrodite) incur postpartum obligations?
↓
Rabbis' Source
"לְבֵן... לְבַת" — "for a son... for a daughter" (Lev. 12:6–8). These extra expressions include tumtum and hermaphrodite, since the Rabbis already used the doubled תלד for caesarean section.
Rabbi Shimon's Source
Bar Livai's baraita: "וּבִמְלֹאת יְמֵי טָהֳרָהּ — upon completion of her purity days" — the extra phrase תִּטְהָר serves as an independent inclusion for all ambiguous births.
↓
✅ Both Rabbis and Rabbi Shimon agree: birth laws fully apply to tumtum and hermaphrodite births.

🐄 Rabbi Shimon's Split Ruling: Humans vs. Consecrated Animals

Human Birth (Rabbi Shimon)

  • Caesarean birth IS a halakhic birth
  • Mother is impure for 7 or 14 days
  • Must observe 33 or 66 days of purity
  • Must bring postpartum sacrifice
  • Source: doubled תֵּלֵד (Lev. 12:5)

Consecrated Animal (Rabbi Shimon concedes)

  • Caesarean animal is NOT a valid firstborn
  • Cannot be sanctified as בְּכוֹר
  • Analogy: firstborn animal laws require פֶּטֶר רֶחֶם — opening the womb
  • A consecrated animal is closer to firstborn than to human birth
  • Rabbi Yoḥanan records this concession explicitly

🔥 The Altar Law: Disqualified Offerings That Ascend

Base verse: "זֹאת תּוֹרַת הָעֹלָה — This is the law of the burnt offering... upon the altar" (Lev. 6:2). The principle: once an offering ascends the altar, it need not descend.
↓
Rabbi Yehuda uses three limiting expressions to exclude three cases from this rule: offering slaughtered at night; blood spilled before sprinkling; blood taken outside the Temple courtyard.
↓
Rabbi Shimon reads "This is the law" as an expansive inclusion — all disqualified offerings that ascended the altar shall not descend. Rabbi Yehuda's exclusions are rejected.
↓
Rabbi Shimon's own exclusions (40b): Three categories are removed from the altar even post factum — animals that copulated with a person; objects of bestiality; animals designated for or worshipped as idols.
↓
The reasoning: "What did you see to include these and exclude those?" — Disgraceful animals (מְאוּסִין) that never should have ascended are categorically different from merely disqualified sacrifices that had valid intent.
