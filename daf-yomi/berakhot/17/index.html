🏠 The Anatomical Parable: Three Zones of the Body

חֶדֶר — The Room

  • = Uterus (inner chamber)
  • Blood found here: definitely impure
  • Presumed to be menstrual blood
  • Renders woman a niddah with certainty

עֲלִיָּה — The Upper Story

  • = Bladder
  • Blood found here: definitely pure
  • Not uterine in origin
  • Does not render woman a niddah

פְּרוֹזְדוֹר — The Corridor

  • = Vagina / vestibule
  • Blood found here: uncertain status
  • May come from uterus or bladder
  • Treated as impure due to safek (doubt)

⚠️ Five Life-Threatening Actions — Rabbi Shimon ben Yoḥai

Rabbi Shimon ben Yoḥai: Five actions where one is liable for one's own death — his blood is upon his own head
↓
🧅 Peeled Foods
Garlic, onion, or egg left overnight — dangerous even stored under covers or in a basket
🥤 Diluted Drinks
Left overnight in metal vessels (Shmuel); natron vessels treated equally (Rav Pappa)
⚰️ Cemetery Sleep
Sleeping there to attract a spirit of impurity — evil spirits may cause death
💅 Nail Clippings
Thrown in public — a pregnant woman may step over them and miscarry; applies only to both hand and foot nails together
🩸 Bloodletting
Engaging in intercourse immediately after letting blood — physically dangerous

🌙 May One Engage in Intercourse by Day?

Rav Ḥisda
It is forbidden to engage in intercourse by day! The verse says 'Love your fellow as yourself' — by day you may see something unattractive about your spouse and come to despise her.
Abaye
This is the reason: by lamp-light, he might notice a blemish on her and she will become repulsive to him.
Rava
If the house is dark, it is permitted even by day — darkness removes the concern entirely.
Rava
A Torah scholar may darken the room with his garment and engage in intercourse by day — he will certainly act with proper intention.
Mishna Challenge
But the mishna implies one may engage in intercourse by the light of a lamp! How can Rav Ḥisda forbid this?
Gemara Resolution
Read the mishna as: 'she must examine the cloth by the light of a lamp' — it is the examination, not the intercourse, that takes place by lamplight.
Household of King Munbaz Praised for 'engaging in intercourse by day' — the Gemara reinterprets this as 'examining their beds by day,' consistent with Rav Ḥisda's ruling.

🧵 Examination Cloths: Permitted vs. Forbidden Materials

❌ Forbidden (School of Menashe)

  • Red cloth — blood not visible against red background
  • Black cloth — blood not visible against dark background
  • Raw flax — coarse texture obscures blood
  • Any cloth that prevents clear visual detection

✅ Permitted / Praised

  • White linen — best visibility; standard material
  • Wool of Parhava — used by Household of King Munbaz; Sages praised them
  • Worn-out flax garments (Rava) — soft texture differs from raw flax; permitted
  • Distinction: raw flax ≠ flax garments (worn down by use)

🩸 Blood in the Corridor: Dispute Among the Amoraim

Mishna: Blood found in the פְּרוֹזְדוֹר (corridor/vagina) is uncertain — may come from the uterus (impure) or bladder (pure). Despite uncertainty, it is treated as impure.
↓
Rav Huna: From vestibule inward → definitely impure (presumed uterine). From vestibule outward → uncertain, but treated as impure by rabbinic decree.
↓
Abaye's challenge: Why is outward blood only uncertain? If we fear blood moved because the woman bent forward or backward, then both directions should be equally uncertain!
↓
Abaye's conclusion: Either apply the concern consistently (both directions = uncertain/impure) OR apply the presumption consistently (inner = definitely impure, outer = definitely pure). There is no middle ground.
↓
Rabbi Ḥiyya: Blood found in the corridor is definite menstrual blood — intercourse involving such blood carries karet liability, not merely rabbinic prohibition.
