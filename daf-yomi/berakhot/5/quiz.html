QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the accepted halakha, what is the ruling for a woman WITH a fixed menstrual cycle (בעלת וסת) who discovers blood?
A. She is impure retroactively for the full 24-hour period
B. She is impure only from the moment of discovery — 'her time is sufficient'
C. She must wait until she consults a Sage before any impurity applies
D. She is completely pure unless two witnesses confirm the blood
Correct: B — The Gemara accepts Rabbi Dosa's lenient ruling: a woman with a fixed cycle is only impure from the moment she discovers blood — דייה שעתה, 'her time is sufficient.' There is no retroactive impurity for her.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Shmuel rule that examination cloths used DURING intercourse do NOT constitute a valid examination?
A. The cloths are considered ritually impure before use and cannot serve as a bedikah
B. The woman is too excited (מהומה) and does not insert the cloth deeply enough
C. Intercourse itself invalidates any examination performed at the same time
D. Only a woman who is alone and undistracted can perform a valid examination
Correct: B — Rav Ketina explains that the woman's excitement and haste during intercourse causes her not to insert the cloth deeply enough. A valid bedikah requires thorough insertion, which she does not perform under these conditions.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Hanina ben Antigonus, what is the ruling for blood STAINS found on women whose 'time is sufficient'?
A. Their blood stains cause impurity retroactively, unlike their flow
B. Their blood stains cause impurity only from the point of discovery, just like their flow
C. Their blood stains are always ritually pure and require no concern
D. Their blood stains require a special rabbinic ruling before any impurity applies
Correct: B — Rabbi Hanina ben Antigonus rules that for these women, blood stains (כתמים) share the same ruling as their sighting — just as their menstrual flow is only impure from the moment of discovery, so too are their blood stains. The leniency is consistent across both.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rabbi Yohanan's ruling regarding uncertain ritual impurity (ספק טומאה) that arises through human involvement?
A. It is always pure, since halakhic doubt defaults to leniency
B. It is pure if the doubt arose in a public domain
C. It is treated as impure — the owner must consult a Sage
D. It depends on whether the person acted intentionally or accidentally
Correct: C — Rabbi Yohanan rules that when doubt about impurity arises through a person's own actions, the item is treated as impure and the owner must consult a Sage. This contrasts with doubt arising without human involvement (e.g., in the public domain), which is treated as pure.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rava resolve the apparent contradiction between Ze'eiri (bed = first-degree impurity) and the baraita brought by Avimi from Bei Ḥozai?
A. Rava rules that the baraita is simply incorrect and Ze'eiri's ruling stands alone
B. Rava reads the baraita as denying 'father of impurity' status (אב) but accepting first-degree — harmonizing both views
C. Rava says the 24-hour retroactive period never affects beds or chairs at all
D. Rava limits Ze'eiri's ruling to cases where the woman was in a private domain
Correct: B — Rava uses an a fortiori (קל וחומר) argument: if a sealed earthenware vessel still becomes impure despite its protection, a bed the woman lay on surely does too. He then reconciles Ze'eiri and the baraita: both agree the bed reaches first-degree impurity; the baraita only denies it becomes an אב הטומאה (father of impurity), the highest tier.
