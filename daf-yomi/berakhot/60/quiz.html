Berakhot 60 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, why does Rabbi Meir permit attributing a blood stain to any gentile woman?
A. Gentile women are entirely exempt from the laws of niddah
B. All gentile women are presumed to experience blood regularly
C. The borrower is always responsible for stains on borrowed garments
D. Gentile women must immerse before returning garments, removing any stain
Correct: B — Rava explains that Rabbi Meir is being lenient: since gentile women are presumed to experience blood regularly, any gentile borrower is a plausible source for the stain — no specific history is required.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rabbi Neḥemya's ruling about a blood stain found on the exterior of an inverted earthenware vessel?
A. The woman is impure, since earthenware always transmits impurity
B. The woman is pure, since the exterior of earthenware is not susceptible to impurity
C. She must wait three days and examine herself before a ruling can be issued
D. It depends on whether the vessel was used in a public or private space
Correct: B — Rabbi Neḥemya's principle: anything not susceptible to ritual impurity cannot render a woman impure from a blood stain. Earthenware becomes impure only when something impure enters its airspace — the exterior is not susceptible.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Three women sleep in one bed; blood is found beneath one of them. One woman examines herself and is found impure (menstruant). What is the ruling?
A. All three remain impure since the blood could have come from any of them
B. Only the one who examined herself is impure; the other two are pure
C. All three must examine themselves before a ruling is possible
D. The one who examined herself is pure; the other two are impure
Correct: B — The mishna rules: if one woman examines herself and is found impure, she is impure and the other two are pure — the blood is attributed to her, exonerating the others.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
To what case does Rav Ḥisda compare the dispute between Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel and Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi about attributing blood stains?
A. Two women who both experienced blood on the same night
B. One impure and one pure person who each walked one of two paths — one pure and one impure
C. A zava and a niddah who shared the same ritual bath on the same day
D. A woman who borrowed a garment but forgot to return it before her period
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda says the dispute mirrors a case of two travelers — one impure, one pure — who each took one of two paths, one pure and one impure, with neither knowing which path they took.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If one of two women sharing a bed is nursing and the other is not nursing, how does the baraita rule regarding attribution of the blood stain?
A. The nursing woman is automatically the source since her body is still active post-birth
B. The nursing woman may attribute the blood to the non-nursing woman
C. Both women are equally suspect and both remain impure
D. The nursing woman is fully exempt since nursing completely suppresses menstruation
Correct: B — The baraita rules that the nursing woman may attribute the blood to the non-nursing woman, since the non-nursing woman is more likely to be menstruating — nursing suppresses regular menstrual flow.
