⚖️ Core Debates of Niddah 43

Abaye
The priest holds his penis with a coarse cloth — no direct skin contact, so the emission cannot be increased.
Rava
Even a soft cloth is fine. When limbs are already quaking, the emission is inevitable — holding cannot increase what is already coming.
Shmuel
Semen not felt by the entire body — or not shot like an arrow — does not render one impure (Lev. 15:16: "flow of seed").
Rava
Dilemma: semen was uprooted with sensation but emerged without sensation — do we follow the moment of uprooting or of emission?
Daf Theme Niddah 43 probes the precise moment impurity takes hold — uprooting vs. emission — and the minimum thresholds for zav and semen impurity across a chain of tannaitic and amoraic disputes.

🪵 Zav with Pole in Body Folds (Reish Lakish)

Zav places a pole or stick in his body folds
↓
Zav moves pure person WITH the pole
No impurity transmitted. Movement-impurity of a zav is compared to his hands — folds are not hands
Pure person moves Zav WITH the pole
Pure person becomes impure. Being moved by the zav's weight/force still triggers impurity
↓
Source: "Without having rinsed his hands" (Lev. 15:11) — the Torah equates zav movement-impurity to hand-touch: must flow through his own hands, not objects stored in body folds

🫳 Priest Sensing Emission While Eating Teruma

Mishna
A priest eating teruma who feels his limbs quaking should grasp his penis to prevent emission and swallow the teruma rather than spit it out.
Objection
Isn't holding the penis like placing a finger in one's eye — it increases the emission! How can the mishna permit this?
Abaye
The mishna speaks of holding with a coarse cloth. Coarse fabric creates a barrier that does not stimulate and therefore does not increase the emission.
Rava
You may say even a soft cloth. The concern of increased emission only applies to drops without quaking. When limbs shake, the emission will come regardless — holding changes nothing.
Baraita Support Rava's logic: 'finger in the eye' only increases when the situation is uncertain. Quaking limbs signal the emission is already physiologically determined — intervention cannot worsen it.

🏹 Shmuel's Two Versions on Semen Impurity

Version 1: Felt by Entire Body

  • Semen not felt by the entire body does not render one impure
  • Source: "The flow of seed" (Lev. 15:16) — must travel like a flowing stream through the whole body
  • Objection: One who awoke to warm flesh is impure (Mikvaot 8:3) — he felt nothing!
  • Answer: He felt the uprooting at the moment it began, even if not at final emission

Version 2: Shot Like an Arrow

  • Semen not shot like an arrow does not render one impure
  • Practical difference: uprooted with sensation, emitted without — Version 1 = impure; Version 2 = pure
  • Also applied to fertilization: semen not shot like an arrow cannot impregnate (but may still render impure per Version 1)
  • This difference is Rava's unresolved dilemma: which moment controls?

⚖️ Rava's Chain of Unresolved Dilemmas

Core Question: Is impurity determined at the moment of uprooting (הינתק) or at the moment of emission (יציאה)?
↓
Dilemma 1 — Jewish male: Semen uprooted with sensation but emitted without — impure or pure? Proof from Mikvaot 8:3 attempted but inconclusive.
↓
Dilemma 2 — Gentile male converting: Semen uprooted before conversion, emitted after — is he impure? Even if we follow uprooting, does that stringency apply to a gentile becoming Jewish?
↓
Dilemma 3 — Jewish zava: Urine uprooted while she was a zava, emitted after she became pure — do we follow uprooting (impure) or emission (pure)?
↓
Dilemma 4 — Gentile zava converting: Urine uprooted as a gentile (not Torah-impure), she converts — is she impure by rabbinic law? Does rabbinic zava status survive conversion?
↓
All four dilemmas remain unresolved (תיקו) — the daf leaves the question of uprooting vs. emission open.
