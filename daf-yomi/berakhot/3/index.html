⚖️ Why Is a Woman Not Retroactively Impure?

Rabbis
Derive ritual bath uncertainty from sota: just as doubt about sota = definite impurity, so too ritual bath doubt = retroactively impure
R. Shimon
Also derives from sota — but the public/private domain distinction applies: public domain doubt = pure, private domain = definitely impure
Gemara
Alternative for R. Shimon: derive end-of-impurity rules from beginning-of-impurity rules — same public/private logic applies throughout
Rava
Shammai's true reason: retroactive impurity would cause dereliction of p'ru u'rvu — a scrupulous husband would never engage in intercourse
Core Dispute Multiple reasons are offered for Shammai's ruling that a woman is only impure from the moment she discovers her flow. The Gemara explores which reason is primary and what practical differences exist between them.

🔄 Sota Analogy: Rabbis vs. Rabbi Shimon

Rabbis

  • Ritual bath ~ sota: doubt treated as definite impurity
  • Both public AND private domain: doubt = retroactively impure
  • Cannot distinguish domains — sota rule is unique to her seclusion
  • Objection: if impure anywhere, why not pure in public domain? → Sota's impurity stems from seclusion, not location

Rabbi Shimon

  • Ritual bath ~ sota: public domain doubt = pure
  • Private domain doubt = definitely impure (like sota who secluded herself)
  • Alternatively: derives from 'beginning of impurity' — same public/private logic
  • Objection answered: sota in private has a 'basis' (husband's warning + seclusion) which ordinary cases lack

📋 Four Reasons for Shammai's Ruling — עונתה די לה

Reason 1 — Sota Analogy (Public Domain): Just as a sota's doubt in a public domain renders her pure, a woman's menstrual uncertainty is also resolved leniently — she is pure retroactively
↓
Reason 2 — Bodily Sensation (Abaye's version): A woman senses her menstrual flow within herself. If she felt it only now, she had no prior flow. Even a sleeping woman would wake from discomfort.
↓
Reason 3 — Blood Would Have Flowed Out (Rava's version): If blood had existed earlier it would have already emerged. Even a contraceptive cloth does not hermetically seal the womb — prior blood escapes naturally.
↓
Reason 4 — P'ru U'rvu / פרו ורבו (Rava's primary reason): Declaring women retroactively impure would cause widespread dereliction of the mitzva to be fruitful and multiply. A scrupulous husband would never risk intercourse.
↓
Practical Divergence: The three reasons yield different outcomes for: sleeping women, mentally incompetent women, women using contraceptive cloths, and blood stains found after the fact.

👥 Shammai and Hillel — Can Retroactive Impurity Be a Safeguard?

Hillel
Don't you concede that pure items in a basket containing a creeping animal are retroactively impure? So too a woman who menstruates should be deemed impure retroactively!
Shammai
The basket is different — a creeping animal can lurk unnoticed. But prior menstrual blood would have already flowed out. Your comparison fails.
Hillel
Granted your reasoning has merit — but shouldn't we impose retroactive impurity as a rabbinic safeguard (גזירה) anyway?
Shammai
Such a safeguard must not be enacted! If it were, the heart of a scrupulous husband might turn away from intercourse entirely — causing him to fail the mitzva of p'ru u'rvu.
Key Principle — פרו ורבו The obligation to be fruitful and multiply carries such weight that even well-intentioned rabbinic safeguards may not be enacted if they would practically prevent couples from fulfilling it.
