Berakhot 59 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yannai, what is the status of a woman whose stain is slightly larger than a split bean when a squashed louse is found in the extra portion?
A. She is impure — the stain exceeds the maximum size attributable to any louse
B. She is pure — a louse actually present on the stain permits attribution of the full stain
C. She is impure unless she was also occupied with bird blood at the same time
D. The matter is unresolved and she must consult a rabbinic authority
Correct: B — Rabbi Yannai rules pure: the limitation (attributing at most a bean-sized stain to a louse) applies only when no louse is actually found. With a squashed louse on the stain, the woman may attribute the entire stain — including what exceeds a bean — to that louse.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rava rule about a woman who was occupied with a single type of staining agent, such as sycamore sap?
A. She may only attribute stains of the exact same color as the agent to it
B. She may attribute stains of several different types and colors to that single source
C. She may not attribute any stain unless she can prove she touched the agent moments before
D. She must wait and see whether a second stain appears before attributing to the agent
Correct: B — Rava rules that occupation with one type of staining agent allows the woman to attribute multiple stains — even of varying colors or types — to that single source. This broadens the grounds for leniency beyond what a strict reading might suggest.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Meir, what is the ruling when a woman urinates while standing and blood is found in the stream?
A. She is pure — the blood may have come from a wound along the urinary tract
B. She is impure — the stream separates from the body and blood visible in it is presumed uterine
C. She is pure only if the blood is black rather than red
D. She is impure only if a man also urinated into the same basin
Correct: B — Rabbi Meir rules impure when standing: once the urine stream separates from the body, blood observed within it is presumed to have originated from the uterus. When sitting, the stream remains in contact with the body, allowing attribution to a wound.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Three women wear the same garment in succession. The garment was clean before the first woman wore it. Blood is found after the third woman removes it. What is the ruling?
A. Only the third woman is impure, because she was the last to wear it
B. Only the first woman is impure, because she wore it closest in time to when it was verified clean
C. All three women are impure
D. The garment is impure but none of the women are rendered impure
Correct: C — All three women are impure. Since the stain cannot be attributed to any one woman in particular, all three bear the status of impurity. This stringent ruling prevents the institution of blood stain impurity from being undermined by shared-use scenarios.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Whose opinion does Shmuel rule is the halakha regarding blood found in a basin after both a man and a woman urinate into it?
A. Rabbi Meir — she is impure even under compound uncertainty
B. Rabbi Shimon — the blood is presumptively from the woman
C. Rabbi Yosei — she is pure
D. Rabbi Ḥanina — the external cause (the man's urine) permits attribution
Correct: C — Shmuel rules that the halakha follows Rabbi Yosei, who deems the woman pure. Even in a case of compound uncertainty — whether the blood is from the man or woman, and whether from a wound or the uterus — Rabbi Yosei rules leniently. Rabbi Abba likewise ruled accordingly in practice.
