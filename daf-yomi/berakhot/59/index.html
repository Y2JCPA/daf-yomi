🩸 Niddah 59 — Stains, Urine, and Shared Garments

כתם שנמצא על האשה — נידה נט
Core Theme Daf 59 examines two interlocking domains: (1) when a woman may attribute a blood stain to an external cause — a louse, bird blood, eye salve — rather than uterine bleeding, and (2) when blood found in urine or on a shared garment renders a woman impure. Major Amoraic disputes weigh external explanations against the presumptive uterine origin of blood. The daf culminates in the famous case of three women sharing one garment.

⚖️ The Squashed Louse: Rabbi Ḥanina vs Rabbi Yannai

Rabbi Ḥanina
If a stain is a split bean and slightly more, and a squashed louse is found in the extra portion — she is impure. The louse explains only the extra portion beyond the bean; it cannot retroactively account for the bean-sized core of the stain.
Rabbi Yannai
She is pure. The rule capping louse attribution at bean-size applies only when no louse is actually present. Where a louse is genuinely squashed on the stain, we attribute the entire stain — including what exceeds a bean — to that louse.
Rabbi Yirmeya's Dilemma
What if she was occupied with bird blood or eye salve exactly the size of a bean, and the stain found is slightly more than a bean? Does Rabbi Ḥanina rule impure here too, or does the external item provide sufficient attribution for the excess — even without a louse?
Practical Ruling A stain ≤ a split bean may always be attributed to a louse without one being present. For stains exceeding a bean: Rabbi Yannai permits attribution when a louse is actually squashed on the stain; Rabbi Ḥanina rules impure regardless.

🚿 Blood in Urine: Three Amoraic Opinions

Rabbi Meir

  • Standing → Impure: stream separates from body; blood at top presumed uterine
  • Sitting → Pure: stream flows along body; blood attributable to a wound
  • Man + woman in same basin: Impure (Reish Lakish: no difference for compound uncertainty)
  • Rabbi Yoḥanan disagrees: Meir rules impure only for single uncertainty

Rabbi Yosei

  • Always Pure in a one-uncertainty case: blood may be from a wound
  • Pure even when man + woman urinate into same basin
  • Halakha follows Rabbi Yosei (Shmuel; Rabbi Abba ruling for Kala)
  • Two uncertainties only strengthen the leniency

Rabbi Shimon

  • Man + woman in basin → Impure: men do not typically discharge blood
  • Presumptive status: all blood is from the woman
  • Standing vs sitting makes no difference — blood is presumed uterine
  • Baraita: woman sitting may attribute to wound only if she is not standing

👗 Three Women, One Garment — Shared Stain Attribution

Setup: A garment is examined before the first of three women wears it — found completely clean
↓
Use: Three women wear the garment in succession, one after another
↓
Discovery: After the third woman removes it, a blood stain is found on the garment
↓
Ruling: All three women are impure — the stain cannot be attributed to any specific woman
↓
Contrast — Lent garment: A woman who lends her garment to a gentile or menstruating woman and finds a stain after its return may attribute it to the borrower → remains pure
↓
Stone or bathhouse bench: Cannot itself become ritually impure, but blood found on it still renders the women who sat on it impure — bench cannot serve as a buffer
