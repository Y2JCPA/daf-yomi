QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to both Abaye and Rava, what is the halakhic status of a woman who discharges semen?
A. She is considered as one who came into contact with impurity (נוגעת)
B. She is considered as one who experienced an emission of impurity (מקור טומאה)
C. She has no impurity status since the semen originated from the man
D. Her status depends on whether she immersed within three days
Correct: B — Both Abaye and Rava rule she has the status of one who experienced an emission — this affects three halakhot: it negates her seven clean days, any amount renders her impure, and she renders impure through carrying.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the key practical difference between Abaye's and Rava's positions on the status of the vagina?
A. Whether a woman must immerse after menstruation
B. Whether another woman inserting an olive-bulk of animal carcass into her vagina renders her impure
C. Whether a man who has relations with a niddah is impure
D. Whether she must count seven clean days after semen discharge
Correct: B — The practical case: if a woman inserts an olive-bulk of animal carcass into another woman's vagina — Abaye (encapsulated) says she is impure; Rava (concealed) says she is pure, since impurity in a concealed space does not transfer.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why is a woman after childbirth who sees blood on her descent to immerse rendered impure, according to Rabbi Avin?
A. Because her immersion was invalid due to the presence of blood
B. Because the blood was already technically outside her body before immersion
C. The Sages gave this blood the status of an unslaughtered bird carcass, which imparts impurity even while still inside the body
D. Because she descended before her days of impurity were complete
Correct: C — Rabbi Avin explains that the Sages made a special rabbinic decree likening this blood to an unslaughtered bird carcass (נבלת עוף טהור), which has the unique property of rendering the swallower impure even while still enclosed inside — impurity from a concealed space.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rava, what is the halakhic status of a person who has animal carcass flesh (נבלה) in the fold of their body?
A. Pure — the fold is a concealed space not susceptible to impurity
B. Pure — only a creeping animal can render impure in a concealed space
C. Impure — animal carcass imparts impurity through carrying (משא), which applies even in a concealed fold
D. Impure — but only if the fold is visible to others
Correct: C — Rava distinguishes: a creeping animal (שרץ) in a fold → pure, because שרץ imparts impurity through contact and concealed parts are not susceptible. But animal carcass (נבלה) → impure, because נבלה imparts impurity through carrying (משא), and the body carries it even in a fold.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What was the core of Rav Shmuel bar Bisna's dilemma, as resolved by the Gemara?
A. He was unsure whether semen could ever render a woman impure according to the Torah
B. He was asking specifically according to Rabbi Shimon's opinion — whether Rabbi Shimon's leniency extended to the woman's full emission-status or only to her immersion timing
C. He was unsure whether the laws of Sinai-era instructions applied to future generations
D. He was debating whether a zava or niddah is more stringent
Correct: B — The Gemara resolves the apparent obviousness of the question: Rav Shmuel bar Bisna was asking specifically according to Rabbi Shimon, who says the woman is like the man. The dilemma: does this mean only same immersion timing (lenient reading) or full emission status (stringent reading)? Abaye clarifies: only immersion timing.
