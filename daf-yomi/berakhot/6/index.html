🩸 Sees Blood vs. Sees a Stain — Detailed Comparison

Sees Menstrual Blood (ראייה)

  • Retroactive period: 24 hours (מעת לעת)
  • Renders impure: beds, chairs, food, drink
  • Earthenware vessel (כלי חרס): NOT retroactively impure
  • Teruma touched: suspended — not eaten, not burned
  • Rav Huna: 24-hr rule applies to kodashim only

Sees Blood Stain (כתם)

  • Retroactive period: to last garment inspection
  • Renders impure: food, drink, beds, chairs
  • Earthenware vessel: IS retroactively impure
  • Teruma touched: suspended — not eaten, not burned
  • Stricter than blood sighting in the earthenware rule

⚖️ Rav Huna's Disputed Ruling on Retroactive Impurity

Rav Huna
The 24-hour retroactive impurity applies only to sacrificial food (קדשים) — it does not apply to teruma!
Gemara Challenge
The baraita says: 'What does she render impure? Food and drink' — doesn't 'food and drink' include teruma? And the mishna in Ḥagiga implies teruma is affected!
Rav Huna (via Ulla)
Ḥaverim in the Galilee purify their wine and oil to the standards of sacrificial food, hoping the Temple will be rebuilt. The baraita refers to this food — non-sacred food at kodashim-level purity.
Rav Shmuel bar Rav Yitzḥak
Received from Rav Naḥman: The baraita's 'food and drink' means non-sacred food prepared to the purity standards of sacrificial food (חולין שנעשו על טהרת קדש) — confirming Rav Huna.
✅ Resolution 'Food and drink' in the baraita = non-sacred food treated at the level of kodashim purity, not ordinary teruma. Rav Huna is vindicated.

📜 Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi & Rabbi Eliezer — Ruling in Doubt

Event: Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi ruled leniently on retroactive impurity, following Rabbi Eliezer's minority opinion against the majority Sages
↓
Discovery: He later 'remembered' something that troubled him about this ruling
↓
What did he remember? NOT that the law follows the Rabbis — the law was actually undecided between Rabbi Eliezer and the Rabbis
↓
He had forgotten that a dispute even existed, and had ruled without acknowledging it — now he realized he had acted without full knowledge
↓
His response: 'Rabbi Eliezer is worthy (כדאי) to rely upon in exigent circumstances (בשעת הדחק)' — the ruling stands
↓
Proof relevance: This case involved food at sacrificial purity level, not teruma — consistent with Rav Huna (since teruma still existed in his day)

🍷 Bridging Kodashim and Teruma — Ulla's Teaching

חברים מטהרים יינם ושמנם בגליל על טהרת קדש — Ulla
🍷 Ḥaverim in the Galilee Ulla taught: Ḥaverim (scrupulously observant Jews) in the Galilee would purify their wine and oil to the standards of sacrificial food (קדש), hoping the Temple would be rebuilt so these items could be offered. Why this matters: The baraita's 'food and drink' rendered retroactively impure by the 24-hour rule refers to this wine and oil — not ordinary teruma. This supports Rav Huna's position that retroactive impurity applies to kodashim-level food, not teruma. Practical consequence: Such food was common in the Galilee, making this a real halakhic category that the Sages needed to address — not a theoretical construct.
