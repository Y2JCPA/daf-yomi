5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
For how long does seeing menstrual blood create retroactive impurity?
A. Back to the last time she examined herself
B. Twenty-four hours (מעת לעת)
C. Back to the last time she immersed in a mikveh
D. Back to when her garment was last checked
Correct: B — A woman who sees menstrual blood renders impure retroactively for a twenty-four-hour period (מעת לעת), as taught in the baraita on 6a. A blood stain, by contrast, is retroactive only to the last garment check.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the halakhic status of teruma that a menstruating woman touched during her 24-hour retroactive period?
A. It is definitely impure and must be burned immediately
B. It may still be eaten, since her impurity is only rabbinic
C. It is suspended — neither eaten nor burned
D. It becomes permissible if a second opinion holds it was not touched
Correct: C — Teruma touched during the retroactive period is 'suspended' (תולין): it cannot be eaten because impure teruma is forbidden, but it is also not burned since the impurity is only doubtful, not certain.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, to what does the 24-hour retroactive impurity of a menstruating woman apply?
A. To teruma only, but not sacrificial food
B. To both sacrificial food and teruma equally
C. To non-sacred food only, not any sacred items
D. To sacrificial food (kodashim) but not to teruma
Correct: D — Rav Huna holds the 24-hour retroactive impurity applies only to sacrificial food (קדשים) — and by extension, non-sacred food treated at kodashim-level purity — but NOT to ordinary teruma.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Ulla teach about ḥaverim in the Galilee, and why is it relevant to Rav Huna's opinion?
A. They never produce teruma, so all their food is non-sacred
B. They purify their wine and oil to the standards of sacrificial food purity
C. They follow Rabbi Eliezer's lenient rulings exclusively
D. They burn suspected-impure food immediately to avoid doubt
Correct: B — Ulla taught that ḥaverim in the Galilee purified their wine and oil to the standards of kodashim, hoping for the Temple's rebuilding. This explains how the baraita's 'food and drink' refers to kodashim-level non-sacred food — not ordinary teruma — thus supporting Rav Huna.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What did Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi 'remember' that troubled him after ruling in accordance with Rabbi Eliezer?
A. That the definitive law follows the Rabbis against Rabbi Eliezer
B. That he had previously ruled the opposite way in a similar case
C. That the matter was disputed and no definitive ruling had been established
D. That Rabbi Eliezer himself had retracted his opinion
Correct: C — He remembered that the halakha had NOT been definitively established — neither for Rabbi Eliezer nor for the Rabbis. Having ruled without acknowledging this unresolved dispute, he then justified the ruling by saying Rabbi Eliezer is worthy to rely upon in exigent circumstances (בשעת הדחק).
