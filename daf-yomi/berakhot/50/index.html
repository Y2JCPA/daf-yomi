⚖️ Bird Carcass Impurity: Is Intent Required?

R. Yochanan ben Nuri
A kal va-chomer: If the carcass of a kosher bird transmits severe impurity (טומאה חמורה) without any intent, surely it transmits lenient food impurity without intent too — the lighter case cannot be stricter!
The Rabbis
Your inference is a pircha (refutation). Severe impurity requires no intent precisely because it does not assume its status via thought. Lenient food impurity does assume its status via thought — so intent is required there.
R. Yochanan ben Nuri
The chicken in Yavne proves intent is not the decisive factor! The Sages declared it impure by giving intent after it became a carcass — even though the lenient impurity required thought, post-mortem intent was accepted.
The Rabbis
Agreed — intent may be given after death. But intent is still required for lenient food impurity. The chicken in Yavne confirms this; it does not eliminate the requirement. Your kal va-chomer remains invalid.
📌 Core Distinction Severe impurity (טומאה חמורה) of a bird carcass — rendering garments impure when swallowed — needs no intent. Lenient food impurity needs intent, but that intent may be formed after detachment or death.

👁️ Blind Judges: Rabbi Meir's Gezeira Shava from Leprous Marks

Rabbi Meir
Deuteronomy 21:5 juxtaposes disputes and leprous marks. Just as leprous marks require a seeing priest — 'as appears to the priest' — so too, disputes require judges who can see. The blind may not adjudicate.
Baraita Extension
Going further: just as disputes require three judges, perhaps leprous marks require three priests for inspection? The gezeira shava cuts in both directions.
Rabbi Yochanan
I permitted my blind neighbor to render monetary judgments. Although I rule that the blind cannot testify, the unattributed mishna in Sanhedrin implies blind judges may rule — and that mishna is primary in its own tractate.
Gemara Reasoning
When two unattributed mishnayot conflict, we follow the one that is primary to its own tractate's subject. Sanhedrin's mishna on daytime judging is that tractate's core topic — it outweighs the Niddah mishna on this point.
🔍 Halakhic Principle A stam mishna (unattributed ruling) carries greater weight when it appears in the tractate where its topic is primary — stam k'rabbi meir contextual weighting.

🌾 Three Obligations Compared: Tithes, Pe'a & Food Impurity

All Three Apply

  • Grain (wheat, barley)
  • Legumes
  • Grapes, olives
  • Garlic & onions (stored)

Tithes Only (no Pe'a)

  • Figs
  • Vegetables (yerakot)
  • Endives (ulshin)
  • Most garden produce

Food Impurity Only

  • Meat
  • Fish
  • Eggs
  • (no tithes, no pe'a)

Exempt from All Three

  • Woad — isatis tinctoria (setim)
  • Safflower — dye plant (kotza)
  • Sefihin used as dyes
  • Not considered human food

⚖️ R. Yochanan ben Nuri's Argument — Step by Step

Premise: A kosher bird carcass transmits severe impurity (טומאה חמורה) — it renders a person's garments impure when an olive-bulk is swallowed — and this impurity applies with no requirement of intent whatsoever.
↓
The Kal Va-Chomer: If the more stringent form of impurity (severe) requires no intent, it follows logically that the less stringent form (lenient food impurity) should also require no intent. The lesser case cannot be stricter than the greater.
↓
The Rabbis' Pircha (Refutation): The argument collapses because its premise is self-defeating. Severe impurity requires no intent precisely because it does not assume its status through thought. Lenient food impurity does assume status through thought — making them fundamentally different categories.
↓
Counter-Proof (R. Yochanan ben Nuri): The chicken in Yavne demonstrates that even when intent is required, it may be given after the animal dies. So a bird that fell into a winepress (intended for a Samaritan's meal) can receive valid post-mortem intent and become impure.
↓
Final Resolution: Intent is required for lenient food impurity — but it may be formed after detachment or death. The winepress case is special: the press made the bird disgusting (נפסל), stripping its food status, so fresh intent was needed to re-establish it as food.
