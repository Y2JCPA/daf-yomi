Berakhot 54 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
In the 2:2 alternating pattern (2 impure, 2 pure), on which days is the woman permitted to have intercourse with her husband?
A. Days 7 and 11
B. Days 8 and 12
C. Days 9 and 13
D. Days 8, 12, and 19
Correct: B — She completes her seven niddah days on day 7, making day 8 the first permitted day. Day 12 is permitted after she has observed sufficient clean ziva days. Day 19 is omitted because her pattern causes bleeding that day regardless.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rav Ashi say day 19 in the 2:2 pattern is NOT rabbinically forbidden?
A. Because she has already completed seven clean days before day 19
B. Because day 19 is already within her new menstrual-day period, not merely its eve
C. Because three consecutive clean days precede day 19
D. Because the rabbinic decree only applies during the eleven ziva days
Correct: B — Rav Ashi explains that the rabbinic prohibition targets the eve of ziva days. Day 19, however, already falls within the start of her new menstrual-day cycle — so no decree applies. The real reason it is impure is simply her biological pattern.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When a woman who saw blood on two consecutive ziva days then miscarries on day 3 without knowing what she miscarried — what is done with her korban (offering)?
A. She brings two offerings: one for ziva and one for childbirth
B. She is fully exempt from any offering
C. She brings one offering but it is not eaten by the priests
D. She waits thirty days and then brings a standard sin-offering
Correct: C — Due to the uncertainty — is she a zava, a yoledet, or neither? — she brings one offering, but the priests may not eat it since its sacred status is in doubt. It cannot be determined whether the offering is for ziva, for childbirth, or is entirely non-sacred.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Mishna (54b), which substances transmit impurity ONLY when moist, NOT when dry?
A. Blood of a niddah and flesh of a corpse
B. Ziva discharge of a zav, his spittle, and his urine
C. Any menstrual blood that has clotted on a garment
D. The saliva of a zava during her days of impurity
Correct: B — The Mishna draws a precise distinction: blood of a niddah and corpse flesh transmit impurity even when dry. But the ziva discharge, spittle, and urine of a zav — and the doubtful blood of a zava — transmit impurity only while moist.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does the Gemara's baraita derive from the term 'the zav' (הַזָּב) in Leviticus 15:4?
A. That a zav's impurity is more severe than that of a menstruating woman
B. That only the zav — and not a stone afflicted with leprosy — renders lying and sitting items impure
C. That a zav must immerse in a mikveh before touching any utensil
D. That a leprous stone transmits the same impurity grade as a zav
Correct: B — The baraita uses the definite article 'the zav' as an exclusion: only the zav himself renders lying/sitting items impure with the full force that transmits impurity to persons. A stone afflicted with leprosy, despite its severe impurity, does not produce this effect — and neither does menstrual blood.
