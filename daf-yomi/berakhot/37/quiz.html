QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye's final ruling (מקובלני), does blood from labor pains negate a woman's count of seven clean days?
A. Yes — it imparts impurity just like regular ziva blood
B. No — we have a tradition it does not negate the count, per the Rabbis
C. Only if the labor pains last more than twenty-four hours
D. It depends on whether she is in the eleven-day ziva period or the seven-day menstrual period
Correct: B — Abaye states explicitly: 'מקובלני — we have a tradition that blood from labor pains does not negate the seven clean days,' ruling in accordance with the Rabbis against the minority view.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rava (לאו) counter Abaye's proof from ziva emissions 'beyond one's control' (אונס)?
A. Such emissions are entirely ignored by halakha and do not impart any impurity
B. They only apply to women, not men who are counting for zavut
C. Even involuntary ziva emissions count toward the three needed to become a full zav — so they do contribute to zavut
D. Rabbi Eliezer explicitly rules that involuntary emissions never negate the count
Correct: C — Rava argues from Zavim 2:2: even a first or second involuntary sighting counts toward the three needed for full zavut. Therefore such emissions do have a classification-creating potential, unlike pure labor blood.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rabbi Marinus's ruling about a zava who gives birth in the middle of her seven clean days?
A. She must restart her entire count from the beginning
B. The birth negates her count and she becomes a full zava
C. The birth does not negate her count — she continues from where she left off
D. She must wait until after her postpartum impurity before resuming the count
Correct: C — Rabbi Marinus rules that birth mid-count does not negate the seven clean days. Rava supports this from Lev. 15:28, deriving that counting begins from cessation of ziva and is not interrupted by birth.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the parable of the king and soldiers, what is Rabbi Ḥanina's conclusion when a woman rests from labor pains but blood CONTINUES?
A. The continued blood is now ziva blood — she is impure
B. The blood was from labor pains (soldiers precede the king) — she is pure from zavut
C. She must wait another twenty-four hours before a ruling can be made
D. She must bring an offering to determine her status
Correct: B — Rabbi Ḥanina reasons: the soldiers (blood) preceded the king (birth), indicating the blood was in service of the birth. When both arrived and now both are gone, the blood was clearly labor-pain blood, and she is pure.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rava, why must the days of birth be counted toward the seven clean days?
A. Because birth is halakhically more severe than ziva and overrides the count
B. Because the Rabbis enacted a rabbinic ordinance including birth days
C. Because Lev. 15:28 requires the seven days to be consecutive — excluding birth days would create an impurity gap breaking the sequence
D. Because Rabbi Eliezer holds that birth and ziva share the same impurity status
Correct: C — Rava derives from Lev. 15:28 that the seven days must be uninterrupted. If birth days didn't count, they would constitute an impurity separating between clean days, violating the requirement for an unbroken sequence of seven clean days.
