Berakhot 25 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Rabbi Yishmael son of Rabbi Yosei quoted his father: a gestational sac full of blood confers what type of impurity?
A. Impurity of a woman after childbirth (birth-impurity)
B. Impurity of a menstruating woman (niddah)
C. No impurity whatsoever
D. Seven-day impurity regardless of content
Correct: B — Rabbi Yosei ruled a sac full of blood gives menstrual (niddah) impurity — not birth-impurity. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi objected that this aligned with Sumakhos, a lone minority opinion, making it unreliable.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman citing Rabba bar Avuh, when does the dispute between Rabbi Yehoshua and the Rabbis about a gestational sac without tissue apply?
A. When the sac is full of flesh
B. When the sac has a visible face-shape
C. When the amniotic fluid inside is turbid
D. When the amniotic fluid inside is clear
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman holds the dispute applies only when the fluid is turbid — turbidity suggests an embryo dissolved inside, giving Rabbi Yehoshua reason to treat it as offspring. When the fluid is clear, everyone agrees it is not an offspring.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
The Gemara rules that an embryo should be examined with oil rather than water. What is the reason given?
A. Oil preserves the embryo's color better for examination
B. Water is too strong and dissolves the embryo
C. Oil is ritually pure while standing water may be impure
D. Water causes the embryo to swell and distort its shape
Correct: B — The Gemara states explicitly that water is too strong (חָזָק) and dissolves the embryo, making examination impossible. Oil is soft, cleans the embryo gently, and allows features — including sex — to be discerned.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Using Abba Shaul's wooden sliver method (moved from below to above), what does a smooth, unobstructed movement indicate?
A. The embryo is female
B. The embryo is male
C. The sex cannot be determined at this stage
D. The embryo lacks offspring status entirely
Correct: B — Abba Shaul: if the sliver is caught (not smooth) during upward movement, the embryo is female — the vaginal groove grips it. If it moves smoothly, the embryo is male. Rav Naḥman adds the direction must be from below to above, not the reverse.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What feature must a sandal fetus have, according to Rav Yehuda in the name of Shmuel, in order to confer birth-impurity status on the mother?
A. It must have the shape of arms and legs
B. It must be at least the size of a lentil
C. It must have the shape of a face
D. It must have grown visible hair
Correct: C — Rav Yehuda says in Shmuel's name: a sandal fetus requires צוּרַת פָּנִים — the shape of a face — to have offspring status. Rabbi Yannai ruled stringently, insisting that even a sandal without a distinct face is considered offspring and the woman is impure.
