🧬 Does a Gestational Sac Confer Birth-Impurity?

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
I have not heard from my teachers the halakha about a sac full of flesh. I cannot rule.
Rabbi Yishmael b. R. Yosei
My father taught: a sac of blood = menstrual impurity; a sac of water or flesh = no impurity at all.
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
Your father agreed with Sumakhos — a lone opinion — on the blood ruling. Therefore his ruling on flesh is also a minority view, and we cannot accept it.
Core Question — שְׁפִיר When a woman discharges a gestational sac (שְׁפִיר), does its content determine whether she has the impurity of childbirth, menstruation, or neither?

⚖️ Rava's Objection — Why Not Firstborn Animals Too?

Rav Naḥman (Rabba bar Avuh)
The Rabbi Yehoshua–Rabbis dispute applies only when fluid is turbid. Turbidity suggests an embryo dissolved inside — Rabbi Yehoshua treats it as offspring on that basis.
Rava
If so, why doesn't a turbid-fluid sac also exempt a firstborn animal? The same logic — a dissolved embryo — should apply to animals as well!
Gemara's Answer
For women, a verse (Gen 3:21) includes a sac in the definition of offspring — Rabbi Yehoshua is only uncertain, and monetary law (human primogeniture) resolves leniently. For animals, there is no such verse; ritual prohibitions require certainty, not mere possibility.
Principle Monetary uncertainty (human pidyon haben) → lenient. Ritual prohibition (animal firstborn — shearing, labor) → cannot be extended by logic alone.

🔴🔵 Rav vs Shmuel — Final Rulings on the Gestational Sac

Rav

  • Whether fluid is clear OR turbid —
  • the woman need NOT be concerned

🔍 How to Examine an Embryo for Sex Determination

Do NOT use water — water is too strong (חָזָק) and dissolves the embryo
↓
Use oil — oil is soft and cleans the embryo so features can be discerned
↓
View only in sunlight — artificial light is insufficient for examination
↓
Abba Shaul's sliver method: take a smooth-tipped wooden sliver and move it along the sex organ from below to above (per Rav Naḥman)
↓
Sliver catches / not smooth → Female (vaginal groove present)
Sliver moves smoothly → Male
↓
Shmuel to Rav Yehuda (Shinnana): Do not issue a ruling of impurity unless the embryo has grown hair — but the woman must privately be concerned in any case

🐟 Sandal Fetus — Halakhic Overview

IssueRuling / Detail
AppearanceFlat like a fish (sandal/flounder); compressed, no distinct limbs
OriginAlways has a twin — the sandal is the twin that was flattened
Requirement for offspring-statusMust have the shape of a face (צוּרַת פָּנִים) — Rav Yehuda / Shmuel
Rabbi Yannai's rulingRebuked colleagues who would have deemed a faceless sandal pure: 'You have deemed offspring pure!'
Why mishna mentions it separatelyWhen twin is male, sandal adds female-birth strictures (80 impure days vs 40) — without the rule you might count only from the male birth
Split birth: female before sunset, sandal afterTwo separate 40/80-day birth-impurity periods run consecutively — she cannot combine them
