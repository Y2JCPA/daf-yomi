↓

💧 The Hymen Under Age Three: Two Opinions

View A
The hymen of a girl under three disappears and grows back. Intercourse before age 3 leaves no lasting mark — hence the comparison to a finger in the eye.
View B
The hymen does not form at all until after age three. There is simply no hymen present to be damaged — that is why intercourse leaves no effect.
Rav Ḥiyya b. Rav Ika
The proposed practical difference is flawed — even View A can explain no blood after age 3: she may have had intercourse before age 3 a second time, not with another man.
Resolution
True practical case: husband had intercourse before 3 and found blood, then again after 3 and found no blood. On View A she remains permitted; on View B she may be forbidden (another man is suspected).
Halakhic Stake A priest's young wife: if she bleeds before age 3 but not after — is she forbidden to her priest-husband as one who had intercourse with another man?

📜 When Are a Minor's Vows Valid?

Minor makes a vow or consecration
↓
Girl under 11y1d / Boy under 12y1d
Vow is always invalid — even if they claim full knowledge of Whose name they invoked
Girl 11y1d–12y1d / Boy 12y1d–13y1d
Examine the vow — valid only if they demonstrate genuine understanding; this applies throughout the entire transition year
Girl 12y1d+ / Boy 13y1d+ (+ two pubic hairs)
Vow is fully valid — even if they claim ignorance, we do not accept their disclaimer
↓
Gemara: each clause of the mishna is necessary — they cannot be derived from one another

🧠 Why Do Girls Mature a Year Before Boys?

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi

  • God vayyiven the woman (Genesis 2:22) — root: binah = understanding
  • God built extra intellectual capacity into women at creation
  • Therefore girls reach vow-maturity at 12, boys only at 13
  • Rav Ḥisda: this is Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's derivation

Rabbi Shimon ben Elazar

  • Males actually mature faster than females in his view
  • Boys frequent their teacher's house — cleverness enters through their feet
  • He reads vayyiven differently: Reish Lakish uses it to teach that God adorned Eve like a bride
  • Rav Shmuel bar Rav Yitzḥak: constant study accelerates male development

👑 Two Remarkable Women Before the Sages

מַעֲשֶׂה בְּאִשָּׁה שֶׁבָּאת לִפְנֵי רַבִּי עֲקִיבָא — כְּתִינוֹק שֶׁטָּבְלוּ אֶצְבָּעוֹ בְּדְבַשׁ
Historical Incidents Yusteni before Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi: Daughter of Asveirus, son of Emperor Antoninus. She asked: at what age is a woman fit for intercourse? (Answer: 12y1d.) Fit to become pregnant? (Answer: 12y1d.) She replied: I married at 6 and gave birth at 7. Rabbi: 'Woe to us that we must be judged by you!' — Resolved: either gentile physiology differs (Ezekiel 23:20), or the tradition was already known to her.

Woman before Rabbi Akiva: Claimed she had intercourse before age 3 and asked whether she may marry a priest. She offered a parable: like a baby whose finger is dipped in honey — at first he cries, but soon he craves it (her body now desires what once caused pain). Rabbi Akiva ruled her permitted. Students asked in astonishment: is this a law from Sinai? He confirmed: yes, it is a received halakhic tradition.
