⚡ Rabbi Eliezer vs. Rabbi Yehoshua: Retroactive Impurity for the Four Women

Rabbi Eliezer
Four women transmit impurity only from the moment of their sighting — not retroactively: a virgin, a pregnant woman, a nursing woman, and an elderly woman whose cycles have ceased.
Rabbi Yehoshua
I only heard this ruling from my teachers regarding a virgin who has never before seen menstrual blood. I did not hear it applied to the other three women you listed.
Rabbi Eliezer
Just as we do not ask one who has never seen the new moon to come and testify, so too we do not impose retroactive impurity on those with no established menstrual cycle to look back upon.
Gemara
Why did the Sages not rule like Rabbi Eliezer during his lifetime? Because he was a Shammuti — a follower of Beit Shammai — and they feared that publicly ruling like him would entrench the authority of Beit Shammai.
Final Ruling The halakha is in accordance with Rabbi Eliezer — but was deliberately withheld during his lifetime to prevent cementing Beit Shammai's influence.

⚖️ Two Types of Halakhic Uncertainty — Which Applies Here?

Equal Proofs — ספק שקול

  • Classic case: two paths, one pure and one impure
  • Person walked one path — exactly 50/50 unknown
  • An unresolvable, symmetric doubt
  • Abaye & Rava: ḥalla of uncertain status is NOT this kind of doubt

The 'Leaning' Case — הכרעה

  • A zav and a pure person load or unload a package together from a donkey
  • Even a minority-probability contact risk triggers rabbinic decree
  • Not a symmetric doubt — a low-probability concern with real stringency
  • Abaye & Rava: ḥalla's uncertain purity IS this kind of case

📜 Abaye's Principle: Uncertain Impurity Mirrors Definite Impurity

כל שטומאתו ודאית מטמא אוכלי חולין — גזרו על ספק טומאתו שיטמא אוכלי חולין
עקרון — אביי Any item that when definitely impure renders non-sacred food (חולין) impure — such as a zav — the Sages decreed that its uncertain impurity likewise renders non-sacred food impure.

However, this does not extend to the retroactive impurity period of a menstruating woman, because that retroactive period is itself only rabbinic. The Sages will not stack one rabbinic decree upon another.

⚡ Shmuel's Four Cases Where Halakha Follows Rabbi Eliezer

Case 1 — The Four Women: A virgin, pregnant woman, nursing woman, and elderly woman all transmit impurity only from the moment of sighting, not retroactively. The halakha follows Rabbi Eliezer against Rabbi Yehoshua.
↓
Case 2 — Woman in Labor: A woman who experiences labor pains and sees blood — the discharge is attributed to childbirth rather than zava blood. She is relieved from counting seven clean days.
↓
Case 3 — Zav/Zava Examining on Days 1 and 7 Only: Rabbi Eliezer rules examination on the first and seventh days alone is sufficient to establish purity. Rabbi Akiva disagrees, saying only one day has truly been counted.
↓
Case 4 — Exterior of Vessels: Vessels rendered impure on their exterior by contact with impure liquids can render teruma impure but not non-sacred food — because this impurity is by rabbinic law, and the Sages limited the decree's scope.
↓
Gemara's Question: What is Shmuel teaching us? Cases 1–3 are explicit in mishnayot! The novelty must be Case 4 — yet Shmuel chose to list all four together to teach that each ruling is firm halakha, not a minority opinion.
