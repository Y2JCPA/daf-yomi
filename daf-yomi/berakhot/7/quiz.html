Berakhot 7 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the halakhic status of ḥalla separated from untithed dough when its ritual purity is uncertain?
A. It is eaten like ritually pure ḥalla
B. It is burned like ritually impure ḥalla
C. It is suspended — neither eaten nor burned
D. It is given to a non-kohen since it has no valid status
Correct: C — The Gemara rules that uncertain-purity ḥalla from untithed dough is suspended: it cannot be eaten (lest it is impure) nor burned (lest it is pure). This middle status is triggered by the minority-risk 'leaning' category of doubt.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Abaye and Rava, the uncertainty about ḥalla's purity resembles which kind of halakhic doubt?
A. Two paths — one pure and one impure — an unresolvable 50/50 doubt
B. The 'leaning' case from Zavim, where minority risk still triggers rabbinic decree
C. A doubt resolved by following the majority (רוב)
D. A doubt resolved by defaulting to the stricter ruling in all cases
Correct: B — Abaye and Rava explicitly reject the symmetric 'two paths' comparison. The case resembles Zavim 3:2, where a zav and a pure person lean together — a minority-probability concern that nevertheless triggers impurity by rabbinic decree.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which of the following is NOT one of Rabbi Eliezer's four women who transmit impurity only from the moment of sighting?
A. A virgin who has never before seen menstrual blood
B. A nursing woman
C. A woman who immersed in a mikveh that day (tevul yom)
D. An elderly woman whose three thirty-day cycles passed without blood
Correct: C — Rabbi Eliezer's four women are: a virgin, a pregnant woman, a nursing woman, and an elderly woman. A 'tevul yom' (one who immersed and awaits nightfall) is a separate halakhic category unrelated to this list.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did the Sages not rule in accordance with Rabbi Eliezer's teaching during his own lifetime?
A. Rabbi Eliezer had not yet provided adequate proof for his ruling
B. A majority of the Sanhedrin had already voted to reject his opinion
C. He was a Shammuti — a follower of Beit Shammai — and ruling like him risked entrenching Shammai's authority
D. His ruling contradicted an explicit biblical verse that took precedence
Correct: C — The Gemara explains: Rabbi Eliezer was a Shammuti, meaning his rulings aligned with Beit Shammai. Publicly endorsing him during his lifetime would have strengthened Beit Shammai's position beyond what could later be corrected. After his death, protest against deviation became possible.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In how many cases does Shmuel rule that the halakha follows Rabbi Eliezer, and what is the Gemara's question about this list?
A. Two cases; the Gemara asks why Shmuel ignored the other cases
B. Three cases; the Gemara asks why Rabbi Yehoshua is not also listed
C. Four cases; the Gemara asks what Shmuel adds since the rulings are explicit in mishnayot
D. Seven cases; the Gemara questions whether all seven are truly disputed
Correct: C — Shmuel lists four cases. The Gemara challenges: since cases 1–3 are stated explicitly in mishnayot as following Rabbi Eliezer, what novel information does Shmuel convey? The answer is that the novelty lies in Case 4 (exterior of vessels), which is not stated in a mishna — yet Shmuel listed it together with the others to signal it is equally firm halakha.
