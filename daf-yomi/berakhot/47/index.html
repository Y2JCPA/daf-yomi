🌾 Ḥalla in the Present — Torah or Rabbinic?

Other Sages
Even according to one who says Teruma applies by Torah law today, Ḥalla likewise applies by Torah law — the two obligations are parallel and inseparable.
Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua
On the contrary! Even if Teruma = Torah law, Ḥalla = Rabbinic law. During the 14 years of conquest and division of the Land, Israel separated Ḥalla but was not obligated in Teruma — proving they have independent triggers.
Baraita
Ḥalla applies by Torah law only when all the inhabitants dwell in the Land (Numbers 15:18–19). When Ezra returned, not all Israel came — so today Ḥalla is Rabbinic only.
Conclusion
Ḥalla today is Rabbinic even for those who hold Teruma is Torah law. Therefore a Rabbinic-status teruma mixture can legitimately abrogate a Rabbinic ḥalla obligation.
The Proof 14 years of conquest + division: Israel separated Ḥalla but was exempt from Teruma — demonstrating these two laws have entirely independent conditions for obligation.

🌿 The Fig Parable — Three Stages of Womanhood

פַּגָּה — Unripe Fig

  • Stage: Minority (קְטַנּוּת)
  • Before two pubic hairs appear
  • Father controls: her finds, earnings, vow annulment
  • Father's kiddushin is fully valid
  • Source: 'The fig tree puts forth her green fruits' (Song of Songs 2:13)

בֹּחַל — Ripening Fig

  • Stage: Young Womanhood (נַעֲרוּת)
  • Begins: age 12 + 1 day with two pubic hairs
  • Father still controls: finds, earnings, vow annulment
  • Both father AND girl must consent to kiddushin
  • Duration: six months, then becomes bogeret

תְּאֵנָה — Ripe Fig

  • Stage: Grown Womanhood (בַּגְרוּת)
  • Six months after na'ara stage begins
  • Father loses ALL rights over her
  • She controls: her own finds, earnings, vows
  • Fully independent woman under halakha

📅 How Are 'Years' Counted in Halakhic Age Thresholds?

Rav
Full years from birth are always required — calculated from the exact hour of birth until that same time in the specified year. This applies throughout the chapter even when 'and one day' is not explicitly written.
Ulla
Only when the mishna explicitly adds 'and one day' are full birth-date years required. When omitted (e.g., the 20-year threshold), completing the calendar year is sufficient — no precise time-of-birth tracking needed.
Challenge to Rav
Rabbi Yosei ben Keifar teaches: 'The twentieth year of which 30 days have passed' counts — implying less than a full year from birth suffices. This contradicts Rav!
Resolution (Gemara)
This is a tanna'ic dispute. Rav follows the tanna who requires full years, derived from: 'a lamb in its first year' (Leviticus 12:6), walled-city house redemption, and Hebrew slave years — all requiring exact anniversary calculation.
Cases Affected The same full-year vs. calendar-year question applies to: sacrificial animal ages (1 year), walled city house redemption (1 year), Hebrew slave service (6 years), and son/daughter halakhic age milestones.
