QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Huna son of Rav Yehoshua, why is Ḥalla today only Rabbinic law even if one holds Teruma is Torah law?
A. Because Ḥalla was never practiced after the Babylonian exile
B. Because during 14 years of conquest and division Israel was obligated in Ḥalla but exempt from Teruma — proving the obligations are independent
C. Because the Sages decreed Ḥalla Rabbinic to prevent confusion with Teruma
D. Because Ḥalla only applies when the Temple is standing
Correct: B — Rav Huna's proof: during the 14 years Israel conquered and divided Eretz Yisrael they separated Ḥalla but were not yet obligated in Teruma. This shows the two laws have independent triggers. Since not all Israel returned with Ezra, the Torah condition for Ḥalla ('when you come into the Land') was not fully met — making it Rabbinic today.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the mishna's fig parable, which fruit represents the na'ara (young womanhood) stage?
A. Paga — the completely unripe fig
B. Te'ena — the fully ripe fig
C. Bochal — the ripening fig
D. The fig is not used; a pomegranate is used for na'ara
Correct: C — The mishna teaches three stages: paga (unripe fig) = minority (ketana); bochal (ripening fig) = young womanhood (na'ara); te'ena (ripe fig) = grown womanhood (bogeret). Na'ara begins when a girl aged 12 and one day develops two pubic hairs, and lasts six months.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Shmuel examined his Canaanite maidservant for signs of bogeret and then gave her four dinars. Why?
A. As payment for her work in the study hall
B. As compensation for the humiliation of being physically examined
C. As a fine for violating Shabbat
D. Because he lost a halakhic wager to her
Correct: B — The Gemara relates that Shmuel examined his maidservant to determine the physical signs of grown womanhood, and afterward paid her four dinars for her humiliation. This is consistent with Shmuel's own ruling that one who humiliates another must pay compensation.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Under what condition does a 20-year-old male without pubic hair assume the status of a saris according to Rav Shmuel bar Yitzḥak?
A. Automatically, at age 20, regardless of other signs
B. Only if he is also thin or malnourished
C. Only if physical signs of a saris had already developed by age 20
D. Only if a court of three judges formally declares him a saris
Correct: C — Rav Shmuel bar Yitzḥak qualifies the mishna: saris status applies only when the person had already developed the distinctive physical signs of underdevelopment (described in Yevamot 80b) by age 20. Without those signs, he may simply be delayed — e.g., due to malnutrition — and remains a minor until most of his years pass.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the core disagreement between Rav and Ulla regarding age calculations in this chapter?
A. Rav uses lunar years; Ulla uses solar years
B. Rav requires full years counted from the exact time of birth; Ulla requires this only when the mishna explicitly adds 'and one day'
C. Rav counts from Rosh Hashana; Ulla counts from the actual birthday
D. Both require full years but disagree about whether hours are counted
Correct: B — Rav holds that all age thresholds in this chapter require full years measured from the precise moment of birth. Ulla disagrees: the phrase 'and one day' in earlier mishnayot signals where full birth-date years are needed; where the phrase is absent (like the 20-year saris threshold), completing the calendar year suffices. The Gemara resolves Rav's position as following a specific tannaitic opinion derived from verses about sacrificial animals and other contexts.
