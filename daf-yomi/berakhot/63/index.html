📅 Fixed Menstrual Cycles and Their Laws

וסת קבוע — מסכת נידה דף ס"ג
Core Theme Daf 63 bridges two major halakhic areas: (1) the precise definition of tasteless saliva (ריר תפל) and the rules governing the seven blood-stain detergents — their required order and method; (2) the heart of the chapter: fixed menstrual cycles (וסת קבוע) — whether established by time, bodily sensation, or specific days and hours — how many occurrences are needed to fix a cycle, and how long a woman must separate in advance. Rava's landmark ruling closes the daf.

💧 What Is Tasteless Saliva? Three Opinions

Baraita
Tasteless saliva is any saliva where the person had not tasted anything since the evening. One condition only.
Rabba bar bar Ḥana
citing Rabbi Yoḥanan
Three conditions required: (1) did not eat any food, (2) passed midnight, and (3) was in a state of sleep — even dozing counts.
Rav Ashi
Dozing = asleep yet not fully asleep, awake yet not fully awake. If called he answers, but cannot distinguish honey from date-honey.
Practical Ruling One who rose early and recited Torah aloud — that saliva is NOT tasteless, as speech weakens its potency. Even a small amount of speech removes this status.

🧼 The Seven Detergents — Strict Order Required

1. Tasteless saliva — applied first; the foundation of stain removal
↓
2. Liquid from split beans — beans that divided naturally, not by human hand, then applied to the stain
↓
3. Urine — must be fermented for at least three days (baraita)
↓
4–7. Additional substances — each substance rubbed three times (going and coming = debate: one rub or two?)
↓
⚠️ Applied in wrong order OR all simultaneously = completely ineffective
↓
✅ Abaye: If applied twice in wrong order, the second application counts; the first is nullified — no dispute between the baraitot

📅 Establishing a Fixed Cycle — Time vs. Sensation

Day-Based Veset (וסת הימים)

  • Based on specific dates or intervals between periods
  • Requires two occurrences on the same date/interval (Shmuel)
  • Example: bled on 15th, then again on 15th → fixed
  • Three deviations in a row → old cycle released, new one established

Sensation-Based Veset (וסת הגוף)

  • Based on physical signs preceding bleeding
  • Only one occurrence is needed to fix it (Shmuel)
  • Includes: yawning, sneezing, stomach/lower abdominal pain
  • Also: heavy head, heavy limbs, trembling, feverish shuddering

⚖️ Rava's Ruling on Pre-Cycle Separation

Woman has an established fixed menstrual cycle (וסת קבוע)
↓
Must she separate from her husband before the fixed time?
How long must she separate?
Rava: one 12-hour period — either sunrise-to-sunset or sunset-to-sunrise, whichever is close to her fixed time
Why two statements from Rava?
(1) Halakha follows R. Yehuda AND (2) separation = 12 hours only.
Without both, we might think either: no advance separation needed, or a full 24-hour separation required
↓
If she deviated from 15th → 20th three times: 15th now permitted; 20th is her new established fixed cycle
