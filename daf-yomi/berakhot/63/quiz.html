QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabba bar bar Ḥana citing Rabbi Yoḥanan, which three conditions must ALL be met for saliva to qualify as 'tasteless saliva' (ריר תפל)?
A. No food eaten, past midnight, and a state of sleep
B. No food or drink since the previous evening only
C. No food since morning, past noon, and recited Torah
D. No food eaten, recited a blessing, and it is dawn
Correct: A — Rabbi Yoḥanan requires all three: (1) no food eaten, (2) past the middle of the night, and (3) a state of sleep (even dozing). The baraita only requires no tasting since evening — Rabbi Yoḥanan adds the midnight and sleep conditions.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Abaye say to resolve the apparent contradiction between the two baraitot about applying detergents out of order?
A. The first application always counts; later applications are redundant
B. Both baraitot agree: the second application counts, the first is nullified
C. The baraitot represent a genuine dispute between two Tannaim
D. Both applications are void; the process must be restarted from the beginning
Correct: B — Abaye reconciles the baraitot: both agree the second application counts and the first does not. 'He has done nothing' in one baraita refers to the first (out-of-order) application being ineffective — not to the second.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Shmuel (as reconstructed by Abaye for Rav Yosef), how many occurrences are needed to fix a sensation-based veset versus a day-based veset?
A. Three occurrences for both types
B. Two occurrences for both types
C. One occurrence for sensation; two occurrences for days
D. Two occurrences for sensation; three occurrences for days
Correct: C — Shmuel distinguishes: a sensation-based veset (yawning, sneezing, pain, etc.) is fixed after only ONE occurrence, while a day-based veset requires TWO occurrences on the same date or at the same interval.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rava's halakhic ruling regarding pre-cycle separation, and how long must the separation last?
A. Halakha follows Rabbi Yosei; no separation required if the hour passes
B. Halakha follows Rabbi Yehuda; she separates for a full 24-hour period
C. Halakha follows Rabbi Yehuda; she separates for one 12-hour period close to her fixed time
D. Halakha follows Rabbi Yehuda; she separates beginning three days before her cycle
Correct: C — Rava issues two rulings: (1) halakha follows Rabbi Yehuda, and (2) the required separation is one 12-hour period (sunrise-to-sunset or sunset-to-sunrise) close to her fixed time. Both rulings are independently necessary.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If a woman's fixed cycle was established on the 15th of the month, but she deviated and bled on the 20th three consecutive times, what is the law?
A. Both the 15th and 20th remain permanently prohibited for her
B. The 20th becomes her new fixed cycle; intercourse on the 15th is now permitted
C. She must observe both days until a rabbi releases her from the 15th
D. The 15th remains forbidden; the 20th has no fixed-cycle status yet
Correct: B — After three consecutive deviations to the 20th, she has established the 20th as her new fixed cycle. This releases her from the 15th, which is now permitted — as stated in the final mishna of this daf (63b).
