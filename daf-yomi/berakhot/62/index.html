⚖️ The Central Dispute: What Makes an Absorbed Liquid Impure?

Rabbi Yoḥanan
A liquid renders a garment impure only when it can emerge and the owner is particular about it — both conditions are required.
Reish Lakish
If the liquid can emerge, it is impure — even if the owner is not particular. Ability to emerge alone is the determining factor.
Rav Pappa
Point of agreement: If liquid cannot emerge and owner is not particular → both agree it is pure. They dispute only when liquid can emerge but owner is not particular.
Practical Stakes This debate determines whether ritually pure items handled on a garment that has absorbed blood-like liquid require immersion or remain fully pure.

🤔 Does an Owner's Concern Determine a Stain's Ritual Status?

Rabbi Abba
How can purity depend on whether the owner is particular about the stain? The blood is either present or it isn't — subjective concern shouldn't determine objective ritual law!
Rav Ashi
Yes, concern is determinative. Proof from Rabbi Ḥiyya: blood certainly from a niddah on a garment — if the owner is particular about it, the garment is impure. If not particular → pure.
Rav Ashi (analysis)
The niddah blood is undeniably present, yet we rule it pure when the owner is unconcerned — proving that מקפיד (being particular) is the legally operative factor for conveying impurity.
Key Concept: מקפיד Being 'particular' — actively caring about an absorbed substance — is what activates its legal status as an impurity-conveyor. Without owner's concern, even confirmed niddah blood does not render a garment impure.

⚗️ Reish Lakish vs. Rabbi Yoḥanan: Full Comparison

Reish Lakish

  • Oven + zav pottery: impurity only from lesser impure liquids absorbed, not primary sources
  • Blood stains: only rabbinic impurity — Sages were lenient, required 'particularity'
  • Liquid that can emerge → impure even if owner is NOT particular
  • Proof from Oholot: 'unable to emerge = pure' implies ability to emerge = impure regardless of concern

Rabbi Yoḥanan

  • Oven + zav pottery: impurity applies to ALL absorbed impure liquids, whether lesser or primary
  • Blood stains: Torah-level concern — 'particularity' activates impurity status
  • Liquid can emerge but owner NOT particular → garment remains pure
  • Proof from Rabbi Ḥiyya: confirmed niddah blood ruled pure when owner is not particular
