Berakhot 62 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Yehuda, which substance is 'borit' — one of the seven stain-removing agents?
A. Alexandrian natron
B. Cimolian earth
C. Ice plant
D. Tasteless saliva
Correct: C — Rav Yehuda identifies borit as ice plant. The Gemara resolves an apparent contradiction by explaining that a baraita listing them separately was using a different classification context.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
If one applies all seven substances and the stain does not disappear, what is the halakhic ruling regarding items previously handled?
A. The items are impure since blood was presumed
B. The items remain pure since the stain is presumed to be dye
C. The items must be examined by a rabbi
D. The garment must be burned and items re-immersed
Correct: B — A stain that survives all seven substances is presumed to be a dye, not blood. Therefore items handled while wearing the garment remain ritually pure.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rabbi Zeira's ruling: if the seven substances are applied a second time and the stain disappears, which items are pure?
A. All items handled at any point
B. Only items handled before the first washing
C. Only items handled between the first and second washings
D. No items at all remain pure
Correct: C — Rabbi Zeira teaches that only items handled between the first and second applications remain pure — since after the second washing confirms the stain was blood, those items' intermediate pure status is preserved while earlier items are impure.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Reish Lakish and Rabbi Yoḥanan regarding absorbed liquids in a garment?
A. Whether soap dissolves dye or blood more effectively
B. Whether absorbed niddah blood requires seven substances to test
C. Whether ability to emerge alone, or also the owner's concern, determines impurity
D. Whether natron from Egypt differs legally from natron from other regions
Correct: C — Reish Lakish holds that if a liquid can emerge from the garment it is impure regardless of whether the owner is particular. Rabbi Yoḥanan requires both ability to emerge AND the owner being 'particular' (מקפיד) before the liquid conveys impurity.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rav Ashi prove that being 'particular' (מקפיד) is the operative factor in a blood stain's ritual status?
A. From the zav's pottery that fell into an oven
B. From Rabbi Ḥiyya's ruling that confirmed niddah blood is pure if the owner is not particular
C. From the mishna about a quarter-log of blood from a corpse
D. From the case of tasteless saliva removing stains
Correct: B — Rav Ashi cites Rabbi Ḥiyya: blood certainly from a niddah on a garment is ruled pure if the owner is not particular about it. Since the blood is undeniably present yet the garment can be pure, the owner's concern is proven to be the legally determinative factor.
