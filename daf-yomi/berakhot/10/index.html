👧 Stains Between Sightings — Pure or Impure?

Ḥizkiyya
A blood stain found on a young girl between her 2nd and 3rd sightings is impure. By this point she has seen blood twice and is building toward an established cycle.
Rabbi Ilai
What distinguishes her from a בְּתוּלַת דָּמִים (menstrual virgin), whose stain is deemed pure as long as her blood is pure? This girl too should remain pure until fully established!
Ḥizkiyya
The cases differ fundamentally: the menstrual virgin's purity is tied to a specific physiological condition, whereas this girl has already begun her cycle and is progressing toward regular menstruation.
Narrator
Between the 1st and 2nd sighting, all agree the stain is pure. The dispute is specifically about the window between 2nd and 3rd sightings.
📍 Practical Implication If Ḥizkiyya is correct, a husband must treat his wife as impure upon finding a stain during this intermediate period, even though she has not yet fully established a veset.

⚖️ Rav vs. Shmuel: Who Gets Perpetual 'Time Is Sufficient'?

Rav

  • Qualification applies only to the first sighting for all four categories
  • After the second sighting, she transmits retroactive impurity like any woman
  • Applies uniformly to: menstrual virgin, elderly woman, pregnant woman, nursing woman
  • No ongoing exemption based on physiological state

Shmuel

  • Qualification applies only to menstrual virgin and elderly woman (first sighting only)
  • Pregnant and nursing women have 'time is sufficient' for all their days in that status
  • Their ongoing physiological state creates a continuous presumption of purity
  • Even the second or third sighting during pregnancy/nursing — time is sufficient

🤰 Combining Pregnancy & Nursing Cycles

The Rule: A woman must pass three expected menstrual cycles (וֶסֶת) without bleeding to earn 'time is sufficient' upon her next sighting
↓
Key Teaching: Cycles during pregnancy and cycles during nursing can be combined to reach the three-cycle total
↓
Combination A: 2 skipped cycles during pregnancy + 1 skipped cycle during nursing = 3 total ✓
↓
Combination B: 1 skipped cycle during pregnancy + 2 skipped cycles during nursing = 3 total ✓
↓
Challenge: How can nursing cycles count before the birth? Answer: Dry birth (no blood), or birth blood and menstrual blood are halakhically discrete and do not interrupt the count
↓
Rabbi Elazar's Note: The baraita may address a woman simultaneously pregnant and nursing — teaching the combination rule incidentally (בְּדֶרֶךְ אַגַּב)
