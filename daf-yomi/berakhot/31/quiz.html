Berakhot 31 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yitzḥak citing Rabbi Ami, what determines whether a child will be male or female?
A. The position of the fetus in the womb at the time of birth
B. Who emits seed first during intercourse
C. Whether intercourse occurs near the woman's immersion in a ritual bath
D. The mother's emotional state during pregnancy
Correct: B — Rabbi Yitzḥak says in the name of Rabbi Ami: if the woman emits seed first, the child is male; if the man emits seed first, the child is female. This is derived from Leviticus 12:2: 'If a woman emits seed and gives birth to a male.'
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the father's 'white seed' contribute to the creation of a person?
A. Skin, flesh, and hair
B. The soul, facial expression, and the ability to walk
C. Bones, sinews, nails, brain matter, and the white of the eye
D. Intelligence, speech, and hearing
Correct: C — The baraita specifies that the father's white seed forms the structural components: bones, sinews, nails, brain matter, and the white of the eye. God contributes the soul and faculties; the mother contributes skin, flesh, hair, and the pupil of the eye.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why are the labor pains when giving birth to a female greater than when giving birth to a male?
A. Female fetuses are larger and heavier than male fetuses
B. The female fetus faces upward and must turn a greater distance to exit face-down
C. Females reside higher in the womb and have farther to descend
D. The female fetus clings more tightly to the womb wall
Correct: B — A male fetus faces downward (toward the ground, from which man was created) and needs minimal turning to emerge. A female fetus faces upward (toward the man, from whom woman was created) and must rotate much further — this additional turning causes greater labor pains.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Shimon ben Yoḥai, why does the Torah require a woman after childbirth to bring an offering?
A. To give thanks to God for her survival of the dangerous birth process
B. To purify herself from the ritual impurity of childbirth blood
C. To atone for a rash vow she made during labor to never have intercourse again
D. To honor the memory of the souls that were not born
Correct: C — R. Shimon ben Yoḥai explains that the agony of labor causes a woman to vow impulsively that she will never have intercourse with her husband again. When she later resumes marital relations, she needs atonement for violating that oath. The offering — combined with the regret it expresses — dissolves the rash oath.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why is circumcision performed specifically on the eighth day and not earlier?
A. The foreskin is not fully formed until the eighth day after birth
B. The first seven days must include at least one Shabbat to make the circumcision valid
C. So that everyone — including the mother, who completes her seven-day impurity — can rejoice together at the celebration
D. Because the baby needs seven days to gain enough strength for the procedure
Correct: C — Rabbi Shimon ben Yoḥai taught: if circumcision were performed before the eighth day, everyone would rejoice while the mother (still in her seven-day birth impurity, unable to fully participate) and father would be sad even as guests celebrate. On the eighth day, the mother's impurity has passed and all can rejoice together.
