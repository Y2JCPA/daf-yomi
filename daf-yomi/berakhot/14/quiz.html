QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Abaye rule that camel riders are prohibited from eating teruma?
A. They are presumed untrustworthy about tithes and separations
B. The friction of riding may cause a seminal emission, rendering them impure
C. Camels are ritually impure animals that transmit impurity to riders
D. They spend nights in the wilderness far from any mikveh
Correct: B — Abaye's concern is halakhic-practical: the physical friction of camel riding risks causing a seminal emission (keri), which renders a person ritually impure. A person in that state is prohibited from eating teruma.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Shmuel, what is the status of a woman who used a pre-examined cloth, pressed it against her thigh, and found blood on it the next day?
A. Pure — the blood may have come from the thigh or an external source
B. Uncertain impurity (safek niddah) only
C. Definitely impure as a menstruating woman (vadai niddah)
D. Impure only if the stain is larger than an olive-bulk
Correct: C — Shmuel rules she is definitely impure as a niddah, and the study hall confirms this. Since the cloth was pre-inspected and found clean, the blood is attributable to her uterus with certainty.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the unresolved dilemma in the Gemara regarding Rav Yosef's report about Rabbi Ḥiyya's change of ruling?
A. Whether the ruling applied to linen cloths or wool cloths only
B. Whether Rabbi Ḥiyya changed from 'definitely impure' to 'uncertain,' or from 'uncertain' to 'completely pure'
C. Whether the box had to be sealed or could remain open
D. Whether the ruling applied only to married women or also to unmarried women
Correct: B — The Gemara cannot determine which way Rabbi Ḥiyya's view shifted in old age: did he start with 'definite impurity' and soften to 'uncertain impurity'? Or did he always hold 'uncertain' and change to 'completely pure'? The baraita about his dispute with Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi resolves it in favor of the latter.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara defend Rabbi Ḥama bar Bisa for preferring to hear Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's ruling over Rabbi Yosei's?
A. Rabbi Yosei's opinion had been formally overruled by the Sanhedrin
B. Rabbi Ḥama personally witnessed Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's ruling firsthand
C. As head of the yeshiva, Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's rulings are more refined through constant debate and more reliably stated
D. Rabbi Yosei was lenient and Rabbi Ḥama sought the stricter opinion
Correct: C — The Gemara defends Rabbi Ḥama: Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, as rosh yeshiva, has his rulings constantly scrutinized in debate, making them more carefully formulated. Rabbi Ḥama reasonably preferred this practical precision.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rav Ashi resolve the apparent contradiction between the mishna's definition of 'after time passed' and the baraita's different description?
A. The baraita applies only on Shabbat; the mishna governs weekdays
B. Both describe the same period — the time needed adapts to circumstances (cloth already in hand vs. must reach for it)
C. The mishna refers to the husband's cloth; the baraita refers to the wife's cloth
D. The baraita is a rejected minority opinion; only the mishna is authoritative
Correct: B — Rav Ashi teaches that the mishna and baraita describe the same time period, but the duration is situational: if the cloth is already in her hand, the period is shorter; if she must reach under the cushion to retrieve it, longer. The two sources simply reflect different circumstances.
