רוכבי גמלים כולם רשעים — כל הישן על גבו לא ישן
🐪 Camel Riders, Donkey Drivers & Ritual Purity Abaye rules that camel riders may not eat teruma — the friction of riding risks causing a seminal emission. A supporting baraita goes further: all camel riders are wicked, suspected of unnatural relations. Donkey drivers are divided: if his donkey walks behind him (leading to suspicious contact), he is wicked; if the donkey walks ahead, he is righteous.

Also on this daf: Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi would curse anyone who sleeps aparkeid (flat on their back), fearing seminal emission. But reciting Shema face-up requires only a slight lean — while sleeping that way is prohibited even with a lean.

🧻 Examined Cloth vs. Unexamined Cloth Left in Box

Examined Cloth (Pre-Checked)

  • Cloth inspected before use — found clean
  • Pressed against thigh without re-inspection
  • Blood discovered the next day
  • Shmuel: definitely impure as a niddah
  • Study hall confirms: definite niddah status
  • Prior inspection removes doubt about other sources

Unexamined Cloth (Not Pre-Checked)

  • Cloth NOT inspected before use
  • Placed in a box without looking
  • Blood found the next day
  • Rav Yosef cites Rabbi Ḥiyya's ruling
  • Dispute: definitely impure? or only uncertain?
  • Stain larger than a split bean required for impurity

📦 Rabbi Ḥiyya vs. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi — Unexamined Cloth

Rav Yosef (citing Rabbi Ḥiyya)
All his days Rabbi Ḥiyya ruled on the unexamined cloth — but in his old age he changed his ruling. The dilemma: did he shift from definite impurity → uncertain, or from uncertain → completely pure?
Rabbi Ḥiyya (to Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi)
Do you not concede that impurity requires a stain larger than a split bean? The cloth was unexamined — the blood may have come from an external source, not the woman herself.
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
The split-bean threshold exists only to exclude blood from a louse. If the stain exceeds that size, she is definitely impure — we do not attribute it to an external source.
Gemara (Resolution)
The fact that Rabbi Ḥiyya disputed his teacher Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi proves he was in his old age — a student would not dispute his teacher otherwise. His final position: she is pure from any impurity.
Baraita Proof The baraita's account of Rabbi Ḥiyya challenging Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi resolves the dilemma: he was old when he changed, shifting from definite impurity to complete purity.
