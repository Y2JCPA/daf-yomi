🏛️ How Many Immersions? Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel

Beit Shammai
The woman must immerse 95 times — we count every obligatory immersion, including those performed after she is already permitted to her husband.
Beit Hillel
The woman must immerse only 35 times — we count only immersions required before she is permitted to engage in intercourse. Post-permission immersions are not tallied.
Gemara
But Beit Shammai — why don't you also count the immersions a zava who gave birth must perform? Those also permit intercourse and should be included!
Gemara
Beit Hillel counts only the 35 pre-permission immersions. Beit Shammai includes immersions for zava status even post-permission — since those immersions are due to the same cause (childbirth as a zava), they are counted together.
Core Dispute Beit Hillel: count immersions that enable marital relations. Beit Shammai: count all obligatory purification acts — including those that follow permission. This reflects a deeper debate about the purpose of immersion counting.

🧬 When Does the Embryo Form? Two Opinions

Rabbi Yishmael

  • Male embryo fully formed in 41 days
  • Female embryo fully formed in 81 days
  • Torah proof: The verse uses double language for female birth — 'תֵּלֵד' appears twice (Lev. 12:2–5), indicating a distinct and longer formation period
  • The 40-day purity period for male / 80-day for female directly parallels embryo formation time
  • Empirical proof: Cleopatra's maidservant experiment showed female embryo formed at 81 days
  • Day-41 discharge: only male-birth strictures; day-81: only female-birth strictures

The Rabbis (Halakha)

  • Both male AND female embryos fully formed in 41 days
  • Double Torah language for female simply specifies it as a separate case — not a longer timeline
  • Cleopatra proof dismissed: 'proof from fools' — the maidservant may have been pregnant before the experiment
  • Day-41 discharge triggers concern for both male and female formation simultaneously
  • Halakha follows the Rabbis: the unattributed first tanna in the Mishna = Rabbis, and the Mishna explicitly restates their view to confirm it is the ruling

✨ Rabbi Samlai's Teaching: The Fetus in the Womb

🗒️ Posture — Folded like a notebook (פִּינְקָס): Hands rest on its temples, arms on its knees, heels on either side of its two buttocks — perfectly compact
↓
🕯️ The Candle above its head: 'When His lamp shined above my head, and by His light I walked through darkness' (Job 29:3) — the fetus gazes from one end of the world to the other
↓
📖 Torah mastery: The entire Torah is taught to the fetus — 'Let your heart hold fast My words; keep My commandments and live' (Proverbs 4:4). The fetus knows all that was once known.
↓
😇 Supreme bliss: No days in a person's life are more blissful than those in the womb — 'If only I were as in the months of old, as in the days when God watched over me' (Job 29:2)
↓
👼 At birth — the slap: An angel slaps the fetus on its mouth. It immediately forgets the entire Torah. 'Sin crouches at the entrance' (Gen. 4:7) — upon entering the world, one is immediately subject to forgetting and temptation.
↓
🤝 The Oath administered before birth: 'Be righteous and do not be wicked. Even if the entire world says you are righteous — consider yourself wicked. The Holy One is pure; His ministers are pure; your soul is pure. Guard it.'

👑 Cleopatra's Experiment — Two Talmudic Versions

ElementVersion 1 (Amud B §6)Version 2 (Amud B §11)
SetupMaidservants sentenced to death; Cleopatra had them engage in intercourse then examined embryos at intervalsGreek queen Cleopatra; maidservants impregnated under strictly controlled conditions
FindingFemale embryo appeared fully formed at 81 daysFemale embryo: 81 days; male embryo: 41 days — directly supporting Rabbi Yishmael
Rabbis' Objection'Proof from fools' — the maidservant may have already been pregnant before the experiment beganShe may have conceived on a later date than assumed, making the timeline unreliable
Rabbi Yishmael's ReplyCleopatra gave the maidservants a purgative medicine beforehand to eliminate any prior pregnancyCleopatra transferred them to a steward's custody to prevent any later intercourse — the date of conception is certain
Remaining ObjectionThe Gemara: even so, how do we know the female embryo wasn't formed earlier than day 81 — perhaps it would have appeared the same if examined on day 41?Even controlled conditions cannot rule out abnormal developmental timing in individual cases

📜 The Mishna: Three Overlapping Strictures on Day 41

הַמַּפֶּלֶת בְּיוֹם אַרְבָּעִים וְאֶחָד — תֵּשֵׁב לְזָכָר וְלִנְקֵבָה וּלְנִדָּה
Mishna — Niddah 30a A woman who discharges on the 40th day: no concern — the embryo is not yet formed. A woman who discharges on the 41st day must observe: • Strictures of a woman who gave birth to a male — relevant if she sees blood on day 34 and again on day 41; her purity count is reset by the earlier bleeding • Strictures of a woman who gave birth to a female — relevant if she sees blood on day 74 and again on day 81; her extended purity period may be interrupted • Strictures of a niddah — she is ritually impure each time she bleeds and does not observe any purity period at all Rabbi Yishmael dissents: Day 41 = male-birth strictures only; Day 81 = female-birth strictures only. The Rabbis' view is the halakha.
