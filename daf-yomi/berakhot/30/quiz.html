QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, what strictures apply to a woman who discharges on the 41st day after immersion?
A. Only the strictures of a woman who gave birth to a male
B. The strictures of a woman who gave birth to a male, a woman who gave birth to a female, AND a niddah
C. Only niddah strictures, since the embryo may not have been viable
D. No special strictures — the embryo is not yet formed on day 41
Correct: B — Day 41 triggers all three sets of strictures because: the embryo may have been male (formed at 41 days), the woman's extended purity periods may be implicated, and her standard niddah impurity also applies — the Rabbis rule all three overlap.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the key distinction between Beit Shammai's count of 95 immersions and Beit Hillel's count of 35?
A. Beit Shammai counts immersions for both male and female births; Beit Hillel only counts for male births
B. Beit Shammai counts all obligatory immersions including post-permission ones; Beit Hillel counts only immersions required before intercourse is permitted
C. Beit Shammai requires immersion every night; Beit Hillel only requires immersion at specific milestones
D. They agree on the count but disagree on who must perform each immersion
Correct: B — Beit Hillel counts the 35 immersions that are prerequisites for permitting marital relations. Beit Shammai counts all 95 obligatory immersions — including those performed after the woman is already permitted, such as immersions due to possible zava status.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Samlai's teaching, what happens to the fetus's Torah knowledge at birth?
A. It is retained in the soul and surfaces through intuition throughout life
B. It is gradually forgotten over the first seven years of childhood
C. An angel slaps the fetus on the mouth at birth, causing it to forget the entire Torah instantly
D. The Torah knowledge is transferred to the mother during labor
Correct: C — At birth, an angel strikes the fetus on its mouth and it forgets all the Torah it learned in the womb. The verse 'Sin crouches at the entrance' (Gen. 4:7) hints at this: upon entering the world, the person is immediately subject to forgetting and the evil inclination.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How did Rabbi Yishmael defend Cleopatra's experiment against the Rabbis' objection that the maidservants may have been previously pregnant?
A. He argued that multiple maidservants were examined, making prior pregnancy statistically unlikely
B. He noted that Cleopatra administered a purgative medicine to them beforehand to terminate any existing pregnancy
C. He claimed the maidservants were too young to have been previously pregnant
D. He cited a second, independent experiment that confirmed the same result
Correct: B — Rabbi Yishmael countered that Cleopatra gave the maidservants a purgative drink before the controlled impregnation, which would have ended any prior pregnancy. This removed the objection that their embryos were formed before the experiment began.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the content of the oath administered to the fetus before it leaves the womb?
A. Swear to study Torah every day and teach it to your children
B. Swear to honor your father and mother all the days of your life
C. Be righteous and not wicked; even if the whole world calls you righteous, consider yourself wicked
D. Swear to observe all 613 commandments and not to transgress them
Correct: C — The angels administer this oath: 'Be righteous and do not be wicked. And even if the entire world says to you that you are righteous — consider yourself wicked.' The oath also invokes God's purity, the purity of His ministering angels, and the purity of the soul entrusted to the person.
