Berakhot 48 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav (as stated by Rav Giddel), to which halakha does the baraita's mention of 'son and daughter' requiring whole years refer?
A. The wayward and rebellious son (בן סורר ומורה)
B. Valuations (ערכין) — the age of the person being valuated
C. A Hebrew slave's six years of service
D. The laws of inheritance from a father
Correct: B — Rav Giddel says that Rav says the baraita refers to valuations (ערכין): the age of a valuated male or female must be calculated in complete whole years, not partial ones.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the Rabbis, what is the halakhic ruling when a woman has visible breast development but no pubic hair is found upon examination?
A. She is definitively a minor, exempt from all levirate obligations
B. She must perform ḥalitza or enter levirate marriage, as the lower sign is presumed to exist
C. The matter is uncertain and requires a Beit Din of three
D. She may choose whether to perform ḥalitza based on her own preference
Correct: B — The Rabbis hold it is impossible for the upper sign to precede the lower. Breast development proves the pubic hair must exist even if not found, making her a full adult subject to ḥalitza or levirate marriage.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rava's presumption state regarding a girl who has reached twelve years and one day?
A. She must still be examined by a court of three male judges before her status is determined
B. She is presumed to have already developed the signs of puberty and requires no examination
C. She is presumed to still be a minor unless signs are positively confirmed
D. She must wait an additional thirty days before her majority status is valid
Correct: B — Rava rules that a minor who has reached her full years (twelve years and one day) does not require examination — there is a standing presumption that she has already developed pubic hair.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
During which age period does Rabbi Yehuda hold that women are NOT deemed credible to examine girls for signs of puberty?
A. Before eleven years and one day
B. Between eleven years and one day and twelve years and one day
C. After twelve years and one day
D. Women are never credible examiners according to Rabbi Yehuda
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda permits women to examine before 11+1 day (hair is just a mole) and after 12+1 day (Rava's presumption applies). But the critical window of 11–12 years requires more rigorous examination — women are excluded during this period.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
A woman examines a girl and testifies that she has NOT yet reached majority, implying the girl can perform refusal (מיאון) to annul her marriage. How is this testimony treated?
A. Accepted as a leniency to free her from the marriage
B. Accepted as a stringency because it restricts the marriage relationship
C. Rejected entirely — only male judges may testify about minority status
D. Accepted only if corroborated by a second female examiner
Correct: B — The baraita teaches that women's testimony is accepted only as a stringency, not a leniency. Testifying she is a minor allows refusal (מיאון), which is a restriction on the marriage — this counts as a stringency, not a leniency.
