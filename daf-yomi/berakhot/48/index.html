⚖️ The Order of Puberty Signs

Rabbi Meir
It is possible for the upper sign (breast development) to appear before the lower sign (pubic hair). Therefore, if only the upper sign is present and no pubic hair is found upon examination, she does not perform ḥalitza and does not enter levirate marriage — she remains a minor.
The Rabbis
It is impossible for the upper sign to precede the lower. Therefore, if breast development is present, we assume the lower sign already exists — even if not found — and she must perform ḥalitza or enter levirate marriage as a full adult.
Practical Consequence The debate determines levirate marriage (יבום) and ḥalitza obligations for a young woman whose childless husband dies: is she a minor exempt from these laws, or a full adult bound by them?

📚 Rav vs Rav Yosef: What Do 'Son and Daughter' Refer To?

Rav (via Rav Giddel)
The baraita's mention of 'son and daughter' as requiring whole years refers to valuations (ערכין) — the ages of a male or female being valuated must be calculated in complete years, not partial.
Rav Yosef
The baraita refers to the wayward and rebellious son (בן סורר ומורה) — whose qualifying age of 13 years and one day must be reckoned in whole years to establish liability.
Abaye's Resolution The two interpretations are complementary, not contradictory. Each Amora simply addresses a different halakha — they do not reject each other's application.

🏙️ Environmental Differences in Puberty Development

City Women

  • Lower sign (pubic hair) appears more quickly than upper sign
  • Attributed to greater physical activity and movement of city life
  • Supports the standard ordering: lower sign precedes upper sign

Daughters of the Wealthy

  • Right breast develops before left breast
  • Reason: it rubs against fine clothing — friction accelerates development
  • Stated by Rabbi Shimon ben Elazar

Daughters of the Poor

  • Left breast develops before right breast
  • Reason: carrying heavy loads on the left side during labor
  • Directly opposite pattern to wealthy daughters

👩 Women's Testimony: Accepted as Stringency Only

Step 1: A woman examines a girl to determine whether signs of puberty have appeared
↓
Step 2 — Accepted (Stringency): Woman says girl has reached majority → accepted to impose ḥalitza obligation, since she cannot simply remarry
↓
Step 3 — Accepted (Stringency): Woman says girl is still a minor → accepted to allow refusal (מיאון), which restricts the marriage
↓
Step 4 — Rejected (Leniency): Woman says girl has reached majority in order to permit levirate marriage → testimony NOT accepted as a leniency
↓
Step 5 — Rejected (Leniency): Woman says girl is a minor to exempt her from ḥalitza → testimony NOT accepted as a leniency
