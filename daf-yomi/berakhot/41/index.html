⚖️ Core Dispute: Does Caesarean Section Constitute Halakhic Birth?

Rabbi Shimon
Caesarean section is a full-fledged halakhic birth. Blood discharged before caesarean delivery is birth blood — the woman is a yoledet, not a zava.
Rabbis
Caesarean birth does not have full halakhic birth status. Pre-caesarean blood is ziva blood and the woman must count seven clean days.
Rav Yosef
The latter clause of the baraita addresses a case where both the infant and blood emerged through the abdominal incision — not vaginally. Their dispute is whether the uterine location itself is impure.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
A woman becomes impure only when blood emerges from her nakedness — i.e., exits vaginally. Internal blood, even in the uterine cavity, does not trigger impurity. Source: Ezekiel 16:36.
Dual Stakes The status of caesarean birth governs both (1) whether sacrificial-animal offspring inherit sanctity, and (2) whether a woman becomes a niddah or a zava after such a delivery.

🐄 Sacrificial Animals Born by Caesarean — Why Two Verses?

Baraita
A caesarean-born animal is unfit for sacrifice. Two verses are required for each disqualification category (caesarean birth, copulation with a person, diverse kinds).
Gemara
"This is the law of the burnt offering" (Lev. 6:2) teaches that the offspring of a sacrificial animal born by caesarean does not inherit the mother's sanctity.
Gemara
First verse: a non-sacred animal with this flaw cannot be consecrated. Second verse: even if a consecrated animal gives birth by caesarean, the offspring acquires no sanctity.
Conclusion
This dual-verse structure applies equally to: (1) caesarean offspring, (2) animals that copulated with a person, and (3) animals born of kilayim — diverse kinds.
Scripture "When a bull or sheep or goat is born" (Lev. 22:27) — 'born' (יִוָּלֵד) excludes caesarean-born animals from firstborn status.

⚔️ Reish Lakish vs. Rabbi Yoḥanan: Is the Uterine Location Itself Impure?

Reish Lakish (First Tanna)

  • The location of the source (uterus) is ritually impure
  • Blood that exits via caesarean incision is impure — it passed through an impure space
  • Dislodged uterus fallen to the ground: woman is impure
  • Two pearl-like drops from the source render her impure
  • Impurity flows from the uterine space itself, not only from vaginal blood exit

Rabbi Yoḥanan (Rabbi Shimon ben Yoḥai)

  • The location of the source is NOT independently impure
  • A woman becomes impure only when blood emerges from her nakedness — Ezek. 16:36
  • Blood exiting via caesarean incision is pure — wrong exit point
  • Impurity requires actual blood flow through the vaginal opening
  • Internal uterine blood or abdominal discharge alone is insufficient

💧 Semen Discharge After Intercourse — Impurity and Immersion

Woman engages in intercourse — she is impure as a ba'alat keri until evening
↓
She immerses in a mikveh — now ritually pure, but semen may remain internally
↓
She later discharges semen — does this trigger new impurity?
↓
First Baraita (Rabbi Shimon): It is sufficient for her to be like the man she lay with — a single immersion until evening suffices
↓
Second Baraita: A woman who lies with a man becomes impure for seven days — appears to contradict Rabbi Shimon!
↓
Gemara's resolution: The seven-day ruling addresses the act of intercourse itself; Rabbi Shimon's leniency applies only when she already immersed and later discharges semen — a lesser, separate contact
↓
Rava adds: A woman may immerse while still lying in bed after intercourse; if she remains supine, semen may not discharge at all — and the immersion is fully valid
