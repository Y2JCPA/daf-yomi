🌱 The Critical Year: Do Puberty Signs 'During the Time' Confer Adulthood?

Rav Hamnuna
If a child develops two hairs during the time (the critical year before full adulthood), this is equivalent to after the time — rendering the child an adult.
Rabbi Zeira
I object from a baraita: the word ish ('man') in the verse applies only to one who is fully adult. Signs during the time cannot automatically confer adult status.
Rav Naḥman
This is not a refutation — it is a tannaitic dispute. The baraita's own internal clauses reflect both views, so both positions have tanna'im supporting them.
Rava (Final Halakha)
The halakha: Development of two hairs during the time is treated as before the time — it does not render one an adult.
📖 Key Term 'During the time' (תוך הזמן) = the 12th year for girls, 13th year for boys — the transitional year before adulthood is confirmed

💍 The 12-Year-Old Girl: Ḥalitza or Refusal?

Gemara's Problem
Rava says a 12-year-old girl cannot perform mi'un (refusal) — implying she is an adult. But if she is adult, she should be able to perform ḥalitza. Why doesn't Rava say so?
First Answer (Rejected)
Perhaps Rava is uncertain whether she has developed hairs. But the Gemara objects: we have a presumption that a 12-year-old has already grown two hairs — uncertainty alone doesn't suffice.
Rav Dimi of Neharde'a
The halakha: If she reached 12 and was examined and no signs found — we are concerned the hairs fell out. This applies when betrothed before 12 and consummated after.
Final Resolution
She was not examined. For ḥalitza we fear she may lack hairs (still a minor). For refusal, presumption is she has hairs. Result: neither option is certain.
⚠️ Halakhic Limbo The girl is trapped between two statuses — too uncertain to perform refusal as a confirmed minor, too uncertain to perform ḥalitza as a confirmed adult

📜 Vows of a Discriminating Minor: Three Positions

Rav Huna

  • Minor 'during the time' with understanding of vows and no confirmed hairs
  • Vows are VALID — like an adult
  • Source: 'When a man or woman shall clearly utter a vow' (Num. 6:2)
  • Ability to articulate vow in God's name → full vow validity

Rava

  • During the time = before the time (per his halakhic ruling)
  • Minor's vows valid only by Rabbinic law
  • Torah vow validity requires confirmed adulthood
  • Discriminating minor ≠ full adult for Torah purposes

Baraita

  • Discriminating minor = adult for a 'vow of prohibition'
  • = adult regarding 'he shall not profane his word'
  • But NOT subject to lashes for violations
  • 'This is the matter' (Num. 30:2) — limits scope of the equation

🌾 Teruma of a Discriminating Minor: Rabbi Yehuda vs. Rabbi Yosei

Mishna (Terumot 1:3): A minor who has not grown two hairs — Rabbi Yehuda: his teruma is NOT valid. Rabbi Yosei: it IS valid.
↓
Abaye's inference: If a discriminating minor is an adult by Torah law, Rabbi Yosei's ruling is sensible. If only rabbinic — Rabbi Yehuda's invalidation is understandable.
↓
Challenge to Rabbi Yosei: He authored Seder Olam, which implies the second sanctification of Eretz Yisrael never lapsed — meaning teruma is Torah law even today!
↓
If teruma is Torah law and Rabbi Yosei validates a minor's teruma, this implies the discriminating minor has full Torah-level adult status — a very strong claim.
↓
Resolution: Rabbi Yosei taught Seder Olam but does not necessarily rule in accordance with it here. The two positions are separable — he may hold teruma is presently rabbinic.
