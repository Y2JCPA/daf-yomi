5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava's final halakhic ruling, if a child develops two pubic hairs 'during the time' (the critical transitional year), this is treated as:
A. After the time — rendering the child an adult immediately
B. Before the time — the child remains a minor
C. Ambiguous — no definitive ruling was reached
D. Dependent on whether the child is male or female
Correct: B — Rava rules explicitly: 'The halakha is that the development of two hairs during the time is considered as before the time, and does not render one an adult.'
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why is a 12-year-old girl who was examined and found to have no pubic hairs unable to perform either refusal (mi'un) OR ḥalitza with certainty?
A. Because she is definitively a minor and both acts are forbidden to her
B. Because she is definitively an adult and both acts are inapplicable
C. Because we fear the hairs fell out (blocking refusal as a minor) while she may still truly be a minor (blocking certain ḥalitza as an adult)
D. Because neither mi'un nor ḥalitza applies to any girl under age 13
Correct: C — The Gemara concludes: We are concerned that hairs fell out (so she cannot do refusal as a confirmed minor), yet adulthood is not certain enough to permit ḥalitza. She is in halakhic limbo.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rav Huna holds that a discriminating minor's vows are fully valid. What additional conditions does he require beyond being within the transitional year?
A. The minor must have two valid witnesses present
B. The minor must be aware of the vow's meaning and that it is in God's name
C. The minor must obtain prior rabbinic approval
D. The minor must have already reached 12 years and one day
Correct: B — Rav Huna derives from Numbers 6:2: if one on the brink of adulthood can articulate that the vow is in God's name — and the other conditions are met — the vow is valid like an adult's.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Regarding a minor who consecrated a food item and others ate it — which pair of authorities hold that the eaters ARE flogged?
A. Rav Kahana and Rav Naḥman
B. Rava and Abaye
C. Rabbi Yoḥanan and Reish Lakish
D. Rabbi Zeira and Rav Hamnuna
Correct: C — The Gemara states: 'Rabbi Yoḥanan and Reish Lakish both say that they are flogged' — holding that a discriminating minor's consecration carries sufficient halakhic force to render eating it a Torah violation.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What challenge does the Gemara raise against Rabbi Yosei's view that a discriminating minor's teruma is valid, based on Seder Olam?
A. Seder Olam explicitly prohibits minors from separating teruma
B. Rabbi Yosei authored Seder Olam, which implies the second sanctification of Eretz Yisrael did not lapse — meaning teruma is Torah law, making a minor's teruma validity hard to explain
C. Seder Olam records Rabbi Yehuda agreeing with Rabbi Yosei on this point
D. Seder Olam holds teruma does not apply at all after the Temple's destruction
Correct: B — If Rabbi Yosei (Seder Olam's author) holds the second sanctification never lapsed, teruma would be Torah law today. Validating a minor's teruma would then require granting the discriminating minor full Torah-level adult status — a very difficult position to maintain.
