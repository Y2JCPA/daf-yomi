QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara's conclusion, what level of impurity does bedding ABOVE a zav impart?
A. Severe impurity — imparts to people and garments just like bedding beneath him
B. Lesser impurity — imparts only to food and drink, not to people
C. No impurity at all — only bedding beneath a zav is impure
D. Identical impurity to the zav himself
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that bedding above a zav is removed from severe impurity and imparts impurity only to food and drink — not to people or garments.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why are Samaritan men presumed to be ba'alei niddah (men who cohabited with a menstruating woman)?
A. Their wives count seven impurity days from every blood emission, including green (pure) blood, making them always presumed impure
B. Samaritan men never observe any menstrual separation laws whatsoever
C. The Sages issued a decree against all Samaritans as a communal penalty
D. Samaritan women are impure from birth according to rabbinic ruling
Correct: A — Samaritan women count seven days of impurity even from green (non-impure) blood, and if red blood appears during those seven days they do not restart — leaving them perpetually in niddah status and their husbands presumed ba'alei niddah.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Abaye say prevents us from applying a generalization-and-detail hermeneutic between "her impurity be upon him" and "every bed upon which he lies"?
A. The two phrases appear in entirely different chapters of Leviticus
B. A different subject is introduced between the two phrases
C. The phrase "he shall be impure seven days" is written between them, creating a distant generalization-and-detail
D. The verse explicitly says not to compare the two cases
Correct: C — Abaye says "he shall be impure seven days" interrupts between the generalization and detail — making it a distant כְּלָל וּפְרָט, treated as a generalization-detail-generalization which expands rather than limits.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the halakhic status of Sadducee women who abandoned their ancestors' practices and follow the ways of the perushim (Sages)?
A. Like Samaritan women — still presumed impure
B. Like a safek (doubtful case) requiring additional stringency
C. Like non-Jewish women — outside the rabbinic system
D. Like Jewish women in all respects
Correct: D — The mishna explicitly rules that Sadducee women who abandoned their ancestors' ways and follow perushim practices are to be treated like Jewish women in all respects.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Rava say Rami bar Ḥama's dilemma about a zava discharging semen is not a real dilemma?
A. A zava is halakhically incapable of discharging semen, so the case is impossible
B. The laws of ziva simply do not apply to the scenario Rami bar Ḥama described
C. Whether she negates all seven days or just one, both are impossible: "seven days" requires all seven complete, and negating only one still violates the requirement for consecutive clean days
D. Seminal discharge is not an impurity for a woman and therefore cannot affect her count at all
Correct: C — Rava shows neither option works: negating all seven contradicts the verse requiring "seven days" (plural, all complete); negating only one day also fails because the verse says "after that she shall be pure," requiring the entire seven-day span to be uninterrupted by impurity of ziva.
