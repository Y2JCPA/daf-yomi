⚖️ Comparing Ba'al Niddah to the Niddah Herself

Baraita
"And her impurity be upon him" (Lev. 15:24) — the man assumes her status. Just as she imparts impurity to bedding beneath her, so does he impart impurity to bedding beneath him.
Rav Aḥai
The verse only removed him from severe impurity — his bedding does not impart impurity to a person who in turn imparts it to their garments.
Gemara
"Her impurity be upon him" is a generalization; "every bed upon which he lies" is a detail — apply the כְּלָל וּפְרָט hermeneutic?
Abaye
"He shall be impure seven days" interrupts between them — this is a distant generalization-and-detail, treated as כְּלָל וּפְרָט וּכְלָל, expanding the rule broadly.
Rava
"And her impurity be upon him" — the Torah means for the impurity to weigh heavily upon him, stringently. It is not a full equation to the woman herself.
Core Verse "וְטֻמְאָתָהּ עָלָיו" — And her impurity be upon him (Lev. 15:24)

🩸 Samaritan vs. Jewish Women's Impurity Practices

Samaritan Women (Problematic Practices)

  • Begin counting 7 impurity days from green (pure) blood emission
  • If red blood appears during those 7 days, they do NOT restart the count
  • Count the very day they cease ziva emissions toward 7 clean days
  • Result: Always in niddah status → husbands are presumed ba'alei niddah

Jewish Women (Correct Halakha)

  • Only red (impure) blood triggers niddah counting
  • A new emission of red blood restarts the seven-day count
  • Day of cessation of ziva is NOT counted toward 7 clean days
  • Part-day status as full day applies in some contexts — debated by Rami bar Ḥama

📅 Zav's Seminal Emission During the Seven Clean Days

Zav ceases his ziva and begins counting seven clean days toward purification
↓
During the count, he experiences a seminal emission (קרי)
↓
Verse: "This is the law of the zav and of him from whom seed goes out" (Lev. 15:32) — the Torah equates them
↓
The seminal emission negates that one day from his count — he does not restart from zero
↓
Rami bar Ḥama's dilemma: Does a zava who discharges semen also negate her count — or is she merely touching it?
↓
Rava refutes: Not a real dilemma — she cannot negate all 7 days (Torah requires seven), nor just one day (Torah says they must all be complete). The emission is irrelevant to her count.

👩 Sadducee Women and the High Priest — A Famous Incident

בנות צדוקים — כשנהגו ללכת בדרכי אבותיהן הרי הן כשומרוניות; עזבו ללכת בדרכי ישראל — הרי הן כישראל
Talmudic Story A Sadducee conversed with the High Priest in the marketplace. Saliva (צִינּוֹרָא) sprayed from his mouth onto the High Priest's garments, rendering them impure.

The High Priest's wife consoled him: "Even wives of Sadducees show their blood to the Sages when uncertain — your garments were already impure before this incident!"

Rabbi Yosei testified: All Sadducee women in our neighborhood show their blood to Sages — except one who refused and was punished.

Halakhic ruling: Sadducee women who follow perushim practices → like Jewish women. Those who follow Sadducee ancestors → like Samaritan women.
