Berakhot 49 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Rabbi Yosei rejects the fire test for vessel holes. What is his objection?
A. Earthenware cannot withstand direct fire
B. Heat causes liquid to contract, so even a large hole may not drip — making the test unreliable
C. The fire test was only valid in Temple times
D. Only cold water tests are halachically accepted
Correct: B — Rabbi Yosei argues that heat causes the liquid inside the vessel to contract. Therefore, even a hole large enough to allow liquid entry might not allow liquid to drip out when heated, making the fire test an unreliable indicator of hole size.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav (as cited by Rav Ḥisda), what is the impurity status of an extra finger that contains a bone but no nail?
A. It has absolutely no impurity status
B. It transmits tent (ohel) impurity only
C. It transmits contact and movement impurity, but not tent impurity
D. It is treated identically to a regular complete finger
Correct: C — Rav holds that a finger with bone but no nail still transmits impurity through contact and through movement (הסט), but it does NOT transmit tent impurity, since the absence of a nail means it lacks the full signs of a complete limb.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does a wooden shield not become ritually impure through zav-treading, even though it can receive corpse impurity?
A. Wooden objects are entirely exempt from zav laws
B. Zav-treading impurity applies only to objects designated for sitting or lying — a shield is not such an object
C. The shield must touch the zav directly to become impure
D. A shield is too large to be moved by a zav
Correct: B — Leviticus 15:6 specifies 'any object whereon the zav sits,' which the Sages interpret as objects designated for sitting or lying. A shield is used for protection, not for sitting, so zav-treading impurity does not apply — though it remains a vessel susceptible to corpse impurity.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why must the Talmud separately state that BOTH a mamzer and a convert are fit for monetary but not capital adjudication?
A. Because each case alone would lead to a mistaken leniency regarding the other
B. Because mamzerim and converts were entirely unknown to earlier halachic sources
C. Because monetary law requires three judges while capital requires twenty-three
D. To establish that all foreign-born Jews have equal judicial status
Correct: A — If we only knew the convert's fitness for monetary law, we might not extend it to the mamzer (whose issue is different). If we only knew the mamzer's, we might not extend it to the convert. Each case contains a unique reason; both are necessary to prevent incorrect reasoning by analogy.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, which case does the mishna (Niddah 49b) add as someone fit to testify but unfit to adjudicate capital cases?
A. A mamzer
B. A convert from a non-Jewish nation
C. One who is blind in one eye
D. A minor who has reached the age of majority
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan identifies one blind in one eye as the novel case of the mishna. Such a person retains sufficient vision to serve as a witness, but capital adjudication demands full perceptual capacity — both eyes — to fulfill the requirement of judging righteously.
