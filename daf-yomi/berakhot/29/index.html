⚔️ When Is a Fetus Halakhically Born?

Rabbi Elazar
Head emergence of a nefel (non-viable newborn) is NOT equivalent to the emergence of most limbs. The head alone does not constitute birth in this case.
Rabbi Yohanan
Head emergence of a nefel IS equivalent to most limbs. Just as a head-first birth exempts the subsequent twin from firstborn status (per Shmuel), so too here — the head alone renders birth complete.
Core Dispute Does Shmuel's rule — that the head of a twin emerging and retreating exempts the second twin — apply to non-viable newborns? Rabbi Elazar says no; Rabbi Yohanan says yes. Rav Pappa and Rav Zevid then debate which tanna'im parallel each position.

📜 Rav Pappa vs Rav Zevid: Reading the Baraita

Rav Pappa
The baraita says piecemeal or reversed (separately). By inference, if born properly head-first, the head alone suffices — supporting Rabbi Yohanan. The second baraita confirms this reading.
Rav Zevid
Since Rabbi Yosei requires both majority of limbs AND proper position together, the baraita means piecemeal AND reversed simultaneously. For a normal birth, only the majority of the head is needed — not a full majority of limbs.
Explicit Baraita Confirms Rav Zevid A second baraita states explicitly: normal birth = majority of head. Abba Hanan, in the name of Rabbi Eliezer, defines this as when the temples emerge (פדחתו).

🌊 Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi: Presumption of Viable Birth

חזקה — בדוקה מעוברת ילדה ולד של קיימא
Key Ruling — חזקה A pregnant woman crossed a river and discharged her fetus into the water — she brings the offering of a woman after childbirth without doubt (not merely as a precaution). Since she was confirmed (beduka) pregnant beforehand, we presume she delivered a viable, fully-formed offspring. The chazakah of confirmed pregnancy creates a presumption of viable birth.

Caveat: This ruling applies when she had a confirmed prior pregnancy. The mishna's case of unknown-sex birth deals with a woman not previously confirmed pregnant.

🛁 Beit Shammai's 95 Immersions: Week-by-Week

Week 1 — 7 Immersions

  • Immerse every night (7 nights)
  • Reason: may have ended niddah/birth impurity on any night
  • Running total: 7

Week 2 — 7 Immersions

  • Immerse every night (7 nights)
  • Reason: if female birth, 14-day period may end on any night of week 2
  • Running total: 14

Week 3 — 14 Immersions

  • Immerse every day (7) AND every night (7)
  • Reason: possible zava counting — immerse by day after 7 clean days
  • Running total: 28

Weeks 4–5 — ~67 Immersions

  • Daily immersions through purity days
  • Accounts for uncertainty in when purity days began
  • Grand total: 95 immersions (Beit Shammai)
