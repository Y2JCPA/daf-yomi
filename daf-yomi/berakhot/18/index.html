⚖️ When Majority Becomes Certainty

בְּשָׁלֹשׁ מְקוֹמוֹת הָלְכוּ אַחַר הָרֹב
Core Principle — Rabbi Yoḥanan Rabbi Yoḥanan teaches that in three specific cases of uncertainty (safek), the Sages followed the majority (rov) and ruled as though the matter were certain. These cases — the uterine source, the afterbirth, and the shaped limb — are strictly limited to matters of a woman's ritual impurity that appear in Mishnah or baraita, not amoraic rulings, not prohibitions, and not cases where an opposing presumptive status (chazakah) counters the majority.

🩸 Disputants on Blood Location

Rav Ketina
Blood found in the outer section is impure due to uncertainty — we cannot confirm it came from the uterus.
Rav Huna
Blood in the inner section is definitely impure (presumed uterine). Blood in the outer section is impure only due to uncertainty.
Rami bar Shmuel & Rav Yitzḥak b. Rav Yehuda
Blood from the vestibule outward — uncertainty renders it pure. From the vestibule inward — impure due to uncertainty.
Rabbi Ḥiyya
Blood in certain locations is definitely impure — not merely a case of doubt.
Gemara Resolution Rami bar Shmuel and Rav Yitzḥak may actually agree with Rabbi Ḥiyya — their respective rulings address different anatomical zones, so no real dispute exists.

⚖️ Rabbi Yoḥanan's Three Cases of Majority

1️⃣ The Source

  • Blood found in the uterine area
  • Rov: most blood originates from the uterus
  • Ruled definitely impure despite the doubt
  • Uncertainty fully resolved by majority

2️⃣ The Afterbirth

  • Afterbirth found in house after miscarriage
  • Rov: most afterbirths contain a fetus
  • House ruled definitely impure
  • Full corpse-impurity (tum'at ohel) applies

3️⃣ Shaped Limb

  • Woman miscarries a shaped hand or foot
  • Rov: such limbs belong to fully formed fetuses
  • Mother ruled impure as after birth
  • Full birth-impurity (leidah) laws apply

🤰 Pregnant Woman Who Miscarried in the River

Pregnant woman crosses a river and miscarries into the water
↓
She does not know whether the fetus was fully formed
↓
Follow the Majority (Rov)
Most pregnant women give birth to fully formed offspring — rule as if the fetus was formed
Practical Ruling
She brings the full offerings of childbirth: a burnt offering + sin offering (bird). The sin offering is eaten, not suspended — ruling is as certain.
↓
Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi: Follow rov — majority of women bear fully formed children. Uncertainty treated as certainty.

🧩 When Does Rabbi Yoḥanan's Principle Apply?

Requirement 1 — Matter of Women's Ritual Purity: The case must involve tum'at nashim. Cases of general ritual impurity (frogs) or prohibitions (non-kosher meat) are excluded.
↓
Requirement 2 — Taught in Mishnah or Baraita: Only tannaitic sources qualify. Rulings derived solely from Amoraic statements (e.g., Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi) are excluded from the list.
↓
Requirement 3 — No Opposing Chazakah: If a majority pointing toward impurity is countered by a presumptive status (chazakah) pointing toward purity, the case is excluded — as in the impure child found near dough.
↓
Dispute — Rabbi Meir vs. Rabbis (Child & Dough): Rabbi Meir: majority overrides → dough is impure. Rabbis: minority is nullified, but chazakah of purity conflicts — uncertainty remains.
↓
Reish Lakish (citing Rabbi Oshaya): The child-and-dough case is driven by a chazakah (children handle nearby items) — not mere majority — so teruma is burned, reflecting elevated certainty.
