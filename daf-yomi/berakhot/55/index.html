💧 Niddah 55 — Corpse Matter & Bodily Fluids of the Zav

נִדָּה נ"ה
Core Theme Daf 55 covers three domains: (1) how a zav or menstruant transmits impurity through sitting on non-susceptible surfaces; (2) which detached parts of a corpse transmit impurity, and whether dryness matters — debated by Reish Lakish and Rabbi Yoḥanan; and (3) the Torah sources for the impurity of ziva, saliva, mucus, and the nine bodily fluids of a zav.
3 / 9

💀 Corpse Flesh: Does Dryness Remove Impurity?

Reish Lakish
Derived from the verse about creeping things (Leviticus 11:31): corpse flesh transmits impurity both moist and dry — except when it is so desiccated it crumbles to dust.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Derived from Numbers 19:16 (the slain in the field): the verse equates corpse flesh to bone — flesh transmits impurity even fully dried, as long as it retains its form and has not disintegrated.
Baraita
"Flesh that crumbled is ritually pure" — this resolves Reish Lakish: only flesh dried to the point of crumbling is pure; firm dried flesh remains impure for both opinions.
Practical Difference A corpse so dried it crumbles: Reish Lakish — pure (like dust); Rabbi Yoḥanan — impure (form still identifiable as flesh).

🦷 Teeth, Hair & Nails — Attached vs. Detached

When Still Attached to Corpse

  • Transmit impurity together with the corpse
  • Considered an integral part of the body
  • Full corpse impurity (טומאת מת) applies
  • Source: Mishna Oholot 3:3

When Detached from Corpse

  • Pure — do NOT independently transmit impurity
  • Teeth: not present at the moment of birth
  • Hair & nails: root renews itself after removal
  • Skin: pure by Torah law (rabbinic decree impure)
  • Rav Adda bar Ahava's two-part test governs

🔑 Rav Adda bar Ahava: What Transmits Corpse Impurity When Detached?

Does this detached body part transmit corpse impurity?
↓
✅ YES — Transmits Impurity
Created with the person at birth
AND
Root does NOT renew if removed
(e.g., bone, flesh — removal is permanent)
❌ NO — Pure When Detached
Not present at birth → teeth
OR
Root renews itself → hair, nails
Skin: pure by Torah; rabbis decreed impurity lest one fashion a mat from human skin (Ulla)
↓
Flesh — special case: Mar bar Rav Ashi rules flesh does not truly renew; regrowth after a wound is new tissue, not restoration of the original — so flesh remains impure

📜 Deriving Ziva & Saliva Impurity from Torah — Step by Step

Base verse (Leviticus 15:2): "When any man has an issue out of his flesh — his issue, it is impure" — explicitly establishes ziva as ritually impure
↓
A fortiori rejected: One might argue — since ziva causes impurity to the person, surely the discharge itself is impure. Rejected: the scapegoat causes impurity to those who handle it, yet the goat itself is pure (Leviticus 16:26)
↓
Rav Yehuda of Diskarta: "Zov" = 1 emission; "zovo" (superfluous) = 2; "his issue, it is impure" = 3 — three emissions required to become a full zav; impurity applies from the first emission
↓
Carrying vs. Contact: One verse derives impurity by carrying; another by contact — both modes of transmission are independently required and derived
↓
Saliva (Leviticus 15:8): "If one who has an issue spits" — amplified by 'and' to include phlegm, mucus, nasal fluid; verbal analogy with yevama (חליצה) teaches impurity requires actual contact, not mere proximity
