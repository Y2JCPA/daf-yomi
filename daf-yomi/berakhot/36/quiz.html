Berakhot 36 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi's final ruling, the halakha follows which opinion in the debate between Rav and Levi?
A. Levi — there are two distinct sources of blood
B. Rav — there is one source, whether the ruling is lenient or stringent
C. Neither — the matter is left unresolved
D. Rav only when his ruling is lenient; Levi when stringent
Correct: B — Rav Ashi ruled explicitly that halakha follows Rav — whether as a leniency or a stringency. Mareimar issued the identical ruling, closing the dispute.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava's interpretation, what is the status of a pregnant woman who experiences blood due to labor pains specifically during the 11 days of ziva?
A. She is a full zava gedola and must count 7 clean days
B. She is a niddah for that day and may not be with her husband
C. She is completely pure and permitted to her husband
D. She must wait until she gives birth to determine her status
Correct: C — Rava reads the mishna as: during days of menstruation she is a niddah, but during the 11 days of ziva she is pure. Labor blood is entirely excluded from creating ziva status.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What verse does Shmuel's father use to derive that labor blood can render a woman a niddah but NOT a zava?
A. "And if a woman has an issue of her blood" (Lev. 15:25)
B. "She shall observe the blood of purity" (Lev. 12:5)
C. "She shall be impure two weeks, as in her menstruation" (Lev. 12:5)
D. "And a woman, if she has an issue in her flesh" (Lev. 15:19)
Correct: C — Shmuel's father derives from "as in her menstruation" — not "as in her ziva" — that labor blood follows the niddah track, not the ziva track. It can make her impure as a niddah but cannot render her a zava.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How did Rav Asi justify his claim that Rav had retracted his ruling, despite Sheila bar Avina's refusal to accept it?
A. He produced written documentation from Rav's academy
B. He cited two other Amoraim who witnessed the retraction
C. He invoked his identity as Isi ben Yehuda — a copper mortar that does not forget — to assert reliable transmission
D. He argued that the retraction was implied by Rav's later rulings on similar cases
Correct: C — Rav Asi declared: "I am Isi ben Yehuda... a copper mortar [asita de-nachsheta] that does not forget," linking himself to a chain of reliable tradents to assert his transmission of Rav's retraction was trustworthy.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Meir, how far before birth do labor pains retroactively attribute blood to labor (rather than ziva)?
A. Only during active labor in the final hours before birth
B. Up to 7 days before birth
C. Up to 30 days before birth
D. Even 40 or 50 days before birth
Correct: D — Rabbi Meir rules that even 40 or 50 days before birth, any blood a woman sees during the 11 days of ziva is attributed to her labor pains and does not render her a zava — the attribution to labor is very broadly construed.
