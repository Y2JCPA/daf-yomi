Inheriting Debts That Require Oaths

Lender holds a promissory note against borrower
↓
Does the borrower die during lender's lifetime?
Yes — Borrower dies first
Lender already owes an oath to borrower's orphans → Cannot bequeath oath to his heirs (Rav & Shmuel)
No — Lender dies first
Lender's heirs take the heirs' oath and collect (R' Elazar)
Key Principle אין אדם מוריש שבועה לבניו — A person cannot bequeath an oath to his children (Rav & Shmuel)

Contradictory New Moon Witnesses

Baraita
If one witness says the moon was 3 oxgoads high and another says 5, their testimony is voided — but they are not disqualified and may join other testimony.
Rav Ḥisda
But I hold that a witness suspected of false testimony in one case cannot testify in monetary matters!
Rava
The baraita means each can join another witness for new moon testimony — becoming 2 against 1. It does not refer to monetary cases.
Resolution Rava saves Rav Ḥisda's position: the contradicted witness joins another for new moon testimony only, where two outweigh one.

Customer vs. Storekeeper & Money Changer

Storekeeper Case

  • Customer asks for produce worth a dinar
  • Storekeeper gives produce
  • Dispute: Did customer pay?
  • Customer takes oath he paid
  • R' Yehuda: Only if produce still in front of them

Money Changer Case

  • Customer asks for small coins worth a dinar
  • Money changer gives coins
  • Dispute: Did customer give the dinar?
  • Customer takes oath he paid
  • Both cases needed — neither can be derived from the other
Why Both? Produce spoils (so storekeeper gives it quickly) — coins don't spoil (so maybe changer waits for payment). Each case teaches a unique chiddush.

Orphans Collecting from Orphans

Lender's orphans want to collect father's debt from borrower's orphans
↓
Who died first?
Lender died first
Heirs take heirs' oath ('Father didn't tell us it was paid') and collect
Borrower died first
Lender already owed an oath to borrower's orphans → Cannot bequeath this oath → No collection
↓
R' Ami's refinement: Even if borrower died first — did lender stand trial before dying?
Stood trial
Court already imposed oath obligation → Cannot bequeath → No collection
Did not stand trial
Heirs take heirs' oath and collect (per R' Elazar)
R' Ami Synthesizes Rav & Shmuel with R' Elazar: the critical factor is whether the court had already imposed the oath obligation on the lender.

Can an Oath Be Bequeathed?

Rav & Shmuel
אין אדם מוריש שבועה לבניו — One cannot bequeath an oath. If the father owed an oath to collect, his children cannot collect at all.
R' Elazar
That is not the correct ruling. The heirs take the heirs' oath — 'Our father never told us it was paid' — and collect.
Rav Naḥman
Is it the court that renders the lender liable? The oath obligation exists from the moment the borrower dies!
Rav Yosef
The baraita supporting R' Elazar follows Beit Shammai, who consider a debt as already collected — so it's not truly inheriting an oath.
Rav Ḥama's Ruling The halakha was not definitively decided: a judge who rules like Rav & Shmuel — valid; a judge who rules like R' Elazar — also valid.
