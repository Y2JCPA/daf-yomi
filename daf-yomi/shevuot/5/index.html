Why Does R' Yehuda HaNasi Switch Methods?

Gemara
R' Yehuda HaNasi normally expounds via klal u'frat (generalizations and details). Why use ribbui u'mi'ut (amplifications and restrictions) for firstborn redemption?
Ameimar
He follows the school of R' Yishmael: when the verse about fish states 'in the water, in the water' before 'in the seas and streams' — two general terms before the detail — we use ribbui u'mi'ut.
Ravina
The Rabbis disagree: as they say in Eretz Yisrael — when two generalizations are juxtaposed followed by a detail, place the detail between them and expound as klal u'frat u'klal.
Gemara
Conclusion: Since the oaths mishna uses ribbui u'mi'ut (not R' Yehuda HaNasi's usual method), he must have formulated it per R' Akiva's opinion, not his own.
PRINCIPLE The same verse can yield different halakhic results depending on the exegetical method applied — klal u'frat vs. ribbui u'mi'ut.

'And It Was Concealed' — Awareness Before & After

Leviticus 5:2–3 states 'v'ne'elam' (and it was concealed) twice
↓
R' Akiva
First 'concealed': unawareness during transgression
Second 'concealed': prior awareness existed
R' Yehuda HaNasi
'It was concealed' (past tense) alone implies prior awareness; 'and he was aware' = awareness after
Second 'concealed' → liability for unawareness of Temple sanctity
BOTTOM LINE Both agree: liability for a sliding-scale offering requires yedi'ah (awareness) at the beginning and end, with a lapse in between. They differ on how the verse teaches this.

Rava vs. Abaye: Does 'Concealed' Imply Prior Awareness?

Rava
'V'ne'elam' (and it was concealed) — not 'v'ne'elam mimenu' (and it is concealed from him). The past-tense formulation implies he was previously aware.
Abaye
Problem #1 — Sota: The verse says 'and it was concealed from her husband's eyes' (Num 5:13). By your logic, the husband was aware of the adultery. But if he knew — the bitter waters cannot examine her, because he sinned by not divorcing her!
Abaye
Problem #2 — Torah: Job 28:21 says 'it was concealed from the eyes of all living.' By your logic, someone once knew the Torah's secrets. But it says 'no man knew'!
Rava
(The Gemara leaves Rava's position challenged.)
ABAYE'S METHOD Abaye disproves Rava's grammatical inference by finding parallel verses where 'v'ne'elam' clearly does NOT imply prior awareness.

Shabbat: 'Carrying Out' vs. 'Bringing In'

The Mishna's Language

  • 'One who carries out (ha-motzi) from one domain to another is liable'
  • Uses generalized 'domain to domain' — not 'private to public'
  • Then lists yetziyyot (carryings out) as 'two that are four' — but explicates bringing in cases too

Ravina's Reading

  • The mishna's language proves: hotza'ah (carrying out) includes hakhnasah (bringing in)
  • Any removal from an item's current place = 'carrying out'
  • The tanna uses one term for all transfers between domains

Rava's Reading

  • No inference can be made from the language
  • The mishna teaches domains (reshuyot), not acts of carrying out
  • Meaning: 'Regarding Shabbat domains, there are two types leading to four prohibited transfers'
DISPUTE Ravina and Rava disagree whether the Shabbat mishna's use of 'yetziyyot' proves that the term covers bringing items in as well.
