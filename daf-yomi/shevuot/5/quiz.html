QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does R' Yehuda HaNasi use ribbui u'mi'ut (amplifications and restrictions) for firstborn redemption, even though he normally uses klal u'frat?
A. Because the verse has two general terms before the specific detail, following R' Yishmael's school
B. Because R' Akiva required him to switch methods
C. Because firstborn redemption is a more stringent mitzvah
D. Because the Torah explicitly mandates this method for monetary laws
Correct: A — Ameimar explains that when two general terms precede the detail (as in the fish verse), even R' Yehuda HaNasi agrees to use ribbui u'mi'ut, following the school of R' Yishmael.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Abaye use to challenge Rava's inference that 'v'ne'elam' (and it was concealed) implies prior awareness?
A. A verse about Shabbat carrying and a verse about tithing
B. The sota verse and the Job verse about Torah being hidden
C. Two different verses about sacrificial offerings
D. A verse about leprosy and a verse about Nazirite vows
Correct: B — Abaye brings two counter-examples: (1) the sota verse says 'it was concealed from her husband' — but if he knew, the bitter waters can't work; (2) Job 28:21 says Torah 'was concealed from all living' — but no one ever fully knew it.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Ravina, why does the Shabbat mishna use the term 'yetziyyot' (carryings out) when it also discusses bringing items in?
A. It is a scribal error that was never corrected
B. The tanna considers any removal of an item from its place as 'carrying out'
C. Bringing items in was only added by later Amoraim
D. The mishna only discusses carrying out, not bringing in
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the tanna refers to any act involving removal of an item from its current place as 'carrying out' (hotza'ah), even when the item is being brought into a private domain.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does R' Ḥanina say the mishna about leprous marks cannot follow R' Akiva's opinion?
A. R' Akiva does not believe in leprous marks at all
B. Under R' Akiva's adjacent-only combination rule, a lime-colored mark could never combine with another shade
C. R' Akiva lists only two shades, not four
D. R' Akiva holds that all shades combine equally
Correct: B — R' Akiva orders shades by brightness and allows only adjacent shades to combine. This means lime (3rd shade) can't combine with baheret (1st — se'et intervenes) or with se'et (lime isn't its toladah), effectively rendering a sub-geris lime mark pure — contradicting the mishna.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Yehuda HaNasi, what does the SECOND mention of 'v'ne'elam' (and it was concealed) in Leviticus 5:2-3 teach?
A. That one needs awareness at the end of the transgression
B. That the offering must be brought within a specific time period
C. Liability for a lapse of awareness regarding the sanctity of the Temple, not just one's impurity
D. That two witnesses are required to establish the transgression
Correct: C — R' Yehuda HaNasi derives prior awareness from the first 'v'ne'elam' alone (past tense implies previous knowledge). The second mention teaches an additional category: liability for being unaware that the place one entered was the Temple (he'elem mikdash), not just unawareness of impurity (he'elem tum'ah).
