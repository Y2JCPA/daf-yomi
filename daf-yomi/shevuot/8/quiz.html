Shevuot 8 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, how does the Yom Kippur goat atone for intentional forbidden relations?
A. When the transgressor was forewarned by witnesses
B. When the transgressor was not forewarned by witnesses, so no death penalty applies
C. Only when combined with the scapegoat sent to Azazel
D. It never atones for intentional forbidden relations under any circumstances
Correct: B — The goat fills the gap: the transgressor acted intentionally but without forewarning (hasra'ah), so he is not liable for the death penalty, yet his sin is too severe for a regular offering.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rav Kahana reject idol worship as a candidate for the goat's atonement?
A. Idol worship has no offering at all
B. Idol worship's differentiated offering (she-goat) reflects stringency, not leniency
C. Idol worship is not a form of impurity
D. The idol worshipper is always subject to the death penalty
Correct: B — Rav Kahana's principle: the goat atones for transgressions where the Torah's differentiation indicates leniency. Idol worship's unique she-goat requirement reflects greater stringency, not leniency.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rav Hoshaya exclude a woman after childbirth (yoledet) from the goat's atonement?
A. She brings a burnt-offering, not a sin-offering
B. The verse says 'for their sins' — her offering is for impurity, not sin
C. She is exempt from all offerings during nursing
D. Her sliding-scale offering is too similar to the goat itself
Correct: B — The verse specifies 'for all their sins' (לכל חטאתם), meaning the goat atones for sins specifically. A yoledet's offering addresses her period of ritual impurity, not a sinful act.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the practical difference between Rava and R' Zeira regarding the goat's 'suspension' of punishment?
A. R' Zeira says the person is fully atoned; Rava says he still owes an offering
B. R' Zeira says it protects if he dies before bringing an offering; Rava says it protects him from suffering while alive
C. R' Zeira says it works only on Yom Kippur; Rava says it works year-round
D. R' Zeira limits it to the internal goat; Rava extends it to the external goat as well
Correct: B — Rava objects that death itself cleanses sin, making R' Zeira's answer unnecessary. Rava instead explains that suspension means the person is protected from suffering (yissurin) in the interim before becoming aware and bringing an offering.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why can't the internal goat atone for both its own cases AND those of the external goat?
A. The internal goat's blood is sprinkled in the wrong location for external cases
B. The verse repeats 'once in the year' — teaching each goat effects only one type of atonement
C. The Kohen Gadol cannot perform both services simultaneously
D. The external goat is a higher-level offering that supersedes the internal one
Correct: B — Exodus 30:10 emphasizes 'once' (achat) twice, teaching that each goat is limited to one category of atonement — it cannot double up and cover another goat's designated cases.
