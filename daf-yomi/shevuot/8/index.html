What Does the Yom Kippur Goat Atone For?

The Torah says the goat atones "from the impurities" — not all impurities
↓
Which impurity is differentiated from all others?
↓
R' Yehuda
Defiling the Temple/sacrificial foods — differentiated by the sliding-scale offering
R' Shimon
Derives the answer from the verse itself — no need for comparison
Core Issue The Gemara systematically eliminates alternative transgressions (idol worship, childbirth offering, leprosy, nazirite) to prove the goat atones specifically for defiling the Temple or its sacrificial foods.

Why Not Idol Worship?

Challenge: Idol worship also has a differentiated offering — a she-goat instead of a ewe
↓
Rav Kahana responds:
↓
Defiling Temple
Differentiated to be lenient (sliding-scale = easier offering)
Idol Worship
Differentiated to be stringent (she-goat = harsher treatment)
↓
The goat atones for transgressions where the Torah was lenient, not stringent
Rav Kahana's Principle The word "from" (מן) limits atonement to impurities the Torah treats leniently — where a lighter offering is prescribed.

Why Not a Woman After Childbirth?

Gemara
A yoledet also brings a sliding-scale offering — why doesn't the goat atone for her?
Rav Hoshaya
The verse says "for all their sins" — not "for all their impurities." A yoledet's offering is for impurity, not sin.
Gemara
But R' Shimon b. Yoḥai says she is a sinner — she swore during labor never to have relations again!
Gemara
R' Shimon follows his own reasoning — he derives the goat's scope from the verse itself, not by comparison to other offerings.
Same Logic for Leper Rav Hoshaya uses the identical argument — "for their sins, not their impurities" — to exclude a metzora, whose offering is also for impurity rather than sin.

Awareness at Beginning vs. End

Awareness at Beginning, Not End

  • Knew he was tamei, entered the Temple
  • By Yom Kippur — forgot his transgression
  • Internal goat (blood inside the Sanctuary) suspends punishment
  • When he later becomes aware → must bring sliding-scale offering

No Awareness at Beginning, Awareness at End

  • Did not know he was tamei when he entered
  • Later became aware of what happened
  • External goat (Yom Kippur mussaf) + Yom Kippur itself atone
  • Both goats are juxtaposed — each covers one awareness pattern
Why Juxtaposed? The Torah places the two goats together to teach that both atone for cases involving awareness — but each handles a different configuration of knowledge.

What Does 'Suspension' Actually Mean?

Gemara
If the goat only suspends punishment but doesn't fully atone — what's the practical benefit?
R' Zeira
If he dies before bringing his offering, he dies without liability for that sin.
Rava
Death itself cleanses all sin — R' Zeira's answer is unnecessary!
Rava
Rather, the goat protects him from suffering in the interim — before he becomes aware and brings his offering.
Rava's View Suspension means active protection: the person is shielded from divine punishment (yissurin) while still owing a future offering once awareness arrives.
