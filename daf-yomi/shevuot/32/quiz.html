Shevuot 32 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, if a plaintiff is pursuing witnesses but does not explicitly ask them to testify, and they deny knowledge under oath, what is the result?
A. They are liable for a false oath of testimony
B. They are exempt because there was no verbal demand
C. They are liable but bring a reduced offering
D. They are liable only if they deny in court
Correct: B — Shmuel teaches that even though the plaintiff's intent is clear from pursuing them, witnesses are liable only if the plaintiff explicitly verbalizes the demand to testify.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the oath on a deposit NOT require the owner to verbally demand return of the deposit?
A. Because the bailee is always considered suspect
B. Because Leviticus 5:21 makes the bailee liable for any false denial of the deposit
C. Because deposit cases always go to court
D. Because the Rabbis enacted a special leniency for deposit claims
Correct: B — The verse 'deals falsely with his colleague in a matter of deposit' (Lev. 5:21) establishes liability for any denial of the deposit, regardless of how the owner raises the claim.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Abaye, why is the false denial of testimony only punishable when it occurs in court?
A. Because oaths only have legal force in court
B. Because Leviticus 5:1 links liability to a place where testimony would result in monetary payment
C. Because the Sanhedrin must witness the denial
D. Because the plaintiff can only demand testimony in a formal court setting
Correct: B — Abaye derives from 'if he does not utter it' (Lev. 5:1) that liability applies only where uttering the testimony would lead to a monetary judgment — which is in court.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In Rabbi Abba's case of the snatched silver ingot, why must the snatcher pay?
A. Because two witnesses testified against him
B. Because he confessed to the theft
C. Because he is obligated to take an oath but cannot (disqualified as a robber), so he must pay
D. Because Rabbi Ami ruled that one witness is sufficient for monetary cases
Correct: C — The defendant admitted snatching, which disqualifies him from taking an oath (status of a robber). R' Abba's principle: one who must swear but cannot must pay.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When is a witness of a husband's death EXEMPT from liability if he falsely denies knowledge?
A. When the husband actually died in a foreign land
B. When there is already a second witness who can testify
C. When he previously told the wife herself, since she can testify on her own behalf
D. When the ketubah was already paid out
Correct: C — Per Eduyyot 1:12, a wife may testify that her husband died based on what she was told. Once the witness told her, his later denial causes no financial loss — she can go to court herself.
