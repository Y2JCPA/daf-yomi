How Do We Know Oath of Testimony Applies Only to Monetary Claims?

R' Eliezer
Multiple instances of 'or' (אוֹ) appear in both oath of testimony and oath on deposit — and there is no priest in their context. This links them.
Rabba bar Ulla
But the oath on an utterance also has multiple 'or' terms, no priest, and applies without a monetary claim!
R' Akiva
The verse says 'of these' (מֵאֵלֶּה) — a restrictive expression: some cases create liability, some do not.
R' Shimon
The Torah made one liable for false oaths in both contexts — just as deposit requires monetary claim, so does testimony.
Core Issue All agree that oath of testimony (shevuat ha-edut) applies only to monetary claims, but they derive this rule through different hermeneutical methods.

Three Approaches to Limiting Oath of Testimony

R' Eliezer

  • Uses multiple instances of 'or' (אוֹ)
  • Links oath of testimony to oath on deposit
  • Both contexts lack a priest
  • Derives monetary-claim requirement from deposit

R' Akiva

  • Uses 'of these' (מֵאֵלֶּה) as restrictive
  • Some oaths create liability, some do not
  • Monetary claims → liable
  • Non-monetary claims → exempt

R' Shimon

  • Uses verbal analogy: 'shall sin' (יֶחֱטָא)
  • Appears in both testimony and deposit contexts
  • Just as deposit = monetary, so testimony = monetary
  • Challenged by scholars in Eretz Yisrael
Common Ground All three Tannaim reach the same conclusion — oath of testimony liability requires a monetary claim (tvi'at mammon).

The Column Stories: When Does Denial Make One a Liar?

Scenario
Plaintiff: 'I counted and gave you 100 dinars alongside this column.'
Defendant: 'I never passed alongside this column.'
Rav Hamnuna
If he says 'it never happened' and witnesses saw money change hands — he is a proven denier. But if he says 'I took money that was mine' — witnesses prove nothing!
Rav Yehuda
'Are you Hamnuna? Enter the study hall — you make your teacher wiser!'
Reish Lakish
Witnesses saw him urinate by the column — he is a denier, since he claimed he never passed there.
Rav Naḥman
That's a Persian court ruling! He meant he never passed there in connection with this matter.
Key Distinction When a person denies being somewhere, context matters — did they deny the location itself, or the transaction alleged to have occurred there?

Rava's Principle: Forgotten Actions Don't Make You a Liar

Person denies ever passing alongside a column
↓
Witnesses testify he urinated there once
↓
Is he a proven denier of truth?
↓
Rav Naḥman (version 2)
Yes — he made an absolute denial that was disproven
Rava's Response
No — a matter not incumbent upon a person to remember, he does and forgets. This is not intentional denial.
Principle Rava: מילתא דלא רמיא עליה דאיניש — עביד לה ולאו אדעתיה — A matter a person has no reason to remember, he does without it registering in his mind.
