QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Eliezer, what textual feature links oath of testimony to oath on deposit?
A. Multiple instances of the term 'or' with no priest in context
B. The phrase 'and it is hidden' in both passages
C. The word 'soul' (nefesh) appearing in both
D. The mention of a sliding-scale offering in both
Correct: A — R' Eliezer derives the connection from multiple instances of 'or' (אוֹ) appearing in both contexts, combined with the presence of an oath and absence of a priest.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara ultimately derive oath of testimony from oath on deposit rather than oath on an utterance?
A. Because the Torah explicitly states to compare them
B. Because they share more common elements (sin, intentionally, claim, denial, past)
C. Because oath on an utterance does not exist biblically
D. Because oath on an utterance has no offering requirement
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that deposit shares more features with testimony (the mnemonic: sin, intentionally, claimed, denied, past) than utterance does (sin-offering, sliding-scale, no one-fifth).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the column story, what is Rava's reason for saying the defendant is NOT a proven liar?
A. The witnesses were unreliable
B. A matter one has no reason to remember, one does without it registering
C. The defendant only denied the financial transaction, not the location
D. Urinating somewhere does not prove one passed there
Correct: B — Rava's principle: מילתא דלא רמיא עליה — something not incumbent upon a person to remember, he does and forgets. The denial was not intentional deception.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What did the scholars in Eretz Yisrael ('the West') actually mock about R' Shimon's proof?
A. That he used a verbal analogy at all
B. That he distinguished intentional from unwitting using 'and it is hidden,' which is absent from deposit too
C. That he compared testimony to deposit instead of utterance
D. That he relied on the word 'or' instead of 'shall sin'
Correct: B — The mockery was that R' Shimon tried to distinguish deposit (no intentional = unwitting equation) from testimony, but 'and it is hidden' (which creates that distinction) is absent from BOTH contexts.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What was Rav Yehuda's reaction to Rav Hamnuna's analysis of the money-counting case?
A. He rebuked him for contradicting established law
B. He praised him, saying 'you make your teacher wiser'
C. He dismissed the argument as characteristic of a Persian court
D. He asked him to bring proof from a Mishnah
Correct: B — Rav Yehuda was so impressed by Rav Hamnuna's distinction (between denying the event entirely vs. claiming the money was rightfully his) that he praised him: 'Enter the study hall — you make your teacher wiser!'
