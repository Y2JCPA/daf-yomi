What Does the Sliding-Scale Offering Atone For?

Core Question: What transgressions require a korban oleh v'yored (sliding-scale offering)?
↓
The Torah mentions impurity — but which type?
↓
Defiling Sacrificial Foods
Derived via R' Yehuda HaNasi's gezera shava of behema
Defiling the Temple
Derived via juxtaposition in Lev 12:4 — 'sacred item' next to 'Temple'
Eating Teruma While Impure
Excluded by the term 'through which' (Lev 5:3)
KEY PRINCIPLE The sliding-scale offering atones for unwitting defilement of the Temple or its sacrificial foods — but not for eating teruma while impure.

What Does 'Through Which' Exclude?

Gemara
The verse states 'through which' (Lev 5:3) — this excludes an impure person who eats teruma from the sliding-scale offering.
Questioner
But maybe 'through which' excludes one who defiles the Temple instead? Since it's so severe, perhaps a sliding-scale offering isn't sufficient!
Gemara
No — defiling the Temple carries karet for intentional violation, just like defiling sacrificial foods. They should have the same offering.
Questioner
On the contrary — eating teruma while impure involves eating, just like eating sacrificial food while impure. That similarity should keep it in!
RESOLUTION Rava resolves the ambiguity using the three mentions of karet for impure consumption of peace-offerings.

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's Gezera Shava

Lev 5:2 mentions both ḥayya (wild animal) and behema (domesticated animal) — but behema IS a type of ḥayya!
↓
Why mention behema separately? It must teach a gezera shava (verbal analogy)!
↓
Here (Lev 5:2)
'An impure behema' — in the context of a sliding-scale offering
There (Lev 7:21)
'An impure behema' — in the context of defiling sacrificial food
↓
Conclusion: The sliding-scale offering atones for defiling sacrificial foods
RAVA'S PRAISE Rava applied Proverbs 20:5 — 'One who draws water from deep wells' — to Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi for this brilliant derivation.

Three Mentions of Karet for Impure Eating of Offerings

VersePurpose
Leviticus 22:3Applies karet to the general case — any impure person who eats any offering
Leviticus 7:20Applies karet to the specific case of eating a peace-offering while impure
Leviticus 7:21Extra — not needed for sacrificial foods (already derived). Apply it to defiling the Temple
IF NOT NEEDED Rava uses the hermeneutical principle: if a verse is not needed for its own topic, apply it to teach another topic — here, that entering the Temple while impure also carries karet.

Impurity That Defiles by Touching vs. by Eating

Standard Impurity

  • Imparts impurity through touching
  • Included in sliding-scale offering liability
  • Examples: animal carcasses, human corpse impurity

Bird Carcass Impurity

  • Imparts impurity only through eating (not touching)
  • One might think it's excluded from liability
  • Included via restriction after restriction amplifies
HERMENEUTICAL RULE 'Or if he will touch an impurity of a man' — restricts to touch-impurity. 'Through which' — a second restriction. Two restrictions together = amplification, including even eating-only impurity.

The Internal Goat of Yom Kippur — Deriving Its Atonement

📖 Lev 16:16 — the goat atones for 'impurities of the children of Israel'
↓
🤔 Which impurities? Idol worship, sexual immorality, and bloodshed are all called 'impurity'
↓
⛔ The word 'from' (mi-) restricts: only some impurities, not all
↓
🔍 Which impurity is unique? Defiling the Temple/sacrificial foods — the only one with a sliding-scale offering
↓
✅ The internal goat atones for defilement of the Temple and its sacrificial foods
R' YEHUDA'S METHOD R' Yehuda derives the goat's scope by comparing it to the unique category of impurity that already has its own offering — defilement of sacred items.
