Shevuot 25 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What stringency do oaths have over vows?
A. Oaths apply to mitzva matters
B. Oaths apply to intangible matters
C. Oaths require witnesses
D. Oaths cannot be annulled
Correct: B — The Gemara states that oaths take effect on intangible matters (e.g., 'I will sleep') just as on tangible matters, whereas vows apply only to tangible matters.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Akiva, why must oaths about the past also generate liability?
A. The verse explicitly mentions past oaths
B. If the verse is amplified to include matters without 'evil or good,' it must also be amplified to include the past
C. A kal va-chomer from vows
D. A tradition from Sinai (halakha l'Moshe mi-Sinai)
Correct: B — R' Akiva argues that if R' Yishmael amplifies the verse to include oaths where 'evil' and 'good' don't apply, the same amplification must extend to oaths about the past.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does R' Yochanan say one who swears not to sleep for three days is flogged immediately?
A. Sleep is a mitzva and the oath doesn't take effect
B. It is physically impossible, making it a shevuat shav (oath in vain)
C. Three days is too long to specify in an oath
D. The oath contradicts a fact known to three people
Correct: B — Since it is impossible to stay awake for three days, the oath is void from the start — it is classified as a shevuat shav, and the taker is flogged immediately.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the core disagreement between Rav and Shmuel regarding an oath about another person's past action?
A. Whether such an oath is a shevuat shav
B. Whether the oath must be invertible to a future tense to generate korban liability
C. Whether oaths about others take effect at all
D. Whether the oath-taker receives malkot or karet
Correct: B — Shmuel requires that an oath be 'invertible' to a future form under one's control to trigger korban liability. Since one cannot swear 'so-and-so will throw a stone,' there is no liability. Rav disagrees.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve Rav Hamnuna's objection to Shmuel from the mishna about 'I did not don phylacteries'?
A. It says the mishna follows R' Yehuda ben Beteira only
B. It says the mishna is taught disjunctively — 'I didn't eat' = korban, 'I didn't don phylacteries' = malkot
C. It says donning phylacteries is not truly a mitzva
D. It says the oath can in fact be inverted to the future
Correct: B — The Gemara answers that the mishna addresses two separate cases: 'I did not eat' triggers a korban (invertible), while 'I did not don phylacteries' triggers only malkot (lashes), which does not require invertibility.
