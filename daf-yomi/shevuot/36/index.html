Ala (אלה) = Oath: The Derivation

Gemara
How do we know ala without the word 'oath' is still an oath? The verse says 'the voice of an ala' (Lev 5:1) — 'voice' is unnecessary, teaching that ala alone carries oath-force.
R' Abbahu
I derive it from Ezekiel 17:13: Nebuchadnezzar 'brought [Zedekiah] into an ala.' And II Chronicles 36:13 calls the same event an oath — proving ala = oath.
PRINCIPLE An ala is legally equivalent to a shevu'a (oath), even when the word 'oath' is absent from the formula.

Three Dimensions of Arur (ארור)

Ostracism (נידוי)

  • Source: 'Curse Meroz' (Judges 5:23)
  • Ulla: Barak ostracized Meroz with 400 shofarot
  • Arur → social exclusion & excommunication

Curse (קללה)

  • Source: Mt. Gerizim/Ebal ceremony (Deut 27:13-15)
  • 'Arur be the man who fashions a graven image'
  • Arur → invoking divine punishment

Oath (שבועה)

  • Source: Saul's oath (I Samuel 14:24)
  • 'Arur is the man who eats bread until evening'
  • Key proof: no conjunctional prefix (ve'arur) → arur is part of the oath itself
TEXTUAL KEY The absence of the prefix 'ו' (ve'arur) proves arur is intrinsic to the oath, not a separate curse.

Three Dimensions of Amen (אמן)

DimensionSourceContext
Oath (שבועה)Numbers 5:21-22The sotah woman says 'Amen, Amen' — accepting the oath the priest administers
Acceptance (קבלה)Deuteronomy 27:26All the people say 'Amen' — agreeing to uphold the Torah's commandments
Confirmation (אמת)Jeremiah 28:6Jeremiah says 'Amen, may Hashem do so' — affirming and praying the statement will be fulfilled
R' YOSEI B'R ḤANINA Amen is not a single concept but carries three distinct legal forces depending on context.

Can 'Yes' and 'No' Be Oaths?

R' Elazar: 'No' (לאו) can be an oath, and 'Yes' (הן) can be an oath
↓
Proof for 'No': 'The waters shall no more become a flood' (Gen 9:15) — Isaiah 54:9 calls this an oath
↓
Proof for 'Yes': Logical reasoning — if 'no' is an oath, 'yes' must also be
↓
Rava qualifies: Only when said twice — 'no, no' or 'yes, yes'
↓
Proof
God's promise uses two negatives: Gen 9:11 AND Gen 9:15
Rule
A single 'yes' or 'no' is not an oath
RAVA'S RULE Repetition transforms a simple statement into a binding oath — 'no, no' or 'yes, yes' carries oath-force.

Shevuat HaPikadon: Oath on a Deposit

📋 Claimant says: 'Give me my deposit in your possession'
↓
🤚 Defendant responds: 'On my oath, nothing of yours is in my possession'
↓
⚖️ This false oath triggers liability for a guilt-offering (asham)
↓
💰 Defendant must return the deposit + pay an additional 1/5 of its value
↓
🔄 Applies to: men & women, relatives & non-relatives, those fit & unfit as witnesses
↓
⚠️ Liable for intentional false oath even if unaware of the offering requirement
MISHNA Unlike other oaths, shevuat hapikadon has the broadest application — it binds virtually everyone and applies both in and out of court.
