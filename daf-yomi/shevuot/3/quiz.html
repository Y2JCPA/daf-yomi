QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Tractate Shevuot list ALL four 'two that are four' categories, unlike Shabbat or Nega'im?
A. Because oaths and Temple impurity are written together in the Torah and share the sliding-scale offering
B. Because Shevuot is the longest tractate and has room for extra material
C. Because the other tractates were compiled later
D. Because the Sages wanted to begin with the most comprehensive list
Correct: A — The Torah writes oaths and Temple defilement together (Leviticus 5:2-4), and both involve a korban oleh v'yored. Once two were taught, all four were included.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the key difference between R' Yochanan and Reish Lakish regarding one who swore 'I will eat this loaf today' and didn't?
A. R' Yochanan says he brings a korban; Reish Lakish says he is flogged
B. R' Yochanan says no lashes because inaction never incurs lashes; Reish Lakish says no lashes because forewarning was uncertain
C. R' Yochanan exempts him entirely; Reish Lakish says he must bring a sliding-scale offering
D. R' Yochanan says he violated a positive commandment; Reish Lakish says he violated a negative one
Correct: B — Both agree he is not flogged, but disagree on the reason: R' Yochanan holds inaction-based prohibitions never incur lashes; Reish Lakish holds the problem is the impossibility of definitive forewarning.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rava resolve the attribution of the mishna to R' Yishmael despite its inclusion of past oaths?
A. He says R' Yishmael actually agrees with R' Akiva on past oaths
B. He says the mishna is a later addition not from R' Yishmael
C. He says the Torah equates false oaths with vain oaths, extending lashes to past oaths even without a korban
D. He says past oaths are listed but carry no liability at all
Correct: C — Rava teaches that the Torah amplifies shevuat sheker to be like shevuat shav — so while R' Yishmael exempts past oaths from a korban, they still incur lashes.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What are the 'additional two' types in the category of awareness of Temple defilement?
A. Entering the Temple unknowingly and eating sacrificial food unknowingly
B. Being aware of impurity but unaware the food was sacrificial, or that the place was the Temple
C. Defiling oneself intentionally in the Temple and eating impure sacrificial food
D. Touching a corpse before entering the Temple and touching a sheretz before eating kodshim
Correct: B — The primary two involve unawareness of one's impurity. The additional two involve awareness of impurity but unawareness of what one encountered — the sacred status of the food or the identity of the Temple.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the Paschal offering mishna as the basis for R' Yochanan's ruling about lav she'ein bo ma'aseh?
A. Because the Paschal offering is a special case that doesn't apply generally
B. Because that mishna might follow R' Yehuda, who explains the exemption as a lav ha'nitak la'aseh (prohibition remedied by a positive mitzva) rather than inaction
C. Because R' Yochanan explicitly rejected that mishna in another context
D. Because the Paschal offering involves an action — burning the leftover meat
Correct: B — The mishna about leaving over Paschal meat could follow R' Yehuda, who says the exemption is because a positive mitzva (burning) follows the prohibition — not because of the inaction principle. So it cannot definitively prove R' Yochanan's view.
