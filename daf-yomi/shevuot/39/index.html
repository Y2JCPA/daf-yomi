Shevuot 39: The Devastating Power of False Oaths

לא ינקה ה׳ — The Lord will not hold guiltless (Exodus 20:7)
CORE THEME The Gemara expounds on the unique severity of false oaths (shevuat sheker): unlike all other transgressions, punishment extends to the swearer's family, the entire world, and strikes immediately — consuming even what fire cannot destroy. The daf then addresses the oath administration procedure and a dispute between Rav and Shmuel on the minimum claim value.
3 / 9

False Oaths vs. All Other Transgressions

All Other Transgressions

  • Torah states venakkeh — 'will clear the guilty'
  • Punishment falls only on the transgressor
  • If one has merit, punishment is suspended 2-3 generations
  • Fire and water can consume the sinner's property

False Oaths (Shevuat Sheker)

  • Torah states lo yenakkeh — 'will NOT hold guiltless'
  • Punishment falls on him, his family, and the whole world
  • Punishment is exacted immediately
  • Consumes even timber and stones — what fire cannot destroy
SOURCE The entire world trembled when God said at Sinai: 'You shall not take the name of the Lord your God in vain' (Exodus 20:7)

Expanding Circles of Punishment for False Oaths

The Transgressor Himself
Punished with the full punishment of the oath
↓
His Family
'Do not allow your mouth to bring your flesh into guilt' (Ecclesiastes 5:5)
'Flesh' = relatives (Isaiah 58:7)
↓
The Entire World
'Because of swearing the land mourns' (Jeremiah 23:10)
Even those who could not protest are punished
KEY DISTINCTION For other sins, punishment reaches the world only if people could protest but didn't. For false oaths, even those who could not protest are affected.

The Oath Administration Procedure in Court

⚠️ Judges warn the defendant about the severity of false oaths — punishment on family and world
↓
❌ If defendant says 'I will not swear' — court immediately rules him liable to pay
↓
😱 If defendant says 'I will swear' — bystanders quote: 'Depart from the tents of these wicked men!'
↓
📋 Judges stipulate: 'Not according to YOUR understanding, but according to the Omnipresent and the court'
↓
🤚 The oath is administered — binding in any language, not only Hebrew
WHY STIPULATE? Because of deceptions like the reed in Rava's court: a man hid coins in a hollow reed, gave it to the claimant to hold, swore he'd 'given him the money,' then took the reed back.

The Covenant at Moab: Who Was Included?

Baraita
Moses administered an oath: 'Not according to your understanding do I administer this oath, but according to the Omnipresent's and mine'
Baraita
'He who stands here with us' — those at Sinai were bound. 'He who is not here' — future generations and converts were also bound
Baraita
Even future mitzvot like reading the Megillah were included, as Esther 9:27 says: 'They ordained what they had already accepted'
R' Shimon b. Tarfon
'The oath of the Lord shall be between them both' — the punishment applies to both the swearer AND the one who administers the oath
IMPLICATION This is why bystanders call the claimant 'wicked' too — by forcing the oath, he shares responsibility for its consequences.
