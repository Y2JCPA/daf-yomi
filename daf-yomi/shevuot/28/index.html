Dissolving Oaths & Conditional Liability

Rava
Once one has eaten the entire loaf, the oath no longer exists — it cannot be dissolved.
Ameimar
Even if he ate the entire loaf, he may still request dissolution — the consequences remain (offering or lashes).
Rava
For conditional oaths: if the condition was fulfilled unwittingly, the oath never takes effect — he is exempt.
Rav Mari
A mishna in Nedarim supports Rava: unwitting vows are automatically dissolved, and so are unwitting oaths.
Core Issue When does an oath cease to exist — and can its consequences still be undone?

Can an Oath Be Dissolved After Completion?

Rava
If one swore 'I will not eat this loaf' and ate it entirely, the oath can no longer be dissolved.
Rav Ashi
If he left an olive-bulk (k'zayit), that is sufficient to request dissolution. Less than that — it is not.
Ameimar
Even if he ate the entire loaf, dissolution is still possible — he still owes an offering (unwitting) or lashes (intentional).
Gemara
Even one tied to the stake for lashes can still have the oath dissolved — the punishment has not yet been carried out.
Key Distinction Rava holds the oath ceases to exist once the act is done; Ameimar holds it persists through its consequences.

Naziriteship Objection to Rava

One takes two vows of naziriteship
↓
Completes first term and separates offering
↓
Requests dissolution of the first vow
↓
Second term counts retroactively as the first — dissolution works!
↓
Rav Ashi: Naziriteship ≠ Oaths — dissolving the first vow simply removes the barrier to the second. The oath, once eaten, is gone entirely.
Resolution The Gemara distinguishes: naziriteship terms are interchangeable, but an eaten loaf leaves nothing to dissolve.

Uncertain Forewarning (Hasra'at Safek)

R' Yochanan

  • Uncertain forewarning IS valid
  • If warned before eating the forbidden loaf (before condition fulfilled)
  • He is LIABLE for lashes
  • Warning need not be certain at the time

Reish Lakish

  • Uncertain forewarning is NOT valid
  • At time of warning, it was unknown if loaf would become forbidden
  • He is EXEMPT from lashes
  • Warning must relate to a definite prohibition
Scenario He ate the forbidden loaf first (intentionally), then ate the condition loaf — the oath took effect retroactively.

Interdependent Oaths on Two Loaves

📜 Takes oath: 'I won't eat A if I eat B' AND 'I won't eat B if I eat A'
↓
🍞 Eats Loaf A — intentionally regarding itself, unwittingly regarding B
↓
🍞 Eats Loaf B — intentionally regarding itself, unwittingly regarding A
↓
✅ EXEMPT — both conditions fulfilled only unintentionally
↓
🔄 Reverse: if aware of consequences for the other loaf each time → LIABLE for offerings
↓
⚡ Both intentionally → Lashes for second loaf; first depends on hasra'at safek dispute
Key With interdependent oaths, you must track awareness separately for each loaf's own prohibition AND its role as a condition.
