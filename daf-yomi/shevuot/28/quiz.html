Shevuot 28 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Ameimar, why can an oath be dissolved even after the entire loaf is eaten?
A. Because the oath automatically renews each day
B. Because consequences remain — he still owes an offering or lashes
C. Because the Torah allows retroactive dissolution of any oath
D. Because eating a loaf is not considered a completed action
Correct: B — Ameimar holds that as long as the consequences of the oath (bringing an offering for unwitting violation, or receiving lashes for intentional violation) have not yet been fulfilled, the oath can still be dissolved.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
If one swears 'I will not eat that loaf if I eat this one,' and eats the condition loaf unwittingly and the forbidden loaf intentionally, what is the ruling?
A. Liable for lashes
B. Liable to bring an offering
C. Exempt — the condition was not fulfilled intentionally
D. Liable for both lashes and an offering
Correct: C — Rava rules that if the condition is fulfilled unwittingly (without awareness of the oath), the oath never takes effect. Therefore, he is exempt even though he ate the forbidden loaf intentionally.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rav Ashi reject the proof from naziriteship against Rava's position?
A. Naziriteship vows cannot be dissolved at all
B. Naziriteship terms are interchangeable, but an eaten loaf leaves nothing to dissolve
C. The baraita about naziriteship is not authoritative
D. Oaths are more severe than naziriteship vows
Correct: B — Rav Ashi distinguishes: dissolving the first naziriteship simply removes a barrier, and the second term retroactively fills its place. But with an oath about eating, once the loaf is consumed, the oath has no remaining substance to dissolve.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If one swears 'I will not eat 10 pieces' and then 'I will not eat 9 pieces,' how many oaths is he liable for?
A. One oath only — the second is subsumed by the first
B. Two oaths — the second adds a prohibition on eating 9
C. Neither — overlapping oaths cancel each other
D. Two oaths — but only if he eats exactly 10
Correct: B — Avimi corrects Eifa: the first oath only prohibited eating 10 pieces. Eating 9 was still permitted. The second oath therefore adds a new prohibition (not to eat 9), making him liable for two oaths.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the dispute between R' Yochanan and Reish Lakish regarding conditional oaths?
A. Whether conditional oaths are valid at all
B. Whether an uncertain forewarning (hasra'at safek) is sufficient for lashes
C. Whether unwitting fulfillment of a condition activates the oath
D. Whether one can take an oath on two loaves simultaneously
Correct: B — When one eats the forbidden loaf before fulfilling the condition, the forewarning was uncertain (it wasn't yet known if the oath would take effect). R' Yochanan holds this is a valid forewarning and he receives lashes; Reish Lakish holds it is insufficient.
