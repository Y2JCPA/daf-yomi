Shevuot 16 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Rav Huna and Rav Naḥman?
A. Whether the mishna requires ALL consecration procedures or just one
B. Whether the Temple can be rebuilt without prophetic authority
C. Whether second tithe may be eaten on the Mount of Olives
D. Whether bowing inward or outward constitutes proper worship
Correct: A — Rav Huna reads the mishna as requiring ALL procedures for consecration; Rav Naḥman reads it as requiring only ONE of the listed procedures.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, why didn't Ezra's reconsecration need all procedures?
A. Because a prophet authorized a simpler ceremony
B. Because the original sanctity never lapsed — it was merely a commemoration
C. Because the Sanhedrin waived the requirements
D. Because the Urim VeTummim confirmed the sanctity still held
Correct: B — Rav Huna holds that the initial consecration sanctified the Temple forever. Ezra's ceremony was only a commemoration (זכר), not a true reconsecration.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why was the upper pond on the Mount of Olives enclosed within Jerusalem's walls despite not being properly consecrated?
A. It contained a sacred spring used for purification
B. It was a defensive weak point (turpa) that enemies could exploit
C. The returning exiles mistakenly believed it was already holy
D. Abba Shaul ruled it had residual sanctity from the First Temple
Correct: B — The upper pond was a strategic vulnerability — a weak point in Jerusalem's defenses — so it had to be enclosed within the walls even though it could not be properly consecrated.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Torah use both 'Tabernacle' (Mishkan) and 'Temple' (Mikdash) in Numbers 19?
A. To distinguish between the desert period and the settled period
B. To teach that impurity laws apply in both, AND that liability extends to impurity contracted inside the courtyard
C. To indicate that the Tabernacle had greater sanctity than the Temple
D. To show that only Kohanim are liable for entering impurely
Correct: B — R' Elazar derives two conclusions from the variation in terms: the law applies to both structures, and one is liable even when impurity is contracted inside the courtyard (not only when entering while already impure).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rava, when is an impure person who bows in the Temple courtyard immediately liable — without any tarrying?
A. When he bows facing outward, away from the Holy of Holies
B. When he bows facing inward, toward the Holy of Holies
C. When he prostrates fully with arms and legs spread
D. When he bows while reciting the verse from II Chronicles
Correct: B — Rava distinguishes: bowing facing inward (toward the Holy of Holies) is 'proper bowing' and creates immediate liability. Bowing outward requires tarrying (the time of a full prostration) for liability.
