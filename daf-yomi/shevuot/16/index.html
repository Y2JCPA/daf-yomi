Must ALL Consecration Procedures Be Performed?

Rav Huna
We learned: 'With all these' — every procedure is indispensable. If even one is missing, the consecration does not take effect.
Rav Naḥman
We learned: 'With one of all these' — any single procedure suffices for consecration to take effect.
Rav Huna
The initial consecration sanctified the Temple for its time and forever. Ezra's reconsecration was merely a commemoration. But new additions require all procedures.
Rav Naḥman
The initial consecration sanctified for its time only. Ezra reconsecrated without a king or Urim VeTummim — proving not all procedures are needed.
Rava
But the mishna explicitly states: 'Any addition not made with all these procedures'!
Rav Naḥman
Emend the mishna to read: 'with one of all these' procedures.
Core Issue This debate hinges on whether the original Temple consecration had permanent or temporary effect — which determines what the Second Temple era required.

Did the First Temple's Sanctity Endure Forever?

Rav Huna
The initial consecration sanctified forever (kidsha l'sha'ata v'kidsha l'atid lavo). Ezra's ceremony was just a commemoration.
Rav Naḥman
The initial consecration was for its time only (kidsha l'sha'ata v'lo kidsha l'atid lavo). Ezra had to truly reconsecrate — and he did so without all procedures.
Abba Shaul
The upper pond on the Mount of Olives was enclosed but never properly consecrated — proof that all procedures are needed!
Practical Difference If sanctity endures forever, then areas already consecrated by Solomon need no new ceremony — but genuinely new areas need everything.

Tabernacle vs. Temple — Why Both Terms Are Needed

If Only 'Tabernacle' (Mishkan)

  • Liable for impurity in the Tabernacle
  • Might think: Only because it was anointed with holy oil
  • Would exclude the Temple (not anointed)

If Only 'Temple' (Mikdash)

  • Liable for impurity in the Temple
  • Might think: Only because its sanctity is eternal
  • Would exclude the Tabernacle (temporary sanctity)
R' Elazar's Derivation The Torah uses BOTH terms to teach two things: (1) liability applies in both Tabernacle and Temple, and (2) one is liable even for impurity contracted inside the courtyard.

📋 Shevuot 16 — Summary Map

Must additions to the Temple be consecrated with ALL procedures?
↓
Rav Huna: Yes — all are indispensable. Original sanctity endures forever, but new areas need full ceremony.
Rav Naḥman: No — any one suffices. Original sanctity lapsed; Ezra proved partial ceremony works.
↓
Separate issue: An impure person in the courtyard must leave by the shortest route. Bowing inward = immediate liability; bowing outward = liable only if he tarried.
