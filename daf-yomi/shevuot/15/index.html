Consecration: From Vessels to Walls to Courtyard

"And so shall you do" (Exodus 25:9)
Implies: replicate Tabernacle procedures in future generations
↓
Vessels
Future vessels consecrated by service alone, not anointment
("them" = only Moses' vessels)
City & Courtyard
Jerusalem & Temple courtyard require specific consecration rituals
Key Principle The phrase "and so shall you do" extends Tabernacle procedures to future generations — but specific verses limit or modify how each element is consecrated.
3 / 9

How Are Future Vessels Consecrated?

Rava
Objection: A baraita teaches that Moses' vessels were consecrated by anointment, but future vessels are consecrated by service alone. But shouldn't "and so shall you do" require anointment in all generations?
Gemara
The verse says "and he anointed them" (Numbers 7:1) — them specifically, excluding future vessels from anointment.
Rava
But maybe "them" means Moses' vessels needed only anointment, while future vessels need both anointment and service?
Rav Pappa
"With which they will serve" (Numbers 4:12) — the future tense makes future vessels dependent on service. And "them" excludes future vessels from anointment entirely.
Resolution Without "them," we'd require both anointment AND service for future vessels. "Them" teaches: only Moses' vessels — anointment. Future vessels — service alone.

Thanks-Offerings for Consecrating Jerusalem

What "Two Thanks-Offerings" Means

  • Refers to the loaves, not the animal flesh
  • Based on Nehemiah 12:31 — Ezra's consecration of Jerusalem's wall
  • "Large" = the leavened loaves, which are 3× the size of unleavened

Makeup of Thanks-Offering Loaves

  • Total: 5 Jerusalem se'ah = 20 tenths of an ephah
  • 10 tenths → leavened loaves (larger portion)
  • 10 tenths → unleavened: matzah loaves, wafers, and soaked flour
  • Leavened loaves are 3× the size of each unleavened type
Rav Ḥisda "Large" cannot mean large animals — the Mishnah in Menachot teaches that a modest and a substantial offering have equal merit before Heaven, provided one directs his heart to his Father in Heaven.

What Consecrates the Temple Courtyard?

Rami bar Ḥama: The courtyard is consecrated with the remainder of the meal-offering (שיירי מנחה), not thanks-offering loaves
↓
Why? Consecration must use an item eaten specifically in that area
↓
Jerusalem
Thanks-offering loaves → eaten anywhere in the city
∴ They consecrate the city
Temple Courtyard
Meal-offering remainder → eaten only in the courtyard
∴ It consecrates the courtyard
Principle The consecrating item must match the sanctity of the space — what is eaten specifically in that zone has the power to consecrate it.

Why Can't the Two Shavuot Loaves Consecrate the Courtyard?

ScenarioWhy It Fails
Build & consecrate on Erev ShavuotThe two loaves aren't yet consecrated — they become holy only when the lambs are slaughtered on the Festival
Build on Erev, consecrate on ShavuotConsecration must occur at the time of building completion
Build & consecrate on ShavuotBuilding the Temple does not override the Festival
Save loaves until after ShavuotThe loaves are disqualified by being left overnight (לינה)
Leave a bit unbuilt, finish right after Festival ends at nightTemple building cannot take place at night — "On the day that the Tabernacle was erected" (Numbers 9:15)
Abaye From "on the day the Tabernacle was erected" we derive: the Temple may be erected during the day, but not at night. This closes every possible workaround for using the Shavuot loaves.
