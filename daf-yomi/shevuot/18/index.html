Two Sin-Offerings for Intercourse with a Niddah

Man engages in intercourse near her expected veset
↓
She experiences menstrual bleeding during intercourse
Entry
He is a Torah scholar who knew it was prohibited near veset — but erred thinking he could finish in time → liable for unwitting entry
Withdrawal
He is not a scholar regarding withdrawal halakha — doesn't know immediate withdrawal is prohibited → liable for unwitting withdrawal
Key Principle Rava: Two separate sin-offerings arise when there is awareness between the two acts — knowing one halakha but not the other creates two distinct lapses of awareness.
3 / 9

How Can One Be Liable for TWO Sin-Offerings?

Gemara
The mishna teaches he is liable for both entry and withdrawal. But how? If he's an am ha'aretz (ignoramus) who knows neither halakha…
Gemara
Then it's one lapse of awareness — like eating two olive-bulks of forbidden fat without awareness. Only one sin-offering!
Gemara
And if a Torah scholar not near her veset? Entry is ones (beyond his control) — no liability. Withdrawal is intentional — karet, not a sin-offering!
Rava
It must be near her expected veset, and a partial Torah scholar — he knows the halakha of entry but not the halakha of withdrawal.
Resolution Because he has awareness between the two acts (he knows entry was wrong when she tells him), each act constitutes a separate unwitting transgression requiring its own sin-offering.

Rava's Analysis: Who Is Liable for Two Offerings?

Man has intercourse near her veset (expected onset)
↓
Is he a Torah scholar regarding the prohibition of intercourse near veset?
Yes — Knows Entry Halakha
He erred thinking he could finish in time → unwitting transgressor → liable for sin-offering #1 (entry)
No — Am Ha'aretz
Entry is part of same lapse of awareness as withdrawal → only one sin-offering total
↓
Does he know the halakha that immediate withdrawal is prohibited?
No — Doesn't Know
Withdraws immediately thinking it's required → unwitting transgressor → liable for sin-offering #2 (withdrawal)
Yes — Knows
If he withdraws anyway → intentional → karet, not a sin-offering
Rava's Case A scholar regarding entry law + ignorant of withdrawal law = two separate sin-offerings.

Mishnaic Sources for Entry and Withdrawal Liability

LiabilityMishnaic SourceCase
WithdrawalMishna (Shevuot)She says 'I have become impure' — he unwittingly withdraws immediately → liable for sin-offering
EntryMishna (Niddah 14a)Blood found on his rag after intercourse → both are impure and liable for a sin-offering
UncertaintyMishna (Niddah 14a)Blood found on her rag → impure only from doubt → exempt from sin-offering
Dispute Rav Adda bar Mattana challenged: Perhaps the Niddah mishna also teaches about withdrawal, not entry? Ravina refuted: 'found on his rag' means he learned after withdrawal — so it must teach about entry.

Biblical Sources for the Prohibition of Withdrawal

Positive Mitzva (Chizkiyya)

  • Leviticus 15:24 — 'Her menstrual flow shall be upon him'
  • Even while she is menstruating, the prohibition remains 'upon him'
  • He must not withdraw immediately — he must wait with a flaccid member
  • This establishes a positive commandment regarding the manner of withdrawal

Negative Prohibition (Rav Pappa)

  • Leviticus 18:19 — 'Lo tikrav' (you shall not approach)
  • 'Tikrav' also means 'withdraw' — as in Isaiah 65:5: 'kerav elekha' = remove yourself
  • The word carries both meanings: approach AND withdraw
  • This establishes a negative prohibition (lo ta'aseh) on immediate withdrawal
Both Sources Needed The Gemara requires two verses: one for a positive mitzva (how to withdraw properly) and one for the negative prohibition (that immediate withdrawal is forbidden).

Obligation to Separate Near the Veset (Expected Onset)

📜 R' Yoshiya derives from Leviticus 15:31: 'Separate the children of Israel from their uncleanness'
↓
⏰ Rabba: Must separate for an onah — half a 24-hour day (daytime or nighttime)
↓
⚠️ R' Yochanan citing R' Shimon ben Yochai: If one does NOT separate, even worthy sons like Aaron's will die
↓
👶 R' Chiyya bar Abba citing R' Yochanan: One who separates will have male children (Lev. 11:47 → 12:2)
↓
📚 R' Yehoshua ben Levi: He will have sons worthy of teaching halakha (Lev. 10:10-11)
Connection The Gemara links the juxtaposition of verses about distinguishing pure from impure with verses about childbirth and Torah teaching to derive these rewards.
