↓
Wage Dispute

Wage Dispute: Who Swears?

Sages to Shmuel
A craftsman says: You fixed two coins as my payment. The employer says: I fixed only one. Who takes the oath?
Shmuel
The employer takes an oath and the craftsman loses the difference. People certainly remember what wages they fixed (b'kiẓutz damim inshi dakri).
Rabba bar Shmuel
But a baraita says: the burden of proof rests upon the claimant (the craftsman)! No oath for the employer at all?
Rav Naḥman
Shmuel reads the baraita disjunctively: Either the craftsman brings proof, or the employer takes an oath and the craftsman loses.
PRINCIPLE Unlike a standard hired-worker oath (where the worker swears), in wage-amount disputes the employer swears — because people remember the price they set.

Craftsman Holding the Cloak

Craftsman repaired a cloak. Dispute: owner says 1 dinar, craftsman says 2 dinars.
↓
Cloak still with craftsman
Owner must bring proof that fee was 1 dinar
Cloak returned — same day
Craftsman takes an oath and receives 2 dinars
Cloak returned — time passed
Burden of proof on craftsman (claimant)
TIMING MATTERS Possession and timing shift the burden: the craftsman's leverage decreases once the item leaves his hands and time passes.

R' Yehuda vs. Rabbis: Worker's Oath

R' Yehuda

  • Worker swears ONLY with partial admission by employer
  • Applies to both: employer admits partial payment AND wage-amount disputes
  • More restrictive on payment claims, but grants oath for wage disputes

Rabbis (Chakhamim)

  • Worker swears even without partial admission (per Mishnah)
  • Per Shmuel: in wage-amount disputes, employer swears, not worker
  • More lenient on payment claims, stricter on wage disputes
RAV SHEISHA'S CHALLENGE Where R' Yehuda is strict the Rabbis are lenient, and vice versa — so would the Rabbis really be more strict than R' Yehuda on wage disputes?

Items Taken from a Homeowner: When Is 'I Purchased It' Credible?

ConditionCredible?
Homeowner not apt to sell + items not concealed + person not secretive❌ Not credible
Homeowner apt to sell items✅ Credible
Items are typically concealed✅ Credible
Person is generally secretive (carries things hidden)✅ Credible
Homeowner claims items were stolen✅ Credible for taker — we don't presume theft
Items typically lent or rented❌ Not credible — must prove purchase
RAVA'S CASE Rava expropriated fabric scissors and a book of aggada from orphans — these are items typically lent or rented, so 'I purchased them' is not believed.

Oath of One Who Was Injured

👤 Witnesses testify person entered defendant's domain whole
↓
🤕 Person emerged injured (bite on back or elbows — cannot be self-inflicted)
↓
🔍 No other person was present besides the defendant
↓
⚖️ Shmuel: If injured in a place where he could injure himself — must take oath to collect
↓
✅ If injured where he cannot injure himself — collects without an oath
R' ḤIYYA The Mishnah refers to injuries like bites on the back or elbows — locations that prove another person caused the harm.
