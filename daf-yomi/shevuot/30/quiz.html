Shevuot 30 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, which group is NOT exempt from shevuat ha'edut?
A. Women
B. Relatives of the litigants
C. Non-relative men who are fit witnesses
D. Those disqualified due to transgressions
Correct: C — The oath of testimony applies to men who are non-relatives and fit to testify. Women, relatives, and those disqualified by transgression are all exempt.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the core dispute between R' Meir and the Rabbis regarding shevuat ha'edut?
A. Whether women can take the oath
B. Whether the oath requires intent
C. Whether a self-imposed oath creates liability outside of court
D. Whether the offering is sliding-scale
Correct: C — R' Meir holds that one who takes the oath on his own is liable even outside of court; the Rabbis hold liability only arises when the denial occurs in court, regardless of who initiates the oath.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Rav Nachman have a duck cast upon him when Rav Huna's wife came to court?
A. To test whether she would be startled
B. To create a reason to stand that wouldn't appear as judicial bias
C. To signal the beginning of the trial
D. To demonstrate a legal principle about lost objects
Correct: B — Rav Nachman needed to rise in deference to a chaver's wife but feared the other litigant would see it as favoritism. The duck gave him an external reason to stand up.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Ulla, the dispute about standing in court applies to whom?
A. Only to witnesses
B. Only to judges
C. Only to litigants
D. To both witnesses and litigants equally
Correct: C — Ulla teaches that witnesses must always stand (from 'the two men shall stand'), and the dispute between R' Yehuda and the Rabbis concerns only whether litigants must stand.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When may a Torah scholar decline to testify before a less prominent judge?
A. Never — testimony is always obligatory
B. Only in monetary cases, not ritual matters
C. Only in ritual matters, not monetary cases
D. Whenever the scholar feels it is beneath his dignity
Correct: B — A scholar's dignity exempts him from testifying in monetary cases before a lesser court, but in ritual matters the principle 'there is no wisdom against the Lord' (Proverbs 21:30) overrides personal honor.
