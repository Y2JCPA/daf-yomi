Extending Oaths: The Hired Worker Exception

Rav Huna
Any oath — even rabbinic — may be extended to include additional oaths, except the oath of a hired worker.
Rav Ḥisda
For everyone the Sages are not lenient and extend oaths — except for a hired worker, for whom they are lenient.
Gemara
What's the difference between them?
Gemara
Whether the court itself initiates extending the oath, even when the other litigant hasn't suggested it.
Why? The hired worker's oath was instituted only to alleviate the employer's concerns — it is not meant to be a general oath mechanism.

Shemittah Abrogates Oaths

וזה דבר השמטה שמוט כל בעל משה ידו
Rav Giddel in the name of Rav Deuteronomy 15:2 — "V'zeh devar ha-shemittah" — 'And this is the matter of the release.'

Since דבר (devar) also means statement, the verse teaches that Shemittah releases not only debts but even a statement — i.e., an oath connected to a debt.

Paid Bailee / Renter: False Oath Scenarios

Exempt from Guilt-Offering

  • Claims 'died' but truth was 'injured' or 'captured' — all exempt claims
  • Claims 'stolen' but truth was 'lost' — both are liability claims
  • Claims 'lost/stolen' but truth was 'died/injured/captured' — liability → exemption = stricter on self

Liable for Guilt-Offering

  • Claims 'died/injured/captured' but truth was 'stolen or lost'
  • Swore to an exemption when truth requires liability
  • This is swearing to be lenient on himself — the oath benefits him
The Principle Exemption → Exemption or Liability → Liability = exempt. Liability → Exemption (lenient on self) = liable.
