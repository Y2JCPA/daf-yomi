QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why can't the oath of a hired worker be extended to include additional claims?
A. Because hired workers are not believed under oath
B. Because the oath was instituted only to alleviate the employer's concerns
C. Because hired workers are exempt from all oaths by Torah law
D. Because extending would violate the prohibition of unnecessary oaths
Correct: B — The hired worker's oath is a special rabbinic enactment designed solely to ease the employer's mind, not a general oath mechanism that can be expanded.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara derive that Shemittah abrogates oaths?
A. From a kal va-chomer: if debts are released, certainly oaths are
B. From the word 'devar' (דבר) in Deuteronomy 15:2, which also means 'statement'
C. From a gezeirah shavah linking Shemittah to Yom Kippur
D. From the phrase 'he shall not exact it' which includes verbal obligations
Correct: B — Rav Giddel in the name of Rav derives it from 'devar ha-shemittah' — since devar means both 'matter' and 'statement,' Shemittah releases even statements (oaths).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
A paid bailee claims the animal 'died' but it was actually stolen. Is he liable for a guilt-offering?
A. Yes — he lied under oath
B. No — both death and theft result in the same liability
C. Yes — he switched from an exemption claim to a liability claim
D. No — he switched from an exemption claim (death) to a liability claim (theft), making himself stricter
Correct: D — For a paid bailee, death is an exemption (he swears and is exempt) and theft requires payment (liability). By claiming death when the truth was theft, he made his situation STRICTER — so he is exempt from a guilt-offering.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the practical difference between Rav Huna and Rav Ḥisda regarding extending oaths?
A. Whether the oath extension applies to biblical or rabbinic oaths
B. Whether the court can initiate extending the oath even without the litigant's suggestion
C. Whether the hired worker must swear at all
D. Whether extension applies to monetary claims or only deposit claims
Correct: B — Both agree a hired worker's oath cannot be extended. They differ on whether the court itself can initiate extending an oath for him, even when the opposing litigant hasn't requested it.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Shmuel hold that false bailee oaths are exempt from a sin-offering for an oath on an utterance (shevuat bitui)?
A. Because bailee oaths are only rabbinic in nature
B. Because the oath cannot be formulated in both positive and negative forms
C. Because there is no possibility of taking the oath about a future event
D. Because the oath was administered by the owner, not taken voluntarily
Correct: C — Shmuel holds that shevuat bitui requires the oath to be applicable to the future. Since the animal already died/was stolen, one cannot swear about a future occurrence of the same event, so it fails this criterion.
