Process of Elimination: What Do Festival Goats Atone For?

Festival goats are juxtaposed with New Moon goats via 'and' (וְ) — both involve sacrificial food cases
↓
Can they atone for what New Moon goats atone for?
No
"It bears the sin" — it and not another
↓
Can they atone for what Yom Kippur goat atones for?
No
"Once a year" — only Yom Kippur
↓
∴ Festival goats atone for no awareness at beginning or end
METHOD The Gemara systematically eliminates every other possibility before arriving at the answer.

R' Meir vs. R' Shimon ben Yehuda: Uniform or Layered?

R' Meir
All goats — New Moon, Festivals, and Yom Kippur — effect the same atonement: defilement of the Temple or its sacrificial foods.
R' Ḥama bar R' Ḥanina
R' Meir's reasoning: the word 'and a goat' (וּשְׂעִיר) juxtaposes all Festival goats with the New Moon goat, making them all equivalent.
R' Shimon ben Yehuda
The goats form a hierarchy: each level atones for everything below it plus an additional category.
R' Shimon ben Yehuda
New Moon goats: pure person eating impure sacrificial food.
Festival goats: that + no awareness at all.
Yom Kippur goats: those + no awareness at start, awareness at end.
CORE DISPUTE R' Meir sees all goats as functionally identical in their atonement scope. R' Shimon sees an ascending ladder of atonement power.

Scriptural Derivations: Why Each Goat Is Limited

New Moon Goat

  • "He gave it to you to bear the sin" (Lev. 10:17)
  • It bears — but another does not bear that sin
  • Exclusive jurisdiction: pure person eating impure קדשים

External Yom Kippur Goat

  • "Once a year" (Ex. 30:10)
  • This specific atonement only once yearly
  • No other goat can encroach on its domain

Internal Yom Kippur Goat

  • "Upon its corners once a year"
  • Blood on inner altar corners
  • Suspends punishment for awareness-at-start cases
NOTE R' Shimon reads 'upon its corners' as limiting only the internal goat to one atonement — the external goat can atone for multiple sins.

Deriving Festival Goat Atonement: The 'And' Connection

R' Ḥama b. R' Ḥanina
The Torah could have written just 'a goat' (שְׂעִיר) for Festivals, but it writes 'and a goat' (וּשְׂעִיר) — juxtaposing all Festival goats with New Moon.
Gemara
Problem: If each goat derives from the one adjacent, that's a juxtaposition from a juxtaposition! R' Yoḥanan says this is forbidden for consecrated matters (הקדשות).
Gemara
Resolution: All goats derive directly from the New Moon goat — not via chain.
Gemara
But Shavuot and Yom Kippur say just 'a goat,' not 'and a goat'!
R' Yona
The verse 'These shall you offer to the Lord on your Festivals' (Num. 29:39) juxtaposes all Festival goats together.
Abaye
And New Moon is also called a 'Festival' (מוֹעֵד) — as proven from Lamentations 1:15 regarding the month of Tammuz.
PRINCIPLE R' Yoḥanan's rule: In the entire Torah one may derive a juxtaposition from a juxtaposition, except in matters of הקדשות (consecrated items).
