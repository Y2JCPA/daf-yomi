Both Litigants Are Suspect — What Happens?

R' Meir
Since neither party can take an oath, they divide the disputed amount equally.
R' Yosei
The oath returned to its place (חזרה שבועה למקומה).
Rava
What exactly is R' Yosei's opinion? What is R' Meir's?
Rav Naḥman
I do not know... but R' Yosei actually says: they divide.
Rav Yosef bar Minyumi
Rav Naḥman ruled in an actual case that the litigants divide the amount.
SURPRISE Despite R' Yosei's cryptic language about the oath 'returning,' multiple traditions report he actually agrees they divide the amount.

Where Does the Oath 'Return'?

Babylonian Sages — Rav & Shmuel

  • Oath returns to Sinai (חזרה שבועה לסיני)
  • God administered the oath at Sinai — including the prohibition of robbery
  • The robber will be punished by Heaven, not the court
  • Proof: Lender's heirs cannot swear → they simply lose the claim

Eretz Yisrael Sages — R' Abba

  • Oath returns to the defendant
  • Since he is disqualified from swearing, he must pay
  • Proof: The snatched metal case — R' Abba rules the snatcher pays
  • Principle: Unable to swear = liable to pay
HALAKHA R' Abba's principle — one liable to swear who cannot swear must pay — becomes a foundational rule in later halakha.

Shimon ben Tarfon's Teachings

קרב לגבי דהינא ואידהן
AGGADIC INTERLUDE Three interpretations:

1️⃣ "The oath of the Lord shall be between them both" (Exodus 22:10) — a false oath brings responsibility upon both litigants

2️⃣ "לא תנאף" — read as לא תַּנְאִיף: Do not cause adultery (assisting an adulterer)

3️⃣ The Euphrates is called 'great' not because of size, but because it is close to Eretz Yisrael — "Draw close to the anointed and become anointed."
