Shevuot 47 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, what happens when ONE litigant is a dice player or interest lender?
A. The suspect litigant swears and collects
B. The opposing party swears and collects
C. They divide the amount equally
D. The case is dismissed
Correct: B — When one litigant is suspect regarding oaths, the opposing (non-suspect) party takes an oath and receives payment.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does R' Abba hold regarding one who is liable to take an oath but cannot?
A. The oath returns to Sinai and Heaven decides
B. He is exempt from payment entirely
C. He must pay since he cannot swear
D. They divide the disputed amount
Correct: C — R' Abba's principle: מחויב שבועה שאינו יכול לישבע — משלם. One obligated in an oath who cannot take it must pay.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the snatched metal case, why couldn't the court simply require the snatcher to take an oath?
A. He had already taken a false oath before
B. He admitted to snatching, making him like a robber who cannot swear
C. There were two witnesses against him
D. The metal was too valuable for an oath
Correct: B — Since the snatcher admitted he grabbed the item (even claiming it was his), he is considered like a robber, and a robber is not permitted to take an oath.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the storekeeper-laborers dispute, according to Rava, why do the laborers swear in the storekeeper's presence?
A. So the storekeeper can cross-examine them
B. So they will feel ashamed to lie before someone who knows the truth
C. Because the storekeeper must confirm their oath
D. To give the storekeeper a chance to confess
Correct: B — Rava explains the laborers swear to the employer in the storekeeper's presence so they will be ashamed to lie, since the storekeeper knows whether or not he actually paid them.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When two contradictory sets of witnesses each sign a different promissory note for the SAME lender and borrower, what is the ruling?
A. Both notes are valid
B. Both notes are invalid
C. The holder collects only the lower amount
D. The question is left unresolved (teyku)
Correct: C — With one lender, one borrower, and two notes signed by contradictory witness sets, the holder is at a disadvantage and can collect only the lower of the two sums.
