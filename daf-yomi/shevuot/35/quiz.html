QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why are witnesses exempt when the plaintiff claims someone promised to give him 200 dinars?
A. Because 200 dinars is too small an amount
B. Because a promise to give a gift is not a valid monetary claim like a deposit
C. Because the plaintiff did not specify the witnesses by name
D. Because the oath was not administered in court
Correct: B — The Gemara derives through a verbal analogy (וְחָטְאָה) that oath-of-testimony liability applies only to deposit-type monetary claims where the plaintiff has a legitimate legal claim, not to unfulfilled promises.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Shmuel, if a plaintiff makes a general demand in the synagogue and his actual witnesses are present, what is the ruling?
A. The witnesses are liable since they were present
B. The witnesses are liable only if they were standing near the plaintiff
C. The witnesses are exempt because the demand was not directed at them specifically
D. The witnesses are liable if they make eye contact with the plaintiff
Correct: C — Shmuel teaches that even if the witnesses are present in the synagogue — even standing right next to the plaintiff — they are exempt unless the plaintiff specifies them by name. This is derived from 'and he is a witness' (Lev. 5:1), implying a specific, directed demand.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why is an oath 'in the name of the Gracious One' considered sacred, but an oath 'in the name of heaven and earth' is not?
A. Because 'Gracious' appears in the Torah but 'heaven' does not
B. Because no other entity is called 'Gracious' — it uniquely refers to God — while heaven and earth exist as independent entities
C. Because heaven and earth are mentioned in the same verse as idols
D. Because 'Gracious' is one of the seven names that may not be erased
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes: titles like 'Gracious' (חנון) and 'Compassionate' (רחום) uniquely describe God, so they must refer to Him. But heaven and earth exist independently, so the oath might refer to the physical entities, not to God.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the halakha regarding suffixes attached to God's names (e.g., the נו in אלהינו)?
A. They may always be erased, per unanimous agreement
B. They may never be erased, per unanimous agreement
C. Tanna Kamma says erasable; Aḥerim say not — and Rav Huna rules like Aḥerim that they may NOT be erased
D. They may be erased only if the scribe made an error
Correct: C — While the Tanna Kamma permits erasing suffixes, Aḥerim hold that the Name sanctifies the attached suffix. Rav Huna rules the halakha follows Aḥerim — suffixes may not be erased.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What famous teaching does the Gemara derive from the view that Abraham's 'My lords' (Gen. 18:3) is sacred — i.e., addressed to God?
A. That one must always stand when addressing God
B. That angels have the same status as God
C. That hospitality to guests is greater than receiving the Divine Presence
D. That one may not interrupt prayer even for guests
Correct: C — R' Yehuda citing Rav teaches that hospitality to guests (הַכְנָסַת אוֹרְחִים) is greater than receiving the Shekhinah — because if Abraham was speaking to God in Gen. 18:3, he interrupted that encounter to welcome his guests.
