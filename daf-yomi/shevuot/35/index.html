The Synagogue Case: General Demand vs. Specific Witnesses

Mishna
If one stood in the synagogue and said: "I administer an oath to you that if you know testimony relevant to me, come testify" — the witnesses are exempt.
Shmuel
This is the halakha even if his witnesses were among the people in the synagogue.
Gemara
Isn't this obvious? Otherwise what novelty does the Mishna teach?
Gemara
No — it is necessary for the case where he is standing right next to those witnesses. You might think proximity = direct address.
Conclusion
Until the plaintiff specifies to whom he directs his demand, he does not render those witnesses obligated. Derived from: "and he is a witness" (Lev. 5:1) — singular, specific.
PRINCIPLE A blanket demand, even in close proximity, cannot create the obligation of שְׁבוּעַת הָעֵדוּת — it must be directed at specific individuals.

Erasability of God's Names

May NOT Be Erased

  • א-ל from אֱלֹהִים (Elohim)
  • י-ה from the Tetragrammaton
  • Full Tetragrammaton
  • Suffixes of God's names (per Aḥerim/Rav Huna)

May Be Erased

  • ש-ד from שַׁדַּי (Shaddai)
  • א-ד from אֲדֹנָי (Adonai)
  • צ-ב from צְבָאוֹת (Tzevaot)
  • Prefixes: ל, ב, ו, מ, ש, ה, כ before the Name
DISPUTE R' Yosei holds צְבָאוֹת may be erased entirely — it refers to Israel's hosts, not God's name. But Shmuel rules: the halakha is NOT like R' Yosei.

Gracious & Compassionate vs. Heaven & Earth

Rava
If an oath "in the name of the Gracious One" is sacred because it refers to God, then an oath "in the name of heaven and earth" should also be sacred — referring to He for Whom heaven and earth are His!
Abaye
The cases are different.
Gemara
Since no other entity is called "Gracious" or "Compassionate" — it clearly refers to God.
Gemara
But heaven and earth exist as independent entities. Perhaps the oath refers to the actual heaven and earth — so the witnesses are exempt.
DISTINCTION Titles uniquely associated with God (חַנּוּן, רַחוּם) are treated as divine names. Titles shared with created things (שָׁמַיִם, אֶרֶץ) are not.
