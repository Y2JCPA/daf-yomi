Atonement Structure for Priests & Awareness of Impurity

Core Question: How do priests achieve atonement for defiling the Temple?
↓
Not Through Internal Goat
No exception made for priests regarding the goat — it is 'of the people'
Through Bull of Aaron
All priests = 'his household' — 'House of Aaron, bless the Lord'
↓
Second Topic: Awareness of impurity in the Temple — 'two that are four'
↓
Tannaitic dispute: Is awareness of impurity alone required, or also awareness of Temple/sacrificial food?
Key Principle The priests' atonement comes through the bull because they are 'House of Aaron,' but not through the people's goat because they contributed no funds toward it.

Which Offering Atones for the Priests?

Baraita
Priests do not achieve atonement through the goat of the people — it is written 'of the people,' excluding priests
Baraita
They achieve atonement through the bull of Aaron — 'his household' was expanded to include all priests
Gemara
But 'his household' suggests only immediate family?
Baraita
Refuted by the verse: 'House of Aaron, bless the Lord' — this refers to all priests
Gemara
The real logic: priests didn't pay for the people's goat, so it can't atone for them. But they are called Aaron's 'household,' so the bull does.
בית אהרן All priests are collectively referred to as the 'House of Aaron' — this is the textual basis for their inclusion in the bull's atonement.

Bull of Aaron vs. Goat of the People

Bull of Aaron (פר אהרן)

  • Purchased from High Priest's own funds
  • Atones for High Priest + all priests
  • 'His household' = all of 'House of Aaron'
  • Two confessions recited over it
  • Blood presented inside the Sanctuary

Internal Goat (שעיר פנימי)

  • Purchased from public funds
  • Atones only for Israelites, not priests
  • 'Of the people' — excludes priests
  • Priests contributed no money toward it
  • Blood presented inside the Sanctuary
Funding Principle A key factor: atonement follows financial contribution. Since priests didn't pay for the people's goat, it doesn't atone for them.

Why Two Confessions Over the Bull?

R' Shimon: Priests get NO atonement from the scapegoat
↓
Bull must cover atonement normally provided by three Yom Kippur offerings
1st Confession
Corresponds to internal goat's atonement
2nd Confession
Corresponds to scapegoat's atonement
Blood Inside
Corresponds to external goat's atonement
↓
R' Yehuda: Priests DO get atonement from scapegoat — so why two confessions?
↓
School of R' Yishmael: First confession = High Priest's own sins; Second = priests' sins. The innocent should atone for the guilty.
מדת הדין The attribute of justice demands: let the innocent come and atone for the guilty — the High Priest must first be absolved before atoning for others.

Consecrating Additions to the Temple

👑 Decision made by the king, a prophet, the Urim v'Tumim, and Sanhedrin of 71
↓
🎵 Two thanks-offerings (תודות) are prepared
↓
🚶 Procession walks to the boundary of the new area
↓
🍞 Inner thanks-offering is eaten at the new boundary
↓
🔥 Outer thanks-offering is burned
↓
✅ Area now has full Temple sanctity — impure entry is punishable
↓
❌ Without this ceremony — no liability for impure entry
Practical Impact Any addition to the Temple not consecrated with the full ceremony does not carry liability for entry while impure — neither offering for unwitting entry nor karet for intentional entry.
