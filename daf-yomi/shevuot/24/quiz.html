Shevuot 24 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yochanan, why does an oath not to eat take effect on non-kosher food?
A. Because the oath is about less than a full measure
B. Because the oath is a more inclusive prohibition (issur kolel) that also covers permitted food
C. Because non-kosher food is not considered real food
D. Because oaths always override Torah prohibitions
Correct: B — R' Yochanan holds the oath is an issur kolel — it includes permitted items alongside forbidden ones, so the broader prohibition takes effect even on already-forbidden food.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does R' Shimon exempt someone who eats non-kosher meat on Yom Kippur from a Yom Kippur offering?
A. Because Yom Kippur does not prohibit eating
B. Because he holds a more inclusive prohibition does not take effect on an existing prohibition
C. Because non-kosher meat is not real food
D. Because he holds eating requires an olive-bulk and the person ate less
Correct: B — R' Shimon consistently holds that an issur kolel does not take effect where another prohibition is already in place, so the Yom Kippur prohibition cannot add to the existing non-kosher prohibition.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rava's distinction between issur kolel and issur mosif?
A. Issur kolel is Torah-level and issur mosif is rabbinic
B. Issur kolel adds items for one person; issur mosif adds people for one item
C. Issur kolel applies to oaths and issur mosif applies to vows
D. There is no distinction — they are the same concept
Correct: B — Rava explains that issur kolel expands the forbidden items for the same person, while issur mosif expands the number of people forbidden from the same item. Even R' Shimon agrees issur mosif takes effect.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In Rava's case of swearing not to eat figs, then swearing not to eat figs and grapes, what is the chiddush?
A. The first oath is automatically annulled
B. Since the second oath takes effect on grapes, it also takes effect on the already-forbidden figs
C. One cannot take two oaths on the same item
D. Grapes and figs are considered the same species
Correct: B — Rava teaches that issur kolel works even for person-generated prohibitions: since the second oath validly adds grapes, it also takes effect on the figs despite the first oath already covering them.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why doesn't the Karetot mishna (listing 4 sin-offerings from one eating) include an oath as a 5th prohibition?
A. Because oaths can never take effect on forbidden items
B. Because the tanna only lists prohibitions that occur on their own, bring fixed sin-offerings, and cannot be dissolved
C. Because oaths do not require offerings
D. Because the case already includes five prohibitions
Correct: B — The Gemara gives multiple answers: the tanna lists only automatic (not person-generated) prohibitions, only those requiring fixed (not sliding-scale) offerings, and only those that cannot be dissolved by a Sage.
