Shevuot 4 — Resolving Contradictions & Methods of Exegesis

CORE THEME The Gemara resolves a contradiction between two unattributed mishnayot regarding lashes for prohibitions without action, introduces the principle that a mishna does not move from its place, and transitions into a major discussion of Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's exegetical method — ribbui u'mi'ut (amplifications and restrictions) vs. klal u'frat (generalizations and details).
3 / 9

Two Contradictory Unattributed Mishnayot

Gemara
The mishna on 27b implies one is not flogged for violating an oath without action ("I will eat" → didn't eat)
Gemara
But the mishna on 2a implies one is flogged for a lav she'ein bo ma'aseh (prohibition without action)
R' Yochanan
I follow the later unattributed mishna (27b) — no lashes without action
Resolution
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi initially held one is flogged, then retracted. He left the first mishna because a mishna does not move from its place
PRINCIPLE Ein mishna zaza mi'mekomah — Once students learned a mishna, it was not removed even if the editor changed his view

Rabbi Yishmael vs. Rabbi Akiva: Capital Prohibitions

R' Yishmael

  • A lav nitten la'azharas misa (prohibition backing a capital offense)…
  • One IS flogged when no actual death penalty is imposed
  • This allows Shabbat carrying to have lashes in non-capital cases
  • The mishna on 2a works according to his view

R' Akiva

  • A lav nitten la'azharas misa…
  • One is NOT flogged even when no death penalty applies
  • Holds one is liable for oaths about the past (via ribbui u'mi'ut)
  • Not liable for entering Temple unaware it was the Temple
KEY DISTINCTION The mishna is interpreted according to R' Yishmael, and the liability discussed is for lashes — not offerings

Rebbi's Exegetical Method: Ribbui U'Mi'ut

Gemara
Why does R' Yehuda HaNasi agree with R' Akiva on oaths about the past?
Answer
R' Akiva uses ribbui u'mi'ut (amplifications & restrictions) to include past oaths. Rebbi uses the same method
Proof
Regarding pidyon haben — Rebbi says redeem with anything except promissory notes, using ribbui u'mi'ut on Numbers 18:16
Ravina to Ameimar
But doesn't Rebbi use klal u'frat? He limits the awl for a Hebrew slave's ear to metal instruments!
CHALLENGE Ravina questions whether Rebbi consistently uses ribbui u'mi'ut, citing a baraita where Rebbi appears to use klal u'frat regarding the Hebrew slave's ear-piercing
