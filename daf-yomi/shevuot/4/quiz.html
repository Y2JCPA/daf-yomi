QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does R' Yochanan follow the mishna on 27b rather than the one on 2a?
A. Because the mishna on 27b is attributed to a named Tanna
B. Because Rebbi retracted, and the later unattributed mishna reflects his final view
C. Because the mishna on 2a was a scribal error
D. Because R' Akiva explicitly endorsed the mishna on 27b
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that Rebbi initially held one is flogged for a prohibition without action, then retracted. The later mishna reflects his final position, and 'a mishna does not move from its place' explains why the earlier one remains.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the source for the prohibition against cutting off one's baheret (leprous mark)?
A. An explicit verse in Leviticus stating 'Do not remove your mark'
B. A rabbinic enactment by the Men of the Great Assembly
C. The verse 'Observe the leprous mark' (Deut. 24:8) — 'Observe' implies a prohibition
D. It is derived from a kal va'chomer from Shabbat prohibitions
Correct: C — R' Avin says in the name of R' Ile'a: Wherever it states 'Hishamer' (Observe), 'Pen' (Lest), or 'Al' (Do not), it constitutes a prohibition. 'Observe the leprous mark' thus prohibits removing it.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does R' Yishmael differ from R' Akiva regarding a 'lav nitten la'azharas misa'?
A. R' Yishmael says one is never flogged; R' Akiva says one is always flogged
B. R' Yishmael says one is flogged when no death penalty is actually imposed; R' Akiva says one is not flogged
C. Both agree one is flogged, but disagree about the number of lashes
D. R' Yishmael applies this only to Shabbat; R' Akiva applies it to all prohibitions
Correct: B — R' Yishmael holds that even for a prohibition backing a capital offense, one is flogged when the death penalty is not actually imposed. R' Akiva disagrees and holds one is never flogged for such prohibitions.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the pidyon haben dispute, what does R' Yehuda HaNasi exclude using ribbui u'mi'ut?
A. Land, slaves, and promissory notes
B. Only promissory notes
C. Only land and slaves
D. Coins from foreign nations
Correct: B — Using ribbui u'mi'ut (amplify-restrict-amplify), Rebbi includes everything except the item most fundamentally dissimilar to silver shekels — promissory notes. The Rabbanan, using klal u'frat, exclude more: land, slaves, and notes.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What challenge does Ravina raise against the claim that Rebbi uses ribbui u'mi'ut?
A. A baraita shows Rebbi ruling on the Hebrew slave's ear-piercing in a way consistent with klal u'frat
B. Rebbi never discusses pidyon haben at all
C. Ravina proves ribbui u'mi'ut was invented after Rebbi's time
D. A different baraita shows Rebbi explicitly rejecting ribbui u'mi'ut
Correct: A — Ravina cites a baraita about piercing a Hebrew slave's ear where Rebbi limits valid tools (not just any item taken by hand), which appears to use klal u'frat rather than ribbui u'mi'ut — contradicting the claim that Rebbi consistently uses the latter method.
