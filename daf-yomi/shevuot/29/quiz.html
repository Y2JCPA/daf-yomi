Shevuot 29 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Ulla, what qualifies as 'known to people' for purposes of an oath denying the obvious?
A. Known to at least 2 witnesses
B. Known to at least 3 people
C. Known to the entire city
D. Known to the court
Correct: B — Ulla says a fact is considered 'known to people' when it is known to at least three people.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rava explain the Mishna's phrasing 'If I did not see a camel flying'?
A. It should be emended to 'I saw a camel flying'
B. It is part of a conditional: 'All produce is forbidden to me if I did not see...'
C. It refers to a large bird the person calls a camel
D. It is a rhetorical statement, not a real oath
Correct: B — Rava explains it as part of a conditional self-prohibition: all produce is forbidden if he didn't see the impossible event — making the prohibition permanent and the oath vain.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Moses say 'according to God's understanding and mine' when administering the oath at Sinai (Amud B conclusion)?
A. Because the people might privately redefine words
B. Because 'elohim' could refer to idolatry
C. To prevent the oath from ever being nullified (da'at rabbim)
D. Because Moses did not trust the people
Correct: C — The Gemara concludes that Moses' formulation was to make it an oath with public consent (da'at rabbim), which cannot be dissolved — not because of concern about private meanings.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the key difference between shevuat bitui and shevuat shav when violated unwittingly?
A. Bitui requires lashes; shav requires an offering
B. Bitui requires a sliding-scale offering; shav is exempt
C. Both require a sliding-scale offering
D. Both are exempt
Correct: B — The Mishna teaches that unwitting violation of shevuat bitui requires a sliding-scale offering, while unwitting shevuat shav is completely exempt.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Shmuel, what is the legal status of someone who answers 'Amen' to an oath administered by another?
A. He has merely agreed but is not bound by the oath
B. He is liable only if in the presence of a court
C. He is considered as if he expressed the oath with his own mouth
D. He is liable only for an oath of testimony
Correct: C — Shmuel derives from the sotah passage (Numbers 5:22) that answering Amen is legally equivalent to pronouncing the oath oneself.
