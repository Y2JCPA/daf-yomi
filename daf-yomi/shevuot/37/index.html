Rav Kahana's Triple Dilemma: Forewarned False Oath

Rav Kahana
If one intentionally swore falsely on a deposit and witnesses forewarned him — what is the halakha?
Rav Kahana
Option A: Since oath on deposit is a chiddush — always brings a guilt-offering, even with forewarning
Rav Kahana
Option B: With forewarning → lashes (standard punishment), no offering
Rav Kahana
Option C: We impose both lashes and a guilt-offering
Students
A baraita teaches: one receives lashes for intentional false oath on deposit — implying forewarning, and no offering!
Rava bar Itai
That baraita follows R' Shimon. According to the Rabbis, he brings both lashes and a guilt-offering!
The Novelty Normally in the Torah, intentional transgression does NOT require an offering — the oath on deposit is the exception.

Oath on Deposit vs. Oath of Testimony

Oath on Deposit (שבועת הפיקדון)

  • Intentional → lashes (per R' Shimon) or guilt-offering (per Rabbis)
  • Unwitting → guilt-offering worth at least 2 silver shekels
  • Applies to monetary claims between individuals
  • Unique: offering required even for intentional transgression

Oath of Testimony (שבועת העדות)

  • Intentional → sin-offering (sliding scale)
  • Unwitting → sin-offering (sliding scale)
  • Can be as inexpensive as 1/6 of a dinar
  • Same consequence whether intentional or unwitting
Key Stringency Rav Kahana's version: The stringency of the oath on deposit is the minimum cost — 2 silver shekels vs. potentially 1/6 of a dinar for testimony.

The Nazirite Analogy: Resolving the Dilemma

Baraita compares impure nazirite to one who swears falsely on a deposit
↓
Impure nazirite who intentionally became impure → flogged
↓
"Shall you say the same about one who swore on a deposit, who is not flogged?"
↓
Inference
Not flogged → but does bring a guilt-offering even with forewarning!
Nazirite's offering
Not for atonement — for purification to restart naziriteship in purity
↓
Conclusive refutation of Rabba's position: offering applies even with witnesses and forewarning
Resolution The nazirite comparison proves that one who takes a false oath on a deposit brings an offering even when forewarned — no lashes.

Denial of a Claim with Witnesses vs. Promissory Note

R' Yochanan
One who denies a monetary claim to which there are witnesses → liable for a guilt-offering
R' Yochanan
One who denies a debt with a promissory note → exempt from a guilt-offering
Rav Pappa
Witnesses can die, so the denial could have succeeded → it counts as a real denial
Rav Huna b. R' Yehoshua
Notes can also be lost! Rather: a promissory note creates a lien on land (shibud karka'ot), and oaths don't apply to land
R' Yochanan
Therefore: witnesses who deny knowing about land ownership are also exempt from an oath of testimony offering
Principle A promissory note is treated as a lien on real property — and the oath on a deposit does not apply to land claims.
