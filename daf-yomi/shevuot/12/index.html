Unused Communal Offerings: What Happens Next?

Lambs consecrated for the תמיד (daily offering) remain unused at year's end
↓
Rabbis
May be redeemed even without a blemish — court stipulates consecration is conditional
R' Shimon
Cannot be redeemed unblemished — but may be offered as קייץ המזבח (supplementary offerings)
Key Principle The Gemara explores whether communal offerings can be redirected when no longer needed for their original purpose, and what mechanism permits it.

Can Unused Daily-Offering Lambs Be Redeemed?

R' Yochanan
Lambs consecrated for the תמיד that are not needed — according to the Rabbis, they may be redeemed even without a blemish.
R' Shimon
They may not be redeemed unblemished. The court does not make tacit stipulations (תנאי בית דין) on communal offerings.
R' Yitzchak / R' Yochanan
According to R' Shimon, one supplements the altar with them — קייץ המזבח — so the altar does not remain idle.
Context The Gemara tried to identify the Rabbis' view from existing sources but concludes R' Yochanan learned it through oral tradition.

R' Shimon: Burnt-Offering vs. Sin-Offering Reuse

A communal offering was lost and a replacement was sacrificed
↓
Originally a Burnt-Offering (עולה)
The animal itself may be used as קייץ המזבח — same category (עולה → עולה)
Originally a Sin-Offering (חטאת)
The animal itself may not be used — must graze until blemished, then proceeds buy supplementary offerings
↓
Reason for distinction: People might confuse a sin-offering before atonement with one after atonement — rabbinic decree
R' Shmuel bar Rav Yitzchak R' Shimon distinguishes: same-category reuse (עולה to עולה) is permitted directly, but cross-category (חטאת to עולה) requires selling the animal first.

Lost Yom Kippur Bull & Goat: R' Yehuda vs. R' Shimon

R' Yehuda

  • The original animals must be left to die (מתות)
  • A sin-offering whose owner already achieved atonement → dies
  • Applies to Yom Kippur bull, goat, and public idol-worship goats

R' Elazar & R' Shimon

  • Animals graze until blemished (ירעו)
  • Then sold — proceeds go to communal gift offerings
  • A communal sin-offering is not left to die
  • But the animal itself is not sacrificed directly (supports R' Shmuel bar Rav Yitzchak)
Abaye's Proof Abaye cites this baraita to support the distinction: if R' Shimon allowed direct reuse of sin-offerings, why would the baraita require grazing? It must be that cross-category reuse is prohibited.

Rules of Supplementary Offerings (קייץ המזבח)

RuleDetails
PurposeKeep the altar from remaining idle when no obligatory offerings are pending
Source animalsSurplus lambs from daily offerings (תמיד) — per R' Shimon
White figs analogySupplementary offerings are to the altar like dessert (קינוח) is to a person (Rav Chanina)
Bird offerings?No — Rav Nachman bar Rav Chisda: one does not supplement with bird burnt-offerings
Reason (birds excluded)Surplus comes from communal lambs, and there is no communal bird burnt-offering (Rav Shimi of Neharde'a)
Rava's Objection Rava called the bird-exclusion ruling an absurdity (בורכא), but Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak defended it based on the communal-offering source of supplementary animals.

Shmuel's Principle: Consecration vs. Slaughter

🐐 A goat is consecrated as a communal sin-offering for a Festival
↓
📋 Consecration defines only the general category — it is a sin-offering with blood on the outer altar
↓
🔪 The precise nature is defined at slaughter — for which occasion it actually atones
↓
🔄 If not used on its Festival → can be used on New Moon, Yom Kippur, or another Festival
↓
✅ R' Shimon concedes this flexibility for communal sin-offering goats
Shmuel's Rule קדושתן — their consecration — defines only the broad category (חטאת or עולה). שחיטתן — their slaughter — defines their specific purpose.
