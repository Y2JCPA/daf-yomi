5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yitzchak, what verse proves that a creditor acquires collateral?
A. "You shall not steal" (Exodus 20:13)
B. "And it shall be a righteousness for you" (Deuteronomy 24:13)
C. "Love your neighbor as yourself" (Leviticus 19:18)
D. "You shall not take a millstone as collateral" (Deuteronomy 24:6)
Correct: B — R' Yitzchak reasons: if the creditor doesn't own the collateral, then returning it cannot be called 'righteousness' — therefore the creditor must acquire it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rav Yosef consider a creditor holding collateral to be a paid bailee?
A. The creditor charges interest on the loan
B. The debtor pays the creditor a storage fee
C. Safeguarding collateral exempts the creditor from other costly mitzvot — the savings count as payment
D. The creditor can sell the collateral at a profit
Correct: C — Since safeguarding collateral is part of the mitzva of lending, the creditor is exempt from other mitzvot requiring expense (ha'osek b'mitzva). This financial benefit makes him a paid bailee.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel's view that Shevi'it does not abrogate debt when collateral is worth only half the loan?
A. He means the entire debt survives, supporting Shmuel
B. He means only the portion matching the collateral is protected — the collateral is just a reminder
C. He means the debtor forfeits the collateral entirely
D. He means the creditor must return the collateral at Shevi'it
Correct: B — The Gemara rejects the link to Shmuel: RSBG only means the portion of debt commensurate with the collateral's value is not abrogated. The rest of the debt is still subject to Shevi'it cancellation.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the Mishna, which of the following is NOT listed among those who take an oath and receive payment?
A. A hired worker claiming unpaid wages
B. A storekeeper relying on his ledger
C. A borrower who claims he already repaid
D. One whose opponent is suspect regarding oaths
Correct: C — The Mishna lists hired workers, robbery victims, injured persons, those facing suspect oath-takers, and storekeepers — but not a borrower claiming repayment, who would follow the standard Torah oath rules.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is R' Yehuda's qualification regarding the hired worker's oath?
A. The worker must have two witnesses to support his claim
B. The oath applies only when the employer makes a partial admission (modeh b'miktzat)
C. The worker can only claim wages up to one dinar
D. The oath must be taken within 30 days of the work
Correct: B — R' Yehuda holds the oath is only administered when there is partial admission — e.g., the worker claims 50 dinars and the employer admits to owing 25, creating a modeh b'miktzat scenario.
