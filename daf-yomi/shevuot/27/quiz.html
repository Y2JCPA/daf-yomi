Shevuot 27 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
If someone swears to eat matza on Pesach (a mitzva) and fails to do so, what is the halakha?
A. Liable for a sliding-scale offering
B. Liable for lashes
C. Exempt — already sworn from Sinai
D. Liable for a fixed sin-offering
Correct: C — Since one is already under oath from Sinai to perform mitzvot, an additional oath about a mitzva does not generate liability for a shevuat bitui offering.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava, what is the difference between swearing 'I will not eat this loaf' vs. 'I will not eat it'?
A. No difference — both apply to the whole loaf
B. 'This loaf' makes one liable per olive-bulk; 'it' only for eating the whole loaf
C. 'It' makes one liable per olive-bulk; 'this loaf' only for the whole loaf
D. 'This loaf' is a vain oath; 'it' is an oath on utterance
Correct: B — Rava teaches that 'I will not eat this loaf' creates liability for each olive-bulk eaten, while 'I will not eat it' creates liability only upon eating the entire loaf.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara conclude that 'to do evil or to do good' refers to optional matters rather than mitzvot?
A. Because mitzvot cannot be evil
B. Because the juxtaposition creates a logical contradiction when applied to mitzvot, and 'or' is needed to include doing good to others
C. Because R' Akiva said so explicitly
D. Because the verse appears in Leviticus, which deals only with optional matters
Correct: B — If applied to mitzvot, 'doing evil' (refraining from mitzva) becomes 'doing good' and vice versa — a contradiction. Additionally, 'or to do good' is needed to include doing good to others, which is only necessary if discussing optional matters.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
One swears to harm another person ('I will strike so-and-so') and does not follow through. What is the ruling?
A. Liable — it is an oath on utterance
B. Liable — it is a vain oath
C. Exempt — harming others is not fully in one's power
D. Exempt — it is a mitzva to refrain from harm
Correct: C — Since it is not within one's full power to carry out harm to others (the other person might resist, flee, etc.), this oath does not generate liability when broken.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rava, if someone took three identical oaths on a loaf and a chakham dissolves the first oath, what happens?
A. All oaths are dissolved automatically
B. The second oath takes effect in its place
C. He must bring three separate offerings
D. The third oath takes effect, skipping the second
Correct: B — Rava rules that redundant oaths lie dormant but are not void. When the first oath is dissolved by a halakhic authority, the second oath retroactively takes effect — 'if you find room for it, it goes into effect.'
