Rabbi Yishmael's View: What Does the External Goat Atone For?

Gemara
According to Rabbi Yishmael, one who had no awareness at the beginning but had awareness at the end must bring an offering. So what does the external goat atone for?
Answer
It atones for a case of no awareness at all — neither beginning nor end.
Challenge
But the Festival and New Moon goats already atone for that!
Resolution
R' Yishmael holds like Rabbi Meir: all goats of the additional offerings (New Moon, Festivals, Yom Kippur) share the same atonement — defiling the Temple or its sacrificial foods.
Gemara
Then why does the Torah juxtapose the internal and external goats?
Answer
To teach: just as the internal goat atones only for Temple defilement, so too the external goat — not for other transgressions.
Rabbi Meir's Principle All goats of the musaf offerings effect the same type of atonement: defilement of the Temple or its sacred foods (טומאת מקדש וקדשיו).

The New Moon Goat: 'A Sin to the Lord'

וּשְׂעִיר עִזִּים אֶחָד לְחַטָּאת לַה׳ (במדבר כח:טו)
Rav Yehuda says Shmuel says Rabbi Yehuda's reasoning: The verse states "וּשְׂעִיר עִזִּים אֶחָד לְחַטָּאת לַה׳" (Numbers 28:15) — 'A goat for a sin-offering to the Lord.'

The phrase חַטָּאת לַה׳ (literally 'a sin to God') alludes to a sin that only God knows about — where there was no awareness at the beginning or the end.

But doesn't Reish Lakish need this phrase for his teaching: God says, 'This goat shall atone for My having diminished the moon'?

Resolution: The verse could have said חַטָּאת לַה׳ (sin-offering of the Lord). Instead it says חַטָּאת לַה׳ (sin-offering to the Lord) — yielding both derivations.

Deriving the Festival Goats' Atonement

Problem: We know the New Moon goat atones for no-awareness cases. What about Festival goats?
↓
Can we compare Festivals to New Moon?
Rejected: New Moon is more frequent
↓
Can we compare Festivals to Yom Kippur?
Rejected: Yom Kippur has more extensive atonement
↓
Solution (R' Ḥama b. R' Ḥanina):
The verse says "וּשְׂעִיר" — 'And a goat' — the extra vav links Festival goats to the New Moon goat mentioned earlier in the passage
↓
✅ Festival goats atone for the same cases as New Moon goats: no awareness at beginning or end
Textual Key The word "וּשְׂעִיר" (AND a goat) rather than just "שְׂעִיר" (a goat) creates the link between Festival and New Moon offerings.

Rabbi Yehuda vs. Rabbi Shimon: New Moon Goats

Rabbi Yehuda

  • New Moon goats atone for no awareness at all
  • Derived from "חַטָּאת לַה׳" — a sin only God knows
  • Festival goats atone for the same category
  • Both cover defilement of Temple/sacred foods

Rabbi Shimon

  • Festival goats atone for no-awareness cases
  • New Moon goats atone for something different
  • New Moon goat: a pure person who ate impure sacrificial food (טהור שאכל את הטמא)
  • Derived via verbal analogy (גזירה שוה) with the frontplate (ציץ)
Core Dispute R' Yehuda: New Moon and Festival goats share the same function. R' Shimon: They serve different purposes — Festivals cover unawareness, New Moons cover impure food eaten by a pure person.
