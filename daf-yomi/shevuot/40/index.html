What Does a Monetary Claim Really Mean?

Rav
A claim for a gold dinar means the value of a gold dinar — so admitting to silver triggers an oath
Shmuel
A claim for a gold dinar means the specific coin — admitting to silver is a different type entirely
R' Elazar
The mishna about coins refers to 'a dinar of coins' — all coins are one type (min), even a peruta
Rav Ashi
For Shmuel: saying 'gold dinar' is like saying 'coined gold dinar' — always referring to the coin itself
Core Dispute Does naming a coin in a claim refer to its monetary value or to the physical coin itself? This affects when partial admission triggers an oath.

Gold Dinar Claim: Value or Object?

Rav
"I have a gold dinar in your possession" means: you owe me the value of a gold dinar
Shmuel
It means you owe me an actual gold dinar — the specific coin
Gemara
Proof for Rav: The mishna says if defendant admits to silver, he's liable — all coins are one type. If the claim was for actual gold, admitting silver should be a different type!
R' Elazar
The mishna means 'a dinar of coins' — the claim was for coins generically. All coins including a peruta are one min (type)
Key Phrase שכולן מין מטבע הן — 'They are all one type; they are all coins' — This phrase is read differently by each side of the dispute.

Claim for a Gold Dinar: When Is There Oath Liability?

Claimant says: "You owe me a gold dinar"
↓
Rav (Value)
Claim = value of gold dinar
Admitting silver/copper = partial admission
→ Liable for oath
Shmuel (Object)
Claim = the actual coin
Admitting silver = different type entirely
→ Exempt from oath
Baraita Support R' Ḥiyya teaches a baraita supporting Rav: If one claims a sela and defendant admits to a sela minus 2 ma'a — liable. Minus only 1 ma'a — exempt (denial less than 2 ma'a).

Oath Thresholds: Partial Admission vs. One Witness

Partial Admission (מודה במקצת)

  • Claim must be at least 2 silver ma'a
  • Defendant admits to part, denies part
  • Denial must be at least 2 ma'a
  • Standard oath mechanism

One Witness (עד אחד)

  • Claim can be as low as 1 peruta
  • Witness supports part of the claim
  • Derived from Deut. 19:15 — witness 'rises up' for oath
  • Lower threshold because witness adds credibility
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak / Shmuel Where two witnesses would make one liable to pay (from 1 peruta), one witness makes one liable to take an oath — at the same threshold.

Vessels and Land: How Movable Property Binds Land

📋 Claimant sues for vessels AND land
↓
🏠 Defendant admits to land, denies vessels → Exempt (land not subject to oath)
↓
🏺 Defendant admits to part of vessels claim → Liable for oath
↓
🔗 Oath on vessels binds the land too — must swear about both!
↓
📖 Source: Mishna Kiddushin 26a — movable property binds land to the oath
Why Teach It Here? Kiddushin mentions this incidentally; Shevuot is the primary source since its subject is the halakhot of oaths.
