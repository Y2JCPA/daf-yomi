Shevuot 40 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav, what does a claim for a 'gold dinar' refer to?
A. The specific gold coin itself
B. The value of a gold dinar
C. Any gold item worth a dinar
D. A gold bar weighing one dinar
Correct: B — Rav holds that naming a coin in a claim refers to its monetary value, not the physical coin. This is why admitting to owing silver when gold was claimed still constitutes partial admission.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Shmuel's opinion require a lower threshold (1 peruta) when one witness supports the claim?
A. Because one witness is more reliable than partial admission
B. Because Deuteronomy 19:15 implies a witness can 'rise up' to impose an oath at any amount
C. Because the Rabbis enacted a special leniency for witnesses
D. Because a peruta is the minimum for any monetary claim
Correct: B — From Deuteronomy 19:15 — a single witness cannot establish guilt for 'any iniquity or sin,' but CAN impose an oath. Since two witnesses create payment liability from 1 peruta, one witness creates oath liability at the same threshold.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If one claims another owes wheat AND barley, and the defendant admits to barley only, what is the dispute?
A. All agree he is liable
B. All agree he is exempt
C. Shmuel says liable, R' Ḥiyya bar Abba (in R' Yoḥanan's name) says exempt
D. Rav says exempt, Shmuel says liable
Correct: C — Shmuel holds this is partial admission of a single combined claim (liable). R' Ḥiyya bar Abba in R' Yoḥanan's name holds these are two separate claims, and admitting to one while denying the other is complete denial of one claim, not partial admission (exempt).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does an oath on vessels affect a simultaneous claim for land?
A. Land is always exempt from oaths regardless
B. The oath on vessels 'binds' the land, requiring an oath on both
C. The defendant must pay for the land without an oath
D. The land claim is dismissed entirely
Correct: B — When a defendant is liable for an oath on movable property (vessels), that oath 'binds' the land claim as well — he must swear about both. This principle is taught in Kiddushin 26a and discussed primarily here in Shevuot.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
R' Ḥiyya bar Abba dismisses the baraita about ox-and-sheep claims by attributing it to which Tanna?
A. Rabbi Akiva
B. Rabbi Meir
C. Rabban Gamliel
D. Rabbi Elazar
Correct: C — R' Ḥiyya bar Abba argues the baraita follows Rabban Gamliel, who uniquely holds that even cross-type admissions (claiming wheat, admitting barley) constitute partial admission and trigger oath liability — a position the other Rabbis reject.
