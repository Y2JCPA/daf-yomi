QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yochanan, what is the ONE case where even R' Meir considers an oath to be a 'general' denial (one liability)?
A. When the defendant says 'and nothing of yours' with the conjunction
B. When the defendant says 'nothing of yours' without the conjunction
C. When the defendant addresses all claimants in the plural: 'nothing of yours (all of you) is in my possession'
D. When the defendant says 'on my oath' only once at the end
Correct: C — R' Yochanan holds that R' Meir considers both 'nothing of yours' and 'and nothing of yours' to be specific. The only general formulation for R' Meir is addressing all claimants collectively in the plural.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does R' Shimon exempt the defendant from a guilt-offering in the rape/seduction case?
A. Because the oath was technically true
B. Because the father is primarily claiming a fine, which cannot be collected through self-admission
C. Because the defendant did not say 'Amen' explicitly
D. Because rape and seduction claims require two witnesses
Correct: B — According to Ravin's version of R' Yochanan, R' Shimon holds the father claims only the fine (kenas). Since a fine is not paid based on self-admission, the oath denial has no practical monetary consequence, so no guilt-offering is required.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Abaye reject Rava's wheat-barley-spelt analogy for explaining R' Shimon?
A. Because wheat and barley are the same species
B. Because in the rape case the defendant makes a blanket denial, not a denial limited to one item
C. Because the analogy only works for three items, not two
D. Because Rava forgot to include the oath formula
Correct: B — Abaye noted that in Rava's analogy the defendant denies only the wheat specifically, while in the rape case the defendant denies everything ('I never raped or seduced'). A blanket denial that is partly false creates liability, unlike a specific truthful denial about one item.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the Mishna's rules for modeh b'miktzat (partial admission), why is the defendant exempt if he admits to one peruta when two silver ma'a are claimed?
A. Because the admission is too small relative to the claim
B. Because one peruta is made of copper, which is not the same type as silver ma'a
C. Because the claim must exceed 100 dinars
D. Because the defendant must admit to at least half the claim
Correct: B — The Mishna states the admission must be of the same type as the claim. Since silver ma'a and copper perutot are different types of currency, admitting to a peruta when silver is claimed does not constitute valid partial admission.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why is someone exempt from an oath when they admit to part of a debt claimed against their deceased father?
A. Because debts are not inherited under Torah law
B. Because they are treated as 'returning a lost item' since they could have denied the entire claim
C. Because the claimant needs witnesses to prove the original debt existed
D. Because partial admission only applies to the original debtor, not heirs
Correct: B — Since the heir could easily have denied the entire debt with no witnesses to contradict them, their partial admission is treated as voluntarily returning a lost item (meshiv aveida). The Torah does not penalize such honesty with an oath requirement.
