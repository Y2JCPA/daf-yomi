Three Categories of Oath Violation

Shevuat Shav (Vain)

  • Denying what is known to all
  • E.g., calling a stone column 'gold'
  • Source: Exodus 20:7 — 'lo tisa'
  • Lashes if intentional; exempt if unwitting

Shevuat Sheker (False)

  • Swearing falsely about the past
  • E.g., 'I ate' when one did not
  • Source: Leviticus 19:12
  • R' Akiva: offering for unwitting violation

Oath on Utterance (Future)

  • 'I will eat' / 'I will not eat'
  • Violated by acting contrary to oath
  • Source: Numbers 30:3 — 'lo yachel'
  • Dispute: lashes or only offering?
RAVIN REPORTS R' Yirmeyah → R' Abbahu → R' Yochanan: past false oaths violate Leviticus 19:12; future oath-breaking violates Numbers 30:3

Lashes Despite No Action?

R' Yosei HaGelili
Any prohibition without an action — one is not flogged, except three cases: oaths, substituting sacrificial animals, and cursing with God's Name
R' Shimon b. Yochai
Source for lashes for oaths: 'The Lord will not absolve him' (Exodus 20:7) — the heavenly court won't absolve, but the earthly court flogs and absolves
Rav Pappa
Perhaps the verse means he is never absolved at all — not even after lashes?
Abaye
If it said 'he will not be absolved' — yes. But it says 'the Lord will not absolve' — implying the earthly court can absolve through lashes
TEXTUAL PROOF The word 'the Lord' (Hashem) limits the non-absolution to the heavenly court, leaving room for earthly courts to administer lashes

R' Abbahu's Derivation: Which False Oath Gets Lashes?

R' Yochanan: 'in vain…in vain' appears twice in the verse → extra mention applies to false oaths
↓
R' Abbahu asks: Which false oath gets lashes?
Future: 'I will not eat' → ate
❌ Involves an action — lashes already apply normally, no derivation needed
Future: 'I will eat' → didn't eat
❌ R' Yochanan & Reish Lakish both agree: no lashes for this
↓
✅ Must be past oaths: 'I ate' / 'I didn't eat' — lashes despite no action
R' ABBAHU'S CONCLUSION The doubled 'in vain' extends lashes to false oaths about the past, parallel to oaths taken in vain
6 / 9

Why No Lashes for 'I Will Eat' (and Didn't)?

R' Yochanan
No lashes because it is a prohibition that does not involve an action (lav she'ein bo ma'aseh) — he simply failed to eat
Reish Lakish
No lashes because the forewarning is uncertain (hasra'at safek) — at any moment before the deadline, he might still eat; uncertain forewarning is no forewarning
Rava
The Torah explicitly extended liability via the doubled 'in vain' — just as an oath in vain refers to the past, so the false oath that receives lashes refers to the past
PRACTICAL DIFFERENCE R' Yochanan's reason (no action) applies broadly; Reish Lakish's reason (uncertain forewarning) is specific to time-limited future oaths

Reconciling the Mishna: R' Yishmael vs. R' Akiva

IssueR' YishmaelR' Akiva
Oaths about the pastNo offering liabilityOffering liability — just like future oaths
'This is an oath on an utterance' excludes…Past oaths (I ate / I didn't eat)Future violation by omission (I will eat → didn't) from lashes
Mishna consistencyFirst clause follows R' Yishmael, latter follows R' Akiva — problematicEntire mishna follows R' Akiva — preferred
LogicFuture clause excludes past oath — mismatchFuture clause excludes future scenario — internally consistent
RESOLUTION The Gemara prefers reading the entire mishna as R' Akiva: 'this is an oath on an utterance' excludes future omission from lashes (but not from offering)
