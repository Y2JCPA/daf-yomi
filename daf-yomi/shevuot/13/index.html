Does Yom Kippur Atone Without Repentance?

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
For all transgressions in the Torah, Yom Kippur atones whether or not one repented — with three exceptions.
Rabbis (Sifra)
The word "akh" (yet) in the verse limits Yom Kippur's atonement — it atones only for those who repent.
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
The exceptions: denying God (porek ol), distorting Torah (megaleh panim), and nullifying circumcision (meifer brit).
Rabbis
Hikkaret tikkaret means cut off in this world and the World-to-Come. "His sin is upon him" — death cleanses if he repented.
Core Dispute Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi sees Yom Kippur as inherently powerful enough to atone for most sins without repentance; the Rabbis require repentance for all atonement.

The Three Severe Exceptions — Numbers 15:31

"Scorned the Word of the Lord"

  • Porek ol malkhut shamayim — divests the yoke of Heaven (denies God)
  • Megaleh panim ba-Torah — reveals facets of Torah contrary to its meaning
  • These attack the foundations of divine authority and revelation

"Nullified His Commandment"

  • Meifer brit milah — nullifies the covenant of circumcision
  • Circumcision is the physical sign of the covenant
  • Rejecting it symbolizes rejection of the entire covenant relationship
Key Principle These three sins represent a total rejection of the God-Israel relationship: denying God, corrupting His Torah, and repudiating His covenant.

Can the Mishna Follow Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi?

Gemara
The latter clause of the mishna follows Rabbi Yehuda (priests atone via scapegoat). So the first clause must also be Rabbi Yehuda — not Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi!
Rav Yosef
The entire mishna can follow Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi. On the scapegoat-for-priests question, he simply holds like Rabbi Yehuda.
Abaye
Does Rabbi Yehuda also hold like Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi on atonement without repentance? Or is the agreement only one-directional?
Rav Yosef
It is specifically one-directional: Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi holds like Rabbi Yehuda, but Rabbi Yehuda does not hold like Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi on repentance.
Proof The unattributed baraita in the Sifra (attributed to Rabbi Yehuda) derives from "akh" (yet) that Yom Kippur atones ONLY for those who repent — contradicting Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi.

Atonement VehicleRabbi YehudaRabbi Shimon

Internal Goat (blood inside)Atones for Israelites for tum'at Mikdash v'kodashavAtones for Israelites for tum'at Mikdash v'kodashav

The Sifra's Logical Argument (Rejected)

💡 Premise: Sin-offering and guilt-offering atone, and Yom Kippur atones
↓
⚖️ Sin-offering/guilt-offering atone only for those who repent → so Yom Kippur too?
↓
❌ Refutation: Sin-offerings don't atone for intentional sins; Yom Kippur DOES
↓
🔓 Since Yom Kippur is more powerful, perhaps it atones even without repentance?
↓
📖 Counter: "Akh" (yet) in Leviticus 23:27 LIMITS — repentance IS required
Attribution This unattributed Sifra baraita is attributed to Rabbi Yehuda, proving he disagrees with Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi on whether repentance is needed.
