Is Drinking Included in Eating?

Gemara
The verse says tirosh (wine) and writes 'you shall eat' — drinking is included in eating!
Challenge
Perhaps the verse refers to wine consumed via anigeron (a beet-and-wine stew), which is eaten, not drunk?
Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov
Derive it from Deuteronomy 14:26 — yayin (wine) and shekhar (strong drink) are written alongside 'you shall eat.'
Rava
The mishna proves it too: one who swears not to eat and then eats and drinks brings one offering — drinking must be included in eating.
Abaye
But the mishna's latter clause says swearing 'I will not eat and I will not drink' yields two offerings — contradicting you!
Rava
The mishna means he said 'I will not drink' first, then 'I will not eat.' Eating is not included in drinking, so two separate oaths.
Key Principle Shtiyas biklal achilah — Drinking is a subcategory of eating. An oath not to eat automatically covers drinking, but not vice versa.

Rava vs. Abaye: The Mishna's Proof

Rava
If drinking is not included in eating, why would the mishna need to teach that one who swore not to eat and then ate and drank brings only one offering? It's obvious — he never swore about drinking!
Abaye
But the latter clause: 'I will not eat and I will not drink' → he ate and drank → two offerings. If drinking is already in eating, the second oath adds nothing!
Rava
The mishna actually means he said 'I will not drink' first, then 'I will not eat.' Eating is NOT included in drinking, so both oaths take effect independently.
Gemara
But then if he says 'I will not eat AND I will not drink' (eat first), he'd bring only one offering. Why didn't the mishna teach that stronger case?
Gemara
Actually, even in that order — by saying 'I will not drink' separately, he reveals that his 'eating' oath meant eating alone, not including drinking. So: two offerings either way.
Gilui Da'at Adding 'I will not drink' retroactively reveals that 'I will not eat' was intended to cover eating alone — a principle of revealed intention.

One Offering vs. Multiple Offerings

General Oath → One Offering

  • 'I will not eat' → eats wheat, barley, spelt bread → ONE offering
  • 'I will not drink' → drinks wine, oil, honey → ONE offering
  • A single oath covers all items of that category

Itemized Oath → Multiple Offerings

  • 'I will not eat wheat BREAD, barley BREAD, spelt BREAD' → THREE offerings
  • 'I will not drink wine, oil, honey' → THREE offerings
  • Each specification creates an independent oath
Key Factor Repeating the word 'bread' before each grain individuates each statement as a separate oath. Specifying liquids by name when a simpler phrasing was available does the same.

Why Specifying Liquids Creates Separate Oaths

Alternative PhrasingWhy It Doesn't Work
'I will not drink these'Implies only these specific cups, not these types in general
'Liquids such as these'Could mean this volume — not less, not more
'From these types'Could mean the types generally but not these specific ones before him
'These and their types'This WOULD work — so specifying wine, oil, honey must mean he intended separate oaths
Rav Pappa When all three liquids are placed before him, he could have used simpler language. By naming each one — wine, oil, and honey — he intended to individuate each as a separate oath.

Rav Ashi's Precision from the Mishna

שתייה בכלל אכילה
Textual Proof The mishna says: 'On my oath I will not eat' — and he ate inedible foods or drank unfit liquids, he is exempt. This implies if the liquids were fit, he'd be liable — even though he only swore about eating. This proves drinking is included in eating (שתייה בכלל אכילה).
