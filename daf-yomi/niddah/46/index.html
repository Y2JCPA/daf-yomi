Niddah 46: Puberty Signs & the Minor on the Brink

Core Question: If a child develops two pubic hairs during the transition year, is this a valid sign of puberty?
↓
R' Yochanan / R' Yehoshua b. Levi
During the time = after the time → valid sign
Rav Hamnuna / Rabbi Zeira
During the time = before the time → mere mole
↓
Rava's ruling: During the time is considered as before the time
↓
Second topic: Does a discriminating minor (mufla ha-samukh le-ish) have Torah or rabbinic adult status for vows?
KEY THEME This daf resolves the status of puberty signs during the transition year and explores the legal standing of a minor approaching adulthood.
3 / 9

Two Hairs During the Time: Valid Sign or Mole?

Rav Hamnuna
The Mishnah implies: hairs after the time make one adult — meaning hairs during the time do not.
R' Yochanan
During the time is equivalent to after the time — two hairs at any point in the transition year are a valid sign of puberty.
R' Yehoshua b. Levi
I agree — during the time counts as after the time.
Rabbi Zeira
A baraita on vows says 'man' includes a boy of thirteen years and one day — implying hairs during that year are insufficient!
Gemara
This is a conclusive refutation (teyuvta) of R' Yochanan and R' Yehoshua b. Levi.
REFUTATION The baraita about vows (Numbers 6:2) proves that hairs during the transition year are not a valid sign of maturity.

Rav Nachman's Resolution: The Baraita's Internal Contradiction

First clause: From age 9 to 12+1 day → mole
Implies: in the 13th year itself → sign
↓
Last clause: At 13+1 day → sign
Implies: in the 13th year itself → mole
↓
Contradiction! How to resolve?
↓
Rav Nachman
Tannaitic dispute: one holds during = after, one holds during = before
Gemara's Rejections
Both clauses agree during = before; they differ on boy vs. girl or which Tanna
FOUR RESOLUTIONS The Gemara offers four ways to harmonize the baraita without requiring a tannaitic dispute about 'during the time.'

R' Yehuda HaNasi vs. R' Shimon ben Elazar: The Transition Year

R' Yehuda HaNasi

  • For a girl: the 12th year is 'after the time' → hairs are a valid sign
  • For a boy: the 13th year is 'before the time' → hairs are a mole
  • More lenient for girls reaching maturity

R' Shimon ben Elazar

  • For a girl: the 12th year is 'before the time' → hairs are a mole
  • For a boy: the 13th year is 'after the time' → hairs are a valid sign
  • More lenient for boys reaching maturity
KEY POINT These tannaitic positions allow the Gemara to resolve the baraita's contradictions without assuming a dispute about 'during the time.'

The Discriminating Minor (Mufla ha-Samukh le-Ish)

📜 A child approaching adulthood who understands the meaning of vows
↓
✅ His/her vows of prohibition (neder issur) take effect
↓
✅ The prohibition of 'he shall not profane his word' applies
↓
⚠️ Intentional violation of an oath — equated to an adult
↓
❌ But NOT liable to bring an offering for misuse of consecrated property (me'ilah)
↓
📖 'This is the matter' (Numbers 30:2) — limits adult status to vow-related matters only
EXCLUSION The word 'this' (zeh) teaches that only for vows is a discriminating minor treated as an adult — not for offerings or other penalties.
