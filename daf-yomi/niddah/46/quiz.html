QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is Rava's final ruling about two pubic hairs that develop during the transition year?
A. During the time is considered as after the time — it is a valid sign
B. During the time is considered as before the time — it is a mole
C. It depends on whether the child is male or female
D. The hairs are valid only if they persist until after the transition year
Correct: B — Rava rules definitively that 'during the time is considered as before the time' (toch ha-zman ki-lifnei ha-zman), meaning the hairs are treated as a mole and do not confer adult status.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara refute R' Yochanan and R' Yehoshua ben Levi's position?
A. From a Mishnah about marriage age
B. From a baraita about vows that specifies 'thirteen years and one day'
C. From a logical argument about biological development
D. From a dispute between Rav and Shmuel
Correct: B — Rabbi Zeira cites a baraita on Numbers 6:2 that includes a boy of 'thirteen years and one day' for vows — implying that hairs during the thirteenth year are insufficient, which refutes the view that during = after.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why is a discriminating minor (mufla ha-samukh le-ish) NOT liable to bring an offering for me'ilah (misuse of consecrated property)?
A. Because minors cannot consecrate property at all
B. Because the verse says 'this is the matter' — limiting adult status to vows only
C. Because me'ilah applies only to Kohanim
D. Because offerings can only be brought by those over twenty
Correct: B — The word 'this' (zeh) in Numbers 30:2 limits the minor's adult-equivalent status to vow-related matters only, excluding liability for an offering due to misuse of consecrated items.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the core disagreement between Rav Kahana and R' Yochanan/Reish Lakish?
A. Whether girls and boys have the same transition year
B. Whether a discriminating minor's adult status is by Torah law or rabbinic law
C. Whether two hairs during the time count as a sign
D. Whether a minor can take a Nazirite vow
Correct: B — Rav Kahana holds the minor's status is rabbinic, so others who eat food he consecrated are not flogged. R' Yochanan and Reish Lakish hold it is Torah law, so they are flogged.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Shmuel resolve the problem of a rabbinic husband nullifying a minor orphan girl's vows?
A. He rules the husband cannot nullify her vows
B. He says the orphan girl's vows never take effect at all
C. He argues it works either way: rabbinic husband nullifies rabbinic vows, or if Torah vows — adults need not prevent a minor's violations
D. He distinguishes between vows made before and after the betrothal
Correct: C — Shmuel provides a two-pronged resolution: if vows are rabbinic, a rabbinic husband can nullify them; if Torah-level, it is comparable to a minor eating neveilah — the court (or husband) is not commanded to intervene.
