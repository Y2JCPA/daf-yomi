Prayer, Marriage & Modesty for Sons

Sages of Alexandria
Many people married appropriate women and acted modestly — it didn't help them have sons!
R' Yehoshua
They should pray to the One to Whom sons belong: "Children are a heritage of the Lord" (Psalms 127:3)
Gemara
If it's about prayer, what does marrying well and modesty add?
Gemara
Prayer without marrying appropriately and modesty does not suffice — both are needed.
R' Ḥama b. R' Ḥanina
"Fruit of the womb is a reward" — reward for men who withhold to let their wives emit seed first.
Takeaway Meriting children requires three elements: appropriate spouse, modest conduct, and prayer.

Why Do All Dying Women Have Niddah Status? (Beit Shammai)

Initial suggestion: Fear of the Angel of Death causes bleeding (like Esther — vatitḥalḥal)
↓
❌ But the Mishnah (39a) says trepidation eliminates menstrual flow!
↓
Resolution: Extended worry contracts muscles; sudden fear relaxes them
↓
❌ But then men should be zavim from fear too — yet zav discharge from external cause is not impure!
↓
✅ Real reason: Rabbinic decree to protect the honor of living menstruating women
Key Insight The Sages enacted that utensils of all dying women (and men) require immersion so that living niddot and zavim would not be embarrassed.

Quarter-Log of Blood After Death: Whose Impurity?

First Tanna

  • Transmits impurity by touching & carrying as niddah blood
  • Also transmits impurity in a tent as corpse blood (quarter-log)
  • R' Ze'eiri: Even first tanna agrees only if blood displaced while alive

Rabbi Yehuda

  • Does NOT transmit niddah impurity — blood displaced after death
  • Exception: Woman on mashber (travailing chair) — blood displaced while alive
  • Principle: Blood does not nullify blood

Rabbi Yosei

  • Regarding the mashber case: blood does NOT transmit tent impurity
  • Reason: It did not come from a corpse (displaced while alive)
Dispute The debate centers on whether blood displaced after death can carry niddah impurity, and whether living blood can carry corpse-tent impurity.

Rabbi Yehuda vs. Rabbis: Blood Nullification Cases

ScenarioRabbisR' Yehuda
Killed on a bed, blood drips into holeImpure (quarter-log)Impure — blood seeps through bed
Crucified on wood, blood runs downImpure (quarter-log)Pure — blood from life remains on wood
Logic of nullificationLiving blood nullifies death-blood as each drop falls inBlood does not nullify blood
Distinction On a bed, blood seeps through so no blood remains above. On wood, blood may remain on the surface — so R' Yehuda can say the death-blood stayed on the tree.

Woman Observing Days of Purity & the Paschal Offering

👶 Woman gives birth → observes 7/14 days of impurity
↓
🛁 Immerses in mikveh → enters period of dam tohar (blood of purity)
↓
📜 Initial ruling: She may pour water from a vessel to rinse the Paschal offering but may NOT touch the water
↓
🔄 Later ruling: Status like one who touched a met-impure person — first-degree for kodshim only
↓
💧 For non-sacred items (including Paschal rinsing water): She may touch the water itself
↓
⚠️ Beit Shammai: Her status is like one impure from corpse contact — even non-sacred vessels become impure
Abba Shaul A tevul yom has first-degree status for kodshim: renders two items impure and disqualifies a third (4th degree).
