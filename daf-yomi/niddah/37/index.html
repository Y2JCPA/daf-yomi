Niddah 37: Labor Blood and the Zava's Count

כל דבר שמטמא סותר? או דילמא דבר שעושה אותה זבה סותר
CORE QUESTION When a zava experiences blood emission due to labor pains, does this negate her count of seven clean days? The Gemara explores whether any impurity-causing substance negates the count, or only a substance that causes zava status.

Reconciliation Beyond Death

שמע מינה עבדו רבנן שלמא בהדי הדדי
AGGADAH Upon Rav Asi's death, Sheila bar Avina — who had been excommunicated by Rav Asi — prepared his own shrouds, fearing Rav Asi would criticize him before Rav in the next world. He soon died, and their biers were brought together. Those present saw myrtle branches flying between the biers, and concluded: "The Sages have made peace with one another."

Does Labor Blood Negate a Zava's Count?

Rava
Dilemma: Does every substance that imparts impurity negate the count of seven clean days? Or only a substance that causes zava status?
Abaye
An emission of ziva due to ones (duress) should prove the point — it doesn't cause zav status, yet it negates the count!
Rava
No! Ones-discharge does cause zav status — the Mishnah (Zavim 2:2) says: after the third sighting, one is not examined for ones. So ones can make a zav.
Abaye
But according to Rabbi Eliezer, who says one examines even after the third — ones never causes zav status. Would you say it doesn't negate the count?
Rava
According to R' Eliezer — indeed, ones does not negate the count.
KEY PRINCIPLE Rava holds: only a substance that causes zava/zav status can negate the count of clean days.

Father of R' Avin: What Determines Negation?

A zav has a discharge during his clean days
↓
What type of discharge?
Ziva discharge
Causes 7-day impurity → Negates all 7 clean days
Seminal emission
Causes 1-day impurity → Negates only that day
RABBIS' VIEW A substance that causes zav status negates the full count; one that doesn't (like labor blood) does not negate the count.

Does Birth Count Toward Seven Clean Days?

Abaye

  • Birth does NOT negate the count
  • But birth days do NOT count toward the seven clean days
  • Derives from Leviticus 12:2 — days of birth impurity are like days of menstruation
  • Just as menstruation days are unfit for ziva counting, so too birth days

Rava

  • Birth does NOT negate the count
  • Birth days CAN count toward the seven clean days
  • Abaye's baraita follows R' Eliezer, not the Rabbis
  • If no bleeding occurs during birth impurity, those days count
DISPUTE Both agree labor blood doesn't negate — but they disagree whether the days of birth impurity count toward the seven clean days.
