Examination Requirements: When and Why?

Core Question: Must a woman examine herself before/after intercourse?
↓
Before Intercourse
R' Zeira asks Rav Yehuda → No, lest the husband fear sin and separate
After Intercourse
R' Abba asks Rav Huna → No, same concern about husband's conscience
↓
Mishna does require examination before intercourse — but what does this mean?
↓
R' Ami: This is called the 'bedikah of virtuous women' — those who fulfill the Sages' words
KEY THEME This daf balances the ideal of thorough examination against the practical concern that excessive checking may cause a husband to needlessly separate from his wife.

Should She Examine Before Intercourse?

R' Zeira
What is the halakha — should a woman examine herself before intercourse?
Rav Yehuda
She should not examine herself.
R' Zeira
But what would be the problem if she did?
Rav Yehuda
The heart of a scrupulous husband (ba'al nefesh) might strike him with pangs of conscience, and he will separate from his wife out of fear she is impure.
PRINCIPLE Excessive examination can undermine marital harmony — the Sages weigh halakhic precision against practical domestic consequences.

Post-Intercourse Examination: The Timing Problem

R' Abba's Dilemma: Should she examine after intercourse to establish liability for a korban chatat?
↓
Rav Huna: Can she do a full bedikah within the minimum onset period?
↓
Baraita: The onset period = time for the organ to exit and a cloth to enter — like items standing beside a doorpost
↓
Full Internal Exam
Takes too long — cannot establish certainty within the onset period
External Wipe (Kinuach)
Quick enough — could theoretically establish liability
↓
Rav Huna still says no — same concern: the husband's conscience
KEY DISTINCTION The Gemara distinguishes between a full internal bedikah (examination) and a quick external kinuach (wipe) — only the latter fits within the minimum onset period.

What Does 'Virtuous Women's Cloth' Mean?

R' Ami (R' Yannai)

  • Pre-intercourse exam = 'bedikah of tzenu'ot'
  • Anyone who fulfills the Sages' words is called 'virtuous'
  • This is not beyond the letter of the law — it IS the halakha

Rava's Interpretation

  • 'Virtuous' = those who use a FRESH cloth each time
  • Even if the previous cloth showed no blood, they don't reuse it
  • Non-virtuous women reuse the same cloth for multiple examinations
RAVA'S CHALLENGE If 'virtuous' simply means following the Sages, then one who doesn't follow them should be called wicked — not merely 'not virtuous.' Therefore Rava redefines the term as referring to extra care with fresh cloths.

Examination Rules: Fixed vs. Unfixed Cycle

ConditionHas Fixed Veset (וסת קבוע)No Fixed Veset
Awake before intercourseMust examineMust examine
Sleeping before intercourseNot required (too much effort)Must examine — even if sleeping
Handling pure items (טהרות)Must examine for both purity and intercourseMust examine for both
Not handling pure itemsNot required for intercourse alone (per Shmuel)Must examine even for intercourse alone
SHMUEL'S RULE A woman without a fixed cycle is forbidden to engage in intercourse until she examines herself. R' Abba bar Yirmeya clarifies: the key variables are whether she has a veset, whether she is awake, and whether she handles taharot.

A Woman Without a Veset: R' Meir vs. R' Chanina

R' Meir
A woman without a fixed cycle is forbidden to have intercourse. She loses her ketubah, produce rights, sustenance, and worn property. He must divorce her and may never remarry her.
R' Chanina ben Antigonus
She is permitted to have intercourse, but must examine with two cloths — one before and one after. These may work to her detriment (finding blood) or her betterment (finding none).
Abba Chanan
Woe to her husband!
Shmuel
The halakha follows R' Chanina ben Antigonus.
NEVER REMARRY? Even if she later establishes a fixed cycle, he cannot remarry her — lest she marry another, be 'cured,' and the first husband claim the divorce was in error, rendering her children mamzerim.
