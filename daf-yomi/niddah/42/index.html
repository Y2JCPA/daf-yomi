Niddah 42: Impurity Classification of Discharged Semen

Core Question: What is the impurity status of a woman who discharges semen?
↓
Emission (רואה)
Negates 7 clean days
Impure by any amount
Impure while still inside
Contact (נוגעת)
Does not negate count
Requires minimum amount
Impure only outside body
↓
Rabbis: Emission — R' Shimon: Contact (at least re: inside/outside)
↓
Rav Shmuel bar Bisna's dilemma: What does R' Shimon hold about the other two criteria?
Daf Theme This daf explores the precise classification of semen discharge impurity and transitions into defining the halakhic status of the vaginal space itself — encapsulated or concealed.

Reconciling Rava and R' Shimon on Semen Discharge

Rava
It is impossible for a woman not to discharge semen over three days.
R' Shimon
Semen can remain pure after three days — she may never discharge it.
Gemara
No contradiction! Rava speaks of a woman who turns in bed — she will certainly discharge.
Gemara
R' Shimon speaks of a woman who does not turn — she won't discharge at all.
Gemara
The Torah's verse (Lev. 15:18) — 'impure until evening' — refers to a woman who doesn't turn. If she turns, she is prohibited from terumah for three full days.
Key Distinction Movement in bed determines whether semen will be discharged. The Torah's baseline assumes a still position.

Rava and the Shabbat Circumcision

👤 A man asks Rava: May I circumcise my son on Shabbat?
↓
✅ Rava answers: Yes, you may
↓
🤔 After the man leaves, Rava wonders: Why would he ask such a basic question?
↓
🏃 Rava chases after him and asks: How did the birth happen?
↓
🔊 The man reveals: I heard the baby cry on Friday night before Shabbat, but the full birth was on Shabbat
↓
👶 Rava rules: The baby's head emerged before Shabbat — it is considered born on Friday
↓
❌ Therefore circumcision is on the following Friday, NOT Shabbat — and a delayed circumcision does NOT override Shabbat
Principle הוציא ראשו — once the head emerges from the birth canal, the child is considered born. Circumcision not at its proper time (day 8) does not override Shabbat.

Encapsulated vs. Concealed: Status of the Vaginal Space

Abaye: Encapsulated (בלוע)

  • The vaginal space fully encapsulates objects
  • An olive-bulk of carcass placed inside does NOT impart impurity
  • Similar to something swallowed — no contact, no carrying

Rava: Concealed (בית הסתרים)

  • The vaginal space is a concealed area of the body
  • An olive-bulk of carcass placed inside DOES impart impurity
  • Not impure by contact, but impure by CARRYING (טומאת משא)
  • Derives support from Leviticus 15:18
Practical Case If another woman inserts a כזית of animal carcass into the vaginal space, does the woman become impure? Abaye says no (encapsulated); Rava says yes (concealed — she carries it).
