QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why do the Rabbanan reject R' Yochanan ben Nuri's kal va'chomer about the bird carcass?
A. Because food impurity as a general category requires hechsher, while severe impurity does not
B. Because the bird carcass is too small to transmit impurity
C. Because R' Yochanan ben Nuri misquoted the source verse
D. Because food impurity is only rabbinic and cannot be derived from Torah-level impurity
Correct: A — Rava/Rav Pappa explain that the general category of lenient food-impurity requires hechsher (liquid contact), unlike severe impurity as a category. This breaks the kal va'chomer, even though the specific bird carcass is an exception.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the School of R' Yishmael, why doesn't the carcass of a kosher bird need hechsher from a creeping animal?
A. Because it is inherently impure and will transmit severe impurity to humans (in the throat)
B. Because birds are not mentioned in the verse about seeds
C. Because bird carcasses are too dry to absorb liquid
D. Because the bird was already rendered impure at the moment of death
Correct: A — The School of R' Yishmael derives from Leviticus 11:37-38 that only items which never transmit severe impurity require hechsher. Since the bird carcass does transmit severe impurity (in the throat), it is exempt.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rav Kahana prove from the mishna about teruma dill?
A. That undesignated dill is considered prepared for a spice dish (kutach), not as a pot ingredient
B. That all dill is exempt from tithing obligations
C. That teruma dill can never become impure
D. That dill must be tithed before being placed in a pot
Correct: A — The mishna says teruma dill loses its status after flavoring a pot. Rav Kahana reasons: if undesignated dill were a pot ingredient, it should lose status even before being placed in a pot. Since it doesn't, undesignated dill must be considered a kutach (spice dish) ingredient.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What was the final ruling regarding spices like costus, peppers, and safflower?
A. They may be bought with second-tithe money but don't contract food-impurity
B. They contract food-impurity but may not be bought with second-tithe money
C. They neither may be bought with second-tithe money nor contract food-impurity
D. They both may be bought with second-tithe money and contract food-impurity
Correct: C — R' Yochanan ben Nuri reports that the Sages voted and concluded: these spices may NOT be bought with second-tithe money and do NOT contract tum'at okhalin. This follows his logic that the two statuses must be consistent.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Mishna, what is the relationship between Sabbatical Year eradication (bi'ur) and sanctity (kedusha)?
A. Eradication implies sanctity, but sanctity does not necessarily imply eradication
B. Sanctity implies eradication, but eradication does not necessarily imply sanctity
C. They always apply together — one cannot exist without the other
D. They are completely independent obligations with no connection
Correct: A — The Mishna teaches that all produce subject to eradication has Sabbatical sanctity, but some produce (like roots preserved in the ground) has sanctity without an eradication obligation. The stronger obligation entails the weaker, but not vice versa.
