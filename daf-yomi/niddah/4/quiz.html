QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Chizkiyya, why is the produce in the basket deemed pure when a sheretz is found inside?
A. Because the basket was covered and nothing could have entered
B. Because the sheretz is presumed to have entered after the produce was removed
C. Because the sheretz is too small to transmit impurity to produce
D. Because the basket was in a public domain where uncertain impurity is pure
Correct: B — Chizkiyya holds that since the basket was examined before the produce was placed inside, the sheretz must have entered only after the produce was removed, and therefore the produce remains pure.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the possibility that a raven threw the sheretz into the basket?
A. Ravens are not commonly found near baskets of produce
B. A raven lacks intent (da'at) and cannot be assumed to have performed a purposeful act
C. Ravens are themselves ritually impure and would contaminate the basket regardless
D. The basket was covered, making it impossible for a raven to access it
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes between a person, who acts with intent and can provide a plausible alternative explanation, and a raven, which acts without intent. Only intentional agents can break the presumption of impurity.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the practical halachic ruling of the Rabbis regarding retroactive impurity of a niddah?
A. She is impure from the moment she discovers the blood and no earlier
B. She is impure retroactively to her last examination, no matter how long ago
C. She is impure retroactively for the lesser of 24 hours or the time since her last examination
D. She is impure retroactively for exactly 24 hours in all cases
Correct: C — The Rabbis take a middle position: retroactive impurity is capped at 24 hours, but if her last examination was more recent than 24 hours ago, it only extends back to that examination.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say Rabba proposed that 'a woman can sense her flow' as the reason for the Rabbis' ruling?
A. Because it is the accepted halachic reasoning
B. Because he wanted to sharpen Abaye's analytical skills by presenting a flawed argument
C. Because Shmuel had taught this reason previously
D. Because it explains why the 24-hour limit was chosen
Correct: B — The Gemara reveals that Rabba did not seriously hold this position — he presented it as a pedagogical exercise to hone Abaye's mind (le'ḥaddedei le'Abaye). The real reason is that it was a rabbinic enactment (takkanah).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the case of uncertain impurity in a private domain, why is the loaf found on the ground deemed pure?
A. Because the impurity was only rabbinic (madaf) and the strict rule applies only to Torah-level impurity
B. Because the loaf was found in a public domain, not a private one
C. Because the shelf was examined beforehand and found clean
D. Because a raven was seen carrying the loaf
Correct: A — The Gemara offers two answers: (1) the loaf lacks consciousness and cannot be 'asked,' so it is pure in any domain; (2) the impurity involved is only rabbinic-level (madaf — light impurity), and the strict private-domain rule applies only to Torah-level impurity.
