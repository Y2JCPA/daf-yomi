5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava's reading, what is Rabbi Meir's position on attributing stains to a gentile woman?
A. She must have already experienced a menstrual flow
B. She need only be of age to experience bleeding, even without having bled
C. Any gentile woman regardless of age suffices
D. Only a gentile woman currently menstruating qualifies
Correct: B — Rava explains that Rabbi Meir is the LENIENT opinion — she need only be fit to see menstrual blood, even if she has never actually experienced bleeding. The first tanna is more stringent, requiring prior actual bleeding.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel allow attributing the stain to a שומרת יום כנגד יום on her second day?
A. Because she has already completed her purification
B. Because her uterus is considered 'open' and she is likely to bleed
C. Because she has no presumptive status of purity
D. Because she has already immersed in a mikveh
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that on her second day (the day after a discharge), her uterus is considered open and bleeding is likely, making attribution reasonable even though she lacks a presumptive status of seeing blood.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the scriptural source for Rabbi Neḥemya's ruling that stains on non-susceptible items don't cause impurity?
A. Leviticus 15:19 — regarding the niddah laws
B. Isaiah 3:26 — 'clean she shall sit upon the ground'
C. Numbers 19:15 — regarding open vessels
D. Leviticus 11:32 — regarding vessels that become impure
Correct: B — Rav Mattana cites Isaiah 3:26: 'clean she shall sit upon the ground' — teaching that sitting on the ground (which is not susceptible to impurity) means she remains clean/pure from blood stains.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the case of two women grinding at a mill, if blood is found beneath the OUTER woman, what is the ruling?
A. Both women are impure
B. Both women are pure
C. Only the outer woman is impure; the inner woman is pure
D. Only the inner woman is impure; the outer woman is pure
Correct: C — The outer woman is impure because the blood is beneath her. The inner woman is pure because the outer woman pushes INWARD (toward the mill), not outward — so the blood beneath the outer position could only be from the outer woman.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara distinguish between 'retroactive' and 'future' impurity when attributing stains?
A. Because retroactive impurity is more severe than future impurity
B. Because attributing retroactively doesn't worsen the borrower's status, but future attribution would ruin her clean-day counting
C. Because retroactive impurity only applies to gentile women
D. Because the Torah explicitly makes this distinction in Leviticus
Correct: B — Retroactive attribution merely confirms that items the already-impure woman touched were impure — no new harm. But attributing toward the future would ruin her counting of clean days, which is why the baraita restricts attribution in that direction.
