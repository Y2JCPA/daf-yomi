Niddah 7: Uncertain Purity & The Four Women

DAF OVERVIEW The daf transitions from the complex laws of challah of uncertain purity — exploring when untithed dough is treated like terumah — to the foundational Mishnah of four women whose time suffices (dayyan sha'atan). R' Eliezer and R' Yehoshua debate how many women are exempt from retroactive impurity, and the Gemara reveals why R' Eliezer's ruling was only adopted after his passing.

Challah of Uncertain Purity: What Level of Doubt?

Untithed dough with uncertain purity — what kind of uncertainty?
↓
NOT This
Equal proofs (two paths — one pure, one impure)
Even chullin would be impure here
Rather This
Leaning (haseita) — zav & pure person unloading a donkey with heavy load
Impure for terumah, pure for chullin
ABAYE & RAVA The uncertainty of challah occupies a middle ground — not a full doubt (which would affect even non-sacred food) but a lesser doubt that only matters because terumah is mixed into the dough.

Why Doesn't a Tevulat Yom Render Untithed Dough Impure?

Questioner
If untithed dough is treated like challah, a woman who immersed today (tevulat yom) should render it impure with her touch!
Abaye
My principle: Only an impurity source that definitely renders chullin impure (like a zav) triggers the decree for uncertain impurity of untithed dough.
Abaye
A tevulat yom doesn't render chullin impure even when definitely impure — so no decree was issued for her uncertain contact with untithed dough.
KEY PRINCIPLE The rabbinic decree treating untithed dough like challah only extends to impurity sources that would affect non-sacred food when definite.

The Four Women Whose Time Suffices (דיין שעתן)

WomanDefinitionWhen Status Ends
Virgin (בתולה)Never seen menstrual blood, even if marriedFirst sighting of blood
Pregnant (מעוברת)From when fetus is visibly apparentAfter birth
Nursing (מניקה)Actively nursing her childWhen she weans or stops nursing
Elderly (זקנה)3 consecutive 30-day cycles without blood, near old ageAfter first sighting
R' ELIEZER All four women transmit impurity only from the moment of sighting — not retroactively for 24 hours or from last examination.

R' Eliezer vs. R' Yehoshua: How Many Women?

R' Yehoshua
I heard from my teachers that dayyan sha'atan applies only to a virgin.
R' Eliezer
You did not hear, but I did hear. You heard about one woman; I heard about many women.
R' Eliezer
A parable: We don't tell someone who didn't see the new moon to come testify — only one who saw it. My tradition is broader than yours.
Gemara
During R' Eliezer's lifetime, they followed R' Yehoshua. After his passing, R' Yehoshua restored R' Eliezer's ruling.
WHY THE DELAY? R' Eliezer was a Shammuti (follower of Beit Shammai). If they followed him in one matter, people might follow him in others — and during his lifetime, they couldn't protest out of respect.

Nursing Woman Who Stopped: R' Meir vs. Rabbis

R' Meir

  • If she gave child to wet nurse, weaned, or child died
  • She transmits impurity for 24 hours retroactively
  • Or from her most recent examination
  • Loses her special dayyan sha'atan status

The Rabbis

  • Even if she stopped nursing
  • Her time is still sufficient
  • Retains dayyan sha'atan status
  • Only first sighting — second sighting reverts to normal rules
NOTE All agree: dayyan sha'atan applies only to the FIRST sighting. At the second sighting, she is like any other woman — 24 hours or from last examination.
