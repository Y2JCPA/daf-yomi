Is the Place of Ziva Considered a Source (Makor)?

Rava
The verse mentions 'issue' twice, referring to a second sighting of ziva of a leper. A special verse was needed because the leper's impurity comes from his tzara'at, not his ziva.
Rava
Since a verse was needed even for the second sighting of a leper's ziva, conclude: the place of ziva is NOT a makor (source). Otherwise the first sighting alone would suffice!
Rav Yehuda of Diskarta
How do you know the verse refers to a zav who is also a leper? Perhaps it speaks of an ordinary zav!
Rav Yehuda of Diskarta
Your kal vachomer can be refuted: the scapegoat causes impurity to the dispatcher, yet the goat itself is pure — a living animal cannot become tamei!
Key Issue Can something cause impurity to others without itself being impure? The scapegoat proves it can.

Abaye Resolves: Leper's First Sighting of Ziva

Rav Yosef's dilemma: Does the first sighting of ziva of a metzora (leper) impart tum'at massa (impurity through carrying)?
↓
Abaye: Rav Yosef himself derives from "This is the law of the zav" (Lev. 15:32) that zav laws apply to adults and minors alike
↓
This frees Lev. 15:33 — "whether male or female" — to include lepers
↓
"Male"
Includes a male leper regarding his bodily emissions
"Female"
Includes a female leper regarding her bodily emissions
↓
Result: Torah compares leper to full-fledged zav — first sighting of leper's ziva does impart impurity through carrying
Resolution Abaye shows Rav Yosef already has the tools to resolve his own dilemma from the verses he himself expounds.

Physical Characteristics: Ziva vs. Semen

זובו דומה למי בצק של שעורים
Rav Huna's Teaching Ziva: Similar to water of barley dough; comes from basar met (flaccid flesh); runny, resembling the white of an unfertilized egg.

Semen: Comes from basar chai (erect flesh); viscous, resembling the white of a fertilized egg.

Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel: Yoledet Who Didn't Immerse

Beit Hillel
Don't you concede that a niddah who didn't immerse after 7 days and then saw blood is impure as a menstruating woman? The same should apply to a yoledet who didn't immerse!
Beit Shammai
No! A niddah who immersed and immediately saw blood is still tamei. But a yoledet who immersed and then saw blood is pure (in her days of purity). They are not comparable!
Beit Hillel
A yoledet b'ziva proves our point! If she immersed in her days of purity and saw blood — pure. If she didn't immerse and saw blood — impure! Same principle!
Beit Shammai
Even a yoledet b'ziva who didn't immerse — her blood imparts impurity only while moist, not when dry. She is not a full niddah.
Core Dispute Beit Shammai: blood of a yoledet who didn't immerse imparts impurity only while moist. Beit Hillel: it has full niddah status — impure whether moist or dry.
