Niddah 55 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the practical difference between Reish Lakish and R' Yochanan regarding corpse flesh impurity?
A. Whether moist flesh is impure
B. Whether a crumbled corpse transmits impurity
C. Whether bones are impure
D. Whether flesh attached to a living person is impure
Correct: B — Reish Lakish derives from "from any" that even crumbled flesh is impure; R' Yochanan compares to a bone, which does not crumble, so crumbled flesh is pure.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Adda bar Ahava, why are teeth excluded from corpse impurity?
A. They are too small to transmit impurity
B. They are not present at birth
C. Their roots renew themselves
D. They are considered like hair
Correct: B — Teeth fail the first criterion: they were not created with the person at birth. Bones are the paradigm — present at birth and non-renewing.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Rav Yehuda of Diskarta say we need the verse 'his issue, it is impure' (Lev. 15:2)?
A. To teach that ziva is impure only when moist
B. To refute the scapegoat analogy — that something can cause impurity without itself being impure
C. To teach that a zav must bring a korban
D. To teach the number of emissions required
Correct: B — Without this verse, one could argue (like the scapegoat) that ziva causes impurity to others but is not itself impure. Lev. 15:33 teaches emission counts, not impurity of ziva itself.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why might one have thought the saliva of a zav transmits impurity without physical contact?
A. Because a zav's breath is impure
B. From a gezeirah shavah with chalitzah, where the yevama spits before (not on) her brother-in-law
C. Because airborne particles are considered contact
D. Because the Torah uses the word 'toward' not 'on'
Correct: B — A verbal analogy from chalitzah (Deut. 25:9) — the yevama spits 'before' her brother-in-law without touching him — might suggest proximity alone suffices. The verse specifies 'on' to require contact.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Ulla, why did the Sages decree that the skin of a dead person is impure, even though it is pure by Torah law?
A. Because skin does not renew itself
B. Because skin was created at birth
C. Lest a person fashion donkey rugs from the skins of deceased parents
D. Because skin is compared to flesh in all respects
Correct: C — Ulla states that by Torah law the skin of a dead person is tahor. The Sages decreed it tamei as a protective measure lest someone disrespectfully use the skins of deceased parents as donkey coverings.
