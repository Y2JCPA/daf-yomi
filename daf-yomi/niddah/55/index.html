Sources of Corpse & Zav Impurity

Reish Lakish
Corpse flesh is impure moist or dry from "from any impurity" (Lev. 22:5) — even crumbled flesh is impure
R' Yochanan
Derived from "bone of a man" (Num. 19:16) — corpse is like a bone: dry but not crumbled
Rav Adda bar Ahava
Only items created at birth whose root does not renew transmit corpse impurity
Rav Yehuda of Diskarta
"His issue, it is impure" (Lev. 15:2) is needed to teach that ziva itself is impure, not just that it causes impurity
Key Theme This daf explores the boundaries of impurity — which substances, in which states, transmit tum'ah, and the scriptural sources for each rule.

Source for Corpse Flesh Impurity When Dry

Reish Lakish
The verse says "mi-kol tum'ato" — from any impurity of a dead person (Lev. 22:5). This amplification includes moist and dry flesh.
R' Yochanan
The verse equates a dead man with "etzem adam" — a bone (Num. 19:16). Just as a bone is dry and impure, so corpse flesh is impure even when dry.
Gemara
What is the practical difference? A corpse so dry it crumbles: Reish Lakish says impure ("from any"); R' Yochanan says pure (unlike a bone, which doesn't crumble).
Resolution A baraita states crumbled flesh is pure — but only when it becomes like flour (dust), which even Reish Lakish would concede is no longer "flesh."

Zav Impurity: Contact vs. Carrying

Contact (Maga)

  • Person who touches zav becomes tamei
  • Question: Does ziva transmit impurity to garments by contact?
  • Compared to neveilah: contact makes person impure but not garments

Carrying (Masa)

  • Carrying ziva transmits impurity to person and garments
  • Derived from Lev. 15:2: "his issue, it is impure"
  • Aherim: verse juxtaposes zav's issue to the zav himself
Aherim's Principle Just as the zav himself transmits impurity to person and garments by both contact and carrying, so too his ziva does the same (Lev. 15:32–33).

Rav Yehuda of Diskarta: Parsing Leviticus 15:33

📖 "Zov" — indicates one emission of ziva
↓
📖 "Zovo" (his issue) — superfluous letter indicates two emissions
↓
👨 "For a male" — two emissions make him a zav, but only if not accidental (unlike a woman)
↓
👩 "Or a female" — for the third emission, even an accident obligates a korban
↓
⚠️ Since this verse teaches emission counts, we still need Lev. 15:2 to teach that ziva itself is tamei
Scapegoat Objection Without Lev. 15:2, one might argue like the scapegoat: it causes impurity to others yet is itself pure. So too ziva might cause impurity without being impure itself.
