Niddah 48 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to both Rav and Rav Yosef, how are 'years of a son and daughter' calculated?
A. By calendar years (Tishrei to Tishrei)
B. As full years from date of birth
C. By lunar months only
D. By the agricultural cycle
Correct: B — Both agree that years are calculated as full years from the date of birth/event — they only differ on which halakhic context (valuations vs. Yotze Dofen) the baraita refers to.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the Torah's source for requiring only the lower sign for a woman's majority?
A. Leviticus 25:15 about crop years
B. Numbers 5:6 equating man and woman for sins
C. Exodus 21:2 about the Hebrew slave
D. Deuteronomy 22:5 about male and female garments
Correct: B — Numbers 5:6 ('A man or woman, when they commit any of the sins of men') equates women to men for all Torah punishments, meaning the same sign (lower) that establishes a man's majority applies to women.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel, why do city girls develop the lower sign (pubic hair) before the upper sign?
A. Because of better nutrition in cities
B. Because they frequent the bathhouses
C. Because they carry heavy loads
D. Because of earlier marriage in cities
Correct: B — Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel explains that city girls frequent bathhouses, and the warm water stimulates hair growth, causing the lower sign to appear first.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
During the uncertain year (ages 11-12), how does R' Shimon say a woman examiner's testimony is accepted?
A. Fully accepted for all purposes
B. Not accepted at all
C. Accepted only to create stringencies (le-ḥumra)
D. Accepted only with corroborating witnesses
Correct: C — R' Shimon holds that during the uncertain period, a woman's testimony is accepted le-ḥumra (to be stringent) but not le-kula (to be lenient) — e.g., if she says the girl is an adult, the girl cannot perform mi'un (refusal).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When R' Zeira finally met R' Elazar in Eretz Yisrael, what did R' Elazar clarify about R' Yosei ben Keifar's opinion?
A. He never said it at all
B. He said it is the definitive halakha
C. He said it merely 'stands to reason' based on the Mishna's omission
D. He said it applies only to women
Correct: C — R' Elazar clarified that he did not rule definitively but said it 'stands to reason' — his reasoning was that the Mishna omits the phrase 'and one day' only in the last mishna, suggesting support for R' Yosei ben Keifar's position.
