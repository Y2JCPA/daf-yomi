Presumptive Impurity: Can It Be Resolved?

R' Meir
Any item with presumptive impurity (חזקת טומאה) remains impure forever — even if you examine and find nothing.
Rabbis
One searches until reaching bedrock or virgin soil. If nothing is found, we assume an animal removed the impurity.
R' Meir
A sycamore tree in Kefar Sava was examined — nothing found. Later the wind uprooted it and a skull was stuck in the roots!
Rabbis
That proves nothing — they simply didn't examine thoroughly enough. Our rule: search to bedrock, then it's pure.
R' Meir
But with blood from three women — even you agree all are impure when all examinations come up clean!
Rabbis
The cases differ: with stones, an animal could have taken the corpse. With blood — where else could it have come from?
Distinction The Rabbis agree with R' Meir on blood (impurity can't vanish) but disagree on stones (an animal could have removed it). The difference: blood has no plausible alternative explanation.

Sha'atnez Lost in a Garment: What Can You Do With It?

Prohibited

  • Wearing the garment
  • Selling to a gentile (lest a Jew buy a piece)
  • Making a saddlecloth for a donkey (lest one cut a piece for clothing)

Permitted

  • Making a shroud for the dead
  • Dyeing it — wool & linen absorb dye differently, revealing the lost thread (Rav Ḥisda)
  • Wearing it after dyeing if no diverse kinds is found
Rav Ḥisda's Proof Just as the Rabbis say a raven may have taken the corpse from beneath the stones, so too: if dyeing reveals no foreign thread, we say it fell out.

The Dead Are Free From Mitzvot

Rav Yosef
The fact that we may bury the dead in sha'atnez shrouds proves that mitzvot will be nullified in the future — otherwise the resurrected would violate the prohibition!
Abaye
But R' Yannai said shrouds of sha'atnez are permitted only for the eulogy, not for actual burial!
Rav Yosef
R' Yoḥanan disagrees — he permits even burial in sha'atnez.
R' Yoḥanan
"Set apart among the dead" (Psalms 88:6) — once a person dies, he becomes חפשי, free from the mitzvot.
Psalms 88:6 בַּמֵּתִים חׇפְשִׁי — R' Yoḥanan reads ḥofshi (free) as liberation from all commandments upon death.
