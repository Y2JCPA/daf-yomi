Niddah 61 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
When blood is found under the middle woman of three sharing a bed, who is impure?
A. Only the middle woman
B. The middle and inner women
C. The middle and outer women
D. All three women
Correct: D — Since the blood is under the middle woman, it could have come from either neighbor or from her — all three are suspect and therefore impure.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does entering the bed via the SIDE (rather than the foot) make all three women impure regardless of blood location?
A. Because the bed is considered broken
B. Because each woman passed over the spot where blood was found
C. Because side-entry invalidates the bed's purity
D. Because R' Meir says so and the Rabbis agree
Correct: B — When entering via the side, each woman climbed over the place where the blood was discovered, so any of them could have deposited it there.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
On what point do the Rabbis AGREE with R' Meir?
A. That piles of stones remain impure forever
B. That examination can never be trusted
C. That all three women are impure when all examinations come up clean
D. That a raven cannot remove a corpse
Correct: C — The Rabbis agree with R' Meir about the blood case: since the blood must have come from one of the women and cannot have been removed by an animal, all three remain impure even after clean examinations.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda, how can one resolve a garment with lost sha'atnez?
A. Burn the garment
B. Unravel and re-weave it
C. Dye it — wool and linen absorb dye differently, revealing the foreign thread
D. Soak it in water and check for floating threads
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda teaches that dyeing the garment will reveal any foreign thread because wool and linen absorb dye differently. If no difference is found, the thread presumably fell out.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does R' Yoḥanan derive from Psalms 88:6 ('Set apart [ḥofshi] among the dead')?
A. The dead will be resurrected wearing their burial shrouds
B. Once a person dies, they are free from the mitzvot
C. The dead should be buried in white garments only
D. Sha'atnez is permitted for all mourners
Correct: B — R' Yoḥanan interprets ḥofshi (free/set apart) to mean that death liberates a person from all commandments — therefore burying someone in sha'atnez shrouds is permitted.
