Deriving the Laws of Zava from Leviticus 15:25

Leviticus 15:25
"If a woman has a discharge of her ziva blood for many days not in the time of her menstruation..."
↓
A woman becomes a zava only if she bleeds close to but not during her days of menstruation
↓
The verse extends zava status through day 11 after menstruation — but not the 12th day
↓
Lesser Zava (1–2 days)
Counts one clean day per day of bleeding
Greater Zava (3 days)
Must count seven clean days
Key Verse "Or if she has a discharge beyond the time of her menstruation" — this phrase extends zava status to days separated from menstruation, up to and including the 11th day.

Step-by-Step Derivation of the Ziva Window

📖 Verse establishes zava status for bleeding close to menstruation (day after)
↓
📏 "Beyond the time" extends to one day separated from menstruation
↓
🔢 A kal va-chomer extends to days 4–10: each is fit for counting and fit for ziva
↓
1️⃣1️⃣ "Not in the time of her menstruation" includes the 11th day
↓
🚫 The 12th day is excluded — it is no longer within her days of ziva
Logic The 11th day is included because it is still fit for counting clean days after bleeding that ended on day 4. The 12th day fails this test.

Greater Zava vs. Lesser Zava — Days of Bleeding

Woman bleeds during her 11 days of ziva
↓
1 Day of Bleeding
"All the days" — even one day creates impurity
Must count 1 clean day
2 Consecutive Days
"Days" (plural) — includes two days
Must count 1 clean day per day
3 Consecutive Days
Full zava gedola status
Must count 7 clean days
Derivation The verse "all the days of the issue of her ziva she shall be as in the days of her menstruation: she is impure" — "days" = 2, "all" = even 1.

Zava vs. Zav — Impurity Through Intercourse

Zava (זבה)

  • Becomes impure through 3 consecutive days of bleeding
  • Does NOT become impure through 3 sightings in one day
  • ✅ Renders a man who has intercourse with her impure
  • Derived from: "She shall be impure" (היא טמאה)

Zav (זב)

  • Becomes impure through 3 sightings even on a single day
  • More stringent path to impurity than a zava
  • ❌ Does NOT render a woman he has intercourse with impure
  • "She" — only she (zava) transmits, not he (zav)
Refuted Kal Va-Chomer One might argue: if a zava (less stringent) transmits impurity through intercourse, surely a zav (more stringent) should too! The verse "she shall be impure" refutes this — only the zava transmits this way.

The Failed A Fortiori Argument

Questioner
A zava doesn't become impure through 3 sightings in one day, yet she transmits impurity through intercourse...
Questioner
A zav becomes impure even through 3 sightings in one day — surely he should also transmit impurity through intercourse!
Torah
"היא טמאה" — She is impure. The emphasis on 'she' teaches: a zava renders impure through intercourse, but a zav does not.
Baraita
This is a gezeirat ha-katuv — a Scriptural decree that overrides the logical kal va-chomer.
Principle A Scriptural exclusion (mi'ut) can override even a valid kal va-chomer inference.
