Ḥalla can be Torah-level while teruma is rabbinic — ḥalla applied during conquest years

Can Teruma Nullify the Ḥalla Obligation?

R' Meir & R' Yehuda
Dough mixed with teruma is still obligated in ḥalla — the teruma is nullified by the majority of non-sacred produce.
R' Yosei & R' Shimon
The dough is exempt from ḥalla — ḥalla doesn't apply to teruma, and the teruma mixture exempts the whole dough.
The Gemara
If R' Yosei holds teruma today is rabbinic, then rabbinic teruma can abrogate rabbinic ḥalla. But can rabbinic teruma abrogate Torah-level ḥalla?
The Gemara (rejects)
Perhaps R' Yosei holds teruma is Torah-level while ḥalla is rabbinic — so Torah-level teruma easily overrides rabbinic ḥalla.
PRINCIPLE A rabbinic obligation can potentially be abrogated by another rabbinic-level status, but a Torah obligation cannot easily be overridden by a rabbinic status.

Ḥalla vs. Teruma: Torah or Rabbinic Today?

Sages of Rav's Academy

  • Teruma today: Rabbinic
  • Ḥalla today: Torah-level
  • Reason: During the 14 years of conquest & division, ḥalla applied but teruma did not
  • Ḥalla preceded teruma historically

Rav Huna b. R' Yehoshua

  • Teruma today: Torah-level
  • Ḥalla today: Rabbinic
  • Reason: 'When you come' = when all of you come, not some
  • Ezra's return was partial — not all Jews ascended
KEY VERSE Numbers 15:18–20 — 'When you come into the land… from the first of your dough you shall set apart a cake.' The Gemara derives: 'when ALL of you come, not when SOME of you come.'

What Are the Signs of Bogeret (Grown Womanhood)?

R' Yosei HaGelili
When a fold appears below the breast — the breast has grown enough to create a crease.
R' Akiva
When the breasts sag onto the chest — indicating full development.
Ben Azzai
When the areola darkens at the tip of the breast.
R' Yosei
When one places a hand on the nipple, it depresses and is slow to return — sufficient breast tissue has developed.
R' Yehuda HaNasi
The halakha is stringent according to ALL opinions — whichever sign appears first establishes bogeret status.
HALAKHA R' Shimon ben Yoḥai adds: The three stages visible above (breast development) correspond to three stages below (pubic hair development).

What Makes a 'Tyrian Courtyard' for Tithing?

Does produce in this courtyard require tithing?
↓
R' Shimon / R' Yoḥanan
Vessels are safe inside (like Tyre's guarded courtyards)
R' Akiva
Has a permanent watchman who locks/unlocks — not just any resident
↓
R' Neḥemya
Hidden from outsiders — one isn't ashamed to eat there
R' Yosei
Outsiders cannot freely enter — residents would challenge them
R' Yehuda
Two nested courtyards — only the inner one obligates tithing
↓
R' Yehuda HaNasi: Be stringent per all opinions — if ANY definition is met, tithing is required
CONNECTION Rav Pappa and Rav Ḥinnana disagree whether R' Yehuda HaNasi's 'stringent per all' ruling was stated about signs of maturity or about the Tyrian courtyard — both apply the same principle.
