Tissue Discharge: When Is a Woman Impure?

Rabbis
If a woman discharges an amorphous piece of tissue with blood, she is impure. Without blood — she is pure.
R' Yehuda
Whether blood emerged or not, she is impure — there was certainly undetected blood.
Shmuel
R' Yehuda only deems impure when the tissue resembles one of the four impure blood colors.
R' Yoḥanan
All agree on the four colors! They only disagree when the tissue is lost and the color is unknown — R' Yehuda follows the majority.
Rav Hoshaya
A baraita from Neharde'a lists red, black, green, and white tissue — R' Yehuda deems impure in all cases!
Core Issue Is opening of the womb possible without a discharge of blood? This underlying question drives the entire dispute.

Woman Discharges Amorphous Tissue

↓
Blood emerges with it
She is impure (all agree)
No blood emerges
Dispute: Rabbis say pure, R' Yehuda says impure
R' Yehuda's Reasoning There was certainly undetected blood that emerged with the flesh — opening of the womb is impossible without blood.

Shmuel vs. R' Yoḥanan: Scope of the Dispute

Shmuel

  • R' Yehuda only deems impure when tissue has appearance of 4 impure blood colors
  • If tissue looks like other colors → even R' Yehuda agrees she is pure
  • Dispute is about the status of the tissue itself

R' Yoḥanan

  • If tissue matches 4 colors → all agree impure
  • If tissue matches other colors → all agree pure
  • Dispute is only when tissue is lost and color unknown
  • R' Yehuda: follow the majority (most tissue = impure color)
Challenge Rav Hoshaya's baraita from Neharde'a poses difficulties to both positions — R' Yehuda deems impure even for green and white tissue.

Mishna: Discharge Resembling Small Objects

🐚 Woman discharges item like a shell, hair, soil, or mosquito
↓
🔴 Item is red in color
↓
💧 Cast item into water
↓
✅ If it dissolved → it was blood → she is impure
↓
❌ If it did not dissolve → she is pure
Note Items resembling fish, grasshoppers, or creeping animals follow the standard rule: impure only if blood emerges with them.

Opening the Womb Without Blood

Rabbis
Opening of the womb is possible without blood discharge. Therefore, tissue alone does not make her impure.
R' Yehuda
Opening of the womb is impossible without blood. Therefore, tissue always implies blood.
Tanna Kamma
If she discharged and we don't know what — she brings an offering but it is not eaten (uncertain status).
R' Yehoshua
She brings an offering and it is eaten — she is certainly either a yoledet or a zavah.
Connection Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak says the dispute between R' Yehoshua and the Tanna Kamma parallels R' Yehuda vs. the Rabbis in our Mishna.
