QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara require a simple Tiberian cup for examining blood that looks like diluted wine?
A. The cup is made of thinner material, making it more transparent so redness is more noticeable
B. The cup is made of a special red clay that enhances the blood color
C. The cup was consecrated by the Sanhedrin for ritual use
D. The cup's larger size allows for more accurate volume measurement
Correct: A — Abaye explains that a Tiberian cup uses less material (100 dinars for a 2-log cup instead of 200), making it thinner and more transparent, so the wine's redness is more visible through the cup wall.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the fundamental disagreement between Rabbi Yehuda and the Rabbis regarding tissue discharge without blood?
A. Whether the tissue itself can be considered blood
B. Whether opening of the womb is possible without a discharge of blood
C. Whether green and white tissue can ever be impure
D. Whether a Sage must examine the tissue before ruling
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that the core dispute is whether opening of the womb is possible without blood. R' Yehuda says it is impossible (so blood must have been present), while the Rabbis say it is possible.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rav Hoshaya's baraita from Neharde'a challenge Shmuel's explanation of the dispute?
A. It shows R' Yehuda deems impure even for green and white tissue, not only the four impure blood colors
B. It shows the Rabbis agree with R' Yehuda in all cases
C. It introduces a fifth color of impure blood
D. It proves that tissue can never render a woman impure
Correct: A — Shmuel said R' Yehuda only deems impure when the tissue matches the four impure blood colors. But the baraita shows R' Yehuda disagrees with the Rabbis even regarding green and white tissue, contradicting Shmuel's limitation.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Sumakhos say about examining tissue, and how does it differ from the Rabbis' position?
A. A Sage should tear the tissue open; blood found inside renders impure — the Rabbis only count external blood
B. The tissue should be dissolved in water; Sumakhos requires hot water while the Rabbis require cold
C. The tissue should be examined by two Sages; the Rabbis require only one
D. Sumakhos says all tissue is impure regardless of examination
Correct: A — Sumakhos (in the name of R' Meir) says to tear the tissue open — if blood is found inside, the woman is impure. The Rabbis only deem impure when blood emerges externally with the tissue, not when found inside.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Meir, if a woman discharges a fetus in animal form and its sex is unknown, what are the practical consequences?
A. She is prohibited from intercourse for 14 days, then permitted until day 40, but cannot enter the Temple until day 80
B. She is impure for 7 days and then pure for 33 days only
C. She is permanently impure until the next examination
D. She follows only the female stringencies — 14 days impurity plus 66 days purity
Correct: A — When the sex is unknown, she observes the stringencies of both: 14 days impurity (female stringency), but permitted intercourse after day 40 (male leniency: 7+33), and cannot enter the Temple until 80 days (female stringency).
