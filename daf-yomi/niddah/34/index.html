Impurity of Gentile Blood & Emissions

Beit Shammai
Blood of a gentile woman and blood of a leper during days of purity are ritually pure
Beit Hillel
Gentile woman's blood is like her saliva and urine — impure while moist. Leper's blood during purity days is also impure while moist
Gemara
Why do Beit Shammai not accept the decree that gentiles are like zavim?
Beit Shammai
That decree applies only to males. For females, we need a conspicuous marker to prevent confusion with Torah-level impurity
Rava
Gentile man's ziva is impure (even per Beit Shammai). His semen is pure (even per Beit Hillel) — it serves as the conspicuous marker
Core Tension The Sages must balance extending impurity decrees to gentiles while maintaining clear distinctions so people don't confuse rabbinic and Torah-level impurity

Mishna: Blood of Gentile Women

Beit Shammai

  • Blood of gentile menstruant/zava — pure
  • Blood of leper in days of purity — pure
  • Decree about gentiles as zavim applies only to males
  • No decree on female blood to avoid confusion with Torah impurity

Beit Hillel

  • Blood of gentile woman — impure while moist
  • Blood of leper in days of purity — impure while moist
  • Status is like her saliva and urine
  • Rabbinic impurity applies to gentile women's emissions
Key Point Both schools agree that by Torah law, gentile women do not have niddah or ziva impurity — the dispute is about the scope of the rabbinic decree

Why Beit Shammai Exempt Gentile Women's Blood

Should gentile women's blood impart impurity by rabbinic decree?
If impure moist & dry
People will think it's Torah-level impurity and burn teruma that contacts it
If impure only moist
People will wrongly think Jewish women's blood is also impure only when moist
↓
Therefore: gentile women's blood imparts no impurity at all — this is the conspicuous marker (heker)
Principle Beit Shammai require a conspicuous marker (heker) — an obvious distinction between rabbinic and Torah-level impurity to prevent errors with teruma

Rava: Gentile Male Emissions

Rava
Ziva of a gentile man is impure — even per Beit Shammai
Rava
Because his semen (which is pure) serves as the conspicuous marker distinguishing rabbinic from Torah impurity
Rava
Semen of a gentile is pure — even per Beit Hillel
Rava
The Sages needed his semen to remain pure to serve as the heker for his ziva being only rabbinic
Baraita
Semen of a Jew is impure wherever found — even in a gentile woman's womb. Semen of a gentile is pure wherever found — even in a Jewish woman's womb
Proof The baraita confirms that gentile semen is pure even by rabbinic law, since it contrasts it with impure urine in the same context

Rav Pappa's Unresolved Dilemma

תיקו
Teiku What about semen of a Jew in a gentile woman's womb after three days? Within three days it is impure (per baraita). But after three days: does semen become foul in a gentile womb as it does in a Jewish womb? Jewish women's bodies are hot because they are concerned about mitzvot. But gentiles eat sheratzim (creeping animals), which also makes their bodies hot. The dilemma stands unresolved.
