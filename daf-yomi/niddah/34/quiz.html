Niddah 34 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why do Beit Shammai rule that gentile women's blood does not impart impurity at all?
A. Because gentile women have no biological menstrual cycle
B. To serve as a conspicuous marker distinguishing rabbinic from Torah impurity
C. Because the Torah explicitly exempts gentile women
D. Because Beit Shammai reject all rabbinic decrees about gentiles
Correct: B — Beit Shammai require a heker (conspicuous marker) — making gentile women's blood completely pure ensures people won't confuse rabbinic impurity with Torah-level impurity and accidentally burn teruma
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava, what is the impurity status of a gentile man's semen?
A. Impure by Torah law
B. Impure by rabbinic decree when moist
C. Pure — even according to Beit Hillel
D. Impure by rabbinic decree whether moist or dry
Correct: C — Rava rules that gentile semen is pure even according to Beit Hillel, because the Sages needed it to remain pure as a conspicuous marker for the rabbinic impurity of gentile ziva
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rav Pappa's unresolved dilemma (teiku)?
A. Whether gentile women have niddah impurity by Torah law
B. Whether semen of a Jew in a gentile womb becomes foul after three days
C. Whether a female leper's blood is impure during days of purity
D. Whether Beit Shammai's decree applies to convert women
Correct: B — Rav Pappa asks whether a gentile woman's body heat causes Jewish semen to become foul after three days as it does in a Jewish womb. The Gemara cannot resolve whether gentiles' diet of creeping animals makes their bodies equally hot
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How do Beit Hillel derive that a female leper's blood during days of purity is impure?
A. From a gezeira shava linking leper to niddah
B. From a kal va'chomer from a male leper
C. By applying 'if it is not needed for its own matter' (im eino inyan) from Leviticus 15:33
D. From an explicit verse in Leviticus 13
Correct: C — Beit Hillel argue that 'whether it be a male' is unnecessary for male lepers (derivable a fortiori from females), so the extra phrase is applied to teach that female leper's blood during purity days is impure
QUESTION 5 OF 5
On what case do Beit Shammai concede to Beit Hillel regarding post-birth blood?
A. A woman who gave birth to twins
B. A woman who gave birth as a zava (yoledet b'zov)
C. A woman who immersed in a mikveh before her days ended
D. A woman whose days of impurity ended on Shabbat
Correct: B — Beit Shammai concede that when a woman gives birth as a zava and must count seven clean days, any blood she sees during those days imparts impurity whether moist or dry
