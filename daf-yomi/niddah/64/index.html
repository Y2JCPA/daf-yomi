Establishing Cycles & Attributing Blood

Rav
A woman who sees blood on the 15th, 16th, 17th of consecutive months has established a veset (cycle) of month + 1 day — the first sighting counts toward the pattern.
Shmuel
The cycle is not established until she skips a day three times. The first sighting doesn't count because she hadn't yet skipped.
Gemara
Both agree with Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel that chazakah requires 3 occurrences — they disagree whether the first sighting counts as part of the pattern.
KEY PRINCIPLE A fixed menstrual cycle (veset kavua) requires establishing a consistent pattern — the debate is whether the starting point of the pattern counts toward the required repetitions.

Establishing a Skipping Cycle (Veset HaDilug)

Rav
She saw blood on the 15th, then 16th, then 17th of consecutive months → she has established a veset of month + 1 day.
Shmuel
Not yet established! She must skip three times — she needs a fourth sighting (e.g., the 18th) to confirm the pattern.
Rav
The 15th counts as the first of the pattern — so 15th → 16th → 17th = three data points, establishing the cycle.
Shmuel
The 15th doesn't count because she didn't arrive at it by skipping from a 14th — so only two skips have occurred.
RESOLUTION Both agree with Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel that chazakah requires 3 times — the dispute is whether the initial sighting is part of the count.

Displacing an Established Veset

Woman accustomed to see blood on the 15th of each month
↓
She deviates and sees on the 16th instead
↓
15th
Still prohibited — old veset not yet displaced
16th
Also prohibited — new potential veset day
↓
She deviates again: sees on the 17th, then 18th
↓
After 3 deviations: old veset (15th) is displaced, new veset of month+1 day is established on the 19th
BARAITA RULING When a woman has an existing established cycle, displacing it requires 3 deviations — even Rav agrees, since the original date was already fixed.

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi vs. Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel — Chazakah

R' Yehuda HaNasi

  • Chazakah established after 2 occurrences
  • Woman whose 2 husbands died may not marry a 3rd
  • Pattern presumed dangerous after twice

Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel

  • Chazakah established after 3 occurrences
  • She may marry a 3rd husband; only after his death is she forbidden a 4th
  • Both Rav and Shmuel agree with this position
NOTE The Gemara initially suggests Rav follows R' Yehuda HaNasi, but rejects this — both Rav and Shmuel follow Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel's 3-occurrence rule.

Women Compared to Grapevines

דורקטי = דור קטוע
MISHNA Women's hymen blood is like wine from grapevines: some produce red, some black; some in abundance, some meagerly. R' Yehuda says: A vine without grapes is a durkati (dry vine) — likewise, a woman who never bleeds is unable to bear children. R' Ḥiyya explains: durkati = dor katua (truncated generation). Just as leaven is good for dough, so too blood is good for a woman.

CaseBeit ShammaiBeit Hillel
Young girl — not yet reached puberty4 nights attributed to hymenUntil the wound heals
Young woman — reached puberty, hasn't yet menstruatedFirst night onlyUntil end of Shabbat (4 nights, Wed–Shabbat)
Young woman — saw blood before marriageOnly the first act of intercourse (mitzvah)All intercourse throughout the first night
CLARIFICATION Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak: The first case (pre-puberty girl) applies even if she has previously seen menstrual blood — as inferred from the mishna's structure.
