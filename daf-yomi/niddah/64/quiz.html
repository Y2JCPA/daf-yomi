QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav, why is a veset established after seeing blood on the 15th, 16th, and 17th of consecutive months?
A. The 15th counts as the first occurrence, giving 3 data points for the pattern
B. Two intervals are always sufficient regardless of circumstances
C. Rav follows Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi that 2 occurrences suffice
D. The pattern of skipping only needs to happen twice
Correct: A — Rav holds that the initial 15th sighting counts as the first element of the pattern, so 15th→16th→17th provides 3 occurrences (2 skips), satisfying even Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel's requirement.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the Gemara mean by 'durkati' (דורקטי)?
A. A woman who bleeds excessively
B. A truncated generation (dor katua) — a woman unable to bear children
C. A type of grapevine that produces red wine
D. A woman whose cycle has not yet been established
Correct: B — R' Ḥiyya explains durkati as 'dor katua' — a truncated generation. Just as a vine without grapes is barren, a woman who never bleeds cannot give birth.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Beit Hillel, how long is blood attributed to the torn hymen for a pre-pubescent girl?
A. Four nights only
B. The first night only
C. Until the wound heals
D. Until the end of Shabbat
Correct: C — Beit Hillel says that for a young girl who has not reached puberty, blood is attributed to the hymen until the wound heals — with no fixed time limit.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In Rav's 'noḥeret' test, what indicates that the hymen wound has healed?
A. She sees blood when sitting but not when standing
B. She no longer sees blood at all
C. She inconsistently sees blood regardless of sitting surface or position
D. She sees blood only when sitting on cushions
Correct: C — If she sometimes sees blood on all surfaces and sometimes doesn't — regardless of strain — the wound has healed, and the blood is now menstrual blood.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the dispute about daytime intercourse, what does Levi hold?
A. Daytime intercourse doesn't count at all
B. Daytime intercourse counts toward the 4-night allowance, consuming her permitted occasions
C. Daytime intercourse extends the total allowance to 8 occasions
D. Daytime intercourse is always prohibited
Correct: B — Levi holds that daytime intercourse counts toward the total, so if she had relations 2 days and 2 nights, her 4 permitted occasions are used up — she has 'lost her nights.'
