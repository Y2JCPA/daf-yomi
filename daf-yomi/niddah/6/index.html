Retroactive Impurity: What Does It Affect?

Woman sees menstrual blood
↓
Impurity is retroactive for 24 hours
↓
Definitely Impure
Beds, chairs, food, drink, sealed earthenware vessels
Disputed
Does retroactive impurity apply to teruma or only kodashim?
Core Issue The Gemara extensively debates Rav Huna's position that the 24-hour retroactive impurity applies only to sacrificial food, not teruma, and attempts to refute him from multiple baraitot.

Seeing Blood vs. Finding a Stain

Sees Menstrual Blood

  • Retroactive impurity for 24 hours
  • Renders impure: beds, chairs, food, drink, sealed earthenware
  • Does NOT disrupt count of menstruation/ziva periods
  • Does NOT retroactively render impure a man who had intercourse (Rabbis)
  • R' Akiva: DOES render impure a man who had intercourse
  • Teruma she touched: status suspended — not eaten, not burned

Finds a Blood Stain

  • Retroactive impurity to last examination
  • Renders impure: food, drink, beds, chairs, sealed earthenware
  • DOES disrupt her count of 11 ziva days
  • DOES retroactively render impure a man who had intercourse
  • Counts 7 days from time she saw the blood
  • Teruma she touched: status suspended — not eaten, not burned
Baraita This baraita supports Rava's position that beds and chairs are included in the list of items rendered impure retroactively.

Rava's Kal VaChomer: Beds & Chairs

Gemara
Why was the earthenware vessel halakha more obvious to Rava than beds and chairs?
Rava
If an earthenware vessel with tzamid patil — normally protected from tum'ah — is rendered impure by her movement, then kal vachomer beds and chairs should be too!
Gemara
But if you knew the baraita, it already states both! Why do you need the kal vachomer?
Rava
From the baraita alone, one might say beds and chairs transmit impurity to person or garments they touch, but not that a person then transmits to his garments. The kal vachomer proves the stronger level.
Key Insight Rava needs the a fortiori argument to establish the degree of impurity transmission from beds/chairs — not just contact impurity, but that a person who touches them renders his garments impure too.

Rav Huna's Position & Challenges

↓
Why isn't this listed in Mishnah Chagigah 20b among stringencies of kodashim over teruma?
↓
That mishnah lists only impurities with a Torah-law connection; retroactive impurity is rabbinic
↓
Baraita says she renders impure food and drink — doesn't that include teruma?
↓
Rav Huna: No — it refers only to sacrificial food
Pattern The Gemara brings a series of challenges to Rav Huna, each time forcing him to reinterpret sources as referring to kodashim rather than teruma.

R' Yehuda's Examination After Eating Teruma

R' Yehuda
Women of priestly families must examine themselves even after concluding their teruma meal.
Gemara
Why? What was, was — if she was impure while eating, what good is examining now?
Rav Chisda
To protect the remaining teruma before her from retroactive impurity.
Gemara
This implies retroactive impurity DOES apply to teruma — refuting Rav Huna!
Rav Huna
No — R' Yehuda means she examines to determine whether to burn the remaining teruma as definitely impure, versus merely suspending it.
Rav Huna's Defense According to Rav Huna, the examination isn't about retroactive impurity affecting teruma, but about establishing certainty for the obligation to burn it.
