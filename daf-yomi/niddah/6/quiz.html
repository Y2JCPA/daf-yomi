QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the baraita, what is the key difference between seeing menstrual blood and finding a blood stain regarding a man who had intercourse?
A. Seeing blood retroactively renders him impure; a stain does not
B. A stain retroactively renders him impure; seeing blood does not (per the Rabbis)
C. Both retroactively render him impure
D. Neither retroactively renders him impure
Correct: B — The baraita teaches that finding a blood stain renders impure a man who had intercourse retroactively, while seeing blood does not (according to the Rabbis; R' Akiva disagrees). This is because stain-impurity extends back to the last examination, with greater uncertainty.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rava need a kal vachomer argument if the baraita already lists beds and chairs alongside earthenware vessels?
A. To prove beds and chairs transmit impurity at all
B. To prove the stronger level — that a person who touches them then renders his garments impure
C. To establish that the baraita is authoritative
D. To show earthenware vessels are more stringent than beds
Correct: B — Without the kal vachomer, one might interpret the baraita as saying beds and chairs transmit impurity to a person but not to the degree that the person then transmits impurity to his garments. The a fortiori from earthenware vessels proves the stronger transmission.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rav Huna explain why his distinction (kodashim vs. teruma) isn't listed in Mishnah Chagigah?
A. It was a later teaching unknown to the Mishnah's author
B. The Mishnah only lists Torah-law impurities, and retroactive impurity is rabbinic
C. The Mishnah is incomplete and missing many cases
D. Rav Huna disagrees with the Mishnah
Correct: B — Rav Huna argues that Mishnah Chagigah lists only types of impurity connected to Torah law (derara d'oraita), but retroactive impurity of a menstruating woman is rabbinic in nature, so it wouldn't be included.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the incident with Rabban Gamliel's maidservant baking bread, how does the Gemara reconcile this with Rav Huna's view?
A. The bread was actually chulin, not teruma
B. The 'teruma' was teruma of the thanks-offering loaves — which is kodashim
C. Rabban Gamliel disagrees with Rav Huna
D. The incident occurred before the Temple was destroyed
Correct: B — The Gemara reinterprets 'teruma bread' as the teruma portion of thanks-offering loaves (sacrificial food), where the dough was separated during kneading. This keeps the case within kodashim, preserving Rav Huna's distinction.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What was Ulla's testimony about Chaverim in the Galilee, and why is it relevant?
A. They observed teruma purity, proving teruma was common after the Temple's destruction
B. They purified wine and oil to kodashim standards, hoping the Temple would be rebuilt
C. They rejected retroactive impurity entirely
D. They followed Rabbi Eliezer's lenient opinion exclusively
Correct: B — Ulla testified that Chaverim in the Galilee maintained sacrificial-food purity standards for their wine and oil, anticipating the Temple's rebuilding. This explains how a case involving kodashim-level purity could arise in the post-Temple era of Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, supporting Rav Huna's reinterpretation.
