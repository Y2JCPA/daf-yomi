What Does the Mishna's First Clause Teach?

Rav Yehuda
The mishna teaches that during the 11 days of ziva, a woman does not require examination at all — she has a presumption of purity.
Gemara
But the latter clause says 'if she sat and did not examine, she is pure' — implying examination is required l'chatchila!
Rav Yehuda
The latter clause refers to days of menstruation, not ziva. During menstruation she requires examination, but if she didn't examine — still pure.
Rav Chisda
The first clause is needed for R' Meir's opinion: a woman without a fixed cycle is forbidden to her husband during menstruation days, but permitted during ziva days.
Gemara
If R' Meir permits intercourse during ziva, why does he say her husband must divorce her?
Rav Chisda
Lest the matter lead to failure during the days of menstruation — they might come to have intercourse at the wrong time.
Resolution Rava offers a third explanation: the mishna teaches that during ziva days, a woman who bleeds does not impart retroactive impurity for 24 hours, since she had a presumption of purity.

R' Meir: Fear Drives Away Blood

Not in Hiding

  • Veset time arrives
  • She does not examine herself
  • She is presumed IMPURE
  • Blood is expected at cycle time

In Hiding (Danger)

  • Veset time arrives
  • She does not examine herself
  • She is presumed PURE
  • Fear suppresses menstruation
  • R' Meir: אימה מסלקת את הדמים
R' Meir's Principle Psychological stress (fear/anxiety) has a physiological effect — it can delay or suppress menstruation. Therefore a woman in hiding has no presumption of bleeding.

The Chicken Analogy 🐔

תרנגולת זו שרגילה להטיל ביום ולדלג ביום
Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua's Proof A chicken that normally lays an egg every other day (day 1 ✓, day 2 ✗, day 3 ✓, day 4 ✗) deviates and skips two days, then lays on day 6.

When it resumes its routine, does it follow the original schedule (lay on day 7, skip day 8)? Or the new rhythm (skip day 7, lay on day 8)?

Obviously it follows the order ahead of it — the routine restarts from the deviation point. Likewise, a woman who deviates from her cycle counts her new days from when she actually experienced bleeding.
