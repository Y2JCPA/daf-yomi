Does a Deformed Corpse Impart Ohel Impurity?

R' Yochanan
A deformed corpse still imparts tumat ohel (impurity under the same roof).
Reish Lakish
Once the corpse loses its form, it no longer imparts ohel impurity.
Ravina
R' Yochanan follows Rabbi Eliezer, who says ashes of burned corpses impart ohel impurity at a quarter-kav.
Gemara
The proof from the mishna about openings is inconclusive — it can be read either way.
Core Issue If a corpse is deformed or burned, does it retain its capacity to spread impurity through a shared roof (ohel)?

How Can a Burned Corpse Retain Its Form?

Abaye

  • Burned on a hard leather spread (קטבלא)
  • Leather doesn't burn
  • Corpse retains its shape on the surface

Rava

  • Burned on a marble slate (אפודרים)
  • Marble resists fire
  • Shape preserved by the stone base

Ravina

  • Corpse was charred
  • Not fully reduced to ashes
  • Form remains despite burning
Practical Point Each Amora explains a realistic scenario where fire doesn't destroy the corpse's shape — needed for R' Eliezer's ruling on ohel impurity of burned remains.

Woman Discharges a Shaped Hand or Foot

Woman discharges a recognizable hand or foot with discernible fingers/toes
↓
Is this from a fully formed fetus?
↓
Presumption
Most women give birth to fully formed fetuses → hand/foot came from a whole fetus
Concern
Maybe from a sealed/deficient body? → Rejected based on majority
↓
Mother is impure as a yoledet (woman after childbirth)
↓
But: Rav Ḥisda & Rabba b"R Huna — she gets days of impurity but not days of purity
Why No Purity Days? Perhaps the majority of the fetus emerged long before this limb — meaning the period of purity has already passed. Since we don't know when the rest emerged, we cannot grant purity days.

Fetus Extends Its Hand — Is This Childbirth?

Rav Huna
If a fetus extends its hand from the womb and pulls it back, the mother is impure as a yoledet. Proof: Tamar's son Zerach (Genesis 38:28–29).
Rav Yehuda
Objection! A baraita says: if a fetus extends its hand, the mother need not be concerned about childbirth impurity!
Rav Naḥman
Rav Huna himself explained: she has childbirth impurity but the fetus does not have firstborn status, since partial emergence isn't full birth.
Gemara
The baraita means the fetus extending a hand doesn't affect firstborn status — not that the mother is pure.
Biblical Source Genesis 38:28–29 — Zerach put out his hand during Tamar's labor, then drew it back and Peretz was born first. The hand's emergence was treated as a birth event.
