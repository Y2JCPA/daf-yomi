Niddah 31 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Baraita, in which compartment of the womb does the fetus reside during the first trimester?
A. Upper compartment
B. Middle compartment
C. Lower compartment
D. It floats freely throughout
Correct: C — The Baraita teaches that during the first three months the fetus resides in the lower compartment, then middle, then upper — ascending as it grows.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why are labor pains greater when giving birth to a female, according to the Gemara?
A. A female fetus is larger
B. A female fetus must turn its face around before birth
C. The mother's body resists female births
D. Female fetuses descend faster
Correct: B — A female fetus emerges facing upward (the way a female engages in intercourse), so it must turn its face around before birth, causing additional pain. A male fetus already faces downward.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does the father contribute to the creation of a person, according to the Baraita of Three Partners?
A. Skin, flesh, and hair
B. Spirit, soul, and wisdom
C. Bones, sinews, nails, brain, and white of the eye
D. Blood, organs, and hearing
Correct: C — The father emits white seed, from which bones, sinews, nails, brain, and the white of the eye are formed. The mother's red seed forms skin, flesh, hair, and the black of the eye.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Shimon ben Yoḥai, why is circumcision performed on the eighth day?
A. It takes 8 days for the baby to be strong enough
B. So that both parents can celebrate — the mother's 7-day impurity period will have ended
C. The number 8 symbolizes transcendence
D. To correspond to the 8 days of Sukkot
Correct: B — R' Shimon ben Yoḥai explains that after giving birth to a male, the mother is impure for 7 days. Circumcision is on day 8 so that the parents are permitted to each other and can fully rejoice at the celebration.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does R' Ḥinnana bar Pappa illustrate the miraculous nature of pregnancy?
A. By comparing it to planting a seed in fertile soil
B. By noting that a sealed flask may lose its contents — yet God preserves a fetus in an open, downward-facing womb
C. By describing how angels guard the fetus
D. By comparing the womb to the Holy of Holies
Correct: B — R' Ḥinnana bar Pappa contrasts human limitations — even a sealed, upward-facing flask may lose its contents — with God's ability to preserve a fetus in an open womb facing downward. Additionally, unlike a scale where heavier items descend, the heavier the fetus grows, the more it ascends.
