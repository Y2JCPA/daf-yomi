How Much Missing Body = Non-Viable?

R' Zakkai
If a fetus is missing legs from above the knee, the mother is pure — a tereifa cannot survive 12 months.
R' Yannai
Only if missing up to the orifices (lower openings) — a tereifa can survive 12 months.
R' Yochanan
In the name of R' Yosei ben Yehoshua: only if missing up to the navel. I disagree with R' Elazar's ruling on removed thighs.
Rav Pappa
This dispute is about missing parts from below upward. But if missing from above downward — even any amount of skull — the mother is pure.
Key Factor The underlying dispute: Can a tereifa (fatally wounded creature) survive beyond 12 months?

Mashed Face vs. Flat Face

R' Yochanan
A fetus with a mashed face (features partially discernible) — the mother is impure.
Reish Lakish
A mashed face — the mother is pure.
Rav Pappi
Correction: They agree a mashed face = impure. The dispute is about a completely flat face (no features at all).
Rav Pappi
Reverse the positions: R' Yochanan says flat face = pure; Reish Lakish says flat face = impure.
R' Ḥiyya
My sons ruled stringently on a flat face. I told them: Go deem pure what you deemed impure! Your stringency leads to leniency — 33 days of purity.
Stringency → Leniency Declaring the woman impure from birth also grants her 33 clean days — so ruling 'impure' can paradoxically be more lenient overall.

Two Backs & Two Spines: Rav vs. Shmuel

Rav

  • Woman: fetus is NOT viable offspring → mother is pure
  • Animal: fetus is FORBIDDEN for consumption
  • Derives from Rav Ḥanin bar Abba's teaching
  • Halakha follows Rav in ritual matters

Shmuel

  • Woman: fetus IS viable offspring → mother is impure
  • Animal: fetus is PERMITTED for consumption if found in womb
  • Only forbidden if it emerges into the airspace of the world
  • R' Yirmeya bar Abba tried to rule like Shmuel but was corrected
Ruling Rav Huna to R' Yirmeya: Follow Rav — the halakha is in accordance with Rav in ritual matters, whether lenient or stringent.
