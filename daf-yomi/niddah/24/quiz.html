Niddah 24 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the underlying dispute between R' Zakkai and R' Yannai about a fetus missing its lower legs?
A. Whether a tereifa can survive beyond 12 months
B. Whether legs are considered essential organs
C. Whether the fetus has a human form
D. Whether the mother felt labor pains
Correct: A — R' Zakkai holds a tereifa cannot survive 12 months, so even missing legs above the knee means non-viable. R' Yannai holds a tereifa can survive, so more must be missing before the fetus is considered non-viable.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did R' Ḥiyya tell his sons to reverse their ruling on the flat-faced fetus?
A. Because they lacked the authority to rule
B. Because their stringency (impure) paradoxically led to a leniency (33 days of purity)
C. Because a flat face is always considered viable
D. Because they forgot to consult a baraita
Correct: B — Declaring the woman impure from childbirth also grants her 33 days of pure blood — so the 'strict' ruling actually creates a lenient outcome. R' Ḥiyya recognized this and corrected them.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, what happens if a fetus is missing part of its body from above (the skull)?
A. The three-way dispute between R' Zakkai, R' Yannai, and R' Yochanan applies
B. The mother is impure only if the majority of the skull is missing
C. The mother is pure even if only a small amount of skull is missing
D. It depends on whether the fetus has a discernible face
Correct: C — Rav Pappa clarifies that the dispute about missing body parts applies only from below upward. If missing from above downward, even any amount of skull renders the mother pure.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the halakha follow Rav (not Shmuel) regarding a fetus with two backs and two spines?
A. Because Rav was older than Shmuel
B. Because the halakha follows Rav in ritual matters
C. Because a baraita explicitly supports Rav
D. Because Shmuel later retracted his opinion
Correct: B — Rav Huna states the established principle: the halakha follows Rav in ritual (issura) matters, whether his opinion leads to leniency or stringency.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does a fetus in the form of a lilith render the mother impure?
A. Because demons have special impurity status
B. Because it has a human face and is considered viable — it just has wings
C. Because any discharged fetus renders the mother impure
D. Because R' Yosei always rules stringently
Correct: B — Shmuel teaches that a lilith-form fetus has a human face and is viable offspring — the wings are simply an unusual feature. Since it could survive, the mother has birth impurity.
