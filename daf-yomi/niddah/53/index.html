Twilight Examinations & the Halakhot of Stains

R' Yehuda ben Agra (for R' Yosei)
We must be concerned she saw blood at twilight — counting as two sightings
Rabbanan
We are not concerned about the possibility of seeing at twilight
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
Both are correct: R' Yehuda ben Agra — where she did not examine; the Rabbis — where she did examine
Rava
The key distinction: examined during R' Yehuda's twilight but not during R' Yosei's twilight
Core Issue Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi harmonizes the dispute by tying it to whether the woman examined herself during the different twilight periods defined by R' Yehuda and R' Yosei.

Two Definitions of Twilight (Bein HaShemashot)

Rabbi Yehuda's Twilight

  • Time to walk half a mil before stars emerge
  • A longer, more extended period
  • Woman examined herself during this window
  • Status: transitional period between day and night

Rabbi Yosei's Twilight

  • A blink of an eye (כהרף עין) before stars emerge
  • An extremely brief moment
  • Woman did NOT examine during this window
  • Status: uncertain — possibly day or night
Key Point R' Yosei's twilight occurs at the very end of R' Yehuda's twilight — whether it is subsumed within or follows it is debated.

Rava Challenges the Study Hall

Sages in Rav's Study Hall
She examined herself during R' Yehuda's twilight but not her robe, and not during R' Yosei's twilight
Rava
Were her hands in her private parts the entire twilight? Perhaps she saw blood after removing them!
Sages
We meant a case where she placed her hands there for the entire twilight period of R' Yehuda
Rava
But the concern about R' Yosei's twilight remains — its uncertain status means possible second sighting
Rava's Precision Rava insists the examination must be continuous — any gap creates an opportunity for undetected blood.

Attributing a Stain to a Subsequent Sighting

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi

  • Stain attributed to sighting if blood seen within 24 hours
  • Counting of ziva days begins from stain discovery
  • More stringent for ziva: earlier start date
  • Admits: 'I ruin her situation'

R' Shimon ben Elazar

  • Stain attributed only if blood seen same day
  • If next day: stain is independent — ziva count from sighting day
  • More lenient for ziva: later start date
  • Rabbi says: 'He fixes her situation'
Why It Matters Since stain impurity is rabbinic (de-rabbanan), the lenient position is preferred — R' Shimon ben Elazar's approach results in fewer days of ziva.
