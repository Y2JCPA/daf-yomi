Niddah 69: Examinations, Clean Days & Corpse Impurity

Core Question: When a zav/zava examines on the 1st and 8th days, which days count?
↓
Rav
Same as 1st & 7th — examination at the beginning is sufficient
R' Chanina
We require examination on the last of 7 days — only 8th day counts
↓
Rav further teaches: Examination at the end alone is also sufficient
↓
Mishna: Corpses of impure people (zav, zava, metzora) transmit impurity until flesh decays
Key Principle Rav rules leniently on both ends: a single examination — whether at the start or the end of seven clean days — is sufficient.
3 / 9

Examining on the 1st & 8th Days

Gemara
According to R' Eliezer, examining on 1st and 7th days = pure in between. What if they examined on the 1st and 8th?
Rav
1st and 8th is the same as 1st and 7th. R' Eliezer requires only an examination at the beginning — no need for one on the last day.
R' Chanina
We require examination on both first and last of the seven days. Here the 8th day is not the last of the seven — so no days count.
Gemara
Objection to Rav: A baraita says the Sages agree that examining on 1st and 8th = only the 8th day counts!
Rav
Those 'Sages' are R' Yehoshua and R' Akiva, not R' Eliezer. R' Eliezer would say all seven days count by chezkat tahara.
Machloket The core dispute: Does R' Eliezer require examination on the last of the seven days, or only the first?

Zava Examines on Her 3rd Day

A zava performs her purity examination on her 3rd day of bleeding
↓
Does the 3rd day count toward her 7 clean days?
↓
Rav's Ruling
She counts apart from the 3rd day — the 3rd day itself does not count
Samaritan View
The day bleeding stops counts toward the 7 clean days
↓
Case: After seeing she was clean on day 3, she didn't examine again until day 7 — this is sufficient
Rav Sheshet Challenged Rav Yirmeya bar Abba: Did Rav rule like the Samaritans? No — Rav meant counting begins after the 3rd day.

Rav's Two Halakhot on Examination Timing

Halakha 1: Beginning Only

  • Examination on the 1st day is sufficient
  • No examination needed on the last day
  • Clean status established by chezkat tahara (presumption of purity)
  • Derived from the 1st & 8th day discussion

Halakha 2: End Only

  • Examination on the 7th day is sufficient
  • No examination needed on the 1st day
  • Even without establishing initial presumption
  • Derived from the zava who examines after day 3
Why Both? One might think only beginning-examination works (via presumption). Rav teaches that end-examination also works — even without an initial chazaka.

Must Counting Be Before the Court?

Gemara
The baraita about 15 immersions seems to prove the Rabbis don't require counting before the court (beit din).
R' Akiva
We do require seven days of counting before the court before immersion.
Rav Acha b. Rav Yosef
No proof! The baraita already needed revision. Perhaps the woman said: 'I counted clean days but I don't know how many.'
Gemara
If she counted at least one day, she shouldn't need the 8th immersion — it doesn't add up!
Gemara
Rather, she says: 'I don't know if I counted any clean days at all.' So she may need all 15.
Resolution The baraita is reinterpreted so it offers no conclusive proof about whether counting must occur before the court.
