The Pit Case: Layers of Uncertainty

A woman cast something resembling a nefel (non-viable newborn) into a pit
↓
Was it actually a nefel or just congealed blood?
Doubt #1
Might be congealed blood → no impurity
Doubt #2
Even if nefel, martens/hyenas may have dragged it away
↓
Double uncertainty (sfek sfeka) — does not override certainty of purity
Alternative View Some say martens and hyenas are so common there that they certainly dragged it away — making this two certainties, not uncertainty vs. certainty.

Is Veset Concern Torah Law or Rabbinic?

The Sages
Does the obligation to examine at one's veset (projected period) apply by Torah law or rabbinic law?
Rav Naḥman
Rav Huna said in Rav's name: A woman with a fixed cycle who missed her examination and later found blood — pure items from the veset time onward are impure, plus 24 hours retroactively.
Version 1
This stringency proves veset concern is Torah law.
Version 2
The impurity is only because she ultimately saw blood. If she didn't see blood — no concern. This proves veset is rabbinic.
Two Versions Rav Naḥman's statement is reported in two conflicting versions — one supporting Torah-level concern, one supporting rabbinic-level concern.

Rav vs. Shmuel: Missed Examination with Fixed Cycle

Rav

  • If later examination finds impurity → she is impure
  • If later examination finds purity → she is pure
  • Possibly holds veset concern is rabbinic
  • R' Zeira: Or she examined very close to the veset time

Shmuel

  • Even if later examination finds purity → she is impure
  • Reason: A woman's period comes at its usual time
  • Holds veset concern is Torah law
  • Blood was assumed present; later purity doesn't override
R' Naḥman bar Yitzḥak Confirms they disagree on the fundamental question: is veset concern Torah or rabbinic law?

Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel: Examination Cloths

Beit Shammai
A woman needs two cloths per act of intercourse (before and after), or must use a lamp to see.
Beit Hillel
Sufficient to examine with two cloths for the whole night — once before the first act and once after the last.
Beit Shammai
What if a mustard-seed drop of blood comes during the first act, and semen from the second act covers it?
Beit Hillel
Even by your system — what if blood appears and semen from that same act squashes and covers it?
Beit Shammai
A drop squashed after one act is not comparable to one squashed after two acts. Our concern is more reasonable.
R' Yehoshua
I see the statement of Beit Shammai as correct in this case.
His Students' Reaction "Our teacher, how you have weighed us down!" R' Yehoshua replied: Better I weigh you down in this world so your days in the World-to-Come will be lengthened.

Key Distinction The strict examination requirements apply specifically to women who handle ritually pure items (taharot), not to ordinary marital relations.
