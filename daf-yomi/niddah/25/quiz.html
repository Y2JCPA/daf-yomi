Niddah 25 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did R' Yehuda HaNasi reject R' Yosei's teaching that a sac full of flesh confers childbirth impurity?
A. Because R' Yosei was not a reliable authority
B. Because the blood ruling followed Sumakhos (a minority), making the flesh ruling also suspect
C. Because a sac full of flesh can never be an offspring
D. Because R' Yehuda HaNasi had a contradictory teaching from his own teachers
Correct: B — R' Yehuda HaNasi reasoned that since R' Yosei's ruling about blood followed the minority opinion of Sumakhos/R' Meir, the flesh ruling might similarly follow a rejected minority (R' Yehoshua), making the entire transmission unreliable.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What did the Gemara conclude about whether the dispute between R' Yehoshua and the Rabbis applies to turbid or clear amniotic fluid?
A. The dispute applies only to turbid fluid
B. The dispute applies only to clear fluid
C. The dispute applies to both equally
D. The question was left unresolved (teiku)
Correct: B — The Gemara proves from R' Yehoshua's verse about 'garments of skins' that his argument doesn't depend on turbidity. If the dispute were only about turbid fluid, a verse would be unnecessary — turbidity alone logically suggests a dissolved embryo.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman, why must the wooden sliver be moved from below to above when examining an embryo's sex?
A. Because the embryo is oriented head-down in the sac
B. Because moving side to side could give a false male reading due to the womb walls
C. Because the Torah requires examination in an upward direction
D. Because moving downward could damage the embryo
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman clarified that side-to-side movement is unreliable because the sides of the womb could interfere with the sliver, making it seem caught when it isn't due to a male organ but rather the womb walls.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Shmuel's distinction regarding a woman who discharges a gestational sac?
A. She is definitely pure in all cases
B. She is definitely impure in all cases
C. She must be concerned for impurity, but receives days of purity only if the embryo has hair
D. She is impure only if the amniotic fluid is turbid
Correct: C — Shmuel distinguished between stringency and leniency: the woman must be concerned she is impure (stringency of doubt), but the beneficial days of purity that follow childbirth impurity are only granted when the embryo has visibly grown hair.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does R' Yitzḥak add to the halakha that a sandal fetus requires the shape of a face?
A. The face must have all features — eyes, nose, and mouth
B. The face need not be in its proper location; even on the back of the head suffices
C. The face must be visible without magnification
D. Only the outline of a face is needed, not distinct features
Correct: B — R' Yitzḥak compares it to a person slapped so hard their face was pushed back — since the sandal fetus was mashed, the face may have been displaced to the back of the head, but it still counts as having a face.
