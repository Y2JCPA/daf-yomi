↓
Did tissue develop inside?
Yes — Flesh
R' Yehoshua: It is an offspring → childbirth impurity
Rabbis: Not an offspring → pure
No Tissue

Core Issue The daf examines when a discharged gestational sac confers the impurity of childbirth, debating the significance of tissue, fluid clarity, and embryo identification methods.

R' Yosei's Ruling on Gestational Sacs

Students
A woman discharges a gestational sac full of flesh — what is the halakha?
R' Yehuda HaNasi
I have not heard from my teachers the halakha in this case.
R' Yishmael b. R' Yosei
My father said: Full of blood → impure as niddah. Full of flesh → impure as yoledet (woman after childbirth).
R' Yehuda HaNasi
Had you quoted only one new ruling, we'd accept it. But since the blood ruling follows the individual opinion of Sumakhos/R' Meir, the flesh ruling may also follow an individual — R' Yehoshua — whom we don't follow.
Principle If part of a transmitted teaching follows a rejected minority opinion, the entire transmission is suspect and cannot be relied upon as halakha.

R' Yehoshua vs. the Rabbis: Sac Without Tissue

R' Yehoshua

  • A gestational sac without developed tissue IS an offspring
  • Woman has impurity of childbirth (yoledet)
  • Proof: God made 'garments of skins' — skin proves creation already occurred
  • Existence of a sac = proof of offspring

The Rabbis (Majority)

  • A sac without developed tissue is NOT an offspring
  • The woman is pure
  • Halakha follows this majority view
  • R' Yehuda HaNasi rejects R' Yosei for possibly following R' Yehoshua
Halakha The Rabbis' majority view prevails: a sac without developed tissue does not confer childbirth impurity.

Turbid vs. Clear Amniotic Fluid

Gestational sac discharged — what is the amniotic fluid like?
Turbid (עכור)
R' Oshaya: This is where R' Yehoshua & Rabbis disagree
Rav Naḥman / Rabba bar Avuh: Agrees — dispute is here
Clear (צלול)
R' Yehoshua b. Levi: Dispute applies here
Gemara: Dilemma — does he mean only clear, or both?
↓
Proof from R' Yehoshua's verse: His argument about 'garments of skins' doesn't depend on turbidity → dispute must apply to clear fluid
↓
✅ Conclusion: Dispute applies when fluid is clear — R' Yehoshua b. Levi is correct
Resolution The Gemara concludes that R' Yehoshua's verse-based proof shows the dispute applies to clear fluid. If it were only about turbid fluid, a verse would be unnecessary since turbidity logically suggests a dissolved embryo.

How to Examine an Early Embryo

🫒 Place embryo in oil (soft, cleans the surface)
↓
☀️ View only in sunlight for clarity
↓
🪵 Take a smooth-topped sliver of wood
↓
⬆️ Move sliver along sex organ from below to above (not side to side — Rav Naḥman)
↓
🔵 If sliver catches → Male (member interfered)
↓
🔴 If sliver moves smoothly → Female
↓
📏 Alternative: Female identified by line like a cracked barley grain (Rav Adda bar Ahava)
Rav Naḥman's Clarification The sliver must be moved from below to above. If moved side to side, the womb walls may cause interference, producing a false result.

When to rule impure?Shmuel: Only when embryo has grown hair
Shmuel's distinctionMust be concerned she is impure (stringency), but no days of purity until hair grows
Shmuel's expertiseIdentified a 41-day embryo; caught a man who had relations with his niddah wife
