Niddah 14 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the baraita, why are sailors considered 'all righteous'?
A. They don't ride animals
B. They are in constant danger and pray to God
C. Sea travel prevents seminal emission
D. They immerse in water regularly
Correct: B — The baraita explains that sailors face constant danger at sea, so their hearts are always turned to God in prayer, making them righteous.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the practical difference (nafka mina) between R' Zeira's two versions regarding louse blood?
A. Whether a sin offering is required
B. When a squashed louse is found near the blood on the cloth
C. Whether the split bean measure applies
D. Whether the cloth needs prior examination
Correct: B — If the area is 'examined' (baduk), a louse found nearby must have come from elsewhere, so the blood is definitely hers. If merely 'narrow' (daḥuk), the louse could have entered this one time, creating uncertainty.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the definition of 'after time passed' (aḥar zman) in the Mishna?
A. 24 hours after intercourse
B. Time to descend from bed and rinse her face (pubic area)
C. Until the next morning
D. Time to light a candle
Correct: B — The Mishna defines 'after time' as the period needed for her to descend from the bed and rinse her face — a euphemism for washing her pubic area.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did R' Ḥama bar Bisa choose Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's opinion over R' Yosei's?
A. R' Yosei's ruling was too lenient
B. Rabbi was the Nasi and must be obeyed
C. Constant debate in Rabbi's yeshiva made his reasoning sharper
D. R' Yosei had already retracted his opinion
Correct: C — R' Ḥama reasoned that as head of the yeshiva, Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi was surrounded by constant scholarly dispute, which sharpened his legal reasoning beyond that of R' Yosei.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
On what point do the Rabbis concede to R' Akiva regarding impurity?
A. When blood is found on the husband's cloth
B. When blood is found immediately after intercourse
C. When a woman sees a blood stain before intercourse and then has relations
D. When the examination cloth was not checked beforehand
Correct: C — The Rabbis concede to R' Akiva that when a woman sees a blood stain and then engages in intercourse, she transmits 7-day impurity to the man, even though this impurity is rabbinic in nature.
