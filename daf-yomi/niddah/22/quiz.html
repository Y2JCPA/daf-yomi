5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, what is the core dispute between R' Eliezer and the Rabbis regarding blood found in a piece of tissue?
A. Whether it is the manner of a woman to see blood in tissue
B. Whether the uterine source can be impure while the woman herself is pure
C. Whether blood in tissue counts as a full emission
D. Whether the tissue itself transmits impurity
Correct: B — Rava reframes the dispute: both sides agree it's not the normal manner. R' Eliezer holds the woman is pure but the source is impure (so blood transmits first-degree impurity), while the Rabbis hold both woman and source are entirely pure.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rav Huna initially say a man who extracts semen with a sliver of wood is pure?
A. The verse requires semen to emerge 'from his flesh' naturally
B. Semen becomes impure only when substantial enough to block the tip of the penis, and that amount cannot be extracted with wood
C. Extracting semen is forbidden, so the halakha doesn't apply
D. Wood absorbs the semen, nullifying its impurity
Correct: B — Rav Huna argues that semen impurity requires a minimum quantity — enough to block the penile tip — and this amount cannot be extracted with a sliver of wood.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, why does a zav's seminal emission negate only ONE day of his seven clean days rather than the entire count?
A. Seminal emission is a lesser form of impurity than ziva
B. It is a Torah edict: bits of ziva mixed with semen negate only one day, while pure ziva negates the entire count
C. The seminal emission purifies the remaining ziva
D. The emission occurs too quickly to affect more than one day
Correct: B — Rav Huna explains it as a Torah edict (gezeirat hakatuv): pure ziva negates all seven days, but bits of ziva mixed in semen (tzaḥtzoḥei ziva) negate only one day, derived from 'so that he is impure by it.'
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the unresolved question (teiku) on this daf?
A. Whether dry blood can ever be impure
B. Whether the dissolution test for discharged items requires a full 24-hour soaking period
C. Whether Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel's mashing method is valid
D. Whether creature-shaped discharges ever cause impurity
Correct: B — The Gemara leaves unresolved whether blood items need the full 24-hour soaking period required for dried carcasses, or whether blood (being softer) dissolves faster and a shorter period suffices.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does R' Yochanan say R' Yehuda agrees with the Rabbis that creature-shaped discharges require blood to emerge for the woman to be impure?
A. Creatures are always non-kosher and therefore cannot cause impurity
B. Blood can plausibly dry into the form of tissue, but not into the form of a fish or grasshopper
C. The Torah only mentions creature forms in the context of purity
D. R' Yehuda only rules strictly regarding items from the uterus itself
Correct: B — R' Yochanan explains that R' Yehuda's stringency about tissue (impure even without visible blood) is because blood can dry and take on tissue form. But blood cannot plausibly become a creature shape, so R' Yehuda agrees that visible blood is required in those cases.
