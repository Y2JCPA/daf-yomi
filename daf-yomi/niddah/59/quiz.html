QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yannai, why is a woman with a stain slightly larger than a gris deemed pure when a squashed louse is found on it?
A. Because any stain with a louse is automatically pure regardless of size
B. Because the visible louse accounts for the excess, and the remaining gris-sized stain can be attributed to a second louse
C. Because a squashed louse proves the entire stain is from lice
D. Because the woman was occupied with lice and can attribute stains to her occupation
Correct: B — R' Yannai's reasoning is precise: the squashed louse visibly accounts for the blood beyond the gris. Once subtracted, the remaining stain equals a gris, which can then be attributed to another louse.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does R' Meir deem a woman who finds blood in her urine while standing as temei'ah but not while sitting?
A. Standing causes more blood pressure in the uterus
B. When sitting with a steady stream, the urine flows continuously and does not return to the uterus to bring blood
C. The Torah specifically mentions standing as a cause of impurity
D. Sitting prevents blood from leaving the body entirely
Correct: B — Shmuel explains the mishna refers to nitkalnach (steady stream). When sitting, the urine flows continuously without returning to the uterus. Standing requires straining, which can push urine back to the uterus, bringing blood with it.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rava's novel ruling about stain attribution?
A. A woman can never attribute stains to external sources
B. If one type of external stain is found on her, she may attribute other stains of different colors to external sources as well
C. All stains below two gris can be attributed to lice
D. A woman occupied with any item can attribute all stains to that item
Correct: B — Rava rules that finding one type of unexplained stain (like sycamore sap) allows her to attribute other differently-colored stains to unknown external sources too — since one substance sprayed on her unknowingly, others could have as well.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why can't a woman 'split' a large stain by assuming external blood landed in its middle?
A. The Rabbis decreed against all forms of stain division
B. The baraita teaches we do not cast external blood into the middle of a stain to divide it into sub-gris portions
C. Stains always spread evenly and cannot have a middle section
D. Only a squashed louse can divide a stain, not other substances
Correct: B — The Gemara entertains the possibility of placing the external blood in the middle of the stain to create two sub-gris halves, but the baraita explicitly rejects this approach — the stain is treated as a single unit.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the dispute between Reish Lakish and R' Yochanan regarding R' Meir's view on compound uncertainty (sfek sfeika)?
A. Reish Lakish says R' Meir is lenient with sfek sfeika; R' Yochanan says he is strict
B. Reish Lakish says R' Meir is strict even with sfek sfeika; R' Yochanan says R' Meir is lenient when there is compound uncertainty
C. Both agree R' Meir is strict; they disagree about R' Yosei
D. Both agree R' Meir is lenient; they disagree about who the halakha follows
Correct: B — Reish Lakish derives from the mishna's language that R' Meir deems her impure even with the compound uncertainty of man-or-woman plus wound-or-uterus. R' Yochanan holds R' Meir is strict only with a single uncertainty.
