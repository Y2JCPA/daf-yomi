QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why do Beit Hillel exempt a woman who sees blood on the 11th day from bringing a sin offering?
A. Because the 12th day cannot combine as a day of ziva, so she cannot become a zavah gedolah
B. Because the 11th day is not part of the ziva period
C. Because she immersed properly before intercourse
D. Because the bleeding is considered menstrual, not ziva
Correct: A — Beit Hillel's key reasoning is that since day 12 is unfit for ziva, the 11th day's bleeding cannot lead to zavah gedolah status. Therefore, requiring an offering would actually be a leniency — permitting a non-sacred animal into the Temple.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Beit Hillel's response when Beit Shammai demand they 'apply their method equally' regarding impurity and offerings?
A. They concede and agree to require the offering
B. They argue the impurity decree is a stringency, but the offering requirement would be a leniency
C. They reject both the impurity decree and the offering requirement
D. They cite a verse that exempts the 11th day entirely
Correct: B — Beit Hillel distinguish between the impurity decree (a stringency to prevent confusion) and the offering requirement (which would be a leniency, as it permits bringing a non-sacred animal into the Temple courtyard).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why does Rav Huna's framework treat the woman herself as definitely impure but her husband as only contingently impure?
A. Because the husband immersed separately
B. Because women have a higher standard of impurity than men
C. Because she frequently sees blood (so a stronger decree applies) while he does not
D. Because the husband was unaware of her status
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that rabbinic decrees are calibrated to frequency: since she commonly sees blood, a definite decree of impurity applies to her; since he does not commonly see blood, his status remains contingent.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What do Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel AGREE upon regarding a lesser zavah?
A. That immersion at night is valid for a lesser zavah
B. That the 11th day is treated like all other ziva days
C. That immersing at night without observing a clean day is invalid, and intercourse without a clean day renders both liable
D. That the sin offering is always required
Correct: C — The baraita explicitly states that both schools agree: immersion at night for a lesser zavah is invalid, and if she immersed in the first evening without a clean day and had intercourse, she transmits impurity to mishkav u'moshav and both are liable for a sin offering.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara conclude that the 'wayward conduct' clause of the mishna reflects only Beit Hillel's opinion?
A. Because Beit Shammai never discuss wayward conduct
B. Because Rabbi Yehuda directed his challenge specifically to Beit Hillel about this clause
C. Because the term 'wayward' is a Beit Hillel legal category
D. Because Beit Shammai would permit the intercourse in this case
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda's challenge — 'You call this wayward? He intended to have intercourse with a shomeret yom!' — is directed specifically to Beit Hillel, indicating this clause represents their view. This resolves the difficulty with Rav Huna, as Beit Shammai would hold she is definitely impure, not contingently so.
