Niddah 27 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, under what condition can an afterbirth NOT be attributed to a prior offspring?
A. When the afterbirth emerges before the offspring is born
B. When more than 5 days have passed since birth
C. When the afterbirth is found in a different room
D. When the mother has had multiple pregnancies
Correct: A — Shmuel specifies that an afterbirth emerging BEFORE the offspring is born cannot be attributed to that offspring — only an afterbirth that comes after the birth.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Abaye explain that R' Chiyya's sons were born 3 months apart despite the rule against superfetation?
A. He says the rule against superfetation doesn't apply to twins
B. One drop of semen split into two — one completed at month 7, the other at month 9
C. He says R' Chiyya's wife was an exception to the general rule
D. He claims they were actually born only days apart and the story is exaggerated
Correct: B — Abaye resolves the contradiction by explaining that both twins were conceived simultaneously from one drop that split, but their forms completed at different gestational stages.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is R' Meir's response when the Sages argue that a fetus disintegrates between the womb and the house?
A. He concedes the point entirely
B. He says the fetus never disintegrates
C. Disintegrating once is not comparable to disintegrating twice
D. He says impurity applies regardless of disintegration
Correct: C — R' Meir distinguishes between one transfer (womb to room) and two transfers (womb to inner room, then to outer room), arguing that one disintegration is not the same as two.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabba, why does R' Shimon rule that a house with an afterbirth is tahor?
A. Because an afterbirth has no inherent impurity
B. Because corpse dust mixed with other matter is nullified — same applies to a fetus dissolved in blood
C. Because the offspring was never alive
D. Because only a kohen would be affected by such impurity
Correct: B — Rabba explains that just as corpse dust loses its impurity when mixed with other substances (gangilon), a disintegrated fetus mixed with birth blood is similarly nullified.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Under which burial condition does corpse dust remain tamei?
A. Buried in clothing in a wooden coffin
B. Buried on a brick floor
C. Buried naked in a marble coffin or on a stone floor
D. Buried in any coffin regardless of material
Correct: C — Corpse dust is tamei only when no other substance could have decomposed with the body — naked burial in marble or on stone ensures all dust is purely from the corpse.
