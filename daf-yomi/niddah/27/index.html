Niddah 27: Afterbirth, Twins & Impurity

Afterbirth discharged — can it be linked to a prior birth?
↓
Shmuel
Up to 10 days after birth, and only if afterbirth comes after the offspring
R' Yochanan
Up to 23–24 days after birth
↓
Can twins be born far apart?
↓
Yes — Yehuda & Chizkiyya (sons of R' Chiyya) born 3 months apart
↓
Does an afterbirth in a house create corpse-impurity?
R' Meir
House is tamei — no afterbirth without an offspring
R' Yosei, R' Yehuda, R' Shimon
House is tahor — offspring disintegrated
Core Theme This daf explores the temporal and spatial limits of attributing an afterbirth to an offspring, and whether a disintegrated fetus can still generate tum'ah.

Afterbirth in the House: Is It Tamei?

R' Meir — Tamei

  • No afterbirth exists without an offspring
  • The offspring renders the house impure
  • If moved to outer room → tahor (offspring disintegrated in transit)
  • But disintegrating once ≠ disintegrating twice

R' Yosei, R' Yehuda, R' Shimon — Tahor

  • Offspring disintegrated before reaching the house
  • Just as it disintegrates when moved to outer room…
  • …so too it disintegrated moving from womb to inner room
  • No intact fetus = no corpse impurity
R' Meir's Logic R' Meir distinguishes: one transfer (womb → room) may preserve the fetus, but two transfers (womb → inner room → outer room) would destroy it.

R' Shimon's Nullification Principle (Rabba's Explanation)

A corpse decomposes into dust
↓
Is the dust mixed with any other substance?
No mixing
Dust is tamei — e.g., buried naked in marble coffin or on stone floor
Mixed
Dust is tahor — e.g., buried in clothing, wooden coffin, or brick floor
↓
R' Shimon applies the same logic: disintegrated fetus mixed with birth blood = nullified
Rabba's Principle The halakha of a corpse in its ultimate state (dust) mirrors its initial state: if mixed with other matter, its impurity is nullified (gangilon).
