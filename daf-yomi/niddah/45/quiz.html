Niddah 45 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the unresolved difficulty (kashya) on Amud A?
A. The baraita contradicts R' Yannai's view that R' Meir requires a full three years
B. R' Yochanan cannot explain why thirty days equal a year
C. The mishna contradicts the baraita about betrothal age
D. Rav Ḥisda's proof from 'finger in the eye' is rejected
Correct: A — The baraita states R' Meir accepts two years and one day, contradicting R' Yannai's claim that R' Meir requires a full three years. The Gemara leaves this as an unresolved difficulty.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda, what does the mishna's comparison to 'placing a finger in the eye' teach?
A. That intercourse with a girl under three causes permanent damage
B. That the hymen disappears and regrows, just as the eye tears and tears again
C. That the act has no halakhic significance whatsoever
D. That the girl becomes a zona
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda argues the mishna could have simply said 'it is nothing.' By comparing it to an eye — which tears and heals repeatedly — the mishna teaches that the hymen disappears and comes back.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
During the 'examination year,' what are minors tested on regarding vows?
A. Whether they can recite the vow formula in Hebrew
B. Whether they understand in Whose name they vowed and the meaning of the vow
C. Whether they have grown two pubic hairs
D. Whether their parents consent to the vow
Correct: B — The mishna specifies that during the examination year (12th for girls, 13th for boys), the child is tested on whether they understand the meaning of their vow and in Whose name they vowed.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does R' Yehuda HaNasi hold that girls' vows are examined one year earlier than boys'?
A. Because girls are physically stronger at a younger age
B. Because the Torah explicitly states girls mature earlier
C. Because 'vayyiven' (He built the woman) implies God gave women greater bina (understanding)
D. Because girls are obligated in fewer mitzvot and need less time to learn
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda explains that R' Yehuda HaNasi derives from 'vayyiven' (Genesis 2:22) that God granted women greater bina (understanding/intelligence), so girls reach intellectual maturity for vows earlier.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Reish Lakish (citing R' Shimon ben Menasya) interpret the word 'vayyiven' differently?
A. He reads it as 'He built her a house' — referring to the institution of marriage
B. He reads it as braiding/styling hair — God beautified Eve before presenting her to Adam
C. He reads it as 'He gave her bones (etzem)' — referring to physical creation
D. He reads it as building the Beit HaMikdash
Correct: B — Reish Lakish says 'vayyiven' means God braided Eve's hair, as in overseas cities braiding is called 'benayita' — related to 'building.' This uses the verse for a different teaching, leaving no proof for women's greater understanding.
