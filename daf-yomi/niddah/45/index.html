One Day vs. Thirty Days in a Year

R' Yochanan
Just as one tanna says one day in a year = a full year, another tanna says thirty days = a full year. So a girl can be betrothed from age 2 years and 30 days.
R' Yannai
R' Meir requires a full three years — no partial-year equivalence applies.
Gemara
But the baraita explicitly states R' Meir accepts two years and one day! This is difficult for R' Yannai.
Conclusion The baraita remains difficult (kashya) according to R' Yannai's reading of R' Meir.

The Hymen Dilemma: Under Age Three

A man engages in intercourse with a girl under three
↓
Mishna: It is like placing a finger in the eye (עין)
↓
Possibility A
Hymen disappears and regrows
(like the eye that tears and tears again)
Possibility B
Hymen is never removed until after age three
Rav Ḥisda's Proof The mishna could have simply said 'it is nothing' — by saying 'finger in the eye' it teaches that just as the eye tears and heals, the hymen disappears and regrows.
5 / 9

Practical Ramifications of the Dilemma

If Hymen Disappears & Regrows

  • Blood under age 3 = hymen blood (tahor)
  • No blood after age 3 → hymen didn't regrow in time
  • Not necessarily a zona
  • Blood emitted under 3 does NOT render impure

If Hymen Never Removed Until 3

  • Blood under age 3 = menstrual blood (tamei)
  • No blood after age 3 → another man must have been involved
  • She would be a zona — forbidden to a Kohen
  • She is rendered impure from blood under 3
Rav Ḥiyya b. Rav Ika Objects to the first formulation: even if the hymen regrows, who says it doesn't heal immediately? She could still be a zona! The Gemara reformulates the ramification around purity of blood, not zona status.

Key Point During the examination year, the child is tested: does she know in Whose name she vowed? Before that year — always invalid. After maturity — always valid. No exceptions.

Why Every Clause of the Mishna Is Needed

📋 Clause 1: 'Vows examined at 11+1' → Teaches: even a sharp 10-year-old is NOT examined — age is absolute
↓
📋 Clause 2: 'At 12+1 vows are valid' → Teaches: examination does NOT continue forever into adulthood
↓
📋 Clause 3: 'Examined throughout 12th year' → Teaches: even if she failed after 30 days, keep examining — 30-day rule doesn't apply here
↓
📋 Clause 4: 'Before — always invalid' → Teaches: even if she SAYS she understands, too young is too young
↓
📋 Clause 5: 'After — always valid' → Teaches: even if she SAYS she doesn't understand, an adult's vow stands
Talmudic Method The Gemara's systematic challenge — 'why do I need this clause?' — reveals that each line of the Mishna preempts a specific mistaken inference.
