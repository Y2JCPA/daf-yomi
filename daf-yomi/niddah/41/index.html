Blood, Source & the Outer Chamber

Rav Yosef
Reinterprets the baraita: both offspring and blood emerged through the caesarean incision — the dispute is about the impurity of the source itself
Tanna Kamma
The location of a woman's source (uterus) is impure — blood from it is impure regardless of exit path
R' Shimon
The source is pure — only uterine blood that exits vaginally is impure
Reish Lakish
If the blood is impure, the woman is impure too
R' Yochanan
Even if the blood is impure, the woman remains pure — impurity requires flow through her ervah
Core Question Does blood from the uterus carry impurity inherently, or only when it exits through the vaginal canal?

Caesarean birth: offspring and blood both exit through the abdominal incision

R' Shimon
Blood is pure — only vaginal emergence creates impurity
Key Dispute Is the uterus (mekom ha-makor) itself a locus of tumah, or does impurity only attach when blood exits through the vagina?

Does Impure Blood Make the Woman Impure?

Reish Lakish
According to the Tanna Kamma who deems the blood impure, the woman is also impure as with menstruation
R' Yochanan
Even per the Tanna Kamma, the woman remains pure — impurity of niddah requires flow through her ervah
R' Yochanan
Source: Leviticus 20:18 — 'he has made naked her source' — she is impure only when blood emerges from her nakedness
R' Yochanan's Principle A woman does not become a niddah unless the blood flow emerges through her genitalia (ervah)

Source (Makor) Impurity — Two Cases

Dislodged Uterus (Reish Lakish / R' Yehuda Nesia)

  • Uterus falls out of the body onto the ground
  • She is impure — from Ezekiel 16:36: 'your foundation was poured out'
  • Not 7-day niddah impurity (requires 'blood')
  • Rather: impurity until evening — body contact with impure source

Pearly Drops (R' Yochanan)

  • Source discharges two whitish, pearl-like (margaliyyot) drops
  • She is impure
  • Not 7-day impurity (not among the 5 impure blood colors)
  • Rather: impurity until evening — contact with uterine discharge
  • Must be two drops — one drop might originate elsewhere
Common Thread Both cases produce only tumah until evening (tum'at erev), not the 7-day niddah impurity, because neither involves recognized impure blood

What Is the Outer Chamber (Prozdor)?

Reish Lakish
The outer chamber = any place visible when a girl sits with legs spread — blood reaching there makes her impure
R' Yochanan
Objection: That area is already 'exposed' for sheretz contact impurity — why would the Torah need 'in her flesh' to teach this?
R' Yochanan
Rather: the outer chamber extends until the area between the teeth-like projections (bein ha-shinayim) inside the vagina
Unresolved A dilemma is raised: Is the area between the teeth-like projections (bein ha-shinayim) itself considered inside or outside? The Gemara leaves this open
