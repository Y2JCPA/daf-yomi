Niddah 10 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Giddel citing Rav, after which sighting does a young girl (who saw blood, missed 3 cycles, then saw again) begin transmitting retroactive impurity when seeing at regular 30-day intervals?
A. After the 1st sighting
B. After the 2nd sighting
C. After the 3rd sighting
D. After the 4th sighting
Correct: C — Rav Giddel says the 1st and 2nd times her time is sufficient; after the 3rd time she transmits impurity retroactively for 24 hours or from examination to examination.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the dispute between Ḥizkiyya and R' Yoḥanan regarding a young girl's blood stain found between her 2nd and 3rd sightings?
A. Both say she is impure
B. Ḥizkiyya says impure, R' Yoḥanan says pure
C. R' Yoḥanan says impure, Ḥizkiyya says pure
D. Both say she is pure
Correct: B — Ḥizkiyya holds she is impure since seeing blood would render her impure; R' Yoḥanan holds she is pure since she hasn't yet established a presumptive menstrual status.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does R' Zeira distinguish between a menstrual virgin's stain and a young girl's stain?
A. The virgin is older than the young girl
B. The virgin has a common alternative blood source (hymenal blood); the young girl does not
C. The virgin has already established a menstrual pattern
D. The Torah explicitly exempts virgins from stain impurity
Correct: B — R' Zeira explains that a menstrual virgin's secretion (sirfah) from her torn hymen is common, so stains are attributed to it. A young girl lacks such a common alternative source.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥilkiya bar Tovi, if a young girl whose time to see blood has not arrived continuously discharges blood for all seven days, how many sightings does this count as?
A. Seven sightings (one per day)
B. Two sightings
C. Three sightings
D. One sighting
Correct: D — Rav Ḥilkiya bar Tovi rules that continuous bleeding for all seven days counts as only one sighting — she still needs two more sightings to establish her menstrual status.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does R' Yehuda say young girls who have reached maturity should be examined with oil rather than by hand?
A. Oil is more accurate at detecting menstrual blood
B. Manual examination may scratch them, producing blood mistaken for menstrual blood
C. Oil has purifying properties that neutralize impurity
D. Hand examination is forbidden by Torah law for minors
Correct: B — R' Yehuda is concerned that manual examination could scratch the girl, and the resulting blood would be mistakenly assumed to be menstrual, ruining her presumptive purity status.
