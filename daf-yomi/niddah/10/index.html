Young Girl's Path to Established Menstrual Status

Young girl (tinokot) has not yet menstruated
↓
Sees blood 1st time → her time is sufficient
↓
Sees blood 2nd time → still her time is sufficient
↓
Sees blood 3rd time — pattern established
R' Yehuda HaNasi
Presumption after 2 occasions — 3rd sighting confirms
R' Eliezer
3 missed cycles → returns to young girl status
Key Principle The Gemara concludes the baraita follows R' Eliezer on missed cycles, but R' Yehuda HaNasi on establishing presumption after two occasions.

Rav Giddel's Ruling: Young Girl Sees Blood at Regular Intervals

Young girl sees blood, then 3 cycles pass without bleeding, then sees again
↓
She then sees blood at regular 30-day intervals
1st & 2nd Sighting
Her time is sufficient (dayah sha'atah)
After 3rd Sighting
Retroactive impurity — 24 hours or from last examination
↓
Rav Kahana's variant: If she missed 3 more cycles between sightings — after 2nd time she already transmits retroactively
Distinction The more disrupted her pattern, the fewer sightings needed to impose retroactive impurity.

Who Is the Tanna of the Baraita?

Gemara
The baraita says a young girl becomes an established woman on the 3rd sighting — that sounds like Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, who says presumption is established after 2 occasions.
Gemara
But the latter clause says: if 3 cycles pass without bleeding, she returns to young girl status — that is Rabbi Eliezer's view!
Gemara
Perhaps it is R' Yehuda HaNasi who agrees with R' Eliezer on missed cycles?
Gemara
But R' Yehuda HaNasi only said R' Eliezer is worthy to rely upon in exigent circumstances — not as a general rule!
Conclusion
The tanna is R' Eliezer, who follows R' Yehuda HaNasi on establishing presumption after 2 occasions.
Resolution A composite position: R' Eliezer's rule on missed cycles + R' Yehuda HaNasi's rule on establishing a pattern.

Blood Stains Between Sightings: Ḥizkiyya vs. R' Yoḥanan

Gemara
A young girl finds a blood stain (ketem) between her 1st and 2nd sightings — she is pure. All agree.
Ḥizkiyya
Between 2nd and 3rd sightings — she is impure. Since if she saw blood she'd be impure, her stain is also impure.
R' Yoḥanan
She is pure. She hasn't yet attained presumptive status of one who sees menstrual blood — so we don't render her impure for a stain either.
R' Ilai
Objection to Ḥizkiyya: A menstrual virgin's stain is pure while her blood is pure — what's the difference?
R' Zeira
A menstrual virgin's secretion (sirfah) from her hymen is common, so stains are attributed to that. A young girl's secretion is not common — so stains are assumed menstrual.
Practical Rule Stains are evaluated based on whether there is a common alternative source of blood to attribute them to.

Attribution of Blood Stains: Menstrual Virgin vs. Young Girl

Menstrual Virgin (Besulat Dam)

  • Blood from torn hymen is common (sirfah)
  • Stains presumed to be from hymen
  • Stain is pure while hymenal blood flows
  • Has an alternative source to attribute blood to

Young Girl (Tinokot)

  • No common alternative secretion
  • Stains presumed to be menstrual (per Ḥizkiyya)
  • Disputed: R' Yoḥanan says pure until status established
  • Lacks an alternative source for blood
R' Zeira's Principle The purity of a stain depends on whether there is a common non-menstrual source to which it can be attributed.

Continuous Discharge: How Many Sightings?

AuthorityRuling on Continuous 7-Day Discharge
Rav Ḥilkiya bar ToviContinuous bleeding for all 7 days = one sighting. She still needs 2 more sightings to establish status.
Rav Shimi bar ḤiyyaConstant dripping is treated like continuous discharge — also counts as only one sighting.
Gemara's QuestionIf she stops and restarts during 7 days — that could count as two sightings.
Gemara's ClarificationRav Ḥilkiya meant specifically continuous discharge (not stop-and-start) = one sighting.
Practical Impact A young girl who bleeds continuously for a week is still only credited with one sighting toward establishing her menstrual status.
