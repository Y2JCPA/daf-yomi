Niddah 63 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, what disqualifies saliva from being 'tasteless' (rok tafel)?
A. Sleeping too long
B. Rising early and eating before testing
C. Drinking water after midnight
D. Being awake all night
Correct: B — The baraita teaches that 'since the evening' excludes one who arose early in the morning and ate — that saliva is no longer tasteless.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rav Ashi define the state of mitnamnem (dozing)?
A. Fully asleep with dreams
B. Asleep yet not asleep — responds when called but cannot reason logically
C. Light sleep that lasts less than 30 minutes
D. Sleep while sitting upright
Correct: B — Rav Ashi defines dozing as being asleep yet not fully asleep, awake yet not fully awake — one responds when called but cannot provide logical answers, though can remember things when reminded.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Shmuel, how many occurrences are needed to establish a sensation-based veset for symptoms listed in the Mishna?
A. Three occurrences
B. Two occurrences
C. One occurrence
D. Four occurrences
Correct: C — Shmuel teaches that for physical sensations listed in the Mishna (sneezing, yawning, shuddering), even one occurrence of bleeding accompanied by the sensation establishes a fixed cycle.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If the seven stain-removal substances are applied in reverse order (second group before first), what is the result?
A. Everything is valid
B. Nothing counts at all
C. Only the substances applied second count
D. Only the first group applied counts
Correct: C — Abaye resolves the two baraitot: both agree that only the substances applied second in sequence count — the ones applied first must be redone.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How did Abaye help Rav Yosef recover Shmuel's teaching about establishing a veset?
A. He found it written in Rav Yosef's notes
B. He reminded him that Rav Yosef himself had taught it to his students
C. He brought witnesses who heard the tradition
D. He derived it independently from a baraita
Correct: B — Rav Yosef had fallen ill and forgotten his studies. Abaye restored the tradition by reminding Rav Yosef that he himself had originally taught it, reconstructing the teaching detail by detail.
