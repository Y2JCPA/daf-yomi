What Is Tasteless Saliva (Rok Tafel)?

Baraita
Saliva where the person had not tasted anything since the evening — even if she ate after nightfall, but did not eat for the rest of the night.
Rav Pappa
Perhaps this means she did not eat anything all night long?
Rava
No — 'since the evening' means even if she ate after nightfall. It excludes only one who arose early and ate before testing.
R' Yochanan
Tasteless saliva requires three conditions: (1) did not eat, (2) passed midnight, and (3) was in a state of sleep.
Practical Point If one rose early and learned Torah aloud, that speech weakens the saliva's potency and disqualifies it as rok tafel.

Defining Mitnamnem — The Dozing State

Objection: A baraita says awake all night = tasteless saliva, but sleep all day ≠ tasteless. So sleep doesn't matter!
↓
Resolution: 'Awake all night' means dozing on and off (mitnamnem), not fully awake
↓
Rav Ashi's definition of dozing:
Asleep
Yet not fully asleep — responds when called
Awake
Yet not fully awake — cannot reason logically but remembers things when reminded
Definition Mitnamnem: Asleep yet not asleep, awake yet not awake. Responds to being called but cannot provide logical answers.

Establishing a Veset: Days vs. Sensations

Days-Based Cycle

  • Bleeding on the same date of the month
  • Requires 2 occurrences to establish (Shmuel)
  • Does not need to repeat a 3rd time
  • Example: bleeding on the 20th twice

Sensation-Based Cycle

  • Bleeding accompanied by a physical symptom
  • Requires only 1 occurrence to establish (Shmuel)
  • Symptoms listed in Mishna: sneezing, yawning, shuddering
  • Unlisted symptoms (garlic, onion, pepper triggers): need 3 times
Key Distinction Shmuel: Listed sensations establish a veset after once; unlisted sensations (heavy head, trembling, belching, dietary triggers) require three occurrences.

Rav Yosef Forgets — Abaye Restores the Tradition

Shmuel
The mishna requiring 3 times to establish a day-cycle is the view of Rabban Gamliel b'Rabbi in the name of Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel.
Rav Yosef
I do not remember this tradition... (Rav Yosef had fallen ill and forgotten his learning.)
Abaye
You yourself taught us this! You said: the Rabbis hold that even one occurrence suffices — no need to repeat a 2nd or 3rd time.
Abaye
We asked: if no 2nd time needed, why mention no 3rd? You answered: 'no 2nd' = sensations; 'no 3rd' = days.
Gemara
Why attribute to Rabban Gamliel b'Rabbi? Just say it's Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel! Answer: To teach that Rabban Gamliel b'Rabbi holds like his grandfather.
Moving Scene Rav Yosef's illness caused him to forget his teachings. His devoted student Abaye patiently reconstructed the forgotten tradition detail by detail.
