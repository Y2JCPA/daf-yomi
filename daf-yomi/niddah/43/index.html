Niddah 43: Thresholds of Impurity

כל שכבת זרע שאינה יורה כחץ — אינה מטמאה
CORE THEME This daf explores the precise boundaries of ritual impurity: When does discharge create tum'ah? Must it leave the body? How much is required? The Gemara maps the interplay between location (internal vs. external), sensation, and quantity in defining impurity for the zav, niddah, and ba'al keri.

Zav Impurity Through a Pole in Body Folds

Reish Lakish
If a zav places a pole in his own body folds and moves a pure person — the person remains pure
Reish Lakish
If a pure person places a pole in his folds and moves the zav — the pure person becomes impure
Gemara
The verse uses the language of touch for movement-impurity: 'Whoever a zav touches…' (Lev 15:11) — the zav's movement must be with external body parts, like his hands
Gemara
Body folds are not external — so a zav cannot impart movement-impurity through them
PRINCIPLE A zav's movement-impurity (hesset) requires contact with the external portions of his body — folds are considered internal

When Does Impurity Begin? Internal vs. External

Niddah (Woman)

  • Impure when blood moves from uterus to vagina
  • Does NOT need to leave the body
  • Internal movement is sufficient
  • Derives from: 'her issue in her flesh be blood' (Lev 15:19)

Zav

  • Not impure until discharge exits the body
  • Must emerge 'out of his flesh' (Lev 15:2)
  • Internal presence alone is insufficient
  • Torah specifies: discharge must come OUT

Ba'al Keri

  • Not impure until semen exits the body
  • 'The flow of seed GOES OUT from a man' (Lev 15:16)
  • Emission must physically leave
  • Internal uprooting alone is not enough
KEY DISTINCTION The niddah is unique: impurity begins internally. For the zav and ba'al keri, the Torah requires the discharge to exit the body

May a Priest Hold His Penis to Prevent Emission?

R' Eliezer
Anyone who holds his penis and urinates is as though he brings a flood to the world!
Abaye
The mishna means he holds it with a coarse cloth — a soft cloth is forbidden lest it increase the emission
Rava
Even a soft cloth is permitted — once the semen is already uprooted, holding it won't increase flow
Gemara
But a baraita compares it to a finger in the eye — it keeps tearing! Wouldn't holding increase emission?
Rava
That applies to drops. When his limbs are quaking, a second heating is uncommon — so no concern
PRACTICAL HALAKHA When a priest eating terumah feels an imminent emission, he may hold his penis to prevent it — Abaye requires a coarse cloth; Rava permits even a soft cloth

Shmuel: When Does Seminal Emission Create Impurity?

Man experiences seminal emission
↓
Version 1
Was the emission felt by his entire body?

✅ Felt → Impure
❌ Not felt → Pure

Reason: 'flow of seed' = fit to fertilize
Version 2
Was the semen shot like an arrow?

✅ Shot → Impure
❌ Merely seeped → Pure

Reason: Only forceful emission counts
↓
Practical difference: One who felt it fully but it was not shot like an arrow — impure per Version 1, pure per Version 2
OBJECTION A mishna states: One who had sexual thoughts at night and found his flesh warm is impure — even without feeling the emission! Rav Huna resolves: it refers to intercourse in a dream, where sensation certainly occurred

Minimum Quantity of Zav Discharge — The Dispute

PositionMinimum AmountSource
Rabbis (Mishna)Any amount suffices'His issue' — no minimum specified
R' Natan / R' YishmaelEnough to block the tip of the penis'His flesh be stopped from his issue' (Lev 15:3)
ShmuelFollows R' Natan/R' YishmaelRequires blockage-level quantity
Additional ruleMust be moist, not dry'Run' implies liquid flow
VERSE ANALYSIS The Rabbis use 'his flesh be stopped' to teach that only a moist discharge imparts impurity, not to set a minimum quantity. R' Yishmael uses 'run' for the moisture requirement instead
