Miscarriage: Does It Count as a Birth?

Rav Pappi
A miscarriage is not a proper birth. Her blood is considered blood of a zava. The baraita about 24-hour retroactive impurity is only rabbinic — they didn't impose it on a woman who miscarries.
Rav Pappa
The reason a pregnant woman isn't retroactively impure is that her head and limbs feel heavy. Even if the miscarriage isn't a 'birth,' she still had that heaviness during pregnancy — so her time is sufficient.
Core Dispute Is 'דיה שעתה' for pregnant women based on the birth event itself, or on the physical state of pregnancy?

Bleeding Before Pregnancy Is Visible

Woman sees blood and only afterward her fetus becomes known to all
↓
R' Yirmeya asks R' Zeira: Is her time sufficient since she was already pregnant?
↓
Possible Leniency
She saw blood close to when her pregnancy became apparent
R' Zeira's Ruling
Since she didn't yet feel the heaviness of pregnancy, she is impure retroactively like any other woman
Principle R' Zeira: The leniency depends on the woman's physical experience of pregnancy (heavy head and limbs), not the biological fact of pregnancy alone.

Must a Pregnant Woman Examine at Her Fixed Cycle?

The Dilemma

  • An elder asks R' Yochanan
  • Only according to the view that fixed-cycle examination is Torah law
  • Perhaps since her blood has stopped, no exam is needed?

Proof: R' Meir's Mishna

  • Woman hiding from danger — if her cycle time comes, she is pure
  • Reason: fear stops menstrual blood
  • Implies: without fear, she'd be impure if she didn't examine

R' Yochanan's Answer

  • Even though fixed-cycle exam is Torah law...
  • Fear stops blood → she need not examine
  • Similarly: pregnancy stops blood → she need not examine
Analogy Just as fear (פחד) stops the menstrual flow and exempts from examination, so too pregnancy stops the flow and exempts from examination.

Nursing Woman: How Long Is 'Her Time Sufficient'?

OpinionDuration of ProtectionReasoning
R' MeirEntire nursing period — even 4-5 yearsMenstrual blood spoils and becomes milk (דם נעכר ונעשה חלב)
R' Yehuda, R' Yosi, R' ShimonOnly first 24 monthsHer limbs are dislocated; strength doesn't return for 24 months
If child dies within 24 monthsImmediately like all womenNo longer nursing — physical cause removed
Practical Difference A woman nursing for 4-5 years: per R' Meir her time is sufficient the whole time; per the Sages, after 24 months she transmits impurity retroactively.

Defining the 'Elderly Woman' (זקנה)

Sage 1
An elderly woman is one who does not take offense (אינה מקפדת) when people call her 'Mother, Mother' (אמא, אמא).
Sage 2
An elderly woman is one who is not embarrassed (אינה מתביישת) when called 'Mother, Mother.'
Gemara
The difference: a woman who is embarrassed but does not take offense — per one view she qualifies; per the other she does not.
R' Zeira & R' Shmuel bar Rav Yitzchak The two Sages dispute the precise emotional threshold for 'elderly' status, which determines when דיה שעתה applies.
