Niddah 9 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, why is a pregnant woman's time sufficient even after a miscarriage?
A. Because her head and limbs felt heavy during pregnancy
B. Because the miscarriage counts as a proper birth
C. Because retroactive impurity is only rabbinic
D. Because a miscarriage purifies the blood
Correct: A — Rav Pappa holds that the physical state of pregnancy — heavy head and limbs — is the reason her cycle is assumed to have stopped, regardless of whether the miscarriage qualifies as a 'birth.'
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What proof does R' Yochanan bring that a pregnant woman need not examine at her fixed cycle time?
A. A baraita about nursing women whose children died
B. R' Meir's ruling that a woman hiding from danger is pure if her cycle time passes
C. The incident at Hitlo village
D. Reish Lakish's statement about the 30-day cycle
Correct: B — R' Yochanan proves from R' Meir's mishna: just as fear stops the blood flow and exempts from examination, pregnancy likewise stops the flow and exempts from examination.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the fundamental dispute between R' Meir and the Sages about nursing women?
A. Whether a nursing woman can establish a fixed cycle
B. Whether the blood becomes milk (R' Meir) vs. the body remains weakened for 24 months (Sages)
C. Whether the child must be alive for the exemption to apply
D. Whether nursing women need to examine themselves
Correct: B — R' Meir holds menstrual blood spoils and becomes milk — so protection lasts as long as nursing. The Sages hold the limbs are 'dislocated' and don't recover for 24 months — so protection is time-limited.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the difficulty about an elderly woman seeing blood at even intervals?
A. It rejects the baraita entirely
B. It says even intervals always make her impure
C. It emends the baraita or attributes it to R' Dosa, who says a fixed cycle means her time is sufficient
D. It distinguishes between intervals longer and shorter than 30 days
Correct: C — The Gemara either reverses the text of the baraita or reads it as saying even intervals actually fix a cycle (per R' Dosa), so her time remains sufficient — only uneven intervals cause her to return to normal status.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did the Sages reject R' Eliezer's proof from the girl of Hitlo village?
A. Because she was too young to menstruate
B. Because R' Eliezer was not present at the ruling
C. Because the ruling was made in exigent circumstances (famine or concern for pure items)
D. Because the village of Hitlo was outside the Land of Israel
Correct: C — The Sages argued that the lenient ruling was issued under special circumstances — either during a famine or because the girl had handled many pure items — and therefore cannot serve as precedent for normal cases.
