Niddah 58 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, why does Shmuel rule that a woman who sat on examined ground and found blood is pure?
A. Because she didn't sense the emission
B. Because ground is not susceptible to ritual impurity, following R' Neḥemya
C. Because ground is easy to examine thoroughly
D. Because blood on the ground is always from an external source
Correct: B — Rav Ashi explains that Shmuel follows R' Neḥemya's principle: items not susceptible to tumah (like the ground) cannot become impure from blood stains. The stain decree applies only to items that are susceptible to impurity.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why is a woman impure when blood is found on the tip of her big toe?
A. Because the toe touches the heel, which touches the vagina
B. Because impurity travels from place to place
C. Because when walking, the toe may be positioned directly under the vagina
D. Because the toe is considered part of the inner side of the body
Correct: C — The Gemara rejects the idea that impurity travels place to place. Rather, the tip of the big toe is different because during walking it can be positioned directly under the vagina and blood could drip onto it.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If a woman wearing three robes finds a stain on the uppermost robe but did NOT pass through a butcher market, what is her status?
A. Pure, because the stain is far from her body
B. Pure, because outer robes are exposed to external factors
C. Impure, because she cannot attribute the blood to an external source
D. It depends on the size of the stain
Correct: C — The baraita teaches that the determining factor is not which robe bears the stain, but whether there is a plausible external attribution. Without one, she is impure even if the stain is on the outermost robe.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the maximum stain size that R' Ḥanina ben Antigonus allows to be attributed to a killed louse?
A. A grain of barley
B. A sela coin
C. A split bean
D. An olive's bulk
Correct: C — R' Ḥanina ben Antigonus rules that a stain up to the area of a split bean (gris) may be attributed to a louse, even if the woman doesn't remember killing one.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Eliezer son of R' Tzadok, what determines the status of blood found on an examination cloth placed under a pillow?
A. The color of the blood
B. The size of the stain compared to a sela
C. Whether the stain is round (pure) or elongated (impure)
D. Whether the cloth was examined before being placed there
Correct: C — R' Eliezer son of R' Tzadok distinguishes by shape: a round stain suggests a crushed louse (pure), while an elongated stain is consistent with an internal examination and indicates menstrual blood (impure).
