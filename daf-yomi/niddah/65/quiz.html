QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara's resolution, how were Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's four nights distributed across life stages?
A. 4 nights as a na'arah
B. 2 as minor, 2 as na'arah
C. 1 as minor, 3 as na'arah
D. 1 as minor, 2 as na'arah, 1 as bogeret
Correct: C — The Gemara concludes: 1 night as a minor (since all acts as a minor count as one) and 3 nights as a na'arah, counting every 2 months as one period within the 6-month na'arut.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Minyamin Saksana's intended ruling never take effect?
A. Shmuel overruled him publicly
B. He passed away before reaching his destination
C. Rav sent a letter correcting him
D. The local court rejected his authority
Correct: B — Minyamin misunderstood Rav's position and intended to rule leniently for a grown woman. He died on the road before issuing the ruling. Shmuel cited Proverbs 12:21: 'No mishap shall befall the righteous.'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How long is an 'onah' (עונה) — a 'period of time' — according to the Gemara's resolution?
A. 24 hours
B. 6 hours
C. 12 hours
D. A full day and night
Correct: C — Rav Shmuel bar Rav Yitzḥak harmonized the two views: whether measured as a full day/night at the equinox or half-day plus half-night at the solstice, an onah always equals 12 hours.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the final halakha regarding the wedding night according to Rav, Shmuel, R' Yoḥanan, and Reish Lakish?
A. The bride has the entire night
B. The groom consummates and then separates
C. They may have relations for four nights
D. It depends on whether she saw blood before marriage
Correct: B — All four authorities agree: the groom performs bi'at mitzvah (the consummation) and then separates (porush), overriding the mishna's more lenient allowance of the entire night.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say the onah for marriage may be longer than 12 hours?
A. Because marriage is a mitzvah deserving more time
B. Because the ketubah signing may cause delays
C. Because Beit Hillel are lenient about time
D. Because nighttime hours are longer in winter
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that due to the marriage contract (ketubah) — as some delay the consummation while completing and signing it — the Sages extended the period to a night and half a day, beyond the standard 12-hour onah.
