Niddah 65: Allocating the Four Nights & Defining Time Periods

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi gave a virgin bride four nights within twelve months
↓
How are four nights distributed across minor → young woman → grown woman?
↓
Resolution
1 night as minor
3 nights as young woman
(every 2 months = 1 period)
Final Halakha
Rav & Shmuel: Groom performs bi'at mitzvah then separates
KEY TENSION The mishna grants multiple nights, but the later consensus of Rav, Shmuel, R' Yoḥanan, and Reish Lakish restricts this to a single act of consummation.

How Are Four Nights Distributed?

A virgin married — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi gave her 4 nights in 12 months
↓
Can all 4 be during na'arut (young womanhood)?
↓
❌ Shmuel: Na'arut lasts only 6 months — not enough for 4 quarterly visits
↓
Resolution: 1 night as ketanah (minor), 3 nights as na'arah
Counting every 2 months as one period of absence
KEY PRINCIPLE All acts of intercourse while she is a minor count as only one act (Rav to Rav Ḥinnana bar Shelamya).

Rav Ḥinnana bar Shelamya Asks Rav

Rav Ḥinnana bar Shelamya
A girl married before puberty — when her time to see blood arrives, does she still get four nights?
Rav
All intercourse while she is a ketanah (minor) counts as only one act. The remaining three complete the four nights.
Rav (on teeth)
Also: When a person's teeth fall out, his food diminishes — as it says: 'Cleanness of teeth… and lack of bread' (Amos 4:6).
NOTE "Cleanness of teeth" (ניקיון שיניים) is a euphemism for having no teeth at all.

The Story of Minyamin Saksana

לֹא יְאֻנֶּה לַצַּדִּיק כׇּל אָוֶן
NARRATIVE Minyamin Saksana was traveling to Shmuel's place. He intended to rule leniently based on Rav — permitting a grown woman to attribute blood to her hymen the entire first night, even if she had already seen menstrual blood.

But he misunderstood Rav's position. Before he arrived, he passed away on the road.

Shmuel cited: "No mishap shall befall the righteous" (Proverbs 12:21) — God prevented an erroneous ruling from being issued in Rav's name.

What Is 'A Period of Time' (עונה)?

R' Ḥiyya bar Abba / R' Yoḥanan

  • Either the day or the night
  • Applies at the equinox (Nisan/Tishrei)
  • Day = Night = 12 hours

R' Ḥana bar She'ona / Rabba bar bar Ḥana

  • Half a day and half a night
  • Applies at the solstice (Tammuz/Tevet)
  • Half long day + half short night = 12 hours
RESOLUTION Rav Shmuel bar Rav Yitzḥak: They do not disagree — both equal 12 hours, just calibrated to different seasons.
