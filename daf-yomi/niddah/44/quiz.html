QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, from what verse do we derive that a one-day-old baby girl can become impure as a niddah?
A. Leviticus 15:19 — the extra word 'And' in 'And a woman'
B. Leviticus 15:33 — 'whether male or female'
C. Leviticus 13:2 — 'a person'
D. Numbers 19:18 — 'the people'
Correct: A — The Gemara derives this from the seemingly superfluous 'And' (ו) before 'a woman' in Leviticus 15:19, which serves to include even a one-day-old baby girl in the laws of niddah.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the core disagreement between R' Yehuda and R' Yishmael ben R' Yochanan ben Beroka regarding zav impurity for a baby?
A. Whether a baby can become a zav at all
B. The source verse — 'any man' vs. 'male or female' — and whether superfluous Torah language is always halakhically significant
C. The minimum age for zav impurity
D. Whether both boys and girls can become zavim
Correct: B — R' Yehuda derives the inclusion of a baby from 'any man' (Lev 15:2). R' Yishmael derives it from 'male or female' (Lev 15:33) and says 'any man' is just normal speech — invoking the principle that the Torah speaks in human language.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What analogy does Mar son of Rav Ashi use to explain post-mortem fetal movement?
A. A fish flopping on dry land
B. The tail of a lizard twitching after being severed
C. A flame flickering before going out
D. A leaf trembling in the wind
Correct: B — Mar son of Rav Ashi compares the fetus's post-mortem spasms to a lizard's severed tail — the movement is purely reflexive and does not indicate the fetus is independently alive.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the final halakha, what happens regarding a firstborn son born after his father's death?
A. He receives a double portion as usual
B. He receives a double portion but does not reduce others' shares
C. He does NOT receive a double portion, because his father cannot 'acknowledge' him
D. He inherits nothing at all
Correct: C — The Gemara rules per Rava (Pumbedita version): Deut 21:17 requires the father to 'acknowledge' the firstborn, which is impossible posthumously. Therefore a posthumous firstborn does not receive a double portion.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the mishna about a girl of three years and one day, if one of those with whom relations are forbidden engages in intercourse with her, what is the legal outcome?
A. Both parties are liable
B. Neither party is liable
C. He is executed; she is exempt because she is a minor
D. She is liable; he is exempt due to her age
Correct: C — The mishna states that the man who violates a forbidden relation with her is executed by the court, but she is entirely exempt from punishment because she is a minor and cannot bear legal responsibility.
