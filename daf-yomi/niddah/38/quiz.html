Niddah 38 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Levi, during which days is blood from labor pains ritually pure?
A. All days from the onset of labor until birth
B. Only during the eleven days of ziva
C. Only during the seven days of menstruation
D. Only during the first three days of ziva
Correct: B — Levi holds that birth purifies blood from labor pains only during the days of ziva. During menstruation days, she has the status of a niddah.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the gematria basis for the 271-day gestation period?
A. The word 'leidah' (birth)
B. The word 'herayon' (conception)
C. The word 'ubbar' (fetus)
D. The word 'yoledet' (woman giving birth)
Correct: B — Mar Zutra derives the 271-day gestation from the gematria of 'herayon' (הריון) in Ruth 4:13, which equals 271.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Eliezer disagree with the Rabbis about labor-pain blood during the 80 days of purity?
A. He doesn't believe in days of purity at all
B. He applies the dayyo principle — the kal va-chomer conclusion can't exceed its source
C. He holds that labor pains always create impurity
D. He follows a different verse than the Rabbis
Correct: B — Rabbi Eliezer invokes dayyo: since labor-pain blood outside the 80 days is impure, a kal va-chomer cannot make it more lenient within the 80 days than it is outside them.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the second version of the dispute, what does Rabbi Levi say about a lesser zava?
A. A lesser zava is also purified by birth
B. A lesser zava must observe a clean day for each day of discharge — birth doesn't purify her
C. A lesser zava doesn't exist during pregnancy
D. A lesser zava becomes a greater zava automatically during labor
Correct: B — In the second version, Rabbi Levi limits the purifying effect of birth to a greater zava only. A lesser zava (1-2 days of bleeding) must still observe clean days.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why can't a woman bleed with labor pains for 26 consecutive days without becoming a greater zava (according to R' Yosei and R' Shimon)?
A. Because labor pains can only last 14 days
B. Because 26 days inevitably includes 3 consecutive ziva days of bleeding
C. Because the Torah limits labor to 25 days
D. Because after 26 days the child is considered non-viable
Correct: B — With only 14 days of labor-pain purity available, a span of 26 days must include at least 5 days of ziva, making 3 consecutive days of ziva bleeding inevitable — rendering her a greater zava.
