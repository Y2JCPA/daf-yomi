When Does Birth Purify Blood of Labor Pains?

Rav
Blood from labor pains is pure even during the days fit for new counting after the zava count is negated — as long as pains began during days of ziva.
Levi
Blood from labor pains is pure only during the eleven days of ziva themselves. During days of menstruation, she is impure as a niddah.
Gemara
Rabbi Meir says labor pains can last 40–50 days. This works for Rav, but for Levi — how can she stay pure that long?
Levi
The mishna doesn't mean she's pure for all those days. During menstruation days she's a niddah; during ziva days she's pure. She simply isn't a zava.
Core Principle The birth of a child can purify blood emitted during labor — but the scope of that purification is debated between Rav and Levi.

Rav vs. Levi: Scope of Purity from Labor Pains

Rav (via Rav Adda bar Ahava)

  • Blood during labor pains is pure during days of ziva
  • Also pure during days fit for new counting after negation
  • As long as pains began during ziva and continue without 24-hour rest
  • Can explain Rabbi Meir's 40–50 day period of purity

Levi

  • Blood during labor pains is pure only during the 11 days of ziva
  • During days of menstruation — she has status of niddah
  • Rabbi Meir's mishna means she won't become a zava — not that she's pure throughout
  • She still cycles between niddah and ziva status
Key Difference Rav extends the purifying effect of labor beyond the strict ziva window; Levi limits it to the eleven ziva days only.

Greater Zava vs. Lesser Zava — Second Version

R' Levi
Birth purifies blood only for a greater zava (bleeding 3 consecutive days during ziva). A lesser zava (1–2 days) must still observe clean days.
Abba Shaul / Rav
Birth purifies even a lesser zava. The verse says both "days" and "many days" — including lesser ziva too.
R' Levi
Leviticus 15:25 says "many days" — referring specifically to a zava gedola. Only she is rendered pure by labor pains.
Gemara
Both agree: labor-pain blood during menstruation days makes her a niddah. Only ziva-day blood is potentially purified by the child.
Scriptural Basis "And if a woman has an issue of her blood many days…she is impure" (Leviticus 15:25) — does "many days" include lesser ziva or only greater?

Rabbi Meir's 150-Day Calculation Without Greater Ziva

1️⃣ 2 days of bleeding on days 10–11 of ziva (not enough for greater zava)
↓
2️⃣ 7 days of menstruation (bleeding doesn't count as ziva)
↓
3️⃣ 2 days of bleeding on days 1–2 of next ziva period
↓
4️⃣ 50 days of labor pains before birth — child renders her pure
↓
5️⃣ 80 days of purity after giving birth to a female
↓
6️⃣ 7 days menstruation + 2 more days of ziva = ~150 days total
R' Meir's Point By carefully sequencing menstruation, ziva, labor, and post-birth purity, a woman can bleed for 150 days without ever becoming a greater zava.

25 vs. 26 Days of Labor-Pain Bleeding

Woman bleeds with labor pains for consecutive days
↓
≤25 Days (with child)
2 days ziva + 7 menstruation + 2 ziva + 14 days labor = no greater zava status
26+ Days (with child)
Inevitably includes 3 consecutive ziva days of bleeding → becomes a zava gedola
↓
Rava clarifies: "Without a child" in the baraita means a non-viable newborn (nefel) — labor pains don't purify for a nefel
Rava vs. Rav Sheshet Rav Sheshet emended the baraita to read "with a child." Rava preserved the text — "without a child" means a non-viable birth, where labor pains don't purify.
