Can Minors Perform Ḥalitza or Yibum?

R' Meir
A minor boy and a minor girl may not perform ḥalitza nor enter into yibum (levirate marriage).
Rabbis
We agree about ḥalitza — the Torah says "man" (Deut. 25:7). But why prohibit yibum?
R' Meir
A minor boy may turn out to be a saris (sexually underdeveloped), and a minor girl an aylonit — they'd be violating forbidden relations with no mitzvah fulfilled!
Rabbis
We follow the majority — most minors do not become sexually underdeveloped. No need for concern.
Principle R' Meir is concerned for the minority (חושש למיעוט), while the Rabbis follow the majority.

Why Immerse a Baby Girl in Babylonia?

Rav Yosef reports a baby girl immersed in Pumbedita (Babylonia)
↓
But Shmuel rules: Teruma outside Eretz Yisrael — a menstruant may not eat it, but touching does not disqualify it!
↓
Mar Zutra: The immersion was needed for smearing teruma oil on the baby
↓
Because smearing is equivalent to drinking (סיכה כשתיה), derived from Lev. 22:15 and Psalms 109:18
Halakha Smearing oil on one's body has the same halakhic status as consuming it — so teruma oil requires ritual purity even in Babylonia.

Jewish Girls vs. Samaritan Girls — Niddah from the Cradle?

Jewish Girls (בנות ישראל)

  • The verse "And IF a woman" (Lev. 15:19) includes minors in niddah laws
  • When a girl sees blood, she is separated as a niddah immediately
  • No blanket decree needed — the Torah already covers them
  • We rely on family observance of the halakhot

Samaritan Girls (בנות כותים)

  • Samaritans do not properly observe niddah laws
  • R' Meir's concern for the minority applies
  • Rabbis decreed: treated as niddah from the cradle
  • No reliance on their families separating them properly
Distinction Jewish girls are covered by Torah law when they actually menstruate; Samaritan girls needed a rabbinic decree because their community doesn't observe the halakhot.

When Do Purity Laws Apply to Girls?

📜 "And IF a woman has an issue" (Lev. 15:19) — the word 'if' includes even a one-day-old girl in niddah laws
↓
❓ Can we derive zava from niddah? No — zava is more lenient (one clean day suffices for lesser zava)
↓
📜 "And IF a woman has an issue of blood many days" (Lev. 15:25) — separate verse needed for zava
↓
👧 A ten-day-old girl is included in the laws of zava (needs 7 niddah days + 3 zava days minimum)
↓
🔄 The verse about niddah then serves a different purpose: to exclude men from impurity due to red semen
Logic Since zava laws could be derived from niddah laws, the niddah verse is repurposed to exclude men from red-semen impurity.
