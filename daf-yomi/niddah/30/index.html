Immersions After Childbirth & Embryo Formation

Beit Shammai
A woman returning from a journey must immerse 95 times to cover all possible endings of her purity period after giving birth
Beit Hillel
She need only immerse 35 times — we count only immersions required before she is permitted to her husband
R' Yishmael
A male embryo forms in 41 days, a female in 81 days — derived from the Torah doubling impurity periods for a female birth
Rabbis
Cleopatra's experiment proved both male and female embryos form in 41 days. You cannot derive formation time from impurity periods!
Core Tension This daf weaves together two major disputes: how many immersions uncertainty demands, and whether Torah's impurity laws encode biological timelines.

Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel: Counting Immersions

Beit Shammai — 95 Immersions

  • 35 immersions during the first 3 weeks
  • 7 immersions in the 3rd week (nightly)
  • 59 immersions after the 3rd week
  • 1 extra immersion for twilight arrival (Rav Yirmeya of Difti)
  • Counts immersions even AFTER she is permitted to her husband

Beit Hillel — 35 Immersions

  • 28 immersions for possible end of impurity (male, female, or zava)
  • 7 immersions in the 5th week for possible end of niddah
  • No immersion needed after period of purity ends
  • Only counts immersions BEFORE permission to engage in intercourse
Key Distinction Beit Hillel only counts immersions that unlock the woman's permission to be with her husband. Beit Shammai also counts immersions required after she is already permitted.

Which Immersions Count? Childbirth vs. Ziva

Gemara
According to Beit Hillel — shouldn't you also count immersions required because she might be a zava?
Beit Hillel
We only count immersions required before she is permitted to her husband. Zava immersions occur after permission.
Gemara
But Beit Shammai counts post-permission immersions — so why don't they count zava immersions too?
Beit Shammai
We count immersions connected to childbirth, including yoledes b'ziva (birthing zava). Pure ziva immersions are a separate matter.
Distinction Beit Shammai distinguishes between a yoledes b'ziva (zava status linked to childbirth — counted) and standalone ziva (not counted).

Rabbi Yishmael vs. the Rabbis: Embryo Formation

R' Yishmael
Male formation: 41 days. Female formation: 81 days. The Torah's impurity periods (40 days for male, 80 for female) parallel formation time.
Rabbis
You cannot derive embryo formation time from impurity periods. Cleopatra experimented on condemned maidservants and found both form in 41 days!
R' Yishmael
I bring proof from the Torah — you bring proof from fools?! The extra mention of 'gives birth' for a female teaches the doubling applies to formation too.
Rabbis
Cleopatra gave the women a purgative drug to ensure they conceived at the same time. The experiment was controlled.
Torah Source R' Yishmael derives from Leviticus 12:5 — the verse adds an extra mention of 'gives birth' for a female, teaching that formation time is also doubled.
