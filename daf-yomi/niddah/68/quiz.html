Niddah 68 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Rava publicly retract his ruling about washing hair on erev Shabbat?
A. Because Rav Pappa showed that chafifah must be proximate to immersion
B. Because washing hair on Shabbat itself is forbidden
C. Because Rabbi Yehuda disagreed with him
D. Because the woman in the story refused to immerse
Correct: A — Rav Pappa cited Ravin's letter and the principle that immersion must be immediately after hair washing (samuch l'chafifatah). A full Shabbat gap between Friday washing and Saturday night immersion violates this requirement.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the final halakha, when may a woman wash her hair during the day before immersing at night?
A. Only on motzaei Shabbat
B. On any weekday when she can immerse that same night
C. Only if she has servants to help her
D. Never — she must always wash at night
Correct: B — The Gemara resolves the apparent contradiction: 'may wash by day' refers to weekdays when the gap is short; 'only at night' refers to motzaei Shabbat, when erev Shabbat washing would create too long a gap.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If a woman examined herself on the 7th morning and found pure, skipped the twilight examination, immersed, and later found blood — what is her status?
A. Impure from the time of immersion
B. Pure from immersion until her later examination
C. Impure retroactively for 7 days
D. An uncertain zavah gadolah
Correct: B — The Mishna rules that since her last known examination during her menstrual days found her pure, she has a presumptive status of purity (chezkat tahara) from immersion until the later examination when she found blood.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the Rabbis' argument against Rabbi Yehuda's requirement to examine from mincha time onward?
A. Mincha is too late in the day for a proper examination
B. After any examination she might see blood, so a morning exam is equally valid
C. The Torah only requires one examination total
D. Women cannot be expected to examine themselves that frequently
Correct: B — The Rabbis argue: since a woman doesn't keep her hand in place the entire twilight period, blood could appear after ANY examination. Therefore, there is no logical distinction between examining in the morning versus at mincha time.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara defend Rava's position that there is no retroactive 24-hour impurity during ziva days against the challenge from our Mishna?
A. By saying the Mishna's 24-hour rule refers to a young bride's case from daf 64b
B. By saying Rava retracted this position as well
C. By distinguishing between blood seen and blood not seen
D. By limiting the rule to women with fixed cycles
Correct: A — Rava deflects the challenge by explaining the Mishna's 24-hour retroactive impurity refers to the case of a young bride (from 64b), teaching that after her hymenal-blood days end, she transmits retroactive impurity like all women — not a statement about ziva days.
