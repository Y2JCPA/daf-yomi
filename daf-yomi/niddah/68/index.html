Niddah 68: Chafifah Timing & Seventh-Day Examination

When must a woman wash her hair (chafifah) relative to immersion?
↓
Weekday
May wash during the day and immerse that night
Motzaei Shabbat
Must wash hair at night, after Shabbat, immediately before immersion
↓
Mishna: What if she examined on the 7th morning but not at twilight?
↓
Found Pure in Morning
Presumptive status: Pure from immersion until later examination
Found Impure in Morning
Presumptive status: Impure — immersion was ineffective
HALAKHA The Gemara reconciles: on weekdays she may wash by day; on motzaei Shabbat she must wash at night immediately before immersing.

Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak: Same-Night Chafifah

Woman (to Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak)
Come back tomorrow — hinting she had not yet washed her hair while it was still daylight
Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak
Are you lacking kettles (dudei) to heat water? Lacking buckets (tashtekei)? Lacking servants?
Gemara
This implies Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak permits a woman to wash and immerse on the same night
INFERENCE Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak holds chafifah (hair washing) and tevilah (immersion) may both be performed the same evening.

Rava's Ruling, Challenge & Retraction

Rava's original teaching: A woman may wash on erev Shabbat and immerse motzaei Shabbat
↓
Rav Pappa challenges: Ravin sent that this is not permitted! We require immersion immediately after chafifah (סמוך לחפיפתה)
↓
Rava appoints an interpreter (meturgeman) to publicly announce his retraction
↓
Corrected ruling (R' Yochanan): A woman may not wash erev Shabbat and immerse motzaei Shabbat
KEY PRINCIPLE סמוך לחפיפתה טבלה — Her immersion must be proximate to her hair washing. Rava publicly retracted his lenient ruling.

Halakha: Weekday vs. Motzaei Shabbat Immersion

Weekday Immersion

  • May wash hair during the day
  • Immerse that same night
  • Permitted because daytime washing is shortly before nighttime immersion
  • Leniency: concern she won't wash properly at night

Motzaei Shabbat Immersion

  • May NOT wash on erev Shabbat
  • Must wash hair after Shabbat ends
  • Must immerse immediately after washing
  • Gap of an entire day violates 'samuch' requirement
RESOLUTION The two halakhot are not contradictory: 'may wash by day' = weekday; 'only at night' = motzaei Shabbat, when erev Shabbat washing would create too long a gap.

Rabbi Yehuda vs. the Rabbis: When Must She Examine?

Rabbi Yehuda
Any woman who did not examine from mincha time onward on the 7th day — even if she found pure that morning — her status is impure
Rabbis
Even if she examined only on the 2nd day of menstruation and found pure, that is sufficient for transition
Rabbis (to R' Yehuda)
Must she keep her hand in place the entire twilight? After any exam, she might see blood — so what difference between mincha and morning?
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
I asked R' Yosei and R' Shimon while walking: what if she examined the 7th morning, found pure, skipped twilight, then later found impure?
DISPUTE R' Yehuda requires a late-afternoon examination; the Rabbis accept even an early examination as establishing her transition to purity.
