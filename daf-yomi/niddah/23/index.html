Verbal Analogies, Animal-Form Discharges & Defining 'Human Form'

Rav Aḥa b. Rava
A gezeirah shavah not free at all can still teach halakhot, but it can be refuted
R' Yishmael
If free on one side, one derives halakhot and cannot refute it
Rabbanan
Even free on one side — one can still refute it
R' Meir
A woman who discharges an animal-form entity is impure, since yetzirah is written for animals too
Rabbanan
She is impure only if the discharged entity has human form
Core Issue Daf 23 explores the hermeneutical rules of gezeirah shavah and applies them to determine when a non-human discharge renders a woman tamei leidah.

Why Reject the Man–Sea Monster Analogy?

Gemara
The analogy between man and sea monster is free on one side. What refutation exists?
Gemara
Man is unique: a person becomes tamei while alive, unlike animals (only after death) or sea monsters (never)
R' Yoḥanan
R' Meir's reason: yetzirah (formation) is written for animals just as for man
R' Ami
If so, a mountain-shaped discharge should also cause impurity — 'He Who forms mountains'! (Amos 4:13)
R' Ḥiyya b. Abba
A woman cannot discharge a mountain! She discharges a clod, not a mountain.
Torah vs. Nevi'im R' Ḥiyya bar Abba rules: one derives Torah matters from Torah verses, not from the Prophets (Nevi'im) like Amos.

R' Yoḥanan's Alternative: Eye Similarity

Rabba b. bar Ḥana / R' Yoḥanan: R' Meir's reason is that animal eyes resemble human eyes
↓
Objection: A snake also has round pupils like a human!
↓
Answer: The Mishnah omitted snake to teach that Rabbanan disagree even where yetzirah is written
↓
Objection: Bekhorot 40a says animal pupils being round like a human's is a blemish!
↓
Resolution: 'Similar' refers to the pupil shape; 'dissimilar' refers to the surrounding eye
Reconciliation The pupil (bat ayin) of certain animals is round like a human's, but the overall eye structure differs.

Animal-Form Fetus: Firstborn & Survival

R' Meir

  • Animal-form fetus = full offspring
  • Next son = firstborn for inheritance
  • Next son does NOT need pidyon haben
  • Animal-form fetus may be viable

Rabbanan

  • Only human-form = true offspring
  • Next son requires pidyon haben unless preceded by human-form birth
  • Sandal, afterbirth, sac — exempt from pidyon but not inheritance
Rava's Principle Even if viable, the animal-form fetus doesn't affect firstborn inheritance status — the Torah requires the father would mourn (ono → onen) the child's death.

Human Fetus in an Animal's Womb

שחט בהמה ומצא בה דמות יונה — אסורה
Unresolved Question (Teiku) Rav Adda bar Ahava asks Abaye: According to R' Meir, if a human-shaped fetus is found inside a slaughtered animal, may it be consumed?

R' Yoḥanan taught that a pigeon found inside a slaughtered animal is forbidden — but a pigeon has no hooves at all. A human fetus has solid feet (resembling hooves), so perhaps it differs.

The question remains unresolved.
