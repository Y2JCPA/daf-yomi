Niddah 17 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, what does 'in the presence of any living being' refer to?
A. Even mice in the room
B. Canaanite slaves and maidservants
C. Any domesticated animal
D. Insects like flies and gnats
Correct: B — Shmuel corrects Rav Yehuda: it refers not to mice but to households that engage in intimacy before their Canaanite slaves, who cite the verse about a 'nation like a donkey.'
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What protects peeled garlic, onion, or egg left overnight from the danger of evil spirits?
A. Wrapping them in cloth
B. Placing them in a sealed basket
C. Leaving their roots or shells on
D. Storing them in metal vessels
Correct: C — The Gemara states that even if tied and sealed in a basket, the danger remains — only leaving the roots or shells intact prevents the evil spirit from resting on them.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the Sages' teaching about nail disposal, what is the level of one who buries nail clippings?
A. Pious (ḥasid)
B. Righteous (tzaddik)
C. Average person (beinoni)
D. Wicked (rasha)
Correct: B — Burning = pious (fully eradicated), burying = righteous (might be dug up), throwing in public = wicked (a pregnant woman may miscarry).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the anatomical meaning of the 'upper story' (aliyya) in the Mishna's house parable?
A. The uterus
B. The cervix
C. The bladder
D. The vaginal canal
Correct: C — The Mishna maps the anatomy as: inner room = uterus, corridor (perozdor) = vaginal canal, upper story (aliyya) = bladder. Blood from the upper story is ritually pure.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Abaye's core logical challenge to Rav Huna's distinction between blood found inward vs. outward from the vestibule?
A. The anatomy does not support a vestibule between bladder and corridor
B. If we worry about forward bending (outward), we must equally worry about backward staggering (inward)
C. Blood cannot travel from the bladder to the corridor
D. The Mishna never mentions a vestibule at all
Correct: B — Abaye argues that concern about positional movement must be applied symmetrically — if she might bend forward causing bladder blood to appear outward, she might stagger backward causing it to appear inward. You can't apply the concern in only one direction.
