Privacy During Intimacy: What Counts as a 'Living Being'?

Rav Yehuda
Does 'in the presence of any living being' mean even in the presence of mice?
Shmuel
Shinnana! No — it refers to houses where they are intimate in the presence of Canaanite slaves.
Gemara
Those who permit this cite: 'Remain here im the donkey' (Gen. 22:5) — a nation (am) like a donkey.
Rabba b. Rav Huna
I would sound bells (zagei) on the bed canopy so people kept away.
Abaye
I would drive away even flies (didevei) from my bed.
Rava
I would drive away gnats (peruḥei).
MODESTY The Sages went to extraordinary lengths for privacy — from bells on the canopy to chasing away insects.

PracticeDanger / ConditionMitigation

Drinking diluted beverages left overnightDangerous in metal vessels (Shmuel, R' Yoḥanan)Natron vessels are equally dangerous (Rav Pappa)
Sleeping in a cemeterySpirit of impurity may endanger himEspecially dangerous if done to invite spirits
Throwing nail clippings in publicPregnant woman passing over may miscarryCut something else afterward; see nail disposal rules
Bloodletting then immediate intercourseChildren will be weak (vittakin); if both let blood → ra'atan diseaseTaste something after letting blood (Rav)
R' SHIMON B. YOḤAI One who performs these bears liability for his own life — 'his blood is upon his own head.'

Disposing of Nail Clippings: Three Levels

🔥 Burns them — eradicates them entirely
↓
⬇️ This person is called ḥasid (pious)
↓
🕳️ Buries them — they might be dug up
↓
⬇️ This person is called tzaddik (righteous)
↓
🚮 Throws them in a public area
↓
⬇️ This person is called rasha (wicked) — a pregnant woman may miscarry
NOTE The Gemara initially limits the danger to scissors and cutting hand and foot nails together, but then concludes: we are concerned in all cases.

Intercourse by Day: Prohibition and Exceptions

Rav Ḥisda
It is prohibited to be intimate by day — as it says: 'Love your fellow as yourself' (Lev. 19:18).
Abaye
The reason: he might see something repulsive in his wife and she will become repugnant to him.
Rav Huna
Jews are holy and do not engage in intercourse by day.
Rava
If the house is dark, it is permitted. A Torah scholar may create darkness with his garment.
V'AHAVTA Rav Ḥisda derives the prohibition from 'love your neighbor' — intimacy must preserve dignity and affection.

The House Parable: Anatomy of Niddah Law

Inner Room (Ḥeder)

  • Represents the uterus
  • Blood from here is definitely impure
  • The 'source' (makor) of menstrual blood

Corridor (Perozdor)

  • Represents the vaginal canal
  • Blood found here: uncertain origin
  • Presumed impure — assumed from the source

Upper Story (Aliyya)

  • Represents the bladder
  • Blood from here is ritually pure
  • Connected to corridor via an open vestibule
MISHNA The Sages used the parable of a house to map the anatomy: inner room, corridor, and upper story — each with a different halakhic status.
