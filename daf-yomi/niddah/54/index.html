Why Not Day 19 in the 2-on/2-off Pattern?

Gemara
Day 19 should be permitted — the 11 ziva days ended on day 18, so no clean day is needed!
Rav Sheshet
The mishna calling one who doesn't wait a 'glutton' (gargeran) means it is actually prohibited to engage in intercourse on that day
Rav Ashi
It is not prohibited — she is already in her new menstrual period. But she still needs a clean day for day 17's bleeding (the 10th of ziva)
Rav Ashi
Since day 18 had bleeding, it couldn't serve as the clean day for day 17. So day 19 must be that clean day
Practical Difference Rav Sheshet: the day after ziva is rabbinically prohibited. Rav Ashi: it's permitted in principle, but here day 19 is needed as a clean day for a different reason.

Do Birth-Impurity Days Count Toward Ziva Clean Days?

Rav Kahana
A baraita shows: days of birth-impurity without bleeding are reckoned in the counting of 7 clean days for ziva
Rav Pappa
No proof! Perhaps only because she might have had a male — those extra 7 days (beyond male's 7) count only due to uncertainty
Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua
But the uncertainty includes female birth too! Yet despite that possibility, the days still count — so they must count even for a definite female
Gemara
Conclusion: Indeed, conclude that such days are reckoned in the counting
Halakhah Days of birth-impurity on which a woman does not see blood count toward her seven clean days required for purification from ziva status.

Mishna: Impurity Sources — Moist vs. Dry

Impure Moist AND Dry

  • 🩸 Blood of a menstruating woman (dam niddah)
  • 💀 Flesh of a corpse
  • Transmit impurity by contact and carrying in either state

Impure ONLY When Moist

  • Zav discharge (ziva)
  • Mucus and saliva of a zav
  • Carcass of a sheretz (creeping animal)
  • Animal carcass (neveilah)
  • Semen (shichvat zera)
  • Exception: if soakable → impure even when dry
The Soaking Test If a dried substance can be soaked in lukewarm water for 24 hours and restored to its original state, it transmits impurity even when dry.
