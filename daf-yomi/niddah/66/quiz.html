Niddah 66 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
After bleeding 3 times during intercourse with her first husband, what must the woman do?
A. Examine herself with a tube
B. Divorce and marry another man
C. Abstain from intercourse permanently
D. Wait for her next menstrual cycle
Correct: B — R' Yochanan explains she should divorce rather than examine, because 'not all fingers are equal' — a different husband may not cause bleeding.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the dispute between Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel and the Sages regarding blood from a wound in the uterus?
A. Whether the woman may remain married
B. Whether the blood is ritually impure for purposes of eating pure food
C. Whether a tube examination is required
D. Whether she needs to immerse in a mikveh
Correct: B — R' Shimon b. Gamliel holds blood from a wound becomes impure by passing through the uterus (an impure 'place'), while the Sages hold such blood remains pure since it doesn't originate in the uterus.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why must a woman wash her hair with hot water before immersion, according to Ameimar?
A. Cold water is not valid for ritual purposes
B. Cold water hardens the hair, trapping dirt that becomes an interposition
C. Hot water is required by Torah law
D. Cold water causes the mikveh to become invalid
Correct: B — Cold water causes hair to harden, preventing dirt from being removed. The remaining dirt would serve as an interposition (חציצה) invalidating the immersion.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What principle does R' Zeira derive regarding concealed body parts and mikveh immersion?
A. They must be fully submerged in water
B. They must be fit for water contact even if actual contact is not indispensable
C. They do not need any preparation
D. They must be examined by a second person
Correct: B — R' Zeira's principle: like flour that must be fit for mingling with oil even when mingling isn't indispensable, concealed areas must be fit for water contact even if actual immersion of those areas isn't strictly required.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Rav Chanan of Neharde'a prohibit standing on a wooden plank in the mikveh?
A. Wood absorbs mikveh water and invalidates it
B. It resembles immersing in a bathhouse
C. She will be afraid of falling and won't immerse properly
D. Wood creates an interposition between her feet and the water
Correct: C — Rav Chanan disagrees with Rav Kahana's 'bathhouse decree' reasoning. The real concern is that fear of slipping prevents proper immersion — and this applies to any material, not just earthenware.
