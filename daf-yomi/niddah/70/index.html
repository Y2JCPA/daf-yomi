Niddah 70: The Questions of the Alexandrian Sages

Overview This daf records the famous encounter between Rabbi Yehoshua and the sages of Alexandria, who posed twelve questions in four categories: three halakhic (including the intermingled leper offerings), three aggadic (resolving contradictory verses), three of 'ignorance' (Lot's wife, resurrection purity), and three of conduct (how to become wise and wealthy).

The Intermingled Leper Offerings

Two wealthy lepers each bring sin, guilt, and burnt offerings
↓
Offerings become intermingled; one set is sacrificed
↓
One leper dies — what happens to the survivor?
↓
Problem A
Cannot sacrifice a sin offering for the dead
Problem B
Cannot bring a second sin offering — the first may have been his
↓
R' Yehoshua: Transfer all property → become destitute → bring a bird sin offering (permitted in uncertainty)
Key Principle A bird sin offering may be brought even in cases of safeik (uncertainty), providing a halakhic escape route.

Whose Opinion Does Rabbi Yehoshua Follow?

Shmuel
R' Yehoshua's case is where the guilt offering was already sacrificed before the intermingling occurred.
Rav Sheshet
Is it possible a great man like Shmuel would say this? Whose opinion does this follow?
Rav Sheshet
If R' Yehuda — once the guilt offering was brought as a wealthy man, the status is fixed. He cannot then bring a bird offering!
Rav Sheshet
If R' Shimon — the sin offering fixes status, so why require the guilt offering to be pre-sacrificed? Just bring a new animal with a stipulation!
Gemara
Shmuel agrees with R' Shimon that sin offering fixes status, but disagrees about allowing stipulations on guilt offerings.
Resolution Shmuel adopts R' Shimon's view on one point (sin offering determines status) but rejects his permissive approach to conditional offerings.

Three Aggadic Contradictions Resolved

God's Desire for Life vs. Death

  • Ezekiel 18:32 — 'I have no pleasure in death… turn and live'
  • I Samuel 2:25 — 'The Lord desired to kill them'
  • Resolution: Ezekiel = sinners who repent; Samuel = those who do not repent

God Shows Favor vs. No Favor

  • Numbers 6:26 — 'The Lord shall show favor to you'
  • Deuteronomy 10:17 — 'Who shows no favor'
  • Resolution: Numbers = before judgment is sealed; Deuteronomy = after the decree

God Chose Zion vs. Rejects It

  • Psalms 132:13 — 'The Lord has chosen Zion'
  • Jeremiah 32:31 — 'This city has been a provocation'
  • Resolution: Psalms = before Solomon married Pharaoh's daughter; Jeremiah = after
Method R' Yehoshua resolves each contradiction by distinguishing temporal contexts — the same God acts differently based on human choices and circumstances.

Three Questions of 'Ignorance'

Alexandrian Sages
Does Lot's wife (a pillar of salt) transmit tumat met (corpse impurity)?
R' Yehoshua
A corpse transmits impurity, but a pillar of salt does not — even if it was once a person.
Alexandrian Sages
Does the Shunammite's son, revived by Elisha, transmit corpse impurity?
R' Yehoshua
A corpse transmits impurity, but a living person does not — even if he was once dead.
Alexandrian Sages
Will the resurrected dead in the future require sprinkling with red heifer ashes?
R' Yehoshua
When they come to life, we will be clever for them and clarify the halakha then!
Alternate Version Some say R' Yehoshua answered: 'When Moshe Rabbeinu comes with them, he will tell us the halakha.'
