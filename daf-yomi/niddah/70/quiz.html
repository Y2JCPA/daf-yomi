QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why can't the surviving leper simply bring a new sin offering after his companion dies?
A. Because the intermingled animal may have been his — and one cannot bring a second sin offering
B. Because sin offerings cannot be brought after midday
C. Because a leper can only bring one animal in his lifetime
D. Because the surviving leper is now ritually impure from the death
Correct: A — The sacrificed animal might have been the survivor's sin offering. If so, bringing another would be a prohibited duplicate. And if the remaining animal is his, he can't sacrifice it because it might belong to the dead man, and one may not sacrifice a sin offering for the dead.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yehoshua resolve the contradiction between 'I have no pleasure in death' (Ezekiel) and 'the Lord desired to kill them' (Samuel)?
A. One verse refers to Jews and the other to non-Jews
B. One refers to sinners who repent; the other to sinners who do not repent
C. One is metaphorical and the other is literal
D. One refers to spiritual death and the other to physical death
Correct: B — R' Yehoshua distinguishes based on the sinner's response: God desires life for those who repent (Ezekiel), but those who refuse to repent, like Eli's sons, face Divine judgment (Samuel).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehoshua, does Lot's wife (now a pillar of salt) transmit corpse impurity?
A. Yes, because she was once a living person
B. Yes, but only for seven days after her transformation
C. No, because a pillar of salt is not a corpse
D. No, because she was punished by God and therefore exempt
Correct: C — R' Yehoshua rules simply: a corpse transmits impurity, but a pillar of salt does not — regardless of its origin. The transformation changed the halakhic status of the object.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What determines whether a leper brings wealthy or destitute offerings according to Rabbi Shimon?
A. His status when the guilt offering (asham) is brought
B. His status when the sin offering (chatat) is brought
C. His status when the bird offerings are brought on day one
D. His status at the time of his initial diagnosis
Correct: B — R' Shimon holds that 'everything follows the sin offering' — the leper's financial status at the time the chatat is brought determines whether the remaining offerings must be those of a wealthy or destitute person.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does the Gemara conclude about the relationship between Torah study and prayer for achieving wisdom?
A. Prayer alone is sufficient if one is sincere
B. Study alone is sufficient if one is diligent
C. Both are necessary — prayer without study does not suffice
D. Neither is sufficient without a great teacher
Correct: C — R' Yehoshua first recommends increased study, then adds prayer after the Alexandrians object. The Gemara concludes both are indispensable: 'this (prayer) without that (study) does not suffice' — wisdom requires human effort paired with Divine assistance.
