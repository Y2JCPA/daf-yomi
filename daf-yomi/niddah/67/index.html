Niddah 67: Laws of Valid Immersion

Woman prepares for tevilah
↓
Is there any chatzitzah (interposition) on her body?
No interposition
Proceed to immerse
Interposition present
Must remove it first — mud, food, scabs, dry mucus, eye shadow all invalidate
↓
When may she immerse?
Standard rule
Night only — lest her daughter imitate and immerse on day 7
Exceptions
Daytime on day 8 — danger of lions, thieves, cold, or gatekeepers
Key Theme Valid immersion requires both a clean body (no chatzitzah) and proper timing (nighttime, with limited exceptions).

Proper Posture During Immersion

R' Yochanan
A woman who opens her eyes very wide or closes them very tightly during immersion — the tevilah is invalid. Water cannot reach the creases around the eyes.
Reish Lakish
A woman immerses the way she naturally stands — not forcing arms to sides or legs together, but not spreading widely either.
Mishnah (Nega'im)
A man with a leprous mark between his legs stands like one who is hoeing. A woman stands like one who is weaving. Under the breast — like one nursing her son.
Principle The body should be in its natural resting position — neither forced open nor tightly closed — so water reaches all surfaces.

Hair Knots & Interposition

How many hairs are tied in a knot?
1 hair
Interposes — invalidates immersion (Rabba bar Rav Huna & R' Yochanan)
2 hairs
Uncertain — Rabba bar Rav Huna doesn't know; R' Yochanan says does not interpose
3+ hairs
Does not interpose — cannot be tied tightly enough to block water
Resolution R' Yochanan's tradition: only a single knotted hair interposes. Two or more do not prevent water from reaching the body.

Torah Law vs. Rabbinic Decrees on Chatzitzah

Torah Law (D'Oraita)

  • Interposition must cover majority of body
  • Person must be particular (makpid) about its removal
  • Both conditions required → invalidates immersion

Rabbinic Decree #1

  • Covers majority of body
  • Person is not particular
  • Decreed invalid — due to similarity to Torah case

Rabbinic Decree #2

  • Covers minority of body
  • Person is particular
  • Decreed invalid — due to similarity to Torah case

No Decree (Permitted)

  • Covers minority of body
  • Person is not particular
  • No decree — we don't decree upon a decree (גזירה לגזירה)
R' Yitzchak's Framework The Sages extended Torah law in two directions but stopped short of a third extension, applying the principle that one does not issue a decree to safeguard another decree.

When May a Niddah Immerse?

Rav (initial)
At her time (end of day 7) — night only.
Not at her time (day 8+) — day or night.
R' Yochanan
Whether at her time or not — always night only. Her daughter might follow her example and immerse during the daytime of day 7.
Rav (retracted)
I retract. Whether at her time or not — night only, because her daughter might follow her example.
(via R' Chiyya bar Ashi)
Conclusion Both Rav and R' Yochanan agree: a niddah immerses only at night to prevent confusion with daytime immersion on the invalid seventh day.
