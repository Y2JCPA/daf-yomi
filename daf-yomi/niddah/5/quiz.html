5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara interpret the baraita as the universally-held view rather than the mishna?
A. Because the baraita is older than the mishna
B. Because when a ruling can be read as lenient or stringent, we choose the stringent interpretation
C. Because R' Dosa's opinion is always preferred
D. Because the mishna was written by a single author
Correct: B — The Gemara's principle is that when there's ambiguity, we interpret toward stringency (chumra). The baraita's retroactive impurity (stringency) is attributed to all, while the mishna's leniency is attributed to R' Dosa alone.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Chanina ben Antigonus, what is the status of blood stains for a woman whose 'time suffices' (dayah sha'atah)?
A. Impure retroactively, like all other women
B. No impurity at all from stains
C. Impure from that point onward only, not retroactively
D. Impure only if found on her garment, not bedding
Correct: C — R' Chanina ben Antigonus holds that for 'dayah sha'atah' women (pregnant, nursing, elderly), blood stains share the same ruling as their sighting — impure from now onward, but not retroactively.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Shmuel rule that a pre-intercourse examination cloth does not reduce the 24-hour retroactive period?
A. Because examination cloths are inherently unreliable
B. Because the cloth was not witnessed by two people
C. Because the woman is excited and hurried, making the exam improper
D. Because the cloth must be white to be valid
Correct: C — Rav Ketina explains Shmuel's reasoning: since the woman is excited (mehuma) and hurried for intercourse, the examination is not thorough enough to count as a proper exam for the 24-hour reduction.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does R' Yochanan resolve the problem that a bed 'lacks consciousness to be asked' (ein bo da'at lisha'el)?
A. He rules that beds are always impure regardless
B. He says uncertain impurity caused by human involvement gives even vessels the status of having consciousness
C. He exempts beds from impurity entirely
D. He requires two witnesses to confirm the impurity
Correct: B — R' Yochanan's principle: when uncertain impurity comes about 'by the hand of a person' (human involvement), even an inanimate vessel on the ground is treated as having 'da'at lisha'el' — consciousness to be asked — and is deemed impure.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the objection to R' Yochanan from the baraita about a man wrapping in his cloak near pure/impure items?
A. The baraita refers to the public domain, where uncertain impurity is ruled pure
B. The baraita is rejected as non-authoritative
C. R' Yochanan only applies his rule to women, not men
D. The cloak is not considered a vessel
Correct: A — Per Rav Hoshaya's teaching: uncertain impurity in the private domain is impure, but in the public domain it is pure. The baraita's case occurs in the public domain, so items remain pure even under R' Yochanan's principle.
