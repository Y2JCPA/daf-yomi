Niddah 5: Stains, Retroactivity & Uncertain Impurity

When a woman discovers blood, what is the scope of retroactive impurity?
↓
Fixed Cycle (Veset Kavua)
Stain → retroactive impurity
Sighting at fixed time → no retroactive impurity
Time Suffices (Dayah Sha'atah)
Pregnant / nursing / elderly → impurity only from now onward
↓
What about items she handled during the retroactive period?
↓
Her Bed (Ze'eiri)
Bed impure retroactively — transmits impurity to person & garments
Items She Touched
Impure if within 24 hours or last examination
KEY PRINCIPLE When a ruling can be read as either lenient or stringent, we interpret toward stringency (chumra) in matters of ritual purity.

Interpreting the Mishna: Stringency Wins

Gemara (Question)
Why not interpret the mishna (more authoritative) as the universal view, and the baraita as only R' Dosa?
Gemara (Answer)
Since the text can be read as a leniency (all agree: fixed time = no retroactivity) or a stringency (all agree: not at fixed time = retroactive impurity)…
Gemara (Answer)
We interpret it in the way that leads to stringency — the baraita reflects the universal view, and the Rabbis add a further chumra.
PRINCIPLE When ambiguity exists, the Gemara prefers the interpretation that yields a stricter ruling in purity law.

When Does a Young Girl's "Time" Arrive?

Time Has NOT Arrived

  • Before days of na'arut (young womanhood)
  • Per R' Chanina: stains cause NO impurity at all
  • Even soiled sheets are not a concern

Time HAS Arrived

  • Days of na'arut have begun
  • Stains cause impurity from now onward
  • Treated like other women for stain law
DEFINITION A girl's "time to see" (higi'a z'man) = when the days of her na'arut (young womanhood) have arrived.

Examination Cloth Before Intercourse — Shmuel's Ruling

🧪 Woman uses an examination cloth (ed) before intercourse
↓
💡 Shmuel says: This cloth does NOT reduce the 24-hour retroactive period
↓
❓ Why not? Rav Ketina explains: She is excited and hurried (shemehuma) for intercourse
↓
⚠️ Therefore it is not considered a proper examination
↓
✅ But it does reduce the time from examination to examination (a shorter period)
↓
📏 Why? Lest you say the Sages only protect against the loss of items in a 24-hour window — the mishna teaches otherwise
RAV KETINA A woman who is mehuma (excited/hurried) for intercourse cannot perform a thorough examination — so it doesn't count for the full 24-hour reduction.

Ze'eiri: Retroactive Bed Impurity

Woman sat on a bed handling pure items, then saw blood
↓
Dayah Sha'atah
Bed is pure
(no retroactive impurity)
24-Hour Retroactivity
Bed is impure
(Ze'eiri's ruling)
↓
Impure bed transmits to a person who touches it → that person transmits to garments
ZE'EIRI The retroactive 24-hour period of a niddah renders her bed and chair impure — they transmit impurity to people, who transmit to garments.
