Testing an Unknown Stain with Seven Substances

Unknown stain found on garment
↓
Immerse garment, handle pure items
↓
Apply 7 substances in order, 3× each
↓
Did the stain disappear?
No — didn't disappear
It's a dye — pure items remain pure, no re-immersion needed
Yes — disappeared/faded
It's blood — pure items are impure, must re-immerse
Key Rule Substances must be applied in order, separately, and 3 times each — otherwise the test is invalid

Rules for Applying the Seven Substances

1️⃣ Apply each substance 3 times over the stain
↓
2️⃣ Apply them separately — not mixed together
↓
3️⃣ Apply them in the prescribed order of the Mishna
↓
❌ Wrong order → test is invalid
↓
❌ All 7 applied simultaneously → test is invalid
Bottom Line Failure to follow the exact protocol means no determination can be made about whether the stain is blood or dye

The Soap (Tzafon) Complication

Applied only 6 of the 7 substances — stain didn't disappear
↓
Then applied soap (tzafon) and stain disappeared
↓
Result: Pure items handled are impure
↓
Why? If all 7 had been applied first, perhaps the stain would have disappeared — so impurity by uncertainty
Important Soap removes both blood AND dye — so soap alone proves nothing. But the incomplete test (only 6 substances) means we cannot rule out blood

Double Washing — Does Particularity Matter?

Baraita
If one applied 7 substances and stain didn't disappear, then applied them a second time and it did disappear — items handled between washings are pure
R' Zeira
Items handled between 1st and 2nd washing: pure. Items handled after 2nd washing: impure
R' Zeira
Because by repeating the test, he showed he was particular about the stain — and then it disappeared, proving it was blood
R' Abba
Does purity really depend on whether the owner is particular? If items before the 2nd washing are pure, those after should be too!
Rav Ashi
Yes — purity does depend on particularity. Proof: R' Ḥiyya says one can apply 7 substances to certain menstrual blood, nullify it, immerse, and the garment is pure
R' Ḥiyya's Principle Even certain menstrual blood can be nullified by applying the 7 substances — proving that once the owner is no longer particular about the stain, it loses its impure status

Absorbed Impure Liquids in an Oven

Mishna (Kelim)
A chamber pot of a zav that absorbed impure liquids fell into an oven — if the oven is heated, it is impure
Reish Lakish
Only lesser impurity liquids (e.g., tears of tamei met) require heating. Greater impurity liquids (e.g., urine of a zav) make the oven impure even without heating
R' Yochanan
Both lesser and greater impurity liquids require the oven to be heated — absorbed substances don't impart impurity until they emerge
R' Yochanan
Proof: The stain test! Blood absorbed in a garment that doesn't come out after 7 substances is not impure — same principle for absorbed liquids
Reish Lakish
Blood stains are only rabbinic impurity — the Sages were lenient. Liquids of a zav are Torah impurity — different standard!
Core Dispute Does an absorbed impure substance retain its impurity status even before it emerges? R' Yochanan says no; Reish Lakish distinguishes by severity
