Niddah 13 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say the word 'diligent' (מרבה) in the Mishna applies only to women?
A. Because men are completely prohibited from self-examination for semen — even once
B. Because women need to check more often than men
C. Because 'diligent' is a term of praise only used for women
D. Because men already know their status without checking
Correct: A — The Gemara clarifies that for men, even a single self-examination for semen is prohibited due to the risk of arousal. The qualifier 'diligent' only makes sense for women, for whom frequent checking is praiseworthy.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why is a man who examines himself for zov (gonorrhea-like discharge) considered praiseworthy?
A. Because zov is more dangerous than semen
B. Because he must count discharges — 2 means impurity, 3 means he must bring a korban
C. Because zov examination cannot cause arousal
D. Because the Torah explicitly commands zov examination
Correct: B — The practical halakhic consequence of counting zov discharges (impurity at 2, obligation to bring an offering at 3) makes careful examination important and praiseworthy, despite the general prohibition on male self-examination.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the core disagreement between Abaye and Rava regarding the priest eating teruma?
A. Whether the priest may eat teruma at all during quaking
B. Whether a soft cloth may be used to hold the penis once semen is already being emitted
C. Whether the emission renders the teruma impure retroactively
D. Whether quaking limbs are a definitive sign of impending emission
Correct: B — Abaye permits only a coarse cloth (worried soft cloth might increase emission). Rava permits even a soft cloth, reasoning that once semen is uprooted from the body, subsequent contact won't increase it — especially when limbs are quaking.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav, why does one who intentionally causes an erection deserve ostracism rather than simply being told it is 'prohibited'?
A. Because the Torah mandates ostracism for this specific act
B. Because he actively arouses the evil inclination upon himself, leading to escalating sins
C. Because ostracism is the standard penalty for all sexual transgressions
D. Because 'prohibited' would imply there is no punishment
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that ostracism (rather than a simple prohibition) is fitting because the person deliberately invites the evil inclination, which R' Ami describes as an escalating force — today one sin, tomorrow another, eventually idolatry.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara ultimately explain that 'those who play with children' delay the Messiah?
A. They teach children bad habits that persist into adulthood
B. They distract children from Torah study
C. They marry minor girls who cannot bear children, wasting seed and preventing souls from being born
D. They corrupt the next generation's morals, making them unworthy of redemption
Correct: C — After rejecting homosexuality (punished by stoning) and general seed-wasting (punished by flood), the Gemara concludes it refers to marrying girls too young to bear children. Per R' Yosei, Mashiach cannot come until all destined souls are born — wasting seed delays this.
