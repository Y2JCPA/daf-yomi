Niddah 18 — Majority as Certainty

אמר ר׳ יוחנן: בשלשה מקומות הלכו חכמים אחר הרוב ועשאום כוודאי
CORE THEME R' Yoḥanan identifies three cases where the Sages treated an uncertain situation as a certainty based on the majority:

1️⃣ The Source — blood from the vestibule inward is presumed from the uterus
2️⃣ The Afterbirth (שִׁלְיָא) — an afterbirth implies an offspring was present
3️⃣ A Shaped Limb — a recognizable hand or foot implies a complete fetus

The Gemara then challenges: why only three? What about other majority-based rulings? And what happens when a majority clashes with a chazakah (presumptive status)?
3 / 9

Reconciling R' Ḥiyya and Rav Ketina

Rav Huna
Blood found in the inner section (vestibule inward) is definitely impure. Blood in the outer section is impure only due to uncertainty.
R' Ḥiyya
The blood is definitely impure — I speak of blood found from the vestibule and inward.
Rav Ketina
The blood is impure only due to uncertainty (safek) — I speak of blood found from the vestibule and outward.
Gemara
According to Rav Huna, no contradiction! R' Ḥiyya and Rav Ketina refer to different locations.
RESOLUTION Another distinction: R' Ḥiyya speaks of blood on the floor of the corridor (presumed from uterus); Rav Ketina speaks of blood on the roof (uncertain source).

R' Yoḥanan's Three Certainties from Majority

CaseScenarioReasoning
1️⃣ The Source (המקור)Blood found from vestibule inwardMajority of blood there comes from the uterus → definitely impure
2️⃣ Afterbirth (שִׁלְיָא)Afterbirth found in the house after miscarriageThere is no afterbirth without an offspring → house impure with corpse-impurity
3️⃣ Shaped Limb (אֵבֶר מְרֻקָּם)Miscarried a recognizable hand or footMost women bear fully formed fetuses → mother has childbirth impurity
PRINCIPLE In these three cases, the Sages treated rov (majority) as equivalent to vadai (certainty), not merely as a safek.

Nine Stores: Fixed (קבוע) vs. Separated (פריש)

Fixed in Place (קבוע)

  • Person bought meat from one of the stores
  • 9 kosher stores + 1 non-kosher store
  • Treated as 50/50 — does NOT follow majority
  • The meat is prohibited

Separated (פריש)

  • Meat found on the street outside
  • Same 9 kosher + 1 non-kosher stores
  • Item separated from its place → follow the majority
  • The meat is permitted
KEY PRINCIPLE כל קבוע כמחצה על מחצה דמי — Any item fixed in its place is treated as equally balanced (50/50), and we do not follow the majority.
7 / 9

Majority vs. Presumptive Status (Chazakah)

R' Meir
Most children handle items within reach, but a minority do not. Append that minority to the dough's chazakah of purity → the dough is pure.
Rabbis
When following a majority, the minority is as if it doesn't exist. So it's majority (impure) vs. chazakah (pure) — majority wins. Dough is impure.
Reish Lakish / R' Oshaya
This presumption about children is strong enough that teruma may be burned based on it.
R' Yoḥanan
When majority conflicts with chazakah, it's not strong enough to burn teruma. The dough is set aside — neither eaten nor burned.
IMPLICATION R' Yoḥanan's three certainties specifically exclude cases where a majority is opposed by a chazakah — in those cases, the uncertainty is not treated as certainty.
