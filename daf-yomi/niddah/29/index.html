When Is a Fetus Considered Born?

Mishna
If a fetus emerged in pieces or reversed (feet first), it is considered born when most of its limbs emerge.
R' Elazar
Even if the head is among the limbs that emerged, as long as the majority did not emerge, the fetus is not considered born.
R' Yochanan
If the head is among the emerged limbs, the fetus is considered born — the head alone is sufficient to exempt from primogeniture.
Shmuel
With twins, emergence of one's head does not exempt the other from primogeniture — but only for a non-viable newborn (nefel).
Gemara
Do R' Elazar and R' Yochanan disagree about Shmuel's ruling, or is their dispute limited to a fetus emerging in pieces?
Core Issue Does the emergence of a fetus's head constitute birth even when the fetus is dismembered or non-viable?

R' Elazar vs R' Yochanan: Head Among Pieces

R' Elazar
If the fetus emerged in pieces, even if the head is among them, it is not considered born until the majority of limbs emerge.
R' Yochanan
Even in pieces, if the head is among the emerged limbs, the fetus is considered born — the head exempts from primogeniture (bekhor).
Gemara
Perhaps both agree a whole non-viable newborn's head = birth. They disagree only about a dismembered fetus.
Rav Pappa
This Amoraic dispute parallels a Tannaitic dispute: the first Tanna vs R' Yosei in a baraita about emergence "in its proper manner."
Practical Impact This dispute affects both tum'at leidah (childbirth impurity) and pidyon haben (primogeniture obligations for twins).

Shmuel's Position on Twins & Primogeniture

Non-Viable Newborn (Nefel)

  • Head of first twin emerges then retreats
  • Does NOT exempt second twin from bekhor status
  • Shmuel: emergence of nefel's head is not considered birth

Viable Offspring

  • Head of first twin emerges then retreats
  • DOES exempt second twin from bekhor status
  • Even Shmuel agrees: head emergence = birth for viable babies
The Question R' Elazar may agree with Shmuel (head of nefel ≠ birth), while R' Yochanan may disagree — or they may dispute only the dismembered case.

How Many Immersions Are Required?

Beit Shammai

  • 95 immersions required
  • Must immerse for every possible transition point
  • Accounts for all uncertainties across the alternating weeks

Beit Hillel

  • 35 immersions required
  • Reduced calculation of transition uncertainties
  • Still accounts for multiple possible birth timings

R' Yosei b. R' Yehuda

  • Only 1 immersion required
  • Sufficient to immerse at end of entire required period
  • Most lenient position
Note The dispute centers on how many uncertain status-transitions require their own immersion vs. a single immersion at the end.
