Niddah 29 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, when a fetus emerges reversed (feet first), when is it considered born?
A. When any limb emerges
B. When the head emerges
C. When most of its limbs emerge
D. When the entire body emerges
Correct: C — The Mishna teaches that for a reversed or dismembered fetus, birth status is established when the majority of limbs have emerged.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the key disagreement between R' Elazar and R' Yochanan?
A. Whether reversed birth counts as birth at all
B. Whether the head among emerged pieces constitutes birth
C. Whether twins can both be firstborns
D. Whether a non-viable newborn has any legal status
Correct: B — R' Yochanan holds that if the head is among the emerged limbs (even in pieces), the fetus is considered born; R' Elazar disagrees and requires the majority of limbs.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Shmuel, in what case does a twin's head emerging NOT exempt the other twin from primogeniture?
A. When both twins are female
B. When the first twin is a non-viable newborn (nefel)
C. When the birth is reversed
D. When the mother is a zava
Correct: B — Shmuel distinguishes between viable and non-viable newborns: the head emergence of a nefel does not constitute birth for primogeniture purposes.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why can't the returning woman have intercourse during the fourth week, when she is experiencing bleeding that should be 'pure blood' (dam tohar)?
A. Because all blood after childbirth is impure
B. Because she might have given birth long before returning, and her purity period already ended
C. Because Beit Shammai forbids all intercourse for 95 days
D. Because bleeding always creates niddah status
Correct: B — Since we don't know when she gave birth, her birth may have been long before her arrival, meaning her period of pure blood (dam tohar) may have already passed before the fourth week.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does R' Shimon prohibit the woman from having intercourse on the 21st day (end of the possible zava period)?
A. Because she hasn't immersed yet
B. Because she might bleed later that day, retroactively nullifying her seven clean days
C. Because the 21st day might still be within her childbirth impurity
D. Because R' Shimon requires 30 clean days for a zava
Correct: B — R' Shimon is concerned that if she has intercourse on the 7th clean day after immersion and then bleeds, it retroactively invalidates all seven clean days, meaning she was impure during intercourse.
