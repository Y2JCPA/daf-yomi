Niddah 15 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Sages of Yavne, what defines the 'after time passed' period?
A. Time to extend her hand under the pillow and retrieve a cloth
B. Time to descend from the bed and rinse her face
C. Time to walk to the mikveh and immerse
D. Time equivalent to 24 hours
Correct: B — The Sages of Yavne defined 'after time passed' as the time it takes for the woman to descend from the bed and rinse her face. This is the standard cited in the baraita.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does R' Yehuda b. R' Yoḥanan b. Zakkai say the husband is completely pure after the time window?
A. He holds the husband never becomes impure from intercourse
B. He follows Shammai — impurity begins only when she notices blood
C. He holds blood stains are not a source of impurity
D. He relies on a presumption that she immersed
Correct: B — R' Yehuda b. R' Yoḥanan b. Zakkai holds like Shammai's principle of דיה שעתה — a woman's 'time is sufficient,' meaning impurity is not applied retroactively before she noticed bleeding.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the challenge from the ḥaver's storehouse against the rule that uncertainty cannot override certainty?
A. It concedes the rule has exceptions for produce
B. It says the storehouse case involves certainty vs. certainty — a ḥaver certainly tithes
C. It distinguishes between food impurity and bodily impurity
D. It says the rule only applies to women, not produce
Correct: B — The Gemara offers two resolutions: (1) Rav Ḥanina Ḥoza'a says it's certainty vs. certainty since a ḥaver certainly tithes, or (2) R' Oshaya says both factors are uncertain since the grain may have been exempt from tithing.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Yoḥanan, when may a husband presume his wife immersed without asking her?
A. Whenever she has a fixed cycle
B. When he was away for 7+ days after her expected period and enough time elapsed for immersion
C. When she is a young girl who is embarrassed to be asked
D. When she found a blood stain but not menstrual blood
Correct: B — R' Yoḥanan's ruling applies when the husband was away during her expected period and enough time passed for her to both experience bleeding and immerse. This only works when the bleeding itself is uncertain.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the dispute between Rav Huna and Rabba bar bar Ḥana regarding a woman with a veset of days alone?
A. Whether she needs to examine herself at all
B. Whether the concern at her expected period is Torah law or rabbinic law
C. Whether she transmits impurity retroactively
D. Whether her husband must bring a guilt offering
Correct: B — Rav Huna holds the concern is Torah-level (requiring examination before intercourse), while Rabba bar bar Ḥana holds it's rabbinic (so she retains a presumption of purity even without examining).
