Defining 'After Time Passed' (אחר זמן)

Sages of Usha
We don't accept R' Akiva's view on retroactive impurity. What exactly is 'after time passed'?
R' Eliezer b. R' Tzadok
The Sages of Yavne explained: the time it takes to descend from the bed and rinse her face (פניה).
Rav Ḥisda
The critical period is the time to extend her hand under the pillow, retrieve a cloth, and examine herself.
Rav Ashi
If a cloth is already in her hand, the period is the time to descend and clean her pubic area with it.
Resolution The baraita poses a difficulty to Rav Ashi — if the cloth is in her hand, why does the baraita use the longer 'descend and rinse' timeframe without distinguishing cases?

Blood Stain (כתם) — Retroactive Impurity?

Rav

  • A woman who sees a blood stain renders the man impure retroactively
  • Follows R' Meir's opinion
  • Blood stain means impurity applied to past contact

Shmuel

  • She does NOT render him impure retroactively
  • Only future intercourse is affected
  • Follows the Rabbis' opinion
  • Impurity applies from the stain onward
Context Both agree the Rabbis concede to R' Akiva that a blood stain (כתם) transmits 7-day impurity — they disagree only on whether it applies retroactively.

Husband Away — R' Yoḥanan's Presumption of Immersion

📅 Woman has a fixed menstrual cycle (veset kavua)
↓
✈️ Husband is away for 7+ days after her expected period
↓
🧮 Husband calculates: enough time for her to see blood AND immerse?
↓
🛁 If yes — presume she immersed; intercourse permitted without asking
↓
⚠️ Exception: This only applies when it's uncertain whether she saw blood — not when it's certain
Young Girl? R' Abba: R' Yoḥanan's rule applies even to a young girl who might be embarrassed to immerse — because the bleeding itself is uncertain, so we have doubt upon doubt (ספק ספיקא).
