QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara's resolution, why does Levi (two sources) still require 3 periods of no bleeding for pregnant/nursing women?
A. Because blood during purity days is still impure according to Levi
B. The 3 periods ensure the blood stopped briefly after impurity days, and purity days then count even with bleeding
C. Levi actually does not require 3 periods — the Gemara rejects the baraita
D. Because the two sources can switch and become unreliable
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that for Levi, the blood need only stop briefly after impurity days. Once she enters purity days, those days count toward the 3 periods even if she bleeds, since that blood comes from the pure source.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rava's clarification of the mishna's statement that 'a pregnant woman who experiences labor pains is a menstruating woman'?
A. It applies to all labor bleeding regardless of timing
B. It applies only when labor occurs during the 11 days of ziva
C. It applies only when labor occurs during menstruation days; during ziva days she is pure
D. It applies only to the first pregnancy
Correct: C — Rava explains the mishna means: during menstruation days she is a niddah; during the 11 ziva days, labor blood is pure. A baraita is cited to support this reading.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the key principle (klal) for determining if a woman who bled during ziva days gives birth as a zava?
A. Whether she bled for exactly 3 consecutive days
B. Whether her state adjacent to giving birth was labor pains or resting from labor
C. Whether she immersed before giving birth
D. Whether the bleeding occurred within two weeks of birth
Correct: B — The baraita states the principle: if she was experiencing labor pains adjacent to birth, she is not a zava; if she was resting from labor adjacent to birth, she gives birth as a zava.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Shmuel's view on labor bleeding during ziva days differ from Rav's?
A. Shmuel says she is completely pure; Rav says she is a niddah for one day
B. Shmuel requires a clean day by rabbinic decree; Rav says she is impure for one day but immerses that evening
C. Shmuel says she is a full zava; Rav says she is a lesser zava
D. They agree — both say she observes one clean day
Correct: B — Rav says she is a menstruating woman for one day (immerses in the evening). Shmuel imposes a rabbinic decree to observe one clean day, out of concern she might rest from labor and actually become a lesser zava.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Ḥananya's unique ruling about the third day of bleeding during ziva?
A. Even one hour of labor on the third day means she is not a zava
B. The third day must have labor pains for the full day to avoid zava status
C. The third day is irrelevant — only the first two days matter
D. She needs labor pains for at least half the third day
Correct: A — Ḥananya holds that even if she rested for nearly the entire third day, one hour of labor pains on that day is enough — she is not a zava. Resting on the third day only counts if she rested the entire day.
