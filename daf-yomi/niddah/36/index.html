Rav vs. Levi: Pregnant & Nursing Women

Challenge
According to Levi (two sources), why require 3 periods of no bleeding for pregnant/nursing women? Blood after impurity days comes from the pure source!
Rav's View
With one source, 3 periods of cessation are needed to establish that her menstrual emissions have truly stopped.
Resolution for Levi
She needs blood to stop for any amount of time after impurity days. Then her days of purity count toward the 3 periods even if she bleeds during them.
Key Distinction Both Rav and Levi agree on the 3-period requirement — they differ on what counts as a valid 'clean' period.

Shammai vs. Hillel: Blood After Purity

Beit Shammai

  • Generally: impure only from time of sighting (dayah sha'atah)
  • Blood during days of purity: AGREE — impure only from sighting
  • She was still considered one whose emissions ceased

Beit Hillel

  • Generally: retroactive impurity from last clean examination
  • Blood during days of purity: AGREE — impure only from sighting
  • No 24-hour retroactive impurity since prior time was purity days
Rare Agreement Even Hillel agrees there's no retroactive impurity here — since the 24 hours prior fall within her days of purity, retroactive impurity cannot apply.

The Mishna: Labor Pains During Ziva Days

🤰 Pregnant woman experiences labor pains with bleeding during 11 days of ziva
↓
⏸️ Labor pains cease for 24 hours but bleeding continues
↓
👶 She then gives birth
↓
⚠️ The 24-hour rest proves the bleeding was NOT from labor
↓
📜 She is classified as a yoledet b'zov — one who gives birth as a zava
R' Eliezer vs. R' Yehoshua R' Eliezer: any 24-hour rest suffices. R' Yehoshua: must be a full night-and-day period (like Shabbat eve and its day), and resting from pain — not necessarily from blood flow.

Labor During Ziva Days: Three Amoraic Views

Rav
She is a menstruating woman for one day — forbidden to her husband that day, immerses in the evening.
Shmuel
She must observe one clean day by rabbinic decree, lest she rest from labor and actually become a lesser zava.
R' Yitzḥak
She is completely pure — labor blood during ziva days is nothing at all.
Rava
The mishna means: during menstruation days = niddah; during ziva days = pure. A baraita confirms this.
Resolution Rava clarifies the mishna applies only to labor during menstruation days. Labor during ziva days produces pure blood.
