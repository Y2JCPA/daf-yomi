QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Mishna specifically mention ḥalitza when it already says an adult girl is obligated in all mitzvot?
A. To exclude R' Yosei, who holds even a minor girl can perform ḥalitza
B. To teach that ḥalitza requires a beit din
C. To emphasize that women are obligated in ḥalitza even though it's time-bound
D. To teach that ḥalitza takes priority over yibum
Correct: A — R' Yosei held that 'man' (ish) is specified in the ḥalitza passage but not the woman's age, so even a minor could do ḥalitza. The Mishna's explicit mention excludes his view — the woman is juxtaposed to the man and must also be an adult.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Yehuda, when does a girl lose the right to perform mi'un (refusal)?
A. As soon as she grows two pubic hairs
B. When the black pubic hair becomes preponderant over the white skin
C. When the nipple is surrounded by hair
D. When she reaches age twelve
Correct: B — R' Yehuda holds that mi'un continues past two hairs — until the black hair is preponderant (שירבה השחור). R' Elazar rules the halakha follows R' Yehuda, but even R' Yehuda concedes that intercourse after two hairs ends the right to refuse.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why can't mi'un (refusal) undo a marriage after intercourse following the growth of two hairs?
A. Because the husband has acquired property rights
B. Because mi'un is rabbinic and cannot override Torah-level betrothal created by intercourse
C. Because the girl has already been counted in a census
D. Because a beit din must approve all refusals after puberty
Correct: B — Mi'un is a rabbinic enactment effective only against rabbinic-level marriage (a minor married by her mother/brothers). Once she grows two hairs and has intercourse, the betrothal becomes Torah-level (קידושין דאורייתא), which mi'un cannot dissolve.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the halakha regarding the minimum length of the two hairs, given the three opinions of R' Yishmael, R' Eliezer, and R' Akiva?
A. The halakha follows R' Akiva exclusively
B. The halakha follows R' Yishmael as the most lenient
C. The halakha follows all three to be stringent — whichever yields the stricter result applies
D. The halakha is that any visible hair suffices
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda citing Mar Ukva rules that we follow all three opinions le-ḥumra (to be stringent). In any given situation, whichever standard produces the more stringent result is the one applied.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Ḥanina ben Antigonus (identified as 'the Rabbis'), when CAN stains lead to ziva?
A. Whenever a stain is found on any garment
B. When stains are found on three different pre-checked garments, or two days of actual flow plus one day with a stain
C. Only when the stain is larger than a gris (split bean)
D. Stains can never lead to ziva under any circumstances
Correct: B — R' Ḥanina ben Antigonus holds that stains alone don't create zava concern, but they can contribute to ziva in specific cases: stains on three separately checked garments, or two days of actual blood flow combined with a stain on the third day.
