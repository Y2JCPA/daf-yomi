Niddah 33: Levels of Impurity — Bedding, Bearing & Uncertain Status

Core Question: What level of impurity does bedding above a zav and bedding of a bo'el niddah carry?
↓
Verses in Leviticus 15 separate these cases from severe impurity
↓
Lesser Impurity
Imparts impurity only to food & drink, not to people or garments
Severe Impurity
Imparts impurity to people who then impart to garments they wear
Key Theme This daf traces how Scripture carefully calibrates degrees of impurity through precise language, and applies this to the uncertain status of Samaritan garments.

Deriving the Impurity of Bedding Above a Zav

Verse: "Whoever touches anything that was under him" (Lev. 15:10)
↓
Bedding beneath a zav is already known from "whoever touches his bed" (Lev. 15:5) — so what does this verse add?
↓
It must refer to bedding above a zav (moshav she'al gabei zav)
↓
"Shall be impure" — lesser impurity: impurity to food & drink only
↓
Objection: Perhaps it still imparts impurity to people/garments, just not person→garment chain?
↓
Rejected: "shall be impure" indicates lesser status specifically
Borne Item (Nisa) The verse also teaches about an item borne by a zav — derived from reading vehanoseh as vehanisa (the borne one).

The Bo'el Niddah: Between Generalization & Detail

Baraita
"Her impurity be upon him" — generalization: he imparts impurity like her to people and earthenware
Baraita
"Every bed upon which he lies shall be impure" — detail: his bedding carries only lesser impurity
Gemara
But this looks like a klal u'frat (generalization and detail)! The general includes only what the detail specifies!
Abaye
"He shall be impure seven days" interrupts the klal and frat — they are distant from each other, so the rule doesn't apply
Rava
Even distant klal u'frat can be applied. But here "ve'khol" (and every) is an amplification (ribbui), not a detail at all!
Practical Result The bedding of a bo'el niddah imparts impurity only to food and drink — lesser impurity — not to people or garments.

Impurity Levels: Menstruating Woman vs. Bo'el Niddah

Niddah (Menstruating Woman)

  • Impure for 7 days from onset
  • Her bedding imparts severe impurity
  • Bedding → person → garments chain
  • Imparts impurity to people & earthenware

Bo'el Niddah (Her Partner)

  • Impure for 7 days from intercourse
  • His bedding imparts lesser impurity
  • Bedding → food & drink only
  • He personally imparts impurity like her to people & earthenware
Key Distinction The man himself is comparable to a niddah, but his bedding is downgraded to lesser impurity by "every bed upon which he lies shall be impure."

Zava Who Discharges Semen — Does She Negate Her Count?

Rami bar Ḥama
Dilemma: A zava counting 7 clean days discharges semen. Does this negate her count? Is it because she is a zava (negates) or because she is touching semen (does not negate)?
Rava
"Commensurate with his sharpness is his error!" Even if it negates — how much? All 7 days is too much (a zav only negates 1 day). One day is impossible since a zava needs 7 consecutive clean days!
Gemara
Refutation: A zav also needs 7 consecutive days! Yet he negates only 1 day — because the verse means no ziva impurity between them, not semen impurity.
Gemara
Same logic for a zava: "after all of them" means no ziva separating — semen is excluded. So she might negate only 1 day.
Conclusion
Rava's dismissal is rejected. Rami bar Ḥama's dilemma stands unresolved (teiku).
Principle The requirement for 7 consecutive clean days (Lev. 15:28) means no ziva impurity may intervene — but semen impurity may be a different category entirely.

Burning Teruma for Uncertain Impurity: The Contradiction

SourceRuling on TerumaContext
Our Mishna (Niddah 33)Do NOT burn terumaGarments upon which a Samaritan lay — uncertain bo'el niddah impurity
Teharot 4:5DO burn terumaGarments of an am ha'aretz — uncertain niddah sat on them
Rav Pappa's answerSamaritan = ḥaverMore careful about purity than am ha'aretz, so less concern
Rav Shmuel's rejectionIrrelevant distinctionIf he's called bo'el niddah, ḥaver status doesn't help!
Difficulty Rav Pappa could not resolve the contradiction to Rav Shmuel's satisfaction and left embarrassed — a rare and striking Talmudic moment.
