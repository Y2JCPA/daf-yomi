Niddah 20: The Science of Blood Examination

חמשה דמים טמאין באשה
CORE THEME This daf explores the precise color standards for determining whether blood renders a woman ritually impure. The Sages debate how to define the red and black colors mentioned in the Mishnah, what background cloth to use for examination, and whether individual Sages possess the expertise and humility required to render such rulings.
3 / 9

Attributing Red Stains: Eye Salve & Sycamore Sap

Gemara (Question)
R' Meir attributed a stain to eye salve (bekilor) and R' Yehuda HaNasi to sycamore sap. Doesn't this prove impure blood can be many shades of red?
Gemara (Answer)
No — they attributed those stains because they resembled the specific blood types listed in the Mishnah, not because any shade of red qualifies.
Ameimar
At the bloodletter: 'The red of the Mishnah is like this first horn of blood.' The second horn's color had already changed.
Rav Ashi
Anyone like me who cannot distinguish between the first and second horn should not examine blood at all.
TAKEAWAY Distinguishing shades of impure blood requires extraordinary precision — even great Sages acknowledged their limitations.

Defining 'Black' Blood: Shades of Impurity & Purity

SageComparisonStatus
Rabba bar Rav HunaḤeret = dried ink (lower, darker portion of inkwell)Standard
Rav Yehuda / ShmuelBlack wax (kekir), black ink, black grapeImpure ⛔
R' ElazarBlack olive, black tar, black ravenPure ✅
UllaGarments of the inhabitants of Siva'aImpure ⛔
R' YoḥananCloths of bath attendants (olyarin) from overseasImpure ⛔
KEY DISTINCTION Black deeper than ink = impure. Black lighter than ink = pure. The Mishnah's ḥeret refers specifically to the dark sediment at the bottom of the inkwell.

Should a Sage Examine Blood? Attitudes of Humility

Refuse to Examine

  • R' Yoḥanan: R' Ḥanina's wisdom intimidates me — our rulings always contradicted
  • R' Zeira: I don't even understand Babylonians' nature — how can I judge blood?
  • Ulla in Pumbedita: If R' Elazar deferred in R' Yehuda's locale, so should I

Choose to Examine

  • R' Elazar: R' Ḥanina's humility inspires me — if he risks uncertainty, shouldn't I?
  • R' Ḥanina: Examines without water, relying on superior expertise
  • Rava: Identifies blood by smell — confirmed by Ifera Hurmiz's 60 samples
INSIGHT The same reality — R' Ḥanina's greatness — led R' Yoḥanan to withdraw and R' Elazar to step forward. Humility can lead to opposite conclusions.

Rava and Ifera Hurmiz: The 60 Blood Samples

👑 Ifera Hurmiz (mother of King Shapur) sends blood to Rava for examination
↓
👃 Rava smells the blood and declares: 'This is blood of desire' — not menstrual blood
↓
🤔 King Shapur dismisses it: 'Like a blind man escaping a chimney — a lucky guess'
↓
🧪 She sends 60 different blood samples — Rava correctly identifies every one
↓
🪮 The last sample was louse blood — Rava didn't know it, but by divine guidance sent her a lice comb
↓
💬 Ifera Hurmiz exclaims: 'Jews, you must dwell in the chamber of people's hearts!'
NOTE This story illustrates the extraordinary expertise required for blood examination — and the divine assistance (siyata d'shmaya) that accompanies it.
