Can Jumping Establish a Menstrual Cycle?

Rav Huna (Version 1)
If she jumped and saw blood three times, she has established a veset kavua (fixed cycle).
Gemara
A cycle of days alone? But on days she didn't jump, she didn't bleed!
Gemara
A cycle of jumps alone? But a baraita says accidental patterns don't create cycles!
Resolution
She establishes a cycle for the combination: jumping on specific days.
Key Principle Neither the day alone nor the action alone creates a veset — only the combination of both can establish a fixed pattern.

Rav Ashi's Sunday Case

She jumps on Sunday → sees blood ✓
Again jumps on Sunday → sees blood ✓
↓
Next week: jumps on Shabbat → no blood ✗
But on Sunday (without jumping) → sees blood!
↓
You might think
The day (Sunday) caused menstruation — she has a cycle for Sundays
Baraita teaches
Shabbat's jump caused delayed bleeding — the jump's effect hadn't yet arrived
Rav Ashi The delayed onset doesn't prove the day is the cause — the jump from the previous day can still be responsible.
5 / 9

Rav Huna: Two Versions

Version 1

  • Jumped 3× and saw blood each time
  • Establishes veset for days AND jumps combined
  • Neither days nor jumps alone suffice
  • Accidental pattern + timing = valid cycle

Version 2

  • Same case: jumped 3× and saw blood
  • Establishes veset for days only, NOT jumps
  • Sunday case reveals day is the true cause
  • Jumping was coincidental, not causal
Practical Difference In Version 1, she must worry about menstruation when she jumps on that day. In Version 2, she must anticipate menstruation on that day regardless of jumping.

One Source or Two Sources of Blood?

Levi
There are two distinct sources — one for birth blood, one for niddah blood. A cycle from one source doesn't transfer to the other.
Rav
Birth blood and niddah blood both come from one source.
Gemara
According to Levi, it's clear why a woman in yemei tohar doesn't examine — different source. But according to Rav?
Resolution
Even with one source, a woman does not establish a cycle from her days of purity that transfers to her days of impurity.
Bottom Line Whether blood comes from one source or two, patterns during yemei tohar (days of purity) do not create binding cycles for days of impurity.
