Niddah 56: Fluids, Carcasses & Retroactive Impurity

Which fluids of a zav carry severe tum'ah?
↓
Included
Urine & saliva — gather, release, reabsorb
Excluded
Blood (no gathering) & milk (no reabsorption)
↓
Dried sheretz with intact skeleton — still tamei (Reish Lakish)
↓
Retroactive impurity: stain on robe / sheretz in alleyway
↓
Presumption
Jews examine when sweeping / laundering (R' Meir)
Remedy

Core Theme The Gemara establishes criteria for which bodily fluids carry severe impurity, and how retroactive impurity operates for discovered stains and carcasses.

Which Fluids of a Zav Are Severely Impure?

Gemara
A zav's urine comes from the same place as ziva — shouldn't it be severely impure by logic alone?
Baraita
Blood from the penis also comes from a place of impurity, yet it is tahor — so the logical inference fails.
Torah
"And this shall be his impurity" (Lev. 15:3) — "and this" includes urine as severely impure.
Torah
"His issue — it is impure" (Lev. 15:2) — "it" excludes blood from the penis.
Gemara
But why not reverse the derivations — include blood and exclude urine?
Key Verse Two textual markers — "and this" (amplification) and "it" (exclusion) — together define which zav fluids carry severe tum'ah.

R' Yochanan's Criterion: The Saliva Test

Which fluids carry severe impurity like saliva?
↓
R' Yochanan (in name of R' Shimon b. Yochai):
Three conditions — like saliva
↓
✓ Gathers
Fluid accumulates before emission
✓ Emitted
Comes out of the body
✓ Reabsorbed
If not emitted, body reabsorbs it
↓
Apply to each fluid:
↓
Urine ✓
Gathers, emitted, reabsorbed → severely impure
Blood ✗
Does NOT gather first → pure
Milk ✗
Gathers, emitted, but NOT reabsorbed → only liquid impurity
R' Shimon b. Yochai The paradigm fluid is saliva: it gathers in the mouth, is expelled, and if not expelled it is swallowed back. Only fluids matching all three traits carry severe tum'ah.

Dried Sheretz: Intact vs. Partial Skeleton

Skeleton Intact

  • Reish Lakish: Still transmits tum'ah
  • Even though the flesh has dried
  • The intact bone structure preserves its status as a complete creature
  • Rava: Applies to Mechoza lizards

Skeleton Partial

  • Mishna's rule: dry sheretz is tahor
  • R' Zeira: This refers to partial bones
  • Without complete structure it loses identity
  • Derived from "them" vs. "of them" (Lev. 11:31-32)
Reconciliation R' Zeira resolves Reish Lakish with the Mishna: "moist = even partial" transmits impurity; "dry = only if fully intact skeleton."

Retroactive Impurity: Two Parallel Cases

Sheretz in AlleywayBlood Stain on Robe
TriggerCreeping animal carcass foundBlood stain discovered
Retroactive from…Last examination or sweepingLast examination or laundering
Dilemma"Examined" vs. "thoroughly swept"?"Examined" vs. "thoroughly laundered"?
Practical differenceFound in a hole (swept but not examined)Found on robe's folded side
Resolution (R' Meir)Jews examine when sweepingWomen examine when laundering
ConclusionPresumptive status = examinedPresumptive status = examined
R' Meir's Principle There is a presumption (chazakah) that Jewish people examine their spaces and garments at the time of cleaning — sweeping includes checking, laundering includes inspecting.

R' Acha's Remedy: Re-Launder to Date the Stain

👗 Blood stain found on robe after laundering
↓
❓ Was the stain there before or after the previous laundering?
↓
🧼 Launder the robe a second time
↓
👀 Observe: did the stain's appearance change?
↓
✅ Changed → stain is NEW (post-laundering) — earlier items remain pure
↓
❌ Unchanged → stain is OLD (pre-laundering) — earlier items are impure
Rebbi's Alternative Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi says you can tell by inspection alone: a new stain penetrates (makdir) the fabric; an old stain forms a crust (maglid) that can be scraped off.
