Deuteronomy 21:5 juxtaposes disputes and nega'im
↓
Nega'im → Disputes
• Viewed only by day → judge by day
• Viewed by sighted priests → no blind judges
Disputes → Nega'im
• No relatives as judges → no relative priests viewing nega'im

Do nega'im require 3 priests like 3 judges?
↓
❌ No — 'Brought to Aaron or one of his sons' (Lev. 13:2)
Hekesh The juxtaposition (hekesh) transfers rules bidirectionally — but the Torah explicitly limits priestly viewing to even a single priest.

The Blind Judge in R' Yochanan's Neighborhood

Gemara
A blind man near R' Yochanan judged cases, and R' Yochanan said nothing.
Gemara
But R' Yochanan himself said one blind in one eye is unfit to judge! (from the nega'im comparison)
R' Yochanan
The Sanhedrin mishna says monetary cases may conclude at night — unlike nega'im. So the comparison breaks down.
Gemara
Why prefer the Sanhedrin mishna over this one?
Answer 1
Sanhedrin's mishna reflects many Sages; this one is only R' Meir.
Answer 2
Sanhedrin teaches this in context of the halakhot of judges — it is the primary source.
Principle When two unattributed mishnayot conflict, prefer the one representing many Sages or the one taught in its primary context.

Mishna: Overlapping Obligations

ObligationImplies...But Not Vice Versa — Adds:
Tithes (Ma'aser)Susceptibility to food impurity (tumat okhalin)Meat, fish, eggs — impure but no tithes
Pe'a (corner of field)Obligation to titheFigs & vegetables — tithes but no pe'a
Pe'a Criteria Food + protected + grows from earth + gathered as one + stored for preservation. Figs fail 'gathered as one' (harvested over time).

Does Intent While Attached/Alive Count?

R' Yochanan
Intent to designate produce while attached to the ground is not considered intent for impurity.
Rava
Proof: A kosher bird carcass requires new intent even if one intended to eat it while alive.
R' Zeira
That's a bird that fell from a height — it wasn't before us alive. Had there been prior intent, it would suffice!
Abaye
The chicken of Yavne was present while alive, yet needed new intent as a carcass!
R' Zeira
That was a 'wild' chicken — one raised in the wild, not before the Sages while alive.
Machshava The core dispute: Does a change in status (alive→dead, attached→detached) nullify prior intent regarding food impurity?

Rav Pappa vs. Mareimar: The Marsh Hen

Rav Pappa

  • Marsh rooster (תרנגול דאגמא) — PROHIBITED (non-kosher)
  • Marsh hen (תרנגולת דאגמא) — PERMITTED (kosher)
  • Mnemonic: 'Ammonite man' excluded, not Ammonite woman
  • The chicken of Yavne was a marsh hen — no prior intent possible

Mareimar

  • Marsh hen is PROHIBITED
  • Reason: It was seen mauling and eating prey
  • A bird of prey (dores) is a sign of being non-kosher
  • This is the bird called geiruta
Siman Rav Pappa's mnemonic: Just as 'An Ammonite shall not enter the assembly' excludes only the male, so the male marsh bird is prohibited but the female is permitted.
