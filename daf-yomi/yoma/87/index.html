🌟 Righteous vs. Wicked: Generational Impact

Righteous ✅

  • Accumulate merit for themselves
  • Merit extends to children and grandchildren
  • Continues until the end of all generations
  • Example: Aaron's sons deserved ordination as Levites due to his merit
  • "Fortunate are the righteous!"

Wicked ❌

  • Render themselves liable for punishment
  • Liability extends to children and grandchildren
  • Continues until the end of all generations
  • Example: Canaan's children lost ordination they otherwise deserved
  • "Woe to the wicked!"

🕍 Does Yom Kippur Atone for Sins Against a Person?

Rav Yosef bar Ḥavu
The mishna says Yom Kippur doesn't atone for sins between people — but I Samuel 2:25 says God judges between men! Isn't that a contradiction?
Rabbi Abbahu
No contradiction. The mishna means Yom Kippur cannot atone until the offender has first appeased the victim. Once the victim forgives, God can then complete the atonement.
Rav Yosef bar Ḥavu
But 'who shall entreat for him?' implies no intercession is possible at all for sins against God — how does that fit?
Rabbi Abbahu
Once the victim forgives, it becomes purely a matter between the sinner and God — and God will then judge him with mercy through Yom Kippur.
📌 Ruling Sins between people require genuine human forgiveness first. Only then does Yom Kippur atone for the divine dimension of the offense.

🤝 How to Properly Seek Forgiveness (Amud A)

Verbal appeasement required — even for words alone, you must appease the person you wronged (Rabbi Yitzḥak, from Proverbs 6:2–3)
↓
Three rows of three people — Rav Ḥisda: appear before the offended party with three rows of three witnesses, as in Job 33:27 (yashor = rows)
↓
Ask no more than three times — Rabbi Yosei bar Ḥanina derives from Genesis 50:17 (three uses of 'please forgive') that three requests is the maximum
↓
Rabbi Yirmeya & Rabbi Abba: Rabbi Yirmeya sat at Rabbi Abba's doorstep; dirty water was poured on his head and he exclaimed: 'I have been sentenced!' — Rabbi Abba then forgave him
↓
Rabbi Zeira's method: When wronged, he would pace before the offender to make it easy for that person to come and apologize
↓
Rav & the Butcher: Rav came to apologize on Yom Kippur eve — but the butcher died before receiving him. A grave warning about delaying reconciliation.

🌅 What Is the Ne'ila Prayer? (Amud B)

Rav
Ne'ila is a full Amida prayer of seven blessings, added as the gates of heaven close at day's end. It also exempts one from the evening prayer.
Shmuel
Ne'ila is not a full Amida — it is only a confession: 'What are we? What are our lives?' No separate Amida is required.
Ulla bar Rav (practice)
Led ne'ila opening with 'You have chosen us' (full Amida) and closing with 'What are we, what are our lives?' (confession). Rava praised him for combining both views.
Tanna'im (baraita)
All agree ne'ila includes seven blessings — the dispute is only whether a full confession must be recited within the ne'ila prayer itself.
⏳ Ne'ila Ne'ila = 'closing of the gates' — the final prayer of Yom Kippur as the sun sets and the heavenly decree is sealed.
