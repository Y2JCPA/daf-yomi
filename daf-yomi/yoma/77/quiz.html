QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why was Gabriel cast out from behind the divine curtain (פַּרְגּוֹד)?
A. He refused to carry out the decree against Jerusalem
B. He passed the embers through the cherub's hand first, cooling them to reduce casualties
C. He falsely reported completing his mission
D. He interceded for Israel without divine permission
Correct: B — Rav Ḥana bar Bizna teaches that Gabriel, out of mercy, passed the embers through the cherub's hand to cool them — sparing Jewish lives. Though compassionate, this deviation from the direct command resulted in his punishment.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Who replaced Gabriel during his exile, and for how long?
A. Michael, for forty days
B. Samael (Esau's angel), for seven days
C. Dubiel, the ministering angel of Persia, for twenty-one days
D. Uriel, for thirty days
Correct: C — Dubiel, the ministering angel of Persia, served in Gabriel's place for twenty-one days — corresponding exactly to Daniel 10:13: 'the prince of the kingdom of Persia stood opposed to me for twenty-one days.'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
From which verse does Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak ultimately derive that going barefoot is an affliction?
A. David weeping barefoot ascending the Mount of Olives (2 Sam. 15:30)
B. The High Priest's barefoot Temple service (Lev. 16)
C. Isaiah walking naked and barefoot for three years (Isa. 20:3)
D. 'Withhold your foot from being barefoot' as a consequence of sin (Jer. 2:25)
Correct: D — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak derives it from Jeremiah 2:25 — 'Withhold your foot from being barefoot' implies that going barefoot is the punitive consequence of sinful behavior, establishing it as an affliction.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What specific leniency did Rava grant regarding Yom Kippur and crossing water?
A. Torah scholars may cross any river to attend a public shiur
B. People on the right bank of the Euphrates may cross to guard their fruit fields
C. A son may wade across to visit a sick parent regardless of depth
D. Anyone may cross still water if it is below knee-level
Correct: B — Rava permitted those living on the right side of the Euphrates to wade through the water specifically to guard their fruit on Yom Kippur — financial loss from unguarded crops constitutes a legitimate need.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rabbi Yehuda ben Pazi teach about the future river flowing from the Holy of Holies?
A. It will heal the sick but only those who immerse completely
B. It will divide the Land of Israel into twelve tribal portions
C. Even the Angel of Death will not be permitted to cross to its far side
D. It will flow only on Shabbat and Yom Tov
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehuda ben Pazi states that even the Angel of Death (מַלְאַךְ הַמָּוֶת) has no permission to cross the future river (Ezek. 47:5 — 'waters that cannot be crossed'), symbolizing that death itself will be powerless in the messianic era.
