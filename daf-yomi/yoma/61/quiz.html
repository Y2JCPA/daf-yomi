Yoma 61 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Ulla, what is the halakhic status of a goat that was slaughtered before the blood of the bull was presented?
A. The goat is valid; only the blood presentation must be redone
B. It is as though he did nothing — the slaughter is considered void
C. The service is valid but the goat must be re-consecrated
D. Only the lottery for the goat must be repeated
Correct: B — Ulla ruled that slaughtering the goat before presenting the bull's blood is as though he did nothing — the prescribed order of the Yom Kippur service is essential and cannot be reversed.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
If blood spills after completing all Holy of Holies sprinklings but partway through the Sanctuary (Heichal) sprinklings, what does Rabbi Elazar and Rabbi Shimon require?
A. Restart from the very beginning of all indoor sprinklings
B. Restart only from the beginning of the Sanctuary sprinklings
C. Continue from the exact point the blood spilled
D. The entire day's service is invalidated
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar and Rabbi Shimon hold that atonement already achieved at a completed location is not undone. Only the current incomplete stage — the Sanctuary — must be restarted from its beginning.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rabbi Yochanan makes a striking observation about Rabbi Meir and Rabbi Elazar/Shimon's dispute. What is it?
A. They argue about a case not addressed by the Torah at all
B. Both opinions are derived from the very same verse in Leviticus 16
C. Their dispute is purely practical with no textual basis
D. Rabbi Meir's view was actually derived from the leper's law, not Yom Kippur
Correct: B — Rabbi Yochanan notes that despite reaching opposite conclusions, both Rabbi Meir and Rabbi Elazar/Shimon derive their opinions from the single verse 'Aaron shall make atonement upon its corners once a year' (Leviticus 16:18).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Regarding the leper's guilt-offering that was slaughtered not for its own sake, what does Rabbi Yochanan rule?
A. The leper may bring a new guilt-offering as a replacement
B. The service is valid; only the blood application need be redone
C. The leper has no remedy — the purification rite cannot be completed
D. The priest must perform the blood application with a different animal
Correct: C — According to Rabbi Yochanan (aligned with Rabbi Elazar and Rabbi Shimon), once the guilt-offering was slaughtered not for its own sake, there is no remedy. The verse's use of 'it' (Lev. 14:12) implies the same animal must be used, leaving no valid path forward.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
On what basis does the Gemara conclude that the oil poured on the leper's head (as opposed to the thumbs) is not indispensable?
A. Because the head anointing is a rabbinic addition, not biblical
B. Because the verse about the head uses the word 'remainder,' implying it is secondary to the thumb application
C. Because the Mishnah explicitly exempts head anointing from invalidation
D. Because Rabbi Elazar and Rabbi Shimon explicitly permit omitting it
Correct: B — The Gemara derives that the head anointing is not indispensable from the verse's phrasing: 'of the rest of the oil…the priest shall put upon the tip of the right ear…and upon the thumb' — the thumb application is the primary act; the head receives only the 'remainder,' marking it as secondary and non-essential.
