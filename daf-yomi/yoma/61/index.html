⚖️ The Great Restart Dispute

Rabbi Meir
'One sin-offering, not two.' Each location's rite forms a single indivisible unit. If blood spills at any point, the High Priest must return to the very beginning of all sprinklings — no completed location is saved.
Rabbi Elazar & Rabbi Shimon
'Atonement achieved is not undone.' Completed locations remain fully valid. If blood spills, resume only from the start of the current unfinished location — prior stages stand.
Rabbi Yochanan's Observation Remarkably, both opinions derive from the same single verse: 'Aaron shall make atonement upon its corners once a year' (Leviticus 16:18) — yet reach opposite halakhic conclusions!

📜 Same Verse, Opposite Rulings — Rabbi Yochanan Explains

Rabbi Meir
The verse says 'once a year' — I read this as: God commanded one sin-offering, not two. Only one animal's blood may be sprinkled on the inner altar in total. If blood spills mid-sequence, a new animal's blood cannot complete what the first began. Restart everything.
Rabbi Elazar & Rabbi Shimon
The verse says 'once a year' — I read this as: each location's rite must be completed in one continuous session. But once a location is fully completed, its atonement is achieved. If blood spills afterward, only the current incomplete location must restart.
The Source Verse (Leviticus 16:18) וְכִפֶּר עָלָיו אַהֲרֹן עַל קַרְנֹתָיו אַחַת בַּשָּׁנָה — 'Aaron shall make atonement upon its corners once a year'

🏛️ Three Stages of Yom Kippur Indoor Sprinklings

1. Holy of Holies

  • 8 sprinklings: 1 upward + 7 downward
  • Performed for both bull and goat blood
  • First and innermost location
  • If spilled: ALL opinions — restart here
  • Must be completed before moving on

2. Sanctuary / Heichal

  • 8 sprinklings on the Parokhet curtain
  • Performed after Holy of Holies
  • R. Meir: if spilled, restart from very beginning
  • R. Elazar/Shimon: restart Sanctuary only
  • HoK atonement already achieved (per R. E/S)

3. Golden Altar (Mizbeach HaZahav)

  • 7 sprinklings on corners and top
  • Final indoor sprinkling stage
  • R. Meir: if spilled, restart from very beginning
  • R. Elazar/Shimon: restart Altar only
  • Prior stages' atonement stands (per R. E/S)

🧤 The Leper's Guilt-Offering — A Dead End?

וְהִקְרִיב אֹתוֹ לְאָשָׁם — 'And he shall offer it as a guilt-offering' (Leviticus 14:12)
Rabbi Yochanan's Ruling If a leper's guilt-offering was slaughtered not for its own sake (e.g., intended as a burnt-offering), or if the blood was never placed on the leper's thumbs and toes — the leper has no remedy whatsoever.

Per Rabbi Elazar and Rabbi Shimon: once the invalid slaughter occurred, it cannot be undone. Since one cannot simply 'resume from where one stopped,' the leper cannot bring a substitute — he is permanently stuck.

Rav Ḥisda challenges: The verse says 'and offer it for a guilt-offering' — the word 'it' implies the very same animal. Even Rabbi Meir would say there is no remedy here!

A baraita confirms: the language 'must' in such cases means 'has no remedy' — as proven from the case of the bald nazirite who cannot shave.
