⚔️ Rav vs. Shmuel: Non-Priest Slaughter

Rav
Red heifer slaughtered by non-priest = INVALID
Reason: verse names Elazar — a specific priest is required

Bull of High Priest by non-priest = valid
Reason: slaughter is not classified as a Temple service
Shmuel
Red heifer slaughtered by non-priest = valid
Reason: it is a chok — outside Temple service rules

Bull of High Priest by non-priest = INVALID
Reason: 'before him' means the High Priest must do it himself
Curious Inversion Rav and Shmuel perfectly switch positions — each deems one slaughter valid and the other invalid, but for opposite objects. The Gemara concludes Rav aligns with Rabbi Zeira: red heifer by non-priest is invalid.

⚔️ Rav vs. Shmuel: The Full Dispute

Rav
The word Elazar in Numbers 19:3 designates a named priest — therefore a non-priest who slaughters the red heifer invalidates it.

But slaughter of the bull by a non-priest is valid, since shechitah is not a priestly act per se.
Shmuel
Elazar was a one-time instruction to Moses — future generations may use any priest or even a non-priest for the heifer.

But 'before him' (לְפָנָיו) in the bull's verse demands the High Priest act himself — a non-priest's slaughter of the bull is invalid.
Supporting Voice Rabbi Zeira rules: non-priest slaughter of the red heifer is invalid. Rav endorses this ruling — confirming his side of the debate.

📜 Deriving Requirements from 'Elazar' and 'Before Him'

Two key phrases in Torah:
'Give it to Elazar the priest' (Num. 19:3) — Red Heifer
'He shall slaughter the bull before him' (Lev. 16) — Bull of High Priest
↓
Rav reads 'Elazar'
Named priest → priestly identity required at slaughter
→ Non-priest slaughter of heifer is invalid
Shmuel reads 'before him'
High Priest must act personally
→ Non-priest slaughter of bull is invalid
↓
A fortiori challenge: If the actual korban (bull) allows non-priest slaughter (per Rav), shouldn't the heifer — which is not a korban — certainly allow it? Rav answers: the explicit verse 'Elazar' overrides the logic.

👁️ Attention Requirement: Slaughter vs. Burning

Slaughter (שְׁחִיטָה)

  • Attention required from moment of slaughter onward
  • It is the start of the Temple rite — primary importance
  • Derived from 'Elazar' or 'before him' — mindful presence demanded
  • One verse covers this stage specifically

Burning (שְׂרֵפָה)

  • Attention also required during the burning
  • One might have thought: later stage, less critical — WRONG
  • A separate verse establishes attention for burning too
  • Without explicit inclusion, burning could have been excluded

📖 Ulla: Each Phrase Introduces a Distinct Condition

'Give it to Elazar the priest' — Slaughter by a priest (stand-in for Elazar); only one heifer goes out, not two simultaneously
↓
'He shall take it out' — The word 'it' (אוֹתָהּ) excludes taking a second heifer alongside it. As we learned in a baraita: only this heifer, alone.
↓
חֻקַּת הַתּוֹרָה — 'The statute of the Torah' — The word chukat teaches that all relevant stages must be performed by a man (not a woman) and during the day
↓
Inclusion and exclusion: 'This' (זֹאת) excludes certain stages; 'law/statute' (חֻקָּה) includes others. The Gemara asks: what logic determines which stages are included vs. excluded?
↓
Answer (Abaye): Stages invalid for a woman (because a verse specifies a serving priest, who is male) are also included in the daytime/male requirements — by the same logic as the Elazar derivation.
