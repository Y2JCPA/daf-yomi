Yoma 33 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye (transmitting Abba Shaul), which service comes FIRST in the daily Temple sequence?
A. Slaughter of the daily morning offering
B. Setting up the large wood arrangement on the outer altar
C. Removing ashes from the inner incense altar
D. Burning the incense
Correct: B — Abaye lists the large wood arrangement (מערכה גדולה) as the very first act — it is the primary fire of the outer altar upon which all offerings depend, and must be established before anything else.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the priest remove ashes from only FIVE of the seven lamps before the daily blood offering, saving two for later?
A. He runs out of time before the slaughter
B. The two eastern lamps face inward — cleaning them would force the priest to turn his back on the incense altar, an impropriety during the incense service
C. The two western lamps are lit separately and belong to a different ritual
D. The Torah explicitly commands cleaning only five lamps in the morning
Correct: B — Reish Lakish explains the split: cleaning the two inward-facing lamps requires turning away from the incense altar. To preserve proper orientation during the incense service, those two lamps are deferred until afterward.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Abaye derive that the five-lamp ash-removal precedes the blood of the daily offering?
A. From the physical layout — the candelabrum stands closer to the Sanctuary entrance than the slaughter area
B. The phrase 'in the morning, in the morning' with the two logs generates extra instances — one is reassigned to place the five lamps before the blood
C. From an explicit ruling in tractate Tamid that the lamps always precede the offering
D. From logical reasoning — ashes must be cleared before blood is sprinkled anywhere nearby
Correct: B — The phrase בַּבֹּקֶר בַּבֹּקֶר ('in the morning, in the morning') appears twice for the two logs, generating three 'morning' instances. One is used for the logs themselves; the two extras are redistributed — one placing five lamps before blood, one placing blood before the remaining two lamps.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What challenge does Rav Pappa raise against Abaye's derivation?
A. The five lamps should follow the blood offering, not precede it
B. The extra 'in the morning' phrase could equally be assigned to the inner altar, making IT precede the blood offering rather than the five lamps
C. The two-log requirement is derived incorrectly — logs carry no morning obligation
D. Abba Shaul's tradition directly contradicts the opinion of the majority of Sages
Correct: B — Rav Pappa argues that the 'spare' morning phrase could logically go to the inner altar's ash-removal rather than to the five lamps. Abaye responds that the inner altar's priority over the lamps is already established by a separate derivation (the Sanctuary layout), so the extra phrase is not needed there.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Gemara, what is the correct order of the final three inner-Sanctuary service steps?
A. Incense → Two remaining lamps → Limbs of daily offering
B. Two remaining lamps → Incense → Limbs of daily offering on outer altar
C. Daily blood sprinkling → Incense → Two remaining lamps
D. Five lamps → Two lamps → Incense
Correct: B — After the blood of the daily offering is sprinkled, the two remaining lamps are cleaned. Then incense is burned on the inner altar. Only then are the limbs of the daily offering burned on the outer altar. Exodus 30:7 confirms: 'when he removes the ashes from the lamps, he shall burn incense' — lamps precede incense.
