🪵 Which Wood Arrangement Comes First?

Gemara
The large arrangement comes first — it is the primary service of the outer altar where all offerings are burned.
Challenge
Reverse them! The first verse refers to the incense arrangement and the second to the large arrangement.
Response
It is reasonable that the large arrangement — the more significant service — is mentioned first in Scripture.
Counter
On the contrary! The second arrangement takes precedence because coals from it are taken inside the Sanctuary for the incense!
Resolution
Even so, the large arrangement comes first — without its fire burning, there are no coals to take inside at all.
Principle The large arrangement is foundational; the incense arrangement depends upon coals drawn from it.

📖 Deriving Sequence from בַּבֹּקֶר בַּבֹּקֶר ('In the Morning, In the Morning')

Key Phrase: בַּבֹּקֶר בַּבֹּקֶר — 'in the morning, in the morning'
Written twice in the verse about the two logs (Lev. 6:5) → generates three instances of 'morning' (two explicit + one implicit)
↓
One instance is needed for the two logs themselves — but two remain available for reassignment to other services
↓
Extra 'morning' #1
Assign to five lamps → they must precede the blood of the daily offering
Extra 'morning' #2
Assign to daily blood offering → it must precede the two remaining lamps
↓
Established Sequence: Five lamps → Blood of daily offering → Two lamps

🕯️ Why Split the Lamps — 5 Then 2?

Reish Lakish

  • The priest cleans 5 lamps first, then pauses
  • The 2 eastern lamps face inward — cleaning them forces the priest to turn his back on the incense altar
  • During the incense service, one must not face away from the altar
  • So those 2 lamps are deferred until after the incense
  • Reason: spatial/positional sanctity concern

Rabbi Yoḥanan

  • Derives split directly from Exodus 30:7
  • 'When he removes the ashes from the lamps, he shall burn incense'
  • Verse interrupts lamp-cleaning with the incense command
  • 5 lamps cleaned → incense burned → 2 lamps cleaned
  • Reason: biblical verse dictates the interruption

⚔️ Rav Pappa Challenges Abaye's Derivation

Abaye
One extra 'in the morning' is assigned to the five lamps — placing them before the blood of the daily offering.
Rav Pappa
Why not assign that extra phrase to the inner altar ash-removal instead? It also has multiple 'morning' instances — make IT precede the blood offering!
Abaye
The inner altar's position before the five lamps is already established by a separate derivation — the extra phrase is not needed there.
Ravina
But is 'in the morning, in the morning' for the two logs actually superfluous? Isn't it needed to obligate placing logs — how can you reassign it?
Gemara
The obligation to add logs derives from the word 'upon it' (עָלֶיהָ) — therefore 'in the morning' IS free for timing derivations.
Talmudic Method When a verse contains redundant language, the extra words teach additional laws — here, the precise sequence of Temple services.
