🐐 Which Azazel Goat Is Sent First?

Rav Pappi
In the name of Rava: Send the first goat — the one whose lot for Azazel was drawn first takes priority.
Rav Shimi
In the name of Rava: Send the last goat — the final one designated for Azazel is the one sent.
Gemara
Rav Pappi's position aligns with Rabbi Yosei's ruling on the Paschal offering: the first item designated for a mitzva always takes priority over later ones.
Key Derivation The 'first takes priority' principle is proven from Rabbi Yosei on the Paschal offering — not from the Temple containers, where a different logic applies.

📜 Mishna: The Two Yom Kippur Goats — Requirements & Procedure

Ab initio: Both goats must be identical in appearance (color), height, and monetary value
↓
They must be purchased together at the same time (not on different days)
↓
If not identical, they are still valid after the fact — Torah's amplification ('goat, goat') permits it
↓
High Priest draws lots — one for God (sin-offering), one for Azazel (scapegoat)
↓
If one goat dies before the lottery → take a replacement for the surviving goat immediately
↓
If one goat dies after the lottery → bring a new pair, draw lots again with a verbal stipulation

🎲 What Happens If a Goat Dies?

One of the two Yom Kippur goats dies or is lost
↓
Before the Lottery
Neither goat yet designated — take a replacement for the surviving goat immediately and proceed
After the Lottery
Bring a new pair of goats; draw lots again with a stipulation naming which replaces which
↓
Rabbi Yehuda adds: If the God-goat's blood spills before sprinkling → scapegoat is left to die. If scapegoat dies → God-goat's blood is spilled. Both are nullified together — they are an indivisible pair.

🔢 Torah's Amplification Across Three Paired Rituals

Yom Kippur Goats

  • שני שני שני — threefold 'two' → identical ab initio
  • 'Goat, goat' repeated → valid post facto even if not identical
  • Resolution: ab initio = identical; post facto = valid anyway
  • Leviticus 16:5 — the foundational case

Leper's Lambs

  • 'Two lambs' (Lev. 14:10) — plural minimum is two
  • 'Lamb, lamb' amplification → valid if not identical
  • 'This shall be' → covers all leper atonement halakhot
  • Parallel derivation to Yom Kippur goats

Leper's Birds

  • 'Two birds' (Lev. 14:4) — plural minimum is two
  • 'Bird, bird' amplification → valid if not identical
  • 'This shall be' → covers all leper purification rites
  • Consistent derivation pattern across all three cases

⚖️ Are Goats Slaughtered Without Lots Liable?

Baraita
If the High Priest slaughtered both goats outside before drawing lots, he is liable for both — for neither was fit as a sin-offering or scapegoat.
Gemara
But why unfit? An animal 'lacking time' (not yet its day) is disqualified — but lots are drawn that same day. So why do missing lots disqualify?
Rav Ḥisda
An offering is only 'lacking time' if its time is on a different day. Same-day readiness is not a time-deficiency — but lacking the lottery is like lacking a required act, which does disqualify.
Ravina
From Rav Ḥisda's ruling we learn: lacking the lottery = lacking an action = invalid offering. The lot is not merely procedural — it is constitutive of the animal's sacred status.
Legal Principle The lottery (גורל) is not a mere formality — without it, neither goat has the status of a valid offering. Performing the rite without lots is a transgression carrying liability for both goats.
