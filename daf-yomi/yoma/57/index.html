⚔️ The Mixed Blood Controversy

Question
What if the blood of the bull and blood of the goat become mixed together before all sprinklings are completed?
Rava
Present once upward and seven downward — then again once upward and seven downward. The mixture fulfills both the bull's and goat's obligations.
Rabbi Yirmeya
Foolish Babylonians! Your logic is flawed: the second 'once upward' completes the bull's count of eight — you are attributing the goat's sprinkling to the bull!
Rabbi Yirmeya
The correct solution: once upward + seven downward with intention for the bull; then once upward + seven downward with intention for the goat.
Rava
When Rav Pappa repeated the refuted logic for the Sanctuary stage, Rava rebuked him: 'Now they will call us the foolish of the foolish — we teach them and they do not learn!'
Core Problem Each blood offering has distinct, intentional obligations. One set of sprinklings cannot simultaneously fulfill two separate requirements — intention and order both matter.

🩸 The Yom Kippur Blood Sprinkling Sequence

Enter Holy of Holies with the bull's blood (דם הפר)
↓
Sprinkle once upward (אַחַת) — toward the Ark Cover above
↓
Sprinkle seven times downward, counting: אַחַת, אַחַת וְאַחַת, אַחַת וּשְׁתַּיִם...
↓
Exit, take the goat's blood — re-enter the Holy of Holies
↓
Again: once upward + seven downward for the goat's blood (דם השעיר)
↓
The High Priest sprinkles opposite the curtain — not directly on it (Rabbi Elazar's testimony confirms this)
↓
Pour bull's blood into the goat's blood, then pour the remainder onto the base of the outer altar

🏛️ Rabbi Elazar Sees the Curtain in Rome

ראיתי אותה ברומי, והיו עליה כמה טיפי דמים של יום הכיפורים
Historical Witness — פרוכת Rabbi Elazar, son of Rabbi Yosei, reported seeing the Temple curtain (פָּרֹכֶת) in Rome after it was taken in the destruction. He saw drops of blood on it and identified them as specifically from the Yom Kippur bull and goat — because the drops appeared in their precise sequential order, a pattern unique to Yom Kippur alone. He also identified separate stains from the bull for an unwitting communal sin and the goats for idolatry, recognizing those as a cluster (not in sequence).

🔀 Three Stages of the Mixed Blood Problem

Stage 1: Mixed Before Sanctuary

  • Bull + goat blood mix before entering Sanctuary
  • Rava's flawed answer: 1↑ + 7↓, then 1↑ + 7↓
  • Rabbi Yirmeya: 'Foolish Babylonians!'
  • ✓ Correct: full set for bull intention, full set for goat intention
  • Total: 2 × (1 up + 7 down) = 16 sprinklings

Stage 2: Mixed During Sanctuary

  • Bull + goat blood mix mid-Sanctuary service
  • Rav Pappa repeats Rava's old flawed logic
  • Rava: 'Now they'll call us foolish of the foolish!'
  • ✓ Correct (Rava): 7↓ for bull, then 1↑ + 7↓ for goat
  • Omit the opening 'once upward' for the bull

Stage 3: Cups Confused

  • The actual vessels (cups) are confused — not blood mixed
  • Don't know which cup is bull, which is goat
  • ✓ Solution: 3 full sets total
  • Cup A: 1↑+7↓ (bull) + 1↑+7↓ (goat)
  • Cup B: 1↑+7↓ — covers all permutations

🥛 Solving the Confused Cups: Logic of Three Sets

Two cups of blood — which is bull's? which is goat's? — are confused
↓
Perform from Cup A: once up + seven down labeled 'for the bull'
↓
Perform from Cup A again: once up + seven down labeled 'for the goat'
↓
Perform from Cup B: once up + seven down
↓
If Cup A = Bull's Blood
Set 1 (A, bull) ✓ Set 3 (B, goat) ✓ Set 2 (A, goat) = extra — harmless
If Cup A = Goat's Blood
Set 2 (A, goat) ✓ Set 3 (B, bull) ✓ Set 1 (A, bull) = extra — harmless
