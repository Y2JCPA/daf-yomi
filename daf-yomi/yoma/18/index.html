🔍 Rava's Resolution: The Detained Watch Explains Everything

Baraita
The High Priest takes four loaves in one case and five in another — these clauses follow different tannaitic opinions (Rabbis vs. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi).
Rava
No — the entire baraita follows Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi. The difference of 4 vs. 5 loaves is not a dispute; it depends on whether a watch is detained.
Rava
When a festival begins Sunday night, one watch is forced to stay extra. That detained watch takes 2 loaves, reducing what remains for the HP from 10 to 8 — so HP takes 4.
Objection
If so, what does the last clause of the baraita add when it says 'Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi says: The HP takes five'? It already said that!
Answer
The last clause covers a third case: no outgoing watch. There, the HP also takes five — from the ten loaves, but via a different calculation than the standard case.
Key Term — משמר עצור 'Detained watch': a priestly family compelled to remain beyond their assigned week because a festival (e.g., Sukkot) began on Sunday night, leaving no time for the changeover.

👑 High Priest Qualifications: First Temple vs. Second Temple

First Temple Era

  • Greater than brethren in strength
  • Greater in beauty
  • Greater in wisdom
  • Greater in wealth
  • ✅ Wisdom was a real standard
  • Source: 'greater than his brethren' (Lev. 21:10)
  • Aḥerim: wealth can be supplemented by other kohanim

Second Temple Era

  • HP may not know how to read
  • Elders read the service order to him
  • Asked: 'Did you forget, or never learn?'
  • ❌ Wisdom no longer guaranteed
  • Appointments were often political or financial
  • Mishna reflects this diminished standard
  • Rav Yosef: the two sources describe different eras

😴 The Yom Kippur Eve Vigil: Step by Step

Morning of Erev Yom Kippur: Animals are paraded before the HP to keep his mind engaged and prevent seminal emission (goats are included per Tosefta)
↓
Elders of the court read the Yom Kippur service order to the HP and say: 'My Master, High Priest — read it yourself, lest you forgot or never learned'
↓
HP is handed over from court elders → Elders of the Priesthood, who escort him to the Temple Mount
↓
Taken up to the House of Avtinas (incense preparation chamber) for the night
↓
Oath administered: HP swears he will perform the service exactly as instructed and not deviate toward Sadducean practice
↓
Elders weep; HP weeps — mutual emotion over the gravity of the day and the suspicion implied by the oath
↓
If he is a scholar → he teaches Torah all night to stay awake
↓
If not a scholar → Torah scholars teach before him; if he reads Scripture he reads, otherwise it is read to him
