QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, why does the baraita state that the High Priest sometimes takes four loaves and sometimes five?
A. It reflects a dispute between the Rabbis and Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi about how many loaves are baked
B. It depends on whether a priestly watch is detained due to a festival beginning on Sunday night
C. It depends on whether the High Priest himself participates in the watch service that week
D. Four is the rule; five is an exceptional case requiring three witnesses
Correct: B — Rava resolves that the entire baraita follows R. Yehuda HaNasi. The variable is a detained watch: when a festival forces a watch to stay extra, it takes 2 loaves, leaving only 8 for the HP's calculation — yielding 4 instead of 5.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rav Yosef explain the contradiction between the mishna (which assumes an HP may not know how to read) and the baraita (which requires the HP to surpass his brethren in wisdom)?
A. The baraita's 'wisdom' means financial acumen, not Torah knowledge
B. The baraita describes First Temple standards; the mishna reflects Second Temple reality
C. The baraita is a minority view rejected by the majority of Sages
D. The mishna refers only to emergency appointments, not the usual High Priest
Correct: B — Rav Yosef distinguishes eras: the baraita requiring wisdom describes First Temple practice when that standard was upheld. The mishna, which accounts for an HP who may not be able to read, reflects the Second Temple period when appointees were often unqualified.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which food is NOT among the five listed in the Gemara as causing seminal emission?
A. Garlic
B. Arugula
C. Honey
D. Cress (purslane)
Correct: C — The five are garlic, cress, purslane, eggs, and arugula (identified as the 'orot' gathered in II Kings 4:39). Honey does not appear on this list. R. Meir's acrostic via Sumakhos covers garlic, eggs, and wine.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the folk saying Ravina cites to explain why the HP is privy to the sins of his fellow priests?
A. A shepherd who knows his flock can lead them back to the right path
B. If the beloved son of your sister becomes a policeman, walk straight in front of him in the marketplace
C. The judge who knows the accused must recuse himself to preserve justice
D. Tell no secret to one who will later sit in judgment over you
Correct: B — Ravina says: people say — if your beloved nephew becomes a dayyala (policeman/enforcer), walk straight before him, because he knows your deeds. The HP's bull atones for his brethren's sins, meaning he is aware of those sins — so one should behave properly around him.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How was the High Priest kept awake throughout the night before Yom Kippur?
A. He was made to stand in cold water at intervals and recite confessions
B. Young kohanim snapped their fingers and clapped near him all night
C. If a scholar he taught Torah; if not, scholars taught before him; if he read Scripture he read, otherwise it was read to him
D. He performed the daily tamid offering repeatedly through the night as practice
Correct: C — The mishna states: if he was a scholar he would teach Torah all night; if not, Torah scholars taught before him. If accustomed to reading Scripture he would read; if not, others would read to him — all to prevent him from sleeping and risking ritual impurity.
