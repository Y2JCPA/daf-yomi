QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Yosef, what is the practical halakhic difference between R. Yoḥanan and R. Ḥanina?
A. Whether the High Priest must fast on Yom Kippur before the service
B. Whether omitting semikha (laying hands on the offering) invalidates the inauguration
C. Whether the seven-day sequestering applies to all priests or only the High Priest
D. Whether reading the Torah portion aloud is required for a valid inauguration
Correct: B — Rav Yosef proposes semikha (laying hands on the offering) as the practical difference: R. Yoḥanan holds its omission invalidates the inauguration since it is written in the portion; R. Ḥanina holds it does not invalidate.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the baraita derive from the verse 'And he shall place his hand… and it shall be accepted' regarding semikha for ordinary offerings?
A. Semikha is a prerequisite and its omission renders the offering invalid
B. Semikha must be performed by the owner and cannot be delegated to a priest
C. Even without semikha the offering is accepted, though one who willfully omits it incurs guilt
D. Semikha was only required during the wilderness period and not in the Temple
Correct: C — The baraita learns that one who treated semikha as peripheral and omitted it — the offering is still accepted ('it shall be accepted'). However, the verse ascribes fault to that person for neglecting the positive act.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What gezerah shavah (verbal analogy) does Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak use to link the Exodus and Leviticus inauguration portions?
A. The phrase 'seven days' appearing in both Exodus 29 and Leviticus 8
B. The word 'anoint' (mashach) used identically in both portions
C. The phrase 'the opening of the Tent of Meeting' (petach ohel mo'ed) appearing in both portions
D. The expression 'as the Lord commanded Moses' repeated in both narratives
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak derives a gezerah shavah from 'the opening of the Tent of Meeting,' which appears in both Exodus 29:32 and the Leviticus implementation, thereby connecting the two portions so each informs the requirements of the other.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Ravina, why does R. Ḥanina hold that omitting the seven-day anointment does NOT invalidate the inauguration?
A. Anointment was abolished after the death of Moses and has no ongoing requirement
B. The seven-day anointment requirement is derived from Leviticus, not the Exodus command — and R. Ḥanina only invalidates for Exodus-portion details
C. R. Ḥanina holds that anointment and wearing multiple garments are redundant and only one is required
D. The verse about anointment is a general statement applying to all generations, not specifically to inauguration
Correct: B — R. Ḥanina's rule is that only details in the Exodus inauguration portion invalidate. The seven-day anointment requirement is derived from Leviticus 16:32, so per R. Ḥanina, its omission does not invalidate the inauguration.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
On Amud Bet, what does Abaye clarify about the dispute regarding how Moses dressed Aaron and his sons?
A. Abaye rules the entire question is moot since the inauguration cannot be repeated in any generation
B. Abaye says all opinions agree the sons were dressed before Aaron, since sons are mentioned first in Leviticus 8
C. Abaye clarifies that for the tunic and mitre, all agree Aaron was dressed before his sons, since Aaron is mentioned first in both the command and implementation portions
D. Abaye rules that Moses dressed all the priests simultaneously, with no required order between Aaron and his sons
Correct: C — Abaye limits the dispute: for the tunic (kutonet) and mitre (mitznefet), everyone agrees Aaron came first — because in both the Exodus command and the Leviticus implementation, Aaron is mentioned before his sons regarding these garments.
