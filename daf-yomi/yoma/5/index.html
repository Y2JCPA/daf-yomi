⚖️ The Core Inauguration Dispute

R. Yoḥanan
Failure to perform any detail written in the inauguration portion — whether in Exodus or Leviticus — invalidates the entire inauguration
R. Ḥanina
Only failure to perform details written in the Exodus portion (the Sinaitic command) invalidates; details found only in Leviticus do not invalidate
The Daf's Task Four Amoraim — Rav Yosef, Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak, Rav Pappa, and Ravina — each propose a ritual where this dispute yields a concrete halakhic difference

🤲 Rav Yosef: Semikha — The First Proposed Difference

Rav Yosef
The practical difference is semikha (laying hands on the head of the offering). Per R. Yoḥanan, omitting semikha during inauguration invalidates the inauguration entirely
Baraita
For ordinary offerings throughout generations, semikha does not invalidate: 'And he shall place his hand… and it shall be accepted' — the offering is accepted even if semikha was neglected, though guilt is incurred
Key Principle One who treats a positive act (semikha) as peripheral and omits it: the offering stands — but the Torah still ascribes culpability to the one who neglected it

🔒 Rav Pappa: Seven-Day Sequestering

Rav Pappa's Claim: Sequestering the priest for seven days at the Tent of Meeting is the practical difference between R. Yoḥanan and R. Ḥanina
↓
R. Yoḥanan
Written in the inauguration text → failure to sequester invalidates the entire inauguration
R. Ḥanina
Sequestering appears in Leviticus 8 only, not the Exodus command → its omission does not invalidate
↓
Gemara's Question: For ordinary priesthood throughout generations — where is the source that no sequestering is ever required?
↓
Answer: Sequestering ('You shall not go out from the door of the Tent of Meeting for seven days') is contextually unique to the one-time inauguration and has no parallel requirement for ordinary priestly service

👘 Ravina: Multiple Garments & Anointment for Seven Days

Multiple Garments (Bigdei Kehunah Gedolah)

  • Source: 'Seven days shall the son that is priest in his stead don them' (Exodus 29:30)
  • Only one who wore the High Priest's garments for 7 days AND was anointed for 7 days assumes the High Priesthood
  • Ab initio requirement — R. Yoḥanan: omitting it invalidates the inauguration
  • R. Ḥanina: written in Exodus → this detail DOES invalidate per both opinions

Anointment (Mishcha) for Seven Days

  • Source: 'The priest who shall be anointed and consecrated to serve in his father's stead' (Leviticus 16:32)
  • Verse is needed to exclude other derivations — dedicated to the 7-day anointment requirement
  • R. Ḥanina: derived from Leviticus only → omission does NOT invalidate
  • Alternative: derive from logical inference — if garments require 7 days, anointment likewise requires 7 days

👗 Amud Bet: How Did Moses Dress Aaron and His Sons?

The Question Reframed: Not 'what happened historically' (that is already fixed) — but 'what is the correct procedure for future inaugurations?' The verse order matters for establishing the law
↓
Dispute — Sons of R. Ḥiyya vs. R. Yoḥanan: One says Moses dressed Aaron entirely first, then dressed his sons; the other says he dressed them all simultaneously, garment by garment
↓
Abaye's Clarification: Regarding the tunic (kutonet) and mitre (mitznefet) — all agree Aaron was dressed before his sons, since Aaron is mentioned before his sons in both the Exodus command AND the Leviticus implementation
↓
Gezerah Shavah (Verbal Analogy): Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak: the phrase 'the opening of the Tent of Meeting' (petach ohel mo'ed) appears in BOTH inauguration portions (Exodus 29:32 and Leviticus 8) — linking the two and allowing details from each to inform the other
↓
R. Yosei bar Ḥanina: Trousers (mikhnasayim) were worn during the inauguration but are not written explicitly in the inauguration Torah portion — they are derived from context. The tenth of an ephah (meal-offering) is similarly derived
