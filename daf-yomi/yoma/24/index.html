⚖️ Rav vs. Levi: Non-Priest Liability for Temple Services

Rav
A non-priest is liable to death by God's hand for only four services: (1) sprinkling blood on the altar, (2) burning incense, (3) lighting the menorah, (4) pouring the wine libation.
Levi
A non-priest is liable for five services — Rav's four, plus (5) removal of the ashes (terumat hadeshen), derived from 'in everything pertaining to the altar.'
Gemara
Rav reads: 'I give you the priesthood as a service of gift' — death attaches only to the explicitly enumerated priestly gift-services, not to ash removal.
Gemara
Levi reads 'in everything pertaining to the altar' as encompassing all altar actions — ash removal is performed on the altar and thus carries the death penalty for a non-priest.
Key Verse (Numbers 18:7) 'You and your sons shall keep your priesthood in everything pertaining to the altar and to that within the veil; and you shall serve; I give you the priesthood as a service of gift.' — The entire daf pivots on how to parse this verse.

⚖️ Rav vs. Levi: Which Services Carry the Death Penalty for a Non-Priest?

Rav — Four Services

  • 🩸 Sprinkling sacrificial blood on the altar
  • 🌿 Burning incense (ketoret)
  • 🕎 Lighting the menorah (hadlakat hamenorah)
  • 🍷 Pouring the wine libation (nisuch hayayin)
  • 'Service of gift' — only enumerated services carry death

Levi — Five Services

  • 🩸 Sprinkling sacrificial blood
  • 🌿 Burning incense
  • 🕎 Lighting the menorah
  • 🍷 Pouring the wine libation
  • 🔥 Removal of ashes (terumat hadeshen)
  • 'In everything pertaining to the altar' — all altar actions included

📜 Parsing Numbers 18:7 — Logic of the Dispute

Numbers 18:7
'Everything pertaining to the altar… within the veil… you shall serve… a service of gift'
↓
Rav's Reading
'Service of gift' limits liability to the four explicitly gifted services
Levi's Reading
'Everything pertaining to the altar' is a generalization expanding liability to all altar actions
↓
Rav's Result
Ash removal excluded — no death penalty for non-priest
Levi's Result
Ash removal included — non-priest liable for death
↓
Both positions are confirmed by independent baraitot — the dispute is tanna'itic in origin

🔥 Rabbi Avin's Dilemma: Minimum Amount of Ash Removal

תְּרוּמַת הַדֶּשֶׁן — terumat hadeshen (removal of the ashes)
Unresolved Question (Teiku) Rabbi Avin asked: How much ash must be removed to fulfill the mitzva of terumat hadeshen? Option 1: Analogize to teruma of the tithe (Levite's portion to the priest) → 1/100 of the total ash. Option 2: Analogize to great teruma (farmer's portion to the priest) → 1/40 to 1/60 of the ash. Option 3: Analogize to challah (dough offering) → 1/24 of the ash. The Gemara leaves the dilemma unresolved — the minimum quantity remains uncertain.

👘 Priestly Garments — Rules and the Ash-Removal Service

Worn garments remain valid: The verse includes worn garments for Temple service — they may be used as long as they have not become tattered.
↓
High Priest's linen on Yom Kippur: After the Yom Kippur service he removes his linen garments in the Tent of Meeting — they are then permanently set aside and never worn again (Leviticus 16:23).
↓
Does ash removal require four garments? The Gemara explores whether tanna'im dispute whether terumat hadeshen is a full Temple service. Initial suggestion: it is the subject of their dispute.
↓
Resolution: All tanna'im agree ash removal is a bona fide Temple service requiring all four priestly garments. Their actual dispute concerns a different matter entirely.
↓
Non-priest liability: Rav — no death penalty for ash removal; Levi — liable, since all altar actions are covered by 'everything pertaining to the altar.' Both positions have baraita support.
