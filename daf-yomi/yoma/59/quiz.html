Yoma 59 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Akiva, why is the inner altar encircled by foot?
A. Because two First Temple priests testified to this method
B. Because the perimeter of the inner altar is like the perimeter of the outer altar
C. Because the inner altar stands entirely in the north of the Sanctuary
D. Because the Torah explicitly commands foot encircling for all altars
Correct: B — Rabbi Akiva derives the method by analogy: just as the outer altar is encircled by foot for sprinkling, so too the inner altar — they share the same halakhic rule.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the term 'tohoro' (טָהֳרוֹ) of the altar mean, according to Rabba bar Rav Sheila?
A. The golden plating that covers the altar
B. The top surface where incense is burned
C. Half of the altar — like the blazing light of noon
D. The purified ash layer left from prior offerings
Correct: C — Rabba bar Rav Sheila explains 'tohoro' as 'half the altar,' based on the colloquial expression 'tehar tihara' — the brilliance of the sun at midday, i.e., directly overhead — meaning the halfway point.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Mishna, where is the remainder of the High Priest's bull blood poured?
A. The eastern base of the outer altar
B. The northern base of the outer altar
C. The southern base of the outer altar
D. The western base of the outer altar
Correct: D — The Mishna rules that the remainder of blood from inner offerings (the Yom Kippur bull and goat) is poured at the western base of the outer altar, derived from the verse in Leviticus 4:7.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
From which verse does Ulla derive that blood is not subject to me'ila (misuse of consecrated property)?
A. 'You shall pour it on the ground like water' (Deuteronomy 12:16)
B. 'The blood shall be a sign for you' (Exodus 12:13)
C. 'I have given it to you upon the altar to make atonement' (Leviticus 17:11)
D. 'It is most holy, as is the sin-offering' (Leviticus 6:18)
Correct: C — Ulla reads 'I have given it to you' as indicating the blood is transferred to human use for atonement — it no longer belongs to the Most High — so misuse of consecrated property laws do not apply.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Shimon ben Yoḥai, where is the remainder of BOTH inner and outer sin-offering blood poured?
A. Western base — the Sanctuary entrance faces west
B. Eastern base — the main orientation of the Temple Mount
C. Northern base — aligned with the inner altar's position
D. Southern base — the Sanctuary entrance is positioned toward the south
Correct: D — Rav Ashi explains Rabbi Shimon ben Yoḥai's reasoning: the Sanctuary entrance faces south, so the priest exits southward and naturally arrives at the southern base, where he pours both remainders.
