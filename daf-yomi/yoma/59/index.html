🧭 Where to Sprinkle the Seven: North or South?

Chananya
The seven sprinklings on the inner altar are made on the north side — that is where the corner presentations end, so that is where the blood is placed on top.
Rabbi Yosei
The seven sprinklings are made on the south side — the sequence of corner applications ends differently, making the south the final placement.
Common Ground Both agree: wherever the corner presentations finish, that is where the seven sprinklings are placed — they dispute only which side that is.

🤲 Encircling the Inner Altar: By Hand or By Foot?

Rabbi Akiva
The perimeter of the inner altar is like the perimeter of the outer altar — just as the outer altar is encircled by foot for sprinkling, so too is the inner altar. The priest physically walks around it.
Rabbi Yosei
The inner altar stands entirely in the north of the Sanctuary, making foot encircling impossible. The priest stands in place and reaches to each corner — encircling by hand.
First Temple Confirmation Rabbi Yishmael reports: two surviving High Priests from the First Temple gave contradictory testimony — one said 'I encircled by hand,' the other 'I encircled by foot.' The ancient dispute mirrors the Tannaic one.

🩸 Sequence of the Yom Kippur Blood Service

Inner Altar Sprinkling: Priest sprinkles blood on the tohoro — the pure gold surface. Coals are first raked aside; sprinkling is performed on the gold, not on ash or embers.
↓
Corner Applications: Blood applied to the four corners of the inner altar. Whether by walking around (foot) or reaching (hand) is the central dispute of this daf.
↓
Seven Sprinklings: Seven sprinklings applied at the location where the corner presentations conclude — north side (Chananya) or south side (Rabbi Yosei).
↓
Exit to Outer Altar: Priest descends the ramp carrying the remaining blood, proceeds to the outer altar in the courtyard.
↓
Pouring the Remainder: Blood of the inner offering poured at the western base; blood of the outer sin-offering poured at the southern base — per the Mishna.
↓
Canal to Kidron River: All mixed remainders flow via the channel beneath the altar to the Kidron River, where the blood is sold to gardeners as fertilizer.

💧 Where Is the Remainder of Blood Poured?

Tanna Kamma (Mishna)

  • Inner offering (bull/goat) remainder → Western base of outer altar
  • Outer sin-offering remainder → Southern base of outer altar
  • Source: 'All its blood he shall pour at the base' (Lev. 4:7) — different verses for each

Rabbi Yishmael

  • Both inner and outer remainder → Western base
  • Rationale: priest exits Sanctuary heading west; the western base is the natural destination for both

Rabbi Shimon ben Yoḥai

  • Both inner and outer remainder → Southern base
  • Rationale: Sanctuary entrance is positioned toward the south; priest naturally exits southward and pours at the southern base

⚖️ Blood and Me'ila: No Misuse Liability

כִּי הַדָּם הוּא בַּנֶּפֶשׁ יְכַפֵּר — Leviticus 17:11
Key Halakhic Principle By Torah law, sacrificial blood is not subject to me'ila (misuse of consecrated property). The Rabbis added only a rabbinic-level prohibition.

Ulla's derivation (Lev. 17:11): 'I have given it to you upon the altar' — once designated for atonement, the blood is given to you, not retained by the Most High, so misuse laws do not apply.

Rabbi Yoḥanan's derivation (Lev. 17:11): 'The blood makes atonement by reason of the life' — the blood retains the same exempt status before atonement as after atonement; just as it is exempt afterward, it is also exempt beforehand.
