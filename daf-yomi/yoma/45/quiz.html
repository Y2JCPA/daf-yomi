Yoma 45 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, how many truly distinct types of gold exist?
A. Seven
B. Six
C. Five
D. Three
Correct: C — Rav Ashi holds there are only five types of gold. 'Gold' and 'good gold' are not independent categories — each of the five types has a regular grade and a superior grade.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the High Priest ascend the altar ramp in the middle on Yom Kippur, unlike other priests?
A. It is the most direct path to the altar top
B. Due to his eminence — he should not walk on the side
C. The sides are ritually impure on Yom Kippur
D. Biblical law requires it for the High Priest
Correct: B — The Gemara explains: due to the eminence of the High Priest, it is beneath his dignity to walk on the side. He ascends and descends in the center, reflecting his elevated status.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which verse proves that the fire for the Menorah must come from the outer altar?
A. "From upon the altar before the Lord" (Lev. 16:12)
B. "A perpetual fire shall be kept burning on the altar, it shall not go out" (Lev. 6:6)
C. "It is the burnt-offering on the flame on the altar all night" (Lev. 6:2)
D. "Aaron's sons shall put fire on the altar" (Lev. 1:7)
Correct: B — The 'perpetual fire' verse (Lev. 6:6) resolves the impasse between two equally valid logical derivations. The fire I commanded to be perpetual — used for the candelabrum — must come from that altar, i.e., the outer altar.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Rabbis (opposing Rabbi Meir), what happens to limbs and fats not fully consumed on the altar the previous evening?
A. They receive their own dedicated fourth arrangement
B. They are removed from the altar and disposed of
C. They are returned to the large arrangement to complete burning
D. They are placed on the ramp until the next morning's large arrangement
Correct: C — The Rabbis hold that leftover limbs and fats are simply returned to the large arrangement, where their burning is completed. Rabbi Meir disagrees, holding that 'and the fire' alludes to a separate fourth arrangement for them.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara need a verse to prove the Yom Kippur coal pan fire must come from the outer altar, rather than relying on logic?
A. No logical argument exists — only a verse can establish this
B. Logic actually proves it must come from the inner altar
C. Two equally valid logical derivations contradict each other, so a verse is required
D. The Rabbis decreed a verse is needed for all Yom Kippur laws
Correct: C — There are two competing logical derivations: one comparing to the candelabrum (outer altar) and one comparing to the daily incense (inner altar). Since neither argument defeats the other, a verse — 'from upon the altar before the Lord' (Lev. 16:12) — is required to settle the matter.
