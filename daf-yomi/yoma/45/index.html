🪵 Wood Arrangements: Normal Days vs. Yom Kippur

Rabbi Yehuda

  • Normal days: 2 arrangements
  • Yom Kippur: 3 arrangements
  • Third mention → thin wood chips only, not a full arrangement

Rabbi Yosei

  • Normal days: 3 arrangements
  • Yom Kippur: 4 arrangements
  • Adds fire-upkeep arrangement
  • Plus: Yom Kippur incense arrangement

Rabbi Meir / Mishna

  • Normal days: 4 arrangements
  • Yom Kippur: 5 arrangements
  • Adds: limbs & fats arrangement
  • Plus: Yom Kippur incense arrangement

The Rabbis

  • Agree: 5 arrangements on Yom Kippur
  • Leftover limbs/fats returned to large arrangement — no 4th needed
  • Differ from Meir on normal days

🥇 The Five Types of Gold Paz (פז) — gleams like pearls; named for its lustrous glistening
Shaḥut (שחוט) — highly malleable, spun like thread; contraction of shenitve keḥut
Closed Gold (סגור) — so refined it is 'closed off' from impurities
Ophir Gold / Good Gold — superior grades rather than independent categories

Rav Ashi: Only 5 true types exist. 'Gold' and 'good gold' are not independent — each of the five types has a regular grade and a superior grade.

Ascends the altar ramp in the center — ordinary priests walk on the eastern side; the High Priest walks in the middle due to his eminence
↓
Sanctifies hands and feet from a golden flask — not from the communal laver, as befits his elevated status
↓
Extra handful of incense — a peras in the morning + a peras in the afternoon + an additional handful burned in the Holy of Holies

Five wood arrangements — a special extra arrangement on Yom Kippur for the incense burned before the Ark

🪵 How Many Wood Arrangements on the Altar?

Rabbi Yehuda
Normally just 2 arrangements. 'Fire on the altar all night' (Lev. 6:2) teaches: one large arrangement, one for incense. The third verse mention teaches only about thin wood chips for kindling — not a separate arrangement.
Rabbi Yosei
Normally 3 arrangements. 'Aaron's sons shall put fire on the altar' (Lev. 1:7) teaches that the kindling must happen atop the altar — this requires its own arrangement. So: large, incense, and fire-upkeep.
Rabbi Meir
Normally 4 arrangements. The phrase 'and the fire' is superfluous — it alludes to a fourth arrangement specifically for limbs and fats not fully consumed the previous evening.
The Rabbis
We disagree with Meir: leftover limbs and fats are simply returned to the large arrangement. But we agree there are 5 arrangements on Yom Kippur — the fifth is for the Yom Kippur incense coal pan.
Universal Agreement All opinions agree: an extra wood arrangement is added on Yom Kippur for the incense burned in the Holy of Holies. The debate is only about the count on regular days.

🕎 Proving the Candelabrum Fire Must Come from the Outer Altar

Where must the fire for lighting the Menorah (candelabrum) come from?
↓
Argument A
Compare to daily incense → both are inner-sanctuary acts → could use inner altar fire
Argument B
Compare to Yom Kippur coal pan → both use outer-sanctuary vessels → must use outer altar fire
↓
Two equally logical derivations cancel each other — a verse is required!
↓
"A perpetual fire shall be kept burning on the altar, it shall not go out" (Lev. 6:6) — the 'perpetual fire' I commanded must come from that altar: the outer altar

⛏️ Proving the Yom Kippur Coal Pan Fire Comes from the Outer Altar

Where must coals for the Yom Kippur incense coal pan come from?
↓
Argument A
Compare to candelabrum (just proven) → outer altar fire
Argument B
Compare to daily incense → inner altar fire?
↓
Again: two equally logical derivations — a verse is required!
↓
"He shall take a pan of coals from upon the altar before the Lord" (Lev. 16:12)
Both phrases needed: 'from upon the altar' = outer altar; 'before the Lord' rules out the inner altar being implied
