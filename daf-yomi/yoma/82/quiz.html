Yoma 82 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, at what age does a healthy boy begin completing the Yom Kippur fast by Torah law?
A. Age 10
B. Age 11
C. Age 12
D. Age 13
Correct: D — Boys reach halakhic majority (bar mitzvah) at 13, at which point the Torah obligation applies. Girls reach majority at 12. Ages 10–11 involve rabbinic completion for training purposes according to Rav Huna.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabba bar Shmuel's baraita, what is the distinction between training a child 'one year' vs. 'two years' before majority?
A. One year is for girls, two years is for boys
B. One year is for a feeble child, two years is for a healthy child
C. One year means partial fasting, two years means full fasting
D. There is no difference — the mishna is redundant
Correct: B — Rabba bar Shmuel's baraita resolves the apparent redundancy: 'one year before' applies to a feeble child who needs less preparation, while 'two years before' applies to a healthy child who trains longer.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When a pregnant woman craves forbidden food, what is the first measure taken before feeding her directly?
A. Ask three rabbis for a ruling
B. Give her a full portion immediately
C. Insert a reed with the food's juice and place it on her mouth
D. Wait and see if the craving passes on its own
Correct: C — The baraita teaches that one first uses a thin reed dipped in the juice of the craved food and places it on her mouth. If this settles her mind, she need not eat the food itself. Only if unsatisfied is she fed the item directly.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara derive that one must allow oneself to be killed rather than commit murder?
A. From the verse 'with all your soul'
B. From the case of the betrothed woman
C. Through pure logical reasoning — 'what makes your blood redder?'
D. From a teaching of Rabbi Eliezer about idol worship
Correct: C — Murder is uniquely derived through logical reasoning (sevara), not a biblical verse. Rava (or the Gemara) argues: if someone orders you to kill another or be killed, there is no rational ground to prefer your life — 'who says your blood is redder than his?'
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In both stories at the end of the daf, how did Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi and Rabbi Ḥanina respond to pregnant women craving food on Yom Kippur?
A. They immediately permitted the women to eat forbidden food
B. They ruled the women must fast regardless of craving
C. They instructed bystanders to whisper 'Today is Yom Kippur' to the women
D. They gave each woman a small partial portion to calm her craving
Correct: C — Both sages instructed others to whisper that today is Yom Kippur. In both cases the women's cravings subsided upon hearing this — demonstrating that spiritual awareness can itself resolve a danger, making the halakhic dispensation unnecessary.
