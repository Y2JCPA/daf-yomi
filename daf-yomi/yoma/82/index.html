Pregnant woman craving food

⚖️ Must Children Complete the Fast Before Majority?

Rav Huna
A child of 10–11 must complete the Yom Kippur fast by rabbinic law. The mishna's 'one year vs. two years' reflects the difference between completing the fast (one year) and mere training (two years).
Rabbi Yoḥanan
There is no rabbinic obligation to complete the fast before majority. Only gradual training applies — 'training' never means 'completion.'
Rabba bar Shmuel (baraita)
The distinction is between children: 'one year' applies to a feeble child, 'two years' to a healthy child — both refer only to training, not full completion.
Takeaway The debate hinges on whether pre-majority fasting can ever constitute a rabbinic obligation to complete, or whether it is purely educational training (חִינּוּך) in all cases.

🤰 Halakhic Procedure — Pregnant Woman Craving Food

Step 1: She smells food (even consecrated meat or pig meat) and is overcome by a craving — potential life-threatening situation
↓
Step 2: Insert a thin reed into the juice of the craved item and place it on her mouth
↓
Step 3: If her mind becomes settled — do not feed the forbidden food itself; she is satisfied
↓
Step 4: If her mind is not settled — feed her the food directly, even if forbidden (pig meat, consecrated meat, etc.)
↓
Alternative (Stories): Whisper to her 'Today is Yom Kippur' — sometimes spiritual awareness itself resolves the craving without any halakhic override
↓
Principle: וָחַי בָּהֶם — 'You shall live by them,' not die by them (Lev. 18:5)

☠️ The Three Cardinal Sins — יֵהָרֵג וְאַל יַעֲבֹר

עֲבוֹדָה זָרָה Idol Worship

  • Source: 'With all your soul' — Deut. 6:5
  • R. Eliezer: if 'with all your might' means even your money, 'with all your soul' means even your life
  • Give your life rather than worship idols
  • Applies even under coercion

גִּלּוּי עֲרָיוֹת Forbidden Relations

  • Source: Case of the betrothed woman (Deut. 22:26)
  • Others may kill the pursuer to save her
  • Therefore: she herself must die rather than submit
  • Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi derives via kal v'chomer

שְׁפִיכַת דָּמִים Murder

  • Source: Pure logic (סְבָרָא) — no verse needed
  • Rava: 'What makes you think your blood is redder than his?'
  • If told 'kill another or be killed' — let yourself be killed
  • No rational basis to prefer one life over another's

לָחֲשׁוּ לָהּ שֶׁהַיּוֹם יוֹם הַכִּפּוּרִים — They whispered to her: Today is Yom Kippur (Yoma 82b)
📖 The Power of Awareness — Two Stories Story 1 (Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi): A pregnant woman smelled food and craved it on Yom Kippur. He instructed: 'Whisper to her that today is Yom Kippur.' Her craving subsided — no food needed.

Story 2 (Rabbi Ḥanina): The same scenario, the same instruction. Upon hearing it was Yom Kippur, the woman said: 'Had I known, I would not have craved it' — and needed nothing.

Lesson: Sometimes the halakhic exception need not be invoked at all. Spiritual consciousness can itself be the remedy.
