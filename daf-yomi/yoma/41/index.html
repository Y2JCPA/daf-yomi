🎲 Core Question: When Are Offerings Designated?

Rav Ḥisda's Rule: Offerings (nests/bird pairs) are designated only at the moment of taking them or at the moment of performing the sacrificial rite

🎲 Rav Ḥisda's Ruling — Challenges and Resolutions

Rav Ḥisda
Bird pairs (nests) become designated for their specific role as sin-offering or burnt-offering only at the time of taking them or at the time of performing the sacrificial rite — not by verbal declaration in between.
Baraita Objection
The baraita on Yom Kippur goats focuses on the lottery moment — which falls between taking and rite. This implies actions at that intermediate moment can designate! Contradiction to Rav Ḥisda?
Rava
No contradiction. The baraita uses the lottery to prove: even a lottery — which doesn't normally consecrate — still works here by verse; verbal designation nonetheless does not. The moment is irrelevant.
Second Objection (Sliding-Scale)
A baraita about sliding-scale offerings discusses a person designating money between taking birds and performing the rite — again contradicting Rav Ḥisda!
Rav Sheshet / Rav Pappa
Resolved: read that case as where the person designated while still poor (Rav Sheshet), or as where he had taken only one bird, not both (Rav Pappa). Either way, no real contradiction to Rav Ḥisda.
Key Verse "He shall make it a sin-offering" (Lev. 16:9) — the lottery designates; verbal declaration alone does not.

🙏 Mishna: High Priest's Second Confession Ritual

Step 1 — Crimson Strip: High Priest ties a strip of crimson wool (לָשׁוֹן שֶׁל זְהוֹרִית) to the head of the scapegoat (שָׂעִיר הַמִּשְׁתַּלֵּחַ)
↓
Step 2 — Positioning the Scapegoat: The scapegoat is positioned facing the gate from which it will be dispatched into the wilderness
↓
Step 3 — Positioning the Slaughtered Goat: The same positioning ritual is performed for the goat designated to be slaughtered — placed opposite its own location
↓
Step 4 — Return to Bull: High Priest returns and places both hands upon his bull a second time, entering into the full confession posture
↓
Step 5 — Confession Formula: "אָנָּא הַשֵּׁם — Please God, I have sinned, I have done wrong, and I have rebelled before You — I and my family and the children of Aaron Your holy people. Please, by Your name, grant atonement!"

🔴 Three Crimson Strips — Requirements Compared

Scapegoat Strip

  • Tied to the head of the scapegoat on Yom Kippur
  • Must be divided before the goat descends into Azazel
  • Half remains on the goat's head; half tied to the cliff
  • Requires a minimum amount — must be divisible into two functional halves
  • Rav Yosef: the need to divide is why a minimum is required

Red Heifer Strip

  • Cast into the fire while the heifer burns (Num. 19:6)
  • Must reach the heart of the fire — not just catch flame at the edge
  • Rav Yosef: requires weight so it falls into the burning mass
  • Rami bar Ḥama: this weight requirement is its own minimum-amount basis
  • No division — burned entirely in the fire

Leper's Strip

  • Part of the purification ritual of the מְצֹרָע (person with tzara'at)
  • Tied with cedar wood and hyssop to a living bird for sprinkling
  • Serves a distinct purification function from the other two strips
  • Rav Dimi (R. Yochanan): also requires a minimum amount
  • Each of the three strips has its own unique ritual role and minimum
