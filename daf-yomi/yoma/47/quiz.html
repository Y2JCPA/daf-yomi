Yoma 47 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the High Priest use a spoon for the incense during the Yom Kippur service?
A. The Torah explicitly commands the use of a spoon
B. It is impossible to bring the coal pan and incense in one act without it
C. Using bare hands would make the incense impure
D. A spoon allows precise measurement of the quantity
Correct: B — The Gemara demonstrates it is impossible to perform the service any other way — the Torah requires one combined act of bringing, and without the spoon there is no feasible method.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In which hand does the High Priest carry the coal pan, and why?
A. Left hand, because the incense spoon is primary
B. Right hand, because it is heavier with live coals
C. Either hand — there is no fixed halakhic rule
D. Right hand, because it is the primary service vessel
Correct: B — The Gemara explains the arrangement is for comfort — the coal pan is heavier because it contains coals, so it goes in the stronger right hand. Even when the weights are equal, the arrangement is not changed.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What merit did Kimḥit attribute to having all seven sons serve as High Priests?
A. She donated generously to the Temple treasury
B. She studied laws of purity with great diligence
C. The beams of her house never saw the braids of her hair
D. She fasted every Monday and Thursday throughout her life
Correct: C — Kimḥit said she was so modest that even within her own home the beams never saw her uncovered hair. The Sages noted that while others practiced hair covering, her level was uniquely extraordinary.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the halakhic status of flour caught between the fingers during the scooping of a meal-offering handful?
A. Definitely part of the handful — burned together with it
B. Definitely part of the remainder — eaten by the priests
C. Doubtful — burned separately after the handful is burned
D. Completely invalid and must be discarded
Correct: C — Yehoshua ben Uza'a resolved his own dilemma: the in-between flour is of doubtful status. Rabbi Ḥanina rules that the handful is burned first, and then the in-between flour is burned separately.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, what is the standard (non-disputed) technique for taking a handful of meal-offering?
A. Using a measuring vessel and then transferring to the hand
B. Fingertips pointed downward into the vessel, scooping flour upward
C. Back of hand downward, fingers collecting flour upward into the palm
D. Passing the hand across the sides of the vessel
Correct: B — Rav Pappa states it is obvious that the ab initio method is fingertips downward — this is the normal human way of taking a handful. His dilemmas concern non-standard techniques whose validity is uncertain.
