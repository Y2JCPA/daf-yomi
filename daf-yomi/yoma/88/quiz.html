QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the baraita, where is havdala inserted in the evening Amida after Yom Kippur?
A. In Modim (the thanksgiving blessing)
B. In Atah Ḥonen (the blessing for wisdom/knowledge)
C. As a separate blessing recited after the Amida
D. In Shema Koleinu (Hear our voice)
Correct: B — Havdala is inserted into Atah Ḥonen — the first middle blessing of the weekday Amida — because it concerns wisdom and distinction, and there is no equivalent slot in the abridged ne'ila prayer, proving Maariv must still be recited.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the Rabbis' position on 'tevila bizmanah' (immersion at its proper time)?
A. It is a Torah-level commandment requiring immersion before sunrise
B. It is a rabbinic mitzva requiring immersion before the final prayer of the day
C. It is not a mitzva, permitting delay until the following day if necessary
D. It is optional and may be skipped entirely on Yom Kippur
Correct: C — The Rabbis hold that tevila bizmanah is NOT a mitzva — therefore, if immersing on Yom Kippur is impossible (e.g., no reed to hold between teeth), one may simply wait until the following day.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the contradiction between the two baraitot about Rabbi Yosei's ruling on Yom Kippur immersion?
A. Rabbi Yosei retracted his earlier opinion in a later teaching
B. The two baraitot address different types of ritual impurity
C. The 'Rabbi Yosei' prohibiting daytime immersion is actually Rabbi Yosei bar Yehuda
D. One baraita reflects Babylonian custom, the other Palestinian
Correct: C — The Gemara resolves the contradiction by identifying the Rabbi Yosei of the restrictive baraita as Rabbi Yosei bar Yehuda — a distinct sage who holds that immersion need only be done by end of day, which coheres with the other ruling.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the school of Rabbi Yishmael, what should a person do who sees a seminal emission on Yom Kippur?
A. Rejoice, as it is a sign his prayers were accepted and sins forgiven
B. Worry the entire year that he and his fast may have been rejected
C. Immediately confess all sins out loud before the congregation
D. Fast an additional day as a form of atonement
Correct: B — Rabbi Yishmael's school treats the emission as potentially ominous — a sign of divine rejection. However, if the person survives the year, this proves his accumulated merits protected him despite the troubling sign.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara question the instruction to 'rub the skin in the evening' after a Yom Kippur emission?
A. Rubbing violates the prohibition against washing on Yom Kippur
B. The emission itself purifies the skin, making rubbing unnecessary
C. The Gemara asks: why wait until evening — why not rub immediately?
D. Rubbing on Yom Kippur may unintentionally cause a new emission
Correct: C — The Gemara is surprised that the baraita specifies 'in the evening' — he should rub immediately when he notices! The answer is that the evening rubbing is specifically to prepare for a clean, unobstructed immersion once Yom Kippur has ended.
