⚔️ Rome vs. Persia: Who Conquers Whom?

Rabba bar bar Ḥana (R. Yoḥanan / R. Yehuda b. Elai)
Rome will fall to Persia. Kal va-chomer: if the First Temple (built by Shem's descendants) was destroyed by Babylonia (also Shem's kin), all the more so the Second Temple — built with Persia's help — should fall back into Persia's hands.
Rav
Persia will fall to Rome. Students asked: 'The builders fall to the destroyers?!' Rav replied: 'Yes — it is the King's divine decree.' A baraita confirms: Persia destroyed synagogues, and the decree stands.
Underlying Principle גְּזֵרַת מֶלֶךְ הִיא — It is the King's decree. Divine justice sometimes transcends human logic; builders fall to destroyers by heavenly design.

⚔️ The Rome–Persia Prophecy — Four Voices

R. Yehoshua b. Levi (citing R. Yehuda HaNasi)
Rome falls to Persia. Jeremiah 49:20 — 'The youngest of the flock shall drag them away.' Persia is the youngest nation; it will conquer Edom (= Rome).
Rabba bar Ulla (clarifying objection)
How do we know Persia is 'youngest'? From Daniel 8:20 — the ram's two horns are Media and Persia. The smaller horn that grew later is Persia — confirming it is the youngest.
Rabba bar bar Ḥana (R. Yoḥanan / R. Yehuda b. Elai)
Independent proof: Kal va-chomer from the Temples. If the First Temple's builders (Shem) were undone by their own kin, surely the Second Temple built by Persia will return to Persia.
Rav (+ baraita support)
Persia falls to Rome. Divine decree: builders fall to destroyers. Persia aided the Second Temple's construction; Rome destroyed it — so Rome takes Persia. Reinforced: Persia also destroyed synagogues.
Textual Anchor צְעִירֵי הַצֹּאן — 'Youngest of the flock' (Jer. 49:20) = Persia, identified via the smaller horn in Daniel's vision

📜 Mezuza in the Parhedrin Chamber — The Dispute

The Rabbis

  • Parhedrin chamber requires a mezuza by Torah law
  • Reason: The High Priest resides there for 7 days before Yom Kippur
  • דִּירַת אֹנֶס שְׁמָהּ דִּירָה — a compelled residence is still legally a 'dwelling'
  • No special decree needed — the obligation arises naturally from the stay
  • Consistent with: involuntary lodging creates full halakhic obligations

Rabbi Yehuda

  • Mezuza was present only due to a royal/divine decree
  • Reason: Residence due to a mitzva or compulsion ≠ true dwelling
  • דִּירַת מִצְוָה לָא שְׁמָהּ דִּירָה — mitzva-compelled stay is not a 'dwelling'
  • Without the decree, the chamber would be exempt like all other Temple chambers
  • Consistent with: R. Yehuda requires permanent-style dwelling for sukka too

🏠 Abaye & Rava Untangle the Sukka-Mezuza Connection

Rava's initial explanation: R. Yehuda holds a temporary stay creates only a rabbinic mezuza obligation — not a Torah-level one. Hence the 'decree' language.
↓
Abaye objects (from sukka): If R. Yehuda treats Parhedrin as a rabbinic 'house,' he should also require tithing produce brought into a sukka — yet he doesn't! The sukka would 'fix' the produce like a house.
↓
Abaye's alternative: During the full 7 days, everyone agrees the chamber is a dwelling. The dispute concerns other times — after/before the sequestering period.
↓
Rava refutes Abaye: The Mishna says 'a sukka built on the festival' — implying the dispute is precisely about the active use period, not before or after.
↓
Final resolution: Both disputes (sukka + mezuza) hinge on the same principle — R. Yehuda requires a well-built, permanent-style structure. A mitzva-compelled temporary stay never meets that standard, on Torah or rabbinic level.
