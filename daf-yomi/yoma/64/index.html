⚖️ What Happens When One Yom Kippur Goat Dies?

Rav
If the Azazel goat dies, sacrifice the surviving goat of the first pair to God. Living beings are not permanently rejected — ein dichuy b'chai.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
The surviving goat of the first pair grazes until it becomes unfit. Living beings ARE permanently rejected once disqualified — yesh dichuy b'chai.
The Core Dispute Once a consecrated living animal is set aside as unfit, does it permanently lose its eligibility — or does it retain sanctity and remain eligible if circumstances change?

📖 Deriving from Blemished Animals — The Textual Dispute

Rav
Derived from temporary blemish (מום עובר): an animal disqualified by a blemish may be sacrificed once healed. So too, any living being set aside as unfit retains eligibility when circumstances change.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
The verse says בָּהֶם — 'in them.' Only blemished animals are re-accepted once healed. Other disqualified living beings remain permanently rejected.
Rav
בָּהֶם means: on their own they are not accepted — but mixed with fit animals, temporarily blemished limbs are valid. As Rabbi Eliezer ruled in the case of mixed burnt-offering limbs.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
The mixture ruling is derived from the Torah's choice of בָּהֶם vs. בם — the fuller spelling includes an extra lesson. The basic bam form would suffice; bahem teaches the mixture leniency separately.
Verse in Dispute "Because their corruption is in them, there is a blemish in them; they shall not be accepted for you" (Leviticus 22:25)

🏛️ Full Position Comparison: Rav vs. Rabbi Yoḥanan

Rav

  • Living beings are NOT permanently rejected (אין דחייה בחי)
  • Surviving goat of first pair is sacrificed to God
  • Second goat of second pair grazes until unfit
  • Derives from: temporary blemish — animal heals and is then offered
  • Follows Rabbi Yosei: first-designated animal takes priority
  • Aligns with the Rabbis, not Rabbi Yehuda

Rabbi Yoḥanan

  • Living beings ARE permanently rejected once disqualified (יש דחייה בחי)
  • Surviving goat of first pair grazes until unfit
  • Second pair's goat is sacrificed to God
  • Derives: 'בָּהֶם' limits leniency to blemished animals only
  • Mishna's language supports him: asks 'which one shall graze?'
  • Rabbi Yehuda and Rabbis both agree with him — for different reasons

🥇 Rabbi Yosei — The First-Designated Animal Takes Priority

Rabbi Yosei's general rule: When two animals become eligible at the same time, the first one designated for the mitzvah takes priority over the replacement.
↓
Rejected source — Shekalim coins (מעות): Rabbi Yosei ruled that the first-designated coins take priority. But this case differs — the later container was not yet fit when the first was designated. Not a true parallel.
↓
Correct source — Paschal lamb (קרבן פסח): One separated an animal as his Paschal lamb; it was lost; he separated another. When both are simultaneously available, the first is offered — unless the second is superior.
↓
Key condition: Both animals must become fit at the same moment. Only then does the first-designated animal take automatic priority by virtue of its earlier designation.
↓
Rav's application: The Azazel goat of the first pair was designated first; when it survives alongside the second pair, it retains its priority and is offered to God.
