Yoma 64 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav, if the Azazel goat dies after the lottery and a second pair is brought, what happens to the surviving goat of the first pair?
A. It grazes until it becomes unfit for sacrifice
B. It is sacrificed to God
C. A new lottery is held between it and the second pair
D. It is sent off the cliff in place of the Azazel goat
Correct: B — Rav holds ein dichuy b'chai — living beings are not permanently rejected. The surviving goat of the first pair retains its eligibility and is sacrificed to God, with the second pair's goat grazing until unfit.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
From which halakhic case does Rav derive that living beings are not permanently rejected?
A. An animal that is too young (lacking time)
B. A permanently blemished animal
C. An animal with a temporary blemish
D. A communal sin-offering that became unfit
Correct: C — Rav derives from the temporary blemish (mum over): an animal disqualified while blemished regains its eligibility once the blemish heals, proving that living beings are not permanently rejected.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yoḥanan limit the leniency for temporarily blemished animals using Leviticus 22:25?
A. The word לָכֶם (for you) restricts it to Israelites
B. The word בָּהֶם (in them) limits the leniency to blemished animals only
C. The verse applies only to Paschal lambs, not Yom Kippur offerings
D. The verse only addresses animals that were never consecrated
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan reads בָּהֶם as 'it is they — blemished animals — that are re-accepted when healed.' This explicitly limits the leniency to that category, leaving other disqualified living beings permanently rejected.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which ruling of Rabbi Yosei does Rav ultimately use to support giving priority to the first pair's goat?
A. The ruling about Shekalim half-shekel coins in containers
B. The ruling about a lost and replaced Paschal lamb
C. The ruling about a blemished goat before the Yom Kippur lottery
D. The ruling about communal sin-offerings that cannot die
Correct: B — The Paschal lamb ruling is the correct source: when the lost animal is found and both animals are simultaneously fit, the first-designated one takes priority unless the second is superior. The Shekalim case is rejected as a parallel because the containers were not simultaneously fit.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda in the mishna, if the blood of the goat sacrificed to God spilled before being sprinkled on Yom Kippur, what happens to the scapegoat?
A. It is sent off the cliff immediately to complete whatever is possible
B. It is sacrificed on the altar in place of the first goat
C. It is left to die
D. A new pair of goats is purchased and the entire service restarted
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehuda holds that disqualified animals — even communal offerings — are permanently rejected. Once the God-goat's blood is invalidated, the scapegoat is left to die rather than being sent. The Rabbis disagree, holding communal sin-offerings are never left to die.
