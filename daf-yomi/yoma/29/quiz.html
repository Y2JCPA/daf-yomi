Yoma 29 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What mnemonic does the Gemara use to illustrate that thoughts of transgression are worse than transgression itself?
A. A cold oven that requires more heat to warm than a hot oven
B. The smell of roasting meat being more appetizing than eating the meat
C. Moonlight appearing brighter than sunlight from a distance
D. A hind beloved to her mate at each renewed encounter
Correct: B — The Gemara uses the smell of roasting meat — the aroma is more enticing and stimulating than actually eating the meat — to illustrate that desire (the thought) is stronger and more powerful than its fulfillment.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Zeira, why is Queen Esther specifically likened to a hind (אַיֶּלֶת)?
A. Because she was swift and graceful like a deer fleeing danger
B. Because she was hunted by her enemies as a hind is hunted
C. Because just as a hind is beloved to her mate anew at each encounter, Esther found renewed miraculous favor before Ahasuerus each time
D. Because she emerged from hiding as a hind emerges from the forest
Correct: C — Rabbi Zeira explains that a hind's womb is narrow and she is beloved to her mate as if for the first time at each encounter. So too, Esther's favor before Ahasuerus was miraculously renewed each time she appeared — a fresh miracle every hour.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why is Esther considered the end of miracles recorded in Scripture, despite Hanukkah occurring centuries later?
A. The Rabbis simply overlooked Hanukkah when finalizing the canon
B. Hanukkah was a minor miracle unworthy of scriptural recording
C. Permission was not granted to record the Hanukkah miracle in Scripture, unlike Purim
D. Hanukkah's miracle was performed by the Hasmoneans, not by God directly
Correct: C — The Gemara explicitly answers: additional miracles were performed after Purim, but Purim was the last miracle for which permission was granted to record it in the written Scripture (Tanakh). Hanukkah's miracle, while real, was never incorporated into Tanakh.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the father of Rabbi Avin, what is the ruling if the kometz (handful) of a meal-offering was removed at night?
A. The handful may be re-taken the following morning and the offering is valid
B. The offering is disqualified and must be burned — it cannot be restored
C. The offering is valid but the priest must perform a corrective immersion
D. The offering is held overnight and becomes valid at the next dawn
Correct: B — The father of Rabbi Avin teaches that night-time Temple service disqualifies — not merely delays. Even the handful of a meal-offering removed at night renders the entire offering disqualified, and it must be burned. A new handful cannot restore it.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Ravina resolve the contradiction between the baraita (night service disqualifies) and the mishna (shewbread arranged on Sunday simply waits until next Shabbat)?
A. The baraita applies only to animal sacrifices, not to bread or meal-offerings
B. Shewbread is a special exception and immune to the night-service disqualification rule
C. The shewbread is only consecrated when Shabbat day arrives — Sunday night arrangement precedes consecration, so no 'night service' has occurred
D. The mishna follows a minority opinion that night service is merely premature throughout
Correct: C — Ravina explains that shewbread is not consecrated until Shabbat day itself arrives. Therefore, arranging it on Sunday night is merely placing bread on a table — the consecration has not yet occurred. There is no disqualifying 'night service' because no service has yet taken place. The baraita addresses offerings already in a consecrated state.
