⚖️ Resolving the Contradiction: Which Mishna Follows Which Tanna?

Rav Pappa
The mishnayot follow different tannaim: the first mishna (incense first) follows the Rabbis; the middle clause follows Rabbi Shimon Ish HaMitzpa; the final clause returns to the Rabbis.
Abaye
I reject that — it makes one mishna follow three different tannaim. Better: the first mishna gives a general directive for the High Priest's preparation week, while the later mishna gives the precise Yom Kippur sequence.
Bottom Line Abaye: first mishna = general overview of services the Kohen Gadol should know; later mishna = actual Yom Kippur order. One tanna throughout — no splitting required.

🔥 Burnt-Offering vs Sin-Offering: Sprinkling Procedures

Burnt-Offering (Olah)

  • Blood sprinkled on two corners: northeast and southwest
  • Each sprinkling covers two walls → counts as four
  • Applied to the upper half of the altar (above the red line)
  • Called a sprinkling that is two — one throw, dual coverage

Sin-Offering (Chatat)

  • Blood sprinkled on four corners separately
  • Applied to the lower half of the altar (below the red line)
  • One precise sprinkling per corner
  • Procedures are non-interchangeable — blood cannot switch mid-rite

✨ What Is Tohoro of the Incense Altar?

First Proposal
Tohoro refers to the middle section of the incense altar — suggesting blood was sprinkled half above and half below, proving a split above/below-the-line precedent exists.
Rabba bar Sheila
No — tohoro means the top surface of the altar: the gleaming pure gold visible after ashes are cleared. Proof: "Like the very sky for purity [latohar]" (Exodus 24:10).
Ruling Tohoro = the pure gold top of the incense altar after ashes are swept away — not a middle section. The proposed proof that blood was ever split above/below the red line collapses.

🏛️ Four Chambers of the Beit HaMoked

ארבע לשכות היו בבית המוקד — משנה מידות
לשכת בית המוקד — Hall of the Hearth Four chambers opened into the Hall of the Hearth like small rooms (kitoniyyot) opening into a large central hall (teraklin). Two chambers were located in the sacred (kodesh) area; two chambers were in the non-sacred (chol) area. This layout sets up the next dispute: which chamber was used for the southern function, and what does its sacred/non-sacred status imply for the Kohen Gadol's movements on Yom Kippur?
