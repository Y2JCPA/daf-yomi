📜 Yoma 69 — Temple, Prayer, and the Struggle Against Evil

כְּנֶסֶת הַגְּדוֹלָה — The Great Assembly
Central Themes Three grand threads interweave: (1) a Talmudic debate on whether priests may sleep upon their sacred vestments and whether shatnez (diverse kinds) in the High Priest's belt is implicated; (2) the legendary encounter of Shimon HaTzaddik with Alexander the Great that rescued the Temple from Samaritan scheming; and (3) the theological audacity of Ezra and the Great Assembly, who restored divine epithets in prayer and — in a breathtaking moment — actually captured the evil inclination for idolatry.

🛌 May Priests Derive Benefit from Priestly Vestments?

First View
The mishna says priests place vestments under their heads — learn from this that it is permitted to derive benefit from priestly vestments.
Objection
The mishna only permits placing them next to their heads, not underneath. Sleeping on them would violate the prohibition of shatnez (diverse kinds woven into the High Priest's belt).
Counter-argument
Shatnez is only violated by wearing — spreading it out and lying upon it should be permitted.
Rav Yehoshua ben Levi / Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi was asked directly: Is lying upon shatnez permitted? He gave no clear answer — the question remained unresolved.
Rav Ashi
The vestments may indeed be placed under their heads — since priestly vestments are consecrated Temple property (hekdesh), personal benefit from them falls outside ordinary prohibitions.
Conclusion The mishna's intent remains uncertain; no definitive proof can be derived from it on whether benefit from priestly vestments is permitted or forbidden.

🏛️ Shimon HaTzaddik Meets Alexander the Great

The Samaritans' Plot (25th of Tevet): The Samaritans petitioned Alexander the Macedonian to destroy the Temple, and he granted them permission.
↓
Shimon HaTzaddik Acts: He donned his priestly vestments and led Jerusalem's nobles out by torchlight — some from one side, some from the other — to intercept Alexander.
↓
The Encounter at Antipatris: When Alexander saw Shimon HaTzaddik approaching, he descended from his chariot and bowed before him.
↓
Alexander Explains: 'Before every battle I see this man's image in a vision leading me to victory — I honor the divine image he embodies.'
↓
Shimon's Appeal: 'Is it possible that the Temple — where we pray for you and your kingdom — should be destroyed by those who mislead you?'
↓
The Samaritans Punished: Stabbed in their heels, dragged by horses to Mount Gerizim — their own temple on Gerizim was destroyed instead. This day became a holiday!

🔥 Capturing the Evil Inclination for Idolatry

Ezra's Generation Prays: 'It is this evil inclination that caused our exile — let us destroy it!' They cried out: Woe, woe!
↓
Heaven Responds: A note fell from the heavens inscribed with the word Truth — God accepted their prayer
↓
They fasted three days and nights — a fiery lion cub emerged from the Holy of Holies (the evil inclination for idolatry)
↓
Problem: Can't Kill It
One hair fell — its shriek was heard 400 parasangs away. Feared Heaven would show mercy on something in such pain.
Then: Sexual Inclination
Emboldened, they prayed to destroy the sexual evil inclination too. It was delivered — but Zechariah warned: the world would be destroyed!
↓
Compromise: They blinded the sexual inclination instead of destroying it — egg-laying ceased but the world was not destroyed. Idolatry vanished from Israel.

📖 The Great Assembly — Restoring God's Attributes in Prayer

Moses' Original Formula

  • Called God: 'The great, mighty, and awesome God'
  • Based on direct experience of divine revelation and miracles
  • Full expression of all three divine attributes
  • Set as the standard formula for prayer

Jeremiah Omits 'Awesome'

  • Omitted 'awesome' from prayer
  • Reasoning: Foreigners rampage in His Temple — where is His awesomeness?
  • Not false testimony — honest witness to the Temple's destruction
  • Authentic prayer reflecting lived suffering

Daniel Omits 'Mighty'

  • Omitted 'mighty' from prayer
  • Reasoning: Foreigners enslave His children — where is His might?
  • Not false — authentic expression of exile and subjugation
  • A prophet cannot speak falsehood even in prayer

Great Assembly Restores All Three

  • Restored: great, mighty, AND awesome
  • 'His might = He restrains Himself toward the wicked'
  • 'His awesomeness = Israel survives among hostile nations'
  • Authority: They knew the Torah's truth would not be falsified — bold reinterpretation, not erasure
