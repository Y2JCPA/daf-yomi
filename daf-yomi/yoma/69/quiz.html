QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Rav Ashi say that priests may place their vestments under their heads without concern for deriving prohibited benefit?
A. The prohibition of diverse kinds (shatnez) does not apply to the High Priest's belt at all
B. Since the vestments are consecrated Temple property (hekdesh), the usual personal-benefit prohibitions do not apply to individual priests
C. Sleeping is not considered 'wearing' and thus no shatnez violation is possible
D. The vestments are only prohibited during Temple service, not while priests rest
Correct: B — Rav Ashi argues that priestly vestments belong to the Temple treasury (hekdesh) — they are not the priests' personal property. Therefore, personal benefit derived from them falls outside the ordinary prohibition framework that governs private garments.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was Alexander the Great's reason for descending from his chariot and bowing before Shimon HaTzaddik?
A. He recognized the High Priestly vestments as a symbol of universal divine authority
B. His advisors told him to honor the Jewish nation's religious leader
C. Before every battle, he saw a vision of this man's image leading him to victory
D. He feared a Jewish military counterattack if he showed disrespect
Correct: C — Alexander explained that before each battle he would see the image of Shimon HaTzaddik in a vision leading him to victory — so he bowed to honor the living person whose image had guided his military successes.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How did the Great Assembly justify restoring 'mighty' and 'awesome' to the prayer formula after Jeremiah and Daniel had omitted them?
A. They ruled that Jeremiah and Daniel had committed a halachic error that needed correction
B. They reinterpreted the attributes: God's patience with the wicked IS His might; Israel's survival among nations IS His awesomeness
C. They received a direct prophetic command to restore the original Mosaic formula
D. They ruled that prayers during exile were invalid and needed to be reset to the original
Correct: B — The Great Assembly reinterpreted rather than contradicted: restraining divine punishment while the wicked prosper demonstrates God's truest might (He overcomes His own inclination to punish), and Israel's miraculous survival among hostile nations demonstrates His awesomeness.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When the Sages captured the evil inclination for idolatry and considered also eliminating the evil inclination for sexual sin, what did Zechariah the prophet warn?
A. Destroying both evil inclinations would cause Israel to become arrogant and forget God
B. Heaven would not permit two such requests in a single generation
C. Destroying the sexual evil inclination would end procreation and destroy the world
D. The evil inclination for sexual sin was too powerful to be captured
Correct: C — Zechariah warned that the evil inclination for sexual relationships is also the drive for procreation — eliminating it would mean the end of all reproduction and the destruction of the world. So instead they merely blinded it, reducing (but not eliminating) its power.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Abaye, what condition must be met for skipping between Torah portions during public reading to be permitted?
A. The congregation must explicitly consent to the skip before the reading begins
B. The reader must be able to complete the skip within the time it takes to roll the Torah scroll
C. Both sections must pertain to the same topic so the congregation will not be left confused or waiting
D. Skipping in Torah is never permitted — only in Prophets
Correct: C — Abaye distinguishes: in Torah reading, skipping is only permitted when both sections share a single topic (preventing listener confusion or delay). The Yom Kippur portions — 'After the Death' and 'But on the Tenth' — both deal with Yom Kippur, satisfying this condition.
