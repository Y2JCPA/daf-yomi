Yoma 56 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did the Sages not use two pedestals with written labels to distinguish the blood bowls for the High Priest?
A. Writing was prohibited inside the Temple on Yom Kippur
B. The High Priest was fasting all day and too weakened to keep written labels in mind
C. There was insufficient space in the Sanctuary for two stands
D. Rabbi Yehuda ruled that writing is halakhically invalid for ritual identification
Correct: B — The Gemara explicitly explains that because the High Priest fasts throughout the entire day's service, he grows weak and the writing will not stay on his mind — he may pay no attention to it and err.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, why does Rabbi Yehuda permit the east/west eiruv for a single Sage even though he rejects Breira?
A. Eiruv is rabbinic, and Breira only applies to Torah-level laws
B. The Sage had already arrived at one side by twilight — reality was determined; only the person's knowledge was lacking
C. Rabbi Yehuda makes a blanket exception when only one Sage is involved
D. The eiruv was deposited before the question arose, removing the need for clarification
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan teaches this is not true Breira at all: the Sage physically arrived at one location by twilight. The condition resolved itself in reality; the person simply did not yet know which side.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was the specific error Rava identified in the prayer leader's description of the Yom Kippur service?
A. He described the sprinkling order as upward before downward, reversing it
B. He forgot to include the goat's blood in the innermost sanctum
C. He combined Rabbi Yehuda's single-pedestal framework with the Rabbis' two-pedestal sprinkling description
D. He said the High Priest sprinkled seven times instead of eight
Correct: C — Rava explains that one part of the prayer followed Rabbi Yehuda (single pedestal) while another followed the Rabbis (their description of how the bowls were managed). The two positions are mutually exclusive.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What physical distinction prevents the High Priest from confusing the bull's blood with the goat's blood?
A. The bull's blood bowl is significantly larger due to the animal's greater size
B. The goat's blood is white and bright while the bull's blood is red and dark
C. The bowls are made of different metals — gold for bull, silver for goat
D. The goat's blood is thicker, making it obviously different in consistency
Correct: B — The Gemara states that goat's blood appears white and bright relative to the bull's blood, and the bull's blood is red and dark relative to the goat's blood — the visual difference is unmistakable.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the sprinkling pattern the High Priest performs in both the Holy of Holies and the Sanctuary?
A. Seven times upward and once downward from each blood
B. Once upward and seven times downward from both the bull's blood and the goat's blood
C. Eight sprinklings in total, all directed downward
D. Seven alternating sprinklings per blood, totaling fourteen per location
Correct: B — The Gemara establishes that in both locations the High Priest sprinkles once upward and seven times downward from the bull's blood, and repeats the same pattern — once upward and seven times downward — from the goat's blood.
