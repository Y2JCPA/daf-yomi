🔄 Does Rabbi Yehuda Accept Breira?

Rabbi Meir
Breira works! Retroactive clarification is valid — a condition can be resolved after the fact and applied back to the original act.
Rabbi Yehuda
No Breira! A condition must be clear at the moment of the act. Future events cannot retroactively clarify what already happened.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
The east/west eiruv case is different — the Sage had ALREADY arrived by twilight. The condition was resolved in reality; the person merely lacked knowledge of it.
Rava
The prayer leader described the service mixing Rabbi Yehuda's pedestal count with the Rabbis' framework. One statement contradicts the other — you cannot blend the two positions!
Core Question Does Rabbi Yehuda accept Breira, and how does this ruling shape the Yom Kippur blood service — specifically, which bowl sits on which pedestal?

⚖️ Rabbi Yehuda's Rules for Eiruv Conditions

Permitted: 'If the Sage comes from the east, my eiruv is to the east; if he comes from the west, my eiruv is to the west' — one Sage, two possible outcomes
↓
Forbidden: 'If Sage A comes from here AND Sage B comes from there, I will go wherever I choose' — two conditions stipulated simultaneously
↓
Source (Ayo's baraita): Rabbi Yehuda explicitly says a person cannot stipulate conditions about two matters at once — this proves he rejects Breira
↓
Resolution (Rabbi Yoḥanan): The permitted east/west eiruv is not true Breira — the Sage arrived by twilight; reality was already determined, the person simply lacked knowledge
↓
Principle: Absence of knowledge ≠ absence of determination. If reality was fixed at the critical halakhic moment, no retroactive clarification is required

📝 One Pedestal or Two? The Blood Bowl Dispute

The Rabbis
Two pedestals — one for the bull's blood and one for the goat's blood. Each bowl rests on its own dedicated stand throughout the service.
Rabbi Yehuda
Only ONE pedestal. The High Priest transfers the bowls back and forth on a single stand as he alternates between the bull and goat blood.
Gemara
Why not use two pedestals with written labels identifying each bowl? Because the High Priest is fasting and weakened — the writing will not stay on his mind and he may err!
Rava
The prayer leader said the High Priest 'placed the spoon on the pedestal' (singular — Rabbi Yehuda) yet described sprinkling as the Rabbis do. He combined incompatible positions!
⚠️ Halakhic Conflict One cannot describe the Yom Kippur service by blending the Rabbinic and Rabbi Yehuda frameworks — the pedestal count and the bowl management are inseparable.

🩸 Sprinkling in the Two Locations

Holy of Holies (Kodesh HaKodashim)

  • Bull's blood: 1 upward + 7 downward
  • Goat's blood: 1 upward + 7 downward
  • Sprinkled on the parokhet (curtain) facing the Ark
  • Performed inside the innermost chamber

Sanctuary (Heikhal)

  • Bull's blood: 1 upward + 7 downward
  • Goat's blood: 1 upward + 7 downward
  • Sprinkled on the curtain opposite the Ark from outside
  • Pattern mirrors the innermost sanctum exactly

🔴 Why No Mix-Up Between the Two Bowls? The Gemara asks: why aren't we concerned the High Priest will confuse the bull's blood bowl with the goat's blood bowl? Answer 1 (Rav Yehuda): The High Priest must collect all of the bull's blood — the bowl will be noticeably fuller/larger, distinguishing it naturally. Answer 2 (Visual): The goat's blood is white and bright compared to the bull's blood, while the bull's blood is red and dark compared to the goat's — the difference is immediately visible to the eye.
