👰 Must the High Priest Have a Designated Backup Wife?

Rabbi Yehuda
The Sages must designate a second wife for the High Priest lest his first wife die before Yom Kippur — he needs beito (his house) to perform the Yom Kippur atonement.
Rabbis
There is no end to such concerns! If we worry about one wife dying, we must worry about the second dying too — requiring a third, a fourth, ad infinitum.
Rabbi Yehuda
One and two are qualitatively different. The chance of one wife dying is realistic; the chance of two wives dying simultaneously is far-fetched enough not to require a third.
Gemara
But 'his house' is singular — beito, not batav. How can the High Priest be married to two wives at once? The Gemara labors through multiple conditional divorce formulas to resolve this.
📖 Source Verse "And he shall atone for himself and for his house" — beito = his wife (Leviticus 16:11)

⚖️ Status of the Two High Priests — Rabbi Yosei's Ruling

Original High Priest

  • Separated from service only as a precaution before Yom Kippur
  • Returns to full service after the holiday concludes
  • If the second priest dies during service — the original steps in
  • Retains all High Priestly privileges and status

Second (Replacement) High Priest

  • Cannot serve as High Priest — the original is alive
  • Cannot serve as common priest — was elevated above that rank
  • Exception: if he performed service in violation, it is b'dieved valid
  • Rabbi Yosei: after the original's death, he may return — no fear of hatred

👰 The Backup Wife Debate — Mishna and Gemara

Rabbi Yehuda
The Sages designated a second wife so that if the first dies, the High Priest can immediately marry her and perform Yom Kippur with 'his house' intact.
Rabbis
If the first wife dies, he can remarry. If the second dies too, he can remarry again. There is no end to this — we do not pre-designate a backup wife.
Rabbi Yehuda
Two wives and one wife differ! Once there are two designated wives, the risk of both dying is remote enough that no third is needed.
Rabbis
By your logic, you should also not designate a replacement High Priest — yet you agree we do! If we're consistent about one precaution, we must be consistent about all.
🔄 Asymmetry All agree: a replacement High Priest is designated. The debate is only about a replacement wife — where the Rabbis draw the line.

📜 The Conditional Divorce (גֵּט עַל תְּנַאי) Problem — Five Attempts

Problem: 'His house' requires exactly one wife — but the High Priest needs a backup. How can he be married to two women without violating the singular requirement?
↓
Attempt 1: Marry second wife, then divorce her
❌ Rejected: if the first wife then dies, he has no wife at all
↓
Attempt 2: 'This is your get on condition you do not die on Yom Kippur'
❌ Rejected: Rava — a condition she cannot actively fulfill is not valid severance
↓
Attempt 3: 'Divorced if one of you dies'
❌ Rejected: what if neither dies? He remains married to two wives throughout
↓
Attempt 4: To wife #2: 'Divorced if wife #1 does NOT die'
❌ Rejected: if wife #1 does die, get #2 is retroactively void — he performed part of the service with two wives
↓
Final Solution: To wife #1: 'Divorced if wife #2 does not die.' To wife #2: 'Divorced if you do not enter the Holy of Holies.' → At the moment he enters, wife #2 is divorced; if wife #1 dies before, wife #2's get is already effective

😢 High Priest as Acute Mourner (אוֹנֵן) on Yom Kippur

Baraita (13b): A High Priest who becomes an onen (acute mourner — day a close relative dies) may sacrifice the Yom Kippur offerings — Yom Kippur service is not interrupted
↓
However, he may not eat from the offerings — an onen is forbidden from eating sacred food (kodashim)
↓
Rabbi Yehuda disagrees: The High Priest is removed from service entirely when he is an onen — he should not officiate at all, and the replacement takes over
↓
Abaye challenges Rabbi Yehuda: The replacement himself could become an onen — who then officates? The same problem recurs with every fallback priest
↓
Rava responds: We must re-examine the baraita's language — the phrase 'the entire day' requires fresh analysis to reconcile the conflicting positions
