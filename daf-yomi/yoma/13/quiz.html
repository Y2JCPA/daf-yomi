Yoma 13 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yosei, what is the permanent status of the second (replacement) High Priest after Yom Kippur, while the original High Priest is still alive?
A. He returns to serving as a common priest
B. He may serve as High Priest on weekdays only
C. He is fit to serve as neither High Priest nor common priest
D. He becomes High Priest if the original retires voluntarily
Correct: C — Rabbi Yosei rules the second priest is permanently disqualified from both roles: the original is alive so he cannot be High Priest, and he was elevated above common priesthood so he cannot return to it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why do the Rabbis reject the requirement to designate a backup wife for the High Priest?
A. Bigamy is prohibited by rabbinic decree
B. The concern leads to infinite regress — worry about one wife leads to worry about two, three, and more
C. A second wife cannot fulfill the 'his house' requirement under any circumstances
D. Only the High Priest himself may choose a second wife, not the Sages
Correct: B — The Rabbis argue: if we worry about the first wife dying, we must also worry about the second — requiring a third designation, then a fourth, with no logical stopping point.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rava's rule about conditional divorce that invalidates the formula 'This is your get on condition you do not die on Yom Kippur'?
A. A get cannot take effect before the date written on it
B. A condition involving death is automatically void
C. A condition the wife cannot actively fulfill — dependent purely on fate — does not constitute valid severance
D. Conditional divorce is only valid when witnessed by the Sanhedrin
Correct: C — Rava distinguishes: 'Do not drink wine for the life of so-and-so' is valid severance because it is an active obligation. But 'do not die' is not something the wife can do — the condition fails as not genuinely fulfillable by her.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does the singular form 'beito' (his house) in Leviticus 16:11 teach about the High Priest's marital status on Yom Kippur?
A. He must own a home in Jerusalem
B. He must be married — to exactly one wife; betrothal and two wives both fail
C. His entire household must immerse in a mikveh before the service
D. He must have children to inherit his role
Correct: B — 'Beito' means wife — the singular demands exactly one wife. A betrothed woman (not yet fully married) does not qualify, nor does a levirate widow, and being simultaneously married to two wives violates the singular form.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the baraita on Daf 13b, what is the rule when a High Priest becomes an acute mourner (onen) on Yom Kippur?
A. He is completely exempt from all service and leaves immediately
B. He may both sacrifice and eat, since Yom Kippur supersedes mourning
C. He sacrifices the Yom Kippur offerings but may not eat from the sacred food
D. He performs only the incense service and no other offerings
Correct: C — The baraita rules that an onen High Priest continues to sacrifice on Yom Kippur — the service cannot be abandoned — but he is forbidden from eating kodashim as an acute mourner.
