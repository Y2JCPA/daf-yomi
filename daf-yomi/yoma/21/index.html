✨ Counting the Temple's Ten Miracles

Mishna (Avot 5:5): Ten miracles were performed in the Temple
↓
Gemara: Why not count the Ark? Why not perpetual miracles like golden fruits?
↓
✅ Counted
Occasional miracles visible to all — outside the Sanctuary
❌ Not Counted
Perpetual miracles (golden fruits) • Hidden miracles inside the Sanctuary (Ark, cherubs)
↓
Result: Both occasional-visible and perpetual-hidden miracles coexisted — only the former appear in the Mishna's list of ten

📐 The Ark That Occupied No Space

מקום הארון אינו מן המידה — The space of the Ark is not included in the measurement
🔢 Ancient Tradition from Our Ancestors The Holy of Holies measured 20 cubits × 20 cubits. The Ark stood in the center. Yet when measured from the north wall to the Ark: 10 cubits. From the Ark to the south wall: 10 cubits. That accounts for all 20 — yet the Ark itself (2.5 cubits wide) took up no space at all.

Why isn't this counted in the Mishna's ten? The Tanna counts only miracles visible to all — the Holy of Holies was entered by the High Priest alone, once a year.

🔥 What Did the Altar Fire Look Like?

Mishna / Baraita
One of the ten Temple miracles: the altar fire was crouched and appeared like a lion — majestic and powerful
R. Ḥanina (Deputy High Priest)
I personally saw the fire in the Temple — it was crouched like a dog, not a lion at all!
Gemara
No contradiction: R. Ḥanina saw the Second Temple fire. In the Second Temple, fire was humanly kindled and appeared like a dog.
Rav Shmuel bar Inya
Proof that divine fire never descended in the Second Temple: The verse says 'Go up to the hills and get wood and build the house' — no mention of divine fire, only human effort!
⚖️ Resolution First Temple: divine fire descended from heaven → appeared like a lion. Second Temple: humanly lit fire → appeared like a dog.

🔥 Six Kinds of Fire (Yoma 21b Baraita)

Standard Fire

  • Consumes solids (dry things)
  • Does NOT consume liquids
  • Our ordinary fire

Fever / Illness

  • Consumes liquids (body fluids)
  • Does NOT consume solids
  • The fire of sickness in the body

Angel's Fire

  • Consumes both solids AND liquids
  • Seen in Manoaḥ's offering (Judges 13)
  • Supernatural origin

Fire of Gehenna

  • Consumes solids, liquids, AND bones
  • Nothing physical escapes it
  • Most severe natural extreme

Altar Fire (Divine)

  • Consumes solids, liquids, bones, AND iron
  • Descended from heaven in First Temple
  • Appeared as a crouching lion

Fire of Sinai

  • Consumes even wet and green wood
  • The great fire at Revelation
  • Beyond natural fire in every way

💨 The Pillar of Smoke — Omen of the Year's Harvest

Miracle: Even all the winds in the world could not move the altar smoke — it rose straight to heaven
↓
Rabbi Yitzḥak bar Avdimi: Yet the smoke's lean was observed as an agricultural omen!
↓
🌬️ West Wind → Smoke Leans East
Good sign!
Ample rain expected
Crops will be plentiful
Fruit can be stored
Everyone was glad
🌬️ East Wind → Smoke Leans West
Bad sign!
Dry conditions ahead
Harmful to wheat crop
Everyone was sad
(But good for olives!)
↓
Mnemonic (Rav Yosef / Mar Zutra): Table = bread (wheat) was in the north • Menorah = oil (olives) was in the south — each wind benefits one and harms the other
