⚖️ Regular Day vs. Yom Kippur Temple Service

Every Other Day

  • Coal pan: silver
  • Scoops 4 kav → transfers to 3 kav pan
  • Coal pan: heavy, short handle
  • Incense: peras (½ maneh) AM + peras PM
  • Priests ascend ramp on eastern side
  • Sanctifies hands/feet from standard laver
  • Wood arrangements: 3 (R. Yosei) / 4 (R. Meir)

Yom Kippur

  • Coal pan: gold
  • Scoops 3 kav directly — no transfer
  • Coal pan: light and long-handled (supports with arm)
  • Incense: daily amount + extra handful in Holy of Holies
  • High Priest ascends ramp in the center
  • Sanctifies hands/feet from golden flask (כבוד)
  • Wood arrangements: 4 (R. Yosei) / 5 (R. Meir)

🐄 Must the Red Heifer Be Slaughtered by a Priest?

Tanna (Baraita)
All slaughterings are valid if performed by a non-priest — except that of the red heifer, which requires a priest.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
'Go outside and teach that baraita outside!' — A common priest may slaughter the red heifer. The verse specifying Elazar applied only to the original heifer of Moses' time.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
So convinced was he that he rejected even his own teacher's opposing opinion. 'Before him' (Num. 19:3) teaches about the Elazar-adjacent sprinkling, not the slaughter itself.
The Core Question Does 'And Elazar the priest shall take its blood' (Numbers 19:4) imply only a priest may slaughter — or is slaughter open to any qualified person?

✋ Who May Perform Each Stage of Parah Adumah?

פרה אדומה — Red Heifer Rite
Numbers 19: four distinct stages, each with its own eligibility rules derived from Scripture
↓
① Slaughter
Priest required
Elazar in the original; common priest valid for later generations (R. Yoḥanan)
② Gather Ashes
Non-priest qualified
'A man who is pure' — 'man' includes non-priests; 'pure' excludes the impure
③ Sanctify (mix with water)
Rabbis: women may
R. Yehuda: men only
Dispute on Numbers 19:17 pronoun: plural 'they shall take' vs. singular 'he shall put'
④ Sprinkle Waters
Men only — all agree
Not women, tumtum, or androginus. 'A man who is pure shall take hyssop' (Num. 19:18)

🔥 The Yom Kippur Bull Service — Step by Step

High Priest slaughters the bull and receives its blood in a bowl (מזרק)
↓
Blood given to a stirring priest standing on the fourth row of tiles in the Sanctuary — stirred continuously to prevent coagulation
↓
High Priest scoops coals from the outer altar using the special gold coal pan of 3 kav — lighter and long-handled, supported by the arm
↓
High Priest fills his hands with incense — the daily peras plus an extra handful placed into the gold pan
↓
Ascends the ramp through the center (not the side like other priests) and proceeds into the Holy of Holies
↓
'No man shall be in the Tent of Meeting' (Lev. 16:17) — the stirring priest's presence in the Sanctuary is examined against this verse

🪵 How Many Wood Arrangements on Yom Kippur?

Rabbi Meir
On every other day: four arrangements of wood on the altar. On Yom Kippur: five.
Rabbi Yosei
On every other day: three arrangements. On Yom Kippur: four.
Rabbi Yehuda
On every other day: two arrangements. On Yom Kippur: three. And: no difference in the ramp, and no difference in the laver — the golden flask was not a Yom Kippur distinction.
Pattern of R. Yehuda Rabbi Yehuda consistently minimizes the distinctions of Yom Kippur — disagreeing on the wood arrangements, the ramp ascent, and the golden flask. His minority views appear throughout the mishna.
