🐐 The Scapegoat Journey to Azazel

High Priest confesses all of Israel's sins over the goat and hands it to the designated dispatcher
↓
Dispatcher leads goat from Jerusalem through 10 booths over 12 mil — at each booth, food and water offered (never needed, but offered for psychological support)
↓
Crimson strip divided: half tied to the cliff rock, half tied between the goat's horns
↓
Goat pushed backward off the cliff — torn limb from limb before reaching halfway down
↓
If the crimson strip turned white → sins atoned ("Though your sins be as scarlet, they shall be white as snow" — Isaiah 1:18)
↓
If strip did not turn white → result not publicized, to prevent public despair

🏕️ How Many Booths? How Far to the Cliff?

Rabbi Meir
10 booths, distance of 12 mil (90 ris at 7.5 ris per mil). People from all booths escort the dispatcher, except the last person in the last booth, who stops short of the cliff.
Rabbi Yehuda
9 booths, distance of 10 mil. Same principle — each booth provides escorts and the offer of food and water for the journey.
Rabbi Yosei
My son Elazar taught: with proper placement of an eiruv, even 2 booths could suffice for 10 mil — an individual may walk 2,000 amot beyond the Shabbat boundary with an eiruv.
Practical Note No escort ever actually needed the food or water offered at the booths — but it was offered anyway, so he would not feel rushed or distressed on his journey.

🔴 The Crimson Strip — Mechanism of Atonement

Dispatcher divides the crimson strip (lashon shel zehorit) into two equal halves
↓
Half #1
Tied to the rock at the top of the cliff
Half #2
Tied between the goat's horns
↓
Why not tie the whole strip to the goat? If the goat turns its head, the observer cannot see whether the strip turned white — result would be unknown
↓
Strip turns WHITE ✓
"Though your sins be as scarlet, they shall be white as snow" (Isaiah 1:18) — rejoice, atonement granted!
Strip stays RED ✗
Atonement not indicated — not publicized to avoid causing the people to despair

⚖️ Are the Scattered Limbs of the Goat Permitted for Benefit?

Rav
The limbs are permitted for benefit. The verse says 'he shall send the goat away in the wilderness' — once sent into the wilderness, it is hefker (ownerless) and may be used.
Shmuel
The limbs are forbidden for benefit. The word גְּזֵרָה (gezeira) means 'cut off' — the goat is entirely set apart; no benefit may be derived from it whatsoever.
Rava
It is reasonable to rule like the one who permits benefit. The scapegoat is not sanctified in its body — it merely serves as a vehicle for atonement. Once it has served that purpose, its remains are not forbidden.
Key Derivation Shmuel uses the term gezeira to teach that the cliff must be rough (atzum) and hard, located in a desolate wilderness — not in a settled area.

📜 Mishpatim vs. Chukim — Leviticus 18:4

משפטים — Ordinances

  • Laws that human reason demands — we would have written them ourselves
  • Examples: prohibitions on idolatry, sexual immorality, bloodshed, theft, blasphemy
  • Satan and the nations of the world do NOT challenge these laws
  • "You shall do My ordinances" (Lev. 18:4) — follow what is rational
  • These laws have clear, accessible reasoning

חוקים — Statutes

  • Laws with no obvious logical reason — Satan and nations mock them
  • Examples: prohibition on pork, sha'atnez (wool+linen), metzora purification, scapegoat ritual
  • "You shall keep My statutes" — I am the Lord your God, I have decreed it; you have no right to question
  • School of Rabbi Yishmael: Azazel atones for sins of Uzza and Azael (Genesis 6:2)
  • Obedience itself is the religious act — not comprehension
