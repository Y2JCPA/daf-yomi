🐟 What Did Israel Really Cry For in Egypt?

Rav
"We remember the fish which we ate in Egypt" (Numbers 11:5) — they cried over literal fish. Egypt had abundant fish freely available; now they had only manna. This reading praises Israel as not being promiscuous.
Shmuel
"Fish" is a euphemism for forbidden sexual relations. The Torah uses "eating" as a metaphor for intimacy. They wept over relatives now prohibited by the new laws of Sinai — weeping "for their families" (Numbers 11:10).
Textual Support for Each View Rav: "A garden enclosed is my sister the bride" (Song of Songs 4:12) — Israel was praised for fidelity in Egypt. Shmuel: "Moses heard the people weeping for their families" — they mourned newly forbidden kin-relations after Sinai.

🧬 Did the Manna Produce Bodily Waste?

Baraita (Sages)
The manna was completely absorbed by the 248 limbs — no waste at all. "Man ate the bread of the mighty [abirim]" (Psalms 78:25) — just as the ministering angels eat and produce no waste, so too Israel eating manna produced no bodily waste.
Rabbi Elazar ben Perata
The manna caused even other food eaten from gentile merchants to be fully digested. The manna purified the entire system — but the verse "you shall have a spade among your weapons" (Deuteronomy 23:14) applied to that other food, not manna.
Rabbi Yishmael's Reading Do not read abirim (mighty) — read eivarim (limbs). The manna was absorbed by all 248 limbs of the body. (Yoma 75b)

🤍 The Manna Whitened Israel's Sins

לָבָן כְּזֶרַע גַּד — White like coriander seed (Exodus 16:31)
Spiritual Power of the Manna (Yoma 75a) The manna was called לָבָן — lavan (white) not only for its color, but because it whitened Israel's sins. Fearing the manna would cease if they sinned, the people devoted themselves to introspection and repentance.

Like a prophet who reveals what is in the holes and cracks of the soul, the manna exposed inner spiritual states. When a husband and wife came to Moses in dispute — the manna revealed who had sinned, since it fell at each person's door according to their righteousness.

The word gad (coriander) also hints to haggada — a tale that draws the heart, like life-giving water.

🌅 Daily Descent of the Manna — A Triple Miracle

Layer 1 — Bottom Dew: At night, dew descends first upon the camp, forming a clean protective base on the ground
↓
Layer 2 — Manna descends upon the lower dew, resting on top of it — protected from the earth's impurity (Numbers 11:9: "the manna fell upon it")
↓
Layer 3 — Upper Dew: A second layer of dew falls on top of the manna, keeping it fresh — like food preserved in a box
↓
Morning Collection: "The people shall go out and gather a day's portion every day" (Exodus 16:4) — no stockpiling; constant daily reliance on God
↓
Bonus Miracle: Women's perfumes fell together with the manna — derived from "or beat it in a mortar" (Numbers 11:8), items ground in a mortar include spices (Rav, citing Rabbi Ḥama son of Rabbi Ḥanina)
