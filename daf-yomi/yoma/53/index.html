😤 Sadducees vs. Rabbis: When to Light the Incense

Sadducees
Light the incense outside in the Sanctuary, then carry the smoking coal pan into the Holy of Holies. From 'I will appear in a cloud' (Lev. 16:2) — the cloud must already exist upon entry!
Sages
Place the incense on the coals only after entering the Holy of Holies. 'Before the Lord' (Lev. 16:13) means in God's direct presence — not outside it!
⚠️ Stakes Death penalty is at stake: one who enters the Holy of Holies for no valid purpose — e.g., with deficient incense — incurs liability. The debate over timing is therefore a matter of life and death for the High Priest.

🪔 The Incense Offering: Step by Step

High Priest fills a coal pan with burning coals from the inner altar
↓
He takes a double handful (melo ḥofnav) of incense spices — including the required smoke-raiser (maaveh ashan) that causes smoke to rise straight like a staff
↓
He enters the Holy of Holies holding the coal pan in his right hand and the incense ladle in his left
↓
He places the incense upon the fire only while standing before the Lord — the cloud fills the space above the Ark Cover
↓
He recites a brief prayer (not prolonged, lest the people fear he died inside)
↓
He exits walking backward, face turned toward the Ark, until he passes through the curtain

🌿 What Does 'It Shall Cover' Teach? The Smoke-Raiser Dispute

Rav Yosef
The verse primarily teaches the obligation to include a leaf of the smoke-raiser. A second derivation adds the root as a supplement.
Abaye
You have it backwards! The root raises smoke better than the leaf — so the primary verse teaches the root, and the leaf is the addition.
Rav Sheshet
The primary verse teaches inclusion in the Tent of Meeting incense. A separate verse 'and so shall he do' extends this requirement to the Temple incense.
Rav Ashi
One verse establishes the mitzva itself; the other teaches that omission renders the incense invalid (indispensable requirement, not merely preferred).
📌 All Agree The smoke-raiser must be included to cause the smoke to rise in a straight column (כְּמַקֵּל — like a staff), fulfilling the verse 'it shall cover the Ark Cover.'

🚶 Walking Backward: A Protocol of Reverence

Source: High Priest exits the Holy of Holies walking backward — derived from King Solomon's posture when leaving Gibeon, and from the verse comparing his departure to his arrival
↓
Temple Personnel
Priests, Levites, and Israelites all depart facing inward — backs never turned toward the sacred space
Student & Teacher
Walk backward until out of sight — as Rabbi Elazar did with Rabbi Yoḥanan; Rava did with Rav Yosef (who was blind)
After Prayer (Amidah)
Take 3 steps backward, then say Shalom. If he did not do so — better not to have prayed at all!
↓
Rav Yosef blessed Rava for honoring him: 'May God's will be that you lift your head over the entire city!' — reward for walking backward as a blind man's student

🪨 Where Did the Ark Go? Three Opinions

Exiled to Babylonia

  • Opinion of Rabbi Shimon ben Yoḥai
  • Source: II Kings 20:17 — 'nothing [דָּבָר] shall be left'
  • דָּבָר = the Ark, which contains the דִּבְרוֹת (Commandments)
  • Supported by mishna's language: 'taken into exile' not 'buried'
  • Ulla cites Rabbi Matya ben Ḥarash's question to RSbY in Rome

Buried in Its Place

  • Opinion of Rabbi Yehuda ben Lakish
  • Source: I Kings 8:8 — 'they are there to this day'
  • The Ark was hidden in a chamber beneath the Temple Mount
  • King Josiah secreted it away before the Babylonian conquest
  • Consistent with Talmudic tradition of the Ark's concealment

Practical Result Either Way

  • The Even HaShetiya (Foundation Stone) remained in the Holy of Holies
  • Rose three finger-breadths above the floor
  • High Priest rested his coal pan on it on Yom Kippur
  • Traditional locus of Creation: the world was founded from this stone
  • David and Samuel designated it — from days of the early prophets

🙏 The High Priest's Brief Prayer

יְהִי רָצוֹן מִלְּפָנֶיךָ ה׳ אֱלֹהֵינוּ שֶׁתְּהֵא שָׁנָה זוֹ גְּשׁוּמָה וְחַמָּה
תְּפִלָּה קְצָרָה — The Short Prayer May it be Your will, Lord our God and God of our ancestors:

• That this year be one of rain, dew, and warmth
• That it not be a year of drought, famine, or heat
• That the rule of power not depart from the house of Judah
• That Your nation Israel not depend on one another for sustenance

Why brief? The people waited outside in fear. A prolonged absence caused them to worry the High Priest had died inside. He therefore did not extend his prayer — lest the people be gripped with terror.

Additional: Rabbi Ḥanina ben Dosa prayed to stop rain when he was suffering — and to restart it when others were inconvenienced — demonstrating that sincere prayer for communal need is always heard.

📋 Yoma 53 — Summary Map

Central Dispute: Sadducees vs. Rabbis — light incense before or after entering?
↓
Sadducees: Before entry — 'I will appear in a cloud' (Lev. 16:2)
Rabbis (Halakha): After entry — 'before the Lord' (Lev. 16:13)
↓
Required ingredient: Smoke-raiser (maaveh ashan) — smoke must rise straight as a staff. Four Amoraim debate which verse establishes the obligation and whether leaf or root is primary.
↓
Exiting the Holy of Holies: Walk backward — a model extended to Temple personnel, students, and all who conclude the Amidah (3 steps back + Shalom)
↓
After the Ark's exile: The Even HaShetiya (Foundation Stone) marks the sacred center of creation. Debate: was the Ark exiled to Babylonia or buried beneath the Temple?
