⚔️ Does the Daily Offering's End Override Shabbat?

Rav Huna
The beginning of the daily offering overrides Shabbat, but its end does not. Remaining unconsumed limbs need not be burned on Shabbat.
Bar Kappara / Rava
Even on Shabbat, a separate wood arrangement is made for remaining limbs — Rabbi Meir says 'every other day', implying all days including Shabbat.
Mishna Evidence Rabbi Meir: 'On every other day there are four arrangements' — 'every other day' (בכל יום) includes Shabbat.

🪵 Rules for Separate Wood Arrangement (Remaining Limbs)

Situation: Limbs of a burnt-offering remain on the altar from the previous night, not fully consumed
↓
Rabbi Meir's Rule: Make a separate wood arrangement specifically to finish burning these limbs
↓
Key Condition: Fire must have already taken hold of the limbs — if not yet burning, no separate arrangement is made
↓
Valid AND disqualified offerings: Both qualify for a separate arrangement once fire has taken hold
↓
Bar Kappara's Ruling: This applies even on Shabbat — the mitzva of burning completes the service
↓
Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov: Bar Kappara's teaching was needed — one might have thought only when Yom Kippur follows Shabbat (Sunday), not mid-week

⚖️ Shabbat vs. Ritual Impurity: Two Different Standards

Shabbat

  • Fully pushed aside (דְּחוּיָה) for communal offerings
  • Both beginning and end of daily offering override it
  • Source: 'In its season' — even on Shabbat
  • Rav Ḥisda: since fully set aside, the end overrides Shabbat too

Ritual Impurity (טֻמְאָה)

  • Only tolerated/permitted (הוּתְּרָה) for communal offerings
  • Only the beginning of the service overrides impurity
  • Rav Ḥisda: end does NOT override impurity
  • Preference: avoid impurity wherever possible

💧 Why Override? Rabba vs. Rav Ḥisda on Impurity and Shabbat

Rabba
The end of the daily offering's service is like its beginning. Since the beginning overrides both impurity and Shabbat, so does the end — burning remaining limbs overrides both prohibitions.
Rav Ḥisda
Shabbat is fully set aside for communal offerings → even the end overrides it. But impurity is only tolerated → only the beginning overrides impurity, not the end of service.
Abaye's Challenge If 'in its season' makes the daily offering override Shabbat — why doesn't the same logic override the impurity prohibition equally? Both should apply or neither.
