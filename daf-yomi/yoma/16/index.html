📐 Whose Opinion Does Tractate Middot Follow?

R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov
Between the Israelite courtyard and priests' courtyard there was an additional stair — 1 cubit high — plus a Levite platform of 1.5 cubits. These extra 2.5 cubits fully conceal the Sanctuary entrance from the Women's Courtyard.
The Rabbis
We do not count that extra stair and platform. Without those 2.5 cubits, the threshold of the Sanctuary entrance is exposed half a cubit above the Women's Courtyard floor — visible, not hidden.
Gemara
Since mishnayot in Middot describe the entrance as concealed, Middot must follow R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov — only his extra 2.5 cubits make concealment geometrically possible!
Rav Adda bar Ahava
This is not a definitive proof. The halakhot of this tractate can still be interpreted according to a different opinion — the geometry can be resolved another way.
Central Question Do the architectural mishnayot of Middot reflect R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov or the Rabbis? The answer determines whether the Sanctuary entrance was geometrically concealed from the Women's Courtyard — and by extension, which Tanna authored Middot's rulings.

🏛️ The Four Chambers of the Women's Courtyard

Southeast Chamber

  • Wood Chamber — Lishkat HaEtzim
  • Priests with physical blemishes sorted wood
  • Disqualified wood set aside from altar use
  • Also: Shewbread preparation (per some accounts)

Northeast Chamber

  • Chamber of the Hasmoneans
  • Altar stones desecrated by Greek kings
  • Stored until a prophet could rule on them
  • Neither used nor discarded — held in limbo

Northwest Chamber

  • Chamber of the Lepers — Lishkat HaMetzora'im
  • Lepers immersed here for purification ritual
  • Required before re-entry into the community
  • Separate from the general mikveh system

Southwest Chamber

  • R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov: I forgot its purpose
  • Abba Shaul: Wine and oil stored here
  • Used for libation offerings of Temple service
  • Demonstrates loss of Temple knowledge over time

⚖️ Is the Altar Centered? Disproving Rabbi Yehuda's Authorship of Middot

Tractate Middot
The altar stood precisely opposite the Sanctuary entrance: 10 cubits directly facing the entrance, 11 cubits to the north, 11 cubits to the south. This places the altar at the center of the courtyard's width.
Rabbi Yehuda
My calculation of altar dimensions differs. Under my view, the combined ramp-and-altar placement would put most of the altar's mass in the southern portion of the courtyard — not centered.
Gemara
If you say Middot follows Rabbi Yehuda — do you find the altar in the middle of the courtyard? No! Most of it stands in the southern part. Therefore Middot cannot follow Rabbi Yehuda.
Narrator
The full N-S width calculation: ramp + altar = 62 cubits (32 + 32 minus 2 overlap). The remaining space is distributed north and south. The alignment of 10/11/11 only works under specific dimensional assumptions.
Key Numbers Total courtyard width = 135 cubits (S→N). Ramp = 32 cubits; Altar = 32 cubits; 2 cubits overlap at top of ramp → combined = 62 cubits. The '10 cubits opposite entrance / 11 north / 11 south' formula proves the altar is centered only under the standard (non-Yehuda) dimensions.

Combined ramp + altar footprint: 62 cubits total (32 + 32 − 2 overlap)
