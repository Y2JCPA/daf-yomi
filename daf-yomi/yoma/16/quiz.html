QUESTION 1 OF 5
What was stored in the northeast chamber of the Women's Courtyard, according to the mishna in Middot?
A. Wood sorted by priests with physical blemishes
B. Altar stones desecrated by gentile kings, stored by the Hasmoneans
C. Wine and oil for the Temple libation offerings
D. Vessels used by the lepers for purification immersion
Correct: B — The northeast chamber was the Chamber of the Hasmoneans, where altar stones defiled by Greek kings were stored until a prophet could determine whether they should be used or discarded.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What additional architectural feature does R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov describe between the Israelite courtyard and the priests' courtyard?
A. A gate 20 cubits high and 10 cubits wide, like the other Temple entrances
B. Fifteen curved stairs mirroring those ascending from the Women's Courtyard
C. An extra stair of 1 cubit plus a Levite platform of 1.5 cubits, totaling 2.5 cubits
D. A wall of 5 cubits with a narrow doorway for priests only
Correct: C — R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov adds one stair (1 cubit high) between the Israelite and priests' courtyards, and the platform on which the Levites stood (1.5 cubits) — adding a total of 2.5 cubits of elevation not counted by the Rabbis.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara argue that Tractate Middot must follow R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov rather than the Rabbis?
A. Middot explicitly names R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov as its author in an opening passage
B. Without R' Eliezer's extra 2.5 cubits, the Sanctuary entrance would be visible from the Women's Courtyard, contradicting Middot's description
C. The Rabbis dispute the 135-cubit measurement of the Women's Courtyard used throughout Middot
D. R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov is the only Tanna who counts all 15 stairs between the courtyards
Correct: B — The geometric proof: Middot implies the Sanctuary entrance is concealed from below. Only with R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov's additional 2.5 cubits does the math produce concealment. The Rabbis' calculation leaves the threshold exposed by half a cubit, contradicting Middot.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara prove that Tractate Middot does NOT follow Rabbi Yehuda's opinion?
A. Rabbi Yehuda holds the altar was copper, but Middot describes a stone altar
B. Under Rabbi Yehuda's altar dimensions, the altar would not be centered in the courtyard — contradicting Middot's 10/11/11-cubit alignment
C. Rabbi Yehuda disputes the total 135-cubit width of the Temple courtyard
D. Rabbi Yehuda requires the ramp to face east, but Middot says it faced south
Correct: B — In Middot, the altar aligns precisely opposite the Sanctuary: 10 cubits facing the entrance, 11 cubits to the north, 11 to the south — centered. Under Rabbi Yehuda's larger altar dimensions, most of the altar would fall in the southern half of the courtyard, disproving his authorship.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What was Rav Adda bar Ahava's response to the proof that Middot follows R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov?
A. He agreed that R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov authored all of Tractate Middot
B. He demonstrated an alternative calculation that proved the Rabbis were correct
C. He conceded the point but noted a different mathematical error in R' Eliezer's reasoning
D. He said this is not definitive — the halakhot of the tractate can still be interpreted according to a different opinion
Correct: D — Rav Adda bar Ahava cautioned that the architectural argument, while compelling, is not a definitive proof. The halakhot in Middot could still be reconciled with a view other than R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov's — the geometric issue may have another resolution.
