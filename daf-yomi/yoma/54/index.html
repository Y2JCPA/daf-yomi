🏛️ The Ark, the Cherubs, and the Foundation Stone

Where Was the Ark Buried?

  • Ulla: Buried in its place beneath the Temple — 'they are there to this day' means forever
  • Rav Naḥman (Tosefta/Rabbis): Buried in the Chamber of the Woodshed (Beit HaEtzem)
  • A priest banged the floor, found a hollow space, realized it was the Ark's hiding spot — and died

The Cherubs in the Temple

  • Priests rolled up the curtain on Festivals to show pilgrims the cherubs clinging to one another
  • Rav Ḥisda objects: the Torah forbids seeing sacred objects uncovered — 'lest they die!'
  • Rav Naḥman: Like a bride once married — Israel at the Temple is God's beloved, fully revealed

Even Shetiyya — Navel of the World

  • Named שְׁתִיָּה because the world was founded (הֻשְׁתַּת) from it
  • Rabbi Eliezer: Heavens created from Zion; earth created separately
  • The Rabbis: Both heaven and earth created from Zion — 'Out of Zion, the perfection of beauty' (Ps 50:2)

🏛️ Debating the Ark's Fate: Buried or Exiled?

Ulla
The Ark was buried in its place beneath the Temple. Proof: 'And they are there to this day' (I Kings 8:8) — wherever 'there' appears with 'to this day,' it means forever.
Objection
But Nebuchadnezzar's armies traversed the land! They would have found it. Rabbi Yehuda: No person passed through Judea for 52 years after the destruction — but eventually they did.
Ulla (Reply)
The formula 'there… to this day' signals permanence. Compare the Jebusites verse — it lacks 'there,' so it is not permanent. The Ark's verse has it — proof it remains forever.
Counter
Sennacherib already scrambled all nations — 'I have removed the bounds of peoples.' Who can prove any original nation still dwells in its land? The Jebusite argument is undermined.
Talmudic Principle כָּל מָקוֹם שֶׁנֶּאֱמַר שָׁם עַד הַיּוֹם הַזֶּה — לְעוֹלָם הוּא
Wherever 'there' appears with 'to this day,' it means forever

📦 How the Ark's Hiding Place Was Discovered

Rav Naḥman (Tosefta): The Rabbis say the Ark was buried in the Chamber of the Woodshed (Beit HaEtzem) beneath the Temple Mount
↓
Supporting Mishna: A certain priest working in the Beit HaEtzem noticed one tile that was different from the others — slightly out of place
↓
What was he doing? Rabbi Ḥelbo: He was striking the floor with his axe — and discovered an empty hollow space beneath the misplaced tile
↓
He understood this hollow space was the entrance to the Ark's concealed chamber — but before he could tell anyone, he died
↓
Conclusion: His death confirmed the site's sacred secrecy — the Ark's exact location remains hidden, as intended by those who buried it

📜 Paradox Resolved: Seen and Yet Unseen I Kings 8:8 records two contradictory statements about the staves of the Ark:

'The ends of the staves were seen' — yet — 'they could not be seen without'

Resolution (Rabbi Yehuda): The staves pushed outward against the parokhet (curtain) from within the Holy of Holies, creating two visible protrusions — like two breasts of a woman pressing against her garment.

As Song of Songs (1:13) says: 'My beloved is like a bundle of myrrh that lies between my breasts' — the Ark's presence felt intimately even when veiled.

👼 The Cherubs: Intimacy Revealed and Desecrated

Rav Ketina: On each pilgrimage Festival, priests rolled up the curtain and showed the people the cherubs — clinging to one another — saying: 'God's love for you is like a man's love for his wife'
↓
⚠️ Objection — Rav Ḥisda
'They shall not go in to see the sacred objects as they are being covered, lest they die' (Numbers 4:20). How could the priests permit this viewing?
✅ Answer — Rav Naḥman
Like a bride: modest in her father's house, but free before her husband once married. Israel coming to the Temple is the beloved coming to the Divine home — full revelation is permitted.
↓
Reish Lakish (tragedy): When gentiles destroyed the Second Temple and entered, they found the cherubs still clinging to each other. They carried them into the streets and mocked Israel before the nations.

🌍 From Where Was the World Created?

Tosefta / Rabbis
The world was created from the Foundation Stone (Even Shetiyya). Why called שְׁתִיָּה? Because the world was hushtat (founded) from it. The stone in the Holy of Holies is the navel of creation.
Rabbi Yehoshua
The world was created from the sides — snow was commanded to become earth; rains fell outward. Creation expanded from the periphery inward. (Job 37:6)
Rabbi Eliezer the Great
The heavens were created from Zion, and the earth was created separately. 'These are the generations of heaven and earth' — each has its own origin point.
The Rabbis
Both heaven and earth were created from Zion — 'Out of Zion, the perfection of beauty, God has shone forth' (Ps 50:2). Zion is the singular source of all creation's beauty and being.
Key Verse מִצִּיּוֹן מִכְלַל יֹפִי אֱלֹהִים הוֹפִיעַ (תהלים נ:ב)
