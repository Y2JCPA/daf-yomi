QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman citing the Tosefta, where was the Ark of the Covenant buried?
A. Beneath the Foundation Stone in the Holy of Holies
B. In the Chamber of the Woodshed (Beit HaEtzem)
C. Under the altar in the Temple courtyard
D. It was taken to Babylon by Nebuchadnezzar
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman cites the Tosefta teaching that the Rabbis say the Ark was buried in the Chamber of the Woodshed (Beit HaEtzem), confirmed by the story of a priest who discovered the hollow space there before he died.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Talmud resolve the contradiction in I Kings 8:8 — that the staves 'were seen' yet 'could not be seen without'?
A. The verse describes two different arks that existed at different times
B. The staves were sometimes extended and sometimes retracted by the priests
C. The staves pushed against the curtain — visible as protrusions but not directly seen through it
D. The contradiction is left unresolved as a mystery (teiku)
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that the staves pushed outward against the parokhet (curtain), creating two protrusions visible from outside — like two breasts pressing against a garment — but the staves themselves were not directly seen through the curtain.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rav Naḥman uses what analogy to justify showing the cherubs to Festival pilgrims, despite the prohibition of seeing sacred objects uncovered?
A. A king who opens his treasury on national holidays
B. A bride who is modest while engaged but free before her husband once married
C. A teacher who reveals hidden wisdom to his most advanced students
D. A shepherd who brings the flock inside the house during festivals
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman analogizes Israel to a bride: modest in her father's house while engaged, but free before her husband once married. When Israel comes to the Temple on pilgrimage, they are God's beloved entering the Divine home — full revelation is permitted and celebrated.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why was the Foundation Stone called 'Even Shetiyya' (אֶבֶן שְׁתִיָּה)?
A. Because it was set (שִׁית) in place by King Solomon himself
B. Because water libations (שְׁתִיָּה = drinking) were poured upon it
C. Because the world was founded/created (הֻשְׁתַּת) from it
D. Because it was the stone upon which the High Priest sat (יָשַׁת) on Yom Kippur
Correct: C — The Tosefta explains: it is called שְׁתִיָּה because the world was הֻשְׁתַּת (founded/created) from it. This stone in the Holy of Holies is the cosmic center point from which all creation originated.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What did Reish Lakish say happened when the gentiles entered and destroyed the Second Temple?
A. They found the Holy of Holies empty — the cherubs and Ark had already been hidden
B. The cherubs were facing away from each other, symbolizing God's anger at Israel
C. They found the cherubs clinging to each other, carried them into the streets, and mocked Israel
D. They were struck blind when they entered, fulfilling the verse 'lest they die'
Correct: C — Reish Lakish teaches that when the destroyers entered the Second Temple, they found the cherubs clinging to one another in an embrace. They carried these images out into the public streets and mocked Israel before the nations with this sight.
