Yoma 49 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Which of the following performers disqualifies a blood rite according to the baraita on Yoma 49a?
A. An elderly priest who performs the rite very slowly
B. A mourner on his first day of mourning (onen)
C. A priest who is overly tired from prior service
D. A priest with a minor speech impediment
Correct: B — The baraita explicitly lists a first-day mourner (onen) alongside non-priests, drunk priests, and blemished priests as performers who disqualify the blood rite.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Rav Sheshet initially rule in a way that contradicted the baraita about carrying blood?
A. He held the baraita was a minority opinion and could be disregarded
B. He disagreed on principle with the baraita's conclusion
C. He was simply unaware of the baraita at the time
D. He believed carrying blood was not a rite at all, only receiving and sprinkling
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that Rav Sheshet was initially unaware of the baraita. Once he learned of it, he turned around and used it as his own objection against Rav Ḥisda.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rabbi Ḥanina derive from the phrase 'with a bull' (בְּפַר) in Leviticus 16:3?
A. The High Priest may enter with any large animal, not only a bull
B. The second High Priest must slaughter a new bull himself and cannot use his predecessor's blood
C. The High Priest may use the blood of the bull even if another priest slaughtered it
D. The bull must be offered before the goat in every Yom Kippur sequence
Correct: B — Rabbi Ḥanina interprets 'with a bull' to require personal involvement — the High Priest must enter with a bull he himself slaughtered, and cannot rely on blood from a predecessor's animal.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why is the incense-taking rite in the Holy of Holies called the most difficult Temple service?
A. It requires reciting a lengthy prayer while holding the coal pan above his head
B. The High Priest must transfer incense from a ladle directly into his cupped palms without any secondary vessel, in a constrained posture
C. The incense must be weighed and measured to exactly one handful using a scale inside the Holy of Holies
D. It must be performed in total darkness with no lamplight permitted
Correct: B — The rite requires the High Priest to hold the ladle awkwardly against his body and scoop incense directly into his cupped hands — an extraordinarily difficult maneuver requiring great precision and physical control.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What was the actual question Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi posed to Rabbi Ḥanina about cress on Shabbat?
A. Whether a non-Jew may purchase cress for a Jew on Shabbat for medicinal purposes
B. Whether cress juice counts as a cooked food and is therefore rabbinically forbidden
C. Whether one may perform the melachah of grinding in order to prepare cress as medicine on Shabbat
D. Whether cress is considered a food or a medicine for the purpose of Shabbat blessings
Correct: C — The Gemara clarifies that the question was specifically about grinding cress (a Torah-prohibited labor) on Shabbat for medicinal use — when the situation does not clearly involve danger to life, the permissibility is not obvious.
