🧱 The One-Cubit Partition: Location vs. Sanctity

A one-cubit partition stood between the Sanctuary and the Holy of Holies
↓
Rabbi Natan
Its status was never determined — neither inside nor outside
Ravina objects
Why uncertain? Scripture gives precise measurements — 20 + 40 cubits!
↓
Resolution: Biblical measurements count interior space only — the partition wall is not included in either 20 or 40 cubits. Its location is clear; its legal sanctity is what remained unresolved.

🧱 The Partition's Status: A Legal Dispute

Rabbi Natan
The one-cubit partition between Sanctuary and Holy of Holies — the Sages never determined its status. Was it legally part of the Holy of Holies (inside) or part of the Sanctuary (outside)?
Ravina
Why should there be any uncertainty? Scripture says the Temple was 60 cubits long: 20 for the Holy of Holies and 40 for the Sanctuary. The partition must fall on one side!
Ravina (resolves)
The walls themselves are not counted in these measurements — only the interior space is counted. So the partition fits neither the 20 nor the 40. Its location is clear; the uncertainty concerns its sanctity.
Key Distinction Not where the partition stood — but whether it shared the holiness of the Holy of Holies or the Sanctuary. This had practical halakhic consequences for the High Priest's service.

🕯️ The High Priest's Entry into the Holy of Holies

Outer curtain hooked open on the south side — High Priest enters from the south
↓
Walks northward through the space between the two curtains
↓
Inner curtain hooked open on the north side — enters Holy of Holies from the north (indirect approach = mark of respect)
↓
Places the coal pan between the two staves of the Ark (in Second Temple: the place of the Ark)
↓
Piles the incense atop the coals — the entire chamber fills with smoke
↓
Exits the same way he entered — face toward the Holy of Holies, backing out, never turning his back on the Ark

🏺 Josiah Buries the Ark

דברי הימים ב לה:ג — "תְּנוּ אֶת אֲרוֹן הַקֹּדֶשׁ בַּבַּיִת"
Historical Tradition Josiah, king of Judea, commanded the Levites to bury the Ark before the Babylonian exile.

He saw the verse: "The Lord will bring you and your king… to a nation that you have not known" (Deut 28:36) and feared the Ark would be taken captive to Babylon.

Proof from II Chronicles 35:3 — "Put the sacred Ark in the house that Solomon built" — implies it had been removed for hiding and was to be returned.

Buried together with the Ark: the jar of manna, the flask of anointing oil, Aaron's staff with its blossoms, and the chest sent by the Philistines.

🏛️ First Temple vs. Second Temple: The Curtain Problem

First Temple

  • One curtain over the one-cubit partition
  • The actual Ark of the Covenant present
  • Coal pan placed between the Ark's two staves
  • Ark buried by Josiah before the Babylonian destruction

Second Temple

  • Two curtains: outer (south-facing) and inner (north-facing)
  • No Ark — only the place of the Ark (Even HaShetiyah)
  • 'Reaches the Ark' = reaches where Ark once stood
  • Mishna describes Second Temple practice, not First
