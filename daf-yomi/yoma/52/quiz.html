Yoma 52 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Natan, what was uncertain about the one-cubit partition?
A. Its physical location between the two chambers
B. Its legal sanctity — whether it shared the holiness of the Holy of Holies or the Sanctuary
C. Whether it was made of wood or stone
D. Whether Solomon or Moses constructed it
Correct: B — Rabbi Natan said the Sages never determined the partition's legal status — its physical location was not in doubt, but whether its sanctity matched the Holy of Holies or the Sanctuary remained unresolved.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How did Ravina explain why the partition is not included in the Temple's biblical measurements?
A. The partition was added after the Temple was built and measured
B. Biblical measurements count interior space only — walls and partitions are excluded
C. The partition was too thin to include in the cubit count
D. Solomon deliberately omitted it from his architectural plans
Correct: B — Ravina proved from the mishna's Temple dimensions that Scripture counts only usable interior space. Walls are not counted, so the one-cubit partition fits neither the 20-cubit Holy of Holies nor the 40-cubit Sanctuary count.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How many indeterminate verses did Isi ben Yehuda identify in the Torah?
A. Three
B. Four
C. Five
D. Seven
Correct: C — Isi ben Yehuda enumerated five verses whose meaning cannot be definitively resolved: Se'et (Gen 4:7), Meshukkadim (Ex 25:34), Mahar (Ex 17:9), Arur (Gen 49:7), and Vekam (Deut 31:16).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did King Josiah bury the Ark before the Babylonian exile?
A. A prophet explicitly commanded him to hide it
B. He saw a verse foretelling exile and feared the Ark would be taken captive
C. The Levites refused to carry it any longer
D. He wanted to renovate the Temple and needed space
Correct: B — Josiah saw Deut 28:36 — 'The Lord will bring you and your king to a nation you have not known' — and feared the Ark would be exiled to Babylon, so he commanded the Levites to bury it for safekeeping.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the Second Temple, what did the mishna mean by 'when he reaches the Ark'?
A. He physically retrieves the Ark from storage
B. He reaches the place where the Ark once stood
C. He touches the golden Ark cover
D. He walks to where the two staves protrude
Correct: B — The Gemara explains: in the Second Temple there was no Ark — it had been buried by Josiah. The phrase 'reaches the Ark' means reaching the location where the Ark formerly stood, known as the Foundation Stone (Even HaShetiyah).
