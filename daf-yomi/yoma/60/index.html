⚔️ Whose Order Counts? Rabbi Yehuda vs. Rabbi Neḥemya

Rabbi Yehuda
The indispensable order applies only to services performed in white garments inside the Holy of Holies — the innermost space entered once a year
Rabbi Neḥemya
The indispensable order applies to all white-garment services — both inside and outside the Sanctuary — but gold-garment services are excluded
Key Verse (Lev. 16:34) "Once a year" — each Tanna derives a different scope for indispensable ordering from this and related limiting phrases

🔢 Mishna: The Ordered Service of Yom Kippur

Governing Rule: Each action of the Yom Kippur service must follow the Torah's stated sequence
↓
If the High Priest performed one action before another — he has done nothing and must repeat it in order
↓
If the blood of the bull spills before completing sprinklings in the Sanctuary → slaughter a new bull and restart from the beginning
↓
If the blood of the goat spills before its sprinklings are complete → slaughter a new goat and restart its service
↓
If blood spills before the golden altar sprinkling → must also restart the golden altar stage
↓
Each stage is discrete: spilling partway through requires restarting only that stage — not the entire day's service

⚔️ Three-Way Tannaitic Dispute on Order

Tanna Kamma
All Yom Kippur services must be in order — no exceptions for white vs. gold garments, inside vs. outside
Rabbi Yehuda
Only services in white garments performed inside the Holy of Holies require strict order; gold-garment services outside are not bound by this rule
Rabbi Neḥemya
All white-garment services (inside or outside) require order; gold-garment services and the pouring of blood remainders at the base are excluded
Practical Difference Does disorder in services performed in gold garments (e.g., outside the Sanctuary) invalidate them? Yehuda: No. Neḥemya: Also no — but for a different reason.

🩺 Me'ilah, Blood, and the Priestly Vestments

Why are the four white vestments subject to me'ilah (misuse of consecrated objects)?
↓
Rabbis
Vestments require interment after Yom Kippur → remain consecrated → me'ilah applies directly
Rabbi Dosa
Vestments are fit for ordinary priests → no interment needed → yet me'ilah still applies via halakhic derivation
↓
Source: Removal of ashes + heifer whose neck is broken = two verses teaching the same prohibition → benefit forbidden after the mitzva is fulfilled
↓
Three verses about blood exclude it from me'ilah; one excludes from notar; piggul exclusion is derived separately from a Mishna

🫙 Rabbi Ḥanina: Premature Incense Scooping

כמאן — לא כרבי יהודה ולא כרבי נחמיה
Rabbi Ḥanina's Ruling If the High Priest scooped the incense before slaughtering the bull, it is as though he did nothing — the scooping is void and must be repeated after the bull is slaughtered in its proper order.

The Gemara notes this ruling fits neither Rabbi Yehuda (scooping occurs outside the Holy of Holies) nor Rabbi Neḥemya (he excludes pouring of remainders, not scooping). The Gemara ultimately reconciles it with Rabbi Yehuda: scooping is considered an act performed for the interior and thus governed by the inner-service order.
