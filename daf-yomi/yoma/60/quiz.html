Yoma 60 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda, for which Yom Kippur services is the order indispensable?
A. All Yom Kippur services regardless of garments or location
B. Services in white garments performed inside the Holy of Holies only
C. Services in gold garments performed outside the Sanctuary
D. All white-garment services whether inside or outside the Sanctuary
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda limits the indispensable ordering to white-garment services performed specifically inside the Holy of Holies — the sacred space entered once a year.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
If the blood of the bull spills before the High Priest finishes the sprinklings in the Sanctuary, what must he do?
A. Continue with the goat's blood immediately to compensate
B. The entire day's service is invalidated and cannot continue
C. Complete whatever sprinklings remain using the available blood
D. Begin the bull's blood service from the beginning with new blood
Correct: D — The Mishna rules that spilling the blood before completing the presentations requires starting the bull's blood service from the beginning — a new bull must be slaughtered.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara explain why priestly vestments are subject to me'ilah even according to Rabbi Dosa, who says they need not be interred?
A. Because linen garments always carry intrinsic sanctity requiring interment
B. Because Rabbi Dosa actually agrees they must be interred after use
C. Derived from two verses as one — removal of ashes and the heifer whose neck is broken — prohibiting benefit after the mitzva
D. Because all items used in Temple service automatically carry me'ilah
Correct: C — The Gemara derives that benefit is forbidden after the mitzva from two converging cases (ashes and the broken-necked heifer). This 'two verses as one' source applies to the vestments even according to Rabbi Dosa.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
With whose opinion does the Gemara initially say Rabbi Ḥanina's ruling on premature incense scooping is inconsistent?
A. Only Rabbi Neḥemya — it contradicts his exclusion of blood pouring
B. Only Rabbi Yehuda — it contradicts his inside-only requirement
C. Neither Rabbi Yehuda nor Rabbi Neḥemya as initially stated
D. The Tanna Kamma alone, since he requires order for all services
Correct: C — The Gemara says 'with whose opinion is this? Not Rabbi Yehuda and not Rabbi Neḥemya' — scooping occurs outside the Holy of Holies (problematic for Yehuda) yet Neḥemya excludes blood pouring, not scooping. The Gemara then resolves it.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Neḥemya, what does one of the two limiting expressions ('once a year') exclude from the requirement of indispensable order?
A. Services performed in white garments inside the Sanctuary
B. The burning of incense on the golden altar
C. The lottery over the two goats performed in the courtyard
D. Pouring the remainders of blood at the base of the outer altar
Correct: D — Rabbi Neḥemya uses one restriction to exclude gold-garment services and the other to exclude the pouring of the remainders of blood at the base of the altar from the requirement of indispensable ordering.
