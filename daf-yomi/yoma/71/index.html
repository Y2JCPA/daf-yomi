📜 Ritual Order Meets Vestment Law

יומא עא
Daf 71 Overview The daf opens by resolving which verses in Leviticus 16 are written out of order — Abaye, Rava, and Rabba bar Rav Sheila debate proofs that certain rites (burning remains, re-entering the Sanctuary) occur later than their scriptural position implies. A tradition of five immersions and ten sanctifications anchors the debate. After the story of Shemaya and Avtalyon's rebuke of the High Priest, a new Mishna opens: the High Priest wears eight garments, the common priest four — launching a precise analysis of how thread-strand counts (6, 8, 12, 28) are derived from the word shesh and comparative exegesis.

📖 Which Verse Is Out of Order? — Three Proofs

Rav Ḥisda
Tradition (קבלה): The High Priest performs five immersions and ten sanctifications. If the verses were in order, we'd have only three changes of garments — three immersions and six sanctifications. Something must be out of order.
Abaye
Proof: The verse says 'he shall exit and make his burnt-offering.' The word 'exit' implies this is the first exit from the Sanctuary — so it cannot follow verses already describing earlier exits. It is out of order.
Rava
Different proof: 'Aaron shall come and remove the linen garments which he wore when he went into the Sanctuary' — that extra phrase is superfluous unless the verse is out of order, teaching the linen garments require burial (גניזה).
Rabba bar Rav Sheila
Objection to Rava: Perhaps order is maintained! The extra immersion comes from interrupting white-garment service with the goat sent to Azazel — no need to declare the verse out of order at all.
Rava (Resolution)
The goat to Azazel was definitely sent earlier, before these rites. The verse states: 'the lot fell upon the goat for Azazel — he shall be presented alive before the Lord' — implying it had already been dispatched.
Principle Scripture in Leviticus 16 is generally in order — but tradition of five immersions forces the Sages to identify exactly which verses deviate from that order.

🔢 Five Immersions & Ten Sanctifications — Reconstructed Order

Tradition (קבלה): High Priest immerses five times and sanctifies hands and feet ten times on Yom Kippur
↓
Each garment change = 1 immersion + 2 sanctifications (hands and feet before and after)
↓
5 changes: Golden → White → Golden → White → Golden
Morning golden vestments → white for Yom Kippur service → golden for afternoon → white for Holy of Holies exit → golden to conclude
↓
Without out-of-order verses: only 3 changes visible in Scripture → only 3 immersions, 6 sanctifications — a deficit of 2 immersions and 4 sanctifications
↓
With rearranged verses: Two additional garment changes recovered → 5 × 1 immersion = 5 immersions; 5 × 2 sanctifications = 10 sanctifications ✓

👘 High Priest vs. Common Priest: Vestments

Common Priest — 4 Garments

  • כתונת — Tunic (linen, shesh × 6)
  • מכנסיים — Trousers (linen)
  • מצנפת — Mitre/Turban (linen, shesh × 6)
  • אבנט — Belt (linen)

High Priest — 8 Garments

  • כתונת — Tunic (linen, shesh × 6 strands)
  • מכנסיים — Trousers (linen)
  • מצנפת — Mitre (linen, shesh × 6 strands)
  • אבנט — Belt (multi-color, 24 total strands)
  • חשן — Breastplate (gold thread, 28 strands)
  • אפוד — Ephod (gold thread, 28 strands)
  • מעיל — Robe (12 strands, no gold)
  • ציץ הזהב — Golden Head-plate

🕊️ Life, Torah, and the Shemaya & Avtalyon Encounter

Pumbedita farewell blessing (Rabba):
'May the One Who grants life to the living grant you a long, good, and established life'
↓
Psalm 116:9: 'I shall walk before the Lord in the lands of the living'
Rav Yehuda: refers to Babylonia, whose air preserves life; or: the markets of Neharde'a
↓
Years of Life (Proverbs 3:2)
Rabbi Elazar: 'Years of life' = good years, not years of suffering or illness — years that are truly worth living
Shemaya & Avtalyon Incident
High Priest, envious of their following, sneers: 'Let the descendants of gentiles come in peace.'
They reply: 'Descendants of gentiles who do the deeds of Aaron — peace. Son of Aaron who does not — no peace.'
↓
Rabbi Berekhya: If Torah is ceasing from your children, marry the daughter of a Torah scholar — her children will be raised in Torah's atmosphere. As Job 14:9: 'from the scent of water it will blossom.'
