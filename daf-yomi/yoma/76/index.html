🍞 Why Did Manna Fall Daily?

Question posed to Rabbi Shimon ben Yohai: Why didn't the manna fall once a year to cover all needs, rather than descending every day?
↓
Parable — the King and His Son: A king who gave his son a yearly allowance: the son visited only once a year. Daily provision creates daily relationship.
↓
Reason 1 — Daily Faith: A father of four or five children would worry each night: 'Will manna come tomorrow?' So everyone directed their hearts to their Father in Heaven every day.
↓
Reason 2 — Freshness: Manna fell daily so it could be eaten while hot and fresh, providing the best possible nourishment.
↓
Reason 3 — Ease of Travel: The Israelites moved frequently through the desert; carrying heavy stores of food would be a terrible burden on the journey.

🍞 Rabbi Elazar HaModa'i: How Much Manna Fell?

R. Elazar HaModa'i
The manna that fell for Israel was so vast it accumulated until all the kings of East and West could see it! (cf. 'You prepared a table before me in the presence of my enemies' — Psalms 23:5)
Rabbis
Rabbi, you interrupted our discussion — we were analyzing the manna passage!
R. Elazar HaModa'i
I AM interpreting the manna! Consider: the Flood rose 15 cubits above the mountains (Genesis 7:20). Could it truly be 15 cubits from every valley and every peak alike?
Isi ben Yehuda
I agree: the manna accumulated in vast quantities until visible to all nations — confirming that the attribute of divine goodness vastly exceeds the attribute of retribution.
Key Principle The attribute of goodness is greater than the attribute of retribution: just as the Flood (retribution) rose 15 cubits, the manna (goodness) fell in immeasurably greater abundance.

🌊 Comparing Flood Waters to Manna

Question: How do we measure God's goodness vs. retribution?
↓
Flood (retribution): 'Fifteen cubits above the mountains were the waters' — Genesis 7:20
↓
Problem
Mountains vary in height — how could the water be exactly 15 cubits above both valleys AND peaks?
Resolution
The water miraculously leveled to exactly 15 cubits above every point — this was the minimum measure of retribution
↓
Implication for Manna: If even retribution was so great, the manna (attribute of goodness) was proportionally far greater — visible to all kings of East and West

🚫 Yom Kippur Afflictions: Five or Six?

Mishna: Five Afflictions

  • 1. Eating
  • 2. Bathing
  • 3. Smearing with oil
  • 4. Wearing shoes
  • 5. Conjugal relations
  • Drinking is subsumed within 'eating'

Gemara's Challenge

  • But a mishna elsewhere lists six!
  • Eating
  • Drinking
  • Bathing
  • Smearing oil
  • Wearing shoes
  • Conjugal relations

Resolution via Verbal Analogy

  • Drinking is included in eating — 'afflict your souls' covers both
  • Gezeirah shavah: sheikhar by nazir = strong wine (drunk, not eaten)
  • Therefore sheikhar on Yom Kippur = liquid drink, not food
  • Final proof: 'tirosh' (wine) 'takes away the heart' — it is drunk

🍷 Is Tirosh Wine or Sweet Fruit?

Baraita
One who vows not to benefit from tirosh is forbidden sweet foods but is permitted wine! Therefore tirosh means sweet fruit, not wine.
Challenge
'Your vats shall overflow with tirosh' (Proverbs 3:10) — vats hold wine, not fruit!
Answer
Vats can hold unfermented grape juice. Tirosh is still just fresh grape juice, not wine.
Hosea 4:11
'Harlotry, and wine, and tirosh take away the heart' — if tirosh were the same as wine, why list both? Tirosh must be an intoxicant — i.e., wine!
Rav Kahana's Wordplay Tirosh is written as tirash but read as tirosh. If one merits: he becomes a rosh (head, leader). If one does not merit: he becomes a rash (pauper). Wine elevates the worthy and ruins the unworthy.

🛢️ Daniel Proves Oil & Bathing Are Afflictions

דניאל י׃ג — וְסוֹךְ לֹא סָכְתִּי עַד מְלֹאת שְׁלֹשֶׁת שָׁבֻעִים יָמִים
Daniel 10:2–3 — Foundational Source Daniel mourned for three weeks: 'I ate no pleasant bread, neither did meat nor wine enter my mouth, neither did I anoint myself at all.' The angel responded: 'From the first day that you set your heart to understand and to afflict yourself before God, your words were heard.'

Since the angel called Daniel's abstention from oil an affliction, the Gemara derives that smearing oil is comparable to drinking in its physical comfort — and abstaining from it on Yom Kippur fulfills 'afflict your souls.'

For bathing: 'He afflicted you and let you hunger' (Deuteronomy 8:3) — the Gemara links abstaining from water (bathing) to the broader concept of affliction.
