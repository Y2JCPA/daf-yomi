QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Shimon ben Yohai, what was the PRIMARY reason the manna fell daily rather than once a year?
A. To prevent spoilage in the desert heat
B. So the Israelites would direct their hearts to their Father in Heaven every day
C. Because Moses had instructed them to collect only a day's portion at a time
D. To test whether the Israelites would trust God's promise
Correct: B — Rabbi Shimon's parable of the king and son illustrates the point: just as a son who receives a daily allowance visits his father daily, the daily manna ensured Israel turned to God in prayer and dependence every single day.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara ultimately prove that sheikhar (strong drink) on Yom Kippur refers to something drunk as liquid, not eaten as food (like anigron sauce)?
A. A verbal analogy (gezeirah shavah) with sheikhar in the nazirite laws, where it clearly means wine
B. The word sheikhar only appears in priestly contexts involving liquid libations
C. Rabbi Akiva explicitly rules that sheikhar is always liquid in halachic contexts
D. The verse says 'drink' explicitly alongside every mention of sheikhar
Correct: A — The Gemara uses a gezeirah shavah: 'sheikhar' in the nazirite passage (Numbers 6:3) unambiguously means strong wine that is drunk, so 'sheikhar' in the Yom Kippur context also means wine consumed as a beverage.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rav Kahana notes that 'tirosh' is written as 'tirash' in the Torah. What lesson does he derive from this discrepancy?
A. Tirash refers to unfermented grape juice while tirosh refers to fermented wine
B. The scribes originally spelled it tirash before a later vowel shift
C. If one drinks wine appropriately, he becomes a rosh (leader); if not, he becomes rash (poor)
D. The alternation signals that wine is permitted in small quantities but forbidden in excess
Correct: C — Rav Kahana reads the written form (tirash) as containing 'rash' (poor/impoverished) and the spoken form (tirosh) as containing 'rosh' (head/leader): wine either elevates the worthy or ruins the unworthy, depending on how it is used.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
From which biblical figure's experience does the Gemara derive that abstaining from smearing oil is considered an affliction on Yom Kippur?
A. Moses, who did not eat or drink during his forty days on Sinai
B. Joshua, for whom manna fell during Moses's absence
C. Daniel, who did not anoint himself during three weeks of mourning
D. Elijah, who fasted forty days in the wilderness
Correct: C — Daniel 10:2–3 describes Daniel's mourning as including 'neither did I anoint myself at all.' When the angel says Daniel 'afflicted himself before God,' the Gemara takes this as proof that abstaining from oil constitutes an affliction akin to abstaining from food or drink.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The mishna lists five Yom Kippur afflictions, yet another mishna appears to list six. How does the Gemara resolve this contradiction?
A. One mishna follows the opinion of Beit Shammai and the other follows Beit Hillel
B. The sixth activity (wearing shoes) is disputed and some Tannaim omit it
C. Drinking is included within the category of eating, so the two listings actually describe the same five activities
D. Conjugal relations is only rabbinically prohibited, reducing the Torah-level list to five
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that drinking is subsumed under the prohibition of eating, since both are covered by 'afflict your souls.' Once drinking is counted separately in one mishna and merged into eating in the other, both lists describe the same five afflictions.
