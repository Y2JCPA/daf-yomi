Yoma 25 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Sheshet, at what point in the priests' dressing did the lottery take place?
A. Before they put on any garments — wearing non-sacred clothes
B. After they had fully donned their sacred priestly garments
C. While actively in the process of putting on sacred garments
D. After the High Priest signaled with a special blessing
Correct: C — Rav Sheshet proved from the baraita that the lottery occurred while priests wore sacred garments. The Gemara confirms they were actively dressing — putting on sacred trousers while non-sacred garments were still partially on, dressing from below upward to avoid improper exposure.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What two facts did Abaye use to prove the Chamber of Hewn Stone was half in sacred space and half in non-sacred space?
A. Its size and the number of doorways described in the baraita
B. The lottery was in the east (requiring sacred space) and the Elder sat in the west (requiring non-sacred access)
C. The High Priest entered from one side and the other priests from the other
D. Priests wore sacred garments only in the eastern half of the chamber
Correct: B — Abaye argued: (1) The lottery was held in the east — this must be in sacred space to fulfill 'In the House of God we walked with the throng.' (2) The Elder sat in the west — chambers fully within sacred space cannot have openings toward non-sacred areas, so the west must be non-sacred.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How many spigots did Ben Katin install on the large laver, and why is this number significant?
A. 13 spigots — one for each of the 13 services in the second lottery
B. 2 spigots — replacing the original design with a more efficient system
C. 12 spigots — proving the sprinkler collects the blood (exactly 12 priests use the laver)
D. 10 spigots — corresponding to the 10 daily priestly services
Correct: C — Ben Katin installed 12 spigots, one per priest. If the slaughterer collected the blood, there would sometimes be 13 participants (when a non-priest slaughtered). Since there are only 12 spigots, the sprinkler must be the blood collector — keeping the count at 12.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to ben Azzai citing Rabbi Yehoshua, what principle determined the order of carrying the animal's limbs up to the altar ramp?
A. The order in which the limbs were cut during skinning
B. The relative meatiness and quality of each cut
C. The way the animal moves when walking alive — front parts first
D. Random assignment by a supplementary lottery
Correct: C — Ben Azzai said before Rabbi Akiva in Rabbi Yehoshua's name: the sequence follows how the animal walks when alive. The front limbs, which initiate walking, are carried up to the altar first — the living form of the animal guides the offering sequence.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why, according to Rava, is the right leg carried up to the altar together with the head?
A. They are the two heaviest parts and require two priests to lift
B. The head is bony and low in meat, so the fleshier right leg accompanies it to enhance the offering
C. The right leg is always first because the Torah commands the right side to take precedence
D. Both parts belong to the same priest who won the lottery for the head
Correct: B — Rava explained that the head has much bone in it, making it relatively low in meat. To compensate and honor the altar offering, the fleshier right leg accompanies the head. This pairing balances the bony head with a more substantial cut.
