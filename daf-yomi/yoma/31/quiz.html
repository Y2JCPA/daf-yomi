Yoma 31 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Yosef, do interpositions that normally invalidate immersion also invalidate the High Priest's 'reminder' immersion?
A. No — since it is ancillary, leniencies apply and interpositions are ignored
B. Yes — rabbinic ordinances are established according to the same rules as Torah law
C. Only if the interposition covers the majority of the body
D. The Gemara leaves this as an unresolved question (teiku) without Rav Yosef's input
Correct: B — Rav Yosef rules that all ordinances the Sages instituted follow the same standards as Torah law — so interpositions invalidate even this reminder immersion with no leniency.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What source does Rav Yosef cite to prove that partial body entry counts as halachic entry?
A. A Kohen who stretched his arm into the Holy of Holies
B. Someone who extended a vessel into a prohibited domain on Shabbat
C. The metzora (leper) who extended only his thumbs into the Temple courtyard
D. A non-priest who reached into the Temple treasury with one hand
Correct: C — The metzora extended only his thumbs (and big toes) into the Temple courtyard for the purification rite — this was accepted as valid entry, proving that partial body entry legally counts as entry.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How many total immersions does the High Priest perform on Yom Kippur?
A. Three
B. Four
C. Five
D. Seven
Correct: C — The mishna states: five immersions and ten sanctifications. The five immersions correspond to the transitions between golden and white garments throughout the Yom Kippur service.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How do the Rabbis explain why the High Priest performs only one sanctification per garment transition (rather than two)?
A. The Torah explicitly restricts sanctification to the moment of donning garments only
B. Two sanctifications would be an undue burden on the Kohen Gadol on Yom Kippur
C. The Torah juxtaposes removal to donning: just as donning requires sanctification while dressed, so does removal — one sanctification covers both
D. Sanctification at removal was a later rabbinic enactment that the Rabbis waived on Yom Kippur
Correct: C — The Rabbis derive from the Torah's juxtaposition of garment removal and donning that the sanctification for removal occurs while still dressed in the outgoing garments — the same moment — so one act serves both the removal and the upcoming donning.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What accommodation was made on Yom Kippur eve for a High Priest who was old or an istenis (physically delicate)?
A. He was fully exempt from immersion and could substitute hand-washing
B. A deputy Kohen Gadol performed the avodah in his place
C. Water was heated before Yom Kippur began and added to the cold mikveh water to warm it
D. He was permitted to immerse in a heated mikveh even on Yom Kippur itself
Correct: C — They heated water on Yom Kippur eve — before the fast — and mixed it into the cold ritual bath, making immersion tolerable for an elderly or delicate High Priest without violating any Yom Kippur restrictions.
