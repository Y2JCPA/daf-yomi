Yoma 66 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, why may the bull and goat of Yom Kippur not be kept until the following year?
A. The Torah explicitly prohibits keeping sacred animals beyond one year
B. Rabbinic decree — since they appear fit, one might accidentally sacrifice them at the wrong time
C. They become physically unfit for sacrifice after twelve months
D. They must be sold to fund the following year's Yom Kippur offerings
Correct: B — Rava explains it is a rabbinic decree specifically because these animals appear fit for their sacred purpose — unlike clearly disqualified animals, they create a genuine risk of accidental, untimely sacrifice.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Which laws are derived from the word עִתִּי ('appointed') in Leviticus 16:21?
A. The goat must be all-white and without blemish
B. Only a High Priest may lead the scapegoat to the wilderness
C. The escort must be designated in advance, the service proceeds on Shabbat, and it is valid even in impurity
D. The goat must reach the cliff before midday
Correct: C — The baraita derives three laws from עִתִּי: pre-designation the day before; performance even on Shabbat; and validity even when the escort is ritually impure.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Eliezer give evasive or indirect answers to his students' pressing halakhic questions?
A. He believed students should arrive at conclusions independently
B. He considered the questions improper or disrespectful
C. He never uttered any teaching he had not received directly from his own teacher
D. He was prohibited from ruling on Temple matters outside the Sanhedrin
Correct: C — The Gemara explicitly rules out that he was merely distancing them — rather, Rabbi Eliezer's lifelong principle was never to teach anything he had not personally received from his teacher, so unprecedented cases left him silent.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to one amoraic view, what was the punishment for a person who worshipped the Golden Calf in the presence of witnesses but without a formal warning?
A. Death by sword
B. Death by plague
C. Death by dropsy
D. No punishment — the Torah requires both witnesses and warning for capital liability
Correct: B — According to Levi, the three tiers were: witnesses + warning → sword; witnesses without warning → plague; no witnesses → dropsy (בַּמַּגֵּפָה).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Who were the 'Babylonians' in the mishna who would pull the scapegoat's hair, and how does Rabba bar bar Ḥana identify them?
A. Diaspora Jews from Babylon who came for Yom Kippur pilgrimage
B. Alexandrians from Egypt — called 'Babylonians' in Eretz Yisrael as a term of scorn
C. Roman soldiers stationed at the Temple Mount
D. A group of priests from the northern tribes who opposed Jerusalem's customs
Correct: B — Rabba bar bar Ḥana clarifies they were Alexandrians from Egypt. In Eretz Yisrael, people from Egypt were derogatorily called 'Babylonians.' A ramp was built so they could not grab the goat and delay its departure.
