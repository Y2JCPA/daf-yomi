⚠️ Why Can't the Yom Kippur Bull and Goat Be Kept Until Next Year?

Challenge
A sin-offering whose year has passed is not killed — it is left to graze! Why should the Yom Kippur animals be different?
Rava
By rabbinic decree — there is a concern that a mishap may occur. A dedicated animal must be uprooted quickly.
Challenge
What mishap? Other disqualified animals are left over without concern — why are these different?
Rava
Those unfit animals inspire no preoccupation — no one will accidentally sacrifice them. But the Yom Kippur bull and goat appear perfectly fit, and one might accidentally sacrifice them on the wrong day!
Resolution The rabbinic decree targets appearance-of-fitness: animals that look fit for their sacred purpose create a real risk of accidental, untimely sacrifice.

📋 Four Laws Derived from 'Appointed' (עִתִּי) — Leviticus 16:21

"And he shall send it away with an appointed man into the wilderness"
Leviticus 16:21 — the word עִתִּי carries multiple legal derivations
↓
עִתִּי = Designated
The escort must be designated the day before Yom Kippur — not chosen ad hoc
עִתִּי = At Its Time
Sent at the appointed time — even on Shabbat; the service is not delayed
עִתִּי = Always
Performed even in a state of ritual impurity (טֻמְאָה) — service is not suspended
אִישׁ = Man
A non-priest (Israelite) is fully qualified — this is not a kohen-exclusive service

🙏 The High Priest's Confession Over the Scapegoat

High Priest approaches the scapegoat (שָׂעִיר הַמִּשְׁתַּלֵּחַ) and places both his hands firmly upon its head
↓
Confesses aloud: אָנָּא הַשֵּׁם — "Please, God, Your people the house of Israel have sinned, done wrong, and rebelled before You"
↓
Utters the Explicit Name of God (שֵׁם הַמְפֹרָשׁ) — not a substitute name; this is the climax of the rite
↓
Priests and people in the Temple courtyard kneel and prostrate themselves, responding: "Blessed is the Name of His glorious kingdom forever and ever"
↓
High Priest hands the goat to the pre-designated appointed man who leads it on the ramp toward the wilderness of Azazel

⚔️ Punishments for Golden Calf Worship — Two Amoraic Views

View of Rav

  • 🗡️ Sacrificed or burned incense to the calf → death by sword
  • 🤗 Embraced or kissed the calf → death by plague
  • 👀 Rejoiced from afar → death by dropsy (בַּמַּגֵּפָה)

View of Levi

  • ⚠️ With witnesses + warning → death by sword
  • 👁️ With witnesses, no warning → death by plague
  • 🕵️ Without witnesses → death by dropsy

Tribe of Levi — Exception

  • ✅ Did not participate in the Golden Calf at all
  • 📜 "Who is for God, let him come to me — all the children of Levi gathered to him" (Exodus 32:26)
  • 🏆 Their loyalty qualified them as God's sacred servants

🤫 Rabbi Eliezer's Teaching Principle Students pressed Rabbi Eliezer with sharp halakhic edge cases: May the escort carry a sick goat on his shoulder on Shabbat? If he pushed the goat and it didn't die, must he descend and kill it? What is the fate of a known wicked person in the World-to-Come?

Each time, Rabbi Eliezer deflected with proverbs or irrelevant questions. The Gemara clarifies: it was not because he was distancing them with empty words. Rather — he never in his life uttered a single teaching he had not received directly from his teacher. Faced with unprecedented cases outside his received tradition, he could not rule.
