🏕️ Where Are the Bulls Burned? Rava vs. Abaye

Rabbi Yosei
The bulls are burned at the place of the ashes (shefekh hadeshen) — "Where the ashes are poured out shall it be burned" (Lev. 4:12).
Rabbis
The bulls are burned north of Jerusalem, outside all three camps — derived from the precedent of the High Priest's bull sin-offering.
Rava
The tanna who disagrees with Rabbi Yosei is Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov, who reads shefekh hadeshen as requiring a slanted place — not the ash pit itself.
Abaye
No proof from there! Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov may agree ash must be present and only add that the place be slanted. They disagree only on that detail.
Key Verse "Where the ashes are poured out [shefekh hadeshen] shall it be burned" (Lev. 4:12)

👕 When Do the Bull & Goat Render Garments Impure?

Bull and goat of Yom Kippur carried outside Jerusalem
↓
Garments of those who carry or burn them become impure — "And he who burns them shall wash his garments" (Lev. 16:28)
↓
"Them" — they themselves render impure; but not their ashes. Impurity ends when?
First Tanna
Impurity ends once bull/goat become full ash — form fully destroyed
R. Elazar b. R. Shimon
A charred mass whose form is destroyed still renders impure — only complete ash ends impurity
↓
Practical difference: the intermediate charred-mass state before becoming full ash

📡 Three Ways the Temple Learned the Goat Arrived

Mishna: Relay Platforms

  • Platforms (dirkaot) built along the entire route
  • People stood on them waving scarves
  • Signal passed platform to platform
  • News reached Jerusalem via chain relay
  • Jerusalem ← wilderness in real time

Rabbi Yehuda: Distance Calculation

  • Jerusalem to Beit Ḥiddudo = 3 mil
  • A priest walks 1 mil per service interval
  • After 3 service intervals pass = goat arrived
  • No platforms needed — pure calculation
  • Abaye: proves Beit Ḥiddudo is in the wilderness

Rabbi Yishmael: Crimson Strip

  • Crimson strip (lashon shel zehorit) tied at Sanctuary entrance
  • When goat reached wilderness: strip turned white
  • "Though your sins be as crimson, they shall be white as snow" (Isa. 1:18)
  • A miraculous sign of atonement achieved
  • Platforms not needed — divine confirmation

📜 The Torah Reading Ceremony of Yom Kippur

Torah scroll handed to the Synagogue Attendant (chazzan)
↓
Attendant gives it to Head of the Synagogue (rosh ha-knesset)
↓
Head of Synagogue gives it to the Deputy High Priest (Segan)
↓
Deputy places it in the hands of the High Priest (Kohen Gadol)
↓
High Priest stands and reads aloud — may wear linen vestments or his own white robe (reading is NOT a Temple service)
↓
High Priest recites eight blessings: Torah, Temple Service, Thanksgiving, Forgiveness of Sin, Temple, Israel, Priests, and a general prayer
↓
Note: The Torah reading and burning of the bull/goat happen simultaneously — one cannot witness both
