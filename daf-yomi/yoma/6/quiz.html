QUESTION 1 OF 5
What does the Gemara derive from the Torah using both 'he girded him' (singular) and 'he girded them' (plural) during the inauguration?
A. Aaron and his sons were girded simultaneously
B. Moses dressed Aaron first, then his sons sequentially
C. The High Priest's belt is identical to the common priest's belt
D. Only Aaron required a belt during the inauguration ceremony
Correct: B — The double language — singular then plural — teaches that Moses dressed Aaron before his sons. The verse about 'girding them' additionally implies the High Priest's belt is distinct from the common priest's.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
After the Gemara objects that the High Priest would not knowingly cohabit with a wife of uncertain status, how is the reason for his separation restated?
A. He is separated to ensure full concentration on studying the Yom Kippur service
B. The concern is that she may retroactively become a niddah, defiling him for past relations
C. The Torah explicitly requires the High Priest to be celibate before Yom Kippur
D. He must separate to avoid any chance of contracting tumah from her clothing
Correct: B — The revised concern (per Rabbi Akiva's rule) is retroactive niddah impurity: if she becomes a niddah, the impurity is backdated up to 24 hours, meaning past relations are retroactively defiling.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rav Ḥisda argue that the mishna's concern about niddah applies even according to the Rabbis, who dispute Rabbi Akiva's 24-hour retroactivity?
A. The Rabbis agree with Rabbi Akiva in all cases involving the High Priest
B. Regular inspections mean any blood found is nearly immediate — the Rabbis agree impurity is then non-retroactive only briefly
C. The Rabbis hold that the High Priest's wife must be examined by a specialist daily
D. Rav Ḥisda rules that all women are considered niddah by default before Yom Kippur
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda explains that the Rabbis only disagree with Rabbi Akiva for blood discovered long after the fact. If the wife is inspected regularly (as expected), discovered blood is virtually immediate — and even the Rabbis agree that impurity is certain in such a case.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Rabbi Zeira deduce about the immersion timing of a ba'al niddah (one who had relations with a menstruant)?
A. He immerses at night, identical to the niddah herself
B. He immerses during the day, unlike the niddah who immerses at night
C. He need not immerse if he was unaware of her status at the time
D. He immerses at dawn of the seventh day, a unique middle position
Correct: B — Rabbi Zeira deduces that the ba'al niddah immerses during the day (not at night like the niddah). If he immersed at night after seven days, that night would already be the eighth day — causing a practical problem for the High Priest's Yom Kippur preparation timeline.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Rav Naḥman and Rav Sheshet regarding tumat met (corpse impurity) in public Temple service?
A. Whether priests must immerse between corpse contact and service even in public
B. Whether corpse impurity is fully 'permitted' (הותרה) for all priests, or merely 'overridden' (דחויה) so pure priests must serve when available
C. Whether the High Priest himself is exempt from corpse impurity during Yom Kippur
D. Whether a priest who touched a corpse within 24 hours may still perform the Yom Kippur service
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman holds tumat met is fully permitted in public (no need to seek out pure priests). Rav Sheshet holds it is merely overridden — if pure priests from the same family unit are available on that day, they must serve and the impure ones stand aside.
