📜 Whose Mishna Is This? Rav Ḥisda's Challenge

Rav Ḥisda
This mishna cannot reflect Rabbi Akiva's view — it would be contradictory if so!
Rabbi Akiva
The verse says the pure person sprinkles upon the impure person — valid sprinkling requires specific intent to sprinkle on an impure person. Accidental sprinkling on an animal or pure person is invalid.
Rav Ḥisda
But our mishna assigns routine daily sprinkling to the High Priest with no mention of intent. This must follow the Rabbis, not Rabbi Akiva — intent is not required for valid sprinkling.
Conclusion The mishna follows the Rabbis: sprinkling is valid even without intending to sprinkle specifically on an impure person.

💧 The Sprinkling Paradox: Is the Sprinkler Pure or Impure?

Numbers 19:19: "The pure person will sprinkle upon the impure person"
→ implies the sprinkler is pure
↓
Numbers 19:21: "He who sprinkles the purification water will wash his clothes"
→ implies the sprinkler becomes impure!
↓
Resolution A
The sprinkler is pure relative to those who merely touch the water — not absolutely pure. Both verses coexist.
Resolution B (Rabbis)
"He who sprinkles" in v.21 means the one who carries the water — the carrier becomes impure, not the one who sprinkles it.

🧩 Solomon's Wisdom and the Red Heifer's Unknowable Logic

אָמַרְתִּי אֶחְכָּמָה וְהִיא רְחוֹקָה מִמֶּנִּי — "I said I would become wise, but it eludes me" (Kohelet 7:23)
King Solomon's Lament — Invoked by Rabbi Akiva Rabbi Akiva explains that the red heifer's paradox — purifying the impure while rendering the pure impure — cannot be resolved through normal logical reasoning. Even Solomon, the wisest of men, admitted defeat before this law. No a fortiori inference can unlock its secret.

🕯️ Incense vs. Lamps: A Direct Contradiction Between Tractates

Mishna in Yoma

  • 1. Burns the incense
  • 2. Removes ashes from the lamps
  • → Incense comes before lamps
  • → Implies lamps are cleaned last

Mishna in Tamid

  • 1. Removes ashes from five lamps
  • 2. Burns the incense
  • 3. Removes ashes from two lamps
  • → Lamps partially come before incense
