QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Rav Ḥisda say the mishna cannot follow Rabbi Akiva's view?
A. Rabbi Akiva held the High Priest was exempt from daily service during sequestering
B. Rabbi Akiva required specific intent to sprinkle on an impure person, but the mishna implies no such intent is needed
C. Rabbi Akiva held that only a Levite could sprinkle purification water
D. Rabbi Akiva disputed whether the High Priest could perform the Yom Kippur service at all
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda argues that since Rabbi Akiva requires the sprinkler to intend sprinkling on an impure person, the mishna's routine assignment of daily sprinkling — without any mention of intent — contradicts Rabbi Akiva's position.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What do the Rabbis say the phrase 'he who sprinkles the purification water' (Num. 19:21) actually refers to?
A. The kohen gadol who sprinkles on Yom Kippur specifically
B. Any person who stands near the purification water
C. The one who carries the purification water
D. The person who prepares the water by mixing it with the ashes
Correct: C — The Rabbis reinterpret 'he who sprinkles' in Num. 19:21 to mean the one who carries the water. This resolves the contradiction: the actual sprinkler (v.19) remains pure, while the carrier (v.21) becomes impure.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Akiva invoke the verse 'I said I would become wise, but it eludes me' (Kohelet 7:23)?
A. To prove that Torah study must precede all other wisdom
B. To explain why the High Priest must study the service for seven days before Yom Kippur
C. To show that even Solomon could not resolve the red heifer's paradox through logical reasoning
D. To demonstrate that only God can determine who is ritually pure
Correct: C — Rabbi Akiva invokes Solomon's lament to establish that the red heifer's paradox — purifying the impure while rendering the pure impure — is beyond rational resolution. No a fortiori inference can unlock this divine decree.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Abaye resolve the contradiction between the order of service in Yoma and Tamid?
A. The mishna in Tamid was authored by Rabbi Shimon Ish HaMitzpa who simply disagrees
B. Five lamps are cleaned before incense; the remaining two lamps are cleaned after incense — Yoma refers only to those final two
C. The High Priest follows a different order than ordinary priests on regular days
D. Incense is burned only after all seven lamps are fully cleaned without exception
Correct: B — Abaye resolves the contradiction by distinguishing the lamps: five are cleaned before incense (as Tamid states), then incense is burned, then the final two lamps are cleaned (as Yoma states). The two mishnas describe different parts of the same sequence.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Abba Shaul's source for the order of Temple service (lamps → incense → lamps)?
A. An oral tradition transmitted from Moses at Sinai regarding daily service
B. The ruling of Rabbi Shimon Ish HaMitzpa preserved in the Tosefta
C. A biblical derivation from the juxtaposition of verses in Exodus 30:7–8
D. Rav Huna's teaching based on a close reading of tractate Tamid
Correct: C — Abba Shaul derives the order directly from Exodus 30:7–8: 'Every morning when he removes the ashes from the lamps, he shall burn it' (incense), then 'he shall burn it' again — implying: ashes of lamps first, then incense, then the remaining lamps.
