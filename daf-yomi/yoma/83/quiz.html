QUESTION 1 OF 5
If a patient says he needs food on Yom Kippur but a doctor says he doesn't, what is the halakha?
A. Listen to the doctor — medical expertise always overrides patient's feelings
B. Feed the patient — he knows his own suffering better than anyone
C. Wait for a second doctor to break the tie before deciding
D. Feed him only water as a halakhic compromise
Correct: B — Rabbi Yannai rules we listen to the patient when he claims he needs food. Mar bar Rav Ashi extends this dramatically: even 100 doctors cannot override him. The verse 'The heart knows the bitterness of its soul' (Prov. 14:10) is the source.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Abaye say is the sign that a bulmos patient has recovered?
A. He can walk and speak normally without assistance
B. His fever breaks and breathing stabilizes
C. He can distinguish between good food and bad food by tasting
D. He explicitly says 'I am no longer hungry'
Correct: C — Abaye teaches that recovery from bulmos is determined by the ability to taste and discriminate between good and bad food. Under bulmos, a person eats indiscriminately — so restored discrimination signals recovery.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the dispute between the first Tanna and Ben Teima, what exactly do they disagree about regarding bulmos?
A. Whether honey or meat should be given first to restore eyesight
B. Whether one may feed a bulmos patient on Shabbat at all
C. Which is more severe — tevel or teruma — determining which to give the patient first
D. How many doctors must authorize feeding before proceeding
Correct: C — The practical dispute arises when only tevel (untithed produce) and teruma are available and tithes cannot be separated. The first Tanna holds teruma is more severe so feed tevel first; Ben Teima holds tevel is more severe so feed teruma first.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How did Rabbi Meir know the innkeeper named Kidor was wicked before any evidence emerged?
A. He recognized him from a previous encounter on the road
B. He analyzed the innkeeper's name, which alluded to a verse about wickedness
C. Rabbi Meir had a prophetic dream warning him
D. The innkeeper's behavior at dinner was suspicious
Correct: B — Rabbi Meir practiced name analysis — he saw that 'Kidor' alluded to the verse כִּי דוֹר תַּהְפֻּכֹת הֵמָּה (Deut. 32:20), 'a generation of perverseness.' This led him to withhold his purse, while Rabbi Yehuda and Yosei did not and were robbed.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Gemara, what is the halakha regarding feeding a person bitten by a mad dog the lobe of the dog's liver?
A. It is universally permitted as a proven medical remedy
B. It is universally forbidden as an ineffective folk remedy
C. It is disputed — the first Tanna forbids it; another Tanna permits it given the mortal danger
D. It is permitted only if two doctors certify the bite was from a truly mad dog
Correct: C — The Gemara records a dispute: the first Tanna holds one may not feed the patient the dog's liver lobe, while another opinion permits it. The practical difference is whether this folk remedy is considered effective enough to override the prohibition given the life-threatening situation.
