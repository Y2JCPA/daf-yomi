🩺 Doctor vs. Patient: Whose Word Decides?

Rabbi Yannai
If the patient says he needs food but the doctor says he doesn't — we listen to the patient and feed him. 'The heart knows the bitterness of its soul' (Prov. 14:10).
Gemara
But if the doctor says he needs food and the patient says he doesn't — we listen to the doctor. Why? Because illness causes tunba (mental confusion), dulling the patient's awareness of his own condition.
Mar bar Rav Ashi
Even if 100 expert doctors say the patient does not need food, but the patient himself says he does — we listen to the patient and feed him!
Core Rule Patient's self-knowledge overrides doctors when he claims need; doctors override the patient's denial when he resists eating.

⚖️ Bulmos: The Priority Order for Feeding

Bulmos (בולמוס) — severe life-threatening hunger causing impaired vision and unbearable pangs. Forbidden foods may be given to save his life.
↓
🥇 Step 1: Always offer permitted food first if available at all
↓
🥈 Step 2: If only forbidden foods exist, feed those with the least severe prohibition first
↓
🥉 Example: Tevel (untithed) vs. Teruma — disputed which is more severe (see slide 3)
↓
✅ Recovery sign: When he can distinguish good food from bad — or by tasting (Abaye)
↓
🍯 Treatment: Honey and sweet foods restore eyesight. Rav Naḥman: sheep's tail with honey is especially effective

📜 Tevel vs. Teruma: Which Prohibition is More Severe?

First Tanna

  • Feed the patient Tevel (untithed produce)
  • Do not feed him Teruma
  • Teruma is the more severe prohibition
  • Non-Kohen who eats Teruma intentionally — death penalty
  • Therefore save Teruma for last resort

Ben Teima

  • Feed the patient Teruma
  • Do not feed him Tevel
  • Tevel is the more severe prohibition
  • Tevel = no portion designated at all; entire fruit is forbidden
  • At least Teruma has a portion set aside for the Kohen

📛 Rabbi Meir and the Innkeeper Named Kidor

כִּי דוֹר תַּהְפֻּכֹת הֵמָּה (דברים לב:כ) — "For they are a generation of perverseness"
Famous Story Rabbi Meir, Rabbi Yehuda, and Rabbi Yosei were traveling together. Rabbi Meir would analyze names to discern character. At an inn, the host's name was Kidor. Rabbi Meir refused to leave his purse with him, citing the verse כִּי דוֹר תַּהְפֻּכֹת — 'a generation of perverseness' (Deut. 32:20). Rabbi Yehuda and Yosei left their purses — they were stolen. To recover them, the Rabbis got the innkeeper drunk and spotted lentils on his mustache. They went to his wife and said: 'Your husband told us — the sign is lentils.' She believed them and gave back the purses. Afterward, Rabbi Yehuda and Rabbi Yosei also began analyzing names.

🐕 Mad Dog: Signs and Treatment Dispute

Five Signs of a Mad Dog (כלב שוטה):
1. Mouth always open
2. Saliva constantly dripping
3. Ears floppy/drooping
4. Tail hanging between legs
5. Walks along the edge of the road
↓
A person was bitten — life-threatening danger
↓
Tanna Kamma
Do NOT feed him the lobe of the dog's liver — ineffective folk remedy, remains forbidden
Other Tanna
May feed him the dog's liver lobe — folk remedy is permitted given mortal danger
