↓

↓

🕯️ The Incense Privilege: Exclusivity and Divine Wealth

יִתְּנוּ קְטוֹרָה בְּאַפֶּךָ וְכָלִיל עַל מִזְבְּחֶךָ (דברים ל״ג:י׳)
Tosefta — A Once-in-a-Lifetime Service No priest ever burned the incense twice. A new priest was always found, because the Sages were insistent: whoever burns the incense becomes wealthy.

The verse states: "They shall put incense before You and whole burnt-offerings on Your altar" (Deut. 33:10). The Torah's blessing of wealth is linked specifically to the incense, not the burnt-offering — because incense is infrequent (twice daily, fixed amount), while burnt-offerings are frequent (many obligatory and voluntary offerings each day).

Rava adds: From this same verse, you will not find a young Torah scholar who rules on halakha unless he comes from the tribe of Levi or Issachar.

💬 Why Does Incense — Not Burnt-Offerings — Bring Wealth?

Rav Pappa
The verse (Deut. 33:10) mentions both incense AND burnt-offerings together. If the blessing of wealth comes from this verse, why do we say only incense makes one wealthy — not burnt-offerings as well?
Abaye
There is a critical difference: Burnt-offerings are frequent — countless obligatory and voluntary offerings are brought daily throughout the year.
Abaye
Incense is infrequent — only twice a day, always a fixed amount. The Torah's blessing of wealth attaches to the rarer, more exclusive service, not the common one.
Rava
From this same verse: Torah scholars who issue rulings come only from Levi (as written: "they shall teach Your laws to Yaakov") or from Issachar ("and the sons of Issachar who understood the times").
Principle Frequency diminishes the special blessing. The Torah's reward attaches most powerfully to the rarest, most singular forms of divine service.

⚖️ Is There a Separate Afternoon Lottery?

Rabbi Yochanan rules: No separate lottery for the afternoon daily offering. The same priest who won a privilege in the morning retains it for the corresponding afternoon task.
↓
Challenge — Baraita: "Just as they hold a lottery in the morning, so too they hold a lottery in the afternoon" — implying a distinct afternoon lottery!
↓
Resolution — Rav Shmuel bar Rav Yitzḥak: No contradiction — the two cases are different situations
↓
Weekday
Same priest keeps morning privilege in afternoon. Only 4 lotteries total — consistent with the mishna. No new lottery needed.
Shabbat
New afternoon lottery IS held! Priestly rotations (mishmarot) renew each Shabbat — outgoing mishmar serves morning, incoming mishmar serves afternoon.

🔢 How Many Priests for the Daily Offering?

Morning: 9 Core Priests

  • Slaughters the animal
  • Sprinkles the blood
  • Removes ashes from inner altar
  • Trims the menorah lamps
  • Carries the head + right hind leg
  • Carries the two forelegs
  • Carries the tail + left hind leg
  • Carries the breast, throat, spleen
  • Carries the fine flour and wine

Sometimes 10–14

  • +1 for wine libation (→10)
  • +1 for meal-offering (→11)
  • +1 for griddle-cake (→12)
  • +1 for variant libation (→13)
  • Rabbi Ḥiyya: sometimes 14
  • Baraita (R. Yehuda): up to 17

Afternoon: 11 Priests

  • Same 9 as morning offering
  • +2 priests carry the two logs
  • Log-placement is a positive mitzvah
  • Must be done by two separate priests
  • Derived by R. Shimon ben Yochai
  • from change of verb to plural

Ram Offering: 11

  • 5 priests carry the flesh portions
  • Intestines: 1 priest
  • Fine flour: 1 priest
  • Wine libation: 1 priest
  • Griddle-cake: 1 priest
  • Second wine: 1 priest → total 11
