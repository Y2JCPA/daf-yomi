Yoma 9 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why was the Second Temple destroyed?
A. Idolatry and sexual immorality
B. Baseless hatred (sinat chinam)
C. Failure to observe Shabbat properly
D. Corruption of the High Priests
Correct: B — The Gemara explicitly states the Second Temple was destroyed due to sinat chinam (baseless hatred), even though the people observed Torah, mitzvot, and gemilut chasadim — making this sin uniquely severe.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Approximately how many High Priests served during the 420 years of the Second Temple?
A. Fewer than 50
B. About 100
C. Over 300
D. Exactly 42
Correct: C — After deducting the long tenures of Shimon HaTzaddik (40 years), Yoḥanan (80 years), and a few others (~10 years), over 300 High Priests served the remaining years — fulfilling 'the years of the wicked will be shortened.'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why was the Tabernacle at Shiloh destroyed in the era of Samuel?
A. The entire nation had committed idolatry
B. The Ark was captured by the Philistines
C. Eli's sons delayed women's post-birth offerings and degraded consecrated items
D. King Saul disobeyed a prophetic command
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan ben Torta teaches that the Tabernacle at Shiloh was destroyed because Eli's sons delayed the bird-offerings of women after childbirth and degraded consecrated items — so severe it was as if they had committed immorality directly.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the significance of First Temple sins being 'exposed' vs. Second Temple sins being 'hidden'?
A. Exposed sins always receive harsher punishment
B. Exposed sins had their end of punishment revealed by prophets; hidden sins did not
C. Hidden sins required double the atonement offerings
D. There is no difference — both exiles lasted the same duration
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan and Rabbi Elazar teach: since First Temple sins were openly practiced, prophets revealed when the punishment would end (70 years). Since Second Temple sins were concealed, no end to their punishment was ever revealed — the exile continues.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What was Reish Lakish's argument that the Second Temple generation was superior to the First Temple generation?
A. They brought more sacrifices to the Temple
B. They produced more righteous High Priests
C. Even under foreign subjugation, they engaged in Torah study
D. They had prophets who guided them throughout
Correct: C — Reish Lakish argued the Second Temple generation was superior because even under foreign subjugation they studied Torah. Rabbi Yoḥanan countered that having Torah available made their failure to overcome sinat chinam all the more condemnable.
