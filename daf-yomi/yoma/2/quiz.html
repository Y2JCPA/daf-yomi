QUESTION 1 OF 5
How many days before Yom Kippur is the High Priest removed to the Chamber of Parhedrin?
A. Three days
B. Five days
C. Seven days
D. Fourteen days
Correct: C — The Mishna states explicitly: seven days prior to Yom Kippur the Sages remove the High Priest to the Chamber of Parhedrin — matching the seven days of the Inauguration and the Red Heifer sequestering.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Yehuda say the Sages would designate an additional wife for the High Priest before Yom Kippur?
A. To honor the High Priest with a higher social status
B. Lest his wife die, since the Torah's phrase 'his house' requires him to have a living wife on Yom Kippur
C. To ensure he would not be lonely during the seven-day sequestering
D. Because the Torah requires two witnesses from the HP's household
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda derives from Leviticus 16:6 — 'atone for him and for his house' — that 'house' means wife. If his wife died before Yom Kippur, the HP could not perform the atonement service, so the Sages designated a backup.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When choosing between two verbal analogy candidates — tzivah and tzivoto — what principle does the Gemara apply?
A. Always follow the majority of usages in the Torah
B. Where terms are identical in form, derive from the identical term rather than a merely similar one
C. Always follow the later verse in canonical order
D. Derive from whichever verse appears in a more stringent halakhic context
Correct: B — The Gemara rules that when identical forms are available (tzivah/tzivah), those take priority over merely similar forms (tzivah/tzivoto). The school of Rabbi Yishmael used similar forms only because no identical form was available.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject extending 7-day sequestering to Shemini Atzeret (the Eighth Day of Assembly)?
A. Because Shemini Atzeret has no dedicated sacrificial service
B. Because Sukkot itself does not require sequestering, and a fortiori the lesser Shemini Atzeret cannot
C. Because the verbal analogy only applies to Yom Kippur by rabbinic decree
D. Because Shemini Atzeret lasts more than one day
Correct: B — Rav Ashi argues: if Sukkot — the primary festival preceding Shemini Atzeret — requires no sequestering, then by a fortiori reasoning the subordinate Eighth Day cannot require sequestering either.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the core structural reason the Gemara derives sequestering for Yom Kippur rather than for Festival offerings?
A. Yom Kippur is the holiest day and the Torah says so explicitly in Leviticus
B. One derives sequestering before a service performed on a single day from sequestering before a service performed on a single day — matching Inauguration exactly
C. The Chamber of Parhedrin was architecturally designed only for Yom Kippur use
D. Festival offerings involve multiple priests, while Yom Kippur has only one, making derivation impossible for Festivals
Correct: B — The Gemara's decisive argument: we derive '7 days before a one-day service' (Yom Kippur) from '7 days before a one-day service' (Inauguration). Festivals span multiple days and recur three times yearly — neither criterion matches the Inauguration's structure.
