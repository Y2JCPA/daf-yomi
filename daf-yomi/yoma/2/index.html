🕍 The Seven-Day Sequestering Protocol

7 days before Yom Kippur — Sages remove the High Priest from his own home
↓
HP is installed in the Chamber of Parhedrin, a dedicated Temple room for this period
↓
Elders of the court read the Yom Kippur service to him each day to ensure mastery and readiness
↓
Rabbi Yehuda adds: Sages would also designate a second wife for the HP as a contingency
↓
Reason: Torah requires atonement for 'him and his house' (Lev. 16:6) — his house = his living wife
↓
This entire law is compared by the Gemara to the sequestering of the priest who burns the Red Heifer (tractate Para)

⚖️ Two Cases of Priestly Sequestering

High Priest — Yom Kippur

  • Sequestered 7 days in Chamber of Parhedrin
  • Elders read the Yom Kippur order of service to him daily
  • Purpose: ensure ritual purity, halakhic readiness
  • Rabbi Yehuda: also designate a backup wife
  • Source: derived by gezera shava from the Inauguration

Priest — Red Heifer (Para)

  • Sequestered 7 days in the northeast chamber ('bira')
  • Intentionally rendered impure by the Sages, then immersed
  • Purpose: refute Sadducean requirement of full-sunset purity
  • Used dung, stone, and earth vessels (immune to impurity)
  • Source: Mishna in tractate Para 3:1

📖 The Verbal Analogy (Gezera Shava) Debate

Gemara
From where is 7-day sequestering derived? The word tzivah (commanded) appears in the Inauguration: 'The Lord commanded to do' — and also in the Red Heifer passage. This verbal analogy links them.
Objection
But why derive from the Inauguration? Another verse uses tzivoto — 'On the day He commanded [tzivoto] the children of Israel.' That also contains the root tzavah!
Response
Where identical forms exist (tzivah/tzivah), we derive from those — not from merely similar forms (tzivah/tzivoto). Identical terms take priority in verbal analogy.
Objection
The school of Rabbi Yishmael derived a verbal analogy between veshav/veshav in leprosy of houses even though other occurrences existed. Doesn't that undermine the identical-form rule?
Response
That case had no identical form available elsewhere — so even a similar form was used. Here, identical forms exist, so we must use the identical form.
Halakhic Principle Gezera shava (verbal analogy) must use the most precise linguistic match available. Identical forms override similar forms.

❌ Rejecting Extensions of the Sequestering Rule

Question: Could the 7-day sequestering apply beyond Yom Kippur?
↓
Atonement offerings on Festivals?
No designated priest known in advance — impossible to sequester an unidentified individual
Fixed Festival service (3x/year)?
Must derive once-a-year from once-a-year. Festivals recur three times — they fail this test
Shemini Atzeret (one day)?
Sukkot itself requires no sequestering — a fortiori the lesser Eighth Day cannot require it
↓
✅ Only Yom Kippur satisfies all criteria: one priest, one day, once a year, uniquely holy
