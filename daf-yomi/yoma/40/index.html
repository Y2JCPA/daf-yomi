🎲 Is Drawing the Lots Indispensable (Me'akev)?

Central Question: Is the drawing of the lots (גּוֹרָל) on Yom Kippur me'akev — so indispensable that its omission invalidates the service?
↓
Rabbi Yannai
Both R. Yehuda AND R. Neḥemya agree: lots are indispensable
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Only R. Neḥemya holds lots indispensable; R. Yehuda says they are NOT
↓
Multiple baraitot tested against both positions — Rava and others neutralize each challenge; the dispute remains unresolved

🤺 Rabbi Yannai vs. Rabbi Yoḥanan: Two Versions of the Dispute

Rabbi Yannai
Both Rabbi Yehuda AND Rabbi Neḥemya hold that drawing the lots is indispensable. The Yom Kippur service is invalid without it.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Only Rabbi Neḥemya considers the lots indispensable. Rabbi Yehuda holds the lots are NOT indispensable — the service remains valid even if omitted.
Gemara Challenge
If Rabbi Yannai is correct that both agree lots are indispensable, then whose opinion does the baraita permitting service without lots follow? It seems to contradict you!
Rabbi Yannai (defense)
Rabbi Shimon did not know precisely what the Sages were debating — whether they meant the drawing or the placing of the lots — so his response covered both possibilities ambiguously.
Key Term מְעַכֵּב (me'akev) = indispensable; omitting it entirely invalidates the service retroactively

⚖️ Rabbi Yehuda vs. Rabbi Shimon: The Azazel Goat's Required Survival

Rabbi Yehuda

  • The Azazel goat must remain alive until the lot is drawn
  • The lot drawing is the critical designating act
  • Verse: goat stood alive 'before the Lord' — requires the lot ceremony to be completed
  • Focus: the lot itself consecrates/designates the goat

Rabbi Shimon

  • The goat must remain alive until it effects atonement through speech
  • Verse: 'to make atonement over it' = atonement through verbal confession (וִדּוּי)
  • The confession recited over the goat is the critical act
  • Focus: verbal designation, not physical lot placement

📋 Yom Kippur Blood Service: Required Sequence

1. Draw the lots — High Priest draws lots designating each goat: one for God, one for Azazel (גּוֹרָל לַה' וְגוֹרָל לַעֲזָאזֵל)
↓
2. Slaughter the bull — High Priest's personal bull is slaughtered as a ḥatat (חַטָּאת)
↓
3. Bull's blood — Holy of Holies — Sprinkled before and upon the Ark cover; then toward the curtain (פָּרֹכֶת)
↓
4. Slaughter the goat — The goat designated for God is slaughtered as a sin-offering
↓
5. Goat's blood — Holy of Holies — Sprinkled in the same manner as the bull's blood inside the Kodesh HaKodashim
↓
Sequence Rule: Advancing the goat's service ahead of the bull's corresponding service invalidates the goat's service. Advancing the bull's ahead of the goat's does not invalidate.

✋ The Left-Hand Lot Problem — Rabbi Akiva's Ruling

Students' Question:
The High Priest drew the lot for God with his left hand (invalid). May he transfer the lot to his right hand?
↓
Rabbi Akiva: Do not transfer it to the right hand
↓
Simple Reading
Reason: not to give minim (heretics) an opening → implies transfer would otherwise be permitted, suggesting lots are not indispensable
Rava's Reinterpretation
Students actually ask: even if he may transfer, does the left-drawn lot remain valid? Akiva says: don't try regardless, lest heretics claim the left draw is acceptable
↓
Rava's conclusion: No proof either way about indispensability — the baraita is neutralized
