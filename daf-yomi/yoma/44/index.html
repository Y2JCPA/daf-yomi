⚖️ Is the Clearance Between Hall and Altar Torah or Rabbinic Law?

Rav Aḥa bar Ahava
The prohibition in the area between the Hall and the altar is Torah law. The Sages derived it from the same transmitted tradition as the Sanctuary — it has biblical status, not merely rabbinic.
Rava
The prohibition there is only Rabbinic. It was limited to that area because it is open to the Sanctuary with no barrier — so a priest might accidentally walk in. Elsewhere, separation exists and no fence is needed.
Rav Aḥa — Challenge
If it were merely Rabbinic, why limit it only to between the Hall and altar? Priests could also accidentally enter other sacred areas — the Sages should have extended the fence more broadly!
Rava — Response
Reasonable to limit it there precisely because it is contiguous with the Sanctuary — one sanctity, no barrier. Elsewhere priests are separated by walls and would not accidentally wander in.
Gemara's Note The debate remains unresolved. The practical prohibition is accepted by all — but its source (Torah or Rabbinic) is disputed between Rav Aḥa bar Ahava and Rava.

🪣 The Coal Pan: Regular Days vs. Yom Kippur

Regular Day

  • 🥈 Scoop coals with a silver pan
  • Pour coals into a gold pan (3 kav capacity)
  • Silver scoop holds 4 kav → ~1 kav scattered
  • Pan body is heavy with thick sides
  • Handle is short
  • No ring on the handle
  • Color: greenish gold (Kalistra gold)

Yom Kippur

  • 🥇 Scoop coals with gold pan directly
  • Bring that same pan into the Holy of Holies — one pan only
  • Pan is light (easier to carry while walking backward)
  • Sides are thin and soft
  • Handle is long
  • Has a ring that clatters: "his sound shall be heard"
  • Color: red gold (finest quality)

Leviticus 16:17 — Atonement Order & Incense Atones "And have made atonement for himself, and for his household, and for all the assembly of Israel"

The Torah's sequence is legally significant: High Priest first → household second → all Israel third. Each stage of atonement is a prerequisite for the next.

Does incense effect atonement? Yes — Rabbi Ḥananya derives this from the plague narrative (Num. 17:12): "He put on the incense and made atonement for the people," halting the deadly plague. This proves incense carries atoning power.

🔢 Coal Pan Sizes — Resolving the kav Contradiction

Mishna: Regular day — priest scoops with 4-kav silver pan, pours into 3-kav gold pan
↓
⚠️ Contradiction: One baraita: 1 kav was scattered. Another baraita: 2 kav were scattered!
↓
Rav Ḥisda
2-kav baraita follows R. Yishmael b. R. Yoḥanan ben Beroka: on Yom Kippur he used a 2-kav pan → 2 kav scattered from a 4-kav scoop
Rav Ashi
Both follow Rabbi Yosei: his desert se'a = 5 Jerusalem kav (not 6). Difference in scattered coals reflects which se'a measurement you apply
↓
Yom Kippur resolution: High Priest scoops with gold pan → carries it directly into Holy of Holies — no transfer needed, one pan throughout
