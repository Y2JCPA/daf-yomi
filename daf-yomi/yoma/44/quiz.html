QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why was a silver pan used to scoop coals on regular days instead of gold?
A. Silver is more heat-resistant than gold and safer near fire
B. It would be degrading to use the dignified gold pan for the labor-intensive scooping; gold is reserved for carrying the coals
C. The Torah explicitly mandates silver for the daily afternoon service
D. The silver pan was the only size that fit exactly 4 kav
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that gold should not be subjected to the strenuous labor of scooping from the fire — it is reserved for the dignified act of carrying. On Yom Kippur, the High Priest scoops directly with gold because that same pan goes into the Holy of Holies.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rav Aḥa bar Ahava derive from the baraita about the area between the Hall and the altar?
A. The prohibition there is purely a rabbinic fence around the Sanctuary
B. The prohibition there is Torah law, derived by the same tradition as the Sanctuary itself
C. There is no clearance prohibition in that area at all
D. Only the High Priest — not ordinary priests — must leave that area
Correct: B — Rav Aḥa bar Ahava reasons that since the Sages applied the clearance requirement to the area between the Hall and altar using the same logic as the Sanctuary, this must reflect a Torah-level prohibition transmitted as tradition — otherwise the Sages would have extended the rabbinic fence more broadly.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How did Rabbi Ḥananya prove that incense effects atonement?
A. From the fact that incense precedes the blood presentations on Yom Kippur
B. From the plague narrative where Aaron placed incense and 'made atonement for the people,' stopping the plague (Num. 17:12)
C. From Leviticus 16:17 which links the word 'atonement' directly to the incense altar
D. From the daily incense being called a 'most holy' offering in the Torah
Correct: B — Rabbi Ḥananya cites Numbers 17:12 where Aaron 'put on the incense and made atonement for the people,' halting a deadly plague. This explicit use of the root כ-פ-ר (atonement) in connection with incense proves it has intrinsic atoning power.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What distinguishes the Yom Kippur coal pan from the regular daily coal pan?
A. It is made of silver on the inside and gold on the outside
B. It is lighter, has thin sides, a long handle, and a ring that makes noise as the High Priest walks
C. It holds exactly 4 kav like the regular pan but is a different color
D. It is identical in construction but consecrated separately for Yom Kippur use
Correct: B — The Yom Kippur pan is light (for ease of carry), thin-sided, has a long handle, and bears a ring that clatters — fulfilling 'his sound shall be heard when he goes in' (Ex. 28:35). The regular pan is heavy with thick sides and a short handle.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Leviticus 16:17 as interpreted in the Gemara, what is the required order of atonement on Yom Kippur?
A. All Israel first, then the High Priest's household, then the High Priest himself
B. The household and all Israel are atoned simultaneously, then the High Priest
C. The High Priest's own atonement must precede his household's, which must precede all Israel's
D. All three atonements happen simultaneously in a single service
Correct: C — The verse's sequence — 'for himself, and for his household, and for all the assembly' — is legally precise. The High Priest's personal atonement is completed first, then his household's, then all Israel's. Each stage is a prerequisite for the next.
