QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Rabbis, why can't synagogues in large cities contract leprosy-impurity (tzara'at habayit)?
A. Synagogues are inherently holy and therefore immune from all impurity
B. They belong to the public — all of Israel — and thus lack the private ownership required by the verse
C. They are too large for the impurity signs to cover a meaningful portion
D. The verse in Leviticus 14 only applies to residential dwellings, not communal buildings
Correct: B — The Rabbis hold that large-city synagogues are public property belonging to all of Israel. The verse 'land of your possession' (Lev. 14:34) requires private ownership — which such synagogues lack — so tzara'at cannot apply to them.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the Gemara, the Chamber of Hewn Stone (Lishkat HaGazit) was located in the portion of which tribe?
A. Levi
B. Judah
C. Benjamin
D. Ephraim
Correct: C — The Gemara teaches that while the Temple Mount, Heikhal, and Altar were in Judah's portion, the Chamber of Hewn Stone was in Benjamin's portion — alluded to by Moses' blessing: 'he rests between his shoulders' (Deut. 33:12).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the precise point of dispute between Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi and the Rabbis regarding the priestly belt?
A. Whether the High Priest's belt on Yom Kippur is made of linen or kilayim
B. Whether a common priest's belt throughout the year is made of kilayim or pure linen
C. Whether the belt counts among the four or the eight priestly garments
D. Whether a replacement High Priest must wear the same belt as the original
Correct: B — All authorities agree that the High Priest's Yom Kippur belt is linen (explicit in the Torah). The dispute — clarified by Ravin — concerns only the common priest's belt during the rest of the year: Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi says kilayim; the Rabbis say linen.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yosei, what is the permanent status of a replacement High Priest once the original recovers?
A. He continues as High Priest, since he was formally appointed
B. He returns to serving as a common priest with four garments
C. He may serve as High Priest only on future Yom Kippurim
D. He can serve neither as High Priest nor as a common priest
Correct: D — Rabbi Yosei holds that once elevated to the High Priesthood, one cannot descend to common priesthood (sanctity does not diminish). Yet he also cannot serve as High Priest since the original has reclaimed that role — leaving the replacement permanently suspended from service.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which historical incident does Rabbi Yosei cite as proof for his ruling about the replacement High Priest?
A. Aaron's sons Nadav and Avihu, who were disqualified after their elevation
B. Yosef ben Elem of Tzippori, who served as replacement and was thereafter barred from both roles
C. The dispute between Hillel and Shammai over High Priest succession during the Second Temple era
D. King David's permanent designation of Zadok over Abiathar
Correct: B — Rabbi Yosei cites the case of Yosef ben Elem of Tzippori: when a High Priest was disqualified, Yosef ben Elem served in his place. Once the original recovered, King Yanai ruled that Yosef ben Elem could serve in neither capacity — a real precedent confirming Rabbi Yosei's position.
