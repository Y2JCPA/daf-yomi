QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yochanan, from where is the law sequestering the High Priest before Yom Kippur derived?
A. From Sinai — the cloud rested six days and God called Moses on the seventh
B. From the inauguration of Aaron and his sons (Lev. 8:34): 'to do, to make atonement'
C. From a tradition (halacha l'Moshe miSinai) with no scriptural basis
D. From the laws governing a nazirite's period of preparation
Correct: B — Rabbi Yochanan derives sequestering from Lev. 8:34 — 'as has been done this day… to make atonement.' Just as the inauguration required seven days of preparation, so does Yom Kippur.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Reish Lakish's challenge to deriving sequestering from the inauguration?
A. The inauguration lasted eight days, not seven
B. If derived from the inauguration, missed Yom Kippur service could not be compensated — yet the mishna designates a backup priest
C. The inauguration used different animals than Yom Kippur requires
D. The inauguration was performed by Moses, not Aaron, so the analogy fails
Correct: B — Reish Lakish argues: inauguration days could not be made up if missed, so deriving from there would imply Yom Kippur also has no makeup — yet the mishna designates a substitute priest, contradicting this.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
From where does Reish Lakish derive the requirement to sequester the High Priest?
A. From the inauguration of Aaron, same as Rabbi Yochanan
B. From the laws of the Red Heifer preparation
C. From Sinai — the cloud rested six days and God called Moses on the seventh
D. From the verse about Aaron entering the sanctuary (Lev. 16:3)
Correct: C — Reish Lakish derives from the Sinai event: six days of cloud-covered preparation followed by God calling Moses on the seventh, modeling priestly preparation before entering divine service.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which of the following best explains why the High Priest is NOT sequestered before Shavuot?
A. Shavuot lasts two days, making preparation impractical
B. Shavuot's offerings are not linked to the inauguration by gezera shava and its service is not exclusive to the High Priest
C. Shavuot does not require a bull or ram offering at all
D. The Gemara considers Shavuot but leaves the question unresolved
Correct: B — Multiple distinctions apply: Yom Kippur's bull/ram is connected to the inauguration by gezera shava; Yom Kippur service is High Priest–exclusive; and Yom Kippur is an 'initial service' — none of which characterize Shavuot.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi's reasoning, the bull and ram used by the High Priest on Yom Kippur come from:
A. The Temple treasury (hekdesh funds)
B. Voluntary donations collected from the public
C. Half from the High Priest personally and half from the community
D. The High Priest's personal property, derived by gezera shava from the inauguration
Correct: D — Rav Ashi establishes a gezera shava linking Yom Kippur's bull and ram to the inauguration's bull and ram — and since Aaron's inauguration offerings were personal, so too are the High Priest's Yom Kippur offerings.
