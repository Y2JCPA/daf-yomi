📜 Deriving Sequestering from the Inauguration

Lev. 8:34: "As has been done this day, so the Lord commanded — to make atonement for you"
↓
R. Yochanan: לַעֲשׂוֹת (to do) implies: just as the inauguration required 7 days of preparation, so does Yom Kippur's atonement service
↓
Rav Dimi reports: R. Yochanan derived one case from the inauguration (Yom Kippur); R. Yehoshua ben Levi derived two (also the Red Heifer)
↓
Reish Lakish's source instead: From Sinai — cloud rested 6 days, God called Moses on the 7th, teaching priestly preparation before divine service
↓
Tension: Mishna requires 7 days, but Sinai shows 6. Reish Lakish: the 7-day mishna is R. Yehuda ben Beteira's opinion — the baseline is 6

⚔️ Why Not Sequester Before Shavuot Too?

Gemara
Shavuot also involves 'seven days of preparation for one day's service' — shouldn't the priest be sequestered beforehand as well?
Rabbi Abba
Shavuot is preceded only by weekdays — there is no verse connecting it to the inauguration as there is for Yom Kippur.
Gemara
According to R. Eliezer b. R. Shimon who says Yom Kippur also has two rams — now what distinguishes it from Shavuot?
Ravina
Yom Kippur is service exclusive to the High Priest — derive it from the inauguration, which was also exclusive to a single officiant. Shavuot is not restricted this way.
Ravina (alt.)
Alternatively: Yom Kippur is an initial service (חינוך) — derive it from the inauguration, also an initial service. Shavuot is not inaugural.
Convergence Multiple answers reinforce the same boundary: Yom Kippur is uniquely tied to the inauguration through gezera shava, High Priest exclusivity, and 'initial service' status — none of which apply to Shavuot.

🎺 Yom Kippur vs. Rosh HaShana — Why No Sequestering Before RH?

Yom Kippur ✅

  • Bull + ram linked to inauguration by gezera shava
  • Service performed exclusively by High Priest
  • Offerings come from High Priest's personal property
  • An 'initial service' — analogous to inauguration
  • Sequestering required — 7 days

Rosh HaShana ❌

  • Bull + ram NOT linked to inauguration by gezera shava
  • Service not exclusively restricted to High Priest
  • Offerings come from communal property
  • Not an 'initial' service in the relevant sense
  • No sequestering required

🤝 Reish Lakish Challenges Rabbi Yochanan

Rabbi Yochanan
Sequestering before Yom Kippur is derived from the inauguration: 'to do, to make atonement' (Lev. 8:34).
Reish Lakish
If from the inauguration — then just as inauguration offerings could never be compensated (a missed day is lost), the same should apply to Yom Kippur. Yet the mishna designates a backup priest!
Rabbi Yochanan
Then from where do you derive the sequestering law?
Reish Lakish
From Sinai: the cloud rested on the mountain six days, and on the seventh God called to Moses — demonstrating preparation before entering Divine service.
Gemara
But the mishna requires seven days of sequestering — Sinai gives only six!
Reish Lakish
The mishna stating seven days follows R. Yehuda ben Beteira's stringent opinion; the fundamental law requires only six days.
Stakes of the Dispute The source of sequestering determines whether a disqualified priest's service can be compensated. Yochanan accepts makeup service; Reish Lakish avoids the problem entirely by deriving from Sinai rather than inauguration.
