Yoma 22 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Before the lottery was instituted, how was the privilege of removing the ashes from the altar determined?
A. The High Priest assigned the task each morning by name
B. A race up the altar ramp — first priest within 4 cubits of the top wins
C. The eldest kohen on duty always received the honor
D. Whoever arrived at the Temple courtyard first was assigned the task
Correct: B — The mishna describes a footrace up the ramp; the first priest to reach within four cubits of the top of the altar won the right to remove the ashes.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, how many separate ordinances were instituted regarding the ash-removal lottery?
A. One — instituted solely because of the dangerous racing incident
B. Two — first when many priests showed up, second after the race turned violent
C. Three — a gradual reform process over multiple High Priests
D. None — the mishna describes only a one-time emergency measure
Correct: B — Rav Ashi distinguishes two ordinances: the first when they saw unexpectedly many priests competing, the second after the race itself produced a dangerous injury.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Elazar say it is forbidden to count the Jewish people directly?
A. It is a Rabbinic fence around the Torah with no explicit biblical source
B. Hosea 2:1 compares Israel to uncountable sand — numbering them violates this verse
C. Counting invites the evil eye and is therefore Rabbinically prohibited
D. It was associated with Roman census practices that Jews had to avoid
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar derives from Hosea 2:1 — 'the number of Israel will be like the sand of the sea which cannot be measured' — that directly counting Israel violates a negative commandment.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why did Saul's royal dynasty not continue to future generations?
A. Because Saul committed the grave sin of sparing Agag of Amalek
B. Because Saul had no surviving male heirs after the war with the Philistines
C. Because his ancestry was too perfect — leaving no room for Divine merit to operate
D. Because David's military victories made Saul's line politically obsolete
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan in the name of Rabbi Shimon ben Yehotzadak explains that Saul's flawless lineage paradoxically doomed his dynasty — with no flaw for Divine mercy to 'overlook,' there was no basis for continuity.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why is the sin involving Bathsheba NOT included among David's two formally counted transgressions?
A. The Gemara rules it was not actually a sin since Bathsheba's husband Uriah had issued a bill of divorce
B. David was punished personally for it — he fasted, mourned, and suffered — so it was settled separately
C. Bathsheba had consented, which the Gemara treats as a mitigating factor removing it from the count
D. The Gemara does not discuss Bathsheba at all in this passage
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that David was punished in his own body for the Bathsheba incident (his child died; he fasted and lamented), so that sin was already paid for and no longer counted against him.
