QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Abbahu, why is the measure for foods that cause ritual impurity an egg-bulk rather than an olive-bulk?
A. The Torah explicitly states 'egg-bulk' in the impurity passage
B. The verse uses the doubled expression okhel ye'akhel, and the esophagus holds exactly one chicken egg's volume
C. The Sages of Jabez voted to institute this larger measure for leniency
D. The bar yokhani bird's egg was the standard Temple unit of measure
Correct: B — Rabbi Abbahu derived the egg-bulk from okhel ye'akhel — 'food that may be eaten' — a doubled expression indicating food consumed in one swallow. The esophagus holds no more than a chicken egg, establishing that as the threshold.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Elazar advise someone who ate forbidden fat to write down the exact amount?
A. To confess precisely before the court and reduce punishment
B. So the priest can calculate the correct sin-offering animal
C. Because a future court might increase the minimum measure, and he needs to know if he is still obligated
D. Because the Sages required written records of all unwitting sins
Correct: C — If a future court raises the minimum measure for liability, someone who ate less than the new threshold would be exempt. Writing down the amount lets him know — in that future scenario — whether he still owes an offering.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the dispute between Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel about drinking on Yom Kippur?
A. Shammai: any liquid at all; Hillel: only a revi'it creates liability
B. Shammai: a quarter-log; Hillel: two cheeks full (a cheekful)
C. Both agree on a cheekful but disagree whether it must be water
D. Shammai: a cheekful; Hillel: a quarter-log
Correct: B — Beit Shammai hold the measure is a quarter-log — a fixed volume. Beit Hillel hold it is two cheeks full (melo lugmav) — an anatomical, person-relative measure. Rav Hoshaya notes this is a rare instance where Hillel may be more stringent.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Reish Lakish's ruling about someone who eats in an excessive manner on Yom Kippur?
A. He is doubly liable because he compounded the sin through gluttony
B. He is exempt, since eating beyond satiation is not halachically considered 'eating'
C. He must bring two separate sin-offerings — one for eating, one for the excess
D. He is liable only if he ate the date-bulk, even while overeating
Correct: B — Reish Lakish rules that eating beyond satiation — forcing oneself to eat when full — is not classified as halachic 'eating,' and thus carries no Yom Kippur liability. A parallel ruling applies to a non-priest who overate teruma.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When Rabbi Yoḥanan says 'measures and punishments are halakha transmitted to Moses from Sinai,' what was the Gemara's initial surprise?
A. That Moses received so many details despite the Torah's brevity
B. That Sinai traditions can override explicit Torah verses
C. That punishments are written explicitly in the Torah, so why would they need oral transmission from Sinai?
D. That different tractates give different measures for the same punishment
Correct: C — The Gemara is surprised because punishments are stated openly in the Torah — so how are they 'transmitted from Sinai' as oral tradition? The resolution is that while the punishments appear in writing, their precise measures and thresholds are part of the oral Sinaitic tradition.
