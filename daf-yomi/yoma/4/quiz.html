QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Rabbi Yoḥanan and Reish Lakish about the High Priest's sequestering?
A. Whether the High Priest must be sequestered at all before Yom Kippur
B. Whether the source is derived from the inauguration of the Mishkan or from the revelation at Sinai
C. Whether the sequestering lasts 6 days or 7 days
D. Whether the High Priest is sequestered in the Temple or at home
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan derives the 7-day sequestering from the inauguration of the Mishkan (Aaron and sons sequestered 7 days), while Reish Lakish derives it from Moses' 6-day wait in the cloud at Sinai before being called to receive the Torah.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yoḥanan respond to Reish Lakish's objection that the inauguration used blood while Yom Kippur uses water?
A. He concedes the point and agrees Sinai is the better source
B. He argues both blood and water are liquids and therefore legally equivalent
C. He cites Rabbi Ḥiyya's teaching that water replaced blood
D. He says the type of sprinkling is irrelevant to the law of sequestering
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan cites Rabbi Ḥiyya's teaching that 'water replaced blood' — the water sprinkling on Yom Kippur fulfills the same ritual function as the blood sprinkling at the inauguration, making the comparison valid.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yosei HaGelili's calculation, on what date were the Tablets shattered — and why is this problematic?
A. 17th of Tammuz — which matches the accepted tradition perfectly
B. 6th of Sivan — the same day the Torah was given
C. 23rd of Tammuz — which contradicts the accepted tradition of the 17th of Tammuz
D. 1st of Av — which is too late according to all opinions
Correct: C — Rabbi Yosei HaGelili holds the Torah was given on the 6th of Sivan, with 6 days of post-revelation sequestering plus 40 days on the mountain — this yields the 23rd of Tammuz, conflicting with the widely accepted tradition that the Tablets were broken on the 17th of Tammuz.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What lesson does the Gemara derive from the verse 'And He called unto Moses, and the Lord spoke unto him' (Leviticus 1:1)?
A. That only Moses was ever permitted to enter the Tent of Meeting
B. That God always communicates through a cloud or fire
C. That one must always call out to a person before speaking to them — a lesson in etiquette
D. That prophets must undergo ritual purification before receiving divine speech
Correct: C — The verse teaches derech eretz (proper conduct): God called Moses before speaking to him, demonstrating that one should never address another person abruptly. This principle is derived from God's own behavior as a model for human conduct.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the school of Rabbi Yishmael resolve the contradiction between Moses publicly hearing God at Sinai and the private voice in the Tent of Meeting?
A. The two events involved entirely different modes of Divine speech
B. A verbal analogy: just as the sea split for Israel, so the cloud split to give Moses private entry
C. The Tent of Meeting was actually open to all of Israel to enter
D. Moses had a unique spiritual faculty allowing him to hear what others could not
Correct: B — The school of Rabbi Yishmael uses a gezeirah shavah (verbal analogy): 'Moses came into the cloud' parallels 'Israel went into the sea on dry land.' Just as the sea split miraculously to let Israel through, the cloud split to allow Moses private access — containing the voice within.
