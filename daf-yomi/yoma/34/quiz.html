Yoma 34 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye, what offering immediately precedes the High Priest's griddle-cake offering (chavitin)?
A. The daily morning Tamid offering
B. The meal-offering (Minchah)
C. The libations (Nesachim)
D. The burning of wood on the altar
Correct: B — Abaye derives that the meal-offering (Minchah) precedes the chavitin because the verse 'to bring offerings by fire to the Lord' groups them together, and the Minchah as a category comes before the chavitin in the sequence.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Where in the afternoon service does the incense offering fit, according to Rabbi Yoḥanan?
A. Before the afternoon Tamid is slaughtered
B. Before the limbs are taken to the altar ramp
C. Between taking the limbs up to the ramp and pouring the libations
D. After all the libations are poured
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan derives from the verse 'and the other lamb' (Numbers 28:8) that the afternoon incense is burned after the limbs are taken up to the altar ramp but before the libations are poured.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How much were the High Priest's morning linen garments worth on Yom Kippur?
A. 8 maneh (800 dinars)
B. 12 maneh (1,200 dinars)
C. 20 maneh (2,000 dinars)
D. 4 maneh (400 dinars)
Correct: B — The morning garments came from Pelusium in Egypt and were worth 12 maneh (1,200 dinars). The afternoon garments from India were worth 8 maneh (800 dinars).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rabbi Yehuda says iron blocks were heated before Yom Kippur and placed in the mikveh water. How does Abaye justify this practice?
A. Heating water is not considered a melachah at all on Yom Kippur
B. The High Priest is personally exempt from all melachah restrictions
C. Rabbi Yehuda only prohibits unintentional acts for Torah-level prohibitions; heating water is only rabbinic
D. The heating was done entirely by Gentile Temple servants
Correct: C — Abaye distinguishes: Rabbi Yehuda prohibits unintentional (grama) acts only when they relate to Torah-level prohibitions throughout the Torah. Heating water with iron blocks is only a rabbinic restriction, so the unintentional continued heating on Yom Kippur is permitted.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Abaye prefer the view that Musafim (additional offerings) precede the frankincense vessels?
A. A direct verse in Numbers explicitly orders Musafim before frankincense
B. The repetition 'morning, morning' proves the complete Tamid sequence (including Musafim) precedes frankincense
C. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi rules explicitly that Musafim come first
D. Frankincense is categorized with meal-offerings which come very late in the day
Correct: B — Abaye reasons that the doubled phrase 'morning, morning' (Lev. 6:5) teaches that the entire Tamid morning sequence — including Musafim — is completed before the frankincense vessels are burned, since frankincense is not part of the core Tamid sequence.
