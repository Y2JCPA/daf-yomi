QUESTION 1 OF 5
From which verse does the Gemara ultimately derive that non-priests may flay and cut the animal?
A. Lev. 1:5 — 'The sons of Aaron shall sacrifice the blood'
B. Lev. 1:7 — 'The sons of Aaron shall put fire on the altar'
C. Lev. 1:9 — 'The priest shall burn all of it on the altar'
D. Lev. 1:8 — 'The sons of Aaron shall lay out the pieces'
Correct: C — Lev. 1:9 ('The priest shall burn all of it') is superfluous because burning was already taught in Lev. 1:8. The extra verse comes to teach that pre-blood-collection services like flaying and cutting may be performed by non-priests.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject using the wood-arrangement verse (Lev. 1:7) to permit non-priests to flay the animal?
A. The wood verse explicitly states that only priests may perform it
B. The wood verse is needed for its own sake — to teach that wood placement itself requires a priest despite not being atoning
C. Flaying occurs after blood collection, which is already priestly
D. A separate Mishnaic tradition explicitly required priests for flaying
Correct: B — One might have thought that since placing wood does not itself effect atonement, a non-priest could do it. The verse is therefore still needed to establish that wood placement is a priestly act — it cannot be 'freed up' to exclude flaying.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the basis of Rabbi Zeira's objection to Rabbi Yoḥanan's original ruling?
A. A death penalty requires explicit mention of מִיתָה in the Torah text
B. There is no service that is valid at night yet prohibited for a non-priest — wood arrangement is valid at night, so it should not carry a death penalty for non-priests
C. Wood arrangement is not listed among the Temple services in the Mishnah
D. Non-priests are only liable for services performed inside the Sanctuary building
Correct: B — Rabbi Zeira argued that genuine priestly services (carrying a death penalty for non-priests) are daytime services by definition. Since wood placed on the altar at night remains valid, wood arrangement cannot be the kind of priestly service that makes a non-priest liable to death.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the plural verb in Lev. 1:8 ('they shall lay out') function in the Gemara's analysis?
A. It teaches that all priests in the Temple must participate in laying out the limbs
B. It teaches that the limbs must be arranged in a specific order
C. It teaches that laying out the pieces requires two priests performing the act together
D. It excludes Levites from performing this service
Correct: C — The plural form וְעָרְכוּ ('they shall lay out') teaches that arranging the limbs on the altar must be done by two priests together, not by a single priest alone.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say the mishna's lottery list beginning with 'slaughter' does not contradict the ruling that wood arrangement also requires a lottery?
A. Slaughter and wood arrangement are assigned in the same lottery draw simultaneously
B. The mishna focuses on slaughter because it is irreparable if done at night, whereas wood arrangement done at night can be corrected by redoing it
C. Wood arrangement is actually excluded from the lottery but still requires a priest
D. The mishna's list is merely illustrative and not meant to be exhaustive
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the mishna emphasizes slaughter because slaughter performed at night is permanently invalid and cannot be undone. Wood arrangement, while also a priestly act, can be redone if performed incorrectly — so the mishna highlights the more critical, irreversible case.
