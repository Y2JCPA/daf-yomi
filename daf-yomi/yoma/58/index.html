🩸 Mix the Blood or Present Separately?

Rabbi Yoshiya
'Aaron shall make atonement upon it' — one act of atonement. The bull and goat blood must be mixed together before sprinkling.
Rabbi Yonatan
But the verse says 'of the blood of the bull and of the blood of the goat' — each blood is presented by itself, not combined.
Rabbi Yoshiya
Then why does the verse say 'once'? It comes to count the first of the seven sprinklings as one — not to separate the blood types.
Rabbi Yonatan
And the alternate baraita also says 'once' counts the first sprinkling — yet that baraita explicitly teaches the blood should be mixed. The verse naming each blood separately proves they are mixed before use.
📜 Disputed Verse "Of the blood of the bull and of the blood of the goat" (Leviticus 16:18) — does naming each blood separately mean they are kept separate, or does it merely describe what goes into the combined mixture?

🫙 Rami bar Ḥama's Vessel Dilemma

High Priest holds a full bowl of blood and an empty bowl
Mishna: 'placed the blood from the full bowl into the empty bowl'
↓
Rami bar Ḥama asks Rav Ḥisda:
Did he physically insert the full bowl inside the empty bowl — or simply pour from one into the other?
If inserted (nested)
Does the outer bowl form an interposition (ḥatzitzah) between the priest and the service vessel?
If poured
Simple transfer of liquid — no interposition concern; this is what the mishna already stated earlier
↓
Rav Ḥisda: The mishna's phrasing 'placed the full into the empty' implies insertion — and this IS valid service
↓
Alternate reading of Rami's question: Is nesting vessels a proper manner of service (derech avodah) at all, regardless of interposition?

↩️ Order of Sprinkling the Four Altar Corners

Rabbi Yosei HaGelili

  • Begins at the southeast corner
  • Proceeds: SE → NE → NW → SW
  • Goes in a leftward (counterclockwise) direction
  • Derives inner altar order from outer altar rules
  • Skips the first western corner he physically encounters on exit

Rabbi Akiva

  • Begins at the northeast corner (where Yosei ends)
  • Does not derive inner altar order from outer altar rules
  • Cites Rami bar Yeḥezkel: Solomon's sea faced south first
  • Ends where Rabbi Yosei HaGelili begins
  • Both agree: skip the first corner encountered from the west

🏛️ 'He Shall Go Out to the Altar' — The Exit Sequence

Finish inner sprinklings — priest has been on the western (inner) side of the golden altar, close to the curtain
↓
'And he shall go out to the altar' (Lev. 16:18) — Rabbi Neḥemya: wasn't he already at the altar? The verse teaches he was on the inner side, not yet at the altar proper
↓
Apply mixed blood to four corners — from below upward on all corners, except the corner directly before him (above downward)
↓
Cleanse the altar — sprinkle seven times on the top surface of the altar, sprinkling downward
↓
Begin from a corner — Rabbi Yosei HaGelili: start SE; Rabbi Akiva: start NE; both agree the first western corner is skipped
↓
Pour remaining blood at the base of the outer altar — service complete
