🥚 How Big Is a Grogeret (גְּרוֹגֶרֶת)?

Rav Yehuda (Rava)
The grogeret is larger than an egg-bulk. The Sages have a tradition: this amount settles the mind of the one who eats — less than this leaves one still afflicted on Yom Kippur.
Rav Zevid
This is not so! The grogeret is less than an egg-bulk. The proofs from sukka eating and zimmun for Grace After Meals contradict the larger-than-egg position.
The Question On Yom Kippur one is liable for eating a גְּרוֹגֶרֶת (grogeret — large date-bulk). Is this measure greater than, equal to, or less than a כַּבֵּיצָה (egg-bulk)? The answer affects the minimum eating that triggers כָּרֵת.

📖 Rabbi Tzadok's Telling Meal When they gave Rabbi Tzadok less than an egg-bulk of food to eat, he did three things:

• Held it in a cloth — did not wash hands for ritual purity
• Ate it outside the sukka
• Did not recite Grace After Meals afterward

Each omission signals a sub-threshold meal. This story becomes a battlefield: does it prove the grogeret is smaller than an egg-bulk (consistent with eating dates outside sukka), or can the evidence be explained away?

⚖️ The Core Dispute: Grogeret vs Egg-Bulk

Rava citing Rav Yehuda
Grogeret > Egg-bulk

Received tradition: the grogeret is the amount that settles the mind of one fasting. It is a meaningful, substantial quantity — necessarily larger than a mere egg-bulk.
Rav Zevid
Grogeret < Egg-bulk

Multiple proofs — the sukka incident, the zimmun rule, and the chametz debate — all point to the grogeret being the smaller of the two measures.
Why It Matters The minimum eating quantity on Yom Kippur that triggers karet liability depends entirely on resolving this dispute.

🌿 The Sukka-Based Proof Chain

Premise: A fixed meal of egg-bulk volume must be eaten inside the sukka on Sukkot
↓
If grogeret > egg-bulk
Then eating a grogeret of dates outside the sukka would be forbidden — but we have an incident of sages eating dates outside! Apparent contradiction.
If grogeret < egg-bulk
Then dates (below egg-bulk threshold) may be eaten outside the sukka — fully consistent with Rabbi Tzadok's practice and the sages' date-eating incident.
↓
Rava's rebuttal: Dates are fruit — and fruit need not be eaten in the sukka regardless of quantity! The sukka proof proves nothing about the grogeret's size.

🔍 Two Proof-Lines — Zimmun vs Chametz

Zimmun Proof (79b) → Grogeret < Egg

  • Zimmun for Grace After Meals requires only an olive-bulk
  • The Gemara shows: egg-bulk satisfies zimmun too
  • If grogeret > egg-bulk, paradox: more (egg) suffices but even more (grogeret) supposedly does not
  • This logical tension supports grogeret ≤ egg-bulk
  • Rav Zevid's primary proof

Chametz Proof (79b) → Grogeret > Egg

  • Beit Shammai: leaven measure is smaller than leavened-bread measure
  • They derived this by finding a measure one step above olive-bulk
  • If grogeret > egg-bulk, Beit Shammai skip egg and reach grogeret as their upper measure
  • The argument structure only works if grogeret is the next step up from egg
  • Supports Rav Yehuda's position — but Gemara ultimately deflects this too
