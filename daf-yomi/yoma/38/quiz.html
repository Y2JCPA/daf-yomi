Yoma 38 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What triggered the miracle that calmed the sea for Nicanor's second door?
A. The sailors prayed together on the deck
B. Nicanor cast the first door overboard as an offering
C. Nicanor embraced the door and said 'Cast me in with it'
D. A priest on board recited the Priestly Blessing
Correct: C — Nicanor stood up and embraced the second door, declaring he would go into the sea with it rather than let it go. This act of self-sacrifice immediately caused the sea to rest from its rage.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What justification did the Houses of Garmu and Avtinas give for keeping their Temple crafts secret?
A. They were paid to keep it secret by the High Priest
B. The techniques were too complex to teach in words
C. They feared the Temple would be destroyed and unworthy people would misuse the craft
D. They planned to write it all down in a book for future generations
Correct: C — Both families stated that their ancestors knew the Temple was destined to be destroyed, and they feared an unworthy person would learn the craft and use it — shewbread for idolatry, incense for impure purposes.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What made Ben Kamtzar's writing technique so remarkable — and what sets him apart from the other craftsmen?
A. He wrote in ancient script no one else could read; unlike others he had good reasons
B. He held four quills at once and wrote four letters simultaneously; unlike others he gave no justification
C. He could write backwards with both hands; he refused due to a private vow
D. He used invisible ink; he taught the technique only to his eldest son
Correct: B — Ben Kamtzar held four quills between his fingers and could write all four letters of a word simultaneously. Unlike Garmu and Avtinas who gave a pious reason, Ben Kamtzar offered no justification — making his refusal pure selfishness.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Elazar, what does it mean that 'the name of the wicked shall rot'?
A. The wicked person's name is erased from all public records
B. Their name is pronounced in a distorted way as a warning
C. People do not give their children the names of the wicked
D. A curse is recited over their name every Yom Kippur
Correct: C — Rabbi Elazar explains that the name 'rots' because people do not name their children after wicked individuals — the name simply falls out of use and is forgotten, unlike the names of the righteous which are passed down.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Reish Lakish teach about the relationship between a person's choices and divine assistance?
A. God equally assists all people regardless of their choices
B. God only helps those who have never sinned before
C. If one comes to impurify, the door is opened; if one comes to purify, he is actively helped
D. Free will is an illusion — God predetermines whether one is righteous or wicked
Correct: C — Reish Lakish derives from Proverbs 3:34 that divine assistance is asymmetric: those who choose impurity are merely given the opportunity (the door opens), while those who seek purity receive active divine help — free will is real but not abandoned by God.
