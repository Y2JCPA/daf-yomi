🏛️ Temple Craftsmen: Praised vs. Criticized

Praised — Nicanor

  • Brought copper doors from Alexandria
  • Storm arose; one door cast overboard
  • Embraced second door; offered his own life
  • Sea calmed immediately — miracle occurred
  • Door reappeared at port of Akko

Criticized — Garmu, Avtinas, Kamtzar

  • Refused to teach shewbread preparation
  • Refused to teach incense blending formula
  • Refused to teach four-letter writing technique
  • Sages dismissed them; replacements inferior
  • Restored but Ben Kamtzar never vindicated

🍞 House of Garmu — Secrecy Confronted

Sages
We dismissed you and brought Alexandrian craftsmen in your place — but their shewbread was vastly inferior. God created everything for His own honor (Proverbs 16:4). Return to your posts.
House of Garmu
Our fathers knew the Temple is destined to be destroyed. We feared an unworthy person would learn our craft and use it to bake bread for idolatry — so we kept the secret.
Sages
We acknowledge their piety: they never ate fine bread at home — only coarse bread — so no one could suspect them of benefiting from Temple materials.
Tradition
Same story for House of Avtinas: refused to teach the incense formula for the same reason. Their women wore no perfume lest they be suspected of using Temple incense.
📖 Shared Verse "God made everything for His sake" (Proverbs 16:4) — cited by Sages in both cases to justify restoring each family to their original post

Rabbi Akiva Recounts a Story

בֵּית אַבְטִינַס
🌿 A Descendant's Laughter and Tears Rabbi Yishmael ben Loga once went out to a field with a descendant of the House of Avtinas to gather herbs. He noticed the young man laugh and then cry.

Why did you cry? — "Because I saw an herb used in the incense formula, and I mourned that the glory of my fathers' house has been lost."

Why did you laugh? — "Because I was comforted that God removed the formula from our hearts — for if we had taught it, we might have brought Heaven's honor low."

Later, Rabbi Yoḥanan ben Nuri found an old man of the same family who possessed a scroll containing the full formula and spice locations — but by then the Temple was gone.

⚖️ Rabbi Elazar's Chain: Names, Righteousness, and Free Will

Ben Azzai's Principle: No one can usurp another's divinely appointed portion — "by your name they shall call you, in your place they shall seat you, from your own they shall give you"
↓
Righteous names are blessed (זֵכֶר צַדִּיק לִבְרָכָה): people name children after the righteous; their memory endures through generations
↓
Wicked names rot: people do not name children after the wicked — Pharaoh's name was not reused by later Egyptian kings; Sisera and Sennacherib's names were recycled as curses
↓
R. Elazar's hermeneutic: From a blessing of the righteous, infer a curse for the wicked — and from a curse of the wicked, infer a blessing for the righteous
↓
One righteous person sustains the world: "The righteous is the foundation of the world" (Proverbs 10:25). R. Yoḥanan: before the sun sets on one righteous person, another is already rising
↓
Reish Lakish on free will: One who comes to impurify himself — the door is opened for him. One who comes to purify himself — he is actively helped (Proverbs 3:34)
