Yoma 55 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Tosefta, how does the High Priest sprinkle blood toward the Ark cover?
A. He places his finger directly on the gold surface
B. He swings upward like a whip, directing blood toward — but not touching — the cover
C. He sprinkles from a distance of four cubits
D. He shakes the bowl so drops fall naturally
Correct: B — The Tosefta compares the motion to a whip-strike: the finger swings upward, sending blood toward the Ark cover without the finger or blood actually resting upon it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the total number of sprinklings the High Priest performs with the bull's blood in the Holy of Holies?
A. Seven downward only
B. One upward and six downward
C. One upward and seven downward
D. Seven upward and one downward
Correct: C — Both Rabbi Meir and Rabbi Yehuda agree: one upward (upon the Ark cover) plus seven downward (before the Ark cover), for a total of eight sprinklings — the same for the goat.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why must the High Priest count the first upward sprinkling together with each subsequent downward sprinkling (e.g., 'one and one,' 'one and two')?
A. To ensure he does not lose track of whether he completed seven downward sprinklings
B. Because the Kohen Gadol is obligated to verbalize every Temple service
C. The verse says 'he shall sprinkle' twice — the second is superfluous unless it teaches the first must be referenced with each count
D. To remind him to switch hands between sprinklings
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan derives from the doubled 'he shall sprinkle' in Leviticus 16:14 that the upward sprinkling must be counted alongside each downward one — so the High Priest will not forget the first sprinkling and count only downward ones.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the fundamental reason Rabbi Yehuda rules there was only one golden pedestal in the Temple?
A. The Temple treasury could not afford two golden pedestals
B. He is concerned that mixing blood vessels might lead to inadvertent use of a sin-offering whose owner has died, invalidating it
C. He holds that the bull's blood must be immediately disposed of before the goat's blood is sprinkled
D. Writing inscriptions on vessels is prohibited in the Temple
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda's concern is specifically about a sin-offering bird (nest) whose owner has died — such an offering must be left to die. If multiple blood-monies are mixed and an owner's death is confirmed, the entire mixed fund is invalidated, and writing alone cannot prevent the mixture.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the apparent contradiction between Rabbi Yehuda's ruling and the thirteen inscribed collection horns in the Temple (which used writing to separate funds)?
A. The horns were from a later period when the one-pedestal rule no longer applied
B. Writing on vessels can distinguish different types of donations but cannot guard against post-facto death of a sin-offering's owner
C. Rabbi Yehuda agrees writing works for shekels but not for blood vessels
D. The horns were only used outside the Temple, not in the inner courts
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes: written labels on shekel horns separate different categories of donations (new shekels, old shekels, birds) before any problem arises. But Rabbi Yehuda's concern is that a sin-offering owner may already have died — a post-facto event that writing cannot retroactively prevent.
