🪶 The Whip-Like Sprinkling Technique

כְּמַצְלִיף — like one who whips
Tosefta Ruling The High Priest does not sprinkle blood on top of the Ark cover. Instead, he sprinkles toward it — like one who lashes with a whip, with the motion directed at the Ark cover and before it. The Tosefta specifies: he holds his finger below and swings upward, so the blood flies toward the cover without resting upon it. This ensures the sprinkling satisfies 'upon the Ark cover' (directed toward) and 'before the Ark cover' (downward) as two distinct acts.

🔢 How Many Times Does the High Priest Count?

Rabbi Meir
The High Priest counts: 'One; one and one; one and two; one and three; one and four; one and five; one and six; one and seven.' — Always naming the upward sprinkling alongside each downward one.
Rabbi Yehuda
The High Priest counts: 'One; one and one; one and two; one and three; one and four; one and five; one and six; one and seven.' — Identical formula! Both agree on the counting itself.
Gemara
They do not disagree about the rite itself — both say one upward and seven downward. The difference is only in local custom of expression, not in the number of sprinklings performed.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
The reason the first upward sprinkling must be counted with each downward one: the verse says 'he shall sprinkle' twice — once for the upward act, once to teach it must be counted together with the downward ones, lest the High Priest err.
Bottom Line One upward + seven downward = eight total sprinklings, for both the bull and the goat.

🏆 One Pedestal or Two? — The Post-Sprinkling Blood Placement

Rabbi Meir — Two Pedestals

  • One golden pedestal for the bull's blood
  • A second golden pedestal for the goat's blood
  • Kept separate to prevent mixing
  • Writing on vessels is acceptable as a safeguard
  • The thirteen inscribed collection horns prove writing prevents confusion

Rabbi Yehuda — One Pedestal

  • Only one golden pedestal existed in the Temple
  • Blood placed there sequentially, not simultaneously
  • Concern: sin-offering whose owner has died renders it invalid
  • If birds' funds are mixed and an owner dies — the entire nest is invalidated
  • Writing alone cannot guard against this — decree applies regardless
