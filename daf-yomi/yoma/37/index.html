📜 Deriving 'Ana' (Please) in the High Priest's Confession

Question: How do we know the confession must begin with Ana (Please)?
↓
Source A — Yom Kippur (Leviticus 16:21)
Word kapparah (atonement) appears in the confession passage
Source B — Golden Calf (Exodus 32:30)
Moses says Ana before seeking kapparah for Israel
↓
Gezerah Shavah: Shared word kapparah links both passages — just as Moses used Ana, so too must the High Priest begin his Yom Kippur confession with Ana

⚖️ Abaye's Challenge: Can We Derive 'Ana' from the Heifer?

Questioner
Why not derive the requirement for 'Ana' from the heifer whose neck is broken (eglah arufah), which also involves atonement language?
Abaye
What was, was — the Golden Calf preceded the mitzva of the heifer. You cannot derive rules for an earlier event from a later one.
Abaye
However, you can still derive from the heifer that God's Name must be mentioned in the confession — that requirement comes from the heifer ceremony.
Resolution
Two separate derivations: 'Ana' from the Golden Calf passage (gezerah shavah on kapparah), and mentioning God's Name from the eglah arufah.
Key Principle A gezerah shavah can only draw from contemporaneous or later sources — historical chronology constrains legal derivation.

🏛️ The High Priest's Position at the Lottery Ceremony

Location: Eastern side of the Temple courtyard, farthest from the Holy of Holies
↓
Orientation: To the north of the altar — implying the altar itself stands in the south of the courtyard
↓
Deputy High Priest (Segan) stands to the right of the High Priest
↓
Head of the patrilineal family of the priestly watch currently on duty stands to his left
↓
Gemara's Inference: The altar is not in the north; it stands in the south — the High Priest positions himself north of it

🌟 Temple Benefactors: Their Gifts to the Divine Service

Ben Katin

  • Made 12 spigots for the basin (kiyor)
  • 12 priests could sanctify hands and feet simultaneously
  • Built a wheel-and-pulley machine to lower the basin into a pit overnight
  • Prevented water from becoming invalid by lying unused

King Munbaz

  • Donated funds for gold handles on all Yom Kippur vessels
  • Handles chosen: gold unsuitable for knife blades (too soft)
  • Also made vessel bases and grinding stones of gold (per baraita)
  • Proselyte king of Adiabene who embraced Torah observance

Queen Helene

  • Mother of King Munbaz; also a convert
  • Fashioned a golden chandelier above the Sanctuary entrance
  • Its polished surface cast sparks of light across Jerusalem at sunrise
  • Created a golden tablet inscribed with the sota (wayward wife) passage

⚖️ The Two Goat Lottery: Casting Lots on Yom Kippur

Two goats stand before the High Priest at the lottery receptacle (kalpי) east of the courtyard
↓
Aaron places lots upon the two goats (Leviticus 16:8) — but how many lots per goat?
↓
❌ Rejected Reading
Two lots per goat (four total)?
✓ Correct Reading
One lot for God, one lot for Azazel — one here, one there
↓
Material of the lots: any material is valid — Torah specifies none. In practice, historically made from box wood (eshkroah)
