Yoma 20 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Elijah, how do we know Satan has no power to prosecute on Yom Kippur?
A. God commands Satan to rest on Yom Kippur as part of the holiday laws
B. The gematria of HaSatan equals 364 — one day short of the year
C. The High Priest's prayer on Yom Kippur silences Satan
D. Satan is occupied judging the nations of the world on that day
Correct: B — Elijah teaches that the numerical value of HaSatan (השטן) is 364 — meaning Satan has power on 364 days of the year, but not on the 365th. That powerless day is Yom Kippur.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the Mishna, when does ash removal begin on Yom Kippur, and why does this differ from a regular day?
A. At dawn, because the High Priest must begin his service at sunrise
B. At the crow of the rooster, same as every day
C. From midnight, due to the large crowds requiring more preparation time
D. At the first watch of the night, to complete it before the fast begins
Correct: C — On Yom Kippur, ash removal begins from midnight — earlier than a regular day's rooster-crow — because the large crowds and the extensive Yom Kippur service require the High Priest to begin preparations well before dawn.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rav's interpretation of the term keriat hagever in the Mishna?
A. The crowing of the rooster at pre-dawn
B. The call of the man — a Temple crier who announced the morning service
C. The prayer of the High Priest beginning the service
D. The sound of the Temple gates being opened
Correct: B — Rav interprets keriat hagever as 'the call of the man' — referring to a professional Temple crier like Gevini, who would call out to the priests to arise for their morning service. This is supported by a baraita describing Gevini's role.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
King Agrippa heard Gevini the Crier's voice and sent him gifts. From how far away did he hear him?
A. One parasang
B. Two parasangs
C. Three parasangs
D. Five parasangs
Correct: C — The Gemara recounts that King Agrippa heard Gevini's voice from three parasangs away and was so impressed by the power of his call that he sent him gifts. This anecdote is cited to illustrate the exceptional reach of a Temple crier's voice.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Levi, why can't voices be heard as far during the day as at night?
A. People are more active and create competing noise during daylight hours
B. The heat of the sun causes sound waves to dissipate more quickly
C. The noise generated by the solar sphere traversing the sky drowns out earthly sounds
D. Demons interfere with sound transmission during the hours of daylight
Correct: C — Rabbi Levi teaches that the sphere of the sun generates a constant noise — like a carpenter's saw — as it traverses the sky. This cosmic sound drowns out earthly voices during the day. At night, absent solar noise, voices travel much farther, which is why the High Priest's voice carried so remarkably.
