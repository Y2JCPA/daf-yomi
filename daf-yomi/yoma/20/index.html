😈 Satan Has No Power on Yom Kippur Elijah teaches: Satan has no license to prosecute on Yom Kippur. The source? The numerical value of HaSatan (השטן) equals 364 — one day short of the 365-day year. That one silent day is Yom Kippur, on which Satan is utterly powerless to accuse Israel.

🔥 The Midnight Deadline for Burning Limbs

Background: Limbs of offerings may be burned on the altar all night long
↓
Rule from Zevachim: Limbs that fall off before midnight must be returned to the fire — they remain sacred
↓
After midnight: Fallen limbs assume the legal status of deshen (ashes) — they may not be returned
↓
Rav's derivation: Two verses — 'goes up on its firewood all night' + 'in the morning' — teach that morning (post-midnight) ends the burning period
↓
Rav Kahana objects: The mishna allows ash removal earlier or later than rooster-crow — if midnight were Torah law, how could they deviate?
↓
Rabbi Yoḥanan resolves: Midnight is learned from 'all night' implying a cutoff; Yom Kippur's midnight start is a rabbinic stringency due to large crowds

📢 What is Keriat HaGever?

Rav
Keriat hagever means the call of the man — a Temple crier like Gevini would call out to wake the priests for the morning service.
Rabbi Sheila
Keriat hagever means the call of the rooster — the natural pre-dawn crow is the time-marker the mishna intends.
Rav
I interpreted this before Rabbi Ḥiyya as 'call of the man' and he raised no objection. Why do you contradict me?
Rabbi Sheila
[Recognizing Rav's stature as a student of Rav's teachers] Both positions have baraita support — the dispute stands.
Outcome A baraita is taught in accordance with Rav (citing Gevini the Temple Crier) and a baraita in accordance with Rabbi Sheila (citing the rooster). Both traditions preserved.

📣 Gevini the Crier vs. The High Priest's Voice

Gevini the Temple Crier

  • Voice heard 3 parasangs away
  • King Agrippa sent gifts upon hearing him
  • Called: 'Arise, priests, to your service!'
  • Full strength — no fasting, no extra duties
  • Supports Rav's reading of keriat hagever

High Priest on Yom Kippur

  • Voice heard even farther than Gevini
  • Weakened by the Yom Kippur fast
  • Must perform the entire Temple service alone
  • Yet his voice surpassed Gevini's reach
  • Testifies to the spiritual power of the day
