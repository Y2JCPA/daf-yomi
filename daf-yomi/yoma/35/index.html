👘 How Much Were the High Priest's Yom Kippur Vestments Worth?

Rabbis (Tanna Kamma)
Morning linen garments: 18 maneh. Afternoon linen garments: 12 maneh. Total: 30 maneh.
Rabbi Meir
Morning linen garments: 16 maneh. Afternoon linen garments: 12 maneh. Total: 28 maneh — not thirty!
Rav Huna b. Rav Ilai
All agree: morning garments must be superior to afternoon garments. It is written "he shall leave them there" — the afternoon garments are set aside for a lesser purpose.
Gemara
The Ezekiel verse about "other garments" — does 'other' mean superior or inferior? Answer: inferior. The afternoon garments are of lesser sanctity than the morning ones.
✅ Unanimous Agreement Both Rabbi Meir and the Rabbis hold that the morning linen vestments — worn during the most sacred inner service — must exceed the afternoon vestments in value.

⚖️ Three Archetypes — No Excuse Stands

😔 The Poor

  • "I was impoverished — no time to study"
  • Rebuttal: Poorer than Hillel the Elder?
  • Hillel earned only ½ dinar per day
  • Gave ¼ dinar just to enter the study hall
  • Left ¼ dinar for himself and his family
  • Climbed rooftop in Tevet snowfall to listen
  • Found frozen under 3 cubits of snow at dawn
  • Verdict: ❌ Poverty is no excuse

💰 The Wealthy

  • "I managed vast estates and affairs"
  • Rebuttal: Wealthier than Elazar ben Ḥarsum?
  • Owned 1,000 villages and 1,000 ships
  • His mother made him a tunic of 20,000 dinars
  • Tunic so fine it was transparent — unusable
  • Traveled disguised; pressed into his own labor
  • Yet devoted himself entirely to Torah study
  • Verdict: ❌ Wealth is no excuse

😈 The Wicked

  • "I was handsome — evil inclination consumed me"
  • Rebuttal: More handsome than Joseph?
  • Potiphar's wife pursued Joseph relentlessly
  • Threatened prison, blindness, forced subjugation
  • Joseph countered each threat with Psalms
  • Refused at enormous personal cost
  • Preserved his spiritual reward forever
  • Verdict: ❌ Temptation is no excuse

🌨️ Hillel's Legendary Friday in Tevet

Every day: Hillel earns ½ dinar
¼ to guard of study hall · ¼ for family sustenance
↓
One Shabbat eve in Tevet (winter):
Could find no work — earned nothing
↓
Guard refuses entry without payment
Hillel climbs to the roof and lies over the skylight
to hear Shemaya and Avtalyon teach
↓
Snow falls through the night
Hillel is buried under three cubits of snow
↓
At dawn, Shemaya notices darkness in the hall
They find Hillel — barely alive in the shape of a man
↓
Sages declare: "This man deserves to have Shabbat desecrated for him"
They bathe him, anoint him, and warm him by the fire

🏺 Joseph Resists Potiphar's Wife — Blow by Blow

She commands: "Submit to me — lie with me."
↓
Joseph: "No."
↓
She threatens: "I will incarcerate you in prison."
Joseph: "God releases prisoners" — Psalms 146:7
↓
She threatens: "I will blind your eyes."
Joseph: "God restores sight to the blind" — Psalms 146:8
↓
She threatens: "I will bend you low."
Joseph: "God lifts up those who are bowed" — Psalms 146:8
↓
The Gemara's teaching: Had Joseph submitted, it would have been decreed that he be with her in the World-to-Come.
↓
Because he refused — Joseph preserves his eternal reward.
Hillel obligates the poor · Elazar obligates the wealthy · Joseph obligates the wicked

📜 The High Priest's First Confession

אָנָּא הַשֵּׁם, חָטָאתִי, עָוִיתִי, פָּשַׁעְתִּי לְפָנֶיךָ — אֲנִי וּבֵיתִי
MISHNA — Vidui over the Bull The High Priest comes and stands next to his bull — positioned between the Entrance Hall and the altar, head to the south, face to the west. The priest stands to the east, facing west, hands placed on the bull's head, and recites the confession:

"Please, God — I have sinned, I have done wrong, and I have rebelled before You: I and my family. Please, God, grant atonement for the sins, wrongs, and rebellions that I have sinned, done wrong, and rebelled before You — I and my family. As it is written in the Torah of Moses Your servant: ‛For on this day atonement shall be made for you...'"
