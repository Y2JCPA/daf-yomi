Yoma 35 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Yosef, how did the Chamber of Parva get its name?
A. It was built by a Persian king named Parva
B. Parva means 'fine linen' in Aramaic
C. It was named after a Persian sorcerer due to a particular incident
D. It was the name of the High Priest who first used the chamber
Correct: C — Rav Yosef explains that Parva was the name of a Persian sorcerer whose name became associated with this chamber due to a particular incident connected to him.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
On what point do both Rabbi Meir and the Rabbis agree regarding the High Priest's Yom Kippur vestments?
A. The total value of both sets is exactly 30 maneh
B. The afternoon vestments are made of superior linen
C. The morning vestments must be of greater value than the afternoon vestments
D. Both sets must be purchased new each year
Correct: C — Although Rabbi Meir and the Rabbis dispute the exact total (28 vs. 30 maneh), all agree that the morning vestments — worn during the holier inner service — must exceed the afternoon vestments in value.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Hillel's fellow priests refuse to let Rabbi Elazar ben Ḥarsum wear the tunic his mother made?
A. It was not woven from the required linen fabric
B. It was too expensive and considered arrogant
C. It was so fine and transparent he appeared naked
D. It had not been dedicated to the Temple treasury
Correct: C — The tunic, worth 20,000 dinars, was so sheer and transparent that wearing it was like wearing nothing — his priests refused on grounds of modesty and propriety.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How did Hillel manage to study Torah on the day he could find no work and had no money to pay the guard?
A. He convinced the guard to accept a promissory note
B. He climbed onto the roof and listened through the skylight
C. He asked a friend to lend him the entrance fee
D. He studied at home alone from memory
Correct: B — Hillel climbed to the roof and lay over the skylight to hear Shemaya and Avtalyon. He was found at dawn covered under three cubits of snow — and the Sages permitted violating Shabbat to save him.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does the Gemara say would have happened to Joseph in the World-to-Come had he submitted to Potiphar's wife?
A. He would have forfeited his share in the World-to-Come entirely
B. He would have been destined to be with her in the World-to-Come
C. He would have been reincarnated as a punishment
D. Nothing — earthly sins do not affect the World-to-Come
Correct: B — The Gemara teaches that had Joseph submitted, it would have been decreed in Heaven that he be with Potiphar's wife in the World-to-Come. His refusal preserved his eternal reward and made him the exemplar against whom the 'wicked' are judged.
