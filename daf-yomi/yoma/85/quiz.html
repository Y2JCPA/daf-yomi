Yoma 85 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, whose proof for violating Shabbat to save a life is completely unrefutable?
A. Rabbi Yishmael's proof from the thief breaking in
B. Rabbi Akiva's proof from removing a priest from the altar
C. Rabbi Shimon ben Menasya's 'many Shabbatot' argument
D. Shmuel's proof from 'V'chai bahem'
Correct: D — Rava explicitly states that all other proofs can be refuted because they involve certain situations that may not apply to cases of uncertainty. Only Shmuel's derivation from 'V'chai bahem — live by them, not die by them' (Leviticus 18:5) is universal and unassailable.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
When a foundling is found in a city that is exactly half Jewish and half gentile, what is his legal status?
A. He is treated as a gentile due to equal doubt
B. He is treated as a Jew — we give him the benefit of the doubt
C. He has no legal status until his lineage is proven
D. He must wait until adulthood to determine status
Correct: B — The baraita rules that in a 50/50 city, the foundling is treated as Jewish. Reish Lakish explains this applies to damages law — if his innocuous ox gores, he pays like a Jew (half-damages).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When rescuers uncover debris from a trapped person's feet upward, according to Rav Pappa, where must they check for signs of life?
A. At the feet — warmth indicates life
B. At the heart — a heartbeat suffices
C. They may stop at the heart since it was exposed first
D. They must continue all the way to the nose to check for breathing
Correct: D — Rav Pappa clarifies that even when digging from below (feet first), one must continue uncovering until reaching the nose to check for breath — the heart is insufficient. The nose is the definitive location for confirming life.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Elazar ben Azarya, what is the fundamental limitation of Yom Kippur's power of atonement?
A. Yom Kippur requires a sacrifice in addition to repentance
B. Yom Kippur only atones for sins between a person and God, not between people
C. Yom Kippur atones for all sins automatically, even without repentance
D. Yom Kippur cannot atone for intentional transgressions
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar ben Azarya derives from 'From all your sins before God you shall be cleansed' (Leviticus 16:30) that Yom Kippur atones only for sins 'before God' — i.e., between a person and the Almighty. Sins between people require first appeasing the wronged party.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The mishna states that repentance atones for minor transgressions. What does the Gemara find surprising about this ruling?
A. Repentance should require a sin-offering even for minor sins
B. If repentance atones for a negative mitzva (more severe), why mention a positive mitzva (less severe)?
C. Repentance alone should be insufficient without Yom Kippur
D. Minor transgressions should not require repentance at all
Correct: B — The Gemara asks: once we know repentance atones for a negative mitzva — which is more severe — it is obvious that it atones for a positive mitzva (less severe). Why does the mishna need to state the lesser case after establishing the greater?
