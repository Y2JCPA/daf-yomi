🚢 Two Versions: Rav Dimi vs. Ravin (Rabbi Yoḥanan's Ruling)

Rav Dimi
Rabbi Yoḥanan said: One who slaughters a Paschal lamb outside the Temple during the rest of the year — whether for its own sake or not for its own sake — is exempt.
Ravin
Rabbi Yoḥanan said: One who slaughters a Paschal lamb outside the Temple during the rest of the year — whether for its own sake or not for its own sake — is liable.
Gemara
Ravin's version is challenged: How can one be liable for slaughtering it for its own sake? The Paschal lamb is not fit to be offered at that time — the Mishna in Zevahim exempts anyone who slaughters an offering lacking time!
Rabbi Yirmeya
Uprooting an offering's designation outside the Temple is not called uprooting. Since it was slaughtered outside, it never becomes a peace-offering — so even 'not for its own sake' carries no liability for some.
Rav Ashi
Final ruling: One who slaughters a Paschal lamb outside during the rest of the year not for its own sake is liable. Rav Yirmeya of Difti disagrees and says exempt — but the law follows Rav Ashi.
Core Question Can slaughtering outside the Temple 'uproot' a Paschal lamb's designation and convert it into a peace-offering — triggering liability?

⏰ Lacking Time by the Owner — Who Is Exempt Outside?

Always Exempt (Lacking Time)

  • 🧍 Zav — must count 7 clean days first
  • 🧍‍♀️ Zava — must count 7 clean days first
  • 👶 Woman after childbirth — before purification complete
  • 🩹 Leper — before sprinkling on 7th/8th day
  • Their offerings are not yet fit even inside the Temple

Exception: Leper's Guilt-Offering

  • Slaughtered for its own sake: Exempt
  • Slaughtered not for its own sake: Liable
  • A guilt-offering not slaughtered for its sake inside the Temple is still valid — so it is fit
  • Ruling: Rav Ḥilkiya bar Tovi

The Guiding Principle (הוֹאִיל)

  • Liability requires the offering to be fit for inside service
  • If it cannot fulfill its purpose inside right now → Exempt
  • הוֹאִיל ('since it could come to be fit') can sometimes create liability even now
  • Rav Ḥisda applies הוֹאִיל to the Yom Kippur goats — they could become additional offerings

🔄 Does Slaughtering Outside 'Uproot' an Offering's Designation?

Background: A Paschal lamb slaughtered for its own sake outside on non-Passover days is exempt — it is not fit as a Paschal offering at that time.
↓
The question: What if one slaughters it not for its own sake — intending it as a peace-offering? Can the designation be uprooted outside the Temple?
↓
Rabbi Yirmeya's principle: 'Uprooting an offering's designation outside the Temple is not called uprooting.' The Paschal lamb never becomes a peace-offering through outside slaughter — so there is no liability.
↓
The counter: A peace-offering requires explicit designation; without it, the default remains the Paschal status. If explicitly slaughtered not for its sake, it requires uprooting — and perhaps outside slaughter achieves that.
↓
Final ruling — Rav Ashi: One who slaughters a Paschal lamb outside during the rest of the year not for its own sake is liable. Outside slaughter does uproot the Paschal designation sufficiently to create liability.
