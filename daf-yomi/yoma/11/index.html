🏛️ The Gate of Nicanor Exception

שַׁעַר נִיקָנוֹר — לִשְׁכַּת פַּרְהֶדְרִין
Temple Gates & Mezuza All eastern Temple gates were exempt from mezuza — except the Gate of Nicanor. Why? The Chamber of Parhedrin — where the High Priest resided during his Yom Kippur preparation week — was located just inside that gate. The residential character of the chamber created a genuine mezuza obligation, pulling the gate into the requirement. All other Temple gates lacked any adjacent residence and remained exempt.

🌆 Why No Mezuza on Meḥoza's City Gates?

Abaye
The city of Meḥoza has a Jewish majority — its city gates should require a mezuza! Why do the Sages not affix one to the gates there?
Rav Safra
Simple — the gates of Meḥoza are made of metal, and metal vessels are not obligated in the mitzva of mezuza.
Abaye
That reasoning is incorrect! Metal doorposts can still obligate mezuza. The real reason is danger — gentiles also live there and would suspect Jews of using the mezuza for witchcraft or sorcery.
Rabbi Yehuda (tradition)
This danger is real. In Tzippori, an examiner of mezuzot was caught by a Roman official during a period of decrees and nearly executed on the spot. Publicly visible mezuzot invite persecution.
Ruling City gates with a Jewish majority are halakhically obligated in mezuza — but danger from gentile suspicion can override the obligation in mixed cities.

🐄 Structures: Obligated vs. Exempt from Mezuza

✅ Obligated in Mezuza

  • Residential homes
  • City gates with Jewish majority
  • Gate of Nicanor (Temple) — due to Parhedrin chamber
  • Synagogues
  • A woman's private house
  • House jointly owned by partners

❌ Exempt from Mezuza

  • Storehouse for hay
  • Cattle barn
  • Woodshed / general storehouse
  • Bathhouse
  • Tannery (burseki)
  • Ritual bath (mikveh) building
  • Temple Mount, chambers, courtyards
  • Gatehouse, portico, open porch, corridor balcony

🏠 How the Gemara Derives Mezuza Exemptions from 'Your House'

Base verse: "Write them on the doorposts of your houses" (Deut. 6:9) — the source of all mezuza obligation
↓
Potential inclusion: Gatehouse, portico, open porch, balcony corridor — people pass through these regularly
↓
Exclusion #1 — Residence: "House" = designated for dwelling. Passages and transit structures are excluded. (Rav Kahana)
↓
Potential inclusion: Bathroom, tannery, bathhouse, mikveh building — enclosed, permanent structures with doorposts
↓
Exclusion #2 — Dignity: "House" = a place of honor where one enters respectfully. Shameful or malodorous places are excluded. (Rav Yehuda)
↓
Potential inclusion: Temple Mount, chambers, and courtyards — grand structures with doorposts
↓
Exclusion #3 — Non-sacred: "House" = a non-sacred space. Sacred Temple spaces have their own laws and are excluded from mezuza.
