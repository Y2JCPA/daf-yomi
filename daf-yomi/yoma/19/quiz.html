Yoma 19 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
In which Temple chamber did priests with physical blemishes perform a useful task?
A. Chamber of Salt
B. Chamber of the Exile
C. Chamber of Wood
D. Chamber of Hewn Stone
Correct: C — The Chamber of Wood is where blemished priests, who could not perform full Temple service, would inspect firewood to ensure it was free of worms and fit for the altar — contributing despite their limitation.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did the Elders insist the High Priest take an oath regarding the incense service?
A. To confirm he had memorized the procedure correctly
B. To prevent him from lighting the incense outside before entering the Holy of Holies
C. To verify he was ritually pure before beginning
D. To confirm his genealogy as a valid kohen gadol
Correct: B — The Sadducees believed incense should be lit before entering the Holy of Holies. The Elders required the oath to ensure the High Priest would follow the Pharisaic tradition of preparing the incense inside.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What dual purpose did the Gemara identify for imposing hardship on the High Priest?
A. To strengthen his body and sharpen his focus
B. To test his knowledge of halacha and Temple law
C. To expose insincere Sadducees and prevent haughtiness in the righteous
D. To fulfill the requirement of suffering before Yom Kippur atonement
Correct: C — The Gemara argues: (1) A Sadducee who lacks true piety will resign rather than endure the difficulty; (2) Even a righteous High Priest benefits — being occupied with toil prevents pride in his elevated position.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the 'tzerada' finger, and how was it used?
A. The index finger, pointed at the High Priest to command attention
B. The pinky finger, tapped on his shoulder to wake him gently
C. The middle finger, snapped loudly against the thumb to startle him awake
D. The ring finger, used to gesture for him to rise and walk
Correct: C — Rav Yehuda explains: 'tzerada' means the rival (tzara) of 'that one' (da = the thumb). The strong middle finger was snapped against the thumb, producing a sharp sound to jolt the High Priest from drowsiness.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did prominent men of Jerusalem stay awake all night during the High Priest's vigil?
A. It was a required communal fast and vigil for all Jewish men
B. They were reciting the entire book of Psalms on his behalf
C. The noise of their Torah study in the city would prevent the High Priest from falling asleep
D. They were standing guard at the Temple gates against intruders
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that prominent Jerusalemites stayed awake learning Torah so the city would be filled with noise and activity. This ambient sound in the night prevented the High Priest from succumbing to sleep in the quiet.
