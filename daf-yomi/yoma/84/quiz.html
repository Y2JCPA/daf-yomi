Yoma 84 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the baraita cited in the Gemara, how should one kill a mad dog?
A. Strike it directly with a heavy staff
B. Kill it only with a thrown object
C. Feed it poisoned food from a distance
D. Have a gentile dispatch it to avoid Jewish involvement
Correct: B — Consistent with Shmuel's view, the baraita rules the dog must be killed only with a thrown object — direct contact with a mad dog is dangerous, as even being rubbed by one can cause serious illness.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What did Shmuel rule about following the majority in life-saving situations?
A. One always follows the majority, even for pikuach nefesh
B. One does not follow the majority when a life may be at stake
C. One follows the majority only when gentiles outnumber Jews 9 to 1
D. The majority rule applies only in a different courtyard, not the same one
Correct: B — Shmuel taught that the Sages did not apply the normal principle of following the majority in pikuach nefesh cases. We excavate a collapsed building even when the majority of those present were gentile.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does the Gemara teach about violating Shabbat for ongoing multi-week medical treatment?
A. Only the first Shabbat may be violated; a gentile handles subsequent ones
B. A court must grant permission for each additional Shabbat
C. All required future Shabbatot may be violated to complete the treatment
D. One stops treatment on Shabbat and resumes on weekdays only
Correct: C — The baraita explicitly states that one violates not only the current Shabbat but all future Shabbatot needed for treatment. The principle: 'violate one Shabbat so he may keep many Shabbatot.'
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Gemara, who should ideally perform Shabbat-violating acts for a dangerously ill person?
A. A gentile or Samaritan, to minimize the Jewish violation
B. Any available bystander who acts quickly
C. The patient themselves whenever physically possible
D. The greatest Jewish scholars — specifically not gentiles or Samaritans
Correct: D — The Gemara insists these acts be performed by the greatest of Israel's scholars, not gentiles or Samaritans. This demonstrates that saving life is a Torah imperative, not a reluctant concession — fulfilling it publicly teaches that message.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the diagnostic sign of the disease tzefidna, according to the Gemara?
A. Swelling of the throat that prevents swallowing
B. A visible rash appearing shortly after eating hot food
C. Blood coming from the gums when something is placed between the teeth
D. Yellowing of the skin and whites of the eyes
Correct: C — The Gemara states the sign of tzefidna is blood emerging from the gums when one places something between the teeth. The disease originates from eating hot wheat bread together with fried fish remains.
