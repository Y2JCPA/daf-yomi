📖 Overview

פִּקּוּחַ נֶפֶשׁ דּוֹחֶה שַׁבָּת
⚖️ Core Principle of Daf 84 This daf explores the paramount value of pikuach nefesh (saving a life), which overrides Shabbat and Yom Kippur. Spanning medical remedies for dangerous conditions — mad dog bites, throat disease, tzefidna — to dramatic rescue scenarios, the Gemara teaches that one who is vigilant in saving a life is praiseworthy and need not wait for rabbinic permission. Even multiple future Shabbatot may be violated for a single illness requiring ongoing treatment.

🐕 Dangerous Conditions and Their Remedies

Mad Dog Bite (כלב שוטה)

  • Kill the dog only with a thrown object — avoid direct contact
  • Being rubbed causes illness; being bitten causes death
  • Abaye's remedy: Write a spell on hyena skin
  • Spell: 'I, [name], son of [name], write against you — kanti kanti kloros…'
  • Then remove and wear one's own garment inside-out

Tzefidna (צְפִידְנָא — Gum Disease)

  • Cause: Hot wheat bread eaten with fried fish remains
  • Sign: Blood from gums when touching between the teeth
  • Rav Aḥa: water with steeped leaven, olive oil, and salt
  • Rav Yeimar: leaven itself, olive oil, and salt
  • Rav Ashi: white of a rotten egg with olive oil and salt

Throat/Mouth Pain (כְּאֵב גָּרוֹן)

  • Rabbi Matya ben Ḥarash: place medicine in mouth on Shabbat
  • Potentially life-threatening — permitted without delay
  • Cleopatra revealed remedy under oath 'not to tell the God of the Jews'
  • Loophole: 'I will reveal it to His people'
  • Rabbi Yoḥanan demonstrated the remedy on himself before Rav Ḥiyya

💬 Is Bloodletting for Serunkhi Permitted on Shabbat?

Rav Ḥiyya bar Abba
In whose opinion did you act, Rabbi Yoḥanan? Was it not Rabbi Matya ben Ḥarash, who permits placing medicine in the mouth on Shabbat for throat pain?
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Indeed — this confirms the ruling. The throat is life-threatening territory, and placing medicine there is permitted on Shabbat even without awaiting a court decision.
Baraita
One seized with yerakon is fed donkey meat; one bitten by a mad dog is fed the lobe of its liver; one with mouth pain — medicine is placed there on Shabbat.
Rav Ashi (objection)
The Rabbis say 'these have no remedy' — but what do they exclude? Not bloodletting for serunkhi! They exclude only donkey meat and dog liver as valueless remedies. Bloodletting IS permitted.
Resolution The Rabbis' phrase 'these have no remedy' targets donkey meat and liver lobe as medically useless, while confirming that bloodletting for serunkhi is a genuine, Shabbat-permitted treatment — consistent with the baraita of Rabbi Yannai.

🏥 Pikuach Nefesh — When and How to Act on Shabbat

Life-threatening situation arises on Shabbat or Yom Kippur
↓
Act immediately
Do not wait for a court's permission — one who is vigilant is praiseworthy
Who should act?
The greatest Jewish scholars — not gentiles or Samaritans — to affirm Torah's value of life
How many Shabbatot?
Violate all future Shabbatot needed for recovery, not only the current one
↓
Child fell into the sea? — Cast a net even if it also catches fish (a normally-forbidden labor)
↓
Door locked on a frightened child? — Break it open; need not amuse him from outside and wait
↓
Fire spreading toward people? — Place a barrier to extinguish it; a new pikuach nefesh principle beyond the previous cases

⚕️ Violating Multiple Shabbatot for One Illness

Doctor assesses: Patient needs treatment beginning today (Shabbat) for 8 days
↓
We violate this Shabbat — uncontroversially permitted as clear pikuach nefesh
↓
Question: What about the 7 subsequent Shabbatot the treatment requires?
↓
The baraita answers: violate all of them — 'they did not say only the current Shabbat but even future Shabbatot'
↓
Reason: Interrupting treatment mid-course may be more dangerous than the illness itself
↓
Underlying principle: חַלֵּל עָלָיו שַׁבָּת אַחַת כְּדֵי שֶׁיִּשְׁמֹר שַׁבָּתוֹת הַרְבֵּה
Violate one Shabbat so that he may observe many Shabbatot
