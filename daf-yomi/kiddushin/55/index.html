The Mishna's Rule: Stray Animals Near Jerusalem

זכרים — עולות, נקבות — שלמים
Mishna (Shekalim 20a) An animal found straying from Jerusalem to Migdal Eder is presumed to be a korban.

Males → presumed burnt-offerings (עולות)
Females → presumed peace-offerings (שלמים)

Since most animals in Jerusalem were designated for offerings, we follow the majority.

How to Redeem the Found Animal

Gemara
Males could also be peace-offerings — why assume only burnt-offerings?
R' Oshaya
The finder consecrates the animal's value to cover all possibilities — money goes to both burnt-offerings and peace-offerings.
Gemara
This follows R' Meir: intentional misuse desacralizes even items with inherent sanctity.
R' Yochanan
You can't tell someone to sin in order to gain! Instead, wait for the animal to develop a blemish, then redeem it.
R' Yochanan
Bring two substitute animals with stipulations: one as burnt-offering, one as peace-offering — whichever applies, the other is voluntary.
Principle R' Yochanan rejects causing intentional misuse — even for a permitted goal, one may not be told to sin.

R' Meir vs. R' Yehuda: When Does Misuse Desacralize?

R' Meir

  • Intentional misuse (מזיד) → desacralizes
  • Unwitting misuse (שוגג) → does NOT desacralize
  • Applies to BOTH most sacred and lesser sanctity offerings
  • Intent to withdraw to non-sacred state is key
  • Basis for R' Oshaya's redemption method

R' Yehuda

  • Unwitting misuse (שוגג) → desacralizes
  • Intentional misuse (מזיד) → does NOT desacralize
  • Items with inherent sanctity are NEVER desacralized by misuse
  • Riding a consecrated animal repeatedly — all commit me'ila
  • Drinking from a golden Temple cup — all commit me'ila
Resolution R' Meir: one who acts intentionally intends to withdraw the item from sanctity → even inherent sanctity is removed. R' Yehuda: one who acts unwittingly merely uses the item, with no intent to withdraw it.

What Could a Found Male Animal Be?

PossibilityResolution
Burnt-offering (עולה)Primary assumption — bring substitute with stipulation
Peace-offering (שלמים)Also possible — bring second substitute with stipulation
Thanks-offering (תודה)Bring a third animal + bread (לחם) with stipulation
Guilt-offering (אשם)Ruled out — guilt-offerings are second-year animals; found animal is first-year
Leper's / Nazirite's guilt-offeringRuled out — uncommon, no need for concern
Paschal offering (פסח)If in time → owners guard it carefully; if past time → treated as peace-offering
Firstborn (בכור) / Animal tithe (מעשר)Irrelevant — these too are eaten only when blemished, same rule already applies
Method The Gemara systematically eliminates each possibility to justify the mishna's simple presumption of burnt-offering vs. peace-offering.

R' Yochanan's Solution: Redeeming the Found Animal

Find stray animal near Jerusalem
↓
Wait until the animal develops a blemish (מום)
↓
Animal now has only value sanctity — may be redeemed
↓
Bring two unblemished animals of equal value
↓
Stipulation A
If found animal was a burnt-offering → Animal #1 is its replacement; #2 is voluntary peace-offering
Stipulation B
If found animal was a peace-offering → Animal #2 is its replacement; #1 is voluntary burnt-offering
↓
Finder may now eat the found animal
Key By using conditional stipulations (תנאי), the finder covers all possibilities without sinning.
