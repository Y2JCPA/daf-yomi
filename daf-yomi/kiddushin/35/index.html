How Do We Know Which Mitzvot Apply to Women?

Problem: How to derive the general rule that women are exempt from mitzvot aseh she'hazman grama?
↓
Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov: Torah is juxtaposed to tefillin (Exodus 13:9) — women are exempt from tefillin → exempt from all positive time-bound mitzvot
↓
By inference: Women are obligated in every positive mitzva that is not time-bound
↓
Separately: Women are equated to men for punishments, judgments, and capital cases — but exempt from three specific prohibitions
Key Concept The Gemara builds a comprehensive framework for which commandments apply to women, using hermeneutical principles like juxtaposition, verbal analogy, and shnei ketuvim haba'im k'echad.

R' Yoḥanan ben Beroka's Challenge

Gemara
According to R' Yoḥanan ben Beroka, women are obligated in procreation. Then women are exempt from only one non-time-bound mitzva — Torah study. Why can't we derive from that single case?
Gemara
Torah study and redemption of the firstborn are shnei ketuvim haba'im k'echad — two verses that come as one — and they do not teach a precedent.
Gemara
But then let procreation and fear of parents — both obligating women — also be two verses as one and not teach a precedent!
Gemara
Both are necessary: without procreation stated, we'd say women don't 'conquer'; without fear of parents, we'd say a married woman lacks the power to fulfill it.
Principle Shnei ketuvim haba'im k'echad ein melamdim — two verses teaching the same point cannot establish a general rule, because if it were general, one verse would suffice.

The Tefillin Derivation (Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov)

Exodus 13:9 — "That the Torah of the Lord may be in your mouth"
Entire Torah is juxtaposed to tefillin
↓
Is tefillin a positive time-bound mitzva?
Yes (majority view)
Women are exempt from tefillin → exempt from all positive time-bound mitzvot → and by inference, obligated in non-time-bound ones
No (R' Meir)
R' Meir holds tefillin is not time-bound, but he also holds shnei ketuvim don't teach — so matza & assembly can't create a general obligation anyway
Who Are the 'Sages of Pafunya'? Rava identifies the scholar behind this derivation as Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov, nicknamed 'the Sages of Pafunya' for his brilliance.

R' Yehuda's Problem: Three Verses as One

ScholarTefillin Time-Bound?Two Verses Teach?Resolution
MajorityYes ✓NoTefillin derivation works directly
R' MeirNo ✗NoMatza & assembly can't create a rule anyway
R' YehudaNo ✗YesMatza, rejoicing & assembly = three verses as one — all agree three don't teach
Resolution For R' Yehuda, adding simḥa (rejoicing) to matza and hakhel creates three verses — and universally, three verses that come as one do not teach a precedent.

Equating Women to Men: Three Sources

Punishments (Rav / R' Yishmael)

  • Numbers 5:6 — "When a man or woman shall commit any sin"
  • Women are equated for all punishments
  • Including obligation to bring offerings

Judgments (R' Eliezer's School)

  • Exodus 21:1 — "Set before them" (plural)
  • Women equated for monetary cases
  • Includes damages and civil law

Capital Cases (Ḥizkiyya's School)

  • Exodus 21:29 — "Killed a man or woman"
  • Owner pays ransom for either victim
  • Same death penalties apply
Why All Three? Each source is necessary: from punishments alone we might exclude judgments; from ransom alone we might say it's only because of loss of life; from judgments alone we might exclude capital liability.

Women's Three Exemptions from Prohibitions

✂️ Do not round the head — derived from juxtaposition with beard prohibition
↓
🪒 Do not destroy the beard — "your beard" (singular) = yours, not your wife's
↓
⚱️ Do not contract corpse impurity — "sons of Aaron," not daughters of Aaron
↓
🔗 Rounding is linked to destroying: whoever has no beard prohibition has no rounding prohibition
↓
📝 Logical reason for beard exemption: women ordinarily do not have beards
Dual Derivation The beard exemption is derived both logically (women don't have beards) and textually (singular zekanekha vs. plural roshekhem).
