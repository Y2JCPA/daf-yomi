Kiddushin 35 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why can't we derive a general rule from Torah study and firstborn redemption (both exempting women)?
A. They are two verses that come as one and don't teach a precedent
B. They are rabbinic rather than biblical mitzvot
C. They apply only to Levites
D. Women are actually obligated in both
Correct: A — Two verses teaching the same lesson (shnei ketuvim haba'im k'echad) cannot establish a broader rule — if the principle were general, one verse would suffice.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov, how do we derive that women are exempt from positive time-bound mitzvot?
A. From the verse about Shabbat observance
B. From the juxtaposition of the entire Torah to tefillin in Exodus 13:9
C. From the principle of kal va'chomer from Pesach sacrifice
D. From an explicit list in Deuteronomy
Correct: B — Exodus 13:9 juxtaposes the entire Torah to tefillin. Since tefillin is a positive time-bound mitzva from which women are exempt, women are exempt from all such mitzvot.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why are women exempt from the prohibition of 'do not round the corners of your head'?
A. It is a positive time-bound mitzva
B. Women are linked to it through rounding being juxtaposed with destroying the beard, from which women are exempt
C. The verse explicitly excludes women
D. It is only a rabbinic prohibition
Correct: B — The prohibitions of rounding and destroying are juxtaposed: anyone not included in the beard prohibition is also not included in the rounding prohibition. Since women are exempt from destroying their beard, they are also exempt from rounding.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does R' Yehuda's position (two verses DO teach a precedent) get resolved regarding women's exemption from time-bound mitzvot?
A. He simply disagrees with the exemption
B. Matza, rejoicing, and assembly form THREE verses as one — all agree three don't teach
C. He derives it from a different verse entirely
D. He holds tefillin is time-bound, so the standard derivation works
Correct: B — By adding simḥa (rejoicing) to matza and hakhel, there are three verses obligating women in time-bound mitzvot. Everyone agrees that three verses coming as one do not teach a broader precedent.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What role does the gezera shava of 'corners of the beard' play in the Gemara's argument?
A. It proves women must wear tefillin
B. It connects the priestly beard prohibition to the general one, confirming women's exemption applies to all Jews
C. It establishes that all Jews must follow priestly purity laws
D. It teaches that eunuchs are obligated in beard prohibitions
Correct: B — The verbal analogy links 'corners of the beard' in Leviticus 19:27 (all Jews) with Leviticus 21:5 (priests). Since women are excluded from the priestly context ('sons of Aaron'), the gezera shava extends that exclusion to the general prohibition as well.
