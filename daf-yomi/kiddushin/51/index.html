Can Simultaneous Acts Succeed Where Sequential Ones Cannot?

Rabba
Any matters that cannot be accomplished sequentially also cannot be accomplished simultaneously
Rava
Even if they cannot be done sequentially, they can take effect simultaneously
Gemara
Tests Rabba's principle against tithes, animal tithe, thanks-offering loaves, and betrothal of relatives
Core Issue This daf systematically challenges Rabba's principle through multiple domains — agricultural tithes, animal consecration, sacrificial loaves, and marriage law — then pivots to the Abaye-Rava dispute on kiddushin she'ein mesura l'bi'ah.

↓
Remaining Produce
✅ Rendered fit for consumption — he did tithe properly
The Two-Tenths
❌ Ruined — unclear which tenth is actual tithe and which is not
Challenge to Rabba If what can't be done sequentially can't be done simultaneously, the produce should remain entirely untithed — yet it doesn't!

Rabba's Defense: Tithe Takes Effect Partially

Objection
According to Rabba, one cannot separate two-tenths sequentially, so why does the remainder become fit?
Rabba
Tithe is different — tithe takes effect partially. If one says 'let half of each grain be tithe,' it works!
Rabba
So here, half of each grain in the designated portion is tithe, the other half is not — the remainder is properly tithed
Objection
But animal tithe cannot take effect partially — you can't consecrate half an animal!
Key Concept Ma'aser chal al chatzyim — Tithe status can attach to a fraction of each individual unit, unlike animal consecration.

Thanks-Offering: 80 Loaves Instead of 40

Hizkiyya

  • 40 of the 80 loaves are consecrated
  • Owner intended to consecrate only 40
  • Extra 40 are insurance against loss/impurity
  • R' Yehoshua b. Levi: all agree if he said 'let 40 be holy' — 40 are holy

R' Yochanan

  • None of the 80 loaves are consecrated
  • Owner intended to bring a large offering of 80
  • Since only 40 can be consecrated, the condition fails
  • R' Yehoshua b. Levi: all agree if he said 'only if all 80 holy' — none are holy
Resolution Per R' Yehoshua ben Levi, the dispute is about interpreting the owner's unspecified intent — not about whether simultaneous consecration works. This accords with Rabba.

Betrothal Not Given to Consummation (Amud B)

Abaye
If he says 'one of you is betrothed' to a woman and her sister — each requires a get out of uncertainty
Rava
Neither is betrothed — this is kiddushin she'ein mesura l'bi'ah (betrothal not given to consummation)
Abaye
If he said 'the one fit for intercourse' — neither is betrothed, since he can't know which one to be with
Rava
Saying 'one of you' to sisters already = simultaneous betrothal of relatives = automatically void
The Five Women A man gathered a basket of figs and said to five women (including two sisters): whoever is fit for intercourse is betrothed. The Sages ruled: the sisters are not betrothed. Both Abaye and Rava read this incident to support their view.
