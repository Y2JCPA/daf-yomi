Can a Master Designate for His Minor Son?

Reish Lakish
I raise a dilemma: Can a master designate a Hebrew maidservant for his minor son?
Reish Lakish
The verse says 'if he designates her for his son' — does 'his son' mean any son, even a minor?
R' Zeira
The verse excludes the wife of a minor from adultery laws — implying a minor has no valid marriage!
Gemara
But if a minor can never be married, why does the verse need to exclude him? Perhaps it proves designation can work for a minor!
Rav Ashi
The verse excludes a yavam aged 9+ who had intercourse with his yevama — his intercourse is valid, yet she's still not fully married.
Resolution The dilemma about a minor son is resolved by R' Yannai: Designation applies only to an adult man, because it requires consent.

Two Requirements for Valid Designation

Adult Man Only

  • Designation cannot be performed for a minor son
  • The son must be capable of marriage
  • Derived from: a minor cannot give legal consent

With Consent

  • The verse ye'adah implies ya'adah — 'he must inform her'
  • R' Yosei b'R Yehuda: Original money was NOT for betrothal, so consent needed
  • Rav Naḥman b. Yitzḥak: Even if money was for betrothal, the verse requires consent
R' Yannai Designation applies only to an adult man, and designation applies only with consent — these are interconnected principles.

How Yi'ud (Designation) Is Performed

📜 Master acquires the daughter as a Hebrew maidservant from her father
↓
👥 Master arranges for two witnesses to be present
↓
💍 Master says: 'You are hereby mekuddeshet to me' or 'me'oreset to me'
↓
⏰ Valid even at the end of six years, even close to sunset on her last day
↓
👰 Once designated, he must treat her as a wife — not as a slave
Dispute R' Yosei b'R Yehuda disagrees: Designation is valid only if enough time remains for her to perform work worth one peruta — that amount constitutes the betrothal money.

Father Sells Daughter, Then Betroths Her to Another

R' Yosei b'R Yehuda
If a father sells his daughter and then betroths her to another man — he mocks the master, but she is betrothed to the second man.
Rabbis
If the master wants to designate her, he may do so — the second man's betrothal is void.
Rav Aḥa b. Rava
The parable of 'be betrothed after 30 days' where a second man betroths within 30 days — she's betrothed to the second. This matches R' Yosei b'R Yehuda.
Gemara
The parable teaches that even if the master stated a future date for designation, the father's subsequent betrothal to another still prevails.
Practical Impact According to the Rabbis, the master has a prior claim from the moment of acquisition. According to R' Yosei b'R Yehuda, the father retains the power to betroth her elsewhere.
