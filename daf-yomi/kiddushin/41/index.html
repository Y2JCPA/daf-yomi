Kiddushin 41: Agency in Betrothal

Mishna: A man can betroth by himself or by agent; a woman can become betrothed by herself or by agent
↓
Gemara asks: If agent works, why mention 'by himself'?
↓
Rav Yosef
Mitzva bo yoter: Better to do a mitzva yourself than via agent
Yesh Omrim
For a man, it's actually prohibited — he must see her first (Rav)
Core Theme This daf establishes the legal framework of shelichut (agency) — who can appoint agents, for what purposes, and the Torah sources behind it.

Why Does the Mishna Mention 'By Himself'?

Gemara
If betrothal through an agent is valid, isn't 'by himself' obvious?
Rav Yosef
To teach: Mitzva bo yoter mi-bishlucho — it is more fitting to perform the mitzva oneself than through an agent.
Gemara
What is the source for this principle?
Rav Safra
I personally singe the animal head for Shabbat — even though others could do it.
Rava
I personally salt the shibuta fish for Shabbat — honoring Shabbat through personal effort.
Principle Mitzva bo yoter mi-bishlucho — A mitzva performed personally has greater value than one delegated to an agent.

Man vs. Woman: Why Betrothal in Person?

Man Betrothing

  • Rav: Prohibited to betroth without seeing her
  • Reason: He may find her repulsive afterward
  • Violates 'Love your neighbor as yourself' (Lev. 19:18)
  • Must betroth in person, not just via agent

Woman Becoming Betrothed

  • Rav Yosef: Merely preferable in person, not required
  • Reish Lakish: Women say 'Better to sit as two than sit alone'
  • She will accept whoever her agent accepts
  • No concern she'll find him repulsive
Reish Lakish טב למיתב טן דו מלמיתב ארמלו — 'Better to sit as two bodies than to sit lonely like a widow.' A woman's desire for marriage means agency poses no risk of later resentment.

Protecting a Minor Daughter

אסור לאדם שיקדש את בתו כשהיא קטנה, עד שתגדל ותאמר: בפלוני אני רוצה
Rav's Ruling It is prohibited for a person to betroth his daughter when she is a minor, until she grows up and says: 'I want to marry so-and-so.'

A father has the legal right to betroth his minor daughter, but Rav rules it is forbidden — she must be old enough to form her own opinion and consent to the match.

Where Does the Torah Teach Shelichut (Agency)?

Divorce: 'And he sends her' (veshilleḥah) — Deut. 24:1
Root ש-ל-ח = shaliaḥ (agent)
↓
Can we derive betrothal agency from divorce?
Objection
Divorce can be done against her will; betrothal cannot!
Answer
Deut. 24:2 juxtaposes marriage and divorce — 'she goes and becomes another man's wife'
Key Derivation The Torah uses the verb וְשִׁלְּחָהּ (and he sends her) — from the same root as שָׁלִיחַ (agent). This wordplay is the Torah's basis for the entire law of agency.
