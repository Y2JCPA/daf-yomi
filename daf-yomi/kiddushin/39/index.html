Orla Outside Eretz Yisrael: How Far Does It Reach?

Levi
Said to Shmuel: Aryokh, supply me with the produce — I won't pick it myself, but I will eat it. Orla is not a concern abroad.
Shmuel (Aryokh)
Complied — suggesting leniency when one does not personally gather the orla produce.
Pumbedita Sages
The prohibition of orla does not apply at all outside Eretz Yisrael.
R' Yochanan
Conceal the leniency for uncertain orla. Destroy certain orla. Whoever says orla doesn't apply abroad will have neither child nor grandchild serving Hashem.
PRACTICE R' Yochanan's ruling became normative: certain orla is forbidden abroad; uncertain orla is permitted.

Shmuel's Derivation: Kilayim of Grafting Applies Everywhere

"You shall keep My statutes" (Vayikra 19:19)
Statutes already given before Sinai — i.e., to Bnei Noach
↓
Juxtaposition of animal crossbreeding and field diverse kinds in the same verse
↓
Animal Kilayim
Mating species together — applies everywhere, not land-dependent
Field Kilayim
Refers specifically to grafting (not planting seeds together) — also applies everywhere
↓
"Your field" excludes diverse seeds — only grafting is universal
DISTINCTION Kilayim of seeds in a vineyard = rabbinic outside E"Y. Kilayim of grafting = Torah law everywhere, derived from pre-Sinaitic statutes.

The Mishna's Bold Claim

כל העושה מצוה אחת מטיבין לו ומאריכין לו ימיו ונוחל את הארץ
MISHNA Anyone who performs one mitzva — goodness is bestowed upon him, his life is lengthened, and he inherits the World-to-Come.

Anyone who does not perform one mitzva — goodness is not bestowed, his life is not lengthened, and he does not inherit the World-to-Come.

When Does One Mitzva Matter?

Rav Yehuda's Reading

  • One mitzva in addition to existing merits
  • Tips the scale when merits and sins are close to balanced
  • Compared to fulfilling the entire Torah

Rav Shemaya's Clarification

  • The mitzvot listed in Pe'a (honoring parents, kindness, peace)
  • Even when sins are perfectly equal to merits
  • One of these special mitzvot tilts the balance
RESOLUTION The mishna doesn't mean any random mitzva earns reward — it means one extra mitzva tips an already close balance toward the side of merit.
