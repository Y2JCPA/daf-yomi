Kiddushin 22: The Pierced Slave & Canaanite Slave Acquisition

Eved Nirtza: Slave who wishes to stay must declare amor yomar — twice
↓
Multiple conditions must be met: mutual love, wife & children for both, both healthy
↓
All Met
Ear is pierced at the door/doorpost
Not Met
Slave is not pierced — goes free
↓
Mishna: Canaanite slave acquired by kesef, shtar, or chazakah
📖 Key Principle "Anyone who acquires a Hebrew slave is like one who acquires a master for himself" — because the slave must be treated as an equal.

Amor Yomar: The Double Declaration

📢 Slave must declare desire to stay — amor yomar (doubled verb)
↓
⏳ Must say it at the beginning of the final peruta of work (Rava)
↓
⏳ Must also say it at the end of the final peruta
↓
💬 "I love my master, my wife, and my children — I will not go out free"
↓
❌ If stated only once (beginning OR end) — he is not pierced
Rava's Insight "Beginning of six" means the start of the last peruta of work; "end of six" means the end of that peruta — both while still technically a slave.

🤔 Unresolved Rav Beivai bar Abaye's dilemma: If both are ill — is it "with you" (same condition = OK) or do we require "fares well" (well-being required)? Teiku.

Why the Ear? Why the Door?

Rabban Yoḥanan b. Zakkai
Why is the ear different from all other limbs? This ear heard on Sinai: "For to Me the children of Israel are slaves" — they shall not be slaves to slaves. Yet this man acquired a master for himself!
R' Shimon b. R' Yehuda HaNasi
Why the door and doorpost? They were witnesses in Egypt when God passed over them and delivered Israel from slavery to freedom. Yet this man went and acquired a master — let him be pierced before these witnesses!
🌸 Ḥomer (Allegory) Both sages read the law as a moral parable: the ear that heard God's declaration of freedom, and the door that witnessed redemption, testify against voluntary servitude.

"With You" — Equal Treatment of a Hebrew Slave

Master's Standard

  • Fine bread (pat nekiyah)
  • Aged wine (yayin yashan)
  • Soft bedding and sheets

Slave Must Receive the Same

  • Not inferior bran bread (pat kibbar)
  • Not cheap new wine
  • Not sleeping on straw
Famous Saying "Anyone who acquires a Hebrew slave is like one who acquires a master for himself" — because of the obligation to provide equal living conditions.

Piercing Procedure: Ear, Awl, and Door

Bring slave to beit din (court)
↓
Bring him to the door or doorpost — must be upright and attached
↓
Take the awl (martzea) and place it through his ear into the door
↓
He bores through the ear until reaching the door — both ear and door are pierced together
⚠️ Key Detail A detached door is invalid — just as a doorpost is upright and attached, so too the door must be upright and attached (Exodus 21:6).
