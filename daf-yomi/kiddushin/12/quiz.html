Kiddushin 12 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, why does Beit Shammai require a minimum of one dinar for betrothal?
A. Because the Torah explicitly states this amount
B. Because the daughters of Israel should not be treated like ownerless property
C. Because one dinar is the minimum wage for a day's work
D. Because the Hebrew maidservant's price is always one dinar
Correct: B — Rava offers a dignity-based rationale: allowing betrothal with as little as one peruta would be disrespectful to Jewish women, treating them as hefker (ownerless).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rav Dimi resolve the apparent contradiction between his peruta valuation (⅛ issar) and Ravin's (⅙ issar)?
A. They follow two different Tannaim
B. One refers to Babylonian currency and the other to Eretz Yisrael currency
C. Both follow the same Tanna — the difference is whether the copper issar increased or decreased in value relative to silver
D. Ravin's transmission was inaccurate
Correct: C — The peruta's value in silver is fixed (1/192 dinar). The issar-to-peruta ratio changes because the copper issar fluctuates: when strong (24/dinar) = 8 perutot/issar; when weak (32/dinar) = 6 perutot/issar.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the blue marble stone case, why did Abaye and Rava reject Rav Ḥisda's comparison to Rabbi Ḥanina's captive woman ruling?
A. Rabbi Ḥanina's ruling only applies in Eretz Yisrael
B. The leniency for a captive woman (who makes herself repulsive) cannot be extended to the stringent area of married-woman prohibitions
C. Rav Ḥisda was not an authority on betrothal law
D. The witnesses in the captive case were proven unreliable
Correct: B — Abaye and Rava distinguish: the captive woman case has built-in leniency (she resists assault), but with betrothal and the prohibition of a married woman (eishet ish), we must be stringent and consider distant witnesses.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Sages of Neharde'a, for which act would Rav actually administer flogging?
A. Betrothing in the marketplace
B. Living in one's father-in-law's house
C. Betrothing through sexual intercourse without prior arrangement
D. Tormenting a messenger of the Sages
Correct: C — Neharde'a limited Rav's flogging to betrothal through intercourse (without or even with prior arrangement, per one version), considering the other cases not severe enough to warrant lashes.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The Gemara calculates that 2 sela contain 1,536 perutot. Why is this called 'close to two thousand'?
A. The Gemara rounds up to the nearest thousand
B. Different currency standards yield different totals
C. Since it passes the halfway point of the second thousand (1,500), it is considered 'close to two thousand'
D. The baraita was using approximate figures from a different era
Correct: C — 1,536 exceeds 1,500 (the midpoint between 1,000 and 2,000). Since it has passed the halfway mark of the second thousand, the Gemara considers it reasonable to call it 'close to two thousand.'
