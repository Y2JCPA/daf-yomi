Kiddushin 12: The Value of Betrothal

DAF OVERVIEW This daf dives deep into the monetary mechanics of betrothal. How much is a peruta worth? Does the value matter at the place of betrothal or anywhere in the world? What happens when someone betroths with an item of uncertain value? The Gemara also catalogs Rav's disciplinary floggings for indecent betrothal practices — a window into rabbinic standards of dignity in marriage.

Why Does Beit Shammai Require One Dinar?

Beit Shammai
Betrothal by money is derived from the Hebrew maidservant (אמה העבריה). Just as her redemption requires significant value, so too betrothal requires at least one dinar.
Gemara
But why specifically one dinar? Why not half a dinar or two perutot?
Sages
Since the comparison excludes one peruta as too small, the Sages established the minimum at one dinar — a coin of substantial value.
Rava
The real reason: בנות ישראל אינן הפקר — the daughters of Israel should not be treated like ownerless property! One peruta is disrespectful.
KEY PRINCIPLE Rava reframes the debate: it's not just legal derivation — it's about human dignity in the betrothal process.

How Much Is a Peruta? Two Traditions

Rav Dimi / R' Simai

  • 1 peruta = ⅛ Italian issar
  • Based on the first Tanna's calculation
  • Applies when issar is strong (24 issar/dinar)
  • 192 perutot per dinar

Ravin / R' Dostai, R' Yannai, R' Oshaya

  • 1 peruta = ⅙ Italian issar
  • Matches Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel's view
  • Applies when issar is weak (32 issar/dinar)
  • Still 192 perutot per dinar
RESOLUTION Rav Dimi explains: both follow the same Tanna. The peruta's silver value is fixed at 1/192 of a dinar. The issar-to-peruta ratio fluctuates because copper issar values change relative to silver.

The Blue Marble Stone Case

A man betroths a woman with a blue marble stone (כוחלא)
↓
Is the stone worth one peruta here?
Rav Ḥisda
Not betrothed. Only local value matters. Witnesses elsewhere are irrelevant — "they are not here now."
Abaye & Rava
Betrothed. If witnesses in Idit know it was worth a peruta there, we must account for them.
PRACTICAL OUTCOME Descendants of this woman's second marriage remained in Sura, but the Sages avoided marrying into the family — following Abaye and Rava's stringency over Rav Ḥisda.

Shmuel's Rule: Betrothal with a Date

Shmuel
If a man betrothed a woman with a single date, she is betrothed — even if a whole kor of dates is only worth one dinar, because somewhere that date might be worth a peruta.
Rav Yosef
Applied practically: A man betrothed with a myrtle branch in the marketplace. Ruling: Flog him (per Rav, for marketplace betrothal) and require a get (per Shmuel, in case it's worth a peruta somewhere).
SHMUEL'S CONCERN Shmuel's principle creates a stringency: virtually any item might be worth a peruta somewhere, so betrothal with low-value items requires a get to dissolve.
