Betrothal with a Promissory Note

Shmuel
One who sells a promissory note and then forgives the debtor — the debt is forgiven. The obligation is non-transferable.
Rabbis
Since the seller can forgive the debt, the woman will not rely on collecting via the note — she is not betrothed.
R' Meir
The woman does rely on the note. She thinks: He would not forfeit my benefit and forgive the debt of others.
Alternative
Or: R' Meir simply disagrees with Shmuel's rule — the seller cannot forgive the debt after selling the note.
Shmuel's Principle A promissory note represents a personal obligation to the creditor, not a transferable asset — the seller can always forgive the debt.

Oral Loan Transfer: Rav Huna's Rule

Person A says to Person B:
"I have 100 dinars with you — give it to C"
↓
Was this stated in the presence of all three parties?
Yes — All 3 Present
C acquires it (Rav Huna citing Rav)
No
C does not acquire
↓
But does this apply to a loan (spent money) or only a deposit (intact item)?
Rabbis
Only a deposit — the item is intact.
A loan has no tangible object to transfer.
R' Meir
No difference — loan or deposit, the third party acquires it.
Ma'amad Sheloshtan Rav's rule of acquiring in the presence of three parties (מעמד שלשתן) is the mechanism tested for transferring a loan for betrothal.

Document of Betrothal: Three Views

R' Meir

  • Signatory witnesses effect the divorce/betrothal (עדי חתימה)
  • Document without signed witnesses = NOT betrothed
  • Document must be written for her sake (לשמה)

R' Elazar

  • Witnesses of transmission effect the divorce/betrothal (עדי מסירה)
  • Even unsigned document = BETROTHED if handed over before witnesses
  • Betrothal is valid regardless of document formalities

Rabbis

  • Uncertain whether R' Meir or R' Elazar is correct
  • Betrothal not effected via the document itself
  • Appraise the paper: if worth a peruta → betrothed
Reish Lakish's Question Must a betrothal document be written for her sake (לשמה)? We juxtapose 'becoming' to 'leaving' — just as a get must be written for her, so must a betrothal document.

Does a Craftsman Acquire Through Enhancement?

👩 Woman gives jewelry to a craftsman for repair
↓
🔨 Craftsman enhances the vessel with his labor
↓
❓ Does the craftsman acquire partial ownership via the enhancement? (קנין אומן בשבח כלי)
↓
✅ R' Meir: YES — he partly owns it, so he can betroth her with it
↓
❌ Rabbis: NO — he is merely owed wages, which is a loan
↓
🔄 Alternative: Everyone agrees no craftsman acquisition — dispute is about adding a nofekh
Practical Impact If a craftsman acquires ownership through enhancement, he transfers real property at betrothal — not just forgiving a debt.
