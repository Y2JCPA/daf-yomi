Kiddushin 59 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Mishnah here use the term 'another' instead of 'agent' when discussing someone who betroths a woman for himself?
A. To teach that even a non-agent who does this is a scoundrel
B. Because only non-agents can betroth on behalf of others
C. To distinguish this case from divorce law
D. Because the betrothal is invalid when done by a non-agent
Correct: A — The Gemara explains that using 'another' teaches the chiddush that even someone who is NOT a formal agent — and therefore less relied upon — is still called a scoundrel if he acts for himself.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why was Ravin the Pious not considered a scoundrel for betrothing the woman to himself instead of his son?
A. He had his son's permission in advance
B. The woman's family refused to give her to the son
C. The betrothal was actually invalid
D. His son had already married someone else
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the family would only agree to let her marry the father, not the son — Ravin had no choice if the match was to happen at all.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the 'ani mehapech b'charara' principle, what is the status of someone who takes what a pauper was actively pursuing?
A. He must return the item and pay damages
B. The transaction is void
C. He is called wicked (rasha)
D. He is excommunicated by the court
Correct: C — Rabbi Abba himself states the principle: one who snatches a loaf that a pauper was pursuing is called 'rasha' (wicked). This is a moral designation, not a legal invalidation — the acquisition stands.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the decisive proof that refutes Reish Lakish's view that a statement cannot nullify a prior statement?
A. The terumah case where the owner cancels the agent's appointment
B. Rav Pappa's derivation from 'ki yuttan'
C. A husband can verbally cancel a get sent through an agent
D. The case of Rav Giddel and Rabbi Abba's land
Correct: C — The Gemara brings the halakha that a husband who sends a bill of divorce via an agent can cancel it by telling the agent verbally — this is clearly a statement nullifying a prior statement, conclusively refuting Reish Lakish.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Rav Giddel refuse to accept the disputed land as a gift from Rabbi Abba?
A. He suspected it was stolen property
B. Accepting gifts from scholars was forbidden
C. He lived by the verse 'he who hates gifts shall live' (Proverbs 15:27)
D. The land was in a dangerous area controlled by violent men
Correct: C — Rav Giddel refused the gift based on the principle from Proverbs 15:27 — 'sonei matanot yichyeh' (he who hates gifts shall live). Neither sage took the land, which became known as 'the Land of the Sages.'
