The Architecture of Agency (Shelichut)

Central Question: From where in the Torah do we derive the law of shelichut (agency)?
↓
For Offerings
Exodus 12:6 — "The whole assembly shall slaughter it"
Exodus 12:3 — "Every man a lamb for a household"
For Land
Numbers 34:18 — "One prince of every tribe"
Rejected as source for agency — applies to zachin (acting in one's benefit)
Limits
Ein shaliach l'dvar aveirah — No agency for transgression
Exception: Me'ilah (consecrated property)
Key Principle The Gemara systematically examines every potential biblical source for agency, testing whether each verse teaches shelichut or a different halakha entirely.

Can One Paschal Offering Serve All Israel?

R' Yonatan
All of Israel can fulfill their obligation with one Paschal offering, as the verse says: "The whole assembly of the congregation of Israel shall slaughter it" (Exodus 12:6).
Gemara
But not everyone can eat an olive-bulk from one offering! How can they fulfill their obligation?
R' Yonatan
The verse proves it — "the whole assembly shall slaughter it" — yet only one person slaughters per group. This shows agency works for offerings.
Gemara
But perhaps it's different there — the one slaughtering has partnership in the offering. That's not true agency!
Core Tension The Gemara demands a proof of agency where the agent has no personal stake — mere partnership is not enough.

Searching for the Source of Shelichut in Offerings

Verse 1: "The whole assembly shall slaughter it" (Ex. 12:6)
↓
❌ Rejected — the slaughterer is a partner, not a pure agent
↓
Verse 2: "Every man a lamb for a household" (Ex. 12:3)
↓
❌ Also a partner! But now we have two verses teaching the same thing
↓
✅ Im eino inyan — if the halakha doesn't apply where it's written, apply it elsewhere → Agency even without partnership
↓
❌ But this verse is needed for R' Yitzchak's halakha (adult vs. minor)!
↓
R' Yitzchak's halakha derived instead from Ex. 12:4: "According to every man's eating" — only an adult can acquire for others
Method The Gemara uses im eino inyan l'gufo, tnehu inyan l'acherim — if a verse is superfluous for its own context, redirect it to teach a new law.

Three Principles from Numbers 34:18 — "Take One Prince"

Shelichut (Agency)?

  • Rav Giddel/Rav: Princes acted as agents for their tribes
  • ❌ REJECTED — minors were included, and minors can't appoint agents
  • Agency requires the principal's conscious appointment

Zachin (Benefit in Absence)?

  • One can act in a person's interest without their knowledge
  • ❌ REJECTED — some got hills when they wanted valleys!
  • What seems beneficial may actually be disadvantageous

Court-Appointed Steward

  • ✅ ACCEPTED — court appoints a steward for orphans
  • Authority to act even to temporary disadvantage
  • If the ultimate result benefits the orphans
Final Reading The verse about tribal princes teaches court authority over orphan estates — the power to act even against short-term interests for long-term benefit.

Brothers Dividing Inheritance — Rules of Ona'ah (Fraud)

Error MarginLegal ResultRava's Qualification
Less than 1/6Transaction is valid and acquiredOnly if the disadvantaged brother did not appoint an agent — an agent must achieve benefit
Exactly 1/6Acquired, but the excess must be returnedApplies only to movable property — land has no ona'ah
More than 1/6Transaction is voidOnly if they did not agree to court appraisal — court appraisal follows R' Shimon b. Gamliel's rule
Land by measureError of any size — must be correctedPer Rabba: anything measured/weighed/counted, even less than 1/6, is returned
Rav Nachman Brothers who divide an inheritance are treated as purchasers from each other — standard commercial fraud rules apply.

Ein Shaliach L'dvar Aveirah — No Agency for Sin

Gemara
Mishna: One who sends fire via a competent person — only the agent is liable. But why? The sender appointed him — agency should make the sender liable!
Principle
There is no agency for transgression. When the Master (God) and the student (human sender) conflict — whose words do you obey? The agent bears responsibility.
Gemara
But the Baraita says: If an agent performed his agency and misused hekdesh (consecrated property), the owner is guilty of me'ilah!
Answer
Me'ilah is different — a gezeirah shavah (verbal analogy) of "sin" links it to terumah, where agency works. So agency applies even for this transgression.
Key Distinction Normally, an agent who sins bears sole responsibility. Me'ilah is the exception — derived by analogy to terumah.
