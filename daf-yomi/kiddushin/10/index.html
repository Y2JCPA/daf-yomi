Kiddushin 10: When Does Intercourse Take Legal Effect?

R' Zeira: R' Yehuda HaNasi concedes rapist/seducer pays fine even for non-virgin
↓
Key Question: Does the beginning or end of intercourse effect acquisition?
↓
Ameimar in name of Rava: Completion of intercourse effects acquisition
↓
Second Dilemma: Does intercourse effect betrothal only or also marriage?
Effects Marriage
He inherits her property
Priest becomes impure to bury her
He nullifies her vows alone
Effects Betrothal Only
No inheritance rights
No impurity obligation
Cannot nullify vows alone
Core Sugya The Gemara explores the precise legal mechanics of betrothal through intercourse — both its timing and its scope.

Fine of Rapist/Seducer for Non-Virgin

R' Zeira
R' Yehuda HaNasi concedes that rapist and seducer pay the fine even for a non-virgin
Gemara
Why is the fine different from the death penalty? For death — they are treated as if she were a non-virgin betrothed woman. But for the fine — they pay as if she were a virgin?
Gemara
The verse says "the man only" (Deut. 22:25) — levado indicates only one man is executed for intercourse with a virgin
R' Oshaya
"Both of them shall die" (Deut. 22:22) — only when both deserve punishment. If one is a minor, neither is executed
R' Yonatan
"The man only" teaches that sometimes only one of them is liable to the death penalty
Key Derivation The word levado ('only') is used differently by R' Yehuda HaNasi and the Rabbis, yielding different halakhic conclusions about fines vs. capital punishment.

When Does Intercourse Effect Betrothal?

Dilemma: Does the beginning (ha'ara'a) or end (gemar bi'ah) of intercourse effect acquisition?
↓
Case 1: He begins intercourse, she reaches out and accepts kiddushin from another man mid-act
Beginning Effects
She is betrothed to the first man — the second's kiddushin is meaningless
End Effects
She is betrothed to the second man — the first hasn't completed the act
↓
Case 2: A Kohen Gadol (High Priest) betroths a virgin through intercourse — he may only marry a virgin
Beginning Effects
Valid — she is still a virgin at moment of betrothal
End Effects
Problem — she is no longer a virgin at the moment of betrothal
Resolution Ameimar in the name of Rava: One who engages in intercourse intends the completion of the act — acquisition takes effect at the end.

Betrothal Only vs. Marriage Through Intercourse

If Intercourse = Marriage

  • ✅ He inherits her property upon her death
  • ✅ If a Kohen, he becomes tamei to bury her
  • ✅ He can nullify her vows alone (without her father)
  • ✅ She may eat teruma if married to a Kohen

If Intercourse = Betrothal Only

  • ❌ No inheritance rights
  • ❌ No impurity obligation for burial
  • ❌ Cannot nullify her vows alone
  • ❌ Needs chuppah before eating teruma
Stakes This question has major practical consequences for property rights, ritual purity, and religious authority over vows.

Abaye's Proof from the Mishna (Ketubot 46b)

Father's Rights (Betrothal)Husband's Additional Rights (Marriage)
Receives kiddushin (money, document, or intercourse)Enjoys profits of her property during her lifetime
Rights to items she findsInherits her property
Rights to her earningsBecomes impure to bury her (if Kohen)
Nullifies her vowsNullifies her vows alone
Accepts her get (bill of divorce)Full marital obligations
Abaye's Argument The Mishna lists intercourse under betrothal, then separately says 'if she married' — implying intercourse effects only betrothal. The Gemara rejects this: 'if she married' may refer only to money/document betrothal.

Ben Bag Bag & R' Yehuda ben Beteira: Teruma for the Betrothed

Ben Bag Bag
I heard you say a non-priest's daughter betrothed to a Kohen may eat teruma. Is this true?
R' Yehuda ben Beteira
Don't you know the kal va'chomer? A Canaanite maidservant — intercourse doesn't let her eat teruma, yet money does. A betrothed woman — intercourse does let her eat teruma, so surely money should too!
R' Yehuda ben Beteira
But what can I do? The Sages decreed she may not eat teruma until she enters the chuppah (wedding canopy)
Gemara
This proves intercourse effects marriage — otherwise why would R' Yehuda ben Beteira be so certain intercourse allows teruma?
Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak
No — he speaks of intercourse with a chuppah vs. money without a chuppah. The kal va'chomer still works.
Ulla's Reason The Sages prohibited a betrothed woman from eating teruma lest she share it with her father's household (non-priests) while still living at home.
