Kiddushin 78 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Yehuda, why does a High Priest who has intercourse with a widow receive two sets of lashes?
A. One for 'shall not take' and one for 'shall not profane'
B. One for the widow prohibition and one for profaning offspring
C. One from the Torah and one from rabbinic law
D. One for betrothal and one for the marriage ceremony
Correct: A — Rav Yehuda teaches that the High Priest violates two separate prohibitions: 'shall not take' (Lev. 21:14) and 'shall not profane' (Lev. 21:15), each carrying its own set of lashes.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the fundamental disagreement between Abaye and Rava regarding forbidden priestly unions?
A. Whether a High Priest can marry a widow at all
B. Whether betrothal alone (without intercourse) triggers lashes
C. Whether a ḥalutza is forbidden by Torah or rabbinic law
D. Whether a convert's daughter is fit for the priesthood
Correct: B — Abaye holds that betrothal alone violates 'shall not take' and incurs lashes, while Rava holds that 'shall not take' is only a means to prevent profanation — so only intercourse triggers liability.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Gemara, what is the status of the prohibition against a priest marrying a ḥalutza?
A. A full Torah prohibition derived from the verse
B. A rabbinic prohibition, with the verse serving only as an asmakhta (support)
C. Permitted entirely — there is no such prohibition
D. A Torah prohibition that applies only to High Priests
Correct: B — Though a baraita derives the prohibition from the extra 'and' in 'and a woman divorced,' the Gemara concludes this is merely an asmakhta — the actual prohibition is rabbinic.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the practical halakhic ruling regarding a convert's daughter marrying a priest?
A. She is completely forbidden by Torah law per R' Yehuda
B. The halakha follows R' Yosei that she is fit, but priests customarily required at least one born-Jewish parent
C. She is permitted only if both parents converted before age three
D. She is forbidden unless the High Court grants special permission
Correct: B — The halakha follows R' Yosei that a convert's daughter is fit. However, after the Temple's destruction, priests adopted the stringency of R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov, requiring at least one born-Jewish parent.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the mishna about declaring a child a mamzer, why does the Gemara say 'even if both parents agree' adds something new?
A. Because two witnesses are always more credible than one
B. Because the mother knows who the father is, yet she is still not credible for this declaration
C. Because the child must also agree for the declaration to be valid
D. Because both parents have a financial interest in the child's status
Correct: B — The father's lack of credibility could be explained by his uncertainty about paternity. The mother's testimony is stronger since she knows the father's identity — yet even she is not deemed credible to declare the child a mamzer.
