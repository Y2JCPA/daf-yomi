When Should a Father Marry Off His Son?

Rava
While your hand is still on your son's neck — find him a wife.
First Opinion
The appropriate age is from sixteen until twenty-two.
Second Opinion
From eighteen until twenty-four.
Gemara
This parallels the dispute of R' Yehuda and R' Neḥemya on Proverbs 22:6 — 'Train a child in the way he should go.'
INSIGHT The window for marriage matches the window for education — while a father still has influence, he must both teach and marry off his son.

Grandfather's Obligation to Teach Torah

First Baraita

  • "Teach them to your sons" (Deut. 11:19)
  • Your sons — but NOT your sons' sons
  • "Your sons and sons' sons" (Deut. 4:9) = merit, not obligation
  • Teaching your son earns credit as if teaching all future generations

Second Baraita

  • "Your sons" = sons but NOT daughters
  • "Sons and sons' sons" = actual obligation to teach grandsons
  • Grandfather IS obligated to teach grandson Torah
  • This is the view Shmuel follows (Zevulun ben Dan)
R' YEHOSHUA BEN LEVI Whoever teaches his grandson Torah — it is as if he received it from Mount Sinai! (Deut. 4:9–10)

Dividing Study Into Thirds

SourceTeaching
Rav Safra / R' Yehoshua ben Ḥananya"Veshinnantam" — read as veshillashtam (from shalosh, three)
The SagesDivide each day into thirds: Bible, Mishna, Talmud
Why each day?No one knows how long they will live — can't calculate a third of a lifetime
The Early Sages (Soferim)Called 'counters' because they counted every letter in the Torah
Midpoint of Torah lettersThe vav in gaḥon (Lev. 11:42)
Midpoint of Torah wordsDarosh darash (Lev. 10:16)
Midpoint of Torah verses"Then he shall be shaven" (Lev. 13:33)
PRACTICAL RULE Since one cannot predict the length of life, the division into thirds applies to each individual day of study.

Enemies Who Become Beloved

את והב בסופה — אהבה בסופה
R' ḤIYYA BAR ABBA A father and son, or rabbi and student, studying Torah together become enemies due to the intensity of debate — but they do not leave until they love each other.

Source: "Vahev beSufa" (Num. 21:14) — read as ahava besofa, love at its end.
