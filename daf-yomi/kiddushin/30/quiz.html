Kiddushin 30 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, when should a father arrange his son's marriage?
A. After the son completes his Torah education
B. While the father still has authority over his son
C. When the son reaches age 30
D. Only after the son requests it
Correct: B — Rava told R' Natan bar Ami: 'While your hand is still on your son's neck' — meaning while you still have influence and control — find him a wife.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What resolution does the Gemara offer regarding the Zevulun ben Dan example?
A. A father must teach all five subjects just like Zevulun's grandfather
B. Only the grandfather's role is emulated, but Bible alone is the minimum requirement
C. Zevulun ben Dan's case was exceptional and should not be followed
D. The baraita overrules Shmuel entirely
Correct: B — The Gemara reconciles by saying: emulate Zevulun ben Dan in that a grandfather taught him, but not in the scope — only Bible is strictly required.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why must the division of study into thirds be applied daily rather than over a lifetime?
A. The Torah explicitly states it must be daily
B. Because no one knows how long they will live
C. Because the evil inclination renews itself daily
D. Because the Soferim established a daily counting practice
Correct: B — The Gemara asks: who knows the length of his life to calculate a third? Therefore, the division into Bible, Mishna, and Talmud applies to each day.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does R' Ḥiyya bar Abba mean by 'enemies in the gate'?
A. Torah scholars who compete for communal positions
B. Nations that oppose Israel's Torah study
C. Father and son or rabbi and student who argue intensely in study but end with love
D. Students who refuse to share Torah insights with each other
Correct: C — R' Ḥiyya bar Abba explains that study partners become 'enemies' due to intense debate, but they do not leave until they love each other — derived from 'Vahev beSufa' read as 'love at its end.'
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the parable about the bandage, what does the bandage represent?
A. Prayer
B. The evil inclination
C. Torah study
D. Repentance
Correct: C — God says: I created the evil inclination (the wound) and I created Torah as its antidote. The Torah is the bandage — as long as it stays on, the wound heals. Remove it and the wound festers.
