Chazakah in Court & the Laws of Yichud

Chazakah (Presumptive Status)
Can courts act on it?
R' Yochanan: Yes for people
Courts flog (niddah), stone (incest), and burn (daughter-in-law) based on chazakah
But not for teruma
R' Yochanan: Teruma based on chazakah is set aside — neither eaten nor burned
↓
Separate topic: Yichud — seclusion prohibitions
↓
A man may not be secluded with two women
A woman may be secluded with two men
Key Theme This daf examines the power of presumptive status in court and transitions to the laws of seclusion (yichud) between men and women.

Chazakah for Lineage: Children Clinging to Mother

Tanna Kamma
Children clinging to a woman proves she is their mother only for teruma — her children may eat teruma if she married a Kohen.
Tanna Kamma
But not for lineage — her daughters may not marry Kohanim based on this presumption alone.
R' Yochanan
If children cling to her, they are considered hers in all regards, even for lineage.
R' Yochanan
This follows my principle: courts act on chazakah (presumptive status) even for capital punishment.
Context R' Yochanan consistently holds that chazakah has strong legal force — sufficient even for the most severe court actions.

Burning Teruma on Chazakah: The Child & Dough Dispute

R' Meir — Dough is Pure

  • Most children handle items within reach
  • But a minority do NOT touch things
  • Minority + presumption of purity = purity wins
  • The two factors together weaken the majority

Rabbis — Dough is Impure

  • Most children handle items within reach
  • The minority is treated as nonexistent
  • Majority vs. presumptive purity → majority wins
  • Reish Lakish: This proves we burn teruma on chazakah
R' Yochanan Disagrees R' Yochanan says the child-and-dough case is NOT a chazakah that warrants burning teruma — the dough is set aside, neither eaten nor burned.

R' Yochanan: What IS the Chazakah for Burning Teruma?

Started dough in a house with creeping animals (tamei) and frogs (tahor)
↓
Pieces of an unidentified creature found in the dough
Majority are sheratzim
Dough is impure — teruma may be burned
Majority are frogs
Dough is pure
Distinction R' Yochanan accepts chazakah for burning teruma only when backed by a clear majority — not from behavioral patterns like children touching things.

Mishna: The Laws of Yichud (Seclusion)

Mishna
A man may not be secluded with two women — but one woman may be secluded with two men.
R' Shimon
Even one man may be secluded with two women when his wife is with him, because she guards him.
Mishna
A man may be secluded with his mother or daughter and sleep alongside them with bodily contact.
Mishna
But when they have grown up, each sleeps in their own garment — though they may still share a bed.
School of Eliyahu
Why the asymmetry? Because women are of light mind (da'atan kallot) — more easily persuaded.
Practical Rule Two men can keep each other in check, but two women cannot be relied upon to prevent a single man from sinning.
