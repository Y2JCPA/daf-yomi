Kiddushin 17 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
If a Hebrew slave was sick for 4 out of 6 years, what is his obligation?
A. He goes free — the verse says 'in the seventh he shall go out'
B. He must work 4 additional years to compensate
C. He is treated as one sick for all 6 and must complete actual labor
D. He works one extra year as a compromise
Correct: C — The Gemara establishes that sick for 4 years is treated like sick for all 6 — requiring completion of actual labor. The lenient rule (no makeup) only applies up to 3 years of illness.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is R' Meir's gezera shava for deriving 5 sela per type of severance gift?
A. "Giving" (netina) — from the fine for a gored slave
B. "Empty" (reikam) — from firstborn redemption
C. "Blessing" (berakha) — from tithing laws
D. "Empty" (reikam) — from the olat re'iya pilgrimage offering
Correct: B — R' Meir connects 'you shall not send him away empty' (Devarim 15:13) to 'none shall appear before Me empty' (Shemot 34:20) regarding firstborn redemption, which costs 5 sela.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does a Hebrew slave serve the master's SON after the master dies, but not a brother?
A. Because the Torah explicitly lists 'son' as an heir
B. Because the son stands in his father's place for yi'ud and ancestral field — and yibum itself proves the son has priority
C. Because brothers have no inheritance rights at all
D. Because the son pays the remaining debt of the father
Correct: B — The son replaces the father for designation (yi'ud) of a maidservant and ancestral field redemption. The brother's claim via yibum actually proves the son's priority, since yibum only applies when there is no son.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Elazar ben Azarya, when does a master NOT need to give a severance gift?
A. When the slave fled before completing his term
B. When the slave was sick for all 6 years
C. When the master's house was not blessed due to the slave
D. When the slave is released by the Jubilee Year
Correct: C — R' Elazar ben Azarya reads the verse literally: 'of that with which God has blessed you' means only if the house was actually blessed due to the slave. The doubled verb 'ha'anek ta'anik' is merely stylistic, per his principle that 'the Torah spoke in the language of people.'
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which type of slave does NOT serve the master's son after the master dies?
A. A Hebrew slave (eved ivri)
B. A Hebrew maidservant (amah ivriyah)
C. Both a Hebrew slave and a Hebrew maidservant
D. None — all slaves serve the son
Correct: B — A Hebrew maidservant is juxtaposed to the pierced slave (nirtzah) by the verse 'and also to your maidservant you shall do likewise' — and serves neither the son nor the daughter. A regular Hebrew slave, however, does serve the son.
