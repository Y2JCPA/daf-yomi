Does Silence = Consent to Betrothal?

Rava
From the baraita: 'She wanted' = said yes; 'She did not want' = silent. Silence after receiving money is NOT betrothal.
Pum Nahara (R' Huna b. R' Yehoshua)
Your proof fails! In the baraita the money was a deposit — she stays silent because she fears breaking it. That doesn't prove silence rejects betrothal money given to her.
Rav Aḥai
Are all women learned in halakha? Even with betrothal money, she may fear: 'If I throw it and it breaks, I'll be liable!' Her silence proves nothing.
Ravina
We never heard R' Huna b. R' Yehoshua's objection — so we ignore it. But those who did hear it should be concerned about a possible betrothal.
PRINCIPLE Silence is ambiguous — it cannot be assumed to indicate consent to kiddushin without additional context.

The Belt-Snatcher: Rav Nahman's Case

👜 Man snatches a belt from a woman selling belts
↓
She says: 'Give it back!'
He says: 'If I give it to you, will you be betrothed to me?'
↓
She takes it and is silent
↓
Rav Nahman
❌ Not betrothed
She can say: 'I took back my own property'
Rava's Objection
But a baraita says betrothal with robbed or grabbed property is valid!
↓
Resolution: The baraita refers to where there was prior arrangement (shidukhin)
KEY DISTINCTION Prior arrangement (shidukhin) transforms the context — silence after arrangement = consent; silence without arrangement = merely reclaiming her property.

Prior Arrangement vs. No Prior Arrangement

With Shidukhin (Prior Arrangement)

  • He gives money & says 'Be betrothed'
  • She remains silent
  • ✅ She IS betrothed
  • Even with robbed/grabbed money
  • Silence = implied consent in context of prior agreement

Without Shidukhin

  • He gives money & says 'Be betrothed'
  • She remains silent
  • ❌ She is NOT betrothed
  • She can claim: 'I took my own property back'
  • Silence ≠ consent without prior context
SOURCE Rav Nahman resolves the contradiction between the belt case and the robbery-betrothal baraita by distinguishing whether shidukhin preceded the act.

He gives deposit money, then says 'Be betrothed'Silent❌ Not betrothed (Rava's proof)
RULE The betrothal declaration must be simultaneous with the transfer. Retroactive declarations are invalid even with her consent.

R' Asi's Ruling: Peruta Minimum for Land

קנין סודר
HALAKHA R' Asi said in the name of R' Mani: Just as a woman cannot be acquired for less than a peruta, so too land cannot be acquired for less than a peruta.

Objection: A baraita says land CAN be acquired for less than a peruta!

Resolution (R' Ya'akov): The baraita refers to kinyan sudar (symbolic exchange) — where even a vessel worth less than a peruta can effect acquisition. Direct monetary acquisition of land still requires a peruta.
