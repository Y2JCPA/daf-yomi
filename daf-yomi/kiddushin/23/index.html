How Can a Slave Acquire His Freedom?

Mishna
A slave is freed by money or bill of manumission (שטר).
R' Meir
Money must come from others — a slave has no independent acquisition (אין קנין לעבד בלא רבו).
Rabbis
The slave can give the money himself, provided the money originally belonged to others.
Gemara
How does R' Meir's view work? If freeing is against the slave's interest, how can others act against him without his presence?
Abaye
Since money can acquire a slave without his consent, it can also free him without consent.
Rava
With money, the master's receipt frees the slave. With a document, the slave's receipt frees him — they are fundamentally different.
Core Principle A slave cannot independently own property — everything he acquires belongs to his master. This creates a paradox: how can he hold money to buy his own freedom?

Tanna Kamma

Abba Shaul

R' Meir: Freeing a Slave With Money

Gemara
R' Meir says money must come from others. This implies a slave cannot give money himself — he has no independent acquisition.
Gemara
But R' Meir also says freeing is against the slave's interest (he loses benefits). How can others free him without his consent?
Abaye
Since money can acquire a slave against his will (transferring from one master to another), it can also free him against his will.
Gemara
Then a document received by others should also free him without consent!
Abaye
No — a bill of sale and a bill of manumission are completely different documents. But a coin is a coin regardless of purpose.
Key Distinction Money is fungible — the same coin works for acquisition and emancipation. Documents are text-specific — a sale document and a freedom document are fundamentally different.

Money vs. Document: Rava's Distinction

💰 Money (Kesef)

  • The master receives the money
  • The master's receipt causes emancipation
  • Others giving money is not 'acting against' the slave
  • The slave need not consent
  • Same coin works for purchase or freedom

📜 Document (Shtar)

  • The slave (or agent) receives the document
  • Receipt by the slave/agent causes emancipation
  • Others receiving = acting on slave's behalf
  • Cannot act against someone's interest in absence
  • Bill of sale ≠ bill of manumission
Rava The mechanism differs: with money, the master's hand frees; with a document, the slave's hand frees. This explains why a document requires the slave's involvement but money does not.

R' Meir vs. Rabbis: The Core Dispute

Rav Sheshet
Everyone agrees: אין קנין לעבד בלא רבו — a slave has no independent acquisition. The dispute is about conditional gifts.
Rav Sheshet
Case: Someone gives 100 dinars to the slave saying: 'On condition that your master has no rights to it.'
R' Meir
When the slave acquires the money, the master automatically acquires it. The condition is void — you can't stipulate away the master's rights.
Rabbis
The condition is effective. The master does not acquire this money. The slave owns it and can use it for his emancipation.
Dispute Can a condition override the automatic transfer of a slave's acquisitions to his master? R' Meir says no — the condition is meaningless. The Rabbis say yes — conditions can create exceptions.

📋 Kiddushin 23 — Summary Map

Mishna: A slave is freed by money or document
↓
Problem: אין קנין לעבד בלא רבו — slave cannot own property independently
↓
R' Meir: Only others can give money to master. Conditions on gifts to slaves are void.
Rabbis: Slave can give money himself if given with a condition or as a conduit for emancipation.
↓
Document: Slave must receive it himself (R' Meir) — because the slave's receipt is what effects freedom, unlike money where master's receipt effects freedom (Rava)
