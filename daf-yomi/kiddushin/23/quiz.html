Kiddushin 23 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abba Shaul, what happens to minor slaves of a convert who dies without heirs?
A. They acquire themselves and go free
B. Anyone who seizes them acquires them
C. They remain ownerless indefinitely
D. They are freed by the beit din
Correct: B — Abba Shaul distinguishes between adult slaves (who acquire themselves) and minor slaves (who can be acquired by whoever seizes them). The Tanna Kamma holds both adults and minors acquire themselves.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does R' Meir say a slave can be freed by money given by others but NOT by himself?
A. The slave's money is ritually impure
B. A slave has no independent acquisition — everything he gets belongs to his master
C. The Torah explicitly prohibits slaves from handling money
D. The slave might be coerced into paying
Correct: B — The principle of אין קנין לעבד בלא רבו means a slave cannot independently own property. Any money he acquires automatically becomes his master's, so he cannot possess money to buy his own freedom.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rava's key distinction between freeing a slave with money versus a document?
A. Money is more valuable than a document
B. With money, the master's receipt frees; with a document, the slave's receipt frees
C. Documents require witnesses but money does not
D. Money works only for Canaanite slaves while documents work for all slaves
Correct: B — Rava explains that money operates through the master receiving it (so others aren't acting against the slave), while a document operates through the slave (or his agent) receiving it, which means others cannot receive it without his consent since it would be acting against his interest in his absence.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Sheshet, what do R' Meir and the Rabbis actually disagree about?
A. Whether a slave can own property at all
B. Whether a condition ('on condition your master has no rights') can override the master's automatic acquisition
C. Whether emancipation requires a document or money
D. Whether the slave must consent to being freed
Correct: B — Rav Sheshet says both sides agree a slave has no independent acquisition. The dispute is whether a donor's condition — 'on condition your master has no rights to it' — can effectively prevent the master from acquiring the money given to the slave.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say a slave CAN appoint an agent despite not being able to receive his own bill of manumission (per R' Shimon ben Elazar)?
A. Because the Torah explicitly permits it
B. Because a slave has some involvement in manumission laws — he can receive another slave's document
C. Because his master can appoint the agent for him
D. Because agency is a rabbinic institution with different rules
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes this from the case of priests (where Israelites have no involvement in Temple service at all). A slave has partial involvement in manumission — he can accept another slave's freedom document — so the halakhot are relevant to him, enabling agency.
