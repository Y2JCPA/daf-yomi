Kiddushin 79 — Status, Presumption & Proof

Core Question When a father betroths his daughter while traveling and she simultaneously betroths herself in the city — and she is now a grown woman (bogeret) — do we presume she was already a grown woman when the father acted? Rav says yes: her current status is projected backward. Shmuel says we must be concerned about both betrothals. The Gemara tests this dispute through analogies to a deficient mikveh and a moribund gift-giver, exploring the limits of chazakah (presumptive status).
3 / 9

Why Both Cases Are Needed in the Mishna

Father Betroths & Appoints Agent

  • A father is expert in lineage (baki b'yuchsin)
  • If he betrothed on his own, he must have found superior lineage
  • Without this case, we might think only the father's independent choice overrides the agent

Woman Betroths & Appoints Agent

  • A woman is particular about her spouse (makpida)
  • If she chose someone herself, she clearly prefers him
  • Without this case, we might think only a woman's personal pickiness overrides the agent
Conclusion Both cases are necessary: the father's expertise and the woman's selectiveness are different reasons why self-betrothal overrides the agent.

Father Betrothed on the Way — She Is Now a Bogeret

Rav
She is a grown woman (bogeret) at present. We presume she was already a bogeret when the father acted — his betrothal does not take effect.
Shmuel
We must be concerned about both betrothals. Perhaps she only became a bogeret now, and she was still a na'arah when the father acted.
Gemara
When exactly? The dispute must be about the day the six months ended — the boundary between na'arah and bogeret.
Rav
Since she is a bogeret now, there is a chazakah that she was already a bogeret in the morning when the father acted.
Shmuel
Perhaps the signs of puberty (simanim) appeared only now — she may have still been a na'arah earlier that day.
Key Timing The dispute applies specifically to the final day of the six-month na'arah period, where her exact moment of transition to bogeret is uncertain.

The Mikveh Analogy — Retroactive Status

A mikveh was known to have 40 se'ah of water
↓
Items were immersed in it for purification
↓
Mikveh is later measured and found deficient
↓
Are all prior immersions retroactively invalid?
↓
Mishna's Ruling
All items are retroactively impure — current deficiency is projected backward
Challenge to Shmuel
If current status is projected back for mikveh, why not project bogeret status backward too?
Shmuel's Answer The mikveh case is different: the item's chazakah of impurity aligns with the current deficiency. Here, the body was naturally due to mature — we cannot assume it happened earlier.

Rabbi Ya'akov vs. Rabbi Natan — Moribund Gift-Giver

IssueRabbi Ya'akovRabbi Natan
Core principlePrior status governs — leave property where it wasCurrent status governs — current condition determines burden of proof
Giver claims he was dyingBelieved without proof — property returns to himIf now healthy, he must prove he was dying
Recipients claim he was healthyThey must prove he was healthy to keep itIf he is now dying, they must prove he was healthy
Aligned with...Shmuel (prior status matters)Rav (current status is paramount)
But Not Exactly The Gemara shows both Rav and Shmuel can explain their view under either Tanna — the analogies are imperfect because bodily maturation is inevitable, unlike illness.

Rav Yosef's Ruling & Shmuel's Protest

Rav Yosef b. Rav Menashya
Ruled in an actual case in accordance with Rav — disregarded the father's betrothal entirely.
Shmuel
Sarcastically: "Is wisdom measured out to everyone in a small measure and to this one in a large measure?" — How dare he rule against me!
Mar Zutra → Rav Ashi
Ameimar said: Halakha follows Shmuel.
Rav Ashi
Halakha follows Rav.
Gemara Conclusion
The halakha is in accordance with Rav — current bogeret status is projected backward.
Practical Halakha If a woman is currently a bogeret, we presume she was already a bogeret when her father attempted to betroth her — his betrothal is void.
