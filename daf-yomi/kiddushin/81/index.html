Kiddushin 81: The Laws of Yichud & The Power of Temptation

Yichud (Seclusion)
When is a man forbidden to be alone with a woman?
↓
On the Road
Requires 3 men — one may leave to urinate
In the City
2 men suffice — others are present nearby
↓
Even the pious are not immune — Rav: 'Who says men like us are trusted?'
↓
Court may flog for seclusion violations and even for bad rumors
KEY THEME This daf explores the halakhic boundaries of seclusion, the real dangers of temptation even for the righteous, and the principle that sinful intention itself requires atonement.

How Many Men for Yichud on the Road?

Rav (via Rav Yehuda)
Two men may be secluded with one woman in a city, but on the road three are required — one may leave to urinate, leaving one man alone with her.
Gemara
The mishna about sotah supports this — two Torah scholars accompany the husband, making three total!
Gemara (refutes)
No — those two scholars serve as witnesses about whether he had intercourse, not for yichud purposes.
Rav (to Rav Yehuda)
'Raise your feet from Gehenna!' — Walk quickly away from that woman ahead of us on the road.
Rav Yehuda
But didn't you say it's permitted for men of fit morals?
Rav
I meant men like R' Ḥanina bar Pappi who have proven they can withstand temptation — not men like you and me!
MUSSAR Even Rav himself did not trust his own ability to resist temptation — humility before the yetzer hara is the first line of defense.

Court Responses to Seclusion Violations

ScenarioRulingAuthority
Secluded with an unmarried womanCourt flogs but she is not forbidden to marry a kohenRav / Rav Ashi
Secluded with a married womanCourt does not flog — to avoid rumors about her children being mamzerimRav Ashi
Secluded with a married womanCourt flogs and publicly proclaims reasonMar Zutra
Subject of a bad rumorCourt flogs even without confirmed facts — donkey bridle placed on shouldersRav / Mar Zutra
DISPUTE Rav Ashi refused to flog for seclusion with married women, fearing people would hear about the flogging but not hear the reason — and assume the worst about her children.

Leniencies That Permit Seclusion

Husband in Town

  • Rabba: If her husband is in the city, there is no concern
  • People are afraid to sin — the husband might return at any time
  • Exception: Rav Beivai at Rav Yosef's house — his wife was Beivai's friend, so the ladder was removed

Open Entrance

  • Rav Yosef: An open entrance to the public domain removes yichud concerns
  • Passersby may see inside at any moment
  • The fear of being caught prevents sin

Room Arrangements

  • Rav Kahana: Men outside, women inside = no concern
  • Baraita teaches the opposite arrangement is safe
  • Abaye: Act stringently in both cases — arrange pitchers/reeds as barriers
PRACTICAL HALAKHA Abaye arranged rows of pitchers and Rava arranged reeds between men and women — any crossing would create noise and alert others.

The Story of Peleimu vs. Satan

😤 Peleimu would say daily: 'An arrow in the eye of Satan!'
↓
👤 On Erev Yom Kippur, Satan appeared disguised as a poor man at his door
↓
🍽️ Peleimu brought him food at the door, then inside, then at the table
↓
🤢 Satan coughed up phlegm, acted revolting, then pretended to die
↓
😱 People cried: 'Peleimu killed a man!' — he fled to the bathroom
↓
👹 Satan revealed himself: 'Why did you provoke me?'
↓
🙏 Satan taught: Say 'Let the Merciful One rebuke the Satan' — don't provoke directly
LESSON One should not boast of immunity to the yetzer hara. Instead of provoking it, pray humbly: 'May the Merciful One rebuke the Satan.'

R' Ḥiyya bar Ashi and the Disguised Wife

R' Ḥiyya bar Ashi prays daily:
'Save us from the evil inclination'
↓
His wife wonders: He hasn't been intimate in years due to old age —
why does he still pray this?
↓
She disguises herself as the prostitute 'Ḥaruta'
and walks past him repeatedly in the garden
↓
He propositions her and brings her a pomegranate from the treetop as payment
↓
He discovers it was his wife — but says:
'I intended to transgress regardless'
↓
He fasted for the rest of his life
until he died in his anguish
PRINCIPLE Even though no actual sin occurred, sinful intent itself requires atonement. R' Ḥiyya's prayer was justified — the yetzer hara never fully retires.
