Why Does the Mishna Say 'Ways' (דרכים)?

Gemara
Why does the Mishna about an etrog use the term 'way' (דרך) instead of 'matter'?
Answer
Because the etrog's way of growing is like a vegetable — it needs irrigation beyond rain, and tithing follows harvest time.
Gemara
But the Mishna about a koy (uncertain animal) also uses 'ways' — and that's not about manner of growth!
Conclusion
Wherever distinctions exist, the Mishna uses 'ways'. Where there are none, it uses 'matters'.
Proof
R' Eliezer says an etrog is like a tree 'in every matter' — confirming that undistinguished cases use 'matter.'
LINGUISTIC PRECISION The Mishna's word choices are never arbitrary — 'ways' signals that multiple possibilities or distinctions apply.

What Does 'Three Ways' Exclude?

Mishna: A woman is acquired in three ways
(money, document, intercourse)
↓
Why specify a number?
To exclude other possible modes!
Standard View
Excludes wedding canopy (חופה) — it does not effect betrothal
Rav Huna's View
Rav Huna holds canopy does work, so the number excludes symbolic exchange (חליפין)
↓
Why not symbolic exchange?
It works with items worth less than a peruta,
and a woman cannot be acquired with less than a peruta
KEY LOGIC Even though betrothal with money is derived from the field of Ephron (where ḥalifin works), ḥalifin fails for betrothal because it can operate below a peruta's value.

Excluded Modes: Betrothal vs. Divorce

Betrothal Exclusions

  • Wedding canopy (חופה) — does not create betrothal
  • Or: Symbolic exchange (חליפין) per Rav Huna
  • Reason: ḥalifin works with less than a peruta
  • A woman requires at least a peruta's value

Divorce Exclusions

  • Ḥalitza (חליצה) — does not release a married woman
  • Potential a fortiori: if a yevama is released by ḥalitza...
  • ...shouldn't a wife who CAN get a get also be released by ḥalitza?
  • Refuted: 'A scroll severs her — and nothing else' (Deut. 24:3)
VERSE "ספר כריתת" — 'A scroll of severance' (Deuteronomy 24:3) limits divorce exclusively to a written document.

Deriving Betrothal with Money

Gemara
From where do we derive that a woman is acquired with money (כסף)?
Rav Yehuda / Rav
From Exodus 21:11 — 'she shall go out for nothing, without money' (בְּאֵין כָּסֶף). No money for this master, but there is money for another master.
Gemara
Who is this 'other master'?
Rav Yehuda / Rav
Her father. He receives money when she leaves his authority — i.e., betrothal money.
Gemara
Maybe the money goes to her, not her father?
Rav Yehuda / Rav
No — 'I gave my daughter to this man' (Deut. 22:16). The father marries her off, so he takes the money.
DERIVATION The extraneous phrase 'without money' (בְּאֵין כָּסֶף) in Exodus 21:11 is the Torah's source that betrothal requires money — and that the father receives it.
