QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara's conclusion, when does the Mishna use the term 'ways' (דרכים) instead of 'matters' (דברים)?
A. When discussing agricultural laws
B. When distinctions or different possibilities exist within the topic
C. When the halakha is derived from the Torah
D. When there is a dispute among the Tannaim
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that 'ways' is used wherever distinctions exist within an issue, while 'matters' is used when no such distinctions apply.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why can't symbolic exchange (ḥalifin) effect betrothal, even though betrothal with money is derived from the field of Ephron?
A. Ḥalifin requires witnesses and betrothal does not
B. Ḥalifin can work with an item worth less than a peruta, and a woman cannot be acquired with less than a peruta
C. Ḥalifin is only valid for real estate transactions
D. The Torah explicitly prohibits ḥalifin for betrothal
Correct: B — Symbolic exchange can operate with an item worth less than a peruta, but a woman cannot be acquired with anything worth less than a peruta, so ḥalifin is fundamentally incompatible with betrothal.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What verse refutes the a fortiori argument that a married woman should be releasable through ḥalitza?
A. "I gave my daughter to this man" (Deuteronomy 22:16)
B. "She shall go out for nothing, without money" (Exodus 21:11)
C. "A scroll of severance" (Deuteronomy 24:3)
D. "Being in her youth, in her father's house" (Numbers 30:17)
Correct: C — The verse 'ספר כריתות' (a scroll of severance) teaches that ONLY a written document severs the marriage bond — blocking the kal va-ḥomer from ḥalitza of a yevama.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
From which verse does Rav Yehuda in the name of Rav derive that betrothal can be effected with money?
A. "And Avraham weighed out to Ephron the silver" (Genesis 23:16)
B. "She shall go out for nothing, without money" (Exodus 21:11)
C. "If a man sells his daughter as a maidservant" (Exodus 21:7)
D. "When a man takes a wife" (Deuteronomy 24:1)
Correct: B — The phrase 'without money' implies: no money for THIS master (the current one), but there IS money for another master — her father, who receives betrothal money.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why can't we derive the father's right to a young woman's betrothal money from the fines paid by a rapist to the father?
A. Because fines are paid only for a minor, not a young woman
B. Because the rapist pays the woman directly, not the father
C. Because we do not derive monetary matters from fines (אין למדין ממון מקנס)
D. Because fines are a rabbinic enactment, not biblical
Correct: C — The Gemara invokes the principle that monetary laws cannot be derived from fines, as fines are considered a unique legal category from which ordinary monetary rights cannot be inferred.
