Whose Money Funds Kibbud Av? And Who Can Forgo Honor?

Rav Yehuda
A son must honor his father from the son's money
Rav Natan bar Oshaya
The obligation is funded from the father's money
The Sages
The halakha follows the view that it comes from the father's money
Gemara
But the verse equates honoring parents with honoring God 'with your wealth' — implying monetary loss!
Gemara
The son's loss is the neglect of his own work (bittul melakha) while attending to his father
HALAKHA The son honors his father from the father's funds, but must sacrifice his own work time — that is his personal cost.

Whose Money Pays for Kibbud Av va'Em?

Rav Yehuda
From the son's money — honor requires personal sacrifice
Rav Natan bar Oshaya
From the father's money — the son serves, the father pays
Gemara
Objection: 'Honor the Lord with your wealth' — just as God is honored with monetary loss, so too parents!
Gemara
Answer: The son's monetary loss is bittul melakha — he must leave his own work aside
The Sages
Ruling: Halakha follows — from the father's money
KEY PROOF The verbal analogy (gezeira shava) between 'Honor your father' and 'Honor the Lord with your wealth' is resolved: the son's 'wealth' is his lost work time.

Can a Son Feed His Father Ma'aser Ani?

A father and son may give each other poor man's tithe (ma'aser ani)
↓
If kibbud av is from son's money — isn't he paying his obligation with charity funds?
↓
The case is where the son already covered basic needs with his own money — this is surplus
↓
But R' Yehuda curses one who feeds his father ma'aser ani!
↓
Even for surplus — it is a disrespectful matter (ganuva milta) to feed a parent with charity
R' YEHUDA "May a curse come upon one who feeds his father the poor man's tithe" — even beyond basic needs, it dishonors a parent.

Rav Huna Tests His Son Rabba

🧪 Rav Huna wants to test if Rabba will honor him even under provocation
↓
👘 He tears expensive silk garments in front of Rabba
↓
❓ But isn't this lifnei iver — causing his son to sin by provoking anger?
↓
✅ Rav Huna had forgone his honor in advance — so anger would not be a sin
↓
❓ But doesn't tearing clothes violate bal tashchit (do not destroy)?
↓
🪡 He tore at the seam so it could be repaired
↓
😤 He did it when Rabba was already angry about something else, so he wouldn't notice the seam detail
INSIGHT The Gemara carefully examines every detail — the test was designed so that no prohibition was violated, yet the son's character was genuinely tested.

Levels of Honor: Who Can Forgo?

Rabbi (Rav)

  • A rabbi can forgo his honor
  • After studying, Torah becomes 'his Torah' (Psalms 1:2)
  • But students must still show some reverence
  • Rava was angry when Rav Mari didn't stand at all

Nasi

  • A Nasi can forgo his honor
  • Rabban Gamliel served drinks at his son's wedding
  • R' Yehoshua accepted — citing Avraham serving guests
  • R' Tzadok: God Himself serves all creatures!

King (Melekh)

  • A king cannot forgo his honor
  • 'You shall set a king over you' (Deut. 17:15)
  • His awe must remain upon the people
  • Royal honor is not the king's to waive
RAV ASHI'S PRINCIPLE Honor can be forgone at every level except royalty — a king's honor belongs to the institution, not the individual.

Who Is a 'Zaken' (Elder) You Must Stand For?

InterpretationSourceMeaning
Simple elder (aged person)?Initial reading of Lev. 19:32❌ Rejected — 'elder' has a specific meaning
A wise man (chakham)Tanna Kamma — Num. 11:16'Elders of Israel whom you know' = known for wisdom
One who acquired wisdomR' Yosei HaGelili — Prov. 8:22Zaken = zeh kanna (this one acquired)
KEY TERM Zaken (זקן) — not merely old age, but wisdom. R' Yosei HaGelili reads it as an acronym: zeh she'kanna chokhma — 'this one who has acquired wisdom.'
