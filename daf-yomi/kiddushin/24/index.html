Wife Redeeming Husband's Second Tithe

Tanna Kamma
A woman must add one-fifth (chomesh) when redeeming her husband's ma'aser sheni.
R' Shimon b. Elazar / R' Meir
A woman may redeem without adding one-fifth.
Gemara
What's the case? If she uses his money for his tithe — she's his agent, obviously she adds a fifth!
Gemara
If her money for his tithe — even the Rabbis would agree she's exempt! The Torah says 'if a man shall redeem his tithe' — not a woman redeeming another's.
Gemara
It must be: a third party gave her 100 dinars on condition she redeem her husband's tithe. But this contradicts both sides' positions on acquisition!
The Problem R' Meir holds a wife's acquisitions belong to her husband — so this should count as the husband redeeming his own tithe. The Rabbis hold the opposite, yet rule she must add the fifth!

Resolving the Contradiction: Abaye vs. Rava

Abaye

  • Simply reverse the opinions in the tithe baraita
  • R' Meir says: she must add one-fifth
  • Rabbis say: she need not add one-fifth
  • This aligns each side with their position on acquisition

Rava

  • Don't reverse — the tithe is from her father's inheritance
  • R' Meir: Tithe = property of the Temple treasury (הקדש), husband doesn't acquire it → no fifth needed
  • Rabbis: Tithe = common property (חולין), husband acquires it like other assets → fifth required
  • Each side is consistent with their view of tithe's status
Key Dispute Is ma'aser sheni sacred Temple property or ordinary property? This determines whether a husband acquires his wife's inherited tithe.

Slave Emancipation: From Tooth & Eye to All Extremities

Torah (Shemot 21:26-27):
Master knocks out slave's tooth or blinds eye → slave goes free
↓
What about other permanent injuries?
↓
Why not say tooth & eye are two verses that come as one
and cannot teach beyond themselves?
↓
Both are needed!
• Tooth alone → might include milk teeth (which regenerate)
• Eye alone → might exclude teeth (not present from birth)
↓
Since both are necessary, they can teach a general principle:
Any exposed blemish that doesn't regenerate
Principle Tooth and eye are not redundant — each teaches something the other cannot, so together they establish a broader category of emancipating injuries.

Hermeneutical Analysis: Generalization, Detail, Generalization

Verse ElementHermeneutical RoleImplication
"If a man smites" (שמות כא:כו)Generalization (כלל)Any injury at all
"Tooth" / "Eye"Detail (פרט)Only these specific injuries?
"To freedom shall he send him"Generalization again (כלל)Expands beyond tooth/eye
Result: כלל ופרט וכללItems similar to the detailExposed blemish + doesn't regenerate
Objection: must also lose function?Beard pulled / jaw dislocated → still emancipatedFunction loss NOT required
Counter: hand withered temporarily?Will regenerate → NOT emancipatedMust be permanent
Limits The amplification includes injuries without loss of function (like a dislocated jaw) but excludes temporary injuries that will heal.

Twenty-Four Extremities: Is a Bill of Manumission Required?

R' Shimon
Slave is emancipated through any of the 24 extremities, but requires a get shichrur (bill of manumission).
R' Meir
He does not require a bill of manumission.
R' Eliezer
He requires one.
R' Tarfon
He does not require one.
R' Akiva
He requires one.
The Mediators
Compromise: R' Tarfon is correct for tooth/eye (Torah explicit), R' Akiva is correct for other limbs (derived, not explicit).
R' Shimon's Reasoning He derives a gezeirah shavah between 'sending away' (שילוח) in the slave verses and 'sending away' in divorce — just as a woman needs a document, so does a slave.
