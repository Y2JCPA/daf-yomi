Kiddushin 24 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, why does R' Meir say a woman need not add one-fifth when redeeming tithe?
A. Because she is using her own money, not her husband's
B. Because the tithe she inherited is Temple property and the husband doesn't acquire it
C. Because the Torah exempts all women from adding one-fifth
D. Because the tithe was already redeemed once before
Correct: B — Rava explains that the tithe came from her father's inheritance, and R' Meir holds ma'aser sheni is Temple treasury property (הקדש), so the husband has no ownership claim over it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why can't the Gemara treat tooth and eye as 'two verses that come as one' (which would limit the halakha to only those cases)?
A. Because there are actually three verses, not two
B. Because each teaches something the other cannot — both are necessary
C. Because the Torah explicitly lists other body parts as well
D. Because the principle of 'two verses as one' only applies to monetary laws
Correct: B — Tooth alone might include milk teeth (which regenerate); eye alone might exclude teeth (not present from birth). Since each is genuinely needed, they can teach a general principle.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the result of applying the hermeneutical rule of כלל ופרט וכלל (generalization-detail-generalization) to the slave emancipation verses?
A. Only tooth and eye injuries free a slave
B. Any injury at all frees a slave
C. Any exposed, permanent blemish that doesn't regenerate frees a slave
D. Only injuries that cause loss of function free a slave
Correct: C — The generalization-detail-generalization yields: items similar to the detail — exposed blemishes that do not regenerate. Loss of function is NOT required (e.g., dislocated jaw still counts).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What compromise did 'those who discuss before the Sages' reach regarding the bill of manumission?
A. A bill is always required regardless of the injury
B. A bill is never required — the slave is automatically free
C. For tooth/eye (explicit in Torah) no bill is needed; for other limbs (derived) a bill is needed
D. A bill is needed only if the master intended to injure the slave
Correct: C — R' Tarfon's view (no bill needed) applies to tooth and eye since the Torah explicitly grants freedom. R' Akiva's view (bill needed) applies to other limbs, where the law is derived rather than explicit.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara cite the case of the rooster crowing into a glass vessel?
A. To prove that animals can be held liable for property damage
B. To challenge the ruling that striking NEAR a slave's eye (not on it) doesn't emancipate — since sound can cause legally recognized damage
C. To demonstrate that indirect damage is always exempt from liability
D. To show that only physical contact counts as an injury
Correct: B — The baraita says striking near (not on) the eye doesn't count, implying sound-caused damage is legally insignificant. But the rooster case shows sound CAN cause recognized damage — creating a contradiction the Gemara begins to resolve.
