How Are Women Exempted from the Baldness Prohibition?

Isi ben Yehuda
Women are exempt from the prohibition against making bald spots for the dead (korcha)
Abaye
The exemption is derived via a verbal analogy (gezera shava) from priests — 'sons of Aaron' excludes daughters
Rava
The exemption is derived from phylacteries — 'between your eyes' links baldness to tefillin, from which women are exempt
Gemara
Why doesn't Rava use Abaye's reasoning? He doesn't derive halakha from the difference between keraḥ and korḥa
Gemara
Why doesn't Abaye use Rava's reasoning? He says phylacteries' placement is itself derived from the baldness verse
Core Issue Both Abaye and Rava agree women are exempt from korcha — they disagree only on the Scriptural derivation

Abaye's Derivation: Women Exempt from Korcha

Priests: 'Sons of Aaron shall not make baldness' (Lev. 21:5)
↓
'Sons' excludes daughters of Aaron from this prohibition
↓
Gezera shava: 'baldness' (priests) ↔ 'baldness' (all Jews, Deut. 14:1)
↓
Conclusion
Just as priestly women are exempt, all women are exempt from korcha
Objection
But the gezera shava is needed for other halakhot (multiple bald spots, entire head)!
↓
Answer: The Torah wrote korḥa instead of keraḥ — derive both halakhot from it
Key Insight The extra letter in korḥa allows the gezera shava to serve double duty

Abaye vs. Rava: Source of Women's Exemption

Abaye — From Priests

  • Gezera shava: 'baldness' (priests) ↔ 'baldness' (all Jews)
  • 'Sons of Aaron' excludes daughters → women exempt
  • Derives from difference between keraḥ/korḥa
  • Says phylacteries' placement is derived FROM baldness, not the reverse

Rava — From Phylacteries

  • Gezera shava: 'between your eyes' (baldness) ↔ 'between your eyes' (tefillin)
  • Women exempt from tefillin → women exempt from korcha
  • Does NOT derive halakha from keraḥ vs. korḥa distinction
  • Uses a different scriptural link entirely
Agreement Both agree on the result — women are exempt. The dispute is purely about the hermeneutic method.

Which Sprinkling Excludes Women?

Mishna
Women do not perform sprinkling (zerika)
Gemara
Red heifer? 'Elazar' is written — even other priests can't do it!
Gemara
Inside Holy of Holies? 'The anointed priest' — obviously not women!
Gemara
It must refer to sprinkling blood of a bird-offering
Gemara
Kal va-chomer: If a sheep (anyone can slaughter) requires a priest for sprinkling, then a bird (priest must pinch) certainly requires a priest for sprinkling
Result The mishna's exclusion of women from sprinkling refers specifically to the blood of bird-offerings

Waving (Tenufah) for Sota & Nazirite Women

📖 R' Elazar asks R' Yoshiya: How do we know the sota herself waves her meal-offering?
↓
🔗 Gezera shava: 'hand' (sota, Num. 5:25) ↔ 'hand' (peace-offering, Lev. 7:30)
↓
✋ Peace-offering: owner waves → sota: owner (the woman) also waves
↓
🤝 How? Owner places hands beneath the offering; priest places hand under hers — they wave together
↓
🔗 Nazirite woman derived via gezera shava: 'palms' (nazirite, Num. 6:19) ↔ 'palms' (sota, Num. 5:18)
↓
✅ Both sota and nazirite women actively participate in waving their own meal-offerings
Exception Though women are generally exempt from waving (tenufah), the sota and nazirite woman are explicit exceptions
