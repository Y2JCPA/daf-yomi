Kiddushin 36 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye, how is the exemption of women from korcha (baldness for the dead) derived?
A. Gezera shava of 'baldness' from priests — 'sons of Aaron' excludes daughters
B. Gezera shava of 'between your eyes' from phylacteries
C. An explicit verse in Deuteronomy excluding women
D. A kal va-chomer from the exemption of women from tefillin
Correct: A — Abaye derives the exemption through a gezera shava linking 'baldness' stated regarding priests to 'baldness' stated regarding all Jews. Since 'sons of Aaron' excludes daughters of Aaron, the same exclusion applies to all women.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rava reject Abaye's derivation?
A. He holds the verbal analogy is used for a different purpose entirely
B. He does not derive halakha from the distinction between keraḥ and korḥa
C. He believes daughters of Aaron ARE included in the priestly prohibition
D. He holds women are actually obligated in korcha
Correct: B — Rava does not accept that halakha can be derived from the slight linguistic difference between keraḥ and korḥa, which is crucial to Abaye's reasoning that the gezera shava can serve double duty.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
To which type of sprinkling does the Gemara conclude the mishna's exclusion of women refers?
A. Sprinkling of the ashes of the red heifer
B. Sprinkling performed inside the Holy of Holies
C. Sprinkling the blood of a bird-offering
D. Sprinkling the blood of a peace-offering
Correct: C — The Gemara eliminates the red heifer (limited to Elazar) and Holy of Holies (limited to the anointed priest) and concludes the mishna refers to sprinkling the blood of a bird-offering, derived through a kal va-chomer from sheep offerings.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How do both the priest and the sota woman participate in the waving of her meal-offering?
A. They each wave half the offering separately
B. The woman places her hands beneath the offering and the priest places his hand under hers — they wave together
C. The priest waves first, then the woman waves afterward
D. The woman instructs the priest who waves on her behalf
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the owner (sota) places her hands beneath the offering, and the priest places his hand under the hand of the owner and waves it together with her.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara derive that a nazirite woman also waves her meal-offering?
A. An explicit verse in the nazirite chapter states she waves
B. A kal va-chomer from the sota's obligation to wave
C. A gezera shava of the term 'palms' from the sota passage
D. A tradition transmitted from Sinai (halakha le-Moshe mi-Sinai)
Correct: C — The Gemara derives the nazirite woman's waving through a gezera shava: 'palms' stated regarding naziriteship (Numbers 6:19) is linked to 'palms' stated regarding a sota (Numbers 5:18).
