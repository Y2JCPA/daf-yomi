QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is Rav Ḥiyya bar Avin's proof that a gentile inherits from his father by Torah law?
A. "I have given Ar to the children of Lot as an inheritance" (Deut. 2:9)
B. "He shall reckon with his purchaser" (Lev. 25:50)
C. "I have given mount Seir to Esau" (Deut. 2:5) — as Esau was a gentile
D. A kal va'chomer from the inheritance laws of Jewish converts
Correct: A — Rav Ḥiyya initially cited Esau, but the Gemara objected that Esau may have been an apostate Jew, not a gentile. So the proof shifts to Lot's children, who were certainly gentiles.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the baraita, which emancipation mode applies to a Hebrew maidservant but NOT a Hebrew slave?
A. Release at the Jubilee Year
B. Release through death of the master
C. Release through signs of puberty (simanim)
D. Release after six years of service
Correct: C — A Hebrew maidservant goes free when she shows signs of puberty — a mode unique to her. The six years, Jubilee, and death of master apply specifically to the Hebrew slave in the baraita's framework.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Abaye disagree with Rava regarding the resale of a Hebrew slave for theft?
A. Abaye says a slave can never be sold a second time under any circumstances
B. Abaye distinguishes between one victim and two victims, while Rava distinguishes between one theft and two thefts
C. Abaye says the slave can always be resold; Rava says he can never be resold
D. Abaye requires the slave's consent for resale; Rava does not
Correct: B — Rava's distinction is one theft vs. two separate thefts. Abaye argues 'for his theft' covers even multiple thefts from one person — the key distinction is whether it was one victim or two.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the core dispute between R' Akiva and R' Eliezer regarding 'bevigdo vah'?
A. Whether the verse applies to slaves or maidservants
B. Whether the vocalization (keri) or the written form (ketiv) of the Torah is authoritative
C. Whether the father or the master performs designation
D. Whether a maidservant requires a bill of divorce after designation
Correct: B — R' Akiva follows the vocalization (bevigdo = garment/designation), while R' Eliezer follows the written form (bevagdo = betrayal/deceit). R' Shimon accepts both readings as authoritative.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara struggle to resolve whether designation (yi'ud) effects marriage or only betrothal?
A. There are no relevant baraitot on the subject
B. Each proof is refuted — one can distinguish between betrothal effected by the father vs. betrothal effected by the woman herself
C. The Torah explicitly states it is betrothal, but the Rabbis decree it is marriage
D. Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak forbids further discussion of the topic
Correct: B — The Gemara brings multiple proofs, but each is refuted by distinguishing between actions done through the father's authority vs. those done independently. This distinction undermines attempts to prove the matter from analogous cases.
