Source for Gentile Inheritance

Rav Ḥiyya bar Avin / R' Yoḥanan
By Torah law a gentile inherits his father — proof: "I have given Ar to the children of Lot as an inheritance" (Deut. 2:9)
Rava
The source is: "He shall reckon with his purchaser" (Lev. 25:50) — implying the purchaser himself, not his heirs
Gemara (against Rava)
But the verse doesn't explicitly exclude heirs — it's only an inference, not a direct proof!
Gemara (against Rav Ḥiyya)
Perhaps Lot's children inherited only due to the honor of Avraham — not as a general rule for all gentiles
Distinction Each sage rejects the other's proof: Rava's is merely an inference; Rav Ḥiyya's may reflect Avraham's merit, not a universal principle.

Hebrew Slave vs. Hebrew Maidservant

Eved Ivri (עבד עברי)

  • Leaves after 6 years of service
  • Leaves at the Jubilee Year
  • Leaves at death of master
  • Can be sold again for separate thefts

Amah Ivriyah (אמה עבריה)

  • Leaves through signs of puberty (simanim)
  • Cannot be sold a second time
  • Can be redeemed against master's will
  • If designated — becomes wife, not slave
Mishna Contradiction The Mishna says a maidservant has one more mode of emancipation — implying all slave modes apply to her too. Rav Sheshet resolves: the baraita refers to a case where the master already designated her as his wife.

Can a Hebrew Slave Be Sold Again?

A thief is sold as a slave to repay his theft.
Can he be sold a second time?
↓
Rava's View
One theft: Cannot be sold twice
Two separate thefts: Can be sold again
Abaye's View
One victim: Cannot be sold again, even for multiple thefts
Two victims: Can be sold again
↓
Baraita ruling: If stolen goods = 1000 but slave value = 500 → he is sold and sold again to repay the full debt
Key Phrase "For his theft" (בגנבתו) — Abaye reads this as covering even many thefts from one victim; Rava limits it to a single theft episode.

Bevigdo Vah — Garment or Betrayal?

R' Akiva
בבגדו relates to beged (garment) — once the master spreads his garment over her (designation), her father can no longer sell her
R' Eliezer
בבגדו relates to bagad (betrayal) — since the father dealt deceitfully by selling her once, he cannot sell her again
R' Shimon
Both readings are authoritative! She cannot be sold after designation (garment) or after first sale (betrayal)
Underlying Principle R' Akiva holds the vocalization (קרי) is authoritative; R' Eliezer holds the written form (כתיב) is authoritative; R' Shimon accepts both.

Rabba bar Avuh's Dilemma: Does Designation = Marriage or Betrothal?

Practical IssueIf Marriage (Nisuin)If Betrothal (Erusin)
Husband inherits her property?✅ Yes — he inherits as husband❌ No — not yet married
Kohen becomes impure to bury her?✅ Yes — she is his wife❌ No — only betrothed
Can he nullify her vows alone?✅ Yes — without her father❌ No — father still has authority

Unresolved The Gemara attempts multiple proofs but each is refuted. The core question — does yi'ud (designation) effect full marriage or only betrothal — remains a pivotal dispute.
