Kiddushin 27: Acquiring Movable Property Through Land & Oath Extension

Core Question: How does kinyan agav (acquisition through land) work?
↓
Must property be on the land?
No — property need not be piled on the land
Must seller say 'by means of'?
Yes — must state explicitly + say 'Acquire it'
↓
Second Topic: Gilgul Shevua — Extending oaths from movable property to land claims
↓
Derived from Sota: 'Amen, Amen' includes oath extension → applied to monetary law via kal va-chomer
Halakha Property need not be on the land, but the seller must explicitly state the acquisition is 'by means of' the land.

Must Movable Property Be 'Piled On' the Land?

Suggested Proof
Rabban Gamliel said tithes are given 'and its place is rented' — implying property must be on the land
Gemara
Rejected: Rabban Gamliel only did this for convenience — so the Sages wouldn't have to transport the tithes
Suggested Proof
A document of sale is acquired wherever it is when buyer takes the land — so property need not be on the land!
Gemara
Rejected: A document is the 'bridle of the land' (sirsur ha-karka) — it's part of the land itself
Final Proof
The Mishna equates documents with other movable property — 'no difference between a document and other movables'
Gemara
Accepted: We do NOT require that the property be piled on the land
Conclusion Movable property can be acquired through land even if it is not physically located on that land.

Three Types of Sale Documents (Rav Huna via Rav Ḥiyya bar Avin)

TypeSeller's StatementCan Retract Document?Can Retract Field?
Type 1"Acquire this field… and write him a document"✅ Yes❌ No — field already acquired
Type 2"Acquire this field on condition you write a document"✅ Yes✅ Yes — if document not yet given
Type 3Document written before the transaction❌ Document acquired automatically❌ Once buyer takes possession, both acquired
Key Distinction The word 'on condition' (al menat) makes the sale contingent on the document, allowing full retraction.

The Halakha of Kinyan Agav — Final Ruling

📦 Seller has movable property + land to transfer
↓
🗣️ Seller must say 'Acquire it' (kneh)
↓
🔗 Seller must say 'by means of' the land (agav)
↓
🏞️ Property does NOT need to be physically on the land
↓
✅ Buyer acquires both land and movable property together
Practical Rule Three requirements: (1) explicit 'Acquire,' (2) explicit 'by means of,' (3) land + movables transferred together. But no need for physical placement on the land.

Ten Fields in Ten Countries — Can One Act of Possession Acquire All?

Shmuel's View

  • Sold 10 fields in 10 countries
  • Takes possession of one → acquires all
  • Supported by baraita
  • Rationale: 'The mass of the earth is one'

Rav Aḥa's Analogy (Version 1)

  • 10 animals joined by one bridle (afsar)
  • Hand him the bridle → acquires all
  • Fields are like animals joined together
  • Supports Shmuel's position

Rav Aḥa's Analogy (Version 2)

  • 10 animals with one bridle
  • Say 'Acquire this one' → only acquires that one
  • Each field is like separate animal
  • Rejected: land is connected, animals are separate
Resolution The Gemara distinguishes: animals are separate entities, but land is fundamentally one continuous mass — acquiring one plot can transfer them all.

Gilgul Shevua — Extending Oaths (from Sota to Monetary Law)

Ulla asks: What is the Torah source for gilgul shevua (oath extension)?
↓
Sota says 'Amen, Amen' (Numbers 5:22) — double amen includes oath extension
↓
She swears about this man AND about another man; about betrothal period AND marriage
↓
But Sota involves prohibition (issur). How do we extend to monetary (mamon) matters?
↓
School of R' Yishmael: Kal va-chomer — if oath extends in the severe case of Sota, certainly in monetary cases
Application In the Mishna's case: partial admission on movable property forces an oath, which is then extended to cover the land claim as well.
