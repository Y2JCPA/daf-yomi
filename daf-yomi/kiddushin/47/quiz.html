QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, if a man says 'Be betrothed with these' dates and she eats them one by one, why is she still betrothed?
A. Because each date is worth a peruta
B. Because once she accepted them they became her property — she eats her own food
C. Because eating counts as an act of acquisition
D. Because the man explicitly transferred ownership by saying 'these'
Correct: B — Rava's principle is that 'with these' makes all items her property at once. When she eats them, she is eating her own food — the betrothal already took effect.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the key difference between saying 'with these' and 'with this, and this, and this' when she eats the items?
A. 'With these' requires a blessing; 'with this and this' does not
B. 'With these' combines their value; 'with this and this' requires each to independently be worth a peruta
C. 'With these' is valid only with three items; 'with this and this' works with any number
D. There is no practical difference according to the Gemara
Correct: B — 'With these' treats the items as one combined unit. 'With this and this' treats each as a separate betrothal attempt, so when she eats them, earlier ones become a loan and only the last must be worth a peruta.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rabba reject the view that the tanna'im disagree about accident liability for an unspent loan?
A. Because the Torah explicitly assigns liability to the lender
B. Because a loan is no worse than a borrowed item — and a borrower is always liable for accidents
C. Because accident liability only applies to real property, not money
D. Because both tanna'im explicitly agree on this point in the baraita
Correct: B — Rabba argues via kal va-chomer: if a borrower of an item (which is returned intact) bears accident liability, certainly a loan borrower (who spends the money and repays with different funds) must bear liability too.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why can't Rav Huna's case of the borrowed ax be mapped onto the loan dispute?
A. Because an ax is too cheap to serve as an example
B. Because a borrowed item is returned intact while a loan is repaid with other money — the disputes involve different principles
C. Because Rav Huna lived in a different generation than the tanna'im
D. Because the ax case involves real property while loans involve movable property
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes: a borrowed item (returned intact) maintains a connection to the owner, so everyone agrees use = acquisition. A loan (repaid with different money) involves a more complete transfer, which is the actual subject of the tannaic dispute.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the dispute about betrothal with a promissory note, what does the Gemara conclude the case must involve?
A. A promissory note from a third party who owes the man money
B. Her own promissory note — meaning he is forgiving her debt as betrothal money
C. A promissory note worth exactly one peruta
D. A promissory note that has already been paid
Correct: B — The Gemara reasons that if it were a third party's note, it would be identical to 'a loan in the possession of others' already stated in the baraita. Therefore it must be her own note — and the dispute is whether forgiving a loan counts as giving something of value.
