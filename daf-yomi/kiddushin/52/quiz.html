QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is Rava's opinion on 'betrothal not given to consummation' conclusively refuted?
A. The baraita of 5 sons and 5 daughters shows such betrothal is valid even when the parties were never identified
B. A verse explicitly states that all betrothals are valid
C. Abaye brought a logical proof from the laws of divorce
D. R' Yoḥanan ruled explicitly against Rava
Correct: A — The baraita teaches that when a father betroths 'one of your daughters to one of my sons' — where the couple was never identified and consummation is impossible — each daughter still needs bills of divorce, proving such betrothal is valid and refuting Rava.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the mnemonic YA"L KGA"M (יע"ל קג"ם) represent?
A. Six cases where the halakha follows Rava over Abaye
B. Six cases where the halakha follows Abaye over Rava
C. Six types of invalid betrothal
D. Six requirements for a valid bill of divorce
Correct: B — YA"L KGA"M lists six exceptional cases where the halakha follows Abaye in his disputes with Rava. Normally halakha follows Rava, but these six — including kiddushin not given to consummation — are exceptions.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Five Women mishna prove that betrothal with stolen property is invalid?
A. The mishna explicitly states 'stolen property cannot be used for betrothal'
B. The mishna specifies the figs were Shevi'it (ownerless), implying ordinary figs from her field would be theft and invalid
C. R' Yoḥanan states this principle directly
D. The sisters objected to the betrothal
Correct: B — The mishna emphasizes the produce was from 'their field' AND was 'of Shevi'it' — implying the reason betrothal worked is because Shevi'it produce is ownerless. In a non-Shevi'it year, taking from their field would be theft and betrothal would fail.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In Rava's ruling about the brewer who used beer sediment for betrothal, why doesn't the owner's comment ('give her the better stuff') count as consent?
A. The owner was not present when the betrothal occurred
B. Beer sediment has no monetary value
C. Unlike teruma (which the owner must anyway separate), betrothal gives the owner no benefit — his comment stems from embarrassment, not genuine consent
D. The woman had already refused the betrothal
Correct: C — The Gemara distinguishes teruma (where the owner is obligated to tithe anyway, so approval is plausible) from betrothal (where the owner gains nothing). The owner's polite comment about 'better quality' is attributed to embarrassment rather than true relinquishment of rights.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Meir in the mishna, if someone intentionally betroths a woman with hekdesh (consecrated Temple property), what is the result?
A. She is not betrothed, and he is liable for me'ilah
B. She is betrothed, because intentional use triggers desacralization (me'ilah)
C. She is betrothed only if the Temple treasurer consents
D. The betrothal is doubtful and requires a get
Correct: B — R' Meir holds that intentional misuse of hekdesh triggers me'ilah, which desacralizes the property retroactively. Once the item becomes profane (chulin), it has monetary value and the betrothal takes effect.
