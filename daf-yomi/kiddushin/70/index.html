Who Ascended from Babylonia? Scriptural Sources

GroupSourceProof
Converts & freed slavesEzra 6:21"All who separated from the impurity of the nations" — those who abandoned idolatry joined Ezra
MamzerimNehemiah 7:61"Tel Melah, Tel Harsha" — place names hinting at licentiousness and silenced paternity
FoundlingsNehemiah 7:61"Could not tell their fathers' houses nor their offspring" — gathered from the marketplace
Uncertain lineageNehemiah 2:19Tobiah the Ammonite married Jewish women — disputed whether offspring are mamzerim
Key Dispute The proof from Tobiah works only according to the opinion that a gentile's offspring with a Jewish woman is a mamzer. According to the view that status follows the mother, Tobiah's descendants would be of unflawed lineage.

Consequences of Marrying for Money or Unsuitable Lineage

💍 A man marries a woman of flawed lineage for money
↓
👶 His offspring will act inappropriately — "they have begotten strange children" (Hosea 5:7)
↓
💸 "The new moon shall devour them with their portions" — property lost within a single month
↓
📦 Not just his portion — "their portions" (plural) means her dowry is lost too
↓
⛓️ Elijah binds him and the Holy One straps him — as one liable to lashes
↓
📝 Elijah writes, God signs: "Woe to he who disqualifies his offspring and brings a flaw to his family"
Rav Hamnuna The imagery of Elijah binding and God strapping conveys that marrying for money while ignoring lineage carries both earthly and heavenly consequences.

Rav Yehuda vs. Rav Naḥman: The Neharde'a Confrontation

Rav Naḥman
Why did you excommunicate that man?
Rav Yehuda
He caused discomfort to a sage's agent — deserving the punishment for disrespecting a Torah scholar
Rav Naḥman
Then flog him, as Rav would do! Why excommunication?
Rav Yehuda
I punished him more severely — excommunication exceeds flogging
Rav Naḥman
And why did you proclaim him a slave?
Rav Yehuda
He habitually calls people slaves. Shmuel said: One who disqualifies others does so with his own flaw (b'mumo posel)
Rav Naḥman
Shmuel meant only to suspect him — not to publicly proclaim him a slave!
Shmuel's Principle כל הפוסל במומו פוסל — Anyone who disqualifies others does so with his own flaw. The accuser's charges reflect his own hidden blemish.

The Hasmonean Claim — Shmuel's Ruling

The Claim

  • The litigant declares: "I come from the house of the Hasmonean kings!"
  • He challenges Rav Yehuda's proclamation that he is a slave
  • Claims royal lineage as proof of status

Shmuel's Ruling

  • Anyone who claims Hasmonean descent is a slave
  • Only one free Hasmonean girl survived — she fell from a roof and died
  • All remaining 'Hasmoneans' descend from Herod's Edomite slaves

The Aftermath

  • Multiple marriage contracts torn up in Neharde'a
  • Residents pursued Rav Yehuda to stone him
  • He silenced them by threatening to reveal the 'dove's house' vs. 'raven's house' families
Historical Note The Hasmonean dynasty ended under Herod, who married into the family but was himself an Edomite slave. The last free Hasmonean — a young girl — chose death over capture.
