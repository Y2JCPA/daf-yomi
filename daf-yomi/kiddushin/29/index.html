Kiddushin 29: A Father's Duties

כל מצוות הבן על האב — אנשים חייבין ונשים פטורות
MISHNA & GEMARA The Mishna establishes a foundational framework: mitzvot incumbent on a father toward his son — circumcision, pidyon haben, Torah, marriage, and a trade — obligate men but not women. The Gemara explores priorities when resources are limited, the Torah sources for each obligation, and why women are exempt from both studying and teaching Torah.

Self-Redemption vs. Son's Redemption: Only 5 Sela

Setup
A father must redeem himself and his firstborn son, but has only 5 sela — enough for one.
All Agree
His own mitzva takes precedence — מצוה דידיה עדיפא.
R' Yehuda
A Torah-mandated debt is as if written in a document — he can collect from liened property for himself and use free assets for his son.
Rabbanan
A Torah-mandated debt is NOT like a documented loan — buyers don't know about it. He can only redeem himself.
KEY DISPUTE מלוה הכתובה בתורה — Is a Torah-based obligation considered as if documented for purposes of collecting from sold property?

The Mishna's Framework: Who Is Obligated in What?

Men Obligated, Women Exempt

  • Father's duties toward his son (circumcise, redeem, teach)
  • Positive time-bound mitzvot (מצוות עשה שהזמן גרמא)
  • Teaching Torah — derived from velimadtem

Both Men & Women Obligated

  • Mitzvot of a father upon his children (honor, fear)
  • Positive non-time-bound mitzvot
  • All negative commandments (with three exceptions)

Three Exceptions to Prohibitions

  • Do not round the corners of your head
  • Do not destroy the corners of your beard
  • Priests: Do not contract corpse impurity
SOURCE Leviticus 19:27 and 21:1 — these prohibitions apply only to men despite being negative commandments.

Why Are Women Exempt from Teaching Torah?

וְלִמַּדְתֶּם אֹתָם אֶת בְּנֵיכֶם
"And you shall teach them to your sons"
(Devarim 11:19)
↓
Reading #1: velimadtem
You shall teach — obligation to teach others
Reading #2: ulmadtem
You shall study — obligation to learn yourself
↓
"Your sons — not your daughters"
Others are not commanded to teach women Torah
↓
Whoever others are not commanded to teach
→ is not commanded to teach herself
→ is not commanded to teach her son
LOGIC CHAIN The Gemara links three exemptions: if women need not learn, they need not teach themselves, and therefore need not teach their sons.

Pilgrimage Festival vs. Pidyon HaBen

Scenario
A man has money for either redeeming his firstborn or ascending to Jerusalem for the רגל — not both.
Rabbanan
He redeems his son first, then goes up. The verse in Shemot 34:20 lists redemption before pilgrimage.
R' Yehuda
He ascends first — the רגל is a mitzva that passes (מצוה עוברת), while pidyon haben can be fulfilled later.
Rabbanan respond
The order of the verse is dispositive: "All the firstborn you shall redeem" comes before "none shall appear before Me empty."
PRINCIPLE Does a time-sensitive mitzva override one that can wait? Or does the Torah's textual order establish priority?
