Kiddushin 65: Disputed Betrothal & Witness Requirements

האומר לאשה קדשתיך והיא אומרת לא קדשתני
CORE THEME When betrothal is disputed, each party bears the consequences of their own claim: the one who claims betrothal is forbidden to the other's relatives. The daf then examines whether betrothal with one witness has any legal standing — with Rav ruling it does not, even if both parties concede.

Why Does the Mishna List All These Cases?

Questioner
Once we learn 'I betrothed you / You didn't' — the principle is clear. Why state more cases?
Gemara
Case 1 alone might suggest: a man isn't credible (he doesn't care what he says — he can marry another). But a woman who claims betrothal — since it forbids her to all others — perhaps she is more credible?
Gemara
The mother/daughter case is needed because you might think: since the Torah deems a father credible about his daughter's betrothal, perhaps the Sages made a mother credible by rabbinic law?
Mishna
No — each case teaches that only the claimant bears the restriction. A mother's claim does not bind her daughter.
KEY TEACHING A father has Torah-level credibility (ne'emanut) about his daughter's betrothal. A mother does not — not even rabbinically.

Witnesses of Seclusion: Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel

Beit Shammai

  • Witnesses of seclusion are NOT witnesses of intercourse
  • Seclusion alone does not create a presumption of intimacy
  • No second get required from seclusion alone

Beit Hillel

  • Witnesses of seclusion ARE treated as witnesses of intercourse
  • We presume intimacy occurred during seclusion
  • A second get IS required
  • Exception: Divorced from betrothal — since he is 'not accustomed to her,' no presumption of intercourse
AGREEMENT Beit Hillel concede to Beit Shammai: for a woman divorced from betrothal (not nisuin), seclusion does NOT imply intercourse — he is not accustomed to her.

Betrothal with One Witness — Is It Valid?

A man betroths a woman in the presence of only one witness
↓
Rav / Rav Kahana
🚫 No concern — betrothal has not taken effect, even if both parties concede
Rav Pappa
⚠️ Concern exists — the betrothal may have taken effect
SOURCE Rav Kahana derives from a gezeira shava: 'דבר' (matter) in Deut. 24:1 and Deut. 19:15 — betrothal, like monetary cases, requires two witnesses.

The Gezeira Shava Challenge: Admission of a Litigant

Rav Ashi
If you derive betrothal rules from monetary law via gezeira shava of 'דבר' — then just as in monetary law, admission of a litigant equals 100 witnesses — shouldn't mutual admission of betrothal also be valid?
Rav Kahana
There's a critical difference: in monetary matters, admission harms only oneself. But in betrothal, their mutual admission affects others — forbidding each other's relatives. Since third parties are impacted, personal admission is insufficient.
LEGAL DISTINCTION הודאת בעל דין (admission of a litigant) works in monetary law because only the admitter is affected. In betrothal, relatives become forbidden — so the admission impacts third parties and cannot stand alone.
