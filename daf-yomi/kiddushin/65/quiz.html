Kiddushin 65 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
If a man says 'I betrothed you' and the woman denies it, what is the halakha?
A. Both are forbidden to each other's relatives
B. He is forbidden to her relatives; she is permitted to his
C. She is forbidden to his relatives; he is permitted to hers
D. Neither is restricted since there is no proof
Correct: B — Each party bears the consequences of their own claim. Since he claims betrothal, he is forbidden to her relatives. Since she denies it, she remains permitted to his relatives.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Mishna need to state the case where the WOMAN claims betrothal (and he denies it), after already teaching the reverse?
A. To teach that women can initiate betrothal
B. Because you might think her claim is more credible since it restricts her to all other men
C. To show that betrothal requires mutual consent
D. Because women's testimony is always believed in betrothal matters
Correct: B — Since a woman who claims betrothal is restricting herself from marrying anyone else, you might think her claim must be true and should bind him as well. The Mishna teaches this is not so.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Gemara's conclusion, why does the mother's claim that 'You betrothed ME' not make the daughter forbidden?
A. Because a mother is never believed about her daughter's status
B. Because only a father has Torah-level credibility about his daughter's betrothal, and the Sages did not extend this to a mother
C. Because the daughter was not present during the alleged betrothal
D. Because the mother has a financial interest in claiming betrothal
Correct: B — The Torah explicitly grants a father ne'emanut (credibility) regarding his daughter's betrothal. You might think the Sages extended this rabbinically to a mother — the Mishna teaches they did not.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the halakha regarding betrothal performed with only one witness, according to Rav and Rav Kahana?
A. It is fully valid betrothal
B. It is valid only if both parties admit to it
C. There is no concern that it has taken effect, even if both parties concede
D. It is valid rabbinically but not by Torah law
Correct: C — Rav rules that betrothal with one witness has no legal standing whatsoever. Even if both the man and woman agree the betrothal occurred, it is not recognized.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why can't mutual admission ('admission of a litigant equals 100 witnesses') validate one-witness betrothal?
A. Because betrothal requires a physical kinyan that cannot be replaced by words
B. Because the admission affects third parties (relatives become forbidden), unlike monetary admission which harms only oneself
C. Because women cannot serve as witnesses to their own betrothal
D. Because the Torah explicitly requires three witnesses for betrothal
Correct: B — In monetary law, admission works because only the admitter is harmed. In betrothal, the admission renders each party's relatives forbidden — affecting innocent third parties. Therefore personal admission alone is insufficient.
