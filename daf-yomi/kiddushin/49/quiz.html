Kiddushin 49 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
In an ordinary document (שטר פשוט), where must the witnesses sign?
A. On the back of the document
B. Inside (on the written side)
C. On a separate sheet attached to it
D. Either side is acceptable
Correct: B — An ordinary document requires witnesses to sign inside, on the written side. A tied document has them sign on the back. Switching these invalidates the document.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Ulla, why does even R' Shimon agree that misleading with enhanced lineage invalidates betrothal?
A. Because lineage fraud is a Torah prohibition
B. Because the woman says: 'I don't want a shoe larger than my foot'
C. Because lineage cannot be verified later
D. Because the man acted deceitfully
Correct: B — Ulla explains that a woman doesn't want a husband of significantly higher social standing than herself — she would feel inadequate. This is expressed as 'a shoe larger than my foot.'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If a man says 'on condition I am righteous' but is completely wicked, what is the status of the betrothal?
A. Not betrothed — he lied about his condition
B. Betrothed — perhaps he had thoughts of repentance
C. Pending — requires investigation of his character
D. Betrothed only if he repents within 30 days
Correct: B — The Gemara rules she is betrothed because perhaps at that very moment he had thoughts of repentance (הרהורי תשובה) in his mind, which could transform his status.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the minimum standard for 'I am a student of Torah' as a betrothal condition?
A. He must be like Shimon ben Azzai or Shimon ben Zoma
B. He must know all six orders of Mishna
C. He can answer one question on any topic of his studies, even Tractate Kalla
D. He must have been ordained by a recognized sage
Correct: C — The Gemara sets a modest standard: anyone who can respond appropriately when asked about any topic in his studies — even if only in the minor Tractate Kalla — qualifies as a 'student.'
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Gemara's 'ten kav' list, which place received nine measures of wisdom?
A. Babylonia
B. Rome
C. Jerusalem
D. Eretz Yisrael
Correct: D — Nine of ten measures of wisdom descended to Eretz Yisrael. Jerusalem received nine of beauty. Rome received nine of wealth. Babylonia received nine of poverty.
