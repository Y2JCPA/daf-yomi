Do Conditions Require a Double Formulation (Tenai Kaful)?

R' Meir
Any condition that is not doubled — specifying both the result of fulfillment and non-fulfillment — is not a valid condition.
R' Meir
Proof: Moshe told Gad & Reuben — IF you cross the Jordan armed → you get Gilead. IF NOT → you inherit in Canaan. Both sides stated!
R' Chanina b. Gamliel
You cannot derive general rules from that case. The double formulation was necessary there for a different reason.
R' Chanina b. Gamliel
Without 'in the land of Canaan,' I'd think they only inherit in Gilead if they fail the condition — not in Canaan at all. It's not about doubling!
Core Dispute R' Meir: Every valid tenai must state both outcomes explicitly. R' Chanina ben Gamliel: The doubled language in Numbers was needed for substantive clarification, not as a formal requirement for all conditions.

Consecration vs. Sale vs. Betrothal Condition

Consecrated Field (Hekdesh)

  • Crevices ≥10 tefachim NOT measured
  • But only if filled with water (unfit for sowing)
  • If dry — could be sown, so included
  • Mar Ukva bar Ḥama's ruling

Sold Field (Sale)

  • Crevices ≥10 tefachim NOT measured
  • Even if NOT filled with water
  • Buyer doesn't want one field that looks like 2-3 parcels
  • Rav Pappa's reasoning

Betrothal Condition

  • Compared to consecration, not sale
  • Husband can say: I'll go to the trouble of sowing the difficult areas
  • He still possesses a beit kor
  • More lenient standard
Why the Difference? A buyer needs contiguous, usable land. But for hekdesh or kiddushin, the total area is what matters — even if farming it is inconvenient.

The Tenai Kaful Dispute: Gad & Reuben

R' Meir
Moshe's condition is the paradigm for all conditions: 'If they cross → Gilead. If not → Canaan.' Both sides must be stated.
R' Chanina b. Gamliel
The phrase 'in the land of Canaan' was substantively needed — without it, 'among you' might mean only Gilead!
R' Meir
'Among you' already means anywhere you have taken possession — including Canaan. So 'land of Canaan' is extra → teaches tenai kaful!
R' Chanina b. Gamliel
My parable proves it: A father divides property and conditions one son's share on payment. Without doubling, the son gets nothing if he fails to pay.
Halachic Stakes If R' Meir is right, any condition in kiddushin, sales, or other transactions that fails to state both outcomes is void — the act takes effect unconditionally.

R' Chanina ben Gamliel's Parable of the Father

Father divides property among his sons
↓
Son A → Field A
Son B → Field B
Son C → Field C if he pays 200 dinars
↓
Son C pays 200 dinars
Son C inherits Field C
Son C does NOT pay
Without double formulation: gets nothing
With double formulation: inherits with brothers from remainder
The Point R' Chanina ben Gamliel argues that the double formulation in the Gad/Reuben case was needed to grant them Canaan as a fallback — not to establish a general formal requirement for all conditions.

Biblical Proofs for Compound Conditions

🔥 Cain (Bereishit 4:7) — 'If you do well, it shall be lifted up; if not, sin crouches at the door'
↓
📖 R' Meir: This proves tenai kaful is required even by Hashem
↓
💬 R' Chanina b. Gamliel: Without doubling, we'd think no good deed = no punishment either. The verse teaches active punishment.
↓
💍 Avraham & Eliezer (Bereishit 24) — 'Take a wife for my son… if they will not give her, you are released from the oath'
↓
📖 R' Meir: Another proof that conditions need both sides stated
↓
💬 R' Chanina b. Gamliel: Without this, we'd think Eliezer must bring her even against her family's wishes. The verse teaches he need not.
Pattern For every biblical proof R' Meir cites, R' Chanina ben Gamliel shows the 'extra' clause was needed for a substantive teaching — not merely formal doubling.
