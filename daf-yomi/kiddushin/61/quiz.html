QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Mar Ukva bar Ḥama say crevices in a consecrated field are not measured — even though any size field can be consecrated?
A. Because they are filled with water and unfit for sowing
B. Because the owner did not intend to consecrate them
C. Because crevices cannot be redeemed at a fixed rate
D. Because they belong to a different halachic domain
Correct: A — Mar Ukva bar Ḥama explains that the crevices ≥10 tefachim are filled with water, making them unfit for sowing. Since the Torah's valuation is based on 'the sowing of a ḥomer of barley,' land that cannot be sown is excluded from measurement.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why are crevices in a SOLD field excluded even when not filled with water, unlike in a consecrated field?
A. The buyer has a higher standard of sanctity for his field
B. A buyer doesn't want to pay for one field that appears as two or three parcels
C. Sold fields can never include crevices by Torah law
D. The seller is obligated to fill in crevices before the sale
Correct: B — Rav Pappa explains that a buyer purchasing a beit kor of land expects a contiguous, easily farmable unit. Deep crevices or tall boulders make the field look like multiple separate parcels, which is unacceptable to the buyer regardless of water content.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Meir, what happens if someone makes a condition (tenai) without stating both the positive and negative outcomes?
A. The condition is valid but unenforceable
B. The condition is void — the act takes effect unconditionally
C. The condition is partially valid for the stated outcome only
D. The transaction is entirely nullified
Correct: B — R' Meir holds that a condition lacking the double formulation (tenai kaful) is not a valid condition at all. This means the underlying act (kiddushin, sale, etc.) takes effect unconditionally, as if no condition was ever stated.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does R' Chanina ben Gamliel explain the double formulation in God's words to Cain ('If you do well… if you do not do well…')?
A. It proves that tenai kaful is a Divine requirement
B. Without it, we'd think not doing well means no punishment either — just no reward
C. It shows that Cain's condition was uniquely doubled
D. It demonstrates that even God follows R' Meir's rule
Correct: B — R' Chanina ben Gamliel argues the second clause was substantively necessary: without 'if you do not do well, sin crouches at the door,' one might think that failing to do good simply means no reward — but no active punishment either. The doubling teaches that sin brings active consequences.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does the Gemara derive from the two separate clauses about consent in Avraham's instructions to Eliezer?
A. Eliezer needed permission from both Avraham and Sarah
B. Both the woman herself AND her family must consent to the marriage
C. Eliezer was free to use his own judgment about the match
D. The condition was only valid because it was doubled
Correct: B — The Gemara derives two separate consent requirements: 'If they will not give her' teaches that the family must agree, and 'If the woman is not willing to follow you' teaches that she herself must also consent. Eliezer may not bring Rivkah against either party's wishes.
