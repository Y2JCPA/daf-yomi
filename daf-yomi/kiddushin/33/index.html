The Obligation to Stand: Scope & Limits

"Before the hoary head you shall stand"
(Leviticus 19:32)
↓
Does standing require monetary loss?
No
Standing is juxtaposed to reverence — neither requires financial loss
Implication
Craftsmen engaged in work are exempt from standing before scholars
Core Principle The Torah balances honoring elders with protecting livelihoods — standing is mandatory only when it costs nothing.

Deriving the Standing Obligation

Leviticus 19:32: "Before the hoary head you shall stand and revere the face of an elder"
↓
"Elder" and "hoary head" are one concept — a Torah scholar (not merely old)
↓
Does reverence require giving money?
No
Standing = no monetary loss
∴ Reverence = no monetary loss
Further
Standing applies only without neglect of work
(e.g., piercing pearls)
Hekesh The verse juxtaposes standing and reverence — each limits the other, creating a mutual definition of honor without financial burden.

Craftsmen: Stand for Bikkurim but Not Scholars?

Gemara
We learned: Craftsmen engaged in work need not stand before Torah scholars
Challenger
But the Mishna (Bikkurim 3:3) says craftsmen do stand before those bringing first fruits!
R' Yochanan
Indeed — they stood for bikkurim bearers but not for Torah scholars
R' Yosei bar Avin
This shows how beloved is a mitzvah in its proper time (mitzvah b'sha'atah)!
Gemara
Or perhaps it's different — without this honor, people won't bring bikkurim at all. It's a practical enactment, not a general rule.
Takeaway The special honor for bikkurim may be pragmatic encouragement rather than proof that all timely mitzvot override work obligations.

Bathhouse: Inner vs. Outer Rooms

Inner Rooms

  • Everyone is unclothed
  • No obligation to stand
  • Standing bestows no honor in this setting
  • Torah contemplation is forbidden

Outer Rooms

  • People are still dressed
  • Obligation to stand applies
  • R' Ḥiyya was criticized for not standing here
  • R' Yehuda HaNasi expected honor in this zone
Key Distinction The verse says "you shall stand and revere" — standing is required only in a place where reverence is meaningful.

How Often Must a Student Stand?

PositionFrequencyReasoning
Initial viewEvery time the teacher passesFull obligation of honor
Limiting viewOnce morning, once eveningTeacher's honor should not exceed honor of Heaven (Shema frequency)
R' Shimon ben ElazarElder should not trouble others"An elder, and you shall fear" — the elder too must fear God
ResolutionTwice daily is the obligationBut a considerate elder minimizes impositions
R' Elazar's Warning A scholar who does not stand before his teacher is called wicked, will not live long, and his Torah will be forgotten (Ecclesiastes 8:13).

Must a Son Who Teaches Stand Before His Father?

Shmuel
To Rav Yehuda: "Big-toothed one, stand before your father!"
Gemara
But Rav Yeḥezkel was special — a man of great deeds. Even Shmuel himself stood for him!
Gemara
Then what was Shmuel telling Rav Yehuda?
Resolution
"Sometimes your father comes from behind me and I don't see him — you should stand, and don't worry about my honor"
Practical Ruling Shmuel's instruction was about not letting deference to one's rebbe prevent honoring one's father.
