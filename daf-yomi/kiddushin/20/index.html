Can a Father Sell His Daughter to Relatives?

R' Eliezer
The verse says "as a maidservant" — she can be sold to all relatives, even those she cannot marry.
Rabbis
He cannot sell her to relatives — only where designation (yi'ud) is possible.
Baraita 1
He can sell her to his father (grandfather can designate her to his other son), but not to his son.
Baraita 2
He cannot sell her to his father nor to his son.
Gemara
Baraita 1 follows the Rabbis — they concede where designation is possible via another son. Baraita 2 is a stricter reading of the Rabbis.
Resolution The Rabbis agree sale to the grandfather is valid because he could designate her to his other son (her uncle) — a possibility of yi'ud exists.

"If He Comes In Begappo" — Two Readings

Rava: With His Body

  • "Begappo" = begufo (with his body)
  • A Hebrew slave is NOT freed through loss of extremities
  • Unlike a Canaanite slave who goes free if master knocks out his eye
  • Master must pay compensation but slave stays

R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov: Alone

  • "Begappo" = alone
  • If he enters with wife & children → master may give him a Canaanite maidservant
  • If he enters alone → master may NOT give him a Canaanite maidservant
  • Marital status at purchase determines rights
Abaye's Challenge Abaye objected: We already know from "She shall not go out as the men slaves do" (Exodus 21:7) that Hebrew slaves aren't freed by injury! Rava answers: Without the extra verse, we'd think the master pays compensation AND the slave goes free.

Partial Redemption: The Dilemma

ScenarioIf Partial Redemption AllowedIf NOT Allowed
Slave bought for 100; redeemer pays 50; value rises to 200Slave is half-redeemed → redeemer pays only 100 more (half of 200) → Total: 15050 is just a payment on account → redeemer pays 150 more (200 – 50) → Total: 200
Result for redeemer✅ Leniency — pays less total❌ Stringency — pays more total
Analogy to ancestral fieldN/A"Ge'ullato" = full redemption only, like a field
Analogy to thief soldN/A"He is sold" = all of him, not part
Rav Sheshet's Ruling Just as a thief is sold entirely — "all of him and not part" — so too a slave sold to a gentile: "he is redeemed" means fully redeemed, not partially.
