The Extra Finger: When Does Removal Emancipate?

Slave has six fingers on one hand
↓
Master severs the extra finger
↓
Can the finger be counted along the back of the hand?
Yes — in line
Classified as a finger
Slave is emancipated
No — protrudes elsewhere
Classified as a mere growth
Slave is not freed
RAV HUNA The determining factor is position: the extra finger must be on the same line as the other five to count as a true limb.

The Elders of Nezonya vs. Rav Hamnuna

Rav Ḥisda
Go and ostracize the Elders of Nezonya — they disrespect the Sages by not attending my lecture.
Elders of Nezonya
Why should we come? We asked Rav Ḥisda a question and he couldn't resolve it!
Rav Hamnuna
Ask me — I'll resolve anything you raise!
Elders of Nezonya
If a master castrates his slave's testicles — is the slave emancipated?
Rav Hamnuna
(Unable to answer)
Elders of Nezonya
You're not Hamnuna (hot fish) — you're Karnuna (cold fish)!
NARRATIVE Rav Hamnuna returns to Rav Ḥisda, who resolves the dilemma from a baraita about 24 extremities — a teaching Rav Hamnuna should have known.

Is the Tongue an 'Exposed' Limb?

Ben Azzai
The tongue is exposed — it is visible when one speaks. Severing it frees the slave.
R' Yehuda HaNasi
I include castration but do not need to list the tongue separately.
Gemara
But Rabbi — you rule that sprinkling purification water on one's mouth is valid! Doesn't that prove the tongue is exposed?
Gemara (answer)
That ruling refers to the lips, not the tongue.
Gemara (challenge)
But a baraita explicitly says sprinkling on the tongue is valid per Rabbi!
Resolution
Rabbi agrees the tongue is exposed — his statement means castration is included and it goes without saying for the tongue.
KEY SHIFT Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi doesn't exclude the tongue — he considers it obvious. His chiddush is that castration of the testicles also emancipates.

Mishna: Acquiring Large vs. Small Animals

Large Animal (בהמה גסה)

  • Acquired by passing (מסירה)
  • Transfer of reins or bit
  • R' Meir & R' Eliezer's view
  • Rav initially said pulling, then retracted

Small Animal (בהמה דקה)

  • Acquired by lifting (הגבהה)
  • R' Meir & R' Eliezer's view
  • Rabbis: also by pulling (משיכה)
  • R' Shimon: both types only by lifting
BARAITA The Rabbis say both large and small animals are acquired by pulling (meshikha). Rav ultimately adopted this view.
