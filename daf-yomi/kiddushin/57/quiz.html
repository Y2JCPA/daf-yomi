Kiddushin 57 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Shimon HaAmasoni stop interpreting the word 'et' throughout the Torah?
A. He found a verse where 'et' contradicted another halakha
B. He reached 'et Hashem' and could not find what to include alongside fear of God
C. Rabbi Akiva proved his method was wrong
D. The Sanhedrin ruled against his method
Correct: B — When Shimon HaAmasoni reached 'Et Hashem Elokekha tira,' he could not imagine whose fear could be added to fear of God, so he abandoned the entire method. Rabbi Akiva later resolved it by including Torah scholars.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the School of R' Yishmael derive that leper's birds are forbidden for benefit?
A. From the word 'et' in the verse about leprosy
B. From a gezeirah shavah with sacrificial birds
C. By comparing enabling/atoning rites inside and outside the Temple
D. From the three repetitions of the purification verse
Correct: C — R' Yishmael's school compares the paired enabling/atoning rites: inside the Temple (guilt-offering/offerings) and outside (leper's birds/eglah arufah). Since inside both are forbidden, outside both are forbidden too.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the core disagreement between R' Yochanan and Reish Lakish regarding leper's birds?
A. Whether the freed bird or slaughtered bird is forbidden
B. Whether the prohibition begins at slaughter or at the moment they are taken
C. Whether the birds must be from a specific species
D. Whether the prohibition applies to both birds or only one
Correct: B — R' Yochanan holds the prohibition begins at slaughter (the ritual act), while Reish Lakish holds it begins when the birds are taken/designated, derived from the eglah arufah which is forbidden during its lifetime.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why can't nazirite's hair be redeemed (transferred to money) like regular consecrated property?
A. Because the Torah explicitly forbids redeeming it
B. Because the verse uses 'kadosh' (holy) rather than 'kodesh' (consecrated property)
C. Because hair has no inherent monetary value
D. Because the nazirite vow cannot be annulled
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes between 'kadosh' used for nazirite hair and 'kodesh' used for consecrated Temple property. The different conjugation means the redemption rules of hekdesh don't apply to nazirite hair.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the School of R' Yishmael, why is the prohibition 'You shall not cook a kid in its mother's milk' written three times?
A. Once for cattle, once for sheep, once for goats
B. Once for eating, once for benefit, once for cooking
C. Once for the kid, once for the milk, once for the mixture
D. Once for Jews, once for converts, once for residents
Correct: B — The School of R' Yishmael derives three separate prohibitions from the three repetitions: against eating meat in milk, against deriving benefit from it, and against cooking it — establishing the comprehensive prohibition that became normative halakha.
