When Does Conditional Betrothal Work?

A man states a betrothal tied to a future condition
↓
Can he fulfill it now?
e.g., she exists, is eligible, no third party needed → Betrothed
Cannot fulfill now?
e.g., needs conversion, emancipation, husband's death → Not Betrothed
Key Principle A betrothal conditioned on a future event the man cannot currently perform is invalid — the act must be in his power (בידו) at the time of the statement.

R' Meir vs. R' Chanina ben Gamliel: Must Conditions State Both Sides?

R' Meir
A condition must state both the positive and the negative — like the condition of Bnei Gad and Bnei Reuven.
R' Chanina b"G
The negative is implied — stating one side is sufficient.
R' Meir
The sota verse hinnaki is written without a yod — hinting at both meanings: be free and be choked.
R' Tanḥum
Indeed, the truncated spelling supports R' Meir — it alludes to both outcomes.
R' Chanina b"G
The unusual spelling is still needed — to teach it's not merely a prohibition but carries severe punishment.
Application This debate about double formulations informs how we read conditional betrothal — must both outcomes be spelled out?

"If Your Wife Gives Birth to a Girl — She Is Betrothed to Me"

Man says to another: "If your wife bears a daughter, she is betrothed to me"
↓
Wife not visibly pregnant
The girl does not yet exist in any recognizable form → Not Betrothed
Pregnancy is discernible
The fetus is already recognizable → Betrothed if a girl is born
R' Chanina The mishna's rule that it's invalid applies only when the wife is not pregnant. If she is visibly pregnant, the unborn child is considered sufficiently 'in the world.'

Can 'In His Power' (בידו) Validate Conditional Betrothal?

R' Yochanan
Teruma separated from detached for attached produce is valid — because he has the power to detach it. The same logic applies: if it's in his power, the act works.
R' Abba bar Memel
Objection! A master who gives a peruta to his Canaanite maidservant and says 'be betrothed after I free you' — he has the power, yet it shouldn't work!
Gemara
Different case — a maidservant has the status of property, and after emancipation she becomes a new person. The betrothal is to someone who doesn't yet exist.
R' Oshaya
What about giving a peruta to your own wife saying 'be betrothed after I divorce you'?
Gemara
Even though divorce is in his power, betrothal requires her consent — once divorced, the matter is no longer exclusively his.
Principle בידו (in his power) validates only when the entire act depends solely on him — if a third party's consent is needed after the condition is met, it fails.

Rabba vs. Rav Yosef: How Far Does R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov Go?

Rabba

  • R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov's leniency applies only to fodder — produce that has already grown stalks
  • Soft, unripe produce (agam) is too undeveloped to be designated
  • Applied to pregnancy: only when the fetus is discernible

Rav Yosef

  • R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov extends even to soft, early-stage produce (agam)
  • As long as it exists in some form, it can be designated
  • Applied to pregnancy: even when the fetus is not yet noticeable
Agam (אגם) R' Elazar derives the meaning from Isaiah 58:5 — "Is it to bow down his head ke'agmon" — like a soft, drooping plant.
