Three Methods of Acquiring Land (Kinyanei Karka)

Mishna: Property that serves as a guarantee (nekhasim she'yesh lahem achrayut) = land
↓
💰 Money (Kesef)
Derived from Jeremiah 32:44
"They shall acquire fields with money"
📜 Document (Shtar)
Derived from Jeremiah 32:11
"I took the deed of purchase"
🏠 Possession (Chazakah)
Physical use of the land
(fencing, locking, breaching)
Key Rule Movable property (metaltelin) is acquired only by pulling — but can be acquired together with land (agav) using any of the three methods.

Does Money Alone Acquire Land?

Ḥizkiyya
Land can be acquired by money — as it says: "They shall acquire fields with money" (Jeremiah 32:44)
Gemara
But the same verse says "and write a document and sign" — maybe you need both money AND a document?
Ḥizkiyya
"They shall acquire" appears at the beginning of the verse — teaching that money itself effects acquisition. The document is merely proof.
Rav
Money alone works only where the custom is NOT to write documents. Where they DO write documents — no acquisition until the document is given!
Rav
But if the buyer stipulates he wants to acquire from the time of the money transfer — then money suffices even where documents are customary.
Practical Tip Rav Idi bar Avin would always stipulate: "If I wish to acquire by money, I will; if by document, I will" — reserving the right to choose how the transaction is finalized.

Does a Document Alone Effect Acquisition?

Gemara
We already said the document in Jeremiah 32:44 is merely proof. So where do we derive that a document can effect acquisition?
Gemara
From Jeremiah 32:11 — "And I took the deed of purchase" — the document itself effects the acquisition.
Shmuel
A document effects acquisition only for a gift. For a sale, the buyer must still give money — the document alone is not enough.
Rav Hamnuna
Objection! A baraita says: If he wrote "My field is sold to you" on paper — even paper worth less than a peruta — it is sold! This proves a document works for sales too!
Rav Hamnuna
He resolved it himself: The baraita refers to one selling a field due to its poor quality — the seller wants to be rid of it quickly.
Rav Ashi
The entire baraita refers to a gift. The sale language is used to enhance the recipient's power — so he can collect from the giver's other property if there's a lien.
Rav Ashi's Insight Writing sale language in a gift deed grants the recipient a buyer's collection rights while still allowing the document alone to complete the acquisition (as it would for a gift).

Acquiring Movable Property Agav (Together With) Land

A person wants to transfer movable property without the recipient physically pulling it
↓
Transfer land to the recipient using money, document, or possession
↓
State that the movable property is transferred together with (agav) the land
↓
❓ Unresolved Question
Must the movable property be physically piled on the land?
✅ Established
Even tiny land (e.g. beit sela) can bring along large amounts of movable property
Practical Use This method was crucial for sick people on their deathbed who needed to transfer large amounts of property quickly without the recipient being present to perform pulling.

The Prosbol and Tiny Land — How Small Can Agav Land Be?

Rav Shmuel bar Bisna

  • Even land of 'any area' works for agav
  • Example: Stick a needle into a tiny plot of land
  • The needle is acquired along with the land
  • Shows agav works with minimal land

Rav Yosef's Critique

  • "You disgust me!" (kevastan)
  • Did the Tanna go to all that trouble to teach about a needle?
  • The teaching must have broader application

Rav Ashi's Resolution

  • Who says the needle didn't have a pearl worth 1,000 dinars hanging on it?
  • Tiny land can transfer items of enormous value
  • The size of the land doesn't limit the value transferred
Key Point The Gemara establishes that even the smallest plot of land can serve as a vehicle for acquiring movable property of any value through agav.
