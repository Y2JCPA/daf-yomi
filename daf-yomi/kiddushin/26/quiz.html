Kiddushin 26 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, which methods can be used to acquire land (nekhasim she'yesh lahem achrayut)?
A. Only money
B. Money, document, or possession
C. Only pulling (meshikhah)
D. Money and document only
Correct: B — The Mishna explicitly states that property that serves as a guarantee (land) can be acquired by money (kesef), document (shtar), or taking possession (chazakah).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav, when does money alone NOT suffice to acquire land?
A. When the land is worth more than 200 zuz
B. When the seller is a minor
C. In a place where the custom is to write documents
D. When there are no witnesses present
Correct: C — Rav teaches that money alone works only where the custom is NOT to write documents. Where documents are customary, no acquisition occurs until the document is given — unless the buyer explicitly stipulates otherwise.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rav Ashi say the baraita about 'My field is sold to you' actually refers to a gift?
A. Because the Tanna made an error
B. To enhance the recipient's power to collect from liened property
C. Because sales require three witnesses
D. Because the paper was worth less than a peruta
Correct: B — Rav Ashi explains that sale language is used in a gift deed to grant the recipient the stronger collection rights of a buyer — the ability to collect from the giver's other property if a lien is discovered — while still allowing the document alone to effect acquisition.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the story of the Madonite, why didn't the Sages suggest transferring property through a third party pulling on the recipient's behalf?
A. It was Shabbat and pulling was forbidden
B. The giver feared the third party might seize the property
C. Third-party acquisition was not yet established in halakha
D. The movable property was too heavy to pull
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the giver did not rely on a third party because he feared the intermediary might seize and consume the property. Given this concern, his only remedy was agav — transferring movable property by means of land.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What key question about kinyan agav (acquiring movable property with land) remains unresolved on this daf?
A. Whether agav works for gifts or only sales
B. Whether the land must be in Eretz Yisrael
C. Whether the movable property must be physically piled on the land
D. Whether agav requires witnesses
Correct: C — The Gemara brings multiple attempted proofs about whether movable property must be physically placed (tzavurin) on the land for agav to work, but each proof is deflected. The question remains unresolved (lo ipshetah).
