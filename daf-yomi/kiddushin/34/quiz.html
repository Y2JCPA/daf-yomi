Kiddushin 34 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why can't women's exemption from positive, time-bound mitzvot be derived from the mitzva of simcha (Festival joy), in which women appear to be obligated?
A. Women's obligation in simcha is only indirect — through husband or community
B. Simcha is not actually a time-bound mitzva
C. Simcha applies only to Kohanim
D. The Torah never explicitly mentions women regarding simcha
Correct: A — Abaye explains that women are not directly obligated in simcha. A married woman is gladdened by her husband, and a widow by those around her. Since the obligation is indirect, simcha cannot serve as a source to obligate women in all time-bound mitzvot.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara prefer to juxtapose tefillin with Torah study rather than with mezuza?
A. Mezuza is a Rabbinic obligation, not Biblical
B. Tefillin and Torah study are juxtaposed in both paragraphs of Shema, while tefillin and mezuza are juxtaposed only in the second
C. Torah study is more important than mezuza
D. Tefillin and mezuza are never mentioned together
Correct: B — The double juxtaposition of tefillin and Torah study (in both the first and second paragraphs of Shema) creates a stronger textual link than the single juxtaposition of tefillin with mezuza (only in the second paragraph).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the principle of שני כתובים הבאים כאחד (two verses that come as one)?
A. Two verses teaching the same law reinforce each other and establish strong precedent
B. Two verses teaching the same lesson are treated as exceptions and do NOT set precedent for other cases
C. Two adjacent verses must always be read together as a single unit
D. When two verses contradict, the later one overrides
Correct: B — When two separate verses teach the same halakhic principle, they are considered redundant. The principle states that such cases are treated as exceptions rather than establishing a general rule that can be applied elsewhere.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Torah need the word "ha-ezrach" to specifically exempt women from sukka, if they are already generally exempt from time-bound mitzvot?
A. To teach that converts are also exempt from sukka
B. Because a gezera shava from Pesach (both say '15th day') might otherwise obligate women, just as they are obligated in matza
C. Because sukka is actually not a time-bound mitzva
D. To exempt women from building the sukka but not from eating in it
Correct: B — Rava explains that without the explicit exclusion, one might derive a verbal analogy between Sukkot and Pesach (both mention 'the 15th day'). Since women are obligated in matza on Pesach, this analogy would obligate them in sukka as well. The explicit verse is needed to prevent this derivation.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Yochanan, what is the significance of general rules (כללות) stated in the Mishna?
A. They are absolute and binding with no exceptions
B. They apply only to men, never to women
C. One cannot derive practical halakhot from them — they are not all-inclusive even when they state exceptions
D. They are mere suggestions that individual communities may follow or ignore
Correct: C — R' Yochanan teaches that general statements in the Mishna — even those that explicitly list exceptions using the word 'except' — should not be understood as comprehensive. The proof is from the eiruv rule that says 'all foods except water and salt,' yet truffles and mushrooms also cannot be used.
