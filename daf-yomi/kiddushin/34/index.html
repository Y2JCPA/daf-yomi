Women & Positive Time-Bound Mitzvot: The Principle and Its Limits

General Principle: Women are exempt from positive, time-bound mitzvot (מצוות עשה שהזמן גרמא)
↓
Source: Derived from tefillin → linked to Torah study (both exempt women)
↓
But are there exceptions?
Women ARE obligated
Matza, Simcha, Hakhel — time-bound yet obligatory
Women ARE exempt
Torah study, procreation, pidyon haben — NOT time-bound yet exempt
↓
R' Yochanan: One does not learn halakha from general statements — even with stated exceptions
Key Insight The Mishna's categories are guidelines, not absolute rules. Each mitzva's obligation requires its own textual derivation.

Categorizing Mitzvot: Time-Bound vs. Not Time-Bound

Positive Time-Bound (Women Exempt)

  • 🏗️ Sukka — 7 specific days per year
  • 🌿 Lulav — during Sukkot
  • 📯 Shofar — Rosh Hashana
  • 📿 Tefillin — not Shabbat/night

Positive Non-Time-Bound (Women Obligated)

  • 🚪 Mezuza — always applies
  • 🏠 Ma'akeh (parapet) — permanent obligation
  • 📦 Returning lost items — whenever found
  • 🐦 Shiluach haken — whenever encountered
Note This clean division is the Mishna's framework, but the Gemara immediately challenges it with exceptions on both sides.

How Do We Derive Women's Exemption from Tefillin?

Torah Study (Deut. 11:19)
"ולמדתם אותם את בניכם" — your sons, not daughters → Women exempt
↓
Tefillin is juxtaposed to Torah study in both paragraphs of Shema (Deut. 6:7–8 and 11:18–19)
↓
∴ Women are exempt from tefillin
↓
But why not compare tefillin to mezuza (also in the passage)?
Tefillin ↔ Torah Study
Juxtaposed in both paragraphs of Shema → stronger link
Tefillin ↔ Mezuza
Juxtaposed only in second paragraph → weaker link
Follow-Up The Gemara asks: if so, derive that women are exempt from mezuza too! Answer: Mezuza promises long life — surely women also need life!

Why Does the Torah Need a Special Verse to Exempt Women from Sukka?

Gemara
The verse says "ha-ezrach" (the homeborn) in Lev. 23:42 to exclude women from sukka. But if women are already exempt from all time-bound mitzvot, why is this verse needed?
Abaye
Without this verse, I'd say: "teshvu k'ein taduru" — reside in the sukka as you live at home. Just as husband and wife dwell together, both should be obligated in sukka!
Rava
Without the verse, I'd derive a gezera shava: Sukkot says "the 15th of the 7th month" and Pesach says "the 15th of this month." Since women are obligated in matza, they should be obligated in sukka too!
Gemara
The same logic applies to re'iyah (pilgrimage appearance). The Torah specifies "your males" (Ex. 23:17) — because without it, we'd derive from hakhel that women must appear too.
Principle Each specific exclusion in the Torah is necessary to counter a potential gezera shava (verbal analogy) that would otherwise obligate women.

The Simcha Question: Are Women Directly Obligated?

אשה — בעלה משמחה (קידושין לד:)
Abaye's Teaching Women are not directly obligated in simcha (Festival joy). Rather, a married woman is rendered joyful by her husband — the obligation falls on him.

The Gemara challenges: What about a widow? She has no husband!

Answer: The obligation falls on those present with her — they must ensure she rejoices on the Festival.

This means simcha cannot serve as a source to obligate women in all time-bound mitzvot, since women's obligation in simcha is only indirect.
