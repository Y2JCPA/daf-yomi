QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav's explanation, why does Rabbi Meir treat priestly tunics differently from other consecrated property?
A. Because priestly tunics were never truly consecrated
B. Because priests were given permission to benefit from them incidentally — 'Torah was not given to angels'
C. Because the tunics belong to individual priests, not the Temple
D. Because garments are a lower category of sanctity than money
Correct: B — Rav explains that priestly garments were given on condition that priests may benefit from them even outside the service, since it's impossible to wear them only at the exact moment of service. This 'Torah not given to angels' principle means only intentional desacralization is effective.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the contradiction between Rabbi Meir's position and the teaching that one is liable for misuse of old shekels from the chamber remainder?
A. It distinguishes between shekels used for offerings and shekels used for maintenance
B. It says the old shekels lost their sanctity over time
C. It emends the teaching — attributing the chamber remainder view to Rabbi Yehuda, not Rabbi Meir
D. It says the chamber remainder is a rabbinic, not biblical, category of sanctity
Correct: C — The Gemara resolves the contradiction by textual emendation (אימא ר' יהודה), re-attributing the statement about liability for the chamber remainder from Rabbi Meir to Rabbi Yehuda.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the key difference between Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel regarding the fourth-year vineyard?
A. Beit Shammai say it must be eaten in Jerusalem; Beit Hillel say it can be redeemed
B. Beit Hillel derive a verbal analogy (gezerah shavah) from 'holy' linking it to second tithe; Beit Shammai reject this analogy
C. Beit Shammai say the fruit is forbidden; Beit Hillel say it is permitted
D. Beit Hillel require the fruit to be destroyed; Beit Shammai allow it to be eaten
Correct: B — Beit Hillel use the gezerah shavah of 'kodesh' (holy) from Leviticus 19:24 and 27:30 to apply second-tithe rules (adding one-fifth, disposal) to the fourth-year vineyard. Beit Shammai reject this verbal analogy.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Ravin's corrected version of bar Padda's teaching, when is one who unwittingly misuses hekdesh liable to bring an offering?
A. Whenever the misuse involves Temple property worth more than a perutah
B. Only when one ate consecrated food (consuming it completely), not when one merely used consecrated money
C. Only when the misuse was in the Temple itself
D. Whenever the property was desacralized by the misuse
Correct: B — Ravin distinguishes: if one ate consecrated food unwittingly (the food is consumed and gone), one is liable for an offering. But if one merely used consecrated money, it retains its sanctity and one is not liable.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara conclude that Beit Hillel's position on the fourth-year vineyard serves as an unattributed mishna supporting Rabbi Meir?
A. Because Beit Hillel explicitly cite Rabbi Meir as their source
B. Because Beit Hillel's view that all fruit goes to the winepress (no peret/olelot) implies the grapes are consecrated — matching Rabbi Meir's view that second tithe is sacred property
C. Because Beit Hillel always follow Rabbi Meir's opinions
D. Because the mishna was written after Rabbi Meir's time and incorporated his rulings
Correct: B — Beit Hillel say all the fruit goes to the winepress with no obligation to leave gifts for the poor, implying the grapes have sanctity. Since they equate fourth-year produce with second tithe, this aligns with Rabbi Meir's view that second tithe is consecrated — and since halakha follows Beit Hillel, this functions as an unattributed mishna supporting Rabbi Meir.
