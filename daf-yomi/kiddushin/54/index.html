Rabbi Meir on Consecrated Property: Intentional vs. Unwitting Misuse

Core Question: Is consecrated property (hekdesh) desacralized when misused?
↓
Intentional Misuse
Property is desacralized — R' Meir
Unwitting Misuse
Property not desacralized — R' Meir
↓
Halakha follows R' Meir on second tithe (= consecrated)

Key Principle The Gemara tests Rabbi Meir's position through priestly garments, shekels, and Jerusalem's walls — ultimately resolving the halakha via unattributed mishnayot.

Does Misuse Desacralize Hekdesh?

Rav
R' Meir holds: consecrated property is not desacralized if misused unwittingly, but is desacralized if misused intentionally.
Gemara
We reviewed all of R' Meir's statements — we never found him holding this position about hekdesh in general!
Rav
The mishna refers specifically to priestly tunics (bigdei kehuna) that have not worn out and are still fit for service.
Rav
These garments were given on condition that priests may benefit from them — because the Torah was not given to ministering angels (lo nitnah Torah l'malakhei ha-shareit).
Gemara
So only intentional desacralization works for these garments, since the priests already have permitted use of them.
Principle לא ניתנה תורה למלאכי השרת — The Torah was not given to ministering angels. Priests cannot avoid all incidental benefit from their garments.

Testing R' Meir: Old Shekels & the Chamber Remainder

Challenge: R' Meir says one is liable for misuse of old shekels (remainder of the chamber)
↓
But the remainder funds city walls & towers — and passersby cannot avoid benefiting from their shade!
↓
By R' Meir's own logic (Torah not given to angels), there should be no liability!
↓
Resolution: Emend the mishna — the view about the chamber remainder is R' Yehuda's, not R' Meir's
Textual Emendation The Gemara resolves the contradiction by re-attributing the statement about old shekels from R' Meir to R' Yehuda.

Is Jerusalem Itself Consecrated?

R' Yishmael b"R Yitzchak
Stones from Jerusalem's walls and towers that fell — one is liable for misuse of hekdesh by using them. This is R' Meir's view.
Gemara
Emend: say this is R' Yehuda's view, not R' Meir's.
Gemara
But wait — does R' Yehuda hold Jerusalem is consecrated at all? In Nedarim he says: one who says 'like Jerusalem' has said nothing!
Gemara
Answer: Two tanna'im disagree about what R' Yehuda actually holds regarding Jerusalem's sanctity.
Resolution The tension between different sources attributed to R' Yehuda is resolved by positing a tannaitic dispute about his true position on Jerusalem's consecration.

Fourth-Year Vineyard: Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel

Beit Shammai

  • No adding one-fifth (חומש) when redeeming
  • No obligation of disposal (ביעור) in 4th year of shemitah cycle
  • Owner MUST leave פרט (fallen grapes) and עוללות (incomplete clusters) for the poor
  • The vineyard is considered the owner's property

Beit Hillel

  • Adding one-fifth DOES apply (derived from second tithe via גזירה שווה of 'holy')
  • Disposal obligation DOES apply
  • ALL fruit goes to the winepress — no פרט or עוללות
  • Grapes have sanctity like second tithe — no gifts to the poor
Gezerah Shavah Beit Hillel derive from קדש (Lev. 19:24) and קדש (Lev. 27:30): just as second tithe has one-fifth and disposal, so does the fourth-year vineyard. Beit Shammai reject this verbal analogy.
