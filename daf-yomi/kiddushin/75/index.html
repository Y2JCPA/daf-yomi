Whose Widow May You Marry?

Principle: "Anyone whose daughter you may marry, you may marry his widow"
↓
All Tannaim agree on the rule — but differ on the derivation from High Priest + widow
First Tanna
Intercourse via transgression → disqualifies her from priesthood
R' Yosei
Offspring unfit → disqualifies her from priesthood
R' Shimon b. Gamliel
All offspring unfit (male + female) → disqualifies
Key Principle Each Tanna derives the same rule from the High Priest/widow case but applies a different criterion — transgression, unfit offspring, or universally unfit offspring — yielding different practical rulings.

What's the Difference Between First Tanna and R' Yosei?

Gemara
They both say the same thing: whose daughter you may marry → you may marry his widow!
R' Yochanan
The practical difference is a Jewish woman who had relations with a second-generation Egyptian convert (mitzri sheni).
First Tanna
Like a High Priest with a widow — his intercourse is a transgression, so he disqualifies her. Same for the Egyptian — his intercourse is forbidden, so she's disqualified from priesthood.
R' Yosei
Like a High Priest with a widow — the offspring is unfit (ḥalal), so he disqualifies her. But a 2nd-gen Egyptian's offspring is fit (3rd generation may enter the congregation) — so he does not disqualify her.
Practical Outcome A Jewish woman who had relations with a second-generation Egyptian convert: First Tanna says she is disqualified from priesthood; R' Yosei says she is not.

Three Tannaim: The High Priest/Widow Derivation

First Tanna

  • Focus: Transgression
  • High Priest + widow = forbidden act → disqualifies
  • Any forbidden intercourse disqualifies
  • Includes: 2nd-gen Egyptian ✗

R' Yosei

  • Focus: Offspring unfit
  • High Priest + widow = offspring is ḥalal → disqualifies
  • Only if offspring would be unfit
  • Excludes: 2nd-gen Egyptian ✓
  • Includes: Ammonite/Moabite ✗

R' Shimon b. Gamliel

  • Focus: All offspring unfit
  • High Priest + widow = all offspring (M+F) unfit → disqualifies
  • Only if both sons AND daughters unfit
  • Excludes: Ammonite/Moabite ✓ (females permitted)
Key Verse Ammonite/Moabite: "An Ammonite man shall not enter the congregation" — but not an Ammonite woman. Since female offspring are fit, R' Shimon b. Gamliel does not disqualify.

Widow of Questionable Lineage: A Dispute

ספק ספיקא — לקולא
Counter-Opinion Rav Ḥisda says all Tannaim agree she is unfit for priesthood.

But R' Yehoshua and R' Yehuda ben Beteira testified she is fit — because it is a case of compound uncertainty (sfek sfeka), and we rule leniently in such cases.

Rav Ḥisda's statement explicitly excludes their lenient view.

The Status of Samaritans (Kutim)

Are Samaritans (Kutim) considered valid converts?
R' Yishmael
They are lion converts (geirei arayot) — converted out of fear, not sincerity → remain gentiles
R' Akiva
They are true converts (geirei emet)
↓
What about the Jewish priests who assimilated among them?
R' Yishmael's view
"From among themselves" = from the thorns — unfit priests of flawed lineage
R' Akiva's view
"From among themselves" = from the chosen ones — fit, proper priests
Key Verse II Kings 17:32 — "And made unto them from among themselves priests of the high places" — interpreted in opposite ways by R' Yishmael and R' Akiva.
