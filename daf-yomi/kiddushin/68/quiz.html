QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is the offspring of a man and a niddah NOT a mamzer, even though niddah appears in the list of forbidden relations in Acharei Mot?
A. Because niddah is only a rabbinic prohibition
B. Because the verse 'ut'hi' teaches that betrothal (havaya) applies even during impurity
C. Because the punishment for niddah is only lashes, not karet
D. Because the niddah prohibition is temporary and will eventually be permitted
Correct: B — Chizkiyah derives from 'ut'hi niddata alav' (Lev. 15:24) that the root shared with 'havaya' (betrothal) teaches that betrothal remains effective even during a woman's impurity, so the child is not a mamzer.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rav Pappa prove that betrothal IS effective for women forbidden by ordinary lavim?
A. From a kal va'chomer from yevama
B. From the verse 'the beloved and the hated' — where 'hated' means a prohibited marriage yet the Torah calls her a 'wife'
C. From the principle that we juxtapose to stringency
D. From the verse about a beautiful captive woman
Correct: B — Rav Pappa interprets 'If a man has two wives, the beloved and the hated' (Deut. 21:15) — 'hated' refers to a marriage involving a lav violation, yet the Torah still calls her a 'wife,' proving betrothal is effective.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Gemara, what is the status of a child born to a Jewish woman and a gentile man?
A. A gentile, following the father
B. A mamzer
C. Jewish but considered 'disqualified' (pasul)
D. Fully valid with no disqualification
Correct: C — The Gemara concludes the child is Jewish (from 'your son') but is neither a mamzer nor fully valid — rather, the child is called 'disqualified' (pasul), since the gentile father is entirely outside the betrothal framework.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the general principle linking betrothal to lineage expressed as 'ki yihyu / v'yaldu lo'?
A. Children always follow the father's status regardless of betrothal
B. Where betrothal is effective, children follow the father; where it is not, children follow the mother
C. Children of forbidden unions are always mamzerim
D. Betrothal determines whether the marriage is valid but has no effect on offspring status
Correct: B — The Gemara derives from 'If he has two wives...and they have borne him children' that where betrothal applies, offspring follow the father; where it does not (gentile, maidservant), offspring follow the mother.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How do the Rabbis (who do NOT expound the Torah's reasons) derive that the intermarriage prohibition extends beyond the seven Canaanite nations?
A. From the reason 'for he will turn away your son' which applies universally
B. From an oral tradition going back to Sinai
C. From the beautiful captive verse, which shows betrothal is ineffective with any gentile before conversion
D. From a rabbinic enactment extending the biblical law
Correct: C — Since the Rabbis don't expound the Torah's stated reasons, they cannot use 'for he will turn away your son.' Instead, they derive from the beautiful captive law (Deut. 21:13) — 'and after that...she shall be your wife' — implying that before conversion, betrothal with ANY gentile is ineffective.
