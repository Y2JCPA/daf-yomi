Why Isn't a Niddah's Child a Mamzer?

Gemara
If betrothal is ineffective with all women listed in Acharei Mot, a niddah is on that list — her child should be a mamzer!
Abaye
All concede that intercourse with a niddah or sotah does not produce a mamzer.
Chizkiyah
The verse says "ut'hi niddata alav" (Lev. 15:24) — the root הוי teaches that havaya (betrothal) applies even during impurity.
Gemara
We could juxtapose forbidden relatives to niddah (leniency) or to a wife's sister (stringency). Why choose stringency?
Chizkiyah
When we can juxtapose to leniency or stringency, we always choose stringency.
Key Derivation The wordplay between ut'hi and havaya (betrothal) uniquely exempts niddah from the rule that karet-level prohibitions block betrothal.

Rav Acha bar Yaakov's Kal Va'chomer

A yevama is forbidden only by a regular lav (lashes)
↓
Yet betrothal with a yevama by someone other than the yavam is ineffective
↓
Those forbidden by death penalty or karet — kal va'chomer!
↓
Betrothal is certainly ineffective with them
↓
But what about other lav-level prohibitions?
Objection
The same kal va'chomer should make betrothal ineffective for all lavim!
Rav Pappa's Answer
"If a man has two wives, beloved and hated" — the Torah calls a lav-prohibited marriage a valid marriage
Deut. 21:15 "Beloved" = permitted marriage; "Hated" = marriage violating a prohibition. Yet the Torah still calls both "wives" — proving betrothal is effective for ordinary lavim.

Rabbi Akiva vs. the Rabbis on Mamzer Status

Rabbis (Majority View)

  • Mamzer only from unions punishable by karet or death
  • Betrothal effective for ordinary lav violations
  • "Hated wife" = woman married in violation of a lav
  • Widow + High Priest offspring = challal, not mamzer

Rabbi Akiva

  • Mamzer from any union involving a prohibition
  • Betrothal ineffective even for ordinary lavim
  • Exception per R' Simai: widow + Kohen Gadol = challal
  • "Hated wife" refers to this exception case only
R' Simai's Baraita "He shall not profane" (yeḥallel, Lev. 21:15) — R' Akiva agrees a widow married to a High Priest produces challalim, not mamzerim.

Matrilineal Descent: 'Your Son' from a Jewish Woman

R' Yochanan
In the name of R' Shimon ben Yochai: "For he will turn away your son" (Deut. 7:4) — your grandson from a Jewish daughter married to a gentile is called "your son."
R' Yochanan
But your son from a gentile woman is not called your son — he is her son.
Ravina
Learn from this: the son of your daughter born to a gentile is called "your son" in all regards — he is Jewish!
Gemara
Does Ravina hold that offspring of a gentile with a Jewish woman is a mamzer? Since he's Jewish and betrothal doesn't apply to a gentile...
Gemara
No — he is not a mamzer but is called "disqualified" (pasul). A gentile is outside the betrothal framework entirely.
Matrilineal Principle This sugya is a primary Talmudic source for matrilineal descent: the child of a Jewish mother and gentile father is Jewish, though considered pasul (disqualified).
