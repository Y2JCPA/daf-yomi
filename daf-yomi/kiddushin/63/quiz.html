QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yehuda HaNasi, why did the Sages invalidate betrothal that depends on a husband's death, even though by Torah law it works?
A. Due to enmity (eivah) it could cause between wife and husband
B. Because it is a davar shelo ba la'olam
C. Because the husband might object
D. Because it constitutes a loan, not a gift
Correct: A — R' Yehuda HaNasi holds that by Torah law such betrothal is valid, but the Sages prohibited it due to the enmity (eivah) it would engender — a married woman's betrothal pending her husband's death breeds hatred.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why can't a man betroth a woman 'by the monetary value of the donkey ride she already took'?
A. Because riding a donkey has no monetary value
B. Because a past service creates a debt, and one cannot betroth with a loan (milveh)
C. Because she must explicitly consent to the ride as betrothal
D. Because Reish Lakish requires a separate peruta in all cases
Correct: B — A past service already performed creates a debt obligation — effectively a loan (milveh). The Gemara teaches that one cannot betroth a woman with a loan. A future service, however, provides a new benefit and can serve as betrothal money.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rav Yosef bar Ami say is the meaning of 'on condition that my father wants the betrothal'?
A. The father must verbally express approval
B. The father must give a gift to the groom
C. The father must not object within 30 days
D. The father must sign a document of consent
Correct: C — Rav Yosef bar Ami interprets the condition as meaning the father must not object for 30 days. If he remains silent for that period, it constitutes acceptance. This allows the entire mishna to follow one unified reason.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Rav Asi distinguish between a father's claim and a woman's own claim about who betrothed her?
A. A woman cannot legally testify about her own betrothal
B. The father has a Torah-granted credibility that a woman lacks
C. A woman may be 'protecting' (colluding with) the man she identifies
D. A woman's testimony requires two witnesses to corroborate
Correct: C — Rav Asi concedes that when a woman herself claims she was betrothed, the man who steps forward is not believed to marry her — because she may be 'protecting him' (megina alav), colluding to marry the man she desires. A father has no such personal motive.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rav Asi mean when he calls his rulings 'roof breakers' (torei digri)?
A. They are groundbreaking innovations in halakha
B. They are difficult and seemingly incomprehensible, yet he stands by them
C. They overturn established precedent like breaking through a roof
D. They apply only in extreme, roof-level emergencies
Correct: B — Rav Asi acknowledges that his rulings — that the father is believed for stoning but a woman is not believed on her own admission — are difficult and hard to comprehend. Yet he maintains them as correct, calling them 'roof breakers' to express their challenging nature.
