QUESTION 1 OF 5
In the case of three men betrothing sequentially with 'from now and after X days,' why doesn't the middle man's betrothal ever take effect according to Abaye?
A. If the formula is a condition, the first man's betrothal already took effect; if it's a retraction, the last man's shorter delay takes effect first
B. The middle man's money was insufficient for a valid betrothal
C. The woman explicitly rejected the middle man's betrothal
D. The middle man spoke the formula incorrectly
Correct: A — Abaye explains that whether the formula is a condition (first man's betrothal effective from now) or a retraction (last man's shorter delay expires first), the middle man is never the one whose betrothal takes effect.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is R' Yochanan's metaphor of a 'sheraga' (row of bricks) meant to illustrate?
A. That betrothals stack on top of each other permanently
B. That each betrothal leaves a gap before taking effect, allowing others to intervene
C. That only the strongest betrothal survives
D. That betrothals must be performed in a specific physical arrangement
Correct: B — R' Yochanan holds that since each betrothal has a delay before taking effect, there are gaps in which other betrothals can slot in — like bricks in a row with spaces between them.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Abaye reject Rava's explanation that 'the get is completed by death'?
A. Because death cannot have legal consequences
B. Because a get removes her from the yavam's authority, while death brings her into it — they work in opposite directions
C. Because Rava was not qualified to rule on this matter
D. Because the get was physically destroyed before death
Correct: B — Abaye argues that divorce and death cannot complete each other because they have contradictory effects: a get frees her from yibum obligation, while death creates the yibum obligation. They pull in opposite directions.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the practical difference between Rav Huna and Rav Yehuda regarding a conditional get that is torn before the condition is fulfilled?
A. Both agree the get is invalid
B. Both agree the get is valid
C. Rav Huna says valid (took effect at delivery), Rav Yehuda says invalid (never took effect)
D. Rav Huna says invalid, Rav Yehuda says valid
Correct: C — Rav Huna holds the get takes effect immediately upon delivery (condition is just for remarriage), so tearing doesn't matter. Rav Yehuda holds the get takes effect only when the condition is fulfilled, which is impossible if the document is gone.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Yochanan (as reported by R' Asi), what do ALL authorities agree upon regarding the phrase 'al menat' (on the condition)?
A. That 'al menat' is always invalid in divorce
B. That 'al menat' means the act takes effect retroactively from now
C. That 'al menat' delays the effect until the condition is met
D. That 'al menat' requires witnesses to be valid
Correct: B — R' Yochanan teaches that all agree 'al menat' (on the condition) is equivalent to saying 'from now' — the act takes retroactive effect. The real dispute is only about 'from today and after my death.'
