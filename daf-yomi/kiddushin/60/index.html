When Does 'From Now and After...' Take Effect?

Rav
The expression 'from now and after thirty days' is uncertain — we don't know if it's a condition or a retraction
Shmuel
It is clearly a condition — betrothal takes effect from now if he doesn't retract
R' Yochanan
Even if 100 men betroth her this way, all their betrothals are effective — like a row of bricks with gaps
Abaye
Either way you analyze it — condition or retraction — the middle man's betrothal is never effective
Core Question Does 'from now and after X days' mean the act takes effect now with a condition, or is it a retraction delaying effectiveness to a later date?

Three Men Betroth Sequentially

Man #1 says: 'You are betrothed to me from now and after 30 days'
↓
Man #2 says: '...from now and after 20 days'
↓
Man #3 says: '...from now and after 10 days'
↓
If Condition
Man #1's betrothal is valid
(took effect first, 'from now')
She needs a get from #1 only
If Retraction
Man #3's betrothal is valid
(his 10-day delay ends first)
She needs a get from #3 only
Abaye's Conclusion Either way, Man #2 (the middle man) never creates a valid betrothal — she needs a get from #1 and #3 but not from #2.

R' Yochanan vs. Rav — Can Multiple Betrothals All Be Effective?

Rav — Uncertainty

  • The phrase is inherently ambiguous
  • Could be condition OR retraction
  • Only first and last man require a get
  • We apply doubt to the formula itself, not to each person's intent

R' Yochanan — All Effective

  • Even 100 betrothals can take effect
  • Like a row of bricks (שרגא) with gaps
  • Each betrothal leaves space for the next
  • The delayed period allows intervening betrothals
Key Metaphor R' Yochanan's שרגא (sheraga) — a row of bricks: each man's betrothal leaves a gap before it takes effect, and another betrothal can slot into that gap.

'From Today and After My Death' — Divorce and Yibum

Baraita
If he says 'This is your get from today and after my death' — it is a safek get. She performs ḥalitza but not yibum.
Rav Ḥanina
This works for Rav (uncertainty) and Shmuel (who follows the Rabbis). But for R' Yochanan, the get should leave a 'remnant' of marriage — making it invalid!
Rava
The get removes her partially, and death completes the severance. Together they fully dissolve the marriage.
Abaye
Impossible! A get removes her from the yavam, but death brings her to the yavam. They work in opposite directions!
Abaye
Rather, it's a rabbinic decree — lest people confuse this case with 'from today if I die,' which everyone agrees is a valid get.
Principle כל גט שאינו גומר — Any get that leaves a remnant of the marital bond is worthless. A get must completely sever the marriage.

Rav Huna vs. Rav Yehuda — When Does a Conditional Act Take Effect?

IssueRav HunaRav Yehuda
'On condition you give me 200 dinars'Get takes effect immediately upon receiptGet takes effect only when she pays
If get is torn/lost before paymentValid — already took effectInvalid — never took effect
Betrothal with same formulaBetrothal immediate upon giving moneyBetrothal only when condition fulfilled
Why state both cases?Betrothal draws near — maybe only there?Divorce pushes away — maybe only there?
Both needed because...He's not ashamed to demand payment (divorce)She's ashamed to claim money (betrothal)
Necessity The Gemara explains why both disputes (betrothal and divorce) must be stated — neither can be derived from the other due to the different dynamics of drawing near vs. pushing away.

R' Yehuda HaNasi on 'Al Menat' (On the Condition)

📜 Husband gives get saying: 'On the condition that you give me 200 dinars'
↓
🔑 R' Yehuda HaNasi: 'Al menat' = like saying 'from now' — retroactive effect
↓
✅ Get takes effect immediately, even though condition is fulfilled later
↓
🏛️ Rabbis disagree: effect only from when condition is fulfilled
↓
📍 R' Zeira: In Babylonia we thought this was disputed — but R' Yochanan says all agree 'al menat' = from now
↓
⚖️ The real dispute is only about 'from today and after my death' — condition or retraction?
R' Yochanan via R' Asi All concede that 'on the condition' (al menat) means retroactive effect from now. The dispute is only about 'from today and after my death.'
