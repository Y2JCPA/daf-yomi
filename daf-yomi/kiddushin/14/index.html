Can Money or a Document Acquire a Yevama?

Gemara
The verse says the yavam takes her as a wife — perhaps she can be acquired like any wife, through money or document too?
Baraita
The verse states "v'yibmah" (and perform levirate marriage) — only intercourse completes the acquisition, not money or document.
Gemara
But maybe "v'yibmah" teaches he can marry her without her consent?
Baraita
If so, the verse should say just "v'yibem". The extra word "otah" (her) teaches both: without consent and only through intercourse.
Key Rule Two halakhot from one phrase: yibum can be performed against her will, and only bi'ah — not money or document — completes the acquisition.

Deriving Chalitza from Scripture

Deuteronomy 25:10 — "His name shall be called in Israel: the house of him whose shoe was removed"
↓
What does "in Israel" teach?
R' Shmuel bar Yehuda
Chalitza must be in a Jewish court, not a gentile one
R' Tarfon
The matter must be announced publicly — all present say: "Chalutz ha-na'al!"
↓
Both are derived because "in Israel" appears twice in the passage (v. 7 and v. 10)
Resolution The public announcement is derived from "his name shall be called" — not from "in Israel" — freeing that phrase for the other derivations.

Married Woman vs. Yevama: How the Bond Dissolves

Married Woman (Eishet Ish)

  • Forbidden to others by penalty of strangulation
  • Released by husband's death
  • Released by bill of divorce (get)
  • Cannot leave through chalitza — "a scroll of severance" excludes it

Yevama

  • Forbidden to others by a mere prohibition (lav)
  • Released by death of yavam (kal va-chomer)
  • Released by chalitza
  • Cannot leave through get — "so shall it be done" (kein) excludes it
Rav Ashi The yavam both creates and dissolves the bond: his existence creates the obligation, and his chalitza or death dissolves it — parallel to a husband.

Mishna: Hebrew Slave — Modes of Acquisition & Release

CategoryAcquired ByReleased By
Hebrew Slave (Eved Ivri)Money or DocumentCompleting 6 years, Jubilee, or deducting money
Hebrew Maidservant (Amah)Money or DocumentAll above plus signs of puberty (simanim)
Pierced Slave (Nirtza)Piercing the ear with an awlJubilee or death of the master
Extra Mode The maidservant has one more mode of release than the male slave — puberty automatically emancipates her, as her father's authority over her ceases.

Deriving That a Hebrew Slave Is Acquired with Money

📖 "Out of the money that he was bought for" (Lev. 25:51) — but this discusses sale to a gentile!
↓
👩 "Then he shall let her be redeemed" (Ex. 21:8) — the maidservant deducts money, so she was acquired with money
↓
❓ Perhaps only a female, since women are betrothed with money?
↓
📜 "A Hebrew man or a Hebrew woman" (Deut. 15:12) — the verse juxtaposes them, equalizing their acquisition
↓
❓ That verse is about a court-sold thief — what about one who sells himself?
↓
🔗 Verbal analogy (gezeirah shavah): "sachir" (hired worker) appears in both passages, linking self-sold to court-sold
Alternative For the tanna who rejects the gezeirah shavah, the connecting "and" (vav) in Lev. 25:47 links the self-sold passage to the sold-to-a-gentile passage.
