Kiddushin 14 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why can't a yevama be acquired through money or a document like an ordinary wife?
A. Because the yevama has no father to receive the money
B. Because the verse says "v'yibmah" — only intercourse completes the acquisition
C. Because the yavam has not yet given a ring
D. Because chalitza must come first
Correct: B — The verse "and perform levirate marriage with her" (v'yibmah) teaches that only intercourse — not money or document — completes the acquisition of a yevama.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the competing derivations from "in Israel" regarding chalitza?
A. Only one derivation is accepted
B. The phrase "in Israel" appears twice in the passage, allowing two derivations
C. R' Tarfon overrules R' Shmuel bar Yehuda
D. The public announcement is a rabbinic enactment, not biblical
Correct: B — "In Israel" appears in both Deut. 25:7 and 25:10, so one teaches chalitza requires a Jewish court and the other is used for additional derivations. The public announcement comes from "his name shall be called."
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why can't a married woman leave her marriage through chalitza (by a fortiori reasoning from yevama)?
A. Because chalitza is degrading to the husband
B. Because the Torah says "a scroll of severance" — only a written document severs the marriage
C. Because a married woman's bond is too strong for chalitza
D. Because her husband would need to agree to chalitza
Correct: B — The verse states "sefer keritut" (a scroll of severance), teaching that for a married woman, only a written document (get) — and nothing else — can dissolve the marriage while the husband lives.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What additional mode of emancipation does a Hebrew maidservant have that a male Hebrew slave does not?
A. She can be redeemed by her future husband
B. She is freed by showing signs of puberty (simanim)
C. She is freed after three years instead of six
D. She can perform chalitza to free herself
Correct: B — The mishna teaches that a Hebrew maidservant has one extra mode of release: she acquires herself (is emancipated) through simanim — the physical signs of puberty.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the first tanna in the baraita, what is TRUE about a slave who sells himself voluntarily?
A. He can be sold for more than six years and cannot be pierced
B. He can be sold for only six years and can be pierced
C. He receives a severance gift upon emancipation
D. His master may give him a Canaanite maidservant
Correct: A — The first tanna distinguishes self-sold from court-sold: a self-sold slave can serve more than six years, cannot be pierced, receives no severance gift, and cannot be given a Canaanite maidservant. R' Elazar disagrees and equates them.
