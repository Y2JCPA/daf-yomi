Mitzvot Upon Entering Eretz Yisrael

Jews enter Eretz Yisrael — which mitzvot apply everywhere?
↓
R' Shimon b. Yochai
Chodosh, Kilayim, and Orla all apply in and outside Israel — derived by kal vachomer
R' Elazar b. R' Shimon
Mitzvot commanded before entry apply everywhere; those commanded after entry apply only in Israel — with exceptions
↓
Practical difference: Orla outside Israel — is it halakha l'Moshe miSinai or mere local custom?
Key Theme This daf explores which agricultural mitzvot transcend the borders of Eretz Yisrael, and the logical and scriptural bases for applying them universally.

Proving Moshe Died on 7 Adar

📅 Israel crossed the Jordan on 10 Nisan (Joshua 4:19)
↓
Subtract 3 days of preparation (Joshua 1:11)
↓
Subtract 30 days of mourning for Moshe (Deut. 34:8)
↓
10 Nisan − 33 days = 7 Adar — Moshe's death
Born & Died Same Day From 'I am 120 years old today' (Deut. 31:2) — the word 'today' teaches Moshe was also born on 7 Adar. God completes the years of the righteous from day to day.

Three Mitzvot Apply Upon Entry to the Land

R' Shimon b. Yochai
Three mitzvot were commanded immediately upon entering Israel: Chodosh (new crop), Kilayim (diverse kinds), and Orla
R' Shimon b. Yochai
And all three apply both in and outside Eretz Yisrael
Gemara
What is the logic?
R' Shimon b. Yochai
Kal vachomer: If Chodosh — which is not permanent, not a prohibition against benefit, and has dissolution — applies everywhere, then surely Kilayim and Orla do too!
The Kal Vachomer Chodosh is the most lenient of the three, yet it applies everywhere. Kilayim is permanent, prohibits benefit, and has no dissolution. Orla prohibits benefit and has no dissolution. A fortiori they apply outside Israel.

R' Elazar b. R' Shimon's Rule & Exceptions

Commanded BEFORE Entry

  • Applies in and outside Eretz Yisrael
  • Not linked to the land itself
  • Example: Chodosh, Kilayim, Orla

Commanded AFTER Entry

  • Applies only in Eretz Yisrael
  • Linked to inheritance and settlement
  • Exceptions: Shemitat kesafim & emancipation of slaves
Why Exceptions? Shemitat kesafim (abrogation of debts) and freeing slaves in Yovel are obligations of the body, not of the land — so they apply everywhere despite being commanded after entry.

Shemitat KesafimJuxtaposed with release of land — perhaps only when/where Yovel applies?'Because the Lord's abrogation has been proclaimed' (Deut. 15:2) — applies in any case
Emancipation of Slaves'Throughout the land' (Lev. 25:10) — perhaps only in Eretz Yisrael?'It shall be a Jubilee' — applies in all places
Both'Throughout the land' teaches: when Yovel applies in Israel it also applies outside; when it doesn't, it doesn't anywhereDepends on whether Yovel is in effect, not on location
Key Distinction These mitzvot are time-dependent (when Yovel is practiced) rather than place-dependent — a critical difference from standard land-based mitzvot.
