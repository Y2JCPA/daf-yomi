Freeing a Pregnant Maidservant

R' Yosei HaGelili
If the master says 'You are free but your child remains a slave,' the offspring is emancipated along with the mother.
Rabbis
The master's condition is upheld — the child remains a slave. As the Torah states: 'The wife and her children shall be her master's' (Exodus 21:4).
Rava
Actually, that verse is R' Yosei HaGelili's source — it shows children follow their mother. So if she's freed, they're freed too!
Twist Rava reinterprets the prooftext: the verse the Rabbis cite actually supports R' Yosei HaGelili's position that children follow their mother's status.

R' Tarfon's Method: Purifying Mamzer Lineage

👤 A mamzer (male) seeks to purify his children's lineage
↓
He marries a Canaanite maidservant (שפחה כנענית)
↓
Their child has the status of a slave (not mamzer)
↓
The master emancipates the child
↓
✅ The child is now a free Jew — no mamzer status!
Halakha Shmuel rules: The halakha follows R' Tarfon. A mamzer can purify his offspring through a Canaanite maidservant.

Ab Initio or After the Fact?

Gemara
Did R' Tarfon permit a mamzer to marry a maidservant from the outset (לכתחילה), or only validate it after the fact (בדיעבד)?
R' Simlai
I told my host, a mamzer: 'Had I known your status earlier, I would have advised you to marry a maidservant.' This implies it's permitted ab initio!
Gemara
Perhaps R' Simlai meant he'd advise: 'Go steal and be sold as a Hebrew slave' — then you could marry a maidservant?
Gemara
But Hebrew slavery wasn't practiced in R' Simlai's time — no Jubilee year! So it must mean R' Tarfon spoke ab initio.
Conclusion The Gemara proves that R' Tarfon permits a mamzer to marry a Canaanite maidservant ab initio (from the outset), not merely after the fact.

R' Tarfon vs. R' Eliezer on Mamzer + Maidservant

R' Tarfon

  • Child of mamzer + maidservant = slave only
  • Once emancipated, child is a free Jew
  • Offspring in a maidservant's womb is like offspring in an animal's womb — father's status doesn't transfer
  • Mamzer lineage is effectively purified

R' Eliezer

  • Child of mamzer + maidservant = mamzer slave
  • Derives from: 'Even to the tenth generation none of his shall enter' (Deut. 23:3)
  • 'Of his' (ממנו) — child follows the parent with flawed lineage
  • Mamzer status cannot be erased
Core Dispute Does the father's mamzer status transfer to a child born of a Canaanite maidservant? R' Tarfon says the womb determines status; R' Eliezer says flawed lineage always follows.
