Kiddushin 31: The Depths of Honoring Parents

כַּבֵּד אֶת אָבִיךָ וְאֶת אִמֶּךָ
CORE THEME This daf explores the mitzvah of כיבוד אב ואם (honoring parents) through dramatic real-life stories — from a gentile who sacrificed a fortune, to rabbis who physically served their parents — asking how far this obligation truly extends, and how to fulfill it even after a parent's death.
3 / 9

Why the Torah Reverses the Order

כיבוד (Honor)

  • "Honor your father and your mother" (Exodus 20:11)
  • Father listed FIRST
  • Because a child naturally honors the mother (she persuades gently)
  • Torah emphasizes what doesn't come naturally

מורא (Fear/Reverence)

  • "A man shall fear his mother and his father" (Leviticus 19:3)
  • Mother listed FIRST
  • Because a child naturally fears the father (he teaches Torah strictly)
  • Torah emphasizes what doesn't come naturally
PRINCIPLE The Torah specifically emphasizes the duty that does not come naturally to a person.

Sinning in Private & the Divine Presence

R' Yitzḥak
One who transgresses in private pushes away the feet of the Shekhinah — as if saying God is absent from that place. "The heaven is My throne and the earth is My footstool" (Isaiah 66:1).
R' Yehoshua b. Levi
It is prohibited to walk four cubits with an arrogant posture, as "The entire world is full of His glory" (Isaiah 6:3). Arrogance chases God from that place.
Rav Huna b. R' Yehoshua
I will not walk even four cubits with an uncovered head — the Shekhinah is above my head and I must act respectfully.
INSIGHT God's presence fills the world — both secret sin and public arrogance demonstrate a disregard for that reality.

When Both Parents Ask for Water

Son asks: My father says "Give me water" and my mother says "Give me water" — whom do I serve first?
↓
R' Eliezer & R' Yehoshua agree: Honor your father first — because both you and your mother are obligated in his honor.
↓
Parents Married
Father takes precedence — mother is also obligated to honor him
Mother Divorced
Both parents are equal — neither takes precedence
NOTE R' Yehoshua deduced from the questioner's tearful eyes that he was a widow's son — the question was personal, not theoretical.

Dama ben Netina: The Model of Kibbud Av

💎 The Sages came to buy precious stones for the ephod — 600,000 (or 800,000) gold dinars' profit
↓
🔑 The key to the chest was under his sleeping father's head
↓
🚫 Dama refused to wake him, losing the entire sale
↓
🐄 The next year, Hashem rewarded him: a parah adumah (red heifer) was born in his herd
↓
💰 He asked only for the money he lost — not a penny more
↓
📖 R' Ḥanina: If a gentile who is not commanded earns such reward, how much more so one who is commanded!
KAL VA'ḤOMER One who is commanded and fulfills the mitzvah is greater than one who is not commanded — because overcoming the yetzer hara of obligation is harder.

Exemplary Acts of Kibbud Av va'Em

SageAct of HonorLesson
Avimi (son of R' Abbahu)Despite having 5 ordained sons, he personally ran to open the door for his father, calling "Yes, yes!" so his father wouldn't feel ignoredEven accomplished children must serve parents personally
R' TarfonBent over so his mother could use him as a step to climb into and out of bedThe Sages told him: "You haven't reached even half" — would you stay calm if she threw your purse into the sea?
Rav YosefHearing his mother's footsteps, he would say: "I will stand before the arriving Shekhinah"A parent's approach is like the arrival of the Divine Presence
R' YoḥananSaid: "Fortunate is one who never saw his parents" — he was orphaned at birthHonoring parents properly is so difficult that never being tested may be a blessing
THEME Each story raises the bar — there is virtually no upper limit to the honor due to parents.

📋 Kiddushin 31 — Summary Map

Torah's Design: Honor → father first; Fear → mother first — emphasizing what is not natural
↓
How Far? Dama ben Netina lost 600,000+ gold dinars rather than wake his father — and asked only for what he lost
↓
In Life: Run to serve, stand for their arrival, never embarrass them — even at great personal cost
After Death: Quote them with honor — "So said Father, my teacher, may I be an atonement for his resting soul"
