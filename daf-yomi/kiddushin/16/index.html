Who Writes the Maidservant's Document?

Ulla
The verse 'If he takes himself another wife' (Exodus 21:10) juxtaposes a maidservant to a betrothed woman — just as betrothal uses a document, so too a maidservant is acquired by document.
Rav Huna
The master writes the document — just as a husband writes the betrothal document.
Rav Ḥisda
The father writes it — it functions as a document of sale, not betrothal.
Gemara
According to Rav Ḥisda, the document is not analogous to betrothal at all — so what is Ulla's source?
Practical Difference If the master writes it, the document resembles kiddushin. If the father writes it, it resembles a bill of sale — fundamentally different legal models.

Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov's Alternative Derivation

Verse: 'She shall not go out as the men slaves do' (Exodus 21:7)
↓
Inference: She can be acquired in the manner of Canaanite male slaves
Document?
Canaanite slaves can be acquired by document → so can a maidservant
Possession?
Leviticus 25:46 limits possession to Canaanite slaves only
↓
Conclusion: Document is included because it parallels betrothal ('If he takes another'); possession is excluded
Key Logic A document can release a Jewish woman (as a get), proving its power in personal-status matters — whereas possession has no parallel in marriage.

Document vs. Possession — Which Applies?

Document Included

  • Can release a Jewish woman (bill of divorce)
  • Parallels betrothal — 'If he takes another'
  • Marriage never uses possession
  • Applies to Hebrew maidservant

Possession Excluded

  • Leviticus 25:46 — 'bequeath them' limits to Canaanite slaves
  • Can acquire ownerless property of a dead convert
  • But has NO role in marriage/betrothal
  • Does not apply to Hebrew maidservant
Decisive Factor Since we are comparing a maidservant to betrothal, the mode must be one that functions in marriage — document does, possession does not.

Four Modes of Emancipation (R' Shimon)

⏱️ Six years of service — applies to both men and women
↓
📯 Jubilee Year — frees even a pierced slave; applies to both
↓
👂 Ear piercing expiry — applies only to a male Hebrew slave
↓
👧 Signs of puberty — applies only to a female Hebrew maidservant
Why Only 3 Each? R' Shimon counts 4 total modes but only 3 per gender: men lack puberty-based emancipation, and women cannot have their ears pierced.
