QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Ulla, from which verse do we derive that a Hebrew maidservant can be acquired through a document?
A. 'If he takes himself another wife' (Exodus 21:10)
B. 'She shall not go out as the men slaves do' (Exodus 21:7)
C. 'Six years he shall labor' (Exodus 21:2)
D. 'And you shall bequeath them to your children' (Leviticus 25:46)
Correct: A — Ulla derives it from the juxtaposition of a maidservant to a betrothed woman in 'If he takes himself another wife' — just as betrothal uses a document, so does acquiring a maidservant.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject 'possession' (ḥazakah) as a mode of acquiring a Hebrew maidservant?
A. Possession is limited to Canaanite slaves by Leviticus 25:46, and marriage never uses possession
B. Possession can only be used for land, not people
C. The Torah explicitly prohibits possession for any Hebrew person
D. Possession was abolished after the destruction of the Temple
Correct: A — Leviticus 25:46 ('bequeath them') limits possession to Canaanite slaves. Additionally, since the maidservant is compared to betrothal, and marriage never involves possession, document is the appropriate parallel.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the disagreement between Rav Huna and Rav Ḥisda regarding the document of a Hebrew maidservant?
A. Whether the document must be written on parchment or papyrus
B. Whether the master or the father writes the document
C. Whether two or three witnesses are required
D. Whether the document is given to the maidservant or kept by the court
Correct: B — Rav Huna says the master writes it (like a betrothal document), while Rav Ḥisda says the father writes it (like a document of sale). This affects whether the document parallels kiddushin or a sale.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why is Reish Lakish's opinion — that a father's death frees a Hebrew maidservant — conclusively refuted?
A. The Torah explicitly states the father's death has no effect
B. Baraitot listing modes of emancipation omit father's death, and the kal va-ḥomer is refutable since puberty changes her body but the father's death does not
C. Reish Lakish later retracted his own opinion
D. The maidservant passes to the father's heirs upon his death
Correct: B — Two baraitot enumerate all modes of emancipation without including the father's death. The kal va-ḥomer from puberty fails because puberty changes her body (making her a 'new person'), whereas the father's death causes no change in her.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Shimon, why are there only three modes of emancipation for each gender rather than four?
A. One mode requires a court of 23 judges, which is unavailable for one gender
B. Men cannot be freed by signs of puberty, and women cannot be freed through ear-piercing expiry
C. Men are freed by the Jubilee but women are not
D. Women cannot be freed after six years of service
Correct: B — R' Shimon counts four total modes (six years, Jubilee, ear-piercing expiry, signs of puberty) but each gender has only three: men lack puberty-based emancipation, and women are never subject to ear-piercing.
