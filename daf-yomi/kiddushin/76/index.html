Why Were Samaritans Disqualified?

R' Yishmael
They exempted married women from ḥalitza and levirate marriage, reading 'outside' as describing only betrothed women
R' Akiva
The offspring of a yevama who married a stranger is a mamzer, since one who violates a prohibition produces a mamzer
R' Eliezer
Samaritans are not well versed in the details of mitzvot — their gittin and kiddushin may be invalid
Rabban Shimon b. Gamliel
Any mitzva Samaritans embraced, they observe more strictly than Jews!
Three Opinions The Sages offered different halakhic grounds — flawed levirate practice, ignorance of mitzvot details, or assimilated mamzerim — to explain the ban on marrying Samaritans.

Samaritan Lineage Flaws: Additional Views

Rav Naḥman / Rabba bar Avuh:
A mamzer from a sister and a mamzer from a brother's wife were assimilated among them
↓
All Samaritans are mamzerim due to uncertainty (safek mamzer)
↓
Rava's view:
A Canaanite slave and maidservant were assimilated among them
↓
Their descendants may include slaves, further flawing their lineage
Historical Fact The Gemara clarifies these are not halakhic teachings but reports of what actually happened — the specific incidents that tainted Samaritan lineage.

Do Families Need Investigation?

R' Meir (Mishna's View)

  • All priestly families must be investigated
  • Check 4 mothers on each side (8 total)
  • Levite/Israelite women require one more generation
  • Investigation is the default requirement

The Rabbis

  • All families retain presumptive fitness (ḥezkat kashrut)
  • No investigation needed unless objection is raised
  • Only investigate when a specific challenge arises
  • Rav Ḥama bar Gurya: when objection registered, all agree to investigate
Resolution Rav attributes the Mishna to R' Meir, while the Rabbis hold that families are presumed fit unless challenged. Both agree that a registered objection triggers investigation.
