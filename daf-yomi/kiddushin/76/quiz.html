Kiddushin 76 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Eliezer, why were the Samaritans disqualified for marriage?
A. They worshipped idols alongside God
B. They were not well versed in the details of mitzvot
C. They descend from non-Jewish nations
D. They rejected the Oral Torah entirely
Correct: B — R' Eliezer held that Samaritans were not well versed in mitzvot details — specifically betrothal and divorce — meaning their marriages and divorces could be invalid, producing mamzerim.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How many women must a priest investigate when marrying a priest's daughter?
A. 4 women total
B. 6 women total
C. 8 women total
D. 10 women total
Correct: C — The Mishna requires investigating '4 mothers which are 8' — 4 generations of mothers on the maternal side and 4 on the paternal side, totaling 8 women.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does prior service at the Temple altar exempt a family from further lineage investigation?
A. Temple service purifies any lineage flaw
B. The Sanhedrin would have verified lineage before allowing service
C. Only Kohanim of the family of Aharon served, whose lineage is known
D. A divine sign would appear if the lineage was flawed
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the Sanhedrin (sitting in the Hewn Chamber) would not have allowed someone to serve at the altar without first examining their lineage.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Abaye resolve the contradiction between the Mishna (public officials have verified lineage) and the teaching that a mamzer may judge monetary cases?
A. The Mishna is overruled by the later teaching
B. The Mishna refers specifically to officials in Jerusalem
C. Monetary judges are not considered public officials
D. A mamzer who serves publicly loses his mamzer status
Correct: B — Abaye explains that the Mishna's rule about public officials applies specifically to Jerusalem, where they were especially particular about lineage. Elsewhere, even a mamzer could judge monetary cases.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say charity collectors have verified lineage?
A. They handled sacred funds requiring priestly oversight
B. They were appointed by the Sanhedrin directly
C. Their quarrels with people would have exposed any lineage flaw
D. They needed to swear oaths that required pure lineage
Correct: C — Charity collectors forcibly took collateral, leading to quarrels. If there were any flaw in their family's lineage, these conflicts would have brought it to public attention.
