QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Mishna need to state that a ḥalal's daughter is unfit 'forever'?
A. To distinguish from Egyptian/Edomite converts, where the restriction lifts after 3 generations
B. To teach that even a bat kohen who becomes a ḥalala can never eat teruma again
C. To indicate that ḥalal status applies even to mamzerim
D. To clarify that ḥalal status overrides conversion
Correct: A — The Gemara explains that without this, one might compare ḥalal status to Egyptian/Edomite converts, whose descendants are permitted after three generations. The Mishna teaches that ḥalal status through the male line is permanent.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Yoḥanan's gezera shava, why is the daughter of a ḥalala (who married an Israelite) fit for priesthood?
A. Because a ḥalala's sin is forgiven after one generation
B. Because the verbal analogy teaches that profanation follows males, not females
C. Because an Israelite husband's status always overrides the mother's
D. Because the Torah explicitly permits it in Leviticus 21:7
Correct: B — R' Yoḥanan links 'among his people' in Lev. 21:15 to Lev. 21:4 (tum'at kohen), teaching that just as corpse impurity applies to male kohanim only, profanation also transmits only through males.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is R' Dostai ben Yehuda's unique position regarding ḥalalim?
A. Ḥalal status never transmits to any offspring
B. Both Israelite men and Israelite women serve as a 'mikveh of purity' for ḥalalim/ḥalalot
C. Only a kohen gadol can create ḥalal status
D. Ḥalal status expires after ten generations
Correct: B — R' Dostai reads 'among his people' to mean profanation occurs only when both parents are ḥalalim. Therefore, an Israelite woman who marries a ḥalal purifies the lineage, just as an Israelite man purifies a ḥalala's lineage.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does a High Priest receive separate lashes for a woman who was a widow, then divorcée, then ḥalala, then zona — but only in that order?
A. Because Rava holds that a prohibition takes effect where another exists only when it expands the scope (issur mosif)
B. Because the Torah lists these prohibitions in this specific order
C. Because each status change requires a separate act of intercourse
D. Because R' Shimon holds that all prohibitions always stack
Correct: A — Rava explains that while normally a prohibition cannot take effect atop an existing one, an 'expanded prohibition' (issur mosif) can. Each stage in this order adds new people or restrictions: divorcée adds common kohen, ḥalala adds teruma prohibition, zona adds disqualification in Israel.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the baraita exclude a High Priest's widowed sister from the prohibition of marrying a widow?
A. Because she is already forbidden as his sister, and this follows R' Shimon that ein issur ḥal al issur
B. Because the Torah explicitly exempts sisters from all marriage prohibitions
C. Because a kohen's sister can never become a widow
D. Because Rava holds that issur mosif does not apply to relatives
Correct: A — Following R' Shimon's principle that a prohibition cannot take effect where another already exists (ein issur ḥal al issur), since the sister is already forbidden to him, the widow prohibition cannot be added. She was never included in 'shall he take' (eligible to marry), so she is excluded from 'shall he not take.'
