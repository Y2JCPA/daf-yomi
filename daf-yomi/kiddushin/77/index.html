Why 'Forever'? Comparing Ḥalalim to Converts

Questioner
Why does the Mishna say the daughter of a ḥalal is unfit forever? Isn't that obvious?
Gemara
Lest you compare it to Egyptian and Edomite converts, whose descendants are permitted after three generations.
Gemara
The Mishna teaches: ḥalal status is not like convert status — it never expires through the male line.
Distinction Convert restrictions are generational (3 generations); ḥalal status through male descent is permanent.

↓
Linked to: "He shall not defile himself, being a chief man among his people" (Lev. 21:4)
↓
Just as tum'a (impurity from a corpse) applies to males but not females…
↓
So too ḥilul (profanation): males transmit ḥalal status, not females
↓
Daughter of ḥalala + Israelite
Fit — mother's ḥalala status doesn't transmit
Daughter of ḥalal + Israelite
Unfit — father's ḥalal status transmits
Objection & Answer But it says "offspring" (not "son") — so the kohen's own daughter is a ḥalala. The gezera shava teaches that from the granddaughter onward through females, the status does not transmit.

R' Dostai ben Yehuda vs. the Sages

Sages (Mishna)

  • Israelite men are a 'mikveh' for ḥalalot — their daughters are fit
  • Israelite women are NOT a mikveh for ḥalalim
  • Ḥalal + Israelite woman → daughter still unfit

R' Dostai ben Yehuda

  • Israelite men purify ḥalalot — same as Sages
  • Israelite women also purify ḥalalim
  • Ḥalal + Israelite woman → daughter is fit
  • Derives from: "among his people" — only profanes when both parents are ḥalalim
Key Phrase "Among one people he profanes" — R' Dostai reads this to mean profanation occurs only when both parents share ḥalal status, not when one is an Israelite.

Rava: Expanded Prohibition (Issur Mosif)

👰 Widow — forbidden only to a High Priest, permitted to a common kohen
↓
📜 Becomes Divorcée — now forbidden also to a common kohen → issur mosif!
↓
⚠️ Becomes Ḥalala — now forbidden to eat teruma → further expansion!
↓
🚫 Becomes Zona — the label of zenut disqualifies even in Israel (e.g., adultery) → expanded scope
↓
⚖️ High Priest receives separate lashes for each prohibition — because each one added to the previous
Rava's Principle A prohibition normally cannot take effect where another already exists. But if the new prohibition expands the scope (issur mosif) — forbidding the person to additional people or things — it does take effect.
