Deriving the Three Modes of Betrothal

Can document (shtar) effect betrothal?
↓
Attempt: Kal v'chomer from money — if money, which can't release a wife, can betroth, surely a document, which can release her, can betroth!
↓
❌ Refuted: Money is unique — it redeems hekdesh and ma'aser sheni
↓
✅ Solution: Hekesh — "v'yatz'ah...v'hayta" (Deut. 24:2) juxtaposes becoming (betrothal) to leaving (divorce)
Core Principle Each mode of betrothal requires its own scriptural source — logical derivation alone is insufficient due to unique properties of each mode.

Can Document Be Derived by Kal V'Chomer?

Baraita
If money, which cannot release a woman from her husband, can betroth her — surely a document, which can release her (as a get), should be able to betroth!
Gemara
Money is unique — it can redeem hekdesh (consecrated property) and ma'aser sheni (second tithe). A document cannot!
Gemara
Therefore we need a pasuk: "V'yatz'ah...v'hayta l'ish acher" (Deut. 24:2) — a hekesh juxtaposing 'becoming' to 'leaving.'
Gemara
Just as leaving (divorce) is done through a document, so too becoming (betrothal) can be done through a document.
Hekesh The juxtaposition of 'v'yatz'ah' (leaving) and 'v'hayta' (becoming) in the same verse is the scriptural basis for betrothal by document.

Saneigor vs. Kateigor — Why Not Divorce by Money?

Abaye's Argument

  • Money brings in (betrothal) ✅
  • Money releases (divorce)? ❌
  • "Can a saneigor (advocate) become a kateigor (prosecutor)?"
  • Same instrument shouldn't both unite and sever

But What About Documents?

  • Document brings in (betrothal shtar) ✅
  • Document releases (get) ✅
  • Answer: The WORDS are different!
  • A betrothal document and a get have distinct content — coins are indistinguishable
Key Distinction Documents used for betrothal vs. divorce have different texts, making them functionally distinct. Coins, however, are interchangeable — so money cannot serve for both.

Rava's Derivation: Divorce Requires Writing

Rava
"V'chatav lah sefer keritut" (Deut. 24:3) — she is divorced through writing, and she is not divorced through money.
Gemara
Maybe the verse means: she is divorced through writing, but she is not betrothed through writing?
Rava
No — the hekesh "v'yatz'ah v'hayta" already established betrothal by document. So the exclusion must apply to divorce.
Gemara
Why interpret it that way and not the reverse?
Rava
A verse about divorce logically excludes a mode of divorce — not a mode of betrothal!
Rava vs. Abaye Abaye uses the saneigor/kateigor logic to exclude money from divorce. Rava derives it directly from the verse "v'chatav lah" — divorce requires writing, not money.

Can Chuppah Effect Betrothal? The Failed Derivation

Can chuppah (wedding canopy) effect kiddushin?
↓
Argument: Chuppah completes marriage — surely it can begin it!
↓
Attempted Proof
Money + intercourse + document all effect acquisition → their common denominator should include chuppah
Refutation
All three work against her will in some case (get, yibum, amah ivriya) — chuppah only works willingly!
↓
Rav Huna: For marriage specifically, even money doesn't work against her will — so this refutation fails!
↓
Rava: Two refutations — (1) Mishna lists THREE modes, not four; (2) Chuppah only works AFTER betrothal, so you can't derive independent betrothal from it
Abaye's Response Abaye counters: (1) The Mishna only lists what is explicit in Torah; (2) Rav Huna's kal v'chomer already accounts for chuppah needing prior betrothal — that's its strength, not its weakness.
