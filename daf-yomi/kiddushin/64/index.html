Father's Credibility: The 'In His Power' Principle

Father makes a claim
about his daughter's status
↓
Is the claimed act currently
within his power to perform?
YES — In His Power
✅ Father is deemed credible
(e.g., betrothing a minor daughter)
NO — Not In His Power
❌ Father is not credible
(e.g., betrothing adult daughter,
claiming captivity)
KEY PRINCIPLE A father's testimony about his daughter is believed only when the act he describes is one he currently has the legal authority to perform.

MISHNA The father's credibility is calibrated to the nature of the claim — ritual matters vs. punitive matters, and whether the act remains within his legal authority.

Can a Father Disqualify His Daughter from Priesthood?

Gemara
But a father can marry his minor daughter to a ḥalal, disqualifying her from priesthood! So he should be believed about captivity too.
R' Dostai b. Yehuda
Israelite women are a 'ritual bath of purity' for ḥalal status — she is not disqualified by marrying a ḥalal.
Gemara
But he can marry her to a mamzer, disqualifying her!
R' Akiva
Betrothal does not take effect with those subject to a prohibition — including a mamzer. So the father cannot disqualify her this way either.
Gemara
But per R' Simai, R' Akiva agrees betrothal works for a widow to a High Priest — that produces ḥalalim, not mamzerim!
R' Yeshevav
R' Akiva holds all forbidden unions produce mamzerim — even a widow to a High Priest. So betrothal never takes effect for any prohibition.
RESOLUTION The Mishna follows a combination of R' Dostai, R' Akiva, and R' Yeshevav — under which a father has no mechanism to disqualify his daughter, so he is not believed when claiming she was taken captive.

Migo (Why Would I Lie?) — R' Yehuda HaNasi vs. R' Natan

A man says: "I have children"
(exempting wife from yibum)
↓
We apply migo: If he were lying,
he could simply divorce her instead
↓
At death, he now says:
"I have no children"
R' Yehuda HaNasi
Migo = like witnesses
→ Can uproot presumption
→ She is exempt from yibum
(he cannot retract)
R' Natan
Migo = like a presumption
→ Cannot uproot another presumption
→ Original status reinstated
(she needs yibum)
KEY DISPUTE The core question: Is the logical argument of migo as strong as actual witness testimony, or merely equivalent to a legal presumption (ḥazaka)?

Unspecified Betrothal: Who Is Included?

Adult Daughters

  • ❌ NOT included in unspecified betrothal
  • Father has no authority to betroth them
  • Even if she appointed him as agent
  • Principle: A person does not set aside a mitzva imposed upon him for one not imposed upon him

Minor Daughters

  • ✅ INCLUDED in unspecified betrothal
  • Father retains full betrothal rights
  • Father keeps the betrothal money
  • Betrothal is the father's obligation/privilege
PRINCIPLE A person does not set aside a matter from which he derives benefit (betrothing minor — he keeps money) to perform one from which he does not (betrothing adult — she keeps money).

Two Groups of Daughters: R' Meir vs. R' Yosei

Scenario
Father has daughters from two wives. He says: "I betrothed my elder daughter" but doesn't know which one he meant.
R' Meir
All are forbidden except the youngest of the younger group — she certainly cannot be called 'elder.'
R' Yosei
All are permitted except the eldest of the older group — 'elder' without specification means the absolute oldest.
Scenario
If he says: "I betrothed my younger daughter" but doesn't know which one—
R' Meir
All forbidden except the eldest of the older group — she certainly isn't 'younger.'
R' Yosei
All permitted except the youngest of the younger group — 'younger' means the absolute youngest.
CORE DISPUTE R' Meir treats unresolved doubt strictly — anyone who might be the one is forbidden. R' Yosei reads 'elder' and 'younger' as referring to the absolute eldest/youngest.
