QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is a father believed when he says he betrothed his minor daughter but not his adult daughter?
A. Because betrothing a minor is still within his legal power
B. Because minors cannot testify to contradict him
C. Because the Torah explicitly grants this credibility
D. Because minor betrothal requires only one witness
Correct: A — The Gemara establishes the principle that a father is credible when the act he claims to have done is currently 'in his power' (בידו). Since he can still betroth a minor but not an adult, he is believed only about the minor.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why can't the father disqualify his daughter by marrying her to a mamzer, according to the Mishna's view?
A. A mamzer cannot marry at all
B. R' Akiva holds betrothal doesn't take effect with prohibited relations
C. The father loses authority once she reaches age 6
D. Marrying a mamzer only disqualifies sons, not daughters
Correct: B — The Mishna follows R' Akiva, who holds that betrothal does not take effect (אין קידושין תופסין) with those subject to a prohibition. Since betrothal with a mamzer is void, the father cannot use this route to disqualify his daughter.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Yehuda HaNasi, what is the legal strength of a 'migo' (Why would I lie?) argument?
A. It is equivalent to a legal presumption (ḥazaka)
B. It is equivalent to witness testimony and can uproot a presumption
C. It has no legal standing whatsoever
D. It is valid only for monetary matters
Correct: B — R' Yehuda HaNasi holds that migo is as strong as actual witness testimony. Therefore, once a man's claim is accepted via migo, he cannot later retract it — just as witnesses cannot be contradicted by a mere presumption.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the case of two groups of daughters, what does R' Yosei hold when the father says 'I betrothed my elder daughter'?
A. All daughters are forbidden
B. All are permitted except the eldest of the older group
C. All are forbidden except the youngest of the younger group
D. Only daughters from the older wife's group are forbidden
Correct: B — R' Yosei interprets 'elder' in its plain, absolute sense — the oldest daughter of all. Therefore only she is certainly betrothed, and all others are permitted.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the apparent contradiction between the Mishna (multiple daughters need a get) and Rava's principle that betrothal not given to consummation is invalid?
A. Rava's principle only applies to rabbinic prohibitions
B. The case involves only two daughters — one adult (excluded) and one minor (certainly betrothed)
C. The Mishna disagrees with Rava entirely
D. The father specifies which daughter after the betrothal
Correct: B — The Gemara narrows the case to only two daughters — one adult and one minor. Since the adult is automatically excluded, the minor is certainly the one betrothed. There is no sister-wife problem, so Rava's principle is not violated.
