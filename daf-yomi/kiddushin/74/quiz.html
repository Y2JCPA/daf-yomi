Kiddushin 74 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman, how long is a father deemed credible to identify his firstborn?
A. Only immediately after birth
B. Seven days
C. Thirty days
D. Forever
Correct: D — The father's credibility is forever, derived from Deuteronomy 21:17 — 'He shall acknowledge the firstborn.' Unlike the midwife (immediately) or mother (seven days), the father has no time limit.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Abba Shaul's term 'beduki' mean in context?
A. The child has been disqualified from the congregation
B. The mother's claim about the father should be examined and believed
C. The child's lineage has been confirmed as flawed
D. The father has been identified through witnesses
Correct: B — Abba Shaul renames the 'shetuki' (silenced one) as 'beduki' (examined one), meaning we examine (bodekin) the mother's claim about the father — and if she says he was of unflawed lineage, she is believed.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why was Abba Shaul's ruling necessary if Rabban Gamliel already deemed the mother credible?
A. Rabban Gamliel only applied to married women
B. Abba Shaul extended credibility to the daughter's fitness and to cases where most potential fathers would disqualify
C. Abba Shaul restricted the mother's credibility
D. Rabban Gamliel's ruling was overturned
Correct: B — Abba Shaul's teaching was needed to extend credibility to the daughter's status and to cover cases where most potential fathers would produce a disqualified child — broader than Rabban Gamliel's ruling.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is 'shuda dedayyanei' and why does it matter for the judge's credibility?
A. A binding precedent that ensures consistent rulings
B. Judicial discretion where re-judging may yield a different result
C. A written verdict that cannot be challenged
D. A majority-rule voting system among judges
Correct: B — Shuda dedayyanei is judicial discretion based on the judge's intuitive sense of justice. Because there's no guarantee of the same result in a retrial, the judge's testimony about his original verdict is uniquely important.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman citing Rabba bar Avuh, what is the core dispute between the first tanna and Rabbi Yehuda regarding mamzer status?
A. Whether a convert's child is a mamzer
B. Whether the child of a Kohen and divorcée is a mamzer
C. Whether offspring of a brother-sister union is a mamzer, or only offspring of a married woman with another man
D. Whether a shetuki is considered a mamzer
Correct: C — The first tanna holds that even offspring of a brother-sister union is a mamzer, while Rabbi Yehuda holds only offspring of a married woman and another man qualifies as a mamzer — a narrower definition.
