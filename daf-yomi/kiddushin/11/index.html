How Much Money Does Betrothal Require?

Beit Shammai
A woman must be betrothed with at least one dinar — anything less is beneath her dignity and below Torah standards.
Beit Hillel
Even one peruta is sufficient for betrothal — the Torah does not specify a minimum amount for kiddushin.
Gemara
Three Amoraim offer different explanations for Beit Shammai's reasoning: R' Zeira (dignity), Rav Yosef (Tyrian coinage), and Reish Lakish (maidservant derivation).
Core Dispute This daf explores the minimum monetary value required for valid betrothal and the broader principle of how to interpret 'money' mentioned in the Torah.

Ben Bag Bag: Why Simfon Doesn't Apply to Slaves

Can a buyer claim simfon (defect) to annul a slave purchase?
Exposed Blemish
Buyer saw it and bought anyway — no valid claim
Hidden Blemish
Slave is for labor — hidden blemishes don't matter
↓
What if the slave is a thief or gambler (kuvyustus)?
↓
Buyer should have suspected this — common among slaves. Transaction stands!
↓
What if the slave is an armed bandit wanted by the government?
↓
Such matters generate publicity — buyer assumed the risk
Key Point Ben Bag Bag rejects the a fortiori inference from slaves to wives because no valid simfon claim exists for slaves at all.

R' Zeira's Explanation: A Woman's Dignity

R' Zeira
A woman is particular about herself (makpid) — she won't agree to be betrothed for less than one dinar.
Abaye
If so, R' Yannai's daughters won't accept less than three kav of dinars! If one accepts a single dinar, is it not betrothal?
R' Zeira
If she reaches out her hand and accepts — she waives her dignity. I meant where she was betrothed at night (didn't see the amount) or through an agent without specifying.
Abaye
So only in cases of uncertainty do we assume she expects at least a dinar.
Limitation R' Zeira's reasoning applies only where she didn't explicitly see/accept the amount — not when she actively consents.

Three Explanations for Beit Shammai's One-Dinar Minimum

R' Zeira

  • Based on woman's dignity
  • She is particular — won't accept less than a dinar
  • Applies only at night or via agent
  • Psychological / social reasoning

Rav Yosef

  • Based on Rav Asi's principle
  • Torah money = Tyrian coinage
  • Smallest Tyrian coin = 1 dinar
  • Legal / monetary standard reasoning

Reish Lakish

  • Based on Chizkiyya
  • Derived from Hebrew maidservant
  • She must 'deduct' from purchase price
  • Can't deduct from a peruta — must be at least a dinar
Halacha Despite these arguments, the halacha follows Beit Hillel — one peruta suffices for betrothal.

Rav Asi's Principle: Torah Money vs. Rabbinic Money

📜 Every set amount of money in the Torah = Tyrian coinage (silver)
↓
🏛️ Rabbinic monetary amounts = provincial coinage (worth ~⅛ of Tyrian)
↓
👶 Firstborn redemption: 5 sela = Tyrian silver
↓
🐂 Slave killed by ox: 30 sela = Tyrian silver
↓
⚖️ Rapist/seducer fine: 50 sela = Tyrian silver
↓
👋 Striking another person: 1 sela = provincial coin (½ dinar)
Gemara's Question The Gemara challenges: betrothal money is derived from the Torah (Ephron's field), yet Beit Hillel allows a peruta! Resolution: Rav Asi's rule applies only where a specific amount is stated.
