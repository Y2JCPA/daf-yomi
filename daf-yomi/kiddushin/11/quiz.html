QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Ben Bag Bag reject the a fortiori inference from slave acquisition to betrothal?
A. Because slaves cannot be acquired with money
B. Because no valid simfon (blemish) claim exists for slaves
C. Because slaves are more valuable than wives
D. Because the Torah explicitly forbids the comparison
Correct: B — Ben Bag Bag holds that simfon claims are never valid for slaves — exposed blemishes were seen and accepted, hidden blemishes don't matter for labor, and criminal behavior is foreseeable or public.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Zeira, in what situation does Beit Shammai's one-dinar minimum apply?
A. When she explicitly accepts the betrothal amount
B. When she is betrothed at night or through an agent without specifying an amount
C. When her father arranges the betrothal
D. In all cases without exception
Correct: B — R' Zeira limits Beit Shammai's ruling to cases where the woman didn't see/specify the amount — at night or via an unspecified agent. If she extends her hand and accepts, she waives her dignity.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the relationship between Tyrian coinage and provincial coinage according to Rav Asi?
A. Provincial coinage is worth double Tyrian coinage
B. They are equal in value
C. Provincial coinage is worth about one-eighth of Tyrian coinage
D. Tyrian coinage is worth one-tenth of provincial coinage
Correct: C — Provincial coinage was worth approximately one-eighth the value of Tyrian coinage. Torah-specified amounts use the higher Tyrian standard; rabbinic amounts use the lower provincial standard.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the challenge from second-tithe money being permitted in copper coins?
A. The verse about tithes is only rabbinic
B. The definite article 'the' in 'the money' serves as an amplification to include all coinage
C. Second tithe is an exception to Rav Asi's rule
D. Copper coins were actually silver-plated
Correct: B — The verse says 'the money' (ha-kesef) rather than simply 'money' (kesef). The definite article serves as a ribbui (amplification), expanding the law to include all types of coinage, even copper.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Reish Lakish's reasoning, based on Chizkiyya, for Beit Shammai's one-dinar requirement?
A. The Torah equates betrothal with purchasing land
B. A Hebrew maidservant must be able to deduct from her purchase price, which is impossible if it's only one peruta
C. One dinar is the minimum wage mentioned in the Torah
D. Reish Lakish derives it from the word 'taking' in the verse about Ephron's field
Correct: B — Chizkiyya teaches that a maidservant can deduct from her purchase price to leave early. If she was purchased for only one peruta, there's nothing to deduct from. Beit Shammai derive betrothal from the maidservant case, requiring at least one dinar.
