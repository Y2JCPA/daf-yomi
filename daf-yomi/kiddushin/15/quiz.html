QUESTION 1 OF 5
How does the Gemara derive that the Hebrew slave's RIGHT ear is pierced?
A. A gezera shava from the leper's 'right ear' (Leviticus 14:14)
B. An explicit verse states 'right ear' for the slave
C. A kal va'chomer from the Kohen Gadol's ear
D. A tradition from Moshe at Sinai (halakha l'Moshe mi'Sinai)
Correct: A — R' Eliezer teaches a verbal analogy (gezera shava): 'ear' is stated regarding the slave and 'right ear' is stated regarding the leper, so just as the leper's right ear is specified, the slave's right ear is pierced.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the First Tanna, why can't a self-sold slave be pierced?
A. Because 'his ear' excludes a self-sold slave
B. Because the Torah says 'the slave shall say' — only court-sold slaves qualify
C. Because a self-sold slave serves only six years
D. Because piercing requires a court order
Correct: A — The First Tanna derives from the seemingly superfluous 'his ear' (instead of just 'ear') that one who sells himself may not be pierced. The word 'ear' alone would have sufficed for the gezera shava.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does R' Elazar need 'to him' in the severance gift verse to exclude the creditor?
A. Because R' Elazar holds like R' Natan, who allows courts to redirect debts — so without 'to him,' the creditor could seize the gift
B. Because the slave's creditor is mentioned explicitly in the Torah
C. Because severance gifts are considered monetary obligations
D. Because R' Elazar considers the severance gift a form of wages
Correct: A — R' Natan's principle allows a court to redirect debts through a chain of creditors. Without 'to him,' the severance gift could be seized by the slave's creditor under this rule. R' Elazar uses 'to him' to block this possibility.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the core disagreement between R' Yosei HaGelili and R' Akiva regarding a Hebrew slave redeemed from a gentile?
A. Whether the slave must work off additional years
B. Whether redemption by relatives leads to freedom or slavery
C. Whether the gentile master receives full payment
D. Whether the slave goes free at the Jubilee
Correct: B — R' Yosei HaGelili holds redemption by relatives leads to freedom (comparing it to self-redemption via the preceding phrase), while R' Akiva holds redemption by relatives leads to slavery (comparing others' redemption to self-redemption via the following phrase).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does 'double the hire of a hired worker' teach about a Hebrew slave, according to the baraita?
A. The slave receives double wages upon release
B. The slave works day and night — meaning his master may provide him a Canaanite maidservant
C. The slave must serve for twelve years instead of six
D. The slave's labor is worth twice that of an ordinary worker
Correct: B — The baraita explains that unlike a hired worker who works only during the day, a Hebrew slave 'works' day and night — meaning the master may provide him with a Canaanite maidservant. This isn't literal nighttime labor but refers to this additional arrangement.
