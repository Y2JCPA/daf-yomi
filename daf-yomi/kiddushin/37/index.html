Which Mitzvot Apply Outside Eretz Yisrael?

Mishna: Mitzvot dependent on the land apply only in Eretz Yisrael
↓
Gemara asks: What defines "dependent on the land"?
↓
❌ Not by the word 'Coming'
Tefillin & pidyon peter chamor say 'coming' but apply everywhere
✅ Rav Yehuda's Principle
Obligation of the body = everywhere
Obligation of the land = only in Eretz Yisrael
Core Principle The geographic scope of a mitzva depends on whether it obligates the person (chovot ha-guf) or the land and its produce (chovot karka).
3 / 9

Two Categories of Mitzvot

Chovot Ha-Guf (Body Obligations)

  • Obligations upon the person
  • Apply in Eretz Yisrael AND outside
  • Examples: Tefillin, Shabbat, pidyon peter chamor
  • Derived from idol worship (Deut. 12:2)

Chovot Karka (Land Obligations)

  • Obligations tied to the earth and its produce
  • Apply ONLY in Eretz Yisrael
  • Examples: Terumah, ma'aser, shemittah
  • Derived from 'in the land' (Deut. 12:1)
Rav Yehuda This is the definitive principle the mishna teaches — not the presence of the word 'coming' in the verse.

The Derasha from Deuteronomy 12:1

"In the land" → All mitzvot apply only in Eretz Yisrael?
↓
"All the days you live upon the earth" → All mitzvot apply everywhere?
↓
Both phrases together create a tension — include and exclude areas outside the land
↓
Next verse (Deut. 12:2): Destroying idol worship — an obligation of the body that applies everywhere
Resolution Just as idol worship is a body obligation applying everywhere, so too all body obligations apply everywhere. Land obligations apply only in Eretz Yisrael.

Does Chadash (New Crop) Apply Outside Eretz Yisrael?

Tanna Kamma
All land-dependent mitzvot apply only in Eretz Yisrael, except orla and kilayim (halakha l'Moshe mi-Sinai).
R' Eliezer
Even chadash — the prohibition of new crop — applies outside the land!
Gemara
Does R' Eliezer rule more stringently (adding chadash to the list) or more leniently (removing orla/kilayim)?
If Stringent
R' Eliezer reads 'in all your dwellings' (Lev. 23:14) as meaning wherever you dwell — so chadash applies everywhere.
If Lenient
R' Eliezer says: Only chadash applies outside (from the verse), but orla and kilayim do not — rejecting the halakha l'Moshe mi-Sinai.
Key Phrase "B'chol moshvoteichem" (in all your dwellings) — does it mean wherever you live or does it refer to the era after settling Eretz Yisrael?

Rabbi Yishmael vs. Rabbi Akiva: 'Coming' and 'Dwelling'

R' Yishmael
When both 'coming' and 'dwelling' appear, the mitzva applies only after inheritance and settlement of the land.
R' Akiva
But Shabbat mentions 'dwellings' (Lev. 23:3) — does Shabbat only apply after settlement?
R' Yishmael
My principle requires both terms together. And Shabbat is derived through a kal va-chomer anyway.
Gemara
Their underlying dispute: Did the Jews offer nesakhim (libations) in the wilderness?
R' Yishmael
No — 'when you come to the land of your dwellings' (Num. 15:2) means nesakhim began only in Eretz Yisrael.
R' Akiva
Yes — they did sacrifice nesakhim in the wilderness. 'Dwelling' teaches a different halakha.
Practical Ramification The terms 'coming' (bi'ah) and 'dwelling' (moshav) determine not just where a mitzva applies, but when it begins.
