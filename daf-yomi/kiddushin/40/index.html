Miracles for Resisting Temptation

Gemara
Anyone who encounters forbidden intercourse and is saved from it — a miracle is performed for him.
R' Tzadok
A noblewoman tried to entice me. I said: 'One who does such an act eats non-kosher food.' Then I climbed into her oven — 'One who does this act falls into Gehenna!'
Rav Kahana
I was enticed while selling baskets. I jumped from a rooftop to escape — Elijah caught me, but scolded me: 'You troubled me to travel 400 parasangs!'
Rav Kahana
What caused my temptation? Poverty! Elijah then gave me a basket full of dinars so I wouldn't need that trade.
Psalms 103:20 "Mighty in strength who fulfill His word" — referring to those who resist forbidden desires.

MitzvaSource of Worldly RewardKey Phrase
Honoring Parents (כיבוד אב ואם)Deuteronomy 5:16"That your days may be long and it may go well with you"

Making Peace (הבאת שלום)Psalms 34:15 + verbal analogy"Seek peace and pursue it"
Torah Study (תלמוד תורה)Deuteronomy 30:20"For that is your life and the length of your days"

Why Not Shiluaḥ HaKen? Rav Idi explains: only mitzvot that benefit both Heaven and people earn worldly profits. Dispatching the mother bird benefits Heaven alone.

The Righteous & The Wicked — Two Dimensions

Righteous Who Is Good (צדיק טוב)

  • Good toward Heaven ✓
  • Good toward people ✓
  • Enjoys profits in this world
  • Principal reward in World-to-Come

Righteous Who Is Not Good (צדיק שאינו טוב)

  • Good toward Heaven ✓
  • Bad toward people ✗
  • No worldly profits listed
  • e.g., Dispatching the mother bird

Evil Wicked (רשע רע)

  • Evil toward Heaven ✗
  • Evil toward people ✗
  • Punished on both dimensions

Wicked, Not Evil (רשע שאינו רע)

  • Evil toward Heaven ✗
  • Not evil toward people
  • Less severe category
Isaiah 3:10 "Say of the righteous who is good" — implying there exists a righteous person who is not good.

One Sin Tips the Cosmic Scales

Every person should view themselves as
exactly half-liable, half-meritorious
↓
What does the next act do?
One Mitzva ✓
Tips balance to merit — for self and the entire world
(R' Elazar b. R' Shimon)
One Sin ✗
Tips balance to liability — for self and the entire world
"One sin destroys much good"
↓
R' Shimon ben Yoḥai:
Completely righteous who rebels at the end — loses all prior merit
Completely wicked who repents at the end — wickedness forgotten
↓
Reish Lakish qualifies: This applies only when one regrets past mitzvot — not merely sinning
Ecclesiastes 9:18 "One sin destroys much good" — a single transgression can squander a lifetime of merit.

The Threefold Cord (Mishna)

וחוט המשולש לא במהרה ינתק
Ecclesiastes 4:12 One who engages in Bible (מקרא), Mishna (משנה), and proper conduct (דרך ארץ) — "a threefold cord is not quickly broken."

One who has none of these three is not part of civilized society.
