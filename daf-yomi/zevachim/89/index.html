Tadir Kodem: The Frequency Hierarchy

📅 Tamid (Daily offering) — most frequent, always first
↓
🕎 Musaf Shabbat — every week
↓
🌙 Musaf Rosh Chodesh — every month
↓
🎺 Musaf Rosh Hashanah — once a year
↓
🍞 Musaf Pesach — once a year (less frequent category)
Source "Besides the burnt offering of the morning, which is for a daily burnt offering, you shall offer these" (Numbers 28:23) — the daily offering is sacrificed first.

How Do We Extend Tadir Beyond the Tamid?

Gemara
The verse only proves the Tamid comes before Musaf. How do we know any more-frequent offering precedes a less-frequent one?
R' Ile'a
From "Ka'eileh ta'asu" — 'Like these you shall offer daily' (Num. 28:24). The word כָּאֵלֶּה is extra — it teaches the general principle of frequency-based precedence.
Gemara
But that word is needed to teach that the same Musaf offerings are brought each day of Pesach!
R' Ile'a
The phrase "ta'asu" (you shall offer) already teaches that all days are equal. So "ka'eileh" is indeed superfluous and available for the tadir principle.
Abaye
We don't even need that verse! The phrase "which is for a daily burnt offering" is itself superfluous — it generalizes: whatever is more frequent precedes.
Resolution Both R' Ile'a and Abaye agree on the principle — they disagree only on which verse is the source. The Torah's redundant language teaches that tadir applies universally.

Chatat vs. Olah: A Split Precedence

Chatat Wins (Blood)

  • Blood of Chatat is placed before blood of Olah
  • Reason: Chatat effects atonement (meratzeh)
  • All 4 placements precede the Olah's blood
  • Source: "Offer the one for a sin offering" (Num. 8:12)

Olah Wins (Burning)

  • Limbs of Olah are burned before portions of Chatat
  • Reason: Olah is entirely consumed (kalil) on the altar
  • Source: "A second bull you shall take for a sin offering" (Num. 8:8)
  • The word 'second' puts Chatat's burning after the Olah
Key Insight The same two offerings switch priority depending on which stage of the service we are discussing — a remarkable Talmudic nuance.

Why Do All 4 Blood Placements of the Chatat Precede?

Gemara
Only the first placement of the Chatat's blood effects atonement. Why should the remaining three also precede the Olah's blood?
Ravina
The Levites' consecration Chatat did not effect atonement at all, yet the Torah still gave it priority. This proves all Chatat blood placements precede.
Eretz Yisrael Sages
Simpler answer: once the Kohen begins the Chatat's blood service, he completes all placements before moving to another offering.
Two Approaches Ravina derives the rule from the unique case of the Levites' consecration. The Sages of Eretz Yisrael invoke a procedural principle: once you start, you finish.
