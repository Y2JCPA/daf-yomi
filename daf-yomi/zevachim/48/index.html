↓

↓

Why List Sin Offerings Before Burnt Offerings?

Gemara
The Torah explicitly states northward slaughter only for burnt offerings (Lev 1:11). Why does the mishna list sin offerings first?
Answer
Since the sin offering's location is derived through interpretation (Lev 6:18), it is dear to the tanna — so he gives it precedence.
Gemara
But then teach the external sin offerings first — those are what the verse directly references!
Answer
The Yom Kippur sin offerings are taught first because their blood enters the innermost sanctum — making them dearest of all.
Principle The ordering of the mishna reflects the tanna's pedagogical priorities: derived halakhot are beloved, and inner-sanctum offerings hold the highest status.

Deriving Northward Slaughter for Bulls

Leviticus 1:11 — Sheep (כֶּבֶשׂ) burnt offering: slaughter on the north side of the altar
↓
What about a bull burnt offering? Where is the source?
↓
Leviticus 1:10 — The conjunctive vav (וְאִם) links the flock passage to the preceding cattle passage
↓
The upper passage (bull) learns from the lower passage (sheep) — northward slaughter applies to both
Vav Ha-Chibur The letter vav at the start of Lev 1:10 connects the passages, allowing us to derive the law of one from the other.

R' Akiva vs. Rabbis: Provisional Guilt Offering for Uncertain Me'ilah

Rabbi Akiva

  • We learn the upper passage from the lower passage
  • The provisional guilt offering passage follows the me'ilah guilt offering
  • One who is uncertain whether he committed me'ilah must bring an asham talui
  • Derives from the juxtaposition of the passages

Rabbis (Chakhamim)

  • Also agree we learn upper from lower passages
  • But use a gezeira shava to limit the asham talui
  • Just as sin offerings are only for acts punishable by karet — so too asham talui
  • Me'ilah is NOT punishable by karet → no asham talui required
Rav Pappa's Clarification Both sides agree on learning upper from lower passages. The dispute is about the gezeira shava comparing the asham talui to the sin offering for forbidden fats (Lev 4:27).

What the Gezeira Shava Teaches Each Opinion

OpinionSource VerseDerivation
RabbisLev 5:17 ↔ Lev 4:27 — "commandments not to be done"Asham talui only for sins punishable by karet with a fixed sin offering for unwitting violation
R' AkivaSame gezeira shavaAsham talui only for uncertain violation of a sin requiring a fixed (not sliding-scale) sin offering
R' Akiva excludesTumah of Mikdash/KodshimSliding-scale offering (oleh v'yored) — not fixed → no asham talui
Rabbis respondPrinciple: no gezeira shava for halfOnce compared, the offerings must be completely similar — both derivations apply

Fixing Northward Slaughter for Sin Offerings

📖 Lev 6:18 establishes that sin offerings are slaughtered where burnt offerings are — in the north
↓
🐐 Lev 4:24 singles out the king's goat sin offering for northward slaughter
↓
❓ Why single it out if we already know the general rule?
↓
🔒 To teach: if NOT slaughtered in the north, the sin offering is disqualified even after the fact (b'di'eved)
↓
📜 Lev 4:29 & 4:33 repeat the rule for lamb and she-goat sin offerings — confirming all sin offerings are disqualified if not in the north
L'Kav'oah Lo Makom The Torah specifies northward slaughter for the king's sin offering to fix the place — teaching disqualification, not merely a preferred practice.
