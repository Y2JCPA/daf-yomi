QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Rabbis, why is one liable for burning an olive-bulk of incense outside the courtyard even after placing a larger amount in a vessel?
A. Because designation by vessel is considered insignificant — it creates no binding obligation
B. Because the vessel sanctifies only an olive-bulk at a time
C. Because the Torah explicitly states the olive-bulk measure for incense
D. Because the Rabbis follow a stricter interpretation of vessel consecration
Correct: A — The Rabbis hold that designation by placing in a vessel is 'nothing' — it creates no obligation to burn the entire contents. Therefore each olive-bulk stands independently and generates liability.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In Rava's analysis, if one designated 4 log of wine for a ram's libation and poured 3 log outside, why is one liable?
A. Because 4 log is the required amount for a ram
B. Because 3 log is a valid libation measure for a lamb
C. Because any amount of consecrated wine generates liability
D. Because the vessel consecrated exactly 3 log
Correct: B — Three log of wine is a valid libation for a lamb (Numbers 28:14). Since the Rabbis hold vessel designation is meaningless, the 3 log stands as an independently valid measure, generating liability.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What challenge did Reish Lakish raise against R' Yochanan's claim that R' Elazar derived the water libation from R' Akiva's verse?
A. R' Akiva never discussed the water libation
B. The verse in Numbers doesn't mention water at all
C. If derived from the same source as wine, water should require 3 log and apply year-round — but R' Elazar limits it to Sukkot
D. Reish Lakish held the water libation was only rabbinic
Correct: C — Reish Lakish argued that if both libations share the same verse-source, their rules should match — 3 log and year-round. Since R' Elazar limits the water libation to Sukkot, it must derive from a different source.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does R' Elazar b'R' Shimon differ from the first Tanna regarding the water libation?
A. He holds the water libation is not a Torah obligation
B. He requires exactly 3 log in the service vessel — more than 3 log invalidates the consecration
C. He holds one is liable for any amount of water poured outside
D. He derives the water libation from a different verse than R' Akiva
Correct: B — R' Elazar b'R' Shimon holds there is a precise measure — the vessel must contain exactly 3 log. If filled with more, the consecration is ineffective and pouring outside does not generate liability.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What principle does Rav Ashi use to defend Abaye against Rava's refutation?
A. The inner sanctum always takes precedence over the outer sanctum
B. Different rites in the same location can be compared, but identical rites across locations cannot
C. Identical rites (incense to incense) can be compared across sanctums, even though different rites in the same sanctum cannot
D. All measures for liability are derived exclusively from Torah verses
Correct: C — Rav Ashi distinguishes: the Rabbis reject comparing different rites (libation vs. incense) within the outer sanctum, but they DO compare the same rite (incense) performed in the outer vs. inner sanctum.
