QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why can't a Paschal offering slaughtered not for its sake become an animal tithe?
A. Because animal tithe is kodshei kodashim
B. Because the verse limits tithe to 'the tenth' animal specifically
C. Because animal tithe requires libations
D. Because animal tithe can only come from cattle
Correct: B — The verse states 'ha-asiri yihyeh kodesh' — THE tenth shall be holy. The definite article limits tithe status exclusively to the actual tenth animal passing under the rod.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Yosei b. R' Avin, why does a Paschal offering become a shelamim rather than a chatat?
A. Because shelamim are eaten by all Jews while chatat is eaten only by kohanim
B. Because offerings of lesser sanctity are diverted only to other offerings of lesser sanctity
C. Because the Torah explicitly says so in Leviticus 3:6
D. Because a chatat requires specific intent to be valid
Correct: B — R' Yosei b. R' Avin's principle is that kodashim kalim (lesser sanctity) are diverted to kodashim kalim. A chatat is kodshei kodashim (most sacred), so the Paschal cannot become one.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara need three separate verses for leftover Paschal offerings becoming peace offerings?
A. Each verse addresses a different species of animal
B. One is for Pesach Rishon, one for Pesach Sheni, and one for both
C. Each verse covers a different combination of whether the time and/or year have passed
D. Three verses are needed to override three separate prohibitions
Correct: C — The three scenarios are: (1) both time and year passed, (2) time passed but not year, (3) neither passed. Without each verse, we'd limit the halakha to only the most 'disqualified' cases.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the corrected version of Mavog's statement, why is a sin offering slaughtered for Nahshon's atonement considered fit?
A. Because Nahshon's sin offering was unique to the Tabernacle dedication
B. Because Nahshon is dead and there is no atonement for the dead, so it's not a valid change of owner
C. Because all sin offerings follow one law regardless of the owner
D. Because the sin offering of a prince has special status
Correct: B — Since Nahshon is dead and there is no atonement for the dead, designating him as the 'owner' is meaningless — it does not constitute a valid shinui ba'alim (change of owner).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why would slaughtering a standard sin offering for someone obligated to bring a nazirite's sin offering render it unfit?
A. Because a nazirite's sin offering requires a different species
B. Because it constitutes a valid change of owner (shinui ba'alim), since the nazirite is alive and obligated in a chatat
C. Because a nazirite's sin offering is kodshei kodashim
D. Because the nazirite must slaughter the offering himself
Correct: B — Unlike Nahshon who is dead, a living nazirite is a real person obligated in a sin offering. Therefore, slaughtering someone else's sin offering for his atonement is a genuine shinui ba'alim, which disqualifies a chatat.
