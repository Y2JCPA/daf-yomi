Zevachim 9: Why Specifically a Peace Offering?

ואם זבח שלמים קרבנו מן הצאן (ויקרא ג:ו)
DAF OVERVIEW The Gemara explores why a Paschal offering (korban Pesach) slaughtered not for its sake becomes specifically a peace offering (shelamim) — and not any other sacrifice. A series of challenges asks: why not animal tithe, firstborn, substitute, or thanks offering? Each is refuted through scriptural derivation. The daf then discusses three necessary verses for leftover Paschal offerings, and concludes with Mavog's halakha about a sin offering slaughtered for the sake of Nahshon's sin offering.
3 / 9

Why Does the Paschal Become a Peace Offering?

Initial Answer
Offerings eaten by everyone (Paschal) are diverted to other offerings eaten by everyone (shelamim), not to offerings eaten only by priests (sin/guilt offerings).
R' Yosei b. R' Avin
Offerings of lesser sanctity (kodashim kalim) are diverted to other offerings of lesser sanctity — i.e., shelamim — not to offerings of the most sacred order (kodshei kodashim).
KEY PRINCIPLE The Paschal offering can only 'fall' into a category that shares its fundamental halachic characteristics.

Challenge: Why Not Animal Tithe (Ma'aser Behemah)?

Paschal offering slaughtered for the sake of animal tithe
↓
Both are kodashim kalim — so it should become tithe!
↓
If so: no libations required, and selling it would violate 'lo yiga'el' (Lev. 27:33)
↓
Answer: 'הָעֲשִׂירִי יִהְיֶה קֹּדֶשׁ' — 'The tenth shall be holy' (Lev. 27:32). The tenth is tithe; another animal slaughtered for its sake is not.
SCRIPTURAL EXCLUSION The definite article 'ha-asiri' (the tenth) limits tithe status to the actual tenth animal counted under the rod.

Thanks Offering Challenge — The 'Bread' Objection

The Challenge

  • A todah is kodashim kalim — same as Paschal
  • If diverted to todah, it would require 40 loaves
  • This would be a practical consequence

The Refutation

  • A Paschal offering itself doesn't require bread
  • Can its leftover suddenly require bread?
  • No leftover of a todah itself requires bread
  • So a leftover from another category certainly doesn't
LOGIC The Gemara distinguishes: shelamim leftover requiring libations is acceptable because shelamim inherently require libations. But a todah leftover doesn't require bread — so another offering's leftover certainly shouldn't.

Three Verses for Leftover Paschal Offerings

📅 Scenario 1: Both Passover eve and its first year have passed — completely disqualified as Paschal
↓
📅 Scenario 2: Passover eve passed, but still in first year — could still serve for Pesach Sheni
↓
📅 Scenario 3: Neither time nor year passed — still fully eligible as a Paschal offering
↓
📜 All three verses are necessary — each scenario requires its own source that it may be sacrificed as a shelamim
WHY ALL THREE? Without each verse, we might think the more 'eligible' the animal remains for Pesach, the less it can become a peace offering. Each verse teaches otherwise.
