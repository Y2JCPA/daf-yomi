Zevachim 3 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, why is a get written for the sake of a gentile woman invalid?
A. Because a gentile woman is 'of the same type' as a Jewish woman for divorce
B. Because removing the gentile-woman intent leaves a stam get, which is pasul
C. Because the Torah explicitly forbids writing a get for a gentile woman
D. Because the scribe had improper intent during the writing
Correct: B — Rava's key principle: strip away the invalid intent and assess what remains. A stam (unspecified) get is pasul, unlike a stam offering which is kasher.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the first proof from Gittin that a stam get is invalid (the case of two men with the same name)?
A. The mishna is discussing a rabbinic, not biblical, disqualification
B. The get was specifically designated for the first man's divorce, so it's not truly stam
C. The get lacked proper witnesses
D. The two men lived in different towns
Correct: B — Each attempted proof is rejected because the get in question was written with a specific person in mind — it was designated, not unspecified (stam). The Gemara seeks a case proving stam itself is invalid.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rav Yosef bar Ami resolve the apparent contradiction between type deviation and owner deviation?
A. He says one ruling is Rav's and the other is Shmuel's
B. Each disqualification derives from a different verse with different scope
C. He applies the stam principle differently to each case
D. He says the contradiction stands and Rav retracted one statement
Correct: B — 'V'shachat otah l'chatat' governs type (another chatat still qualifies as 'l'chatat'). 'V'nislach lo' governs owner ('he' excludes a similar person). Different verses, different rules.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Elazar, what is the actual source for Rav's ruling that non-sacred intent doesn't disqualify an offering?
A. A kal vachomer from gittin
B. The principle that 'not of its type' doesn't affect it
C. The verse 'they shall not desecrate the sacred items' — only sacred intent desecrates sacred items
D. A tradition from Moshe at Sinai (halacha l'Moshe miSinai)
Correct: C — R' Elazar derives from Leviticus 22:15 that only intent involving sacred items (other offerings) can desecrate sacred items — non-sacred (chulin) intent cannot.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why can't the verse 'el tocho' (into its interior) be used to override R' Eliezer's kal vachomer about partitions in earthenware ovens?
A. Because R' Eliezer doesn't accept that verse as authoritative
B. Because that verse is needed to teach that food kneaded in clay placed in an impure oven is still tamei
C. Because the verse only applies to creeping animals, not to ovens
D. Because a kal vachomer always overrides a verse
Correct: B — The verse 'el tocho' is expended teaching that clay-covered food in an impure oven is still impure — it cannot simultaneously be used to exclude R' Eliezer's kal vachomer about partitions.
