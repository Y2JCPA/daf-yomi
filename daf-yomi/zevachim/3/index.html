Daf 3: Wrong Intent — Of Its Type vs. Not of Its Type

Core Principle from Rav:
Sin offering slaughtered l'shem olah (burnt offering) = Pasul
Sin offering slaughtered l'shem chulin (non-sacred) = Kasher
↓
Rava asks: Why does a get written for a non-Jewish woman invalidate it?
A gentile woman is 'not of its type' — it should be ignored!
↓
Rava's Resolution — Get
Remove the gentile-woman intent → the get is stam (unspecified) → Pasul, because a stam get is invalid
Rava's Resolution — Korban
Remove the non-sacred intent → the offering is stam (unspecified) → Kasher, because a stam offering is valid
KEY INSIGHT The disqualification isn't about wrong intent directly — it's about what remains when you strip the invalid intent away. For gittin, stam is pasul; for korbanos, stam is kasher.

Proving a Get Must Be Written for a Specific Woman

Gemara
Derive from the mishna: A man wrote a get, reconsidered, and another man with the same name and wife's name wants to use it — it's pasul!
Challenger
Perhaps that's different — the get was specifically designated for the first man's divorce. A stam get might still be valid.
Gemara
Then derive from: He has two wives with identical names, wrote a get to divorce the older — he can't use it for the younger!
Challenger
Same problem — it was designated for the other wife specifically.
Gemara
Then: He told the scribe 'write for whichever wife I choose' — pasul for both!
Challenger
Perhaps that's because there is no retroactive designation (bereirah), not because stam is invalid.
Gemara
Final proof: A scribe writing tofsei gittin in advance must leave blank the names, date, and the key phrase 'harei at muteret l'chol adam' — because it must be written lishmah!
CONCLUSION Shmuel (via Rav Yehuda): Even the essential formula of a get must be written lishmah — for that specific woman. A stam get is pasul.

Rava's Contradiction: Get vs. Korban

Korbanos (Offerings)

  • Sin offering → slaughtered l'shem olah = PASUL
  • Sin offering → slaughtered l'shem chulin = KASHER
  • Wrong intent 'of its type' (another korban) disqualifies
  • Wrong intent 'not of its type' (non-sacred) does NOT disqualify
  • Stam (unspecified) offering = Kasher

Gittin (Divorce)

  • Get written for wrong Jewish woman = PASUL
  • Get written for a gentile woman = also PASUL
  • A gentile woman is 'not of its type' — yet it still disqualifies!
  • This seems to contradict the korban principle!
  • Stam (unspecified) get = Pasul
RAVA'S RESOLUTION Strip away the invalid intent: for gittin, you're left with stam which is pasul. For korbanos, you're left with stam which is kasher. The difference isn't about the wrong intent — it's about the default status.

Earthenware Vessel: Partitions & Impurity

Baraita: 'Into whose interior' (Lev. 11:33)
Food in its interior becomes tamei
But not food in the interior of its interior
↓
Even a metal vessel inside an earthenware vessel protects food within it — though metal is 'not the same type' as earthenware!
↓
Rava's Answer
Metal & earthenware are both vessels — they ARE of the same type regarding impurity laws
R' Eliezer's View
A partition (mechitzah) inside an oven also protects food, via kal vachomer from corpse-tent impurity
CHALLENGE If corpse impurity (severe) is blocked by a partition because it works through 'tenting' — should earthenware impurity (milder but NOT tent-based) also be blocked? The Rabbis say no; R' Eliezer says the kal vachomer overrides.

Can a Verse Override a Kal VaChomer?

Gemara
R' Eliezer's kal vachomer overrides the principle that 'not of its type' doesn't affect. If so, apply it to korbanos too!
Gemara
If slaughtering for another korban disqualifies, kal vachomer — slaughtering for chulin should certainly disqualify!
R' Elazar
Rav's reasoning is actually from a verse: 'V'lo yechalelu et kodshei Bnei Yisrael' (Lev. 22:15) — only sacred intent desecrates sacred items, not non-sacred intent.
Gemara
If a verse can override a kal vachomer — let the verse 'el tocho' override R' Eliezer's kal vachomer about partitions!
Gemara
That verse is needed for another halakha: food kneaded in clay and placed in an impure oven is still tamei — lest you think the clay interposes.
PRINCIPLE A biblical verse (pasuk) can come to exclude an a fortiori (kal vachomer) inference — but only if the verse is not already expended teaching another halakha.

Rav Yosef bar Ami: Deviation in Type vs. Deviation in Owner

ScenarioType DeviationOwner Deviation
Sin offering → for another sin offering✅ Kasher (same type)—
Sin offering → for a burnt offering❌ Pasul (different type)—
Sin offering → for person owing a sin offering—❌ Pasul (same type of obligation)
Sin offering → for person owing a burnt offering—✅ Kasher (different type of obligation)
PrincipleSame type = Kasher; Different type = PasulSame type = Pasul; Different type = Kasher
SEEMING CONTRADICTION For type deviation, 'of its type' is fine but 'not of its type' disqualifies. For owner deviation, it's the opposite! 'Of its type' disqualifies but 'not of its type' is fine.
