Zevachim 52: Remainder Blood & the Altar's Base

וְאֶת כׇּל דַּם הַפָּר יִשְׁפֹּךְ אֶל יְסוֹד מִזְבַּח הָעֹלָה (ויקרא ד:ז)
DAF OVERVIEW The Gemara analyzes where the remainder of sacrificial blood (שיריים) must be poured, debating whether failure to pour it disqualifies the offering. The discussion extends to the bull of Yom Kippur, the significance of blood placement above or below the red line (חוט הסיקרא), and whether the inner altar's offerings have different rules for completing atonement.

Where Does the Remainder Blood Go?

Blood sprinkled on the external altar
↓
Could remainder go on the inner altar?
❌ No
The inner altar has no base (יסוד) — impossible to pour there
✅ Therefore
Remainder poured on the base of the external altar
KEY VERSE "At the base of the altar of burnt offering" (Leviticus 4:25) — teaches that remainder blood goes to the external altar's base
4 / 9

What Does "At the Base of the Altar" Teach?

Initial Reading
The verse teaches that sprinkling must be done where there is a base — i.e., on the part of the altar that has a יסוד
Gemara
It says "altar of" burnt offering — not just "base of the burnt offering." The extra word teaches something else
Gemara
Without "altar of," we'd think blood goes on the upright wall of the base. With it, we learn it goes on the upper surface of the base
PRECISION The Torah's wording distinguishes between the side of the base and its upper surface — blood must go on top
5 / 9

Rabbi Yishmael vs. Rabbi Akiva: A Fortiori for the Base

Rabbi Yishmael

  • Remainder of sin offering blood does not effect atonement
  • Yet it requires the upper surface of the base
  • Kal va-ḥomer: Initial sprinkling of burnt offering, which does effect atonement — certainly requires upper surface

Rabbi Akiva

  • Remainder does not effect atonement AND does not come for atonement
  • Yet it requires the upper surface of the base
  • Kal va-ḥomer: Burnt offering sprinkling, which effects and comes for atonement — certainly requires upper surface
BOTH AGREE The verse "at the base of the altar of burnt offering" ultimately teaches: give a base to the altar — all remainder blood must be poured on the base

The Bull of Yom Kippur — Source for Base-Pouring

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
The seemingly extra word "of the bull" in Leviticus 4:7 teaches about another bull — the bull of Yom Kippur also requires pouring blood on the base
Rabbi Yishmael
No verse needed! Kal va-ḥomer: If the anointed priest's bull, which is not obligatory, requires base-pouring — the Yom Kippur bull, which is obligatory, certainly does
Rabbi Akiva
Kal va-ḥomer from a different angle: The anointed priest's bull, whose blood doesn't enter the Holy of Holies, requires it — certainly the Yom Kippur bull, whose blood does enter, requires it
THREE VIEWS Rebbi derives it from a verse; R' Yishmael from the obligatory nature; R' Akiva from entry into the innermost sanctum
