Zevachim 52 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why can't the remainder of external altar blood be poured on the inner altar?
A. The inner altar has no base (יסוד)
B. It would be a forbidden mixture of bloods
C. The inner altar is only for incense
D. The Kohen Gadol would be the only one permitted
Correct: A — The Gemara explains that the inner altar itself does not have a base, making it impossible to pour remainder blood there. Therefore the verse teaches it goes on the external altar's base.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the phrase 'altar of burnt offering' (rather than just 'burnt offering') teach?
A. That only burnt offerings require the base
B. That blood goes on the upper surface of the base, not the side wall
C. That the altar must be built before offering sacrifices
D. That the blood must be sprinkled by the officiating priest only
Correct: B — Without the word 'altar of,' one might think blood goes on the upright wall (side) of the base. The extra word teaches it must be placed on the upper surface of the base.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, what is the actual dispute between Rabbi Yishmael and Rabbi Akiva?
A. Whether remainder blood must go on the base at all
B. Whether the bull of Yom Kippur requires base-pouring
C. Whether failure to squeeze (מיצוי) bird sin offering blood disqualifies
D. Whether blood placed below the red line effects atonement
Correct: C — Rav Pappa says both agree that failure to pour remainder blood does not disqualify. Their dispute is about whether failure to squeeze the blood of a bird sin offering after sprinkling disqualifies the offering.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi derive that the bull of Yom Kippur requires blood on the base?
A. From a kal va-ḥomer based on the anointed priest's bull
B. From the seemingly superfluous word 'of the bull' in Leviticus 4:7
C. From a gezeirah shavah linking Yom Kippur to daily offerings
D. From the general principle that all offerings require base-pouring
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi derives it from the extra word 'of the bull' in the verse, which he reads as referring to another bull — the bull of Yom Kippur. R' Yishmael and R' Akiva instead use kal va-ḥomer arguments.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the Amud B discussion, why is the analogy from bird sin offering (below the line placed above) to animal sin offering (above the line placed below) rejected?
A. Bird offerings are less sacred than animal offerings
B. Animal sin offering blood is eventually sacrificed below the line when remainder is poured on the base
C. The red line applies only to bird offerings
D. Animal sin offerings have four placements while birds have one
Correct: B — The analogy is rejected because animal sin offering blood does eventually go below the red line — when the remainder is poured on the base. So placing it below isn't entirely foreign to that offering, unlike the bird offering which has no connection to above the line.
