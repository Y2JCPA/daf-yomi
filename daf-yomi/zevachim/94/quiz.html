Zevachim 94 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yehuda, which items require laundering when sprinkled with sin-offering blood?
A. Only items currently susceptible to ritual impurity
B. Any item fit to become ritually impure, even if not yet susceptible
C. Only woven garments made of wool or linen
D. All items including hard vessels and hides
Correct: B — R' Yehuda holds that any item 'fit' (ראוי) to become impure requires laundering, even if not yet susceptible — broadening the scope beyond R' Elazar's position.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What example does Abaye give as a practical difference between R' Yehuda and R' Elazar?
A. A leather shoe worn on Shabbat
B. A copper vessel used for cooking
C. A cloth patch less than 3×3 fingerbreadths
D. An earthenware vessel with absorbed blood
Correct: C — A small patch is not yet susceptible to impurity (too small), but is 'fit' to become impure if designated for patching — so R' Yehuda would require laundering but R' Elazar would not.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava's principle, what constitutes 'laundering' for leather?
A. Any contact with water
B. Soaking in water for any duration
C. Rubbing — merely placing water is not laundering
D. Scraping with a dry implement
Correct: C — Rava establishes that laundering without rubbing (שפיכה) is not considered laundering. For leather, merely applying water is permitted even on Shabbat — only rubbing constitutes forbidden laundering.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why is placing a cloth garment into water on Shabbat forbidden, even without rubbing?
A. Because cloth is more valuable than leather
B. Because soaking a cloth IS its laundering — שרייתו זהו כיבוסו
C. Because the cloth might shrink and be ruined
D. Because of a rabbinic decree against all water use on Shabbat
Correct: B — Rava teaches that for fabric, 'its soaking is its laundering' (שרייתו זהו כיבוסו) — unlike leather, which requires rubbing to constitute laundering.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What did Rava do after Rav Pappa pointed out his error about laundering shoes on Shabbat?
A. He ignored the challenge and maintained his ruling
B. He privately changed his opinion but did not announce it
C. He placed an interpreter before him and publicly retracted his ruling
D. He deferred the question to the Sanhedrin for a final ruling
Correct: C — Rava publicly retracted through an interpreter (meturgeman), correcting his earlier statement and teaching that placing water is permitted but laundering (rubbing) shoes is prohibited on Shabbat.
