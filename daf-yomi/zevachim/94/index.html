What Must Be Laundered? Fit vs. Susceptible

R' Yehuda
The verse says "garment" — any item fit to become ritually impure (רָאוּי לְקַבֵּל טֻמְאָה) must be laundered, even if not yet susceptible.
R' Elazar
The verse teaches: just as a garment is already susceptible to impurity as-is, only items presently susceptible require laundering.
Gemara
What practical difference exists between these two opinions? Is there something fit for impurity but not yet susceptible?
Core Dispute R' Yehuda broadens the obligation to potential susceptibility; R' Elazar limits it to current susceptibility. The mishna follows R' Yehuda.

Practical Differences: Abaye vs. Rava

Abaye's Case

  • A cloth patch less than 3×3 fingerbreadths
  • Not yet susceptible to impurity on its own
  • But fit to become impure — owner could designate it to patch a garment
  • R' Yehuda: requires laundering; R' Elazar: does not

Rava's Case

  • A garment intended for an image (decoration not yet added)
  • Considered incomplete → not yet susceptible to impurity
  • But fit — owner can void his intention, making it immediately susceptible
  • Alternate version: an unfinished hide (עוּצְבָא) intended to be trimmed
R' Shimon b. Menasya An unfinished hide intended for trimming is ritually pure until actually trimmed — supporting the distinction between fit and susceptible.

Where on the Garment Must Be Laundered?

Blood of sin offering sprays onto part of a garment
↓
Must the entire garment be laundered?
Initial Thought
Even partial spraying requires laundering the whole garment
Derived Answer
"That on which it shall be sprinkled" — launder only the sprayed area
↓
Vessels are excluded — blood is scraped off, not laundered, since it doesn't absorb
Principle Laundering applies only to materials that absorb blood (fabric, sackcloth, leather) — not hard vessels from which blood can simply be scraped.

Does Leather Require Laundering?

Rabbis
If blood sprays onto leather, one scrapes it off — laundering is not applicable to leather.
Others (Aḥerim)
If blood sprays onto leather, one launders it — leather is subject to laundering just like garments and sackcloth.
Abaye
Our mishna listing hide among items requiring laundering follows the opinion of Others, not the Rabbis.
R' Ḥiyya bar Ashi
Many times I stood before Rav on Shabbat and placed water on his leather shoes — implying cleaning leather isn't laundering.
Contradiction If our mishna says leather requires laundering, why does the Shabbat mishna say applying water to leather is permitted?
