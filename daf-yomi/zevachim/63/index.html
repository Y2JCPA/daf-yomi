↓

Ramp Slope Ratios (Rami bar Ḥama)

Minor Ramps

  • 1 cubit rise per 3 cubits run
  • Steeper grade
  • Used for specific altar services

Main Altar Ramp

  • 1 cubit rise per 3½ cubits + 1⅓ fingerbreadths
  • Slightly gentler slope
  • Easier for priests carrying sacrificial portions
PRACTICAL REASON The main ramp had a gentler slope so priests could ascend safely while carrying heavy sacrificial portions.

Can the Handful Be Removed in the Sanctuary?

R' Elazar
A meal offering whose handful was removed in the Sanctuary is valid — just as the bowls of frankincense were removed from the Shulḥan (Table) inside the Sanctuary.
R' Yirmeyah
Objection! The verse says "from there" (Lev. 2:2) — meaning from a place where a non-priest's feet may stand, i.e., the courtyard, not the Sanctuary!
Ben Beteira
"From there" teaches something else: if the priest took the handful with his left hand, he may return it and retake it with his right hand from the same place.
Resolution
"From there" renders the entire courtyard valid — it was needed lest we require the north side specifically, like other most-sacred offerings.
KEY POINT The verse "from there" teaches that the entire courtyard is valid for taking the handful, not that the Sanctuary is excluded.

Why Not Require the North Side for Meal Offerings?

Could we derive from other most-sacred offerings that the handful must be taken in the north?
↓
From Burnt Offering?
Refuted: it is entirely burned — unique
From Sin Offering?
Refuted: it atones for karet — unique
From Guilt Offering?
Refuted: its rites involve blood — unlike meal offering
↓
Common element also fails: all involve blood, meal offering does not → North not required
LOGIC Each attempted analogy is refuted because every blood-offering has a feature not shared by the meal offering, so no derivation can stand.

Deriving the Southwest Corner for Meal Offerings

Verse PhraseIndicatesSide of Altar
"Before the Lord" (לפני ה׳)Facing the SanctuaryWest side
"In front of the altar" (פני המזבח)Where priests ascend the rampSouth side
Both combinedIntersection of west & southSouthwest corner
R' ELIEZER'S PRINCIPLE When two verses seem to conflict, adopt the reading that fulfills both requirements simultaneously. Bringing the meal offering to the south side also satisfies "before the Lord" since the altar stood in the north, making its south side face the Sanctuary entrance.

Bird Sin Offering Procedure at the Altar

🐦 Priest brings bird sin offering to the altar
↓
✋ Pinching (melikah) — valid anywhere on the altar
↓
🩸 Sprinkling blood — designated at the southwest corner
↓
💧 Squeezing out remaining blood at the base of the altar
↓
✅ Must place "blood of the soul" below the red line on the altar
B'DIAVAD If the priest pinched or sprinkled blood anywhere on the altar (not just the southwest corner), the offering is still valid after the fact.
