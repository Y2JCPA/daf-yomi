5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why was the main altar ramp built with a gentler slope than the minor ramps?
A. To make it easier for priests carrying sacrificial portions
B. To accommodate the length of the altar
C. Because the Torah specifies its exact measurements
D. To allow non-priests to ascend during festivals
Correct: A — Rami bar Ḥama explains the main ramp had a gentler slope (1 cubit rise per 3½+ cubits run vs. 1:3) specifically to help priests ascend while carrying heavy sacrificial portions.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What analogy does R' Elazar use to argue that removing a meal offering's handful in the Sanctuary is valid?
A. The incense offering performed in the Sanctuary
B. The removal of frankincense bowls from the Shewbread Table
C. The Yom Kippur blood service in the Holy of Holies
D. The lighting of the Menorah inside the Sanctuary
Correct: B — R' Elazar draws a parallel to the bowls of frankincense removed from beside the Shewbread on the Table in the Sanctuary — this removal parallels taking a handful from a meal offering.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why can't we derive that a meal offering's handful must be taken in the north side of the courtyard from other most-sacred offerings?
A. Because meal offerings are actually lesser-sacred offerings
B. Because the Torah explicitly states the handful is taken in the south
C. Because all those offerings involve blood-rites, which meal offerings lack
D. Because the north side was reserved exclusively for animal slaughter
Correct: C — The Gemara refutes each attempted analogy (burnt, sin, and guilt offerings) and concludes that the common element among them all — that their rites are performed with blood — distinguishes them from a meal offering.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara reconcile "before the Lord" (west) and "in front of the altar" (south) regarding where to bring a meal offering near the altar?
A. The priest may choose either the west or south side
B. The offering is brought to the southwest corner, satisfying both
C. "Before the Lord" is not taken literally
D. The two verses refer to different types of meal offerings
Correct: B — By bringing the meal offering to the southwest corner of the altar, both verse phrases are fulfilled simultaneously — it is at both the west side and the south side.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Regarding a bird sin offering, what is the distinction between where pinching (melikah) and blood-sprinkling must occur?
A. Both must be at the southwest corner
B. Pinching is at the top of the altar; sprinkling is at the base
C. Pinching is valid anywhere on the altar; sprinkling is designated for the southwest corner
D. Pinching is in the north; sprinkling is in the south
Correct: C — Rav Ashi clarifies the mishna: pinching is valid anywhere on the altar, but the southwest corner is the designated place specifically for the sprinkling of the bird sin offering's blood.
