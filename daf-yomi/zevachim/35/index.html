Zevachim 35 — Blood Collection, Interposition & Piggul Intent

R' Yehuda: Blood spilled on floor may be collected — priests are vigilant
↓
But blood of exudate (dam hatamtzit) mixes with blood of the soul on the floor?
R' Yehuda's Answer
Blood does not nullify blood — mixture may be sprinkled
Rabbis' View
Mixed blood on floor is unfit — drain plugging is just for honor
↓
Mishna: Intent to eat/burn wrong category items — offering is fit (R' Eliezer: unfit)
↓
Items that never render piggul: hide, bones, horns, fetus, placenta, etc.
Core Theme This daf explores which blood is valid for altar service, how priests navigated the blood-filled courtyard, and which items/intents can create piggul disqualification.
3 / 9

Blood of Exudate (Dam HaTamtzit)

Gemara
R' Yehuda allows collecting blood from the floor — but dam hatamtzit is mixed in! How can this be valid?
R' Yehuda
Dam hatamtzit is considered blood — one who consumes it receives karet, not merely lashes.
R' Elazar
But R' Yehuda himself concedes that dam hatamtzit does not effect atonement! As it says: 'the blood that makes atonement by reason of the soul' (Lev. 17:11).
Gemara
Rather, R' Yehuda holds: Blood does not nullify blood (ein dam mevatel dam). The fit blood in the mixture is not nullified by the exudate.
Key Principle Ein dam mevatel dam — Blood does not nullify blood. Even mixed with unfit blood, the valid blood retains its status for sprinkling.

Passover Eve — Walking in Blood

🔒 Drain of the Temple courtyard is plugged on Passover eve
↓
🩸 Priests walk in blood up to their ankles — demonstrating love of service
↓
❓ Isn't blood an interposition (chatzitza) between feet and floor?
↓
💧 Moist substances do not interpose — blood, ink, honey, milk when wet
↓
👗 But garments would be soiled — soiled garments invalidate the rite!
↓
🪵 They only walked in blood while carrying wood to the altar arrangement — not an actual rite
↓
🧱 For actual rites, priests walked on raised platforms above the blood
Resolution Carrying wood to the altar is not classified as an actual rite (avodah), so soiled garments during that task do not invalidate the service.

Bulls That Are Burned — What Gets Disqualified?

Sacrificial Portions (Emurim)

  • Susceptible to piggul through improper intent
  • Rendered disqualified by being left overnight
  • Contact with tevul yom disqualifies
  • One who eats them after piggul — liable for karet

Flesh of the Bulls

  • Never rendered piggul by intent on portions (per Gemara's answer)
  • Subject to misuse of consecrated property (me'ilah)
  • Burned at place of ashes — me'ilah applies until fully scorched
  • R' Elazar: flesh IS rendered piggul — disputed
R' Elazar's View R' Elazar holds that piggul intent regarding the sacrificial portions also disqualifies the flesh. The Gemara brings multiple challenges to this position.

Items That Don't Render Piggul

Intent to eat/burn these items beyond designated time:
↓
Wool of lambs • Hair of goats • Hide • Gravy • Spices • Neck tendon
Crop • Bones • Tendons • Horns • Hooves • Fetus • Placenta • Milk • Eggs
↓
Do They Render Piggul?
No — intent regarding these items does not disqualify the offering
Are They Rendered Piggul?
Disputed — R' Elazar says the rest of the offering CAN render them piggul
↓
Not liable for piggul, notar, or ritual impurity prohibitions for consuming these items
Rava's Proof The Mishna mentions milk and eggs are not subject to karet, but omits fetus and placenta — implying they are subject to karet through piggul of the main offering, supporting R' Elazar.
