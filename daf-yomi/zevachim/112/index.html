Blood placed on altar inside the courtyard first
↓
Remaining blood then offered on altar outside
↓
Is the remaining blood a mere שְׁיָרִים (remainder)?
↓
R' Nechemya
Liable — remainder blood offered outside carries liability
Chakhamim
Exempt — remainder blood has no sacrificial significance outside
PRINCIPLE The mishna's first clause follows R' Nechemya, who holds one is liable even for the shirayim (remainder) of blood offered outside.

Two Cups of Blood — Liability Matrix

ScenarioLiability
Both cups placed inside✅ Exempt — valid service completed
Both cups placed outside❌ Liable — all blood misused
First cup inside, second cup outside✅ Exempt — second cup is disqualified
First cup outside, second cup inside❌ Liable — all blood was fit for inside
KEY DISTINCTION The second cup after valid placement inside is disqualified (pasul), not merely remainder (shirayim). Even R' Nechemya agrees one is exempt for disqualified blood.

The Lost Sin Offering Analogy

Gemara
Why does the mishna need the analogy of a lost sin offering? What does it add?
R' Yehuda HaNasi
A sin offering lost when its substitute was separated — if one is slaughtered, the other must be put to death. It is truly disqualified.
Gemara
So the analogy teaches: exempt because the animal is disqualified, not just a remainder.
Gemara
But if two sin offerings were separated from the outset as a guarantee, the unused one grazes until blemished → sold → proceeds buy an olah.
Rav Huna / Rav
A guilt offering consigned to grazing that was slaughtered — it is fit as a burnt offering. So 'consigned to grazing' = still fit = liable if offered outside.
INSIGHT The analogy clarifies that disqualified ≠ consigned to grazing. Only truly disqualified offerings are exempt outside; those merely 'grazing' retain fitness.

Blemished Animals Offered Outside — R' Shimon vs. Rabbanan

Rabbanan

  • Permanently blemished outside → Exempt
  • Temporarily blemished outside → Exempt
  • Doves not yet ready → Exempt
  • Pigeons past their time → Exempt
  • No karet = no prohibition at all

R' Shimon

  • Permanently blemished → Exempt
  • Temporarily blemished → Prohibition (no karet)
  • Doves not yet ready → Prohibition (will become fit)
  • Pigeons past their time → Exempt
  • Anything fit after time passes → prohibition
R' SHIMON'S RULE Any animal that will become fit with the passage of time carries a prohibition if offered outside — but not karet.
