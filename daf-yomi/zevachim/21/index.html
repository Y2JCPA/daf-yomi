The Basin (Kiyor): Use, Timing & Disqualification

Gemara
We intentionally render the Red Heifer priest impure then immerse him — yet he doesn't need fresh sanctification. This proves impurity doesn't disqualify kiddush yadayim v'raglayim!
Gemara (Responds)
The Red Heifer is different — a tevul yom (one who immersed that day) is fit for it. You can't generalize from it to regular Temple service.
R' Ḥiyya bar Yosef
Basin water is disqualified at sunset for daytime rites (permitting factors) and at dawn for nighttime rites (burning limbs).
Rav Ḥisda
Even for permitting factors, water is disqualified only at dawn — no need to sink the Basin overnight.
R' Yoḥanan
Once sunk at night, the Basin may not be raised until needed. There is a mitzvah to keep it sunk all night ab initio.
Core Issue When does linah (leaving overnight) disqualify the Basin's water — and must the Basin be sunk into its pit all night?

Red Heifer: Deliberate Impurity of the Priest

🔴 Priest designated for the Red Heifer (Parah Adumah)
↓
✋ Priest sanctifies hands and feet from the Basin
↓
⚠️ Sages deliberately render him tamei (impure)
↓
💧 He immerses immediately in a mikveh
↓
🔥 He proceeds to burn the heifer as a tevul yom
↓
🎯 Purpose: Refute the Sadducees who required sunset before service
Key Principle Impurity does not disqualify sanctification of hands and feet — but only for the Red Heifer. Other Temple rites are different.

Dipping in the Basin vs. Washing From It

Can a priest sanctify by dipping hands into the Basin?
↓
"From It" (מִמֶּנּוּ)
Torah says wash from it — meaning water must flow onto hands, not immerse in it
Perhaps...
"From it" could include water still inside the Basin — dipping may be valid
↓
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak: Proof from a baraita — cave water immersion is invalid
↓
Inference: Dipping in the Basin (unlike cave water) would be valid
↓
Gemara rejects: The baraita teaches that cave water is unfit despite being valid for full-body immersion — no proof about Basin dipping
Exodus 30:19 "And Aaron and his sons shall wash their hands and their feet from it (מִמֶּנּוּ)" — the source of the debate.

Three Views: When Is Basin Water Disqualified?

R' Ḥiyya bar Yosef

  • Daytime rites (permitting factors): disqualified at sunset
  • Nighttime rites (burning limbs): disqualified at dawn
  • If raised at any point during the night → disqualified for day service
  • Two separate thresholds for day vs. night

Rav Ḥisda

  • Even permitting factors: disqualified only at dawn
  • No mitzvah to sink the Basin overnight
  • Basin can remain raised all night
  • Most lenient position

R' Yoḥanan

  • Once sunk, Basin may not be raised all night
  • Water disqualified if Basin is raised at dawn
  • Rabbinic decree: keep it sunk to prevent accidental dawn exposure
  • Can raise briefly before dawn, then sink again
Resolution R' Yoḥanan agrees with R' Ḥiyya bar Yosef on the result (sunset/dawn thresholds) but adds a rabbinic obligation to sink the Basin all night ab initio.

Abaye vs. Rava: Reconciling R' Yoḥanan

R' Yoḥanan
Statement 1: The Basin must be sunk all night.
R' Yoḥanan
Statement 2: If a priest sanctified before dawn for ash removal, he doesn't need to sanctify again the next day.
Rava
No problem! Statement 2 follows R' Elazar b'R' Shimon (sanctification not disqualified overnight). Statement 1 follows Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi.
Abaye
Both statements follow Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi — so how can both be true?
Gemara
Resolution: For ash removal, the priest raises the Basin, sanctifies, then sinks it again before dawn — so both statements hold.
Clarification "Does not need to sanctify again" means he need not — not that he may not. The water remains fit.

Ben Katin's Mechanism & the Temple Morning Signal

SignalDetails
⚙️ Ben Katin's WheelA mechanism (mugni) to sink and raise the Basin into its pit of water
🔊 Sound of the WheelPriests would hear the sound of the mechanism and know: the time for sanctification has arrived
🗣️ Gevini the CrierA separate signal — the crier would announce the beginning of Temple service
🎯 Two MarkersBoth signals served the same purpose — whoever heard either one would come to begin service
❓ Raising or Sinking?Debate: Did they hear the wheel raising the Basin (challenging Rav Ḥisda) or sinking it (after being up all night)?
💡 ResolutionEven sinking uses the wheel, or: the priest deliberately used the wheel when sinking so the sound would alert others
Tamid 28b Source for the daily Temple procedure — priests waited for the sound of the mechanism before beginning morning service.
