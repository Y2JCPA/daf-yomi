Zevachim 21 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why do the Sages deliberately render the Red Heifer priest impure before the ritual?
A. To test whether he is a valid priest
B. To refute the Sadducees who required sunset before the ritual
C. To ensure he immerses in fresh mikveh water
D. To demonstrate that impurity always disqualifies sanctification
Correct: B — The Sages deliberately made the priest impure and had him immerse immediately to publicly demonstrate that a tevul yom (one who immersed that day before sunset) is fit for the Red Heifer — contradicting the Sadducean position.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why is immersing one's hands in cave water insufficient for sanctification of hands and feet?
A. Cave water is always ritually impure
B. The Torah requires water to flow from a sacred vessel, not mere immersion
C. Cave water is too cold for proper sanctification
D. Cave water lacks the required minimum volume
Correct: B — The Torah states priests must wash 'from it' (the Basin) — sanctification requires water from a consecrated vessel. Cave water, while valid for full-body immersion in a mikveh, lacks the sanctity of the Basin.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the practical difference between Rav Ḥisda and R' Yoḥanan regarding the Basin at night?
A. Rav Ḥisda requires sinking; R' Yoḥanan does not
B. R' Yoḥanan holds there is a mitzvah to sink the Basin all night; Rav Ḥisda does not
C. Rav Ḥisda disqualifies water at sunset; R' Yoḥanan at dawn
D. They disagree on whether the Basin needs water for four priests
Correct: B — Both agree water is disqualified at dawn for day service. The difference is that R' Yoḥanan holds there is a rabbinic mitzvah to keep the Basin sunk all night (to prevent accidental dawn exposure), while Rav Ḥisda sees no such obligation.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Yosei son of R' Ḥanina, what is the minimum water capacity of the Basin?
A. Enough for one priest (the Kohen Gadol)
B. Enough for two priests (Aaron and one son)
C. Enough for four priests (Moses, Aaron, and his two sons)
D. Enough for all the priests of the daily watch
Correct: C — R' Yosei b'R' Ḥanina derives from Exodus 40:31 — 'that Moses and Aaron and his sons might wash' — that the Basin must hold enough for four people: Moses, Aaron, and Aaron's two sons.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What was the purpose of the sound made by Ben Katin's mechanism?
A. To frighten away birds from the altar
B. To signal the priests that the time for sanctification had arrived
C. To announce the end of the nighttime service
D. To indicate that the Basin's water had been replaced with fresh water
Correct: B — The sound of Ben Katin's wheel mechanism — used to raise or sink the Basin — served as one of two signals (along with Gevini the crier) telling priests that the time for morning sanctification had arrived.
