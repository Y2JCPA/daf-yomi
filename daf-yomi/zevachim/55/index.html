Zevachim 55: Communal Peace Offerings & the Open-Door Requirement

Question: Where must communal peace offerings (Shavuot sheep) be slaughtered?
↓
Rabba bar Rav Ḥanan: Juxtaposed with sin offering → north
↓
Rava objects: Sin offering's own rule comes from juxtaposition with burnt offering — double hekesh problem!
↓
Rava's solution: Use Numbers 10:10 — burnt offerings directly juxtaposed with communal peace offerings → most sacred order → north
Core Principle Communal peace offerings share the stringencies of most sacred offerings, while individual peace offerings follow lesser sanctity rules requiring only that the Sanctuary doors be open.
3 / 9

Deriving Northward Slaughter for Communal Peace Offerings

Rabba bar Rav Ḥanan
Leviticus 23:19 juxtaposes the two Shavuot sheep with the sin offering → communal peace offerings must be slaughtered in the north, like a sin offering.
Rava
But the sin offering's own northward rule is derived from juxtaposition with the burnt offering (Lev. 6:18). Can a hekesh-derived law teach via another hekesh? In kodashim — no!
Rava
Rather, use Rav Mari b. Rav Kahana's source: Numbers 10:10 directly juxtaposes burnt offerings with communal peace offerings → they are kodshei kodashim → slaughtered in the north.
Hekesh Rule In consecrated matters, a halakha derived via juxtaposition (hekesh) cannot then be used to teach another halakha via a second juxtaposition.

Communal vs. Individual Peace Offerings

Communal Peace Offerings (שלמי ציבור)

  • The two Shavuot sheep
  • Status: Kodshei Kodashim (most sacred)
  • Slaughtered in the NORTH
  • Eaten only by MALE PRIESTS
  • Derived from Numbers 10:10 juxtaposition

Individual Peace Offerings (שלמי יחיד)

  • Thanks offering, nazirite's ram, etc.
  • Status: Kodashim Kalim (lesser sanctity)
  • Slaughtered ANYWHERE in the courtyard
  • Eaten by ANY ritually pure Jew
  • Must be slaughtered when Sanctuary doors are OPEN
Key Distinction The original Lev. 23:19 hekesh is repurposed: it teaches that communal peace offerings are eaten only by male priests, like a sin offering — not the northward slaughter rule.

The Nazirite's Ram Challenge

Abaye
If the Lev. 23:19 hekesh teaches that communal peace offerings are eaten only by male priests — then Numbers 6:14 juxtaposes the nazirite's ram with a sin offering too! Should it also be eaten only by males?
Rava
No — Numbers 6:19 says the priest takes the foreleg. By inference, the owner eats the rest. The verse itself limits the priestly portion.
Gemara
But at least the foreleg should be restricted to male priests! Yet the Mishna says priests' wives and slaves may eat it — kashya (difficulty remains).
Gemara
Alternative answer: The foreleg is called kodesh but not kodshei kodashim, so the sin-offering juxtaposition doesn't apply to eating restrictions.
Rava
The nazirite hekesh actually teaches: if the nazirite shaved after any one of the three offerings, he fulfills his obligation bedi'avad.
Resolution The nazirite's ram juxtaposition with the sin offering teaches the shaving rule, not eating restrictions.

Mishna: Thanks Offering & Nazirite's Ram — Rules of Lesser Sanctity

HalakhaRule
Sanctity LevelKodashim Kalim (lesser sanctity)
Slaughter LocationAnywhere in the Temple courtyard (azarah)
Blood ApplicationTwo placements that are four (שתיים שהן ארבע)
Who May EatAny ritually pure Jew (כל אדם)
Where EatenThroughout Jerusalem
PreparationAny manner of food preparation
Time LimitDay of sacrifice + following night until midnight
Priestly PortionsEaten by priests, their wives, children, and slaves
Note The priestly portions (breast and thigh) follow the same time and location rules as the owner's portions, but are restricted to the priestly household — including women and slaves.

Peace Offerings & the Open-Door Requirement

One wants to slaughter a peace offering (שלמים)
↓
Are the doors of the Sanctuary (Heikhal) currently open?
✅ Open
Valid. Slaughter may proceed anywhere in the courtyard.
❌ Closed/Locked
Disqualified (pasul). 'At the entrance' means when it functions as an entrance.
↓
What about a curtain covering the entrance?
↓
R' Zeira: A curtain is made to serve as an open entrance → Valid
Source Rav Yehuda citing Shmuel: 'He shall slaughter it at the entrance to the Tent of Meeting' (Lev. 3:2) — when the entrance is open, not when it is locked.
