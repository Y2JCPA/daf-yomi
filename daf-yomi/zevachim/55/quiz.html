QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Rava reject Rabba bar Rav Ḥanan's derivation that communal peace offerings must be slaughtered in the north?
A. The verse in Leviticus 23:19 is not about Shavuot offerings
B. A law derived via juxtaposition cannot teach another law via juxtaposition in kodashim
C. Peace offerings are never slaughtered in the north
D. The sin offering is not actually slaughtered in the north
Correct: B — Rava objects that the sin offering's own northward requirement comes from a juxtaposition with the burnt offering (Lev. 6:18). In consecrated matters, a double hekesh (juxtaposition deriving from a juxtaposition) is not valid.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What happens if one slaughters a peace offering before the doors of the Sanctuary are opened?
A. The offering is valid but requires an additional sacrifice
B. The offering is valid since the courtyard itself is sanctified
C. The offering is disqualified (pasul)
D. The offering must be re-slaughtered after the doors open
Correct: C — Shmuel derives from 'at the entrance to the Tent of Meeting' (Lev. 3:2) that slaughter must occur when the entrance is actually open and functioning as an entrance. Closed doors = disqualified.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Gemara's conclusion, what does the juxtaposition of the nazirite's ram with the sin offering in Numbers 6:14 teach?
A. The nazirite's ram must be slaughtered in the north
B. Only male priests may eat the nazirite's ram
C. If the nazirite shaved after any one of the three offerings, he fulfills his obligation bedi'avad
D. The nazirite's ram has the status of kodshei kodashim
Correct: C — Rava explains that the juxtaposition teaches that if the nazirite shaved after sacrificing any one of his three offerings (burnt, sin, or peace), he has fulfilled his shaving obligation after the fact.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What was the function of the two wickets in the Chamber of Knives?
A. To allow priests to bring knives into the courtyard
B. To render the entire courtyard fit for eating most sacred offerings and slaughtering lesser sanctity offerings
C. To separate the men's courtyard from the women's courtyard
D. To allow smoke from the altar to escape the courtyard
Correct: B — The wickets, placed in the corners of the Chamber of Knives (one facing NW, one NE), created openings so that all parts of the courtyard were considered 'before the Tent of Meeting,' rendering them valid for slaughter and eating of offerings.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does R' Zeira rule regarding a curtain covering the entrance to the Sanctuary?
A. It disqualifies offerings slaughtered while it is in place
B. It is valid only if the curtain is partially drawn aside
C. A curtain is designed to serve as an open entrance, so it does not disqualify
D. It is valid only for offerings of lesser sanctity
Correct: C — R' Zeira teaches that a curtain is inherently meant to function as an open entrance — unlike a closed or locked door, a curtain does not prevent the area from being considered 'at the entrance to the Tent of Meeting.'
