When Are Limbs on the Altar Considered Consumed?

Rabba
The second midnight (following night) renders limbs consumed — if dislodged after that, they are not returned.
Rav Ḥisda
Dawn following the first evening renders them consumed — a kal va-ḥomer from midnight.
Rav Yosef
Who says midnight only works when limbs are on the altar? Midnight effects consumption wherever the limbs are.
Eretz Yisrael
The halakha follows Rav Yosef — midnight renders all limbs consumed regardless of location.
KEY PRINCIPLE After midnight passes, the mitzvah of burning limbs is considered fulfilled — they exit the laws of me'ilah (misuse of consecrated property).

When Does 'Consumption' Occur? Rabba vs. Rav Ḥisda

Rabba
Limbs on the altar are consumed at the second midnight (the midnight following the night they were placed).
Rav Ḥisda
Dawn renders them consumed — if midnight (which doesn't cause linah) causes consumption, surely dawn (which does cause linah) causes consumption!
Rabba
If limbs separated before midnight and returned after dawn — the second midnight still renders them consumed.
Rav Ḥisda
If not returned by dawn, they are never subject to time-based consumption. They burn on the altar until reduced to ash, but the time-mechanism doesn't apply.
COMMON GROUND Both agree: limbs NOT on the altar by midnight are not considered consumed at that point.

Rav Yosef's Challenge & the Ruling from Eretz Yisrael

Limbs separate from the altar before midnight
↓
Does midnight effect consumption only on the altar?
Rabba & Rav Ḥisda
Midnight only works on top of the altar — limbs off the altar are not consumed
Rav Yosef
Midnight effects consumption wherever the limbs are found
↓
✅ Ruling from Eretz Yisrael: Halakha follows Rav Yosef
PRACTICAL IMPACT R' Ḥiyya bar Abba & bar Kappara confirm: limbs separated before midnight and returned after — one may not benefit from them, but no me'ilah liability applies.

Linah (Left Overnight) on Top of the Altar: Rabba vs. Rava

Rabba

  • Linah is NOT effective on top of the altar
  • Compares altar top to the Shewbread Table — loaves left many days are still valid
  • If limbs left overnight on altar then descend → they SHALL ascend back

Rava

  • Linah IS effective even on top of the altar
  • Compares altar top to the courtyard floor — flesh is disqualified at dawn
  • If limbs left overnight on altar then descend → they shall NOT ascend back
GEMARA CONCLUSION Rava did NOT accept Rabba's teaching — he holds that linah disqualifies limbs even atop the altar.
