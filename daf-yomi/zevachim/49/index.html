Can Secondary Status Be More Stringent?

Challenger
Second tithe may be redeemed, but food purchased with second-tithe money may not be redeemed. R' Yehuda says it must be buried — that's more stringent!
Gemara
That's not true stringency. The purchased food has weaker sanctity — it simply cannot grasp its redemption money. The sanctity can't transfer to a third item.
Mar Zutra
What about temura (substitution)? It takes effect on a permanently blemished animal — even though direct consecration doesn't!
Gemara
Substitution derives sanctity from a consecrated animal, so it has stronger sanctity. Direct consecration comes from a non-sacred animal — a weaker source.
Mar Zutra
The Paschal offering doesn't require semicha, libations, or waving — but its remainder (sacrificed as a peace offering) does! More stringent!
Gemara
Once the time for the Paschal offering passes, it becomes a peace offering entirely. Those aren't added stringencies — they're inherent to what it now is.
Conclusion All challenges refuted: secondary status is never more stringent than the primary. This confirms that a burnt offering not slaughtered in the north is disqualified even after the fact.

Deriving the Guilt Offering's Northern Requirement

Source: "In the place where they slaughter the olah, they shall slaughter the asham" (Lev. 7:2)
↓
Olah must be in the north → Asham slaughter must also be in the north
↓
"And its blood shall be sprinkled around the altar" — blood must be collected right after slaughter
↓
Blood collection (kabbalah) must also be in the north
↓
The word "ve'et" (וְאֶת) includes the person collecting the blood
↓
The collector himself must stand in the north
Three Requirements From Leviticus 7:2 the Gemara derives: (1) slaughter in the north, (2) blood collection in the north, (3) the collector stands in the north.

Guilt Offering of a Leper vs. Standard Guilt Offerings

Asham Metzora (Leper)

  • Has unique requirements (blood placed on ear, thumb, toe)
  • "Left its category" to teach a new halakha
  • Leviticus 14:13 — slaughtered where sin/burnt offering is slaughtered
  • Returned to general category: "as the sin offering, so is the guilt offering"

All Other Ashamos

  • Standard guilt offering procedures
  • Learn from the asham metzora that slaughter must be in the north
  • Disqualified if slaughtered elsewhere
  • "To the priest, it is most holy" — applies to all ashamos
Key Move Once the verse returns the leper's guilt offering to the general category ("as the sin offering, so is the guilt offering"), it can teach the northern requirement to all guilt offerings.

Mar Zutra bar Rav Mari Challenges Ravina

Mar Zutra b"R Mari
Maybe the verse only returns the leper's asham for priestly rites — blood placement and burning fats — since the verse says "to the priest." Slaughter doesn't need a priest!
Ravina
If that were so, the verse should say "as the sin offering, so is it." Why say "so is the guilt offering"? To teach it returns fully to the category of all guilt offerings.
Mar Zutra b"R Mari
Why juxtapose the leper's asham to both a sin offering and a burnt offering? One should suffice!
Ravina
If only to the chatat — the chatat itself learned from the olah. We'd incorrectly think a hekkesh can teach via another hekkesh. Both are needed to prevent that error.
Principle Established A halakha derived via hekkesh (Scriptural juxtaposition) cannot then teach another halakha via hekkesh — ein hekkesh lameid mei-hekkesh (in consecrated matters).
