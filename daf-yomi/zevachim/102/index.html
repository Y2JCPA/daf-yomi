Was Moses a High Priest? & Who Receives Priestly Shares?

Rav
Moses served as a Kohen Gadol during the seven days of inauguration.
Objection
A baraita about Elisheva calls Moses a king — implying he was not a priest!
Gemara
He was a king as well — in addition to being a High Priest.
R' Shimon ben Yoḥai
God told Moses: 'You were to be the priest, he the Levite — now reversed.' Aaron is called 'Levite' as proof.
Rabbis
Moses was a priest only during the seven days of inauguration, then it expired.
Some Say
The priesthood expired only for Moses's descendants — Moses himself remained a priest.
KEY VERSE "Moses and Aaron among His priests" (Psalms 99:6) — cited as proof that Moses retained priestly status.

Why Couldn't Moses or Aaron Inspect Miriam's Leprosy?

Miriam is stricken with tzara'at (leprosy)
↓
Who can inspect her leprous marks?
Moses?
❌ A non-priest may not inspect leprous marks (or: excluded by Lev. 13:2)
Aaron?
❌ A relative may not inspect leprous marks
God Himself
✅ 'I am a Priest — I will quarantine her for seven days'
HONOR God bestowed great honor on Miriam by personally serving as the inspecting priest.

Elisheva's Five Joys — and Her Mourning

🎉 Five Reasons for Joy

  • Brother-in-law Moses — King of Israel
  • Husband Aaron — Kohen Gadol
  • Son Elazar — Deputy High Priest (Segan)
  • Grandson Pinehas — Priest Anointed for War
  • Brother Nahshon — Prince of Judah, first to bring offering

😢 Her Mourning

  • Sons Nadav and Avihu died on that very same day
  • The day of the Tabernacle's dedication became a day of both joy and grief
OBJECTION The baraita calls Moses a king — implying not a priest. The Gemara answers: he was a king as well, in addition to priesthood.

Tannaitic Dispute: Did Moses Retain the Priesthood?

R' Yehoshua ben Korḥa
God's burning anger at Moses at the bush had no stated consequence — unlike every other anger in Torah.
R' Shimon ben Yoḥai
The consequence is stated: Aaron is called 'the Levite' — meaning Moses lost the priesthood to Aaron.
Rabbis
Moses was priest for the seven days of inauguration only. Then it expired.
Some Say
It expired only for his descendants: 'His sons are named among the tribe of Levi' — but Moses himself stayed a priest.
DOUBLE PROOF If the verse in Chronicles only assigns descendants to Levi, a second verse confirms: "Moses and Aaron among His priests" (Psalms 99:6).

Moses and Pharaoh — Respect for Kingship

SageInterpretation of 'Stand by the River'
Reish LakishPharaoh is a king — be amiable toward him
R' YoḥananPharaoh is wicked — be insolent toward him
R' YANNAI Fear of kingship should always be upon you. Moses told Pharaoh 'your servants will bow to me' — but did not say this of Pharaoh himself, preserving royal dignity even for a wicked king.

Which Priests Receive a Share of Sacrificial Meat?

Mishna's principle: Any priest unfit for service that day does not receive a share
↓
Objection: What about exceptions?
Blemished Priest
Unfit for service but may partake of meat → receives share (derived from 'every male')
Impure Priest
Fit for communal service but may not partake → no share
Minor
Fit to partake but not to serve → no share
↓
Resolution: The mishna's principle means unfit to partake, not unfit for service
KEY RULE The mishna only teaches its explicit statement — we cannot derive the inverse that every fit priest necessarily receives a share.
