Zevachim 84 — The Altar Renders Acceptable

זֹאת תּוֹרַת הָעֹלָה הִוא הָעֹלָה עַל מוֹקְדָהּ עַל הַמִּזְבֵּחַ — Leviticus 6:2
Core Question When a disqualified offering ascends the altar, does it stay or come down? The Mishna lists specific disqualifications where it shall not descend, and the Gemara traces each to its scriptural or logical source. The pivotal debate: does the distinction depend on whether the disqualification occurred in sanctity (during Temple service) or not?

The Mishna's List: Ascended → Shall Not Descend

DisqualificationCategory
Blood, portions, or limbs left overnight (לינה)Time-based
Offering that emerged from the courtyard (יוצא)Location-based
Offering that became ritually impure (טמא)Purity-based
Slaughtered with intent beyond its time (פיגול)Intent-based
Slaughtered with intent outside its area (חוץ למקומו)Intent-based
Blood collected/sprinkled by unfit personsPersonnel-based
Key Rule All these are disqualified — yet once they ascend the altar, they shall not descend. The altar sanctifies what is placed upon it.

Three-Way Dispute: What Descends from the Altar?

R' Yehuda
Slaughtered at night, blood spilled on the floor, or blood that emerged outside the curtains — even if it ascended, it shall descend.
R' Shimon
In all these cases, if it ascended it shall not descend — because the disqualification occurred in sanctity (בקודש).
Tanna Kamma
The six listed disqualifications — those stay. But the three R' Yehuda mentions? Those shall descend.
R' Shimon
My principle: any disqualification in sanctity — the sacred area renders it acceptable. Only disqualifications not in sanctity — those descend.
Principle R' Shimon's rule: פסולו בקודש (disqualification occurred during Temple service) = stays on altar. Not in sanctity = descends.
5 / 9

Disqualification IN Sanctity vs. NOT in Sanctity

In Sanctity — Shall NOT Descend

  • Left overnight (לינה)
  • Emerged from courtyard (יוצא)
  • Became impure (טמא)
  • Piggul — beyond its time
  • Outside its designated area
  • Collected by unfit persons
  • Slaughtered at night (R' Shimon)
  • Blood spilled on floor (R' Shimon)

NOT in Sanctity — Shall Descend

  • Animal that copulated with a person (רובע)
  • Object of bestiality (נרבע)
  • Set aside for idol worship (מוקצה)
  • Worshipped as a deity (נעבד)
  • Payment to a prostitute (אתנן)
  • Price of a dog (מחיר כלב)
  • Mixed kinds (כלאים) / Tereifa / Caesarean
  • Blemished animals (dispute: R' Akiva vs. R' Ḥanina)
R' Akiva vs. R' Ḥanina R' Akiva: blemished animals that ascended shall not descend. R' Ḥanina the deputy HP: my father would reject blemished animals from the altar.

R' Yehuda's Scriptural Derivation (Leviticus 6:2)

"This is the law of the burnt offering:
It is the burnt offering on the pyre upon the altar"
↓
"This" (זאת)
Excludes one category from staying on altar
"It" (היא)
Excludes a second category
"The" (ה)
Excludes a third category
↓
R' Yehuda excludes: slaughtered at night, blood spilled, blood left courtyard
Method Three restrictive terms (מיעוטים) in one verse — each excludes one type of disqualified offering that must descend even if placed on the altar.

R' Yehuda's Chain of Derivations (Baraita)

🩸 Blood left overnight → derived from sacrificial portions left overnight being fit
↓
🥩 Portions left overnight → derived from meat of peace offering (eaten 2 days + 1 night)
↓
🚪 Emerged from courtyard → derived from private altars (במה), which have no boundary
↓
🫧 Became impure → derived from communal offerings, which may be offered when impure
↓
⏰ Piggul → blood sprinkling effects acceptance for piggul status, proving it's still an offering
↓
📍 Outside its area → juxtaposed to piggul (beyond its time)
↓
👤 Unfit persons → derived from impure priests who serve for communal offerings
Gemara's Challenge Can one derive the unfit from the fit? Answer: the tanna relied on the verse (Lev 6:2) which amplified the halakha broadly; these derivations are merely supports (אסמכתא).
