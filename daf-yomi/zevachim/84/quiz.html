QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Shimon, what is the defining criterion for whether a disqualified offering that ascended the altar shall descend?
A. Whether the disqualification occurred in sanctity (during Temple service)
B. Whether the offering was from a voluntary or obligatory sacrifice
C. Whether the blood was already sprinkled
D. Whether the offering was an animal or meal offering
Correct: A — R' Shimon's principle: פסולו בקודש — if the disqualification occurred in the course of Temple service (in sanctity), the altar renders it acceptable and it shall not descend. If the disqualification was inherent (not in sanctity), it shall descend.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Which THREE cases does R' Yehuda say shall descend from the altar even if they ascended, disagreeing with R' Shimon?
A. Slaughtered at night, blood spilled on floor, blood emerged outside the curtains
B. Left overnight, became impure, emerged from courtyard
C. Piggul, outside designated area, collected by unfit persons
D. Bestiality, idol worship, prostitute payment
Correct: A — R' Yehuda derives from three exclusionary terms in Leviticus 6:2 ('this,' 'it,' 'the') that these three cases — nighttime slaughter, blood spilled on the floor, and blood that left the courtyard — shall descend even if placed on the altar.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the baraita derive that sacrificial portions left overnight should not descend from the altar?
A. From the meat of the peace offering, which may be eaten for two days and one night
B. From the incense offering, which burns all night
C. From the Tamid offering, which is brought morning and evening
D. From the showbread, which sits on the table for a week
Correct: A — The baraita derives by comparison: if the meat of a peace offering remains fit despite being kept overnight (since it may be eaten for two days and one night), sacrificial portions left overnight similarly retain their status and need not descend.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the halakha if a burnt offering ascends the altar alive and is then slaughtered atop the altar?
A. It is flayed and cut into pieces in its place atop the altar
B. It must be removed and the altar requires re-sanctification
C. It must descend to be flayed below and then re-ascend
D. The offering is completely disqualified and must be burned outside
Correct: A — The Mishna teaches that while a live animal that ascends should descend (since fitness requires slaughter), if one did slaughter it atop the altar, one flays and cuts it in its place — it is not removed from the altar.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Yoḥanan, what happens if one slaughters an offering at night inside the Temple and then offers it outside the Temple courtyard?
A. He is liable for karet for offering outside the Temple
B. He is exempt because the nighttime slaughter disqualified it entirely
C. He receives lashes but not karet
D. The offering must be returned to the altar inside the Temple
Correct: A — R' Yoḥanan rules that even though R' Yehuda holds an animal slaughtered at night shall descend from the altar, it retains enough status as an offering that one who offers it outside the Temple courtyard is liable for karet.
