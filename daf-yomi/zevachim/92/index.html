The Mishna's Core Rule

תורת החטאת — זו תורה אחת לכל החטאות
MISHNA Blood of a sin offering (חטאת) designated for presentation that was sprayed on a garment — that garment requires laundering in a sacred place.

"This is the law of the sin offering" (Lev. 6:18) — there is one law for all sin offerings: both those that are eaten and those that are wholly burned.

Why Is Bird Sin Offering Blood Excluded?

Gemara
If "one law for all sin offerings" — bird blood should also require laundering!
Reish Lakish / Bar Kappara
The verse says the sin offering "shall be slaughtered" (שחיטה) — birds are killed by melikah (pinching), not slaughter
Rav Yosef
"The priest that offers it for sin shall eat it" — 'it' excludes another similar offering, i.e., the bird sin offering
Gemara
If Rav Yosef's verse suffices, why do we also need "This is the law"?
Gemara (answers)
Without "this," we'd say "shall eat it" is just the manner of speech of the verse — so both verses are needed
RESULT Bird sin offerings are excluded from laundering by multiple converging derivations

Rabba's Approach: Sprinkling Language Points to Internal Offerings

"That on which it shall be sprinkled" (Lev. 6:20)
הזאה = sprinkling
↓
Which offerings use the term "sprinkling"?
Internal Sin Offerings
Lev. 4:6 uses הזאה (sprinkling)
→ Primary subject of the verse
External Sin Offerings
Lev. 4:25 uses נתינה (placing)
→ Included via "the law of"
Bird Sin Offerings
Also uses sprinkling (Lev. 5:9)
→ Excluded by "this is"
RABBA'S KEY INSIGHT The laundering passage primarily addresses internal sin offerings; eaten offerings are included only through amplification of "the law of the sin offering"
