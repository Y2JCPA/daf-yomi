Simultaneous Intentions: One Statement or Two?

Gemara
The mishna's phrase "zeh ha-klal" (this is the principle) supports R' Yochanan — the dispute covers even one rite
Gemara
This poses a difficulty for Ilfa, who limits the dispute to two rites
Abaye
When one states both intentions simultaneously, R' Meir concedes both sanctities apply
Rava
Even when stated together, R' Meir holds only the first-mentioned sanctity takes effect
Core Issue When two competing intentions or designations are expressed in a single act, does the first one mentioned prevail, or do both take effect?

Temura: Substituting for Two Offerings

A person says: "This animal is the substitute of a burnt offering, the substitute of a peace offering"
↓
R' Meir
It is only the substitute of a burnt offering — first designation alone takes effect
R' Yosei
If intended from the outset, both sanctities take effect on the animal
Temura 25b R' Yosei distinguishes: if the original intent was for both — both apply. If he changed his mind mid-statement — only the first applies.

Abaye vs. Rava: "To Split" — Both at Once

Abaye

  • When one explicitly says BOTH intentions simultaneously, R' Meir concedes
  • Both sanctities take effect on the animal
  • Shechita is progressive — each siman can carry a different intent
  • The mishna's case involves sequential intentions during two simanim

Rava

  • Even when stated together, R' Meir maintains only the first sanctity applies
  • Proof: the mishna's case of two intentions during one shechita — and still R' Yehuda disagrees
  • Shechita during a single act is comparable to saying 'to split'
  • R' Yehuda still attends only to the first statement
Abaye's Defense Shechita is not instantaneous — it is progressive (from beginning to end). Each siman (gullet/windpipe) can carry a separate intention, so it's not truly simultaneous.

Challenges to Abaye's Position

ChallengeAbaye's Resolution
Shechita with two intentions in one act — R' Yehuda still disagrees!Shechita is progressive: first siman = beyond time; second siman = outside area
Kemitza (handful removal) is a single act — R' Yehuda still disagrees!The dispute is about burning two permitting factors (handful + frankincense), not the kemitza itself
A sinner's meal offering has no frankincense — how to divide?In that case, they do not disagree (or: per Rav Ashi, the priest had different intents during his steps toward the altar)
Pattern Abaye consistently argues that every seemingly simultaneous act can be divided into sequential parts, preserving his position that R' Meir concedes in truly simultaneous cases.

Rav Dimi vs. R' Yochanan: Does R' Meir Follow R' Yehuda?

Rav Dimi
R' Meir follows R' Yehuda's principle: attend only to the first statement
Abaye
But R' Yochanan says R' Meir and R' Yosei don't actually disagree on that principle!
R' Yochanan
All agree: if one says 'let this take effect and then that' — only the first applies. If 'this shall not take effect unless that does' — both apply
R' Yochanan
They disagree about ambiguous phrasing: R' Meir reads it as retraction; R' Yosei reads it as intent for both
Rav Dimi
He (R' Yochanan) says they don't disagree — but I say they do
R' Yosei's Logic He said 'substitute for a burnt offering, substitute for a peace offering' — not 'and a peace offering' — to convey that all of it is a burnt offering AND all of it is a peace offering.
