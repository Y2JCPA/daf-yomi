May One Reduce the Status of Sacred Foods?

R' Yishmael
Priests may eat offerings with teruma spices or non-sacred spices as they choose.
Rabba
Teruma of spices is only rabbinic, not Torah-level — so this case proves nothing about reducing sanctity.
Abaye
But R' Shimon permits buying teruma with ma'aser sheni money — that limits teruma's eating conditions!
Rav Yosef
Better proof: R' Shimon permits cooking shevi'it vegetables in teruma oil, clearly bringing teruma to unfitness.
Core Issue Does R' Shimon permit actively bringing sacred food to a diminished status (ab initio), or only after the fact (b'dieved)?

How May Priests Eat Offerings?

R' Yishmael's Rule

  • Roasted, boiled, or cooked
  • Non-sacred spices permitted
  • Teruma spices also permitted
  • Priests choose how to eat

The Problem

  • Teruma spices have eating restrictions
  • Adding them to offerings limits consumption time
  • This 'brings teruma to unfitness'
  • Is this permitted ab initio?
Key Term Mevi'a lidei pesul (מביא לידי פסול) — bringing consecrated food to a state where it may become unfit by imposing additional restrictions on its consumption.

Abaye Challenges Rabba

Abaye
R' Shimon permits buying teruma with ma'aser sheni money — this limits teruma to Jerusalem and bans it for an onen (acute mourner)!
Rabba
(Silent — no response)
Rav Yosef
Why didn't you cite Shevi'it 8:7? R' Shimon permits cooking shevi'it vegetables in teruma oil — forcing teruma to be eaten before the removal deadline!
Abaye
But Rabba would say: that teruma oil is vegetable oil — teruma only by rabbinic law, same as spices!
Rav Yosef
If it were vegetable oil, the mishna should teach the opposite case — teruma vegetables in shevi'it oil. It must mean olive oil, Torah-level teruma!
Turning Point Rav Yosef proves from the mishna's language that Torah-level teruma is at stake, undermining Rabba's dismissal.

Ab Initio or After the Fact?

Does R' Shimon permit bringing sacred food to unfitness?
↓
Rabba's View
Only after the fact (b'dieved) — when items are already intermingled
Rav Yosef's View
Even ab initio (l'chatchila) — R' Shimon allows it from the start
↓
If only b'dieved — what do the Rabbis say?
↓
Has a Remedy
Like guilt + peace offerings: let animals graze until blemished → prohibited to cook together
No Remedy
Like mixed flesh of different sanctity levels: eat both according to the stricter rule
Ravina's Objection Teruma oil does have a remedy — squeezing the oil out of the vegetables! So the Rabbis would still prohibit it.

Squeezing as a Remedy? Rav Yosef Responds

Amount of SqueezingProblem
Squeeze a lotCauses loss of Sabbatical Year produce (forbidden)
Squeeze a littleOil and vegetables remain intermingled — ineffective
ConclusionSqueezing is NOT a viable remedy — no remedy exists
Result Since there is no remedy, even the Rabbis would not prohibit the mixture after the fact — supporting Rav Yosef's reading of the mishna as referring to an ab initio dispute.

The Uncertain Leper's Offering (Amud B)

🤔 Man is uncertain whether he is a leper — must bring a guilt offering with a stipulation
↓
🐑 If he IS a leper → animal serves as an asham (guilt offering)
↓
🕊️ If he is NOT a leper → animal becomes a shelamim (peace offering)
↓
📍 Slaughtered in the north (like guilt offering), blood placed on thumb/toe/ear
↓
🤲 Hands placed on animal's head + wine libations + breast/thigh waved (like peace offering)
↓
🕐 Eaten by male priests, day of sacrifice + that night only (like guilt offering — stricter rule)
Implication R' Shimon allows the offering to be brought with reduced eating time even ab initio — since the man has no other way to purify himself from possible leprosy.
