QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Rabba dismiss Abaye's proof from teruma spices on offerings?
A. Teruma of spices is only rabbinic, not Torah law
B. Spices are nullified in the mixture
C. The spices enhance the offering and are therefore permitted
D. R' Yishmael's view is rejected by the majority
Correct: A — Rabba argues that teruma of spices applies only by rabbinic law, so it cannot prove that R' Shimon permits reducing Torah-level sanctity.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rav Yosef prove the mishna about shevi'it vegetables refers to Torah-level teruma oil?
A. He cites a baraita that explicitly states olive oil
B. If it were vegetable oil (rabbinic teruma), the mishna would teach the reverse case
C. He argues from the location of the mishna in Masechet Shevi'it
D. He shows that vegetables cannot produce oil
Correct: B — Rav Yosef reasons that if the teruma oil were only rabbinic (vegetable oil), the mishna should have taught the more instructive reverse case — teruma vegetables in shevi'it oil. The fact that it specifies teruma oil indicates Torah-level olive oil.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rav Yosef reject squeezing as a viable remedy for separating teruma oil from shevi'it vegetables?
A. Squeezing is forbidden on Shabbat
B. Squeezing a lot wastes shevi'it produce; squeezing a little is ineffective
C. Squeezing would render the teruma impure
D. The oil has already been absorbed and cannot be extracted
Correct: B — Excessive squeezing causes forbidden loss of Sabbatical Year produce, while minimal squeezing fails to separate them — so squeezing is not a real remedy.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the case of an uncertain leper, why is the concern of reducing eating time overridden?
A. The offering is only rabbinic in nature
B. The person has no other way to purify himself from possible leprosy
C. R' Shimon always permits reducing sanctity ab initio
D. The guilt offering and peace offering have identical eating times
Correct: B — Since the uncertain leper has no alternative means of purification, the necessity of bringing the offering overrides the concern of reducing the available eating time.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does R' Yehuda son of R' Shimon ben Pazi resolve the timing dilemma of burning the handful of the uncertain leper's oil?
A. The handful is never burned at all
B. The priest burns it simultaneously with the sprinklings
C. The sprinklings are done with a stipulation — if not a leper, they count as fuel for the altar, not as a sacrificial rite
D. A second log of oil is brought for the sprinklings
Correct: C — The priest sprinkles with a stipulation: if the man is not a leper, the sprinklings are merely like adding fuel to the altar fire, not a sacrificial act — thus avoiding the prohibition against offering what is partly consumed.
