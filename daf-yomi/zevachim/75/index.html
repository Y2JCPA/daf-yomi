Abaye's Challenge to Rav Yosef

Abaye
The baraita (Tosefta 8:22) says intermingled offerings of individuals each get 4 blood placements — implying they belong to men who require semicha (laying of hands)!
Abaye
The baraita pairs 'offering of an individual' with 'communal offering' — just as communal offerings are from men, so too these individual offerings belong to men.
Rava
Can you even understand this baraita as written? It says 'alive vs. slaughtered' — but what difference does alive vs. slaughtered make? The real question is whether the blood is in separate cups!
Rava
Rather, 'alive' means separate cups (like living animals, each distinct). 'Slaughtered mixed' means blood combined in one cup. The baraita only discusses post-slaughter blood — no bearing on Rav Yosef's case.
Resolution Rava deflects Abaye's challenge by reinterpreting the baraita entirely — it never discusses live intermingled animals at all

R' Yehuda HaNasi: Blood Measure vs. Water of Purification

Blood on the Altar

  • Must assess (אומדין) if enough blood for each offering's placement
  • If not enough blood from each — disqualified (פסול)
  • Requires a minimum measure

Water of Purification (Mei Chatat)

  • R' Yehuda HaNasi citing R' Eliezer: sprinkling of any amount purifies
  • Even half-fit, half-unfit water works
  • No minimum measure required
Resolution Either R' Yehuda HaNasi stated R' Eliezer's view without agreeing, or: sprinkling of purification water and blood placement are two separate halakhot (discrete categories)

Temple Profit vs. Dignity of the Bechor

Rami bar Ḥama
If a kohen dedicates his bechor to bedek habayis (Temple maintenance), may it be weighed by the litra? Is Temple profit or bechor dignity paramount?
R' Yosei bar Zevida
The mishna says intermingled offerings are eaten as bechor — meaning they are not weighed by litra, even at a loss to hekdesh. Dignity wins!
Rav Huna & R' Chizkiyya
Not comparable! The mishna has two bodies with two sanctities. Here, bedek habayis and bechor are two sanctities in one body — Temple profit may override.
Rav Yosei bar Avin
If one says 'redeem my bechor' that I dedicated — we don't listen! The Torah prohibits redeeming bechor. Similarly, dedication shouldn't remove the litra prohibition.
Gemara
You can't compare — redemption is d'Oraisa (Torah prohibition). Weighing by litra is only d'Rabbanan (rabbinic dignity concern).
R' Ami's Resolution Can the kohen transfer to the Temple anything beyond what he himself received? Just as the kohen may not weigh it by litra, neither can the Temple — the restriction transfers with the animal
