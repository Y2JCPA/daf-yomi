Where Are the Bulls & Goats Burned?

Baraita (Rabbis)
The bulls and goats are burned north of Jerusalem, outside the three camps.
R' Yosei HaGelili
They are burned at the place of the ashes (shefekh hadeshen), where altar ashes were poured.
Rava
Who disagrees with R' Yosei HaGelili? It is R' Eliezer ben Yaakov, who reads shefekh as meshupakh — the place must slope downward.
Abaye
Perhaps R' Eliezer ben Yaakov also agrees it must be the ash-deposit site — they only disagree about whether the ground must slope!
Key Verse "Where the ashes are poured out shall it be burned" (Leviticus 4:12) — read as describing the location or the terrain.

Garment Impurity from Burning Yom Kippur Offerings

RoleRenders Garments Impure?
One who burns the offerings✅ Yes — explicit in verse
One who kindles the fire❌ No
One who arranges the wood❌ No
One who assists at the time of burning✅ Yes — considered 'the one who burns'
After offerings become ash (Tanna Kamma)❌ No longer impure
After flesh is incinerated (R' Shimon)❌ No longer impure
Rava's Distinction The practical difference: a charred mass whose form is distorted but not yet ash. Tanna Kamma says it still transmits impurity; R' Shimon says it does not.

Slaughtering & Offering Outside the Courtyard

One slaughters an offering
outside the Temple courtyard
↓
Then offers it up
outside the courtyard
↓
Is he liable for both acts?
↓
Rabbis
Liable for both — each is an independent prohibition with its own karet / sin offering
R' Yosei HaGelili
Exempt for offering up — slaughtering outside already rendered it unfit, and one is only liable for offering up a fit item
Rabbis' Rebuttal Even slaughtering inside and offering up outside — the moment he took it out he rendered it unfit, yet he is still liable! So too here.

Impure Person Eating Sacrificial Food

Rabbis

  • Impure person who eats pure kodshim → liable
  • Impure person who eats impure kodshim → also liable
  • Liability stems from body's impurity, regardless of food's status
  • Pure person who eats impure kodshim → exempt

R' Yosei HaGelili

  • Impure person who eats pure kodshim → liable
  • Impure person who eats impure kodshim → exempt
  • He merely ate something already impure
  • Prohibition applies only to eating pure sacrificial food while impure
Rabbis' Rebuttal Even eating originally pure food — once the impure person touches it, it becomes impure. Yet he is still liable! So impurity of the food is irrelevant.

Source for Prohibition Against Offering Outside

הִשָּׁמֶר לְךָ פֶּן תַּעֲלֶה עֹלֹתֶיךָ בְּכָל מָקוֹם אֲשֶׁר תִּרְאֶה
Key Derivation Punishment (karet): "Any man that offers up a burnt offering… and will not bring it to the Tent of Meeting… shall be cut off" (Lev. 17:8–9)

Prohibition: "Take heed to yourself lest you offer up your burnt offerings in every place that you see" (Deut. 12:13)

R' Avin says R' Elazar says: Wherever the Torah says hishamer (observe), pen (lest), or al (do not) — it is a negative commandment (lav).
