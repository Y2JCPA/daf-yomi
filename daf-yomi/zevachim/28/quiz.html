Zevachim 28 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the problem the Gemara raises about intent to eat the skin of the tail beyond its designated time?
A. The skin of the tail is too small to constitute an olive-bulk
B. Intent shifts consumption from altar (burning) to person (eating), which shouldn't create piggul
C. The skin of the tail is never part of the offering
D. One cannot have piggul intent about a peace offering
Correct: B — The tail is burned on the altar, so intent to eat its skin shifts consumption from altar to person. According to the Rabbis, such shifted intent should not create piggul.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rav Huna resolve the contradiction between the two mishnayot?
A. The first mishna follows Rabbi Eliezer and the second follows the Rabbis
B. The skin of the tail is NOT considered like the tail — it is eaten by people, not burned
C. The mishna refers to a kid (goat), whose tail is not burned on the altar
D. The skin of the tail has a special exemption from piggul laws
Correct: B — Rav Huna holds that the skin of the tail is not considered part of the tail for altar purposes. Since it is eaten (not burned), intent to eat it beyond its time is normal piggul — no shifting of consumption types.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why do Shmuel and Rav Huna reject Rav Ḥisda's approach (that the mishna discusses a kid)?
A. A kid's tail cannot create piggul
B. If the mishna discusses a kid, it teaches nothing new — we already know from Ḥullin 122a that skin of the tail has the status of meat
C. Rav Ḥisda's reading contradicts the Rabbis' view
D. A kid does not have a significant tail
Correct: B — If referring to a kid, the mishna's mention of 'skin of the tail' adds nothing new, since Ḥullin 122a already teaches that skin beneath the tail has the halakhic status of meat.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does R' Yoḥanan (citing Zavdi bar Levi) establish that karet applies to notar rather than to intent regarding outside the designated area?
A. Through a kal va-ḥomer from the sin offering
B. Through the gezera shava of 'sacred'–'sacred' (Lev. 19:8 and Exodus 29:34)
C. Through the acronym zayin-beit comparing notar and piggul
D. Through the doubled verb he'akhol ye'akhel
Correct: B — R' Yoḥanan uses a gezera shava linking 'sacred' in Leviticus 19:8 ('he has profaned the sacred item') to 'sacred' in Exodus 29:34 (regarding notar of the consecration offerings), definitively connecting karet to notar.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rava derive from the doubled verb 'he'akhol ye'akhel' (הֵאָכֹל יֵאָכֵל) in Leviticus 7:18?
A. That piggul applies only to peace offerings
B. That two separate acts of eating are required for liability
C. That piggul applies to two types of consumption: eating by a person and burning on the altar
D. That the offering must be eaten on two separate days
Correct: C — Rava reads the doubled verb as teaching that piggul intent applies to two forms of 'consumption' — human eating and altar burning. Improper intent regarding either renders the offering piggul.
