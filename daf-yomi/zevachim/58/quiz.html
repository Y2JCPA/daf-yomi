Zevachim 58 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yosei, why are kodshei kodashim slaughtered atop the altar valid?
A. The entire altar stands in the north of the courtyard
B. The altar has special sanctity that overrides location requirements
C. Slaughtering atop the altar is equivalent to slaughtering on the ground
D. The Torah explicitly permits slaughter on the altar
Correct: A — R' Yochanan explains that R' Yosei holds the entire altar stands in the north. The phrase 'as if in the north' addresses only the concern that 'beside the altar' requires ground-level slaughter.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What verse do both R' Yosei and R' Yosei b"R Yehuda use to derive their respective positions?
A. Leviticus 1:11 — 'on the side of the altar northward'
B. Exodus 20:21 — 'An altar of earth… your burnt offerings and your peace offerings'
C. Leviticus 4:7 — 'at the base of the altar of the burnt offering'
D. Leviticus 16:12 — 'coals of fire from off the altar'
Correct: B — R' Yochanan states both Tannaim derive their opinions from Exodus 20:21. R' Yosei reads 'all of it' for both offerings; R' Yosei b"R Yehuda reads 'half and half.'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Yosei b"R Yehuda, if one slaughtered a kodshei kodashim offering on the ground where the northern half of the altar once stood (after the altar was minimized), what is the status?
A. Valid, since it was originally altar space
B. Valid, since it is in the northern courtyard
C. Disqualified, because it is no longer on or beside the altar
D. Disqualified, because the ground was never sanctified
Correct: C — Rav Asi reports that R' Yosei b"R Yehuda concedes such slaughter is disqualified. The Gemara clarifies this refers to a case where the altar's dimensions were reduced.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In R' Yosei's principle about inner/outer altar transfers, what is the example of an item taken from the outer altar to be placed on the inner altar?
A. Remaining blood of the inner sin offerings
B. The two bowls of frankincense of the shewbread
C. Coals taken daily from the outer altar for burning incense inside
D. The ashes of the red heifer
Correct: C — The Gemara identifies the daily incense coals (taken from the outer altar for the inner altar) as the case R' Yosei's principle addresses, derived from the Yom Kippur coals rule.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara prove that R' Yosei holds the entire altar was in the north of the courtyard?
A. From R' Yosei's reading of Exodus 20:21 about burnt offerings
B. From the placement of the second wood arrangement 4 cubits from the SW corner
C. From the location where the remaining blood of sin offerings was poured
D. From the dimensions of the Sanctuary entrance
Correct: B — R' Yosei places the second wood arrangement 4 cubits from the SW corner, which must be opposite the Sanctuary entrance. The Gemara shows this only works if the entire altar is in the north, where the 5 southernmost cubits face the entrance.
