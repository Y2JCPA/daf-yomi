Where Is the Altar? Slaughter Location & Sacred Geography

R' Yosei
Offerings of most sacred order slaughtered atop the altar are valid — it is as if slaughtered in the north.
R' Yosei b"R Yehuda
The northern half of the altar counts as north; the southern half counts as south — and offerings slaughtered on the southern half are disqualified.
R' Yochanan
R' Yosei holds the entire altar stands in the north of the courtyard. Both Tannaim derive their views from one verse: Exodus 20:21.
Core Issue The altar's geographic position in the Temple courtyard determines whether kodshei kodashim slaughtered upon it are valid.

Mishna: Slaughtering Atop the Altar

R' Yosei
Kodshei kodashim slaughtered atop the altar — their status is as if slaughtered in the north. They are valid.
R' Yosei b"R Yehuda
From the midpoint southward = status of south → disqualified.
From the midpoint northward = status of north → valid.
Background Kodshei kodashim (most sacred offerings) must be slaughtered in the northern section of the Temple courtyard (Leviticus 1:11).

R' Yochanan's Explanation of R' Yosei

R' Yochanan says: R' Yosei holds the entire altar stands in the north of the courtyard
↓
Then why say 'as if' in the north?
↓
Lest you say the verse requires slaughter beside the altar on the ground — 'al yerech' (Lev. 1:11)
↓
R' Yosei teaches: atop the altar is still valid, even though it's not on the ground beside it
Key Point R' Yochanan's statement about R' Yosei is independent — not derived from the Mishna, but a separate tradition.

One Verse, Two Readings — Exodus 20:21

R' Yosei

  • All of the altar is fit for slaughtering an olah (burnt offering)
  • All of it is also fit for shelamim (peace offering)
  • The verse mentions shelamim to teach that even peace offerings may be slaughtered atop the altar
  • This is needed because one might think only olah (with narrow slaughter area) qualifies

R' Yosei b"R Yehuda

  • Half the altar is fit for olah (the northern half)
  • Half is fit for shelamim (the southern half)
  • If ALL were fit for olah, why mention shelamim?
  • Shelamim can be slaughtered anywhere — it would be obvious!
Verse "An altar of earth you shall make for Me, and you shall slaughter upon it your burnt offerings and your peace offerings" — Exodus 20:21

R' Yosei b"R Yehuda's Concession

Location of SlaughterStatus
Northern half of the altar surface✅ Valid (counts as north)
Southern half of the altar surface❌ Disqualified (counts as south)
Ground opposite northern half of altar❌ Disqualified — not 'beside the altar'
Ground in the actual northern courtyard✅ Valid
Rav Aḥa of Difti Asks What does 'on the ground opposite the altar' mean? The case is where one minimized the altar's dimensions and slaughtered on the ground where the northern half previously stood.

The Altar Must Be on Solid Ground

מזבח אדמה תעשה לי
Halakhic Rule "An altar of earth you shall make for Me" (Exodus 20:21) — the altar must be attached to the earth. One may not build it atop tunnels or arches. If one dug tunnels beneath it, the altar itself would be unfit for use.
