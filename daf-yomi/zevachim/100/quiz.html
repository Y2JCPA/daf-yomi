QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Mari, why does the Paschal offering override acute mourning in the mishna in Pesaḥim?
A. Because the mishna discusses the day of burial, where aninut is only rabbinic
B. Because eating the Pesach is indispensable
C. Because the death occurred after midday
D. Because the Pesach was already slaughtered
Correct: A — Rav Mari distinguishes between the day of death (Torah-level aninut) and the day of burial (rabbinic-level aninut). The mishna in Pesaḥim discusses burial on the 14th when death was on the 13th, making the aninut rabbinic and overridable.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rav Ashi consider Rav Mari's resolution problematic?
A. Because there is no difference between Torah and rabbinic aninut
B. Because Rabbi Shimon uses the mishna as proof against Rabbi Yehuda — but if they discuss different cases, it's no proof
C. Because the mishna never mentions acute mourning
D. Because Rav Mari contradicts a baraita about nazirites
Correct: B — If the baraita discusses the day of death (Torah) and the mishna discusses the day of burial (rabbinic), Rabbi Yehuda could easily dismiss Rabbi Shimon's proof by pointing out they address different situations. The fact that Rabbi Shimon treats them as comparable undermines Rav Mari.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the practical significance of eating the Paschal offering being 'me'akev' (indispensable)?
A. It means the offering must be eaten raw
B. It means women are exempt from the offering
C. It means the Sages suspended their decree of aninut to allow the mourner to eat it
D. It means the offering can be brought even in impurity
Correct: C — Because eating the Paschal offering is essential to fulfilling the mitzva (unlike other sacrificial meat where consumption is not indispensable), the Sages made an exception and did not apply their rabbinic decree of aninut to prevent the mourner from eating it.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In Abaye's resolution, what happens if a relative dies AFTER midday on the 14th of Nisan?
A. The mourner is completely exempt from the Paschal offering
B. The mourner's aninut does not apply regarding the Pesach since he was already obligated
C. The mourner must bring a replacement offering the next day
D. The mourner must wait until the second Pesach (Pesach Sheni)
Correct: B — Abaye holds that once the obligation for the Paschal offering takes effect at midday, a subsequent death does not impose aninut with regard to this mitzva. The mourner immerses and eats the Pesach in the evening.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabba bar Rav Huna, what is the difference between a burial that occurs before sunset and one that occurs after sunset on the 14th of Nisan?
A. Before sunset: may eat all sacrificial meat in the evening. After sunset: may eat only the Pesach, not other sacrificial meat
B. Before sunset: full mourning. After sunset: no mourning at all
C. Before sunset: Torah-level aninut. After sunset: rabbinic aninut
D. There is no difference — both permit eating the Pesach
Correct: A — If burial occurs before sunset, the mourner immerses and eats all sacrificial meat in the evening. If burial occurs after sunset (the night of the 15th itself), the Sages only suspend aninut for the Paschal offering (because eating it is me'akev) but not for other sacrificial meat.
