Zevachim 107 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava citing R' Yona, how is the prohibition (not just the punishment) for slaughtering outside the Temple derived?
A. From the juxtaposition of 'there…there' in Deuteronomy 12:14
B. From the word 'or' in Leviticus 17:8
C. From a verbal analogy of 'bringing'
D. From the vav connecting two passages
Correct: A — Rava explains that R' Yona derives it from the double use of 'there' in Deut. 12:14, juxtaposing offering up (which has an explicit prohibition) with slaughtering, teaching that punishment implies prohibition.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject deriving liability for sprinkling outside from the 'common element' of slaughtering and offering up?
A. Because sprinkling is not a sacrificial rite
B. Because the Torah explicitly wrote a verse for sprinkling — teaching that each rite needs its own source
C. Because sprinkling can only be done by a Kohen
D. Because sprinkling is only relevant to bird offerings
Correct: B — The Gemara reasons that the fact that the Torah bothered to write a specific verse for sprinkling liability proves that one cannot derive liability from the common element of two other rites.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Abbahu, if one slaughtered AND sprinkled AND offered up outside in a single lapse of awareness, how many sin offerings must be brought?
A. One
B. Two
C. Three
D. It depends on the dispute between R' Yishmael and R' Akiva
Correct: B — All agree that the total is two sin offerings: one for offering up (ha'ala'ah) and one for all other rites combined, based on Deut. 12:14 which separates 'offer up' from 'do.'
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What was Rav Beivai's objection to the derivations connecting offering up outside to offerings slaughtered outside?
A. The derivation contradicts a verse in Deuteronomy
B. It would require the mishna in Karetot to list 37 cases of karet instead of 36
C. It conflicts with R' Akiva's position
D. It would make all offerings outside the Temple equally liable
Correct: B — If offering up an offering slaughtered inside vs. outside are two independent prohibitions, the count of karet cases should be 37, not the 36 listed in the mishna in Karetot 2a. The Gemara concedes this is difficult (kashya).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the purpose of the baraita's distinction between 'in the camp' and 'outside the camp' in Leviticus 17:3?
A. To teach that liability applies in the Levite camp but not the Israelite camp
B. To include liability for slaughtering non-sacred animals
C. To clarify that slaughtering in the wrong area of the courtyard (e.g., south instead of north) does NOT incur karet — only slaughtering entirely outside
D. To distinguish between the three camps for purposes of purity
Correct: C — Without 'outside the camp,' one might think slaughtering a burnt offering in the south of the courtyard (instead of the required north) incurs liability. The verse clarifies that karet applies only for slaughtering entirely outside the Temple courtyard.
