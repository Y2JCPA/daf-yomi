The Altar's Southeast Corner & the Temple's Location

Rav
The southeast corner had no physical base — the base only extended along the north and west sides
Levi
The base existed on all four sides physically, but no blood was sprinkled on the southeast corner
Rav
"In his portion the altar (מזבח) will be built" — Benjamin's blessing refers to the altar itself
Levi
"In his portion the Sanctuary (מקדש) will be built" — a place sanctified for blood
Key Issue The altar straddled the border of Benjamin and Judah. The southeast corner's status — whether it lacked a physical base or merely lacked blood service — reflects a deeper dispute about the meaning of Benjamin's blessing.

Benjamin: Host of the Divine Presence

חֹפֵף עָלָיו כָּל הַיּוֹם וּבֵין כְּתֵפָיו שָׁכֵן
Deuteronomy 33:12 "He covers it throughout the day, and He dwells between his shoulders" — Benjamin was perpetually focused on the site of the Temple. He merited becoming the host of the Holy One, as the Holy of Holies was built in his portion.

Rav vs. Levi: What Did the Southeast Corner Lack?

Rav
The SE corner had no physical base built on those sides
Levi
There was a physical base, but no blood was sprinkled there
Rav
Genesis 49:27 — "In his portion the altar will be built" (the altar is the focus)
Levi
Genesis 49:27 — "In his portion the Sanctuary will be built" — a place sanctified for blood
Gemara
Mishna Middot: Base extends along all of north, all of west, one cubit south, one cubit east — supports Rav
Levi
"Consume" in the Mishna refers to consuming blood, not physical extent
Underlying Question Does Benjamin's blessing (Genesis 49:27) refer to hosting the physical altar or to the sanctified blood service of the Temple?

Levi's Baraita: How to Build the Altar

🪵 Place a wooden frame 32 × 32 cubits, height 1 cubit — fill with damp stones, plaster, lead & tar → this is the base (יסוד)
↓
🪵 Place a frame 30 × 30 cubits, height 5 cubits — fill with stones and mixture → this is the main body
↓
🪵 Place a frame 28 × 28 cubits, height 3 cubits — fill with stones and mixture → this is the place for the wood arrangement (מערכה)
↓
🪵 Place a frame 1 × 1 cubit at each corner — fill with stones and mixture → these are the horns (קרנות)
↓
⚠️ Cannot cut the base after construction — "unhewn stones" (אבנים שלמות) — must use removable inserts to create gaps
Proof for Levi This baraita describes a base of 32 × 32 cubits on ALL four sides — supporting Levi's view that the base was physically present even at the southeast corner.

David & Samuel Locate the Temple Site

The Search Method

  • David and Samuel sat in Ramah discussing the "beauty of the world" (נויו של עולם)
  • The Temple must be "higher than all places in Eretz Yisrael"
  • They examined the Book of Joshua for border descriptions
  • For most tribes: borders "went down" (וירד)
  • For Benjamin: borders only "went up" (ועלה) — never down
  • Conclusion: Benjamin's portion is the highest

The Final Decision

  • Initially chose Ein Eitam — the highest point in Benjamin
  • Lowered it: "He dwells between his shoulders" — not atop the head
  • Sanhedrin must be in Judah's portion; Divine Presence in Benjamin's
  • Ein Eitam was too far from Judah's border
  • "We found it in the field of the wood" — Benjamin the wolf
  • Doeg the Edomite was jealous of David's achievement
Psalms 132:6 "We heard of it as being in Ephrath" — refers to Joshua (from Ephraim), whose book revealed the location. "We found it in the field of the wood" — refers to Benjamin the wolf, in whose portion the Temple was built.
