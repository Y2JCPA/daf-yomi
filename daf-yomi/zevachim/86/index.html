Bones, Tendons & the Altar: The Core Framework

Bones, tendons, horns, hooves of a korban olah
↓
Attached to Flesh
They ascend the altar with the offering
Separated from Flesh
They descend — even if already atop the altar (R' Yehuda HaNasi)
KEY VERSE "The priest shall make the whole smoke on the altar" (Lev. 1:9) — 'the whole' includes bones and tendons, but only when attached.
3 / 9

Reconciling the Two Verses

Verse 1
"The priest shall make the whole smoke on the altar" (Lev. 1:9) — includes bones, tendons, horns, hooves
Verse 2
"You shall offer your burnt offerings, the flesh and the blood" (Deut. 12:27) — excludes non-flesh parts
R' Yehuda HaNasi
If attached to the flesh → they ascend.
If separated → even if on top of the altar, they descend.
First Tanna
Both verses teach about consumed flesh: return consumed flesh to the fire, but do not return consumed tendons and bones.
MISHNA RULE Items not meant for altar consumption ascend only if attached to the flesh. If separated — they shall not ascend.

R' Zeira & Rabba: When Did Separation Occur?

Bones/tendons separated from the flesh of the olah
↓
Before Zerikah (Blood Sprinkling)
They were already separated when the flesh became permitted for the altar → shall not ascend.
They are permitted for any use — even to fashion knife handles!
After Zerikah
At the time of zerikah they were still attached → the Mishnah's rule applies: shall not ascend only because they later separated
RABBA'S PRINCIPLE Zerikah (blood sprinkling) is the moment that determines the status of bones and tendons — were they attached or separated at that critical instant?

Gezerah Shavah: Olah vs. Asham

Burnt Offering (Olah)

  • "He shall have the hide of the burnt offering" (Lev. 7:8)
  • Entire animal ascends on the altar
  • Separated bones — permitted to the priest
  • Phrase "he shall have" is superfluous → available for gezerah shavah

Guilt Offering (Asham)

  • "The priest that makes atonement, he shall have it" (Lev. 7:7)
  • Only designated portions go on the altar
  • Bones clearly permitted to the priest
  • Just as asham bones are permitted → so too olah bones (when separated)
PRINCIPLE A gezerah shavah derived from superfluous words (mufneh) cannot be refuted — making this analogy between olah and asham irrefutable.

Misuse of Consecrated Property (Me'ilah) for Bones

Offering TypeBefore ZerikahAfter Zerikah
Sin Offering / Guilt Offering (Kodshei Kodashim)Liable for me'ilahNOT liable — bones not for the altar
Burnt Offering (Olah)Liable for me'ilahSTILL liable for me'ilah (per Baraita)
RAV ADDA BAR AHAVA'S OBJECTION If Rabba says separated olah bones are permitted after zerikah, why does the Baraita say one is always liable for me'ilah with olah bones?

When Must Hardened Limbs Be Returned? (Amud B)

Rav
Divide the night in two halves:
• Before midnight → mitzva of burning; dislodged limbs must be returned
• After midnight → time for ash removal; dislodged limbs need not be returned
Rav Kahana
Objection! The Mishnah in Yoma says ash removal on Festivals is at the first watch (before midnight), and on regular days at the rooster's crow (after midnight). This contradicts a fixed midnight rule!
R' Yochanan
"All night until the morning" — 'until the morning' means add another morning to the night: arise before dawn for ash removal. No fixed hour is set by Torah law.
RESOLUTION R' Yochanan: The Torah requires burning all night but sets no fixed hour for ash removal — the Rabbis established different times based on practical needs.
