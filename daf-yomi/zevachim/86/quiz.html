Zevachim 86 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yehuda HaNasi, what happens to bones and tendons that separated from the flesh of an olah while already on the altar?
A. They remain on the altar since they already ascended
B. They descend from the altar
C. They are burned separately
D. The Kohen decides on a case-by-case basis
Correct: B — R' Yehuda HaNasi holds that if bones and tendons separated from the flesh, even if they are already on top of the altar, they shall descend. This is derived from reconciling the two verses about what ascends the altar.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabba, what is the status of bones that separated from an olah BEFORE the sprinkling of its blood (zerikah)?
A. They must still ascend the altar
B. They are subject to me'ilah forever
C. They are permitted for ordinary use — even for making knife handles
D. They must be buried
Correct: C — Rabba explains that if bones separated before zerikah, the sprinkling permits them for any use — just as the hide of an olah is permitted to the priests. One may even fashion knife handles from them.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does R' Yochanan reject Rav's midnight-based division of the night for burning and ash removal?
A. The Torah never mentions midnight
B. The Mishnah in Yoma shows ash removal occurs at different times — first watch on Festivals, rooster's crow on regular days
C. Rav's view contradicts R' Yehuda HaNasi
D. Midnight is too late for the Kohanim to begin work
Correct: B — Rav Kahana objects that the Mishnah in Yoma records different ash removal times: first watch on Festivals (before midnight) and rooster's crow on regular days (after midnight). This contradicts a fixed midnight rule, leading R' Yochanan to offer a different explanation.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why was ash removal performed earlier on Festivals than on regular days?
A. It was a special Festival mitzvah
B. The Kohen Gadol needed extra time for the Festival service
C. Masses of Jews arrived early to bring their offerings, filling the courtyard before dawn
D. The Festival offerings required a longer burning period
Correct: C — On Festivals, large numbers of Jews arrived in Jerusalem and came to the Temple early to offer their sacrifices. The ashes needed to be removed earlier to make room for the many offerings of the day.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the significance of the gezerah shavah (verbal analogy) between the phrases 'he shall have' regarding olah and asham?
A. It teaches that olah bones are always forbidden
B. It proves that bones separated from an olah are permitted, just as asham bones are permitted to the priest
C. It teaches that the hide of an asham goes to the priest
D. It establishes that both offerings have identical laws in all respects
Correct: B — The verbal analogy links the olah to the asham: just as bones of an asham (which are not sacrificed on the altar) are permitted to the priest, so too bones that separated from an olah are permitted. Because the phrase is superfluous (mufneh), this analogy cannot be refuted.
