From Noah's Ark to the Temple: The Law of Sacrifice

Noah's Sacrifices
Males, females, blemished, unblemished — all were valid
↓
What was excluded?
Missing Limb
Derived from "of every living being" (Gen. 6:19)
Tereifa
Derived from "with you" — similar to Noah, who was tamim
↓
After Sinai: Gentiles may still offer sacrifices outside the Temple
Key Theme This daf traces the evolution of sacrifice laws from Noah through the Tabernacle, establishing which animals and which people may offer.

Animals Fit for Noah's Sacrifice

Permitted

  • Male and female animals
  • Blemished (מום) animals
  • Unblemished animals
  • Pure (טהור) animals and birds

Excluded

  • Animals missing a limb (מחוסר אבר)
  • Tereifa — mortally wounded animals
  • Impure (טמא) species
  • Old or castrated animals (for the Ark)
Principle Before the Tabernacle, animal offerings followed the same relaxed rules as bird offerings — no requirement for male or unblemished status.

Deriving the Disqualification of Missing Limbs

Genesis 6:19
"And of every living being (חי) of all flesh"
↓
Why does the Torah add the word חי (living)?
↓
R' Elazar: It means bring animals whose limbs are all living — excluding one missing a limb (מחוסר אבר)
↓
Could it exclude a tereifa instead?
↓
No — tereifa is derived from a separate verse: "to keep seed alive" (Gen. 7:3), since a tereifa cannot reproduce
R' Elazar An animal missing a limb is forbidden as a sacrifice even for Bnei Noach (gentiles), derived from the Torah's description of the animals entering the Ark.

Can a Tereifa Give Birth?

Gemara
The tereifa exclusion from "to keep seed alive" works if a tereifa cannot give birth.
Challenger
But according to the opinion that a tereifa can give birth, how do we exclude it?
Gemara
From "to keep them alive with you" (Gen. 6:19) — animals similar to you, i.e., Noah, who was not a tereifa.
Challenger
But perhaps Noah himself was a tereifa!
Gemara
The Torah calls him תמים — complete, meaning physically whole.
Challenger
Maybe תמים means morally complete?
Gemara
He is already called צדיק (righteous) — so תמים must refer to his body.
Challenger
Perhaps צדיק = good deeds, תמים = good character?
Gemara
Even so — would Hashem say: bring tereifot like you, but not whole animals? That is absurd!
Resolution Noah was certainly not a tereifa. The disqualification of tereifa animals is derived from "with you" — similar to Noah's wholeness.

Three Verses, Three Exclusions for the Ark

Verse (Genesis)PhraseWhat It Excludes
6:19"Of every living being" (חי)Animal missing a limb (מחוסר אבר)
6:19"To keep them alive with you" (אתך)Tereifa (mortally wounded)
7:3"To keep seed alive" (לחיות זרע)Old or castrated animals
Note Each phrase in the Torah's instructions to Noah serves a distinct halakhic purpose, excluding different categories of unfit animals.

How Did Noah Know Which Animals Were Pure?

❓ The Torah's distinction of pure/impure species was not yet given
↓
🚢 Rav Ḥisda: Noah passed all animals before the Ark — those the Ark accepted (drew in) were pure
↓
🐾 R' Abbahu: The pure animals came to Noah on their own, in pairs — derived from "they that went in, went in male and female"
↓
📖 R' Shmuel bar Naḥmani: "Pure" means animals not used in the performance of sin
Insight The Ark itself served as a divine filter, miraculously identifying which animals were fit for entry and later for sacrifice.
