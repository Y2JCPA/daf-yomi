QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why were blemished animals permitted for Noah's sacrifices?
A. Because the concept of blemishes did not exist before Sinai
B. Because animals were juxtaposed with birds, for which blemish/gender rules don't apply
C. Because Noah had too few unblemished animals after the flood
D. Because blemished animals were considered more humble offerings
Correct: B — The Gemara derives that since animals are juxtaposed with birds in Genesis 8:20, and birds have no requirement for unblemished or male status, animals before the Tabernacle likewise had no such requirement.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the phrase 'of every living being (חי)' exclude from sacrifice?
A. Animals that are tereifa (mortally wounded)
B. Animals that are too old to reproduce
C. Animals that are missing a limb (מחוסר אבר)
D. Female animals
Correct: C — R' Elazar teaches that the superfluous word 'חי' (living) means: bring an animal whose limbs are ALL living — excluding one that is missing a limb.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara prove that Noah was not a tereifa?
A. Because the Torah says he lived to 950 years
B. Because 'righteous' covers his deeds, so 'complete' (תמים) must refer to his body
C. Because God explicitly told him he was healthy
D. Because only healthy people could survive the flood
Correct: B — Since the Torah already calls Noah 'righteous' (צדיק) for his actions, the additional term 'complete' (תמים) must refer to his physical wholeness, proving he was not a tereifa.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda, how did Noah identify which animals were pure?
A. God told him directly which species were pure
B. Pure animals came in groups of seven, impure in pairs
C. He passed all animals before the Ark — those the Ark accepted were pure
D. Angels marked the pure animals with a sign
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda says Noah caused all animals to pass before the Ark. Those that the Ark accepted (drew in) were known to be pure; those it did not accept were impure.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What restriction applies to a Jew regarding a gentile's sacrifice outside the Temple?
A. A Jew may not even instruct a gentile on how to sacrifice
B. A Jew may assist but not act as the gentile's agent
C. A Jew may not assist or act as agent, but may instruct
D. A Jew has no restrictions regarding gentile sacrifices
Correct: C — Rav Asi rules that a Jew may not assist or fulfill agency for a gentile's sacrifice outside the Temple. However, Rabba permits instructing them, as Rava did when Ifera Hurmiz sent an offering.
