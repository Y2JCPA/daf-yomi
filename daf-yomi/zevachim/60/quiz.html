QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yosei, what was the height of the copper altar built in Moses's time?
A. 3 cubits
B. 5 cubits
C. 10 cubits
D. 15 cubits
Correct: C — R' Yosei derives from a gezerah shavah of 'square' (comparing the copper altar to the golden incense altar) that the copper altar was 10 cubits high. The verse stating '3 cubits' refers to the measurement from the surrounding ledge upward.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the core methodological disagreement between R' Yehuda and R' Yosei regarding the gezerah shavah?
A. Whether gezerah shavah can override an explicit verse
B. Whether to derive a portable vessel from another portable vessel, or from a permanent structure
C. Whether the golden altar or copper altar was built first
D. Whether Ezekiel's prophecy has legal authority
Correct: B — R' Yosei holds we derive a portable vessel (copper altar) from another portable vessel (golden incense altar). R' Yehuda holds we derive the external altar from the external altar described in Ezekiel, even though it is a permanent edifice.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What proof does Rava bring that R' Yehuda requires blood to be presented on the altar itself?
A. R' Yehuda says the altar must be intact to eat offerings
B. R' Yehuda requires a priest to fill a cup of mixed blood on Passover eve and pour it on the altar
C. R' Yehuda says blood that falls on the courtyard floor is invalid
D. R' Yehuda interprets 'beside the altar' literally
Correct: B — Rava reasons: If R' Yehuda held the courtyard floor truly had full altar status, blood spilling on the floor would already fulfill the mitzva. Yet R' Yehuda requires pouring a cup of mixed blood on the altar on Passover eve — proving blood specifically needs the altar.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does R' Yishmael need a verse (Deuteronomy 12:6) to prove second tithe cannot be eaten after the Temple's destruction?
A. Because the kal va-chomer from firstborn and first fruits can be refuted — each has a unique connection to the altar
B. Because second tithe is a Rabbinic obligation
C. Because second tithe was never associated with Jerusalem
D. Because the common element derivation proves the opposite
Correct: A — A logical derivation from firstborn (requires blood on altar) can be challenged by noting second tithe has no altar component. First fruits can be challenged because they require placement beside the altar. Since each source has a unique stringency, R' Yishmael needs the explicit juxtaposition in Deuteronomy 12:6.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the problem of deriving second tithe via a 'double juxtaposition' (the firstborn's meat is itself derived from its blood)?
A. It is permitted because Moses received this tradition at Sinai
B. Second tithe is considered non-sacred (חולין), so the restriction on double juxtapositions for consecrated matters does not apply
C. The derivation actually uses a kal va-chomer, not a juxtaposition
D. R' Yishmael holds juxtapositions can always chain without limitation
Correct: B — The rule against a 'matter derived via juxtaposition teaching another matter via juxtaposition' applies to consecrated matters. Since second tithe is classified as non-sacred (חולין), this restriction does not apply, and the derivation is valid.
