Zevachim 27 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Mar Zutra, why doesn't intent to sprinkle blood in the wrong place the next day render the offering piggul?
A. Because sprinkling in the wrong place doesn't permit the meat for consumption
B. Because piggul only applies to eating, not sprinkling
C. Because the blood was not actually sprinkled yet
D. Because wrong-place intent is not considered a real intent
Correct: A — Mar Zutra derives from Leviticus 7:18 that only sprinkling that permits meat for consumption can create piggul. Since wrong-place sprinkling never permits the meat, it cannot generate piggul — even combined with beyond-time intent.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Reish Lakish distinguish between cases where misapplied blood effects atonement and where it disqualifies?
A. Whether the blood touched the altar base or not
B. Whether the blood was placed in silence (no intent) vs. with improper intent
C. Whether the offering was a sin offering or a burnt offering
D. Whether the blood was placed above or below the red line
Correct: B — Reish Lakish holds that misapplied blood placed in silence (without improper intent) effects atonement, while blood placed with stated intent to consume beyond its designated time disqualifies the offering.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Hisda citing Avimi, in which scenario do R' Yosei and R' Shimon disagree about gathering misapplied blood?
A. Below-line blood placed above the red line
B. Above-line blood placed below the red line
C. Inside blood placed outside, or outside blood placed inside
D. Blood placed on the ramp instead of the altar
Correct: C — All agree that above/below misapplication on the same altar cannot be gathered. The dispute is only about inside (golden altar) vs. outside (external altar) misapplication — R' Yosei says gather it like spilled blood; R' Shimon says the altar absorbed it.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is R' Shimon's criterion for which disqualified offerings do NOT descend from the altar once placed upon it?
A. Only burnt offerings and sin offerings
B. Offerings whose disqualification occurred in sanctity (during Temple service)
C. Any offering that a kohen placed on the altar
D. Offerings that were slaughtered properly but had blood misapplied
Correct: B — R' Shimon derives from 'ha'ola' that all offerings disqualified 'in sanctity' — meaning during the course of Temple service — do not descend. Offerings with inherent pre-existing disqualifications (bestiality, idolatry, tereifa) do descend.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is R' Eliezer's novel teaching about the inner golden altar?
A. Only Yom Kippur blood may be placed on the golden altar
B. The golden altar cannot sanctify any disqualified offering
C. Even items never fit for the golden altar (like meal offerings) do not descend once placed on it
D. The golden altar has the same red line as the external altar
Correct: C — The baraita already taught that misapplied blood doesn't descend — but blood is sometimes fit for the golden altar (Yom Kippur). R' Eliezer's chiddush is that even a handful of meal offering, which is never fit for the golden altar, is sanctified and does not descend.
