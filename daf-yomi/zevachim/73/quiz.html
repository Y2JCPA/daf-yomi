Zevachim 73 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yehoshua (as reported by R' Yehuda), what is the rule for items occasionally sold by unit?
A. They are nullified in a simple majority
B. They are nullified in a ratio of 1:100
C. They are never nullified
D. They are nullified only by rabbinic authority
Correct: C — R' Yehoshua (per R' Yehuda) holds that even items only occasionally sold by unit — like circles of dried figs — can never be nullified, even in a ratio of 1:300.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why can't we simply remove one animal at a time from the mixture and sacrifice it based on 'kol d'parish'?
A. Because the animals are too intermingled to separate
B. Because taking from a fixed location (kavu'a) is treated as half-and-half
C. Because the Torah explicitly forbids it
D. Because we cannot identify which animal is disqualified
Correct: B — The principle of kavu'a dictates that taking something from its fixed location is treated as an equally balanced uncertainty (half-and-half), not as following the majority.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rava's final reason for prohibiting the workaround of moving the animals from their places?
A. Moving animals causes them physical harm
B. It is a decree lest one permit taking from a fixed location in another case
C. Ten priests might come and sacrifice simultaneously
D. The animals become permanently rejected once moved
Correct: B — Rava's final answer is a gezeirah (rabbinic decree): if we permit circumventing kavu'a by moving animals, people may come to permit taking directly from a fixed location in other situations.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What challenge does the mishna in Kinnim pose to Rava's ruling?
A. It shows that bird offerings need not be separated at all
B. It shows that a priest who acted without consulting can produce valid offerings from a mixture
C. It proves that all intermingled offerings must die
D. It demonstrates that bird offerings follow different nullification rules
Correct: B — The Kinnim mishna shows that when a priest sacrificed intermingled birds without consulting, half are valid — proving that offerings from a mixture can work after the fact, contradicting Rava.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, why does even R' Meir agree that disqualified animals in a mixture are not nullified?
A. Because animals are always sold by unit count
B. Because living creatures are inherently significant (chashivi)
C. Because the Torah lists animals as an exception to nullification
D. Because the disqualification is of Torah-level severity
Correct: B — Rav Ashi explains that living creatures possess inherent significance (ba'alei chayyim chashivi) that prevents nullification, independent of whether they are formally 'counted items.'
