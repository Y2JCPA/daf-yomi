QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why do the Rabbis hold that a guilt offering (asham) whose blood enters the Sanctuary is NOT disqualified?
A. Because the verse says "whose blood" (דָּמָהּ) — limiting the disqualification to sin offerings only
B. Because a guilt offering is not a Kodshei Kodashim offering
C. Because a guilt offering has no blood service
D. Because Rabbi Eliezer agrees on this point
Correct: A — The Rabbis derive from "whose blood" (דָּמָהּ) in Leviticus 6:23 that only the blood of THIS offering — the sin offering — is disqualified by entering the Sanctuary, excluding all other offerings.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the fatal flaw in the tzad hashaveh (common element) argument combining burnt offering and sinner's meal offering?
A. Neither is a Kodshei Kodashim offering
B. Neither has a fixed monetary value — unlike a guilt offering which does
C. Both require sprinkling on the inner altar
D. Both atone for sins — so they cannot be compared to a guilt offering
Correct: B — Rava of Barnish objects that both burnt offerings and sinner's meal offerings lack a fixed value, whereas a guilt offering has a fixed value — breaking the common element argument.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Shimon brother of Azarya, what happens when peace offerings (shelamim) are slaughtered for the sake of a burnt offering (olah)?
A. They are completely disqualified
B. They are fit because olah has greater sanctity
C. They are fit only if done inadvertently
D. They are fit and fully propitiate God
Correct: B — Shimon brother of Azarya's principle: offerings sacrificed for the sake of offerings with GREATER sanctity remain fit. Olah (Kodshei Kodashim) has greater sanctity than shelamim (Kodashim Kalim), so the shelamim is fit.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Abaye prove from Shimon brother of Azarya's ruling about a firstborn animal slaughtered as peace offerings?
A. That the firstborn animal is a Kodshei Kodashim offering
B. That lesser-sanctity offerings sacrificed for greater sanctity are fit but do NOT propitiate
C. That peace offerings have greater sanctity than firstborn
D. That Shimon brother of Azarya disagrees with the Rabbis on two points
Correct: B — Since a firstborn animal never serves to propitiate God, Abaye reasons that when Shimon says it is "fit" when slaughtered as shelamim, he must mean fit but NOT propitiating — suggesting this is the rule for all such cases.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rabbi Eliezer respond to Rabbi Yehoshua's argument that his logic would disqualify a burnt offering slaughtered for the sake of a sin offering?
A. He concedes the point and modifies his position
B. He argues that a sin offering is valid for its sake all year, while a Paschal offering is valid only at its designated time
C. He cites a different verse to support his position
D. He distinguishes between Kodshei Kodashim and Kodashim Kalim
Correct: B — Rabbi Eliezer distinguishes the Paschal offering from a regular sin offering: a sin offering is fit for its own sake year-round, but a Paschal offering is fit for its own sake only at its designated time — justifying the stricter rule for Pesach.
