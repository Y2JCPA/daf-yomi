Festival Cooking: Scour After Every Offering?

R' Tarfon
If one cooked a sin offering from the beginning of the Festival, one may cook in the same vessel the entire Festival without scouring and rinsing.
Rabbis
One must perform scouring and rinsing before the end of the eating period (זמן אכילה) of each particular offering.
Rav Naḥman / Rabba bar Avuh
R' Tarfon's reasoning: the meat of each day becomes a purging agent (מבליע) for the absorption of the prior day — continuous use cleanses the vessel.
R' Natan citing R' Tarfon
This leniency applies only to scouring and rinsing — piggul and notar still apply during the Festival as normal.
RESOLUTION R' Tarfon does not truly treat the entire Festival as one day. Rather, the constant reuse of the vessel means each cooking session purges the prior absorption, making separate scouring unnecessary.

Scouring & Rinsing: Hot or Cold Water?

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi

  • Both scouring and rinsing with cold water
  • This is a mitzva requirement, not the same as purging (הגעלה)
  • Purging absorbed taste requires hot water — but scouring/rinsing is a separate obligation
  • Two verbs (מריקה ושטיפה) teach two distinct actions, not two temperatures
  • Scouring = inside of cup; Rinsing = outside of cup

Rabbis (Ḥakhamim)

  • Scouring with hot water; Rinsing with cold water
  • Analogous to purging vessels of gentiles — hot water removes absorbed taste
  • Two different verbs indicate two different methods
  • If both were the same, the Torah would have repeated one verb (שטיפה ושטיפה)
  • The distinction in language proves a distinction in temperature
TEXTUAL BASIS "It shall be scoured and rinsed in water" (Lev. 6:21) — the use of two distinct verbs drives the entire debate.

When Sacred Meat Touches Other Meat

A piece of meat touches a sin offering
↓
Did it touch the flesh (בשר) itself?
Yes — flesh
The touching section becomes sacred like the sin offering
No — sinews/bones/horns/hooves
Not disqualified — these are not "flesh"
↓
Did the sin offering absorb into only part of the piece?
Partial absorption
Slice off the absorbed section; rest remains permitted
Full absorption
Entire piece must be eaten under sin offering restrictions
VERSE "Whatever shall touch its flesh shall be sacred" (Lev. 6:20) — only actual flesh contact transfers status, and only the section that absorbs.

Can a Positive Mitzva Override a Temple Prohibition?

Gemara
If sacrificial meat touched a disqualified sin offering, why not say the positive mitzva of eating the sacrifice overrides the prohibition?
Rava
A positive mitzva does not override a prohibition that relates to the Temple (מקדש).
R' Shimon ben Menasya
Proof: breaking a bone of the Paschal offering to eat marrow is prohibited — even though eating the marrow is a positive mitzva.
Rav Ashi
Actually, the verse "shall be sacred" is itself a positive mitzva — so here we have both a prohibition and a positive mitzva opposing the eating. A positive mitzva cannot override two obligations.
KEY RULE עשה אינו דוחה לא תעשה שבמקדש — A positive mitzva does not override a prohibition connected to the Temple service.
