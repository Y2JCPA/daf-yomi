QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman citing Rabba bar Avuh, why does R' Tarfon permit using the same vessel throughout the Festival without scouring?
A. Because the entire Festival is treated as a single day for all halakhot
B. Because each day's cooking purges the previous day's absorbed taste
C. Because Festival offerings are exempt from the laws of absorption
D. Because copper vessels do not absorb taste during Festivals
Correct: B — R' Tarfon's leniency is not based on treating the Festival as one day universally (piggul and notar still apply). Rather, the continuous cooking means each new batch of sacrificial meat purges what was absorbed from the previous day.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the dispute between Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi and the Rabbis regarding scouring and rinsing?
A. Whether scouring and rinsing are required at all
B. Whether both are done with cold water or scouring with hot and rinsing with cold
C. Whether the obligation is biblical or rabbinic
D. Whether they apply to earthenware or only copper vessels
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi holds both scouring and rinsing are performed with cold water — they are a distinct mitzva, not the same as purging. The Rabbis hold scouring uses hot water (like purging gentile vessels) and rinsing uses cold, as indicated by the two different verbs.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If meat touches only the sinews or bones of a sin offering, what is its status?
A. It becomes sacred like the sin offering
B. It must be entirely burned
C. It is not disqualified — only flesh contact transfers status
D. It requires scouring and rinsing of the vessel
Correct: C — The verse specifies "whatever shall touch its flesh shall be sacred" — sinews, bones, horns, and hooves are not classified as "flesh," so contact with them does not transfer the sin offering's sacred status.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why can't the positive mitzva of eating sacrificial meat override the prohibition against eating meat that absorbed from a disqualified sin offering?
A. Because the meat has already lost its sanctity
B. Because a positive mitzva never overrides a prohibition
C. Because a positive mitzva does not override a prohibition relating to the Temple
D. Because the disqualified sin offering is considered non-sacred
Correct: C — Rava establishes the principle that a positive mitzva does not override a prohibition relating to the Temple. Rav Ashi adds that here there are actually both a prohibition AND a positive mitzva ('shall be sacred') opposing the eating — making it even clearer that the mitzva to eat cannot override.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
From where does the Gemara derive that all offerings must be slaughtered with a knife?
A. From the laws of the Paschal offering in Exodus
B. From the burnt offering via Abraham's knife at the Akeidah, extended through Leviticus 7:37
C. From an explicit command in the laws of the sin offering
D. From the meal offering's requirement for a utensil
Correct: B — The burnt offering requires a knife — derived from Abraham taking the knife (ma'akhelet) to slaughter at the Akeidah, where the ram was offered as a burnt offering. Leviticus 7:37 then connects all offerings, extending the knife requirement universally.
