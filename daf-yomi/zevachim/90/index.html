Sacrificial Portions Taken Outside the Courtyard

R' Eliezer
Portions taken outside the courtyard are disqualified — one is not liable for piggul, notar, or tum'ah
R' Akiva
One is liable for me'ilah, piggul, notar, and tum'ah — the portions remain fit
Rav Pappa
If returned before sprinkling — all agree they are fit. They disagree only when portions are outside during sprinkling
R' Eliezer
Sprinkling is not effective (lo mo'il) for portions that are outside the courtyard
R' Akiva
Sprinkling is effective (mo'il) even for portions outside the courtyard
Core Dispute Does zerikat ha-dam (blood sprinkling) retroactively render fit portions that were outside the azarah at the time of sprinkling?

Two Loaves vs. Sacrificial Portions — Rav Pappa's Distinction

Two Loaves (Shavuot)

  • Not part of the offering itself
  • If outside during sprinkling — ALL AGREE sprinkling does NOT help
  • Dispute is only about returned loaves before sprinkling

Sacrificial Portions (Eimurim)

  • Part of the offering itself
  • If returned before sprinkling — ALL AGREE they are fit
  • Dispute is when they are OUTSIDE during sprinkling
Rav Pappa's Logic Because eimurim (sacrificial portions) are integral to the offering, the dispute centers on their status during sprinkling. The two loaves, being external to the offering, receive a stricter ruling.

The Sota's Meal Offering — Where Does It Rank?

Gemara
Dilemma: Does a minḥat sotah precede a voluntary meal offering?
Side A
Voluntary should precede — it requires oil and frankincense
Side B
Sotah's should precede — it clarifies her transgression
Gemara
Proof from mishna: only sinner's precedes voluntary — implying sotah does not?
Gemara
Rejected: the mishna says 'comes because of sin' — and the sotah also comes because of sin (she secluded herself)
Baraita
This offering precedes that, as this comes from wheat and that from barley — sinner's meal offering precedes sotah's
Unresolved The Gemara does not conclusively resolve whether the minḥat sotah precedes a voluntary meal offering. The baraita addresses sinner vs. sotah, not voluntary vs. sotah.

The Idol Worship Exception: Sin Offering After Burnt Offering

OfferingTypePrecedence
Bull of Anointed Priest (Kohen Gadol)Sin Offering1st — highest sanctity
Bull for Unwitting Communal SinSin Offering2nd — precedes burnt offering
Bull for Communal Idol WorshipBurnt Offering3rd — precedes goat despite being a burnt offering
Goat for Communal Idol WorshipSin Offering4th — despite being a sin offering
Why the Exception? Ravina: The verse says ka-mishpat ('according to the ordinance,' Numbers 15:24), mandating the precise order stated in the verse — burnt offering before sin offering. Eretz Yisrael tradition: The word ḥatat for idol worship is written without an alef, hinting it differs from standard sin offerings.
