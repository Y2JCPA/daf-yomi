5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, when do R' Eliezer and R' Akiva disagree about sacrificial portions (eimurim) taken outside?
A. When they are outside during the blood sprinkling
B. When they are returned before the blood sprinkling
C. When they were never brought into the courtyard at all
D. When they are burned outside the courtyard
Correct: A — Rav Pappa says all agree returned portions are fit. The dispute is only when they remain outside during the blood sprinkling — R' Eliezer says sprinkling is ineffective for them, R' Akiva says it is effective.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rav Pappa distinguish between the two loaves of Shavuot and the sacrificial portions (eimurim)?
A. The two loaves are more sacred than eimurim
B. The two loaves are not part of the offering itself, while eimurim are
C. The eimurim require oil and frankincense
D. The two loaves can never leave the courtyard
Correct: B — Rav Pappa explains that the two loaves are external to the offering, so all agree sprinkling doesn't help when they're outside. Eimurim, being integral to the offering, have a broader scope for R' Akiva's lenient ruling.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does a bird offering take precedence over a meal offering despite meal offerings being brought as both communal and individual?
A. Birds are more expensive
B. Bird offerings involve blood presentation, which is more important
C. Birds come from living creatures
D. Meal offerings are only voluntary
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the fact that bird offerings are 'types whose blood is sprinkled' is of greater importance than the communal/individual distinction that favors meal offerings.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the burnt offering for communal idol worship precede its accompanying sin offering, contrary to the usual rule?
A. Idol worship is the most severe sin
B. The Torah writes 'ka-mishpat' (according to the ordinance), mandating the verse's order
C. The burnt offering for idol worship is more costly
D. The Sanhedrin ruled it should be this way
Correct: B — Ravina explains that the verse (Numbers 15:24) uses the term 'ka-mishpat' (according to the ordinance), which mandates following the precise order stated — burnt offering first, then sin offering.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the leper's guilt offering (asham) uniquely precede the sin offering?
A. It is more expensive than the sin offering
B. It comes to render the leper fit for contact with sanctified items
C. The leper's sin offering is only a bird
D. It was instituted by a later rabbinic decree
Correct: B — The mishna states the leper's asham precedes his sin offering 'because it comes to render one fit' (l'hakhshir) — it is central to the purification process that allows the former leper to resume contact with sacred things.
