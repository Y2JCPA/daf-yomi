Zevachim 104 — Hides, Burning Places & Overnight Disqualification

DAF OVERVIEW This daf explores whether blood sprinkling can save the hide when flesh is disqualified, details three distinct burning locations in and around the Temple, and raises the question of whether linah (overnight) disqualifies the flesh of bulls and goats that are burned.
3 / 9

Does Blood Accept the Hide Independently?

R' Yehuda HaNasi
The blood effects acceptance of the hide by itself after flaying — even if the flesh is later disqualified.
R' Elazar b'R' Shimon
The blood does not effect acceptance of the hide by itself.
R' Elazar b'R' Shimon
But if the disqualification arose after sprinkling while hide is on the flesh — the flesh was accepted for a time, so the priest may flay it and keep the hide.
R' Yehuda HaNasi
If the hide is still on the flesh when disqualification occurs — whether before or after sprinkling — the hide follows the flesh and both are burned.
KEY DISTINCTION They disagree on hides already separated from the flesh. For hides still attached, timing of disqualification matters differently for each opinion.

When Is the Hide Saved from Burning?

Disqualification arises in the offering's flesh
↓
Was the hide already flayed from the flesh?
YES — Already Flayed
R' Yehuda HaNasi: Hide goes to priests (blood accepted it)
R' Elazar b'R' Shimon: Hide burned (blood doesn't accept independently)
NO — Still Attached
Was blood already sprinkled?
• Before sprinkling → Both burn (all agree)
• After sprinkling → R' Elazar: Flay & priests keep hide; Rebbi: Both burn
R' CHANINA The Deputy Kohen Gadol testified: "In all my days I never saw a hide go out to burning" — he holds like R' Yehuda HaNasi.

R' Eliezer vs R' Yehoshua: Flesh & Blood Interdependence

R' Yehoshua

  • No blood → no flesh; no flesh → no blood
  • Flesh and blood are interdependent
  • Source: "the flesh AND the blood" (Deut. 12:27)
  • Concedes: if sprinkled anyway — accepted after the fact

R' Eliezer

  • Blood is sprinkled even without flesh
  • Source: "the blood shall be poured out"
  • Verse teaches: flesh is tossed onto altar (like blood is sprinkled)
  • Proves: there is a gap between the ramp and the altar
CONNECTION R' Yehuda HaNasi argues: even R' Yehoshua would agree blood saves the hide — because where priests suffer a loss, the blood should still effect acceptance.

Does Linah Disqualify Bulls That Are Burned?

R' Yirmeya
Dilemma: Linah (being left overnight) disqualifies most offerings. Does it also disqualify bulls/goats that are burned — whose flesh is neither eaten nor offered on the altar?
Rava
Abaye raised this too. I resolved it: piggul intent for burning these bulls does nothing — so linah shouldn't disqualify either!
Gemara
Rejected! Perhaps improper intention doesn't disqualify, but actual overnight passage does disqualify — they are different mechanisms.
Gemara
Proof from Me'ilah 9a: These offerings are susceptible to disqualification through tevul yom, mechusar kippurim, and linah!
ANALYSIS The Gemara debates whether the mishnah in Me'ilah refers to the flesh or only the eimurim (sacrificial portions). The latter clause discussing flesh suggests the former clause also addresses flesh.
