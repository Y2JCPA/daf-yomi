Zevachim 104 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yehuda HaNasi, when does blood sprinkling effect acceptance of the hide by itself?
A. After the hide has been flayed, even if flesh is disqualified
B. Only when the flesh is still fit for the altar
C. Only before the blood is sprinkled
D. Never — the hide always follows the flesh
Correct: A — R' Yehuda HaNasi holds that once the hide is separated from the flesh, blood effects acceptance of the hide independently, even if the flesh is later disqualified.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the disagreement between R' Yochanan and Reish Lakish about the 'bira'?
A. Whether the bira is in Jerusalem or outside it
B. Whether the bira is a specific place on the Temple Mount or a name for the entire Temple
C. Whether disqualified offerings may be burned in the bira
D. Whether the bira existed during the First Temple period
Correct: B — R' Yochanan says the bira is a specific place on the Temple Mount. Reish Lakish says the entire Temple is called bira, citing I Chronicles 29:19.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Nachman's version, where were bulls and goats burned if disqualified AFTER blood sprinkling?
A. In the great ash-place in the Temple courtyard
B. On the Temple Mount
C. Outside the three camps (outside Jerusalem)
D. On the altar itself
Correct: B — Rav Nachman's version teaches that bulls/goats disqualified after sprinkling were burned on the Temple Mount, while those disqualified before sprinkling were burned in the great ash-place in the courtyard.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What was Rava's attempted proof that linah does NOT disqualify bulls that are burned?
A. A verse in Leviticus exempting burned offerings
B. The principle that piggul intent for their burning has no effect
C. R' Yehoshua's ruling about flesh and blood
D. The mishnah about hides going to priests
Correct: B — Rava argued: since piggul intent regarding the burning of these bulls has no effect, linah should likewise not disqualify them. The Gemara rejected this, noting the two mechanisms are different.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Shimon, when do the garments of those carrying bulls to be burned become tamei?
A. As soon as they exit the Temple courtyard
B. Only when the fire is ignited in the majority of the offering
C. When they pick up the poles
D. When both front and rear carriers have exited
Correct: B — R' Shimon holds that garment impurity applies only to those who actually burn the offerings, and only once the fire is ignited in the majority — not merely upon exiting the courtyard.
