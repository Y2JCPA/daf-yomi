QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does R' Yosei HaGelili place the Basin south of the altar rather than north?
A. The south was closer to the water source
B. The north must be vacant of vessels for slaughtering (Lev. 1:11)
C. The Basin needed to face the entrance of the Sanctuary
D. The priests always approached from the south
Correct: B — Leviticus 1:11 requires the north to be vacant (tzafona) for slaughtering sacrifices, so the Basin cannot be placed there. Since R' Yosei HaGelili holds the altar is in the north, the Basin must go south.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the fundamental disagreement between R' Yosei HaGelili and R' Eliezer ben Yaakov?
A. Whether the Basin must be between the altar and the Sanctuary
B. Whether the entire altar stands in the north or the south of the courtyard
C. Whether a damaged altar disqualifies sacrifices
D. Whether the Entrance Hall has the same sanctity as the Sanctuary
Correct: B — R' Yosei HaGelili holds the entire altar stood in the north, while R' Eliezer ben Yaakov holds it stood entirely in the south, since 'northward' means the north must be vacant of everything — even the altar.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Yosei, what does the verse mean when it says the copper altar 'was too small to receive' Solomon's offerings?
A. The altar literally could not fit all the offerings
B. It is a euphemism — the copper altar was disqualified from use
C. Solomon needed to build a larger replacement
D. The altar had been physically damaged
Correct: B — R' Yosei argues that 'too small' is a euphemism (like calling someone a 'nanas'/dwarf to mean disqualified), indicating the copper altar of Moses was disqualified once the Temple was inaugurated.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does R' Yehuda derive that Moses' altar was 10×10 cubits rather than 5×5?
A. From the total area needed for Solomon's offerings
B. From a gezera shava of 'square' linking it to Ezekiel's altar, which was measured from center
C. From the dimensions of the Tabernacle courtyard
D. From the height of the courtyard curtains
Correct: B — R' Yehuda uses a verbal analogy (gezera shava): both Moses' altar and Ezekiel's altar are called 'square.' Since Ezekiel's altar was measured from its center, so too Moses' altar — making it 10×10 cubits total.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does R' Yosei respond to R' Yehuda's objection that a 10-cubit-tall altar would let the priest be seen over the 5-cubit courtyard walls?
A. The priest would crouch down during service
B. The altar had a sunken pit for the priest to stand in
C. The courtyard curtains were actually 15 cubits high on the sides
D. Visibility was not a halakhic concern
Correct: C — R' Yosei cites Numbers 4:26, which juxtaposes the Tabernacle and altar (both 10 cubits high), and notes that the curtains on the sides were 15 cubits tall — high enough to conceal the priest performing service atop the altar.
