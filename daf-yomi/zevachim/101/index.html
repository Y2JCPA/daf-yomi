Why Was the Sin Offering Burned on the Eighth Day?

Moses
Commanded Aaron: Eat the offerings even in acute mourning (aninut) — 'For so I am commanded'
Aaron
'There have befallen me such things as these — would it have been good in the eyes of the Lord?'
Shmuel
No contradiction: one baraita follows R' Yehuda, the other follows R' Nechemya
Rava
Both follow R' Nechemya — distinguish offerings of a particular time (eat in aninut) from offerings of all generations (do not eat)
Core Issue The eighth day of the Tabernacle's inauguration: Aaron's sons die, and the question arises whether an onen (acute mourner) may consume sacrificial meat.

Why Was the Sin Offering Burned?

R' Nechemya
It was burned due to acute mourning (aninut) — as Aaron said: 'There have befallen me such things as these'
R' Yehuda & R' Shimon
It was burned due to ritual impurity (tum'ah) that befell it
R' Yehuda & R' Shimon
If due to aninut — why not burn all three sin offerings?
R' Yehuda & R' Shimon
If due to aninut — they could have eaten it that evening!
R' Yehuda & R' Shimon
If due to aninut — Pinehas wasn't mourning! He could have eaten it!
Three Challenges R' Yehuda and R' Shimon raise three objections to the aninut theory, each of which R' Nechemya must answer.

Rava's Resolution: Both Baraitot Follow R' Nechemya

Is this an offering of a particular time
(unique to inauguration)?
Yes — e.g., Meal Offering
Eat it even in aninut
Moses commanded this
No — Offering of All Generations
Do not eat in aninut
Aaron was correct to burn it
Rava's Principle The inauguration's unique offerings override aninut, but regular offerings (like the New Moon chatat) do not.

R' Nechemya's Answers to the Three Challenges

Challenge: Burn All Three?

  • Only the New Moon offering was burned
  • The other two were 'offerings of a particular time'
  • Aninut doesn't disqualify unique inauguration offerings

Challenge: Eat at Night?

  • R' Nechemya holds: aninut at night is by Torah law
  • Therefore they could not eat it even in the evening
  • Impurity then came upon it, forcing them to burn it

Challenge: Pinehas?

  • Pinehas was not yet a priest at this time
  • R' Elazar/R' Chanina: he became a priest only after killing Zimri
  • Rav Ashi: only after making peace among the tribes (Joshua 22)
Note R' Nechemya consistently maintains his position by holding nighttime aninut is Torah-level and that Pinehas was not yet a kohen.
