The Core Question: Leftover Blood

וְהַנִּשְׁאָר בַּדָּם יִמָּצֵה אֶל יְסוֹד הַמִּזְבֵּחַ חַטָּאת הִוא
STARTING POINT All offerings require that leftover blood (שְׁיָרֵי הַדָּם) be placed on the base of the altar (יְסוֹד הַמִּזְבֵּחַ).

R' Yehuda HaNasi derives this from Leviticus 5:9: "And the rest of the blood shall be squeezed out at the base of the altar."

The seemingly superfluous phrase "of the blood" (מִן הַדָּם) teaches that this requirement applies to all offerings — not just outer sin offerings whose blood requires four placements.

Sprinkling vs. Pouring: Can One Substitute for the Other?

R' Yishmael
Sprinkling is included in pouring (זְרִיקָה בִּכְלַל שְׁפִיכָה). If blood should be sprinkled but was poured, the obligation is fulfilled.
R' Akiva
Sprinkling is NOT included in pouring, and pouring is NOT included in sprinkling. They are entirely separate actions.
R' Yishmael
Proof: The blessing over the Paschal offering (whose blood is poured) covers the Festival offering (whose blood is sprinkled) — because sprinkling is included in pouring.
R' Akiva
Neither blessing exempts the other! Each offering needs its own separate blessing because each blood-service method is distinct.
WHY IT MATTERS If R' Akiva is correct, Deut. 12:27 ("shall be poured") cannot teach that pouring fulfills a sprinkling obligation — freeing the verse to teach that one placement suffices.

R' Yosei HaGelili vs. R' Yishmael: Blood of Tithe & Pesach

R' Yosei HaGelili

  • Derives tithe & Pesach blood placement from Numbers 18:17 (firstborn verse)
  • "You shall sprinkle THEIR blood" — 'their' includes tithe & Pesach
  • Deut. 12:27 is FREE → available to teach one placement suffices
  • Numbers 18:17 also teaches: substitutes of these offerings are NOT sacrificed

R' Yishmael

  • Numbers 18:17 speaks ONLY about the firstborn animal
  • Needs Deut. 12:27 to derive tithe & Pesach blood placement
  • Deut. 12:27 is OCCUPIED → cannot teach one placement suffices
  • Derives substitutes via verbal analogy (gezeira shava): "passing" / "passing"
RESOLUTION Those tanna'im who cannot free Deut. 12:27 instead follow Beit Hillel — deriving that one placement suffices from the halakha of a sin offering itself.

Substitutes of Firstborn, Tithe & Pesach: Are They Sacrificed?

"They are sacred" (Numbers 18:17) — they are sacrificed, but not their substitutes (תְּמוּרָה)
↓
Firstborn Substitute
NOT sacrificed
Eaten when blemished
Tithe Substitute
NOT sacrificed
Derived via gezeira shava: "passing" (Exodus 13:12 ↔ Lev. 27:32)
Pesach Substitute
Depends on timing
↓
Before Pesach sacrifice
NOT sacrificed — "It is the Lord's Passover" (Exodus 12:27): it, not its substitute
After Pesach sacrifice
Sacrificed as a peace offering — Lev. 3:7: "If he offers a lamb" includes the substitute
R' YEHOSHUA'S RIDDLE R' Yehoshua heard two rulings — one that the Pesach substitute IS sacrificed and one that it is NOT — and couldn't explain them. The Gemara resolves: it depends on whether substitution occurred before or after the Pesach was sacrificed.
