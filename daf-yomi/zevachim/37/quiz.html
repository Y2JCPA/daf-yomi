QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does R' Yehuda HaNasi say the phrase 'of the blood' in Leviticus 5:9 is superfluous?
A. Because the verse already explicitly mentions blood placement on the base
B. Because the entire verse is already discussing blood, making the phrase redundant
C. Because bird offerings don't actually require blood placement
D. Because the word 'blood' appears twice in the same verse
Correct: B — The verse is entirely about blood, so specifying 'of the blood' is unnecessary. This superfluity teaches that ALL offerings — not just outer sin offerings — require leftover blood placement on the altar base.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Akiva, if a priest was supposed to sprinkle blood but instead poured it, what is the result?
A. The obligation is fulfilled, since pouring is a valid form of placement
B. The obligation is NOT fulfilled — sprinkling and pouring are entirely separate actions
C. The obligation is fulfilled only for a sin offering
D. The priest must repeat the service but no additional offering is needed
Correct: B — R' Akiva holds that sprinkling is not included in pouring, and pouring is not included in sprinkling. They are completely separate actions, and one cannot substitute for the other.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What determines whether the substitute of a Paschal offering is sacrificed as a peace offering?
A. Whether the owner designated it before or after Pesach night
B. Whether the substitution occurred before or after the Paschal offering was sacrificed
C. Whether the substitute animal has a blemish
D. Whether the owner recited the proper blessing
Correct: B — If substitution occurred AFTER the Pesach was sacrificed, the substitute has the status of a peace offering and is sacrificed. If BEFORE, 'It is the Lord's Passover' excludes the substitute from being sacrificed.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why is the blemished firstborn considered a unique halakha in the Torah?
A. It is the only offering that can be eaten outside Jerusalem
B. It is the only blemished offering given to priests for consumption rather than redeemed
C. It is the only offering that doesn't require blood placement
D. It is the only offering that both priests and non-priests may eat
Correct: B — Normally blemished offerings are redeemed and the money given to the Temple. The blemished firstborn is unique: it cannot be redeemed but is given directly to the priest to eat — a concept found nowhere else in the Torah.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
For tanna'im who cannot derive 'one placement suffices' from Deuteronomy 12:27, what is their alternative source?
A. They derive it from Numbers 18:17 about the firstborn
B. They derive it from Leviticus 5:9 about the bird sin offering
C. They follow Beit Hillel who says even a sin offering needs only one placement, and derive all others from that
D. They derive it from Exodus 12:27 about the Paschal offering
Correct: C — Beit Hillel rules that even a sin offering (which normally requires four placements) achieves atonement with just one placement after the fact. If the strictest case needs only one, certainly all other offerings do as well.
