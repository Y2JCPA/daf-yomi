QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the mishna list all three interpositions (vessel, animal, another person's feet) separately?
A. Each teaches a different disqualification penalty
B. Each refutes a potential mistaken leniency — vessel isn't flesh, animal isn't human, yet all interpose
C. Only vessels actually disqualify; the others are rabbinic
D. To show that the list is exhaustive — nothing else disqualifies
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that each case is necessary because without it, you might think the next category (closer to human flesh) would not interpose. The progression vessel → animal → person closes each logical gap.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Eliezer, when is a priest's service valid if one foot is on a vessel and one on the floor?
A. Always, since one foot touches the floor
B. Only if the vessel is a sacred vessel
C. Only if he could still stand on one foot after the vessel is removed
D. Never — both feet must be on the floor
Correct: C — R' Eliezer's test is practical: if the priest can still stand and serve on one foot after the vessel or stone is removed, the service is valid. The key is whether he is dependent on the interposing object.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the fundamental interpretive disagreement between the Rabbis and R' Shimon regarding 'with his finger and put it'?
A. Whether 'finger' means the index finger or any finger
B. Whether a verse's qualifying word modifies both the preceding and following text, or only the following text
C. Whether the verbal analogy from the leper passage applies
D. Whether 'finger' refers to the right or left hand
Correct: B — The Rabbis hold that 'finger' modifies both the collection (preceding) and the placing (following), while R' Shimon holds it modifies only the placing (following text). This is a dispute about the hermeneutical rule of juxtaposition.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does R' Elazar b'R' Shimon's position differ from both his father and the Rabbis?
A. He requires both hands for all rites
B. He holds 'finger' modifies only the immediately preceding phrase — not the following text or text further before it
C. He holds that the left hand is always valid
D. He derives everything from the leper passage alone
Correct: B — Abaye notes that R' Elazar b'R' Shimon uniquely reads 'finger' as modifying only the immediately preceding phrase. His father reads it as modifying only the following text, and the Rabbis read it as modifying both directions.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why doesn't R' Yochanan's rule (finger or priesthood = right hand) disqualify the left-hand conveyance of limbs to the ramp?
A. Conveyance of limbs is not performed by priests
B. The verse about conveyance doesn't mention priesthood
C. The rule applies only to rites that are indispensable for atonement, and conveyance is not
D. The left hand is used only for the right-side limb as a special exception
Correct: C — The Gemara limits R' Yochanan's rule to rites that are me'akvei kaparah — indispensable for achieving atonement. Conveying limbs to the ramp, while performed by priests, is not such a rite, so left-hand use does not disqualify it.
