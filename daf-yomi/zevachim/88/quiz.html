QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, what is unique about blood-collection cups compared to other liquid service vessels?
A. They sanctify both liquid and dry items
B. They sanctify items even when perforated
C. They sanctify items placed on their exterior
D. They sanctify items even without full measures
Correct: A — Shmuel distinguishes cups from liquid measures: cups sanctify dry items too, based on Numbers 7:13 where cups held flour for meal offerings.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, when does a service vessel sanctify a partial amount of an item?
A. When the vessel is brand new
B. When the priest intends to add more later
C. When the item is a liquid rather than dry
D. When the vessel is used in the Sanctuary rather than the courtyard
Correct: B — R' Yoḥanan teaches that if the priest initially intended to add more to the vessel, each partial amount placed inside becomes sanctified immediately, even before the full measure is reached.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which priestly garment atones for idol worship, and what is the scriptural basis?
A. The breastplate — 'breastplate of judgment'
B. The frontplate — 'upon Aaron's forehead'
C. The ephod — 'without ephod or teraphim' (Hosea 3:4)
D. The robe — because its bells ward off foreign worship
Correct: C — The ephod atones for idol worship. Hosea 3:4 links the absence of an ephod with the presence of teraphim (idols), implying that the ephod's presence prevents idol worship.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does R' Yehoshua ben Levi's teaching pose a challenge to R' Inini bar Sason's list of garment-based atonements?
A. R' Yehoshua ben Levi says vestments have no sanctity at all
B. He says bloodshed and malicious speech have no offering-based atonement — they are atoned by eglah arufah and incense respectively
C. He says only four garments effect atonement, not all eight
D. He says atonement requires both a garment and an offering together
Correct: B — R' Yehoshua ben Levi teaches that bloodshed and malicious speech lack offering-based atonement, being atoned instead by the eglah arufah and incense — creating tension with R' Inini's claim that the tunic and robe atone for these very sins.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How many bells were on the High Priest's robe according to the first opinion in the baraita, and how were they arranged?
A. 36 total — 18 per side, alternating with pomegranates
B. 72 total — 36 per side, alternating with pomegranates
C. 72 total — arranged in three rows of 24
D. 36 total — all on the front of the robe
Correct: B — The first opinion states 72 golden bells with 72 clappers, suspended on the skirts with 36 bells and 36 pomegranates on each side, alternating as stated in Exodus 39:26.
