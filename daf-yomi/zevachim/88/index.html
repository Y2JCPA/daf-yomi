Zevachim 88 — Vessels, Vestments & Atonement

מה קרבנות מכפרין, אף בגדי כהונה מכפרין
Core Theme This daf transitions from the laws of service vessels (klei shareis) — what they sanctify and under what conditions — to the profound teaching of Rabbi Inini bar Sason that each of the eight priestly vestments (bigdei kehunah) effects atonement for a specific sin, paralleling the atonement of offerings.

Service Vessels: Liquids vs. Dry Items

Liquid Measures

  • Sanctify liquids (wine, oil, blood)
  • Do NOT sanctify dry items
  • E.g., measuring cups for wine libations

Dry Measures

  • Sanctify dry items (flour, incense)
  • Do NOT sanctify liquids
  • E.g., measuring vessels for meal offerings

Blood Cups (Shmuel)

  • Sanctify both liquids AND dry items
  • Proof: Numbers 7:13 — cups held flour
  • Even dry flour not mixed with oil is sanctified
Shmuel's Distinction Liquid measures don't sanctify dry items, but blood-collection cups do — because the Torah shows cups used with flour.

Does Meal Offering Count as 'Dry'?

Shmuel
Numbers 7:13 proves cups sanctify dry items — 'both of them full of fine flour mingled with oil.'
Rav Aḥa of Difti
But meal offerings are mixed with oil — that makes them a liquid! You can't prove dry items from this.
Ravina
The verse teaches even the dry portions of flour that didn't absorb oil are sanctified by the cup.
Ravina
Alternatively: compared to blood, even a meal offering mixed with oil is considered a 'dry' item.
Resolution Either way, the verse proves cups sanctify dry items — whether referring to unmixed flour or to the relative dryness compared to blood.

When Do Service Vessels Sanctify?

Three Conditions (Shmuel)
↓
1. Whole
Vessel must be unperforated — if perforated, only sanctifies if still usable for its original purpose
2. Full Measure
Must contain a complete measurement fit for offering
3. From Within
Only contents inside the vessel are sanctified, not items touching the outside
↓
R' Yoḥanan's Exception: If the priest intended to add more, even a partial amount placed inside becomes sanctified immediately
Heaped Measures The two versions of Shmuel's statement differ on whether contents heaped above the rim are sanctified — 'from within' may exclude overflow.

The Eight Priestly Vestments & Their Atonement

GarmentAtones ForSource / Logic
Tunic (כתונת)BloodshedJoseph's brothers dipped the tunic in blood (Gen. 37:31)
Trousers (מכנסיים)Sexual immorality"To cover the flesh of their nakedness" (Ex. 28:42)
Mitre (מצנפת)ArroganceItem placed on high atones for an elevated heart (R' Ḥanina)
Belt (אבנט)Sinful thoughts of the heartWorn over the heart — atones for sins of that location
Breastplate (חושן)Improper judgmentsCalled "breastplate of judgment" (Ex. 28:15)
Ephod (אפוד)Idol worship"Without ephod or teraphim" (Hosea 3:4)
Robe (מעיל)Malicious speechItem producing sound (bells) atones for evil sound

R' Inini bar Sason Just as offerings effect atonement, so too priestly vestments effect atonement — derived from the juxtaposition of the passages in Leviticus.

Construction of the Robe's Bells & Pomegranates

🧵 The robe is sewn entirely of sky-blue wool (techelet)
↓
🍎 Pomegranates are fashioned from sky-blue, purple, and scarlet wool twined together — shaped like unripe pomegranates with closed crowns
↓
🔔 72 golden bells with 72 clappers are made (first opinion) — or 36 bells (R' Dosa/R' Yehuda)
↓
⚖️ Bells and pomegranates alternate: 36 of each per side (or 18 per R' Dosa)
↓
🔊 "A bell and a pomegranate, a bell and a pomegranate, upon the skirts of the robe around it" (Ex. 39:26)
Parallel Dispute R' Inini bar Sason notes: the same disagreement (36 vs. 72) exists regarding the total number of shades of leprous marks (nega'im).
