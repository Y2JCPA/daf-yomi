QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the proof from the daily tamid preceding the Shabbat mussaf?
A. Because the tamid is actually holier than the mussaf
B. Because Shabbat sanctity elevates both the tamid and the mussaf equally
C. Because the mussaf should actually come first
D. Because the rule of תדיר doesn't apply to Temple offerings
Correct: B — The Gemara argues that Shabbat sanctity affects not only the mussaf but also the daily offering brought on Shabbat, making them equal in sanctity — so the tamid's precedence proves nothing about frequency vs. sanctity.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rava's distinction between peace offerings and the תדיר/מקודש dilemma?
A. Peace offerings are holier than sin offerings
B. Peace offerings are merely common (מצוי), not obligatorily frequent (תדיר)
C. Peace offerings cannot be brought on Shabbat
D. Peace offerings are always sacrificed after sin offerings
Correct: B — Rava distinguishes between מצוי (something that happens to occur often, like voluntary peace offerings) and תדיר (an offering with a fixed, obligatory frequency). The dilemma of frequency vs. sanctity applies only to תדיר.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Shmuel, what happens to contributed oil per Rabbi Tarfon's view?
A. It is entirely poured into the basins near the altar
B. It is entirely burned on the altar like wine libations
C. A handful is burned on the altar and the remainder is eaten by priests
D. It is distributed to the priests without any being placed on the altar
Correct: C — Shmuel derives from the word קרבן in Leviticus 2:1 that contributed oil has the same law as a meal offering: the priest removes a handful (קומץ) to burn on the altar, and the remainder is eaten by the priests.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the practical difference between דון מינה ומינה and דון מינה ואוקי באתרה regarding contributed oil?
A. Whether oil can be contributed at all
B. Whether oil is burned or eaten
C. The minimum amount: 1 log (Rabbis) vs. 3 log (Rebbi)
D. Whether a non-priest may bring the oil
Correct: C — The Rabbis hold דון מינה ומינה — oil is equated to meal offerings in all aspects, requiring only 1 log. Rebbi holds דון מינה ואוקי באתרה — only the core law (that oil can be contributed) is derived from meal offerings, but the amount follows wine libations (3 log).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the contradiction between the two clauses of the mishna about oil in the Temple courtyard?
A. The first clause refers to weekdays and the second to Shabbat
B. The first clause discusses the remainder (eaten) and the last clause discusses the handful (burned)
C. Both clauses refer to the same oil in different stages of processing
D. The mishna is discussing two different types of oil
Correct: B — According to Rabbi Zeira (supported by the Gemara), the first clause about oil distributed in the courtyard refers to the remainder after the handful is removed (eaten by priests), while the last clause about oil on the altar refers to the handful itself (burned). This resolves the apparent contradiction.
