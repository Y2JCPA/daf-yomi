Zevachim 68: Pinching, Purity & Disqualification

Bird offering pinched by someone or in some way not fully valid
↓
Was the bird initially fit for sacrifice?
Yes — disqualified during service
Meat does NOT cause tum'ah in the throat
No — disqualified before service
Meat DOES cause tum'ah in the throat like neveilah
KEY PRINCIPLE The Mishna's rule: disqualification during Temple service prevents carcass-impurity; disqualification before service does not.

Rabbi Yehoshua's Parable: The Sheep's Seven Sounds

🐑 A living sheep makes one sound — its voice
↓
📯 Two horns → two trumpets
↓
🎵 Two shinbones → two flutes
↓
🥁 Skin → a drumhead
↓
🎸 Large intestines → harp strings
↓
🎻 Small intestines → lyre strings
↓
🐦 Similarly: one uncertain sacrifice → seven replacement birds
MASHAL Rabbi Yehoshua uses this vivid parable to explain why uncertainty about the original sacrifice multiplies the woman's obligation from one bird to seven.

Rav vs. R' Yochanan: Non-Priest Pinching

Rav
Left hand and nighttime pinching — meat does NOT cause tum'ah.
Rav
But a non-priest pinching or pinching with a knife — meat DOES cause tum'ah.
R' Yochanan
A non-priest pinching — meat does NOT cause tum'ah.
R' Yochanan
But a priest pinching with a knife — meat DOES cause tum'ah.
Gemara
Rav's logic: left hand has validity on Yom Kippur; night has validity in burning fats — so these don't cause tum'ah.
Gemara
Challenge: Non-priests also have validity — they may slaughter animal offerings! Why does Rav rule differently?
Rav
Slaughter is not a full-fledged sacrificial rite (avodah), so it cannot validate non-priest pinching.
CORE DISPUTE Rav holds non-priest pinching is fundamentally invalid (creates neveilah); R' Yochanan holds it merely disqualifies the offering but doesn't create carcass-impurity.

Is Slaughter a Full Sacrificial Rite (Avodah)?

Challenge to Rav: Non-priests can slaughter offerings — doesn't that show they have a role in avodah?
↓
R' Zeira: Slaughter of red heifer (parah adumah) by non-priest is invalid — implying slaughter IS avodah!
↓
Rav Sheisha b. Rav Idi: Red heifer is not a sacrifice — it's kodshei bedek habayit (Temple maintenance). Its rules don't apply to offerings.
↓
Analogy: Like examining leprous marks — requires a priest but isn't Temple service
RESOLUTION The red heifer requiring priestly slaughter doesn't prove slaughter is avodah — it's a separate priestly requirement unrelated to sacrificial rites.

Private Altar (Bamah) vs. Temple Service

Private Altar (Bamah)

  • Non-priests may pinch bird offerings
  • Less stringent requirements
  • Valid before the Temple was built
  • Items that 'emerged' still valid for sacrifice

Temple (Beit HaMikdash)

  • Only priests may pinch bird offerings
  • Strict requirements for all avodah
  • Higher level of sanctity
  • Cannot derive Temple laws from bamah laws
GEMARA'S ANSWER One cannot derive Temple service laws from private altar laws. The baraita citing bamah for 'items that ascended shall not descend' actually relies on the verse "This is the law of the burnt offering" (Lev. 6:2) — the bamah is merely supportive, not the source.
