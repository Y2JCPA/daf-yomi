QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara conclude that all three camps must have existed in Shiloh?
A. Because the Torah explicitly states that Shiloh had three camps
B. Because without all three, the distinct sending-out rules for different impurities would collapse
C. Because the High Priest required all three camps to perform the Yom Kippur service
D. Because the Levites needed their own separate camp for musical duties
Correct: B — The Gemara proves that without a Levite camp, zavim and temei meitim would share one zone, and without an Israelite camp, zavim and metzora'im would share one zone — both contradicting Torah verses that require distinct areas for each.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What did the Levite camp provide in the wilderness that it did NOT provide in Shiloh?
A. A place for the Sanhedrin to convene
B. Refuge for unintentional killers
C. Storage for communal offerings
D. Quarters for non-Levite priests
Correct: B — The Gemara resolves that the teaching 'there were only two camps in Shiloh' means the Levite camp did not serve as a city of refuge there, though it did in the wilderness, as derived from Exodus 21:13.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Meir, why could nazirite offerings be sacrificed on a private altar in Gilgal?
A. Because nazirite offerings are considered the most holy category
B. Because a nazirite's vow is voluntary, making his offerings 'fitting' (yesharot)
C. Because the Torah explicitly permits nazirite offerings on private altars
D. Because nazirite offerings do not require priestly participation
Correct: B — R' Meir holds that since no one is compelled to become a nazirite, the resulting offerings are considered voluntary/fitting (yesharot) — derived from 'every man whatsoever is fitting (hayashar) in his own eyes' (Deut. 12:8).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What was Rabba's objection to Shmuel's initial formulation about the R' Meir/Rabbis dispute?
A. A baraita contradicts Shmuel by listing nazirite sin offerings on private altars
B. A baraita omits the nazirite's cooked foreleg from private altar rules, proving the dispute extends to peace offerings
C. Shmuel's reading contradicts the plain meaning of Deuteronomy 12:8
D. The Rabbis never mention nazirite offerings at all in the original baraita
Correct: B — Rabba noted that a baraita lists breast-and-thigh and terumah of loaves as applying only to the great altar, but omits the nazirite's foreleg — this only makes sense if the Rabbis hold the nazirite's peace offering was NOT sacrificed on a private altar, proving the dispute covers burnt and peace offerings, not sin and guilt offerings.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How do the Rabbis (in the dispute with R' Yehuda) distinguish between public and individual offerings in Gilgal?
A. The public could bring any offering on the great altar; an individual could only bring olot and shelamim even on the great altar
B. Both public and individual could bring any offering, but only on the great altar
C. The public was limited to Paschal offerings; individuals had no restrictions
D. Individuals could bring compulsory offerings on private altars but not on the great altar
Correct: A — The Rabbis derive from 'every MAN whatsoever is fitting' that only an individual (man) is limited to voluntary offerings (olot and shelamim), even at the great public altar. The public, however, could sacrifice compulsory offerings at the Tabernacle in Gilgal.
