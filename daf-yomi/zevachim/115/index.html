Zevachim 115: Slaughter Outside & Pre-Tabernacle Service

Can one be liable for slaughtering an offering outside the courtyard when the offering's time has not arrived?
↓
Sin Offering
Not for its sake → disqualified inside → exempt outside
Guilt Offering (R' Ḥilkiya)
Not for its sake → fit inside → liable outside
↓
The daf then shifts to pre-Tabernacle era: private altars permitted, firstborn served, all offerings were olot
Key Principle Liability for slaughter outside depends on whether the offering would be fit if brought inside the courtyard

Guilt Offering Not at Its Time — Outside the Courtyard

R' Ḥilkiya
One who slaughters a guilt offering whose time has not arrived, outside the courtyard, not for its sake — is liable, because if slaughtered inside not for its sake it would be fit.
Gemara
Can we find a baraita to support this? The baraita lists burnt offerings and guilt offerings whose time hasn't arrived, but omits sin offerings…
Gemara
The omission of the sin offering implies it is exempt — because a sin offering slaughtered not for its sake is disqualified. The guilt offering's inclusion implies liability.
Challenger
Perhaps the baraita follows R' Eliezer, who equates guilt and sin offerings — and discusses slaughter at its proper time, not before!
Core Dispute R' Ḥilkiya holds that a guilt offering not for its sake remains fit, so slaughtering it outside creates liability — unlike a sin offering

Sin Offering vs. Guilt Offering — Not for Its Sake

Sin Offering (חטאת)

  • Slaughtered not for its sake → DISQUALIFIED
  • R' Eliezer derives: guilt offering is the same
  • If disqualified inside → exempt for slaughter outside
  • Source: Leviticus 7:7 — 'As the sin offering, so the guilt offering'

Guilt Offering (אשם)

  • Majority view: not for its sake → still FIT
  • R' Ḥilkiya: therefore LIABLE if slaughtered outside
  • R' Eliezer disagrees: juxtaposes with sin offering
  • Baraita derives from Lev. 17:3 — 'ox, lamb, goat' = all cases
R' Eliezer's Principle כחטאת כאשם — 'As the sin offering, so the guilt offering' (Lev. 7:7). R' Eliezer applies this to disqualify a guilt offering slaughtered not for its sake.

Deriving Liability for Slaughter Outside (Lev. 17:3–4)

Leviticus 17:3 — 'שור או כשב או עז'
'An ox, or lamb, or goat'
↓
Each word teaches: in any case of that animal — even if its time hasn't arrived
↓
Leviticus 17:4 — 'ואל פתח אהל מועד לא הביאו'
'Has not brought it to the Tent of Meeting'
↓
Fit to be brought inside
Liable for slaughter outside
Not fit to be brought inside
Exempt from liability
Verse Logic The inclusive terms ('ox, lamb, goat') expand liability, while 'not brought to the Tent of Meeting' limits it to offerings that are actually fit for the altar

Before the Tabernacle Was Established

🏕️ Private altars (במות) were permitted
↓
👶 Sacrificial service performed by the firstborn (בכורות)
↓
🐄 All kosher animals fit: domesticated, wild, bird; male & female; blemished & unblemished
↓
🔥 All offerings were brought as burnt offerings (עולות)
↓
🔪 Burnt offering required flaying (הפשט) and cutting (ניתוח)
↓
🌍 Even today, gentiles may sacrifice on private altars
Baraita This baraita preserves the rules of the pre-Tabernacle era — a time when the sacrificial system operated without a centralized sanctuary
