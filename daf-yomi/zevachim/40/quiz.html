Zevachim 40 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, where are the seven indispensable placements found?
A. Bull of Yom Kippur and goat of Yom Kippur
B. Red heifer and leper purification
C. Anointed priest's bull and communal bull
D. Daily tamid offering and Musaf offering
Correct: B — Rav Pappa identifies the red heifer (parah adumah) and the purification of one afflicted with leprous marks (metzora) as the cases where seven sprinklings are indispensable.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Shimon need a special derivation ('so shall he do') for the four placements on the inner altar?
A. Because four placements are never mentioned explicitly
B. Because he derives only two corners from the verse, and two more via juxtaposition
C. Because he doesn't believe the inner altar requires blood at all
D. Because the four placements only apply on Yom Kippur
Correct: B — R' Shimon holds that the plural 'karnot' (corners) in each verse yields only two corners, totaling four by juxtaposition. Since two are not written explicitly, 'so shall he do' is needed to make all four indispensable.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rabbi Shimon derive from the phrase 'in the Tent of Meeting'?
A. Blood must be placed on all four corners
B. The bull of Yom Kippur requires semicha
C. If the roof of the Sanctuary was breached, the priest may not sprinkle
D. The blood must be sufficient for immersion
Correct: C — R' Shimon uses 'in the Tent of Meeting' to teach that a breached roof disqualifies the Sanctuary — it is no longer a 'tent,' and sprinkling is invalid.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Torah need BOTH 'in the blood' and 'and the priest shall immerse' regarding the blood in the vessel?
A. One refers to the bull's blood and one to the goat's blood
B. 'In the blood' teaches sufficient measure from the outset; 'immerse' teaches enough must remain for actual immersion
C. One is for the inner altar and one is for the outer altar
D. They refer to two different priests performing the service
Correct: B — Without 'in the blood,' we might allow adding blood mid-service. Without 'and immerse,' we might allow wiping the vessel sides instead of true immersion. Both are needed.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yishmael argue that the halakhot of 'et, in the blood, and immersion' apply to the bull of Yom Kippur without a special verse?
A. He derives it from the word 'statute' written about Yom Kippur
B. He says it is a tradition from Sinai (halakha l'Moshe mi'Sinai)
C. He uses a kal va'chomer: if different species share blood rites, surely the same species does
D. He derives it from the phrase 'in the Tent of Meeting'
Correct: C — R' Yishmael reasons: if two different animal types (e.g., communal bull and Yom Kippur goat) share blood rites, then certainly two offerings of the same type (bull and bull) should share them — a classic a fortiori inference.
