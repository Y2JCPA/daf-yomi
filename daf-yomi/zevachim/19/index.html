Zevachim 19 — Interpositions & Sanctification

על בשרו — Upon his body (Vayikra 6:3)
CORE THEME This daf explores what constitutes an interposition (chatzitzah) between a priest and his vestments or sacred vessels, then pivots to the laws of sanctifying hands and feet (kiddush yadayim v'raglayim) — when it is indispensable and how it is performed.

Priestly Dignity: Huna bar Natan & King Izgadar

ממלכת כהנים וגוי קדוש
AGGADIC INTERLUDE Huna bar Natan stood before Izgadar, King of Persia, whose belt had risen too high. The king lowered it and quoted: "A kingdom of priests, and a holy nation" (Shemot 19:6). Ameimar said: This fulfilled "Kings shall be your foster fathers" (Yeshayahu 49:23).

Reed vs. Sash on a Wounded Finger

Mishna (Eiruvin 103b)
A priest injured on his finger on Shabbat may wrap it with a reed during Temple service
R' Yehuda b'R Chiyya
Only a reed is permitted — a sash (tziltzul) counts as an extra garment and is forbidden
R' Yochanan
Extra garments are only prohibited where vestments are worn. On the hand, a sash is not an extra garment
Gemara
But shouldn't the sash interpose between the priest's hand and the sacred vessel?
R' Yochanan
The wound is on the left hand (service uses right), or on a part of the right hand not used for service
KEY DISPUTE Is an extra item on the priest problematic as an extra garment, as an interposition, or both?

Interposition Rules: Rava / Rav Chisda

Location on BodyItem SizeInterposes?
Where vestments ARE wornEven one extra thread✅ YES — interposes
Where vestments NOT worn≥ 3×3 fingerbreadths✅ YES — interposes
Where vestments NOT worn< 3×3 fingerbreadths❌ NO — does not interpose
Exception: Sash (tziltzul)Even < 3×3✅ YES — because it is significant
R' YOCHANAN DISAGREES R' Yochanan holds that any fabric in a place where vestments are not worn does not interpose, regardless of size

Sanctification of Hands & Feet: When Is It Indispensable?

Morning Sanctification

  • Required for ALL priests — High Priest and ordinary
  • If omitted entirely, service is DISQUALIFIED
  • Derived by gezeirah shavah of 'statute' from vestments

Yom Kippur: Between Garment Changes

  • 5 immersions, 10 sanctifications by Torah law
  • If omitted, service is VALID after the fact
  • 'And put them on' (Vayikra 16:4) — only vestments are indispensable
SOURCE Chizkiyya: Comparing Aaron to his sons — what is indispensable for ordinary priests is indispensable for the High Priest, and vice versa
