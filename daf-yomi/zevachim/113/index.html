Did the Flood Descend on Eretz Yisrael?

Reish Lakish
The flood descended upon Eretz Yisrael. Therefore, the site where the parah adumah is burned must be inspected for graves of flood victims.
R' Yochanan
The flood did not descend on Eretz Yisrael. All of Eretz Yisrael is already inspected — 'outside its pit' means slaughtered inside the walls of Jerusalem.
Reish Lakish
'All on the dry land died' (Bereishit 7:22) — this includes Eretz Yisrael! If no flood there, why did they die?
R' Yochanan
They died from the heat that accompanied the floodwaters, which spread even to Eretz Yisrael — but the water itself did not fall there.
Core Dispute This machloket affects whether the red heifer's burning site needs special inspection for corpse-impurity from flood victims.

Private Altar (Bamah) vs. Temple Altar

Not Required at Private Altar

  • No blood placement around all sides
  • No waving of meal offerings
  • No priestly vestments required
  • No service vessels required
  • No washing of hands and feet
  • No red line partition for blood
  • Non-priests may serve

Equal at Both Altars

  • Piggul — improper intent renders offering invalid
  • Notar — leftover portions beyond permitted time
  • Prohibition of eating kodshim while tamei
R' Yehuda Adds There is no meal offering sacrificed on a private altar at all.

What Does 'Outside Its Pit' Mean?

Reish Lakish
'Outside its pit' means outside the place that was inspected to ensure it is not a gravesite (which would cause tumah).
R' Yochanan
All of Eretz Yisrael is already inspected! Rather, it means the kohen slaughtered the parah adumah inside the walls of Jerusalem — not outside as the Torah requires.
Gemara
Why doesn't R' Yochanan say it means not opposite the entrance to the Temple? He holds that's also pasul!
Gemara
R' Yochanan uses 'lo tzmikha' style: It's obvious for 'not opposite the entrance' — the chiddush is that even inside the walls (closer to the Temple) is also invalid.
Key Verse "And it shall be brought outside the camp, and it shall be slaughtered before him" (Bamidbar 19:3)

Red Heifer: Location Requirements

Parah Adumah must be slaughtered outside the walls of Jerusalem
↓
Must it be opposite the entrance to the Temple?
R' Yochanan
Yes — Slaughter is juxtaposed with sprinkling: 'and it shall be slaughtered… and sprinkle toward the Tent of Meeting'
Reish Lakish
No — 'Outside the camp and it shall be slaughtered' means any location outside the camp suffices
↓
Must the burning also be opposite the entrance?
R' Yochanan
Yes — Burning is juxtaposed with sprinkling in adjacent verses
R' Oshaya
No — 'With its dung (pirshah) shall be burned' — where its soul departs (poreshet), there it is burned
Hekesh Principle R' Yochanan derives location requirements through juxtaposition (hekesh) of slaughter, sprinkling, and burning in Bamidbar 19:3-5.

The Boiling Waters of the Flood

דור המבול — בחמין קלקלו ובחמין נידונו
Rav Chisda's Teaching The generation of the flood sinned with boiling heat (forbidden relations) and were punished with boiling heat (scalding floodwaters).

Proof: The word שָׁכָכוּ (vayashoku) used for the calming of the waters (Bereishit 8:1) parallels שָׁכָכָה (shakhakha) — the assuaging of King Achashverosh's boiling anger (Esther 7:10). Both describe heat being cooled down.
