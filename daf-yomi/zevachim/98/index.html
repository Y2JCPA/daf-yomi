The Master Verse: What Each Offering Teaches

Leviticus 7:37
"This is the law of the burnt offering, meal offering, sin offering, guilt offering, inauguration offering, and peace offering"
↓
Each offering in the verse teaches a universal principle applied to all offerings
↓
Meal Offering
Absorption sanctifies what it touches
Sin Offering
Non-sacred animals, daytime, right hand
Guilt Offering
Fetal sac & placenta not sacred
↓
Inauguration
Leftovers incinerated; no live animals burned
Peace Offering
Components can render piggul
KEY CONCEPT One verse unifies all offering types — each named offering contributes a unique halakhic principle that applies universally.

Why Both Meal & Sin Offering for Absorption?

Gemara
Why does the Torah state the absorption rule for both meal offering and sin offering?
R' Akiva
If only meal offering — I'd say it absorbs because it's soft, but meat would not absorb.
R' Akiva
If only sin offering — I'd say it absorbs because of its fattiness, but dry meal would not.
Gemara
Therefore both are necessary — each covers a case the other cannot.
TALMUDIC METHOD This is a classic tzrikha (צריכא) — explaining why two seemingly redundant teachings are both necessary.

Rava's Dilemma: Layered Blood on a Garment

Blood of a sin offering on a garment requires laundering (כיבוס)
↓
What if two layers of blood are on the garment?
↓
Sin Below, Burnt Above
Sin offering blood already absorbed → garment requires laundering
Burnt Below, Sin Above
Sin offering blood touches but doesn't absorb (garment already saturated)
↓
Rava resolves: When sin offering blood is on top and cannot absorb → no laundering required
RAVA'S RULING The obligation to launder depends on absorption into the garment, not mere contact. If the garment already absorbed other blood, the sin offering blood cannot penetrate.

Immersion & Interposition: Stains That Block Tevilah

Blood Stain

  • Regular person → blood interposes, immersion invalid
  • Butcher → blood stain is normal, does NOT interpose
  • Key: one is not makpid (particular) about usual stains

Fat Stain (רבב)

  • Regular person → fat stain interposes, immersion invalid
  • Fat seller → fat stain is normal, does NOT interpose
  • Same principle of being accustomed to the substance

Both Stains Together?

  • A butcher AND fat seller has BOTH stains
  • Is he particular about two stains together?
  • Or not particular since each is normal for him?
  • TEIKU — Unresolved
PRINCIPLE Interposition (חציצה) depends on whether the person is makpid — particular about the substance. A stain one is accustomed to does not invalidate immersion.

Guilt Offering: Deriving the Possible from the Impossible

🐑 A guilt offering (אשם) is always a male animal
↓
🚫 A male animal can never have a fetal sac or placenta
↓
📜 The verse teaches: for guilt offerings, fetal sac/placenta are not sacred
↓
🔄 This is extended to all offerings — even female ones
↓
⚖️ This Tanna holds offspring are sanctified only at birth, not in utero
↓
💡 And one may derive the possible (female case) from the impossible (male case)
דבר שאינו יכול A remarkable hermeneutical principle: a law stated about an impossible scenario (male animal with fetal sac) can still teach us about real scenarios (female animals).
