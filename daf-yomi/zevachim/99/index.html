Who Gets a Share of Sacrificial Meat?

Leviticus 6:19
"The priest who effects atonement shall eat it"
↓
Does this mean only the priest who actually performed the service?
Too narrow
All priests of the mishmar (watch) eat — not just the one who served
Reish Lakish's reading
Any priest fit for atonement receives a share
↓
"Shall eat it" = shall receive a share
Fit for partaking → gets a share
Unfit for partaking → no share
KEY PRINCIPLE A priest must be fit to eat sacrificial meat at the time of service in order to receive a share of it.

Deriving the Rule: Who Gets a Share?

Reish Lakish
"The priest who effects atonement shall eat it" (Lev 6:19) — only a priest fit for atonement partakes.
Gemara
But the whole mishmar (priestly watch) eats, even though only one priest sprinkled the blood!
Reish Lakish
It means any priest fit for service — not necessarily the one who performed it.
Gemara
But a minor eats sacrificial meat and he's unfit for service!
Reish Lakish
"Shall eat it" means shall receive a share. Minors eat what others give them — they don't get their own portion.
VERSE The word יאכלנה ("shall eat it") is reinterpreted as entitlement to a share, not merely permission to eat.

The Blemished Priest Exception

A blemished priest (בעל מום) is unfit for service
↓
Should he be excluded from shares?
Without the verse
He would be excluded — he can't perform atonement
Lev 6:22
"Every male among the priests shall eat it" — includes blemished priests
↓
Why include blemished over tevul yom (immersed that day)?
Blemished priest
Can eat kodshim even though he can't serve — closer fit
Tevul yom
Cannot eat kodshim at all right now — too removed
R' YOSEF'S READING "Shall eat it" teaches: only one fit to eat gets a share. A blemished priest can eat; a tevul yom cannot.

Reish Lakish's Dilemma: Blemished AND Impure

Maybe He Gets a Share

  • A blemished priest is already unfit for service
  • The Torah included him despite unfitness
  • Adding impurity shouldn't matter — he's already excluded from service
  • What difference does it make why he can't serve?

Maybe He Does Not

  • The principle: must be fit to EAT to receive a share
  • An impure priest cannot eat kodshim
  • Blemished priest was included because he CAN eat
  • Impurity removes the basis for the exception
RESOLUTION (Rabba) From the baraita about the Kohen Gadol as onen: he sacrifices but does not eat and does not receive a share for evening — proving fitness to eat is required.

The Acute Mourner (Onen): Touching vs. Eating

Contradiction
Our mishna: An onen may touch sacrificial meat.
Chagiga: An onen must immerse — implying he's impure!
R' Abba bar Memel
R' Yehuda HaNasi teaches: one who eats teruma with 3rd-degree impurity may not eat teruma but may touch it.
R' Abba bar Memel
Similarly, the Sages were stricter about eating (requiring immersion) but lenient about touching.
Gemara
The mishna says an onen doesn't get a share — but if others invite him, he may eat in the evening.
Contradiction
But Pesachim 91b says an onen may eat the Paschal offering but NOT other kodshim!
DISTINCTION Touching kodshim and eating kodshim have different standards of purity for an onen — the Sages imposed a higher bar for consumption.
