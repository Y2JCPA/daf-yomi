QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Reish Lakish, what does "the priest who effects atonement shall eat it" teach?
A. Only the priest who sprinkled the blood may eat the meat
B. Any priest fit for performing the Temple service may receive a share
C. All priests of that day's watch must eat the meat
D. Only the Kohen Gadol receives a share of sin offerings
Correct: B — Reish Lakish derives that any priest who is FIT for service (not necessarily the one who performed it) is entitled to a share — since the entire priestly watch partakes.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Torah's phrase "every male" include a blemished priest rather than a tevul yom (one who immersed that day)?
A. A blemished priest can eat kodshim even though he can't serve; a tevul yom cannot eat yet
B. Blemished priests are more common than tevul yom
C. A tevul yom will be fit by evening so he doesn't need inclusion
D. The blemished priest has higher sanctity than a tevul yom
Correct: A — The Gemara reasons: it makes sense to include one who CAN eat (blemished) over one who CANNOT eat (tevul yom), since receiving a share is about fitness to partake.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve Reish Lakish's dilemma about a priest who is both blemished AND impure?
A. He receives a share because the Torah already included blemished priests
B. He receives half a share as a compromise
C. He does NOT receive a share — proven from the onen Kohen Gadol who serves but doesn't get a share
D. The dilemma is left unresolved (teiku)
Correct: C — Rabba brings proof from the Kohen Gadol as an acute mourner: he sacrifices but cannot eat and receives no share — proving that fitness to EAT is required for receiving a share.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does R' Abba bar Memel resolve the contradiction about whether an acute mourner may handle sacrificial meat?
A. The two mishnayot refer to different types of sacrifices
B. The Sages imposed a stricter standard for EATING than for TOUCHING
C. One mishna follows R' Yehuda and the other follows R' Shimon
D. An onen may touch meat only if he immersed first
Correct: B — Just as one who eats teruma with third-degree impurity may touch but not eat teruma, the Sages required immersion for an onen to EAT but not to TOUCH sacrificial meat.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Asi, why can an acute mourner sometimes eat the Paschal offering but not other kodshim?
A. The Paschal offering has a lower level of sanctity
B. It depends on whether death and burial were on the same day — if not, nighttime aninut doesn't apply
C. An onen always eats the Paschal offering regardless of circumstances
D. The Paschal offering is a communal obligation that overrides mourning
Correct: B — Rav Asi distinguishes: when death is on the 13th and burial on the 14th, day-of-burial alone does not extend aninut into the night (per R' Shimon, nighttime aninut is rabbinic), so the mourner may eat that evening.
