When Are Mixtures Nullified?

Blood or substance is mixed with another
↓
Is it min b'mino (same type)?
Same Type
Follow the majority (רוב)
Different Type
Follow the flavor (נותן טעם)
Core Principle This daf establishes the fundamental distinction between min b'mino (same-type mixtures) governed by majority, and min b'she'eino mino (different-type mixtures) governed by flavor.

Blood Mixed with Blood — The Mishna's Cases

Tanna Kamma
If water mixed with blood and it still appears like blood — it is fit for presentation.
R' Yehuda
Blood does not nullify blood (דם אינו מבטל דם). Therefore, present the mixed blood on the altar.
Tanna Kamma
If fit blood mixed with unfit blood (פסולין) — the entire mixture is poured into the drain.
R' Eliezer
Even blood mixed with dam hatkaza (exudate blood) — I deem it fit for presentation.
Tanna Kamma
Even so: if the priest already presented the mixed blood without consulting — the offering is fit (בדיעבד).
Key Distinction R' Yehuda holds same-type substances (blood + blood) cannot nullify each other. The Tanna Kamma focuses on appearance (מראה) as the determining factor.

R' Yochanan: Order of Mixing Matters

Blood and water are mixed
↓
Which fell into which?
Water into Blood
If appearance is blood → Fit ✅
Blood into Water
Each drop nullified on arrival → Unfit ❌
↓
Rav Pappa's Exception: For כיסוי הדם (covering the blood), even blood into water — if it looks like blood, the mitzvah applies
R' Yochanan (via R' Chiyya bar Abba) Each drop of blood falling into water is nullified individually. But Rav Pappa holds: אין דחייה אצל מצוות — there is no permanent rejection regarding mitzvot.

Reish Lakish: Three Meats Mixed Together

Meat TypeProhibitionMinimum Quantity
Piggul (פיגול)Intent to eat beyond designated timeOlive-bulk (כזית)
Notar (נותר)Leftover past its permitted timeOlive-bulk (כזית)
Tamei (טמא)Ritually impure sacrificial meatOlive-bulk (כזית)
Ruling If one eats all three mixed together — exempt from lashes. Each type is less than a majority, and it's impossible that one type doesn't overpower another. Since we can't identify which prohibition applies, the forewarning (התראה) is uncertain and invalid.
6 / 9

Three Conclusions from Reish Lakish

1. Prohibitions Nullify

  • Prohibited items nullify each other in a majority
  • Just as permitted items nullify prohibited ones
  • Each meat is nullified by the other two combined

2. נותן טעם Is Rabbinic

  • The principle that flavor imparting prohibits is not Torah law
  • If it were Torah law, he should be liable — the different meats impart flavor to each other
  • Since exempt → flavor rule is only rabbinic

3. Uncertain Forewarning

  • התראת ספק — uncertain forewarning is not a valid forewarning
  • Witnesses can't specify which prohibition will be transgressed
  • Therefore: no lashes can be administered
Analytical Note These three principles are derived from a single statement of Reish Lakish — a classic Talmudic move of extracting multiple legal principles from one ruling.

Rava's Challenge: The Wheat-Rice Dough

Rava
Objection! A dough of wheat and rice: if it tastes like wheat, it's obligated in challah — even if rice is the majority!
Gemara
Perhaps the challah obligation there is only rabbinic (מדרבנן)?
Rava
But the next clause says one fulfills the mitzvah of matzah on Pesach with this dough — that must be Torah law!
Gemara
We must distinguish: min b'she'eino mino (different types like wheat/rice) → follow flavor. Min b'mino (same type like piggul/notar meat) → follow majority.
Resolution Rava's challenge forces a key distinction: Reish Lakish's rule of majority applies only to same-type mixtures. Different-type mixtures are governed by taste (נותן טעם), which is Torah law in that context.
