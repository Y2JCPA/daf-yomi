If It Ascended the Altar — Does It Descend?

Disqualified offering placed on the mizbe'ach
↓
Disqualified in Sanctity
Shall not descend
e.g. night slaughter, spilled blood, leftover (notar)
Not Disqualified in Sanctity
Shall descend
e.g. bestiality, blemished (major), wrong gender
↓
Exception: R' Akiva — small blemishes (cornea) shall not descend
KEY RULE The core principle of Daf 85: items whose disqualification occurred within sanctity (b'kodesh) remain on the altar; those disqualified before sanctity must be removed.

Sacrificial Portions Before Blood Sprinkling

Ulla
Sacrificial portions of kodshim kalim (lesser sanctity) offered before zerikat ha-dam (blood sprinkling) — they shall not descend. They have become the bread of the altar (lechem ha-mizbe'ach).
R' Zeira
I can prove this from the mishna: even when blood was spilled entirely — if portions ascended they don't descend. Kal va-chomer: here the blood is still intact and could be sprinkled!
Gemara
No — the mishna may refer only to kodshei kodashim (most sacred offerings), whose portions are already sanctified for the altar before blood sprinkling. Kodshim kalim may be entirely unfit before zerikah.
CORE DISPUTE Are sacrificial portions of lesser-sanctity offerings considered 'bread of the altar' even before their blood is sprinkled?

Two Versions of R' Yochanan's Dilemma

Version 1 (R' Chiyya bar Abba)

  • R' Yochanan's dilemma: Do lesser sanctity portions offered before zerikah descend?
  • R' Ami: You should also ask about me'ilah (misuse)!
  • R' Yochanan: Me'ilah depends on zerikah, not ascent — no dilemma there
  • Resolution: They shall NOT descend, and NOT subject to me'ilah

Version 2 (Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak)

  • R' Yochanan's dilemma: Are these portions subject to me'ilah?
  • R' Ami: You should also ask about descent!
  • R' Yochanan: Descent is no dilemma — they are bread of the altar
  • Resolution: They shall NOT descend, and NOT subject to me'ilah
NOTE Both versions reach the same conclusion — the portions stay on the altar and are exempt from me'ilah — but disagree about which point R' Yochanan found obvious vs. uncertain.

R' Akiva on Blemished Animals (Mum)

A blemished animal ascends the altar
↓
Major Blemish
All agree: shall descend
Minor Blemish (e.g. cornea)
R' Akiva: shall not descend
Since such blemishes are kosher for bird offerings
↓
R' Yochanan's conditions for R' Akiva:
↓
Consecration before blemish ✓
Animal was once fit → shall not descend
Blemish before consecration ✗
Never truly fit → shall descend
EXCEPTION R' Akiva concedes a female burnt offering (olah) must descend — only males are valid, so it's like a pre-existing blemish.

Is Bestiality a Disqualification for Birds?

R' Yirmiya
The Torah equates rove'a (active bestiality) and nirba (passive bestiality). Since birds cannot be the active party, perhaps neither disqualification applies to birds?
R' Yirmiya
Or perhaps nirba still applies to birds — a sin was committed with the bird regardless?
Rabba
Proof from the mishna: R' Akiva deems fit blemished animals because such blemishes are kosher for birds. But he does not deem fit animals of bestiality — implying birds of bestiality are also disqualified!
Rav Nachman b. Yitzchak
A baraita explicitly lists a bird that was the object of bestiality among disqualified bird offerings. Proof confirmed.
CONCLUSION Bestiality disqualifies birds as well — even though birds cannot be the active party, the passive disqualification (nirba) still applies.
