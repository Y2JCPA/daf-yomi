QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Ulla, what is the status of sacrificial portions of lesser sanctity offerings placed on the altar before blood sprinkling?
A. They shall not descend — they are 'bread of the altar'
B. They shall descend — they are not yet sanctified
C. They remain but require blood sprinkling afterward
D. They are subject to me'ilah and must be removed
Correct: A — Ulla rules that once these portions ascend the altar, they become 'lechem ha-mizbe'ach' (bread of the altar) and shall not descend, even though their blood has not yet been sprinkled.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What conditions does R' Yochanan set for R' Akiva's rule that blemished animals shall not descend?
A. Any blemish, as long as the animal was consecrated
B. Only minor blemishes (like cornea), and consecration must precede the blemish
C. Only major blemishes that developed after consecration
D. Any blemish on a male animal only
Correct: B — R' Yochanan limits R' Akiva's leniency to small blemishes (like on the cornea, which are kosher for bird offerings), and only when the animal was consecrated before the blemish appeared — meaning it was once fully fit for the altar.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve R' Yirmiya's dilemma about whether bestiality disqualifies birds?
A. Birds are exempt since they cannot be the active party
B. Only domesticated birds are disqualified
C. Bestiality does disqualify birds — proven from the mishna and a baraita
D. The dilemma is left unresolved (teiku)
Correct: C — Rabba proves from the mishna that R' Akiva does not deem fit animals of bestiality despite deeming fit blemished animals (which are kosher for birds). Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak brings a baraita explicitly listing birds of bestiality as disqualified.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In both versions of R' Yochanan's dilemma, what is the agreed conclusion about lesser sanctity portions offered before zerikah?
A. They descend and are subject to me'ilah
B. They stay on the altar but are subject to me'ilah
C. They descend but are not subject to me'ilah
D. They shall not descend and are not subject to me'ilah
Correct: D — Both versions — whether R' Yochanan's doubt was about descent or me'ilah — reach the same conclusion: the portions shall not descend from the altar (they are bread of the altar), and they are not subject to the prohibition of me'ilah (misuse of consecrated property).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does R' Akiva concede that a female burnt offering (olah) must descend from the altar?
A. Female animals are never sanctified
B. It is comparable to a blemish preceding consecration — the animal was never fit as an olah
C. The Torah explicitly states female olahs must descend
D. Female animals are considered to have major blemishes
Correct: B — Since a burnt offering (olah) must be male, a female animal was never fit for this purpose. R' Akiva's leniency only applies when the animal was once genuinely fit — a female olah is like a case where the disqualification preceded consecration.
