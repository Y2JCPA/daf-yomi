Mixed Offering Blood: How Many Placements?

R' Eliezer
When blood requiring four placements is mixed with blood requiring one, place four times.
R' Yehoshua
Place only one time — one placement suffices bedi'avad (after the fact).
R' Eliezer
But you violate Bal Tigra — 'Do not diminish' from the required four!
R' Yehoshua
And you violate Bal Tosif — 'Do not add' beyond the required one!
Core Dispute When two types of offering blood are mixed, should we follow the stricter or more lenient requirement for altar placements?

Bal Tosif vs. Bal Tigra: The Counter-Arguments

R' Eliezer
Placing only one violates Bal Tigra (do not diminish) — you're skipping three required placements!
R' Yehoshua
Placing four violates Bal Tosif (do not add) — you're adding three extra placements for the 1-placement blood!
R' Eliezer
Bal Tosif applies only when the blood is by itself, not in a mixture.
R' Yehoshua
Then Bal Tigra likewise applies only when the blood is by itself!
R' Yehoshua
Furthermore: placing four is an active transgression (ma'aseh). Placing one is merely passive (shev v'al ta'aseh) — which is less severe.
Key Principle R' Yehoshua argues: a transgression through inaction is always preferable to one through action (שב ואל תעשה עדיף).

Intermingled Cups: R' Eliezer's Approach

Cup of disqualified blood mixed among cups of fit offerings
↓
One cup was already sacrificed on the altar
↓
R' Eliezer
Remaining cups are fit — we assume the disqualified one was already used
Rabbis
Even if all but one were sacrificed, pour remaining blood into the amah (drain)
↓
R' Elazar clarifies R' Eliezer: Cups must be sacrificed two by two (in pairs), not one at a time
Rav Dimi's Challenge The Mishna implies R' Eliezer permits even a single remaining cup — but R' Ya'akov explains 'one' means 'one pair' (two cups).

Why Teach the Dispute Twice? (Limbs & Cups)

Limbs (Evarim)

  • Fit limb mixed with disqualified limb on the altar
  • Blood already presented — atonement complete
  • Without this case: Maybe R' Eliezer is lenient only because blood was already done

Cups of Blood (Kosos)

  • Fit blood cup mixed with disqualified blood cup
  • Blood presentation is the issue itself — atonement not yet achieved
  • Without this case: Maybe Rabbis are strict only because blood hasn't been presented yet
Talmudic Reasoning Both cases are necessary (צריכא): each case alone would suggest a narrower rule than intended. Together they show the dispute is categorical.

The 'Mixing' Question (Yesh Bilbul / Ein Bilbul)

🫙 Flask of mei chatat (purification water) mixed with regular water
↓
🔬 Key question: Do the liquids blend uniformly (yesh bilbul) or remain distinct (ein bilbul)?
↓
🧑‍⚖️ Rabbis: There IS mixing → each drop contains both → each sprinkling is diluted → disqualified
↓
🧑‍🏫 R' Eliezer: Sprinkle twice to ensure some pure mei chatat reaches the person
↓
❓ Gemara asks: Does R' Eliezer hold there is NO mixing, or mixing + sprinkling combines?
Para 9:1 This mishna from Masechet Para is brought to probe R' Eliezer's underlying theory about mixtures — crucial for understanding his rulings on mixed blood.
