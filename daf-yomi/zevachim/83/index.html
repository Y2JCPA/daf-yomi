What Does the Altar Sanctify?

Rabban Gamliel
Any item suited to ascend upon the altar — whether consumed by fire or not — if it ascended, it shall not descend.
R' Yehoshua
Only items suited to be consumed by fire on the altar — if they ascended, they shall not descend. Derived from "the burnt offering on the pyre."
R' Shimon
Even if the offering was fit and libations unfit, or libations fit and offering unfit, or both unfit — it shall not descend.
Core Principle The mizbe'ach (altar) has the power to sanctify items that ascend upon it — the dispute is over how broadly this sanctification applies.

Does Entry Alone Disqualify Sin Offering Blood?

R' Eliezer
Blood of an external sin offering brought into the Sanctuary is disqualified even if not sprinkled. Derived from: "when he goes in to atone" (Lev 16:17) — meaning before atonement.
R' Shimon
The blood is disqualified only if actually sprinkled inside. Derived from: "whose blood was brought in to atone" (Lev 16:27) — referring to after atonement was performed.
R' Yehuda
If the priest brought the blood inside unwittingly (b'shogeg), the blood remains fit. Only intentional entry disqualifies.
Key Distinction R' Eliezer derives outside from outside (external offering from prohibition of entry). R' Shimon derives animal from animal (sin offering from Yom Kippur bull/goat).

Rava's Unresolved Dilemma: Blood Returned to the Curtain

Yom Kippur blood sprinkled on golden altar in the Sanctuary
↓
Priest brings remainder back toward the Parokhet (Curtain)
Perhaps Valid
Curtain & altar are in one place (both in Sanctuary) — no disqualification
Perhaps Invalid
Since going to the altar is called "going out" (Lev 16:18), returning to the Curtain = "bringing in"
Teyku The dilemma stands unresolved — the Gemara provides no definitive answer.

Rabban Gamliel vs. Rabbi Yehoshua: Altar Sanctification

Rabban Gamliel

  • Any item suited to ascend — even if not consumed by fire
  • Derives from: "upon the altar" (Lev 6:2)
  • Includes items like blood and libations
  • No difference between items that had fitness vs. never had fitness
  • Learns altar sanctifies from: "Whatever touches the altar shall be sacred" (Ex 29:37)

Rabbi Yehoshua

  • Only items suited for burning by fire on the altar
  • Derives from: "the burnt offering on the pyre" (Lev 6:2)
  • Needs two verses — one for items that had fitness, one for items that never did
  • Restoring dislodged parts learned from: "that the fire has consumed" (Lev 6:3)
  • Same verse also excludes incense from this rule
Source Both derive from Leviticus 6:2 — they disagree on which phrase is the operative source for the altar's sanctification.

Derivation Chain: Repeated 'Sin Offering' (R' Yehuda)

📖 Lev 16:27 says: "the bull of the sin offering and the goat of the sin offering"
↓
❓ Why repeat 'sin offering' twice? Could have said: "the bull and goat of the sin offering"
↓
🐂 One mention teaches that the Yom Kippur bull & goat render garments impure when burned
↓
🔁 The second mention includes all sin offerings that are burned — they also render garments impure
↓
📌 This is R' Yehuda's derivation; R' Meir says the extra term is unnecessary
Halakhic Impact R' Yehuda uses this to show that any burned sin offering — not just Yom Kippur offerings — renders garments of the one who burns them tamei (impure).
