QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why can one heir effect substitution of a father's offering but two heirs cannot?
A. Because two heirs are considered partners, and partners cannot effect substitution
B. Because the verse uses a singular verb form, limiting substitution to one heir
C. Because two heirs would need to split the substitute animal
D. Because the Torah only permits substitution for the original owner
Correct: B — The doubled word 'hamer yamir' includes an heir, but the singular form of the subject teaches that only ONE heir — not two — can effect substitution.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why doesn't the same singular-form limitation apply to second tithe redemption by heirs?
A. Because tithe redemption is a rabbinic obligation, not biblical
B. Because the father could redeem tithe in partnership while alive, so heirs can too
C. Because tithe has no substitution concept
D. Because the verse for tithe uses a plural form
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes: the father could redeem tithe even in partnership with another person while alive, so his heirs inherit that ability. Substitution, by contrast, cannot be done by partners at all.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, how does a father's offering relate to his heirs?
A. The heirs fully acquire it and it atones for their own sins
B. The heirs do not acquire it, but it atones for them incidentally (מכופיא)
C. The heirs acquire it but cannot bring it on the altar
D. The offering must be redeemed and a new one brought by the heirs
Correct: B — Rav Ashi resolves the tension: heirs do NOT acquire the offering (so two can bring a minḥa together), but it atones for them incidentally (מכופיא), which is sufficient for one heir to effect substitution.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rav Sheshet son of Rav Idi's position on offerings slaughtered not for their sake?
A. They are invalid and cannot be sacrificed
B. They atone fully but the owner brings a second offering as extra merit
C. They are sacrificed based on prior consecration but do NOT atone — the second offering atones
D. They partially atone, requiring the second offering to complete atonement
Correct: C — Rav Sheshet b. Rav Idi reasons: if the first offering atoned, there would be no purpose for the second. The first is sacrificed because it was already consecrated, but the second offering is what provides actual atonement.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rav Pappa reject the proof from the Shavuot goats that offerings can atone for post-designation sins?
A. He says the goats are burnt offerings, not sin offerings
B. He says communal offerings are different because the court makes a non-verbal stipulation — the slaughter itself designates them
C. He says both goats atone for the same sin simultaneously
D. He says Rabbi Shimon's teaching is not accepted as halakha
Correct: B — Rav Pappa distinguishes communal from individual offerings: for communal offerings, the court stipulates that the slaughtering knife designates them (הסכין מקדשתן), so the second goat is effectively designated at slaughter, not in advance. This cannot prove anything about individual offerings.
