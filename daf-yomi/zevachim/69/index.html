What Does the Mishnah Add? R' Yochanan's Resolution

Gemara
The mishnah's first clause (disqualified inside the azarah) adds: shechitah of sacrificial birds inside the courtyard does not make them neveilah.
Gemara
The latter clause (disqualified outside) adds: melikah of non-sacred birds outside the courtyard does make them neveilah.
Baraita
Supports R' Yochanan: Non-priest pinching, piggul, notar, or tamei — none transmit throat-impurity.
Key Ruling A baraita confirms R' Yochanan: bird offerings disqualified inside the azarah (even by a non-priest) do not transmit tumat beit ha-beli'ah.

R' Yitzchak's Uncertainty: Kemitzah vs Melikah by a Non-Priest

R' Yitzchak heard two halakhot:
One about kemitzah (handful) by a zar
One about melikah by a zar
↓
One descends from the altar if it ascended
One does not descend
But which is which?
↓
Chizkiyah resolves:
Kemitzah by zar → descends
Melikah by zar → does not descend
Reasoning Chizkiyah's logic: melikah by a non-priest should stay on the altar, but the Gemara challenges this distinction using the laws of private altars (bamot).

The Tereifah Bird Debate: Does Slaughter/Pinching Purify?

R' Meir

  • Kal vachomer: Animal slaughter purifies even a tereifah from neveilah-impurity
  • Bird impurity is lesser (no touch/carry, only swallowing)
  • Therefore slaughter surely purifies a tereifah bird
  • Extended: Melikah also purifies, as it renders the bird fit for consumption

R' Yosei

  • Agrees slaughter purifies a tereifah bird (from the kal vachomer)
  • Applies dayyo principle: conclusion cannot exceed its source
  • Since the source (animal) is purified only by slaughter, not pinching
  • A bird tereifah is purified by slaughter but NOT by melikah

R' Yehuda

  • Rejects the kal vachomer entirely
  • A slaughtered tereifah bird remains a neveilah
  • Derives from Lev. 17:15 — 'tereifah' is extra, teaching slaughtered tereifah still impure
  • Neither slaughter nor pinching purifies
Three-Way Dispute R' Meir: both slaughter and melikah purify. R' Yosei: only slaughter purifies. R' Yehuda: neither purifies a tereifah bird.

The Dayyo Principle — Limiting Kal Vachomer

דיו לבא מן הדין להיות כנדון
Torah Source From Miriam's punishment (Numbers 12:14): If her father rebuked her, she'd hide 7 days. God's rebuke should warrant 14 days — yet the Torah says only 7. Dayyo: the derived law (Divine rebuke) cannot exceed its source (human rebuke).

R' Yosei applies this to block R' Meir's extension from slaughter to melikah.
