Zevachim 69 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yochanan, what does invalid melikah inside the azarah NOT cause?
A. Disqualification of the offering
B. Neveilah-impurity (tumat beit ha-beli'ah)
C. A prohibition on eating the meat
D. The need to bring a replacement offering
Correct: B — R' Yochanan holds that bird offerings disqualified inside the azarah — even by a non-priest's melikah — do not transmit tumat beit ha-beli'ah (throat-impurity), as confirmed by the baraita.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was R' Yitzchak's uncertainty regarding a non-priest performing kemitzah vs melikah?
A. Whether either is valid at all
B. Which one descends from the altar if it ascended, and which does not
C. Whether they transmit impurity
D. Which requires a service vessel
Correct: B — R' Yitzchak heard that one stays on the altar and one descends, but could not remember which halakhah applied to which case. Chizkiyah resolved that kemitzah descends and melikah does not.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Sheshet, what determines whether bird offerings were sacrificed on private altars (bamot)?
A. Whether the owner was a priest or non-priest
B. Whether meal offerings were sacrificed on private altars — birds follow the same rule
C. The size of the altar
D. Whether the altar was anointed
Correct: B — Rav Sheshet links birds and meal offerings: since neither is mentioned at Sinai (only slaughtered animal offerings appear in Exodus 24:5), they share the same disputed status regarding bamot.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does R' Yosei disagree with R' Meir about melikah purifying a tereifah bird?
A. He holds melikah is never a valid form of slaughter
B. He applies the dayyo principle: the conclusion of a kal vachomer cannot exceed its source
C. He rejects that slaughter purifies any tereifah
D. He holds birds cannot become tereifah
Correct: B — R' Yosei accepts R' Meir's kal vachomer that slaughter purifies a tereifah bird, but applies dayyo: since the source (animal) is purified only by slaughter, the derived law (bird) cannot extend to melikah.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does R' Yosei b'R' Avin explain that R' Meir does accept the dayyo principle?
A. R' Meir's real source is a verse (Lev. 11:46), not a kal vachomer, so dayyo doesn't apply
B. R' Meir holds dayyo is only rabbinic, not Torah-level
C. R' Meir applies dayyo differently than R' Yosei
D. R' Meir received a tradition from Sinai overriding dayyo
Correct: A — R' Yosei b'R' Avin explains that R' Meir's kal vachomer was illustrative; his actual derivation is from Lev. 11:46 ('the law of the beast and the fowl'), a direct verse-based equation that is not subject to the dayyo limitation.
