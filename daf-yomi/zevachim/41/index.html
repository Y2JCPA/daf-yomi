The Derivation Chain Problem

Diaphragm & 2 kidneys are explicitly stated for the anointed priest's bull (Lev 4:8–9)
↓
Derived by hekkesh → applies to the communal sin bull
↓
Can this now teach via hekkesh → the idol worship goats?
↓
Problem
This would be hekkesh from hekkesh — not done in consecrated matters!
Solution

Can Hekkesh Teach via Kal VaChomer?

Gemara
The goat of Yom Kippur's laws are derived from idol worship goats — but those goats learned their laws via hekkesh from "as he did" (Lev 4:20)!
Gemara
Can a law derived by hekkesh then teach via kal vachomer? There's a principle that it cannot in consecrated matters!
Rav Pappa
The school of R' Yishmael holds: A matter derived by hekkesh can teach again via kal vachomer, even in consecrated matters.
Rav Pappa
And as for the communal bull's diaphragm/kidneys — the verse "with the bull" re-roots the law so it's as if explicitly written there.
TANNAIC DISPUTE The Tanna of Rav's school amplifies all laws through the single juxtaposition, while R' Yishmael's school requires an additional derivation for the diaphragm and kidneys.

The King & His Beloved Servant

משל למלך בשר ודם שכעס על אוהבו — מיעט בסרחונו מפני חיבתו
PARABLE — SCHOOL OF R' YISHMAEL Why doesn't the Torah detail the diaphragm and kidneys for the communal sin bull?

Like a king who grew angry at his beloved servant — he speaks little of the offense due to his great affection.

Because Israel is beloved by God, the Torah does not elaborate on their sin offering's details.

Similarly, the anointed priest's bull says "before the Curtain of the Sanctuary" (full title), while the communal bull says only "before the Curtain" — when the majority sins, even the relationship with the sacred space diminishes.

Piggul with Half a Permitting Factor

Rabbi Meir

  • If piggul intent during handful but not frankincense → offering is piggul
  • One who eats it is liable for karet
  • Per Reish Lakish: R' Meir's true reason is "one who acts, acts with initial intent"
  • Silent burning of frankincense carries forward the earlier piggul intent from the handful

Rabbis (Chachamim)

  • No karet liability unless piggul intent during the entire permitting factor
  • Both handful and frankincense must be burned with improper intent
  • Half a permitting factor is insufficient to create piggul
  • Silent action = proper intent, not continuation of prior intent
REISH LAKISH'S READING R' Meir does NOT hold that half a permitting factor alone creates piggul. Rather, he holds that performing an action silently continues the original intent — so both factors end up having piggul intent.
