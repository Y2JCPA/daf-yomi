Which Blood Does the Baraita Mean?

Initial Reading
"Blood requiring the base" = remainder blood of a standard sin offering (שיירי חטאת)
Gemara
Objection: Piggul applies only to services indispensable for atonement — pouring remainder is not indispensable!
Second Attempt
Perhaps it refers to the last three placements of a sin offering's blood?
Gemara
Objection: That blood goes on the corner (קרן), not the base! And we already said the last three placements don't cause piggul.
Conclusion
It refers to remainder blood of inner sin offerings (חטאות הפנימיות) — per R' Neḥemya.
R' NEḤEMYA R' Neḥemya holds that one who offers the remainder blood of any sin offering outside the Temple is liable — both inner and outer.

Why Not Distinguish Within Remainder Blood Itself?

Question: Why contrast remainder blood with drain blood?
Why not contrast inner vs. outer sin offering remainders?
↓
The baraita follows R' Neḥemya, who holds one is liable for offering remainder blood outside — even for outer sin offerings
↓
So there is no lenient ruling for outer sin offerings to contrast with inner ones
↓
The tanna chose drain blood as the contrast to create a clean three-for-three comparison: three stringencies vs. three leniencies
STRUCTURE The baraita's structure is driven by the need to present three parallel rulings — requiring a contrast category with three corresponding leniencies.

Rav Pappa vs. Ravina: Blood from the Corner

Rav Pappa

  • Blood from the last three placements requires laundering
  • Proof: Mishna says blood spraying from the corner after placement does NOT require laundering
  • Implies: blood fit for placement on the corner DOES require laundering

Ravina's Clarification

  • "From the corner" in the mishna means literally from the corner itself — after blood was already placed
  • This supports Rav Pappa's inference
  • Blood still awaiting placement retains its laundering obligation
MISHNA 93a If sin offering blood sprayed onto a garment from the corner or base of the altar, the garment does not require laundering — because the blood has already fulfilled its purpose.

Amud B: Deriving Indispensable Services for the Communal Bull

📖 "As he did with the bull" (Lev. 4:20) — refers to the bull of the anointed priest: all sprinklings are indispensable
↓
🐐 "A sin offering" — includes goats for unwitting communal idol worship: same procedure as communal bull
↓
🚫 "So shall he do with this" — excludes Festival goats and New Moon goats
↓
❓ Why include idol worship goats and exclude Festival/New Moon goats?
↓
✅ Idol worship goats atone for a known mitzvah transgression — similar to the communal bull
↓
❌ Festival/New Moon goats atone for defilement of the Temple — a different category
PRINCIPLE Inclusion/exclusion is based on similarity of purpose: offerings that atone for a specific mitzvah violation are grouped together.
