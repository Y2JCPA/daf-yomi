QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the baraita, what three halakhic consequences apply to 'blood that requires the base'?
A. Laundering, piggul intent, and liability for offering outside
B. Laundering, me'ila, and disqualification if it enters the Sanctuary
C. Piggul intent, notar, and liability for offering outside
D. Laundering, disqualification if delayed, and me'ila
Correct: A — The baraita lists three stringencies for blood requiring the base: it requires laundering of a sprayed garment, improper intent renders it piggul, and one who offers it outside the Temple is liable.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the interpretation that the baraita refers to the remainder blood of a standard (outer) sin offering?
A. Because the remainder blood of an outer sin offering is never poured on the base
B. Because piggul applies only to services indispensable for atonement, and pouring the remainder is not indispensable
C. Because R' Neḥemya disagrees with the baraita entirely
D. Because the remainder blood has already become disqualified
Correct: B — The Gemara cites another baraita: piggul applies only to indispensable services. Since pouring the remainder is not indispensable for atonement, piggul cannot apply to it — contradicting the first baraita if it referred to standard remainder blood.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the baraita contrast remainder blood with drain blood rather than contrasting inner vs. outer sin offering remainders?
A. Because outer sin offerings have no remainder blood
B. Because R' Neḥemya holds one is liable for offering even outer remainder blood outside, so there is no leniency to contrast
C. Because the drain blood is more commonly encountered
D. Because inner and outer sin offerings follow identical rules
Correct: B — Since the baraita follows R' Neḥemya, who holds that offering even the remainder of an outer sin offering outside the Temple creates liability, there would be no three-for-three contrast between inner and outer. The tanna chose drain blood to create a clean parallel of three stringencies vs. three leniencies.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why are the goats for unwitting communal idol worship included in the same procedure as the communal bull, while Festival and New Moon goats are excluded?
A. Because idol worship goats are more expensive
B. Because idol worship goats atone for a known mitzvah transgression, similar to the communal bull
C. Because Festival and New Moon goats are individual offerings
D. Because idol worship goats are brought only once a year
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the idol worship goats are similar to the communal bull because both atone for the known transgression of a specific mitzvah. Festival and New Moon goats atone for defilement of the Temple — a different category — and are therefore excluded.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the Gemara's reasoning for treating the seven sprinklings as indispensable but semicha and pouring the remainder as non-indispensable?
A. The seven sprinklings are performed by the Kohen Gadol, while the others are not
B. The Torah explicitly states each ruling without need for reasoning
C. The seven sprinklings are indispensable in all cases universally, while semicha and pouring the remainder are not universally indispensable
D. Semicha and pouring the remainder were added by rabbinic decree
Correct: C — The Gemara's principle: a service that is indispensable universally (in all offerings where it applies) is indispensable here too. Semicha and pouring the remainder are not universally indispensable, so they cannot be deemed indispensable for the communal bull either.
