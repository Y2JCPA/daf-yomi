Mixed Sacrificial Blood: What Happens?

Rava
R' Eliezer and the Rabbis don't disagree about actually mixed blood — they disagree about intermingled cups (כוסות שנתערבו)
R' Eliezer
View improperly-placed blood as though it were water — present the blood
Rabbis
We do not view it as water — pour it all into the Temple drain (אמה)
R' Yehuda
Actually, R' Eliezer holds his view for both mixed blood and intermingled cups
KEY ISSUE When blood of different offerings becomes mixed, can the priest still perform valid placements, or must everything be discarded?

Remainder Blood of Sin Offering + Burnt Offering Blood

Abaye
The Rabbis only require pouring into the drain when the first portion of sin offering blood mixes with burnt offering blood
Abaye
But if the remainder (שיריים) mixes with burnt offering blood — all agree place it below the red line, since both go to the same place
Rav Yosef
The places are not the same! Remainder blood goes on the bench (דוכן) — the top surface of the base — not the side of the altar
Reish Lakish
I agree with Abaye — same place, so place it below the red line
R' Yochanan
This case of remainder blood mixed with burnt offering blood is still part of the dispute between R' Eliezer and the Rabbis
PARALLEL Babylonian pair (Abaye/Rav Yosef) and Eretz Yisrael pair (Reish Lakish/R' Yochanan) have the same dispute

Three Verses — Blood of Offerings Doesn't Nullify

"They are holy" (Num. 18:17)

  • Source: firstborn offerings
  • "They" — even if mixed with other blood, it is sacrificed
  • Others: "they" means firstborns themselves are holy, but substitutes are not

"Blood of the bull and blood of the goat" (Lev. 16:18)

  • Source: Yom Kippur service
  • Two bloods mentioned together — neither nullifies the other
  • Others: High Priest doesn't actually mix them; places each separately

"Blood...blood" (Lev. 1:5)

  • Double mention of "blood" includes all offering types
  • Expanded step by step: substitute → thanks/peace → guilt → firstborn/tithe/Paschal
  • Others: don't derive anything from the repetition
PRINCIPLE דם עולין אין מבטלין זה את זה — Blood of offerings that ascend to the altar do not nullify one another

The Baraita's Step-by-Step Expansion of "Blood, Blood"

🔵 First "blood" — burnt offering mixed with its substitute (both sacrificed even if intermingled alive)
↓
➡️ Expand to thanks/peace offering blood — similar as vow/gift offerings
↓
➡️ Expand to guilt offering blood — similar as requiring two-that-are-four placements
↓
🔵 Second "blood" — includes firstborn, tithe, and Paschal (only one placement each)
↓
✅ Conclusion: Any blood placed below the red line mixed with burnt offering blood — present it
METHOD The Gemara shows how each expansion requires a new derivation because each offering type has fewer similarities to a burnt offering
