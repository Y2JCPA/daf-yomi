Can Prohibited Animals Be Nullified in a Majority?

Gemara
Why are both mishnayot needed — here in Zevachim and in Avoda Zara — to teach that a prohibited animal prohibits the entire mixture?
Answer
From Avoda Zara alone, one might think offerings should be treated leniently to avoid Temple loss. From Zevachim alone, one might think non-sacred mixtures allow benefit.
Gemara
But why not simply nullify the prohibited animal in the majority (bitul b'rov)?
Gemara
Perhaps because animals are significant (davar shebeminyan) — sold individually, not by weight?
Gemara
This works for Reish Lakish (also counted = significant), but for R' Yochanan (exclusively counted), animals are sometimes sold in herds!
Core Issue The Gemara struggles to explain why prohibited animals cannot be nullified in a majority, especially according to R' Yochanan's stricter definition of 'significant items.'

Why Are Both Mishnayot Necessary?

A prohibited animal is mixed with permitted ones
↓
The entire mixture is prohibited — taught in two places
↓
Only Avoda Zara?
One might say: with offerings, nullify to avoid Temple loss
Only Zevachim?
One might say: for non-sacred benefit, nullify in majority
Conclusion Both mishnayot are needed: Zevachim teaches the rule applies to offerings; Avoda Zara teaches it applies to ordinary benefit.

What Makes an Item 'Significant' (Davar SheBeminyan)?

R' Yochanan
We learned in R' Meir's mishna: only an item whose manner is exclusively to be counted (darko limanot) is significant and cannot be nullified.
Reish Lakish
We learned: any item whose manner is also to be counted — even if sometimes sold in bulk — is significant and cannot be nullified.
Gemara
Animals are often sold individually but sometimes sold as part of a herd.
Gemara
For Reish Lakish: animals qualify as significant. For R' Yochanan: they do not — so why can't they be nullified?
Key Dispute This machloket about R' Meir's standard determines whether animals — which are usually but not exclusively sold by unit — can be nullified in a majority.

R' Meir vs. The Rabbis on Nullification

Rabbi Meir

  • Any item whose manner is to be counted renders its mixture prohibited
  • Bundles of forbidden fenugreek mixed with permitted ones — ALL must be burned
  • Even secondary mixtures (תערובת של תערובת) are prohibited

The Rabbis (Chachamim)

  • Only SIX specific items are sufficiently significant to prohibit a mixture
  • Forbidden bundles are nullified in a ratio of 1:200
  • General items can be nullified in a simple majority (bitul b'rov)
R' Akiva R' Akiva adds a seventh item to the Rabbis' list: loaves of a homeowner, bringing the total to seven items that always prohibit their mixture.

Rav Pappa's Resolution

אמר רב פפא: האי תנא, תנא דליטרא קציעות הוא
תירוץ According to R' Yochanan — who holds animals are NOT exclusively counted and thus not inherently significant — the tanna of our mishna who says prohibited animals cannot be nullified follows the opinion of the tanna of the litra of dried figs, who applies a broader standard of significance.
