Why Not Return Pots to Kilns?

Challenger
If kindling cleanses earthenware, why does the Torah say to break Temple pots? Just return them to the kilns!
R' Zeira
A baraita teaches: Kilns may not be built in Jerusalem because of the great smoke they produce.
Abaye
But if there are no kilns, are there scrap heaps in the courtyard? The same baraita says no scrap heaps in Jerusalem either!
Gemara
Abaye forgot what Shemaya taught in Kalnevo: the shards of earthenware were miraculously absorbed in their place.
Miracle Broken Temple pottery did not accumulate — it was absorbed into the ground miraculously, one of the wonders of the Temple courtyard.

Why Is the Temple Oven Made of Metal?

If kindling cleanses earthenware from within...
↓
Why did Rav Naḥman (citing Rabba bar Avuh) say:
The Temple oven must be metal?
↓
Two Loaves (Shavuot) and Shewbread
are baked in the oven
↓
These are sanctified by the oven itself
(not kneaded in a service vessel)
↓
The oven is therefore a service vessel (kli shareit)
— and service vessels cannot be earthenware
R' Yosei b. R' Yehuda Even he, who allows wooden service vessels, agrees that earthenware is too insignificant a material for sacred use.

Rav Yitzḥak bar Yehuda Chooses a Teacher

Rami bar Ḥama
You left me for Rav Sheshet — do you think just being near him (alkafta analogy) makes you like him?
Rav Yitzḥak bar Yehuda
Master, you resolve questions through reasoning (sevara). When I find a contradicting mishna, it refutes your answer entirely.
Rav Yitzḥak bar Yehuda
Rav Sheshet resolves through citing a mishna. A contradicting mishna is merely a dispute between sources — it doesn't refute his answer.

Ask me something — I'll resolve it from a mishna too!
Methodology This passage highlights two approaches to Talmudic reasoning: logical deduction vs. textual citation. Mishna-based answers are more resilient to challenge.

Partial Cooking — Does the Whole Vessel Need Scouring?

Rami bar Ḥama's View

  • Only the part where meat was cooked needs scouring
  • Analogous to blood sprinkling on a garment
  • Just as you launder only the stained area...
  • ...scour only the cooking area

Baraita (Tosefta 10:15)

  • The ENTIRE vessel requires scouring and rinsing
  • Cooking flavor spreads throughout the vessel
  • Unlike blood, which doesn't penetrate the whole garment
  • Derived from: "in a copper vessel" (Lev. 6:21)
Rami's Concession "If this baraita is taught, it is taught" — Rami bar Ḥama accepted the authoritative teaching, demonstrating intellectual honesty.
