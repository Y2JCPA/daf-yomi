Zevachim 93 — Impure Blood, Laundering & Sprinkling Fitness

Core Question: Does impure sin-offering blood sprayed on a garment require laundering?
↓
Rav Huna b. R' Yehoshua infers: If blood was once fit then disqualified → no laundering
↓
But what if disqualification is simultaneous with contact?
R' Elazar
Impure purification water still works → simultaneous impurity doesn't block function
Rabbis
Impurity disqualifies even simultaneously → garment may not need laundering
KEY THEME The daf explores when blood or purification water retains its ritual function despite becoming impure — and whether "fitness" must exist before, during, or throughout the sprinkling process.

Does Impure Sin-Offering Blood Require Laundering?

Rami bar Ḥama
If blood of a ḥatat sprayed onto an impure garment — making the blood itself impure — does the garment require kibbus (laundering)?
Rav Huna b. R' Yehoshua
From the way he asked — pure blood onto impure garment — we see he holds: if blood was once fit then disqualified, no laundering is needed.
Rav Huna b. R' Yehoshua
His real question: does the exemption apply only when disqualification comes after contact? Or even when they happen simultaneously?
Rav Ḥisda
This depends on the dispute between R' Elazar and the Rabbis regarding mei ḥatat — as explained by Rabba and resolved by Abaye.
PRINCIPLE The question hinges on whether simultaneous disqualification is treated like prior disqualification or like a fresh event.

R' Elazar vs. Rabbis: Impure Purification Water

Mei Ḥatat (purification water with red heifer ashes) becomes impure
↓
Can it still purify tumat met (corpse impurity)?
R' Elazar
✅ Yes — even impure water purifies. Proof: we sprinkle on a niddah who has corpse-impurity; the water becomes impure on contact yet still works.
Rabbis
❌ No — once impure, the water loses its purifying power.
↓
R' Elazar follows R' Akiva — who says passing over impurity = resting on it (per Rabba)
CONNECTION The niddah case is the model for simultaneous impurity: the water becomes impure at the very moment it touches her, yet R' Elazar says it still purifies.
5 / 9

Rabba vs. Abaye: Why Does R' Akiva Deem the Water Impure?

Rabba's Explanation

  • R' Akiva holds: passing a vessel over impurity = resting on it
  • So the jug of mei ḥatat passing over the oven with a sheretz becomes impure
  • This is a Torah-level principle: ma'avir k'maniaḥ
  • Extends to niddah case: water is already impure before touching her

Abaye's Resolution

  • Everyone agrees passing ≠ resting — not a Torah principle
  • R' Akiva's ruling is a rabbinic decree (gezeirah): lest the vessel come to rest
  • But during actual sprinkling, once water is airborne — it has 'set forth' and cannot rest
  • So even R' Akiva agrees: in the act of sprinkling, water stays pure
ABAYE'S KEY MOVE By reframing R' Akiva's ruling as a decree rather than a principle, Abaye limits its scope — sprinkling in mid-air cannot trigger the decree.

Rava's Baraita: Requirements for Dipping & Sprinkling

🏺 "In the blood" — There must be enough blood in the vessel for dipping from the outset
↓
👆 "The priest shall dip" — There must be enough blood remaining for dipping throughout the rite
↓
🩸 "Sprinkle of the blood" — He must sprinkle from the blood in the vessel, not residue on his finger
↓
🔄 The priest must re-dip his finger for each new sprinkle — leftover blood on the finger is pasul
↓
❌ Cannot wipe blood from sides or bottom of the vessel onto his finger
R' ELAZAR'S RULING The remainder of blood on the priest's finger after sprinkling is unfit (pasul) for further sprinkling. Each sprinkle requires a fresh dip.
