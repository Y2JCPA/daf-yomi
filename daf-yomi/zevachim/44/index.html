Piggul: From Peace Offerings to All Sacrifices

Torah states piggul only regarding peace offerings (Lev 7:18)
↓
Does piggul apply only to offerings eaten for two days?
Narrow Reading
Only offerings like peace offerings (2 days + 1 night)
Expansive Derivation
Gezeirah shavah of 'iniquity' and 'profanation' extends piggul to all offerings
↓
Piggul applies to anything with mattirin (permitting factors) — for person or altar
KEY PRINCIPLE The Torah specified peace offerings to teach that piggul requires permitting factors (mattirin), not to limit piggul to peace offerings alone.

Ze'eiri: Impurity Levels & Their Penalties

Ze'eiri
The lenient impurity = sheretz (creeping animal). The stringent = tum'at met (corpse).
Ze'eiri
Sheretz + ma'aser sheni (lenient food) = violation of a prohibition (lav)
Ze'eiri
Sheretz + terumah (stringent food) = death at the hand of Heaven (misah bidei shamayim)
Gemara
Without a verse, we'd infer via kal va'chomer: corpse impurity + lenient food should also = death!
Ze'eiri
That's why the Torah explicitly states corpse impurity + ma'aser sheni = only a lav, not death. Dayo — it is sufficient.
DAYO PRINCIPLE A conclusion derived from a kal va'chomer cannot be more stringent than its source case.

The Gezeirah Shavah Chain: Extending Piggul

Piggul stated for peace offerings: 'It shall be piggul… bear his iniquity' (Lev 7:18)
↓
Notar verse: 'He has profaned… bear his iniquity' (Lev 19:8)
↓
Link 'iniquity' in piggul ↔ 'iniquity' in notar
↓
Impurity verse: 'They shall not profane My holy name' (Lev 22:2)
↓
Link 'profanation' in notar ↔ 'profanation' in impurity
↓
Result: Piggul extends to bird offerings, meal offerings, and even the log of oil of a leper
METHOD A double gezeirah shavah: piggul → notar (via 'avon'), notar → tum'ah (via 'chilul'), extending piggul to all sacred items.

Log of Oil of the Leper: Piggul Dispute

R' Meir
The log of oil of a leper is subject to piggul — the blood of the guilt offering renders it permitted for placement on the leper.
Rabbanan
But a person can bring his guilt offering today and the log of oil ten days later! How can the blood render it piggul?
R' Meir
I spoke only of a log of oil that comes together with the guilt offering — not one brought separately.
R' Yirmeya
The baraita follows R' Meir — just remove meal offerings of libations from the piggul-exempt list.
Abaye
No need to remove anything! The tanna taught: oil with the offering = piggul applies. Oil or libations alone = piggul does not apply.
ABAYE'S RESOLUTION Items that come with their offering follow its piggul status; items brought independently do not.

Priestly Gifts: 'Every...' Inclusions (Numbers 18:9)

Every Offering

  • Includes: Log of oil of a leper
  • Challenge: Oil is not 'from the fire' — none goes on the altar
  • Resolution: 'Every' broadens beyond fire-offerings

Every Meal Offering

  • Includes: Omer offering & Sotah meal offering
  • Challenge: These don't achieve atonement
  • Omer permits new crop; Sotah clarifies guilt

Every Sin Offering

  • Includes: Bird sin offering
  • Challenge: Bird killed by melikah (pinching), not shechitah
  • Without the verse, it would be like neveilah (unslaughtered carcass)

Every Guilt Offering

  • Includes: Nazir's & leper's guilt offerings
  • Challenge: These qualify rather than atone
  • Leper's is already explicit — so verse teaches about nazir's
PATTERN Each 'every' (כל) in Numbers 18:9 includes an offering that might have been excluded on logical grounds.
