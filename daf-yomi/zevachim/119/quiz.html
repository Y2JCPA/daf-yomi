Zevachim 119 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara's calculation, how many years did the Tabernacle stand in Shiloh?
A. 370
B. 369
C. 357
D. 380
Correct: B — The Gemara calculates: 480 (Exodus to Temple) − 40 (wilderness) − 14 (Gilgal) − 57 (Nov/Gibeon) = 369 years. The text says '370 less one.'
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does R' Yoḥanan say second tithe was NOT eaten in Nov and Gibeon?
A. Because the Torah explicitly prohibits it there
B. Because there was no Ark in Nov/Gibeon, and tithe is linked to the Ark via a gezera shava of 'there'
C. Because second tithe can only be eaten in Jerusalem
D. Because Nov and Gibeon were not in Eretz Yisrael
Correct: B — R' Yoḥanan derives via the verbal analogy of 'sham' (there): since the Ark was in Kiriath Jearim, not Nov/Gibeon, the connection to second tithe means it wasn't eaten there either.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In R' Shimon's four principles, what happens if one consecrates during a PERMITTED period and offers during a FORBIDDEN period?
A. No violation at all
B. Positive mitzva and prohibition, with karet
C. Positive mitzva and prohibition, but NO karet
D. Only a positive mitzva, no prohibition
Correct: C — R' Shimon teaches that consecrating during a permitted period and offering during a forbidden period violates both a positive mitzva and a prohibition, but does NOT carry the punishment of karet.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Shimon ben Yoḥai, what do BOTH 'menuḥa' and 'naḥala' refer to?
A. Shiloh
B. Jerusalem
C. Nov and Gibeon
D. Gilgal
Correct: B — R' Shimon ben Yoḥai holds that both 'rest' and 'inheritance' refer to Jerusalem, meaning bamot were only forbidden once the Temple was built — which explains why Manoaḥ could sacrifice on a private altar during the Shiloh period.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rabba refute Rav Kahana's claim that karet applies for offering up animals consecrated during a permitted period?
A. He shows the verse refers to a different time period
B. He argues the word is written with aleph (וַאֲלֵהֶם = 'to them'), not ayin, and cites R' Shimon's baraita
C. He proves that offering up is less severe than slaughter
D. He demonstrates that karet never applies outside Jerusalem
Correct: B — Rabba rejects reading וַאֲלֵהֶם (aleph, 'to them') as עֲלֵיהֶם (ayin, 'about them'). The verse simply means 'say to them' — relay the command to Aaron, his sons, and Israel. He supports this with R' Shimon's baraita explicitly stating no karet in this case.
