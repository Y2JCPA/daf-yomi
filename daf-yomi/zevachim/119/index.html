The Math: 480 Years from Exodus to Temple

🏜️ 40 years in the wilderness
↓
⚔️ 14 years in Gilgal: 7 conquering + 7 dividing the land
↓
🏛️ Shiloh destroyed → Ark to Kiriath Jearim for 20 years
↓
👑 David reigns 7 years in Hebron + 33 in Jerusalem = 53 years after Shiloh's destruction
↓
⛰️ Nov & Gibeon: 53 + 4 years of Solomon = 57 total
↓
🧮 480 − 40 − 14 − 57 = 369 years for Shiloh
SOURCE "In the fourth year of his reign" (II Chronicles 3:2) — the 480th year after the Exodus (I Kings 6:1)

The Verse that Governs Bamot

כִּי לֹא בָאתֶם עַד עָתָּה אֶל הַמְּנוּחָה וְאֶל הַנַּחֲלָה
DEUTERONOMY 12:9 "For you have not as yet come to the rest (מנוחה) and to the inheritance (נחלה)"

The Torah uses two separate terms to create an interim period between Shiloh and Jerusalem — during which bamot are once again permitted.

What are 'Rest' (Menuḥa) and 'Inheritance' (Naḥala)?

R' Yehuda
Menuḥa = Shiloh
Naḥala = Jerusalem
Bamot forbidden at Shiloh, permitted at Nov/Gibeon, forbidden again at Jerusalem
R' Shimon
Menuḥa = Jerusalem
Naḥala = Shiloh
Both terms can apply to either place
School of R' Yishmael
Both menuḥa and naḥala refer to Shiloh
R' Shimon b. Yoḥai
Both menuḥa and naḥala refer to Jerusalem — bamot only forbidden once the Temple was built
CHALLENGE If both terms refer to the same place, the verse should say menuḥa naḥala — why use the separate phrasing el hamenuḥa ve'el hanaḥala?

Was Ma'aser Sheni Eaten in Nov & Gibeon?

Reish Lakish
The Mishna should also teach the law of second tithe in Nov and Gibeon!
R' Yoḥanan
Second tithe is derived from the Ark via a gezera shava of "שָׁם" (there). Since the Ark was not in Nov/Gibeon (it was in Kiriath Jearim), no second tithe was eaten there.
Reish Lakish
But the Paschal offering also uses "there" — yet everyone agrees it was offered in Nov/Gibeon!
R' Yoḥanan
The Mishna follows R' Shimon: only Pesaḥ and fixed-time public offerings were brought. Animal tithe (no set time) was not brought — and grain tithe is juxtaposed to animal tithe.
R' YEHUDA'S VIEW According to R' Yehuda, unfixed-time offerings were sacrificed on a great public altar, so second tithe was eaten in Nov and Gibeon (Rav Adda bar Mattana).
