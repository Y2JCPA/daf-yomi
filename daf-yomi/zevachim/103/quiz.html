Zevachim 103 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, when does a Kohen NOT acquire the hide of a burnt offering?
A. When the offering was slaughtered not for its sake
B. When the altar did not acquire the flesh (e.g., disqualified before blood sprinkling)
C. When the offering belongs to a woman
D. When the offering is from Kodshei Kodashim
Correct: B — The Mishna states that if the altar did not acquire the flesh — such as when the offering was disqualified before blood sprinkling — the Kohanim do not get the hide. An offering slaughtered she-lo lishmah still gives the hide to Kohanim.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the dispute between R' Yehuda and R' Yosei b'R' Yehuda about 'a man's burnt offering'?
A. R' Yehuda excludes women's offerings; R' Yosei excludes children's
B. R' Yehuda excludes consecrated property offerings; R' Yosei excludes converts' offerings
C. R' Yehuda excludes individual offerings; R' Yosei excludes communal ones
D. R' Yehuda excludes blemished offerings; R' Yosei excludes stolen ones
Correct: B — R' Yehuda derives that 'a man's olah' excludes offerings from consecrated (surplus) property, while R' Yosei b'R' Yehuda says it excludes offerings of converts who died without heirs.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did R' Yehuda retract his ruling that Kohanim don't get hides from surplus-funded Olos?
A. Rava proved from a verse that the definite article includes surplus offerings
B. R' Nechemya showed it would nullify Yehoyada HaKohen's teaching about surplus Asham funds
C. R' Yishmael's kal va-chomer proved surplus offerings are included
D. The Mishna in Shekalim explicitly states Kohanim get those hides
Correct: B — R' Nechemya (or R' Shimon) challenged R' Yehuda: Yehoyada HaKohen taught that surplus Asham money goes to buy Olos, with hides going to Kohanim. Denying Kohanim those hides would undermine this established institution.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, why is the verse about the Kohen receiving the Olah hide necessary?
A. To extend the rule to all Kodshei Kodashim via a kal va-chomer
B. Because without it, we'd think the hide is burned with the flesh on the altar
C. To exclude offerings of lesser sanctity from the rule
D. To teach that only male Kohanim receive hides
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi holds that normally hide follows flesh. Since Olah flesh is entirely burned, we'd assume the hide is burned too. The verse is needed specifically for Olah — other offerings don't need it because the hide naturally follows the flesh to whoever receives it.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does R' Yishmael derive from the gezeira shava of 'lo tihyeh' between Olah and Asham?
A. Kohanim receive hides of both Olos and Ashamos
B. Bones of an Olah not attached to flesh are permitted and need not be burned
C. The same Kohen who sprinkles blood receives the hide
D. An Onein is excluded from both Olah and Asham service
Correct: B — R' Yishmael uses the verbal analogy: just as Asham bones belong to Kohanim after blood sprinkling, so too Olah bones not attached to flesh are permitted and do not need to be burned on the altar.
