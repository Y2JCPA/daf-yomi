Zevachim 12 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yochanan, what is Ben Beteira's view of a Korban Pesach slaughtered on the morning of the 14th?
A. It is fit whether for its sake or not
B. It is unfit whether for its sake or not
C. It is fit only if slaughtered for its sake
D. It is fit only if slaughtered not for its sake
Correct: B — R' Yochanan says Ben Beteira deems it pasul in either case — since part of the day (afternoon) IS fit for the Pesach, morning slaughter is invalid regardless of intent.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say BOTH the apostate case and the imbecile case of dichuy are necessary?
A. One involves a Torah prohibition and one involves a rabbinic prohibition
B. Each case alone could lead to a mistaken leniency — voluntary vs. involuntary, and in-one's-power vs. not
C. One applies to a Chatat and the other to an Olah
D. One was taught by R' Yochanan and the other by R' Abbahu
Correct: B — Without the apostate case, we'd think involuntary disqualification (imbecile) doesn't apply. Without the imbecile case, we'd think voluntary disqualification (apostate who can recant) isn't permanent.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Abaye resolve R' Abbahu's challenge about when a Korban Pesach can be validly designated?
A. It can only be designated after noon on the 14th
B. An offering whose time arrives that same day is not considered mechusar zeman
C. The Pesach can be designated any time in Nisan
D. Designation is valid only at the time of slaughter
Correct: B — Abaye's principle: if the offering's proper time will arrive later that same day, the morning designation does not create a disqualifying 'time has not yet arrived' status.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What offering does Ben Azzai ADD to the list of those disqualified when sacrificed not for their sake?
A. The Asham (guilt offering)
B. The Shelamim (peace offering)
C. The Olah (burnt offering)
D. The Mincha (meal offering)
Correct: C — Ben Azzai, based on a tradition from 72 elders, adds only the Olah to the Chatat and Pesach. He derives this via a kal vachomer: if the partially-eaten Chatat is disqualified, the entirely-burned Olah should certainly be.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the 'certain elder' say the case of a court ruling (permitting the fat, then retracting) is the PRIMARY example of dichuy?
A. Because court rulings are more authoritative than individual actions
B. Because in that case the offering ITSELF was disqualified (rendered unnecessary), not just the owner
C. Because it involves a dispute between Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel
D. Because the Torah explicitly addresses this scenario
Correct: B — In the apostate and imbecile cases, only the person was disqualified while the offering remained inherently valid. When the court rules the fat is permitted, the offering itself becomes unnecessary — making dichuy even more obvious.
