QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yochanan, if one slaughters an animal intending to sprinkle its blood for idol worship, what is the status of the animal?
A. Permitted — intent at slaughter doesn't affect other rites
B. Forbidden — intent from one rite affects another
C. Permitted — we don't derive outside-Temple from inside
D. Forbidden — but only if the blood was actually sprinkled
Correct: B — R' Yochanan holds that one CAN have intent from one rite (shechita) affect another (zerika), and we derive outside-Temple halakhot from piggul laws inside the Temple. The animal is forbidden even if the blood was never sprinkled.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say BOTH disputes (idol worship and shelo lishmo) between R' Yochanan and Reish Lakish are necessary?
A. They involve different animals
B. Each case contains a chiddush the other couldn't teach
C. One is d'oraita and the other d'rabbanan
D. They were taught by different Amoraim
Correct: B — If only idol worship were taught, we'd think Reish Lakish only disagrees because of outside-vs-inside Temple. If only shelo lishmo were taught, we'd think R' Yochanan only rules strictly inside the Temple. Each has a unique chiddush.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rav Ashi reject the kal vachomer from chutz limkomo (outside designated area) to shelo lishmo?
A. Chutz limkomo carries karet
B. Chutz limkomo applies to ALL offerings, but shelo lishmo only to Pesach and chatat
C. Chutz limkomo is only d'rabbanan
D. Chutz limkomo doesn't disqualify across rites
Correct: B — Rav Ashi objects that chutz limkomo disqualifies ALL slaughtered offerings, whereas shelo lishmo only disqualifies a Paschal offering and a sin offering. This asymmetry breaks the kal vachomer.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does R' Yehoshua reject R' Eliezer's proof that an asham shelo lishmo is unfit from the word 'הוא'?
A. The word הוא doesn't appear for asham at all
B. For asham, הוא appears only after burning the fats, not at shechita
C. The word הוא only applies to chatat
D. R' Yehoshua doesn't accept derash from individual words
Correct: B — R' Yehoshua distinguishes: for chatat and Pesach, 'הוא' is stated at shechita. For asham, it appears only after burning sacrificial portions — and since the offering is valid even without burning, the word cannot disqualify the asham.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why can't we derive that an asham shelo lishmo is pasul from the common element (tzad hashaveh) of chatat and Pesach together?
A. Chatat and Pesach have different blood placements
B. Both chatat and Pesach share a karet element that asham lacks
C. The Torah explicitly permits asham shelo lishmo
D. Chatat and Pesach are both brought on specific dates
Correct: B — The Gemara answers that chatat (atones for karet-level sins) and Pesach (failure to bring carries karet) both share an element of karet. Since asham has no karet connection, the common-element derivation fails.
