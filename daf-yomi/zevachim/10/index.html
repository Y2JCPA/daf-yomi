Intent Across Rites & Deriving Halakhot

R' Yochanan
If one slaughters intending to sprinkle blood for idolatry — the animal is forbidden. We derive outside-Temple laws from inside-Temple laws (piggul).
Reish Lakish
The animal is permitted. One cannot have intent from one rite affect another, and we do not derive outside from inside the Temple.
Gemara
Both disputes — idol worship and shelo lishmo — are necessary. Each case has a chiddush that couldn't be derived from the other.
Core Principle Can machshava (intent) during one sacrificial service (e.g., shechita) disqualify based on a different service (e.g., zerika)?

R' Yochanan vs Reish Lakish: Intent Across Rites

R' Yochanan

  • Intent during shechita for zerika to idolatry → animal is FORBIDDEN
  • One CAN have machshava from one rite affect another
  • We DERIVE outside-Temple halakhot from inside-Temple (piggul)
  • Same logic: shechita with intent to sprinkle shelo lishmo → PASUL

Reish Lakish

  • Intent during shechita for zerika to idolatry → animal is PERMITTED
  • One CANNOT have machshava from one rite affect another
  • We do NOT derive outside-Temple from inside-Temple laws
  • Shechita with intent to sprinkle shelo lishmo → KASHER
Tzrikhuta Both cases (idol worship & shelo lishmo) must be stated — neither could be derived from the other alone.

Rav Yirmeya's Kal VaChomer & Objections

Rav Yirmeya
Kal vachomer supporting R' Yochanan: If intent regarding time (chutz lizmano) disqualifies across rites — shelo lishmo (which is stricter at the slaughter stage) should certainly disqualify across rites!
Rava bar Ahilai
Objection: Chutz lizmano has its own stringency — it carries karet! You can't say shelo lishmo is automatically stricter.
Rava bar Ahilai
Correction: The kal vachomer should be from chutz limkomo (outside designated area) — which has no karet.
Rav Ashi
Objection: Chutz limkomo applies to all offerings, but shelo lishmo only disqualifies Pesach and Chatat!
Rav Ashi
Better source: Derive from shinui ba'alim (deviation of owner) — it's fit at slaughter but unfit at zerika, just like shelo lishmo.
Final Kal VaChomer If shinui ba'alim — where saying 'for so-and-so' at slaughter is fit — still disqualifies at zerika, then shelo lishmo — where even slaughter itself is unfit — certainly disqualifies at zerika.

R' Eliezer vs R' Yehoshua: Is Asham Shelo Lishmo Unfit?

R' Eliezer
The word "הוא" (it) appears for chatat, Pesach, AND asham. In each case: for its sake = fit; not for its sake = unfit.
R' Yehoshua
For chatat and Pesach, "הוא" is stated at shechita. But for asham, "הוא" appears only after burning the fats — and since an offering is valid even without burning, the asham is fit.
R' Eliezer
But the verse says: "As is the chatat, so is the asham" (Lev. 7:7) — they are juxtaposed! Just as chatat shelo lishmo is pasul, so too the asham!
Gemara
R' Yehoshua keeps finding distinctions between chatat and asham to block the comparison: blood placement, karet, number of applications…
The Word הוא The restrictive word "it" in sacrificial verses teaches: specifically this offering, for its own sake — otherwise unfit. But where the word appears matters.
