Zevachim 7 — Offerings, Intent & Atonement

חטאת מכפרת על מי, עולה לאחר דורון
DAF OVERVIEW This daf examines when offerings atone for sins committed after designation, debates whether a todah slaughtered for another's todah is valid, presents Rava's framework distinguishing sin offerings by wrong type vs. wrong owner, identifies the burnt offering as a gift (doron) rather than essential atonement, and derives the Paschal offering's lishmah requirement from Torah verses.

Can Offerings Atone for Post-Designation Sins?

🐐 Shavuot goats slaughtered simultaneously, blood in two cups
↓
Blood of first goat sprinkled
↓
❓ For what sin does the second goat atone?
↓
R' Zeira's Answer
It atones for impurity occurring between the two sprinklings
Inference
Post-designation atonement was obvious to R' Yirmeya — he only asked about post-slaughter
↓
❌ Gemara rejects: Perhaps R' Yirmeya was unsure about both — post-designation and post-slaughter
KEY QUESTION R' Yirmeya's dilemma: Does an offering atone only for sins before slaughter, or even for sins between slaughter and sprinkling?

Todah Slaughtered for Another's Todah

Rabba
A todah slaughtered for the sake of another person's todah is fit — it was slaughtered as a todah!
Rav Ḥisda
It is unfit — it must be slaughtered for the sake of his own shelamim (peace offering).
Rabba
Proof from Abba Ḥanin: A todah for a shelamim = fit, but a shelamim for a todah = unfit. Because a todah is called a shelamim, but not vice versa.
Gemara
Perhaps the baraita means another todah of his own — designated for a different deliverance — not another person's todah.
Gemara
But if another's todah were truly unfit, the baraita should have taught that case as the stronger example!
Gemara
The baraita needed to specify shelamim-for-todah to teach that a shelamim is NOT called a todah, despite the reverse being true.
PRINCIPLE A todah is called a shelamim (תודה נקראת שלמים), but a shelamim is NOT called a todah — this asymmetry drives the halakhic distinction.

Rava's Framework: Sin Offering Deviations

ScenarioType DeviationOwner DeviationStatus
Chatat slaughtered for another chatat✅ Still a chatat—Fit (כשרה)
Chatat slaughtered for an olah❌ Wrong type—Unfit (פסולה)
Chatat slaughtered for another person who owes a chatat—❌ Wrong owner, same obligationUnfit — 'he shall be forgiven' = specifically the owner
Chatat slaughtered for another person who owes an olah—Different obligation typeFit — the other person is not comparable to the owner
SOURCE "ושחט אותה לחטאת" — as long as it's slaughtered as a chatat, even another chatat, it's valid. "ונסלח לו" — specifically he, the owner, not another who also needs a chatat.

Burnt Offering (Olah) as Gift — Rava's Proof

Without Repentance

  • Cannot sacrifice at all
  • "The sacrifice of the wicked is an abomination" (Proverbs 21:27)
  • Repentance is a prerequisite

With Repentance

  • God forgives immediately — before he even moves from his place
  • So why bring an olah at all?
  • Conclusion: The olah is a GIFT (דורון) — appeasement after forgiveness
R' SHIMON'S MASHAL Why does a chatat come before an olah? Like an advocate (פרקליט) who enters before the king to secure a pardon — once he succeeds, the gift is brought in after him.

Rava on Burnt Offerings of Heirs

👤 Owner dies, leaving a consecrated olah
↓
👥 Heirs are responsible to bring the offering
↓
❌ Slaughtered with wrong type (shinui kodesh) → Unfit — must bring another
↓
✅ Slaughtered for wrong owner (shinui ba'alim) → Fit — the offering has no legal owner after death (Rava)
↓
⚡ Rav Pineḥas b. R' Ami disagrees: The heir IS the owner — wrong-owner deviation disqualifies
↓
❓ Rav Ashi clarifies: Does Rav Pineḥas mean the heir is fully the owner, or merely that the olah atones for the heir's own sins?
↓
✔️ Rav Pineḥas: The heir is actually the owner — must bring another if slaughtered for someone else
DISPUTE Rava: After death, an olah has no owner — shinui ba'alim cannot apply. Rav Pineḥas: The heir inherits full ownership status.

📋 Zevachim 7 — Summary Map

🐐 Post-Designation Atonement
Can offerings atone for sins after designation? Shavuot goats suggest yes, but Gemara leaves it unresolved.
↓
🙏 Todah for Another's Todah
Rabba: Fit (still a todah). Rav Ḥisda: Unfit (must be for his own). Key: todah is called shelamim, not vice versa.
↓
⚖️ Chatat Rules
Wrong type (another chatat) = Fit
Wrong type (olah) = Unfit
Wrong owner (owes chatat) = Unfit
Wrong owner (owes olah) = Fit
🎁 Olah = Doron
Burnt offering is a gift to God after forgiveness.
Chatat = advocate (פרקליט)
Olah = gift brought after pardon
↓
📜 Korban Pesach
Three verses derive: (1) lishmah for type, (2) lishmah for owner, (3) both are indispensable — unique to the Paschal offering.
