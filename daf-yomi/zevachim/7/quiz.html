Zevachim 7 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Zeira, for what does the second Shavuot goat atone when both are slaughtered simultaneously?
A. Impurity occurring between the two sprinklings
B. Sins committed before the designation of both goats
C. Intentional sins from the previous year
D. It serves as a backup in case the first goat is disqualified
Correct: A — R' Zeira answered that it atones for any incident of impurity (tumah) that occurred between the sprinkling of the blood of the first goat and the sprinkling of the second.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rav Ḥisda hold that a todah slaughtered for the sake of another person's todah is unfit?
A. A todah can only atone for the person who designated it
B. It must be slaughtered for the sake of his own shelamim
C. Only a Kohen Gadol may transfer todah obligations
D. The loaves accompanying the todah would be invalidated
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda holds that a todah must be slaughtered for the sake of its owner's own peace offering (shelamim), not for another person's todah.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava, a chatat slaughtered for a person who owes an olah (not a chatat) is fit. Why?
A. Because 'he shall be forgiven' only excludes someone with the same type of obligation
B. Because an olah is a higher-level offering than a chatat
C. Because the Torah permits transferring chatat obligations freely
D. Because the other person's olah obligation has already been fulfilled
Correct: A — Rava derives from 'venislach lo' (he shall be forgiven) that the disqualification for wrong owner applies only when the other person is comparable to the owner — i.e., also obligated in a chatat. One who owes an olah is not comparable.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rava's proof that the olah (burnt offering) is a 'doron' (gift) rather than essential atonement?
A. The Torah never mentions atonement in connection with the olah
B. If one repents, God forgives immediately — so the olah must serve another purpose
C. The olah is always brought after the chatat, proving it is secondary
D. The olah does not require semicha (leaning of hands)
Correct: B — Rava reasons: Without repentance, one cannot bring a sacrifice at all. With repentance, God forgives immediately. So the olah must be a gift (doron) brought to appease God after forgiveness is already granted.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara derive that performing the Paschal offering lishmah (for its sake) is indispensable (me'akev)?
A. From Exodus 12:27 mentioning 'zevach' (sacrifice)
B. From a kal vachomer from the sin offering
C. From the repetition in Deuteronomy 16:2 — 'vezavachta' (and you shall sacrifice)
D. From the principle that all offerings require lishmah
Correct: C — The first verse (Deut. 16:1) teaches the mitzvah of lishmah for type; Exodus 12:27 teaches lishmah for owner. The repetition in Deuteronomy 16:2 ('vezavachta') teaches that both requirements are indispensable.
