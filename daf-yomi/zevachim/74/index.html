Core Question: Can We Assume the Lost Item Is the Prohibited One?

A prohibited item is mixed with permitted items
↓
One item separates or is lost from the mixture
↓
Can we say the lost item was the prohibited one?
Rav / Reish Lakish
Yes — we assume the prohibited item is the one that left
Shmuel (re: idolatry)
No — idol worship's uncertainty remains forever
KEY THEME This daf explores whether separation from a mixture can retroactively resolve the prohibited status of the remaining items.

Rav's Ring Ruling & Rava's Challenge

Rav (via Rav Naḥman)
An idol worship ring mixed with 100 permitted rings — if one falls into the Great Sea, all are permitted. We assume the lost one was prohibited.
Rava
But the Mishna says offerings mixed with prohibited animals — even 1 in 10,000 — all must die! Why not say the first to die was the prohibited one?
Rav Naḥman
Rav follows R' Eliezer, who says if one head was already sacrificed, we assume it was the disqualified one, and all the rest may be offered.
Rava
But R' Elazar says R' Eliezer only permits them two by two — not one by one! How can Rav permit rings without qualification?
Rav Naḥman
Indeed — Rav also means the rings may only be sold two at a time, ensuring at least one is certainly permitted.
PRINCIPLE Even when we assume the lost item is prohibited, we still require safeguards — selling two at a time guarantees at least one is permitted.

Rav's 40/60 Split: Which Group Bears the Prohibition?

1 idol ring mixed with 100 permitted rings
↓
40 separate to one place, 60 to another
↓
Which group is assumed to contain the prohibited ring?
Group of 40
If all 40 mix with other rings → permitted (prohibition follows the majority — the 60)
Group of 60
If all 60 mix with other rings → prohibited (the prohibited ring is assumed to be here)
NOTE Rav's ruling applies only when the entire subgroup separates — not when a single ring leaves a group, as the majority principle wouldn't apply the same way.

Shmuel vs. The Baraita: Compound Uncertainty (Sfek Sfeka)

Shmuel

  • Idol worship is uniquely stringent
  • Its uncertainty and compound uncertainty are prohibited forever
  • No amount of additional mixtures can dilute the prohibition
  • Disregard Rav's leniency regarding the 40/60 split

Baraita (Objection)

  • A cup of idol worship falls into a storeroom — all prohibited (first uncertainty)
  • One cup separates and falls into 10,000 cups — still uncertain
  • From those 10,000, one falls into another 10,000 — permitted
  • Compound uncertainty of idol worship is permitted!
RESOLUTION The Gemara must reconcile Shmuel's blanket stringency with this baraita that explicitly permits compound uncertainties of idol worship.

Reish Lakish: The Teruma Barrel in the Dead Sea

Reish Lakish
A sealed teruma barrel mixed with 100 non-sacred barrels cannot be nullified — sealed barrels are significant (davar she-b'minyan).
Reish Lakish
But if one barrel falls into the Dead Sea, all remaining barrels are permitted — we assume the one that fell was the teruma barrel.
Gemara
Why do we need both Rav Naḥman's ring ruling and Reish Lakish's barrel ruling?
Gemara
From the ring case alone, I'd think leniency applies only to idol worship items which have no permitting factors. Teruma can be sold to a Kohen!
Gemara
From the barrel case alone, I'd think leniency applies only when the falling is noticeable. A ring's falling is not noticeable. Both are needed.
DISTINCTION Each ruling teaches a unique chiddush: the ring case — even when there are no permitting factors; the barrel case — even when permitting factors exist.

Rabba vs. Rav Yosef: Does Size Matter for Separation?

PositionBarrel Falls?Fig Falls?Reasoning
Rabba✅ Remaining items permitted❌ Remaining items NOT permittedA barrel's fall is noticeable; a fig's fall is not — people won't know it was permitted
Rav Yosef✅ Remaining items permitted✅ Remaining items permittedJust as entering the mixture prohibited everything, leaving the mixture permits everything — regardless of size
CORE DISPUTE Is the leniency based on publicizing the separation (Rabba), or on the symmetric logic of entering and leaving a mixture (Rav Yosef)?
