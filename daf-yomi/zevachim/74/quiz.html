Zevachim 74 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav, if an idol worship ring falls into the Great Sea from a mixture of 101 rings, why are the rest permitted?
A. We assume the ring that fell was the prohibited one
B. The sea water nullifies the prohibition
C. Once mixed, the prohibition is automatically canceled
D. The remaining 100 rings constitute a majority
Correct: A — Rav's principle is that we assume the item that separated from the mixture was the prohibited one, thereby permitting the rest.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rav Naḥman say the permitted rings must be sold two at a time?
A. To follow R' Eliezer, who requires pairs so at least one is certainly permitted
B. Because each ring is worth half the normal price
C. To prevent the buyer from using it for idol worship
D. Because Shmuel required it as an additional stringency
Correct: A — R' Eliezer (whose opinion Rav follows) only permits items from a mixture in pairs, ensuring that at least one of any two is certainly permitted.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the key difference between Rabba and Rav Yosef regarding Reish Lakish's ruling?
A. Rabba limits the leniency to noticeable items (barrels) while Rav Yosef extends it even to small items (figs)
B. Rabba applies it to teruma and Rav Yosef applies it to idol worship
C. Rabba requires two items to fall and Rav Yosef requires only one
D. Rabba says it works ab initio and Rav Yosef says only after the fact
Correct: A — Rabba holds the leniency works only when the separation is noticeable (like a barrel), so people know why the rest became permitted. Rav Yosef says the logic applies symmetrically regardless of size.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara need BOTH the ring ruling (Rav Naḥman) and the barrel ruling (Reish Lakish)?
A. One teaches leniency where there are no permitting factors; the other where permitting factors exist
B. One applies to Eretz Yisrael and the other to Babylonia
C. One is for liquids and one is for solids
D. One applies ab initio and the other only after the fact
Correct: A — The ring case (idol worship) has no permitting factors, and the barrel case (teruma) has permitting factors. Without both rulings, we might think the leniency only applies in one type of case.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In Rav's 40/60 split ruling, why does the group of 40 NOT render other rings prohibited when re-mixed?
A. We follow the majority — the prohibited ring is presumed to be in the larger group of 60
B. 40 is less than the required ratio for nullification
C. The 40 rings were purified by their separation
D. Rav holds that any group under 50 is automatically permitted
Correct: A — Rav applies the principle of majority (rov): since 60 is the majority, we presume the prohibited ring is among the 60, making the group of 40 entirely permitted items.
