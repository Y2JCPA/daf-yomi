Dilemma: Offering Partially Outside the Courtyard

R' Elazar
If the majority of a limb of a burned offering exited the courtyard, but the majority of the animal did not — do we follow the limb or the animal?
Gemara
The dilemma of R' Elazar shall stand unresolved (teiku).
Rabba bar Rav Huna
What if 5 people carry an offering — 3 have left and 2 remain inside? Do we follow the majority of carriers or the animal?
Gemara
This dilemma also shall stand unresolved.
TWO TEIKUS Both versions — following the limb vs. the animal, and following the carriers vs. the animal — remain unresolved.

Bulls/goats that are burned leave the Temple courtyard
↓
Then they are returned inside the courtyard
↓
Do garments of those who now carry them become tamei?
↓
Possibility 1
Once they left, they are permanently tamei — garments impure
Possibility 2
Once returned, status reverts — garments not impure
↓
R' Abba bar Memel brings proof from the Mishna: latter carriers still inside are tahor — implying return cancels tumah
↓
Ravina rejects: The verse requires leaving the camp first — those inside simply haven't met the verse's condition yet

R' Meir vs. Rabbis: Impurity of Offerings to Food & Drink

R' Meir

  • Bulls that are burned → transmit tumah to food & drink
  • Red heifer (parah adumah) → transmit tumah to food & drink
  • Scapegoat (sa'ir la'azazel) → transmit tumah to food & drink
  • Based on school of R' Yishmael: sources of human tumah don't need liquid susceptibility
  • All three render people tamei → all transmit to food

Rabbis (Chakhamim)

  • Bulls that are burned → DO transmit tumah to food & drink
  • Red heifer → DOES transmit tumah to food & drink
  • Scapegoat → does NOT transmit tumah at all
  • A living animal cannot render food/drink tamei
  • Bulls & red heifer must first contract tumah from elsewhere
KEY DIFFERENCE R' Meir applies R' Yishmael's principle broadly — any source of human tumah automatically transmits to food. The Rabbis require the animal to first contract tumah, and since the scapegoat is alive, it cannot.

The School of R' Yishmael's Principle

וְכִי יִפֹּל מִנִּבְלָתָם עַל כׇּל זֶרַע זֵרוּעַ אֲשֶׁר יִזָּרֵעַ טָהוֹר הוּא (ויקרא יא:לז)
FOUNDATIONAL RULE From Leviticus 11:37–38 (seeds and water): Just as seeds — which can never become tamei enough to transmit to humans — need hechsher (liquid contact) to become susceptible to tumah, so too all items that cannot transmit tumah to humans need hechsher.

This excludes the carcass of a kosher bird (neveilat of tahor), which can transmit tumah to a person who swallows it — and therefore does not need hechsher to transmit tumah to food.
