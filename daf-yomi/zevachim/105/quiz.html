Zevachim 105 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
In the dilemma about 5 people carrying an offering where 3 have exited and 2 remain — what is the unresolved question?
A. Do we follow the majority of carriers (who left) or the majority of the animal (still inside)?
B. Are the 3 who left permitted to re-enter the courtyard?
C. Must all 5 carriers wash their garments or only the 3 who exited?
D. Does the offering become pasul once the majority of carriers exit?
Correct: A — Rabba bar Rav Huna's dilemma is whether we follow the majority of people (3 outside) or the animal (majority still inside) to determine the halakhic status of the offering's exit.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why do the Rabbis hold that the scapegoat does NOT transmit impurity to food and drink?
A. Because the scapegoat is sent to Azazel, not burned
B. Because a living animal cannot render food and drink impure
C. Because the scapegoat never leaves the Temple courtyard
D. Because the Torah does not mention garment impurity for the scapegoat's sender
Correct: B — The Rabbis hold that a living being (chai) cannot transmit tumah to food and drink. Unlike the bulls and red heifer which are dead when burned, the scapegoat is alive when sent away.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Zeira, what determines whether a source of impurity is treated as an av hatumah (primary source) with countable degrees?
A. Whether it is mentioned explicitly in the Torah
B. Whether it has been rendered susceptible by liquid contact
C. Whether it can render a person tamei through physical contact
D. Whether it originated from the Temple service
Correct: C — R' Zeira establishes the principle: if an item can make a person tamei through contact, it is an av hatumah and we count first/second degrees. If not (like a kosher bird carcass which only transmits by swallowing), it is treated as first-degree food tumah.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Ravina reject R' Abba bar Memel's proof that returning an offering to the courtyard cancels its tumah status?
A. Because the Mishna is discussing a different type of offering
B. Because the verse requires leaving the camp before impurity applies — the rear carriers simply haven't left yet
C. Because the offering was already burned and cannot return
D. Because R' Abba bar Memel misquoted the Mishna
Correct: B — Ravina explains that the verse 'and afterward he may come into the camp' (Lev 16:28) requires first leaving the camp. The rear carriers aren't tahor because the offering returned — they simply haven't yet left, so the verse's condition hasn't been met.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the practical difference between R' Meir and the Rabbis regarding bulls that are burned and the red heifer?
A. R' Meir says they transmit tumah to food; the Rabbis say they do not
B. Both agree they transmit tumah to food, but the Rabbis require them to first contract tumah from an external source
C. R' Meir requires hechsher for them; the Rabbis do not
D. R' Meir says they are burned inside the courtyard; the Rabbis say outside
Correct: B — Both R' Meir and the Rabbis agree that bulls and the red heifer transmit tumah to food and drink. The difference is that R' Meir (via R' Yishmael's principle) says they do so inherently, while the Rabbis say they must first contract tumah from another source (as Rav Dimi reports from Eretz Yisrael).
