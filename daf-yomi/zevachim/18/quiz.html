Zevachim 18 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What hermeneutic method links the disqualification of wine-drinkers, vestment-lackers, and unwashed priests?
A. Kal va-chomer (a fortiori)
B. Gezeirah shavah (verbal analogy) from the word "statute"
C. Hekesh (juxtaposition)
D. Binyan av (paradigm case)
Correct: B — The word "חוקה" (statute) appears in the passages about wine (Lev. 10:9), vestments (Ex. 28:43), and washing (Ex. 30:21), creating a gezeirah shavah that links all three.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say the verse "that you may put difference between the holy and the common" is still needed?
A. To teach that non-priests may not serve at all
B. To teach Rav's practice — one who drank wine should not issue halakhic rulings
C. To teach that vestments must be the correct color
D. To teach that wine is forbidden on Shabbat
Correct: B — Once the gezeirah shavah derives the Temple service disqualification, the verse is needed for a separate halakha — as demonstrated by Rav, who would not lecture publicly after drinking wine on Festivals.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
An ordinary priest wore all eight High Priestly vestments and performed a service. According to the Gemara, what is the status of his service?
A. Valid, since he wore more than required
B. Disqualified — excess vestments invalidate just like lacking vestments
C. Valid, but he receives lashes
D. Disqualified only for atonement-essential rites
Correct: B — Leviticus 1:8 teaches that "the priests" serve in their priestly state — an ordinary priest in High Priestly vestments has excess vestments, which disqualifies service just as lacking vestments does.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Shmuel, what is the law if priestly vestments drag on the ground?
A. The service is disqualified
B. The service is valid
C. The service is valid only if the priest uses a belt
D. The service is valid only for non-essential rites
Correct: B — Shmuel rules that dragging vestments are fit, but raised vestments (that don't reach the feet) are unfit. Rav is stricter — both dragging and raised vestments are unfit.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The Gemara discusses whether a five-cornered garment requires tzitzit. What is the conclusion regarding the principle of "extra material"?
A. Everyone agrees extra material is as if it doesn't exist
B. Everyone agrees extra material does exist — the dispute is about a specific verse
C. The matter is left unresolved (teiku)
D. Only Rav holds that extra material exists
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that everyone agrees an extra piece is considered as though it exists. The tanna who obligates a five-cornered garment in tzitzit does so only because the verse "with which you cover yourself" specifically amplifies the law, not because he ignores the extra corner.
