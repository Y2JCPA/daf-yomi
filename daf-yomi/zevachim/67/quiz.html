5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is R' Yehoshua's key distinction that ultimately silences R' Eliezer in the baraita?
A. The bird sin offering is eaten entirely by priests
B. When designation, location, AND procedure are all changed, it is a fundamentally different case
C. Bird offerings are never subject to me'ilah
D. The guilt offering proof is irrelevant because it uses shechitah, not melikah
Correct: B — R' Yehoshua argues that changing all three factors — designation, location, and procedure — distinguishes the bird olah→chatat case from all of R' Eliezer's animal-offering proofs, where at most two factors were changed.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Adda bar Ahava, what happens to a bird burnt offering sacrificed below the red line as a sin offering?
A. It remains a burnt offering and me'ilah still applies
B. It is completely disqualified and must be discarded
C. After pinching one siman, it loses olah status and becomes a chatat
D. It counts as neither an olah nor a chatat
Correct: C — Rav Adda bar Ahava explains that once one siman (gullet or windpipe) is pinched, the bird burnt offering is removed from its olah status and becomes a sin offering.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rava say R' Eliezer stopped responding to R' Yehoshua?
A. R' Eliezer ran out of proof texts
B. R' Eliezer accepted R' Yehoshua's reasoning about the transformation after pinching
C. The debate was interrupted by other sages
D. R' Eliezer conceded that me'ilah never applies to bird offerings
Correct: B — Rava notes that R' Eliezer could have brought another proof (guilt offering with ownership change), but didn't — indicating he grasped R' Yehoshua's deeper principle as explained by Rav Adda bar Ahava.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the Kinnim challenge about two women's confused birds, why doesn't Rav Adda's principle apply to validate the offerings?
A. The birds were of different species
B. The principle only applies to one person's offerings, not across different owners
C. Women's offerings are subject to different rules
D. The priest did not perform melikah correctly
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes: R' Yehoshua's principle of olah→chatat transformation applies only to one person's offering re-designated, not when two different people's offerings are confused.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
A woman who vowed a nest for a male child and gave birth must bring 4 birds total. What is the correct distribution?
A. 2 above the red line and 2 below
B. 3 above the red line and 1 below
C. 4 above the red line
D. 1 above the red line and 3 below
Correct: B — The obligatory pair requires 1 olah (above) + 1 chatat (below), and the vow pair requires 2 olot (both above). Total: 3 above, 1 below.
