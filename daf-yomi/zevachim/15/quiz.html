Zevachim 15 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the Gemara's final conclusion about blood conveyed improperly (not by foot)?
A. It permanently disqualifies the offering
B. It can be corrected by reconveying properly
C. It depends on whether it was conveyed toward or away from the altar
D. It is a dispute left unresolved (teiku)
Correct: B — The Gemara reaches a conclusive refutation (teyuvta) of Ulla/R' Yochanan's position, ruling that improperly conveyed blood CAN be corrected.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did the scholars of Eretz Yisrael 'laugh' at the distinction between greater and lesser conveying?
A. Because it contradicts a mishna about bird offerings where all conveying is by hand
B. Because R' Shimon never made such a distinction
C. Because lesser conveying doesn't exist in practice
D. Because the altar was too far away for hand conveying
Correct: A — A bird sin offering (chatat ha'of) is pinched and sprinkled on the altar directly — all conveying is by hand. Under the proposed distinction, R' Shimon could never disqualify it through intent, which seems untenable.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In R' Yirmeya's scenario, if a priest's hand is severed mid-sprinkling before the blood reaches the altar, what is the ruling?
A. Valid — the blood left while the priest was whole
B. Not valid — the blood must reach the altar while conditions hold
C. Valid — we follow the moment of the act, not the result
D. It depends on whether the priest intended to complete the sprinkling
Correct: B — R' Zeira rules it invalid. 'And sprinkle' must be fulfilled like 'and place upon the corners' — the blood must actually reach the altar. Conditions must hold throughout the entire process.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rava, what is the core dispute between R' Eliezer and the Rabbis?
A. Whether conveying by hand counts as real conveying
B. Whether blood taken outside and brought back in counts as 'necessary' conveying
C. Whether a non-priest can convey blood
D. Whether spilled blood can be recollected
Correct: B — Rava explains: all agree collected outside→inside is necessary and inside→outside is unnecessary. They dispute the case of blood brought in, taken out, and brought back — is the return trip 'necessary' conveying (Rabbis) or not (R' Eliezer)?
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the scriptural source that a non-priest who performs sacrificial rites disqualifies the offering?
A. 'Any stranger who approaches shall be put to death' (Numbers 18:7)
B. 'Speak to Aharon and his sons, that they separate themselves from the sacred items of bnei Yisrael' (Leviticus 22:2)
C. 'You shall not do any work' (Leviticus 23:3)
D. 'No stranger shall eat of the holy thing' (Leviticus 22:10)
Correct: B — Levi derives from Leviticus 22:2 that 'bnei Yisrael' (non-priests) must separate from performing sacrificial rites, so they do not profane/disqualify the offerings.
