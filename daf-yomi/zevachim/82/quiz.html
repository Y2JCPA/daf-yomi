QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Akiva, what is the scope of disqualification when blood is brought into the Sanctuary?
A. Only sin offerings are disqualified
B. Only kodshei kodashim (most sacred offerings) are disqualified
C. All offerings — both kodshei kodashim and kodashim kalim — are disqualified
D. Only communal sin offerings are disqualified
Correct: C — Rabbi Akiva derives from 'kol' (any) that kodshei kodashim are included, and from 'v'khol' (and any) that kodashim kalim are also included — extending the disqualification to ALL offerings.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rav Huna son of Rav Yehoshua reject Shmuel's wine parable?
A. The parable involves a student, not a teacher
B. Since scouring/rinsing already applies to all offerings, the passage's context is already broad — so mentioning 'sin offering' should limit, not expand
C. The parable contradicts Rabbi Akiva's position
D. Shmuel was not a reliable authority on this matter
Correct: B — Rav Huna argues that since the passage already applies broadly (re: scouring/rinsing of all offerings), mentioning 'sin offering' should narrow the scope, not expand it — like a teacher who previously used both hot and cold water now specifying only hot.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What happens to meat of an offering that is brought inside the Sanctuary?
A. It is disqualified, just like blood
B. It is not disqualified — the verse specifies 'blood,' not meat
C. It must be burned immediately
D. It becomes permitted for non-priests to eat
Correct: B — The baraita teaches that meat entering the Sanctuary is NOT disqualified, because the verse specifically mentions 'blood' being brought inside. This is the opposite of meat going OUTSIDE its boundary, which IS disqualified.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rava interpret the phrase 'sacred place within' (kodesh penimah) in Leviticus 10:18?
A. 'Sacred place' and 'within' both refer to the Holy of Holies
B. 'Sacred place' refers to the courtyard; 'within' refers to the Sanctuary
C. 'Sacred place' refers to the Sanctuary (Heikhal); 'within' refers to the Holy of Holies
D. 'Sacred place within' is a single term referring to the entire Temple complex
Correct: C — Rava explains that each word teaches independently: 'sacred place' (kodesh) = the Sanctuary/Heikhal, and 'within' (penimah) = the Holy of Holies. Blood entering either location disqualifies the offering.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yosei HaGelili, what does Leviticus 6:23 actually discuss?
A. All offerings whose blood is improperly brought inside
B. Only the standard individual sin offering
C. Bulls and goats that are burned — inner sin offerings whose blood is supposed to go inside
D. Peace offerings and guilt offerings exclusively
Correct: C — Rabbi Yosei HaGelili holds that this verse does not address standard sin offerings improperly brought inside. Rather, it speaks of the bulls and goats that are burned (parim ha-nisrafim and se'irim ha-nisrafim) — inner offerings whose blood properly enters the Sanctuary.
