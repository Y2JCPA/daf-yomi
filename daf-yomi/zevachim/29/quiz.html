Zevachim 29 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yannai, what happens when a priest mixes piggul intent (beyond time) with outside-area intent in the same offering?
A. The offering is piggul and karet applies
B. The offering is disqualified but not piggul — intentions displace each other
C. The offering remains valid since the intents cancel out
D. Only the first intent expressed takes effect
Correct: B — R' Yannai derives from 'lo yeḥashev' that intentions displace one another — mixing other improper intentions with piggul intent prevents the offering from being rendered piggul, though it is still disqualified.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the singular 'it shall be' (rather than 'they shall be') in Leviticus 7:18 teach?
A. Only one offering can be piggul at a time
B. Half a k'zayit beyond time and half a k'zayit outside area combine to disqualify
C. The offering can only be disqualified by one priest
D. Piggul applies only to individual offerings, not communal ones
Correct: B — The singular form teaches that half an olive-bulk of beyond-time intent and half an olive-bulk of outside-area intent join together to constitute the minimum measure needed to disqualify the offering.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does R' Akiva argue that retroactive disqualification of an offering is possible?
A. From the principle that all Torah prohibitions apply retroactively
B. From the analogy to a zav/zava whose purity is retroactively negated by a new discharge
C. From a gezeira shava between 'third day' and 'third year'
D. From the rule that intent follows the final act
Correct: B — R' Akiva cites the case of a zav or zava who has presumptive purity after immersing, but a new discharge the same day retroactively negates their purity and renders impure everything they touched.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Mishna, what is the critical condition for piggul to carry karet liability?
A. The offering must be a voluntary sacrifice
B. The intent must be expressed verbally
C. The permitting factor (blood) must be sacrificed in accordance with its mitzva
D. At least two of the four blood services must have beyond-time intent
Correct: C — The Mishna states explicitly that piggul with karet applies only 'provided that the permitting factor is sacrificed in accordance with its mitzva' — meaning no competing outside-area intent corrupted any of the blood services.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Ben Azzai, what does the word 'it' in Leviticus 7:18 exclude?
A. Communal offerings from piggul liability
B. Sin offerings from the piggul rule
C. Offerings brought late from a delayed vow from 'it shall not be accepted'
D. Offerings of non-priests from being disqualified
Correct: C — Ben Azzai teaches that 'it' — specifically the piggul offering — is included in 'it shall not be accepted,' but an offering from someone who was merely late in fulfilling a vow is not included and remains valid.
