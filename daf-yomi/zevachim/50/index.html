Can Hekesh-Derived Law Teach Via Binyan Av?

R' Yirmeya
A hekesh-derived law cannot teach via binyan av. Proof: Why does the Torah explicitly state that a guilt offering (asham) must be slaughtered in the north?
R' Yirmeya
If hekesh could teach via binyan av, we could derive the asham's northern slaughter from the sin offering (chatat) — both are kodshei kodashim!
Gemara
Challenge: Why not derive asham from the burnt offering (olah) instead? Because olah has a unique feature — it is entirely burned (kalil).
Gemara
Similarly, chatat has a unique feature — it atones for sins punishable by karet. So neither alone can serve as source!
Gemara
Perhaps derive from two sources together? But each pair also has a unique common feature that the third lacks. No proof from here.
Key Refutation Every attempted binyan av is refuted because each source offering has a distinctive stringency (pirka) not shared by the target.

Why Can't We Derive Asham's Northern Slaughter?

Derive Asham From…Refutation (Pirka)
Olah aloneOlah is entirely burned on the altar (kalil)
Chatat aloneChatat atones for sins of karet
Chatat + Asham → OlahBoth chatat & asham atone for transgressions; olah does not
Olah + Asham → ChatatBoth olah & asham are male; individual chatat is female
Olah + Chatat → AshamBoth olah & chatat can be communal; asham is individual only
Result Every binyan av combination fails — each has a unique refutable feature (mah l'X she'ken…). The Torah must state northern slaughter for each independently.

Can Gezeirah Shavah Teach Via Hekesh?

Rav Pappa
Yes. The Torah juxtaposes todah (thanks offering) to shelamim (peace offering) in Leviticus 7:11-12.
Rav Pappa
We know shelamim can be purchased with ma'aser sheni money — derived via gezeirah shavah (the word "sham/there" in Deut. 27:7 and 14:23).
Rav Pappa
Through the hekesh of todah to shelamim, we learn todah can also be purchased with ma'aser sheni money. GS taught → Hekesh derived!
Mar Zutra b. Rav Mari
Objection: Ma'aser sheni is merely chullin (non-sacred). This doesn't prove the principle works in the realm of hekdesh (consecrated matters)!
Ravina
Response: The restriction applies whenever the derived matter is hekdesh — regardless of whether the teaching matter is hekdesh. Since shelamim is hekdesh, the proof stands.
Dispute Rav Pappa and Mar Zutra disagree whether this proof is valid for consecrated matters. This dispute affects several later derivations on the daf.

Rav Pappa's Chain: GS → Hekesh

Deut. 27:7 — "You shall sacrifice shelamim and eat there"
Deut. 14:23 — "You shall eat ma'aser sheni there"
↓
Gezeirah Shavah on "sham/there"
→ Shelamim can be bought with ma'aser sheni money
↓
Lev. 7:11-12 — Todah is juxtaposed (hekesh) to shelamim
↓
Hekesh teaches: Todah can also be bought with ma'aser sheni money
↓
Conclusion: A law derived via GS can teach via hekesh
Significance This is the key proof that gezeirah shavah-derived laws can transmit halakha through juxtaposition — a building block for the meta-hermeneutical matrix.

Amud B: Can Kal VaChomer Build on Derived Laws?

Resolved (Proven Yes)

  • GS → Hekesh (Rav Pappa)
  • GS → GS (Rami bar Ḥama)
  • GS → KvC (derived via kal vachomer from above)
  • KvC → Hekesh (via KvC, per Rav Pappa)
  • KvC → GS (via kal vachomer)
  • KvC → KvC (via kal vachomer)

Unresolved (Teyku)

  • GS → Binyan Av
  • KvC → Hekesh (per Mar Zutra, who rejects Rav Pappa)

Resolved (Proven No)

  • Hekesh → Hekesh (Rava / Ravina)
  • Hekesh → Binyan Av (R' Yirmeya)
Pattern Gezeirah shavah is the most powerful method — it can chain with almost everything. Hekesh is the weakest — it cannot teach via itself or via binyan av.
