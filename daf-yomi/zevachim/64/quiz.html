QUESTION 1 OF 5
Which THREE rites are performed at the southwest corner BELOW the red line?
A. Bird sin offering, meal offerings, pouring remaining blood
B. Wine libation, water libation, bird burnt offering
C. Bird sin offering, wine libation, meal offerings
D. Pouring remaining blood, bird burnt offering, water libation
Correct: A — The Mishna lists three matters below the red line at the southwest corner: the bird sin offering (derived from comparison to meal offerings), bringing meal offerings near the altar (hagasha), and pouring the remaining blood (shefikhat shirayim).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does R' Yoḥanan say the bird burnt offering is ideally performed at the SOUTHEAST corner?
A. It faces the Sanctuary entrance
B. It is closest to the place of the ashes where the crop and feathers are placed
C. The red line is most visible there
D. It is closest to the ramp for quick ascent
Correct: B — R' Yoḥanan explains that the southeast corner is closest to the beit ha-deshen (place of ashes), where the crop and feathers of the bird burnt offering must be placed.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why do priests performing libations or bird burnt offerings ascend DIRECTLY to the southwest corner instead of circling the altar?
A. To save time during busy festival days
B. The southwest corner has special sanctity requiring direct approach
C. Lest the wine/water absorb smoke or the bird die from inhaling smoke
D. The ramp is too narrow for circling with liquid vessels
Correct: C — R' Yoḥanan explains two concerns: the wine or water might absorb the altar's smoke, and a live bird for the burnt offering might die from inhaling the smoke during the extended walk around the altar.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rava prove that the High Priest received the wine ONLY at the southwest corner, not before circling?
A. From a verse in Leviticus about libation procedures
B. From the Mishna's use of 'is given wine' (passive) rather than 'is told to pour wine'
C. From the tradition that the High Priest always walks empty-handed
D. From the fact that no wine vessels were kept at the ramp
Correct: B — Rava notes the precise language: the Mishna says the High Priest 'is given' (nitnah) wine to pour — implying he receives it at the destination. Had he already been carrying it, the Mishna would say he 'is told' to pour.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why must the blood of the bird sin offering be sprinkled BELOW the red line, despite a logical argument that it should be above?
A. Because all bird offerings require blood below the line
B. Because the verse says 'yimmatze' — the blood must drain by itself to the base, which is blocked by the ledge above
C. Because the red line marks the boundary of the altar's sanctity
D. Because R' Yoḥanan rules that bird offerings follow the same rules as animal burnt offerings
Correct: B — Rava explains that 'yimmatze' (passive: 'it shall be drained') means the blood must trickle down to the base on its own. The surrounding ledge (sovev), located one cubit above the red line, would block this natural drainage — so the blood must be sprinkled below the ledge.
