QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why don't firstborn, tithe, and Paschal offerings require 'two placements that are four' like a burnt offering?
A. Because they are offerings of lesser sanctity
B. Because two (or three) verses teaching the same rule do not extend to other cases
C. Because they require only one placement by rabbinic decree
D. Because their blood is poured, not sprinkled
Correct: B — Abaye explains that the burnt offering and sin offering verses both teach the same rule about blood placement — two verses that come as one (שני כתובים הבאים כאחד) do not teach other cases. Adding the guilt offering makes three verses, which certainly don't extend.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was R' Yosei HaGelili's main argument against R' Tarfon's position on the firstborn's eating duration?
A. The firstborn is holy of holies, not lesser sanctity
B. The firstborn is a priestly gift like sin/guilt offerings, so it should be eaten for one day and one night
C. The Torah never specifies an eating period for the firstborn
D. The firstborn should be eaten only at night like the Paschal offering
Correct: B — R' Yosei HaGelili argued that the firstborn shares key features with sin and guilt offerings — all are gifts to the priest and none come as vow or gift offerings — so it should follow their one-day-and-one-night eating period.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How did R' Akiva resolve the debate about the firstborn offering's eating duration?
A. He ruled it should be eaten for one day and one night
B. He cited Numbers 18:18 which juxtaposes firstborn flesh with the breast and thigh of peace offerings
C. He invoked the principle of two verses that come as one
D. He derived it from the Paschal offering laws
Correct: B — R' Akiva cited the verse 'their flesh shall be yours, as the breast of waving and as the right thigh' (Numbers 18:18), which directly juxtaposes the firstborn with peace offerings, establishing a two-day-and-one-night eating period.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Elazar ben Azarya, why must the Paschal offering be eaten only until midnight?
A. Because the Torah says 'you shall eat it in haste'
B. Because midnight is when the priests finish their Temple duties
C. Because 'on that night' parallels the plague of the firstborn, which struck at midnight
D. Because it is a rabbinic safeguard to prevent transgression
Correct: C — R' Elazar ben Azarya connects 'on that night' (regarding eating the Paschal offering) to 'on that night' (regarding the plague), and since the plague struck at midnight, the eating obligation also ends at midnight.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the problem of deriving the flour measure for matza of a thanks offering via a chain of juxtapositions?
A. It says the chain of juxtapositions is permitted in this case
B. It says the first derivation is from a superfluous word ('תביאו'), not a juxtaposition, so the chain is not problematic
C. It derives the measure from an entirely separate verse
D. It says the matza measure is a halakha l'Moshe mi'Sinai
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the term 'תביאו' (you shall bring) is a superfluous word, not a juxtaposition. Since the first link in the chain isn't a hekkesh, the second derivation (matza from leavened bread) via juxtaposition is valid.
