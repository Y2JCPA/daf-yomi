Where on the Corner? R' Yochanan vs. R' Elazar

One Sage
The kohen places blood within a cubit on either side of the corner — anywhere in that zone is valid.
Other Sage
He sprinkles on the outer edge of the corner itself, and the blood flows down both sides of the altar.
Gemara
According to R' Elazar b'R' Shimon (blood goes on the actual 1×1 cubit projection), all agree it must be on the corner.
Gemara
They only disagree according to Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, who allows placement anywhere above the red line.
Baraita
The kohen dips his right index finger, gathers blood between thumb and pinky, and sprinkles on the outer edge.
Resolution
L'chatchila (ideally): on the edge. B'dieved (after the fact): within a cubit on either side is also valid.
TECHNIQUE Right index finger (haymanit — the most dexterous), thumb above, pinky below, sprinkling downward on the edge.

Rabbi vs. R' Elazar b'R' Shimon: Valid Placement Zone

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi

  • Upper bloods (e.g., sin offering): anywhere from the red line and above
  • Lower bloods: anywhere from the red line and below
  • Broader valid zone for blood placement
  • Based on Ezekiel 43:15 — 'domain of the corners is four cubits'

R' Elazar b'R' Shimon

  • Bird burnt offering: anywhere above the red line (agrees with Rabbi)
  • Animal sin offering: only on the actual corner projection
  • Based on Leviticus 4:25 — 'put it upon the corners of the altar'
  • Narrower valid zone — the literal 1×1 cubit corner
EZEKIEL 43:15 Rav Adda bar Ahava: The four 1×1 cubit corners total 4 cubits of domain — meaning blood may be placed up to 4 cubits below the actual corners.

The Red Line (Chut HaSikra)

וְנָתַתָּה אֹתָהּ תַּחַת הַכַּרְכֹּב לַמִּזְבֵּחַ מִלְּמָטָּה וְהָיְתָה הָרֶשֶׁת עַד חֲצִי הַמִּזְבֵּחַ
SOURCE Rav Acha bar Rav Ketina derives the red line from Exodus 27:5: 'Put it under the ledge round the altar beneath, and the net will reach halfway up the altar.'

The Torah itself provides for a separation on the altar — dividing the zone for upper bloods (sin offerings) from lower bloods (burnt offerings, guilt offerings).

This red line (chut hasikra) circumscribed the altar at its midpoint (Middot 35b).

Burnt Offering: Rav vs. Shmuel — 'Two That Are Four'

Rav
Place blood on one side of a corner, then the other side of the same corner. Repeat on the diagonal corner. = 2 corners × 2 sides = 4 placements covering all 4 walls.
Shmuel
One gamma-shaped (γ) placement on each of two diagonal corners — the bent shape covers two walls at once. 2 placements × 2 sides each = 4.
Baraita (Stam)
Leviticus 1:5 says 'sprinkle around' — not like a thread circling, but a γ shape. Supports Shmuel.
R' Yishmael
Compare to Lev. 8:15 (inauguration sin offering): just as that was discrete, four placements — so too the burnt offering. Supports Rav.
KEY DIFFERENCE Rav: 4 discrete applications on 2 corners. Shmuel: 2 gamma-shaped applications on 2 corners. Both achieve blood on all 4 sides.
