Zevachim 53 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, at which corner does the kohen begin sprinkling the blood of a sin offering?
A. Northeast
B. Southeast
C. Southwest
D. Northwest
Correct: B — The Mishna states the kohen ascends the ramp, turns right to the ledge, continues east, and comes first to the southeast corner.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the resolution to the dispute about placing blood within a cubit of the corner vs. on the edge itself?
A. Only the edge is valid even b'dieved
B. The edge is l'chatchila; within a cubit is valid b'dieved
C. Within a cubit is l'chatchila; the edge is only b'dieved
D. Both are equally l'chatchila
Correct: B — The Gemara resolves that the baraita describes the optimal (l'chatchila) method — on the edge — but b'dieved, within a cubit on either side is also valid.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did the southeast corner of the altar have no base?
A. It was damaged during construction
B. It was in Yehuda's portion, not Binyamin's
C. The Torah explicitly prohibits a base there
D. It was added later during the Second Temple
Correct: B — The altar's SE corner base fell in Yehuda's tribal portion. Since the altar's sanctity was tied to Binyamin's territory, that corner had no base.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Shmuel say the kohen performs 'two placements that are four' for a burnt offering?
A. One placement on each of all four corners
B. One gamma-shaped (γ) placement on each of two diagonal corners
C. Two placements on the same corner, repeated twice
D. Pouring blood around the altar like a thread
Correct: B — Shmuel holds that one gamma-shaped placement on each of two diagonal corners covers all four walls — each bent placement touches two sides.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
From which verse does Rav Acha bar Rav Ketina derive the existence of the red line (chut hasikra) on the altar?
A. Leviticus 4:25 — 'put it upon the corners'
B. Ezekiel 43:15 — 'from the altar and above, four corners'
C. Exodus 27:5 — 'the net will reach halfway up the altar'
D. Leviticus 1:5 — 'sprinkle the blood around'
Correct: C — Rav Acha bar Rav Ketina derives the red line from Exodus 27:5, which describes the net reaching 'halfway up the altar,' indicating a midpoint separation.
