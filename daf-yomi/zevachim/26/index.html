Legs Outside the Courtyard: When Is the Offering Disqualified?

Animal stands in courtyard, legs move outside after shechita
↓
Sever legs → Collect blood
Fit — cut flesh to bone (Rav Ḥisda/Avimi), no blemish created
Collect blood → Sever legs
Disqualified — blood/fat from legs mixed with collected blood
Core Issue The sequence of severing legs and collecting blood determines whether blood or fat from outside the azarah contaminates the offering.

Shmuel's Father

Shmuel
No — 'That they may bring them to the Lord' — all of it must be inside.
Shmuel's Father
Animal suspended in the air and slaughtered?

Valid! ❌ Wrong — we require 'on the side of the altar.'
Shmuel's Father
Slaughterer suspended, animal on the ground?
Shmuel
Not valid! ❌ Wrong — only the slaughter must be at the altar's side, not the slaughterer.

Priest suspended in the air and collected blood?
Shmuel
Valid! ❌ Wrong — this is not a normal manner of ministration.

Most Sacred vs. Lesser Sanctity — Location Requirements

Kodshei Kodashim (Most Sacred)

  • Slaughter: North of the courtyard
  • Collection: North of the courtyard
  • Standing in south, hand reaches north → slaughter valid, collection invalid
  • Head + most of body in north → counts as entering
  • Animal convulses south then returns → still fit

Kodashim Kalim (Lesser Sanctity)

  • Slaughter: Anywhere in the courtyard
  • Collection: Anywhere in the courtyard
  • Standing outside, hand reaches in → slaughter valid, collection invalid
  • Head + most of body inside → does NOT count as entering
  • Animal convulses outside then returns → DISQUALIFIED
Key Difference For kodashim kalim, an animal that leaves the courtyard even momentarily is disqualified — the Gemara debates whether this applies to all the meat or only to altar portions (tail, diaphragm, kidneys).

Shmuel's Ruling — Atonement Without Consumption

וַאֲנִי נְתַתִּיו לָכֶם עַל הַמִּזְבֵּחַ לְכַפֵּר עַל נַפְשֹׁתֵיכֶם
Shmuel's Chiddush When the Mishnah says the offering is disqualified (pasul), it means the meat is unfit for consumption — but the owner has achieved atonement.

Source: 'And I have given it to you upon the altar to atone for your souls' (Leviticus 17:11) — once blood reaches any location on the altar, atonement is effected.

But the verse emphasizes 'to atone' — for atonement and nothing else, meaning the meat remains forbidden.
