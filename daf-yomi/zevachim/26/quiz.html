Zevachim 26 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
If an animal's legs moved outside the courtyard after shechita, what must be done BEFORE collecting the blood?
A. Bring the animal fully back inside
B. Sever the legs (cutting flesh to the bone)
C. Perform a new shechita
D. Sprinkle water on the animal
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda in the name of Avimi explains that one cuts the flesh to the bone (without fully severing), which avoids creating a blemish, and then collects the blood — ensuring no blood/fat from outside contaminates the collection.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Shmuel, what does it mean when the Mishnah says an offering is 'disqualified' (pasul)?
A. No atonement and no consumption
B. Atonement achieved but meat may not be consumed
C. Meat may be consumed but no atonement
D. The offering must be repeated entirely
Correct: B — Shmuel derives from Leviticus 17:11 that once blood reaches any part of the altar, atonement is achieved. However, the verse limits it 'to atone' — meaning the meat remains forbidden for consumption.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Shmuel's father's correction when Shmuel says slaughtering an animal suspended in the air is valid?
A. The animal must face north
B. We require slaughter 'on the side of the altar'
C. Suspended animals are considered blemished
D. The blood cannot be properly collected
Correct: B — Shmuel's father corrects him based on Leviticus 1:11 — the Torah requires slaughter to take place 'on the side of the altar' (yerech ha-mizbeach), and an airborne animal does not satisfy this requirement.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rava, when does improper intent (machshava) disqualify an offering?
A. Whenever anyone has improper intent during any part of the service
B. Only when expressed by a fit person, regarding a fit item, in a fit place
C. Only during the sprinkling of the blood
D. Only if the Kohen Gadol expresses it
Correct: B — Rava's principle states three conditions must be met for intent to disqualify: the person must be fit for the service in question, the item must be fit, and the place must be fit. This is why an unfit person's intent only matters during shechita (where even the unfit are valid).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara limit the baraita's ruling that a lesser-sanctity animal that left the courtyard is disqualified?
A. It applies only to bird offerings
B. Perhaps it refers only to altar portions (tail, diaphragm, kidneys), not all the meat
C. It applies only if the animal was outside for more than one hour
D. It was a minority opinion rejected by the Sages
Correct: B — The Gemara suggests the baraita may refer specifically to the tail, diaphragm, and two kidneys — portions burned on the altar that are never meant to leave the courtyard. The remaining meat of lesser-sanctity offerings, which may be eaten outside after sprinkling, might not be disqualified.
