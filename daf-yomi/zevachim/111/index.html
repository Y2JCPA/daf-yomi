Libations Outside: Why the Dispute?

Rav Adda bar Rav Yitzḥak
Both agree libation must be consecrated in a service vessel first. They disagree about the overfill (שפיכת מידות) — is liquid above the rim also consecrated?
First Tanna
Yes — overfill is consecrated. If one collects 3 log of overfill and pours it outside, he is liable.
R' Elazar b'R' Shimon
No — only wine within the walls of the vessel is consecrated. Overfill poured outside = exempt.
Ravina
Actually, everyone agrees wine libations are valid without a service vessel. The real dispute is whether water libation liability can be derived from wine.
KEY POINT Three different explanations are offered for the same Tanna'ic dispute — each reframes the fundamental disagreement.

Were Libations Offered in the Wilderness?

Rabbi Yishmael

  • Libations were NOT offered in the wilderness
  • Numbers 15:2 ('When you come into the land') teaches that libations begin upon entering Eretz Yisrael
  • 'Lakhem' (plural 'you') = public altar used by everyone
  • The verse introduces libations for the great public altar

Rabbi Akiva

  • Libations WERE offered in the wilderness
  • Numbers 15:2 teaches that libations are required even on private altars (bamot)
  • 'Land of your dwellings' = altars in all your dwelling places = private altars
  • The verse extends an existing obligation to a new context
CONNECTION This Yishmael/Akiva dispute underpins the question of whether libations need service-vessel consecration — since private altars had no service vessels.

Private Altars (Bamot) and Service Vessels

Were libations brought on private altars (bamot)?
↓
YES — Rabbis
Private altars have no service vessels → libations are valid without consecration in a vessel → Liable for unconsecrated libation poured outside
NO — R' Yehuda HaNasi
No precedent for libation without a service vessel → libation must be consecrated first → Exempt for unconsecrated libation poured outside
RAVA B'RABBA This is Rava son of Rabba's explanation: the Tanna'ic dispute about liability for unconsecrated libations outside depends on whether bamot required libations.

Bird Offerings: Pinching vs. Slaughter — Inside vs. Outside

ScenarioLiability for Offering Up Outside
Pinch inside → offer up outside✅ Liable
Pinch outside → offer up outside❌ Exempt (invalid pinching)
Slaughter inside → offer up outside❌ Exempt (slaughter disqualifies bird)
Slaughter outside → offer up outside✅ Liable
PARADOX The proper method done inside (pinching) creates liability outside, while the improper method done outside (slaughter) also creates liability — each context has its own rules of validity.

What Is Rabbi Shimon Really Saying?

Gemara
Rabbi Shimon says: Any killing that creates liability outside should also create liability when done inside. But which clause is he disputing?
Gemara (Attempt 1)
Is he disputing pinching? If nape-pinching inside → liable, then outside → also liable? Rejected — his wording doesn't fit.
Gemara (Attempt 2)
Is he disputing slaughter of birds? If slaughter outside → liable, then inside → also liable? Rejected — he explicitly exempts slaughter inside.
Ze'eiri
Rabbi Shimon refers to slaughter at night inside the courtyard. The first tanna exempts (nighttime disqualifies), but Rabbi Shimon holds one is liable.
RESOLUTION Ze'eiri identifies a hidden case: slaughtering an animal offering at night inside, then offering it up outside. The first tanna says nighttime slaughter disqualifies it (exempt); Rabbi Shimon disagrees.
