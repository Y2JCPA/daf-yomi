Private vs. Public Altar: Status Transfer & Ritual Rules

R' Zeira
If a private altar offering was brought inside the public altar area and then taken back outside — what is its status?
Gemara
Does the partition absorb it permanently? Or does it revert to private altar status when removed?
Rabba
Offerings slaughtered in the south that descended — shall not ascend again. The altar only consecrates what is fit for it.
Rav Yosef
They shall ascend again — the altar's sanctity endures even after descent.
Gemara
The cases are not identical — R' Zeira's dilemma involves two separate places, not just altar vs. courtyard.
TEKU Both R' Zeira's dilemma and Rabbi Yannai's related question remain unresolved (תיקו).

Rabba vs. Rav Yosef: Offerings That Descended the Altar

Mishna (Me'ila 2a)
Most sacred offerings slaughtered in the south (instead of the north) are disqualified, yet if they ascended the altar — they shall not descend.
Gemara
But if they did descend — should they ascend again?
Rabba
They shall NOT ascend again. The altar consecrates only what is fit for it — and this consecration is lost upon descent.
Rav Yosef
They SHALL ascend again. The altar's consecration endures even after the offering leaves.
Gemara
This may not resolve R' Zeira's case — the altar and courtyard are one place, but private and public altars are two separate places.
KEY DISTINCTION The altar sanctifying what's on it (one location) may differ from a partition absorbing between two separate domains.

Rabbi Yannai's Related Dilemma

אברי עולת במה קטנה שעלו וירדו מהו?
🔥 DILEMMA Rabbi Yannai asks: Limbs of a private altar burnt offering that ascended the altar and then descended — do they ascend again?

The Gemara clarifies: If fire has not yet taken hold — they certainly do not ascend again.
The dilemma is only where fire has taken hold (אחזה בהן האור) — does that create a stronger claim to return them?

Rav vs. Shmuel: Night Slaughter on a Private Altar

One Sage (Night = Non-Sacred)

  • I Samuel 14:34 — slaughter at night was of non-sacred animals (חולין)
  • I Samuel 14:32 — daytime concern was for sacrificial offerings
  • Night slaughter is invalid even on a private altar
  • Private altar follows public altar rules for daytime requirement

Other Sage (Night = Small Altar)

  • Both verses refer to sacrificial offerings
  • Daytime = offerings of the great public altar (במה גדולה)
  • Night = offerings of a small private altar (במה קטנה)
  • Night slaughter is valid on a private altar
SOURCE The contradiction is from I Samuel 14:32–34 — Saul's war with the Philistines, where he erected a stone as a private altar.
