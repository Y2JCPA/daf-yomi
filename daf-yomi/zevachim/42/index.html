Piggul in Inner Blood Presentations

R' Meir
If the priest had piggul intent during any one of the three sets of inner sprinklings, the offering is piggul and one is liable for karet.
Rabbis
There is no karet liability unless the priest had piggul intent during the entire permitting factor — not just part of it.
Reish Lakish
R' Meir's reason: anyone who performs an action performs it with his initial intent — silence continues the first intention.
Rav Yitzchak bar Avin
The case is improper intent during slaughter (a complete permitting factor), not during the sprinklings themselves.
Core Issue Can piggul intent during only part of a multi-step blood service (half a permitting factor) render the entire offering piggul?

R' Meir vs. The Rabbis on Partial Piggul Intent

R' Meir
Intent during the first, second, OR third set of sprinklings — the offering is piggul with karet liability.
Rabbis
No karet unless piggul intent was present during the performance of the entire permitting factor.
Gemara
The baraita says 'whether first, second, or third' — this proves R' Meir holds one can render piggul with half a mattir!
Reish Lakish
Not necessarily — R' Meir's reason is that silence continues the original intent, so all sets are covered by the first intent.
Key Principle כל העושה — על דעת ראשונה הוא עושה
Anyone who performs an action performs it with his initial intent.

Rav Yitzchak bar Avin's Resolution

Priest sprinkles blood of first bull inside the Holy of Holies
↓
The remaining blood spills!
↓
Must slaughter a new bull for the next set of sprinklings
↓
Did the priest have piggul intent during the slaughter?
R' Meir
Piggul — slaughter is one complete permitting factor, not half
Rabbis (= R' Eliezer)
Not piggul — each separate slaughter is its own act; must taint all of them
Resolution The case involves piggul intent during slaughter of replacement animals, not during sprinklings — so each intent involves a whole mattir, not half.

Identifying 'The Rabbis' — Rava's Key Move

R' Eliezer's Position

  • Burning part of a meal offering outside = exempt
  • Must sacrifice the whole item outside to be liable
  • Completion of the entire service is required
  • This is 'the Rabbis' who oppose R' Meir

Challenge to R' Eliezer

  • Rava himself says R' Eliezer concedes regarding blood
  • Even part of blood sprinkled outside = liable
  • Each individual sprinkling is its own act (Yoma 60a)
  • So R' Eliezer cannot be 'the Rabbis' here!
Difficulty Since R' Eliezer treats each blood sprinkling independently, he would agree with R' Meir — so who are the Rabbis?
