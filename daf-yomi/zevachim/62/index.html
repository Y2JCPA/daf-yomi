Rebuilding the Second Temple Altar

Problem: Returning exiles must rebuild the altar — but how?
↓
Size
Verse juxtaposes altar with Temple → may extend up to 60 cubits
Location
Multiple traditions: vision, ashes of Yitzchak, scent, prophetic testimony
Features
Corners, ramp, base, square shape are indispensable
↓
Altar is fit for sacrifices even without the Temple
KEY PRINCIPLE The altar could function independently of the Temple, but its physical features — corners, ramp, base, and square shape — were absolutely indispensable.

Why Was the Second Temple Altar Larger?

Rav Yosef (initial)
The Second Temple altar reached its full ideal measure — the First Temple altar was not ideal.
Gemara
But David's Temple plans were dictated by God! "All this in writing, as the Lord has made me wise by His hand" (I Chron. 28:19)
Rav Yosef (revised)
The First Temple size was ideal. But in the Second Temple era, they needed to expand and found a verse to permit it.
Rav Yosef
The verse juxtaposes "House" with "altar" (I Chron. 22:1) — just as the House was 60 cubits, the altar may extend up to 60 cubits.
VERSE "This is the House of the Lord God, and this is the altar of burnt offering for Israel" (I Chronicles 22:1)

How Did They Know the Altar's Location?

R' Elazar

  • Saw a vision of the altar already built
  • Michael the archangel standing and sacrificing upon it

R' Yitzchak Nappacha

  • Saw the ashes of Yitzchak (the Akeidah)
  • Placed at that exact location

R' Shmuel bar Nachmani

  • From the whole Temple they smelled incense
  • From the altar's spot they smelled burned limbs

R' Yochanan

  • Three prophets ascended from exile
  • Testified about size, location, and that sacrifices work without a Temple
NOTE Each opinion preserves a different mode of revelation: vision, sacred memory, sensory evidence, and prophetic testimony.

Why Is the Ramp on the South Side?

Rav Huna
"He shall slaughter it on the yerekh (thigh/side) of the altar northward" — the altar's thigh is in the north, so its face (ramp) faces south.
Gemara
Maybe the person is sitting — face and legs both point north?
Rava
Place the man lying face-down — legs north, face south. Also, "ravua" (square) hints at "ravutz" (crouching/lying).
Abaye
But ravua is needed to teach that the altar must be square!
Rava
The Torah uses ravua instead of merubba — it teaches both: square shape and lying-down orientation.
R' YEHUDA Alternate source: "Its steps shall look toward the east" (Ezek. 43:17) — all turns on the altar go to the right, which only works if the ramp is in the south.
