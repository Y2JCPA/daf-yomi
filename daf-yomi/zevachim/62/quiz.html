Zevachim 62 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why was the Second Temple altar expanded beyond its First Temple size?
A. The First Temple altar was too small by divine command
B. They needed more space and found a verse juxtaposing altar with Temple (60 cubits)
C. The prophets commanded it to be exactly 60 cubits
D. The returning exiles couldn't determine the original size
Correct: B — Rav Yosef explains that the First Temple size was ideal, but in the Second Temple era there was a practical need to expand. They found support in I Chronicles 22:1, which juxtaposes the House (60 cubits) with the altar.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Shmuel bar Nachmani, how did they identify the altar's location?
A. They saw Michael the archangel sacrificing there
B. Three prophets testified about it
C. They smelled burned animal limbs at that spot while the rest of the Temple smelled of incense
D. They found the ashes of Yitzchak at that location
Correct: C — R' Shmuel bar Nachmani's unique approach uses sensory evidence: the entire Temple area smelled of incense, but the altar's specific location was identified by the scent of burned animal limbs.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which of the following is NOT indispensable for the altar's fitness?
A. The corners (keren)
B. The height measurement
C. The square shape
D. The base (yesod)
Correct: B — The baraita teaches that corners, ramp, base, and square shape are indispensable, but the measurements of length, width, and height are not indispensable.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rava derive that the ramp must be on the south side of the altar?
A. From the verse about Ezekiel's altar facing east
B. From the positioning of the twelve oxen under Solomon's Basin
C. The word 'ravua' (square) alludes to 'ravutz' (crouching/lying) — legs north, face south
D. A prophet testified that the ramp faces south
Correct: C — Rava argues that 'ravua' hints at 'ravutz' (lying down), picturing the altar like a person lying face-down with legs to the north and face to the south, placing the ramp (face/entrance) on the south side.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why was there a gap between the ramp and the altar?
A. To prevent impurity from transferring between them
B. So that the flesh of offerings could be tossed onto the altar, paralleling how blood is tossed
C. Because the altar was built on a higher foundation than the ramp
D. To allow drainage of sacrificial blood
Correct: B — Deuteronomy 12:27 juxtaposes flesh and blood — just as blood is presented by tossing from the ground, the flesh must also be tossed. The gap between ramp and altar enables this requirement.
