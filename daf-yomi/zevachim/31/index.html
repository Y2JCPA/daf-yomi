Combining Half-Measures of Piggul Intent

Setup
One intends to eat ½ olive-bulk beyond its time, then ½ olive-bulk outside its area, then ½ olive-bulk beyond its time again.
Rava
Piggul 'awakes as one asleep' — the two halves of piggul intent join. The middle thought is disregarded as insufficient.
Rav Hamnuna
It is a combination of improper intentions — the middle thought interferes and prevents piggul.
Bar Kappara
When the piggul intent totals a whole olive-bulk, a half-measure of area intent cannot interfere.
Core Question When half-measures of piggul intent are separated by a different disqualifying intent, do they combine or does the intervening intent block them?

Rava vs. Rav Hamnuna: The Dormant Piggul

Case
Intent: ½ zayit beyond time → ½ zayit outside area → ½ zayit beyond time
Rava
The piggul 'awakes as one asleep' (Psalms 78:65) — the two half-measures of chutz lizmano join. The middle intent is only half a measure, so it is disregarded.
Rav Hamnuna
It is a combination of improper intentions (eiruv machshavot). The middle thought interferes and the offering is not piggul.
Principle Can a half-measure that is insufficient to disqualify on its own still block the combination of two other half-measures?

Proofs from Ritual Impurity

Rava's Proof (Teharot 1:5)

  • Mix egg-bulk of 1st-degree + egg-bulk of 2nd-degree → 1st-degree
  • Separate into two portions → each is 2nd-degree
  • Both fall on teruma simultaneously → impart 2nd-degree impurity
  • Conclusion: 1st-degree impurity lay DORMANT and resurfaced
  • Similarly, piggul intent lies dormant and resurfaces

Rav Hamnuna's Distinction

  • Impurity case: full measure EXISTS at outset, then splits
  • Piggul case: full measure NEVER existed at outset
  • You cannot revive what was never established
  • Counter-proof from Me'ila 17b: mixed impurities join only at lower level
  • Conclusion: intervening intent permanently blocks piggul
Key Difference Rava sees piggul intent as dormant and recoverable; Rav Hamnuna says it was never established and cannot be recovered.

R' Yannai: Dogs' Eating = Eating

וְהַכְּלָבִים יֹאכְלוּ אֶת־אִיזֶבֶל בְּחֵלֶק יִזְרְעֶאל
Novel Ruling If one intends that dogs will eat the offering the next day, it is piggul. Source: "And the dogs shall eat Jezebel in the portion of Jezreel" (II Kings 9:10) — Scripture calls canine consumption 'eating.'

R' Ami objects: If so, should intent for non-sacred fire to consume it the next day also count as burning?

Rava's Dilemma: Time Limit for Eating an Offering

Intent to eat an olive-bulk in more time than it takes to eat a half-loaf of bread
Like Altar Burning
No time limit for the altar's 'consumption' → Still piggul
Like Human Eating
Eating slower than this rate is not eating → Not piggul
↓
Abaye's proof from mishna: eating + burning don't join → but eat + eat would join even at burning rate
↓
Gemara rejects: mishna may refer to large fire that burns within eating-time
Unresolved The Gemara does not conclusively resolve whether offering consumption follows altar timing or human eating timing.
