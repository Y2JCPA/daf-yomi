5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, what happens when one has intent for ½ olive-bulk beyond time, ½ olive-bulk outside area, then ½ olive-bulk beyond time?
A. The offering is fit
B. The offering is piggul — the piggul intent 'awakes as one asleep'
C. The offering has the status of eiruv machshavot (mixed intentions)
D. The offering requires a new slaughter
Correct: B — Rava holds that the two half-measures of piggul intent join together, and the intervening half-measure of area intent is disregarded as insufficient. He describes the piggul as 'awaking as one asleep' (Psalms 78:65).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rav Hamnuna's key distinction between the impurity case and the piggul case?
A. Impurity is biblical while piggul is rabbinic
B. In impurity the full measure existed at the outset; in piggul it never did
C. Impurity applies to food while piggul applies to sacrifices
D. Impurity requires witnesses while piggul does not
Correct: B — Rav Hamnuna argues that in the impurity case, a full requisite measure was present at the outset and was divided, so it can resurface. In the piggul case, there was never a complete measure established, so it cannot be revived.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does R' Yannai rule that intent for dogs to eat the offering the next day renders it piggul?
A. Because dogs are considered domesticated animals under halakha
B. Because the verse about Jezebel calls canine consumption 'eating'
C. Because any destruction of the offering counts as consumption
D. Because the Rabbis enacted a gezeira about animals eating kodshim
Correct: B — R' Yannai derives from II Kings 9:10 ('And the dogs shall eat Jezebel') that Scripture itself categorizes canine consumption as 'eating,' so intent for dogs to eat the offering qualifies as intent to eat.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why do intentions to eat half an olive-bulk and burn half an olive-bulk NOT join together?
A. Because burning is faster than eating
B. Because eating and burning are fundamentally different categories of consumption
C. Because the Torah explicitly prohibits combining them
D. Because half an olive-bulk is never significant
Correct: B — The mishna explicitly teaches that eating and burning are different types of consumption that cannot be combined. This is a categorical distinction — they are separate modes of 'consumption' (human eating vs. altar burning).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Rava's unresolved dilemma regarding the time limit for eating offerings?
A. Whether one must eat the offering before sunrise
B. Whether eating at a rate slower than consuming a half-loaf of bread still counts as 'eating'
C. Whether the Kohen or the owner must eat the offering
D. Whether eating at night counts as eating during the day
Correct: B — Rava asks whether we compare offering consumption to altar burning (no time limit) or to ordinary human eating (must be within the time it takes to eat a half-loaf). The Gemara does not conclusively resolve this dilemma.
