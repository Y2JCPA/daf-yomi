Bird Offering Brought to Altar
↓
Is it a sin offering or burnt offering?
Sin Offering (חַטָּאת)
Must be sacrificed below the red line
Pinch nape but do not fully sever
Sprinkle blood on altar wall
Burnt Offering (עוֹלָה)
Must be sacrificed above the red line
Pinch nape and fully sever head
Squeeze out all blood at once
Key Principle Changing the location, procedure, or intent of a bird offering can disqualify it entirely.

Is Separating the Head of a Bird Sin Offering Prohibited?

Rav Aḥa b. Rava
If 'shall not separate' merely means not required, then regarding a pit — 'does not cover it' — would that also mean covering is merely optional?
Rav Ashi
The cases are different! With a pit, the next verse says 'the owner shall pay' — proving he must cover it.
Rav Ashi
But for a bird burnt offering, the verse says 'the priest shall bring it' — the word 'it' distinguishes burnt from sin offering.
Gemara
Since the Torah already differentiated the two offerings, the phrase 'shall not separate' is not a prohibition — it simply means one does not have to.
Conclusion Separating the head of a bird sin offering is not prohibited (אִיסּוּר) — it is simply not required (לֹא צָרִיךְ).

Blood Squeezing: Head vs. Body

Squeezed Body Blood Only

  • ✅ Offering is VALID
  • Most blood is found in the body
  • Derived from 'It is a burnt offering' (Lev. 1:17)
  • Ravina: This stands to reason — the body contains more blood

Squeezed Head Blood Only

  • ❌ Offering is DISQUALIFIED
  • The head contains less blood
  • The verse states restrictively: 'It is' (הִוא)
  • Insufficient blood application to validate the offering
Ravina There is no conclusive proof from the verse itself, but logic dictates: most blood is in the body, so body-blood suffices.

What Rite Was Changed? Pinching or Sprinkling?

Gemara
The Mishna says a sin offering done as a burnt offering is disqualified. What rite changed?
Suggestion
Perhaps the pinching changed — he fully severed the head, as in a burnt offering?
Objection
Then the Mishna would not follow R' Elazar b. R' Shimon, who allows full severance even for a sin offering!
Resolution
The change was in sprinkling (מִצּוּי): he squeezed out all the blood at once instead of sprinkling it. Even R' Elazar b. R' Shimon agrees this disqualifies.
Proof
The latter clause says a sin offering above the red line is disqualified. Since pinching is valid anywhere on the altar, the change must be in sprinkling location.
Key Distinction Pinching (מְלִיקָה) is valid anywhere on the altar, but sprinkling (הַזָּאָה) must be in the designated location.
