Does Pinching Purify a Bird Tereifa?

Chachamim
Just as slaughter purifies a bird tereifa from tumah, so too melikah (pinching) — which permits bird offerings for consumption — should purify its tereifa from impurity.
R' Yosei
The dayyo principle applies: a kal va'chomer derivation cannot be more stringent than its source. It is sufficient that a bird tereifa be like an animal tereifa — only slaughter purifies, not pinching.
Gemara
This proof is rejected: the case involves non-sacred slaughter teaching to sacred pinching. Outside kodashim, chaining derivations is uncontroversial — the question only arises within the realm of consecrated matters.
Dayyo R' Yosei invokes dayyo: a conclusion derived by kal va'chomer cannot exceed the stringency of the premise from which it is derived.

↓

↓
🩸 Blood — left overnight is fit (derived by paradigm from sacrificial portions)
↓
🔗 This proves: Binyan Av → Binyan Av — a chained derivation works!

Gemara's Challenge: Fit vs. Unfit Sources

The Problem

  • Sacrificial portions left overnight are disqualified (not fit)
  • But the source — meat of shelamim — is permitted (fit)
  • Can we derive the law of something not fit from something fit?
  • Similarly: Temple courtyard ≠ private altar (no sacred boundary)

The Answer

  • The Tanna relies on Leviticus 6:2 as the real source
  • "This is the law of the burnt offering… all night"
  • The verse amplifies the halakha broadly
  • The baraita's derivations are merely asmakhtot (supports)
Asmakhta The Gemara resolves the logical gap by explaining the derivations are Rabbinic supports (asmakhtot), not the actual source — the verse itself is the true basis.
