QUESTION 1 OF 5
What verse does Rav Huna cite to prove that awareness during slaughter is indispensable (me'akev)?
A. "And he shall slaughter the bull" (Lev. 1:5)
B. "With your will you shall slaughter it" (Lev. 19:5)
C. "He who sacrifices shall sacrifice his offering" (Num. 15:4)
D. "As the sin offering is, so is the guilt offering" (Lev. 14:13)
Correct: B — Leviticus 1:5 establishes the basic mitzvah of intentional slaughter, but Shmuel pressed for a source that it's indispensable. Rav Huna then cited Leviticus 19:5 — 'with your will' — to prove conscious intent is me'akev.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Abaye, what principle unites R' Elazar b"R Yosei, R' Eliezer, and R' Shimon ben Elazar?
A. Slaughter must always be performed in the north
B. One person's intention is effective for another person's action
C. The owner must always be present during the sacrifice
D. Piggul can only be created by the kohen performing the service
Correct: B — Abaye groups these three sages as all holding that even when two people are involved — one intending and one acting — the intention of one governs the action of the other.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does R' Eliezer NOT necessarily accept R' Shimon ben Elazar's ruling about Shabbat?
A. Shabbat liability requires planned, constructive labor by the doer himself
B. Shabbat laws are rabbinic, not biblical
C. Cross-person intent only applies to sacred matters
D. R' Eliezer holds Shabbat violations require witnesses
Correct: A — The Gemara explains that Shabbat prohibits only מלאכת מחשבת — planned, constructive labor. Since the person who carried the item out didn't intend a creative act, R' Eliezer might say cross-person intent doesn't apply to Shabbat.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which sprinklings of the Yom Kippur bull and goat are indispensable?
A. Only the sprinkling between the staves of the Ark
B. The sprinklings on the Ark, Curtain, and Golden Altar — missing even one disqualifies
C. All sprinklings including pouring the remainder on the altar base
D. Only the sprinklings on the Curtain and the Golden Altar
Correct: B — The Mishna states that the three sets of sprinklings — between the staves of the Ark, on the Curtain, and on the Golden Altar — are all indispensable. However, pouring the remainder on the western base of the external altar is NOT indispensable.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How is the blood of a leper's guilt offering collected?
A. Entirely by hand, as the verse says 'the priest shall put'
B. Entirely in a vessel, like all other guilt offerings
C. Two priests collect simultaneously — one by hand for the leper, one in a vessel for the altar
D. First in a vessel, then transferred to the priest's hand
Correct: C — The Tosefta derives from two verses that both methods are required: 'the priest shall put' teaches collection by hand (for placing on the leper), while 'as the sin offering is, so is the guilt offering' teaches a vessel is needed for the altar. Two priests therefore collect simultaneously.
