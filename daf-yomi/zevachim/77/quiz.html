QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why can't an uncertain leper bring his lamb as both a leper's guilt offering and a provisional guilt offering (asham talui)?
A. A leper's guilt offering is a first-year lamb, but a provisional guilt offering requires a second-year ram
B. The blood applications are performed differently for each type
C. A provisional guilt offering cannot be eaten, but a leper's guilt offering must be
D. R' Shimon holds one cannot bring any guilt offering voluntarily
Correct: A — Ravina rebuked Rav Reḥumi: a leper's asham is a keves (first-year lamb, Lev. 14:10), while an asham talui must be an ayil (second-year ram, Lev. 5:15) — they are different animals entirely.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Eliezer, what happens to sin offering limbs that became mixed with burnt offering limbs?
A. They are all sent to the place of burning
B. They are all placed on the altar, with the sin offering flesh treated as wood
C. The priest must separate them by visual inspection
D. They wait until they decay and then are buried
Correct: B — R' Eliezer rules that all limbs go on the altar. The sin offering flesh is viewed as pieces of wood — fuel, not an offering — based on the derivation from Leviticus 2:11–12.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why do the Rabbis distinguish between sin offering limbs and blemished animal limbs in a mixture?
A. Sin offering limbs are more valuable than blemished limbs
B. Blemished limbs are 'repulsive' (me'usin) to the altar, while sin offering limbs are not
C. Sin offering limbs have already been sanctified but blemished limbs were never sanctified
D. The Torah explicitly permits sin offering limbs as wood but not blemished limbs
Correct: B — The Rabbis concede that sin offering limbs in a mixture can be burned as wood because they are not inherently repulsive. Blemished limbs, however, are repulsive to the altar and may not be placed on it even as fuel.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Where does the uncertain leper's priest redeem the leftover oil, and why?
A. Outside the courtyard, because non-sacred items cannot be inside
B. Inside the courtyard — the non-sacred oil is 'found' there, not 'brought' in
C. At the altar itself, because that is where offerings are redeemed
D. In the Chamber of Oils, which is designated for this purpose
Correct: B — Redeeming outside would disqualify the oil (leaving the courtyard). Redeeming inside is permitted because the non-sacred oil is 'found' in the courtyard on its own — the priest didn't actively bring a non-sacred item in.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Elazar, how must R' Eliezer's rule about sacrificing mixed fit and blemished heads be carried out?
A. All heads must be sacrificed simultaneously
B. Each head must be sacrificed individually after inspection
C. The heads must be sacrificed two by two, ensuring at least one of each pair is fit
D. Only the majority of heads may be sacrificed
Correct: C — R' Elazar explains that R' Eliezer only permitted sacrificing the heads in pairs — each pair guarantees that at least one head is certainly from a fit animal. Sacrificing one by one risks offering the single blemished head alone.
