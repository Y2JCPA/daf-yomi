Mixed Limbs on the Altar: Burn or Discard?

R' Eliezer
When sin offering limbs mix with burnt offering limbs, place them all on the altar — the sin offering flesh is treated as if it were wood, not as an offering.
Chachamim
Wait until all the limbs decay beyond recognition, then send them all to the place of burning in the courtyard.
R' Eliezer
The Torah itself permits burning leaven and honey as wood on the altar (Lev. 2:11–12) — so too any prohibited substance may be burned for the sake of wood.
Chachamim
The verse says "them" — only leaven and honey may be burned as wood. Other prohibited substances may not go on the altar at all.
Core Dispute Can items forbidden as offerings still be placed on the altar as mere fuel? R' Eliezer says yes broadly; the Rabbis limit this to leaven and honey only.

The Uncertain Leper's Oil Dilemma

A person of uncertain leper status must bring a log of oil
↓
R' Shimon: Bring it as a voluntary gift offering with a stipulation
↓
Priest stipulates: If not a leper → sprinkling is not a rite
↓
Problem: After handful removal + refilling, some oil was never part of the handful
↓
Solution
Priest redeems the oil by paying its value to the Temple treasury
But where?
Must redeem inside the courtyard — the non-sacred oil is found there by itself, not actively brought in
Key Principle Non-sacred oil found in the courtyard after redemption differs from bringing non-sacred items into sacred space.

Can the Uncertain Leper Donate Oil?

Gemara
But doesn't R' Shimon hold that one may not donate oil as a voluntary gift?
R' Shimon
The remedy of a person (refuato shel adam) is different — this is the only way for the uncertain leper to achieve ritual purification.
Rav Reḥumi
Why not bring a lamb as a guilt offering and stipulate: if not a leper, let it be a provisional guilt offering (asham talui)?
Ravina
Torah, Torah! Where is your learning? A leper's guilt offering is a lamb (1st year), but a provisional guilt offering must be a ram (2nd year)!
Ravina's Rebuke The leper's asham (Lev. 14:10) requires a first-year keves (lamb), while the provisional asham talui (Lev. 5:15) requires a second-year ayil (ram) — they are incompatible.

Mishna: Sin Offering Limbs Mixed with Burnt Offering Limbs

R' Eliezer

  • Place all limbs on the altar
  • Sin offering flesh = wood (not an offering)
  • Derives from Lev. 2:11–12: leaven & honey may be burned as wood → so may other forbidden items
  • "Them" teaches that leaven/honey have a prohibition against offering — but all items may serve as wood

Chachamim (Rabbis)

  • Wait for all limbs to decay
  • Then send to place of burning
  • "Them" = only leaven & honey as wood
  • Other prohibited substances may not go on the altar even as fuel
Note Both agree the sin offering limbs are not offered — the dispute is whether they can be placed on the altar as mere combustible material.

Mishna (Amud B): Fit + Blemished Burnt Offering Limbs Mixed

🐑 Limbs of fit burnt offerings become intermingled with limbs of blemished burnt offerings
↓
✋ R' Eliezer: If the head of one was sacrificed → all heads may be sacrificed
↓
✋ Same rule: If one's legs were sacrificed → all legs may be sacrificed
↓
🚫 Rabbis: Even if all but one limb were sacrificed, the remaining limb might be the unfit one
↓
🔥 Rabbis: All limbs go to the place of burning in the courtyard
R' Eliezer's Logic Assume the first limb sacrificed was the unfit one — all subsequent limbs are presumed fit.
