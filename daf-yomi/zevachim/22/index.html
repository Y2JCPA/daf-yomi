Can a Small Conduit Vessel Replace the Basin?

Rav Adda bar Aḥa
One may drill a hole in the Kiyor (Basin) and place a small vessel as a conduit. A priest may sanctify hands and feet from it — provided the Basin holds enough water for four priests.
Gemara Challenge
But the Torah says "from it" (מִמֶּנּוּ) — meaning from the Basin itself, not another vessel!
Gemara Resolution
The verse repeats "they should wash" (יִרְחֲצוּ) — this amplifies the law to include any service vessel (כְּלִי שָׁרֵת).
Gemara Challenge
If so, even a non-sacred vessel should be valid!
Gemara Resolution
"From it" excludes non-sacred vessels. Service vessels are included because they require anointing like the Basin; non-sacred vessels do not.
PRINCIPLE The Torah uses both an inclusive term ("they should wash") and an exclusive term ("from it") — the Gemara must determine what each includes and excludes.

Abaye's A Fortiori: Why Not a Non-Sacred Vessel?

The base (כַּן) of the Basin was anointed together with the Basin (Exodus 40:11)
↓
Yet water from the base is unfit for sanctification (R' Yehuda, from Exodus 30:18)
↓
A fortiori: A non-sacred vessel, which was never anointed, should certainly be unfit!
↓
Mar Zutra refutes:
↓
Base
Its inside is not made for use — it's just a stand
Non-Sacred Vessel
Its inside is made for use — so the comparison fails!
CONCLUSION Since the a fortiori is refuted, we return to the textual derivation: "from it" directly excludes non-sacred vessels.

Three Measures: Mikveh vs. Basin vs. Hand-Washing

Mikveh (40 Se'ah)

  • Liquids may complete the measure
  • "Add a se'ah, remove a se'ah" — valid (up to majority)
  • Creatures originating in water count (R' Shimon b. Gamliel)
  • Even liquefied fish eye suffices (R' Yitzḥak bar Avdimi)

Basin (Kiyor)

  • Same liquids that complete mikveh may complete Basin measure
  • Mikveh water is fit for Basin use (Reish Lakish)
  • Dispute: Must it be spring water? (R' Yishmael) or any water? (Rabbis)

Hand-Washing (¼ Log)

  • Liquids that complete mikveh may NOT complete hand-washing measure
  • "Add a se'ah, remove a se'a" method does NOT work
  • Stricter standard than mikveh or Basin
REISH LAKISH'S RULE Any liquid that can complete a mikveh can complete the Basin — but cannot complete the quarter-log for ritual hand-washing.

Must Basin Water Be Flowing (Spring) Water?

R' Yirmeya / Reish Lakish
Water of a mikveh (standing water) is fit to be used as water of the Basin.
Gemara Challenge
A baraita says innards must be washed "with water" — not wine, not mixed wine. It excludes Basin water because it has a modifier in its name. This implies Basin water is special — i.e., flowing spring water!
R' Yishmael
Basin water must be מַיִם חַיִּים — flowing spring water.
Rabbis (via R' Yoḥanan)
It may be any type of water. No spring water requirement.
TANNAITIC DISPUTE R' Yishmael requires spring water for the Basin; the Rabbis permit any water — a dispute reported by R' Yoḥanan.

Uncircumcised Priest: Derivation from Ezekiel

Verse / PhraseDerivation
"No stranger...shall enter My Sanctuary" (Ezek. 44:9)"Stranger" = one whose actions are estranged from Heaven (apostate priest)
"Uncircumcised in heart" (Ezek. 44:9)Clarifies "stranger" refers to a priest, not a gentile
"Or uncircumcised in flesh" (Ezek. 44:9)Extends disqualification to the physically uncircumcised
"To profane My house" (Ezek. 44:7)Service is desecrated after the fact — not merely prohibited
Both phrases neededHeart alone → maybe flesh is OK (heart directed to Heaven); Flesh alone → maybe heart is OK (not physically repulsive)
RAV ḤISDA "We did not learn this from the Torah of Moses; we learned it from the words of Ezekiel son of Buzi." — An unusual source for a Temple disqualification.
