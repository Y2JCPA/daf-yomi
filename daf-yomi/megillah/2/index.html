📜 Who Reads When — The Megilla Schedule

Rule 1: Walled cities (מוּקָּפוֹת חוֹמָה) always read on the 15th of Adar
↓
Rule 2: Unwalled towns (עֲיָרוֹת) read on the 14th of Adar
↓
Rule 3: Villages (כְּפָרִים) may advance their reading to the nearest Monday or Thursday (market/court day)
↓
Shabbat exception: When the 14th falls on Shabbat, both villages and large towns advance to Thursday the 12th
↓
Authority: All these dates enacted by the Great Assembly (אַנְשֵׁי כְּנֶסֶת הַגְּדוֹלָה); alluded to in Esther 9:31

📖 Two Derivations for Reading on 11th–13th

Rav Shemen bar Abba (R. Yoḥanan)
From "in their times" — בְּעִתֵּיהֶם (Esther 9:31). The compound plural implies multiple times were enacted, not only the standard 14th and 15th.
Gemara objection
That plural is needed to show that walled and unwalled cities celebrate at different times — not to add extra days!
Rav Shemen bar Abba
If so, the verse would say "in its time." The double plural עִתֵּיהֶם adds exactly two extra days — the 11th and 13th.
R. Shmuel bar Naḥmani
From "as the days" — כִּימֵי (Esther 9:22). The plural כִּימֵי (literally 'like the days of') implies more than two days.
How the 11th is derived Rav Shmuel bar Yitzḥak: The 13th is a fast day (Ta'anit Esther) when Torah-reading assemblies gather. The preceding weekdays of assembly (11th, 12th) are thus included in the reading window.

🏙️ Unwalled Towns vs. Walled Cities vs. Shushan

Unwalled Towns (פְּרָזִים)

  • Read Megilla on the 14th of Adar
  • Source: "Therefore the Jews of the villages… make the fourteenth a day of gladness" (Esther 9:19)
  • Majority of Jewish communities fall in this category
  • May advance to Mon/Thu when 14th conflicts with Shabbat

Walled Cities (מוּקָּפוֹת חוֹמָה)

  • Read Megilla on the 15th of Adar
  • Must be walled since days of Joshua bin Nun (Mishna tanna)
  • Nearby towns within one mil share the city's date
  • Source: gezerah shavah between two uses of פְּרָזִי

Shushan — Special Case

  • Reads on the 15th despite not being walled since Joshua
  • Reason (Rava): the miracle occurred there on the 15th
  • "Every province and every city" (Esther 9:28) — doubled phrasing honors Shushan distinctly
  • No other city receives this honor by miracle-proximity alone

🔍 Deriving the Full 11th–15th Range

Problem: The Megilla text mentions only the 14th and 15th — where is the source for reading on the 11th–13th?
↓
Path A
"In their times"
עִתֵּיהֶם (Esther 9:31)
→ Rav Shemen bar Abba
Path B
"As the days"
כִּימֵי (Esther 9:22)
→ R. Shmuel bar Naḥmani
↓
Both paths establish 13th and 15th as added days. How do we reach the 11th?
↓
Rav Shmuel bar Yitzḥak: The 13th is a fast day with Torah-reading assemblies. Assembly days preceding it (11th, 12th) are included as preparation days.
↓
Upper bound — "It shall not pass" (Esther 9:27): Purim cannot be delayed past the 15th. Range is fixed: 11th–15th only.

⚖️ Joshua vs. Ahasuerus — The Wall Dispute

Tanna of our Mishna
A city must be walled since Joshua bin Nun's time to read on the 15th. Derived via gezerah shavah: פְּרָזִי in Deuteronomy 3:5 ↔ פְּרָזִי in Esther 9:19.
Rabbi Yehoshua ben Korḥa
Walled since Ahasuerus's time suffices — we compare to Shushan, the paradigm walled city, which was walled in Ahasuerus's era, not Joshua's.
Gemara: Why not Ahasuerus? (Mishna tanna)
To honor the Land of Israel, which lay desolate in Ahasuerus's time. Using Joshua's era recognizes Israel's ancient walled cities.
Rava
Shushan is entirely different — it reads on the 15th because the miracle occurred there, not because of any wall criterion. The wall rule applies to all other cities.
Ruling Halacha follows the Mishna's tanna: walled since Joshua's time. Shushan reads on the 15th as a unique miracle-honor. The gezerah shavah (פְּרָזִי / פְּרָזִי) is the operative derivation.
