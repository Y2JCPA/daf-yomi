🕍 Sanctity Persists Beyond the Temple

מִקְדָּשׁ מְעַט — "Yet I have been to them as a little sanctuary" (Yechezkel 11:16)
Core Theme Megillah 29 explores how holiness persists in exile: the Shechina accompanies Israel to Egypt, Babylon, and Edom; synagogues serve as mikdash me'at ('little sanctuaries'); and Babylonian synagogues will one day be transported to Eretz Yisrael. The daf then pivots to detailed laws of the four special Shabbatot of Adar and the debates over which Torah portions are read.

🕍 The Shechina Follows Israel Into Every Exile

כָּל מָקוֹם שֶׁגָּלוּ — שְׁכִינָה עִמָּהֶם — Wherever they were exiled, the Shechina was with them
Rabbi Shimon ben Yochai "Come and see how beloved Israel is before the Holy One, Blessed be He" — wherever they were exiled, the Divine Presence went with them.

• Exiled to Egypt → Shechina accompanied them
• Exiled to Babylon → Shechina accompanied them
• Exiled to Edom → Shechina accompanied them

And when they are ultimately redeemed, the Shechina will return with them.

Abaye adds: The Shechina in Babylon rests specifically in the ancient synagogue of Huzal and the synagogue destroyed-and-rebuilt in Neharde'a.

📜 The Four Special Shabbatot of Adar

1st — Shekalim

  • Rosh Chodesh Adar or the preceding Shabbat
  • Topic: Half-shekel Temple tax collection
  • Debate: Ex. 30 ('When you take the count') vs. Num. 28 ('My offerings')

2nd — Zachor

  • Shabbat immediately before Purim
  • 'Remember what Amalek did' (Deut. 25:17–19)
  • Mitzva to remember and obliterate Amalek

3rd — Parah

  • Shabbat after Purim
  • Red Heifer purification rite (Num. 19)
  • Prepares the people for Pesach purity

4th — HaChodesh

  • Shabbat before or on Rosh Chodesh Nisan
  • 'This month shall be for you' (Ex. 12:1–2)
  • Announces the month of Pesach

📖 Which Portion is 'Parshat Shekalim'?

Rav
Read 'Command the Children of Israel: My offering, the provision of My offerings' (Numbers 28) — the communal offerings purchased with the shekalim are detailed there.
Shmuel
Read 'When you take the count of the Children of Israel' (Exodus 30) — the actual obligation to give the half-shekel is written in that passage.
Rav Yosef
Three occurrences of תְּרוּמָה (contribution) in Numbers 28 allude to three collections: at the Exodus, for the Tabernacle, and the annual collection — supporting Rav's view.
Baraita (per Shmuel)
A baraita is explicitly taught in accordance with Shmuel: 'When you take the count' is Parshat Shekalim; the haftara is the story of Jehoiada the priest (II Kings 12).
Practical Difference on Rosh Chodesh Adar that falls on Shabbat According to Rav, 'My offerings' (Num. 28) serves double duty as both the New Moon and Shekalim portion — only 6 readers read from it. According to Shmuel, 'When you take the count' displaces the regular New Moon reading entirely.

🔀 Kappendarya: Using a Synagogue as a Shortcut

May one pass through a synagogue as a shortcut (kappendarya)?
↓
Path existed BEFORE synagogue was built
Permitted — public right of access cannot be annulled by later sanctity (Rabbi Abbahu)
Entered synagogue for another purpose
Permitted to exit the other side — since initial entry was not for shortcutting (Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak)
Entered specifically to use as shortcut
Prohibited — degrades the sanctity of the synagogue
