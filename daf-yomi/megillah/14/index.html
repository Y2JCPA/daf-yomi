🤝 The Parable of the Mound and the Ditch

לאחד שהיה לו תל בתוך שדהו ולאחד שהיה לו בור בתוך שדהו
Mashal Ahasuerus had a 'mound' — surplus hatred of the Jews he wished to unload. Haman had a 'ditch' — obsessive desire to destroy them that he needed someone else to authorize. Each thought he was getting the better deal: Ahasuerus rid himself of his problem for free; Haman gained royal permission at no cost. Together they sealed destruction — a partnership of mutual convenience masking mutual catastrophe.

🎵 Why Is Hallel Not Recited on Purim?

Baraita
Before Israel entered the Land, all lands were equal for Hallel. After entering, only miracles performed in Eretz Yisrael warrant Hallel. The Purim miracle occurred in Persia — outside the Land — so no Hallel.
Rav Naḥman
The reading of the Megilla itself IS the recitation of Hallel. No separate Hallel is needed because the Megilla reading fulfills that obligation.
Rava
When leaving Egypt we became truly free — 'servants of God, not of Pharaoh.' After Purim we were still servants of Ahasuerus. The full formula of liberation that triggers Hallel was not achieved.
Gemara
Challenge to both Rav Naḥman and Rava: If the Baraita's reason (miracle outside the Land) already explains it, why do you need your additional reasons?
The Question Stands Each Amora offers his own independent reason. The Gemara presses each one — the debate remains open as multiple valid explanations coexist.

👩‍🔮 The Seven Prophetesses

Sarah

  • Perceived hidden angelic speech — 'Is anything too wonderful for the Lord?'
  • Inferred: she knew what the angel said before Abraham told her

Miriam

  • Prophesied before Moses's birth: 'My mother will bear a savior of Israel'
  • Called 'sister of Aaron' — prophecy preceded Moses's existence

Deborah

  • 'Wife of Lappidoth' — made wicks (lappidot) for the Sanctuary
  • Sat under a palm tree for tzniut — avoided yiḥud with male visitors

Hannah

  • 'My horn is exalted' = anointed kings (David, Solomon); not 'my pitcher'
  • 'No rock like God' — no artist like God who fashions embryos within embryos

Abigail

  • Reasoned halachically with David about bloodguilt and menstrual law
  • Named among prophetesses — her wisdom to restrain David was divinely inspired

Huldah

  • Prophesied while Jeremiah was away returning the Ten Tribes
  • Josiah chose her for her compassion; named after weasel (ḥuldah) — haughtiness criticized

Esther

  • 'Clothed herself in royalty' = ruaḥ hakodesh (Holy Spirit) rested upon her
  • The unusual phrasing proves prophetic inspiration, not mere royal garments

🗡️ Abigail's Encounter with David

Nabal insults David's men — refuses provisions; David vows to kill every male in Nabal's household before morning
↓
Abigail rides out secretly to intercept David — 'by the covert (beseter) of the mountain' alludes to menstrual blood (niddah) she will raise
↓
Halachic argument #1: David must not shed innocent blood — guilt of bloodshed falls on the pursuer even when the cause is just
↓
Halachic argument #2: Nabal is a rebel against the anointed king — he is already liable to death, so David need not act in anger
↓
Abigail reveals her thigh — David is inflamed with desire and walks three parasangs driven by passion's fire
↓
Prophetic parting words: 'Your soul shall be bound in the bond of life' — and she hints she hopes to be his wife; 'while speaking, she holds the spindle'
