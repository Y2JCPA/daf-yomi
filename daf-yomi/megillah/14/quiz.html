QUESTION 1 OF 5
Rabbi Abba bar Kahana says Ahasuerus removing his ring was more effective than 48 prophets and 7 prophetesses. What is the explanation?
A. The ring was a sacred object with inherent spiritual power
B. The prophets never actually prophesied specifically for the Jewish people's benefit
C. The existential threat of the decree caused immediate repentance, while the prophets' warnings had gone unheeded for generations
D. Haman's decree was cancelled by Ahasuerus himself, making it ultimately harmless
Correct: C — The prophets warned the Jewish people over many generations but were ignored. Haman's genocidal decree — sealed by the royal ring — created immediate mortal fear that drove the people to sincere repentance, achieving what centuries of prophecy could not.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava, why is Hallel not recited on Purim?
A. The Purim miracle occurred outside Eretz Yisrael and therefore does not qualify
B. Reading the Megilla itself serves as the Hallel for Purim
C. After Purim the Jews remained servants of Ahasuerus — not fully free — so the Exodus formula of liberation does not apply
D. The miracle was too private and personal to warrant public praise
Correct: C — Rava teaches: When leaving Egypt we became servants of God and not of Pharaoh — complete freedom. After Purim, however, we remained subjects of Ahasuerus. Since Hallel celebrates the transition to full freedom, it cannot be recited for Purim.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the verse call Miriam 'the sister of Aaron' rather than 'the sister of Moses' when identifying her as a prophetess?
A. She was only a half-sister of Moses through a different mother
B. Her prophecy was given before Moses was born — at that time only Aaron existed as her brother
C. Moses did not wish to be associated with prophecy or miracles
D. Aaron was the elder sibling and therefore takes precedence in all genealogical references
Correct: B — Miriam's famous prophecy — that her mother would bear a son who would save Israel — was given before Moses was born. At the time of that prophecy, only Aaron was her brother. The Torah therefore calls her 'sister of Aaron' to signal that her prophetic career predated Moses's very existence.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did King Josiah send emissaries to Huldah rather than to the prophet Jeremiah?
A. Jeremiah had gone to bring back the Ten Tribes from exile (Rabbi Yoḥanan), and separately, women are more compassionate and Josiah hoped for a gentler word (School of Rabbi Sheila)
B. Huldah was considered a more reliable and accurate prophet than Jeremiah at the time
C. Jeremiah had refused to prophesy for kings and was under a self-imposed vow of silence
D. Huldah was a close relative of Josiah and therefore uniquely trusted
Correct: A — Two independent answers are given: Rabbi Yoḥanan says Jeremiah was simply not present — he had gone to return the Ten Tribes from their exile. The School of Rabbi Sheila offers a different reason: women are by nature more compassionate, and Josiah hoped Huldah would deliver a less devastating prophecy about his kingdom's fate.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does the Gemara derive from the verse 'Esther clothed herself in royalty' (Esther 5:1) rather than 'in royal garments'?
A. She deliberately chose the simplest royal robe to appear humble before Ahasuerus
B. She borrowed the regalia of a previous queen to appear more legitimate
C. The Holy Spirit (ruaḥ hakodesh) rested upon her — confirming her status as a prophetess
D. She was figuratively 'wearing' the prayers of all the Jewish people as her garment
Correct: C — The Gemara notes the verse is unusual: it says she 'clothed herself in royalty' not 'in royal garments.' This teaches that what clothed her was not fabric but the Holy Spirit — ruaḥ hakodesh — which is proof of her prophetic inspiration and confirms her place among the seven prophetesses.
