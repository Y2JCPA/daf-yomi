5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
From which verse does the Gemara derive that Megilla may only be read during the day?
A. Deuteronomy 6:4 — 'Hear O Israel'
B. Esther 9:28 — 'these days shall be remembered and kept'
C. Psalms 113:3 — 'from sunrise to sunset'
D. Leviticus 23:24 — 'a memorial of blowing'
Correct: B — The Gemara derives the daytime requirement for Megilla from Esther 9:28: 'these days shall be remembered' — 'days,' implying daytime and not nights.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi testified he read the Megilla as a minor before Rabbi Yehuda. How did the Sages reject this proof?
A. A minor's testimony is invalid in all cases
B. One cannot bring proof from the behavior of the very one who permits it
C. The Megilla was not read on the correct day
D. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi was actually an adult at the time
Correct: B — The Sages gave a two-part rejection: (1) one cannot cite the permitter's own practice as proof of permissibility, and (2) his testimony was given while he himself was still a minor.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
From which text does Rabbi Zeira derive that daybreak (amud hashachar) is the beginning of daytime?
A. Genesis 1:5 — 'God called the light, Day'
B. Leviticus 15:28 — 'she shall count for herself seven days'
C. Nehemiah 4:15 — 'from the rising of the morning till the stars appeared'
D. Deuteronomy 26:13 — 'I have removed the sacred things out of my house'
Correct: C — Rabbi Zeira derives from Nehemiah 4:15 that 'rising of the morning' (i.e., daybreak) marks the beginning of day, and 'till the stars appeared' marks the end.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Mishna specifically teach that a zava ketana (woman who observes a day for a day) must wait until sunrise to immerse?
A. Because she is more stringent than a nidda
B. Lest one think she can immerse at night, since she counts only one day — so we teach that counting requires daytime
C. Because the Torah explicitly requires her to wait for sunrise
D. Because her immersion must coincide with sunrise prayers
Correct: B — One might think she can immerse at night since she only counts one clean day (not seven). The Gemara teaches that 'counting' can only occur during the day, so immersion is only valid after that daytime count — meaning she must wait until after sunrise.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which of the following is listed in Mishna 20b as a nighttime mitzva (valid all night)?
A. Sounding the shofar on Rosh HaShana
B. Taking the lulav on Sukkot
C. Reaping the omer on the night following the first day of Passover
D. Reading the Megilla on Purim night
Correct: C — The Mishna lists nighttime mitzvot including reaping the omer (on the night following the first day of Passover) and burning the fats and limbs of offerings — all valid throughout the entire night.
