⏰ Timing of Mitzvot: Sunrise, Daybreak, and the Full Day

Mishna 1: Megilla, circumcision, immersion, and sprinkling of purification waters — all require sunrise
↓
Question: What counts as 'day'? Daybreak (amud hashachar) or sunrise (netz hachama)?
↓
Rava: From Genesis 1:5 — 'God called the light, Day' — the moment light appears already is called day
↓
Rabbi Zeira: From Nehemiah 4:15 — 'from rising of the morning till the stars appeared' — daybreak begins the day; stars begin the night
↓
Mishna 2: A long list of mitzvot are valid the entire day — from sunrise until nightfall
↓
Counterpart: Night mitzvot (omer reaping, burning fats and limbs of offerings) are valid the entire night

🧏 The Deaf Person and the Megilla

Rabbi Yehuda
A deaf person may read the Megilla even ab initio. Although my teacher Rabbi Elazar ben Azarya requires one to make Shema audible to one's own ears, Megilla is different.
Rabbi Yosei
A deaf person is disqualified even ab initio from reading the Megilla — hearing is integral to the mitzva of 'remembering' these days.
Gemara
Perhaps Rabbi Yehuda actually holds like his teacher — that hearing is required — yet permits a deaf person only post facto. The baraita about teruma can be read accordingly.
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
Proof that minors may read: when I was a minor, I read the Megilla before Rabbi Yehuda! The Sages replied: One cannot bring proof from the testimony of the very one who permits it.
Double Rejection The Sages rejected Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's proof on two grounds: (1) you cannot cite the permitter's own behavior as proof, and (2) his testimony was given while he himself was a minor

🌅 Daybreak vs. Sunrise: Derivation of Daytime

Mishna states: These mitzvot are valid if done 'after daybreak' (בעלות השחר) — but the ideal is sunrise
↓
Rava's Proof
Genesis 1:5: 'God called the light, Day' — at the first appearance of light (daybreak), it is already called 'day'
Difficulty Raised
Then 'darkness He called night' would imply any darkness is night — even the brief period between sunset and full dark?
↓
Rabbi Zeira's Proof — Nehemiah 4:15: 'from the rising of the morning till the stars appeared' — 'rising of morning' = daybreak = start of day
↓
Nehemiah 4:16 confirms: night = stars appearing — thus resolving Rava's difficulty about intermediate darkness

☀️🌙 All-Day Mitzvot vs. All-Night Mitzvot

☀️ Valid All Day

  • Megilla reading (Purim)
  • Hallel (Rosh Chodesh, Yom Tov)
  • Shofar (Rosh HaShana)
  • Lulav (Sukkot)
  • Mussaf prayer
  • Confession over bulls (Sanhedrin / Kohen Gadol)
  • Tithing declaration (Maaser)
  • Laying hands on an offering
  • Slaughtering an offering
  • Waving offerings (tenufah)
  • Bringing meal-offerings near the altar
  • Sota drinking bitter waters
  • Breaking neck of egla arufa
  • Purifying a metzora (leper)

🌙 Valid All Night

  • Reaping the omer (night after 1st day of Passover)
  • Burning the fats of offerings on the altar
  • Burning the limbs of offerings on the altar

💧 The Woman Who Observes a Day for a Day

וְסָפְרָה לָּהּ שִׁבְעַת יָמִים (ויקרא טו:כח) — She shall count for herself seven days
Special Case: Zava Ketana A zava ketana (woman who experienced one or two days of irregular bleeding) must observe one clean day before immersing — unlike a full zava who counts seven. Why specify she must wait until sunrise? One might think: since she counts only one day (not seven like a zava gedola or a nidda), perhaps she can immerse as soon as that single day ends — even at night. Therefore the Mishna teaches explicitly: she too must wait until after sunrise, because counting can only occur during the day, and immersion is only valid after the count is complete. Also: 'All the days of her issue shall be to her as the bed of her menstruation' (Lev. 15:26) — she cannot immerse on the same day as her bleeding.

📋 Megillah 20 — Summary Map

Mishna 1: Megilla, circumcision, immersion, sprinkling — valid only after sunrise
↓
Ab initio: Must wait for netz hachama (sunrise)
Post facto: Amud hashachar (daybreak) suffices — derived from Nehemiah 4:15–16
↓
Mishna 2: All-day mitzvot (Megilla, hallel, shofar, lulav, offerings…) valid from sunrise to nightfall
↓
Mirror rule: Night mitzvot (omer reaping, burning fats and limbs) valid from nightfall to dawn
