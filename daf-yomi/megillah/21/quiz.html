Megillah 21 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why may multiple people read the Megilla simultaneously while Torah may only be read by one person at a time?
A. The Megilla is shorter than the Torah, making it easier to follow multiple voices
B. The Megilla is beloved (חביבה) to the congregation, so they pay close attention and are not distracted
C. The Rabbis wanted to speed up the Purim service for efficiency
D. Multiple readers were the established custom in Babylonia from ancient times
Correct: B — The Gemara explains: חביבה מגילה על הציבור — the Megilla is cherished by the congregation, so people listen carefully and remain focused even when multiple voices read together, unlike the Torah where distraction is a concern.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
From which verse does Rabbi Abbahu derive that Torah must be read standing, and that a teacher cannot sit on a raised couch while a student sits on the ground?
A. "And Moses went up to God" (Exodus 19:3)
B. "And I sat on the mount forty days" (Deuteronomy 9:9)
C. "But as for you, stand here with Me" (Deuteronomy 5:28)
D. "You shall rise before the aged" (Leviticus 19:32)
Correct: C — Rabbi Abbahu derives from ואתה פה עמד עמדי — 'But as for you, stand here with Me' — that just as Moses stood at Sinai, the teacher and student must both stand and be at equal height during Torah transmission.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How many blessings does Rav Sheshet from Katrazya recite before reading the Megilla, and what do they correspond to?
A. One blessing — על מקרא מגילה only
B. Two blessings — the miracle and the שהחיינו
C. Three blessings — alluded to by the letters מ (מקרא), נ (נסים), ח (החיינו)
D. Four blessings — one for each section of the Megilla
Correct: C — Rav Sheshet recited three blessings alluded to by מ-נ-ח: על מקרא מגילה (the mitzva of reading), שעשה נסים לאבותינו (miracles for our ancestors), and שהחיינו (sustaining us to this time).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rava, which of the three Torah readers is praiseworthy for reading four verses instead of three?
A. Only the first reader, due to the privilege of precedence
B. Only the last reader, due to the principle of elevating sanctity
C. Only the middle reader, like the central lamp of the menorah
D. Any of the three readers — first, middle, or last — each has its own source of distinction
Correct: D — Rava teaches that all three are praiseworthy for reading 4 verses: the first due to the privilege of precedence (as in the Temple treasury chambers), the middle because the central position is dignified (like the central menorah lamp), and the last because one elevates sanctity rather than downgrading.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did the Sages institute that every Torah reader recites blessings both before AND after their reading?
A. To double the merit of Torah learning for the congregation
B. As a decree accounting for those who arrive mid-reading and those who leave early
C. Because the first blessing was considered insufficient on its own
D. To align the Torah reading practice with the Megilla reading practice
Correct: B — The Gemara explains the decree: those who arrive in the middle of the Torah reading need to hear a blessing beforehand (so each reader says one before), and those who leave early need to have already heard a blessing after a reading (so each reader says one after). This ensures everyone hears both blessings.
