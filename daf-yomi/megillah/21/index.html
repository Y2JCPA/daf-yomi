🪑 Megilla vs. Torah: Sitting or Standing?

ואתה פה עמד עמדי — Devarim 5:28
Core Distinction Megilla: May be read while sitting — no requirement to stand.
Torah: Must be read while standing, derived from "But as for you, stand here with Me" (Devarim 5:28).

Rabbi Abbahu adds: Just as Moses stood at Sinai to receive Torah, so too a teacher must not sit on a raised couch while a student sits on the ground — they must be at equal height.

Historical note: From Moses until Rabban Gamliel, Torah study was conducted only while standing. Once Rabban Gamliel died, weakness descended and they began to study sitting.

📖 One or Two Reading the Megilla?

Mishna
Whether one person reads the Megilla or two people read it together, they have fulfilled their obligation.
Baraita
This is NOT so for Torah reading — only one person reads at a time. One reads, one translates into Aramaic, but no more than one person reads Torah simultaneously.
Gemara
Why may multiple people read Megilla? Because the Megilla is beloved (חביבה) to the congregation — they pay close attention and will not be distracted by the different voices.
Abaye
The custom of reciting a blessing applies only to the blessing recited BEFORE the Megilla reading — whether to say it depends on local custom. The blessing after is its own matter.
Principle חביבה מגילה על הציבור — The Megilla is cherished by the congregation, ensuring attentiveness even with multiple voices.

🙏 Blessings for Megilla Reading

Before Reading — 3 blessings (Rav Sheshet from Katrazya recited these, alluded to by letters מ-נ-ח):
↓
מ — על מקרא מגילה: '…Who commanded us concerning the reading of the Megilla'
↓
נ — שעשה נסים לאבותינו: '…Who performed miracles for our ancestors in those days at this time'
↓
ח — שהחיינו וקיימנו: '…Who has kept us alive, sustained us, and brought us to this time'
↓
After Reading (where customary): Blessed are You… Who takes up our grievance, judges our claim, avenges our wrong, punishes our enemies… Blessed is He Who punishes the wicked.
↓
The word 'over' (על) implies precedence — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak derives from "Ahimaaz overran [vaya'avor] the Cushite" (II Samuel 18:23) that 'avor' means to precede or go before.

📊 Minimum 10 Verses & Rava's Teaching

Minimum 10 verses must be read at any public Torah reading (Rav Shimi bar Ashi's baraita). A generic verse like 'And God spoke to Moses saying…' does not count toward the 10.
↓
3 readers × 3 verses each = only 9. Why 10? What do they correspond to?
↓
Rav Asi
3 readers = Kohanim, Leviim, Yisraelim — the three divisions of the Jewish people
R. Yehoshua ben Levi
10 verses = 10 idlers sitting in the synagogue, always available for communal needs
Rav Yosef
10 verses = 10 utterances of Creation (9 explicit 'God said' + 'Bereishit' itself = 10)
↓
Rava: If any reader reads 4 verses, he is praiseworthy — first (privilege of precedence), middle (dignity like central menorah lamp), last (principle: elevate sanctity, do not downgrade)
