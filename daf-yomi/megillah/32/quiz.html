Megillah 32 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Mattana's final ruling, what is the correct sequence for reading from the Torah?
A. Close the scroll, recite the blessing, then open and read
B. Recite the blessing before opening the scroll at all
C. Open the scroll, see the place, recite the blessing while open, then read
D. Open the scroll, read a few words silently, bless, then read aloud
Correct: C — Rav Mattana rules: open the scroll, see the place, recite the blessing immediately without closing, and then read. This aligns with Rabbi Yehuda and eliminates the extra open-close-open steps of Rabbi Meir.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was Ulla's reason for the opinion that a blessing should not be recited over an open Torah scroll?
A. Holding an open scroll during a blessing is physically tiring and disrespectful
B. Onlookers may think the blessing text is actually written in the Torah
C. The open scroll may blow shut and interrupt the blessing
D. It violates the rule against unnecessary handling of sacred objects
Correct: B — Ulla explains that blessing over an open scroll risks misleading bystanders into thinking the blessing is part of the Torah's written text — the same concern that applies to reading the Aramaic Targum.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When rolling a Torah scroll to a new section, why should one tighten from the inside (near roller) rather than the outside (far roller)?
A. The near roller is stronger and won't damage the parchment
B. Halacha requires always using the dominant hand which faces inward
C. To avoid extending one's arm past the Torah scroll out of respect
D. The outside roller is reserved for the gabbai to control
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan taught to tighten from the inside so that one does not reach one's arm past the Torah scroll. Extending the arm beyond the scroll is considered disrespectful to the sacred text.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Abaye objected to Rabbi Yoḥanan's teaching about reading Torah without melody. What was the basis of his objection?
A. Rabbi Yoḥanan misquoted the verse from Ezekiel
B. It is unjust to apply such a harsh verse to someone who simply cannot carry a tune
C. Melody is only required for Torah, not for Mishna
D. The verse from Ezekiel refers to idol worshippers, not Torah scholars
Correct: B — Abaye challenges: just because a person lacks musical ability you apply the verse 'statutes not good'? Rav Mesharshiya therefore reinterprets the verse to mean one who studies without explaining the underlying reasons — an intellectual failure, not a vocal one.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What happens, according to Rabbi Yoḥanan (as explained by Abaye), to one who holds an uncovered Torah scroll?
A. He is obligated to fast as atonement
B. The reading is invalidated and must be repeated
C. He will be buried literally without a burial shroud
D. He will be buried without the Torah advocating on his behalf
Correct: D — Abaye explains that 'buried uncovered' means the Torah will not speak in his defense before the Heavenly Court — he disrespected it, so it cannot advocate for him. The Gemara rejects the literal reading (buried without a shroud) as inconceivable.
