Megillah 15 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Ulla, what does it signify when both a prophet's name AND his father's name are mentioned in a prophecy?
A. The prophet was of priestly descent
B. Both the prophet and his father were prophets
C. The prophecy was of special national importance
D. The prophet belonged to the tribe of Judah
Correct: B — Ulla teaches: when a prophet's name and father's name are both recorded, it indicates the father was also a prophet — both names are therefore worth preserving.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the Gemara say the word vatitḥalḥal ('Esther was exceedingly distressed,' Esther 4:4) means according to Rav?
A. She trembled with righteous anger
B. She fainted and fell to the ground
C. She began to menstruate out of fear
D. She tore her garments in mourning
Correct: C — Rav interprets vatitḥalḥal as the ḥalalim (cavities) of her body opening — she began to menstruate from extreme fear, rendering her impure at a critical moment.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Who does the Gemara identify as 'Hathach,' the chamberlain appointed to attend Esther?
A. Mordecai operating in disguise
B. The prophet Elijah sent to help her
C. The prophet Daniel
D. Ezra the Scribe
Correct: C — Rav teaches that Hathach was the prophet Daniel, who had been 'cut down' (ḥatakh) from his greatness and demoted from his high position by enemies of the Jews.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rabbi Elazar citing Rabbi Ḥanina says: 'Whoever reports a saying in the name of he who said it...' — what is the result?
A. He fulfills the mitzvah of Torah study
B. He brings redemption to the world
C. He merits a portion in the World to Come
D. He gains the blessing of long life
Correct: B — The proof-text is Esther reporting Mordecai's discovery of the assassination plot in Mordecai's name — this act of attribution ultimately led to Ahasuerus's life being saved and the chain of events culminating in Israel's redemption.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabba, what was Esther's strategic reason for inviting Haman to her banquet?
A. To honor him openly and lower Ahasuerus's suspicions
B. To fulfill 'Pride goes before destruction' — making Haman arrogant before his downfall
C. To give herself time to compose her petition to the king
D. To prevent Haman from leaving Shushan before she could act
Correct: B — Rabba derives Esther's strategy from Proverbs 16:18: 'Pride goes before destruction.' By elevating Haman and inflating his pride, Esther set up the inevitable mechanism of his destruction.
