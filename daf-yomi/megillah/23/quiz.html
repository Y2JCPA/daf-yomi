QUESTION 1 OF 5
How many people read from the Torah on Yom Kippur, and to what does this correspond according to Rav Yehuda?
A. Five, corresponding to the five officers who saw the king's face
B. Six, corresponding to those beside Ezra's right and left — with Zechariah and Meshullam being the same person
C. Seven, corresponding to the seven officers of Ahasuerus
D. Ten, corresponding to the full minyan for kedushah
Correct: B — Rav Yehuda taught that six readers on Yom Kippur correspond to the six at Ezra's right and six at his left (Neh. 8:4). The 'seventh' on the left, Zechariah, is identified as the same person as Meshullam — one man, two names — keeping the count at six.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did the Sages rule that a woman should not read the Torah in public, even though she technically counts toward the quorum of seven?
A. Because women are exempt from all time-bound commandments
B. Because the Torah itself forbids women from reading publicly
C. Out of respect for the congregation (kavod ha-tzibbur)
D. Because a woman's reading does not constitute a valid Torah reading
Correct: C — The Tosefta explicitly states women are eligible to count toward the seven readers, but the Sages instituted that women should not read out of kavod ha-tzibbur. This is a rabbinic enactment, not a Biblical prohibition.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Ulla, why must the maftir read from the Torah before reading the haftara from the Prophets?
A. To fulfill the minimum of three verses required of every Torah reader
B. To ensure he counts as one of the seven required Torah readers
C. Out of respect for the Torah, so it does not appear that the Prophets are more important
D. Because one may not read from the Prophets without first reading from the Torah by rabbinic decree
Correct: C — Ulla says the maftir reads Torah first solely for kavod ha-Torah — so that people do not think the Prophets supersede the Torah in importance. This is why, according to one opinion, the maftir's Torah reading does not count toward the seven.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What minimum number of haftara verses must the maftir read, and what is the reasoning behind this number?
A. Seven verses, one corresponding to each Torah reader
B. Ten verses, corresponding to the required minyan
C. Twenty-one verses, corresponding to seven readers times three verses each
D. Three verses, the same minimum as any individual Torah reader
Correct: C — The baraita states the haftara must contain at least 21 verses because each of the seven Torah readers reads a minimum of three verses. The haftara should correspond proportionally to the full Torah reading. An exception applies when the topic concludes before 21 verses.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara derive from Scripture that ten men are required for acts of kedushah?
A. From the ten times the word 'priest' appears in Leviticus 27, the chapter on Temple assessments
B. From the ten men who escorted Ezra when he first read the Torah publicly to the community
C. By gezera shava: 'among' in Lev. 22:32 → 'congregation' in Korah (Num. 16) → ten spies called 'congregation' in Num. 14:27
D. From the ten plagues of Egypt, each representing one man required for communal sanctity
Correct: C — Rabbi Ḥiyya bar Abba teaches a chain of gezera shava: 'among' in the sanctification verse (Lev. 22:32) is linked by verbal analogy to 'congregation' in the Korah narrative (Num. 16:21), which in turn links to 'congregation' applied to the ten faithless spies (Num. 14:27) — establishing ten as the threshold for kedushah.
