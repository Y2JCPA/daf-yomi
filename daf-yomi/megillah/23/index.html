🤔 Who Authored the Mishna? The Tanna Dispute

Gemara
Who is the tanna of the mishna that permits adding extra Torah readers?
Objection A
If it is Rabbi Yishmael — problem! His baraita says one may not add readers beyond the set number.
Objection B
If it is Rabbi Akiva — that is also difficult for its own reason.
Rava
It is a tanna of the school of Rabbi Yishmael — not Rabbi Yishmael himself.
Resolution
Two of Rabbi Yishmael's students disagreed about his view — hence the apparent contradiction between mishna and baraita.
Key Principle When a master's students disagree about his ruling, two seemingly contradictory sources may both authentically reflect his school — just different disciples' transmission.

🔢 Biblical Parallels for Each Reader Count

3 Readers — Weekday

  • 3 guards of the door (II Kings 25:18)
  • Minimum for public Torah reading
  • Foundation of the entire institution

5 Readers — Festival

  • 5 officers who saw the king's face (II Kings 25:19)
  • Taught by Rav Yosef from a baraita
  • Festival joy merits more readers

6 Readers — Yom Kippur

  • 6 at Ezra's right + 6 at his left (Neh. 8:4)
  • Zechariah = Meshullam — one man, two names
  • Taught by Rav Yehuda to Ya'akov of Mina

7 Readers — Shabbat

  • 7 officers who saw the king's face (Esther 1:14)
  • Shabbat's supreme holiness demands most readers
  • Taught by Rabbi Yitzḥak bar Naḥmani

👩 Women, Minors, and the Torah Reading

כָּל מַעֲלִין לַתּוֹרָה, אֲפִילוּ קָטָן וַאֲפִילוּ אִשָּׁה. אֶלָּא שֶׁאָמְרוּ חֲכָמִים אִשָּׁה לֹא תִקְרָא בַּתּוֹרָה מִפְּנֵי כְּבוֹד הַצִּבּוּר
Tosefta Megilla 3:11 All people count toward the quorum of seven Torah readers — even a minor and even a woman.

Nevertheless, the Sages ruled: a woman should not read the Torah in public, out of kavod ha-tzibbur — respect for the congregation.

This is a rabbinic enactment, not a Torah-level prohibition. She is technically eligible; the restriction protects communal dignity.

📖 Does the Maftir Count Toward the Seven?

Question
The maftir reads from the Torah and then the haftara from the Prophets. Does he count as one of the seven required Torah readers?
Rav Huna
He does count — he reads from the Torah and fulfills a complete aliyah before adding the haftara.
R. Yirmeya bar Abba
He does not count — his Torah reading is incidental; the real purpose is the haftara from Prophets.
Ulla
The maftir reads Torah first only for kavod ha-Torah — so it does not appear that the Prophets are greater than the Torah.
Baraita
The haftara must be at least 21 verses, corresponding to 7 readers × 3 verses each. Exception: where the topic ends sooner.
Exception Noted If the subject matter concludes before 21 verses — e.g., the haftara beginning 'Add your burnt offerings' (Jer. 7:21) — we may read fewer than 21 verses.
