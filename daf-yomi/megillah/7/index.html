📜 Was the Book of Esther Divinely Inspired?

Rabbi Eliezer
'Haman thought in his heart' — only divine inspiration could reveal a person's private thoughts
Rabbi Akiva
'Esther found favor in the eyes of all who saw her' — such universal favor is miraculous
Rabbi Meir
'The thing became known to Mordecai' — how else would he have learned of the conspiracy?
Shmuel
'They confirmed above what they took upon themselves below' — heaven ratified Israel's earthly acceptance of Purim
Rava
Every proof can be refuted by natural explanation — except Shmuel's. That one stands uncontested.
Bottom Line The Book of Esther was stated with Divine Spirit (רוּחַ הַקֹּדֶשׁ). The irrefutable proof: heaven confirmed below what Israel enacted on earth (Esther 9:27)

👸 Esther's Request to the Sages

Esther's appeal: 'Establish me for future generations' — she requested that Purim be permanently recorded in Scripture
↓
The Sages hesitated: they feared permanently recording Israel's salvation would arouse jealousy among the nations
↓
Scriptural anchor sought: 'Write this for a memorial in the book' (Exodus 17:14) — the Sages debated whether this verse points to the Megilla
↓
A baraita debate: 'Write this' = book of Exodus; 'a memorial' = Deuteronomy; 'in the book' = the Book of Esther itself
↓
Sages agreed: once Scriptural support was found, they established Purim for all generations. Esther's request was fulfilled.

📖 Proofs for Divine Inspiration — and Rava's Refutations

Rabbi Eliezer
'Haman thought in his heart' (6:6) — inner thoughts known only through divine inspiration
Rava refutes
Haman may have spoken aloud and it was simply overheard
Rabbi Akiva
'Esther found favor in the eyes of all' — miraculous universal appeal
Rava refutes
Per R. Elazar: she appeared to each nation as one of their own people — no miracle needed
Rabbi Meir
'The thing became known to Mordecai' (2:22) — he knew of the conspiracy supernaturally
Rava refutes
Per R. Ḥiyya bar Abba: Mordecai sat at the king's gate and simply heard the conspirators speaking
Shmuel
'They confirmed above what they took upon themselves below' (9:27) — celestial ratification
Rava concedes
✓ No refutation is possible. This proof is superior to all others.
Rav Yosef adds 'These days of Purim shall not cease from among the Jews' (9:28) — only divine foresight could guarantee eternal observance

🍷 Purim Feast, Intoxication, and the Rabba–Rabbi Zeira Incident

Rava rules: A person is obligated to become intoxicated on Purim until unable to distinguish 'cursed is Haman' from 'blessed is Mordecai'
↓
Followed literally
Rabba and Rabbi Zeira prepared a Purim feast together and became deeply intoxicated
Tragic consequence
Rabba arose and slaughtered Rabbi Zeira. Next day he prayed and revived him.
↓
The following year, Rabba invited Rabbi Zeira to feast again
↓
Rabbi Zeira declined: 'Not every hour is a miracle' — one cannot rely on supernatural rescue a second time
↓
Additional ruling (Rava): A Purim feast eaten at night does not fulfill the obligation — the verse says 'days of feasting,' not nights

⚖️ Shabbat, Yom Tov, and Yom Kippur — Key Distinctions

Yom Tov יוֹם טוֹב

  • Food preparation: permitted — 'only that which every person must eat' (Ex. 12:16)
  • Preparation aids (e.g., sharpening knife): debated — Sages prohibit; Rabbi Yehuda permits
  • For you: not for gentiles, not for dogs
  • Violation penalty: not specified as death or karet

Shabbat שַׁבָּת

  • Food preparation: prohibited
  • Intentional violation: death penalty by human court (stoning)
  • Unintentional violation: bring a sin-offering
  • Damages: one who causes harm on Shabbat is exempt from payment

Yom Kippur יוֹם כִּפּוּר

  • Food preparation: prohibited
  • Intentional violation: karet (divine excision) — no human court penalty
  • If one was flogged by court: exempt from karet (R. Ḥananya ben Gamliel)
  • Damages: also exempt — equated with Shabbat on this point
