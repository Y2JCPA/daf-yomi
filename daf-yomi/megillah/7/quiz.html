Megillah 7 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, which proof for the divine inspiration of the Book of Esther cannot be refuted?
A. Rabbi Eliezer's — 'Haman thought in his heart'
B. Rabbi Akiva's — 'Esther found favor in the eyes of all'
C. Shmuel's — 'They confirmed above what they took upon themselves below'
D. Rav Yosef's — 'These days shall not cease from among the Jews'
Correct: C — Rava states that every other proof has a natural explanation, but Shmuel's — that heaven ratified what Israel enacted below — is irrefutable and stands as the strongest proof.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What are the minimum quantities required for fulfilling the Purim mitzvot of mishloach manot and matanot la'evyonim?
A. One portion to one person; one gift to one poor person
B. Two portions to two people; two gifts to two poor people
C. Two portions to one person; two gifts to two poor people
D. One portion to two people; one gift to each poor person
Correct: C — The verse implies sending two food portions to a single individual (mishloach manot), and distributing two separate gifts to two different poor people (matanot la'evyonim).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What lesson does Abaye draw from eating 60 dishes at Marei bar Mar's home after leaving Rabba's house satiated?
A. One should always eat before attending a Purim feast
B. The poor man is hungry and does not know it — he was hungrier than he realized
C. Purim portions should consist of elaborate cooked dishes
D. It is forbidden to be satiated before fulfilling mishloach manot
Correct: B — Abaye uses this as an illustration of the folk saying: 'The poor man is hungry and does not know it' — he had been so underfed at Rabba's house that he ate 60 courses without realizing his hunger.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the key halakhic difference between Shabbat and Yom Kippur regarding violation?
A. Yom Tov violation incurs karet; Shabbat incurs death by court
B. Shabbat violation incurs death by human court; Yom Kippur violation incurs karet
C. Both incur death by human court equally
D. Both incur only karet; no human court is involved
Correct: B — The mishna states that intentional Shabbat desecration is punishable by a human court (stoning), while intentional Yom Kippur violation results in karet — divine punishment — with no human court penalty.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Zeira refuse to feast with Rabba on Purim the following year?
A. He had taken a vow to fast on Purim
B. He was still angry about the slaughtering incident
C. He could not rely on a miracle happening a second time
D. Rabba had failed to send him proper mishloach manot
Correct: C — Though Rabba revived Rabbi Zeira after slaughtering him while intoxicated, Rabbi Zeira declined the next year's invitation because 'not every hour is a miracle' — one cannot depend on supernatural rescue repeating itself.
