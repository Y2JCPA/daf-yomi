🌍 Three Kings Who Ruled the Entire World

Baraita
Three men ruled over the entire world: Ahab, Ahasuerus, and Nebuchadnezzar.
Re: Ahab
Kings traveled to Ahab from afar (I Kings 20:31) — a sign of universal dominion over all nations.
Re: Ahasuerus
He reigned "from Hodu to Cush" — either opposite ends of the world, or adjacent lands representing total global control.
Gemara Q
But what of Solomon, Sennacherib, Darius, and Cyrus — did they not also claim to rule the entire world?
Resolution
Each is excluded: Solomon later became a commoner; Sennacherib lacked complete conquest; Darius lacked seven provinces; Cyrus only claimed God gave him all kingdoms — not proof of actual dominion.
Mnemonic שׁ-ס-ד-כ: Shlomo, Sennacherib (Sancheriv), Darius, Koresh (Cyrus)

🔢 The Fatal 70-Year Miscalculation

Jeremiah's Prophecy (29:10): After 70 years for Babylonia, I will return you to Eretz Yisrael
↓
Belshazzar's Error
Counted 70 years from Nebuchadnezzar's first conquest of Jehoiakim. Celebrated prematurely using Temple vessels. Killed that very night.
Ahasuerus's Error
Thought he learned from Belshazzar, but also counted from wrong starting point — the first exile, not Zedekiah's exile. Celebrated and gave Haman power.
↓
Correct count: Should start from destruction under Zedekiah — 11 years after Jehoiachin. True 70 years ends in Ahasuerus's 14th year — the very year of Purim

⚠️ וַיְהִי — The Word That Signals Woe

וַיְהִי בִּימֵי אֲחַשְׁוֵרוֹשׁ (אֶסְתֵּר א:א)
Rav's Teaching The word וַיְהִי (vayhi, 'and it came to pass') should be read as two words: וַי (vai = woe) and הִי (hi = mourning/lamentation). Wherever vayhi appears in Scripture, it foreshadows an impending calamity. Similarly, the word הוּא (hu, 'this is') signals that the person remained unchanged — Ahasuerus stayed wicked from beginning to end, just as Abraham stayed righteous.
