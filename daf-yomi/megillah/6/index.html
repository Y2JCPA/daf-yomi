📅 First Adar or Second Adar for Megillah?

First Tanna
Re-read the Megillah in Second Adar. The four special portions (Shekalim, Zakhor, Para, HaḤodesh) belong to First Adar.
R. Eliezer b. R. Yosei
Do not re-read in Second Adar. All Second Adar mitzvot can be fulfilled in First Adar — we don't pass up a mitzva opportunity (ein ma'avirin al hamitzvot).
Rabban Shimon b. Gamliel
Re-read in Second Adar. Purim must fall in the Adar adjacent to Nisan — to juxtapose the redemption of Purim with the redemption of Passover.
Halakha (Rabbi Yoḥanan) The law follows Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel, who said it in the name of Rabbi Yosei: read the Megillah in the Second Adar.

🐚 Zebulun's Complaint and Hidden Treasures (Deut. 33:19)

Zebulun's Complaint: "Master of the Universe! To my brothers You gave fields and vineyards — to me You gave mountains and hills!"
↓
God's reply: The sea and its hidden riches are yours — treasures no ordinary land can offer.
↓
"Treasures" = the ḥilazon — the creature from whose blood the blue tekhelet dye for tzitzit is produced
↓
"Hidden" = the tarit (sardine) — abundant in Zebulun's coastal waters
↓
"Sand" = white glass — the sand of Zebulun's shore is uniquely suited for producing fine white glass, a precious commodity
↓
Counterpoint resolved: Rabbi Yoḥanan himself saw Tzippori flowing with milk and honey — yet Zebulun still preferred cultivable plains to mountains. Even so, fields were preferable.

🏛️ Caesarea and Jerusalem — Diametrically Opposed Fates

🏛️ Caesarea (Edom / Rome)

  • Represents the empire of Rome/Edom
  • "Ekron shall be uprooted" (Zephaniah 2:4)
  • Home of theaters and circuses
  • When Caesarea rises → Jerusalem falls
  • "Both settled" or "both destroyed" — do not believe it

✡️ Jerusalem (Israel)

  • Represents the Jewish people
  • Synagogues and houses of study
  • "He shall be a remnant for our God" (Zech. 9:7)
  • When Jerusalem rises → Caesarea falls
  • Basis: "One people shall be stronger than the other" (Gen. 25:23)

📖 Torah Labor and Provoking the Wicked

Rabbi Yitzḥak
"I labored and found" — believe him.
"I labored and did not find" — do not believe.
"I did not labor but I found" — do not believe.
This applies to Torah; business success depends on Heavenly assistance.
Rabbi Yitzḥak
If a wicked man's hour is smiling upon him, do not provoke him. "Contend not with evildoers" (Ps. 37:1).
Rabbi Yoḥanan (b. R. Shimon b. Yoḥai)
It is permitted to provoke the wicked in this world! "They who keep the Torah contend with them" (Prov. 28:4).
Resolution
No contradiction: Don't provoke = in his personal affairs. Permitted = in matters of Heaven. Also: a completely righteous person may provoke; an intermediate person may not — and when the wicked man's hour smiles upon him, even the righteous should refrain.
Principle Torah success is earned through toil alone. When the wicked man enjoys divine assistance, restraint is required of all.

📅 Megillah Reading in an Intercalated Year

Court intercalates the year — a Second Adar is added. The Megillah was already read in First Adar.
↓
R. Eliezer b. R. Yosei
No re-reading. First Adar suffices — all Second Adar mitzvot can be performed then. We do not pass up a mitzva when it arises.
Rabban Shimon b. Gamliel
Must re-read in Second Adar. Purim belongs in the Adar adjacent to Nisan — juxtaposing the redemption of Purim with the redemption of Passover.
↓
Halakha: Re-read in Second Adar. Textual support: "this second letter of Purim" (Esther 9:29) — implying a second reading exists.

📋 Megillah 6 — Summary Map

6a — City Identifications: Hammath = hot springs of Tiberias; Rakkath = Tiberias (full of mitzvot); Kitron = Tzippori (like a bird on a mountain); Ekron = Caesarea
↓
Zebulun's Hidden Wealth: No fields, but given ḥilazon (tekhelet), tarit (sardine), and white glass from sand — the sea's treasures
↓
Caesarea ↔ Jerusalem: When one rises the other falls (Gen. 25:23 — "one people stronger than the other")
↓
Torah Labor (6b): Only "I labored and found" is credible. Business depends on Heaven; Torah depends on toil.
Provoking the Wicked: Permitted in matters of Heaven (R. Yoḥanan); forbidden in personal matters (R. Yitzḥak); never when his hour smiles.
↓
Mishna + Dispute: R. Eliezer b. R. Yosei — First Adar suffices. Rabban Shimon b. Gamliel — must be Second Adar (adjacent to Nisan). Halakha: Second Adar.
