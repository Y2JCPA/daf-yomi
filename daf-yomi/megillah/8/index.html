📋 Four 'What Is the Difference Between' Mishnayot

Vow vs. Gift Offering

  • Vow (נדר): obligated to replace if lost or died
  • Gift (נדבה): no obligation to replace
  • Both: liable for delaying pledge
  • Both: equal sanctity while the animal lives

Zav: Two vs. Three Emissions

  • Two emissions: impurity + 7 clean days
  • Three emissions: also obligated in korban
  • Both: defile lying and sitting surfaces
  • Derived from double mention in Leviticus 15

Quarantined vs. Confirmed Leper

  • Confirmed: lets hair grow, rends garments
  • Quarantined: exempt from both obligations
  • Both: expelled from all three camps
  • Confirmed: shaves all hair + brings birds

Torah Scroll vs. Tefillin/Mezuzot

  • Torah scrolls: may be written in any language
  • Tefillin & mezuzot: only in Ashurit script
  • Rabban Shimon: even scrolls only in Greek
  • All three: stitched with sinews; render hands impure

⚖️ Vow Offering (נֶדֶר) vs. Gift Offering (נְדָבָה)

Vow Offering — נֶדֶר

  • Formula: "It is incumbent upon me to bring a burnt-offering"
  • If animal dies or is lost: owner is obligated to bring a replacement
  • The obligation rests on the person — he bears it on his shoulders
  • Verse: "upon him to make atonement" — liability follows the person, not the animal
  • Delays in bringing either offering: violates bal te'acher

Gift Offering — נְדָבָה

  • Formula: "This animal is a burnt-offering"
  • If animal dies or is lost: owner is not obligated to replace it
  • The sanctity rests on the specific animal, not the person
  • Once the animal is gone, the owner's obligation is discharged
  • Delays in bringing either offering: violates bal te'acher

🔬 Rabbi Simai's Proof: Two vs. Three Emissions (Leviticus 15)

Leviticus 15:2–3
"due to his emission… when his flesh runs with his emission"
Two mentions of the word emission (זוֹב) in these verses
↓
Each mention corresponds to one emission — so the Torah designates two emissions as the threshold for establishing zav status and impurity
↓
Two Emissions
Renders lying/sitting surfaces impure
Must count 7 clean days
No korban obligation
Three Emissions
All the above, plus:
Obligated to bring a korban (birds + grain offering) after purification
↓
"From his emission" (מִזּוֹבוֹ) in Leviticus 15:13 — the extra prefix teaches that even the two-emission zav must count 7 clean days before immersing

💬 Rav Pappa vs. Abaye: The Phrase "From His Emission"

Rav Pappa
One verse uses 'from his emission' (מִזּוֹבוֹ) to include the two-emission zav in counting 7 clean days — yet another verse uses the same phrase and you say it excludes him? How can the identical expression both include and exclude?
Abaye
If the Torah intended to exclude the two-emission zav from counting, the verse would simply remain silent! The very fact that it says 'from his emission' teaches inclusion — he counts when clean from his emission, not from any concurrent leprosy.
Rav Pappa
But perhaps 'from his emission' is needed for a different inclusion — that he counts from emission-cleanness, not from leprosy-cleanness. If so, the phrase is already occupied and cannot also teach the two-emission inclusion!
Abaye
That derivation is unnecessary — it is already logical! Since the entire passage addresses a zav, it obviously speaks of emission-based counting. The phrase must therefore remain free to include the two-emission zav in the 7-day count.
Ruling "From his emission" in Leviticus 15:13 includes the two-emission zav in the obligation to count seven clean days — a requirement derived from the superfluous phrase, not from a gezeira shava.

✍️ Writing Sacred Texts: Language, Script, and Validity

Torah Scrolls (סִפְרֵי תּוֹרָה) — Tanna Kamma: May be written in any language. The sanctity of the text is not language-dependent.
↓
Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel disagrees: In practice, the Sages only permitted Torah scrolls to be written in Greek — due to its precision — besides the original Hebrew (Ashurit).
↓
Tefillin (תְּפִלִּין) and Mezuzot (מְזוּזוֹת): Must be written only in Ashurit (square Hebrew script). Any other script renders them pasul — invalid.
↓
What is equal for all three: They must be stitched with sinews (גִּידִין); they all render the hands of anyone who touches them ritually impure (טְמֵאוֹת יָדַיִם).
↓
Rava resolves a contradiction: A Hebrew verse written in Aramaic translation within a Torah scroll is invalid in either language — it must be in proper Hebrew script to count toward the scroll's validity.
