QUESTION 1 OF 5
How did Rav resolve the contradiction between Onkelos composing the Targum and Ezra's assembly already establishing it?
A. Onkelos composed an entirely new translation that replaced Ezra's version
B. Ezra composed the Torah Targum; Onkelos composed only the Targum to the Prophets
C. Onkelos had forgotten the tradition from Ezra's time and re-established it by learning from Rabbi Eliezer and Rabbi Yehoshua
D. Both composed independent translations and the Rabbis chose Onkelos's as more accurate
Correct: C — Rav explains the Targum originated at Ezra's time. Onkelos, however, had forgotten it and re-established it through his teachers Rabbi Eliezer and Rabbi Yehoshua — so both statements are true.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why was Yonatan ben Uzziel forbidden by a Divine Voice to translate the Writings (Ketuvim)?
A. His translation of the Prophets contained too many errors
B. The Writings contain End of Days secrets that should not be publicized to all
C. Only Onkelos had divine authorization to compose Aramaic translations
D. He had already revealed too much and needed to study more first
Correct: B — The Writings contain hidden End of Days secrets — e.g., Zechariah 12:11 about the mourning for Ahab and Josiah in the valley of Megiddo. Revealing these would be harmful, hence the Heavenly Voice intervened.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
The angel rebuked Joshua at Jericho for two specific failures. What were they?
A. Neglecting the morning tamid and failing to pray Maariv
B. Failing to circumcise the Israelites and neglecting Shabbat
C. Neglecting the afternoon daily offering and neglecting Torah study at night
D. Failing to build an altar and not reading the Torah to the people
Correct: C — The angel said: 'Yesterday afternoon you neglected the tamid offering, and now tonight you neglect Torah study.' Joshua then spent the night studying Torah with the nation in the depths of halacha.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why were Daniel's companions frightened even though they did not see the vision?
A. They heard a loud noise from the vision that only Daniel could interpret
B. Daniel's face shone with divine light, terrifying them
C. Their guardian angels saw the vision even though they themselves could not, causing them to sense danger
D. They were sinful and therefore unworthy to see the vision, which frightened them
Correct: C — Although the men themselves did not see the vision, their guardian angels (מַזָּלָם) did — and this perception transferred a sense of dread to them, causing them to flee. Ravina derives from this that unexplained fear is caused by one's angel seeing unseen danger.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi, which condition is required for a city to read Megilla on the 15th of Adar as a 'walled city'?
A. The city must currently have intact walls surrounding it
B. The city must have been walled before it was settled, from the days of Joshua bin Nun
C. The city must have a population of at least ten thousand
D. The city's walls must have been built during the Second Temple period
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi rules that a city walled after settlement reads on the 14th like a village. Only a city walled before settlement — 'from the days of Joshua bin Nun' — reads on the 15th. This is derived from Leviticus 25:29: 'a residential house in a walled city.'
