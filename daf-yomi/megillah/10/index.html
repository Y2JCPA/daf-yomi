🏛️ Rabbi Yitzchak and the Temple of Onias

Rabbi Yitzchak
I heard that one may sacrifice offerings in the Temple of Onias in Egypt at the present time — it is not a house of idol worship but a legitimate sanctuary.
Other Sages
Did you actually say this halakha regarding the Temple of Onias?
Rabbi Yitzchak
No, I did not say that. (retracts his ruling)
Rava
By God! He did in fact say this — but he retracted it due to the difficulty raised by Rav Mari from our Mishna.
Rav Mari (objection)
The Mishna teaches: after the Temple was destroyed, improvised altars remain forbidden in Jerusalem. Its sanctity is permanent — so how could Onias be permitted?
Core Issue Is the Temple of Onias a valid place of worship, or is offering there a violation of the prohibition of bamot (improvised altars)?

🌆 Shiloh vs. Jerusalem — Two Tiers of Sacred Sanctity

Shiloh — The Tabernacle

  • Sanctity was temporary
  • After destruction: improvised altars permitted
  • "Rest" — Deuteronomy 12:9
  • Initial consecration did not sanctify forever
  • Tanna'im dispute: Rabbi Eliezer vs. Rabbi Yehoshua on details

Jerusalem — The Temple

  • Sanctity is permanent and eternal
  • After destruction: improvised altars forbidden
  • "Inheritance" — Deuteronomy 12:9
  • Initial consecration sanctified Jerusalem forever
  • This permanence is what undermines the Temple of Onias ruling

🌿 Isaiah 55:13 — Purim's Cast Prefigured in Prophecy

"Instead of the thorn [na'atzutz]" → Haman the Wicked
He turned himself into an object of idolatry — decreeing all must prostrate before him. 'Thorn' elsewhere refers to idols.
↓
"Shall the cypress [berosh] come up" → Mordecai
Berosh = rosh (chief). He was the chief of the spices — "pure myrrh [mor deror]" is rendered mira dachya in Aramaic, echoing Mordecai.
↓
"Instead of the nettle [sirpad]" → Vashti the Wicked
She was the granddaughter of Nebuchadnezzar, who burned [saraf] the ceiling [refidat] of the Temple — hence sirpad.
↓
"Shall the myrtle [hadas] come up" → Esther the Righteous
She was named Hadassah (myrtle): "He raised up Hadassah, that is Esther" (Esther 2:7).
↓
"An everlasting sign that shall not be cut off" → The miracle of Purim itself
The celebration of Purim endures forever — an eternal memorial to the salvation.

📖 Five Sages — Five Prologues to the Megilla

וְהָיָה לַה' לְשֵׁם לְאוֹת עוֹלָם לֹא יִכָּרֵת — ישעיהו נה:יג
Aggadic Introductions Rabbi Yonatan: Isaiah 14:22 — "I will cut off from Babylon name and remnant" = Vashti cut off; her descendants = Haman's sons.

Rabbi Shmuel bar Naḥmani: Isaiah 55:13 — thorn/cypress/nettle/myrtle = Haman/Mordecai/Vashti/Esther.

Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi: Deuteronomy 28:63 — God will rejoice to cause you to perish? But God does not rejoice in the wicked's downfall — He causes others to rejoice. The angels could not sing as Egypt drowned: "My creations are drowning and you wish to sing?"

Rabbi Abba bar Kahana: Ecclesiastes 2:26 — wisdom/knowledge/joy given to the good = Mordecai/Esther; the sinner gathers and gives to the righteous = Haman.

Rabba bar Oferan: Jeremiah 49:38 — "I will set My throne in Elam" = Ahasuerus; "destroy the king" = Vashti; "the princes" = Haman and his sons.
