QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye, when the weekly Torah portion is adjacent to Shekalim, how should the Shabbat reading be arranged to prevent confusion?
A. Seven people read the regular portion, with the seventh doubling as maftir for Shekalim
B. Six people read up to (but not including) Shekalim, then one person repeats and reads Shekalim
C. The regular portion is skipped entirely and only Shekalim is read that week
D. Both portions are combined and read consecutively without any repetition
Correct: B — Abaye requires that six readers cover the portion up to — but not including — the Shekalim section, then one reader 'repeats' by reading Shekalim from 'When you take the count.' This makes clear that Shekalim is a distinct, special reading and not a mere extension of the regular portion.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the core reason Rav gives for advancing the Shekalim reading to the previous Shabbat when the New Moon of Adar falls mid-week?
A. So that the congregation has time to physically travel to Jerusalem to donate their shekalim
B. Because the New Moon offering must be brought before the Shekalim announcement
C. So that there are two full weeks for the announcement before shekalim collection begins
D. Because Shmuel's position contradicts an explicit ruling of Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
Correct: C — Rav holds that reading Shekalim on the following Shabbat would leave fewer than two weeks before the New Moon of Nisan — insufficient time for the public announcement needed before collecting shekalim donations. Two full weeks of announcement are required.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the third of the four special Shabbatot, and what is its haftara?
A. Zakhor — haftara: 'I will remember My covenant' (Leviticus 26)
B. Para — haftara: 'Then will I sprinkle clean water upon you' (Ezekiel 36:25–38)
C. HaChodesh — haftara: 'Thus says the Lord God' (Ezekiel 45)
D. Shekalim — haftara: 'And Jehoash said to the priests' (II Kings 12)
Correct: B — The third Shabbat is Parashat Para (Red Heifer, Numbers 19), read on the Shabbat immediately after Purim. Its haftara is Ezekiel 36:25–38: 'Then will I sprinkle clean water upon you' — thematically connecting ritual purification to preparation for Pesach.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Mishna at the end of Daf 30b, which Torah portion is read on Rosh HaShana?
A. After the death of Aaron's sons (Leviticus 16)
B. The Festivals of Leviticus (Leviticus 22:26–23:44)
C. The seventh month, on the first of the month (Leviticus 23:23–25)
D. And in the beginnings of your months (Numbers 28:11–15)
Correct: C — On Rosh HaShana the congregation reads the passage beginning 'In the seventh month, on the first of the month' (Leviticus 23:23–25), which describes the special character of the first of Tishrei as a day of complete rest and sacred assembly.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the dispute between Rabbi Ami and Rabbi Yirmeya about 'resuming regular order' on the fifth Shabbat, whose opinion does Abaye support and why?
A. Rabbi Yirmeya — resuming haftarot makes more sense since the Torah portions were never actually skipped
B. Rabbi Ami — resuming Torah portions, proved by the Mishna's reference to interrupting 'the matter of the day' on special days
C. Neither — Abaye holds one resumes both Torah portions and haftarot simultaneously
D. Rabbi Ami — because the Babylonian academies always prioritized Torah portion order over haftara order
Correct: B — Abaye supports Rabbi Ami (resume Torah portions). His proof: the Mishna states that on special days we interrupt the regular reading for 'the matter of the day.' This only makes sense if there is a fixed weekly Torah portion order worth interrupting — if the reference were to haftarot, there would be nothing to interrupt on weekdays when there is no haftara.
