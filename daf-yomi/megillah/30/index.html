📖 How to Read Shekalim When Adjacent to the Weekly Portion

Rabbi Yitzchak Nappacha
Six people read the regular weekly portion through the section adjacent to Shekalim, and a seventh reads Shekalim as a separate maftir reading.
Abaye
No! People will think they merely extended the regular reading. Six should read up to — but NOT including — the Shekalim section, then one person repeats and reads Shekalim from 'When you take the count.'
Gemara
A baraita supports Abaye: when the regular weekly portion IS 'When you take the count,' its first part is read as the regular portion and the last section is repeated for Shekalim.
Abaye
Even Rabbi Yitzchak Nappacha must concede the baraita's logic — 'repeating' Shekalim means reading it on a subsequent Shabbat, not doubling within the same service.
Core Concern How do we ensure the congregation recognizes Shekalim as a distinct special reading and not merely an extension of the regular weekly portion?

🌙 Rav vs. Shmuel: New Moon of Adar Falls Mid-Week

Rav — Advance to Previous Shabbat

  • Read Shekalim on the Shabbat BEFORE the New Moon
  • Reason: Need two full weeks for announcement before collecting shekalim donations
  • Reading after the New Moon leaves insufficient time
  • Proof: Mishna says 'advance to previous Shabbat' when mid-week
  • Applies even if New Moon falls on Friday
  • Tannaitic parallel: 'during which the New Moon occurs'

Shmuel — Delay to Following Shabbat

  • Read Shekalim on the Shabbat AFTER the New Moon
  • Reason: Only one Shabbat of announcement before New Moon of Nisan is needed
  • The following Shabbat provides sufficient preparation time
  • Emends baraita: 'on which' (not 'during which') New Moon occurs
  • School of Shmuel teaches accordingly
  • Tannaitic parallel: 'on which the New Moon occurs' (i.e., Shabbat itself)

⚔️ Zakhor Reading and the Purim Timing Problem

Question: When must פרשת זכור (Zakhor, Deut. 25:17–19) be read relative to Purim?
↓
Rav
Zakhor must PRECEDE Purim — advance to previous Shabbat if Purim would otherwise come first. Reason: must remember Amalek's destruction before celebrating Haman's downfall.
Shmuel
Zakhor may be read AFTER Purim — no strict requirement that it precede the festival. Read on following Shabbat when New Moon of Adar falls mid-week.
↓
Special case — Purim falls on Shabbat:
Rav Huna: Both Rav and Shmuel agree — read Zakhor on THAT Shabbat, do not advance
Rav Nachman: Disagrees — still advance to previous Shabbat

🏛️ Public Fast Day Schedule and the Order of Readings

Morning until midday: Community gathers in synagogue — town leaders examine the people's affairs and conduct, determining how to address the communal crisis requiring the fast
↓
Midday until evening: Public Torah reading (וַיְחַל, Exod. 32:11–14 and 34:1–10), Mincha prayer, and communal supplications — the community turns to repentance and petition
↓
Why this order? Morning is the standard time for courts to convene and community business to be conducted; investigation naturally precedes prayer and fasting
↓
Rabbi Ami vs. Rabbi Yirmeya: 'Resuming regular order' on the 5th Shabbat — Rabbi Ami says resume regular Torah portions; Rabbi Yirmeya says resume regular haftarot sequence
↓
Abaye supports Rabbi Ami: The Mishna's reference to interrupting 'the matter of the day' on special days implies Torah portions — only Torah has a fixed weekly order worth interrupting
