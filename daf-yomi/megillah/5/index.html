📜 Cities, Timing, and Doubt

מְגִלָּה ה׳
Core Theme Daf 5 asks: who reads when, and where? A large city (ten idlers) reads on the 14th; walled cities since Joshua read on the 15th. Megilla timing can be advanced but never postponed — except for certain mitzvot. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's unusual Purim acts and Tiberias's ambiguous walled status frame the daf's closing debates.

⚖️ Quorum for Megilla Reading — Rav's Two Statements

Rav
At its proper time (14 Adar), the Megilla may be read even privately — no quorum of ten required.
Rav
But at an improper time (villages advancing to assembly day), a quorum of ten is required.
Questioner
But Rav also said Shabbat eve is the 'proper time' for reading — doesn't that mean private reading is allowed then too?
Gemara
Rav's 'proper time' statement was made only to exclude Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi's opinion about Purim on Shabbat. The two rulings address entirely separate questions.
Resolution Rav's two statements are not contradictory — they address distinct halakhic questions about quorum and calendar.

🏙️ Large City vs. Village

Large City (עיר גדולה)

  • Has ten idlers (בטלנין) present in the synagogue
  • Men with no professional responsibilities
  • Reads Megilla on 14 Adar — no advancing
  • Reading may be done even privately (on the 14th)

Village (כפר)

  • Fewer than ten idlers — even if densely populated
  • Reads on the day of assembly (Mon/Thu before 14 Adar)
  • Must read with a quorum of ten
  • Reading is 'advanced' to accommodate farmworkers

🏛️ Tiberias: Walled Since Joshua?

Hezekiah
I am uncertain whether Tiberias was walled since Joshua's time — so I read the Megilla on both the 14th and the 15th of Adar.
Questioner
The Sea of Galilee borders one side. A sea is not a halakhic wall! Shouldn't this resolve the doubt?
Gemara
For property law (sale of walled-city homes, Lev. 25:30), Hezekiah had no doubt — a sea is not a wall. His uncertainty was specifically about Megilla law: what counts as 'walled' for the 15th-reading rule?
Rav Asi
Similarly, I read the Megilla in Huzal (Babylonia) on both days — uncertain if it was walled since Joshua's time. Huzal was an early settlement (בני בנימין).
Practical Ruling When a city's walled status since Joshua is uncertain, read on both the 14th and 15th of Adar — but do not recite a blessing on the doubtful day.
