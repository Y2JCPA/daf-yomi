🕯️ Four Sages, Four Paths to Longevity

Rabbi Perida

  • Never led Grace after Meals before a priest
  • Never ate meat before priestly portions were set aside
  • Honored the priesthood — when the priest was also a scholar

Rabbi Neḥunya ben HaKana

  • Never honored himself at another's expense
  • Never went to sleep holding a grudge
  • Was always openhanded with money

Rabbi Neḥunya the Great

  • Never accepted gifts from the house of the Nasi
  • Never exacted retribution against wrongdoers
  • Was always openhanded with money

Rabbi Zeira

  • Never was angry inside his own home
  • Never walked before someone greater than him
  • Never rejoiced at a colleague's downfall

⚖️ Should a Torah Scholar Yield to a Priest at Grace After Meals?

Rabbi Perida
I never led Grace after Meals in the presence of a priest — I always gave the priest the privilege to lead. This was my lifelong practice.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Any Torah scholar who allows someone else to lead Grace in his presence — even a High Priest who is an ignoramus — is a sinner!
Gemara (Resolution)
No contradiction: Rabbi Perida spoke of a priest who was also a Torah scholar. He deferred to the priesthood only when learning was equal — not to an ignorant priest.
Ruling Torah scholarship takes precedence over priestly lineage. Honoring a scholar-priest is praiseworthy; yielding to an ignorant priest is forbidden.

🤲 Famous Models of Humility, Forgiveness, and Generosity

Rav Huna — Honor Without Burdening

  • Carried a hoe on his shoulder returning from fieldwork
  • Did not burden his disciples to carry it for him
  • Model: Never honor oneself at another's expense

Mar Zutra — Nightly Forgiveness

  • Before sleeping each night declared:
  • "I forgive anyone who has wronged me"
  • Model: Never let resentment survive the night

Job — Openhanded with Money

  • Always left at least a perutah with the shopkeeper
  • Kept money circulating for those in need
  • Model: Generosity as spiritual practice

Rabbi Elazar — Refusing Gifts

  • Refused gifts sent from the house of the Nasi
  • Declined their invitations entirely
  • Reason: They honor me; therefore I live long

👑 The Crown of Torah — Daily Study and Its Reward

הליכות עולם לו (חבקוק ג:ו) — אַל תִּקְרֵי הֲלִיכוֹת אֶלָּא הֲלָכוֹת
School of Eliyahu Anyone who studies halakhot every day is guaranteed a place in the World-to-Come.

Source: "His ways [halikhot] are eternal" (Habakkuk 3:6)
Do not read it as halikhot (ways) — read it as halakhot (laws).

Warning: One who makes use of the taga — the crown of Torah — by having a Torah scholar serve him personally will perish. Reish Lakish cautioned even those who would serve one who has mastered four orders of Mishna.
