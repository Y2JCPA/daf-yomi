Megillah 28 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Rabbi Neḥunya ben HaKana attributed his longevity partly to never going to sleep with resentment. Which Sage's nightly practice illustrated this virtue?
A. Rav Huna, who carried his own hoe from the field
B. Mar Zutra, who declared forgiveness for all who wronged him before sleeping
C. Job, who always left a perutah with the shopkeeper
D. Rabbi Elazar, who refused gifts from the Nasi
Correct: B — Mar Zutra would say each night before bed: 'I forgive anyone who has wronged me' — the living example of never holding a grudge overnight.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How did the Gemara resolve the apparent contradiction between Rabbi Perida's deference to priests and Rabbi Yoḥanan's ruling that yielding to an ignorant High Priest is a sin?
A. Rabbi Perida lived before Rabbi Yoḥanan and was unaware of the ruling
B. Rabbi Perida only deferred to priests who were also Torah scholars
C. Rabbi Yoḥanan's ruling applied only in Babylonia, not Eretz Yisrael
D. The two rulings refer to different prayers, not Grace after Meals
Correct: B — The Gemara answers: Rabbi Perida spoke of equal stature — he honored the priesthood only when the priest was also a Torah scholar, not an ignoramus.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara rule that grass sprouting in a ruined synagogue should not be picked?
A. The grass has inherent sanctity and cannot be used for personal benefit
B. Picking it would constitute forbidden labor on synagogue property
C. It would cause anguish to those who see the synagogue in such disrepair
D. The grass is needed to feed animals designated for Temple offerings
Correct: C — The Sages ruled not to pick the grass because seeing it reminds onlookers of the synagogue's ruin, causing them anguish.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What wordplay does the School of Eliyahu use from Habakkuk 3:6 to teach that daily study of halakhot guarantees the World-to-Come?
A. Read 'Torah' (instruction) as 'torah' (law), teaching that law equals life
B. Read 'olam' (world) as 'eternal,' connecting learning to eternal reward
C. Read 'halikhot' (ways) as 'halakhot' (laws), connecting daily practice to eternity
D. Read 'yom' (day) as 'today,' urging immediate study
Correct: C — "His ways [halikhot] are eternal" (Habakkuk 3:6) — the School of Eliyahu reads halikhot as halakhot: one who studies halakhot daily is guaranteed eternal life.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda, when is a eulogy for a Torah scholar permitted inside a synagogue?
A. Only on Shabbat afternoon when the community is already assembled
B. Only when the Nasi or Exilarch grants special permission
C. When the public attends in large numbers — making it a genuine communal event
D. Only when the eulogy lasts less than one hour
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda said a 'public eulogy' means the community comes to honor the scholar — like Rafram who said 'due to my honor and the deceased's honor, all will attend.'
