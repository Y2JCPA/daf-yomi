↓
Mordecai refuses to mount until he has bathed and trimmed his hair — Esther had already closed every bathhouse and barbershop in Shushan
↓

↓

👆 Esther Reveals the Enemy — But Which One?

R. Elazar
When Esther said 'An adversary and enemy is this wicked Haman!' (Esther 7:6), she was actually pointing at Ahasuerus — an angel came and redirected her hand toward Haman
R. Abbahu
When Ahasuerus first responded to Esther, he spoke through an interpreter (he doubted her). Later he addressed her directly — showing he came to believe her
Gemara
When Ahasuerus stormed to the garden: was he going to kill Esther or Haman? The ministering angels uprooted trees in the garden to portend destruction — he returned resolved against Haman
Harbonah
'Also, the gallows 50 cubits high stands in Haman's house' — Rabbi Elazar: Harbonah too was in on the plot, but remembered favorably for turning against Haman at the right moment
Angelic Intervention Throughout this daf, angels shape the narrative — redirecting Esther's finger, uprooting trees, and rewriting Mordecai's record after Shimshai erased it

🕍 'Light, Gladness, Joy, and Honor' — Rav Yehuda's Four Interpretations

אוֹרָה — Light

  • = Torah
  • As written: 'The commandment is a lamp and Torah is light' (Prov. 6:23)
  • They returned to Torah study

שִׂמְחָה — Gladness

  • = Yom Tov
  • As written: 'You shall rejoice on your festivals' (Deut. 16:14)
  • They resumed festival observance

שָׂשׂוֹן — Joy

  • = Circumcision (Brit Milah)
  • As written: 'I rejoice at Your word' (Ps. 119:162)
  • They resumed performing brit milah

יְקָר — Honor

  • = Tefillin
  • As written: 'All peoples will see you are called by God's name and fear you' (Deut. 28:10)
  • They donned phylacteries again

🎵 Reading the Ten Sons of Haman

פַּרְשַׁנְדָּתָא וְדַלְפוֹן... וַיְזָתָא — עֲשֶׂרֶת בְּנֵי הָמָן
Halakhic Ruling — Rav Adda from Jaffa When chanting the Megilla, all ten names of Haman's sons must be read in a single breath, because they all died simultaneously.

Rabbi Ḥanina bar Pappa taught: All biblical songs are formatted like half-brick upon whole brick (alternating open/closed lines). But the list of Haman's sons and the kings of Canaan are written whole-brick upon whole-brick — solid columns — to show they fell in a heap, one atop another, with no survivors in between.

The large letter ו (vav) in וַיְזָתָא alludes to the six sons of Haman who died alongside him in the spiritual domain.
