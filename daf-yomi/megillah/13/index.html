🏛️ Why Was Mordecai Called 'Yehudi'?

First View
Mordecai was literally from the tribe of Yehuda (Judah). He is called Yehudi because of his tribal lineage — a straightforward reading.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Mordecai was actually from the tribe of Binyamin (Benjamin). He is called Yehudi because he repudiated idol worship (כפר בעבודה זרה) — for anyone who repudiates idolatry earns the title Yehudi.
Extension
The same principle applies to Bithiah, Pharaoh's daughter — she is called Yehudiyya because descending to bathe in the Nile signified her repudiation of Egyptian idolatry.
Key Principle The name 'Yehudi' transcends tribal identity — it designates one who rejects idolatry. Both Mordecai (Benjamin) and Bithiah (Egyptian princess) earn this designation through their rejection of false gods.

🌿 What Was Esther's Real Name? — Four Opinions

Hadassah Was Real, Esther Was Secondary

  • Rabbi Yehuda: Real name was Hadassah (הדסה). She was called Esther because she concealed (מסתרת, masteret) her identity — 'Esther had not yet made known her kindred' (Est. 2:20)
  • Rabbi Neḥemya: Real name was Hadassah. Esther was her non-Hebrew name — the nations named her after Istahar (Venus) on account of her surpassing beauty
  • Ben Azzai: She was neither tall nor short but of medium height — like a myrtle branch (hadas). Hence the name Hadassah

Esther Was Real, Hadassah Was Secondary

  • Rabbi Meir: Her real name was Esther. She was called Hadassah (myrtle) because the righteous are compared to myrtle — a fragrant, evergreen plant symbolizing virtue
  • Shmuel's view on 'advancement': Esther's keeper gave her pig meat thinking it a delicacy — he meant it kindly but it was non-kosher. Esther maintained her Jewish diet throughout
  • Rabbi Yoḥanan: He gave her vegetables (ירקות) — completely avoiding kashrut issues, as Daniel's attendants did (Daniel 1:16)

🤝 Rachel's Modesty: Giving Away the Secret Signs

Jacob meets Rachel and proposes marriage. She accepts but warns: 'My father Laban is a swindler — he will deceive you on the wedding night.'
↓
Jacob gives Rachel secret signs (סימנים) so she can identify herself to him in the dark. He reassures her: 'I am his equal in cleverness — I can outwit him.'
↓
Laban schemes to switch Leah for Rachel. Rachel realizes: 'Now my sister will be embarrassed — Jacob will ask for the signs and Leah will not know them!'
↓
Rachel gives Leah all the secret signs — sacrificing her own marriage to protect her sister from public humiliation. This is tzniut (modesty) of the highest order.
↓
The reward (Job 36:7): In merit of Rachel's extraordinary modesty, Shaul HaMelech (King Saul) descended from her — himself a paragon of modesty, concealing Samuel's promise of kingship (I Sam. 10:16).

🎲 Haman Casts the Pur — How Adar Was Selected

Haman casts pur (פור) — the lot
Seeking the most auspicious month to destroy the Jewish people
↓
Months Rejected
Nissan: Month of Pesach redemption — protected
Other months: Each had merits shielding Israel — patriarchs' birthdays, holidays, giving of Torah
Adar Chosen!
Moshe Rabbeinu died in Adar — Haman rejoiced, seeing a terrible omen for Israel. He did not know Moshe was also born in Adar, which softens the decree.
↓
Rava (citing Reish Lakish): God never strikes Israel unless He has already created the remedy. Mordecai and Esther — the cure — were in place before Haman was promoted.
עַד שֶׁלֹּא בָּרָא הַמַּכָּה, בָּרָא לָהּ רְפוּאָה

⚔️ Haman's Case to Ahasuerus — Arguing for Genocide

Haman
יֶשְׁנוֹ (yeshno) — 'There is one people' (Est. 3:8). Read as yashen (asleep): this nation is asleep regarding the commandments — they have abandoned their covenant with God.
Ahasuerus
But there are Sages among them who do observe the mitzvot — you cannot condemn an entire people on account of the many!
Haman
They are one people (עם אחד) — all alike. The scholars do not truly observe either. And: they do not eat our food, do not intermarry with us, and do not keep the king's laws.
Haman
You fear a bald spot in your kingdom? No need — I will pay 10,000 silver talents (ככר כסף) from my own wealth to fund the entire operation.
Rabbi Abba
'The silver is given to you; the people also' (Est. 3:11) — Ahasuerus was more wicked than Haman! Haman offered to pay; Ahasuerus handed over the people for free, treating Jewish lives as worthless.
Wordplay (Gematria/Derash) Haman's word 'yeshno' (there is) is read as 'yashen' (asleep) — implying the Jews have fallen spiritually asleep and God has, as it were, turned away. This doubles as accusation and as implicit prayer that God awaken to save them.
