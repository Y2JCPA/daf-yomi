Megillah 9 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
How did the 72 elders alter the opening verse of Genesis (1:1) for King Ptolemy?
A. They transliterated it into Greek letters without changing meaning
B. They wrote 'God created in the beginning,' reversing the word order
C. They omitted it entirely to avoid theological confusion
D. They added a marginal note explaining the Hebrew grammar
Correct: B — They reversed 'Bereishit bara Elohim' to read 'God created in the beginning' so that 'Bereishit' could not be misread as a proper name (a deity) who created God — protecting monotheism from polytheistic misinterpretation.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did the 72 elders substitute 'short-legged beast' for 'arnevet' (hare) in the list of non-kosher animals?
A. The hare was considered a sacred animal in Egyptian religion
B. There was no accurate Greek equivalent for the Hebrew word
C. King Ptolemy's wife was named Arnevet and they feared giving offense
D. The hare had already been listed in a prior verse
Correct: C — Ptolemy's wife was named Arnevet (hare). Writing her name among impure animals could have led Ptolemy to accuse the Jews of mocking his queen — so they substituted a neutral description to protect the mission.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ḥiyya bar Abba, what is the scriptural basis for Greek being the one language (beyond Hebrew) honored for Torah scrolls?
A. Deuteronomy 6:6 implies only one foreign language may supplement Hebrew
B. Genesis 9:27 — 'God shall enlarge Japheth and he shall dwell in the tents of Shem'
C. The Septuagint was completed without error, proving divine approval
D. King Solomon established Greek as a sacred tongue at the Temple's dedication
Correct: B — From 'God shall enlarge Japheth and he shall dwell in the tents of Shem' (Gen. 9:27), the Gemara derives that the beauty of Japheth — specifically the Greek language — has a divinely designated place within the sacred domain of Torah (Shem's tents).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the halakhic status of a High Priest who served temporarily and then was replaced by the original High Priest?
A. He returns fully to his role as a common priest with no restrictions
B. He becomes High Priest permanently since he was already appointed
C. He may serve neither as High Priest nor as a common priest
D. He must wait one year before resuming ordinary priestly service
Correct: C — He cannot return as High Priest — that would cause jealousy and bitterness. He cannot return as a common priest either — the principle is 'we elevate to higher sanctity but do not lower.' The incident of Rabbi Yosef ben Elem of Tzippori illustrates this ruling.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Shimon, what is the key limitation of the great public altar at Nob and Gibeon (vs. a personal altar)?
A. Only the High Priest could officiate — no ordinary priests
B. The public could only offer Paschal lambs and obligatory offerings — not voluntary ones
C. Offerings had to be consumed within the altar precinct, not in the city
D. Animal sacrifices were forbidden; only grain offerings were permitted
Correct: B — Rabbi Shimon teaches that even the great public altar was restricted: the public sacrificed only Paschal lambs and compulsory offerings — voluntary offerings were not permitted. This distinguishes it from the Tabernacle at Shiloh and the Temple in Jerusalem.
