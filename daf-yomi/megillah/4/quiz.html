QUESTION 1 OF 5
How does Rabbi Elazar resolve the apparent contradiction between Lod and Ono being 'walled since Joshua' and I Chronicles crediting Elpaal with building them?
A. Elpaal was a descendant of Joshua and inherited his legal status
B. The verse in Chronicles is considered non-authoritative for halachic purposes
C. The cities were walled from Joshua's time, destroyed in the War of the Concubine of Gibeah, and Elpaal rebuilt them
D. Elpaal built new walls around previously unwalled towns, and the Sages ruled leniently
Correct: C — Rabbi Elazar explains that these cities were originally walled since Joshua's time, but were destroyed when all of Benjamin's cities were razed during the civil war over the Concubine of Gibeah. Elpaal rebuilt them, so the verse accurately credits him with 'building' — but their original walled status is what determines their Megilla reading date.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does 'lishnota' mean in Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi's statement that one must read the Megilla at night and lishnota during the day?
A. To study the tractate of Mishna relevant to Purim
B. To recite special Purim prayers alongside the reading
C. To repeat the Megilla reading
D. To teach the Megilla to one's children
Correct: C — Some students mistakenly understood 'lishnota' as referring to Mishna study, but Rabbi Yirmeya corrected them: it means to repeat (lishna) the full Megilla reading during the day. This is confirmed by Rabbi Ḥelbo citing Ulla Bira'a.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rabba's reason that the Megilla is not read on Shabbat?
A. The festive Purim meal cannot be held on Shabbat
B. Someone might carry the Megilla through a public domain to bring it to an expert reader
C. The poor cannot receive matanot l'evyonim on Shabbat
D. Megilla reading is considered a form of labor prohibited on Shabbat
Correct: B — Rabba explains that since everyone is equally obligated, someone who cannot read might carry the Megilla in a public domain on Shabbat to reach an expert who can read for them — a Torah-level Shabbat violation. This same logic explains why shofar is not blown on Shabbat-Rosh HaShana.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yosei, when the 14th of Adar falls on Friday, how do walled cities observe Purim?
A. They read on Friday, the 14th, together with large towns
B. They read on Shabbat, the 15th, since that is their designated day
C. They advance their reading to Thursday, the day of assembly
D. They read on Sunday, the 16th, as a make-up day
Correct: C — Rabbi Yosei rules that walled cities advance to Thursday. His reasoning: 'in every year walled cities do not precede large towns' — so walled cities cannot read before large towns. Since the 15th is Shabbat (forbidden), they must go back to Thursday, even though this means they read before the large towns.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Rav Yosef say the Megilla is not read on Shabbat?
A. Shabbat's sanctity is incompatible with the joyful noise of Purim celebration
B. The poor await the Megilla reading day to receive gifts, and those gifts cannot be distributed on Shabbat
C. The Megilla scroll has special writing rules that create Shabbat concerns when handled
D. Reading the Megilla in public constitutes a prohibited labor under Shabbat law
Correct: B — Rav Yosef reasons that the poor come to the Megilla reading because that is when matanot l'evyonim (gifts to the poor) are distributed. If the reading were on Shabbat, gifts could not be given, defeating the purpose of the poor's attendance. The reading must occur on a weekday when gift-giving is possible.
