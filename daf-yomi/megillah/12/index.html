🔢 The Seventy-Year Exile: Who Counted Wrong?

Jeremiah's prophecy: 70 years of Babylonian exile before redemption
↓
Belshazzar's error
Counted from Jehoiakim's exile — declared the 70 years complete, celebrated with the Temple vessels, then was killed
Daniel's error (Rava)
Also miscounted — calculated from Nebuchadnezzar's accession year incorrectly, mourning over a miscalculated deadline
↓
True fulfillment: Darius the Mede completes the correct 70 years after Belshazzar's death — Jews permitted to return to Eretz Yisrael
↓
Two separate prophecies: 70 years 'for Babylonia' = permission to return (lifekida); 70 years for building the Temple counts differently (Rava)

🎉 Was Ahasuerus Wise to Feast in Shushan?

Rav
Ahasuerus was foolish — feasting only for Shushan's residents alienated the distant nobles of all 127 provinces who were not invited.
Shmuel
Ahasuerus was wise — feasting near his own throne meant that if rebellion arose, he could quickly suppress it from the seat of power.
Rav
This foolishness extended to the letters he sent: 'Every man shall rule in his own house' — even a lowly weaver is master in his home! The decree mocked itself.
Rava
The first letters (about male authority) were dismissed as obvious — but they gave cover for Haman's second, deadly letters to also appear as royal edicts.
📖 Esther 1:5 After 180 days of displaying royal glory, Ahasuerus made a seven-day feast 'for all people present in Shushan the capital' — the setting for Vashti's refusal and the unraveling that follows.

🏛️ The Splendor of Ahasuerus's Feast (Esther 1:6–8)

Hangings & Textiles

  • ḥur: Rav — lace-like fabric pierced with many holes (ḥarei ḥarei); Shmuel — carpets of fine white wool
  • karpas: fine linen drapery (green-blue)
  • sky-blue (tekhelet): hung on silver rods and marble pillars

Floor & Furnishings

  • bahat: precious stones that ingratiate themselves with their owners — people pay enormous sums for them (R. Asi)
  • dar: many rows arranged in circles (darei darei) — Rav
  • soḥaret: from seḥor seḥor (around and around) — circular marble bands

Drinking & Vessels

  • Gold vessels, each different (shonim) from one another — not shavim (same)
  • Why shonim? The Temple vessels had been changed (shinu) from their sacred purpose — a wordplay of rebuke
  • Each guest received wine from his own country so he felt entirely at home (R. Elazar on 'none did compel')
  • Drinking was like the Torah's laws — no one was compelled beyond their capacity (R. Meir)

🤔 Why Were Jews Deserving — Yet Miraculously Saved?

Students ask R. Shimon bar Yoḥai: Why did the Jews of Ahasuerus's generation deserve annihilation?
↓
Answer: They bowed to Nebuchadnezzar's idol at the plain of Dura — an act of apparent idol worship that warranted destruction
↓
Follow-up question: If they truly sinned, why was a miracle performed? Is God showing favoritism?
↓
Resolution
They only appeared to worship outwardly — in their hearts they did not believe. The decree was real, but so was their inner faithfulness.
Cause of decree
They enjoyed Ahasuerus's feast and derived benefit from it — this participation in royal excess brought the retributive decree of Haman.

📏 Vashti: The Measure-for-Measure Principle

Vashti's sin: She forced Jewish maidens to work naked on Shabbat — humiliating them in their holy time
↓
On the seventh day of the feast (Shabbat), Ahasuerus commands Vashti to appear before the men wearing only her crown — naked
↓
Divine punishment: R. Yosei bar Ḥanina — Vashti was struck with leprosy (tzaraat), rendering her unable to appear before the king
↓
According to another tradition: a tail grew on her — a further degradation mirroring her degradation of others
↓
Principle: בְּמִדָּה שֶׁאָדָם מוֹדֵד בָּהּ מוֹדְדִין לוֹ
With the measure that a person measures to others, so are they measured — on Shabbat she sinned, on Shabbat she was punished
