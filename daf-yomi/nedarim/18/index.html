Nedarim 18: Layered Vows & Ambiguous Language

Can a second vow take effect when the first is still active?
↓
Rav Huna
Second identical vow does not take effect
Gemara's Refutation
Nazir lehazzir teaches naziriteship does layer
↓
Mishna: When vow language is ambiguous, how do we rule?
↓
Stam nedarim lehachmir — Unspecified vows are treated stringently
KEY PRINCIPLE Ambiguous vow language is presumed to refer to the more sacred referent, making the vow binding.

Does Naziriteship Layer on Prior Naziriteship?

Rav Huna
If one says 'I am a nazirite today, I am a nazirite today' — the second vow does not take effect. It is subsumed by the first.
Rav Hamnuna
Objection! The verse says nazir lehazzir (Numbers 6:2) — this teaches naziriteship takes effect upon prior naziriteship!
Rav Huna
The verse refers to one who accepted two terms simultaneously ('I am a nazirite twice'), not identical sequential statements.
Rav Hamnuna
If 'today/tomorrow' — it's obvious the second takes effect for the extra day. The verse must address 'today/today' — refuting you!
A FORTIORI ARGUMENT If oaths (which are more stringent) don't layer, one might think naziriteship shouldn't either. The verse nazir lehazzir overrides this logic.

Why Are Oaths More Stringent Than Vows?

Oath (Shevu'a) — More Stringent

  • Written: 'The Lord will not hold guiltless one who takes His name in vain' (Exodus 20:6)
  • Takes effect on matters with no substance (davar she'ein bo mamash)
  • An oath upon an oath does NOT take effect

Vow (Neder) — More Stringent

  • Takes effect on mitzvot — can prohibit even permitted commandments
  • Naziriteship upon naziriteship DOES take effect (nazir lehazzir)
  • Vows create prohibitions on objects, not on the person directly
RESOLUTION Oaths are considered more stringent overall because of the divine warning against taking God's name in vain.

Rava: Dissolution of the First Oath

A person says: 'I swear I will not eat' then again: 'I swear I will not eat'
↓
He eats — liable for only one oath violation
↓
He then requests dissolution (she'eilah) of the first oath from a sage
↓
Does the second oath now take effect?
↓
Rava's Proof
Mishna says 'liable for only one' — not 'there is only one.' The second oath exists but lacked room to operate.
Implication
Once the first is dissolved, the second takes its place immediately.
RAVA'S PRINCIPLE A dormant oath that lacked a 'span of time' to take effect is not void — it activates when the blocking oath is dissolved.

R' Meir vs. R' Yehuda: Regional Differences

R' Meir
All unspecified teruma and dedications are forbidden everywhere. We always assume the more stringent referent.
R' Yehuda
It depends on local usage. In Judea, people know the Temple chamber teruma — so unspecified 'teruma' is forbidden.
R' Yehuda
In the Galilee, they only know teruma given to priests — so unspecified 'teruma' is permitted.
R' Yehuda
Conversely, Galileans don't know priestly dedications — so 'cherem' in the Galilee means dedication to Heaven and is forbidden. In Judea it's permitted.
UNDERLYING LOGIC R' Yehuda holds that vow interpretation follows common local parlance — what people in that region typically mean by a given term.
