QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, why are bargaining vows not binding even according to the Rabbanan?
A. Because the Mishna uses present tense 'both want' — showing they never intended to vow
B. Because bargaining vows are automatically dissolved by Rabbinic decree
C. Because monetary vows are never valid
D. Because a vow requires two witnesses to be binding
Correct: A — Rava notes that the Mishna says 'both want' (present tense), indicating they were satisfied with three dinars from the outset and never truly intended to vow.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara permit entering the host's house and drinking cold water despite the guest's vow?
A. Because the vow was made under duress
B. Because a person speaks in exaggerated terms (לשון הבאי) and meant only to refuse a full meal
C. Because konam vows are never binding on water
D. Because the host dissolved the vow himself
Correct: B — The Gemara establishes that people speak in exaggerated terms. The guest's vow about 'a drop of cold water' was really about refusing a meal, not literally forbidding water.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Ravina argue that the cold water case cannot prove that buyer-seller vows are mere exaggeration?
A. Because commercial transactions have stricter rules than social invitations
B. Because the host meant a full meal when he said 'a drop of cold water' (the righteous say little and do much), but the buyer-seller case is uncertain
C. Because the buyer and seller used the word konam explicitly
D. Because Rav Ashi disagrees with the principle of exaggerated speech
Correct: B — Ravina distinguishes: the host's 'drop of cold water' was an understatement for a full meal (צדיקים אומרים מעט ועושים הרבה). The guest's vow thus refers to the meal. But in buyer-seller vows, it's genuinely uncertain whether they exaggerated.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the practical difference between Shmuel's view and Rav Yosef's version of Rav Asi's statement?
A. Shmuel requires three judges; Rav Asi requires only one
B. Shmuel says the four vows need no authority at all; Rav Asi limits a חכם to dissolving only vows similar to these four or mistaken vows
C. Shmuel permits regret-based dissolution; Rav Asi prohibits all dissolution
D. There is no practical difference between them
Correct: B — Shmuel holds the four vows are already dissolved and need no halakhic authority. Rav Yosef's version of Rav Asi says a חכם can only dissolve vows similar to these four (unintended) or mistaken vows — implying regret alone is insufficient.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rav Huna's method of dissolving vows differ from the approach implied by Rav Yosef's version of Rav Asi?
A. Rav Huna requires a panel of three; Rav Asi requires only one judge
B. Rav Huna dissolves based on simple regret (חרטה); Rav Asi's view implies a חכם needs a concrete opening, not mere regret
C. Rav Huna only dissolves bargaining vows; Rav Asi dissolves all vows
D. Rav Huna requires the vower to fast; Rav Asi does not
Correct: B — Rav Huna asks 'Is your heart still upon you?' and dissolves based on regret alone. Rav Yosef's version of Rav Asi implies a חכם cannot broach dissolution based on regret — he needs to find a specific factor (פתח) the vower didn't consider.
