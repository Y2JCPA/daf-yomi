Nedarim 24 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov, what is the status of nidrei ziruz (vows of exhortation)?
A. They are fully binding vows
B. They are not considered vows at all
C. They require dissolution by a halakhic authority
D. They are valid only if a kinyan was made
Correct: B — R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov holds that vows of exhortation — made solely to socially persuade — are not real vows and have no halakhic force.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov concedes the vow is real in the 'gift for your son' case?
A. Because the vower used God's name
B. Because the vower said 'I am not a dog' — revealing genuine reciprocity concerns
C. Because the gift was worth more than a perutah
D. Because the recipient explicitly accepted the vow
Correct: B — The statement 'I am not a dog, that I benefit from you and you don't benefit from me' shows the vow stems from genuine personal dignity, not mere social encouragement.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rav Ashi deflect the proof from vows impeded by ones (unavoidable circumstances)?
A. He says the mishna follows the Rabbis, not R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov
B. He reinterprets who initiated the vow — the guest, not the host
C. He argues ones always nullifies any vow automatically
D. He claims the mishna is discussing oaths, not vows
Correct: B — Rav Ashi explains that the potential guest initiated the vow by pressing the host to swear. Since the host didn't volunteer the vow to encourage, it's not exhortation — even R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov would call it a real vow.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the difference between nidrei havai (vows of exaggeration) and oaths of exaggeration?
A. Both are permitted
B. Both are forbidden
C. Exaggerated vows are permitted; exaggerated oaths are forbidden
D. Exaggerated vows are forbidden; exaggerated oaths are permitted
Correct: C — Because oaths are more severe, one is presumed not to swear lightly. Therefore exaggerated oaths are still treated as binding, while exaggerated vows are not.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Ravina's creative challenge regarding the oath 'I saw as many people as those who ascended from Egypt'?
A. Perhaps the person was dreaming and saw them in a vision
B. Perhaps he saw an anthill and called the ants 'those who ascended from Egypt'
C. Perhaps he counted all people who ever walked the road over many years
D. Perhaps 'those who ascended from Egypt' refers only to the tribal leaders
Correct: B — Ravina suggests to Rav Ashi that perhaps the person saw an anthill full of ants and metaphorically called them 'those who ascended from Egypt' — making the oath technically true rather than exaggerated.
