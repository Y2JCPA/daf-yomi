Nedarim 30 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yochanan, why can't a pre-arranged second betrothal automatically take effect after divorce?
A. Because a divorce invalidates all prior agreements
B. Because a divorced woman is like property redeemed by others — she is independent
C. Because two perutot cannot create two separate betrothals
D. Because betrothal requires witnesses each time
Correct: B — R' Yochanan distinguishes self-redemption from others' redemption. A divorced woman is independent like property redeemed by others — the original owner's intent no longer controls the outcome.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does a vow against 'seafarers' NOT include those who travel from Akko to Jaffa?
A. Because that route is inland
B. Because they are considered land-dwellers due to the short trip
C. Because the vow only covers international waters
D. Because Akko and Jaffa are in the same country
Correct: B — The Akko-to-Jaffa trip is a short coastal voyage. Those travelers are considered land-dwellers, not seafarers. True 'seafarers' are those who customarily depart to distant locations.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
A vow against 'those who see the sun' includes blind people. Why?
A. Because blind people can sense sunlight through warmth
B. Because the phrase means 'those the sun shines upon,' excluding only fish and fetuses
C. Because blind people once had sight
D. Because the Rabbis always interpret vows broadly
Correct: B — The Gemara explains: he didn't say 'those who see' (excluding blind) but 'those who see the sun' — meaning those exposed to sunlight. The phrase excludes fish and fetuses, not blind people.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why are women excluded from the category of 'dark heads' (sheḥorei harosh)?
A. Because women typically have lighter hair
B. Because women's heads are always covered, so they are not characterized by visible hair
C. Because the Torah uses a masculine grammatical form
D. Because women are exempt from vows
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that 'dark heads' refers to those who sometimes cover and sometimes uncover their heads — men. Women always cover their heads, and children always uncover theirs, so neither group is called 'dark heads.'
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve Rav Pappa's challenge that 'noladim' in Genesis 48:5 refers to those ALREADY born?
A. It rejects the verse as irrelevant to halakha
B. It shows that noladim can mean either past or future, but vows follow colloquial usage where it means 'not yet born'
C. It says the verse is an exception to the general rule
D. It says R' Meir disagrees with the verse's usage
Correct: B — The Gemara brings a counter-verse (I Kings 13:2) where nolad clearly means future. It concludes the word can mean either, but in colloquial usage — which governs vows — noladim means those not yet born.
