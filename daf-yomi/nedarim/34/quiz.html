Nedarim 34 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Version 2 of the dispute, why is the 'peruta of Rav Yosef' not a concern when the owner's property is forbidden to the finder?
A. Because it is uncommon
B. Because the finder waives it
C. Because it goes to hekdesh automatically
D. Because the Torah exempts small amounts
Correct: A — The Gemara explains that the small incidental benefit (peruta of Rav Yosef) that the finder gains is not a concern because it is not common (lo shechiach).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In Rava's teaching about ownerless bread consecrated as hekdesh, what determines the amount of me'ilah repayment?
A. The market value of the bread
B. The actual benefit the person derived
C. A fixed Temple penalty amount
D. The weight of the bread
Correct: B — Rava distinguishes between taking the loaf to eat (full value repayment) and bequeathing it to children (only discretionary benefit repayment), showing that me'ilah is measured by actual benefit derived.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava, if someone says 'my loaf is konam for you' and then gives the loaf as a gift, what is the status?
A. Permitted, since it is no longer 'his' loaf
B. Permitted, since a gift is a new transaction
C. Forbidden, since the prohibition follows the recipient
D. Forbidden, but only rabbinically
Correct: C — Rava rules that the emphasis is on 'for you' — the prohibition attaches to the recipient regardless of how the loaf changes hands. A gift does not remove the konam.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does the phrase 'my loaf' come to exclude, according to Rava's final answer?
A. A case where the loaf was stolen
B. A case where the loaf was given as a gift
C. A case where he invited the person to eat before the vow
D. A case where the loaf became ownerless
Correct: C — Rava explains that 'my loaf' excludes a case where the owner had already invited the other person to eat before making the vow — that portion is considered already belonging to the invitee.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara conclude 'kashya' (it is difficult) regarding Version 2's strict opinion?
A. Because the opinion contradicts an explicit verse
B. Because it cannot explain why the mishna requires payment to go to hekdesh
C. Because Rava explicitly refutes it
D. Because it contradicts the rule of the peruta of Rav Yosef
Correct: B — The strict opinion (finder's property forbidden to owner → may not return) only addresses the reverse case in the mishna, where there is no reason for payment to go to hekdesh. This creates an unresolved difficulty.
