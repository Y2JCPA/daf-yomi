Vows on Foods: What About Their Derivatives?

A person vows a food is forbidden (konam)
↓
Does the derivative bear the name of the original food?
Yes (e.g., date honey)
R' Yehuda ben Beteira: Derivative is also forbidden
No (e.g., apple wine ≠ 'wine')
Permitted — derivative is a different category
↓
Rabbis / First Tanna: Even named derivatives are permitted unless the item is not commonly eaten raw (R' Shimon ben Elazar's principle)
Core Principle The scope of a vow depends on common usage, regional norms, and the relationship between a food and its derivative.

Named Derivatives: Forbidden or Permitted?

First Tanna
One who vows dates are forbidden is permitted to eat date honey. Same for late grapes and their vinegar.
R' Yehuda ben Beteira
If the derivative bears the name of the original (דבש תמרים — date honey), then the derivative is also forbidden.
Rabbis
We permit the derivative even when it carries the original's name.
Key Question The Gemara asks: The Rabbis seem identical to the First Tanna! What is the difference between them?

R' Shimon ben Elazar's Principle — The Hidden Distinction

Is the food commonly eaten in its original form?
Yes (e.g., dates)
Vow on the food → both the food and its derivative are forbidden
(First Tanna disagrees; Rabbis agree with R' Shimon ben Elazar here)
No (e.g., late grapes)
Vow on the food → he intended only the derivative
The food itself is permitted, derivative is forbidden
Rabbis vs. First Tanna The First Tanna makes no distinction — derivative always permitted. The Rabbis follow R' Shimon ben Elazar: for items not commonly eaten raw, the vow targets the derivative, not the food itself.

Unspecified Terms: Meaning Depends on Context

Standard Meanings

  • 🍷 "Wine" = grape wine (not apple wine/cider)
  • 🫒 "Oil" = olive oil (in Eretz Yisrael)
  • 🍯 "Honey" = bee honey (not date honey)
  • 🥗 "Vinegar" = wine vinegar (not late-grape vinegar)
  • 🥬 "Vegetables" = garden vegetables (not wild/field)

Regional Variation: Oil

  • In Eretz Yisrael → olive oil forbidden, sesame permitted
  • In Babylonia → sesame oil forbidden, olive permitted
  • Where both are used → BOTH forbidden
  • Even if only a minority uses one type — safek d'oraita l'chumra
Principle An unspecified vow follows the common local usage (לשון בני אדם). Where usage is mixed, we rule stringently on uncertain Torah prohibitions.
