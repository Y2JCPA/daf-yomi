5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Akiva, what happens when part of a vow is found to be invalid?
A. Only the invalid part is dissolved
B. The entire vow is dissolved
C. The vow must be brought before a court
D. A new vow must be taken to replace it
Correct: B — R' Akiva's foundational principle is 'neder shehutar miktzato, hutar kulo' — a vow dissolved partially is dissolved completely. This is the key innovation he brought to the earlier ruling about benot shuaḥ figs.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the case of the litigant who deposited documents and was prevented by ones from returning, what does Rava rule?
A. The documents are void because the condition was not met
B. The documents remain valid because ones exempts him
C. The case must be retried from the beginning
D. The court may extend the deadline at its discretion
Correct: B — Rava argues that the Torah itself exempts victims of ones (citing Deuteronomy 22:26 regarding the raped maiden), and this principle applies broadly — not just to capital cases but also to monetary and vow-related matters.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara distinguish the case of the man who gave a conditional get and then died from the litigant's case?
A. Because death is not considered ones
B. Because the husband's deeper intent was to free his wife from yibum — he would have given the get anyway
C. Because a get has stricter requirements than monetary documents
D. Because the wife was already considered divorced
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that had the husband known he would die, he would have immediately finalized the get to spare his wife from levirate marriage. His deeper intent aligned with the outcome. The litigant, by contrast, never intended to forfeit his rights.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Shmuel rule that the man who arrived at the river but couldn't cross due to the ferry is NOT exempt by ones?
A. Because shouting across a river doesn't count as arrival
B. Because a ferry being on the wrong side is a foreseeable circumstance he should have anticipated
C. Because the get was already delivered by the agents
D. Because Shmuel never accepts the ones exemption
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that ones which is common and foreseeable (ones d'shkhiach) — like a ferry not being available — does not qualify for exemption. The person should have anticipated this possibility and stipulated accordingly.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the final halakha, under what conditions does an asmakhta (conditional obligation) take effect?
A. Whenever the person makes a verbal commitment
B. Only when performed before an eminent court and the person deliberately chose not to fulfill the condition (not impeded by ones)
C. Only in cases involving monetary disputes over 200 zuz
D. Whenever a kinyan (act of acquisition) is performed, regardless of venue
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes with two requirements for a valid asmakhta: (1) it must be effected before an eminent court (beit din chashuv), and (2) the person's failure to fulfill the condition must have been deliberate, not due to circumstances beyond their control.
