Intimations of Vows: Wicked, Virtuous & Gift Offerings

A person says: 'Like the vows of...'
The Wicked
Binding vow — naziriteship, offering, or oath
The Virtuous
Not binding — virtuous people don't vow
Gift Offerings of the Virtuous
Binding — virtuous do bring voluntary offerings
Key Principle The character of the model person determines whether a vow-by-analogy takes effect.

Shmuel's Three Formulas for 'Vows of the Wicked'

FormulaApplies ToExample Context
הריני — 'I am hereby'NaziriteshipSaid while a nazirite passes before him
עלי — 'I accept upon myself'Offering (Korban)Obligates to bring a sacrifice
ממנו — 'From it' (I will not eat)Oath (Shevua)Restricts from a specific item
Shmuel Each formula must be paired with a specific context — otherwise the vow is ambiguous and may not take effect.

Why 'I Am Hereby' Means Naziriteship

Gemara
Perhaps 'I am hereby' means: I am hereby accepting a fast, not naziriteship?
Shmuel
The case is where a nazirite was passing in front of him — context clarifies intent.
Gemara
And 'from it' — perhaps he means 'I will eat from it,' not 'I will not eat'?
Rava
The case is where he explicitly said 'I will not eat from it.'
Gemara
If he said it explicitly, why do we need this ruling at all?
Gemara
Lest you say: since he didn't utter the word 'oath' (שבועה), it doesn't take effect — the Mishna teaches it is still a valid intimation of an oath.
Principle An intimation of an oath (yad l'shevua) is binding even without the explicit term 'oath.'

Neder (Vow) vs. Nedava (Gift Offering)

Neder — נדר

  • Says: 'It is incumbent upon me to bring an offering'
  • Obligation falls on the person
  • Risk of delay — violating בל תאחר
  • Virtuous people avoid this

Nedava — נדבה

  • Designates a specific animal as an offering
  • Obligation is on the animal
  • Risk of misuse (מעילה) of consecrated property
  • Virtuous people do bring these — using Hillel's method
R' Meir's View One should abstain from vows (nedarim), but gift offerings (nedavot) are acceptable — the risk can be mitigated.

Hillel the Elder's Method: Avoiding Misuse of Offerings

🐄 Bring the animal unconsecrated to the Temple courtyard
↓
🙌 Consecrate the animal only upon arrival in the azara
↓
✋ Immediately perform semicha (laying of hands)
↓
🔪 Slaughter the animal right away
↓
✅ No time gap = no opportunity for misuse (מעילה)
Result They said about Hillel the Elder that no person ever misused his burnt-offering in his lifetime.

R' Meir vs. R' Yehuda: Should One Vow at All?

R' Meir
Better than vowing and paying, or vowing and not paying, is one who does not vow at all. (Ecclesiastes 5:4)
R' Yehuda
Better than both is one who vows and pays — fulfilling a vow is meritorious.
Gemara
Neither distinguishes between vows and gift offerings — yet the Mishna does! Who is the tanna?
Gemara
It can still be R' Meir: he only opposes nedarim (vows), not nedavot (gift offerings).
Ecclesiastes 5:4 "Better that you should not vow, than that you should vow and not pay."
