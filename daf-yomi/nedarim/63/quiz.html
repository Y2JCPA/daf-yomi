Nedarim 63 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Zeira, why did the Tannaim disagree about the date of the second rainfall?
A. It determines when one who vows 'until the rain' is released
B. It determines when to begin fasting for drought
C. It determines when to start asking for rain in prayer
D. It determines the planting season for wheat
Correct: A — R' Zeira explains that the second rainfall date is significant for someone who vows 'until the rain' — their vow expires at that date.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the contradiction between R' Zeira (vow follows the expected date) and Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel (vow follows actual rainfall)?
A. R' Zeira's statement applies to 'until the rain' (singular); RSBG's applies to 'until the rains' (plural)
B. R' Zeira retracted his opinion
C. They are discussing different geographic regions
D. RSBG's statement is rejected as a minority opinion
Correct: A — The Gemara distinguishes between 'until the rain' (singular — goes by the expected date per R' Zeira) and 'until the rains' (plural — depends on actual rainfall per RSBG).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If someone vows 'until the beginning of Adar' and doesn't know it's a leap year, when does the vow expire?
A. Beginning of the first Adar
B. Beginning of the second Adar
C. End of the first Adar
D. It depends on the dispute between R' Meir and R' Yehuda
Correct: A — Abaye explains that when the vower didn't know it was a leap year, all agree he meant the first Adar — the only Adar he expected to exist.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Yehuda, why does a vow of 'wine is konam until Passover' expire at the night of Passover?
A. Because the vower presumably didn't intend to prevent himself from drinking the four cups at the Seder
B. Because Passover always begins at night by Torah law
C. Because wine is forbidden on Passover itself
D. Because vows always expire at nightfall of the specified date
Correct: A — R' Yehuda holds we follow the vower's presumed intent — he would not have wanted to prevent himself from fulfilling the mitzvah of the four cups on Seder night.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When someone vows to prohibit benefit from a woman he is being pressured to marry, what is the halakha?
A. She is permitted to benefit from him — his intent was only to refuse marriage
B. She is fully prohibited from all benefit
C. She needs a halakhic authority to dissolve the vow
D. The vow is void because it was made under duress
Correct: A — The Mishna rules that vows made to deflect social pressure are interpreted narrowly — he only intended to refuse marriage, not to prohibit all forms of benefit.
