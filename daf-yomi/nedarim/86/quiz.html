QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does R' Yirmeya reject R' Ila's analogy of a field being sold?
A. Because fields cannot be consecrated
B. Because the seller still possesses the field at the time of his statement, unlike the woman
C. Because the buyer would not agree to the condition
D. Because consecration doesn't apply to land
Correct: B — R' Yirmeya argues that R' Ila's case works because the field is still in the seller's possession when he speaks. The woman's handiwork, however, already belongs to her husband — she has no current ownership to consecrate.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rav Ashi's objection to Rav Sheisha's analogy of a field pledged for ten years?
A. Ten years is too long a period for consecration
B. Pledged fields cannot be consecrated at all
C. A ten-year pledge has a fixed endpoint, but divorce has no fixed timeframe
D. The field's produce belongs to the creditor permanently
Correct: C — Rav Ashi points out that a ten-year pledge has a known endpoint, so the owner knows exactly when the field returns. A woman has no way of knowing when or if she will be divorced, making the situations fundamentally different.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava's principle, which three things abrogate existing liens?
A. Hekdesh, shemittah, and yovel
B. Hekdesh, chametz on Pesach, and freeing a slave
C. Terumah, maaser, and bikkurim
D. Konamot, nedarim, and shevuot
Correct: B — Rava teaches that hekdesh (consecration to the Temple), chametz on Pesach (the prohibition of leavened bread), and shichrur (emancipation of a slave) all have the power to override and abrogate existing financial liens.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the Mishna, if a husband thought his daughter vowed but it was actually his wife, and he nullified it, what is the ruling?
A. The nullification stands for both wife and daughter
B. The vow is permanently binding and cannot be nullified
C. He must nullify the vow again with correct knowledge
D. The vow is automatically void since he attempted nullification
Correct: C — The Mishna rules that mistaken nullification is invalid. The word 'otah' (her) in the Torah implies the husband must direct his nullification at the correct person and vow. He must repeat the nullification once he realizes his error.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara ask 'why do I need R' Yochanan ben Nuri's reason of perhaps he'll divorce her?'
A. Because divorce is forbidden in this case
B. Because if konamot abrogate liens, the vow should take effect immediately without needing the divorce rationale
C. Because R' Yochanan ben Nuri lived before Rava
D. Because the husband can simply nullify the vow instead
Correct: B — If konamot truly abrogate liens like hekdesh does (per Rava's principle), then the woman's konam should take effect immediately — there's no need for R' Yochanan ben Nuri's concern about future divorce. The Gemara resolves that the vow does take effect right away, and the divorce reasoning is an additional safeguard.
