Can a Woman Consecrate Future Handiwork? — Analogies, Objections & Resolution

Core Question
Shmuel rules like R' Yochanan ben Nuri that a husband must nullify his wife's konam on her handiwork. But how can she consecrate something not yet in her possession?
Amud A
Five Amoraim propose and reject field analogies, each one refining why a woman's situation is unique
Amud B
Rav Ashi resolves: Konamot are inherently stringent — they abrogate liens like hekdesh, chametz, and freeing a slave
Mishna
New topic: If a husband nullified the wrong vow (confused wife/daughter, figs/grapes), he must nullify again
Key Principle Konamot carry the severity of inherent sanctity (kedushah she-ba-guf) and can override existing financial liens

R' Ila's Field Analogy & R' Yirmeya's Objection

R' Ila
If I say: 'This field I'm selling you — when I buy it back, let it be hekdesh' — it works! Similarly, a woman can consecrate future handiwork
R' Yirmeya
Not comparable! In your case, the field is still in his possession when he speaks. But the woman's handiwork doesn't exist yet
R' Yirmeya
Her case is like saying: 'This field I already sold you — when I buy it back, let it be hekdesh.' Would that work?
Key Distinction R' Ila's analogy fails because the woman has no current possession of her handiwork — it already belongs to the husband

Escalating Field Analogies — Each Amora Refines

R' Ila: Field I'm about to sell → consecrate for when I buy back
↓
❌ R' Yirmeya: Field is still yours now — woman's handiwork is not!
↓
Rav Pappa: Like a pledged field (mashkon) — owner retains the field itself, other has rights to produce
↓
❌ Rav Sheisha: Owner can redeem a pledge anytime — a woman can't choose when to be divorced!
↓
Rav Sheisha: Like a field pledged for 10 years — fixed term, can't redeem early
↓
❌ Rav Ashi: 10 years is a fixed endpoint — divorce has no fixed time!
Pattern Each analogy gets closer to the woman's situation but still fails — proving the answer must lie outside the field analogy framework

Why Each Field Analogy Fails

Field Analogies

  • Selling now → still in possession
  • Already sold → fully transferred
  • Pledged → can redeem anytime
  • Pledged for 10 years → fixed endpoint

Woman's Handiwork

  • Not in her possession now
  • Not fully transferred — she retains her body
  • Cannot choose when to divorce
  • No fixed timeframe for marriage to end
Conclusion No field analogy perfectly captures the woman's situation — a different legal mechanism is needed

Rav Ashi's Resolution: The Power of Konamot

הקדש וחמץ ושחרור מפקיעין מידי שעבוד
Rav Ashi / Rava Konamot are different — they have the severity of kedushah she-ba-guf (inherent sanctity).

Rava's principle: Three things abrogate existing liens:
1️⃣ Hekdesh — consecrating property to the Temple
2️⃣ Chametz on Pesach — the prohibition overrides ownership
3️⃣ Freeing a slave — emancipation cancels the master's lien

A konam works the same way — the husband's lien on her handiwork is abrogated by the vow's inherent sanctity.
