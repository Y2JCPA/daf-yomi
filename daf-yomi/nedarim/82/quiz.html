5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
When a woman vows 'I am removed from the Jews,' why can her husband nullify only his part?
A. Because it is classified as a vow between him and her (beinav le-veinah)
B. Because it is classified as a vow of affliction (innui nefesh)
C. Because the husband has no power to nullify vows at all
D. Because the vow was made without proper intention
Correct: A — The Gemara proves that since nullification doesn't extend to all men, this cannot be an affliction vow. It must be 'beinav le-veinah' — a matter between husband and wife — so he nullifies only regarding himself.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Shmuel (citing Levi), which vow can a husband NOT nullify?
A. 'Benefit from so-and-so is konam for me'
B. 'Benefit from me is konam for so-and-so'
C. 'I will not eat bread today'
D. 'The produce of this country is konam for me' when no alternative exists
Correct: B — When a wife vows that HER benefit is forbidden to a third party ('benefit from me is konam for so-and-so'), this is entirely between her and that person — the husband has no standing to nullify it.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say the mishna about 'produce of this storekeeper' follows R' Yosei rather than the Rabbis?
A. Because Rav Huna established that the entire chapter follows R' Yosei
B. Because R' Yosei is explicitly named as the author of that mishna
C. Because the Rabbis don't discuss storekeeper vows at all
D. Because only R' Yosei permits nullification of storekeeper vows
Correct: A — Rav Huna stated that 'our entire chapter is in accordance with R' Yosei.' The Gemara resolves the contradiction with Shmuel by noting that the mishna reflects R' Yosei's stricter view, while Shmuel follows the Rabbis' broader definition of affliction.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the 'two loaves' dispute, what does R' Yochanan hold?
A. The husband can nullify both loaves since they are part of one vow
B. The husband can nullify only the fine loaf (whose abstention causes deprivation), not the poor loaf
C. The husband cannot nullify either loaf because food vows are not affliction
D. The husband must nullify both loaves or neither — partial nullification is impossible
Correct: B — R' Yochanan holds that each component of the vow is assessed independently. Only the fine-quality loaf, whose abstention constitutes actual deprivation, qualifies as affliction that the husband can nullify.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rav Yosef initially resolve the objection to Shmuel from the mishna about 'produce of this country'?
A. He says the mishna refers to a case where she said 'that YOU bring' — limiting only what the husband brings
B. He says the mishna is discussing a different type of vow entirely
C. He says Shmuel retracted his statement
D. He says the mishna follows a rejected minority opinion
Correct: A — Rav Yosef explains that the woman specified 'that you bring' — she only forbade produce her husband brings from that country. Since she can still obtain it herself or from others, there is no affliction, and the husband cannot nullify. This resolution is ultimately rejected.
