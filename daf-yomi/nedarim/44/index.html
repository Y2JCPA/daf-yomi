When Does Hefker (Ownerless Status) Take Effect?

Baraita
One who declares a field ownerless for a day, week, month, year, or shemitah cycle — as long as no one took possession, he may retract.
Gemara
The first clause follows the Rabbis (hefker is immediate), but the latter clause follows R' Yosei (hefker requires acquisition). A contradiction!
Ulla
The entire baraita follows the Rabbis. Declaring hefker for a limited period is uncommon, so the owner retains certain rights and may retract.
Reish Lakish
The entire baraita follows R' Yosei. The three-day rule is a rabbinic ordinance so people won't forget that hefker is real once someone acquires the property.
Core Issue The Gemara resolves an internal contradiction in the baraita about retracting hefker, with Ulla and Reish Lakish offering fundamentally different frameworks.

Ulla vs. Reish Lakish: Who Taught the Baraita?

Ulla
Both clauses follow the Rabbis. Hefker takes effect immediately by Torah law, but declaring hefker for a limited time is unusual — the owner clearly reserved rights, so he may retract.
Reish Lakish
Both clauses follow R' Yosei. Hefker only works when someone acquires. The three-day limit on retraction is a rabbinic ordinance so people don't forget hefker can be permanent.
Gemara
To Reish Lakish: Why not make hefker irrevocable from day one?
Rabba
Because of swindlers (rama'im) who declare hefker to dodge tithing, then immediately reclaim the field!
Rabba's Concern Swindlers would exploit immediate hefker to avoid ma'aser obligations, so the Sages required a three-day waiting period.

Rabbis vs. Rabbi Yosei on Hefker

The Rabbis (Chachamim)

  • Hefker takes effect immediately upon declaration
  • No one needs to acquire the property
  • Owner loses rights as soon as he declares
  • Limited-duration hefker is unusual — owner retains some rights

Rabbi Yosei

  • Hefker only takes effect when someone acquires the property
  • Until acquisition, owner can retract
  • Three-day irrevocability is a rabbinic safeguard
  • Prevents people from thinking hefker is always reversible
Practical Difference According to the Rabbis, hefker exempts produce from tithing immediately. According to R' Yosei, tithing obligations persist until someone else acquires the property.

The Swindler Problem & the Three-Day Solution

🧑‍🌾 Owner declares field hefker to exempt produce from ma'aser (tithes)
↓
🔄 Immediately retracts and reclaims the field
↓
❌ Result: Produce never truly tithed — a sham!
↓
📜 Sages' fix: Hefker doesn't fully take effect until three days pass
↓
⏳ Within three days: owner may retract (no hefker benefit)
↓
✅ After three days: hefker is irrevocable — no gaming the system
Tithing Safeguard If retracted after three days, the person must tithe from that produce itself to avoid mixing Torah-obligated and exempt produce.
