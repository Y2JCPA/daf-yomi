Nedarim 25 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, when someone takes an oath referring to 'those who left Egypt,' whose understanding determines the meaning?
A. The swearer's private understanding
B. The understanding of an ordinary person
C. The understanding of a Torah scholar
D. The literal biblical number of 600,000
Correct: B — Rav Ashi holds that oaths are interpreted according to the understanding of an ordinary person (da'ateinu), not the swearer's private intent. We assume he means people, not ants (shumshemanei).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the story of Rava's hollow cane, how did the borrower technically fulfill his oath?
A. He had a friend pay the debt secretly
B. He hid the money inside a cane and handed it to the creditor before swearing
C. He paid with counterfeit coins
D. He swore on a different Torah scroll that wasn't valid
Correct: B — The borrower placed the owed coins inside a hollow cane, gave the cane to the creditor to hold, and then swore he had returned everything 'in his possession' — technically true at that moment, but deeply deceptive.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why couldn't Moses simply make Israel swear to 'fulfill the mitzvot'?
A. The word mitzvot didn't exist yet
B. They might interpret 'mitzvot' as commandments of a human king
C. The oath would only cover positive commandments
D. Mitzvot only refers to rabbinical obligations
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that 'mitzvot' could be interpreted as commandments of a king (melekh), not necessarily God's commandments, leaving a loophole in the oath.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the practical difference between Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel regarding the man who forbade figs to people eating in his courtyard?
A. Beit Shammai permits everyone; Beit Hillel prohibits everyone
B. Beit Shammai permits only his father; Beit Hillel permits his father and brother
C. Beit Shammai permits family but prohibits others; Beit Hillel permits everyone
D. Both agree everyone is prohibited but disagree about the penalty
Correct: C — Beit Shammai says the father and brother (whom he didn't intend to prohibit) are permitted, but the others remain prohibited. Beit Hillel says everyone is permitted, because a partially dissolved vow is entirely dissolved.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The case of Rav Kahana and Rav Asi illustrates which concept?
A. Vows of exaggeration (nidrei havai)
B. Oaths taken under duress (ones)
C. Unintentional oaths (shvuot shoggin)
D. Oaths that follow court understanding
Correct: C — Each swore sincerely that Rav held like his opinion. Since each genuinely believed he was correct, the one who was wrong took an unintentional oath (shvuat shoggin), which is dissolved like an unintentional vow.
