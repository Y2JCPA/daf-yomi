When Does Uncertain Consecration Take Effect?

A person makes a conditional vow or uses an unspecified term of consecration
↓
Is the uncertainty about sanctity that emerges by itself (e.g. firstborn) or by human volition (e.g. nazir vow)?
By Itself (Firstborn)
Stringent — shearing/working forbidden despite monetary leniency
By Human Volition
Person does not intend to enter uncertainty → lenient (R' Zeira)
Core Issue The Gemara resolves conflicting mishnayot on whether uncertain consecration is treated leniently or stringently, exploring multiple resolutions across Amud A and B.

R' Zeira's Distinction: Types of Sanctity

Abaye
You said uncertain naziriteship is lenient per R' Eliezer. But uncertain firstborns are forbidden to shear and work — that's stringent despite uncertainty!
R' Zeira
Don't compare קדושה הבאה מאליה (sanctity by itself, like a firstborn) to קדושה הבאה בידי אדם (sanctity by human volition). A person doesn't intend to consecrate in doubt.
Abaye
But what about uncertain liquid impurity? The same mishna says it's treated as impure — and that's not about human consecration!
R' Zeira
That works according to Shmuel, who holds liquids are susceptible to impurity by Torah law...
Gemara
But according to Rav, liquids are not susceptible to impurity by Torah law at all — R' Zeira's answer collapses.
Result R' Zeira's resolution is rejected according to Rav. The Gemara seeks a new approach.

New Resolution: R' Yehuda vs R' Shimon

Rabbi Yehuda — Lenient

  • "If there are 100 kor in this heap, I am a nazir"
  • Heap was stolen or lost — cannot verify
  • Person is NOT a nazirite
  • One does not enter himself into uncertainty
  • This explains the lenient mishna in Teharot

Rabbi Shimon — Stringent

  • Same case: conditional vow on heap's volume
  • Heap was stolen or lost — cannot verify
  • Person IS a nazirite
  • One does enter himself into uncertainty
  • This explains the stringent mishna here in Nedarim
Resolution The two contradictory mishnayot reflect two different Tannaitic opinions: Teharot follows R' Yehuda, Nedarim follows R' Shimon.

Rava's Principle: Why R' Yehuda Is Lenient

R' Yehuda says one does not enter uncertainty. Why?
↓
Because uncertain naziriteship is MORE stringent than definite naziriteship!
↓
How is uncertainty worse?
Definite Nazir
Has a remedy: shave hair, bring offerings, naziriteship ends
Uncertain Nazir
No remedy: cannot bring offerings (might be חולין בעזרה), therefore cannot shave
Rava's Rule Wherever uncertainty is MORE stringent than certainty, a person does not enter himself into that state of uncertainty.

Rav Huna Challenges Rava: Permanent & Samson Nazirites

Rav Huna bar Yehuda
What if he said: "I am a permanent nazir" if the heap has 100 kor? The naziriteship never ends either way — uncertainty isn't more stringent!
Rava
Even a permanent nazir can lighten heavy hair with a razor and bring offerings. An uncertain permanent nazir cannot — so uncertainty is still stricter.
Rav Huna bar Yehuda
What about a נזיר שמשון — nazirite like Samson? He could never shave, even with certainty. Now uncertainty equals certainty!
Rava
The concept of נזיר שמשון is not taught — it was a one-time divine order, not replicable through vows.
Rav Huna bar Yehuda
But Rav Adda bar Ahava says it IS taught in a baraita!
Rava
If it is taught, it is taught. I cannot dispute a baraita.
Concession Rava concedes that for a nazir-like-Samson, R' Yehuda would agree uncertain naziriteship takes effect, since uncertainty is not more stringent.
7 / 9

Rav Ashi's Alternative Resolution

נזירות אינה חלה אלא בהפלאה
Key Insight Rav Ashi offers a different resolution: R' Yehuda's lenient ruling on uncertain naziriteship is actually R' Yehuda in the name of R' Tarfon, who holds that naziriteship takes effect only through explicitness of intent (הפלאה). A conditional vow is inherently lacking in explicit intent, so it never takes effect — regardless of whether uncertainty is more or less stringent.
