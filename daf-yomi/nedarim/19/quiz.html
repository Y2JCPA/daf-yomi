QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does R' Zeira distinguish between the sanctity of a firstborn and the sanctity of a nazir vow?
A. Firstborn sanctity is more severe than nazir sanctity
B. Firstborn sanctity arises automatically, while nazir sanctity depends on human intent — and a person doesn't intend to consecrate in doubt
C. Firstborn sanctity is only rabbinic while nazir sanctity is biblical
D. The firstborn belongs to the priest while the nazir vow affects only the individual
Correct: B — R' Zeira distinguishes between קדושה הבאה מאליה (sanctity that emerges by itself, like a firstborn) and קדושה הבאה בידי אדם (sanctity by human volition). Only with voluntary sanctity can we say a person doesn't intend to consecrate uncertainly.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava, why does R' Yehuda rule that a conditional nazirite vow does not take effect?
A. Because all conditional vows are invalid by Torah law
B. Because the heap was stolen and the condition can never be fulfilled
C. Because uncertain naziriteship is MORE stringent than definite naziriteship — the uncertain nazir has no remedy
D. Because R' Yehuda always treats doubtful prohibitions leniently
Correct: C — Rava explains that a definite nazir can shave and bring offerings to end his naziriteship, but an uncertain nazir cannot bring offerings (lest they be non-sacred animals in the Temple) and therefore can never shave. Since uncertainty is stricter, a person does not enter himself into it.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rava initially claim the concept of a 'nazirite like Samson' (נזיר שמשון) does not apply?
A. Because Samson violated his naziriteship and it was annulled
B. Because Samson's naziriteship was a one-time divine order that cannot be replicated through human vows
C. Because the Torah never explicitly calls Samson a nazirite
D. Because permanent naziriteship already covers Samson's case
Correct: B — Rava argues that Samson's naziriteship was a unique divine decree, not something that can be created through a human vow, so the category of 'nazir like Samson' doesn't exist. However, he concedes when shown a baraita that does teach this concept.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the dispute between Shmuel and Rav regarding the impurity of liquids that affects R' Zeira's resolution?
A. Shmuel says liquids are impure by Torah law; Rav says they are not susceptible to impurity by Torah law at all
B. Shmuel says all liquids are pure; Rav says they are impure
C. They disagree about whether liquids in the Temple are different from ordinary liquids
D. Shmuel says impurity of liquids is rabbinic; Rav says it is biblical
Correct: A — According to Shmuel, liquids are susceptible to impurity by Torah law (only their ability to render other items impure is rabbinic), which supports R' Zeira. According to Rav, liquids are not susceptible to impurity by Torah law at all, which undermines R' Zeira's claim that the mishna follows R' Eliezer.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rav Ashi resolve the contradiction within R' Yehuda's statements about uncertain naziriteship?
A. He says R' Yehuda changed his mind over time
B. He says the lenient ruling is actually R' Yehuda quoting R' Tarfon, who holds naziriteship requires explicit, unconditional intent (הפלאה)
C. He says the stringent ruling is a later interpolation
D. He says R' Yehuda distinguishes between Judea and Galilee for naziriteship as well
Correct: B — Rav Ashi explains that the lenient baraita is R' Yehuda in the name of R' Tarfon, who holds that naziriteship only takes effect through הפלאה — explicit, unconditional intent. A conditional vow inherently lacks this explicitness, so it never takes effect regardless of the stringency analysis.
