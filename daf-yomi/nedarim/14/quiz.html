Nedarim 14 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does associating an item with pig meat NOT create a valid vow?
A. Because pig meat is too repulsive to be compared to
B. Because the Torah requires vows to associate with items forbidden by vow, not by Torah law
C. Because pig meat is not technically forbidden — only eating it is
D. Because the rabbis decreed that animal-based vows are invalid
Correct: B — Numbers 30:3 ('a vow to the Lord') teaches that a vow must associate an item with something forbidden by means of a vow (neder), not with something inherently forbidden by Torah law.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava, why does the mishna require dissolution for 'You are like my mother' while the baraita says 'he has said nothing'?
A. The mishna is stricter because it was composed later
B. The mishna refers to an ignoramus who needs rabbinic guidance; the baraita refers to Torah scholars
C. The mishna deals with wives while the baraita deals with other relatives
D. The baraita was rejected and is not authoritative
Correct: B — Rava distinguishes between a Torah scholar, who knows the vow is void and needs no dissolution, and an ignoramus (am ha'aretz), for whom the rabbis required dissolution to prevent him from taking vows lightly.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When does vowing 'by a Torah scroll' take effect?
A. Whenever one touches the scroll during the vow
B. Only when one specifies 'what is written in it,' invoking God's name
C. Only when three witnesses are present
D. Never — a Torah scroll cannot be used for vows
Correct: B — Vowing by the physical Torah scroll alone has no effect. But mentioning 'what is written in it' invokes the names of God contained in the scroll, which makes the vow binding.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
A man says: 'Sleeping is konam for my eyes today if I sleep tomorrow.' According to Rav, may he sleep today?
A. Yes — the prohibition only applies tomorrow
B. No — lest he sleep tomorrow and retroactively violate today's prohibition
C. Yes — but only if he designates a guardian to keep him awake tomorrow
D. No — because sleeping can never be prohibited by a vow
Correct: B — Rav rules strictly: he may not sleep today because if he sleeps tomorrow, today's sleep would retroactively become a violation of his vow. Rav Naḥman disagrees, trusting the person to be careful.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman, why does holding a Torah scroll in one's hands change the effect of vowing 'by what is written in it'?
A. Holding it constitutes a formal oath ceremony
B. The physical contact transfers sanctity to the vower
C. When holding it, his thoughts naturally focus on God's names written inside
D. Holding the scroll fulfills the biblical requirement of 'raising one's hand'
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman explains that when holding the scroll, the person's thoughts naturally turn to the sacred content — the names of God (azkarot) — written in it. When the scroll is on the ground, he might assume it's blank parchment.
