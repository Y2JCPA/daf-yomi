What Does 'Dagan' Include?

R' Meir
Dagan includes anything placed in a pile after harvest — including dry cowpea (pol ha-mitzri)
Rabbis
Dagan means only the five species of grain: wheat, barley, oats, spelt, and rye
R' Meir
I distinguish: Tevua = only 5 species, but Dagan = all produce that is midgan (piled)
Rav Yosef
But the verse in II Chronicles lists both dagan and tevua — if dagan means everything piled, why add tevua?
Abaye
Tevua in that verse comes to include fruits and vegetables, which are not harvested and piled like grain
CONSENSUS All agree that one who vows 'tevua' is forbidden only the five species of grain.

Produce of the landAll produce from the groundTruffles & mushrooms

NOTE 'Produce' (tevuat) is narrower than 'growths' (gidulei) — the broader term always captures more.

Truffles & Mushrooms: Earth or Not?

One vows: 'Produce of the land is forbidden to me'
↓
Are truffles & mushrooms included?
Baraita on vows
They are permitted — not 'produce of the land'
Berakhot 40b
Blessing is shehakol — 'not from the ground'
↓
Abaye's resolution: They grow from the earth but draw sustenance from the air
↓
Emend the mishna: Blessing is for items that don't draw sustenance from the ground
HALACHA If he said 'growths of the ground' — truffles and mushrooms ARE forbidden, since they do grow from earth.

Garment Vows: What Is Forbidden?

Forbidden (= 'Garments')

  • Standard woolen & linen clothing
  • Money belt (punda)
  • Sash (pesakiyya)
  • Tanner's apron (sekurtiyya)
  • Leather spread (katavliyya)
  • Leather sock (anpilya)
  • Trousers and hat

Permitted (≠ 'Garments')

  • Sackcloth
  • Sheets
  • Coarse curtain (ḥamila)
  • Wool fleece (raw, unwoven)
  • Uncombed flax bundles
SHABBAT CONNECTION One may go out on Shabbat in sackcloth, sagos, or curtain (they count as garments for Shabbat) — but not in a box, basket, or mat (those are burdens).
