Nedarim 8: Motivational Oaths & Dream Excommunication

נשבעתי ואקיימה לשמור משפטי צדקך (תהלים קיט:קו)
CORE THEME This daf explores when additional oaths can take effect atop existing obligations, how dream excommunication must be dissolved, the role of a husband as agent for his wife's vow, and a dramatic teaching about the sun of righteousness in the World-to-Come.

Can One Swear to Fulfill a Mitzva?

Gemara
From where do we derive that one may take an oath to fulfill a mitzva? From: "I have sworn and confirmed it, to observe Your righteous ordinances" (Psalms 119:106)
Objection
But every Jew is already under oath from Sinai! An oath cannot take effect upon a preexisting oath (שבועה אין חלה על שבועה)!
Resolution
It teaches that it is permitted to motivate oneself (לזרז את עצמו) to fulfill mitzvot through an oath, even though the oath is not technically valid.
PRINCIPLE A self-imposed oath to do what one is already obligated to do serves as a motivational tool, not a new legal obligation.

Why Does Rav Giddel's Torah Study Oath Take Effect?

Rav Giddel / Rav: One who says "I will rise early and study this chapter" has taken a great vow to the God of Israel
↓
Challenge: He is already obligated to study Torah from Sinai — an oath can't take effect on a preexisting oath!
↓
Answer: Since one can technically fulfill Torah study with just Shema morning and evening...
↓
...the vow to study a specific chapter or tractate goes beyond the baseline obligation — so the oath takes effect!
KEY INSIGHT The minimal obligation of Torah study can be fulfilled with Kri'at Shema alone. Therefore a vow to study more is a new commitment upon which an oath can take effect.

Dissolving Dream Excommunication (Rav Yosef)

🌙 One who is excommunicated in a dream (נידוי בחלום) requires 10 people to dissolve it
↓
📚 Ideally: 10 people who have studied halakha (Torah scholars)
↓
📖 If unavailable: 10 who have recited Mishna (even without halakhic study)
↓
🚶 If even that is unavailable: Sit at a crossroads and greet people with shalom until 10 halakha scholars pass by
↓
⚠️ Even if the dreamer knows who excommunicated him, that person cannot dissolve it alone — he may have been Heaven's agent only to impose, not to release
RAV ASHI'S RULING If the excommunication was both imposed and dissolved in a dream, it still must be dissolved by 10 people — "just as grain cannot grow without straw, a dream cannot occur without idle matter."

Three Halakhot from Ravina's Wife's Vow

HalakhaSource in the Incident
A husband can serve as agent to express his wife's regret to a courtRavina came before Rav Ashi to express his wife's regret
A Sage may not dissolve a vow in the location of his teacherRavina, himself a great Sage, deferred to Rav Ashi
The husband may serve as agent only if 3 judges are already assembledRav Ashi's condition: if assembled — yes; if not yet — no
Excommunication may be dissolved even in one's teacher's localeDistinguished from vow dissolution
An expert individual Sage can dissolve excommunication aloneWithout needing two additional judges
DISTINCTION Vow dissolution requires deference to one's teacher and 3 assembled judges. Excommunication dissolution has more lenient rules.

The Sun of Righteousness — Malachi 3:19–20

Reish Lakish

  • There is no Gehenna in the World-to-Come
  • God removes the sun from its sheath (מנרתיקה)
  • The righteous are healed by it and rejuvenated — "leaping as calves"
  • The wicked are punished by the same sun — "it burns as a furnace"
  • Refers to the World-to-Come

Abaye

  • The dust illuminated by sunbeams (חירגא) during the day has healing power
  • Derives from "with healing in its wings" — the sun's rays
  • Refers to healing in this world
  • Disagrees with Reish Lakish's eschatological reading
VERSE "But to you that fear My name shall the sun of righteousness arise with healing in its wings" — Malachi 3:20
