Nedarim 41: Illness, Intelligence & Visiting the Sick

מי שנפל חולי ועמד — מוחלין לו על כל עוונותיו
DAF OVERVIEW This daf explores the spiritual dimensions of illness — from the forgiveness of sins upon recovery, to the devastating loss of Torah knowledge during sickness, to the laws of bikur cholim. It then returns to vow law: what healing and shared activities are permitted when one is forbidden from benefiting another.
3 / 9

"In Want of All Things" — What Is True Poverty?

Rav (via R' Ami)
"In want of all things" means without a lamp and without a table.
Rav Ḥisda
It means without a wife.
Rav Sheshet
It means without an attendant.
Rav Naḥman
It means without intelligence (דעת).
Abaye
We have a tradition: A poor person is only one lacking in intelligence — supporting Rav Naḥman.
Eretz Yisrael Saying
One who has דעת has everything. One who lacks it — what has he acquired?
KEY PRINCIPLE Multiple Amoraim interpret Deut. 28:48's curse of exile differently, but the conclusion elevates intelligence (דעת) as the most essential possession.

Spiritual Dimensions of Illness & Recovery

🤒 A person falls ill
↓
📜 Heaven reviews all his sins
↓
✨ Upon recovery — all sins are forgiven (R' Alexandri / R' Ḥiyya bar Abba, from Psalms 103:3)
↓
👶 He returns to the days of his youth (Rav Hamnuna, from Job 33:25)
↓
🧠 But illness causes forgetting Torah — 'all his lying down is overturned' (Rav Yosef, Psalms 41:4)
INSIGHT Recovery brings spiritual renewal but intellectual loss — forgiveness of sin comes at the cost of forgotten learning.

Rav Yosef & Rebbi — Torah Lost and Restored Through Illness

Rav Yosef falls ill and forgets his Torah
↓
Abaye restores his learning by reviewing what Rav Yosef had originally taught him
↓
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi knew 13 aspects; taught 7 to R' Ḥiyya
↓
Rebbi falls ill — forgets all 13
↓
7 Restored
R' Ḥiyya reviews the 7 he was taught
6 Lost?
A launderer (כובס) who overheard Rebbi restores the remaining 6 via R' Ḥiyya
TAKEAWAY Rebbi said to the launderer: "You made me and Ḥiyya" — even an ordinary person who preserves Torah can save the greatest scholars.

Heavenly Fire vs. Earthly Fire

Hananiah, Mishael & Azariah

  • Fire of a furnace — man-made
  • Anyone is capable of extinguishing it
  • A great miracle — but from earthly fire

A Sick Person with Fever

  • Fire of Heaven — divine origin
  • No human can extinguish it
  • Recovery is a GREATER miracle than the furnace
  • R' Alexandri / R' Ḥiyya bar Abba
CHIDDUSH The miracle of healing from fever surpasses the miracle of the fiery furnace — because heavenly fire cannot be fought by human means.

Visiting the Sick — When Is Bikur Cholim Appropriate?

IllnessVisit?Reason
Fever (חולה שאחזו חמה)✅ YesShmuel: Bikur cholim applies to fever patients
Intestinal illness (חולי מעיים)❌ NoEmbarrassment — frequent need to relieve oneself
Eye illness❌ NoSpeech is harmful to the patient (Amud B)
Headaches❌ NoSpeech is harmful to the patient
Buredam (בורדם)❌ NoLike a 'flowing spring' — severe embarrassment (R' Yoḥanan)
SOURCE R' Yosei ben Perata in the name of R' Eliezer excludes intestinal illness, eye illness, and headaches from bikur cholim obligations.
