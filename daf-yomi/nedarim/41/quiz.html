Nedarim 41 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman, what does 'in want of all things' (Deut. 28:48) refer to?
A. Without a lamp and table
B. Without a wife
C. Without intelligence (דעת)
D. Without salt and fat
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman interprets 'want of all things' as lacking intelligence. Abaye confirms this tradition, and the sages of Eretz Yisrael say: one who has דעת has everything.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How did Abaye restore Rav Yosef's forgotten Torah learning?
A. He brought Rav Yosef new teachings from other academies
B. He reviewed before Rav Yosef the teachings that Rav Yosef had originally taught him
C. He read to Rav Yosef from written scrolls
D. He asked the launderer to teach Rav Yosef
Correct: B — Rav Yosef forgot his learning after illness. Abaye restored it by reviewing what he had learned from Rav Yosef, leading to the recurring Talmudic formula where Abaye reminds Rav Yosef of his own teachings.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does R' Alexandri say the miracle of healing from fever is greater than the rescue from the fiery furnace?
A. Because fever lasts longer than the furnace
B. Because fever is heavenly fire that no human can extinguish, while the furnace was man-made fire
C. Because more people suffer from fever than were in the furnace
D. Because fever requires prayer while the furnace required only faith
Correct: B — The furnace was earthly fire that anyone could theoretically extinguish. Fever is fire of Heaven — recovery from it is a greater miracle because no human intervention can put out divine fire.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the Mishna, what does 'cure of mamon' refer to, according to the Gemara's conclusion?
A. Healing for a fee
B. Healing his animal/property
C. Healing with expensive medicine
D. Healing that increases his wealth
Correct: B — The Gemara rejects the interpretation of 'free vs. for a fee' and concludes that 'cure of nefesh' means healing the person's body, while 'cure of mamon' means healing his animal — which is his property.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why do R' Meir and the Rabbis disagree about working in the same vineyard row at a distance?
A. They disagree about whether vineyard work is considered 'benefit'
B. R' Meir issues a decree lest they come to work closely and loosen earth for each other; the Rabbis do not issue such a decree
C. R' Meir considers all vineyard work forbidden under vows; the Rabbis permit agricultural work
D. They disagree about the definition of 'distance'
Correct: B — Both agree that working in close proximity is prohibited (as one loosens the earth for the other). The dispute is whether to decree against distant work as a precaution — R' Meir says yes (gezeirah), the Rabbis say no.
