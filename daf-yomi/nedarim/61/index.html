"For a Year" vs. "This Year" in Leap Years

Questioner
If the mishna means exactly 'this year,' why state the halakha? It's obvious that 'this year' includes a leap month!
Questioner
Rather, he said 'for a year' — and the mishna teaches the remainder of that year applies. So 'for a year' = 'this year.'
Gemara
No. He indeed said this year. The chiddush is: don't follow the majority of years (non-leap) to limit the vow to 12 months.
Gemara
The tanna teaches: 'this year' means until the actual end of the year — even if it has 13 months.
KEY POINT 'This year' follows the actual calendar year, not a standard 12-month assumption.

The Jubilee Year Dilemma

One vows: 'Wine is forbidden to me for a Jubilee cycle'
↓
Is the 50th year (Jubilee) included in the vow?
↓
Rabbanan
The 50th year is the end of the previous cycle → Included in the vow
R' Yehuda
The 50th year begins the next cycle → Not included in the vow
SOURCE Leviticus 25:10 — 'You shall sanctify the 50th year.' The Rabbanan derive: count it as the 50th, but NOT as the 1st of the next cycle.

Rabbanan vs. R' Yehuda — Jubilee & Shemittah Counting

Rabbanan (Sages)

  • Jubilee Year is NOT counted in the next 7-year cycle
  • Year after Jubilee = Year 1 of new cycle
  • Support: 'Six years you shall sow' — need full 6 working years
  • Jubilee closes out the old cycle

Rabbi Yehuda

  • Jubilee Year IS counted as Year 1 of the next cycle
  • Only 5 sowing years in the cycle after Jubilee
  • Counter: 'Produce for 3 years' — but Jubilee needs 4!
  • Both sides can limit their verse to other cycles
STANDOFF Each side challenges the other with a verse, and each deflects by limiting the verse to non-adjacent Shemittah cycles.

"Until Before Passover" — Reversed Attributions

Gemara
Our mishna: R' Meir says 'until before Passover' = until it arrives. R' Yosei = until it ends.
Gemara
But in Kiddushin 64b, R' Meir accounts for all uncertain meanings, while R' Yosei takes only the narrowest meaning!
R' Chanina bar Avdimi / Rav
The attributions must be reversed. A baraita confirms: R' Meir = until it ends; R' Yosei = until it arrives.
Gemara
This aligns: R' Meir always considers the broader uncertain range; R' Yosei always takes the narrow reading.
PRINCIPLE R' Meir: a person does NOT place himself in uncertainty (so 'before Passover' extends through the end). R' Yosei: a person DOES accept uncertainty.

Mishna: Fixed vs. Unfixed Time-Bound Vows

Language UsedFixed Occasion (e.g., Passover)Unfixed Occasion (e.g., Harvest)
"Until it arrives"Forbidden until the occasion arrivesForbidden until it arrives
"Until it will be"Forbidden until the occasion endsForbidden until it arrives (same!)
"Until it passes"Forbidden until the occasion endsForbidden until it ends
KEY DISTINCTION For unfixed occasions (harvests), 'until it will be' and 'until it arrives' are equivalent — because the exact timing is uncertain, we use the earliest possible endpoint.
