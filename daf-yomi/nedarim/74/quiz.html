QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Akiva, why can a yavam NEVER nullify a yevama's vows?
A. Because the zikah (levirate bond) is not substantial enough to create a marital relationship
B. Because the Torah explicitly forbids it
C. Because only a father can nullify an unmarried woman's vows
D. Because the yevama has already been widowed once
Correct: A — R' Akiva holds that the zikah is not substantial — the yevama is not the yavam's full-fledged wife, so he has no authority over her vows until actual levirate marriage is consummated.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rav Ami explain R' Eliezer's position that a yavam can nullify vows even with two yevamin?
A. The case involves a yavam who already performed ma'amar (levirate betrothal), and R' Eliezer follows Beit Shammai that ma'amar = full betrothal
B. R' Eliezer holds that both yevamin jointly nullify the vows
C. The case is where one yavam already performed chalitzah
D. R' Eliezer derives it from a gezeirah shavah
Correct: A — Rav Ami explains the Mishna deals with a case where one yavam performed ma'amar. R' Eliezer follows Beit Shammai that ma'amar effects a full acquisition, giving him vow-nullification authority.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is R' Akiva's refutation of R' Eliezer's kal vachomer argument?
A. A betrothed woman has no one else with authority over her, but a yevama's other yevamin share authority — so the cases are not comparable
B. The Torah never granted yevamin any marital rights
C. Vow nullification requires a kesubah, which a yevama lacks
D. A yevama can refuse the marriage, unlike a betrothed woman
Correct: A — R' Akiva breaks the kal vachomer: with a betrothed woman, no others share authority. With a yevama, brother-yevamin also have a share — this critical difference invalidates the comparison.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Gemara, how does R' Yehoshua's logic differ from R' Eliezer's regarding two yevamin?
A. With two yevamin, one brother can undo the other's ma'amar through intercourse or a get — so the betrothal is not secure enough to grant vow-nullification power
B. R' Yehoshua holds that two yevamin must jointly nullify the vows
C. R' Yehoshua requires a beit din ruling before any nullification
D. R' Yehoshua considers zikah non-substantial in all cases
Correct: A — R' Yehoshua argues that with two yevamin, the second brother can nullify the first's ma'amar — betrothal that can be undone by a third party cannot serve as the basis for vow nullification.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the challenge to Rav Ami from R' Elazar's view that ma'amar per Beit Shammai only removes a rival wife?
A. The yavam was obligated by beit din to provide the yevama sustenance, giving him vow authority since her vows are contingent on her supporter's consent
B. R' Elazar actually agrees with Rav Ami's interpretation
C. The Gemara leaves it as an unresolved difficulty (teiku)
D. A special takanah of the Sanhedrin granted yevamin vow-nullification rights
Correct: A — The Gemara explains that the yavam was court-ordered to support the yevama. Per Rav Pinchas citing Rava, a woman's vows are contingent on her supporter's consent — so his obligation to sustain her grants him authority over her vows.
