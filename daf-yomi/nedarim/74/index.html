Can a Yavam Nullify a Yevama's Vows?

R' Eliezer
A yavam can always nullify her vows — whether she awaits one or two yevamin. A woman acquired from Heaven deserves the same authority as one acquired through betrothal.
R' Yehoshua
Only if she waits for one yavam — then the bond is exclusive and substantial. With two, neither has sole authority.
R' Akiva
A yavam can never nullify her vows. The zikah (levirate bond) is not substantial enough — she is not his full-fledged wife.
Core Issue Does the levirate bond (zikah) create enough of a marital connection that the yavam gains the husband's power to nullify vows?

The Three Opinions: Yavam & Vow Nullification

R' Eliezer

  • Yavam CAN nullify vows
  • Applies to one OR two yevamin
  • Woman 'acquired from Heaven' = woman acquired through betrothal
  • Holds zikah IS substantial

R' Yehoshua

  • Yavam CAN nullify — only with ONE yavam
  • With two yevamin — CANNOT nullify
  • Zikah is substantial only when exclusive
  • Shared authority = no authority

R' Akiva

  • Yavam can NEVER nullify vows
  • Applies to one OR two yevamin
  • Zikah is NOT substantial
  • Yevama ≠ full-fledged wife
Key Term Zikah (זיקה) — the levirate bond connecting a yevama to her yavam(in) before actual levirate marriage takes place.

R' Eliezer vs. R' Akiva: The Kal VaChomer

R' Eliezer
A man nullifies vows of a woman he acquired for himself through betrothal. Kal vachomer — a woman acquired for him from Heaven (yevama) should be the same!
R' Akiva
No! A betrothed woman — others have no authority over her. But a yevama — others (brother-yevamin) share authority. The cases are not comparable.
R' Yehoshua
Akiva, your refutation works for two yevamin. But what about one yavam — where no one else shares authority?
R' Akiva
A yevama is simply not a full-fledged wife. The yavam lacks the power to nullify vows regardless of numbers.
Logical Structure R' Akiva refutes the kal vachomer by showing the cases differ in a critical feature: exclusivity of authority.

Rav Ami's Interpretation of the Dispute

What is the case in the Mishna?
Rav Ami: Where one yavam already performed ma'amar (levirate betrothal)
↓
R' Eliezer (like Beit Shammai)
Ma'amar effects a full-fledged acquisition — yavam can nullify vows
R' Yehoshua
Ma'amar works only with one yavam — with two, another brother can undo the betrothal
R' Akiva
Zikah is not substantial at all — no nullification until actual levirate marriage
Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel Beit Hillel: ma'amar does not have full force. Beit Shammai: ma'amar = full betrothal. R' Eliezer follows Beit Shammai.

R' Elazar's Challenge to Rav Ami

כל הנודרת — על דעת בעלה היא נודרת
Difficulty R' Elazar holds that ma'amar per Beit Shammai only removes a rival wife (tzarah) from the levirate bond — it does not effect a full acquisition. How then does R' Eliezer allow vow nullification?

Resolution: The yavam was obligated by beit din to provide the yevama sustenance. Since he must support her, he gains authority over her vows — as Rav Pinchas in the name of Rava taught: a woman's vows are contingent upon her husband's (or supporter's) consent.
