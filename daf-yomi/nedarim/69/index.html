Core Framework: Nullification Mechanics on Daf 69

Father nullifies his portion of a na'arah's vow
↓
Husband dies before hearing the vow
↓
Beit Shammai
Father nullifies only the husband's half — nullification severs (חותך)
Beit Hillel (Halakha)
Father must re-nullify the entire vow — nullification weakens (מיגז גייז)
KEY RULING The halakha follows Beit Hillel: nullification weakens the general force of the vow rather than completely severing one half.

Does Nullification Sever or Weaken?

Beit Shammai
Whoever nullifies first completely severs (חותך) his half. The father only needs to nullify the husband's remaining portion.
Beit Hillel
Nullification merely weakens (מיגז גייז) the overall force of the vow. The father must re-nullify the entire vow — including his own share.
R' Natan
The ruling that the father nullifies only the husband's portion — that is the view of Beit Shammai.
Gemara
Halakha follows Beit Hillel: the husband's hafarah weakens the general force of the neder.
PRACTICAL DIFFERENCE If nullification severs, the father addresses only what remains. If it weakens, he must address the whole vow anew.

Dissolving Ratification vs. Nullification via a Sage

She'eilah on Ratification (הקמה)

  • ✅ A sage CAN dissolve a husband's ratification
  • This allows the husband to then nullify the vow
  • R' Yochanan rules explicitly: שאלה מועילה בהקמה

She'eilah on Nullification (הפרה)

  • ❌ A sage CANNOT dissolve a husband's nullification
  • Once nullified, a vow cannot be 're-activated'
  • R' Yochanan: אין שאלה בהפרה — no request is possible
R' YOCHANAN'S RULE Ratification can be undone through a halakhic authority; nullification cannot. This asymmetry reflects the finality of hafarah.

Double Ratification: What If the First Is Dissolved?

Husband says: "מקויים ליכי, מקויים ליכי"
(It is ratified for you, it is ratified for you)
↓
A sage dissolves the first ratification
↓
Does the second ratification take effect?
↓
Rabba's Question
Perhaps the second was meaningless — applied to an already-ratified vow
Resolution via Rava
Like double oaths: if the first is dissolved, the second takes effect
PRINCIPLE Rava's parallel: "I swear I won't eat, I swear I won't eat" — dissolving the first oath activates the second. Same applies to ratification.

Conditional Ratification with Nullification

Rabba
If he says: "It is ratified and nullified for you, and the ratification won't take effect unless the nullification does" — what is the din?
R' Meir
Normally I hold the first statement is determinant — like the substitute case: burnt-offering takes priority over peace-offering.
R' Yosei
If he intended both from the outset, both take effect — since one can't say two things simultaneously, sequential phrasing doesn't defeat intent.
Gemara
Even R' Meir concedes here: when one explicitly conditions the first on the second, the nullification takes effect.
CONSENSUS An explicit condition ("ratification won't take effect unless nullification does") overrides the normal rule that the first statement is determinant.
