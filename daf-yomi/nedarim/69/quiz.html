QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Beit Hillel, when the father nullifies his portion of a na'arah's vow, what happens to the vow?
A. His half is completely severed and removed
B. The general force of the entire vow is weakened
C. The vow is fully nullified without the husband
D. His portion is suspended until the husband acts
Correct: B — Beit Hillel holds that nullification weakens (מיגז גייז) the overall force of the vow rather than severing one half. Therefore, when the father later needs to complete the nullification, he must re-nullify the entire vow.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Yochanan, what can a halakhic authority dissolve regarding a husband's actions on his wife's vow?
A. Both ratification and nullification
B. Neither ratification nor nullification
C. Ratification but not nullification
D. Nullification but not ratification
Correct: C — R' Yochanan rules that a sage may be requested to dissolve ratification (הקמה) of a wife's vow, allowing the husband to then nullify it. However, nullification (הפרה) cannot be dissolved — once a vow is nullified, that action is final.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If a husband says 'It is ratified for you' twice and a sage dissolves only the first ratification, what happens?
A. The vow is no longer ratified at all
B. The second ratification takes effect
C. Both ratifications are dissolved automatically
D. The husband must nullify before the day ends
Correct: B — The Gemara resolves this using Rava's parallel of double oaths: just as dissolving the first oath activates the second, dissolving the first ratification causes the second ratification to take effect.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the halakha if the husband says 'It is ratified and nullified simultaneously' (בבת אחת)?
A. The nullification takes effect since it benefits the wife
B. Both take effect and a sage must resolve the conflict
C. The vow remains ratified — simultaneous contradictory actions fail
D. The vow is completely dissolved as if never made
Correct: C — Rabba's principle: any two halakhic statuses that cannot be implemented sequentially cannot be realized simultaneously either. Since one cannot ratify and then nullify, one cannot do both at once — so the ratification (the first word spoken) stands.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does even R' Meir agree that nullification takes effect when the husband explicitly says 'ratification won't apply unless nullification does'?
A. R' Meir always follows the second statement in conditional cases
B. The explicit condition overrides the normal rule that the first statement is determinant
C. R' Meir holds nullification always overrides ratification
D. The Rabbis overruled R' Meir's position in this case
Correct: B — R' Meir normally holds that the first statement is determinant (as in the substitute-offering case). However, when one explicitly conditions the first action on the second, even R' Meir agrees the condition controls — so the nullification takes effect.
