Nedarim 12 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara initially think notar and piggul prove that vows reference an item's original status?
A. Because notar and piggul apply after blood-sprinkling, when me'ilah no longer applies, yet the vow works
B. Because notar and piggul are Torah prohibitions that always apply
C. Because the mishna explicitly states this principle
D. Because notar and piggul only apply before blood-sprinkling
Correct: A — Notar and piggul apply after sprinkling, when the offering's original me'ilah prohibition has ended. If the vow still takes effect, it must reference the original forbidden status.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rav Huna son of Rav Natan deflect the proof from notar and piggul?
A. He argues that notar and piggul never create valid vows
B. He suggests the case involves a burnt-offering (olah), whose meat remains forbidden even after sprinkling
C. He claims the mishna is not authoritative
D. He says the vow references a different type of prohibition entirely
Correct: B — A burnt-offering (olah) may never be eaten, so me'ilah continues to apply to its meat even after blood-sprinkling. The vow could reference the current (still-forbidden) status.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Shmuel, what is required for the 'neder issar' (vow of prohibition referencing a fast day) to take effect?
A. The person must be a kohen
B. The person must have been continuously obligated by a prior vow on that day
C. The fast day must be biblically mandated
D. The person must have fasted on that day the previous year
Correct: B — Shmuel teaches that the neder issar only works if the person was already bound by a vow to fast on the referenced day — either on that one occasion or continuously every year on that date.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara suggest that teruma of the thanks-offering loaves could be separated during kneading?
A. To prove that vows always reference original status
B. To show that the thanks-offering requires exactly four loaves
C. To deflect Ravina's proof — if teruma is separated before sprinkling, the vow references current forbidden status
D. To establish a new halacha about baking procedures
Correct: C — If teruma can be separated during kneading (before blood-sprinkling), then the loaves are already forbidden at that point. A vow made then references the current status, eliminating Ravina's proof about original status.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the dispute about vowing 'like the meat of a firstborn,' what is the likely basis of the disagreement between R' Ya'akov and R' Yehuda?
A. Whether firstborn animals are actually holy
B. Whether a non-kohen can make vows about priestly gifts
C. Whether one references the original forbidden status or the current permitted status of the firstborn after sprinkling
D. Whether the meat of a firstborn is considered notar
Correct: C — The Gemara suggests their dispute parallels the central question of the sugya: R' Ya'akov holds one references the original (forbidden) status and the vow works; R' Yehuda holds one references the current (permitted) status and it doesn't.
