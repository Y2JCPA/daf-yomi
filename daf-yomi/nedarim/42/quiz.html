QUESTION 1 OF 5
During the Sabbatical Year, why may one who is forbidden benefit from another eat produce that overhangs the field?
A. Because overhanging produce is always permitted
B. Because Sabbatical Year produce is ownerless (hefker)
C. Because the Rabbis made a special exception for hunger
D. Because overhanging produce belongs to the public domain
Correct: B — During Shevi'it, all produce is rendered ownerless (hefker), so eating it is not considered deriving benefit from the vow-maker's property.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the Gemara's conclusion, what is the difference between saying 'this property' (נכסים אלו) and 'my property' (נכסי)?
A. "This property" is a stronger vow that cannot be annulled
B. "My property" includes future acquisitions while "this property" does not
C. "This property" attaches the prohibition to the item itself; "my property" ties it to current ownership
D. There is no practical difference between the two formulations
Correct: C — "This property" creates a prohibition on the specific item that persists regardless of ownership changes. "My property" means property currently in my possession — once it leaves possession (e.g., becomes hefker), the prohibition lapses.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara conclude that Rav/Shmuel and R' Yochanan/Reish Lakish do NOT actually disagree?
A. Because one pair retracted their position
B. Because each pair was addressing a different formulation of the vow — one discussed "this property" and the other "my property"
C. Because the Mishna explicitly rules like both of them
D. Because the difference is only relevant during Shevi'it which is rabbinic today
Correct: B — Rav & Shmuel spoke about "this property" (prohibition persists) while R' Yochanan & Reish Lakish spoke about "my property" (prohibition lapses with ownership). They address different cases, so there is no actual dispute.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Shimon ben Elyakim, why may the forbidden party not enter the field during Shevi'it even when trees are in the middle?
A. Because the land is not actually ownerless during Shevi'it
B. Because of a rabbinic decree lest he linger in the field longer than needed for eating
C. Because entering constitutes a form of benefit separate from eating
D. Because R' Shimon ben Elyakim holds Shevi'it is only rabbinic
Correct: B — R' Shimon ben Elyakim explains the prohibition as a rabbinic decree (gezeira): if he enters, he might remain standing in the field longer than necessary for eating, which would constitute forbidden benefit.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What proof does the Gemara bring from Bava Kamma regarding a father's vow against his son?
A. That a father cannot vow against his own children
B. That inheritance overrides all vows
C. That a person can extend a prohibition beyond his possession — but only with explicit language ('during my lifetime and upon my death')
D. That vows between family members are automatically void
Correct: C — If a father says "benefit from my property is konam for you" and dies, the son inherits because it's no longer the father's property. But if he says "during my lifetime and upon my death," the son does not inherit — proving a prohibition can be extended beyond possession with explicit wording.
