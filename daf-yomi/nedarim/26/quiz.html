Nedarim 26 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, in which formulation do Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel disagree?
A. 'All of you are forbidden except father'
B. 'So-and-so and so-and-so are forbidden, father is permitted'
C. 'Father is forbidden and so-and-so is permitted'
D. 'None of you are forbidden'
Correct: A — Rava holds the dispute is specifically when one says 'All of you except father.' When one enumerates individuals ('so-and-so and so-and-so'), all agree everyone is permitted.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the underlying principle behind Beit Hillel's position that all are permitted?
A. A vow taken in error is never valid
B. A vow partially dissolved is dissolved completely (נדר שהותר מקצתו הותר כולו)
C. One cannot take vows against family members
D. The first expression always governs
Correct: B — Beit Hillel follow Rabbi Yosei that the conclusion of one's statement is primary. By excluding father, the vow is partially canceled, and per Rabbi Akiva's principle, a partially dissolved vow is fully dissolved.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In Rava's explanation of the linked chain ('so-and-so like so-and-so'), if the middle person's prohibition is dissolved, what happens?
A. All prohibitions are dissolved
B. Only the middle person is permitted
C. Those after him are permitted; those before him remain forbidden
D. Those before him are permitted; those after him remain forbidden
Correct: C — In a linked chain, dissolving a prohibition breaks the chain downstream. Those mentioned after the middle person (linked to him) are freed, but those mentioned before him retain their independent prohibitions.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the difficulty from Rabbi Meir permitting all onions in the kuferi onion case?
A. Rabbi Meir changed his opinion in this case
B. The case is where he said 'such-and-such onion forbidden, kuferi permitted' — the 'this one and that one' formulation where all agree
C. The onion case is a special exception to the general rule
D. Rabbi Meir was ruling according to Beit Hillel
Correct: B — The Gemara explains the vow-maker used the enumerating formulation ('this onion and that onion are forbidden, kuferi is permitted'), which according to Rava is the case where all opinions — including Rabbi Meir's — agree that everything is permitted.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Rav Pappa's core objection to Rava from Rabbi Akiva's mishna?
A. Rabbi Akiva never said a partially dissolved vow is fully dissolved
B. If all agree in the 'this one and that one' case that all are permitted, who are the Rabbis who disagree with Rabbi Akiva in the latter clause?
C. The mishna contradicts the existence of linked chains
D. Beit Shammai should agree with Beit Hillel according to Rava
Correct: B — Rav Pappa argues: if Rava says all agree that 'this one and that one' formulation permits everyone, then the latter clause of Rabbi Akiva's mishna — where the Rabbis disagree — has no basis, since that clause should also be a case of universal agreement.
