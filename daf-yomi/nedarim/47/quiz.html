Nedarim 47 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
If someone says "Entering YOUR house is konam for me" and the owner sells the house, what happens?
A. The prohibition persists on the house
B. The prohibition is lifted
C. The prohibition transfers to the buyer
D. The vow must be formally annulled
Correct: B — When the vow references 'your house,' it is tied to the owner's relationship with the property. Once the owner sells, the connection is severed and the prohibition is lifted.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What proof does Rava bring to show that one can forbid property beyond one's period of ownership?
A. A case of a wife borrowing money from creditors
B. A case of betrothal with orla fruit money
C. A father who forbids benefit to his son 'in lifetime and after death'
D. A case of communal property in Jerusalem
Correct: C — Rava cites the mishna in Bava Kamma where a father explicitly extends the prohibition beyond his death — and the son indeed cannot inherit, proving the vow's power extends past ownership.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rava reject the proof from the wife who borrows money when her husband vowed against her benefiting from him?
A. Because the vow was improperly formulated
B. Because the wife's case is b'dieved (after the fact) since she has no other support
C. Because creditors are a third party unaffected by vows
D. Because monetary debts override vows entirely
Correct: B — Rava argues that even if replacements are forbidden l'khatchila, the wife's situation is b'dieved — she has no other means of support, so the indirect benefit is permitted out of necessity.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the Mishna's mutual vow formulations, if Reuven says to Shimon 'You are konam to me,' who is forbidden and from what?
A. Reuven is forbidden from Shimon's property
B. Shimon is forbidden from Reuven's property
C. Both are forbidden from each other's property
D. Neither is forbidden — the vow is invalid
Correct: B — 'You are konam to me' means the vow-taker (Reuven) forbids the other person (Shimon) from his own property. Therefore Shimon is forbidden from benefiting from Reuven's possessions.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When both parties are mutually forbidden from each other's property, what type of property may they still both use?
A. Property owned by their families
B. Property belonging to olei Bavel — communal national property
C. Property dedicated to the Temple (hekdesh)
D. Property of the local beit din
Correct: B — The Mishna specifies that even mutually forbidden parties may benefit from property of 'olei Bavel' — communal property belonging to the nation as a whole, which is not considered any individual's possession.
