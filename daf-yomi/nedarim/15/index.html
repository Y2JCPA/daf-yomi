Conditional Vows: Caution or Confidence?

Rav Yehuda
One must not perform the conditional act today — lest he fail to keep the prohibition tomorrow and retroactively violate the vow
Rav Nachman
One may perform the conditional act — a person is careful with a direct prohibition and will not violate it
Gemara
A person is not careful with a mere condition but is careful with a direct prohibition (issur)
Core Dispute If a vow is conditional — 'X is forbidden to me today if I do Y tomorrow' — may one do Y today, triggering the condition that could cause retroactive violation?

Careful with Conditions vs. Prohibitions

Rav Yehuda
'Sleeping is konam for my eyes tomorrow if I sleep today' — he may not sleep today, lest he also sleep tomorrow and retroactively violate lo yachel
Rav Nachman
He may sleep today. Tomorrow's sleep is a direct prohibition — people are careful with direct prohibitions
Gemara
The reverse case — 'konam today if I sleep tomorrow' — all agree he may sleep today, since tomorrow is only a condition, not a prohibition, and people are careful with prohibitions
Key Distinction Condition (תנאי) = people are NOT careful → concern for retroactive violation. Direct prohibition (איסור) = people ARE careful → no concern.

Can a Vow (Neder) Apply to Sleep?

Mishna: 'Sleeping is konam for me' — he may not sleep
↓
But sleep has no substance (אין בו ממש)! Vows only apply to things with substance
↓
Gemara's Answer
He said: 'Sleeping is konam for my eyes' — eyes have substance, so the vow attaches to them
Ravina's Answer
The vow does not take effect by Torah law. Violation of lo yachel is only rabbinic
Oaths vs. Vows Oaths (שבועות) apply to things with AND without substance. Vows (נדרים) apply only to things WITH substance — unless the Rabbis extend the prohibition.

Lo Yachel: Torah Law vs. Rabbinic Law

Torah-Level לא יחל

  • Vow applies to something with substance (דבר שיש בו ממש)
  • Vow is properly formulated with a valid object
  • Full biblical prohibition of 'He shall not profane his word'
  • Violation carries lashes (מלקות)

Rabbinic-Level לא יחל

  • Ravina: Vow on something without substance (sleep, speech)
  • Custom treated as binding — violating custom = like violating a vow
  • Baraita: 'Things permitted but treated as prohibited — you may not permit them'
  • Rabbinic prohibition modeled on lo yachel
Ravina's Innovation The Sages extended lo yachel to cover even vows that don't technically take effect by Torah law — breaking one's word itself violates rabbinic standards.

R' Yochanan: Oath Not to Sleep for Three Days

🗣️ A person swears: 'I will not sleep for three days'
↓
🧠 R' Yochanan rules: This is a שבועת שוא (oath in vain) — it is physically impossible to fulfill
↓
⚡ The court flogs him immediately for taking a vain oath
↓
😴 He may sleep immediately — the oath never took effect
↓
📌 Implication: A vow on sleep must have a time limit, otherwise it's meaningless
R' Yochanan An oath or vow that is physically impossible to keep is void — but the person is still punished for making it.
