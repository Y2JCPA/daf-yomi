Nedarim 79: Silence, Affliction & the Scope of Nullification

Husband hears wife's vow
↓
Silent
Vow is ratified regardless of intent
Explicitly Nullifies
Vow is cancelled — but scope depends on vow type
Ratifies
Vow is permanently locked in — can only seek dissolution from a sage
Core Issue The Gemara refutes R' Chanina's view that silence-to-annoy allows later nullification, then defines which vows qualify for husband's nullification and what happens after divorce.

↓

Ratification vs. Nullification: Asymmetry

Baraita
Silence ratifies a vow, but silence does not nullify a vow.
Baraita
If he ratified in his heart — it is ratified. If he nullified in his heart — it is not nullified.
Gemara
Where is nullification more stringent than ratification?
R' Yochanan
One can request a sage to dissolve a ratification, but one cannot request dissolution of a nullification.
Asymmetry Ratification is easier to create (silence, mental intent) but can be undone by a sage. Nullification requires explicit speech but is irreversible.

Three Refutations of R' Chanina

ObjectorSourceThe Problem for R' Chanina
Rav KahanaNumbers 30:15 — "If he altogether holds his peace"The verse specifically treats silence-to-annoy as ratification, not a basis for later nullification
RavaMishna 76b — Vow on Friday near nightfallIf silence-to-annoy allowed later nullification, a husband who refrained on Shabbat should be able to nullify afterward — but he cannot
Rav AshiMishna 87b — "I didn't know this was a vow"Per R' Meir, even mistaken silence cannot be undone — surely intentional silence-to-annoy cannot either
תיובתא Each objection is called a conclusive refutation (teyuvta) — R' Chanina's opinion is definitively rejected.

Mishna: What Vows Can a Husband Nullify?

First Tanna

  • Vows of affliction (inui)
  • Examples: "If I bathe" / "If I don't bathe"
  • "If I adorn myself" / "If I don't adorn myself"
  • Broad definition of what constitutes affliction

Rabbi Yosei

  • Those examples are not vows of affliction
  • True affliction: "All produce in the world is konam for me"
  • "Produce of this country" — NOT affliction (can get produce elsewhere)
  • "Produce of this storekeeper" — NOT affliction, unless it's his only source
R' Yosei's Standard A vow is only one of affliction if it creates an inescapable deprivation — if an alternative source exists, it's not true affliction.

The Gemara's Resolution: Two Categories of Nullifiable Vows

📖 Verse teaches husband can nullify vows between him and her — even without affliction
↓
❓ But Mishna lists only affliction vows — so what about 'between him and her' vows?
↓
💡 First answer: Both are nullifiable, but affliction nullification survives divorce; relationship vows revive after divorce
↓
🚫 Rejected — R' Yochanan b. Nuri implies nullification is permanent even for non-affliction vows
↓
✅ Final answer: Affliction vows — nullified for himself and all future husbands. Relationship vows — nullified only for himself
Resolution The Mishna lists affliction vows because only those are nullified universally — both for the current husband and for others.
