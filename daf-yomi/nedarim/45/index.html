Hefker, Quorum & Shared Property After Vows

Gemara
Two baraitot contradict each other — one says hefker takes effect immediately, the other says it doesn't leave the owner's possession until someone else acquires it.
Resolution
The baraita where hefker is not immediate refers to a declaration before two people; the one where it is immediate refers to a declaration before three people.
R' Yochanan
In the name of R' Shimon ben Yehotzadak: Hefker before three — valid. Before two — not valid.
R' Yehoshua ben Levi
By Torah law, even one person suffices. The Sages required three so that one acquires and two testify.
🔑 Key Issue Is the three-person requirement for hefker fundamental to the declaration, or merely a practical rabbinic measure to ensure proper testimony?

The Contradiction in Baraitot

הפקר
⚡ Problem One baraita teaches that when an item is declared hefker (ownerless), it does not leave the owner's possession until another person acquires it.

Another baraita teaches the opposite — the item is ownerless immediately upon declaration.

How can both be true?

Why Three? Torah Law vs. Rabbinic Enactment

R' Yochanan
Three people are fundamentally required for a valid hefker declaration. With fewer, it simply doesn't work.
R' Yehoshua ben Levi
By Torah law, even one person is enough for hefker to take effect.
R' Yehoshua ben Levi
The Sages required three for a practical reason: one person acquires the item, and the other two serve as witnesses to testify that it was declared ownerless.
💡 Insight According to R' Yehoshua ben Levi, the three-person requirement is not fundamental to hefker itself — it is a rabbinic safeguard to ensure proper acquisition and testimony.

R' Yochanan vs. R' Yehoshua ben Levi on Hefker

R' Yochanan (quoting R' Shimon ben Yehotzadak)

  • Three people required by law
  • Fewer than three = not valid hefker
  • Quorum is essential to the declaration
  • Item stays with owner if declared before two

R' Yehoshua ben Levi

  • Torah law: even one person suffices
  • Three is a rabbinic requirement only
  • Purpose: 1 acquires + 2 testify
  • Quorum is practical, not fundamental
⚖️ Nafka Mina If someone declared hefker before only one or two people — according to R' Yehoshua ben Levi it may be valid mi-d'Oraita, while R' Yochanan holds it is simply not hefker.

Mishna: Partners Who Vowed Not to Benefit from Each Other

IssueRuling
Partners vowed mudarim from each otherCannot derive benefit from one another
Shared courtyard (ḥatzer)Each has a portion and benefits from the other's share
Tanna Kamma's rulingProhibited — entering the courtyard benefits from the other's portion
R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov's rulingPermitted — each enters only their own portion
🏠 Context The dispute hinges on whether entering a shared courtyard constitutes benefiting from the other partner's property, or whether each can be seen as using only their own share.
