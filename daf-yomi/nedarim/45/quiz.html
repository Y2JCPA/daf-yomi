Nedarim 45 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara's resolution, why do the two baraitot about hefker not contradict each other?
A. One refers to hefker before 2 people, the other before 3 people
B. One refers to movable property, the other to land
C. One is the opinion of Beit Shammai, the other Beit Hillel
D. One refers to hefker of consecrated items, the other to ordinary items
Correct: A — The Gemara resolves the contradiction by distinguishing between declaring hefker before two people (not valid) and before three people (valid and immediate).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Yehoshua ben Levi, why did the Sages require three people for hefker?
A. Three people form a beit din, which is required for property transfers
B. So that one acquires the item and two serve as witnesses
C. Because hefker is compared to giving gifts, which requires three witnesses
D. To prevent fraud by requiring a public declaration
Correct: B — R' Yehoshua ben Levi explains that by Torah law even one person suffices. The Sages required three for a practical purpose: one to acquire the item and two to testify about the declaration and acquisition.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the fundamental disagreement between R' Yochanan and R' Yehoshua ben Levi regarding hefker?
A. Whether hefker applies to land or only movable property
B. Whether the three-person requirement is fundamental or merely a rabbinic safeguard
C. Whether hefker can be retracted after declaration
D. Whether the owner must physically abandon the item
Correct: B — R' Yochanan holds the three-person quorum is essential to a valid hefker declaration. R' Yehoshua ben Levi holds that by Torah law even one person suffices, and three is only a rabbinic measure for practical purposes.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the Mishna, why does the Tanna Kamma prohibit both partners from entering their shared courtyard?
A. Because the courtyard was originally owned by a third party
B. Because each partner benefits from the other's portion when entering
C. Because entering a courtyard requires the other's verbal permission
D. Because the vow specifically mentioned the courtyard
Correct: B — The Tanna Kamma holds that since each partner has a share in the courtyard, entering it means benefiting from the other's portion, which violates the vow not to derive benefit from each other.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov justify permitting both partners to use the shared courtyard?
A. He holds that vows do not apply to real estate
B. He says each partner can be viewed as entering only their own portion
C. He requires them to build a physical partition first
D. He holds the vow is automatically annulled for shared property
Correct: B — R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov permits entry because each partner can be conceptually viewed as entering only their own portion of the courtyard — 'this one enters into his own and that one enters into his own' — so no forbidden benefit occurs.
