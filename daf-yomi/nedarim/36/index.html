Can a Kohen Sprinkle Blood for One Forbidden by Vow?

Rav Shimi bar Abba
Objection from Tosefta: A kohen who vowed to prohibit another from benefit may still sprinkle the blood of his sin-offering and guilt-offering — implying kohanim can perform all sacrificial rites!
Gemara
This refers specifically to the sin-offering and guilt-offering of a metzora (leper). These are for completing purification, not atonement requiring knowledge.
Gemara
As it says: 'This is the law of the metzora' (Vayikra 14:2) — the same law applies to adults and minors, proving da'at (knowledge) is not required.
PRINCIPLE Offerings that complete a purification process (mechusrei kappara) do not require the conscious knowledge of the one being purified.

Piggul Proof: Agents of Heaven or Agents of Ours?

Kohanim sacrifice an offering with piggul intent (intending to eat it past its time)
↓
If done intentionally → they must pay
If done unwittingly → exempt from payment
But either way: the offering is piggul!
↓
If Agents of Heaven
Their actions are independent of the owner — piggul makes sense ✓
If Agents of Ours
Owner should say: 'I appointed you to do it right, not wrong!' — piggul shouldn't apply ✗
↓
Gemara's resolution: Piggul is a special Torah decree — 'He who offered it, it will not be imputed' (Vayikra 7:18) — so no proof either way.
TEYKU-LIKE The dilemma of whether kohanim are sheluchei d'Shamaya (agents of Heaven) or sheluchei didan (our agents) remains unresolved from this proof.

R' Yochanan: Which Offerings Require Da'at (Knowledge)?

Require Knowledge

  • Sin-offering for eating chelev (forbidden fat)
  • Cannot separate for another without his knowledge (R' Elazar)
  • If one became a shoteh (imbecile) after separating — offering permanently disqualified (R' Yirmeya / R' Yochanan)
  • Paschal lamb for another adult — invalid without knowledge

Do NOT Require Knowledge

  • Offerings of mechusrei kappara (those lacking atonement)
  • Purification offerings for minor sons and daughters
  • Sin-offering of a metzora (leper)
  • Guilt-offering of a metzora
  • Offering for a yoledet (woman after childbirth)
R' YOCHANAN Everyone who brings an offering requires da'at — EXCEPT mechusrei kappara, proven by the fact that a father brings purification offerings for his minor children.

Who Gets Tovat Hana'ah — the Discretionary Benefit?

R' Yirmeya
If one separates teruma from his own produce for another's pile — who chooses which kohen gets it? Without his produce, the pile wouldn't be tithed!
R' Zeira
The verse says: 'You shall tithe all the produce of your seed… and you shall give' (Devarim 14:22). The owner of the pile has the right to choose.
R' Yirmeya
Objection: Our mishna says one may separate teruma for someone under a vow — if the owner gets tovat hana'ah, that's a benefit! So it must belong to the separator!
Sages
No — the mishna could be where the owner said 'Anyone may come separate.' That avoids the agency problem.
R' Abbahu / R' Yochanan
Resolution: One who consecrates an animal for another — he adds the fifth, and the owner makes a temurah. Similarly, one who separates teruma from his produce for another — the discretionary benefit is his.
RULING Tovat hana'ah (the right to choose which kohen receives teruma) belongs to the one who separates the teruma from his own produce.
