5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava's resolution, why does R' Yehuda's interpretation of 'without seeing' EXCLUDE a blind person from exile?
A. Because 'into the forest' already includes a blind person, so 'without seeing' must exclude
B. Because a blind person cannot be negligent
C. Because 'without knowledge' already excludes a blind person
D. Because the Torah explicitly exempts the blind from all penalties
Correct: A — R' Yehuda derives inclusion of a blind person from 'into the forest' (he can enter a forest). Since 'without seeing' would be redundant for inclusion, it must serve to EXCLUDE.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the dispute between Rav and Shmuel regarding the gift to a daughter whose husband is under a vow?
A. Whether the father can give any gift at all
B. Whether saying 'do as you please' effectively blocks the husband's acquisition
C. Whether the daughter must be divorced first
D. Whether the vow applies to monetary gifts
Correct: B — Rav holds that only an explicit restriction ('what you place in your mouth') works; 'do as you please' fails and the husband acquires. Shmuel holds even 'do as you please' effectively blocks the husband.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What principle does R' Meir hold regarding a married woman's acquisitions?
A. A wife acquires independently and keeps her own property
B. A wife's hand is like her husband's hand — he acquires what she acquires
C. A wife can only acquire movable property, not land
D. A wife's acquisitions belong to her father until the husband pays a bride-price
Correct: B — R' Meir's principle (yad isha k'yad ba'alah) is that a married woman has no independent right of acquisition — anything she acquires automatically belongs to her husband.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rav Ashi resolve the contradiction between the Mishna in Eiruvin (wife CAN acquire eiruv) and the Baraita (wife CANNOT)?
A. The Mishna is R' Meir and the Baraita is R' Yehuda
B. The Mishna speaks of a wife who owns her own courtyard in the alleyway
C. The Baraita refers to eiruv techumin, not eiruv chatzeirot
D. The wife in the Mishna was divorced
Correct: B — Rav Ashi explains that the Mishna deals with a wife who has her own courtyard (property the husband renounced rights to). She acquires the eiruv for herself via that courtyard, and thereby for others.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If a woman vowed to be a nazirite in 30 days while she was a widow, and then remarried within those 30 days, what happens?
A. Her new husband can nullify the vow before it takes effect
B. The vow is automatically void upon remarriage
C. The vow stands — her new husband cannot nullify it
D. She must ask a Sage to release her from the vow
Correct: C — The Torah states that a widow's or divorcee's vow 'shall stand against her' (Numbers 30:10). Since no husband existed to nullify it when she made the vow, her subsequent husband has no power over it.
