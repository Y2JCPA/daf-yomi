What Are Bible Teachers Really Paid For?

Gemara
Moses taught Torah for free — so must we. Then why may Bible teachers take payment?
Rav
The payment is for watching the children (שְׁמִירַת תִּינוֹקוֹת), not for teaching Torah itself.
R' Yochanan
The payment is for teaching punctuation and cantillation (פִּסּוּק טְעָמִים), a technical skill beyond the Torah obligation.
Gemara
Each view has implications for nedarim, Shabbat reading, and whether cantillation is d'Oraita.
Core Principle Teaching Torah must be free — but the Gemara debates what ancillary services justify a teacher's salary.

Nedarim Implications: Teaching One Forbidden to Benefit

Mishna
If benefit is forbidden by vow, the teacher may not teach him Bible — but may teach his sons.
R' Yochanan
This works perfectly: teaching cantillation is a benefit embedded in teaching Bible, so it's forbidden.
Gemara
But according to Rav — does an adult need watching? Where's the benefit?
Rav
The Mishna speaks of a minor student. For an adult, teaching is permitted since no watching benefit exists.
Gemara
But the Mishna says 'teach his sons' — can a minor have sons?
Rav
The Mishna is incomplete: minor = forbidden; adult = he and his sons are permitted (payment is for watching the son).
Textual Resolution According to Rav, the Mishna addresses two cases: a minor (forbidden) and an adult with sons (permitted, since payment covers watching the child).

New vs. Review Reading on Shabbat

New Passage (Forbidden)

  • Children may NOT read a new passage on Shabbat
  • R' Yochanan: Would require paying for cantillation instruction
  • Alternative: Fathers must supervise new material — takes away from oneg Shabbat
  • Alternative: Children eat more on Shabbat and are sluggish (Shmuel)

Review (Permitted)

  • Children MAY review previously learned passages
  • No new cantillation instruction needed
  • Less parental supervision required
  • Lighter cognitive load for well-fed children
Shmuel's Insight A change in eating routine (שִׁנּוּי וֶסֶת) causes intestinal ailment — children who eat and drink more on Shabbat cannot concentrate on new material.

Nehemiah 8:8 — Components of Torah Reading

Verse PhraseInterpretation
"They read in the book, in the Torah of God"The Biblical text (מִקְרָא)
"Distinctly" (מְפֹרָשׁ)The Aramaic translation (תַּרְגּוּם)
"They gave the sense"Division into verses (פְּסוּקִים)
"Caused them to understand the reading"Cantillation notes (טְעָמִים) — or: vocalization traditions (מָסוֹרוֹת)
Rav Ika bar Avin / Rav Chananel / Rav This verse proves that punctuation and cantillation are integral to Torah — supporting the view that they are d'Oraita and cannot be charged for separately.

Shabbat Payment — The Principle of Havla'ah (Incorporation)

💰 A day laborer watching a child/cow/seeds does NOT receive Shabbat pay separately
↓
⚖️ If items are lost on Shabbat, a day laborer has NO financial responsibility
↓
📅 A weekly/monthly/yearly laborer receives Shabbat pay incorporated into the total
↓
🔒 If items are lost on Shabbat, the long-term laborer IS financially responsible
↓
✅ Teachers' Shabbat work is incorporated into weekly salary — therefore permitted
Havla'ah Principle When Shabbat payment is absorbed into a longer pay period, it is not considered direct payment for Shabbat labor.
