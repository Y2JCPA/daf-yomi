Nedarim 40 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Akiva say that not visiting the sick is like spilling blood?
A. Because the sick person feels emotionally abandoned
B. Because without visitors, no one knows the sick person's needs and cannot help
C. Because it violates a biblical commandment explicitly
D. Because the sick person's family will seek revenge
Correct: B — Rabbi Akiva's point is practical: without visitors, no one discovers what the sick person needs, so no one comes to his aid — making the non-visitor complicit in his potential death.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the Gemara's clarification of Rav Dimi, how does NOT visiting cause death?
A. The non-visitor actively prays for the person's death
B. The sick person dies of loneliness
C. The non-visitor fails to pray at all — neither for life nor death
D. The non-visitor spreads negative rumors about the sick person
Correct: C — The Gemara rejects the idea that anyone would pray for death. Rather, the non-visitor simply never prays for the sick person, and that absence of prayer is tantamount to causing death.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Rava initially hide his illness but later publicize it?
A. He first wanted privacy, then needed financial help
B. He first feared his mazal would suffer, then wanted enemies' joy to trigger divine mercy and friends' prayers
C. He was embarrassed at first but became desperate
D. He wanted to test which of his students were loyal
Correct: B — Rava's two-stage strategy: initially protect his fortune (mazal), then leverage both enemies (whose rejoicing causes God to reverse His wrath per Proverbs 24:17-18) and friends (who will pray for mercy).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why should one NOT visit during the first three hours of the day?
A. The sick person is sleeping and should not be disturbed
B. The sick person feels relieved after sleep, so the visitor won't think prayer is needed
C. Morning is reserved for personal prayer, not visits
D. The Divine Presence is not yet present at the sickbed
Correct: B — Rav Sheisha explains that in the morning, the sick person's condition appears improved after rest, leading the visitor to conclude prayer is unnecessary — defeating the purpose of the visit.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why must a visitor to the sick sit below the level of the sickbed?
A. To show humility before the sick person
B. Because the Divine Presence (Shekhinah) rests above the bed of the sick
C. To avoid accidentally touching impure items on the bed
D. Because sitting higher would block the light the sick person needs
Correct: B — The baraita derives from Psalms 41:4 ('The Lord will support him upon the bed of suffering') that the Shekhinah rests above the sickbed. Sitting lower shows deference to God's presence.
