Vow Within a Vow vs. Oath Within an Oath

Mishna
There is a neder b'tokh neder (vow within a vow) — each nazirite vow takes effect separately
Mishna
But there is no shevua b'tokh shevua (oath within oath) — a repeated oath on the same matter does not take effect
Rav Huna
A vow stacks only when the second adds something — e.g., 'nazirite today' then 'nazirite tomorrow' adds a day
Shmuel
Even 'nazirite today, nazirite today' — the second vow takes effect as an additional obligation
Core Principle Vows (nedarim) prohibit the object, so multiple prohibitions can layer onto the same item. Oaths (shevuot) bind the person, so a duplicate oath on the same act cannot add a new personal obligation.

Two Verses, Two Exemptions

להרע או להיטיב — לא יחל דברו
Background — Amud A Opening One who swears to nullify a mitzva and fails to do so is exempt on two grounds:

1️⃣ "To do evil or to do good" (Lev. 5:4) — teaches that oaths apply only to permitted matters → exempt from offering

2️⃣ "He shall not profane his word" (Num. 30:3) — teaches a separate exemption from the prohibition of violating an oath

Both verses are necessary: one for the korban, one for the lav.

Mishna: Neder vs. Shevua — Stacking Rules

Neder b'Tokh Neder ✅

  • "I am a nazirite if I eat" × 2
  • He eats → obligated in TWO terms of naziriteship
  • Each vow creates a separate 30-day obligation
  • Vow attaches to the OBJECT — multiple layers can coexist

Shevua b'Tokh Shevua ❌

  • "I swear not to eat" × 2
  • He eats → liable for only ONE oath violation
  • Second oath does not take effect
  • Oath binds the PERSON — duplicate personal obligation is redundant
Key Distinction Nedarim operate on the cheftza (object) — each vow adds a new layer of prohibition. Shevuot operate on the gavra (person) — a person already bound cannot be re-bound to the same act.

Rav Huna vs. Shmuel: When Does a Second Vow Take Effect?

Rav Huna
The mishna's case of neder b'tokh neder is specifically: "I am a nazirite today" then "I am a nazirite tomorrow"
Rav Huna
Since 'tomorrow' adds a day beyond the first 30, it is a genuinely new obligation that takes effect
Rav Huna
But if he said 'nazirite today, nazirite today' — the second is entirely redundant and does not take effect
Shmuel
Even 'nazirite today, nazirite today' — the second vow takes effect
Shmuel
A person can apply two obligations of naziriteship one after the other, even without adding new content
Gemara's Question to Rav Huna If Rav Huna is correct, why doesn't the mishna simply teach that sometimes there is a vow within a vow and sometimes not — instead of contrasting vows with oaths?

Challenging Rav Huna from the Mishna

Mishna: Neder b'tokh neder takes effect; Shevua b'tokh shevua does not
↓
What is the parallel oath case where a second oath does NOT take effect?
↓
If oath = figs then grapes
These are different items — the second oath SHOULD take effect! ❌
If oath = figs then figs
Same item — second oath doesn't take effect ✅
↓
If the oath case is 'figs, figs' → the parallel vow case must be 'today, today'
↓
But the mishna says a vow within a vow TAKES EFFECT — difficulty for Rav Huna!
Rav Huna's Defense The vow case is 'today/tomorrow'; the parallel oath is 'figs/figs & grapes' — the second oath doesn't take effect because it partially overlaps with the first.

Rabba's Case: Figs and Grapes Oath

StepActionHalakhic Status
1Swears not to eat figsFirst oath takes effect
2Swears not to eat figs and grapesSecond oath takes effect — grapes are NEW
3Eats figs → sets aside offeringOffering covers first oath violation
4Then eats grapesOnly a half-measure (chatzi shiur) of second oath
5No offering for grapes aloneExempt — one doesn't bring offering for half-measure
Problem for Rav Huna Rabba's ruling shows that when a second oath adds new content (grapes), it takes effect even for the overlapping content (figs). Rav Huna would say the second oath doesn't take effect at all. Resolution: Rav Huna simply disagrees with Rabba (who was his student).
