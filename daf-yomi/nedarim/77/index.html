Can Vows Be Nullified on Shabbat for Non-Shabbat Purposes?

Rav Zuti (school of Rav Pappi)
Vows may be nullified on Shabbat only for the purpose of Shabbat.
Rav Ashi
The Mishna implies otherwise — it specifies nightfall as the deadline. If non-Shabbat vows couldn't be nullified at all on Shabbat, why mention nightfall?
Gemara Resolution
It depends on the time-limit dispute: 'All day' view allows non-Shabbat nullification; '24-hour' view restricts to Shabbat-purpose only.
TANNAITIC DISPUTE The 'all day' view (Chachamim) must allow non-Shabbat nullification on Shabbat — otherwise a vow heard on Shabbat could never be nullified. The '24-hour' view (R' Yosei b. R' Yehuda) can wait until after Shabbat.

Rav Nachman's Comprehensive Ruling on Dissolution

🧍 A halakhic authority may dissolve a vow while standing
↓
👤 He may act alone (no court of three needed, if he is an expert)
↓
🌙 Dissolution may be performed at night
↓
🕎 Dissolution may be performed on Shabbat
↓
👨‍👩‍👦 Dissolution may be performed by relatives
↓
⏰ Even if they had the opportunity to dissolve before Shabbat
HALAKHA Rabba in the name of Rav Nachman establishes the broadest leniencies for dissolution of vows — codifying the flexible nature of this process compared to formal court proceedings.

Must a Halakhic Authority Broach Regret?

Rabban Gamliel
He dismounted his donkey, wrapped himself in a shawl, and sat before dissolving a vow — because one must broach dissolution based on regret (charatah), requiring careful examination.
Rav Nachman
One need not broach dissolution based on regret. Therefore a halakhic authority can dissolve a vow even while standing.
PRACTICAL IMPACT If regret must be broached, the authority needs to sit and examine the case carefully. If not, dissolution can happen quickly and informally.

Nullification Formula: What Works and What Doesn't

❌ Invalid Formulas

  • "I don't want you to vow" (ee efshi)
  • "This is not a vow"
  • These are not valid nullification language

✅ Ratification (unintended!)

  • "You have done well"
  • "There are none like you"
  • "I would have vowed the same" — these RATIFY the vow

📋 On Shabbat Specifically

  • Don't say "It is nullified / canceled"
  • Instead say: "Take this and eat/drink"
  • R' Yochanan: Must also cancel in his heart
BARAITA On Shabbat, the husband avoids explicit nullification language and instead invites her to eat or drink, but must also intend nullification in his heart.
