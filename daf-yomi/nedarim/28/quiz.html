Nedarim 28 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say it is permitted to mislead tax collectors despite Shmuel's principle of dina d'malkhuta dina?
A. The mishna refers to a collector with no fixed amount or who is self-appointed
B. Dina d'malkhuta only applies outside of Eretz Yisrael
C. Tax evasion is always permitted according to halakha
D. Shmuel later retracted this principle
Correct: A — Rav Kahana/Shmuel explains the collector has no fixed rate (collects arbitrarily), and R' Yannai's school says the collector is self-appointed — neither represents legitimate government authority.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Beit Hillel, if a robber compels a vow about one's wife and the person adds 'and my children,' what is the status?
A. Wife is permitted, children are forbidden
B. Both wife and children are forbidden
C. Both wife and children are permitted
D. Only children are permitted, wife is forbidden
Correct: C — Beit Hillel permit adding beyond what is compelled — the entire vow, including the addition about children, has no force since it was made under duress.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara explain that a person's mental stipulation ('today') can limit a vow, when normally devarim shebalev are insignificant?
A. Mental stipulations are always valid for vows
B. The rule changes when the vow involves produce
C. Under circumstances of duress (ones), mental stipulations are valid
D. Shmuel established a special leniency for tax situations
Correct: C — The Gemara distinguishes: normally unspoken thoughts have no legal force, but under duress (ones) they are given effect, allowing one to silently limit the vow's duration.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Bar Padda, what happens when saplings consecrated 'until they are cut' are redeemed before being cut?
A. The redemption is permanent and they lose sanctity
B. They become consecrated again and the cycle repeats until cut
C. The redemption is invalid entirely
D. They achieve partial sanctity that cannot be redeemed
Correct: B — Bar Padda holds that the condition 'until cut' means sanctity renews after each redemption. Only after cutting can a final, permanent redemption take place.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the contradiction between the Mishna (Beit Shammai forbid initiating a vow) and the Baraita (Beit Shammai forbid initiating an oath)?
A. The Mishna and Baraita refer to different time periods
B. Each source highlights the far-reaching position of a different side of the debate
C. The Baraita is rejected as unreliable
D. Beit Shammai changed their position between the two sources
Correct: B — The Mishna teaches about vows to show how strict Beit Shammai are (forbidding even initiating a vow), while the Baraita teaches about oaths to show how lenient Beit Hillel are (permitting even initiating an oath).
