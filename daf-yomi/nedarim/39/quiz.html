Nedarim 39 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, why may the visitor stand but not sit with the ill person?
A. Sitting provides forbidden benefit by saving the ill person the expense of hiring someone to sit with him
B. Standing is less comfortable and therefore not considered real benefit
C. Sitting implies the visitor is staying permanently
D. The ill person's property includes only chairs, not floor space
Correct: A — Shmuel explains that in places where one takes payment for sitting (but not standing) with the ill, the visitor's sitting spares the ill person expense — constituting forbidden benefit from the visitor's property.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Ulla explain the distinction between standing and sitting?
A. Standing is a form of respect; sitting is casual
B. The ill person excluded existential needs from the vow, and standing suffices for the mitzva — sitting is unnecessary
C. Sitting causes the visitor to benefit from the ill person's furniture
D. Standing visits are shorter and therefore less of a burden
Correct: B — Ulla holds the ill person's property is forbidden to the visitor but existential needs were excluded. Since bikur cholim can be fulfilled by standing, sitting is not an existential need and remains prohibited.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What challenge does the baraita about the son's illness pose, and for whom?
A. It challenges Ulla, because the son should also be visited
B. It challenges Shmuel, because visiting the son should be the same as visiting the person himself
C. It challenges both equally
D. It challenges Reish Lakish's Torah derivation
Correct: B — The baraita says when the son is ill, the visitor may only inquire in the marketplace. Ulla explains this easily (existential needs apply to the person, not his son). But for Shmuel, since the visitor's property is forbidden to the ill, there should be no difference between the person and his son.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which of the seven things created before the world is proven from the verse 'For its hearth is ordained of old' (Isaiah 30:33)?
A. The Temple
B. The Throne of Glory
C. Gehenna
D. Repentance
Correct: C — The 'hearth' (tofteh) is identified as Gehenna, and 'ordained of old' indicates it was created before the world. This teaching is brought to resolve Rava's homily about Moses and Korah.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Abaye say 'no fixed measure' means regarding bikur cholim?
A. There is no fixed reward for the mitzva
B. One may visit as many times as needed — there is no limit on frequency
C. There is no minimum time required per visit
D. The mitzva applies to any illness regardless of severity
Correct: B — Abaye rejects Rav Yosef's interpretation (unlimited reward) because all mitzvot have unknown rewards. Instead, Abaye explains that bikur cholim has no fixed measure for frequency — one may visit many times a day.
