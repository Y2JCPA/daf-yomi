Nedarim 62: The Crown of Torah

עשה דברים לשם פעולתן, ודבר בהם לשמן
CORE THEME This daf explores the tension between Torah scholars' privileges and the prohibition against using Torah as a personal tool. Through dramatic stories — Rabbi Tarfon in a sack, Belshazzar's demise — the Gemara warns against treating Torah as a crown for glory or a kordom (hoe) for livelihood, while acknowledging that scholars may identify themselves in genuine need.

When Are Figs in the Field Permitted?

🔪 Have most knives (harvesting tools) been set aside?
↓
Yes
Figs are hefker (ownerless) — permitted to eat and exempt from tithes
No
Figs still belong to the owner — eating them is theft and they require tithing
KEY INCIDENT R' Yehuda HaNasi ate the figs; R' Yosei bar R' Yehuda did not — suspecting the owner's invitation was out of embarrassment, not genuine intent to declare them ownerless.

The Dramatic Story of Rabbi Tarfon

Narrator
Rabbi Tarfon was eating figs after most knives were set aside. A man placed him in a sack and carried him toward the river.
R' Tarfon
"Woe to Tarfon, for this man is killing him!"
Narrator
The man heard it was Rabbi Tarfon and fled. But R' Tarfon was distressed for the rest of his life — he had used Torah for personal benefit.
Gemara
Why the distress? He was very wealthy — he should have paid money to save himself rather than invoking his Torah status.
BACKSTORY The man had been robbed of grapes all year and mistakenly thought R' Tarfon was the thief.

Using the Crown of Torah — The Belshazzar Kal VaChomer

Belshazzar

  • Used sacred Temple vessels
  • Vessels had already become non-sacred after being robbed (Ezekiel 7:22)
  • Temporary, removable sanctity
  • Result: Killed that very night (Daniel 5:30)

One Who Uses Torah

  • Uses the crown of Torah for personal gain
  • Torah lives and endures forever
  • Permanent, irremovable sanctity
  • Result: Uprooted from the world — kal vachomer!
SOURCE Rabba bar bar Chana said in the name of R' Yochanan: Whoever makes use of the crown of Torah is uprooted from the world.

Learn Torah Lishmah — For Its Own Sake

אלא למד מאהבה, וסוף הכבוד לבוא
BARAITA A person should not say:
• "I will read Torah so they call me Sage"
• "I will study Mishna so they call me Rabbi"
• "I will review so I sit in the academy"

Rather, learn out of love — "To love the Lord your God" (Deut. 30:20).
The honor will come on its own: "Its ways are ways of pleasantness, and all its paths are peace" (Proverbs 3:17).
7 / 9

May a Scholar Identify Himself in Need?

Rava
In a time of need, a person may make himself known where people don't recognize him. Proof: Obadiah told Elijah — "I, your servant, have feared the Lord from my youth" (I Kings 18:12).
Questioner
But R' Tarfon was distressed for revealing his identity! Doesn't this contradict Rava?
Rava
R' Tarfon was different — he was very wealthy. He had another way to save himself (paying money), so using Torah status was unnecessary.
PRINCIPLE Invoking Torah status is permitted only when there is no alternative means of resolving the situation.
