Time-Bound Vows: When Do They Expire?

A person vows: "Wine is konam for me"
↓
Did he say 'this' (today/week/month/year) or 'one' (day/week/month/year)?
"This..."
Forbidden until the end of that period (nightfall / end of week / Rosh Chodesh / Rosh HaShana)
"One..."
Forbidden for a full measured period from the moment of the vow (24 hours / 7 days, etc.)
Key Distinction "This day" = until nightfall. "One day" = full 24-hour period from time of vow.

Growths of Teruma: A Preliminary Discussion

גידולי גידולין של תרומה — חולין
Background The Gemara resolves that R' Yannai permits teruma based on growths of growths (גידולי גידולין), not direct growths. This applies even to items whose seeds do not cease (e.g., onions), provided the increase of the growths of growths exceeded the original prohibited portion.

Mishna: 'This' Period — When Does the Vow End?

Vow LanguageForbidden UntilBoundary Rule
"Today" (היום)NightfallEnd of that calendar day
"This week" (השבת)End of ShabbatShabbat belongs to the passing week
"This month" (החודש)End of monthRosh Chodesh belongs to the next month
"This year" (השנה)End of yearRosh HaShana belongs to the next year
"This shemitah cycle"End of 7-year cycleShemitah year belongs to the passing cycle
Pattern Some endpoints (Shabbat, Shemitah) are included in the current period; others (Rosh Chodesh, Rosh HaShana) begin the next.

"This" vs "One" — Two Different Durations

"This Day / Week / Month"

  • Runs until the END of that calendar period
  • "Today" → until nightfall
  • "This week" → through Shabbat
  • "This year" → through end of Elul
  • Duration depends on WHEN in the period the vow was taken

"One Day / Week / Month"

  • Runs for a FULL measured period from time of vow
  • "One day" → 24 hours from now
  • "One week" → 7 full days from now
  • "One year" → 12 months from now
  • Duration is always the same regardless of when vow was taken
Practical Impact If someone vows at noon "today" — forbidden only 6 more hours. If "one day" — forbidden for 24 full hours.

Must One Dissolve an Expired Vow?

R' Yirmeya
Even when the vow expires at nightfall, one must request dissolution (התרת נדרים) from a halakhic authority before drinking wine.
Rav Yosef
This is a rabbinic decree: if we let him drink at nightfall after saying "today," someone who said "one day" might also stop at nightfall — confusing the two categories.
Abaye
If so, decree the reverse too! Someone who said "one day" should keep the vow past 24 hours, lest people confuse it with "today"!
Rav Yosef
No — people might confuse "today" with "one day," but not the reverse. The confusion only goes one direction.
Principle The Sages imposed extra stringency on vows to prevent confusion between similar-sounding formulations.

R' Natan: The Gravity of Vowing

🏛️ One who vows is as if he built a bamah (personal altar) — forbidden since the Temple was established
↓
🔥 One who fulfills the vow is as if he burned offerings on that bamah — compounding the sin
↓
📜 Therefore, even after fulfilling a vow, one should seek its annulment (hatara) retroactively
↓
✅ This supports R' Yirmeya: request dissolution even after the vow naturally expires
R' Natan's View It is always better to have the vow dissolved entirely than to merely fulfill it, since the very act of vowing is problematic.
