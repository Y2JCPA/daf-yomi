QUESTION 1 OF 5
If someone says 'Wine is konam for me today' at noon, when may he drink wine?
A. At noon the next day (24 hours later)
B. At nightfall that evening
C. After requesting dissolution from a halakhic authority
D. At noon the next day, after requesting dissolution
Correct: C — While the vow technically expires at nightfall, R' Yirmeya requires requesting dissolution (hatara) before drinking — a rabbinic decree to prevent confusion with 'one day' vows.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the Mishna, if one vows 'this week,' is Shabbat included in the prohibition?
A. No — Shabbat begins the next week
B. Yes — Shabbat is considered the end of the current week
C. Only if the vow was taken before Wednesday
D. It depends on whether Shabbat has already begun
Correct: B — The Mishna teaches that Shabbat is considered part of the passing week, so the vow prohibiting wine 'this week' includes Shabbat.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rav Yosef say the rabbinic decree requiring dissolution applies to 'today' vows but not the reverse?
A. Because 'today' vows are more common
B. Because people might confuse 'today' with 'one day' but not the reverse
C. Because 'one day' vows are biblically mandated to last 24 hours
D. Because R' Natan only discussed 'today' vows
Correct: B — Rav Yosef explains that the confusion only goes one direction: someone who says 'today' might be confused with 'one day,' but someone who says 'one day' will not confuse it with 'today.'
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Natan, what is one who takes a vow and then fulfills it compared to?
A. One who brings a proper offering in the Temple
B. One who builds a personal altar and then sacrifices upon it
C. One who makes a donation to the Temple treasury
D. One who swears falsely in God's name
Correct: B — R' Natan says vowing is like building a bamah (personal altar, which is forbidden), and fulfilling the vow is like burning offerings on it — compounding the sin. One should seek annulment even after fulfillment.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In a deficient (29-day) month with a two-day Rosh Chodesh, how does the first day of Rosh Chodesh (day 30) affect a vow taken 'this month'?
A. It is part of the old month, so the vow still applies
B. It is considered part of the new month, so the vow from the previous month has expired
C. The vow extends through both days of Rosh Chodesh
D. The status depends on whether the vow was taken in the morning or evening
Correct: B — Even though the first day of a two-day Rosh Chodesh is technically the 30th of the old month, since people call it 'Rosh Chodesh of the new month,' it is treated as part of the new month for vow purposes.
