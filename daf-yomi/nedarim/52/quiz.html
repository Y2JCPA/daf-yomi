QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Rabbis, when does a vow on meat extend to eggs cooked with it?
A. Always — eggs absorb meat flavor
B. Only when he specifies 'this meat' (a particular piece)
C. Only when the eggs are the majority of the dish
D. Never — eggs are a separate food category
Correct: B — The Rabbis explain that R' Tarfon's strict ruling about eggs applied only when the person said 'this meat,' specifying a particular piece. Only then does the principle of noten ta'am (gives flavor) extend the prohibition to mixtures.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the apparent contradiction in R' Yosei's rulings on milk/whey vs. lentils/ashishim?
A. R' Yosei changed his mind over time
B. Milk and whey are more closely related than lentils and ashishim
C. Local linguistic custom — in R' Yosei's locale, 'milk' includes whey but 'lentils' doesn't include ashishim
D. The two rulings are by different Tannaim named R' Yosei
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that in R' Yosei's locale, whey was called 'milk whey' — so the term 'milk' encompassed whey. Vows follow local language conventions, making both rulings internally consistent.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If someone vows 'wine is forbidden to me' (generally), what is the status of a cooked dish flavored with wine?
A. Forbidden — the dish has wine flavor
B. Permitted — a general vow covers only the wine itself
C. Forbidden only if wine is the majority ingredient
D. Depends on local custom
Correct: B — A general vow on wine covers only wine itself. The person is permitted to eat a dish with wine flavor. Only a specific vow ('this wine is konam') would extend the prohibition to dishes that absorb its flavor.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rami bar Ḥama's dilemma regarding the mishna about olives and grapes?
A. Whether olives and grapes are considered the same category
B. Whether the prohibition on oil/wine is because of the word 'these' or the phrase 'I will not taste'
C. Whether konam vows are more stringent than regular vows
D. Whether oil is considered a derivative of olives or a separate food
Correct: B — The mishna uses both 'these' (specifying particular olives/grapes) and 'I will not taste.' Rami bar Ḥama asks which element is the operative cause that extends the prohibition to the oil and wine that emerge from them.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does the Gemara conclude about the word 'these' (specifying a particular food) in a vow?
A. It has no special halakhic significance
B. It definitely extends the vow to derivatives — the unresolved question is whether 'I will not taste' also works alone
C. It only matters for meat vows, not produce
D. It restricts the vow to only that specific item, not derivatives
Correct: B — From R' Tarfon's ruling about 'this meat,' the Gemara establishes that saying 'these' certainly extends a vow to derivatives via noten ta'am. The remaining dilemma is whether 'I will not taste' can independently achieve the same result.
