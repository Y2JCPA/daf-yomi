When Does a Vow Extend Beyond the Named Food?

A person vows that a food is forbidden to him
↓
Did he specify 'this' item or vow generally?
General vow
Only the food itself is forbidden — byproducts and derivatives are permitted
'This' item / specific
Anything that absorbs its flavor (noten ta'am) is also forbidden
Core Principle The scope of a vow depends on whether one specifies a particular item or speaks generally. A specific vow extends to flavor-transfer; a general vow does not.

Are Gravy and Sediments Included in a Meat Vow?

Tanna Kamma
One who vows against meat is permitted to eat gravy and sediments (kifa).
R' Yehuda
He is prohibited from gravy and sediments! R' Tarfon even prohibited eggs cooked with meat.
Rabbis
That case was different — R' Tarfon's ruling applied only when he said 'this meat', specifying a particular piece.
Rabbis
A specific vow means anything mixed with it that gives flavor (noten ta'am) is forbidden. A general vow covers only the food itself.
Key Distinction The dispute resolves around specific vs. general vows — R' Tarfon's strict ruling applied only to a vow on 'this meat,' not meat in general.

Milk & Whey: The Role of Local Custom

Rabbis' Locale

  • Milk is called milk
  • Whey is called whey — a separate name
  • Vow on 'milk' does NOT include whey
  • R' Yosei's strict ruling seems contradictory

R' Yosei's Locale

  • Whey is called 'milk whey'
  • The word 'milk' is used for whey too
  • Vow on 'milk' DOES include whey
  • Not about halakhic transformation — it's language
Resolution R' Yosei is consistent — vows follow local usage of language. In his locale, 'milk' encompasses whey; in the Rabbis' locale, it does not.

Wine in Cooked Dishes: Specific vs. General

One vows that wine is forbidden
↓
Did he say 'this wine' or 'wine' generally?
"Wine is forbidden"
A dish with wine flavor is permitted
"This wine is konam"
If the wine falls into a dish and gives it flavor, the dish is forbidden
Noten Ta'am The principle of noten ta'am (gives flavor) only applies when a specific item was identified in the vow. General category vows do not extend to flavor absorbed by other foods.

Mishna: Grapes, Olives, and Their Derivatives

🍇 Vows 'grapes are forbidden' → Wine is permitted
↓
🫒 Vows 'olives are forbidden' → Oil is permitted
↓
🎯 Says 'these grapes/olives are konam, that I will not taste them'
↓
🍷 Now wine and oil that emerge from them are also forbidden
↓
❓ But which word triggers this — 'these' or 'I will not taste'?
The Dilemma The mishna's expanded prohibition uses both the word 'these' and the phrase 'I will not taste.' Rami bar Ḥama asks: which element is the operative cause?
