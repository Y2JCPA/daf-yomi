Nedarim 16 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara initially attribute the mishna to Rabbi Meir rather than Rabbi Yehuda?
A. R' Yehuda does not distinguish between 'korban' and 'ha-korban' — both are invalid without 'ke-korban'
B. R' Yehuda holds all vows are valid regardless of wording
C. R' Meir is always the default anonymous opinion in mishnayot
D. R' Yehuda does not accept the concept of nedarim at all
Correct: A — R' Yehuda requires the comparative 'ke-korban' (like an offering) for any vow to take effect. Since the mishna treats some non-comparative phrasings as valid, it cannot follow his view.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Abaye, when does 'she'okhal' (that I will eat) actually mean 'I will NOT eat'?
A. When the person is being pressured to eat and keeps saying 'I will eat'
B. When the person has been refusing to eat and says 'an oath she'okhal' to reinforce his refusal
C. When the person is speaking in Aramaic rather than Hebrew
D. When the oath is taken before witnesses
Correct: B — Abaye teaches that if someone repeatedly refused to eat ('I will not eat, I will not eat') and then said 'an oath she'okhal,' the oath reinforces the refusal — 'she'okhal' means 'I will not eat' in that context.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why can an oath NOT override a mitzva, according to the Gemara's derivation?
A. Oaths are only rabbinic in origin
B. The verse 'lo yachel devaro' implies: HIS word he cannot profane, but he can profane it for God's desires
C. Oaths require witnesses, and witnesses would refuse to attest
D. An oath only applies to past actions, not future ones
Correct: B — The Gemara derives from 'he shall not profane HIS word' that only personal commitments are binding — one may set aside his own word when it conflicts with a divine obligation.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the key difference between Abaye and Rava regarding how a vow overrides a mitzva?
A. Abaye says the vow must be taken in the Temple; Rava disagrees
B. Abaye frames it as forbidding the BENEFIT of the mitzva object; Rava says mitzvot aren't given for benefit, so the vow forbids the ACTION
C. Abaye says only vows about Shabbat can override; Rava extends it to all mitzvot
D. Abaye requires intent to override; Rava does not
Correct: B — Abaye says one forbids the benefit of sukka, making it a forbidden item. Rava objects that mitzvot were not given for personal benefit (מצוות לאו ליהנות ניתנו), so the vow must target the act itself: 'dwelling in a sukka is konam.'
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the distinction between 'lekorban' and 'la lekorban' that resolves the contradiction with Rabbi Meir's position?
A. 'Lekorban' is Hebrew and 'la lekorban' is Aramaic — different legal force
B. 'Lekorban' (for an offering) renders the vow effective; 'la lekorban' (not for an offering) means the vow does not take effect
C. 'Lekorban' applies only to animal offerings; 'la lekorban' applies to meal offerings
D. Both are effective, but 'la lekorban' requires dissolution by a sage
Correct: B — The Gemara resolves the contradiction by distinguishing the prefixes: 'lekorban' means the food is designated as an offering (vow effective), while 'la lekorban' means the food is explicitly NOT an offering (vow does not take effect).
