Nedarim 58 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Shimon, why is Sabbatical Year produce treated as an 'item that can become permitted' regarding mixtures?
A. Because it can be redeemed like second tithe
B. Because it may be eaten before the bi'ur deadline arrives
C. Because its sanctity expires automatically after seven years
D. Because a court can annul its prohibition
Correct: B — R' Shimon argues that since Shevi'it produce may be eaten before the time of bi'ur (removal), it has the status of an item that can become permitted during that period.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the indicator used by R' Ḥanina ben Antigonus to determine if sixth-year onions have become Sabbatical Year produce?
A. The taste of the onion changes
B. The onion can be uprooted by pulling its leaves
C. The leaves turn dark green
D. The onion doubles in size
Correct: B — R' Ḥanina ben Antigonus says if the plant can be uprooted by pulling its leaves, the leaves are fresh and recent Shevi'it growth, making the onion forbidden.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does R' Yitzchak claim Sabbatical Year produce is different from orla regarding growth neutralizing prohibition?
A. Shevi'it is rabbinic while orla is biblical
B. Shevi'it has a time limit but orla does not
C. Shevi'it prohibition is engendered by the ground, so nullification works through the ground
D. Shevi'it applies only in Israel while orla applies everywhere
Correct: C — R' Yitzchak's principle is that since Sabbatical Year prohibition comes through the ground, its nullification can also be effected through the ground via new growth.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What challenge does the Gemara raise against R' Yitzchak's 'ground-based' principle?
A. Teruma is also ground-based but can be neutralized
B. Untithed first tithe is ground-based, yet its growth does NOT neutralize the obligation
C. Orla trees also grow from the ground
D. Ḥalla is separated from ground-grown produce
Correct: B — The Gemara shows that a litra of untithed first tithe sown and grown to ten litra still requires tithing from a different place — the ground-based growth did not neutralize the prohibition.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the possibility that the ḥasayot case involves crushed produce?
A. The baraita explicitly states the produce was not crushed
B. Crushing would make the produce inedible and the case meaningless
C. One who is suspect about Shevi'it observance would not bother to crush the produce
D. Ḥasayot cannot be crushed due to their structure
Correct: C — The Gemara reasons that someone who is already suspect about Sabbatical Year observance would not go to the trouble of crushing the produce to eliminate its prohibition.
