Can Permitted Growth Neutralize a Prohibition?

A forbidden item is planted and produces new growth
↓
Does permitted growth neutralize the original prohibition?
Sabbatical Year
Yes — prohibition is engendered by the ground, so nullification works through the ground (R' Yitzchak)
Orla / Kilayim
R' Yochanan & R' Yonatan say no — permitted growth does not neutralize
Core Question The Gemara searches for proof whether permitted growth can neutralize the prohibition of the original plant

Two Categories of Forbidden Items

Can Become Permitted (דבר שיש לו מתירין)

  • Untithed produce — permitted through tithing
  • Second tithe — permitted through redemption
  • Consecrated items — permitted through redemption
  • New crop (חדש) — permitted after the Omer offering
  • NO measure for neutralization — never nullified in a mixture

Cannot Become Permitted (דבר שאין לו מתירין)

  • Teruma and Teruma of the tithe
  • Ḥalla (חלה)
  • Orla — fruit of first 3 years
  • Kilei HaKerem — forbidden vineyard crops
  • The Sages DID set a measure for neutralization
Principle If a prohibition will eventually lapse on its own, the Sages were stricter — it is never nullified in a mixture, since one can simply wait for it to become permitted

Where Does Sabbatical Year Produce Fit?

Rabbanan
Shevi'it produce cannot become permitted — yet the Sages did not set a measure for neutralization. It prohibits any amount!
R' Shimon
I agree it prohibits any amount — but only regarding bi'ur (removal). It can become permitted, since it may be eaten before the removal deadline.
R' Shimon
After the time for removal passes, the Sages did set a measure — enough to impart flavor (נותן טעם).
Gemara
This seems to prove permitted growths neutralize the original! But perhaps this is only a stringency, not a leniency.
Key Distinction R' Shimon splits Shevi'it into two phases: before bi'ur (can become permitted → strict) and after bi'ur (cannot → has a measure of nullification)

Sixth-Year Onions Sprouting in Shevi'it

Onions planted in the sixth year receive rain in Shevi'it and sprout new growth
↓
What color are the leaves?
Dark Green (שחורין)
Leaves are fresh = new Shevi'it growth → Forbidden
Yellowish-Green (הוריקו)
Leaves are withered = sixth-year product → Permitted
↓
R' Ḥanina ben Antigonus: test by pulling the leaves — if plant uproots easily, leaves are fresh → forbidden
Gemara's Objection Perhaps the onions were crushed (מדוכנין) and no longer edible — not that permitted growth neutralized the prohibition

R' Yitzchak's Distinction: Ground-Based Prohibitions

Gemara
The ḥasayot proof should refute R' Yochanan and R' Yonatan, who say orla and kilayim growth does not neutralize!
R' Yitzchak
Shevi'it is different. Its prohibition is engendered by the ground — so its nullification is also effected by the ground.
Gemara
But untithed tithe (מעשר) is also engendered by the ground, yet growth does not neutralize it!
Gemara
A litra of untithed first tithe sown and grown to ten litra — the growth is still obligated in tithes, and the original litra must be tithed from elsewhere.
Challenge R' Yitzchak's principle is questioned: tithe is also ground-based, yet its growth doesn't neutralize — the distinction needs further refinement
