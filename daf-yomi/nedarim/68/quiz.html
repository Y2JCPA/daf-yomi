QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the School of R' Yishmael, which verse teaches that father and betrothed husband jointly nullify vows?
A. "And if she be to a husband" (Num 30:7)
B. "Between a man and his wife, between a father and his daughter" (Num 30:17)
C. "Her father heard her vow" (Num 30:5)
D. "And her husband heard it" (Num 30:8)
Correct: B — The School of R' Yishmael derives joint nullification from Numbers 30:17, which mentions both the man-wife and father-daughter relationships. Rava uses 30:7 for this purpose instead.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava, what does the phrase "between a man and his wife" (Num 30:17) teach?
A. Father and husband must nullify together
B. The husband can nullify only vows affecting the marital relationship
C. If the husband dies, authority reverts to the father
D. A husband's nullification severs half the vow
Correct: B — Rava uses this verse to limit the husband's nullification power to vows that are 'beinav le'veinah' — vows that negatively impact the relationship between husband and wife.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the two-olives case, if we say the husband's nullification 'severs' his share, what is the result when she eats both olives?
A. She is exempt from any punishment
B. She is flogged, because one olive remains fully forbidden
C. She receives lashes for both olives
D. She must bring a korban but is not flogged
Correct: B — If 'severing' is correct, the husband removes his half entirely, leaving one olive with a complete prohibition. Eating that olive constitutes a full violation warranting flogging.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If the husband heard the vow, was silent, and then died the next day, can the father nullify alone?
A. Yes, because the husband never explicitly ratified
B. Yes, because the husband's silence is meaningless
C. No, because silence through the end of the day constitutes ratification
D. No, but only if the father also heard the vow
Correct: C — Silence through the end of the day on which the husband heard the vow is treated as ratification. Once the vow is ratified, the father cannot nullify it alone.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When the father dies before hearing the vow, but the husband already nullified it, what happens?
A. The husband's nullification stands on its own
B. The vow is automatically nullified
C. The husband cannot nullify alone — the vow stands
D. A beit din takes over the father's role
Correct: C — The husband can only nullify vows in partnership with the father. Without a living father, the husband has no independent authority, and the vow remains in effect.
