Nullifying a Betrothed Woman's Vows — Sources & Scenarios

A betrothed na'arah (young woman) makes a vow
↓
Who can nullify? Both father and husband must act together
↓
Source: Rava
Derived from "and if she be to a husband" (Num 30:7)
Source: School of R' Yishmael
Derived from "between a man and his wife, between a father and his daughter" (Num 30:17)
Core Issue Both sources agree that father and betrothed husband jointly nullify a na'arah's vows — they disagree on which verse teaches this.
3 / 9

Which Verse Teaches Joint Nullification?

Rava
The phrase "and if she be to a husband" (Num 30:7) teaches that father and betrothed husband nullify her vows together.
School of R' Yishmael
The verse "between a man and his wife, between a father and his daughter" (Num 30:17) is the source for joint nullification.
Gemara
What does the School of R' Yishmael do with "and if she be to a husband"?
School of R' Yishmael
That verse teaches Rava's other ruling: if the betrothed dies without ratifying, the father can nullify alone.
Gemara
And what does Rava do with the School of R' Yishmael's verse?
Rava
"Between a man and his wife" teaches that a husband can only nullify vows that affect their relationship (devarim she'beinav le'veinah).
Key Insight Both tannaim use both verses — they simply assign different halakhot to each verse.
4 / 9

Rava vs. School of R' Yishmael — Verse Assignments

Rava

  • "If she be to a husband" (30:7) → Father + husband nullify jointly
  • "Between a man and his wife" (30:17) → Husband can only nullify vows affecting the marriage

School of R' Yishmael

  • "Between a man and his wife…father and daughter" (30:17) → Father + husband nullify jointly
  • "If she be to a husband" (30:7) → If betrothed dies, father nullifies alone
Note The practical halakha is the same — both agree on joint nullification. The dispute is purely about which verse teaches which rule.

Severing vs. Weakening — The Two Olives Case

She vows not to eat two olives
↓
Betrothed nullifies his portion
↓
She eats both olives before the father nullifies
↓
Severing (חותך)
One olive is fully forbidden. She is flogged for eating a complete kezayit.
Weakening (מיגז)
The entire vow is weakened. No single olive carries a full prohibition — no flogging.
Dilemma Does the husband's nullification remove his half entirely (sever) or dilute the whole prohibition (weaken)?

Death Scenarios — When Does Authority Revert to the Father?

Husband's Status Before DeathCan Father Nullify Alone?
Husband never heard the vow✅ Yes — authority reverts to father
Husband heard and was silent (same day)✅ Yes — authority reverts to father
Husband heard and nullified (same day)✅ Yes — authority reverts to father
Husband heard and ratified❌ No — vow is confirmed
Husband was silent and died the next day❌ No — silence = ratification
Rule Authority reverts to the father only when the husband did not ratify the vow before dying.
7 / 9

When the Father Dies — Does Authority Pass to the Husband?

👨‍👧 Father hears the vow and nullifies it
↓
💍 Husband has not yet heard the vow
↓
💀 Father dies before husband hears
↓
❌ Husband cannot nullify alone — authority does NOT revert to husband
Asymmetry When the husband dies → authority reverts to father. When the father dies → authority does NOT revert to husband. The father's role is primary.
