Nedarim 20 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Yehuda, who does NOT need to request dissolution of an ambiguous vow?
A. An ignoramus
B. A Torah scholar
C. A kohen
D. A nazirite
Correct: B — Rav Yehuda explains the mishna distinguishes between a Torah scholar — who clearly never intended the vow to take effect — and an ignoramus, who is treated stringently.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the 'punishment' for an ignoramus who violated his naziriteship and seeks dissolution?
A. Monetary fine
B. Lashes
C. Must observe naziriteship for the same days he was lax
D. Excommunication
Correct: C — The baraita teaches that he must observe the nazirite prohibitions for the same number of days he behaved permissively. R' Yosei limits this to 30 days for long nazirite terms.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does the Gemara derive from 'That His fear may be upon your faces' (Exodus 20:17)?
A. The obligation to wear tefillin
B. The virtue of shame (busha)
C. Fear of punishment
D. The requirement to veil one's face
Correct: B — The baraita interprets 'fear upon your faces' as shame — blushing — and teaches that shame leads to fear of sin and is a sign that one's ancestors stood at Sinai.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Ameimar, who are the 'ministering angels' that R' Yochanan ben Dehavai cited?
A. Actual angels who appeared to him
B. The patriarchs
C. The Sages, using an honorary title
D. Elijah the Prophet
Correct: C — Ameimar reasons that if they were actual angels, we'd follow their ruling. Since the halakha rejects ben Dehavai, they must be Sages — called 'ministering angels' as an honor.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Ravina teaches that 'do not drink from one cup while looking at another' applies even to what case?
A. Drinking wine and beer at the same meal
B. A man with his own two wives — he must not think of one while with the other
C. Eating two types of sacrificial meat
D. Studying two tractates simultaneously
Correct: B — Ravina says the prohibition of thinking about another woman during intimacy is obvious for an unrelated woman. The novel teaching is that it applies even to a man's own two wives.
