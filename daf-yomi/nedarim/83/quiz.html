QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does a nazira whose husband secretly nullified her vow NOT receive lashes for drinking wine?
A. Because she acted under duress
B. Because the vow was fully nullified — naziriteship cannot take effect partially
C. Because wine is only a rabbinic prohibition for a nazira
D. Because she didn't know about the nullification, so she gets a lighter penalty
Correct: B — Rav Yosef teaches that naziriteship is indivisible. The husband's nullification cancels the entire vow — including wine, grape products, and impurity — so there is no transgression at all.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Abaye, what offering does a nazira bring after her husband nullifies her vow?
A. Three full nazirite offerings (sin, burnt, peace)
B. A bird sin-offering only, because it can be brought for uncertainty
C. A bird burnt-offering only
D. No offering at all
Correct: B — Abaye holds that no offering is brought for partial naziriteship. The bird sin-offering she brings is explained as a safek (uncertainty) offering — since bird sin-offerings can be brought in cases of doubt.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara explain that avoiding corpse impurity causes a nazira 'affliction' (innui)?
A. Corpses are inherently distressing to encounter
B. She fears that if she cannot attend others' funerals, no one will attend hers (R' Meir / Kohelet 7:2)
C. The Sages decreed that any nazirite prohibition counts as affliction
D. She cannot inherit property from the deceased
Correct: B — R' Meir expounds Kohelet 7:2: 'The living shall lay it to his heart' — one who eulogizes, weeps for, and buries others will receive the same. A woman barred from funerals suffers knowing she may not be treated likewise.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the Mishna's case of 'property of people is konam for me,' why can't the husband nullify the vow according to Rava?
A. Because a husband is not included in 'people'
B. Because she can sustain herself from leket, shikcha, and pe'ah — so the vow doesn't cause affliction
C. Because konam vows cannot be nullified by a husband
D. Because the vow was made before marriage
Correct: B — Rava holds the husband IS included in 'people,' so she can't eat from his property. Yet he still can't nullify because she has leket, shikcha, and pe'ah available — meaning the vow doesn't cause true affliction.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the key difference between Ulla and Rav Nachman's positions on the 'people' vow?
A. Ulla says the husband can nullify; Rav Nachman says he cannot
B. Both say husband ≠ 'people,' but Rav Nachman focuses on the post-divorce scenario as the reason he can't nullify
C. Ulla says she can eat leket; Rav Nachman says she cannot
D. Rav Nachman includes the husband in 'people'; Ulla does not
Correct: B — Both agree the husband is not 'people.' Ulla gives two independent reasons he can't nullify. Rav Nachman's reasoning centers on the divorce scenario: even if she's later divorced and the husband becomes 'people,' she still has leket/shikcha/pe'ah — so the vow never reaches the level of affliction.
