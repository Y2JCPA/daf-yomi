Nedarim 83 — Partial Nullification & 'People' Vows

Core Question: Can a husband partially nullify a nazirite vow?
↓
Rav Yosef: Naziriteship cannot take effect partially — nullification cancels all
↓
Abaye refines: No offering is brought for partial naziriteship either
↓
New Mishna: 'Property of people is konam' — husband cannot nullify

Husband ≠ 'people'; two independent reasons he can't nullify

Husband = 'people'; she survives on leket, shikcha, pe'ah

The Problem: Partial Nullification of Naziriteship

A nazira whose husband nullified her vow without her knowledge drinks wine
↓
She does not receive 40 lashes — the vow was nullified
↓
But wait: Maybe the husband only nullified the affliction parts (wine = pain to abstain)?
↓
Wine 🍷
Causes suffering to abstain — husband can nullify?
Grape seeds 🌰
No suffering to abstain — husband cannot nullify?
⚠️ Implication If partial nullification works, eating grape seeds or skins would still carry the penalty of 40 lashes even after nullification!

Rav Yosef & Abaye: Can Naziriteship Be Divided?

Rav Yosef
Naziriteship cannot take effect partially (ein nazirut l'chatza'in). Since one cannot be a nazirite while accepting only some prohibitions, the husband's nullification cancels the entire vow.
Abaye
Your wording implies naziriteship can't take effect partially, but an offering IS brought for partial observance. That can't be right!
Abaye
Rather, say: Naziriteship cannot take effect partially, and no offering is brought for partial naziriteship either.
Gemara
Objection: A baraita says a nazira whose husband nullified must bring a bird sin-offering! If no offering for partial naziriteship, why?
Gemara
A bird sin-offering can be brought in cases of uncertainty (safek). That explains why only a sin-offering — not the full three offerings of a completed nazirite.
💡 Resolution If full offerings were required, she'd bring three animals (sin, burnt, peace). Since only a bird sin-offering is required, it must be due to uncertainty — not partial naziriteship.

Ordinary Vows vs. Nazirite Vows: Nullification Scope

Ordinary Vow (Neder)

  • Husband can nullify only the affliction component
  • Non-affliction parts remain in force
  • Each element of the vow is independent
  • Partial nullification is effective

Nazirite Vow (Nezirut)

  • Cannot take effect partially
  • All prohibitions are one unit: wine, grape products, impurity, haircut
  • Nullification cancels the entire vow
  • No offering for partial observance
⚖️ Principle Naziriteship is an all-or-nothing commitment — unlike a regular vow, it cannot be sliced into components for selective nullification.

Impurity from the Dead = Affliction?

והחי יתן אל לבו — המספיד יספדוהו, והמבכה יבכוהו, והקובר יקברוהו
📖 Kohelet 7:2 R' Meir expounds: 'And the living shall lay it to his heart' — one who eulogizes others will be eulogized; one who weeps for others will be wept for; one who buries others will be buried. A woman who cannot attend funerals due to her nazirite status suffers distress knowing she may not receive the same in return. Therefore, even the prohibition against corpse impurity (tumat met) is a matter of innui (affliction) — and the husband can nullify this aspect too.
