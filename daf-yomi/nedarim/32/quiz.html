Nedarim 32 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why was Moshe endangered at the lodging place according to R' Yehuda bar Bizna?
A. He was negligent about circumcising his son
B. He failed to offer a sacrifice
C. He forgot to recite Shema
D. He disrespected the elders of Israel
Correct: A — R' Yehuda bar Bizna teaches that Moshe was negligent about circumcision, and the destructive angels Af and Ḥeima came and swallowed him until Tzipporah performed the circumcision.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Eliezer, what sustains heaven and earth (based on Jeremiah 33:25)?
A. Circumcision
B. Torah study
C. Prayer
D. Acts of kindness
Correct: B — Rabbi Eliezer disagrees with the view that circumcision sustains creation. He holds that Torah sustains heaven and earth, since 'you shall contemplate it day and night' — making Torah the 'covenant of day and night.'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did the priesthood transfer from Melchizedek (Shem) to Avraham?
A. Melchizedek refused to serve as priest
B. Melchizedek blessed Avraham before blessing God
C. Avraham defeated him in battle
D. Melchizedek was not circumcised
Correct: B — Melchizedek placed the blessing of the 'servant' (Avraham) before the blessing of the 'Master' (God). Avraham objected, and God immediately transferred the priesthood to Avraham's line.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the gematria-based significance of 'Abram' (243) becoming 'Abraham' (248)?
A. He gained 5 years of life
B. He gained mastery over 5 additional limbs after circumcision
C. He acquired 5 additional mitzvot
D. He was given 5 children
Correct: B — Rami bar Abba teaches that after circumcision, Avraham gained mastery over 5 additional limbs (two eyes, two ears, and the tip of the sex organ), going from 243 to 248 — total control over his body.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the Mishna, what is the practical difference between one forbidden from all benefit (muddar hana'a) and one forbidden from food benefit (muddar ma'achal)?
A. Muddar hana'a may not even set foot on property or borrow non-food items; muddar ma'achal may
B. Muddar ma'achal has stricter rules about lending
C. There is no practical difference
D. Muddar hana'a applies only to relatives
Correct: A — The key distinction is that muddar hana'a cannot set foot on the other's property or borrow any utensils, while muddar ma'achal may do both — only food-preparation utensils (sieve, strainer, millstone, oven) are restricted.
