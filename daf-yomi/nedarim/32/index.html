Nedarim 32 — The Power of Brit Milah

גדולה מילה שאילמלא מילה לא נתקיימו שמים וארץ
CORE THEME This daf launches an extended aggadic exploration of circumcision's cosmic significance — from Moshe's near-death for delaying it, to Avraham's wholeness through it, to gematria connecting Avraham's name to mastery over his limbs. The daf then pivots to Melchizedek losing the priesthood for blessing Avraham before God, and concludes with the Mishna distinguishing between one forbidden from all benefit vs. one forbidden only from food benefit.

What Makes Circumcision So Great?

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
Avraham was engaged in mitzvot more than anyone, yet was called wholehearted (תמים) only after circumcision: 'Walk before Me and be wholehearted' (Gen. 17:1)
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
Circumcision is equal to all the mitzvot — 'For according to these words I have made a covenant' (Ex. 34:27)
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
Without circumcision, heaven and earth would not endure — 'If not My covenant day and night…' (Jer. 33:25)
Rabbi Eliezer
It is the Torah, not circumcision, that sustains heaven and earth — the same verse in Jeremiah refers to Torah, as 'you shall contemplate it day and night' (Joshua 1:8)
KEY DISAGREEMENT Both agree the Jeremiah verse proves something sustains creation — they disagree whether 'covenant day and night' refers to milah (always on the body) or Torah (studied day and night)

Divination: The Consequences

One Who Divines

  • Attempts to tell his own fortune
  • The sign will injure him — measure for measure
  • R' Yehuda HaNasi: 'כי לא נחש ביעקב' (Num. 23:23)
  • Read as 'lo' (לו) = divination harms him

One Who Does NOT Divine

  • Brought inside a partition closer to God
  • Even ministering angels cannot enter there
  • Ahava b. R' Zeira: 'Now it is said to Jacob and Israel what God has wrought'
  • Matters are revealed to Israel that angels don't know
TEXTUAL NOTE The verse reads לֹא (no) with lamed-alef, meaning 'no divination in Jacob' — so the punishment is not from the verse itself but from the principle of measure for measure
