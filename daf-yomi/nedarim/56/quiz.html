QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Rabbis, if one vows that a house is forbidden, what is the status of the upper story?
A. Permitted — the upper story is not part of the house
B. Prohibited — the upper story is included in 'house'
C. Permitted only if it has a separate entrance
D. Depends on whether the upper story was built at the same time
Correct: B — The Rabbis hold that an upper story (aliyya) is included in the category of 'house,' so it is also prohibited. R' Meir disagrees and permits entry to the upper story.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Abaye say the Rabbis also need the verse 'in the house' to include the upper story for leprosy?
A. Because leprosy only applies to stone buildings
B. Because you might think only what is attached to the ground qualifies as 'house' for leprosy
C. Because the Rabbis actually agree with R' Meir about leprosy
D. Because the upper story was typically made of different materials
Correct: B — Abaye explains that since the verse says 'a house of the land of your possession,' one might conclude that only structures attached to the ground qualify — excluding an upper story. The verse is needed even according to the Rabbis.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the final understanding of the structural difference between a bed and a dargash?
A. A bed has legs, a dargash does not
B. A bed uses straps through holes; a dargash uses straps through loops (karvitin)
C. A dargash is made of leather, a bed is made of wood
D. A bed is for sleeping, a dargash is purely ornamental
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that in both a bed and dargash, straps go through the frame. The difference is that a bed's straps go directly through holes, while a dargash's straps go through loops (karvitin) attached to the frame — which can be loosened to collapse it.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara prove that the outskirts of a city are considered part of the city for vows?
A. From a verse about the cities of refuge
B. From Joshua 5:13, where Joshua is described as 'in Jericho' while in its outskirts
C. From the laws of Shabbat boundaries
D. From a teaching of R' Meir about city walls
Correct: B — Since Jericho was 'completely shut,' Joshua could not have been inside the city proper. Yet the verse says he was 'in Jericho' — proving he was in the outskirts, and that outskirts are considered part of the city.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why is the area under the lintel treated differently for leprosy than for vows on a house?
A. Because leprosy is a biblical law while vows are rabbinic
B. Because the verse 'the priest shall go out from the house' requires exiting the entire structure
C. Because the lintel is considered part of the roof, not the house
D. Because vows are interpreted strictly while leprosy is interpreted leniently
Correct: B — For leprosy, the specific verse states 'the priest shall go out from the house,' meaning he must leave the entire structure — including the area under the lintel. For vows, the doorstop is the boundary based on common usage of the term 'house.'
