Nedarim 33 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, what formulation of the vow explains why food-preparation utensils like sieves are forbidden?
A. Benefit from your food is forbidden to me
B. Benefit that leads to preparation of your food is forbidden to me
C. All your property is forbidden to me
D. Your food and anything that touches it is forbidden to me
Correct: B — Rava explains the mishna refers to a vow formulated as 'benefit that leads to preparation of your food is forbidden to me,' which extends beyond direct food consumption to include preparatory tools.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the status of Rav Pappa's dilemma about borrowing a horse or ring to attend a feast?
A. It is resolved — borrowing is permitted
B. It is resolved — borrowing is forbidden
C. It remains unresolved (teiku)
D. It is resolved based on the mishna about garments
Correct: C — The Gemara attempts to resolve the dilemma from the mishna permitting lending garments and rings, but this proof is refuted. The dilemma remains unresolved.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does the concept of 'driving away the lion' (mevareiach ari) mean in the context of vows?
A. Actively giving someone a gift
B. Preventing potential harm rather than conferring benefit
C. Performing an act of kindness that must be repaid
D. Protecting Temple property from damage
Correct: B — Repaying someone's debt is compared to driving away a lion — you're preventing harm (the creditor collecting), not giving the debtor anything new. This is why it's permitted even when benefit is forbidden by vow.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Ḥanan, what happens when someone voluntarily supports another man's wife while the husband is overseas?
A. The husband must reimburse him after taking an oath
B. The supporter lost his money — he has no claim
C. The wife must repay from her own property
D. The court forces the husband to pay without an oath
Correct: B — Ḥanan rules that one who voluntarily pays another's obligation without being asked 'placed his money on the antler of a deer' — a risky venture with no guaranteed return. He loses his money.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the 'peruta of Rav Yosef' and why is it relevant to returning a lost item?
A. A fee the finder charges the owner for returning the item
B. The finder's exemption from giving charity while occupied with the mitzvah — an indirect benefit
C. A Torah-mandated reward for fulfilling the mitzvah of returning lost items
D. The depreciation in value of the lost item while in the finder's possession
Correct: B — Rav Yosef taught that one tending to a lost item is like a paid bailee because while occupied with the mitzvah, he is exempt from giving charity. This 'payment' (the saved peruta) could constitute forbidden benefit if the owner's property is forbidden to the returner.
