Nedarim 2 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is a 'kinui' in the context of Nedarim?
A. A substitute term that has the same legal force as the original vow language
B. A formal cancellation of a vow by a sage
C. A type of offering brought to the Temple
D. A specific prayer recited when taking a vow
Correct: A — A kinui is a colloquial substitute word (e.g., 'konam' for 'korban') that carries the same binding legal force as the proper term.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the fundamental difference between a neder (vow) and a shevua (oath)?
A. A neder is biblical while a shevua is rabbinic
B. A neder forbids the object while a shevua forbids the person from acting
C. A neder requires witnesses while a shevua does not
D. A neder can be annulled while a shevua cannot
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that a neder operates on the object ('this loaf is forbidden'), while a shevua operates on the person ('I will not eat this loaf'). This distinction is why the Mishna groups vows with dedications rather than oaths.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Mishna in Nedarim list all four categories (vows, dedications, oaths, nazirite vows) rather than just vows?
A. Because Rabbi Akiva required it
B. Because all four are mentioned in the same verse
C. Because vows and oaths are juxtaposed in the Torah, and once two were taught, all were included
D. Because the Mishna always lists every related category
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that since vows and oaths appear together in Bamidbar 30:3, both had to be taught. And once the Mishna was teaching two categories, it included all four.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is R' Akiva's position regarding someone who says 'I am ostracized (menudeh) from you'?
A. It is definitely a valid vow
B. It has no legal effect whatsoever
C. He is uncertain but inclines toward treating it stringently as a vow
D. It is valid only if said before witnesses
Correct: C — The Mishna states that R' Akiva 'was uncertain about this halakha but was inclined to rule stringently,' treating 'menudeh ani mimkha' as a vow prohibiting benefit from the other person.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why does the Mishna explain intimations (yadot) before substitutes (kinuyim), even though substitutes were mentioned first?
A. Because intimations are more common in practice
B. Because the Mishna's style is to explain the concluding topic first
C. Because intimations are more stringent
D. Because substitutes were already explained in Tractate Nazir
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the Mishna's literary convention is to explain the last-mentioned topic first, bringing proofs from multiple Mishnayot in Shabbat where the concluding case is explained before the opening case.
