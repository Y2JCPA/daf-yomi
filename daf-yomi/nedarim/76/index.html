When Can Vows Be Nullified? Preemptive Power & Time Limits

R' Eliezer
Just as impure seeds planted in the ground become pure, vows nullified preemptively should also be valid
Rabbis
The verse says 'her husband may ratify or nullify it' — only a vow already taken can be nullified
Mishna
Nullification is valid all day on the day the vow was heard — with both leniency and stricture
R' Yosei b. R' Yehuda
A vow can be nullified for a full 24-hour period from the time it was heard
Halakha R' Yehoshua ben Levi and R' Hiyya rule: the halakha is not like the pair — nullification is only until the end of that day, not a full 24 hours.

R' Eliezer's Seeds Analogy (Amud A)

R' Eliezer
Impure seeds sown in the ground become pure. Seeds already sown that contact impurity — should they not all the more so be pure?
R' Eliezer
Similarly, since a husband can nullify vows after they are taken, preemptive nullification should certainly work!
Gemara
This proves R' Eliezer holds preemptively nullified vows do not take effect at all — like seeds already sown that never become impure
Rabbis
We asked him: Does immersing a pure vessel in a mikveh protect it from future impurity? Obviously not!
Key Insight The Gemara clarifies R' Eliezer's position: preemptively nullified vows never take effect, rather than taking effect momentarily and then being nullified.

Then a mikveh before impurity should purify — but it doesn't! ❌
Resolution R' Eliezer responds with the seeds analogy — a kal vachomer from purity law that supports preemptive nullification.

Do the Rabbis Accept This Type of Kal VaChomer?

R' Eliezer's Kal VaChomer

  • Seeds sown → become pure
  • Already sown + impurity → all the more so pure
  • Vows nullified after → works
  • Vows nullified before → should also work

Rabbis' Similar Kal VaChomer

  • A sold maidservant goes free at na'arut
  • An unsold daughter — can she be sold as a na'arah?
  • Kal vachomer: if the sold one goes free, the unsold one certainly cannot be sold!
  • This proves the Rabbis DO use this type of reasoning

Why Rabbis Reject R' Eliezer Here

  • The verse states: 'Her husband may ratify or nullify'
  • Only what is eligible for ratification is eligible for nullification
  • A vow not yet taken cannot be ratified → cannot be nullified
  • The drasha overrides the kal vachomer
Principle An explicit verse (drasha) overrides a logical kal vachomer — the Rabbis agree with the reasoning in theory but reject it here due to the pasuk.

End of Day vs. 24 Hours (Amud B)

First Tanna
The verse says 'on the day he hears' — he can nullify only until the end of that calendar day
R' Yosei b. R' Yehuda
The verse says 'from day to day' (מִיּוֹם אֶל יוֹם) — this means a full 24-hour period from the time he heard
First Tanna
'From day to day' just teaches that nullification works at night too — not only during daytime hours
R' Yosei b. R' Yehuda
'On the day he hears' limits it to one day's worth — not a full week from Sunday to Sunday. But within that, it's 24 hours!
Each Verse Limits the Other 'On the day he hears' prevents extending beyond 24 hours. 'From day to day' prevents limiting to daytime only. The dispute is which verse sets the primary framework.
