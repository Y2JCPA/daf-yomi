What Counts as Affliction?

Rabbis (First Tanna)
Not bathing is affliction — the husband may nullify such a vow as it causes nivvul (disfigurement)
Rabbi Yosei
Not bathing is not affliction — she can simply refrain, and temporary lack of bathing does not constitute disfigurement
Core Question When does discomfort from not bathing rise to the level of innui (affliction) that a husband can nullify?

Structure of the Bathing Vow

She says: "The benefit of bathing is konam for me forever if I bathe"
↓
If She Bathes
Benefit of bathing becomes permanently forbidden
If She Doesn't Bathe
She suffers nivvul (disfigurement)
Dilemma Either path leads to harm — this is why the husband may nullify according to the Rabbis

Rabbi Yosei vs. the Rabbis on Disfigurement

Rabbis
Not bathing causes nivvul — the husband can nullify because this is a vow of affliction
R' Yosei
She can simply not bathe today — one day without bathing is not called disfigurement
Gemara
If so, the mishna should say: R' Yosei holds the condition can be fulfilled — not that it isn't affliction
Gemara (revised)
Rather, she said: konam forever if I bathe today — R' Yosei says skipping one day isn't nivvul
Key Distinction The debate hinges on whether temporary lack of bathing qualifies as disfigurement

Rav Yehuda's Vow-Oath Combination

📜 She vows: "Benefit of bathing is konam forever if I bathe today"
↓
✋ She also swears an oath: "I will not bathe today"
↓
🔒 The oath prevents her from bathing today
↓
⛓️ Since she doesn't bathe today, the vow's condition is met — bathing is forever forbidden
↓
🔑 The husband can nullify the vow, breaking the permanent prohibition
Ravina's Challenge If so, the mishna should say "vows and oaths" — Rav Ashi responds: oaths are included in the category of vows, as proven from Mishna 9a

Rava: Two Types of Affliction

Yom Kippur Affliction

  • Verse: "You shall afflict your souls" (Lev. 16:29)
  • Requires affliction felt immediately
  • Eating & drinking — felt right away
  • Bathing — not felt right away → no karet
  • Only eating/drinking/labor carry karet

Vow Affliction

  • Verse: "Every vow to afflict the soul" (Num. 30:14)
  • Includes anything that leads to affliction
  • Not bathing → eventually causes suffering
  • Broader standard than Yom Kippur
  • Husband may nullify such vows
Rava's Principle Yom Kippur requires immediate, felt affliction — vow law encompasses anything that eventually leads to affliction
