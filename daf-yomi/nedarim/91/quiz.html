QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara conclude that the mishna about 'I am defiled to you' must refer to a priest's wife who was raped?
A. Because only a priest's wife would make such a claim
B. Because an Israelite's wife raped is not forbidden, and a willing participant gets no ketubah — only a priest's wife raped fits both conditions
C. Because the Torah specifically addresses priests' wives in this context
D. Because Rava explicitly states this in his question
Correct: B — The Gemara eliminates other possibilities: an Israelite's wife who was raped is still permitted to her husband, and any wife who was willing loses her ketubah. Only a priest's wife who was raped is both forbidden (due to his priestly status) AND entitled to her ketubah.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the fundamental disagreement between Rav Hamnuna and Rava about a woman who claims 'You divorced me'?
A. Whether divorce requires witnesses
B. Whether the presumption that a woman won't lie to her husband's face outweighs the motivation to escape a difficult marriage
C. Whether a verbal divorce is valid
D. Whether the husband must respond to her claim
Correct: B — Rav Hamnuna holds that since the husband knows the truth, she wouldn't dare lie to his face (אינה מעיזה פניה). Rava counters that marital hardship (קשה לה תותיה דגברא) gives her sufficient motivation to be brazen.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rava explain that the woman claiming 'Heaven is between us' is believed, even though it doesn't involve self-demeaning?
A. Because the husband cannot know if she's lying
B. Because she must specify embarrassing intimate details ('doesn't shoot like an arrow'), which she wouldn't fabricate
C. Because the court always believes women in intimate matters
D. Because there are witnesses to support her claim
Correct: B — Rava argues that the woman must describe graphic intimate details about her husband's performance in court. The shame of making such specific claims publicly means she would only do so if telling the truth.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Rav Nahman rule that the women in both 'merchant' stories are unreliable?
A. Because merchants are known to be trustworthy
B. Because the women volunteered claims and quickly blamed specific men, suggesting they had already set their eyes on someone
C. Because the husbands denied the events and husbands are always believed
D. Because there were no witnesses to corroborate the women's stories
Correct: B — In both cases, the woman initiated a claim unprompted and immediately redirected blame to specific men (aloe merchants / oil merchants). This pattern suggests she already had someone in mind — she 'cast her eyes upon another man' — undermining her credibility.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Rava permit the wife in the 'snake and cress' story despite a man being found hiding in her home?
A. Because the wife denied knowing the man
B. Because an adulterer who had sinned would be suspected of murder and would have let the husband eat the poisoned food rather than save him
C. Because the man was a known Torah scholar
D. Because the snake venom was not actually lethal
Correct: B — Rava applies the principle from Ezekiel 23:37 — 'they committed adultery and blood is on their hands' — that an adulterer is suspected of murder. Since this man saved the husband's life by warning about the snake-tasted cress, he presumably had not yet sinned, and the woman remains permitted.
