Nedarim 66: Dissolving Vows & Preserving Peace

KEY PRINCIPLE Rabbi Akiva taught: A vow that is partially dissolved is entirely dissolved. The daf explores how courts find openings to dissolve vows — through Shabbat/Festivals, health exceptions, family honor, and personal dignity — and tells vivid stories of sages going to great lengths (or refusing to) to restore shalom bayit (marital peace).

Rabbi Akiva's Principle: Partial Dissolution = Full Dissolution

A person vows: "All of you are forbidden to me" (one statement covering a group)
↓
Benefit from one person in the group is permitted
↓
✅ Benefit from ALL of them is permitted
Neder she'hutar miktzato — hutar kulo
↓
But if he said individually:
"Not this one, and not that one"
Each is a separate vow — dissolving one does not dissolve the others
Middle one permitted?
From him and below = permitted
From him and above = forbidden
R' AKIVA A vow partially dissolved is dissolved entirely — but only when all prohibitions stem from a single vow statement.

Mistaken Vows: Wine & Onions

🍷 Wine Vow

  • "Wine is konam — it is bad for my intestines"
  • Court responds: But aged wine is good for intestines!
  • Vow dissolved for aged wine → dissolved for ALL wine
  • R' Abba: Even just "not harmful" suffices; "good" strengthens the claim

🧅 Onion Vow

  • "Onions are konam — they are bad for my heart"
  • Court responds: But kuferi onions are good for the heart!
  • Dissolved for kuferi → dissolved for ALL onions
  • R' Meir applied this ruling in an actual case
MECHANISM When the reason for a vow doesn't apply to one type, the entire category is dissolved — the stated reason reveals the vow was made in error.

Should a Sage Waive His Honor for Shalom Bayit?

The Vow
A man told his wife: "Benefiting from me is konam until you give R' Yehuda and R' Shimon your food to taste" — to humiliate her cooking
R' Yehuda
Tasted the food. Kal va'chomer: If the Torah allows God's Name to be erased for the sotah ceremony to bring peace between spouses — even when guilt is uncertain — surely I can waive my honor!
R' Shimon
Refused to taste. "Let all the widow's children die before Shimon budges!" People must not grow accustomed to manipulating sages through vows.
R' Shimon
Two reasons: (1) Preserve dignity of Torah scholars, (2) Discourage people from taking such vows in the future
TENSION Both positions are legitimate — one prioritizes immediate peace, the other long-term respect for Torah authority and discouraging vow abuse.

Dissolving Vows Through Honor & Family Reputation

Court's QuestionPurpose
"Did you know people will say: He always divorces his wives over vows?"Stigma to the husband — known as a serial divorcer
"They'll call your daughters 'daughters of divorce'"Stigma to his children — damaged marriage prospects
"People will ask: What did their mother do wrong?"Stigma to the wife — implied she was at fault
If he says: "Had I known, I would not have vowed"✅ The vow is dissolved — it was made without full awareness
MISHNA The court may broach dissolution by raising the issue of a person's own honor and the honor of his children — social consequences he hadn't considered.

The Babylonian & His Wife: Lost in Translation

🗣️ Husband says: "Cook me two lentils" (meaning 'some lentils') → She cooks exactly two lentils
↓
😤 Next day he says: "Cook me a se'a" (meaning 'a lot') → She cooks a full se'a measure — way too much
↓
🫤 He says: "Bring me two butzinei" (Babylonian = small gourds) → She brings two lamps (Eretz Yisrael meaning)
↓
😡 He says: "Break them on the head of the bava" (Babylonian = gate) → She breaks them on Bava ben Buta's head!
↓
🙏 Bava ben Buta blesses her: "You fulfilled your husband's wish — may God give you two sons like Bava ben Buta!"
LESSON A humorous but poignant tale about miscommunication in marriage — and the remarkable patience and grace of Bava ben Buta.
