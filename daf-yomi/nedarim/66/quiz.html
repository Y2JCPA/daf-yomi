Nedarim 66 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Akiva, what happens when a vow is partially dissolved?
A. Only the specific exception is permitted
B. The entire vow is dissolved
C. A new vow must be taken for the remainder
D. The court must dissolve each part separately
Correct: B — Rabbi Akiva's foundational principle: neder she'hutar miktzato, hutar kulo — a vow partially dissolved is entirely dissolved.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Shimon refuse to taste the woman's food, even though it would restore peace to her marriage?
A. He was fasting that day
B. He wanted to preserve scholarly dignity and discourage manipulative vows
C. The food was not kosher
D. He held that a sage may never waive his honor
Correct: B — R' Shimon gave two reasons: maintaining the dignity of Torah scholars, and preventing people from becoming accustomed to taking vows that exploit sages.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the wine vow case, what does R' Abba clarify about the Mishna's reasoning?
A. Aged wine must be proven medically beneficial
B. Even just showing the wine is 'not harmful' suffices — 'good' is an added argument
C. The vow only dissolves if the person actually drinks aged wine
D. Wine vows can never be dissolved
Correct: B — R' Abba explains the Mishna says 'and furthermore it is good' — meaning the primary argument is that the exception isn't harmful, and the benefit claim strengthens but isn't essential.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How did R' Yishmael son of R' Yosei dissolve the vow requiring the wife to show something 'beautiful' (yafeh)?
A. He declared her objectively beautiful
B. He ruled the vow was invalid from the start
C. He reinterpreted 'yafeh' as 'fitting' — her name Likhlukhit 'fit' her
D. He asked the husband to retract the vow
Correct: C — R' Yishmael creatively reinterpreted 'yafeh' (beautiful/fitting) — declaring it was 'yafeh' (fitting) that she was called Likhlukhit, since the name matched her appearance.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What caused the repeated misunderstandings between the Babylonian man and his Eretz Yisrael wife?
A. She was deliberately disobedient
B. They spoke different dialects of Aramaic with different word meanings
C. She could not cook properly
D. He spoke Hebrew and she spoke Aramaic
Correct: B — The comedic misunderstandings arose because the same Aramaic words (butzinei, bava) had different meanings in Babylonian and Eretz Yisrael dialects.
