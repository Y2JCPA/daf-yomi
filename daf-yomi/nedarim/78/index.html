Nullification vs. Dissolution: Who Has Which Power?

Torah states: "Zeh HaDavar" (This is the thing) regarding vows
↓
Husband (Ba'al)
Nullifies (Meifer) vows
Cannot dissolve them
Halakhic Authority (Chakham)
Dissolves (Matir) vows
Cannot nullify them
Key Principle "Zeh HaDavar" teaches exclusivity — each authority has its own distinct power over vows, and neither can exercise the other's role.
3 / 9

Hafarah vs. Hatarah: Two Distinct Powers

Husband — Hafarah (Nullification)

  • Can nullify (meifer) his wife's vows
  • Cannot dissolve (matir) vows
  • Power is time-limited (same day he hears)
  • Derived from "Zeh HaDavar"

Chakham — Hatarah (Dissolution)

  • Can dissolve (matir) vows retroactively
  • Cannot nullify (meifer) vows
  • No time limit once petitioned
  • Also derived from "Zeh HaDavar"
Why the Distinction? One might think: if a husband who cannot dissolve can still nullify, then surely a sage who can dissolve should also nullify! "Zeh HaDavar" excludes this logic.

Gezeira Shava: Linking Vows to Shechutei Chutz

"Zeh HaDavar" appears in Vows (Num. 30:2)
↓
"Zeh HaDavar" also appears in Slaughtering Outside the Temple (Lev. 17:2)
↓
Gezeira shava connects the two passages
↓
Vows → Shechutei Chutz
"Heads of the tribes" applies to slaughtering outside too → She'elah on hekdesh is possible
Shechutei Chutz → Vows
"Aaron, his sons, and all Israel" applies to vows → Three laymen can dissolve vows
Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov The gezeira shava is the source for authorizing three ordinary laymen to dissolve vows — derived from "all the children of Israel."

Who Can Dissolve Vows: Expert or Laymen?

Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov
The gezeira shava teaches that three laymen can dissolve vows — just as "all of Israel" is mentioned regarding offerings.
Gemara
But it says "heads of the tribes" — implying leaders, not ordinary people!
Rav Ḥisda / R' Yoḥanan
"Heads of the tribes" teaches a different halakha: a single expert (yachid mumcheh) can dissolve vows by himself.
Gemara
So we have two options: one expert alone, or three laymen together.
Practical Halakha Vows can be dissolved either by one expert sage or by a panel of three laymen — both are valid.

Ben Azzai's Teaching: Beit Shammai's Source

📖 "Moses declared the Festivals of the Lord to Israel" (Lev. 23:44)
↓
🗓️ R' Yosei HaGelili: Festivals are stated — but Shabbat is not stated with them
↓
📜 Ben Azzai: Festivals are stated — but the portion on vows is not stated with them
↓
❓ Rav Asi bar Natan objects: But vows are adjacent to the Festival portions!
↓
💡 Rav Sheshet explains: Festivals require expert judges (for the calendar), but vows do not require experts
↓
✅ Conclusion: Three laymen can dissolve vows — this is Beit Shammai's source
Rav Sheshet's Insight The juxtaposition teaches by contrast: Festivals need expert sanctification, but vows can be handled by ordinary people.
