Nedarim 78 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, what does the phrase "Zeh HaDavar" teach about the husband's power over vows?
A. He can both nullify and dissolve vows
B. He can nullify but cannot dissolve vows
C. He can dissolve but cannot nullify vows
D. He has no power over vows at all
Correct: B — "Zeh HaDavar" teaches exclusivity: the husband can nullify (meifer) vows but cannot dissolve (matir) them — dissolution is the sage's domain.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the source for three laymen being able to dissolve vows, according to Beit Hillel?
A. An explicit verse in Numbers 30
B. A gezeira shava linking vows to slaughtering offerings outside the Temple
C. A tradition from Moses at Sinai
D. A kal va'chomer from the husband's power
Correct: B — The gezeira shava connects "Zeh HaDavar" in the vows passage to the same phrase in Leviticus 17:2 about offerings, which addresses "Aaron, his sons, and all Israel" — teaching that even ordinary Israelites (laymen) can participate in dissolving vows.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rav Ḥisda (or R' Yoḥanan) derive from the phrase "the heads of the tribes"?
A. Only tribal leaders can dissolve vows
B. Vows require a court of 23 judges
C. A single expert can dissolve vows alone
D. The husband must be from a prominent family
Correct: C — "Heads of the tribes" — using the singular sense of leadership — teaches that a single expert (yachid mumcheh) can dissolve vows by himself, without needing a panel of three.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Ḥanina, a husband who is silent "to annoy" (lemeikat) his wife can nullify her vow for how long?
A. Only on the same day he hears it
B. Up to three days later
C. Up to ten days later
D. Indefinitely
Correct: C — R' Ḥanina rules that when the husband's silence is specifically intended to annoy — not to sustain the vow — he retains the ability to nullify for up to ten days.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why do Beit Shammai need an alternative source for three laymen dissolving vows?
A. They hold that only experts can dissolve vows
B. They reject the gezeira shava between vows and shechutei chutz
C. They believe the husband can also dissolve vows
D. They hold that vows cannot be dissolved at all
Correct: B — Beit Shammai reject the gezeira shava because they hold there is no she'elah (petition for dissolution) on consecrated property — so the analogy between vows and shechutei chutz doesn't work for them. They instead derive the halakha from Ben Azzai's teaching about the Festivals.
