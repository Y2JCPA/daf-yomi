Can a Husband Nullify His Wife's Vows Before She Makes Them?

Mishna
If a husband says 'All vows you make until I return are ratified' — it has no effect.
Mishna
But if he says 'All vows you make until I return are nullified' — this is disputed.
R' Eliezer
They are nullified. If he can nullify vows already in effect, surely he can nullify those not yet in effect (kal va'chomer).
Rabbis
They are not nullified. Only a vow eligible for ratification (already made) is eligible for nullification (Numbers 30:14).
Core Dispute Does a husband's power to nullify vows extend to vows his wife has not yet made? R' Eliezer says yes via kal va'chomer; the Rabbis derive from the Torah that nullification requires an existing vow.

Pre-Emptive Ratification vs. Pre-Emptive Nullification

Pre-Emptive Ratification

  • Husband says: 'All future vows are ratified'
  • All agree: Has NO effect
  • Ratification cannot apply to non-existent vows
  • No dissenting opinion recorded

Pre-Emptive Nullification

  • Husband says: 'All future vows are nullified'
  • R' Eliezer: They ARE nullified
  • Rabbis: They are NOT nullified
  • Derives from Numbers 30:14 — nullification requires ratification eligibility
Asymmetry Everyone agrees pre-emptive ratification fails. The dispute is only about pre-emptive nullification — R' Eliezer sees nullification as more powerful than ratification.

Rabbi Eliezer's Kal Va'Chomer and the Rabbis' Response

R' Eliezer
If a husband can nullify vows that already reached the status of a prohibition (already in effect)...
R' Eliezer
...surely he can nullify vows that have not yet reached the status of a prohibition!
Rabbis
The verse states: 'Her husband may ratify it, or her husband may nullify it' (Numbers 30:14).
Rabbis
Only what is eligible for ratification (an existing vow) is eligible for nullification.
Rabbis
A vow not yet made cannot be ratified — therefore it cannot be nullified either.
Hermeneutic Principle The Rabbis link ratification and nullification through the juxtaposition in the verse (hekesh) — the scope of one defines the scope of the other.

Dilemma: Does the vow take effect even momentarily?
Takes effect momentarily
If another person associated (talah) his vow with hers → his vow takes effect
Never takes effect at all
If another person associated his vow with hers → his vow has no substance
Practical Difference The nafka mina (practical difference) is when a third party says 'I vow like her' — does his vow stand if hers was pre-emptively nullified?

Attempts to Resolve R' Eliezer's View

📖 Proof from Mishna: R' Eliezer says 'not yet reached the status of a prohibition' → vows never take effect?
↓
❌ Rejected: 'Have not reached' could mean 'not yet' — they may still take effect momentarily
↓
📖 Proof from Tosefta: R' Eliezer compares to one who stipulates his own vows won't take effect → his vows never take effect at all
↓
❌ Rejected: The analogy supports the kal va'chomer but the cases need not be identical in all respects
↓
📖 Proof from Rabbis' mikveh objection: Their rebuttal assumes R' Eliezer holds vows never take effect at all
↓
✅ This proof stands — the Rabbis' analogy only works if R' Eliezer holds vows do not take effect at all
Resolution The Rabbis' mikveh analogy proves that according to R' Eliezer, pre-emptively nullified vows never take effect at all.
