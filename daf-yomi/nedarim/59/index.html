Can Planting Neutralize a Prohibition?

R' Yannai
A teruma onion that was planted — if its growths exceeded the original, it is permitted.
Rami bar Ḥama
But the Mishna says growths of konamot are forbidden when seeds don't cease — so growths do NOT neutralize!
R' Abba
Konamot are different — since one can request dissolution, they have the status of davar sheyesh lo matirin and are never nullified.
Gemara
But teruma can also be dissolved, yet it IS nullified in 1:100!
Gemara
Resolution: With konamot there is a mitzva to dissolve them (R' Natan), but with teruma there is no such mitzva.
Key Principle An item whose prohibition can and should be dissolved (like konamot) is never nullified by majority — unlike teruma, where dissolution is possible but not obligatory.

Konamot vs. Teruma: Nullification by Majority

Konamot (Vow-Prohibitions)

  • One CAN request dissolution by a Sage
  • There is a MITZVA to dissolve vows (R' Natan)
  • Status: Davar sheyesh lo matirin
  • Therefore: NOT nullified by majority

Teruma (Priestly Tithe)

  • One CAN request dissolution of designation
  • There is NO mitzva to dissolve teruma
  • Status: NOT davar sheyesh lo matirin
  • Therefore: Nullified in ratio of 1:100
R' Natan "Anyone who vows, it is as if he built a personal altar outside the Temple; and one who fulfills that vow, it is as though he burns an offering upon it."

Tithed Onions Sowed: Tithe the Whole Crop?

R' Yoḥanan
A litra of tithed onions that one sowed — one must tithe the entire crop, including the original litra's worth.
Rav Ḥisda
Who listens to you? The original litra was already permitted — where did it go? It should be subtracted from the titheable crop!
Rabba
But the Mishna says sixth-year onions that sprouted in Shevi'it — if their leaves are black, they are forbidden. The permitted part wasn't subtracted there either!
Rav Ḥisda
That mishna refers to the additional growth only, not to the original onion itself!
Dispute The core question: does the permitted identity of the original planted produce persist in the crop that grows from it, or is it absorbed into the new growth?

Exertion vs. Passive Growth: Does It Matter?

🌱 Forbidden produce sprouts on its own (no exertion)
↓
❌ Original prohibition is NOT neutralized by the growth (R"ShBG)
↓
👨‍🌾 Person actively sows or plants the forbidden produce
↓
✅ Original prohibition IS neutralized by the majority growth
↓
⚠️ Exception: Untithed produce sowed — penalty: must still tithe the original portion
Penalty The verse says "you shall tithe all the produce of your seed" — people normally sow tithed seeds. One who sows untithed seeds is penalized: the original portion is NOT neutralized.
