Nedarim 57: Vow Language & the Onion Growth Dilemma

Mishna: How does the exact wording of a vow affect what is forbidden?
↓
"Konam UPON me"
Produce, replacements, and growths are forbidden
"Konam FOR me"
Only produce forbidden; replacements & growths permitted
↓
Exception: Items whose seed doesn't cease (e.g. onions) — even growths of growths are forbidden
↓
Gemara: Sabbatical-Year onion planted in 8th year — do permitted growths neutralize the prohibition?
Core Theme The precision of vow language determines the scope of prohibition, and the biological nature of the item (does its seed persist?) creates critical exceptions.

Two Vow Formulations: Drastically Different Results

"Konam UPON Me / Upon My Mouth"

  • The produce itself is forbidden
  • Its replacements (חליפין) are forbidden
  • Anything that grows from it is forbidden
  • Stricter formulation — treats item as intrinsically prohibited

"Konam FOR Me — I Won't Eat/Taste"

  • The produce itself is forbidden
  • Its replacements are permitted
  • Anything that grows from it is permitted
  • Lenient formulation — only a personal eating restriction
Key Distinction "Upon me" treats the item as having inherent prohibition status. "For me, I won't eat" is merely a personal commitment not to consume — the item itself is not transformed.

Seeds That Cease vs. Seeds That Don't Cease

Person vowed using the lenient formula: "Konam for me, I won't eat"
↓
Does the seed cease to exist after planting?
Yes — Seed Ceases (e.g. wheat, barley)
Growths are permitted
The original item no longer exists
No — Seed Persists (e.g. onions, bulbs)
Even growths of growths are forbidden
The original prohibited item remains intact
דבר שזרעו כלה This biological distinction — whether the seed is consumed in producing the new plant or persists through growth cycles — is the halachic pivot point.

The Sabbatical-Year Onion Dilemma

Yishmael of Kefar Yamma
An onion uprooted in the Sabbatical Year, then planted in the 8th year — its growths exceeded the original. Do the permitted growths neutralize the prohibited principal?
R' Ami
I don't have a ready answer for this...
R' Yitzchak Nappacha
Yes! R' Chanina Terita'a said in the name of R' Yannai: A teruma onion planted — if growths exceed the principal, it is permitted. Same applies here!
R' Yirmeya
Master! You are abandoning the opinion of two Sages to follow one?
The Challenge R' Yirmeya objects: both R' Yochanan (re: orla) and R' Yonatan (re: kilayim) hold that prohibited items are NOT neutralized by excess growth. Why follow R' Yannai alone?

Three Opinions: Do Growths Neutralize the Original Prohibition?

R' Yannai (Lenient)

  • Case: Teruma onion planted
  • Growths exceeded principal
  • Ruling: Permitted
  • Growths neutralize the prohibition

R' Yochanan (Strict)

  • Case: Orla vine grafted onto old vine
  • Even if new fruit is 200× the original
  • Ruling: Original fruit forbidden
  • "It was there from the outset" — not neutralized

R' Yonatan (Strict)

  • Case: Onion planted in vineyard (kilayim)
  • Vineyard uprooted, most growth is permitted
  • Ruling: Still forbidden
  • Prohibited principal retains its status
Score Two Sages (R' Yochanan & R' Yonatan) say growths do NOT neutralize. One Sage (R' Yannai) says they do. The dilemma remains unresolved.
