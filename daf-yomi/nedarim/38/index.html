Nedarim 38: Moses, Torah & the Limits of Vows

DAF OVERVIEW Amud A explores Moses's unique qualities — his wealth from the Tablets' gem waste, the question of whether Torah was exclusively his gift to give, and the four traits required for Divine Presence.

Amud B returns to nedarim halakhot: marrying one's daughter to someone bound by a vow, permissible minor benefits for a son, the kos shel shalom, and rules for visiting and healing.

Moses's Wealth from the Tablets

🪨 God commands Moses: "Hew for you (פְּסָל לְךָ)" two tablets of stone
↓
💎 The Tablets were made of precious gems (sapphire)
↓
✂️ "For you" — the waste from hewing belongs to you
↓
💰 R' Ḥama b. R' Ḥanina: Moses became wealthy from this waste alone
KEY DERIVATION The word לְךָ (for you) in "Hew for you" teaches that the valuable remnants of the sapphire Tablets belonged to Moses.

Was Torah Given Exclusively to Moses?

R' Yosei b. R' Ḥanina
Torah was given only to Moses and his descendants — "Write for you" means the writing is yours, just as the Tablets' waste is yours.
R' Yosei b. R' Ḥanina
But Moses acted with generosity (tovat ayin) and gave it to all Israel.
Rav Ḥisda
Objection: "The Lord commanded me to teach you" — Moses was commanded to teach Israel from the start!
Gemara
That verse means: God commanded Torah to me, and I chose to teach you on my own.
Gemara
But "write this song and teach it to the children of Israel" — doesn't that prove a command to teach?
Gemara
That refers only to the song of Ha'azinu, not the whole Torah.
RESOLUTION The Torah text itself was given to all Israel from the outset. R' Yosei b. R' Ḥanina meant only the profound analysis (pilpul) was exclusive to Moses, which he generously chose to share.

All Prophets Were Wealthy — The Proofs

Moses

  • "I have not taken one donkey" — not even with payment
  • Gem waste from the Tablets: "Hew for you"

Samuel

  • "Whose ox have I taken, whose donkey?"
  • Traveled with his own donkey — he was self-sufficient

Amos

  • Owner of flocks and sycamore trees
  • Prophesied by choice, not financial need

Jonah

  • Paid full fare for a ship to Tarshish
  • Could afford costly sea passage on his own
R' YOḤANAN Wealth ensured prophets were independent — their prophecy was never motivated by material need.

Marrying One's Daughter to a Mudar Hana'ah

A vows: benefit from B is forbidden
↓
Can B marry his daughter to A?
Father's property forbidden to groom
Father provides a maidservant → groom benefits → ❌ Prohibited
Groom's property forbidden to father
Groom supports bride → father benefits? Already taught this is permitted → Not novel
↓
Resolution: Daughter is a bogeret (grown woman) who consents on her own — benefit comes from her, not her father
RAV HUNA'S RULING One bound by a vow may marry the other's daughter — provided she is a grown woman who consents independently.
