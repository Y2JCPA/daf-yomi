Nedarim 49 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, one who vows 'cooked foods are forbidden to me' — what may he still eat?
A. Only raw foods
B. Roasted and boiled foods
C. Only thick cooked foods
D. Nothing cooked in any manner
Correct: B — The Mishna states he is permitted roasted and boiled foods, since they are not defined as 'cooked' in common parlance.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the dispute between R' Yoshiya and the Tanna of the Mishna?
A. R' Yoshiya follows Torah language, the Tanna follows people's language
B. They disagree about the authority of Chronicles
C. Both follow people's language — their locales used 'cooking' differently
D. R' Yoshiya's view is rejected entirely
Correct: C — The Gemara says both follow the language of people (lashon bnei adam), but in R' Yoshiya's locale, even roasting was called 'cooking.'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Abaye's criterion for determining what qualifies as a 'tavshil' (cooked food) regarding vows?
A. Anything prepared with heat
B. Anything cooked in a pot with liquid
C. Anything eaten as accompaniment to bread
D. Anything served at a formal meal
Correct: C — Abaye says a 'tavshil' is something eaten with bread. A thick dish eaten on its own doesn't qualify, which is why thick cooked food is permitted.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does R' Zeira say the Babylonians' custom affects the halacha of vows on cooked food?
A. They use Torah language rather than common language
B. They eat thick porridge with bread, so even thick dishes count as 'cooked food'
C. They have a tradition from Moshe at Sinai about cooking definitions
D. Their rabbis ruled more strictly on all vows
Correct: B — R' Zeira says Babylonians 'eat bread with bread' — thick porridge with bread. Since they eat it with bread, it qualifies as a tavshil there, broadening the vow's scope.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When a Roman matron accused R' Yehuda of being a drunk, what did he attribute his ruddy complexion to?
A. Eating beets without salt
B. Having 24 bathrooms on his route
C. The verse 'A man's wisdom makes his face shine'
D. Drinking wine only for mitzvot
Correct: C — R' Yehuda denied drinking wine (except for kiddush, havdala, and the four cups) and cited Ecclesiastes 8:1: 'A man's wisdom makes his face to shine.'
