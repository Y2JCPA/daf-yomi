Nedarim 22: Methods of Dissolving Vows & The Sin of Anger

A person takes a vow and seeks dissolution
↓
A Sage must find a valid opening (petaḥ) to dissolve it
↓
❌ Invalid Methods
Heavenly record book • Sword metaphor • 'Vowing = forbidden altar' • 'Vower is wicked'
✅ Valid Method
Broaching based on regret (Rava/Rav Naḥman)
Key Theme The Gemara systematically rejects multiple dramatic methods of dissolving vows, concluding that simple regret is the proper basis.
3 / 9

R' Yannai's Method: The Heavenly Record Book

R' Yannai the Elder
Had you known that when you vow, they open your pinekas (record book) in heaven and examine your actions — would you have vowed?
His Grandson
No! I would not have vowed.
R' Abba
The source: 'And after vows to make inquiry' (Proverbs 20:25) — after one vows, his actions are reviewed in heaven.
Gemara
We do not broach dissolution this way — a person might be embarrassed to admit he has no regret and will lie.
⚠️ Rejected Although R' Yannai used this method successfully, it is not an acceptable model because it pressures the vower into false regret.

Abaye vs. Rava: Can We Use R' Natan's Teaching?

R' Natan
One who vows is as if he built a personal altar. One who fulfills the vow is as if he sacrificed on it.
Rav Kahana's Version
First clause (vowing = altar): Abaye permits broaching; Rava forbids. Latter clause (fulfilling = sacrifice): Abaye permits; Rava forbids.
Rav Tavyumei's Version
Latter clause: All agree we do NOT broach. First clause: Abaye permits; Rava forbids.
Gemara Conclusion
Halakha: We do not broach dissolution with either clause.
Key Dispute Both versions of the dispute agree Rava is stricter. The halakha follows Rava — no dramatic statements about the severity of vows may be used to dissolve them.

The Consequences of Anger

🔥 R' Shmuel bar Naḥmani: One who gets angry — all kinds of Gehenna rule over him (Ecclesiastes 11:10)
↓
🤕 Additionally, hemorrhoids afflict the angry person (Deuteronomy 28:65)
↓
😤 Rabba bar Rav Huna: The angry person treats even the Divine Presence as unimportant (Psalms 10:4)
↓
📚 R' Yirmiya of Difti: Anger causes one to forget his learning and increase foolishness (Ecclesiastes 7:9)
↓
⚖️ Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak: The angry person's sins outnumber his merits (Proverbs 29:22)
↓
📖 Rav Adda: Had Israel not sinned, only Torah + Joshua would have been given — the rest of Tanakh came from angering God
Connection This section on anger connects to the theme of vows — both anger and vowing are expressions of losing control that lead to spiritual harm.

Vows Invoking God's Name

R' Asi's Strict View

  • A vow invoking 'the God of Israel' cannot be dissolved
  • Exception: vow made in clear error (e.g. 'she stole my purse' — and she didn't)
  • Told a woman: Had you said 'by mohi' (substitute name), I would dissolve it — but 'by the God of Israel' I cannot

Rava / Rav Naḥman's Ruling

  • Halakha: It IS permitted to broach dissolution based on regret
  • One DOES attend to dissolving vows invoking God's name
  • This overrides R' Asi's stricter position
Halakha Rava said in the name of Rav Naḥman: We may broach dissolution based on regret, and we do dissolve vows even when God's name is invoked.
