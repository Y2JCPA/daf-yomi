Nedarim 22 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject R' Yannai's method of telling a vower that his heavenly record book will be examined?
A. The vower might be embarrassed to say he has no regret and will lie
B. It reveals heavenly secrets that should remain hidden
C. R' Yannai did not have the authority to dissolve vows
D. The verse in Proverbs does not actually support this interpretation
Correct: A — The Gemara explains that upon hearing such a frightening statement, a person might be too embarrassed to admit he doesn't regret his vow, and will falsely claim regret — making the dissolution invalid.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Natan, what is a person who vows compared to?
A. One who worships idols
B. One who builds a personal altar outside the Temple
C. One who desecrates Shabbat
D. One who takes God's name in vain
Correct: B — R' Natan teaches that one who vows is as if he built a bamah (personal altar) outside the Temple, which is prohibited. One who fulfills the vow is as if he sacrificed an offering on it.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the final halakha regarding how a Sage may broach dissolution of a vow?
A. Only by citing the verse about the sword (Proverbs 12:18)
B. By telling the vower he is called wicked
C. Based on the vower's regret
D. Only if the vow was made in error
Correct: C — Rava said in the name of Rav Naḥman: The halakha is that it is permitted to broach dissolution based on regret. All the dramatic methods (heavenly record, sword, altar, wicked) are rejected.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Yirmiya of Difti, what specific consequence does anger cause?
A. Physical illness and hemorrhoids
B. Gehenna and spiritual punishment
C. Forgetting one's learning and increasing foolishness
D. The Divine Presence departing from him
Correct: C — R' Yirmiya of Difti said that one who gets angry forgets his learning and increases foolishness, based on 'Anger rests in the bosom of fools' (Ecclesiastes 7:9) and 'A fool unfolds folly' (Proverbs 13:16).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How did Rav Seḥora ultimately find an opening to dissolve his own vow?
A. He realized the vow was made in error about a factual matter
B. He cited Shmuel's teaching that one who vows is called wicked
C. Rav Naḥman's anger at him meant the vow no longer brought 'glory from fellow men' per the Mishnah in Avot
D. He waited until Rav Naḥman calmed down and tried again
Correct: C — Rav Seḥora used R' Yehuda HaNasi's teaching from Avot 2:1 — choose a path that brings glory from fellow men. Since Rav Naḥman was upset with him, the vow now caused the opposite of glory, which was an unforeseen consequence he could use as an opening for dissolution.
