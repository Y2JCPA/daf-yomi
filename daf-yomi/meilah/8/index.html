⚖️ The Great Griddle-Cake Debate

Rabbi Yochanan
The chavitin (High Priest's griddle-cake) is NOT sanctified in halves — it must be brought as a whole before dividing.
Rabbi Elazar
Since the chavitin is sacrificed in halves (half in the morning, half at dusk), it follows that it IS sanctified in halves — each half stands on its own.
Mishna (50b) — Objection
The mishna states the High Priest brings a full tenth of an ephah and only then divides it — implying it must come whole!
Rabbi Elazar — Reply
The mishna describes only the l'chatchila ideal. B'dieved (after the fact), half a tenth brought in the morning is valid. Rav Giddel confirms.
Core Question Can a sacrificial item acquire sanctity in a partial or divided state, or must it be complete at the moment of consecration?

🔢 Half-Tenth Sanctification — Who Holds What?

A priest sets aside half a tenth of an ephah as a meal offering, intending to add more later
↓
Rav
Not sanctified — a meal offering requires a full tenth; partial measure does not achieve holiness
Rabbi Yochanan
Sanctified — derives from the chavitin that halves can be consecrated, and extends this to all meal offerings
↓
Gemara resolves: Yochanan learns from chavitin that all offerings can be sanctified in halves. Rav disputes this derivation — but Rav does derive analogously, as shown from his ruling on oil and frankincense.

🏺 Can Individual Meal Offering Components Be Sanctified Alone?

Rav

  • ✅ Flour alone is sanctified by a service vessel
  • ✅ Oil alone is sanctified by a service vessel
  • ✅ Frankincense alone is sanctified by a service vessel
  • 📜 Flour proof: shewbread — sanctified in vessel, no oil
  • 📜 Oil proof: leper's log of oil — sanctified alone
  • 📜 Frankincense proof: bowls on shewbread

Rabbi Ḥanina

  • ❌ Flour is NOT sanctified without oil
  • ❌ Oil is NOT sanctified without flour
  • 🔗 All components must be together in the vessel simultaneously
  • ❓ Challenge: Why were the measuring vessels for flour and oil anointed if they cannot sanctify anything alone?
  • 💡 Answer: The tenth-vessel was anointed for measuring; the log-vessel for the leper's oil — but neither sanctifies independently

🤲 Kometz Removed Inside the Sanctuary — Valid?

Rabbi Elazar
If a priest removed the kometz (handful) while inside the Sanctuary rather than the courtyard — it is valid. The Sanctuary is holier, so a fortiori removal there should be acceptable.
Rabbi Yirmeya — Objection
Baraita: The verse states 'he shall bring it to Aaron's sons the priests and remove from there his handful' (Lev. 2:2) — 'from there' implies the courtyard, excluding the Sanctuary!
Resolution (Rabbi Ya'akov)
The verse is needed only to permit removal anywhere in the entire courtyard — even distant corners. The verse does not exclude the Sanctuary. A kal v'chomer from other offerings confirms validity.
Key Verse — Leviticus 2:2 וְהֵבִיא אֶל בְּנֵי אַהֲרֹן הַכֹּהֲנִים וְקָמַץ — "And he shall bring it to Aaron's sons the priests, and he shall remove his handful from there"

🕌 Peace Offerings Slaughtered in the Sanctuary — Rabbi Yochanan's Ruling

The Ruling (Rabbi Yochanan): Peace offerings (shelamim) slaughtered inside the Sanctuary rather than the courtyard are valid
↓
Verse: Leviticus 3:2 says 'slaughter it at the entrance of the Tent of Meeting' — 'entrance' is not a precise limit; the entire sacred complex may be implied
↓
Kal v'chomer: If burnt offerings (olot) — slaughtered in the courtyard — are valid, and the Sanctuary is holier, then peace offerings slaughtered there are certainly valid
↓
Gemara's use of this ruling: Cited as proof that Rabbi Yochanan does derive halakha from analogous cases — meaning he can derive meal offering laws from the chavitin
↓
Baraita objection: Rabbi Yehuda ben Beteira raises a case where enemies surround the courtyard — may one then slaughter inside the Sanctuary? Suggests Yochanan's ruling is not self-evident
