Me'ilah 8 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Who holds that the High Priest's griddle-cake offering (chavitin) is NOT sanctified in halves and must be brought whole?
A. Rabbi Elazar
B. Rabbi Yochanan
C. Rav
D. Shmuel
Correct: B — Rabbi Yochanan holds the chavitin is not sanctified in halves — it must be brought as a whole tenth of an ephah before being divided. Rabbi Elazar disagrees, holding that since it is ultimately offered in halves, it is sanctified in halves.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
If a priest sets aside exactly half a tenth of an ephah for a standard meal offering, intending to add more later, what does Rav rule?
A. It is not sanctified — a meal offering requires a full tenth
B. It is sanctified, since the intention to complete it is legally sufficient
C. It is sanctified only if oil is also placed in the vessel
D. It depends on which service vessel was used
Correct: A — Rav holds that a half-tenth set aside for a meal offering is not sanctified. Even if one intends to add the rest, the partial amount cannot achieve sanctified status — a full tenth is required.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ḥanina, what is required for a service vessel to sanctify a meal offering?
A. Only the flour needs to be placed in the vessel first
B. Oil alone, placed in an anointed vessel, is sufficient
C. All components — flour, oil, and frankincense — must be present together in the vessel
D. The vessel must be designated by the priest before use
Correct: C — Rabbi Ḥanina holds that no individual component can be sanctified separately. Flour without oil, or oil without flour, is not sanctified — all components must be together in the service vessel simultaneously.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rabbi Elazar rules that removing the kometz (handful) inside the Sanctuary is valid. What is the basis for this leniency?
A. A verse explicitly permits it when the courtyard is crowded
B. The Sanctuary is holier than the courtyard, so by kal v'chomer removal there is certainly valid
C. A baraita rules it valid when done unintentionally
D. The same logic that validates a left-handed removal validates an interior removal
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar reasons a fortiori (kal v'chomer): if kometz removal is valid in the courtyard, it is certainly valid in the Sanctuary, which has a higher degree of holiness. The verse's mention of 'from there' was needed only to permit the entire courtyard.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara cite Rabbi Yochanan's ruling about peace offerings slaughtered in the Sanctuary?
A. To prove that slaughter in the Sanctuary is valid for all offerings
B. To demonstrate that the Sanctuary is holier than the courtyard
C. To show that Rabbi Yochanan does make halakhic derivations from analogous cases
D. To dispute Rabbi Elazar's ruling about the kometz
Correct: C — The Gemara invokes this ruling to prove that Rabbi Yochanan does in fact derive halakha from analogous cases (gezerah shavah / analogy). This counters the suggestion that Yochanan's position on meal offerings cannot be derived from the chavitin.
