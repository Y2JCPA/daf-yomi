🏺 The Four Avoda Stages and the Law of Piggul

Stage 1 — Kemitza (קְמִיצָה): The priest removes a handful from the meal offering. Improper intent here triggers piggul if the handful is later burned.
↓
Stage 2 — Netina (נְתִינָה): The handful is placed into the sacred service vessel. Improper intent here is equally effective.
↓
Stage 3 — Holakha (הֲלָכָה): The vessel with the handful is conveyed to the altar. Intent here also suffices.
↓
Stage 4 — Haktara (הַקְטָרָה): The handful is burned on the altar — the permitting factor is completed. This stage alone can generate piggul.
↓
⚠️ Special rule: The meal offering of a sinner and the sota's offering — if removed not for their sake but the remainder is eaten with proper intent — the offering is neither piggul nor disqualified, merely not fulfilling its specific mitzva.
↓
🔑 The permitting factor principle: For piggul to apply, the handful/frankincense must be burned — even with improper intent. Without any burning, no piggul can take effect.

⚖️ Rav Huna vs. Rava: Does Piggul Apply to a Lacking Remainder?

Gemara Dilemma
If the remainder became lacking in measure (less than an olive-bulk) between the removal and the burning of the handful — can the burning of the handful still render that remainder piggul and remove it from the laws of misuse of consecrated property?
Rav Huna
Even per Rabbi Akiva who holds that sprinkling works for meat that left the courtyard: the meat remains intact — an external factor disqualified it. But a lacking remainder is intrinsically deficient. Therefore burning the handful is NOT effective for it — it cannot become piggul.
Rava
On the contrary! Even per Rabbi Eliezer who holds that sprinkling does NOT work for meat that left: that is because the handful was burned after the meat left. But if the handful is burned while the remainder is still present — even if lacking in measure — the burning is fully effective and piggul applies!
Rava
Proof: Rabbi Ḥiyya's mishna omits 'an olive-bulk' when describing the remainder — because it discusses a case where the remainder became lacking. Yet piggul still applies! This proves lacking remainders can become piggul.
Abaye's Challenge Abaye rejects Rava's proof: Rabbi Ḥiyya's mishna follows Rabbi Elazar (who requires a full handful for liability) — not because the remainder was lacking. Therefore the proof collapses. Rava concedes the proof but maintains his ruling independently.

📜 Three Outcomes: Valid, Pasul, or Piggul

✅ Valid Offering

  • All four stages performed in silence
  • No specific improper intent at any stage
  • Remainder is permitted for consumption by the priests
  • Also: sinner/sota offering removed not for its sake but eaten properly → valid

❌ Pasul (Disqualified)

  • Intent to eat remainder or burn handful outside the designated area
  • The permitting factor was NOT sacrificed according to its mitzva
  • Remainder is forbidden but eating it does NOT incur karet
  • Also: area intent precedes time intent (Rabbi Yehuda)

☠️ Piggul (פִּגּוּל)

  • Intent to eat the remainder or burn the handful beyond its designated time
  • The permitting factor WAS burned (even with improper intent)
  • Eating the remainder incurs karet
  • Rabbi Yehuda: time intent must precede area intent for full piggul liability

🫒 New Mishna: Eating and Burning Do Not Combine

אֲכִילָה וְהַקְטָרָה אֵינָן מִצְטָרְפִין
Mishna — Menachot 12b If one's intent was to partake of half an olive-bulk of the remainder AND to burn half an olive-bulk of it outside its designated time or area — the offering is fit. The reason: eating (by a person) and burning (on the altar) are distinct modes of consumption and do not join together to form a full olive-bulk. Rabbi Yirmeya: This mishna follows Rabbi Eliezer, who holds that human consumption and altar consumption can combine for piggul — and therefore explicitly teaches they do not combine here for the half-measure. Abaye: Even the Rabbis agree in this case — do not infer that ½ olive-bulk eating + ½ olive-bulk eating would combine. They do not combine either, since this combination is never taught.
