QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, under what condition do service vessels sanctify items placed inside them?
A. Whenever any item is placed inside them, automatically
B. Only when the items are placed there with specific intent to be sanctified
C. Only when the vessel is raised above the ground
D. Only when a Kohen Gadol performs the service
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan teaches that service vessels sanctify only with specific intent. Because the priest returning the handful does not intend for it to be re-sanctified, the vessel does not sanctify it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did the senior Amora Avimi come to the study hall of Rav Ḥisda, who was actually Avimi's own student?
A. To publicly challenge Rav Ḥisda's rulings on meal offerings
B. To teach Rav Ḥisda the laws of the service vessels
C. Tractate Menaḥot had been forgotten by Avimi and he came to review it
D. Rav Ḥisda had sent for him to adjudicate a dispute
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that tractate Menaḥot was 'uprooted' from Avimi — he forgot it — so he came before his own student Rav Ḥisda to help recall his learning, reversing their teacher-student roles.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman's count, how many priests does Avimi's method of performing the meal offering service require?
A. One priest performing all steps sequentially
B. Two priests dividing the labor
C. Three priests — one to raise the vessel, one to sanctify, one to remove the handful
D. Four priests, including one to supervise
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman counted three priests: one to raise the vessel off the ground, one to place the meal offering inside (sanctifying it), and one to remove the kometz (handful).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rava's proof that removing a handful from a vessel resting on the ground is valid?
A. A ruling by Rav Ḥisda that ground-level removal is always permitted
B. The frankincense bowls are removed from the Table of Shewbread, which stands on the ground
C. The Mishnah in tractate Tamid explicitly permits it
D. A biblical verse stating that the floor of the Sanctuary is like a raised surface
Correct: B — Rava proves it from the shewbread Table: the frankincense bowls are removed from it as part of the Temple service, and the Table stands on the ground — proving ground-level removal is valid.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When blood sprays onto a garment during sin-offering sprinkling, according to the Gemara's conclusion, when does the garment require laundering?
A. Always, regardless of timing, since sin-offering blood always requires laundering
B. Only if the entire sin-offering service was then disqualified
C. If the blood sprayed before a particular sprinkling left the priest's hand
D. Only if the blood sprayed after the priest completed all of his sprinklings
Correct: C — The Gemara concludes: if the blood sprayed onto the garment before that sprinkling left the priest's hand, the garment requires laundering. If it sprayed after the sprinkling already left his hand (as mere residue), no laundering is required.
