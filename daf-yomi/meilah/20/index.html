🔁 Is Salt Indispensable? Rav and the Challenge

Rav
Only sacrificial rites that are repeated in the verses are indispensable (me'akev). Salt is not simply repeated — therefore, if one forgot to salt the meal offering, it is still fit. This matches the tanna of our Mishnah.
Challenge
But isn't salt written twice? Both "Every meal offering you shall season with salt" (Lev. 2:13) AND "You shall sacrifice salt with all your offerings" (Lev. 2:13) — that IS a repetition!
Resolution A
The second verse is not a mere repetition — it comes to extend salting to other altar items. It is expository, not redundant. Therefore Rav's rule is not violated.
Resolution B
Alternatively: Rav holds that the word "covenant" (brit) written regarding salt is treated as if the verse were repeated — making salt indispensable even within his framework.
Rav Yosef to Abaye Could it even enter your mind that a non-priest would approach the altar to salt the handful? Since it is inconceivable, the scenario must be interpreted differently.

↓

🔥 Which Items Require Salting?

✅ Requires Salting

  • Handful (kometz) of the meal offering
  • Frankincense accompanying a meal offering
  • Frankincense brought as a standalone offering
  • Limbs of a burnt offering (olah)
  • Meal offering of priests (entirely burned)
  • Daily meal offering of the High Priest

❌ Does Not Require Salting

  • Blood — sprinkled, not burned; unlike the handful
  • Wood — excluded by 'your meal offering'
  • Remainder of meal offering eaten by priests
  • Libations (wine poured as drink offerings)

🪵 Wood as a Voluntary Offering — Amud B

Baraita source: "When one brings a korban minḥa" (Lev. 2:1) — the superfluous word korban teaches that one may voluntarily donate wood as an offering to be burned on the altar.
↓
Minimum amount: a log (log) of wood must be donated. The wood is then burned on the altar.
↓
Rava adds: According to Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, voluntarily donated wood requires removal of a portion (like a handful from a meal offering) that is sacrificed separately.
↓
Rav Pappa objects: This ruling of Rava is disputed — the analogy to meal offering may not extend this far.
↓
Practical implication: The phrase "every meal offering of yours" in the salt verse excludes wood from the salting requirement — wood is not a meal offering per se.
