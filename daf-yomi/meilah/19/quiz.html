5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye, what is the fundamental dispute between Rabbi Shimon and the Rabbis regarding biblical interpretation?
A. Whether non-priests may perform any sacrificial rites
B. Whether a verse is interpreted by both the matter preceding and the matter succeeding it
C. Whether the term 'law' alone can invalidate a sacrificial rite
D. Whether pouring oil is indispensable for the meal offering
Correct: B — Abaye identifies the root dispute as whether a verse is interpreted by referring to both the matter that precedes it and the matter that succeeds it. The Rabbis hold this principle applies; Rabbi Shimon uses the vav instead.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rav's core principle regarding the terms 'law' (חוק) and 'statute' (חוקה) in sacrificial contexts?
A. Wherever 'law' alone appears, the rite is entirely optional
B. Wherever both 'law' and 'statute' are written for a rite, failure to perform it invalidates the offering
C. Only 'statute' language signals an indispensable rite; 'law' has no such effect
D. Neither term teaches indispensability unless accompanied by a direct command
Correct: B — Rav holds that when both 'law' and 'statute' appear together for a sacrificial rite, they jointly teach that the absence of that rite invalidates the offering.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say the 'statute' written about the meal offering does NOT make its rites indispensable via Rav's rule?
A. Because no statute is actually written about the meal offering
B. Because the statute written refers to the eating of the meal offering, not to the sacrificial rites themselves
C. Because Rabbi Shimon specifically exempted the meal offering from all general rules
D. Because the meal offering is a voluntary gift and different rules apply
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that the 'statute' written regarding the meal offering (Lev. 6:11) refers to the eating, not the sacrificial rites — so it does not trigger Rav's indispensability rule for the rites.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Shmuel reject Rav's proof from Aaron's eighth-day service that the meal offering must be taken by hand (not a vessel)?
A. Because Aaron used a vessel on that day and the verse is describing a mistake
B. Because Aaron's eighth-day service was a one-time inauguration event, not a binding precedent for all generations
C. Because the verse about Aaron does not mention the word 'handful'
D. Because Aaron's service predated the Sinai revelation and cannot be cited as law
Correct: B — Shmuel holds one cannot derive a general rule for all generations from a temporary, one-time situation. Aaron's eighth-day service was the inauguration of the Tabernacle — a singular event.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi explaining Rabbi Eliezer, why do 'in front of the altar' and 'before the Lord' both indicate the western side for the meal offering?
A. Because the altar faced west toward the setting sun
B. Because Rabbi Eliezer holds the entire altar stood in the northern part of the Temple courtyard, so both phrases converge at the southwest corner
C. Because 'before the Lord' always means west and overrides any other directional phrase
D. Because the priest carrying the meal offering always faced the Ark, which was to the west
Correct: B — Rav Ashi explains that Rabbi Eliezer holds the entire altar stood in the northern section of the courtyard. This means 'in front of the altar' (the altar's southern face) and 'before the Lord' (the western direction) both point to the same southwestern corner.
