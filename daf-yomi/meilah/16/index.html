⚔️ The Great Piggul Dispute

Rabbi Meir
Intent of piggul during either the handful burning or the frankincense burning alone is sufficient to render the entire meal offering piggul — each permitting act stands independently.
The Rabbis
Piggul intent during only one of the two permitting factors is insufficient. Both the handful and frankincense must be involved for full piggul liability.
🔑 Core Principle Rav refines the dispute: it applies only when one factor was performed in silence (no intent stated) — if one factor already carried piggul intent, all agree the offering is piggul.

🎓 Rav vs. Shmuel — Scope of the Dispute

Rav
The Rabbi Meir / Rabbis dispute applies only when the first act was done in silence and the second with piggul intent (or vice versa). If the first act already had piggul intent, even the Rabbis agree it is piggul — because the rite was performed in full piggul manner.
Shmuel
The dispute applies even when the first act was performed with piggul intent — so the Rabbis can still disagree with Rabbi Meir even in that scenario, maintaining that both permitting factors must bear the intent.
📜 Baraita Evidence Rav Aḥa bar Rav Huna raised a baraita: 'He placed the handful with intent and the frankincense in silence' — yet the Rabbis still disagree. Rabbi Ḥanina resolves: this baraita involves two priests (two separate intentions).

🫙 Conveying the Handful — Two Opinions

Reish Lakish

  • Conveying the handful is part of the permitting factors
  • Intent during conveying of handful alone ≠ piggul
  • Conveying is only one of two factors (handful + frankincense)
  • Parallel to: burning only the handful with intent — Rabbis say not piggul
  • 📌 Consistent with Rabbis' position on partial intent

Rabbi Yoḥanan

  • Conveying the handful = like removing the handful
  • Since removing the handful alone renders it piggul, so does conveying
  • Non-permitting rite (conveying) 'adopts the law' of the permitting rite
  • Any partial permitting factor performed with intent = piggul
  • ⚠️ Challenged: why does slaughter of one Shavuot lamb not render two loaves piggul?

🌾 Sesame-Seed Burning & Piggul Threshold

Scenario: Priest repeatedly burns sesame-seed amounts of the handful/frankincense, each time intending to eat a sesame-seed amount of the remainder the next day
↓
Opinion A — Piggul: 'Its manner of consumption is thus' — since the offering is consumed in small portions, burning in small portions with piggul intent is valid intent. Renders the offering piggul.
↓
Opinion B — Not Piggul: 'Its manner of burning is not thus' — a sesame seed is less than an olive-bulk (minimum for piggul). Since neither portion meets the threshold alone, intent is legally defective.
↓
Cannot assign to named amoraim: The Gemara warns against assuming this maps onto Rabbi Meir / Rabbis / Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi without proof — each may hold their ruling only in their specific context.
↓
Conclusion: The debate is an independent dispute about whether minimum-measure intent applied cumulatively constitutes valid piggul intent.
