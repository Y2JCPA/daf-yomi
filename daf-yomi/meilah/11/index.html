✊ How Is the Handful Properly Removed?

Abaye
How do the priests properly remove the handful from a meal offering?
Rava
They remove it as people normally remove handfuls — by folding all fingers over the palm of the hand.
Abaye
But each finger has a sacred function! The middle finger measures a cubit, the forefinger sprinkles blood on the altar, the thumb is used in the leper's purification rite — how can the little finger also fold in?
Gemara
The little finger is used only for leveling: the priest scoops with a full hand so the handful is not lacking, then wipes excess flour away with the little finger from below.
Baraita Confirms Three middle fingers scoop; thumb wipes excess from above; little finger levels from below — this is the hardest rite in the Temple

⚖️ Three Tannaitic Opinions on Frankincense Deficiency

Rabbi Meir

  • Must remove a full handful at the start
  • Must burn the entire handful on the altar
  • Even one kernel missing — invalidates
  • Derives from: "all the frankincense" (Lev. 2:2)
  • Most stringent position

Rabbi Yehuda

  • Must remove a full handful at the start
  • Need not burn the entire handful
  • Partial burning is sufficient
  • Middle position — strict on removal, lenient on burning
  • Interprets "all" as modifying removal only

Rabbi Shimon

  • Even a pinch is sufficient for removal
  • Need not remove or burn a full handful
  • Most lenient position
  • Dispute applies only to frankincense with a meal offering
  • All agree: frankincense brought alone requires full measure

🤔 Rav Pappa's Unresolved Dilemmas

קָמַץ מִן הַצְּדָדִין, מַהוּ? — If he took the handful from the sides, what is the law?
תֵּיקוּ — All Left Unresolved 1. Handful from the sides: If the priest passed the back of his hand over the flour so it collected via the side of his palm rather than fingertips forward — is this a valid handful?

2. Handful stuck to the vessel's side: If the handful was pressed against the inside wall of the service vessel rather than dropped directly in — is it sanctified?

3. Handful on the underside: If the priest overturned the vessel and pressed the handful into an indentation on the bottom — does placement in the vessel require the interior?

Rav Pappa raises each question; none receives a definitive answer.
