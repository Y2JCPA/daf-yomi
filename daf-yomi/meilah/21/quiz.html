QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara conclude that blood does not require even a minimal symbolic amount of salt?
A. Blood is not considered a genuine offering and is categorically exempt
B. A minimal amount of salt might coagulate the blood and prevent it from being properly sprinkled
C. The verse 'you shall season' applies only to solid offerings, not liquids
D. The priests historically never salted blood, establishing an unbroken custom
Correct: B — Even if blood is excluded from the main salting requirement, one might think a token amount fulfills a mitzvah. The Gemara rejects this: applying any salt to blood risks coagulating it, which would disqualify it from being sprinkled on the altar.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rava resolve the contradiction between Ze'eiri's ruling (cooked blood has no prohibition) and the baraita (curdled blood retains its prohibition)?
A. Ze'eiri is a minority opinion rejected by the baraita
B. Ze'eiri discusses fire-curdled blood; the baraita discusses sun-curdled blood
C. Ze'eiri discusses animal blood; the baraita discusses human blood
D. The baraita predates Ze'eiri and is overridden by his later ruling
Correct: B — Rava distinguishes the mechanism: fire fundamentally transforms blood and disqualifies it from the altar, removing the prohibition. Sun-curdled blood (discussed in the baraita) may remain altar-eligible, retaining the prohibition.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the baraita, in how many locations was salt maintained in the Temple, and where?
A. Two: the Chamber of Salt and the top of the altar
B. Four: Chamber of Salt, the ramp, the altar, and the Sanhedrin chamber
C. Three: the Chamber of Salt, the ramp, and the top of the altar
D. One: only in the Chamber of Salt, distributed as needed
Correct: C — The baraita lists three locations: the Chamber of the Salt (storage), the ramp (applied to ascending limbs), and the top of the altar (final application). Each has a different misuse-of-consecrated-property status.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
From which verse does the Gemara derive that the Temple treasury — not the individual — provides salt for offerings?
A. 'You shall not omit salt from your meal offering' (Lev. 2:13)
B. 'It is an everlasting covenant of salt' (Num. 18:19), by analogy to the shewbread covenant
C. 'Every meal offering of the priest shall be wholly made to smoke' (Lev. 6:16)
D. 'And you shall offer salt with all your offerings' (Lev. 2:13b)
Correct: B — The Gemara uses a verbal analogy (gezerah shavah): 'everlasting covenant of salt' (Num. 18:19) parallels 'everlasting covenant' of the shewbread (Lev. 24:8). Just as shewbread is funded from the Temple treasury (public shekalim), so too is salt.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the practical halakha (ruling of Abaye) for salting meat to extract blood before cooking?
A. Submerge the meat in heavily salted water for one hour
B. Apply salt to both sides, wait for the blood to drain, then rinse before cooking
C. Salt only the outer surface and cook immediately over high heat
D. Salt must be applied three times with rinsing in between each application
Correct: B — Abaye derives from the Temple practice that applying salt to both sides and allowing the blood to drain is sufficient — then rinse and cook. This became the basis for the halachic practice of meliḥah (salting meat) before cooking.
