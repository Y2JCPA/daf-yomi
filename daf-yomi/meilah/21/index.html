🩸 Cooked Blood: Does the Prohibition Remain?

Ze'eiri
Blood that one cooked — one does not transgress a prohibition by drinking it. It has been disqualified from altar use and therefore exits the prohibition.
Abaye
Objection! A baraita states that blood curdled by the sun retains its prohibition against drinking. How do you reconcile this with Ze'eiri's ruling?
Rava
No difficulty. Ze'eiri speaks of blood curdled by fire — fundamentally transformed, disqualified from the altar. The baraita speaks of blood curdled by the sun — still potentially suitable for altar use.
Abaye
But even sun-curdled blood should be disqualified! As Rabbi Mani asked: once blood cannot be presented on the altar in its current state, has it not lost its prohibition status entirely?
Rava
Perhaps the baraita discusses blood of external sin offerings (sprinkled on the outer altar), while Ze'eiri discusses blood of inner sin offerings — different categories, different rules.
Rav Ḥisda's Ruling Congealed blood of external sin offerings — one who eats it is liable, for the verse says 'the priest shall take of the blood,' implying it retains its status even when solid. Rava extends this to inner sin offering blood as well.

🔥 Fire-Curdled vs. ☀️ Sun-Curdled Blood

Curdled by Fire

  • Fundamentally transformed by heat
  • Disqualified from altar use
  • No drinking prohibition (Ze'eiri)
  • Analogous to roasted limbs: not 'pleasing aroma'
  • Like a different substance entirely

Curdled by Sun

  • Natural process, not artificial heat
  • May still be suitable for altar use
  • Drinking prohibition may remain (baraita)
  • Appearance still blood-like
  • Category depends on which altar (inner vs. outer)

📜 Parsing Leviticus 2:13 — 'You Shall Season with Salt'

"You shall season [timlaḥ]" — positive commandment: salt must be applied to every offering brought on the altar fire
↓
"You shall not omit [tashbit] salt" — bring salt that 'never rests' (shovetet): this refers to salt from Sodom (Dead Sea region), always available in the Temple
↓
Tevonehu — Rabba bar Ulla: Do not think one must mix in massive quantities like straw [teven] mixed into clay — only a modest amount is required
↓
Tevonehu — Rav Ashi: Do not think the offering must be suffused with the taste of salt as bina (understanding) fills a person — a simple application suffices
↓
Practical ruling (Abaye): When salting meat to extract blood, apply to both sides, wait for drainage, then rinse and cook — this is sufficient for both halachic and ritual purposes

💰 Who Must Provide Salt — Individual or Temple Treasury?

One vows: "It is incumbent upon me to bring a meal offering"
Must he supply salt from home, or does the Temple provide it?
↓
Analogy to Frankincense
Frankincense accompanies the meal offering in one vessel → individual provides it
∴ individual must bring salt
Analogy to Wood
Wood applies to all offerings and comes from Temple treasury
∴ Temple provides salt
↓
Decisive verse: "It is an everlasting covenant of salt" (Num. 18:19)
Verbal analogy to "everlasting covenant" of shewbread (Lev. 24:8)
Just as shewbread is funded publicly → Temple treasury provides salt
