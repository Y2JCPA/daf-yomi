⚔️ Three Disputes: Rabbi Yoḥanan vs. Reish Lakish on Meal Offering Deficiencies

Reish Lakish
Oil mixed outside courtyard: VALID. After handful removal, pouring and mixing are merely a priestly mitzva — location is not essential.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Oil mixed outside: DISQUALIFIED. The service uses a service vessel — which requires the Temple courtyard even if priesthood itself is not mandatory.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Lacking before handful: Owner refills from home — offering may continue. Handful removal has not yet established it.
Reish Lakish
Lacking before handful: Disqualified — cannot be refilled once it has become lacking.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Remainder lacking after handful: Handful IS burned on the altar; remainder IS eaten by priests.
Reish Lakish
Remainder lacking after handful: BOTH disqualified — the lacking remainder drags down the handful.
Central Question Does removal of the handful 'lock in' the offering so that subsequent deficiencies cannot invalidate it — or can a lacking remainder still retroactively disqualify everything?

🫙 Dispute #1 — Oil Mixed Outside the Temple Courtyard

Reish Lakish
Valid. The verse 'he shall pour oil upon it and mix it' (Lev. 2:6) teaches: from the removal of the handful onward, pouring and mixing are solely a priestly mitzva — not essential sacrificial service. Therefore location outside the courtyard does not disqualify.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Disqualified. Granted that a non-priest need not perform the mixing — but the use of a service vessel still requires the Temple courtyard. The two requirements (priesthood, location) are independent; removing one does not remove the other.
Analogy Offered Slaughtering differs from eating: a servant may serve his master in the master's place — so slaughter by a non-priest inside is valid. But eating outside is disrespectful. Similarly: location matters even when role does not.

📊 Three Disputes at a Glance

Oil Mixed Outside Courtyard

  • RY: Disqualified — service-vessel rites require courtyard
  • RL: Valid — post-handful rites are mitzva only, not essential service
  • Source: Lev. 2:6 'he shall pour oil and mix it'
  • Key: Are the two requirements (priesthood / location) separable?

Lacking Before Handful Removal

  • RY: Owner refills from home — offering proceeds
  • RL: Disqualified — cannot be refilled once lacking
  • Proof text: Leper's log (Nega'im 14:10) — analog dispute
  • Key: Does lack before establishment disqualify permanently?

Remainder Lacking After Handful

  • RY: Handful burned; remainder eaten — fully valid
  • RL: Both disqualified — lacking remainder drags all down
  • Disputed via: R. Yehoshua baraita; shewbread baraita
  • Key: Does handful removal 'lock in' the offering's validity?

✋ Deriving That Left-Hand Removal Disqualifies

Mishna (Menachot): If the priest removed the handful with his left hand, the meal offering is unfit (פסול)
↓
Question (Gemara): From where in the Torah is this derived?
↓
Rabbi Zeira: From the leper's purification (Lev. 14:15): 'the priest shall pour the log of oil onto his own left palm' — the word 'left' appears four times in that passage
↓
Challenge: That verse is needed for the leper's own rite — to specify oil must be poured on the priest's left hand there!
↓
Answer: Of the four mentions of 'left hand' — two govern the leper's specific ceremony; the remaining two are available for derivation of general Temple law
↓
Principle applied: A restriction stated after another restriction serves to amplify (מיעוט אחר מיעוט לרבות) — the added specification of 'left' for the leper teaches that elsewhere only the right hand is valid for Temple service

📋 Rav Malkiyu: Two Verses, Two Cases of Lacking

VerseInitial InterpretationGemara's Resolution
Lev. 2:2 — 'of its fine flour' (מסלתה)Meal offering must be full measure; if lacking → disqualified entirelyTeaches: if lacking BEFORE handful, refilling is REQUIRED — an active obligation, not just permission
Lev. 2:3 — 'that which is left shall be Aaron's and his sons''Remainder given to priests only if it was whole/intact at time of offeringTeaches: lacking AFTER handful — remainder is disqualified for priestly consumption
Ze'eiri's ruling from Lev. 2:3Priests receive the remainder of the meal offeringBut NOT the 'remainder of the remainder' — if the remainder itself becomes lacking, the residue left is not given to priests
Rav Malkiyu's challengeBoth verses seem to disqualify lacking meal offerings — why need two?One verse = before handful (fill or disqualify); other = after handful (remainder validity). Two separate rules.
