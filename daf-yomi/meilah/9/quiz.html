QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is Reish Lakish's reason for permitting oil that was mixed outside the Temple courtyard?
A. Mixing oil is not considered Temple service at all and has no location requirement
B. After the handful is removed, pouring and mixing are merely a priestly mitzva, not essential sacrificial service
C. The verse in Leviticus explicitly permits mixing outside the courtyard
D. Only the burning of the handful must occur inside; all other rites may be performed outside
Correct: B — Reish Lakish derives from Lev. 2:6 that from removal of the handful onward, pouring and mixing are solely a mitzva of the priesthood — meaning the courtyard-location requirement does not apply to these post-removal rites.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, what should happen when a meal offering becomes lacking in measure BEFORE the handful is removed?
A. The offering is immediately disqualified and a new one must be brought from the start
B. The priest removes the handful from whatever flour remains
C. The owner brings additional flour from his home to fill the missing measure
D. The offering is set aside until the following day
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan holds that since the handful has not yet been removed, the offering is not yet 'established.' Therefore the owner may — and must — refill the missing measure from his own flour before the rite proceeds.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
From which passage does Rabbi Zeira derive that removing the handful with the left hand disqualifies the meal offering?
A. The meal offering laws in Leviticus 2, which require the priest's service
B. The leper's purification ceremony (Lev. 14), where 'left hand' appears four times
C. The incense offering, where only the right hand may approach the altar
D. The shewbread arrangement, which specifies the priests' right-hand duties
Correct: B — Rabbi Zeira uses the leper's purification passage (Lev. 14:15ff), where 'left hand' appears four times — two govern the leper's specific ceremony, and two remain available to teach that for general Temple service, only the right hand is valid.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Ze'eiri derive from the verse 'that which is left of the meal offering shall be Aaron's and his sons''?
A. The priests receive the entire meal offering once the handful has been removed
B. The priests may eat the remainder of the meal offering — but not the 'remainder of the remainder'
C. The priests must eat the remainder on the same day the handful was burned
D. The priests only receive the remainder if it was mixed with oil before the handful was removed
Correct: B — Ze'eiri reads the verse as guaranteeing the priests the intact remainder — but not residual scraps if the remainder itself becomes lacking. 'The remainder' they receive, but 'the remainder of the remainder' does not qualify.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the baraita about the shewbread, if the bread breaks BEFORE the frankincense bowls are lifted from the Table, what is the ruling?
A. Both the bread and the frankincense become disqualified and neither may be consumed or burned
B. The bread is unfit for consumption by the priests, but the frankincense is still burned on the altar
C. The broken bread may still be eaten since the frankincense has not yet been burned
D. The ceremony must be postponed until the following Shabbat when fresh bread is set out
Correct: B — The baraita rules that broken bread is unfit for priestly eating — but the frankincense (analogous to the 'handful') is still burned. Rabbi Yoḥanan cites this as proof that the burning rite proceeds even when the 'remainder' is impaired, supporting his position.
