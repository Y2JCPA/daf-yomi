⚔️ How Do You Fix a Board Sukka?

Beit Shammai
Remove one board from among them. This negates the roof-like appearance and renders the sukka valid.
Beit Hillel
Move the boards away from each other, creating gaps. This disrupts the roof-structure and validates the sukka.
Rabbi Meir
Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel actually agree: only removing a board works. Moving boards alone is insufficient — the decree of the roof still applies.
Rabbi Yehuda
They genuinely disagree. Beit Shammai requires removal; Beit Hillel holds that moving boards creates enough disruption to the roof-like appearance.
Core Issue Wide boards (4+ handbreadths) resemble a permanent roof — does merely moving them fix the problem, or must one board be removed entirely?

🏚️ The Decree of the Roof (גזירת תקרה)

Gemara's Question
Why does Beit Hillel require moving the boards? Their usual reason is 'prepare it' — perform an action. But moving boards should be insufficient if the roof-decree applies!
Gemara's Answer
The rationale IS the decree of the roof. Moving is not enough — only removing one board renders it fit. Both Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel agree on this.
Rabbi Ḥiyya bar Abba / Rabbi Yoḥanan
The first mishna deals with planed boards — their dispute there is about 'prepare it, not from already-prepared.' The decree of the roof is a separate concern applying here.
Rav (via Rav Yehuda)
This mishna also involves the decree of the roof — once the Sages issued it for wide boards, we apply it consistently throughout. Rabbi Yehuda's latter clause addresses negating the decree, not invoking it.
גזירת תקרה — The Roof Decree Boards ≥ 4 handbreadths wide look like a real roof. The Rabbis decreed one may not sit under them on Sukkot, lest the sukka be confused with a permanent dwelling.

🎯 Arrow Shafts as Schach: Rav's Ruling

Convex (Rounded Top) ✓ Valid

  • Shafts with convex / rounded surfaces
  • Sukka roofed with these is fit
  • No decree of the roof applies
  • They do not resemble flat permanent roofing
  • Rav Yehuda citing Rav explicitly permits these
  • No rabbinic decree issued against them

Concave (Hollow / Grooved) ✗ Invalid

  • Shafts with concave / hollow channels
  • Sukka roofed with these is unfit
  • Resembles roofing planks or gutters
  • Falls under the decree of the roof
  • Rav rules them unfit — no extra decree needed
  • The shape itself is the disqualifying factor

🛏️ Metal Skewers & Bed-Frame Boards as Schach

Metal skewers or long bed-frame boards
laid across the sukka as roofing material
↓
No gaps between them
Solid roof appearance — invalid schach (metal) + roof decree applies
Gaps equal to their width
Place valid schach in the gaps — sukka is fit!
↓
Critical rule: fit schach must equal or exceed the invalid material in coverage
↓
Rava: Skewers placed lengthwise across the full width → valid schach between them numerically dominates → sukka valid even if spaces appear equal

🔗 Domains, Bed Boards & Rabbi Ami bar Tavyomei

Mishna rules: Metal skewers with equal gaps + valid schach inserted = sukka is valid
↓
Challenge: This seems to refute Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua, who holds that an area enclosed by posts with gaps equal to their own width is NOT a valid private domain (רשות היחיד) on Shabbat — yet the mishna treats equal gaps as valid here!
↓
Reply: 'Like the skewers' does not mean spaces are exactly equal — it means merely similar in appearance. The fit schach may actually be slightly greater than the invalid material.
↓
Rava adds: Even granting equal widths, lengthwise placement makes the valid schach numerically greater — the Shabbat domain dispute is not refuted.
↓
Rabbi Ami bar Tavyomei: Even a single long bed-board alone invalidates a sukka → the mishna's 'long boards of the bed' refers to the full frame with crossboards together, not lone planks in isolation.
