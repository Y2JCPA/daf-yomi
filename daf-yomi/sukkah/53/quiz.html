Sukkah 53 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What did Hillel say when rejoicing at the Simchat Beit HaShoeva?
A. Happy is our youth that did not shame our old age
B. If I am here, everyone is here; if I am not here, who is here?
C. Because you drowned others, they drowned you
D. We saw no sleep in our eyes the entire Festival
Correct: B — Hillel declared 'If I am here, everyone is here' — teaching that one who is fully present before God brings the Divine Presence, making everyone truly present.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did the Angel of Death appear sad when Solomon saw him?
A. He had been sent to take King Solomon himself
B. He was forbidden from entering the Temple Mount
C. He had been tasked with taking the two Cushite scribes but could not find them
D. David had written God's Name to stop him from acting
Correct: C — The Angel of Death was sad because he was instructed to take Elihoreph and Ahijah but could not find them — Solomon had sent them to Luz, the one place the Angel of Death cannot enter.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
By how many cubits did the waters of the depths subside after David cast in the shard with God's Name?
A. 1,000 cubits
B. 15,000 cubits
C. 16,000 cubits
D. 50,000 cubits
Correct: C — The waters subsided 16,000 cubits — far too much. David recited the 15 Songs of Ascents (Psalms 120–134), raising the waters 1,000 cubits per psalm, leaving them at a healthy 1,000 cubits below the surface.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Rabbis (not Rabbi Yehuda), what is the minimum number of daily trumpet blasts in the Temple?
A. Seven blasts
B. Nine blasts
C. Twenty-one blasts
D. Forty-eight blasts
Correct: C — The Rabbis hold the minimum is 21 blasts: 3 for opening the gates, 9 for the morning tamid sacrifice, and 9 for the musaf additional offering.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What happened when Levi performed a kidda bow before Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi to demonstrate the acrobatics of Simchat Beit HaShoeva?
A. He was praised and asked to teach the technique to others
B. He successfully juggled eight torches alongside the bow
C. He strained his thigh and became permanently lame
D. He was rebuked for showing off before the Nasi
Correct: C — Levi strained his thigh performing the kidda (prostration with nose to the ground) before Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi and became lame. The Gemara connects this to Rabbi Elazar's teaching that one should never speak presumptuously before Heaven.
