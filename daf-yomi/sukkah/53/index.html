🕯️ Performers at the Simchat Beit HaShoeva

The Pious (Chassidim)

  • Danced before the assembled crowds
  • 'Happy is our youth that did not shame our old age'
  • OR: 'Happy is our old age that atoned for our youth'
  • Songs and praise honoring God with total joy

Hillel the Elder

  • 'If I am here, everyone is here; if not, who is here?'
  • Saw a skull floating — taught measure-for-measure
  • His very presence embodied the Divine Presence
  • Model of humble yet complete devotion

Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel

  • Juggled 8 flaming torches simultaneously
  • Performed kidda — nose to the ground while bowing
  • Levi tried to imitate and strained his thigh
  • His lameness connected to speaking presumptuously

Rabbi Yehoshua ben Chananya

  • 'We saw no sleep the entire Festival'
  • Morning sacrifice → Musaf → celebration → back again
  • Gemara challenges: impossible to go 3 days without sleep
  • Resolution: brief naps on each other's shoulders

💬 Hillel's Two Teachings at the Celebration

אם אני כאן — הכל כאן; ואם אין אני כאן — מי כאן?
הלל הזקן — Hillel the Elder 'If I am here, everyone is here; if I am not here, who is here?'

Hillel taught that when a great person dedicates himself fully to God's service, he draws all of creation into genuine presence — for he embodies the Divine Presence itself. One who is truly here makes the world here.

The Floating Skull: He also saw a skull floating on the water and said: 'Because you drowned others, they drowned you — and those who drowned you will themselves be drowned.'

This is the way of the world: measure for measure. The cycle of divine justice is inevitable and complete.

Solomon sees the Angel of Death looking sad
↓
Angel: I must take Elihoreph and Ahijah (I Kings 4:3), Solomon's Cushite scribes — but I cannot find them
↓
Solomon sends them to Luz — the one city the Angel of Death has no power to enter
↓
Next day
Angel of Death returns looking happy
Why happy?
They died at the very gate of Luz — the destination Solomon sent them to
↓
No one escapes the decree of Heaven. Fleeing the Angel of Death brought them to the exact place decreed for their end.

💧 David Digs the Drainpipes — The Waters of the Deep

David digs the שיתין (drainpipes) for the Temple foundations
↓
The תהום (depths) rise up and threaten to flood and inundate the entire world
↓
David asks: Is it permitted to write God's Name on a shard and cast it into the depths to stop them? — Silence. No one answered.
↓
David declares: 'Whoever knows what to say and remains silent — may he be strangled in his throat!'
↓
Ahitofel reasons by kal v'chomer: If God's Name may be erased to restore peace between husband and wife, surely it may be written to save the entire world from flood
↓
David writes the Sacred Name on a shard, casts it into the deep — waters subside 16,000 cubits
↓
Too far — David recites the 15 Songs of Ascents (Ps. 120–134), raising waters 1,000 cubits per psalm, to a healthy depth of 1,000 cubits below. The 15 stairs of the Temple correspond to these 15 psalms.

🎺 Minimum Trumpet Blasts: Rabbi Yehuda vs. the Rabbis

Rabbis
The minimum is 21 daily blasts: 3 for opening the gates, 9 for the morning tamid sacrifice, 9 for the musaf additional offering. The maximum on Shabbat-Sukkot is 48.
Rabbi Yehuda
The minimum shall not be fewer than seven blasts: 3 for opening the gates and 4 more for the musaf — because each terua must be flanked by tekiot, forming units of three.
Rabbi Yehuda's Source
Numbers 10:5–6 writes 'sound a terua' and 'they will sound a terua' — each terua requires a tekia before and after it (tekia-terua-tekia), so each act of sounding = 3 blasts.
Rabbis' Counter
Numbers 10:7: 'When assembling the people, sound a tekia and NOT a terua' — this proves a lone tekia is itself a distinct, stand-alone mitzva, not merely part of a larger unit.
Maximum: 48 Blasts On Shabbat eve during Sukkot: 3 (gates) + 3 (upper gate) + 3 (lower gate) + 3 (water vessel) + 3 (altar) + 9 (tamid) + 9 (musaf) + 6 (Shabbat onset) + 9 (additional Shabbat) = 48
