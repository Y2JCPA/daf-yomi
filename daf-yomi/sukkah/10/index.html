📏 How Much Space Must Separate Two Stacked Sukkot?

Rav Huna
One handbreadth of space is sufficient. We derive this from the laws of tents of ritual impurity — a space of one handbreadth creates a distinct halakhic domain.
Rav Ḥisda & Rabba bar Rav Huna
Four handbreadths are required. We do not recognize a legally significant area measuring less than four handbreadths — anything smaller is considered negligible.
Shmuel
Ten handbreadths are required. The very criterion for a sukka's fitness is ten handbreadths of height — so the lower sukka must have that full measure of independent space beneath the upper.
The Logic of Each Opinion Each authority derives his measure from a different halakhic parallel: tents of impurity (1 tefach), minimum significant area (4 tefachim), or the sukka's own height requirement (10 tefachim). The debate reflects a deeper question: which analogy best defines 'independence' for a sukka?

🧵 Does a Sheet Spread Inside the Sukka Invalidate It?

Mishna
Three cases all render the sukka unfit: (1) a sheet spread over the roofing against the sun; (2) a sheet spread under the roofing against falling leaves; (3) a sheet as a canopy over a four-post bed frame.
Rav Ḥisda
The mishna only invalidates sheets spread for functional reasons. A sheet spread for decoration — to beautify the sukka — does not invalidate it. The Tosefta confirms: decorative curtains and sheets are expressly permitted.
Key Principle Intent matters: Functional coverings create an interposition that undermines the sukka's integrity. Decorations fulfill hiddur mitzvah (beautification of the commandment) and are welcomed, not condemned.

🎨 Sukka Decorations — What Is Permitted and What Is Restricted

✅ Permitted & Encouraged

  • Colorful curtains and sheets hung for beautification
  • Ornamental nuts, peaches, pomegranates, grapes
  • Bottles of wine, oil, and flour
  • Wreaths of grain and bundles of garlic or onions
  • Decorations do not diminish the sukka's measured height
  • Decorations within 4 tefachim of roofing: Rav Naḥman rules they do not interpose

⚠️ Cautions & Restrictions

  • Once hung, decorations become set-aside (muktzeh) for the entire Festival
  • A prior stipulation permits using them during the Festival
  • A shirt spread to dry atop the sukka must be removed — mar'it ayin (appearances)
  • Decorations hanging 4+ tefachim below roofing: disputed by Rav Naḥman vs. others whether they interpose and disqualify

📖 Reciting Shema Naked Under Bed Netting

כִּילָה (kilah) — bed netting or canopy
Halakha — Rav Taḥalifa bar Avimi citing Shmuel Shmuel's ruling: One who sleeps naked under bed netting and must recite Shema may extend his head out from beneath the netting and recite — the netting is considered a garment covering his body.

Baraita objection: A naked person under netting may not extend his head and recite Shema.

Resolution: The two sources address different cases. The baraita refers to netting 10+ handbreadths high — a distinct tent that does not count as a garment. Shmuel's permission applies only when the netting is under 10 handbreadths, functioning as a close-fitting covering akin to clothing.
