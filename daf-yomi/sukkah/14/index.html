📐 Rav vs. Shmuel: When Are Boards Forbidden for Sukka Roofing?

Rav
The dispute between Rabbi Yehuda and Rabbi Meir concerns boards WITH four handbreadths in their width. Below four handbreadths: both agree the sukka is fit.
Shmuel
No — the dispute concerns boards WITHOUT four handbreadths. With four handbreadths everyone agrees the sukka is UNFIT. Rabbi Yehuda permits only narrow boards.
Rav Pappa
Clarifying Shmuel: under 3 handbreadths = fit (all agree — like mere straw). Four or more handbreadths = unfit (all agree). The 3–4 range is the actual dispute between Rabbi Meir and Rabbi Yehuda.
Baraita Confirms Rav A baraita teaches: cedar boards under 4 handbreadths — everyone agrees the sukka is fit. At 4 handbreadths — Rabbi Meir says unfit, Rabbi Yehuda permits, citing a historical precedent: during a time of danger, 4-handbreadth boards were used to roof a porch and eat the Passover offering there.

🌾 Grain Handles: Does Besasan Remove Ritual Susceptibility?

Handles of food-grain: craftsman performs besasan on the threshing floor
↓
Rabbi Elazar: besasan = untied their binding
Handles are severed from grain-service → no longer subordinate → Rabbis: ritually pure; Rabbi Yosei: still susceptible (can serve as handles later)
Rabbi Yochanan: besasan = trampled them
Trampled handles lose handle-function → Rabbis: ritually pure; Rabbi Yosei: when sukka is dismantled they regain handle-function → still susceptible
↓
Key insight: Both readings agree handles used for sukka roofing can regain handle-function when the roof is dismantled — this is why even Rabbi Yosei's view applies in the sukka context

🍴 Aggadic Teaching: Prayer Like a Pitchfork

וַיֶּעְתַּר יִצְחָק לַה' לְנֹכַח אִשְׁתּוֹ כִּי עֲקָרָה הִוא (בראשית כה:כא)
Rabbi Elazar's Insight Why are the prayers of the righteous likened to a pitchfork (eter)? Just as a pitchfork turns grain from one side to another, so too prayer turns God's attribute of strict judgment toward the attribute of mercy.

Proof: "And Isaac entreated [va-yetar] the Lord for his wife, because she was barren" — the root עתר evokes the pitchfork, and indeed God's decree was reversed.

🪵 Rabbi Yehuda vs. Rabbi Meir on Boards for Sukka Roofing

Rabbi Yehuda — Permits

  • One may roof a sukka with boards like house-ceiling boards
  • A single board of 4 handbreadths: sukka is fit (but do not sleep beneath it)
  • Two boards do NOT combine to form the measure of unfitness
  • Historical proof: used 4-handbreadth cedar boards for roofing during time of danger (gentile decree)

Rabbi Meir — Prohibits

  • Boards are prohibited — rabbinic decree of 'the roof' (looks like a permanent house ceiling)
  • Two boards join together like sheets to constitute 4 handbreadths of unfitness
  • Even narrow boards combine to invalidate the sukka
  • The concern: one who roofs with boards may treat the sukka as a house, defeating its purpose

↩️ Turning a Board on Its Side: Rav Huna vs. the Question

Rav Ḥisda & Rabba bar Rav Huna
We asked Rav Naḥman when he visited Sura: if one takes an unfit 4-handbreadth board and turns it on its side so that only 3 handbreadths are exposed — what is the halakha?
Rav Naḥman
The sukka is FIT. The board in its current orientation does not present the problematic 4 handbreadths.
Rav Huna
Didn't I tell you to rule according to my opinion? Even Rav Naḥman agrees with me that the sukka is fit when the board is turned. A baraita also supports this.
Baraita Support A baraita confirms: where one introduces only 3 handbreadths of a 4-handbreadth board (i.e., turned on its side), the sukka is fit — supporting Rav Huna's ruling that the decree follows the board's current presentation.
