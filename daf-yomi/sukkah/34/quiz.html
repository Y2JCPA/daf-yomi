QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abba Shaul, what halakhic teaching is derived from the plural form 'willows of the brook'?
A. Two willow branches must be taken for the lulav bundle
B. Willows must be gathered from two separate brooks
C. There are two distinct willow mitzvot: lulav bundle and Temple altar-circling
D. The willow mitzva applies on both day and night
Correct: C — Abba Shaul interprets the plural 'willows' as indicating two separate mitzvot: the willow included in the lulav bundle, and the willow used to circle the Temple altar on Hoshana Rabba.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Which combination of characteristics correctly distinguishes a valid willow from a tzaftzafa?
A. Willow: white stem, round leaf, smooth edge — Tzaftzafa: red stem, elongated leaf, notched edge
B. Willow: red stem, elongated leaf, smooth edge — Tzaftzafa: white stem, round leaf, serrated edge
C. Both have red stems, but the willow grows near water while the tzaftzafa grows in mountains
D. They are visually identical; only their growing location differs
Correct: B — The Gemara provides three distinguishing criteria: a willow has a red stem, elongated leaf, and smooth edge; a tzaftzafa has a white stem, round leaf, and serrated (notched) edge.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the practical halakhic consequence of the name-swap between Babylon and Bursif discussed by Rava bar Yosef?
A. Which shofar tones are required on Rosh Hashana
B. Whether an animal with a needle in its stomach is a tereifa
C. The validity of bills of divorce, which require the precise city name
D. Which Shabbat carrying prohibition applies in each city
Correct: C — A get (bill of divorce) must record the precise city name where it was written. If the names Babylon and Bursif have been swapped in common usage, a scribe must use the correct contemporary name to avoid invalidating the divorce.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yishmael in the Mishna, the lulav bundle is fit even if the tops of two of the three myrtle branches are severed. What does Shmuel rule, and why did he tell myrtle sellers to equalize their prices?
A. Shmuel rules like Rabbi Akiva; he told sellers that topped branches are fully equivalent
B. Shmuel rules like Rabbi Yishmael; he told sellers to charge less for topped branches
C. Shmuel rules like Rabbi Tarfon (topped branches must be replaced); he equalized prices so the poor would not be penalized
D. Shmuel ruled there is no difference between the opinions since all agree one whole myrtle suffices
Correct: C — Shmuel rules like Rabbi Tarfon, who requires intact myrtle branches. He told sellers to equalize prices not because topped branches are fit, but so that poor buyers would not be forced to pay a premium for the whole branches they actually need.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
An etrog has boil-like blemishes covering less than half its surface. What is its halakhic status?
A. Unfit, because any blemish on an etrog renders it invalid
B. Fit, because blemishes on a minority of the surface do not disqualify it
C. Unfit, because boil-like blemishes are worse than ordinary blemishes
D. Fit only if the blemishes are on the bottom half of the etrog
Correct: B — The Mishna rules that boil-like blemishes on a majority of the etrog render it unfit, but blemishes on a minority do not. The etrog in this question is therefore fit.
