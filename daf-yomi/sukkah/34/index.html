🌿 Two Interpretations of 'Willows of the Brook'

Abba Shaul
The plural 'willows of the brook' (Leviticus 23:40) teaches two willow mitzvot: one for the lulav bundle, and one for circling the Temple altar on Hoshana Rabba.
Rabbis
The verse teaches only the lulav willow. The Temple altar-circling willow is a halakha l'Moshe miSinai — an oral tradition from Sinai, not derived from any verse.
Rabbi Asi
A verse proves the Temple willow: 'He set it as a tzaftzafa' — this shows the tzaftzafa is distinct from the valid willow, implying the willow has a Temple role.
Abaye
Perhaps that verse merely explains the preceding verse: God placed Israel by great waters — it was a tzaftzafa. This cannot serve as proof for the Temple willow mitzva!
Bottom Line The Rabbis maintain the Temple willow-circling is pure Sinaitic oral law. Abba Shaul derives both willow mitzvot from the plural form in the verse.

🔍 Willow (ערבה) vs. Tzaftzafa — Telling Them Apart

✅ Valid Willow (ערבה)

  • 🔴 Stem is red
  • 📏 Leaf is elongated
  • 〰️ Leaf edge is smooth
  • 🌊 Grows near water / brooks
  • Fit for the lulav bundle
  • Fit for Temple altar-circling

❌ Tzaftzafa (invalid)

  • ⚪ Stem is white
  • ⭕ Leaf is round
  • ⚡ Leaf edge is serrated / notched
  • ⛰️ Grows in the mountains
  • Not fit for lulav bundle
  • Not fit for Temple circling

📜 The Four Species: Rabbi Yishmael's Ruling (Mishna 34b)

3 Myrtle Branches (הדסים) — Fit even if the tops of two of the three are severed, so long as one intact branch remains. (Rabbi Tarfon disagrees; Shmuel rules like Rabbi Tarfon.)
↓
2 Willow Branches (ערבות) — Two required, derived from the verse's structure and tradition.
↓
1 Lulav (לולב) — One palm frond; kappot is written defectively (without vav) in the Torah, signaling the singular.
↓
1 Etrog (אתרוג) — 'The fruit of a beautiful tree'; held separately from the lulav bundle according to Rabbi Yishmael, because the verse lists it with a distinct conjunction.
↓
Shmuel's ruling: He told myrtle sellers to price topped and whole branches equally — not because topped branches are fit (Rabbi Tarfon requires replacement), but so poor people would not be overcharged for whole ones.

🔄 Abaye & Rava bar Yosef: Linguistic Drift in Our Own Generation

אמר אביי: אנא נמי אמינא — הובלילא בי כסאי קרו ליה, ובי כסאי הובלילא קרו ליה
Changed Names — Practical Stakes Abaye observed: The first stomach compartment once called huvlila is now called bei kasei, and vice versa. The practical difference: a needle found embedded in the thick wall of the second compartment renders the animal a tereifa (forbidden). If you call the wrong compartment 'second,' you may permit a forbidden animal.

Rava bar Yosef observed: The city once called Babylon is now called Bursif, and the city once called Bursif is now called Babylon. The practical difference: a bill of divorce (get) must record the precise city name where it was written — an error can invalidate the divorce entirely.
