🏚️ The Old Sukka: Built Before the Festival

Beit Shammai
An old sukka — built 30+ days before the festival without intent for Sukkot — is unfit. A sukka must be built lishmah, dedicated to the mitzva of the festival.
Beit Hillel
An old sukka is fit. The Torah does not require that a sukka be built specifically with festival intent — only that it meet the physical requirements of valid s'chach and walls.
Definition An 'old sukka' is any booth established 30+ days before the Festival without explicit intent to fulfill the Sukkot mitzva.

📜 Competing Verse Interpretations

Beit Shammai
Lev. 23:34: 'The festival of Sukkot is seven days unto the Lord' — the sukka must be dedicated to God. This requires lishmah: intent for the festival at time of construction.

Deut. 16:13: 'You shall prepare for you the festival of Sukkot' — must be built specifically for this festival period.
Beit Hillel
Lev. 23:34 teaches (per Rav Sheshet / R. Akiva) that s'chach wood is forbidden for personal use during the festival — not lishmah.

Deut. 16:13 permits building the sukka even during Chol HaMoed (intermediate days), so pre-festival intent is not required.
R. Yehuda ben Beteira Just as Heaven's name rests upon the Festival peace-offering, so too it rests upon the sukka — the sukka has intrinsic sanctity regardless of when it was built.

⚔️ Lishmah Compared: Sukka vs. Tzitzit

Sukka

  • Beit Hillel: No lishmah required for construction
  • 'For you' (Deut. 16:13) excludes a stolen sukka
  • Physical requirements (shade, walls) are primary
  • Can be built during Chol HaMoed (Beit Hillel)
  • No parallel to spinning requirement

Tzitzit

  • 'For you' (Deut. 22:12) = spun for your obligation
  • Threads must be spun lishmah from the very start
  • Shmuel: even balls of thread require lishmah spinning
  • 'For them' (Num. 15:38) excludes stolen ritual fringes
  • Lishmah is an active production requirement

🌳 Rava: Sukka Beneath a Tree

Sukka constructed beneath a tree
↓
Tree shade > sunlight
Tree is the primary roofing — it acts as invalid s'chach. Mishna: 'It is as though he established it inside a house.' Sukka is unfit.
Sunlight > tree shade
Tree does not dominate the shade. If branches are lowered and combined with valid s'chach (majority valid), the sukka may be fit — no decree needed for this case.

🔑 The 'For You' Principle — Stolen Sukka

חַג הַסֻּכּוֹת תַּעֲשֶׂה לְךָ שִׁבְעַת יָמִים — דברים טז:יג
לָכֶם — For You The Torah writes 'you shall prepare the sukka for you' (Deut. 16:13). The Gemara derives: establish the sukka for you — and not a sukka belonging to another. A stolen sukka is invalid. This parallels the Four Species, where 'for you' on the first day also excludes stolen items. Note: for tzitzit, a separate verse ('for them' — Num. 15:38) independently excludes stolen fringes, freeing 'for you' to teach the lishmah requirement there.
