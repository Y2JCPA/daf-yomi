QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Beit Shammai, why is an old sukka (built 30+ days before the festival without intent) unfit?
A. It may have become structurally unsound over time
B. It was not built with intent for the Sukkot festival (lishmah)
C. It lacks a sufficient number of walls
D. It was built before Rosh Hashanah and thus outside the permitted window
Correct: B — Beit Shammai derives from 'seven days unto the Lord' (Lev. 23:34) that the sukka must be established for God's sake — i.e., with festival intent (lishmah). A sukka built without this intent is therefore invalid according to Beit Shammai.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Beit Hillel interpret 'The festival of Sukkot is seven days unto the Lord' (Lev. 23:34)?
A. It requires lishmah intent for the construction of the sukka
B. It teaches that the s'chach wood is forbidden for personal use during the festival
C. It permits building the sukka on the first day of Sukkot itself
D. It defines the minimum number of days one must sleep in the sukka
Correct: B — Beit Hillel, following Rav Sheshet citing Rabbi Akiva, uses this verse to teach that the wood of the sukka becomes set apart (forbidden for personal use) during the festival — not to require lishmah at construction time.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava, when is a sukka built beneath a tree valid?
A. Never — any living tree overhead automatically invalidates the sukka
B. Only if the tree has shed all its leaves before Sukkot
C. When the sunlight filtering through the tree exceeds the tree's shade
D. Only if the tree branches are physically tied back before building
Correct: C — Rava rules that the mishna's invalidation applies only when the tree's shade exceeds its sunlight — making the tree the primary 'roofing.' If sunlight dominates, the tree is not the main cover, and the sukka may be valid. He derives this from the mishna's comparison to 'being inside a house.'
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In Rabbi Yirmeya's analysis of stacked sukkot, when are BOTH sukkot unfit?
A. When both have more shade than sunlight, and the upper sukka is more than 20 cubits high
B. When neither sukka has proper walls on all sides
C. When both sukkot were built before the Sukkot festival (old sukkot)
D. When the lower sukka's s'chach is made of invalid materials
Correct: A — Both stacked sukkot are unfit when each has shade greater than sunlight (seemingly valid on its own), but the upper sukka is more than 20 cubits above the ground — rendering it invalid for excessive height — while the lower remains invalid because it sits beneath the upper roof.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why is a stolen sukka invalid, and from which verse is this derived?
A. From 'unto the Lord' — stolen property cannot be dedicated to God
B. From 'for you' (Deut. 16:13) — the sukka must belong to the person fulfilling the mitzva
C. From 'in sukkot shall you dwell' — one must dwell in one's own home
D. From 'seven days' — a thief forfeits his festival obligations
Correct: B — The Gemara derives from 'לָכֶם' (for you, Deut. 16:13) that the sukka must be established for yourself — and not one belonging to another. This parallels the Four Species on the first day of Sukkot, where 'for you' similarly excludes a borrowed or stolen lulav.
