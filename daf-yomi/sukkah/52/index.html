⚔️ For Whom Will the Final Eulogy Be?

Rabbi Dosa
The eulogy is for Messiah ben Yosef, who is killed in the war at the end of days. The mourning is for his death in battle against the enemies of Israel.
The Rabbis
The eulogy is for the evil inclination itself, which God will slaughter at the end of days. The righteous will weep because it seemed to them like a great mountain; the wicked will weep because it seemed like a mere thread.
📜 Zechariah 12:12 "The land will eulogize, each family separately" — each group mourning privately, men and women apart, indicating great personal grief

😈 The Evil Inclination's Escalating Grip

Initial Contact
Rav Asi: It begins like a strand of spider's web — thin, barely noticed, easy to break
↓
It Grows
The person engages, yields, returns — the strand thickens into habit
↓
Final Stage
Ultimately becomes like thick cart ropes — nearly impossible to break free
↓
Remedy: Torah
School of Rabbi Yishmael: Drag it to the study hall. Torah dissolves stone, shatters iron.
Remedy: Starve It
Rabbi Yoḥanan: The small organ — if you starve it, it is satiated; if you feed it, it craves more.

📛 Seven Names of the Evil Inclination

Moses

  • Called it "uncircumcised"
  • "Circumcise the foreskin of your heart" (Deut. 10:16)
  • Implies it is foreign, impure, needing removal

David

  • Called it "impure"
  • "Create in me a pure heart" (Ps. 51:12)
  • Implies the old heart was impure — the evil inclination defiles

Solomon

  • Called it "enemy"
  • "If your enemy is hungry, give him bread" (Prov. 25:21)
  • Read: give your evil inclination the bread of Torah and water of Torah

Isaiah / Ezekiel / Joel

  • Isaiah: "stumbling block" — obstacle in the path
  • Ezekiel: "stone" — "I will remove the heart of stone"
  • Joel: "hidden one" — lurks unseen; God will drive it to a barren land

🚶 Abaye Follows the Travelers

כָּל הַגָּדוֹל מֵחֲבֵרוֹ — יִצְרוֹ גָּדוֹל מִמֶּנּוּ
📖 The Parable of the Road Abaye overheard a man say to a woman: "Let us rise early and walk together." Certain they intended sin, he followed them for miles — only to see them part ways innocently at a fork in the road. They said: "The road was long and our companion pleasant."

Abaye thought: Had it been someone I hate (himself), he could not have held back. Devastated, he leaned against a doorpost — until an old man comforted him: "The greater the person, the greater their evil inclination."

🕯️ The Strength of the Young Priests at the Water-Drawing

Four poles, fifty cubits high — immense golden candelabra erected in the Temple courtyard for Simchat Beit HaShoeva
↓
Four young priestly trainees climbed each pole holding jugs of oil, filling the bowls at the top
↓
Compared to the son of Marta bat Baitos — a famously mighty priest who could carry two thighs of an ox, yet these boys surpassed him
↓
Wherein their superiority? Not weight — two thighs are heavier than 30 log of oil. Rather: they carried while climbing a fifty-cubit ladder, whereas he walked on flat ground
↓
Result: Every courtyard in Jerusalem was illuminated by the light of the celebration — a spiritual and physical radiance
