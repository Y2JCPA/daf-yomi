Sukkah 38 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the mishna, what should a traveler do if he arrives home for a meal and has not yet taken the lulav that day?
A. He is exempt since he was traveling and could not find one
B. He takes the lulav at his table, interrupting his meal
C. He waits until after the meal is fully complete
D. He must fast until he locates a lulav
Correct: B — The mishna teaches that he takes the lulav at his table — he interrupts his meal to fulfill the mitzva of lulav.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rava resolve the contradiction between interrupting a meal for lulav (required) vs. not interrupting for Shema (not required)?
A. Lulav is a Torah-law mitzva; the Shema obligation in question is Rabbinic
B. Shema is more important and always overrides eating
C. The Shema rule applies only on Shabbat, not weekdays
D. There is no contradiction — both always require interruption
Correct: A — Rava distinguishes: lulav is d'Oraita (Torah law) and requires interrupting a meal; the Shema case may be d'Rabbanan (Rabbinic), which does not.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When a Canaanite slave recites hallel on behalf of an adult male, how does the adult fulfill his obligation?
A. He listens silently and fulfills through shome'a k'oneh alone
B. He is fully exempt and need not do anything
C. He repeats each word after the slave, word for word
D. He must recite the entire hallel independently afterward
Correct: C — When a slave, woman, or minor leads hallel for an adult, that adult repeats every word after them — though the Sages express that this is a shameful situation.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
From where does Rabbi Shimon ben Pazi derive the principle of shome'a k'oneh (hearing = reciting)?
A. From Moshe hearing the Torah at Sinai without speaking
B. From King Josiah fulfilling his obligation when Shaphan read the Torah to him
C. From the Levites listening to the Kohanim's blessings in the Temple
D. From the congregation responding Amen to the prayer leader
Correct: B — The verse praises Josiah for humbling himself when he 'heard' the Torah — implying his hearing the reading from Shaphan was itself the halakhic act of fulfillment.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rava rule about reciting 'Blessed is one who comes in the name of the Lord' during hallel?
A. One may pause after 'Blessed is one who comes' for a moment of reflection
B. This verse should be repeated twice as a sign of special praise
C. It must be recited as a single continuous unit without pausing mid-phrase
D. Only the prayer leader recites this verse; the congregation is silent
Correct: C — Rava rules one must not say 'Blessed is one who comes' and then pause before 'in the name of the Lord' — the whole phrase must be recited as one unbroken unit.
