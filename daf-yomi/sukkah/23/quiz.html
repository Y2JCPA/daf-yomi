Sukkah 23 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the mishna's stated principle, when is a sukka that leans on a tree considered fit?
A. When the tree provides less than half the shade of the sukka
B. When the sukka can remain standing on its own if the tree were removed
C. Only when the tree is fully uprooted before Sukkot begins
D. When the tree is used only as schach and not as a structural wall
Correct: B — The mishna explicitly states: if the sukka is able to remain standing in and of itself after the tree is removed, it is fit. The sukka must be structurally self-supporting independent of the tree.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the incident on the ship, what happened to Rabbi Akiva's sukka, and how did Rabban Gamliel use this?
A. The sukka was washed overboard; Rabban Gamliel ruled it was never valid
B. The sukka survived the voyage; Rabban Gamliel conceded it was valid
C. Wind uprooted it; Rabban Gamliel cited this as proof it was unfit
D. Rain flooded it; both agreed it was invalid from the start
Correct: C — The wind blew and uprooted Rabbi Akiva's sukka. Rabban Gamliel said to him: 'Akiva, where is your sukka?' — using the fact that it could not withstand the wind as evidence that it lacked the structural integrity required of a valid sukka.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Abaye explains Rabbi Meir's decree against using an animal as a sukka wall. What is Abaye's specific concern?
A. Concern lest the animal flee and the wall disappear entirely
B. Concern that an animal creates forbidden noise on Yom Tov
C. Concern lest the animal die, contract, and leave an invalid gap near the schach
D. A Torah prohibition against deriving benefit from an animal on the Festival
Correct: C — Abaye says the concern is lest the animal die. Upon death the carcass contracts, creating a gap of more than three handbreadths between the wall and the schach. Lavud cannot bridge it, and the owner may not notice in time to repair it.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara counter Rava's position that Rabbi Meir's concern is lest the animal flee?
A. Flight is even more uncommon than death, so no decree applies
B. If the animal flees it would be noticeable, so the owner would fix it — no need to decree
C. An animal used as a wall is always tied, making flight impossible
D. Rabbi Meir only prohibits wild animals, not domesticated ones, and wild animals don't flee
Correct: B — The Gemara presents the case of an elephant with a rider: if the elephant flees, it would be clearly noticeable. Since the owner would notice and correct the situation, there is no need for a preemptive decree — thus Rava's rationale is weakened.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Abaye resolve the apparent contradiction between two mishnayot about Rabbi Meir's stance on potential death?
A. He distinguishes between concern for human death versus animal death
B. He assigns each mishna to a different tanna: the lenient one to Rabbi Meir, the stringent one to Rabbi Yehuda
C. He rules that Rabbi Meir changed his opinion after a specific incident
D. He declares the contradiction unresolvable and treats both mishnayot as disputed
Correct: B — Abaye resolves: the mishna that is NOT concerned about potential death (Yevamot) follows Rabbi Meir; the mishna that IS concerned (Gittin) follows Rabbi Yehuda. Each tanna maintains a consistent position across tractates — Rabbi Meir is lenient about potential death, Rabbi Yehuda is stringent.
