🔄 Which Direction of Curvature Disqualifies?

Rava
Only a lulav curved forward (away from the spine, like a sickle) is unfit. Curved backward toward the spine is fit — it still appears as one unified unit.
Rav Naḥman
A lulav curved to either side has the same status as one curved forward — it is unfit in all lateral cases.
Anonymous Opinion
A lulav curved to the side is like one curved backward (toward the spine) — it is fit.
Practical Ruling The dispute between Rav Naḥman and the anonymous opinion is unresolved. The stricter position (Rav Naḥman) is generally followed: side-curvature disqualifies.

🌱 Myrtle Branch: Fit vs. Unfit Conditions

✅ Fit

  • Three leaves emerge from each base — meshulash (Rav Yehuda)
  • Two leaves from one base + one leaf covering them (Rav Kahana)
  • Majority of leaves fell — if dense-leaf pattern still intact
  • Top snipped — if majority of the branch remains
  • Grown in a pot (if it could survive independently)

❌ Unfit

  • Stolen — gezulah
  • Completely dry — yavesh
  • From an asheira (idolatrous tree)
  • From an ir ha-nidaḥat (incited city)
  • Top was cut off — rosh niklam
  • Dense-leaf (meshulash) pattern entirely lost

🕊️ Why Myrtle = the 'Dense-Leaved Tree'

דְּרָכֶיהָ דַרְכֵי נֹעַם וְכָל נְתִיבוֹתֶיהָ שָׁלוֹם — משלי ג:יז
דְּרָכֶיהָ דַרְכֵי נֹעַם The Torah commands taking "boughs of a dense-leaved tree" (עֵץ עָבֹת — Lev. 23:40). The Gemara identifies this by elimination: • Oleander: dense-leaved ✓, but poisonous — contradicts "its ways are ways of pleasantness" (Abaye) ✗ • Oriental plane tree: leaves do not fully cover the wood of the branch ✗ • Myrtle: leaves cover the branch ✓, pleasant and non-harmful ✓, grows in a braid-like chain pattern ✓ Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov adds: the word avot (dense) implies the leaves obscure the branches — uniquely true of myrtle.
