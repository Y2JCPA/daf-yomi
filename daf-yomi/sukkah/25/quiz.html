Sukkah 25 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna on Sukkah 25a, which group is explicitly exempt from the mitzva of sukka?
A. Torah scholars studying at home
B. Those traveling to perform a mitzva
C. Anyone eating a full meal outdoors
D. Kohanim during Temple service
Correct: B — The Mishna states that 'those on the path to perform a mitzva' (הולכי דרכים לדבר מצוה) are exempt from sukka. The ill and their caretakers are also exempt, as are those eating only casual snacks.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why is a groom who marries a virgin exempt from reciting Shema on his wedding night, while a groom marrying a widow is not?
A. Marrying a widow is not a mitzva
B. The virgin bride's wedding has more guests
C. The virgin-groom is overwhelmed with anxiety about his obligation; the widow-groom is not similarly preoccupied
D. The Shema exemption applies only to first marriages
Correct: C — Rav Huna explains that Shema requires concentration, and the groom of a virgin is so intensely preoccupied with fulfilling his marital obligation that he cannot achieve required kavvanah. Marrying a widow does not generate the same overwhelming anxiety.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
The Gemara contrasts a groom's exemption with the case of one whose ship sank at sea. What is the key distinction?
A. Sea travel is forbidden on holidays
B. A shipwreck victim is not Jewish
C. The groom is preoccupied with a mitzva he must perform; the shipwreck victim is preoccupied with personal distress
D. The shipwreck victim has more pressing matters than the groom
Correct: C — The Gemara rules that only preoccupation arising from an active mitzva obligation grants exemption from another mitzva. Personal distress or tragedy — however consuming — does not generate a halakhic exemption.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
From which mitzva is a mourner exempt on the first day of mourning, and what is the source?
A. Sukka — because a sukka is associated with joy
B. Shema — because concentration is impossible while grieving
C. Phylacteries (tefillin) — the word 'splendor' (pe'er) connects tefillin to Ezekiel's mourning verse
D. Pesach sacrifice — impurity of burial
Correct: C — Rav (via R. Abba bar Zavda) derives from Ezekiel 24:17 that a mourner is told not to display 'splendor' (pe'er). Since tefillin are also called pe'er, the mourner is exempt. This applies only to the first day — Amos 8:10 shows primary bitterness lasts one day.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Abaye rule that the wedding celebration cannot simply be moved into the sukka?
A. Eating in a sukka cancels wedding joy
B. The sukka is too small for a full wedding party
C. Rooftop sukkot create a seclusion (yichud) prohibition since walls block street visibility
D. A sukka is not a dignified enough venue for a wedding
Correct: C — Abaye explains that sukkot were typically built on rooftops. Moving the celebration there would leave the bride and groom behind walls that block visibility from the street, violating the prohibition of seclusion (yichud) between a man and a woman.
