📜 The Mishna: Exemptions from Sukka

הולכי דרכים ביום — פטורין מן הסוכה
MISHNA — SUKKAH 25a Three categories exempt from the sukka:

1. 🚶 Mitzva travelers — those on the path to perform a mitzva are exempt during travel

2. 🤒 The ill and their caretakers — illness or caregiving duty overrides the sukka obligation

3. 🍎 Casual eating — a light snack (achilat arai) may be eaten outside the sukka

The Mishna assumes these categories — the Gemara immediately asks: what is the Scriptural source?

💬 Is a Groom Exempt from Shema?

Baraita
A groom is exempt from reciting Shema on his wedding night. He is too preoccupied to achieve the required concentration (kavvanah).
Questioner
'When you walk along the way' — does 'the way' not include a mitzva traveler? Yet the Torah commands them to recite Shema! Why should a groom be exempt?
Rav Huna
The 'way' implies one who can concentrate. A groom is overwhelmed with the mitzva obligation before him — he cannot achieve required intent for Shema.
Questioner
Then even one who marries a widow should be exempt — he too performs a mitzva of marriage!
Baraita
No: marrying a virgin causes intense anxiety about fulfilling the obligation. Marrying a widow does not generate the same overwhelming preoccupation.
RULING A groom marrying a virgin is exempt from Shema on his wedding night. A groom marrying a widow is obligated.

🎉 Wedding Party Exemptions & Rabbi Zeira's Solution

Wedding Party Exemptions (7 Days)

  • Groom + groomsmen + all celebrants exempt from sukka
  • Exempt from prayer — cannot muster proper intent
  • Exempt from phylacteries — excess joy and levity
  • Cannot move wedding INTO sukka (Abaye): rooftop sukka creates forbidden seclusion (yichud)
  • Exemption is because they are osek b'simchat chatan — fully engaged in the mitzva of wedding joy

Rabbi Zeira's Personal Story

  • Married on Erev Sukkot — festival began that night
  • Ate his meals in the sukka ✅
  • Rejoiced in the wedding home ✅
  • Fulfilled two mitzvot simultaneously
  • "All the more so, my heart rejoiced!"
  • Demonstrates one CAN fulfill both if conditions permit
