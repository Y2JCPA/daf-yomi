🏚️ What Does Meduvlelet Mean?

Rav
Meduvlelet means an impoverished/sparse sukka — roofing spread thin with no single gap of 3 handbreadths. This is ONE halakha: shade exceeds sunlight, so it is fit.
Shmuel
Meduvlelet means the roofing is tilted and uneven. The mishna teaches TWO halakhot: (1) a tilted sukka is fit if shade exceeds sunlight; (2) a dense sukka is fit even if stars are invisible.
Practical Result Both agree the sukka is fit — they dispute whether the mishna teaches one principle or two independent rulings.

📏 Two-Layer Roofing: When Is It Fit?

Sukka has two layers of s'chach — upper and lower — with a gap between them
↓
Upper layer ≥ 1 handbreadth wide
'Lower and cast' (gud asik) applies — upper layer is legally projected downward onto lower layer → Fit
Upper layer < 1 handbreadth wide
Proceed to check gap size between layers
↓
Gap < 3 handbreadths
Lavud applies — gap treated as closed, layers combine → Fit
Gap ≥ 3 handbreadths
Layers are legally separate — upper is invalid s'chach, lower inadequate alone → Unfit

⬇️ Abaye vs. Rava: The Double-Layer Dispute

Abaye
Two-layer roofing is fit only if the gap between layers is less than three handbreadths so that lavud applies and they legally combine. A gap of 3+ handbreadths renders it unfit.
Rava
Even if the gap exceeds three handbreadths: if the upper layer is ≥ 1 handbreadth wide, we apply 'lower and cast' — it descends legally to join the lower layer. Proof: impurity laws of double floorboards in Ohalot tractate.
Rav Kahana's Ruling Rav Kahana confirmed Rava's position in the study hall: a handbreadth-wide upper layer suffices to combine layers regardless of gap — just as floorboards of that width create impurity barriers across large spaces.

☀️ Shade vs. Sunlight — Two Standards

From Above (Roofing Ratio)

  • Shade > sunlight → ✅ Fit
  • Shade = sunlight → ❌ Unfit
  • Shade < sunlight → ❌ Unfit
  • Strict standard — measured through the s'chach itself

From Below (Ground Projection)

  • Shade > sunlight → ✅ Fit
  • Shade = sunlight → ✅ Fit (lenient view)
  • Shade < sunlight → ❌ Unfit
  • Lenient standard — measured on the ground inside

Dense Roofing (Stars Invisible)

  • Thick like a house → ✅ Fit
  • Stars invisible → ✅ Still fit
  • No transparency minimum required
  • A baraita explicitly validates this

📐 Applying 'Lower and Cast' — Step by Step

Situation: S'chach is laid in two layers — an upper and lower board — with significant space between them
↓
Key Question: Does the upper layer have a width of at least one handbreadth?
↓
If yes: Apply gud asik mechitza — we legally extend the upper layer downward, treating it as if it rests directly on the lower layer
↓
Result: The two layers legally merge into one valid roofing, regardless of actual gap size between them
↓
Talmudic Proof: Drawn from Ohalot laws — floorboards one handbreadth wide create impurity barriers even across gaps, confirming this principle transfers to sukka law
