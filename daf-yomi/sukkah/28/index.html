🏠 Head in Sukka, Table in House

Beit Shammai — Unfit

  • Ruling: The sukka is invalid
  • The table in the house draws the person out
  • Dwelling is determined by where one eats
  • Even Elders of Beit Shammai ate outside Jerusalem

Beit Hillel — Fit

  • Ruling: The sukka is valid
  • Head and most of body are inside the sukka
  • The body's location is primary, not the table
  • Cites incident: Elders of Beit Shammai themselves reclined this way

מעשה ברבי אליעזר ששבת בגליל העליון
📚 Rabbi Eliezer in the Upper Galilee Rabbi Eliezer stayed in the Upper Galilee and was asked thirty halakhot about the laws of Sukka. To twelve he said: 'I heard this from my teachers.' To eighteen he said: 'I have not heard.'

When challenged — 'Do you only repeat what you heard? Can you not say anything on your own?' — he replied: 'Now you have forced me to say something I did not hear from my teachers.' He then described his character: he had never in his life said anything he had not heard from his teacher, and had never caused his teacher's feet to wait.

👶 Deriving Sukka Exemptions from the Verse

"כָּל הָאֶזְרָח יֵשְׁבוּ בַּסֻּכֹּת"
Leviticus 23:42 — 'All the homeborn shall dwell in sukkot'
↓
הָאֶזְרָח (definite article)
Excludes women — only the male homeborn is included
כָּל (all)
Includes capable minors — those no longer needing their mothers
↓
Halakha l'Moshe miSinai confirms women's exemption
Rava: needed to block a gezerah shavah from Shemini Atzeret that would obligate them

⚖️ Abaye vs. Rava — Why Is a Derivation Needed to Exempt Women?

Abaye
The verse 'the homeborn' actually comes to obligate women — to teach they are included like men. The exemption derives separately from the general rule: women are exempt from time-bound positive mitzvot. No special halakha l'Moshe miSinai is needed.
Rava
A halakha l'Moshe miSinai is specifically required to exempt women. Why? Because one might otherwise derive via gezerah shavah: the word 'sukkot' appears regarding Sukkot AND Shemini Atzeret — just as women are obligated on Shemini Atzeret, so too they should be obligated in sukka!
Rava
The oral tradition blocks this analogy. The verse 'the homeborn' then serves to include minors capable of performing the mitzva in the obligation.
Resolution The definite article 'הָאֶזְרָח' is additionally needed for Yom Kippur — to obligate women in fasting, based on Rav's teaching equating women to men in all Torah punishments and prohibitions.

🌧️ Permanent Residence in the Sukka — The Seven-Day Rules

Beautiful vessels are brought up to the sukka; ordinary vessels remain in the house below
↓
Bible and Mishna study — conducted inside the sukka; one's primary learning space shifts there
↓
Deep Talmudic analysis (שקלא וטריא) — must be conducted outside the sukka (Rava), requiring intense, undistracted focus
↓
Rain permits leaving: when dripping would ruin a dish of porridge — one may vacate the sukka
↓
Shammai the Elder: removed the sukka roof for his newborn grandson — going beyond the letter of the law even for an infant
