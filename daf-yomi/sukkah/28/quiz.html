Sukkah 28 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Beit Hillel, what is the ruling when a person's head and most of his body are in the sukka but his table is in the house?
A. Unfit — the table draws him to eat in the house
B. Fit — the body's location inside the sukka is primary
C. He must move the table into the sukka to fulfill the mitzva
D. It depends on whether he actually eats at the table
Correct: B — Beit Hillel rules the sukka is fit because his head and most of his body are inside. Beit Hillel even cites an incident where Elders of Beit Shammai themselves reclined this way.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava, why was a halakha l'Moshe miSinai specifically needed to exempt women from sukka?
A. Because women are generally exempt from all positive mitzvot
B. To block a gezerah shavah from Shemini Atzeret that would obligate women
C. Because the verse 'the homeborn' explicitly excludes women by its grammar
D. Because Beit Shammai ruled women are fully exempt by biblical law
Correct: B — Rava explains that the word 'sukkot' appears in connection with both Sukkot and Shemini Atzeret. Since women are obligated on Shemini Atzeret, one might derive they are obligated in sukka too. The halakha l'Moshe miSinai blocks this analogy.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What did Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai describe as 'a great matter' (דבר גדול)?
A. The disputes of Abaye and Rava
B. The hermeneutical principles of kal v'chomer and gezerah shavah
C. The secrets of the Ma'aseh Merkavah — the Design of the Divine Chariot
D. The minutiae of the scribes
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that 'a great matter' refers to the secrets of the Ma'aseh Merkavah, the transcendent mysteries of the Divine Chariot. 'A small matter' refers to the halakhic disputes of Abaye and Rava.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rava, where must in-depth Talmudic analysis (shakla v'tarya) be conducted during Sukkot?
A. Inside the sukka, like all other forms of Torah study
B. Outside the sukka, because it requires intense, undistracted concentration
C. In the house only if it is raining
D. Either location — Rava makes no distinction between types of learning
Correct: B — Rava distinguishes: Bible and Mishna study belong inside the sukka, but deep analytical Talmudic discussion (shakla v'tarya) must be outside. The sukka is for dwelling, but certain learning requires focused conditions difficult to maintain there.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The mishna says a minor who 'does not need his mother' is obligated in sukka. According to the school of Rabbi Yannai, what defines this threshold?
A. A minor who has reached the age of bar mitzva
B. A minor who wakes at night without calling out for his mother
C. A minor who can eat and dress himself independently
D. A minor who is at least six years old
Correct: B — The school of Rabbi Yannai defined 'does not need his mother' as a child who wakes during the night without calling out for her — indicating the level of independence that triggers the obligation in sukka.
