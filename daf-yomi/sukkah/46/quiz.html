Sukkah 46 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Yehuda in the name of Shmuel, when is the blessing over the lulav recited?
A. Only on the first day of the Festival
B. On all seven days of the Festival
C. Only on days the mitzva is Torah-mandated
D. On the first and seventh days only
Correct: B — Shmuel holds the lulav blessing is recited all seven days — even on days 2–7 when the mitzva is only by rabbinic law, since rabbinic mitzvot also require blessings.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
From where does the Gemara derive that one may recite 'Who commanded us' over a purely rabbinic mitzva?
A. From 'Remember the Sabbath day' (Exodus 20:8)
B. From 'Do not deviate from their ruling' (Deuteronomy 17:11)
C. From 'You shall teach them to your children' (Deuteronomy 6:7)
D. There is no Scriptural source — it is entirely rabbinic
Correct: B — The Gemara derives it from 'Do not deviate from the ruling they tell you' (Deut. 17:11) — obeying rabbinic authority is itself a Scriptural command, so one can say 'Who commanded us.'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the baraita cited in the Gemara, what blessing does one recite when building a sukka?
A. לֵישֵׁב בַּסֻּכָּה — to dwell in the sukka
B. שֶׁהֶחֱיָנוּ — Who has given us life (Shehecheyanu)
C. Both blessings simultaneously
D. No blessing is recited until one actually enters
Correct: B — The baraita states that when one establishes (builds) a sukka for himself, he recites Shehecheyanu. When he enters to reside in it, he recites the blessing of leisheiv ba-sukka.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Zeira prohibit giving the four species as a gift to a child on the first day of the Festival?
A. Children are exempt from lulav and etrog entirely
B. The four species become muktzeh once a child handles them
C. A child can acquire objects but cannot transfer them back, so the adult would lose ownership
D. The Rabbis required each person to purchase their own set
Correct: C — On the first day, lulav must belong to you (לָכֶם). A child can legally acquire but cannot transfer ownership back to the adult. Gifting to a child thus permanently deprives the adult of the ability to fulfill his Torah obligation.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the Diaspora, when does the halakha (following Sura/Mareimar) permit using the etrog after Sukkot?
A. Immediately after nightfall on the seventh day of Sukkot
B. On the eighth day (first day of Shemini Atzeret)
C. Only after both days of Shemini Atzeret have passed
D. Never — the etrog's sanctity is permanent
Correct: B — The Gemara states that in Sura they acted in accordance with Mareimar and derived benefit from the etrog on the eighth day. The stated halakha follows this practice — the etrog is permitted from the eighth day onward.
