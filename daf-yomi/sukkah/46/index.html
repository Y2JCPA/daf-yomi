🌿 The Lulav Blessing Dispute

Rav Yehuda (Shmuel)
The blessing over lulav is recited all seven days of the Festival — even when the mitzva is rabbinic on days 2–7.
Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi
Only on the first day is lulav a Torah obligation. On days 2–7 it is rabbinic only — no blessing is recited.
Rav (Hanukkah analogy)
Proof from Hanukkah candles: one recites a blessing even though that mitzva is entirely rabbinic. Rabbinic mitzvot require blessings!
Tanna'itic Parallel
This dispute mirrors the tefillin debate: Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi says bless every donning; the Rabbis say once per day suffices.
Core Principle Rabbinic mitzvot require blessings — 'Who commanded us' is derived from 'Do not deviate from their ruling' (Deut. 17:11), making rabbinic authority Scripturally grounded.

📿 Tefillin Blessing — Every Donning or Once Per Day?

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi

  • Recite a blessing every time tefillin are donned
  • Abaye rules the halakha follows this view
  • Mar Zutra observed Rav Pappi blessing every donning
  • Sages of Rav Ashi's school blessed even when touching the tefillin anew

The Rabbis

  • Recite blessing only once per day
  • Rava rules the halakha follows this view
  • Tefillin are worn continuously — one unbroken mitzva act
  • No interruption, no new blessing required

🏠 Sukka Blessings — Step by Step

Building the Sukka: Recite שֶׁהֶחֱיָנוּ (Shehecheyanu) — 'Who has given us life, sustained us, and brought us to this time'
↓
Entering to Reside: Recite לֵישֵׁב בַּסֻּכָּה — 'Who has sanctified us with His mitzvot and commanded us to dwell in the sukka'
↓
Multiple Mitzvot Simultaneously (Baraita): Recite one general blessing: 'Who has sanctified us with His mitzvot and commanded us concerning the mitzvot'
↓
Rabbi Yehuda disagrees: Recite a separate blessing for each and every individual mitzva present
↓
Divine Attribute (Rabbi Zeira): Unlike a human king whose vessel fills up, God fills even those already 'full' — the more one has, the more one can receive

🍋 Can the Etrog Be Used After the Festival?

The seventh day of Sukkot ends — the etrog has fulfilled its mitzva
↓
Reish Lakish
Etrog was set aside only for its mitzva. Once fulfilled, it is permitted immediately — even on the seventh day itself.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Etrog is set aside for the entire day. Forbidden on the seventh day; permitted only from the eighth day onward.
↓
What about sukka boards? Abaye: The sukka is suitable even at twilight of the seventh day (in case a meal arises). Therefore its boards remain forbidden into the eighth day regardless.
↓
Halakha (Eretz Yisrael): Etrog is permitted on the eighth day. Diaspora: Permitted only after both days of Shemini Atzeret (Sura follows Mareimar).

👧 Two Laws of Rabbi Zeira — Children & Honesty Law 1 — Do not give the four species to a child on the first day of the Festival.
A child can acquire objects but cannot legally transfer them back. On the first day, lulav must be 'yours' (לָכֶם — Lev. 23:40). Gifting it to a child means the adult loses ownership and cannot retrieve it to fulfill his own mitzva.

Law 2 — Do not promise a child something you won't give.
Doing so teaches the child to lie. As Jeremiah 9:4 states: 'They have taught their tongues to speak lies.' A parent's word must be kept — integrity begins at home.
