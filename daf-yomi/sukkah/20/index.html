⚖️ Resolving the Mat Dispute — Rav Pappa vs. Rava

Abaye (objection to Rava)
If the dispute were about a small mat, the mishna should have said 'both a large mat AND a small mat' — putting the undisputed case first. The order proves they argue about the large mat.
Baraita (further proof)
'With a large mat one may roof a sukka' — Rabbi Eliezer forbids roofing with a large mat. He is ruling strictly, not leniently. Rava's resolution collapses.
Rav Pappa
Rava's resolution is rejected. For a small mat, everyone agrees: without designation it is presumed for lying — susceptible to impurity, invalid as skhakh.
Rav Pappa
They dispute only the large mat: Rabbis say its default is for roofing → valid skhakh. Rabbi Eliezer says even a large mat defaults to lying → invalid skhakh.
Key Principle Rabbi Eliezer's ruling is a stringency: he presumes all undesignated mats are for lying upon, regardless of size. The Rabbis distinguish based on size.

📏 Mat Types — Skhakh Validity and Ritual Impurity

Small Reed Mat

  • Presumed purpose: lying upon
  • ❌ Invalid as skhakh (all agree)
  • Susceptible to ritual impurity
  • Exception: explicit designation for roofing → valid

Large Reed Mat

  • Rabbis: presumed for roofing → ✅ valid skhakh
  • Rabbi Eliezer: still presumed for lying → ❌ invalid
  • Not susceptible to impurity (Rabbis)
  • Dispute center of the entire sugya

Papyrus / Bulrush Mat

  • Large: ✅ valid skhakh (not for lying)
  • Small: ❌ invalid (for lying)
  • Rabbi Yishmael b. R. Yosei: both valid
  • His father: neither is presumed for lying — both permitted

🔤 What Are Ḥotzalot? — Identifying Ancient Objects

Mishna (Keilim): All ḥotzalot become impure with corpse-impurity
Rabbi Dosa — they are treated as vessels
↓
Gemara asks: What are ḥotzalot?
Rav Avdimi bar Hamduri
Marzovelei
Sacks/pouches used as covers for ale vats
Rabbi Abba clarifies
Mats that serve as covers for vats of ale — woven from papyrus or bulrushes
↓
Contradiction resolved: ḥotzelet with upturned edge (גְּדַנְפָּא) = vessel → susceptible to corpse-impurity.
ḥotzelet without upturned edge = not a vessel → not susceptible.

✨ Reish Lakish Praises Rabbi Ḥiyya and His Sons

אֲנִי כַּפָּרַת רַבִּי חִיָּיא וּבָנָיו
Reish Lakish "I am the atonement for Rabbi Ḥiyya and his sons."

When Torah laws were forgotten from the Jewish people in Eretz Yisrael, Rabbi Ḥiyya and his sons went out into the towns and villages — teaching, planting flax, weaving nets, catching deer, feeding the poor, and teaching Torah — ensuring it would never be forgotten.

Their self-sacrifice in restoring Torah to the people earned them this extraordinary tribute from Reish Lakish, one of the greatest Amoraim.

🛏️ New Mishna: Sleeping Beneath a Bed in the Sukka

Mishna ruling: One who sleeps beneath a bed inside the sukka did not fulfill his sukka obligation.
↓
Reason: The bed constitutes a halachic tent (אֹהֶל) — a barrier between the sleeper and the skhakh above. It is as if he is inside a tent within the sukka.
↓
Rabbi Yehuda: We had a custom of sleeping beneath the bed in front of the Sages, and they said nothing against it. (Implied: it was acceptable.)
↓
Rabbi Shimon: Tavi, the Canaanite slave of Rabban Gamliel, slept beneath the bed. Rabban Gamliel joked to the Elders: 'See my slave Tavi — he knows he is exempt from sukka!' (A slave is exempt; sleeping beneath the bed reinforces exemption, not fulfillment.)
↓
Gemara question: Is the space beneath a bed even 10 tefachim high — the minimum for a halachic tent? The Gemara resolves this from a mishna about tents formed naturally, not by human intention.
