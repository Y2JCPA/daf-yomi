Sukkah 20 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, about which size mat do Rabbi Eliezer and the Rabbis actually disagree?
A. A small mat only
B. A large mat only
C. Both sizes equally
D. Neither — they agree on all mats
Correct: B — Rav Pappa concludes that everyone agrees a small mat defaults to 'for lying upon' (invalid skhakh). The dispute concerns only the large mat: Rabbis permit it as skhakh; Rabbi Eliezer forbids it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What structural feature determines whether a ḥotzelet is susceptible to corpse-impurity?
A. Its size (large vs. small)
B. The material it is woven from (papyrus vs. reed)
C. Whether it has an upturned edge, making it a vessel
D. Whether it was designated for a specific use
Correct: C — The Gemara resolves the contradiction between the mishna and baraita by distinguishing: a ḥotzelet with an upturned edge (gedanpa) qualifies as a vessel, susceptible to corpse-impurity. Without that edge, it is not a vessel.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Reish Lakish declare himself 'the atonement' for Rabbi Ḥiyya and his sons?
A. They personally taught Reish Lakish Torah as a youth
B. They restored Torah laws that had been forgotten in Eretz Yisrael
C. They wrote down the Oral Torah to prevent its loss
D. They defended Reish Lakish against the Sanhedrin
Correct: B — Reish Lakish praised them because when Torah laws were forgotten from the Jewish people in Eretz Yisrael, Rabbi Ḥiyya and his sons went out and restored them through public teaching and extraordinary effort.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did sleeping beneath a bed in a sukka NOT fulfill the obligation?
A. Beds are susceptible to impurity and thus disqualify the sukka
B. One must see the stars through the skhakh to fulfill the mitzva
C. The bed forms a halachic tent, creating a barrier between the person and the skhakh
D. The space beneath the bed is too small to count as a dwelling
Correct: C — The bed constitutes a halachic tent (ohel) that acts as a barrier between the sleeper and the skhakh — as if the person is dwelling inside a tent within the sukka, not directly under the skhakh itself.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What did Rabbi Yishmael report in the name of his father Rabbi Yosei about papyrus and bulrush mats?
A. Both large and small are invalid skhakh because they are too lightweight
B. Large ones are valid skhakh but small ones are invalid
C. Both large and small are valid skhakh since neither is presumed made for lying
D. Both are invalid because papyrus mats are susceptible to ritual impurity
Correct: C — Rabbi Yishmael reported his father's ruling: both plaited and woven mats of papyrus and bulrushes may be used as skhakh — since without explicit designation they are not produced for the purpose of lying upon them, and are therefore not susceptible to impurity.
