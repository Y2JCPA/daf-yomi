QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, why is an eight-cubit sukka with alternating boards and gaps considered fit by everyone?
A. Eight cubits exceeds the minimum size so all gaps are permitted
B. Fit roofing remains in the center and no unfit section alone reaches four cubits
C. Rabbi Meir rules that any gap filled with waste is automatically valid
D. Lavud bridges all the gaps so no opening technically exists
Correct: B — Rav Huna explains that the alternating arrangement leaves fit roofing in the center of the sukka. Even those who hold that four cubits of unfit roofing renders a sukka unfit agree it is fit here — because no single unfit stretch reaches four cubits.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Rav Aḥa and Ravina?
A. Whether lavud applies to gaps larger than four handbreadths
B. Whether lavud applies only at the side of a sukka or also in the center
C. Whether unfit roofing ever renders a sukka unfit
D. Whether Abaye's diminishment ruling applies to small sukkot
Correct: B — Abaye rules that lavud applies when a gap of less than three handbreadths is diminished. Rav Aḥa extends lavud to the center of the sukka; Ravina limits it to the sides only, drawing support from the mishna on skylight impurity.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does pi tikra yored v'sotem mean, and who applies it to sukkot?
A. The thatch falls down and blocks light; applied by Rava
B. The edge of the roof descends and seals like a wall; applied by Abaye
C. The walls rise up and support the roof; applied by both Abaye and Rava
D. Roofing placed before walls is invalid; applied by Rabbi Meir
Correct: B — Pi tikra yored v'sotem is a legal fiction treating the roof's overhang as though it extended downward to the ground, forming a virtual wall. Abaye applies this to validate a portico sukka without posts; Rava rejects it.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Abaye permit eating the tzaḥanta fish of the Bav River?
A. They have clearly visible fins and scales
B. They are caught separately from non-kosher species
C. The majority of fish in that river are kosher species
D. The Bav River's saltiness prevents non-kosher fish from surviving
Correct: C — The Gemara rejects both the salty-water explanation (non-kosher fish do live in salty water) and scale-checking theories, and concludes that Abaye relied on rov — the majority of fish in that river are kosher, so one may follow the majority.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Rava's counter-argument against Abaye in the portico debate?
A. The alleyway crossbeam Tosefta proves lavud never applies in sukkot
B. Shmuel already ruled explicitly against this principle
C. If pi tikra yored applied, removing the middle wall of a beam-roofed sukka would still be valid — an absurd result
D. A portico with posts is also unfit, contradicting Abaye's logic
Correct: C — Rava argues: if Abaye's principle were correct and the roof edge forms a virtual wall, then with straight separated beams even removing the middle wall entirely would leave the sukka fit — since each beam's edge would 'descend.' Rava considers this an obvious absurdity, refuting Abaye's position.
