🏛️ Pi Tikra Yored V'Sotem: Does the Roof's Edge Descend?

Abaye
A portico without posts on its open side — the sukka IS fit. We say pi tikra yored v'sotem: the edge of the roof descends and seals the opening, forming a virtual wall.
Rava
The sukka is UNFIT. We do NOT say the edge of the roof descends and seals. Without actual posts or walls, the open side remains open.
Rava
Proof: If your principle were correct, then with straight separated beams, even removing the middle wall entirely would leave the sukka fit — but surely that cannot be!
Abaye
The Gemara explores whether this dispute parallels Rav vs. Shmuel — but ultimately rejects that equation. Even Abaye would concede Shmuel's case is distinct.
Core Principle פי תקרה יורד וסותם — 'The edge of the roof descends and seals' is a legal fiction treating the roof's overhang as an extended downward wall. Abaye applies it to a portico; Rava rejects the fiction entirely for sukkot.

🪵 Rav Huna's Eight-Cubit Sukka Solution

Setup: A sukka exactly 8 cubits (48 handbreadths) wide. Roofing begins from the side.
↓
Step 1: Place a 4-handbreadth board of fit roofing at the side.
↓
Step 2: Leave a 4-handbreadth stretch of unfit roofing (waste from threshing floor/winepress).
↓
Step 3: A second 4-handbreadth stretch of unfit roofing in the middle — two unfit sections total 8 hb.
↓
Result: Fit roofing remains in the center. No single unfit stretch reaches 4 cubits. Everyone agrees the sukka is fit!

🔗 Lavud in the Center: Rav Aḥa vs. Ravina

Abaye (premise)
A space of 3 handbreadths in a large sukka — if diminished with branches or boards (fit or unfit), lavud applies and the gap is treated as closed.
Rav Aḥa
Lavud applies even in the center of the sukka. Proof from Tosefta: a crossbeam of an alleyway projecting past the wall is treated as legally continuous.
Ravina
Lavud does NOT apply in the center. Beams are different — carrying is rabbinic, so we are lenient. Proof from the mishna: skylight impurity is not bridged by lavud in the center.
Each Side's Proof-Text Rav Aḥa: Tosefta's crossbeam (alleyway). Ravina: Mishna's skylight (ritual impurity) — objects opposite a sub-handbreadth skylight remain pure; lavud is not applied centrally.

🏠 Breached House as Sukka & 🐟 Fish Rulings

Rabbi Yehuda bar Elai — Breached House

  • A house whose wall was breached and one then roofed over the exposed area:
  • → His father's ruling: if the house was ≥ 4 cubits wide, it is a fit sukka
  • Rabbi Yishmael b. Rabbi Yosei challenged him to clarify
  • The breach creates open-air space properly roofed as a valid sukka structure

Abaye — Permitting Small Fish

  • Avroma (abramis): Rabbi Yehuda bar Elai permits — hard to distinguish from non-kosher in mixed nets
  • Tzaḥanta of the Bav River: Abaye permits eating them
  • Salty water? — Rejected: non-kosher fish survive salty water too
  • ✅ True reason: rov — the majority of fish in that river are kosher, so we follow the majority
