🌍 Seventy Bulls, Seventy Nations

אָמַר רַבִּי אֶלְעָזָר: שִׁבְעִים פָּרִים כְּנֶגֶד מִי? כְּנֶגֶד שִׁבְעִים אוּמּוֹת
Aggadic Gem Rabbi Elazar teaches that the seventy bulls sacrificed over the seven days of Sukkot correspond to the seventy nations of the world — Israel brings atonement on behalf of all humanity. Rabbi Yoḥanan mourns: "Woe unto the nations that lost something and do not know what they lost." When the Temple stood, the altar atoned for them; now that it is destroyed, who atones for the nations?

🎺 Trumpet Blasts: One Blast or Many?

R' Aḥa bar Ḥanina
Separate trumpet blasts should be sounded for each additional offering when Shabbat coincides with a Festival — just as the Shabbat song and Festival song are recited separately.
Rava bar Shmuel (Baraita)
"And on your New Moons" (Numbers 10:10) — the plural 'Moons' teaches that when Shabbat and the New Moon coincide, one combined blast covers all additional offerings together.
Ravina
A compromise: one does not sound separate blasts per offering, but extends the duration of the single blast to honor the added additional offerings.
Conclusive Refutation The baraita defeats R' Aḥa: on Shabbat the Shabbat psalm alone was sung — Shabbat and Festival were not combined. This proves each day's character stands alone without separate distinguishing blasts.

🌏 Intermediate Day Prayers: Inside vs. Outside Eretz Yisrael

Inside Eretz Yisrael

  • One day of Yom Tov — no calendar uncertainty
  • Each intermediate day is clearly identified
  • Recite the exact Musaf passage for that day's bulls
  • Seven distinct passages over the full Festival
  • Straightforward progression: 13, 12, 11, 10, 9, 8, 7

Outside Eretz Yisrael

  • Two days of Yom Tov due to calendar uncertainty
  • Uncertainty about each intermediate day too
  • Rava: The seventh day's passage is superseded — only six distinct passages recited
  • Ameimar (Neharde'a): Repeat previous day's offering, then add the next day's
  • Abaye: Third opinion — different approach entirely

🕍 How 24 Priestly Watches Divided the Festival Bulls

Day 1 — 13 bulls: 16 watches sacrifice the bulls, rams, and goat; 4 watches sacrifice 2 daily sheep each; 4 watches sacrifice 1 daily sheep each
↓
Day 2 — 12 bulls: 15 watches sacrifice bulls, rams, and goat; 5 watches sacrifice 2 sheep each; 4 watches sacrifice 1 sheep each
↓
Day 4 — 10 bulls: 13 watches sacrifice bulls, rams, and goat; 3 watches sacrifice 2 sheep each; 8 watches sacrifice 1 sheep each
↓
Day 7: All 24 watches are fully equal — each brings one offering. No differentiation.
↓
Day 8 (Shemini Atzeret): Returns to the standard lottery used on other pilgrimage Festivals — a fresh start with a completely different offering configuration
↓
Key arithmetic: 70 total bulls ÷ 24 watches → 22 watches sacrifice three bulls; 2 watches sacrifice only two bulls

🌍 The Seventy Bulls: From Temple to Nations

70 bulls over 7 days of Sukkot
13+12+11+10+9+8+7 = 70
↓
Rabbi Elazar's Teaching
The 70 bulls correspond to the 70 nations of the world — Israel's Festival offerings atone for all humanity during Sukkot
The King's Parable
After a great feast for all servants, the king tells his beloved: "Make me a small feast — just us." Shemini Atzeret is Israel's intimate day alone with God
↓
Rabbi Yoḥanan: "Woe unto the nations that lost something and do not know what they lost!"
The Temple stood as the world's altar of atonement. With its destruction, the nations lost their intercessor without realizing it.
