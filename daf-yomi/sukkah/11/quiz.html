Sukkah 11 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna in Daf 11, what must be true of schach for the sukka to be valid?
A. It must be at least ten handbreadths above the ground
B. It must not be susceptible to ritual impurity and must grow from the ground
C. It must be cut at least three days before Sukkot
D. It must be placed by a Torah scholar
Correct: B — The Mishna establishes the principle: anything not susceptible to ritual impurity and whose growth is from the ground is valid for schach. Vessels, animal hides, and plants still attached to the ground fail this test.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Rav and Shmuel regarding climbing plants used as schach?
A. Whether climbing plants can ever serve as schach
B. Whether cutting alone suffices or one must also physically move the branches
C. Whether the plants must be replanted after use
D. Whether the sukka must be entirely rebuilt
Correct: B — Rav holds that cutting the plants from the ground is sufficient preparation — their cutting is their preparation (תיקון). Shmuel requires that one also move or lift the branches to perform a visible act of detachment.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does a sheet spread over four posts (kinofot) invalidate a sukka, while a sheet over two posts (naklitin) does not raise the same concern?
A. Four posts are always taller than ten handbreadths
B. Four posts create a flat-roofed full tent; two posts create only an inclined surface
C. Naklitin are made of permitted materials; kinofot are not
D. Two posts require rabbinic but not Torah-level permission
Correct: B — Four posts (kinofot) support a flat roof creating a full tent-within-a-tent structure, invalidating the sukka. Two posts (naklitin) form an inclined surface — though the Gemara notes their being fixed in the bed still creates tent-like issues.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Shmuel (as cited by Rav Mattana), what is the status of tzitzit attached to a garment without first cutting the strands?
A. Valid — cutting afterward renders them fit
B. Valid only if a blessing was recited
C. Unfit forever — no remedy exists
D. Unfit until Shabbat ends
Correct: C — Rav Mattana reports that Shmuel ruled tzitzit attached without pre-cutting are פסולין לעולם — unfit forever with no remedy. This contrasts with Rav's view that subsequent cutting can render them valid.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yehuda derive by Torah law that the lulav must be bound together with the myrtle and willow?
A. From the verse 'I made Israel dwell in sukkot' by analogy to schach
B. Via a verbal analogy (גז׳׳ש) from the word 'taking' in the four species to 'taking' in another context
C. From the requirement that all mitzvot performed together must be physically connected
D. From the rabbinic enactment of Hillel who tied them together
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda derives the lulav binding requirement through a verbal analogy (גזירה שווה) — the term 'taking' (לקיחה) written regarding the four species is linked to 'taking' elsewhere, establishing that all four species must be bound as one unit.
