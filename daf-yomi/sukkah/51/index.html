🎵 What Is the Primary Essence of Temple Song?

Tanna — Instruments Primary
The primary essence of song is musical instruments. Therefore, only those qualified for Temple service (Levites or Israelites) may play — not slaves. The voice merely accompanies.
Tanna — Voice Primary
The primary essence of song is singing with the mouth. Instruments merely accompany — even slaves may play them, since the sacred requirement is fulfilled by vocal song.
Practical Halakhic Difference Whether one who played instruments on the Temple platform (דּוּכָן) can be elevated to presumptive status of distinguished lineage (yichus) and eligibility to receive tithes.

👥 Three Opinions: Who Were the Temple Musicians?

Rabbi Meir

  • Musicians were slaves of priests
  • Holds: primary essence of song is the voice
  • Instruments are secondary — even slaves may play
  • Playing on platform does not confer elevated status

Rabbi Yosei

  • Musicians were free Israelites
  • Holds: primary essence of song is instrumental
  • Only those of proper status may perform the sacred role
  • Platform service can elevate one's presumptive lineage

Rabbi Ḥanina ben Antigonus

  • Musicians were Levites
  • Most restrictive view — Levitical service exclusively
  • Aligns Temple music with the sanctity of the Levitical tribe
  • Consistent with Levites' designated role in Sanctuary worship

🎙️ Does the Song of Drawing Override Shabbat?

Rabbi Yosei bar Yehuda
The Song of the Drawing of the Water overrides Shabbat. It is uniquely tied to the Sukkot water libation and carries special status distinct from other Temple songs.
Rabbis
The song of the Drawing of the Water does not override Shabbat. The mishna says explicitly: the flute of the Place of Drawing overrides neither Shabbat nor the Festival.
Rav Yosef
The core dispute is actually about the Levitical song accompanying daily offerings (shir shel korban). The Drawing song is mentioned because it is the most pressing case — but the same logic governs both.
Rabbi Yirmeya bar Abba
No — the dispute of Rabbi Yosei bar Yehuda and the Rabbis is only about the Drawing song. The song accompanying offerings overrides Shabbat according to everyone.
Key Inference from the Mishna "This is the flute of the Place of the Drawing" — by saying "this," the mishna implies another flute (accompanying offerings) does override Shabbat. This supports Rav Yosef.

💧 Simchat Beit HaSho'eva — Sequence of the Great Celebration

End of the first Festival day: Priests and Levites descend from the Israelites' Courtyard to the Women's Courtyard to prepare
↓
Golden menorot erected: Four bowls atop each candelabrum, four ladders — youths of the priestly families filled and lit them, illuminating all of Jerusalem
↓
Pious men and men of action danced with flaming torches, juggling them before the crowds, singing passages of praise and song before God
↓
Levites performed on lyres, harps, cymbals, and trumpets — standing on the fifteen steps descending from the Israelites' Courtyard
↓
Two priests at the Upper Gate held trumpets — at cockcrow they sounded tekia, terua, tekia and led the procession downward
↓
At the Eastern Gate, facing east, the participants declared: "Our ancestors' backs were to the Sanctuary and their faces to the east — but our eyes are turned to God!"

🏙️ Three Unparalleled Glories

כָּל מִי שֶׁלֹּא רָאָה שִׂמְחַת בֵּית הַשּׁוֹאֵבָה — לֹא רָאָה שִׂמְחָה מִיָּמָיו
Baraita — Three Things Never Witnessed 1. Celebration of the Drawing of the Water — One who did not see it never saw celebration in his life.

2. Jerusalem in its glory — One who did not see it never saw a beautiful city.

3. The Temple in its full construction — One who did not see it never saw a glorious building.

Which Temple? Herod's Temple — built of alternating green-gray and white marble, rows offset to allow ventilation. The Rabbis advised against gold plating: "Leave it — it already resembles the waves of the sea."
