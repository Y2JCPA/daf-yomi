📏 The Ten-Handbreadth Boundary

R. Yosei
The Divine Presence never descended below ten handbreadths, and Moses and Elijah never ascended above ten handbreadths from the ground. "The heavens are the Lord's; the earth He gave to mankind" (Ps. 115:16).
Gemara
But it is written: "And God descended onto Mount Sinai" (Ex. 19:20)! And: "Moses went up to God" (Ex. 19:3)! These seem to contradict your principle!
R. Yosei
God remained ten handbreadths above the mountaintop; Moses remained ten handbreadths below the heavens. The boundary was never breached.
Gemara
But Job 26:9 implies Moses "grasped the face of the Throne of Glory" — surely that is above ten handbreadths!
R. Yosei
The Throne was extended downward to ten handbreadths for Moses. He grasped it from below — the boundary held. Heaven and earth remain eternally separate domains.
The Governing Principle Ten handbreadths is the inviolable threshold between the divine and earthly realms. This same cosmic measure becomes the minimum interior height of a valid sukka — the sukka echoes the sacred dwelling of the Shekhinah.

🏛️ Deriving the Thickness of the Ark Cover

Problem: The Torah specifies no thickness for the kapporet (Ark cover) — yet this dimension is needed to calculate the total height of the Ark, which anchors the 10-handbreadth sukka derivation.
↓
✓ Shewbread Table Border
"A border of one handbreadth around" (Ex. 25:25) — a vessel-to-vessel analogy. Accepted: one derives vessel dimensions from vessels.
✗ Frontplate (Tzitz)
Only two fingerbreadths wide — but rejected: the tzitz is an ornament, not a vessel. Vessel-to-ornament derivation is invalid.
✗ Crown of Vessels
The Torah left the crown's dimensions unspecified — rejected: an unspecified dimension proves nothing about a minimum.
↓
Rav Huna's confirmation (5b): "Upon the face [penei] of the Ark cover" (Lev. 16:14) — no human face measures less than one handbreadth. Thus Ark cover = 1 handbreadth thick.
↓
Final Result: Ark height = 1.5 cubits = 9 hb + Ark cover = 1 hb = Total: 10 handbreadths

👼 What Does the Face of a Cherub Look Like?

R. Abbahu
The face of a keruv is like the face of a child [keravya] — for in Babylonia, a child is called ravya, and ke-ravya = "like a child." Therefore the cherubs in the Tabernacle had young, childlike human faces.
Abaye
Ezekiel 10:14 says: "The face of the first was the face of a cherub, the face of the second was the face of a man." He distinguishes cherub from man! How can you say a cherub's face is a human child's face?
R. Abbahu
Ezekiel himself resolves this: in his first vision (Ez. 1:10) he described the same creature as having "the face of a man" — what he later calls cherub, he earlier called man. A cherub IS a human face — specifically a youthful child's face.
Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov
Tradition confirms: the face of a cherub is no smaller than one handbreadth. This supports the verbal analogy between penei (face of) the Ark cover and the cherubs' faces — both ≥ 1 handbreadth.
Halakhic Significance This establishes that "face" = at least one handbreadth — confirming the Ark cover's thickness and anchoring the calculation for the minimum height of a sukka.

🌿 Two Views on Deriving the Minimum Sukka Height

Rabbi Meir — Derives from Scripture

  • All Tabernacle/Temple cubits = 6 handbreadths (intermediate cubit)
  • Tabernacle height = 10 cubits = 60 handbreadths
  • Ark (9 hb) + Ark cover (1 hb) = 10 handbreadths
  • Cherubs = one-third of Tabernacle = 20 handbreadths tall
  • Wings spread above their heads → canopy at 10 hb above Ark cover
  • The cherub-wing canopy represents a valid covered space = 10 handbreadths ✓
  • → Sukka minimum height = 10 handbreadths (derived)

Rabbi Yehuda — Requires Sinai Tradition

  • Cubit of Tabernacle vessels = 5 handbreadths (small cubit)
  • Ark = 1.5 × 5 = 7.5 hb; Ark cover = 1 hb → total = 8.5 hb
  • Cherubs = one-third of 50 hb = 16⅔ handbreadths
  • The calculation does not produce a neat 10-handbreadth result
  • → Scriptural derivation breaks down
  • → Must rely on Halakha l'Moshe miSinai
  • → The 10-handbreadth minimum was transmitted orally to Moses at Sinai

📐 Step-by-Step: Building the Sukka-Height Proof (R. Meir)

Anchor: The Tabernacle's height — Each beam was 10 cubits (Ex. 26:16). With a 6-handbreadth cubit (R. Meir), 10 cubits = 60 handbreadths.
↓
The Ark's total height — Ark body = 1.5 cubits = 9 hb. Ark cover (kapporet) = 1 hb (derived by vessel-analogy). Total: 10 handbreadths from floor.
↓
The cherubs' height — The cherubs occupied one-third of the Tabernacle's 60 hb = 20 handbreadths tall, standing atop the Ark cover.
↓
The wings spread above their heads — The cherubs' wings arched upward and formed the divine canopy over the Ark cover. The space beneath the outstretched wings = the model for sacred covered space.
↓
Conclusion for sukka law — The canonical divine dwelling-space begins at 10 handbreadths of interior height (from the floor to roofing). A sukka replicates this sacred canopy: minimum interior height = 10 handbreadths.

📜 Are Halakhic Measures Halakha L'Moshe miSinai?

כָּל הַמִּדּוֹת הֲלָכָה לְמֹשֶׁה מִסִּינַי — All the measures are a halakha transmitted to Moses from Sinai (Rav)
הלכה למשה מסיני — A Law Transmitted to Moses from Sinai Rav (cited by R. Ḥiyya bar Ashi): Halakhic measures — olive-bulk, dried fig-bulk, egg-bulk, and others — are a halakha l'Moshe miSinai: received orally at Sinai, not derived from Torah text.

The Gemara challenges: But these measures ARE written in the Torah! Deut. 8:8 lists wheat, barley, vines, figs, pomegranates, olive oil, and honey — these are the very sources for the olive-bulk, fig-bulk, etc. They are not purely oral!

Resolution: The scriptural verses are merely asmakhtot — textual supports or memory-aids found after the fact. The measures themselves were given orally to Moses at Sinai, and later generations located Scriptural anchors for them. This is Rabbi Yehuda's position: the 10-handbreadth sukka minimum has no valid scriptural derivation and thus must be transmitted tradition.

📋 Sukkah 5 — Summary Map

Opening Question: What is the minimum height of a sukka, and what is its source?
↓
Foundation: The cosmic boundary of 10 handbreadths — God above, humanity below. Derived from R. Yosei's principle and confirmed by the Ark + cover = 10 hb.
↓
R. Meir
6-hb cubit → Tabernacle = 60 hb → Cherubs = 20 hb → Wing canopy at 10 hb above Ark cover
→ Scriptural derivation ✓
R. Yehuda
5-hb cubit → Math breaks down → Cannot derive 10 hb from scripture
→ Must be Halakha l'Moshe miSinai
↓
Supporting thread: Ark cover thickness = 1 handbreadth — derived vessel-to-vessel from the shewbread table border, confirmed by Rav Huna from "face of the Ark cover" ≥ human face ≥ 1 hb, and by the cherub's face (= child's face ≥ 1 hb, per R. Abbahu).
↓
Bottom line: Whether derived scripturally (R. Meir) or by oral tradition (R. Yehuda), the minimum sukka height = 10 handbreadths — the threshold of sacred space, mirroring the boundary between heaven and earth.
