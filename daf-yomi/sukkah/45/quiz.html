QUESTION 1 OF 5
Where were the willow branches for the Temple Sukkot ritual gathered, and why was the place called Motza?
A. The Kidron Valley — named for its flowing (mozeh) stream
B. Motza, below Jerusalem — because it was exempted (mutz'at) from the king's taxes
C. The Mount of Olives — named for the abundance (motz'i) of olive oil there
D. Jericho — because water flowed out (yotze) from its springs
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that Motza was a Roman military colony (kelanya) and was called Motza because it was exempted (mutz'at) from royal taxation — the word means 'going out' from the burden of tax.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why were the willow branches eleven cubits tall rather than ten?
A. Ten cubits were needed to reach the altar height, plus one cubit for a decorative top
B. The extra cubit ensured they could be seen above the Temple walls
C. So they would lean one cubit over the top of the ten-cubit altar, fulfilling 'until the horns of the altar'
D. Eleven was a symbolic number corresponding to the eleven spices of the incense
Correct: C — Rabbi Abbahu derives from Psalms 118:27 that the branches must reach 'until the horns of the altar' — the altar was ten cubits tall, so eleven-cubit branches would overhang exactly one cubit over the top.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What did Rabbi Shimon bar Yoḥai claim about his ability to protect the world?
A. His Torah study atones for the sins of his generation only
B. He and the Sanhedrin together can absolve the world from judgment
C. He can absolve the entire world from judgment from the day he was created until now
D. He can protect Israel but not the nations of the world
Correct: C — Ḥizkiya said in the name of Rabbi Yirmeya quoting Rabbi Shimon bar Yoḥai: 'I am able to absolve the entire world from judgment for sins from the day I was created until now' — reflecting his extraordinary spiritual merit.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
From which verse and how did Abaye derive that there are 36 righteous people in every generation?
A. From the 36 altars in the Temple, each representing a righteous soul
B. From Isaiah 30:18 — the gematria of לו ('for Him') equals 36
C. From the 36 tractates of the Mishnah, each protected by one righteous person
D. From Psalms 118:27 — the word isru has a numerical value of 36
Correct: B — Abaye said the world has no fewer than 36 (lamed-vav) righteous people who greet the Divine Presence daily, derived from the word לו in Isaiah 30:18 ('Blessed are all who wait for Him'), whose gematria is 36.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the final halakhic ruling regarding blessings over lulav and sukka, and who transmitted it?
A. Bless lulav 7 days and sukka 1 day — ruling of Shmuel, accepted by Rav Yosef
B. Bless sukka 7 days and lulav 1 day — ruling of Rabbi Yoḥanan as reported by Rabba bar bar Ḥana
C. Bless both lulav and sukka all 7 days — Rabbi Yoḥanan as reported by Ravin, accepted by Rav Yosef
D. No blessing is needed after the first day of Sukkot for either mitzva
Correct: C — Ravin came from Eretz Yisrael and reported that Rabbi Yoḥanan said to bless both sukka and lulav all seven days. Rav Yosef ruled to accept Ravin's version of Rabbi Yoḥanan's opinion over the version transmitted by Rabba bar bar Ḥana.
