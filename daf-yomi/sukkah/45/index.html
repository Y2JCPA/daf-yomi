⚖️ How Many Days to Bless? The Great Lulav–Sukka Dispute

Shmuel
Lulav blessing = 7 days. Sukka blessing = 1 day. A sukka obligation technically exists all year; it only becomes distinctively obligatory on Sukkot — so we bless it just once.
Rabbi Yoḥanan (via Rabba bar bar Ḥana)
Sukka blessing = 7 days. Lulav blessing = 1 day. The Torah explicitly states the sukka mitzva for 7 days; the lulav mitzva is stated for only 1 day — so bless each accordingly.
Ravin (from Eretz Yisrael)
Rabbi Yoḥanan actually said: bless BOTH sukka and lulav all seven days!
Final Ruling — Rav Yosef Accept Ravin's report of Rabbi Yoḥanan: recite a blessing over both the sukka mitzva and the lulav mitzva throughout all seven days of the Festival.

🌿 The Willow Branch Ritual — Step by Step

Descend to Motza — a place below Jerusalem; called Motza because it was exempted (mutz'at) from the king's tax burden
↓
Gather murbiyyot (long willow branches), eleven cubits high
↓
Carry branches to the Temple and stand them upright at the sides of the altar, placed on the altar base
↓
Branches lean one cubit over the top of the ten-cubit altar, fulfilling Psalms 118:27: "until the horns of the altar"
↓
On Shabbat: Branches gathered on Erev Shabbat and stored in golden basins of water so they would not dry out
↓
Rabbi Yoḥanan ben Beroka: On the seventh day, they also brought palm branches (lulav) to the Temple — to honor Israel, who like a palm has only one heart

📏 Deriving the One-Cubit Overhang from Psalms 118:27

Psalms 118:27 — "Bind with dense-leaved branches (ba'avotim) on the Festival until the horns of the altar"
↓
Rabbi Abbahu
Isru = encircle/bind
Branches must encircle the altar and reach up to the horns (top corners)
Mareimar (ruling)
Branches rest on the altar base, not the ground — otherwise 11-cubit branches could not overhang a 10-cubit altar
↓
Conclusion: Willow branches stand on the altar base and lean one cubit above the altar's top surface

✨ Rabbi Shimon bar Yoḥai's Three Bold Claims

ראיתי בני עליה והם מועטים — "I have seen the spiritually elevated, and they are few" (Sukkah 45b)
Rabbi Shimon bar Yoḥai (via Ḥizkiya → Rabbi Yirmeya) 1. Mitzvah objects must stand as they grow in nature
Derived from "Acacia wood, standing" (Exodus 26:15) — the Tabernacle beams stood upright, in the direction they grew.

2. I can absolve the entire world from judgment
From the day I was created until now — the merit of my righteousness suffices for all.

3. The truly righteous (bnei aliyah) are very few:
• If 1,000 exist — I and my son are among them
• If 100 exist — I and my son are among them
• If only 2 — they are I and my son
• If only 1 — it is I alone

🌟 How Many Truly Righteous Exist? A Tension in the Gemara

Rabbi Shimon bar Yoḥai
Those who truly see the Divine Presence through a bright (clear) partition are extremely few — perhaps only I and my son qualify.
Abaye
The world has no fewer than 36 righteous people in every generation who greet the Divine Presence each day — derived from לו (gematria = 36) in Isaiah 30:18: "Blessed are all who wait for Him."
Resolution Two different spiritual tiers: Abaye's 36 righteous greet the Divine Presence regularly. Rabbi Shimon refers to an even rarer elite — those who perceive God through a perfectly transparent, unblemished bright partition with no obscurity whatsoever.
