🌿 Bitter Herbs of the Marsh — Two Explanations

Rav Ḥisda
Bitter herbs that grow in a marsh (מָרוֹר דְּבֵי דָּשָׁא) fulfill the Passover obligation — even though they carry a locational modifier in their name.
Objection from Baraita
For every mitzva requiring hyssop, only standard hyssop qualifies. Greek hyssop, stibium hyssop, desert hyssop, and Roman hyssop are all invalid. The same logic should disqualify 'marsh' bitter herbs!
Abaye
There is a distinction: hyssop subspecies were differentiated and named BEFORE the Torah was given, so the Torah's use of 'hyssop' is precise and excludes them. Bitter herb subspecies were NOT differentiated before Sinai — any bitter herb qualifies.
Rava
Simpler: 'Marsh bitter herbs' is not a distinct species name at all. 'Marsh' only tells you where they typically grow. The plant is simply מָרוֹר — bitter herbs — and therefore fully valid.
Bottom Line Abaye and Rava both validate marsh bitter herbs but disagree on the reasoning: pre-Sinai differentiation (Abaye) vs. mere locational descriptor (Rava).

🌾 Do Stems and Straw Count as Handles? Intent Decides.

Grapes Harvested for Winepress

  • Ruling: Stems are NOT handles
  • Reason: Stems are not wanted — discarded at the press
  • Result: No handle-status; no susceptibility issues
  • Source: Rabbi Abba citing Rav Huna
  • Logic: Since straw (grain) isn't a handle, stems (grapes) certainly aren't

Grain Harvested for S'chach Roofing

  • Ruling: Straw is NOT a handle for the grain
  • Intent is roofing — grain is incidental/unwanted
  • Result: Straw valid for s'chach; grain has no handle
  • Source: Rav Menashya bar Gadda citing Rav Huna
  • Disputed in baraita: fig branches with figs, vines with grapes

Grain Harvested for Food (Reconsidered for S'chach)

  • Original intent: food. New intent: s'chach
  • Rabbis: Reconsideration removes handle status
  • Aḥerim: Straw must be greater than the grain (handles) for valid s'chach
  • Dispute: Does change of mind mid-process affect legal status?
  • Gemara: this may reflect underlying tannaitic dispute

📦 Hyssop Bundle — How Many Stalks Required?

Background: A hyssop (אֵזוֹב) bundle is required for sprinkling in purification rituals. How many stalks must the bundle originally contain and how many must remain?
↓
Rav Ḥisda's framing: The tannaitic dispute concerns a bundle of two items — this is where Rabbis and Rabbi Yosei differ.
↓
First reading of Rabbi Yosei: Since he says remnants of 2 are valid, his origins were apparently also 2 stalks. The Rabbis dispute this — implying they allow origins of 2 as well.
↓
Challenge from baraita: Rabbi Yosei explicitly states — if origins were 2 and remnants are 1, it is unfit. It is fit only when origins are 3 and remnants are 2.
↓
Contradictory baraita: Another baraita says origins of 2, remnants of 1 = fit. And unfit only when origins and remnants are both 1 — this seems to contradict Rabbi Yosei!
↓
Resolution: Emend the contradictory baraita: 'unfit only when its origins, like its remnants, are one' — meaning a single stalk throughout. Two different tannaitic traditions are preserved.
