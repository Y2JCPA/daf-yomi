🌳 What Does 'Hadar' Mean? — Four Interpretations

Baraita
"Fruit of a beautiful tree" — a tree whose trunk and fruit taste alike. What tree is that? The etrog. (But why not pepper? Because pepper cannot be taken as a single valid unit for the mitzva.)
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
Read not hadar (beautiful) but hadir (sheep pen): just as a pen holds large and small, blemished and unblemished animals, so too the etrog bears fruit of all sizes year-round.
Rabbi Abbahu
Read not hadar but haddar — one that dwells on its tree from year to year, always present, always ripening.
Ben Azzai
Read it as idur: in Greek, water is called udor. It is a fruit that grows near water — the etrog needs abundant irrigation to thrive.
Why not pepper? Pepper also tastes like its trunk — but it cannot be taken as a single unit for the mitzva: one pepper is too small; a bundle of many is an invalid taking (lo itztaref).

🚫 Categories of Legally Unfit Etrogs

Idolatry / Incited City

  • Etrog from a worshipped tree (asherah)
  • Or from a city whose residents were incited to idolatry
  • Reason: the entire city is fated for burning
  • Its measure is thus diminished — Unfit

Orla

  • Fruit of a tree in its first three years
  • Debate: no permission to eat — OR — no monetary value
  • Orla may not be sold in the marketplace
  • Status: Unfit by all opinions

Impure Teruma

  • Teruma that became ritually impure
  • Forbidden to eat; must be burned
  • No permission to eat it for anyone
  • Status: Unfit

Pure Teruma

  • L'chatchila: should not take it
  • B'diavad: Fit (if one did take it)
  • Dispute: risks impurity OR only priests may eat
  • Practical case: etrog with teruma flesh, non-sacred peel

🍋 Physical Blemishes — What Disqualifies the Etrog's Form

נִטְּלָה פִּטְמָתוֹ — פָּסוּל / הֶחֱזִיר אֶתְרוֹג — פְּסוּלָה
Physical Disqualifications חזזית — Boil-like Blemishes:
• Majority of the etrog affected → Unfit
• Minority affected → Fit
• Rav (via Rav Ḥisda): the ruling applies only when blemish arose while still on the tree, not after picking

נִטְּלָה פִּטְמָתוֹ — Removed Pitam:
• The pestle-like protuberance at the top was removed → Unfit
• Taught by Rabbi Yitzḥak ben Elazar

Peeled Etrog:
• Fully peeled → Unfit (explicit in mishna)
• Rava: peeled like a red date (only thin outer skin removed, inner peel intact) → Fit
