🍷 Reversing Attributions: Who Fears Potential Death?

Gemara
Contradiction! In the sukka case, Rabbi Meir is concerned about potential death — yet in the tithes case, he permits drinking before separating, showing no such concern?
Abaye
Reverse the attributions in the baraita. Rabbi Meir is concerned about potential death; Rabbi Yehuda is not.
Gemara
Now there is a contradiction in Rabbi Yehuda — unconcerned about death in sukka, yet his tithes ruling implies concern about the wineskin bursting?
Gemara
Rabbi Yehuda's strictness on tithes is not about the wineskin bursting. It is because he rejects breira — one cannot retroactively designate tithes after drinking the wine.
Key Distinction Two separate principles: 'concern for potential death' and 'rejection of breira' — Rabbi Yehuda holds the latter, not the former

💨 Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov: The 'Standing by Air' Principle

כל מחיצה שאינה עומדת מאליה — פסולה
Core Halakhic Rule Any partition that stands by means of air — i.e., by an intangible animating force such as the life force of a living creature — is not a valid partition (mechitza).

Practical difference between two versions of his statement:
An inflated wineskin used as a partition — if the wineskin itself holds its shape, it is valid (the air merely fills it). But if the air alone provides its structure, it is invalid as a partition 'standing by air.'

A human being who stands as a partition: the person establishes the partition intentionally and is therefore fit.

📜 Writing a Get on a Living Creature: Rabbis vs. Rabbi Yosei HaGelili

Rabbi Yosei HaGelili — Forbidden

  • Derives from sefer (scroll): a get must resemble a scroll
  • Just as a scroll is neither alive nor food — so too a get
  • A living creature or food cannot be the surface of a valid get
  • Derives 'total severance' from keritut (expanded form of the root)

The Rabbis — Permitted

  • Had the Torah written basefer (the scroll), it would exclude other surfaces
  • Plain sefer teaches only that writing is required — not a specific surface
  • A living creature or food may validly serve as a get
  • Derive 'total severance' from karet — the get must sever all ties between husband and wife

🌳 Does a Sukka Between Swaying Trees Need a Different Wall?

Mishna: One who builds a sukka between trees, using them as walls — the sukka is valid.
↓
Challenge (Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov): A partition that cannot withstand a typical wind is not a partition. Trees sway — shouldn't they be invalid walls?
↓
Answer 1: The tree trunk does not sway in a typical wind. Only the foliage moves. The trunk constitutes the wall.
↓
New Challenge: But the foliage also sways! If foliage forms part of the measured wall height, it should disqualify the sukka.
↓
Final Answer: The case is where the foliage hangs within 3 handbreadths of the ground — the principle of lavud treats that gap as closed, and the solid trunk provides the full valid wall.
