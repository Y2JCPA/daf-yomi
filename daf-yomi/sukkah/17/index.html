⚖️ Rabba vs. the School of Rav: What Renders a Sukka Unfit?

School of Rav
Rav taught: Space without roofing renders the sukka unfit at 3 handbreadths. Unfit roofing renders it unfit at 4 handbreadths.
Rabba
I proved from the mishna that 3 handbreadths of space renders the sukka unfit. But the mishna's case is really about dofen akuma — give me a different proof for the 4-handbreadth rule!
School of Rav
According to you, Rabba — with less than 4 cubits of unfit roofing and less than 3 handbreadths of space, does the sukka become unfit when they combine?
Rabba
No! Since unfit roofing is measured in cubits and space in handbreadths, their measures are different — they cannot combine. But your view uses the same unit for both, creating an inescapable combining problem!
Core Dispute Rabba: space=3 handbreadths, unfit roofing=4 cubits — different units, no combining. School of Rav: both measured in handbreadths — why don't they combine? The Gemara traces this debate through Sura and Nehardea traditions.

🏚️ The Three Mishna Cases and the Curved Wall Principle

Why does the mishna need three cases?
All invoke dofen akuma — the curved wall allows valid schach to 'extend' over unfit areas
↓
Case 1: Distanced Roofing
Valid schach placed 3+ handbreadths from the wall — unfit. Unique: teaches that even empty space adjacent to a wall can disqualify.
Case 2: Breached House
Roof patched with valid schach, but 4+ cubits of original (unfit) roof remain between schach and wall — unfit. Teaches: unfit roofing also disqualifies.
Case 3: Portico Courtyard
Courtyard on 3 sides with roofed portico; schach placed in center, but portico roof is 4+ cubits wide — unfit. Adds: even entirely valid schach in center can be disqualified by surrounding structure.
↓
Each case is necessary: the breached-house case alone would suggest only unfit roofing disqualifies; the portico case alone would suggest only surrounding structures matter. All three together establish the full scope of dofen akuma.

📐 Abaye Challenges Rabba: The Minimal Sukka Problem

Rabba
Space and unfit roofing have different measures (3 handbreadths vs. 4 cubits), so they cannot combine. This is why the sukka remains fit when each is below its own threshold.
Abaye
But master — in a minimally-sized sukka (7×7 handbreadths), aren't the measures equal? Three handbreadths of space and four handbreadths of unfit roofing together would fill most of the sukka!
Rabba
In the minimal sukka, the unfitness is not because the measures are equal — it is because 3 handbreadths of space alone already renders that sukka unfit! There is no actual combining occurring.
Abaye
But what about the case where there is slightly less than 3 handbreadths of space and slightly less than 4 handbreadths of unfit roofing in the minimal sukka — does that combine to render it unfit?
Rabba
No — since their measures are not equal, they do not combine. The sukka remains fit unless either factor independently reaches its required threshold.
Rabba's Principle Unequal measures (space vs. unfit roofing) do not combine to render a sukka unfit — each must independently reach its own threshold. This differs from how unlike fabrics combine for ritual impurity.

🧵 The Ritual Impurity Analogy — Do Unlike Things Combine?

Challenge to Rabba: A baraita teaches that garment + sackcloth, sackcloth + hide, hide + mat join together for ritual impurity — even though they have different minimum measures!
↓
Why do they combine? Rabbi Shimon explains: all these materials share a common quality — they are each fit to contract ritual impurity through sitting (tum'at midras). Shared fitness = combination.
↓
But is a 1×1 handbreadth cloth even useful? An unused rag cannot contract impurity. Rabbi Shimon ben Lakish in the name of Rabbi Yannai: Yes — a cloth that small is fit to serve as a patch on a hairnet, giving it halakhic utility.
↓
Application to Sukka: Space (absent schach) and unfit roofing (present but invalid schach) do not share a common quality. They are fundamentally different types of unfitness — therefore they cannot combine.
↓
Conclusion: The fabric analogy fails for the sukka case precisely because the fabrics share a common fitness quality while space and unfit roofing do not. Rabba's ruling stands.

🌍 Sura vs. Nehardea: Two Transmissions of the Ruling

Sura — School of Rav

  • Space (no schach): unfit at 3 handbreadths
  • Unfit roofing: unfit at 4 handbreadths
  • Both measured in handbreadths — raises combining question
  • Problem: board of 4 handbreadths makes sukka fit, not unfit — tension with this view for center cases
  • Rav's own school's transmission

Nehardea — Rav Yehuda citing Shmuel

  • Unfit roofing in the center: unfit at 4 cubits
  • Side (near wall): 4 handbreadths — different measures for different locations
  • Proof: Board of 4 handbreadths placed atop sukka = sukka is fit — confirming that 4 handbreadths is not enough to disqualify the center
  • Two sheets combine; two boards do not — sheets can reach 4 cubits together, boards cannot
  • Cedar beams ≥4 handbreadths wide: universally unfit
