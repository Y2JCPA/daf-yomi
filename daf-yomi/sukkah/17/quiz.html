Sukkah 17 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the mishna, how much space between valid schach and the sukka wall renders the sukka unfit?
A. Two handbreadths
B. Three handbreadths
C. Four handbreadths
D. Four cubits
Correct: B — The mishna states explicitly: if one distanced the roofing from the walls at three handbreadths, the sukka is unfit — since three handbreadths of open space adjacent to the wall invalidates it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rabba argued that space and unfit roofing have different measures and therefore cannot combine. What is Rabba's measure for unfit roofing that renders a sukka unfit?
A. Three handbreadths
B. Four handbreadths
C. Four cubits
D. Seven handbreadths
Correct: C — Rabba holds that unfit roofing renders the sukka unfit only with four cubits — a measure in cubits, distinct from the three handbreadths for space. Because the units differ, these factors cannot combine.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Who challenged Rabba with the argument that in a minimally-sized sukka (7×7 handbreadths), the measures of space and unfit roofing effectively become equal?
A. Rava
B. Abaye
C. Rav Yehuda
D. Rav Pappa
Correct: B — Abaye raised this challenge. Rabba responded that in the minimal sukka, the unfitness stems from 3 handbreadths of space alone already being disqualifying — not from a combination of the two factors.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Shimon, why do different fabrics (cloth, sackcloth, hide, mat) combine to constitute the minimum measure for ritual impurity?
A. Because the Torah lists them together in a single verse
B. Because they are all made from natural materials
C. Because they all share fitness to contract impurity through sitting (midras)
D. Because they are all used for the same purpose
Correct: C — Rabbi Shimon teaches that these unlike fabrics combine because they share a common halakhic quality: each is fit to contract ritual impurity through sitting (tum'at midras). Shared fitness enables combination.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What proof does the Gemara cite to support the Nehardea position that unfit roofing in the CENTER of the sukka requires four cubits (not four handbreadths) to render it unfit?
A. A baraita about cedar beams four handbreadths wide
B. The fact that two boards do not combine to render the sukka unfit
C. A mishna stating that a board four handbreadths wide placed atop the sukka renders it FIT
D. Rabbi Meir's ruling about unfit roofing
Correct: C — The Gemara cites a mishna: a board four handbreadths wide placed atop the sukka renders it fit — meaning four handbreadths of unfit material in the center is NOT enough to disqualify it, supporting the Nehardea view that the center requires four cubits.
