⚖️ The Great Sukka Height Dispute

Rabbis
A sukka more than 20 cubits high is unfit. The roofing is too far above to be noticed, shading from the walls rather than the schach, and the structure resembles a permanent dwelling.
Rabbi Yehuda
A sukka even 40 or 50 cubits high is fit. Queen Helene's tall sukka in Lod was accepted by the Elders in silence — and she observed all rabbinic directives.
Three Additional Mishna Disqualifications ① Height under 10 handbreadths ② Fewer than three walls ③ Sunlight through roofing exceeds its shade

📋 Mishna: Valid vs. Invalid Sukka

✅ Valid Sukka

  • Height: at least 10 handbreadths
  • Height: no more than 20 cubits (Rabbis)
  • At least 3 walls
  • Shade from roofing exceeds sunlight
  • Rabbi Yehuda: even above 20 cubits is valid

❌ Invalid Sukka

  • Height exceeds 20 cubits (per Rabbis)
  • Height under 10 handbreadths
  • Fewer than 3 walls
  • Sunlight through schach exceeds its shade

📜 Three Sages, Three Sources for the 20-Cubit Limit

Rabba
Source — Lev 23:43: 'So that your generations will know that I caused Israel to dwell in sukkot.'
Up to 20 cubits the eye catches sight of the roofing and recognizes the mitzva. Above that, the roofing is too high to be noticed.
Rabbi Zeira
Source — Isa 4:6: 'A sukka for shade in the daytime from the heat.'
Up to 20 cubits one sits in the shade of the roofing (schach). Above 20 cubits the shade comes from the walls, not the schach.
Rava
Source — Lev 23:42: 'In sukkot shall you reside seven days.'
The Torah requires a temporary dwelling. A structure over 20 cubits is the height of a permanent home — one does not treat it as temporary.
Why Each Sage Rejected the Others Rabba & Rava: Isa 4:6 is messianic prophecy, not practical law. Rabba & Rabbi Zeira: Lev 23:42 is undercut by Abaye's 'steel partitions' objection. Rabbi Zeira & Rava: Lev 23:43 doesn't address shade or permanence.

🏗️ Abaye's Challenges and the Responses

Rabbi Zeira's Rule: Above 20 cubits, shade comes from the walls, not the schach → unfit
↓
⚡ Abaye's Objection
A sukka built between the mountains of Ashterot Karnayim gets shade from the mountains, not the roofing. Should that also be unfit?
✅ Rabbi Zeira's Reply
Remove the mountains hypothetically — the roofing still casts shade. That sukka is fit. But above 20 cubits, even without walls, the schach cannot shade the interior.
↓
Rava's Rule: Above 20 cubits it resembles a permanent dwelling → unfit
↓
⚡ Abaye's Objection
A sukka with steel partitions under 20 cubits is clearly permanent — yet Rava would call it fit?
✅ Rava's Reply
Up to 20 cubits, even steel walls are deemed temporary because the height itself signals temporariness. Only height determines permanent vs. temporary character.

👑 Queen Helene's Sukka — Rabbi Yehuda's Proof

Queen Helene of Adiabene, a famous convert to Judaism, erected a tall sukka (well above 20 cubits) in the city of Lod.
↓
The Elders of Israel entered and exited her sukka throughout the festival and said nothing — implying they considered it valid.
↓
The Rabbis' counter: Perhaps the Elders were silent because her sons were minors, who are exempt from sukka, and the sukka was built for them — not because the height was acceptable.
↓
Rabbi Yehuda's rebuttal: She had seven sons. It is statistically impossible that all seven were minors simultaneously — at least some must have been adults obligated in the mitzva.
↓
Rabbi Yehuda adds the clincher: 'Furthermore, she performed all her actions only in accordance with the directives of the Sages' — proving she would not violate even rabbinic rulings.
