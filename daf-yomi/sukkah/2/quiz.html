Sukkah 2 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, which of the following renders a sukka unfit?
A. The sukka has exactly three walls
B. The height is exactly 10 handbreadths
C. The shade of the roofing exceeds the sunlight passing through
D. The height exceeds 20 cubits
Correct: D — The Mishna explicitly rules that a sukka more than 20 cubits high is unfit (per the Rabbis). Three walls, exactly 10 handbreadths, and shade exceeding sunlight are all marks of a valid sukka.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
From which verse did Rabba derive that a sukka above 20 cubits is unfit?
A. Isaiah 4:6 — 'a sukka for shade from the heat'
B. Leviticus 23:42 — 'in sukkot shall you reside seven days'
C. Leviticus 23:43 — 'so that your generations will know'
D. Deuteronomy 16:13 — 'you shall make the festival of Sukkot'
Correct: C — Rabba derived the limit from Lev 23:43 — the sukka must be recognizable as the mitzva dwelling. Up to 20 cubits the eye catches sight of the roofing (schach); above that it cannot.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was Abaye's challenge to Rabbi Zeira's shade-based reasoning?
A. One never looks upward while sitting in a sukka
B. A sukka between the mountains of Ashterot Karnayim gets shade from the mountains — should it too be unfit?
C. Isaiah 4:6 describes the messianic era and is not a halakhic source
D. A sukka with steel walls provides no shade regardless of height
Correct: B — Abaye noted that a sukka nestled between mountains in Ashterot Karnayim receives shade from the mountains, not the roofing. Rabbi Zeira replied: hypothetically remove the mountains and the roofing still shades — unlike a sukka above 20 cubits.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How did Rabbi Yehuda use the story of Queen Helene to support his ruling?
A. He proved that women are fully obligated in the mitzva of sukka
B. The Elders entered her tall sukka without objection, and she performed all her actions per the Sages
C. He showed that a sukka without proper walls can still be valid
D. He argued that royalty is exempt from normal sukka height requirements
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda cited the Elders' silence in Queen Helene's tall sukka as proof of its validity, and added that she observed all rabbinic law — refuting claims that the Elders may have had other reasons to stay silent.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Huna (quoting Rav), in what specific case do the Rabbis and Rabbi Yehuda disagree about a sukka above 20 cubits?
A. When the sukka's walls do not reach the roofing
B. When the sukka has exactly three walls
C. When the sukka is less than four by four cubits in area
D. When the sukka is constructed with steel partitions
Correct: C — Rav Huna said the dispute applies specifically when the sukka is not four by four cubits. This aligns with Rabbi Zeira: a spacious sukka of 4×4 cubits generates enough roofing-shade even above 20 cubits, but a smaller one does not.
