💧 The Water Libation Ceremony (Nisuch HaMayim)

Fill a golden jug (3-log capacity) with water drawn from the Siloam pool
↓
Procession enters the Temple through the Water Gate — shofar sounds: tekiah, teruah, tekiah
↓
Priest ascends the altar ramp, turns right, and circles the altar (standard altar ascent rule)
↓
Water poured into the western basin (thin perforation); wine simultaneously into the eastern basin (broad perforation)
↓
Crowd shouts: "Raise your hand!" — ensuring water goes into the water basin, not poured on the priest's feet like the infamous Sadducee

🏺 The Two Altar Basins

Wine Basin — East

  • Located on the east side of the altar
  • Opening: broad — wine is thick and flows slowly
  • Blackened visibly from years of wine residue
  • Used for wine libations throughout the year

Water Basin — West

  • Located on the west side of the altar
  • Opening: thin — water flows faster and needs to slow down
  • Also appeared blackened — from prior wine use before dedication
  • Used only during Sukkot for the water libation rite

😏 The Heretics' War of Joy

Sason (heretic)
I am superior to Simcha! In Isaiah it is written 'sason and simcha' — sason comes first!
Simcha (heretic)
No — I am superior! In Esther it is written 'simcha and sason' — simcha comes first!
Sason (heretic)
You Jews are all destined to draw water for me in the World-to-Come — for it is written: 'With sason you shall draw water'!
Rabbi Abbahu
If the verse said 'for sason' you'd be right. But it says 'with sason' — joy is our instrument for drawing, not a person we serve!
Source Verse וּשְׁאַבְתֶּם מַיִם בְּשָׂשׂוֹן — "With joy [sason] you shall draw water from the springs of salvation" (Isaiah 12:3)

🛖 When to Dismantle the Sukka

You finish eating your final meal on the 7th day of Sukkot
↓
Can you dismantle the sukka right away?
❌ Not yet
Mitzva obligation continues until nightfall — the 7th day is not over at the meal's end
✅ After nightfall
Begin bringing vessels down from the sukka — out of honor for Shemini Atzeret beginning
↓
No vessels to bring down? Light a lamp inside the sukka to mark the transition and distinguish Sukkot from Shemini Atzeret

⛩️ The Rule of Altar Ascent

כָּל הָעוֹלִין לַמִּזְבֵּחַ עוֹלִין דֶּרֶךְ יָמִין וּמַקִּיפִין וְיוֹרְדִין דֶּרֶךְ שְׂמֹאל
Halacha All who ascend the altar: (1) ascend via the right side of the ramp, (2) circle the altar clockwise, (3) descend via the left.

Exception: the priest performing the water libation turns left immediately to access the two basins at the southwest corner of the altar.

Historical note: A Sadducee once poured the water libation over his own feet (rejecting the Oral Law). The furious crowd pelted him with their etrogim — demonstrating how deeply beloved this rabbinic rite was to the Jewish people.
