QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Ḥizkiya, what happens when water for the libation is placed in a consecrated Temple barrel?
A. The water becomes sanctified and fit for use on the altar
B. The water is disqualified if left overnight due to lina (overnight) rules
C. The water may be used only if poured within one hour
D. The barrel loses its consecrated status upon contact with the water
Correct: B — Ḥizkiya rules that Temple vessels consecrate their contents even without explicit intent. Once consecrated, the water becomes subject to the law of lina — remaining overnight disqualifies it, just like other sanctified offerings.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why can't exposed water (that may contain serpent venom) be strained and used for the water libation?
A. Straining is a form of labor prohibited on Yom Tov
B. Rabbi Neḥemya's leniency for strained water applies only to common persons, not Temple libations
C. The straining process itself renders the water impure
D. Exposed water is disqualified only because it spent the night outside, not due to venom
Correct: B — Rabbi Neḥemya permits strained exposed water for an ordinary person. However, the Gemara limits this: his ruling was stated regarding common use, not regarding the elevated standards required for Temple libation service.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the disagreement between Rav Yehuda and Rav Eina about the celebration of Simchat Beit HaShoeva?
A. Whether it lasted five or six days
B. Whether women were permitted to participate in the celebration
C. Whether the celebration was called Sho'eva (Drawing) or Ḥashuva (Significant)
D. Whether the flute or the harp was the primary instrument
Correct: C — Rav Yehuda and Rav Eina debate the name of the celebration: one calls it Sho'eva (named for the water-drawing ceremony) and the other calls it Ḥashuva (emphasizing its supreme significance). Mar Zutra rules the correct name is Sho'eva.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the root of the dispute between Rabbi Yosei bar Yehuda and the Rabbis about whether the flute overrides Shabbat?
A. Whether the water libation is biblically or rabbinically ordained
B. Whether the primary essence of sacrificial song is vocal or instrumental
C. Whether Simchat Beit HaShoeva applies to the first day of Sukkot
D. Whether flute playing constitutes a prohibited Shabbat labor
Correct: B — Rav Yosef identifies the core dispute: Rabbi Yosei bar Yehuda holds that the primary form of biblical song (shir) is instrumental, giving the flute biblical status (and thus the ability to override Shabbat). The Rabbis hold that vocal song is primary and instruments are secondary.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi interpret the verse about the Menorah ('candelabrum of pure gold') using klal u'prat, and what is his conclusion about wooden Temple vessels?
A. 'Candelabrum of' = general; 'pure gold' = particular; 'it shall be made' = general again → includes wood and all materials
B. 'Candelabrum of' = general; 'pure gold' = particular; 'it shall be made' = general again → only gold is permitted; wooden vessels are unfit
C. 'Candelabrum of' = amplification; 'pure gold' = restriction → metals only; wood is included
D. 'Pure gold' restricts only the Menorah itself; other Temple vessels may be made of any material including wood
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi uses klal-prat-klal: 'candelabrum of' (general) → 'pure gold' (particular) → 'it shall be made' (general again). In this method, only what is similar to the particular is included — i.e., only metals. Wood is therefore excluded, making wooden Temple service vessels unfit.
