🥁 Does the Flute Override Shabbat?

Rabbi Yosei bar Yehuda
The flute overrides Shabbat — instrumental song accompanies the Tamid offering and has biblical status.
The Rabbis
The flute does not override even a Festival day — the primary essence of song is the voice, not instruments.
Rav Yosef
Their dispute hinges on whether shir (song) in the Torah primarily means instrumental music — as proven by the debate over wooden Temple service vessels.
Rav Pappa
Rav Yosef is correct: the flute debate flows directly from the question of whether instrumental song is the essence of sacrificial worship.
Mishna Rule The flute is played 5 or 6 days at Simchat Beit HaShoeva — it overrides neither Shabbat nor Yom Tov according to the Mishna.

🪣 Water Libation: When Is It Disqualified?

Three log of water are drawn for the water libation on Sukkot (Nisuch HaMayim)
↓
If brought in a consecrated barrel → water becomes consecrated → subject to lina (overnight) disqualification, like all Temple offerings
↓
Ḥizkiya: Temple vessels consecrate their contents even without intent — so the barrel automatically consecrates the water
↓
Rabbi Yannai / Rabbi Zeira: Even if vessels only consecrate with intent, Rav's ruling still stands — the measure (3 log) was known and intended
↓
If water spilled or was left exposed overnight → disqualified (exposed water = serpent-venom risk; cannot strain away the danger for Temple use)
↓
Rabbi Neḥemya permits strained exposed water for a common person — but this leniency does not extend to Temple libations

📛 What Was the Celebration Called?

Rav Yehuda
The celebration was called Sho'eva (שׁוֹאֵבָה) — the Drawing — named after the drawing of water for the libation.
Rav Eina
The celebration was called Ḥashuva (חֲשׁוּבָה) — the Significant — emphasizing its supreme importance among festival rituals.
Mar Zutra
The correct teaching is Sho'eva — Drawing — as the name directly reflects the water-drawing ceremony at the heart of Simchat Beit HaShoeva.
Talmudic Note Both names capture a truth: the celebration was named for the joyful water-drawing AND was considered the most significant expression of Sukkot joy.

🎼 Two Views on the Essence of Sacrificial Song

Rabbi Yosei bar Yehuda

  • Primary shir = instrumental music
  • Levitical song during Tamid = flute playing
  • Therefore flute has biblical status
  • Overrides Shabbat (like Tamid itself)
  • Wooden Temple vessels are fit — interprets by ribbui u'miut (amplifications & restrictions)

The Rabbis

  • Primary shir = vocal song
  • Instruments are secondary accompaniment
  • Flute has only rabbinic status
  • Does not override even Yom Tov
  • Wooden Temple vessels are unfit — interprets by klal u'prat (generalizations & details)

📯 The Mishna: The Flute of Simchat Beit HaShoeva

חֲלִיל זֶה דּוֹחֶה לֹא אֶת הַשַּׁבָּת וְלֹא אֶת יוֹם טוֹב
משנה — Mishna (Sukkah 5:1) The flute is played on Sukkot for five or six days.

• Five days when the first day of Sukkot falls on a weekday (the six non-Shabbat days minus one Shabbat)
• Six days when the first day falls on Shabbat (all six intermediate days are weekdays)

This is the flute of the Place of the Drawing of the Water — it overrides neither Shabbat nor the Festival (per the Mishna's ruling, in agreement with the Rabbis).

The Gemara's entire discussion of whether the flute overrides Shabbat is the Amoraim probing the reasoning behind the Tanna'itic ruling.
