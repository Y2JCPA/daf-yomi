🌙 Travelers & Guardians: Sukka Exemptions

Day Travelers

  • Exempt from sukka during the day
  • Obligated at night when they stop
  • Active travel overrides sukka obligation

Night Travelers

  • Exempt from sukka at night
  • Obligated during the day when they stop
  • Exemption tracks the time of travel

City Guardians

  • Exempt during their guard shift
  • Day guards: obligated at night; night guards: obligated by day
  • Off-duty time requires sukka

Garden & Orchard Guards

  • Exempt both day and night
  • Abaye: cannot leave post even briefly
  • Rava: a sukka would summon thieves by revealing location

😴 Brief Nap with Phylacteries — Is It Permitted?

Baraita (1st)
A person may take a brief nap with phylacteries, but substantial sleep is forbidden.
Abaye
We should worry a brief nap will become deep sleep — just as we forbid any napping outside the sukka, lest a brief rest become extended sleep!
Rav Mesharshiyya
Your guarantor who keeps you from sleeping too long himself needs a guarantor! The same concern of slipping into deep sleep applies to him too.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
The permissive baraita deals with one who sleeps in another's lap — he relies on the other to rouse him, so deep sleep is not a concern.
Resolution of Three Conflicting Baraitot One baraita allows a brief nap (day, in another's lap); one forbids substantial sleep (day, alone); one is for night — remove phylacteries or leave, then sleep.

🍽️ Three Sages, Three Approaches

סוכה כ״ו ב׳ — מתניתין
Mishna — Casual Eating Outside the Sukka Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai: Given a cooked dish to taste → ate it inside the sukka (stringent).

Rabban Gamliel: Brought two dates and a bucket of water → ate outside the sukka, treating it as a casual snack (lenient).

Rabbi Tzadok: Given less than an egg-bulk → wrapped it in a cloth, did not wash hands, and ate it outside the sukka — below the threshold requiring hand-washing altogether.
