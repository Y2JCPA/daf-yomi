Sukkah 26 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rami bar Yeḥezkel, what is the duration of a 'brief nap' in the context of phylacteries?
A. The time to walk 50 cubits
B. The time to walk 100 cubits
C. The time to recite Shema once
D. 60 heartbeats
Correct: B — Rami bar Yeḥezkel taught that a brief nap equals the time to walk 100 cubits — confirmed also by a supporting baraita.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rava say garden guardians are exempt from sukka even though they could theoretically build one there?
A. Their labor counts as a mitzvah that overrides sukka
B. Building a sukka would reveal their location and attract thieves
C. They are considered travellers under halacha
D. The sukka walls would block their view of the garden
Correct: B — Rava: 'A breach summons the thief.' A sukka signals the guardian's position, inviting thieves to enter elsewhere.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How did Rabbi Yoḥanan resolve the contradiction between baraitot that permit and forbid napping with phylacteries?
A. One baraita refers to Torah scholars, the other to ordinary people
B. One baraita refers to day, the other to night
C. The permissive baraita discusses sleeping in another's lap, where a watcher can rouse you
D. One baraita is a minority opinion and is rejected
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan distinguishes: the permissive baraita deals with one who sleeps in another's lap — the companion ensures he doesn't fall into deep sleep.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What quantity of food constitutes a 'casual meal' that may be eaten outside the sukka?
A. Any amount eaten in under five minutes
B. Less than a ke-zayit (olive-bulk)
C. Two or three egg-bulks of bread
D. Half a standard meal
Correct: C — Rav Yosef rules a casual meal is two or three egg-bulks of bread — though Abaye challenges this with common eating habits.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the mishna (26b), how do Rabban Gamliel and Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai differ in their practice?
A. Rabban Gamliel ate in the sukka; Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai ate outside it
B. Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai ate his taste inside the sukka; Rabban Gamliel ate two dates outside
C. Both ate outside the sukka, disagreeing only about hand-washing
D. Both ate inside, but disagreed about the size of a casual meal
Correct: B — Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai (stringent) ate his taste of cooked food inside the sukka; Rabban Gamliel (lenient) ate two dates and water outside, treating it as a casual snack.
