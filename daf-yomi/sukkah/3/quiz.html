Sukkah 3 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Shmuel bar Yitzḥak, what must a sukka be large enough to contain?
A. One's entire body lying down
B. His head, most of his body, and his table
C. Four cubits by four cubits of space
D. His head and most of his body only
Correct: B — Rav Shmuel bar Yitzḥak rules that a sukka must hold one's head, most of his body, and his table. Though Rabbi Abba was surprised this follows Beit Shammai, he confirmed it is the established halakha.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why were the Elders not surprised or troubled by Queen Helena's small-room sukka?
A. The rooms together exceeded the required minimum size
B. Women are exempt from the mitzva of sukka entirely
C. Her sons used the proper large sukka; she chose small rooms out of modesty
D. Her sukka had extra s'chach covering to compensate for size
Correct: C — The Rabbis explain that Queen Helena's sons resided in a proper full-sized sukka. She sat in smaller rooms out of modesty, to avoid the public eye — so the Elders saw nothing improper in her practice.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's minimum requirement for a fit sukka?
A. Any size that provides shade suffices
B. Must hold one's head and most of his body
C. Must hold head, body, and table
D. Must be at least four cubits by four cubits
Correct: D — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi requires that a sukka have an area of at least four by four cubits. This matches his general threshold for what constitutes a halakhic 'house,' which triggers obligations like mezuza and tzaraat.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which legal obligation does NOT apply to a structure smaller than four cubits by four cubits?
A. Prohibition of chametz on Pesach
B. Mezuza and tzaraat laws
C. Shabbat carrying restrictions
D. Prohibition of mixed species (kilayim)
Correct: B — A structure smaller than 4×4 cubits lacks the status of a halakhic 'house,' and is therefore exempt from mezuza, tzaraat (ritual house impurity), eruv joining, city-limit extension, and division among heirs.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How can a sukka that is more than twenty cubits high be rendered fit?
A. Add additional layers of s'chach to lower the roof
B. Build the walls all the way up to the s'chach
C. Place cushions or blankets on the floor to raise the floor level
D. Suspend a secondary canopy at the twenty-cubit mark
Correct: C — The Gemara rules that placing cushions (כרים) or blankets (כסתות) on the floor raises the effective floor level, reducing the floor-to-s'chach height to twenty cubits or less and rendering the sukka fit.
