📜 How Many Walls Does a Sukka Require?

Rabbis
Ktiv (written spelling) is authoritative.
'Basukkot' written 3× — two defective (no vav) + one full (with vav, counts as two) = 4 sukkot; subtract 1 for the mitzva → 3 walls: two full walls + one handbreadth.
Halakha l'Moshe reduces one wall.
Rabbi Shimon
Kri (vocalization) is authoritative.
All three instances vocalized as plural 'basukkot' → 4 sukkot; subtract 1 → 3 full walls required.
Halakha l'Moshe extends a partition — it never reduces one.
The Core Question Does the Torah's written spelling or its vocalization determine halakhic derivation from the word 'basukkot'?

🌾 Seven Species & Their Halakhic Measures

Barley

  • Corpse bone = barley-grain size
  • Impurity via contact & carrying
  • Does NOT impart tent (ohel) impurity

Vine (Wine)

  • Quarter-log (revi'it) for nazirite
  • Liable to lashes for drinking this amount
  • Halakha l'Moshe mi-Sinai

Fig

  • Dried fig-bulk for Shabbat carrying
  • Minimum food quantity for liability
  • Applies to all fit-for-eating foods

Pomegranate

  • Broken wooden vessel: hole = pomegranate-size → becomes pure
  • Broken vessels cannot contract impurity
  • Standard for ordinary householders' vessels

Olive

  • 'A land of olive oil and honey'
  • Olive-bulk: dominant measure in Torah law
  • Eating forbidden foods, Yom Kippur, etc.

Date (Honey)

  • Large date-bulk for Yom Kippur eating
  • Liable only if eating this minimum
  • Date-honey = standard for this halacha

✍️ Three Opinions on the Rabbis–R. Shimon Dispute

Option 1
Ktiv vs. Kri dispute:
Rabbis: derive from writing → 3 walls (1 minimal).
R. Shimon: derive from vocalization → 3 full walls.
'Basukkot' roofing requires its own verse.
Option 2
All agree kri is authoritative:
Rabbis: need a verse for roofing (s'chach), so fewer wall derivations.
R. Shimon: roofing is obvious — all derivations go to walls.
Option 3
All agree ktiv is authoritative:
Rabbis: halakha l'Moshe reduces one wall → 2 full + 1 handbreadth.
R. Shimon: halakha l'Moshe only extends, never reduces → 3 full walls.
Rav Mattana
Isaiah 4:6 supports R. Shimon:
'A sukka for shade…from storm and rain' — implies substantial enclosure on all sides, requiring three full walls for meaningful shelter.
Isaiah 4:6 "וְסֻכָּה תִּהְיֶה לְצֵל יוֹמָם" — A sukka that provides real shelter implies three full walls

📍 Positioning the One-Handbreadth Wall (Rav's Ruling)

Setup: Per the Rabbis, a valid sukka may have 2 full walls + 1 wall of only one handbreadth
↓
The Question (Rav Kahana & Rav Asi ask Rav): Where exactly does the minimal one-handbreadth wall get positioned?
↓
Rav rules: Place the handbreadth-wall adjacent to one of the full walls, within 3 handbreadths of it
↓
Lavud Principle applies: Any gap less than 3 handbreadths is legally treated as closed — the small wall 'joins' the full wall
↓
Result — the ג (gimel) shape: Two full walls meet at a corner; the handbreadth wall stands near one end, completing the legally required third wall via lavud
