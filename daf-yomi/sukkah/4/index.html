🪵 Do Four Posts Make a Sukka?

Rabbi Ya'akov
Four posts with roofing overhead constitute a fit sukka — if the posts, when grooved and split, would yield enough wood to form valid walls, the sukka is valid.
The Rabbis
Unfit. Four bare posts without actual partitions do not make a valid sukka — we require conspicuous walls, not theoretical projections.
Rav Huna
The dispute only applies when posts are at the edge of a roof — there, Rabbi Ya'akov invokes dofen akuma from the existing house walls below.
Gemara
Refuted! A baraita shows Rabbi Ya'akov deems fit even posts driven into the ground (no house walls present) — Rav Huna's restriction cannot stand.
True Principle Rabbi Ya'akov's actual reasoning: consider whether the posts, if grooved and split into an L-shape, would yield planks wide enough to constitute valid walls. If yes — the sukka is fit.

📏 Reducing a Too-Tall Sukka: What Counts?

Sukka is more than 20 cubits high — must reduce to qualify
↓
Straw — Verbally Nullified
Owner declares: "I will not move this straw" — treated as fixed dirt
Cushions/Blankets Nullified
Owner nullifies for Yom Tov period — but will inevitably need them during the festival
Palm Fronds (Hutzin) Drooping
Ends of fronds fall within 20 cubits — measure only those fronds
↓
✅ Valid
Nullified straw counts as dirt; if fronds below 20 cubits provide more shade than sun → fit sukka
❌ Invalid
Cushion nullification is void — person will use them on Yom Tov, nullification is rendered irrelevant

🏛️ Platform Cases: When Does Dofen Akuma Rescue the Sukka?

Platform at Middle Wall

  • Platform built opposite the entire middle wall
  • Area slightly more than 7×7 handbreadths (4 sq. cubits)
  • Two real walls + dofen akuma bends middle wall = 3 walls
  • ✅ Sukka is FIT

Platform at Side Wall

  • Platform built along a side wall
  • ≥ 4 cubits to opposite wall → only 2 walls total → ❌ UNFIT
  • < 4 cubits to opposite wall → dofen akuma reaches across → ✅ FIT
  • Novel: curved wall applies even to roofing structures, not just breached houses

Platform in Center

  • Platform in middle of sukka — no wall nearby
  • ≥ 4 cubits to wall in every direction → ❌ UNFIT
  • < 4 cubits in all directions → dofen akuma from all four walls → ✅ FIT
  • Novel: curved wall can apply simultaneously in multiple directions

🌿 Palm Fronds Drooping Inward: Does It Fix a Short Sukka?

Abaye
Just as fronds drooping to below 20 cubits help a too-tall sukka, fronds drooping to within 10 handbreadths should help a too-short sukka — measure only the fronds below 10 handbreadths!
Rava
Rejected. Fronds drooping to within 10 handbreadths create a seruḥa — a sagging, cave-like residence. People do not dwell in a sagging residence; this is not a valid dirat kavod (proper dwelling).
Asymmetry Explained Fronds drooping downward from above (toward the ground): creates a cramped, sagging space — invalid. Fronds drooping inward from a too-tall sukka: simply lower the effective roofline — valid. Same motion, opposite rulings, because of the dwelling-quality requirement.

📦 Where Does the 10-Handbreadth Minimum Come From?

וְעָשִׂיתָ אֶת-הָאָרֹן עֲצֵי שִׁטִּים — שְׁתַּיִם וָחֵצִי אַמָּתוֹ אָרְכּוֹ (שמות כה:י)
Source: The Ark of the Covenant Taught by Rav, Rabbi Ḥanina, Rabbi Yoḥanan, and Rav Ḥaviva:

The Ark was 1½ cubits tall (Exodus 25:10). The Temple cubit = 6 handbreadths, so the Ark body = 9 handbreadths.

Add the 1-handbreadth kapporet (golden cover) = 10 handbreadths total.

The Shekhinah (Divine Presence) spoke from above the kapporet — meaning the minimum vertical space for divine dwelling is 10 handbreadths. A sukka commemorates divine shelter; therefore 10 handbreadths is the minimum valid height.
