⚖️ How Many Meals Must One Eat in the Sukka?

Rabbi Eliezer
A person must eat 14 meals in the sukka — one by day and one by night for each of the 7 days. Derived from 'In sukkot shall you reside': just as one's home has fixed meals, so too the sukka.
Rabbis
There is no fixed quota. A sukka is like a permanent home — just as at home one eats when hungry and skips meals when not, so too in the sukka. No mandatory count.
Common Ground Both agree: on the first night of Sukkot there IS an obligation to eat in the sukka — derived by verbal analogy (gezerah shavah) with Passover's first-night matza requirement ('fifteenth day' appears in both).

🌙 First Night Obligation: The Passover Analogy

Question
If sukka resembles a permanent home (Rabbis' view), then even on the first night one should not be obligated — why is the first night different?
Rabbi Yoḥanan
citing Rabbi Shimon ben Yehotzadak
Verbal analogy (gezerah shavah): 'Fifteenth day' appears in both Sukkot (Lev. 23:34) and Passover. Just as Passover first night requires eating matza — 'In the evening you shall eat matzot' (Ex. 12:18) — so too Sukkot first night requires eating in the sukka.
Leviticus 23:42 / Exodus 12:18 The shared phrase 'fifteenth day' creates a binding analogy. If one missed the first-night meal, Rabbi Eliezer allows compensation on Shemini Atzeret — though the Gemara questions what food could serve as compensation.

🏠 Rabbi Eliezer vs. Rabbis: Sukka Rules During the Festival

Rabbi Eliezer

  • Cannot move from one sukka to another during the Festival
  • Cannot build a new sukka during Chol HaMoed if none was built before
  • Source: 'Prepare for yourself the festival of Sukkot for seven days' — one sukka for all seven
  • Exception: If a sukka collapses, it may be rebuilt during Chol HaMoed
  • Praises those who 'do not leave their homes on the Festival'

Rabbis

  • May move from sukka to sukka during the Festival
  • May build a new sukka during Chol HaMoed
  • 'For yourself' only excludes a stolen sukka, not movement
  • 'All homeborn in Israel shall dwell in sukkot' — any valid sukka suffices
  • Greeting one's teacher on the Festival is a separate obligation

👑 The Steward of King Agrippas Consults Rabbi Eliezer

Question 1: I am accustomed to eating only one meal a day. Is that sufficient to fulfill my sukka obligation each day?
↓
Rabbi Eliezer's Answer: No — you must add an extra meal for the sukka. Personal custom does not override the halakhic requirement of two meals daily (day + night).
↓
Question 2: I have two wives — one in Tiberias, one in Tzippori — each with her own sukka. May I travel between them during the Festival?
↓
Rabbi Eliezer's Answer: No — you may not depart from one sukka to another. Remain in one sukka for the entire seven days of the Festival.
↓
Broader Principle: Rabbi Eliezer praised those who do not leave their homes on the Festival, calling it virtuous — though this creates tension with the obligation to greet one's teacher.

📖 Rabbi Eliezer in Upper Galilee: Resolving the Contradiction

Rabbi Eliezer stayed in the sukka of Yoḥanan son of Rabbi Elai in Upper Galilee on Sukkot
↓
❓ Problem: Didn't Rabbi Eliezer forbid moving from one sukka to another? And didn't he praise those who don't leave home on Yom Tov?
↓
Resolution 1
The incident was on Yom Tov itself — he had no other sukka, so this was his only sukka (no 'moving' from another).
Resolution 2
The incident occurred on Chol HaMoed, not Yom Tov — the praise of staying home applies specifically to Yom Tov.
↓
Meanwhile: Yoḥanan twice asked Rabbi Eliezer about spreading a sheet (shade) over the sukka — Rabbi Eliezer deflected both times by redirecting to other guests, avoiding a direct halakhic ruling
