🏛️ The Portico Sukka Dispute

Abaye
The edge of the roof descends and seals — valid both in a field portico and a roofed portico-sukka. Where roofing is level with the portico roof, the edge creates a valid wall.
Rava
I distinguish by posts: A portico with posts on its open side → sukka is fit. A portico without posts → sukka is unfit. The edge-of-roof principle does not apply without structural posts.
Rav Ashi
I found Rav Kahana placing roofing atop a portico without posts and challenged him. He replied: The disparity between the walls was visible from inside and outside — they were plainly two separate structures, so the sukka is fit.
Regional Traditions In Sura: taught as Abaye's edge-of-roof principle. In Pumbedita: taught as Rava's distinction — posts on the open side determine validity.

🌿 Extending Roofing — What Does the Tosefta Teach?

Rabba & Rav Yosef
The Tosefta refers to branches extending before the entrance, with one side wall extending alongside the roofing. Lest you say it lacks a valid third wall — the extending wall is counted.
R' Yoḥanan (via Rabba bar bar Ḥana)
Tosefta addresses a sukka where its majority has shade exceeding sunlight, but the minority has sunlight exceeding shade. The extension saves it — lest you say the minority disqualifies the whole.
Rabbi Oshaya / Rabbi Abba
Tosefta teaches: unfit roofing less than 3 handbreadths in a small sukka. Novel rule: unfit roofing combines with fit roofing to compose the required measure — and one may sleep beneath it, nullified by the majority.
Proof — R' Yitzḥak ben Elyashiv Liquid mortar combines with water to complete 40 se'a for a mikveh — yet mortar itself cannot purify. So too, unfit elements can render the whole fit.

⛺ Circular Hut & Leaning Sukka — Rabbi Eliezer vs. Rabbis

Rabbi Eliezer

  • Circular hut (sloped from center): UNFIT
  • Leaning sukka (against wall): UNFIT
  • Reason: requires distinct vertical walls
  • Concedes: lifted 1 handbreadth off ground → FIT
  • Concedes: distanced 1 handbreadth from wall → FIT
  • Baraita version: Rabbi Eliezer deems sloped tent FIT

Rabbis (Majority)

  • Circular hut (sloped): FIT
  • Leaning sukka: FIT
  • Reason: incline of a tent = legal status of a tent
  • A sloped wall still functions as a valid wall
  • Baraita version: Rabbis deem sloped tent UNFIT
  • Rav Yosef acted leniently based on the baraita

⚖️ Unfit Roofing: Nullification and Combination

Tosefta rule: Fit roofing that consists of waste products extending from the sukka has the status of the sukka itself
↓
Condition: The sukka has 3 walls, required area, and majority shade — the extension merely adds to it
↓
Challenge (Rav Hoshaya): Why state this? Unfit roofing less than 3 handbreadths should simply be treated like empty space — and space that small doesn't invalidate!
↓
Rabbi Abba's distinction: Unfit roofing ≠ empty space. Unlike space, it actively combines with fit roofing to compose the required measure of shade
↓
Moreover, one may sleep beneath the unfit roofing — it is nullified by the surrounding majority of fit roofing
↓
Model: Liquid mortar + water → completes 40 se'a for mikveh. Mortar cannot itself purify, yet it completes the measure. Unfit elements can enable fitness in others.
