Sukkah 19 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, what is the decisive factor in determining whether a portico sukka is valid?
A. Whether the roofing is level with the portico's roof
B. Whether there are posts on the open side of the portico
C. Whether the portico has four cubits of unfit roofing beneath it
D. Whether it was built in a field or a courtyard
Correct: B — Rava holds that posts on the open side of the portico are the deciding factor: with posts → fit; without posts → unfit. This is the Pumbeditan formulation of the dispute, distinguishing Rava from Abaye's edge-of-roof principle.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rabbi Eliezer concedes that a leaning sukka (branches resting against a wall) becomes valid if:
A. Its shade exceeds sunlight over the majority of its area
B. It is lifted one handbreadth off the ground or distanced one handbreadth from the wall
C. The mat used for its roofing was explicitly designated as roofing material
D. It has roofing extending at least three handbreadths beyond the wall
Correct: B — The baraita records that Rabbi Eliezer concedes: lifting the sukka one handbreadth off the ground creates a vertical wall base, or distancing it one handbreadth from the wall creates a valid gap — either renders it fit.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What novel principle does Rabbi Abba establish about unfit roofing that extends from a sukka?
A. Unfit roofing always invalidates the entire sukka regardless of its amount
B. Unfit roofing less than three handbreadths is treated exactly like empty space
C. Unfit roofing combines with fit roofing to compose required shade, and one may sleep beneath it as it is nullified by the majority
D. Unfit roofing can only be counted if it appears in the minority portion of one wall
Correct: C — Rabbi Abba distinguishes unfit roofing from empty space: unlike mere space, unfit roofing combines with fit roofing to compose the required measure of shade. Furthermore, one may sleep beneath it since it is nullified by the surrounding majority of fit roofing.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
A large reed mat with no designation — what is its presumed status?
A. Susceptible to ritual impurity; unfit for sukka roofing
B. Not susceptible to impurity; fit for sukka roofing — presumed made for roofing
C. Susceptible to impurity only according to Rabbi Eliezer
D. Must be evaluated based on whether shade exceeds sunlight
Correct: B — Large mats are not typically used for lying upon, so an undesignated large mat is presumed to have been produced for roofing. Therefore it is not susceptible to ritual impurity and is fit for use as sukka roofing.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Abaye challenged Rav Yosef for sleeping inside a netted bridal canopy within the sukka. How did Rav Yosef defend himself?
A. The canopy was less than ten handbreadths high and thus not a real tent
B. He cited a baraita where the attributions are reversed — Rabbi Eliezer deems it fit and the Rabbis deem it unfit
C. The canopy's netting was made of fit roofing material and did not invalidate the sukka
D. He argued that one may always sleep in any tent within a valid sukka
Correct: B — Rav Yosef cited a baraita in which the positions are reversed from the mishna: Rabbi Eliezer deems the sloped-tent sukka fit and the Rabbis deem it unfit. Rav Yosef then validated the baraita by showing it is supported by another tannaitic source, justifying his reliance on it.
