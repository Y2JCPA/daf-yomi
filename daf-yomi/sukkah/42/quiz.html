QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, what specific scenario does the baraita describe when it rules one is exempt for carrying lulav on Shabbat?
A. He carried it upright, which is not the normal manner of carrying
B. He carried it in a vessel before he had fulfilled his mitzva obligation
C. He carried it after already fulfilling the mitzva
D. He carried it to give to someone else who had not yet fulfilled the mitzva
Correct: B — Rava explains the baraita deals with a case where the lulav was carried inside a vessel — constituting normal carrying — but the person had not yet fulfilled his mitzva obligation.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the very first thing a father must teach a child who has just begun to speak?
A. The full text of the Shema
B. The verse: 'Torah tziva lanu Moshe, morasha kehillat Yaakov' (Deuteronomy 33:4)
C. How to recite blessings before eating
D. The blessing over the Torah (Birkat HaTorah)
Correct: B — Rav Hamnuna teaches that the first verse a father teaches a child who has learned to speak is Deuteronomy 33:4 — 'Moses commanded us Torah, an inheritance of the congregation of Jacob.'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Under what condition may one eat meat that a minor has slaughtered (shechita)?
A. Once he reaches bar mitzva age, retroactively
B. When he knows how to slaughter, provided an adult is standing over him supervising
C. When he can eat an olive-bulk of roasted meat himself
D. Never — a minor's shechita is categorically invalid
Correct: B — Rav Huna specifies that a minor's shechita may be eaten, but only provided that a competent adult stands over him and oversees the slaughter.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did the court change the Temple procedure for taking the lulav when the first day of Sukkot fell on Shabbat?
A. The lulavim were damaged by overnight storage in the exedra
B. People were arriving so late that the mitzva could not be fulfilled properly
C. People came to blows in the chaos of snatching their lulavim from the attendants
D. The priests objected to non-sacred items being stored in the Temple courtyard
Correct: C — When attendants threw the lulavim before the crowd, the scramble to retrieve them turned violent — people struck one another. The court, seeing a danger to life, decreed that people should fulfill the mitzva at home that day.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When does the mitzva of the willow branch encircling the altar last seven days (rather than six)?
A. When the first day of Sukkot falls on Shabbat
B. When Hoshana Rabba (the seventh day of the willow mitzva) falls on Shabbat
C. Every year — the willow branch mitzva is always seven days
D. When both the first and seventh days of Sukkot fall on weekdays
Correct: B — The willow branch mitzva lasts seven days when Hoshana Rabba itself (the seventh day) falls on Shabbat. Because it is a Torah-level mitzva on that day, it overrides Shabbat — adding the full seventh day.
