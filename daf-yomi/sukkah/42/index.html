🌿 Carrying Lulav on Shabbat: Exempt or Liable?

Baraita
If one already fulfilled the mitzva of lulav and then carries it out on Shabbat, he is liable for a sin-offering — the lulav is now an ordinary object with no mitzva protection
Gemara Challenge
But is this even possible? A lulav is over ten tefachim tall — carrying it upright is not the normal manner of carrying, so there should be no liability at all!
Rava
The case is where he carried the lulav inside a vessel — that counts as normal carrying. And he had not yet fulfilled his obligation. The baraita exempts one who erred while performing a mitzva
Gemara Challenge
But Rava himself said: one who performs a mitzva unwittingly is exempt! Why does the baraita need to restrict the exemption to the unfulfilled case? The mitzva-doer should always be exempt!
Core Principle One who errs in the course of performing a mitzva is exempt from a sin-offering — the mitzva act itself provides legal shelter from Shabbat liability

🔄 When Does the Mitzva Override Shabbat?

Does the first day of Sukkot fall on Shabbat?
✅ Yes
Lulav taken on Shabbat in the Temple — Torah mitzva overrides
(Outside the Temple: Rabbis prohibited, due to carrying concern)
❌ No
Lulav taken for six days only — no Shabbat conflict
↓
Does the seventh day of the willow branch (Hoshana Rabba) fall on Shabbat?
✅ Yes
Willow branch circling on Shabbat — Torah mitzva overrides even set-aside (muktzeh) prohibition
❌ No
Willow branch taken for six days only
↓
Both mitzvot: 6 or 7 days — the number depends on which day of the week Shabbat falls that year

🏛️ Temple Lulav Procedure When First Day = Shabbat

Friday afternoon: All the people bring their personal lulavim to the Temple Mount before Shabbat
↓
Temple attendants receive the lulavim and arrange them in the exedra (large hall) on the Temple Mount for safekeeping
↓
Shabbat morning (first day of Sukkot): Everyone rises early and streams to the Temple to retrieve their lulav
↓
The attendants throw the lulavim before the assembled crowd — each person scrambles to retrieve their own
↓
Chaos erupts: people struck one another in the frenzy; the court saw potential for serious injury
↓
Court's Reform: Each person fulfills the mitzva at home — they do not bring lulavim to the Temple that day, eliminating the dangerous scramble

🐦 Erring in Mitzvot: Three Cases, One Principle

Lulav Carrier on Shabbat

  • Carried lulav in a vessel on Shabbat
  • Intending to perform the mitzva
  • Exempt from sin-offering
  • Mitzva performance = protective intent
  • Primary case in the baraita

Priest Who Misidentified Bird

  • Burnt-offering bird found among sin-offering birds
  • Priest ate it thinking it was a sin-offering
  • Exempt — erred in a mitzva context
  • Rav Huna citing Rabbi Yosei: same rule applies
  • Teaches the principle is general, not isolated

Uninspected Daily Offering (Objection)

  • Slaughtered daily offering without inspecting for blemish
  • Blemish found afterward — sacrifice invalid
  • Rabbi Yosei: liable? (apparent objection)
  • Rav Huna: that baraita text is corrupt
  • Reliable version: also exempt — consistent with the rule
