QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the view that the 8th day has the same status as the 7th day of Sukkot, what blessing is recited on the 8th day regarding the sukka?
A. Shehecheyanu only, not the sukka blessing
B. The blessing 'to reside in the sukka' (לישב בסוכה)
C. No blessing at all — one simply sits in the sukka
D. Both Shehecheyanu and the sukka blessing together
Correct: B — According to the view that the 8th day has the status of the 7th day, the laws of Sukkot continue — so one recites לישב בסוכה (to reside in the sukka) just as on all previous days of the festival.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Ravina distinguishes the 8th day of Sukkot from the 7th day of Passover. What is his key argument for why only Shemini Atzeret needs its own Shehecheyanu?
A. Sukkot is longer than Passover so gets more blessings
B. The 8th day is distinct even from the 7th day of Sukkot, but the 7th of Passover is not distinct from the 6th
C. The 7th day of Passover also requires its own Shehecheyanu
D. Matza is obligatory on all seven nights of Passover
Correct: B — Ravina argues that the 8th day of Sukkot breaks sharply even from the day before it — different lulav, sukka, and sacrifice rules. But the 7th day of Passover has the exact same laws as the 6th day, so no distinction exists that would warrant a separate blessing.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak proves the 8th day is distinct by citing a verse from Numbers 29:35. What unique term in that verse marks the 8th day as independent?
A. The phrase 'you shall do no manner of servile labor'
B. The word עֲצֶרֶת (solemn assembly/atzeret)
C. The mention of a burnt-offering
D. The phrase 'on the eighth day'
Correct: B — The verse uses the term עֲצֶרֶת (atzeret/solemn assembly) specifically for the 8th day. Other days of the festival lack this designation, marking Shemini Atzeret as a standalone gathering with its own identity.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov derive that first fruits (bikkurim) require waving (תנופה)?
A. From an explicit verse that directly commands waving of bikkurim
B. By a verbal analogy (גזירה שווה) between the word 'hand' in the bikkurim verse and 'hand' in the peace-offering verse
C. From the same verse that requires overnight stay in Jerusalem
D. From the proximity of bikkurim and wave-offering laws in the Torah
Correct: B — Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov uses a gezeira shava (verbal analogy): the word יָד (hand) appears in the bikkurim passage and in the peace-offering passage — linking their waving requirements. Just as peace-offerings are waved, so too are first fruits.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the final halachic ruling of the Gemara regarding reciting Shehecheyanu on Shemini Atzeret?
A. Rav Sheshet's position prevails — do not recite Shehecheyanu
B. It remains an unresolved dispute (תיקו)
C. Rav Naḥman's position prevails — recite Shehecheyanu, confirmed by a baraita
D. Recite Shehecheyanu only if one also fulfills the sukka obligation that night
Correct: C — The Gemara records that Rav Naḥman rules to recite Shehecheyanu on the 8th day, and then notes that a baraita was taught in accordance with his opinion — giving his ruling authoritative halakhic support.
