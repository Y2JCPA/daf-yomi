❓ Is There Disqualification with Mitzvot?

Rabbi Yirmeya
Dilemma: if the myrtle top was severed on Festival eve, and a gallnut berry grew in that place on the Festival itself — is it now fit? Does the prior disqualification permanently linger?
Rav Pappa
From the blood-covering case we derive: there is NO disqualification with mitzvot. Once the cause of unfitness is removed, the obligation to perform the mitzva returns in full.
Rabbi Yirmeya
But was Rav Pappa certain of this principle, or was he himself in doubt? Perhaps his ruling on the blood case was tentative — and the question of mitzvot-disqualification remains open.
Gemara
The dilemma remains unresolved — תֵּיקוּ. It is the certainty of Rav Pappa's ruling itself that is at issue.
Blood-Covering Precedent (Chullin) If wind covered sacrificial blood (exempting the owner), then wind uncovered it — he must cover it again. This implies a prior exemption does not permanently discharge the obligation.

🔗 Does Lulav Require Binding?

Rabbi Yehuda — Binding Required

  • Lulav requires binding by Torah law
  • Derives via gezerah shavah: 'taking' (לְקִיחָה) by lulav = 'taking' by Paschal lamb
  • Paschal lamb required binding of the bundle → so too lulav
  • Consequence: lulav derives from sukka → picked berries disqualify branch permanently like invalid schach

The Rabbis — No Binding Required

  • Lulav does NOT require binding by Torah law
  • Four species taken independently, not as a bound bundle
  • No valid gezerah shavah link to the Paschal lamb
  • Consequence: even if lulav derives from sukka, picking berries does not cause permanent disqualification

🌊 Willow Branch Requirements (New Mishna)

Stolen willow → Unfit (מצוה הבאה בעבירה)
↓
Completely dry willow → Unfit
↓
From an asheira (idolatrous tree) or from a city incited to idolatry → Unfit
↓
Top severed or leaves severed → Unfit
↓
Tzaftzafa (a different look-alike species) → Unfit
↓
Aravei nachal (ערבי נחל): grows by a brook — OR — leaf is elongated like a brook; both are valid identifiers of the correct species
