⚗️ Sabbatical-Year Produce: When Is Sanctity Transferred?

Rav Ashi (1st position)
The dispute — whether sanctity of Shabbat-year produce passes via redemption or only via purchase — applies to original Sabbatical-Year produce itself.
Ravina
Objection! The baraita discusses a Shabbat-year sela used to buy a garment — that is secondary produce, not original. Your position is refuted!
Rav Ashi (revised)
Corrected: The dispute applies only to secondary produce (money imbued with Shabbat-year sanctity). As for original produce — everyone agrees: purchase works; mere redemption does not.
Key Principle Original Shabbat-year produce is deconsecrated only via purchase (קנייה), not redemption (פדיון). The debate is about secondary-level sanctity.

🌿 Lulav Obligation: Temple Era vs. Post-Destruction

🕍 In the Temple

  • Lulav taken all 7 days — Torah law
  • Based on: 'You shall rejoice before the Lord seven days'
  • Outside Temple: only Day 1 obligation
  • New grain: prohibited only until omer offering

🌍 Post-Destruction (RYbZ Ordinance)

  • Lulav taken all 7 days everywhere — rabbinic ordinance
  • זֵכֶר לַמִּקְדָּשׁ — In memory of the Temple
  • Applies throughout the Diaspora and Land of Israel
  • New grain: prohibited the entire 16th of Nisan

🌾 Why Prohibit New Grain All Day? — The Omer Rebuilding Debate

When would the Temple be rebuilt that creates a problem?
↓
Rebuilt during the day (16th Nisan)
Dawn already permitted eating — omer was never brought yet. Problem seems real!
Rebuilt at night (eve of 16th)
No chance to cut the omer that night — entire following day is problematic.
↓
Resolution from verse: 'Until this selfsame [etzem] day' (Lev. 23:14) — עֶצֶם הַיּוֹם = the essence of the day, not the preceding night
↓
RYbZ's ordinance stands: Prohibit all day as precaution — when Temple is rebuilt, the public will not accidentally eat new grain before the omer

⛵ The Ship Story — Lulav as Gift and 'For Yourselves'

Torah (Lev. 23:40)
'And you shall take for yourselves on the first day' — לָכֶם: the lulav must be yours. A borrowed lulav does not fulfill the obligation on Day 1.
Incident on Ship
Rabban Gamliel, R. Yehoshua, R. Elazar b. Azarya, and R. Akiva traveled during Sukkot. Only RG had a lulav — bought for 1000 zuz. He gave it as a gift to each in turn; each returned it.
Rava
If one gives an etrog 'on condition that you return it' — and the recipient takes it, fulfills his obligation, and returns it — he has fulfilled his obligation, since it was a valid gift at the moment of use.
Gemara
Why mention the price of 1000 zuz? To show how beloved mitzvot were to the Sages — willing to pay exorbitant sums for proper mitzva performance.
Halakha Day 1 of Sukkot: lulav must be owned outright (לָכֶם). A genuine gift — even conditional — works. A loan does not.

🏙️ The Custom of Jerusalem — Mitzva with All-Day Love

כָּךְ הָיָה מִנְהָגָן שֶׁל אַנְשֵׁי יְרוּשָׁלַיִם
מִנְהַג יְרוּשָׁלַיִם Rabbi Elazar bar Tzadok teaches: The people of Jerusalem would leave home with lulav in hand — they went to synagogue with it, learned Torah with it, prayed with it, and visited the sick with it, all day long.

What does the baraita teach us? How vigilant they were in mitzva performance and how much they cherished mitzvot.

Mar bar Ameimar: My father would pray while holding the four species — expressing love for the mitzva. (Gemara: permitted, since holding them is itself the mitzva, not a distraction from it.)
