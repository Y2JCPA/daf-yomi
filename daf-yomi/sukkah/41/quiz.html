QUESTION 1 OF 5
Before Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai's ordinance, for how many days was the lulav taken in the Temple versus outside the Temple?
A. 7 days in Temple; 7 days outside
B. 7 days in Temple; 1 day outside
C. 1 day in Temple; 7 days outside
D. 1 day in both places
Correct: B — The Mishna states: in the Temple, lulav was taken 7 days (Torah law); outside, only 1 day. RYbZ's ordinance extended the 7-day practice everywhere as zecher l'mikdash.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What verse does Rabbi Yochanan cite as the biblical source for instituting commemorative ordinances (zecher l'mikdash)?
A. 'You shall rejoice before the Lord your God seven days' (Lev. 23)
B. 'And you shall take for yourselves on the first day' (Lev. 23:40)
C. 'For I will restore health unto you and heal your wounds' (Jer. 30:17)
D. 'Until this selfsame day' (Lev. 23:14)
Correct: C — Rabbi Yochanan derives the concept of zecher l'mikdash from Jeremiah 30:17, which implies the nations will recognize Jerusalem's restoration — hence we institute practices to ease the transition when the Temple is rebuilt.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi's final position, where does the dispute about Sabbatical-Year produce (redemption vs. purchase) apply?
A. Original Sabbatical-Year produce only
B. Secondary produce (money imbued with Shabbat-year sanctity) only
C. Both original and secondary produce equally
D. Only produce grown in the seventh year of the cycle
Correct: B — After Ravina's objection, Rav Ashi revised: the dispute applies only to secondary produce. For original produce everyone agrees: purchase deconsecrates; redemption alone does not.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rava rules: if one gives an etrog 'on condition you return it,' and the recipient fulfills his obligation and returns it — what is the halakhic outcome?
A. He does not fulfill his obligation — the condition invalidates ownership
B. He fulfills his obligation — it was a valid gift at the moment of use
C. It is a doubt (safek) and he must take another etrog
D. He fulfills his obligation only if the giver explicitly waived the condition
Correct: B — Rava rules the obligation is fulfilled. At the moment of use the recipient genuinely owned the etrog as a gift; returning it afterward does not retroactively cancel the gift at that time.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rabbi Elazar bar Tzadok describe as the custom of the people of Jerusalem regarding the lulav during Sukkot?
A. They waved the lulav in all six directions three times during Hallel
B. They gave their lulav to their children after morning prayer
C. They carried the lulav with them all day — to synagogue, Torah study, prayer, and visiting the sick
D. They would only hold the lulav while reciting the blessing
Correct: C — Rabbi Elazar bar Tzadok describes carrying the lulav all day long in every activity. The Gemara explains: this teaches us how vigilant and cherishing the people of Jerusalem were in mitzva performance.
