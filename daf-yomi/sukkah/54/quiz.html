Sukkah 54 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara conclude that Rav Kahana's statement follows Rabbi Yehuda rather than the Rabbis?
A. Rav Kahana explicitly stated 'I follow Rabbi Yehuda' in a parallel passage
B. The emphatic phrase 'at all' would be superfluous if he merely excluded Rabbi Yoḥanan's view
C. The Rabbis' opinion was rejected by all Amoraim of Rav Kahana's generation
D. The Gemara concludes Rav Kahana follows neither and offers a third view
Correct: B — The Gemara argues: if Rav Kahana only meant to exclude Rabbi Yoḥanan, the word 'at all' adds nothing — its presence signals a broader exclusion, consistent with Rabbi Yehuda's more restrictive position.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov, why were trumpets NOT sounded upon arriving at the 10th stair?
A. The 10th stair was outside the area of priestly holiness
B. Only Levites, not priests, were stationed at the 10th stair
C. Having already sounded for the gate-opening, blasting again at the stairs was redundant
D. The water for the libation had not yet been drawn at that point
Correct: C — Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov's reasoning: the trumpets already sounded for the opening of the gates — sounding them again at the 10th stair for the same procession serves no independent purpose.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rava's actual explanation for why the blast count on Shabbat during Sukkot does not exceed 48?
A. Fewer animal offerings were brought on Shabbat than on weekdays
B. The Levites rested from singing on Shabbat afternoon
C. Water was not drawn for the libation on Shabbat, so the associated trumpets were omitted
D. The gate-opening ceremony was abbreviated on Shabbat
Correct: C — Rava: since drawing water is prohibited on Shabbat, the water libation trumpets (normally three blasts) were not sounded — this is why the total stays at 48 rather than exceeding it.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why is Rosh HaShana postponed when it would cause the first day of Sukkot to fall on Shabbat eve?
A. To ensure the water libation ceremony falls on a full weekday
B. To prevent Yom Kippur from falling on Sunday, creating two consecutive days without cooking
C. To allow the Paschal lamb sacrifice to occur on its proper calendar date
D. To ensure Shemini Atzeret never coincides with a weekday
Correct: B — If Yom Kippur (10 Tishrei) fell on Sunday, it would create back-to-back days of no food preparation alongside Shabbat. The postponement (adding a day to Elul) prevents this hardship.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, why does the New Moon song precede the Shabbat song when Rosh Chodesh falls on Shabbat — contrary to the usual rule that the more frequent takes precedence?
A. New Moon offerings are more numerous and therefore more significant than Shabbat offerings
B. The Levites received a special tradition from Moses to always sing New Moon first
C. To inform the people that the court had established the New Moon at its proper time
D. Because the New Moon musaf is sacrificed before the Shabbat musaf in the Temple service
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan: the New Moon song is placed first specifically to serve as a public announcement that the court sanctified the month on time — this proclamation function overrides the normal frequency principle.
