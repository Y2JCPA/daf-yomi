🎺 The 48 Trumpet Blasts of Shabbat-Sukkot

Mishna: 48 blasts on Shabbat eve during Sukkot — the highest count on any Temple day
↓
Why not more?
Rava: On Shabbat, water-drawing is forbidden → no blast for libation → count stays at 48
Challenge
Rabbi Aḥa bar Ḥanina: Blast for each additional offering → should exceed 48 on Rosh HaShana-Shabbat!
↓
Gemara's Resolution: The tanna only enumerates cases that occur every year; rare calendar overlaps (Passover-Shabbat, Rosh HaShana-Shabbat) are intentionally omitted

⚖️ Rav Kahana's Statement — Which Opinion Does He Follow?

Gemara
It is not obvious that Rav Kahana's ruling follows Rabbi Yehuda. Perhaps he follows the Rabbis and merely excludes Rabbi Yoḥanan's view?
Counter
If he only meant to exclude Rabbi Yoḥanan, the phrase 'at all' (כלל) would be completely superfluous — its presence must signal something broader.
Gemara
Exactly — the emphatic 'at all' proves Rav Kahana intends a sweeping exclusion, not a narrow one. This aligns him with the more restrictive position of Rabbi Yehuda.
Principle
A redundant phrase in a Talmudic ruling is never truly redundant — every word carries halakhic weight and signals something about the ruling's scope.
Hermeneutic Key The Talmud's interpretive principle: unnecessary emphasis (like 'at all') signals inclusion or exclusion beyond the obvious — it never appears without purpose.

🎺 When Were Trumpets Sounded? Three Positions

Opinion A — Arriving at the 10th Stair

  • Trumpets sounded as priests descended to the 10th stair on the way to the Water Gate
  • NOT sounded at the moment of pouring the water libation
  • The stair-arrival marks the solemn procession moment
  • Contradicts Opinion B — these two are mutually exclusive

Opinion B — Pouring the Water Libation

  • Trumpets sounded at the actual pouring of water upon the altar
  • NOT sounded at the 10th stair
  • The climactic ritual act itself is the trigger
  • The mishna's phrasing seems to support this view

Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov

  • Trumpets sounded at opening of the Temple gates
  • NOT sounded at the 10th stair — redundant after gate-blast
  • Reasoning: why blast twice for the same procession?
  • Gate-opening blast suffices for the entire descent

🌾 Rava's Sharp Critique of Rabbi Aḥa bar Ḥanina

מאן האי דלא חייש אקמחיה
Rava "Who is this who is not concerned about the flour that his mill is producing?"
(מאן האי דלא חייש אקמחיה — who makes unconsidered statements?)

Rava identifies two problems with Rabbi Aḥa bar Ḥanina's approach:

1. Internal contradiction: The mishna's own blast order contradicts the explanation Rabbi Zeira offered to support Rabbi Aḥa bar Ḥanina.

2. Missing the obvious: The real reason the count stays at 48 on Shabbat-Sukkot is simple — no water is drawn on Shabbat, so no trumpet is sounded for the libation. Six blasts are thereby eliminated.

📅 Why Sukkot Eve (Hoshana Rabbah) Never Falls on Shabbat Eve

Potential problem: If 1st Tishrei (Rosh HaShana) falls on Thursday → 15th Tishrei (Sukkot) falls on Shabbat eve
↓
Calendar rule: Rosh HaShana is postponed — a day is added to Elul, pushing everything one day forward
↓
Primary reason
Prevents Yom Kippur from falling on Sunday — two consecutive days (Shabbat + Yom Kippur) without cooking food
Side effect
Also ensures 21st Tishrei (Hoshana Rabbah / Shabbat-Sukkot eve) cannot occur — preserving the mishna's 48-blast maximum
↓
Baraita (Aḥerim): Between one Shavuot and the next, or one Rosh HaShana and the next, there is a difference of only four days — the calendar's regularity enforces these patterns
