Sukkah 8 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What ratio does the Talmud use to approximate the diagonal of a square relative to its side length?
A. 1 and one-third cubits
B. 1 and two-fifths cubits
C. 1 and one-half cubits
D. 1 and three-tenths cubits
Correct: B — The Talmud states that for every square whose side is 1 cubit, its diagonal measures 1 and 2/5 (i.e., 7/5) cubits — an approximation of √2 ≈ 1.414 used throughout Talmudic geometry.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Asi's resolution, where are the 24 people positioned relative to the sukka in Rabbi Yoḥanan's calculation?
A. Inside the sukka along its walls
B. Outside the sukka, forming a circle around it
C. In the four corners of a square sukka
D. Seated at a table in the sukka's center
Correct: B — Rav Asi resolves the difficulty by explaining that the 24 people sit outside the sukka surrounding it. Their 24-cubit outer ring creates an inner circle of 18 cubits, yielding a 4×4 interior — the minimum required size.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why is the outer booth in the craftsmen's double-booth scenario exempt from the mitzva of mezuza?
A. It has no permanent doorpost
B. Even the inner booth it serves is itself exempt from mezuza
C. It belongs to a non-Jewish craftsman
D. It is too small to require mezuza
Correct: B — A gatehouse of an exempt structure is itself exempt. The inner booth — being temporary like a sukka — is exempt from mezuza. Therefore, the outer booth serving as its gatehouse is also exempt.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The mnemonic גנב"כ (Gimmel-Nun-Beit-Kaf) refers to booths built by which groups?
A. Shepherds, guards, gardeners, tax-collectors
B. Gentiles, women, animals, Samaritans
C. Priests, Levites, Israelites, converts
D. Judges, scribes, scholars, merchants
Correct: B — גנב"כ stands for Goyim (gentiles), Nashim (women), Behema (animals), and Kutim (Samaritans). All of their booths can be valid for sukka provided they were built for shade.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda, what is the single decisive condition for any non-standard booth to fulfill the mitzva of sukka?
A. It must be built by a Jewish adult male
B. It must have been built specifically during Elul or Tishrei
C. It must have been established to provide shade from its roofing
D. It must contain at least two walls and a roof of valid schach
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda rules that regardless of who built the booth, it is valid for sukka if and only if it was made to provide shade from its roofing (לְצֵל) — the functional essence of a sukka.
