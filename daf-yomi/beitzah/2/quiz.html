Beitzah 2 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is Beit Shammai's ruling regarding an egg laid on a Festival?
A. It may not be eaten on the Festival
B. It may be eaten on that very day
C. It may only be eaten after the Festival ends
D. It must be given to the poor
Correct: B — Beit Shammai permit eating the egg on the Festival itself. Beit Hillel prohibit it — and the Gemara spends this entire daf explaining why.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman's reading, what type of chicken does the mishna discuss?
A. A chicken designated for eating
B. A chicken that was sick and died
C. A chicken designated for laying eggs
D. A chicken purchased before the Festival
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman says the mishna refers to a laying-hen — the chicken is muktze (set aside, not for eating), and so is its egg.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Per Rav Naḥman, which Tanna does Beit Hillel follow in holding that muktze is genuinely prohibited?
A. Rabbi Akiva
B. Rabbi Shimon
C. Rabbi Meir
D. Rabbi Yehuda
Correct: D — Rav Naḥman holds that Beit Hillel follow Rabbi Yehuda, who takes muktze seriously as a real prohibition on Shabbat and Yom Tov.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rabba's reason for prohibiting an egg laid on a Festival that falls on Sunday?
A. The egg is muktze because the hen was not designated for food
B. The egg was fully formed on the preceding Shabbat — one holy day cannot prepare for another
C. The egg is nifsal (rendered unfit) by emerging on a holy day
D. Rabba agrees it is permitted but only after nightfall
Correct: B — Rabba applies the hachanah (preparation) principle: the egg was completed on Shabbat. Since one holy day cannot prepare for another, the egg is forbidden — derived from the manna verse (Exodus 16:5).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Rabba permit fully developed eggs found inside a hen that was slaughtered on Yom Tov?
A. They were pre-designated before Yom Tov began
B. The hen was designated for eating, so her eggs are too
C. Finding such eggs is an uncommon event — the Sages do not decree for uncommon cases
D. They have shells and are therefore not considered 'laid' eggs
Correct: C — Rabba explains that rabbinic decrees are only issued for common situations. Since finding fully-formed eggs inside a slaughtered hen is rare (uncommon matter), no decree was enacted.
