🥚 The Festival Egg Dispute

Beit Shammai
An egg laid on a Festival may be eaten on that very day.
Beit Hillel
An egg laid on a Festival may not be eaten — it is forbidden.
The Gemara's Question What type of chicken? What is the reason? Is this muktze, nolad, or a rabbinic decree — and who does each school follow among the Tannaim?

⚖️ The Tannaitic Debate Behind the Scenes

Rabbi Yehuda — Muktze Is Real

  • Items not pre-designated for use before Shabbat/Yom Tov are fully prohibited
  • Both moving and benefiting from muktze are forbidden
  • The laying-hen's egg is muktze — it may not be eaten
  • Beit Hillel follow this opinion (per Rav Naḥman)
  • Stringent — major Tannaitic position

Rabbi Shimon — Muktze Is Not Prohibited

  • Muktze is not a genuine prohibition on Shabbat or Yom Tov
  • Items may be moved and used even without prior designation
  • The laying-hen's egg may be eaten — no muktze concern
  • Beit Shammai follow this opinion (per Rav Naḥman)
  • Lenient — the Gemara's unattributed Shabbat mishna follows this

🌿 Rabba's Nolad Principle

וְהָיָה בַּיּוֹם הַשִּׁשִּׁי וְהֵכִינוּ אֵת אֲשֶׁר יָבִיאוּ — שמות טז:ה
נוֹלָד — Nolad (Something Newly Born/Created) Rabba rejects the muktze explanation entirely. Even for a chicken designated for eating, the egg is forbidden — because any egg laid on Yom Tov was fully formed the day before and merely emerged today.

If Yom Tov falls on Sunday, the egg was prepared on Shabbat — and one holy day cannot prepare for another.

This derives from the manna verse: "On the sixth day they shall prepare what they bring in" — implying preparation must occur on an ordinary weekday.

A regular Yom Tov (not after Shabbat) egg is rabbinically forbidden as a decree, lest one confuse it with the Sunday case.

💬 Abaye Challenges Rabba

Abaye
If the reason is only hachanah (preparation), an egg laid on a regular Yom Tov — not after Shabbat — should be permitted entirely!
Rabba
It is forbidden by rabbinic decree — so that people not confuse a Yom Tov after Shabbat with a regular Yom Tov.
Abaye
But a baraita explicitly permits fully developed eggs found inside a slaughtered hen on Yom Tov — even on a Sunday!
Rabba
Finding fully-formed eggs inside a hen is an uncommon occurrence. The Sages do not issue decrees for rare cases — לא גזרו.
Rule Established לא גזרו — The Sages do not enact preventive decrees for uncommon situations (Eiruvin 63b).
