QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Shimon, why may a firstborn with a permanent blemish NOT be slaughtered after falling into a cistern on Yom Tov?
A. Blemishes may never be examined on a Festival
B. The animal is muktze — it was not prepared before the Festival for slaughter
C. One may not physically raise an animal from a cistern on Yom Tov
D. Only a Sage, not merely an expert, may authorize slaughter of a firstborn
Correct: B — Rabbi Shimon holds that the firstborn was not among the animals 'prepared' (muchan) before the Festival — regardless of its blemish status, it is muktze and may not be slaughtered.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the novel ruling of Rabba bar Rav Huna regarding a firstborn born with its blemish?
A. It may never be slaughtered on Yom Tov under any circumstances
B. It requires examination by two independent experts
C. Experts may examine it ab initio on a Festival, and if the blemish is permanent, it may be slaughtered
D. It must be examined on erev Yom Tov or the opportunity is lost
Correct: C — Rabba bar Rav Huna reasons that since the animal was born blemished, it never held full firstborn sanctity, so experts may examine it ab initio on Yom Tov and permit slaughter if the blemish is permanent.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the contradiction between two baraitot on blemish-examination rules?
A. One baraita is a later Amoraic interpolation
B. The baraitot address different types of firstborn animals
C. One baraita was transmitted by Adda bar Ukhmei, who was known to confuse the opinions of Rabbi Yehuda and Rabbi Shimon
D. One baraita follows Beit Shammai, the other Beit Hillel
Correct: C — The Gemara uses source criticism: one baraita was transmitted by Adda bar Ukhmei, who regularly transposed the opinions of Rabbi Yehuda and Rabbi Shimon — making it an unreliable source.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What specific scenario is Hillel asking Rava about regarding muktze for 'half of Shabbat'?
A. Whether tzaar ba'alei chayyim overrides muktze on Shabbat
B. Whether an item that was fit, became unfit, and became fit again during Shabbat remains prohibited
C. Whether food designated on erev Shabbat needs re-designation if moved
D. Whether a partially eaten food retains its muktze status
Correct: B — Hillel asks whether muktze applies to an item that loses its fitness mid-Shabbat and then regains it — specifically, does the period of unfitness render it permanently muktze for the rest of Shabbat?
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the proof from grapes and figs left on the roof to dry?
A. The case involves food that was never fit to eat at all
B. Rav Kahana rules that food placed on a roof is automatically muktze
C. The baraita may address fruits that are partially dried — fit for some people but not others — a case of partial fitness, not mid-Shabbat loss of status
D. The proof is rejected because it involves a rabbinic rather than biblical muktze
Correct: C — The Gemara answers that the baraita may concern fruits only partially dried — fit for some people but not others. This is a case of incomplete fitness requiring prior designation, not a case of an item losing and regaining status mid-Shabbat.
