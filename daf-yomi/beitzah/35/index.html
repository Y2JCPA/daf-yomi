⚖️ Does Shabbat Fix Produce for Tithes?

The Rabbis
Even if one declared intent to eat the fruit tomorrow while at a storage area, this fixes the produce. All the more so Shabbat fixes produce and creates a tithing obligation.
Rabbi Eliezer
If one was eating from a cluster of grapes (work incomplete) and Shabbat arrived, he may finish eating — Shabbat does NOT fix produce if the work is not done.
Rabbi Natan
When Rabbi Eliezer said 'he may finish,' he did not mean in the courtyard itself. Rather, he may exit and finish in a place that does not fix the produce for tithes.
Ravin (from Eretz Yisrael)
Rabbi Yochanan ruled: Shabbat does NOT fix produce for tithes if work is incomplete — the halakha follows Rabbi Eliezer's principle.
Key Principle The completion of agricultural labor — not sacred time or ritual acts — is the decisive factor for creating the tithing obligation

📜 Rabbi Yochanan's Threefold Ruling

בֵּין לְעִנְיַן שַׁבָּת, בֵּין לְעִנְיַן תְּרוּמָה, בֵּין לְעִנְיַן חָצֵר
Ravin brought from Eretz Yisrael Rabbi Yochanan issued a unified ruling covering three distinct cases:

(1) Shabbat — does not fix produce for tithes if work is incomplete
(2) Separation of teruma — does not fix produce for tithes if work is incomplete
(3) Entering a courtyard — does not fix produce for tithes if work is incomplete

Each ruling independently excludes a different Tanna's lenient position.

📊 Three Opinions Excluded by Rabbi Yochanan

Shabbat → excludes Hillel

  • Baraita: Hillel holds Shabbat fixes produce for tithes
  • One who gathers fruits and Shabbat arrives — Hillel requires tithing
  • Rabbi Yochanan: Shabbat does NOT fix if work is incomplete

Courtyard → excludes R. Ya'akov

  • Mishna: Rabbi Ya'akov — a courtyard fixes produce even if work incomplete
  • One transporting figs through a courtyard — Rabbi Ya'akov requires tithing
  • Rabbi Yochanan: Courtyard does NOT fix if work is incomplete

Teruma → excludes R. Eliezer

  • Mishna: Rabbi Eliezer — separating teruma fixes remaining produce
  • Fruits from which teruma was separated before work complete
  • Rabbi Yochanan: Teruma does NOT fix the remainder if work incomplete

💼 Does a Transaction Fix Produce for Tithes?

General rule: A commercial transaction normally renders produce keva (fixed) for tithes — the buyer must tithe before eating
↓
Critical exception (baraita): If the work of the produce is not yet complete at the time of purchase, the transaction does NOT fix it for tithes
↓
Example: One who acquires figs from an am ha'aretz in a place where people press and dry figs — if work is incomplete, the sale does not obligate tithing
↓
Three lessons from this baraita: (1) Transaction only fixes completed work; (2) Am ha'aretz's produce with incomplete work may be eaten without tithing; (3) This is the normative ruling
↓
Further nuance (Mishna): One who exchanges fruits with a friend where both intend to eat — this is not a commercial transaction and does not fix produce for tithes regardless of work status
