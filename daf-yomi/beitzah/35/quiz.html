Beitzah 35 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yochanan (brought by Ravin from Eretz Yisrael), which statement is correct regarding produce with incomplete work?
A. Shabbat alone does not fix it, but teruma and a courtyard do
B. All three — Shabbat, teruma, and a courtyard — do not fix it for tithes
C. Only a courtyard fails to fix produce; Shabbat always creates the obligation
D. Teruma always fixes produce for tithes regardless of work status
Correct: B — Rabbi Yochanan ruled on all three cases: Shabbat, separation of teruma, and entering a courtyard — none of these fix produce for tithes when the work of the produce is not yet complete.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rabbi Yochanan's ruling that Shabbat does not fix produce for tithes was intended to exclude whose opinion?
A. Rabbi Eliezer
B. Rabbi Ya'akov
C. Hillel
D. Rabbi Natan
Correct: C — The Gemara explicitly states that the ruling about Shabbat comes to exclude Hillel, who held — per a baraita — that Shabbat does fix produce for tithes even when work is incomplete.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
The Mishna at the end of Daf 35 permits a strenuous activity on a Festival. What is it?
A. Pressing olives in a vat to make oil
B. Lowering produce through a skylight to protect it from rain
C. Separating teruma from fruits before a Festival meal
D. Carrying heavy stones to build a sukkah
Correct: B — The Mishna states that one may lower (mashilin) produce through a skylight into the house on a Festival to prevent it from being ruined by rain. This is permitted despite being strenuous because of the potential for monetary loss.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How many distinct versions of the Mishna's word for 'lowering produce' are discussed by the Amoraim?
A. Two — mashilin and mashchilin (only Rav Yehuda and Rav Natan's versions)
B. Three — with Mar Zutra adding a third
C. Four — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak adds two more variants
D. Five — mashilin, mashchilin, mashirin, mashchirin, and manshirin
Correct: D — Rav Yehuda and Rav Natan provide two versions (mashilin / mashchilin). Mar Zutra confirms both. Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak then adds three more: mashirin, mashchirin, and manshirin — each supported by a distinct biblical or mishnaic source.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
A baraita teaches that one who acquires figs from an am ha'aretz does not need to tithe them. What is the key condition for this exemption?
A. The am ha'aretz must have already separated teruma before the sale
B. Fewer than a specific minimum amount was acquired
C. The work of the figs is not yet complete at the time of the transaction
D. The purchase was made outside of the Land of Israel
Correct: C — The baraita teaches that a transaction fixes produce for tithes only when the work is completed. If the work of the figs is not yet complete (e.g., they have not yet been dried), the sale does not obligate the buyer to tithe — consistent with Rabbi Yochanan's unified principle.
