Beitzah 21 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Rav Ḥisda permits slaughtering a jointly-owned animal (half-gentile) on a Festival. What is his reasoning?
A. The gentile's share is considered nullified (bitul) within the majority
B. It is impossible to extract even an olive-bulk of meat without slaughtering the whole animal
C. The gentile has sold his share to the Jew before the Festival
D. The Sages permitted it as a rabbinic enactment to prevent financial loss
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda's reasoning is that the act is physically indivisible — you cannot obtain any meat at all without slaughtering the whole animal, so the Jewish benefit compels the act.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yosei HaGelili, what does the phrase 'for you' (לכם) in Shemot 12:16 exclude from permitted Festival labor?
A. Labor performed by a gentile on behalf of a Jew
B. Labor performed on Shabbat following a Festival
C. Labor performed solely for the benefit of dogs
D. Labor performed after sunset on Yom Tov
Correct: C — Rabbi Yosei HaGelili reads 'for you' as excluding dogs — Festival labor may only be performed for human (Jewish) benefit, not for animals.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What concern leads Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov to prohibit inviting a gentile even on Shabbat?
A. The gentile may violate Shabbat while cooking in the Jewish home
B. One may come to write down the guest's dietary requests
C. Wine remnants left in cups touched by the gentile become forbidden and muktze
D. The extra food prepared for the guest constitutes a melacha
Correct: C — Once a gentile drinks from a cup, the wine remnants become yayin nesech — forbidden wine — which cannot be moved even indirectly (unlike mere muktze, which can be moved with a vessel).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the incident of Shimon the Timnite, what did he do on the night of the Festival, and what was Rav Yehuda ben Bava's reaction?
A. He baked bread for soldiers and was praised for his community sacrifice
B. He was absent from the study hall because he was baking for soldiers; Rav Yehuda rebuked him saying his gain was outweighed by his loss
C. He refused to bake for soldiers and was punished by the authorities
D. He baked for the soldiers on condition they share the bread, and Rav Yehuda approved
Correct: B — Shimon the Timnite skipped the study hall to bake for soldiers. Rav Yehuda ben Bava rebuked him: 'I wonder if your gain was not outweighed by your loss' — citing the verse 'only that which every soul must eat.'
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which of the following is NOT one of Rabban Gamliel's three stringencies following Beit Shammai?
A. Not insulating hot food on a Festival ab initio for use on Shabbat
B. Not reassembling a broken candelabrum on a Festival
C. Not lighting Hanukkah candles during the intermediate days of a Festival
D. Only baking thin loaves (not thick ones) on a Festival
Correct: C — Rabban Gamliel's three stringencies are: (1) not insulating food on Yom Tov for Shabbat, (2) not reassembling a candelabrum, and (3) baking only thin loaves. Hanukkah candles are unrelated.
