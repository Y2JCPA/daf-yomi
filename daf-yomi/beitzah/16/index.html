🕯️ Two Ways to Honor Shabbat

Shammai the Elder

  • Set aside the finest food found each day throughout the week for Shabbat
  • Found something better the next day? Eat yesterday's, save today's
  • Shabbat is primary — the whole week serves it
  • "This is for Shabbat" — constant dedication to the day

Hillel the Elder

  • Ate well every day, trusting God's provision each morning
  • Every weekday action done for the sake of Heaven
  • Every day is sanctified when lived in God's service
  • "Blessed be the Lord, day by day" (Psalms 68:20)

💰 The Annual Parnassah Decree

Rosh HaShana: Each person stands in judgment — their full annual livelihood is decreed for the coming year
↓
The decree covers all personal expenditures — food, clothing, housing — for the entire year
↓
Critical exception: Expenses for Torah study, Shabbat, Yom Tov, and Rosh Chodesh are NOT included in the decree — God supplements these separately
↓
Source: "Blow the shofar at the New Moon, at the concealed time for our Festival" (Ps. 81:4) — Rosh HaShana, when the moon is hidden
↓
The word חֹק (ḥok) means "allotment" — proven from "they ate their ḥukkam that Pharaoh gave them" (Gen. 47:22)

🕯️ Shammai and Hillel on Shabbat Honor

שַׁמַּאי — Shammai

  • Method: Each day, set aside best available food for Shabbat
  • If better food found later in the week — swap it in, eat the earlier find
  • Theology: Shabbat is the pinnacle; all weekdays are its preparation
  • "This is for Shabbat" — constant verbal dedication
  • Emphasis on the sanctity of the day itself

הִלֵּל — Hillel

  • Method: Ate the best food available each day of the week
  • Did not 'save' for Shabbat — trusted God to provide for tomorrow
  • Theology: Every moment lived for Heaven is holy in itself
  • "Blessed be the Lord, day by day" (Ps. 68:20)
  • Emphasis on constant, daily Divine service

✨ The Neshamah Yeteirah — Extra Shabbat Soul

וַיִּנָּפַשׁ — once it rests, oy, the extra nefesh departs!
Rabbi Shimon ben Lakish The Holy One gives every person an additional soul (נְשָׁמָה יְתֵרָה) on Shabbat eve, expanding their spiritual capacity for joy and rest. At the conclusion of Shabbat, this extra soul is removed. Source: "He ceased from work and was refreshed [וַיִּנָּפַשׁ — vayinafash]" (Exodus 31:17). Read it as: וַי — נֶפֶשׁ — once it ceases [Shabbat ends], woe (vay)! the nefesh is gone.

🍲 Eruv Tavshilin: What Qualifies?

Is the item being used a cooked dish (תַּבְשִׁיל) — roasted, boiled, pickled, or salted fish?
✅ Yes — Cooked Dish
Valid for eruv tavshilin; may be roasted, boiled, pickled, or koleyas (Spanish soft fish)
❌ Bread Alone
Not sufficient — bread does not 'accompany' itself; requires an accompanying cooked dish
↓
Minimum quantity: one olive-bulk (כְּזַיִת) of the cooked dish required (Rabbi Abba)
↓
Does the person relying on the eruv know it exists? (Rav Huna's requirement)
Knows — Valid
Eruv is effective; permitted to cook on Yom Tov for Shabbat's needs
Unknown Resident
Community leaders (Shmuel's father, R. Ami, R. Asi) may prepare a single eruv for an entire city

⚖️ Eiruv When Yom Tov Falls on Friday

First Tanna
If Yom Tov falls on Shabbat eve (Friday), one may prepare neither eiruv teḥumin nor eiruv ḥatzerot on that day. Both must be prepared before Yom Tov or Shabbat is compromised.
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
One may prepare eiruv ḥatzerot on Yom Tov Friday — carrying is permitted on Yom Tov. But eiruv teḥumin is forbidden — walking beyond the boundary is not allowed on Yom Tov itself.
Rav
Halakha follows the First Tanna — stricter ruling. Neither eiruv may be prepared on Yom Tov that falls on Friday.
Shmuel
Halakha follows Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi — eiruv ḥatzerot may (and should) be prepared on Yom Tov Friday. Forbidding it would cause the masses to accidentally carry on Shabbat without an eiruv!
Practical Concern (Shmuel) If people refrain from preparing eiruv ḥatzerot on the Festival, they may mistakenly carry between domains on Shabbat — a Torah violation. The leniency protects the community (tikun ha-rabim).
