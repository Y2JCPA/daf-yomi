⚖️ Core Dispute: Carrying on Festivals

Beit Shammai
One may NOT carry a minor child, lulav, or Torah scroll into the public domain on a Festival — these are not needed for food preparation, so the Festival permit does not cover them.
Beit Hillel
One MAY carry them. Since carrying was permitted for food needs on a Festival, it is permitted entirely (הותרה — heter hokhsil). The permission is categorical, not item-specific.
The Core Question Does permitting carrying for food prep on a Festival expand to permit ALL carrying? Beit Hillel says the permission is categorical; Beit Shammai says it is limited to food needs only.

🔀 Explaining the Root of the BS/BH Dispute

Why do Beit Shammai & Beit Hillel argue about carrying a child, lulav, or Torah scroll on a Festival?
↓
Rav Yitzḥak bar Avdimi
They dispute heter hokhsil: does food-prep permission expand to all carrying? BS says no.
Rabba's Alternative
Maybe they dispute whether a carrying prohibition exists at all on Festivals! BH might derive from Jeremiah 17 that it applies only to Shabbat.
↓
Rav Yosef refutes Rabba: If BH held there is no carrying prohibition on Festivals, they would also permit muktze — but BH prohibits muktze on Festivals!
↓
Conclusion: Both sides agree a carrying prohibition exists on Festivals. The dispute is solely about heter hokhsil.

🏛️ Rabba vs Rav Yosef: What Exactly Do They Dispute?

Rabba
Perhaps BS and BH disagree about whether any carrying prohibition applies on Festivals at all. BH holds: 'Neither carry forth a burden' (Jeremiah 17:22) applies only to Shabbat — Festivals have no such prohibition.
Rav Yosef
Objection! If BH truly held there is no carrying prohibition on Festivals, they should permit muktze objects as well — yet BH forbids muktze on Festivals! This proves BH acknowledges a carrying prohibition; they dispute only heter hokhsil.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
I agree: BS and BH dispute heter hokhsil. Proof: cooking a sciatic nerve on a Festival yields multiple floggings under BS — because cooking non-food is not covered by the Festival's food-prep permit.
Key Distinction Muktze prohibition is rooted in handling restrictions, not the carrying-out prohibition. Rav Yosef's proof stands: BH's prohibition of muktze shows they accept that a Festival carrying framework exists — they only disagree about its scope.

🌾 Gifts to Priests on Festivals: Three Tannaitic Opinions

Tanna Kamma (Mishna)

  • Challah: may be separated and brought on Festival
  • Beit Shammai: neither teruma nor gifts from animal may be brought to priest
  • Beit Hillel: both may be brought
  • BS analogy: just as teruma may not be brought, so too other gifts

Rabbi Yosei

  • BS and BH did not dispute gifts from slaughtered animals
  • Both agree: animal gifts may be brought on Festival
  • They disputed teruma only
  • BS: may not bring teruma to priest on Festival
  • Halakha follows Rabbi Yosei (ruling of Shmuel)

Aḥerim

  • BS and BH did not dispute teruma
  • Both agree: teruma may not be brought on Festival
  • They disputed animal gifts only
  • BH: may bring; BS: may not bring
  • Rava: mishna means slaughtered yesterday, separated today

⚗️ Rava's Mustard Case — Food Processing Logic

Case: Rava's host had bundles of unprocessed mustard — seeds still in stems. He asked: may one crush the stalks and eat from them on a Festival?
↓
Rava's Ruling: Permitted — crushing mustard is direct food preparation, exactly the kind of act the Torah permits on Festivals (אך אשר יאכל — only what is eaten).
↓
Abaye's Challenge: Cites baraita — grain husked on Festival eve may be winnowed on the Festival only in small amounts with irregular tools, not normal implements. Why should mustard be more lenient?
↓
Rava's Defense: The baraita's restrictions apply to grain processed on the same day as Festival; mustard was pre-processed before Festival began — a lighter standard applies.
↓
Abaye's Second Challenge: If Festival-day rubbing creates a newly fit product, has it become obligated in teruma separation retroactively? This would be a case where teruma separation occurs on a Festival!
↓
Gemara Responds: Not difficult — Festival food-prep rules and teruma-obligation rules operate on independent legal tracks and do not interact in this problematic way.
