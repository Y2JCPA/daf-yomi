Beitzah 3 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabba, why is an egg laid on Yom Tov forbidden?
A. It is a decree due to liquid that seeped from fruit on Yom Tov
B. It is muktzeh because it was not prepared before Yom Tov
C. It resembles fruit that was plucked on Yom Tov
D. The egg is forbidden only by custom, not law
Correct: B — Rabba holds the egg is muktzeh — since the hen was designated for laying eggs, not as food, the egg was never 'set aside' (prepared) for eating before Yom Tov, raising a possible Torah-level concern.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Abaye's objection to Rabbi Yitzchak's reason for the egg prohibition?
A. Liquid from fruit is actually permitted on Yom Tov, so it cannot be the basis for a decree
B. Decreeing against eggs to prevent violation of another rabbinic decree would be a gezeirah l'gezeirah
C. An egg is more stringent than liquid and should not be compared to it
D. Rabbi Yitzchak's ruling contradicts the explicit text of the Mishnah
Correct: B — Abaye argues that liquid from fruit is itself only forbidden as a rabbinic decree. Issuing a further decree against eggs to prevent violation of that decree constitutes a 'gezeirah l'gezeirah' — a decree upon a decree — which is generally not permitted.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yochanan prove that the egg prohibition is a decree related to liquid from fruit?
A. He cites a baraita that explicitly states this reason
B. He raises a contradiction between two of Rabbi Yehuda's rulings and resolves it by reversing their attributions
C. He derives it from the muktzeh laws in tractate Shabbat
D. He demonstrates that Rabba's muktzeh reasoning leads to an impossible outcome
Correct: B — Rabbi Yochanan notes that Rabbi Yehuda permits liquid from fruit yet seems to forbid eggs — a contradiction. By reversing which ruling is attributed to Rabbi Yehuda, Rabbi Yochanan shows both cases are governed by the same logic: the egg ban is a decree parallel to the liquid ban.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Ravina's resolution of the contradiction raised by Rabbi Yochanan?
A. He agrees with Rabbi Yochanan that the attributions must be reversed
B. He says Rabbi Yehuda personally permits the egg but argues rhetorically that those who forbid liquid must also forbid the egg
C. He rules that Rabbi Yehuda's statement about eggs is not halacha
D. He introduces a third opinion that neither permits nor forbids the egg
Correct: B — Ravina argues there is no need to reverse the attributions. Rabbi Yehuda himself permits the egg, but his statement is a conditional argument: 'According to your position (forbidding liquid), you must also forbid the first-day egg.' No contradiction exists in Rabbi Yehuda's own view.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, why does Rabbi Yochanan hold that a Yom Tov egg cannot be nullified in a mixture?
A. Because Rabbi Yochanan holds an egg is always sold exclusively by unit and is therefore significant
B. Because all Torah prohibitions can never be nullified regardless of the ratio
C. Rabbi Yochanan follows the tanna of the litra of dried figs, who holds even rabbinically-forbidden counted items cannot be nullified
D. Because once an egg touches another egg, both become forbidden by contact
Correct: C — Rav Pappa explains that Rabbi Yochanan follows the tanna of the litra of dried figs case, who rules: any item that is 'counted' — even if its prohibition is only rabbinic — cannot be nullified by majority, regardless of the ratio in the mixture.
