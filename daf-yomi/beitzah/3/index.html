🥚 Three Amoraim: Why Is the Yom Tov Egg Forbidden?

Rabba
The egg is muktzeh — it lacked preparation before Yom Tov. The hen was designated for laying eggs, not as food, so the egg was never 'set aside' for eating. Possible Torah-level concern.
Rav Yosef
The egg is forbidden as a rabbinic decree due to fruit plucked on Yom Tov — just as that newly-separated produce is forbidden, so too an egg laid that day. Both are 'newly created' on Yom Tov.
Rabbi Yitzchak
The egg is a decree due to liquid that seeped from fruit on Yom Tov — since that liquid is rabbinically forbidden, an egg laid that day shares the same prohibited status. Rabbi Yochanan agrees.
Core Question The Mishnah rules an egg laid on Yom Tov is forbidden. But WHY? Three Amoraim give three distinct reasons — each carrying different halachic consequences for doubtful cases and mixtures.

⚖️ The Decree-on-a-Decree Problem

Rav Yosef / Rabbi Yitzchak: Egg is forbidden as a decree due to liquid from fruit, which is itself forbidden on Yom Tov
↓
Abaye's Challenge: But liquid from fruit is itself only forbidden as a decree — lest one purposely squeeze fruit and perform the labor of threshing
↓
Do we issue a decree to prevent violation of another decree? (גזירה לגזירה) — Generally prohibited!
Rabba's view
Exactly why Rabba rejects both opinions — he requires a direct Torah-level concern, not a chain of rabbinic decrees
Rav Yosef's reply
The egg has an additional resemblance to plucked fruit — both are newly-created on Yom Tov. This extra factor justifies the decree independently

📊 Three Opinions Compared

Rabba

  • Reason: Muktzeh — lack of preparation
  • Level: Possible Torah concern
  • Doubt case: Strict — safek d'orayta l'chumra
  • Baraita result: Uncertain egg remains forbidden
  • Key factor: Hen designated for eggs, not food

Rav Yosef

  • Reason: Decree due to plucked fruit
  • Level: Rabbinic decree
  • Doubt case: Lenient — safek d'rabbanan l'kula
  • Baraita result: Uncertain egg is permitted
  • Key factor: Egg resembles newly-separated produce

Rabbi Yitzchak

  • Reason: Decree due to liquid from fruit
  • Level: Rabbinic decree
  • Doubt case: Lenient — safek d'rabbanan l'kula
  • Baraita result: Uncertain egg is permitted
  • Key factor: Rabbi Yochanan explicitly adopts this view

💧 Rabbi Yehuda's Apparent Contradiction

Rabbi Yehuda (Statement 1 — Shabbat 143b)
Liquid from fruit that seeped on its own is permitted — it retains the status of food, not liquid, since it came from produce intended for eating. Food that comes from food = food.
Rabbi Yehuda (Statement 2 — Two-day Yom Tov)
An egg laid on the first Festival day is forbidden even on the second day — implying eggs are treated more strictly than liquid that seeped, creating an apparent contradiction.
Rabbi Yochanan's Resolution Reverse the attributions! The lenient ruling on liquid belongs to the other opinion; the strict ruling on eggs belongs to Rabbi Yehuda. Since both rulings are flipped, they must share the same underlying reason — proving the egg ban is a decree linked to liquid from fruit.

🔄 Ravina Resolves Without Reversing

Rabbi Yochanan's solution: Reverse the attributions entirely to harmonize the egg and liquid rulings
↓
Ravina objects: No reversal needed! Rabbi Yehuda can consistently hold both positions
↓
Ravina's reading: Rabbi Yehuda personally permits the egg — but argues: 'According to your logic (forbidding liquid), you must also forbid the first-day egg.' It is a rhetorical argument, not his own ruling.
↓
Ravina son of Rav Ulla adds: Rabbi Yehuda forbids the egg for an entirely different reason — the hen was not designated as food. This is independent of the liquid-from-fruit logic.
↓
Result: Multiple valid resolutions exist. Rabbi Yochanan's reversal of attributions is not the only path to coherence.
