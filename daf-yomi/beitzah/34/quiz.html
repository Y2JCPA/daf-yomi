Beitzah 34 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is it forbidden to heat new tiles on Yom Tov in order to roast on them?
A. Tiles are muktzeh and may not be handled on Yom Tov
B. Heating tests whether they burst and/or hardens them into proper vessels — both are forbidden labors
C. The smoke produced by heating tiles constitutes a prohibited act
D. Tiles may only be used for roasting on weekdays, not Festivals
Correct: B — Two reasons are given: (1) heating new tiles tests whether they will burst — a prohibited examination; (2) it hardens them into proper vessels — the forbidden labor of makeh b'patish (completing a utensil).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How did Rabbi Yirmeya respond to Rabbi Zeira's proof from the tiles regarding the twitching bird?
A. He accepted the proof and ruled the slaughter forbidden on Yom Tov
B. He said the tile ruling proves one may slaughter the bird freely
C. He rejected the analogy — hardening vessels is unrelated to examining an animal's mortal flaw
D. He deferred the ruling to the court in Jerusalem
Correct: C — Rabbi Yirmeya rejected the analogy entirely: making/hardening a vessel is a specific forbidden labor; conducting a halachic examination of an animal's physical condition is a completely different category.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the baraita about multiple people cooking together on Shabbat, why is the person who only placed the empty pot on the stove liable?
A. Placing any object on a stove always constitutes a prohibited labor independently
B. He enabled the cooking — all participants who perform any part of the act share full liability as partners
C. The Sages issued a special decree making bystanders liable
D. He is actually exempt; only those who added fire or food are truly liable
Correct: B — All partners who each perform any part of a prohibited act together share liability. Placing the pot is an essential enabling act in the cooking process, making that person a full partner in the Shabbat violation.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which of the following is permitted on Yom Tov regarding a slaughtered animal's head and feet?
A. Smearing them with clay to loosen the skin
B. Trimming them with special shears
C. Scalding them with hot water or singeing them in fire to remove hair
D. Rubbing them with oil to soften the hide
Correct: C — The Tosefta explicitly permits scalding the head and feet in hot water or singeing them in fire to remove hair. Smearing with clay and other processing methods are forbidden as weekday-manner activities.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What did Rav Naḥman rule regarding Shabbat's power to establish a tithe obligation?
A. Shabbat never establishes a tithe obligation — only Yom Tov does
B. Shabbat establishes tithes only for items whose work was fully completed before Shabbat
C. Shabbat establishes the tithe obligation for both completed and uncompleted items
D. Shabbat and Yom Tov are identical in their effect on tithe obligations
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman ruled that Shabbat establishes the tithe obligation for both items whose labor is complete and those whose labor is not yet complete — a broader ruling than Rava expected, which prompted Rava to challenge him.
