🐓 Rabbi Yirmeya vs. Rabbi Zeira: Slaughtering a Twitching Bird on Yom Tov

R. Yirmeya
A bird was trampled and is twitching but cannot stand. May we slaughter it on a Festival? Do we assume on Yom Tov that it has a fatal flaw — or must we treat it as fully viable?
R. Zeira
We already learned that one may not whiten new tiles in order to roast on them. Rabba bar bar Ḥana citing R. Yoḥanan: heating new tiles hardens them — it completes them as vessels. This is forbidden on Yom Tov.
R. Yirmeya
That is no proof at all! The tile case is forbidden because one is hardening them into proper vessels — that is making a utensil. Examining whether a bird has a fatal flaw is a completely different category.
Narrator
The question remains unresolved: is examining the bird's mortal status a prohibited labor, or merely a halachic inquiry that accompanies a permitted act of slaughter?
Core Tension Does the act of slaughtering a twitching bird on Yom Tov constitute a forbidden 'examination' (like testing tiles), or is examination of an animal's flaw fundamentally different from completing a vessel?

🪨 New Tiles: Two Reasons the Prohibition Applies

אין מלבנין את הרעפים לצלות בהן
Prohibited on Yom Tov The mishna teaches: one may not whiten new tiles in order to roast on them on a Festival.

Two reasons are given:

1. Testing: Heating new tiles for the first time tests whether they will burst — this is a forbidden examination on Yom Tov.

2. Hardening (Makeh b'Patish): The heat hardens the tiles into proper vessels — this is the forbidden labor of completing a utensil.

Rabba bar bar Ḥana citing Rabbi Yoḥanan: The ruling specifically addresses new tiles, because only they require this initial hardening process.

🏺 New Ovens, Stoves & Slaughtered Animals on Yom Tov

✅ Permitted

  • Carry new ovens/stoves in a courtyard
  • Place items on top of new vessels
  • Scald animal heads and feet with hot water
  • Singe heads and feet in fire to remove hair
  • Blow a fire using a tube (unusual manner = shinui)
  • Prepare kundas and akaviyot (bitter vegetables requiring great effort)

❌ Forbidden

  • Anoint new ovens with oil
  • Rub new ovens with a rag
  • Cool new ovens with water
  • Smear animal heads/feet with clay
  • Trim vegetable tops with their special shears
  • Blow fire with bellows (weekday manner)
  • Mend or sharpen a skewer

🌿 Does Shabbat Establish a Tithe Obligation?

Muktzeh item on Shabbat
Its labor was not completed before Shabbat began
↓
Rava asks Rav Naḥman: Does Shabbat itself create a tithe obligation for this item?
↓
Rav Naḥman rules
YES — Shabbat obligates tithes for both completed and uncompleted items
Rava challenges
Why differ from Yom Tov? On a Festival, muktzeh items do not acquire a tithe obligation
↓
Mar Zutra's proof from the Mishna: R. Eliezer permits designating stored Shemitah produce on Shabbat eve — implying Shabbat does establish an obligation even for previously set-aside items
↓
Gemara refutes: There it is different — by saying 'I will eat from here tomorrow' he immediately establishes a meal and fixes the fruit's status on his own terms
