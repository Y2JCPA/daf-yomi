🔥 Rav's Ruling: When Are Stove Ashes Permitted?

לֹא שָׁנוּ אֶלָּא שֶׁהִסִּיקָהּ מֵעֶרֶב יוֹם טוֹב — אֲבָל הִסִּיקָהּ בְּיוֹם טוֹב — אֲסוּרִין
Core Halakha (Amud A) The Mishna states that ashes of a stove are prepared (not muktzeh) for use on a Festival. But Rav Yehuda said in the name of Rav: this applies only if the stove was lit on the Festival eve — so the ashes already existed when the Festival began.

If the stove was first ignited on the Festival itself, those ashes are prohibited. They did not yet exist at the start of the Festival and could not have been designated in advance.

This ruling is confirmed word-for-word by a supporting baraita.

↓

Creating a cavity in loose material is not halachic 'digging' — permitted
Compacted earth requiring crushing
Crushing is a prohibited labor (toladah of grinding) — forbidden
↓

🐾 Why Can't Ashes Cover Koy Blood? The Central Debate

Rabba
Stove ashes are prepared only for definite obligations — covering a bird or undomesticated animal. A koy's status is uncertain, so one never intended those ashes for this purpose.
Gemara
But the 'making a hole' concern applies equally to definite cases — why would that be what disqualifies ashes for the koy specifically?
Rava
The real reason: one who covers koy blood might forget and crush the earth to refine it — performing prohibited labor for a merely uncertain mitzva. We do not permit this risk.
Gemara
A positive mitzva overrides a prohibition! Even if some labor occurs, shouldn't the mitzva of covering blood push it aside?
Rava
That principle applies only when the mitzva and the violation occur in one single act — as with circumcising a leprous child. Here, taking the earth and covering the blood are two separate acts. No override.
Rava's Final Position One's initial intention when preparing ashes was for definite mitzvot only. Uncertain cases (the koy) simply fall outside the scope of that preparation — making the ashes halachically unprepared for this purpose.

📋 Rava's Principle — Expanding the Logic of Prepared Intent

Earth Prepared for Excrement

  • Brought in to cover baby's excrement on a Festival
  • ✅ Also permitted to cover blood of a slaughtered animal
  • Reason: covering blood is a mitzva — clearly within the owner's intent when bringing in earth

Earth Prepared for Bird's Blood

  • Brought in to cover a bird's blood (definite mitzva)
  • ✅ Also permitted to cover excrement (Sages of Neharbela)
  • Reason: one who prepares for a greater purpose certainly intends minor uses too

Earth Prepared for Koy's Blood

  • Brought in to cover koy's blood (uncertain mitzva)
  • ⚠️ Disputed: Rava permits using it for excrement
  • ❌ May not be used for blood of a definite undomesticated animal — uncertain intent cannot expand to definite use
