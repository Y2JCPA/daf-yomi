⚖️ Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel: Leaven Removal Measures

Beit Shammai
For שאור (leaven/sourdough): an olive-bulk triggers removal. For חמץ (leavened bread): a larger date-bulk is required — it is fit for eating and thus more significant.
Beit Hillel
Both שאור and חמץ require only an olive-bulk. The Torah wrote both terms to teach that neither is forbidden in less than an olive-bulk — they are equal.
Rabbi Yosei bar Ḥanina
The dispute concerns only removal (ביעור) before Pesach. Regarding the measure that determines liability for eating chametz — all agree: an olive-bulk for both.
Resolution Beit Shammai requires a date-bulk of leavened bread before one must remove it; Beit Hillel requires only an olive-bulk for both שאור and חמץ. For eating violations, everyone agrees: olive-bulk applies to both.

🥛 Eggs Found in Slaughtered Chicken — May They Be Eaten with Milk?

Tanna Kamma (Baraita)
Fully formed eggs found inside a slaughtered chicken are permitted to eat with milk — they are considered independent entities, not the chicken's meat.
Rabbi Yaakov
Even eggs still attached by sinews, once fully formed, are already independent — permitted with milk, and do not convey ritual impurity as the chicken's flesh would.
Abaye
Perhaps Rabbi Yaakov's leniency applies only regarding milk. But for ritual impurity (טומאה), should we not be stringent and treat attached eggs as part of the bird?
Rav Yosef
No — Rabbi Yaakov holds the eggs are independent for both purposes. Treating them as bird-flesh for impurity would proliferate impurity unnecessarily, which we avoid.
Halakha Fully formed eggs inside a slaughtered hen are permitted with milk and do not convey the ritual impurity of the bird's flesh. The cluster of eggs still attached by sinews is not considered part of the meat.

🛒 Mistaken Egg Transactions — Two Cases of Mekach Ta'ut

Case 1: Live vs. Slaughtered Chicken Eggs

  • Buyer requested eggs of a live chicken (for hatching chicks)
  • Sellers gave eggs of a slaughtered chicken (cannot hatch)
  • Rabbi Ami: Void — mistaken transaction (מקח טעות)
  • Even if eggs are edible, buyer's specification controls the deal
  • Ruling needed lest we assume he only wanted them for food

Case 2: Fertilized 'Rooster Eggs'

  • Buyer asked for eggs of a rooster (fertilized/premium)
  • Sellers gave eggs a hen had absorbed from the ground
  • Ruling: Also void — mistaken transaction
  • Buyer wanted specifically fertilized eggs, not ordinary ones
  • Ruling needed lest we assume he wanted them merely for eating

🩸 Covering Blood (כיסוי הדם) on Yom Tov

Torah requirement: After slaughtering a wild animal (חיה) or bird, one must cover the blood with earth — כיסוי הדם (Vayikra 17:13)
↓
The Yom Tov problem: Digging up earth for a new purpose on a Festival may constitute forbidden labor (מלאכה) — creating tension with the mitzvah
↓
Beit Shammai: One may slaughter and then dig up earth to cover — covering blood is a mitzvah that permits moving earth on Yom Tov
↓
Beit Hillel ab initio: One may NOT slaughter unless earth was prepared on Erev Yom Tov — plan ahead; do not create the problem
↓
Beit Hillel b'di'eved (concession): If one already slaughtered without preparing earth, he may dig with a shovel and cover — the mitzvah is not forfeited
↓
Rabbi Zeira / Rav: One who slaughters on Yom Tov is obligated to cover. If no earth is available, he waits until after Yom Tov — but the obligation remains
