QUESTION 1 OF 5
A buyer asked for eggs of a live chicken and was given eggs of a slaughtered chicken. What did Rabbi Ami rule?
A. The transaction stands since all eggs look identical
B. The transaction is void — it is a mistaken transaction (מקח טעות)
C. The buyer must pay half price as compensation
D. The ruling depends on whether the buyer wanted them for food or hatching
Correct: B — Rabbi Ami ruled it a mistaken transaction (מקח טעות) — void. The buyer specified live-chicken eggs, and that specification controls the deal regardless of how he might have used them.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
May fully formed eggs found inside a slaughtered chicken be eaten with milk?
A. No — they are considered part of the chicken's meat in all respects
B. Only if they were not attached to the chicken by sinews
C. Yes — they are considered independent entities, not subject to the meat-and-milk prohibition
D. Only after a waiting period following the slaughter
Correct: C — The baraita rules that fully formed eggs inside a slaughtered chicken may be eaten with milk. They are considered independent — not the chicken's meat — and therefore exempt from the prohibition of meat and milk.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How close must a rooster be for a hen to lay eggs only during the day (potentially making a Festival-morning egg forbidden)?
A. Within four cubits of the hen
B. Within the same courtyard or enclosure
C. Close enough for the hen to hear the rooster's call during the day
D. Always within sixty houses, regardless of sound
Correct: C — Rav Gamda said in Rav's name: the rooster must be close enough for the hen to hear it calling during the day. If it is too far to be heard by day, the hen is considered without a nearby mate and may lay at night.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
On what point do Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel actually disagree regarding chametz measures?
A. Whether verbal nullification (bitul) is sufficient to fulfill the obligation
B. The minimum measure for removal (ביעור): Beit Shammai require a date-bulk for leavened bread; Beit Hillel require olive-bulk for both
C. Whether Rabbinic or Biblical law governs chametz on Pesach
D. The definition of what constitutes leavened bread vs. sourdough
Correct: B — Per Rabbi Yosei bar Ḥanina, the dispute concerns the measure that triggers the obligation of removal only. Beit Shammai require a date-bulk for chametz (leavened bread) vs. olive-bulk for sourdough; Beit Hillel require olive-bulk for both. For eating violations, all agree: olive-bulk.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If one transgressed and slaughtered a bird on Yom Tov without preparing earth beforehand, what do Beit Hillel permit him to do?
A. Nothing — the mitzvah of covering the blood is cancelled for that slaughter
B. He must wait until after Yom Tov to cover the blood
C. He may dig up earth with a shovel and cover the blood after the fact
D. He must ask a non-Jew to dig up the earth for him
Correct: C — Beit Hillel concede that b'di'eved (after the fact), if one already slaughtered without preparing earth, he may dig with a shovel and cover the blood — the mitzvah of כיסוי הדם is not forfeited even in this case.
