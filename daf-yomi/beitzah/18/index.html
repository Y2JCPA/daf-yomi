🪣 Four Amoraim, Four Reasons: Why Immerse Vessels on Shabbat Is Forbidden

Rabba

  • Lest one carry the impure vessel 4 cubits through the public domain to reach a ritual bath
  • Applies even if mikveh is in one's courtyard (general decree)
  • Shabbat decree extended to Yom Tov by further rabbinic enactment

Rav Yosef

  • Decree lest one wring out the vessel after immersion
  • Extended to non-wringable vessels (stone, metal) by secondary decree
  • Focused on the post-immersion act, not the act of immersion itself

Rav Beivai

  • Lest people delay purifying their vessels before Yom Tov
  • Relying on the ability to immerse them on the Festival itself
  • A practical concern about procrastination and pre-Festival preparation

Rava

  • Immersion looks like repairing the vessel — tikkun kli (תיקון כלי)
  • Vessel was unfit for use; immersion renders it fit — resembles melacha
  • The most conceptually fundamental of the four reasons

⚔️ Abaye Challenges Rabba — Step by Step

Rabba
One may not immerse a vessel on Shabbat — lest he carry it four cubits in the public domain to reach a ritual bath.
Abaye
What if he has a pit full of mikveh-quality water in his own courtyard? No public domain, no carrying — what reason remains?
Rabba
The Sages issued a general decree — since most people lack such a pit, the prohibition applies uniformly to all.
Abaye
Fine for Shabbat. But on Yom Tov, carrying between domains is permitted! Your entire reason falls away — why is immersion still forbidden?
Rabba
The Sages decreed Yom Tov to be like Shabbat in this matter — so that people do not distinguish between them and come to err.
📌 Pattern Each of Rabba's answers generates a further decree. This cascading structure reveals the weakness of his reason — motivating the search for deeper foundations.

⚗️ Drawing Water on Yom Tov with an Impure Vessel — The Baraita

Scenario: One needs water from a spring or ritual bath on Yom Tov. The only available vessel is ritually impure.
↓
Action permitted: One may draw water using the impure pail. As it is filled, the pail is completely submerged — and thus becomes ritually pure through immersion.
↓
Why permitted? The purification is incidental (תוך כדי מילוי) — the primary purpose is drawing water, not immersing the vessel. The purification happens as a byproduct.
↓
Contrast with forbidden case: Deliberately immersing a vessel specifically to purify it is prohibited — because purification is now the sole intention, and it resembles tikkun kli.
↓
Key principle: Intent matters. Incidental purification during a permitted act ≠ deliberate immersion of a vessel for the purpose of ritual fitness.

🤝 Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel Agree: Stone Vessels and Water Purification

כלי אבנים אינם מקבלים טומאה — Stone vessels do not receive impurity
🤝 Rare Agreement — Both Schools Concur The Ruling: One may bring ritually impure water into contact with ritually pure water in stone vessels (כלי אבנים) on Yom Tov in order to purify the impure water.

Why permitted? Stone vessels (כלי אבנים) cannot contract ritual impurity under Torah law — so the act of mixing impure water with pure water through a stone vessel does not create any new prohibited state.

What remains prohibited: One may NOT immerse the impure water itself (i.e., pour it directly into the mikveh) — that constitutes a distinct prohibited act.

The Gemara later probes whose opinion the mishna reflects — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi or the Rabbis — and concludes it can be read either way with careful analysis.
