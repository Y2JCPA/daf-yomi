Beitzah 32 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Neḥemya, how may vessels be handled on Shabbat and Yom Tov?
A. Only if they are unbroken and in original condition
B. Only in the manner of their designated use
C. Any use that serves a Festival food need
D. Only for direct food preparation, never for other purposes
Correct: B — Rabbi Neḥemya holds that all vessels may be handled only in the manner of their designated use — a minority (stringent) view that goes beyond standard muktze rules and limits any secondary use of vessels.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Abaye reject Rav Yosef's proof from Rabbi Meir that hollowing a lamp constitutes creating a vessel?
A. Rabbi Meir only discussed metal vessels, not clay
B. Rabbi Meir's ruling applies to vessels that can hold liquids, but a lamp holds only a wick and cannot
C. Rabbi Meir held that unfired clay never achieves vessel status
D. Rabbi Meir was discussing Shabbat impurity rules, which don't apply to Yom Tov
Correct: B — Abaye argues that Rabbi Meir grants vessel status to unfired clay because such vessels can hold solids. But a lamp is not designed to hold liquids or contents — only a wick — so the analogy to vessel-creation fails.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yehuda permit cutting a wick on Yom Tov, given that using a knife is forbidden?
A. Using a dull knife so that no clean edge is formed
B. Having a non-Jew perform the cutting
C. Burning it at the mouths of two candles simultaneously so no single new wick is completed
D. Soaking the wick first so it tears rather than cuts
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehuda permits cutting by fire — placing the wick between two lit candles so both ends are burned simultaneously. Since no single prepared wick-vessel results, the prohibition of 'completing a vessel' is avoided.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rav Naḥman rules that large lavatory stones may be arranged together on Yom Tov, unlike drawing two barrels together. What is his reasoning?
A. Lavatory stones are not vessels, so no vessel is being created
B. Arranging them is not done in the usual manner of building
C. The need for them overrides the prohibition since it involves human dignity
D. They were already designated for this use before Yom Tov
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman permits arranging lavatory stones because doing so does not resemble the usual manner of building — the act lacks the formal character of construction (binyan) that the Sages prohibited.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which of these is NOT among Rav Natan bar Abba's 'three whose lives are not lives'?
A. One who depends on another's table for sustenance
B. One whose wife rules over him
C. One whose children have abandoned Torah study
D. One whose body is overcome by suffering
Correct: C — The three are: one dependent on others' table, one whose wife rules over him, and one whose body is overcome by suffering. The teaching about children and Torah study does not appear in this list.
