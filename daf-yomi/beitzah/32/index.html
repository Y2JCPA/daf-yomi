⚔️ Shmuel's Ruling on Fastenings — Defended and Challenged

Gemara
Shmuel permits untying fastenings on doors. Which tanna agrees?
Baraita
Fastenings attached to the ground: one may untie them, but not unravel or cut them — first clause solved!
Gemara
But Shmuel also permits unraveling — the second clause. Which tanna supports that?
Gemara
The baraita follows Rabbi Neḥemya (minority view). But two later tannaim reported his position differently — one permitting unraveling, one forbidding it.
Rabbi Neḥemya's Principle All vessels may be handled only in the manner of their designated use — a minority opinion with sweeping implications for both Shabbat and Yom Tov

🏺 Hollowing a Lamp — Which Tanna Forbids It?

Mishna: One may not hollow out clay to form a lamp on Yom Tov — he thereby creates a vessel
↓
Gemara asks: Who holds that unfired, unhollowed clay already becomes a vessel upon hollowing?
↓
Rav Yosef (first attempt)
Rabbi Meir — unfired clay can already receive ritual impurity, so hollowing creates a vessel
Rav Yosef (second attempt)
Rabbi Eliezer b. R. Tzadok — ḥaraniyyot pots are complete once covered; implies vessel-status from partial labor
↓
Abaye challenges both: Those opinions apply only where the vessel can hold something — a lamp is not meant to hold liquids, so the analogy fails!

🪔 Bar Kappara's Six Laws of a Wick on Yom Tov

Three Stringencies 🚫

  • May not spin (לִשְׁזוֹר) a wick from raw fibers — forbidden labor
  • May not twist it ab initio — similar to spinning
  • May not cut it with a knife — completes a vessel (a new prepared wick)

Three Leniencies ✅

  • May fold it double to improve burning
  • May singe it in fire to prepare it for lighting
  • Rabbi Yehuda: may cut by fire — burning two wicks simultaneously at their junction, so no new single wick is 'completed'

💰 Rav Natan bar Abba — Three Whose Lives Are Not Lives

כָּל הַמִּסְתַּכֵּל בְּשֻׁלְחַן אֲחֵרִים — עוֹלָמוֹ חָשַׁךְ לוֹ
שְׁלֹשָׁה חַיֵּיהֶן אֵינָן חַיִּים Rav Natan bar Abba said in Rav's name:

🔸 The callous wealthy — they are not descendants of our forefathers but of the mixed multitude (עֵרֶב רַב), for it is written: 'He will have compassion on you' — our ancestors were promised compassion by nature.

🔸 One who depends on another's table — the world is dark for him: 'He wanders abroad for bread: where is it?' (Job 15:23).

Three whose lives are not lives:
1. One who looks to others' tables
2. One whose wife rules over him
3. One whose body is overcome by suffering
