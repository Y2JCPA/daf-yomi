🥚 Core Dispute: Is the Egg Permitted After the Ordinance?

Rabba
After Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai ordained that witnesses may testify all day, the uncertainty that required two-day Rosh HaShana is gone — therefore the egg is now permitted.
Rav Yosef
The egg remains prohibited. A rabbinic decree does not lapse automatically — it requires an explicit court vote to annul. No such vote was taken regarding the egg.
Rava
The egg is still prohibited. Even Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai concedes that when witnesses arrive after mincha, both days remain sacred — proving two-day Rosh HaShana was never truly abolished.
Final Halakha Rava rules: The halakha follows Rav — the egg is prohibited — in all three related cases, whether the ruling is lenient or stringent.

📅 Why Was the Egg Originally Prohibited?

Before the Ordinance: Witnesses could only testify until mincha on Rosh HaShana
↓
If witnesses arrived after mincha, that day was holy and the following day was also kept holy — two days, either could be the true Rosh HaShana
↓
Day 1 is the true Rosh HaShana
Egg laid on Day 1 is muktzeh — set aside from Yom Tov for use on the next Yom Tov
Day 2 is the true Rosh HaShana
Egg laid on Day 2 is muktzeh — set aside from Yom Tov for use on the next Yom Tov
↓
Either way, the egg (nolad — born on Yom Tov) is prohibited on the other day of Yom Tov

🗣️ Abaye Challenges Rabba — Amora vs. Tanna

Abaye
But didn't both Rav and Shmuel rule that the egg is prohibited even after Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai's ordinance? How can you rule leniently against them?
Rabba
Your question is misplaced! I cite the teaching of Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai, a distinguished Tanna — and you cite Rav and Shmuel, who are Amoraim? An Amora cannot override a Tanna!
Gemara
But according to Rav and Shmuel — doesn't the mishna (Rosh HaShana 30b) indicate the special status was revoked? Answer: The mishna refers to outside Jerusalem; Rav and Shmuel rule about inside Jerusalem.
Hierarchical Principle An Amora cannot dispute a Tanna's explicit ruling. Rabba's citation of Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai gives his lenient view Tannaitic authority.

⚖️ Three Opinions: The Egg After the Ordinance

Rabba — Permitted

  • Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai's ordinance removed the cause of uncertainty
  • Once the reason for two days disappears, the egg prohibition lapses
  • Cites Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai as Tannaitic authority

Rav Yosef — Prohibited

  • Decrees require an explicit vote to annul — never happened for the egg
  • Proof: Mount Sinai's prohibition needed explicit divine permission (Deut. 5:26)
  • Parallel: fourth-year vine decree required explicit vote to revoke

Rava — Prohibited

  • Even Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai concedes: witnesses after mincha → both days remain holy
  • Two-day Rosh HaShana was therefore never truly abolished
  • Final ruling: halakha follows Rav in all three related cases

The Fourth-Year Vine: Proof That Decrees Need Votes

כְּרֶם רְבָעִי — לְעַטֵּר שְׁוָקִים שֶׁל יְרוּשָׁלַיִם בְּפֵירוֹת
🍇 Neta Revai — נֶטַע רְבָעִי Fruit of a fourth-year grapevine has the status of second-tithe and must be brought to Jerusalem. Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai ordained that those living far away could redeem it with money instead — in order to adorn the markets of Jerusalem with fruit.

Later, Jerusalem was repopulated and the ordinance became unnecessary. Yet it did not lapse automatically. Rabbi Eliezer ben Hyrcanus had such a vine east of Lod; his colleagues had to formally vote to annul the decree for him.

This baraita proves: even a rabbinic ordinance enacted for a specific reason requires an explicit court vote to be revoked.

🏛️ The Rule: How a Rabbinic Decree Is Annulled

Step 1 — Enactment: A court enacts a rabbinic decree for a specific reason
↓
Step 2 — Change in Circumstances: The reason for the decree no longer applies
↓
Step 3 — Does it lapse automatically? No! The decree remains fully in force
↓
Step 4 — Required: A court of equal or greater stature must convene and explicitly vote to annul the decree
↓
Biblical Proof (Rav Yosef): The prohibition to approach Mount Sinai required explicit divine permission to end — "Return to your tents" (Deut. 5:26) — even though the revelation was over
↓
Exception — Egg vs. Testimony: Rav Adda and Rav Shalman add: unlike testimony (entrusted to courts who can distinguish), the egg prohibition is entrusted to all people — so it must remain uniform and cannot be changed situationally
