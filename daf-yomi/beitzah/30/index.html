💬 Women Filling Jugs — Is Modification Required?

Rav Ḥanan bar Rava
The Sages say: modify weekday manner on Yom Tov as much as possible. But women fill their jugs on Yom Tov without any modification at all!
Rav Ashi
It is impossible for them to do otherwise. If she fills a large jug using a small one, she increases her walking. If she fills a small jug from a large one, she increases her burden.
Rav Ashi
If she covers the jug with a wooden cover, it may fall and break. Therefore: no modification is practically possible — and they are fully permitted.
Ruling When genuine modification is impossible, the weekday manner of carrying is permitted on Yom Tov.

🤫 Leave the Jews Alone

הנח להם לישראל — מוטב שיהיו שוגגין ואל יהיו מזידין
הנח להם לישראל Rava bar Rav Ḥanin asked Abaye: Women clap, strike their thighs, and dance on Yom Tov — all rabbinically prohibited — yet we say nothing to them!

Abaye replied: "Leave the Jews alone; it is better that they be unwitting sinners than intentional sinners."

When people engage in behavior that cannot be corrected, reproving them only transforms shogeg (unwitting) violations into meizid (deliberate) ones — which is worse.

📚 Rabbinic vs. Torah Prohibitions — Does 'Leave Them' Apply?

Initial View: Rabbinic Only

  • Some understood the principle applies only to rabbinic prohibitions
  • For Torah-level prohibitions, one must rebuke the sinner
  • Torah law is too serious to overlook — silence is not an option

Gemara Rejects: Both Apply

  • The Gemara states: 'This is not so — it is no different'
  • Even for Torah-level prohibitions: if uncorrectable, do not rebuke
  • Better an unwitting sinner than an intentional one — in all cases
