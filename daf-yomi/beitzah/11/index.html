⚖️ Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel: Yom Tov Preparations

Beit Shammai
One may not take a large pestle from a mortar for other purposes on Yom Tov — it appears like weekday preparation.
Beit Hillel
The pestle is permitted for cutting meat on Yom Tov — it serves a direct Festival food need.
Beit Shammai
One may not remove the shutters of a shop on Yom Tov — removing them resembles demolishing.
Beit Hillel
One may remove the shutters and even replace them — if not permitted, people will not remove them and food access is blocked.
Point of Agreement Both Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel agree: once the pestle has been used to cut Festival meat, it becomes muktzeh and may not be moved further on Yom Tov.

🐦 Fledglings Found Before the Nest

Fledglings were designated inside the nest for Yom Tov — but found outside the nest
↓
Another nest within 50 cubits (direct line)
Prohibited — fledglings may have jumped from the other nest; designation is in doubt
No other nest within 50 cubits
Permitted — they must be the designated fledglings; no other source exists

Special case (Gemara): Another nest is within 50 cubits but situated around a corner — fledglings cannot jump there directly → Permitted

🔨 The Pestle — Dispute and Abaye's Clarification

Beit Shammai
The large pestle may not be taken from the mortar for any other use on Yom Tov (e.g., cutting meat).
Beit Hillel
The pestle is permitted — it may be used to cut meat or serve another food need on Yom Tov.
Abaye
This dispute applies only to the pestle. A wooden anvil for breaking bones is permitted by all — it is obviously a Yom Tov food tool.
Abaye (alt. version)
Even a new wooden anvil is permitted — we do not fear one will reconsider and use it as furniture rather than for bones.
Beit Shammai (baraita)
One may not lead a butcher with a knife to an animal on Yom Tov — he might reconsider and choose a different animal, having handled the knife for nothing.
Gemara Distinction Beit Shammai's concern about reconsidering applies to an animal (one might switch to a fatter one) but not to bones — bones are uniform, so no reconsideration is likely.

🧂 Laws of Salting Meat on Yom Tov

Basic rule: One may salt meat for roasting on Yom Tov, but not for cooking in a pot — the Tosefta states this explicitly.
↓
Shmuel rules: One may salt several pieces of meat at one time on Yom Tov, even if only one piece is needed — because it is all a single act of salting.
↓
Rav Adda bar Ahava employed artifice: he would salt bone by bone, then ask aloud 'Who wants bones?' — implying he had salted only what was requested, not in advance.
↓
Fats of the animal: One may not salt fats on Yom Tov (done to prevent decomposition, not for food) — even if the animal was slaughtered on Yom Tov itself.
↓
Rabbi Yehoshua vs. the majority: Dispute about turning over fats. Rav Mattana: halakha follows Rabbi Yehoshua — or does not follow him (two versions reported in Gemara).

🚪 Shop Shutters on Yom Tov

Beit Shammai

  • May not remove shutters from a store on Yom Tov
  • Reason: resembles the prohibited labor of demolishing
  • Shutters are heavy boards removed from hinges — not merely opening a door

Beit Hillel

  • May remove shutters and even replace them on Yom Tov
  • If only removal is allowed but not replacement, people will not remove them — blocking food access
  • Replacing is permitted to ensure the initial removal takes place

R. Shimon ben Elazar (baraita)

  • Both Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel agree that removing shutters is permitted
  • The dispute is only about whether one may replace them afterward
  • This baraita contradicts the mishna's plain reading — the Gemara reconciles them
