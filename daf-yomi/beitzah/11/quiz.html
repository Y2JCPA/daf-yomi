Beitzah 11 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye, what is the ruling regarding a wooden anvil for breaking bones on Yom Tov?
A. Forbidden according to Beit Shammai, just like the pestle
B. Permitted by all — unlike the pestle, this dispute does not apply to it
C. Forbidden if it is new, permitted only if it is already used
D. Depends on whether it was designated before Yom Tov
Correct: B — Abaye clarifies that the dispute between Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel applies only to the pestle. A wooden anvil for breaking bones is permitted by all, as it is clearly a food-preparation tool and not subject to the same concern.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Shmuel, how many pieces of meat may one salt at one time on Yom Tov?
A. Only one piece at a time, to avoid resemblance to weekday salting
B. Only as many pieces as will be eaten at that specific meal
C. Several pieces at once, even if only one is needed — it constitutes one act
D. As many as desired, but only if they were designated before Yom Tov
Correct: C — Shmuel rules that one may salt several pieces of meat at one time even if only one piece is needed, because the entire salting is considered a single unified act.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When fledglings designated for Yom Tov are found outside the nest, and there is another nest within 50 cubits but situated around a corner — what is the ruling?
A. Prohibited — the 50-cubit rule applies regardless of the layout
B. Permitted — fledglings cannot jump around a corner, so the other nest is irrelevant
C. Prohibited — we always rule stringently when fledglings are involved
D. Permitted only if the fledglings are too young to fly or jump
Correct: B — The Gemara rules that if the other nest is around a corner rather than in a direct line, the fledglings could not have reached it by hopping. Therefore the designated fledglings are permitted even though another nest is within 50 cubits.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which of the following is NOT one of the three leniencies enumerated by Ulla?
A. Spreading out a slaughtered animal's hide on Yom Tov
B. Replacing the shutters of shops on Yom Tov
C. Replacing a bandage in the Temple
D. Salting multiple pieces of meat at once on Yom Tov
Correct: D — Ulla's three leniencies are: (1) spreading out a hide, (2) replacing shop shutters, and (3) replacing a bandage in the Temple. Salting multiple pieces of meat at once is a separate ruling attributed to Shmuel, not part of Ulla's list.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
On what point do both Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel agree regarding the pestle on Yom Tov?
A. It may be used freely to cut meat throughout the Festival day
B. It may never be moved on Yom Tov under any circumstances whatsoever
C. Once used to cut Festival meat, it becomes muktzeh and may not be moved again
D. It is permitted only if it was designated for food use before Yom Tov
Correct: C — The baraita teaches that despite their dispute about initial use, both Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel agree: once the pestle has been used to cut meat for the Festival, it has served its purpose and becomes muktzeh — it is then forbidden to move it further.
