QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, if someone brings grain stalks inside specifically to husk and eat casually (not to grind), what is required before eating?
A. He may eat freely since the grain is still unprocessed
B. He must separate teruma before eating
C. He need only separate ma'aser, not teruma
D. He must first bring the stalks to the granary for threshing
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi holds that even unprocessed grain brought in for casual use obligates one to separate teruma. This contrasts with Rabbi Yosei b. Rabbi Yehuda, who requires full processing (threshing, piling in granary) before the obligation takes effect.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Resh Lakish, if a Levite takes first tithe (ma'aser rishon) while the grain is still on the stalks before threshing, what is the legal status?
A. The tithe is invalid and must be re-separated after the grain is threshed
B. The tithe is valid; the Levite must still process the stalks and separate terumat ma'aser
C. The tithe is valid and the Levite owes nothing further to the priest
D. The owner must first give teruma gedola to the priest before the Levite may keep anything
Correct: B — Resh Lakish rules that the designation as first tithe stands ('its name stands'), but the Levite must thresh the stalks in the standard granary manner and must still separate terumat ma'aser (one tenth of the tithe) for the priest.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What method does Abaye (in the name of Rav Yosef) permit for husking grain on a Festival?
A. Using a wooden pestle and mortar as on a weekday
B. Using a hand-mill with reduced speed
C. Rubbing kernels with one finger on another
D. Soaking the grain in water to loosen the husk
Correct: C — On a Festival one may husk grain by placing kernels between two fingers and rubbing — 'one finger on another.' This informal, non-standard method distinguishes Festival husking from the prohibited weekday-style labor.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Abba Elazar ben Gimmel interpret 'your teruma shall be reckoned to you as grain from the threshing floor' (Numbers 18:26)?
A. Terumat ma'aser may be estimated by volume rather than exact weight
B. Terumat ma'aser must be separated only from fully processed produce — at the granary or winepress
C. The Levite's first tithe is equal in holiness to the priest's teruma gedola
D. Teruma may only be given while grain remains at the threshing floor before transport
Correct: B — Abba Elazar ben Gimmel derives an analogy: just as teruma gedola is separated only from processed granary/winepress produce, so too terumat ma'aser must be taken only from fully processed grain — not from stalks or bundles in early stages.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The Gemara notes that Rav's wife and Rabbi Ḥiyya's wife peeled barley for their husbands on Shabbat by the cupful. What does this demonstrate about Shabbat law?
A. Women are exempt from the Shabbat prohibition on threshing
B. Peeling grain by the cupful on Shabbat is permitted — it does not constitute the forbidden labor of threshing
C. The Shabbat prohibition on threshing applies only to wheat, not barley
D. Peeling a cupful of grain creates a tithing obligation even on Shabbat
Correct: B — The Gemara uses these examples to prove that peeling grain by the cupful on Shabbat is fully permitted. Shabbat forbids only melekhet machshevet — planned, constructive labor. Casual hand-peeling is not the forbidden labor of threshing (דישה), so it carries no Shabbat prohibition.
