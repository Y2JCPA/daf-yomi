⚖️ The Core Dispute: When Does Teruma Obligation Begin?

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
One who brings stalks inside to husk and eat casually must still separate teruma first — the obligation takes effect even for unprocessed grain on stalks.
Rabbi Yosei b. R. Yehuda
The teruma obligation takes effect only when grain is fully processed — threshed and piled in the granary. Stalks brought in for casual use are not yet subject to teruma.
Abaye (first version)
This dispute applies only to grain stalks, which have a standard processing route. But for legume bundles, everyone agrees they are not yet obligated in tithes.
Abaye (alternate version)
Even for legumes the same logic may apply — the same dispute between Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi and Rabbi Yosei b. Rabbi Yehuda governs their tithing obligation as well.
Key Question At what stage of food processing does the obligation to separate teruma (תרומה) and tithes (מעשר) take effect — at first use, or only after full granary processing?

📜 First Tithe Taken Early — Resh Lakish's Ruling

Scenario
A Levite precedes the priest and takes ma'aser rishon (first tithe) while grain is still on the stalks — before the owner threshes or piles it.
Resh Lakish (ruling)
The tithe designation stands. But the Levite must thresh and process the stalks normally — and must still separate terumat ma'aser (a tenth of the tithe) for the priest.
Rav Pappa (challenge)
If there's a Torah source for this — shouldn't even first tithe taken from processed grain require prior separation of teruma gedola by the Levite before giving it away?
Abaye (answer)
Teruma gedola is separated only from the granary and winepress — i.e., from fully processed grain. First tithe taken while on stalks is exempt from prior teruma gedola obligation.
Numbers 18:26 "And your teruma shall be reckoned to you as grain from the threshing floor" — Abba Elazar ben Gimmel: terumat ma'aser is separated only from fully processed produce, just like teruma gedola

👐 Husking and Winnowing on Shabbat and Yom Tov

Question: How may one husk grain on a Festival (Yom Tov) in a permitted manner?
↓
Abaye (in name of Rav Yosef): One finger on another — place kernels between two fingers and rub gently. No tool; hand-rubbing only.
↓
Rav Avya: Agrees — one finger on another is the permitted Festival method. Deviates from weekday instrument-use.
↓
Winnowing question: How may one blow off chaff on Shabbat? Rav Adda bar Ahava said in name of Rav: Blow with the mouth only — not with a fan or vessel.
↓
Underlying principle: Shabbat prohibits only melekhet machshevet — planned, constructive labor. Informal husking by hand is permitted; forming a pile on Shabbat does not constitute threshing (דישה).

⚖️ Tithes vs. Shabbat — Where Each Is More Stringent

Shabbat Leniencies

  • Forming a pile (עֵירוּם) on Shabbat: permitted — not constructive labor
  • Peeling a cupful of barley: permitted (Rav's wife and R. Ḥiyya's wife did so regularly)
  • Husking finger-on-finger: permitted on Yom Tov
  • Prohibition requires melechet machshevet — only planned, creative work forbidden

Tithe Stringencies

  • Forming a pile (עֵירוּם) establishes tithing obligation — eating before tithing forbidden
  • Peeling a handful of barley: triggers tithing obligation
  • R. Elazar: tithing can be more stringent than Shabbat in certain cases
  • Granary standard applies: processed grain must be tithed regardless of how casually done
