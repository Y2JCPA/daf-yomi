Beitzah 17 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
When a Festival falls on Shabbat, how many Amida blessings do Beit Hillel require?
A. Six blessings
B. Seven blessings
C. Eight blessings
D. Nine blessings
Correct: B — Beit Hillel say seven blessings, merging Shabbat and Festival into one middle blessing. Beit Shammai require eight — inserting a separate Festival blessing between the third and concluding standard blessings.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Ravina object to concluding the blessing 'Who sanctifies Israel, Shabbat, and the Seasons'?
A. The formula is too long for a Festival prayer
B. It implies Israel sanctifies Shabbat — but only God sanctifies Shabbat; Israel sanctifies Festivals
C. The word 'Seasons' must always precede 'Shabbat' in liturgy
D. This formula belongs exclusively to Rosh Hashanah
Correct: B — Ravina argued: Israel sanctifies the Festivals by declaring the new month and setting the calendar — but Shabbat is fixed by God and sanctified by God alone. Therefore Shabbat must precede Israel: 'Who sanctifies Shabbat, Israel, and the Seasons.'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the function of eiruv tavshilin?
A. To merge two courtyards for carrying on Shabbat
B. To permit cooking and baking on a Festival for the Shabbat that immediately follows
C. To allow one to eat before morning prayers on a Festival
D. To purify food that became ritually impure on the eve of the Festival
Correct: B — Eiruv tavshilin (joining of cooked foods) is a dish set aside before the Festival that symbolically 'begins' the cooking process, thereby permitting one to cook on the Festival day for the Shabbat that follows.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Gemara's baraita, if one forgot to prepare eiruv tavshilin, which activities are forbidden on the Festival for Shabbat?
A. Only baking bread is forbidden
B. Only cooking liquids is forbidden; baking is permitted
C. Baking, cooking, and insulating food are all forbidden
D. Nothing — the eiruv is a recommendation, not a requirement
Correct: C — The baraita explicitly states: one who did not prepare eiruv tavshilin may neither bake, nor cook, nor insulate food on the Festival for Shabbat. All three preparatory activities are prohibited.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
On what point do Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel agree regarding immersing vessels when a Festival falls on Sunday?
A. One may immerse vessels on Shabbat if the Festival is the next day
B. One may not immerse a vessel on Shabbat under any circumstances
C. Immersion on the Festival itself is always forbidden
D. Vessels need not be immersed for a Festival — only for Shabbat
Correct: B — Despite disagreeing about the correct time to immerse (before Shabbat vs. on the Festival), both Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel agree that immersing a vessel on Shabbat is prohibited, as Rabba explains it resembles completing/repairing a vessel — a form of prohibited labor.
