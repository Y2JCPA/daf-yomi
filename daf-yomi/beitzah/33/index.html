🎋 Bamboo Branch for a Skewer

Rav Naḥman
Taking a bamboo branch as a skewer is prohibited. It was never designated as a vessel before Yom Tov — pure muktze!
Rav Sheshet
It is permitted. The branch is fit for use and qualifies as prepared for the Festival.
Gemara (Version 1)
A wet branch — everyone agrees it is permitted (fit for animal fodder). The dispute is about a dry branch.
Gemara (Version 2)
A dry branch — everyone agrees it is permitted (fit for fuel). The dispute is about a wet branch.
Key Principle Muktze applies when an item had no designated use before Yom Tov — but fitness for any use (fodder or fuel) may exempt it

🌿 Wet vs. Dry Branch — Two Talmudic Versions

Version 1: Dispute = Dry Branch

  • 🟢 Wet branch: ALL PERMIT — fit for animal fodder, not muktze
  • 🔴 Dry branch: IN DISPUTE
  • Rav Naḥman: prohibited — not fit for fodder, not designated as vessel
  • Rav Sheshet: permitted — still fit for kindling/fuel

Version 2: Dispute = Wet Branch

  • 🟢 Dry branch: ALL PERMIT — fit for fuel and kindling
  • 🔴 Wet branch: IN DISPUTE
  • Prohibitor: not fit for human consumption → muktze
  • Permitter: fit for animal fodder → permitted

🔥 Rava: Firebrands from the Wood Storehouse

בֵּיצָה ל״ג עַמּוּד א׳
Rava's Ruling A woman may not enter a wood storehouse to take a firebrand (a partly burnt piece of wood used to stoke a bonfire) — because it was not designated as a vessel before the Festival, making it muktze. Apparent contradiction: Rava once told his attendant, "Roast a duck for me — and throw its innards to the cat!" (implying leniency on muktze for non-designated items). Resolution: Rava is strict like Rabbi Yehuda only in clear cases of preparation-muktze (like raw firewood). His personal instruction about the duck involved a different class of items.

🔥 Prohibited Ways to Produce New Fire on Yom Tov

Core Rule: One may not produce new fire on a Festival by any means
↓
❌ Rubbing two pieces of wood against each other
↓
❌ Striking stones against each other to create sparks
↓
❌ Using hot dirt or superheated earth
↓
❌ Striking tiles against each other
↓
❌ Concentrating sunlight through water (or similar lens effect)
↓
❌ Whitening tiles by heating them to roast food — also forbidden
↓
Reason: Creating new fire on Yom Tov is like an act of creation — one brings something entirely new into existence
