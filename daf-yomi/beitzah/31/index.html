🏡 What Qualifies as a Karpef?

Rabbi Yehuda
A karpef is any enclosure near a city. Proximity to the city is the defining criterion — whether or not it requires a key to enter.
Rabbi Yosei
A karpef is any place one regularly enters with a key — it must be locked and secured. Distance from the city is irrelevant, provided it is within the Shabbat limit.
Gemara
Rabbi Yosei's precise phrasing — 'any place into which one enters with a key' — indicates a habitual, standing requirement of locked access, not merely an occasional feature.
Practical Difference Rabbi Yehuda: open enclosure near a city qualifies. Rabbi Yosei: only a locked enclosure qualifies, even if distant — but must be within the Shabbat limit (תחום שבת).

🌲 Gathered vs. Scattered Wood

✅ Gathered Wood (Permitted)

  • Piled up intentionally before the Festival
  • Permitted from open fields
  • Permitted from a karpef
  • Shows pre-Festival mental designation for kindling use
  • Even dry, detached wood qualifies if gathered

❌ Scattered Wood (Muktzeh)

  • Not pre-designated for use before the Festival
  • Forbidden from open fields
  • Permitted only from inside a karpef
  • Rava: reed/vine leaves — always 'scattered' even if grouped
  • Wind-dispersible items treated as already scattered

🍃 Reed Leaves and Vine Leaves

רָבָא אָמַר: עֲלֵי גָּמִי וַעֲלֵי דֶּקֶל, אַף עַל פִּי שֶׁכְּנָסָן — הָרוּחַ מְפַזַּרְתָּן
Rava's Ruling Reed leaves and vine leaves, even if gathered together in one spot, are considered scattered — because if a wind comes, it will scatter them. Since they lack permanence in their gathered state, they are treated as already dispersed and are therefore muktzeh and forbidden on a Festival.

🏠 Locked House Full of Produce

A house is filled with produce and locked on all sides before the Festival — produce is technically inaccessible
↓
A hole forms naturally in a wall or roof during the Festival
↓
Rabbi Meir: One may remove produce through the hole — or even proactively make a new hole
↓
Gemara challenges: Making a hole = dismantling a tent (אֹהֶל), a biblically prohibited labor?
↓
Rav Naḥumi bar Adda: Rabbi Meir speaks of a Festival — the leniency applies on Yom Tov but not on Shabbat
↓
Shmuel: Knotted rope fastenings on cellar doors — may untie the knot, but may not unravel the rope into fibers or cut it
