Beitzah 31 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, from where may one bring wood on a Festival?
A. From any field, whether scattered or gathered
B. Only from wood gathered in a karpef
C. From any gathered pile, whether in a field or karpef
D. Only wood explicitly set aside verbally before the Festival
Correct: B — Shmuel rules wood may only be brought from a karpef. The mishna's permission for gathered piles in open fields is not accepted as mainstream halakha by Shmuel.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rava rule that reed leaves and vine leaves are muktzeh even when gathered together?
A. They have no practical use on a Festival
B. They were never explicitly designated before the Festival
C. A wind can scatter them, so they are treated as already scattered
D. They are attached to the ground and cannot be moved
Correct: C — Rava rules that because wind would scatter them, gathered reed/vine leaves lack permanence — they are treated as if already dispersed and therefore forbidden as muktzeh.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the key distinction between Rabbi Yehuda and Rabbi Yosei's definitions of a karpef?
A. Rabbi Yehuda requires a key; Rabbi Yosei requires proximity to the city
B. Rabbi Yehuda requires proximity to the city; Rabbi Yosei requires a locked enclosure within the Shabbat limit
C. Both agree on the definition but dispute the Shabbat limit requirement
D. Rabbi Yehuda speaks of Shabbat; Rabbi Yosei speaks of Festivals
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda focuses on nearness to a city; Rabbi Yosei focuses on being locked (requiring a key), with the added requirement that it be within the Shabbat limit.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which tool is permitted for chopping wood on a Festival, and why?
A. A saw, because it is the most efficient
B. An ax used on its normal face, because wood is needed for a mitzva
C. A cleaver, because it constitutes an unusual (shinui) manner of chopping
D. Any tool, as long as the wood is for a permitted purpose
Correct: C — A cleaver is permitted because it represents a deviation (shinui) from the normal weekday method. Ax, saw, and sickle — being standard tools — are forbidden on the Festival.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Regarding knotted rope fastenings on cellar doors, what does Shmuel permit on a Festival?
A. Cutting the rope entirely
B. Untying the knot only
C. Unraveling the rope into its component fibers
D. Both untying and unraveling
Correct: B — Shmuel permits only untying the knot itself. Unraveling the rope into its constituent fibers or cutting it entirely remains forbidden even on a Festival.
