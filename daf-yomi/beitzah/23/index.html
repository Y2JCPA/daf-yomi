⚖️ The Core Dispute: Unintentional Acts on Shabbat

Rabbi Yehuda
An unintentional act is prohibited. Even without intent, if a forbidden labor will inevitably result, the action is forbidden from the outset.
Rabbi Shimon
An unintentional act is permitted. Without explicit intent to perform the prohibited labor, the action is allowed — even if a forbidden outcome might occur.
The Rabbis
We agree unintentional acts are prohibited (like R. Yehuda), and we add a decree: even acts that merely resemble prohibited ones are forbidden — e.g., brushing an animal is prohibited because it resembles combing.
R. Elazar ben Azarya
The halakha follows Rabbi Shimon. Therefore combing animals, grinding pepper, and the cow's strap are all permitted. R. Naḥman citing Shmuel concurs: unintentional acts are permitted.
Final Ruling — Rav Naḥman citing Shmuel The halakha follows Rabbi Shimon: an unintentional act (where the prohibited labor is not intended) is permitted on Shabbat and Yom Tov.

🌿 Placing Spices on a Hot Surface — Yom Tov Rules

May one place spices on a hot surface on Yom Tov to enhance fragrance?
↓
🔥 Hot metal surface
Prohibited
Risk of extinguishing or kindling — forbidden labor on Yom Tov
🟫 Hot earthenware (initially)
Permitted
No concern of extinguishing or kindling fire
🟫 Hot earthenware (Rabba)
Also Prohibited!
Creates a new scent in the earthenware itself — making new things on Yom Tov is forbidden
↓
But permitted: Pressing aromatic wood between fingers or snipping a piece to release its natural fragrance — no new scent is created in any vessel

🔥 The Ketura Controversy — Rav Geviha from Bei Katil

Rav Geviha
I hereby teach at the Exilarch's entrance: Ketura is permitted on a Festival! — without further explanation.
Ameimar
What do you mean by ketura? If it means tying ornamental knots — that's a craftsman's skilled act, prohibited. If it means burning incense — that creates a new thing, also prohibited. So what exactly did you permit?!
Rav Geviha
I meant simple smelling — inhaling an already-existing pleasant fragrance. That is permitted.
Alt. Version (Ameimar)
Even burning with fire on Yom Tov resembles creating something new. The only permissible act is smelling a fragrance that already exists without any new action.
Halakhic Bottom Line Smelling existing fragrances on Yom Tov: permitted. Burning incense or creating new scents: forbidden as making a new thing (בורא דבר חדש).

🐄 R. Elazar ben Azarya's Three Leniencies vs. The Rabbis

✅ R. Elazar Permits

  • 🐄 Cow's strap on Shabbat: The strap between the horns is an ornament for the cow — not a burden, so carrying rules don't apply
  • 🪮 Combing with fine comb on Yom Tov: Even if wounds might incidentally result, the act is unintentional — permitted per R. Shimon
  • 🫙 Grinding pepper on Yom Tov: Considered a legitimate human need for the Festival meal

❌ The Rabbis Prohibit

  • 🐄 Cow's strap: The strap is a burden, not ornament — forbidden as carrying on Shabbat
  • 🪮 Combing: Certainly causes wounds (prohibited), and brushing also decreed forbidden since it resembles combing
  • 🫙 Grinding pepper: A weekday-type labor; not appropriate to Yom Tov even for a Festival need

🤔 Was It Really 'His Cow'?

  • The mishna's singular 'his cow' implies he owned only one
  • But Rav teaches: R. Elazar had 12,000 calves tithed from his herds annually — an extremely wealthy man!
  • Resolution: It was a neighbor's cow — R. Elazar did not protest, effectively ruling it permitted
  • Or: One specific cow happened to go out this way, drawing his implicit approval

🐟 Trapping on Yom Tov — A Step-by-Step Guide

Fish from ponds: ❌ Prohibited — even with intent to eat that day. Fish in a pond can escape; taking them is an act of hunting, a forbidden Yom Tov labor.
↓
Placing food before pond fish: ❌ Also prohibited — it is not one's normal domestic practice (לא הוכן), and serves the prohibited trapping goal.
↓
Animals/birds from enclosures (בֵּיבָרִין): ✅ Permitted — they are considered already captured (תפוסים). Taking them is not an act of hunting.
↓
Placing food before penned animals: ✅ Also permitted — treated like feeding any household animal, a normal domestic act on Yom Tov.
↓
Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel's principle: Not all enclosures are equal! Only enclosures from which the animal cannot realistically escape make the animal 'already caught.' A large open enclosure where the animal can flee = not yet captured.
