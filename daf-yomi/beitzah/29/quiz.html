Beitzah 29 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, what specifically does the mishna prohibit when it says 'not by measure'?
A. Mentioning the name of any measure (e.g., 'one kav')
B. Any estimation of quantity on a Festival
C. Using a vessel that is formally designated as a measuring vessel
D. Requesting any item from a grocer by amount
Correct: C — Shmuel interprets the mishna as prohibiting only a vessel formally designated for measuring. An ordinary vessel used incidentally to measure remains permitted according to Shmuel.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Abba Shaul ben Botnit collect 300 jugs of wine to return to the Temple treasury?
A. He had sold the wine on a Festival in violation of halakha
B. His customers had overpaid due to incorrect weights
C. He believed he had unfairly profited from the foam at the top of his measures
D. The wine belonged to the Temple by rabbinic decree
Correct: C — Abba Shaul believed the foam at the top of filled measures gave him more product than customers paid for — he collected this surplus over time and brought 300 jugs to the Temple treasury.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the practical halakha according to Abaye regarding a woman measuring flour on a Festival for challah separation?
A. Permitted, following Rav's more lenient ruling
B. Prohibited, following Shmuel's ruling
C. Permitted only if she uses a non-designated vessel
D. Permitted only on the intermediate days of a Festival
Correct: B — Abaye reasons that since a baraita already permitted the practice, Shmuel must have stated his prohibition specifically to override that baraita. Therefore the practical halakha follows Shmuel — it is prohibited.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
On a Festival, one goes to a familiar grocer. Which request is clearly permitted according to the Tosefta?
A. 'Fill me exactly one kav of nuts'
B. 'Give me fifty nuts and twenty eggs'
C. 'Weigh me half a litra of honey'
D. 'Measure me two log of oil'
Correct: B — The Tosefta explicitly permits going to a familiar grocer and requesting items by count — 20 eggs, 50 nuts, 10 peaches, etc. — as long as no measure name is invoked. This mirrors how a homeowner counts in his own house.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Regarding sifting flour a second time on a Festival, which position represents the majority view?
A. Permitted, because it honors the Festival with better bread
B. Forbidden, because it is unnecessary extra effort
C. Permitted only if a foreign object fell in after the first sifting
D. Disputed without a clear majority ruling
Correct: B — The majority view prohibits sifting flour a second time on a Festival as unnecessary extra effort (tircha yeteira). Only Rabbi Papeyyas and Rabbi Yehuda ben Beteira dissent, permitting it to improve bread quality for Festival honor.
