⚖️ Two Readings of 'Not by Measure'

Shmuel
Vessel interpretation: The prohibition is using a vessel designated as a measure. An ordinary vessel used incidentally to measure is perfectly permitted.
Rava
Name interpretation: The prohibition is naming the measure. 'Fill this vessel' is fine — but 'give me one kav' or 'two kav' is forbidden.
Rabbi Yehuda
Stringent on Festival joy: even a non-designated vessel must not be used for measuring on the Festival.
Rabbis
Lenient on Festival joy here: only a formally designated measuring vessel is prohibited — ordinary vessels are fine.
The Mishna's Rule 'Fill this vessel for me' ✓ — 'Give me one kav' ✗. Return value after the Festival; do not name a price.

🔄 The Apparent Contradiction: Who Is Stricter?

Rabbi Yehuda

  • Stringent HERE (Daf 29): prohibits use of any measuring vessel on a Festival
  • Lenient on Daf 28a: permits weighing meat against a vessel
  • Resolution: on 28a the vessel is NOT designated for measuring — permitted
  • Principle: formally designated measuring vessels are uniquely problematic

The Rabbis

  • Lenient HERE (Daf 29): permit use of non-designated vessels for measuring
  • Stringent on Daf 28a: prohibit weighing meat on a Festival
  • Resolution: on 28a, weighing meat IS acting in a weekday commercial manner
  • Principle: weekday-style commerce is what violates Festival spirit

🌾 Measuring Flour for Challah — Rav vs. Shmuel

Rav
Permitted: A woman may measure flour on a Festival to know the exact dough amount — this allows her to separate challah generously and accurately.
Shmuel
Prohibited: Measuring flour is forbidden on a Festival even for the purpose of challah separation.
Abaye
Since a baraita already permitted it, Shmuel must have stated his ruling specifically to override that baraita. Practical halakha: follows Shmuel — prohibited.
Key Principle When a Sage explicitly contradicts a baraita, he intends to establish the practical halakha against the baraita's ruling

Abba Shaul ben Botnit — 300 Jugs of Wine

אבא שאול בן בוטנית — שלש מאות חביות יין
🍷 A Story of Extraordinary Scrupulousness Abba Shaul ben Botnit, a grocer-Sage, collected 300 earthenware jugs of wine he believed he had wrongly kept — profit from the foam at the top of his measures.

The treasurers replied: 'You need not do this — buyers always account for foam and vessel remnants when paying.'

Abba Shaul: 'Even if the buyers waive it, we do not wish to benefit from it.'

Resolution: The treasurers told him to use it for communal needs — following the ruling that stolen items whose owner is unknown should fund communal purposes, thereby making atonement.

🌾 Sifting Flour a Second Time on a Festival

May flour be sifted a second time on a Festival?
↓
Majority — Forbidden
Second sifting = unnecessary extra effort (tircha yeteira) — prohibited on a Festival
R. Papeyyas & R. Yehuda ben Beteira — Permitted
Second sifting improves bread quality and honors the Festival — permitted
↓
Special case: a pebble or wood chip fell in after first sifting
↓
All agree: Remove it by hand — and Ravina rules: if removing by hand is permitted, re-sifting for the same purpose is certainly permitted
