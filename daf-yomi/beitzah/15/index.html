🍲 Eiruv Tavshilin: How to Cook on a Festival for Shabbat

Problem: It is forbidden to cook on a Festival with the initial intent to prepare food solely for Shabbat
↓
Before the Festival begins, one sets aside a cooked dish and bread — the eiruv tavshilin — symbolically beginning Shabbat preparations
↓
Effect: This 'joins' Shabbat cooking to the pre-Festival preparation, permitting one to cook on the Festival for Shabbat
↓
If the eiruv is eaten or lost before Shabbat, one may not rely on it to continue cooking — it must remain intact
↓
Derivation: Shmuel finds the source in the manna verse; Rava and Rav Ashi debate whether the ordinance honors Shabbat or the Festival itself

🏛️ Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel: How Many Dishes for Eiruv Tavshilin?

Beit Shammai
Two cooked dishes are required for a valid eiruv tavshilin — one dish alone is insufficient.
Beit Hillel
One cooked dish is entirely sufficient to fulfill eiruv tavshilin.
Both Agree
A fish and the egg cooked with it count as two separate dishes, satisfying even Beit Shammai's stricter requirement.
Why Prepare It the Day Before? Both schools agree the eiruv must be prepared on Festival eve, not on the Festival itself — lest one be negligent and omit it entirely. The Sages decreed this as a safeguard.

📿 Tefillin Overtaken by Shabbat on the Road

One is traveling with tefillin when Shabbat or a Festival begins unexpectedly
↓
Tefillin Already on His Head
He wears them pair by pair into his home, covering them with his hand in the interim between pairs
Tefillin Not on His Head
He places them in the nearest house to the wall — he need not don them just to carry them home
Tefillin Are Safeguarded
He may leave them in place entirely — no carrying is required at all
↓
Why allow carrying at all? Although most thieves are Jewish (and respect tefillin), dogs may defile them — this is the real danger justifying carrying them to safety

🎉 The Mitzva of Festival Joy: Rabbi Eliezer vs. Rabbi Yehoshua

Rabbi Eliezer

  • One must choose a single path for fulfilling Festival joy
  • Option A: Eat and drink entirely — fulfill joy in a completely physical manner
  • Option B: Sit and study Torah — fulfill joy in a completely spiritual manner
  • Dividing your attention leaves both obligations unfulfilled

Rabbi Yehoshua

  • Divide the Festival day into two halves
  • Half devoted to eating, drinking, and physical celebration
  • Half devoted to the study hall and Torah learning
  • Both obligations can — and should — be satisfied together

📖 Why Did the Sages Establish This Ordinance?

כבוד שבת / כבוד יום טוב
Two Reasons for Eiruv Tavshilin (Rava vs. Rav Ashi) Rava: In deference to Shabbat — by preparing an eiruv, one will select a choice portion for Shabbat and ensure its honor is not overlooked in the Festival bustle.

Rav Ashi: In deference to the Festival — people will learn: if one may not bake on a Festival for Shabbat without starting the day before, all the more so one may not bake on a Festival for an ordinary weekday.
