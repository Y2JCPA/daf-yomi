⚰️ Burial on Festival Days — Who May Perform It?

Someone dies during a Festival
↓
First Day
Gentiles must attend to the burial — Jews may not perform melakhah
Second Day
Jews may attend to burial — second day treated like a weekday for the dead
↓
This rule applies even on two days of Rosh HaShana (Rav Ashi)
↓
Mar Zutra
Jews bury on 2nd day only if burial was already delayed; fresh death — still uncertain
Rav Ashi
Even a fresh death — do not delay; Sages equated 2nd day with weekday for the dead
Ravina's caveat
In era of ḥabarei: adjust practice to avoid Persian accusations

🐥 Chick Hatched on a Festival — Permitted or Forbidden?

Rav
A chick that hatched on a Festival is prohibited. The egg was muktze on Festival eve — the chick is born from something forbidden, so it is forbidden too.
Shmuel / R. Yoḥanan
A chick hatched on a Festival is permitted. Just as a calf born on a Festival may be slaughtered, the same logic applies to the chick.
Rav Kahana & Rav Asi
Rav, what distinguishes a chick from a calf born on a Festival — you permit the calf! Why not the chick?
Rav
A calf inside its mother is already prepared — its mother was available for slaughter before the Festival. But a chick's egg was itself muktze. The chick is born from muktze and shares that status.
Core Distinction Calf: prepared through its mother who was fit before the Festival. Chick: egg itself was muktze on Festival eve — readiness (hakhana) was never established.

🐄🐣🤕 Calf vs. Chick vs. Tereifa's Calf — Muktze Analysis

Calf Born on Festival

  • Mother fit for slaughter before Festival
  • Calf 'prepared' through its mother
  • All agree: Permitted
  • May be slaughtered and eaten on the Festival

Chick Hatched on Festival

  • Egg was muktze on Festival eve
  • No pre-Festival readiness established
  • Rav: Prohibited (muktze via egg)
  • Shmuel: Permitted (same logic as calf)

Tereifa Mother's Calf

  • Mother cannot be eaten (terminally ill)
  • Calf not prepared for human consumption
  • But: prepared to be fed to dogs
  • Rabba: Not fully muktze — still valid for Festival

🏛️ The Ḥabarei — Persecution Reshaping Practice

חַבְרַאי — כהני האש הפרסיים שהאשימו את היהודים שעובדים ביום טוב
Historical Context Ravina warns: In the era of the ḥabarei — Persian Zoroastrian fire-priests — Jews who buried their dead on the second Festival day were falsely accused of performing forbidden labor. Though the halakha permits burial by Jews on the second day, Ravina ruled that in such dangerous times the practice must be adjusted.

This is a striking Talmudic precedent: external political threat modifies communal observance without altering the underlying law. The ruling stands — but survival overrides its application.

🥚 Rav Huna's Rule: An Egg Is Fully Formed Upon Emergence

Rav Huna citing Rav: 'An egg is fully formed upon its emergence' — it attains its complete legal status only when fully laid.
↓
First interpretation rejected: Does Rav mean the egg has no status until completely out? But the mishna already implies eggs inside the mother are irrelevant — this teaches us nothing new.
↓
Second interpretation rejected: Does Rav prohibit partially emerged eggs? The baraita's ruling on this is already covered by the mishna — Rav would not merely repeat it.
↓
Final interpretation: An egg laid on a Festival is permitted only if the entire egg emerged on a weekday — partial emergence before Shabbat/Festival is insufficient to permit it.
↓
Additional meaning: 'Fully formed upon emergence' also means the egg can be incubated to hatch chicks — it is a complete, functional egg in every legal sense only after full emergence.
