🧂 Can Salt & Water Be Nullified in Dough?

Rabbi Abba
The small amount of water and salt a woman borrows should be nullified in the dough — just as wheat from one person can be nullified in barley from another!
Sages of Eretz Yisrael
They laughed at him! Wheat mingled with barley remains distinct as a separate species. But salt dissolves into the dough — it cannot be recovered!
Rabbi Oshaya
They did well to laugh. The cases are dissimilar — when different species mix, the minority remains identifiable. When water and salt merge into dough, they are fully absorbed.
Rav Safra
Moses! But Rabbi Chiyya taught: pebbles mixed into wheat — the owner must be compensated. This proves money cannot be nullified even when mixed!
Abaye
You compare ritual prohibitions to monetary law? Prohibited items can be nullified — but one's money belongs to someone and cannot simply be nullified away!
Final Resolutions (Amud B) Abaye: Rabbinic decree — lest women habitually make dough in partnership. Rava: Spices are added for taste, and taste is never nullified.

🏺 Borrowed Vessel — When Was the Loan Made?

Borrowed Erev Yom Tov

  • Follows the borrower's feet
  • Even if physically received only on Yom Tov itself
  • Rabbi Yochanan: date of acquisition determines limit
  • Vessel's Shabbat rest is established at borrower's location
  • The mishna itself teaches this ruling

Borrowed on Yom Tov Itself

  • Follows the lender's feet
  • Special case: borrower is a regular customer
  • Lender mentally pre-designated it before Yom Tov
  • Vessel's limit remains at lender's location
  • Not obvious — the mishna must state this

🐂 The Fattener's Ox

שׁוֹר שֶׁל מַפְטֵם — כְּרַגְלֵי הַלּוֹקֵחַ
שמואל — Shmuel's Ruling An ox belonging to a fattener (one who fattens oxen for market) is considered as the feet of all people — specifically, it follows the feet of whoever acquires it on the Festival, even if the buyer travels from another city.

Since the fattener's ox is perpetually intended for sale, it has no fixed owner and therefore adopts the Shabbat/Yom Tov limit of its actual buyer.

💰 Nullification: Ritual Law vs. Monetary Claims

Rav Safra
Rabbi Chiyya taught: if someone removes pebbles from a granary and they become mixed with wheat, the owner must compensate. Money with claimants cannot be nullified!
Abaye
Distinguish! Money with claimants (the granary owner knows the pebbles are his) cannot be nullified — but ownerless money might be different.
Rav Safra
Rav Chisda: forbidden carcass meat is not nullified in slaughtered meat. Yet Rabbi Yochanan ben Nuri says ownerless prohibited objects CAN be nullified in ritual law!
Abaye
You cannot compare ritual prohibitions (issur) to monetary law (mammon)! Forbidden substances can be nullified — but money belongs to a person and cannot simply disappear!
Core Principle Bitul (nullification) is a tool of ritual law — prohibited substances in a mixture may lose their status. But monetary claims persist regardless of physical mixing. One's property cannot be nullified away.
