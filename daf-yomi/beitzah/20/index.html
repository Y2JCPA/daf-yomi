🙌 The Great Semicha Dispute

Beit Shammai
One may not place hands (semicha) on a sacrifice on Yom Tov — this constitutes prohibited labor, even in the Temple courtyard.
Beit Hillel
One may place hands on a sacrifice on Yom Tov — semicha is integral to the sacrificial service and is therefore permitted.
Hillel the Elder
I declared my animal ownerless (hefker) before Yom Tov — then brought it as a burnt-offering with semicha unchallenged, since it had no formal 'owner.'
Beit Shammai
When Hillel did this, we gained the upper hand and sought to establish halakha in accordance with our view once and for all.
Beit Hillel
A disciple brought all the finest sheep of Kedar to the Temple courtyard — the people placed hands freely, and Beit Hillel prevailed.
Historical Significance The semicha dispute was among the most persistent disagreements between the two schools — ultimately resolved in favor of Beit Hillel. Semicha on Yom Tov is permitted.

🐑 Hillel's Cunning Stratagem — Step by Step

The Problem: Hillel the Elder wished to perform semicha on his burnt-offering on Yom Tov — but Beit Shammai strictly forbade this as a prohibited labor.
↓
Hillel's Move: He designated his animal as hefker (ownerless) before the Festival, so technically there was no 'owner' performing semicha — only an anonymous worshipper.
↓
Beit Shammai's Reaction: When they learned of Hillel's workaround, they gained the upper hand and attempted to legislate the halakha in their favor.
↓
The Counter-Move: A Beit Hillel disciple brought all the finest sheep of Kedar (כבשי קידר) to the Temple courtyard and invited anyone to come place hands freely.
↓
Result: Beit Hillel regained the upper hand — and halakha was decided in their favor: semicha is permitted on Yom Tov.
↓
Abaye's Moral: A Torah scholar whose colleague says something insulting should not respond with more than was said — escalation only worsens the conflict, as this episode illustrates.

🔥 May Olat Re'iyah Be Sacrificed on Yom Tov?

Beit Hillel
Just as slaughter for a common person is permitted on Yom Tov, slaughter for the Most High should also be permitted — a fortiori!
Beit Shammai
But vow-offerings and gift-offerings may not be slaughtered on Yom Tov — why should appearance-offerings be any different?
Beit Hillel
Vow-offerings have no fixed time — but the olat re'iyah has a fixed time: the Festival itself. Fixed-time offerings may be sacrificed on Yom Tov.
Beit Shammai
The verse says: 'Only that which every soul must eat, that alone may be done for you' (Exodus 12:16) — offerings are for God, not for you, and are excluded.
Abba Shaul / Beit Hillel
Just as your stove is closed on Shabbat yet your Master's stove (the altar) is open — so too on Yom Tov, God's service continues unimpeded.
Abba Shaul's Variant Abba Shaul presents a stronger Beit Hillel position: even vow-offerings and gift-offerings MAY be brought on Yom Tov — and the two formulations reflect a deeper dispute about the nature of the prohibition.

📋 Categories of Offerings on Yom Tov

Permitted — All Agree

  • Shalmei re'iyah (appearance peace-offerings)
  • Shalmei simcha (Festival joy peace-offerings)
  • Two Shavuot loaves and their accompanying lambs
  • Daily communal tamid burnt-offerings

Disputed Between Schools

  • Olat re'iyah (appearance burnt-offerings) — Beit Hillel permits; Beit Shammai forbids
  • Semicha on any offering — Beit Hillel permits; Beit Shammai forbids
  • Nedarim/nedavot on Yom Tov — forbidden per Beit Shammai; permitted per Abba Shaul's Beit Hillel

Forbidden — All Agree

  • Nedarim (vow-offerings) — no fixed time, no Festival connection
  • Nedavot (gift-offerings) — no fixed time, purely voluntary
  • Private offerings unrelated to the Festival's unique obligations

📋 Beitzah 20 — Summary Map

Core Dispute:
Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel — semicha on Yom Tov, and which offerings may be sacrificed
↓
Beit Shammai:
Semicha = prohibited labor; olat re'iyah forbidden; verse limits Yom Tov acts to food-for-humans only
Beit Hillel:
Semicha permitted; fixed-time offerings may be brought; God's service takes precedence
↓
Resolution: Halakha follows Beit Hillel — semicha is permitted; fixed-time offerings may be sacrificed on Yom Tov
↓
Further Dispute — Rava vs. Rabba bar Rav Huna:
If vow-offerings were improperly slaughtered on Yom Tov — is the prohibition rabbinic or Torah-level? This determines whether blood is sprinkled when meat becomes impure.
