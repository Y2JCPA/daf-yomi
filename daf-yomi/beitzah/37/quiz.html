Beitzah 37 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did the Sages prohibit acts like betrothing and judging on Shabbat and Festivals?
A. Because they involve physical labor forbidden on Shabbat
B. As a decree lest one come to write down the proceedings
C. Because they are disrespectful to the sanctity of the day
D. Because they involve monetary transactions prohibited on Shabbat
Correct: B — The Gemara (37a) explains: the reason is a rabbinic decree (shevut) lest one come to write down the details of the legal proceedings — a prohibited labor on Shabbat.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the mishna, what is the ONE halakhic difference between Shabbat and a Festival?
A. Carrying in the public domain is permitted on a Festival but not on Shabbat
B. Torah study obligations are greater on Shabbat than on a Festival
C. Work involving the preparation of food (אוכל נפש) is permitted on a Festival but not Shabbat
D. Prayers are longer and more numerous on Shabbat than on a Festival
Correct: C — The mishna states explicitly: there is no difference between a Festival and Shabbat except for work involving food preparation (אוכל נפש), which is permitted on a Festival but remains prohibited on Shabbat.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rav Pappa resolve the contradiction between two mishnayot regarding whether lowering produce is a Festival-only permission?
A. He says one mishna discusses Shabbat and the other a Festival
B. He distinguishes between the opinions of Rabbi Eliezer and Rabbi Yehoshua
C. He attributes one mishna to Beit Shammai and the other to Beit Hillel
D. He says the contradiction is unresolvable and leaves it standing
Correct: C — Rav Pappa rejects Rav Yosef's attribution to R. Eliezer vs. R. Yehoshua and argues instead: one mishna follows Beit Shammai, who prohibit strenuous non-food acts on Festivals, and the other follows Beit Hillel, who permit them.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If a person borrows a vessel from another on the eve of a Festival (before it begins), whose travel limits govern the vessel during the Festival?
A. The lender's feet, since he is the legal owner of the vessel
B. The borrower's feet, since the Festival began while the vessel was in his possession
C. Both equally — the vessel may only travel within the stricter of the two limits
D. Neither — borrowed vessels have no teḥum restrictions on Festivals
Correct: B — The mishna rules: if borrowed before the Festival, the vessel follows the borrower's feet — when the Festival began (and the object 'established its place of rest'), it was in the borrower's domain.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the breirah dispute about two partners who bought a barrel before a Festival, what position does the Gemara ultimately attribute to Rabbi Yoḥanan?
A. There is no breirah (retroactive designation) under any circumstances
B. There is breirah in matters of Torah law but not rabbinic law
C. There is breirah at least in matters of rabbinic law (such as eiruv teḥumin)
D. The question of breirah was never addressed by Rabbi Yoḥanan
Correct: C — The Gemara reverses the initial attribution: Rabbi Yoḥanan holds there IS breirah (יש ברירה), at least in matters of rabbinic law like eiruv teḥumin. Whether he accepts breirah in Torah-level matters is challenged from the case of inheriting brothers.
