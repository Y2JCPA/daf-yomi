📋 Three Categories of Shevut

Purely Optional

  • No mitzva involved whatsoever
  • E.g., judging, betrothing, ḥalitza
  • Prohibited: decree lest one write
  • Betrothal OK if he already has wife/children

Mitzva Aspect

  • Has some mitzva dimension
  • E.g., separating terumot and tithes
  • Needed only for pre-Festival untithed produce
  • Also prohibited as shevut

Full-Fledged Mitzva

  • Constitutes a complete mitzva obligation
  • E.g., consecrating, valuations, ḥerem
  • Still prohibited on Shabbat and Festival
  • Rabbi Yitzḥak: all three are shevut

⚖️ Resolving the Contradiction About Lowering Produce

Rav Yosef
The two mishnayot reflect a dispute between Rabbi Eliezer (who prohibits strenuous acts to prevent monetary loss on a Festival) and Rabbi Yehoshua (who permits them via artifice, e.g., raising one animal then changing his mind).
Abaye
How do you know that is correct? Perhaps Rabbi Eliezer's rule applies only in that specific cow-and-calf case, or Rabbi Yehoshua's permission applies only where artifice is possible. You cannot prove a general principle from one case.
Rav Pappa
Reject Rav Yosef's answer entirely! The contradiction is between Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel — Beit Shammai prohibit non-food strenuous acts on Festivals; Beit Hillel permit them. Each mishna follows one view.
Gemara
Rav Pappa's answer is also challenged: perhaps Beit Shammai limited their prohibition only to carrying in the public domain, not to strenuous physical acts like lowering produce from a roof.
Status Both resolutions stand as possibilities — the Gemara does not conclusively resolve which tannaitic dispute underlies the contradiction about lowering produce on Festivals.

🦶 Feet of the Owner — The Core Rules

Basic Rule: Animals and vessels on Shabbat/Festivals are governed by the travel limits (teḥumin) of their owner — they may only travel as far as the owner may travel
↓
If an owner delivers his animal to his son or shepherd before a Festival, it still follows the owner's feet — the transfer does not shift the Shabbat resting place
↓
Exception — One Shepherd Town: If there is only one shepherd in town, the animal follows the shepherd's feet, because the owner implicitly relies entirely on him
↓
Jointly owned vessels: If a vessel is designated for one brother's exclusive use, it follows his feet alone, not the other brothers'
↓
Borrowed before Festival: Follows the borrower's feet — the Festival began while the vessel was in his possession
↓
Borrowed on the Festival itself: Follows the lender's feet — the Festival began while still with the lender. Ruling: Rabbi Yoḥanan — halakha follows Rabbi Dosa: animals given to a shepherd follow the shepherd's feet

🔄 Breirah — Does Retroactive Designation Work?

Rav
Partners who bought a barrel: each may carry his portion to his own place — breirah works for the barrel. But for a live animal: the limits absorb from each other (a live animal cannot be split before Festival ends), so neither may carry it beyond the stricter limit.
Shmuel
Both barrel and animal: each partner may carry his portion to his own place. Breirah works in both cases — the division retroactively designates each person's portion from the start of the Festival.
Rabbi Hoshaya
There IS retroactive designation (יש ברירה) — each partner may take his portion to his own place for both barrel and animal.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
The Gemara reverses their positions: Rabbi Hoshaya actually holds there is no breirah; Rabbi Yoḥanan holds there is breirah — at least in matters of rabbinic law such as eiruv teḥumin.
Key Complication Rabbi Yoḥanan's acceptance of breirah is challenged from the case of inheriting brothers (Torah law) and from eiruv teḥumin — showing breirah is disputed and may differ between Torah-level and rabbinic-level contexts.
