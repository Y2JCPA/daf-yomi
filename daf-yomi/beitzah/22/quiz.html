5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, why is insulating hot food on a Festival for Shabbat forbidden even when an eiruv tavshilin was properly established?
A. Insulation is not considered ochel nefesh (food preparation) and is not covered by the eiruv
B. The eiruv must be re-established separately on each day of the Festival
C. Insulation is a Torah-level prohibition on all Festivals regardless of eiruv
D. Only a rabbinic court may grant permission for insulation on a Festival
Correct: A — Rava explains that hatmanah (insulation) does not fall under the category of ochel nefesh — preparing food for human need — so the eiruv tavshilin, which permits food-related labor, does not cover it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
The baraita permits extinguishing a flame on Yom Tov in one specific circumstance. What is it?
A. To conserve oil or wax for future use
B. So that the house or a cooking pot not become filled with smoke
C. When a Torah scholar requests it for his comfort
D. Only when the flame was lit by a gentile before the Festival
Correct: B — The baraita rules that extinguishing to prevent a house or pot from becoming smoky is a genuine household need and is therefore permitted — as opposed to extinguishing merely to save the oil or wood.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rav Ashi raised a second objection against Ameimar applying eye ointment via a gentile on Shabbat. What was the problem with Ameimar's own behavior?
A. Ameimar recited a blessing, which constitutes active participation on Shabbat
B. Ameimar paid the gentile, making it a prohibited business transaction
C. Ameimar opened and closed his eye, thereby assisting the gentile in the prohibited act
D. Ameimar held the ointment container, completing the forbidden labor himself
Correct: C — Rav Ashi pointed out that even when a gentile performs the labor, the Jewish person must not assist. By opening and closing his eye to allow the ointment in, Ameimar became an active participant, which is forbidden.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Ameimar permitted eye treatment on the second day of Rosh HaShana, following the Sages of Neharde'a. What is the foundation of this leniency?
A. The second day of Rosh HaShana is entirely rabbinic in origin and therefore fully lenient
B. Medical treatment is always permitted on any second day of Yom Tov by Torah law
C. The Sages of Neharde'a hold the two days of Rosh HaShana are like any two-day Festival, not a unique single sanctity
D. Eye pain is categorized as pikuach nefesh, permitting all labor on any Yom Tov day
Correct: C — Ameimar and the Sages of Neharde'a dispute the view that the two days of Rosh HaShana form one continuous sanctity. They treat them as two separate days — like regular two-day Diaspora Yom Tov — allowing the standard second-day leniencies.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara question the baraita's teaching that one may not bake thick bread specifically on Passover?
A. Thick bread is forbidden year-round due to waste of flour
B. The same concern about unnecessary exertion applies to all Festivals, not just Passover
C. Passover is more lenient than other Festivals regarding baking
D. The baraita is only addressing priests baking shewbread in the Temple
Correct: B — The Gemara notes that the prohibition on thick bread is rooted in unnecessary exertion (tircha yeteira), which is equally applicable on all Festivals. Singling out Passover requires explanation — the answer is that Passover adds the concern of leavening.
