🕯️ Permissions, Prohibitions & Personal Need on Yom Tov

Rava
Mishna = eiruv was made, yet insulation is still forbidden on Yom Tov for Shabbat. Reason: insulating is not ochel nefesh and is not covered by the eiruv.
Abaye
Mishna = partial eiruv: he established the eiruv for cooking but not for insulation. Each permitted act requires its own intent when the eiruv is established.
Ameimar
I permit applying medicinal eye ointment on Shabbat via a gentile — and even on the second day of Rosh HaShana, following the Sages of Neharde'a.
Rav Ashi
But Rava ruled that a person in pain may apply ointment themselves! And you must not assist the gentile by opening and closing your eye — that makes you a direct participant.
Core Theme Daf 22 traces the outer boundaries of permitted Yom Tov labor — from eiruv tavshilin to extinguishing flames, medical treatment, and Rabban Gamliel's contested rulings.

🫙 What Case Does the Mishna Describe?

Rava
An eiruv was made, yet insulation (hatmanah) on Yom Tov for Shabbat remains forbidden. Insulation is not a form of cooking; the eiruv does not cover it.
Abaye (objects)
Ḥananya cites Beit Shammai: one may not bake on Yom Tov for Shabbat unless an eiruv tavshilin was made. This supports separate establishment for each act — against Rava.
Abaye (resolves)
Mishna = split intent: he made an eiruv for this (cooking/baking) but not for that (insulation). Each category needs explicit intent at the time of establishment.
Narrator
If one forgot eiruv tavshilin entirely: others may bake one loaf, cook one dish, and kindle one lamp for him — but no more.
Practical Rule The eiruv tavshilin must be established before Yom Tov and must cover each category of Shabbat preparation intended.

📅 Two Days of Rosh HaShana: One Sanctity or Two?

Rav Ashi's Position

  • Two days of Rosh HaShana = one continuous sanctity
  • Egg laid on Day 1 is forbidden on Day 2
  • Leniency for burial of the dead is a rabbinic exception only
  • Rosh HaShana stringency differs from regular two-day Yom Tov
  • Therefore: medical treatment on Day 2 requires stricter standard

Ameimar — Sages of Neharde'a

  • Two days of Rosh HaShana = two separate days
  • No practical halakhic difference from regular Diaspora two-day Yom Tov
  • Day 2 leniencies apply just as in any second day of Yom Tov
  • Eye ointment via gentile is permitted on Day 2 for pain
  • Follows the tradition of the Neharde'a academies

👁️ Medicinal Eye Ointment on Shabbat

כָּחַל — to paint or apply ointment to the eye; כְּחִילָה — the act of applying eye medicine
Ameimar's Ruling & Rav Ashi's Challenge Ameimar permitted applying medicinal eye ointment (kechal) on Shabbat via a gentile. He even ruled it permitted on the second day of Rosh HaShana for eye pain.

Rav Ashi's first challenge: Didn't Rava say a person in pain may apply it themselves? Why require a gentile?
Ameimar's implicit position: The illness was not dangerous enough to permit direct self-application; a gentile was required as a safeguard.

Rav Ashi's second challenge: Ameimar himself opened and closed his eye while the gentile applied the ointment — thereby assisting the gentile. This makes the patient a direct participant in the prohibited labor, which is forbidden even when a gentile does the primary act.
