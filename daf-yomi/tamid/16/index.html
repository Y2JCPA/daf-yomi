⚖️ The Core Dispute: Partial Permitting Factors and Piggul

Rabbi Meir
If the priest burns the handful with piggul intent but the frankincense in silence, the meal offering is piggul. Piggul intent during any permitting act — even only part — renders the offering invalid.
Rabbis
The meal offering is not piggul unless he had piggul intent during both the burning of the handful and the burning of the frankincense. The entire permitting factor requires piggul intent.
Exception — All Agree For a sinner's meal offering and a sota's jealousy offering (which contain no frankincense), even the Rabbis agree: piggul intent during the handful alone renders the offering piggul — the handful is the sole permitting factor.

🗣️ Rav vs. Shmuel: When Exactly Does the Dispute Apply?

Rav
The dispute applies only when the handful was placed in silence and the frankincense with piggul intent. But if the handful was placed first with piggul intent and frankincense in silence — all agree it is piggul, since whoever performs the first rite with intent performs the whole service accordingly.
Shmuel
The dispute applies even when the handful was placed with piggul intent first and the frankincense in silence afterward. Rabbi Meir says piggul; Rabbis say not. There is no prior-act exception. The Yom Kippur baraita supports this view.
The Debate Within the Debate Rava, Rabbi Ḥanina, and Rav Ashi each attempt to reinterpret the Yom Kippur baraita to align with Rav's view — using strategies like 'two priests,' 'alternating intent and silence,' and 'explicit intent for first and third sets only.'

🩸 Reconciling the Three Blood-Presentation Counts on Yom Kippur

43 presentations (Baraita A): The standard baseline count — bull and goat blood inside the Holy of Holies, sprinkled on the Curtain, and on the inner altar, across three sets
↓
47 presentations (Baraita B): A different tally — adds four additional sprinklings by counting certain applications differently; the Gemara says this is not difficult to reconcile with 43
↓
48 presentations (Baraita C): According to Rabbi Eliezer's opinion, who adds one more presentation to the 47 count — reconciled as a minority view
↓
Rabba's solution for the piggul problem: Four bulls and four goats were used — the first blood spilled, requiring replacement animals, meaning later presentations were done with technically disqualified but still operative blood
↓
Rava's solution: Even with one bull/goat, presentations made with disqualified blood still combine with valid presentations for purposes of rendering the offering piggul

🫙 Conveying the Handful: Two Amoraic Views

Reish Lakish

  • Conveying the handful to the altar is only part of the permitting factors
  • Analogous to burning only the handful without the frankincense
  • Piggul intent during conveying alone does NOT render the offering piggul
  • Requires intent during burning of both handful and frankincense to achieve full piggul

Rabbi Yoḥanan

  • Conveying the handful is treated like removing the handful from the meal offering
  • Just as piggul intent during removing alone suffices (it is the sole act at that stage)...
  • ...conveying the handful with intent — even for only one permitting factor — creates piggul
  • The handful is the primary permitting item; its conveyance carries full piggul significance
