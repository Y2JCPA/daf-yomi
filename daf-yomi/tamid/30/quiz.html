Tamid 30 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, where must one finish writing a Torah scroll?
A. Anywhere in the column
B. At the end of any line
C. At the very end of the column
D. At the beginning of a new sheet
Correct: C — Rav Ashi rules that one must finish the Torah scroll specifically at the very end of the column — not merely anywhere within it as Rav permits.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Shimon, how did Moses write the final verses of the Torah about his own death?
A. He dictated them to Joshua to write
B. He wrote them with tears instead of ink
C. He left blank spaces for Joshua to complete
D. He was unaware of the content he was writing
Correct: B — Rabbi Shimon holds that God dictated and Moses wrote all verses including his death — but Moses wrote those final verses with tears flowing onto the parchment, not with ordinary ink.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the minimum number of columns permitted on a Torah scroll sheet (other than the very last sheet)?
A. One
B. Two
C. Three
D. Four
Correct: C — The Sages rule that sheets in the beginning and middle of the scroll must contain at least three columns. Only the final sheet may have even a single verse or letter.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If a scribe omits God's name and writes subsequent words, what does Rabbi Yosei rule?
A. Begin the entire Torah scroll over from the start
B. Leave the text as-is and add a marginal note
C. Scrape off subsequent words, suspend them above the line, and write God's name in the gap
D. Write God's name in the outer margin beside the error
Correct: C — Rabbi Yosei: scrape off the words written after the omission, suspend them above the line, write God's name in the cleared space, then the suspended words remain above as a correction.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rav teach about one who purchases a Torah scroll in the marketplace?
A. He has fulfilled the mitzva of writing a Torah scroll completely
B. He is like one who snatches a mitzva — proper fulfillment is to write it oneself
C. It is halakhically forbidden to purchase a pre-written Torah scroll
D. He must have a scribe recheck and resign the entire scroll
Correct: B — Rav teaches that buying a ready-made Torah scroll is like snatching a mitzva hastily from the marketplace — the proper and full fulfillment of the mitzva is to write the scroll oneself or actively commission its writing as a personal act.
