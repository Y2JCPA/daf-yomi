⚖️ Tereifa & the Limits of A Fortiori Reasoning

טְרֵפָה — קַל וָחֹמֶר
Two Sugyot The daf opens in an extended debate: why does the Torah need a verse to exclude a tereifa (fatally injured animal) from sacrifice — can't we derive it from a kal va'ḥomer (a fortiori)? Rav Ashi and Rav Aḥa Sava trade refutations until every proposed inference collapses, and the Gemara then explains why three separate verses (from Leviticus and Ezekiel) are each necessary. The daf pivots to the Mishna listing persons who disqualify a meal offering's handful, and to Rav's ruling — in the name of ben Beteira — that an improperly removed handful may be returned and redone, with Rav Naḥman clarifying precisely when the rite becomes irrevocably complete.

Rav Ashi vs. Rav Aḥa Sava: Every A Fortiori Collapses

Rav Ashi
The a fortiori is refutable from the start. If you derive tereifa's disqualification from a blemished animal, note what is unique about it: the Torah rendered those who sacrifice like that which is sacrificed — a blemished priest also cannot perform the service. Tereifa has no such parallel.
Rav Aḥa Sava
An animal born by caesarean section proves otherwise! The Torah did NOT render those who sacrifice like what is sacrificed for it — yet it is still disqualified from the altar.
Rav Ashi
What is notable about a caesarean-born animal? It is not sanctified as a firstborn, whereas a firstborn tereifa IS sanctified. The two sources cannot combine.
Rav Aḥa Sava
A blemished animal IS sanctified as a firstborn — so that feature does not distinguish them. Their common element: both are permitted for consumption yet forbidden for the Most High. Derive tereifa from the common element!
Rav Ashi
What is notable about their common element? Their prohibition was never permitted — they were always unfit. But a tereifa's prohibition WAS temporarily permitted: the pinching of a bird sin offering renders it fit for priests despite being a tereifa. The inference collapses again.
Conclusion (both amudim) The inference reverts each time — the aspect of this case is not like that case and vice versa. A scriptural verse is therefore indispensable.

Three Verses — Each Covering a Different Scenario

Problem: Multiple verses seem to exclude tereifa from sacrifice — are they all needed, or is one sufficient?
↓
"Of the Herd" (Lev. 1:3)
Excludes tereifa that was already tereifa when sanctified — owner consecrated it in its unfit state
"Well-watered Pastures" (Ezek. 45:15)
Excludes tereifa that never ate from well-watered pastures — i.e., born tereifa and unfit from birth
"Under the Rod" (Lev. 27:32)
Excludes tereifa rendered tereifa after sanctification — owner sanctified it healthy, then it became unfit
↓
Each verse is indispensable — without all three, one could limit the exclusion to only the scenario covered by the other two

Ben Beteira's Ruling vs. Standard Position

Standard View

  • Non-priest or unfit priest removes handful → offering is permanently disqualified
  • Left-hand removal → no remedy
  • Once an unfit rite is performed, the offering cannot be restored
  • Sanctification in a service vessel makes disqualification irrevocable

Ben Beteira's View

  • Left-hand removal: return the handful to the meal offering vessel
  • Remove again with the right hand — offering is restored
  • Rav extends: applies also to handful removed by a non-priest
  • Debate: does this apply even after the handful is sanctified in a service vessel?
  • Rav Naḥman: only if not yet sanctified in the vessel

Rav Naḥman: When Is the Handful Rite Complete?

Background: An unfit person (non-priest, acute mourner, etc.) removes the handful from a meal offering.
↓
Question: Is this considered a performed sacrificial rite — and if so, can ben Beteira still permit returning the handful?
↓
Rav Naḥman's ruling: Yes — removal of the handful IS considered performance of a sacrificial rite…
↓
…BUT the rite is not complete until the handful is placed inside a service vessel (sanctification, קִדּוּשׁ כְּלִי).
↓
If sanctification in the vessel has NOT yet occurred: ben Beteira permits returning the handful — the rite is incomplete and the offering can be saved.
↓
If the unfit person already placed the handful in the service vessel: the rite is irrevocably complete — the offering is disqualified.

📋 Tamid 6 — Summary Map

Can tereifa be excluded from sacrifice by a fortiori alone?
↓
Rav Ashi: No — any proposed source (blemished animal, caesarean) has a unique feature that prevents the inference
Rav Aḥa Sava: Challenges each refutation with a counter-example, forcing a common-element argument — which also collapses
↓
Three verses required — "of the herd," "well-watered pastures," "under the rod" — each covering a distinct tereifa scenario

Mishna: Unfit persons disqualify the handful but ben Beteira permits return & redo — valid only before sanctification in the service vessel (Rav Naḥman)
