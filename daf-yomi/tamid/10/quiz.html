QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Torah write BOTH 'upon the blood of the guilt offering' (Lev. 14:17) AND 'upon the place of the blood' (Lev. 14:28) regarding leper purification?
A. They apply to completely different body parts — one to the thumb, one to the big toe
B. Each covers a different scenario: one when blood is still present, one after it has been wiped away
C. The second verse is a stylistic repetition with no independent legal significance
D. One applies to a wealthy leper's right side and the other to a poor leper's right side
Correct: B — With only the first verse, one might think oil is placed only while blood is still present. With only the second, only after blood is removed. Both together teach that oil is placed on the right thumb and big toe regardless of whether blood remains.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava, what is the PRIMARY halakhic purpose of the seemingly redundant 'right foot' phrase in the wealthy leper passage (Lev. 14:17)?
A. To confirm that the oil must be placed on the right side rather than the left side of the leper
B. To teach a verbal analogy (gezerah shavah) connecting 'foot' here to 'foot' in the yibum passage
C. To distinguish the rite of a wealthy leper from that of a poor leper in all details
D. To establish that only a Kohen Gadol, not an ordinary priest, performs leper purification
Correct: B — Rava holds the phrase isn't needed for the leper's own law — the location is already obvious. So it serves as a gezerah shavah, connecting 'foot' here to 'foot' in the yibum passage to derive laws about that rite.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rava clarifies Reish Lakish's principle about the right hand. What is Rava's ruling?
A. Only when BOTH 'finger' AND 'priesthood' appear together in a verse is the right hand required
B. Either 'finger' OR 'priesthood' alone in a verse is sufficient to require the right hand
C. The right hand rule applies only inside the Sanctuary, not in the Temple courtyard
D. The right hand rule applies only to rites involving animal blood, not meal offerings
Correct: B — Rava rules that either condition alone — mention of finger OR priesthood — suffices to mandate the right hand for atonement-critical rites. Rabbi Shimon disagrees, requiring both conditions together.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Abaye challenges the right-hand rule with the carrying of limbs to the altar ramp. Why does the Gemara reject this challenge?
A. Because Levites, not priests, actually carry the limbs — so priesthood is not involved
B. Because 'priesthood' is written for this rite but it is not indispensable for atonement (not m'akev)
C. Because the verse explicitly says 'either hand' for the carrying of limbs
D. Because the altar ramp is outside the Temple and the rule only applies inside
Correct: B — The right-hand rule applies only to rites that are indispensable for atonement (m'akev). Carrying limbs to the ramp, while priestly, does not preclude atonement if done with the left hand — so the restriction does not apply.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yehuda son of Rabbi Ḥiyya derive that the removal of the handful (kometz) from a meal offering must be performed with the right hand?
A. From the phrase 'with his finger' explicitly written in the kometz passage itself
B. From the Torah's comparison of the meal offering to a sin offering and a guilt offering, which require the right hand
C. From a verbal analogy with the leper passage based on the word 'hand'
D. From the general rule that all Temple rites default to the right hand unless specified
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda son of Rabbi Ḥiyya derives it from the verse that compares the meal offering to a sin offering and guilt offering. Just as those offerings require the right hand, so does the removal of the handful from a meal offering.
