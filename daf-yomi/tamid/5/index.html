🌾 Omer Offering: Disqualified or Valid?

Rav
An omer meal offering from which a handful was removed not for its own sake is disqualified entirely.
Reish Lakish
It is valid — the handful may be burned on the altar, but only after a second, proper omer is brought for its own sake.
Rava
Improper intent invalidates only when the one performing the service is fit for Temple service, performing the right service, in the right vessel, for the right offering.
Core Question Does improper intent (שלא לשמה) in removing the omer's handful invalidate entirely, or merely defer the burning until a valid omer is brought?

⏳ The 'Time Not Yet Arrived' Principle

Problem: Burning the handful before new crop is permitted looks like offering 'whose time has not arrived' (טרם זמנו)
↓
Rav Adda bar Ahava: An offering is not 'time not arrived' if it is to be brought on that very same day
↓
✓ Accepted
Since new crop is permitted on the same day as the omer, the handful is fit for sacrifice from the outset — no 'time not arrived' issue
✗ Objection (Rav Adda b. Rav Yitzchak)
The baraita says meal offerings have a 'general prohibition that was permitted' — but the omer handful does not fit this category cleanly
↓
Answer: The handful was initially fit for sacrifice — it was never under a 'prohibition permitted'; the permission preceded any restriction

🩺 Leper Purification: Rav Sheshet vs. Rav Pappa

Rav Sheshet
Objection: If 'same day' nullifies 'time not arrived,' why must the priest repeat the oil placement if he placed it before the blood from the guilt offering?
Rav Pappa
Leper laws are uniquely ordered — the verse states זֹאת תִּהְיֶה תּוֹרַת הַמְּצֹרָע (Lev. 14:2). The word תִּהְיֶה ('shall be') mandates the order remain fixed — as it was prescribed, so it must remain.
Rav Pappa (self-objection)
But then: why does slaughtering the leper's sin offering before the guilt offering not invalidate the sin offering, if order is fixed?
Rav Pappa (retreat)
Rav Pappa withdraws and defers to Reish Lakish's true reasoning: the illumination of the eastern horizon itself permits the new crop — not the omer's sacrifice.
Key Verse זֹאת תִּהְיֶה תּוֹרַת הַמְּצֹרָע — 'This shall be the law of the leper' (Leviticus 14:2). תִּהְיֶה signals eternal, fixed order in leper purification ritual.

🐦 Unique Halakhot: Birds vs. Meal Offerings

Birds Only (עופות)

  • Bring atonement for zav, zavah, yoledet who have not yet brought their offering
  • Slaughtered by melikah (pinching the nape) — unique to birds
  • The general prohibition on pinching a non-sacred bird was permitted when consecrated for the altar (sanctity creates the allowance)

Meal Offerings Only (מנחות)

  • Must be placed in a service vessel (כלי שרת)
  • Require waving (tenufah) and bringing toward the altar corner
  • Require oil, frankincense, and salt
  • The general prohibition on new crop (חדש) was permitted — only through the omer offering

🌅 Reish Lakish's True Reasoning — Inferred, Not Stated

עמוד השחר מתיר את החדש — 'The illumination of the eastern horizon permits the new crop' (cf. Menachot 68b)
The Real Basis Reish Lakish never stated explicitly that an omer handful taken שלא לשמה is valid. His position was inferred from a separate ruling: a meal offering from new crop brought before the omer is disqualified only if brought on the 14th or 15th of Nisan — but not on the 16th itself. This reveals his view: it is the illumination of the eastern horizon (עמוד השחר) on the 16th that permits new crop — not the omer's actual sacrifice. Therefore, once dawn breaks on the 16th, even a handful from a defective omer is inherently fit to be burned.
