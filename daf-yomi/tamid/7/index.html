🏺 Do Service Vessels Sanctify Without Intent?

Challenge
When the priest returns the handful to the meal offering vessel, it should become sanctified by the vessel — and therefore disqualified!
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Service vessels sanctify items only when placed there with specific intent to be sanctified. Since the priest returns the handful without that intent, it is not sanctified.
Counter-Dilemma
But Reish Lakish raised a dilemma: what if someone collects blood in a vessel not for the sake of that sprinkling — is it sanctified or not?
Rav Amram
Even if vessels sanctify without intent, you can return the handful without sanctification — e.g., by using a heaped bowl (levisa) where the handful lands above the rim, not inside the vessel.
Core Principle The dispute turns on whether a service vessel sanctifies automatically upon contact, or only when the item is placed there with deliberate intent (לשמה).

🥣 Solutions: Returning the Handful Without Sanctification

Problem: Returning the handful to the meal offering vessel may sanctify — and thus disqualify — it
↓
Solution 1: Levisa
Use a heaped bowl — grain mounded above the rim. The handful lands on the heap, not inside the vessel, so no sanctification occurs.
Solution 2: Broken Vessel
Return the handful using a vessel with a hole or crack — a vessel unfit for service cannot sanctify its contents.
↓
Objection: If the meal offering was heaped, how did the priest remove the handful in the first place? A furrow is created upon removal, making a hollow — now it is inside the vessel!
↓
Resolution: The handful was removed from a different vessel than the one to which it is returned — so no furrow exists in the heaped bowl.

👴 Avimi Visits His Own Student

Gemara
Did Avimi really learn in the study hall of Rav Ḥisda? But Rav Ḥisda himself said: I received many blows (kulfei) from Avimi because of this halakha — Avimi used to mock him for not knowing it!
Resolution
Avimi was indeed the teacher, not the student. But tractate Menaḥot was uprooted from him — he forgot it entirely — so he came before Rav Ḥisda to recover what he had taught him.
New Question
If Rav Ḥisda was Avimi's student, why does the Gemara say Avimi encountered him rather than calling him 'his student'?
Answer
Scripture teaches us not to shame a person by publicly identifying their teacher's name when the student has surpassed them — dignity in learning overrides precise attribution.
Human Moment A great scholar who forgot his learning humbled himself before his own student — a model of intellectual honesty and dedication to Torah recovery.

📐 Rav Naḥman Questions Avimi: Three-Priest Problem

Q1 — How to remove a handful? Avimi pointed to a vessel resting on the ground. Rav Naḥman objected: a vessel must be raised for sanctification!
↓
Q2 — How to sanctify meal offerings? Avimi again pointed to a vessel on the ground. Rav Naḥman pressed: but mustn't the vessel be lifted?
↓
Rav Naḥman's Conclusion: If raising the vessel is required for both sanctification and removal of the handful, you need three priests — one to hold the vessel aloft, one to sanctify, one to remove the handful!
↓
Avimi's Response: "And let it require even three priests!" There is no halakhic problem with requiring three priests for a single rite.
↓
Refutation from Mishna (12a): The mishna lists all rites of the meal offering and does not include 'raising the vessel' — proving no such requirement exists.

⚖️ Rava's Two Rulings on Vessels Resting on the Ground

Removal of Handful — VALID ✓

  • Rava rules: Obviously permitted
  • Proof: The frankincense bowls were removed from the Table of the Shewbread — and the Table stands on the ground!
  • The act of removal does not require the vessel to be elevated
  • Rava had no doubt on this point

Sanctification in Vessel on Ground — UNCLEAR ✗

  • Rava raises a dilemma: is sanctification valid?
  • Option A: Derive from meal offering — sanctification requires the vessel to be raised
  • Option B: Derive from blood collection — no elevation required for sanctification
  • Rava leaves this unresolved (תיקו)

🩸 Laws of Blood Sanctification — Full Measure Requirement

Source / DerivationRuleConsequence if Violated
Rav Taḥlifa bar Avimi citing a verseBlood must be collected as a full measure in a single vessel — not split across two vesselsBlood collected in halves is not sanctified and sprinkling is invalid
"And the priest shall dip" (Lev. 4:6)At the time of dipping, there must be a full measure of blood in the vessel sufficient for dippingIf less than a full measure remains, subsequent sprinklings are invalid
"In the blood"There must initially be a full measure present in the vessel when dipping beginsCannot rely on prior valid dips to excuse a depleted vessel
"Of the blood"The priest must sprinkle from the blood of this specific matter — not from residue on his finger from a previous sprinklingSprinkling from finger-residue of prior sprinklings is invalid (Rava)
Rabbi Zerika citing Rabbi ElazarEven for a sin offering, blood not collected as a full measure in one vessel is unsanctifiedApplies equally to sin offering blood — not just meal offering handfuls
