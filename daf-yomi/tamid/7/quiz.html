QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, under what condition do service vessels sanctify items placed in them?
A. Automatically, whenever an item touches the interior of a service vessel
B. Only when the item is placed there with specific intent to be sanctified
C. Only when the high priest personally places the item in the vessel
D. Only during Yom Kippur service
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan rules that service vessels sanctify only when items are placed there with specific intent (לשמה). Since the priest returns the handful without intent to sanctify it, it remains unsanctified and the offering is not disqualified.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is a 'levisa' (heaped bowl) and why does Rav Amram propose using one?
A. A bowl with holes, so the handful falls through before being sanctified
B. A bowl filled and mounded above the rim, so the handful rests atop the heap rather than inside the vessel
C. A sacred bowl reserved only for the High Priest's use
D. A large mixing bowl used only for communal meal offerings
Correct: B — A levisa is a bowl filled beyond its brim so the grain is heaped above the rim. When the handful is returned, it lands on the heap — not inside the vessel — and therefore cannot be sanctified by the vessel's interior. This avoids disqualification.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Avimi come to Rav Ḥisda's study hall, even though Rav Ḥisda was originally Avimi's student?
A. To test whether Rav Ḥisda had become a qualified teacher
B. To deliver a formal lecture at Rav Ḥisda's invitation
C. Because Avimi forgot tractate Menaḥot and came to recover it from his student
D. Because Rav Ḥisda had discovered new rulings that Avimi wanted to learn
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that tractate Menaḥot was 'uprooted' from Avimi — he forgot it. He came before his own student Rav Ḥisda to help him recall what he had previously taught. This is why Rav Ḥisda said he had received 'blows' from Avimi — Avimi used to correct him on this material.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Rava rule regarding the removal of a handful from a vessel resting on the ground?
A. It is invalid — the vessel must always be raised off the ground
B. It is valid, as proven from the frankincense bowls removed from the Table of the Shewbread
C. It is a matter of dispute between Abaye and Rava, left unresolved
D. It is valid only if three priests are present
Correct: B — Rava rules it is obviously permitted to remove a handful from a vessel on the ground. His proof: the bowls of frankincense were removed from the Table of the Shewbread while the Table stood on the ground — yet this removal was valid.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rava, what does 'from the blood of the matter' (Lev. 4:6) serve to exclude?
A. Blood from an animal that died of natural causes
B. Blood mixed with water or wine
C. The residual blood remaining on the priest's finger from a previous sprinkling
D. Blood collected during the night rather than the day
Correct: C — Rava teaches that 'from the blood of the matter' excludes sprinkling from the remainder of blood left on the priest's finger from a prior sprinkling. Each sprinkling must come freshly from the blood in the vessel, not from residue already on his finger.
