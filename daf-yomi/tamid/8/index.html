🍰 The Central Debate: Griddle-Cake in Halves

Rabbi Yochanan
The High Priest's griddle-cake offering is not sanctified in halves. A half-tenth placed in the vessel lacks sanctity entirely.
Rabbi Elazar
Since the offering is sacrificed in halves — half in the morning and half at dusk — it can be sanctified in halves from the outset.
Rabbi Yochanan
The Mishna (50b) states the High Priest brings a full tenth then divides it — proving the whole must be brought before any division.
Rabbi Elazar
That rule is only l'chatchila. B'dieved, if half a tenth was brought in the morning it is valid — the offering's divided nature proves halves can be sanctified.
Gemara's Resolution Rabbi Yochanan derives from the griddle-cake that all meal offerings cannot be sanctified in halves — the griddle-cake itself being the source of the rule. Rabbi Elazar disagrees, reading the griddle-cake as uniquely permitting halves by design.

⚖️ Can a Half-Tenth Be Sanctified?

A kohen sets aside half a tenth of an ephah for a meal offering, intending to add more flour later
↓
Rav
Not sanctified — a half-measure lacks sanctity even when one intends to complete it
Rabbi Yochanan
Is sanctified — intent to complete the measure grants immediate sanctity to the half
↓
Gemara challenges: Does Rabbi Yochanan's view contradict his own derivation from the griddle-cake?
↓
Resolution: Rabbi Yochanan derives all meal offerings from the griddle-cake — since the griddle-cake itself is brought whole first, standard offerings also cannot be sanctified as halves. But b'dieved, a half with intent is valid.

🫙 Meal Offering Sanctification: Rav vs. Rabbi Chanina

Rav

  • Flour is sanctified by a service vessel even without its oil
  • Oil and frankincense are each sanctified independently of each other
  • Parallel: Shewbread has no oil yet is fully sanctified
  • Parallel: Leper's log of oil has no flour yet is sanctified
  • Service vessel sanctifies any valid component placed inside it

Rabbi Chanina

  • Flour, oil, and frankincense are not sanctified separately
  • All components must be placed together in the vessel for sanctification to take effect
  • The tenth-measure vessel was fashioned only for measuring, not for independent sanctification
  • The log-vessel was fashioned to sanctify the leper's oil — a special case, not a general rule
  • Neither substance is sanctified without the other

🤲 Kemitza: Where May the Handful Be Removed?

Rabbi Elazar
A handful removed inside the Sanctuary building is valid — the Temple as a whole is analogous to 'the entrance of the Tent of Meeting.'
Rabbi Yirmeya (objection)
A baraita derives from Leviticus 2:2 — 'he shall remove from there his handful' — that kemitza must be performed in the Temple courtyard, not inside the Sanctuary.
Rabbi Ya'akov (resolution)
The verse is needed only to teach that kemitza is valid anywhere in the entire courtyard — lest one think it must be done specifically near the altar. The Sanctuary itself remains excluded.
Key Verse "And he shall bring it to Aaron's sons the priests; and he shall remove from there his handful" (Leviticus 2:2) — 'from there' expands kemitza to the whole courtyard

🕊️ Peace Offerings Slaughtered in the Sanctuary

וְשָׁחַט אֹתוֹ פֶּתַח אֹהֶל מוֹעֵד (ויקרא ג:ב)
Rabbi Yochanan's Ruling Peace offerings slaughtered inside the Sanctuary building are valid, despite the verse stating 'at the entrance of the Tent of Meeting' (Leviticus 3:2). The word 'entrance' designates the courtyard as ideal but does not exclude the Sanctuary, which lies within the sacred precincts.

Method: Rabbi Yochanan employs analogy — just as burnt offerings slaughtered in the north of the courtyard are valid despite no verse requiring northward slaughter for peace offerings, so too the Sanctuary is included.

This connects to Rabbi Yochanan's broader principle of deriving one kodashim matter from another.
