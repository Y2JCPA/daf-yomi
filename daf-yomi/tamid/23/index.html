🍯 Oil in the Sinner's Handful

Rabbi Yochanan
If oil was added to the kometz (handful) of a sinner's meal offering — the offering is unfit. Oil has no place in this offering (Lev. 5:11).
Reish Lakish
The halakha follows Rabbi Yehuda: the oil is nullified in the handful. Since oil and meal are the same substance-type, the minority is disregarded. The offering remains valid.
📜 Background The meal offering of a sinner (Lev. 5:11) must contain no oil. But what if oil was added after the handful was removed? The debate hinges on Rabbi Yehuda's principle: a substance in contact with the same type is not nullified — does this apply here?

🔥 Rava's Dilemma — Oil on Wood Before the Handful

Rava — the dilemma
If the priest squeezed the oil onto the wood first, then placed the handful on top — what is the law? Are substances contiguous to items that ascend the altar considered part of those items?
Rav Ashi
Rava's dilemma cannot be resolved from the R' Yochanan / Reish Lakish dispute about bones and meat. The circumstances differ — each may hold differently in Rava's case.
⚖️ The Principle at Stake Rabbi Yochanan: substances contiguous to altar items are considered part of them (as in the bone/meat liability case). Reish Lakish: they are not. Rava's dilemma asks whether either position resolves the oil-on-wood scenario.

⚖️ Nullification Dispute — Rav Ḥisda vs. Rabbi Ḥanina

Rav Ḥisda

  • Follow the potentially nullifying substance (the majority)
  • If the majority can become like the minority → they are identical → no nullification
  • 🐄 Carcass meat in majority slaughtered meat → nullified ✅
  • 🥩 Slaughtered meat in majority carcass → NOT nullified ✅ (carcass can become carcass)
  • Supported by: mishna on handful mixed with un-removed-handful offering

Rabbi Ḥanina

  • Follow the potentially nullified substance (the minority)
  • If the minority can become like the majority → they are identical → no nullification
  • 🐄 Carcass meat in majority slaughtered → nullified ✅ (carcass can't become slaughtered)
  • 🥩 Slaughtered meat in majority carcass → NOT nullified ✅ (slaughtered can become carcass)
  • Supported by: mishna on two un-removed-handful offerings

📖 Rabbi Zeira's Verbal Analogy — A Torah Edict

גזירה שווה: 'הקטרה' האמורה בקומץ — 'הקטרה' האמורה בשיריים (רבי זירא)
🔑 גזירה שווה — Gezeirah Shavah The word הַקְטָרָה (burning) appears both with regard to the handful and with regard to the remainder.

Just as when two handfuls are mixed together, neither nullifies the other and both are burned — so too a remainder and a handful never nullify each other.

This is a Torah-level edict: the mixture of handful and remainder is governed by its own rule, independent of general nullification principles. Neither Rav Ḥisda nor Rabbi Ḥanina can override it.
