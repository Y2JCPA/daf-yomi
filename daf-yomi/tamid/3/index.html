⚖️ Three Amora'im Resolve the Contradiction in Rabbi Shimon

Rabba

  • Meal offering not-for-its-sake: valid — intent not recognizable to onlookers
  • Slaughtered offering not-for-its-sake: invalid — no action proves its identity
  • Key test: Can observers see the improper intent from what the priest does?

Rava

  • Meal offering not-for-its-sake: valid — verse unifies all meal offerings
  • וְזֹאת תּוֹרַת הַמִּנְחָה — 'one law' applies to all meal offering types
  • Slaughtered offerings: וּשְׁחָטָהּ לְחַטָּאת — 'it' means must be for itself

Rav Ashi

  • Meal offering not-for-its-sake: valid — vessel intent is irrelevant
  • Pan vs. deep pan is a vessel distinction only — owner can fulfill vow either way
  • Slaughtered offerings: one manner of slaughter cannot prove identity — disqualified

🎯 Rabba's Principle: Does the Action Reveal Improper Intent?

Priest performs a Temple rite
with intent to designate it as a different offering
↓
Intent IS recognizable
Onlookers can deduce wrong intent from the action itself
e.g., goat slaughtered as a guilt offering — rams have white wool, goats have black hair
Intent NOT recognizable
Actions appear normal; observers cannot tell anything is wrong
e.g., handful removed from a meal offering — all removals look the same
↓
❌ DISQUALIFIED — Appearance of sin erodes public trust
vs. ✅ VALID — No visible wrongdoing; owner fulfills obligation

⚔️ The Gemara Hammers Rabba With Counterexamples

Gemara
A bird sin offering whose blood was sprinkled below the line for the sake of a burnt offering — shouldn't it be valid? Its actions prove what it is!
Rabba
Rabbi Shimon never discussed bird offerings at all. His ruling was only that meal offerings differ from most slaughtered offerings.
Gemara
What about slaughter in the northern courtyard — required only for most-sacred offerings — done for lesser-sanctity offerings? Surely that's recognizable!
Rabba
No — one can say the Torah permits lesser offerings in the north too. And southern slaughter? People might say: 'Perhaps these are actually most-sacred offerings slaughtered improperly.'
Gemara
A goat sin offering slaughtered as a guilt offering — a guilt offering is a ram with white wool; this goat has black hair. Completely recognizable!
Gemara
And a calf or bull slaughtered as a Paschal or guilt offering — these animals are never used for those offerings. Anyone can see it!
Result Rabba's position faces mounting challenges — Rava and Rav Ashi each offer cleaner resolutions

📜 Rava: The Verse Itself Unifies All Meal Offerings

וְזֹאת תּוֹרַת הַמִּנְחָה (ויקרא ו:ז) — וּשְׁחָטָהּ לְחַטָּאת (ויקרא ד:לג)
Leviticus 6:7 Rava teaches: the verse וְזֹאת תּוֹרַת הַמִּנְחָה — 'And this is the law of the meal offering' — uses a singular 'law,' indicating one unified law governs all meal offerings. Therefore, removing a handful from a pan meal offering for the sake of a deep-pan meal offering still fulfills the owner's obligation.

Contrast — Leviticus 4:33: וּשְׁחָטָהּ לְחַטָּאת — 'And he shall slaughter it for a sin offering.' The word it signals the offering must be designated specifically for itself. Any other intent disqualifies.

Rav Aḥa son of Rava adds: This derives the rule for all slaughtered offerings — they must each be slaughtered for their own specific designation.

🏺 Rav Ashi: Intent About Vessel Type Is Always Irrelevant

Scenario: A priest removes a handful from a pan meal offering while declaring intent for a deep-pan meal offering
↓
Rav Ashi rules: The offering is valid. Intent about the type of vessel (pan vs. deep pan) is inconsequential — it carries no legal weight.
↓
Support from Mishna 102b: One who vows a deep-pan offering and brings it in a pan has still fulfilled his vow. The two vessel-types are legally interchangeable for fulfilling an obligation.
↓
Contrast with slaughtered offerings: Even though all offerings share one manner of slaughter and one manner of blood-sprinkling — the Torah's word 'it' disqualifies any slaughtered offering not designated for itself.
↓
Challenge from Rav Aḥa son of Rava: What about a dry meal offering for the sake of an oil-mixed offering? These look visibly different — yet Rabbi Shimon calls it valid! How does vessel-intent theory explain that?
↓
Resolution: The 'intent-about-manner-of-preparation' (dry vs. oiled) is similarly inconsequential — just as vessel-type intent carries no weight, neither does oil-quantity intent. Rabbi Shimon consistently regards preparation-mode intent as irrelevant.
