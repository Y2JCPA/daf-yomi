QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye, what is the essential requirement for the tzitz to effect acceptance for a sin?
A. The sin must have been committed by the High Priest himself
B. The general prohibition of that sin must sometimes be permitted before the Lord, i.e., within the Temple
C. The sin must involve blood and not other portions of the offering
D. The sin must have been committed on Yom Kippur specifically
Correct: B — Abaye derives from 'that they may be accepted before the Lord' that the tzitz covers only sins whose general prohibition is occasionally suspended within the Temple itself — not sins suspended outside it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the tzitz NOT effect acceptance for left-hand service, even though that prohibition is suspended on Yom Kippur?
A. Left-hand service never occurs in any valid Temple context
B. The verse 'it shall not be accepted' explicitly excludes it by name
C. Per Abaye, the sin must be in the sacred item itself; the hand is not a sacred item — and per Rav Ashi, 'hakodashim' requires the object to be holy
D. Left-hand service on Yom Kippur is actually a Torah violation, not a rabbinic one
Correct: C — Abaye: 'Aaron shall bear the sin of the sacred items' — the tzitz atones for sins committed in/with the sacred item, not procedural violations. Rav Ashi adds: 'hakodashim' (plural) means the sin's object must itself be holy.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What verse does Rav Ashi cite to permanently exclude blemished animals from tzitz atonement?
A. 'And it shall be upon Aaron's forehead' (Exodus 28:38)
B. 'Every one that is ritually pure may eat of it' (Leviticus 7:19)
C. 'It shall not be accepted' and 'it shall not be acceptable for you' (Leviticus 22:23, 22:20)
D. 'Aaron shall bear the sin committed with the sacred items' (Exodus 28:38)
Correct: C — Rav Ashi cites Leviticus 22:23 and 22:20 — these absolute formulations ('shall not be accepted,' 'shall not be acceptable for you') permanently exclude blemished offerings, unlike impurity which has no such absolute exclusion.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the baraita on Amud B, for whom does the tzitz NOT effect acceptance?
A. Priests who acted with intention (b'meizid)
B. Gentiles (non-Jews)
C. Women who bring individual offerings
D. Converts within their first year of conversion
Correct: B — The verse states 'that it may be accepted for THEM before the Lord' — the word 'for them' teaches that tzitz acceptance applies only to the Jewish people, not to gentiles whose offerings became impure.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Ravina ultimately resolve the contradiction between the two baraitot regarding impure blood that was sprinkled?
A. One baraita follows Rabbi Eliezer and the other follows Rabbi Yosei regarding penalizing intentional acts
B. The baraitot address different types of offerings: individual vs. communal
C. Whether impurity was contracted unwittingly or intentionally the offering is accepted; the baraitot differ on whether the owner or the priest contracted the impurity
D. One baraita applies only to Yom Kippur while the other applies to regular days
Correct: C — Ravina resolves: in all circumstances (unwitting or intentional impurity, unwitting or intentional sprinkling) the offering is accepted. The two baraitot simply address different subjects — the owner's impurity vs. the priest's impurity.
