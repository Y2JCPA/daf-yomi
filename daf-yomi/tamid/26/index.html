🍯 Does Remainder Impurity Invalidate the Offering?

Rabbi Eliezer
Even if the remainder became impure, was burned, or was lost — the handful is sacrificed and the offering is valid. The handful stands on its own.
Rabbi Yehoshua
If the remainder became impure, the offering is invalid — the handful may not be sacrificed. The remainder and handful are interdependent.
Rav
Rabbi Yehoshua's ruling applies only when ALL the remainder became impure. If only PART became impure, what remains is still significant and the offering is fit.
Rava
The same logic applies to partial loss or partial burning — what remains is significant and the remaining portion is sacrificed normally. This accords with Rabbi Yehoshua.
📌 Key Principle Rabbi Yehoshua's baraita teaches: any offering with an olive-bulk of meat or fat remaining — the priest sprinkles the blood. Less than that, he does not sprinkle.

✋ Must the Handful Be Sanctified in a Service Vessel?

The Rabbis

  • Handful MUST be sanctified in a service vessel before burning
  • Transferring to left hand renders it unfit — permanently
  • Returning it to right hand cannot restore its validity
  • Reason: vessel sanctification is an indispensable step
  • Source: baraita — 'the handful requires sanctification in a service vessel'

Rabbi Shimon

  • Service vessel is NOT required for the handful
  • Once removed and sanctified, it may be burned even from the hand
  • Source: 'It is most holy, as the sin offering' (Lev. 6:10) — sin offering burns without special vessel requirement
  • If put in left hand — return to right hand; it remains valid
  • Rabbi Yannai: no restrictions on manner of bringing it up to altar

⏰ When Does Burning the Handful Permit the Remainder?

The Question: From precisely which moment does the handful's sacrifice render the שיירים (remainder) permitted for consumption by the priests?
↓
Rabbi Ḥanina: From the moment the fire takes hold of the handful — i.e., when it first ignites on the altar
↓
Rabbi Yoḥanan: Only once the majority of the handful is consumed by fire
Proof: 'smoke of the land went up as smoke of a furnace' (Gen. 19:28) — a furnace releases smoke only after fire consumes the majority
↓
Baraita (via Ravin bar Rav Adda): Items sacrificed by day (handful, frankincense, incense) — from when may they be burned after sunset? Once begun during the day, burning may continue into the night
↓
Challenge: If lit concurrent with sunset, majority cannot be consumed before nightfall — contradiction with Rabbi Yoḥanan?
Rabbi Elazar resolves: Baraita refers to items already arranged on the altar before sunset; once on the altar, they may burn through the night
