🌾 Minority Prevents Majority — Sources

Handful (Kometz)

  • 'His handful' appears twice in Torah (Lev. 2:2, 6:8)
  • Repetition teaches: full handful required
  • Minority prevents majority from permitting remainder to priests

Tenth of Ephah

  • 'The priest shall take from it his handful'
  • Implies entire tenth must be present for handful to be valid
  • Minority prevents majority

Wine Libation

  • 'So shall it be done' — statute language
  • Entire prescribed measure required
  • Minority of wine prevents majority from counting

Fine Flour + Oil

  • Juxtaposed in the same verse
  • Each is indispensable to the other
  • Handful + frankincense: each prevents the other (same verse grouping)

🌿 Must the Lulav Be Bound Together?

Rabbis
A lulav is valid whether bound with myrtle and willow or not. Binding is not required for fulfillment of the mitzva — the four species need not be physically joined.
Rabbi Yehuda
Binding IS required! Via gezera shava: 'taking' of the four species (Lev. 23:40) = 'taking' of the hyssop bundle at Pesach (Ex. 12:22). Just as hyssop must be bundled, so must the lulav species.
Rabbis
The gezera shava is unwarranted. 'Taking' is a general term that does not require bundling. Failure to bind does not invalidate the four species.
Baraita Resolution
There IS a mitzva to bind them — fulfilling 'This is my God and I will beautify Him' (Ex. 15:2), i.e., hiddur mitzva. But failure to bind does NOT invalidate the mitzva. This follows the Rabbis.
🤝 Unity Lesson The four species represent distinct types of Jews (fruit+fragrance, fruit only, fragrance only, neither). Only bound together as one can Israel be accepted before God.

🏛️ Unauthorized Entry into the Sacred Areas

אֶל פְּנֵי הַכַּפֹּרֶת — before the Ark Cover (Vayikra 16:14)
Rabbis vs. Rabbi Yehuda — Lev. 16:2 The verse: 'That he not come at all times into the holy place, within the Curtain, before the Ark Cover.'

Rabbis: 'Holy place' and 'within the Curtain' describe one continuous area and one prohibition — unauthorized entry into the Sanctuary incurs death at the hand of Heaven. The phrase 'before the Ark Cover' is a paradigm teaching that el penei anywhere in Torah indicates a specific required direction.

Rabbi Yehuda: Two distinct areas, two distinct penalties — entering the Sanctuary (holy place) = lashes; entering within the Curtain, i.e., the Holy of Holies = death at the hand of Heaven.

Further implication: The school of Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov taught that wherever the Torah uses el penei ('before'), it establishes that the action must be performed facing that specific direction.
