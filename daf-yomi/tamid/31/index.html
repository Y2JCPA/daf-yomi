🌾 What Should Rabbi Shimon Shezuri Do With His Mixed Tevel?

Rabbi Shimon Shezuri
My untithed produce (tevel) became mixed with a larger quantity of properly tithed produce. I came to Rabbi Tarfon — what must I do?
Rabbi Tarfon
By Torah law the minority tevel is nullified in the majority — but by rabbinic law you are still obligated to separate tithes from the mixture.
Rabbi Shimon Shezuri
Can I take produce from a gentile — exempt from Torah tithes — and use it to cover the rabbinic tevel in my mixture?
Rabbi Tarfon
No — and I cannot send you to an am ha'aretz either, since most amei ha'aretz do not tithe. Their produce is presumed tevel and cannot redeem your tevel.
Key Principle Rabbinic tevel cannot be remedied by tithing from another unreliable source — the substitute produce must itself be reliably exempt.

✂️ Two Types of Parchment for Torah Scroll Repair

✅ 'New' Parchment

  • Processed with oak gall (tannic acid)
  • Can be sewn to repair a scroll tear
  • Compatible with other gall-treated parchment
  • Tear ≤2 lines: sewing restores fitness

❌ 'Old' Parchment

  • Not processed with gall
  • Cannot be sewn to render a scroll fit
  • Incompatible with new parchment
  • Any qualifying tear leaves scroll unfit

📋 Writing a Valid Mezuza — Format Rules

Two words per line: Rav (via Rav Ḥananel) rules — a mezuza written with two words on each line is fit.
↓
Dilemma raised: what if one line has 2 words and the very next line has 3 words — is the mezuza still valid?
↓
Rabbi Yoḥanan rules: even alternating 2-and-3 words per line is fit — confirmed by Rabba bar bar Ḥana (and Rav Aḥa bar bar Ḥana) in his name.
↓
Rav Ḥisda's rule for the final line: the last two words al ha'aretz (Deut. 11:21 — 'upon the earth') must appear alone on the final line.
↓
Dispute: some write al ha'aretz at the end of the final line; others at the beginning — a debate about which half of Deut. 11:21 receives emphasis.
