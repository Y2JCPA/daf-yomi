Tamid 11 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why must the Mishna list all three examples (stone, salt, frankincense) that disqualify the handful, rather than just one?
A. Each example teaches a principle that cannot be derived from the others
B. The Torah explicitly lists all three and the Mishna follows suit
C. To show that exactly three items can ever be found in a handful
D. Because three examples create a legal presumption (chazakah)
Correct: A — A stone is unfit for sacrifice; salt was not mixed with the whole offering; frankincense is actually fit for the altar. Each case is necessary because it could not be derived from the others.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Zutra bar Toviyya in the name of Rav, how does the priest physically scoop the handful?
A. He scoops with his thumb and forefinger pinched together
B. He bends his three middle fingers until their tips reach the palm of his hand
C. He fills a fully closed fist and then levels with a utensil
D. He uses only the tips of all five fingers
Correct: B — The priest bends his three middle fingers inward until the fingertips touch his palm, so the flour fills that enclosed space. The thumb and little finger remain above to level any overflow.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the fundamental difference between Rabbi Meir's and Rabbi Yehuda's positions on frankincense?
A. Rabbi Meir requires the entire handful to be burned; Rabbi Yehuda requires only one pinch burned ultimately
B. Rabbi Meir requires only one pinch burned; Rabbi Yehuda requires the entire handful
C. They agree on burning requirements but disagree about how much to scoop initially
D. Rabbi Meir permits a decreased frankincense offering; Rabbi Yehuda does not
Correct: A — Rabbi Meir derives from 'all the frankincense' that the entire handful must be burned. Rabbi Yehuda derives from 've'et' that ultimately only one pinch need be burned on the altar.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara prove that non-sacred oil mixed into a meal offering disqualifies it?
A. From a verse that explicitly forbids non-sacred oil in the Temple
B. From the meal offering of a sinner, which has no oil — any added oil would disqualify it
C. From the rule that all non-sacred items are forbidden in the Temple courtyard
D. From an explicit teaching of Rabbi Akiva about Temple boundaries
Correct: B — The meal offering of a sinner contains no oil at all. If non-sacred oil or another offering's oil did not disqualify, mixing it in would have no effect — but the Gemara shows it does disqualify, proving the principle.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Regarding the frankincense in the two bowls that accompany the shewbread, what do all tannaim agree upon?
A. The bowls' frankincense must be burned entirely to permit the shewbread for consumption
B. Only one pinch of the bowl's frankincense needs to be burned
C. The bowls follow the same dispute as meal offering frankincense
D. The shewbread bowls are exempt from all frankincense burning requirements
Correct: A — All agree that the frankincense in the two bowls must be burned completely, because this act permits the shewbread to be eaten. The tannaitic dispute applies only to frankincense accompanying a meal offering, not to these independent bowls.
