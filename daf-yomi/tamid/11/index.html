✋ How Is the Handful Removed? The Finger Debate

Abaye
How do priests properly remove the handful from a meal offering?
Rava
They remove it as people normally do — folding all their fingers over the palm of the hand.
Abaye
But each finger has a distinct sacred role! The little finger measures; the middle finger measures a cubit; the forefinger sprinkles blood; the thumb wraps tefillin. Can we fold all of them?
Gemara
The little finger is used only for leveling — the priest first scoops with all fingers so it is not lacking, then wipes away overflow with thumb and little finger.
Rav Zutra b. Toviyya
He bends his three middle fingers until the fingertips reach the palm — the flour fills that space. This is the standard measure of a handful.
Key Ruling The three middle fingers scoop; the thumb and little finger level from above and below. This is the hardest rite in the Temple service.

🫙 Does Increasing the Oil Disqualify the Meal Offering?

Priest separates two log of oil instead of one log for the meal offering
↓
Rabbi Eliezer
Disqualified
Extra oil — even from another meal offering — invalidates it
Implication
Non-sacred oil also disqualifies a meal offering when mixed in
↓
Proof from the Sinner's Meal Offering
It has no oil at all — if non-sacred or foreign oil were mixed in, it would be disqualified. This confirms such oil does disqualify.

🌿 Three Tannaitic Opinions on Frankincense

Rabbi Meir

  • Must scoop a full handful at the outset
  • Must burn the entire handful on the altar
  • Any decrease — even one pinch — disqualifies
  • Derives from: "all the frankincense" — totality required throughout

Rabbi Yehuda

  • Must scoop a full handful at the outset
  • Ultimately only one pinch need be burned
  • Decrease below one pinch disqualifies
  • Derives from: "ve'et" (וְאֵת) — an addition, implying a part suffices

Rabbi Shimon

  • Even taking a partial handful at the outset is valid
  • Only one pinch need be burned ultimately
  • Most lenient of all three positions
  • A pinch is the minimum functional unit in all frankincense law

📦 When Does the Dispute Apply? Meal Offering vs. Shewbread Bowls

לְבוֹנָה הַבָּאָה בִּפְנֵי עַצְמָהּ — דִּבְרֵי הַכֹּל פּוֹסֶלֶת (מנחות י״א.)
Critical Distinction The dispute between Rabbi Meir, Yehuda, and Shimon applies only to frankincense accompanying a meal offering (לְבוֹנָה הַבָּאָה עִם הַמִּנְחָה). For frankincense that comes by itself — and for the two frankincense bowls placed with the shewbread (לֶחֶם הַפָּנִים) — all agree: the entire bowl must be burned, because it is the burning of the frankincense that permits the shewbread for consumption. There is no 've'et' derivation to allow a partial amount.
