⚖️ The Great Piggul Debate: Lacking Remainder

Rav Huna
Even according to Rabbi Akiva, burning the handful is NOT effective in rendering a lacking remainder as piggul. The deficiency is intrinsic to the remainder itself — unlike an external disqualification such as leaving the Temple.
Rava
On the contrary! Even by Rabbi Eliezer's opinion, burning the handful IS effective — it renders even a lacking remainder as piggul and removes it from the laws of misuse of consecrated property.
Rava
Proof: Rabbi Ḥiyya's mishna omits 'or an olive-bulk' for the remainder — implying the remainder was already lacking in measure, yet piggul still applies!
Abaye
No proof from there! Rabbi Ḥiyya follows Rabbi Elazar, who holds one is not liable for burning less than the full handful — so he omits the olive-bulk for the handful too, not because of the remainder's lack.
Core Question If the remainder of a meal offering became lacking in measure after the handful was removed but before it was burned — can burning the handful still render the remainder piggul?

🫱 The Four Rites That Can Create Piggul

1. Removal (קְמִיצָה / Kemitzah) — The priest removes the handful from the meal offering
↓
2. Placement in Vessel (נְתִינַת כְּלִי / Nesinas Kli) — The handful is placed into a Temple service vessel
↓
3. Conveying to the Altar (הוֹלָכָה / Holachah) — The vessel is carried toward the altar
↓
4. Burning on Altar (הַקְטָרָה / Haktarah) — The handful is burned on the altar as the permitting factor
↓
If any rite is performed with intent to eat the remainder or burn the handful outside its designated time or place → the offering becomes פִּגּוּל and one is liable to כָּרֵת

📏 Does a Lacking Remainder Become Piggul?

Handful removed from meal offering with piggul intent
↓
Remainder becomes lacking in measure (less than olive-bulk) before the handful is burned on the altar
Rav Huna
Burning the handful is NOT effective — the intrinsic deficiency of the remainder blocks piggul from taking effect
Rava
Burning the handful IS effective — renders even a lacking remainder as piggul and removes it from מְעִילָה (misuse) liability

⚔️ Rabbi Eliezer vs. The Rabbis — Cross-Intent for Piggul

Rabbi Eliezer

  • Intent can cross between eating and burning domains
  • Intent to eat what is normally burned = invalidates
  • Intent to burn what is normally eaten = invalidates
  • Half olive-bulk eaten + half olive-bulk burned combine for piggul
  • Basis: Mishna 12b follows this view (per Rabbi Yirmeya)

The Rabbis

  • Intent must match the normal manner of the item
  • Eating intent applies only to portions normally eaten
  • Burning intent applies only to portions normally burned on altar
  • Half + half of different types do NOT combine
  • Abaye: mishna can be read in accord with Rabbis too

🍽️ Eating & Burning Do Not Combine

אֲכִילָה וְהַקְטָרָה אֵין מִצְטָרְפִין
Mishna Ruling — Daf 12b If one intends to eat half an olive-bulk of the remainder and burn half an olive-bulk of it outside its place or after its time — the offering is fit (כָּשֵׁר). Each intent alone falls short of the required measure (olive-bulk), and the two categories cannot join.

The Gemara notes: this holds according to the Rabbis. But according to Rabbi Eliezer, eating-consumption and altar-consumption do combine — and the mishna may follow him.
