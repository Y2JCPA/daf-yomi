🏺 Does the Vessel Join Non-Touching Contents?

Two half-tenths placed in one vessel — not touching each other
↓
Sons of Rabbi Ḥiyya
Vessel joins in any case — touching or not. Mishna says 'joins,' implying always.
Rav Kahana
Vessel joins only when item requires the vessel for sanctification — meal offerings qualify; not everything does.
↓
Unresolved (תיקו): Can handful be removed from one half on behalf of the other non-touching half?

🗣️ Rav Kahana vs. Sons of Rabbi Ḥiyya

Sons of R. Ḥiyya
The mishna says a vessel joins its contents — this applies whether the contents touch each other or not!
Rav Kahana
Only when the item requires a vessel in order to be sanctified does the vessel join it. A meal offering needs a vessel — so it joins. But this is not universal.
Sons of R. Ḥiyya
What if one who immersed that day inserts his finger between the two half-tenths without touching either — are both rendered impure through the vessel's joining?
Rav Kahana
Can one remove a handful from this half-tenth on behalf of that half-tenth, when they are in one vessel but not in contact?
The Stakes The vessel's joining power determines both ritual impurity transmission and the validity of kemitza (handful removal) across non-touching halves.

🤏 Rava's Baraita Proof — and Abaye's Rebuttal

Verse (Lev. 6:8): 'He shall take up his handful' — implies from a single, unified tenth
↓
Baraita: One may not bring a tenth of an ephah in two vessels
↓
Rava infers
One vessel = one unit → handful from one part can permit the remainder of the other part
Abaye objects
Baraita's 'two vessels' = hollowed kefiza inside larger vessel; 'one vessel like two' = hen trough with dividing ridge — neither proves joining of simply-separate halves

⚖️ Three Half-Tenths: Rava vs. Abaye

Rava

  • Only the replacement half joins legally with the original
  • Replacement impure → original also impure (they complete each other)
  • Original or found half impure → only those two affected
  • Handful from found half: its remainder + original eaten; replacement not eaten
  • Handful from original: found half and replacement both not eaten
  • Handful from replacement: its remainder eaten; found and original not eaten

Abaye

  • All three are 'residents of one cabin' (בני חד ביתא)
  • Any one becomes impure → both remaining become impure
  • Handful from any one → both remaining halves are not eaten
  • Reason: All three were at some point designated for the same issaron
  • Objection (Rav Pappa): Even the half from which handful was taken cannot fully permit its own remainder — 1/6 of the handful was extraneous
  • Objection (Rav Yitzḥak): The handful itself cannot be sacrificed — 1/3 of it is non-sacrificial

מנחות כד ע"ב
כולהו בני חד ביתא נינהו — They Are All Residents of One Cabin Abaye's bold principle: all three half-tenths — the original, the replacement separated while it was lost, and the found original — are legally bonded to one another because each was at some point designated to complete the same single issaron meal offering. This bond means that impurity or handful removal touching any one of them affects the legal standing of all three.

Rav Pappa challenges: even the half from which the handful was taken is not fully permitted — one-sixth (danka) of that handful was not properly sacrificed on its behalf. Rav Yitzḥak bar Mesharshiyya adds: the handful itself cannot be sacrificed — one-third of it was intended for an extraneous half-tenth, rendering it non-sacrificial. Rav Ashi resolves: the matter depends entirely on the priest's intention when removing the handful.
