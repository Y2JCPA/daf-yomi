QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Kahana, in what circumstance does a vessel legally join its non-touching contents into one unit?
A. Whenever any two food items are placed together in the same vessel
B. Only when the item requires a vessel in order to be sanctified
C. Only when the contents are of equal volume
D. Only for liquids, not dry meal offerings
Correct: B — Rav Kahana limits the joining principle: the vessel joins its contents only when the item requires the vessel for its sanctification. Since meal offerings require a vessel for sanctification, they are joined. Items that do not require a vessel for sanctification are not legally joined by the vessel.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Abaye undermine Rava's proof from the baraita that one may remove the handful from one half on behalf of the other?
A. He argues the baraita discusses liquid offerings, not flour offerings
B. He claims the baraita is a later amoraic addition with no tannaitic authority
C. He reinterprets the baraita's cases as a vessel-within-a-vessel and a trough with a dividing ridge — neither of which is simply two non-touching halves in one vessel
D. He accepts the proof but limits it to the Temple courtyard only
Correct: C — Abaye argues that 'two vessels' in the baraita means a smaller kefiza hollowed out within a larger vessel, and 'one vessel like two' means a hen trough divided by a ridge into separate compartments. Neither case actually proves that two separate, non-touching halves in one plain vessel may permit each other.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the basis of Abaye's 'one cabin' (בני חד ביתא) principle for the three half-tenths?
A. They are all placed in the same physical vessel at the same time
B. They all came from the same batch of flour
C. Each was at some point designated to complete the same single issaron meal offering, creating a permanent legal bond
D. The replacement half-tenth was formally consecrated together with the original
Correct: C — Abaye's reasoning is that all three half-tenths — original, replacement, and found — were each at some point designated for the same one issaron. This shared designation creates a legal bond among them, such that impurity or handful removal affecting any one of them affects all others.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rav Pappa's objection to Abaye's ruling that the remainder of the half from which the handful was removed is eaten?
A. The handful cannot be removed until both halves are touching
B. One-sixth (danka) of the handful was set aside for an extraneous half and was not properly sacrificed, so it cannot permit even the remainder of the half from which it was taken
C. The priest who removes the handful must declare his intention aloud
D. The handful is only valid if it weighs exactly one-tenth of an ephah
Correct: B — Rav Pappa points out that even the half from which the handful was taken is problematic: one-sixth (danka) of that handful was effectively intended for an extraneous half-tenth and therefore was not properly sacrificed — meaning the handful cannot fully permit even its own remainder to be eaten.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rav Ashi resolve the objections of Rav Pappa and Rav Yitzḥak bar Mesharshiyya against Abaye's ruling?
A. He rules that Abaye's principle is rejected outright and Rava's position stands
B. He says a second handful must be removed to cover the extraneous portion
C. He rules that the matter depends on the priest's intention when removing the handful — the handful's validity is determined by what the priest intends it to permit
D. He limits Abaye's ruling to cases where all three half-tenths are touching each other
Correct: C — Rav Ashi resolves the objections by stating that the validity of the handful removal depends on the priest's intention at the time of removal. By intending the handful to permit the relevant remainder, the priest can determine which portion is permitted — addressing the concern about extraneous fractions of the handful.
