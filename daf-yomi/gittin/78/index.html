Gittin 78: Domains, Proximity & Delivering the Get

Husband throws get to wife in his house
↓
Where does it land?
His bed / His domain
Not divorced — still his territory
Her bed / Her lap / Her basket
Divorced — considered her domain
Thrown on ground
Depends on proximity — closer to him or her
Core Issue When a get is delivered within the husband's house, the Gemara must determine what counts as the wife's domain — her bed, her lap, her basket, or the ground near her.
3 / 9

His Bed vs. Her Bed

Rava
If he throws the get onto his bed where she sits — she is not divorced.
Rava
But if he throws it onto her bed — she is divorced.
Gemara
But her bed is in his house! This is like vessels of a buyer in the domain of the seller — can the buyer acquire through them?
Gemara
Answer: The bed is 10 tefachim high — it is its own domain.
Gemara
But the legs stand on his floor!
Gemara
People are not particular about the tiny space under the bed legs.
R' Eliezer (Baraita) Confirms Rava's ruling: his bed → not divorced; her bed → divorced.

Why Does Her Basket Work? Five Opinions

Shmuel / R' Oshaya

  • Basket is hanging from her body
  • Not resting in husband's domain at all

Reish Lakish

  • Tied to her is sufficient
  • Need not be hanging off the ground

Rav Adda bar Ahava

  • Basket placed between her thighs
  • Husband not particular about where she sits

R' Yochanan

  • No special setup needed
  • The place of her lap and basket belong to her
  • Husband transfers that space to her by default
Rava's Explanation R' Yochanan's reasoning: a husband is not particular about the place of his wife's lap or basket — it is as though he transferred ownership of that space to her.

"Take This Promissory Note" — Verbal Declaration Required

Husband hands wife a document or she finds it behind him
↓
Does he say "This is your get" (זה גיטך)?
Yes
✅ Valid get — provided she received it from him
No — says 'take this note'
❌ Not valid — even if she reads it and sees it's a get
No — she finds it
❌ Not valid — she must also receive it from his hand
↓
"Found behind him" = she pulled it from his belt, not picked up from ground
↓
Rava: "Take your get from the ground" = meaningless — she must receive it from him
Mishna Even if she was sleeping when he placed it in her hand — when she wakes and reads it, it is not valid unless he tells her: This is your get.

Proximity of the Thrown Get: Rav vs. R' Yochanan

Rav's ViewR' Yochanan's View
"Closer to her"Within her 4 amotActually closer to her — even 100 amot away
"Closer to him"Within his 4 amotActually closer to him — even 100 amot away
"Equally balanced"Between their 4 amot zones — part in eachBoth can guard it, or neither can guard it
Result if closer to herDivorcedDivorced
Result if closer to himNot divorcedNot divorced
Result if equally balancedSafek (uncertain)Safek (uncertain)
Rabba & Rav Yosef "Equally balanced" may refer to conflicting witnesses: one group says it fell closer to him, the other says closer to her.

R' Yochanan's 'Guarding' Standard

R' Yochanan
"Closer to her" = she can guard it and he cannot. "Closer to him" = he can guard it and she cannot.
R' Eliezer
If a get is closer to her but a dog snatches it — she is not divorced.
Gemara
But once it was closer to her, the divorce took effect! Must she guard it forever?
Gemara
Rather, R' Eliezer means: if a dog could take it and he could guard it but she could not — it's in his domain.
Shmuel
"Closer to her" = she could bend down and take it. But do not rule leniently unless the get reaches her hand.
Practical Ruling Rav Mordekhai reports: in a real case where the get was closer to the woman but was lost, and the husband died — she still required chalitzah due to uncertainty.
