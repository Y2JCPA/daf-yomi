QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Beit Shammai, what happens when a man writes a get for his wife but does not give it to her?
A. She is fully divorced
B. She is disqualified from marrying a kohen
C. Nothing happens — it has no legal effect
D. She must receive it within 30 days
Correct: B — Beit Shammai hold that merely writing the get — even without giving it — disqualifies the woman from marrying into the priesthood, though the marriage itself remains intact.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What 'investigation' does Shmuel require when a rumor circulates that a kohen wrote a get for his wife?
A. Whether the rumor can be abolished
B. Whether local people use 'writing' to mean 'giving'
C. Whether the wife admits to receiving a get
D. Whether two witnesses confirm the rumor
Correct: B — Since in Neharde'a they do not abolish rumors, the investigation is linguistic: do locals say 'wrote a get' when they mean 'gave a get'? If so, the rumor is much more serious.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Shmuel's ruling that 'she must leave' not cast aspersions on the children from the first husband?
A. Because the children were born before the rumor started
B. Because the presumption is that the divorce occurred close to the first husband's death
C. Because Shmuel does not believe the rumor is true
D. Because children of kohanim have special protections
Correct: B — Since she is only removed from the second husband (not the first), people will explain that the first husband divorced her close to his death, preserving the legitimacy of their children.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Mishna's view, about what case do Beit Hillel and Beit Shammai disagree regarding a divorced couple seen together?
A. When witnesses saw them engage in intercourse
B. When witnesses confirm seclusion but did not see intercourse
C. When they stayed at an inn but were not secluded
D. When only one witness saw them together
Correct: B — The Mishna discusses witnesses of seclusion without witnesses of intercourse. Beit Hillel equate seclusion with intercourse (requiring a second get), while Beit Shammai do not.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is a 'bare bill of divorce' (get kere'ach)?
A. A get that has no witnesses at all
B. A folded get where the number of folds exceeds the number of witness signatures
C. A get written without the wife's name
D. A get that was torn before delivery
Correct: B — A get kere'ach is a folded and tied bill of divorce where some folds lack witness signatures — the number of folds is more than the number of witnesses who signed.
