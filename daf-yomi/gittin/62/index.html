Gittin 62: Purity, Livelihood & Divorce Agency

DAF OVERVIEW Amud A addresses how a chaver (meticulous observer) can prepare challah or teruma oil for an am ha'aretz while preserving ritual purity — permitted for the sake of livelihood.

Amud B introduces the Mishna on divorce agency: the critical distinction between an agent of receipt (שליח קבלה) and an agent of delivery (שליח הולכה), and whether the husband can retract once the get is handed over.

Why Does the Wife of an Am Ha'aretz Take Food?

Gemara
If we worry she steals food from her husband to give to others, shouldn't we also worry she exchanges her food with the other woman's?
Rav Yosef
She rationalizes: 'The ox may eat from its threshing' (השור אוכל מדישתו) — while preparing food, she feels entitled to eat from it. That's not the same as exchanging.
Gemara
So her taking is self-justified eating, not deliberate theft or exchange. We cannot learn from here that every am ha'aretz is suspected of exchanging food.
PRINCIPLE A person's self-serving rationalization (eating while working) does not prove a broader suspicion of deliberate deception.

Chaver Preparing for an Am Ha'aretz: Two Cases

🍞 Kneader (Challah)

  • Cannot prepare the challah loaf directly in purity for the am ha'aretz
  • May prepare ALL the dough in purity
  • Separates challah and places it in a kefisha (basket) or anchuta (vessel) that doesn't contract impurity
  • Am ha'aretz takes both dough and challah

🫒 Olive Presser (Teruma)

  • Cannot prepare teruma oil directly in purity for the am ha'aretz
  • May press ALL olives in purity
  • Separates teruma oil into vessels of dung, stone, or clay — which don't contract impurity
  • Am ha'aretz takes both ordinary oil and teruma
SOURCE Baraita (Tosefta Demai 3:1) — testified by R' Yosei ben HaMeshullam in the name of R' Yochanan his brother, in the name of R' Elazar ben Chasma.

Why Are Both Cases (Kneader & Olive Presser) Needed?

R' Yochanan: The leniency is for the sake of livelihood (פרנסה)
↓
If only kneader was taught...
Kneader Only
His pay is small → more in need of help. Maybe we wouldn't be lenient with a well-paid olive presser.
Olive Presser Only
His work is uncommon (seasonal) → greater livelihood concern. Maybe we wouldn't be lenient with the year-round kneader.
↓
Both are needed: each has a unique reason for leniency
TALMUDIC METHOD Classic tzrikhuta — explaining why neither case alone would suffice to teach the other.

Mishna: Agency for a Get — When Can the Husband Retract?

ScenarioAgent TypeCan Husband Retract?
Husband says: Receive this get for my wifeAgent of Delivery (husband's)✅ Yes — until get reaches wife
Husband says: Deliver this get to my wifeAgent of Delivery (husband's)✅ Yes — until get reaches wife
Wife says to agent: Receive my get for meAgent of Receipt (wife's)❌ No — divorce effective on receipt by agent
Husband to wife's agent: I don't want you to receive; deliver it insteadConverted to Delivery (husband's)✅ Yes — until get reaches wife
R' Shimon b. Gamliel: Wife says Take my getAgent of Receipt (wife's)❌ No — 'take' = 'receive'
KEY DISTINCTION שליח קבלה (agent of receipt) = wife's agent → get effective immediately upon agent's receipt.
שליח הולכה (agent of delivery) = husband's agent → get effective only when it reaches the wife.

Does 'Deliver' (הולך) Equal 'Acquire' (זכי)?

Rav Acha b. Rav Avya
From the Mishna: If the wife appointed an agent of receipt and the husband said 'deliver' — he still cannot retract. So 'deliver' = 'acquire'!
Rav Ashi / Gemara
No proof. The Mishna may mean the husband said 'receive' to the wife's agent. The novel teaching is that even when the husband says 'receive' (language he can't normally use), it's valid as a delivery instruction.
Rav Acha b. Rav Avya
Another proof: The Mishna says he can retract only because he said 'I don't want.' Implying: without that cancellation, 'deliver' would equal 'acquire'!
Gemara
This too can be rejected — the emphasis may be on the cancellation itself, not on equating 'deliver' with 'acquire.'
UNRESOLVED The Gemara brings multiple proofs and rejections but does not conclusively resolve whether הולך כזכי — whether saying 'deliver' has the same legal force as 'acquire.'
