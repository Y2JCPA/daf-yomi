Key Principle A rumor requires more than hearsay — it needs circumstantial evidence and must be confirmed in court to have binding legal force.

When Does Licentious Behavior Require Divorce?

R' Meir
A woman who ate in the marketplace, walked arrogantly, or nursed publicly — she must leave her husband.
R' Akiva
Only when the women spinning by moonlight (מוזרות) gossip about her promiscuity — indicating it's well-known fact.
R' Yochanan b. Nuri
If so, no daughter of Avraham could remain married! Women commonly slander their peers.
R' Yochanan b. Nuri
The Torah requires a clear matter (דבר) — just as witnesses establish facts, so too the 'unseemly matter' for divorce must be clearly established.
Rava
A rumor of licentious intercourse does not disqualify a woman from marrying a kohen. We assume people merely saw flirtatious behavior.
Rava's Rule Rava follows R' Yochanan ben Nuri — rumors of impropriety alone cannot establish legal facts without clear evidence.

What Constitutes a Valid Rumor (קול)?

Ulla

  • 🕯️ Candles lit in her home
  • 🛏️ Beds made (bridal custom)
  • 👥 People entering and leaving
  • 🗣️ People say: 'She WAS betrothed today'

Levi

  • 🕯️ Candles lit
  • 🛏️ Beds made
  • 🧵 Women spinning by candlelight
  • 🎉 Rejoicing and saying: 'She WAS betrothed'

R' Yochanan

  • 🕯️ Candles lit + beds made
  • 👥 People entering and leaving
  • Explicit statement = full rumor
  • No explicit statement = mere explanation
Critical Detail All authorities require circumstantial evidence beyond a mere 'echo' — and the Gemara insists the statement must be in PAST tense ('was betrothed'), not future ('is becoming betrothed').

The Explanation (Amatla) — Negating a Rumor

A rumor of betrothal circulates with proper evidence
↓
Does an explanation (אמתלא) emerge?
No explanation
Rumor stands — she is treated as betrothed
Explanation emerges
Check: when did it surface?
↓
Rabba bar Rav Huna: Valid if within 10 days of the rumor
↓
Rav Zevid: Even a reasonable possibility of explanation suffices — no need for it to be stated explicitly
↓
Rav Pappa objects: Mishna says 'provided there is no explanation' — implying it must be actually stated
Dispute Rav Zevid: a reasonable possibility of explanation negates the rumor even if unstated. Rav Pappa: the explanation must be explicitly mentioned.

Rav Ashi's Two Principles on Rumors

📋 A rumor that was not confirmed in court (בית דין) is not a significant rumor
↓
💍 A rumor that spreads after marriage — we are NOT concerned about it
↓
❓ What about a rumor after betrothal but before marriage?
↓
⚖️ Rav Ashi: After betrothal — we ARE concerned
↓
🔓 Rav Ḥaviva: Even after betrothal — we are NOT concerned
↓
✅ Halakha: We are NOT concerned — following Rav Ḥaviva
Final Ruling The halakha follows Rav Ḥaviva: a rumor that circulates after either betrothal or marriage does not require the couple to separate.
