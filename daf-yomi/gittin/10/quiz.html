QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why does Rabbi Shimon validate a get with clearly gentile (muvhakin) names?
A. He holds gentiles can serve as valid witnesses
B. He assumes it was transmitted before valid Jewish witnesses
C. He doesn't require witnesses for a get at all
D. He follows the opinion that signatures alone effect divorce
Correct: B — Rabbi Shimon holds that if the names are unambiguously gentile, one can assume the get was properly transmitted before two valid Jewish witnesses, following Rabbi Elazar's principle that witnesses of transmission effect the divorce.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does a Jew signing last on a get validate a Samaritan co-signer, but NOT on other documents?
A. Get witnesses are more carefully vetted by the court
B. Get witnesses must sign in each other's presence, so the Jew knew who was co-signing
C. The Torah explicitly permits Samaritan witnesses for gittin
D. Samaritans are considered experts specifically in divorce law
Correct: B — Rav Pappa explains that get witnesses may not sign one without the other — they must sign in each other's presence. So if a Jew signed after a Samaritan, he knew and approved of his co-signer. For other documents, witnesses can sign separately, so the Jew may not have known a Samaritan signed.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How do Abaye and Rava disagree about Rabban Gamliel's ruling in the village of Otnai?
A. Abaye says it was a gift document; Rava says it was a get
B. Abaye emends 'witnesses' to 'witness' (singular); Rava says Rabban Gamliel validates even two Samaritan witnesses
C. Abaye says the ruling was overturned; Rava says it stands
D. Abaye says it involved gentile witnesses; Rava says they were Samaritans
Correct: B — Abaye emends the mishna text from 'its witnesses' to 'its witness' to maintain unanimity that only one Samaritan witness is valid. Rava keeps the text as-is and says Rabban Gamliel genuinely disagrees with the first tanna, validating even two Samaritan witnesses.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What problem does the Gemara identify with gift documents from gentile courts, and how is it resolved?
A. Gifts require three witnesses; resolved by majority rule
B. The document effects acquisition but a gentile document is 'merely a shard'; resolved by dina d'malkhuta dina
C. Gifts are prohibited from gentiles; resolved by a special decree
D. The recipient must be present; resolved by agency law
Correct: B — Unlike a sale where money already changed hands and the document is mere proof, a gift is acquired through the document itself. A gentile-written document should be legally meaningless ('a shard'). Shmuel resolves this with dina d'malkhuta dina — the law of the kingdom is binding.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What key distinction does the Gemara draw between divorce agency and betrothal agency?
A. Divorce requires two agents; betrothal requires only one
B. Divorce agency works against the recipient's will; betrothal requires the woman's consent
C. Only a kohen can serve as agent for betrothal
D. Betrothal agency cannot be retracted while divorce agency can
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes that divorce agency is valid even against the woman's will — a get need not be given with her consent. Betrothal agency, however, requires the woman's consent, as a woman can only be betrothed willingly. This is why the baraita does not list agency among shared halakhot of gittin and betrothal.
