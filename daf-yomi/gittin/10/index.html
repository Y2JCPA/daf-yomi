Gentile Names on a Get: First Tanna vs. Rabbi Shimon

First Tanna
Even if names are clearly gentile (muvhakin), the get is invalid by rabbinic law — we fear it was transmitted before the same gentile witnesses who signed it.
R' Shimon
If the names are unambiguously gentile, we can assume it was transmitted before valid Jewish witnesses — therefore it is valid.
Gemara
Both may agree with R' Elazar that witnesses of transmission (edei mesirah) effect the divorce. Their dispute is only about the presumption when gentile names appear.
Key Principle R' Elazar holds that the witnesses who observe the transmission of the get — not the signatories — are what legally effects the divorce.

Agency: Divorce vs. Betrothal

Divorce (Get)

  • Agency valid against recipient's will
  • Woman's consent is not required
  • Husband can appoint agent to deliver get without her agreement
  • Retraction of agency applies by Torah law (R' Meir)

Betrothal (Kiddushin)

  • Agency not valid against recipient's will
  • Woman's consent is required
  • Cannot betroth a woman through an agent if she objects
  • Retraction also possible but agency structure differs
Distinction This difference explains why the baraita lists retraction among shared halakhot of gittin and manumission but not betrothal — the nature of agency differs fundamentally.

Why Does a Jew Signing Last Only Help for Gittin?

A Jew signs last on a document with a Samaritan
↓
Why did the Jew sign below the Samaritan?
Get / Manumission
Witnesses must sign in each other's presence — the Jew knew who his co-signer was ✅
Other Documents
Witnesses may sign separately — perhaps the Jew left space for an elder and a Samaritan filled it ❌
↓
Rav Pappa: Get witnesses may not sign one without the other
Rav Ashi's Reason This co-signing requirement is a rabbinic decree due to cases where the husband says "all of you are witnesses" — if any one fails to sign, the get is invalid.

How Many Samaritan Witnesses? Abaye vs. Rava

Mishna
An incident: A get was brought before Rabban Gamliel in the village of Otnai with Samaritan witnesses, and he deemed it valid.
R' Elazar
The Sages deemed it valid only with one Samaritan witness — not two.
Abaye
Emend the Mishna: read "its witness" (singular) — even Rabban Gamliel only validated one Samaritan witness.
Rava
No emendation needed. Rabban Gamliel disagrees with the first tanna and validates even two Samaritan witnesses on a get.
Textual Dispute Abaye preserves unanimity by emending the text; Rava preserves the text by recognizing a genuine maḥloket.
