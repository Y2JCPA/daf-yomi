Gittin 69: The Talmudic Medical Compendium

גיטין ס״ט
OVERVIEW This daf presents an extraordinary catalogue of folk remedies and medical treatments for ailments ranging from eye disease to bladder stones. The remedies combine herbal medicine, sympathetic magic, and incantations. A key halakhic-medical moment occurs when Rav Ami challenges Rav Ashi on diagnosing whether blood from the mouth originates from the lungs (treatable) or the liver (fatal).

Remedies for Eye Diseases (Beroketi)

🦂 Dry a seven-colored scorpion in the shade
↓
⚗️ Grind 2 parts stibium + 1 part dried scorpion
↓
👁️ Apply 3 eyebrushes full per eye — no more!
↓
⚠️ Exceeding the dose will cause the eye to burst
WARNING The Gemara emphasizes precise dosage — a rare instance of the Talmud warning about overdose risk in its remedies.

Night Blindness vs. Day Blindness (Hemeralopia)

Night Blindness

  • Tie patient's leg to a dog's leg with hair rope
  • Children throw pottery shards behind him
  • They chant: 'The dog is old, the rooster is foolish'
  • Eat 7 pieces of raw meat from 7 houses
  • Eat them in the city junkyard
  • Untie rope and recite incantation
  • Blow into the dog's eye

Day Blindness

  • Bring 7 animal spleens
  • Roast on a bloodletter's pottery shard
  • Patient sits inside, helper sits outside
  • Patient says: 'Blind man, give me food'
  • Helper responds: 'Seeing man, take and eat'
  • After eating, break the shard
  • If shard is not broken — blindness returns
KEY PATTERN Both remedies use sympathetic magic — transferring the condition away from the patient through ritual action and verbal formulas.

Diagnosing Blood from the Mouth

Patient has blood coming from the mouth
↓
Test with a straw of wheat
↓
Blood sticks to straw
From the lungs (ריאה)
→ Has a remedy
Blood doesn't stick
From the liver (כבד)
→ No remedy — liver has dissolved
RAV AMI'S CHALLENGE Rav Ami questioned: A punctured lung makes an animal tereifa while only complete removal of liver does — so lungs seem more vulnerable! Rav Ashi answered: if blood exits the mouth, the liver must have already dissolved entirely.

Heart Remedies: Palpitations vs. Heaviness

Ravina
For heaviness of the heart: soak 3 barley loaves in kamka less than 40 days old, then drink diluted wine.
Rav Aḥa of Difti
These foods will make the heart palpitate even more!
Ravina
You misheard me — I said this for heaviness, not palpitations!
Ravina
For palpitations: soak 3 wheat loaves in honey, then drink undiluted wine.
Ravina
For heart pain: 3 egg-bulks of mint + 1 of cumin + 1 of sesame seeds.
DISTINCTION The Gemara carefully distinguishes between three heart conditions: palpitations (fast beating), heaviness (sluggishness), and pain — each requiring a different remedy.

Escalating Remedies for Spleen Illness

LevelRemedyIncantation
1st attemptDry 7 leeches in shade; drink 2–3 daily with wineNone
2nd attemptPlace female goat's spleen in oven, stand facing it"As this spleen dries, so shall the spleen of so-and-so"
3rd attemptPlace goat spleen between bricks of a new houseSame formula
4th attemptTake hand of one who died on Shabbat, place on spleen"As this hand dried, so shall the spleen dry"
5th attemptRoast fish in blacksmith's shop, eat with quenching waterNone
Last resortOpen a barrel of wine and drink generouslyNone
RAV AḤA BAR RAVA "If he has a barrel of wine he wouldn't need a remedy!" — Suggesting instead that one should simply eat bread every morning, which benefits the entire body.
