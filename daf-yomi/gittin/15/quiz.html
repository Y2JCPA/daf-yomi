QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Mishna list multiple cases of invalid agent declarations if Daf 2a already taught the requirement?
A. To teach these are invalid even after the fact (b'di'eved)
B. To introduce R' Yehuda's dissenting opinion
C. To distinguish between healthy and deathbed cases
D. To clarify which half of the get matters
Correct: A — The Gemara explains that without this Mishna, we might think the Daf 2a requirement was only l'khatchila (ab initio), but the get would be valid b'di'eved (after the fact). This Mishna teaches it is truly invalid.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, when the agent says 'half was signed in my presence,' which half did he witness?
A. The latter half of the writing, missing the essential first portion (toref)
B. The first witness's signature only
C. The first half of the writing only
D. Half of each witness's signature
Correct: A — Rav Ashi explains the agent witnessed the latter half being written. Since R' Elazar taught that even one line written for her sake suffices, seeing the first half would make it valid — so it must be the latter half, missing the essential toref.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rav Ashi's core principle about authenticating a get?
A. A get cannot be validated by combining agent testimony with signature ratification
B. Two agents can always substitute for one agent's complete testimony
C. Ratification is always preferable to agent testimony
D. The agent must be one of the signatory witnesses
Correct: A — Rav Ashi holds that authentication must be entirely through one method — either fully through the agent's declaration (which carries rabbinic credibility equivalent to two witnesses) or fully through standard signature ratification. Mixing the two is invalid.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rav Ḥisda defend his strict position against the inference from the Mishna's structure?
A. The Mishna uses the style 'not only this but also that' — escalating novelty, so no inference can be drawn from sequence
B. He cites a different Mishna that supports his view
C. He argues the Mishna is discussing a deathbed case
D. He claims R' Yehuda agrees with him
Correct: A — Rav Ḥisda argues the Mishna's style is 'lo zu af zu' (not only this but also that), presenting cases of increasing novelty. Therefore, one cannot infer that the Mishna limits invalidity only to the lesser cases it explicitly mentions.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the tangential discussion about Shabbat domains, what is the halakha regarding combining an embankment with a fence?
A. They combine — the halakha follows Mareimar against Rav Ḥisda
B. They do not combine — the halakha follows Rav Ḥisda
C. They combine only if both are at least 5 tefachim
D. The question is left unresolved (teiku)
Correct: A — The Gemara states explicitly that the halakha follows Mareimar: an embankment of 5 tefachim and a partition of 5 tefachim do join together to form the required 10-tefachim partition, rejecting Rav Ḥisda's non-combination principle in this context.
