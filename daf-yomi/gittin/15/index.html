Rabbi Shimon HaNasi: Return to Sender

Rav Yosef
The halakha follows R' Shimon HaNasi: money should be returned to the heirs of the sender.
Gemara
But we maintain that a shkhiv mera's (deathbed) statement is considered written and delivered!
Rav Yosef
This halakha applies to a healthy person (bari), not someone on his deathbed.
Gemara
But it says 'heirs of the sender' — implying he died. And we say it's a mitzvah to fulfill the dead's wishes!
Resolution
Emend the statement: Return to the sender (not heirs) — the sender is still alive.
Unresolved Was R' Shimon HaNasi himself a Nasi, or did he speak in the name of the Nasi? The Gemara leaves this teiku — unresolved.

Mishna: Invalid Agent Declarations

Agent's StatementStatus
"Written in my presence, not signed in my presence"❌ Invalid
"Signed in my presence, not written in my presence"❌ Invalid
"All written, half signed in my presence"❌ Invalid
"Half written, all signed in my presence"❌ Invalid
Agent 1: "Written" + Agent 2: "Signed"❌ Invalid
Two agents: "Written" + one: "Signed"❌ Invalid (R' Yehuda: Valid)
One: "Written" + two: "Signed"✅ Valid
Why Repeat? The Gemara asks: we already learned this from Daf 2a! Answer: to teach these cases are invalid even after the fact (b'di'eved), not just l'khatchila.

"Half Written in My Presence" — Which Half?

Agent says: Half of the get was written in my presence
↓
Which half did the agent witness?
First half?
R' Elazar: Even one line written for her sake suffices — agent need not check rest
Latter half (Rav Ashi)
He saw the latter half but not the first half, which is the primary section (toref) — Invalid
Rav Ashi The agent witnessed the latter half being written but cannot testify about the essential first portion (toref) of the get.

Can Two Types of Credibility Combine?

Rav Ḥisda
Even if two witnesses testify about the second signature and the court ratifies it — the get is still invalid.
Rava
The concern: people might confuse this with standard document ratification and rely on one witness for two signatures.
Rav Ashi
Even if the agent says: I am the second witness who signed! — still invalid. You cannot combine agent credibility with signature ratification.
Rav Ashi
A get must be authenticated entirely one way: either fully through ratification or fully through the agent's declaration.
Key Principle Ein m'tzarfin — A get cannot be validated by a combination of two types of credibility. It must be wholly one method or the other.

Rav Ḥisda vs. Rava & Rav Ashi: How Far Does Invalidity Extend?

Rav Ḥisda

  • Even full court ratification of 2nd signature doesn't help
  • Strictest position
  • Agent + any ratification = invalid
  • Gemara attempts to disprove from Mishna

Rava

  • Agent + one other witness on 2nd signature = invalid
  • Concern: confusion with standard ratification
  • Could extract money improperly with one witness

Rav Ashi

  • Even if agent IS the second signatory = invalid
  • Principle: can't mix credibility types
  • Either all-agent or all-ratification
Rav Ḥisda's Defense The Mishna's style is "not only this but also that" (lo zu af zu) — escalating novelty — so one cannot infer limits from sequence.
