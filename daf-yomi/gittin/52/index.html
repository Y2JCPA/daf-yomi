Can a Steward Tithe Orphans' Produce?

Mishna
A steward — whether appointed by the father, the court, or self-assumed — must tithe the orphans' produce.
Baraita
"You shall offer" — you, not stewards! A steward may not separate teruma.
Rav Ḥisda
No contradiction! The Mishna speaks of tithing to feed the orphans. The Baraita speaks of tithing to store — which a steward may not do.
Resolution A steward may tithe produce only for the orphans' immediate consumption, not to set aside for future use.

Selling & Buying Fields for Orphans

May a steward sell a distant field to buy a nearby one?
↓
❌ No — even though proximity is normally favorable
↓
May a steward sell a bad field to buy a good one?
↓
❌ No — the new field might become blighted
↓
May a steward sell fields to buy slaves?
❌ No
Fields → slaves is forbidden
✅ Yes (reverse)
Slaves → fields is permitted
(land is more stable)
R. Shimon b. Gamliel Even selling slaves to buy fields is forbidden — the purchased fields may not have secure title (meshappeyan).

Freeing Orphans' Slaves

Tanna Kamma

  • Steward may not free orphans' slaves directly
  • But may sell the slave to another person
  • That buyer may then free the slave

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi

  • Even the slave himself may acquire his freedom
  • He writes a document promising his value
  • He becomes a ger toshav (half-free) until paid
Underlying Concern Orphans' assets must be preserved — freeing a slave is an irreversible loss of property.

Selling Orphans' Property: The Announcement Rule

📢 Normally, orphans' property requires a public announcement before sale
↓
💰 Exception: Head tax (karga) — sell immediately, no announcement needed
↓
🍞 Exception: Sustenance for orphans — sell immediately
↓
⚰️ Exception: Burial expenses — sell immediately
↓
📜 Rav Ashi signed such a deed for Ze'eira the orphan's mother, who sold land for head tax without announcement
Sages of Neharde'a When the need is urgent — taxes, food, or burial — delay itself is the harm, so no announcement is required.
