Gittin 80 — Invalid Divorces: Kingdoms, Names & Errors

DAF OVERVIEW This daf examines categories of invalid gittin: dating by a non-legitimate kingdom, changing names in the document, and a scribe's mix-up of get and receipt. It also addresses retroactive invalidation of rival wives' remarriages when a forbidden relation is discovered to be an ailonit (sexually underdeveloped woman). The Gemara explores whether dating requirements stem from shalom malkhut (peaceful relations with government) and debates the scope of Rabbi Meir vs. the Rabbis regarding these invalidations.

Why Date a Get by the Kingdom?

Ulla
The Sages instituted dating by the ruling kingdom for shalom malkhut — maintaining peaceful relations with the government, which is particular about documents in its domain.
Gemara
But would the Sages be so stringent — invalidating the get and penalizing the woman — merely for the sake of peaceful relations?
R' Meir
Yes! A get dated by a non-legitimate kingdom is invalid and the offspring is a mamzer.
Rabbis
Even dating by a mere city guardsman (santar) suffices — the calendrical system used is irrelevant. She is divorced.
TERMINOLOGY Non-legitimate kingdom = the Roman Empire, which the Gemara says had 'neither their own script nor their own language' — they borrowed from other nations.

The Governor of Bascar: A Test Case

A get is dated by the governor (istandera) of the city of Bascar
↓
Rav Naḥman bar Rav Ḥisda asks Rabba: Is this valid?
↓
Rabba: Even R' Meir concedes this is valid!
↓
Governor (istandera)
Valid — a senior official; it is complimentary to the kingdom
Guardsman (santar)
R' Meir: Invalid — an unimportant official; it is demeaning to the kingdom
DISTINCTION The key factor is whether dating by that official honors or demeans the ruling kingdom.

R' Meir vs. Rabbis: Scope of Invalidity

Rabbi Meir

  • Wrong kingdom dating → get is INVALID
  • Changed names → get is INVALID
  • Offspring = mamzer in both cases
  • Stringent on shalom malkhut

Rabbis (Chakhamim)

  • Wrong kingdom dating → get is VALID
  • Changed names → get is INVALID
  • Offspring unflawed for wrong kingdom
  • Mamzer only for changed names
RAV ASHI'S PROOF The mishna teaches changed names as a separate clause — proving it reflects the Rabbis' view. If R' Meir taught it, he would have combined it with the kingdom clause.

Rival Wives, Ailonit & Retroactive Invalidation

👤 A man dies childless; his widow is forbidden to the yavam (e.g., wife's sister)
↓
👥 The rival wife (tzara) is exempt from yibum/ḥalitza due to the forbidden relation
↓
💍 The rival wife remarries another man without ḥalitza
↓
⚠️ The forbidden relation is discovered to be an ailonit — her marriage was never valid!
↓
🔄 Retroactively, there was no exemption — the rival wife needed ḥalitza all along
↓
🚫 She must leave both her new husband AND the yavam, plus all penalties apply
RAV HAMNUNA A shomeret yavam who had licentious relations is like an adulteress — forbidden to her yavam. The Gemara debates whether the mishna's case of 'marriage' implies the same for illicit relations.
