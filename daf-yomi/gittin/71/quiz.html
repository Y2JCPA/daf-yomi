Gittin 71 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the baraita, who is classified as a 'cheresh' (deaf person)?
A. One who cannot hear or speak
B. One who speaks but cannot hear
C. One who hears but cannot speak
D. One who lost hearing later in life
Correct: B — The baraita defines a cheresh as one who speaks but cannot hear. One who hears but cannot speak is an ilem (mute). Both are considered halakhically competent.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Abaye say the 'from their mouths' requirement does not prevent a deaf-mute from authorizing a get?
A. Because gittin don't require witnesses
B. Because 'from their mouths' applies only to testimony, not divorce
C. Because writing is equivalent to speech by Torah law
D. Because the Rabbis made a special decree for divorce
Correct: B — Abaye distinguishes: the verse 'from their mouths' (Deut. 17:6) applies specifically to court testimony, requiring it to be oral. There is no such Torah-level requirement for divorce instructions.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel's distinction regarding a deaf-mute authorizing a get?
A. Whether the wife consents to the divorce
B. Whether the deaf-mute can also read
C. Whether the deaf-mute was deaf from birth or became deaf later
D. Whether the get is for a first or second marriage
Correct: C — RSBG distinguishes: a deaf-mute from birth cannot give written get instructions, but one who was previously competent and became deaf-mute later can — his writing demonstrates his da'at remains intact.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the mishna, why is the get void when the husband only says 'Write it' to bystanders who then instruct the scribe?
A. Because the husband must personally hand the get to his wife
B. Because bystanders cannot serve as witnesses
C. Because he did not personally instruct the scribe and witnesses
D. Because 'write' is not a valid Hebrew term for divorce
Correct: C — The mishna requires the husband himself to say to the scribe 'Write' and to the witnesses 'Sign.' When bystanders relay these instructions, it raises the question of whether verbal directives can be delegated to an agent.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did the Rabbis forbid divorcing a wife who became an imbecile, according to Rabbi Yitzḥak?
A. Because she cannot consent to the divorce
B. Because an imbecile's marriage is void retroactively
C. So that she will not be treated as 'ownerless' and exploited
D. Because the get cannot be properly delivered to her
Correct: C — Rabbi Yitzḥak says that by Torah law she may be divorced even against her will. However, the Rabbis forbade it to protect her — without a husband to protect her and unable to protect herself, she would be vulnerable to exploitation.
