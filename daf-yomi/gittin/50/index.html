Even if husband has property — guarantor is NOT responsible
Rule In all cases except a ketubah, a guarantor is responsible even if the debtor has no property at the time of the obligation.

Why Is the Ketubah Guarantor Exempt?

Reason 1: Mitzva Intent

  • The guarantor's intent is to perform a mitzva
  • He encourages the woman to consent to marriage
  • He does not truly intend to obligate himself financially

Reason 2: No Real Loss

  • The woman does not lose anything in exchange
  • The husband does not borrow money from the wife
  • Unlike a loan, the ketubah is a Rabbinic institution
  • Therefore: no basis for guarantor liability
Principle A guarantee motivated by mitzva rather than commercial intent, where no actual loss occurs, does not create a binding obligation.

Ketubah Collection: From Husband or Only Orphans?

Mar Zutra
The Mishna's rule that a ketubah is collected from ziburit (inferior land) applies only when collecting from orphans.
Ravina
The Mishna itself gives the reason: 'More than a man wants to marry, a woman wants to be married.'
Ravina
If it applied only to orphans, the reason should be 'because they are orphans' — not the woman's desire to marry!
Gemara
✅ Conclusive refutation (teyuvta) of Mar Zutra. Ketubah is collected from ziburit even from the husband himself.
Halakha A woman collects her ketubah from inferior-quality land (ziburit) whether she collects from her husband or from his orphans.

Can a Creditor Collect Idit (Superior Land) from Orphans?

Mar Zutra b. R' Naḥman
Even if a promissory note stipulates collection from idit (superior land), a creditor collects from orphans only ziburit.
Abaye
This makes sense: normally a creditor gets beinoni (intermediate), but from orphans only ziburit. Kal va-ḥomer — he certainly can't get idit!
Rava
That comparison fails! By Torah law (per Ulla), a creditor only gets ziburit. The Rabbis upgraded it to beinoni to encourage lending.
Rava
For orphans, they reverted to Torah law. But here the note stipulates idit — that's Torah-level by agreement. He should collect idit even from orphans!
Gemara
Avram Ḥoza'a's baraita: From orphans — only ziburit, even for injured parties who by Torah law collect idit.
Gemara
Resolution: That baraita follows R' Yishmael, who appraises the injured party's property, and the injured party's idit equaled the orphans' ziburit.
Ulla's Principle By Torah law, a creditor collects from ziburit, based on Devarim 24:11 — a debtor brings out his least valuable vessel as a pledge.
