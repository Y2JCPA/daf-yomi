Gittin 84 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, if a husband conditions the get on his wife marrying a specific man, whom may she marry?
A. Only the specified man
B. Any man except the specified man
C. Neither the specified man nor anyone else
D. Any man she chooses
Correct: C — Rava rules she may marry neither him (lest people say husbands trade wives) nor another man (since the condition must be fulfilled first). A baraita supports this view.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is R' Yehuda ben Teima's principle regarding impossible conditions in a get?
A. The get is invalid because the condition can never be met
B. The husband is hyperbolizing — the condition is void and the get is valid
C. The wife must attempt the condition before the get can take effect
D. A beit din must assess whether the condition is truly impossible
Correct: B — R' Yehuda ben Teima holds that when a husband attaches an impossible condition, he is merely exaggerating (מגזים) to cause distress, not creating a real condition. The halakha follows his view.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How do Abaye and Rava disagree about a condition to eat pig meat?
A. Abaye says the get is invalid; Rava says it is valid
B. Abaye says it's like an impossible condition (void); Rava says it's a binding condition
C. Both agree the condition is void but disagree about when
D. Abaye says she must eat it; Rava says she must not
Correct: B — Abaye treats a forbidden condition as equivalent to an impossible one — since she shouldn't do it, the condition is void and the get is valid. Rava holds she physically CAN eat pig, so the condition is binding.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does a condition to eat pig meat NOT violate the rule against 'stipulating counter to Torah'?
A. Because pig meat is only rabbinically forbidden
B. Because eating pig is a positive commandment, not negative
C. Because the wife has a choice — she can refuse and remain married, so no Torah law is necessarily uprooted
D. Because the husband did not physically hand her the pig meat
Correct: C — Ravina explains that 'stipulating against Torah' applies only when the condition necessarily uproots a mitzvah. Here, the wife can choose not to eat and simply remain married — no Torah obligation is definitively overridden.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Safra, what type of qualification invalidates a get?
A. Any qualification, whether written or oral
B. Only a qualification stated orally before witnesses
C. Only a qualification written inside the get document itself
D. Only a qualification made after the get is delivered
Correct: C — Rav Safra clarifies that only a written qualification inside the get renders it invalid. An oral restriction — even one stated before the essential part was written — does not invalidate the document.
