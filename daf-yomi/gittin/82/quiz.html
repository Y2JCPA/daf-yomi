QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye, why is only ONE disqualified witness permitted on a folded get?
A. Because ratification can use any three (not necessarily consecutive) witnesses, risking reliance on two invalid ones
B. Because the Torah explicitly limits disqualified witnesses to one
C. Because a relative can only sign once
D. Because Ben Nannas ruled that only one slave may sign
Correct: A — Since the court can pick any three signatures for ratification — not necessarily consecutive ones — allowing more than one disqualified witness could result in the court unknowingly relying on two invalid signatures.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Ravina prove that 'ella' means 'except' rather than 'on the condition'?
A. From a verse in Deuteronomy about divorce
B. From Mishnah Nega'im — interpreting 'ella' as 'on condition' for house-leprosy produces an absurd result
C. From the principle that conditions must be stated twice
D. From Rabbi Eliezer's own ruling on conditional gittin
Correct: B — The Mishnah in Nega'im states all houses get leprosy-impurity 'ella' gentiles' houses. If 'ella' meant 'on condition,' it would absurdly mean Jewish houses are impure only on condition that gentile houses aren't — which makes no sense.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the Rabbis' response to Rabbi Yochanan's proof from Leviticus 21:7 ('a woman divorced from her husband')?
A. The verse only applies to Kohanim, not to ordinary Israelites
B. Kehunah disqualification is different — even an otherwise invalid divorce disqualifies a woman from marrying a Kohen
C. The verse refers only to a complete divorce
D. Rabbi Yochanan misquoted the verse
Correct: B — The Rabbis argue that priestly disqualification is a special category — even an invalid divorce disqualifies a woman from the kehunah. This cannot prove that such a divorce is valid for all purposes.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rabbi Abba resolve whether an exception in betrothal follows the same rules as divorce?
A. He leaves it unresolved (teiku)
B. He rules that betrothal is always stricter than divorce
C. He derives from the juxtaposition of 'she departs' and 'she becomes' that both divorce and betrothal follow the same rules
D. He rules that betrothal cannot have exceptions under any opinion
Correct: C — Rabbi Abba concludes that the juxtaposition of the clauses in Deuteronomy 24:2 — 'and she departs his house' and 'and she becomes another man's wife' — requires that divorce and betrothal be treated identically regarding exceptions.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In Abaye's levirate marriage case, why is Shimon's betrothal considered ineffective?
A. Because Shimon was a disqualified witness
B. Because betrothal with exceptions is always invalid
C. Because Shimon's betrothal added no new prohibition — she was already forbidden to all men except Shimon through Reuven's betrothal
D. Because two brothers cannot betroth the same woman
Correct: C — Reuven's betrothal already made her forbidden to all men except Shimon. When Shimon then betrothed her saying 'forbidden to all except Reuven,' it did not create any new restriction she didn't already have, so it never took effect. She is therefore the wife of only one dead man and may perform yibum with Levi.
