Divorce with Exceptions: Does Partial Severance Count?

Rabbi Eliezer
A get that permits her to marry all men except one is valid — even partial severance is divorce
Chachamim
The get is invalid — divorce requires complete severance (keritut) of the marital bond
Ravina
The word ella means 'except' — proven from Mishnah Nega'im about houses and leprosy
Rabbi Abba
Does the same dispute apply to betrothal with an exception? The juxtaposition of 'she departs' and 'she becomes' suggests yes
Core Issue Can a husband sever the marriage bond while retaining a single restriction on whom his ex-wife may marry?

Abaye's Rules for Signing a Folded Get

📜 A relative may sign a folded and tied get (get mekushar)
↓
✍️ The relative can sign at the beginning, middle, or end — no designated place
↓
🔍 Ratification requires confirming any three witnesses — not necessarily consecutive
↓
⚠️ Because non-consecutive witnesses can be used, only one disqualified witness is allowed
↓
👤 Rabbi Ami ruled: Complete a bare get with even a slave, per Ben Nannas
Key Insight Since the court can pick any three signatures for ratification, allowing multiple disqualified witnesses would risk relying on two invalid signatures.

The Mishna's Core Dispute: Divorce with an Exception

Husband
"You are permitted to marry any man except (ella) so-and-so"
Rabbi Eliezer
Permitted to remarry — even partial permission constitutes divorce
Chachamim
Prohibited from remarrying — the bond is not fully severed, so she remains his wife
Mishna
Remedy: Take back the get, give it again, and say "You are permitted to any man" without exception
Mishna
If the exception was written inside the get — even if erased — the get is invalid
Practical Halakha The exception must never appear in the written text of the get. Even erasing it does not fix the document.

What Does 'Ella' (אלא) Mean?

Husband says: "You may marry anyone ella so-and-so"
Ella = Except
He excluded one man from the divorce — left out part of the get
Ella = On Condition
Full divorce conditioned on her not marrying that man
↓
Ravina proves from Mishnah Nega'im 12:1:
"All houses get leprosy-impurity ella gentiles' houses"
↓
If ella = 'on condition' → nonsensical result: Jewish houses impure only if gentile houses aren't?!
↓
Conclusion: Ella means 'except'
Significance This determines the exact nature of the dispute: Rabbi Eliezer validates even an exception-based divorce, which is the stronger position.

Rabbi Eliezer's Scriptural Sources

Rabbi Yannai's Verse

  • "She goes and becomes another man's wife" (Deut. 24:2)
  • Even if permitted to only one other man — she is divorced
  • Partial severance still counts as keritut

Rabbi Yochanan's Verse

  • "A woman divorced from her husband" may not marry a Kohen (Lev. 21:7)
  • Even divorce only 'from her husband' — not from all men — disqualifies her from kehunah
  • All the more so: exception of one man is valid divorce

Rabbis' Response

  • "Another man" means any man — she must be free to marry all
  • Kehunah disqualification is different — even invalid divorce disqualifies
  • Full keritut (severance) is required for a valid get
Note Both Rabbi Yannai and Rabbi Yochanan support Rabbi Eliezer, but from different verses — and the Rabbis have counter-arguments for each.
