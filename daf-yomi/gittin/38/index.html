Can Gentiles Acquire Each Other as Slaves?

Gentiles cannot acquire Jews as slaves
Gentiles cannot acquire each other as the slave himself
↓
Can they acquire each other for labor rights (ma'aseh yadav)?
↓
Kal va'chomer: If a gentile can acquire a Jew for labor (Lev. 25:47), surely a gentile can acquire a gentile!
↓
Challenge: Maybe only via money, not via conquest/possession?
↓
Rav Pappa
Ammon & Moab's land was purified through Sihon's conquest → conquest works for gentile-on-gentile
Proof for Jews
Num. 21:1 — Canaanites captured Israelites in war → conquest acquires even Jews
Principle Conquest is a valid mode of acquisition for slaves — derived from Sihon's purification of Ammon/Moab's land and from the Canaanite capture of Israelites.

Slave Who Escapes Prison — Is He Free?

R' Shemen bar Abba / R' Yochanan
A slave who escapes from prison is emancipated. Moreover, his master is forced to write him a get shichrur (bill of manumission).
Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel (Mishna)
A slave redeemed from captivity — whether redeemed as a slave or as a free person — remains a slave.
Rabba bar bar Chana / R' Yochanan
Everywhere RSBG rules in our Mishna, the halakha follows him — except three cases. This isn't one of them!
Gemara
But then R' Yochanan contradicts himself! He says the escaped slave is free, yet rules like RSBG that redeemed slaves stay enslaved?
Tension Two rulings of R' Yochanan appear contradictory — one freeing the escaped slave, the other keeping the redeemed slave enslaved. This sets up the Abaye vs. Rava resolution.
5 / 9

Resolving R' Yochanan's Contradiction

Abaye's Resolution

  • Mishna (RSBG) refers to before the owner's despair (ye'ush)
  • R' Yochanan's prison escape ruling is after despair
  • No contradiction — different scenarios
  • Clean resolution with no difficulty

Rava's Resolution

  • Mishna is after despair — yet slave stays enslaved
  • Why? Chizkiyya's concern: slaves might let themselves be captured hoping for emancipation
  • Prison escape is different: the slave risked death to escape
  • Someone willing to die to escape won't voluntarily seek capture
Key Distinction Rava's resolution hinges on Chizkiyya's takanah: we don't free redeemed slaves lest they engineer their own capture — but an escapee who risked his life clearly didn't seek captivity.

The Story of Shmuel's Maidservant

📍 Shmuel's maidservant was taken captive
↓
💰 Others redeemed her to be a maidservant and sent her back to Shmuel
↓
📩 They sent a message: 'We hold like RSBG — she remains your maidservant. Even per the Rabbis, we redeemed her as a maidservant.'
↓
⏳ They assumed it was before Shmuel's despair — but it was actually after
↓
✅ Shmuel did not enslave her — she was a free woman in every respect
↓
📜 He did not even require a get shichrur — consistent with his general view on ye'ush
Shmuel's Position Shmuel held that after the owner's despair, the slave is fully free — no bill of manumission needed.

Is It Permitted to Emancipate a Slave?

Rav Yehuda / Shmuel
Anyone who emancipates his slave violates a positive mitzva: 'Of them may you take your bondmen forever' (Lev. 25:46).
Gemara (Objection)
R' Eliezer entered a synagogue, couldn't find a minyan, and freed his slave to complete the quorum of ten!
Gemara (Answer)
Freeing a slave for the sake of a mitzva is different — a mitzva d'rabbim (communal obligation) overrides.
R' Yishmael
'Forever' is optional (reshut) — you may keep them forever, not that you must.
R' Akiva
'Forever' is an obligation (chovah) — you must keep them as slaves permanently.
Gemara
R' Eliezer explicitly holds it's an obligation — yet he freed his slave for the minyan. This proves: mitzva overrides the prohibition.
Halakha It is generally prohibited to free a Canaanite slave, but performing a mitzva — such as completing a minyan — overrides this prohibition.
