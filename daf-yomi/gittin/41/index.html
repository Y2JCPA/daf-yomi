The Half-Slave Problem: From Lien to Liberation

A slave is half-free or designated as a creditor's lien
↓
Can the slave marry?
Neither a maidservant nor a free woman
↓
No marriage possible
The world was created for habitation (Isaiah 45:18)
Solution
Court forces the master to emancipate — tikkun ha-olam
KEY THEME Gittin 41 explores who bears the cost when a half-slave must be freed — the master, the creditor, or the slave himself — and how different forms of emancipation work legally.

Who Writes the Promissory Note?

Tanna Kamma
The slave writes a promissory note for his own value to the creditor. The master who emancipated him is exempt — damaging a friend's lien carries no liability.
R' Shimon b. Gamliel
The original master who emancipated him writes the note and pays. One who damages his friend's lien (mazik shi'abudo shel chavero) is liable.
UNDERLYING DISPUTE Is damaging someone's lien — where you harm the value of collateral that doesn't belong to you — considered actionable damage?

Rav vs. Ulla: Who Emancipated the Slave?

Rav's Reading

  • The creditor (2nd master) emancipated the slave
  • Court forces creditor to complete the emancipation
  • Dispute: damage to a friend's lien (shi'abud)
  • Rav calls the creditor 'his master' — he controls the slave

Ulla's Reading

  • The creditor emancipated but had no authority to do so
  • Court forces the original master to formally free him
  • Dispute: is non-evident damage (garmi) considered damage?
  • Ulla objects: a creditor is not truly a 'master'
KEY DIFFERENCE Rav frames the dispute around lien damage; Ulla frames it around non-evident damage (hezeik she-eino nikar). Each reading hinges on the precise meaning of 'master' and 'emancipation' in the Mishna.

Designated Field Flooded — Can the Creditor Collect?

Debtor designates a specific field as repayment for a debt
↓
A river floods and destroys the field permanently
↓
Can the creditor collect from other property?
↓
Ami Shappir / R' Yochanan
Creditor does NOT collect from other property — he bears the loss
Shmuel's Father
Creditor DOES collect from other property
RESOLUTION Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak limits R' Yochanan's ruling to where the debtor explicitly said: 'You will have payment only from this field.' Otherwise, the creditor may collect from other property.

The Half-Slave's Impossible Situation

לא תהו בראה — לשבת יצרה
BEIT HILLEL RETRACTS A half-slave cannot marry a maidservant — his free half forbids it. He cannot marry a free woman — his slave half forbids it. Should he remain idle? But the Torah says: 'He formed it to be inhabited' (Isaiah 45:18)! Therefore the court forces the master to free him, and the slave writes a promissory note for half his value. Beit Hillel ultimately retracted to agree with Beit Shammai.

Half-Emancipation: Bill vs. Money

MethodR' Yehuda HaNasiRabbis (Chakhamim)
Bill of Manumission (shtar)✅ Acquires half-freedom — derived from juxtaposition (hekkesh) with money in Lev. 19:20❌ Does NOT acquire — derived from gezeira shava of 'lah' with divorce: just as you can't half-divorce a wife, you can't half-free a slave
Money Redemption (kesef)✅ Acquires half-freedom✅ Acquires half-freedom — all agree
Derivation MethodJuxtaposition (hekkesh) is preferable here because the gezeira shava is refutableVerbal analogy (gezeira shava) is generally preferable
RAV YOSEF REFUTED Rav Yosef tried to limit the dispute to money only, but a baraita explicitly states R' Yehuda HaNasi and the Rabbis disagree about a bill of manumission — a conclusive refutation (teyuvta).
