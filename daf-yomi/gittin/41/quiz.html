QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Tanna Kamma, why does the slave (not the master) write the promissory note?
A. Because the master who freed him is exempt — damaging a friend's lien carries no liability
B. Because the slave volunteered for emancipation
C. Because the creditor forgave the debt
D. Because the court always assigns the note to the freed party
Correct: A — The Tanna Kamma holds that one who damages his friend's lien (mazik shi'abudo shel chavero) is exempt. Since the master isn't liable for damaging the creditor's security interest, the slave himself must write the note.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the key difference between Rav's and Ulla's explanations of the Mishna?
A. Rav says the master freed the slave; Ulla says the slave freed himself
B. Rav frames the dispute around lien damage; Ulla frames it around non-evident damage
C. Rav discusses a full slave; Ulla discusses a half-slave
D. Rav follows Beit Shammai; Ulla follows Beit Hillel
Correct: B — Rav explains the dispute as being about damage to a creditor's lien (shi'abud), while Ulla explains it as concerning non-evident damage (hezeik she-eino nikar) — whether causing someone to be forced to emancipate counts as damage.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Beit Hillel ultimately retract and agree that a half-slave must be freed?
A. Because Beit Shammai had a stronger textual proof
B. Because the half-slave cannot marry anyone and the world was created for procreation
C. Because the master refused to keep a half-slave
D. Because Roman law required full emancipation
Correct: B — A half-slave can marry neither a maidservant (his free half forbids it) nor a free woman (his slave half forbids it). Since the world was created 'to be inhabited' (Isaiah 45:18), the court forces full emancipation.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does R' Yehuda HaNasi prefer the hekkesh (juxtaposition) over the gezeira shava for half-emancipation via a bill?
A. He holds that hekkesh is always stronger than gezeira shava
B. The gezeira shava comparing emancipation to divorce is refutable — a woman cannot leave marriage via money, but a slave can
C. He received a tradition from his teacher rejecting the gezeira shava
D. The gezeira shava only applies to full emancipation, not partial
Correct: B — Although gezeira shava is generally preferable, this particular analogy between divorce and emancipation is refutable: a woman cannot leave marriage via money at all, while a slave can leave servitude via money. This fundamental difference weakens the analogy.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Shimon ben Gamliel, why can a woman seize a designated field that was sold, but a creditor cannot?
A. A woman's ketubah has higher legal priority than any debt
B. It is not the way of a woman to go to court, so she is given a more direct remedy
C. The field was originally her property before the marriage
D. A creditor implicitly waives his rights when the field is sold
Correct: B — R' Shimon ben Gamliel distinguishes between them because 'it is not the way of a woman to go to court' (ein darkah shel isha lachazer al batei din). Since pursuing other property requires litigation, the woman is given the stronger right to seize the designated field directly.
