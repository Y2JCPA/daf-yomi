Gittin 22: Pots, Borders & Who Writes the Get

קיום הגט אינו אלא בחותמיו
DAF OVERVIEW This daf explores the legal fiction of a perforated pot — is a plant in it "attached to the ground"? The Gemara applies this to divorce law, property acquisition, and tithing obligations at the border of Eretz Yisrael. Amud B shifts to whether a get on erasable material is valid and concludes with the Mishna: anyone may physically write a get, because validity depends on the signatories, not the scribe.

Get Written on a Leaf in a Perforated Pot

Rava
A get written on a leaf attached in a perforated pot is invalid — the Sages decreed against it lest one detach the leaf and give it alone.
Implication
If he gives her the entire pot with the leaf inside, it should be valid — he can take the pot and hand it to her.
Rava
Even so, we decree it invalid. A get fully written while attached to the ground is invalid by consensus.
PRINCIPLE A perforated pot connects its contents to the ground — so a get written on its leaf is treated as written on something attached (מחובר).

Perforated Pot on the Border of Eretz Yisrael

Abaye
We follow the perforation (נקב) — if the hole faces Eretz Yisrael, the produce is obligated in tithes.
Rava
We follow the branches (נוף) — if the growth extends outside Eretz Yisrael, it is exempt from tithes.
Gemara
If the plant took root in the ground, all agree we follow the roots. The dispute is only when it did not take root.
CONTEXT The perforated pot creates a legal connection to the ground beneath — but does the point of connection or the location of growth determine status?

Two Gardens on Terraces — Who Owns the Vegetables?

R' Meir

  • Vegetables belong to the upper garden owner
  • Reason: If he removes his earth, the vegetables die
  • He controls the source of nutrients

R' Yehuda

  • Vegetables belong to the lower garden owner
  • Reason: If he fills his airspace with earth, the vegetables are buried
  • He controls the space of growth
DISTINCTION The Gemara distinguishes: this dispute is about control over the plant's survival, not about defining where it grows — so it doesn't resolve the Abaye/Rava border question.

Get on Erasable Material (Amud B)

A get is written on erased paper (ניר מחוק) — material that can be forged
↓
Is it valid? Per R' Elazar (Tanna): Yes — witnesses of transmission (עדי מסירה) read the get before delivery
↓
R' Elazar (Amora)
Valid only if brought to court immediately. After time, we fear she erased a stipulation.
R' Yochanan
Valid even after 10 days — witnesses would remember any stipulation and refuse to verify a forgery.
SECOND DISPUTE R' Elazar (Amora): Erasable surfaces valid for gittin only — other documents must last (Jeremiah 32:14). R' Yochanan: Valid for all documents — the verse is merely good advice (עצה טובה).
