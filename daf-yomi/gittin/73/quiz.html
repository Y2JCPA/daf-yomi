QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Elazar in the name of Rav, if a shechiv mera proceeds from one illness directly to another, what is the status of his gift?
A. The gift is void since it was a new illness
B. The gift requires reassessment by a court
C. The gift is valid — the second illness stems from the first
D. The gift is valid only if witnesses confirm continuity
Correct: C — R' Elazar says in the name of Rav that when a shechiv mera proceeds directly from one illness to another without walking with his staff in between, his gift is valid because the second illness is clearly a continuation of the first.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara explain the Sages' ability to permit a married woman to remarry even when Torah law would nullify the get?
A. They invoke a special Torah verse granting emergency powers
B. Anyone who betroths does so contingent on rabbinic approval — they can expropriate the betrothal
C. They rely on a doubt (safek) to be lenient
D. They apply the principle of pikuach nefesh
Correct: B — The Gemara invokes the principle 'kol ha-mekadesh ada'ata de-rabbanan mekadesh' — all betrothal is performed contingent upon the Sages' approval, giving them power to retroactively annul the marriage.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the difference between 'if I die from this illness' and 'if I am not healed from this illness' when a house collapses on the husband?
A. Both formulations produce a valid get
B. Both formulations produce an invalid get
C. The first is invalid (he didn't die from illness); the second is valid (he was never healed)
D. The first is valid; the second is invalid
Correct: C — With 'if I die from this illness,' the condition is unfulfilled because he died from the collapse, not the illness. With 'if I am not healed,' the condition is fulfilled because he was indeed never healed from his illness.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rava, what is the status of an uncommon unavoidable accident (ones she'eino matzui) in a contractual condition?
A. It is included in the condition and the condition is fulfilled
B. It is not included in the condition — people don't intend to cover uncommon events
C. It depends on the specific wording of the condition
D. It requires a rabbinical court to determine case by case
Correct: B — Rava rules that an uncommon unavoidable accident is not included within a general condition. In the land sale case, the seller who accepted responsibility for accidents did not need to pay when the government diverted a river through the land.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rava's reading of the baraita about seclusion after divorce, what does R' Yosei son of R' Yehuda hold?
A. Concern for betrothal only when intercourse is witnessed
B. No concern for betrothal even with intercourse
C. Concern for betrothal even without witnessing intercourse — mere seclusion suffices
D. Concern only when the husband explicitly states intent to remarry
Correct: C — According to Rava's interpretation, R' Yosei son of R' Yehuda holds that even if witnesses did not see intercourse, mere seclusion in the dark creates concern that they may have performed a betrothal.
