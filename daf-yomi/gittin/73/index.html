Does Recovery Nullify the Get?

Rav Huna
If the husband recovers from his illness, the conditional get is nullified — even without an explicit condition.
Rabba & Rava
The get remains valid even if he recovers — there is a rabbinic decree lest people say a get can take effect after death.
Gemara
But by Torah law the get is nullified! How can Rabbis permit a married woman to remarry?
Gemara
Kol ha-mekadesh ada'ata de-rabbanan mekadesh — betrothal is contingent on rabbinic will. They can expropriate the kiddushin retroactively.
Principle The Sages hold the extraordinary power to retroactively annul a marriage — the foundation for many rabbinic interventions in divorce law.

Kiddushei Kesef vs. Kiddushei Biah

Betrothal with Money (Kesef)

  • Sages expropriate the money (hefker beit din hefker)
  • Money is rendered ownerless retroactively
  • Betrothal never took effect
  • Straightforward mechanism

Betrothal with Intercourse (Biah)

  • Ravina's challenge: What can the Sages expropriate?
  • Rav Ashi's answer: Sages declare the intercourse to be licentious (be'ilat zenut)
  • Retroactively not an act of kiddushin
  • Radical rabbinic power over personal status
Ravina → Rav Ashi Even betrothal through intercourse can be annulled — the Sages retroactively redefine the act itself as non-kiddushin.

Seclusion After Divorce: Concern for Re-Betrothal?

Rav Nachman / Rabba bar Avuh
If they saw intercourse — concern for betrothal. If he then gave money — concern only for licentiousness, not betrothal.
R' Yosei b'R' Yehuda
Even if he gave money after intercourse, we are also concerned for betrothal — perhaps the money was kiddushin.
Abaye
The baraita never mentions money! Rather: intercourse alone — first tanna says no betrothal concern; R' Yosei b'R' Yehuda says yes.
Rava
R' Yosei b'R' Yehuda says even without witnessing intercourse — mere seclusion creates concern for betrothal.
Three Interpretations Rav Nachman, Abaye, and Rava each read the same baraita differently — each reading changes which tanna aligns with Beit Hillel.
