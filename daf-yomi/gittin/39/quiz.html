Gittin 39 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Meir, why does the consecration of a slave take effect on his monetary value?
A. Because a slave is movable property
B. Because a person does not make a statement of consecration for naught
C. Because the Torah explicitly states slaves can be consecrated
D. Because the slave consents to the consecration
Correct: B — R' Meir's principle is 'ein adam motzi devarav l'vatalah' — a person doesn't speak for nothing. The statement is interpreted to have meaningful effect, i.e., consecrating the slave's monetary value.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the first tanna hold that me'ilah does NOT apply to a consecrated slave?
A. Because the slave was never truly consecrated
B. Because me'ilah only applies to Temple vessels
C. Because a slave is equivalent to land, and me'ilah doesn't apply to land
D. Because the slave's owner retains full rights
Correct: C — Slaves are compared to land in several areas of halakha. Since me'ilah (misuse of consecrated property) does not apply to land, it likewise does not apply to slaves.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the dispute between R' Shimon b. Gamliel and the first tanna regarding the slave's hair?
A. Whether hair can be consecrated independently
B. Whether hair ready to be cut is considered as if already detached
C. Whether a slave's hair belongs to the master or the slave
D. Whether cutting hair constitutes damage to consecrated property
Correct: B — R' Shimon b. Gamliel holds that hair ready to be cut is considered 'as if already cut' — making it movable property subject to me'ilah. The first tanna holds it is still attached and retains the status of land.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did R' Zeira challenge whether rulings were heard explicitly or derived by inference?
A. Because inferred rulings are always invalid
B. Because the gezeira shava could support opposite conclusions
C. Because R' Yehoshua ben Levi was known to be unreliable
D. Because explicit rulings carry a Torah-level obligation
Correct: B — The wife-slave analogy can be drawn in two directions: wife freed regardless of age → slave too (against Abba Shaul), OR wife freed from prohibition but not monetary bond → slave too (supporting Abba Shaul). An inference is unreliable when it can go either way.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Yehuda HaNasi, when the master despairs of recovering his slave, how can the slave be emancipated?
A. By money or by bill of manumission
B. Only by a bill of manumission
C. Automatically — despair itself frees the slave
D. Only by paying his monetary value to the Temple
Correct: B — Normally both money and a bill of manumission work. But when the master despairs, the monetary hold is abrogated — so the money path is closed, and only a bill of manumission (get shichrur) can complete the emancipation.
