Gittin 31 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, what may one rely upon when using previously set-aside produce for tithes?
A. The presumption (chazakah) that the produce still exists
B. Witnesses who confirm the produce is intact
C. A rabbinical decree that lost produce is still valid
D. The principle that tithes can be separated from any produce
Correct: A — The Mishna teaches that one may separate teruma or tithe based on the presumption that the designated produce or money is still extant, without needing to verify each time.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does R' Yochanan interpret 'from the time until the same time' (מעת לעת)?
A. From when it was set aside until 24 hours later
B. From when it was found lost, going back 24 hours
C. From one festival to the next festival
D. From the time of separation until the next check
Correct: B — R' Yochanan holds that the 24-hour retroactive concern runs backward from the moment the loss was discovered. Any produce rendered fit within that window must be re-tithed.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say the mikveh ruling disagrees with R' Elazar the Tanna?
A. The mikveh ruling requires checking every day
B. The mikveh ruling goes back to the last measurement, not just 24 hours
C. The mikveh ruling applies only in the public domain
D. The mikveh ruling does not require re-immersion
Correct: B — The mikveh mishna deems impure ALL items immersed since the mikveh was last measured — a broader retroactive scope than R' Elazar's 24-hour (me'et le'et) window.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Yehuda, why must wine set aside for tithes be checked at three specific times?
A. Because the Torah requires three annual inspections
B. Because weather changes at these times may cause wine to turn to vinegar
C. Because these are the times when wine is sold to partners
D. Because new wine is produced at these three seasons
Correct: B — R' Yehuda identifies three seasonal transitions — after Sukkot (east wind), blossom-fall, and grape-filling — when weather changes risk turning wine into vinegar, making it unfit for its designated purpose.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Rabba reject Rav Yehuda's interpretation that ḥarishit means 'plowing' the sea?
A. Because the word ḥarishit cannot mean plowing grammatically
B. Because the verse continues with Jonah suffering from heat, not wind strength
C. Because the sea cannot actually be plowed
D. Because Jonah was on land, not at sea, at that point
Correct: B — Rabba notes that the continuation of the verse — 'the sun beat upon Jonah's head' — indicates extreme heat, not powerful wind. He therefore explains ḥarishit as 'silencing' all other winds, leaving only the hot east wind.
