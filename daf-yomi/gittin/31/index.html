Setting Aside Produce for Tithes: What If It's Lost?

One sets aside produce or money for separating terumot, tithes, or redeeming ma'aser sheni
↓
He may rely on the presumption (chazakah) that the produce/money still exists
↓
What if the produce/money is discovered lost?
R' Yochanan
Concern goes back 24 hours before discovery
R' Elazar b. Antigonus
Concern goes back to 24 hours after it was originally set aside
Key Issue How far back must one retroactively re-tithe produce that was rendered fit using the now-lost designation?

Teruma vs. Teruma of the Tithe

Standard Teruma (תרומה גדולה)

  • Separated by the homeowner
  • Taken by estimate — no precise measurement required
  • Can be designated by thought alone
  • No need to physically separate before consuming remainder

Teruma of the Tithe (תרומת מעשר)

  • Normally separated by the Levite
  • Learned from the verse: also taken by estimate
  • Also designated by thought
  • The homeowner also has permission to separate it
Derivation Since the Torah mentions both terumot together, they are compared: rules of standard teruma apply to teruma of the tithe as well.

Mishna: Setting Aside Produce for Future Tithes

📦 A person sets aside produce or money for future teruma, tithes, or ma'aser sheni redemption
↓
✅ He may later use it, relying on the presumption (chazakah) that it still exists
↓
❌ If he discovers it was lost — he must re-tithe retroactively
↓
⏳ Retroactive concern applies 'from the time until the same time' (מעת לעת)
↓
🔁 All produce rendered fit during that window must be tithed again
R' Elazar This ruling is the statement of the Tanna R' Elazar in the Mishna.

What Does 'From Time to Time' (מעת לעת) Mean?

R' Yochanan
It means from the time it was checked and found lost, going back 24 hours. Any produce rendered fit within those 24 hours must be re-tithed.
R' Elazar b. Antigonus (in name of R' Elazar b. R' Yannai)
It means from the time it was originally set aside until 24 hours later. Only produce rendered fit after that first 24-hour period is in doubt.
Gemara
The Mishna's wording — 'from the time until the same time' — fits R' Yochanan's reading more naturally. R' Elazar b. Antigonus's reading is difficult with the Mishna's language.
Practical Difference R' Yochanan's window moves with discovery; R' Elazar b. Antigonus's window is fixed from the moment of designation.

R' Yehuda: Three Times to Check Wine for Vinegar

Time of YearSeasonReason
When the east wind blows after SukkotTishrei (Autumn)Weather change may sour wine
When blossoms fall and grape buds emergeSpringSeasonal shift affects fermentation
When water enters unripe grapesSummerMoisture change risks souring
Exception If the summer heat continues until Sukkot (unusual weather), one does not check after Sukkot — the east wind concern doesn't apply.

Mikveh Found Deficient — Retroactive Impurity

A mikveh was known to have 40 se'a
↓
People immersed and were deemed pure
↓
Mikveh is later measured and found deficient
↓
All items immersed since it was last measured are retroactively impure — whether in private or public domain
R' Elazar (Amora) This mikveh ruling disagrees with R' Elazar the Tanna — it goes back to the last measurement, not merely 24 hours.
