Borders, Expertise & Availability: Who Must Declare?

Gemara
The mishna must follow R' Elazar — he concedes that existing signatures must be valid (ziyufo migufo)
Rav Ashi
Actually, the mishna follows R' Yehuda, who requires both writing and signing for her sake
Gemara
Why didn't we say R' Yehuda first? Because we prefer unattributed mishnayot = R' Meir, and halakha = R' Elazar
Rabba
The Tannaim all agree the reason is lishmah — they argue over which border towns are 'learned'
Rava
They all agree the reason is ratification — they argue over which towns have available witnesses
Key Theme The Gemara shows that both Rabba and Rava can independently explain every Tannaitic opinion in the mishna, so no proof can be brought from the Tannaim to settle their dispute.

Rabba vs. Rava: Resolving the Tannaitic Dispute

Rabba — Lishmah

  • All Tannaim agree: reason is expertise in lishmah
  • First Tanna: nearby towns are learned
  • R' Gamliel: only towns within the boundary are learned
  • R' Eliezer: don't distinguish areas — treat all non-settlement as overseas

Rava — Ratification

  • All Tannaim agree: reason is witness availability
  • First Tanna: nearby towns' witnesses are available
  • R' Gamliel: only boundary towns have available witnesses
  • R' Eliezer: even boundary towns — witnesses not considered available
Takeaway Neither Rabba nor Rava can be proven right from the Tannaim — each resolves every opinion consistently with his own reasoning.

Delivering a Get FROM Eretz Yisrael to Overseas

First Tanna
One who delivers a get from EY to overseas does not need to declare
Later Rabbis
Even delivering from EY requires the declaration — as a decree
Gemara
What's the concern?
Later Rabbis
People might confuse delivering (EY→overseas) with bringing (overseas→EY) and skip the declaration when it's truly needed
According to Rava The Later Rabbis don't actually disagree — they merely explain the First Tanna's reasoning. The First Tanna taught concisely but agrees the declaration is required.

Region-to-Region Inferences

Mishna: Region to region overseas → must declare
↓
What about within one region overseas?
↓
Rava's Reading
Within one region overseas — no declaration needed (witnesses available nearby)
Rabba's Reading
Don't infer that. Instead infer: region to region within EY — no declaration needed
Gemara's Refinement The inference about EY teaches that no declaration is needed even ab initio (le-khatchila), not merely after the fact (be-di'avad).
