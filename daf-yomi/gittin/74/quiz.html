QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yochanan, what is the practical difference between R' Meir and R' Yosei regarding a woman whose conditional divorce is uncertain?
A. Whether the paramour brings a provisional guilt-offering (asham talui)
B. Whether the woman needs a second get
C. Whether the husband must provide sustenance
D. Whether the woman may remarry
Correct: A — R' Yochanan explains that according to R' Meir the uncertainty will eventually be resolved (so no asham talui), while R' Yosei holds the uncertainty is permanent, requiring a provisional guilt-offering.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
If the get is torn or lost before the wife pays the 200 dinars, what is the difference between Rav Huna and Rav Yehuda?
A. Rav Huna says she needs a new get; Rav Yehuda says she does not
B. Rav Huna says she does not need a new get; Rav Yehuda says she does
C. Both agree she needs a new get
D. Both agree the original get is still valid
Correct: B — Rav Huna holds the divorce takes effect retroactively from when the get was given, so no second get is needed. Rav Yehuda holds divorce only takes effect upon payment, so the destroyed get was never effective and a new one is required.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the Tzaidan incident, what do the Rabbis (according to R' Shimon ben Gamliel's citation) say the wife should do when she has lost her husband's coat?
A. She must find the exact coat or the get is invalid
B. She should give him the monetary value of the coat
C. She should give him a replacement coat of equal quality
D. The condition is automatically waived
Correct: B — R' Shimon ben Gamliel cites the Tzaidan incident where the Sages ruled she gives the monetary value of the lost coat to fulfill the condition. However, the unnamed Rabbis in the Mishna actually disagree and require the specific item.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When a husband says 'This is your get on condition you give me 200 dinars' and later says 'It is waived for you,' what does R' Yochanan rule?
A. She is divorced because the husband forgave the debt
B. She is not divorced because the condition was never fulfilled
C. She is divorced according to the Rabbis but not according to R' Shimon ben Gamliel
D. It depends on whether she attempted to pay
Correct: B — R' Yochanan rules she is not divorced. Waiving a condition is not the same as the condition being fulfilled. The get required an act of giving, and that act never took place.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is R' Zeira's halakha (in the name of Shmuel) that distinguishes the Rabbis from R' Yosei?
A. The husband must bring a sin-offering
B. The wife may remarry immediately
C. The husband is obligated to provide the wife sustenance
D. The paramour must bring a guilt-offering
Correct: C — R' Zeira says in Shmuel's name: wherever the Sages say 'uncertainty whether divorced or not,' the husband must provide sustenance. This specific formulation is used only by the Rabbis, not by R' Yosei, making it the key practical difference between their otherwise similar positions.
