Conditional Divorce: When Does the Get Take Effect?

Husband gives conditional get
"On condition that you give me 200 dinars"
↓
When does the divorce take effect?
Rav Huna
Immediately upon receiving the get — payment is a subsequent obligation
Rav Yehuda
Only when she pays — no payment means no divorce
Core Issue This daf explores multiple dimensions of conditional divorce: timing, uncertain status, substitution of objects, and waiver of conditions.
3 / 9

Status of the Paramour's Sin-Offering

R' Meir
The paramour does not bring an asham talui (provisional guilt-offering). The matter will eventually be clarified — if the husband dies, the man is exempt; if he survives, the man brings a full chatat.
R' Yosei
Her status is fundamentally uncertain. Even if the husband dies, we don't know when the get took effect. The paramour must bring an asham talui.
R' Yochanan
This is the practical difference (nafka mina) between R' Meir and R' Yosei — the provisional guilt-offering.
Key Distinction R' Meir holds the uncertainty is temporary and will resolve; R' Yosei holds it is permanent and unresolvable.

R' Yosei vs. The Rabbis on Uncertain Divorce

R' Yosei

  • Her sexual intercourse has uncertain status
  • Paramour must bring asham talui
  • No explicit ruling on sustenance

The Rabbis (Chakhamim)

  • Uncertainty whether divorced or not divorced
  • Same uncertainty as R' Yosei
  • Husband must provide sustenance (per R' Zeira/Shmuel)
R' Zeira's Rule Wherever the Sages say 'uncertainty whether divorced or not' — the husband is obligated to provide her sustenance. This applies only to the Rabbis' formulation, not R' Yosei's.

Rav Huna vs. Rav Yehuda: Retroactive Divorce

Wife receives get with condition:
"Give me 200 dinars"
↓
The get is torn or lost
before she pays
Rav Huna
Divorce is retroactive from when get was given.
She does not need a second get.
Rav Yehuda
Divorce takes effect only upon payment.
She needs a second get since the first is gone.
Parallel in Kiddushin The same dispute applies to betrothal: if a man says 'You are betrothed on condition I give you 200 dinars' — does betrothal take effect immediately or only upon payment?

The Lost Coat of Tzaidan

Rabbis (Chakhamim)
He said "give me my coat" — he specified a particular item. If she lost it, she cannot substitute money. The get is invalid.
R' Shimon b. Gamliel
She can give its monetary value. There was an incident in Tzaidan where the Sages ruled she gives the value of the coat and is divorced.
Gemara
The Mishna is incomplete (chisura mechsera) — the dispute about substituting value must be inserted before R' Shimon b. Gamliel's supporting incident.
Itztalit (איצטלית) The coat mentioned is an itztalit — a cloak or wrap. The question is whether naming a specific object creates an absolute requirement or merely identifies what value is owed.
