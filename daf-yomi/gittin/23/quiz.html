Gittin 23 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why can an incompetent person (deaf-mute/minor) write a get with supervision, but a gentile cannot?
A. A gentile's handwriting is invalid
B. A gentile acts on his own will and won't follow the supervisor's intent
C. A gentile cannot hold a pen according to halakha
D. A gentile is not allowed to touch parchment
Correct: B — Rav Huna explains that a gentile has his own da'at (halakhic competence/will), so he may not follow the supervisor's lishma intent. An incompetent person, lacking independent halakhic will, naturally follows the supervisor.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Meir, what must be done 'for her sake' (lishma) in a get?
A. The writing of the entire document
B. The writing of the essential part (toref)
C. The signing by the witnesses
D. Both the writing and the signing
Correct: C — Rabbi Meir holds that signatory witnesses (edei ḥatima) effect the divorce. Therefore, the verse 'and he writes her' refers to the signing, and only the signing must be lishma.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the School of Rabbi Yannai say slaves CAN serve as agents (in matters that apply to them), but gentiles cannot?
A. Slaves are more trustworthy than gentiles
B. Slaves are 'members of the covenant' (bnei brit) while gentiles are not
C. Slaves have Torah knowledge while gentiles do not
D. Slaves live in Jewish households
Correct: B — The verse 'so you also' (atem gam atem) is expounded to mean agents must be bnei brit — members of the covenant. Slaves, who are circumcised and keep some mitzvot, qualify; gentiles do not.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why are women who are hostile to another woman still credible to deliver her get, even though they cannot testify about her husband's death?
A. The rabbis trusted women more for gittin
B. The written document itself proves the divorce — testimony only supplements it
C. These women would not lie about a get
D. A get requires two witnesses anyway, so one hostile woman doesn't matter
Correct: B — The Mishna explains that with a get, 'the writing proves' (ha-ketav mokhiach). The document itself is the primary evidence of divorce, so the messenger's potential bias is less consequential than in death testimony, which relies solely on the witness.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the principle of 'ubar yerech imo' (a fetus is part of its mother) resolve the problem of a maidservant acquiring freedom for her unborn child?
A. It means the child doesn't need a bill of manumission
B. It means the mother is freeing herself, not acting as agent for a separate person
C. It means the fetus has no legal standing
D. It means the master's declaration is automatically effective on a fetus
Correct: B — Since the fetus is considered part of the mother's body, the master is effectively freeing a portion of the maidservant herself — not appointing her as an agent for a separate person. This avoids the rule against a slave receiving manumission for a fellow slave from a shared master.
