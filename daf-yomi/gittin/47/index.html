Redeeming a Jew Sold to Gentiles

Lakunya
I was captured — redeem me!
R' Ami
The Mishna says one who sells himself is not redeemed, but his children are. Here, though, there's danger to life — he should be redeemed.
The Sages
But this man is an apostate — he eats neveilot and tereifot!
R' Ami
Perhaps he eats them only due to appetite (l'tei'avon), not out of principle.
The Sages
When permitted and forbidden foods are both available, he chooses the forbidden — that's ideology, not temptation.
R' Ami
Go — they do not allow me to redeem you.
DISTINCTION Eating forbidden food l'tei'avon (from appetite) vs. l'hakh'is (to rebel) — only the latter makes one an apostate beyond redemption.

Reish Lakish & the Gladiators

⛓️ Reish Lakish sells himself to gladiators, bringing a bag with a round stone
↓
📜 He knows the tradition: captors fulfill a final request before killing their captive
↓
🪢 His request: 'Let me tie you up and strike each of you one and a half times'
↓
💪 Each single strike kills — Reish Lakish says to the dead: 'You still owe me half a strike!'
↓
🏠 He escapes home, lives with total trust in God — 'My belly is my pillow'
↓
😢 At death he left only a kav of saffron, quoting: 'They leave their wealth for others' (Ps. 49:11)
CHARACTER STUDY Reish Lakish's physical strength and radical trust in Providence — he grieved not poverty but that he hadn't used all his possessions.

Gentile Acquisition of Land in Eretz Yisrael

Rabba

  • Gentile acquisition does NOT abrogate sanctity of the land
  • Source: 'The land is Mine' (Lev. 25:23)
  • 'Your grain' = your gathering into a pile, not gentile's gathering
  • Gentile CAN dig pits and caves (Ps. 115:16)
  • Tithes still apply to produce from gentile-owned land

R' Elazar

  • Gentile acquisition DOES abrogate sanctity for tithes
  • Source: 'The tithe of your grain' (Deut. 12:17) — not gentile's grain
  • 'Your grain' = literal — only a Jew's produce is obligated
  • Gentile CANNOT dig pits and caves (Ps. 24:1)
  • Produce grown by gentile is exempt from tithes
IRONY Rabba gives the gentile physical rights (digging) but not spiritual impact. R' Elazar gives spiritual impact (removing tithes) but not physical rights. Each derives from different verses in Psalms.

Rav Ashi: Two Ordinances on First Fruits

Original Torah law:
Seller brings first fruits (sanctity not abrogated)
↓
Problem: Jews sold land freely, thinking the ongoing obligation proved no real harm
↓
📜 1st Ordinance:
Sellers should NOT bring first fruits — to discourage selling land to gentiles
↓
Problem: Poor Jews still sold land, and now fields stayed permanently with gentiles
↓
📜 2nd Ordinance:
Sellers MUST bring first fruits — to penalize them into repurchasing the field
PRACTICAL WISDOM The Sages adjusted policy when they saw unintended consequences — a model of responsive legislation.
