Gittin 8: Boundaries of the Holy Land

KEY THEME This daf maps the geographic and halakhic boundaries of Eretz Yisrael — from boats on the Mediterranean to the special status of Syria — and concludes with a debate about whether a slave's manumission document can be split to grant freedom without transferring property.

Boat vs. Flowerpot: Is the Soil Connected?

Position A
A boat is made to move — its soil is not considered attached to the ground. But a flowerpot, which stays in place, is considered part of the ground.
Position B
A boat rests on water, which is like solid earth — so there's no barrier. But a perforated flowerpot has airspace beneath it, creating a real separation from the ground.
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
Everyone agrees about rivers within Eretz Yisrael — a get written on a boat there is valid. The dispute is only about the Great Sea (Mediterranean).
PRINCIPLE Whether soil in a vessel has the status of Eretz Yisrael depends on its connection to the ground — through water or through air.

Mediterranean Sea: Rabbis vs. Rabbi Yehuda

Rabbis (Chakhamim)

  • Draw a string from Turei Amnon to the River of Egypt (Wadi el-Arish)
  • East of the string = Eretz Yisrael
  • West of the string = Outside Eretz Yisrael
  • Islands judged by this line
  • Verse "the Great Sea for a border" teaches only about islands

Rabbi Yehuda

  • Anything directly across from Eretz Yisrael is part of it
  • The sea itself counts as Eretz Yisrael (Numbers 34:6)
  • Side islands: string from Kefalorya to the Atlantic Ocean
  • Much broader definition of the border
  • Islands are obviously included — no verse needed
VERSE "And for the western border, you shall have the Great Sea for a border" (Numbers 34:6) — is the sea itself the border, or part of the land?

Buying a Field in Syria on Shabbat

One who buys a field in Syria is like buying in the outskirts of Jerusalem
↓
One may write a bill of sale (ono) for this purchase — even on Shabbat?
↓
Problem
Writing on Shabbat is a capital offense!
Solution (Rava)
Tell a gentile to write it — ordinarily prohibited (amira l'nakhri), but here permitted for the mitzvah of settling Eretz Yisrael
KEY RULE The mitzvah of settling Eretz Yisrael (yishuv Eretz Yisrael) overrides the rabbinic prohibition of instructing a gentile to work on Shabbat.

Slave's Bill of Manumission: Freedom + Property?

Scenario
A slave brings a document stating: "You and my property are transferred to you" — he acquires himself. But does he acquire the property without court ratification?
Abaye (initial)
Since he acquired himself, he acquires the rest of the property too — it's all one statement.
Rava
Freedom works like a get — the bearer can testify. But property requires ratification like any legal document. We can split the statement.
Abaye (reversed)
Fine — then since he can't acquire the property, maybe he can't acquire himself either!
Rava (final)
No — we divide the statement: he acquires himself (like a get) but not the property (needs ratification). This follows Rabbi Shimon.
HALAKHA A document can be divided — accepted for one purpose (manumission) and rejected for another (property transfer).
