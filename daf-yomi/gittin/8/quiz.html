Gittin 8 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak say the dispute about a boat is only regarding the Mediterranean Sea?
A. Because rivers in Eretz Yisrael are universally agreed to be part of EY
B. Because the Mediterranean has saltwater which affects purity
C. Because boats cannot travel on rivers
D. Because the Torah only mentions the Great Sea
Correct: A — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak clarifies that everyone agrees rivers within Eretz Yisrael give the boat EY status. The dispute concerns only the Mediterranean Sea and whether it counts as part of EY.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yehuda determine the western border of Eretz Yisrael differently from the Rabbis?
A. He excludes all islands from Eretz Yisrael
B. He says the entire sea directly across from Eretz Yisrael is part of it
C. He draws the border at the Jordan River
D. He says only the coastline counts as the border
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda interprets Numbers 34:6 — 'the Great Sea for a border' — to mean the sea itself is part of Eretz Yisrael, not merely its edge. The Rabbis use the verse only to include islands.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why is it permitted to instruct a gentile to write a bill of sale for Syrian land on Shabbat?
A. Because Syria is fully outside Eretz Yisrael and Shabbat doesn't apply
B. Because writing a bill of sale is only a rabbinic prohibition
C. Because the mitzvah of settling Eretz Yisrael overrides the rabbinic decree of amira l'nakhri
D. Because the gentile is writing for his own benefit
Correct: C — Normally, instructing a gentile to do prohibited work on Shabbat (amira l'nakhri) is rabbinically forbidden. But the Sages waived this decree for the mitzvah of settling Eretz Yisrael (yishuv Eretz Yisrael).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How can one enter Syria while remaining ritually pure?
A. By immersing in a mikveh upon entry
B. By traveling in a chest, box, or cabinet to avoid touching the soil
C. By walking only on paved Roman roads
D. It is impossible — Syria's air and soil are both impure
Correct: B — Unlike lands fully outside EY where both soil AND air were decreed impure, in Syria only the soil was decreed impure. Therefore, traveling in an enclosed container (chest, box, cabinet) avoids soil contact and preserves purity.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rava's final position, what happens when a slave brings a document saying 'All of my property is transferred to you'?
A. He acquires both himself and the property
B. He acquires neither himself nor the property
C. He acquires himself but not the property
D. He acquires the property but remains a slave
Correct: C — Rava holds that we divide the statement: the slave acquires himself (like a woman bringing her own get), but does not acquire the property (which requires court ratification like any legal document). This follows Rabbi Shimon's principle of dividing statements.
