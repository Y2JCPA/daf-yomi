Gittin 26 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the mishna, what must a scribe leave blank when pre-writing the standard part (tofes) of a get?
A. Only the man's name and the date
B. The man's name, woman's name, and the date
C. The man's name, woman's name, date, and per Shmuel — 'permitted to any man'
D. Everything — nothing may be pre-written
Correct: C — The mishna lists the man's name, woman's name, and date. Shmuel adds that the essential phrase 'You are hereby permitted to marry any man' must also be left blank, since it too must be written lishmah.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Shmuel say the first clause of the mishna follows Rabbi Elazar?
A. Because R' Elazar holds signatory witnesses effect divorce
B. Because R' Elazar holds witnesses of transmission effect divorce, so the writing must be for her sake
C. Because R' Elazar invalidates all pre-written documents
D. Because R' Elazar permits writing the entire get in advance
Correct: B — R' Elazar holds that witnesses of transmission (not signing) effect the divorce. Therefore, the act of writing itself must be lishmah (for her sake), which is why even the tofes must be carefully regulated.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the explanation following R' Meir, what is the 'ordinance' (takana) designed to prevent?
A. Scribes charging too much for writing gittin
B. A husband impulsively throwing a pre-prepared get at his wife during a quarrel
C. A wife forging her own bill of divorce
D. Witnesses refusing to sign the document
Correct: B — Per R' Meir's framework, if the essential part could be pre-written, a husband might impulsively throw a ready-made get at his wife during a fight. The takana ensures he must wait for the essential details to be written, giving time to cool down.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why is Ulla's explanation of the date requirement needed?
A. Because the 'produce' reason doesn't apply to divorce from betrothal — a husband has no rights to a betrothed woman's produce
B. Because the 'daughter of his sister' reason doesn't apply to betrothal
C. Because R' Elazar requires three separate dates on every get
D. Because without a date, the scribe cannot prepare the tofes
Correct: A — The 'produce' reason for dating a get doesn't apply to betrothal divorces, since the husband has no property rights during betrothal. Ulla provides an alternative reason — 'for the benefit of the child' — that applies universally.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the contradiction between the first clause (permitting pre-writing tofes of a get) and R' Elazar's explicit ruling in the last clause (forbidding it)?
A. The first clause is R' Meir and the last clause is R' Elazar
B. Two tannaim disagree about what R' Elazar's actual opinion is
C. The first clause discusses loans, not gittin
D. R' Elazar changed his mind between the two rulings
Correct: B — The Gemara answers that there are two tannaim who disagree about R' Elazar's opinion — one attributes the lenient view to him (first clause) and another attributes the strict view to him (last clause).
