Who Is Believed: Husband or Shalish?

Rav Huna
The husband is credible — if he meant to divorce her, he would have given the get directly to his wife, not to a third party.
Rav Ḥisda
The shalish (third party) is credible — the husband himself deemed him trustworthy by entrusting him with the get.
R' Abba
Objection to Rav Huna! A Tosefta teaches: the shalish's word outweighs both litigants — even more than 100 witnesses of admission!
Gemara
Monetary cases are different — debts can be forgiven (meḥilah), so parties accept the shalish's word. But ritual matters like divorce cannot be forgiven.
Core Dispute The fundamental question: does entrusting someone with a document grant them credibility about its purpose?

Gittin vs. Shetarot: What Does the Baraita Mean?

Baraita 1: "Likewise for gittin" — shalish is credible
↓
Does gittin mean bills of divorce or monetary documents?
Challenge
Baraita 2 says: "Likewise for shetarot"
If shetarot = monetary docs, then gittin = divorce!
Rejection
Two separate baraitot — both could refer to monetary documents using different terms
Terminology Gittin can mean divorce documents or any legal documents. Shetarot typically means monetary documents. Since the baraitot are separate, no proof can be drawn.

When Is the Shalish Believed?

ConditionShalish Credible?Reason
Document in shalish's possession✅ YesHe controls the item and could act as he wishes
Document no longer in possession (torn)❌ NoLost basis for credibility — needs witnesses
Monetary dispute✅ YesDebts can be forgiven (meḥilah)
Ritual matter (divorce)❓ DisputedRav Huna: No / Rav Ḥisda: Yes
Wife claims 'you divorced me'✅ NormallyRav Hamnuna: she won't be insolent before husband
Wife claims divorce + supporting factor exists❌ NoWhen something supports her claim, she might be bold enough to lie
Rav Hamnuna's Principle A wife who says "You divorced me" is normally believed — the presumption is she would not be brazen enough to lie to her husband's face. But this fails when external factors embolden her.

Mishna: Who Receives the Na'arah's Get?

Rabbis (Tanna Kamma)

  • Both she AND her father can receive the get
  • The Torah grants her an additional hand beyond her father's
  • Divorce is effective the moment either one receives the get

Rabbi Yehuda

  • Only her father receives her get
  • Two hands cannot acquire for one person simultaneously
  • Where father is alive, her hand has no legal standing
Na'arah Me'orasah A betrothed young woman (na'arah me'orasah) is between ages 12–12.5. The dispute concerns whether she has independent legal capacity while her father is alive.

Can a Minor Girl Be Divorced? The Safeguarding Test

📜 A minor who knows how to safeguard her get — can be divorced
↓
❓ What does 'safeguard' mean? She guards something else due to her get...
↓
🧠 R' Yoḥanan: She lost her get but guards a substitute thinking it's her get
↓
⚠️ Rav Huna bar Manoaḥ objects: That's just an imbecile, not a capable minor!
↓
✅ Rav Huna bar Manoaḥ's answer: She distinguishes between her get and less important items — guarding the get specifically
Practical Standard The test is whether the child understands relative value and importance — she safeguards the get because she recognizes its significance compared to other objects.
