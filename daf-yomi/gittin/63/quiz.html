QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Rav Nachman rule the divorce is invalid when the agent misrepresents the wife's instructions?
A. Because the wife never actually wanted the divorce
B. Because the husband relied on the agent's false statement and was genuinely deceived
C. Because a get must always be hand-delivered to the wife
D. Because the agent acted without any authorization at all
Correct: B — Rav Nachman's ruling hinges on the husband being genuinely misled by the agent's false claim. Unlike cases where the husband knows the agency is technically flawed, here the husband actually relied on the falsehood.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
The Gemara rejects challenges from the mishnah ('receive for my wife') and the minor girl case using the same reasoning. What is that reasoning?
A. In those cases the wife explicitly consented; here she did not
B. In those cases the husband KNOWS the agency is invalid and intends delivery; here he genuinely ERRS
C. In those cases the agent was appointed by beit din; here he was not
D. In those cases the get was written properly; here it was not
Correct: B — The key distinction is knowledge vs. error. When a husband knows receipt agency is impossible (he can't appoint for receipt; a minor can't appoint agents), we presume he intended delivery. When he is deceived, no such presumption applies.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Natan, what is the significance of the husband saying 'Here you are' (הילך) versus 'Deliver'?
A. 'Here you are' means the divorce is immediately invalid
B. 'Here you are' is equivalent to 'Acquire' — the husband cannot retract
C. 'Here you are' requires two witnesses; 'Deliver' requires three
D. There is no practical difference between the two expressions
Correct: B — According to Rabbi Natan, 'Here you are' is comparable to 'Acquire' (זכה), giving the agent immediate acquisition rights. With 'Deliver,' the husband can still retract; with 'Here you are,' he cannot.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When Rav rules that the agent has dual agency (both husband's delivery agent and wife's receipt agent), what is the practical consequence if the husband dies childless?
A. She is fully divorced and free to remarry anyone
B. She must perform ḥalitza but may not enter levirate marriage
C. She must enter levirate marriage with the brother
D. The marriage is retroactively annulled
Correct: B — Due to the uncertainty — maybe the agent was for delivery (not yet divorced) or for receipt (already divorced) — she performs ḥalitza but cannot enter levirate marriage, applying the stringent approach in ritual law.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What reason does the Gemara give for Rav's opinion that a woman cannot appoint an agent to receive her get from her husband's agent?
A. It would violate a biblical prohibition against multiple agents
B. The wife must be present when the get is written
C. It shows contempt for the husband, or is decreed invalid due to the 'courtyard acquired thereafter' analogy
D. The husband's agent automatically becomes the wife's agent, making a second agent redundant
Correct: C — Two reasons are given: (1) refusing to receive from the husband's agent directly could be seen as contempt, and (2) it parallels the case of a courtyard acquired after the get was placed — her agent designated after the husband gave the get to his agent is like property acquired too late.
