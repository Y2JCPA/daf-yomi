When Agents Misrepresent & The Limits of Agency in Divorce

Rav Nachman
If the agent misrepresents what the wife said (delivery vs. receipt), even if the get reaches her — she is not divorced
Challengers
But we know the principle: once a husband decides to divorce, he says 'let her be divorced any way she is divorced!'
Gemara
Those cases are different — there, the husband knows the agency is invalid. Here, he genuinely errs based on the agent's false claim.
Rav
A woman cannot appoint an agent to receive her get from the hand of her husband's agent.
R' Chanina
A woman can appoint an agent to receive from the husband's agent.
Core Tension Does a husband's general intent to divorce override technical flaws in agency designation?

Agent Misrepresentation: Rav Nachman's Case

Wife says to agent:
'Bring me my get'
(שליחות הולכה)
↓
Agent tells husband:
'Your wife said: Receive my get for me'
(שליחות קבלה — false!)
↓
Husband says: 'Here you are, as she said'
↓
Rav Nachman's Ruling
Not divorced — even when get reaches her hand. The agent negated his own agency by misrepresenting his role.
Why Not 'Any Way'?
Husband genuinely erred — he relied on the agent's false claim of receipt agency.
Key Principle The agent's misrepresentation causes the husband to rely on a falsehood, invalidating the entire agency.

Three Challenges to Rav Nachman — All Rejected

Challenge 1: Mishnah — 'Receive for My Wife'

  • Husband says 'Receive this get for my wife' — she's divorced if he doesn't retract
  • Husband can't designate receipt agent — yet it works!
  • Principle: 'Let her be divorced any way'
  • ❌ REJECTED: There husband KNOWS receipt is invalid — here he genuinely ERRS

Challenge 2: Minor Girl

  • Minor girl says 'receive my get' — not valid until it reaches HER hand
  • But once it reaches her — she IS divorced
  • Husband must have intended delivery agency
  • ❌ REJECTED: Husband KNOWS minors can't appoint agents — here he ERRS on agent's word

Challenge 3: Baraita (Tosefta 6:2)

  • Agent misrepresents; husband says 'deliver/acquire/receive'
  • If husband doesn't retract — once get reaches her, she's divorced
  • This directly contradicts Rav Nachman!
  • ⚡ Gemara resolves: expressions don't match to assertions as assumed
Pattern Each challenge invokes 'let her be divorced any way' — each is rejected because those cases involve a husband who KNOWS the agency is flawed, unlike here where he genuinely relies on a lie.

'Here You Are' vs. 'Deliver' — R' Natan & R' Yehuda HaNasi

R' Natan
If husband says 'Deliver this get' — he can retract.
If he says 'Here you are' — he cannot retract.
R' Yehuda HaNasi
Even if the husband says 'Deliver' — he cannot retract his decision.
Gemara
A baraita proves R' Natan's view: 'Here you are' is comparable to 'Acquire' (זכה) — the agent gains immediate acquisition rights.
Conclusion
According to R' Natan: 'Here you are' = 'Acquire'.
The language of giving determines the agent's power and the husband's ability to retract.
Halacha The husband's choice of words — 'deliver' vs. 'here you are' — determines whether the agent is merely a courier or gains acquisition power on behalf of the wife.

Scenario ElementDetail
Wife says to agent'Receive my get for me' (שליחות קבלה)
Agent tells husband'Your wife said: Receive my get for me'
Husband responds'Deliver and give it to her'
Agent's statusBOTH husband's agent for delivery AND wife's agent for receipt
If husband dies childless before get reaches wifeShe performs ḥalitza (maybe agent was for delivery — not yet divorced)
Why not levirate marriage?Maybe agent was for receipt — she was already divorced and forbidden to brother
Underlying uncertaintyDoes 'deliver' = 'acquire'? Stringent in ritual law
Monetary vs. Ritual Rav himself rules that 'deliver' = 'acquire' for monetary matters (lenient in doubt), but is stringent for divorce law — requiring both ḥalitza and forbidding yibum.

Can a Woman Appoint an Agent to Receive from Husband's Agent?

Rav
A woman cannot appoint an agent to receive her get from the hand of her husband's agent.
R' Chanina
A woman can appoint an agent to receive her get from her husband's agent.
Gemara (Reason 1)
Rav's reason: It could be construed as contempt for the husband — she won't even deal with his agent directly.
Gemara (Reason 2)
Alternatively: A decree due to her courtyard acquired thereafter — her agent is like property she acquired after the husband placed the get elsewhere.
Courtyard Analogy Just as a get placed in a courtyard she later purchases is invalid, so too her agent (=her 'courtyard') designated after the husband gave the get to his agent may be invalid.
