The Deaf-Mute Agent Problem

Gemara
If a deaf-mute (cheresh) brings a get — is he fit to serve as agent?
Mishna 23a
A deaf-mute, imbecile, and minor are unfit as agents — they lack legal competence (da'at).
Rav Yosef
He was competent when appointed but became a deaf-mute before making the declaration.
Gemara
This works for Rava (ratification concern), but for Rabba (lishmah testimony) — who will testify?
Gemara
Answer: This refers to later generations who already know the lishmah requirement. The deaf-mute case is uncommon — the Sages did not decree for it.
Key Principle מילתא דלא שכיחא לא גזרו בה רבנן — The Sages did not issue decrees for uncommon situations.

Why Not Decree for the Uncommon?

Objection: Woman's Case

  • A woman bringing her OWN get is also uncommon
  • Yet the Mishna requires her to make the declaration
  • This seems to contradict the 'uncommon matter' exemption

Resolution: Uniformity

  • שלא תחלוק בשליחות — So you won't distinguish between agencies
  • The woman IS an agent, just an unusual one
  • Exempting her would cause confusion about all agents
  • The deaf-mute's exemption is based on inability, not policy
Distinction The woman CAN speak but the situation is rare — decree applies for uniformity. The deaf-mute CANNOT speak — decree is physically impossible.

Two Agents & Shmuel's Question

Shmuel
If two people bring a get from overseas, must they say 'written and signed in our presence'?
Rav Huna
No. If they testified 'she was divorced in our presence' she'd be considered divorced — so they are certainly believed about the get.
Gemara
This works for Rava (two witnesses suffice for ratification). But for Rabba?
Gemara
Rabba agrees: this is after people learned the lishmah rule. Two agents is uncommon — no decree applies.
Parallel The same 'uncommon matter' principle exempts both the deaf-mute agent and the case of two agents from the declaration requirement.

R' Yehoshua ben Levi & R' Shimon bar Abba

R' Shimon bar Abba brings a get before R' Yehoshua ben Levi
↓
Must I say 'written and signed in my presence'?
↓
R' Yehoshua ben Levi: Not required — later generations are experts in lishmah
↓
But Rabba requires it even in later generations as a decree lest they forget!
↓
Resolution: R' Shimon bar Abba had a second person with him (unmentioned due to his honor, as that man was unlearned)
Conclusion R' Yehoshua ben Levi holds the declaration is because they are not experts in lishmah — in later generations this concern is resolved.
