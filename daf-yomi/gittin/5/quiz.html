QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is a deaf-mute agent exempt from the declaration requirement?
A. Because a deaf-mute can never be appointed as an agent
B. Because he became deaf-mute after appointment, and this is an uncommon case the Sages didn't decree for
C. Because two witnesses accompany him instead
D. Because the husband already consented to the divorce
Correct: B — Rav Yosef explains the case involves someone who was competent when appointed but became deaf-mute. The Gemara exempts him because it is an uncommon situation (milta d'lo shechicha), and the Sages did not extend their decree to uncommon cases.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why must a woman who brings her own get still make the declaration, even though it's an uncommon case?
A. Because women are always required to testify about divorce
B. Because the Sages wanted uniformity — so people won't distinguish between types of agency
C. Because she serves as both agent and witness
D. Because R' Yehoshua ben Levi specifically required it
Correct: B — The Gemara explains the decree applies to the woman so that people will not make distinctions (שלא תחלוק בשליחות) between different types of agency, maintaining a uniform rule for all agents.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why is the husband exempt from the declaration when he delivers his own get?
A. Because the husband is not considered an agent
B. Because the concern is that the husband might contest — but he himself is delivering it, so if he objected he'd simply tear it up
C. Because two witnesses are always present at the delivery
D. Because the husband already testified by writing the get
Correct: B — The entire purpose of the declaration is to prevent the husband from later contesting the get's validity. When the husband himself delivers the get, there is no concern — if he didn't want the divorce, he could simply destroy the document.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the core dispute between R' Yochanan (2 witnesses) and R' Chanina (3 witnesses) regarding the delivery quorum?
A. Whether the declaration is needed at all
B. Whether the agent can serve as witness AND whether a witness can serve as judge
C. Whether overseas gets require ratification
D. Whether Rabba or Rava's reasoning applies
Correct: B — Both agree ratification requires a court. R' Yochanan holds a witness can become a judge, so the agent + 2 others = court of 3. R' Chanina holds a witness cannot become a judge, requiring 3 additional people beyond the agent.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve R' Yehoshua ben Levi's ruling that R' Shimon bar Abba didn't need to make the declaration?
A. R' Yehoshua ben Levi disagreed with both Rabba and Rava
B. The get was delivered within Eretz Yisrael, not overseas
C. R' Shimon bar Abba had a second person with him, making them two agents — an uncommon case exempt from the decree
D. The get had already been ratified by a different court
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that R' Shimon bar Abba had another (unlearned) person with him, making two agents. Since two agents is uncommon, the Sages' decree didn't apply. The second person wasn't mentioned out of respect for R' Shimon bar Abba's honor.
