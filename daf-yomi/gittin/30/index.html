The Ferry Case: Condition Not Fulfilled

Man tells agents:
'If I don't arrive within 30 days, this is a get'
↓
He arrives at the river on Day 30
but the ferry is on the other side
↓
He shouts: 'See that I have arrived!'
↓
Shmuel: This is not considered an arrival
→ The get is valid
Principle Physical presence at the destination is required — verbal declaration and intent are insufficient to fulfill a condition.

Failed Appeasement: Two Versions of Rav Yosef

Setup
A man deposits a get: 'If I don't appease my wife within 30 days, let this be a get.' He tries, but she refuses.
Rav Yosef (v.1)
Did he give her a tarkeva full of dinars? He didn't try hard enough — get is valid.
Rav Yosef (v.2)
Must he give her a tarkeva full of dinars? He tried his best — get is NOT valid.
Gemara
Version 1 holds: Ones (duress) has no standing in gittin — condition unfulfilled regardless of fault.
Gemara
Version 2 holds: Ones has standing in gittin — circumstances beyond control excuse non-fulfillment.
אונס The core dispute: Does the law recognize circumstances beyond one's control (ones) when evaluating conditional divorce?

Mishna: Lending Against Tithes

ElementRule
ArrangementIsraelite lends money to Kohen, Levi, or poor person
RepaymentCreditor separates teruma/tithes from his produce; value deducted from loan
PresumptionBorrower is presumed alive — no need to verify each time
If borrower diesMust obtain permission from heirs to continue
If stipulated before Beit DinNo permission needed from heirs
If poor person becomes richArrangement ends — borrower keeps remaining money
Mutual Benefit The Kohen/Levi gets money up front; the Israelite avoids searching for a recipient each harvest.

How Does the Borrower Acquire the Tithes?

Rav

  • Applies to associates (רגילין) of the Kohen/Levi
  • Regular arrangement creates presumptive ownership (chazakah)
  • The specific Kohen effectively already 'owns' the tithes

Shmuel

  • Acquisition through a third party (kinyan acherim)
  • Others acquire the tithes on behalf of the Kohen/Levi
  • Then the lender reclaims them as debt repayment

Ulla (R' Yosei)

  • Rabbinic ordinance (takanah)
  • Sages treated one who doesn't acquire as if he acquires
  • Based on individual opinion of R' Yosei
Why Disagree? Rav and Shmuel reject Ulla because we don't establish a Mishna per an individual opinion. Shmuel and Ulla reject Rav because the Mishna doesn't mention 'associates.'

Death vs. Wealth: A Folk Saying

מיתה שכיחא, עשירות לא שכיחא
Rav Pappa's Proverb Why did the Sages enact an ordinance for death but not for wealth?

Death is common (שכיח) — so an ordinance was needed to allow continuing the arrangement with other poor people.

Wealth is uncommon (לא שכיח) — no ordinance was enacted.

'If someone tells you your friend died — believe it.
If someone tells you your friend became rich — don't believe it.'
