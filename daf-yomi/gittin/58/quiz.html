QUESTION 1 OF 5
How does the Gemara reconcile the conflicting reports about the number of tefillin baskets found at Beitar?
A. One report is from Beitar, the other from Jerusalem
B. One refers to head tefillin, the other to arm tefillin — which differ in size
C. One counts only soldiers, the other counts civilians too
D. The reports are from different time periods of the siege
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the two accounts don't contradict — one Sage counted tefillin shel rosh (head) and the other tefillin shel yad (arm), which differ in size due to how they are fashioned.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the literal interpretation that the sons of Zion were 'covered in fine gold (paz)'?
A. Gold was forbidden for personal adornment
B. Only two istira-weights of paz existed in the entire world — not enough to cover them
C. The word paz actually means beauty, not gold
D. The verse is speaking metaphorically about Torah knowledge
Correct: B — The school of R' Sheila taught that only two istira-weights of paz existed — one in Rome and one in the rest of the world — making it impossible to literally cover people in it. Rather, their beauty surpassed gold.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How did R' Yehoshua ben Chananya identify the captive child in Rome as a Torah prodigy?
A. He tested the child with complex halakhic questions
B. The child recited the continuation of a verse R' Yehoshua quoted from Isaiah
C. Other prisoners told him about the child's knowledge
D. The child was wearing tzitzit despite being in prison
Correct: B — R' Yehoshua stood at the prison entrance and recited the beginning of Isaiah 42:24. The child completed the verse, demonstrating both his Torah knowledge and his understanding that Jewish suffering was connected to sin — revealing exceptional wisdom.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the Sicarius laws, why does the court advance a claim (to'anin) on behalf of a second buyer but NOT on behalf of the first buyer?
A. The first buyer is assumed to be working with the Sicarius
B. Buyers and heirs may not know the acquisition history, but the first buyer was present for the original transaction
C. The first buyer already profited from three years of produce
D. The court only advocates for those who paid full market price
Correct: B — Rav Sheshet explains that heirs and subsequent buyers generally don't know how their predecessor obtained the property, so the court claims on their behalf. But the first buyer was directly involved in the acquisition from the Sicarius and should know the circumstances.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Rav Yosef say the law of unjust seizure (anparot) does not apply in Babylonia?
A. Babylonian courts did not recognize gentile property claims
B. There were no cases of unjust seizure in Babylonia
C. Since accessible courts existed, failure to protest the seizure was treated as waiving one's rights
D. The Exilarch had special authority to return seized property automatically
Correct: C — Since Babylonia had a regional seat of government (bei davar) where one could lodge complaints, an owner who failed to protest was presumed to have waived his rights to the property — making the special anparot protections unnecessary.
