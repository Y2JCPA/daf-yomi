QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yosei, why can't a husband tell agents to relay instructions to a scribe to write a get?
A. Verbal directives cannot be delegated to an agent (milei lo mimseran l'shaliach)
B. The scribe might be embarrassed and refuse
C. Agents are not trustworthy enough for divorce documents
D. The Torah requires the husband to write the get himself
Correct: A — R' Yosei's principle is that verbal directives (milei) cannot be transferred to a third party through an agent. The husband must personally instruct the scribe and witnesses.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How did Isi ben Yehuda characterize R' Tarfon's teaching style?
A. A well-stocked store
B. A pile of nuts — remove one and all the others tumble
C. A full storehouse
D. A basket of fragrant spices
Correct: B — R' Tarfon was likened to a pile of nuts: when a student asked him one question, he would cite sources from all disciplines of Torah — like removing one nut causes the whole pile to shift.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If a husband tells ten people 'All of you write a get,' what must happen?
A. All ten must write separate copies
B. One writes it in the presence of all ten
C. Any one of them may write it alone
D. Five must write and five must sign
Correct: B — When the husband says 'all of you write,' one person writes the get but must do so in the presence of all ten. Without 'all of you,' one may write on behalf of all without the others present.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
A man says 'Write a get for my wife' while lucid, then develops kordeyakos and says 'Don't write it.' What is the law?
A. The retraction is valid and they may not write the get
B. The retraction is nothing — they write and give the get
C. They must wait until he recovers to confirm
D. The original instruction is also invalidated retroactively
Correct: B — The mishna rules that a retraction made while afflicted with kordeyakos (temporary insanity) is legally meaningless. The original lucid instruction stands.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Rava object to Rav Yehuda's formula for instructing agents to write a get?
A. It was not in proper Hebrew
B. It was too long — the husband might truncate it and say only 'all of you,' causing invalidation
C. It did not include enough witnesses
D. It relied on verbal delegation, which R' Yosei forbids
Correct: B — Rava noted that Rav Yehuda's complex formula ('all of you or each one of you') was so long that husbands might shorten it to just 'all of you' — which would invalidate the get if one agent was absent. Rava simplified: say 'each of you' and avoid 'all of you' entirely.
