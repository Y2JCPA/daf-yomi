Gittin 24 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why can't a woman simply receive her own get when she serves as the delivery agent bringing it from abroad?
A. Because women are not trusted to testify about their own divorce
B. Because the delivery agency never returns to the husband — the mission is incomplete
C. Because she needs two witnesses to receive the get
D. Because the get must be delivered by a man
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the agency must 'return to the husband' (חזרה שליחות), meaning the delivery agent must complete the mission. If she becomes the recipient, the delivery is never properly concluded.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the solution the Gemara accepts for how a wife can bring her own get?
A. The husband stipulates the divorce takes effect only before a specific court
B. She places the get on the ground and picks it up again
C. She serves as delivery agent, then appoints a third-party receipt agent at the court
D. She delivers it to two witnesses who then hand it back to her
Correct: C — The accepted solution is that she acts as the husband's delivery agent, and upon arriving at the court, she appoints a separate agent to receive the get on her behalf, keeping the two agencies distinct.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why is a get written by scribes practicing (not for an actual divorce) invalid even if the names match?
A. Because the scribes are not qualified witnesses
B. Because the Torah requires the get be a 'scroll of severance' — written for actual divorce
C. Because practice documents are written on invalid materials
D. Because the husband did not sign it
Correct: B — The verse 'He gives a scroll of severance' teaches that the get must be written for the purpose of actual divorce (lishmah). A practice document, even with matching names, was never written for the sake of severance.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does the last clause of the mishna — writing a get for 'whichever wife I choose' — teach us?
A. That a husband cannot divorce two wives simultaneously
B. That a scribe must know which wife is being divorced
C. That there is no retroactive clarification (אין ברירה) for gittin
D. That a husband must personally hand the get to his wife
Correct: C — The Gemara explains this clause teaches that we do not apply retroactive clarification (bereirah). Even after the husband chooses a wife and gives her the get, we cannot say it was retroactively written for her — it must be specified from the outset.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What broader legal principle does Rava derive from the mishna about the husband who wrote a get for his older wife?
A. A woman's marriage contract is void if her husband has two wives with the same name
B. Two people with identical names in one city can present a promissory note to collect debts
C. A get written for one wife automatically applies to all wives
D. Courts must verify the identity of every person presenting a legal document
Correct: B — Rava reasons: since the mishna implies the husband CAN still divorce the older wife (for whom the get was written), even though the younger wife could fraudulently claim it — the same applies to monetary documents. A person with an identical name cannot invalidate someone else's valid promissory note.
