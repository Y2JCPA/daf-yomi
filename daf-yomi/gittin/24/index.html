Gittin 24: Agency, Identity & Lishmah

Wife Brings Her Own Get
From outside Eretz Yisrael
↓
Problem: Once the get reaches her hand, she's already divorced — why must she testify?
↓
Various solutions proposed involving conditional delivery and dual agency
↓
Amud A
Agency mechanics for a wife delivering her own get
Amud B
A get must be written lishmah — for this woman specifically
Core Theme The precision required in both the delivery and the writing of a get — agency must be legally coherent, and the document must be created for the specific divorce.

Abaye vs. Rav Yosef: Where Does the Mishna Take Place?

Abaye
The entire mishna — including the wife bringing her own get — discusses a case outside Eretz Yisrael, which is why she must say 'it was written and signed in my presence.'
Rav Yosef
The first and last clauses are outside Eretz Yisrael, but the middle clause (women not deemed credible) takes place in Eretz Yisrael.
Rav Ashi
The mishna's precise language supports Abaye — it says she must state it was written and signed in her presence, implying outside Eretz Yisrael.
Rav Yosef
My proof: the mishna says 'the writing proves it' — not 'writing and statement.' No declaration is needed, so it's in Eretz Yisrael.
Key Issue Outside Eretz Yisrael, the agent must declare the get was written and signed in their presence to confirm its validity (לשמה).

The Woman's Agency Problem

A woman brings her own get from outside Eretz Yisrael
↓
❓ Once the get reaches her hand, she's already divorced!
Why must she testify before a court?
↓
Rav Huna: Husband stipulated divorce only in the presence of a specific court
↓
❓ But once she arrives at that court, she's immediately divorced — same problem!
↓
Rav Huna bar Manoaḥ: Husband said 'Place the get on the ground and pick it up'
↓
❌ Rava rules: 'Take your get from the ground' = as though he said nothing
Rejected If the husband tells her to take the get from the ground, it's as if she took it on her own — not a valid giving (נתינה).

The Dual Agency Solution

📤 Husband appoints wife as agent for delivery (שליח הולכה)
↓
🚶‍♀️ She carries the get to the designated court
↓
👤 Upon arrival, she appoints a third-party agent for receipt (שליח קבלה)
↓
📥 She hands the get to that agent, who receives it on her behalf
↓
📣 She testifies: 'It was written and signed in my presence'
↓
✅ Divorce takes effect through the receipt agent
Why Not Self-Receive? She cannot be both delivery and receipt agent because the agency never returns to the husband (לא חזרה שליחות אצל הבעל) — the first mission is never completed.

Agent Receiving from Agent: Two Reasons It's Usually Forbidden

Degradation of Husband

  • Husband wants to give the get directly to his wife
  • Appointing an agent to receive from his agent is demeaning
  • Here: husband himself instructed this — no degradation
  • This reason permits our case ✅

Decree Due to Courtyard

  • Concern: people might confuse agent-receipt with her courtyard acquiring the get
  • A courtyard acquires automatically — different mechanism
  • This is a blanket decree — no exceptions
  • This reason may still prohibit our case ❌
Practical Difference If the prohibition is about the husband's honor, our case works because he consented. If it's a general decree, even his consent may not help.
