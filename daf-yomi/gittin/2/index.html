Gittin 2: The Agent's Declaration

בפני נכתב ובפני נחתם
Core Question When an agent brings a get (bill of divorce) from overseas, he must declare: 'It was written in my presence and it was signed in my presence.' The Mishna defines when this is required, and the Gemara debates why.

The Mishna's Rule: When Must the Agent Declare?

📜 Agent brings a get from overseas to Eretz Yisrael
↓
🗣️ Must state: 'Written in my presence, signed in my presence'
↓
🔄 Same requirement: Eretz Yisrael → overseas
↓
🌍 Same requirement: region to region within overseas
↓
✅ Within Eretz Yisrael: no declaration needed (but can be contested)
Key Principle The declaration replaces the normal court process of authenticating signatures on a document.

Why Must the Agent Declare? — The Core Gemara Dispute

Rabba
People overseas are not experts in the requirement that a get must be written lishmah — for the sake of this specific man and woman.
Rava
The concern is that witnesses are not available to ratify the get if the husband later claims it's a forgery.
Rabba
The declaration testifies that the writing and signing were performed for the correct parties.
Rava
The declaration bars any subsequent objection by the husband, since distant witnesses can't be summoned.
לשמה vs. קיום Rabba's reason: ensuring lishmah (proper intent). Rava's reason: ensuring kiyum (document verification).

Three Practical Differences Between Rabba and Rava

Test Case: What scenario distinguishes the two opinions?
Two agents bring the get
Rabba: Still must declare (lishmah concern remains)
Rava: No need — two witnesses can ratify
Region to region in Eretz Yisrael
Rabba: No declaration (people in E"Y know lishmah)
Rava: Must declare — witnesses may be unavailable
Same region overseas
Rabba: Must declare (overseas people don't know lishmah)
Rava: No need — witnesses are locally available
נפקא מינה These three cases reveal whether the concern is knowledge of the law or access to witnesses.

Rabba vs. Rava — Side by Side

Rabba: Lishmah Concern

  • Overseas people don't know get must be written for her sake
  • Declaration = testimony about proper intent
  • Two agents still must declare
  • Within Eretz Yisrael — no concern (people are experts)
  • Same region overseas — still must declare

Rava: Witness Availability

  • Distant witnesses can't be summoned to verify signatures
  • Declaration = preemptive ratification
  • Two agents = two witnesses, no declaration needed
  • Region to region in E"Y — must declare (witnesses far)
  • Same region overseas — no need (witnesses nearby)
Summary Each opinion leads to different practical rulings in edge cases involving distance and number of agents.
