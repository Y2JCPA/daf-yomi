Gittin 2 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What must an agent say when delivering a get from overseas to Eretz Yisrael?
A. This get is valid and binding
B. It was written in my presence and signed in my presence
C. The husband authorized me to deliver this get
D. I witnessed the husband give this get willingly
Correct: B — The Mishna requires the specific formula: 'בפני נכתב ובפני נחתם' — 'It was written in my presence and it was signed in my presence.'
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabba, why is this declaration required?
A. To verify the husband's identity
B. Because overseas people may not know the get must be written lishmah (for her sake)
C. Because the wife might not recognize the agent
D. To ensure the get was delivered within the required time
Correct: B — Rabba holds that people overseas are not experts in the requirement that a get must be written specifically for the sake of the divorcing couple (lishmah).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If TWO agents bring a get from overseas, what is the practical difference between Rabba and Rava?
A. Both agree no declaration is needed
B. Both agree declaration is still needed
C. Rabba: still must declare; Rava: no need (two witnesses can ratify)
D. Rava: still must declare; Rabba: no need
Correct: C — According to Rava, the concern is witness availability — two agents serve as witnesses. According to Rabba, the concern is lishmah knowledge, which remains regardless of how many agents there are.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say one witness is sufficient for this declaration, despite the woman having a presumption of being married?
A. Because divorce is only a rabbinic matter
B. Because most scribes are experts in lishmah, so the Sages' requirement is only an extra precaution
C. Because the husband already consented to the divorce
D. Because two witnesses already signed the get itself
Correct: B — Since most professional scribes (סופרים) are trained in the lishmah requirement, the rabbinic testimony requirement is merely a precaution — and for precautionary measures, one witness suffices.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda, what is the status of Ashkelon regarding the borders of Eretz Yisrael?
A. It is fully within Eretz Yisrael
B. It is considered like south of Eretz Yisrael (outside)
C. It is on the border but treated as inside
D. Its status depends on whether the agent is coming or going
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda rules that Ashkelon itself is 'like south of Eretz Yisrael' — meaning it is outside the borders and the declaration requirement applies.
