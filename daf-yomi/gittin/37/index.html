Gittin 37 — Prosbol Mechanics & Shemittah Debt Rules

DAF OVERVIEW This daf explores the practical mechanics of prosbol — how much land is needed, creative transfers of land, and who is exempt. It then addresses the cancellation of debts in the Sabbatical Year: which types of loans are affected, how a creditor must verbally release the debt, and the famous story of Rabba and Abba bar Marta. The daf concludes with the mishna on a captured Canaanite slave redeemed by others.

How Much Land Does a Borrower Need for Prosbol?

Borrower has no land — creditor wants to write a prosbol
↓
Creditor transfers any amount of his own field to the borrower
↓
Rav
Enough land to grow a stalk of cabbage
Rav Yehuda
A place for an oven and stove suffices
Hillel (Tosefta)
Even a perforated pot on the ground counts as land
KEY DETAIL A non-perforated pot resting on stakes does NOT count — the pot has no connection to the ground. But a perforated pot is considered attached to the earth.

Creative Workarounds for Prosbol

🌴 Rav Ashi — Would transfer a palm tree stump still in the ground to the borrower
↓
👥 Sages of Rav Ashi's school — Transferred debts to each other as a court: "You are hereby a court, and the debt is given to you"
↓
🤝 R' Yonatan to R' Ḥiyya bar Abba — Transferred a debt this way and asked: "Do I need anything else?" Answer: No.
↓
🏠 Guarantor's land — If borrower has no land but guarantor does, prosbol may be written on guarantor's land
↓
🔗 Debtor's debtor — If someone owes the borrower money and that person has land, prosbol can be based on that land (R' Natan's principle)
R' NATAN'S PRINCIPLE From Numbers 5:7 — a creditor can collect directly from a debtor's debtor: "And gives it to him in respect of whom he has been guilty."

Which Loans Does Shemittah Cancel?

Mishna (Shevi'it 10:1)
Shemittah abrogates debt both with a promissory note and without a promissory note.
Rav & Shmuel
With note = note with property guarantee.
Without note = note without guarantee.
Oral loans — certainly canceled.
R' Yoḥanan & Reish Lakish
With note = note without property guarantee.
Without note = oral loan.
But a note with a property guarantee — NOT canceled by Shemittah.
PRACTICAL DIFFERENCE According to R' Yoḥanan & Reish Lakish, a promissory note with a property guarantee (שעבוד נכסים) is strong enough to survive the Sabbatical Year.

Repaying a Canceled Debt in Shemittah

Creditor's Obligation

  • Must say: "I abrogate the debt" (משמט אני)
  • Derived from Deut. 15:2 — דבר = statement
  • Rabba: May look hopefully at the debtor to hint he'd like repayment

Debtor's Response

  • Should say: "Nevertheless, I want to repay you" (אף על פי כן)
  • Must NOT say: "I give this in payment of my debt"
  • Should say: "This is my money and I give it as a gift"
  • Initiative must come from the debtor
ABAYE'S OBJECTION Abaye challenged Rabba: If the creditor hints, isn't the initiative coming from him? Rabba responded: Hinting is permitted — the debtor still makes the final decision.

The Story of Rabba & Abba bar Marta

Narrator
Abba bar Marta owed Rabba money. He brought payment during Shemittah.
Rabba
"I abrogate this debt."
Abba bar Marta
Takes the money and leaves! He took Rabba's words literally.
Abaye
Finds Rabba sad. Goes to Abba bar Marta and asks what happened.
Abaye
Teaches him: "Say: Nevertheless, I want to repay you." Abba bar Marta goes back and Rabba accepts.
Rabba
"This scholar was not knowledgeable from the beginning" — he had to be taught the proper formula.
LESSON The Shemittah debt cancellation involves a ritual formula. Both parties have scripted roles — the creditor abrogates, and the debtor must insist on repaying.
