Gittin 7: Restraint, Borders & Sacred Boundaries

אמרתי אשמרה דרכי מחטוא בלשוני — I will guard my ways from sinning with my tongue (Tehillim 39:2)
OVERVIEW This daf weaves together themes of self-restraint — from controlling one's household gently, to resisting revenge against tormentors, to refraining from joyful song after the Temple's destruction. It then transitions to geographic boundaries of Eretz Yisrael and their halakhic implications for bills of divorce.

Don't Rule Your Home with Fear

📜 Rabba bar bar Ḥana: A person should say things with calmness so others accept his instruction
↓
⚠️ R' Abbahu: Never impose excessive fear upon members of your household
↓
😰 A great man (R' Ḥanina ben Gamliel) frightened his household excessively
↓
🍖 His terrified family nearly fed him a limb from a living animal (ever min ha-ḥai)
↓
🛡️ Hashem protects the righteous — they sought to feed him, but he did not actually eat it
PRINCIPLE Fear-based leadership causes those around you to cut corners and commit grave errors.

Mar Ukva vs. His Tormentors

Mar Ukva
📩 First letter to R' Elazar: People torment me and I have power to hand them to the government. May I?
R' Elazar
📜 "I will guard my ways… I will keep a curb upon my mouth while the wicked is before me" (Tehillim 39:2) — Stay silent.
Mar Ukva
📩 Second letter: They torment me greatly! I cannot bear it!
R' Elazar
📜 "Resign yourself to Hashem and wait patiently (hitḥolel)" (Tehillim 37:7) — He will strike them down as ḥalalim (corpses). Go early and stay late at the beit midrash.
Narrator
✅ Result: Geneiva, Mar Ukva's tormentor, was placed in a kolar (neck iron) by the government.
LESSON Divine justice operates on its own timeline — the righteous person's role is restraint and Torah study, not retaliation.

Song Forbidden After the Churban

Mar Ukva's Proof

  • Hosea 9:1 — "Rejoice not, O Israel, to exultation, like the peoples"
  • Proves ALL song is forbidden — vocal and instrumental
  • This is the verse Mar Ukva chose to send

Why Not Isaiah 24:9?

  • Isaiah 24:9 — "They do not drink wine with a song"
  • Problem: Could be limited to instrumental music only
  • Because prior verse (24:8) mentions tabrets, harp — instruments
  • Would leave vocal song permitted
KEY POINT Mar Ukva specifically chose the Hosea verse to teach that both vocal and instrumental music are forbidden after the destruction.

Kina, Dimonah, Adadah — A Hidden Drasha

Someone wrongs you and you feel jealousy/resentment (קינה / kina)
↓
You remain silent (דוממ / domem) — from דימונה / Dimonah
↓
He who dwells for all eternity (עדי עד / adei ad) — from עדדה / Adadah — performs judgment on your behalf
RAV GEVIHA The cities listed in Yehoshua 15:22 are read as an allusion: jealousy + silence = God judges for you.
