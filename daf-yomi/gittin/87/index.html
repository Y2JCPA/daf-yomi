Gittin 87: Document Format, Signatures & Layout

Can one get serve multiple couples?
↓
R' Yochanan
One date for all = general wording (valid)
Separate dates = separate formula (invalid)
Reish Lakish
Separate mention of each couple = separate formula even with one date
General = 'We have divorced our wives'
↓
Mishna: Rules for signature placement, column layout, and mixed-language signatures
↓
Valid signature formats: name alone, father's name alone, or full name without the word 'witness'
Key Theme The physical layout and wording of a get — including who signs, where they sign, and in what language — determines its validity.

What Is 'General Wording' vs. 'Separate Formula'?

R' Yochanan
If one date is written for all couples, it is a general wording — valid for all. If separate dates, it's a separate formula — only the last is valid.
Reish Lakish
Even with one date, if each couple is mentioned separately it's a separate formula. General wording means: 'We, so-and-so, have divorced our wives.'
R' Abba
According to R' Yochanan — with one date, perhaps the witnesses only intended to sign for the last couple? Like a greeting appended after a get!
R' Abbahu (in R' Yochanan's name)
If the greeting says 'Ask about the well-being of X' — invalid. But 'AND ask about...' with a conjunctive vav — valid, as it's a continuation.
Gemara
Similarly, the get reads: 'So-and-so divorced so-and-so, AND so-and-so divorced so-and-so' — the vav links them as one document.
Principle The conjunctive vav ('and') transforms separate clauses into a unified document, ensuring witness signatures apply to all.

Day-Written / Night-Signed Problem

Challenge to R' Yochanan: If separate dates are written for each couple but witnesses sign only once at the end...
↓
The witnesses signed on the last date only — earlier couples have a get written on a different date than signed!
↓
This is like a get written by day, signed at night — inherently invalid!
↓
Mar Kashisha b. Rav Chisda answers: Each formula says 'On Sunday, on Sunday' — all share the same date.
Resolution R' Yochanan's case works when all formulas bear the same calendar date, avoiding the written-day/signed-night problem.

Ravina's Challenge: 'He Writes HER' — One Get per Woman

The Problem (Reish Lakish)

  • General wording: 'We have divorced our wives'
  • Multiple women divorced with ONE document
  • Torah says 'v'khatav LAH' — he writes for HER
  • Implies: not for her AND another woman

The Resolution

  • After the general formula, scribe adds individual clauses
  • 'So-and-so divorced so-and-so, AND so-and-so divorced so-and-so'
  • Each woman has her own specific formulation
  • The general heading is supplemented by individual entries
Parallel Similarly, freeing two slaves with one document requires TWO separate documents given simultaneously — each person needs their own document.

Mishna: Physical Placement of Signatures

📜 Get written in two columns, witnesses sign below second column → Valid
↓
⬆️ Witnesses signed at the top of the column → Invalid
↓
↔️ Witnesses signed on the side → Invalid
↓
🔄 Witnesses signed on the back of a standard (non-folded) get → Invalid
↓
🔃 Two gets placed end-to-end, witnesses in middle → the one whose text reads into the signatures is valid
↓
⬆️⬇️ Two gets placed top-to-top, witnesses in middle → Both invalid (signatures face away from both)
Rule Witness signatures must appear below the text of the get, reading in the same direction — physical proximity and orientation matter.
