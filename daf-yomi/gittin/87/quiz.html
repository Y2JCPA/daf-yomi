QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yochanan, what makes a multi-couple get have a 'general wording' (making all valid)?
A. One date written for all couples
B. The phrase 'We have divorced our wives'
C. Each couple mentioned with the conjunctive vav
D. Separate dates for each couple
Correct: A — R' Yochanan holds that when one date is written for all couples, it is considered a general wording and all are valid. Reish Lakish requires the 'We have divorced' formulation.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does R' Abbahu say a greeting appended to a get with 'AND ask about the well-being of X' (with a vav) is valid?
A. The vav makes it a separate document
B. The vav indicates continuation, so witnesses signed the get too
C. The greeting becomes part of the get formula
D. The vav cancels the greeting entirely
Correct: B — The conjunctive vav ('and') makes the greeting appear as a continuation of the previous matter, so the witnesses are presumed to have signed the bill of divorce, not just the greeting.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Mishna, where must witnesses sign for a two-column get to be valid?
A. Below the first column
B. Between the two columns
C. Below the second column
D. On the back of the document
Correct: C — The Mishna states that if a get overflows into a second column, witnesses sign below the second column and it is valid. Signing at the top, side, or back of a non-folded get is invalid.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What was the practice of the 'scrupulous people of Jerusalem' regarding get signatures?
A. They signed only in Hebrew, never Greek
B. They signed with their full name but omitted the word 'witness'
C. They always included three witnesses instead of two
D. They signed with symbols instead of names
Correct: B — The Mishna teaches that the scrupulous people of Jerusalem would sign with their full name (so-and-so son of so-and-so) but would not write the word 'witness' — and this was considered valid and refined.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say a person would not use his father's name as a signature symbol, even though sages like Rav used symbols (a fish)?
A. Using a father's name as a symbol is considered disrespectful/brazen
B. A father's name is too common to be a unique identifier
C. Symbols must be non-textual images only
D. Only ordained rabbis were permitted to use symbols
Correct: A — The Gemara distinguishes between personal symbols (like Rav's fish) and using one's father's name as a symbol — the latter would be considered brazen (chutzpah), so we assume 'Ya'akov' after 'Reuven' is part of Reuven's full signature, not a separate symbol.
