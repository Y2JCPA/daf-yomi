Acquiring Produce vs. Acquiring Land & Collection from Property Tiers

R' Yochanan
קנין פירות כקנין הגוף — Acquiring produce is like acquiring the item itself. A buyer during Jubilee brings bikkurim and recites the verses.
Reish Lakish
קנין פירות לאו כקנין הגוף — Acquiring produce is not like acquiring the item. He brings bikkurim but does not recite, since the land isn't truly his.
Mishna
Damages are collected from superior land (עידית), debts from intermediate (בינונית), and ketubah from inferior (זיבורית) — all for the betterment of the world.
🔑 Key Theme This daf bridges two major topics: the theoretical debate about the nature of land acquisition during Jubilee, and the practical mishna establishing a tiered system of property collection — both framed as tikkun ha'olam.

Is Acquiring Produce Like Acquiring the Land?

R' Yochanan
When a buyer purchases land during Jubilee, קנין פירות כקנין הגוף — it's as if he owns the land itself. He can say 'the land which Hashem gave me' and recite the bikkurim verses.
Reish Lakish
קנין פירות לאו כקנין הגוף — He only acquired the right to produce, not the land. He brings bikkurim but cannot recite the verses, since calling it 'my land' would be false.
Gemara
Both cases are necessary: explicit produce sale (where Reish Lakish's view is obvious) and Jubilee sale (where the buyer intended to buy the land itself). Neither can be derived from the other.
💡 Why Both Cases? If we only had the Jubilee case, we might think Reish Lakish agrees the buyer recites since he intended to buy the land. If we only had the produce case, we might think R' Yochanan agrees he doesn't recite since he never intended to buy the land.

Bikkurim Recitation: When Does the Buyer Recite?

A person purchases a field and brings first fruits (bikkurim)
↓
Is the Jubilee Year practiced?
No Jubilee
Permanent acquisition → brings bikkurim AND recites (all agree)
Jubilee practiced
Land reverts → R' Yochanan: recites ✅
Reish Lakish: does not recite ❌
📖 Rav Chisda's Distinction The dispute applies only to land sold during the second Jubilee cycle. During the first Jubilee, all agree the buyer recites — people didn't yet rely on the land being returned.

Proofs Brought For and Against R' Yochanan

Supporting R' Yochanan

  • Mishna (Bikkurim 1:11): One who acquires a tree and its land — brings bikkurim and recites
  • Mishna (Bikkurim 1:6): One who acquires three trees acquires the surrounding land and recites
  • Verse: 'The land which Hashem gave me' — ownership language implies full acquisition

Supporting Reish Lakish

  • Firstborn takes double portion in field returning in Jubilee — implying father still 'possessed' it at death
  • Abaye: Husband needs wife's authorization to litigate about her property — showing produce ≠ ownership
  • Verse: 'According to the number of years of crops he sells' — it's produce, not land, that is sold
⚖️ Key Rebuttal Reish Lakish deflects the Bikkurim proofs by saying those mishnayot refer to when Jubilee was not practiced — or per Rav Chisda, to the first Jubilee cycle.

ObligationLand QualityRationale
🔴 Damages (נזיקין)Superior — עידיתTorah law: 'of the best of his field shall he pay'
🟡 Creditor's debt (בעל חוב)Intermediate — בינוניתStandard collection; balances creditor & debtor interests
🔵 Ketubah (כתובה)Inferior — זיבוריתSo men won't hesitate to marry (Talmudic enactment)
🔵 Ketubah — R' MeirIntermediate — בינוניתR' Meir: woman also deserves intermediate-quality collection
🟢 Orphans' debtsInferior — זיבוריתProtection of orphans' inheritance
📖 R' Yishmael's View By Torah law, R' Yishmael says we appraise the injured party's best land, not the damager's. The Sages enacted that we appraise the damager's best land — for the betterment of the world.

When Can a Creditor Collect from Sold (Liened) Property?

📋 Debtor owes money to a creditor
↓
🔍 First check: Does the debtor still have unsold property?
↓
✅ If YES → Collect from debtor's unsold property, even if it's inferior quality
↓
❌ If NO unsold property remains → Creditor may collect from liened (sold) property
↓
⚠️ Exception: Produce value and enhancement can NEVER be collected from liened property
↓
⚠️ Exception: Wife & daughters' sustenance — only from unsold property, never liened
🏛️ Tikkun HaOlam These rules protect buyers who purchased property in good faith. If creditors could always seize sold land, no one would buy property — undermining the entire marketplace.
