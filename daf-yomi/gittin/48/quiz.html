Gittin 48 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Reish Lakish, why can't a buyer during the Jubilee recite the bikkurim verses?
A. Because the produce doesn't belong to him
B. Because acquiring produce is not like acquiring the land itself
C. Because bikkurim can only be brought from inherited land
D. Because the Jubilee cancels all agricultural obligations
Correct: B — Reish Lakish holds that קנין פירות לאו כקנין הגוף — acquiring produce rights is NOT equivalent to owning the land. Since the buyer doesn't truly own the land, he cannot truthfully say 'the land which Hashem gave me.'
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rav Chisda say there is no dispute about land sold during the FIRST Jubilee cycle?
A. The first Jubilee was never actually practiced
B. People didn't yet rely on the land being returned, so the sale was treated as permanent
C. Torah law explicitly addresses only the first Jubilee
D. R' Yochanan and Reish Lakish lived after the first Jubilee
Correct: B — During the first Jubilee cycle after entering Eretz Yisrael, people didn't yet trust that land would actually be returned. Therefore, all agree the buyer recites — the sale was treated more like a permanent acquisition.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
From what quality of land does a creditor collect a debt, according to the Mishna?
A. Superior quality (עידית)
B. Intermediate quality (בינונית)
C. Inferior quality (זיבורית)
D. Whichever quality the creditor prefers
Correct: B — The Mishna establishes a three-tier system: damages from superior (עידית), debts from intermediate (בינונית), and ketubah from inferior (זיבורית). This protects debtors from losing their best land to every creditor.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If a robber sells a stolen field and the buyer improves it, what can the buyer collect from the robber's LIENED (sold) property?
A. Both the purchase price and the value of improvements
B. Only the value of improvements and produce
C. Only the purchase price
D. Nothing — he can only collect from unsold property
Correct: C — The purchase price can be collected even from the robber's liened property, because that obligation was known from the time of sale. But produce value and land enhancement can only be collected from unsold property, since those amounts were unpredictable.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did the Sages enact that a finder who returns a lost item need not take an oath?
A. Because the Torah explicitly exempts finders from oaths
B. Because taking oaths is always prohibited
C. So that people won't be deterred from returning lost items
D. Because the finder already fulfilled a mitzvah and deserves trust
Correct: C — This is a tikkun ha'olam enactment: if finders feared being subjected to oaths, they might avoid returning lost items altogether. Removing the oath requirement encourages people to do the right thing.
