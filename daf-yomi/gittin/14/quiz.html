QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the initial explanation that ma'amad sheloshtan works because the borrower benefits from a new lender?
A. Because the Torah explicitly forbids transferring debts
B. Because harsh creditors like Beit Bar Elyashiv would never grant extensions, making the rule circumstantial
C. Because Shmuel disagreed with this reasoning
D. Because the borrower must give explicit consent in all cases
Correct: B — Huna Mar challenged that borrowers transferred to harsh creditors like Beit Bar Elyashiv would not benefit at all, making the halakha depend on circumstances rather than being absolute. This led to Mar Zutra's explanation that it's a decree without a stated reason.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What are the three halakhot that Mar Zutra says the Sages enacted 'without a reason'?
A. Ma'amad sheloshtan, writing property to wife makes her steward, marrying son in father's house transfers house
B. Ma'amad sheloshtan, agent delivery of debts, gifts of a dying person
C. Acquisition in error, transfer by agent, deathbed gifts
D. Ma'amad sheloshtan, prosbol, writing a get
Correct: A — Mar Zutra lists three: (1) acquisition in presence of three parties, (2) writing all property to one's wife makes her only a steward (eppitrofeya), and (3) marrying one's eldest son in his house transfers ownership of the house to the son.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the case of the gardeners, why did Rav Naḥman ultimately rule that the acquisition reverts?
A. Because ma'amad sheloshtan was not properly performed
B. Because the landowner did not actually want the money
C. Because the transfer was based on a mistaken calculation — an acquisition in error reverts
D. Because Rava overruled Rav Naḥman's authority
Correct: C — Rava pointed out that the gardener wasn't refusing to pay — he was claiming the money was rightfully his because no surplus actually existed. Rav Naḥman agreed: since the basis for the transfer was a miscalculation, it was an acquisition in error, which reverts.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the core of the dispute between Rav and Shmuel regarding an agent sent to deliver a debt payment?
A. Whether the agent can keep the money
B. Whether bearing financial responsibility for loss means the money is still in the sender's domain (allowing retraction)
C. Whether the recipient must be present when the agent is dispatched
D. Whether the agent needs a formal act of acquisition
Correct: B — Rav holds the sender bears responsibility but cannot retract — the obligation is binding. Shmuel holds that since the sender bears loss responsibility, the money is still in his domain, so he can retract. The dispute is whether responsibility implies continued ownership.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rav Pappa resolve the contradiction between the two baraitot about a gift sent via agent when the recipient dies?
A. One baraita deals with a healthy giver, the other with a dying giver
B. One baraita deals with money and the other with property
C. If the recipient died before the giver — return to sender; if the giver died first — give to heirs (mitzva to fulfill the deceased's wishes)
D. If the agent accepted responsibility — heirs get it; if not — return to sender
Correct: C — Rav Pappa explains both baraitot deal with a healthy person. If the recipient died in the giver's lifetime, the transfer never completed — return to sender. If the giver died first, it's a mitzva to fulfill the wishes of the deceased, so the money goes to the recipient's heirs.
