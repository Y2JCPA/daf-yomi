When Can a Husband Void a Get?

Husband sends get with an agent
↓
Has the get entered the wife's possession?
Not yet
Husband can void the get — by reaching agent, sending second agent, or reaching wife directly
Already delivered
Cannot void — divorce has taken effect, even if he was searching to cancel from the start
Key Principle Once the get enters the wife's possession, no retroactive cancellation is possible — the divorce stands.

Three Ways to Cancel a Get Before Delivery

📤 Husband sends get with Agent #1
↓
🏃 Option 1: Husband reaches the agent (even incidentally) and says it's void
↓
📨 Option 2: Husband sends Agent #2 to tell Agent #1 the get is void
↓
👩 Option 3: Husband reaches his wife before the get does and tells her it's void
↓
🚫 All three methods effectively cancel the divorce
Gemara's Clarification The Mishna says 'he reached the agent' — not 'he pursued the agent.' Even an incidental encounter suffices. We do not say he merely intends to vex his wife.

Why State Each Case Separately?

Gemara
Why teach sending a second agent? A person's agent is like himself — it's obvious!
Answer
Lest you say the second agent's power cannot exceed the first agent's, and only the husband himself can cancel.
Gemara
Why teach the case of reaching his wife? Also obvious!
Answer
Lest you say when he tells her directly, he surely only intends to vex her — not actually cancel.
Gemara
Why teach sending an agent to her?
Answer
Lest you say the husband doesn't care if an agent exerts himself for nothing, so he's only vexing her. The Mishna teaches: it's still void.
Principle Each seemingly redundant case in the Mishna addresses a unique potential misconception about the husband's intent.

Formulations: Which Words Void a Get?

StatementEffect
בטל הוא — 'It is void' (batel hu)✅ Get is voided
אי אפשי בו — 'I do not desire it'✅ Get is voided
'It shall not be effective / shall not release / shall not divorce'✅ Prescriptive — voided
'It shall be pottery / like pottery'✅ Prescriptive — voided
פסול הוא — 'It is invalid'❌ No effect
'It is not a bill of divorce'❌ No effect — mere description
'It does not release / does not divorce'❌ Descriptive — no effect
'It is pottery / it is like pottery'❌ Descriptive — no effect
Key Distinction Prescriptive language ('shall be/shall not') = effective cancellation. Descriptive language ('is/does not') = merely stating a false fact, no legal force.

Batel: Get vs. Gift

Bill of Divorce (Get)

  • Saying 'batel' means: let it become void from now
  • This is a prescriptive, forward-looking statement
  • The husband has the power to cancel — so the effective meaning applies
  • Result: Get is voided

Gift (Matana)

  • Saying 'batel' after receiving means: it was void from the start
  • This is a descriptive, backward-looking statement
  • Recipient already acquired it — cannot undo acquisition
  • Result: Gift stands, statement has no effect
Abaye's Rule The word batel has dual meaning. In each context, we interpret it in the way that is legally effective for that domain.

Can a Voided Get Be Reused?

Rav Naḥman
He may go back and divorce with it. He only nullified the agency, not the document itself.
Rav Sheshet
He may not go back and divorce with it. The get itself was rendered void.
Challenge
R' Yoḥanan says speech can retract betrothal within 30 days — shouldn't speech also void the get itself?
Resolution
Betrothal is speech vs. speech. But a get is a tangible object — speech cannot destroy a physical document.
Halakha
The halakha follows Rav Naḥman — the get may be reused.
Legal Logic Cancellation nullifies the agency of delivery, not the document. A physical object (the get) cannot be undone by mere speech.
