Gittin 79 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel (via Rav Yehuda), why can the wife acquire the get thrown up to the roof?
A. The roof has a parapet making it a secured courtyard
B. The get was thrown with sufficient force
C. The husband verbally designated the roof as her domain
D. The roof is considered part of the courtyard below
Correct: A — Shmuel explains the roof has a ma'akeh (parapet), making it a secured courtyard so the airspace can acquire the get.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why do even the Rabbanan (who disagree with Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi) agree the get is valid in the airspace?
A. They actually agree with Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi about airspace
B. For gittin, the key is that the get is secured (mishamer), not that airspace = at rest
C. The Rabbanan's view only applies to monetary law
D. The woman verbally accepted the get while it was in the air
Correct: B — Ulla and R' Asi explain: the Rabbanan only disagree about Shabbat. For gittin, what matters is that the get is secured within the partitions — a different principle than 'airspace = rest.'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If the get's writing was erased while still on its upward arc (ascent), what is the halakha?
A. She is divorced — it already left his domain
B. She is not divorced — it was not yet destined for her domain
C. She is divorced only if there was a parapet
D. It depends on whether she saw the get in the air
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman says: on the ascent, the get is not yet destined for her domain (it could blow elsewhere), so if erased then, she is not divorced.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Abaye's rule about nested courtyards NOT apply to nested baskets?
A. Baskets cannot serve as a woman's domain
B. The outer basket's walls do not service the inner basket
C. Baskets are too small to be valid receptacles
D. The Rabbanan decreed against using baskets for gittin
Correct: B — Unlike courtyard walls which inherently service all space within them, the walls of an outer basket do not service the inner basket. Additionally, her basket inside his basket is like a buyer's vessel in a seller's domain.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Beit Hillel's concern about an outdated get (get yashan)?
A. The ink may have faded making it unreadable
B. People will think the divorce preceded the children's conception, implying illegitimacy
C. The husband may have changed his mind during the interval
D. The witnesses' signatures may no longer be valid
Correct: B — Beit Hillel fear that people will say the divorce happened on the date written on the get — before children were born — making those children appear to have been conceived through illicit relations.
