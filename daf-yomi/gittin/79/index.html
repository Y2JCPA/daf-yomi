Gittin 79: Airspace, Domains & Validity of the Get

Husband throws get between roof and ground
↓
Is the receiving domain secured (partitions/parapet)?
Yes — Secured
She is divorced once it enters the airspace
No — Unsecured
The get is not acquired — not divorced
Key Principle A get thrown between domains is effective only when the receiving domain's airspace is secured — by parapets, lavud, or extending partitions.

↓
Does the get reach the airspace of the roof?
Yes
She is divorced
No
Not divorced
↓
Case 2: Husband on roof, throws get downward
↓
Once it leaves the roof area — she is divorced, even if erased or burned mid-air
Mishna The Mishna distinguishes between throwing up (must reach her airspace) and throwing down (effective once it leaves his domain).

How Is the Roof's Airspace 'Secured'?

Gemara
The airspace of the roof is unsecured — the get can blow away! How is she divorced?
Shmuel (via Rav Yehuda)
We deal with a roof that has a parapet (ma'akeh) — so it is a secured courtyard.
Ulla bar Menashya / Avimi
The get is within 3 tefaḥim of the roof surface — lavud treats it as if already resting on the roof.
Gemara (Case 2)
Below — the courtyard airspace is also unsecured! How is she divorced when the get leaves the roof?
Shmuel / R' Elazar / Ulla
The lower partitions of the courtyard extend beyond the upper roof partitions — the get will certainly fall inside.
Two Solutions Parapet or lavud secures the roof; extending lower walls secure the courtyard below.

Airspace at Rest: Shabbat vs. Gittin

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi

  • An object in airspace is considered at rest (kelutah ke-mi she-naḥah)
  • Applies to both Shabbat and gittin
  • Once the get enters the airspace of her domain — it's as if it landed

The Rabbanan

  • Disagree with Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi — but only regarding Shabbat
  • For Shabbat: object must actually come to rest
  • For gittin: airspace works because the get is secured (mishamer) within the partitions — even Rabbanan agree she is divorced
Ulla & R' Asi The Mishna works even according to the Rabbanan — the key for gittin is that the get is secured, not that airspace = rest.

Erased or Burned Mid-Flight: When Does It Count?

ScenarioDivorced?Reason
Writing erased on descent (entering her domain)✅ YesAlready left his domain and is heading into hers — secured
Writing erased on ascent (still arching upward)❌ NoNot yet destined for her domain — could blow elsewhere
Get burned — throwing preceded the fire✅ YesValid get was thrown; fire came after it entered her airspace
Get burned — fire preceded the throwing❌ NoGet was heading toward destruction from the start — never truly 'given'
Rav Naḥman / Rabba bar Avuh The critical factor is whether at the moment of destruction, the get was destined to arrive in her domain.

Rav Ḥisda: Domains Are Divided for Gittin

📐 Rav Ḥisda: A house, courtyard, and roof within the same property are separate domains for gittin
↓
📜 Proof: The Mishna says she is divorced once the get reaches the roof's airspace — implying roof ≠ courtyard
↓
🏘️ Adjacent roofs on Shabbat: each roof is its own domain (no eiruv = no transfer)
↓
💍 But for gittin: if the get falls onto an adjacent roof belonging to the husband, she is divorced — husband is not particular about lending a neighboring roof
↓
⚖️ Shabbat is stricter about domain separation than gittin
Rava's Distinction On Shabbat, roofs are strictly separated. For gittin, a husband is not particular (lo kapid) about lending an adjacent roof he owns.
