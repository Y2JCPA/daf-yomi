Gittin 45 — Slaves, Scrolls & Safeguards

DAF OVERVIEW This daf transitions from penalties for selling slaves outside Eretz Yisrael to the fate of slaves who flee to Eretz Yisrael, then pivots to purchasing sacred items from gentiles — exploring who may write a Torah scroll, the lishma requirement, and price limits enacted mipnei tikkun ha'olam (for the betterment of the world).
3 / 9

Who Bears the Penalty? The Mouse & the Hole

Abaye
Why do we penalize the purchaser (who must free the slave and loses his money)? We should penalize the seller and make him return the money!
Rav Yosef
לאו עכברא גנב אלא חורא גנב — It is not the mouse that steals, but the hole that steals. Without the buyer, the sale couldn't happen.
Abaye
But without the mouse, the hole would have nothing! Both parties enabled the transgression!
Rav Yosef
We penalize wherever the forbidden item (the slave) currently is — that is with the purchaser.
PRINCIPLE The penalty falls on the party who currently possesses the object of the transgression.

Three Readings of "You Shall Not Deliver" (Deut. 23:16)

The Baraita's Reading

  • Metaphorical: a gentile escaping idol worship
  • "Master" = his idolatrous past
  • Remedy: accept to abandon idolatry → may dwell in Eretz Yisrael

Rabbi Yoshiya

  • One who sells his slave to a Jew outside Eretz Yisrael
  • "He shall dwell with you" = slave stays in Eretz Yisrael
  • Objection: "escaped TO you" doesn't fit — slave is leaving, not arriving

R' Aḥai b. R' Yoshiya

  • Literally: a slave who fled from abroad TO Eretz Yisrael
  • "Escaped to you" fits perfectly
  • Slave is emancipated and remains in Eretz Yisrael
KEY DISPUTE Each opinion must account for precise wording: "to his master," "escaped to you," and "he shall dwell with you."

Rabban Shimon b. Gamliel & the Lishma Requirement

Gemara
R' Shimon b. Gamliel requires parchment be prepared lishma (for the sake of the mitzva). Surely the writing must also be lishma — so how could a gentile's scroll be valid?
Rabba bar Shmuel
The Tzaidan scribe was actually a convert who reverted to gentile ways — but he remains a Jew!
Gemara
If he reverted, he's a heretic (מין) — that's even worse!
Rav Ashi
He reverted out of fear (מחמת יראה), not rejection of Judaism. He is still considered a faithful Jew.
RESOLUTION A convert who reverts due to fear — not conviction — retains full Jewish status and may write sacred texts.
