Gittin 34 — Intent, Identity & Institutional Safeguards

DAF OVERVIEW This daf pivots from the court's authority over orphans' property to a major debate between Abaye and Rava on whether gilui da'at (disclosure of intent) can void a get. Three dramatic case studies are brought as proofs. The Mishnah then introduces Rabban Gamliel's ordinances: requiring all known names in a get, replacing the widow's oath with a vow, mandating witness signatures on a get, and Hillel's prosbol.

Court Authority: Money vs. Prohibition

Monetary Matters (Mamona)

  • Orphans dividing inherited property
  • Court appoints an apotropos (steward)
  • Rav Naḥman: Grown orphans CANNOT protest
  • Court's honor overrides exact distribution

Prohibition Matters (Issura)

  • Voiding a get after it is sent
  • Involves permitting a married woman to remarry
  • Court's honor does NOT override prohibitions
  • Stricter standard applied to divorce law
KEY DISTINCTION The Gemara resolves Rav Naḥman's apparent contradiction: preserving court authority applies in monetary law but not where issur (prohibition) is at stake.

The Case of Giddul bar Re'ilai

📜 Giddul sends a get via an agent to his wife
↓
🧶 Wife is weaving: "Come back tomorrow"
↓
Agent returns and reports to Giddul
↓
😊 Giddul exclaims: "Baruch HaTov v'HaMeitiv!"
(Blessed is He Who is good and does good)
↓
Abaye
He was happy, but the get is NOT voided — this is mere gilui da'at
Rava
His joy shows clear intent — the get IS voided
CORE DISPUTE Gilui da'at b'gittin — Is implicit disclosure of intent (without explicit cancellation) sufficient to void a get?

Rava's Proof: Rav Sheshet's Forced Get

Rava
Rav Sheshet extracted consent for a get from a man by force. The man told witnesses: "This is what Rav Sheshet told you — void the get."
Rava
Rav Sheshet required a new get — proving implicit intent voids it!
Abaye
No! The husband himself voided it. He just blamed Rav Sheshet to avoid being lashed for defying the court.
Abaye
This was an explicit cancellation disguised as someone else's order — not gilui da'at at all.
ANALYSIS Both agree the get was voided — they disagree on why. Rava sees implicit intent; Abaye sees explicit (but disguised) cancellation.

Abaye's Proof: Rav Yehuda's Persistent Extraction

📜 Rav Yehuda extracts consent for a get from R' Yirmeya Bira'a's son-in-law
↓
❌ The man voids the get
↓
📜 Rav Yehuda extracts consent again
↓
❌ The man voids the get again
↓
📜 Rav Yehuda extracts consent a third time
↓
🎃 Tells witnesses: "Place gourd pieces in your ears and write the get!"
↓
🏃 Man runs after witnesses — but they can't hear him
ABAYE'S ARGUMENT If gilui da'at mattered, the witnesses seeing him run should void the get! Yet Rav Yehuda approved it. Rava responds: Since they can't hear him, perhaps he's yelling "Hurry up and deliver it!"
