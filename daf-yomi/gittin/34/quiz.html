QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Rav Naḥman allow the court to override exact property distribution for orphans but not allow voiding a get for the court's honor?
A. Property division is monetary (mamona) where court honor prevails; divorce involves prohibition (issura) which is stricter
B. Orphans are minors and cannot protest, but a wife is an adult with full legal standing
C. Property can always be redistributed later, but a get is permanent
D. The Torah explicitly forbids voiding a get but allows unequal inheritance
Correct: A — The Gemara draws a fundamental distinction: in monetary matters (mamona), preserving the court's authority takes precedence. In matters of prohibition (issura), such as permitting a married woman to remarry, the court's honor cannot override the law.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the case of Giddul bar Re'ilai, what is the significance of his exclamation 'Baruch HaTov v'HaMeitiv'?
A. It is a formal legal declaration that voids the get
B. It reveals his happiness that the get was not delivered — the dispute is whether this gilui da'at voids the get
C. It is a blessing required whenever good news is received, unrelated to the get
D. It constitutes a vow prohibiting the delivery of the get
Correct: B — Giddul's blessing reveals his relief that the get was not delivered. Abaye holds this mere disclosure of intent (gilui da'at) does not void the get, while Rava holds it does — since his intent not to divorce is clear.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rava explain the man running after the witnesses when Rav Yehuda told them to plug their ears with gourd pieces?
A. The man was trying to physically stop them from writing
B. The man may have been yelling 'Hurry up and deliver it!' — not voiding the get
C. The man had changed his mind and wanted to sign the get himself
D. Running is not a legally recognized form of protest
Correct: B — Rava argues that since the witnesses cannot hear the man, his running is ambiguous — he could be shouting 'ashur!' (make haste) and 'hayya!' (give it speedily) to end his suffering, not trying to void the get.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabban Gamliel's ordinance, when must ALL of a person's known names be listed in a get?
A. Always, regardless of circumstances
B. Only when the person is known to have multiple names in different places
C. Only when divorcing a wife in a foreign country
D. Only when the husband and wife have the same name
Correct: B — Rav Ashi clarifies that the requirement to list all names applies only when the person is known to have multiple names. If he travels to a new place where no one knows his other name, only the local name is needed.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Rabban Gamliel replace the widow's OATH with a VOW when collecting her ketubah from orphans?
A. Vows are more lenient than oaths and easier for widows to make
B. Courts had refrained from administering oaths, leaving widows unable to collect — the vow restored their ability to collect
C. Oaths were abolished entirely by Rabbinic decree after the Temple's destruction
D. The orphans requested vows instead of oaths as a compromise
Correct: B — The courts stopped administering oaths to widows (likely due to the severity of a false oath). This left widows unable to collect their ketubah at all. Rabban Gamliel's takkanah allowed the orphans to impose a vow instead, restoring the widow's ability to collect — benefiting both parties.
