Where Can Witnesses Sign?

Mishna
If witnesses signed at the top of the column or on the side, the get is invalid.
Gemara
But didn't Rav sign on the side?
Gemara
Rav positioned the top of his signature facing the text, making it clear he was signing that document.
Gemara
But what about two gittin written in opposite directions with signatures between them?
Resolution
Those signatures were perpendicular (like a door bolt), so they don't clearly face either text. Rav only signed on the side for letters and rulings, not gittin.
Key Rule Signatures on a get must appear below the text. Side-signing is only acceptable for non-legal documents.

R' Yirmeya's Clarification The scribe's handwriting validates only when the scribe also signed as a witness — the text alone is insufficient.

Surnames in a Get: How Many Generations?

Tanna Kamma

  • Ancestral surname valid for 10 generations
  • Broad recognition window
  • Practical for well-known families

R' Shimon ben Elazar

  • Valid for only 3 generations
  • Based on venoshantem (Deut. 4:25)
  • After grandchildren, a name is 'old' and forgotten
  • R' Ḥanina follows this view
Verse Source "When you beget children and children's children" — after three generations, a matter is considered yashan (old).

Why Does Gentile Compulsion Disqualify from Kehuna?

Gemara
If gentiles can't legally compel — it should carry no weight at all! If they can — it should be fully valid!
Rav Mesharshiyya
By Torah law, gentile compulsion is valid. The Rabbis invalidated it so women wouldn't manipulate gentile courts to free themselves.
Gemara
Rejected! If Torah-valid, then unlawful gentile compulsion should still disqualify from kehuna — but Shmuel says it doesn't!
Resolution
Gentile compulsion is fundamentally not a get. The kehuna disqualification in lawful cases is a rabbinic decree — to prevent confusion with valid Jewish compulsion.
Underlying Logic Unlawful gentile compulsion is too dissimilar from lawful Jewish compulsion to cause confusion — so no decree was needed.
