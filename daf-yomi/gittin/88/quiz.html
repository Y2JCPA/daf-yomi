Gittin 88 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why did Rav's practice of signing on the side not contradict the mishna's invalidation of side signatures?
A. Rav only signed on the side for letters and rulings, not for gittin or monetary documents
B. Rav had special dispensation as a leading authority
C. The mishna was discussing a different era's practice
D. Rav signed on the side but added a second signature below
Correct: A — The Gemara concludes that Rav signed on the side only for letters and rulings where signature placement is inconsequential. For gittin and monetary documents, signatures must be below the text.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Yirmeya, when does the mishna say that a scribe's handwriting plus one witness validates a get?
A. When the scribe's handwriting in the text body is recognized by two people
B. When the scribe also signed the get as a witness
C. When the scribe testifies orally that he wrote the text
D. When the scribe's handwriting matches a sample kept in the court
Correct: B — R' Yirmeya clarifies that the scribe must have actually signed the get as a witness — merely recognizing the scribe's handwriting in the body of the text is not sufficient.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does a get compelled lawfully by gentiles disqualify the wife from marrying a kohen, according to the Gemara's conclusion?
A. Because gentile compulsion is Torah-valid but rabbinically invalidated
B. Because the wife initiated the compulsion and is therefore at fault
C. Because people might confuse it with lawful Jewish compulsion and mistakenly validate Jewish-compelled gittin
D. Because it might be confused with lawful Jewish compulsion, leading people to dismiss the validity of Jewish-compelled gittin
Correct: D — The Gemara's conclusion rejects Rav Mesharshiyya's view. The kehuna disqualification is a rabbinic decree to prevent people from equating gentile compulsion with Jewish compulsion and thereby undermining the validity of properly compelled gittin.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
On what basis did Rav Yosef justify Babylonian courts compelling husbands to give gittin?
A. Babylonian scholars were equivalent to ordained judges
B. They acted as agents (sheluḥin) of the ordained judges in Eretz Yisrael
C. Emergency powers granted during the exile
D. The Exilarch gave them special authority
Correct: B — Rav Yosef argued that Babylonian courts derived their authority as agents of the ordained judges in Eretz Yisrael, similar to their authority in cases of admissions and loans — but only for commonly arising matters.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Shimon ben Elazar, for how many generations is an ancestral surname valid in a get?
A. One generation
B. Three generations
C. Seven generations
D. Ten generations
Correct: B — R' Shimon ben Elazar limits ancestral surnames to three generations, derived from the verse 'children and children's children' (Deuteronomy 4:25) — after which a name is considered 'old' (yashan) and forgotten. The Tanna Kamma permits up to ten generations.
