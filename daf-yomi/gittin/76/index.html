Must a Condition Be Compounded? (Tenai Kaful)

R' Meir
A condition must be compounded (tenai kaful) — stated in both positive and negative forms. If not, the condition is void and the get is valid.
Rabbis
A condition is valid even without double formulation. If unfulfilled, the get is not valid.
R' Shimon b. Gamliel
Every condition in the Torah is compounded — but which side does this support?
Two Readings of RSBG Some say RSBG supports R' Meir (compound conditions are two verses teaching one thing — cannot derive a general rule). Others say he supports the Rabbis (we do learn from the Torah that conditions must be compounded).

When Fulfillment Becomes Impossible

Husband says: 'Get on condition you serve my father 2 years' or 'nurse my son 2 years'
↓
The father dies or the son dies
↓
Is the get valid?
R' Meir
Not valid — the condition was not fulfilled (here he did compound it)
Rabbis (= RSBG)
Valid — she can say: 'Give me your father and I will serve him!' The hindrance is not from her
Resolving the Contradiction R' Meir's two baraitot are reconciled: where the get is valid, he didn't compound the condition (so it's void). Where it's invalid, he did compound it. The 'Rabbis' in the second baraita are actually RSBG.

Key Distinction When the second condition is of a different kind, the husband is offering a choice. When it is the same kind with a different amount, he is retracting and replacing the first condition.

Geographic Conditions: Abaye's Explanation

Galilee Condition

  • Antipatris is in Judea, Kefar Otnai is in Galilee
  • Husband's dual condition: If I reach Galilee → get valid immediately
  • OR if I tarry 30 days without returning → get valid
  • He reached Antipatris and returned → neither fulfilled → not divorced

Overseas Condition

  • Akko is part of Eretz Yisrael (Rav Safra: Sages took leave there)
  • Husband's dual condition: If I reach overseas → get valid immediately
  • OR if I tarry 30 days without returning → get valid
  • He reached Akko and returned → neither fulfilled → not divorced
Abaye's Method Abaye resolves each mishna by reading the husband's statement as containing two alternative conditions, neither of which was met when he turned back from a location that did not cross the relevant boundary.

What Does 'Departing from Your Presence' Mean?

Rav Huna
'Your presence' is a euphemism for sexual intercourse. If he doesn't have relations with her for 30 days, the get is valid — even if he was going and coming.
R' Yochanan
'Your presence' means literally being in her presence. Since he was not secluded with her for 30 days, the get is not considered outdated (get yashan). When 30 consecutive days of absence are fulfilled, the get takes effect.
Gemara
A baraita supports R' Yochanan: going and coming without seclusion means the get is valid and not considered outdated.
Practical Difference For Rav Huna, the get takes effect after the 30 days of 'going and coming' themselves. For R' Yochanan, those 30 days only preserve the get from being outdated — divorce happens when 30 consecutive days of absence are eventually completed.
