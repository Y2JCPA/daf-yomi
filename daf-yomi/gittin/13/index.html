Is Emancipation Detrimental to a Slave?

R' Meir
A priest's slave who flees may still eat teruma. But once freed, he may never eat teruma again — emancipation harms him!
Rabbis
The master can sell him to an Israelite for 4 dinars at any time — disqualifying him from teruma anyway.
R' Shmuel b. R' Yitzchak
Even for a slave of an Israelite, emancipation is detrimental: he loses the right to a Canaanite maidservant.
Gemara
But he gains the right to marry a free Jewish woman!
R' Shmuel b. R' Yitzchak
A slave prefers licentiousness — a maidservant is lowly, available, and unrestricted for him. He considers emancipation a loss.
R' Meir's Logic Since emancipation can be detrimental, a master cannot force it upon a slave through a third party — and the slave cannot acquire the document on his own behalf.

Mishna: Giving Documents vs. Money After Death

Get / Shtar Shichrur

  • "Give this get to my wife"
  • "Give this manumission to my slave"
  • If giver dies → ❌ Do NOT give
  • Agency is canceled at death
  • Document requires living principal

Monetary Gift

  • "Give 100 dinars to so-and-so"
  • If giver dies → ✅ DO give
  • Gift obligation survives death
  • Rav: Only if dinars are 'piled in a corner'
  • Money treated as already designated
Key Distinction A get requires the husband to be alive as principal of the agent. A monetary gift, once designated, can be delivered posthumously.

Why Must Dinars Be 'Piled Up'?

Rav Zevid
The mishna discusses a healthy person. Transfer works via ma'amad sheloshtan (Rav Huna/Rav) — which requires the money to be like a deposit, i.e., piled and identifiable.
Rav Pappa
The mishna discusses a shechiv mera (deathbed). Verbal instructions suffice — but money must be piled to exclude concern about buried dinars of unknown location.
Rav Zevid
It can't be a shechiv mera! The mishna says one must say "Give" — but for a deathbed person, even "Write" suffices (per R' Shimon Shezuri).
Rav Ashi
Who says the mishna follows R' Shimon Shezuri? Perhaps it follows the Rabbis, who require "Give" even for a deathbed person!
Halakha We are NOT concerned about buried dinars — even unspecified money can be given.

Ma'amad Sheloshtan: Three-Party Transfer

Party A (Owner/Lender) has money held by Party B (Guardian/Borrower)
↓
A says to B in presence of C: "Give my money to C"
↓
Does C acquire it immediately?
↓
Yes — Rav Huna/Rav
All three present → C acquires. No physical act of kinyan needed.
Rava's Clarification
Works for deposits AND loans — Rav said so explicitly
Ma'amad Sheloshtan A unique rabbinic mechanism: when all three parties are present, a verbal instruction transfers the monetary obligation directly — no kinyan required.
