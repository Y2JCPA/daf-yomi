Gittin 70: Health, Healing & Mental Competence

DAF OVERVIEW Amud A presents a remarkable catalog of Talmudic medical advice — remedies for skin disease, fatal wounds, aḥilu (bone fever), and dietary wisdom including Elijah's famous 'rule of thirds.' Amud B pivots to a critical halakhic debate: if a husband orders a get while lucid but then becomes temporarily insane, can the court write it immediately? Reish Lakish compares him to a sleeping person (write now), while R' Yochanan compares him to an imbecile (wait for recovery).

Rav Pappa adds
Even overeating dates can cause aḥilu — despite their benefits
General rule
Filling one's stomach with anything in excess causes aḥilu
IRONY Dates satisfy, warm, strengthen, and act as a laxative — yet even beneficial foods harm when eaten to excess.

Abaye's Remedy for Aḥilu (from His Mother)

🍽️ Eat and drink first (unlike most medicines taken on empty stomach)
↓
🚻 Enter the bathroom and relieve oneself
↓
🧼 Exit and wash hands
↓
🥣 Eat a fistful of shetita (lentils) mixed with a fistful of aged wine
↓
🛏️ Wrap in a sheet and sleep — let NO ONE wake him
↓
⏰ When he awakens on his own, remove the sheet
↓
⚠️ If he doesn't follow procedure exactly, the illness returns
UNIQUE Most medicinal drinks are taken on an empty stomach for 3, 7, or 12 days. Aḥilu is the exception — taken after eating, and continued until healed.

Elijah's Rule of Thirds

אכול שליש ושתה שליש והנח שליש
PROPHETIC WISDOM Elijah the Prophet said to Rabbi Natan:

🍞 Eat a third of your fill
🍷 Drink a third of your fill
🫁 Leave a third empty

So that when you become angry, you will become full — anger expands the body, and if your stomach is already full, you will be harmed.

R' Ḥiyya adds: Dip food in wine or vinegar to avoid intestinal disease. Stop eating from a meal you enjoy. Never delay when you need to relieve yourself.

Writing a Get for One Who Became Insane

Mishna
If he said 'Write a get for my wife' while lucid, then became temporarily insane and retracted — his latter statement is nothing.
Reish Lakish
The court writes and gives the get immediately — even while he is insane. His initial instruction stands!
R' Yochanan
They write the get only once he is healed and returns to a sound state of mind.
Reish Lakish
I compare the insane man to a sleeping person — if he said 'write' and fell asleep, we write it without waking him.
R' Yochanan
I compare him to an imbecile (shoteh) — his mind is confused, he lacks intent.
Gemara
A sleeper needs no action to awaken. The insane person requires a remedy — that's a meaningful difference.
Gemara
But unlike an imbecile, the temporarily insane person has a remedy available — lean red meat + diluted wine (from 67b).
KEY DISTINCTION Reish Lakish: Temporary insanity = sleep (passive state, original intent valid)
R' Yochanan: Temporary insanity = imbecility (mind is confused, must wait for clarity)
