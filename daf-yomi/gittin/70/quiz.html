Gittin 70 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why can even dates cause aḥilu despite being beneficial?
A. Dates are only beneficial when eaten with meat
B. Any food eaten to excess can cause aḥilu
C. Dates lose their nutritional value in summer
D. Dates are harmful when combined with wine
Correct: B — The Gemara establishes that filling one's stomach with ANYTHING — even beneficial dates that satisfy, warm, and strengthen the body — can cause aḥilu when eaten to excess.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is unique about the remedy for aḥilu compared to other medicinal drinks?
A. It must be taken for exactly 12 days
B. It is taken after eating, not on an empty stomach
C. It requires fasting for three days first
D. It must be administered by a physician
Correct: B — Most medicinal drinks are taken on an empty stomach for 3, 7, or 12 days. The aḥilu remedy is exceptional — taken AFTER eating and drinking, and continued until healed rather than for a set number of days.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does R' Yochanan NOT compare a temporarily insane person to a sleeping person?
A. Because a sleeping person cannot authorize a get
B. Because a sleeper awakens on his own without any action, but the insane person requires a remedy
C. Because sleeping and insanity have the same halakhic status
D. Because the mishna explicitly distinguishes between them
Correct: B — R' Yochanan argues that a sleeper needs no action to awaken (he wakes on his own), but a temporarily insane person REQUIRES an active remedy — this meaningful difference makes the sleeping analogy inappropriate.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How can a man whose throat has been slit authorize a get but a temporarily insane man may not (per R' Yochanan)?
A. The slit-throat victim has more time to live
B. The slit-throat victim's mind is lucid even though his body is dying; the insane man's mind is confused
C. Shmuel disagrees with R' Yochanan on this point
D. The slit-throat case only applies when both pipes are partially severed
Correct: B — The critical factor is MENTAL clarity, not physical health. The mortally wounded man's mind remains lucid (he simply can't speak), while the temporarily insane person's mind is confused — and a get requires mental competence.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the core factor that distinguishes temporary insanity from permanent imbecility in Reish Lakish's view?
A. The temporarily insane person can still communicate
B. The imbecile never gave an initial instruction
C. A remedy exists for temporary insanity but not for imbecility
D. The Torah treats them differently by explicit decree
Correct: C — Reish Lakish argues that because a REMEDY EXISTS for temporary insanity (lean red meat + diluted wine), the person's competence is merely suspended — unlike an imbecile for whom no cure exists and who therefore cannot authorize a get.
