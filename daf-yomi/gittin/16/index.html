Water Connections: What Joins and What Doesn't?

Do NOT Connect

  • A flowing stream (נִצּוֹק)
  • Water descending an incline (קַטַפְרֵס)
  • Liquid that merely rendered an item moist (מַשְׁקֶה טוֹפֵחַ)
  • These don't connect for impurity OR purity

DOES Connect

  • Liquid moist enough to render another item moist (טוֹפֵחַ עַל מְנָת לְהַטְפִּיחַ)
  • This level of moisture joins with other liquid
  • But does it apply beyond mikvah laws?
Key Distinction The critical threshold is טוֹפֵחַ על מנת להטפיח — moisture substantial enough to make something else moist. Below this level, no connection exists.

Ilfa's Dilemma: Moisture Connecting Two Halves of a Hand

Ilfa
If one washes half a hand, then pours water on the other half — does moisture on the first half connect the two washings?
Gemara
The dilemma must involve moisture at the level of טוֹפֵחַ על מנת להטפיח — otherwise it clearly doesn't connect.
Gemara
But we already know that level of moisture does connect! So what's the dilemma?
Gemara
Perhaps that rule applies only to mikvaot, per R' Yehuda — and hand-washing is a different matter entirely.
Unresolved Ilfa's dilemma remains תֵּיקוּ — unresolved. The connection between mikvah rules and hand-washing rules is never settled.

R' Yehuda's Mikvah: Two People in 40 Se'ah

Mikvah has exactly 40 se'ah
Two people come to immerse
↓
Immerse Together
✅ Both are tahor (pure)
Immerse Sequentially
First is pure ✅
Second is impure ❌
(water clings to first person → mikvah now < 40 se'ah)
↓
R' Yehuda's View: If the first person's feet still touch the water when the second immerses → second is also pure
↓
R' Yehuda holds: water on a person's body connects to the mikvah water (טוֹפֵחַ על מנת להטפיח)
Principle R' Yehuda considers the water clinging to the first person's body as still connected to the mikvah, maintaining the 40 se'ah minimum.

Split Testimony on a Get from Overseas

📜 A get arrives from overseas requiring the declaration: 'It was written and signed in my presence'
↓
👤 Witness 1 says: 'It was written in my presence'
↓
👤 Witness 2 says: 'It was signed in my presence'
↓
❌ The Mishna rules: The get is invalid — neither witness testified to the complete process
↓
🔑 R' Yochanan qualifies: This applies only when the two are not both agents delivering the get
Key Rule The full declaration — written and signed in my presence — must come from a single witness. Splitting it between two people is insufficient.
