Gittin 42: The Half-Slave's Legal Identity

Master frees half his slave
What happens to the other half?
↓
Rabba
If master sold or gifted other half → all agree slave acquired half-freedom
Abaye's Challenge
Contradicting baraitot suggest the dispute applies even when fully released
↓
Further cases: ox killing a half-slave, injury compensation, and whether a slave awaiting a get shichrur still has a 'master'
Core Theme The daf explores the tangled legal identity of a half-slave half-freeman — from property transfers to tort liability to the meaning of 'master.'

Freeing Half a Slave: When Does Everyone Agree?

Rabba
The dispute between Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi and the Rabbis applies only when the master freed half and kept the other half.
Rabba
But if he freed half and sold or gifted the other half — since the slave left him entirely — all agree the slave acquired half-freedom.
Abaye
Do they not disagree even when the slave is released entirely? Two baraitot contradict each other!
Abaye
One says: If a master gives property to his two slaves, they free each other. The other says: They did not acquire even themselves!
Key Issue Can a slave acquire partial freedom when the master retains no ownership at all?
4 / 9

The Two Contradicting Baraitot

Baraita #1

  • Master writes property to his two slaves
  • They acquired the property
  • Each owns half of the other
  • They free each other

Baraita #2

  • Master says: 'All my property to my two slaves'
  • They did NOT acquire even themselves
  • All the more so they did not acquire the property
Abaye's Reading Baraita #1 follows R' Yehuda HaNasi (partial emancipation works); Baraita #2 follows the Rabbis (it does not).
5 / 9

Reconciling the Baraitot: Multiple Approaches

Approach 1: Both follow the Rabbis.
'All to each' = they acquire.
'Half to each' = they don't.
↓
Challenge: The baraita itself distinguishes these cases — implying the first clause is 'all to each' and they still don't acquire!
↓
Approach 2: The second clause explains the first — 'they didn't acquire' means specifically when master said 'half to each.'
↓
Approach 3: One document vs. two.
One document for two slaves = invalid emancipation.
Two documents = valid.
Principle You cannot emancipate two slaves with a single document (shtar). Separate documents are required.

Temporary Injury: Abaye vs. Rava

Gemara
When is 'the principal NOT consumed'? When an ox strikes the slave's hand and it withers but will heal.
Abaye
The injurer pays major loss of livelihood (decrease in slave-market value) and minor loss of livelihood (actual lost wages).
Rava
He pays only daily lost wages — not the decrease in value, since the slave will heal.
Gemara
Problem for Rava: An ox pays only damage (market value), not livelihood. So what does the ox owner pay?
Gemara
Answer: The case involves a person striking the slave, not an ox. Or: Rava need not follow this amora's ruling.
Distinction An ox owner pays nezek (damage) only; a person who injures pays shevet (lost livelihood) as well.
