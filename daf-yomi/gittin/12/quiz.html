QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the proof from pe'a that R' Eliezer and the Rabbis disagree about seizing property on behalf of a creditor?
A. Each side's reasoning may be specific to pe'a and not generalizable
B. Pe'a is a rabbinic institution while seizing property is biblical
C. R' Eliezer later retracted his opinion about pe'a
D. The pe'a dispute is about a different kind of acquisition entirely
Correct: A — The Gemara shows that R' Eliezer's leniency may be limited to pe'a (where one could become poor) and the Rabbis' stringency may derive only from the specific verse about pe'a — so neither view necessarily extends to seizing property.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does a slave with consecrated hands manage to eat, according to the Gemara?
A. The master feeds him from non-consecrated funds
B. He performs work worth less than a peruta, which doesn't become consecrated
C. The Temple treasury provides him with food directly
D. Consecration is suspended during mealtimes
Correct: B — Items worth less than one peruta do not become consecrated. The slave borrows to eat, then repays from accumulated sub-peruta earnings.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Temple treasury prefer that a consecrated slave eat more than the minimum?
A. It fulfills a mitzvah to feed the hungry
B. A well-fed slave increases in value, benefiting the treasury
C. The Torah prohibits starving anyone under temple jurisdiction
D. The slave would otherwise escape to avoid starvation
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that it is preferable to the treasury that the slave eat well and become stronger, thereby increasing his value as temple property.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Yoḥanan, when someone severs a slave's hand, why do the medical cost savings from strong medicine go to the master rather than the slave?
A. The slave has no legal standing to receive money
B. The master owns all property associated with the slave, including cost savings
C. The strong medicine was purchased by the master
D. The slave consented to the painful treatment voluntarily
Correct: B — Despite the slave enduring extra pain from the strong medicine that shortened healing time, the financial savings belong to the master because the slave's body and its associated costs/benefits belong to the master.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is R' Meir's argument that emancipation can be detrimental to a slave?
A. A freed slave loses the protection of his master's household
B. A freed slave must pay taxes that a slave is exempt from
C. A slave of a Kohen loses the right to eat teruma upon emancipation
D. A freed slave cannot own property for seven years after release
Correct: C — R' Meir argues that if the slave belongs to a Kohen, emancipation disqualifies him from eating teruma — a tangible loss that makes freedom a detriment rather than a benefit.
