QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yoḥanan, why did Rabban Gamliel decree that a husband cannot void a get in absentia?
A. To prevent deserted wives (agunot)
B. To prevent mamzerim from unknowingly invalid divorces
C. To uphold the authority of the court
D. To ensure proper witnesses are present
Correct: B — R' Yoḥanan holds the concern is mamzerim: if the wife doesn't hear about the voiding (because it was done before only 2 people, per Rav Naḥman), she may remarry and bear illegitimate children.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara explain the Sages' power to override Torah-level marriage when a husband voids a get against the decree?
A. The Sages have direct legislative authority over Torah law
B. All betrothals are made contingent upon Rabbinic approval, so Sages can retroactively expropriate them
C. The husband's action is considered a Rabbinic violation only, not affecting Torah status
D. The get is never truly void since agents already delivered it
Correct: B — The principle of אפקעינהו — anyone who betroths does so contingent upon the Sages' will (אדעתא דרבנן מקדש). When the husband defies their decree, they retroactively expropriate the betrothal.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the key difference between the two explanations for Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel's requirement to nullify instructions before all agents?
A. One requires a court and the other does not
B. One concerns the number of witnesses and the other concerns the number of judges
C. One is based on partially invalidated testimony being fully invalid, the other on needing the same forum to revoke as to create
D. One applies only to writing the get and the other only to delivering it
Correct: C — Explanation 1: partially invalidated testimony is entirely invalidated, creating risk of unknowing agents delivering an invalid get. Explanation 2: a matter created before 10 people requires 10 to revoke — a procedural principle about form.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Ravina challenges the concept of retroactive expropriation of betrothal. What is his objection?
A. Expropriation only works for Rabbinic marriages, not Torah-level ones
B. If the husband betrothed via intercourse rather than money, there is nothing to expropriate
C. The betrothal happened too long ago to be retroactively annulled
D. Witnesses to the betrothal cannot be overridden by Rabbinic decree
Correct: B — Ravina asks: expropriation works when betrothal was with money (the Sages declare the money ownerless). But if betrothal was via intercourse, what can be expropriated? The Gemara answers: the Sages declare it licentious intercourse (בעילת זנות).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why does the mishna mention BOTH that judges may sign a prosbol AND that witnesses may sign?
A. To teach that a prosbol requires signatures from both judges and witnesses
B. To teach that terminology doesn't matter — judges can sign a witness-style prosbol and vice versa
C. To prove Rav Naḥman's position that two judges suffice
D. To distinguish between Eretz Yisrael and Babylonian practice
Correct: B — The Gemara explains: the dual mention teaches that the precise wording doesn't matter — whether the prosbol uses judge terminology or witness terminology, either judges or witnesses may sign it. The key is that it was properly written and signed.
