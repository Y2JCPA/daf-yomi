Gittin 27 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabba, why does the Mishna here rule a delayed-found get invalid while Bava Metzia allows returning lost documents?
A. Our Mishna is about a place where caravans are frequently found; Bava Metzia is about a place where they are not
B. Our Mishna discusses a get; Bava Metzia discusses monetary documents which are less strict
C. Our Mishna is the earlier ruling that was later overturned by Bava Metzia
D. Our Mishna requires witnesses; Bava Metzia does not
Correct: A — Rabba resolves the contradiction by distinguishing between locations with frequent caravans (more identity confusion risk) and locations without them.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In a place where caravans ARE frequently found, when does Rabba say we should still return the document?
A. When the husband confirms in writing
B. When it is NOT established that two people with the same name live in the town
C. When the get is less than 30 days old
D. Never — in a caravan area, a found get is always invalid
Correct: B — Rabba holds that even in a busy area, we only refuse to return a document if it's established (muḥzaq) that two people share the same name in that town.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rav Ashi's unique requirement for returning a lost get to its owner?
A. The husband must come to court personally
B. Two witnesses must testify they saw it fall
C. The claimant must identify a hole next to a specific letter — a clear-cut siman
D. The get must be found within the same city it was written
Correct: C — Rav Ashi requires a siman muvhak (clear-cut distinguishing mark) because he is uncertain whether regular simanim work by Torah law. Identifying a hole's precise location qualifies.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why was Rabba bar bar Ḥana uncertain about the basis for the court returning his lost get?
A. He didn't know if the get was really his
B. He couldn't tell if they relied on his distinguishing mark (simanim by Torah law) or his visual recognition (trusted as a Torah scholar)
C. The court didn't explain their reasoning as required
D. He had two gets and wasn't sure which one they returned
Correct: B — Each basis would establish a different legal precedent: simanim as Torah law vs. special trust given to Torah scholars for visual recognition. The practical outcome was the same but the principle differed.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rabbi Natan define 'not immediately' (the time after which a found get is invalid)?
A. The time it takes to walk from one end of town to the other
B. The time it takes for a caravan to pass by and camp in that spot
C. As long as no person is seen standing or passing nearby
D. A fixed period of one hour
Correct: B — R' Natan uses a practical, time-based measure — the amount of time for a caravan to pass through and encamp. This contrasts with R' Shimon ben Elazar's surveillance-based approach.
