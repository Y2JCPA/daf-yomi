Gittin 46 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Version 1 of Rav Naḥman's statement, why is a man prohibited from remarrying a wife he divorced due to her bad reputation?
A. He might later cast aspersions on the get, making her children mamzerim
B. To deter women from acting improperly
C. Because a vow taken publicly cannot be dissolved
D. Because the divorce was a mistaken transaction
Correct: A — Version 1 focuses on preventing potential harm: the husband might later claim the get was given under false pretenses, retroactively invalidating the divorce and making her children from a second marriage mamzerim.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the practical difference between the two versions of Rav Naḥman's ruling?
A. Whether the husband needs to give a get at all
B. Whether the husband must say 'Be aware' or merely state the reason
C. Whether the wife keeps her ketubah
D. Whether a sage can dissolve the vow
Correct: B — Version 1 (harm prevention) only requires stating the reason; Version 2 (deterrence) requires the formal declaration 'Be aware (הוי יודעת)' to the wife, in order to publicize the consequence.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yitzḥak, how many people must be present for a vow to be considered 'public' and undissolvable?
A. 3 — derived from 'yamim rabbim'
B. 7 — like the days of creation
C. 10 — derived from 'congregation' (edah) in the Gibeonite episode
D. 12 — like the tribes of Israel
Correct: C — Rabbi Yitzḥak derives from the verse about the Gibeonites that 'leaders of the congregation' swore — and a congregation (edah) consists of at least 10, as derived from the episode of the spies.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When a husband divorces his wife because of HIS OWN vow (konam), why is he permitted to remarry her?
A. Because Rabbi Natan's principle about vows applies only to Temple offerings
B. Because the tikkun ha-olam restriction was instituted only for cases of her vow, not his
C. Because a husband's vow is automatically dissolved upon divorce
D. Because only vows about specific foods count
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the betterment-of-the-world concern — deterring women from improper behavior or preventing harm — applies only when she took the vow. When he vows, there is no such concern and he may remarry her.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Abaye resolve the apparent contradiction that Rabbi Yehuda seems concerned about harm in the ailonit case but unconcerned in the vow case?
A. He reverses the opinions in both mishnayot
B. He says R' Yehuda changed his mind over time
C. He says R' Yehuda follows R' Elazar for vows needing dissolution (no concern) and R' Meir for other cases (concern applies)
D. He says the two mishnayot discuss different time periods
Correct: C — Abaye maintains both mishnayot as stated and explains that R' Yehuda follows R' Elazar (no harm concern for vows requiring court dissolution, since a husband wouldn't disgrace his wife) and R' Meir (harm concern applies in other cases like ailonit).
