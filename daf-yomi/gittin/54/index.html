Gittin 54: Penalties for Unintentional Transgressions

קנסו שוגג אטו מזיד
Core Theme The Gemara resolves a series of apparent contradictions among the Tannaim regarding when the Sages penalize unintentional offenders. It distinguishes between Torah-level and rabbinic prohibitions, between Shabbat and Shemitah, and between common and uncommon cases — then shifts to priestly liability for disqualifying offerings and rules of credibility.
3 / 9

Why Penalize Shemitah but Not Shabbat?

R' Meir (Reason 1)
People don't count planting years from Shabbat — so no one will remember it was planted on Shabbat. No need for a penalty.
Gemara Challenge
But sometimes the 30th day before Rosh Hashana falls on Shabbat — planting that day counts as a full year for orla. People would remember!
R' Meir (Reason 2)
Alternatively: Jews are suspected of violating Shemitah but not suspected of violating Shabbat. So a penalty is needed only for Shemitah.
R' Yehuda
In my locale, Shemitah was treated as extremely serious — calling someone a Shemitah violator was a grave insult. No penalty needed for something people already avoid.
Proof Story A man insulted another: 'Convert, son of a convert!' The reply: 'At least I don't eat Shemitah produce like you!' — showing how seriously Shemitah was regarded.

Key Dispute R' Meir holds the Sages did not penalize an unintentional offender on account of an intentional one (קנסו שוגג אטו מזיד). The Rabbis hold they did.

Orla Nuts from Perekh: Nullification Rules

🌰 Orla nuts from Perekh fall into permitted nuts
↓
Whole nuts = significant items (davar she-be-minyan) — not nullified
↓
What if the nuts were broken (no longer significant)?
R' Meir & R' Yehuda
Whether broken intentionally or unintentionally — NOT nullified
R' Yosei & R' Shimon
Unintentionally broken → nullified ✅
Intentionally broken → NOT nullified ❌
Contradiction Resolved R' Yehuda penalizes the unintentional offender here because without a penalty, people would use artifice — intentionally breaking nuts to circumvent the rule.
6 / 9

R' Yosei's Apparent Contradiction on Nullification

Orla Nuts (Strict)

  • Intentionally broken nuts are NOT nullified
  • Penalty imposed on intentional offender
  • Common scenario — easy to exploit

Orla Sapling in Vineyard (Lenient)

  • Even if intentionally gathered, produce IS nullified in 1:200
  • No penalty imposed
  • Uncommon — no one renders an entire vineyard forbidden for one sapling
Resolution (Rava / R' Yochanan) There is a presumption that a person does not render his entire vineyard forbidden for one sapling. Since the scenario is uncommon, the Sages did not impose a penalty.

Mishna: Priestly Liability for Disqualified Offerings

הכהנים שפיגלו במקדש מזידין — חייבין
New Mishna If priests disqualified an offering through improper intention (piggul — intending to sprinkle blood, burn fats, or eat the offering after its appointed time) and did so intentionally, they are liable to pay the owner the value of the offering.
