Rav Huna's Parallel: Get = Gift for a Shechiv Mera

Rav Huna
A shechiv mera's get is like his gift: if he recovers, both are revoked. And just as 'Write a get' means write and give, so too 'Give a gift' needs no formal kinyan.
R' Avina
Based on Rav Huna, I can collect Geneiva's bequest of 400 dinars from wine — no act of acquisition needed!
R' Abba
Not so fast. Geneiva gave only a portion of his estate. A shechiv mera giving part of his estate requires a kinyan.
Gemara
Geneiva's case is different — he gave instructions due to imminent death (metzaveh machamat mita). The principle: מצוה לקיים דברי המת — it is a mitzva to fulfill the words of the deceased — applies even to partial gifts.
Key Distinction A standard shechiv mera gift of part of an estate requires kinyan, but instructions given due to imminent death invoke the principle of fulfilling the deceased's wishes.

Voice from a Pit: Is It Human or a Demon?

Appointing Agents: 'Write' vs. 'Give' a Get

Said to 2: 'Write & give'

  • They must write it themselves
  • They sign as witnesses
  • Cannot delegate to a scribe

Said to 3: 'Write & give'

  • They must write it themselves
  • They also give it to the wife
  • 3 people = still personal instruction

Said to 3: 'Give a get'

  • R' Meir: They are a court (beit din)
  • They tell others to write it
  • Court has authority to delegate
Mishna Rule 'Write' = do it yourself. 'Give' to three people = they become a court with power to delegate the writing.

R' Meir vs. R' Yosei: Can a Court Delegate Writing?

R' Meir
When a man tells three people 'Give a get to my wife,' he has made them a beit din. They may instruct others to write it.
R' Akiva
I received this tradition in prison: telling three 'Give a get' makes them a court — they tell others to write.
R' Yosei
We told R' Ḥanina of Ono: Even if he said this to the Sanhedrin in Jerusalem, they must learn to write and write it themselves!
Shmuel / R' Yehuda HaNasi
The halakha follows R' Yosei: מילי לא מימסרן לשליח — verbal directives cannot be delegated to another agent.
Halakha Shmuel rules like R' Yosei: verbal instructions (milei) cannot be transferred to a third party, so agents must write the get themselves.
