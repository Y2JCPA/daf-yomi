"If" vs "On the Condition" — Retroactivity

Rav Huna / Rav
Anyone who says "on the condition that" (עַל מְנָת) means the agreement takes effect retroactively from now.
Our Rabbis
If he said "if the sun emerges" and died at night — the get is valid, per R' Yosei: the date in the document proves when it takes effect.
Stam Mishna
The get is invalid — it cannot take effect after the husband's death. We do not hold like R' Yosei.
Key Distinction "On the condition" = retroactive from now. "If" = takes effect only when condition is fulfilled — too late if husband dies first.

Time Conditions: What Does "After" Mean?

Husband SaysGet Valid After
"If I don't return after a Shemittah cycle"One full year after the 7-year cycle ends
"If I don't return after one year"One month after the year ends
"If I don't return after one month"One week after the month ends
"If I don't return after a week"Sun–Tue = after Shabbat; Wed–Fri = before Shabbat
"If I don't return after the Festival" (R' Yehuda HaNasi)30 days after the festival — but Sages did not accept this as majority ruling
Pattern "After" always adds one smaller time unit beyond the stated period.

Write & Give Within 12 Months: R' Yosei vs Rabbis

R' Yosei

  • Get written within 12 months, given after → Valid
  • Husband said "Write and give if I don't come" — meaning: write now, give later
  • The word order matters: writing is authorized immediately

Rabbis (Majority)

  • Get written within 12 months, given after → Invalid
  • No difference in formulation — court cannot parse subtle word order
  • Writing and giving must both occur after condition is fulfilled
Rav Ashi's Reading R' Yosei infers from word order: "Write and give if I don't come" = write NOW, give if I don't come. Not that conditions don't matter.

→ She is divorced
His Domain

Even if get is on her bed
→ Not divorced
↓

Simultaneous Acquisition: Get & Courtyard Together

Rav Ilish
Problem: What a wife acquires belongs to her husband! How can her courtyard acquire the get if it's really his?
Rava
Consider: Is her hand owned by her husband? Yet she is divorced when the get reaches her hand!
Rava
The get and her full ownership of the courtyard enter her possession simultaneously (גִּטָּהּ וַחֲצֵרָהּ בָּאִין כְּאֶחָד).
Rav Ashi
Proof from a slave: a master's hand owns the slave's body. Yet a bill of manumission placed in the slave's hand works — because the bill and his hand enter his possession simultaneously.
Principle The get severs the husband's rights at the exact moment it arrives — so the courtyard becomes fully hers at the instant it acquires the get.
