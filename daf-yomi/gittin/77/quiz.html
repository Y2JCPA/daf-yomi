Gittin 77 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Huna in the name of Rav, what is the legal effect of saying 'on the condition that' (עַל מְנָת)?
A. The condition takes effect only in the future when fulfilled
B. The agreement takes effect retroactively from the time it was stated
C. The condition is void and the get is automatically valid
D. The get requires additional witnesses to be valid
Correct: B — Rav Huna says in the name of Rav: anyone who states a condition using 'on the condition' is like one who says the agreement takes effect retroactively from now — unlike 'if,' which only takes effect when the condition is fulfilled.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
If a husband says 'This is your get if I don't return after one year,' when does the get become valid?
A. Immediately when the year ends
B. One week after the year ends
C. One month after the year ends
D. One year after the year ends
Correct: C — The baraita teaches a consistent pattern: 'after' a period adds one smaller time unit. After a Shemittah = +1 year; after a year = +1 month; after a month = +1 week.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rava say a get placed in a wife's courtyard is effective, even though her acquisitions belong to her husband?
A. The husband waives his property rights when he initiates divorce
B. The courtyard was never truly the husband's property
C. The get and full ownership of the courtyard enter her possession simultaneously
D. Rabbinic decree overrides the Torah law of property rights
Correct: C — Rava's principle: גִּטָּהּ וַחֲצֵרָהּ בָּאִין כְּאֶחָד — the get severs the husband's rights at the exact moment it arrives, so the courtyard becomes fully hers simultaneously with the divorce.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the deathbed case, what method did Rava instruct to deliver the get on Shabbat?
A. Have a messenger physically hand the get to the wife
B. Transfer ownership of the place where the get rests, and she performs חֲזָקָה by locking/opening the door
C. Declare the divorce verbally before witnesses
D. Wait until after Shabbat and rely on retroactive effect
Correct: B — Rava advised transferring ownership of the location where the get was resting. The wife would then perform an act of land acquisition (locking/opening the door), thereby acquiring both the place and the get within it.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Ulla and R' Oshaya regarding courtyard acquisition of a get?
A. Whether a courtyard can acquire a get at all
B. Whether she must be physically near the courtyard or can be far away
C. Whether the courtyard must be fully enclosed
D. Whether the husband must throw the get or can place it gently
Correct: B — Ulla requires her to be standing next to the courtyard (courtyard = extension of hand, requiring proximity). R' Oshaya says even if she's in Tiberias and the courtyard is in Tzippori, she's divorced — a guarded courtyard acquires regardless of distance.
