Gittin 21: Surfaces, Slaves & Severance

וְכָתַב לָהּ סֵפֶר כְּרִיתֻת וְנָתַן בְּיָדָהּ
CORE THEME This daf explores creative methods of delivering a get — through slaves, courtyards, and unusual surfaces — while defining what constitutes valid writing material and what complete severance (keritut) requires.

Can a Woman Transfer Document Ownership?

Rava
Proof from a guarantor (arev): his commitment on a promissory note shows people understand document transfer.
Rav Ashi
A man may understand document transfer — but does a woman? That's the real question!
Rav Ashi
Better proof: A woman may write her own get and give it to her husband to deliver — she clearly understands transfer.
KEY PRINCIPLE Ratification of a get depends on its signatories (witnesses), not on who physically writes or holds it.

Rava: Divorce via Slave or Courtyard

Husband writes a get and places it in his slave's hand
↓
Husband writes a deed of gift transferring the slave to his wife
↓
Wife acquires the slave → the slave is like her courtyard → she is divorced
LIMITATION This works only if the slave is bound (tied up). A mobile slave = a mobile courtyard, which cannot acquire property.
5 / 9

Why Rava Teaches Both: Slave AND Courtyard

Slave Case

  • Get placed in bound slave's hand
  • Deed of gift transfers slave to wife
  • Without this teaching: might decree against it due to similarity to unbound slave

Courtyard Case

  • Get placed in husband's courtyard
  • Deed of gift transfers courtyard to wife
  • Without this teaching: might confuse with courtyard acquired from a third party (invalid)
ABAYE'S CHALLENGE Courtyard is derived from "her hand" — just as her hand acquires even against her will, a courtyard should too. But a gift requires consent!

Valid Surfaces for a Get: R' Yosei HaGelili vs. Rabbis

SurfaceR' Yosei HaGeliliRabbis
Scroll / parchment✅ Valid✅ Valid
Living creature (slave's hand)❌ Invalid✅ Valid
Food item❌ Invalid✅ Valid
Cow's horn (attached)❌ Invalid✅ Valid (give whole cow)
Detached horn❌ Invalid❌ Invalid (requires cutting = extra step)
SOURCE R' Yosei HaGelili derives from "sefer" (scroll): just as a scroll is not alive and not food, so too a get. The Rabbis read sefer as sefirat devarim — referring to content, not surface.

What Does "Sefer Keritut" Teach?

Rabbis
"And he writes her" → divorce requires writing, not money. "Keritut" → must be complete severance.
R' Yosei HaGelili
"Sefer" → defines valid surface (not alive, not food). "Keritut" → teaches both: writing not money, AND complete severance.
PRACTICAL RULE A condition like "on condition you never drink wine" = not severance (invalid). "On condition for 30 days" = valid severance — the bond is temporary.
