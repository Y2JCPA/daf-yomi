Gittin 56 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Bar Kamtza decide to inform against the Jews to Rome?
A. The host physically attacked him
B. The Sages sat silently while he was humiliated, implying approval
C. He was a Roman spy all along
D. The host refused to pay him money he was owed
Correct: B — Bar Kamtza reasoned that since the Sages were present and did not protest his humiliation, they must have approved of it — this silence drove his decision to denounce them.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was R' Yochanan's critique of R' Zekharya ben Avkolas?
A. His cowardice prevented him from fighting Rome
B. His excessive humility (over-caution) destroyed the Temple
C. He should have blemished the offering himself
D. He was secretly allied with Bar Kamtza
Correct: B — R' Yochanan said R' Zekharya's 'excessive humility' — his unwillingness to allow any halakhic compromise even in an emergency — led directly to the destruction of the Temple and exile.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What did the zealots do to force the people of Jerusalem into battle?
A. They opened the city gates to the Romans
B. They assassinated the Sages
C. They burned the storehouses of wheat and barley
D. They poisoned the city's water supply
Correct: C — The zealots burned the massive food stores that could have sustained Jerusalem for 21 years, creating a famine that left the people no choice but to fight.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What was Vespasian's 'snake and honey barrel' argument to Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai?
A. Honey attracts snakes, so Jerusalem attracted enemies
B. You must break the barrel (destroy the city) to kill the snake (the zealots)
C. The Jews are the snake that must be removed from the barrel of the empire
D. A wise man knows when to sacrifice honey for safety
Correct: B — Vespasian argued that destroying Jerusalem was necessary to eliminate the zealots within it — just as one breaks a barrel to kill a snake wrapped around it. The Gemara says R' Yochanan should have responded: use tongs to remove the snake and spare the barrel.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai ask only for Yavne and not for Jerusalem itself?
A. He believed Jerusalem was already lost spiritually
B. He feared Vespasian would refuse a large request, leaving him with nothing
C. Yavne had a larger population than Jerusalem
D. The zealots had already surrendered Yavne to Rome
Correct: B — Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai calculated that asking to spare Jerusalem entirely would be refused, and then he would receive nothing at all. He made modest requests to guarantee at least some salvation — preserving Torah through Yavne and its Sages.
