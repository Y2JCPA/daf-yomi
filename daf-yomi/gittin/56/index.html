From Banquet to Destruction — The Chain of Catastrophe

🎭 Bar Kamtza is humiliated at a feast while Sages remain silent
↓
🐂 Bar Kamtza blemishes the emperor's offering — lip or eyelid
↓
⚖️ R' Zekharya blocks both sacrificing it and killing Bar Kamtza
↓
⚔️ Rome wages war — siege, famine, destruction
R' Yochanan The excessive humility (ענוותנותו) of R' Zekharya ben Avkolas destroyed our Temple, burned our Sanctuary, and exiled us from our land.

The Banquet Escalation — Bar Kamtza's Three Offers

Bar Kamtza
I will pay for my portion of the feast — just let me stay.
Host
No. You must leave.
Bar Kamtza
I will pay for half the feast — don't humiliate me.
Host
No. You must leave.
Bar Kamtza
I will pay for the entire feast — just let me stay!
Host
No. He seized him by the hand and threw him out.
Turning Point Bar Kamtza concluded: The Sages sat there and did not protest — they must have approved. He resolved to denounce them to Rome.

The Blemished Offering — R' Zekharya's Fatal Caution

🐂 Emperor sends a choice calf as an offering to the Temple
↓
Bar Kamtza blemishes its upper lip (or eyelid) — invalid by halakha, valid by Roman standards
↓
❓ Should the priests sacrifice it anyway for peace with Rome?
Sages say YES
Sacrifice it — maintain peace with the government
R' Zekharya says NO
People will say blemished animals may be offered!
↓
❓ Then should we kill Bar Kamtza to prevent his report?
Sages say YES
Eliminate the informer before he reaches Rome
R' Zekharya says NO
People will say one who blemishes offerings is executed!
Result Both options blocked — Bar Kamtza's slander reached Rome, and war began.

Nero's Signs — Why He Fled Rome's Mission

🏹 Nero shot arrows in all four directions — every arrow fell on Jerusalem
↓
👦 He asked a child to recite his verse: 'I will lay My vengeance upon Edom by the hand of My people Israel' (Ezekiel 25:14)
↓
💭 Nero understood: God wants to destroy His Temple and blame the agent
↓
🏃 He fled, converted to Judaism, and R' Meir descended from him
Insight Even a Roman general recognized the theological danger of being God's instrument of punishment — Edom (Rome) would ultimately be held accountable.

Zealots vs. Sages — The Standoff Inside Jerusalem

The Sages

  • Urged: Make peace with Rome
  • Warned the zealots: You will not succeed in battle
  • Counseled patience — wait for the siege to break

The Zealots (Biryonim)

  • Refused all negotiation
  • Wanted to fight Rome head-on
  • Burned the storehouses of food to force battle
  • Created a devastating famine
Consequence The zealots destroyed 21 years of provisions — forcing starvation and ending any hope of outlasting the siege.
