Can a Slave Have No Monetary Value?

R' Abba
The verse about teruma distinguishes slaves 'born in the house' — they may eat teruma even with no monetary value. This proves a slave can be worthless.
Gemara
But if you say a slave sold 'for the penalty' has value — every slave can be sold for the penalty if an ox gores him! So no slave is truly valueless.
Gemara
A tereifa slave (fatally wounded) has no penalty value, since there's no ox-goring penalty for a tereifa.
Gemara
But isn't he fit to stand before his master in honor? That gives him value!
Gemara
We're dealing with a tereifa who is also menuvval (disgusting) or mukeh sheḥin (afflicted with boils) — standing before the master would be no honor.
TEIKU The underlying question — whether selling a slave 'for the penalty' constitutes a real sale — remains unresolved.

Half-Slave Half-Freeman Betroths a Free Woman

A half-slave half-freeman betroths a free woman. Is it valid?
↓
Like 'Betroth Half of Me'?
When a free man says 'be betrothed to half of me' → valid, because she's fit to marry all of him
Like 'Betroth Half a Woman'?
When a man betroths half a woman → invalid, because he left part out of acquisition
↓
But here his Jewish half betrothed her fully — he didn't leave any part of her out
↓
Baraita proof: ox kills half-slave → half ransom to 'his heirs' — implying he can marry!
KEY PROOF The mention of 'heirs' implies the half-slave can betroth and marry, otherwise he would have no heirs to receive the ransom.
5 / 9

Rav Adda bar Ahava vs. Rava on 'His Heirs'

Rav Adda bar Ahava
The ox didn't kill him — it made him a tereifa. 'His heirs' means the half-slave himself collects while alive.
Rava
Two refutations: (1) 'His heirs' cannot reasonably mean the victim himself. (2) It discusses ransom, and Reish Lakish says ransom is paid only after death, not while alive.
Rava
Rather, 'his heirs' means it is fitting for heirs to take the ransom — the betrothal question remains open.
REISH LAKISH'S RULE Ransom (kofer) is paid only after the victim's death, never during the victim's lifetime — even if the victim is a tereifa.

Half-Maidservant Betrothed, Then Emancipated

Rav Yosef bar Ḥama / Rav Naḥman

  • Emancipation abrogates the first betrothal entirely
  • The second betrothal to Shimon takes effect
  • Her new free status resets her marital eligibility

R' Zeira / Rav Naḥman

  • Emancipation completes Reuven's first betrothal
  • Shimon's second betrothal does not take effect
  • Proof: Leviticus 19:20 — 'if she was free, they are put to death' as married
ABAYE'S CHALLENGE According to R' Yishmael's school, the verse refers to a Canaanite maidservant betrothed to a Hebrew slave — emancipation would cancel that betrothal, not complete it. So R' Zeira's proof is inconclusive.

Forcing Emancipation of a Half-Maidservant

מעשה באשה אחת שהיתה חציה שפחה וחציה בת חורין וכפו את רבה ועשאה בת חורין
INCIDENT Rav Huna bar Ketina says in the name of R' Yitzḥak: An incident occurred with a half-maidservant half-free woman, and the court forced her master to emancipate her.

Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak explains: Not because of the mitzva to procreate (R' Yoḥanan ben Beroka's view), but because men were taking liberties with her — she could neither marry as a slave nor as a free woman, so the court intervened to allow her to marry.
