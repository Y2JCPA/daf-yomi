Writing a Valid Get: Materials, Surfaces & Signatures

Writing a Get
What materials and surfaces are valid?
↓
Ink/Material
Must produce permanent writing:
Deyo, sam, sikra, komos, kankantom
Surface
Almost anything:
Olive leaf, cow horn, slave's hand
Invalid
Fruit juice, liquids —
anything not permanent
↓
Special concern: Witnesses who cannot sign
How do they participate?
↓
Rav
Tear a paper stencil;
they fill in gaps with ink
Shmuel
Write names with mei millin (faint lead);
they trace over in ink
KEY PRINCIPLE A get must be written with permanent materials, but the Rabbis created accommodations for illiterate witnesses — especially for gittin, so Jewish women are not left unable to remarry.

Mishna: Valid Writing Materials for a Get

Hebrew TermMeaningStatus
דיו (Deyo)Ink (deyota in Aramaic)✅ Valid
סם (Sam)Arsenic-based paint (samma)✅ Valid
סיקרא (Sikra)Red dye / red lead (sikreta)✅ Valid
קומוס (Komos)Tree resin (kuma)✅ Valid
קנקנתום (Kankantom)Iron sulfate — cobblers' black✅ Valid
מי פירות (Fruit juice)Any liquid or non-permanent ink❌ Invalid
מי טריא / עפצאGallnut water (R' Chanina adds)✅ Valid
RULE The governing principle: anything that produces כתב של קיימא — permanent, lasting writing — is valid for a get.

Writing Surfaces: What Can a Get Be Written On?

Rabbis (Majority)

  • Write on anything — olive leaf, cow horn, slave's hand
  • If written on cow horn → must give her the entire cow
  • If written on slave's hand → must give her the slave
  • The key: the woman must possess the surface

R' Yosei HaGelili

  • May NOT write on any living thing (בעל חיים)
  • May NOT write on food (אוכלין)
  • Disagrees with using a slave's hand or cow horn
  • Limits valid surfaces to inanimate, non-food objects
NOTE The halakha follows the majority — a get on a living creature or food item is valid, as long as the surface is given to the woman.

Ink Over Red Dye on Shabbat: How Many Violations?

R' Yochanan & Reish Lakish
Writing ink over sikra (red dye) on Shabbat = two sin-offerings: one for writing (the new ink letters) and one for erasing (the old red dye letters).
Both Agree
Ink over ink or red dye over red dye = exempt. Nothing has changed — no new writing, no erasing.
Some Say (Liable)
Red dye over ink: Liable — because he erases the superior ink writing beneath.
Some Say (Exempt)
Red dye over ink: Exempt — because he is destroying (מקלקל), replacing better ink with inferior dye. One who acts destructively is exempt on Shabbat.
PRINCIPLE The quality hierarchy matters: ink > red dye. Upgrading = writing + erasing. Downgrading = possibly just destruction (מקלקל).

Can Witnesses Sign Using a Stencil/Template?

Reish Lakish asks R' Yochanan:
Can we write witness names in red dye, then have them trace over in ink?
↓
R' Yochanan: No — the upper writing is not considered writing for documents.
↓
Reish Lakish objects: But you taught that overwriting IS writing for Shabbat liability!
↓
R' Yochanan responds: We don't compare gittin law to Shabbat law — we cannot be lenient with documents based on Shabbat rules.
↓
Rav's Solution
Tear a stencil from blank paper;
witnesses fill in gaps with ink
Shmuel's Solution
Write names with mei millin
(dilute, non-permanent lead);
witnesses trace over in ink
KEY DISTINCTION Shmuel does NOT mean actual lead writing (which R' Chiyya taught is valid writing). He means mei millin — a diluted, impermanent form that doesn't count as real writing.

R' Shimon ben Gamliel: Stencils Only for Gittin?

Rabbis (Tanna Kamma)
The stencil method for illiterate witnesses may be used for any document — gittin, manumission, contracts.
R' Shimon ben Gamliel
The stencil accommodation applies only to gittin. For manumission and other documents: if witnesses can't read and sign, they simply don't sign.
R' Elazar
R' Shimon ben Gamliel's reason: שלא יהו בנות ישראל עגונות — so that Jewish women should not be left stranded, unable to remarry.
Rava
The halakha follows R' Shimon ben Gamliel — stencils only for gittin.
Rav Gamda (in Rava's name)
Actually, the halakha does NOT follow R' Shimon ben Gamliel — but only regarding reading documents to witnesses, not regarding stencil signing.
PRACTICAL LAW Rav Kahana flogged someone who used stencils for non-divorce documents. The leniency for illiterate witnesses is strictly limited to gittin.
