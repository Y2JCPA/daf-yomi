Gittin 19 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, what is the key requirement for writing materials used in a get?
A. Must be black in color
B. Must produce permanent writing
C. Must be water-soluble
D. Must be plant-based
Correct: B — The Mishna states one may write with anything that produces כתב של קיימא (permanent writing), and one may NOT write with anything that does not produce permanent writing — regardless of color or source.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does R' Yochanan say that writing on top of red dye cannot be used for witness signatures, even though it counts as writing for Shabbat?
A. Red dye is not a valid ink
B. We don't compare gittin law to Shabbat law to be lenient with documents
C. Witnesses must write directly on parchment
D. The red dye makes the signature illegible
Correct: B — R' Yochanan explicitly states: 'Because we compare bills of divorce to Shabbat, shall we perform an action?' — the standards for valid document signatures are stricter than Shabbat liability.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the practical difference between Rav's and Shmuel's solutions for witnesses who cannot sign?
A. Rav uses ink, Shmuel uses red dye
B. Rav cuts a stencil from blank paper; Shmuel writes names with faint/dilute lead for tracing
C. Rav has someone else sign; Shmuel has the witness make a mark
D. Rav requires two witnesses; Shmuel requires three
Correct: B — Rav tears blank paper into a stencil that the witnesses fill with ink. Shmuel writes their names with mei millin (diluted, impermanent lead) for them to trace. Both ensure the witnesses physically produce the writing.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does R' Shimon ben Gamliel limit the stencil accommodation to bills of divorce only?
A. Because gittin require fewer witnesses
B. So that Jewish women should not be left stranded (agunot)
C. Because gittin are read publicly
D. Because the Torah specifies special rules for gittin
Correct: B — R' Elazar explains R' Shimon ben Gamliel's reasoning: שלא יהו בנות ישראל עגונות — without this leniency, a husband who can only find illiterate witnesses might leave without divorcing his wife, trapping her.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What three conditions does Ameimar require for a Persian document with Jewish witnesses to be valid for collecting from liened property?
A. Written in Hebrew, signed by three, and dated
B. Witnesses read Persian, paper processed with gall, and final line recaps content
C. Notarized by a court, witnessed by Jews, and written on parchment
D. Read by two gentiles, signed in ink, and sealed
Correct: B — Ameimar requires: (1) the Jewish witnesses actually know how to read Persian, (2) the paper is gall-processed so it cannot be forged, and (3) the document recaps its essential content in the final line — meeting standard document validity requirements.
