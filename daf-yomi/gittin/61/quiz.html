QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda, what does R' Yosei mean when he says taking a lost item from a deaf-mute is 'full-fledged robbery'?
A. Full-fledged robbery by Torah law
B. Full-fledged robbery by rabbinic law
C. Robbery on account of the ways of peace
D. Robbery punishable by lashes
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda explains that R' Yosei means it is full-fledged robbery by rabbinic law — not Torah law. The practical difference is that the court can forcibly expropriate the item, unlike mere 'darkhei shalom' robbery.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the mishna permit a ḥaver's wife to grind with an am ha'aretz's wife, but forbid assisting a Sabbatical-Year suspect?
A. The am ha'aretz is not Jewish
B. According to Abaye, most amei ha'aretz tithe; according to Rava, the am ha'aretz here tithes by definition
C. Grinding is not considered real work
D. The ḥaver's wife is not responsible for another's sins
Correct: B — Abaye says most amei ha'aretz do tithe, so the concern is minimal and overridden by darkhei shalom. Rava says the am ha'aretz here (per R' Meir) is defined as one who doesn't eat in purity but does tithe — so tithes aren't an issue at all.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
At what point must the wife of a ḥaver stop assisting the wife of an am ha'aretz?
A. When she begins sifting the flour
B. When water is poured into the flour
C. When the dough is placed in the oven
D. When the grain is ground into flour
Correct: B — Once water is poured into the flour, the dough becomes susceptible to ritual impurity. At that point, the ḥaver's wife must stop, because continuing would mean assisting in creating impure food.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why must a man tithe both what he gives to and receives from his mother-in-law?
A. She is suspected of deliberately stealing tithes
B. She may exchange his spoiled food for her own untithed food out of concern for her daughter
C. All amei ha'aretz are suspected of not tithing
D. The food changes legal status when it enters her home
Correct: B — R' Yehuda explains that a mother-in-law wants the best for her daughter and is embarrassed to tell her son-in-law his food was spoiled, so she may quietly swap it for her own (untithed) food.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Yirmeya, when may one deposit produce with a priest suspected of eating in impurity?
A. Only in a sealed earthenware vessel
B. Only when the produce has not yet become susceptible to impurity (never contacted liquid)
C. Only if the priest's wife is not present
D. Never — it is always prohibited
Correct: B — R' Yirmeya resolves the contradiction by distinguishing: produce that never contacted liquid is not susceptible to impurity, so even if the priest touches it, no impurity is imparted. Such produce may be deposited with him.
