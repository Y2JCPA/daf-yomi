Gittin 61: Mipnei Darkhei Shalom

מפני דרכי שלום
CORE THEME This daf explores the expansive Rabbinic principle of mipnei darkhei shalom (on account of the ways of peace) — a framework that governs interactions between individuals of unequal status: rich and poor, Jew and gentile, ḥaver and am ha'aretz. The Gemara asks: when does protecting social harmony override strict legal categories, and when must we draw the line to avoid actively assisting transgression?

Lost Items of a Deaf-Mute, Imbecile, or Minor

Tanna Kamma
Taking a lost item found by a deaf-mute, imbecile, or minor is considered robbery on account of the ways of peace — not full-fledged robbery.
R' Yosei
It is full-fledged robbery (גזל גמור)!
Rav Ḥisda
R' Yosei means full-fledged robbery by rabbinic law, not by Torah law.
Gemara
What's the practical difference? If it's full-fledged robbery by rabbinic law, the court can forcibly expropriate the item from the robber.
KEY DISTINCTION Darkhei shalom = moral censure only. Rabbinic robbery = court-enforceable recovery.

Poor Person Gleaning Olives from a Treetop

A poor person climbs an olive tree and gathers olives at the top
↓
Some olives fall to the ground beneath the tree
↓
Olives were in his hand first
Full-fledged robbery by Torah law — he already acquired them
Olives fell before he grasped them
Taking them is robbery on account of darkhei shalom only
STORY Rav Kahana ate dates knocked down by a man throwing sticks at a palm tree. The man protested: 'I threw them with my hand!' — meaning he had possession. Rav Kahana praised him as a student of R' Yoshiya of Huzal: 'A righteous man is the foundation of the world' (Proverbs 10:25).

Sustaining Gentiles: Mipnei Darkhei Shalom

Gifts to the Jewish Poor

  • Gleanings (לקט)
  • Forgotten sheaves (שכחה)
  • Corner of the field (פאה)
  • These are by Torah law designated for Jewish poor

Gentile Poor — Darkhei Shalom

  • One does not protest if gentile poor take gleanings, sheaves, or pe'ah
  • One sustains poor gentiles alongside poor Jews
  • One visits sick gentiles alongside sick Jews
  • One buries dead gentiles alongside dead Jews
PRINCIPLE All of these acts of inclusion are performed mipnei darkhei shalom — to maintain peaceful relations between Jewish and gentile communities.

Wife of a Ḥaver Assisting Wife of an Am Ha'aretz

Mishna
A ḥaver's wife may lend a winnow and sieve, and may select, grind, and sift with an am ha'aretz's wife. But once she pours water into flour, the ḥaver's wife must stop — the dough becomes susceptible to impurity.
Gemara
Why can't she help a Shemitah suspect but can help an am ha'aretz?
Abaye
Most amei ha'aretz do tithe their produce. The minority concern is overridden mipnei darkhei shalom.
Rava
The am ha'aretz here is defined per R' Meir: one who doesn't eat non-sacred food in purity. He does tithe — so there's no issue with tithes at all, only purity.
ABAYE vs. RAVA Abaye: It's a probabilistic leniency (most do tithe). Rava: There's no leniency needed — the am ha'aretz in question tithes by definition.
