Valid — clearly not Jewish witnesses, no confusion
Ambiguous Names
Invalid — treated as document prepared by a common person
Key Principle The validity of gentile-signed documents depends on whether names are clearly identifiable as gentile to prevent mistaken reliance on them as Jewish witnesses.

Where Are Gentile Signatures Valid?

R' Akiva
Documents prepared by a common person (hedyot) with gentile signatures are valid — even for gittin and manumission
Rabbis
Documents from a hedyot are invalid — except for gittin and manumission
Rabban Shimon b. Gamliel
Even gittin/manumission are valid only in a place where Jews do not sign documents
Gemara
We don't decree against places where Jews don't sign — people don't confuse one place with another, though they may confuse one name with another
R' Elazar b. R' Yosei All agree documents from gentile courts are valid. The dispute is only about documents prepared by a common person outside of court.

Mishna: Retracting Bills Given to an Agent

R' Meir

  • Can retract BOTH get and manumission
  • Proof: Priest's slave loses right to teruma when freed
  • Therefore emancipation is NOT purely beneficial
  • Agent cannot acquire something detrimental in absence

Rabbis (Halakha)

  • Can retract GET but NOT manumission
  • Emancipation = benefit (zakhin le'adam)
  • Master may withhold food — slavery has no guaranteed benefit
  • Slave eats teruma only because he is master's ACQUISITION, not his right
Core Dispute Zakhin le'adam she'lo befanav — One may act in a person's interest in their absence. The Rabbis hold emancipation is beneficial; R' Meir counters that a priest's slave loses teruma rights.

Seizing Property on Behalf of a Creditor

Rav Huna
From the Rabbis' ruling on manumission, we learn: if someone seizes property on behalf of a creditor, the creditor acquires it
R' Yitzchak b. Yosef
Even when the seizure is to the detriment of other creditors?
Rav Huna
Yes — just as the agent acquires manumission despite the master's loss
R' Yirmeya (waking up)
Children! R' Yochanan says: One who seizes to the detriment of others does NOT acquire
R' Yirmeya
The mishna is different — the master said 'Give', which equals 'Acquire on his behalf'. Independent seizure for a creditor is not the same!
R' Yochanan's Rule Tofes le'baal chov bi'mkom she'chaav la'acherim — lo kanah. Seizing property for a creditor when it harms others does not effect acquisition.
