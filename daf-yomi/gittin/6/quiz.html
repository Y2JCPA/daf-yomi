Gittin 6 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, what is sufficient for the agent's testimony about the writing of a get?
A. Seeing the scribe write every word
B. Hearing the sound of the quill and scroll
C. Having two witnesses present
D. Being in the same room as the scribe
Correct: B — Rav Ashi holds that hearing the sound of the quill (kulmusa) and the scroll is sufficient — the agent need not visually observe the scribe writing.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Shmuel hold that Babylonia is NOT like Eretz Yisrael for gittin, despite its great academies?
A. Babylonian scribes don't know the laws of lishmah
B. The academies are too far from population centers
C. Scholars are preoccupied with studies and don't recognize signatures
D. Babylonia lacks a proper beit din
Correct: C — Shmuel agrees witnesses are needed for ratification but holds that Babylonian scholars are too preoccupied with their Torah study to recognize people's signatures, making them unavailable as witnesses.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was the concern about a scribe who goes to the market in the middle of writing a get?
A. He might forget which woman the get is for
B. Someone might commission him to write a different get, changing his intent
C. The ink might dry and invalidate the document
D. He might share confidential information
Correct: B — The concern is that while in the market, someone else might ask the scribe to write a get for a different woman, and the scribe might switch to writing for that other woman's sake (lishmah). The baraita teaches this concern is disregarded.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Yishmael tell the man from the Village of Sisai to make the declaration, even though Sisai was within Eretz Yisrael's boundary?
A. He didn't know where Sisai was located
B. To protect the woman from needing to find witnesses if the husband contests
C. The village had been recently conquered by gentiles
D. He was following a stringent minority opinion
Correct: B — Rabbi Yishmael ruled stringently to protect the woman — once the declaration is made, the husband cannot later contest the get's validity, sparing her from needing to find witnesses.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How did Abaye defend Rabbi Evyatar's reliability after Rav Yosef challenged him for writing a verse without scoring the parchment?
A. He proved Rav Yosef's rule was incorrect
B. He argued the rule about scoring is a tradition one might not have heard, not a matter of reasoning
C. He showed that Rabbi Evyatar actually did score the parchment
D. He cited a different rabbi who also wrote without scoring
Correct: B — Abaye distinguished between laws derived from reasoning (where ignorance suggests unreliability) and traditions (where one simply may not have heard). The scoring rule is a tradition, so not knowing it doesn't impugn R' Evyatar's authority. Abaye further noted that God Himself confirmed R' Evyatar's interpretation.
