Babylonia's Status for Bills of Divorce

Rav Ashi
Hearing the sound of the quill (kulmusa) and the scroll is sufficient testimony for the agent
Baraita
Even if agent is in the house and scribe in the loft — the get is valid
Rav
Babylonia is like Eretz Yisrael — academies make witnesses frequently available
Shmuel
Babylonia is like outside Eretz Yisrael — scholars are preoccupied with study and don't know signatures
Rav Huna
Since Rav came to Babylonia, we made ourselves like Eretz Yisrael for gittin
Core Issue Can Babylonia's great academies substitute for the proximity and familiarity of Eretz Yisrael in validating bills of divorce?

When Can the Agent Testify?

Agent must say: "Written and signed in my presence"
↓
Must the agent see the scribe writing?
↓
Rav Ashi
No — hearing the sound of the quill (kulmusa) and scroll suffices
Proof
Baraita: agent in house, scribe in loft — get is valid despite no visual contact
Rav Ashi's Principle Auditory evidence of the writing process is sufficient — the agent need not visually observe the scribe

The Scribe Who Enters and Exits

Baraita
Even if the scribe was entering and exiting all day, the get is valid
Gemara
This is obvious! Why would interruptions invalidate it?
Gemara
The case is where the scribe went to the market and returned
Gemara
Concern: Perhaps someone in the market commissioned a different get and the scribe now writes for another woman
Baraita
This concern is disregarded — the get remains valid
Chiddush We do not suspect the scribe changed his intent (lishmah) merely because he left and returned during the writing process

Babylonia's Status: Rav vs. Shmuel

Rav

  • Babylonia = Eretz Yisrael for gittin
  • Central academies (yeshivot) exist
  • Scholars are frequently available as witnesses
  • Can ratify documents easily

Shmuel

  • Babylonia = outside Eretz Yisrael for gittin
  • Scholars are preoccupied with their studies
  • They don't recognize signatures
  • Agent must still declare "written and signed in my presence"
Key Agreement Both agree the declaration is needed to ratify the document (not only for lishmah) — they disagree on whether Babylonian scholars are practically available as witnesses

The Incident of the Village of Sisai

📜 A man brings a get before Rabbi Yishmael and asks if he must make the declaration
↓
❓ R' Yishmael asks: Where are you from? — Answer: Village of Sisai
↓
✅ R' Yishmael rules: You must declare — to protect the woman from needing witnesses later
↓
🗣️ Rabbi Elai objects: Sisai is within EY's boundary, closer than Akko!
↓
🤫 R' Yishmael responds: Be silent! Since the divorce was already issued as permitted, it stands
Practical Principle The declaration "written and signed in my presence" provides extra protection — once given, the husband cannot later contest the get's validity
