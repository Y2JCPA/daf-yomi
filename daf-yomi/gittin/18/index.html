When Do Dates Matter? Timing Disputes in Divorce Law

Rav
The three-month waiting period begins from the giving of the get
Shmuel
The three-month count begins from the writing of the get
Gemara
Baraitot support both positions — and the halakha follows Shmuel: from the time of writing
KEY THEME Gittin 18 explores how the timing of legal documents — their writing, signing, and delivery — determines their legal force, across divorce, debt, and witness law.

Pre-Written Get: Does It Create a Problem?

A man writes a get but puts it in his pocket, hoping his wife will appease him
↓
She does not appease him — he gives her the get. Is it valid?
↓
Rava's Answer
Valid. A person does not hasten a calamity upon himself — it is unlikely he'd prepare a get in advance, so the Sages did not enact an ordinance against it.
Overseas Gittin
Written in Nisan, arrive in Tishrei — it is public knowledge they are delayed, so the woman must bring witnesses to prove when she received it.
PRINCIPLE אין אדם מקדים פורענות לעצמו — A person does not hasten calamity upon himself.

When Does the Three-Month Waiting Period Begin?

Rav
From the time of giving the get — when the divorce actually takes effect
Shmuel
From the time of writing the get — the document establishes the timeline
R. Natan bar Hoshaya
Objects to Shmuel: Two women divorced the same day — one must wait, one can remarry? That seems unreasonable!
Abaye
Not difficult — the date on each get proves her individual status. Each case stands on its own documentation.
HALAKHA The halakha follows Shmuel: the three-month count begins from the time of writing.

Supporting Baraitot: Rav vs. Shmuel

Supports Rav (From Giving)

  • An agent delivers a get after a three-month delay
  • Once the get reaches her hand — she must wait three more months
  • No concern for outdated get — he was not secluded with her during delivery
  • Practiced by: Rav Pappa, Rav Huna b. R. Yehoshua

Supports Shmuel (From Writing)

  • A man deposits a get with a trustee: 'Give it after three months'
  • Once given — she may marry immediately, no additional wait
  • No concern for outdated get — he was not secluded with her
  • Practiced by: Rav Kahana, Rav Pappi, Rav Ashi
FINAL RULING The halakha is that counting begins from the time of writing — following Shmuel.

Ketubah Debt & Shemitah Cancellation

PositionWhen Is the Ketubah Debt Canceled by Shemitah?
RavOnly when she both collects partial payment AND establishes the rest as a debt in court
ShmuelWhen she either collects partial payment or establishes it as a debt in court
Baraita for RavPartial collection alone or establishment alone is insufficient
Baraita for ShmuelRape fines, seduction fines — treated as debts once either condition is met
CONTEXT The Sabbatical Year (Shemitah) cancels debts by Torah law. The question is when a ketubah transforms from a potential claim into an active debt subject to cancellation.

Ten Signatories: Witnesses or Stipulation?

R' Yochanan
When a husband tells ten men 'All of you write a get' — two are witnesses, the rest sign to fulfill his stipulation (tenai)
Reish Lakish
All ten function as witnesses — each signature carries the weight of testimony
Gemara
The dispute only applies when he said 'All of you' — otherwise the mishna rules: one writes, two sign
R' Yehoshua b. Levi
A man came whose ten signatories signed on different days — this is the exact case where the dispute matters
KEY PHRASE כולכם כתובו גט לאשתי — 'All of you write a get for my wife'
