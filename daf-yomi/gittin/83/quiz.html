QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Rava consider R' Elazar ben Azarya's refutation the strongest?
A. Because R' Elazar ben Azarya was the Nasi
B. Because it addresses the fundamental definition of keritut (severance) and has no counter-argument
C. Because it was supported by a biblical verse that the others lacked
D. Because R' Yehoshua endorsed it explicitly
Correct: B — Rava states that all refutations can be countered except R' Elazar ben Azarya's, which argues from the core meaning of 'sefer keritut' — that divorce must completely sever the bond. This definitional argument cannot be undermined.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is R' Tarfon's objection to R' Eliezer's position on conditional divorce?
A. It creates mamzerim retroactively
B. It contradicts the principle that forbidden is forbidden to all
C. It could prevent the fulfillment of yibum — uprooting a Torah mitzvah
D. It doesn't meet the definition of complete severance
Correct: C — R' Tarfon argues that if the woman marries the brother of the forbidden man and he dies childless, she cannot perform yibum with the forbidden man. The husband's stipulation thus uproots a Torah obligation.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does R' Yehoshua mean by 'One does not refute a lion after his death'?
A. R' Eliezer was known to be fierce and dangerous
B. A deceased Sage might have answered the objection — it's unfair to refute him posthumously
C. Only Torah scholars of R' Eliezer's stature may be challenged
D. Lions are a symbol of the tribe of Yehuda from which R' Eliezer descended
Correct: B — R' Yehoshua's principle is that after a Sage passes away, one should not reject his opinion based on difficulties, as he possibly would have provided answers had the challenges been presented during his lifetime.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In Rav Ashi's dilemma, if the husband initially forbade Reuven and Shimon, then said 'You are permitted to Reuven,' why might this include Shimon?
A. Because Reuven and Shimon are always treated as a pair in halakha
B. Because he mentioned Reuven first only because Reuven was listed first in the original restriction
C. Because permitting one automatically permits all
D. Because the second get overrides the first entirely
Correct: B — The possibility is that he mentioned Reuven only because Reuven was the first name in his original restriction — he 'opened with' Reuven and intended to permit both. The dilemma remains unresolved (teyku).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara distinguish the case of yibum from R' Shimon bar Elazar's challenge that one person cannot prohibit and another permit?
A. Yibum is a rabbinic institution, not a Torah law
B. The yavam himself caused the prohibition by existing as a brother, so he can logically remove it
C. The widow consented to yibum, unlike the divorced woman
D. Yibum applies only to firstborn sons
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that in yibum, the yavam's very existence as a brother is what prevents the widow from remarrying freely. Since he caused the prohibition, it makes sense that he (through yibum, divorce, or chalitzah) can remove it — unlike a first husband restricting and a second husband's death permitting.
