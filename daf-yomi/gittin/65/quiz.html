QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, at what stage can a minor girl receive her own get (even from Torah-level betrothal)?
A. Stage 1: Pebble/nut distinction
B. Stage 2: Ages ~6-8, when purchases are valid
C. Stage 3: Age of vows
D. Only after reaching full majority (age 12)
Correct: B — Rava teaches that at the second stage of development (~ages 6-8), when a minor's purchases and sales of movable property are valid, a minor girl can receive her own bill of divorce — even from betrothal enacted by her father (Torah law).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say the maidservant redeeming second tithe does NOT prove a minor can acquire for others?
A. The maidservant was actually an adult
B. The produce was from an unperforated pot — tithing is only rabbinic, and Sages ruled leniently
C. The maidservant was a Canaanite, not Hebrew
D. Second tithe redemption doesn't require acquisition
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes the produce grew in an unperforated pot (atzitz she'eino nakuv), making the tithing obligation rabbinic only. The Sages applied leniencies in rabbinic matters, so this cannot prove a minor can acquire for others at the Torah level.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Rabbi Elazar and the Rabbis regarding the wife's instructions to her agent?
A. Whether a woman can appoint an agent at all
B. Whether specifying a place is a binding condition or a mere indication
C. Whether the get must be written in the specified place
D. Whether the husband's consent is needed for agent deviation
Correct: B — The Rabbis hold that when the wife specifies a place, she is insistent (makpedет) — it's a condition. Rabbi Elazar holds she is merely indicating a place (mar'eh makom), not setting a condition, so deviation doesn't invalidate the get.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rav Yosef resolve the contradiction between the two baraitot about eiruv with the wrong food?
A. One follows Rabbi Elazar and one follows the Rabbis
B. Both follow the Rabbis: valid when using one's own food, invalid when using another person's food
C. One refers to eiruv techumin and one to eiruv chatzerot
D. The valid baraita is about Shabbat and the invalid one is about Yom Tov
Correct: B — Rav Yosef maintains both baraitot follow the Rabbis. The distinction is ownership: when the agent deviates but uses the principal's own food, it's valid. When using another person's food, that person only authorized the specific item, so deviation invalidates the eiruv.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Natan, why is 'patruha' a valid divorce expression but 'pitruha' is not?
A. Patruha is Hebrew while pitruha is Aramaic
B. Patruha means 'release her' (related to divorce); pitruha means 'exempt her' (unrelated to divorce)
C. Patruha is used in the Torah text; pitruha is a later coinage
D. Patruha implies permanent separation; pitruha implies temporary leave
Correct: B — Rabbi Natan, a Babylonian, distinguished the vowel forms: patruha means 'release her,' which connotes divorce, while pitruha means 'exempt her,' which is unrelated to divorce. Rava notes that the Eretz Yisrael tanna of the Mishna did not make this linguistic distinction.
