Rava's Three Stages of a Minor's Legal Capacity

🪨 Stage 1 — Pebble & Nut: Given a pebble, throws it away; given a nut, keeps it
↓
✅ Acquires property for himself only, not on behalf of others
↓
👧 Minor girl at this stage: can be betrothed by mother/brother (rabbinic), can refuse (mi'un)
↓
🛒 Stage 2 — Ages ~6–8: Purchase and sale of movable property is valid
↓
📜 Minor girl at this stage: can receive her own get (even from Torah-level betrothal by father)
↓
🙏 Stage 3 — Age of Vows: Vows and consecrations are valid
↓
👠 Minor girl at this stage: can perform ḥalitza. Land sale: must wait until age 20
Principle Legal capacity develops incrementally — each stage unlocks new halakhic powers for both acquisition and personal status.

Can a Minor Acquire Property on Behalf of Others?

Shmuel (per Rav Ḥisda)
A minor cannot acquire property on behalf of others.
Rav Avya
But a Mishna in Ma'aser Sheni says a Hebrew maidservant (a minor!) can redeem second tithe on behalf of her master!
Gemara
That case involves tithes today — rabbinic law only. The Sages ruled leniently.
Gemara
But there are no Hebrew maidservants today! That institution ended with the Jubilee Year.
Gemara
The produce grew in an unperforated pot (atzitz she'eino nakuv) — tithing is only rabbinic.
Resolution The maidservant proof is deflected: the case involves only rabbinic-level obligations, where the Sages applied leniencies regarding minors' acquisition.

Agent Deviates from Instructions: Husband vs. Wife

Husband Says: 'Give get in Place X'

  • Agent gives it in Place Y → ❌ Invalid
  • Husband is insistent on the place
  • His specification is a condition
  • All opinions agree: divorce is invalid

Husband Says: 'Give get to my wife — she is in Place X'

  • Agent gives it in Place Y → ✅ Valid
  • He merely indicated her location
  • Not a condition, just helpful info
  • All opinions agree: divorce is valid

Wife Says: 'Receive get in Place X'

  • Agent receives in Place Y → Dispute!
  • Rabbis: ❌ She was insistent on place
  • R' Elazar: ✅ She merely indicated a place
  • She is not particular about location
Key Distinction The critical question is whether specifying a place constitutes a binding condition (hakpadah) or a mere indication (mar'eh makom).

Eiruv with the Wrong Food: Three Resolutions

Agent told: use dates → used dried figs (or vice versa). Valid?
↓
Baraita contradiction: One says valid, one says invalid
↓
Rabba's Answer
Valid baraita = R' Elazar (mere indication)
Invalid baraita = Rabbis (insistence)
Rav Yosef's Answer
Both follow Rabbis:
Valid = his own food
Invalid = another's food
↓
Abaye challenges Rav Yosef: What about tower vs. dovecote — same contradiction exists!
↓
Rav Yosef: Same distinction — fruit of the tower vs. fruit of the dovecote may belong to different owners
Insight Rav Yosef maintains a unified approach: the Rabbis always treat deviation as problematic, but only when someone else's property is involved, since that person authorized only a specific item.

Expressions of Divorce: Valid vs. Invalid Instructions

ExpressionStatusReason
"Write a get and give it to her"✅ ValidClear divorce intent
"Divorce her" (garshuha)✅ ValidDirect divorce language
"Write a letter and give it to her"✅ ValidUnderstood as get
"Send her" / "Separate her" / "Banish her"✅ Valid (Baraita)Implies sending away
"Release her" (patruha)⚠️ DisputeR' Natan: Valid — means release
"Exempt her" (pitruha)❌ InvalidR' Natan: Means exempt, not divorce
"Sustain her"❌ InvalidNo divorce connotation
"Treat her according to the law" (nimus)❌ InvalidAmbiguous — not divorce language
"Treat her appropriately"❌ InvalidToo vague
Rava's Note R' Natan, a Babylonian, distinguished patruha (release) from pitruha (exempt). The Eretz Yisrael tanna of our Mishna did not distinguish between them.
