Why Must a Get Be Dated?

R' Yochanan
To prevent a husband from shielding his niece-wife from punishment for adultery by backdating the divorce
Reish Lakish
To establish when the wife regains rights to the produce of her usufruct property (peirot)
Core Dispute Both agree dates are required — they disagree whether the concern is criminal cover-up or financial clarity.

Life Under Foreign Rule

רחמנא! או בטולך או בטולא דבר עשו
Historical Context A Persian priest (ḥabbara) seized a lamp on a Persian holiday. Rabba lamented: "Merciful One! Let us live in Your shadow or under the Romans!"

The Gemara asks: But R' Ḥiyya taught that God exiled Israel to Babylonia because they couldn't endure Roman decrees!

Resolution: R' Ḥiyya spoke of Babylonia before Persian rule arrived; Rabba spoke after the Persians made life more difficult.

Who Must Produce the Get?

Mishna: One witness to writing + two witnesses to signing = valid
↓
R' Ami says R' Yochanan qualifies this:
Writing witness is the agent
He counts as two for writing + two signatories = Valid
Signatory witnesses produce it
Only one non-agent witness to writing = Invalid
Key Principle The agent who delivers a get from overseas has the attestation power of two witnesses regarding the writing.
5 / 9

R' Asi Challenges R' Ami — Twice!

R' Ami (1st time)
If signatory witnesses produce the get, it is invalid — two agents overseas must both attest to writing and signing.
R' Asi
But the Mishna implies the Rabbis invalidate even when both produce it? You confirmed my inference!
R' Ami (2nd time)
Actually — even if signatory witnesses produce it, it is valid. Two agents need not both attest.
R' Asi
But last time you said the opposite! Which is it?
R' Ami
What I say now is a spike that will not move (sikta d'lo nitalta). I retract my earlier ruling.
Takeaway R' Ami's final, authoritative position: the get is valid even when produced by signatories, not the writing witness.

Mishna: When Is a Get Valid?

WrittenSignedStatus
DaySame day✅ Valid
NightSame night✅ Valid
NightFollowing day✅ Valid (same calendar date)
DayThat night❌ Invalid (Rabbis) / ✅ Valid (R' Shimon)
R' Shimon's Rule All documents written by day and signed at night are invalid — except gittin, since a get is not used to collect money.

Why Date a Get? — The Core Dispute

R' Yochanan

  • Reason: Daughter of his sister (bat aḥoto)
  • Concern: Husband married to his niece may cover up her adultery by claiming an earlier divorce
  • The date prevents backdating the get
  • Follows the Rabbis who invalidate day-write/night-sign

Reish Lakish

  • Reason: Produce of usufruct property (peirot nikhsei melog)
  • Concern: Wife needs proof of when she regained property rights
  • The date establishes the cutoff for husband's rights to produce
  • Explains R' Shimon: wife's rights begin at writing, not signing
Reversal Alert The Gemara notes that elsewhere R' Yochanan and Reish Lakish hold opposite positions on when produce rights transfer — the opinions must be reversed in that other dispute.

📋 Gittin 17 — Summary Map

Mishna: Get must be written and signed on the same calendar date
↓
Exception: R' Shimon validates gittin signed the night after writing
↓
R' Yochanan: Date prevents backdating to shield an adulterous wife

↓
Abaye & Rav Yosef: Even imprecise dating (cycle/year/month) is valid — a limited benefit suffices
