Gittin 17 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yochanan, why did the Sages require a date on a get?
A. To establish when the wife regains usufruct produce rights
B. To prevent a husband from backdating the divorce to shield his wife from adultery charges
C. To ensure the get was written and signed on the same day
D. To prevent the husband from remarrying too quickly
Correct: B — R' Yochanan says the date requirement is 'due to the daughter of his sister' — a man married to his niece might backdate a get to cover up her adultery.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
When does R' Shimon say a get written by day and signed that night is valid?
A. Only when there are two witnesses to both writing and signing
B. Only for gittin, since they are not used to collect money
C. Only when the agent of the get delivers it personally
D. Only when the date is removed from the document
Correct: B — R' Shimon holds all documents written by day and signed at night are invalid EXCEPT gittin, because gittin are not used to collect money, so a slight date discrepancy doesn't matter.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What did Rav Yosef answer when Abaye asked about a husband who cut out the date after writing?
A. The get is invalid since it now lacks a date
B. We are not concerned about a deceiver — it was dated when written
C. It depends on whether the wife noticed the date was removed
D. The get must be rewritten with a new date
Correct: B — Rav Yosef ruled that since the get was properly dated when written, we are not concerned about a deceiver (ein chosheshin l'rama'i) who later removes the date.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Ami's FINAL ruling, what happens when signatory witnesses (not the writing witness) produce the get?
A. The get is invalid because only one witness attests to writing
B. The get is valid — R' Ami retracted his earlier strict position
C. It depends on whether the witnesses are from overseas
D. The get requires additional attestation from a beit din
Correct: B — R' Ami initially ruled the get invalid when produced by signatories, but later reversed himself, calling his new ruling 'a spike that will not move' — the get is valid.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say the opinions of R' Yochanan and Reish Lakish about usufruct produce must be 'reversed'?
A. Because they were misquoted by their students
B. Because elsewhere they hold opposite positions on when produce rights transfer
C. Because the Mishna contradicts both of their views
D. Because the halakha follows neither of them
Correct: B — In another dispute, R' Yochanan says produce rights revert at writing and Reish Lakish says at giving — the opposite of what this sugya requires. The Gemara resolves this by reversing the attributions in that other dispute.
