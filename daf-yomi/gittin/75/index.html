Giving Money Against the Recipient's Will

Rava (Version 1)
From Hillel's ordinance (takanat pruzbul for walled-city houses) we can infer: giving against the recipient's will is NOT valid giving.
Rav Pappa
Perhaps Hillel's ordinance was only needed where the money was given not in the purchaser's presence. In his presence — willing or not — it's valid!
Rava (Version 2)
Actually, from Hillel we learn: if she gives him 200 dinars in his presence — willing or unwilling — it IS valid. The ordinance was only for giving not in his presence.
Rav Pappa
Perhaps even in his presence, with consent = valid, but against his will = NOT valid. Hillel's ordinance addressed the purchaser hiding, a unique problem.
Two Versions The Gemara presents two versions of Rava's position — one restrictive and one permissive — each challenged by Rav Pappa (or Rav Shimi bar Ashi).

'The Paper Is Mine' vs. 'Return the Paper'

❌ Paper Is Mine

  • "This is your get, but the paper is still mine"
  • She receives only the writing, not the document
  • He never fully gave the get — NOT divorced
  • She has no ownership of the physical bill

✅ Return the Paper

  • "This is your get on condition you return the paper"
  • The get belongs entirely to her at the moment of giving
  • Returning the paper is merely a future stipulation
  • She IS divorced — condition fulfilled later
Key Distinction In the first case he never transfers full ownership. In the second, ownership transfers immediately and the return is an independent condition (tenai).

Whose Opinion Does the Baraita Follow?

Rav Ḥisda
The baraita follows RSBG: since she can appease him with money instead of the actual paper, it's as if she received it fully.
Abaye
RSBG only allows substituting value when the item is lost. The paper still exists! Rather, it follows R' Meir: the condition is void because it wasn't a tenai kaful (compound condition).
Rava
If so, had he compounded the condition, the get wouldn't be valid? But conditions derive from Bnei Gad/Reuven — here the condition and action concern the same matter, voiding it!
Rav Adda bar Ahava
Correct: the condition is void because the condition and the action are about the same matter (both involve the get paper). The get is valid without the condition.
Rav Ashi
It follows Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi: 'on the condition' means from now (מעכשיו). The get is effective immediately, even if the paper must be returned later.
Five Opinions! A rare stretch of Gemara with five different Amoraim each offering a different explanation for the same baraita.

Shmuel's Deathbed Get Formula

📋 A shkhiv mera (dying person) wants to give a conditional get
↓
🚫 First say: "If I do NOT die, this will NOT be a get" — because a person doesn't hasten calamity upon himself
↓
✅ Then say: "If I die, this WILL be a get" — affirmative before negative (derived from Bnei Gad/Reuven)
↓
🚫 Then say: "And if I do NOT die, this will NOT be a get" — completing the compound condition (tenai kaful)
↓
📜 Result: All requirements met — condition before action, affirmative before negative, compound condition
Rava's Correction Shmuel's original formulation was challenged because the affirmative must precede the negative. Rava corrected the formula to satisfy all technical requirements while respecting human sensitivity about mentioning death.
