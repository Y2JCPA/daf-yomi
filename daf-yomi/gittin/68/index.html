Gittin 68: Solomon, the Shamir, and the King of Demons

🥩 Rav Sheshet detects diseased meat at the Exilarch's house and avoids a pit trap
↓
📖 The Gemara interprets shidda and shiddot (Ecclesiastes 2:8) as male and female demons
↓
🏛️ Solomon needs the shamir to cut Temple stones without iron tools
↓
⛓️ Benayahu captures Ashmedai, king of demons, using wine and a sacred chain
↓
🐦 The shamir is obtained from the wild rooster (hoopoe) using a glass trap
↓
👑 Solomon removes Ashmedai's chain — Ashmedai hurls him away and seizes the throne
KEY THEME This daf narrates the epic aggadic account of Solomon's quest for the shamir and his fateful encounter with Ashmedai — a cautionary tale about the limits of human power.

Rav Sheshet Outsmarts the Exilarch's Servants

🍖 Rav Sheshet is served meat at the Exilarch's house but detects something wrong
↓
🔍 He tastes the flavor of white leprous spots and refuses to eat
↓
📜 Following Rav Ḥisda's rule: white spots in black skin = disease affliction
↓
✅ They check the animal's skin and confirm the disease — servants grow angrier
↓
🕳️ Servants dig a covered pit to trap Rav Sheshet as he leaves
↓
👦 A child's verse — "Turn to your right or to your left" — alerts him to danger
↓
🚶 His servant confirms the mat over the pit; they walk safely around it
THREE CLUES Rav Sheshet knew of the trap from: (1) Rav Ḥisda's snort, (2) the child's verse, and (3) the servants' known lack of integrity.

Interpreting 'Shidda and Shiddot' (Ecclesiastes 2:8)

Babylonia

  • Shidda = male demons
  • Shiddetin = female demons
  • Solomon used demons to help build the Temple
  • Needed them to locate the shamir

Eretz Yisrael

  • Shiddeta = carriages
  • Refers to royal luxury vehicles
  • Part of Solomon's wealth and pleasures
  • A more naturalistic reading
R' YOḤANAN There were 300 types of demons in Shiḥin, but "I do not know what the form or nature of a demon itself is."

The Quest for the Shamir

🏛️ Solomon must build the Temple
without iron tools (I Kings 6:7)
↓
The Sages tell him: Use the shamir,
the creature Moses used for the ephod stones
↓
❓ Where is the shamir found?
↓
Capture male & female demons
and torment them together
↓
Demons say: "We don't know —
ask Ashmedai, king of demons"
↓
Solomon sends Benayahu son of Jehoiada
with chain, ring, wool, and wine
THE SHAMIR A miraculous worm-like creature that could cut the hardest stone — essential because no iron tools could be used in Temple construction.

Benayahu's Plan to Capture Ashmedai

🏔️ Ashmedai's routine: ascends to heavenly study hall, returns to earthly study hall, then drinks from his sealed pit
↓
⬇️ Benayahu digs a pit below Ashmedai's and drains the water out
↓
🧶 He plugs the drain with fleeces of wool
↓
⬆️ He digs a pit above and pours wine into Ashmedai's pit
↓
🌳 Benayahu climbs a tree and waits
↓
🍷 Ashmedai resists at first — quotes Proverbs 20:1 — but eventually drinks and falls asleep
↓
⛓️ Benayahu descends and locks the sacred chain around Ashmedai
ASHMEDAI'S WORDS "Wine is a mocker, strong drink is riotous; and whosoever wallows in it is not wise" (Proverbs 20:1) — yet even the king of demons could not resist.
