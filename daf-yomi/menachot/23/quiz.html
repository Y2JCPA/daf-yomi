Q1
According to Reish Lakish, why is it permitted to rub the Kometz of a Minchas Chotei in leftover oil from another Minchah?
A. The prohibition of oil never applies to the Kometz
B. "Lo yasim" only prohibits designating oil for the Minchas Chotei at the start ‚Äî after Kemitzah, adding oil is permitted
C. Leftover oil has no sanctity
D. The Kometz absorbs the oil and it disappears
Correct: B. Reish Lakish interprets "Lo yasim aleha shemen" as prohibiting the initial designation of oil for this Minchah. Once Kemitzah has been performed, the prohibition no longer applies, and the Kometz can even be rubbed in leftover oil.
Q2
What is the machloket between R' Yochanan and Reish Lakish regarding "Chiburei Olim" (items connected to altar items)?
A. Whether connected items need salt
B. R' Yochanan says items contiguous to altar items are treated AS altar items; Reish Lakish disagrees ‚Äî connected items retain their own status
C. Whether bones need to be removed before offering
D. Whether oil and flour must be in the same vessel
Correct: B. R' Yochanan holds that Chiburei Olim k'Olim damu ‚Äî things connected to items going on the altar are treated as if they themselves go on the altar. So a bone completing a k'zayis of meat counts. Reish Lakish disagrees: the bone can be separated and has no mitzva of its own.
Q3
In the Mishnah, if two un-Kemitzah'd Menachot get mixed but the Kohen CAN still take a Kemitzah from each separately, what is the status?
A. Both are Pasul
B. Both are fit ‚Äî take Kemitzah from each portion separately
C. Only the larger one is fit
D. They must be offered together as one Minchah
Correct: B. The Mishnah rules that if the Kohen can still remove a handful from each Minchah independently, both remain fit. The mixing doesn't invalidate them as long as proper Kemitzah is still possible.
Q4
How does Rav Chisda's approach to nullification differ from R' Chanina's?
A. Rav Chisda says nothing is ever nullified
B. Rav Chisda follows the majority (nullifier): if it can become like the minority, the minority isn't nullified. R' Chanina follows the minority (nullified): if it can become like the majority, it isn't nullified.
C. They only disagree about blood mixtures
D. R' Chanina says everything is always nullified
Correct: B. Rav Chisda looks at the larger quantity: can it theoretically become like the smaller? If yes, the smaller retains identity. R' Chanina looks at the smaller: can it become like the larger? If yes, it retains identity. They arrive at opposite results in cases like Nevelah/Shechutah mixtures.
Q5
What does R' Zeira derive from the Torah using the same word "burning" for both the Kometz and the Shirayim?
A. Both must be burned at the same time
B. Just as one Kometz doesn't nullify another, one batch of Shirayim doesn't nullify another ‚Äî and Kometz and Shirayim don't nullify each other either
C. The Shirayim must be burned, not eaten
D. Burning language means the Minchah needs no salt
Correct: B. R' Zeira uses the shared "burning" language to establish a principle: none of the Minchah components nullify each other when mixed. Kometz retains identity in Shirayim, and Shirayim retain identity when mixed with other Shirayim.
