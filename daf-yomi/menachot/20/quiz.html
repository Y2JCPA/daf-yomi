How does Rav Yosef's second answer explain why salt can be Me'akev even though the Torah doesn't repeat the salting commandment (per Rav's principle)?

Correct: B. Rav Yosef's second answer: since "Brit Melach Olam" (eternal covenant of salt) is written, the word "Brit" is equivalent to having the rite repeated in a second verse. Covenant language indicates a fundamental, indispensable requirement.

A. He reads "Melach" as an acronym for different types of offerings
B. A Gezeirah Shavah: "Brit Melach Olam" (salt) parallels "Brit Kehunas Olam" (Kehunah) ‚Äî just as Korbanos require Kehunah, they require salt

Correct: B. R' Shimon draws a Gezeirah Shavah between "Brit Melach Olam" (Numbers 18:19) and "Brit Kehunas Olam" (Numbers 25:13). Just as Korbanos cannot exist without Kehunah, they cannot exist without salt. The parallel "covenant" language links the two.

Correct: B. The Klal-Prat-Klal derives: items similar to the Prat (the Kometz ‚Äî which "comes as a requirement" for the Shirayim and permits it) need salt.
