🔗 Gezeirah Shavah — Two Approaches

We learn communal Shelamim waving from individual Shelamim — but how far does the analogy go?

Rabbanan

  • 📖 "Don mimenah u'don mimenah"
  • 💡 Infer from the source AND derive ALL details from it
  • 🐑 Individual Shelamim: wave only breast & thigh
  • → So communal Shelamim: also wave ONLY breast & thigh (not the whole animal)

Rebbi (R' Yehuda HaNasi)

  • 📖 "Don mimenah v'oki b'atra"
  • 💡 Infer the basic law from the source, but interpret details according to this case's own context
  • 🐑 Learn THAT waving is required — but how? Look at the Shavuot verse itself
  • → Wave the WHOLE animal (breast, thigh, and everything else)
The core debate: When you learn a law via Gezeirah Shavah, do you import ALL the details from the source case? Or just the basic concept, then apply local rules?

🔄 Wait — Who Holds What?

Rav Pappa and Ravina give opposite explanations of the dispute!

Rav Pappa's Version

  • Everyone agrees: "Don mimenah u'don mimenah"
  • Rebbi reads the verse differently — "upon" the lambs means wave the WHOLE lamb
  • Rabbanan read it as "next to" — so wave only breast & thigh
  • 💡 Dispute is about the VERSE, not the Gezeirah Shavah principle

Ravina's Version (Opposite!)

  • Everyone agrees: "Don mimenah v'oki b'atra"
  • Rabbanan say: apply the rule locally → wave only breast & thigh
  • Rebbi says: the verse for communal Shelamim specifies the whole animal → wave all parts
  • 💡 Dispute is about what the LOCAL verse teaches
Takeaway: The same machlokes can be explained through different lenses — the practical conclusion stays the same, but the reasoning shifts!

📖 "Al" (עַל) — Upon or Next To?

Rebbi's reading of "al" as "etzel" (next to) — and the precedent from Shewbread
Challenge: Verse says "upon [al] the bread" — doesn't that mean literally ON TOP?
↓
Rebbi's proof from Shewbread:
"Put pure frankincense upon [al] each row" (Vayikra 24:7)
↓
But the frankincense was placed in bowls NEXT TO the bread, not on top of it!
↓
Conclusion: "Al" can mean "next to" (etzel) — so "upon the bread" can mean "alongside the bread"
Rabbanan counter: Shewbread is the exception — there's an explicit verse teaching the frankincense goes in bowls. You can't generalize from a special case!

🔪 Shechitah (slaughter of the lambs)

⚖️ R' Yosei — Why Women Don't Wave

The Gemara explores WHY women and gentiles are excluded from waving
T"K
"Benei Yisrael" → only sons (males) of Israel wave. Women and gentiles: no waving at all.
R'Y
🤔 Wait — we already know Semichah excludes women and gentiles. Maybe waving is the same?
T"K
No! Semichah is different — only the OWNER can do it (no agent). But waving can be done by a priest!
R'Y
Exactly! Since a priest CAN wave on someone's behalf, there's no reason to exclude women's offerings from waving. The offerings are still waved — just by the priest alone. 💡
Key distinction: Semichah has no agent — the owner MUST do it personally. Tenufah CAN be done by a priest. So women's offerings still get waved; the women just don't participate personally.

🏹 Lulav — An Arrow in Satan's Eye!

The Gemara connects Tenufah to the waving of the Lulav on Sukkot
Rav Acha bar Ya'akov would wave his lulav with the same extend-and-return motion as Tenufah and declare:

"This is an arrow in the eye of Satan!" 🏹👁️

Meaning: despite the Satan's efforts to discourage mitzvot, we persist joyously.
The Gemara warns: Don't actually say this! It draws the Satan's attention. Better to just wave with joy and kavanah without taunting.
Connection: R' Yosei bar Rav Avin derives from here that even a non-essential mitzva (like waving, which doesn't invalidate the korban if skipped) still has the power to prevent calamity. Every mitzva matters!
