QUESTION 1 OF 5
What does "Don mimenah u'don mimenah" mean in the context of Gezeirah Shavah?
A. Infer the basic law, then apply local rules for the details
B. Infer from the source case AND derive all details from that same source
C. You can only learn one law per Gezeirah Shavah
D. The law applies in both directions equally
Correct: B. "Don mimenah u'don mimenah" means: infer from the source and derive all the details from the source too. This is the Rabbanan's approach — since we learn waving of communal Shelamim from individual Shelamim, we also import the detail that only the breast and thigh are waved.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rebbi proves that "al" (upon) can mean "next to" from which precedent?
A. The blood sprinkled "upon" the altar actually goes on the side
B. The frankincense placed "upon" the Shewbread was actually in bowls next to it
C. The Kohen Gadol's garments placed "upon" his head were actually on his shoulders
D. The Omer placed "upon" the altar was actually waved in front of it
Correct: B. The verse says "put pure frankincense upon (al) each row" of Shewbread, but we know the frankincense was placed in bowls next to the rows, not physically on top of the bread. This proves "al" can mean "next to."
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How many times are the communal Shelamim of Shavuot waved?
A. Once — after slaughter only
B. Twice — while alive and after slaughter
C. Three times — alive, during slaughter, and after
D. They are not waved at all
Correct: B — Twice. R' Shimon's Mishna teaches that communal Shelamim (the Shavuot lambs) require Tenufah both while alive and after slaughter. This is unique — individual Shelamim are only waved after slaughter.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rav Acha bar Ya'akov waved his lulav in the Tenufah style and declared it was what?
A. A praise to Hashem
B. A blessing on the four species
C. An arrow in the eye of the Satan
D. A sign of the covenant
Correct: C — "An arrow in the eye of the Satan." The Gemara warns against actually saying this, as it draws the Satan's attention. But the idea is that performing mitzvot joyously defeats the forces that try to discourage us.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does R' Yosei say Semichah (placing hands) is different from Tenufah regarding women?
A. Women are exempt from time-bound mitzvot
B. Semichah requires the owner personally — no agent; Tenufah can be done by a priest
C. The Torah explicitly excludes women from Semichah but not from Tenufah
D. Women cannot enter the Azarah where Semichah takes place
Correct: B. Semichah must be done by the owner — there's no shelichut (agency). Since women can't personally do Semichah, their offerings skip it. But Tenufah CAN be performed by a priest as an agent, so women's offerings still get waved — the priest just does it alone.
