Order of the Four Passages

Position (Right→Left)Passage
1 (Rightmost)"Kadesh" — Exodus 13:1-10
2"V'haya ki yevi'akha" — Exodus 13:11-16
3"Shema" — Deuteronomy 6:4-9
4 (Leftmost)"V'haya im shamo'a" — Deuteronomy 11:13-21
If passages are swapped: Inner for inner or outer for outer (e.g., swapping positions 2 and 3) — invalid. But swapping inner for outer (1↔4) — also invalid according to the accepted view.

The Titora — Base of the Tefillin

Rav Chananel / Rav: The titora (base upon which the compartments rest) is halakha leMoshe miSinai.

The titora must be made from the same hide as the compartments. If the strap is still attached to the titora when it hangs, the tefillin are valid.
4 / 7

The Letter Shin (ש) on the Head Tefillin

Halakha leMoshe miSinai: The letter shin (ש) is embossed on the outside of the head tefillin — on both sides.

One side has a 3-pronged shin (normal ש), the other has a 4-pronged shin. This is unique to tefillin and has no parallel elsewhere in Jewish law.
5 / 7

Torn Straps

Abaye asked Rav Yosef: If the strap tore, may one tie it?

Rav Yosef: The knot is halakha leMoshe miSinai — it must form the shape of the letter daled (ד) for the head and yud (י) for the arm.

Together with the shin on the box: ש + ד + י = שדי (Shaddai) — one of God's Names.
6 / 7

Sewing Straps

Rav Acha bar Rav Yosef to Rav Ashi: May one sew a torn strap and hide the stitching inside?

Answer: The black side must face outward — the stitching must not be visible. The straps must be black on the outer side, which is also halakha leMoshe miSinai.
7 / 7

Key Takeaways — Menachot 34

1. Four passages in strict order: Kadesh, V'haya ki, Shema, V'haya im — right to left.

2. The titora (base) is halakha leMoshe miSinai; must be from the same hide.

3. Shin on both sides of the head tefillin — 3-pronged and 4-pronged.

4. Knots form daled (head) and yud (arm) — with the shin: שדי (Shaddai).

5. Black side of straps must face outward — halakha leMoshe miSinai.
