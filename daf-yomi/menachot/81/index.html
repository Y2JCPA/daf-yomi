🔄 The Substitute Problem

The dilemma: If someone has a thanks offering animal (A) and isn't sure which animal is the "real" offering vs. a substitute — how do they get loaves for the right one?
❓ If Animal A is the thanks offering → needs loaves. If A is a substitute → does NOT need loaves. But we don't know which it is!
↓
Rav Yitzchak bar Shmuel's proposal: Bring a NEW animal + loaves and say conditionally: "If A is the substitute, THIS is my thanks offering + these are its loaves. If A is the thanks offering, these are its loaves."
↓
Rav Nachman objects: Separating a substitute deliberately incurs 40 lashes! You can't build a halachic solution on an act that's forbidden!

🌊 The Waving Problem

Rav Ila's proposal (while ill, visited by Abaye): Place loaves outside the Temple courtyard wall. If the animal is the thanks offering → those are its loaves. If not → they revert to non-sacred status.
↓
❌ Problem: The four teruma loaves must be waved!
↓
Wave outside courtyard?
❌ Verse requires "before the Lord" — only valid inside the Temple courtyard
Wave inside courtyard?
❌ Would bring non-sacred food into the Temple courtyard — forbidden
↓
💡 Both options are blocked. No workaround found.

🏆 Rav Sheisha's Alternative

Rav Sheisha, son of Rav Idi: What if we follow Chizkiya's opinion that loaves inside the Temple courtyard ARE consecrated by slaughter — then we can bring the loaves inside and wave them normally!
R' Yochanan: loaves outside the wall (but in Jerusalem) → consecrated at slaughter. Chizkiya: must be inside the courtyard.
↓
If following Chizkiya — bring loaves inside the courtyard conditionally. If the animal is the thanks offering → loaves are consecrated. Can then wave inside.
↓
But per R' Yochanan: loaves inside courtyard would be unconditionally consecrated even before slaughter — creating other problems.

💰 Thanks Offering From Second-Tithe Money

ScenarioPermitted?
Thanks offering animal from non-sacred money, loaves from second-tithe money❌ Must use non-sacred money for both
Thanks offering from second-tithe money, loaves from non-sacred money✅ Permitted
Both thanks offering and loaves from second-tithe money✅ Permitted
Loaves made from second-tithe wheat (the grain itself)❌ Forbidden — must buy with second-tithe money, not use the actual wheat
Why can't the loaves come from second-tithe wheat directly? A Gezerah Shavah links thanks offering to peace offering: just as peace offerings are not the same species as second tithe, so too thanks offering loaves cannot be of the same species (i.e., actual second-tithe wheat).

📖 Rav Huna: "Loaves of a Thanks Offering" Vow

If someone vows: "I obligate myself to bring loaves of a thanks offering" — does he also need to bring the animal?
↓
Rav Huna: Yes — he must bring both the animal AND the loaves. Why? Because he knows loaves don't come without a thanks offering — so he's implicitly vowing both.
↓
Why did he only say "loaves"? He cited the END of the matter — the last step of a thanks offering is the loaves. But he meant the whole package.

⚠️ The 40 Lashes Rule on Substitutes

Background: If someone says "this animal shall be a substitute for my consecrated animal" — he incurs 40 lashes (Torah prohibition). The intent is to prevent people from trading their best animal for an inferior substitute.

Implication: Any halachic remedy that requires deliberately separating a substitute is off the table — even if it would solve the loaves problem. You cannot plan to violate a prohibition as part of your solution.

Central Theme: Resolving uncertainty about a thanks offering animal and its loaves

Conditional substitute proposal: blocked (40 lashes). Loaves outside courtyard: blocked (waving). Loaves inside courtyard per Chizkiya: possible but complex.
