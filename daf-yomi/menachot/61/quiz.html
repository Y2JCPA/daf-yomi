Menachot 61 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, waving (Tenufah) is performed in which direction(s)?
A. Only east and west
B. All four compass directions, then raised and lowered
C. Only raised up and lowered down
D. Only toward the Mizbeach and back
Correct: B. The priest extends the offering to all four directions and brings it back, then raises and lowers it. The Gemara explains: extending and bringing back halts harmful winds; raising and lowering halts harmful dew.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
When a Jewish woman brings a Shelamim, who performs the Tenufah?
A. The woman waves it herself
B. The woman and the priest together
C. The priest alone
D. No waving is done at all
Correct: C — The priest alone. Rav Yehuda explains: offerings of both men and women require waving, but only male Jews actually participate in the waving act. For women's offerings, the priest waves alone.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Shimon, which of these offerings requires Semichah (placing hands) while the animal is alive?
A. Communal Shelamim of Shavuot
B. Individual Shelamim
C. The Omer meal offering
D. The Two Loaves
Correct: B — Individual Shelamim. R' Shimon's pattern: Individual Shelamim get Semichah + Tenufah after slaughter (but no Tenufah alive). Communal Shelamim get Tenufah alive + after slaughter (but no Semichah). Asham Metzora gets Semichah + Tenufah alive (but no Tenufah after slaughter).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
For the Shavuot waving, where does Chanina ben Chachinai say to place the Two Loaves relative to the Two Lambs?
A. On top of the lambs
B. Underneath the lambs
C. Between the thighs of the two lambs
D. Next to the lambs, side by side
Correct: C — Between the thighs. Chanina ben Chachinai's creative solution resolves two seemingly contradictory verses: one says "upon the bread" and one says "upon the two lambs." By placing the loaves between the thighs, they are simultaneously on the lambs and the lambs are on them.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which two offerings require BOTH Tenufah (waving) AND Hagashah (bringing near)?
A. The Two Loaves and the Shewbread
B. The Omer meal offering and the Sotah meal offering
C. The Metzora's guilt offering and the Bikkurim
D. Individual Shelamim and Communal Shelamim
Correct: B — Omer and Sotah. The Mishna lists these as the only two offerings that require both Tenufah and Hagashah. Most offerings require only waving (like Shelamim) or neither (like Shewbread and Nesachim).
