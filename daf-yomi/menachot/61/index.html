🥖🐑 Stacking Order — What Goes Where?

For the Shavuot offering: Two Loaves + Two Lambs — who's on top?

Tanna Kamma

  • 🥖 Loaves on TOP
  • 🐑 Lambs on bottom
  • 📖 "Upon the bread of the first fruits" → lambs waved upon the bread = bread is the base
  • ⚡ But wait — another verse says "upon the two lambs"!

R' Yosei b"HaMeshullam

  • 🐑 Lambs on TOP
  • 🥖 Loaves on bottom
  • 📖 "Upon the two lambs" → loaves go ON the lambs
  • 💡 Reads "upon the bread" as "next to" (al = etzel)
Chanina ben Chachinai — creative solution: Place the loaves between the thighs of the two lambs! 🐑🥖🐑
This way the loaves are simultaneously "on" the lambs AND the lambs are "on" the loaves.

📚 General Stacking Rule for Waving

For ALL offerings that require waving — the layering order
🥖 Loaves (if applicable)
Always on the very top
↓
🦵 Breast & Thigh
Next layer down
↓
🔥 Sacrificial Portions (Eimurim)
Fat, tail, kidneys — on the bottom
↓
✋ Priest's Hands
Placed underneath everything
Three priests needed: One brings the parts from the slaughter area, one waves them, and a third burns them on the Mizbeach. Why? Because you can't rearrange mid-service — the stacking order for carrying ≠ the order for waving.

⚖️ Who Actually Performs Tenufah?

The Mishna says a puzzling thing: "Both men and women… by Jews and not by others"
Rav Yehuda's resolution: The offerings of both men and women require waving. But the actual waving is performed only by male Jews. If a woman brings a korban, the priest alone waves it — she doesn't participate.
Owner Offering Requires Waving? Who Waves?
Jewish Man ✓ Yes Owner + Priest together
Jewish Woman ✓ Yes Priest alone
Ger (Convert) ✓ Yes Owner + Priest together
Gentile ✗ No N/A
בְּנֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל — בני ישראל מניפין ואין הגויים מניפין
Source: "Benei Yisrael" (Lev. 7:29) — Benei = sons (males wave); Yisrael = Jews (not gentiles). Gerim are included because another verse adds "the stranger who dwells among you."

🧺 Bikkurim — Who Waves the First Fruits?

Both the priest AND the owner wave together — derived by a Hekesh
GM
The verse about Bikkurim says the priest takes the basket and waves. But the Shelamim verse says the owner's hands bring it. How do both apply?
ANS
🤝 The priest places his hands beneath the owner's hands, and they wave together!
GM
Why does the Mishna attribute this to R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov and not R' Yehuda? Both say Bikkurim need waving!
Rava
R' Eliezer b"Y's verse comes first in the Torah — it "opens" the topic.
R"N
R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov's "strength is great" — Mishnato kav v'naki — the Halacha always follows him! 💪
R' Eliezer b"Y's source: "And the priest shall take the basket from your hand" (Devarim 26:4) — from your hand implies the owner is holding it while the priest takes it → they wave together.

Three offerings, three mitzvot — each gets exactly TWO of the three

The elegant pattern: Every offering gets exactly 2 out of 3, and each mitzva is "missing" from exactly one offering. R' Shimon loves finding these symmetrical structures in Halacha.
