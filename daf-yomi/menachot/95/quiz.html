Menachot 95 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Dimi's clarification, when do both Amoraim agree that the Shewbread is NOT disqualified during the Wilderness journeys?
A. When the bread is removed from the Table before the journey
B. When the bread remains on the Table throughout the journey
C. When the bread is covered with a cloth during travel
D. When the journey lasts less than one day
Correct: B — When bread stays on the Table. Rav Dimi resolves the dispute by clarifying that if the bread remained on the Shulchan throughout the journey, both Amoraim agree it remains valid. The real dispute is only about a case where the bread was removed from the Table before the journey began.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
The verse "And the continual bread shall remain upon it" (Bamidbar 4:7) is used to support which position in the journey dispute?
A. The bread is disqualified during the journey — the verse describes an obligation, not a reality
B. The bread is NOT disqualified — "continual" means it retains its sanctity as long as it's on the Table
C. The bread must be replaced fresh each journey
D. The bread is treated as Yotzei during travel
Correct: B — Not disqualified. The phrase "the continual bread shall remain upon it" indicates that even during journeys the bread is called "the continual bread" — implying it retains its sacred character as long as it stays on the Table.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the First Tanna (Tanna Kamma) of the Mishna, which stages of preparing the Two Loaves and Shewbread happen OUTSIDE the Temple courtyard?
A. Only the baking
B. Only the grinding of the flour
C. The kneading and forming (but the baking is inside)
D. All stages — everything outside
Correct: C — Kneading and forming are outside; baking is inside. The First Tanna rules that the kneading and forming of the dough occur outside the Temple courtyard, but the baking must take place inside. This split creates the famous tension that Rav Sheshes identifies.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rav Sheshes raises a sharp question about the Mishna's inner contradiction. What is the heart of his challenge?
A. If kneading is outside, the dough can become Chametz — forbidden for the Shewbread
B. If the measuring vessel doesn't sanctify (implied by kneading outside), why must baking be inside? And if the oven sanctifies (implied by baking inside), wouldn't leaving unsanctified bread overnight before Shabbat cause Linah?
C. R' Yehuda and R' Shimon both read from the same verse — so one of them must be wrong
D. The Mishna fails to specify whether the oven is inside or outside the Azarah
Correct: B — The measuring vessel / oven contradiction. Rav Sheshes's challenge: (1) If kneading is done outside → implies the measuring vessel does NOT sanctify. (2) But then why must baking be inside? Maybe the oven sanctifies. (3) But if the oven sanctifies during baking, and preparation before Shabbat leaves bread in the oven unsanctified overnight → Linah would disqualify it. Both resolutions create new problems.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
R' Shimon says the Two Loaves and Shewbread are valid whether baked inside the Temple courtyard or in Beit Pagei. What is Beit Pagei?
A. A special bakery room inside the Temple
B. An area outside the Temple Mount entirely
C. The storage area between the Azarah and the Heichal
D. The name of the baker's guild in Jerusalem
Correct: B — Outside the Temple Mount. Beit Pagei was a location outside the Temple Mount entirely. R' Shimon's ruling is remarkably lenient: the bread is valid even when baked in this distant location, so long as the final product meets the specifications. This is the most permissive position of the three Tanna'im.
