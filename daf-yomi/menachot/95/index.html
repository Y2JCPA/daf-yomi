🏕️ The Wilderness Journey — Does Shewbread Disqualify?

When the Jewish people traveled in the desert, the Mishkan was dismantled and the Shulchan was carried — with the Shewbread still on it. Was the bread disqualified (as "Yotzei")?

Opinion A — Disqualified During Journey

  • 📖 "As they encamp, so shall they journey" (Bamidbar 2:17)
  • 🔗 Journey = same rules as encampment
  • ✂️ During encampment: leaving the Mishkan disqualifies
  • 💡 Therefore: during journeys too, leaving the Mishkan disqualifies
  • ⚠️ If removed from the Table → disqualified

Opinion B — NOT Disqualified During Journey

  • 📖 "The continual bread shall remain upon it" (Bamidbar 4:7)
  • 🔗 "Continual" = retains status even during the journey
  • ✅ As long as bread stays on the Table: valid
  • 💡 "Tent of Meeting shall journey" — it remains the Tent of Meeting even in transit
  • ⚠️ Only disqualified if removed FROM the Table

💡 Rav Dimi's Resolution

When Rav Dimi came from Eretz Yisrael to Babylon, he clarified the real dispute:
Case 1: Bread remains on the Table during the journey
→ EVERYONE agrees: NOT disqualified
↓
Case 2: Bread was REMOVED from the Table before the journey began
→ This is where R' Yochanan and R' Yehoshua ben Levi dispute
↓
Disqualified
One view:
"As they encamp, so they journey" = journey mirrors encampment rules.
Outside the Mishkan boundaries → Yotzei → Pasul
Not Disqualified
Other view:
"Then the Tent of Meeting shall journey" = still the Tent of Meeting.
The Mishkan's sanctity travels with it.

📜 Inside vs. Outside — Three Opinions

Where must the Two Loaves and Shewbread be prepared — inside or outside the Azarah?
Stage First Tanna R' Yehuda R' Shimon
Kneading (Lisha) 🌍 Outside 🏛️ Inside 🌍 Outside (or inside)
Forming (Itzuv) 🌍 Outside 🏛️ Inside 🌍 Outside (or inside)
Baking (Afiya) 🏛️ Inside 🏛️ Inside 🏛️ Inside OR Beit Pagei
Overrides Shabbat? ✗ No ✗ No ✗ No
R' Shimon's bold position: The Two Loaves and Shewbread are valid whether baked inside the Temple courtyard or in Beit Pagei — an area outside the Temple Mount entirely. He disagrees with both the First Tanna and R' Yehuda on the baking location.

🤔 The Inner Contradiction — Rav Sheshes's Challenge

The First Tanna says: knead/form outside, but bake inside. This seems contradictory!
RS
You say kneading is done OUTSIDE → the dry measure (1/10 ephah vessel) must NOT sanctify the flour. 🤔
?
Correct — if it sanctified, you couldn't knead outside!
RS
But you say baking must be INSIDE — as if the flour IS already sanctified! ❓
?
Maybe the oven is what sanctifies — not the measuring vessel! Then baking inside is required because the oven consecrates it at that moment.
RS
But then — if the oven sanctifies DURING baking, and baking doesn't override Shabbat... the bread would be LEFT OVERNIGHT in the oven unsanctified, and be Pasul from Linah! ⚠️
Rav Sheshes's difficulty: No matter how you read the Mishna, there's a tension. Rabba called this person "a formidable man, tough as iron." The dilemma remains sharp until Rava's resolution (see next slide).

🔍 Attempted Resolutions — Rava and Rav Ashi

The Core Contradiction:
If measure doesn't sanctify → why must baking be inside?
If oven sanctifies → why doesn't bread go Pasul from Linah before Shabbat?
↓
Rava's reading
The Mishna itself is difficult from a different angle:
"Baking inside" implies oven sanctifies
But "doesn't override Shabbat" implies it DOESN'T
This is the real tension. Rav Sheshes → iron-tough challenge.
Rav Ashi's attempt
Maybe "baking inside" just means "where vigilant priests supervise" — not about sanctity!
Rejected! If vigilance matters for baking, it should also matter for kneading.
↓
The Gemara concludes: Rav Ashi's explanation is an error (baruta). You can't differentiate kneading from baking based on "vigilance." The difficulty stands.

⚔️ R' Yehuda vs. R' Shimon — Same Verse, Different Readings

R' Abbahu bar Kahana: Both Tanna'im derive their positions from the same verse!
Source verse: וְלָקַחְתָּ סֹלֶת וְאָפִיתָ אֹתָהּ שְׁתֵּים עֶשְׂרֵה חַלּוֹת
"And you shall take fine flour and bake it into twelve loaves" (Vayikra 24:5)

R' Yehuda — Everything Inside

  • 📖 "And you shall take [in the Mishkan] and bake"
  • 💡 Both taking (preparation) AND baking happen in the same place
  • 🏛️ The context: the verse speaks about the Mishkan → everything inside
  • ⚡ Kneading and forming must also be inside the Azarah

R' Shimon — Baking Can Be Anywhere

  • 📖 "And you shall bake" — the Torah specifies only the final product (12 loaves)
  • 💡 Baking just means the bread must be correctly made — location is flexible
  • 🌍 Valid even from Beit Pagei (outside Temple Mount entirely)
  • ⚡ As long as the final loaves meet the spec, where doesn't matter
