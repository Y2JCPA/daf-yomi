🗺️ Table Orientation — N-S or E-W?

Opinion A — N to S (across the Heichal width)

  • 🏛️ Table placed along the width of the Sanctuary
  • ✅ Priests can stand E and W of the Table to arrange/remove
  • 💡 Plenty of room on both sides
  • 📐 Simple spatial arrangement

Opinion B — E to W (along the Heichal length)

  • 🏛️ Table placed along the length of the Sanctuary
  • ⚠️ Space calculation gets very tight!
  • 📐 2.5 + 1 + 2.5 + 1 + 2.5 + 1 = 10.5 cubits
  • 💡 But the Sanctuary is only 10 cubits wide — so there's a problem
Resolution for Opinion B: Moses' Table was positioned slightly to the west, not in line with Solomon's tables. Since it was recessed westward (like a teacher elevated above students), the spatial calculation works out perfectly.

📜 R' Yosei ben R' Yehuda's Third View

R' Yosei's creative reading: The "Tables" (plural) in Chronicles refers to three specific tables in the Temple — not Solomon's ten in the Heichal!
TableLocationMaterialPurpose
Ulam Table 1Entrance Hall (inside)SilverNew Shewbread placed here first
Ulam Table 2Entrance Hall (inside)GoldOld Shewbread placed here after removal
Heichal TableSanctuary (Moses' Table)GoldThe actual Shewbread — always here
The logic: New loaves were briefly placed on the silver table, then moved to the gold table in the Heichal. Old loaves were placed on the gold table in the Ulam when removed. This "three-table" system explains the plural without requiring all 10 Solomon tables to be used.

👨‍🏫 "Like a Student Before His Teacher"

The Gemara describes Solomon's 10 tables as positioned slightly lower and to the east of Moses' Table — "like a student who sits before his teacher."
🏛️
West (Raised Position)
Moses' Table
Higher • More holy • Like a Rebbe
↓
East (Lower Position)
5 tables on each side
Lower • Like students • Flanking the teacher
Why was Moses' Table elevated? Because the Temple was built on a slope going downward from west to east. The westernmost placement = the highest point = the position of honor = the Rebbe's seat.
