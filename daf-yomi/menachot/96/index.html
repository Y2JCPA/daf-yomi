📏 The Table's Frame — Above or Below?

R' Yosei — Frame Above

  • 📐 The one-handbreadth frame surrounded the Table's TOP edge
  • 🍞 The frame supported the loaves — no panels needed at all!
  • 💡 "There were no panels there" — the frame did the job
  • 📖 Source: "You shall make a frame of one handbreadth" (Shemot 25:25)

Rabbanan — Frame Below

  • 📐 The frame was under the Table surface (at the legs)
  • 🍞 Panels WERE needed to support the loaves from tilting
  • 💡 The Table surface was completely flat — no raised edge
  • 📖 This view needs all 28 rods + 4 panels
Why does it matter? If the frame was above, the Table was a receptacle (had walls) — which affects whether it's susceptible to ritual impurity. If the frame was below, the Table surface was just a flat board.

⚗️ Is the Table Susceptible to Tumah?

Problem: The Table is a wooden vessel "designated to rest in place" (not carried around).
Rule: Wooden vessels fixed in place are NOT susceptible to impurity!
↓
Proof it IS susceptible:
עַל הַשֻּׁלְחָן הַטָּהֹר
"Upon the pure Table" (Vayikra 24:6)
If it can be called "pure," it must also be possible to become "impure"!
↓
Reish Lakish's resolution:
The Table WAS carried — they lifted it to show the Shewbread to the pilgrims!
If it's carried while full → it's like a sack → susceptible to Tumah

✨ The Great Miracle of the Shewbread

RYL
A great miracle was performed with the Shewbread: its condition at the time of its removal was the same as at the time it was set. ✨
?
But it sat on the Table for an entire week! How did it stay warm and fresh?
RYL
That's the miracle! Just as it was set on the Table warm from the oven on Shabbat, so too when it was removed the following Shabbat — still warm, still fresh. 🔥
Showing the Pilgrims: On the three Pilgrimage Festivals, the priests would lift the Shulchan and display the miraculous bread to the Olei Regel, saying: "See how beloved you are before the Omnipresent!" — for He performs this miracle continually on your behalf.

🍞 What Happens to the Old Loaves?

Old loaves removed from the Table
↓
Bowls of Levonah (frankincense) burned on the Mizbeach
This is the "matir" — the act that permits the priests to eat the bread
↓
12 loaves divided equally between the incoming and outgoing Kohen watches (Mishmarot)
6 loaves to each Mishmar
↓
The Kohen Gadol receives half → 6 loaves
The remaining Mishmar splits the other 6
Yom Kippur on Shabbat? When Yom Kippur falls on Shabbat, the old loaves aren't removed until Saturday night. They stay on the Table for 9 days — and are still as fresh as when they were set! (The miracle extends even past the usual 7 days.)

🔧 The Rods and Shabbat — Rabbi Akiva's Principle

Rv
Wait — if the rods are commanded by the Torah (from Shemot 25:29), why don't they override Shabbat? 🤔
RA
R' Akiva's principle: "Any labor that CAN be performed on Erev Shabbat does NOT override Shabbat." 📖
Rv
So the rods are placed on Friday and removed on Friday?
RA
Exactly! The bread and bowls MUST be set on Shabbat itself (that's essential). But the rods can be done before Shabbat — so we do them on Friday. ✅
