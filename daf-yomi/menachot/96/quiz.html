Menachot 96 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yosei, why were no gold panels needed to support the Shewbread?
A. The loaves were so light they didn't need support
B. The Table's frame was above the surface and supported the loaves directly
C. The rods alone were sufficient
D. The Shewbread was stacked without any separation
Correct: B. R' Yosei holds the frame was above the Table's surface, creating a raised edge that supported the loaves — making panels unnecessary. The Rabbanan disagree and say the frame was below the Table.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the problem that the Table — a wooden vessel "designated to rest in place" — should not be susceptible to ritual impurity?
A. The Table was actually made of metal, not wood
B. Reish Lakish: the Table was carried (lifted) to display the Shewbread to pilgrims, so it's "carried full and empty"
C. The gold overlay made it a metal vessel, not a wooden one
D. It was a special Rabbinic decree
Correct: B. Reish Lakish explains that the priests lifted the Table (with its Shewbread) on the Festivals to display the miracle to the pilgrims. Since it was carried both full and empty — like a sack — it qualifies as a portable wooden vessel susceptible to impurity.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What miracle did R' Yehoshua ben Levi describe about the Shewbread?
A. The Shewbread multiplied itself each week
B. Its removal condition was the same as when it was set — still warm and fresh after 7 days
C. The frankincense never burned out
D. The Table itself glowed with divine light
Correct: B. R' Yehoshua ben Levi taught that the Shewbread was as warm and fresh when removed (after sitting for a full week) as it was when first placed on the Table. This miracle was demonstrated to the pilgrims on the Festivals.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did the priests perform a simultaneous exchange of old and new Shewbread?
A. To prevent the bread from touching the ground
B. Because the Torah says "Lechem Tamid" — continual bread — the Table must never be empty
C. To ensure the bread stayed warm
D. It was a Rabbinic enactment for efficiency
Correct: B. The Torah calls it "Lechem Tamid" (continual bread) — the bread must be "before Me always." To ensure the Table was never without bread, two priests laid down the new loaves at the exact same moment two others removed the old.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why don't the arranging and removal of the gold rods override Shabbat?
A. Rods are only Rabbinic, not Torah law
B. R' Akiva's principle: any labor that can be done on Erev Shabbat does not override Shabbat
C. The rods are not considered sacred vessels
D. Arranging rods is not considered "labor" (melacha)
Correct: B. R' Akiva states a principle: any Temple labor that can be performed before Shabbat does not override Shabbat. Since the rods can be placed on Friday and removed on Friday, they don't need to be handled on Shabbat itself — unlike the bread and bowls, which must be set specifically on Shabbat.
