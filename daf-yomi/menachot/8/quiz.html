1. What is the machloket between R' Yochanan and R' Elazar about Chavitei Kohen Gadol?

A) Whether the Chavitin require oil
B) Whether the Chavitin can be sanctified in a Kli Shares in halves (half-Isaron at a time)
C) Whether the Kohen Gadol must bake them himself
D) Whether they are offered only in the morning
R' Yochanan says the Chavitin cannot be sanctified in halves ‚Äî the full Isaron must be placed in the Kli Shares at once ("bring a Minchah, then divide"). R' Elazar says since they're offered in halves, they can be sanctified in halves too.

2. According to Rav, can a Minchah that requires oil be sanctified without it?

A) No ‚Äî oil is always required for sanctification
B) Yes ‚Äî we learn from Lechem ha'Panim (sanctified without oil)
C) Only if the Kohen intends to add oil later
D) Only for Minchas Chotei
Rav holds each component can be sanctified independently: flour without oil (from Lechem ha'Panim), flour without Levonah (from Minchas Nesachim), without both (from Minchas Chotei), oil alone (from Log of Metzora), Levonah alone (from Bazichin).

3. According to Shmuel, what can Mizrakot (blood bowls) sanctify that regular liquid measures cannot?

A) Other vessels
B) Dry items (like flour/frankincense), not just liquids
C) Animals designated for sacrifice
D) Items already sanctified by another vessel
Regular liquid measures only sanctify liquids, and dry measures only dry items. But Shmuel derives from "Both of them full of fine flour" (Bamidbar 7:13) that Mizrakot (blood bowls) can sanctify dry items as well ‚Äî proving that flour can be sanctified without oil.

4. Why does R' Elazar say Kemitzah done in the Heichal (Sanctuary) is valid?

A) The Torah explicitly permits it
B) He learns from the removal of Levonah bowls from the Shulchan, which takes place in the Heichal
C) All Avodos can be performed anywhere in the Mikdash
D) It's only a Rabbinic requirement to do Kemitzah in the Azarah
R' Elazar derives Minchah from Minchah: removing the Bazichin (Levonah bowls) from the Shulchan in the Heichal is analogous to Kemitzah. Just as that Minchah-related removal is valid in the Heichal, so is Kemitzah of a regular Minchah.

5. What is R' Elazar's ruling when Lechem ha'Panim broke after the time for removal arrived (but before actual removal)?

A) Both the bread and Levonah are Pasul
B) The bread is Pasul but the Levonah IS burned ‚Äî because once the time arrives, it's as if removal happened
C) The bread is still valid if it can be reassembled
D) A new batch must be baked
R' Elazar holds that "before/after removal" means before/after the TIME for removal. Once the time comes, it's as if the removal already happened. This works because the Levonah was always separate from the bread (its "Kometz" was clearly designated), unlike a regular Minchah's Kometz.
