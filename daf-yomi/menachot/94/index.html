⚖️ Tenufah vs. Semikha — Head-to-Head

Dimension 🫸 Tenufah (Waving) 🤲 Semikha (Placing Hands)
Applies to live animals? ✓ Yes ✓ Yes
Applies to slaughtered animals? ✓ Yes (breast, thigh) ✗ No
Applies to items without life? ✓ Yes (Omer, Sotah, loaves) ✗ No — only living animals
All co-owners must perform it? ✗ One acts for all ✓ Each one individually
Can an agent perform it? ✗ No ✗ No
The paradox: Tenufah has broader scope (applies to slaughtered animals + non-living items), yet only one partner waves. Semikha has narrower scope (living animals only), yet ALL partners must place their hands. The verse "korbano" overrides any kal va'chomer.

🏛️ The Kohen Gadol's Special Semikha

From Masechet Tamid: When the Kohen Gadol wanted to sacrifice the daily offering (Tamid), he placed his hands on each limb as it was handed to him before throwing it on the altar.

But wait — isn't Semikha only for live animals?

Abaye answers: This isn't Semikha in the halachic sense at all. The Kohen Gadol places his hands on the limbs as a mark of honor and prestige (Kavod Kohen Gadol) — not as a fulfillment of the mitzva of Semikha. It's a ceremonial gesture to distinguish the High Priest's service.

📜 New Mishna: Two Loaves vs. Shewbread

Rule 🐑 Shtei HaLechem (Two Loaves / Shavuot) 🕎 Lechem HaPanim (Shewbread)
Kneaded One by one One by one
Baked One by one Two by two
Mold used? No mold mentioned ✅ Defus (mold) — before AND after baking
Amount 2 loaves × 2/10 ephah each 12 loaves × 2/10 ephah each
Chametz or Matzah? Chametz (leavened!) Matzah (unleavened)
The Defus (מוּד): The baker used a mold for the Shewbread when forming the dough AND again when removing it from the oven — to ensure the loaves kept their special shape and didn't get misshapen.

🛶 What Shape Was the Lechem HaPanim?

The Gemara debates two Amoraim: R' Chanina vs. R' Yochanan

R' Chanina — "Open Box" 📦

  • 📐 Wide flat base, two parallel walls on the sides
  • 🔓 Open on two ends (like a rectangular box with no lid and no short ends)
  • ✅ Bowls of frankincense can rest on the flat top
  • ✅ Rods can rest stably on the flat surface
  • 📌 Panels placed on the ground (not on the Table)

R' Yochanan — "Rocking Boat" 🛶

  • 📐 Narrow base, two walls angling outward and upward
  • 🛶 Triangular cross-section, rocks if not supported
  • ✅ Explains why the panels were essential (to stop it rocking)
  • 📌 Panels placed on the Table (not on the ground)
  • ⚠️ Bowls placed in a specially prepared flat spot
A Baraita supports R' Yochanan: "There were four gold panels there, which split up at their upper ends like forked branches — because the bread resembled a rocking boat and was supported by them."

🔍 "Granted / But According to..." — Gemara's Logic

The Gemara uses the classic bishlamah... ella pattern to test each opinion:
Question Box (R' Chanina) Boat (R' Yochanan)
Where do the bowls rest? ✓ On the flat top Made a special flat spot
How do the rods rest? ✓ On the flat surface Made a protrusion at the base
Why do panels exist? Prevent crushing from weight above ✓ Loaves need lateral support
Where are panels placed? On the ground ✓ On the Table (per Baraita)
R' Yehuda: "Bread supports panels + panels support bread"? ✗ Doesn't work (panels on ground) ✓ Works perfectly (mutual leaning)
Bottom line: The Baraita about the forked gold panels aligns with R' Yochanan's "rocking boat" shape. The structure of the Temple service required the panels precisely because the narrow-based boat shape was unstable without lateral support.
