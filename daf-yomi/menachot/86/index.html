🚫 Forbidden Oil Sources

💧

Soaked Olives

Olives soaked in water — the resulting oil is disqualified for meal offerings

🫙

Pickled Olives

Olives preserved in brine — oil pressed from them is invalid

🍂

Boiled Oil

Oil that was cooked — disqualified for sacred use

🌱

Immature Olive Oil

Oil from olives under ⅓ grown — debated between R' Chiyya and R' Shimon b"R

✅

Standard Pressed

Oil from mature, fully grown olives pressed in the normal way — valid for offerings

🧴

Residue Oil

Oil extracted from the skins and pits after full pressing — disqualified

⚔️ The Immature Olive Debate

R' Chiyya
Sap from olives that haven't reached ⅓ of their growth is not considered oil at all — it's just plant sap
Would toss it away
R' Shimon b"R
That same sap is considered oil — it has oil properties even before full ripeness
Would dip food into it
Practical ruling: valid if used for an offering (b'dieved) per R' Chiyya's broader view that it has sap-like quality only
The mnemonic: "The wealthy are parsimonious" — R' Shimon, son of Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, was wealthy and didn't waste it. This helps remember who holds it IS oil (R' Shimon = wealthy, keeps it) and who tosses it (R' Chiyya, treats it as worthless sap).

🌿 "Oil of Myrrh" in Esther

שִׁשָּׁה חֳדָשִׁים בְּשֶׁמֶן הַמֹּר
Esther 2:12 — "Six months with oil of myrrh"
The Gemara asks: what is this "oil of myrrh"? Two opinions:
🌸

Rav Huna bar Chiyya

Oil of myrrh = setakhta — a fragrant cosmetic oil used to beautify and smooth skin

🌿

Rav Yirmeya bar Abba

Oil from olives that haven't reached ⅓ growth (anpikanon) — acidic, used as a depilatory. Removes hair, pampers skin.

A Baraita confirms Rav Yirmeya: R' Yehuda says anpikanon = immature olive oil. Women spread it because it removes hair and beautifies skin. This links our Mishnaic discussion about immature olive oil directly to the famous preparation rituals of the Megillah.

💡 "Refined Pounded Oil" — For Menorah Only

שֶׁמֶן זַיִת זָךְ כָּתִית לַמָּאוֹר
Vayikra 24:2 — "Pure beaten olive oil for illumination"
The verse specifies the best oil for the Menorah. Does this mean only the Menorah gets refined oil — not meal offerings?
💡

Menorah

Must use refined, pounded oil (Grade 1). The verse specifies this for illumination.

🍞

Meal Offerings

May also use pounded oil — derived from Shemot 29:40 ("pounded oil" in context of the daily offering's meal offering).

Why specify "for illumination" then? The Torah requires Grade 1 for the Menorah specifically — to conserve expense (haḥisakhon). Meal offerings may use Grade 2 or 3, which is cheaper.

📋 Menachot 86 — Summary

Oil Grades: Five grades of olive oil. Only Grade 1 (free-flowing, first press) is required for the Menorah. Grades 2–3 are valid for meal offerings. Later grades: household only.
The Debate: Immature olive oil — sap or oil? R' Chiyya: just sap (tosses it). R' Shimon b"R: it's oil (dips food). "Wealthy are parsimonious" = the mnemonic.
Esther Connection: "Oil of myrrh" = immature olive oil (Rav Yirmeya) or fragrant setakhta (Rav Huna). Links Temple law to Purim narrative.
