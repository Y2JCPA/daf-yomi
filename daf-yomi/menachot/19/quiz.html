How does R' Shimon derive that Yetzikah (pouring oil) requires a Kohen?
A. A Kal va'Chomer from Kemitzah
B. The "Vav HaMosif" in "v'Hevi'ah" ‚Äî the connecting Vav links "Bnei Aharon" backward to Yetzikah
C. A tradition (Halacha l'Moshe mi'Sinai)
D. The verse "Bnei Aharon" is interpreted to cover all acts before Kemitzah
Correct: B. R' Shimon uses "Vav HaMosif" ‚Äî the connecting Vav in "v'Hevi'ah el Bnei Aharon" links "Bnei Aharon" both forward (to Kemitzah) and backward (to Yetzikah). The Gemara notes he doesn't apply this everywhere ‚Äî elsewhere an earlier verse in context blocks it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rav's general rule for determining which Minchah rites are Me'akev (indispensable)?
A. Any rite mentioned in the Torah is indispensable
B. Any rite that the Torah repeats (writes twice) is indispensable ‚Äî a repetition signals Me'akev status
C. Only rites performed by the Kohen Gadol are indispensable
D. Only rites listed in the Mishnah's "not indispensable" list are excluded
Correct: B. Rav holds that whenever the Torah writes a sacrificial rite twice, the repetition signals that the rite is Me'akev (indispensable). This is also supported when both "law" and "statute" appear together, or when "covenant" language is used (as with salt).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why doesn't the repetition of "Hagashah" (bringing the Minchah near) in the Torah make it Me'akev according to Rav?
A. Hagashah is not a sacrificial rite at all
B. The second verse about Hagashah is establishing the location (the corner of the Mizbeach), not repeating the mitzva ‚Äî a location verse doesn't create Me'akev status
C. Hagashah was only required in the Tabernacle, not the Temple
D. Shmuel disagrees with Rav on this specific case
Correct: B. Rav answers that the second "Hagashah" verse isn't a repetition of the commandment ‚Äî it comes to establish the place where the Minchah is brought (the southwestern corner of the Mizbeach). A verse that defines location doesn't count as a repetition that creates Me'akev status.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The Torah writes "statute" regarding the Minchah. Why doesn't this make Minchah rites indispensable according to Rav's rule?
A. "Statute" regarding Minchah was written only about the Minchah of the Kohen Gadol
B. The "statute" verse for Minchah refers to the eating of the Shirayim, not to the sacrificial rites ‚Äî Rav's rule applies only when the statute refers to the rites themselves
C. Shmuel holds that "statute" never creates Me'akev status
D. The Minchah already has "law" written, making the "statute" redundant
Correct: B. Rav distinguishes: the "statute" written about Minchah ("it is a statute forever" ‚Äî Vayikra 6:11) refers to the priestly eating of the Shirayim, not the sacrificial rites. Rav's principle only applies when the statute is written in the context of the rites themselves.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Where does the Minchah get brought according to the resolution of "before the Lord" vs. "in front of the altar"?
A. The northern side of the Mizbeach
B. The southwestern corner ‚Äî satisfying both "before the Lord" (west) and "in front of the altar" (south)
C. The eastern side, facing the entrance
D. Any corner is valid
Correct: B. The two verses seem to contradict: "before the Lord" suggests the western side (facing the Kodesh HaKodashim), while "in front of the altar" suggests the southern side. The resolution is the southwestern corner, which satisfies both. R' Eliezer (per Rav Ashi) holds the whole altar was in the north, so the southern side IS "before the Lord."
