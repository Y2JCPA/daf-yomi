Menachot 70 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Rabba discusses produce that was tithed, replanted, and then grew further. What is the core halachic doubt?
A. Whether replanting invalidates all prior tithes automatically
B. Whether we follow the original tithed growth or the added new growth for tithing obligations
C. Whether only Kohanim may replant tithed produce
D. Whether replanted produce may be eaten in Jerusalem only
Correct: B. The sugya asks whether post-replanting growth inherits the status of the original (already tithed) produce, or whether the newly added growth requires fresh tithing. This becomes especially sharp when the original seed remains physically present.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rabba distinguish between substances whose seed disintegrates and those whose seed does not disintegrate?
A. Only non-disintegrating seeds are subject to Torah law
B. If the seed disintegrates, everyone agrees new growth is fresh produce needing new tithes; the doubt is specifically when the original seed remains intact
C. Disintegrating seeds are exempt from all agricultural mitzvot
D. Non-disintegrating seeds are always exempt from Chalah
Correct: B. Rabba states explicitly: his question is not about seeds that disintegrate — there it's obvious new growth is a new entity and must be tithed. His uncertainty is only where the original seed persists, blurring old/new identity.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which grains are obligated in Chalah according to the sugya?
A. Any grain that can be ground into flour
B. The five species only: wheat, barley, spelt, rye, oats
C. Wheat and barley only
D. Including rice and millet in Chutz LaAretz only
Correct: B. The Gemara lists and identifies the five grain species as those obligated in Chalah. Rice and millet are explicitly excluded.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Reish Lakish derive that Chalah applies only to the five grains?
A. Through a Gezerah Shavah between "bread" by Chalah and "bread" by Matzah
B. Through a direct list in the Torah by name
C. From a Halacha L'Moshe MiSinai with no textual source
D. From the laws of Bikkurim in Devarim
Correct: A. Reish Lakish uses the words "bread" in both contexts — Chalah and Matzah. Since Matzah is only from five grains, Chalah is similarly limited to those species.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why are rice and millet excluded from Matzah/Chalah categories according to the Gemara?
A. They are always considered kitniyot and therefore rabbinically forbidden
B. They cannot become true chametz; they spoil/decay (sirchon) rather than leaven
C. They were not common in Temple-era Israel
D. They are exempt only after the Temple's destruction
Correct: B. The defining criterion is capacity for true leavening. Rice/millet do not leaven in the halachic sense; they sour/decay. Therefore they are outside the five-grain framework.
