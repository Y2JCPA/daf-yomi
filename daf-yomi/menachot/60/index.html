The Service Vessel (Kli Sharet)

  • After kemitza, the handful must be placed into a kli sharet (sacred service vessel) â€” this sanctifying step is mandatory.
  • Without being placed in a kli sharet, the handful cannot be brought to the altar.
  • The kli sharet sanctifies the handful, creating an obligation: if the vessel breaks or is lost, the process cannot simply restart.
  • This parallels the requirement for blood: it too must be collected in a valid service vessel to be used for sprinkling.

Salting the Handful

  • "Every offering of your meal offering shall you salt with salt; neither shall you allow the salt of the covenant of your God to be lacking" (Lev. 2:13).
  • The handful must be salted before being placed on the altar fire.
  • Dispute: Does the salting requirement apply to the handful alone, the priestly remainder, or both?
  • The comprehensive verse suggests both â€” yet practically, the priests' portion is eaten, not burned, so the altar-specific salting focuses on the handful.

Burning the Handful

  • The handful (kometz) is placed on the altar fire â€” this is called haktara (burning as an offering).

  • The burning must be done by a priest â€” not a non-priest, not a priest who is ritually impure.
