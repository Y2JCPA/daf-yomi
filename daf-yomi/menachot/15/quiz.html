1. What is R. Yochanan's conclusion about why R. Yehudah holds that an impure loaf causes BOTH Shtei ha'Lechem loaves to go to burning?

A) R. Yehudah holds the Tzitz does not help impure food, so the Zerikah cannot permit the pure loaf
B) R. Yehudah had a fixed tradition (lamud aruch) that a Korban Tzibur is never divided ‚Äî regardless of the Tzitz question
C) R. Yehudah holds that impurity spreads from one loaf to the other during baking
D) R. Yehudah rules that both loaves must be burned whenever any part of a Korban becomes impure
R. Yochanan states that R. Yehudah had a "lamud aruch b'fiv" ‚Äî a settled, fixed tradition ‚Äî that a Korban Tzibur (communal offering) is never divided. This is not based on reasoning about the Tzitz at all. Three proofs (the Bazichim, the Pesach, and this Mishnah) all demonstrate that R. Yehudah's position is independent of the Tzitz debate.

2. According to Rav Kahana, why does the Todah create Pigul in its loaves?

A) The loaves and the Todah are waved together (Tenufah)
B) The verse places "Todah" before "Chalos" (loaves), teaching that the loaves are themselves called "Todah" ‚Äî they share the same name and time-limit
C) The loaves are baked from the same flour as the Todah meat offering
D) A Todah is a Korban Tzibur, so all its parts must be treated as one unit
Rav Kahana derives from the unusual word order in the verse "v'Hikriv Al Zevach ha'Todah Chalos" ‚Äî placing "Todah" before "Chalos" ‚Äî that the loaves are called "Todah." Since they share the name (and thus the time-designation) of the Todah, when the Todah is rendered Pigul by an improper intent, the loaves are rendered Pigul too.

3. Why can't the Todah loaves render the Todah itself Pigul?

A) The loaves are offered after the Todah, so their time has already passed
B) The loaves are called "Todah," but the Todah is never called "bread" ‚Äî only the secondary item takes the name of the primary
C) Pigul intent in the loaves only disqualifies the loaves ‚Äî it never affects animal offerings
D) The loaves have a different time-limit than the Todah
The Gemara answers: the loaves are secondary and take the name of the primary (the Todah). The name "Todah" transfers from primary to secondary ‚Äî but not backwards. The Todah meat is never called "bread," so intent regarding the loaves cannot Mefagel the Todah.

4. If the Shavuot lambs are slaughtered with intent to eat the LOAVES the next day, what happens to the lambs themselves?

A) The lambs also become Pigul
B) The lambs remain Kosher ‚Äî only the loaves become Pigul
C) Both the lambs and loaves become Pasul but not Pigul
D) The lambs remain Kosher, and the loaves are also Kosher
The Mishnah teaches the parallel rule for the Shavuot offering: if the lambs were slaughtered with intent to eat the LOAVES tomorrow ‚Äî the loaves become Pigul, but the lambs remain Kosher. The lambs are the Matir for the loaves; the loaves cannot Mefagel their own Matir (the lambs).

5. In R. Elazar's question about combined intent (¬Ω k'Zayis Todah meat + ¬Ω k'Zayis loaves, both for tomorrow), what is the answer?

A) Pasul ‚Äî combining meat and bread intents never creates Pigul
B) Pigul + Karet ‚Äî since the loaves are called "Todah," they are the same Min as the Todah meat, so the halves combine
C) The question remains unresolved (Teiku)
D) Kosher ‚Äî the intent was split between two different items
The answer is Pigul + Karet. Since the loaves are called "Todah," they are considered the same Min (species) as the Todah meat. Therefore, ¬Ω k'Zayis intent for the Todah meat and ¬Ω k'Zayis intent for the loaves combine ‚Äî just as two meat-intents combine ‚Äî to form a full k'Zayis of Pigul intent.
