🗿 The Broken Tablets in the Ark

לוחות ושברי לוחות מונחין בארון
"The whole tablets AND the broken tablets were placed in the Ark." (Berakhot 8b / referenced here)
Q
Why keep the broken tablets alongside the new whole ones? They were shattered — shouldn't they be discarded?
A
A Torah scholar who has forgotten his learning through no fault of his own — his broken knowledge is still sacred and must be honored, just like the broken tablets.
What does this teach us? Even what seems broken, forgotten, or incomplete retains its dignity and sanctity. The shards of the first tablets did not lose their holiness just because they were no longer whole. So too, a Torah scholar who has suffered loss of learning remains a Torah scholar.

📚 Can You Ever Stop Torah Study?

RS
The verse says "this book of the Torah shall not depart from your mouth." Does that mean you can NEVER stop for even one moment? 😰
RY
R' Yishmael: No — "you shall observe and do all that is written in it" — the verse itself comes to permit worldly activities alongside Torah study! 📖
RS
But R' Shimon bar Yochai disagrees — if you fully trust in God, you should be able to study Torah ALL day and night, and your needs will be provided!
RY
But in practice, Abaye notes: many followed Rashbi's approach and failed to support themselves. Many followed R' Yishmael and succeeded. The Rabbis rule like R' Yishmael.

⏰ "Barkai!" — Timing the Dawn

From Masechet Yoma: An appointed priest would go up to the roof and call: "Barkai!" (There is light!) when he could see light in the east reaching Hebron — signaling that the time for offering the Tamid (daily sacrifice) had arrived.
P1
"Barkai!" — I see the light spreading as far as Hebron! Time to begin the morning service! 🌅
MS
Mattitya ben Shmuel: We waited for the disk of the sun to actually emerge before calling, not just the first light — to make sure it was truly day.
Why does this appear here? The Gemara connects the daily Shewbread exchange (which happens on Shabbat morning) to the arrival of dawn and the general question of when Shabbat-morning Temple service begins. Timing was everything in the Beit HaMikdash.
