Menachot 99 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded
QUESTION 1 OF 5
The Gemara teaches that the broken tablets were kept alongside the whole ones in the Ark. What halachic/moral lesson does this teach?
A. Both sets of tablets were equally valid for reading the Ten Commandments
B. A Torah scholar who forgot his learning through no fault of his own retains his honor and dignity
C. The broken tablets were kept as a memorial to the sin of the Golden Calf
D. Moshe should have preserved both tablets since both were sacred
Correct: B. The broken tablets teach that even what is shattered retains its sanctity. Applied practically: a Torah scholar who has lost learning through illness or age — his "broken" scholarship is still honored, just as the shards remained in the Ark alongside the whole tablets.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Which Psalm do the Levi'im sing on Friday?
A. Psalm 93 — "The Lord reigns"
B. Psalm 92 — "A song for the Shabbat day"
C. Psalm 81 — "Sing joyfully to God"
D. Psalm 94 — "God of vengeance"
Correct: A — Psalm 93. On Friday the Levi'im sing Psalm 93 ("The Lord reigns, He is clothed in majesty") — corresponding to the sixth day when God completed creation and prepared His kingdom. Psalm 92 is reserved for Shabbat itself.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When Yom Kippur falls on Shabbat, when can the old Shewbread be distributed to the Kohanim?
A. It is discarded since it stayed on too long
B. Saturday night after the fast ends
C. The following Sunday morning
D. It stays another full week
Correct: B. Since the Levonah cannot be burned on Yom Kippur, the bread stays on the Table until Saturday night (after the fast). It remains miraculously fresh — the miracle extends beyond the normal 7 days to accommodate this timing.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The Mishna describes priests who ate the Yom Kippur goat raw when YK fell on Friday. Who were these priests really?
A. Babylonian exiles who came to serve in the Temple
B. Alexandrian priests — called "Babylonians" as a term of disparagement by those in Eretz Yisrael
C. Kohanim who converted from the tribe of Levi
D. The Kohen Gadol's personal assistants
Correct: B. Both R' Yochanan (citing a Baraita) and R' Yosei confirm: these were Alexandrian (Egyptian) priests, not Babylonian. The people of Eretz Yisrael called them "Babylonians" because they disliked Babylonians generally — a term of disparagement.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If a priest arranged both bread AND bowls of frankincense AFTER Shabbat (instead of on Shabbat), and then burned the frankincense on the next Shabbat — is this valid?
A. Yes — as long as the bowls were eventually burned on a Shabbat
B. No — the bread must be set on Shabbat specifically
C. Yes, if he waits an extra Shabbat (leaves it two weeks) to make up for the late start
D. No — but he can leave it on the Table until the following Shabbat and then burn the frankincense validly
Correct: D. The Mishna teaches: if bread and bowls were set after Shabbat, don't remove them on the next Shabbat. Leave them on the Table until the following Shabbat (two weeks total from setting). Leaving bread on the Table for extra days does NOT invalidate it — the Levonah burning on a proper Shabbat will then be valid.
