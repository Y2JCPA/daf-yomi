🍳 Machavat vs. Marcheshet — What's the Difference?

Machavat (מַחֲבַת)

  • 🍳 Flat pan — no cover
  • 🫓 Product is hard/firm (Kasheh)
  • 🔥 Cooked on an open, flat surface
  • 💡 Less oil; the dough firms up
  • 📖 "All that is made on (al) the Machavat"

Marcheshet (מַרְחֶשֶׁת)

  • 🫕 Deep pot — has a cover
  • 💧 Product is soft/trembling (Rocheshet)
  • 🔥 Deep-fried; the oil is deep
  • 💡 More oil; the dough stays soft and "trembles"
  • 📖 "All that is made in (b') the Marcheshet"
R' Chanina ben Gamliel's reason: Marcheshet is deep; Machavat is flat. The Gemara derives this from the prepositions: "in the Marcheshet" (deep, things go into it) vs. "on the Machavat" (flat, things sit on it).
וְכָל מַעֲשֵׂה מַרְחֶשֶׁת וְכָל נַעֲשָׂה עַל מַחֲבַת

🤔 But Why These Names?

R' Yosei HaGelili's approach — the names hint at what the offering atones for
Initial theory: "Marcheshet" → Rocheshet (trembling/murmuring)
= atones for secret thoughts of the heart? 🤫
↓
Problem: You could flip it! "Machavat" could also mean "hidden" (mechubeh) — atoning for secret musings. The names don't clearly point one way.
↓
Resolution: Focus on the prepositions instead: "in" (deep) vs. "on" (flat). The vessel shape is what matters — not a metaphor about what the offering atones for.
Practical rule: If you vow a Machavat offering, you cannot bring a Marcheshet offering instead (and vice versa). Each is its own distinct type of Mincha.

⚔️ Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel

Are "Machavat" and "Marcheshet" vessel names or product names?

Beit Shammai — Uncertain!

  • 🤷 We're not sure if the names refer to the vessel or the product
  • 📋 If you vow a "Marcheshet" → bring BOTH types (one of each) to cover both possibilities
  • 💰 Must bring TWO offerings to fulfill the neder

Beit Hillel — Certain!

  • ✅ There WAS a specific vessel in the Temple called "Marcheshet"
  • 🫕 It was a deep pot (like a kelabus) with indentations
  • 🍳 And another vessel called "Machavat" — flat
  • 📋 One offering suffices — use the correct vessel
Halacha follows Beit Hillel: Machavat and Marcheshet are specific vessels. One offering in the correct vessel fulfills the vow.

🔥 What Counts as an "Oven"?

If you vow "a meal offering baked in an oven" — which ovens qualify?
Baking Method Valid for "Oven" Neder? Reason
Standard Temple oven (Tanur) ✓ This is THE oven
Small portable oven (Kupach) ✗ "Oven" — not a Kupach
Roofing tiles ✗ "Oven" — not tiles
Arab desert pits (Yamai shel Araviyim) ✗ "Oven" — not a pit
Source: "A meal offering baked in an oven" (Vayikra 2:4) — the word "oven" appears twice in the Torah, which through a hermeneutical principle excludes all alternative baking methods.

🫓 Loaves vs. Wafers — Can You Mix?

If you vow "a baked meal offering" without specifying — loaves, wafers, or both?

Tanna Kamma / R' Yehuda

  • 📖 The verse says "Korban" (offering) — singular
  • → Bring ALL of one type: 10 loaves OR 10 wafers
  • ❌ Cannot bring 5 loaves + 5 wafers
  • 💡 "Korban" = one unified offering

R' Shimon

  • 📖 The verse uses "Mincha" twice — two types mentioned
  • → You MAY mix: 10 loaves, 10 wafers, or any combination
  • ✅ 5 loaves + 5 wafers is permitted
  • 💡 Both types are one "category" of baked offering
What are the two types?
🫓 Challot — thick loaves mixed with oil
🫓 Rekikin — thin wafers smeared with oil
Both are baked in an oven, but the preparation differs.

📖 The Verse Reading — Korban vs. Mincha

R"Y
The verse says: "And when you bring a Korban Mincha baked in an oven" — "Korban" means one thing. You can't split it into two types!
R"S
🤔 But the verse continues: "Challot... and Rekikin..." — the Torah itself describes TWO forms! If they were truly one, why mention both?
R"Y
The verse lists them as options — bring one OR the other. "Korban" forces you to pick one type for the entire offering.
R"S
If you want 10 loaves — fine. 10 wafers — fine. 5 and 5 — also fine! They're all "Minchat Ma'afeh Tanur" — one category, two forms. ✅
R' Shimon's additional point: The oven doesn't consecrate the offering. Even if baked outside the Temple grounds, the offering is valid after the fact (be'dieved), as long as it's eventually brought to the Mizbeach.
