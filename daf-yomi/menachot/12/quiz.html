1. If a priest removes the Kometz with intent to eat the Kometz itself (not the Shirayim) the next day, what is the result?

A) Pigul ‚Äî it's still Chutz li'Zmano intent, which always creates Pigul
B) Pasul, no Karet ‚Äî eating the Kometz is abnormal; only normal-item intents create Pigul
C) Valid ‚Äî abnormal intents have no legal effect whatsoever
D) Pigul with double Karet ‚Äî eating sacred food with improper intent
The Mishnah rules that intent regarding items not normally eaten/burned does not create Pigul ‚Äî only Pasul. The Kometz is normally burned on the altar, not eaten. Therefore, intent to eat the Kometz "tomorrow" = Pasul, no Karet. Pigul only applies when the intent involves an item's normal use.

2. Can half a k'Zayis intent to eat tomorrow plus half a k'Zayis intent to burn tomorrow combine to create Pigul?

A) Yes ‚Äî both intents are Chutz li'Zmano and they join together
B) No ‚Äî eating and burning are different acts and never combine for Pigul
C) Only if they occur in the same Avodah (Kemitzah)
D) Yes, but only if both amounts are of normally consumed/burned items
Eating and burning intents never combine for Pigul, even if both are Chutz li'Zmano and both involve normally consumed/burned items. Only same-type intents combine: eating + eating (for normally eaten items) or burning + burning. The Mishnah explicitly teaches that eating + burning = Pasul, no Karet.

3. What is the condition for two half-k'Zayis intents to combine and create Pigul?

A) Both must be Chutz li'Mkomo (outside designated area)
B) One must be Chutz li'Zmano and one Chutz li'Mkomo
C) Both must be the same type (eat+eat or burn+burn) AND both Chutz li'Zmano
D) They can always combine as long as they're from different Avodos
Two half-k'Zayis intents combine to create Pigul only when they meet two conditions: (1) they must be the same type ‚Äî both eating normally-eaten items or both burning normally-burned items, and (2) both must be Chutz li'Zmano (not mixed with Chutz li'Mkomo). Any other combination = Pasul, no Karet.

4. According to Rava, why does Haktarah of a Chaser Kometz create Pigul for the deficient Shirayim?

A) A Chaser Minchah becomes Kosher once the Kometz is removed
B) The deficient Shirayim are still physically present in the Azarah, unlike Yotzei which left
C) R. Akiva's opinion about Zerikah applies equally to Haktarah
D) Deficiency in the Shirayim is irrelevant after Kemitzah
Rava's reasoning is that even R. Eliezer (who says Zerikah doesn't help Yotzei) agrees Haktarah helps Chaser. The critical distinction: Yotzei has physically left the Azarah; Chaser is still present inside the Azarah. Physical presence makes the Haktarah effective for those Shirayim.

5. What does the new Perek (Pe'rek Ha'Kometz et Ha'Minchah) establish about the conditions for effective Pigul intent?

A) Any intent to consume or burn the offering outside its time creates Pigul
B) Only Chutz li'Zmano intent for items in their normal mode (normally eaten or burned) creates Pigul
C) Only the Haktarah Avodah can create Pigul ‚Äî other Avodos are irrelevant
D) Intent must involve the entire Kometz, not just a partial amount
The Perek establishes that effective Pigul intent requires: (1) the intent is Chutz li'Zmano (not Chutz li'Mkomo), and (2) the intent concerns items in their normal mode ‚Äî eating normally-eaten items (Shirayim) or burning normally-burned items (Kometz). Intent to eat what's burned or burn what's eaten = Pasul only, no Karet.
