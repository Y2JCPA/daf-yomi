What does the term 'it' teach regarding the sin offering of a king?
A. The animal must be in the north, but the slaughterer need not be in the north
B. Only a king may bring this offering
C. The offering must be slaughtered at dawn
D. The animal must face Jerusalem
Correct: A. The Gemara concludes that 'it' teaches the animal must be positioned in the north of the Temple courtyard, but the person performing the slaughter does not personally need to stand in the north.

How does the Gemara prove liability for castrating an animal that was already castrated?
A. From the verse listing 'bruised, crushed, detached, or cut'
