QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Rabbi Meir and the Rabbis about blemishing a consecrated animal?
A. Whether the prohibition applies to all animals or only kosher species
B. Whether the prohibition applies to already-blemished animals or only currently unblemished ones
C. Whether the punishment is lashes or karet
D. Whether indirect blemishing is prohibited
Correct: B. Rabbi Meir derives from "no blemish in it" that the prohibition is comprehensive and includes even blemishing an already-blemished animal. The Rabbis derive from "perfect to be accepted" that the prohibition only applies to animals currently fit for the altar.
