Menachot 105 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded
QUESTION 1 OF 5
If someone says "I am obligated in Minchot" (plural) — what is the minimum they must bring?
A. One Mincha — plural is just formal language
B. Two Minchot — the minimum plural
C. Five Minchot — one of each type
D. Ten — "Minchot" implies many
Correct: B — Two. The minimum plural is two. "Minchot" creates an obligation for at least two Mincha offerings. Each must be brought separately with its own vessel and Kemitzah.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Someone vowed a Mincha and specified a type, but now can't remember which type. What must they bring?
A. Nothing — if they can't remember, the vow is void
B. The simplest type (Solet)
C. All five types of Mincha — to cover every possibility
D. Ask a Beit Din to release the vow
Correct: C. When in doubt about which type was vowed, the person must bring all five types to cover every possibility. Failing to fulfill a vow is a Torah prohibition, so we err on the strict side.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the difference between "Harei Alai" (upon me) and "Harei Zu" (this is) when making a vow?
A. "Harei Alai" creates a personal obligation (must replace if lost); "Harei Zu" consecrates a specific object (no replacement if lost)
B. They mean the same thing
C. "Harei Zu" is more serious than "Harei Alai"
D. "Harei Alai" is only for animal offerings; "Harei Zu" is only for meal offerings
Correct: A. "Harei Alai" (upon me) creates a personal obligation that attaches to the person — if the consecrated item is lost or destroyed, they must bring a replacement. "Harei Zu" (this is) consecrates a specific object — if that object is lost, the obligation disappears with it.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The Gemara teaches that for animal, bird, AND flour offerings, the Torah uses the same phrase "a fire-offering, a pleasing aroma." What does this teach?
A. All offerings must be burned on the same altar
B. Whether one gives much or little, what matters is directing one's heart toward Heaven
C. Flour offerings are equivalent in cost to animal offerings
D. God prefers flour offerings over animal offerings
Correct: B. This is one of the most famous teachings in Menachot: "Whether one gives much or little — what matters is that the heart is directed toward Heaven." The identical phrase for bull, bird, and flour shows God values sincere intent equally, regardless of the offering's monetary worth.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Can two separate Mincha vows be combined into a single vessel and offered together?
A. Yes — as long as they're the same type
B. Yes — to save time and resources
C. No — each vow is a separate obligation requiring its own vessel and Kemitzah
D. Only if they were vowed on the same day
Correct: C. Each Mincha vow is an independent obligation. Even if both are the same type, they cannot be combined. Each requires its own vessel, its own mixing with oil, and its own Kemitzah. Two separate vows = two separate offerings.
