🛢️ When Is Oil Mixed — Before or After Baking?

Chachamim

  • Oil is mixed with the flour before baking
  • Verse: "Fine flour mixed with oil" → it is mixed while flour
  • Proof: thanks-offering loaves — impossible to mix oil into baked loaves
  • Only a quarter-log of oil per offering — too little to distribute across baked loaves

Rabbi (Yehuda HaNasi)

  • Oil is mixed with loaves after baking
  • Verse: "Loaves of fine flour mixed with oil" → mixed as loaves
  • The breaking into pieces happens after baking, then oil is applied
Chachamim's decisive proof: With only a quarter-log of oil available, how could you distribute it meaningfully among multiple baked loaves? It must be mixed while still flour, where the oil can be properly incorporated.

✂️ Breaking Meal Offerings — The Mishna

Verse: "You shall break it in pieces" (Vayikra 2:6) — how many breaks?
Offering TypeFoldingSeparated?Why?
🇮🇱 Israelite's minchahFold 1→2, then 2→4✅ Yes, separatedHandful must be removed — need distinct pieces
🧑‍⚕️ Kohen's minchahFold 1→2, then 2→4❌ Not separatedNo handful removed — pieces stay connected
👑 High Priest's griddle-cakeNo folding at all❌ N/ARabba: only folded into 2 (not 4) since verse not doubled
R' Shimon's rule: Any meal offering in which no handful is removed → no breaking into pieces. The breaking exists to facilitate the removal of the handful.

All agree: When pieces are made, they must be olive-bulk sized — not crumbs.

📏 "Break It" — But Not Into Crumbs

How much to break the meal offering pieces?
↓
"Break it in pieces" → must be broken more than once
↓
"Break it" → but not its pieces — don't break the pieces further
↓
Result: Break into pieces, but each piece must remain at least olive-bulk sized
(k'zayit — the standard halachic measure for eating)
The Torah's balance: "Pieces" means genuinely broken — not just scored. "It" means stop before you turn them into crumbs. The halacha requires real pieces of a meaningful size.

🙏 Rav Yosef's Ruling on the Hamotzi Blessing

Rav Yosef derives from our Mishna: All meal offerings that are broken are broken into olive-bulk pieces — and the Kohen says "Hamotzi" (Who brings forth bread) before eating them.
Kohen stands up to eat his meal offering portion in Jerusalem → first time → recites Shehecheyanu
↓
Kohen takes the pieces to eat → recites הַמּוֹצִיא לֶחֶם מִן הָאָרֶץ
↓
Rav Yosef's principle:
Pieces of bread ≥ olive-bulk → Hamotzi
Pieces of bread < olive-bulk → Mezonot only

💧 Deep Pan vs. Shallow Pan — Oil Requirements

RequirementDeep Pan (Marcheshet)Shallow Pan (Machavas)
Oil placed in vessel first?✅ Yes✅ Yes (derived from deep pan)
Pouring of oil required?✅ Yes (derived from shallow pan)✅ Yes (verse says "pour oil")
Mixing required?✅ Yes (derived from shallow pan)✅ Yes (verse says "mixed with oil")
Texture of resultSoft (deep-fried in oil)Hard (shallow-fried)
The Gezerah Shavah between pan types: Each pan teaches the other. The shallow pan teaches pouring and mixing to the deep pan. The deep pan (where oil is placed first in an empty vessel) teaches the shallow pan the same requirement.
