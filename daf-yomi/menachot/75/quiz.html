QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Chachamim, at what stage is oil mixed into a meal offering?
A. After the loaves are baked and broken into pieces
B. While the offering is still flour — before baking
C. After the handful is removed for the altar
D. During the kneading stage only, not at any other time
Correct: B. The Chachamim hold that "fine flour mixed with oil" means the oil is mixed while the offering is still flour, before baking. Their decisive proof: only a quarter-log of oil is used — far too little to distribute meaningfully among multiple baked loaves. Therefore it must be mixed at the flour stage.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rabbi (Yehuda HaNasi) differ from the Chachamim regarding oil mixing?
A. Rabbi says no oil is mixed at all — it's poured on top after the handful
B. Rabbi says oil is mixed with the loaves after baking, based on "loaves of fine flour mixed with oil"
C. Rabbi requires double the amount of oil compared to Chachamim
D. Rabbi holds mixing applies only to the deep-pan offering, not oven-baked
Correct: B. Rabbi derives from the verse "loaves of fine flour mixed with oil" (Vayikra 2:4) — since it says "loaves," the mixing happens after baking when the offering has already become loaves. He holds the priest breaks the baked loaves into pieces and then applies and mixes the oil.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Mishna, what is the key difference between breaking an Israelite's meal offering and a Kohen's meal offering?
A. The Israelite's offering is broken into 8 pieces; the Kohen's into 4
B. The Israelite's is folded and the pieces are separated; the Kohen's is folded but pieces are not separated
C. The Kohen's offering is not broken at all
D. The Israelite's offering must be broken by a Kohen; the Kohen breaks his own
Correct: B. Both are folded 1→2→4. However, the Israelite's offering is then separated at the folds (creating distinct pieces from which a handful can be removed). The Kohen's offering is folded but NOT separated — since no handful is removed from it, there is no need for distinct separated pieces.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Rav Yosef derive from our Mishna about the Hamotzi blessing?
A. Kohanim never recite Hamotzi over Temple offerings
B. Over pieces of bread that are at least olive-bulk sized, one recites Hamotzi; smaller pieces require only Mezonot
C. The Hamotzi blessing can only be recited in the Temple courtyard
D. One must recite Shehecheyanu before Hamotzi on all bread
Correct: B. Since our Mishna states that pieces must be olive-bulk sized, and since the Kohen recited Hamotzi before eating his meal offering pieces, Rav Yosef derives: olive-bulk pieces of bread trigger the Hamotzi blessing. Smaller pieces (less than an olive-bulk) would only require the Mezonot blessing.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does R' Shimon say about which meal offerings require breaking into pieces?
A. All meal offerings must be broken, without exception
B. Only meal offerings brought by Kohanim require breaking
C. Only meal offerings from which a handful is removed require breaking into pieces
D. No meal offerings require breaking — it is optional
Correct: C. R' Shimon rules: the breaking into pieces exists to facilitate the removal of the handful. Therefore, any meal offering from which no handful is removed (such as the Kohen's and High Priest's offerings, which go entirely to the altar) has no requirement for breaking into pieces.
