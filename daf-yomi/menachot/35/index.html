Menachot 35

Blessing Between Arm & Head Tefillin, Night-Time Tefillin & Shabbat
2 / 7

Speaking Between Arm & Head Tefillin

Rav Chisda: If one spoke between donning the arm and head tefillin, he must recite a new blessing on the head tefillin.

If he did not speak, one blessing covers both.

Abaye & Rava clarify: Did not speak → 1 blessing. Spoke → 2 blessings (the original + a new one).

Tefillin at Night?

R. Yosi HaGelili

  • Night is NOT a time for tefillin
  • Shabbat/Yom Tov is NOT a time for tefillin
  • Source: "From days to days" — only weekdays, only daytime

R. Akiva

  • Night IS a valid time for tefillin
  • But one might fall asleep and not guard them
  • Therefore: permitted but discouraged
4 / 7

Tefillin on Shabbat?

Core dispute: Is Shabbat a "time for tefillin"?

The verse says tefillin are an "אוֹת" (sign). Shabbat is also an "אוֹת" (sign). If Shabbat is already a sign, tefillin are redundant → exempt on Shabbat.

This is the accepted halakha: no tefillin on Shabbat or Yom Tov.
5 / 7

Rav Chisda & Rabba bar Rav Huna

Rav Chisda and Rabba bar Rav Huna would pray the evening prayer with tefillin still on — following the view that night IS a valid time.

But Rabba bar Rav Huna also says: if it's uncertain whether it's nighttime, one should not don tefillin. Seeming contradiction!

Resolution: One who is already wearing tefillin need not remove them at dusk. But one should not put on new tefillin at dusk.
6 / 7

Tefillin's Sanctity

Key principle: Tefillin require a "clean body" (guf naki) — one must not pass gas while wearing them, and must constantly be mindful of them.

This is why the Sages were cautious about wearing tefillin at night (risk of falling asleep) and why the mitzva is primarily daytime.
7 / 7

Key Takeaways — Menachot 35

1. Speaking between arm and head tefillin requires a new blessing on the head.

2. Night tefillin: disputed. R. Yosi HaGelili says no; R. Akiva says permitted but discouraged.

3. No tefillin on Shabbat — Shabbat itself is already an "ot" (sign).

4. Already wearing tefillin at dusk: don't need to remove. But don't put on new ones.

5. Guf naki requirement — must be mindful and maintain bodily cleanliness while wearing tefillin.
