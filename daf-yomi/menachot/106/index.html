🫒 Surplus Oil — Where Does It Go?

Every Mincha requires oil mixed in and poured on top. What happens when there's leftover oil?
Oil is prescribed per offering — 1 log per Issaron.
What if the Kohen adds extra? Or if some remains unused?
↓
Surplus from the offering
Goes to Beit HaMokhed
(the Chamber of the Hearth)
Burned on the altar fire
✅ Has sanctity — cannot be wasted
Oil in the vessel after Kemitzah
Remains with the Shirayim
(the remainder eaten by Kohanim)
⚠️ The Kometz was taken from the TOP
Oil drains to the bottom → Kohanim get it
The practical issue: Oil is poured both into the flour and on top. When the Kohen takes the Kometz (handful), most of the oil stays in the vessel. The Talmud discusses whether this "extra oil" at the bottom was meant to be part of the Kometz or belongs to the Kohanim's portion.

📜 Vowing "Oil Mincha" — Valid Types

These CAN Have Extra Oil

  • 🌾 Minchat Solet (fine flour) — oil mixed in + poured on
  • 🍳 Machabat (griddle) — oil on the griddle + on top
  • 🫕 Marcheshet (deep pan) — soaked in oil
  • 🍞 Challot (baked loaves) — oil mixed in
  • 📌 All 5 types include oil as required

Oil Ratio: 1 Log per Issaron

  • 📏 Standard: 1 log of oil per Issaron of flour
  • 📏 Kohen Gadol's daily Mincha: uses same ratio
  • ⚠️ Adding more oil: does not disqualify
  • ⚠️ Adding less oil: does NOT disqualify either!
  • 💡 The measures are ideal, not minimum requirements

🔢 The Five Mincha Types — A Complete Review

TypeHebrewPreparationKey Feature
Fine FlourסֹלֶתRaw flour + oilNo cooking; Kemitzah from raw flour
Oven-baked Loavesמַאֲפֵה תַנּוּר חַלּוֹתBaked thick loavesOil mixed in before baking

Griddle vs. Deep-pan: These two are the most commonly confused. The Machabat (griddle) is flat, oil doesn't pool — producing a drier result. The Marcheshet (deep-pan) has walls, oil pools — producing a moist, soaked result. The tradition says: "If it crackles (rotsehevet) — it's a Marcheshet; if it doesn't — it's a Machabat."

🧮 "I Vow All Five Types" — How Are They Brought?

Q
If I somehow end up obligated to bring all five Mincha types — say, from a doubt vow — can I combine them in one go?
A
Each type requires its own offering process. The Solet needs Kemitzah from raw flour. The baked types need their own baking. The griddle and deep-pan need cooking. They cannot be combined.
Q
What's the maximum total Issaron one could be obligated for?
A
If someone forgot what they vowed AND said "Minchot" (plural) AND forgot the amount — they could theoretically owe 60 Issaron of each of all 5 types = 300 Issaron! The Gemara notes this is theoretically correct, even if extreme.

💡 Why "Oil on Top" Matters — The Halving Rule

For baked Minchot (loaves and wafers): the offering must be broken into pieces (Pitat) before the Kohen takes the Kometz. Why?
Whole loaves are baked → then broken into pieces (not crumbled)
↓
Oil is poured on the broken pieces → soaks in evenly
↓
Kohen takes Kometz from the broken pieces + oil
↓
Kometz burned; remainder eaten by Kohanim
The breaking ensures the oil is distributed throughout — not just on the surface
