🚫 Forbidden Sources for Meal Offering Grain

The Mishna: Even when selecting grain from approved locations, one may not bring grain from:
Forbidden SourceReasonIf Brought Anyway?
Fertilized field (dung-enriched)Grain quality is artificially enhanced, not naturally superior✅ Valid (b'dieved)
Irrigated fieldWatered artificially — not naturally ripened by rain✅ Valid (b'dieved)
Field of trees (shaded)Grain grown under tree shade is inferior quality✅ Valid (b'dieved)
The principle: L'chatchila (ideally), only the finest, naturally grown grain is used. B'dieved (after the fact), even suboptimal grain is valid.

☀️ Best Source: Southern Fields

R' Yochanan: One brings the Omer only from the southern fields of Eretz Yisrael
↓
Why southern? The sun rises and shines upon those fields all day — from sunrise to sunset. Maximum sunlight = superior grain quality.
↓
Abba Shaul confirms: The Omer would come from the valley of Beit Mikle
↓
That valley: ~3 se'a in size, a southern field. Sun rose upon it AND set from it. Maximum solar exposure.

🌱 The Plowing Regimen at Beit Mikle

The Mishna and Baraita on preparing the field for optimal grain:
Year 1: Plow entire field in the first half of the year → sow half the field, leave other half fallow
↓
Year 2: Plow entire field again in the first half → sow the other half (previously fallow)
↓
Mishna: plow (once implied). Baraita: plow AND plow again. Contradiction?
↓
Resolution: Mishna = cultivated field (already broken in, needs one plowing). Baraita = uncultivated field (harder soil, needs double plowing).

😄 "Are You Bringing Straw to Afarayim?"

The Gemara's aside: Afarayim (sometimes called Aforayim) was famous for producing the finest grain in Israel — so exceptional that it became the standard of comparison in folk sayings.

When Pharaoh's sorcerers Yochana and Mamre saw Moshe and Aharon performing signs in Egypt — the world's center of sorcery — they taunted:

"Are you bringing straw to Afarayim?!"

Meaning: You're bringing magic tricks to the masters of magic? That's like bringing straw to the grain capital of the world.

Moshe's reply: "As people say: To a city rich in herbs, take herbs — the best audience knows your work."

🔄 Cultivated vs. Uncultivated — The Plowing Question

Cultivated Field

  • Soil already broken and prepared
  • One plowing suffices in year 2
  • The Mishna's case
  • Easier to manage

Uncultivated Field

  • Hard, unworked soil
  • Must plow AND re-plow in year 2
  • The Baraita's case
  • Needs extra preparation
Resolution confirmed by a Baraita: Even in the second year, one plows in the first half before sowing — proving plowing is not skipped in year 2, as the Baraita explicitly requires it.
