⚔️ R' Yoshiya vs. R' Yonatan

How do we read "of the herd or of the flock" (Vayikra 1:2)?

R' Yoshiya

  • 📌 Default: items joined together ("and") unless stated otherwise
  • 📖 "Of the herd or of the flock" — the "or" is needed to separate them
  • 💡 Without "or," you'd need to bring BOTH an ox and a sheep
  • ⚡ Uses "or" to derive: one type is enough for a vow offering

R' Yonatan

  • 📌 Default: items already separate unless joined
  • 📖 "Or" is unnecessary for separation — uses it for a different drasha
  • 💡 Even an unspecified vow (stam neder) only requires ONE type of animal
  • ⚡ Derives this from the same "or" — it teaches sufficiency even without specification
Key Principle: Both agree you only need one type. They argue about which verse teaches it — because their default rules about conjunctive reading differ.

🤔 The Todah Paradox — Bread + Libations?

Q
Wait — Todah already comes with bread (40 loaves!). Why would it ALSO need libations? 🤔
A
Good question! That's exactly why the Torah needs a special word ("or") to include Todah. Without it, we'd assume bread exempts from libations.
Q
But the Nazir's ram also comes with bread AND requires libations! 🐑
A
True, but the Nazir has only 2 types of bread. Todah has 4 types — so the argument for exemption is stronger. Hence the extra verse. 💡

Nazir's Ram 🐏

  • 🍞 2 types of bread (loaves + wafers)
  • 🍷 Requires libations (explicit in Bamidbar 6)
  • ✅ No special derivation needed

Todah 🙏

  • 🍞 4 types of bread (chametz + 3 matzah)
  • 🍷 Requires libations (derived from "or")
  • ⚡ Needs special inclusion precisely because of the extra bread

🤕 The Leper's Offerings — Triple Inclusion

A leper (Metzora) brings 3 offerings. Each needs a separate verse to include it for libations.

Abaye's Approach

  • 📖 "The sacrifice" → Leper's Chatat
  • 📖 "The burnt offering" → Leper's Olah
  • 📖 "For the one lamb" → Yoledet's (woman after birth) Olah
  • 💡 Each inclusion is matched to a specific offering type

Rava's Approach

  • 📖 All three inclusions refer to the Leper's 3 offerings
  • 💡 "What group has exactly 3 offerings needing 3 inclusions?"
  • 🤕 Only the Metzora: Chatat + Olah + Asham
  • ⚡ Elegant: one sugya, one answer — the Metzora
Why is the Metzora special? Normally, Chatat and Asham don't get libations (they're sin-related). But the Metzora's guilt offering serves to render him fit (not just atone), so it needs special inclusion for Nesachim.
