Red Heifer Ashes: Me'ila or Not?

Rav Ashi explains a historical back-and-forth about misuse of consecrated property (me'ila) with regard to the red heifer's ashes:

By Torah Law:
The animal itself → me'ila applies
Its ashes → me'ila does not apply
↓
1st Ordinance: People were treating ashes disrespectfully, making salves for wounds → Sages decreed me'ila on ashes
↓
Problem: People stopped sprinkling in cases of uncertain impurity (fear of me'ila)
↓
2nd Ordinance: Reverted to Torah law — no me'ila on ashes

Bull of Unwitting Sin & Goats of Idol Worship

When the Sanhedrin issues an erroneous ruling and the community sins — who pays for the sin offering?

One Baraita

  • R. Yehuda: New collection
  • R. Shimon: From the chamber

Another Baraita

  • Opinions are reversed!
  • Which is the later, authoritative version?
Resolution (Rabba Zuti): A third baraita explicitly states R. Shimon holds these come from the chamber, deriving it from Numbers 28:2 — "My food that is presented to Me... you shall observe to sacrifice to Me in its due season."

R. Shimon and Negligence

Sages
We know R. Shimon is concerned about negligence (from the chavitin discussion). So he should require a new collection to ensure the offerings get funded!
R.A.
Rav Ashi disagrees: R. Shimon worries about negligence only for things that don't provide atonement (like a deceased KG's offering). For communal sin offerings that do provide atonement, people won't be negligent.

R. Yochanan's Dilemma: What Happens Twice Daily?

When the KG dies and no replacement is appointed — is a complete tenth brought morning and afternoon, or only in the morning?

Rava
Proof from Tamid 31b: the 8th priest always carries the chavitin. If it were canceled in the afternoon, sometimes there'd be no 8th priest!
R.Y.
R. Yirmeya rejects: "Foolish Babylonians, dwelling in a dark land, making dark halakhot!" The Mishna doesn't teach hypothetical cases.
Rava
Rava then brings a better proof from Lev. 6:13 — "סֹלֶת מִנְחָה תָּמִיד" — it is like the temidin (daily offerings), requiring both morning and afternoon.
R.N.
Definitive answer: A baraita explicitly states — complete tenth in the morning AND complete tenth in the afternoon.

How Much Frankincense?

Abba Yosei ben Dostai

  • ✌️ Two handfuls per day
  • One handful morning, one afternoon
  • Logic: We never find half a handful being sacrificed

The Rabbis

  • ☝️ One handful per day
  • Half morning, half afternoon
  • Logic: We never find a tenth of an ephah requiring two handfuls
Halakha: R. Yochanan rules in accordance with Abba Yosei ben Dostai — two handfuls.

Doubled or Not? Oil & Frankincense When KG Dies

The flour is doubled (complete tenth morning + afternoon). But what about frankincense and oil?

ComponentNormal KGAfter KG Dies (No Replacement)
Fine Flour 1 tenth, split half/half Complete tenth morning + complete tenth afternoon (doubled)
Frankincense Abba Yosei: 2 handfuls
Rabbis: 1 handful		 2 handfuls (1 morning, 1 afternoon) — NOT doubled
Oil 3 log (1.5 + 1.5) 3 log (1.5 + 1.5) — NOT doubled
Principle: What the Torah explicitly doubled (flour), it doubled. What it did not reveal as doubled (oil, frankincense), is not doubled.

Key Takeaways — Menachot 52

1. Red heifer ashes went through two ordinances: me'ila added (abuse prevention) then removed (people feared uncertain sprinkling).

2. Communal sin offerings: R. Shimon says from the chamber; R. Yehuda says new collection.

3. When the KG dies: complete tenth morning AND afternoon — proven from "סֹלֶת מִנְחָה תָּמִיד."

4. Frankincense: Abba Yosei says 2 handfuls daily (halakha follows him). Rabbis say 1 handful split.

5. Oil and frankincense are NOT doubled when KG dies — only flour is doubled.

6. New Mishna begins: All meal offerings come as matza except leavened bread of the todah and the two loaves of Shavuot.
