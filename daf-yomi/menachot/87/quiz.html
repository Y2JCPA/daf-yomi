QUESTION 1 OF 5
Which portion of a wine cask is used for Temple libations?
A. The top third — the freshest, most fragrant wine
B. The middle third — clear, settled, free of scum and sediment
C. The bottom third — the richest, most concentrated wine
D. The entire cask is used without distinction
Correct: B. The Mishna requires wine from the middle third of the cask. The top has chalk-like scum (hagir) and the bottom has sediment — both are disqualified. The clear middle is ideal for libations.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Temple treasurer know when to stop drawing wine from the top of the cask?
A. He counts the seconds after opening the spigot
B. He smells the wine and stops when the aroma changes
C. He watches for chalk-like scum to emerge, then knocks with his reed to signal closing the spigot
D. He uses a color chart to identify when the wine is clear enough
Correct: C. The treasurer sits with a measuring reed. When the spigot is opened and chalk-like scum (hagir) begins to flow, he knocks with the reed — a signal to close the spigot. He waits, then reopens to collect the clear middle-third wine.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
R' Yosei b"R Yehuda says scummy wine is unfit even b'dieved. What verse does he derive this from?
A. "Holy of holies" — applying the highest standard to all offerings
B. "Unblemished they shall be for you" — applied equally to animals, meal offerings, AND libations (Bamidbar 28:19–31)
C. "You shall bring the best of the first fruits" — a general rule of excellence
D. A tradition from Sinai, not a specific verse
Correct: B. The verse "unblemished they shall be for you" appears in the context of animal offerings, then meal offerings, then libations (Bamidbar 28:19, 20, 31). R' Yosei b"R Yehuda reads this as requiring flawless quality for all three — including wine, which therefore cannot have flour-like scum.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does the term "pounded" (katit) mean in the context of olive oil production?
A. Oil extracted using a millstone grinder
B. Oil from olives crushed in a mortar (not a millstone) — the first press from that crushing
C. Oil that has been heated and clarified
D. Oil combined with fragrant spices in a mixing vessel
Correct: B. R' Yehuda explains: "pounded" means the olives were crushed using a mortar — not ground with a millstone. The first oil that emerges from this mortar-crushing is considered "pounded oil" and is of the highest grade.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Ravina and Rav Ashi disagree about an apparent contradiction in the Mishna regarding sweet wine. What are their two approaches?
A. Ravina says the Mishna follows R' Yehuda; Rav Ashi says it follows R' Meir
B. Ravina says the Mishna text is corrupt and all sweet wines are invalid; Rav Ashi says the text is correct and two types of sweet wine have different rulings
C. Ravina says sweet wine is valid only for peace offerings; Rav Ashi says only for burnt offerings
D. Both agree the text is corrupt but disagree on how to fix it
Correct: B. Ravina believes the Mishna's text was corrupted — he would combine both clauses and rule all listed wines invalid. Rav Ashi defends the text as accurate: there are two distinct varieties of sweet wine, one of which is valid b'dieved and the other which is never valid.
