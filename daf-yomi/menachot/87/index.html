🪣 The Treasurer's Inspection — Step by Step

1
Treasurer arrives at the wine cask with a measuring reed in hand
2
The spigot is opened — wine begins to flow from the cask
3
Treasurer watches: if chalk-like scum [hagir] comes out first → that's the top layer. Knock with the reed → close the spigot.
4
Once clear wine flows → this is the middle third. Collect this for libations.
5
Stop before reaching the bottom sediment. Never use the top scum or bottom dregs.

📏 The Three Zones of a Wine Cask

🫧
TOP — Scum / Hagir
Flour-like residue, disqualified. Treasurer stops here.
🍷
MIDDLE — Clear Wine ✅
Pure, settled, no scum/sediment. This is collected for libations.
💩
BOTTOM — Sediment / Dregs
Murky dregs. Never used for sacred libations.

⚗️ "Refined" vs. "Pounded" — What's the Difference?

Term
Meaning
Application
Refined (זך)
Zach
Clean, free-flowing oil — flows naturally with no pressure applied
✅ Menorah only (required)
Pounded (כָּתִית)
Katit
Olives crushed in a mortar (not a millstone) — first-press from crushing
✅ Menorah + Meal offerings
R' Yehuda's reading
"Pounded" = specifically mortar-crushed, not millstone-ground
Technical distinction for Grade 1
One might think: since "refined pounded oil" is specified for the Menorah, it's therefore excluded from meal offerings. The verse "pounded oil" in Shemot 29:40 (in the context of the daily offering) proves otherwise — pounded oil IS valid for meal offerings too.

❓ Ravina vs. Rav Ashi — A Textual Puzzle

The Contradiction:
The Mishna seems to say two opposite things — one clause says sweet wine (sun-dried) is valid if brought; another says it's invalid.
Ravina's solution:
The text is corrupt. Combine the two clauses — ALL the listed wines are invalid even b'dieved. The "valid" ruling was a scribal error.
Rav Ashi's solution:
The text is correct! The two clauses describe different types of sweet wine — one variety is valid b'dieved, another type is never valid. The Mishna distinguishes between them.

📋 Menachot 87 — Summary

The Wine Rule: Only clear, properly settled wine from the middle third of the cask is valid for libations. Scum on top and sediment on bottom are disqualified.
The Inspector: The Temple treasurer sits with a reed. Opens the spigot, watches for chalk-like scum. Knocks to close when it appears, reopens to collect the clear middle portion.
Oil terms: "Refined" (zach) = free-flowing, no pressure. "Pounded" (katit) = mortar-crushed first press. Both valid for the Menorah. Meal offerings may also use pounded oil per Shemot 29:40.
