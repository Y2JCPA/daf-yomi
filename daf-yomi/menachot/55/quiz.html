Menachot 55 — Quiz

1. Why can't R. Elazar b. R. Yosei's dried figs example prove the "as they were" position?

✅ b) Rav Dimi (from Eretz Yisrael) explains: dried figs are unique because one can boil them and restore them to their original fresh size. This makes them a special case that can't prove the general principle.

2. What is Rav Pappa's interpretive principle stated in this daf?

✅ b) Rav Pappa says: "We exert ourselves and interpret a mishna according to two reasons (scenarios) under one Tanna rather than attributing it to two different Tannaim." Single authorship is always preferred.

3. The Mishna says one is liable for leavening a meal offering. For how many actions separately?

✅ c) One is liable for each stage separately: kneading, shaping, baking, and smoothing — each is an independent lav, derived by comparing each action to baking (which emerged from the general prohibition).

4. What does R. Aptoriki say about a klal u'frat that are far apart in the Torah?

✅ c) R. Aptoriki rules that a generalization and a detail that appear far apart in the Torah cannot be analyzed using the klal u'frat hermeneutic — they are simply two separate teachings.

5. According to Rav Ashi, why is the sin offering/burnt offering placement NOT a case of klal u'frat (general and specific)?

✅ b) Rav Ashi points out the verse about the king's sin offering (Lev. 4:24) comes BEFORE the general verse about all sin offerings (Lev. 6:18) — making it a frat u'klal (specific then general), where the general broadens the specific to include all sin offerings.
