Mishna recap: The thanks offering (Korban Todah) comes with 40 loaves — 10 leavened and 30 matza (3 types × 10 each). This daf explains the flour quantities behind those 40 loaves.

📐 Why 3 Se'a = 1 Ephah?

From where do we know 1 ephah = 3 se'a?
↓
Rav Chisda: "The ephah and the bat shall be of one measure" (Yechezkel 45:11)
↓
The bat (liquid measure) = 3 se'a → therefore the ephah (dry measure) = 3 se'a
↓
Jerusalem se'a > wilderness se'a. Jerusalem measure: 5 se'a = 6 wilderness se'a.

📖 How Many Tenths for Leavened Bread?

Source for 10 tenths of leavened bread?
↓
Rav Yitzchak bar Avdimi: the word תִּהְיֶינָה (tihyena — "they shall be") is spelled with two yods
↓
Extra yod (= 10) → leavened loaves must be made from 10 tenths of flour
↓
But maybe it means 10 half-kav instead of 10 tenths?
↓
Rava: the verse speaks in the language of tenths throughout — not other measures

📏 Where is 10 Tenths for Matza Derived?

Why does the matza also need 10 tenths of flour?
↓
Verse says "with cakes of leavened bread" — linking matza to leavened bread
↓
Matza must correspond to leavened bread in quantity → also 10 tenths
↓
But isn't this a circular derivation? (A derived from B, B derived from A?)
↓
No — the 10 tenths for leavened bread is also derived independently "from itself," not solely from the Two Loaves

📋 Menachot 77 — Summary Map

Central Theme: Calculating the flour quantities for the thanks offering's 40 loaves
↓
5 Jerusalem se'a = 2 ephahs. 1 ephah leavened + 1 ephah matza = 10 tenths each.
30 kav total: 15 for leavened, 15 for matza (5 per type). Each matza type = 2 loaves/kav.
↓
🎓 Key Takeaway: Every flour quantity is precisely derived — each of the 40 loaves has an exact share of the total flour, down to the fraction of a tenth.
