מנחות ל״ח

Menachot 38

Tzitzit Knots, Severed Strings, Windings & Loose Ends
2 / 7

The Uppermost Knot

Rabba: The uppermost knot of the tzitzit (where the strings meet the garment) is required by Torah law.

Proof: If it were only rabbinic, you couldn't wear tzitzit in a cemetery (competing with the mitzva obligation of the dead, who can't fulfill mitzvot). Since we CAN wear tzitzit in a cemetery, the knot must be Torah-level.
3 / 7

Severed Strings

Rav Adda / Rav: If a string was severed at its base (where it connects to the garment), the tzitzit are unfit.

Rav Nachman: If severed above the knot (in the hanging portion), the tzitzit remain fit — as long as enough string remains to tie a small knot.
4 / 7

Windings vs. Loose Strings

Rabba bar bar Chana

  • Once had tzitzit that were entirely windings — no loose strings
  • Showed them to Rav

Rav

  • "The cloak is beautiful but the tzitzit are unfit"
  • Must have BOTH windings AND loose strings
  • "Twisted cords" (gedilim) = windings; "strings" (petilim) = loose ends
5 / 7

How Much Winding, How Much Loose?

Ratio: One-third windings, two-thirds loose strings.

The windings should be near the garment (top), with the loose strings hanging below. The Gemara derives this from the requirement of both "gedilim" (twisted) and "petilim" (strings).
6 / 7

Knots Between Sets of Windings

The sons of R. Chiyya had tzitzit with knots between each set of windings. The Gemara initially tries to prove from this that knots between windings are required, but rejects the proof — they may have simply been following a custom, not a requirement.
7 / 7

Key Takeaways — Menachot 38

1. Uppermost knot is Torah law — proven from the cemetery argument.

2. String severed at the base: unfit. Severed above the knot: fit if enough remains for a small knot.

3. Must have both windings AND loose strings — entirely windings is invalid.

4. Ratio: 1/3 windings, 2/3 loose strings.

5. Knots between sets of windings: practiced but not conclusively proven as required.
