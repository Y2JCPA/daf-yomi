🌾 Chadash — The New Crop Prohibition

Torah prohibition: וְלֶחֶם וְקָלִי וְכַרְמֶל לֹא תֹאכְלוּ עַד עֶצֶם הַיּוֹם הַזֶּה

"You shall not eat bread, parched grain, or fresh grain until this very day — until you bring the offering of your God" (Vayikra 23:14)

The five grains (wheat, barley, spelt, oats, rye) that took root after the previous year's Omer are called Chadash — new crop — and are forbidden until the next Omer is brought.

Yashan (Old Crop)

  • ✅ Took root before 16 Nisan
  • ✅ Permitted at all times
  • ✅ Can be used for Menachot offerings

Chadash (New Crop)

  • 🚫 Took root after 16 Nisan
  • 🚫 Forbidden until next Omer
  • 🚫 Cannot be used for Menachot

⏰ When Does the Omer Permit New Grain?

The verse: עַד עֶצֶם הַיּוֹם הַזֶּה — "Until this very day"

Chachamim

  • 📅 Permitted once the Omer is offered on 16 Nisan
  • ⏰ Practically: people wait until morning as a hiddur (enhanced mitzvah)
  • 🌾 "Etzem ha'yom" = the day the Omer is brought
  • ⚡ Waiting the whole day = rabbinic, not Torah

R' Yehuda

  • 📅 Forbidden the entire 16 Nisan
  • ⏰ Permitted only from 17 Nisan morning
  • 🌾 "Etzem ha'yom" = this very day is also forbidden
  • ⚡ Full day prohibition is Torah law
Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak resolves: Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai's post-Temple institution was stated according to R' Yehuda's opinion — which is why he prohibited all of 16 Nisan (the day of "waving") even without the Temple.

🏛️ Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai's Takkanah

🕍 Before: Temple standing
New grain permitted only after Omer (mid-morning of 16 Nisan)
↓
💔 Temple Destroyed — no Omer offering
↓
❓ New reality: With no Temple and no Omer, new grain becomes permitted at dawn of 16 Nisan (Chachamim) or 17 Nisan (R' Yehuda)
↓
⚠️ Concern: When the Temple is rebuilt, people will say "last year we ate at dawn — why not now?" They won't realize that now the Omer is needed.
↓
📜 RYBZ's Institution: Even without the Temple, the entire day of 16 Nisan (the "day of waving") remains forbidden. This way, people will not confuse the post-Temple and Temple-era practices.

🌍 Chadash in Chutz LaAretz

Applies in Eretz Yisrael

  • ✅ All agree — Torah prohibition
  • 🌾 Grain that took root after 16 Nisan forbidden until next Omer
  • 📜 Practiced fully in all periods

Chutz LaAretz — Machlokes

  • ⚖️ Chachamim: Torah prohibition applies everywhere
  • 🤔 Some hold: only Rabbinic outside Israel
  • 📅 In practice: 16 Nisan is the date (accounting for calendar uncertainty)
  • 🔔 Many Ashkenazic communities were lenient; Sephardim and Yekkes are strict
The Gemara notes: The five types of grain (wheat, barley, spelt, oats, rye) are the only ones subject to Chadash. Rice, millet, and similar grains are not included — they cannot become chametz (leavened), so they are not in the same halachic category.

🔄 Why Do Everything Atypically?

Before the Omer, harvesting and processing the new grain is permitted for the Omer itself — but in unusual ways
Action Normal Way Before Omer (Atypical)
Harvesting Sickle + cutting Picking by hand
Grinding Mill (animal/water-powered) Hand mill only
Sifting Inside the sifter On top of the sifter
Why? The atypical actions serve as a reminder that the grain is still forbidden to eat. If done normally, someone might forget and pop a kernel in their mouth. Done unusually — they remember there's something different about this grain today.

Abaye's challenge: Fine, picking by hand is a reminder. But why doesn't one forget about the prohibition while grinding and sifting?
Answer: Those are also done atypically — the unusualness creates the reminder at every stage.

✅ What Does the Omer Permit?

The Omer offering of 16 Nisan permits the new crop of the five grains for:
# Permission Granted Applies Where
1 🍞 Eating new grain (Chadash) Eretz Yisrael (and debate re: Diaspora)
2 🌾 Offering Menachot from new grain Beis HaMikdash
3 🫓 Using new grain for the Two Loaves (Shavuot) Beis HaMikdash
Note: The Two Loaves (Shtei HaLechem) of Shavuot must come from the new crop — specifically from the year's first wheat harvest. The Omer of 16 Nisan "opens the door" for all these permissions.
