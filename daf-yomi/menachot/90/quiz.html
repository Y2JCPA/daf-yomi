Menachot 90 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded
QUESTION 1 OF 5
If a metzora's guilt offering (asham metzora) was slaughtered not for its own sake or lacked blood placement on thumb/toe, what is the ruling?
A. It is completely rejected and not offered on the altar
B. It is offered on the altar and still requires libations, but the metzora must bring another asham for full purification
C. It is converted into a voluntary burnt offering
D. It is eaten by the priests but no libations are brought
Correct: B. The flawed asham metzora still goes on the altar and still needs libations. But it does not complete the metzora's process, so he must bring a second proper asham to be permitted in sacrificial consumption status.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why can't the flawed asham metzora simply be reclassified as a voluntary offering (like some flawed olot)?
A. Because voluntary offerings are never allowed for metzoraim
B. Because the Temple had no voluntary-libation system
C. Because there is no category of voluntary guilt offering (asham nedavah)
D. Because asham offerings may never be slaughtered except on Yom Kippur
Correct: C. A flawed burnt offering can sometimes continue as a voluntary olah because voluntary olot exist. But there is no such thing as a voluntary guilt offering. Therefore, the asham cannot be converted and must stay within asham framework.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Mishna on this daf, what is the difference between overflow from liquid measuring vessels and dry measuring vessels?
A. Both are non-sacred
B. Both are sacred
C. Liquid overflow is sacred; dry overflow is non-sacred
D. Liquid overflow is non-sacred; dry overflow is sacred
Correct: C. The Mishna distinguishes: overflow from liquid vessels (beirutzei middot lach) is sacred, while overflow from dry vessels (beirutzei middot yavesh) is non-sacred. This becomes part of a larger discussion with R' Akiva's position.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If a supplier contracted to provide flour at 3 se'a per sela, and later market price shifted to 4 se'a per sela, what must he supply to the Temple?
A. Still 3 se'a per sela, because contract terms are fixed
B. 4 se'a per sela, because the Temple treasury is always at an advantage
C. 3.5 se'a per sela as a compromise
D. He may cancel and renegotiate the contract
Correct: B. Temple contracts are treasury-favorable. If market changes in a way that gives more quantity for the same price, the supplier must provide the better rate (4 se'a per sela). The Temple gets the advantage, generating potential surplus.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What happens to surplus libations if they are not used the same day and are left overnight?
A. They remain valid indefinitely and can be used any later day
B. They are sold immediately regardless of timing
C. They are disqualified by overnight stay (lina)
D. They are poured out in the Kidron Valley
Correct: C. Surplus libations should ideally be used with another offering that day. If left overnight, they become disqualified through lina. If not disqualified and still usable, they may be sold and proceeds used to buy supplemental olot.
