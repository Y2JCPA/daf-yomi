🧍 Metzora Guilt Offering — Event Timeline

Step 1 — Asham Metzora is slaughtered
The metzora's guilt offering is brought, but potentially with an issue (e.g., slaughtered not for its own sake).
Step 2 — Blood application may be missing
No blood was placed on thumb/toe as required for metzora purification ritual.
Step 3 — Does it go on the altar?
Yes. The offering is still brought up to the altar and still requires its libations.
Step 4 — Is the metzora fully permitted?
No. He still needs another proper guilt offering to complete permission to eat kodashim.
R' Yochanan's principle: Unlike a burnt offering that can become a voluntary olah when flawed, an asham cannot become a voluntary offering (there is no voluntary guilt offering). So it retains asham framework including libations, but fails to complete metzora atonement.

💸 Temple Supply Contracts — Treasury Advantage

Contract Signed
Merchant agrees to deliver flour at a fixed rate (e.g., 3 se'a per sela).
Market Price Changes
Price drops (e.g., now 4 se'a per sela).
Who Benefits?
The Temple. Supplier must provide according to the more favorable current rate to the treasury.
Resulting Surplus
Temple receives extra flour/libation value. Surplus is sold and proceeds used for supplementary altar offerings.

🧠 Two Baraitot, Two Emphases

Baraita like R' Chiyya bar Yosef
Focuses on surplus libations lifecycle: use same day if possible; overnight disqualification if delayed; if sold, proceeds become supplemental olot.
Baraita like R' Yochanan
Focuses on contract economics: if supplier pledged one rate and market drops, he must now supply at the better rate for the Temple — treasury always advantaged.

📋 Menachot 90 — Summary

Metzora Asham: Even when flawed, still offered with libations; but person still needs another asham to complete purification status.
Temple Measures: Most vessels measured heaped; High Priest's flour vessel had a special leveled calibration. Liquid overflow sacred; dry overflow generally not.
Treasury Policy: Supply contracts always favor the Temple. Surplus libations are reused if possible, otherwise sold for supplemental olot.
