📜 "You Shall Bring" — Seven Laws from One Phrase

"You shall bring for the meal offering of your first fruits" (Vayikra 2:14) — the repeated phrase "you shall bring" teaches multiple halachot
#HalachaDerivation
1🌿 Preferred: standing grainL'chatchila from standing crop
2📦 If not: from sheaves"You shall bring" — any manner
3💧 Preferred: moist grainL'chatchila fresh and tender
4🌾 If not: from dry grain"You shall bring" — even dry
5🌙 Preferred: reaped at nightL'chatchila nighttime
6☀️ If not: reaped by day = valid"You shall bring" — even daytime
7📅 Overrides Shabbat"You shall bring" — even on Shabbat
Additional teachings: "You shall bring" also teaches: from anywhere in Eretz Yisrael (not only near Jerusalem) and even in a state of ritual impurity.

📅 Does Reaping the Omer Override Shabbat?

R' Yishmael / Most Tanna'im

  • ✅ Reaping the Omer overrides Shabbat
  • Derived from "you shall bring"
  • Mishna assumes reaping on Shabbat
  • On Shabbat: 3 se'a reaped (or 5 per R' Yishmael)

Rabbi (Yehuda HaNasi)

  • ❌ Reaping the Omer does NOT override Shabbat
  • Can reap on Friday instead
  • Based on R' Akiva: labor possible before Shabbat doesn't override
  • Multiple mishnayot about Shabbat reaping are "not like Rabbi"

⚖️ Reaped at the Wrong Time — Valid?

Rabbi (Yehuda HaNasi)

  • If reaped during the day (instead of night) → Valid (Kasher)
  • Night reaping is a mitzvah l'chatchila, not essential
  • Follows from: reaping doesn't override Shabbat (since it's not indispensable at night)

R' Elazar b"R Shimon

  • If reaped during the day → Invalid (Pasul)
  • Night reaping is essential
  • Follows from: reaping overrides Shabbat (proving it MUST be done at night — no flexibility)
The logic: If reaping MUST happen at night (R' Elazar), that's why it overrides Shabbat — because it can't be done earlier. If reaping at night is merely preferred (Rabbi), then it could be done before Shabbat, so it doesn't override.

🔥 The Burning of Fats Analogy

Why does R' Elazar b"R Shimon think reaping at the wrong time invalidates?
↓
Potential challenge: R' Shimon (his father) taught that burning fats/limbs overrides Shabbat just because it's a mitzvah to do at night — not because daytime would invalidate!
↓
Resolution: Burning fats is different — the slaughter already overrode Shabbat, so it's natural to complete the process. But reaping the Omer has no prior Shabbat labor — so if it overrides Shabbat, it must be because the timing is essential.

✂️ Reaping for Mitzvah — The Torah's Permission

The Torah says: רֵאשִׁית קְצִירְכֶם — "the first of your harvest"

"Your harvest" = personal harvest. The Omer must come before it.
But reaping for other purposes is not "your harvest" and may precede the Omer:
"Your harvest" = personal reaping for grain consumption
↓
NOT "your harvest":
🐄 Animal fodder
🌳 Protecting saplings
📚 Beis Midrash space
🪦 Mourning area
IS "your harvest":
🍞 Personal use
💰 Sale
🌾 General harvest
↓
Even when permitted, don't bundle into sheaves — minimize involvement
