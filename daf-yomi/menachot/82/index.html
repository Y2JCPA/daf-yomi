🪙 Peace Offerings From Second-Tithe Money

The Gezerah Shavah — "there" and "there":
Peace offering verse: "You shall sacrifice peace offerings and you shall eat there" (Devarim 27:7)
↓
Second tithe verse: "You shall eat before the Lord your God in the place He shall choose there" (Devarim 14:23)
↓
Verbal analogy links the two → peace offerings may be purchased with second-tithe money
↓
Thanks offerings derived from peace offerings → they too may be purchased with second-tithe money

🌾 Why Tithe Wheat Can't Make Thanks Offering Loaves

If thanks offerings can be bought with second-tithe money, why not make the loaves from second-tithe wheat directly?
↓
The reasoning chain:
Peace offerings are NOT the same species as second tithe (animals ≠ grain)
↓
Therefore: the thanks offering loaves cannot be the SAME species as second tithe either
↓
Actual second-tithe wheat IS the same species as second tithe → excluded
↓
Wheat purchased with second-tithe money is NOT second tithe itself → permitted

💎 Rabbi Ami: Two Sanctities Collide

Rabbi Ami's ruling: If one designates second-tithe money specifically for a peace offering, the money does NOT assume the status of a peace offering.
Why? The sanctity of a peace offering is not strong enough to override the sanctity of second tithe. The second-tithe kedushah remains intact.
↓
❓ But a Mishna (Maaser Sheni 1:4) says one ideally buys peace offerings with second-tithe money?
↓
Resolution: only the flesh of the animal gets peace offering sanctity. The hide retains second-tithe status. The two sanctities coexist separately — they don't fully merge.

🐑 Paschal Offering Debate — Possible From Impossible

The question: Can we derive a law for the Paschal offering of all generations from the Paschal offering in Egypt? (The Egyptian Pesach had unique rules that couldn't apply today — e.g., door-blood, no blood on altar.)

R' Eliezer

  • Yes — "You shall perform this service in this month" means all Pesach services follow Egypt's model
  • We derive the generations-Pesach from Egypt-Pesach
  • Therefore: a leftover Paschal offering is brought from non-sacred money

R' Akiva

  • Initially: one cannot derive the possible from the impossible
  • Egypt Pesach didn't require altar blood — generations-Pesach does: they're different!
  • But the wilderness Pesach refutes this — it DID require altar blood yet was from non-sacred money
6 / 9

⚖️ The Logic of "Possible From Impossible"

Can you derive a practical halacha from a model that is theoretically impossible to replicate today?
↓
R' Akiva initially
❌ No — you can't. The two cases are structurally different.
R' Eliezer
✅ Yes — the verse explicitly links all generations to Egypt's service.
↓
R' Akiva reformulates: even if I concede one can derive possible from impossible, the different requirements between Egypt Pesach and generations Pesach still breaks the analogy
↓
Wilderness Pesach refutes R' Akiva — it had altar blood AND was non-sacred. So the analogy holds.
