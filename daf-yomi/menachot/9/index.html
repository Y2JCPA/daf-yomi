üèõÔ∏è Service vs. Eating in the Heichal

Avodah (Service)

  • A slave serves in front of his master
  • It's more fitting to serve in the holier place
  • "Tefel (Azarah) shouldn't be more stringent than the Ikar (Heichal)"
  • ‚úì Shechitah in Heichal ‚Äî valid (R' Yochanan)

Achilah (Eating)

  • A slave does NOT eat in front of his master
  • It's less fitting to eat in the holier place
  • The "Tefel/Ikar" logic does NOT apply
  • Eating in Heichal only allowed because of an explicit verse: "B'Kodesh ha'Kodoshim Tochalenu"

ü´ó Belilah (Mixing) Outside the Azarah

Reish Lakish: Valid

  • Verse order: Pouring oil ‚Üí Levonah ‚Üí "bring to the Kohanim" ‚Üí Kemitzah
  • Only from Kemitzah onward is it "Mitzvas Kehunah"
  • Pouring & mixing: valid by a Zar (non-Kohen)
  • Since Kehunah not required ‚Üí Azarah not required either

R' Yochanan: Pasul

  • Agrees: a Zar can do the mixing
  • But: since mixing is done in a Kli Shares
  • Even without Kehunah, the Kli requires it be done inside the Azarah
  • Supported by a Baraisa

üìâ Minchah Became Chaser ‚Äî Before Kemitzah

What "establishes" a Minchah so it can become permanently disqualified?

R' Yochanan

  • Kemitzah establishes the Minchah
  • Before Kemitzah ‚Üí bring flour from home and refill
  • The Kli alone doesn't lock it in

Reish Lakish

  • Kidush Kli (vessel sanctification) establishes it
  • Once in the Kli ‚Üí permanently established
  • Cannot bring more flour to fill it
Teyuvta! R' Yochanan refutes Reish Lakish from the Mishnah in Nega'im: The Log of oil of a Metzora that became Chaser before pouring ‚Äî just refill it! This proves Kidush Kli alone doesn't establish it. Reish Lakish is refuted.
