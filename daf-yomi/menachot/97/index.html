📖 Shemot 25:29 — Four Vessels from Four Words

Source: וְעָשִׂיתָ קְעָרֹתָיו וְכַפֹּתָיו וּקְשׂוֹתָיו וּמְנַקִּיֹּתָיו
"And you shall make its dishes, its pans, its covers, and its tubes" (Shemot 25:29)
Hebrew WordRav Ketina's InterpretationFunction
קְעָרֹתָיו — Ke'arotavMolds (Defus)Shape the dough before and after baking
כַפֹּתָיו — KapotavBowls (Bazichim)Hold the frankincense on top of each stack
קְשׂוֹתָיו — KesotavPanels (Senifin)4 gold panels supporting the loaves

"With which it shall be covered" — the final phrase teaches that the bread is covered (supported) by the rods. The Torah accounts for every piece of the Table's apparatus!

📏 Two Sizes of Cubits in the Temple

R' Meir — Selective Small Cubits

  • 📐 ALL Temple measurements use a 6-handbreadth cubit ("medium")
  • ⚠️ EXCEPT: Golden Altar, altar corners, base, and ledge
  • 💡 Those exceptions use a 5-handbreadth cubit
  • 📖 Source: "And this shall be the higher part" (Ezekiel 43:13) — "this" = only this

R' Yehuda — All Vessels Use Small Cubits

  • 📐 ALL vessel measurements use a 5-handbreadth cubit
  • 💡 "Like this" = all vessels are like the Golden Altar
  • 📖 Source: "Like this shall be the higher part" — "like" expands to all
  • 🏛️ Building measurements still use 6-handbreadth cubits

🔴 The Red Line and the Altar's Height

The altar had a red line (chut ha-sikra) dividing upper and lower blood applications.
🔺 Upper Section (above the red line)
Bird burnt offerings, bird sin offerings
Blood squeezed on the upper wall
↕️ 🔴 Red Line
🔻 Lower Section (below the red line)
Most animal offerings
Blood sprinkled on the lower corners
Critical calculation: If you squeeze bird blood ONE CUBIT below the ledge — is it still "upper"? The Gemara calculates the altar is 58 handbreadths tall (halfway = 29). The ledge is at handbreadth 35 — SIX handbreadths above halfway. So even one cubit (5 hb) below the ledge is still above the midpoint. ✅

🏰 Shushan the Capital — On the Eastern Gate

Q
Why was a depiction of Shushan (the Persian capital) painted on the Eastern Gate of the Temple?
A
So that the Jews would always remember WHERE they came from — Persia granted them permission to rebuild the Temple! Fear of the kingdom would keep them loyal.
The Measuring Rods: Two cubit-measuring rods were kept in the Shushan Gate chamber — one in the NE corner (half a fingerbreadth longer than Moses' cubit) and one in the SE corner (a full fingerbreadth longer).

Why two sizes? Artisans would receive materials measured by the small rod and return their work measured by the large rod — ensuring they never accidentally misuse Temple property.

🔥 Ezekiel's Verse — Altar and Table United

הַמִּזְבֵּחַ עֵץ שָׁלוֹשׁ אַמּוֹת... וַיְדַבֵּר אֵלַי זֶה הַשֻּׁלְחָן אֲשֶׁר לִפְנֵי ה׳
"The altar was of wood... and he said to me: This is the Table that is before the Lord" (Yechezkel 41:22)
Q
The verse begins with "altar" and ends with "Table"! Why?
RY
R' Yochanan and R' Elazar both say: When the Temple stands — the altar atones. Now that the Temple is destroyed — a person's table atones for him (through feeding the poor).
A beautiful teaching: The verse links altar and table deliberately. Just as the altar effected atonement through offerings, so too a person's dinner table — when used to feed the hungry and needy — serves the same spiritual function today.
