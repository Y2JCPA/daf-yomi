⚖️ R' Meir vs. Rabbis — How Many Tenths Vessels?

OpinionVessels for a TenthSource
R' MeirTWO vessels (each holds 1/10 ephah)"A tenth, a tenth" — double mention = two vessels
RabbisONE vessel (holds 1/10 ephah)"And one tenth-part" — the word "one" means only one
Half-tenthOne vessel (½ of 1/10)R' Meir: from "and" prefix; Rabbis: from "a tenth, a tenth"
What's the practical difference? R' Yochanan says it affects whether the measuring vessel's overflow (liquid that runs down the outside) is consecrated. Each opinion yields a different conclusion about which overflow is sacred.

📏 R' Shimon's Golden Rule

The Temple Principle
"A measuring vessel that was used for measuring this quantity was not used to measure a different quantity."
Why? The Rabbis said: "We don't need a separate 1½-log vessel — just use the ½-log vessel three times." R' Shimon replied: "By that logic, don't make any vessels except the ¼-log one — you can multiply anything!" The principle is: each dedicated vessel exists for its one specific purpose. No substitutions.
This was the practice in the Temple: 1 vessel = 1 quantity = 1 purpose.
The 1½-log vessel measured oil for the High Priest's griddle-cake (morning + afternoon portions).

💡 R' Chiyya — "Man of My Counsel from Afar"

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi (puzzling aloud):
What was the ¼-log measuring vessel consecrated for? It couldn't be for the sota (the water is already "holy water"). It couldn't be for the thanks offering loaves (they're consecrated through slaughter, not through the vessel). So why did it need to be anointed as a service vessel?
R' Chiyya (who had traveled from Babylon):
It was used to measure the oil for the High Priest's griddle-cake — ¼-log per loaf, for each of the 12 loaves.
Rabbi's Response
"The man of my counsel from a far country"
(Yeshayahu 46:11 — Rabbi applied this verse to R' Chiyya who came from Babylon to Eretz Yisrael)

📊 Second-Grade Oil vs. First-Grade: Vessel Usage

Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak clarifies the Mishna: When the Mishna says that Grade 2 of the first harvest and Grade 1 of the second harvest are "equal" — it means equal for meal offerings. There's no practical preference between them for that purpose. Neither is better than the other for meal offerings, so either may be used freely.

📋 Menachot 88 — Summary

Seven Vessels: 3-log, 2-log, 1-log (liquid libations), ½-log, ¼-log, 1/10-ephah, ½-of-1/10-ephah. Exact sizes debated by R' Meir, Rabbis, R' Yehuda.
R' Shimon's Principle: One vessel = one quantity = one purpose. Never use a vessel to measure a different quantity. This is how the Temple operated.
R' Chiyya's Insight: The ¼-log vessel was for the High Priest's griddle-cake oil (¼-log per loaf). Rabbi praised him with "the man of my counsel from afar" (Yeshayahu 46:11).
