Menachot 108 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded
QUESTION 1 OF 5
How many Shofar-shaped collection horns were there in the Temple (as discussed in this Mishna)?
A. Three
B. Six
C. Seven
D. Thirteen
Correct: B — Six. The Mishna describes six Shofar-shaped collection horns, designated for: new Shekalim, old Shekalim, bird nest funds, bird Olot, wood donations, and frankincense. (A Mishna elsewhere mentions 13 total including 7 more for other purposes.)
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Ami, why were the Temple collection horns necessary?
A. To prevent coins from rusting
B. To prevent quarrels — donors needed to know exactly where their money was going
C. Because large piles of coins were too heavy to move
D. To comply with a specific Torah law about Temple finances
Correct: B. R' Ami explains that separate collection horns prevented quarrels. If everything went into one pile, people might worry their half-shekel was being used for wood while they intended it for bird offerings. Separate labeled horns gave donors confidence and prevented disputes.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why were the collection boxes shaped like a Shofar (horn) — wide at top, narrow at bottom?
A. To symbolize the call to repentance
B. To make them recognizable from a distance
C. Practical: coins could be inserted easily from the wide top but were hard to steal from the narrow bottom
D. This was the only shape available to Temple craftsmen
Correct: C. The horn shape was a security feature. The wide opening made it easy to drop coins in, while the narrow bottom made it very difficult for anyone to reach in and steal. It functioned as a one-way deposit mechanism — practical Temple design.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
One of the six horns was designated for "old Shekalim." What does this mean?
A. Ancient coins from the time of Moshe
B. Overdue half-shekels that should have been paid in a prior year
C. Worn and damaged coins exchanged for new ones
D. Shekalim paid by the elderly
Correct: B. "Old Shekalim" refers to the obligatory half-shekel contribution that someone failed to bring in the prior year and is now bringing late. Having a separate horn for these overdue payments helped the Temple track who had fulfilled their obligation and who had not yet done so.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If someone vows "an animal offering" without specifying the type, what is the minimum they must bring?
A. A bull — the most prestigious animal
B. A ram — the middle-sized offering
C. The smallest valid offering — a female sheep or goat (as a Shelamim)
D. They must specify — an unspecified animal vow is invalid
Correct: C. An unspecified animal vow is fulfilled by bringing the smallest valid animal offering. Since female animals are valid for Shelamim (peace offerings), a female sheep or goat satisfies the vow. The principle throughout Chapter 13 is consistent: bring the minimum that fulfills the vow language.
