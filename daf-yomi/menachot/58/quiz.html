QUESTION 1 OF 5
What renders an offering piggul, and what is the punishment for eating it?
A. Improper intent during the service; eating it carries karet
B. Slaughtering it south of the altar; liable for lashes
C. Using a disqualified priest; no punishment
D. Leaving it overnight; liable for lashes only
Correct: A. Piggul is created when a priest performs a sacrificial rite with intent to consume the offering beyond its designated time. Anyone who eats a piggul offering is liable for karet (divine excision).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the distinction between piggul and notar?
A. Piggul results from improper intent; notar is meat remaining beyond the permitted time
B. They are identical transgressions
C. Piggul applies to animal offerings; notar only to meal offerings
D. Notar is more severe than piggul
Correct: A. Piggul arises from the priest's intent at the time of service, making the offering conceptually disqualified from the start. Notar refers to meat that physically remains past its consumption deadline.
