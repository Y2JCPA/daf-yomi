QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why can't an individual donate a voluntary offering of two loaves (similar to Shavuot)?
A. "You may bring them" is addressed to the community (plural), and "these" limits it to the obligatory offering only
B. Individual leaven offerings were banned after the Golden Calf
C. Only the High Priest may handle leavened items
D. Two loaves can only be baked in the Temple ovens, which individuals can't access
Correct: A. "You may bring" is in the plural, meaning it's addressed to the community. And "these" limits it to only the obligatory two loaves of Shavuot. Neither the community nor an individual may bring additional two loaves as a gift offering.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the practical difference between R. Eliezer and R. Akiva regarding "of it" in the prohibition against burning on the altar?
A. Whether the prohibition applies to communal or individual offerings
B. Whether offerings whose meat is NOT burned (like bird sin offerings) are included in the prohibition
C. Whether the prohibition applies on Shabbat
D. Whether less than an olive-bulk of leaven triggers liability
Correct: B. R. Eliezer: "of it" means only items that had a portion burned in fire are included. R. Akiva: even items that had a portion offered in any manner. The bird sin offering is the key case: its blood is sprinkled but no meat is burned on the altar.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava, how many sets of lashes does one receive for burning a mixture of leaven AND honey on the altar?
A. One set
B. Two sets
C. Three sets
D. Four sets
Correct: D. Rava counts four independent prohibitions: (1) "any leaven" (2) "any honey" (3) "you shall not burn" (general prohibition) (4) "any of it" (for burning even a part). Each generates its own set of lashes.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Abaye disagree with Rava about flogging for a leaven-honey mixture?
A. Abaye holds there's no prohibition against honey at all
B. Abaye holds one is not flogged for a "general prohibition" (lav she'bichlalut) covering multiple acts
C. Abaye holds the mixture becomes nullified
D. Abaye holds one only receives lashes from a court of 71
Correct: B. Abaye's principle: "you shall not burn" is a single verse covering multiple prohibited substances (leaven AND honey). Since it's a "general prohibition" rather than a specific one, it cannot generate separate sets of lashes for each substance.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the difference between Abaye and Rava regarding the minimum size of a handful (kometz)?
A. Abaye: a handful can be less than two olive-bulks; Rava: a handful is never less than two olive-bulks
B. Abaye: a handful must be exactly three olive-bulks; Rava: no minimum
C. They agree on size but disagree on which fingers to use
D. Abaye: only wheat counts; Rava: barley also counts
Correct: A. Abaye holds a handful can be less than two olive-bulks, and burning less than an olive-bulk is significant. Rava holds a handful is never less than two olive-bulks, and burning less than an olive-bulk is not significant. This determines what "any leaven" comes to add.
