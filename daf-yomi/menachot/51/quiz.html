Menachot 51 — Quiz

1. When the Kohen Gadol dies and no replacement is appointed, who funds the chavitin offering according to R. Shimon?

✅ b) R. Shimon derives from "חׇק עוֹלָם" — a statute for the olam (world/community) — that the community pays from the Temple treasury chamber.

2. What does the gezera shava of "כָּלִיל" (wholly) teach?

✅ b) The verbal analogy links "כָּלִיל תׇּקְטָר" (Lev. 6:15) and "כָּלִיל תִּהְיֶה" (Lev. 6:16), teaching that both offerings must be entirely burned and are forbidden to eat.

3. How does R. Abbahu resolve R. Shimon's apparent contradiction (Torah law vs. court ordinance)?

✅ b) R. Abbahu explains there were two sequential ordinances — originally Torah law (community pays), then when the chamber was depleted the heirs were made to pay, but when heirs were negligent it reverted to Torah law.

4. What does the verse "This is the offering of Aaron and his sons" teach about the chavitin?

✅ c) The plural "יַקְרִיבוּ" shows they bring separately: Aaron = the KG's daily chavitin; "his sons" = the initiation offering of ordinary kohanim when they first serve.

5. What does R. Yehuda derive from the extra letter vav (ו) in "וַחֲצִי" ("and half of it")?

✅ b) R. Yehuda expounds the extra vav to teach that a replacement Kohen Gadol must bring a complete new tenth of an ephah of his own, not use the deceased KG's remaining portion.
