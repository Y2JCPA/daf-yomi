Menachot 29 — Quiz

1. What was Moses doing when he found God tying crowns on the letters?

✅ b) When Moses ascended on High (to receive the Torah), he found God sitting and tying decorative crowns onto the letters.

2. In which row did Moses sit in R. Akiva's class?

✅ c) Moses sat in the eighth row and could not follow the discussion. But when R. Akiva said "halakha leMoshe miSinai," Moses was comforted.

3. What did God answer when Moses protested R. Akiva's fate?

✅ b) God's response: "Silence! This is what arose in thought before Me" — one of the Talmud's starkest statements on divine justice.

4. If a scribe omits God's Name and writes the next word, what should he do?

✅ b) Scrape off the word written after the omission, write the Name in its proper place, and suspend the scraped-off words above the line.

5. Which seven letters receive three crowns (tagin)?

✅ b) שעטנ״ז ג״ץ: the letters ש, ע, ט, נ, ז, ג, ץ receive three crowns each — a halakha leMoshe miSinai.
