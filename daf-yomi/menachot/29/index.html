Moses, God & the Crowns

Mosh
Master of the Universe, who is holding You back? [Why add decorations to an already perfect Torah?]
G-d
There is a man who will exist after many generations — Akiva ben Yosef — who will derive mounds of halakhot from each crown.
Mosh
Show him to me!
G-d
Turn around. [Moses is transported to R. Akiva's class and sits in the 8th row, unable to follow the discussion.]
Mosh
His strength weakened. Then a student asks R. Akiva: "What is your source?" R. Akiva answers: "It is a halakha given to Moses at Sinai." Moses is comforted.
3 / 8

Moses's Troubling Question

Mosh
Master of the Universe — You showed me his Torah, now show me his reward!
G-d
Turn around. [Moses sees R. Akiva's flesh being weighed at the meat market after his martyrdom by the Romans.]
Mosh
"This is Torah and this is its reward?!"
G-d
"Silence! This is what arose in thought before Me."
One of the most powerful aggadot in the Talmud. The mystery of divine justice — the greatest Torah scholar receives the cruelest death, and even Moses cannot comprehend it.

Perforated Letters in Tefillin

A man had the leg of the letter ה in "הָעָם" perforated in his tefillin. He asked R. Abahu, who sent him to a sage who said: If a child who is neither wise nor foolish can read it correctly, it's valid.

Similarly for a perforated ו in "וַיַּהֲרוֹג" — same principle: a child's reading test.

Suspending God's Name

R. Shimon Shezuri

  • May suspend the entire Name above the line
  • But NOT part of the Name

R. Shimon b. Elazar (in R. Meir's name)

  • Do not suspend the Name at all
  • Place the entire sheet of parchment in geniza

The Letters That Require Crowns

Seven letters receive three crowns (tagin):
שעטנ״ז ג״ץ — ש, ע, ט, נ, ז, ג, ץ

These decorative strokes on top of the letters are halakha leMoshe miSinai — a law given to Moses at Sinai. R. Akiva would derive halakhot from each one.

Key Takeaways — Menachot 29

1. God ties crowns on Torah letters for R. Akiva to expound — centuries later.

2. Moses visits R. Akiva's class, doesn't understand, but is comforted when Akiva credits "halakha leMoshe miSinai."

3. Moses sees R. Akiva's martyrdom — "This is Torah and this is its reward?!" God: "Silence."

4. Practical STaM rules: perforated letters validated by a child's reading; overflow rules for column ends; geniza for sheets with erroneous Divine Names.

5. שעטנ״ז ג״ץ — the seven crowned letters.
