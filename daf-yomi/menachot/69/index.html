🍞 The Two Loaves — What Makes Them Valid?

The Torah says: וְהֵבֵאתֶם אֶת עֹמֶר רֵאשִׁית קְצִירְכֶם אֶל הַכֹּהֵן
The Two Loaves of Shavuot must be Bikkurim — "first fruits" brought to the altar this year.
🤔 Rabba's Question:
What if there was a drought and wheat didn't grow this year — can we use last year's wheat for the Two Loaves?
↓
Possibility A
"Bikkurim" = first fruits of the land this year
❓ If last year's wheat — not from this year's crop
→ Might be valid (the land produced it previously)
Possibility B
"Bikkurim" = first fruits brought to the altar this year
→ The altar already received wheat from the Omer this year
→ Cannot reuse — the altar's "first" was already consumed
↓
Conclusion: The Two Loaves cannot come from last year's wheat — because the altar already "consumed" from this year's fruit (the Omer). The "first" for the altar is already gone.

✅ What Do the Two Loaves Permit?

The Two Loaves of Shavuot serve as a communal "first fruits" offering — permitting new wheat for various uses
What Is Permitted What Stage of Growth Is Required?
🌾 Grain (wheat/barley) Must have taken root (השרשה) before the Two Loaves
🍎 Fruit (Bikkurim) Rami bar Chama's dilemma: budding of leaf or formation of leaf?
🌸 New Menachot from new wheat Wheat must be new crop permitted by Omer
Key principle for grain: Even if the grain only took root before the Two Loaves — it is permitted by them. We don't require the grain to have already sprouted or formed heads.

This is why Rami bar Chama asks about fruit trees — since even early "taking root" of grain is sufficient, is "budding of a leaf" equally sufficient for trees?

🌸 The Fruit Tree Dilemma

❓ Rami bar Chama asks:
For fruit (Bikkurim) — what stage must the tree reach before the Two Loaves for its fruit to be permitted?
↓
First clarification:
"Budding" and "formation" here don't refer to the fruit itself —
Since even grain "taking root" (an earlier stage) suffices,
fruit budding/formation would obviously be enough!
↓
Real question:
Is the budding of the tree's leaves like "taking root" of grain?
Or is it a later/different stage that doesn't trigger the same permission?
↓
Option A
✅ Leaf budding = "taking root"
→ All fruit from this tree is permitted by the Two Loaves
Option B
❌ Leaf budding ≠ "taking root"
→ Tree's fruit NOT permitted by Two Loaves
↓
🔓 Teiku — The dilemma remains unresolved

🧺 The Wicker Basket Dilemma

Background: Wooden vessels that go through an animal's digestive system — are they considered "digested" (like dung) and exempt from ritual impurity, or do they retain their vessel status?
🐘 Elephant swallows palm leaves → excretes them intact
A basket is then woven from these palm leaves
Question: Is this basket susceptible to ritual impurity?
↓
If "digested"
Like dung/earth vessels
→ NOT susceptible to impurity
(Stone, dung, earth vessels are exempt)
If "not digested"
Still regular wooden vessel
→ IS susceptible to impurity
↓
Attempted proof (Ulla): Wolves swallowed two children — the flesh passed through and was declared ritually pure (considered digested). Similarly, palm leaves should be considered digested.
↓
Rejected: Flesh is soft and truly digested. But the Sages ruled the bones impure (hard matter isn't digested). Palm leaves are intermediate — harder than flesh, softer than bone. Unresolved.

🌾 The Five Grains — Chalah, Chadash & More

Several obligations apply specifically to the five species (Chamisha Minei Dagan)
Grain Hebrew Aramaic Name
Wheatחִטָּהחינטא
Barleyשְׂעֹרָהשערתא
Speltכֻּסֶּמֶתכוסמין
Oatsשִׁבֹּלֶת שׁוּעָלשיבולי תעלא
Ryeשִׁיפוֹןדישרא
Why only these five? Because only these can become chametz (leavened). The Gemara derives from a verbal analogy (Gezerah Shavah) "bread-bread" from Matzah: just as Matzah can only come from grains that can become chametz, so too Chalah, Chadash, and Omer apply only to these five grains.

Rice and millet don't leaven — they ferment/decay (sirchon) — so they are excluded.

🌾 The Omer — Permitting New Grain

The Omer (16 Nisan) permits:
New grain that took root before the Omer was offered
↓
Took root BEFORE Omer
✅ Permitted by the Omer
Even if not yet sprouted
Took root AFTER Omer
🚫 Not permitted by this Omer
Must wait for next year's Omer
↓
🌸 Rami bar Chama's parallel dilemma for fruit:
Does the budding of leaves of a fruit tree = "taking root" of grain?
If yes → Two Loaves permit the fruit
If no → Must wait for a later stage
↓
🔓 Teiku — Unresolved. Requires further study.
