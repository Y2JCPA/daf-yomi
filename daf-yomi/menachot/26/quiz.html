Menachot 26 — Quiz

1. According to R. Yehoshua, when is a meal offering unfit?

✅ b) R. Yehoshua holds the offering is unfit when ALL the remainder is gone. If even an olive-bulk remains, the handful is still burned.

2. Why don't half an olive-bulk of meat and half of fat combine?

✅ b) Meat is eaten and fat is burned on the altar — different destinations prevent combination. Exception: burnt offering where both go to the altar.

3. According to the Rabbis, what happens when the handful goes to the kohen's left hand?

✅ b) The Rabbis hold it is permanently unfit — like blood that spilled to the floor. It cannot be re-sanctified.

4. Can the handful be burned in multiple stages?

✅ d) R. Yehoshua b. Levi says exactly two stages only; R. Yochanan says even several stages work, because even less than an olive-bulk of burning is significant.

5. When does burning the handful permit the remainder? (R. Yochanan)

✅ c) R. Yochanan says the remainder is permitted when fire consumes the majority of the handful, derived from "the smoke of the land went up as the smoke of a furnace."
