When the Remainder Is Gone

After the handful is removed, the remainder is what kohanim eat. What if it's destroyed?

R. Eliezer

  • Offering is fit
  • Burn the handful anyway
  • Parallel: blood can be sprinkled even with no edible meat

R. Yehoshua

  • Offering is unfit
  • Handful serves to permit the remainder — no remainder, no purpose
  • Needs at least an olive-bulk of edible remainder
3 / 7

Rav's Refinement: Partial Loss

Rav says: R. Yehoshua's rule applies only when all the remainder is lost/impure/burned. If only part is gone, the handful is still burned — because "what remains is significant."

This parallels R. Yehoshua's baraita: blood is sprinkled if there's at least an olive-bulk of meat OR an olive-bulk of fat remaining. Half + half do NOT combine (different destinations — eating vs. altar).

Exception: The Burnt Offering

Burnt offering (olah) — entirely consumed on altar
↓
Half olive-bulk meat + half olive-bulk fat DO combine
Because both go to the same destination (the altar)
↓
But the meal offering of libations does NOT help — even though it accompanies the animal, it's not "the offering itself"

Left Hand, Right Hand — Handful Transfer

R. Elazar & R. Shimon

  • If kohen put handful in left hand → return to right
  • Improper intent during transfer CAN create piggul
  • Do NOT require sanctification in a vessel

The Rabbis

  • Once in left hand → permanently unfit
  • Like blood that spilled to the floor before collection
  • Require sanctification in a service vessel
6 / 7

Burning the Handful in Two Stages

R. Yehoshua b. Levi

  • Twice = exactly 2 stages ✅
  • Several times ❌
  • Reason: handful must be ≥ 2 olives; each burning ≥ 1 olive

R. Yochanan

  • Twice ✅
  • Even several times ✅
  • Reason: even less than an olive-bulk of burning is significant
When does burning permit the remainder?
R. Chanina: When fire takes hold of the handful.
R. Yochanan: When fire consumes the majority — derived from "the smoke of the land went up as the smoke of a furnace" (Gen. 19:28).
7 / 7

Key Takeaways — Menachot 26

1. R. Eliezer: burn the handful even if the remainder is gone. R. Yehoshua: need at least an olive-bulk of edible remainder.

2. Partial loss — what remains is significant; the handful is still burned.

3. Meat and fat don't combine (different destinations) — except for an olah where both go to the altar.

4. Left hand disqualifies the handful according to the Rabbis; R. Elazar/R. Shimon allow returning to right hand.

5. Handful can be burned in two stages (R. Yochanan: even several). Remainder is permitted when the majority is consumed by fire.
