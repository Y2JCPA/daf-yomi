According to Rava's resolution, what is the difference between blood cooked by fire and blood congealed by the sun?
A. Both are prohibited
B. Blood cooked by fire is no longer "blood" (no prohibition); blood congealed by sun retains its status (still prohibited)
C. Both are permitted
D. Sun-congealed is no longer blood; fire-cooked retains its status
Correct: B. Fire fundamentally changes the blood's nature ‚Äî it's no longer halachically "blood." But sun-congealed blood can revert to liquid state, so it retains its status as blood and one is still liable for consuming it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What type of salt is preferred for the Mizbeach and why?
A. Istalkis salt because it's coarser and more visible
B. Sodom salt ‚Äî it "never rests" (is always available), derived from "lo tashbit" (shall not cease)
C. Sea salt because it comes from natural water
D. Any salt is equally valid with no preference
Correct: B. The verse "lo tashbit" (you shall not omit/cease) is interpreted to mean: bring salt that "never rests" ‚Äî always available. Sodom salt fits this description. Istalkis salt is acceptable as a backup.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In which location does salt on a Korban become subject to Me'ilah (misuse of consecrated property)?
A. In the Chamber of Salt (Lishkat HaMelach)
B. Only when the salt is ON the limb of the offering itself
C. On the ramp of the Mizbeach
D. On top of the altar
Correct: B. Salt in the Chamber, on the ramp, or on top of the altar is communal stock not yet designated for a specific offering. Only once salt is placed directly on a limb does it become sanctified for that offering and subject to Me'ilah.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Who pays for the salt used on offerings ‚Äî the individual or the community?
A. The individual who brings the Korban
B. The community (Tzibur) ‚Äî derived from "Brit Melach Olam" paralleling "Brit Olam" of Shewbread, which is communally funded
C. Half from the individual, half from the Tzibur
D. The Kohanim pay from their own funds
Correct: B. The Gemara uses a Gezeirah Shavah: "Brit Melach Olam" (salt) parallels "Brit Olam" (Shewbread). Just as the Tzibur funds the Shewbread, the Tzibur funds all salt for offerings.

Correct: B. Ben Bukhri holds Kohanim may voluntarily contribute but aren't obligated.
