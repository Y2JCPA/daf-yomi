Menachot 36 — Quiz

1. How does Rav Ashi derive that tefillin go on the left arm?

✅ b) "יָדְכָה" can be read as "יד כהה" = the weaker hand, which is normally the left.

2. Where does a left-handed person place tefillin?

✅ b) A lefty puts tefillin on their right arm because that is their weaker hand.

3. "Between your eyes" means:

✅ c) "Between your eyes" means the upper forehead/hairline area — derived from the parallel usage in mourning laws.

4. Why must the arm tefillin be on the bicep?

✅ b) "For you" teaches it should be a private sign — on the bicep where it's normally covered by clothing.

5. An ambidextrous person — which arm?

✅ b) Ambidextrous: left arm, like everyone else. Since neither hand is weaker, we follow the standard rule.
