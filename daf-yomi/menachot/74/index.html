🔥 A Kohen's Sin-Offering Meal Offering

The problem: An Israelite who sins may bring a meal offering (Minchat Choteh) as a sin offering. A handful is removed for the altar; the rest is eaten by Kohanim. But what if a Kohen needs to bring a sin-offering meal offering?
❓ Can a Kohen perform the rites of his own sin-offering meal offering?
↓
✅ Yes — derived from "the remainder shall be the priest's, as the meal offering"
↓
But with a twist:
The Kohen's handful is sacrificed on the altar (like an Israelite's)
BUT the remainder is also burned — NOT eaten
(Unlike an Israelite's, where Kohanim eat the remainder)
Key verse: כׇּל מִנְחַת כֹּהֵן כָּלִיל תִּהְיֶה לֹא תֵאָכֵל
"Every meal offering of a Kohen shall be offered entirely; it shall not be eaten" (Vayikra 6:16)

🔄 Kohen's Meal Offering — Similar But Different

Israelite's Sin-Offering Minchah

  • ✅ Handful removed
  • ✅ Handful burned on altar
  • 🍞 Remainder eaten by Kohanim

Kohen's Sin-Offering Minchah

  • ✅ Handful removed (same as Israelite)
  • ✅ Handful burned on altar
  • 🔥 Remainder also burned — not eaten
"As the meal offering" — the Kohen's rite is like an Israelite's meal offering (handful removed), but not like an Israelite's regarding consumption — the fires of the altar get everything.

📜 The Mishna: Altar Power vs. Kohen Power

🔥 Altar Gets Everything

  • Kohen's personal meal offerings
  • Anointed Kohen's (High Priest) daily meal offering
  • Meal offerings accompanying libations (Nesachim)
  • → None of these go to Kohanim
  • "Power of the altar > power of Kohanim"

🍞 Kohanim Get Everything

  • Two Loaves (Shavuot)
  • Lechem HaPanim (Shewbread)
  • → None of these go to the altar
  • "Power of Kohanim > power of altar"

🍷 What About Burnt Offerings and Wine?

Why doesn't the Mishna list Olah (burnt offering) as "entirely for the altar"?
↓
The Olah's hide goes to the Kohanim — so it's not 100% altar
↓
What about bird burnt offerings (no usable hide)?
↓
The crop and feathers are removed — not placed on the altar
↓
What about wine libations?
↓
Wine goes to the drainpipes (shitin) — not technically "placed on the altar"

🍷 Shmuel's Two Rulings on Donations

Donated Wine

  • Shmuel says: Pour it over the altar fires (like an animal offering)
  • Mishna disagrees: Wine goes to the drainpipes, not burned on altar
  • ⚖️ Mishna excludes Shmuel's view

Donated Oil

  • Shmuel says: Remove a handful (like a meal offering) — remainder eaten by Kohanim
  • Mishna supports this: Oil donation is NOT listed as "entirely for altar"
  • ✅ Mishna confirms Shmuel's ruling on oil

🚫 "Shall Be Offered Entirely — It Shall Not Be Eaten"

R' Eliezer extends the prohibition: The verse "shall be offered entirely; it shall not be eaten" doesn't just apply to the Kohen's own meal offering. Every sacrificial item that belongs in the "shall be offered entirely" category carries a prohibition against eating it.
The prohibition "it shall not be eaten" is linked to "shall be offered entirely"
↓
R' Eliezer: Any sacrificial item meant to be entirely burned on the altar → forbidden to eat
Not just the Kohen's meal offering — also the sacrificial portions (emurim) of any offering
