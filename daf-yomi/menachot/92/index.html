⚔️ R' Yehuda vs. R' Shimon — The Two Communal Cases

Ravina: There is a tradition that exactly two communal offerings require Semikha. Which two?

R' Yehuda

  • 1. 🐂 Bull for erroneous Sanhedrin ruling
  • 2. 🐐 Scapegoat of Yom Kippur
  • ❌ Goats for idol worship — NO Semikha
  • 📖 Source: verse specifies "the bull" — only that bull, excluding goats

R' Shimon

  • 1. 🐂 Bull for erroneous Sanhedrin ruling
  • 2. 🐐 Goats for communal idol worship
  • ⚠️ Scapegoat — Kohen Gadol places hands, but he's not the "owner"
  • 📖 Source: "head of the goat" (Vayikra 4:24) includes idol worship goats
Core Disagreement: R' Shimon says Semikha must be by the owner (the one who achieves atonement). Since the Kohen Gadol isn't personally atoned for by the Scapegoat (in R' Shimon's view), the Scapegoat doesn't count — so the goats for idol worship fill the second slot.

🔮 Yom Kippur Atonement — The Big Picture

Vayikra 16:33 — וְכִפֶּר אֶת מִקְדַּשׁ הַקֹּדֶשׁ... וְעַל הַכֹּהֲנִים וְעַל כׇּל עַם הַקָּהָל יְכַפֵּר
Phrase in the Verse Refers To
"The sanctum of the sacred" 🏛️ The Holy of Holies
"The Tent of Meeting" 🕎 The Sanctuary (Heichal)
"The altar" 🔥 The Mizbeach — literally
"He shall effect atonement" 🏗️ The courtyards (Azarot)
"The priests" 👳 The Kohanim — literally
"All the people" 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 The Israelites
"Shall he bring atonement" 🎵 The Levi'im

R' Yehuda

  • All are equated: Kohanim, Levi'im, and Yisraelim are all atoned by the Scapegoat
  • 💡 Therefore Kohen Gadol IS an "owner" → can do Semikha

R' Shimon

  • Kohanim are atoned by the Kohen Gadol's bull, not the Scapegoat
  • Scapegoat is only for Yisraelim
  • 💡 Therefore Kohen Gadol is NOT an "owner" → Semikha is merely functional

👨‍👦 The Heir's Rights — Semikha & Temurah

RḤ
An heir does NOT place hands, and an heir CANNOT effect Temurah (substitution)! ❌
Rv
But the Mishna says the heir DOES place hands and CAN substitute! 🤔
RḤ
Should I reverse my Baraita to match the Mishna?
Rv
No! The Mishna follows the Rabbanan. Your Baraita follows R' Yehuda. Both are valid positions. 💡

Rabbanan (Mishna's View)

  • ✅ Heir places hands on inherited offering
  • ✅ Heir can effect Temurah
  • 📖 "Hamer yamir" (doubled form) → includes heir
  • 🔗 Derive: if heir can substitute (beginning), he can also do Semikha (end)

R' Yehuda

  • ❌ Heir does NOT place hands
  • ❌ Heir CANNOT effect Temurah
  • 📖 "His offering" → not his father's offering
  • 🔗 Derive: if heir can't do Semikha (end), he can't substitute (beginning)

🔗 The Logic Chain — Beginning vs. End of Kedushah

Temurah (Substitution)
= "Beginning of consecration"
Creates new Kedushah on a non-sacred animal
↕️ linked
Semikha (Placing Hands)
= "End of consecration"
Final step before slaughter
↓
Rabbanan's Direction
Beginning → End
If heir CAN substitute,
then heir CAN place hands
R' Yehuda's Direction
End → Beginning
If heir CAN'T place hands,
then heir CAN'T substitute
The "hamer yamir" debate: The doubled form in Vayikra 27:10 is available for exposition. The Rabbanan use it to include the heir. R' Yehuda uses it to include a woman in the laws of Temurah (since the Torah otherwise speaks in masculine form).

📖 "Korbano" — Three Exclusions

The word "his offering" (korbano) appears three times in Vayikra 3. Each teaches a different exclusion:
"Korbano" #1 (Vayikra 3:2)
→ His offering, not an offering of a gentile
↓
"Korbano" #2 (Vayikra 3:8)
→ His offering, not an offering of another person
↓
"Korbano" #3 (Vayikra 3:13)
→ Includes all co-owners in Semikha requirement
R' Yehuda's alternative reading: He uses one of the three for "not his father's offering" (excluding the heir), and derives gentile + another person from a single verse. This is how he frees up the extra "korbano" for his ruling.
