QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishnah, if a Kohen had Piggul intent during the burning of the Kometz but NOT during the Levonah, what do the Chachamim say?
A. It is Piggul and there is Kares
B. There is no Kares unless he had Piggul intent during the entire Matir
C. The Minchah is completely valid
D. It depends on whether the Kometz was burned first
Correct: B. The Chachamim hold that Piggul requires intent during the ENTIRE Matir (both Kometz and Levonah). Intent during only half the Matir disqualifies the offering but does not create full Piggul with Kares liability.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rav's position regarding a case where the Kohen placed the Kometz with Piggul intent and then placed the Levonah in silence?
A. R' Meir and Chachamim still disagree
B. All agree it is Piggul, because "anyone who acts, acts on his initial intent"
C. All agree it is NOT Piggul
D. It is Piggul only if both were placed by the same Kohen
Correct: B. Rav holds that "kol ha'oseh al da'as rishonah hu oseh" ‚Äî anyone who performs a rite does so according to his initial intent. So the silent Levonah automatically inherits the Piggul intent from the Kometz, and all agree it's Piggul.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does R' Chanina reconcile the Baraisa (which shows the Rabbis disagreeing even when Kometz had intent first) with Rav's position?
A. The Baraisa is discussing a different type of Minchah
B. The Baraisa involves two different Kohanim ‚Äî one burned the Kometz with intent, the other burned the Levonah silently
C. The Baraisa is referring to Piggul for eating, not burning
D. The Baraisa is a minority opinion that we don't follow
Correct: B. R' Chanina explains the Baraisa as a case of "two intentions" ‚Äî two different Kohanim. Kohen #1 burned the Kometz with intent, Kohen #2 burned the Levonah silently. Obviously, the second Kohen's silence cannot inherit the first Kohen's intent.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If the Kohen Gadol had Piggul intent during his blood presentations inside the Kodesh HaKodashim on Yom Kippur, what is the logical problem with subsequent presentations on the Parochet?
A. The Parochet presentations must be done by a different Kohen
B. The blood was already disqualified ‚Äî he's essentially "sprinkling water"
C. Yom Kippur blood cannot become Piggul
D. The Parochet requires a separate animal
Correct: B. Once the blood was disqualified by Piggul intent during the first presentations, it loses its status. Subsequent sprinklings are like "sprinkling water" ‚Äî they can't count as valid Avodah. Rabba solves this with four bulls/goats; Rava says even disqualified blood "effects acceptance" for Piggul purposes.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If one of the two Shavuot lambs was slaughtered with intent to eat the OTHER lamb tomorrow, what is the halacha?
A. Both lambs are Piggul
B. Both lambs are Kosher ‚Äî one Matir cannot create Piggul for another Matir
C. The slaughtered lamb is Piggul, the other is Kosher
D. It depends on whether the loaves were already sanctified
Correct: B. The Mishnah teaches that both lambs are Kosher. The principle is that one permitting factor (Matir) cannot create Piggul for a different permitting factor. Each lamb is an independent Matir for its own portion.
