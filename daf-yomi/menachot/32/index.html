Mezuza Placement: Right Side, Upper Third & the Handbreadth Rule

Where to Place the Mezuza

Shmuel: On the right side of the doorpost as you enter.

The right side is determined by the direction of entry ‚Äî even if the person usually exits from that side.

Height: In the upper third of the doorpost ‚Äî but not at the very top (must be at least a handbreadth from the top).

Two Sheets for a Mezuza?

Shmuel: If one wrote a mezuza on two sheets of parchment, it is invalid.

The entire mezuza must be written on a single piece of parchment ‚Äî both passages of Shema together.

The Handbreadth Adjacent to the Public Domain

Rava: It is a mitzva to place the mezuza in the handbreadth of the doorpost closest to the public domain (the outside).

Why? So that one encounters the mezuza immediately upon entering ‚Äî maximum protection. The Rabbis say: so that the whole house is guarded.

Rooms Exempt from Mezuza

Requires Mezuza

  • Room used for dignified dwelling
  • Has a proper doorframe (two doorposts + lintel)
  • At least 4x4 amot in area
  • Used by both men and women

Exempt

  • Bathhouse, tannery (undignified)
  • Sukkah, ship (temporary)
  • Gatehouse (not for dwelling)
  • Less than 4x4 amot
