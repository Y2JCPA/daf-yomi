Menachot 45

What Bonds the Loaves to the Sheep — Waving or Slaughter?

מַה קוֹבֵעַ — הָנָפָה אוֹ שְׁחִיטָה?

2 / 7

The Bond Question

On Shavuot, once the loaves and sheep are bound together (me'akev each other), losing one means losing both.

The core question: What action creates the bond?
• Is it the waving (tenufah) of the sheep with the loaves?
• Or is it the slaughter (shechita) of the sheep?
3 / 7

R. Yochanan's Position

R. Yochanan: Slaughter establishes the bond.

Once the sheep are slaughtered, the loaves are bound to them — losing one after slaughter means both are lost and new ones must be brought.

Ulla raises a dilemma: does waving establish the bond? R. Yochanan's view suggests it doesn't.
4 / 7

The Possible Bond Stages

Loaves and sheep brought to the Temple
↓
They are waved together (tenufah)
↓
If waving = bond
Losing sheep after waving → loaves also lost
If slaughter = bond
Losing sheep after waving (before slaughter) → loaves can still be brought with new sheep
↓
Sheep are slaughtered → definitive bond formed (R. Yochanan)
5 / 7

Sheep Lost After Waving — What Happens?

If sheep are lost BEFORE slaughter (after waving):
No dilemma — they clearly require waving again. Bring new sheep, wave with the loaves again.

If sheep are lost AFTER slaughter:
The bond is formed (R. Yochanan). The loaves cannot be offered. Must bring new loaves AND new sheep.
6 / 7

R. Yirmeya's Clarification

R. Yirmeya: Don't raise a dilemma about sheep lost after waving — obviously they need waving again with new sheep.

The real dilemma is: if both loaves AND sheep are waved together and then the sheep are lost — can the loaves themselves be offered with new sheep, or must new loaves also be brought?
7 / 7

Key Takeaways — Menachot 45

1. The bond between the two loaves and two sheep of Shavuot is created at a specific point.

2. R. Yochanan: slaughter creates the bond (not waving).

3. Before slaughter: losing sheep → bring new sheep and wave again; loaves may survive.

4. After slaughter: bond is complete — lose either and you lose both.

5. R. Yirmeya: the real question is whether the loaves themselves can be re-used with new sheep, or if new loaves are also needed.
