🐄 Does the Offspring of a Thanks Offering Need Loaves?

Context: A thanks offering animal has an offspring. The offspring is also consecrated and may be sacrificed. Does it require the 40 accompanying loaves?
Case 1: Offspring of a voluntary thanks offering — needs loaves?
↓
✅ Both before AND after the owner brought the mother — offspring needs loaves. It's treated as an additional voluntary thanks offering (increasing thanks).
↓
Case 2: Offspring of an obligatory thanks offering — needs loaves?
↓
Before mother brought
✅ Needs loaves — owner not yet atoned
After mother brought
❌ No loaves — offspring is now "leftover of the thanks offering"

🎓 R' Yochanan's Teaching — Atonement With an Enhancement

R' Yochanan's principle (via Rav Chanina's letter): A person achieves atonement even with an "enhancement" of the original consecrated animal — meaning the offspring of a consecrated animal.
If someone separates an obligatory thanks offering and the offspring is sacrificed first → owner achieves atonement with the offspring
↓
Then when the mother is later sacrificed → it no longer needs loaves (the obligation is already fulfilled)
↓
This teaches: even a "derivative" of the original offering can discharge the obligation

🔄 Rava's Three-Replacement Case

The case: Someone separates Animal A for a thanks offering. A is lost → separates Animal B. B is lost → separates Animal C. Then A and B are both found alive. Now all three animals (A, B, C) stand before you. What is the halacha?
If owner achieves atonement with A (the first):
B = no loaves (replacement of A which was used)
C = no loaves (replacement of B)
↓
If owner achieves atonement with B:
A = no loaves (the original, now displaced by B)
C = no loaves (replacement of B which was used)
↓
If owner achieves atonement with C:
A = no loaves. B = no loaves. C = the one that fulfilled the obligation — gets loaves.

📖 "Loaves Are Called a Thanks Offering" — One Direction Only

Rav Kahana's derivation: "Then he shall offer with the sacrifice of thanks offering — loaves" (Vayikra 7:12)
↓
The juxtaposition teaches: loaves are called a "thanks offering"
→ Money separated for a thanks offering may be used to purchase its loaves
↓
Does the reverse work too? If you separate money for loaves — can you use leftover money for the animal?
↓
❌ No — the loaves are called a thanks offering, but the thanks offering animal is not called "loaves." The relationship is one-directional.

📋 Menachot 80 — Summary Map

Central Theme: Thanks offering derivatives — offspring, replacements, and their loaf requirements
↓
Offspring: voluntary = always needs loaves; obligatory = needs loaves only if owner not yet atoned.
Money: thanks offering money can buy loaves; loaf money cannot buy the animal. One-way rule.
↓
🎓 Key Takeaway: Atonement drives the loaf requirement. Once the owner achieves atonement — whether through the original animal, its offspring, or a replacement — the remaining animals no longer need accompanying loaves.
