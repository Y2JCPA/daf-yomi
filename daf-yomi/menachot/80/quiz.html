QUESTION 1 OF 5
For offspring of a voluntary thanks offering, when do they require accompanying loaves?
A. Only if sacrificed before the original mother offering
B. Only if sacrificed after the original mother offering
C. Both before and after — they are treated as additional voluntary thanks offerings
D. Never — offspring are always "leftovers" and don't require loaves
Correct: C. Offspring of a voluntary thanks offering are considered an additional voluntary offering ("increasing thanks"). Therefore, whether sacrificed before or after the original mother, they require loaves.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
For offspring of an obligatory thanks offering, when do they require loaves?
A. Never, because the obligation applies only to the original mother
B. Only if sacrificed before atonement is achieved with the mother
C. Only if sacrificed after atonement is achieved with the mother
D. Always, just like offspring of voluntary offerings
Correct: B. If sacrificed before atonement, the offspring can function in the obligation and needs loaves. If sacrificed after atonement, it is treated as a leftover of the thanks offering and does not require loaves.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rav Kahana derives that "loaves are called a thanks offering." What practical law follows from this?
A. Money separated for loaves can always be used for any peace offering
B. Money separated for the thanks offering animal may be used for loaves
C. Money separated for loaves must be burned if unused
D. The loaves can be eaten outside Jerusalem like ordinary bread
Correct: B. Since the verse links "thanks offering" and "loaves," loaves are halachically included under the category of thanks offering. So leftover funds designated for the thanks offering may be used to buy loaves.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If money was specifically separated for loaves and some remained, can it be used to buy the thanks offering animal?
A. Yes — because the animal is also called "loaves" in the same verse
B. Yes — but only with Rabbinic approval
C. No — loaves are called a thanks offering, but a thanks offering is not called loaves
D. No — because money for loaves automatically loses sanctity if unused
Correct: C. The relationship is one-directional. The verse teaches loaves are included under "thanks offering," but not the reverse. Therefore, money designated for loaves cannot be redirected to purchase the animal.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In Rava's case of three replacements (A lost → B; B lost → C; then A and B found), what determines which animal requires loaves?
A. Always the first animal (A), since it was original
B. Always the last replacement (C), since it was most recent
C. Any one of the three can be brought with loaves, and the others must be burned
D. The one used for atonement is the operative thanks offering; others become replacements/leftovers without loaves
Correct: D. Atonement is the deciding factor. Whichever animal is actually used to achieve atonement is treated as the primary offering (with loaves). The others are derivative replacements/leftovers and do not require loaves.
