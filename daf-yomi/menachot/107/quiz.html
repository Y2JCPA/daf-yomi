Menachot 107 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the minimum wood donation a person can vow to the Temple altar?
A. 1 log
B. 2 logs
C. 5 logs
D. Any amount — there is no minimum for wood
Correct: B — 2 logs. The minimum wood donation to the Temple is 2 logs. This represents the smallest meaningful bundle — enough to make a real contribution to the altar fire. One log alone was considered insufficient.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the minimum amount of frankincense for a voluntary frankincense vow?
A. A full half-log
B. One Kometz (handful)
C. Two Komatzot (handfuls)
D. The same as the Shewbread bowls combined
Correct: B — One Kometz (handful). The minimum for a voluntary frankincense vow is one handful. This mirrors the Kometz burned from the Shewbread bowls on Shabbat — the standard unit of frankincense in Temple service.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was the "Tzinorah" (raven-eliminator) in the Temple?
A. A guard posted to watch for ravens entering the courtyard
B. Iron spikes on the Temple roof to prevent birds from landing and leaving droppings
C. A special incense burned to repel birds
D. A net stretched over the Temple entrance
Correct: B. The Tzinorah was a device — iron spikes — on the Temple roof to prevent ravens and other birds from landing. Bird droppings on sacred surfaces were a serious concern. Someone who vowed a "Tzinorah" was obligated to provide the gold/iron for this Temple maintenance item.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why do the Sages set minimum amounts for Temple donations at all?
A. To raise more money for the Temple treasury
B. To ensure vows are meaningful and dignified — preventing obligations for essentially worthless amounts
C. Because small donations cannot be processed by the Temple administration
D. To discourage people from making vows they might not fulfill
Correct: B. Minimums ensure dignity on both sides. Without them, someone could vow "one grain of flour" — technically creating a real obligation for something meaningless, causing embarrassment for both the donor and the Temple. Each minimum represents the smallest "useful" unit of that item.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The Yom Kippur service requires the Kohen Gadol to bring frankincense into the Holy of Holies. How does this differ from the standard frankincense Kometz?
A. The Yom Kippur frankincense is a full half-log, not a Kometz
B. The Yom Kippur frankincense is a double-handful (Chofnayim) held in both hands — to create a smoke cloud covering the Ark
C. They are identical amounts — one Kometz
D. The Yom Kippur frankincense is measured by weight, not volume
Correct: B. On Yom Kippur, the Kohen Gadol brings a "Chofnayim" (double-handful, both palms full) of frankincense into the Holy of Holies. This larger amount creates enough smoke to form a cloud that covers the Kapporet (Ark cover), as the Torah requires. This is a unique exception to the normal single-Kometz measure.
