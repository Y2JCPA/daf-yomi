QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the Torah source for prohibiting honey on the altar?
A. 'No leaven nor any honey shall you offer as a fire-offering to the Lord' (Lev. 2:11)
B. A tradition from Moshe at Sinai
C. An inference from the prohibition of leaven alone
D. The laws of terumah
Correct: A. Leviticus 2:11 explicitly states 'no leaven nor any honey shall you offer as a fire-offering,' providing a clear Torah prohibition for offering honey on the altar.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Although honey is forbidden on the altar, where may it be brought?
A. As an offering of first fruits to the Temple
B. It may never enter the Temple at all
C. As part of the incense offering
D. Only on Yom Kippur
Correct: A. The verse teaches that while honey cannot be offered on the altar as a fire-offering, it may be brought to the Temple as part of the first-fruit offerings (bikkurim).

QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the dispute about piggul regarding the burning of frankincense on a meal offering?
A. Whether wrong intent during frankincense burning renders the meal offering piggul
B. Whether frankincense can be offered separately
