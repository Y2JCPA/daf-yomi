QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the phrase "that you shall bring to the Lord" exclude from the leavening prohibition?
A. Meal offerings of non-Jews
B. Disqualified meal offerings
C. Meal offerings brought with libations
D. The shewbread
Correct: B. "That you shall bring to the Lord" limits the prohibition to fit meal offerings. A disqualified meal offering (e.g., one that left the courtyard) is excluded from the leavening prohibition.

Why are the two loaves of Shavuot permitted even though they contain leaven?
A. The prohibition against leaven was suspended on Shavuot
B. The two loaves are waved but not burned on the altar ‚Äî and the prohibition is specifically against altar-burning
C. The two loaves are a rabbinic, not Torah, requirement
D. Wheat leaven is permitted; only barley leaven is forbidden
Correct: B. The prohibition states "you shall not burn any of it as a fire-offering to the Lord." The two loaves contain leaven and are waved before God, but they are never placed on the altar fire. Since the prohibition is specifically about burning on the altar, the leavened loaves are permitted.
