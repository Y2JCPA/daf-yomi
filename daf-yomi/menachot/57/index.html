Frankincense as a Permitting Act

  • Most voluntary meal offerings require a portion of frankincense placed alongside them.
  • The frankincense is one of the matirin (permitting acts): it must be burned to "permit" the priests to eat the remainder.
  • Piggul dispute: if a priest burns the frankincense with intent to eat the offering beyond its time, does this create piggul on the entire meal offering?
  • Since frankincense is an essential permitting act, many hold that improper intent during its burning can indeed invalidate the offering as piggul.
