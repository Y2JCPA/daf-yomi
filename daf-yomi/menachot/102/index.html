🚫 Can an Olah Have Piggul?

The Olah is entirely burned on the altar — no one eats it. Can there be Piggul intent (intent to eat beyond the time)?
CH
Chachamim: YES! Even though the Olah is not eaten by people, it is "consumed" by the altar. Intent to burn it beyond the designated time on the altar = Piggul. The altar's "consumption" is like human eating.
RS
R' Shimon: NO. Piggul only applies to offerings that have both a "Matir" (permitting element) AND "Nittar" (permitted portion). The Olah's blood permits it for the altar — but there's no human eating. No human consumption = no Piggul.
The core dispute: Is the altar's "eating" (burning) treated like human eating for purposes of Piggul? Chachamim say yes; R' Shimon says the concept of Piggul was only instituted for offerings where a person could improperly consume leftovers.

🍞 Impure Meal Offering — Before vs. After Kemitzah

A meal offering becomes ritually impure...
↓
Before Kemitzah
The Kometz hasn't been taken yet
No "permitting act" has occurred
❌ The entire offering is invalidated
Cannot perform Kemitzah on impure flour
After Kemitzah
The Kometz was taken while pure
The "permitting act" already happened
⚠️ The Kometz can still be burned
The Tzitz (High Priest's headplate) atones for impurity
The Tzitz principle: The golden headplate of the Kohen Gadol, inscribed "Holy to the Lord," has the power to make impure offerings acceptable (in certain cases). It atones for the impurity of the offering — but only AFTER the permitting act (Kemitzah) has been properly performed.

🔥 The Olah — Unique Among Offerings

What Makes Olah Special

  • 🔥 Entirely burned — "Kalil" (complete offering to God)
  • 🩸 Blood sprinkled like other offerings
  • 🍖 Skin goes to the Kohen (only non-burned part)
  • ⏰ Can be burned all night (until dawn)
  • 📖 Most ancient offering — Noach's first sacrifice

Why Piggul Is Debated

  • ❓ No human eats the meat — only the altar "consumes" it
  • 📖 Piggul literally means "abomination" — relating to eating
  • ⚖️ Chachamim: altar burning = equivalent to eating
  • ⚖️ R' Shimon: eating requires a person, not an altar
  • 💡 Practical difference: does the offerer get karet?

📜 When the Matir Itself Becomes Impure

Q
What if the Kometz (the "handful" that permits the remainder) itself becomes impure?
A
The Tzitz atones! The impure Kometz can still be burned, and the remainder becomes permitted for the Kohanim to eat. Why? Because the Tzitz was given precisely for this purpose — to ensure offerings are not wasted.
Q
But does the Tzitz atone when the Kohen Gadol is NOT wearing it?
RY
R' Yehuda: Only atones while he's wearing it!
R' Shimon: Atones even when not on his forehead — "it shall be on his forehead ALWAYS" = its effect is permanent.
