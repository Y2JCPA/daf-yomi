Context: If something goes wrong after an animal is slaughtered — does that affect whether its accompanying loaves were consecrated at the moment of slaughter?

🔎 R' Yehuda's Resolution

R' Meir's Baraita: R' Eliezer and R' Yehoshua dispute about blemished animals — one says consecrated, one says not.
↓
R' Yehuda clarifies: They do NOT actually disagree about blemished animals — both agree: not consecrated.
↓
The real dispute is about: slaughtered with intent to eat outside its area (chutz limkomo)
↓
R' Eliezer
✅ Consecrated — "outside its area" is a disqualifying thought similar to "beyond its time" which also consecrates
R' Yehoshua
❌ Not consecrated — unlike "beyond its time" which is a serious piggul violation, "outside its area" only results in invalidity (pasul), not piggul

🩸 "Blood Ready to Be Sprinkled = As If Sprinkled"

R' Shimon's principle: Any blood that stands ready to be sprinkled is considered as if it were already sprinkled.
What if the blood was collected in the cup but then spilled before being sprinkled?
↓
R' Shimon: Once blood is fit to be sprinkled, halachically it is treated as already sprinkled
↓
✅ The loaves ARE consecrated — the blood's readiness is sufficient
↓
R' Elazar b"R Shimon follows his father's principle in a related ruling

🔁 Libations Transferred to Another Offering

If libations were separated for one offering, but that offering was disqualified — can those libations be used with a different offering?
↓
Mishna: Yes — sacrifice them with another offering
↓
But Rav Chisda said: oil separated for one meal offering is unfit for another! Why should libations be different?
↓
Rabbi Yannai's answer: The court tacitly stipulates regarding libations — "if this offering needs them, good; if not, they go to another offering"
↓
Why doesn't this tacit stipulation apply to oil?
↓
Oil is an integral part of the meal offering itself. Libations are separate accompaniments — different rules apply.
6 / 9

🤔 Why Not Let Libations Go Free (Non-Sacred)?

If the court stipulates conditionally — why not just stipulate that disqualified libations return to non-sacred status entirely?
↓
Rabbinic decree: things dedicated as libations may not simply revert to non-sacred status
↓
They must be used as libations for some offering — just not necessarily the original one they were separated for
The principle: Consecrated items have a "one-way valve" — they can be redirected to another holy purpose, but they cannot become mundane again through a simple stipulation.

📋 Menachot 79 — Summary Map

Central Theme: When do thanks offering loaves become consecrated — and when can consecrated libations transfer?
↓
R' Eliezer vs. R' Yehoshua: "outside its area" intent — consecrates loaves (R'E) vs. doesn't (R'Y). R' Yehuda clarifies what they actually dispute.
Libations: court tacit stipulation allows transfer to another offering. Oil cannot transfer — it's integral to the offering.
↓
🎓 Key Takeaway: Not every defect in an offering prevents the loaves from being consecrated — the type of defect matters. Similarly, dedicated items can be redirected but not freed.
