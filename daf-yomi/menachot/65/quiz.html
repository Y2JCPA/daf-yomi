Menachot 65 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
The Boethusians/Sadducees interpreted "from the morrow of the Shabbat" to mean what?
A. From the day after the first day of Pesach (Yom Tov)
B. From the day after the last day of Pesach
C. From the Sunday during Pesach — "Shabbat" literally means Saturday
D. From the day after Shabbat HaGadol
Correct: C. The Boethusians read "Shabbat" literally as Saturday. So they held the Omer count begins on the Sunday during Pesach, meaning Shavuot would always fall on a Sunday. The Sages interpret "Shabbat" as Yom Tov (the first day of Pesach), so counting begins on the 16th of Nisan regardless of the day of the week.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why was the Omer harvest ceremony performed with such dramatic call-and-response?
A. To fulfill a Torah obligation of public announcement
B. Because of the Boethusians — to publicly demonstrate the correct date
C. To wake up the townspeople for the ceremony
D. Because the Kohen Gadol required witnesses
Correct: B. The Mishna explicitly says: "Why all this? Because of the Boethusians!" The elaborate ceremony — asking and answering each detail three times — was designed as maximum publicity to counter the Sadducee claim that the Omer should only be reaped on Saturday night.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was special about Petachya (who managed bird offerings in the Temple)?
A. He was the only Kohen who could handle birds
B. He could not only speak 70 languages but combine and interpret mixed speech
C. He was the oldest member of the Sanhedrin
D. He was the only one who knew the Name of God
Correct: B. All Sanhedrin members knew 70 languages, but Petachya (identified as Mordechai, called "Bilshan" — Ba'al Lashon) could combine languages and understand mixed dialects that others couldn't parse. This was essential for understanding people from all backgrounds who came to the Temple.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How did R' Yochanan ben Zakkai respond to the old Boethusian who claimed Moshe arranged Shavuot on Sunday because he loved Israel?
A. He agreed it was a nice thought but not binding
B. He cited a verse proving Moshe never controlled the calendar
C. "Fool! If Moshe loved them, why keep them in the desert 40 years?"
D. He excommunicated the Boethusian
Correct: C. R' Yochanan ben Zakkai's devastating reply: if Moshe loved Israel enough to arrange the calendar for their convenience, why did he keep them wandering 40 years? The Torah isn't arranged for convenience — it gives us Halacha based on divine command.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How many times is each question asked (and answered) during the Omer harvest ceremony?
A. Once
B. Twice
C. Three times
D. Seven times
Correct: C — Three times. "Has the sun set?" "Yes! Yes! Yes!" "Shall I reap?" "Yes! Yes! Yes!" Every question is asked three times and answered three times. The Mishna emphasizes this was all for maximum publicity against the Boethusians.
