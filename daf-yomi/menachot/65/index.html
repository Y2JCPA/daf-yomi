📅 When Does the Omer Count Begin?

The biggest calendar dispute between the Pharisees and the Sadducees

Chachamim (Pharisees / Sages) ✅

  • 📖 "From the morrow of the Shabbat" (Vayikra 23:15)
  • 💡 "Shabbat" here = Yom Tov (the first day of Pesach)
  • 📅 Start counting on the 16th of Nisan — always
  • 🗓️ Shavuot can fall on any day of the week
  • ✅ This is the accepted Halacha

Boethusians / Sadducees ❌

  • 📖 "From the morrow of the Shabbat" — literally!
  • 💡 "Shabbat" = Saturday
  • 📅 Start counting the Sunday during Pesach
  • 🗓️ Shavuot would always fall on a Sunday
  • ❌ Rejected by the Oral Torah tradition
וּסְפַרְתֶּם לָכֶם מִמָּחֳרַת הַשַּׁבָּת

🏛️ R' Yochanan ben Zakkai's Arguments

How the Sages proved "Shabbat" = Yom Tov, not Saturday
Proof #1 — Two contradictory verses:
One says "seven complete weeks" (Vayikra 23:15)
One says "fifty days" (Vayikra 23:16)
↓
Resolution: "Complete weeks" (starting Sunday) only happens when Pesach falls on Shabbat. "Fifty days" works regardless. → The count starts from Yom Tov, not always Sunday.
↓
Proof #2 — Moses in the wilderness:
The Torah says count "from the morrow of the Shabbat." If this means Saturday, there's no guarantee Moshe gave the Omer on a Sunday!
↓
Conclusion: "Shabbat" must mean the day of rest = Yom Tov of Pesach. The count always starts the day after.

🤺 The Old Boethusian Fights Back

BT
Moshe Rabbeinu loved the Jewish people! He knew Shavuot is a beautiful holiday, so he arranged it to always fall on Sunday — giving them a two-day weekend! 🎉
RYBZ
If Moshe loved them so much, why did he keep them wandering in the desert for 40 years? 🏜️
BT
My teacher, you dismiss me with that?
RYBZ
Fool! Will our perfect Torah not be as worthy as your frivolous speech? The Torah doesn't arrange holidays for convenience — it gives us Halacha. 📜
The Boethusian argument is emotional, not halachic. R' Yochanan ben Zakkai demolishes it: the Torah's system is precise and principled, not based on what would be "nice." The count begins from Yom Tov — period.

🗣️ Petachya — The Language Genius

A fascinating tangent about the man who managed bird offerings in the Temple
Petachya was appointed over the bird nest offerings (Kinim) in the Beit HaMikdash. People would deposit money, and he'd arrange the correct doves/pigeons for Zavim, Zavot, women after childbirth, and Metzora'im.
Question: Why specifically Petachya? All Sanhedrin members knew 70 languages!
↓
Answer: Petachya didn't just know 70 languages — he could combine and interpret them. He could understand people speaking a mixture of languages, slang, or dialects that others couldn't parse.
↓
Fun fact: Petachya = Mordechai (from Megillat Esther)!
Called "Bilshan" (Nechemiah 7:7) = Ba'al Lashon (master of language)

🌾 The Dramatic Omer Harvest

A public spectacle — specifically to refute the Boethusians
📅 Eve of the 16th of Nisan — the crowd gathers at the designated field
↓
🌅 The emissary calls out: "Has the sun set?"
The crowd: "YES!"
(Repeated for emphasis)
↓
🌙 "Shall I reap?" → "YES!"
"With THIS sickle?" → "YES!"
"Into THIS basket?" → "YES!"
↓
If it's Shabbat, add: "On THIS Shabbat?" → "YES!"
↓
Every question asked THREE times. Every answer shouted THREE times.
🎭 Maximum publicity!
Why all the drama? Because of the Boethusians! They claimed the Omer should only be reaped on Saturday night (motzaei Shabbat). The Sages made the ceremony as public and loud as possible to demonstrate that the Omer is reaped on the night after the first day of Pesach — regardless of what day of the week it falls on.

📜 Megillat Ta'anit — Commemorating Victories

The Sages' wins over the Sadducees were so important, they became minor holidays
Dates Victory Status
1-8 Nisan Established the Tamid (daily offering) over Sadducee objections No eulogizing
8 Nisan – end of Pesach Fixed the correct date for Shavuot (our daf!) No eulogizing
Context: The Sadducees had real power — they sometimes controlled the Temple. When the Pharisees/Sages won these disputes and established the correct practice, it was a major victory for the Oral Torah. These dates were recorded in Megillat Ta'anit as days when fasting and eulogizing are forbidden.

🐑 Bonus: The Tamid Dispute

The Sadducees also argued about the daily offering — the Sages' proof was elegant

Sadducees

  • 🐑 An individual can donate and bring the Tamid (daily offering)
  • 💰 Private funding = private offering
  • 📖 They read "my offering, my bread" (Bamidbar 28:2) as allowing individual sponsorship

Sages

  • 🐑 The Tamid is a communal offering — always
  • 💰 Funded from the Shekalim (communal half-shekel)
  • 📖 "Command the Children of Israel… my offering" — plural! It's the nation's offering
  • ✅ "You shall observe to offer… at its appointed time" — strict communal schedule
