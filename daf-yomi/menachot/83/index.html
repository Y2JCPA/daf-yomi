👨‍⚕️ Communal Peace Offerings — Males Only

Context: On Shavuot, two lambs are brought as communal peace offerings (Shlamim) alongside the Two Loaves. Who may eat them?
Sin offering: "Every male among the priests may eat it" (Vayikra 6:22)
↓
Guilt offering: "Every male among the priests may eat of it" (Vayikra 7:6)
↓
Communal peace offerings: derived from amplified verse "In the most sacred place…every male may eat it" (Bamidbar 18:10)
↓
✅ Communal peace offerings (Shavuot lambs) are eaten only by male Kohanim

🔗 "This Is the Law" — One Verse, Many Offerings

זֹאת תּוֹרַת הָעֹלָה לַמִּנְחָה וְלַחַטָּאת וְלָאָשָׁם וְלַמִּלּוּאִים וּלְזֶבַח הַשְּׁלָמִים
"This is the law of the burnt offering, meal offering, sin offering, guilt offering, inauguration offering, and peace offering" (Vayikra 7:37)
A single verse links all these offerings — meaning they share certain halachic properties
↓
"Sin offering" teaches: whatever absorbs from an offering becomes sanctified (absorbed substance rule)
↓
"Guilt offering" teaches: fetal sac and placenta are NOT sacred even if found inside an offering

🧬 The Absorbed Substance Rule

If one offering substance physically absorbs into something else — does that other thing become sanctified?
↓
Yes — derived from "sin offering" in the omnibus verse. Just as a sin offering sanctifies what absorbs from it, so does every offering listed.
↓
Example: If a piece of non-sacred meat touches sacrificial meat and absorbs some of its substance → that piece is now sanctified and must be treated as sacred
The Tanna dispute: One Tanna derives this from the sin offering verse directly; another derives it from the amplification of Bamidbar 18:10. Both reach the same conclusion — absorbed substance from any offering carries sanctity.

🐄 Fetal Sac and Placenta — Not Sacred

"Guilt offering" teaches: a fetal sac and placenta are NOT sacred within a guilt offering
↓
Reason: A guilt offering is always male — it never has a fetal sac or placenta. So these are structurally excluded.
↓
By extension from the omnibus verse: no offering sanctifies a fetal sac or placenta found inside it
↓
This Tanna therefore holds: offspring of sacrificial animals are NOT sacred — because if they were, a fetal sac would also be sacred.

🐑 Leftover Paschal Offering → Peace Offering

Rav Nachman citing Rabba bar Avuh: From where do we know a leftover Paschal offering (not sacrificed on Passover eve) is brought afterward as a peace offering?
Verse: "You shall sacrifice the Passover offering…of the flock and the herd" (Devarim 16:2)
↓
❓ But a Paschal offering only comes from sheep or goats — not cattle (herd)!
↓
The "herd" reference teaches: a leftover Paschal offering is sacrificed as an offering from both flock AND herd — i.e., a peace offering (which may come from either)

📋 Menachot 83 — Summary Map

Central Theme: Using one verse to derive common rules for multiple offerings
↓
Omnibus verse (Vayikra 7:37): sin offering → absorbed substance; guilt offering → fetal sac/placenta not sacred.
Communal peace offerings (Shavuot): males only. Leftover Pesach → becomes peace offering with all its laws.
↓
🎓 Key Takeaway: The Torah's grouping of offerings in one verse creates a legal unit — rulings about one offering can teach all. A Paschal offering doesn't expire — it transforms.
