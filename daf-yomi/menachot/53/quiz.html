Menachot 53 — Quiz

1. What is the ultimate source that matza is indispensable (not just a mitzva) for menachot?

✅ b) The baraita derives from "מַצָּה תִּהְיֶה" (Lev. 2:5): the word "תִּהְיֶה" establishes matza as an obligation. If it were merely a mitzva, the Torah would have written "מַצָּה הִיא."

2. What was R. Perida's attitude toward lineage?

✅ c) R. Perida said that a man of Torah is worthy; a man of Torah and lineage is worthy; but a man of lineage without Torah — "let fire devour him," because his lineage highlights his failure to follow his ancestors' path.

3. In R. Ezra's "yadid" homily, who is the "yadid" who builds the Temple?

✅ c) Shlomo is the yadid who builds the Temple, as he was called Yedidya (II Samuel 12:25). Binyamin is the yadid in whose portion the Temple stands.

4. In the aggada of Avraham at the Churban, what was the final message of comfort?

✅ b) A Bat Kol declared: "The Lord called your name a leafy olive tree." Just as an olive's purpose is fulfilled at its end (when fruit is picked), so too Israel will ultimately repent and return.

5. According to R. Yochanan, why were the Jewish people compared to an olive tree?

✅ c) R. Yochanan teaches that just as an olive brings forth its oil only by means of crushing and pressing, so too the Jewish people return to righteousness only through suffering.
