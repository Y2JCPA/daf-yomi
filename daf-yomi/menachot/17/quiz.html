QUESTION 1 OF 5
The sharp scholars of Pumbedita hold that "Haktarah is Mefagel Haktarah." What does this mean?
A. Burning the Kometz can never create Piggul
B. Burning the Kometz with intent to burn the Levonah tomorrow creates Piggul
C. Burning on the Mizbeach is always considered eating
D. Only the Levonah can create Piggul, not the Kometz
Correct: B. The Pumbedita scholars hold that burning one Matir component (the Kometz) with Piggul intent regarding another Matir component (the Levonah) does create Piggul, because the intent involves the Levonah directly, making it equivalent to having intent for the full Matir.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Eliezer, if a Kohen removes the handful with intent to eat the Kometz (which is normally burned) the next day, what is the status of the Minchah?
A. Piggul with Kares liability
B. Pasul (disqualified) but no Kares
C. Completely Kosher
D. The Kometz must be burned again
Correct: B. R' Eliezer holds that cross-category intent (eating what's normally burned, or burning what's normally eaten) disqualifies the offering (Pasul), but most views hold this does not create full Piggul with Kares liability.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is R' Eliezer's source (derivation) for disqualifying cross-category intent?
A. A Kal va'Chomer from Chatas
B. The doubled expression "He'akhol Ye'akhel" ‚Äî two different forms of "eating" in the Piggul verse, equating human consumption with Mizbeach consumption
C. A Gezeirah Shavah from Shelamim
D. A tradition (Halacha l'Moshe mi'Sinai)
Correct: B. R' Eliezer derives it from the verse "He'akhol Ye'akhel" (Vayikra 7:18), which uses two different grammatical forms of eating. He reads this as equating consumption by a person with "consumption" by the Mizbeach (burning), so cross-category intent disqualifies.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the significance of Rav Hamnuna's teaching from R' Chanina about the case of double intent (intent to both burn Levonah tomorrow AND eat Shirayim tomorrow)?
A. It proves Haktarah always creates Piggul for Haktarah
B. It reveals the boundaries ‚Äî the Piggul comes from the eating intent, not the burning intent, showing that Haktarah alone doesn't create Piggul for another Haktarah
C. It proves that double intent always makes an offering Kosher
D. It shows that R' Chanina disagrees with all other Amoraim
Correct: B. Rav Hamnuna's insight was that in this double-intent case, the Piggul is created by the eating intent (which everyone agrees works), NOT by the burning-for-burning intent. This demonstrates R' Chanina's position that Haktarah alone cannot create Piggul for another Haktarah.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If the Kohen burns the Kometz and Levonah one sesame seed at a time, each time intending to eat a sesame seed of Shirayim tomorrow, what are the possible outcomes?
A. All agree it is Piggul
B. Three-way machloket: Piggul (manner of consumption IS in bits), Pasul (manner isn't in bits), or Kosher (each intent too small)
C. All agree it is Kosher ‚Äî sesame seeds are too small
D. It depends on whether the total adds up to a k'zayis
Correct: B. There is a three-way machloket. One view says Piggul (since consumption can happen in small bits, the intent is valid). Another says Pasul but not Piggul (the manner of consumption isn't normally in sesame-seed increments). A third says Kosher (each individual intent is too small to count). Notably, this doesn't neatly align with the R' Meir/Chachamim divide.
