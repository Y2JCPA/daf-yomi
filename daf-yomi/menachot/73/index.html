⚖️ No Exchanging Offering Portions

The Baraita proves step-by-step that Kohanim may not swap their portions
StepYou might think…But the verse teaches…
1Swap meal offerings for animal offerings?❌ "Shall all sons of Aaron have, each like the other" (baked in oven)
2Swap meal offerings for bird offerings?❌ "All that is prepared in the deep pan…" — equal shares
3Swap bird offerings for animal offerings?❌ "And on a pan…shall all sons of Aaron have"
4Swap meal offerings for other meal offerings?❌ "Mixed with oil…shall all sons of Aaron have"
5Swap pan for deep pan offerings?❌ "Or dry, shall all sons of Aaron have"
6Swap within lesser-sanctity offerings?❌ "One as well as another" + "if for a thanks offering"
Bottom line: A Kohen must eat the specific portion assigned to him. He cannot trade his meal offering portion for someone else's animal offering portion, or vice versa. The Torah repeats "all sons of Aaron" multiple times to cover every possible swap scenario.

🌍 Gentile Offerings — Treated as Olah

Rav Huna's rule:
Peace offerings volunteered by gentiles are sacrificed as burnt offerings (Olah) — entirely to the altar
↓
Logical argument
A gentile's intent is that the offering should be entirely sacred to Heaven — they don't intend for any to be eaten
Verse
אִישׁ אִישׁ — "Any man" (doubled) includes gentiles
"Which they will offer to the Lord as a burnt offering"

🤔 Challenge: Gentile Peace Offering Given to an Israelite?

❓ Rav Chama bar Gurya objects:
A Baraita says: If a gentile volunteers a peace offering and gives it to an Israelite — the Israelite eats it! So it's NOT treated as Olah?
↓
Rava resolves:
The Baraita means the gentile specified the peace offering should atone for a specific Israelite (or Kohen) who was already obligated to bring one. It's really the Israelite's offering — the gentile is just paying.
↓
✅ When a gentile volunteers his OWN peace offering → treated as Olah
When designated for a specific Jew's obligation → treated as that Jew's offering

⚔️ R' Yosi HaGelili vs. R' Akiva — Gentile Meal Offerings

R' Yosi HaGelili

  • 📌 Kohanim DO eat the remainder of gentile meal offerings
  • 📖 "Ish Ish" includes gentiles for vows and gift offerings
  • 🍞 Handful removed → remainder eaten by Kohanim (like standard meal offering)
  • 🌾 This is the opinion behind the Mishna's listing

R' Akiva

  • 📌 Gentile offerings are treated as Olah — entirely burned
  • 📖 "Which they will offer as a burnt offering" — everything goes to altar
  • 🔥 Even meal offerings from gentiles: entirely consumed by fire
  • ⚡ More restrictive view: Kohanim do NOT eat gentile offerings

📖 "Ish Ish" — Including Gentiles

אִישׁ אִישׁ מִבֵּית יִשְׂרָאֵל וּמִן הַגֵּר בְּיִשְׂרָאֵל

"Any man of the house of Israel, or of the strangers in Israel, that brings his offering…which they will offer to the Lord as a burnt offering" (Vayikra 22:18)
The doubled "Ish Ish" (any man, any man) comes to include gentiles
↓
All agree: Gentiles can bring vow and gift offerings
Dispute: Whether their offerings are eaten or entirely burned
↓
R' Akiva reads: "as a burnt offering" — teaching ALL gentile offerings are burned
R' Yosi HaGelili reads: only animals become burnt offerings; meal offerings follow normal rules
