Menachot 73 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded
QUESTION 1 OF 5
The Baraita derives step-by-step that Kohanim cannot exchange their offering portions. What is the key phrase repeated throughout these derivations?
A. "Holy of holies"
B. "Shall all the sons of Aaron have"
C. "A pleasing aroma to the Lord"
D. "Each according to his family"
Correct: B. The Torah repeats "shall all the sons of Aaron have" (Vayikra 7:9-10) in connection with different types of meal offerings, teaching that each Kohen must receive his equal share of that specific offering — no swapping allowed.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, what happens to peace offerings volunteered by gentiles?
A. They are eaten by the Kohanim like regular peace offerings
B. They are sacrificed as burnt offerings — entirely burned on the altar
C. They are returned to the gentile
D. They are treated as sin offerings
Correct: B. Rav Huna teaches that a gentile's peace offering is treated as an Olah (burnt offering). The reasoning: a gentile's intent is that the entire offering should go to Heaven, and the verse says "which they will offer to the Lord as a burnt offering."
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does the doubled expression "Ish Ish" (any man, any man) in Vayikra 22:18 teach?
A. That only men, not women, may bring offerings
B. That gentiles are included in the ability to bring vow and gift offerings
C. That a person must bring two offerings, not one
D. That both Kohanim and Levi'im may bring personal offerings
Correct: B. The doubled language "Ish Ish" comes to include gentiles, teaching that they too may volunteer vow offerings and gift offerings to the Temple, like a Jew can.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rava resolve the apparent contradiction between Rav Huna's rule (gentile peace offerings = Olah) and the Baraita that says an Israelite eats the gentile's peace offering?
A. The Baraita refers to a different time period (before the Temple was built)
B. The Baraita means the gentile designated the offering to atone for a specific Israelite who was already obligated — making it effectively the Israelite's offering
C. The Baraita is rejected as inaccurate
D. Rav Huna only meant his rule applies to bird offerings, not animal offerings
Correct: B. Rava explains: the Baraita discusses a case where the gentile specifically designated the peace offering to achieve atonement for a particular Israelite (or Kohen) who already had an obligation. In that case, it functions as the Israelite's offering and is eaten normally. Rav Huna's rule applies when the gentile volunteers his own offering.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Akiva, what happens to the remainder of a meal offering brought by a gentile?
A. Kohanim eat it as usual
B. It is returned to the gentile
C. It is entirely burned on the altar, like an Olah
D. It is thrown into the sea
Correct: C. R' Akiva holds that ALL gentile offerings — including meal offerings — are treated like burnt offerings and entirely consumed by fire on the altar. R' Yosi HaGelili disagrees: the Kohanim eat the remainder of a gentile's meal offering, and the Mishna follows his view.
