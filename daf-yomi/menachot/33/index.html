Gatehouse Exemptions, Ceiling Apertures & Tefillin Compartments

Gatehouse & Garden — Exempt?

Rabba & Rav Yosef: A gatehouse that leads only to a garden (not a house) is exempt from mezuza, because it is made for the purpose of reaching the garden, not as a dwelling entrance.

Abaye and Rava followed this ruling in practice — no mezuza on gatehouses leading to gardens.

Ceiling Aperture — Which Direction?

If a house has a ceiling opening (trapdoor) leading to a loft occupied by another person:

The mezuza goes on the right side as one ascends to the loft, since the upper dwelling is the primary one. The person climbing up is "entering" the loft.

Tefillin: How Many Compartments?

Source for 4 compartments in the head tefillin:

The word "טוֹטָפוֹת" (totafot) appears 3 times in the Torah — once without the plural ending (טֹטֶפֶת) and twice with it (טוֹטָפוֹת). The singular = 2 and each plural = 2, totaling: 2 + 2 = 4 compartments.

R. Akiva's alternative: "טוֹט" in Katpi language = 2; "פוֹת" in Afriki language = 2. Total = 4.

One Hide or Four?

Head Tefillin

  • 4 passages on 4 separate pieces of parchment
  • Each placed in its own compartment
  • All compartments must be on a single base

Arm Tefillin

  • 4 passages on 1 piece of parchment
  • Written in order
  • Single compartment
6 / 7

Writing Order & Mistakes

If the scribe wrote the 4 passages on a single hide for the head tefillin (should be 4 separate hides): Invalid.

If he wrote them on 4 separate hides for the arm tefillin (should be 1 hide): Invalid.

The passages must be in order: Exodus 13:1-10, Exodus 13:11-16, Deuteronomy 6:4-9, Deuteronomy 11:13-21.

Key Takeaways — Menachot 33

1. Gatehouse to a garden: exempt from mezuza.

2. Ceiling aperture: mezuza on the right as one ascends.

3. 4 compartments from "totafot" — either through plural counting or R. Akiva's bilingual derivation.

4. Head tefillin: 4 passages on 4 separate parchments. Arm: 4 passages on 1 parchment.

5. Wrong format (single hide for head, or multiple for arm) = invalid.
