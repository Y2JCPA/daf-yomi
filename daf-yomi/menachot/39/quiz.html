Menachot 39 — Quiz

1. According to Beit Hillel, may one put wool tzitzit on a linen garment?

✅ b) Beit Hillel holds the positive mitzva of tzitzit overrides the prohibition of kilayim.

2. Why were people in Jerusalem cautious about this?

✅ b) If the tekhelet turned out to be counterfeit (plant dye instead of chilazon), one would be wearing kilayim without the mitzva justification.

3. Must tzitzit strings be spun for the sake of the mitzva?

✅ b) The spinning must be done for the sake of the mitzva — "ta'aseh v'lo min ha'asui."

4. How did far-flung communities know when Pesach was?

✅ b) Communities relied on letters sent from the Sanhedrin in Eretz Yisrael, even for karet-level prohibitions like chametz on Pesach.

5. According to Beit Shammai, a linen cloak is:

✅ c) Beit Shammai holds a linen cloak is completely exempt from tzitzit because wool strings would create kilayim.
