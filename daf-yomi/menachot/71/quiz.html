Menachot 71 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Which Amora successfully proves that the Omer permits grain from the moment of "taking root"?
A. R' Yoshiya — from "grain in the ear"
B. Shmuel — from "the sickle is put to grain"
C. Rava — from "which you sow in the field"
D. R' Yitzchak — from "the standing grain"
Correct: C — Rava. All other proofs were challenged. Rava derives it from "which you sow in the field" (Shemos 23:16) — "in the field" means the grain became part of the field, i.e., it took root. Rav Pappa challenged this, and Rava responded: "Wise one! It says 'in the field' — meaning it has become part of the field."
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Of the six actions performed by the residents of Jericho, for how many did the Sages protest?
A. All six — the Sages disapproved of everything
B. Three — they protested the last three but tolerated the first three
C. None — the Sages were silent on all six
D. One — only the breaking of garden walls
Correct: B — Three. The Sages did not protest actions 1-3 (grafting palms, wrapping the Shema, reaping before the Omer). They protested actions 4-6 (consecrated branches, breaking walls for the poor on Shabbat, designating pe'ah from vegetables).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Shimon, may one reap grain that has reached one-third of its growth for animal fodder before the Omer?
A. No — once it reaches 1/3 growth it cannot be reaped before the Omer
B. Yes — any reaping for fodder is "not considered reaping" regardless of growth stage
C. Only with permission from Beis Din
D. Only if the animal is consecrated (Hekdesh)
Correct: B. R' Shimon's position is that "any reaping for fodder is not considered reaping" — even grain at 1/3 growth. The Chachamim disagree and say once it reaches 1/3, it's considered real grain that may not be reaped before the Omer.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The Mishna permits reaping before the Omer for several reasons. Which of the following is NOT one of them?
A. To protect saplings growing alongside the grain
B. To create room for the mourners' area
C. To sell the grain at market before prices drop
D. To prevent neglect of Torah study (clearing space for the Beis Midrash)
Correct: C — Selling at market. The Mishna permits reaping before the Omer for: protecting saplings, the mourners' area, preventing neglect of the Beis Midrash, and for animal fodder. Commercial purposes ("your harvest") must wait for the Omer.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Even when reaping before the Omer is permitted, what restriction applies to how the grain is handled afterward?
A. It must be burned immediately
B. It may not be fashioned into sheaves — it must be left loose
C. It must be given to the Kohanim
D. It must be stored in a separate room from other grain
Correct: B. Even though reaping is permitted for these purposes, the Mishna states one may not fashion the grain into sheaves — it must be left unbound. The principle: as much as possible, we minimize involvement with the new grain before the Omer.
