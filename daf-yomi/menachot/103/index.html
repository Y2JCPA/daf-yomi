🗣️ "I Vow a Mincha" — Which Type?

If someone says "I am obligated to bring a Mincha" without specifying which kind — what must they bring?
RY
R' Yehuda: They must bring a Minchat Solet (fine flour offering). Why? Because when a person says "Mincha" without specifying, the first Mincha in the Torah (Lev 2:1) is fine flour — so that's the default.
CH
Chachamim: They can bring ANY of the five types of meal offering. Why? Because ALL are called "Mincha" in the Torah — saying "Mincha" includes them all.
The five types of Mincha:
1️⃣ Solet (fine flour) · 2️⃣ Machabat (griddle) · 3️⃣ Marcheshet (deep pan)
4️⃣ Ma'afeh Tanur — Challot (baked loaves) · 5️⃣ Ma'afeh Tanur — Rekikin (wafers)

📏 Half an Issaron — Too Little?

Someone vows: "I am obligated to bring HALF an Issaron of fine flour."
(Normal Mincha = one full Issaron minimum)
↓
R' Shimon's view
❌ Exempt — half an Issaron is not a valid Mincha
A vow for less than the minimum is a non-vow
Chachamim's view
✅ Must bring a FULL Issaron
We "round up" — the vow is valid but the minimum quantity applies
The principle: Can you vow less than the minimum measure? The Chachamim say: your vow has force, but you must bring at least the minimum. R' Shimon says: your vow simply doesn't work — you can't create an obligation for something that doesn't exist in halacha.

🫒 Oil and Frankincense — Implied or Explicit?

Included by Default

  • 🫒 "I vow a Mincha" → oil IS included
  • 🌿 Frankincense IS included
  • 📖 Because every standard Mincha has oil and frankincense (Lev 2:1-2)
  • ✅ No need to specify — they come automatically

Can Be Explicitly Excluded

  • 🗣️ "I vow a Mincha WITHOUT oil" → valid!
  • 🗣️ "...WITHOUT frankincense" → valid!
  • 📖 There is a precedent: the Sotah offering and the sinner's offering have no oil/frankincense
  • ⚠️ But this creates a non-standard Mincha
Wait — can you REALLY vow a Mincha without oil? The Gemara debates this. Some say: if the Torah itself has Minchot without oil (Sotah, sinner), then a vow without oil can work. Others say: those are obligatory exceptions — a voluntary vow should always include oil because the standard Mincha does.

⚖️ 60 Issaron in One Vessel

The maximum: One can bring up to 60 Issaron of flour in a single Mincha offering — but not more!
Q
Where does the number 60 come from? Why is that the maximum?
A
It's derived from the Nesachim (libation) measures associated with offerings. The largest combination of offerings and their Mincha accompaniments totals 60 Issaron — so that's the maximum a single vessel can handle.
Q
What if someone vows 61 Issaron?
A
They bring 60 in one vessel and 1 in a separate vessel. You cannot mix more than 60 in one batch — each vessel gets its own Kemitzah.

🌾 Barley or Wheat? The Vow Determines

What flour does a voluntary Mincha require?
↓
Default: WHEAT flour (Solet)
All standard voluntary Minchot use wheat
"Solet" in the Torah = fine wheat flour
↓
Exception: "I vow a Mincha of BARLEY"
Can they bring barley? 🤔
The Omer is barley! So barley Minchot exist...
↓
❌ A voluntary barley Mincha is NOT valid
The Omer is an obligatory offering with special rules.
Voluntary offerings must use wheat.
