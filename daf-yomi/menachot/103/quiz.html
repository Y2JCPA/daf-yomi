Menachot 103 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded
QUESTION 1 OF 5
If someone says "I am obligated to bring a Mincha" without specifying the type, what does R' Yehuda say they must bring?
A. Any of the five types of Mincha
B. Minchat Solet (fine flour offering) — the first Mincha mentioned in the Torah
C. A Machabat (griddle) offering — the most common type
D. They are exempt — a vow must be specific
Correct: B. R' Yehuda rules that an unspecified "Mincha" vow means Minchat Solet (fine flour), because that is the first Mincha described in the Torah (Lev 2:1) — making it the "default" meal offering. The Chachamim disagree and say any of the five types would fulfill the vow.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the maximum number of Issaron (measures of flour) that can be placed in a single vessel for a Mincha?
A. 10
B. 24
C. 60
D. There is no maximum
Correct: C — 60 Issaron. This number is derived from the maximum combination of Nesachim (libation) measures associated with offerings. If someone vows 61 Issaron, they bring 60 in one vessel and 1 in a separate vessel — each with its own Kemitzah.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Someone vows "I will bring a Mincha of barley flour." Is this valid as a voluntary offering?
A. Yes — the Omer proves that barley Minchot exist
B. Yes — any grain can be used for voluntary offerings
C. No — voluntary Minchot must use wheat flour; barley is only for the obligatory Omer
D. Only if they add extra oil to compensate
Correct: C. Voluntary meal offerings must use wheat flour (Solet). While barley exists in the Temple service (the Omer offering), it is an obligatory offering with special rules. A person cannot voluntarily vow a barley Mincha.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If someone vows a Mincha, are oil and frankincense automatically included?
A. Yes — they are part of every standard Mincha by default
B. No — they must be explicitly mentioned
C. Only oil is included; frankincense must be specified
D. Neither is included unless the person specifies both
Correct: A. Oil and frankincense are automatically included in a standard Mincha vow, since the Torah describes them as components of every Mincha (Lev 2:1-2). However, a person CAN explicitly exclude them ("a Mincha without oil"), since precedents exist (Sotah offering, sinner's Mincha).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Chachamim, if someone vows "half an Issaron" for a Mincha, what must they bring?
A. Half an Issaron — exactly as vowed
B. Nothing — the vow is invalid
C. A full Issaron — the minimum measure applies
D. Two Issaron as a penalty for the improper vow
Correct: C. The Chachamim hold that the vow has force, but the minimum quantity still applies. So the person must bring a full Issaron. R' Shimon disagrees and says the vow simply doesn't work — you can't vow something below the halachic minimum.
