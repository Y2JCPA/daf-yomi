Menachot 93 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, which of the following people CAN perform Semikha on an offering?
A. A Shaliach (agent) acting on the owner's behalf
B. An adult male Yisrael who owns the offering
C. A woman who owns her own offering
D. A blind adult male who owns the offering
Correct: B — An adult male Yisrael who owns the offering. The Mishna lists eight categories excluded from Semikha: deaf-mute, imbecile, minor, blind, gentile, slave, agent, and woman. A competent adult Jewish male who personally owns the offering can and must perform Semikha.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
The word "yado" (his hand) appears three times in Vayikra chapter 3. Which three people are excluded through these three repetitions?
A. Slave, agent, and wife
B. Minor, imbecile, and deaf-mute
C. Gentile, blind, and agent
D. Heir, agent, and gentile
Correct: A — Slave, agent, and wife. Each mention of "yado" excludes one person: (1) not the slave's hand; (2) not the agent's hand; (3) not the wife's hand. Each exclusion is more surprising than the last, building toward the wife who is "like his own flesh."
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Is Semikha essential (me'akev) — does omitting it invalidate the offering?
A. Yes — the offering is completely invalid without Semikha
B. Yes — but only if the owner is present in the Azarah
C. No — the offering atones even if Semikha was not done, though the owner is blamed
D. No — Semikha has no halachic significance at all
Correct: C — Non-essential, but blame is incurred. The Gemara derives from the verse: atonement comes through the blood, not the hands. If someone treats Semikha as optional and skips it, the verse ascribes blame as if he didn't atone — but the korban still atones. The same rule applies to Tenufah (waving).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If multiple people jointly own an offering, how many of them must perform Semikha?
A. Only one — like Tenufah (waving), where one partner acts for all
B. All of them — "korbano" includes all co-owners
C. The majority of them suffices
D. Whichever one is the kohen
Correct: B — All co-owners must perform Semikha. Despite an attempted kal va'chomer that would exempt all but one partner (as with Tenufah), the verse "korbano" teaches that all co-owners must each place their hands. Tenufah is different because having multiple people wave simultaneously creates a chatzitzah (interposition).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The Gemara notes that "upon the head" appears three times in Vayikra chapter 3. What do these three repetitions teach?
A. That Semikha must be performed three separate times
B. That it applies to three different types of Shelamim
C. That Semikha is invalid if done on the neck, back, or breast
D. That Semikha requires three Kohanim to validate it
Correct: C — Not neck, back, or breast. Each "upon the head" excludes a different body part: not the neck, not the back, not the breast. All three exclusions are needed because each part is progressively close to the head, and without each explicit exclusion we might think it is a valid alternative.
