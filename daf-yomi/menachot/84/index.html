🌾 Omer and Two Loaves — Eretz Yisrael Only

The rule: Both the Omer (barley, 16 Nisan) and the Two Loaves (wheat, Shavuot) must come from grain grown in Eretz Yisrael. This is the standard opinion.
Verse: "When you come into the land which I give to you, and you shall reap its harvest" (Vayikra 23:10)
↓
The grain for these communal offerings must come from that same land — not from outside Israel
↓
All agree: if brought from Eretz Yisrael → valid. From outside → invalid. This is NOT in accordance with R' Yosei b"R Yehuda.
3 / 9

🌍 R' Yosei b"R Yehuda: Omer From Outside Israel

R' Yosei's position: The Omer may come from outside Eretz Yisrael too
↓
How he reads "when you come into the land": this only teaches WHEN the obligation begins (upon entering), not WHERE the grain must come from
↓
Why? He holds chadash (new grain) is forbidden outside Israel by Torah law, based on "from all your dwellings" (Vayikra 23:17)
↓
If chadash is biblically forbidden even outside Israel → logically, the Omer that permits it should also work from outside Israel

🛡️ Guards of Shemittah Grain — Who Pays?

Background: During the Sabbatical Year (Shemittah), grain may grow on its own (sefiḥin). The court appoints guards to protect some of this volunteer growth for use as the Omer and Two Loaves.
❓ Who pays the guards — the community or the Temple treasury?
↓
Mishna (Shekalim 4:1): They are paid from the Temple treasury chamber collection
↓
❓ Rami bar Chama challenges: The Mishna implies sefiḥin grain IS set aside for the Omer — but why? Is sefiḥin even halachically usable?
↓
Resolution: The guards are appointed as a safeguard — but ideally the Omer comes from specifically sown crops, not volunteer growth

🍇 First Fruits From Inferior Locations

Ulla / R' Yochanan

  • First fruits from inferior locations (valleys, dates from mountain areas) are NOT consecrated
  • Even if brought — they don't attain Bikkurim status
  • Produce on a flowerpot or ship: don't bring at all
  • R' Yochanan's position

Reish Lakish

  • Even inferior-location first fruits CAN be consecrated
  • Produce on a roof or in a ruin: bring AND recite
  • More lenient position
  • Tanna'im dispute this too

📖 Two Loaves — "New" Means First Among All

Verse: "A first offering to the Lord" (Vayikra 2:11) and "when you bring a new meal offering" (Bamidbar 28:26)
↓
Two Loaves must be the FIRST meal offering from the new crop — precedes all other new-grain offerings
↓
The word "new" appears twice → teaches: first of wheat offerings AND first of barley offerings (e.g., sota barley)
↓
Two Loaves also precede first fruits (bikkurim) — "festival of Shavuot, the first fruits of the wheat harvest" — festival comes before its fruits

📋 Menachot 84 — Summary Map

Central Theme: Geographic and quality requirements for communal offerings
↓
Omer/Two Loaves: from Eretz Yisrael (standard). R' Yosei b"R Yehuda: Omer valid from outside too — because chadash is Torah-prohibited even there.
Two Loaves must be first of all new-grain offerings. Bikkurim from inferior locations: R' Yochanan says not consecrated; Reish Lakish says they can be.
↓
🎓 Key Takeaway: The geography and quality of the Omer's source grain affects major halachot about chadash. The stricter you are about where chadash applies, the more flexibility you have about where the Omer comes from.
