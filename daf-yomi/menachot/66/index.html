📅 The Great Debate: When Do We Count?

The Torah says: וּסְפַרְתֶּם לָכֶם מִמׇּחֳרַת הַשַּׁבָּת

"You shall count for yourselves from the morrow after the Shabbat" (Vayikra 23:15)

But what does "Shabbat" mean here?

Chachamim (Our Tradition)

  • 📌 "Shabbat" = Yom Tov (first day of Pesach)
  • 📅 Count starts: 16 Nisan (always)
  • 🌾 Omer brought on 16 Nisan every year
  • ✅ Fixed calendar date

Boethusians (Tzedukim)

  • 📌 "Shabbat" = literal Shabbat during Pesach
  • 📅 Count starts: Sunday after Shabbat of Pesach
  • 🌾 Omer date changes yearly
  • ❌ Shavuot always falls on Sunday

📖 The Proofs: "Shabbat" = Yom Tov

# Tanna Proof
1 R' Yishmael "Count 50 days" — if from Shabbat, the count would never be exactly 50 from Pesach
2 R' Yehuda ben Beseira "Tispar Lach" — counting depends on Beis Din, who set Yom Tov, not Shabbat (which everyone knows)
3 R' Yosi "Shabbat" appears at the end of the count (Shavuot) meaning Regel — so it means Regel at the start too
4 R' Shimon b. Elazar "Six days eat matzah" vs "seven days" — resolved only if Omer is on 16 Nisan (new grain permitted for 6 remaining days)
5 R' Yosi b'R' Yehuda Derives from multiple verses that reaping is at night, counting at night, offering by day — only works with fixed date
Rava's assessment: All proofs can be challenged except the last two Tanna'im in each Baraita — R' Yishmael and R' Yehuda ben Beseira (in Baraita #1), and R' Shimon ben Elazar and R' Yosi b'R' Yehuda (in Baraita #2).

How do we know this sequence?
• מֵהָחֵל חֶרְמֵשׁ בַּקָּמָה תָּחֵל לִסְפֹּר — "From when the sickle is put to grain, begin to count" → Reaping and counting happen together
• שֶׁבַע שַׁבָּתוֹת תְּמִימֹת — "Seven complete weeks" → Must start counting at nightfall (for complete weeks)
• מִיּוֹם הֲבִיאֲכֶם — "From the day you bring" → The offering is brought during the day

🔢 Abaye: Count Days AND Weeks

Ab
There's a mitzvah to count the days and a separate mitzvah to count the weeks!
Q
Where does Abaye learn this from?
Ab
One verse says "count 50 days" and another says "count seven weeks" — both are mitzvot!
Practical impact: This is why we say each night: "Today is X days, which is Y weeks and Z days of the Omer." We count both the total days and the weeks — fulfilling both mitzvot per Abaye.
מִצְוָה לְמִימְנֵי יוֹמֵי וּמִצְוָה לְמִימְנֵי שָׁבוּעֵי

🔥 Parching the Omer Grain

The Torah says: אָבִיב קָלוּי בָּאֵשׁ גֶּרֶשׂ כַּרְמֶל — "Grain in the ear, parched with fire, groats of the fresh ear" (Vayikra 2:14)
🤔 What does "Kali" (parched) mean?
Direct fire? Or something else?
↓
Answer: "Kali" = Kalil (burnished)
A perforated brass vessel, like a sieve
Fire reaches all the grain evenly through the holes
↓
✅ Grain parched inside a vessel — not directly in fire
The verse "interrupted the matter" — parching applies to the stalks, not the ground groats
"Karmel" = Rakh u'Mal (soft and malleable)
The Omer grain must be fresh and tender — not dry or hard. The Gemara brings several examples of compressed words (notarikon) to prove this interpretation.

⚖️ Which Verse Teaches What?

Verse Teaches
מִמׇּחֳרַת הַשַּׁבָּת Count from the day after Yom Tov (16 Nisan)
תִּסְפֹּר לָךְ Counting depends on Beis Din (not automatic like Shabbat)
שֶׁבַע שַׁבָּתוֹת תְּמִימֹת Start at night — for "complete" weeks
מִיּוֹם הֲבִיאֲכֶם The offering is brought by day
מֵהָחֵל חֶרְמֵשׁ בַּקָּמָה Reaping and counting happen together (at night)
שִׁשֶּׁת יָמִים / שִׁבְעַת יָמִים Omer on 16 Nisan — new grain permitted for 6 of 7 Pesach days
