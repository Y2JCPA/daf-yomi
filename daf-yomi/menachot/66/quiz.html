QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Boethusians, what does "mi'macharas ha'Shabbat" mean when determining when to start counting the Omer?
A. The day after the first day of Pesach (16 Nisan)
B. The day after the literal Shabbat that falls during Pesach (always a Sunday)
C. The day after Shavuot
D. The day after the last day of Pesach
Correct: B. The Boethusians (a sect aligned with Tzedukim views) held that "Shabbat" in the verse means the literal Shabbat within Pesach — meaning the Omer is always brought on a Sunday, and Shavuot always falls on Sunday. The Chachamim rejected this entirely.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
R' Yehuda ben Beseira proves that "Shabbat" means Yom Tov (not literal Shabbat) from the word "Tispar lach" (count for yourselves). What does he derive?
A. Each individual must count personally, not rely on others
B. The count depends on Beis Din, who set the calendar — unlike Shabbat which everyone knows
C. The count must be done privately, not publicly
D. The count begins when you personally feel ready
Correct: B. "Lach" indicates the count depends on Beis Din (who set the months and holidays). This rules out literal Shabbat — because everyone knows when Shabbat is, without needing Beis Din. Since the count requires Beis Din, "Shabbat" must refer to Yom Tov.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Abaye teach about the mitzvah of Sefirat HaOmer?
A. It is only a rabbinic obligation today
B. There is a mitzvah to count the days and a separate mitzvah to count the weeks
C. Only the weeks must be counted, not the individual days
D. The count must begin during the day, not at night
Correct: B. Abaye teaches: "Mitzvah l'mimni yomei u'mitzvah l'mimni shavuei" — it is a mitzvah to count the days AND a mitzvah to count the weeks. This explains why our nightly counting includes both the total day count and the week count (e.g., "today is 22 days, which is 3 weeks and 1 day").
QUESTION 4 OF 5
From which verse do we derive that the Omer must be reaped and counted at night, while the offering is brought during the day?
A. A single verse that explicitly states "at night"
B. The interplay of three verses: "from when the sickle is put to grain," "seven complete weeks," and "from the day you bring"
C. A tradition from Moshe at Sinai (Halacha l'Moshe miSinai)
D. The practice of the Kohanim in the Beis HaMikdash
Correct: B. The three verses work together: "Me'Hachel Chermesh baKamah" links reaping to counting (both done together at night). "Sheva Shabbatot Temimot" (complete weeks) requires starting at nightfall. "Mi'Yom Havi'achem" (from the day you bring) means the offering itself is brought by day.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How was the Omer grain parched? What does the Gemara explain about the process?
A. Thrown directly into an open fire
B. Boiled in water until soft
C. Placed in a perforated burnished brass vessel, so fire could reach it evenly through the holes
D. Dried in the sun for three days before being ground
Correct: C. The word "Kali" (parched) is interpreted as related to "Kalil" (burnished/entirely). The Temple used a hollow brass vessel perforated like a sieve, so the fire could take hold of the grain evenly. The grain was not directly in the flame — it was parched inside the vessel. The verse also indicates it was the stalks that were parched, not the ground groats.
