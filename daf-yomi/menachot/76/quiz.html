QUESTION 1 OF 5
How does one perform the 300 rubbings and 500 strikings? What is the pattern?
A. 300 rubbings all at once, then 500 strikes all at once
B. Rub 1× then strike 2×, rub 2× then strike 3× — repeated 100 times
C. Alternate rub and strike in equal numbers until both totals are reached
D. Strike 5× then rub 3× — repeated until done
Correct: B. A Baraita teaches the precise sequence: rub once and strike twice; rub twice and strike three times. This cycle repeats 100 times, giving 300 total rubbings (1+2+1+2... × 100) and 500 total strikings (2+3+2+3... × 100).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
R' Yirmeya raises an unresolved dilemma about rubbing. What is the question?
A. Whether rubbing must be done by a Kohen or can be done by any Israelite
B. Whether moving the hand back and forth counts as one rubbing or two
C. Whether striking and rubbing can be performed simultaneously
D. Whether rubbing applies to barley as well as wheat
Correct: B. R' Yirmeya's dilemma: when rubbing, does a single back-and-forth motion of the hand count as one rubbing (going and returning = one) or two separate rubbings (one going, one returning)? This remains unresolved — Teiku.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Yehuda, which offerings require 12 loaves instead of the standard 10?
A. The thanks offering and the nazirite offering
B. The shewbread and the High Priest's griddle-cake offering
C. The Omer offering and the Two Loaves
D. All burnt offerings and peace offerings
Correct: B. R' Yehuda holds the standard is 10 loaves, with two exceptions that require 12: the shewbread (explicit verse — "bake 12 loaves," Vayikra 24:5) and the High Priest's griddle-cake (derived via Gezerah Shavah from the word "obligation" that links it to the shewbread).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is R' Meir's position on loaf quantities — and which offerings does he say require only 10?
A. Default is 10; exceptions requiring 12 are the shewbread and griddle-cake
B. Default is 12; exceptions requiring only 10 are the thanks offering loaves and nazirite loaves
C. All offerings require exactly 10 — no exceptions
D. Default is 7; exceptions requiring 12 are the most holy offerings
Correct: B. R' Meir holds the standard is 12 loaves. The exceptions that come as 10 are the four types of thanks offering loaves and the two types of nazirite loaves — because their "ten" count is explicitly written in the Torah.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rav Huna rules that baking only one loaf for an oven-baked meal offering fulfills the obligation. What textual basis does he use?
A. The verse says "a meal offering" in the singular, implying one is enough
B. The word matzot is written without a vav, allowing it to be read as the singular matzat — one loaf
C. The High Priest once brought a single loaf and was not penalized
D. An oral tradition from Sinai teaches one loaf is sufficient
Correct: B. Rav Huna derives from the word מַצּוֹת (matzot) in Vayikra 2:4 — it is written without the letter vav, which typically marks the plural. This allows it to be read as מַצַּת (matzat), singular, teaching that even one unleavened loaf fulfills the oven-baked meal offering obligation.
