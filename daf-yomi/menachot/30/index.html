Sewing a Torn Torah Scroll

Rav Ze'eira / Rav Chananel / Rav: If a tear extends into two lines of text, the parchment can be sewn. But if it extends into three lines, it cannot be sewn — the sheet must be replaced.

"Old" vs. "New" parchment: Not literally about age! "Old" = not processed with gall (gallnut solution). "New" = processed with gall. Old sheets may be attached to new ones for individual scrolls but not for communal Torah readings.

R. Shimon Shezuri — Halakha Follows Him

R. Chanina: Wherever R. Shimon Shezuri teaches a halakha in our Mishna, the halakha follows him.

But NOT everywhere he appears — only in the Mishna, not in baraitot. The Gemara clarifies this limitation carefully.

Measuring Large Vessels — Chest Volume

R. Chanina's statement: Explained in relation to a chest (for ritual impurity susceptibility).

A wooden chest holding 40 se'ah is not susceptible to impurity. But how do we measure the volume?

R. Meir

  • Measure the legs and rims as part of the vessel
  • Space between rims IS measured

R. Yosei

  • Legs and rims ARE measured
  • Space between rims is NOT measured

Wine & Oil — Connected Liquids

Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak: R. Chanina's principle applies to wine as well.

R. Meir says: Oil (a thick liquid) that forms a connected stream between two vessels counts as connected for purposes of ritual impurity. Wine (a thinner liquid) does NOT form a connection.

R. Shimon Shezuri says: Even wine can form a connection if poured carefully.

Who Wrote the Last 8 Verses?

R. Yehuda / R. Nechemia

  • The last 8 verses of the Torah (describing Moses's death) were written by Yehoshua
  • Because Moses could not have written about his own death in past tense

R. Shimon

  • Moses wrote all of it
  • God dictated; Moses wrote with tears (b'dema)
  • The entire Torah is from Moses
7 / 7

Key Takeaways — Menachot 30

1. Torn Torah: sewable if tear is within 2 lines; replace the sheet if 3+ lines.

2. "Old" and "new" parchment refers to gall-processing, not age.

3. Halakha follows R. Shimon Shezuri — but only in the Mishna, not baraitot.

4. Measuring vessel volume: dispute about whether spaces between rims count.

5. Last 8 verses: Yehoshua wrote them (R. Yehuda) or Moses wrote them with tears (R. Shimon).
