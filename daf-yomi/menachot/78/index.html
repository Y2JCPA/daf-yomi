🎁 Teruma From Thanks Offering Loaves

וְהִקְרִיב מִמֶּנּוּ אֶחָד מִכָּל קָרְבָּן תְּרוּמָה לַה׳

"He shall present from it one of each offering as a teruma to the Lord" (Vayikra 7:14)
PhraseLaw Derived
"From it"All loaves must be together (not scattered) at the time of teruma separation
"One"No sliced (broken) loaves — must be whole
"Of each offering"All four types must be equal — cannot take from one type for another
"To the priest that sprinkles the blood"The teruma goes to whichever Kohen sprinkled the blood
"And the rest shall be eaten by the owner"Owner (and guests) eat the remaining 36 loaves

📍 All Loaves Must Be Present Together

"From it" — Baraita teaches: teruma may be taken only when all loaves are joined together in one place
↓
❓ What if some loaves were outside when the animal was slaughtered?
↓
❌ Those loaves are NOT consecrated — the slaughter did not effect consecration on them
↓
Even if all loaves formed a crust in the oven except ONE — that one prevents all from being consecrated
↓
❌ Even one unready loaf blocks consecration of the entire set

🚪 "Outside the Wall" — Two Opinions

Reish Lakish

  • ❌ Outside = outside the Temple courtyard wall
  • Verse says "with" [al] the offering → loaves must be nearby (within courtyard)
  • If loaves are outside the courtyard, they aren't "with" the animal
  • More stringent position

R' Yochanan

  • ❌ Outside = outside Beit Pagei's wall (Jerusalem boundary)
  • If outside Beit Pagei → loaves lose their holy status and are disqualified
  • If merely outside courtyard but inside Jerusalem → still fit
  • We don't require strict "nearness" — just within the city

🔥 Loaves Not Yet Crusted = Not Consecrated

If the animal was slaughtered before the loaves finished baking (before crust formed) — are the loaves consecrated?
↓
❌ No — the loaves are not yet "fit" at the time of slaughter, so they cannot be consecrated
↓
What if 39 of 40 loaves had a crust — but one didn't?
↓
❌ Still not consecrated. The entire set must be ready. Even one unfinished loaf disqualifies the whole.
The principle: Consecration of the loaves happens simultaneously with the slaughter of the animal. Both must be fully ready at that moment.

⚖️ Teruma — Who Gets It?

"To the priest that sprinkles the blood of the peace offerings against the altar it shall be given"
↓
The Kohen who performs the blood-sprinkling service receives the 4 loaves of teruma (one per variety)
↓
The remaining 36 loaves go to the owner of the offering — eaten in Jerusalem within the day and night
Split: 4 loaves → Kohen (teruma, 1 per type). 36 loaves → Owner. The whole family can eat — it's a lesser-sacred offering (kodshim kalim).

📋 Menachot 78 — Summary Map

Central Theme: Conditions for consecration of thanks offering loaves
↓
Teruma rules: whole loaves, one per type, all equal, taken when together. Goes to blood-sprinkling Kohen.
Consecration fails: loaves outside the wall, loaves not yet crusted, even one loaf missing or unready.
↓
🎓 Key Takeaway: The loaves and animal form one unified offering — both must be fully ready and in the right place at the moment of slaughter for the loaves to become consecrated.
