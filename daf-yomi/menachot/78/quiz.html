Menachot 78 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded
QUESTION 1 OF 5
The Baraita derives several laws from the phrase "one of each offering" (Vayikra 7:14). What does "one" specifically teach?
A. Only one Kohen may receive the teruma loaves
B. One may not take a sliced (broken) loaf as teruma — it must be whole
C. Only one type of loaf needs to be separated as teruma
D. One must separate teruma before the animal is slaughtered
Correct: B. The word "one" teaches that the teruma loaf must be whole and intact — a sliced or broken loaf may not be used as teruma from the thanks offering loaves.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Which Kohen receives the teruma loaves from the thanks offering?
A. The Kohen Gadol (High Priest)
B. The Kohen who baked the loaves
C. The Kohen who sprinkled the blood of the peace offering on the altar
D. The Kohen on duty that day (determined by lottery)
Correct: C. The verse specifies: "to the priest that sprinkles the blood of the peace offerings against the altar it shall be given." The Kohen who performs the blood-sprinkling service is the one who receives all four teruma loaves (one per variety).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Yochanan, when does "outside the wall" disqualify the thanks offering loaves?
A. When they are outside the Temple courtyard wall
B. When they are outside the wall of Beit Pagei (the Jerusalem boundary)
C. When they are outside the Land of Israel
D. When they are in a different city from the Temple
Correct: B. R' Yochanan holds "outside the wall" means outside the wall of Beit Pagei — the outer boundary of Jerusalem. Loaves outside this boundary are disqualified because they've left the area where they may be eaten. But if they're merely outside the Temple courtyard (still within Jerusalem), R' Yochanan holds they are consecrated.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If 39 of 40 loaves had formed a crust in the oven when the animal was slaughtered, but one loaf hadn't yet — what is the halacha?
A. The 39 ready loaves are consecrated; the one unfinished loaf is not
B. All 40 loaves are not consecrated — even one unfinished loaf blocks the entire set
C. The animal may be slaughtered again to re-consecrate the loaves
D. The Kohen may finish baking the loaf after slaughter and then consecrate them
Correct: B. The Mishna explicitly teaches: "even if all the loaves formed a crust except for one of them — the loaves were not consecrated." Consecration requires the entire set to be ready simultaneously at the moment of slaughter. One unfinished loaf blocks consecration for all 40.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the key difference between R' Yochanan and Reish Lakish's positions on the "outside the wall" rule?
A. R' Yochanan is stricter — he requires the loaves to be inside the Temple courtyard; Reish Lakish only requires inside Jerusalem
B. Reish Lakish is stricter — he requires the loaves inside the courtyard; R' Yochanan only requires inside Jerusalem's outer boundary (Beit Pagei)
C. They agree on the location requirement but disagree on which Kohen receives the teruma
D. They only disagree about whether a second slaughter can fix the problem
Correct: B. Reish Lakish is more stringent — the loaves must be inside the Temple courtyard wall (because "with" means nearby). R' Yochanan is more lenient — the loaves just need to be within Jerusalem's outer boundary (Beit Pagei), where they would still be permitted to be eaten.
