1. What is R. Yosi's reason for exempting from Karet when the Kemitzah is done with intent to burn the Levonah tomorrow?

A) The Levonah is not required for a Minchah to be valid
B) One Matir cannot render another Matir Pigul ‚Äî and Levonah is not from the same Min as the Minchah
C) Intent regarding the Levonah counts only for Chatas offerings, not Menachos
D) The Levonah belongs to the Kohen, not the Mizbe'ach, so Pigul cannot apply
R. Yosi's reasoning is twofold: (1) Levonah is not from the Min (species) of the Minchah ‚Äî the flour/oil and frankincense are different species. (2) His broader principle: "one Matir cannot be Mefagel another Matir." Intent regarding the Levonah (one Matir) during Kemitzah of the Kometz (another Matir) cannot create Pigul.

2. Regarding the two Bazichim (frankincense spoons) of the Lechem ha'Panim ‚Äî do they render each other Pigul according to Chachamim?

A) No ‚Äî they are different portions and never interact for Pigul purposes
B) Yes ‚Äî because they were placed together in one Kli, making them "as one" Matir
C) Only if both are burned by the same Kohen
D) No ‚Äî frankincense as a separate species never creates Pigul
Chachamim hold that when two Matirim are placed together in one Kli (vessel), they become "Ikve'u" ‚Äî fixed as one unit. Since the two Bazichim of Levonah are placed in a shared Kli, they are treated as one Matir. Therefore, burning one Bazich with Chutz li'Zmano intent renders the other Bazich Pigul as well.

3. According to Chachamim, why can't one of the Shavuot lambs render the other Pigul?

A) Animal offerings can never be Mefagel other animal offerings
B) The Shavuot lambs are not Matirim for each other
C) The two lambs are never placed together in one Kli ‚Äî without a shared Kli, one Matir cannot Mefagel another
D) Only the bread can be Mefagel the lambs, not vice versa
Chachamim's rule about the Kli is: two Matirim can be Mefagel each other ONLY when they were placed together in one Kli. The two Shavuot lambs are never put in a shared vessel ‚Äî each is slaughtered separately. Therefore, even Chachamim agree that neither lamb can render the other Pigul.

4. In what does R. Yosi AGREE with Chachamim regarding the Minchah and Karet?

A) R. Yosi agrees that intent for the Levonah creates Pigul
B) R. Yosi agrees that intent to eat the Shirayim tomorrow or burn the Kometz tomorrow = Pigul + Karet
C) R. Yosi agrees that the Shavuot lambs can Mefagel each other
D) R. Yosi agrees that the Levonah must be the same Min as the Minchah
The Mishnah explicitly states: "R. Yosi concedes regarding THIS case" ‚Äî namely, Kemitzah done with intent to eat the Shirayim tomorrow, or to burn the Kometz tomorrow. Both of these are intents within the same "Min" as the Minchah (flour/Kometz and its Shirayim are the same species), so R. Yosi agrees they create Pigul + Karet.

5. What does Reish Lakish's statement about the two Bazichei Levonah add, beyond what could be inferred from the Minchah case?

A) It clarifies that Chachamim disagree with R. Yosi about all Matirim
B) It teaches that R. Yosi's rule applies even when both Matirim are the same species (both are Levonah), not only when they are different species
C) It shows that only Levonah (not other Matirim) is exempt from the Pigul rules
D) It proves that Lechem ha'Panim is treated differently from regular Menachos
One might have thought R. Yosi's exemption is only because Levonah is a different species (Min) from the flour/oil of the Minchah. Reish Lakish teaches: even two Bazichim of Levonah (same species!) cannot Mefagel each other, according to R. Yosi. His rule is absolute: "one Matir never Mefagels another Matir" ‚Äî regardless of whether they are the same species or not.
