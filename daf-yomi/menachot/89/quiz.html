5 questions · Self-graded
QUESTION 1 OF 5
When may libations from different offerings be mixed together?
A. Any time they are from offerings brought on the same day
B. Only when they have the same flour-to-oil ratio (e.g., lamb with lamb)
C. Only if both are from communal offerings
D. Never — all libations must remain separate
Correct: B. The key is ratio compatibility. Offerings with the same flour:oil ratio may be mixed (e.g., lamb with lamb, including different days). Different ratios (lamb vs. bull/ram) may not be mixed.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
If lamb and bull libations were mixed only after each offering's flour and oil had already been fully intermingled separately, what is the halacha?
A. Still invalid — lamb and bull may never be mixed under any condition
B. Valid — because each offering was properly formed first before combination
C. Valid only if the mixture is offered the same day
D. Valid only for communal offerings, not private offerings
Correct: B. The Mishna distinguishes order: if each offering's flour and oil were already properly mixed on their own, and only afterward were the offerings combined, the result remains valid. The disqualification applies when they are mixed before each offering is independently constituted.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
R' Akiva explains the doubled phrase "with oil, with oil" in the thanks offering verses as an "amplification following amplification." What does this produce?
A. Expansion — doubling the total oil from ½-log to 1-log
B. Restriction — limiting total oil for the three unleavened types to ½-log
C. Inclusion — requiring oil for all four types including leavened loaves
D. Exclusion — removing oil entirely from thanks offering loaves
Correct: B. R' Akiva applies the rule that "amplification following amplification" is restrictive. So the doubled "with oil" doesn't increase quantity; it limits the total oil for the three matza-types to only ½-log together.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is unique about the libations of the lamb that accompanies the Omer?
A. Both flour and libations are doubled compared to a regular lamb
B. Neither flour nor libations are doubled
C. Flour is doubled, but oil and wine libations remain the standard lamb quantities
D. Oil is doubled, but flour and wine remain standard
Correct: C. The Omer lamb's meal offering flour is doubled (2 tenths instead of 1), but its libations are not doubled: oil and wine remain at the regular lamb amount of 3 log each.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why was there a dedicated half-log vessel in the Temple according to this sugya?
A. It was used only for one thing: water for the sota ritual
B. It was used only for one thing: oil for the Menorah
C. It served multiple halachic needs, including half-log oil for the thanks offering loaves
D. It was a spare backup vessel and had no fixed halachic use
Correct: C. The half-log vessel measured quantities used in several contexts, notably the total oil for thanks offering loaves (per R' Akiva's restrictive reading) and the water for the sota rite. Its existence reflects practical Temple measurement needs.
