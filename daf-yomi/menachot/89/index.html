⚗️ Mixing Order — When Does It Matter?

✅ VALID — Even if combined afterward
Lamb libations were each independently intermingled (flour + oil mixed within the individual offering), and THEN combined with each other. Even though different offerings are now mixed, it's valid — each was already properly constituted on its own.
❌ INVALID — If combined before individual mixing
Lamb libations of different types (or lamb + bull) were combined BEFORE each offering's flour and oil were mixed together on their own. This disqualifies — the offerings' substances were never individually constituted first.

📖 R' Akiva Decodes "With Oil, With Oil"

"with oil" (1st)
→
First amplification: Teaches that oil is required for thanks offering loaves (might have said 1 log like standard meal offerings)
"with oil" (2nd)
→
Amplification following amplification = restriction principle. The Torah is now limiting, not adding. Result: only ½-log total for ALL three matza-types together.
R' Akiva's Rule
An amplification following an amplification (ribbuy achar ribbuy) restricts — it doesn't double the quantity. The doubled "with oil" doesn't mean more oil; it limits the total to ½-log for all three types of matza loaves combined.

🐑 The Omer Lamb — The Exception

The unique rule: The lamb brought with the Omer offering has a doubled meal offering (2 tenths of flour instead of 1 tenth). But its libations (oil and wine) were NOT doubled — they remain at the standard 3-log each, as for any ordinary lamb.

🔢 Why Only ½-Log for Thanks Offering Loaves?

Without the verse restriction: One might think thanks offering matza loaves each require 1 log of oil (like a standard meal offering). With 3 types × 10 loaves = 30 loaves, that would be 30 log!
With the double "with oil" restriction: R' Akiva says the doubled phrase restricts the total oil for all three matza types combined to just ½-log. Split across 30 matza loaves, that's 1/60-log per loaf — a tiny amount.
Practical result: The ½-log measuring vessel's purpose is confirmed — it was used for the thanks offering loaves' total oil allotment (along with the sota ritual).

📊 Which Libations Can Mix With Which?

Permitted Combinations
✅ Today's lamb offering libations + yesterday's lamb offering libations (same ratio)
✅ Two lamb offerings that were each independently mixed, then combined
❌ Lamb + bull or ram (different oil:flour ratios)
❌ Any two offerings combined BEFORE each was individually mixed

📋 Menachot 89 — Summary

Mixing Rule: Same oil:flour ratio = permitted. Different ratios (lamb vs. bull) = forbidden. Must be individually mixed first, then combined.
R' Akiva's Principle: "With oil" × 2 = amplification following amplification = restriction. Result: only ½-log total for all three matza-type thanks offering loaves.
Omer Lamb Exception: Doubled meal offering flour (2 tenths), but libations (oil + wine) remain at standard 3-log each — not doubled.
