⚖️ When Does Fetal Emergence Establish Birth?

Rabbi Elazar
Emergence of a non-viable newborn's head is NOT equivalent to emergence of most limbs. A majority of the body must emerge to establish birth.
Rabbi Yochanan
Emergence of a non-viable newborn's head IS equivalent to most limbs — the head alone, if among the emerging limbs, legally establishes birth.
Context The dispute concerns a fetus emerging piecemeal or feet-first (reversed). For a normal headfirst delivery, emergence of most of the head (the temples) establishes birth per all opinions.

📜 Rav Pappa vs. Rav Zevid — Reading the Baraita

Rav Pappa
Baraita says piecemeal or reversed — two separate cases. By inference, a normally-delivered fetus only needs head emergence. This supports Rabbi Yochanan.
Rav Zevid
Rav Pappa's reading collapses: Rabbi Yosei's ruling covers both abnormal cases together. Baraita means piecemeal and reversed. Normal delivery requires most of the head to emerge.
A Second Baraita Confirms Rav Zevid An explicit baraita states: 'emerged piecemeal AND reversed — most limbs = birth; emerged normally — most of the head = birth.' Rav Zevid's reading stands.

👶 Defining 'Most of the Head' for Normal Headfirst Birth

Rabbi Yosei

  • Birth established when the temples emerge
  • רוב ראשה — the majority of the head
  • The widest cranial point has cleared the birth canal
  • Aligns with the anatomical moment of irreversible delivery

Abba Ḥanan (name of Rabbi Eliezer)

  • Birth established when the cheekbones emerge
  • A slightly later, stricter threshold
  • Reflects a more conservative definition of 'most of the head'
  • Recorded as a minority position

🌊 Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi — Discharge into a River

Case: A pregnant woman crossed a river and discharged her fetus into the water. The fetus was not recovered and its development is unknown.
↓
Question: Must she bring a korban yoledet (post-childbirth offering)? We don't know if the fetus was fully formed.
↓
Ruling — Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi: She brings the offering out of doubt, without reciting a blessing over the sacrifice.
↓
Principle: Follow the majority — most pregnancies produce a fully-formed, viable fetus. She is presumed to have given birth.
↓
Gemara's Distinction: The mishna's unknown-fetus case involves a woman not previously known to be pregnant — no majority presumption applies there. R. Yehoshua ben Levi's case requires a confirmed pregnancy.
