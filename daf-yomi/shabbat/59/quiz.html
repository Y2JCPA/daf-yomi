Shabbat 59 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yannai, when may a stain larger than a split bean be attributed to a louse?
A. Never — the split bean is an absolute maximum under all circumstances
B. Only if she was actively handling the louse before finding the stain
C. When a squashed louse is physically found on the stain itself
D. Only when the stain is on an outer garment, not an inner one
Correct: C — Rabbi Yannai holds that the rule against attributing oversized stains to a louse applies only when no louse is found. If a squashed louse is present on the stain, the entire stain — even larger than a bean — is attributed to it, and she is pure.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rabbi Meir's ruling when a woman finds blood in her urine while standing?
A. Pure, because the blood mixed with urine and diluted
B. Pure, because we assume the blood came from a wound
C. Impure, because standing creates no barrier between the blood and uterus
D. She must wait and examine herself before a ruling is made
Correct: C — Rabbi Meir rules that when standing, there is no urine stream barrier to separate uterine blood from the external flow — so she is impure. When sitting, the stream provides a barrier and she is pure.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the key disagreement between Reish Lakish and Rabbi Yoḥanan regarding Rabbi Meir's position in compound uncertainty?
A. Reish Lakish says pure; Rabbi Yoḥanan says impure
B. Reish Lakish says impure (same as single uncertainty); Rabbi Yoḥanan says pure
C. Both agree Rabbi Meir is lenient in compound uncertainty
D. Both agree Rabbi Meir is strict regardless of the number of uncertainties
Correct: B — Reish Lakish holds that Rabbi Meir makes no distinction between single and compound uncertainty — she is impure either way. Rabbi Yoḥanan disagrees: Rabbi Meir only declares impurity in a case of single uncertainty; compound uncertainty yields purity. A baraita supports Rabbi Yoḥanan.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If three women wore the same garment sequentially and blood is found after the third (the garment was clean before the first), what is the ruling?
A. Only the third woman is impure, since she wore it last
B. Only the first and third are impure
C. All three are pure, since the cause is uncertain
D. All three are impure, since any one of them may have caused the stain
Correct: D — Since we cannot determine which of the three women caused the blood stain, all three are ruled impure — each must account for the possibility that she was the source.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Rava's ruling when a woman was occupied with one type of item (e.g., eye salve) and then finds stains of several different types on her garment?
A. She is impure — different stain types cannot be attributed to one source
B. She may attribute all the stains to that one source and is pure
C. Only the stain that matches the item's color may be attributed; others are impure
D. She must investigate each stain individually before any ruling
Correct: B — Rava rules that being occupied with one type of item is sufficient to attribute stains of several types to it. All stains are pure — the source item does not need to match every stain precisely.
