⚖️ Blood in Urine: One Uncertainty or Two?

Rabbi Meir
Standing → impure (urine stream creates no barrier; blood may be uterine). Sitting → pure (stream acts as shield). One uncertainty is enough to render her impure.
Rabbi Yosei
Even with one uncertainty (wound vs. uterus), she is pure. We cannot confirm the blood is uterine. Halakha follows Rabbi Yosei (Shmuel).
Rabbi Shimon
When man and woman urinate into the same basin, she is impure — the presumptive status of blood is that it came from the woman, since men do not typically discharge blood.
Halakha Shmuel rules: The halakha follows Rabbi Yosei — uncertainty about the source renders her pure.

🦟 Can a Louse Override the Split-Bean Limit?

Rabbi Ḥanina
If a stain exceeds a split bean — even by just a little — she is IMPURE, even if a squashed louse is found on the extra portion. Once a stain surpasses the bean threshold, no attribution to a louse is valid.
Rabbi Yannai
She is PURE. The rule barring attribution to a louse applies only when no louse is found. If a louse is physically squashed on the stain, we attribute the entire stain — even larger than a bean — to the louse.
Rabbi Yirmeya's Dilemma What if she was occupied with a bean-sized item (bird blood, eye salve) and the stain is slightly larger — can she attribute the extra portion to that item? The Gemara explores whether each sage's reasoning extends to non-louse cases.

🪣 Blood in the Basin: Three Opinions Compared

Rabbi Meir

  • Standing → impure (no barrier between blood and uterus)
  • Sitting → pure (urine stream acts as shield)
  • One uncertainty (wound vs. uterus) → impure if standing
  • Reish Lakish: equally strict in compound uncertainty

Rabbi Yosei

  • Pure even with one uncertainty
  • Source of blood is genuinely unknown
  • Man + woman urinate together → still pure
  • Halakha follows Rabbi Yosei (Shmuel)

Rabbi Shimon

  • Man + woman urinate into basin → impure
  • Presumptive status: blood came from woman
  • Men do not typically discharge blood
  • Even compound uncertainty → impure

👗 Garments, Benches, and Shared Uncertainty

Examination cloth under pillow: Rabbi Eliezer son of Rabbi Tzadok — round stain → pure (crushed during sleep); elongated stain → impure (uterine blood flows in a stream). Shmuel rules: halakha follows Rabbi Eliezer son of Rabbi Tzadok.
↓
Lending a garment: If a woman lends her garment to a gentile woman or a menstruating Jewish woman, and a blood stain is found after return — she may attribute it to the borrower and remains pure.
↓
Three women, one garment: Garment was clean before the first woman; blood is found after the third. Result: all three are impure — we cannot determine who caused the stain.
↓
Stone or bathhouse bench: Cannot become ritually impure (stone = immune; bathhouse bench = attached to floor). Blood stain found there — each woman who sat is impure, since they cannot attribute the stain to the bench itself.
↓
Rava's rule: If a woman was occupied with one type of item (e.g., sycamore sap, eye salve), she may attribute stains of several different types to that source. All are pure — the item need not match each stain exactly.
