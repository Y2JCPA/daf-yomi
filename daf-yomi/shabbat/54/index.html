↓

↓

🤰 The Woman Who Miscarried on Ziva Day 3

Setup
She bled on ziva days 1 and 2, then on day 3 miscarried — but cannot identify whether the expelled matter was a human fetus (requiring yoledet laws) or not
Rav Kahana
This baraita resolves the earlier dilemma about childbirth-during-ziva. She has triple uncertainty: zava, yoledet of a son, or yoledet of a daughter — maximum strictness in every category
Rav Pappa
This does NOT resolve it — we can say she gave birth to a male only. The 7 extra days of uncertainty are then manageable. No proof about the harder case
Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua
Why assume only male? There is certainly also uncertainty about female birth — adding 14 days of impurity! You cannot limit the doubt to just one type
Conclusion
All three uncertainties stand. Her offering is brought but not eaten. Days of uncertain birth do not count toward any clean-day count
Practical Result She cannot count these days toward ziva counting; her offering is brought but priests may not eat it — its status is genuinely uncertain

💧 Impurity of Substances: Moist and Dry

Moist AND Dry — Full Impurity

  • 🩸 Blood of a niddah
  • 💀 Flesh of a corpse
  • Transmit impurity by contact and by carrying
  • Retain impurity status even after drying
  • Source: Chizkiyah — 'her impurity' (Lev. 15:25); Rabbi Yitzchak — 'with her impurity' (Lev. 15:33)

Only When Moist — Impurity Lost on Drying

  • 🤢 Discharge of a zav (gonorrhea-like flow)
  • 💦 Saliva of a zav
  • 🧬 Semen
  • Once dried → no longer transmits impurity
  • Test: soak in lukewarm water 24 hours — if dissolved, was restorable

וְנִדָּתָהּ עָלֶיהָ — "her impurity is upon her" (Vayikra 15:25)
📜 Gemara: Deriving Dry Blood Impurity from Verse The Mishna rules that dry niddah blood is still impure. The Gemara challenges: where is the source? Chizkiyah derives it from the verse "and her impurity is upon her" — the word niddatah (her impurity) encompasses even dried blood. Rabbi Yitzchak adds a second verse: "with her impurity" — this expands the ruling to blood that was dry from the very outset, not merely blood that dried after being moist. The Gemara also rules: a stone afflicted with leprosy is excluded from the zav's lying/sitting impurity — the verse says 'the zav,' not a leprous stone — yet the leprous stone itself defiles a house.
