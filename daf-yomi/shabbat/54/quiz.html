Shabbat 54 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
In the 1-day-impure/1-day-pure cyclic pattern, how many nights of intercourse are permitted per 18-day cycle?
A. 2 nights
B. 4 nights
C. 7 nights
D. 9 nights
Correct: B — The baraita teaches she may engage in intercourse 4 nights out of the 18-day cycle (7 niddah days + 11 ziva days). This pattern ensures she never becomes a greater zava since she never bleeds three consecutive ziva days.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rav Ashi say about day 19 — the day immediately after the 11 ziva days end — in the 2/2 pattern?
A. It is rabbinically forbidden as a safeguard
B. It requires one clean day first
C. It is permitted since she has already entered new niddah days
D. She must immerse in a mikveh before relations
Correct: C — Rav Ashi holds that day 19 is already the beginning of her new niddah period. Therefore, no rabbinic decree applies — she is already subject to niddah law, not ziva law, and the question of a safeguard does not arise.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When a woman bleeds on ziva days 1 and 2 then miscarries on day 3 without knowing what she expelled, what happens to her korban (offering)?
A. It is eaten normally by the kohanim
B. No offering is required due to the doubt
C. It is brought but not eaten, due to the triple uncertainty
D. It is burned entirely as a sin-offering
Correct: C — Since there is uncertainty whether she is a zava, a yoledet of a son, or a yoledet of a daughter — three distinct statuses with different offerings — her offering is brought but the priests may not eat it, as its sacred status is in doubt.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Mishna at the end of Daf 54, which substance loses its power to transmit impurity once it dries?
A. Blood of a niddah
B. Flesh of a corpse
C. Discharge of a zav
D. Both niddah blood and corpse flesh
Correct: C — The Mishna distinguishes clearly: niddah blood and corpse flesh transmit impurity both moist and dry. But zav discharge, zav saliva, and semen transmit impurity only when moist — once dried, they no longer convey impurity.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
From which verse does Chizkiyah derive that dried niddah blood still transmits impurity?
A. 'And she shall count seven days' (Lev. 15:28)
B. 'Her impurity is upon her' (Lev. 15:25)
C. 'Every bed upon which the zav lies' (Lev. 15:4)
D. 'You shall separate the children of Israel' (Lev. 15:31)
Correct: B — Chizkiyah derives from the phrase 'נִדָּתָהּ עָלֶיהָ — her impurity is upon her' (Leviticus 15:25) that the blood retains its impurity even after drying. Rabbi Yitzchak further extends this to blood that was dry from the very outset.
