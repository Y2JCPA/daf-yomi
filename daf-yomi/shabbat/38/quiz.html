Shabbat 38 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Levi, when does the birth of a child render the mother ritually pure from ziva?
A. When she experienced bleeding during any of the seven days of menstruation
B. When she experienced bleeding during the eleven days fit for becoming a greater zava
C. When she experienced bleeding during the eleven days fit for becoming a lesser zava
D. When she experienced no bleeding whatsoever before birth
Correct: B — Levi holds that birth purifies only if she bled during the eleven days fit for greater ziva. Rav (via Abba Shaul) is more lenient and includes days fit for lesser ziva as well.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What maximum number of days did Rabbi Meir claim a woman could experience labor-pain bleeding without ever becoming a greater zava?
A. 40–50 days
B. 100 days
C. 150 days
D. All the days of her life, theoretically unlimited
Correct: C — Rabbi Meir constructed a scenario totaling approximately 150 days by sequencing menstruation, ziva, and post-birth purity periods. The Rabbis countered that using non-viable births one could extend this indefinitely.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did the early pious men (Chasidim Rishonim) restrict marital relations to Wednesday night?
A. Wednesday was considered the most auspicious day for conception in kabbalistic thought
B. To ensure that birth — occurring after 271–273 days — would not fall on Shabbat, requiring midwifery
C. To align with the Temple's weekly service rotation
D. Because the full moon always falls on Wednesday in their calendar
Correct: B — Relations on Sunday, Monday, or Tuesday could result in birth at 271–273 days falling on Shabbat. Wednesday relations meant birth fell on Wednesday, Thursday, or Friday — avoiding Shabbat desecration by the midwife.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the halakhic principle of 'dayo' (דיו) that Rabbi Eliezer invokes against the Rabbis?
A. An a fortiori argument may only be used once per legal discussion
B. The conclusion derived via a fortiori inference need only equal, not exceed, the stringency of its source
C. A rabbinic decree can never override a biblical law derived by inference
D. Two competing a fortiori arguments cancel each other out
Correct: B — Dayo means 'it is sufficient.' In a kal va'chomer, the derived ruling need not be stricter than the case it derives from — it is enough for the conclusion to match the source's level of stringency, no more.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the mishna at the end of Daf 38b, what is a woman's presumptive status during the eleven days that follow her seven days of menstruation?
A. Presumptively impure — any bleeding during this period automatically renders her a zava
B. Presumptively pure — it is unusual to experience bleeding on these days
C. Uncertain — she must be examined before handling terumah or holy items
D. Presumptively a lesser zava, requiring immersion before resuming normal life
Correct: B — The mishna establishes a chazakah (presumptive status) of purity for a woman during her eleven days of ziva, because bleeding on those days is atypical. She is treated as pure unless she actually experiences bleeding.
