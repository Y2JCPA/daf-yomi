🔥 Gentile vs. Jewish Child Extinguishing on Shabbat

Mishna
If a gentile extinguishes a Jew's fire — one may neither say 'extinguish' nor 'do not extinguish,' for his rest is not the Jew's responsibility.
Mishna
If a Jewish child comes to extinguish — one does not listen to him, because his rest IS the Jew's responsibility (chinuch).
Rav Ami
During a fire one may say in the presence of gentiles: 'Anyone who extinguishes will not lose' — indirect encouragement is permitted, explicit instruction is not.
Baraita
Incident at Yosef ben Simai's courtyard in Shiḥin: men from the fortress of Tzippori came to help — Yosef did not permit them, and the Sages praised his stringency.
Core Principle A Jew is responsible for the rest of other Jews (including children) but not for gentiles. However, even regarding gentiles, explicit instruction is forbidden — only indirect speech is tolerated.

🏛️ Incident at Yosef ben Simai — Shiḥin

A fire broke out on Shabbat in the courtyard of Yosef ben Simai in Shiḥin.
↓
Men from the fortress of Tzippori arrived to extinguish the fire — Yosef was the royal steward and they came out of loyalty.
↓
Yosef refused to allow them to extinguish — he acted with exceptional Shabbat stringency even at cost to his property.
↓
A miracle occurred and rain fell, extinguishing the fire — divine confirmation of his piety.
↓
The Sages later said: "You need not rebuke him" — he acted beyond the letter of the law and was praised.

👶 Who May Extinguish? Two Cases Compared

Non-Jew (Akum)

  • Acts for his own benefit or loyalty
  • Jew bears no Shabbat responsibility for him
  • Cannot say: 'Extinguish' or 'Don't extinguish'
  • May say indirectly: 'One who extinguishes won't lose'
  • Gentile's own volition — Jew is passive

Jewish Child (Katan)

  • Not yet obligated in mitzvot himself
  • BUT parent is responsible for child's Shabbat rest
  • Must actively stop him from extinguishing
  • Cannot passively allow child to violate
  • Chinuch (training) applies even to fire cases

🐍 May Harmful Creatures Be Killed on Shabbat?

Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi
All harmful creatures (mazikin) may be killed on Shabbat — snakes, scorpions, and the like pose a life danger.
Rav Yosef
A baraita states: One who kills snakes and scorpions on Shabbat — "the spirit of the pious is not pleased with him." This challenges Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi!
Rabbi Yirmeya
Who says this baraita is accurate? Perhaps it is corrupted — we cannot raise an objection from a doubtful text.
Rava bar Rav Huna
Those overly pious who refuse to kill threatening snakes — "the spirit of the Sages is not pleased with them either!" Excessive stringency that endangers oneself is condemned.
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's Cryptic Remark A Nabatean killed a snake in the study hall on Shabbat. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi said: 'One of its type killed it' — the Gemara debates whether this was praise or censure, resolving it was likely praise based on a related incident.

🐍 Harmful Creature on Shabbat — What Should You Do?

A snake or scorpion appears before you on Shabbat
↓
It is chasing you / actively threatening
Kill it immediately — life danger (pikuach nefesh) overrides Shabbat
It is merely before you, not pursuing
If you kill it → it was sent to be killed by you (hashgacha pratis)
If you don't kill it → it was sent to test your fear of Heaven
↓
In all cases: do not endanger yourself through excess piety — the Sages disapprove of those who do
