Shabbat 36 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the final halakhic ruling regarding the dispute between Rav and Levi about the source(s) of menstrual blood?
A. Halakha follows Levi — two sources, any cessation suffices to enter days of purity
B. Halakha follows Rav — whether as a leniency or as a stringency
C. The dispute remains unresolved with no definitive ruling
D. Halakha follows Rav only as a stringency, not as a leniency
Correct: B — Both Rav Ashi and Mareimar rule that the halakha follows Rav — whether this leads to a leniency or a stringency in a given case. This definitively concludes the Rav/Levi debate.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav, what is the precise status of a woman who emits blood during the eleven days of ziva due to labor pains?
A. She is a full zava and must count seven clean days before immersing
B. She is completely pure and immediately permitted to her husband
C. She is like a menstruating woman — prohibited until she immerses that evening
D. She is a lesser zava and must observe one clean day
Correct: C — Rav holds she is like a niddah for that day — not a full zava, but still prohibited to her husband until she immerses in the mikveh in the evening.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yitzḥak, what is the status of a woman who emits blood due to labor pains during the eleven days of ziva?
A. She is a full zava requiring seven clean days
B. She is a niddah, prohibited until evening immersion
C. She is completely pure and permitted to her husband
D. She must observe one clean day as a lesser zava
Correct: C — Rabbi Yitzḥak takes the most lenient position: labor blood during the eleven days is 'nothing' — she is completely pure and immediately permitted to her husband.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the baraita, when does a laboring woman become a 'one who gives birth as a zava'?
A. Whenever she emits any blood during the eleven days of ziva while pregnant
B. When she has labor pains one day, rests two days, and emits blood on a rest day
C. When she has labor pains for three consecutive days with emissions each day
D. Only if she had a prior ziva episode before the pregnancy began
Correct: B — The baraita specifies: labor pains for one day, then rest for two days, with an emission during the rest — the emission is not attributed to labor and therefore constitutes ziva blood.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rav Asi mean when he identifies himself as 'Isi ben Yehuda... like a copper mortar that does not forget'?
A. He is claiming royal Davidic ancestry tracing to the tribe of Yehuda
B. He is asserting that his transmitted tradition — that Rav retracted — is utterly reliable and unforgettable
C. He is explaining why divine punishment caused him to fall ill after the dispute
D. He is citing a separate tanna named Isi who independently agreed with his position
Correct: B — Rav Asi uses the wordplay on 'asita denehasha' (copper mortar) to assert that his memory and tradition are durable and cannot be forgotten — bolstering his claim that Rav did indeed retract his ruling.
