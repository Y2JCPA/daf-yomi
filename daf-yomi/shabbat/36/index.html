⚖️ Two Great Debates of Daf 36

Rav
One source for menstrual blood. Full cessation required across three periods for a woman to be considered in her days of purity.
Levi
Two sources for blood. Any amount of cessation suffices to enter the days of purity and begin counting seven clean days.
Rav Ashi
The halakha is in accordance with Rav — whether as a leniency or as a stringency. Mareimar rules the same.
Rava
Labor blood during days of menstruation → niddah; during eleven days of ziva → pure. The mishna's case is specifically niddah-time.
📌 Daf Structure Amud A concludes the long Rav/Levi debate with a definitive ruling. Amud B opens a new Mishna about a pregnant woman in labor and examines when her blood renders her a zava, including a colorful story about Rav Asi.

⚖️ Rav vs. Levi — Final Resolution

Rav — One Source

  • Blood has one source (the uterus)
  • Full cessation required across three veset periods
  • Seven clean days must follow complete cessation
  • Halakha follows Rav — both leniently and stringently
  • Ruled definitively by Rav Ashi and Mareimar

Levi — Two Sources

  • Blood has two sources (uterine + secondary)
  • Any amount of cessation enters days of purity
  • Seven clean days can begin after a brief pause
  • Multiple baraitot analyzed and reconciled with this view
  • Position rejected as final halakha

🩸 Three Opinions: Labor Blood During the Eleven Days

Rav
She is like a menstruating woman (niddah) for that day — not a zava, but prohibited to her husband until she immerses in the evening.
Rabbi Yitzḥak
She is completely pure — permitted to her husband immediately. Labor blood during the eleven days is nothing.
Rava
It depends on timing: labor blood during days of niddah = niddah; during days of ziva = pure. The mishna addresses niddah-time bleeding only.
Sheila bar Avina
Ruled like Rav in practice. When Rav Asi claimed Rav retracted, Sheila bar Avina refused to change: "If he retracted, he would have told me — I was his student!"
Halakhic Tension Rav, Rabbi Yitzḥak, and Rava each read the mishna differently. Rav's position remains the strictest; Rava's contextual reading reconciles the mishna with the general principle of labor blood's purity.

📚 The Story of Rav Asi and Sheila bar Avina

During Rav's lifetime: Rav ruled that labor blood during the eleven days renders a woman like a niddah — prohibited to her husband until evening immersion.
↓
Sheila bar Avina ruled in practice according to Rav's position, prohibiting women who experienced labor blood during the eleven days.
↓
After Rav died: Rav Asi approached Sheila bar Avina and declared — "Retract your ruling! Rav himself retracted his opinion."
↓
Sheila bar Avina refused: "If Rav had retracted, he would have told me — I was his student. He did not inform me, so I will not retract."
↓
Rav Asi's sharp retort: "I am Isi ben Yehuda... like a copper mortar [אסיתא דנחשא] that does not forget" — asserting his tradition is utterly reliable.
↓
Afterward: Rav Asi fell gravely ill — alternating between fever and chills — as if in consequence of the confrontation with his elder.
