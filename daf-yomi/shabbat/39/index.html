⚖️ Three Amoraim Explain the Mishna's First Clause

Rav Ḥisda
The first clause is needed only for Rabbi Meir's view: normally he forbids a woman without a fixed cycle to her husband — but during the 11 ziva days she is permitted, since the presumption of purity applies there.
Rava
The mishna teaches that if she bleeds during the 11 ziva days, she does not impart retroactive impurity to objects she touched previously — her prior status was purity, so we do not retroactively defile them.
Rav Huna bar Ḥiyya
(in Shmuel's name)
The mishna teaches that a woman cannot establish a fixed menstrual cycle (veset) through sightings during her ziva days. Those sightings are irregular and do not create a pattern.
Same Mishna — Three Readings Each amora derives an entirely different halakhic principle from the identical opening clause of the mishna

🔄 Fixed Cycle vs Unfixed Cycle

Woman WITH a Fixed Veset

  • Presumed impure if she fails to examine on her veset day
  • Presumed pure on all other days of the month
  • Husband may have relations on non-veset days freely
  • Veset established by 3 consecutive occurrences on the same day

Woman WITHOUT a Fixed Veset

  • Rabbi Meir: forbidden to husband unless she examines first
  • During 11 ziva days: permitted even per Rabbi Meir (Rav Ḥisda)
  • Sightings during ziva days cannot establish a veset
  • Only sightings during niddah days can establish a veset pattern

🐔 The Chicken Analogy — Rav Huna son of Rav Yehoshua A chicken normally lays an egg one day and skips the next. If it deviates from routine and lays two consecutive days, then resumes its normal pattern — does it follow the routine ahead of it (skip the next day) or take hold of the routine behind it (lay the next day)?

Answer: It takes hold of the routine behind it — resuming based on its last actual action.

So too a woman: her 22-day cycle is counted from the day she actually saw blood, not from the day she was expected to menstruate. Rav Pappa's opposing view — counting from the expected date — is rejected.

⚖️ Rabbi Yochanan vs Reish Lakish: Veset in Ziva Days

Reish Lakish
A woman CAN establish a veset during her days of niddah (menstruation), but CANNOT establish one during her days of ziva. Ziva sightings are irregular and establish no pattern.
Rabbi Yochanan
A woman CAN establish a veset even during ziva days. The case: she saw blood on the 1st of one month, then again on the 1st of the next month — even if the second sighting falls within her ziva days, the calendar pattern is established.
Rav Pappa
I initially thought the dispute was about whether ziva sightings count at all toward establishing a veset. But Rav Huna son of Rav Yehoshua corrected me: the dispute is specifically about this recurring-date scenario.
Ravin
(from Eretz Yisrael)
Confirmed: when the sea-farers transmitted Rabbi Yochanan's ruling from Eretz Yisrael, they stated it exactly as Rav Huna son of Rav Yehoshua explained — the case of blood seen on the 1st of consecutive months.
Transmission Confirmed Ravin's arrival from Eretz Yisrael resolved the dispute about how to understand Rabbi Yochanan's ruling
