Shabbat 13 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is a woman's diligent examining hand praiseworthy while a man's should be severed?
A. Women are halakhically commanded to examine; men are not
B. Women are not susceptible to sexual arousal through examination, while men are
C. Men's hands are more likely to introduce impurity into the body
D. The Mishna applies only to married women examining for their husbands
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that self-examination can arouse a man and trigger a seminal emission — precisely the impurity he seeks to detect. Women face no such risk, so their diligence is praiseworthy.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Eliezer, a man who holds his penis and urinates is compared to someone who does what?
A. Commits adultery with his neighbor's wife
B. Worships an idol in the Temple
C. Brings a flood to the world
D. Sheds innocent blood
Correct: C — Rabbi Eliezer's famous ruling is that holding one's penis while urinating is 'as though he brings a flood to the world,' evoking the generation of Noah whose moral corruption brought divine destruction.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which sage uses a gezerah shavah (verbal analogy) from 'under every leafy tree' to compare wasting seed to idol worship?
A. Rabbi Yoḥanan
B. Rabbi Ami
C. Rabbi Yitzḥak
D. Rav Asi
Correct: D — Rav Asi observes that the phrase 'under every leafy tree' appears both in Isaiah 57 (regarding Onan-like behavior) and in Deuteronomy 12:2 (regarding idolatrous worship sites), linking the two sins by verbal analogy.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara on 13b say that 'those who play with children delay the coming of the Messiah'?
A. They engage in forbidden homosexual acts
B. They neglect Torah study in favor of childish games
C. They marry minor girls not yet capable of bearing children, wasting seed
D. They distract scholars from completing the Talmud
Correct: C — The Gemara rejects the homosexuality interpretation (that carries a different penalty) and concludes this refers to men who marry minor girls unable to bear children — causing seed to be wasted and preventing the creation of souls needed before the Messiah comes, per Rabbi Yosei.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did the Sages reject Rabbi Eliezer b. Rabbi Tzadok's leather-pouch solution for the imbecile priest?
A. Leather is forbidden as priestly garb and would invalidate his teruma
B. The imbecile priest might destroy or swallow the pouch
C. A leather pouch warms the penis and may itself cause the seminal emission it was meant to detect
D. Leather pouches cannot retain liquid and are therefore unreliable
Correct: C — The Sages argued that leather, by warming the penis, could itself trigger a seminal emission — creating the very impurity the pouch was designed to catch. The solution adopted in the baraita is a copper (metal) pouch, which does not warm and allows any emission to remain visible.
