🖐️ The Examining Hand

כָּל יָד הַמַּרְבָּה לִבְדֹּק — בַּנָּשִׁים מְשֻׁבַּחַת, וּבָאֲנָשִׁים תִּיקָּצֵץ
Core Contrast The Mishna opens with a striking asymmetry: a woman's diligent examining hand is praiseworthy, while a man's is condemned — even deserving severance. The Gemara unpacks why, expands into the grave prohibition of hotza'at zera levatalah (wasting seed), debates exactly when holding one's penis is permitted, and pivots to a new Mishna: women who cannot examine themselves require competent friends to assist them.

🔍 Why the Asymmetry? Examination in Men vs. Women

Mishna
A diligent examining hand: in women — praiseworthy; in men — should be severed.
Gemara Q
What is different about women vs. men? Both examine the same way — why are women praised while men are condemned?
Gemara A
Women are not susceptible to sexual arousal through self-examination. Men are — the act itself may trigger a seminal emission.
Follow-up Q
If so, even occasional examination by a man should be forbidden — why does the Mishna single out 'diligent' (frequent) examination for severance?
Resolution
Even occasional examination is prohibited. The Mishna highlights diligent examination as the extreme case deserving severance; the baseline prohibition still applies.
Baraita
Exception: A man may examine for discharge (not semen) using a rock or earthenware — hard materials that do not warm the body and cannot cause arousal.
Key Distinction The prohibition targets examination likely to cause seminal emission. Examining for other discharges with cold, hard materials carries no such risk and is permitted.

🤲 Rabbi Eliezer: May a Man Hold His Penis While Urinating?

Rabbi Eliezer: Anyone who holds his penis and urinates is as though he brings a flood to the world — absolutely forbidden
↓
Rabbis' Objection
If he does not hold it, he sprays urine on his legs and appears immoral to onlookers
R. Eliezer's Response
Better that people suspect his children are mamzerim than that he sin before the Omnipresent
↓
Rabbis press further: What if there is no elevated place or loose earth to prevent splashing?
↓
R. Eliezer's Practical Solution
Stand on an elevated place and urinate downward, or stand on loose earth that absorbs urine — problem solved without holding
Permitted Exception (Rava)
Priest holding his penis with a cloth to stop already-uprooted semen: permitted — the emission was inevitable regardless
↓
Gemara (13b): Abaye permits holding below the corona to direct urine flow; from the corona upward is prohibited

💧 Three Opinions on the Severity of Wasting Seed

Rabbi Yoḥanan

  • One who emits seed for naught is liable to death
  • Proof: Er and Onan — 'what he did was evil in God's eyes'
  • Both were killed by God for the same act
  • → Capital severity: treated like those executed by Heaven

Rabbi Yitzḥak & Rabbi Ami

  • It is as though he sheds blood
  • Proof (Isaiah 57): 'You who inflame yourselves… slaying the children in the valleys'
  • Verse juxtaposes illicit arousal with slaying of children
  • → Wasting potential life is equated with murder

Rav Asi

  • It is as though he worships idols
  • Written of Onan: 'under every leafy tree' (Isaiah 57)
  • Written of idols: 'under every leafy tree' (Deut. 12:2)
  • → Gezerah shavah: identical phrase links the two sins

⚗️ The Imbecile Priest: Enabling Him to Eat Teruma

Problem: An imbecile priest may emit semen involuntarily, rendering him ritually impure. How can he ever eat teruma?
↓
Basic Solution (Sages): Immerse him, then give teruma immediately. If he sleeps afterward → impure again. If he stays awake → pure and may eat.
↓
Rabbi Eliezer b. R. Tzadok: Wrap a leather pouch around his penis before giving teruma; check it afterward — no semen means he remained pure.
↓
Sages' Objection: A leather pouch warms the penis and may itself cause a seminal emission — the very impurity it was meant to detect!
↓
Baraita in name of R. Elazar: Use a metal (copper) pouch instead — copper does not absorb or warm, so any semen drop remains clearly visible.
↓
Rav Pappa's Inference: Since even a pouch can cause emission by warmth, priestly trousers that bind the penis too tightly are likewise prohibited — though the official priestly trousers were styled like horsemen's riding breeches and were considered different.
