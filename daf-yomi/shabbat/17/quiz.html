Shabbat 17 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Shimon ben Yohai, which of the following makes a person responsible for his own death?
A. Eating unsalted vegetables before a meal
B. Eating a peeled egg that was left out overnight
C. Drinking wine before morning prayers
D. Sleeping in the marketplace
Correct: B — R. Shimon ben Yohai lists five life-threatening actions. Eating peeled garlic, onion, or egg left overnight is the first — dangerous even if most of it remains and even if stored in a basket.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the Sages, what is the pious way to dispose of nail clippings?
A. Bury them in the ground
B. Throw them into moving water
C. Burn them completely
D. Give them to a Cohen
Correct: C — Three levels: burning = pious (eradicates completely), burying = righteous, throwing = wicked. Burning is highest because it eliminates any risk that a pregnant woman might miscarry by stepping over them.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rav Chisda prohibit intercourse during the day?
A. It is an explicit Biblical prohibition
B. Seeing a flaw in his wife might cause him to find her repulsive
C. The Sages required examination by daylight instead
D. It would interfere with Torah study hours
Correct: B — Rav Chisda derives this from 'Love your neighbor as yourself' — if a man sees a defect he had not noticed, he may find his wife repulsive, violating his obligation of loving care toward her.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the anatomy parable of the mishna, what does the 'upper story' (עלייה) represent?
A. The uterus
B. The vaginal canal
C. The bladder
D. The ovary
Correct: C — The parable maps: inner room = uterus (impure), upper story = bladder (pure), corridor = vaginal canal (uncertain). Bladder blood is pure because it is urinary, not menstrual.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Chiyya, what is the status of blood found in the corridor?
A. Definitely pure — it did not come from the uterus
B. Uncertain, with only rabbinic-level stringency
C. Definite menstrual blood — intercourse carries karet liability
D. Pure unless she had already been established as a niddah
Correct: C — Rabbi Chiyya rules that corridor blood is definite niddah blood at the Torah level — engaging in intercourse makes both partners liable for karet, the most severe penalty in Jewish law.
