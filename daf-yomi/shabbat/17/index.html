🌙 Intercourse by Day — Permitted or Forbidden?

Rav Chisda
Forbidden to engage in intercourse by day — derived from 'Love your neighbor as yourself': seeing a flaw in his wife, he may find her repulsive and violate this principle
Rava
If the house is dark, it is permitted even by day. A Torah scholar may darken with his garment — he will certainly conduct himself with proper modesty regardless
Rabba bar Rav Huna
Would ring bells (zagei) on the canopy before intercourse — so people nearby would know to keep away
Abaye
Would even drive away flies from the room — going beyond the minimum to ensure complete privacy and modesty
Mishna Resolved The phrase 'intercourse by lamp-light' in the mishna is reread as: she examines the cloth by lamp-light — not the act itself by lamp-light

🔬 Corridor Blood — What Level of Impurity?

Rav Huna
From the vestibule inward toward the uterus: definitely impure (Torah-level). From the vestibule outward: only uncertain, rabbinic-level impurity
Abaye
This distinction is incoherent. If we worry that blood moved because the woman leaned forward or backward, then ALL corridor blood is uncertain. If we presume position, ALL corridor blood near the uterus is definite.
Rabbi Chiyya
Blood found in the corridor is definite menstrual blood. If she engages in intercourse, both she and her partner are liable for karet — the full Torah penalty
Core Dispute Does the corridor's blood carry Torah-level (karet) liability, or only rabbinic-level uncertainty? Rav Huna splits the corridor; Abaye questions the split; Rabbi Chiyya treats all corridor blood as definite niddah.
