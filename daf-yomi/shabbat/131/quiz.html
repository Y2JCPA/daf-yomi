Shabbat 131 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Rabbi Yoḥanan clarifies Rabbi Eliezer's position. What is the correct understanding of Rabbi Eliezer's ruling on mitzva preparations and Shabbat?
A. All mitzva preparations override Shabbat without any exception
B. Only preparations for specific mitzvot with a fixed, irreplaceable time override Shabbat
C. No mitzva preparations ever override Shabbat — only the mitzva itself does
D. Only circumcision preparations override Shabbat; all others follow the Rabbis
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan explicitly teaches that Rabbi Eliezer's rule is not universal. It applies only to specific, enumerated mitzvot with fixed deadlines — not to all mitzvot broadly.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Adda bar Ahava, which two mitzvot does Rabbi Eliezer specifically concede do NOT override Shabbat?
A. Lulav and shofar
B. Matza and omer
C. Tzitzit and mezuza
D. Sukka and circumcision
Correct: C — Rav Adda bar Ahava taught — and a baraita confirms — that attaching tzitzit to a garment and affixing a mezuza to a doorpost do not override Shabbat, even according to Rabbi Eliezer.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Yosef, why don't tzitzit and mezuza preparations override Shabbat?
A. They are Rabbinic mitzvot, not Torah obligations
B. They have no fixed time — they are not time-bound mitzvot
C. They involve writing, which is a severe Shabbat prohibition
D. A specific Torah verse excludes them from Rabbi Eliezer's principle
Correct: B — Rav Yosef's reason is that tzitzit and mezuza have no fixed time. Since the obligation is not tied to a specific moment or deadline, there is no urgency that would justify overriding Shabbat.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rabbi Eliezer says 'on that day' (regarding lulav) permits Shabbat override. How does he separately derive that lulav must be performed by day and not by night?
A. From the phrase 'on that day' itself — 'day' implies daytime
B. From the verse 'you shall rejoice before the Lord your God seven days'
C. From a gezerah shavah with the omer offering
D. It is an oral tradition without a biblical source
Correct: B — Rabbi Eliezer derives the day/night distinction from 'you shall rejoice before the Lord seven days,' which implies daytime celebration. This frees up 'on that day' to teach that the mitzva applies even on Shabbat.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Through what gezerah shavah does Rabbi Eliezer derive that sukka preparations override Shabbat?
A. The shared word 'bringing' (הֲבָאָה) between sukka and circumcision
B. The shared term 'fifteenth' between sukka and matza
C. The shared term 'seven days' (שִׁבְעַת יָמִים) between sukka and lulav
D. The shared term 'on that day' between sukka and shofar
Correct: C — Rabbi Eliezer uses the expression 'seven days,' which appears in both the mitzva of sukka and the mitzva of lulav, to derive by verbal analogy that just as lulav facilitators override Shabbat, so too do sukka facilitators.
