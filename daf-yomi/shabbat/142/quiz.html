QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Huna citing Rav, when is it permitted to tilt a barrel sideways to dislodge a stone resting on its mouth?
A. Always, regardless of how the stone came to be on the barrel
B. Only when the stone was forgotten on the barrel before Shabbat, not intentionally placed
C. Only when the barrel contains non-sacred produce rather than wine
D. Only when one first removes all other barrels from the vicinity
Correct: B — Rav Huna taught that this leniency applies only when the stone was forgotten — not intentionally placed. Intentional placement makes the barrel a basis l'davar ha'assur, rendering it forbidden to move entirely.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara permit taking a basket with a stone inside it on Shabbat, despite the stone being muktzeh?
A. The basket's value exceeds the stone's, so the permitted item overrides
B. The basket has a hole, so the stone would fall through — it is not truly serving as a base for the stone
C. The stone can be shaken to one side and removed before lifting the basket
D. Fruit inside the basket outweighs the stone, nullifying its muktzeh status
Correct: B — Rav Hiyya bar Ashi said in Rava's name that it refers to a broken basket with a hole through which the stone would fall. Therefore the basket is not genuinely serving as a base (basis) for the prohibited stone.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rav Ḥisda limits the mishna's permission to move impure teruma to cases where the pure teruma is on the bottom. What is his reasoning?
A. Impure teruma is heavier and naturally settles to the bottom on its own
B. The person needs to access the pure teruma below — this need justifies moving the impure teruma resting on top
C. Impure teruma on the bottom would ritually defile the pure teruma above through contact
D. Only the bottom layer is considered a basis, not the top layer
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda argues that there must be a practical need driving the movement. When pure teruma is on the bottom, one has a genuine reason to move the impure teruma above it. If pure teruma is already on top, there is no such need.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What was Abaye's justification for placing a spoon on bundles of produce to permit moving them on Shabbat?
A. The spoon was the primary object being moved; the bundles were incidental
B. Were he not an important person, he could simply lean on the bundles directly — they are inherently fit for use
C. The spoon transformed the bundles into food-preparation equipment
D. The spoon served as a Shabbat designation marker for the bundles
Correct: B — Abaye explained that his dignity as an important person prevented him from leaning directly on bundles of produce. Since the bundles are normally fit to lean on, an ordinary person could move them without any trick — Abaye only needed the spoon because of his social standing.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Mar Zutra's ruling regarding clever workarounds (tilting, shaking, etc.) used to move muktzeh items on Shabbat?
A. All such workarounds are always permitted since they are indirect, not direct, contact
B. They are permitted only for objects of significant monetary value
C. They are permitted only when the item was forgotten; if intentionally left before Shabbat, no workaround is valid
D. They are permitted only for Torah scholars, not for ordinary people
Correct: C — Mar Zutra rules definitively that all leniencies — shaking, tilting, walking past — apply solely to cases of forgetfulness. When a person deliberately left a prohibited item in a location before Shabbat, he may not employ any artifice or clever technique to move it on Shabbat.
