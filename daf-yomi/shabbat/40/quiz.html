Shabbat 40 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Rabbis, why does a caesarean birth NOT trigger the halakhot of childbirth impurity?
A. The Rabbis hold that only the mother's pain matters, and caesarean is less painful
B. The phrase 'emits seed' requires the child to emerge through the birth canal as seed is emitted
C. The Rabbis follow Rabbi Yehuda who excludes all unusual births
D. Caesarean births were too rare to be addressed and excluded by default
Correct: B — The Rabbis derive from 'emits seed' (Lev. 12:2) that the child must emerge as seed does — through the birth canal. A caesarean delivery bypasses this requirement entirely.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rabbi Shimon include tumtum and hermaphrodite births in childbirth halakhot without using the 'emitted seed' phrase?
A. He holds tumtum and hermaphrodite are not actually included at all
B. He derives their inclusion from bar Livai's baraita based on 'when she is cleansed' (Lev. 12:8)
C. He uses the same phrase the Rabbis use but applies it differently
D. He derives it from a separate section of Leviticus entirely
Correct: B — Rabbi Shimon derives inclusion of tumtum and hermaphrodite from a baraita of bar Livai, freeing him to use 'if she gives birth' (Lev. 12:5) exclusively to include caesarean section births.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Shimon concede that a consecrated animal born by caesarean section does NOT acquire sanctity?
A. He holds caesarean applies to humans for impurity but not animals for sanctity
B. The analogy to firstborn animals (5 shared characteristics) is stronger than to human births (4 characteristics)
C. He fully agrees with the Rabbis regarding all animal-related laws
D. The Torah explicitly excludes caesarean-born animals from the altar
Correct: B — The Gemara reasons it is more logical to derive consecrated animal halakha from firstborn animals — they share five characteristics (its mother, sacrificial animal, male, offspring, same species) vs. only four with human births.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Shimon's expansion of the altar principle, which of these ascends the altar and shall NOT be taken down?
A. An animal that was worshipped as an idol
B. An animal used for bestiality
C. An offering whose blood was collected or sprinkled by an unfit priest
D. An animal set aside for idol worship
Correct: C — Rabbi Shimon includes ritually disqualified offerings — such as those handled by unfit priests — in the rule that what ascends stays. He excludes animals with moral/idolatry-related disqualifications, which do come down.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does the baraita derived from 'This is the law for one who gives birth, whether to a male or a female' teach about multiple births?
A. A woman may bring one offering to cover multiple births from separate pregnancies
B. A woman must bring separate offerings for each individual birth, not one for two
C. A woman with twins brings double the standard offering
D. The offering for a female birth is twice that for a male birth
Correct: B — The baraita teaches that each individual birth requires its own korban. One might have thought a single offering covers multiple births; the verse 'this is the law' teaches that each birth event is distinct and demands its own offering.
