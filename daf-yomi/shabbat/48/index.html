⚖️ Two Signs of Majority: Which Comes First?

Rabbi Meir
It IS possible for the upper sign (breast development) to appear before the lower sign (pubic hair). Therefore if only the upper sign is present, she is still a minor — she neither performs ḥalitza nor enters levirate marriage.
Rabbis
It is IMPOSSIBLE for the upper sign to precede the lower. If breasts appear, we assume pubic hair is already present — even if examination found none. She is an adult and must perform ḥalitza or levirate marriage.
Practical Consequence If a woman's childless husband dies and only breast development is visible with no pubic hair found: Rabbi Meir says she cannot act (still a minor); Rabbis say she is an adult — presume the lower sign exists.

🌿 Determining Status: The Two Signs of Majority

Young woman's husband dies childless
Must determine: minor (no ḥalitza obligation) or adult (ḥalitza/yibbum required)?
↓
✅ Lower sign present (pubic hair)
She is an adult — performs ḥalitza or yibbum (levirate marriage). Undisputed.
⚠️ Upper sign only (breasts developed)
Disputed:
Rabbis → adult (presume lower sign present)
Rabbi Meir → cannot act (still a minor)
❌ Neither sign present
She is a minor — may perform mi'un (refusal) to dissolve a Rabbinic marriage.

📖 Amud A: Do Rav and Rav Yosef Actually Disagree?

Abaye
Do you (Rav Yosef) and Rav disagree about the baraita's 'years of a son and daughter'? You apply it to ḥalitza; Rav applies it to valuations (עֵרֶךְ). Does each of you reject the other's application?
Rav Yosef
No — we do NOT disagree. Both halakhot require full years: both for valuations AND for ḥalitza. Each of us simply emphasized a different application of the same underlying principle.
The Proof If they truly disagreed, whoever requires full years for valuations should also require them for ḥalitza — since both involve Torah obligations. Their harmony is logically necessary, not merely asserted.

🏙️ Development Rates by Social Context (Amud B)

City Girls (בְּנוֹת הָעִיר)

  • Lower sign (pubic hair) appears first
  • Reason: active lifestyle and physical labor
  • Movement accelerates lower development
  • Per Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel

Village Girls (בְּנוֹת הַכְּפָרִים)

  • Upper sign (breasts) appears first
  • Reason: richer food and softer living
  • Better nutrition accelerates upper development
  • Per Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel

Wealthy vs. Poor (R. Shimon b. Elazar)

  • Wealthy: right breast develops before left
  • Reason: rubs against fine soft garments
  • Poor/city: left breast develops first
  • Dispute in baraita: R. Ḥanina disagrees entirely

⚖️ Rava's Presumption and the Logic of Women's Testimony

Rava's Rule: A minor girl who reaches her full years (12 years + 1 day) does not require examination — there is a presumption she has developed signs
↓
Consequence: Women may examine girls after 12y 1d to confirm — but the legal presumption already does the work
↓
Between 11y 1d and 12y 1d (Rabbi Yehuda): Women CANNOT examine — this is the critical window where signs are legally significant and testimony must be expert
↓
Between 11y 1d and 12y 1d (Rabbi Shimon): Women CAN examine — Shimon treats this period as still within the minor's legal status, so the stakes are lower
↓
Accepted only as stringency: A woman's examination can establish that a girl is an adult (ḥalitza required) — but CANNOT establish that she is a minor (to permit mi'un/refusal)
↓
Source (Baraita of Yavne): Rabbi Eliezer son of Rabbi Tzadok — once the lower sign appears, the lower sign alone is sufficient to establish majority
