Shabbat 48 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Rabbis, if a woman's upper sign (breasts) appears but no pubic hair is found upon examination, what is her halakhic status?
A. She is a minor — performs mi'un (refusal) if she wishes
B. She is an adult — we presume pubic hair is present even if not found
C. Her status remains uncertain until she is re-examined
D. She must wait until age 20 for a definitive ruling
Correct: B — The Rabbis hold it is impossible for the upper sign to precede the lower. If breasts are present, we presume pubic hair exists even if examination found nothing. She is therefore a legal adult obligated in ḥalitza.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rava rules that a girl who has reached 12 years and one day does not require examination for signs of puberty. What is his reasoning?
A. Women's testimony is always accepted after age 12
B. The Torah explicitly exempts her from examination at that age
C. There is a presumption (chazakah) that she has already developed signs of puberty
D. She automatically performs refusal (mi'un) at that point
Correct: C — Rava establishes that once a girl reaches 12 years and one day — her full years of majority — there is a halakhic presumption she has developed signs of puberty. This presumption replaces the need for physical examination.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel, in which type of girl does the LOWER sign (pubic hair) appear before the upper sign?
A. Daughters of the wealthy
B. Village girls
C. City girls
D. Daughters of Torah scholars
Correct: C — Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel says city girls develop the lower sign first due to more active lifestyles and physical labor. Village girls, who eat richer food and live more softly, develop the upper sign (breasts) first.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
During the critical period between 11 years + 1 day and 12 years + 1 day, Rabbi Yehuda and Rabbi Shimon disagree about women's examination. What is Rabbi Shimon's position?
A. Women may never examine girls in this age range under any circumstances
B. Women may examine during this period, because Rabbi Shimon treats it as still within the minor's legal status
C. Only male doctors may examine during this period
D. The examination is mandatory and must be done by the court itself
Correct: B — Rabbi Shimon holds that women MAY examine girls between 11y 1d and 12y 1d, because he treats this intermediate period as still falling within the minor's legal framework — whereas Rabbi Yehuda forbids it as the critical window where signs become legally significant.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The Gemara concludes that women's testimony about a girl's examination is accepted only 'as a stringency.' What does this mean practically?
A. Women may only testify in Rabbinic courts, not Biblical ones
B. A woman can testify that a girl is an adult (requiring ḥalitza) but cannot testify that she is a minor (to permit refusal/mi'un)
C. Women's testimony is accepted only if corroborated by a male witness
D. Women may only examine girls under age 11, not older girls
Correct: B — Women's examination testimony is accepted only in the stringent direction: they can establish that a girl has reached majority (making her obligated in ḥalitza), but they are NOT believed to establish that she is still a minor — which would permit the more lenient option of mi'un (refusal).
