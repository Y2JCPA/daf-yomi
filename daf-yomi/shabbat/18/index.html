🩸 Where Was the Blood Found?

Rav Ketina
Blood found anywhere in the passage is impure due to uncertainty (safek). We cannot determine its source.
Rav Huna
Blood in the inner section = definitely impure (presumed from the uterus). Blood in the outer section = impure due to uncertainty.
Rami bar Shmuel & Rav Yitzḥak b. Rav Yehuda
Blood from vestibule outward → uncertainty = pure. Blood from vestibule inward → definitely impure.
Rabbi Ḥiyya
Blood at the source is definitely impure. Rami bar Shmuel and Rav Yitzḥak agree — there is no dispute with this position.
Resolution Rami bar Shmuel and Rav Yitzḥak do not contradict Rabbi Ḥiyya — all agree blood at the source is definitely impure. Their leniency addresses a distinct, separate scenario.

📜 Three Cases Where Majority = Certainty

Dam HaMakor

  • Blood found at the source (uterus)
  • Majority of such blood originates from the uterus
  • → Treated as definitely impure (niddah)
  • Uncertainty fully resolved by rov

Shilya / Afterbirth

  • Afterbirth (shilya) found inside a house
  • Most afterbirths contain a fetus
  • → House is definitely impure (tum'at ohel)
  • Treated as if a corpse is present under the roof

Shaped Limb (Yad / Regel)

  • Woman miscarries a hand or foot with discernible fingers
  • Majority of shaped limbs come from a complete fetus
  • → Mother is definitely impure as after childbirth
  • Both birth offerings required

🔑 Majority vs. Presumptive Status

בשלשה מקומות הלכו חכמים אחר הרוב — Rabbi Yoḥanan, Niddah 18a
Key Exclusion Principle Rabbi Yoḥanan's rule also excludes any case where majority (rov) conflicts with an opposing presumptive status (chazakah). The dispute between Rabbi Meir and the Rabbis illustrates this: a tamei child is found next to tahor dough. Majority of children handle nearby items → dough is likely tamei. But the dough's prior purity (chazakah) opposes the majority. Rabbi Meir: majority wins, burn the teruma. Rabbis: when majority and chazakah conflict, certainty is not established — do not burn. Rabbi Yoḥanan sides with the Rabbis: conflicting chazakah negates certainty.
