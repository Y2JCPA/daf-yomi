Shabbat 18 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, blood found in the OUTER section of the passage is:
A. Definitely pure
B. Definitely impure
C. Impure due to uncertainty (safek)
D. Pure due to uncertainty
Correct: C — Rav Huna rules: inner section = definitely impure (presumed to come from the uterus); outer section = impure only due to uncertainty (safek tum'ah).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Which three cases does Rabbi Yoḥanan cite where uncertainty is treated as certainty?
A. Nine stores, nine frogs, pregnant woman crossing a river
B. Dam HaMakor, Shilya (afterbirth), and a shaped limb
C. Blood in inner section, blood in outer section, blood at source
D. Tamei child near dough, kosher stores, and shaped fetus
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan's three cases are: (1) blood at the source (dam hamakor), (2) afterbirth found in a house (shilya), and (3) a miscarried shaped limb — all tum'at nashim cases where rov creates certainty.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why is the case of nine kosher stores vs. one neveilah store excluded from Rabbi Yoḥanan's list?
A. Because it involves general tum'ah, not tum'at nashim
B. Because the ruling is amoraic, not from a Mishna
C. Because it involves issur (prohibition), not ritual impurity
D. Because the stores number only nine, not ten
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan's three cases concern ritual impurity (tum'ah). The nine stores case involves an issur — whether non-kosher meat may be eaten — which is a separate legal category.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why is Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi's ruling about the pregnant woman who miscarried in a river excluded from Rabbi Yoḥanan's list?
A. Because it involves issur, not tum'ah
B. Because the ruling is from an Amora, not a Mishna or Baraita
C. Because the majority of pregnant women do not form complete fetuses
D. Because the woman crossed water, which changes the legal status
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan's list is limited to cases derived from a Mishna or Baraita. Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi's ruling is an amoraic statement and thus does not qualify, regardless of its content.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the dispute about a tamei child found near tahor dough, why do the Rabbis rule NOT to burn the teruma?
A. Because most children do not touch items within reach
B. Because the child is presumed ritually pure by default
C. Because majority (rov) and presumptive status (chazakah) conflict, preventing certainty
D. Because Rabbi Meir agrees that the dough remains pure
Correct: C — The Rabbis hold that when the majority (most children handle nearby items → dough is likely tamei) conflicts with an opposing chazakah (the dough's prior purity), certainty cannot be established. Rabbi Yoḥanan sides with the Rabbis: this conflict excludes the case from his list of three.
