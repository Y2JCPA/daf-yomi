🔥 Destructive Shabbat Labor: Liable or Exempt?

One performs a labor on Shabbat destructively
↓
General Rule
Exempt
Mishna: destructive intent removes liability
Exception
Liable
Wounding and kindling a fire — always liable (Rabbi Abbahu's baraita)
↓
Why are wounds & fire different?
Rav Ashi: A wound heals the body (removes bad blood); burning for execution is an act of constructive justice — both retain their constructive dimension

🦌 Where Must an Animal Be Trapped? Rabbi Yehuda vs. the Rabbis

Rabbi Yehuda
One who traps a bird into a closet or cage, or a deer into a house, is liable. A house is a complete enclosure.
The Rabbis
One is liable for a bird in a closet, and a deer in a garden, courtyard, or enclosure (bivar). A house alone is too large for a bird.
Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel
Not all enclosures are identical. The rule: if the trapping is inadequate (animal still eludes capture), one is liable. If the trapping is complete, one is exempt from further liability.
Core Question Does the confinement actually 'trap' the animal such that one can readily capture it? That determines liability — not the label of the space.

🏡 Small vs. Large Enclosure — Rav Ashi's Test

An animal is confined in an enclosure on Shabbat
↓
Can one run after the animal and catch it in a single lunge? (Rav Ashi's definition)
↓
Yes — one lunge suffices
Small enclosure
Trapping is complete
One who locked it is liable for trapping
No — animal still evades
Large enclosure
Trapping is inadequate
One who locked it is exempt
↓
Special case — Shmuel: Trapping a lion requires its actual cage. A house is too large. Until the lion enters its cage, it is not considered trapped.

🦌 Types of Deer and Trapping Liability

Liable — Genuine Trapping Required

  • 🦌 Blind deer — cannot see to flee but still capable
  • 😴 Sleeping deer — will flee once awakened
  • Both can potentially escape → trapping them requires real effort → liable

Exempt — Already Effectively 'Pre-Trapped'

  • 🦿 Lame deer — cannot run effectively
  • 👴 Old deer — too slow to escape
  • 🤒 Sick deer — too weak to flee
  • Nature has already done the trapping → confining them adds nothing → exempt

🤝 Joint Trapping — Two People, One Deer

A deer enters a house on its own. One person alone locks the door.
↓
→ That person is liable — performed a complete act of trapping.
↓
Two people together lock the door; neither could have locked it alone.
↓
→ Both are exempt — neither individually performed a complete labor (principle of joint labor exemption).
↓
One person sits in the doorway but does not fill the gap; a second person sits and fills the remaining space.
↓
→ The second person is liable — they completed the trapping. The first person, who contributed but did not complete the act, is exempt.
↓
Rabbi Yirmeya bar Abba / Shmuel: Even one person alone trapping a lion is only liable once it is in its cage — a house is not sufficient confinement for a lion.
