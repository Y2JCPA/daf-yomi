✂️ Daf 137: Circumcision Laws & Shabbat Conflicts

מסכת שבת דף קל״ז
Core Theme The daf opens with a three-way tannaitic dispute — Rav Huna, Rav Yehuda, Rabbi Ḥiyya — about whether one who circumcised the wrong baby on Shabbat is liable for a sin-offering, rooted in whether mitzva intent creates exemption. It then establishes timing rules for delayed circumcision (days 8–12), recovery protocols after illness, the technical requirements for a valid circumcision (removal, uncovering, fleshy child), the blessings recited for different circumcisions, and closes with Rabbi Eliezer's lenient view on suspending a strainer on a Festival.

⚔️ Three Versions of the Tannaitic Dispute

Rav Huna
First clause = LIABLE. Based on Rabbi Shimon ben Elazar's baraita: Rabbi Eliezer and Rabbi Yehoshua both agree that one who circumcised the post-Shabbat baby on Shabbat is liable. They only dispute the Shabbat-eve baby case.
Rav Yehuda
First clause = EXEMPT. Based on Rabbi Meir's baraita: both Tanna'im agree the post-Shabbat baby case is exempt, since the circumcisor was preoccupied with performing a mitzva. They only dispute the Shabbat-eve baby.
Rabbi Ḥiyya
A third version of Rabbi Meir: agrees on Shabbat-eve baby (liable), but reverses the logic — making Rabbi Yehoshua liable in the less severe case and exempt in the more severe. The Gemara challenges this as internally contradictory.
Root of the Dispute Rabbi Eliezer: Unwitting transgression incurs liability regardless of mitzva intent — derived from idolatry: the act itself obligates. Rabbi Yehoshua: If preoccupied with a mitzva, one is exempt — because unlike idolatry, the intent here was to fulfill a commandment.

🤒 Sick Baby: Recovery Before Circumcision

Shmuel's Ruling

  • Baby had a fever (חִמּוּם)
  • Once fever fully breaks: wait 7 days before circumcising
  • Dilemma raised: Is it 7 calendar days or 7 full 24-hour periods?
  • Suggested proof: 'Day of healing = day of birth' — as with birth, no need for time-to-time
  • The luda baraita is cited as possible support

Gemara's Conclusion

  • Proof rejected: Day of healing is stricter than day of birth
  • Birth: 8 calendar days is sufficient (no time-to-time required)
  • Recovery: must wait 7 complete periods — time to time
  • Greater caution after illness than after normal birth
  • Principle: danger to life overrides all — circumcision waits

🍷 Strainer on a Festival: Tent or Tool?

Rabbi Eliezer
PERMITS suspending a strainer on a Festival. Acknowledges it constitutes tent-building, but holds that food-preparation labors are permitted on Festivals — and extends Rabbi Yehuda's leniency even to acts that could have been done the day before.
The Rabbis
PROHIBIT suspending the strainer on a Festival. One may only place wine through a strainer already hung from before the Festival. If suspended unwittingly: Rav Yosef — liable (biblical tent-building prohibition).
Abaye
Challenges Rav Yosef: If suspending a strainer = prohibited tent-building, then hanging a jug on a peg should also be biblically prohibited as tent construction? (Reductio ad absurdum — strainer suspension cannot truly be full tent-building.)
Background Rule (Rabba bar bar Ḥana / Rabbi Yoḥanan) All Tanna'im agree one may not construct a new tent on Shabbat or a Festival. The dispute is only about adding to an existing temporary tent. Rabbi Eliezer's leniency for the strainer is therefore a unique position grounded in food-preparation needs.
