🫧 Which Bodily Fluids Transmit Severe Impurity?

Opening Question
Blood from the opening of the penis comes from an impure place — the same place that emits ziva. Should it not be impure?
Baraita (R. Shimon b. Yochai)
No. For a fluid to transmit severe impurity it must resemble saliva: it gathers together, then exits, and if not expelled it causes pain. Blood from the penis does not gather — it is pure.
Objection
But breast milk gathers first and then exits the body — so it should transmit severe impurity! Yet the Masters ruled otherwise.
R. Yoḥanan (in R. Shimon b. Yochai's name)
Milk does not cause pain if not expelled. All three conditions are required: gather, exit, and retention causes pain. Milk fails the third test.
Counter-Objection
But ziva does not first gather before exiting — yet it transmits impurity! So why require gathering at all?
Resolution Ziva is a Torah prototype and proves impurity can exist without gathering. The three-part rule (gather + exit + pain) governs all other candidate fluids seeking to reach ziva's level of severity.

🔍 Retroactive Impurity: Alleyway Sheretz vs. Bloodstained Garment

🐍 Sheretz Found in Alleyway

  • Carcass discovered → items that passed through are impure retroactively
  • Back to last time alleyway was swept AND examined
  • Dilemma: does sweeping alone imply examination?
  • Practical difference: sweeper said 'I swept but did not examine'
  • Also: carcass found in a hole in the ground — did examination reach there?

🩸 Blood Stain Found on Robe

  • Stain discovered → items she touched are impure retroactively
  • Back to last time robe was laundered AND examined
  • Dilemma: does laundering alone imply examination?
  • Practical difference: launderer said 'I laundered but did not examine'
  • R. Yehuda HaNasi: stain appearance differs before vs. after laundering — inspect the stain itself

🏘️ Samaritan Credibility: The Governing Principle

כָּל דָּבָר שֶׁהֵן חֲשׁוּדִין עָלָיו — אֵין מְקַבְּלִין עֵדוּתָן
כְּלָל — General Rule (End of Daf) Any matter of halakha that Samaritans are suspected of not fulfilling — they are not deemed credible to testify about it.

✅ They ARE trusted to testify: Whether they buried stillborn children in a particular house (transmitting corpse-tent impurity), or did not bury them there.

❌ They are NOT trusted to testify: About overhanging boughs, protruding grave-markers, or field stones — because they are suspected of neglecting the halakhot governing these.

Their house of impurity (where stillborns are buried) transmits tent-impurity like a corpse-house. R. Yehuda disagrees: it transmits only contact impurity, not tent-impurity.
