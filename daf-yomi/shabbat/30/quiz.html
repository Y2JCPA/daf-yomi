Shabbat 30 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Beit Hillel, how many immersions must a woman perform in the baraita's scenario?
A. 35 immersions
B. 95 immersions
C. 28 immersions
D. 66 immersions
Correct: A — Beit Hillel counts only the immersions required before intercourse is permitted — totaling 35. Beit Shammai counts both before and after permission is granted, reaching 95.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rabbi Akiva require regarding the seven clean days of a zava?
A. They must be counted in the court's presence
B. They may be counted privately at home
C. Only three of the seven days must be observed
D. The count begins from the first day of discharge
Correct: A — Rabbi Akiva holds that the seven clean days of a zava must be counted 'in our presence' — they must be actively observed and verified, not merely self-reported.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why do the Rabbis call Rabbi Yishmael's proof from Cleopatra's experiment a 'proof from fools'?
A. The maidservant carrying a female may have conceived before the controlled date
B. Cleopatra's servants were not reliable witnesses
C. Dissecting fetuses is halakhically prohibited
D. The experiment was performed outside the Land of Israel
Correct: A — The Rabbis argue that one cannot confirm the maidservant carrying the female embryo conceived exactly when ordered — she may have conceived earlier, making the embryo older than 81 days at examination.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Samlai's aggadah, what causes the newborn to forget all the Torah it learned?
A. An angel slaps it on the mouth at birth
B. The trauma of birth itself erases memory
C. God withholds the knowledge until adulthood
D. The evil inclination enters and displaces Torah
Correct: A — At the moment of birth, an angel slaps the baby on its mouth, causing it to forget all Torah. The verse 'sin crouches at the entrance' (Gen. 4:7) is interpreted as: the moment one enters the world, forgetfulness and the yetzer hara are waiting.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the content of the oath administered to the fetus before birth?
A. Be righteous; even if the world calls you righteous, consider yourself wicked
B. Observe all 613 commandments faithfully
C. Honor your father and mother above all else
D. Swear to study Torah every day without interruption
Correct: A — The oath is: Be righteous, do not be wicked — and even if the entire world declares you righteous, consider yourself wicked. Know that God is pure, His ministers are pure, and the soul entrusted to you is pure. Return it in purity.
