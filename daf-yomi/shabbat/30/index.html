🧫 When Does a Female Embryo Form? Rabbi Yishmael vs. the Rabbis

Rabbi Yishmael
A male embryo forms in 41 days; a female embryo forms in 81 days. The Torah doubles the impurity period for a female birth — this mirrors doubled formation time.
The Rabbis
Both male and female embryos complete formation on the 41st day. The doubled purity period reflects different laws, not different formation timelines.
Rabbi Yishmael
Proof from Cleopatra's experiment: sentenced maidservants were examined at precise intervals — female embryos were fully formed only after 81 days, males after 41.
The Rabbis
That is a 'proof from fools' — we cannot confirm those women conceived precisely when ordered. The female embryo may have been older than assumed. Halakha: 41 days for both.
Why the Mishna Repeats the Rabbis' View The Rabbis' opinion matches the first tanna — yet the mishna restates it explicitly. This teaches: lest one think that Rabbi Yishmael's scriptural derivation is so compelling that the halakha follows him, the mishna makes clear we rule with the Rabbis.

🛁 Total Immersions After Childbirth: Two Schools

Beit Hillel — 35 Immersions

  • Count only immersions before intercourse is permitted
  • Immersions performed after permission is granted are excluded
  • Woman who immerses during the long day of purity does not need re-immersion when purity ends
  • Principle: count only mandatory gates to permitted status

Beit Shammai — 95 Immersions

  • Count immersions both before and after intercourse is permitted
  • Include immersions for possible zava status
  • Include every nightly immersion throughout the entire sequence
  • Principle: count all halakhically obligatory immersions regardless of timing

⚖️ Woman Who Appears Before the Court at Twilight

Woman arrives at twilight — status uncertain (day or night?). Her halakhic category is unknown.
↓
Scenario A
She is a zava gedola — must count 7 clean days in the court's presence (Rabbi Akiva). Immerse nightly during first week.
Scenario B
Her childbirth impurity period just ended — she must immerse each night of the first week before intercourse is permitted.
Scenario C
She is a zava ketana (1–2 day discharge) — immersion on day of arrival may count; no full seven-day count required.
↓
Rav Yirmeya of Difti: We apply the most lenient valid ruling for each scenario, accounting for all halakhic possibilities simultaneously.

🕯️ The Fetus in Its Mother's Womb

בבטן אמו מלמדין אותו כל התורה כולה — וכיון שיצא לאויר העולם בא מלאך וסטרו על פיו ומשכחו
Aggadah — Rabbi Samlai To what is a fetus comparable? To a folded notebook (lefinkas). It lies with hands on temples, arms on knees, heels pressed to its buttocks.

A candle burns above its head, and by its light the fetus gazes from one end of the world to the other — as it is written: 'When His lamp shined above my head' (Job 29:3).

An angel teaches the fetus the entire Torah while in the womb. No days in a person's life are more blissful than those spent as a fetus.

At the moment of birth, an angel slaps the baby on the mouth — causing it to forget all Torah it ever learned. 'Sin crouches at the entrance' (Gen. 4:7): the moment one enters the world, forgetfulness and the evil inclination arrive.

🤝 The Oath Administered Before Birth

Before emerging into the world, the fetus is brought before angels to receive a solemn oath
↓
'That to Me every knee shall bow' (Isaiah 45:23) — interpreted as the fetus bowing in the womb; 'every tongue shall swear' — the oath itself
↓
The Oath: Be righteous and do not be wicked. Even if the entire world says you are righteous — consider yourself wicked.
↓
Know that the Holy One is pure, His ministers are pure. The soul given to you is pure. Guard it in purity and return it in purity.
↓
Parable (School of Rabbi Yishmael): Like a priest who gives teruma to an am ha'aretz — the soul is entrusted to you though you are unreliable. It will be demanded back. Guard it faithfully.
