Shabbat 152 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What does the Gemara say the phrase 'the almond tree shall blossom' in Ecclesiastes 12 represents?
A. Wisdom flowering in advanced years
B. Hair turning white like almond blossoms
C. The coccyx / hip bone protruding, appearing white
D. A metaphor for spiritual renewal before death
Correct: C — The Gemara interprets the blossoming almond tree as the hip bone (coccyx) becoming prominent in old age, protruding and appearing white like almond blossoms — a sign of physical decline.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Eliezer, where are the souls of the righteous stored after death?
A. In the Garden of Eden
B. Beneath the Throne of Glory
C. In the celestial academy (Yeshiva shel Maalah)
D. With the ministering angels
Correct: B — Rabbi Eliezer derives from I Samuel 25:29 — 'the soul of my lord shall be bound in the bundle of life' — that righteous souls are stored beneath the Throne of Glory.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why was the body of Rav Aḥai bar Yoshiya not decomposed when the diggers found it?
A. He was a great Torah scholar whose body was miraculously preserved by God
B. He had been buried only recently
C. He never harbored envy — 'a tranquil heart is the life of the flesh' (Proverbs 14:30)
D. He had been anointed with special oils before burial
Correct: C — Rav Aḥai cited Proverbs 14:30: 'A tranquil heart is the life of the flesh, but envy is the rotting of the bones.' Because he never harbored envy, his flesh did not rot.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When Rabba asked Rav Naḥman about the fate of middling souls, what was the significance of Rav Naḥman's reply?
A. He said they are purified in Gehinnom before ascending
B. He said they are reincarnated to complete their mission
C. He said even if he were dead he could not tell — implying he himself was a middling person
D. He said they rest in a dreamlike state until the resurrection
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman replied that even if he were dead he could not answer — a self-deprecating admission that he himself was a middling person and would experience that unknown fate firsthand.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The baraita on 'the spirit returns to God who gave it' (Ecclesiastes 12:7) teaches what core obligation?
A. One must recite a deathbed confession (vidui) to return the soul properly
B. Return the soul to God as pure as He gave it — preserve its purity throughout life
C. The soul returns automatically regardless of one's conduct
D. One must recite Shema at the exact moment of death
Correct: B — The baraita teaches: 'Just as He gave it to you in purity, give it back to Him in purity' — meaning one is obligated to safeguard the soul's purity throughout one's entire life.
