QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did the attempt to save the Jewish official bitten by a snake in Pumbedita fail?
A. The snake's venom was too potent for any remedy to work
B. All 13 white donkeys slaughtered for the remedy were found to be tereifot (internally defective)
C. The official refused to eat the donkey meat on religious grounds
D. The one remaining donkey escaped before it could be caught
Correct: B — All 13 white donkeys in Pumbedita were slaughtered as a remedy, but all were found to be tereifot — rendering them halachically invalid. The only remaining donkey was across town, and the official died before it could be brought.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Yosef, what are the three components of Egyptian zitom ('water that stabs')?
A. One-third wheat, one-third saffron, one-third cumin
B. One-third dates, one-third wax, one-third red aloe
C. One-third barley, one-third saffron, one-third salt
D. One-third Alexandrian gum, one-third alum, one-third garden saffron
Correct: C — Rav Yosef identifies Egyptian zitom as one-third barley, one-third saffron, and one-third salt. Rav Pappa disagrees, replacing barley with wheat and adding cumin alongside the salt.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What are the three ingredients in the kos ikarin (cup of roots) as described by Rabbi Yoḥanan?
A. Persian dates, dripping wax, and red aloe
B. Alexandrian gum, alum, and garden saffron
C. Egyptian saffron, cumin, and Persian onions
D. Barley, salt, and Alexandrian gum
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan specifies: a weight of a zuz of Alexandrian gum, a weight of a zuz of alum, and a weight of a zuz of garden saffron — boiled together in wine with Persian onions. It is given to a zava to stop her discharge.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the halachic conclusion regarding a woman who drinks a remedy that causes sterility?
A. It is forbidden for both men and women equally
B. It is forbidden but permitted for medical purposes in a life-threatening situation
C. It is permitted for women, who are not obligated in the commandment to be fruitful and multiply
D. It is permitted only if a rabbi grants specific permission
Correct: C — The Gemara concludes that the prohibition against causing sterility falls on men, who are commanded in pru u'rvu. Women are exempt from this positive commandment and may therefore drink a cup of roots even though it renders them sterile.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the Arab's folk remedy for jaundice, what were the two key elements of his cure?
A. Drinking beer with saffron and sleeping near a fire
B. Eating raw leeks and sleeping in a field where night dew fell on him
C. Fasting for three days and then eating Persian dates
D. Bathing in the river and drinking a cup of palm water
Correct: B — The Arab ate raw leeks (obtained by trading his cloak) and then slept in an open field where the night dew fell upon him. In the morning he was cured of his jaundice — illustrating a folk remedy outside the formal treatments discussed in the Gemara.
