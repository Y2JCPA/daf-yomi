💧 Pure Blood: One Source or Two?

Rav
Pure and impure blood both emerge from one source. The Torah simply declared the blood pure during her days of purity and impure during her days of impurity — no physiological distinction.
Levi
There are two separate sources in a woman's body — one active during days of impurity, one during days of purity. They never operate simultaneously.
Practical Difference A woman who continuously bleeds from within her days of impurity into her days of purity: Rav says the blood is now pure; Levi says be stringent — continuous flow indicates the impure source is still active.

⚖️ Sightings of Ziva for a Leper-Zav

Rava's derivation: The verse uses the word 'issue' (זובו) twice — this indicates a second sighting of ziva for a leper is explicitly included by the Torah.
↓
Challenge from Rav Yehuda of Diskarta: Maybe the verse addresses a simple zav, not a leper-zav? If the kal va-chomer (a fortiori) argument already covers the case, a verse may be unnecessary.
↓
First sighting — Abaye's derivation: The verse 'This is the law of the zav' (Lev. 15:32) compares the leper to a full-fledged zav: just as the zav imparts impurity through carrying, so too the leper's first sighting of ziva does.
↓
Why treat the leper separately? Rabbi Yitzḥak: A zav was once included under ba'al keri (seminal emission) status — he was given his own passage to be stringent: he renders bedding and seat impure as a primary source of impurity.

🤱 Post-Birth Blood: Not Yet Immersed

Beit Shammai
The blood is like saliva or urine — imparts impurity only while moist. The days alone render her pure; immersion is not the turning point. Therefore dry blood has no impurity.
Beit Hillel
Blood imparts impurity whether moist or dry — like menstrual blood. Until she immerses she retains her status, and the blood reflects full menstrual-level impurity.
Both Agree — Woman Who Births as a Zava A yoledet ke-zava must count 7 clean days. If she did not immerse and then sees blood — Beit Shammai concede to Beit Hillel: the blood is impure whether moist or dry.

💧 Rav vs Levi — The Anatomy of Uterine Blood

Rav

  • One anatomical source for all uterine blood
  • Torah declares blood pure or impure based on timing (days of purity/impurity)
  • Continuous bleeding from impurity into purity → blood becomes pure
  • Beit Shammai/Hillel dispute: whether days alone vs. immersion triggers purity
  • Verse 'as in the days of her menstrual sickness' teaches nights are included too

Levi

  • Two physiologically distinct sources in the body
  • Impure source active during days of impurity; pure source during days of purity
  • The two sources are never simultaneously active
  • Continuous bleeding from impurity into purity → stringent (still from impure source)
  • Beit Shammai/Hillel dispute: which source a given blood flow originates from

↓
