QUESTION 1 OF 5
What does Rav Naḥman say about the Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel positions in the mishna about clearing the table?
A. Beit Shammai follows Rabbi Shimon and Beit Hillel follows Rabbi Yehuda
B. Reverse the opinions — Beit Shammai follows Rabbi Yehuda and Beit Hillel follows Rabbi Shimon
C. Both schools ultimately agree with Rabbi Yehuda's stringent ruling
D. The opinions cannot be mapped onto any Tannaitic position
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman says to reverse the attributed opinions: the stringent Beit Shammai (hand-only clearing) aligns with Rabbi Yehuda who prohibits muktzeh, while the lenient Beit Hillel (shake the board) aligns with Rabbi Shimon who permits moving set-aside items.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why is wiping a table with a handleless sponge prohibited even according to Rabbi Shimon?
A. A sponge without a handle is muktzeh and cannot be moved at all
B. Rabbi Shimon prohibits all forms of table-cleaning on Shabbat
C. Squeezing liquid from a handleless sponge is inevitable — a psik reisha that even Rabbi Shimon forbids
D. The Rabbis of the mishna issued a special decree against sponge use
Correct: C — Abaye and Rava both rule that Rabbi Shimon agrees in a case of psik reisha — where the forbidden act (squeezing/sechita) is completely inevitable. Without a handle, one cannot wipe without squeezing. The leather handle allows wiping without inevitable squeezing, making it permitted.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Yehuda citing Shmuel, where do Rabbi Yehuda and the Rabbis actually concede to each other regarding seeping fruit liquid?
A. Rabbi Yehuda concedes that all seeping liquid is prohibited; the Rabbis make no concession
B. The Rabbis concede for olives and grapes; Rabbi Yehuda concedes for all other fruits
C. Rabbi Yehuda concedes for olives and grapes; the Rabbis concede for other fruits
D. Both sides agree that designation before Shabbat is the only relevant factor
Correct: C — Rav Yehuda in Shmuel's name teaches: Rabbi Yehuda concedes to the Rabbis for olives and grapes (pressing fruits whose liquid is always prohibited), and the Rabbis concede to Rabbi Yehuda that for other fruits designated for eating, seeping liquid is permitted.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does the mishna permit when rescuing wine or oil from a broken barrel on Shabbat?
A. Soaking up all the wine with a cloth and storing it in vessels for later
B. Rescuing food sufficient for three meals and inviting others to take for themselves
C. Collecting all the oil in large vessels so none is wasted
D. Leaving everything in place, as active rescue is prohibited on Shabbat
Correct: B — The mishna permits rescuing food sufficient for three meals and inviting others to come take for themselves — each for their personal Shabbat use. However, soaking up wine or deliberately collecting oil in a commercial manner is prohibited as it resembles weekday conduct.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How did Rav Huna son of Rav Yehoshua deal with date pits on Shabbat, and what principle did his method rely upon?
A. He carried them alongside bread, relying on the principle of piggybacking on permitted items
B. He left them in place, ruling they were fully muktzeh and untouchable
C. He rendered them a chamber pot with excrement — repulsive vessels may be moved on Shabbat
D. He used his tongue to move them, avoiding any hand contact
Correct: C — Rav Huna would render the date pits a chamber pot by placing excrement on them. The Gemara explains that repulsive vessels (כלי מאוס) may be moved on Shabbat — their repulsiveness gives them a functional status. He thus moved the entire collection out because of their disgusting state.
