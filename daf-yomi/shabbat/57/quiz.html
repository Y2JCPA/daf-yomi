Shabbat 57 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What verse did the Samaritans interpret to justify not burying stillborn children, and how did they read it?
A. "You shall not murder" — a stillborn was never alive
B. "You shall not remove your neighbor's boundary" — only one with an inheritance requires burial
C. "Honor your father and mother" — a stillborn has no parents to honor it
D. "You shall not covet" — there is no estate to inherit
Correct: B — The Samaritans derived from the boundary verse that burial rites apply only to those who held an inheritance during their lifetime. The Sages disagreed, reading the verse as prohibiting the sale of ancestral burial grounds.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Yehuda bar Ami (citing Rav Yehuda), when is a beit haperas considered pure?
A. When a Samaritan kohen testifies it is pure
B. When it has been trodden underfoot by many people
C. When it has been washed by rain for three seasons
D. When a Torah sage walks over it without becoming impure
Correct: B — Heavy foot traffic grinds small bone fragments below the olive-size threshold and disperses them, removing the impurity concern of the beit haperas.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the mishna (Amud B), what is the status of a blood stain found on the OUTER side of a woman's leg?
A. Impure — blood could drip to any side
B. Impure — the outer leg is near the inner thigh
C. Pure — uterine blood cannot realistically reach the outer leg
D. Disputed between Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel
Correct: C — Uterine blood flows downward along the inner leg. The mishna rules the outer side pure because uterine blood cannot reach there, making an external source more likely.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Shmuel's ruling when a woman examines the ground beneath her, finds it clean, and later discovers a blood stain on her flesh?
A. She is impure — blood on flesh is always presumed uterine
B. She is impure — only a rabbinic-appointed examiner can grant exemption
C. She is pure — the prior clean examination attributes the stain to an external source
D. She must wait seven days and then re-examine
Correct: C — Shmuel rules that a prior clean bedikah of the ground establishes a presumption of purity. A stain found afterward on her flesh is attributed to an external cause, not the uterus.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does a blood stain found anywhere on a night robe (used as a blanket while sleeping) render the woman impure — even if the location would ordinarily indicate purity?
A. Nighttime blood is halachically more severe
B. The robe shifts and moves during sleep, making stain location unreliable
C. A sleeping woman lacks the intent required for purity
D. Night robes always contact the inner thigh and are presumed contaminated
Correct: B — Since the robe moves around while a woman sleeps, a stain could end up anywhere on the garment regardless of where the blood originated. The location is therefore not informative, and any stain on it renders her impure.
