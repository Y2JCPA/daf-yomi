🔴 Two Major Disputes of Niddah 15

Rav
A woman who finds a blood stain retroactively renders impure the man with whom she engaged in intercourse — like Rabbi Meir's stringent opinion
Shmuel
She renders him impure only from now onward — like the Rabbis; two rabbinic decrees do not combine to create retroactive effect
Rav Huna
A wife with a fixed cycle (veset kavua) whose projected day has arrived is forbidden to her returning husband — the concern is Torah-level
Rabba bar bar Ḥana
Even when her projected day arrives, she remains permitted — veset concern is only rabbinic, so presumptive purity (chezkat tahara) still prevails
📌 Core Issue Does rabbinic impurity interact with another rabbinic impurity to create combined stringency? And does a fixed cycle generate Torah-level or merely rabbinic-level concern?

🩸 Blood Stain: Retroactive Impurity or Forward Only?

Rav
Retroactive impurity applies. Like Rabbi Meir: a blood stain renders her husband impure retroactively. The mishna's ruling (Rabbis concede to Rabbi Akiva on stains) confirms this approach.
Shmuel
No retroactive effect. Like the Rabbis: a stain only creates future prohibition. We do not layer two rabbinic decrees — both retroactive impurity AND stain impurity are rabbinic.
Reish Lakish
Agrees with Rav — retroactive impurity applies, following Rabbi Meir
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Agrees with Shmuel — no retroactive effect; follows the Rabbinic position
Why the Mishna Bothered to State This Lest you think: since both retroactive impurity AND blood-stain impurity are rabbinic decrees, they cancel each other. The Gemara teaches they do NOT — because a blood stain is 'there before you', a distinct category of rabbinic impurity.

📅 Fixed Cycle (Veset Kavua): Is Wife Permitted to Returning Husband?

Rav Huna — Stringent

  • Veset kavua concern = Torah law
  • Projected day arrived → wife is forbidden
  • Presumptive purity does NOT override Torah-level doubt
  • Exception: cycle based on days and physical sensation (two conditions needed) — still permitted
  • Cycle of days alone → forbidden when day arrives

Rabba bar bar Ḥana — Lenient

  • Veset kavua concern = rabbinic law only
  • Even if projected day arrived → wife remains permitted
  • Presumptive purity (chezkat tahara) overrides rabbinic-level doubt
  • No distinction between types of fixed cycles
  • Wife is always permitted unless she actually saw blood

⚖️ Uncertainty vs. Certainty — A Foundational Principle

The Principle: A safek (uncertainty) does not override a vadai (certainty). When she certainly saw blood, we cannot assume she immersed to resolve the doubt.
↓
Challenge from a baraita: A ḥaver's storehouse — produce is presumed tithed (certainty), yet we still ask if it requires tithing. Doesn't uncertainty override certainty here?
↓
Answer 1: There it's certainty vs. certainty — the ḥaver certainly tithed. The question is only whether an obligation existed, not whether the act was performed.
↓
Answer 2 (Rabbi Oshaya): Or: it's uncertainty vs. uncertainty — perhaps the produce was never obligated in tithing at all (not yet fully processed for the market).
↓
Second challenge: A maidservant cast a non-viable newborn into a pit — yet a kohen standing over the pit was ruled pure! (Martens/hyenas likely dragged it away.) Uncertainty overrode certainty!
↓
Resolution: Re-read the baraita: perhaps she may have cast a non-viable newborn — it is not certain she did. The case is uncertainty vs. uncertainty, not overriding a certainty.
