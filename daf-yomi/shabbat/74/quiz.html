Shabbat 74 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Hamnuna, what is the decisive criterion for permitted selection (Borer) on Shabbat?
A. The amount selected must be less than a dried fig-bulk
B. One must select food from waste and consume it immediately — not set aside for later
C. One must use only a basket or plate, not a sieve or sorting tool
D. Selection is only permitted by a Torah scholar who knows the laws
Correct: B — Rav Hamnuna holds that the key is: food from waste, eaten immediately. He rejected Rav Yosef's basket/plate distinction as foreign to the baraita text.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Ḥizkiya rule that selecting lupines from their boiled shells is prohibited, even though it appears to be food-from-waste?
A. Lupines are not halachically classified as food
B. The Sages issued a special rabbinical decree for lupines
C. Since the lupine rots the shell and causes it to reek, the shell becomes the 'food' and the lupine becomes the waste
D. Ḥizkiya holds that all forms of Borer are Torah-prohibited
Correct: C — Lupines are boiled seven times; if left inside they cause the shell to reek. The shell is therefore the desired item being protected, and removing the lupine = waste-from-food = prohibited.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rava rules that one who unwittingly makes an earthenware barrel on Shabbat is liable for how many sin-offerings?
A. Three
B. Five
C. Seven
D. Thirty-nine
Correct: C — Rava enumerates seven distinct primary labors: (1) grinding, (2) selecting, (3) kneading, (4) cutting, (5) building, (6) smoothing, and (7) kindling — each performed in the barrel-making process.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the Tabernacle source Rava gives for the prohibited labor of Tying (Kosher)?
A. The Levites tied warp threads on the weaving looms
B. Packages of incense were tied before being offered
C. The Tabernacle tent-curtains were tied to the ground-pegs
D. The High Priest's breastplate was tied with special laces
Correct: C — Rava identifies the source of Kosher as the tying of the Tabernacle tents to their stakes in the ground — a skilled, intentional, and lasting fastening.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The Gemara objects that two stitches unravel immediately and a temporary labor is not liable. How is this resolved?
A. The Sages added a rabbinic prohibition to protect the Torah law
B. The Tabernacle curtains were always sewn with exactly two stitches
C. The two stitches are knotted at both ends, so they do in fact endure
D. Two stitches constitute the minimum for any garment and are therefore always permanent
Correct: C — The Gemara resolves the difficulty by explaining that the stitches are knotted at each end — making them lasting. A prohibited labor requires a durable result to incur Torah liability.
