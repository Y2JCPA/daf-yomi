⚖️ Four Opinions: How to Select (Borer) on Shabbat

Rav Yosef
One may select using a basket or plate, but not a large sieve or basket-sieve — those constitute labor in the usual manner.
Rav Hamnuna
No basket or plate appears in the baraita! Rather: one may select to eat immediately, or set aside for immediate use — but never for later.
Rav Ḥisda
One may select and eat less than a dried fig-bulk, or set aside less than that measure — quantities below the Torah liability threshold.
Rav Ashi
If two food-types are present and one selects both — eating one and setting aside the other — he is liable for the set-aside portion, as that constitutes waste-from-food.
Core Rule Selecting food from waste is permitted; selecting waste from food is prohibited. Manner, tool, quantity, and intended timing all affect liability.

⚒️ Four Labors and Their Tabernacle Roots

Grinding (Tochen)

  • Wheat was pounded in the Tabernacle
  • Rav Pappa: chopping beets finely = Tochen
  • Functional similarity determines liability
  • Lupines: boiled 7× — shells may be the 'food'

Cooking (Bishul)

  • Tanna omitted cooking spices for dye
  • Kneading and baking included instead
  • Rav Aḥa: placing peg in oven = Bishul
  • Intent to strengthen ≠ exemption from cooking

Tying & Untying

  • Rava: Tabernacle tents tied to ground-pegs
  • Must be a skilled, permanent knot
  • Temporary ties may be permitted
  • Untying a permanent knot equally prohibited

Sewing & Tearing

  • Two stitches knotted at ends — they do endure
  • Tearing must be לְשֵׁם תְּפִירָה (for sewing)
  • Rabba & Rav Zeira: Tabernacle curtains torn to re-patch
  • Temporary result = no Torah liability

🌿 Ḥizkiya vs. The Principle: Lupines and Borer

Ḥizkiya
One who selects lupines from their shells after boiling is liable for Borer — even selecting 'food' from 'waste' is prohibited here.
Gemara Challenge
But the principle is that selecting food from waste is always permitted! How can Ḥizkiya rule this prohibited?
Resolution
Lupines are boiled seven times. Left inside, the edible part rots and makes the shell reek. Removing the lupine is therefore like removing the waste-item — the shell has become the 'food'.
Rav Pappa
One who chops beets finely on Shabbat is liable for Tochen (grinding). The fine-chopping action mirrors the grinding of grain — functionally identical labor.
Principle The identity of 'food' versus 'waste' is determined by context and use — not by the item's intrinsic nature.
