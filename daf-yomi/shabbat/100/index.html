⚖️ Central Dispute: Drawing Seawater onto a Boat

Rav Huna
Extend a projection of any size from the side of the boat, then fill a vessel with seawater. We measure the karmelit from the sea floor — since the sea is deeper than ten handbreadths, the boat floats above a private domain.
Rav Ḥisda & Rabba bar Rav Huna
One must create a frame of four by four handbreadths alongside the boat before drawing water. We measure from the surface of the water, which has karmelit status — so a proper private domain must first be established.
Key Stakes The entire dispute turns on one question: where does karmelit measurement begin — the sea floor or the water surface? This determines how much effort is required to legally draw water on Shabbat.

🌊 Drawing Seawater onto a Boat — The Full Dispute

Rav Huna
Extend a projection of any size from the boat's side and fill a vessel. The sea floor is more than ten handbreadths below — the boat is already in a private domain above it.
Rav Ḥisda & Rabba bar Rav Huna
Build a four-by-four handbreadth frame alongside the boat, then draw water into it. The water surface is karmelit — you must carve out a proper private domain first.
Rav Naḥman's Challenge to Rav Huna What if the water level drops below ten handbreadths at low tide? People might draw water without realizing the domain has changed. This practical concern supports requiring the visible 4×4 frame as a consistent marker.

📊 Domain Status: Sea, Swamp & Public Domain Compared

🌊 Sea

  • Status: Karmelit (rabbinic domain)
  • Throwing into sea: Exempt from Torah law
  • No public thoroughfare through it
  • Not enclosed like a private domain
  • Depth dispute: floor vs. water surface measurement

🌿 Swamp

  • Status depends on public passage
  • If public walks through: Public domain
  • Throwing 4 cubits: Liable
  • Mentioned twice in mishna: forced passage = passage
  • Width thresholds matter (4 cubits / 4 handbreadths)

🏙️ Public Domain

  • Full Torah prohibition on carrying 4 cubits
  • Pit of 10 deep × 8 wide = private domain within it
  • Mound of 10 handbreadths over 4 cubits = private domain
  • Wall below 10 handbreadths = public domain surface
  • Wall above 10 handbreadths = exempt domain

🥜 Rava's Unresolved Dilemma

אגוז בכלי וכלי צף על גבי המים — שבת ק"א
תיקו — No Resolution A nut rests inside a vessel. The vessel floats on water. One standing in a different domain wishes to lift the nut on Shabbat. The layered problem:
• Does the vessel's interior constitute a private domain?
• Since the vessel moves with the water, is its domain status fixed?
• Does reaching from another domain create a transfer violation? Three domain-law principles collide simultaneously. Rava raises the dilemma but offers no resolution.
