⚖️ Rabbi Eliezer vs. Rabbi Yehoshua: Retroactive Impurity

Rabbi Eliezer
Four women transmit impurity only from the moment they first see menstrual blood — a virgin, an elderly woman, a convert, and a released captive. Their time is sufficient (dayah sha'atah).
Rabbi Yehoshua
I heard this ruling from my teachers only with regard to a virgin and an elderly woman — not for a convert or a released captive.
Rabbi Eliezer
Like the new moon — we do not call someone who has not seen it to testify, yet we still accept their word. Similarly, prior experience of bleeding is not required for first-sighting status.
Gemara
The Sages did not rule with Rabbi Eliezer during his lifetime because he was a Shammuti — affiliated with Beit Shammai. After his passing, the halakha was ruled in his favor.
Halakha The halakha is in accordance with Rabbi Eliezer. Shmuel enumerates four specific cases where this holds.

🫓 Ḥalla of Uncertain Purity — What Is Its Status?

Untithed dough (ṭevel) — ḥalla separated, but purity of the dough is in doubt
↓
What type of doubt governs this case?
↓
❌ NOT 'Two Paths'
Equal, unresolvable doubt → would imply no impurity at all
✓ Like 'Leaning' (Heset)
Unequal doubt (Zavim 3:2) → uncertain impurity still applies
↓
Status is suspended — neither eaten (may be impure) nor burned (may be pure)

🔬 Abaye's Principle — Uncertain Impurity of a Zav

Abaye
Any item that when definitely impure renders non-sacred food impure — the Sages decreed that its uncertain impurity also renders non-sacred food impure.
Gemara — Objection
What about the 24-hour retroactive impurity of a menstruating woman? A definite niddah renders non-sacred food impure — yet uncertain retroactive impurity does not apply to untithed dough!
Gemara — Answer 1
Non-sacred food prepared at teruma standards is actually a lower bar than untithed dough. The retroactive impurity transmits to teruma-standard food, not to ordinary untithed dough.
Gemara — Answer 2
Alternatively: the 24-hour retroactive period itself is only rabbinic — so we cannot extend it further to untithed dough by yet another rabbinic decree.
Source Rav Shmuel bar Rav Yitzḥak received from Rav Naḥman: retroactive impurity of niddah transmits to non-sacred food prepared at teruma-purity standards.

👩 The Four Women with Dayah Sha'atah

בְּתוּלָה — Virgin

  • Never saw menstrual blood in all her days
  • Includes a married woman who bled only at consummation
  • First menstrual sighting: impurity from that moment only

זְקֵנָה — Elderly Woman

  • Three full 30-day cycles passed without bleeding
  • Must be at a stage of life near old age
  • After the gap: first new sighting has no retroactive effect

גִּיּוֹרֶת — Convert

  • Converted at an age when she could have menstruated
  • Pre-conversion cycles not halakhically tracked
  • First post-conversion sighting: dayah sha'atah

שְׁבוּיָה — Released Captive

  • Released from captivity
  • Menstrual records during captivity unreliable
  • Treated as if first sighting at time of release

🕰️ Why Did They Not Rule with Rabbi Eliezer During His Lifetime?

Rabbi Eliezer was a Shammuti — a follower of Beit Shammai's rulings in practice
↓
The halakha generally follows Beit Hillel over Beit Shammai in all disputes
↓
Problem: If we rule with Rabbi Eliezer during his lifetime, we cannot protest those who follow his stricter Shammai-based rulings — out of honor for him
↓
After his passing, there is no longer a concern of dishonoring him — so we can and do rule with him
↓
Shmuel explicitly rules: The halakha follows Rabbi Eliezer in these four cases
