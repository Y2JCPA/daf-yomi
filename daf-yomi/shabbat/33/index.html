🛏️ Impurity of Bedding Above a Zav

Is bedding above a zav impure?
↓
Yes — derived from Lev. 15:10: "he who bears these things" becomes impure
↓
Severe Impurity?
❌ Verse removes it from severe impurity — does NOT defile people or their garments
Lesser Impurity?
✅ Yes — defiles food and drink only
↓
Objection: let it at least defile a person such that the person defiles their own garments?
↓
No — the verse's limiting language excludes this as well; impurity is restricted to food and drink

⚖️ Man with Niddah — Generalization and Detail (כלל ופרט)?

Baraita
"Her impurity shall be upon him" (Lev. 15:24) — he is compared to her: just as she renders bedding impure to the extent of imparting impurity to a person, so too does he render his bedding impure to that extent.
Rav Aḥai
The verse only removed him from severe impurity to lesser impurity — his bedding does NOT impart impurity to people or their garments. The comparison cannot extend that far.
Abaye
"Her impurity be upon him" is the generalization and "every bed upon which he lies" is the detail — but "he shall be impure seven days" interrupts between them. This makes it a distant כלל ופרט, from which we derive nothing.
Rava
A distant כלל ופרט still works. But this is not a detail at all — "every bed" is expansive (ריבוי) language, not a limiting detail. And "her impurity be upon him" means the impurity weighs upon him stringently — i.e., he is not exempt from the comparison.
Bottom Line A man who has intercourse with a niddah renders his bedding impure — but at lesser severity than the niddah herself: his bedding does not impart impurity to people or garments, only to food and drink.

🩸 Samaritan Women vs. Sadducee Women — Ritual Purity Practices

Samaritan Women

  • Count 7 impurity days from any blood — including green (non-impure) blood
  • If red blood appears during that count, they do NOT restart a new 7-day count
  • Count the very day they cease experiencing ziva emissions toward the 7 clean days
  • These errors cause husbands to be treated as ba'alei niddah (those who have intercourse with menstruating women)
  • Their impurity status: definite — no uncertainty

Sadducee Women

  • Follow Sadducee interpretations of purity law by default
  • If they abandon Sadducee ways → status like a Jewish woman
  • Unspecified case: debated — Mishna implies stricter default applies
  • In practice (per Rabbi Yosei): they show their blood to the Sages
  • Wife of High Priest's contemporary confirmed they feared the perushim

👘 Rav Pappa, Rav Shmuel, and the Samaritan Ḥaver's Garments

The Problem: Two mishnayot appear to contradict — one rules leniently about a Samaritan ḥaver's garments (no teruma burning, no Temple liability), while Teharot 4:5 rules stringently about men who engage in intercourse with niddot.
↓
Rav Pappa's first attempt: The Samaritan is a ḥaver — his scrupulousness reduces the concern. The mishnayot address different cases.
↓
Rav Shmuel rejects: The very definition of a Samaritan man is that he is like one who engages in intercourse with a niddah. You cannot simply exempt him because he is a ḥaver.
↓
Rav Pappa, embarrassed, leaves and consults Rav Shimi bar Ashi, who offers the correct resolution.
↓
Rav Shimi bar Ashi's resolution — ספק ספיקא (compounded doubts):
• Uncertain if he recently had intercourse
• If yes — uncertain if his wife counted from green blood
• If yes — uncertain if red blood appeared during that count
Multiple doubts compound → lenient ruling stands.
↓
Final ruling: Do not burn the teruma; not liable for Temple entry — because the compounded uncertainty prevents applying the stringent ruling of ba'al keri or ba'al niddah.
