Shabbat 105 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
If one writes one letter in the morning and one letter in the afternoon, with awareness of Shabbat in between, what does Rabban Gamliel rule?
A. He is exempt, since the awareness breaks the two acts apart
B. He is liable, since there is no awareness that applies to half a measure
C. He is exempt, but only if he forgot the first letter entirely
D. He must bring two separate sin-offerings, one per letter
Correct: B — Rabban Gamliel holds that yedia (awareness) cannot exempt when the act is only a half-measure — so the two letters combine and he is fully liable.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, what does the word אָנֹכִי at the start of the Ten Commandments stand for as a notarikon?
A. A pleasant statement was written and given
B. Fear of God was written and given to Israel
C. I myself wrote and gave [the Torah]
D. I am the Lord who created heaven and earth
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan taught: אֲנָא נַפְשִׁי כְּתִיבַת יְהָבִית — 'I Myself wrote and gave [the Torah],' emphasizing God's direct, personal transmission.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the Rabbis' ruling on the minimum measure for weaving — both at the beginning of a new fabric and when adding to an existing one?
A. 1 thread for adding; 3 threads for beginning
B. 2 threads in both cases
C. 3 threads for beginning; 1 thread for adding
D. 2 threads for beginning; 1 thread for adding
Correct: B — The Rabbis maintain a uniform standard: 2 threads renders one liable, whether starting a new weave or adding to an existing fabric.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The Gemara asks where we find a case where tearing a garment is necessary in order to sew it. What is the answer?
A. When the seam has fused and must be cut open before resewing
B. When a pocketlike protrusion impedes sewing — one tears and tucks the fabric under the seam
C. When two separate garments must be joined at the edges
D. When the fabric is too thick to pierce with a needle without tearing
Correct: B — The Gemara answers that a pocketlike protrusion can physically block the needle; one must tear the garment and tuck the protrusion under the seam to allow proper sewing.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara ultimately rule that tearing a garment in anger on Shabbat is exempt?
A. Because anger is an involuntary emotion and cannot constitute intentional labor
B. Because tearing is only a secondary category (toladah), never a primary labor
C. Because it is a destructive act performed without constructive intent, which does not meet the standard of prohibited labor
D. Because one is not liable unless the garment is completely destroyed
Correct: C — The mishna establishes that performing a labor destructively — without intent to repair or construct — does not satisfy the halakhic definition of melacha, and therefore carries no liability.
