🎓 Rav Naḥman bar Gurya Questioned in Neharde'a

Students
For what prohibited labor is one who milks an animal liable?
Rav Naḥman bar Gurya
He is liable for milking.
Students
For what labor is one who curdles milk liable?
Rav Naḥman bar Gurya
He is liable for curdling.
Students
For what labor is one who makes cheese liable?
Rav Naḥman bar Gurya
He is liable for cheesemaking.
The Sages
He has told us nothing! He simply repeated the name of each act rather than identifying which Av melacha (primary labor) is violated!
What Was Expected The proper answer: milking is a toladah of Threshing (דש); curdling and cheesemaking relate to selecting/separating or cooking. Rav Naḥman bar Gurya provided no analytical content.

💄 Women's Grooming — Which Shabbat Prohibition?

Applying Eye Shadow (פוך)

  • Prohibited labor: Dyeing (צובע)
  • Pigment colors the skin around the eye
  • Equivalent to dyeing fabric or material
  • Liable for a sin-offering if done unwittingly

Braiding Hair (כוחלת)

  • Prohibited labor: Building (בונה)
  • Gemara challenges: Is this the typical manner of building?
  • Answer: Yes — for women, braiding is typical building
  • K'darko (typical manner) determines liability

Applying Blush (פוקסת)

  • Prohibited labor: Building (בונה)
  • Smoothing/painting the face = constructing appearance
  • Braiding and blush both grouped under Boneh
  • Mishna lists both as actionable Shabbat violations

🚿 Ameimar Permits Sprinkling in Meḥoza

אמימר שרא למישדי מיא במחוזא — Ameimar permitted sprinkling water in Meḥoza (95a)
Local Custom Overrides Rabbinic Decree The Rabbis generally prohibited sprinkling water on floors on Shabbat — lest one come to smooth out holes in an earthen floor, violating Smoothing (Memacheik) or Building (Boneh). Ameimar's reasoning: In Meḥoza, all streets and floors are paved with stone. There are no earthen holes to smooth. Therefore the rabbinic concern does not apply, and sprinkling is permitted. A Sage adds: A wise woman can sprinkle even in an ordinary home by washing vessels in different areas — spreading water naturally across the floor. Further: Now that we rule like Rabbi Shimon — that a מלאכה שאינה צריכה לגופה (labor not needed for its defined purpose) is exempt — even accidental smoothing during sprinkling may not trigger liability.
