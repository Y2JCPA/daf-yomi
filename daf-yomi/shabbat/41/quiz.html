QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why are TWO verses required for caesarean-born animals rather than one?
A. One verse covers non-sacred caesarean animals that cannot be consecrated; the other covers offspring of consecrated animals born by caesarean who lack sanctity
B. One verse applies to firstborn animals; the other applies to animals that copulated with humans
C. One verse covers Temple service offerings; the other covers tithing obligations
D. One verse is for birds; the other is for domesticated mammals
Correct: A — The Gemara concludes: one verse teaches a non-sacred caesarean-born animal cannot be consecrated as an offering; the second teaches that an offspring born by caesarean to an already-consecrated animal does not inherit its mother's sanctity.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the core dispute between the First Tanna and Rabbi Shimon regarding blood that exits through a caesarean incision?
A. Whether the woman must bring a purification sacrifice
B. Whether the location of the uterus (the source) is inherently ritually impure
C. Whether the husband becomes impure through contact with such blood
D. Whether the child born this way requires a separate purification process
Correct: B — The First Tanna holds the source location is NOT inherently impure, so blood exiting through an incision is pure. Rabbi Shimon holds the source IS inherently impure, so blood renders impurity regardless of exit path.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What did Reish Lakish rule in the name of Rabbi Yehuda Nesia about a woman whose uterus dislodged and fell outside her body?
A. She is completely pure since no blood was shed in the normal way
B. She is impure as a full seven-day niddah
C. She is ritually impure, but not as a standard seven-day niddah since that requires blood 'in her flesh'
D. She must wait forty days before immersing, as after childbirth
Correct: C — Reish Lakish rules she is impure based on Ezekiel 16:36 ("your foundation was poured out"), but she cannot be a standard seven-day niddah because Torah niddah impurity requires the blood to be 'in her flesh' — a condition impossible when the uterus has exited the body entirely.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the "place of threshing" (בית הדישה) referred to in the baraita as the threshold for niddah impurity?
A. The cervical opening where the uterus connects to the vaginal canal
B. The area between the labia where blood first becomes externally accessible
C. The inner uterine cavity where menstrual blood accumulates
D. The external skin of the abdomen through which caesarean incisions are made
Correct: B — Rav Yehuda explains that the 'place of threshing' is a euphemism for the area between the labia — the external threshold beyond which blood is considered to have 'exited' the body and renders the woman impure as a niddah.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara reconcile Rava's statement that a woman who engages in intercourse is impure for seven days with Rabbi Shimon's leniency?
A. Rava's rule applies only to kohanot; Rabbi Shimon's leniency applies to Israelite women
B. Rava refers to nighttime intercourse; Rabbi Shimon addresses daytime intercourse
C. Rava's rule applies to ordinary post-intercourse cases; Rabbi Shimon addresses a woman immersed by others while in bed who then remained lying down and did not discharge semen
D. They disagree only about a convert; both agree about a born Jewish woman
Correct: C — The Gemara resolves the contradiction: Rava addresses the typical case where a woman who had intercourse will discharge semen and remains impure seven days. Rabbi Shimon's leniency applies to the unusual case where others immersed her while she lay in bed and she remained there — in that position she may not discharge semen, making the immersion fully effective and impurity limited to until evening.
