🧬 When Does a Discharged Fetus Confer Impurity?

Woman discharges a fetus or fetal matter
↓
Human form present
Discernible face, shaped limbs, or developed tissue → mother impure
Disputed / unusual form
Mashed face, two backs, lilith shape, snake form → debated by Amoraim
No human form
Fluid, blood, formless sac, colors only → mother pure
↓
Key caveat: ruling 'impure' as a stringency can itself be a leniency — always check the practical consequence (Rabbi Ḥiyya)

😶 Mashed Face Fetus — Mother Impure or Pure?

Rabbi Yoḥanan
A woman who discharges a fetus whose face is mashed but not flat — mother is impure. Residual features suffice for it to count as a birth.
Reish Lakish
Mother is pure. Without clearly discernible facial features the fetus does not have the status of a human form.
Rav Pappi
Clarification: when face is merely mashed, everyone agrees mother is impure. The dispute is only when the face is completely flat — and the attributed opinions are actually reversed from how they were first stated.
Rabbi Ḥiyya
His sons ruled impure out of doubt. He corrected them: ruling 'impure' here permits her to her husband after immersion — that is actually a leniency, not a stringency. Rule pure!
Halakhic Trap In impurity law, 'stringent' and 'lenient' can invert — deeming someone impure allows eventual purification and return to marital life, while ruling pure denies that path entirely.

👤 Fetus with Two Backs & Two Spines — Rav vs Shmuel

Rav — Mother Is Pure

  • No such viable entity actually exists in the world
  • The Torah's prohibition (Lev. 11:4) was taught to Moses only theoretically
  • Woman who discharges such a form is pure
  • Cannot survive → not an offspring → no birth-impurity

Shmuel — Mother Is Impure

  • Such an entity can and does exist in the world
  • The Torah prohibition refers to a real living creature
  • Woman who discharges such a form is impure
  • Proof: a priest with two backs/spines is disqualified from Temple service as blemished (Rav Shimi bar Ḥiyya's objection)

🧩 Rav Pappa: Fetus Missing Body Parts

Fetus is lacking part of its body
↓
Missing from below upward (lower body)
Three opinions — must be missing up to:
Knee (R. Zakkai)
Orifices (R. Yannai)
Navel (R. Yoḥanan)
Missing from above downward (skull)
Any amount of skull missing → fetus does not confer impurity (Rav Pappa)
↓
Palm-tree form: lower body formless, upper body formed → mother impure, because the upper body is the essential part (Rav Giddel / Rabbi Yoḥanan)

🌙 Strange Fetal Forms: Lilith & Snake

צורבא מרבנן דאמר מילתא — לימא טעמיה, כי היכי דכי מדכרי ליה מידכר
Unusual Fetal Shapes — Impurity Rulings Lilith fetus (Rav Yehuda citing Shmuel): A fetus shaped like a female demon — winged body, human face — mother is impure after childbirth. The human face determines status.

Snake fetus: Ḥanina (son of R. Yehoshua's brother) ruled the mother impure. Rabbi Yehoshua initially agreed, then reversed himself when reminded of his own reasoning: a snake has no recognizable human features → mother is pure.

Abaye's lesson from this incident: A Torah scholar stating a halakha should give his reason alongside the ruling — so that when colleagues remind him of the reasoning, he can correct himself if he erred.
