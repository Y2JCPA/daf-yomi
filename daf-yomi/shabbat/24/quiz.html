Shabbat 24 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, what is the ruling if a fetus is missing any amount of its skull?
A. Mother is impure only if more than half the skull is missing
B. Mother is pure regardless of how little of the skull is missing
C. It depends on whether the lower body is intact
D. The case is unresolved and we rule stringently
Correct: B — Rav Pappa rules that missing parts 'from above downward' (skull) — even any small amount — renders the fetus non-viable for impurity purposes. Only the lower-body dispute requires a threshold.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Ḥiyya tell his sons that their stringent ruling (impure) was actually a leniency?
A. Ruling impure means she need not bring a sin-offering
B. Ruling impure allows her to immerse in a mikveh and return to her husband
C. Ruling impure shortens the days of her required separation
D. Ruling impure exempts her from the laws of niddah entirely
Correct: B — If deemed impure after a birth, the woman immerses in a mikveh and may resume marital relations. Ruling 'pure' denies her that path. So the seemingly stringent ruling ('impure') is actually the functionally lenient one here.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Rav and Shmuel regarding a fetus with two backs and two spines?
A. Whether it is kosher if found inside a slaughtered animal
B. Whether such an entity can actually exist and live in the world
C. Whether the mother must count seven clean days
D. Whether the fetus requires burial in a Jewish cemetery
Correct: B — Rav holds no such viable entity exists in the world (so the mother is pure); Shmuel holds it can exist (mother is impure). Their entire dispute on the biblical prohibition flows from this factual disagreement.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rava, why is a woman pure when she discharges a gestational sac full of different colors?
A. Multicolored fluid indicates the sac came from an animal, not a human
B. Only red blood inside a sac can confer impurity
C. A liquefied embryo would have reduced the sac's mass; since it is full, no embryo was ever present
D. Different colors show the pregnancy was less than 40 days old
Correct: C — Rava reasons that if an embryo had dissolved inside the sac, some of its mass would have been absorbed and the sac would not be completely full. A full sac therefore proves no embryo formed.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What lesson does Abaye draw from the incident of Rabbi Yehoshua reversing his ruling about the snake fetus?
A. Snake-shaped fetuses must always be ruled pure
B. Unusual fetal cases should be referred to a Sanhedrin of 23
C. A Torah scholar stating a halakha should give his reasoning, so colleagues can remind him and he can self-correct
D. When two great sages disagree about a fetus, we rule leniently
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehoshua first ruled impure, then reversed himself when reminded of the reason behind his own principle (a snake lacks human features). Abaye extracts the meta-lesson: always state the reason alongside the ruling.
