🩸 Does Blood of Labor Pains Negate the Ziva Count?

Rava
Does every substance that imparts impurity negate the count of seven clean days? Or only a substance that can actually render one a zava?
Abaye
Proof from oness (involuntary emission): it does NOT render one a zav, yet it DOES negate the count — showing any impurity-causing substance negates it!
Rava
Rejected: oness CAN render one a zav — the mishna in Zavim 2:2 counts it toward the three required sightings. So oness is no proof.
Abaye
But per Rabbi Eliezer who examines even after the 4th discharge — oness would not render one a zav, yet it still negates the count. Proof stands per Rabbi Eliezer!
Gemara
Conclusion: Per Rabbi Eliezer — any impurity negates. Per the Rabbis — the question remains from baraitot. Abaye's final tradition: labor blood does NOT negate the count.
פסיקת הלכה We have a tradition: blood emitted due to labor pains does not negate the seven clean days of a zava, in accordance with the Rabbis.

👶 Does Birth Count Toward the 7 Clean Days? Rava vs. Abaye

Rava: Birth COUNTS

  • Source: 'She shall count for herself seven days' (Lev. 15:28) — consecutive, uninterrupted days required
  • If birth did NOT count, the days would not be consecutive — violating the Torah's requirement
  • Baraita: 'from her ziva' — counting starts from ziva discharge, yet birth is subsumed within it
  • Rabbi Marinus: birth during ziva count does not negate her count (supports Rava's framework)

Abaye: Birth Does NOT Count

  • Baraita compares birth-impurity to menstrual impurity with respect to ziva
  • Just as days of menstrual impurity don't count toward clean days, days of birth-impurity do not either
  • Another baraita: she must observe 'seven days clean from the impurity of birth' — birth days are excluded
  • This baraita is consistent with the Rabbis' view (per Rav Sheshet)

👑 The King's Soldiers — Reading Blood Cessation

Scenario: Woman has 3 days of labor pains with blood emissions within the 11 days
Then she rests from BOTH labor pains and blood
↓
Is she a zava?
↓
Rav Ḥisda: She IS a zava
Blood ceased before labor pains ended — like soldiers who arrived before the king. Blood was therefore independent of labor; it is true ziva blood.
Rabbi Ḥanina: She is NOT a zava
Blood accompanied labor pains — soldiers escort the king. When pains stopped, blood stopped too, proving the blood was caused by labor, not by ziva.
↓
Mishna challenge to Ḥanina: If she rested from labor but blood continued — she IS a zava. The mishna implies cessation of both simultaneously is a distinct case requiring its own ruling.

📅 How Far Before Birth Do Labor Pains Exempt from Ziva?

כמה לפני לידה מיוחסים צירים? רבי מאיר אומר: אפילו ארבעים וחמישים יום — תלמוד בבלי, נדה לז
Rabbi Meir's Ruling Rabbi Meir says: Even forty or fifty days before birth, labor pains can exempt the accompanying blood from being counted as ziva blood. The Gemara asks: if even fifty days suffice, why does the mishna need to state any limit? The answer: the mishna teaches that even such distant pains are attributed to labor. Rabbi Levi adds: The birth of a child renders the mother ritually pure from ziva only if she experienced bleeding during the eleven days between menstrual periods — otherwise the birth has no purifying effect.
