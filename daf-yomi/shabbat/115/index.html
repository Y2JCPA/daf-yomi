🔥 May Aramaic Translations Be Rescued From Fire?

Rav Huna
Writings in Aramaic or any language other than Hebrew may NOT be rescued from the fire on Shabbat.
Rav Ḥisda
Such writings MAY be rescued — the mishna implies we rescue all sacred texts, and they require burial, proving their sanctity.
Baraita
If written in Aramaic translation or any other language — they are rescued from the fire on Shabbat. This is a conclusive refutation of Rav Huna.
Resolution The baraita explicitly permits rescuing writings in any language — Rav Ḥisda's position is upheld, and Rav Huna's view is conclusively refuted.

📖 Rabban Gamliel and the Aramaic Job Translation

Rabbi Yosei
My father Ḥalafta visited Rabban Gamliel ben Rabbi in Tiberias and found him reading an Aramaic translation of Job at the table of Yoḥanan HaNazuf!
Rabban Gamliel
He commanded a builder: Bury it under the stones of the wall — just as an earlier Rabban Gamliel buried the Aramaic Job when a student presented it.
Tanna Kamma
Books in other languages may be read — and one may rescue them from fire.
Rabbi Yosei
One may not even read such books — the dispute may hinge on reading, not merely on rescuing from fire.
Unresolved Question Do Tanna Kamma and Rabbi Yosei disagree about reading Aramaic books, or only about rescuing them from fire on Shabbat?

📿 Sacred Writings: Rescued vs. Left to Burn

✅ May Be Rescued from Fire

  • Torah scrolls (publicly read)
  • Prophets scrolls (publicly read)
  • Writings / Ketuvim (not publicly read)
  • Texts in Aramaic or any other language
  • Scrolls in improper ink if written in Assyrian script on parchment (dilemma leans this way)
  • Partial scroll with ≥ 85 properly ordered letters

❌ Left to Burn — Do Not Rescue

  • Written blessings (ברכות) — even containing God's Name
  • Amulets (קמיעות) — even with Torah content
  • Texts mentioning Torah matters but not classified as sacred writings
  • Partial Torah scroll with fewer than 85 proper letters
  • Scrolls lacking both valid script AND valid surface material

📏 The 85-Letter Rule and the Portion of the Ark

A Torah scroll is damaged — can a partial scroll be rescued from fire on Shabbat?
↓
The Sages established a minimum: 85 letters written properly and in order are required
↓
Why 85? The Portion of the Ark — וַיְהִי בִּנְסֹעַ הָאָרֹן (Numbers 10:35–36) — contains exactly 85 letters and constitutes its own independent book
↓
Rav Huna: Letters must be juxtaposed (consecutive).
Rav Ḥisda: Even letters scattered throughout the scroll may be counted
↓
A verse originally in Aramaic written in Hebrew, or Hebrew written in Aramaic, does not protect surrounding text — each section must qualify independently
↓
The Ark Portion is so significant that the Torah is considered to have seven books — it stands as its own book between Numbers' fourth and fifth sections
