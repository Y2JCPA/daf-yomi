Shabbat 115 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the mishna, which sacred writings may be rescued from fire on Shabbat?
A. Only Torah scrolls that are read publicly
B. All sacred writings, whether read publicly or not
C. Only writings in Hebrew script
D. Only complete, undamaged scrolls
Correct: B — The mishna states that ALL sacred writings may be rescued — both those read publicly (Torah, Prophets) and those not read publicly (Writings/Ketuvim). The language and completeness of the scroll are separate questions addressed in the Gemara.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was Rav Huna's position on rescuing writings in Aramaic translation, and how was it resolved?
A. Rav Huna permitted rescue; he was refuted by a baraita forbidding it
B. Rav Huna forbade rescue; he was conclusively refuted by a baraita permitting it
C. Rav Huna permitted rescue; Rav Ḥisda forbade it; neither was refuted
D. Rav Huna's view was upheld by the incident with Rabban Gamliel
Correct: B — Rav Huna held Aramaic translations may NOT be rescued. Rav Ḥisda permitted it. A baraita explicitly states they ARE rescued — constituting a conclusive refutation (תיובתא) of Rav Huna.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why may written blessings (berakhot) and amulets not be rescued from fire on Shabbat?
A. Because they were written without proper scribal intent
B. Because their ink is always halakhically invalid
C. Because they are not classified as sacred writings despite containing God's Name and Torah content
D. Because they are too small to qualify under the 85-letter minimum
Correct: C — The Gemara explicitly teaches that blessings and amulets — even containing God's Name and Torah matters — are NOT classified as sacred writings (כתבי הקודש). They may not be rescued and must burn, lest one violate Shabbat to save them.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the connection between the number 85 and the 'Portion of the Ark'?
A. 85 is the number of verses in the book of Numbers
B. The Ark portion (וַיְהִי בִּנְסֹעַ הָאָרֹן) contains exactly 85 letters and is the minimum sacred unit
C. Moshe recited 85 prayers when the Ark traveled
D. The 85 letters correspond to the 85 names of God in the Torah
Correct: B — The two verses of the Ark portion (Numbers 10:35–36) contain exactly 85 letters. The Sages used this as the minimum measure of a rescuable sacred text — and this portion is considered so significant it constitutes an independent book of the Torah.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Regarding the 85 letters needed to rescue a partial Torah scroll, what did Rav Huna and Rav Ḥisda debate?
A. Whether the letters must be from the same parasha or can span multiple sections
B. Whether the letters must be consecutive/juxtaposed, or may be scattered throughout the scroll
C. Whether the count includes vowel markings (nikud) or only consonants
D. Whether Aramaic letters count toward the 85-letter minimum
Correct: B — Rav Huna required that the 85 letters be juxtaposed (consecutive), while Rav Ḥisda held that even letters scattered throughout the scroll count. This determines whether a heavily damaged scroll with isolated letters scattered across its length can still be rescued.
