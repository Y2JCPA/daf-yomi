Shabbat 10 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Kahana citing Rav, what is the halakha for a young girl's FIRST time seeing menstrual blood?
A. She transmits impurity retroactively for 24 hours
B. Her time is sufficient — she does not transmit impurity retroactively
C. She must complete three cycles before any ruling applies
D. She immediately attains full adult-woman status
Correct: B — Rav Kahana citing Rav rules that the FIRST time a young girl sees blood, 'her time is sufficient' — impurity is transmitted only from the moment of discovery, not retroactively. This leniency reflects her inexperience with menstruation.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Ḥizkiyya rule regarding a blood stain found between a young girl's 2nd and 3rd sightings?
A. The stain is pure — she is still like a menstrual virgin until three sightings
B. The stain renders her impure — she has begun establishing a menstrual pattern
C. The stain is ignored since she is still a minor
D. She must consult a Sage before a ruling can be issued
Correct: B — Ḥizkiyya rules the stain makes her impure between the 2nd and 3rd sightings — she has moved beyond the earliest stage. Rabbi Yoḥanan disagrees, holding the stain is still pure until she completes three full sightings.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rav Ḥilkiya bar Tovi rule about a young girl who continuously discharges blood for all seven days of a typical menstrual period?
A. It counts as seven separate sightings — one per day
B. It counts as three sightings — corresponding to the major days of flow
C. It counts as only ONE sighting
D. It does not count at all since her biological time has not arrived
Correct: C — Rav Ḥilkiya bar Tovi rules that even seven continuous days of discharge counts as only ONE sighting for a young girl whose time has not yet arrived — the entire episode is treated as a single prolonged event, not multiple sightings.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How can a pregnant woman's cycle-free periods combine with those of a nursing woman to reach the required three cycles?
A. They cannot combine — each state must independently yield three clean cycles
B. Two cycles from pregnancy plus one from nursing (or vice versa) can be combined
C. Only nursing days count; pregnancy days are excluded from the tally
D. Combination requires a rabbinic court dispensation in each case
Correct: B — The baraita teaches that two cycle-free periods during pregnancy plus one during nursing (or one plus two) combine to form the required three cycles. The Torah does not require all three cycles to occur in the same biological state.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Yehuda prohibit examining young girls by hand to check for menstrual blood?
A. It violates their dignity and is explicitly forbidden by Torah law
B. Hand examination is less medically accurate than visual inspection
C. The scratching may produce blood, falsely creating a presumption of impurity
D. Young girls are presumed completely pure and must never be examined
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehuda warned that examining by hand may scratch the area and produce blood, which would wrongly create a legal presumption of impurity. He prescribed smearing with oil and wiping gently — preserving both the girl's physical integrity and her halakhic status.
