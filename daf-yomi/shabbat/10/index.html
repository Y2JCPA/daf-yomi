🩸 Young Girl's Status: When Does Retroactivity Apply?

Rav Giddel (Rav)
The FIRST time a young girl sees menstrual blood, her time is sufficient — she does NOT transmit impurity retroactively.
Rav Kahana (Rav) — conclusion
After the SECOND sighting, she transmits impurity retroactively for a 24-hour period or from the last examination — like any adult woman.
Baraita
She attains full adult-woman status upon the THIRD sighting — per Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, who rules that presumption of regularity follows three sightings.
Gemara — challenge
But if she then passes THREE cycles without blood and sees again, she RETURNS to young-girl status — her time is sufficient once more! Status is dynamic, not locked.
Key Principle A young girl's menstrual status is not permanent: three consecutive clean cycles can reset her protection back to 'time is sufficient,' regardless of prior sightings.

🔍 Blood Stain Between Sightings — Pure or Impure?

Ḥizkiyya
Stain found between 1st and 2nd sightings: pure. But between 2nd and 3rd: she is impure — she has begun establishing a menstrual pattern.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
(R. Shimon ben Yehotzadak)
Even between the 2nd and 3rd sightings, the stain is pure — until she has seen blood three times, she retains the protection of a menstrual virgin.
Rabbi Ilai — objecting to Ḥizkiyya
What distinguishes this girl from a recently married menstrual virgin, whose stain is pure as long as her blood is pure? The same leniency should apply here!
Gemara — on attribution
It matters WHO said this: if Rabbi Yoḥanan cited Rabbi Shimon ben Yehotzadak (his teacher), it becomes one named ruling against two dissenters — stronger legal weight. Ravin confirmed this attribution upon returning from Eretz Yisrael.
Naḥotei Yamma Tradition When Ravin came from Eretz Yisrael with the seafarers, he reported the ruling as Rabbi Shimon ben Yehotzadak's — meaning Rabbi Yoḥanan was echoing his own teacher's view, not just offering a personal opinion.

💧 Continuous Discharge — How Many Sightings Does It Count?

Rav Ḥilkiya bar Tovi

  • A young girl who continuously discharges blood for all 7 days of a menstrual period…
  • …counts as only ONE sighting
  • Even daily blood over a week is a single prolonged episode
  • Reason: her time to menstruate has not arrived — it is one undivided event
  • She therefore still needs two more sightings to reach adult status

Rav Shimi bar Ḥiyya

  • A woman who constantly drips blood (zav-like flow) is NOT considered to have seen a full sighting
  • The drip is categorically distinct from a true menstrual flow
  • Gemara inference: her streaming blood would count — only the dripping doesn't
  • The distinction tracks the nature of the discharge, not merely its presence

👶 Examining Young Girls — Rabbi Yehuda's Concern

בנות ישראל — חזקתן טהורות עד שיגיעו לפרקן
Halakhic Caution — בנות ישראל The Sages taught: daughters of Israel who have not reached physical maturity carry a presumptive status of purity — adult women need not examine them.

Rabbi Yehuda adds: do not examine by hand — scratching the area may produce blood and falsely establish a presumption of impurity.

His method: smear the area with oil inside and wipe gently — this preserves both the girl's physical integrity and her legal status.
