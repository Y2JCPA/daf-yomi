🍷 How Much Wine to Dilute a Cup? Abaye vs. Rava

Abaye
The Sages determined the color red by diluting Sharon wine 2 parts water to 1 part wine. This supports our quarter-log measure — that is the standard ratio for a cup.
Rava
Sharon wine is uniquely weak! It needs only 2 parts water because its own strength is low. Regular wine requires 3 parts water to 1 part wine. Your proof from Sharon wine does not establish the general standard.
Abaye
Perhaps Rabbi Natan's olive-bulk for congealed wine corresponds to a quarter-log when liquid — so the measures align regardless of the dilution debate.
Rava
Blood is more viscous than wine. An olive-bulk of congealed blood may require less than a quarter-log of liquid blood. You cannot derive wine's liquid measure from blood's congealed measure.
Upshot Standard drinking cup = 3 parts water + 1 part wine. Liability for undiluted wine = the amount needed to produce such a cup. Sharon wine is a special case, not the norm.

🔤 The Three Alef / Ayin Spelling Debates

Problem: Mishnaic words sound similar with alef (א) or ayin (ע) — which is correct?
↓
גרינין — Seeds
Rava bar Ulla: Written with Ayin — from root עור (skin/husk), referring to seed husks removed before measuring
עוממות — Dim coals
Rav Yitzḥak bar Avdimi: Written with Ayin — from אממוהו in Ezekiel 31:8: 'the cedars could not hide it'
מעמצין — Close eyes of dead
Rabbi Ḥiyya b. Abba (R. Yoḥanan): Written with Ayin — from Isaiah 6:10: ועיניו עצם, 'he shut his eyes'
↓
All three resolved: each is spelled with Ayin (ע), anchored to a Biblical verse

🌿 Nothing Created for Naught — Rav Yehuda in the Name of Rav

חמשה אימות — אימת הקטן על הגדול
כל מה שברא הקב״ה בעולמו לא ברא דבר אחד לבטלה Every creature has a healing purpose:
• Snail (שבלול) — its slime heals a wound from chafing
• Fly — crushed, it heals a hornet sting
• Mosquito (יתוש) — crushed, it heals a serpent's bite
• Serpent — its flesh heals skin eruptions
• Spider — its web heals a scorpion's wound

The Five Dreads — weak creatures that dominate the mighty:
🦟 Mafgia (gnat) over the lion | 🦟 Mosquito over the elephant | 🦎 Kuzaz over the scorpion | 🐦 Swallow over the eagle | 🐟 Stickleback fish over the Leviathan

📖 Moral Meanings Encoded in Everyday Aramaic Words

כופתא (Barrel) — acronym for כוף ותיב: "Turn it over and sit" — a barrel doubles as a seat
↓
לבני (Bricks) — allusion to לבני בני: "for the children of children" — bricks are built to endure generations
↓
חוצא (Thorn fence) — Rav Yehuda: thorns placed outside (חוץ) as a protective barrier
↓
לבושא (Garment) — acronym for לא בושא: "no shame" — clothing removes the shame of nakedness
↓
גלימא (Broad outer garment) — allusion to גולם: wearing it transforms one into an unformed, shapeless mass — hidden and covered within
↓
Rabbi Zeira & Rav Yehuda: Why is the grasshopper's antenna soft? — It lives in the plains; a hard antenna would break, blinding it. God designed every fragile thing with its habitat in mind.
