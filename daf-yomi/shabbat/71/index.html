🔗 The Principle of Drawing (גרירה)

טחינה נמי גוררת — דף ע"א ע"א
Core Rule — Amud A When one performs two identical forbidden labors within a single lapse of awareness, the sin-offering brought for one draws with it atonement for the other — because they share one designation (שם אחד) and one lapse. Abaye extends this principle: "Grinding also draws the latter grinding with it" — any repeated labor of the same type within a single lapse is covered by one offering, since the designation of grinding is one (שם טחינה אחת היא).

⚖️ Rabbi Yoḥanan vs. Reish Lakish

Case
One ate two olive-bulks of forbidden fat in one lapse of awareness. He then became aware of the first eating separately, and then became aware of the second eating separately.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
He must bring two separate sin-offerings. Each moment of awareness creates a distinct obligation. The verse says "for his sin… and he shall bring" — implying a separate offering for each.
Reish Lakish
He brings only one sin-offering. Both acts occurred in a single lapse of awareness — intermediate awareness before atonement is attained does not divide them into two separate obligations.
Reish Lakish
The verse "for his sin… and he shall bring" speaks only of a case where awareness of the second sin came after atonement was already attained for the first — only then are they truly split.
Crux Does awareness between acts — before full atonement — sever a single lapse into two obligations?

🔍 Ravina vs. Rav Ashi — Scope of the Dispute

Ravina

  • The dispute applies only when he became aware of the second sin before designating an animal (הפרשה) for the first offering
  • Once an animal is designated, the obligation is 'closed' — further awareness triggers a new, independent duty
  • After designation: both Rabbi Yoḥanan and Reish Lakish agree he must bring two offerings

Rav Ashi

  • The dispute applies in both cases — whether awareness came before or after designation
  • Proof: if they only disputed the pre-designation case, teaching it would be superfluous — a fortiori from post-designation
  • The Tanna must have taught both stages because they are both independently contested
