⚖️ Central Tension: Moment of Uprooting vs. Moment of Emission

Follow Uprooting
Impurity or purity status is determined when the fluid is set in motion internally — even before it exits the body. What the person was at that moment governs.
Follow Emission
Status is determined when the fluid actually exits the body. Until emission, nothing has legally occurred — the person's current status at that moment controls.
Rava's Unresolved Cases A gentile whose semen was uprooted before immersion-conversion; a zava whose urine was uprooted before her clean days ended; a gentile zava who then converted — all hinge on this unresolved dispute.

🍷 Priest Eating Teruma — Abaye vs. Rava

Abaye
The mishna refers to a priest holding his penis with a coarse cloth. This prevents semen from exiting, so the teruma is not rendered impure and may be swallowed.
Rava
Even a soft cloth works. When limbs are quaking, holding does not increase emission — the quaking will expel the semen regardless. Holding cannot make it worse.
Baraita Challenge to Rava Holding is like a finger in the eye: while held, it doesn't flow; once released, it flows more. Rava replies: that concern only applies when limbs are not quaking and semen is dripping slowly — not when full quaking is already underway.

🏹 Shmuel's Two Versions: When Does Semen Render Impure?

Version 1 (Shmuel): Semen that is not felt by one's entire body does not render impure
Source: 'the flow of seed' — must be a significant, body-wide emission
↓
Version 2 (Shmuel): Semen not shot like an arrow does not render impure
Focuses on the force and velocity of the emission itself
↓
Practical Difference
Semen uprooted with sensation but emitted without sensation: impure by v.1 (was felt), but not impure by v.2 (not shot like arrow)
Rava's Related Dilemma
If semen was uprooted with sensation but emerged without — is the man ritually impure? Left unresolved (teku)

📜 Minimum Zav Discharge: Rabbi Yishmael vs. the Rabbis

Rabbi Yishmael

  • Requires discharge large enough to block the tip of the penis
  • Shmuel rules in accordance with this position
  • Source: 'Whether his flesh run with his issue' — implies a visible, flowing quantity
  • Only a moist discharge renders impure (derives from 'run')
  • Mishna's 'any amount' means: any amount above this minimum

The Rabbis

  • Zav becomes impure with any amount of discharge — no minimum
  • They derive the blockage verse differently: it teaches only moist (not dry) discharge renders impure
  • 'His issue' = 1st emission; 'run' = 2nd; full verse = 3rd — three emissions for full zav status
  • Even a partial emission counts toward zav impurity
  • Rabbi Simai: two emissions named impure in one verse, three in another — resolved by R. Simai

👶 New Mishna: Infants Acquire Full Impurity Status

תינוקת בת יום אחד מיטמאה בנידה; תינוק בן יום אחד מיטמא בזיבה
Mishna — Niddah 43b Baby girl, even one day old: becomes impure with niddah impurity upon any blood emission.

Baby girl, ten days old: a three-day consecutive emission after her niddah period renders her a zava gedolah.

Baby boy, even one day old:
• Becomes impure with ziva impurity
• Becomes impure with tzaraat (leprous marks)
• Becomes impure with corpse impurity
• Creates a yibbum (levirate) bond for his widow

The Sages apply all these laws from the very first day of life — no age threshold required.
