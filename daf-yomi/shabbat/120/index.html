🔥 Indirect Extinguishing (Grama) on Shabbat

Rabbis
Indirect extinguishing is permitted on Shabbat. One may spread a moist goat's hide over burning items, or place water-filled earthenware vessels near a fire — even though the vessel will burst and water will flow.
Rabbi Yosei
Indirect extinguishing is prohibited. New earthenware vessels filled with water may NOT be placed near a fire — they will inevitably burst and extinguish it, which is forbidden even indirectly.
Abaye & Rava
Even Rabbi Shimon — who normally permits unintended acts — concedes in a case of psik reisha: where the forbidden result is inevitable, it is prohibited. 'Cut off its head — will it not die?'
⚖️ Bottom Line Rabbi Yosei prohibits all grama (indirect extinguishing); the Rabbis permit it. But all agree: when the forbidden result is inevitable (psik reisha), the act is prohibited regardless of intent.

🧺 Rescuing Property from a Fire on Shabbat

How much may one rescue? A full basket of loaves — even 100 meals — is permitted. Rav Huna: no contradiction with the 3-meal limit. The 3-meal rule applies to item-by-item transfer in vessels; rescuing full sealed containers is a different category.
↓
One may rescue a round cake of dried figs and a barrel of wine, even if very large. The emergency of fire permits carrying quantities not normally permitted.
↓
Call for helpers: One may say 'Come and rescue for yourselves!' Bystanders may take items for their own benefit. This is permitted even though they profit from your emergency.
↓
Post-Shabbat settlement: If the helpers are clever, they make a financial calculation with the homeowner after Shabbat — compensating him for his property they took.
↓
Where to carry? First Tanna: only to a courtyard where an eiruv was established. Ben Beteira: even to a courtyard without an eiruv — the fire's distress expands the leniency.

🏘️ Dispute: Where May Rescued Items Be Carried?

First Tanna

  • May carry only to a courtyard with an established eiruv
  • Carrying already permitted there before the fire
  • Cannot carry to a courtyard without eiruv
  • Standard Shabbat carrying rules remain in force
  • More restrictive — fire does not suspend eiruv laws

Ben Beteira

  • May carry even to a courtyard without an eiruv
  • The panic and distress of a fire creates leniency
  • Broader permission granted to prevent total loss
  • Minority view — more lenient in emergencies
  • Accepted in one direction by later authorities

💧 Rav Yehuda on Water and the Goat's Hide

Rav Yehuda (citing Rav)
If a garment catches fire on one side, one may pour water on the other side. If as a result the fire is extinguished — it is extinguished. This is permitted.
Challenge (Tosefta)
Contradiction! A Tosefta states: A garment that catches fire — one may NOT pour water on it to extinguish the flame. This directly contradicts Rav!
Resolution
Rav stated his ruling in accordance with Rabbi Shimon ben Nannas — the opinion in our mishna. Rabbi Shimon permits indirect action that does not constitute direct extinguishing.
Follow-up challenge
But did Rabbi Shimon permit the hide because it singes and does not burn — did he also permit indirectly causing extinguishing?
Gemara's answer
Yes — Rabbi Shimon's principle extends to indirect extinguishing as well, since the person's direct action (pouring water on the other side) is not itself a prohibited act.
📜 Core Distinction The key is whether the person's direct action constitutes a melacha. Pouring water on an unburned side is not extinguishing — but if the flame inevitably reaches it, the psik reisha rule may apply.

🕯️ Is Indirect Extinguishing Permitted on Shabbat?

Fire burning on Shabbat — person wishes to stop it without direct extinguishing
↓
🐐 Moist Goat's Hide
R. Shimon ben Nannas: Spread it over the box — it singes but does not burn. Permitted — the fire does not spread.
🪣 Shaking Candle off Board
School of R. Yannai: Permitted only if the candle is not yet near flammables. If near straw/wood — inevitable extinguishing, may be forbidden.
🚪 Opening Door Opposite Fire
Abaye: Would curse anyone who opened a door causing wind to extinguish a flame — the wind effect is too direct and foreseeable.
↓
⚠️ Psik Reisha Test: Is extinguishing inevitable from this action? If yes — Abaye and Rava rule even Rabbi Shimon concedes it is prohibited.

📋 Shabbat 120 — Summary Map

🔥 Fire breaks out on Shabbat
↓
Rescuing property
Baskets, barrels, garments — large amounts OK. Call helpers! Wear multiple garments. Carry to eiruv courtyard (Ben Beteira: even without eiruv).
Stopping the fire
Only indirect methods permitted. Direct extinguishing (מְכַבֶּה) is a Torah prohibition.
↓
Is the method grama (indirect)?
↓
✅ Rabbis: Permitted
Grama is permitted on Shabbat. Moist hide, earthenware vessels — all allowed.
❌ R. Yosei: Prohibited
Even indirect extinguishing is forbidden. New water-filled vessels may not be placed near fire.
↓
