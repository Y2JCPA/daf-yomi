⚔️ Abaye vs. Rava: Why Is 'For Himself' Permitted?

Abaye
The reason a Jew may benefit when a gentile acts for himself is that no prohibited labor was done on the Jew's behalf. The Jew did not request it and bears no halachic responsibility.
Rava
The reason is appearances (מראית עין) — bystanders may think the gentile acted for the Jew. When the gentile knows the Jew, this concern is heightened and benefit becomes prohibited.
Rabban Gamliel
Apparent proof against Rava: "Since he made the ramp not in our presence, we will disembark on it." Rabban Gamliel's reasoning focuses on presence, not on appearances to onlookers.
Rava
Rabban Gamliel's case is in a public place where any passerby could see. His reasoning still confirms the mar'it ayin concern — absence from the scene eliminates the appearance of having requested the labor.
Practical Outcome Gentile does NOT know the Jew → permitted to benefit in all cases. Gentile DOES know the Jew → prohibited even if he acted entirely for himself.

🕯️ Gentile's Shabbat Labor: For Himself vs. For a Jew

For Himself (לעצמו)

  • Lit a lamp → Jew may use its light ✓
  • Drew water from a well → Jew may use it ✓
  • Collected grass for his animal → Jew may feed his animal after ✓
  • Key condition: gentile does not know the Jew
  • If gentile knows the Jew → prohibited even here

For a Jew (בשביל ישראל)

  • Lit a lamp for the Jew → prohibited ✗
  • Drew water for the Jew → prohibited ✗
  • Collected grass for the Jew's animal → prohibited ✗
  • Always prohibited regardless of any other factor
  • Rabbinic decree to prevent Jews from directing gentile labor

🚪 Vessel Doors on Shabbat: Abaye vs. Rava

Baraita
Door of a chest, box, or closet: one may remove it from its hinges on Shabbat but may not restore it. Door of a chicken coop: one may neither remove nor restore it.
Gemara's Question
Contradiction! If the Tanna holds building is prohibited for vessels, removal should also be prohibited. If there is no such prohibition, restoration should be permitted. The two halves of the baraita seem irreconcilable!
Abaye
Emend the text: read "one may not remove them." Both removal and restoration are prohibited for vessel doors — just as with the chicken coop.
Rava
No emendation needed. Removal is explicitly permitted ab initio. Restoration is prohibited because it resembles building. The Tanna holds: building = prohibited; dismantling = not fully prohibited for vessels.
Chicken Coop Exception A chicken coop is attached to the ground, triggering both the full building prohibition AND the dismantling prohibition — stricter than freestanding vessels in both directions.

👥 Majority Rule: Bathhouse, Banquet & City

General Principle: When a gentile's Shabbat labor could benefit Jews or gentiles, halakha follows the majority of those involved.
↓
City Bathhouse: Majority gentile residents → a Jew may bathe immediately after Shabbat. Majority Jewish residents → must wait long enough that the water could not have been heated on Shabbat for Jews.
↓
Banquet Lamp: Majority gentile participants → Jew may use the lamp's light (lit with gentiles in mind). Majority Jewish participants → prohibited (lamp presumed lit for the Jews).
↓
Shmuel's Story: At Avin Toran's house a gentile lit a lamp; Shmuel turned away. When the gentile brought more wicks for the table of gentile guests, Shmuel concluded it was for them and permitted it.
↓
Exact Half: When Jews and gentiles are equal in number, rule stringently — treat as if majority are Jewish.
