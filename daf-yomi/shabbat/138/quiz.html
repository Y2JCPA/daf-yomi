Shabbat 138 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Kahana, what is the halakha if one strained wine on Shabbat?
A. It is entirely permitted ab initio
B. One is exempt but it is prohibited
C. One is liable to bring a sin-offering
D. One must bring two sin-offerings — one for each domain violation
Correct: C — Rav Kahana explicitly rules that one who strains wine on Shabbat is liable to bring a sin-offering. The subsequent debate between Rabba and Rabbi Zeira is only about which of the 39 prohibited labors (Borer or Meraked) was violated.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rabba and Rabbi Zeira disagree about which melacha straining wine violates. What is Rabba's reasoning?
A. Straining is Sifting (Meraked): the refuse stays on top of the strainer, food falls below
B. Straining is Selecting (Borer): one takes the food (wine) and leaves the refuse (sediment)
C. Straining is Grinding (Tochen): breaking down the substance into finer components
D. Straining is Cooking (Bishul): it transforms the wine's character
Correct: B — Rabba holds it is Borer (Selecting): the manner of selecting is to take the food and leave the refuse. When straining, one takes the wine and leaves the sediment — exactly the pattern of selecting.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rav (via Rav Huna) permits spreading a large wineskin on Shabbat — but only under what condition?
A. Only if the wineskin was previously spread before Shabbat
B. Only if performed by two people together, not one person alone
C. Only if the wineskin does not create a covered space of one handbreadth
D. Only if it is being spread for the purpose of storing wine, not shelter
Correct: B — Rav rules that spreading a large wineskin is permitted only when done by two people together. A single person spreading it alone would constitute a prohibited tent-building act.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Abaye's ruling about spreading a canopy on Shabbat with ten people?
A. Permitted, because ten people together do not constitute a single builder
B. Permitted, as long as the canopy's roof is less than one handbreadth
C. Prohibited, because it is impossible to avoid momentarily stretching it — creating a temporary tent
D. Prohibited only if the canopy was never spread before Shabbat began
Correct: C — Abaye rules that spreading a canopy on Shabbat is prohibited even with ten people. The reason: it is impossible that it will not be stretched for at least a brief period during the process, which constitutes establishing a temporary tent.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Sages at Yavne (citing Amos 8:12), what will be the tangible sign that Torah has been forgotten from Israel?
A. The daily Tamid sacrifice will cease to be offered properly
B. No one will be able to recite the Shema from memory
C. A woman will circulate with teruma bread among synagogues, unable to find anyone who can rule on its purity
D. The Sanhedrin will fail to sanctify the new month for three consecutive years
Correct: C — The Sages said: It is destined that a woman will take a loaf of teruma bread and circulate among synagogues and study halls to find a ruling on whether it is ritually pure or impure — and no scholar will be able to answer, demonstrating the depths of halakhic forgetting.
