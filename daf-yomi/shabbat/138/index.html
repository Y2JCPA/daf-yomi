🍷 Which of the 39 Melachot Does Straining Wine Violate?

Rabba
Straining = Borer (Selecting). What is the manner of one who selects? He takes the food and leaves the refuse. Here too — one takes the wine and leaves the sediment. Same pattern, same melacha!
Rabbi Zeira
Straining = Meraked (Sifting). What is the manner of sifting? The refuse stays on top of the sifter and the food falls below. Here too — the sediment remains on the strainer above. This is sifting, not selecting!
Undisputed Ruling Rav Kahana rules one is liable to bring a sin-offering for straining wine on Shabbat. The debate concerns only which of the 39 prohibited labors is violated — both Rabba and R. Zeira agree it is forbidden.

⛺ Tent Structures on Shabbat: Permitted vs. Prohibited

✅ Permitted

  • Curtain (פרוכת) — no roof, may spread and dismantle freely (Rav / Rabbi Ḥiyya)
  • Bridegroom's canopy (חופת חתנים) — entirely sloped, no flat roof (Shmuel / Rabbi Ḥiyya)
  • Large wineskin — may be spread by two people together (Rav via Rav Huna)
  • Canopy pre-spread before Shabbat, roof < 1 tefach — restoring is not building (Rav Sheshet b. Rav Idi)
  • Felt hat — permitted on Shabbat; wide brim is not a tent concern (Rav Sheshet b. Rav Idi)

❌ Prohibited

  • Carmanian folding bed — unfolding constitutes building a tent (Rav)
  • Doubled cloak spread over a rope — creates a tent-like canopy beneath it (Rami bar Yeḥezkel)
  • Any canopy with roof ≥ 1 handbreadth — real tent threshold crossed
  • Wineskin spread by one person alone — single-person tent construction
  • Canopy spread fresh on Shabbat — even with 10 people: inevitable momentary stretch = tent (Abaye)

📖 The Torah Is Destined to Be Forgotten from Israel

עֲתִידָה תּוֹרָה שֶׁתִּשְׁתַּכַּח מִיִּשְׂרָאֵל — אָמַר רַב מִשּׁוּם רַבִּי חִיָּיא
📖 Aggadah — Rav in the name of Rabbi Ḥiyya "The Torah is destined to be forgotten from Israel."

Proof text (Deut. 28:59): "And the Lord will make your plagues astonishing (נִפְלָאת)..." — just as plagues will be wondrous/unfamiliar, so too Torah knowledge will become unfamiliar and forgotten.

The Sages at Yavne added (citing Amos 8:11-12): Three things will be lost — Halakha ("the word of the Lord"), End of Days, and Prophecy — as people roam to find the word of God and cannot find it.

The sign of the era: A woman will take a loaf of teruma bread and circulate among synagogues and study halls asking whether it is ritually pure or impure — and no one will be able to answer.

🍞 The Teruma Bread Case — Layers of Forgotten Law

Scenario: A carcass of a creeping animal (שֶׁרֶץ) is found in an oven's airspace. Teruma bread is inside — is it ritually impure?
↓
Layer 1 — The Torah verse (Lev. 11:34): "All food upon which water falls shall be impure." Seems to resolve it explicitly!
↓
Layer 2 — The Mishna complicates this: The oven's airspace transmits impurity, but only at second-degree — not first-degree. The Torah verse applies to first-degree impurity, disqualifying the simple proof.
↓
Layer 3 — Rav Adda bar Ahava's proposal: "Let us view the oven as filled with impurity — the bread would then be first-degree impure and certainly disqualified."
↓
Layer 4 — Baraita rejects this: We do NOT apply the principle of 'viewing the oven as filled.' Only items that directly share airspace with the carcass become impure — not the whole oven.
↓
The lesson: An apparently simple question requires four layers of analysis. When scholars disappear, even this will be unanswerable — fulfilling the prophecy of Torah's forgetting.
