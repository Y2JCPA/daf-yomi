Shabbat 14 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara not require us to consider that blood on the husband's examination cloth might be louse blood?
A. Lice do not produce blood when crushed
B. Rabbi Zeira rules that lice are uncommon in that area, so we assume the blood is uterine
C. The cloth was always pre-examined and known to be clean
D. Only blood on the wife's cloth is halakhically relevant
Correct: B — Rabbi Zeira rules we disregard the possibility of louse blood because lice are not commonly found in that area, making uterine blood the presumed source.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In what case do the two versions of Rabbi Zeira's statement yield a practical difference?
A. When the cloth was not pre-examined before use
B. When the blood was found after a long delay
C. When a crushed louse is found alongside the blood on the cloth
D. When the woman was already a confirmed niddah
Correct: C — The two versions of Rabbi Zeira's ruling diverge only when a squashed louse is found next to the blood — one version deems her pure (the louse explains the blood), the other still deems her impure.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Shmuel's ruling regarding a woman who examined herself with a pre-examined (clean) cloth, pressed it to her thigh without looking, and found blood the following day?
A. She is pure, since the cloth left her sight
B. She is in a state of uncertain impurity and brings a provisional guilt offering
C. She is definitely impure as a menstruating woman
D. The ruling depends on whether the stain is larger than a split bean
Correct: C — Shmuel rules she is definitely impure as a niddah, and the study hall issued this as a practical halakhic ruling.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Ḥama bar Bisa choose to hear Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's ruling rather than Rabbi Yosei's, according to the Gemara's explanation?
A. He personally considered Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi a greater Torah scholar than Rabbi Yosei
B. Rabbi Yosei's ruling on that case was not yet recorded
C. As head of the yeshiva, Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's rulings were constantly scrutinized and refined, making them more reliable
D. Rabbi Yishmael had already told him Rabbi Yosei's ruling before the meeting
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that Rabbi Ḥama wisely preferred Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's ruling because as rosh yeshiva his positions were constantly debated and tested, giving them greater practical authority.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rav Ashi resolve the apparent contradiction between the mishna's and the baraita's definitions of 'after time passed'?
A. The mishna applies to examined cloths; the baraita applies to unexamined ones
B. Both refer to the same time period — whether the cloth is already in hand or must be fetched determines the exact duration
C. The baraita is a stricter later ruling that supersedes the mishna
D. Rav Ashi rules the mishna is in error and defers entirely to the baraita
Correct: B — Rav Ashi resolves the contradiction by saying both texts describe one variable period: if the cloth is already in the woman's hand, the time is shorter; if she must reach for it, the time is longer.
