🧵 Examination Cloths: Procedure and Halakhic Consequences

Woman uses two cloths during intercourse: one for the husband, one for herself
↓
Blood found immediately after intercourse → both definitely impure, each liable for a sin offering
↓
Blood found after time passed (short window) → both impure with uncertainty, exempt from sin offering
↓
Blood found much later → woman retroactively impure, man is exempt
↓
Central Gemara question: Was the cloth pre-examined? And how long was it stored? These determine the ruling.

🩸 Blood on Husband's Cloth — Can It Be Louse Blood?

Questioner
The mishna says blood on the husband's cloth renders them definitely impure — but why? Perhaps it is the blood of a crushed louse, not uterine blood!
Rabbi Zeira (v.1)
We are not concerned about louse blood because lice are not commonly found in that area — therefore we assume the blood is uterine.
Rabbi Zeira (v.2)
We are not concerned about louse blood because lice there are uncommon — they exist, but are rare enough to disregard.
Gemara
Practical difference between the two versions: if a squashed louse is found near the blood on the cloth — in v.1 she is pure; in v.2 she is still impure.
Key Distinction The two versions of Rabbi Zeira's ruling diverge only in the edge case of a crushed louse found alongside the blood on the examination cloth.

📦 Examined vs. Unexamined Cloth: Halakhic Ruling

Woman examines herself and stores the cloth without re-checking — blood found the next day
↓
Cloth WAS pre-examined (found clean)
Blood found after pressing to thigh → definitely impure as niddah
(Shmuel; practical ruling in the study hall)
Cloth was NOT pre-examined
Blood found after placing in a box → Rav Yosef cites Rabbi Ḥiyya ruling all his life on this case
↓
Dilemma: Did Rabbi Ḥiyya rule definitely impure (niddah) or merely uncertain impurity due to the stain?
↓
Baraita resolution: Rabbi Ḥiyya told Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi — stain must be larger than a split bean to establish impurity (otherwise, insect source is assumed)

⚖️ Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi vs. Rabbi Yosei: Unexamined Cloth

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi — Impure

  • Unexamined cloth stored in box → blood found next day
  • Woman is definitely impure as a menstruating woman
  • Follows reasoning of Rabbi Meir: ambiguous blood in that context → assume uterine source
  • Still requires stain larger than a split bean to rule out insect blood
  • His rulings constantly tested as head of yeshiva — deemed most reliable

Rabbi Yosei — Pure

  • Same scenario: unexamined cloth, blood found next day
  • Woman is pure — blood may have come from another source
  • Follows his own line of reasoning: do not assume niddah without clear uterine origin
  • Rabbi Yishmael (his son) was offended when Rabbi Ḥama ignored this ruling
  • Rabbi Ḥama chose Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's view as more authoritative in practice

📖 Story: Rabbi Ḥama bar Bisa Tested by Rabbi Yishmael

נידה יד עמוד ב — רבי חמא בר ביסא ורבי ישמעאל בר רבי יוסי
Aggadic Passage Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi praised Rabbi Ḥama bar Bisa to Rabbi Yishmael son of Rabbi Yosei, calling him a great scholar. When Rabbi Ḥama arrived, Rabbi Yishmael asked him a halakhic question about an unexamined cloth found with blood. He offered two options: answer in the name of his father Rabbi Yosei, or in the name of Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi. Rabbi Ḥama chose Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's ruling — prompting Rabbi Yishmael's sharp rebuke: 'Is this the one called great? He ignores the teacher and follows the student!'

The Gemara defends Rabbi Ḥama: he wisely preferred Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's rulings because as head of the yeshiva, his positions were constantly challenged and refined, making them more reliable in practice.
