⛵ Boats, Partitions & Shabbat Domains

מסכת שבת דף קא — ספינות, מחיצות ורשויות
Central Question When may one carry to or from a boat on Shabbat? This daf examines the domain status of boats of various shapes (deep, wide-top, narrow-bottom), the principle of 'lowering the partition,' whether fish passage invalidates a water barrier, and the rules for carrying between tied boats — including how thin a connecting string may be.
3 / 9

🏛️ Rav Naḥman vs. Rav Yosef: Basket, Boat & Partition

Rav Naḥman
Say 'lower the partition' (גוד אסיק מחיצתא)! The Meishan boat's wide upper rim has sufficient height — treat the partitions as descending to the bottom, making it a valid private domain.
Rav Yosef
Objection! Rav Yehuda said in Rav's name — and some say in Rabbi Ḥiyya's name — that a basket suspended in a courtyard less than 3 handbreadths from the ground may NOT be filled with water on Shabbat. This disproves 'lower the partition'!
Rav Naḥman
The basket is different — its gaps are large enough for goats to pass through. A partition that animals can walk through is not a partition at all. The boat's sides are solid.
Gemara
But here with the boat in water — fish pass through the bottom! Yet the Sages were lenient about water. Learn from this: passage of fish (פירצת דגים) does NOT invalidate a partition in water.
⚖️ Conclusion Fish passage does not breach a water partition — a special Rabbinic leniency unique to aquatic domains.

📐 Three Boat Types & Their Domain Status

Deep Standard Boat

  • Interior depth: 10+ handbreadths
  • Rim: at or below water level
  • Carry OUT to sea: ✅ permitted
  • Carry IN from sea: ❌ forbidden
  • Reason: boat = private domain; sea = karmelit
  • Rabbi Yehuda's ruling (101a)

Meishan Narrow-Bottom Boats

  • Shape: wide on top, narrow at bottom
  • Bottom width: under 4 handbreadths
  • Lacks minimum floor for private domain
  • Status: treated as karmelit
  • Rule: carry only within 4 cubits
  • Rav Huna's ruling (101a)

Two Tied Boats

  • Tied together = treated as one domain
  • Direct carrying between them: ✅ permitted
  • Via a third small boat: ❌ forbidden
  • With mats unfurled + eiruv: ✅ permitted
  • With mats furled: ❌ forbidden
  • Rava, Rav Safra, mishna (101b)

🔗 Can You Carry Between Two Boats on Shabbat?

Are the two boats physically tied together?
❌ Not tied
Each boat is a separate domain — carrying between them is forbidden
✅ Tied
Potentially one merged domain — continue evaluation
Are partitioning mats between them unfurled?
Furled / absent
Forbidden to carry — must first make an eiruv
Unfurled
Permitted with eiruv — carry directly from one to the other
Note (Rav Safra): Even when permitted, one may not carry via a small intermediate boat — it retains its own domain.

🧵 Shmuel's Cloak-String Ruling — Step by Step

Shmuel's Statement: Two boats tied together may carry between them — even if tied with a string from the neckline of a cloak (a minimal, thin string).
↓
Gemara's Challenge: What are the circumstances? If the string can hold the boats, it is obvious. If it cannot hold them — what purpose does it serve?
↓
Resolution: The string CAN physically hold the boats. Shmuel's novelty is not about Shabbat domain — it is to limit his own earlier ruling.
↓
Shmuel's Earlier Ruling: If a boat is tied by a rope capable of holding it, and that rope's end lies in the public domain, the boat conveys tumah (ritual impurity) to the public domain.
↓
The Limitation: A cloak-string, though sufficient for Shabbat domains, does NOT create a tumah connection. Shmuel clarifies: only a robust rope extending into the public domain extends impurity.
↓
Biblical Source for Tumah: "Whoever touches in the open field one slain by sword..." (Numbers 19:16) — the open field (public domain) transmits impurity through contact, including via rope.
