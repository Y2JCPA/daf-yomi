Shabbat 34 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Beit Shammai, what is the status of a gentile niddah's blood?
A. Impure by Torah law
B. Impure while moist, by rabbinic decree
C. Completely ritually pure
D. Impure whether wet or dry
Correct: C — Beit Shammai holds that 'Speak to the children of Israel' limits niddah impurity to Jewish women, rendering gentile blood entirely pure. Their saliva and urine are rabbinic-only to serve as a conspicuous marker.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rava rule that the semen of a gentile man is ritually pure even by rabbinic law?
A. The Torah explicitly exempts gentile semen from all impurity
B. If semen were impure, there would be no conspicuous marker distinguishing it from the more serious ziva impurity
C. Gentile semen is biologically different and cannot convey impurity
D. This is a gezera shava from the laws of a female gentile
Correct: B — Rava explains that the Sages rendered semen pure precisely so that ziva would stand out as the distinctly impure emission. The asymmetry itself is the 'conspicuous marker' (היכר) alerting people that a gentile man's ziva carries special rabbinic impurity.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What are the two sides of Rav Pappa's dilemma regarding semen after 3 days?
A. Whether gentile semen is impure by Torah law vs. rabbinic law
B. Whether semen in a gentile woman spoils faster (making it pure) or slower (keeping it impure) than in a Jewish woman after 3 days
C. Whether a gentile man's semen can impart impurity to a Jewish woman at all
D. Whether the 3-day rule applies equally to semen found in a mikveh
Correct: B — Rav Pappa's dilemma: Jewish women's care for mitzvot heats their bodies, spoiling semen within 3 days. Gentile women may be colder (semen viable longer) or hotter due to frequent relations (semen spoils faster). The dilemma is left unresolved.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rava resolve whether a minor (katan) has impurity for his first ziva sighting?
A. It is pure — a minor's semen doesn't impart impurity, so neither does his first ziva
B. It is impure — a baraita states 'whether adult or minor,' equating them in all zav laws including the first sighting
C. The question remains unresolved (teiku)
D. It is impure only from age 9, when physical maturity begins
Correct: B — Rava cites a baraita: 'This is the law of the zav, whether adult or whether minor.' Since the verse fully equates them, just as an adult's first sighting imparts impurity, so does a minor's — regardless of the minor's semen status.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How do Beit Hillel prove that a female leper's blood during her days of purity imparts impurity?
A. By direct analogy to a male leper, whose bodily emissions clearly impart impurity
B. From a gezera shava linking the female leper's blood to niddah blood
C. The phrase 'whether it be a male' is superfluous for males (derivable by inference) and for the female leper's other emissions — so it must include her blood specifically
D. Because the verse in Leviticus 13 explicitly mentions the female leper's blood discharge
Correct: C — Beit Hillel argues: 'whether it be a male' is unneeded for male lepers (derived by a fortiori) and unneeded for the female leper's saliva/urine (derived from male leper). Therefore, the phrase must come to include the female leper's blood, teaching that it imparts impurity.
