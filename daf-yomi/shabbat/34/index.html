🩸 Two Schools on Gentile Bodily Emissions

Beit Shammai
Blood of a gentile niddah or zava is completely ritually pure. The verse 'Speak to the children of Israel' (Lev. 15:19) applies only to Jewish women, not gentiles.
Beit Hillel
Her blood imparts impurity while moist, like any niddah. The Sages needed to distinguish her blood from her saliva and urine, which are rabbinic only — so her blood must be impure.
Gemara (objecting to Shammai)
If gentile blood is pure, why is her saliva and urine still rabbinicaly impure? The marker argument cuts both ways — let saliva be pure too, and deem blood impure even when dry!
Beit Shammai (response)
No — the conspicuous marker works precisely this way: pure blood stands out against impure saliva/urine, making everyone aware that gentile impurity is categorically different from Jewish impurity.
The Core Tension How do we create recognizable legal distinctions between Jewish and gentile ritual impurity — and which fluid serves as the 'conspicuous marker' (היכר) for each position?

🔍 Gemara Challenges Beit Shammai: The Marker Problem

Beit Shammai: Gentile niddah's blood is pure (Torah level)
Verse 'Speak to children of Israel' excludes gentiles
↓
Gemara objects: Then why is her saliva/urine impure by rabbinic decree — if blood is pure, why aren't those pure too?
↓
Gemara's Challenge
Make saliva/urine pure AND blood impure (even dry) — so the blood marks the difference
Beit Shammai's Answer
Keep blood pure + saliva/urine rabbinicaly impure — pure blood is itself the conspicuous marker that gentile impurity is different

🧬 Rava: Asymmetry in Gentile Man's Emissions

Ziva of a Gentile Man

  • Ritually impure (rabbinic)
  • Impure even per Beit Shammai
  • Reason: his semen is pure, so ziva stands out as the distinctly impure fluid
  • The asymmetry creates the conspicuous marker
  • People know: ziva of a gentile man = special rabbinic impurity

Semen of a Gentile Man

  • Ritually pure — entirely
  • Pure even per Beit Hillel
  • Reason: if semen were impure, ziva would have no marker to distinguish it
  • Semen being pure = the marker that ziva carries special impurity
  • Pure even by rabbinic law (unlike gentile woman's semen situation)

❓ Rav Pappa's Unresolved Dilemma A Jew has relations with a gentile woman. After 3 days, does semen remaining in her womb still impart ritual impurity? The sides of the dilemma: • Side A (semen is now pure): Jewish women are careful about mitzvot — their bodies are 'hot,' causing semen to become foul within 3 days. Gentile women are not careful — their bodies are 'cold,' so the semen remains intact and viable beyond 3 days, and continues to impart impurity. • Side B (semen is now pure): Gentile women are more sexually active — therefore their bodies are actually hotter, causing semen to spoil even faster than in a Jewish woman. The Gemara does not resolve this — teiku.

👦 Does a Minor's First Ziva Sighting Impart Impurity?

Source: The verse 'whether it be a male' (Lev. 15:33) includes a katan (minor) in the laws of a zav — this is established
↓
Rav Yosef's dilemma: An adult's first sighting of ziva imparts impurity. Does a minor's first sighting also impart impurity?
↓
Argument for pure: An adult's first sighting is tied to his semen imparting impurity. A minor's semen does not impart impurity — so perhaps his first ziva sighting is also exempt.
↓
Argument for impure: The verse equates adult and minor comprehensively in zav laws — just as the adult's first sighting imparts, so does the minor's.
↓
Rava resolves from a baraita: 'This is the law of the zav, whether adult or whether minor' — the minor's first sighting imparts impurity, fully equated with an adult.
