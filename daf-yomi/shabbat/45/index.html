🌱 Coming of Age: Halakhic Thresholds for Girls and Boys

Girls

  • Betrothal by intercourse valid: age 3+
  • Vows examined: age 11y 1d (twelfth year)
  • Vows fully valid: age 12y 1d + two hairs
  • Contraceptive cloth needed: ages 11–12
  • God granted extra binah (intuition) — matures first

Boys

  • Levirate intercourse valid: age 9+
  • Vows examined: age 12y 1d (thirteenth year)
  • Vows fully valid: age 13y 1d + two hairs
  • Bill of divorce only at majority
  • School attendance yields cleverness — matures later

🩸 Does the Hymen Regrow Before Age 3?

Opinion A
If a girl under age 3 has intercourse, the hymen disappears but regrows — just like an eye that tears when poked but heals again. She retains her status as a betulah.
Opinion B
The hymen does not disappear at all until after age 3. Before age 3, it simply cannot be permanently removed — the girl is too young for any lasting physical change.
Practical Difference
A priest's wife: no blood found after she turned 3. If Opinion A — she may have had relations before 3, hymen regrew, she is still valid. If Opinion B — she must have been violated after 3, disqualifying her from the priesthood.
Rav Ḥisda
The mishna itself says intercourse with a girl under 3 is 'like placing a finger into the eye' — it keeps tearing and healing. This proves Opinion A: the hymen regrows.
Rav Ḥiyya bar Rav Ika Objects Even according to Opinion A (hymen regrows), one can still argue: maybe she had intercourse before 3 AND after 3 — making the dilemma harder to resolve from the priest-wife case alone.

💍 Nine-Year-Old Yavam: Acquisition and Release

A 9-year-old boy's brother dies childless — he is a yavam obligated in levirate marriage
↓
He performs levirate intercourse with the yevama — this is legally valid intercourse
↓
What are his obligations and options?
Before Majority
He acquires her as a wife by the intercourse. He cannot yet give a bill of divorce — not of majority. She may not remarry.
At Majority (Rav's ruling)
He may engage in intercourse to fully acquire her, or give a bill of divorce alone. No ḥalitza required — the levirate bond was already established.
If She Remarries Before His Majority
She must leave both the yavam and the second husband. Children from both unions are mamzerim.

👼 Yusteni the Roman Princess

בְּשַׂר חֲמוֹרִים בְּשָׂרָם — יחזקאל כג:כ
True Incident — Niddah 45a Yusteni, daughter of Asveirus son of Antoninus (a Roman emperor), came before Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi and asked: at what age is a woman fit for intercourse? He said: twelve years and one day.

She then asked: And for pregnancy? He said: twelve years and one day. She replied: I married at six and gave birth at seven! Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi mourned: 'Woe to me that I spoke improperly before you!'

Gemara resolves: Either (1) gentiles mature physically earlier — 'their flesh is the flesh of donkeys' (Ezekiel 23:20), or (2) Yusteni's early birth was a divine miracle.

🧠 Why Girls Develop Faster: The Vayyiven Derash

The Mishna's Premise: Girls reach halakhic majority (for vows) one full year before boys — 12 vs. 13.
↓
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's Source: 'And He built [vayyiven] the rib into a woman' (Genesis 2:22).
↓
The word vayyiven contains binah (בִּינָה) — understanding and intuition. God deliberately endowed women with greater understanding than men.
↓
Rabbi Shimon ben Elazar Disagrees: Males actually develop faster — since a boy frequents his teacher's house from an early age, 'cleverness enters a person through teachers.'
↓
Resolution: Rabbi Shimon uses vayyiven for Reish Lakish's teaching — God 'built thoughtfully' (deliberated greatly) before creating woman. The binah derivation belongs only to Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi.
