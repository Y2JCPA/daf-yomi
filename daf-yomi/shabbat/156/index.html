🌟 Does Astrology Govern the Jewish People?

Rabbi Ḥanina
A constellation makes one wise and a constellation makes one wealthy — yesh mazal l'Yisrael. The constellation of the hour of birth determines one's character and wealth.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Ein mazal l'Yisrael — there is NO constellation binding the Jewish people. As it is written: "Do not be dismayed by the signs of the heavens" (Jer. 10:2). Rav and Shmuel agree.
Ruling Theme Four stories prove the point — Abraham, the man at the lake, Rabbi Akiva's daughter, Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak: Torah, prayer, and tzedakah override any astrological decree.

⭐ What Shapes a Person — Day of Birth or Hour of Birth?

Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi's Notebook
The day of the week determines character:
• Sunday: great, lacking nothing
• Monday: short-tempered (waters divided)
• Tuesday: wealthy and lustful
• Wednesday: wise, enlightened
• Thursday: charitable (fish/fowl fed by Heaven)
• Friday: diligent
• Shabbat: dies on Shabbat, called great and holy
Rabbi Ḥanina
Not the day — it is the constellation of the hour:
• Sun: glorious, eats/drinks freely
• Venus: wealthy, lustful
• Mercury: wise, educated
• Moon: suffers, builds and tears down
• Saturn: plans foiled
• Jupiter (Tzedek): righteous and charitable
• Mars: sheds blood
Go and Tell Ben Levi Rabbi Ḥanina sent word to Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi: "It is not the constellation of the day that determines — it is the constellation of the hour." Yet ultimately, ein mazal l'Yisrael.

✡️ Four Proofs: Ein Mazal l'Yisrael

Abraham

  • His horoscope: not fit to have a son
  • God said: Emerge from your astrology!
  • Jupiter moved from west to east
  • "Who raised up one from the east" (Isa. 41:2)

Shmuel & Ablet

  • Ablet predicted a man would not return from the lake
  • The man returned safely
  • A snake found cut in two in his bundle
  • He had shared bread with a poor man — tzedakah saved him

Rabbi Akiva's Daughter

  • Chaldeans: snakebite on her wedding night
  • She gave her portion to a hungry poor man
  • Morning: her hairpin found piercing a snake's eye
  • Tzedakah tatzil mimavet (Prov. 10:2)

Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak

  • Chaldeans told his mother: your son will be a thief
  • She never let him go bareheaded
  • One day his head-covering fell — he nearly stole dates
  • Fear of Heaven halted him — fate overridden

🐍 Rabbi Akiva's Daughter — Step by Step

The Prophecy: Chaldean astrologers told Rabbi Akiva that on the very day his daughter enters the wedding canopy, a snake will bite her and she will die.
↓
The Wedding Night: The feast was underway. A poor man knocked at the door — hungry and desperate — but the entire household was absorbed in the celebration and no one heard.
↓
Her Act of Tzedakah: The daughter rose from the feast, took her own wedding portion of food, and gave it personally to the poor man at the door.
↓
The Discovery: She had placed her golden hairpin in a crack in the wall that night. When she removed it in the morning, a snake emerged — dead, with its eye pierced through by the pin.
↓
Rabbi Akiva's Teaching: "Mah asit?" — What did you do to merit this? She told him. He declared: "Tzedakah tatzil mimavet" — Charity saves from death (Prov. 10:2).

🎃 Muktzeh on Shabbat — The Carcass Dispute

Mishna: An animal dies on Shabbat itself. May one cut up the carcass to feed it to dogs?
↓
Rabbi Yehuda — Muktzeh Applies
Forbidden if it died on Shabbat. It was not designated as dog food before Shabbat — it is set-aside (muktzeh) and may not be moved.
Rabbi Shimon — No Muktzeh
Permitted even if it died on Shabbat. Rabbi Shimon holds the prohibition of muktzeh does not apply on Shabbat.
↓
Amoraic Rulings (mnemonic: ערלשחז):
Ulla, Rav Ḥisda, Rav → rule like Rabbi Yehuda (muktzeh applies).
Shmuel, Ze'eiri, Rabbi Yoḥanan → rule like Rabbi Shimon (no muktzeh).
