Shabbat 65 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rami bar Yeḥezkel, under what condition is a woman permitted to go out on Shabbat with a cloth in her ear?
A. The cloth must be prescribed by a physician
B. The cloth must be tied to her ear
C. The cloth must be made of wool
D. The cloth must cover the entire ear
Correct: B — Rami bar Yeḥezkel teaches that the cloth must be tied to her ear — tying is what prevents her from coming to carry it, removing the rabbinic concern.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rabbi Zeira say limits the scope of the dispute between Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi and the Rabbis about a gold tooth?
A. It applies only on Yom Tov, not Shabbat
B. It applies only when the tooth is visible when she smiles
C. It applies only when she has no other tooth to replace it
D. It applies only in a city with a Jewish eruv
Correct: C — Rabbi Zeira says the dispute is specifically when she has no replacement tooth. If she had another tooth, even Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi would agree it is prohibited — she would not be embarrassed to remove the gold one.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say a sela coin is specifically used on a foot wound rather than an earthenware shard?
A. A coin is more comfortable and fits the foot's shape
B. The rust on the coin has medicinal healing properties
C. A coin is lighter and will not burden the foot
D. An earthenware shard is muktzeh on Shabbat
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the benefit of the sela coin comes specifically from its rust, which has healing properties — it is not merely the hardness that helps, making earthenware an inadequate substitute.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rav Huna's ruling regarding women who engage in sexual rubbing with one another?
A. They must bring a guilt-offering
B. They require immersion in a mikveh before resuming marital relations
C. They are disqualified from marrying into the priesthood
D. Their conduct carries no legal consequence under rabbinic law
Correct: C — Rav Huna holds that such women are rendered zona — a status that disqualifies them from marrying a kohen (priest). The Gemara notes this as one possible explanation for Shmuel's father's restriction.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the core question in Abaye's dilemma about a woman who fastens her cloak on a nut?
A. Whether a nut has sufficient monetary value to constitute a prohibited burden
B. Whether using a nut as a button constitutes permissible artifice or prohibited carrying
C. Whether a child's urgent need overrides the prohibition of carrying
D. Whether fastening a garment with a non-standard clasp is itself prohibited
Correct: B — Abaye's dilemma is whether using a nut as a makeshift button to move it to the public domain is permissible artifice (similar to the fire case) or a calculated circumvention of the carrying prohibition. The Gemara leaves it unresolved.
