🪃 Throwing and Liability: When Does Awareness Matter?

One throws an object unwittingly
from domain to domain, or 4 cubits in public domain
↓
Object still in flight — he remembers the prohibition
↓
Object caught / burned / rests in dog's mouth
Exempt — act not completed in normal way
Object lands on ground
Liable — act completed; was unwitting throughout
↓
Core principle: Beginning AND end of the act must both be unwitting for a sin-offering to apply

⚖️ Became Aware Mid-Throw: Rabba vs. Rava

Rabba
Exempt. Even according to Rabban Gamliel (who holds there is no awareness for half a measure), one is exempt here. The moment of awareness mid-flight cannot be acted upon — yet it still breaks the chain of unwitting action. No complete liability arises.
Rava
Liable. Even the Rabbis (who hold awareness of half a measure exempts in normal cases) agree here: since the object will inevitably land the moment it left his hand, the act is already beyond his control. Awareness mid-flight is irrelevant — he is fully liable.
The Gemara's distinction Rabba: awareness — even if unactionable — interrupts the unwitting chain. Rava: inevitable completion means the entire act is treated as unwittingly done from the start.

🔨 Chiseling: Building or Striking a Blow with a Hammer?

Rav — Building (בונה)

  • Chiseling stone is a sub-category of Building
  • Shaping stone is integral to constructing a structure
  • Derives from Tabernacle stone-cutting for the structure
  • Consistent: setting a stone in place = building category

Shmuel — Striking a Blow (מכה בפטיש)

  • Chiseling is a sub-category of Striking a Blow with a Hammer
  • It is a finishing/completion act on a workpiece — not building per se
  • Inserting a pin into a hoe handle: same category (craft completion)
  • Rav Natan bar Oshaya asked Rabbi Yoḥanan — he indicated: striking a blow

📜 When Is a Sin-Offering Owed? The Full Chain

Step 1 — Act Begins Unwittingly
The person starts a prohibited Shabbat labor (throwing, building, carrying) without awareness that it is Shabbat or that the act is prohibited.
↓
Step 2 — No Awareness Intervenes
Throughout the entire act, the person remains unaware. Any mid-act awareness — even if unactionable (e.g., mid-throw) — is disputed: Rabba says it breaks liability; Rava says it does not.
↓
Step 3 — Act Concludes Unwittingly
The prohibited labor completes — object lands, stone is set, chisel finishes — while the person is still unaware. This is the critical moment.
↓
Step 4 — Awareness Comes After Completion
Only after the act is done does the person realize his violation. This post-facto awareness is what triggers the obligation to bring a chata'at (sin-offering).
↓
Special Case — Building: Unlike other labors, building requires no minimum amount. Even the smallest complete act of building — a hole for hiding coins — is enough for full liability.
