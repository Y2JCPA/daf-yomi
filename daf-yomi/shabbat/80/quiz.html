QUESTION 1 OF 5
The Tosefta specifies minimum measures for ink in three forms. What is the measure in all three cases?
A. One letter for dried ink, two letters for quill ink, three for inkwell
B. Two letters for dried ink, two letters for quill ink, two letters for inkwell
C. Enough to fill a quill in all three cases
D. One word in each case, reflecting a complete meaningful unit
Correct: B — The Tosefta specifies the same threshold across all three forms of ink — dried, in the quill, and in the inkwell — each requires enough to write two letters.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava, if someone carries out ink and writes two letters while still walking (never placing the ink down), what is the halachic ruling?
A. Exempt — carrying out requires placing the object at rest in the new domain
B. Exempt — writing and carrying are two separate labors that cannot merge
C. Liable — using the ink for its purpose during transit completes the prohibited act
D. Liable only if he also intended to place it down afterward
Correct: C — Rava rules that consuming the ink through writing while carrying satisfies the placement requirement — the ink was fully used for its intended purpose, completing the act of carrying out.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Someone carries out ½ a dried fig, then — in a SECOND separate lapse of awareness — carries out another ½ dried fig. What is the ruling?
A. Liable — two halves always combine to form a whole dried fig
B. Exempt — the first half is considered 'snatched away' by the second lapse and cannot combine
C. Liable — each half independently creates liability
D. Exempt — a dried fig is never a significant enough measure for liability
Correct: B — With two separate lapses of awareness the first half-fig loses its legal standing — it is 'snatched away' — and the two halves cannot combine. Only within one continuous lapse of awareness can they combine to create liability.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The mishna says the minimum measure for kohl (blue eye shadow) is enough to paint ONE eye. The Gemara objects that women paint both eyes. How does the Gemara resolve this?
A. The mishna uses a round number as an approximation
B. The ruling follows the opinion of a Sage who holds half-measures create liability
C. It refers to a woman with one blind eye who applies kohl only to her one seeing eye
D. Painting one eye at a time is the normal application method
Correct: C — The Gemara (Rav Huna) resolves the apparent absurdity by explaining the mishna refers to a woman with one blind (or missing) eye — she applies kohl to only one eye, making that the minimum meaningful application.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What happened when a man heard of Rav Beivai's success beautifying his daughter with lime, and applied lime to his own daughter all at once instead of limb by limb?
A. His daughter became even more beautiful due to the fuller application
B. There was no difference — the result was the same either way
C. His daughter received a smaller marriage gift of only 200 zuz
D. His daughter was disfigured rather than beautified
Correct: D — The Gemara teaches that lime applied gradually limb by limb has a beautifying effect, while applying it all at once causes burning and disfigurement. The manner of application — which informs the minimum measure — makes all the difference.
