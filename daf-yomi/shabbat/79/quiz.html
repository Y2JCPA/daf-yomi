5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, what is the actual dispute between the Sages regarding promissory notes?
A. Whether a creditor can collect without witnesses present
B. Whether one writes a receipt after repayment
C. Whether the debtor's signature is required
D. Whether an oral admission overrides a written document
Correct: B — Rava clarifies: everyone agrees that when the debtor admits writing the note, the creditor must ratify it. The dispute is whether a receipt is issued upon repayment, which affects whether the document remains valid.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What defines 'ḥifa' as a category of hide?
A. A fully tanned hide ready for sacred writing
B. A hide soaked in water but not yet dried
C. A hide that is salted but not treated with flour or gallnuts
D. A hide taken from an animal slaughtered for ritual purposes
Correct: C — Ḥifa is an intermediate processing stage: the hide has been salted (preserving it) but has not yet undergone the full tanning process involving flour and gallnuts.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the minimum measure of parchment for Shabbat carrying liability?
A. The size used to make an amulet
B. Enough to write two letters of ink
C. Enough to write the shortest portion in the phylacteries
D. A three-by-three handbreadth piece
Correct: C — The mishna states that parchment (klaf) requires the amount needed to write the shortest phylactery portion — which corresponds to the Shema or a similarly brief passage.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rav's ruling about dokhsostos (the inner layer of animal hide)?
A. It is invalid for any sacred writing purpose
B. It may be used for mezuza but not phylacteries
C. It has the same legal status as parchment for writing phylacteries
D. It requires additional gallnut treatment before use
Correct: C — Rav rules that dokhsostos shares the legal status of parchment (klaf), so the portions of the phylacteries may be written on it — just as on the outer surface of the hide.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What principle does the Gemara apply when ruling that phylactery parchment may not be used for a mezuza?
A. Items dedicated to one mitzvah may never be transferred to another
B. One does not downgrade sacred objects to a lesser degree of holiness
C. Parchment used for tefillin becomes permanently consecrated
D. Mezuza requires a separate, lower grade of hide by rabbinic decree
Correct: B — The Gemara applies the principle of 'one does not downgrade in sanctity' (ein moridin b'kedushah) — phylacteries possess greater holiness than a mezuza, so repurposing their parchment downward is prohibited.
