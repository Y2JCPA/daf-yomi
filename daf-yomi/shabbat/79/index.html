📜 Promissory Notes: Must the Creditor Ratify?

First Tanna
If the debtor says 'I repaid' and there are no witnesses to ratify the document, the document has no value.
Rava
Everyone agrees: when a debtor admits he wrote the note, the creditor must ratify it in court. The real dispute is whether one writes a receipt upon repayment.
Core Issue Does the creditor's obligation to ratify depend on the debtor's admission — or is court ratification always required regardless?

🧂 Three Types of Animal Hide (Rabbi Ḥiyya bar Ami / Ulla)

Regular Hide (Untanned)

  • Raw, unprocessed animal skin
  • Minimum measure: size of an amulet
  • Useful for basic wrapping or covering

Ḥifa (Salted Hide)

  • Salted but NOT treated with flour or gallnuts
  • Intermediate processing stage
  • Measure: same as amulet (per mishna)

Tanned Hide / Parchment (Klaf)

  • Fully treated: flour, gallnuts, etc.
  • Used for writing sacred texts
  • Measure: enough to write shortest phylactery portion

📖 Parchment Hierarchy: From Hide to Sacred Text

Raw hide — animal skin before any processing
↓
Ḥifa stage — salted only; intermediate, not yet fit for writing
↓
Full tanning with flour and gallnuts → klaf (parchment), outer side of hide
↓
Dokhsostos = inner layer of hide; Rav rules it valid for phylacteries — same status as parchment
↓
Phylacteries (tefillin) have greater sanctity than mezuza — higher in the hierarchy
↓
Rule: one does not downgrade (אין מורידין בקדושה) — phylactery parchment may NOT be repurposed for a mezuza
