Shabbat 28 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda and Rabba bar Rav Huna, what does a woman who discharges a shaped hand NOT receive?
A. The impurity period of a woman after childbirth
B. The days of purity following the impurity period
C. Any impurity status whatsoever
D. The stringencies of both male and female birth
Correct: B — She observes the impurity period (since the shaped limb indicates a formed fetus), but does not receive the subsequent days of purity — we cannot confirm the entire fetus was discharged.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
From which biblical event does Rav Huna derive that a fetus extending its hand confers childbirth impurity on the mother?
A. The birth of Moses, placed in a basket in the Nile
B. The birth of Perez and Zeraḥ to Tamar — 'as he drew back his hand' (Gen. 38:29)
C. The birth of Jacob and Esau — the younger grasping the elder's heel
D. The binding of Isaac — Abraham's hand holding the knife
Correct: B — Rav Huna cites Genesis 38:29 describing Tamar's twins: 'And it came to pass as he drew back his hand' — indicating that even a hand's emergence from the womb is legally significant.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the mishna, what must a woman do regarding birth impurity if she gives birth to a tumtum?
A. Observe only male birth strictures, since tumtum is presumed male
B. Observe only female birth strictures, since doubt is resolved leniently
C. Wait for the tumtum's sex to be determined before observing any impurity
D. Observe the strictures of both a male and a female birth
Correct: D — Since the sex is unknown, she must observe both: the 14 days impurity and 66 days purity of female birth (which also encompasses the shorter male period), covering all possibilities.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav, why is a tumtum NOT liable for entering the Temple in a state of ritual impurity?
A. A tumtum cannot contract ritual impurity and is therefore never in a state of impurity
B. The verse 'both male and female you shall send out' (Num. 5:3) excludes those of uncertain sex from this liability
C. Tumtum is exempt because the Sages enacted a protective leniency for them
D. Rabbi Akiva's ruling exempts all those born with physical anomalies
Correct: B — Rav derives from Numbers 5:3 that only a definite male or definite female bears liability for entering the Temple while impure — a tumtum or hermaphrodite of uncertain sex does not meet this criterion.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the practical halakhic difference between Rabbi Eliezer and Rabbi Akiva regarding the sliding-scale offering for impurity, as explained by Ḥizkiyya?
A. Rabbi Eliezer requires the person to bring a rich man's offering; Rabbi Akiva allows a poor man's offering
B. Rabbi Eliezer obligates only Kohanim; Rabbi Akiva obligates all Jews
C. Rabbi Eliezer requires knowing the specific cause of impurity; Rabbi Akiva requires only initial awareness of being impure
D. Rabbi Eliezer applies his ruling to teruma only; Rabbi Akiva extends it to Temple entry
Correct: C — Ḥizkiyya explains: Rabbi Eliezer requires knowing the exact cause of impurity (e.g., which carcass), while Rabbi Akiva holds that initial awareness of having been impure — even without knowing the exact cause — creates full liability for a sliding-scale offering.
