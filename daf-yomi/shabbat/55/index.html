💀 Corpse Flesh: Moist vs. Dry — Reish Lakish vs. Rabbi Yoḥanan

Reish Lakish
Derived from creeping things (Lev. 11): just as a creeping thing transmits impurity whether moist or dry, so too corpse flesh transmits impurity even when dry.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Derived from 'one who died' (Num. 19:16): the verse equates a corpse to a sword-slain person — teaching impurity applies whether moist or dry.
Practical Difference
Flesh so dry it crumbles: Reish Lakish says pure — it is no longer considered 'flesh.' Rabbi Yoḥanan says still impure — it retains corpse status.
Baraita Challenge
'Flesh that crumbled is ritually pure' — this challenges Reish Lakish! Answer: that baraita speaks of flesh so powdery it cannot cohere into a solid piece.
Bottom Line Both agree corpse flesh is impure moist AND dry. They differ only on flesh crumbled into dust — Reish Lakish: pure; Rabbi Yoḥanan: impure.

🦷 What on a Corpse Transmits Impurity?

Mishna (Oholot 3:3): Everything in a corpse transmits impurity — except teeth, hair, and nails
↓
When are teeth/hair/nails impure? Only while still attached — once detached they are pure
↓
Rav Adda bar Ahava's Rule: Items transmit corpse impurity only if they are similar to bone
↓
Criterion 1
Created with the person at birth (not grown/acquired later)
Criterion 2
Root does not renew itself — if removed, it does not regrow
↓
✅ Meets Both — Impure
Bones, flesh (when attached), blood
❌ Fails — Pure when detached
Teeth (regrow in youth), hair (regrows), nails (regrow)

👃 What Are 'Nasal Fluids'? — Rav vs. Levi

Rav
Nasal fluids (ניחורין) in the baraita are fluids emitted via the mouth — impure because they contain traces of saliva passing through the same channel.
Levi
Nasal fluids are actual discharge emitted via the nose, and they transmit impurity independently as a distinct category.
Challenge to Rav
If impurity is due to saliva traces — why doesn't the mishna list tears as impure? Rav: tears contain a harmful substance (like poison a gentile might paint on the eye) — different from saliva.
Rav Ḥiyya bar Gurya
Rav held that one can neutralize tears' harmful substance — so they are not analogous to saliva. This explains why tears are listed only as transmitting liquid-impurity, not severe impurity.
Result The baraita lists 9 fluids total. Tears, wound-blood, and zava's milk transmit only lesser liquid impurity (tum'at mashkin); ziva, saliva, and urine transmit severe impurity.

📜 How Do We Know Ziva Is Itself Impure?

אִישׁ אִישׁ כִּי יִהְיֶה זָב מִבְּשָׂרוֹ זוֹבוֹ טָמֵא הוּא (ויקרא טו:ב)
Leviticus 15:2 "When any man has an issue out of his flesh — his issue, it is impure."

The phrase seems redundant. The Gemara unpacks two teachings:

1. Ziva itself is impure (not just the person who emitted it)
2. It transmits impurity by carrying — not only by contact

Why was the verse needed? Rav Yehuda of Diskarta: One might have argued from the scapegoat — the goat causes others' impurity yet is itself pure. Without this verse, we might think ziva similarly causes impurity to others without itself being impure.

How does 'zov / zovo' work? Zov = one emission; zovo = two; 'this is the law' of the zav (Lev. 15:32) implies three — the minimum for zav status.
