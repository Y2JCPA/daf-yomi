🕯️ How the Sages Personally Honored Shabbat

Rabbi Ḥanina & Rabbi Yannai

  • Rabbi Ḥanina wrapped himself in his garment at nightfall on Shabbat eve
  • Said: 'Come and we will go out to greet Shabbat the queen'
  • Rabbi Yannai put on his garments and proclaimed: 'Come O bride, come O bride'

Rabbi Abba, Rav Safra & Rava

  • Rabbi Abba bought meat from 13 butchers for variety and fine quality
  • Placed meats at the door hinge so servants would hurry to prepare
  • Rav Safra personally roasted the head of an animal
  • Rava salted a shibuta fish in honor of Shabbat

Rav Huna, Rav Pappa & Rav Ḥisda

  • Rav Huna personally kindled the Shabbat lamps
  • Rav Pappa personally spun the wicks for the Shabbat lamp
  • Rav Ḥisda personally cut the beets in preparation
  • Each Sage chose one personal act as his Shabbat preparation

💰 The Story of Yosef Who Cherishes Shabbat

Yosef Who Cherishes Shabbat
Had a wealthy gentile as a neighbor
↓
Astrologers prophesy: 'Yosef who cherishes Shabbat will consume all your property'
↓
The gentile sold all his property and bought one precious pearl
Hid it in his hat — Yosef could never acquire it
↓
Wind blew the hat into the sea
A fish swallowed the pearl
↓
The fish was caught on Shabbat eve
Sold to Yosef — he bought it in honor of Shabbat
↓
Yosef found the pearl inside the fish
Sold it for thirteen rooms of gold denarii
↓
"One who lends to Shabbat — Shabbat repays him"

📖 What Is the 'Spice' of Shabbat? — Two Conversations

Exilarch
What does 'The holy one of God is honored' (Isaiah 58:13) refer to?
Rav Hamnuna
That is Yom Kippur — when there is no eating or drinking. Therefore honor it with a clean garment, not food.
Exilarch
How then is Shabbat 'honored,' since Shabbat does have eating and drinking?
Rav Hamnuna
For Shabbat, 'honored' means with both a clean garment AND with fine food and drink — the feast itself is the honor.
Roman Emperor
Why does the fragrance of a cooked Shabbat dish diffuse so wonderfully?
Rabbi Yehoshua ben Ḥananya
We have a spice called shevet (dill) — we place it in Shabbat dishes and it causes this fragrance. The emperor demanded some, but was told: It only works for those who observe Shabbat.
Key Insight The 'extra soul' and spiritual aroma of Shabbat cannot be extracted from its observance — the spice of Shabbat belongs only to Shabbat observers.

📜 Vaykhullu: Reciting, Partnering with God, and Being Forgiven

Obligation: Even an individual praying alone on Shabbat evening must recite Vaykhullu — 'And the heavens and the earth were finished' (Genesis 2:1–3)
↓
Why? Rav Hamnuna taught: Anyone who recites Vaykhullu on Shabbat evening is considered as if he became a partner with the Holy One in the work of Creation
↓
Angelic Reward (Rav Ḥisda / Mar Ukva): The two ministering angels who accompany a person place their hands upon his head
↓
They say: "And your iniquity has passed, and your sin has been atoned" (Isaiah 6:7) — his sins are forgiven
↓
Rabbi Elazar: Always set your table on Friday night, even if you only need it for an olive-bulk of food — the act of preparation honors Shabbat
↓
Rabbi Ḥanina: Also set your table at Shabbat's conclusion — so the departing Shabbat queen sees you honoring her departure as well
