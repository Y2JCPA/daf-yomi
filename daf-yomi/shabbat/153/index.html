🪦 Does a Eulogy Reveal One's Share in the World-to-Come?

Rav Yehuda b. Rav Shmuel
From a person's eulogy it is apparent whether he has a share in the World-to-Come — if the listeners weep and are pained, he has a share.
Abaye
Master (Rabba) — all of Pumbedita hates you. Who will be stirred during your eulogy?
Rabba
It is sufficient for me if you and Rabba bar Rav Ḥanan are stirred. Quality matters more than quantity.
Key Insight Even one or two genuinely moved mourners can testify to a person's spiritual worth — popularity is not the measure of a share in the World-to-Come.

🍽️ Rabban Yohanan ben Zakkai's Parable of the Feast

קהלת ט:ח — בְּכָל-עֵת יִהְיוּ בְגָדֶיךָ לְבָנִים וְשֶׁמֶן עַל-רֹאשְׁךָ אַל-יֶחְסָר
Be Ready at All Times A king invited servants to a feast without announcing the time. The wise adorned themselves and waited at the palace door. The fools returned to their work, saying 'a royal feast takes time to prepare.'

Suddenly the king summoned them — the wise entered adorned, the fools entered filthy. The king declared: 'These who adorned themselves shall sit and eat and drink with me; those who did not shall stand and watch.'

Rabbi Meir adds: standing and watching would itself not be shameful enough — they must serve, looking like servants, not guests.

🧏 Deaf-Mute vs. Minor — To Whom Do You Give the Pouch?

Deaf-Mute (חֵרֵשׁ)

  • Rabbinically exempt — not fully halakhically competent
  • Rabbi Eliezer: has some intelligence (higher status?)
  • Giving to deaf-mute = Rabbinic-level violation
  • Will never 'grow into' full obligation
  • Some say: prefer over minor for this reason

Minor (קָטָן)

  • Currently exempt — under bar/bat mitzvah age
  • Will eventually become fully obligated
  • Should not be habituated to Shabbat desecration
  • Some say: prefer minor — lower current status
  • Gemara: dilemma left unresolved (תֵּיקוּ)
