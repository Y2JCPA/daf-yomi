🌿 Bundle of Fragrant Herbs: Three Views

Rabbi Meir
A bundle of fragrant herbs is a burden. A woman who carries it into the public domain is liable to bring a sin-offering.
Rabbis
It is an ornament — women wear it for fragrance. She is exempt from Torah liability, though it is still rabbinically prohibited.
Rabbi Eliezer
Against Rabbi Meir: she is exempt. Against the Rabbis: she may go out ab initio — it is fully an ornament and entirely permitted.
Gemara's Resolution Rabbi Eliezer made two separate statements in two separate baraitot — one responding to Rabbi Meir (she is exempt), one responding to the Rabbis (she may go out ab initio). There is no contradiction.

💍 Rings on Shabbat: Ulla's Reversal — Men vs. Women

Women (Mishna)

  • Ring WITH a seal — Forbidden ✗
    A seal-ring is not a woman's typical ornament; carrying it is a burden
  • Ring WITHOUT a seal — Permitted ✓
    A plain decorative ring is a woman's ornament

Men (Ulla's Ruling)

  • Ring WITH a seal — Permitted ✓
    A signet ring is a man's ornament and tool of authority
  • Ring WITHOUT a seal — Forbidden ✗
    A plain ring is not a man's typical ornament; it is a burden

Why the Difference?

  • Rav Yosef: Women are a people unto themselves — their ornamental customs differ fundamentally from men's
  • The gap between men and women is greater than the gap between different professions
  • What is an ornament for one group may be a burden for another

🔑 Key in Hand & the Empty Flask

Rabbi Meir
A woman may NOT go out with a key in her hand on Shabbat. If she did, she is liable to bring a sin-offering — it is a plain burden, not an ornament.
Rabbis
She is exempt from Torah liability but it remains rabbinically prohibited.
Rabbi Eliezer
Responding to Rabbi Meir: a woman who goes out with a bundle of herbs or an empty flask of balsam is also liable — the flask retains fragrance and she carries it for that reason.
Rav Adda bar Ahava
Infers from the empty flask: one who carries out less than the minimum measure of food but carries it in a vessel is liable — for the vessel itself.
Rav Ashi's Counter The empty flask case is different — since it retains residual scent, it has the full importance of a perfume flask. You cannot generalize from it to all sub-minimum quantities in vessels.

🏺 Is Balsam Oil Subject to the Mourning Decree?

Amos 6:6 — 'They anoint themselves with the chief ointments but are not grieved for the hurt of Joseph'
Rav Yehuda (Shmuel): refers to balsam oil, the luxury of pre-destruction Jerusalem
↓
Rabbi Yehuda ben Bava: decreed against balsam oil as part of mourning over the Temple's destruction
↓
Sages Disagreed
Did not accept the decree — balsam oil was too uncommon to require prohibition
Rav Yosef's Challenge
If balsam oil is a luxury that causes forgetting the Temple, why no decree? Abaye resolves it.
↓
Abaye's Principle: Sages decreed only against activities combining pleasure + joy together. Balsam oil = pleasure only, not joy → no decree needed.

🏛️ Isaiah 3 — The Sins and Punishment of Jerusalem's Daughters Rava bar Rav Ilai: the daughters of Zion were haughty — walking with outstretched necks and wanton eyes, mincing steps, tinkling ornaments — all to entice men.

Their measure-for-measure punishment (Rabba bar Ulla):
• Sweet smell → stench
• Belt → rope
• Well-set hair → baldness
• Fine garments → sackcloth
• Beauty → shame

Rabbi Abbahu (Amud B): The people of Jerusalem were arrogant, couching immoral invitations in polite euphemisms — 'come cleanse your feet with me' — while swapping wives and defiling each other's beds.
