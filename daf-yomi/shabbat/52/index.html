🌿 Two Hairs: Signs, Status, and Legal Consequence

Two pubic hairs appear on a minor
↓
Girl
Adult status — may perform ḥalitza or enter yibbum; mi'un (refusal) is no longer valid
Boy
Adult status — obligated in all mitzvot; may be liable as a stubborn and rebellious son
↓
Dispute: Do hairs need follicles? Can they be in different locations?
↓
Each hair needs its own follicle (Rav Malkiyyu); hairs may be in different locations — one above the organ, one on the testicles (Shmuel)

⚖️ Mi'un After Two Hairs: Rabbi Yehuda vs. the Rabbis

Rabbis
Once a girl grows two pubic hairs she is a legal adult — mi'un is finished. Two hairs are the sole threshold.
Rabbi Yehuda
She may still perform mi'un even after two hairs appear, UNLESS black hair is preponderant throughout the pubic area. Two sparse hairs are insufficient to close the window.
Rabbi Yehuda (concedes)
I concede one point: if she engaged in intercourse AFTER growing two hairs, she can no longer perform mi'un — intercourse creates a Torah-level betrothal that refusal cannot undo.
Rav Kahana
Colleagues who ruled leniently like Rabbi Yehuda were overruled. The story of Rabbi Yishmael's daughter confirms we follow the Rabbis without exception.
Final Ruling The Sages assembled, counted votes, and declared: Mi'un is valid only until two pubic hairs grow — the opinion of the Rabbis prevails.

👩 The Story of Rabbi Yishmael's Daughter

Rabbi Yishmael's daughter was married as a minor by her mother and brothers after her father's death
↓
She came to the study hall seeking to perform mi'un — to refuse and dissolve the marriage
↓
Sages hesitated: 'Could it be that Rabbi Yishmael's own offspring would stumble on a ruling he himself taught?'
↓
Rabbi Yishmael held that a sota who was taken (raped) remains permitted to her husband — refusal could cast his daughter in the status of a prohibited sota
↓
Rav Kahana's colleagues had been permitting mi'un even after two hairs grew, following Rabbi Yehuda's leniency
↓
Outcome: The incident prompted the Sages to assemble and rule definitively — mi'un ends the moment two pubic hairs appear

📋 Rav Malkiyyu vs. Rav Malkiyya — Two Sages, One Mnemonic

Rav Malkiyyu

  • Rulings that relate to a mishna or baraita
  • Skewer (שפוד) halakha — roasting law
  • Maidservants (שפחות) halakha — per Rav Pappa
  • Follicles: two hairs must each have their own follicle to count as sign of adulthood

Rav Malkiyya

  • Rulings that are independent amoraic statements (not anchored in mishna or baraita)
  • Maidservants halakha — per Rabbi Ḥanina b. Rav Ika's division
  • Standalone halakhic pronouncements of amora'im

Mnemonic (Rav Pappa)

  • שפוד ושפחות — מלכיו
  • Skewer and Maidservants = Rav Malkiyyu
  • Disagreement: Rabbi Ḥanina b. Rav Ika assigns Maidservants to Malkiyya — Gemara notes the dispute

🩸 When a Garment Stain Triggers Zava Concern

כתם — stain; זיבה — zava flow; גריס — split bean; בדוקה — examined garment
New Mishna: Stains, Ziva, and the Split-Bean Measure Scenario: A woman finds a red stain on her garment but doesn't know when it appeared or on which of several days she wore it.

Rabbis (= Rabbi Ḥanina ben Antigonus): Stains alone do not ordinarily create zava concern. But if she wore an examined garment for three days and found a stain of three split beans in one place, she must reckon with possible ziva.

Rava: Rabbi Ḥanina ben Antigonus 'bested' the other Rabbis with this argument — stains on three separate examined robes can trigger ziva concern even without seeing blood flow directly from her body.

Key rule: A stain smaller than one split bean per day (×3 days) can be attributed to a louse — no ziva concern. Only a concentrated stain of three split beans forces the reckoning.
