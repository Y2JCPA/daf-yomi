Shabbat 52 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Rabbis in the mishna, at what point can a young girl NO LONGER perform mi'un?
A. When she turns twelve years and one day old
B. When she grows two pubic hairs
C. When she engages in intercourse for the first time
D. When her husband takes her into his home
Correct: B — The Rabbis rule that once a girl grows two pubic hairs she is a legal adult and mi'un is no longer available. Rabbi Yehuda dissents, requiring the hair to be preponderant, but the Sages ruled in accordance with the Rabbis.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was the decisive lesson drawn from the story of Rabbi Yishmael's daughter?
A. Mi'un remains valid until the hair is preponderant, as Rabbi Yehuda says
B. A daughter of a sage may always perform mi'un as a special privilege
C. Once two pubic hairs grow, mi'un is no longer permitted — the Rabbis' ruling is final
D. Mi'un requires consent of the Nasi before it is performed
Correct: C — The incident prompted the Sages to assemble and rule definitively that mi'un ends with the growth of two pubic hairs. Colleagues who had ruled leniently like Rabbi Yehuda were overruled by Rav Kahana, who cited this very story.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rav Malkiyyu rule is required for two hairs to be a valid sign of adulthood?
A. The hairs must be adjacent to each other
B. Each hair must grow from its own distinct follicle
C. The hairs must both be black in color
D. The hairs must exceed the length of a barleycorn
Correct: B — Rav Malkiyyu rules that each of the two hairs must have its own follicle. This is supported by God's response to Job: for each hair on a person's head, a distinct follicle was created so that two hairs do not draw sustenance from one source.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Shmuel (as cited by Rav Yehuda), where may the two hairs signifying adulthood be located?
A. Both must be in the pubic area, adjacent to each other
B. Both must be on the lower abdomen
C. One may be above the organ and one on the testicles — they need not be adjacent
D. One must be on the right side and one on the left side of the pubic area
Correct: C — Shmuel rules that the two hairs do not need to be in the same location. One can be on the spoon-shaped area above the organ and one on the testicles, and together they constitute a valid sign of adulthood. A baraita confirms this with parallel examples for girls.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Gemara, who is identified as 'the Rabbis' in the mishna about stains on garments?
A. Rabbi Meir
B. Rabbi Akiva
C. Rabbi Ḥanina ben Antigonus
D. Rabbi Yehuda ben Agra
Correct: C — The Gemara identifies 'the Rabbis' in the stains mishna as Rabbi Ḥanina ben Antigonus, who taught in a baraita that while ordinary stains do not create zava concern, three stains found on three separately examined robes can — a position that Rava says 'bested' the other Rabbis.
