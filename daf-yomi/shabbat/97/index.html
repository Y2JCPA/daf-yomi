⚖️ Rabbi Akiva vs. the Rabbis: Throwing Through the Public Domain

Rabbi Akiva
One who throws from private domain to private domain, passing through the public domain, is liable. The object traversed public space — that act of transfer counts.
The Rabbis
He is exempt. The object never came to rest in the public domain; there was no meaningful act of transfer into or within it.
Rabba
Does their dispute concern an object that traveled below ten handbreadths — legally within the public domain — or above ten, where airspace status is contested?
Rav Hamnuna
The baraita resolves it: the dispute is about below ten handbreadths. Above ten, all agree the thrower is exempt by Torah law, though a rabbinic prohibition applies.
Baraita Summary Below 3 handbreadths: liable by all (lavud). Between 3 and 10: Rabbi Akiva liable, Rabbis exempt. Above 10: Torah-exempt, rabbinically forbidden.

📏 The Lavud Principle — Why Exactly Three Handbreadths?

Question: From where do the Sages derive that gaps of less than 3 handbreadths are considered closed (lavud)?
↓
Rav Ḥisda & Rav Hamnuna: a gap under 3 handbreadths is impossible for a goat's leg to pass through — it is functionally sealed
↓
Challenge
If that is the reason, why isn't a gap of exactly 3 also considered lavud?
Answer
A gap of exactly 3 handbreadths admits a goat's leg — the partition fails its purpose and is not treated as closed
↓
Rule confirmed: Only strictly less than 3 handbreadths qualifies as lavud — functionally impenetrable

🙏 Miriam's Leprosy — The Cost of Suspecting the Innocent

וַיַּעַן מֹשֶׁה וַיֹּאמֶר וְהֵן לֹא יַאֲמִינוּ לִי (שמות ד:א)
Reish Lakish One who suspects innocent people of wrongdoing is afflicted in his own body.

Proof: Moses said, "But they will not believe me" — yet Israel were believers! As it says: "The people believed" (Exodus 4:31), and they are children of believers (Abraham believed God). Because Moses unjustly suspected them, he was struck with leprosy on his hand.

⚖️ Divine Attribute of Beneficence vs. Divine Attribute of Punishment

Attribute of Punishment

  • Required two separate actions to manifest
  • Moses' hand: placed in, then withdrawn — two steps
  • Both the affliction and its sign required motion
  • Slower in taking effect by design

Attribute of Beneficence

  • One action sufficed for healing
  • Withdrawal alone restored Moses' hand
  • Goodness is delivered more rapidly
  • Rava: beneficence always outpaces punishment

🐍 Aaron's Staff — A Miracle Within a Miracle

Aaron casts his staff before Pharaoh — it becomes a serpent (Exodus 7:12). This alone is miraculous.
↓
The Egyptian magicians replicate the feat: their staffs also become serpents. Now staff-serpents face staff-serpents.
↓
Aaron's staff swallowed their staffs — but Rabbi Elazar teaches: it did so while it was still a staff, not after reverting to serpent form.
↓
Why does this matter? A staff swallowing serpents is a far greater miracle than a serpent swallowing serpents — nature bent twice over.
↓
Lesson: Divine miracles operate at multiple layers of wonder. The miracle within the miracle magnifies the testimony of divine power.
