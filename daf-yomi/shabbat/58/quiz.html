Shabbat 58 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Rav Ashi say Shmuel specifically mentioned the woman examined the ground (rather than a cloth)?
A. Ground is more comfortable to sit on and easier to examine
B. Ground is not susceptible to ritual impurity, consistent with Rabbi Neḥemya's view
C. Shmuel holds that cloth examination is always unreliable
D. The Rabbis prohibited sitting on cloth during examination
Correct: B — Rav Ashi explains that Shmuel follows Rabbi Neḥemya: items not susceptible to impurity (like the ground) require no checking — thus the woman is pure even rabbinically.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Ketina, where does the 'inner side' of the leg end?
A. Only the inner thigh above the knee
B. Up to and including the place of the joint
C. Below the knee only
D. The entire leg is considered inner regardless of position
Correct: B — Rav Ketina explicitly teaches that 'inner side extends until the place of the joint' — making the joint itself part of the inner (impure) zone.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel's critique of the Rabbis' ruling on louse attribution?
A. They require too large a stain size for attribution
B. Their leniency means no woman would ever be rendered impure from stains
C. They require the louse to have been killed intentionally
D. They do not permit attribution to wounds or butchers
Correct: B — Rabban Shimon argues: since no sheet is entirely louse-free, allowing unlimited attribution without requiring a killed louse means 'you have no woman who is not pure' — an absurd result.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda's principle, is a stain exactly the size of a split bean pure or impure?
A. Pure — 'up to a split bean' includes the split bean's exact size
B. Impure — when the rule leads to leniency, 'up to' excludes the exact measure
C. It depends on whether she killed the louse or not
D. Pure only if she is in a town with pigs
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda: when 'up to' leads to leniency (allowing attribution = purity), it is exclusive of the exact measure. So a stain precisely the size of a split bean is impure — just beyond the limit.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What reason did Rabbi Akiva give his students for his lenient ruling about the healed wound?
A. The wound was far from the vaginal area so blood could not have come from there
B. The Sages gave the laws of stains to purify Israel, not to burden them with unnecessary stringency
C. She had immersed in a mikveh within that week
D. The blood's color proved it came from the wound, not menstruation
Correct: B — Rabbi Akiva declared: 'The Sages did not state the matter of stains to impose stringency on Israel but to purify them.' The entire framework of attribution exists to give women the benefit of the doubt.
