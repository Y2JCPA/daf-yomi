Shabbat 23 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yishmael, for a gezerah shavah (verbal analogy) to be completely valid and irrefutable, it must be:
A. Supported by at least one logical argument
B. Free on both sides — neither term was used to teach something else
C. Free on at least one side
D. Derived from a verse in the Prophets or Writings
Correct: B — Rabbi Yishmael holds that a gezerah shavah is only fully valid when free on both sides — meaning neither of the two shared words was 'available' to teach anything else. This is why the Torah made the animal–human analogy free on both sides: so it could not be refuted.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rabbi Yannai's explanation for Rabbi Meir's ruling about animal-form fetuses is that:
A. Animals and humans share the same skeletal structure
B. Animal eyes are round like human eyes
C. Animal eyes face forward, fixed in the front of the head like a human's
D. Animals were created on the same day as humans
Correct: C — Rabbi Yannai says that land animals (and owls) have forward-facing eyes like humans, unlike birds and snakes whose eyes are on the sides. This frontal eye placement is Rabbi Meir's basis for treating an animal-form fetus as human-like.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rabbi Yoḥanan requires which set of features to be present for a fetus to be considered of 'human face' and render the mother impure?
A. Eyes, nose, and mouth only
B. Any one feature of a human face
C. Forehead, eyebrows, eyes, cheeks, and chin — the entire face
D. Half the face on either side
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan defines the Rabbis' position precisely: the entire human face must be present — forehead, eyebrows, eyes, cheeks, and chin. This is stricter than Ḥasa's view that half a human face is sufficient.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rava rules regarding a fetus with a sealed esophagus vs. a punctured (tereifa) esophagus. Which statement is correct?
A. Sealed esophagus: mother impure; punctured: mother not impure
B. Both render the mother impure
C. Sealed esophagus: mother not impure; punctured (tereifa): mother is impure
D. Neither renders the mother impure since the fetus cannot survive
Correct: C — Rava distinguishes: a sealed esophagus means the fetus has no viable opening and is not considered an offspring → no impurity. A punctured esophagus makes the fetus a tereifa (will die within 12 months), but it is still a living being at birth → mother is impure.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The Gemara asks why the verbal analogy between 'man' and 'sea monster' is rejected. The answer is:
A. Sea monsters are not mentioned in the Torah at all
B. The analogy is free on both sides but lacks rabbinic support
C. The analogy is free on only one side and there is a valid logical refutation against it
D. Rabbi Meir personally rejected this analogy in a baraita
Correct: C — According to the Rabbis, a gezerah shavah that is only free on one side may be used but can be refuted. In this case, not only is the man–sea monster analogy free on only one side, but there is also a logical refutation (pirka) against it — so it is rejected.
