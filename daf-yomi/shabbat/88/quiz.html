Shabbat 88 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
When Israel said Na'aseh v'Nishma, how many crowns did each person receive, and why that number?
A. One crown — for their unified acceptance of the Torah
B. Three crowns — representing Torah, Prophets, and Writings
C. Two crowns — one for na'aseh (we will do) and one for nishma (we will hear)
D. Six crowns — one for each day of Creation
Correct: C — 600,000 ministering angels descended and tied two crowns to each Israelite — one corresponding to 'We will do' and one to 'We will hear.' These are the same two declarations that angels follow when carrying out God's word.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was the core of Moses' argument to the angels about why the Torah belongs to humans?
A. Humans wrote the Torah, so it is rightfully theirs
B. God commanded it directly and the angels had no right to question
C. The Torah's commandments address human struggles (murder, theft, covetousness) that don't apply to angels
D. The Torah is too difficult for angels to understand
Correct: C — Moses argued that the Torah says 'I am the Lord who took you out of Egypt' — did the angels go to Egypt? It says 'Do not murder, do not steal' — is there an evil inclination among angels? The Torah is tailor-made for human beings.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava, what determines whether Torah functions as a drug of life or a drug of death?
A. The amount of time one dedicates to study each day
B. Whether one studies with a teacher or alone
C. Whether one's approach is wholehearted and sincere ('right-handed') or half-hearted and deceitful ('left-handed')
D. Whether one focuses on halacha or aggadah
Correct: C — Rava taught that for those who are 'right-handed' — engaging Torah with strength, good will, and sanctity — it is a drug of life. For those who are 'left-handed' — approaching it insincerely or with deceit — it becomes a drug of death.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Hizkiya's resolution of the apparent contradiction in Psalm 76:9 — the earth both 'feared' and 'was silent'?
A. The earth first feared God's power, then fell silent in reverent worship
B. The earth feared that Israel would reject the Torah, then was silent with relief when they accepted it
C. The earth feared the thunder at Sinai, then fell silent when it ended
D. Fear and silence are synonyms in biblical poetry — there is no contradiction
Correct: B — Hizkiya explains: the earth first feared that Israel might refuse the Torah and the world would return to void, then fell silent with relief when Israel declared Na'aseh v'Nishma — expressing joy through hushed awe.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What was the significance of Moses being the 'third-born child' in the teaching about the threefold Torah?
A. Being third-born made Moses humble enough to receive the Torah
B. It was part of a pattern: the threefold Torah was given to a tripartite people, through the third-born Moses, in Sivan the third month
C. Moses' two older siblings Miriam and Aaron each received one-third of the Torah
D. There is no significance — it is a coincidence mentioned in passing
Correct: B — A Galilean scholar taught before Rav Ḥisda: Blessed is God Who gave the threefold Torah (Torah, Prophets, Writings) to a tripartite people (Kohanim, Levites, Israelites), through Moses the third-born (after Miriam and Aaron), in Sivan the third month — all elements align in threes.
