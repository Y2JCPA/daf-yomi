📜 The Secret of the Angels

נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע — שמות כד:ז
נַעֲשֶׂה וְנִשְׁמָע When Israel preceded 'We will do' before 'We will hear,' 600,000 ministering angels descended and tied two crowns to each Israelite — one for na'aseh and one for nishma. A Divine Voice rang out: 'Who revealed to My children this secret that the ministering angels use?' As it is written: 'Bless the Lord, you His angels, who are mighty in strength, who fulfill His word, listening to the voice of His word' — first they do, then they listen.

👼 Moses Argues for the Torah

Angels
Master of the Universe! What is one born of a woman doing here among us? The Torah is a heavenly treasure — give Your glory to the heavens, not to flesh and blood!
God
Moses, hold onto My Throne of Glory and provide them with an answer as to why the Torah should be given to humans.
Moses
The Torah says 'I am the Lord your God who took you out of Egypt' — did you go down to Egypt? It says 'Do not murder, do not steal, do not covet' — is there jealousy or evil inclination among you?
Angels
(Concede — each angel befriends Moses and gives him a gift)
Moses
Even the Angel of Death revealed his secret to me — use incense to stop a plague — as proven when Aaron halted the plague in the wilderness.
Outcome The Torah was given to Israel. Even the Angel of Death befriended Moses and disclosed his secret. The angels became Moses' allies.

💊 Two Paths with Torah

Right-Handed — Drug of Life

  • Approaches Torah with strength, good will, and sanctity
  • The person of integrity — 'the integrity of the upright guides them'
  • Torah becomes סַם חַיִּים — a drug of life
  • Like Rava: so immersed in study his bleeding fingers went unnoticed
  • Fulfills 'We will do' before 'We will hear'

Left-Handed — Drug of Death

  • Approaches Torah half-heartedly or deceitfully
  • The crooked — 'the perverseness of the faithless destroys them'
  • Torah becomes סַם מָוֶת — a drug of death
  • Like the heretic who mocked Rava's bleeding and paid the price
  • The same verse that uplifts the righteous condemns the faithless

🌍 The Earth Feared and Was Silent

Hizkiya asks: Psalm 76:9 — 'The earth feared, and was silent'. But if it feared, why was it silent? If silent, why did it fear?
↓
The two reactions reflect two different moments at Sinai
↓
First — Fear
The earth feared that Israel might refuse to accept the Torah and the world would revert to chaos
Then — Silence
The earth fell silent with relief when Israel declared: Na'aseh v'Nishma! — We will do and we will hear
