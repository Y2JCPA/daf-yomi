Shabbat 81 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Ulla resolves the apparent contradiction between the Rabbis' measure (spoon) and Rabbi Yehuda's measure (key) for carrying a bone. How?
A. Rabbi Yehuda referred to a decorative key, which is spoon-sized
B. Rabbi Yehuda referred only to the teeth of the key, not the full key
C. The Rabbis' spoon is larger than a regular spoon
D. Ulla rules that Rabbi Yehuda is always more lenient
Correct: B — Ulla explains that Rabbi Yehuda did not mean the entire key — which would be a larger measure — but only the teeth of the key, making his ruling equivalent to the Rabbis' spoon measure.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the baraita, how many sharpened stones may one take into the bathroom on Shabbat?
A. One
B. Two
C. Three
D. As many as needed for the purpose
Correct: C — The baraita states explicitly that three sharpened stones (שלשה אבנים משופות) may be taken into the bathroom on Shabbat — a fixed and limited number.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Reish Lakish rules it is permitted to wipe with a stone that has grass growing on it. What is the halakhic consequence of accidentally detaching the grass?
A. No consequence — unintentional actions are exempt
B. The stone becomes muktzeh and may not be used further
C. One is liable to bring a sin-offering
D. One must perform teshuvah but no offering is required
Correct: C — Reish Lakish rules that one who unwittingly detaches grasses from such a stone on Shabbat is liable to bring a sin-offering, because detaching vegetation (קוצר) is a prohibited Shabbat labor.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rava teaches that Rabbi Yoḥanan's prohibition against wiping with an earthenware shard is based on which reason?
A. Danger from the sharp edges of the shard
B. The shard is considered muktzeh
C. Removal of hairs constitutes a forbidden Shabbat melacha
D. It was enacted due to the witchcraft incident on the boat
Correct: C — Rava teaches that Rabbi Yoḥanan's true reason is the removal of hairs — scraping with the shard's rough surface removes hair, which constitutes a forbidden Shabbat labor. The danger reason was rejected because the shard is also permitted on weekdays.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
On what basis does Rav Ḥisda permit carrying bathroom stones up to a roof-bathroom on Shabbat?
A. Pikuach nefesh — there is a health risk involved
B. Kavod HaBriot — human dignity overrides rabbinic prohibitions
C. The stones are not muktzeh because they are designated for use
D. Carrying to a roof is not a biblical prohibition, only rabbinic
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda invokes the principle of Kavod HaBriot (גדול כבוד הבריות) — great is human dignity, for it overrides a rabbinic negative commandment. Denying someone access to wiping materials compromises human dignity, which the Sages never intended their decrees to do.
