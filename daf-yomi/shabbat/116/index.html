📜 Why Are the Inverted Nuns There?

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
The inverted nun signs mark this portion because it is a book unto itself — the Torah actually has seven books, not five. This is the meaning of Proverbs 9:1: 'she carved its seven pillars.'
Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel
The signs mark it because the portion is out of place here. In the future it will be uprooted and written in its proper location — hence the signs bracket it like a displaced text.
The Verse (Proverbs 9:1) "With wisdom she built her house, she carved its seven pillars" — Rabbi Yonatan in the name of Rabbi Yonatan: these are the seven books of Torah according to Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's opinion (Bereishit, Shemot, Vayikra, Bamidbar-part-1, ויהי בנסוע, Bamidbar-part-2, Devarim).

🔥 Rescuing Sacred Texts from Fire on Shabbat

Fire breaks out on Shabbat — what may be rescued?
↓
✅ Torah Scroll
Rescue it — to an alleyway or courtyard. Casing and money inside rescued incidentally.
❓ Blank Folios
Debated: folios attached to a scroll are rescued with it; standalone blank folios — unresolved.
🚫 Heretics' Scrolls
Do NOT rescue. Let them burn even though God's Name appears — heresy must not spread.
↓
85-Letter Rule: An erased scroll containing ≥85 legible letters (= length of ויהי בנסוע) is rescued. Fewer — let it burn.

📜 Rescued vs. Burned: What the Gemara Decides

Rescued on Shabbat ✅

  • Torah scroll → moved to alleyway
  • Torah scroll casing → rescued with scroll
  • Phylactery casing → with phylacteries
  • Money inside casing → incidentally saved
  • Erased scroll with ≥85 legible letters
  • Blank folios attached to a valid Torah scroll

NOT Rescued — Left to Burn 🚫

  • Torah scrolls written by heretics (מינים)
  • Blank folios in heretics' scrolls
  • "Better the Name erased than heresy spread" (R. Yishmael)
  • Books of the house of Abidan — debated by R. Abbahu
  • Erased scroll with fewer than 85 legible letters

📚 Reading Writings (כתובים) on Shabbat — Three Opinions

Rav
Prohibited only in the place of the study hall during study hours. Elsewhere, or at non-study times — fully permitted. The decree targets distraction from Torah in the beit midrash.
Shmuel
During study-hall hours — prohibited everywhere, whether inside or outside the study hall. Outside those hours — permitted everywhere. The decree follows the time, not the place.
Rav Ashi
The dispute stands as stated. Shmuel's position aligns with Rabbi Neḥemya, who taught that sacred writings may be read in any place, but not during study-hall hours anywhere.
Root of the Decree The Sages prohibited reading Writings on Shabbat to prevent neglect of Torah study in the beit midrash (בטול בית המדרש). The Gemara clarifies the exact scope of this decree.

⚖️ Imma Shalom and the Corrupt Philosopher

אמרה ליה: נהר נהרה נהוריך כשרגא
Story (116a–b) Imma Shalom (wife of Rabbi Eliezer ben Hyrcanus; sister of Rabban Gamliel) wanted to expose a Christian philosopher who claimed never to accept bribes.

Act I: She secretly bribed him with a golden lamp. He cited the avon gilayon (Gospels): 'I came not to diminish Moses's Torah but to add to it — daughters inherit equally with sons.' He ruled in her favor.

Act II: The next day, Rabban Gamliel brought him a Libyan donkey. The philosopher reversed himself: 'I have reached the end of the avon gilayon — where there is a son, the daughter does not inherit.'

Imma Shalom's rejoinder: "Let your light shine like a lamp" — mocking his first golden-lamp bribe and his exposed hypocrisy.
