Shabbat 32 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Meir prohibit a minor girl from entering levirate marriage?
A. Because the Torah requires a 'woman' and she is too young
B. Lest she prove to be an aylonit (sexually underdeveloped), invalidating the marriage retroactively
C. Because most minor girls menstruate and would render the husband impure
D. Because Samaritan law forbids it and we follow their custom
Correct: B — Rabbi Meir is concerned for the minority of girls who will turn out to be aylonit, making the levirate bond retroactively invalid — so he rules against yibum for minors.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava, what is the relationship between the halakha regarding intercourse with a 3-year-old girl and the verse about menstruation from birth?
A. Both are derived from the same verse in Leviticus
B. One contradicts the other, so we rule leniently
C. One is Halacha l'Moshe miSinai and the other is derived from a verse — no contradiction
D. Both are rabbinic decrees with no biblical source
Correct: C — Rava resolves the apparent contradiction: the 3-year threshold is Halacha l'Moshe miSinai (orally transmitted from Sinai), while the one-day-old's niddah status is derived from the verse. They are different categories.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does the word 'and' (ו) in 'and if a woman' (ואשה) in Leviticus 15:19 serve to include?
A. Adult women who are unmarried
B. Women outside the land of Israel
C. Young girls, even from one day old, in the halakhot of niddah
D. Women who experience irregular bleeding (zava)
Correct: C — The extra 'and' is a biblical inclusion (ribbui) expanding the base case of 'a woman' to include young girls — even a one-day-old — in the halakhot of menstrual impurity.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Mar Zutra say the baby girl in Ein Bul needed to be immersed outside Eretz Yisrael?
A. So that her mother could hold her while eating teruma
B. To allow smearing of teruma oil on the baby, since smearing is equivalent to eating
C. Because Samaritan law required it for all baby girls
D. To protect the baby from the impurity of her mother
Correct: B — Mar Zutra explains that 'smearing' teruma oil counts as 'eating' for purposes of impurity. If the baby were tamei, smearing teruma oil on her would be forbidden — so she needed immersion first.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does the mishna mean when it says Samaritan men impart impurity to the 'lower bedding like the upper bedding'?
A. All ten mattresses stacked under a Samaritan man become equally impure
B. The lowest mattress under a man who had relations with a niddah is a rishon l'tumah, like the bedding above a zav
C. Samaritan men make bedding impure only if they sleep on the top mattress
D. The upper bedding of a Samaritan man disqualifies teruma; the lower does not
Correct: B — The Gemara explains: the mishna means the lowest mattress under such a man has the same status as the upper bedding of a zav — a rishon l'tumah — which disqualifies teruma (food and drink).
