⚖️ Rabbi Meir vs. the Rabbis: Minors and Levirate Bonds

Rabbis
A minor boy may not perform ḥalitza because the Torah says 'man' — and we compare a woman to a man. But a minor may enter levirate marriage. We follow the majority: most minors will mature normally.
Rabbi Meir
A minor boy may not enter yibum lest he prove to be a saris (sexually underdeveloped). A minor girl may not enter lest she prove to be an aylonit. I am concerned even for the minority.
Gemara
But Rabbi Meir is only concerned for a common minority — is the minority of menstruating baby girls also 'common'? Yes! Rabbi Yosei reports an incident in Ein Bul where a baby girl menstruated and was immersed for teruma.
Mar Zutra
That immersion was for smearing oil of teruma outside Eretz Yisrael — since smearing equals eating, it would be forbidden without immersion. A verse extends 'drinking' to include smearing.
Bottom Line Samaritan girls are niddah from the cradle — per Rabbi Meir, who heeds the minority. Jewish girls: we interpret 'and if a woman' (ואשה) to include young girls only from the age of sensitivity to menstruation.

📜 Including Young Girls in Niddah: The Verse Logic

Leviticus 15:19
"And if a woman has an issue"
(ואשה כי תהיה זבה)
↓
"A woman" alone
Would include only an adult woman
The word "and" (ו)
Expands to include young girls — even a one-day-old
↓
But: Intercourse of a girl aged 3 years and 1 day is Halacha l'Moshe miSinai, not derived from verse
↓
Rava: These are halakhot transmitted to Moses from Sinai — Sages merely found scriptural support. No contradiction!

🏔️ Halacha l'Moshe miSinai vs. Derived from Verse

Derived from Verse

  • A one-day-old girl is included in the halakhot of a menstruating woman
  • A one-day-old boy is included in the halakhot of a zav
  • Derived from the inclusive word "and" (ו) in Leviticus 15:19
  • Drinking teruma while impure = liability; verse extends to smearing

Halacha l'Moshe miSinai

  • Intercourse with a girl aged 3 years and 1 day has legal effect
  • Seminal emission of a boy aged 9 years and 1 day has legal effect
  • Transmitted orally from Sinai — Sages supported with verses post-facto
  • Cannot be contradicted by a verse; no logical conflict with the other laws

🛏️ Samaritan Men: Lower Bedding Like Upper Bedding

Mishna
Samaritan men impart ritual impurity to the lower bedding like the upper bedding. What does this mean?
First Interpretation
If there are ten mattresses stacked, the lowest one under a Samaritan man is as impure as the highest one above a zav? Rejected — that would make the lowest mattress a rishon, which seems excessive.
Gemara (Accepted)
The mishna means: the lowest mattress beneath a man who has intercourse with a menstruating woman is like bedding above a zav — it is a rishon l'tumah (first-degree impurity) that disqualifies teruma.
Gemara
Source: 'And whoever touches any thing that was under him shall be impure' (Lev. 15:10) — 'under him' refers to multiple items beneath the zav. The uppermost of those items is a rishon, imparting impurity to food and drink.
Key Rule Bedding above a zav = rishon l'tumah → renders food/drink impure. Samaritan men are treated like zavim, so the bedding beneath them follows the same rule.

🍶 Teruma and Baby Girls: Why the Immersion?

Incident in Ein Bul (Rabbi Yosei): A baby girl was immersed in a mikveh before her mother could eat teruma — the infant's impurity would disqualify the teruma.
↓
Question: Why immerse a baby in Eretz Yisrael? Touch of a niddah disqualifies teruma there. But what about outside Eretz Yisrael?
↓
Mar Zutra's answer: The immersion was for smearing oil of teruma on the baby — since smearing = eating, it would be forbidden if she were tamei.
↓
Verse support: 'And if a man eats of the holy thing through error' (Lev. 22:14) — a baraita derives from this verse that drinking and smearing are equivalent to eating for teruma purposes.
↓
Conclusion: Even outside Eretz Yisrael, smearing teruma oil on a tamei infant is forbidden. This is why the immersion was performed — and it reflects the real (common minority) concern about neonatal menstruation.
