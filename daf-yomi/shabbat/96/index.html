🏺 Earthenware Vessel Purification — The Amoraic Dispute

R. Yosei b. Avin
The hole that purifies an earthenware vessel must be large enough for a pomegranate to pass through — this is the single measure regardless of vessel size.
R. Yosei bar Zavda
A vessel is purified only when the majority of the vessel is broken — the pomegranate measure is not universal.
Ulla
Both positions are correct and complementary: pomegranate measure applies to large vessels; majority-broken standard applies to small vessels. These two Sages in the West each addressed a different case.
Background Earthenware vessels (כלי חרס) become impure only from the inside and cannot be purified by immersion — only by breakage. The dispute concerns how much breakage suffices.

📜 Deriving Carrying Out from the Torah — Step by Step

Gemara's Question: Throwing is a subcategory (toladah) of carrying out — but where is the primary prohibition of carrying out itself written in the Torah?
↓
Proposed Source: Moses commanded the Israelites to stop bringing donations for the Tabernacle (Exodus 36:6) — implying they had been carrying items out of their homes
↓
Objection: How do we know this happened on Shabbat? Perhaps Moses spoke during the week, once the work was simply complete.
↓
Gezerah Shavah: 'And they passed a proclamation' (Tabernacle) ↔ 'You shall pass a shofar blast' (Yom Kippur/Shabbat, Leviticus 25:9) — the shared word 'passed' links the Tabernacle scene to Shabbat
↓
Carrying In: Derived by logical inference (kal va-chomer) — if carrying out of one's home into the camp is forbidden, carrying into one's home from the public domain is certainly forbidden
↓
Conclusion: The Tabernacle donations scene on Shabbat is the Torah source for the prohibition of carrying out (הוצאה)

⚖️ Av Melacha vs. Toladah — Why the Distinction Matters

אָב — Primary Category

  • Carrying OUT (הוצאה) is an Av
  • Carrying IN (הכנסה) is also an Av — a separate primary
  • Performing two different Avot in one act of unawareness → two sin-offerings
  • Derived directly from Tabernacle labors

תּוֹלָדָה — Subcategory

  • Throwing (זריקה) is a Toladah of carrying out
  • Passing (מסירה) is a Toladah of carrying out
  • Toladah + Toladah of different Avot → two sin-offerings
  • Av + its own Toladah → one sin-offering only
  • R. Eliezer: even Av + its own Toladah = two offerings

🪵 Who Was the Wood Gatherer?

Numbers 15:32 — וַיִּמְצְאוּ אִישׁ מְקֹשֵׁשׁ עֵצִים בְּיוֹם הַשַּׁבָּת
Numbers 15:32–36 Rabbi Akiva identified the unnamed man found gathering wood on Shabbat as Zelophehad, based on juxtaposition with his daughters' story in Numbers. Rabbi Yehuda ben Beteira rebuked him: 'In either case you will be judged — if you are right, the Torah concealed his identity and you revealed it; if wrong, you have slandered a righteous man.' Rav Yehuda citing Shmuel: The wood gatherer carried four cubits in the public domain and was executed for it — making this verse the Torah's own source for that prohibition. Practical ramification: A hidden scroll cited by Rav listed labors carrying the death penalty — the wood gatherer's specific act clarifies which prohibited labor on that list applies.
