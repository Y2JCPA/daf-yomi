QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Aḥa, in Shabbat disputes involving Rabbi Shimon, when does halakha NOT follow Rabbi Shimon?
A. Only when Rabbi Shimon is more stringent than Rabbi Yehuda
B. In all cases of muktzeh as a category
C. Only for muktzeh machmat issur (set aside due to prohibition)
D. Never — halakha always follows Rabbi Shimon on Shabbat
Correct: B — Rav Aḥa holds that halakha follows Rabbi Shimon in all Shabbat disputes except for the entire category of muktzeh, where we rule like Rabbi Yehuda.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Ravina's more lenient position regarding muktzeh?
A. Halakha follows Rabbi Yehuda in all muktzeh cases
B. Halakha follows Rabbi Shimon except in all muktzeh cases
C. Halakha follows Rabbi Shimon even in muktzeh, except for muktzeh machmat issur
D. There is no difference between Rabbi Shimon and Rabbi Yehuda on muktzeh
Correct: C — Ravina rules like Rabbi Shimon even on ordinary muktzeh. Only the strictest category — muktzeh machmat issur (e.g., a candle, money) — follows Rabbi Yehuda.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Zutei of Rav Pappa's school, when is vow nullification permitted on Shabbat?
A. Never — vow nullification constitutes forbidden labor on Shabbat
B. Always, without any restriction
C. Only for the purpose of Shabbat (to enable Shabbat observance)
D. Only if the vow was originally made on Shabbat
Correct: C — Zutei explicitly teaches: "One may nullify vows on Shabbat for the purpose of Shabbat" — the qualifying phrase implies it is permitted specifically when necessary for Shabbat observance.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What three practical rulings were derived from the story of the alleyway and the cracked roofing?
A. One may cook, carry, and kindle fire for purposes of ritual purity
B. One may seal a window, measure, and tie a knot on Shabbat
C. One may build, demolish, and transfer objects for purity purposes
D. One may write, erase, and extinguish for ritual purity needs
Correct: B — The mishna concludes: "From their statements and their actions, we derived that one may seal a window, measure, and tie a knot on Shabbat" — all three permitted for ritual purity needs.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why was the cracked roofing in the alleyway story a halakhic concern?
A. It was about to fall and posed a danger to life on Shabbat
B. If intact it would constitute a tent spreading corpse-impurity into both houses via the windows
C. The roofing was made of non-kosher materials and required immediate removal
D. Building regulations forbade cracked roofing above an alleyway
Correct: B — A cracked roofing (not fully intact) does not create a halakhic 'tent' over the corpse. Had it been intact, it would have spread corpse-impurity into both adjacent houses through their windows, requiring urgent remediation.
