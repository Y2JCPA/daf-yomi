QUESTION 1 OF 5
On Shabbat, which phrase is permitted when asking a neighbor for wine or oil, and which is forbidden?
A. 'Loan me' (הלויני) is permitted; 'let me borrow' (השאילני) is forbidden
B. 'Let me borrow' (השאילני) is permitted; 'loan me' (הלויני) is forbidden
C. Both phrases are permitted since no money changes hands
D. Neither phrase is permitted — all borrowing is forbidden on Shabbat
Correct: B — The Mishna permits borrowing on Shabbat but forbids using loan language (הלויני), because it implies a formal monetary transaction that may lead to writing. 'Let me borrow' (השאילני) signals a neighborly exchange without that implication.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is permitted when treating a dislocated limb on Shabbat?
A. Vigorous therapeutic exercise of the limb in cold water
B. Applying a bandage or splint to immobilize the joint
C. Rinsing the limb normally — if healing results, it is permitted
D. All treatment is forbidden until after Shabbat
Correct: C — The Mishna forbids deliberate therapeutic movement of a dislocated limb in water, but permits normal rinsing. Incidental healing that results from permitted rinsing is allowed, since the person did not intend the therapeutic act.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Abaye rule that the Sages remained silent when they saw people clapping and dancing on Festivals?
A. Clapping and dancing are in fact permitted on Festivals according to most opinions
B. The Sages only rebuke violations of Torah law, not rabbinic prohibitions
C. It is better they sin unknowingly than presumptuously after being rebuked
D. The Sages had not yet formally prohibited these activities at that time
Correct: C — Abaye cites the principle: 'Leave Israel alone — it is better they sin unknowingly than presumptuously.' When rebuke will not be heeded, rebuking only turns an unwitting sin into a deliberate one. The Gemara clarifies this applies to Torah and rabbinic prohibitions alike.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rav Idi bar Avin's challenge to Rav Yosef's position that Festival loans are not court-enforceable?
A. The Mishna explicitly says Festival loans must be repaid in court
B. If the loan was never enforceable, what does the Sabbatical Year (shemitta) cancel?
C. Taking collateral on a Festival is itself a separate prohibition
D. Rabbi Yochanan's ruling contradicts an unattributed Mishna, which is authoritative
Correct: B — Rav Idi bar Avin challenges Rav Yosef from a Mishna about shemitta canceling debts. If a Festival loan was never legally enforceable to begin with, there is nothing for shemitta to cancel — yet the Mishna implies it does cancel something, suggesting the loan was legally valid.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the final Mishna on Daf 148, how may one count guests or appetizers on Shabbat?
A. It is entirely forbidden to count or enumerate anything on Shabbat
B. One may count from memory only, not from a written list
C. One may read from a written list only if it was prepared before Shabbat
D. One may draw lots to determine the allocation but may not count directly
Correct: B — The Mishna permits counting guests or appetizers on Shabbat from memory, but forbids reading from a written list. Reading a list resembles weekday business activity and may lead one to write, which is why memory-based counting is the only permitted method.
