🪨 Rabbi Meir vs. Rabbis: Does Chazakah of Impurity Ever Break?

Rabbi Meir
Chazakah of impurity is permanent: Any item with a presumptive status of ritual impurity forever remains impure — even if you examine all three piles of stones and find them pure. The original presumption stands.
Rabbis
Search until bedrock: Dig until you reach virgin soil. If no impurity is found, presume an animal dragged it away. A raven could have taken the corpse fragment — rational explanation breaks the chazakah.
Rabbi Meir
Then why do you agree with me about the three women? If all three examine themselves and are found pure, they should be pure by your logic — yet you concede all three remain impure!
Rabbis
The cases differ: a raven can carry away a piece of corpse, but blood cannot simply vanish once issued. There is no rational mechanism by which the blood disappeared, so no alternative explanation exists to break the chazakah.
Core Distinction Rabbi Meir: chazakah of impurity never lifted regardless. Rabbis: chazakah breaks only when there is a rational alternative explanation for the impurity's disappearance (e.g., animal removed corpse fragment).

🪨 Piles of Stones — Chazakah Dispute with Supporting Cases

Rabbi Meir
An olive-bulk of corpse was lost among three stone piles. Examine two and find them pure — the third is still impure. Examine all three and find them pure — all three remain impure. Chazakah of impurity can never be lifted.
Rabbis
Dig beneath each pile until you reach bedrock or virgin soil. If nothing is found, presume an animal removed the impurity. The piles may be declared pure. Sycamore in Kefar Sava · Cave in Shiḥin · Bedrock in Beit Ḥoron — all such cases were resolved this way.
Scriptural Anchor The pit examined in Beit Ḥoron was identified as the pit Ishmael ben Nethaniah filled with corpses (Jeremiah 41). Scripture attributes those deaths to Gedaliah — because he failed to heed warnings of the plot against him.

⚠️ Gedaliah's Failure & Moses' Fear of Og

Gedaliah refused to heed Johanan's warning about Ishmael's assassination plot, treating it as forbidden lashon hara (malicious speech). He was killed — and Scripture assigns the deaths to him for his negligence.
↓
Rava rules: While one may not fully act on lashon hara as established fact, one must be concerned (חושש) and take protective precautions. Gedaliah sinned by ignoring even basic caution.
↓
Before battle with Og: God told Moses 'Do not fear him' — implying Moses already feared. Why? Moses feared Og's protective merit from his youth.
↓
R. Yoḥanan (citing R. Shimon ben Yoḥai): Og told Abraham that Lot was captured (Genesis 14:13), hoping Abraham would die in battle and Og could take Sarah. Yet even this self-serving act generated powerful merit for Og — showing how potent any mitzvah is.
↓
God's reassurance to Moses: Abraham's merit — in whose merit Og benefited — had already been used up by this time. Moses could therefore proceed without fear.

👗 Lost Items in Garments — Examination Methods

Blood Stain Lost

  • Garment potentially renders woman impure
  • Examine the garment section by section
  • Each portion checked separately
  • Remaining stain will be discovered
  • Seven substances used to test authenticity

Seminal Emission Lost

  • New garment: pierce every part with a needle
  • Old garment: immerse in water
  • Semen dissolves; wet spot reveals location
  • Location identified and garment examined
  • Garment is ritually impure until confirmed clean

Sha'atnez (Diverse Kinds) Lost

  • Wool garment into which a linen thread fell
  • Dye the garment — wool and linen absorb differently
  • Linen thread becomes visible by color contrast
  • Thread of flax lost from a wool cloak: if fell out completely, garment permitted
  • שע"טנ"ז = שוע טווי ונוז (combed, spun, woven)
