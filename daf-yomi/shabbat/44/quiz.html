QUESTION 1 OF 5
From which verse does the Gemara derive that a one-day-old baby girl can become a niddah?
A. 'And if a woman has an issue, and her issue in her flesh is blood' (Leviticus 15:19)
B. 'She shall be impure seven days' (Leviticus 12:2)
C. 'Any man from the house of Israel' (Leviticus 17:3)
D. 'And one of them dies, and he has no child' (Deuteronomy 25:5)
Correct: A — The baraita expounds 'if a woman has an issue' — the general term 'woman' (ishah) includes even a one-day-old girl, teaching that the laws of niddah apply from the very first day of life.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rav Sheshet explain is the novel teaching when the mishna specifies that a one-day-old boy disqualifies his mother from teruma?
A. That even a fetus in the womb disqualifies the mother
B. That the baby also disqualifies his father's Canaanite slaves from eating teruma
C. That the mother must wait a full year before regaining teruma rights
D. That the baby enables the mother to eat from priestly tithes (maaser)
Correct: B — Rav Sheshet explains that the mishna's emphasis on 'one-day-old baby' (rather than 'fetus') teaches an additional ruling: since the child has a share in his father's inheritance, he disqualifies even the father's Canaanite slaves from eating teruma.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Gemara, what does a one-day-old baby boy inherit so that he can bequeath it to his paternal brothers?
A. His father's entire estate
B. His mother's property, if she died on the day he was born
C. His maternal grandfather's priestly gifts
D. The shares his father owed him from future inheritance
Correct: B — The mishna teaches that the baby inherits his mother's estate (if she died on the day of his birth), enabling him to bequeath it to his paternal half-brothers — who are not the mother's heirs and would not otherwise receive it.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the dispute between Rabbi Meir and the Rabbis regarding the age at which intercourse with a girl constitutes full halakhic intercourse?
A. Rabbi Meir says two years and one day; the Rabbis say three years
B. Rabbi Meir says exactly three years; the Rabbis say three years and one day
C. Rabbi Meir says the law applies from puberty; the Rabbis say from three years
D. They agree on the age but dispute whether it applies to betrothal or only yibum
Correct: B — Rabbi Meir holds that exactly three years old is sufficient, applying the principle that thirty days in a year are equivalent to a full year. The Rabbis require the complete three years plus one additional day before the law applies.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Under three years of age, the Gemara says intercourse with a girl is 'like a finger placed in an eye.' What does this analogy mean halakhically?
A. It causes pain but does not create any legal bond
B. Just as an eye that is pressed returns to normal, the hymen regenerates and the intercourse has no lasting legal effect
C. It is forbidden but carries no punishment
D. It creates a rabbinic-level betrothal but not a Torah-level one
Correct: B — The Gemara explains: just as pressing a finger into an eye causes the eye to tear up but then return to normal, so too intercourse with a girl under three causes no permanent physical change — the hymen regenerates — and therefore has no legal effect for betrothal, yibum, or impurity.
