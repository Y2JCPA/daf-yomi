Shabbat 5 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
When the Gemara can interpret a text either leniently or strictly with equal justification, which does it prefer?
A. Always the strict interpretation to be safe
B. The lenient interpretation
C. Whichever matches the Babylonian over the Jerusalem Talmud
D. The interpretation that matches the minority opinion
Correct: B — The Gemara explicitly states: since there is a way to read the text as a leniency, we prefer that reading. This governs why the mishna is interpreted per Rabbi Dosa (lenient) rather than the stringent view.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Shmuel initially rule that intercourse while using examination cloths does NOT count as a valid examination?
A. The cloths become ritually impure once wet
B. The woman is excited and hurried (מהומה), lacking the deliberateness required
C. Examination must be performed standing, not during intercourse
D. Only a Sage can certify the validity of an examination cloth
Correct: B — Rav Ketina explains Shmuel's view: the woman is in a state of excitement (מהומה) and haste, and therefore the cloth inspection lacks the deliberateness of a proper examination.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rabbi Ḥanina ben Antigonus rule about blood stains of women whose 'time is sufficient' (e.g., pregnant women)?
A. Blood stains are completely disregarded for such women
B. Blood stains are always retroactively impure regardless of cycle
C. Blood stains follow the same ruling as their menstrual flow — impure from discovery only, not retroactively
D. Blood stains require a 24-hour waiting period before a ruling
Correct: C — Rabbi Ḥanina ben Antigonus holds that for women in the 'time is sufficient' category, their blood stains share the ruling of their menstrual sighting — they cause impurity only from the moment of discovery, not retroactively.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan and Rav Hoshaya's qualification, when is uncertain impurity 'by the hand of man' treated as pure?
A. When the person involved is a Torah scholar
B. When the object in question is a vessel rather than a person
C. When the doubtful contact occurred in a public domain
D. When the uncertainty arose at night rather than by day
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan rules uncertain impurity by human action is treated as impure, but Rav Hoshaya limits this to the private domain (רשות היחיד). In a public domain (רשות הרבים), the standard leniency for doubtful impurity applies.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the basis of Rava's a fortiori (קל וחומר) argument that the bed of a niddah receives full retroactive impurity?
A. If a person becomes fully impure, surely furniture must too
B. If a sealed earthenware vessel — which resists corpse impurity — still becomes retroactively impure from a niddah, certainly a bed must receive full impurity
C. If light impurity can defile a person, heavy impurity must defile vessels
D. If the after-examination cloth is valid, the bed must also be treated strictly
Correct: B — Rava reasons: a sealed earthenware vessel is protected from impurity even inside a tent with a corpse, yet it still becomes retroactively impure from a niddah. A bed and chair have no such protection, so they certainly receive full — not light — retroactive impurity.
