⚖️ Interpreting the Mishna: Leniency Prevails

Question
Why read the mishna per Rabbi Dosa (leniently) and the baraita per everyone — why not reverse them?
Gemara
Since there is a way to interpret the text as a leniency, we always prefer that reading — and a lenient reading that also aligns with the majority opinion is doubly preferred.
Principle
When two equally valid readings exist, the Gemara favors whichever is more lenient. This governs the entire opening discussion of the daf.
📌 Core Rule Blood discovered at her fixed time (וסת) → impure only from that moment. Blood discovered not at fixed time → retroactively impure for 24 hours.

👩 Categories of Women: Retroactive Impurity Rules

Woman with Fixed Cycle (וסת)

  • "Her time is sufficient" — דיה שעתה
  • Impurity begins only at the moment of discovery
  • No retroactive impurity (למפרע)
  • Items she handled beforehand remain pure
  • Blood stains: follow same rule as her menstrual flow

Woman without Fixed Cycle

  • Impure retroactively for up to 24-hour period
  • Or retroactively from last examination — whichever is shorter
  • Items she handled are retroactively impure
  • Blood stains also cause retroactive impurity
  • Stricter standard throughout

Pregnant / Elderly / Nursing

  • Sages declared: their time is sufficient
  • Rabbi Ḥanina ben Antigonus: their blood stains share the ruling of menstrual flow
  • No retroactive impurity
  • Special Rabbinic category — treated like women with וסת

🔍 Examination Cloths (עדים) During Intercourse

Woman uses examination cloths (עדים) during intercourse
↓
Shmuel's initial view
She is excited and hurried (מהומה) — not a deliberate examination; window is NOT reduced
Mishna's ruling
Act has status of an examination — retroactive window is reduced from 24 hrs to exam-to-exam interval
↓
Gemara reconciles Shmuel: even if she is excited, blood on cloth is plainly visible — the examination is valid
↓
Two cloths required — one before intercourse, one after. Both are obligatory; after-intercourse cloth is valid lest a drop of blood be covered by semen

⚖️ Rabbi Yoḥanan: Uncertain Impurity by the Hand of Man

ספק טומאה הבאה בידי אדם — טמא
הלכה Rabbi Yoḥanan rules: In a case of uncertain ritual impurity (ספק טומאה) that arises through the hand of man (בידי אדם) — the owner must consult a Sage, i.e., it is treated as ritually impure.

Rationale: When a person's deliberate action caused the doubtful contact, we rule strictly — unlike impurity that arises on its own.

Rav Hoshaya's limit: This applies only in a private domain (רשות היחיד). In a public domain (רשות הרבים), even hand-caused uncertain impurity resolves as pure.

🛏️ Ze'eiri vs. Rava: What Level of Impurity for Bed and Chair?

Ze'eiri
During the retroactive 24-hour period, the niddah's bed and chair become impure only at the lighter level (טומאה קלה) — they defile people but do not defile vessels.
Avimi b. Bei Ḥozai
The baraita I brought: her bed and chair during the retroactive period are impure at the lighter level, supporting Ze'eiri's position.
Rava
A fortiori (קל וחומר): A sealed earthenware vessel — which resists impurity even from a corpse in a tent — still becomes retroactively impure from a niddah. How much more must a bed and chair receive full impurity!
Resolution
The baraita does not mean light impurity. It means the bed becomes a rishon l'tumah (first degree), capable of transmitting impurity further — Rava's inference stands.
📌 Result Rava's קל וחומר prevails: retroactive impurity of bed and chair is full-strength, not a lighter grade.
