QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, does circumcision of an uncertain foreskin (e.g., flesh that resembles a foreskin) override Shabbat?
A. Yes — all circumcisions override Shabbat regardless of certainty
B. No — only a definite foreskin overrides Shabbat; doubt defers to weekday
C. Yes — but only if two rabbinic authorities confirm the uncertainty
D. Only if the child's life is at risk
Correct: B — The Gemara derives from the verse's emphasis on 'his foreskin' that only a certain, obligatory foreskin overrides Shabbat. An uncertain case — flesh resembling a foreskin that may not require removal — does not override Shabbat and must wait for a weekday.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Rav and Shmuel regarding a child born already circumcised?
A. Rav holds no procedure is needed; Shmuel requires full circumcision
B. Rav permits hatafat dam brit on Shabbat; Shmuel restricts it to weekdays only
C. Rav says wait until the child is 30 days old; Shmuel says act immediately
D. Both agree hatafat dam brit may be done on Shabbat if the 8th day falls on Shabbat
Correct: B — Both Rav and Shmuel agree that hatafat dam brit (drawing covenantal blood) is required for a born-circumcised child. Their dispute is about timing: Rav holds it may be performed on Shabbat when the 8th day is Shabbat; Shmuel holds it must wait for a weekday and does not override Shabbat.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why may a mother nurse her eight-month baby on Shabbat even though the baby is considered muktze?
A. Because nursing is a rabbinic obligation that always overrides Shabbat
B. Because the baby is not truly muktze — the rule only applies to stillborns
C. Because the mother faces danger to her own health if she does not nurse
D. Because the baby's circumcision is being postponed and nursing compensates
Correct: C — Although the eight-month baby is treated as muktze (like a stone), the mother may bend over to nurse him due to danger to herself — if she does not nurse, her accumulated milk poses a risk to her own life, invoking the principle of pikuach nefesh for the mother.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Asi's principle, what is the critical factor that determines whether a child is circumcised on the eighth day with Shabbat override?
A. Whether the child was born at full nine-month term
B. Whether the birth renders the mother ritually impure (tumah leidah)
C. Whether the father is present and consents to the circumcision
D. Whether the birth occurred before or after matan Torah
Correct: B — Rabbi Asi holds that any child whose birth renders his mother ritually impure (tumah leidah) is circumcised on the eighth day with Shabbat override. A child whose birth does not create tumah leidah — such as one born by caesarean section — requires circumcision but not on Shabbat. Abaye challenges this with pre-Sinai births, but Rabbi Asi replies that the halakha was introduced only at Sinai.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In Rava's analysis of home-born slave circumcision, how can the case of a 'home-born child circumcised at one day' arise according to Rav Ḥama's view?
A. If a Jew purchases an infant slave who was born one day ago
B. If a Jew purchased a pregnant maidservant, she immersed before giving birth, and the child is then born
C. If the slave's mother was born in the owner's household but she herself was purchased
D. If the owner immersed the maidservant on the same day as the birth
Correct: B — According to Rav Ḥama, the critical factor is when the mother immersed relative to birth. If the owner purchased a pregnant maidservant and had her immerse before she gave birth, then when the child is born he enters the world already with Jewish slave status — like a purchased slave who is circumcised immediately (treated as 'one day'). This is distinct from the case where conception occurred in the owner's possession.
