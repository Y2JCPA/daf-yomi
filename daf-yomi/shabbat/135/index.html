✂️ Does This Circumcision Override Shabbat?

Tanna Kamma
A definite foreskin overrides Shabbat. An uncertain foreskin does NOT — e.g., flesh that resembles a foreskin but may not require removal.
Rabbi Yehuda
A hermaphrodite's (androgynos) circumcision overrides Shabbat and carries karet if neglected. He derives: 'his definite foreskin' includes the androgynos.
Beit Hillel
One born already circumcised requires hatafat dam brit — drawing covenantal blood. Beit Shammai does not dispute this point.
Beit Shammai
Agreed: hatafat dam brit is required for the born-circumcised child. Rabbi Shimon ben Elazar: this was never the subject of their dispute at all.
Core Rule Only a certain, obligatory circumcision overrides Shabbat. Cases of doubt — uncertain foreskin, born circumcised, caesarean — defer to weekday performance.

🩸 Born Circumcised: When to Perform Hatafat Dam Brit?

Rav
The halakha follows the first tanna's interpretation: one born circumcised requires hatafat dam brit, and if the 8th day falls on Shabbat, it may be performed on Shabbat.
Shmuel
Hatafat dam brit is performed during the week only. Did I say to desecrate Shabbat? Certainly not — this does not override Shabbat.
Rav Adda bar Ahava
In a real case: he inquired after 13 circumcisors on Shabbat who all refused — until one performed hatafat dam brit, acting in accordance with Rav.
Rav Naḥman
That mohel violated Shmuel's ruling! Shmuel only authorized a weekday dripping. The practical halakha: do not desecrate Shabbat for this procedure.
Rav Yosef's Proof Baraita: Rabbi Eliezer HaKappar records that Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel agree a born-circumcised child needs hatafat dam brit — confirming the requirement, even if Shabbat timing is disputed.

👶 The Eight-Month Baby — A Stone on Shabbat

יֶלֶד שְׁמוֹנָה — הֲרֵי הוּא כְּאֶבֶן | An eight-month child is like a stone
Baraita Ruling (Amud A:5) A child born after only eight months of gestation is presumed non-viable — treated like a stone (muktze) and may not be moved on Shabbat.

Exception: His mother may bend over and nurse him due to pikuach nefesh — danger to her own health if milk is not expressed.

Circumcision: Such a child is not circumcised on Shabbat. A nine-month premature baby showing signs of prematurity also generates halakhic doubt.

Thirty-day rule (Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel): Once a child survives 30 days, he is no longer suspected of being a stillborn — mourning obligations and firstborn-redemption apply from that point.

📐 Rabbi Asi's Principle: Tumah Determines Circumcision Timing

Circumcised at 8 Days (Shabbat Override Applies)

  • Birth renders mother ritually impure (tumah leidah — Lev. 12)
  • Standard natural delivery
  • Classic case of milah on the 8th day
  • Even Shabbat is overridden

Not Circumcised on Shabbat (No Override)

  • Birth does NOT render mother impure
  • Caesarean section (yotzei dofen)
  • Born-circumcised child's hatafat dam brit
  • Circumcision still required — but on weekday

Challenges to Rabbi Asi's Rule

  • Abaye: Pre-Sinai males were circumcised (Avraham, Yitzchak) — yet tumah/tahara laws didn't exist yet!
  • Rabbi Asi's reply: Once Torah was given, the halakha was introduced — no proof from pre-Sinai practice
  • Gemara challenge: Caesarean child — mother not impure, yet Rav Huna disputes whether he needs circumcision at all
  • Parallel tannaitic dispute underlies the amoraic debate
