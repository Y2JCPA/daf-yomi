📦 Three Types of Chests — Susceptibility to Ritual Impurity

Opens from the Side

  • Flat top surface exposed
  • One can sit or lie on it
  • Designated for sitting/lying
  • ✅ Becomes ritually impure
  • Susceptible to midras impurity

Opens from Top — With Lip

  • Rim/lip surrounds the opening
  • Lip creates a stable seat surface
  • Can be sat upon without tipping
  • ✅ Becomes ritually impure
  • Lip = functional seating

Opens from Top — No Lip

  • No rim; opening is flush
  • Cannot sit stably on it
  • Not designated for sitting
  • ❌ Does NOT become impure
  • Lack of seating function = exempt

⚔️ Rabbi Ila vs. Rabbi Ḥanina — A Sharp Exchange

Rabbi Ila
May the all-Merciful save us from this opinion! The a fortiori inference leads to an absurdity — small vessels would become more susceptible to impurity than large ones!
Rabbi Ḥanina
On the contrary — may the all-Merciful save us from your opinion! The inference is sound and the ruling stands as stated.
Gemara
The paradox explained: Small vessels are exempt from midras because a zav cannot insert his finger into their airspace. But the a fortiori would then conclude that small vessels are even more stringent — a counterintuitive result.
Kal Va-Ḥomer Gone Wrong A valid-seeming a fortiori inference produces a result that seems to increase stringency on small vessels beyond large ones. Rabbi Ila found this legally alarming — Rabbi Ḥanina disagreed with his concern.

📜 Three Independent Proofs: Earthenware Pure from Treading Impurity

SageSource VerseMethodLogic
ḤizkiyaLev. 15:5 — 'whoever touches his bed shall wash clothes and bathe in water'Comparison (bed to zav)The bed is juxtaposed to the zav himself. Just as a zav does not make himself impure, earthenware does not contract midras impurity.
School of R. YishmaelLev. 15:26 — 'every bed on which she lies… shall be like the bed of her menstrual period'Juxtaposition (hekesh)Her bed is compared to herself. Just as the woman does not transmit midras impurity to herself, earthenware is likewise exempt.
RavaNum. 19:15 — 'any open vessel without a sealed cover becomes impure'Inference from positive (מכלל הן)Earthenware becomes impure only through its inner airspace. Treading is external contact — irrelevant to earthenware's mode of impurity.

🌱 Five Species in a Six-by-Six Handbreadth Garden Bed

The Mishna's Rule: A garden bed measuring six by six handbreadths (טֶרֶסִין שֵׁשׁ עַל שֵׁשׁ) may have five different species planted in it without violating Kilayim (כִּלְאַיִם — prohibited seed mixtures).
↓
The Layout: One species in each of the four corners, and one species in the center of the bed — five total. Each is visually separated from the others by position.
↓
Rav Yehuda's Derivation: From Isaiah 61:11 — 'For as the earth brings forth its growth (צְמָחֶיהָ), and as a garden causes its seeds (זְרוּעֶיהָ) to grow' — plural forms implying multiple varieties simultaneously.
↓
Word Count in the Verse: The verse contains five distinct terms for growth/planting: 'earth brings forth,' 'its growth,' 'garden causes to grow,' 'its seeds,' 'before all nations' — one allusion per species.
↓
Why This Matters: Normally any mixing of species is a Torah prohibition. The precise geometric arrangement in this small space legally defines each species as occupying its own distinct plot.

🏺 Deriving Earthenware's Purity from Treading — Rava's Path

Question: From where do we derive that earthenware (כְּלִי חֶרֶס) is pure from tumaat midras?
↓
Rava: Look at Numbers 19:15 — 'any open vessel without a sealed cover becomes impure'
↓
Positive implication
Open earthenware with an unsealed cover becomes impure through its airspace (inside)
Negative implication
Impurity enters only through the interior airspace — external pressure/treading is not the vessel's mode of contracting impurity
↓
✅ Conclusion: Midras (treading from outside) cannot defile earthenware — its impurity path is internal only. Three sages, three proofs, one ruling.
