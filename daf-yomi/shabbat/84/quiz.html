Shabbat 84 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara's resolution of Ḥananya's ruling, what is the status of a boat drawn by oxen regarding ritual impurity?
A. It cannot become impure since oxen-drawing is not considered 'carrying'
B. It becomes impure only for the full vessels aboard, not the empty ones
C. It can become ritually impure — oxen-drawn transport counts as carrying
D. The question is left unresolved (teku)
Correct: C — The Gemara cites a Mishna as proof that a boat can become ritually impure, and concludes that even carrying by oxen constitutes 'carrying' for purposes of impurity law.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Which of the three chest types does NOT become ritually impure?
A. A chest that opens from the side
B. A chest that opens from the top and has a lip (rim) around the opening
C. A chest that opens from the top without any lip or rim
D. All three chests become impure equally
Correct: C — A chest with a top opening but no lip cannot be sat upon stably and is therefore not 'designated for sitting.' Without this designation it is not susceptible to midras impurity. The other two can function as seats.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What concerned Rabbi Ila when he said 'May the all-Merciful save us from this opinion'?
A. He feared the ruling would prohibit earthenware entirely
B. An a fortiori inference seemed to make small vessels more susceptible to impurity than large ones — a paradoxical result
C. He believed Rabbi Ḥanina had misquoted the source verse
D. He objected to deriving impurity laws from the laws of a zav
Correct: B — The a fortiori reasoning proceeded: small vessels are exempt from midras because a zav cannot insert his finger into their airspace — yet if this reasoning is reversed, it yields a counterintuitive stringency for small vessels. This alarmed Rabbi Ila.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rava derive that earthenware is pure from treading impurity?
A. From the verse comparing a menstruant's bed to herself (Lev. 15:26)
B. From Numbers 19:15 — earthenware becomes impure only through its airspace, not external contact
C. From the verse requiring washing after touching a zav's bed (Lev. 15:5)
D. From a halacha l'Moshe miSinai (oral tradition from Sinai)
Correct: B — Rava derives from Numbers 19:15 that earthenware contracts impurity only when it enters through the inner airspace. Since treading (midras) is external pressure from outside, it is irrelevant to earthenware's mode of becoming impure.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How many species may be planted in a six-by-six handbreadth garden bed without violating Kilayim, and what is the arrangement?
A. Three species — one on each of three sides
B. Four species — one in each corner only
C. Five species — one in each corner and one in the center
D. Six species — one in each corner and one on each of two sides
Correct: C — The Mishna permits five species: one in each of the four corners and one in the center of the bed. Rav Yehuda derives this number from the five distinct growth-related terms in Isaiah 61:11.
