⚖️ Rav vs. Shmuel: Does Missing a Veset Exam Cause Retroactive Impurity?

Case
A woman with a fixed menstrual cycle (וסת) — the projected day arrives and she did not examine herself, then she later examined and found blood.
Rav
If she later found blood: items she touched are retroactively impure. But if she examined later and found no blood, she is pure.
Shmuel
She is impure in both cases — whether she later found blood or not. Concern for veset applies broadly and unconditionally.
Rabbi Zeira
Even Rav may hold veset is Torah law. His leniency when no blood is found later is because it is a case of ספק ספקא — a double doubt.
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
No — Rav and Shmuel genuinely disagree: Shmuel holds veset is Torah law; Rav holds it is Rabbinic, so missing an exam without later finding blood leaves her pure.
Core Question Is the concern for impurity at the projected time of a woman's period (וסת) a Torah law or a Rabbinic enactment? Three Amoraim — Rabbi Zeira, Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak, and Rav Sheshet — each propose a different framing of the Rav/Shmuel dispute.

🕯️ Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel — Examination Cloths During Intercourse

Beit Shammai
A woman must examine with two cloths — one before and one after each and every act of intercourse throughout the night.
Beit Hillel
She need only examine before the first act and after the final act of the night — not between each individual act.
Beit Shammai
According to you: if blood appears during the night she will not know from which act it came — creating retroactive impurity uncertainty for her husband.
Beit Hillel
Even per your view: perhaps while the blood was still present, it was squashed and disappeared. No examination fully eliminates doubt in any case.
Rabbi Yehoshua
I see the statement of Beit Shammai as correct here. Though my students complained it was a heavy burden — the stringency is justified.
Rava's Practical Ruling Even a pious person may engage in intercourse twice without an examination in between. The baraita requiring examination applies only to women who handle ritually pure items — not for regular marital intercourse.

🔍 Three Amoraim on the Source of Veset Obligation

Rabbi Zeira

  • All agree: veset concern is Torah law
  • Rav's leniency stems from a special double-doubt scenario
  • No fundamental disagreement about the law's source

Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak

  • Rav and Shmuel truly disagree about veset itself
  • Shmuel: Torah law — strict in all cases
  • Rav: Rabbinic — pure if no blood found later

Rav Sheshet

  • Mirrors Tannaitic dispute: Rabbi Eliezer vs. Rabbi Yehoshua
  • R. Eliezer: must exam even after elapsed time → Torah view
  • R. Yehoshua: examine now; if clean, pure retroactively → Rabbinic view

👼 The Angel at Conception — Rabbi Ḥanina bar Pappa

A drop destined to become a human being is presented before the Holy One, Blessed be He
↓
The angel asks: "What shall this drop become? Strong or weak? Wise or foolish? Wealthy or poor?"
↓
The angel does NOT ask: "Will this person be wicked or righteous?" — moral character is not predetermined
↓
Rabbi Ḥanina's principle: הכל בידי שמים חוץ מיראת שמים — Everything is in the hand of Heaven except fear of Heaven
↓
Context: Reish Lakish's reading of Job 3:3 — "the night on which it was said: Conceived is a man-child" — refers to this angelic declaration at conception
