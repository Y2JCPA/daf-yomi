🏠 Does an Afterbirth in the House Create Impurity?

Rabbi Meir
The house is impure — we assume the afterbirth contained an offspring that has since disintegrated. The presumption of an offspring creates corpse-level (אוהל) impurity.
Rabbi Shimon
The house is pure — any offspring within the afterbirth is nullified by the blood of childbirth. What has no independent existence cannot convey impurity.
Rabbis (to Rabbi Meir)
You admit the outer room is pure when the afterbirth is moved there. But if no offspring exists there, then no offspring existed in the inner room either — there is nothing to create impurity anywhere!
Rabbi Meir
The outer room is pure only because the offspring would have disintegrated during the move. While the afterbirth rested in the inner room, the offspring was still present.
Bottom Line Rabbi Shimon (and ultimately the Rabbis) hold: an afterbirth alone cannot establish the certainty of an offspring — and without a confirmed offspring, there is no corpse-level impurity.

📅 How Long After a Birth Can an Afterbirth Be Attributed to It?

Shmuel (via Rabba bar Sheila): Up to 10 days — an afterbirth discharged within 10 days of a birth is attributed to that birth.
↓
Rabbi Yoḥanan (via Rabba bar bar Ḥana): Up to 23 days — there was a case where the Sages attributed an afterbirth to a birth 23 days earlier.
↓
Rabbi Yitzḥak (via Rav Aḥa): Up to 33 days — one twin remained in the womb 33 days after the other was born.
↓
Rabbi Menaḥem of She'arim / Beit She'arim: Up to 3 months — one twin remained in the womb a full 3 months after the other was born; both are considered full-term.
↓
Gemara's Resolution: The 33-day figure applies to afterbirth attribution; the 23-day figure applies to twin birth intervals. The two rulings were reported in reverse and required correction.

👶 Twins: How Far Apart Can Their Births Be?

The Problem

  • Can one twin remain in the womb for months after the other is born?
  • The Gemara asks: a woman cannot become pregnant while already pregnant!
  • Abaye resolves: both were conceived simultaneously from one drop of semen — they are not sequential pregnancies

Reported Cases

  • 23 days apart — case cited by Rabbi Yoḥanan (corrected attribution)
  • 33 days apart — case cited by Rav Aḥa (corrected attribution)
  • 3 months apart — Rabbi Menaḥem's case; both twins are sound (שלמים)

Halakhic Implication

  • The second birth does not require a new waiting period — it is part of the same pregnancy
  • The afterbirth of the first birth can be attributed back to a much earlier delivery
  • Validates the principle: one conception, sequential deliveries

💀 When Does a Ladleful of Corpse Dust Make a House Impure?

A תרווד (ladleful) of dust from a corpse is present in a house
↓
Unmixed — pure corpse dust only
All agree: House is impure — full measure, fully identified
Dirt mixed in
Rabbis: Impure
Rabbi Shimon: Pure — the dirt nullifies the measure
Dust scattered on the floor
Rabbis: Impure
Rabbi Shimon: Pure — scattered dust loses its measured status
↓
Rabba's Principle (Rabbi Shimon's reason): Corpse dust in its final state follows the rule of its initial state — just as a corpse mingled with another is not impure, so too its dust once mingled is pure.

🗣️ Rav Pappa's Lesson — Don't Be Silent Before Your Teacher

לעולם יאמר אדם דבר שבלבו לפני רבו ואל יתבייש — תהילים קיט:מו
A Personal Story Rav Pappa sat behind Rav Beivai before their teacher Rav Hamnuna. He stated what seemed like an obvious explanation of Rabbi Shimon's reasoning — and Rav Beivai and Rav Hamnuna laughed at him.

Yet Rav Pappa insisted: "A person should say a matter even as obvious as this one, and not be silent in the presence of his teacher."

He cites the verse: "I speak of Your testimonies before kings and I am not ashamed" (Psalms 119:46) — the willingness to speak, even at risk of ridicule, is itself a form of Torah devotion.
