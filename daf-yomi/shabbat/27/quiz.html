Shabbat 27 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, how many days after a birth can an afterbirth still be attributed to that birth?
A. 3 days
B. 7 days
C. 10 days
D. 23 days
Correct: C — Shmuel (cited by Rabba bar Sheila) rules that an afterbirth discharged within 10 days may be attributed to the most recent prior birth.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rabbi Shimon's reason for ruling that a house containing an afterbirth is pure?
A. The afterbirth itself does not have the size of an olive
B. The offspring within the afterbirth is nullified by the blood of childbirth
C. The gestational sac always disintegrates before death
D. Corpse impurity requires a confirmed human form
Correct: B — Rav Pappa explains that Rabbi Shimon holds the offspring is nullified by the blood of childbirth, giving it no independent halakhic status — and therefore no power to create impurity.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Abaye resolve the problem that one twin remained in the womb for 3 months after the other was born?
A. The second twin was conceived from a later pregnancy
B. Both twins were conceived at the same time from one drop of semen
C. A woman can become pregnant while already pregnant in rare cases
D. The 3-month figure is a scribal error
Correct: B — Abaye explains: both twins were conceived simultaneously from a single drop of semen — they are not sequential pregnancies but one pregnancy with delayed delivery of the second twin.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rabba's explanation for why Rabbi Shimon rules that a ladleful of corpse dust mixed with dirt renders the house pure?
A. The dirt absorbs the impurity and neutralizes it
B. Rabbi Shimon follows a general rule of leniency in all cases of doubt
C. Corpse dust in its final state follows the rule of its initial state — a corpse mixed with another is pure, so too its dust once mixed
D. The quantity of dust falls below the required minimum once mixed
Correct: C — Rabba teaches: Rabbi Shimon holds that the halakha of corpse dust parallels the halakha of an intact corpse. Just as a corpse mingled with another corpse does not impart its impurity, so too corpse dust once mingled with other dust is pure.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What verse does Rav Pappa cite to justify speaking up even when your teacher might laugh at you?
A. "The Torah is not in heaven" (Deut. 30:12)
B. "I speak of Your testimonies before kings and I am not ashamed" (Psalms 119:46)
C. "Honor your father and your teacher" (Lev. 19:3)
D. "Acquire wisdom, acquire understanding" (Proverbs 4:5)
Correct: B — Rav Pappa cites Psalms 119:46 — speaking Torah openly before anyone, even at risk of embarrassment, is itself a fulfillment of the verse and a form of courage in Torah study.
