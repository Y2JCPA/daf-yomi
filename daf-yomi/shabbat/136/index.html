⚖️ May We Circumcise a Possibly-Stillborn Baby on Shabbat?

Questioner
If this baby may be a stillborn (nefal), how can we circumcise him on Shabbat? Desecrating Shabbat for a non-viable infant would be forbidden!
Rav Adda bar Ahava
Circumcise him either way! If he is viable — the circumcision is valid and obligatory. If he is a stillborn — you have merely cut flesh, and no Shabbat prohibition is transgressed!
Baraita
But the Baraita rules: if uncertain whether born at 7 or 8 months, one does NOT desecrate Shabbat for circumcision, crying out to a doctor, or any other need!
Mar son of Ravina
The Baraita only restricts secondary Shabbat violations — calling a doctor, carrying — but circumcision itself may still be performed. The two rulings do not contradict!
Resolution The baraita prohibits incidental Shabbat desecrations (e.g., summoning a physician), but circumcision itself — which carries its own biblical permission — may still be performed on an infant of uncertain viability.

📅 Two Views on the 30-Day Viability Threshold

Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel

  • A newborn is presumed non-viable (nefal) until it survives 30 days
  • Parallel rule for animals: presumed non-viable until 8 days after birth
  • Practical result: no full mourning rites; mother may remain obligated in levirate marriage
  • Rav Yehuda citing Shmuel: halakha follows Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel

The Rabbis (opposing view)

  • A child is presumed alive from birth — no 30-day threshold required
  • Even if a baby died within 30 days, it may still have been a viable living child
  • Practical result: mother may be exempt from levirate marriage
  • The dispute surfaces in: mourning, levirate marriage, and Shabbat desecration

🐄 Abaye's Tannaitic Parallel — The Womb-Born Offspring

A pregnant animal is slaughtered; a living offspring is found inside — what is its status?
↓
Rabbis
It is a nefal (stillborn) — considered dead; slaughter of the mother does not render it permitted
R. Yosei b. R. Yehuda & R. Elazar b. R. Shimon
It has the status of a tereifa — alive but fated to die; slaughter of the mother IS effective
↓
Rava objects: If they dispute tereifa status, why don't they also dispute whether you may eat the offspring?
↓
Resolution: A tereifa-from-the-womb never had a period of fitness — distinct from a tereifa that became defective after birth. Hence the eating question does not arise.

⚧️ Rabbi Yehuda on Hermaphrodite Circumcision on Shabbat

אַנְדְּרוֹגִינוֹס — סָפֵק זָכָר סָפֵק נְקֵבָה
Mishna + Rav Ḥisda's Limitation Rabbi Yehuda rules in the Mishna that a hermaphrodite (androginos) may be circumcised on Shabbat, treating him as a male for this purpose.

Rav Ḥisda clarifies: This classification as male applies only to circumcision on Shabbat — not universally. For valuations (arakhin), the hermaphrodite is not subject at all, since the Torah specifies 'for the male' or 'for the female' — categories a hermaphrodite fits neither definitively.

Source: Sifra on Leviticus 27:3 — the verse's gendered language excludes the androginos from the valuation system entirely.
