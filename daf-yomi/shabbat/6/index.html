⚖️ Does Retroactive Impurity Apply to Teruma or Only Kodesh?

Baraita (Rava)
A menstruating woman renders impure food, drink, beds, chairs, and earthenware vessels retroactively for 24 hours — the baraita explicitly includes teruma.
Rav Huna
The 24-hour retroactive impurity applies only to sacrificial food (kodesh), not to teruma. Teruma's level of sanctity does not require this stringency.
Gemara (Challenge)
The mishna in Ḥagiga would need to list this distinction among purity levels! And the incidents with Rabban Gamliel's maidservant seem to apply retroactivity to teruma bread.
Rav Shmuel bar Rav Yitzḥak
The baraita refers to non-sacred food prepared to kodesh purity standards (ḥaverim in the Galilee). The maidservant's loaves were dough designated during kneading — kodesh-level in practice.
Resolution Rav Huna is vindicated: both the baraita and the maidservant incidents are reinterpreted as involving kodesh-level purity, not ordinary teruma.

🔴 Menstrual Flow vs. 👗 Blood Stain — Different Retroactive Windows

Menstrual Flow (ראתה דם)

  • Retroactive window: 24 hours
  • Renders impure: beds, chairs, food, drink, earthenware
  • Teruma touched during window: suspended — not eaten, not burned
  • Basis: Torah-level concern about when flow actually began

Blood Stain (כתם)

  • Retroactive window: back to last garment check
  • Renders impure: beds, chairs, food, drink, earthenware
  • Teruma touched during window: suspended — same rule
  • Basis: Rabbinic stringency — stain found, source uncertain

🏛️ Rabbi Eliezer and Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi — Ruling in Exigent Circumstances

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
I ruled in accordance with Rabbi Eliezer — applying retroactive impurity to teruma for 24 hours in a particular case.
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
After remembering that Rabbi Eliezer's colleagues disagree, I still said: Rabbi Eliezer is worthy (כדאי) to rely upon in exigent circumstances (בשעת הדחק).
Gemara (Question)
What does 'after he remembered' mean? If halakha was decided against Rabbi Eliezer, how could Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi rely on him at all?
Gemara (Answer)
The halakha had never been decided — not for Rabbi Eliezer, not against him. Once Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi recalled this, he permitted relying on the lone opinion in time of need.
Legal Principle A lone dissenting opinion (דעת יחיד) whose halakha was never formally decided may be relied upon בשעת הדחק — in pressing circumstances.

⚗️ Rav Tovi bar Rav Ketina — How Teruma Dough Acquires Kodesh Status

Stage 1 — Kneading: Dough is still non-sacred (chullin) at this point and may be handled in impurity
↓
Stage 2 — Designation during kneading: The dough is dedicated as teruma and transferred to the priest while being kneaded
↓
Stage 3 — Baking: The priest bakes the loaves — he must still separate challah (priestly portion) from each type of dough
↓
Stage 4 — Elevated status: These loaves are treated with kodesh-level purity standards because the designation occurred during handling
↓
Conclusion: Rabban Gamliel's ruling about the maidservant's loaves involved this elevated category — not ordinary teruma — consistent with Rav Huna's limitation
