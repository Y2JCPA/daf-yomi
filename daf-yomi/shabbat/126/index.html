🔩 The Dragged Bolt: Tied, Hanging, or Both?

Rabbi Yirmeya
We should frame it as: tied is the key condition — whether hanging or not, as long as it is tied (citing Rabbi Yoḥanan's parallel to the wind dispute).
Rabbi Abba
The key condition is prepared beforehand — whether tied or not, it must be designated for this purpose in advance.
Baraita
A dragged bolt: tied, hanging from the door, one edge reaching the ground — permitted in the Temple. Rabbi Yehuda adds further conditions.
Rabbi Yehoshua bar Abba / Ulla
The tanna behind the dragged-bolt ruling is Rabbi Eliezer, consistent with his position on window shutters in the mishna.
Final Ruling Rav Yehuda bar Sheilat → Rav Asi → Rabbi Yoḥanan: Halakha follows Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel

🚪 Covers of Vessels: Which Conditions Apply?

First Tanna (Mishna)
All covers of vessels that have a handle may be moved on Shabbat — the handle alone is sufficient.
Rabbi Yosei
When does this apply? Only for covers of the ground (pit covers). For vessel covers — even without a handle it is permitted in both cases.
Rabbi Yoḥanan (via Rav Asi)
Even the first tanna only permits when the cover has vessel-status — fit for some independent use beyond merely covering.
Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel
A reed used as a bolt is permitted to move. (Gemara asks: does he require vessel-status? This challenges Rabbi Yoḥanan's ruling.)
Gemara's Resolution Rabbi Yoḥanan agrees with Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel in one matter and disagrees in another — partial alignment, not full.

🌾 Produce on Shabbat: Move or Not?

✅ May Be Moved

  • Ritually pure teruma (even though only priests may eat it)
  • Demai — doubtfully tithed produce
  • First tithe from which teruma has been separated
  • Second tithe and consecrated items — if properly redeemed
  • Bundles of straw/wood/twigs prepared before Shabbat for animals

❌ May Not Be Moved

  • Tevel — untithed produce (no permitted use)
  • First tithe whose teruma has not yet been taken
  • Second tithe and consecrated items — not yet redeemed
  • Raw arum and raw mustard — unfit for consumption
  • Bundles not prepared before Shabbat for any use

🧺 Moving Baskets: The Four-and-Five Puzzle

Mishna: One may move four or five baskets of straw or produce on Shabbat — for guests or for space in the Torah study hall.
↓
Gemara's question: If five is permitted, why does the mishna bother mentioning four? The smaller number seems superfluous!
↓
Rav Ḥisda (interpretation 1): One may move only four out of five baskets — not all five at once.
↓
Rav Ḥisda (interpretation 2): Four baskets from a small storeroom; five baskets from a large storeroom.
↓
Critical limit: One may not open a storeroom for the first time on Shabbat. Only continuing prior use is permitted — not initiating a new clearing.
↓
Shmuel: "Four and five" is a figure of speech — meaning a reasonable, moderate quantity. It is not meant as a precise number.
