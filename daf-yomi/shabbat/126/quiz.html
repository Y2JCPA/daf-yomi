QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the baraita, what three conditions make a dragged bolt permitted in the Temple?
A. It must be tied to the door, hanging from it, with one edge reaching the ground
B. It must be made of metal, tied, and used daily
C. It must be hanging, prepared beforehand, and designated by the homeowner
D. It must be attached to the doorpost, reach the ground, and be used by a priest
Correct: A — The baraita specifies all three: the bolt must be tied to the door, hanging from it, and have one edge reaching the ground. Only then is it permitted in the Temple — but not in the rest of the country according to the Tanna Kamma.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rabbi Yoḥanan add to the mishna's rule that vessel covers with handles may be moved on Shabbat?
A. The cover must be made from wood, not metal
B. The cover must have vessel-status — be suited for some independent use
C. The cover must be attached to the vessel at the time of Shabbat
D. The cover must belong to a vessel acquired before Shabbat
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan (transmitted via Rav Asi and Rav Yehuda bar Sheila) clarifies that even the first tanna's ruling only permits covers that themselves qualify as vessels — i.e., fit for some independent use beyond mere covering.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why is untithed produce (tevel) classified as muktzeh and forbidden to move on Shabbat?
A. It is too heavy to move without a vessel
B. It belongs to the Levites and may not be touched by others
C. It is forbidden to eat and thus has no permitted use, rendering it muktzeh
D. Rabbi Yosei issued a special decree against all produce on Shabbat
Correct: C — Tevel is forbidden to eat because tithes have not been separated. Since it has no permitted use on Shabbat, it lacks the designation required for permissibility and is classified as muktzeh.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which tanna does Rabbi Yehoshua bar Abba (citing Ulla) identify as the author of the dragged-bolt halakha?
A. Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel
B. Rabbi Yehuda
C. Rabbi Eliezer
D. Rabbi Yosei
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehoshua bar Abba said in the name of Ulla that the tanna behind the dragged-bolt ruling is Rabbi Eliezer, whose position here is consistent with his view on window shutters discussed in the mishna.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Shmuel explain the mishna's mention of both 'four' and 'five' baskets?
A. Four refers to straw baskets and five to produce baskets
B. Four is permitted on Erev Shabbat and five on Shabbat itself
C. The phrase is a figure of speech meaning a moderate, reasonable quantity
D. Four baskets are permitted if guests arrive; five if Torah study is suspended
Correct: C — Shmuel explains that 'four and five' is an idiomatic expression meaning a moderate amount — it is not intended as a precise numerical limit, but rather conveys the idea of a reasonable quantity needed for the guests or students.
