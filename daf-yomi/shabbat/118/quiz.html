QUESTION 1 OF 5
Rabbi Shimon ben Pazi (citing Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi in the name of Bar Kappara) says eating three Shabbat meals rescues one from three punishments. Which of the following is NOT one of those three?
A. The pangs of the Messiah
B. The judgment of Gehinnom
C. The oppression of exile
D. The war of Gog and Magog
Correct: C — The three rescues from three meals are: pangs of the Messiah, judgment of Gehinnom, and the war of Gog and Magog. 'Oppression of exile' is a separate teaching derived by Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak through a gezeira shava on the word bamotei.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara conclude that the reward for delighting in Shabbat is specifically the 'portion of Jacob' rather than Abraham or Isaac?
A. Jacob was the only patriarch who explicitly observed Shabbat
B. Jacob's inheritance had no limits — he would 'spread abroad' in all directions
C. Abraham and Isaac received no portions of the Land of Israel
D. The verse about Jacob uses the word oneg (delight)
Correct: B — Abraham was told to 'walk through the land' (limited to what he traversed) and Isaac 'saw' the land (limited to sight). Jacob's blessing — 'you shall spread abroad to the west and east' — had no boundaries, making it the paradigm of a boundless, unlimited portion.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When the Gemara questions Rabbi Yosei's aspiration to 'complete Hallel every day,' what is the concern?
A. Hallel may only be recited in the presence of a minyan
B. Reciting full Hallel on an ordinary weekday is considered a form of blasphemy
C. The full Hallel is too long and would cause bitul Torah (neglect of study)
D. Daily Hallel was prohibited after the Destruction of the Temple
Correct: B — The Gemara asks: does reciting full Hallel every day not constitute cursing God? Full Hallel is associated with festivals — saying it on an ordinary day implies either that every day is a festival (untrue) or that the festival has ended (inappropriate). The resolution is that Rabbi Yosei meant reading it as a song or poem, not the formal liturgical Hallel.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rav Yehuda said in the name of Rav: Had the Jewish people properly observed the first Shabbat, no nation would have ruled over them. What scriptural proof is cited?
A. 'Remember the Shabbat day to keep it holy' (Exodus 20:8)
B. 'And it happened on the seventh day, some people went out to gather' (Exodus 16:27) — immediately followed by attacks
C. 'Six days shall you labor' — implying dominion over enemies on the seventh
D. 'Fortunate is the man who keeps Shabbat from desecrating it' (Isaiah 56:2)
Correct: B — The proof is from Exodus 16:27 — the people violated the first Shabbat by going out to gather manna. Immediately afterward (Exodus 17:8), Amalek attacked. The juxtaposition suggests the Shabbat violation opened the door to enemy domination.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Yosei aspire to be among 'the collectors of charity rather than the distributors'?
A. Collectors receive public honor and recognition for their work
B. Collectors physically exert more effort and earn greater heavenly reward
C. Distributors risk giving too little and thereby failing the poor person
D. Distributing charity involves handling large sums, increasing risk of theft
Correct: C — Collectors take fixed, predetermined amounts from donors — a relatively mechanical task. Distributors must assess each poor person's individual need and allocate accordingly. If they misjudge and give too little, they have failed the poor person. This risk of inadvertent sin is why Rabbi Yosei preferred the collector's role.
