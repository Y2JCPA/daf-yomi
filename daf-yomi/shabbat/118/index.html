🍽️ How Many Meals Must One Eat on Shabbat?

First Tanna
One may rescue food for three meals on Shabbat — the standard obligation
Rabbi Ḥidka
One must eat four meals on Shabbat — including Friday night as a separate meal
Rabbi Yosei
One may always rescue food for three meals — explicitly fixing the number at exactly three
Gemara
Even per Rabbi Ḥidka, the charity mishna ('two meals') works — the poor person eats the third meal from the charity plate itself
📌 Scriptural Source The word 'today' (הַיּוֹם) appears three times in Exodus 16 regarding the manna — one for each Shabbat meal: evening, morning, and afternoon

🏛️ Why Jacob's Portion? — Comparing the Three Patriarchs

Abraham

  • 'Rise, walk through the land' (Gen. 13:17)
  • Blessing: traverse the land on foot
  • Limited — only what he could walk

Isaac

  • 'All the land which you see, to you I give it' (Gen. 13:15)
  • Blessing: seeing the land
  • Limited — only what he could see

Jacob ✓ — Best Portion

  • 'You shall spread abroad to the west and east' (Gen. 28:14)
  • Blessing: unlimited expansion in all directions
  • Boundless — no borders, no ceiling
  • → Reward for those who delight in Shabbat!

🔗 Verbal Analogy (Gezeira Shava): Bamot — Shabbat & Exile

Written about Shabbat: 'I will cause you to ride on the bamotei (heights) of the world' (Isaiah 58:14) — promise to those who delight in Shabbat
↓
Written about Jacob/Israel's redemption: 'He made him ride on the bamotei of the earth' (Deuteronomy 32:13) — describing rescue from oppression
↓
Gezeira Shava: Both verses share the word bamotei — linking Shabbat delight to redemption from exile
↓
Conclusion (Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak): One who delights in Shabbat is rescued from the oppression of exile
↓
Bonus teaching (R' Ḥiyya bar Abba / R' Yoḥanan): Proper Shabbat observance atones even for idolatry as pervasive as in the generation of Enosh — 'Fortunate is the man who does this' (Isaiah 56:2)

📜 Rabbi Yosei's 'May My Portion Be Among...'

יְהִי חֶלְקִי מִן — May my portion be among...
Rabbi Yosei — Six Personal Aspirations 🍽️ Those who eat three Shabbat meals

📖 Those who complete Hallel every day (Gemara: as a song/reading, not formal Hallel — full Hallel only on festivals)

🌅 Those who pray Shaḥarit at sunrise and Minḥa at reddening of sunset — R' Ḥiyya bar Abba: even if they thereby miss a communal prayer session

🏥 Those who die of intestinal disease — most righteous die this way; it cleanses sins before death

💰 Charity collectors, not distributors — collectors take fixed amounts; distributors risk inadvertently shortchanging the poor

🌲 Personal legacy: 'I engaged in relations five times and planted five cedars in Eretz Yisrael' — his five sons who became great Sages
