📜 Domains, Dilemmas & the Tabernacle

רְשׁוּת הַיָּחִיד — Private Domain: 10 handbreadths high × 4 handbreadths wide
Core Theme Daf 99 weaves Tabernacle construction details into Shabbat domain law. The Tabernacle's wagons establish the 16-cubit public domain standard, while a cascade of escalating dilemmas from Rav Mordekhai and Rava probe the edges of private domain: columns, walls, pits completed by digging, boards on stakes, and even water poured onto water.

🪡 Tabernacle Curtains: Lower vs. Upper

Lower Curtains

  • Sky blue wool — תְּכֵלֶת
  • Purple wool — אַרְגָּמָן
  • Scarlet wool — תּוֹלַעַת שָׁנִי
  • Fine twisted linen — שֵׁשׁ מָשְׁזָר
  • Clasps in loops — like stars in the sky
  • Higher quality; visible splendor

Upper Curtains

  • Goat hair — עִזִּים
  • Considered inferior material
  • Placed on top, concealing inner glory
  • Teaches: inner beauty over outer display
  • Practical protection from elements
  • Superior placement despite lesser material

🤔 The Column Dilemma: Rav Mordekhai vs. the Sages

Rav Mordekhai
A column in the public domain is 10 handbreadths high and 4 wide. Someone throws an object and it lands on top. Is he liable? The object rested on a private domain — but traveled through public airspace to get there!
Rava
Our mishna already answers this: one who places an object atop a boulder more than 10 handbreadths high is liable. The top of the boulder is private domain — so too the column.
Rav Mordekhai
Perhaps the mishna discusses only a needle placed directly from the side — not an object thrown from afar! A needle resting on the boulder's flank is different from a thrown object landing on top.
Sages
Even a needle must be raised above the ground when placed on the side — it cannot rest perfectly flush. And if there is a protrusion below ten handbreadths, the needle rests in an exempt zone anyway, not private domain.
Rav Yosef
The same mishna resolves your dilemma — the case of taking and placing on the boulder clearly implies the top is treated as private domain regardless of how the object arrives there.
Status The precise dilemma regarding a thrown object onto a column remains unresolved (תֵּיקוּ) — the column was never designated as private domain, unlike a pit or enclosure.

🚂 Deriving the 16-Cubit Public Domain from the Tabernacle

Tabernacle wagon width: Each wagon transporting the beams was 2½ cubits wide
↓
Sides of each wagon: The space between wagon body and wheel + wheel thickness together equaled another 2½ cubits per side (Rava's teaching)
↓
Two wagons in parallel: Two wagons side by side = 2.5 + 2.5 = 5 cubits; their combined outer sides = another 5 cubits → 10 cubits total
↓
Passage between wagons: Levites required working space between the wagons = 6 additional cubits
↓
Total = 16 cubits — 10 (wagons + sides) + 6 (passage) = the standard רְשׁוּת הָרַבִּים width
↓
Gemara's caveat: The Tabernacle pathway may not have actually reached 16 cubits — the derivation is noted as strained, leaving the source somewhat uncertain
