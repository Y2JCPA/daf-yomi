⚔️ Are Weapons Ornaments or Reprehensible?

Rabbi Eliezer
These weapons are ornaments for a man — just as he may go out with other ornaments on Shabbat, he may go out with a sword, bow, shield, or spear.
The Rabbis
Weapons are nothing but reprehensible. In the future they will be eliminated, as it is written: "They shall beat their swords into plowshares" (Isaiah 2:4).
Mishna Ruling A man may NOT go out on Shabbat with a sword, bow, shield (teris), alla (club/kulpa), or spear. These are not permitted ornaments.

🌅 Will Weapons Exist in the Messianic Era?

Shmuel
The only difference between this world and the messianic era is subjugation to foreign kingdoms. Wars and weapons will still exist among nations.
Rabbi Ḥiyya bar Abba
All the prophets prophesied only regarding the messianic era. Weapons will truly be eliminated — Isaiah's vision will be literally fulfilled.
Rav Kahana's Teaching "I was eighteen years old, had learned the entire Talmud, and yet I did not know until now that a verse does not depart from its plain, literal meaning."

💍 Garter vs. Ankle Chains

Garter (קִטְבֵּל)

  • Worn on one leg to hold up a stocking
  • ✅ Ritually pure — cannot become a vessel
  • ✅ Permitted on Shabbat
  • Rav Yehuda: analogous to a bracelet worn on the arm
  • No connecting chain between legs

Ankle Chains (כְּבָלִים)

  • Worn on two legs with a connecting chain
  • ❌ Ritually impure — has vessel status
  • ❌ Forbidden on Shabbat
  • Chain shortens stride — protects the hymen (Rabba bar bar Ḥana)
  • Chain's function = purposeful action → vessel status

📚 Reish Lakish on Torah Partnership

Sharpen each other in halakha → God ensures success: "In your majesty prosper, ride on, in behalf of truth" (Ps. 45:5)
↓
Are agreeable to each other in halakha → God listens: "They that feared the Lord spoke one to another" (Mal. 3:16)
↓
Listen to each other in halakha → God hears their voice: "You who dwell in gardens, the companions heed your voice" (Song 8:13)
↓
⚠️ Do NOT listen to each other → they cause the Shechina to depart from Israel: "Run away, my beloved, be like a gazelle" (Song 8:14)
↓
Cause each other to err in halakha → God still loves them: "His banner [diglo] over me was love" (Song 2:4) — read as dilug (leaping over errors)
