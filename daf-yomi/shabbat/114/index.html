Shabbat 114: The Scholar's Dignity & Yom Kippur on Shabbat

כל משנאי אהבו מות — All those who hate Me love death (Proverbs 8:36)
CORE THEMES This daf weaves together two major themes: the appearance and conduct expected of a Torah scholar — from clean clothes to broad expertise — and the halakhic complications when Yom Kippur falls adjacent to Shabbat, including shofar blasts, havdala, and vegetable preparation.

Changing Garments: Torah Etiquette

👗 Kohen wears garments while removing ashes from the altar
↓
🔄 He changes into different garments before carrying ashes outside the camp (Lev. 6:4)
↓
📜 School of R' Yishmael: The Torah teaches derech eretz (proper conduct)
↓
🍳 The clothes worn to cook for one's master should not be worn to pour wine
↓
👔 Principle: Honor requires dressing appropriately for each role
LESSON The priestly wardrobe change is not merely ritual — it establishes a universal principle of dignified presentation when serving others.

A Torah Scholar's Shoes & Clothes

R' Ḥiyya bar Abba / R' Yoḥanan
It is disgraceful for a talmid chacham to go out in patched shoes
Gemara
But R' Aḥa bar Ḥanina went out in patched shoes!
R' Aḥa b. Rav Naḥman
The prohibition is only for a patch upon a patch — double patching
R' Ḥiyya bar Abba / R' Yoḥanan
A scholar with a grease stain on his clothes is liable to death
Source
"All mesanai love death" — read as masniai: those who cause Me to be hated (Prov. 8:36)
Ravina
Not revav (fat stain) but revad (bloodstain) — an even greater disgrace
KEY PRINCIPLE A Torah scholar who appears unkempt causes people to disrespect the Torah itself — this is a form of chillul Hashem.

Who Are the Banna'in (Builders)?

R' Yoḥanan

  • Banna'in = Torah scholars
  • They are 'builders' who build the world through Torah study
  • Meticulous about stains — even one-sided stain interposes
  • Connects to all the prior teachings about scholarly appearance

Reish Lakish

  • Banna'in = bath attendants (ulairin)
  • They come from overseas and wear distinctive garments
  • Professionally meticulous about cleanliness
  • Their garments are neither black nor white — reddish/colored
R' YANNAI'S WILL "Don't bury me in white (lest I not be acquitted) or black (lest I be among the righteous like a mourner among grooms). Bury me in overseas bath attendants' clothes."

Yom Kippur Adjacent to Shabbat

Yom Kippur falls adjacent to Shabbat
Erev Shabbat (Friday)
No shofar is sounded to herald Shabbat
Motzaei Shabbat (Saturday night)
No havdala is recited between Shabbat and Yom Kippur
↓
Whose opinion is this?
Yehuda b. R' Shimon b. Pazi
This is R' Akiva — equates sanctity of Yom Kippur with Shabbat
Narrator (Amora)
No proof — priests are vigilant and don't need the shofar reminder
DEBATE The core question: Does R' Yishmael's view — that Shabbat fats may be offered on Yom Kippur — require a shofar signal? The Gemara says no, because priests can be trusted to remember.
