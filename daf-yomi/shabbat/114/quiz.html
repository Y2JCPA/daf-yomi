Shabbat 114 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say a Torah scholar with a stain on his clothing is 'liable to death'?
A. Because wearing stained clothes violates a Torah prohibition
B. Because it causes people to hate the Torah (chillul Hashem)
C. Because the stain may contain forbidden substances
D. Because it shows disrespect to one's teacher
Correct: B — The Gemara reinterprets 'mesanai' (those who hate Me) as 'masniai' (those who cause Me to be hated). A disheveled scholar causes people to disrespect Torah, which is a form of chillul Hashem.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Yoḥanan, what qualifies someone as a Torah scholar whose lost objects are returned by visual identification?
A. He can answer halakhic questions on any topic
B. He sets aside personal matters for matters of Heaven
C. He turns his undergarment inward to hide the rough stitching
D. He never wears patched shoes
Correct: C — R' Yoḥanan identifies this level of Torah scholar as someone meticulous even in small matters of appearance — specifically, one who turns his undergarment inward so uneven stitching is not visible.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why doesn't the shofar need to be sounded when Yom Kippur falls on Friday to alert priests about offering Shabbat fats?
A. Because fats from Shabbat may never be offered on Yom Kippur
B. Because the shofar is prohibited on both Shabbat and Yom Kippur by Torah law
C. Because priests are vigilant and can be trusted to remember on their own
D. Because havdala already serves as the reminder
Correct: C — The Gemara responds that 'kohanim zerizin hen' — priests are vigilant and do not need external reminders like the shofar to know their obligations.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rav Yosef's principle regarding rabbinic prohibitions (shevut) and sounding the shofar?
A. A shevut may always be overridden for Temple service
B. A shevut is not overridden in order to permit another action
C. A shevut applies only on Shabbat, not on Yom Kippur
D. A shevut is automatically suspended when two holy days overlap
Correct: B — Rav Yosef establishes that one rabbinic prohibition (sounding the shofar) is not overridden merely to inform people that another action (like trimming vegetables) is permitted.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Who does R' Yoḥanan identify as the 'banna'in' (builders) mentioned in the Mishnah about stains?
A. Professional construction workers who get dirty easily
B. Bath attendants from overseas countries
C. Torah scholars, who build the world through their learning
D. Priests who build and maintain the Temple
Correct: C — R' Yoḥanan says the 'banna'in' are Torah scholars who are engaged in 'building the world' (binyan ha'olam) all their days through Torah study. Reish Lakish disagrees, identifying them as bath attendants.
