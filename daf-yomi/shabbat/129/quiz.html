Shabbat 129 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the halakha as ruled by Mareimar, when may Shabbat be desecrated for a woman in childbirth whose womb is open?
A. Only if she explicitly requests it
B. Never — a gentile must handle her needs
C. Whether or not she explicitly requests it
D. Only during the first day of labor
Correct: C — Mareimar ruled with Mar Zutra: as long as the womb is open, we desecrate Shabbat regardless of whether she requests it — life-saving does not require the patient's request.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
During which post-birth period must a woman's Shabbat needs be performed specifically by a gentile (not a Jew directly)?
A. Days 1–3
B. Days 4–7
C. Days 8–30
D. After 30 days
Correct: C — The Sages of Neharde'a specify: days 1–3 = full desecration; days 4–7 = only if requested; days 8–30 = gentile performs her needs. After 30 days she is treated as fully recovered.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Rabba permit breaking a scholars' bench to build a fire for himself after bloodletting?
A. Bloodletting constitutes a childbirth-level emergency
B. The bench belonged to him, so destruction is permitted
C. Preventing bodily harm overrides the prohibition of wanton destruction
D. Furniture has no legal standing under 'do not destroy'
Correct: C — Rabba told Abaye: 'Do not destroy applies to destruction of my body — preserving my body overrides the prohibition against destroying objects.' The bench is replaceable; the body is not.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
On which days of the week does Shmuel say bloodletting is DANGEROUS (not merely to be avoided)?
A. Sunday and Friday
B. Monday and Thursday
C. Tuesday and Wednesday
D. Wednesday and Shabbat
Correct: B — Shmuel: Monday (2nd day) and Thursday (5th day) are dangerous — the Master said only those who have heavenly merit survive bloodletting on those days.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the halakhic basis cited by Rav Naḥman for permitting all birth-related acts for a woman in childbirth on Shabbat?
A. A direct Mosaic law (halakha le-Moshe mi-Sinai) exempting childbirth
B. Ezekiel 16's rebuke passage, which describes a dangerous abandoned birth
C. The general rule that any rabbinic prohibition yields to human suffering
D. A Talmudic tradition that childbirth always overrides all prohibitions
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman (citing Rabba bar Avuh citing Rav): Ezekiel 16 describes birth in abandoned, dangerous conditions and enumerates birth-care acts — each verse ('navel not cut,' 'not washed,' etc.) is a source permitting those acts on Shabbat.
