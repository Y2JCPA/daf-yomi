⚖️ Mar Zutra vs. Rav Ashi: Must She Ask?

Rav Ashi
If she says 'I need Shabbat desecrated' — desecrate it. If she did not say so — do not desecrate it.
Mar Zutra
As long as the womb is open, whether she said 'I need' or not — desecrate Shabbat on her behalf!
Mareimar
The halakha follows Mar Zutra. A person in mortal danger cannot always speak for themselves — we act proactively to save life.
📌 Core Principle Pikuach nefesh overrides requiring explicit request — we do not wait for the endangered person to ask us to save them.

📅 Post-Birth Timeline: Shabbat Leniencies

Woman Gives Birth
Womb opens → life at risk
↓
Days 1–3
Desecrate Shabbat even if she says she does NOT need it — presumed life-threatening
Days 4–7
Desecrate Shabbat only if she says she needs it — risk possible but not assumed
Days 8–30
Have a gentile perform her needs — no direct Shabbat desecration by a Jew
↓
After 30 days: Treated as a fully recovered person — standard Shabbat rules apply

🔥 Building a Fire on Shabbat: Who Qualifies?

Woman in Childbirth

  • Fire explicitly permitted on Shabbat
  • Even in summer (hot season)
  • Cold after labor = genuine danger
  • Source: Rav Yehuda citing Shmuel

Ordinary Sick Person

  • Fire also permitted — inferred from childbirth
  • kal va-chomer: if for new mother, then all the more so
  • Shmuel's statement extends to all sick
  • Rejected reading: childbirth was not 'only' case

Rabbinic Personal Practice

  • Shmuel: teak armchair broken for warmth
  • Rav Yehuda: ebony table destroyed for fire
  • Rabba: scholars' bench broken
  • Shows urgency — even valuable furniture used

💉 Proper Bloodletting Procedure (Step by Step)

Avoid wheat beforehand — eating wheat then letting blood means you only 'let the wheat'; the wheat replenishes blood immediately
↓
Drink immediately after — wine or broth right away; Shmuel had a dish of spleen; Rabbi Yoḥanan drank wine until he could smell it from his fingernails
↓
Wait before eating — do not eat solid food until the time it takes to walk half a mil has elapsed
↓
Sit away from wind — the body is vulnerable after blood loss; if the blood-letter took too much, the wind could be fatal
↓
Taste something before going outside — encountering a corpse or a pig on an empty post-bloodletting stomach causes serious harm
↓
Wait before rising — 5 things bring one closer to death than life, including rising too quickly; sit briefly and recover first

✂️ Cutting the Umbilical Cord on Shabbat

Mishna
One may tie the umbilical cord on Shabbat. (Tying only — no explicit ruling on cutting.)
Rabbi Yosei
One may even cut the umbilical cord on Shabbat — the prohibition of cutting does not apply here.
Rav Naḥman (citing Rav)
Halakha follows Rabbi Yosei. For twins sharing a cord: even the Rabbis agree cutting is permitted — leaving it endangers both newborns.
Rav Naḥman (citing Rav)
Everything listed in Ezekiel 16's rebuke passage (the abandoned birth scene) may be performed for a woman in childbirth on Shabbat.
📖 Source Verse "וְשָׁרֵּךְ לֹא כֹּרָת" — 'Your navel was not cut' (Ezekiel 16:4) teaches that cutting the cord is a birth necessity performed even on Shabbat
