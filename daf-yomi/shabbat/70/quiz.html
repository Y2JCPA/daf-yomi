Shabbat 70 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Natan, why was the prohibition of kindling fire (havarah) stated separately in the Torah (Ex. 35:3)?
A. To teach that kindling is more severe than all other Shabbat labors
B. To teach that each labor individually incurs its own liability (lav l'chalek)
C. To exclude kindling from the general category of Shabbat labor
D. To restrict the prohibition to residential dwellings only
Correct: B — Rabbi Natan holds havarah was singled out lav l'chalek — as a paradigm teaching that every one of the 39 labors is independently prohibited and incurs its own sin-offering.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Shmuel reject Rabbi Natan's derivation from the kindling verse?
A. Shmuel holds the verse was written after Sinai and is non-binding
B. Shmuel holds like Rabbi Yosei: kindling was singled out only as its own standalone prohibition (lav l'azharah), not as a model for all labors
C. Shmuel disagrees that kindling is a prohibited Shabbat labor at all
D. Shmuel requires a verse from Deuteronomy, not Exodus
Correct: B — Shmuel follows Rabbi Yosei who holds havarah was singled out lav l'azharah — merely as an explicit warning for that specific act, not to teach a general principle about ḥiluk melakhot.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the hermeneutic breakdown of Lev. 4:2, what does the term 'from one' (מאחת) teach?
A. Liability for performing a primary category (av) of labor
B. Liability for performing a subcategory (toladah) of labor
C. Liability for a partial transgression, e.g., writing only shin-mem from the name Shimon
D. Liability only when the act was intentional
Correct: C — 'From one' (מאחת) teaches liability for a partial transgression — such as one who wrote only the letters shin-mem, an incomplete portion of the full name Shimon.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rava asked Rav Naḥman: what is the halakha if someone forgot both that it was Shabbat and that a specific labor was forbidden? What did Rav Naḥman rule?
A. He is liable for two sin-offerings — one for each type of unawareness
B. He is exempt entirely, since double unawareness constitutes complete ignorance
C. He is liable for one sin-offering, as the Shabbat unawareness is dominant and subsumes the labor unawareness
D. He must bring a doubtful guilt-offering (asham taluy) instead
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman ruled that when one forgot both Shabbat and the specific labor simultaneously, the Shabbat unawareness is treated as dominant — he is liable for only one sin-offering, as in the standard case of forgetting Shabbat.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The Gemara notes that stating the number of 39 primary categories of labor serves a practical halakhic purpose. What is it?
A. It limits Shabbat liability to exactly those 39 acts and no others
B. It establishes which labors are permitted on Yom Tov
C. It tells one who performs all 39 labors in a single lapse of awareness how many separate sin-offerings he must bring
D. It corresponds to the 39 lashes given for biblical violations
Correct: C — Stating the number 39 informs the person who performed all primary categories in one lapse of awareness that he owes a separate sin-offering for each — the count itself has legal significance for ḥiluk melakhot.
