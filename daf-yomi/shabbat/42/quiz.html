5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Rav Shmuel bar Bisna asks whether a woman who discharges semen is like one who experienced an 'emission' or like one who 'contacted' an impure substance. What do both Abaye and Rava conclude?
A. She is like one who contacted an impure substance — the semen was external to her
B. She is like one who experienced an emission — she is legally likened to the man
C. The question is unresolved and left as a teku
D. She is pure entirely, since the semen originated from the man
Correct: B — Both Abaye and Rava agree: the woman who discharges semen has the status of one who experienced an emission (בַּעַל קֶרִי), not merely one who contacted impurity. She is likened to the man who engaged in intercourse with her, per Rabbi Shimon's ruling.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the precise practical halakhic difference between Abaye and Rava's dispute about the vagina's legal status?
A. Whether a woman must count seven clean days after intercourse
B. Whether one woman inserting an olive-bulk of animal carcass into another's vagina renders her impure
C. Whether semen can ever be a source of impurity
D. Whether a woman after childbirth must immerse in a mikveh
Correct: B — The Gemara specifies the case: one woman inserts an olive-bulk of animal carcass into another's vagina. Abaye (encapsulated) says she is pure; Rava (beit hastrim) says she is impure.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rava proves the vagina is 'beit hastrim' from a baraita. What does that baraita say that constitutes his proof?
A. It states that a woman must immerse after contact with any impure substance
B. It calls the impurity of semen while still inside the body a 'novelty' because it is in a concealed part of the body
C. It rules that Rabbi Shimon's leniency does not apply to semen inside the body
D. It compares the vagina to the sealed interior of a clay vessel
Correct: B — Rava cites the baraita that derives from the Torah verse that semen inside the woman's body is a halakhic novelty precisely because it is in a 'concealed part of the body' (beit hastrim). This implies the vagina is beit hastrim — not encapsulated.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the Shabbat circumcision incident, the man reports hearing the baby's voice at nightfall on Erev Shabbat, but the birth was not complete until Shabbat. Why does Rava still permit circumcision on Shabbat?
A. Because the voice was not conclusive proof the baby was alive before Shabbat
B. Because once a ruling is given it cannot be retracted
C. Because the baby's head must have emerged before Shabbat, legally commencing the birth at that point
D. Because any doubt about the birth date is resolved leniently for circumcision
Correct: C — Rava reasons that a baby cannot make a sound unless its head has already emerged from the birth canal. Since the voice was heard before Shabbat, the head — and thus the legal birth — must have begun before Shabbat. The circumcision count therefore started before Shabbat, making day 8 fall on Shabbat, which permits circumcision.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rava, why is a person with a creeping animal (שרץ) carcass in body folds pure, but a person with animal carcass (נבלה) flesh in body folds impure?
A. A creeping animal is smaller and therefore below the minimum impurity threshold in body folds
B. A שרץ imparts impurity through contact (blocked by concealment), while נבלה imparts impurity through carrying (not blocked by concealment)
C. Body folds protect against all impurity from creeping animals but not from mammals
D. The Sages enacted a special leniency for creeping animals found in body folds
Correct: B — Rava distinguishes by the mechanism of impurity transmission: a שרץ imparts impurity through contact (מגע), and concealed body parts are not susceptible to contact-based impurity — hence pure. A נבלה imparts impurity through carrying (נשיאה), which applies even to concealed parts of the body — hence impure.
