⚖️ The Core Dispute: When Does Niddah Begin?

Shammai
Her time is sufficient (דייה שעתה). A woman's impurity begins only from the moment she actually sees blood — no retroactive contamination of items she previously handled.
Hillel
From her last self-examination. Impurity is retroactive to whenever she last examined herself and was clean — potentially many hours prior.
Rabbis Rule Neither Shammai nor Hillel exactly — a 24-hour retroactive period applies to women without a fixed cycle (veset), anchored by whichever is shorter: 24 hours or the last examination.

⚖️ Three Opinions on Retroactive Impurity

Shammai

  • דייה שעתה — her time is sufficient
  • Impurity begins at moment of sighting
  • No retroactive contamination
  • Items handled before sighting: pure
  • Most lenient position

Hillel

  • Retroactive to last self-examination
  • All items handled since then: impure
  • Could extend many hours back
  • Most stringent position
  • Does not limit to 24 hours

Rabbis (Accepted Ruling)

  • 24-hour retroactive window maximum
  • Or back to last examination (shorter wins)
  • Applies only without a fixed veset
  • Woman with veset: from moment of sighting
  • Middle path between Shammai and Hillel

🏊 Challenge from the Ritual Bath (Mikveh)

מסכת מקוואות ב:ב — בית שמאי / בית הלל
Gemara's Difficulty A mikveh that once held the required 40 se'a was later found lacking — all items immersed in it are deemed definitely impure retroactively (Mikvaot 2:2). Why isn't a niddah's impurity also treated as definite rather than uncertain?

Resolution: Mikveh water can gradually trickle out undetected — so invalid status may have begun long ago. A woman's blood, however, does not flow gradually in this way. Additionally, the mikveh case has two factors weighing against purity: it is currently lacking and the items immersed have a prior presumptive status of impurity. The niddah case has only one such factor.

📐 Practical Case: Her Time Is Sufficient

Woman sits in bed and handles ritually pure items (טהרות — produce set aside in purity)
↓
She rises from the bed and at that moment sees menstrual blood
↓
She is now impure — her niddah status begins from this exact moment
↓
The items she handled remain ritually pure — she was unquestionably pure at the time she touched them
↓
Broader rule: A woman with a veset who examined herself at the expected time counts her days of impurity only from the moment of sighting — never earlier
