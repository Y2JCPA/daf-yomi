🤰 Does a Gestational Sac Confer Childbirth Impurity?

Rabbi Yehoshua
A gestational sac without developed tissue is still offspring — the woman has the impurity of a woman after childbirth.
The Rabbis
A gestational sac without developed tissue is not offspring — the woman does not have childbirth impurity.
Reish Lakish (R. Oshaya)
The dispute applies when the amniotic fluid is turbid — turbidity suggests an embryo may have liquefied inside.
Rav Naḥman / Rabba bar Avuh
The dispute applies when the amniotic fluid is clear — if turbid, all agree it has offspring status.
Resolution Rabbi Yehoshua is only uncertain (safek) about turbid cases — his verse is an asmakhta. For monetary matters (pidyon haben) we are lenient; for ritual matters (animal firstborn) we are stringent.

Definitely offspring — woman is impure as after childbirth (male + female strictures)

Sac full of flesh

🌊 The Turbid vs Clear Amniotic Fluid Dispute

Reish Lakish
Turbid fluid: this is where the dispute lies — Rabbi Yehoshua says the embryo may have liquefied; the Rabbis disagree.
Gemara's Objection
A baraita shows Rabbi Yehoshua derives offspring status from a verse about skin — not from turbidity. The dispute is about skin, not fluid cloudiness.
Rav Naḥman / Rabba bar Avuh
Clear fluid: this is where the dispute lies — if turbid, all agree it has the status of offspring.
Rava's Objection
Mishna in Bekhorot: a gestational sac does not exempt a firstborn animal. If turbid = offspring by pure logic, why wouldn't it exempt the animal too?
Final Answer Rabbi Yehoshua is uncertain (safek): lenient for monetary pidyon haben; stringent for ritual animal firstborn — hence no exemption for the animal.

🔍 Examining an Embryo's Sex — Step by Step

Not water — water is too strong (חזק) and dissolves the embryo
↓
Use oil — soft, cleans the embryo gently without dissolving it
↓
Sunlight only — examination must be done in natural sunlight, not artificial light
↓
Smooth sliver of wood (Abba Shaul bar Nash): move it along the sex organ — if the sliver is caught (not smooth), the embryo is male
↓
Direction matters (Rav Naḥman): sliver must go from below to above; moving above-to-below gives a false caught result
↓
Female sign (Rav Adda bar Ahava): vagina appears as a line like a cracked grain of barley oriented along the length of the body

🐠 Sandal Fetus — Competing Views on Offspring Status

What Is a Sandal Fetus?

  • Flat, fish-shaped fetus resembling a sandal or flatfish (דג)
  • Always accompanied by a twin — the sandal envelops or flanks the sibling
  • Woman observes impurity strictures of both male and female birth

Rav Yehuda (Shmuel)

  • Requires only the shape of a face
  • Without facial features → not offspring, woman not impure as after birth

Rav Adda (R. Yitzḥak)

  • Requires shape of a face AND shape of a hand (יד)
  • Both features required for offspring status

Rabbi Yannai's Practice

  • Rebuked Sages who wished to deem pure a sandal lacking a face
  • 'You have deemed pure women who discharge offspring!'
  • Practical ruling: face-shape required and strictly enforced
