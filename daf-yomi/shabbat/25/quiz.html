Shabbat 25 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi initially refuse to rule on a gestational sac full of flesh?
A. He believed such a sac could never have halakhic significance
B. He had not heard a ruling on this case from his teachers
C. The question had already been conclusively resolved by Rabbi Yehoshua
D. He held it was clearly not offspring and no ruling was needed
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi explicitly told his students he had not heard this halakha from his teachers (לא שמעתי), so he declined to rule.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Reish Lakish citing Rabbi Oshaya, in which scenario do Rabbi Yehoshua and the Rabbis dispute whether a gestational sac is offspring?
A. When the amniotic fluid is perfectly clear
B. When the gestational sac is filled entirely with blood
C. When the amniotic fluid is turbid
D. When the sac contains visibly developed tissue
Correct: C — Reish Lakish says the dispute applies specifically when the fluid is turbid (עכור), suggesting the embryo may have liquefied — hence Rabbi Yehoshua treats it as offspring.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why must an embryo be examined with oil rather than water to determine its sex?
A. Water is too cold and obscures the embryo's coloration
B. Water is too strong and dissolves the embryo
C. Oil reacts chemically to reveal sex-specific markers
D. Water is reserved exclusively for ritual purification purposes
Correct: B — The Gemara explicitly states water is too strong (חזק) and dissolves the delicate embryo, while oil is soft and cleans it gently without damage.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Rava's objection from the laws of firstborn animals (Bekhorot 19b) prove about the amniotic fluid dispute?
A. A gestational sac can never exempt a firstborn animal under any rabbinic view
B. Rabbi Yehoshua definitively holds a gestational sac is full offspring
C. The dispute must concern clear fluid — Rabbi Yehoshua is only uncertain about turbid cases
D. Sandal fetuses are treated identically to regular gestational sacs for firstborn purposes
Correct: C — If turbid sac = offspring by sheer logic, it should exempt a firstborn animal too — but the mishna says it does not. Therefore the dispute is about clear fluid; turbid is only a safek for Rabbi Yehoshua.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Yehuda in Shmuel's name, what does a sandal fetus require to have the halakhic status of offspring?
A. Visible spine and rib structure
B. Developed limbs including both arms and legs
C. The shape of a face (צורת פנים)
D. Hair growth discernible by naked eye
Correct: C — Rav Yehuda says in Shmuel's name that a sandal fetus requires the shape of a face (צורת פנים) to qualify as offspring and render the woman impure as after childbirth.
