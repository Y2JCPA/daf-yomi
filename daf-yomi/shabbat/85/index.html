🌱 Five Species in One Garden Bed — When Is It Permitted?

Mishna
Five seeds may be planted in a single 6×6 handbreadth garden bed without violating kilaim (diverse kinds)
Rav
This applies only in an isolated bed — not one surrounded by other garden beds. Rabbinic decree: lest one fill the corners and create kilaim with the neighbors
Shmuel
This applies even among garden beds — surrounding beds do not matter; the internal arrangement alone determines permissibility
Core Question When and where may one plant five diverse species in a single 6×6 handbreadth bed without transgressing the Torah prohibition of kilaim?

The Foundation: Five Seeds, Six Handbreadths

חֲמִשָּׁה זְרָעִים בְּשִׁשָּׁה טְפָחִים
כִּלְאַיִם — Diverse Kinds The Sages have an accepted tradition: five seeds in a six-handbreadth space do not draw sustenance from one another and may therefore coexist without violating kilaim.

Rabbi Ḥiyya bar Abba citing Rabbi Yoḥanan: the verse "She is like merchant ships, she brings her bread from afar" teaches that the accepted traditions of the Sages are reliable and substantial — their rulings carry genuine legal weight even without explicit scriptural derivation.

Rav Asi adds: the 6×6 measurement refers to the internal area only — the boundary borders of the bed must be added on top.

⚖️ Rav vs. Shmuel: Which Beds Qualify?

Rav
Isolated/desolate area only. In a bed surrounded by other garden beds, the five-species arrangement is forbidden.
Reason: rabbinic decree (גְּזֵרָה) lest one later fill the corners of the bed, causing the diverse species to intermingle with surrounding plantings
Shmuel
Even among garden beds. The arrangement is permitted regardless of what surrounds the bed.
Reason: the correct internal layout is sufficient — no additional decree is needed for the surrounding context
The Stakes Rav's position is more restrictive: a rabbinic fence prevents planting even in technically valid arrangements when inadvertent future violation is plausible. Shmuel does not extend the decree that far.

⭕ Rav Kahana's Method: Five Circles in One Bed

Start: Take a garden bed measuring 6×6 handbreadths (internal area)
↓
Layout: Draw five circles — one in each of the four corners and one in the center
↓
Plant: Sow a different species inside each of the five circles
↓
Critical rule (School of Rabbi Yannai): The space between the circles must be left completely barren — no seeds at all
↓
Result: All five species remain visually distinct and do not intermingle → fully permitted under kilaim

📐 Two Key Clarifications on the Garden Bed Rule

Rav Asi: Internal vs. Total Area

  • The 6×6 handbreadth requirement = internal planting area only
  • The physical boundary/border of the bed is excluded
  • Borders must be added on top of the 6×6 measurement
  • Ensures genuine separation between species at all points

Rav Kahana: Making It Practical

  • Farmer wants to fill entire garden with vegetables
  • Does not want to leave large distances between rows
  • Solution: five circles with barren space between them
  • Objection answered: inter-circle areas must be completely empty
