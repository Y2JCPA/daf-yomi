🗣️ Ulla vs. Rav Yehuda: Shaking Cloaks in Pumbedita

Ulla
The Rabbis of Pumbedita are desecrating Shabbat by shaking their cloaks to remove dust — this is the prohibition of laundering!
Rav Yehuda
You may continue shaking — this prohibition applies only to new garments where dust visibly whitens the fabric. Old garments present no concern.
Ulla
Even if technically permitted, doing so in front of others raises concern of mar'it ayin — the appearance of wrongdoing!
Rav Yehuda
These are Rabbis — they know the halakhic distinction. There is no concern about appearance among scholars who understand the law.
הלכה Shaking new dark garments = liable (laundering); shaking old/light garments where no whitening occurs = permitted on Shabbat

Worn properly

🛁 Bathing on Shabbat: Permitted vs. Prohibited

✅ Permitted

  • Bathing in hot cave water or Tiberias hot springs
  • Drying oneself with a towel — even ten towels
  • Smearing oil on the body
  • Gentle rubbing/massage with the hand
  • Pouring water over entire body (rinsing fashion)
  • Bath attendants bringing garments if worn over majority of body
  • Swaddling an infant (R. Yoḥanan)

❌ Prohibited

  • Giving wet towel to bath attendants — suspect of wringing
  • Vigorous exercise or strenuous massage
  • Standing on floor of Deyomset therapeutic bathhouse
  • Entering a swampy muddy river — risk of wringing violation
  • Scraping body with a scraper/strigil
  • Taking a drug to induce vomiting
  • Aligning or resetting a broken bone

📚 The Fall of Rabbi Elazar ben Arakh

גָּלֵה לִמְקוֹם תּוֹרָה וְאַל תֹּאמַר שֶׁהִיא תָּבוֹא אַחֲרֶיךָ — רַבִּי נְהוֹרַאי
מוסר השכל Rabbi Elazar ben Arakh — once the greatest of Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai's students — traveled to Phrygia and Deyomset, famed for their wine and hot springs.

He was drawn after their pleasures. His Torah learning was completely forgotten.

Upon returning, he stood to read from the Torah scroll and misread the words — his colleagues had to correct him. The Gemara reads his error as a coded sign of spiritual decline.

Rabbi Nehorai's lesson: "Exile yourself to a place of Torah and do not say that it will follow you — your colleagues will establish it in your hands. Do not rely on your own understanding."

The wine of Phrygia and the waters of Deyomset were said to have caused Israel to lose the ten tribes.
