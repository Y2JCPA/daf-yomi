5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak, how many shared words between circumcision and Shabbat verses are required to derive that circumcision overrides Shabbat?
A. One word — covenant alone is sufficient
B. Two words — sign and covenant together
C. Three words — sign, covenant, and generations all together
D. Four words — an additional holy is also required
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak rules that no single verbal analogy suffices: 'sign' alone would include phylacteries; 'covenant' alone would include adult circumcision; 'generations' alone would include ritual fringes. Only when all three words point uniquely to circumcision's relationship with Shabbat is the derivation valid.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the challenge that Rabbi Yoḥanan's verse ('on the eighth day he shall be circumcised') should also allow atonement offerings on Shabbat?
A. A separate verse explicitly forbids atonement offerings on Shabbat
B. Since the Torah already shows compassion to those lacking atonement, allowing Shabbat violation would be excessive — but Ravina challenges even this
C. The verse 'eighth day' only applies to circumcision grammatically
D. Atonement offerings are rabbinically prohibited on Shabbat, not biblically
Correct: B — The Gemara suggests that since the Torah already shows mercy to those lacking atonement, it would be excessive to also permit Shabbat violation for them. However, Ravina strongly objects that by this logic non-priests should also be permitted to sacrifice — showing the compassion argument alone is insufficient.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rava's kal v'chomer argument that circumcision overrides leprosy proceeds through which chain of logic?
A. Leprosy overrides Temple service; Temple service overrides Shabbat; therefore leprosy overrides circumcision
B. Circumcision overrides Shabbat; Shabbat is more stringent than leprosy; therefore circumcision certainly overrides leprosy
C. Leprosy overrides circumcision when not at the appointed time; circumcision overrides Shabbat; therefore Shabbat overrides leprosy
D. Positive mitzvot override negative mitzvot; circumcision is positive; leprosy prohibition is negative; therefore circumcision overrides leprosy
Correct: B — Rava's kal v'chomer: Circumcision overrides Shabbat (established halakha); Shabbat carries capital punishment and is more stringent than leprosy; therefore circumcision certainly overrides the less stringent prohibition of cutting leprous symptoms.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rav Safra's challenge to Rava, and how does Rava respond?
A. Rav Safra: leprosy has no biblical source; Rava: the baraita provides one
B. Rav Safra: leprosy overrides Temple service which overrides Shabbat, so leprosy may be MORE stringent than Shabbat; Rava: leprosy only defers to Temple service to protect its sanctity, not because leprosy is more lenient
C. Rav Safra: pure leprous spots have no prohibition; Rava: all spots are treated equally
D. Rav Safra: the kal v'chomer is blocked by a biblical verse; Rava: the verse is only rabbinic
Correct: B — Rav Safra constructs a transitivity argument — leprosy → Temple service → Shabbat — suggesting leprosy could be the most stringent. Rava responds that leprosy 'overrides' Temple service only because the Torah protects the Temple's sanctity by excluding lepers, not because leprosy itself is more lenient than Shabbat.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The baraita rules that circumcision overrides leprosy. In which circumstances does this override apply?
A. Only when circumcision is at its appointed time (the eighth day)
B. Only when circumcision is not at its appointed time
C. Both when at its appointed time and when not at its appointed time
D. Only when a majority of the foreskin is affected by leprosy
Correct: C — The baraita explicitly states the rule applies both when circumcision is at its appointed time and when it is not at its appointed time. This contrasts with the rule for Shabbat, where only circumcision at the appointed time (the eighth day) overrides Shabbat — circumcision performed later does not override Shabbat.
