⚖️ Rabbi Yoḥanan vs. Reish Lakish: The Verse and Its Scope

R. Yoḥanan
"On the eighth day he shall be circumcised" (Lev 12:3) — circumcision happens on the eighth day whenever it occurs, even on Shabbat.
Reish Lakish
If so, those lacking atonement — a zav or healed leper — should also bring their offering on Shabbat, since Leviticus also says "on the eighth day" for them!
R. Yoḥanan
Circumcision must be performed during the day, not at night — that too is derived from "on the eighth day." So the verse serves a different function for them.
Reish Lakish
That point (day not night) is already derived from "on the day He commanded" (Lev 7:38). So your verse is still available for the Shabbat override — and the atonement-offering problem remains!
Gemara's Resolution An extra source is still needed for those lacking atonement: one might think the Torah shows them compassion by relaxing the offering's rules entirely — Ravina rebuts this by noting it would absurdly permit non-priests to sacrifice.

🩹 Circumcision vs. Leprosy: Competing Stringencies

Circumcision Overrides Leprosy

  • Foreskin cut even if bright white leprous spot present
  • Applies both at appointed time (8th day) and not at appointed time
  • Rava's kal v'chomer: Circumcision overrides Shabbat; Shabbat is more stringent than leprosy; ∴ circumcision certainly overrides leprosy
  • Abaye: Derived from common denominator of infant and adult — word flesh in both contexts

Leprosy's Counterclaim to Stringency

  • A leper may not serve in the Temple — leprosy overrides Temple service
  • Temple service overrides Shabbat (Shabbat offerings sacrificed at their time)
  • Rav Safra's challenge: By transitivity, leprosy may be MORE stringent than Shabbat — collapsing Rava's kal v'chomer
  • Rava's reply: Leprosy overrides Temple service only to protect the service's sanctity — not a sign of leniency toward the leper

📜 Rava's Structural Critique of the Baraita's Reasoning

Tanna's initial position: "On the eighth day he shall be circumcised" means circumcision proceeds even on Shabbat.
↓
Tanna's difficulty: But "He who desecrates it shall surely die" (Ex 31:14) seems to prohibit all labor!
↓
Tanna's resolution: "Shall be circumcised" overrides Shabbat; "shall die" refers to all other prohibited labors.
↓
Rava's critique: If the resolution was so obvious, what did the tanna ever find difficult? The objection never should have arisen.
↓
Restatement: The tanna actually relied on a kal v'chomer — leprosy overrides Temple service; Temple service overrides Shabbat; yet circumcision overrides leprosy — therefore circumcision overrides Shabbat.
↓
Counter-challenge ("or perhaps"): Shabbat may be MORE stringent than leprosy (capital punishment for Shabbat), invalidating the entire kal v'chomer chain.

✡️ Core Halakhic Ruling of the Daf

בַּיּוֹם הַשְּׁמִינִי יִמּוֹל בְּשַׂר עָרְלָתוֹ (ויקרא יב:ג)
Circumcision at Its Appointed Time Circumcision on the eighth day overrides both Shabbat and leprosy. The override of Shabbat applies only to circumcision performed at its appointed time; circumcision not at the appointed time does not override Shabbat. The override of leprosy applies in both cases — at the appointed time and not at the appointed time.
