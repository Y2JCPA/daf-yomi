Shabbat 149 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Beivai, why did the Rabbis prohibit reading a list of guests or menu items on Shabbat?
A. Lest one come to read business documents
B. Lest one erase the writing
C. Lest one carry the paper in a public domain
D. Lest one write new additions to the list
Correct: B — Rav Beivai says the decree is lest one erase something written, since erasing letters is a prohibited melacha on Shabbat.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the practical difference between 'lest one erase' and 'lest one read documents' when writing is engraved in a tablet?
A. Engraved writing is always forbidden under both opinions
B. Engraved writing is permitted under 'lest one erase' but still forbidden under 'lest one read documents'
C. Engraved writing is forbidden only if it contains Hebrew letters
D. There is no difference — both opinions prohibit engraved writing equally
Correct: B — Since one cannot erase an engraving, the 'lest one erase' concern does not apply and it would be permitted. But under the 'lest one read documents' opinion, engraved text still looks like a document and remains prohibited.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Meir permit using a mirror that is fixed in a wall on Shabbat?
A. A wall-mounted mirror cannot reflect clearly enough to see individual hairs
B. Fixed mirrors are classified as part of the building and not utensils
C. While going to fetch scissors, one will remember it is Shabbat and stop
D. Rabbi Meir holds that plucking hair is only rabbinically prohibited
Correct: C — Because the mirror is fixed, one must leave to find scissors. During that journey, one will remember it is Shabbat and refrain from cutting hair — the pause is the protective mechanism.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why is a father permitted to draw lots with his children at the Shabbat table, even for large versus small portions?
A. The Sages explicitly created an exemption for family games
B. All the food belongs to the father — there is no real transfer of ownership, making it educational
C. Children are not subject to rabbinic decrees about gambling
D. Lots for food are less severe than lots for money
Correct: B — Since all the food on the table is the father's property, no true change of ownership occurs. The lottery is used to educate his family in fairness, not as real gambling.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ya'akov son of the daughter of Ya'akov, what is the consequence for one who causes another to be punished on his account?
A. He loses his portion in the World to Come entirely
B. He must bring a guilt-offering to atone
C. He is not brought within the Divine partition (mechitza) of the Holy One
D. He is excommunicated from the Jewish community
Correct: C — Rabbi Ya'akov teaches that such a person is excluded from the Divine partition (mechitza). This is derived from Naboth's spirit, which served as a lying spirit to bring punishment upon Ahab — even this righteous act of vengeance cost Naboth exclusion from the innermost Divine presence.
