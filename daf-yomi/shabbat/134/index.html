🧪 Festival Preparations: Abaye Challenges Rav Yosef

Abaye
Cumin may be ground on a Festival because it is suitable for cooking. But then why not also mix wine and oil for circumcision salve?
Rav Yosef
There is a difference: cumin need not be beaten for use as a salve, whereas wine and oil must be beaten — and that beating is prohibited.
Abaye
Then prepare the mixture but simply do not beat it!
Rav Yosef
Without beating, the mixture is ineffective as a salve for the wound — it serves no purpose unbeaten.
Abaye
What about curdling cheese on a Festival? Kneading dough is permitted — why not cheese?
Rav Yosef
Kneading produces bread immediately for the Festival. Cheese takes time to mature — there is no immediate Festival benefit.
Abaye
But the Sages of Neharde'a say one-day-old cheese is excellent! So curds curdled today are usable today!
Rav Yosef
They mean: even aged cheese is good — all the more so fresh. But the curdling process itself still needs adequate time.
Festival Leniency Rule Melachot permitted on a Festival must yield immediate benefit for the Festival itself. Preparations with delayed benefit remain prohibited.

⚖️ Permitted vs. Prohibited: Shabbat & Festival Circumcision Care

✅ Permitted

  • Chewing cumin and placing it on circumcision as salve
  • Washing baby fully before and after circumcision (Day 1)
  • Sprinkling hot water by hand on Day 3 (Shabbat)
  • Full washing on Day 3 — per Rabbi Elazar ben Azarya (final ruling)
  • Placing dry compress or dry sponge on a wound
  • Pouring oil/hot water outside the wound so it flows in

❌ Prohibited

  • Grinding cumin on Shabbat (must chew instead)
  • Straining mustard through its strainer on a Festival
  • Sweetening raw mustard with coal on a Festival
  • Curdling cheese on a Festival
  • Constructing a bandage pouch for circumcision ab initio on Shabbat
  • Sprinkling water with any vessel on Shabbat
  • Circumcising a doubtful-timing or hermaphrodite baby on Shabbat

💧 The Third-Day Washing Dispute

הֲלָכָה כְּרַבִּי אֶלְעָזָר בֶּן עֲזַרְיָה
Mishna Dispute — Amud B First Tanna: One may wash the baby before and after circumcision. On Shabbat, only sprinkling by hand is permitted — not with any vessel. Rabbi Elazar ben Azarya: On the third day after circumcision that falls on Shabbat, one may wash the baby — implying full washing is permitted. Rava's Reading: Day 1 = full washing always permitted. Day 3 on Shabbat = sprinkling by hand only (First Tanna). Rabbi Elazar permits full washing even on Day 3. Final Halakha (Rav Dimi & Ravin, citing Rabbi Elazar): The halakha follows Rabbi Elazar ben Azarya — full body washing of the circumcised baby on the third day is permitted even on Shabbat.
