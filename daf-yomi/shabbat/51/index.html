🌾 The Tithing Intent Dispute

Rava
Intent while produce is still attached to the ground counts for tithing — proof from the mishna about hyssop planted for human consumption while still rooted
Rav Ashi
Rava's proof is inconclusive: the courtyard plants are presumed for human consumption by default — this is not genuine prior intent while attached
Rava
The mishna speaks of general categories of food, not every individual circumstance — 'any food of a type that requires tithing becomes impure'
Rav Shimi bar Ashi
Proof from a vineyard declared ownerless: grapes picked before the owner retracted are tithe-free — confirming the mishna discusses categories, vindicating Rava's framework
Underlying Question Do pe'a, tithe, and food-impurity obligations apply categorically (by type) or situationally (every instance)? The Gemara resolves: categorically — with exceptions like tereifa tested against this rule.

🐦 Bird Carcass: The Liquid Contact Requirement

Neveilat Of — carcass of kosher bird swallowed in throat
Renders the person's garments impure while in the throat
↓
Does it need contact with liquid to become susceptible to food-impurity?
↓
No — creeping animal contact
Bird carcass does not need contact with a creeping animal (שֶׁרֶץ) — it is inherently impure
Yes — liquid contact
It does require water or liquid contact to become susceptible to food-impurity
↓
School of Rabbi Yishmael: Seeds require liquid (Lev. 11:38) even though they never transmit severe impurity — all non-severe items must likewise need liquid contact first

🌿 Abaye, Rav Sheshet, and Rav Pappa — The A Fortiori Battle

Abaye
All the more so food-impurity should apply without thought! If severe impurity (bird carcass in throat) which doesn't spread further still needs no thought — how much more should ordinary food-impurity need no thought?
Rav Sheshet
No — the Rabbis reject Rabbi Yoḥanan ben Nuri's inference differently: severe impurity is lenient in other ways (doesn't pass to another person), so no inference can be drawn to ordinary food impurity
Rava / Rav Pappa
The Rabbis' rejection of Rabbi Yoḥanan ben Nuri isn't based on bird-carcass impurity at all — they are invoking an entirely different principle in their rebuttal
Core Issue Does food become susceptible to ritual impurity automatically, or does the owner's intent (מַחֲשָׁבָה) play a role? Multiple Amoraim debate which analogies validly apply.

🫚 Is Dill Considered Food? Three Perspectives

The Chain: Pe'a → Tithe → Impurity

  • Mishna: dill is obligated in pe'a (corner of field)
  • Rule: pe'a obligation implies tithe obligation
  • Rule (50a): tithe obligation implies food-impurity
  • ∴ Dill should contract food-impurity!

The Contradiction

  • Mishna (Okatzin 3:5): costus, asafoetida, peppers, and similar spices do NOT contract food-impurity
  • Rabbi Yoḥanan ben Nuri: if they can be bought with second-tithe money, why don't they become impure?
  • Final ruling: these spices may NOT be bought with tithe-money AND do not contract impurity

Rav Ḥisda / Rav Kahana's Resolution

  • Baraita refers to dill prepared as a spice dish (לִכְמָך) — used as actual food
  • Rav Kahana: once dill has imparted flavor in a pot, it is 'food'
  • Before imparting flavor: subject to teruma prohibition, can contract impurity
  • Dill used purely as a raw spice: neither tithe-obligated nor impurity-susceptible as food

🐟 Fish Kashrus — Fins, Scales, and the Word Kaskeset

CaseKosher StatusReasoning
Has scales (קַשְׂקֶשֶׂת)✅ KosherScales imply fins; Lev. 11:9 lists both signs
Has fins but no scales❌ Not kosherFins alone are insufficient
Has neither sign❌ Not kosherMissing required Torah signs
Why write both signs if scales imply fins?—If only 'scales' written, one might think kaskeset = fins, permitting fin-only fish
Proof kaskeset = scales (armor)—Goliath's armor described with this word (I Sam. 17:5) — overlapping plate-like scales
Why not write only 'scales'?—Rabbi Abbahu / school of Rabbi Yishmael: needed to exclude fish with scale-like structures only on fins

🙏 Blessings Before and After

כָּל הַטָּעוּן בְּרָכָה לְאַחֲרָיו טָעוּן בְּרָכָה לְפָנָיו
Mishna Rule Any item requiring a blessing after partaking requires a blessing before as well.

But the converse is not always true — some items require only a blessing before:
• Vegetables — blessing before eating, no blessing after
• Water (per Rav Pappa) — blessing before; debated whether a blessing after is required

The Gemara asks: what novel case does the mishna add? Vegetables — one might have thought no blessing is needed at all since they are not substantial food.
