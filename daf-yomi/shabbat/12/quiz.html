Shabbat 12 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Yehuda, must a woman examine herself before engaging in intercourse with her husband?
A. Yes — she is always required to examine herself before intercourse
B. No — she is not required to examine herself before intercourse
C. Only if she handles ritually pure items
D. Only if she does not have a fixed menstrual cycle
Correct: B — Rav Yehuda rules that a woman does not need to examine herself before intercourse. Rabbi Zeira raises the dilemma and receives this answer, though the Gemara continues to explore when examination is required.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the Gemara conclude about the length of the 'onset period' (שיעור וסת)?
A. It is enough time for a complete internal examination
B. It is only enough time for an external wipe — not a full internal examination
C. It varies depending on the individual woman
D. It equals the time required to immerse in a mikveh
Correct: B — The Gemara establishes from a baraita that the onset period is only the time needed for an external wipe. A full internal examination takes longer, so blood found after such an examination cannot be attributed to the onset period — eliminating the basis for a guilt-offering.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rabbi Ami say in the name of Rabbi Yannai about the examination performed before intercourse?
A. It is called the examination of purity (bedikah shel taharah)
B. It is called the examination of love/affection (bedikah shel ahavah)
C. It is called the examination of the Sages (bedikah de-Rabbanan)
D. It is prohibited as it needlessly delays a mitzvah
Correct: B — Rabbi Ami teaches that the pre-intercourse examination is called 'Bedikah shel Ahavah' — an examination of love — reflecting its character as an act of affection and care toward her husband. Rava then challenges the implications of calling it merely praiseworthy rather than obligatory.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the baraita, what must the husband of a woman with no fixed menstrual cycle do?
A. He may continue living with her as long as she examines herself before every act of intercourse
B. He must divorce her and is permanently prohibited from ever remarrying her
C. He must divorce her but may remarry her if she is subsequently cured
D. He must bring a guilt-offering for each act of intercourse
Correct: B — The baraita rules that the husband must divorce her and can never remarry her — even if she is later cured. The Gemara explores whether subsequent cure changes the ruling, noting that the husband may claim he would not have divorced her had he known she would recover.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Rabbi Ḥanina ben Antigonus's ruling about a woman who has no fixed menstrual cycle?
A. She is permanently forbidden from intercourse until her cycle becomes regular
B. She may engage in intercourse using two examination cloths — one before and one after
C. She must wait seven clean days before each act of intercourse
D. She is permitted without restriction, like a woman with a fixed cycle
Correct: B — Rabbi Ḥanina ben Antigonus permits intercourse for this woman provided she uses two examination cloths: one before intercourse (confirming current purity) and one after (checking for blood emission). The Gemara notes that Shmuel rules the halakha follows this lenient opinion.
