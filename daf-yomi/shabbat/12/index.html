🤔 The Pre- and Post-Intercourse Examination Debates

Rabbi Zeira
Must a woman examine herself before engaging in intercourse with her husband?
Rav Yehuda
She should not examine herself. [But why not — what is the harm in examining?]
Rabbi Abba
Must a woman examine herself immediately after intercourse — within the onset period — to render her husband liable to bring a provisional guilt-offering?
Rav Huna
Can you find a case where a full examination is even possible within that period? A baraita teaches the onset period is only enough time for an external wipe!
Key Conclusion The 'onset period' (שיעור וסת) equals only the time for an external wipe — not a full internal examination. Blood found after a full bedikah cannot be attributed to the onset period, so liability for a guilt-offering cannot be established this way.

💕 The Examination of Love (בְּדִיקַת אַהֲבָה)

בְּדִיקַת אַהֲבָה — Bedikah shel Ahavah
Rabbi Ami citing Rabbi Yannai The examination a woman performs before intercourse is called the Bedikah shel Ahavah — the examination of love and affection. It is an act of care toward her husband, confirming purity before intimacy. Rava's challenge: If one who skips this examination is merely 'not called virtuous,' the implication is they are also not called 'wicked' — meaning there is no prohibition. But this cannot be correct, as there are cases where the Sages genuinely require a pre-intercourse examination. The term 'examination of love' therefore denotes its ideal character, not that it is purely optional.

📋 Fixed Menstrual Cycle vs. No Fixed Cycle

Has a Fixed Cycle (וֶסֶת קָבוּעַ)

  • May engage in intercourse freely
  • If awake: required to examine before intercourse
  • If sleeping: husband may initiate — waking her for examination would demean her
  • Husband's return from travel = sufficient 'examination' (an even greater sign)
  • Retains all marital rights and financial obligations

No Fixed Cycle (אֵין לָהּ וֶסֶת)

  • Forbidden from intercourse until she examines herself
  • Forfeits right to marriage contract (ketubah) payment
  • Forfeits right to produce payments from her property
  • Forfeits provisions (mezonot) from husband's estate
  • Forfeits right to worn clothing
  • Husband must divorce her and can never remarry her

🧵 Rabbi Ḥanina ben Antigonus — The Two-Cloth Ruling

Woman has no fixed menstrual cycle — is intercourse ever permitted?
↓
Tanna Kamma (Rabbis)
Forbidden entirely
Too great a risk of transgression
Rabbi Ḥanina ben Antigonus
Permitted — with two examination cloths
↓
Two-cloth method: Cloth #1 before intercourse (confirms purity) · Cloth #2 after intercourse (checks for blood)
↓
Shmuel rules: Halakha follows Rabbi Ḥanina ben Antigonus
For a woman handling pure items, examination is also required before intercourse
