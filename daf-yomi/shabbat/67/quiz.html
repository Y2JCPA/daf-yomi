Shabbat 67 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, what is the specific requirement for the knife used in the fever incantation ritual?
A. It must be silver with an iron blade
B. It must be made entirely of iron, including the handle
C. Any sharp metal knife will suffice
D. It must be new and never previously used
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan specifies a knife made entirely of iron — including the handle — not merely the blade. This total-iron requirement appears to be part of the ritual's efficacy.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the nature of the dispute between Rabbi Meir and the Rabbis regarding the locust egg and fox tooth?
A. Rabbi Meir forbids them; the Rabbis permit them as effective remedies
B. Rabbi Meir permits carrying them on Shabbat for healing; the Rabbis forbid them even during the week
C. Both agree they are permitted on weekdays but dispute Shabbat use
D. The dispute only concerns the nail from the crucified, not the other items
Correct: B — Rabbi Meir permits going out with these items on Shabbat for therapeutic purposes. The Rabbis go further and forbid them even on weekdays, classifying them as Amorite superstition.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the unanimous ruling issued by both Abaye and Rava regarding the ways of the Amorite?
A. All Talmudic-era medical remedies are automatically permitted
B. Incantations are only permitted for rabbinically trained healers
C. Any practice containing an element of healing does not fall under the prohibition of Darkhei Emori
D. The ways of the Amorite only apply to practices adopted from non-Jews
Correct: C — Abaye and Rava both rule that if a practice contains an element of healing and appears effective, it does not constitute the forbidden 'ways of the Amorite' (Darkhei Emori).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why is it permitted to beat a fruit tree that is prematurely shedding its fruit, according to the Gemara's explanation?
A. Beating stimulates bark production and is thus medically effective
B. It is actually forbidden as a way of the Amorite
C. So that passersby will see the tree and pray for mercy on its behalf
D. Rabbi Meir permits it but the Rabbis dispute this ruling
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that beating the tree draws public attention so people will pray for mercy for it. The act has a recognized spiritual purpose, placing it outside the category of Amorite superstition.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which of the following practices is explicitly listed as a 'way of the Amorite' forbidden by the Sages?
A. Placing a chip of mulberry wood in a pot to help it cook faster
B. A woman dancing so that her kutaḥ preparation will be successful
C. Using a fish-bone incantation to dislodge a bone from the throat
D. Putting a lump of salt into a candle so it burns brightly
Correct: B — A woman dancing over her kutaḥ has no therapeutic or practical basis — it is pure superstition and is enumerated among the forbidden ways of the Amorite. The others are considered genuinely effective techniques.
