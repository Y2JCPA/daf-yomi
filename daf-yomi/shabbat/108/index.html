🐦 Is Bird Skin Valid for Tefillin?

Rav Huna
One may write phylacteries on the skin of a kosher bird. A bird has skin, therefore it qualifies as a valid writing surface.
Rabbi Zeira
Objection from the olah bird: The verse says 'by its wings' to teach the skin is separate from the flesh. If bird skin were ordinary skin, why state this explicitly?
Rabbi Zeira (alt.)
Actually, 'by its wings' comes to include the skin — proving bird skin is classified as skin. This supports Rav Huna!
Mar son of Ravina
What about fish skin for tefillin?
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzhak
If Elijah comes and declares it valid — valid. If invalid — invalid. The matter is halakhically unresolved.
Bottom Line Kosher animal hides: definitively permitted. Bird skin: permitted, with textual support. Fish skin: unresolved — awaiting Elijah.

✂️ Two Approaches to Deriving Circumcision's Location

Gezerah Shavah from Orla

  • Word orlato in circumcision verse (Lev. 12:3)
  • Same word orlato used for uncircumcised fruit of a tree
  • Just as tree-orla is at the tip, circumcision is performed at the tip of the foreskin
  • Rav taught this to Karna at the Malka River — later confirmed as tannaitic dispute

Derivation from 'Flesh' (Basar)

  • Alternative tannaitic view: derived from the word basar (flesh) in the circumcision verse
  • Cross-reference to another verse using basar specifying the precise location
  • Rav's encounter with Karna reveals this was a live tannaitic debate
  • The Gemara concludes: the issue is indeed a dispute between tanna'im

🏞️ Rav Arrives in Babylonia — The Malka River Encounter

Scene: Shmuel and Karna sit by the Malka River. The water rises and turns murky.
↓
Shmuel predicts: 'A great man comes from the West (Eretz Yisrael) — his intestines ache, and the river rises in his honor.' This man is Rav.
↓
Karna tests Rav with three halakhic questions: (1) tefillin on a lesion, (2) source for circumcision location, (3) why non-kosher hides are invalid for tefillin.
↓
Rav realizes he is being tested. He says: 'May it be God's will that a karna (horn) emerge in your eyes!' — a pointed curse on Karna.
↓
Shmuel hosts Rav: feeds him barley bread, small fried fish, and beer — and deliberately withholds directions to the lavatory.
↓
Medical intention: Shmuel was a physician. The discomfort induced diarrhea to relieve Rav's intestinal illness. Healing through strategic inconvenience.

🌊 The Dead Sea — Shabbat Questions & Wit Rav Dimi (arriving from Eretz Yisrael): 'No person has ever drowned in the Sea of Sodom (Dead Sea) — extreme salinity causes people to float.'

Rav Yosef retorts: 'Sodom is overturned — and so is Rav Dimi's statement!' (Humorous challenge to the claim.)

Ravin's Shabbat question: If one bathes in the Dead Sea on Shabbat, may he open and close his eyes underwater? Rabbi Yirmeya: 'I did not hear a ruling on this exact case' — but analogies from wine-on-the-eye vs. wine-in-the-eye rulings are invoked.

Mar Ukva citing Shmuel: One may soak eye salves from Shabbat eve and apply them on Shabbat — no healing violation.
