⚖️ When Is Discharged Semen Ritually Inert?

R. Elazar b. Azarya
A woman who discharges semen at any moment on the third day is ritually pure. Partial days count — day 3 morning suffices.
R. Yishmael & R. Akiva
We require six complete 12-hour periods (72 full hours). Day 3 morning is not enough — only from evening of day 3 onward.
R. Elazar b. Azarya
This is why Moses commanded only three days of separation at Sinai — my ruling satisfies the women's purity by any third-day moment.
Rabbis (majority)
Moses actually added a fourth day on his own initiative, so that all halakhic opinions would be fully satisfied before the Torah was given.
Core Dispute Does 'third day' mean any moment of day three, or 72 full hours from cohabitation?

⚖️ Third-Day Discharge: Two Opinions

R. Elazar ben Azarya

  • Third day = any moment on day 3
  • Partial days count toward the count
  • Discharge day 3 morning → ritually pure
  • Consistent with 'be ready for the third day' (Ex. 19:15)
  • Supports Moses's 3-day separation command

R. Yishmael & R. Akiva

  • Six complete 12-hour periods required
  • = 72 full hours must elapse
  • Day 3 morning is still too soon → impure
  • Only day 3 evening onward = pure
  • Why Moses's extra day was halakhically necessary

תיקו — תשבי יתרץ קושיות ובעיות
🤔 Unresolved Dilemma — תיקו Rav Pappa asks: What is the halakha for semen of a Jewish man in the womb of a gentile woman?

Argument for shorter period: Jews are meticulous about mitzvot → this concern causes their bodies to run hotter → semen decays faster → may become inert before three days elapse.

Argument for standard rule: The three-day halakha applies universally regardless of the woman's identity or the man's piety.

Rav Pappa raises the question but leaves it without resolution — recorded as an open halakhic dilemma.

🌙 Could the Women Have Immersed Earlier?

Questioner
Some women immersed on Shabbat eve (Friday night) to purify from semen discharge. They were already pure! Why delay Torah until Shabbat morning?
R. Yitzḥak
The verse says: 'From the third day, do not come near a woman' — implying the full third day must elapse before the Torah could be received.
Questioner
But couldn't they immerse at twilight on Shabbat eve and receive the Torah immediately at the start of Shabbat?
Gemara
The verse's phrasing demands the entire third day pass — twilight immersion is not sufficient; morning of Shabbat was the earliest valid moment.
Key Verse Exodus 19:15 — 'Be ready for the third day; do not approach a woman' (שְׁלֹשֶׁת יָמִים)
