Shabbat 113 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the first tanna of the Mishna, what may one use to tie a bucket on Shabbat?
A. A standard rope
B. A belt
C. Either rope or belt
D. Neither — all tying is forbidden on Shabbat
Correct: B — The Mishna permits tying a bucket with a belt (clearly temporary) but prohibits a rope, which may be left permanently. Rabbi Yehuda disagrees and permits rope as well.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rav Naḥman prohibit removing weaver's posts from the ground on Shabbat?
A. The posts are muktzeh and may not be handled
B. Removing them creates holes that one may come to smooth out in a house
C. They are too heavy to be carried without violating the Shabbat carrying limit
D. Weaver's work in all forms is forbidden on Shabbat
Correct: B — Rava argued incidental holes are not prohibited. Rav Naḥman replied: in a house (unlike a field), one may come to smooth the holes — so the Rabbis issued a preventive decree.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the school of Rabbi Yanai, when is folding garments on Shabbat prohibited?
A. When folding garments more than twice
B. When folding in preparation for after Shabbat ends
C. When two people fold the garments together
D. When the garments are white or formal
Correct: C — One person folding alone is permitted by the Mishna. Two people folding together resembles professional craftwork and is therefore prohibited — it restores creases too perfectly.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
From Isaiah 58:13 the Gemara derives that Shabbat speech differs from weekday speech. What is permitted regarding weekday matters on Shabbat?
A. Speaking about them only in private
B. Thinking about weekday affairs (but not speaking of them)
C. Speaking only about essential business, not leisure
D. Neither speaking nor thinking — both are forbidden
Correct: B — The verse forbids 'speaking' of weekday affairs. Since only speech is mentioned, the Gemara concludes that merely thinking about weekday matters is permitted on Shabbat.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What specific act of modesty did Ruth perform while gleaning that the baraita says impressed Boaz?
A. She gleaned only in a designated corner of the field
B. She covered her face and did not look at the male workers
C. She picked upright stalks while standing and fallen stalks while sitting, avoiding immodest bending
D. She gleaned only after all harvesters had completed their work
Correct: C — The baraita teaches that Ruth picked upright stalks standing and fallen stalks sitting — her careful modesty in avoiding bending was what caught Boaz's attention and admiration.
