🪢 The Great Knot Debate: Belt vs. Rope

First Tanna
One may tie a bucket with a belt on Shabbat — temporary. But one may not tie with a rope — it may become permanent.
Rabbi Yehuda
One may tie a bucket even with a rope on Shabbat. Intent is temporary — he will untie it — so it is permitted.
Gemara (problem)
Contradiction! In the baraita, Rabbi Yehuda prohibits even a bow-knot on a bucket rope (decree against tying). Yet in the mishna he permits the rope entirely?
Resolution
Mishna = one's own rope, no confusion possible. Baraita = communal/borrowed rope that may be confused with another. Different contexts, different decrees.
📌 Core Principle A knot tied with intent to undo it (temporary) is permitted on Shabbat. A knot intended to remain indefinitely (permanent) is forbidden as a מְלָאכָה.

🪢 Permitted vs. Forbidden Knots on Shabbat

✅ Permitted (Temporary)

  • Tying a bucket with a belt
  • Tying a cow to a trough with rope from home
  • A single bow-knot (according to Rabbis in baraita)
  • Wrapping a hollow reed around a severed rope (R. Yehuda)
  • Any knot one will certainly undo that same day

❌ Forbidden (Permanent)

  • Tying a bucket with a standard rope (per Rabbis)
  • A full regular knot on a severed bucket rope
  • Any knot intended to remain permanently
  • Even a bow-knot on a borrowed rope (baraita decree)

📜 Rabbi Yehuda's Position

  • Permits rope on bucket — temporary in intent
  • Own rope: no confusion → no decree needed
  • Borrowed rope: stricter (baraita) — confusion possible
  • Context and ownership determine the ruling

🧵 Moving Weaver's Tools on Shabbat

Are weaver's tools permitted to move on Shabbat?
↓
✅ General Tools
Shmuel: Weaver's tools may be moved — they are functional implements (כְּלִי) with permitted use
❓ Upper & Lower Beams?
Dilemma raised to Rav Yehuda: do the large loom beams share this leniency?
❌ Posts Fixed in Ground
Posts embedded in the floor may not be removed — creating holes is the concern
↓
Rava challenges Rav Naḥman: Holes formed incidentally are not prohibited — 'holes come into being on their own!' Why the decree?
↓
Rav Naḥman answers: In a field one will not fill holes. In a house one may come to smooth them out — rabbinic decree applies to prevent this!

👕 Laws of Folding Garments on Shabbat

Mishna permits: One may fold garments even four or five times on Shabbat — removing and refolding is allowed.
↓
School of Rabbi Yanai restricts: Only when one person folds alone. Two people folding together is prohibited.
↓
Why forbidden for two? Two people folding together resembles craftsman's professional work — it restores creases perfectly, constituting forbidden labor.
↓
Rabban Gamliel's household (Tosefta): Did not fold even their white garments — because they had spare garments to change into, folding was unnecessary.
↓
Rav Huna rules: If one has new garments to change into on Shabbat, one should not fold the current garments at all.
↓
Bottom line: Folding is permitted only when genuinely needed for Shabbat use. One may not fold on Shabbat in preparation for after Shabbat.

🗣️ Shabbat Speech & Walking — Isaiah 58:13

Isaiah 58:13
"If you keep your feet from breaking Shabbat, from pursuing your affairs on My holy day… and you honor it by not doing your own ways, nor pursuing your affairs, nor speaking of them"
Gemara derives
Walking: Your walking on Shabbat should not be like weekday walking.
Speech: Your speech on Shabbat should not be like weekday speech — do not discuss business affairs.
Rava (strong objection)
If jumping constitutes 'prohibited Shabbat walking,' then what should one do when encountering a stream? They would be completely stuck! This rule cannot extend that far.
Resolution
Speech about weekday matters is prohibited. But merely thinking about weekday affairs is permitted — the verse says 'speaking,' not 'thinking' (דִּבֵּר, not חָשַׁב).
📖 Hebrew Source אִם תָּשִׁיב מִשַּׁבָּת רַגְלֶךָ… וְכִבַּדְתּוֹ מֵעֲשׂוֹת דְּרָכֶיךָ — Isaiah 58:13
