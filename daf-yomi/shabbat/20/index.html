🩸 The Five Impure Blood Shades and Their Standards

שחור / Black

  • Standard: dry ink (ḥeret)
  • Examined on white linen cloth
  • Deeper = impure; lighter = pure (Ulla)

כרכום / Saffron

  • Standard: crocus flower
  • Middle leaf of the middle layer
  • Must be fresh and in its natural clumps

מי אדמה / Earth Water

  • Standard: Beit Kerem Valley earth
  • Water rises one handbreadth above earth
  • Mixed with a vessel — not by hand

יין מזוג / Diluted Wine

  • Standard: 2 parts water, 1 part wine
  • Specifically from the Sharon region
  • Bar Kappara: deeper than this shade = pure

⚖️ Disputed Ruling: What Happens Beyond the Standard Shade?

Ulla
For all five blood types: deeper than the standard = impure; lighter than the standard = pure. The mishna explicitly states this for black — to teach you would not have assumed it, since black may seem like discolored red.
Rabbi Ami bar Abba
Deeper = impure. But lighter is also impure — any deviation from the exact pure shade in either direction renders the blood impure.
Rami bar Abba
I hold the opposite: deeper than the standard = pure; lighter than the standard = impure. The described shade is the threshold — only at or below it is blood suspect.
Bar Kappara
Deeper = impure; lighter = pure — except for diluted wine, where a shade deeper than wine-color is actually pure. Only wine-color and lighter shades are impure.
Why Mention Black Separately? Since Rabbi Ḥanina teaches that black blood is merely discolored red, one might assume it is pure like faded red. The mishna explicitly rules it impure to override that reasoning.

🌸 How to Examine Saffron-Colored Blood (Karkom)

Obtain fresh karkom (crocus/saffron) flowers — Rabbi Abbahu insists they must still be in their natural clumps of earth, not dried or detached
↓
The crocus has three layers of colored parts (styles/stigmas harvested for saffron), stacked one above the other
↓
Each layer contains three leaves — an upper, middle, and lower leaf within that layer
↓
Locate the middle layer — not the innermost, not the outermost of the three layers
↓
Take the middle leaf of that middle layer — this is the precise halakhic standard
↓
Hold the leaf next to the blood sample and compare: if they match in color, the blood is impure. This single standard resolves four contradictory baraitot.

Obtain fertile earth from the Beit Kerem Valley — the specific source required by halakha
↓
Place the earth into a vessel (not in your hand)
↓
Pour water until it rises one handbreadth above the earth — no more, no less
↓
Do not muddy it by hand — use a vessel only

👑 Ifera Hurmiz and the Limits of Expertise

אִיפְרָא הוֹרְמִיז אִימֵּיהּ דְּשָׁבוּר מַלְכָּא — שַׁדְרָה דְּמָא לְקַמֵּיהּ דְּרָבָא
The Greatest Blood Examiner Ifera Hurmiz, mother of the Persian King Shapur, sought to convert to Judaism and began practicing the laws of menstruation. She sent blood to Rava for examination; Rav Ovadya, sitting nearby, watched as Rava identified each sample. She then sent Rava sixty different types of blood — some impure, some pure. Rava accurately identified every single one. The final sample was lice blood, which Rava also correctly identified. This story illuminates why Rabbi Elazar was called "master of Eretz Yisrael in wisdom" — and why Ulla refused to examine blood in Pumbedita, saying: If Rabbi Elazar himself would decline here, how can I presume to rule?
