QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Mar Zutra's final ruling, how may one dissolve mustard that was kneaded on Shabbat eve?
A. Only with a vessel — hand is forbidden
B. Only by hand — a vessel is forbidden
C. Either by hand or with a vessel — both are permitted
D. Neither method is permitted on Shabbat
Correct: C — Mar Zutra ruled that the halakha follows none of the disputed positions. Both dissolving by hand and dissolving with a vessel are permitted for mustard kneaded on Shabbat eve.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Shmuel reject Rav's prohibition on dissolving mustard by hand?
A. Mustard is not considered real food and has no Shabbat restrictions
B. Dissolving with the hand every day would make the mustard 'donkey food' — so it is clearly not the normal method
C. The hand is always the preferred method for preparing spices
D. Rav's ruling only applied in Babylonia, not in Eretz Yisrael
Correct: B — Shmuel argued that if dissolving by hand were truly the normal method, no one would eat it — it would be fit only for donkeys. Therefore, it is not the standard weekday method, and no prohibition applies.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the key distinction between anumlin (permitted) and aluntit (forbidden) on Shabbat?
A. Anumlin is served hot; aluntit is served cold
B. Anumlin uses wine; aluntit uses vinegar
C. Anumlin is a beverage prepared for drinking pleasure; aluntit is a medicinal/therapeutic preparation
D. Anumlin may only be made on Shabbat eve; aluntit may be made on Shabbat
Correct: C — The Rabbis prohibited aluntit because its preparation is therapeutic (refuah). Anumlin — wine, honey, and pepper — is an ordinary drinking beverage and is therefore permitted on Shabbat.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Abaye disagreed with Rav Adda bar Naresh about someone who accidentally soaked asafoetida on Shabbat. What was Abaye's position?
A. The person is fully liable for a Torah-level sin and must bring a sin-offering
B. The person has done nothing wrong at all — soaking is entirely permitted
C. The act is only a rabbinic violation — soaking in water is not a Torah-level prohibited labor
D. The person must fast but need not bring a sacrifice
Correct: C — Abaye argued: if soaking meat in water does not make one liable, soaking asafoetida cannot either. No Torah-level labor (like cooking) is performed by soaking. It is only rabbinically prohibited so one won't act as on a weekday.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What was the lesson Rav Ḥisda taught his daughters by showing them a pearl immediately but withholding the clod of earth?
A. Material wealth should always be displayed proudly to attract a good marriage
B. That which is hidden and modest is more precious and desirable than what is freely shown
C. Pearls are permitted as jewelry on Shabbat; earth is muktzeh
D. A wife should always give her husband gifts before asking for anything
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda demonstrated that the withheld clod aroused far more curiosity than the freely shown pearl. His lesson to his daughters: modesty and concealment create greater value and attract more desire than open display.
