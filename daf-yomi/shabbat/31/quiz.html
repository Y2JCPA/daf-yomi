Shabbat 31 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the baraita on three partners in creation, which body parts does the father's white seed contribute?
A. Skin, flesh, blood, and hair
B. The soul, speech, and understanding
C. Bones, sinews, nails, and whites of the eyes
D. Pupils of the eyes and facial expression
Correct: C — The father's white seed forms the hard/structural components: bones, sinews, nails, brain, and the whites of the eyes. The mother contributes skin, flesh, blood, hair, and pupils.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rabbi Yitzḥak citing Rabbi Ami: if the woman emits seed first at conception, what is the result?
A. A female child
B. A male child
C. Twins
D. The child's sex is undetermined
Correct: B — "If a woman emits seed first, she gives birth to a male" — derived from Leviticus 12:2. The Sons of Ulam practiced this deliberately to produce male children.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does a woman bring an offering after childbirth, according to the students of Rabbi Shimon ben Yoḥai?
A. To give thanks for surviving the danger of labor
B. To atone for intercourse during pregnancy
C. Because during intense labor pain she swore never to have intercourse again, and the offering addresses that oath
D. To purify herself from the ritual impurity of childbirth
Correct: C — The pain of labor is so intense that women impulsively swear never to have intercourse with their husbands again. The post-birth offering comes to atone for/dissolve that oath.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Meir, why does the Torah require seven days of separation for a menstruating woman?
A. Because the blood itself remains dangerous for seven days
B. So that after the separation, the wife will be as beloved to her husband as at the wedding canopy
C. To align with the seven days of creation
D. Because immersion requires seven days of preparation
Correct: B — Rabbi Meir: without periodic separation, a husband would grow overly familiar with his wife. The seven-day pause renews their desire for each other, making her as cherished as at the time of their marriage.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
During which trimester does the baraita say intercourse is beneficial for BOTH the woman and the offspring?
A. First three months
B. Middle three months
C. Last three months
D. All three equally
Correct: C — In the last three months, the fetus occupies the upper compartment and is preparing to emerge. Intercourse at this stage is said to be beneficial for both mother and child, producing a robust baby.
