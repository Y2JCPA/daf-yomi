QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabba, what is the halakha regarding using a vessel whose primary function is prohibited on Shabbat?
A. It is always prohibited to use or move such a vessel
B. It is permitted to use it for a permitted action, utilizing the object itself
C. It is permitted only if it is also a food vessel
D. It may only be moved to use its place, never to use the object itself
Correct: B — Rabba holds that using a prohibited-primary-use vessel to perform a permitted action (using the object itself) is permitted. This is the majority view, as opposed to Rabbi Neḥemya who restricts movement to designated use only.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rabba say the Tosefta about the mortar (only movable with garlic) does not contradict his ruling?
A. Because mortars are not considered utensils
B. Because that Tosefta reflects the opinion of Rabbi Neḥemya, a minority stringent view
C. Because the Tosefta refers to a case on Yom Kippur, not Shabbat
D. Because garlic is muktze and changes the mortar's status
Correct: B — Rabba explains that the Tosefta follows Rabbi Neḥemya's strict position that a vessel may only be moved for its designated use. Since this is a minority view, it does not challenge Rabba's majority ruling.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rava's ruling regarding a needle whose eye AND point have both been removed?
A. It may be moved freely on Shabbat since it can still extract thorns
B. It may not be moved on Shabbat since it is no longer considered a utensil
C. It may only be moved by a rabbi who rules on its status
D. It becomes more stringently muktze than an ordinary rock
Correct: B — Rava holds the objection valid: since such a needle is no longer a 'vessel' for purposes of ritual impurity (tumah), it logically also loses vessel status for Shabbat and may not be moved as a utensil.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Abaye, what were the second and third stages in the historical expansion of muktze leniency?
A. Stage 2: food vessels; Stage 3: all vessels
B. Stage 2: prohibited-use vessels for the object itself; Stage 3: all vessels even to use their place
C. Stage 2: vessels in a reshut hayachid; Stage 3: vessels in a reshut harabim
D. Stage 2: vessels belonging to Torah scholars; Stage 3: all vessels
Correct: B — Abaye describes: Stage 1 permitted primary-permitted vessels for their object; Stage 2 added prohibited-primary-use vessels for their object; Stage 3 (Rava) extended this to moving any utensil to use its place.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Yosei rule that the large saw and plow blade may NOT be moved on Shabbat?
A. They are used for biblically prohibited labors on Shabbat and are therefore inherently muktze
B. Their owners mentally set them aside out of concern that the tools might be damaged
C. They are too large and heavy to carry in a normal manner on Shabbat
D. They are not considered utensils (כלים) under Jewish law
Correct: B — Rabbi Yosei establishes the category of muktze machmat chesron kis: tools like the large saw and plow blade whose owners set them aside mentally because they fear damage — thus rendering them prohibited to move on Shabbat.
