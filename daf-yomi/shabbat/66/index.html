⚖️ Who Permits the Wooden Leg on Shabbat?

Rava
How do we read the mishna? Does Rabbi Meir permit the wooden leg and Rabbi Yosei prohibit it — or the reverse?
Shmuel
The correct reading: An amputee may NOT go out — and Rabbi Yosei permits him to do so.
Rav Ḥanan bar Rava
I taught this mishna before Rav himself in the school: May not go out — this is the view of Rabbi Meir. Rabbi Yosei permits.
Gemara
Both Shmuel and Rav Huna ultimately reversed their opinions. Final ruling: Rabbi Meir prohibits, Rabbi Yosei permits.
Key Resolution The mishna's author order was disputed until Rav Ḥanan bar Rava's eyewitness testimony settled it. Rabbi Meir does NOT consider the wooden leg a shoe; Rabbi Yosei DOES.

🪵 Three Devices — Three Legal Statuses

Wooden Leg

  • Rabbi Meir: prohibited on Shabbat — not a shoe
  • Rabbi Yosei: permitted — it functions as a shoe
  • With pad-receptacle → status of a wooden vessel
  • Abaye: receives corpse impurity, not treading impurity
  • Rava: receives both — he leans full weight on it

Knee Supports (אִסְקוּפָה)

  • Worn on knees by bilateral amputees
  • Subject to treading impurity if worn by a zav
  • Prohibited on Shabbat
  • Cannot enter the Temple courtyard with them
  • Rabbi Yoḥanan: they ARE shoes — ḥalitza is valid with them

Loketamin

  • Wooden toy shaped like a donkey (R. Abbahu)
  • Worn on shoulders — creates illusion donkey rides you
  • Ritually pure — not a functional vessel
  • Prohibited on Shabbat nonetheless
  • Not a walking device → no treading impurity possible

🌿 Abaye's Foster Mother — Remedies for Fever (Shabbat 66b)

Step 1 — Madder Garlands: 3 garlands maintain the illness • 5 garlands heal it • 7 are effective even against witchcraft
↓
Condition: Do not look at the sun or moon • Do not see rain • Do not hear clanging iron or a rooster's crow while wearing the garlands
↓
Step 2 — One-Day Fever: Take a newly-minted pale dinar → go to salt pools → weigh equal salt → bind the salt at the neckline of his garment with a white thread twisted upward
↓
Step 3 — If Fever Persists: Sit at a crossroads → find a large ant carrying something → place in a copper tube → seal with sixty seals → shake, lift, and say: 'Your burden upon me and my burden upon you'
↓
Step 4 — Last Resort: Take a new jug to the river → say 'River, lend me water for a guest' → circle head seven times → pour water behind you without looking back
↓
General Shabbat Rulings: Oil-and-salt rub ✓ • Strangling/bandaging a dislocated neck vertebra ✓ • Tight swaddling of a newborn to align limbs ✓

🪢 What Are the 'Knots' Young Boys Wear on Shabbat?

Rav Yehuda
The knots are garlands of the madder plant — a medicinal remedy worn by children for healing ailments.
Abaye
But why only boys? Girls benefit from madder too! And why young boys specifically? This explanation doesn't account for the mishna's precision.
Avin bar Huna
The knots are a strap from the right shoe tied to the boy's left arm — a remedy for a son with longing for his father who has difficulty separating.
Gemara
This fits perfectly: it applies specifically to young boys who long for their fathers — not girls, not older children. The mishna's wording is fully explained.
Halakhic Bottom Line These knots are permitted carrying on Shabbat because they serve a remedial function and are worn as a body accessory — not carried as a burden. The mishna reflects prevalent practice among young children.
