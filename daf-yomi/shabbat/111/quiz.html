QUESTION 1 OF 5
What does the Mishna permit for someone with tooth pain on Shabbat?
A. Sipping vinegar directly through the teeth
B. Dipping food in vinegar and eating it in the normal manner
C. Swishing vinegar around the mouth and spitting it out
D. Applying vinegar with a cloth to the painful tooth
Correct: B — The Mishna permits dipping food in vinegar and eating normally — because this is the person's usual manner of eating and the healing is incidental. Sipping vinegar directly is forbidden due to its obvious medicinal intent.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rava's 'Since' (הואיל) principle as applied in this daf?
A. Since an act is permitted in private, it is permitted in public
B. Since an act is permitted before a triggering event, it remains permitted afterward
C. Since a majority of Sages agree, the minority is overruled
D. Since Shabbat is more stringent than Yom Tov, its rules govern
Correct: B — Rava's הואיל principle holds that prior permission extends forward past a trigger point — e.g., since immersion is permitted on Shabbat, it remains permitted on Yom Kippur. However, Rava retracted this for sipping vinegar on Shabbat.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does 'halakha like Rabbi Shimon but not for his reason' mean regarding rose oil?
A. Rabbi Shimon's unintentional-act principle is universally accepted
B. Rose oil is permitted because it is normally cosmetic, not because of Rabbi Shimon's general principle
C. Rabbi Shimon's ruling only applies on Yom Tov, not Shabbat
D. Rav disagreed with Rabbi Shimon but the later Sages overruled Rav
Correct: B — The Gemara accepts Rabbi Shimon's conclusion (smearing rose oil is permitted) but on different grounds: rose oil is a normal cosmetic and its medicinal use is not apparent to observers — not because unintentional acts are categorically permitted.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
For which knots is one liable to bring a sin-offering on Shabbat?
A. Any knot requiring two hands to tie
B. Bow knots on garments, as ruled by Rav Aḥadvoy
C. A camel driver's knot and a sailor's knot — permanent, professionally crafted knots
D. Any knot that cannot be untied within one hour
Correct: C — The Mishna specifies that only a camel driver's knot (on the nose ring) and a sailor's knot (on the ship's ring) incur liability — because they are permanent knots tied with professional craft. Rabbi Meir adds that any knot undoable with one hand is not liable.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What internal contradiction does the Gemara identify in the Mishna about knots (111b)?
A. The Mishna says sailor's knots are both liable and permitted in different clauses
B. The Mishna introduces knots that are prohibited yet not liable, then immediately lists prohibited examples without sufficient explanation
C. The Mishna contradicts the baraita about bow knots
D. The Mishna permits untying knots but forbids tying them, which the Gemara finds inconsistent
Correct: B — The Gemara notes that the Mishna opens by asserting that some knots are not liable for a sin-offering (implying they might be permitted), but then immediately lists examples that are nonetheless prohibited — creating an apparent internal tension that requires resolution.
