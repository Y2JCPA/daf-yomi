QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara instruct a minor wife to perform mi'un (refusal) against her yavam-husband?
A. Because minors legally cannot perform halitzah and need an alternative
B. So the surviving brother can then perform yibum with the minor's adult sister
C. So she can remarry outside the family without restriction
D. To exempt all the brother's wives from levirate obligations entirely
Correct: B — The mi'un dissolves the minor's marriage to the surviving brother, freeing him to perform yibum with her adult sister — the widow of the childless deceased brother whom he is obligated to marry.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara deflect the challenge that havdala-in-Amida should be a 5th case where halakha follows Rabbi Eliezer?
A. Rabbi Eliezer only applied this ruling on Yom Kippur, not weekly Shabbat
B. The ruling was actually stated by Rabbi Eliezer in the name of Rabbi Ḥanina ben Gamliel
C. Havdala in the Amida is universally agreed upon — it is not a disputed ruling
D. Rabbi Elazar later retracted this case from his original list
Correct: B — Rabbi Abba explains that this ruling was not Rabbi Eliezer's own independent position — he cited it in the name of Rabbi Ḥanina ben Gamliel. Therefore it does not count among the cases where halakha follows Rabbi Eliezer specifically.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Sumakhos, after how long is a pregnancy publicly recognized, and what is his proof-text?
A. Two months — derived from the laws of niddah tumah
B. Four months — from the story of Rivka's pregnancy in Genesis
C. Three months — from Genesis 38:24, 'after three months' Tamar's pregnancy was reported
D. Seven months — the minimum for a viable birth
Correct: C — Sumakhos derives three months from Genesis 38:24, where Tamar's pregnancy was reported to Judah 'after three months' — the point at which the pregnancy became evident. Rabbi Meir notes this is only an allusion (remez), but the three-month threshold stands.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ḥanina son of Rav Ika, why is the niddah mishna's 'virgin' (one who never saw blood) excluded from the baraita's list of three virgins?
A. A niddah virgin has a modifier in her definition — 'who has not seen blood' — making her different from the unqualified virgins in the list
B. Menstrual blood eventually comes to a woman, meaning she will later revert from her virgin-blood status
C. The baraita's list only includes items relevant to monetary law, not personal status
D. A woman's virginity is already discussed elsewhere and need not be repeated
Correct: B — Rabbi Ḥanina son of Rav Ika explains: the list includes only items that will NOT later revert to their original state. Since menstrual blood will eventually come to a woman, her 'never-seen-blood' status is temporary — so it is excluded from the baraita's definitive list.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What physical characteristic identifies 'virgin ground' (בתולת קרקע) according to the baraita, and what ritual requires it?
A. Ground covered in untouched vegetation, required for the red heifer (parah adumah) purification
B. Rocky ground near a water source, required for the sotah ritual
C. Ground that raises hard clumps with no loose dirt and no earthenware sherds, required for the eglah arufah (broken-necked heifer)
D. Sandy ground that has never held water, required for drawing water for a metzora
Correct: C — The baraita defines virgin ground as land that raises hard clumps and has no loose dirt. If earthenware sherds are found within it, the ground was previously worked. This is relevant to the eglah arufah ritual (Deuteronomy 21), which requires a nachal eitan — a rough stream running through untilled, virgin land.
