QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, when is a coffin made by a gentile on Shabbat permitted for a Jewish burial?
A. When the city has a majority of gentile residents
B. When the gentile originally made it for one Jew but then changed his mind and offered it to another
C. When the coffin materials were prepared before Shabbat began
D. When a rabbi grants explicit post-Shabbat permission
Correct: B — Shmuel holds the prohibition is based on specific intent toward a named Jew. If the gentile changed his mind, the original prohibition lapses and any Jew may be buried in it — a ruling supported by the mishna's precise wording.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Which action is explicitly FORBIDDEN when caring for a corpse on Shabbat?
A. Smearing oil on the body
B. Rinsing the body with water
C. Moving any of the corpse's limbs
D. Tying the jaw to prevent it from opening further
Correct: C — Moving the corpse's limbs is forbidden because a corpse is muktzeh (set-aside); moving its limbs constitutes a Shabbat violation. Smearing oil, rinsing with water, and tying the jaw are all explicitly permitted.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
The Gemara compares closing a dying person's eyes to which act?
A. Blowing out a fully lit candle
B. Placing a finger on a lamp that is gradually going out
C. Pouring water on burning coals
D. Removing oil from a lamp before it finishes burning
Correct: B — A lamp gradually going out still has a little life left in it — placing one's finger on it immediately extinguishes it. So too, closing a dying person's eyes hastens the moment of death, making the one who does so liable as a murderer.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel's ruling regarding a living day-old baby on Shabbat?
A. One does not desecrate Shabbat for a baby fewer than 30 days old
B. One desecrates Shabbat to save the baby's life
C. One may instruct a gentile to help but may not directly violate Shabbat
D. The ruling depends on whether the baby was born at full term
Correct: B — Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel teaches that a living day-old baby has full sanctity of life — Shabbat is desecrated to save him. By contrast, even the great King David, once dead, does not override Shabbat.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What did Rabbi Ḥiyya tell his wife when a poor person came to their door?
A. Give generously so that God will bless the household with wealth
B. Be quick to give bread to the poor so that others will be quick to give bread to your children
C. Always give a tenth of your bread as charity before eating
D. Give only what you can spare so that your own children do not go hungry
Correct: B — Rabbi Ḥiyya's instruction reflects the Gemara's theme that poverty can befall any family's descendants. By acting quickly to help the poor now, one cultivates the merit that will cause others to help one's own children swiftly in their time of need.
