🤰 Why Is a Pregnant Woman Not Retroactively Impure?

Rav Pappi
The miscarriage is not a proper birth — therefore blood during labor contractions is pure (dam tohar), not zava blood.
Rav Pappa
The reason is that her head and limbs feel heavy. This physical burden disrupts normal menstruation, so we don't impose retroactive impurity.
Rabbi Yirmeya (asks)
What if she saw blood before her pregnancy was visible to anyone? At that moment, no one knew she was pregnant — does her leniency apply?
Rabbi Zeira (answers)
Since she did not yet know she was pregnant, her limbs were not yet heavy at the time of bleeding. The leniency does not apply — she transmits retroactive impurity.
Key Rule The pregnant woman's leniency depends on whether her physical symptoms were already present at the moment she saw blood. Unaware pregnancy = no exemption.

🍼 Nursing Woman: How Long Is Her Time Sufficient?

Rabbi Meir

  • Menstrual blood spoils and becomes milk
  • Time sufficient for as long as she nurses
  • Even if she nurses 4 or 5 years
  • Leniency ends when she stops nursing
  • If child dies: leniency ends immediately

R. Yehuda, R. Yosei, R. Shimon

  • Her limbs become dislocated at birth
  • Spirit does not return for 24 months
  • Time sufficient for exactly 24 months
  • After 24 months: full retroactive impurity resumes
  • Nursing beyond 24 months does not extend leniency

Practical Difference

  • Child dies at month 20:
    R. Meir — impurity resumes immediately
  • Rabbis — still in 24-month window, time sufficient
  • Still nursing at month 26:
    R. Meir — still lenient
  • Rabbis — 24-month window expired, full impurity

👵 Elderly Woman — Acquiring and Losing Special Status

Woman nears old age — people call her 'Imma, Imma' without embarrassment
↓
She goes three full menstrual cycles (~90 days) without seeing blood
↓
She sees blood again — what is her status?
↓
Rabbi Eliezer
Time is sufficient — three missed cycles establishes her as an elderly woman. No retroactive impurity.
The Rabbis
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi initially ruled like R. Eliezer in an emergency, then retracted. No final ruling issued — the question remains unresolved.

💦 The Parah Adumah Paradox — Beyond All Wisdom

אָמַרְתִּי אֶחְכָּמָה וְהִיא רְחוֹקָה מִמֶּנִּי — קֹהֶלֶת ז:כג
חֻקַּת הַתּוֹרָה — The Unknowable Statute The Gemara digresses to the water of purification (מֵי נִדָּה — parah adumah): • The person sprinkled upon becomes pure • The person who sprinkles becomes impure Rabbi Yoḥanan: The verse refers to semen — normally impure, yet the one referenced becomes tahor in a specific sense. Rabbi Elazar: The verse refers to the water of sprinkling — the very act that purifies also conveys impurity to its handler. The Gemara concludes: Even King Solomon — wisest of all men — declared of this law: "I said I would become wise, but it eludes me."
