Shabbat 9 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, why is a pregnant woman not retroactively impure when she sees blood?
A. Her fetus is not considered a proper birth
B. Her head and limbs feel heavy, which disrupts her normal menstrual cycle
C. Pregnancy blood is always classified as dam tohar (pure blood)
D. She is exempt until after she performs a ritual immersion
Correct: B — Rav Pappa's reason is that 'her head and limbs feel heavy' — the physical burden of pregnancy disrupts normal menstruation, so we do not impose retroactive impurity.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rabbi Meir explains that a nursing woman's time is sufficient because:
A. A nursing woman's hormones biologically suppress her cycle
B. Her limbs are dislocated for 24 months after birth
C. Menstrual blood spoils and transforms into milk during nursing
D. She has the same status as an elderly woman while nursing
Correct: C — Rabbi Meir holds that menstrual blood literally spoils and becomes milk. Therefore, as long as she nurses, her blood is 'occupied' as milk, and she does not menstruate normally.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda, Rabbi Yosei, and Rabbi Shimon, for how long is a nursing woman's time sufficient?
A. For as long as she continues nursing the child
B. For eighteen months after birth
C. For exactly twenty-four months after birth
D. Until the child is weaned or reaches age three
Correct: C — These Sages hold that a nursing woman's limbs are dislocated and her spirit does not fully return for 24 months. After 24 months, the leniency expires regardless of whether she still nurses.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If a woman saw menstrual blood before her pregnancy was visible to anyone (including herself), what does Rabbi Zeira rule?
A. She is still lenient because she was factually pregnant at the time
B. She transmits full retroactive impurity since her limbs were not yet heavy
C. She must wait until after birth to determine her status retroactively
D. She follows the lenient ruling since a doubt about impurity is resolved leniently
Correct: B — Rabbi Zeira rules that the pregnant woman's leniency depends on her physical symptoms being present at the time of bleeding. If she didn't yet know she was pregnant, her limbs weren't heavy, so the leniency does not apply.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The Gemara invokes King Solomon's verse 'I said I would become wise, but it eludes me' (Kohelet 7:23) in reference to which topic?
A. The complex dispute about the length of the nursing period
B. The elderly woman's uncertain halakhic status
C. The paradox of purification water that purifies the impure but defiles the one who sprinkles it
D. The question of whether a miscarriage constitutes a proper birth
Correct: C — The parah adumah paradox — where the same water simultaneously purifies the impure person yet defiles the ritually pure person who sprinkles it — is classified as a divine chok (unexplainable statute). Even King Solomon confessed this law was beyond his comprehension.
