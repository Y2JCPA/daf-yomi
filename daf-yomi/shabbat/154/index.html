⚖️ Driving a Laden Animal: Liability on Shabbat

Rami bar Ḥama
One who drives a laden animal on Shabbat: if unwitting — no sin-offering; if intentional — liable to stoning (מיתת סקילה).
Rava (objection)
Objection from baraita: Capital punishment applies only where unwitting violation incurs a sin-offering. Driving an animal carries no sin-offering — so no stoning either!
Gemara (resolution)
The baraita does not teach the inverse by inference. The two principles are independent — stoning and sin-offering do not always mirror each other.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
One who drives a laden animal on Shabbat is entirely exempt — no sin-offering (no personal act), no lashes (the prohibition is an אזהרת מיתה), and no stoning.
Key Principle לא ילקה — A prohibition given as a warning for capital punishment (אזהרת מיתה) does not carry lashes. Driving an animal falls into this category; the labor is performed by the animal, not the person.

📦 Correct Procedure for Unloading a Laden Animal on Shabbat

Animal arrives at the outer courtyard laden with goods — owner must unload before Shabbat ends to prevent animal suffering
↓
Permitted vessels (כלים הניטלין) — remove directly by hand from the donkey's back
↓
Muktze vessels (כלים שאין ניטלין) — untie the ropes and let bags fall on their own
↓
Horns of a blood-letter — repulsive and forbidden to handle; treated as muktze; untie ropes and let fall
↓
Untithed produce (טבל) or chunks of glass — untie ropes; bags fall even if they break. The glass is designated to be broken, so breakage is not a concern
↓
Rabbi Shimon ben Yoḥai on untithed grain: Place head beneath the pile and shift it to one side — the grain falls by itself without direct handling

🍯 Rabban Gamliel's Donkey — Why No Solution Existed

The Situation

  • Donkey laden with honey on Shabbat
  • Rabban Gamliel refused to unload it
  • Waited until Shabbat ended
  • The donkey died of exhaustion (fatigue)

Why Not Untie the Bags?

  • Honey was in fragile clay jugs
  • Jugs would crack upon falling
  • Breaking vessels is prohibited on Shabbat
  • Could not allow them to shatter

Why Not Use Cushions?

  • Suggestion: place cushions/blankets to catch the jugs
  • Rejected: placing cushions beneath the jugs nullifies them (ביטול כלי)
  • Cushions become forbidden for Shabbat use
  • Cannot set aside Shabbat items for a non-Shabbat purpose

Conclusion

  • No halachically permitted solution existed
  • Rabban Gamliel accepted severe financial loss
  • The donkey's death was unavoidable
  • Lesson: financial loss cannot override Shabbat prohibitions

🌳 Abaye vs. Rabba: Using a Living Creature (חי) on Shabbat

Abaye (confronts Rabba)
He found Rabba sliding his son on a donkey's back on Shabbat: 'Master — you are making use of a living creature (שמוש בחי)! The Sages prohibited this.'
Rabba (defends himself)
I placed my son on the sides of the animal (צדדי חי), not directly on its back. Using the sides of a living creature is only a secondary prohibition, not the primary one.
Abaye (challenges with mishna)
Even the sides are prohibited! A sukka with a wall leaning on a tree is valid but one may not enter on a Festival — this proves using the sides of a living thing is forbidden.
Rabba (rejects proof)
In the sukka case, the tree itself forms the wall — that is direct use of the tree body (גוף האילן), not its sides. My son was on the true sides.
Rava (final ruling)
The positions are internally consistent: whoever prohibits using the sides also prohibits sides of the sides; whoever permits sides of the sides also permits sides themselves.
Tanna'im Parallel The Tosefta records a parallel dispute about a sukka whose walls lean against a tree. Abaye says all opinions agree sides are forbidden — the dispute is only about צדדי צדדים (sides of sides). Rava disagrees: the two levels of the dispute are inseparable.
