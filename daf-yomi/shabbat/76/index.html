💬 Eating Under Duress — אכילה דחויה

Rabbi Yochanan
One who carries out cow-straw in a camel's mouthful is exempt. Eating under duress (אכילה דחויה) is not considered eating. Since a camel consuming cow-straw is eating under duress, this does not establish the item's significance.
Reish Lakish
He is liable. Eating under duress is considered eating. Even reluctant consumption by an animal counts as meaningful use — the carrier therefore meets the minimum and is liable.
The Core Dispute If an animal eats food not naturally suited to it (duress eating — אכילה דחויה), does that establish the food's significance for Shabbat carrying liability? Ravin transmitted this dispute reversing Rav Dimi's earlier version.

🥬 Garlic & Onion Leaves: State Changes Everything

Moist — לַח

  • Fit for human consumption
  • Measure: dried fig-bulk (כגרוגרת)
  • Classified as human food
  • Joins with other human foods
  • Higher standard of significance

Dry — יָבֵשׁ

  • No longer fit for human use
  • Measure: cow's mouthful (מלוא פי פרה)
  • Reclassified as animal fodder
  • Joins with other animal fodder
  • Lower standard — larger quantity needed

🍷 The Wine Standard — Birkat HaMazon Cup One who carries out undiluted wine (יַיִן חַי) on Shabbat is liable only when carrying enough to dilute a significant cup. The Tosefta specifies this as the cup used for Birkat HaMazon (Grace after Meals) — a formal, halakhically meaningful cup. Rava confirms: "We too hold by this standard." Pure wine was routinely diluted with water before drinking, so the measure is calibrated to that practical reality.
