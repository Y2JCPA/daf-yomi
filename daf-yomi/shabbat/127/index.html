🏡 Guests, Grain & Goodwill

דָּן אֶת כָּל הָאָדָם לְכַף זְכוּת
Daf at a Glance Shabbat 127 opens with practical disputes about clearing a storeroom on Shabbat — how many baskets, for whom, and when is it even permitted to begin? The daf then pivots to moral philosophy: six mitzvot whose reward exists in both worlds, culminating in three vivid stories about judging others favorably (דָּן לְכַף זְכוּת). The daf closes with a precise survey of which types of produce — teruma, tithe, demai, lupine — may be moved on Shabbat and why.

📦 How Many Baskets May Be Cleared from the Storeroom?

First Reading
The Mishnah's "four or five baskets" sets a fixed maximum — moving more than that is prohibited on Shabbat.
Second Reading
"Four or five" is merely typical language — one may move as many as needed according to the number of guests.
Baraita A
"One may move even four or five baskets of jugs of oil and wine" — implies a ceiling.
Baraita B
"One may move them even in ten and fifteen" — no fixed limit whatsoever!
Gemara Resolution
No dispute at all: Baraita B refers to jugs, not baskets. All agree: minimize walking. Guest count determines how much may be cleared.
Key Rule A storeroom used for the first time on Shabbat: forbidden — but one may make a path to enter and exit. A storeroom already in use: one may clear as many baskets as needed for guests or Torah study.

⚖️ The Worker from the Upper Galilee: A Story of Charitable Judgment

Worker hired for 3 years in the South. At Yom Kippur eve he asks for wages — employer says: "I have no money."
↓
Worker suspects charitably
"Perhaps he spent the money on merchandise, land, or produce — he simply has no cash right now"
Actual truth revealed later
Employer had vowed all his property to God because his son Hyrcanus neglected Torah study
↓
After Sukkot: employer pays full wages plus a feast with three laden donkeys (food, drink, sweets). Blessing: "Just as you judged me favorably, may God judge you favorably."

👥 Two Great Mitzvot: Hospitality and Torah Study

🏠 Hospitality Toward Guests

  • Rabbi Yoḥanan: equal in greatness to rising early for the study hall
  • One of the six merits with reward in both worlds
  • Grounds for clearing storeroom on Shabbat
  • Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi and Rabbi Ḥiyya both personally cleared fields to host students — hospitality and Torah intertwined
  • Subsumed under acts of gemilut chasadim

📖 Rising Early for Torah Study

  • Also one of the six eternal-merit mitzvot
  • Independent grounds to clear storeroom baskets on Shabbat
  • Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi: found a field of bundles and cleared them himself to make room for students
  • Rabbi Ḥiyya did the same — one person moves items for the benefit of all
  • Dilemma left open: does one person move for all, or each for himself?
