🐦 The Bird Trapped Under One's Clothing

Rav
If a bird flew under the flaps of one's clothing on Shabbat and cannot get out, one may sit and secure it until dark, then take it.
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
Objection from the mishna: If the first person stood and went, the first is liable and the second is exempt — not that it is permitted! How does Rav allow this?
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
Actually, the mishna supports Rav: the second trapper is exempt — and Rav's case is exactly that kind of exempt action.
Shmuel
All exempt rulings in the laws of Shabbat are rabbinically prohibited — except three cases where the act is both exempt by Torah law and permitted by rabbinic law.
⚠️ Exempt ≠ Permitted Shmuel's critical distinction: most Torah-exempt Shabbat acts are still rabbinically forbidden. Only three exceptions are fully permitted.

🩸 Do the Eight Creeping Animals Have Skin?

Rabbi Yoḥanan

  • The eight sheratzim do have skin
  • Their skin is distinct from their flesh
  • Blood pools beneath the skin when wounded
  • Therefore: liable for wounding them on Shabbat
  • The mishna's liability ruling follows his view

Reish Lakish (Rabbi Shimon ben Lakish)

  • Their skin is like their flesh — not distinct
  • Sages equate the skin and flesh of these animals
  • No true 'skin' layer means blood does not pool
  • Therefore: wound liability is disputed
  • Yet both agree: trapping them is fully liable

🐟 Fish, Fetuses & Irreversible Wounds

הֲיַהֲפֹךְ כּוּשִׁי עוֹרוֹ וְנָמֵר חֲבַרְבֻּרֹתָיו — יִרְמְיָהוּ יג:כג
Three Remarkable Rulings 🐟 Fish removed from the sea (Shmuel): Once a sela-sized area of skin dries up on a fish out of water, the person who removed it is liable — the fish can no longer survive.

🐄 Fetus detached from womb (Mar bar Hamdurei citing Shmuel): One who reaches into an animal's innards on Shabbat and detaches a fetus is liable — the fetus is a distinct life even before birth.

🐆 Irreversible wound defined (Levi's question to Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi): From where do we know a wound is irreversible? From the verse: "Can a Cushite change his skin, or a leopard its spots?"
