Shabbat 21 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
For blood examination purposes, Sharon wine is treated as equivalent to which type of Carmelite wine?
A. Old, diluted Carmelite wine
B. New, undiluted Carmelite wine
C. Old, undiluted Carmelite wine
D. New, diluted Carmelite wine
Correct: B — The Gemara rules that Sharon wine equals new, undiluted Carmelite wine — not old and not diluted — for purposes of blood color examination.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak, what is the ROOT of the dispute between Rabbi Yehuda and the Rabbis regarding amorphous tissue?
A. Whether amorphous tissue can contain a recognizable fetus
B. Whether the four impure blood colors include green and white
C. Whether the womb can open without a discharge of blood
D. Whether a lost piece of tissue is treated as if it had blood
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak explains that the core disagreement is whether פתיחת הקבר (womb-opening) is possible without a blood discharge, which underlies both this Mishna dispute and a parallel tannaitic debate about offerings.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rabbi Zeira uses בְּבְשָׂרָהּ ('in her flesh', Lev. 15:19) to rule that a woman who finds blood only in a tube she inserted is:
A. Impure as a niddah, since she saw blood
B. Pure, because discharge must be through her own flesh directly
C. In doubt, and must bring an offering
D. Impure only if the blood matches one of the four impure colors
Correct: B — Rabbi Zeira derives from 'in her flesh' that only blood discharged through direct contact with the woman's own flesh renders her impure. A tube is an intervening object, so she remains pure.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Binyamin, if one tears open an amorphous piece of tissue and finds a bone inside, what is the ruling?
A. She is impure as a niddah
B. She is pure, since bones indicate a dry discharge
C. She is impure with the impurity of a woman who gave birth (yoledet)
D. She must wait and observe a full cycle before a ruling
Correct: C — Rabbi Binyamin rules that a bone inside the tissue identifies it as a fetus, making the woman impure with the impurity of a yoledet (woman after childbirth), not as a niddah.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara rule that blood found INSIDE a piece of tissue makes a woman impure, even though blood in a tube does not?
A. Tissue is physically attached to the uterus while a tube is external
B. It is the normal manner of women to see blood within such tissue, satisfying 'in her flesh'
C. The Rabbis only exempted tube blood by rabbinic decree, not by Torah law
D. Tissue blood is considered menstrual blood by definition, regardless of the verse
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes the cases: blood inside tissue qualifies as 'in her flesh' because it is the normal, natural way (דרכה) for blood to appear within gestational tissue. A tube is an unnatural channel and therefore excluded by 'in her flesh.'
