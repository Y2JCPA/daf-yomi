Rabbi Yosei ben Elisha: Examine the Judges

אם ראית דור שצרות רבות באות עליו — צא ובדוק בדייני ישראל (ר׳ יוסי בן אלישע)
📢 Baraita — Judicial Accountability If you see a generation beset by many troubles, go and examine the judges of Israel — for any calamity that comes to the world comes due to the judges of Israel.

Though wicked, they placed their trust in God — so the Holy One brings three calamities corresponding to their three transgressions.

The Divine Presence will not rest on Israel until evil judges and officers are eliminated, as Isaiah says: "I will purge away your dross as with lye, and I will remove all your alloy" (Isaiah 1:25).

⚡ Rav Pappa: Two Corruptions, Two Consequences

The Arrogant (Yehirim)

  • Correspond to Persian fire-priests (guebers)
  • Isaiah: 'I will purge your dross [sigayikh] as with lye'
  • The conceited and haughty are the 'dross' of Israel
  • When arrogance ceases → Persian oppressors cease
  • Pride invites foreign religious persecution

Deceitful Judges

  • Correspond to royal officers (gazirpatei) and taskmasters
  • Zephaniah 3:15: 'The Lord has removed your judgment, cast out your enemy'
  • Judicial corruption directly enables political tyranny
  • When corrupt judges cease → oppressive officers cease
  • Rav Pappa links internal justice to external freedom

🌿 Hops in a Vineyard — Kilayim or Not?

Rav
One who wishes to sow hops in a vineyard — let him sow! Hops do not constitute forbidden diverse kinds (kilayim) in a vineyard.
Rav Amram Ḥasida
I administer lashes for one who sows hops in a vineyard. It IS a forbidden kilayim mixture.
Rabbi Tarfon (Tosefta)
Hops in a vineyard are permitted — there is an explicit leniency in the Tosefta.
Rav Mesharshiya
I give a peruta to a gentile child to do the sowing — avoiding the prohibition via agency, since a minor is not obligated in mitzvot.
Why Not Teach the Leniency to Bashkar? Rav Menashya did not reveal Rabbi Tarfon's lenient ruling because the people of Bashkar were not Torah-savvy — they would not distinguish between applicable and inapplicable cases.

⚰️ Death on a Festival — Burial Responsibilities

A person dies on a Festival (Yom Tov)
↓
First Day of Festival
Neither Jews nor gentiles should attend to burial
(Rav Menashya's ruling to Bashkar)
Second Day of Festival (Diaspora)
Jews may attend to burial — even on the second day of Rosh HaShana
(Rava)

Special case — Maon synagogue: Festival day uncertain (before or after Shabbat?) → Rabbi Yoḥanan: let gentiles handle burial
