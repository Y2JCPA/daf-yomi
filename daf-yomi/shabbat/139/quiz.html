Shabbat 139 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yosei ben Elisha, if a generation is beset by many troubles, what is the first thing one should do?
A. Increase Torah study and fasting
B. Examine the judges of Israel
C. Check the people's observance of Shabbat
D. Look for failures in charitable giving
Correct: B — Rabbi Yosei ben Elisha teaches: 'If you see a generation that many troubles are befalling it, go and examine the judges of Israel' — because any calamity that comes to the world comes due to the judges of Israel.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Ulla teaches that Jerusalem will be redeemed only through what, citing Isaiah 1:27?
A. Prayer and fasting
B. Increased Torah scholarship
C. Righteousness (tzedek/tzedakah)
D. Rebuilding the Temple service
Correct: C — Ulla cites Isaiah 1:27: 'Zion will be redeemed with justice, and those who return to her with righteousness.' Redemption is tied specifically to the practice of justice and righteousness.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Rav Menashya decline to inform the people of Bashkar about Rabbi Tarfon's lenient ruling on hops in a vineyard?
A. Rabbi Tarfon's opinion was rejected by all later authorities
B. The leniency only applied in the Land of Israel, not the Diaspora
C. The people were not Torah-savvy and would fail to distinguish when the leniency applies
D. Hops are biblically prohibited in a vineyard and no leniency is possible
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that Rav Menashya did not want to reveal the leniency because the people of Bashkar were not well-versed in Torah and would not properly distinguish between cases where it applies and where it does not.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Ze'iri rules that filtering through a strainer on Shabbat depends on what key factor?
A. Whether the strainer is owned by the person or borrowed
B. Whether the liquid is clear and already drinkable, or murky
C. Whether the filtering occurs before or after the Shabbat meal
D. Whether one intends to drink the liquid or use it for cooking
Correct: B — Ze'iri rules that clear wine and water may be placed in a strainer on Shabbat without concern for the prohibition of selecting (borer), since the liquid is already drinkable as-is. However, murky liquids may not be filtered, as this constitutes actual selecting.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rava, on the second day of a Festival observed in the Diaspora, who may attend to burial of someone who died?
A. Only non-Jewish gentiles may handle burial
B. Only Kohanim are exempt; all other Jews may attend
C. Neither Jews nor gentiles, just as on the first day
D. Jews themselves may attend to the burial
Correct: D — Rava rules: on the first day of a Festival, gentiles may attend to burial; on the second day of a Festival in the Diaspora, Jews themselves may attend — and this applies even to the second day of Rosh HaShana.
