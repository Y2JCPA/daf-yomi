QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the halachic status of tying the strap to a camel's nose ring on Shabbat?
A. Completely permitted — it is a temporary knot
B. No sin-offering liability, but rabbinically prohibited
C. Full Torah violation requiring a sin-offering
D. Permitted only in a private domain
Correct: B — The Gemara explicitly states there is no sin-offering liability for the camel nose-ring strap knot, yet it is rabbinically prohibited — it resembles the permanent professional knot tied by craftsmen.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
When Rabbi Yirmeya's sandal strap tore in the karmelit, what did Rabbi Abbahu tell him to use?
A. A thin leather cord
B. A strip of cloth
C. A moist reed fit for animal consumption
D. A dry palm frond
Correct: C — Rabbi Abbahu specified a moist reed fit for animal consumption — not a standard tying material — so the binding would not constitute a formal prohibited knot while still keeping the sandal usable.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda, what happens ritually when a sandal's outer strap breaks?
A. It remains ritually impure until fully repaired
B. It becomes ritually pure
C. A sage must inspect it before a ruling can be made
D. It depends on whether the inner strap is intact
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda holds that the outer strap breaking is significant enough to purify the sandal. The majority Rabbis disagree and say it remains impure.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What did Ḥizkiya exclaim about Rabbi Yoḥanan after hearing his 'new face' teaching?
A. "He has surpassed Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel combined."
B. "This is the greatest Amora of his generation."
C. "This is not a human being, but an angel."
D. "He will be the leader of the next Sanhedrin."
Correct: C — Ḥizkiya was so awed by the depth of Rabbi Yoḥanan's 'new face has arrived' insight that he declared: 'This is not a human being, but an angel.' Some say he said: 'This is an ideal human being.'
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rabbi Zeira's famous generational humility statement says: 'If the early sages were like angels, we are like ___; if they were like humans, we are like ___.'
A. scholars … students
B. humans … donkeys
C. prophets … sages
D. angels … humans
Correct: B — Rabbi Zeira quoted Rava bar Zimuna: 'If the early sages were like angels, we are like humans; if the early sages were like humans, we are like donkeys' — a classic Talmudic expression of humility about later generations.
