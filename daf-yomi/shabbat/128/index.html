👑 Daf 128 — Princes, Muktzeh & Shabbat Rescue

כל ישראל בני מלכים הם
Central Theme This daf opens with layered muktzeh rulings for forbidden produce and tithes, then expands into the remarkable principle that כל ישראל בני מלכים הם — every Jew is royalty — which permits moving items that serve even exotic animals. It then turns to rescuing a trapped animal with cushions and blankets, the laws of assisting births, and the sweeping obligation to desecrate Shabbat for a woman in labor.

👑 All Jews Are Princes — Four Tannaim, One Principle

Rabban Shimon b. Gamliel
Glass shards may be moved on Shabbat — they are food for ostriches. Ostriches are common enough that every Jew is considered to own them.
Rabbi Natan (objecting)
If so, even bundles of grapevines should be permitted — they are food for elephants! Why stop at ostriches?
Rabban Shimon b. Gamliel (reply)
Ostriches are common; elephants are not. One does not permit moving items for rare animals no ordinary person keeps.
Rabbi Shimon
All Jews are princes — even rose oil may be applied to a wound on Shabbat, since it is the normal weekday practice of princes.
Rabbi Yishmael & Rabbi Akiva
Even a debtor owing 1,000 maneh — we do not seize the royal cloak off his back. Jewish dignity is inviolable.
Abaye
All four Tannaim share one root: כל ישראל בני מלכים הם. Nothing is too extravagant for any Jew — this justifies moving items suited to royalty.
Shared Principle כל ישראל בני מלכים הם — All Jewish people are princes, so items fit for royalty or their animals are considered fit for everyone.

🥩 Moving Meat on Shabbat — The Three-Way Analysis

Rav Huna — Permits Unsalted

  • Unsalted meat: permitted to move
  • Still fit for consumption by most people
  • Or: qualifies as food for animals
  • Follows Rabbi Shimon: only designated muktzeh is forbidden
  • Rav follows R. Yehuda on eating — but Rav Huna follows R. Shimon on moving

Rav Ḥisda — Forbids Unsalted

  • Unsalted meat: prohibited to move
  • Not properly fit to eat until salted
  • Considered set-aside (muktzeh) from use
  • Incident: Rav Yitzḥak bar Ami saw them move meat only to prevent spoilage (sun→shade)
  • Moving to prevent loss ≠ normal moving for use

Unattributed Baraita — Final Ruling

  • Salted fish: permitted to move
  • Unsalted fish: prohibited to move
  • Meat — both salted AND unsalted: permitted
  • Unattributed baraita = majority / accepted view
  • Aligns with Rav Huna's lenient position on meat

🐄 Animal Fell into Aqueduct on Shabbat

Animal falls into an aqueduct on Shabbat — lifting it out directly is forbidden
↓
Can feed it in place
Bring food and water to the animal where it stands. Wait until after Shabbat to lift it out.
Cannot feed in place
Bring cushions and blankets → throw them under the animal → if it climbs out on its own, this is permitted.
↓
Are the cushions negated (מבטל כלי)? No — placing them under a suffering animal serves a valid purpose; this is not prohibited destruction of a vessel.
↓
Helping a hen walk is different — because the hen lifts itself off the ground, one effectively carries it. Only pushing (with hands or object) is permitted for birds.

👶 Birthing a Woman on Shabbat — Escalating Permissions

Womb opens — active labor begins. Whether or not the woman requests it, Shabbat is desecrated for her immediately. ספק נפשות להקל — doubt about life is resolved leniently.
↓
Call a midwife to travel from place to place and across Shabbat boundaries (תחום) — even long distances.
↓
If she needs a lamp lit — her friend lights it on Shabbat, even if the woman is blind. The lamp is lit for her psychological ease, not only for her physical sight.
↓
If she needs oil — her friend carries it in her hair. No violation of wringing (סחיטה): Rabba & Rav Yosef rule hair does not absorb like fabric. Rav Ashi adds: even if it did, her intent is to bring oil, not to wring.
↓
After birth — womb closes. Now her word matters: if she says she needs Shabbat desecrated → we desecrate. If she says she does not need it → we do not, even against the advice of others.
