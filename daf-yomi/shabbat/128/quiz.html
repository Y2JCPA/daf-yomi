QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the mishna need to explicitly teach that untithed produce (tevel) is muktzeh — isn't it obvious?
A. Because tevel may be moved but not eaten on Shabbat
B. Because the case refers to produce that is only rabbinically forbidden as tevel — one might have thought it is still permitted to move
C. Because tevel is identical in status to regular produce on Shabbat
D. Because tevel is permitted if one forgot to tithe before Shabbat
Correct: B — The mishna teaches the non-obvious case where the produce is permitted by Torah law but only rabbinically forbidden. One might think rabbinically-forbidden-to-eat still allows moving — the mishna teaches it does not.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Which four Tannaim does Abaye identify as all holding 'כל ישראל בני מלכים הם'?
A. Rabbi Akiva, Rabbi Meir, Rabbi Yehuda, Rabbi Shimon
B. Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel, Rabbi Shimon, Rabbi Yishmael, Rabbi Akiva
C. Rabban Gamliel, Rabbi Eliezer, Rabbi Yehoshua, Rabbi Akiva
D. Rabbi Shimon, Rav, Shmuel, and Abaye himself
Correct: B — Abaye (128a) identifies Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel, Rabbi Shimon, Rabbi Yishmael, and Rabbi Akiva as all sharing the principle that Jewish dignity is royal — which informs their respective rulings on moving items, rose oil, and creditors.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rav permit when an animal falls into an aqueduct and it is impossible to feed it in place?
A. Lifting the animal out directly by hand, since it is a case of tzaar ba'alei chayyim
B. Slaughtering the animal in place to relieve its suffering
C. Bringing cushions and blankets and placing them under the animal so it can climb out on its own
D. Instructing a non-Jew to lift the animal out
Correct: C — Rav (128b) permits throwing cushions and blankets under the trapped animal. If it climbs out on its own power, that is permitted — and the cushions are not considered negated vessels since they serve the purpose of preventing death.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the unattributed baraita (128a), what is the final ruling on moving unsalted meat on Shabbat?
A. Forbidden, like unsalted fish
B. Permitted only in a case of great need
C. Permitted — both salted and unsalted meat may be moved
D. Permitted only if it is designated for animal food
Correct: C — The baraita distinguishes fish from meat: unsalted fish is prohibited, but meat — both salted and unsalted — is permitted to move. This unattributed ruling reflects the majority view and aligns with Rav Huna's lenient position.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why is it permitted for a woman's friend to bring oil by carrying it in her hair during childbirth, without violating the melachah of wringing (סחיטה)?
A. Because the quantity of oil is too small to constitute a prohibited labor
B. Because Rabba and Rav Yosef rule that wringing does not apply to hair, since hair does not absorb liquids the way fabric does
C. Because all prohibited labors are automatically suspended once a woman is in active labor
D. Because the oil sits on top of the hair and never enters it
Correct: B — Rabba and Rav Yosef both rule there is no prohibition of sechitah for hair, since hair does not absorb like cloth. Rav Ashi adds that even granting sechitah applies to hair, the friend's intention is to bring the oil to the woman — not to wring it out — so no prohibition applies.
