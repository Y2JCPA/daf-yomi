Shabbat 73 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the last valid day within the eleven days of ziva on which a sighting allows counting seven clean days?
A. The ninth day
B. The tenth day
C. The eleventh day
D. The twelfth day
Correct: C — The eleventh day is the last valid ziva day — a sighting on that day is 'fit for counting' seven clean days. The twelfth day is excluded because it belongs to neither the ziva period nor the next menstrual cycle.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How many clean days must a Zava Gedola count before immersing and becoming pure?
A. One clean day
B. Three clean days
C. Five clean days
D. Seven clean days
Correct: D — A Zava Gedola — who bleeds three consecutive days during the eleven ziva days — must count seven clean days and bring a sacrifice upon purification, as derived from Leviticus 15:28–29.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
The baraita rejects using a kal va-chomer (a fortiori) to derive the impurity of one who has relations with a zav from the case of a zava. Why does this inference fail?
A. Because a zav is not actually impure
B. Because a zava has a stringency that a zav lacks, breaking the logical inference
C. Because the Torah never mentions a zav having relations
D. Because the Rabbis issued a separate decree
Correct: B — The a fortiori argument is rejected because a zava actually has a stringency that a zav does not share — she can impose impurity without requiring the same three-stage tumah process — which breaks the inference.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The school of Elijah promises the World to Come to those who study halakhot every day. On which verse is this teaching based?
A. 'You shall study it day and night' (Joshua 1:8)
B. 'His ways [halikhot] are eternal' (Habakkuk 3:6)
C. 'The Torah of the Lord is perfect' (Psalms 19:8)
D. 'All the paths of the Lord are kindness' (Psalms 25:10)
Correct: B — The teaching reads 'halikhot' (ways) in Habakkuk 3:6 as 'halakhot' (laws), yielding: 'His halakhot are eternal' — those who study them are guaranteed eternal life.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Gemara's resolution, are the eleven days of ziva a halakha l'Moshe miSinai (tradition from Moses at Sinai) or are they derived from biblical verses?
A. They are purely a rabbinic enactment
B. They are a halakha l'Moshe miSinai according to all opinions
C. According to Rabbi Akiva they are derived from verses, not from Sinai
D. They are disputed with no resolution
Correct: C — The Gemara answers that according to Rabbi Akiva's opinion, the eleven days of ziva are derived from biblical verses (as shown by the baraita's detailed derivations), not transmitted as an oral tradition from Sinai.
