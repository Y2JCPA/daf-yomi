📜 Deriving the Laws of Ziva — The Final Baraita

Source verse: Leviticus 15:25 — 'if she has a discharge… not in the time of her menstruation'
↓
Derivation 1: Ziva applies even to bleeding adjacent to menstrual days — explicitly included
↓
Derivation 2: Extends to bleeding 1 through 11 days after menstruation — each day derived individually
↓
Derivation 3: Three consecutive days = Zava Gedola; two days or one day = Zava Ketana
↓
Limit: Day 12 and beyond is excluded — fit for neither ziva counting nor menstrual counting
↓
Closing: Daily halakha study guarantees the World to Come (school of Elijah) — siyum of Niddah

📅 The Eleven Days of Ziva — Inclusion and Exclusion

Verse: 'Not in the time of her menstruation' (Lev. 15:25)
↓
Days 1–11 after menstruation
Included — each day is 'fit for counting' the seven clean days that follow a three-day sighting
Day 12 onward
Excluded — not fit for ziva counting; menstrual cycle begins anew on day 12
↓
Day 11 is the last valid ziva day — a sighting then still allows counting seven clean days

🔢 Zava Gedola vs. Zava Ketana

Zava Gedola (Greater Zava)

  • Bleeds three consecutive days during the 11 ziva days
  • Must count seven clean days before immersion
  • Requires a sacrifice upon purification
  • Transmits tumah to lying and sitting objects (Lev. 15:26)

Zava Ketana (Lesser Zava)

  • Bleeds one or two days during the 11 ziva days
  • Counts one clean day per sighting day
  • No sacrifice required
  • Still transmits impurity to objects designated for lying and sitting

🗣️ Day vs. Night — Rav Shemaya and Rabbi Abba

Rav Shemaya
Perhaps the verse means: if she bleeds during the day she is a zava, but if she bleeds at night she is treated as a menstruating woman — since the verse says 'days'?
Rabbi Abba
No — the verse says 'all the days of her discharge,' indicating that both day and night sightings are encompassed within the ziva framework.
Rav Shemaya
Then why does the verse specify 'days' at all? That word must be doing something!
Rabbi Abba
The word 'days' is needed to derive the eleven-day period of ziva — it does not come to exclude nighttime sightings from counting.
Resolution Both daytime and nighttime sightings during the 11 days count toward establishing ziva status.

🌟 The Closing Teaching — Siyum of Masechet Niddah

כל השונה הלכות בכל יום — מובטח לו שהוא בן העולם הבא
School of Elijah — תנא דבי אליהו Anyone who studies halakhot every day is guaranteed that he is destined for the World to Come, as it is stated: 'His ways [halikhot] are eternal' (Habakkuk 3:6) — read not halikhot (ways) but halakhot (laws).

With this promise, Masechet Niddah — and the entire order of Seder Taharot — reaches its close.
