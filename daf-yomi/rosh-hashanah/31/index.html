🤔 Why These Psalms? Two Tannaitic Views

R. Akiva
Each weekday psalm refers to the past — it commemorates what God performed on that specific day of Creation. Wednesday: Sun & moon created. Thursday: Birds & fish created.
R. Neḥemya
Why distinguish weekdays from Shabbat? All psalms — including weekdays — refer to the future: God's judgment on those who abused creation. The framework must be consistent.
R. Akiva
Shabbat's psalm uniquely speaks of the future world — the era of the great Shabbat of 1,000 years. The six weekday psalms each commemorate their own creation day.
R. Neḥemya
On each day, the psalm speaks of future punishment: Day 2 — those who denied God's sovereignty; Day 4 — sun and moon worshippers; Day 5 — those who neglected praise. All future.
Rav Ketina's Supporting Ruling The world will exist 6,000 years and be desolate for 1,000 — mirroring the six days of creation and the Shabbat of rest. This aligns Shabbat's psalm (Psalm 92) with the future era of divine rest.

🔴 The Ninth Ordinance: What Did Rabban Yoḥanan Ben Zakkai Institute?

Rav Pappa: Fourth-Year Grapevine

  • Fruit of a fourth-year vine has second-tithe status
  • Within one day's walk of Jerusalem: must be brought to the city
  • Purpose: to adorn the markets of Jerusalem with fruit
  • Rabban Yoḥanan expanded the boundary to wherever witnesses can reach in a day
  • After the Temple's destruction, the Sages voted to permit redeeming the fruit anywhere
  • R. Eliezer's vine near Lod — he declared it ownerless rather than redeem it

Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak: Crimson Wool Strip

  • Crimson strip tied on Yom Kippur — turns white if Israel is forgiven
  • Originally tied to the outer Temple entrance — fully visible to all
  • People rejoiced if white, despaired if red — causing public harm
  • Moved inside, then to the goat's horns — still peeked at
  • Ordinance: tie half to a rock near the Azazel cliff, away from public view
  • For 40 years before the Temple's destruction, it never turned white
