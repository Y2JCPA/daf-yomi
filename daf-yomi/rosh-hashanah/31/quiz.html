5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Which psalm did the Levites recite on Thursday (the fifth day), and what was the reason according to Rabbi Akiva?
A. Psalm 94 — God created the sun and moon to rule by day and night
B. Psalm 81 — God created birds and fish on that day to praise His name
C. Psalm 93 — God completed His creation and put on majesty
D. Psalm 48 — God separated the upper and lower waters
Correct: B — Psalm 81 ('Sing aloud to God our strength') was recited on Thursday because on the fifth day of Creation, God created birds and fish whose purpose is to praise His name.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was Rabbi Neḥemya's criticism of the standard interpretation of the weekly psalms?
A. He argued that the Levites should not recite psalms at all on weekdays
B. He held that all weekday psalms refer to the future, just like the Shabbat psalm — the interpretations should be consistent
C. He maintained that the Shabbat psalm refers to the past, not the future
D. He disputed which psalms were assigned to which days
Correct: B — Rabbi Neḥemya asked why we distinguish: if the Shabbat psalm speaks of the future, all psalms should be interpreted as future-oriented — describing God's coming judgment on those who misused creation.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In how many stages did the Divine Presence (Shekhinah) depart from the Temple, and where did it linger for six months?
A. Seven stages; it lingered on the roof of the Temple
B. Five stages; it lingered in the city of Jerusalem
C. Ten stages; it lingered in the wilderness waiting for Israel to repent
D. Three stages; it lingered on the Mount of Olives
Correct: C — The Shekhinah traveled ten stages. After reaching the Mount of Olives, it moved to the wilderness, where it waited six months hoping Israel would repent. When they did not, it departed to its place on high.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What was the final city in the Sanhedrin's ten exiles, and what is notable about it?
A. Tzippori — the highest city in the Galilee, symbolizing elevation
B. Yavne — where Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai established his academy
C. Tiberias — the lowest city in Eretz Yisrael, near Gehinnom
D. Usha — the midpoint of the exile, to which they returned twice
Correct: C — The Sanhedrin's final exile was to Tiberias, described as the lowest city in Eretz Yisrael, situated near Gehinnom — symbolizing the depth of the exile, as derived from Isaiah 26:5.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak, what was the ninth ordinance of Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai, and why was it needed?
A. Fourth-year grapevine fruit must be redeemed in Jerusalem to adorn its markets
B. Priests must remove their sandals before ascending the platform for the Priestly Blessing
C. The crimson wool strip on Yom Kippur should be tied away from public view, to prevent public despair when it did not turn white
D. Converts must set aside a quarter-shekel for their pair of bird-offerings
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak held the ninth ordinance concerned the crimson strip tied on Yom Kippur. When publicly visible, people rejoiced or despaired based on its color. The ordinance moved it from public view to prevent this harm.
