⚖️ Is a Cow's Horn a Valid Shofar?

Rabbis
A cow's horn is disqualified. It is specifically called keren, not shofar. The Torah requires a shofar — and a cow's horn is not one.
Rabbi Yosei
But aren't all shofarot also called keren? The verse in Psalms 69:32 uses shofar language for an ox's horns — so even a cow's horn should qualify!
Rabbis
That verse refers to shor par — an ox as large as a bull — not to the horn being called shofar (Rav Mattana). Furthermore, the golden calf cannot prosecute and then advocate.
Rabbi Yosei
The 'prosecutor cannot advocate' rule applies only inside the Holy of Holies. The shofar is blown outside — so this objection does not apply to me!
Ruling The mishna rules: all shofarot are valid except the horn of a cow, which is called keren and not shofar.

🏛️ Gold in the Holy of Holies — The Prosecutor Principle (Rav Ḥisda)

Rav Ḥisda
The High Priest does not enter the Holy of Holies wearing gold on Yom Kippur, because a prosecutor cannot become an advocate. Gold recalls the sin of the golden calf.
Gemara
But the bull's blood is sprinkled inside on Yom Kippur — that also evokes a calf! Why is that permitted?
Rav Ḥisda
Blood changes form — it is no longer recognizable as a calf. Gold remains gold and triggers the association directly.
Gemara
What about the golden Ark, cover, cherubs, spoon, and coal pan — all gold inside the Holy of Holies?
Rav Ḥisda
Those items are fixed there permanently. The principle is: a sinner seeking atonement should not adorn himself with gold when entering — it is the personal adornment that is prohibited.
Hebrew Principle קָטֵגוֹר לֹא יַעֲשֶׂה סָנֵגוֹר — A prosecutor cannot become a defender

Public Fast Days

  • Curved horn of a ram
  • Mouthpiece plated with silver
  • Two trumpets in the middle
  • Shofar blast is cut short

  • Same shofar blasts as Rosh HaShana

🌀 Abaye's Reasoning: The Layered Horn Problem

אָמַר רַחֲמָנָא תִּקְעוּ בְּשׁוֹפָר אֶחָד — The Merciful One said: Sound with a single shofar
Abaye's Insight (26a) The Torah (Merciful One) commands sounding a single shofar. A cow's horn is composed of multiple concentric layers — it therefore appears as two or three shofarot merged into one, violating the requirement of a singular instrument.

Rabbi Yosei's counter: Since the layers are connected to each other and form one piece, it is counted as a single shofar — just as a bent shofar is still one instrument despite its shape.

The Gemara notes: the tanna stated one reason (keren) and also another reason — both stand independently.
