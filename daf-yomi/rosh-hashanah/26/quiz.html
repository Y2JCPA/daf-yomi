Rosh Hashanah 26 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the mishna's plain language, why is a cow's horn disqualified as a shofar on Rosh HaShana?
A. It is too large to blow comfortably and disrupts the service
B. It is called keren (horn), not shofar — the Torah requires a shofar
C. It was associated with pagan idol worship
D. It produces a tone that is too low for the congregation to hear
Correct: B — The mishna states explicitly: 'because it is a keren' — the Torah requires a shofar, and the cow's horn is denoted by the distinct term keren rather than shofar.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rav Ḥisda's reason for the High Priest not entering the Holy of Holies with gold on Yom Kippur?
A. Gold is too heavy and slows the priest's movements during the service
B. Only silver vessels may be used for the interior atonement service
C. A prosecutor (the golden calf) cannot become an advocate for Israel's atonement
D. Gold belonged to the outer court and was forbidden in the inner sanctum by explicit verse
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda teaches: קָטֵגוֹר לֹא יַעֲשֶׂה סָנֵגוֹר — a prosecutor cannot become a defender. Gold recalls the golden calf sin and therefore cannot serve as an instrument of atonement inside the Holy of Holies.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Abaye, what is the specific problem with using a cow's horn as a shofar?
A. Its hollow center is too narrow to produce a valid shofar sound
B. Its layered structure makes it appear as two or three shofarot, violating the Torah's requirement of a single shofar
C. It is made of the wrong material according to a Torah-level prohibition
D. Its straight shape disqualifies it since Rosh HaShana requires a curved shofar
Correct: B — Abaye explains that the Torah commands sounding a single shofar. A cow's horn is composed of multiple concentric layers, making it look like two or three shofarot combined — this violates the requirement of one unified instrument.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What was the Temple's Rosh HaShana shofar made from, and how was its mouthpiece finished?
A. Curved horn of a ram with a silver-plated mouthpiece
B. Straight horn of a ram with no plating on the mouthpiece
C. Straight horn of an ibex with a gold-plated mouthpiece
D. Curved horn of an ibex with a gold-plated mouthpiece
Correct: C — The mishna (26b) specifies: the Rosh HaShana shofar in the Temple was the straight horn of an ibex, and its mouth (mouthpiece) was plated with gold. Fast-day shofarot used curved ram's horns with silver plating.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How did Reish Lakish discover the meaning of the word 'sekhvi' used in Job 38:36?
A. He found an explanation in an early targum preserved in the academy of Sura
B. The maidservant of Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi used the word in conversation
C. When he traveled to the district of Kan Nishrayya, he heard locals call a rooster by that name
D. He derived it from a cognate in the Aramaic translation of the Torah
Correct: C — Reish Lakish recounts that in the district of Kan Nishrayya, locals called a rooster 'sekhvi' — this clarified the biblical verse in Job 38:36, which asks who placed wisdom in the sekhvi.
