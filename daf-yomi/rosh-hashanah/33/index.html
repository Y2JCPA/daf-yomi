💧 Substances in the Shofar — Permitted vs. Forbidden

Permitted

  • Water — enhances clarity of tone
  • Wine — permitted by Abba Shaul
  • Purpose: produce a clear, beautiful sound
  • Mitzva enhancement justifies insertion

Forbidden

  • Saliva (רוק) — explicitly excluded by Abba Shaul
  • Does not improve quality of sound
  • Only beauty-enhancing liquids are allowed
  • Practical line: intentional tonal improvement

🤔 What Is the Terua? Abaye on the Tannaitic Dispute

Mishna
The terua equals three yevavot — short, staccato whimpers, like broken sobbing
Baraita 1
A single tekia equals an entire terua — the proportions seem to conflict with the mishna's formula
Baraita 2
The terua equals three shevarim — broken blasts, longer than whimpers; a second clear contradiction
Abaye
This is a genuine tannaitic dispute! 'Yom terua' evokes the crying of Sisera's mother — some read it as sobbing (yevavot), others as moaning (shevarim). Since we are uncertain, we sound both to fulfill all opinions.
Key Verse "It shall be a day of terua to you" (Numbers 29:1) — the nature of the 'crying' sound is disputed among tannaim

🎯 Intention and the Shofar Obligation

התוקע לשיר — יצא; השומע מן המתעסק — לא יצא
Halakhic Principle — Kavvanah for Shofar One who sounds the shofar unawares (without intending to perform the mitzva) has not fulfilled the obligation. Similarly, one who hears shofar from such a person has not fulfilled the obligation. However, one who sounds shofar for music — the listener may still fulfill the mitzva if he himself had proper intention, since the listener's hearing is the operative act.
