QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Levi, what is Rabbi Shimon's scriptural basis for permitting relatives to testify together about the new moon?
A. The new moon is a public matter, so private testimony rules do not apply
B. The verse 'This month shall be for you' — לָכֶם — includes all Israel together, even relatives
C. The Sanhedrin has unique authority to waive standard testimony disqualifications
D. Relatives are only disqualified in monetary disputes, not in ritual calendar matters
Correct: B — Rabbi Levi explains that Rabbi Shimon derives his leniency from 'And the Lord spoke to Moses and Aaron — This month shall be for you.' The plural לָכֶם ('for you all') implies all Israel together may testify, even relatives.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was the ruling regarding the man who accepted 200 dinars from the Boethusians but then exposed their plot?
A. He was punished for accepting a bribe, even though he acted righteously
B. He had to return the 200 dinars to the Boethusians since the plan failed
C. The court declared the money ownerless and awarded it to him as a gift
D. He was appointed as a permanent court-appointed witness inspector
Correct: C — The Sages ruled that the 200 dinars he received from the Boethusians were given to him as a gift — the court is authorized to declare money ownerless and award it. The Boethusian mastermind was deemed deserving of death, left to divine punishment.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did the Sages stop using torch relays to announce the new moon and switch to messengers?
A. Torches were unreliable in rainy seasons and prone to accidental extinction
B. Roman authorities prohibited fire signaling after the destruction of the Temple
C. The Samaritans (Kutim) began lighting false torches to mislead the people
D. Messengers could communicate the exact day and hour of sanctification more precisely
Correct: C — The Mishna explicitly states that after the Kutim (Samaritans) began corrupting the process by lighting false torches to confuse the diaspora about when the month was sanctified, the Sages instituted messengers as the official means of communication.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does the Gemara learn from the incident of Rabbi Nehorai traveling to Usha on Shabbat to accompany a witness?
A. One witness alone is sufficient to vouch for a new moon witness before the Sanhedrin
B. Rabbi Nehorai held a special rabbinic status that permitted solo Shabbat travel
C. Even in cases of uncertainty — where the witness may not be present — Shabbat may be desecrated
D. The court in Usha had temporary authority equal to the Jerusalem Sanhedrin
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that even though two witnesses were technically present (the second unnamed to honor Rabbi Nehorai), the story's lesson is: lest you think Shabbat is only overridden when we are certain the witness will be there — we learn that even uncertain cases permit desecrating Shabbat for new moon testimony.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which category of person is explicitly listed in the Mishna as unfit to testify?
A. A freed slave who witnessed the new moon alongside his former master
B. A doctor who practices medicine on Shabbat in life-threatening emergencies
C. One who races pigeons and places wagers on the outcome
D. A father whose son also saw the new moon on the same night
Correct: C — The Mishna lists pigeon racers who wager as unfit witnesses — along with dice players, usurers, and Shemita traders. These are all classified as robbers or those who profit dishonestly, disqualifying them from testimony. A father-and-son pair, while unable to testify together, are each individually valid witnesses.
