Rosh Hashanah 22 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rami bar Hama, why is a gambler disqualified from giving testimony?
A. He does not contribute to the productive settlement of society
B. His winnings resemble robbery because the loser never truly consented
C. Gambling is a form of desecrating God's name
D. He is too unreliable to appear in court on time
Correct: B — Rami bar Hama holds that gambling resembles robbery (גזל) — the loser hoped to win and never truly consented to giving away his money. The winner therefore takes it by a form of coercion.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the key practical difference (nafka mina) between Rami bar Hama and Rav Sheshet?
A. Rami disqualifies only professional gamblers; Rav Sheshet disqualifies all gamblers
B. Both agree that all gamblers, even part-time, are disqualified
C. Rav Sheshet disqualifies only full-time gamblers; Rami disqualifies even occasional gamblers
D. Rav Sheshet requires a court ruling; Rami says disqualification is automatic
Correct: C — The practical difference is the part-time gambler who also has a trade. Rav Sheshet considers him valid (he contributes to society), while Rami bar Hama considers him invalid (each gambling act resembles robbery regardless of his other profession).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the activity of 'mafrichei yonim' (מַפְרִיחֵי יוֹנִים)?
A. Raising pigeons as offerings for the Temple
B. Using carrier pigeons to send messages between cities
C. Racing pigeons and wagering on whose bird returns first
D. Breeding pigeons to sell in the marketplace
Correct: C — Mafrichei yonim are pigeon racers who wager on whose pigeon will return to its loft first. The Gemara includes even arrangements like 'your pigeon against mine,' treating this as a form of gambling.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why are traders in Shemitah produce disqualified as witnesses?
A. They work on Shabbat to harvest and transport their goods
B. They profit from produce that the Torah requires to be ownerless and freely available to all
C. They do business with non-Jews, undermining communal trust
D. They violate the prohibition against leaving the Land of Israel
Correct: B — Seventh-year (Shemitah) produce must be declared hefker — ownerless and available to everyone. Treating it as private property for commercial sale violates this Torah law, demonstrating disregard for halacha and thereby disqualifying the person from testimony.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The Mishnah says these witnesses are invalid 'for any testimony that a woman is invalid for.' What is the significance of this formulation?
A. These witnesses may only testify about matters involving women
B. They are disqualified from all formal testimony, sharing women's legal status in this regard
C. They must bring a female co-witness to validate their testimony
D. They are only valid in rabbinically-enacted courts, not biblical ones
Correct: B — The Mishnah uses women's broad disqualification from formal eidut as the benchmark. By equating these five categories to women in this respect, it signals that they are excluded from all formal testimony — not just moon-sighting testimony.
