⚖️ Two Views: Why Are Gamblers Disqualified?

Rami bar Hama

  • Gambling resembles robbery (גזל)
  • The loser never truly consents — he hoped to win
  • Even occasional gamblers are invalid
  • Disqualification stems from dishonest gain
  • Each gambling act is inherently problematic

Rav Sheshet

  • Gambling ≠ robbery — the loser consents
  • Gamblers lack involvement in yishuvo shel olam
  • They don't contribute to society's upkeep
  • Only full-time gamblers are invalid
  • Part-time gamblers who work a trade remain valid

⚖️ The Great Gambler Debate

Rami bar Hama
A gambler is disqualified because gambling is essentially robbery (גזל). When you lose a bet, you never truly agreed to hand over your money — you only hoped to win. So the winner takes by quasi-force.
Rav Sheshet
Gambling is not robbery! Both parties fully consent. The real problem is that gamblers are not involved in yishuvo shel olam — the productive settlement of the world. They contribute nothing to society.
Rami bar Hama
Therefore even a part-time gambler is disqualified — every bet is a quasi-robbery, regardless of whether he also has a real profession.
Rav Sheshet
Therefore only a professional gambler with no other trade is disqualified. Someone who gambles occasionally but also works remains a fully valid witness.
Key Nafka Mina Part-time gambler who also has a trade: invalid per Rami bar Hama (every bet is quasi-robbery), valid per Rav Sheshet (he does contribute to society through his work)

🕊️ Mafrichei Yonim — Pigeon Racers Explained

מַפְרִיחֵי יוֹנִים — אֲפִילוּ יוֹנְךָ לְיוֹנִי
🕊️ Who Are Pigeon Racers? Those who wager on pigeon races — whose bird will return to its loft first. The Gemara includes even bets structured as 'your pigeon against mine'. The disqualification mirrors that of gamblers: either their gain resembles robbery (Rami bar Hama), or they demonstrate indifference to productive societal contribution (Rav Sheshet). Either way, such conduct undermines the credibility required to serve as a witness.

🌾 Path to Disqualification: Shemitah Traders

Person trades in Shemitah produce
Selling fruits and vegetables of the seventh year
↓
Torah Requirement
Shemitah produce must be declared hefker (ownerless) — it belongs to everyone and cannot be bought or sold
The Violation
Trading it as a commodity treats sacred produce as private property, flouting a core Torah prohibition
↓
Disqualified from all formal testimony until genuine teshuvah (repentance) is demonstrated

📜 The General Principle of Witness Disqualification

The Mishnah's Rule: 'Any testimony a woman is invalid for — these people are also invalid for'
↓
Women are excluded from formal eidut based on the verse 'שְׁנֵי עֵדִים' — rabbinic interpretation limits this to male witnesses in most contexts
↓
These five categories share that same status: like women, they lack the full legal standing required for formal testimony
↓
Stakes are especially high for the new moon: a false testimony could corrupt the entire Jewish calendar — festivals, fasts, and observances all depend on correct dates
↓
Although Beit Din was generally lenient about new moon testimony in other ways (e.g., allowing travel on Shabbat), these disqualifications were strictly enforced
