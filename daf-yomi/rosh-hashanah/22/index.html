👨‍👦 Can Relatives Testify Together About the New Moon?

Rabbi Shimon
Relatives MAY testify together about the new moon. The verse states: 'And the Lord spoke to Moses and Aaron — This month shall be for you.' The word לָכֶם ('for you') encompasses all of Israel together, even relatives.
The Rabbis
Relatives may NOT testify together. The verse merely teaches that this commandment applies to all Israel — it does not override the standard rules disqualifying relatives from joint testimony.
Rav Ḥanan bar Rava
The halakha follows Rabbi Shimon! I stated this before Rav many times and he never objected — his silence implies agreement.
Rav Huna
Silence is not agreement. The fact that Rav did not respond to you does not prove he concurred. Rav Ami and Rav Asi acted in accordance with Rabbi Shimon, but this does not definitively establish the halakha.
Practical Case Toviyya the doctor saw the new moon in Jerusalem with his son and his freed slave. The priests accepted him and his son as witnesses but disqualified the slave — a slave lacks witness standing entirely, regardless of who accompanies him.

⚖️ Fit vs. Unfit Witnesses for New Moon Testimony

✅ Valid Witnesses

  • Free adult Jewish males of good standing
  • Father and son — each testifies separately
  • Toviyya the doctor and his son (jointly accepted)
  • A witness vouched for by his local court of twenty-three
  • Rabbi Nehorai, who traveled on Shabbat to Usha to vouch for a witness

❌ Disqualified Witnesses

  • Dice [קֻבִּיָּא] and game-of-chance players for money
  • Those who lend money with interest (usurers)
  • Pigeon racers who place wagers on races
  • Traders in Shemita (sabbatical year) produce
  • Slaves — disqualified even alongside valid witnesses
  • Close relatives testifying together (per the Rabbis)

🚶 Desecrating Shabbat to Deliver New Moon Testimony

לָכֶם — חִלּוּל שַׁבָּת דּוֹחֶה מִשּׁוּם כָּבוֹד הַחֹדֶשׁ, אֲפִלּוּ בִּסְפֵק
⚗️ Halakhic Ruling A witness who saw the new moon but cannot walk may be carried on a donkey or even in a bed — on Shabbat if necessary. Witnesses may carry weapons for protection against attack. For a journey requiring a full night-and-day of travel, Shabbat is explicitly overridden. The Gemara adds: even when it is uncertain whether the witness in the far city will actually be available — as in the case of Rabbi Nehorai traveling to Usha — we still permit desecrating Shabbat. The sanctification of the month takes precedence.
