🐑 When Do Sheep Conceive? The Animal Tithe Dispute

Rabbi Meir
Sheep conceive in Tishrei when 'the valleys are wrapped in grain.' Animal tithe year begins 1 Elul — juxtaposed to grain tithe, counted close to the crop's completion.
Rabbi Elazar
Sheep conceive in Adar when grain stalks 'shout for joy.' By 1 Tishrei the lambs are six months old — so the new animal tithe year begins then.
Rava
Resolution: Everyone agrees sheep conceive in Adar! The dispute is whether we follow the moment of conception (→ Tishrei, per Rabbi Elazar) or juxtapose animal tithe to grain tithe completion (→ Elul, per Rabbi Meir).
Verse "The flocks are clothed in the meadows… the valleys are wrapped in grain, they shout for joy, they also sing" (Psalms 65:14)

📜 New Year for Documents: Which King's Calendar?

Jewish Kings — 1 Nisan

  • Documents dated by Jewish king's reign
  • New Year begins 1 Nisan
  • Taught by Rav Ḥisda
  • Derived from Scripture on Jewish monarchy
  • E.g., 'Year 2 of King X' resets each Nisan

Gentile Kings — 1 Tishrei

  • Documents dated by gentile king's reign
  • New Year begins 1 Tishrei
  • Taught by Rav Pappa and Rav Naḥman
  • Also taught by Rabbi Zeira
  • Avoids contradiction between two mishnayot

🌙 Proof: Rosh HaShana Is the Day of Universal Judgment

תִּקְעוּ בַחֹדֶשׁ שׁוֹפָר בַּכֶּסֶה לְיוֹם חַגֵּנוּ — כִּי חֹק לְיִשְׂרָאֵל הוּא מִשְׁפָּט לֵאלֹהֵי יַעֲקֹב (תהלים פא:ד–ה)
Psalm 81:4–5 "Blow a shofar at the New Moon, at the covered time for our Festival day"

Which Festival has a covered (hidden) moon? Only Rosh HaShana — it is the only Festival on the 1st of the month, when the moon is not yet visible.

The very next verse: "For this is a statute for Israel, a judgment of the God of Jacob" — Rosh HaShana is the day God judges the entire world, Israel and the nations alike.

Baraita: The heavenly court does not assemble for judgment until the earthly court has first sanctified the new month.

🔔 Jubilee Year Timeline per Rabbi Yishmael son of R. Yoḥanan ben Beroka

1 Tishrei of the 50th year — Jubilee New Year begins; the year is counted and sanctified from Rosh HaShana
↓
Days 1–9 of Tishrei: Hebrew slaves are neither released (shofar not yet sounded) nor re-enslaved to their masters — they eat, drink, and rejoice
↓
10 Tishrei — Yom Kippur: Shofar is sounded throughout the land — "And you shall hallow the fiftieth year and proclaim freedom throughout the land" (Lev. 25:10)
↓
After shofar: Slaves are released to their homes; ancestral land-holdings are returned to original owners
↓
Rabbinic view: Jubilee begins only on Yom Kippur — 'you sanctify years from Tishrei 1, but not individual days before the 10th'
