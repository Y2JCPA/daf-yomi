🐑 When Does the Animal Tithe Year Begin?

Rabbi Meir
The New Year for animal tithes is 1 Elul. Flocks conceive when 'the valleys are wrapped in grain' (Adar). Five months later they are born — in Elul. Just as grain tithe is set aside after completion, so too animal tithe is set aside after the animals are born.
Rabbi Elazar & Rabbi Shimon
The New Year is 1 Tishrei. The flocks conceive in Adar and are born six months later in Tishrei. The animal tithe is set aside at the start of the new year — which is 1 Tishrei.
Rava
Resolution: Both agree flocks primarily conceive in Adar (when valleys are wrapped in grain). The dispute is about timing of separation: Rabbi Meir juxtaposes animal tithe to grain tithe — set aside after completion (Elul); Rabbi Elazar sets it aside at the beginning of the new year (Tishrei).
Source Verse (Psalms 65:14) "The flocks are clothed with the meadows, and the valleys are wrapped in grain; they shout for joy, they also sing."

🌙 Rosh Hashana: The Day of Divine Judgment

תִּקְעוּ בַחֹדֶשׁ שׁוֹפָר בַּכֵּסֶה לְיוֹם חַגֵּנוּ — כִּי חֹק לְיִשְׂרָאֵל הוּא מִשְׁפָּט לֵאלֹהֵי יַעֲקֹב
Proof from Psalms 81:4–5 "Blow a shofar at the New Moon, at the covered time (keseh) for our Festival day" — Which Festival falls when the moon is hidden? Only Rosh Hashana! All other Festivals fall mid-month when the moon is full. The very next verse declares: "For this is a statute for Israel, a judgment of the God of Jacob" — proving that Rosh Hashana (1 Tishrei) is the day God judges the entire world. A further verse in Deuteronomy (11:12) teaches that God's eyes are upon the land from the beginning of the year — from 1 Tishrei.

📜 New Year for Documents: Jewish vs. Gentile Kings

Jewish Kings

  • New Year begins: 1 Nisan
  • Documents dated from Nisan
  • Basis: Nisan is the 'first month' (Exodus 12:2)
  • Even if king was crowned in Adar, year 2 begins next Nisan
  • Authority: Rav Ḥisda (re: biblical verses)

Gentile Kings

  • New Year begins: 1 Tishrei
  • Documents dated from Tishrei
  • Basis: Rav Ḥisda derives from biblical verses
  • Distinction resolves apparent contradiction in Mishna
  • Practical: affects document validity in court

🎺 Rabbi Yishmael's Two-Phase Jubilee (Leviticus 25:10)

1 Tishrei of the 50th Year: The Jubilee is sanctified — the year is hallowed from Rosh Hashana onward ("you shall hallow the fiftieth year")
↓
Interim state (1–10 Tishrei): Hebrew slaves are not yet released to their homes (shofar not yet sounded) but also not enslaved to their masters
↓
Slaves "eat, drink, and rejoice" — a liminal status: free in spirit, awaiting formal proclamation of liberty
↓
10 Tishrei — Yom Kippur: The shofar is sounded — "you shall proclaim liberty (deror) throughout the land" (Lev. 25:10)
↓
After the shofar blast: slaves return to their families, fields revert to original owners, Jubilee takes full legal effect
