QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava's resolution, when do sheep primarily conceive — and what is the actual dispute between Rabbi Meir and Rabbi Elazar?
A. Sheep conceive in Tishrei; the dispute is about which verse to use
B. Sheep conceive in Adar; the dispute is whether to count from conception or from the grain-tithe analogy
C. Sheep conceive in Nisan; the dispute is about late vs. early lambs
D. Sheep conceive in Elul; the dispute is about the Elul vs. Tishrei new year
Correct: B — Rava says everyone agrees sheep conceive in Adar. The dispute is only about what triggers the tithe year: Rabbi Elazar counts six months from conception (→ 1 Tishrei), while Rabbi Meir juxtaposes animal tithe to grain tithe completion (→ 1 Elul).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara identify 'the covered Festival' in Psalm 81:4 specifically as Rosh HaShana?
A. Because Rosh HaShana is the only Festival when shofar is sounded
B. Because Rosh HaShana is the only Festival occurring on the 1st of the month, when the moon is hidden
C. Because the psalm explicitly names Rosh HaShana
D. Because Yom Kippur is called 'covered' due to fasting
Correct: B — The word 'covered' (בַּכֶּסֶה) refers to the hidden moon. Rosh HaShana is uniquely the only Festival that falls on the 1st of the month, when the new moon is not yet visible — making it the only Festival with a 'covered moon.'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the baraita, what must happen before the heavenly court can assemble for judgment on Rosh HaShana?
A. The shofar must be sounded one hundred times
B. The High Priest must complete the Temple service
C. The earthly court must first sanctify the new month
D. The king must be brought in and judged
Correct: C — The baraita teaches: 'the heavenly court does not assemble for judgment until the earthly court has sanctified the month.' This shows the profound halakhic power of the Sanhedrin's calendar decisions — even heavenly proceedings depend on them.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yishmael son of Rabbi Yoḥanan ben Beroka, what is the status of Hebrew slaves between 1 Tishrei and Yom Kippur of the Jubilee year?
A. They are immediately freed on 1 Tishrei when the Jubilee year begins
B. They are neither released to their homes nor can their masters re-enslave them — an intermediate state
C. They continue serving their masters normally until Yom Kippur
D. They are freed but must remain in their masters' houses until Yom Kippur
Correct: B — Rabbi Yishmael holds that while the Jubilee year begins on 1 Tishrei, the shofar releasing slaves is only sounded on Yom Kippur (the 10th). During those ten days, slaves eat, drink, and celebrate — neither formally free nor re-enslaved.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rav Ḥisda gives two reasons the king is judged before his community. What are they?
A. The king's sins are greater; and the Torah commands this order explicitly
B. It is improper for the king to wait outside; and so the king won't hear verdicts and become angry
C. The king intercedes for his people; and royal judgment is simpler
D. The king brings the sacrifices first; and judgment follows the sacrificial order
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda offers two rationales: (1) it is not proper conduct for a king to stand outside waiting while subjects are judged, and (2) if the king overheard bad verdicts for his subjects, he might become angry — so he is brought in first and leaves before the community's judgment.
