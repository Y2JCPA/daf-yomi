⚖️ Can a Heavenly Decree Be Torn Up?

A heavenly sentence is issued against a person or community
↓
Individual
Disputed: Rabbi Meir — prayer always helps; Rabbi Elazar — only before sentence; Rabbi Yitzḥak — even after, it can be rescinded
Community
Rav Shmuel bar Inya: a community's sentence is never permanently sealed — repentance always avails
↓
Critical exception: A sentence accompanied by God's oath cannot be torn up — derived from I Samuel 3:14 (house of Eli)

🩺 Two Sick Men — Why Does One Recover?

Rabbi Meir
Two people fall ill with the same illness; one recovers and one does not. The difference: one prayed and was answered, the other prayed and was not answered. Prayer can always make a difference for an individual.
Rabbi Elazar
Not so. The difference is timing: one prayed before his heavenly sentence was issued and was answered; the other prayed after his sentence was issued and was therefore not answered.
Rabbi Yitzḥak
Even after the sentence is issued it can be torn up — as it says 'Seek the Lord while He may be found' (Isaiah 55:6), implying God is sometimes near and sometimes not, yet repentance can still help.
Core Dispute Does prayer avail after a heavenly sentence has already been decreed against an individual — or only beforehand?

🏛️ Community Sentences — When Can They Be Torn Up?

Verse A (Jer. 4:14): 'Wash your heart from wickedness, that you may be saved' — repentance CAN avert the decree
↓
Verse B (Jer. 2:22): 'Though you wash yourself with lye, the stain of your iniquity is before Me' — repentance CANNOT avert
↓
Resolution: Both verses speak of after the decree — but they differ by one factor
↓
Decree without Oath
Repentance can still tear it up — Jer. 4:14 applies
Decree + God's Oath
Cannot be torn up even by repentance — Jer. 2:22 applies; proved from I Sam. 3:14 (Eli's house)

👤 Individual vs. 🏘️ Community Judgment

Individual

  • Sentence can be permanently sealed (neḥtam)
  • Rabbi Elazar: prayer only effective before sentence
  • Special time of closeness: Ten Days between Rosh HaShana and Yom Kippur (Rabba bar Avuh)
  • Isaiah 55:6 — 'Seek the Lord while He may be found' refers to an individual's ten-day window
  • Sin of Eli's house: atoneable through Torah study (Rava/Abaye) despite divine oath

Community

  • Sentence never permanently sealed (Rav Shmuel bar Inya)
  • Repentance always available — unless oath accompanies the decree
  • Rosh HaShana: all pass before God like benei maron — a flock of sheep
  • All are scanned in a single simultaneous scan (Rabbi Yoḥanan)
  • Psalms 33:15: 'He who fashions their hearts alike, who considers all their deeds'

📜 Megillat Ta'anit — Nullified in Exile?

Rav & Rabbi Ḥanina
Megillat Ta'anit has been nullified. Those festive days were created in a time of joy and miracles. In the present exile, with no Temple and ongoing persecution, the joy is gone and the days no longer apply — fasting is permitted on them.
R. Yoḥanan & R. Yehoshua b. Levi
Megillat Ta'anit has NOT been nullified. The Biblical fasts (4th, 5th, 7th, 10th months) were contingent on the Temple — but Megillat Ta'anit's commemorated miracles stand independently and remain binding.
Rav Yosef / Abaye
Hanukkah is a special case regardless of the general debate — its miracle is well known and universally accepted by all of Israel, making nullification inappropriate even according to those who nullify the rest.
Practical Conclusion Hanukkah was never nullified: its miracle is famous and its observance universal — it stands on its own merits beyond the Megillat Ta'anit dispute.
