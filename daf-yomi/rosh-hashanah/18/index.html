⚖️ Can a Heavenly Sentence Be Rescinded?

Rabbi Meir
Two people with the same illness — one recovers, one does not. The difference: this one prayed a full prayer with intercessors and was answered; that one did not pray with full heart.
Rabbi Elazar
Not so — the issue is timing. This one prayed before the sentence was issued and was answered; that one prayed after the sentence and was not answered.
Rabbi Yitzḥak
Even after a community sentence is decreed, repentance can tear it up — 'Return to Me and I will return to you' (Zechariah 1:3).
Resolution A sentence accompanied by God's oath cannot be rescinded — derived from Eli's house (1 Samuel 3:14). A regular decree may be overturned by communal repentance even after it is issued.

🙏 Two People, Same Illness — Why Different Outcomes?

Rabbi Meir
Quality of prayer is decisive. One prayed wholeheartedly with intercessors and was answered. The other prayed but not with full concentration — so was not answered. Prayer always has power if offered properly.
Rabbi Elazar
Timing of prayer is decisive. Prayer before the heavenly sentence is issued — answered. Prayer after the sentence — not answered. The window matters, not just the intensity.
When Is God Near? (Rabba bar Avuh) 'Seek the Lord while He may be found' (Isaiah 55:6) — for an individual this refers to the Ten Days of Repentance between Rosh HaShana and Yom Kippur, the optimal window for prayer before sentence is sealed.

🏠 The Family Dying at Eighteen

לא יכופר עון בית עלי בזבח ובמנחה — אבל מתכפר בתורה
Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai's Ruling A Jerusalem family whose members consistently died at age eighteen came to Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai. He diagnosed them as descendants of Eli the Priest, whose house was cursed with early death (1 Sam. 2:33). His remedy: engage in Torah study.

Rava: The sin of Eli's house is not atoned through sacrifice or offering — but is atoned through Torah study.
Abaye adds: Torah study alone atones; Torah study combined with acts of lovingkindness both atones and prolongs life.

They followed this advice and lived.

🕯️ Is Megillat Ta'anit Still in Force?

Nullified (Rav & Rabbi Ḥanina)

  • Megillat Ta'anit was established when miracles occurred and there was reason to rejoice
  • Now in exile, the basis for those festive days no longer applies
  • Zechariah's fasts: contingent on conditions — peace → joy; persecution → fasting; neither → optional
  • Without Temple or national sovereignty, the commemorative framework has lapsed

Not Nullified (Rabbi Yoḥanan & Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi)

  • Biblical fast days are contingent on the Temple — but Megillat Ta'anit records separate rabbinic-era miracles
  • Those miracles are independent of the Temple and remain historically binding
  • Hanukkah: its miracle is widely known throughout all Israel — inappropriate to nullify it
  • The mitzva of candle-lighting gives Hanukkah ongoing life beyond mere commemoration
