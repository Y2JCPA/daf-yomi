🌳 When Is the New Year for Trees?

Beit Shammai
The new year for trees is on the 1st of Shevat. Most of the year's rains have already fallen, making it a natural turning point for tree growth.
Beit Hillel
The new year for trees is on the 15th of Shevat (Tu BiShvat). The sap begins rising at this later point in winter, marking a new cycle.
R' Elazar b. R' Oshaya
Even though most of winter is yet to come, since most of the year's rains have already fallen by the 1st of Shevat, it marks the end of the prior rainfall-year — supporting Beit Shammai's logic.
Halakha The halakha follows Beit Hillel — the 15th of Shevat (Tu BiShvat / ט"ו בשבט) is the new year for trees.

📖 Threshing Floor & Winepress — Two Interpretations

Rabbi Yosei HaGelili
"After you have gathered in from your threshing floor and your winepress" — just as grain goes to the threshing floor in its finished state, produce is only obligated in tithes when brought to its final processing place.
Rabbi Akiva
The verse shows: just as grain and wine reach their designated finishing places, so too tithe obligation is tied to reaching the equivalent completion stage — even for produce that lacks a threshing floor or winepress equivalent.
Practical Difference (Rabbi Abbahu) Seedless onions and cowpeas — they have no threshing floor or winepress, so the two positions diverge: does the threshing-floor model apply by analogy, or not?

🍋 Is an Etrog Like a Tree or a Vegetable?

🌳 Like a Tree

  • Orla applies — fruit of first 3 years forbidden
  • Revai — 4th-year fruit must be redeemed
  • Tithe year follows picking time (Rabban Gamliel)
  • New year for tithing: 15th of Shevat

🌿 Like a Vegetable

  • Orla does not apply
  • Revai does not apply
  • Tithe year follows formation of fruit (Rabbi Eliezer)
  • New year for tithing: 1st of Tishrei (per Rabba bar Rav Huna)

🍋 Rabbi Akiva's Two Tithes — Acting Under Doubt

Situation: Rabbi Akiva picked an etrog on the 1st of Shevat in the 2nd or 5th year of the Sabbatical cycle
↓
The Doubt: He wished to follow Beit Hillel but could not recall — did Beit Hillel say 1st or 15th of Shevat?
↓
If 1st of Shevat is the new year → already the 3rd/6th year → must give maaser ani (poor man's tithe)
↓
If 15th of Shevat is the new year → still in the 2nd/5th year → must give maaser sheni (second tithe)
↓
Resolution: Rabbi Akiva set aside both tithes to satisfy either possibility — a stringency born of genuine uncertainty, not adopting conflicting schools
