🌳 When Does the Tree Year Begin?

Beit Shammai
The new year for trees is the 1st of Shevat. By then, most of the year's rains have already fallen and the sap has begun to rise — the old year of rainfall is behind us.
Beit Hillel
The new year for trees is the 15th of Shevat (Tu B'Shevat). The extra two weeks of rainfall are fully absorbed, completing the prior year's moisture cycle. This is the halakha.
Practical Consequence Rabbi Akiva picked an etrog on the 1st of Shevat and set aside two tithes — one per opinion — not to adopt both stringencies, but because he was genuinely uncertain whether Beit Hillel said 1st or 15th of Shevat.

📖 Expounding 'Threshing Floor and Winepress' (Deut. 16:13)

R. Yosei HaGelili
Just as grain at the threshing floor and wine at the press are exposed to sun and wind during processing, so too the four species / produce governed by this verse must be similarly exposed — sheltered or underground produce is excluded.
R. Akiva
Just as grain and wine are edible food products — not waste or refuse, so too produce governed by this verse must be fit for consumption. Refuse and inedible byproducts are excluded; food items qualify.
Practical Difference (Rabbi Abbahu) Seedless onions and cowpeas: R. Yosei HaGelili would exclude them (they are not sun/wind-exposed like threshing floor grain). R. Akiva would include them (they are food items, not waste). See Mishna for details.

⚖️ The Etrog: Tree or Vegetable for Tithing?

🌳 Like an Ordinary Tree (3 Ways)

  • עָרְלָה Orla: Fruit of first 3 years after planting is forbidden
  • נֶטַע רְבָעִי Revai: 4th-year fruit must be redeemed and taken to Jerusalem
  • R. Eliezer: Tithe year follows time of fruit formation — like all fruit trees

🥬 Like a Vegetable (1 Way)

  • Rabban Gamliel: Tithe year follows the time of picking — like vegetables
  • Consequence (Rabba bar Rav Huna): Its new year for tithing would be 1 Tishrei
  • R. Akiva was in doubt between R. Eliezer and Rabban Gamliel — hence two tithes

🍋 Rabbi Akiva's Etrog Incident — Step by Step

The Setting: 2nd or 5th year of the Sabbatical (shemita) cycle. Rabbi Akiva picks an etrog on the 1st of Shevat.
↓
The Problem: Is it still the 2nd/5th year (→ Maaser Sheni, second tithe) or already the 3rd/6th year (→ Maaser Ani, poor man's tithe)?
↓
Per Beit Shammai: 1st Shevat = Rosh Hashana → new year has begun → it is already the 3rd/6th year → set aside poor man's tithe
↓
Per Beit Hillel: 1st Shevat = still the old year → still the 2nd/5th year → set aside second tithe
↓
R. Akiva's Resolution: He set aside both tithes. The Gemara clarifies: this was not adopting both stringencies — it was genuine doubt (safek) about whether Beit Hillel's date was the 1st or the 15th.
↓
R. Yosei bar Yehuda's alternate explanation: R. Akiva was uncertain between Rabban Gamliel (etrog follows picking → 1 Tishrei) and R. Eliezer (follows formation → 15 Shevat), not about Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel at all.
