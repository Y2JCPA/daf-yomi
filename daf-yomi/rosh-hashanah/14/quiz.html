Rosh Hashanah 14 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to which authority is the new year for trees on the 1st of Shevat?
A. Beit Hillel
B. Beit Shammai
C. Rabbi Akiva
D. Rabban Gamliel
Correct: B — The mishna states Beit Shammai holds the new year for trees is the 1st of Shevat. Beit Hillel places it on the 15th of Shevat (Tu BiShvat), and the halakha follows Beit Hillel.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Akiva set aside two tithes when picking an etrog on the 1st of Shevat?
A. He always followed both Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel as a stringency
B. He was uncertain whether Beit Hillel placed the new year on the 1st or 15th of Shevat
C. The etrog requires double tithing by Torah law
D. He was following Rabban Gamliel and Rabbi Eliezer simultaneously
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that Rabbi Akiva wanted to follow Beit Hillel but was uncertain about his tradition — whether Beit Hillel said 1st or 15th of Shevat. Each possibility required a different tithe, so he gave both.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What reason does Rabbi Elazar give (in the name of Rabbi Oshaya) for the 1st of Shevat being significant for tree tithing?
A. It is when tree sap visibly begins to rise
B. It falls exactly at the midpoint of winter
C. Most of the year's rains have already fallen by that date
D. The Sanhedrin traditionally convened on that date
Correct: C — Rabbi Elazar citing Rabbi Oshaya explains: even though most of winter is yet to come, since most of the year's rains have already fallen by the 1st of Shevat, it marks the end of the previous rainfall-year — validating Beit Shammai's date as a logical boundary.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabba bar Rav Huna, if Rabban Gamliel holds that etrog tithing follows picking time (like a vegetable), what is its new year for tithing?
A. 1st of Shevat
B. 15th of Shevat (Tu BiShvat)
C. 1st of Nisan
D. 1st of Tishrei
Correct: D — Rabba bar Rav Huna reasons: since Rabban Gamliel holds etrog follows picking time like a vegetable, its new year for tithing should be the 1st of Tishrei — just like other vegetables, whose tithe year begins in Tishrei.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the practical halakhic difference between Rabbi Yosei HaGelili and Rabbi Akiva in their interpretation of the threshing floor verse?
A. Whether grain and olives can be mixed for tithing purposes
B. When the obligation of sukkah begins relative to the harvest season
C. The tithe status of seedless onions and cowpeas
D. Whether poor man's tithe applies to produce purchased in the market
Correct: C — Rabbi Abbahu identifies the practical difference: seedless onions and cowpeas, which lack a threshing floor or winepress equivalent. Their tithe status depends on which interpretation of the verse one adopts.
