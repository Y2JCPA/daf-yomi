⚖️ Rabba vs. Rav Hamnuna: Sixth-Year Etrog Picked in the Sabbatical Year

Rabba
A sixth-year etrog picked in the Sabbatical Year is exempt from tithes (follows picking = Sabbatical produce) and also exempt from biur — because everyone enters the orchards and touches the fruit, rendering it ownerless!
Abaye
Granted, the more stringent rule applies for biur — it follows formation (sixth year), so biur doesn't apply. But then for tithes, it should also follow formation and be subject to tithes. You can't exempt it from both!
Rabba
Everyone's hand is touching it — it is ownerless in the Sabbatical Year regardless. Even if it were sixth-year produce, tithes don't apply to ownerless fruit. So it is exempt from tithes on two separate grounds.
Rav Hamnuna
A sixth-year etrog picked in Sabbatical is always sixth-year: subject to tithes, exempt from biur. A Sabbatical-year etrog picked after Sabbatical is always seventh-year: exempt from tithes, subject to biur. One rule, applied consistently.
Core Dispute Can an etrog be exempt from both tithes AND biur simultaneously, or must one rule govern it consistently?

📜 Three Positions on Etrog Year Determination

Rabban Gamliel

  • Tithes follow picking time
  • Treated like a vegetable for tithe year
  • New year still = 15 Shevat
  • Split identity: vegetable rule, tree calendar

Avtolemos (5 Elders)

  • Tithes follow picking time
  • Sabbatical Year follows fruit formation
  • Dual rule: different criteria for different laws
  • Preserved in testimony before the Sages

Sages — Voted at Usha

  • Both tithes and Sabbatical Year follow fruit formation
  • Single consistent rule across all laws
  • Majority opinion — overrides Avtolemos
  • Reflected in the baraita of Rabbi Shimon

🤫 Why Was Rabbi Yoḥanan Silent? Rabbi Abba's Analysis

Rabbi Yoḥanan: The people practice with carobs like Rabbi Neḥemya — tithe year follows the time of picking.
↓
Reish Lakish objects: The mishna about white figs shows Sabbatical Year follows formation, not picking — contradicting that practice!
↓
Rabbi Yoḥanan goes silent. Rabbi Abba asks: Why? He could have said — 'I am speaking of Rabbi Neḥemya's opinion, not the Rabbis'!
↓
Rabbi Yosei responds: Reish Lakish would counter — do you abandon the majority (Rabbis) to act on Rabbi Neḥemya's minority view?
↓
Rabbi Abba: But Yoḥanan could have said — 'I only describe the custom of the people; I am not ruling it as law!'
↓
Rabbi Yosei: Reish Lakish would say — then you should stop the people from this custom, since it violates the Rabbis' ruling!
↓
Rabbi Abba's conclusion: He wonders whether Reish Lakish even raised this objection, given its clear refutations — but the tradition confirms that he did.

📅 The Leap Year Question

ט״ו בשבט — ראש השנה לאילנות
Rava / Rabbi Yoḥanan Inquired In a leap year with two months of Adar, does the new year for trees shift from 15 Shevat to 15 First Adar — the penultimate month? Answer (Rav Naḥman / Rabbi Yannai): No. The new year for trees remains in Shevat even during a leap year. The insertion of an extra Adar does not relocate the botanical new year. Tu B'Shvat is Tu B'Shvat.
