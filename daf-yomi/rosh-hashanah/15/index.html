⚖️ Core Dispute: Status of the Sixth-Year Etrog Picked in the Sabbatical Year

Rabba
Exempt from tithes (everyone touches it — it is ownerless) and exempt from biur. Both leniencies apply.
Rav Hamnuna
Always sixth-year produce: subject to tithes, exempt from biur. A 7th-year etrog picked in the 8th year is always Sabbatical — exempt from tithes, subject to biur.
Rabbi Yochanan & Reish Lakish
Always sixth-year produce — agreeing with Rav Hamnuna: subject to tithes, not subject to Sabbatical laws.
Tannaitic Root Avtolemos (5 Elders): etrog follows picking for tithes. But the Sages voted in Usha: it follows fruit formation — making 6th-year etrog subject to Sabbatical produce rules.

⚔️ Rabba vs. Abaye: Is the Sabbatical Etrog Subject to Biur?

Rabba
6th-year etrog picked in Sabbatical: exempt from tithes (ownerless — all enter and touch it) and exempt from biur. Both leniencies.
Abaye
Granted, exempt from tithes. But the fruit formed in the Sabbatical Year! Follow the stringent side — it should be subject to biur!
Rabba
כולא עלמא ידיה בה מושיט — Everyone's hand touches it! It is ownerless in the field. You still wish to require tithes? Even by rabbinic decree this is exempt.
Rabbi Shimon ben Yehuda (baraita)
6th-year etrog picked in Sabbatical: exempt from tithes AND exempt from biur — fully supporting Rabba's position.
Difficulty This baraita's first clause challenges Rav Hamnuna (who says it IS subject to tithes); its latter clause challenges Rabba's opponent. The dispute is thus traced to a tannaitic root.

🌳 Multi-Brood vs. Single-Brood Trees — Tithing at Tu B'Shvat

Multi-Brood Trees

  • Produce two or more crops per year
  • Fruit formed before 15 Shevat → tithed with prior year
  • Fruit formed after 15 Shevat → tithed with coming year
  • Two crops can fall in different tithe years
  • Examples: fig trees, pomegranates, grapes

Single-Brood Trees

  • Only one crop per year — no split possible
  • Fruit formed before 15 Shevat → still tithed with coming year
  • Cannot straddle two tithe years — entire crop goes together
  • Examples: palms, olives, carobs
  • Carob: popular custom follows Rabbi Neḥemya — tithe by picking time

🤫 Why Was Rabbi Yochanan Silent? — Rabbi Abba's Reconstruction

Reish Lakish objects: White figs demonstrate that the Sabbatical Year follows formation time, not picking — but you say carobs follow picking!
↓
Rabbi Yochanan is silent — offers no rebuttal, as if he had no answer.
↓
Rabbi Abba's first suggestion: He could have said — 'I only speak of popular custom, not ruling!'
↓
Rabbi Yosei's answer: Reish Lakish would respond — 'Do you abandon the majority Rabbis to follow Rabbi Neḥemya alone?'
↓
Rabbi Abba's decisive point: He could have said — carob tithe is only rabbinic! By Torah law, only grapes and olives require tithe. Why apply full Sabbatical stringency?
↓
Rabbi Abba concludes: Given this clear refutation existed, he doubts whether Reish Lakish actually raised the objection at all.
