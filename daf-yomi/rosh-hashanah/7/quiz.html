QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda, what is the primary scriptural proof that Nisan is the first month?
A. Exodus 12:2 — 'This month shall be to you the beginning of months'
B. Leviticus 23:39 — Tishrei is called 'the seventh month' at harvest, implying Nisan is first
C. Zechariah 1:7 — Shevat is called 'the eleventh month,' counting from Nisan
D. Esther 3:7 — Nisan is explicitly called 'the first month'
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda derives it from Leviticus 23:39, which calls the harvest month (Tishrei) 'the seventh month.' Since Tishrei is seventh, Nisan — seven months earlier — must be the first month.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak say 'New Year for leap years on Nisan 1' means in the baraita?
A. The court begins deliberating whether to declare a leap year on Nisan 1
B. Nisan 1 is the deadline — after it, the court can no longer declare the prior year a leap year
C. The additional month of Adar II must begin no later than Nisan 1
D. The calendar for the coming leap year is finalized and published on Nisan 1
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak explains that the baraita means Nisan 1 is the end of the leap-year window: once Nisan arrives, the preceding year can no longer have Adar II added to it.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the ruling regarding communal offerings brought on Nisan 1 from the prior year's (old) shekels?
A. Entirely invalid; the offering does not count and must be repeated
B. Valid only if approved in advance by the court
C. Valid b'di'avad (after the fact), though l'chatchilah the new shekels must be used
D. Valid only if no new shekels had yet been collected in that community
Correct: C — The baraita and the Gemara rule that l'chatchilah one must use the newly collected shekels, but if one brought communal offerings from the old contributions, the offerings are valid b'di'avad.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, why are the New Years of the omer (16 Nisan) and the two loaves (6 Sivan) not listed in the mishna?
A. They are Rabbinic enactments, not Biblical New Years
B. They apply only when the Temple stands and the mishna addresses permanent law
C. They do not begin in the evening — the mishna counts only New Years that start at nightfall
D. They depend on individual action rather than a court declaration
Correct: C — Rav Pappa's rule is that the mishna lists only New Years that commence at nightfall. Both the omer (permitting new grain) and the two loaves take effect during the day, not at night — so they are excluded.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which tanna's opinion underlies the mishna's ruling that the first of Elul is the New Year for animal tithes?
A. Rabbi Akiva
B. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
C. Rabbi Meir
D. Rabbi Shimon
Correct: C — The Gemara identifies the mishna's ruling on animal tithes as reflecting the opinion of Rabbi Meir, who holds Elul 1 is the New Year for this tithe. Rabbi Akiva disagrees and holds it is Tishrei 1.
