QUESTION 1 OF 5
How does Rav Ḥisda derive that Nisan is the first month of the year?
A. From Exodus 12: 'This month shall be the beginning of months'
B. From Leviticus 23:39: the harvest is in the seventh month (Tishrei), counting back makes Nisan month one
C. From Zechariah 1:7: 'the eleventh month, which is Shevat'
D. From Esther 3:7: the lots were cast in the first month, Nisan
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda uses Lev. 23:39 — since Tishrei is the gathering/harvest month and is called 'the seventh month,' counting back makes Nisan month one. Iyyar and Marḥeshvan are ruled out as they have no ripening or gathering.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak, what does 'New Year for leap years' on 1st Nisan really mean?
A. The date when the Sanhedrin votes on whether to add an extra month
B. The beginning of the season during which intercalation is discussed
C. The end of the window — once Nisan arrives, the prior year can no longer be declared a leap year
D. The date the extra month of Adar II officially begins
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak resolves the apparent contradiction: the baraita means 1st Nisan is the closing deadline — from that point on, you cannot declare the previous year a leap year.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say the tanna of the mishna omitted 1st Nisan as a New Year for shekel collection?
A. Shekel collection starts in Tishrei, not Nisan
B. The mishna only lists New Years that begin in the evening
C. It is not a definitive New Year — old shekels remain valid post facto for communal offerings
D. Shekel obligations were considered Rabbinic, not Biblical
Correct: C — The mishna lists only definitive New Years. Since the Gemara teaches that communal offerings brought from old shekels are valid bedieved, the shekel New Year is not absolute — so the tanna excludes it.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, why are the New Years for the omer (16th Nisan) and the two loaves (6th Sivan) not counted in the mishna?
A. They are Rabbinic enactments, not Biblical law
B. They are subsumed under the New Year for Festivals
C. They do not begin in the evening — they take effect during the day, not at nightfall
D. They depend on agricultural conditions and cannot be fixed calendar dates
Correct: C — Rav Pappa states the mishna counts only New Years that begin in the evening (with nightfall). The omer permission begins on the 16th of Nisan by day, and the two loaves on the 6th of Sivan by day — neither begins at night.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If someone rents a house 'for a year' and fewer than 30 days remain before the next Nisan, what is the halacha?
A. The rental ends when Nisan arrives regardless of how few days remain
B. The rental is extended to the following Nisan, one full additional year later
C. The rental defaults to a Tishrei-based year instead
D. The tenant must vacate immediately and negotiate a new term
Correct: B — Since no one rents a house for less than 30 days, the Gemara rules that if fewer than 30 days remain before Nisan, the rental period extends to the next Nisan — giving the tenant the full benefit of a meaningful rental period.
