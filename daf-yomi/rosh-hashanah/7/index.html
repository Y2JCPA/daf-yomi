📅 Why Is Nisan the First Month?

Baraita: 1st of Nisan is New Year for months — but how do we know?
↓
Rav Ḥisda
Lev. 23:39 — harvest in seventh month (Tishrei) → count back 7 → Nisan = month 1
Ravina
Zech. 1:7 — "eleventh month, which is Shevat" → count forward → Nisan = month 1
Rav Kahana
Zech. 7:1 — "ninth month, Kislev" → count back → Nisan = month 1
↓
Why not Iyyar? No ripening. Why not Marḥeshvan? No gathering. Nisan alone satisfies all conditions.

🏛️ Deriving Nisan as the First Month

Rav Ḥisda
From Lev. 23:39 — 'when you gather in the fruit of the land in the seventh month.' That seventh month is Tishrei, the harvest month. Count back seven: Nisan is month one.
Gemara
But perhaps 'seventh' means seventh from Iyyar? Then the gathering month would be Marḥeshvan!
Rav Ḥisda
A month of gathering is required — in Marḥeshvan there is no gathering, since it was already gathered in Tishrei. So Marḥeshvan cannot be the month intended.
Ravina
This we did not learn from Moses at all — we learn it from the Prophets. Zechariah 1:7 says 'eleventh month, which is Shevat.' Shevat = 11, so Nisan = 1.
Rav Kahana
From Zechariah 7:1 — 'ninth month, in Kislev.' Count back nine from Kislev: Nisan is month one.
Why not from Exodus 12 directly? The verse 'This month shall be the beginning of months' doesn't specify which month — it could be Iyyar or Tishrei. The later sources are needed to pin it to Nisan specifically.

🔄 Leap Year Cutoff — When Does the Window Close?

Baraita: 1st of Nisan = New Year for leap years
↓
Problem: A baraita says courts may not declare a leap year before Rosh HaShana — how can Nisan be the New Year for this?
↓
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak: 'New Year for leap years' means the end of the window — from Nisan onward, the prior year cannot be made a leap year
↓
Until Purim
Since we ask Pesach laws 30 days before — after Purim, people expect Nisan and assume no leap year
R. Yehoshua & R. Papeyyas
Until Nisan itself — people understand calculation took time and accept a late declaration
↓
Once Nisan arrives: all agree — leap year cannot be declared

📊 Who Authored Which Part of the Mishna?

Rabbi Meir

  • 1st of Elul = New Year for animal tithes
  • 1st of Nisan = New Year for kings
  • His views allow the mishna's count of exactly four
  • Rav Yosef: entire mishna follows Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi synthesizing different tannaim

Rabbi Shimon

  • 1st of Nisan = New Year for Festivals
  • Delay prohibition transgressed if offering skips a Festival
  • Mishna adopts his Festival ruling
  • Other tannaim: delay only after all three Festivals pass

Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak

  • Mishna counts months with multiple New Years
  • Nisan has 2: kings + festivals
  • Tishrei has 2: years + sabbatical/jubilee
  • = Four months, not four individual dates — explains count of four

🪙 Shekel Collection and the Temple's Fiscal New Year

Adar: Annual half-shekel collected from all Israel; new funds earmarked for communal Temple offerings
↓
1st of Nisan: New Year — new shekels take effect; old shekels from prior year lose priority status
↓
L'chatchila (ideally): Communal offerings on 1st Nisan must come from the new annual collection
↓
Bedieved (post facto): If brought from old shekels — valid. If from personal funds donated to community — also valid, provided properly transferred without reservation
↓
Why not in the mishna's list? Because old shekels remain valid, this is not a definitive New Year — the tanna omits non-definitive New Years
