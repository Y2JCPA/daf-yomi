🔍 How Do We Know Nisan Is the First Month?

Gemara
From Exodus 12:2 — 'This month shall be to you the beginning of months' — the month of the Exodus is Month 1!
Objection
Perhaps the verse refers to Iyyar? We need a 'month of ripening,' and Iyyar could qualify...
Gemara
No — a 'month of ripening' is required, and by Iyyar the grain has already ripened. Nisan is the ripening month.
Rav Ḥisda
Proof from Leviticus 23:39 — Tishrei is called 'the seventh month' at harvest. Count back seven: Nisan = Month 1!
Objection
What if 'seventh' means 7th from Iyyar, making Marḥeshvan the gathering month?
Ravina
We learn from Prophets: Zechariah 1:7 calls Shevat 'the eleventh month.' Count forward — Nisan must be Month 1!
Rav Kahana
From Zechariah 7:1 — 'the ninth month, Kislev.' Counting back nine months leads to Nisan as Month 1.
Why Not the Clearest Proof? Esther 3:7 explicitly calls Nisan 'the first month' — but this was rejected as a proof-text because it may merely reflect prior knowledge rather than establish it independently.

🪙 Old vs. New Shekel Contributions for Temple Offerings

Old Contributions (לפני ניסן)

  • Collected during the prior calendar year
  • May not be used l'chatchilah on Nisan 1
  • If used, the offering is valid b'di'avad
  • One may donate old funds to the public — valid if fully transferred without reservation

New Contributions (מניסן ואילך)

  • Collected fresh from Adar of the current year
  • Must be used l'chatchilah for Nisan 1 communal offerings
  • Symbolizes communal renewal of Temple service each year
  • The mitzva: first bring the new, then use old surplus if needed

🧮 Why Are Omer and Two Loaves Excluded from the Mishna?

Rav Pappa's Rule: The mishna counts only New Years that begin in the evening. Both the omer (16 Nisan) and two loaves (6 Sivan) take effect during the day — not at nightfall.
↓
Challenge: The Jubilee Year does not begin at night — it starts with the shofar blast on Yom Kippur during the day — yet the mishna counts it!
↓
Answer: The mishna follows Rabbi Yishmael, who holds Jubilee begins at nightfall like other New Years — consistent with Rav Pappa's rule.
↓
Rav Sheisha's Alternative Rule: The mishna counts only New Years that do not depend on an action. Omer and two loaves depend on bringing the sacrifice itself.
↓
Challenge to Rav Sheisha: The Festival New Year depends on the daily offering — no festival vow-delay violation before it is brought — yet the mishna counts it! Answer: The daily offering is merely a prior condition, not the constitutive act.
