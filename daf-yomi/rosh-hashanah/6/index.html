🔍 Ben Azzai's Derivation & The Gemara's Rejection

Ben Azzai
The word it in the verse about notar (leftover sacrifice) teaches that the sin is imputed to you alone — not to your wife or household members.
Gemara
We reject that source. The verse 'it would be a sin in you' (Deut. 23:22) directly teaches that delay is imputed to you personally — not to your wife.
Question
Why would we even think the wife is liable? Because R. Yoḥanan taught: a man's wife dies only on account of his sins — so perhaps her death indicates she too bears culpability!
Resolution
Therefore the verse must explicitly exclude the wife and household — they did not take the vow and are not personally subject to bal te'acher.
📜 Source Verse Deuteronomy 23:22 — "For the Lord your God will surely require it from you"

🐑 Neder vs Nedavah — Vow-Offering vs Gift-Offering

נֶדֶר — Neder (Vow-Offering)

  • One says: 'I undertake to bring a burnt-offering'
  • No specific animal designated at time of vow
  • If animal later dies or is stolen: owner must replace it
  • Obligation rests on the person
  • Can transgress bal te'acher even before animal is set aside

נְדָבָה — Nedavah (Gift-Offering)

  • One says: 'This specific animal is a burnt-offering'
  • Specific animal is designated at time of vow
  • If animal dies or is stolen: no replacement needed
  • Obligation rests on the animal
  • Rava: even a nedavah can exist before animal is set aside (conditional vow)

💰 Charity Vows — Immediate Obligation

"וּבְפִיךָ" — זוֹ צְדָקָה (דברים כג:כד)
Rava's Ruling For vows of charity, one transgresses bal te'acher immediately upon delay — without waiting for three Festivals to pass. The reason: the poor are present and depend on receiving the charity now. One might have thought that since charity is grouped in the passage dealing with offerings, the same three-Festival rule applies — Rava teaches otherwise.

📅 Firstborn Animal — When Does the Year Count Begin?

Background: A firstborn (bechor) animal must be brought within one year — or the owner transgresses bal te'acher. But when does the clock start?
↓
Abaye: Count from the moment of birth — the animal enters sanctity at birth.
↓
Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov: Count from when the animal is fit for appeasement (ראוי לפייס) — i.e., when it is old enough and eligible to actually be brought.
↓
Gemara's Resolution: They do not disagree — Abaye speaks of a blemished firstborn (given to the priest to eat); Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov speaks of an unblemished firstborn that must be sacrificed on the altar.
↓
Related question: Can a year pass without three Festivals? Yes — in a leap year (Shavuot falls on varying dates: 5th, 6th, or 7th of Sivan depending on whether Nisan and Iyyar are full months).
