👁️ Valid vs. Invalid Moon Testimony

✅ Valid Testimony

  • Saw moon directly with naked eye
  • Heights differ by only 1–2 plow handles (minor discrepancy)
  • Testimony consistent on direction and tilt
  • Deliberate, sustained, confirmable observation

❌ Invalid Testimony

  • Saw moon reflected in water
  • Saw moon reflected in a glass lantern
  • Saw moon through thin clouds
  • Saw moon by chance, could not find it again deliberately

⚖️ Moon Not Seen at Anticipated Time: Does the Court Sanctify Next Day?

R. Elazar b. Tzadok
If the moon was not seen at its anticipated time, the court does not sanctify it the next day — the Heavenly Court has already sanctified it. No further declaration needed.
R. Elazar b. Shimon
In either case — whether the moon was seen or not — the court does not formally sanctify it on its own. From 'You shall sanctify the fiftieth year': only you (the earthly court) can sanctify formally, not Heaven alone.
Abaye
A mishna proves the court's formal declaration is essential: if the court did not say 'Sanctified' before nightfall, the month becomes a full 30 days — meaning earthly declaration cannot be replaced by Heaven's act.
Gemara's Rebuttal to Abaye The mishna needed to state the 30-day ruling anyway — one might have thought the widespread public sighting alone sanctified the month, even without the court's declaration. The mishna corrects this.

📋 Rabban Gamliel's Moon Diagram

לֹא תַעֲשׂוּן אִתִּי — You shall not make [images] with Me (Exodus 20:20)
🎨 Are Images of Celestial Bodies Permitted? Rabban Gamliel kept a diagram of moon phases on a tablet in his attic. He showed these forms to unlearned witnesses who struggled to describe the moon's appearance accurately. Problem: The Torah states: 'You shall not make with Me gods of silver' — interpreted as: do not make images of My heavenly attendants (sun, moon, stars)! Abaye's ruling: The Torah prohibits only images of attendants that can be exactly reproduced. Since no person can truly replicate the sun or the moon in its full glory, the prohibition does not apply to their depictions.
