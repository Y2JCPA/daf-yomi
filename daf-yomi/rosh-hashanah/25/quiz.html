5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What did Rabban Gamliel order Rabbi Yehoshua to do as a test of the court's authority?
A. Publicly declare before the people that the witnesses were truthful
B. Appear before him carrying his staff and money on the day Yehoshua calculated to be Yom Kippur
C. Resign from his position and cease ruling on calendar matters
D. Travel to the site where the moon was sighted and verify it himself
Correct: B — Rabban Gamliel ordered Yehoshua to come with his staff and money — items forbidden on Yom Kippur — on the very day Yehoshua believed was Yom Kippur. This compelled public acceptance of the court's ruling, whatever one privately believed.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the triple use of 'you' (atem) in 'which you shall proclaim' teach about the court's calendar authority?
A. Only the Sanhedrin in Jerusalem may ever sanctify the new month
B. The court's declaration is valid even if they erred, acted intentionally, or were misled by false witnesses
C. The entire Jewish people must unanimously agree before a month can be sanctified
D. Witnesses must be members of the court itself, not outsiders
Correct: B — Rabbi Akiva derives from the threefold emphasis on 'you' that the court's sanctification is binding in all cases: unwitting error (שׁוֹגְגִין), intentional act (מְזִידִין), and being misled by false witnesses (מוּטְעִין) — the proclamation stands regardless.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What vivid analogy did Rabbi Dosa ben Horkinas use to describe the logical impossibility of the early moon testimony?
A. A tree that bears ripe fruit before its blossom has even appeared
B. A student who claims mastery of a tractate he has never opened
C. A woman said to have given birth, yet the next day her belly is visibly between her teeth
D. A soldier who declares victory before the battle has begun
Correct: C — Rabbi Dosa argued: If witnesses say a woman gave birth, how can her belly still be visibly full the very next day? Likewise, if the moon was already sighted as new, it cannot have already waned and vanished so quickly — the testimony is self-contradictory.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Mishna, what happens if the court sees the new moon but does not declare 'Sanctified' before nightfall?
A. The court may reconvene the next morning and complete the declaration
B. The previous month automatically becomes a full thirty-day month
C. The witnesses must re-testify before a different panel the following day
D. The month is declared incomplete regardless of the court's later ruling
Correct: B — The Mishna rules that if the declaration of 'Sanctified' was not completed before nightfall, the previous month is automatically rendered a full (thirty-day) month. The window to sanctify on the twenty-ninth has irrevocably passed.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does 'Jephthah in his generation is like Samuel in his generation' teach about judicial authority?
A. Only the most learned and pious scholars may serve as authoritative judges
B. Jephthah and Samuel happened to hold identical legal opinions on most matters
C. The judge of each generation holds the full authority of that role regardless of personal stature
D. Lesser judges must always defer to greater ones rather than issuing independent rulings
Correct: C — The verse 'go to the judge who shall be in those days' implies you must go to whoever serves in your own generation. Scripture equates even flawed figures like Jephthah to the towering Samuel — the office grants authority, and each generation must heed its own court.
