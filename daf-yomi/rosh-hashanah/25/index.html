🌙 Can the Beit Din's Calendar Ruling Be Wrong Yet Still Binding?

Rabban Gamliel
My court accepted the witnesses' testimony and declared Rosh Chodesh. The sanctification stands — come before me with your staff and money on the day you call Yom Kippur!
Rabbi Yehoshua
The witnesses' account was impossible — the moon cannot appear where they described. Their testimony should have been rejected. My calculations say today is Yom Kippur!
Rabbi Akiva
Scripture says 'these are the appointed times of God… which you shall proclaim' — even if in error, even intentionally, even if misled, your proclamation is the appointed time.
Rabbi Dosa ben Horkinas
If we question Rabban Gamliel's court, we must question every court since Moses. The authority of the Beit Din cannot be undermined — their word is law.
Resolution Rabbi Yehoshua submitted: he carried his staff and money to Rabban Gamliel on what he believed was Yom Kippur. Rabban Gamliel rose and kissed him, saying: 'Come in peace, my teacher and my student.'

📜 The Key Verse: Leviticus 23:4

אֵלֶּה מוֹעֲדֵי ה׳ אֲשֶׁר תִּקְרְאוּ אֹתָם — אפילו שוגגין, אפילו מזידין, אפילו מוטעין
אֵלֶּה מוֹעֲדֵי ה׳ — These Are the Appointed Times "These are the appointed times of God, holy convocations, which you shall proclaim" (Leviticus 23:4). The word אתם (you) is interpreted: even if you err, even if you act intentionally, even if you are misled — your proclamation is the sacred appointed time. The Beit Din's declaration constitutes the reality of the calendar.

⚖️ Two Views on Judicial Authority

Absolute Authority

  • Court's declaration creates halachic reality
  • Binding even if witnesses erred
  • Binding even if court was misled
  • Individuals must conform to court's date
  • Supported by Rabbi Akiva & Rabbi Dosa

Astronomical Truth

  • Observable facts should govern
  • Impossible testimony cannot stand
  • Astronomical impossibility = false witness
  • Rabbi Yehoshua's initial position
  • Rabbi Yochanan ben Nuri's ruling

↓
Rabbi Yehoshua's distress: He was being asked to publicly desecrate what he believed was Yom Kippur by carrying objects
↓
Rabbi Akiva consoles him: Quotes three scriptural proofs that the court's authority is absolute and divinely sanctioned
