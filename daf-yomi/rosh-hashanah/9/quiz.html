Rosh Hashanah 9 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Rabbi Akiva derives the principle of tosefet (adding profane to sacred time) from which verse?
A. 'You shall afflict your souls on the ninth of the month in the evening' (Lev. 23:32)
B. 'In plowing and in harvest you shall rest' (Ex. 34:21)
C. 'From evening to evening shall you rest on your Shabbat' (Lev. 23:32)
D. 'And you shall hallow the fiftieth year' (Lev. 25:10)
Correct: B — Rabbi Akiva interprets 'In plowing and in harvest you shall rest' as referring to pre- and post-Sabbatical year farming — teaching that one must add from the profane to the sacred at both ends of the Shemitah year.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the Gemara, what does the verse 'You shall afflict your souls on the ninth of the month in the evening' actually teach?
A. One must fast literally on the 9th of Tishrei as a separate obligation
B. The Yom Kippur fast is only 12 hours, from the 9th evening to the 10th morning
C. One who eats and drinks on the 9th and then fasts on the 10th is credited as if he fasted on both days
D. Yom Kippur fasting begins only at nightfall of the 10th, not earlier
Correct: C — The verse does not mean to fast on the 9th itself. Rather, it teaches that one who honors Yom Kippur by eating and drinking on the 9th (building strength for the fast) and then fasting on the 10th is credited as though he fasted on both days.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Yosei consider shofar blowing — rather than slave liberation — to be the indispensable criterion of the Jubilee Year?
A. The shofar is mentioned first in the verse, establishing its primacy
B. The shofar is a court obligation that can always be fulfilled; slave liberation depends on each individual owner
C. Without the shofar sound, slaves cannot know they are free to leave
D. The word 'deror' specifically refers to the blowing of the shofar
Correct: B — Rabbi Yosei reasons that the shofar is given over to the court — a communal institution that can always comply. Slave liberation depends on each individual owner; the failure of one owner cannot invalidate the Jubilee for all, but the court's communal act can.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yehuda define the word deror (דְּרוֹר)?
A. A special type of shofar blown only during the Jubilee Year
B. The return of ancestral land to its original owners
C. A person who dwells wherever he pleases and may carry his possessions freely from place to place
D. A formal legal declaration made by the court on Yom Kippur
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehuda explains deror as freedom of movement and residence — like a bird that is free to go anywhere. One who has deror can live and travel where he wishes. This definition supports his view that slave liberation is the core act of the Jubilee.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the baraita on orla, if a tree is planted exactly 30 days before Rosh HaShana, what happens when Rosh HaShana arrives?
A. The tree must be uprooted because the orla prohibition has not yet begun
B. Those 30 days count as a full first year of orla, and Rosh HaShana marks the start of Year 2
C. The orla count does not begin until after the Sabbatical Year concludes
D. The 30 days do not count, and Year 1 of orla begins only at the following Rosh HaShana
Correct: B — The rule is that 30 days before Rosh HaShana is sufficient to count as a complete first year of orla. When Rosh HaShana arrives, it begins Year 2 — demonstrating that 1 Tishrei is the New Year for planting and orla year calculations.
