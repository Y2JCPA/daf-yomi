Rosh Hashanah 4 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Which of the following is NOT cited in the Gemara as proof that Cyrus became corrupt?
A. He limited timber rows in Temple construction
B. He had a she-dog (shegal) sitting beside him
C. He capped his donations at 100 talents of silver
D. He refused to allow the Temple to be rebuilt at all
Correct: D — All three proofs involve Cyrus limiting or tainting the Temple project — but he never refused to allow it. The decree to build was his; the corruption was in its conditions and his later pagan associations.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rabbi Meir holds the most stringent position on Bal Te'acher. What is it?
A. Three Festivals in their proper order must pass
B. Two Festivals must pass before one is liable
C. Even one Festival passing triggers the prohibition
D. Only after the Festival of Sukkot is one liable
Correct: C — Rabbi Meir derives from 'You shall seek Him and come there' that the Torah's positive command to appear at each Festival automatically creates liability for delay the moment one Festival is missed.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What unique requirement does Rabbi Shimon add to the three-Festival rule for Bal Te'acher?
A. The person must have been in Jerusalem for all three Festivals
B. The three Festivals must pass in their correct annual order: Passover, Shavuot, then Sukkot
C. Both the vow and the Festival must occur within the same calendar year
D. Only Temple-related vows are subject to this rule, not charity pledges
Correct: B — Rabbi Shimon holds that Sukkot must be the last of the three Festivals to pass — if the vow was made after Passover, the sequence begins with Shavuot, not Passover, and one only becomes liable after the next Sukkot completes the set.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Gemara, what does 'shegal' mean when used specifically in the verse about Cyrus in Nehemiah 2:6?
A. A royal consort or queen-mother
B. A golden throne or royal seat
C. A she-dog
D. A Persian court official
Correct: C — Rabba bar Lima preserved a tradition that in Nehemiah 2:6, 'shegal' specifically means a she-dog — while in all other biblical contexts (e.g. Psalms 45:10) it means consort. The Gemara even offers an etymology: the dog 'clings' (shogeyl) to its master.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The mishna states that 1 Nisan is the New Year for Festivals. What is the primary practical halakhic application of this ruling according to the Gemara?
A. It determines the date for purchasing Passover sacrifices
B. It establishes when the countdown begins for the Bal Te'acher prohibition on vows
C. It sets the beginning of the Temple service's annual rotation
D. It determines when tithes from the new year's produce take effect
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that 1 Nisan as the Festival New Year is practically relevant for calculating when a person who vows an offering begins accumulating Festival 'strikes' toward the Bal Te'acher violation. The Festival sequence that counts starts from Nisan.
