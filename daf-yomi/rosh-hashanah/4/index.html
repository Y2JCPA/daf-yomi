⚖️ How Many Festivals Trigger Bal Te'acher?

Rabbis
Three Festivals must pass before one transgresses 'You shall not delay' — Deuteronomy 16 lists all three Festivals together, implying all three are required
Rabbi Meir
Even ONE Festival triggers the prohibition — since the Torah commands appearing before God 'at that time,' missing that single moment already constitutes delay
R. Eliezer b. Ya'akov
TWO Festivals must pass — the phrase 'appointed times' is plural, and the minimum plural is two
R. Elazar b. R. Shimon
Once the festival of Sukkot specifically passes, one immediately transgresses — Sukkot is the concluding Festival of the year and closes the opportunity window
Why It Matters The dispute determines exactly when a person who vowed an offering or pledged charity becomes liable for the biblical prohibition of 'You shall not delay to pay it' (Deuteronomy 23:22)

🏛️ Three Proofs That Cyrus Became Corrupt

עַד מְאָה כַכְּרִין כְּסַף — 'Up to a hundred talents of silver' (Ezra 7:22)
Talmudic Textual Analysis Proof 1 — Inferior Construction: Cyrus commanded 'three rows of great stones and a row of new timber' (Ezra 6:4), whereas Solomon's Temple had stone and cedar beams throughout (I Kings 6:36). Cyrus downgraded the building materials.

Proof 2 — The She-Dog (Shegal): When Nehemiah petitioned Cyrus, 'the shegal was sitting by him' (Neh. 2:6). Per Rabba bar Lima's tradition, shegal here means a she-dog — a pagan symbol at the king's side, marking his corruption.

Proof 3 — Capped Donations: Cyrus wrote: 'Up to a hundred talents of silver, up to a hundred measures of wheat... and salt without limit' (Ezra 7:22). He set limits on everything valuable but added 'without limit' only for cheap salt — revealing a stingy spirit toward the Temple.

🐕 What Does 'Shegal' Mean?

Gemara (default reading)
Shegal = royal consort / queen. Proof from Psalms 45:10: 'Upon your right hand stands a shegal in gold of Ophir' — clearly describing a noblewoman, not an animal
Rabba bar Lima
By Cyrus specifically, shegal = she-dog! He received a tradition (קַבָּלָה) that in Nehemiah 2:6 this word refers to a dog seated beside the king — a symbol of pagan power
Gemara (resolution)
Both are true: in all other biblical contexts shegal means consort. Why call the dog 'shegal'? Because the dog clings to (שׁוֹגֵל) its master — a wordplay explaining the double meaning
Broader Significance This philological dispute is one of three independent proofs marshaled to demonstrate that Cyrus, despite initially championing the Temple, became spiritually compromised over time

🔢 The Bal Te'acher Dispute at a Glance

Rabbis (Anonymous)

  • 3 Festivals must pass
  • Order does NOT matter
  • Source: Deut. 16 lists all 3 Festivals
  • Most lenient majority view
  • Rosh Hashanah 1 Nisan begins count

Rabbi Shimon

  • 3 Festivals IN SEQUENCE
  • Must be Passover → Shavuot → Sukkot
  • Out-of-order = not yet liable
  • Mishna follows his opinion
  • Most demanding '3 Festival' view

Rabbi Meir

  • 1 Festival is enough
  • Source: 'You shall seek Him there'
  • Missing ANY opportunity = delay
  • Most stringent of all opinions
  • Positive command implies prohibition

R. Eliezer b. Ya'akov

  • 2 Festivals must pass
  • Source: 'appointed times' (plural)
  • Minimum plural = 2
  • Middle-ground position
  • R. Elazar b.R.Shimon: after Sukkot
