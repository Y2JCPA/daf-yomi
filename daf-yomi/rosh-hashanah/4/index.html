⚖️ How Many Festivals Trigger Bal Te'acher?

Rabbi Meir
Even ONE Festival passing triggers the prohibition — once commanded to appear and you did not bring your obligation, you immediately transgress.
R. Eliezer ben Ya'akov
TWO Festivals must pass — derived from 'at your appointed times' (plural), requiring at minimum two Festivals to elapse.
First Tanna (Rabbis)
THREE Festivals must pass in any order — the full cycle of 'three times a year' defines the window before transgression.
Rabbi Shimon
THREE Festivals must pass IN ORDER (Pesach → Shavuot → Sukkot) — the Torah's mention of Sukkot last was unnecessary unless sequential order matters.
R. Elazar b. Rabbi Shimon
Once SUKKOT passes, one immediately transgresses — Sukkot is the final Festival of the year and its passage marks the absolute deadline.
The Core Prohibition בַּל תְּאַחֵר — 'You shall not delay' (Deuteronomy 23:22): One who vows an offering or owes tithes and agricultural gifts must not let the festival cycle pass without fulfilling the obligation.

🌾 Agricultural Gifts and Bal Te'acher

לֶקֶט וְשִׁכְחָה וּפֵאָה — שְׁלֹשָׁה רְגָלִים עוֹבֵר עֲלֵיהֶם בְּבַל תְּאַחֵר
Three Gifts Owed to the Poor The prohibition בַּל תְּאַחֵר applies not only to Temple vows and offerings but also to three mandatory agricultural gifts: לֶקֶט (leket) — gleanings left for the poor, שִׁכְחָה (shikcha) — forgotten sheaves, and פֵּאָה (pe'ah) — the corner of the field. Once three Festivals pass without distributing these gifts, one has transgressed the prohibition of delay.

📅 Five Opinions on Bal Te'acher

Rabbi Meir

  • 1 Festival triggers violation
  • Source: 'You shall seek His presence' (Deut. 12)
  • Each Festival is an independent command; missing one = immediate transgression
  • Most stringent position

R. Eliezer ben Ya'akov

  • 2 Festivals trigger violation
  • Source: מוֹעֲדֶיךָ — 'your appointed times' (plural)
  • Plural form implies minimum of two
  • Intermediate position

First Tanna (Rabbis)

  • 3 Festivals in any order
  • Source: 'Three times a year' (Deut. 16)
  • Full Pesach–Shavuot–Sukkot cycle is the measure
  • Most widely held view

Rabbi Shimon

  • 3 Festivals in strict order
  • Sukkot listed last in the verse — intentional
  • Sukkot mention was superfluous unless order matters
  • Sequence: Pesach → Shavuot → Sukkot

🏛️ Shemini Atzeret — Independent Festival or Sukkot's Close?

Question: Is Shemini Atzeret (8th Day of Assembly) its own independent Festival, or merely the concluding day of Sukkot?
↓
Independent Festival
פז"ר קש"ב — 6 Distinctions:
Payyis (own lottery), Zman (own blessing), Regel (own pilgrimage status), Korban (unique sacrifice count), Shir (own psalm), Bracha (own festival blessing)
Extension of Sukkot
Practical ruling:
Festival peace-offering (חֲגִיגָה) not brought during Sukkot's seven days may still be brought on Shemini Atzeret — it 'catches up' the Sukkot obligation
↓
Halakhic balance: Shemini Atzeret is its own festival for most purposes, yet still extends the window for the Sukkot chagiga offering

🤏 'If You Grasped Many, You Grasped Nothing'

The Question: How many days does one have to bring the Shavuot Festival peace-offering (חֲגִיגָה)? Should we compare Shavuot to Pesach or to Sukkot?
↓
Option A — Compare to Sukkot: Both appear near each other in the verse (Deut. 16:16). Sukkot has 8 days (including Shemini Atzeret) → Shavuot should also have 8 days.
↓
Option B — Compare to Pesach: Both Pesach and Shavuot are explicitly bounded festivals. Pesach has 7 days → Shavuot likewise has only 1 day (no makeup days).
↓
Principle: אִם אָחַזְתָּ מְרוּבִּין לֹא אָחַזְתָּ, אִם אָחַזְתָּ מוּעָטִין אָחַזְתָּ — 'If you grasped many, you grasped nothing; if you grasped few, you grasped something.' When in doubt, choose the more conservative/smaller measure.
↓
Result: We compare Shavuot to Pesach (fewer makeup days), taking the stricter interpretation over Sukkot's broader window.
