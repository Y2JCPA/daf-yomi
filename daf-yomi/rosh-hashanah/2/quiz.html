Rosh Hashanah 2 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, which date is the New Year for animal tithes?
A. 1 Nisan
B. 1 Elul
C. 1 Tishrei
D. 1 Shevat
Correct: B — The Mishna states that 1 Elul is the New Year for animal tithes. R. Elazar and R. Shimon disagree, holding it is 1 Tishrei.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Rav Ḥisda say it matters that 1 Nisan is the fixed New Year for kings?
A. So the king can offer special sacrifices at the Temple on that day
B. Because legal documents were dated by regnal year, and a fixed New Year prevents fraud
C. To determine when the king's tax collection period begins
D. Because royal coronations must take place in the month of Nisan
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda explains that legal documents were dated by the king's regnal year. A fixed New Year (1 Nisan) allows courts to determine whether any document was improperly antedated or postdated.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If a king ascends the throne on 29 Adar, what happens when 1 Nisan arrives?
A. His reign officially begins only on 1 Nisan
B. Nothing changes — the full year must pass before any year is counted
C. Year 1 of his reign is considered complete, even though he ruled for only one day
D. He must hold a new coronation ceremony on 1 Nisan
Correct: C — The principle מקצת שנה ככולה (a partial year counts as a full year) applies here. His single day in Adar counts as a complete regnal year.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If the previous king died in Adar and the new king began his reign only in Nisan, what year does that Nisan count as for the new king?
A. Year 1 — the first Nisan of his reign is always Year 1
B. Year 2 — Year 1 is effectively skipped since he did not reign at all before that Nisan
C. Year 0 — his reign does not begin until the following Nisan
D. The same shared year as the previous king's last year
Correct: B — Since the new king did not reign at all before that Nisan (the old king died in Adar, and no days of Nisan were spent by the new king), that Nisan counts as Year 2 — Year 1 is skipped entirely.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara prove that the years from the Exodus are counted from Nisan rather than Tishrei?
A. From the verse 'this month shall be for you the head of months' (Exodus 12:2)
B. From the Tabernacle being erected on 1 Nisan in the second year after the Exodus
C. Aaron died on 1 Av in 'the 40th year' and Moses spoke on 1 Shevat in 'the 40th year' — since Av precedes Shevat, the year must have begun before Av, i.e., in Nisan
D. From a rabbinic tradition that the Israelites left Egypt specifically on 15 Nisan
Correct: C — Aaron died on 1 Av (Num. 33:38) and Moses delivered his speech on 1 Shevat (Deut. 1:3), both in 'the 40th year.' Since Av comes before Shevat, Aaron's death was earlier in that year — meaning the 40th year began before Av, pointing to Nisan as the start.
