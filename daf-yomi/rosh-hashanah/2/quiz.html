Rosh Hashanah 2 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda, what is the primary reason the Sages fixed 1 Nisan as the New Year for kings?
A. Because the Torah explicitly designates Nisan as the royal month
B. To prevent fraud in legal documents dated by regnal year
C. Because kings were traditionally coronated in Nisan
D. To align kings' years with the Jubilee cycle
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda explains that documents are dated by the king's regnal year. A fixed annual reset date (1 Nisan) prevents disputes about whether a document was antedated or postdated, protecting the integrity of contracts and loans.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
A king is crowned on 29 Adar. When 1 Nisan arrives one day later, what does the Baraita teach?
A. His first full year only begins the following Nisan
B. The single day does not count; years begin from coronation
C. That one day in Adar counts as his complete first year of reign
D. He must share Year 1 with the king who reigned before him
Correct: C — The Baraita establishes the principle that one day in a year counts as a full year (יום אחד בשנה חשוב שנה). The single day of reign before Nisan 1 is treated as a complete regnal year.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Mishna, who holds that the New Year for animal tithes is 1 Tishrei rather than 1 Elul?
A. Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel
B. Rav Ḥisda and Rav Naḥman
C. Rabbi Elazar and Rabbi Shimon
D. Rabbi Akiva and Rabbi Meir
Correct: C — The Mishna records a dispute: the Tanna Kamma (anonymous first opinion) says 1 Elul is the New Year for animal tithes, but Rabbi Elazar and Rabbi Shimon say it is 1 Tishrei.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yoḥanan prove from Scripture that kings' years are counted from Nisan rather than Tishrei?
A. He derives it from the verse about the Tabernacle being erected on 1 Nisan
B. He links Solomon's regnal year to the Exodus count, which is shown to begin in Nisan via Aaron's death in Av of the 40th year
C. He quotes a verse from Deuteronomy that explicitly states Nisan begins all years
D. He reasons that since Passover is in Nisan, all new years must follow it
Correct: B — R. Yoḥanan connects Solomon's 4th year to the 480th year from the Exodus (1 Kings 6:1). Aaron's death on 1 Av in the 40th year (Num. 33:38) proves the Exodus year begins in Nisan (before Av), and a verbal analogy (gezerah shavah) extends this to all royal years.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If King A dies in Adar and King B takes the throne in the following Nisan, how does the Baraita count their years?
A. Adar counts for King B as his first year; Nisan is lost
B. Adar and Nisan both count as King A's final year
C. Adar is King A's final year; Nisan is King B's first year
D. Neither king gets a year counted since no full year elapsed
Correct: C — The Baraita rules that when succession crosses from Adar to Nisan, the years are divided: Adar counts as the first king's final year, and Nisan counts as the second king's first year — each gets his own year with no overlap or gap.
