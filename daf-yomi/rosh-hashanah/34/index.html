🎺 Deriving the Nine Shofar Blasts

Three verses mention terua on Rosh HaShana
↓
Each terua must be preceded by a tekia and followed by a tekia
↓
3 teruot × 3 blasts (tekia-terua-tekia) = 9 total blasts
↓
All soundings in the seventh month must be alike — derived by verbal analogy
↓
Result: tekia-terua-tekia repeated three times as the required order

🎵 Three Types of Shofar Sounds

Tekia (תְּקִיעָה)

  • Simple, uninterrupted straight blast
  • Frames the terua before and after
  • Length equals one terua
  • Required by Torah law on both sides of each terua

Shevarim (שְׁבָרִים)

  • Three broken, moaning sounds
  • Like a person groaning in anguish
  • One candidate for 'terua' in the Torah
  • Added by Rabbi Abbahu due to uncertainty

Terua (תְּרוּעָה)

  • Series of short staccato blasts
  • Like whimpering or crying
  • Second candidate for 'terua' in the Torah
  • Also retained by Rabbi Abbahu's ruling

🤔 Rabbi Abbahu's Caesarea Compromise

Question
What is the correct form of a terua — moaning (shevarim) or whimpering (short staccato blasts)?
Rabbi Abbahu
I am uncertain! I therefore instituted: tekia-shevarim-terua-tekia, covering both possibilities in one set.
Rav Avira (objection)
But performing both creates a hefsek (interruption)! If terua is moaning, the whimpering in between interrupts; if it is whimpering, the moaning interrupts!
Resolution
Since the doubt existed from the outset, the entire combination is one valid unit — there is no interruption when both sounds are intentionally included to resolve the uncertainty.
Further Question
Why not also do tekia-terua-shevarim-tekia? Perhaps terua means whimpering first and then moaning?
Answer
The normal way: a person first moans (shevarim) and then whimpers (terua) — moaning comes before whimpering in human distress.
Practical Result Tekia-Shevarim-Terua-Tekia became the standard order in all Jewish communities, covering all interpretations of terua.

⏰ Nine Blasts — Must They Be Consecutive?

Must the nine shofar blasts be heard consecutively, or may they be spread throughout the day?
↓
9 blasts at 9 different times
Valid!
He has fulfilled his obligation — blasts need not be consecutive
9 blasts from 9 people simultaneously
Invalid!
Simultaneous blasts cannot be distinguished; one cannot fulfill the mitzva this way
↓
Key principle: Each blast must be individually heard and distinguished — time gaps are permitted, simultaneous blasts are not

🙏 Does the Prayer Leader Fulfill Everyone's Obligation?

Rabbis
Each individual must pray the Musaf of Rosh HaShana himself — the prayer leader cannot fulfill everyone's obligation.
Rabban Gamliel
The prayer leader descends before the ark and fulfills the obligation on behalf of the congregation — as their shliach tzibur (communal agent).
Rabbis
Then why does the prayer leader recite Musaf at all? Only to help those who cannot pray themselves — the unlearned who do not know the prayers.
Rabban Gamliel
If so, why does a learned person descend before the ark? To fulfill the obligation on behalf of those who cannot — and this applies to everyone.
Rabba bar bar Ḥana (citing R. Yoḥanan)
Ultimately the Rabbis concede to Rabban Gamliel. The halakha follows Rabban Gamliel.
Rav
The dispute remains unresolved — one cannot derive the halakha from the word 'halakha' alone; it may simply mean 'the ruling in the dispute.'
Halakhic Outcome Rabbi Yoḥanan rules that the halakha follows Rabban Gamliel — the prayer leader can fulfill the Rosh HaShana Musaf obligation on behalf of the congregation.
