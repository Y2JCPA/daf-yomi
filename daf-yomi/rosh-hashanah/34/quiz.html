Rosh Hashanah 34 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
How many total shofar blasts are required on Rosh HaShana, and how are they structured?
A. Three blasts: one tekia, one terua, one tekia
B. Seven blasts based on the seven days of creation
C. Nine blasts: three sets of tekia-terua-tekia
D. Thirteen blasts corresponding to the thirteen divine attributes
Correct: C — Three Torah verses mention terua on Rosh HaShana, and each requires a tekia before and after it, yielding 3 sets of tekia-terua-tekia = 9 total blasts.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Abbahu institute the order tekia-shevarim-terua-tekia in Caesarea?
A. To add extra beauty and length to the mitzva of shofar
B. Because he was uncertain whether terua means moaning (shevarim) or whimpering (short blasts)
C. Because a verse explicitly requires both shevarim and terua sounds
D. To distinguish Caesarea's practice from Jerusalem's practice
Correct: B — Rabbi Abbahu was uncertain about the nature of terua, so he instituted both shevarim (moaning) and terua (staccato blasts) to ensure the mitzva is fulfilled regardless of which interpretation is correct.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the order tekia-shevarim-terua-tekia not include the reverse (tekia-terua-shevarim-tekia)?
A. A verse explicitly specifies shevarim must precede terua
B. Because whimpering before moaning would be an interruption
C. The natural human pattern is to first moan (shevarim) and then whimper (terua)
D. Because terua is always louder and must come last before the closing tekia
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that when a person experiences distress, they naturally first moan (shevarim) and then whimper (terua) — so the order reflects natural human expression of grief.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If one heard nine shofar blasts distributed across nine different moments in the day, what is the ruling?
A. He has not fulfilled his obligation — the blasts must be consecutive
B. He has fulfilled his obligation — blasts may be spread throughout the day
C. He has only partially fulfilled — he must repeat the last set consecutively
D. It depends on whether he had intention (kavana) at each individual blast
Correct: B — The Gemara rules explicitly that hearing nine blasts at nine different times throughout the day fulfills the obligation — the blasts do not need to be consecutive.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Rabban Gamliel's position regarding the prayer leader on Rosh HaShana, and how does Rabbi Yoḥanan rule?
A. Rabban Gamliel agrees with the Rabbis that each person must pray individually; Rabbi Yoḥanan rules the dispute remains open
B. Rabban Gamliel says the prayer leader fulfills the congregation's obligation; Rabbi Yoḥanan rules the halakha follows Rabban Gamliel
C. Rabban Gamliel limits the prayer leader's role to helping the unlearned; Rabbi Yoḥanan rules the prayer leader helps only those present
D. Rabban Gamliel rules the prayer leader reads on behalf of all; Rabbi Yoḥanan rules the dispute remains unresolved
Correct: B — Rabban Gamliel holds the prayer leader (shliach tzibur) fulfills the Musaf obligation for the entire congregation. Rabbi Yoḥanan ultimately rules the halakha follows Rabban Gamliel, though Rav disputes this conclusion.
