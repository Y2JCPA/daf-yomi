QUESTION 1 OF 5
What proof does Rabbi Eliezer bring that the world was created in Tishrei?
A. Genesis describes 'fruit tree yielding fruit' — trees bore ripe fruit, characteristic of the autumn harvest in Tishrei
B. Genesis describes trees 'just yielding fruit' — trees were budding, characteristic of the spring growth of Nisan
C. The month of Tishrei is called 'the month of the mighty [eitanim],' implying primordial strength
D. Moses explicitly states the world was created in Tishrei in his farewell speech
Correct: A — R. Eliezer argues from Genesis 1:11: 'Let the earth bring forth… fruit tree yielding fruit.' Trees were already bearing ripe fruit, which characterizes the autumn harvest season of Tishrei. R. Yehoshua counters that the same verse can be read as trees just beginning to form fruit — the Nisan springtime.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara derive that Sarah, Rachel, and Hannah were all remembered by God on Rosh HaShana?
A. An explicit verse in Psalms lists all three women and states 'on Rosh HaShana'
B. A gezeira shava (verbal analogy) on the root פ-ק-ד links Hannah's story, Sarah's story, and Rachel's conception
C. The Mishnah in the first chapter of Rosh HaShana states this directly
D. The Talmud derives it from the zodiac sign of Tishrei, which represents fertility
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar derives this through a gezeira shava on פקידה (visitation). 'The Lord revisited [פקד] Hannah' (I Sam 2:21) and 'The Lord revisited [פקד] Sarah' (Gen 21:1) share the same verbal root. By analogy, all three women were remembered on the same occasion — Rosh HaShana, the Day of Remembrance.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the apparent problem that Isaac was born on Passover but Sarah conceived on Rosh HaShana — only ~6.5 months earlier?
A. The Gemara concludes Isaac was actually a premature 6-month baby, which is also viable
B. The Gemara rejects the premise — Sarah conceived before Rosh HaShana, she was only 'visited' then
C. A baraita teaches that year was a leap year with an extra Adar, extending the pregnancy to ~7.5 months — viable for a 7-month birth
D. The Gemara says 'mo'ed' in the verse does not mean Passover but rather Sukkot
Correct: C — A baraita resolves this by noting that the year was a leap year — an extra month of Adar was added. This extended the pregnancy from Rosh HaShana (1 Tishrei) to Passover (15 Nisan) to approximately 7.5 months. The rule is that a 7-month birth is viable, an 8-month birth is not, and a 9-month birth is the norm.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
From which biblical passage does the Gemara prove that Joseph was released from prison on Rosh HaShana?
A. Genesis 41, where Pharaoh dreams and summons Joseph — the date is identified as Rosh HaShana
B. Psalm 81 — 'Sound a shofar at the New Moon, at the covered [כסה] time,' immediately followed by 'This He ordained in Joseph… when he went out over Egypt'
C. A gezeira shava linking the word 'freedom' in Joseph's story to the shofar of Rosh HaShana
D. Leviticus 25, which mandates the Jubilee shofar on Yom Kippur — linked to Joseph's release by analogy
Correct: B — Psalm 81:4 refers to Rosh HaShana — the New Moon when the moon is 'covered' (כסה). The very next verse (81:6) states: 'This He ordained in Joseph for testimony, when he went out over the land of Egypt.' The biblical juxtaposition proves Joseph's release and appointment as viceroy occurred on Rosh HaShana.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
R. Eliezer and R. Yehoshua disagree about whether the final redemption will be in Tishrei or Nisan. What verse does R. Yehoshua cite for Nisan, and how does R. Eliezer interpret that same verse?
A. R. Yehoshua: 'Sound a shofar' (Ps 81) = future redemption in Tishrei; R. Eliezer: it refers to Joseph
B. R. Yehoshua: 'As in the days of your coming out of Egypt' (Micah 7:15) = redemption in Nisan; R. Eliezer: that verse means Passover night is protected from demons
C. R. Yehoshua: 'In the month of Nisan you will be redeemed' (explicit verse); R. Eliezer: that verse only applies to the first redemption
D. R. Yehoshua: the zodiac of Nisan (Aries) symbolizes the Paschal lamb; R. Eliezer: Tishrei's zodiac (Scales) symbolizes divine judgment and redemption
Correct: B — R. Yehoshua cites Micah 7:15 — 'As in the days of your coming out of Egypt' — to argue the final redemption will mirror the Exodus and thus occur in Nisan. R. Eliezer does not use this verse for redemption timing; instead he reads it as teaching that the night of Passover is perpetually 'watched' (שמורים) and protected from harmful forces.
