Rosh Hashanah 11 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Rabbi Eliezer derives the world was created in Tishrei from 'tree yielding fruit' (Gen 1:12). What is his reasoning?
A. Trees bearing ripe fruit describes the autumn harvest season — Tishrei
B. The word 'fruit' in Hebrew shares a root with Tishrei
C. Tishrei is explicitly mentioned as the month of creation in the Torah
D. The Flood began in Tishrei, so creation preceded it by one year
Correct: A — Rabbi Eliezer reads 'tree yielding fruit' as trees already full of ripe fruit at the moment of creation — this describes autumn (Tishrei), not spring when fruit is just beginning to form.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara complete the chain linking Hannah to Rosh HaShana as the day she conceived?
A. Hannah prayed in the Temple on Rosh HaShana, as stated in I Samuel
B. 'Pakad' (revisited) links Hannah (I Sam 2:21) to Sarah (Gen 21:1), who is the baseline for Rosh HaShana conception
C. The shofar is blown on Rosh HaShana, and Hannah heard the shofar at Shiloh
D. Hannah's son Samuel was born exactly one year after Rosh HaShana
Correct: B — The chain: 'zachar' (remembered) links Rachel to Hannah; then 'pakad' (revisited) in I Samuel 2:21 links Hannah back to Sarah in Genesis 21:1 — Sarah being the proof-case for Rosh HaShana conception.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the baraita resolve the apparent problem that Isaac was born on Passover but Sarah conceived on Rosh HaShana — only 6 months earlier?
A. Sarah actually conceived in Adar, not Tishrei — a misreading of the dates
B. Isaac was a premature miracle baby, born at 6 months by divine intervention
C. That year was a leap year with an extra Adar, making the pregnancy 7 full months — viable
D. The angel's visit on Passover was a prophecy fulfilled the following year's Passover
Correct: C — A baraita teaches that the year was a leap year (extra Adar inserted), extending the pregnancy from Rosh HaShana to Passover to 7 months. A 7-month gestation can yield a viable birth.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
From which Psalm and by what logic does the Gemara derive Joseph was released from prison on Rosh HaShana?
A. Psalm 105 — Joseph's story is retold and dated to the New Year
B. Psalm 81 — the Rosh HaShana shofar verse is immediately followed by 'when he went out over the land of Egypt'
C. Psalm 47 — 'God ascends with a teruah' alludes to Joseph's rise to power
D. Psalm 150 — 'Praise Him with shofar blast' is linked to Joseph via gematria
Correct: B — Psalm 81:4 describes the Rosh HaShana shofar at the 'covered moon.' Immediately in 81:6: 'This He ordained in Joseph for testimony, when he went out over the land of Egypt.' The juxtaposition proves Joseph's release was on Rosh HaShana.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Eliezer, what does the phrase 'a night of watching [leil shimurim]' (Exodus 12:42) mean — and how does this differ from Rabbi Yehoshua's reading?
A. Eliezer: God watched over Israel that night in Egypt; Yehoshua: God will watch over Israel in the future redemption in Nisan
B. Eliezer: it is a night protected from demons; Yehoshua: it means the future redemption will come in Nisan
C. Both agree it means protection from demons — they only disagree about which month the Flood began
D. Eliezer: the night was watched by the angels; Yehoshua: Israel watched and waited for God
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehoshua reads 'leil shimurim' as proof the future redemption will come in Nisan — the night is watched for all future redemptions. Rabbi Eliezer reads it literally: Passover night is continuously protected from demons, with no implication about future redemption timing.
