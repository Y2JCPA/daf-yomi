🗓️ Rabbi Eliezer vs. Rabbi Yehoshua: Tishrei or Nisan?

R. Eliezer
The world was created in Tishrei. Proof: 'Let the earth bring forth fruit tree yielding fruit' — trees bore ripe fruit, typical of the autumn harvest.
R. Yehoshua
The world was created in Nisan. Proof: 'And the earth brought forth… tree yielding fruit' — trees were just forming fruit, characteristic of spring.
R. Eliezer
The Patriarchs were born in Tishrei — Solomon's Temple was dedicated in 'the month of the mighty [eitanim],' meaning the month of the mighty Patriarchs.
R. Yehoshua
The Patriarchs were born in Nisan — the Temple was built in 'the month of Ziv,' meaning radiance [ziv], referring to the radiance of the Patriarchs.
R. Eliezer
The final redemption will occur in Tishrei. The night of Passover is 'a night of watching' — protection from demons, not a proof for future redemption.
R. Yehoshua
The final redemption will occur in Nisan — 'As in the days of your coming out of Egypt' (Micah 7:15) parallels the future redemption to the Exodus.
✅ Points of Agreement Both agree: On Rosh HaShana — Sarah, Rachel & Hannah conceived; Joseph left prison; Egyptian slavery ceased. On Passover — Isaac was born. In Nisan — the Exodus occurred.

🌱 Creation Month: Two Readings of Genesis 1

R. Eliezer — Tishrei

  • Verse: 'Let the earth bring forth… fruit tree yielding fruit' (Gen 1:11)
  • Trees bore mature, ripe fruit — characteristic of Tishrei (autumn harvest)
  • Trees were created already full of fruit
  • Patriarchs born in Tishrei: 'month of the mighty [eitanim]'
  • Future redemption: in Tishrei

R. Yehoshua — Nisan

  • Verse: 'And the earth brought forth… tree yielding fruit' (Gen 1:12)
  • Trees were just beginning to form fruit — characteristic of Nisan (spring)
  • Trees were created at the onset of the growing season
  • Patriarchs born in Nisan: 'month of Ziv [radiance]'
  • Future redemption: in Nisan (Micah 7:15)

🔓 Joseph Freed on Rosh HaShana: The Proof from Psalms 81

Psalm 81:4: 'Sound a shofar at the New Moon, at the covered [כסה] time of our Festival'
↓
Which festival falls on the New Moon AND is 'covered'? Rosh HaShana — on 1 Tishrei the moon is hidden (כסה = covered/full darkness)
↓
Psalm 81:6 (immediately following): 'This He ordained in Joseph for testimony, when he went out over the land of Egypt'
↓
✅ The juxtaposition proves: Joseph's release from prison and elevation to viceroy occurred on Rosh HaShana

🐣 Isaac's Birth: Resolving the Chronological Puzzle

Step 1 — Rosh HaShana (1 Tishrei): Sarah is 'remembered' and conceives. The angel told Abraham: 'At the appointed time [מועד] I will return, and Sarah will have a son' (Gen 18:14).
↓
Step 2 — Why Passover? The word מועד always refers to a festival. The angel said he would return at the mo'ed — the next festival after Tishrei is Passover. Therefore Isaac was born on Passover.
↓
Step 3 — The Problem: Rosh HaShana (1 Tishrei) to Passover (15 Nisan) is only ~6.5 months. Can a baby be born viable at 6.5 months?
↓
Step 4 — The Resolution (baraita): That year was a leap year with an extra month of Adar added. Conception to birth thus spanned ~7.5 months — a viable pregnancy, since a 7-month birth survives but an 8-month birth does not.
↓
Step 5 — Mar Zutra's Addition: Even if one holds that only a 9-month birth is viable, here God explicitly pledged 'at the appointed time' — guaranteeing the birth regardless of gestational length.

👩 Sarah, Rachel & Hannah — Remembered on Rosh HaShana Rabbi Elazar derives this through a gezeira shava (verbal analogy) on the root פ-ק-ד (to remember/visit):

• Hannah: 'And the Lord revisited [וַיִּפְקֹד] Hannah' (I Samuel 2:21)
• Sarah: 'And the Lord revisited [וַיִּפְקֹד] Sarah as He had said' (Genesis 21:1)
• Rachel: connected through the same verbal root

The verbal analogy links all three women's conceptions to the same occasion — Rosh HaShana, the Day of Remembrance (יום הזיכרון), when God 'remembers' and acts.
