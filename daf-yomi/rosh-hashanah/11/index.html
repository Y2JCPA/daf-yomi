🗓️ The Great Calendar Dispute: Tishrei vs. Nisan

Rabbi Eliezer
The world was created in Tishrei. The Patriarchs were born and died in Tishrei. The future Messianic redemption will also come in Tishrei.
Rabbi Yehoshua
The world was created in Nisan. The Patriarchs were born and died in Nisan. The future redemption will mirror the Exodus — it will come in Nisan.
Common Ground Both agree: the Exodus happened in Nisan; Sarah, Rachel & Hannah conceived on Rosh HaShana; Joseph left prison on Rosh HaShana; Isaac was born on Passover.

🌱 Which Month Was the World Created?

Rabbi Eliezer
Tishrei — 'tree yielding fruit' (Gen 1:12) means trees already bore ripe fruit at creation. This describes autumn harvest — Tishrei. The 'mighty month' [eitanim] of Kings 8:2 also points to Tishrei.
Rabbi Yehoshua
Nisan — 'herb yielding seed' describes spring growth. 'Fruit tree' means trees just beginning to form fruit — that is Nisan. The 'month of Ziv' in Kings 6:1 alludes to the radiant blossoming of spring.
Scriptural Crux Each tanna reads the same botanical verse (Gen 1:12) differently: Eliezer sees fully-ripened autumn fruit; Yehoshua sees new spring growth just forming.

🐑 How Do We Know Isaac Was Born on Passover?

Gen 18:10 — The angel tells Abraham: 'At the mo'ed (appointed time) I will return to you, and Sarah will have a son.'
↓
'Mo'ed' = a festival day. The angel visited while Abraham served matzot and calf — signs of Passover. So the return would be the next Passover.
↓
Problem: But Sarah conceived on Rosh HaShana (6 months before Passover) — can a 6-month pregnancy yield a viable baby?
↓
Mar Zutra's Principle
A woman who births in her 9th month cannot birth prematurely. But Sarah was past childbearing age — normal rules don't apply; a miracle overrides the rule.
Baraita's Answer
That year was a leap year — an extra Adar was added. Sarah's pregnancy was actually 7 months, which yields a viable birth. Isaac born on Passover ✓

⛓️ Joseph's Release & the Hidden Rosh HaShana

תִּקְעוּ בַחֹדֶשׁ שׁוֹפָר בַּכֵּסֶה לְיוֹם חַגֵּנוּ (תהלים פא:ד)
Psalm 81 — A Concealed New Moon 'Sound a shofar at the New Moon, at the keseh (covered/concealed time) of our Festival day' (Ps 81:4).

Rosh HaShana is unique: it falls when the moon is covered — not yet visible. No other festival falls on the New Moon this way.

Immediately following: 'This He ordained in Joseph for testimony, when he went out over the land of Egypt' (Ps 81:6).

The juxtaposition is the proof: Joseph was released from prison on Rosh HaShana — the day of divine remembrance and liberation. The same day slavery in Egypt later ceased.
