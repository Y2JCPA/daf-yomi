🌙 Overnight Stay in Jerusalem After Festivals

Rule: One who brings a Festival offering must remain overnight in Jerusalem before returning home
↓
Passover source: "You shall roast and eat it in the place which the Lord your God shall choose" — the eating extends into the night, requiring an overnight stay
↓
Sukkot: Derived by analogy from Passover — just as Passover requires overnight, so does Sukkot
↓
Shavuot's seven days for redress: Derived from Rabba bar Shmuel's teaching — Torah commands counting days then sanctifying months, giving Shavuot a full redress period despite having only one day
↓
Rava's response: Shavuot's one-day character is not a basis to limit redress — the seven-day redress period is independent of the Festival's duration

🐑 Why Is the Paschal Lamb Listed in the Baraita?

Gemara
The baraita says one transgresses bal te'acher for the Paschal lamb — but it can only be offered on 14 Nisan! How can the delay prohibition apply to a time-fixed offering?
Rav Ḥisda
The Paschal lamb is mentioned kedi — for no real reason. It was included only because firstborn offerings and tithes are discussed nearby, not because the prohibition truly applies to it.
Rav Sheshet
No — 'Paschal lamb' here refers to the peace-offering brought in place of a Paschal lamb that was lost. The owner replaced it; when the original is found, it must be brought as a peace-offering.
Gemara
But that peace-offering is already covered under 'peace-offerings' earlier in the baraita — why list it again?
Rav Sheshet
One might think such a peace-offering is treated like the Paschal lamb — violation after just one Festival. The baraita teaches it follows ordinary peace-offering rules: three Festivals are required.
Ruling A peace-offering brought in place of a Paschal lamb is governed by the rules of peace-offerings, not by the stringent one-Festival rule of the Paschal lamb itself.

⚖️ Rabbi Meir vs. the Rabbis on Bal Te'acher

Rabbi Meir

  • ONE Festival passing = violation of bal te'acher
  • Strictest standard
  • Peace-offering replacing Paschal lamb: violation after ONE Festival (treated like the Paschal itself)
  • Substitute of a burnt- or peace-offering: must account for Festivals from original consecration
  • No leniency for unusual offering categories

The Rabbis

  • THREE Festivals must pass = violation
  • Standard (lenient) position
  • Peace-offering replacing Paschal lamb: treated like ordinary peace-offerings
  • Substitute for a sin-offering: excluded entirely from the prohibition
  • Verse 'sin in you' protects the offering's validity even after violation
