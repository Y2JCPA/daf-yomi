🌙 Overnight Stay Required in Jerusalem Just as Passover requires remaining overnight in Jerusalem before returning home, so too Sukkot carries this requirement. The verse states: "You shall roast and eat it in the place which the Lord your God shall choose; and you shall turn in the morning and go to your tents" (Deuteronomy 16:7). The phrase "turn in the morning" implies one may only depart after the night passes — requiring an overnight stay after the festival offering.

Gemara
The baraita lists the Paschal lamb among offerings subject to bal te'acher — but is the Paschal lamb actually fit to be sacrificed on all three Festivals? It can only be offered on 14 Nisan! How could one ever delay it?
Rav Ḥisda
The Paschal lamb is mentioned kedi — for no reason at all. It was included only because firstborn offerings, animal tithes, and peace-offerings were listed alongside it. The prohibition of delay does not actually apply to it.
Rav Sheshet
Not so! The baraita refers to the peace-offering brought in place of a lost Paschal lamb. If an owner's Paschal lamb was lost and he designated a replacement, then the original was found and sacrificed — the replacement becomes a peace-offering that CAN be delayed across Festivals.
Gemara
But then it is simply another peace-offering — why list it separately as 'Paschal lamb'? It is redundant!
Rav Sheshet
One might think it should be treated strictly like the Paschal lamb itself — transgressing after just one Festival. The baraita teaches it is treated as a regular peace-offering, requiring three Festivals to pass before one transgresses.
Conclusion A peace-offering replacing a lost Paschal lamb follows the three-Festival rule of regular peace-offerings, not a stricter Paschal-like standard.

🔄 Does Bal Te'acher Apply to Substitute Offerings?

Substitute for Sin-Offering

  • Original sin-offering lost → owner sets aside substitute
  • Original is then found and sacrificed on the altar
  • Substitute is sent to pasture until it becomes blemished
  • ❌ Bal te'acher does NOT apply to this substitute
  • Reason: 'You shall not delay paying IT' — the original, not its replacement
  • The substitute has no viable path to the altar

Substitute for Burnt/Peace-Offering

  • Original consecrated; two Festivals already passed (transgression occurred)
  • Substitute was designated during that period
  • Original is then offered — substitute still awaits its turn
  • ✅ Bal te'acher DOES apply as additional Festivals pass
  • Per Rabbis: transgresses after a total of three Festivals
  • Per Rabbi Meir: transgresses after even the first Festival

🔑 'Sin In You' — The Offering Remains Valid

Question raised: If one violates bal te'acher by delaying an offering, does the offering itself become disqualified and unfit for the altar?
↓
Key verse: Deuteronomy 23:22 — "And it would be sin in you"
↓
Derivation: "Sin in you" — the transgression resides in the person, not in the offering. The offering retains its sanctity.
↓
Practical ruling: Even after transgressing bal te'acher, the offering remains fully valid and must still be brought to the Temple.
↓
Further support from firstborns: A firstborn animal that was delayed is still sacred and must be offered — confirming the principle applies broadly across offering types.
