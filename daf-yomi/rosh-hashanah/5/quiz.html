Rosh Hashanah 5 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Sheshet, what does 'Paschal lamb' refer to in the baraita about bal te'acher?
A. The Paschal lamb itself, which has a special one-Festival stringency
B. A peace-offering brought in place of a Paschal lamb that was lost and then found
C. A firstborn animal offered during the Passover season
D. Any animal sacrificed during the seven days of Passover
Correct: B — Rav Sheshet explains that the 'Paschal lamb' in the baraita refers to a peace-offering that replaced a lost Paschal lamb. When the original was found after a substitute Paschal had been offered, the original must be brought as a peace-offering — and the baraita teaches it follows peace-offering rules.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rav Ḥisda say the Paschal lamb is mentioned in the baraita?
A. To teach that even time-fixed offerings can violate bal te'acher
B. To create a stringent one-Festival rule for all Festival offerings
C. Kedi — for no real reason, only due to textual proximity to firstborns and tithes
D. To derive a new category of offering from the verse in Deuteronomy
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda says kedi — the Paschal lamb has no actual connection to the delay prohibition since it can only be offered at its fixed time. It was included in the baraita only because neighboring topics (firstborns, tithes) are listed nearby.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does 'it would be sin in you' (Deuteronomy 23:21) teach regarding a delayed offering?
A. The offering becomes permanently disqualified once three Festivals pass
B. The sin attaches to the person, but the offering itself remains valid for sacrifice
C. Both the owner and the offering are equally penalized by the delay
D. Only offerings above a certain monetary value are subject to disqualification
Correct: B — The Gemara derives from 'sin in you' — not 'sin in your offering' — that while the person transgresses bal te'acher by delaying, the offering itself is NOT disqualified and remains valid to be brought on the altar.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which phrase in Deuteronomy 23:22 is used to include tithes and firstborn offerings in the delay prohibition?
A. "When you shall vow a vow"
B. "You shall not delay paying it"
C. "For the Lord your God" (seemingly superfluous phrase)
D. "To the Lord your God"
Correct: C — The phrase 'for the Lord your God' appears to be superfluous after 'to the Lord your God' already appeared. The Gemara uses this redundancy to extend the prohibition to additional categories: charity vows, tithes, and firstborn offerings.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Sheshet, why does the Gemara need a verse to exclude the substitute for a burnt-offering from bal te'acher, even though it seemingly can't be sacrificed?
A. Because substitutes for burnt-offerings can actually be offered in some circumstances
B. The verse is needed to include, not exclude, the substitute
C. Two Festivals may have already passed from the original consecration before the substitute was made — the verse clarifies the substitute doesn't inherit that accumulated time
D. Because the Rabbis and Rabbi Meir disagree about whether substitutes can ever be offered
Correct: C — Rav Sheshet explains a case where two Festivals had already passed from when the original was consecrated, but the substitute was only made after that. Without the excluding verse, one might think the substitute 'inherits' the two Festivals and violates immediately upon the third — the verse teaches otherwise.
