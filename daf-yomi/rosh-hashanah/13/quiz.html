QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, when is the tithe year for grain determined?
A. When the grain is planted in the ground
B. When the grain reaches one-third of its full growth
C. When the grain is fully ripe and cut
D. When the grain is brought to the threshing floor
Correct: B — Rabbi Yonatan ben Yosef's baraita derives from the verse 'it shall bring forth fruit for three years' — read not as 'for three' but 'for one-third' — establishing that the tithe year for grain follows when it reaches שליש גידולו, one-third of its growth.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Rabba decree that rice, millet, poppy, and sesame follow 'rooting time' rather than one-third growth?
A. Because these crops are not mentioned in the Torah's tithing laws
B. Because it is impossible to physically inspect them at one-third growth
C. Because they ripen gradually, so applying one-third rule would split one crop between two tithe years
D. Because Rabbi Yonatan ben Yosef's baraita explicitly excluded them
Correct: C — Rabba explained that these crops ripen little by little over time. If tithes followed one-third growth, the farmer would face an impossible situation: parts of the same crop would belong to Year 6 and parts to Year 7, requiring two separate tithe calculations from one pile.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was Rabbi Ḥanina's objection to the proof from Exodus 23:16?
A. The verse only applies to Shemita years, not regular tithing
B. The word 'gathering' (אסיף) in that verse refers to ingathering the year, not harvesting grain
C. Sukkot cannot be used as a baseline because it varies by year
D. The verse contradicts the ruling of Rav Kahana about the omer
Correct: B — Rabbi Ḥanina pointed out that the 'festival of gathering' language in Exodus 23:16 likely refers to the ingathering of the year in general — not specifically to grain harvest. He notes that Deuteronomy 16:13 uses the same phrase about 'your threshing floor and your wine vat,' suggesting a broader meaning. Rabbi Zeira said Rabbi Ḥanina 'cast an axe' on the proof.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Rabba reject Abaye's suggestion to resolve the tithe problem by mixing all the crop together?
A. Because physically mixing produce of different years is prohibited by Torah law
B. Because Abaye's method requires a kohen to be present at the threshing floor
C. Because the Sages hold there is no valid mixing — old and new remain legally distinct even when combined
D. Because the mixing method only works for grain, not for rice or sesame
Correct: C — Rabba clarified that Abaye's solution works only according to Rabbi Shimon Shezuri, who holds that thorough mixing is legally effective. However, the Sages disagree — they hold 'there is no mixing,' meaning old-year and new-year produce remain legally separate even when physically combined, so the decree to follow rooting time is still needed.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Joshua 4:19 contribute to the Gemara's proof about the one-third growth rule?
A. It states explicitly that the omer was brought from grain grown before the conquest
B. It records that the Jewish people entered Canaan on 10 Nissan — leaving only 6 days until Passover, proving grain was already growing and distinguishable before conquest
C. It establishes that the conquest of Canaan occurred during the Shemita year
D. It shows that Rav Kahana's ruling about the omer was accepted by Joshua himself
Correct: B — Since the Jewish people entered Canaan on 10 Nissan, only 6 days remained before Passover and the omer offering. It would be impossible for grain to grow from nothing to omer-ready in 6 days, proving the grain was already partially grown before the conquest. Rav Kahana's ability to rule which grain qualified for the omer demonstrates that Sages could reliably identify the one-third growth threshold.
