Rosh Hashanah 13 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What does the Gemara derive by reading 'lishelosh' as 'lishelish' in Vayikra 25:21?
A. The sixth year of the Sabbatical cycle produces a threefold yield
B. One must separate one-third of the crop as tithe each year
C. The tithe year for grain is determined when it reaches one-third of its growth
D. The Sabbatical year repeats in three-year cycles
Correct: C — Reading lishelish (for one-third) instead of lishelosh (for three) teaches that the tithe year for grain is fixed at the point when the crop reaches one-third of its full growth — a key halakhic threshold.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabba, why must rice, millet, poppy, and sesame follow the time of taking root for tithing purposes?
A. These species are classified as vegetables, not grain
B. They ripen all at once, unlike other crops
C. They ripen gradually over time, making batch-by-batch tithe tracking impossible
D. The Torah explicitly excludes them from the one-third growth rule
Correct: C — Rabba explains that these crops ripen little by little. If tithe year followed one-third growth, different harvested portions of the same planting would span Rosh HaShana and belong to different tithe years — an unworkable situation.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What practical solution does Abaye propose to avoid tithe problems when produce spans two years?
A. Harvest immediately before Rosh HaShana so all produce belongs to one year
B. Declare the entire crop ownerless (hefker) and re-acquire it after Rosh HaShana
C. Pile all produce together on the threshing floor, mix thoroughly, then separate tithes from the combined pile
D. Follow the tithe year of the majority portion of the harvest
Correct: C — Abaye proposes that thoroughly mixing the produce on the threshing floor creates a unified batch, allowing one to separate tithes from the whole without needing to track which portion belongs to which year.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the fundamental disagreement between Rabbi Shimon Shezuri and the Sages about mixing produce?
A. Whether teruma must be separated before or after tithes
B. Whether mixing old and new produce creates a legally unified batch for tithing purposes
C. Whether cowpea is classified as a vegetable or a grain for tithe purposes
D. Whether Babylonian produce is subject to the same tithe rules as produce grown in Israel
Correct: B — Rabbi Shimon Shezuri holds that mixing is legally effective — old and new produce blend into one batch. The Sages disagree, holding that physical mixing does not create legal unification and each portion retains its original tithe-year identity.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve Rabbi Zeira's objection to Shmuel's ruling that the halakha follows Rabbi Shimon Shezuri?
A. Shmuel retracted his ruling after Rabbi Zeira's objection
B. The ruling applies only outside the Land of Israel
C. Rabbi Zeira forgot that Shmuel holds tithe year follows full ripening, meaning all cowpea seeds ripen at the same time and can be tithed together
D. Rabbi Zeira's objection stands and Shmuel's ruling is rejected
Correct: C — The Gemara answers that Rabbi Zeira forgot Shmuel's other principle: tithe year follows full ripening. Since all cowpea seeds fully ripen simultaneously, they naturally belong to the same tithe year — making Shmuel's two rulings perfectly consistent.
