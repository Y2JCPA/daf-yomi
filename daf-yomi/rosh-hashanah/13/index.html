📖 Overview

שליש גידולו — one-third of its growth
🌾 Core Question of Daf 13 When produce spans two calendar years — planted in Year 6, harvested in Year 7 — which year's tithes apply? The Gemara establishes that grain and olives follow when they reach one-third of their growth, trees follow fruit formation, and vegetables follow picking. But crops like rice and sesame ripen irregularly, requiring a special decree: they follow the time they took root.

🔀 Why Do Special Crops Follow Rooting Time?

Rabba
These crops (rice, millet, poppy, sesame) ripen little by little, not all at once. If the tithe year followed one-third growth, different parts of the same crop would belong to different years — making proper tithing impossible in practice!
Abaye
Why issue a special decree? There's a simpler solution: pile the entire stock onto the threshing floor, mix everything thoroughly, then separate teruma and tithes once for the whole batch. Problem solved — no need to track one-third timing!
Rabba
Your mixing solution only works according to Rabbi Shimon Shezuri! The Sages hold there is NO valid mixing — old-year and new-year produce remain legally distinct even when physically combined. The decree to follow rooting time is essential.
Abaye
But Rabbi Yosei ben Keifar explicitly teaches in the name of Rabbi Shimon Shezuri: pile, mix, and separate tithes once. This baraita demonstrates the mixing method is normatively reliable!
⚖️ Final Ruling Rabbi Yitzḥak bar Naḥmani citing Shmuel: The halakha follows Rabbi Shimon Shezuri — the tithe year is determined by full ripening, and mixing is effective.

📏 Halachic Precision — Tiny Amounts Create Total Legal Differences

💧 Ritual Bath (Mikveh)

  • 40 se'a of water → valid immersion ✅
  • 40 se'a minus one kortov → invalid ❌
  • A single drop (kortov) determines purity
  • Cited by Rabbi Zeira to prove that halachic thresholds are exact and reliable

🥚 Food Impurity

  • Egg-bulk (כביצה) of impure food → renders other food impure ✅
  • Less than egg-bulk → does not render impure ❌
  • The exact measure creates the legal threshold
  • Demonstrates: yes, Sages can pinpoint exact legal boundaries

🧵 Cloth & Treading Impurity

  • 3×3 handbreadths cloth → susceptible to midras impurity ✅
  • 3×3 less one nima (single hair) → not susceptible ❌
  • One hair's width determines the entire law
  • Proves halachic thresholds, however minute, are precisely defined

🏺 The Omer Offering Proof — Validating Sages' Precision

Rabbi Yirmeya's challenge: Can Sages really determine exactly when grain reached one-third of its growth? Surely this is too subtle to detect!
↓
Rabbi Zeira's initial response: I always tell you — do not ask questions on established measures. Halachic thresholds are defined precisely by the Sages (illustrated by mikveh, egg-bulk, cloth examples).
↓
Rav Kahana's ruling (when asked by colleagues): Grain that came into Jewish possession and did not reach one-third growth while under gentile ownership is fit for the omer offering.
↓
Rabbi Yirmeya's proof: Rav Kahana could identify which grain qualified — proving Sages can discern the one-third threshold with certainty!
↓
Counter-challenge: Maybe the grain hadn't grown at all before conquest? Joshua 4:19 refutes this — Jews entered Canaan on 10 Nissan, only 6 days before Passover. Too short to grow omer grain from scratch.
↓
Rabbi Ḥanina's axe: Even so, how can Exodus 23:16 ('festival of gathering') mean 'harvesting'? It seems to mean 'ingathering of the year' more broadly — cutting down Rabbi Zeira's original proof!
