🌾 When Is the Tithe Year Determined?

🌳 Trees

  • Follows formation of fruit
  • When fruit first appears on the tree
  • Determines which year's tithe applies

🌾 Grain & Olives

  • Follows one-third growth
  • When produce reaches ⅓ of full size
  • Derived from lishelish (Vayikra 25:21)

🥬 Vegetables

  • Follows time of picking
  • Determined when harvested
  • Not by any growth stage

🍚 Rice, Millet, Poppy, Sesame

  • Follows taking root
  • Rooted before Rosh HaShana → prior year's tithe
  • Special rule due to gradual ripening

🌱 Deriving the One-Third Rule from Scripture

לִשְׁלֹשׁ → לִשְׁלִישׁ
Scriptural Wordplay The verse (Vayikra 25:21) states the sixth year will yield produce for three years (lishelosh). The Sages reread this as for one-third (lishelish) — teaching that the tithe year for grain is fixed when the crop reaches one-third of its full growth. The plain meaning of the verse (blessing the sixth year threefold) is preserved by a separate verse.

🍚 Why Do Rice & Millet Follow Taking Root?

Rabba's Explanation

  • These crops ripen gradually, little by little over an extended period
  • If the year followed one-third growth, different batches of the same planting would belong to different tithe years
  • Therefore: follow taking root — a single unified moment for the whole planting

The Problem Without This Rule

  • First batch ripens: belongs to Year 1 tithe cycle
  • Second batch ripens after Rosh HaShana: belongs to Year 2
  • Same plant — two different tithes — impossible to administer!

🔄 Can Mixing Solve the Tithe Problem?

Rabba
Rice and millet require a special rule — following taking root — because their gradual ripening would make proper tithing of different batches impossible otherwise.
Abaye
Why decree a special rule? There is a simpler solution: pile all the produce together on the threshing floor, mix it thoroughly, and then separate teruma and tithes from the combined pile!
Abaye
Proof: Rabbi Yosei ben Keifar in the name of Rabbi Shimon Shezuri rules that cowpea seeds may be tithed together regardless of when they reached one-third growth — by using this piling-and-mixing method.
Rabba
Rabbi Shimon Shezuri's proof only works because he holds there IS effective mixing — old and new become indistinguishable. But the Sages hold there is NO effective mixing, so his approach fails according to the majority!
Core Dispute Does piling and mixing produce from different tithe-years create a valid unified batch for tithing, or does each portion remain legally distinct regardless of physical mixing?

📜 Shmuel's Ruling and Rabbi Zeira's Objection

Rabbi Yitzchak bar Nahmani reports: Shmuel rules the halakha follows Rabbi Shimon Shezuri — cowpea seeds spanning Rosh HaShana may be tithed together.
↓
Rabbi Zeira objects strongly: Did Shmuel really say this? Doesn't Shmuel hold that the tithe year always follows full ripening — implying each batch is distinct?
↓
Answer: Rabbi Zeira forgot Shmuel's other ruling. Shmuel holds tithe year follows full ripening for all produce — meaning all cowpea seeds become fully ripe at the same time, so they all belong to the same tithe year!
↓
Practical outcome: One may pile all cowpea seeds together, mix thoroughly, and tithe from the combined pile. The two rulings of Shmuel are fully consistent with each other.
