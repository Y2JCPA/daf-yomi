🤔 What Is the Reason for the Shabbat Suspension?

Rabbah
A person who does not know how to blow the shofar might carry it 4 cubits in the public domain to reach an expert — thereby violating Shabbat. The decree was made to prevent this.
Gemara
But if that is the reason, why is shofar not blown even in a city where experts are readily available — where there is no risk of carrying at all?
Rabbah
The rabbis did not distinguish within their decree (lo plug rabbanan). Once enacted broadly, it applies universally — even where the original concern does not exist.
הלכה Lo plug rabbanan: a rabbinic fence, once established, is not dismantled case-by-case even when its rationale does not apply.

🌿 The Lulav Takkanah: Memory as Law

תקן רבן יוחנן בן זכאי שיהא לולב ניטל בגבולין שבעה ימים זכר למקדש
זֵכֶר לַמִּקְדָּשׁ In Temple times, the lulav was waved all seven days of Sukkot only in the Temple; outside Jerusalem, only on the first day. After the Temple's destruction, Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai enacted that the lulav be taken all seven days everywhere — as a living memorial to the Temple. This fulfills: "You shall take for yourselves on the first day..." (Lev. 23:40) and ensures the Temple's memory persists in daily practice.

🏛️ Three Jurisdictions: Shofar Authority on Shabbat

The Temple (Mikdash)

  • Shofar blown on Shabbat
  • Sanhedrin physically present
  • No risk of carrying in reshut harabim
  • Full Torah obligation fulfilled

Yavneh — RYBZ's Center

  • Shofar blown on Shabbat
  • Special takkanah by RYBZ
  • Only in proximity to beit din
  • Rabbinic authority as Temple successor

All Other Communities

  • Shofar NOT blown on Shabbat
  • Rabbinic decree fully in force
  • Lo plug: applies even without the risk
  • Obligation suspended for Shabbat safety

🌾 Chadash Takkanah: Why the Entire Day?

Torah Law: New grain (chadash) is forbidden until the Omer is waved on the 16th of Nissan.
↓
Problem in Exile: When the calendar was uncertain or Jews were far from Jerusalem, the exact moment of the Omer waving was unknown.
↓
After Destruction: There is no Temple and therefore no actual Omer waving — the precise moment of permissibility no longer exists.
↓
RYBZ's Solution: Forbid new grain the entire 16th of Nissan — the day itself, not the moment of waving, marks the transition.
↓
Principle: Where an act no longer occurs, its associated time-marker expands to fill the whole day as a zecher l'Mikdash.
