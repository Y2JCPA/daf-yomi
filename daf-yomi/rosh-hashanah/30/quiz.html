QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara's final answer, what exactly was Jerusalem's 'additional superiority' over Yavne regarding shofar on Shabbat?
A. Jerusalem sounded shofar even without the court present; Yavne required the court
B. In Yavne the shofar was sounded only in the beit midrash; in Jerusalem it was sounded throughout the entire city
C. Jerusalem used a louder shofar audible to more people
D. Jerusalem sounded shofar twice daily; Yavne only once
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that in Yavne the shofar was sounded only inside the beit midrash (even when the court was present), whereas in Jerusalem it was sounded in the presence of the court throughout the entire city — that additional scope is the 'additional superiority.'
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was the Levites' error that originally prompted the restriction on accepting Rosh Chodesh testimony after mincha?
A. They recited the Shabbat psalm instead of the weekday psalm
B. They recited the Rosh HaShana psalm on an ordinary day
C. Uncertain whether the day was sanctified, they recited no special psalm at all
D. They recited two different psalms and contradicted each other
Correct: C — Because witnesses arrived too late to clarify whether the day was Rosh HaShana, the Levites did not know which psalm to recite and so recited nothing. The Gemara states there is no greater error in Temple service than failing to recite the appropriate psalm.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What verse does the Gemara cite as the basis for RYbZ's practice of zecher l'Mikdash (Temple commemoration)?
A. 'Remember the Sabbath day to keep it holy' (Exodus 20:8)
B. 'For I will restore health to you… because they called you an outcast' (Jeremiah 30:17)
C. 'And you shall rejoice before the Lord your God' (Leviticus 23:40)
D. 'On the first day you shall take the fruit of goodly trees' (Leviticus 23:40)
Correct: B — The Gemara derives zecher l'Mikdash from Jeremiah 30:17 — God will heal Zion precisely because the nations called her an 'outcast' with no one seeking her. We therefore actively seek to remember and commemorate the Temple.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak, why did RYbZ prohibit new grain the entire day of the 16th of Nisan?
A. As a rabbinic fence to prevent accidental eating before the omer wave
B. Out of concern the Temple would be rebuilt that same morning
C. He holds like Rabbi Yehuda that Torah law itself prohibits new grain until the end of the entire day
D. He holds like the Sages that one must wait until after the Musaf prayer
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman says RYbZ's ruling follows Rabbi Yehuda's interpretation of 'until this selfsame day' (Lev. 23:14) — the prohibition is a Torah law lasting the full day. RYbZ did not create a rabbinic decree; he publicized and established this existing Torah position.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rabbi Zeira's dilemma about the court 'stirring to rise' concerns which halakhic question?
A. Whether a quorum of ten judges is required for shofar authorization
B. Whether a sick judge's presence counts toward the court
C. Whether shofar on Shabbat is still permitted once judges begin to rise but have not yet stood
D. Whether the court must convene before sunrise to authorize shofar
Correct: C — Rabbi Zeira asks: if the court members have begun rising from their seats (signaling the end of the session) but have not yet physically stood — does the permission to sound shofar still apply? The question is whether 'court present' requires active seating or whether the transitional moment still counts.
