Rosh Hashanah 30 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbah, why did the Sages decree that the shofar should not be blown on Shabbat outside the Temple?
A. Shofar blowing is a Torah-prohibited labor (melacha) on Shabbat
B. Fear that someone might carry the shofar to an expert, violating Shabbat
C. The shofar rite was exclusively a Temple service with no diaspora application
D. Shofar sounds were considered a form of musical entertainment forbidden on Shabbat
Correct: B — Rabbah explains the decree was enacted lest a person who does not know how to blow the shofar carry it 4 cubits in the public domain to reach an expert — directly violating Shabbat. The fear of carrying, not the blowing itself, is the concern.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
The Gemara challenges Rabbah's reasoning: if the concern is carrying to an expert, why is shofar not blown in a city where experts are present? Rabbah's answer invokes which principle?
A. Safek d'Oraita l'chumra — Torah doubts are treated strictly
B. Lo plug rabbanan — a broad rabbinic decree applies even where its reason does not
C. Ein mevatlin issur l'chatchila — one may not create a prohibition initially
D. Chazakah — we assume established status quo continues
Correct: B — Lo plug rabbanan means the rabbis did not insert exceptions into their decree. Once legislated broadly, it applies universally — even in a city full of experts where the original concern (carrying) doesn't exist.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai's takkanah extends the lulav to seven days everywhere. What was the original Torah law before this enactment?
A. The lulav was taken for seven days only in the Temple; outside, only on the first day of Sukkot
B. The lulav was taken for seven days everywhere, even before the Temple was built
C. The lulav was taken only on Shemini Atzeret as the culmination of Sukkot
D. The lulav was taken for three days in the Temple and one day elsewhere
Correct: A — Torah law required waving the lulav all seven days of Sukkot only in the Temple (derived from 'the first day' in Lev. 23:40 referring to the first day outside the Temple). Outside Jerusalem, only the first day of Sukkot was observed. RYBZ extended the seven-day practice to everywhere as a zecher l'Mikdash.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In which location did Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai permit the shofar to be blown on Shabbat after the Temple's destruction?
A. In Jerusalem, near the ruins of the Temple Mount
B. In any city with ten adult men (a minyan)
C. Wherever the beit din (rabbinic court) was located — at the time, Yavneh
D. Only on Mount Scopus, overlooking Jerusalem
Correct: C — RYBZ enacted that the shofar may be blown on Shabbat wherever the beit din is present. In his era, this was Yavneh, which he established as the new center of Torah authority after the Temple's destruction.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What was the practical effect of RYBZ's takkanah regarding new grain (chadash) on the 16th of Nissan?
A. New grain became permanently forbidden until the Temple is rebuilt
B. New grain was permitted from midday of the 16th, approximating when the Omer would have been waved
C. The entire day of the 16th of Nissan was forbidden for new grain, not just from the moment of waving
D. New grain restrictions were lifted entirely since the Omer cannot be brought without a Temple
Correct: C — RYBZ enacted that the entire 16th of Nissan is forbidden for new grain. Since there is no Temple and no actual Omer waving, the precise moment of permissibility cannot be established — so the whole day is treated as restricted, as a memorial to the Temple service.
