Rosh Hashanah 3 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Abbahu, why did the Canaanite king of Arad attack Israel after Aaron's death?
A. He saw Israel was emotionally weakened from mourning Aaron
B. The clouds of glory that had protected Israel were removed, leaving them exposed
C. Moses sent a threatening message that provoked the Canaanite king
D. Israel had wandered into Canaanite territory without permission
Correct: B — Rabbi Abbahu reinterprets 'vayiru' (they saw) as 'vayeira'u' (they were seen by others). The clouds of glory — Israel's divine protective cover — were removed upon Aaron's death, making Israel visible and vulnerable to enemy attack.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda, when do the regnal years of Jewish kings of Israel begin?
A. On the exact day the king was crowned
B. 1 Tishrei of the year of coronation
C. 1 Nisan — even if the king was crowned in a different month
D. 1 Nisan only if the king was crowned during the month of Nisan itself
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda rules that all Jewish kings count their regnal years from 1 Nisan, regardless of when they were actually crowned. A king crowned in Adar would already enter 'Year 2' on the very next Nisan — even if he reigned only a few days in Year 1.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the primary Talmudic proof that gentile kings count their regnal years from Tishrei?
A. The Torah explicitly states that non-Israelite kings use a Tishrei calendar
B. Both Kislev and the following Nisan are called 'the 20th year' of Artaxerxes in the book of Nehemiah
C. A baraita directly teaches that all gentile kings counted from Tishrei
D. Darius the Persian issued a royal decree establishing Tishrei as his new year
Correct: B — In Nehemiah 1:1, the month of Kislev is dated to 'the 20th year' of Artaxerxes. In Nehemiah 2:1, the following Nisan is also 'the 20th year.' Since Kislev precedes Nisan yet both are year 20, the new year must fall in Tishrei — the only month between the previous Nisan and this Kislev.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Rav Yosef reject Rabbi Abbahu's answer that Haggai counted Darius's years from Nisan because 'Cyrus was virtuous'?
A. There is no historical or Talmudic source saying Cyrus was virtuous
B. The verses under discussion speak of Darius, not Cyrus — and Darius was never called a righteous king
C. The principle that virtuous gentile kings count from Nisan is itself disputed
D. Rabbi Abbahu was quoting a different book of Nevi'im than the one being analyzed
Correct: B — Rav Yosef makes two pointed objections: First, if Cyrus and Darius are treated differently, the verses in Ezra and Haggai contradict each other. Second and more fundamentally, Rabbi Abbahu invoked Cyrus's righteousness — but Haggai 1:1 names Darius, and Darius was never described as righteous.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yitzḥak resolve the contradiction in Darius's regnal year counting between different biblical books?
A. Different scribes in Ezra and Haggai used different calendar conventions independently
B. Darius formally converted and adopted the Jewish Nisan calendar midway through his reign
C. While Ezra was in Babylonia, Darius was righteous (counted from Nisan); after Ezra went to Israel, Darius became corrupt (counted from Tishrei)
D. The word 'Darius' in Haggai actually refers to a different Persian king than the Darius in Ezra
Correct: C — Rabbi Yitzḥak explains that Darius's moral character — and therefore his calendar reckoning — changed based on Ezra's proximity. When Ezra's presence in Babylonia kept Darius righteous, his years counted from Nisan like a Jewish king. After Ezra ascended to Eretz Yisrael, Darius lapsed into corruption and his years reverted to Tishrei.
