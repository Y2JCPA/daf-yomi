Rosh Hashanah 29 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Zeira, why must the community's shofar-sounder have intent?
A. Because the shofar sound has no halakhic effect without the sounder's concentration
B. To discharge the hearer's obligation, the sounder must explicitly intend to do so on his behalf
C. The Gemara rules that only the hearer needs intent, never the sounder
D. The community sounder is exempt from needing intent since he sounds for everyone at once
Correct: B — Rabbi Zeira holds that the sounder must have intent to discharge the hearer's obligation. This is challenged and ultimately left as a tannaitic dispute — one baraita requires dual intent, another requires none from the sounder.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the Mishna's explanation for how Moses's raised hands helped Israel defeat Amalek?
A. Moses's hands channeled divine energy directly onto the battlefield
B. The raised arms signaled the troops when to advance or retreat
C. When Israel looked upward and directed their hearts toward God, God granted them victory — the hands were symbolic
D. The hands blocked Amalek's arrows through a sustained miracle
Correct: C — The Mishna teaches that the hands themselves accomplished nothing. Rather, when Israel's gaze at Moses's raised hands prompted them to direct their hearts toward Heaven, God gave them the victory. This parallels the intent required for shofar.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rav Naḥman's ruling regarding a half-slave, half-freeman and the shofar obligation?
A. He is fully obligated like a free man and can discharge others
B. He can discharge his own obligation but not anyone else's
C. He cannot discharge the obligation even for himself
D. He may only discharge other half-slaves of the same status
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman rules — confirmed by a baraita — that a half-slave cannot discharge even his own obligation. His slave component cannot discharge his free component; the two statuses are fundamentally incompatible within a single person.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara find it necessary to explicitly state that Priests are obligated in the mitzvah of shofar?
A. Priests are generally exempt from all positive time-bound commandments
B. One might think the verse 'a day of sounding to you' applies only to laypeople, and priests fulfill it through Temple trumpet-sounding
C. The Torah explicitly exempts priests from shofar in a different verse
D. Priests only become obligated in shofar if they are not on Temple duty that day
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that one might erroneously exempt priests — since they sound trumpets in the Temple, and the verse 'a day of sounding to you' might imply it is directed at non-priests. The baraita therefore explicitly includes them.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What principle underlies Ahava bar Rabbi Zeira's teaching about reciting blessings for others?
A. A blessing recited after personal fulfillment has diminished halakhic force but is still valid
B. Blessings are only effective when recited by one who has not yet fulfilled his own obligation
C. All Israel are responsible for one another, so one who fulfilled his obligation may still discharge others'
D. Only a designated communal representative may recite blessings on behalf of the congregation
Correct: C — Ahava bar Rabbi Zeira's rule rests on the principle of kol Yisrael arevin zeh bazeh — all Israel serve as surety for one another. This communal bond means that personal fulfillment does not extinguish one's capacity to discharge others' obligations.
