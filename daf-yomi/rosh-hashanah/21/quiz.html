QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the minimum elapsed time before the new moon can physically be visible?
A. 28 days exactly
B. 29 days and 6 hours
C. 29 days and 12 hours
D. 30 days exactly
Correct: C — The lunar synodic month is approximately 29 days, 12 hours, and 44 minutes. Therefore the new moon cannot physically appear before 29 days and 12 hours have elapsed. Witnesses claiming to see it before then are automatically invalid.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is 'Molad Zaken' and what is its practical halachic consequence?
A. An elderly witness who testifies about the moon's appearance
B. A new moon born after midday, causing Rosh Chodesh to be postponed one day
C. A month that accidentally received 31 days
D. The calculation used to determine when to add a leap month
Correct: B — Molad Zaken (literally 'old birth') means the astronomical new moon was born after midday (the 6th hour). Because the moon won't be visible until the following night, Beit Din cannot sanctify that day as Rosh Chodesh — it is postponed to the next day.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What automatically happens if no witnesses appear before Beit Din on the 30th day of the month?
A. The month is declared invalid and must be restarted
B. Beit Din sends its own observers to look for the moon
C. Rosh Chodesh falls automatically on day 31, making the month 30 days (Malei)
D. The previous month is retroactively shortened to 28 days
Correct: C — If no valid witnesses appear on the 30th day, Beit Din cannot declare Rosh Chodesh that day. The previous month automatically becomes 30 days (Chodesh Malei) and Rosh Chodesh falls on the 31st day — no declaration is required for this default outcome.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What Torah verse is the source for the requirement that Pesach always fall in the spring season?
A. "This month shall be for you the first of the months" (Shemot 12:2)
B. "Guard the month of spring" — שְׁמוֹר אֶת חֹדֶשׁ הָאָבִיב (Devarim 16:1)
C. "You shall celebrate it in its appointed time" (Bamidbar 9:2)
D. "In the first month, on the fourteenth day" (Vayikra 23:5)
Correct: B — The verse 'Shamor et Chodesh HaAviv' — Guard the month of spring (Devarim 16:1) — requires that Nissan and Pesach always occur in the spring season. This mandates calendar intercalation (adding Adar II) whenever Pesach would otherwise drift before the spring equinox.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Abaye's tradition about the tekufot, how long is each of the four seasons?
A. 90 days and 0 hours each
B. 91 days and 7.5 hours each
C. 30 days and 6 hours each
D. 365 days total, divided unevenly by lunar months
Correct: B — The solar year of 365 days and 6 hours is divided into four equal tekufot (seasons): Nissan, Tammuz, Tishrei, and Tevet. Each season is 91 days and 7.5 hours. This is the Shmuel-based calculation preserved in Abaye's name.
