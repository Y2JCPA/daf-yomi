🌊 When Did the Flood Begin?

Rabbi Yehoshua
The flood began in Iyyar (2nd month from Nisan). This is supported by 'the second month' counting from Nisan — and constituted a supernatural change, since rain does not normally fall in Iyyar.
Rabbi Eliezer
The flood began in Marcheshvan (2nd month from Tishrei). Rain does fall then — so where is the miraculous change? Rav Ḥisda answers: they sinned with boiling heat, and were punished with boiling heat.
Resolution Both views involve a supernatural change. For Rabbi Yehoshua, rain in Iyyar is abnormal. For Rabbi Eliezer (per Rav Ḥisda), the flood waters were supernaturally boiling hot — measure for measure.

🗓️ Which Calendar Basis Do We Follow?

For Year-Counting & Flood
Jewish Sages count years from Creation and date the flood according to Rabbi Eliezer — from Tishrei. This forms the basis of our Jewish calendar year number.
For Astronomical Cycles
The Sages calculate solar and lunar cycles (tekufot) according to Rabbi Yehoshua — from Nisan. The tekufa of Nisan anchors the astronomical spring.
Practical Hybrid We use both: the year number follows Rabbi Eliezer (Tishrei), but astronomical cycle calculations follow Rabbi Yehoshua (Nisan).

📅 Three Roles of 1 Tishrei as New Year

Vegetables (ירקות)

  • Rabbinic tithe obligation
  • Year determined by harvest date
  • Cannot tithe pre-Rosh HaShana crop for post, or vice versa
  • Baraita lists this first — it was dear to the tanna

Tithes (מעשרות)

  • Torah-level obligation on grain, wine, oil
  • Year boundary = 1 Tishrei
  • Includes both animal tithe and grain tithe
  • Second tithe (yrs 1,2,4,5) vs. poor man's tithe (yrs 3,6)

Vows (נדרים)

  • Does NOT reset at calendar Rosh HaShana
  • Counts 12 months day-to-day from vow date
  • Follows ordinary language of people
  • Exception: saying 'this year' means until Rosh HaShana

🌿 Picking Vegetables on Erev Rosh HaShana

Step 1: Picks vegetables before sunset on Erev Rosh HaShana → belongs to the old year
↓
Step 2: Returns and picks more vegetables after sunset → belongs to the new year
↓
Rule: May not set aside teruma or tithe from one batch for the other — they are separate tithe-year categories
↓
If transition spans Year 2→3: Old crop requires second tithe; new crop requires poor man's tithe — completely different obligations
↓
Proof text: Deut. 26:12 — 'When you have made an end of tithing all the tithes… in the third year, the year of the tithe' — the year when one tithe replaces two

⏳ How Long Does a 'Year Vow' Last?

One vows to be prohibited from deriving benefit from another 'for a year' (שנה אחת)
↓
One might think: calendar year
Vow ends at next Rosh HaShana — even if vow was just made
Actual ruling
Count 12 months day-to-day from when the vow was taken — follows ordinary speech (לשון בני אדם)
↓
Exception: If one says 'this year' (שנה זו) — means only until Rosh HaShana, even if one day remains
↓
Principle: Vows are interpreted by the ordinary language of people — not legal calendar constructs
