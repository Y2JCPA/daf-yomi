🌊 When Did the Flood Begin?

Rabbi Yehoshua
The flood began in Iyyar — the second month counting from Nisan. This proves a change in nature, since rain does not normally fall in Iyyar.
Rabbi Eliezer
The flood began in Marcheshvan — the second month counting from Tishrei. The world was created in Tishrei, so Tishrei begins the year.
Rabbi Yehoshua
God changed the acts of Creation: He caused the constellation Kima to rise during the day, removed two stars from it, and thereby brought the floodwaters upon the world.
Rav Ḥisda
Even according to Rabbi Eliezer a change occurred: they sinned with boiling heat (illicit passion), so they were punished measure-for-measure — the floodwaters were supernaturally scalding hot.
🔭 Astronomical Detail God removed two stars from Kima (Pleiades) to bring the flood. After the flood, He took two stars from Ayish (Ursa Major) to replace them — which is why Ayish appears incomplete to this day.

📅 Two Calendrical Systems — The Sages' Synthesis

Rabbi Eliezer — Tishrei

  • Year of Creation begins: Tishrei
  • Flood counted from Marcheshvan (2nd from Tishrei)
  • Used for: historical years, shemita & yovel cycles
  • "In the beginning…" — world created in Tishrei

Rabbi Yehoshua — Nisan

  • Solar & lunar cycles calculated from Nisan
  • Flood counted from Iyyar (2nd from Nisan)
  • Used for: astronomical tekufot calculations
  • "This month shall be for you the first" (Exodus 12)

The Sages' Ruling

  • Count years from Tishrei (R. Eliezer)
  • Calculate sun & moon cycles from Nisan (R. Yehoshua)
  • Both systems operate simultaneously in Jewish law
  • This dual-system underlies the Jewish calendar today

🌿 1 Tishrei: New Year for Vegetables, Tithes & Vows

Vegetables (ירקות) — Tithe by rabbinic law; 1 Tishrei determines which year's tithe applies to vegetables
↓
Tithes (מעשרות) — Grain, wine, oil tithe by Torah law; the word 'tithes' (plural) includes both animal tithe and grain tithe
↓
The Critical Rule: One who picks vegetables before sunset on Rosh Hashana eve (old year) and again after sunset (new year) — may NOT tithe from one batch to exempt the other
↓
Vows (נדרים) — A vow 'for a year' is counted twelve months from day to day (not by the calendar year), following ordinary human language
↓
Why list vegetables first? Though vegetable tithe is only d'rabbanan, the tanna considered this halakha precious and therefore placed it first

📜 Third Year — Does First Tithe Still Apply?

Question
In the third year, the poor man's tithe replaces the second tithe — but does first tithe (given to Levites) still apply, or is only a single tithe taken?
Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi
"When you have made an end of tithing all the tithes…" (Deut. 26:12) — 'all the tithes' implies: in the third year you give both first tithe (to Levites) AND poor man's tithe.
Counter-argument
Perhaps only one tithe is taken — and the verse simply emphasizes thoroughness? The word 'all' might not indicate two tithes.
Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov
Proof from Numbers 18: "When you take from Israel the tithes I have given you… you shall set aside a tithe of the tithe." The Levites still receive first tithe — so terumas ma'aser must still be separated in year 3.
✅ Conclusion In year 3 (and year 6): First tithe goes to Levites as always; Second tithe is replaced by poor man's tithe. The verse 'year of the tithe' refers to the year when there is only ONE of the two tithes given to the owner (second tithe is replaced).

🌾 When Does Produce Become Obligated in Tithes?

At what point is produce חייב במעשרות?
The year of that moment determines which year's tithes apply
↓
🌱 Fenugreek (תלתן)
From time of sprouting for seed — when seed pods form, not mere germination from the ground
🌾 Grain & Olives
From reaching one-third of full growth (הגיע לשליש)
↓
Practical Consequence: If grain reaches ⅓ growth before Rosh Hashana but is harvested after, it belongs to the previous year
↓
Shemita Application (Rabbi Zeira): Produce reaching ⅓ in the Sabbatical Year retains shemita sanctity even if harvested after Sukkot of the 8th year
