Rosh Hashanah 32 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Akiva, where in the Musaf Amida is Kedushah HaYom (Sanctification of the Day) recited?
A. In a separate blessing before Malkhuyot
B. Together with Malkhuyot in the fourth blessing
C. Together with Zikhronot in the fifth blessing
D. After Shofarot as a closing blessing
Correct: B — Rabbi Akiva argued: if no shofar is sounded for Malkhuyot alone, why mention Malkhuyot separately? Therefore Kedushah HaYom and Malkhuyot are recited together in the fourth blessing, followed by the shofar.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yochanan, the ten verses of the Rosh Hashanah Musaf blessings correspond to:
A. The ten praises David composed in Psalms
B. The Ten Commandments given at Sinai
C. The ten utterances through which the world was created
D. The ten plagues brought upon Egypt
Correct: C — Rabbi Yochanan said they correspond to the ten utterances (ma'amarot) of Creation. There are nine 'And God said' phrases in Genesis; 'In the beginning' counts as the tenth, based on 'By the word of God the heavens were made' (Psalms 33:6).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which of the following verses CANNOT be recited as part of the Zikhronot (Remembrances) blessing?
A. 'And the Lord revisited [pakad] Sarah' (Genesis 21:1)
B. 'He remembered that they were but flesh, a wind that passes away' (Psalms 78:39)
C. 'I have remembered My covenant' (Exodus 6:5)
D. A verse about God remembering to punish gentile nations
Correct: B — Verses of remembrance dealing with punishment of Israel may not be used. 'He remembered they were but flesh' (Ps. 78:39) describes God recalling Israel's sinful nature — a punishment theme. Verses about God 'revisiting' (pakad) individuals are equivalent to remembrances and ARE permitted.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why is Hallel not recited on Rosh Hashanah, according to Rabbi Abbahu?
A. Because Rosh Hashanah is a fast day and joy is forbidden
B. Because the shofar-blowing fulfills the obligation of song
C. Because the King sits in judgment with books of life and death open — joyful song is inappropriate
D. Because Hallel requires a Temple sacrifice not offered on Rosh Hashanah
Correct: C — Rabbi Abbahu explains via the angels' question to God: why doesn't Israel sing before You on Rosh Hashanah? God replies: Is it possible that the King sits on His throne of judgment with the books of life and death open, and Israel should sing joyful songs?
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why is the shofar sounded during Musaf rather than during Shacharit, according to Rabbi Yochanan?
A. Because more people attend Musaf and it honors the congregation
B. Because it is easier to fast until Musaf before sounding the shofar
C. It was enacted during persecution — morning guards prevented shofar-sounding, but had left by Musaf
D. Because Musaf is holier than Shacharit on Rosh Hashanah
Correct: C — Rabbi Yochanan taught this was a protective enactment (takkanah) during religious persecution. Gentile authorities placed guards in the morning specifically to prevent shofar-sounding. By the time of Musaf the guards departed, so the Sages decreed to sound it then — and the enactment remained permanent.
