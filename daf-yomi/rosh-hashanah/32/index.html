⚖️ Where Is Kedushah HaYom Recited in Musaf?

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
Kedushah HaYom goes with Malkhuyot — the 4th blessing. The verse 'I am the Lord your God' (Lev. 23:22) refers simultaneously to God's Kingship and to the sanctity of the day.
Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel
Kedushah HaYom goes with Zikhronot — the 5th blessing, the middle of the seven Musaf blessings. On all other festivals, Kedushah HaYom is recited in the middle blessing of the Amida.
Rabbi Akiva
If no shofar is sounded for Malkhuyot alone, why mention it separately? Therefore: recite Malkhuyot together with Kedushah HaYom in the 4th blessing, sound the shofar, then Zikhronot, then Shofarot.
Historical Practice at Usha Rabbi Yochanan ben Beroka led prayer per Rabbi Yochanan ben Nuri's view on day one; Rabbi Chanina led per Rabbi Akiva's view on day two — Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel praised both, showing both positions were legitimate.

🔢 What Do the Ten Verses of Each Blessing Correspond To?

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi

  • The ten praises David recited in the book of Psalms
  • Excludes praises sung with musical instruments
  • Excludes psalms not composed by David himself

Rav Yosef

  • The Ten Commandments (Aseret HaDibrot)
  • The divine words spoken to Moses at Sinai
  • Ten utterances of direct divine command

Rabbi Yochanan

  • The ten utterances (ma'amarot) of Creation
  • Nine 'And God said' phrases in Genesis
  • 'In the beginning' = 10th utterance: 'By the word of God the heavens were made' (Ps. 33:6)

🤫 Why Is Hallel Not Recited on Rosh Hashanah?

Question: Why is Hallel omitted on Rosh HaShanah and Yom Kippur?
↓
Rabbi Abbahu's Answer: The ministering angels asked God why Israel doesn't sing praise before Him on these days
↓
The Scene
The King of Kings sits on His throne of judgment with the books of life and death open before Him
God's Response
'Is it possible that Israel should sing joyful songs at this moment?' — The gravity of judgment precludes Hallel

📯 Why Does the Second Prayer Leader Sound the Shofar During Musaf?

Mishna (Statement)
The second prayer leader (Musaf) sounds the shofar on behalf of the congregation. The first prayer leader (Shacharit) leads Hallel on festivals.
Gemara (Challenge)
Why the second? If it is for 'splendor of the King in the multitude' — Hallel too should use the second leader! And if the first leads because the vigilant perform mitzvot early — the shofar too should be sounded early!
Rabbi Yochanan
The shofar was moved to Musaf during a time of religious persecution. Gentile authorities placed guards in the morning specifically to prevent shofar-sounding. By Musaf time, the guards had departed — so the Sages decreed to sound it then. The practice remained even after the persecution ended.
Halakhic Principle Once the Sages enacted a protective decree, it remained in force permanently — even when the original danger passed. This is standard rabbinic legislative practice.
