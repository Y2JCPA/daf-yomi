Rosh Hashanah 16 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the mishna, on which festival is judgment passed concerning grain?
A. Rosh HaShana
B. Passover
C. Shavuot
D. Sukkot
Correct: B — The mishna states explicitly: 'On Passover judgment is passed concerning grain' — the start of the grain harvest season. Rava adds that two verdicts are issued: one from sowing to Passover and one from Passover to harvest.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rav Ḥisda rejects Job 7:18 as the real basis for Rabbi Yosei's view. Which verse does he ultimately cite?
A. "He Who fashions their hearts alike" (Ps. 33:15)
B. "Sound a shofar" (Ps. 81:4)
C. "To make the judgment...as each day may require" (I Kings 8:59)
D. "For God has heard the voice of the lad where he is" (Gen. 21:17)
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda argues that Job 7:18 could mean 'visit' in a positive sense. Instead, I Kings 8:59 — 'as each day may require' — is the true basis, showing that judgment is rendered daily according to each day's deeds.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why is the shofar sounded both before and during the Amida on Rosh HaShana?
A. To fulfill two separate biblical commands
B. To commemorate the two rams of the Akeidah
C. To confuse the Satan and silence his accusations
D. To signal the beginning and end of the prayer service
Correct: C — Rabbi Yitzḥak explains: the double sounding (seated then standing) makes Satan think the messianic era has arrived, and he is thereby confused and silenced from bringing accusations against Israel before the Heavenly Court.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Kruspedai citing Rabbi Yoḥanan, what happens to the verdict of the 'middling' (beinonim) on Rosh HaShana?
A. They are immediately sealed for life
B. They are immediately sealed for death
C. Their verdict is suspended until Yom Kippur
D. They must fast forty days to receive a verdict
Correct: C — The beinonim — neither wholly righteous nor wholly wicked — have their verdict suspended from Rosh HaShana until Yom Kippur. If they tip toward merit, they are sealed for life; if toward demerit, for death.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rabbi Yitzḥak lists four things that can tear up an evil decree. Which of the following is NOT among the four?
A. Giving charity (tzedakah)
B. Fasting (ta'anit)
C. Changing one's name (shinui shem)
D. Changing one's deeds (shinui ma'aseh)
Correct: B — The four are: charity, crying out in prayer, change of name, and change of deeds. Some authorities add a fifth — change of place — citing God's command to Avraham (Gen. 12:1-2). Fasting does not appear in this list.
