🕯️ When Are Sentences Sealed?

R. Meir
All are judged on Rosh HaShana, and their sentences are sealed on Yom Kippur.
R. Yehuda
All are judged on Rosh HaShana, but sentences are sealed each in its own time — Passover for grain, Shavuot for fruit, Sukkot for water.
R. Yosei
A person is judged every single day — 'You visit him every morning' (Job 7:18).
R. Natan
A person is judged every moment — 'You try him every moment' (Job 7:18).
Rava's Resolution The mishna follows the school of Rabbi Yishmael, which lists four distinct judgment times for different domains of creation.

🌾 Two Judgments per Crop — Rava & Abaye

First Judgment

  • Covers the period from sowing until Passover
  • Crop suffers before Passover → judged at last Passover
  • Rava: derive from baraita — two separate verdicts per crop

Second Judgment

  • Covers the period from Passover until harvest
  • Crop suffers after Passover → judged at this Passover
  • Abaye: one may still pray for a field already afflicted, since a second judgment is yet to come

🐏 The Shofar and the Akeida

עקידת יצחק — Binding of Isaac (Genesis 22) | זכרון עקידה
R. Abbahu — Why a Ram's Horn? Sound a blast before Me with a shofar made from a ram's horn, so that I will remember for you the Binding of Isaac son of Abraham. I will account it to you as if you had bound yourselves before Me.

R. Yitzchak adds: The double blowing of the shofar — once seated, once standing — confuses the Satan and demonstrates Israel's love for the mitzva.
