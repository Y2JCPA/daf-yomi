⚖️ When Is Judgment Sealed?

Rabbi Meir
All are judged on Rosh HaShana and their sentence is sealed on Yom Kippur.
Rabbi Yehuda
All judged on Rosh HaShana, but sentences sealed each in its season: grain/Passover, fruit/Shavuot, water/Sukkot, people/Yom Kippur.
Rabbi Yosei
A person is judged every single day — "You visit him every morning" (Job 7:18); Rav Ḥisda grounds this in I Kings 8:59.
Rabbi Natan
A person is judged every hour — "You test him every moment" (Job 7:18).
Rava's Resolution The mishna follows a tanna from the school of Rabbi Yishmael — four distinct judgment seasons, each for a different domain of creation.

📅 Four Times the World Is Judged

🌾 Passover

  • Judgment on grain
  • Two verdicts: sowing→Passover, then Passover→harvest (Rava)
  • Omer offering: pray for a good grain year (Rabbi Akiva)

🍇 Shavuot

  • Judgment on tree fruits
  • Two loaves offered to pray for fruit
  • Season of firstfruits

💧 Sukkot

  • Judgment on rain / water
  • Water libation poured on the altar
  • "So that the rains of the year be blessed"

🎺 Rosh HaShana

  • All creatures pass before God like benei maron (sheep)
  • Judgment on all humanity
  • "He Who fashions their hearts alike" (Ps. 33:15)

📚 Three Books Opened on Rosh HaShana

Rosh HaShana
Three books opened before the Holy One (Rabbi Kruspedai / Rabbi Yoḥanan)
↓
📗 Wholly Righteous
Immediately inscribed and sealed for life
📒 Middling (Beinonim)
Verdict suspended from Rosh HaShana until Yom Kippur — merit tips toward life, demerit toward death
📕 Wholly Wicked
Immediately inscribed and sealed for death
↓
"Let them be blotted out of the book of the living, but not written with the righteous"
Ps. 69:29 — Rabbi Avin's proof; cf. Exodus 32:32 — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak

🙏 Four Things That Tear Up an Evil Decree (Rabbi Yitzḥak)

1. צְדָקָה — Charity
Giving to those in need invokes divine mercy in return
↓
2. צְעָקָה — Crying Out in Prayer
Sincere supplication at the moment of distress pierces the heavens
↓
3. שִׁינּוּי שֵׁם — Change of Name
A new name signals a new identity before God — as Avram→Avraham, Sarai→Sarah
↓
4. שִׁינּוּי מַעֲשֶׂה — Change of Deeds
Genuine behavioral transformation and repentance
↓
Some add: שִׁינּוּי מָקוֹם — Change of Place
"Go from your land" (Gen. 12:1) → "I will make of you a great nation" (Gen. 12:2)

🐏 The Shofar: Ram's Horn and Its Double Sounding

Rabbi Abbahu
God said: Sound a blast with a ram's horn so that I remember the Akeidah — the binding of Isaac — and reckon it as though you bound yourselves before Me.
Rabbi Yitzḥak
We sound tekia-terua before the Amida (seated) and again during the Amida (standing) — in order to confuse the Satan and silence his accusations against Israel.
Rabbi Yitzḥak
Any year that begins poor and troubled (rasha) will end rich and blessed — for "from the beginning of the year" implies one end to the other.
Why Confusion Works When Satan hears the shofar blown twice — once seated and once standing — he fears the messianic era has arrived and withdraws his accusation before the Heavenly Court.
