Rosh Hashanah 19 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did the Sages restrict Shabbat witness travel to only Nisan and Tishrei?
A. Because only those two months have Torah-ordained holidays
B. Because the Boethusians hired false witnesses to manipulate the calendar
C. Because Roman authorities prohibited travel during other months
D. Because witnesses found long-distance travel in other months too difficult
Correct: B — The Boethusians, a sectarian group, hired false witnesses to give false testimony about the new moon of Tishrei in order to shift when Yom Kippur fell. To prevent future manipulation, the Sages restricted Shabbat travel to only Nisan and Tishrei.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What did R. Yochanan ben Zakkai do regarding the Shabbat witness-travel restriction?
A. He strengthened it, limiting travel to Tishrei alone
B. He transferred enforcement to the Roman courts
C. He abolished all Shabbat witness travel entirely
D. He restored travel on Shabbat for all six months
Correct: D — R. Yochanan ben Zakkai restored the original practice — witnesses could again travel on Shabbat for all six major months — because the Boethusian threat that necessitated the restriction had passed.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Under what conditions may a later Beit Din overturn a decree of an earlier Beit Din?
A. Any later court may always overturn any earlier ruling
B. Only if a majority of the people no longer observe the decree
C. Only if the later court is greater in both wisdom and number
D. Only with the approval of the Nasi (Patriarch)
Correct: C — The Talmudic rule is that a court may overturn another court's decree only if it is greater in both חכמה (wisdom) and מנין (number of members). Otherwise the prior decree remains binding.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why is Chanukah considered impossible to abolish, even in principle?
A. Because it is mentioned explicitly in the written Torah
B. Because it was enacted by a full Sanhedrin of 71 members
C. Because it was established with widespread public proclamation and universal acceptance
D. Because the miracle of the oil was witnessed by thousands
Correct: C — Chanukah was established with pirsum hanes — public celebration of the miracle — and became universally observed throughout Israel. This level of public embedding gives it a permanence beyond ordinary rabbinic decrees.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
For which six months were witnesses originally permitted to travel on Shabbat to testify about the new moon?
A. Tishrei, Cheshvan, Kislev, Tevet, Shevat, Adar
B. Nisan, Iyar, Sivan, Tammuz, Av, Elul
C. Nisan, Av, Elul, Tishrei, Kislev, Adar
D. Only Nisan and Tishrei — it was never more than two
Correct: C — The six months are Nisan (Pesach), Av (Tisha B'Av), Elul (pre-Rosh Hashanah preparation), Tishrei (Rosh Hashanah, Yom Kippur, Sukkot), Kislev (Chanukah), and Adar (Purim) — each anchoring a major observance.
