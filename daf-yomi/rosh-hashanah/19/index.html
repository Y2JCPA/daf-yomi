🎭 The Boethusian Plot

מפני הביתוסים שהיו שולחין עדי שקר להטעות את בית דין
Historical Incident The Boethusians once hired two false witnesses to testify before the court that they had seen the new moon of Tishrei. Their aim: to manipulate when Yom Kippur fell — either forcing the fast to coincide with Shabbat or shifting other observances. In response, the Sages issued a decree limiting Shabbat travel for witnesses to only Nisan and Tishrei, the two months of greatest consequence.

⚖️ Can a Later Court Overturn an Earlier Decree?

Question
R. Yochanan ben Zakkai restored witness travel for all months — but wasn't the original court that restricted it greater than he was? How could he overturn their decree?
Principle
A later court can overturn an earlier court's ruling ONLY if it is greater in both wisdom (חכמה) and number (מנין) — otherwise the decree stands.
Resolution
R. Yochanan ben Zakkai did not 'overturn' anything. The decree was enacted to counter Boethusian deception. Once that threat vanished, the decree lapsed of its own accord — its rationale was gone.
Implication
This illustrates a key concept: decrees tied to specific circumstances expire when those circumstances end, without needing formal abrogation by a superior court.
Core Rule בית דין שאחר בית דין — a subsequent court overturns a prior court only if greater in wisdom and number (גדול ממנו בחכמה ובמנין)

🕎 Why Chanukah Is Permanently Binding

Chanukah established by the Hasmonean court following the miraculous military victory and rededication of the Temple
↓
Question raised: If a later court can overturn an earlier court's decree (when greater in wisdom and number), could Chanukah theoretically be abolished?
↓
Answer: Chanukah was accepted universally throughout Israel with pirsum hanes — public proclamation and celebration of the miracle
↓
Key principle: Once a practice is publicly embedded and universally observed, it takes on a permanence beyond ordinary rabbinic decrees — the people's practice itself reinforces and 'locks in' the enactment
↓
Same logic applies to Purim: Widely-accepted, publicly-celebrated holidays cannot simply be legislated away by a later court

📅 Three Eras of the Witness-Travel Rule

Era 1 — Original Practice

  • Witnesses travel on Shabbat for all 6 major months
  • Maximum calendar accuracy maintained
  • Full trust placed in witnesses' integrity
  • No sectarian interference anticipated

Era 2 — After Boethusian Incident

  • Shabbat travel restricted to Nisan & Tishrei only
  • Protective measure against false testimony
  • Other months calculated from the two anchors
  • Strictness prioritized over convenience

Era 3 — R. Yochanan ben Zakkai

  • All six months restored for Shabbat travel
  • Boethusian threat had fully dissipated
  • Calendar accuracy again the top priority
  • Demonstrates halakhic responsiveness to changing reality
