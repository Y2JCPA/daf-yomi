📜 Core Dispute: Is Megillat Ta'anit Still in Force?

Tanna Kamma
Megillat Ta'anit was nullified after the Temple's destruction — the miracles that inspired its commemorations are gone, so the prohibitions against fasting on those days no longer apply.
Other Tanna'im
Megillat Ta'anit was NOT nullified — these days remain permanently forbidden for fasting even without the Temple. The decrees stand regardless of historical circumstance.
Rav Tovi bar Mattana
Objection! The story of Yehuda ben Shammua (a student of Rabbi Meir, who lived after the Temple's destruction) shows these fast days were still operative post-destruction — so it cannot have been nullified!
Gemara (Resolution)
The halakha: these days WERE nullified — and the halakha is that they were NOT nullified. Both rulings coexist, each applying in its own domain or community context.
Practical Ruling The Gemara issues a dual halakha: nullified AND not nullified. When the Temple stands — not nullified; after destruction — nullified. Both apply depending on era and circumstance.

⚔️ Why Is the 3rd of Tishrei Forbidden for Fasting?

Question: In Temple times, why is fasting prohibited on the 3rd of Tishrei per Megillat Ta'anit?
↓
Answer: It is the day Gedaliah ben Ahikam was assassinated — his death ended the last remnant of Jewish autonomy in the Land, so mourning-like fasting is forbidden on this commemoration
↓
New Question: Then why is the 2nd of Tishrei also forbidden for fasting?
↓
Answer 1
The 2nd is the day after Rosh HaShana (New Moon) — fasting is forbidden on both the day before and the day after any festive day listed in Megillat Ta'anit
Answer 2
The 2nd is the day before Gedaliah's fast day — in Temple times the fast was on the 3rd, making the 2nd a pre-festive protected day

🌙 Messengers for New Moon: Why Go Out in Tishrei?

Mishna
Messengers go out in Elul due to Rosh HaShana — AND in Tishrei due to the Tishrei festivals (Sukkot, Shemini Atzeret).
Gemara Challenge
If messengers went in Elul and everyone knows when Rosh HaShana falls, why send again in Tishrei? Can't communities simply count 15 days from Rosh HaShana to find Sukkot?
Resolution
Perhaps the court added a day to Elul (making it 30 days), so Rosh HaShana would fall on the 31st day after the previous New Moon — distant communities would not know this without messengers!
Rabbi Ḥinnana bar Papi
We have never found that Elul was declared a full month of 30 days — it is always 29 days. So this theoretical concern never materialized in practice.
Gemara's Conclusion Elul was never historically extended, but Tishrei messengers still went out to protect the festival dates — confirmed by the mishna's own language: 'due to the festivals of Tishrei.'

📅 The Problem of Elul Having 30 Days

The Problem

  • If Elul has 30 days, Rosh HaShana falls on Day 31
  • Communities counting 30 days would celebrate Rosh HaShana one day early
  • All Tishrei festivals (Sukkot, Shemini Atzeret) observed on wrong dates
  • Yom Kippur also shifted — severe consequences for improper observance

The Gemara's Principle

  • "Better that Rosh HaShana be ruined than all Tishrei festivals"
  • Protecting Sukkot/Shemini Atzeret outweighs Rosh HaShana confusion
  • Tishrei messengers were the safeguard for the many festivals
  • Elul never had 30 days — but the system was prepared for the possibility

The Resolution

  • Mishna's language is precise: messengers go in Tishrei 'for its festivals'
  • This proves Tishrei messengers served the festivals, not just Rosh HaShana
  • Conclude from here: Elul can theoretically be extended — it just never was
  • The system anticipated flexibility while maintaining stability

🗓️ Leap Year Adar: Length, Purim, and the Ruling from Eretz Yisrael

Mishna's ruling: Messengers go out in Adar due to Purim — but the mishna does not mention a second set of messengers for Second Adar in a leap year.
↓
Dispute between Tanna'im: One view — mitzvot of Second Adar (Purim) also apply in First Adar. Other view — First Adar has no Purim mitzvot whatsoever; they are only in Second Adar.
↓
Baraita on when messengers go: Messengers go out 30 days before a festival — or one month before. Question: Does 'month' mean exactly 30 days, or does it mean a calendar month (which people can track naturally)?
↓
Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi testifies in the name of the holy community of Jerusalem: Both months of Adar in a leap year are short months (29 days) — sanctified on the 30th day, but never declared full months.
↓
Ruling sent from Eretz Yisrael to Mar Ukva (Exilarch): The Adar immediately before Nisan is always short (29 days), in both regular and leap years. The first Adar in a leap year may sometimes be made into a full month (30 days).
