Rosh Hashanah 27 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
If the mouthpiece of a shofar is plated with gold, what is its status?
A. Always valid — gold beautifies the mitzvah
B. Valid on Rosh HaShana but not fast days
C. Invalid — gold at the mouthpiece alters the sound and disqualifies it
D. Valid only if the shofar is also curved
Correct: C — A baraita teaches that gold plating at the place where one puts his mouth invalidates the shofar because it changes the sound. The mishna refers to gold plating on the bell/outside, not the mouthpiece.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why is the Rosh HaShana shofar plated with gold while fast-day shofarot are plated with silver?
A. Silver is more durable and practical for multiple fast days
B. On fast days one bows in supplication (silver); on Rosh HaShana one stands upright and honors the Festival (gold)
C. The Temple had more silver available than gold for fast days
D. Silver amplifies sound better for crying out in prayer
Correct: B — The Gemara explains the symbolism: bowing on fast days fits silver (humility/supplication), while standing upright on Rosh HaShana fits gold (honor of the Festival). The character of the day determines the plating.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was the miraculous event at Sinai that the Gemara invokes to explain the rule about two sounds?
A. The shofar sounded by itself for forty days without anyone blowing it
B. The entire nation heard the sound despite standing miles away from the mountain
C. God spoke 'Remember the Shabbat' and 'Guard the Shabbat' simultaneously in one Divine utterance
D. Lightning and thunder accompanied the shofar making multiple sounds at once
Correct: C — The Gemara establishes that two sounds from one source cannot normally be distinguished. The miracle at Sinai was that God's one Voice spoke both 'Remember' (Exodus 20:8) and 'Guard' (Deuteronomy 5:12) simultaneously — something humanly impossible to produce or perceive.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When Rav Pappa bar Shmuel wanted to implement the full shofar-and-trumpet arrangement, what did Rava tell him?
A. He needed Sanhedrin approval before instituting new practices
B. This arrangement applies only in the Temple, not in outside communities
C. He should wait until the Jubilee Year to reinstate Temple practices
D. The practice is permitted only on Rosh HaShana, never on Yom Kippur
Correct: B — Rava ruled that the mishna's shofar-flanked-by-trumpets arrangement is a Temple-only practice. This was confirmed by a baraita and by the fact that Rabbis corrected both Rabbi Chalafta in Tzippori and Rabbi Chananya ben Teradyon in Sikhni for doing the same thing.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the mishna introduced at the end of Daf 27, what is the status of a cracked shofar that was glued back together?
A. Valid if the crack is less than half the shofar's length
B. Valid if the repaired shofar produces an unaltered sound
C. Unfit — even if it appears whole after being glued
D. Valid if it broke accidentally rather than through negligence
Correct: C — The mishna rules that a shofar cracked and glued back together is unfit even if it looks whole. Similarly, fragments of multiple shofarot glued together to form one shofar are also unfit — structural integrity is essential.
