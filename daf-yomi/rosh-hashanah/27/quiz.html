Rosh Hashanah 27 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
If the mouth of a shofar is plated with gold, what is the halachic ruling?
A. Fit — gold beautifies the Rosh HaShana mitzvah
B. Unfit — the blower's lips touch gold, not the shofar itself
C. Fit on Rosh HaShana only; unfit on fast days
D. Unfit only if the sound is audibly changed
Correct: B — The baraita rules gold plating at the mouth renders the shofar unfit. The blower presses lips to gold, not horn — the instrument being played is effectively gold. Plating on the outside is only disqualified if the sound changes.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
The Gemara proves that two sounds from one source cannot be discerned. What is the source for this principle?
A. A baraita about ten people reading Hallel simultaneously
B. A ruling of Rav about the Temple trumpets
C. The miracle at Sinai — 'Remember' and 'Guard' heard simultaneously from one divine voice
D. Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel's ruling about shofar size
Correct: C — The Gemara invokes the Sinaitic miracle: Israel heard both 'Remember the Shabbat' (Exodus 20:8) and 'Guard the Shabbat' (Deuteronomy 5:12) at once from one divine source — something humanly impossible, proving one source cannot produce two discernible sounds.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Someone blows a shofar into a pit on Rosh HaShana. Who among the listeners fulfills their obligation?
A. Everyone who hears any sound, whether inside or outside the pit
B. Only those standing at the edge outside the pit
C. Only those standing inside the pit who hear the direct shofar sound
D. No one — blowing into a pit is inherently invalid
Correct: C — Rav Huna: those at the edge hear an echo or reverberation, not the shofar sound itself, and do NOT fulfill the obligation. Those inside the pit hear the genuine shofar sound directly and DO fulfill it.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
A shofar cracked and was repaired by gluing. What is its status, and why?
A. Fit, provided the crack does not impede the sound
B. Fit, since the majority of the original shofar remains
C. Unfit — a cracked-and-glued shofar is no longer a whole shofar
D. Fit on a rabbinic level but not on a Torah level
Correct: C — The mishna rules explicitly: a cracked shofar that was glued — even though it appears whole — is unfit. Integrity of the shofar as a single whole object is required; gluing restores appearance, not essential wholeness.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel, what is the minimum size for a valid shofar?
A. At least one handbreadth (tefach) in length
B. Large enough that its sound can be heard across the courtyard
C. Visible protruding on both sides of a closed fist when held in the hand
D. Large enough to hold a revi'it of liquid
Correct: C — Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel's measure: when a person holds the shofar in a closed hand, it must protrude and be visible on both sides — this establishes a functional minimum length based on a human grip.
