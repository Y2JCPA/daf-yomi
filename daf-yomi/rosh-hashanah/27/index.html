🎷 Can Two Sounds Be Discerned at Once?

Mishna
On Rosh HaShana, two trumpets flanked the shofar-sounder — one on each side — and were sounded simultaneously.
Gemara Challenge
But can one really discern two different sounds at once — shofar and trumpet — and know which is which?
Resolution
Two sounds from one source cannot be discerned. Two sounds from two different sources can be distinguished.
Proof from Sinai
The miracle at Sinai: Israel heard both "Remember" (Exodus 20:8) and "Guard" (Deuteronomy 5:12) the Shabbat simultaneously — from one divine voice. Two sounds, one source — indiscernible.
Practical Ruling Shofar (one person) + Trumpets (others) = two distinct sources → distinguishable. The shofar blast is prolonged so onlookers know the mitzvah centers on the shofar, not the trumpets.

🏅 Shofar Plating: Rosh HaShana vs. Fast Days

Rosh HaShana Shofar

  • Ram's horn — bent/curved
  • Mouth plated with gold
  • Gold honors the Festival (כבוד יום טוב)
  • Shofar blast is long; trumpets are short
  • Mouth gold plating → ❌ Unfit
  • Outside gold plating, sound unchanged → ✅ Fit

Public Fast Day Shofar

  • Ram's horn — straight
  • Mouth plated with silver
  • Silver evokes affliction and supplication
  • Shofar blast is short; trumpets are long
  • Outside the Temple: shofar only, no trumpets
  • Rava: Mishna details apply only in the Temple

🕳️ Shofar Blown Into a Pit or Cistern

Person blows shofar downward into a pit, cistern, or large jug (פיטס)
↓
Standing at the edge (outside)
Hears reverberation / echo — not the pure shofar sound → Did NOT fulfill obligation
Standing inside the pit
Hears the true shofar sound clearly → Obligation fulfilled ✅
↓
Core principle (Rav Huna): The sound heard must be genuine shofar sound — not an echo. Intent (כיוון לבו) is necessary but not sufficient; the sound itself must be valid.

⚖️ Jubilee Yom Kippur — Identical to Rosh HaShana?

Mishna
Yom Kippur of the Jubilee Year equals Rosh HaShana for shofar blasts and additional Amida blessings (מלכויות, זכרונות, שופרות).
Rav Shmuel bar Yitzḥak
Per Rav's view: the Jubilee Yom Kippur blasts are blown by each individual, not only the prayer leader — as on Rosh HaShana.
Rav Eina (objection)
But there is a difference — Jubilee blasts are after Ne'ila, not during Musaf! How can it truly be 'the same' as Rosh HaShana?
Rav Sheisha b. Rav Idi
The mishna's equation refers only to the blessings recited — not that every timing detail is identical. The timing of Jubilee blasts remains distinct.
Verse Source (Rava / R. Yehoshua ben Levi) "בַּחֲצֹצְרוֹת וְקוֹל שׁוֹפָר הָרִיעוּ לִפְנֵי הַמֶּלֶךְ" — Psalms 98:6. Trumpets + shofar together: applies only before the Lord the King — i.e., only in the Temple.

📏 Minimum Size, Sound Standards & Intent

Minimum size (Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel): When held in a closed fist, the shofar must protrude visibly on both sides of the hand.
↓
High or low pitch: Any natural tone is valid — כל הקולות כשרין בשופר — all sounds are fit for the shofar.
↓
Drilled-out bone (ruling from Eretz Yisrael): Drilling out the interior bone of a horn and blowing through it — obligation fulfilled. All shofarot are naturally hollow.
↓
Rav Ashi's clarification: The case is drilling the bone instead of removing it normally. Even same-substance material in the bore does not interpose — the sound passes through the horn itself.
↓
Incidental hearing + intent: One passing behind a synagogue who heard the shofar — if he focused his heart (כיוון לבו) to fulfill the mitzvah, he has fulfilled his obligation.
