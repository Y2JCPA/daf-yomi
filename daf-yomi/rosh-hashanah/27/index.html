🔊 Can Two Sounds Be Discerned Simultaneously?

Mishna
On Rosh HaShana, the shofar was flanked by two trumpets — one on each side of the blower.
Gemara Challenge
But can two different sounds — shofar and trumpet — really be properly discerned at the same time?
First Answer
The shofar extended further than the trumpets, so its sound carried beyond — people heard only the shofar's end blast.
Rejection
That doesn't work! Hearing only the END of a shofar blast cannot fulfill one's obligation — you must hear from the beginning.
Final Answer
Two sounds from two different people CAN be discerned. A miracle was needed only at Sinai, where one Voice spoke 'Remember' and 'Guard' simultaneously.
Core Principle Two sounds from two people can be distinguished; two sounds from one source cannot — that was the miracle of Sinai (זָכוֹר וְשָׁמוֹר בְּדִבּוּר אֶחָד).

🥇 Shofar Plating: Rosh HaShana vs. Fast Days

Rosh HaShana Shofar

  • Straight horn of an ibex (יָעֵל)
  • Mouth plated with gold
  • Worshipper stands upright in prayer
  • Gold honors the Festival (כָּבוֹד יוֹם טוֹב)
  • Flanked by two trumpets on the sides

Public Fast Day Shofar

  • Curved horn of a ram (אַיִל)
  • Mouth plated with silver
  • Worshipper bows in prayer
  • Silver expresses humility and supplication
  • Shofar in middle, trumpets on each side

🎵 Did You Fulfill the Mitzvah of Shofar?

Did you hear a valid shofar sound?
↓
Heard from a pit or cistern
If you heard the actual shofar sound — ✅ fulfilled. If you heard only an echo — ❌ not fulfilled.
Heard only the END of a blast
❌ Did not fulfill — one must hear the beginning of the blast, not merely its tail.
Heard shofar alongside trumpets
✅ Two sounds from two people can be discerned — mitzvah is fulfilled.

⚡ The Miracle at Sinai

זָכוֹר וְשָׁמוֹר בְּדִבּוּר אֶחָד נֶאֱמְרוּ — Remember and Guard were spoken in one utterance
Aggadic Principle At Mount Sinai, Israel heard 'Remember the Shabbat' (Exodus 20:8) and 'Guard the Shabbat' (Deuteronomy 5:12) spoken simultaneously — both from one Divine Voice. This was a miracle, because two sounds emanating from one source cannot normally be discerned by human ears. However, two sounds from two different people — such as a shofar-blower and a trumpet-blower — can indeed be heard and distinguished simultaneously.

📋 Jubilee Yom Kippur & the Unfit Shofar

Basic Rule (Mishna): Yom Kippur of the Jubilee Year (יוֹבֵל) shares the same shofar laws and Amida additions as Rosh HaShana.
↓
Rav Shmuel bar Yitzchak: Just as on Rosh HaShana shofar blasts are sounded while standing, so too on Jubilee Yom Kippur they are sounded while standing.
↓
Rav Eina's Objection: But the mishna says Jubilee Yom Kippur is 'like Rosh HaShana' — yet there are clear differences! How can they be fully equated?
↓
Rav Sheisha's Clarification: Rav Shmuel meant only that the additional Amida blessings (Malkhuyot, Zikhronot, Shofarot) recited on both days are identical.
↓
New Mishna: A shofar that cracked and was glued back together is unfit, even if it looks whole. Fragments of shofarot glued together to form one shofar are also unfit.
