Rosh Hashanah 28 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Which verse is cited as potentially requiring intent for the mitzva of shofar?
A. "A day of blowing" (Numbers 29:1)
B. "A memorial of blasts" (Leviticus 23:24)
C. "You shall hear the shofar" (Deuteronomy 16:3)
D. "Sound the great shofar" (Isaiah 27:13)
Correct: B — The Gemara cites Leviticus 23:24 — 'a memorial of blasts' — as the verse that might imply kavanah (intent) is required for the shofar mitzva, since 'memorial' suggests conscious awareness.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava's final resolution, which of the following is correct?
A. Both fulfilling mitzvot and violating Bal Tosif require intent
B. Neither fulfilling mitzvot nor violating Bal Tosif require intent
C. Fulfilling a mitzva requires no intent, but violating Bal Tosif does require intent
D. Fulfilling a mitzva requires intent, but violating Bal Tosif does not
Correct: C — Rava distinguishes the two categories: a mitzva is fulfilled even without kavanah (intent), but one cannot inadvertently transgress Bal Tosif (do not add) or Bal Tigra (do not diminish) — those violations require deliberate intent.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the dispute about mixed blood sprinklings, what is Rabbi Yehoshua's ruling and reasoning?
A. Sprinkle four times — do not violate Bal Tigra by omission
B. Sprinkle four times — mixtures are treated like the stricter offering
C. Sprinkle once — an active Bal Tosif transgression is worse than a passive omission
D. Sprinkle twice — split the difference between the two offerings
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehoshua rules to sprinkle only once. He reasons that violating Bal Tigra (omission) is passive — 'you do not perform it with your own hand' — while Bal Tosif (active addition) is a direct transgression, making it the greater concern.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Rava say that sleeping in a sukka on Shemini Atzeret does NOT violate Bal Tosif?
A. Because the sukka mitzva never truly ends
B. Because Shemini Atzeret is still part of Sukkot by rabbinic law
C. Because Bal Tosif only applies during the prescribed time of the mitzva
D. Because sleeping is not an active performance of the mitzva
Correct: C — Rava's principle is that Bal Tosif (the prohibition against adding to mitzvot) only applies when one adds during the prescribed time of the mitzva. Since Sukkot has ended by Shemini Atzeret, sleeping in a sukka then is outside the mitzva's time and carries no liability.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara ultimately explain the baraita about a priest who adds personal blessings after the Priestly Blessing?
A. The baraita is rejected as contradicting Rava's principle
B. The priest added blessings in the middle of the Priestly Blessing, still within the prescribed time
C. The priest recited extra blessings on a day when no Priestly Blessing was required
D. The baraita refers only to Yom Kippur, which is uniquely strict
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the baraita's prohibition applies when the priest inserted personal prayers in the middle of the Priestly Blessing — not after completing it. Since the mitzva was still ongoing, this falls within the prescribed time and constitutes a valid Bal Tosif concern.
