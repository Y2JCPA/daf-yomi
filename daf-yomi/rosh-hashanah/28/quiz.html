Rosh Hashanah 28 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
If one hears part of a shofar blast inside a pit and part while standing at the rim, what is the halakha?
A. He has not fulfilled his obligation — the blast was split between two locations
B. He has fulfilled his obligation — a pit is a valid place of obligation for those in it
C. He fulfills his obligation only if the majority of the blast was heard inside the pit
D. He must repeat the entire blast from a single location
Correct: B — A pit is a valid place of obligation for those standing in it. Both portions of the blast were heard in halakhically valid contexts, so the obligation is fulfilled — unlike hearing part before dawn, when shofar cannot be blown at all.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rava's final ruling regarding one who sounds the shofar taken from a burnt-offering (olah)?
A. He fulfills his obligation — misuse causes it to become non-sacred before the sound ends
B. He fulfills his obligation only if he was unaware it was consecrated
C. He does not fulfill his obligation — the shofar is the body of the consecrated animal
D. He fulfills his obligation but must pay the value of the animal to the Temple treasury
Correct: C — Rava retracted the distinction Rav Yehuda drew between burnt-offerings and peace-offerings. Both are invalid because the shofar is the body of the sacrificial animal and remains prohibited throughout the blowing. Misuse only occurs after the fact — too late to validate the blow.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava, why does one who accidentally sleeps in a sukkah on Shemini Atzeret NOT violate bal tosif?
A. Sleeping is not considered performance of a mitzva
B. Bal tosif only applies to Torah-level mitzvot, and sukkah on Shemini Atzeret is rabbinic
C. Bal tosif is only violated when the addition occurs during the mitzva's prescribed time
D. Bal tosif requires at least two witnesses to the transgression
Correct: C — Rava teaches that bal tosif applies only when adding to a mitzva during its prescribed time. Shemini Atzeret falls after Sukkot's prescribed time, so no violation of 'do not add' occurs — even if the person had intent.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
One who sounds a shofar purely for musical enjoyment on Rosh Hashanah — with no mitzva intent — what is the ruling?
A. He has not fulfilled his obligation — 'memorial of blasts' requires conscious intent
B. He has fulfilled his obligation — מצוות אין צריכות כוונה (mitzvot do not require intent)
C. He has fulfilled his obligation only if another person heard and had proper intent
D. He has fulfilled his obligation only if he later declares retroactive intent before nightfall
Correct: B — Rava concludes that mitzvot do not require intent (מצוות אין צריכות כוונה). Just as one compelled to eat matza by Persians fulfills the Passover obligation, one who sounds the shofar for music — without specifically intending to fulfill the mitzva — also fulfills it.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the dispute between Rabbi Eliezer and Rabbi Yehoshua about mixed blood offerings, what is Rabbi Yehoshua's ruling and his primary reasoning?
A. Sprinkle 4 times — the more stringent requirement protects against transgressing bal tigra
B. Sprinkle 1 time — extra sprinklings violate bal tosif, and omitting them is merely a passive omission not done by hand
C. Discard the blood — a mixture cannot satisfy either offering's requirement
D. Sprinkle 2 times — a compromise that partially satisfies both obligations
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehoshua rules to sprinkle only once. His reasoning: (1) extra sprinklings would actively violate bal tosif, (2) failing to do four is only an omission — 'not done with your own hand' — which is less severe than an active transgression of adding forbidden sprinklings.
