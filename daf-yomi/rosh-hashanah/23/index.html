🔥 Why the Torch Relay Was Abolished

Originally: torches lit from the Mount of Olives, relayed station-to-station to announce the New Moon across Eretz Yisrael
↓
Problem: Samaritans (Cutheans) lit false torches on wrong nights to confuse Diaspora communities about the calendar
↓
Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai instituted human messengers who could answer questions and clarify ambiguity
↓
Gemara asks: why not keep torches with a modified system — e.g., light only for full months, or for both full and deficient?
↓
Abaye: any partial system still creates confusion — observers fill the gaps with wrong assumptions. Messengers replaced torches permanently.

🔥 Could the Torch System Have Been Fixed?

Questioner
Light torches for both full and deficient months — then observers won't be confused about month length!
Gemara
Still a problem: when the New Moon falls on Shabbat eve, torches can't be lit that night. People will say: 'The month is full; they were just prevented by circumstances.' Error remains.
Questioner
Then light torches only for full months, never for deficient ones — when torches are absent, everyone knows it's deficient. No ambiguity!
Abaye
That causes the opposite error: when circumstances prevent lighting during a full month, observers will wrongly conclude the month is deficient. No modified torch system avoids all confusion.
Conclusion The Rabbis replaced torches entirely with human messengers who could resolve ambiguities in real time

🌲 How Many Species of Cedar?

Rav Yehuda — 4 Species

  • Used for the torch poles (kelonsot):
  • 1. Erez — cedar
  • 2. Katrom
  • 3. Almog — coral wood
  • 4. Brosh — cypress
  • These burn well and are bound together for the torch relay

Rabba bar Rav Huna (School of Rav) — 10 Species

  • From Isaiah 41:19 — 7 trees named:
  • Cedar, shittah, myrtle, oil-tree,
  • cypress, plane, pine
  • Rav Dimi added 3 more from Eretz Yisrael:
  • • Alah (terebinth)
  • • Alon (oak)
  • • Almog (coral wood)
  • Total: 10 cedar species

🪸 The Coral (Almog) Harvest

Load a ship with sand until it sinks deep onto the seabed
↓
6,000 men for 12 months — or 12,000 men for 6 months — work the harvest
↓
Coral attaches to the ship's hull as it rests on the ocean floor
↓
Unload the sand — ship rises, bringing coral up from the depths
↓
Value
Exchanged for twice its weight in silver — extraordinarily precious
Regional Ports
3 ports: 2 Roman (Armai), 1 Persian — only the Persian port is safe for Jewish traders

🌙 The Sun's Cosmic Blind Spot

שִׁלְטוֹן וָפַחַד עִמּוֹ, עֹשֶׂה שָׁלוֹם בִּמְרוֹמָיו (איוב כה:ב)
Rabbi Yoḥanan — Job 25:2 'He makes peace in His high places' — God arranged two acts of cosmic diplomacy: 1. The sun has never seen the concave side of the new moon — sparing the diminished moon embarrassment before its larger counterpart. 2. The sun has never seen the rainbow — whose brilliant colors might seem to rival Divine glory. This is also why the court asks witnesses: did you see the moon in front of or behind the sun? The sun itself studiously avoids the moon's inner face — a lesson in sensitivity built into creation.
