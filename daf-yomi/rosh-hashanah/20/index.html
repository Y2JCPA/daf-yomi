📆 Can the Court Add Days to a Month?

Rav Naḥman
From the mishna: witnesses desecrate Shabbat for Nisan and Tishrei — this proves Adar's length is uncertain, otherwise witnesses would be unnecessary for Nisan.
Rebuttal
Even if Adar is always short, it is still a mitzva to sanctify the month via witness testimony — that is why they may desecrate Shabbat, not because of calendar uncertainty.
Rav Kahana
But when the Temple stood, witnesses could desecrate Shabbat for all months — only because the New Moon offering required it, not as a mitzva of sanctification per se.
Abaye
Resolve this: Nisan and Tishrei may be sanctified early to fix Festival dates; other months may not be sanctified early — thus witnesses only help for Nisan/Tishrei in post-Temple times.
Key Ruling The court may add days to a month for communal need (e.g., separating Shabbat from Yom Kippur), but may not sanctify a month on the thirtieth day when the new moon was not properly seen.

🌿 Ulla's Report: Adding a Day to Elul

לא אדבקן — so that they would not be adjacent (Shabbat and Yom Kippur)
Historical Incident — Ulla in Babylonia Ulla returned from Eretz Yisrael and announced: This year we added an extra day to Elul, pushing Rosh Hashanah from Thursday to Friday — so that Yom Kippur would not fall immediately after Shabbat. Two consecutive forbidden days created serious hardships for the community. Ulla declared: "Do our Babylonian colleagues understand what benefit we did for them?" — the buffer day resolves both practical and religious difficulties.

⚖️ Two Reasons to Add a Day to Elul

🥬 Rabbi Aḥa bar Ḥanina: Vegetables

  • Fresh vegetables wilt if unprepared for two consecutive forbidden days
  • If Shabbat immediately precedes Yom Kippur: no cooking on either day
  • A weekday buffer allows fresh food preparation for Yom Kippur
  • Applies even when Yom Kippur follows a Festival day (not only Shabbat)
  • Counter: not compelling — possible to prepare beforehand or go without

⚰️ Ulla: The Dead

  • If someone dies on Shabbat, burial is forbidden that day
  • If Yom Kippur follows immediately, burial is again forbidden
  • Two consecutive days without burial causes unbearable grief
  • Applies specifically to Shabbat + Yom Kippur adjacency — not other Festivals
  • Practical difference: only Ulla's reason explains Shabbat-specific concern

📖 Intimidating Witnesses: When Is It Permitted?

Rabba bar Shmuel (baraita)
The court may intimidate witnesses to withhold testimony about the new moon so that Rosh Chodesh is pushed off to the next day — for communal need.
Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi
The court may intimidate witnesses so they give inadmissible testimony, ensuring the new moon is seen at its due time — i.e., to push it off, not advance it.
Rav Dimi of Neharde'a
Opposite: the court may intimidate witnesses to testify about a moon not seen in its due time — to advance sanctification to the thirtieth day.
Gemara (rejects Rav Dimi)
Advancing is worse than pushing off: if the new moon was seen, people noticed — pushing it off looks suspicious. But fabricating a sighting is outright false testimony.
Rava's Resolution The baraita permitting manipulation follows Aḥerim, who say Shavuot must always fall on the fiftieth day from the Omer — requiring active calendar adjustments. The majority disagree with Aḥerim.
