🗓️ Is Adar Always Short? — Witnesses and Shabbat

Opening Challenge
If Adar is always short (29 days), the court can calculate when Nisan begins without witnesses — so why should witnesses ever desecrate Shabbat to testify?
Gemara Answers
It is a mitzva to sanctify the New Moon through witness testimony — the court performs the declaration even when calculation alone would suffice.
Rav Naḥman
The mishna says witnesses desecrate Shabbat for Nisan and Tishrei. If Adar is always short, why is this needed at all? The court already knows the date.
Gemara Rejects
If the 30th of Adar falls on Shabbat: make the month full and declare the next day as New Moon — witnesses need not desecrate Shabbat at all in that scenario.
Rav Kahana Objects
When the Temple stood, witnesses desecrated Shabbat for all months — for the New Moon offering. This shows the mitzva-of-testimony reason only covers Nisan and Tishrei!
Resolution The permission to desecrate Shabbat for all months (Temple era) was due to the musaf offering, not testimony. For Nisan and Tishrei alone, testimony itself is the mitzva.

🥬 Ulla Extends Elul — A Strategic Extra Day

Ulla arrives in Babylonia with news: "This year we added an extra day to Elul" — making it 30 days instead of 29.
↓
Purpose: Push Rosh Hashanah off Shabbat, creating a weekday buffer between Shabbat and the Festival so neither encroaches on the other.
↓
Reason 1 — Ulla (vegetables): If Shabbat and Yom Tov are adjacent, vegetables cannot be picked for two consecutive days — pre-picked produce wilts and is no longer fresh.
↓
Reason 2 — Rabbi Aḥa bar Ḥanina (the dead): A corpse cannot be buried on Shabbat or Yom Tov — two consecutive sacred days forces two days of non-burial, causing disgrace.
↓
Practical difference — Yom Kippur after Shabbat: Vegetables are irrelevant (no cooking on Yom Kippur), but burial of the dead still applies urgently.
↓
Ulla said this benefited Babylonians specifically — they lacked easy access to fresh produce; but the dead concern is universal and applies in Eretz Yisrael too.

⚖️ Two Reasons for Separating Shabbat from Festival

🥬 Vegetables (Ulla)

  • Cannot pick vegetables on two consecutive sacred days
  • Pre-picked vegetables wilt — not fresh for the festival
  • Applies when Shabbat precedes Rosh Hashanah or Yom Tov
  • Does NOT apply before Yom Kippur (no cooking permitted)
  • Ulla saw this as primary benefit for Babylonians

⚰️ The Dead (R. Aḥa bar Ḥanina)

  • A corpse may not be buried on Shabbat or Yom Tov
  • Two adjacent sacred days = two days without burial
  • Causes disgrace and suffering to the deceased
  • Applies even when Yom Kippur follows Shabbat
  • Considered weightier — vegetables can be managed; the dead cannot wait

🔭 Shmuel's Calendar Mastery — The Molad Calculation

Shmuel declares: "I am able to fix the calendar for the entire Diaspora without witnesses." — He mastered the science of celestial movement.
↓
Calculate the molad (lunar conjunction): at what hour does the new moon begin?
Molad before midday
≥6 hours remain in the day → moon will be visible that evening → New Moon may be sanctified on this day
Molad after midday
<6 hours remain → moon will not be visible that night → New Moon falls on the following day
↓
Practical use (Rav Ashi): If witnesses claim to have seen the moon but the molad makes this astronomically impossible — their testimony is refuted
