QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara's conclusion, why may witnesses desecrate Shabbat to testify about the new moon for Nisan — even if the court could calculate the date?
A. Because Adar's length is always uncertain and witnesses are needed to determine it
B. Because it is a mitzva to sanctify the New Moon based on witness testimony
C. Because the Temple's New Moon offering requires exact timing
D. Because Babylonian Jews cannot otherwise know when Pesach begins
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that even if the court could calculate the date independently, the sanctification of the New Moon via witness testimony is itself a mitzva — and that mitzva justifies overriding Shabbat for Nisan and Tishrei.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was Ulla's stated reason for adding an extra day to Elul that year?
A. To ensure fresh vegetables could be prepared between Shabbat and Yom Kippur
B. To allow burial of the dead between two consecutive forbidden days
C. To prevent Yom Kippur from falling immediately after Shabbat
D. To align the Babylonian calendar with the Eretz Yisrael calculation
Correct: C — Ulla explained that adding a day to Elul pushed Rosh Hashanah back by one day, so that Yom Kippur would no longer fall immediately after Shabbat. This created a weekday buffer that benefited the community.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the practical halakhic difference between the 'vegetables' reason and the 'dead' reason for avoiding Shabbat–Yom Kippur adjacency?
A. Vegetables applies only in Babylonia; the dead applies only in Eretz Yisrael
B. The dead concern applies even when Yom Kippur follows a Festival; vegetables does not
C. Vegetables applies even when Yom Kippur follows any Festival; the dead concern applies specifically to Shabbat preceding Yom Kippur
D. There is no practical difference — both lead to the same ruling in all cases
Correct: C — The concern over fresh vegetables extends to any two consecutive days when cooking is forbidden, including a Festival followed by Yom Kippur. The concern over burial of the dead is specific to Shabbat immediately before Yom Kippur, since both days forbid burial.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, what is the practical use of knowing that the moon is invisible for 24 hours between the old and new moon?
A. It determines whether the court must add a day to the preceding month
B. It allows Shmuel to calculate the entire Diaspora calendar without witnesses
C. It can refute witnesses who claim to have seen both the old and new moon within 24 hours
D. It proves that both the night and the day must belong to the new month
Correct: C — Rav Ashi explains that if witnesses testify they saw two moons — the old and the new — within a single 24-hour period, they are certainly lying and can be refuted, because the moon is always invisible for a full 24 hours between the end of the old lunar cycle and the beginning of the new one.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the dispute between Abaye and Rava regarding the derivations of Rabbi Yoḥanan and Reish Lakish about the requirement that both night and day belong to the new moon?
A. Abaye says there is a practical difference; Rava says there is no difference at all
B. Abaye says there is no practical difference; Rava identifies a difference regarding the hours before midnight
C. Abaye follows Reish Lakish's Exodus verse; Rava follows Rabbi Yoḥanan's Leviticus verse
D. Abaye limits the rule to Tishrei; Rava extends it to all months
Correct: B — Abaye holds the two derivations are practically equivalent — they interpret different verses to reach the same ruling. Rava disagrees: according to Rabbi Yoḥanan's 'evening to evening' derivation, if the new moon appeared before midnight, the following day may be sanctified; Reish Lakish's derivation would yield a different result for those pre-midnight hours.
