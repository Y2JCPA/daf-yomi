QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why may witnesses desecrate Shabbat to testify about the new moon for Nisan, even if the court could calculate the date on its own?
A. The court's astronomical calculations are considered unreliable
B. It is a mitzva to sanctify the New Moon through witness testimony
C. The Torah forbids calendar calculation without witnesses
D. Witnesses must appear regardless of any other consideration
Correct: B — The Gemara answers that it is a mitzva to sanctify the New Moon through witness testimony — the declaration through witnesses has intrinsic religious value, even when calculation would suffice.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Ulla added a day to Elul to benefit Babylonians. What was his stated reason?
A. So the dead could be buried before the onset of the festival
B. So witnesses could travel to court without desecrating Shabbat
C. So vegetables would not wilt from being unpicked for two consecutive days
D. So the court could recalculate the molad more accurately
Correct: C — Ulla's reason was due to vegetables — if Shabbat and Rosh Hashanah are adjacent, produce cannot be picked for two days and wilts. He felt Babylonians particularly lacked easy access to fresh produce.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the practical halakhic difference between Ulla's reason (vegetables) and Rabbi Aḥa bar Ḥanina's reason (the dead) for separating Shabbat from a festival?
A. Vegetables applies to Pesach; the dead applies to Rosh Hashanah
B. The difference arises when Yom Kippur falls directly after Shabbat
C. Vegetables applies in Eretz Yisrael; the dead applies in Babylonia
D. There is no practical halakhic difference between them
Correct: B — When Yom Kippur directly follows Shabbat, the vegetable concern is irrelevant (no cooking is permitted on Yom Kippur anyway), but the concern for burial of the dead still applies with full force.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, what is the practical use of knowing the exact time of the molad (lunar conjunction)?
A. To determine which months are full (30 days) vs. short (29 days)
B. To calculate how long witnesses have to travel to the court
C. To refute witnesses who claim they saw the moon when astronomy shows it was impossible
D. To set the correct time for the musaf offering on Rosh Hashanah
Correct: C — Rav Ashi explains that knowledge of the molad is used to refute false witnesses: if they claim to have seen the moon but the calculation shows the molad occurred too late (after midday) for visibility, their testimony is rejected.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rava identifies a practical difference between Rabbi Yoḥanan's and Reish Lakish's derivations of the night-and-day rule. What is it?
A. Whether the court must convene before nightfall or after
B. Whether Rosh Hashanah may be sanctified by calculation alone
C. Whether a new moon appearing before midnight can sanctify the current day as New Moon
D. Whether witnesses may testify on the 29th or only the 30th
Correct: C — Rava says the difference involves hours before midnight. Rabbi Yoḥanan derives from 'from evening to evening' that the previous day ends at nightfall — so a new moon seen before midnight but after nightfall may affect whether that day can be sanctified as the New Moon.
