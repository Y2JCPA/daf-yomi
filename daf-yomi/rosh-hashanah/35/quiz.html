Rosh Hashanah 35 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak, who is it that concedes to Rabban Gamliel regarding the Rosh HaShana and Yom Kippur prayers?
A. Rabbi Yehuda
B. Rabbi Meir
C. Rabbi Shimon
D. Rabbi Akiva
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak resolves the apparent contradiction in Rabbi Yoḥanan's rulings: Rabbi Meir concedes to Rabban Gamliel specifically for Rosh HaShana and Yom Kippur blessings, because those prayers are exceptionally complex and most people cannot recite them independently.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the halakhic effect of reciting "And in Your Torah it is written, saying" (V'Toratcha Katuv Leimor), according to Rav Ḥananel citing Rav?
A. It substitutes for the entire Musaf Amida
B. It fulfills the shofar-blowing obligation
C. It is no longer necessary to recite the actual Biblical verses
D. It counts as one of the required ten verses per section
Correct: C — Rav Ḥananel cited Rav's ruling that once the prayer leader recites the introductory phrase "And in Your Torah it is written, saying," the actual Biblical verses of the malkhuyot, zikhronot, and shofarot sections no longer need to be recited — the introductory formula suffices.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How often did Rav Yehuda formally recite the Amida prayer, and why was this considered exceptional?
A. Once a week, because he fasted the other days
B. Once every three days, due to his intense Torah study schedule
C. Once every thirty days, spending the rest of the month in Torah study
D. Twice daily, skipping the afternoon prayer
Correct: C — The Gemara explains Rav Yehuda was unique: he prayed the formal Amida only once every thirty days. This resolved the challenge to Rabbi Elazar's rule — Rav Yehuda's extraordinary preparation was fitting because his prayers were so infrequent, each one was a singular event.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the baraita of Abba son of Rav Binyamin bar Ḥiyya, who is NOT included in the Priestly Blessing?
A. Latecomers who arrived after the priests began
B. Those standing behind the priests, not face-to-face
C. Women and children who stand in a separate section
D. Those who close their eyes during the blessing
Correct: B — The baraita explicitly teaches that those standing behind the priests — not positioned face-to-face in front of them — are not included in the Priestly Blessing. This ruling is used by the Gemara to probe the question of whether physical orientation and presence are required to benefit from the leader's prayer.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara ultimately explain Rabban Gamliel's ruling about those in the fields, according to Ravin citing Rabbi Ya'akov bar Idi?
A. Those in the fields are permanently exempt from Rosh HaShana prayers
B. The prayer leader may exempt them if they intended to hear and fulfill their obligation through him
C. They must recite an abbreviated form of the Amida on their own
D. They must travel to synagogue regardless of the circumstances
Correct: B — The final resolution clarifies that Rabban Gamliel permitted the prayer leader to exempt those in the fields who had the intention to fulfill their obligation by hearing the prayer leader. Their unavoidable absence is treated like an ones (beyond their control), and sincere intention substitutes for physical presence.
