עָרְלָה — שָׁלֹשׁ שָׁנִים אָרֵל — וּבַשָּׁנָה הָרְבִיעִית
📜 The Core Ruling If a tree was planted more than 30 days before Rosh HaShana, its three orla years are complete by that Rosh HaShana — yet the fruit remains forbidden until the 15th of Shevat (Tu B'Shevat) of the fourth year. The tree's new year is 15 Shevat, not Tishrei, so the prohibition lingers across the calendar boundary.

📜 Rabbi Yochanan's Derivation from the Vav

The Torah writes the orla law: "three years it shall be forbidden as orla" (Leviticus 19:23)
↓
Immediately following: "and in the fourth year all its fruit shall be holy" — the vav (ו) links the two laws
↓
This conjunction teaches: there are times a tree is already in its fourth year yet its fruit is still forbidden as orla
↓
This happens when Rosh HaShana passes (ending year 3) but the tree's new year — 15 Shevat — has not yet arrived
↓
Therefore orla continues past Tishrei and lasts until Tu B'Shevat of the fourth calendar year

Gemara Challenge
This baraita must not follow Rabbi Meir! Rabbi Meir holds that even one day in a year counts as a full year — so if a partial year suffices, why doesn't three partial years complete the orla count before Shevat?
Gemara Defense
Even according to Rabbi Meir, the principle applies only when the partial day or year falls at the beginning of the period — not at the end. Orla years end only when the full year closes.
Rava
Can't we argue the opposite via kal va'chomer? In the case of a niddah, the beginning of the day is not counted as a full day at the end of her seven-day period — yet we still require complete days!
Implied Response
The niddah logic cuts differently: partial days count at the start of a cycle, not the finish. The same asymmetry governs orla — entry is lenient, exit is strict.
Core Tension Does Rabbi Meir's 'one day = one year' rule apply at the END of the orla period, or only at the beginning?

🐂 Rabbi Meir on Calf Age

  • עֵגֶל (egel/calf) in Torah = animal in its 1st year
  • One day of a year counts as the full year
  • Principle: partial time at the START counts
  • Applied to bullock age calculations

🐂 Rabbi Elazar on Bullock Age

  • פַּר (par/bullock) = 24 months + 30 days old
  • Animal has entered its 3rd year
  • 30 extra days: one day of new year counts
  • Consistent with Rabbi Meir's beginning-of-year rule

🌱 Orla Year Counting

  • Three full orla years required
  • Year 4 = neta revai (holy fruit)
  • Tree's new year = 15 Shevat
  • Orla ends only at Tu B'Shevat — not Rosh HaShana
