Rosh Hashanah 10 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
If a tree is planted more than 30 days before Rosh HaShana, when does the fourth-year produce restriction finally end?
A. On the third Rosh HaShana after planting
B. On the 15th of Shevat following the third year
C. On the first day of Nisan of the fourth year
D. On the day the three full calendar years are completed
Correct: B — The orla period technically completes at the third Rosh HaShana, but fourth-year produce (נטע רבעי) restrictions extend until the 15th of Shevat — Tu BiShvat, the New Year for Trees — when all such fruit becomes fully permitted.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Rabbi Meir and Rabbi Elazar about orla counting?
A. Whether orla applies to grafted trees vs. planted trees
B. How many days must pass at the start of a year to count as a full halachic year
C. Whether Tu BiShvat is a Torah or rabbinic institution
D. Whether orla applies to trees outside the Land of Israel
Correct: B — Rabbi Meir holds one day at the start of a new year counts as a full year; Rabbi Elazar requires thirty days. Both derive their positions from the same flood verse in Genesis 8:13.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Gemara's final resolution, what does the '30 days' in the baraita actually represent?
A. Thirty days that count as a full year, per Rabbi Elazar
B. The minimum age of a tree before orla laws apply at all
C. The time needed for a planting to take root before year-counting begins
D. The duration of the fourth-year produce redemption period
Correct: C — The Gemara concludes the baraita follows Rabbi Meir throughout. The 30 days = rooting time (חזקת השרשה). Once rooted, even one day into the new year counts as a full year — consistent with Rabbi Meir's position.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Both Rabbi Meir and Rabbi Elazar derive their opposing rulings from the same verse. Which verse is it?
A. The orla verse in Leviticus 19:23
B. The Jubilee counting verse in Leviticus 25
C. The flood narrative — 'In the one and six hundredth year' (Genesis 8:13)
D. The first-fruits offering verse in Deuteronomy 26
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan noted the remarkable fact that both sages expounded the same verse: "And it came to pass in the one and six hundredth year" (Genesis 8:13). Rabbi Meir reads 'year' as referring to year 601; Rabbi Elazar reads it as referring to the final 30 days of year 600.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Eliezer, which event did NOT occur in Tishrei?
A. The world was created
B. The Patriarchs were born
C. Isaac was born
D. Sarah, Rachel, and Hannah were granted children
Correct: C — Both Rabbi Eliezer and Rabbi Yehoshua agree that Isaac was born on Passover (15 Nisan). Rabbi Eliezer holds the world, the Patriarchs' births and deaths, and many salvific events all occurred in Tishrei — but Isaac's birth on Passover is a point of consensus.
