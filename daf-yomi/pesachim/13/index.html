⚖️ Whose Opinion Governs Chametz on Erev Pesach?

Rabbi Meir
Two periods only: permitted, then immediately forbidden. Burn at the start of the sixth hour — no intermediate suspension zone.
Rabbi Yehuda
Three periods: eat during the 4th hour, suspend (neither eat nor burn) during the 5th, then burn at the start of the 6th.
Rabban Gamliel
Three periods: eat during the 4th, feed to your animals during the 5th (you yourself may not eat), burn at the start of the 6th.
Rav Naḥman citing Rav
The halakha is in accordance with Rabbi Yehuda — confirmed also by Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's own practical ruling.
Why Not Rabban Gamliel? Rava challenges: isn't Rabban Gamliel the decisor in this dispute? Rav answers: Rabbi Yehuda's opinion is supported by a specific baraita dealing with the 14th of Nisan falling on Shabbat, making his ruling particularly well-grounded.

🕐 The Three Periods of Erev Pesach (Rabbi Yehuda — Halakha)

4th Hour (Morning)
Fully permitted to eat chametz
↓
5th Hour (Mid-Morning)
Suspended — do not eat; do not yet burn
↓
Start of 6th Hour (Noon)
Burn all remaining chametz immediately
↓
Rav's Practical Ruling
Sell deposited chametz to gentiles during the 5th hour — do not eat it yourself (suspicion)
Rabban Gamliel Adds
During the 5th hour, feeding to your own animals is still permitted even though you may not eat

🔥 Burning Teruma on the Shabbat Eve Before Pesach

The Sages
Do not burn pure teruma — perhaps someone permitted to eat it will appear on Shabbat, and you would have violated a Torah prohibition by burning it unnecessarily. Set it aside separately.
Rabban Gamliel
By that logic, do not even burn doubtful teruma — perhaps Elijah will come on Shabbat and declare it pure retroactively!
The Sages (in response)
Elijah will not come on Shabbat — that possibility is too remote to act upon. Your parallel does not hold.
Final Vote of the Sages
They voted and established halakha with Rabbi Elazar ben Yehuda of Bartota in the name of Rabbi Yehoshua: burn pure and impure teruma together.
Context When the 14th of Nisan falls on Shabbat, burning chametz (and teruma mixed with chametz) raises the question of whether one must separate pure teruma first or burn everything together.

🏛️ The Two Loaves as Public Indicators on the Temple Mount

The Signal System

  • Two disqualified thanks-offering loaves placed on the Temple Mount pillars
  • Both loaves displayed → entire nation may eat chametz freely
  • One loaf removed → suspend eating chametz (5th hour begun)
  • Both loaves removed → burn all remaining chametz (6th hour begun)

The Colonnade (אסתוונית / Istevanit)

  • Rabbi Yehuda: the Temple Mount had a double colonnade
  • A colonnade within a colonnade — visible from afar
  • Raḥava citing Rabbi Yehuda confirms this in both baraita and teaching
  • Loaves on these high pillars functioned as a city-wide clock

Why Were the Loaves 'Disqualified'?

  • Thanks-offerings cannot be brought on Pesach — they contain chametz loaves
  • Pilgrims therefore brought them on the 13th of Nisan instead
  • So many were brought at once, the loaves became leftover (נותר)
  • Rabbi Yannai: they were not left overnight — they lacked a slaughtered animal to go with them (see amud B)

🧩 Why Can't the Thanks-Offering Loaves Be Redeemed? (Amud B)

Thanks-offering animal is slaughtered
Owner said: 'This animal for this offering'
↓
Blood spills before the priest can sprinkle it
The offering cannot be completed
↓
Can we bring another animal?
No — Owner dedicated this specific animal; no substitute is possible
Can we redeem the loaves?
No — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi: slaughter alone permanently consecrates the loaves
↓
Result: Loaves are permanently consecrated but can never be eaten — they must be burned on Erev Pesach as chametz

💰 Charity Collectors and the Rule Against Self-Dealing

והייתם נקיים מה׳ ומישראל (במדבר לב:כב)
והייתם נקיים A baraita teaches: Money collectors for charity who have no poor to give to may exchange their coins — but not with themselves. Food collectors who have no poor to feed may sell the food to others — but not to themselves.

Why? The verse states: "And you shall be clean before the Lord and before Israel" (Numbers 32:22) — one who handles communal property must be above all suspicion.

Application on this daf: Rav Yosef rules that when Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi told his attendant Yoḥanan Ḥakuka'a to deal with deposited chametz, he meant sell it to gentiles — not eat it himself, even though technically permitted, to avoid any appearance of impropriety.
