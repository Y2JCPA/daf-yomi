🪡 What Liquid Rendered the Meat Susceptible to Impurity?

Gemara
A baraita teaches: an impure needle found in an animal — the meat is impure. But which liquid first rendered this sacred meat susceptible to impurity?
Suggestion 1
The blood of slaughter rendered it susceptible to impurity!
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Impossible — blood of consecrated offerings does not render food susceptible to impurity by Torah law.
Suggestion 2
The water kept near the altar (slaughterhouse water) rendered it susceptible!
Rabbi Yosei b. Rabbi Ḥanina
No — that water was brought with the owner's consent and therefore does not render food susceptible either.
Rav Yehuda / Shmuel
The meat was cut from the animal while it was still alive — ordinary liquids rendered it susceptible before it became consecrated meat. Alternatively: chibbat hakodesh — esteem for sacred objects — is the source of susceptibility.
Key Dispute Reish Lakish asks: does chibbat hakodesh apply to a mass of meal-offering such that we count first- and second-degree impurity from contact with it? The Gemara leaves this unresolved (תיקו).

✨ Chibbat HaKodesh — Esteem for Sacred Objects

חיבת הקודש מכשרתן — Pesachim 20a
חיבת הקודש מכשרתן By rabbinic decree, certain sacred items that cannot become impure by Torah law are nonetheless deemed susceptible to impurity — due to their extreme holiness and importance. The reasoning: Owners of such objects will inevitably consult a Sage if they suspect impurity, ensuring halakha is honored. Even a vessel placed on the ground (which cannot answer questions) is treated this way. The concern for sacred honor creates a special category of susceptibility. Reish Lakish's dilemma: Does chibbat hakodesh cause a meal-offering mass itself to become a source from which first- and second-degree impurity are counted? Left unresolved.

🔥 May One Burn Pure Teruma Together with Impure Teruma?

Rabbi Yehoshua

  • Both definite-impure teruma and teruma in abeyance (ספק טמאה) may be burned together
  • On Erev Pesach that falls on Shabbat: burn them both together
  • Burning is not the same as actively rendering impure — it is a passive act

Rabbi Eliezer

  • Pure teruma and impure teruma must be burned separately
  • One may not mix statuses — each is burned on its own
  • Passive causation of impurity is permitted; active rendering impure is prohibited

Rav Ḥisda's Resolution

  • Rabbi Yehoshua's lenient ruling follows Rabbi Shimon
  • Rabbi Yosei: Even according to Rabbi Shimon, burning together is permitted because burning itself is only passive contact
  • The mishna aligns with Rabbi Yosei's reading of Rabbi Shimon's position

🪣 Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel — What to Do with Impure Teruma Wine

Beit Shammai
Impure teruma wine must drip away (be lost entirely). Do not actively render it impure — the risk of stumbling block is too great.
Beit Hillel
It may be poured out all at once. We do not fear a stumbling block in every case.
Rabbi Yishmael b. Rabbi Yosei
I will decide! In a field: pour it at once (lest impurity be forgotten on the way home). In a house: let it drip (the stumbling block is less likely since one remains aware).
Rava
Even burning pure and impure teruma separately involves loss of wood for a second fire — why is that permitted if financial loss is our standard?
Abaye
The Sages were concerned about great financial loss (non-sacred wine being ruined), but they were not concerned about minimal loss (a little extra wood). The two cases are distinct.
Oil vs. Wine Impure teruma oil: placed in a repulsive vessel — no one will drink it. Impure teruma wine: Shmuel taught that wine is used both for drinking AND for sprinkling (a valid use); thus there is a real risk of accidental consumption, requiring different handling.
