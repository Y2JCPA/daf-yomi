🎉 How Is Simchat Yom Tov Fulfilled?

Rabbi Yehuda
Each member of the household should rejoice with what pleases them: men with wine, women with colorful garments, children with nuts and roasted grains.
R' Yehuda ben Beteira
When the Temple stands, rejoicing is fulfilled only through eating sacrificial peace-offerings (שלמים). Once the Temple is destroyed, it is fulfilled through wine.
📜 Source "And you shall rejoice on your Festival" (Devarim 16:14) — the Torah obligates simcha for all members of the household on Yom Tov.

🥜 Rabbi Akiva's Passover Eve Customs

Distribute nuts and roasted grains to children on the eve of Passover
↓
Purpose: children remain awake until the Seder and are moved to ask the Four Questions (מה נשתנה)
↓
Rabbi Akiva similarly never dismissed his students from the study hall — he would teach as long as they wished to listen
↓
Exception: Passover eve, Yom Kippur eve, and Shabbat eve — he would send them home early to prepare
↓
Both customs reflect his commitment to Torah study and proper mitzva fulfillment

👹 Four Cups and the Danger of Pairs (זוגות)

The Problem
The Sages instituted four cups of wine — but drinking an even number (pairs / זוגות) normally exposes one to harmful spirits! How could they enact this?
Rav Naḥman
Scripture calls it "a night of watching" (ליל שימורים) — Exodus 12:42. Passover night is divinely guarded from all demons and harmful spirits. There is no cause for concern.
Rava
The cup of blessing for Grace after Meals on Passover serves an additional mitzva — it is not merely an expression of freedom. Therefore it combines for the good and neutralizes the danger of pairs.
🌙 Key Term ליל שימורים — A Night of Watching: God's protection over Israel on this night removes the ordinary dangers associated with even-numbered cups of wine.
