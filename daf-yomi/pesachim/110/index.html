🍷 The Danger of Pairs (Zugot)

זוגות — שנים שנים
Core Principle Drinking an even number of cups (zugot/זוגות) exposes one to demonic danger. The Gemara records a multi-generational debate over which even numbers carry biblical protection, with each Amora citing a word from the Priestly Benediction (Numbers 6:24–26) that neutralizes the danger at progressively lower counts. A separate concern — witchcraft (kishuf) — applies independently and is not neutralized by these thresholds.

👿 Two Conflicting Reports from Yosef the Demon

Rav Yosef
Yosef the Demon told me: Ashmedai, king of demons, is appointed over all who perform actions in pairs — but a king is not called a mere harmful spirit. A king would not stoop to cause arbitrary harm. Therefore the danger from pairs is limited.
Rav Pappa
Yosef the Demon told me: If one drinks two cups, we demons kill him. If he drinks four cups, we do not kill him — but we harm him. Another difference: harm from two cups is deliberate; harm from four is only incidental.
Resolution Rav Yosef's report (a king won't harm capriciously) explains why four cups — protected by veyishmerekha — yields only incidental harm. Two cups lack any protection and remain mortally dangerous. Rava demonstrated this in practice by dismissing his students after four cups without concern.

🛡️ What To Do If Caught Drinking in Pairs

Drank an even number of cups — about to go outside?
↓
Still at home
Drink one more cup immediately to make the total odd — danger eliminated
Already outside — forgot
Take right thumb in left hand + left thumb in right hand and say: "You [demon] and I are three!"
↓
If demon responds "You and I are two!" — persist: "You and I are three!" until it falls silent
↓
Demon falls silent — danger neutralized. Safe to proceed.

🧙 Demonic Danger vs. Witchcraft — Two Separate Concerns

Demonic Danger (Mazzikin)

  • Triggered by drinking even numbers of cups
  • Safe thresholds exist: 4, 6, 8, 10 cups are protected
  • Ashmedai (king) governs — a king won't arbitrarily harm
  • Remedy: thumb-grip incantation ('You and I are three!')
  • Drinking at a store or changing mind mid-count protects
  • Plates and loaves do NOT count toward pairs
  • In Eretz Yisrael: less concern for pairs generally

Witchcraft (Kishuf)

  • A separate danger — independent of pair-counting rules
  • Safe thresholds (4, 6, 8, 10) do NOT apply — all even numbers dangerous
  • Story: ex-wife's witchcraft made ex-husband lose count at 16 cups
  • Ameimar's incantation targets witches: 'Hot feces in torn date baskets…'
  • Babylonia more stringent than Eretz Yisrael on witchcraft
  • Even symbols written on barrels — Rav Dimi avoided pairs of signs
  • Eggs, nuts, cucumbers: halakha from Sinai that pairs are dangerous

🍽️ What Combines Toward the Pairs Count?

Cups of wine: Each cup of wine counts toward the pairs total — the primary subject of the entire sugya
↓
Diluted drinks (Rav Yehuda / Shmuel): Any diluted drink also combines with other cups to form an even or odd total
↓
Isparegus — spiced wine (Rav Ḥinnana bar Rav Yehoshua): Combines for the good (raises total to odd) but NOT for the bad — does not create a dangerous even pair
↓
Plates and loaves (Rav Avira): Do NOT count toward pairs. Rule: anything whose production was completed by people — vessel or food — does not combine
↓
Cups before vs. during a meal (Rav Naḥman / Rav Mesharshiya): Two cups before + one during = safe; one before + one during = dangerous pair. Rav Mesharshiya: the person's count matters, not the table's presence
↓
Doubt about count (Ravina / Rava): One opinion — be stringent, drink another cup. Other opinion — be lenient, do not add. Mnemonic from laws of ritual purity: smaller piece stricter than larger.
