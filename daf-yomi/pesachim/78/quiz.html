Pesachim 78 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the mishna on 78b, what should one do if the meat of the Paschal lamb became impure but the fat remained pure?
A. Sprinkle the blood, since the fat can still be burned on the altar
B. Do not sprinkle the blood
C. Sprinkle the blood, but the owners must bring a Second Pesaḥ
D. Burn only the fat and the owners are exempt from all obligation
Correct: B — The mishna rules that if the meat is impure, one should NOT sprinkle the blood ab initio — the owners cannot fulfill the obligation to eat the Paschal lamb.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rav Giddel cites Rav as ruling that if one sprinkled the blood despite impure meat, the offering was accepted. In accordance with whose view is this ruling?
A. The Rabbis, who hold eating is essential
B. Rabbi Eliezer, who holds the frontplate appeases for impure portions
C. Rabbi Natan, who holds eating is not essential to the Paschal lamb
D. Rabbi Yehoshua, who holds blood is accepted without meat
Correct: C — Rav's lenient ruling after the fact is in accordance with Rabbi Natan, who holds that eating is not essential (אכילה אינה מעכבת) — therefore even if the meat was impure, the blood-sprinkling fulfilled the obligation.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara ultimately resolve Rabbi Yosei's apparently contradictory agreement with both Rabbi Eliezer and Rabbi Yehoshua?
A. Rabbi Yosei only agreed with Rabbi Eliezer; his agreement with Yehoshua was hypothetical
B. He agreed with Eliezer after the fact (impure meat case) and with Yehoshua ab initio (lost/burned meat case)
C. He distinguished between animal offerings (Eliezer) and meal-offerings (Yehoshua) exclusively
D. He held ritual impurity is always permitted for public offerings, resolving all conflicts
Correct: B — The final resolution: Rabbi Yosei agrees with Rabbi Eliezer regarding a case where meat became impure (bedieved, the offering is accepted) and with Rabbi Yehoshua regarding a case where meat was lost or burned (ab initio, the offering is invalid). These are different scenarios, not contradictory rulings.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rav Pappa described Rabbi Yosei as 'like a document that awards something to two conflicting parties.' What did Abaye say in response?
A. He agreed — Rabbi Yosei indeed issued contradictory rulings without realizing it
B. Rabbi Yosei said what was reasonable in each context, not a ruling favoring both sides
C. Rabbi Yosei only made these statements hypothetically, never as binding law
D. The apparent contradiction is resolved by distinguishing Yom Kippur from Pesaḥ
Correct: B — Abaye responded that Rabbi Yosei did not intend to rule in favor of both — he said what was reasonable in each distinct context of study, whether discussing animal offerings or meal-offerings.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If one slaughtered the Paschal lamb with intent for those who can eat, but then sprinkled the blood with intent for those who cannot eat, what is the ruling according to the baraita in 78b?
A. The offering is valid — the slaughter's intent is determinative
B. The offering is valid if a majority of the registered group can eat
C. The offering is disqualified
D. The owners must bring a Second Pesaḥ but the offering itself is not piggul
Correct: C — The baraita rules the offering is disqualified. Improper intent during the sprinkling of the blood — here, directing it toward those incapable of eating — invalidates the Paschal lamb.
