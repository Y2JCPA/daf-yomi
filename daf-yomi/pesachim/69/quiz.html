QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does sprinkling purifying water not override Shabbat, even according to Rabbi Eliezer?
A. Rabbi Eliezer never addressed this case
B. Sprinkling merely moves liquid and is not a substantial act; it cannot override the Torah prohibition of carrying in the public domain
C. Sprinkling can simply be done after Shabbat with no loss
D. Rabbi Eliezer considers sprinkling a purely rabbinic obligation
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that sprinkling involves only moving liquid from a vessel to a person — it is not a significant act, and therefore cannot override the serious Torah prohibition of carrying in the public domain.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rava classify all babies with respect to circumcision on Shabbat?
A. All babies are healthy and water may not be heated for them
B. Only visibly sick babies may have water heated
C. All babies are considered weak regarding circumcision — heating water is permitted for all
D. The ruling depends entirely on the individual baby's condition
Correct: C — Rava holds that every baby requires hot water for circumcision and is therefore considered weak in this regard. Heating water on Shabbat is permitted for all circumcisions.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Under what two conditions is the Festival peace-offering brought with the Paschal lamb on the 14th of Nisan?
A. When Passover falls on Shabbat and the group has more than ten members
B. When Passover eve falls on a weekday and the Paschal lamb's meat is insufficient for the group
C. Always, regardless of the day or the size of the group
D. Only when the group has fewer than five members
Correct: B — The chagigah of the 14th is brought only when (1) Passover eve is a weekday, not Shabbat, and (2) the Paschal lamb alone cannot satisfy all members of the registered group.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rava's final ruling on slaughtering the Paschal lamb for someone impure from a creeping animal?
A. It may not be slaughtered for them since they cannot currently eat it
B. It may be slaughtered for them — Rabbi Eliezer permits it
C. It depends entirely on whether the majority of the community is also impure
D. Only the sprinkling of the blood is permitted, not the slaughter
Correct: B — After rejecting an earlier position, Rava concludes that Rabbi Eliezer permits slaughtering the Paschal lamb for someone impure from a creeping animal, just as he permits it for an uncircumcised person.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Rav need to rule that the halakha follows Rabbi Akiva in both the Paschal lamb case and the circumcision case separately?
A. Because different rabbis dispute each case
B. Because without both rulings, one might limit the principle to one case alone — each case has a unique rationale that could exclude it from the other
C. Because circumcision overrides Shabbat but the Paschal lamb does not
D. Because Rabbi Eliezer conceded on circumcision but not on the Paschal lamb
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that had Rav ruled only on one, we might have said the other operates by a different logic and is not included. Both rulings are required to establish Rabbi Akiva's principle broadly across both cases.
