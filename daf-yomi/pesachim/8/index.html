🕯️ The Right Light for Bedikat Chametz

Which light source is valid for bedikat chametz?
↓
❌ Sunlight
Cannot illuminate holes and crevices; Rava: a courtyard does not need searching anyway
❌ Torch
Too large to insert into holes and crevices — cannot reach hidden chametz
✅ Lamp (candle)
Small enough for holes; alluded to: "The soul of man is the lamp of God" (Prov. 20:27)

🕯️ Why Not a Torch? The Gemara's Investigation

Gemara
Isn't a torch brighter than a candle? Why shouldn't a torch be valid for searching?
Rava
A torch IS bright — "brightness appears as the light; He has rays at His side" (Hab. 3:4) — this refers to Torah scholars whose brilliance illuminates the world!
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
The disqualification is not about brightness. A small lamp can be inserted into holes and crevices — a torch, with its large flame, simply cannot fit. That is the entire reason.
Key Ruling The lamp's advantage is its small size, not its luminosity. Chametz hides in crevices; only a candle can reach it.

🏠 Which Storage Rooms Require Bedika?

✅ Requires Searching

  • Wine storages — people bring bread in during meals (Eretz Yisrael)
  • Beer storages in Babylonia — treated like wine storages (Rabbi Ḥiyya)
  • Salt storages — people eat there during meals (Rabba bar Rav Huna)
  • Candle storages — same concern (Rabba bar Rav Huna)
  • Wood storages — people may eat nearby (Rav Pappa)
  • Date storages — same reasoning (Rav Pappa)

❌ Does NOT Require Searching

  • Oil storages — fixed quantity measured before meals; one does not return for more
  • Large fish storages — no bread is typically brought in (Rav Ḥisda)
  • Any place where chametz is not typically brought

🦂 Must One Reach into Dangerous Holes?

Tosefta: Do not put your hand into holes and crevices — due to danger
↓
The Gemara asks: What danger exactly?
Scorpion?
But if no chametz fell in, no search needed regardless. This can't be the reason.
Chametz fell in?
But chametz under a rockslide is already nullified (like ownerless property). Why search?
✅ Gentile Neighbor (Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak)
The Tosefta follows Pelimu: a shared wall hole risks the gentile suspecting sorcery — real physical danger
↓
But doesn't mitzva-protection apply? → Where danger is commonplace, one may not rely on miracles

🛡️ The Mitzva Protection Principle — Rabbi Elazar

Rabbi Elazar's Rule: Those on the path to perform a mitzva are not susceptible to harm — neither going nor returning
↓
Source (Isi ben Yehuda): "No man shall covet your land when you go up" (Ex. 34:24) — animals left unguarded during aliyah la'regel are divinely protected
↓
A fortiori argument: If animals, normally vulnerable to predators, are protected — certainly people, who can defend themselves, are protected
↓
Return trip included: Derived from the full arc of "going up" — the mitzva journey encompasses both departure and homecoming
↓
Critical exception: Where danger is commonplace (מצוי) — such as a known hostile neighbor — one may NOT rely on miraculous mitzva protection
