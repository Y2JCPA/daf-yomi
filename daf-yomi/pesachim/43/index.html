⚖️ Who Authored the Mishna? Two Competing Opinions

Rav Yehuda (→ Rabbi Meir)
The mishna follows Rabbi Meir: hardened sourdough leaven is full-fledged chametz — one who eats it is flogged. Leaven-in-mixture is prohibited a fortiori.
Rav Naḥman (→ Rabbi Eliezer)
The mishna follows Rabbi Eliezer: eating full chametz = karet; eating a chametz mixture = lashes only. Hardened leaven may not have been addressed by Rabbi Eliezer at all.
The Core Question Which Tanna holds that BOTH hardened leaven AND leaven-in-mixture fall under the prohibition with lashes — but not karet?

Poor Women

⚖️ Rav Yehuda vs. Rav Naḥman: Who Authored the Mishna?

Rav Yehuda citing Rav
Rabbi Meir: Baraita says 'leavening dough must be burned… one who eats it is flogged.' Hardened sourdough = full chametz. Therefore leaven-in-mixture is prohibited a fortiori — the mishna lists both.
Rav Naḥman
Rabbi Eliezer: Full chametz = karet; chametz-in-mixture = lashes. The mishna lists substances with lashes but no karet. Rabbi Eliezer may simply not have ruled on hardened leaven.
Why Not the Other's View? Each Tanna may have ruled only on the specific case before him — Rabbi Meir on sourdough, Rabbi Eliezer on mixtures. Neither necessarily contradicts the other's silence.

👩 Deriving Women's Equal Chametz Obligation — Step by Step

Problem: Matza obligation is time-bound — women are generally exempt from time-bound positive mitzvot, so perhaps they're also exempt from the chametz prohibition?
↓
Verse (Exodus 12:19): 'For whosoever [ki kol] eats leavened bread, that soul shall be cut off from Israel'
↓
The inclusive term kol expands the karet penalty to include women equally
↓
Reasoning: Since women share the karet penalty, they must also be equally included in the prohibition against eating chametz
↓
Bonus consequence: Because women are now obligated in the chametz prohibition, they are also obligated in eating matza — even though it's a time-bound positive mitzva!

🔗 Ze'eiri and Rabbi Abbahu: Do Substances Combine?

כָּל שְׂאֹר וְכָל דְּבַשׁ לֹא תַקְטִירוּ מִמֶּנּוּ אִשֶּׁה לַה׳ (ויקרא ב:יא)
A Dispute About Mixtures Rabbi Abbahu (Rabbi Yoḥanan): Generally, for all Torah prohibitions, a permitted substance does not join together with a prohibited one to create liability.

Ze'eiri: Exception! Permitted and prohibited do combine for the prohibition of offering leaven on the altar (Leviticus 2:11).

Ze'eiri follows Rabbi Eliezer, who derives from kol that any mixture containing a prohibited substance creates full liability. The Gemara notes this matter ends תיקו — unresolved.
