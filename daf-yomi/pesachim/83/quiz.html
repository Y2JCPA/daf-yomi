Pesachim 83 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ya'akov, to which Paschal lambs does the prohibition of lo tishbor (do not break a bone) apply?
A. All Paschal lambs, even those disqualified before their time
B. Only a Paschal lamb that had a valid time (zman) at some point
C. Only Paschal lambs whose blood was properly sprinkled
D. Only the bones containing visible marrow
Correct: B — Rabbi Ya'akov teaches that lo tishbor applies only to a Paschal lamb that had a valid time — one disqualified before nightfall never entered the prohibition and its bones may be discarded without burning.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rav Mari bar Avuh taught that bones serving as a base for leftover render hands impure. What broader legal principle does this illustrate?
A. Bones are always treated more stringently than flesh
B. An item that is a base (basis) for leftover becomes legally significant like the leftover itself
C. Impurity can only be conveyed by flesh, not bones
D. Only Paschal lamb bones convey impurity — not other offerings
Correct: B — The ruling that basis bones render hands impure reflects the principle that a base for a forbidden/impure item takes on the status of that item — which is why the mishna requires burning them.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Yehuda citing Rav, which sinews are NOT considered like meat and therefore cannot be eaten as part of the Paschal lamb?
A. The sinews of the hind legs
B. The sinews of the neck
C. The fat-sinews around the sciatic nerve
D. The sinews of the shoulder
Correct: B — Rav said all sinews are like meat — except the neck sinews, which are so tough they are not considered edible. All other sinews may be eaten and one may register for the Paschal lamb via them.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the burning of Paschal lamb leftover NOT override Shabbat, according to Rava?
A. The verse 'burn in the morning' implies the next available morning
B. The Shabbat burnt-offering teaches only designated Shabbat rituals override Shabbat
C. Only food preparation overrides Yom Tov — and Shabbat is excluded from that dispensation entirely
D. Rabbi Ya'akov's ruling limits lo tishbor and similarly limits burning
Correct: C — Rava derives from Exodus 12:16 that only eating-related labor overrides Yom Tov — and Shabbat is not included in this dispensation at all. Burning leftover therefore cannot override Shabbat.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Ravina's resolution (citing Shmuel), which component of the sciatic nerve region is forbidden only by rabbinic custom, and therefore requires burning on the 16th as a stringency?
A. The inner sinew directly touching the hipbone
B. The entire right-thigh nerve complex
C. The fat surrounding the outer sciatic nerve
D. The gid itself when found mixed with permitted sinews
Correct: C — Ravina explains that the inner nerve (touching the bone) is Torah-forbidden, but the outer nerve is Torah-permitted. The fat of the outer nerve, however, is rabbinically forbidden by custom — and this is what the mishna rules must be burned on the 16th.
