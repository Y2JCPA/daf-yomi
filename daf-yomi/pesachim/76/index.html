🔥 The Great Absorption Debate

Rav
The upper food prevails — hot food on top heats the bottom food and causes absorption of forbidden flavor into the permitted food.
Shmuel
The lower food prevails — cold food on the bottom cools what falls onto it, preventing absorption. But if the lower food is hot, it dominates.
Stakes of the Debate This dispute resolves multiple Mishna cases: gravy dripping onto cold earthenware, gravy dripping into flour, and oil smeared on Paschal lamb meat — each requires a different explanation depending on which position you hold.

💨 Does Aroma (ריחא) Forbid Food?

Rav
Aroma is significant — ריחא מילתא היא
Fatty kosher meat roasted in the same oven as lean non-kosher meat is forbidden. The fat causes the kosher meat to absorb the non-kosher aroma even without contact.
Levi
Aroma is not significant — ריחא לאו מילתא היא
Even lean kosher meat roasted with fatty non-kosher meat is permitted. Aroma has no real substance and cannot transfer forbidden status.
Tannaitic Parallel (Rav Mari) Rabbi Meir vs. Rabbi Yehuda dispute whether bread placed on a barrel of teruma wine acquires forbidden status through aroma. Rabbi Meir forbids it; Rabbi Yehuda permits. Rav aligns with Rabbi Meir; Levi with Rabbi Yehuda.

🐦 The Bird That Fell Into Kutah

תחתון גובר — הקור מצנן
Case Study — Meat & Milk A raw young bird fell into kutaḥ — a condiment containing milk. Was it a forbidden meat-milk mixture?

Rav Ḥinnana bar Rava of Pashronya ruled: Permitted — rinse it off. Since the bird was raw and cold, it could not have absorbed the milk from the cold kutaḥ.

Rava praised the ruling: "Who is wise enough to permit something as complicated as this, if not Rav Ḥinnana? He recognized the leniency even where it appeared prohibited."

⚠️ Important caveat: This applies only if the bird was raw. If it was roasted, the outer peel must be removed — roasting softens the meat and allows flavor absorption.

🏛️ Mishna: Five Communal Offerings Override Ritual Impurity

Rule: These five communal offerings may be brought even when the community is ritually impure — but they may not be eaten in a state of impurity.
↓
1. The Omer — barley offering brought on 16 Nisan
↓
2. The Two Loaves (שתי הלחם) — wheat loaves brought on Shavuot
↓
3. The Showbread (לחם הפנים) — twelve loaves replaced each Shabbat
↓
4. Communal Peace-Offerings (שלמי ציבור) — includes the two lambs of Shavuot and Festival goat sin-offerings
↓
5. New Moon Goats (שעירי ראשי חדשים) — musaf sin-offerings of Rosh Chodesh
↓
⚠️ Excluded: The Festival peace-offering of 15 Nisan — though time-bound and communal in character, each individual brings it personally, so it does NOT override ritual impurity.
