QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, when hot Paschal lamb gravy drips onto cold earthenware, what is the ruling and why?
A. The earthenware is fully forbidden because upper heat always prevails
B. The gravy is cooled by the cold earthenware, preventing absorption — one need only remove the affected spot
C. One must rinse the earthenware and it is fully permitted with no removal needed
D. The earthenware must be broken, as all absorbed flavors cannot be removed
Correct: B — Shmuel holds the lower food prevails — cold earthenware cools the hot gravy before absorption occurs. However, the Mishna still requires removing the affected spot; Shmuel explains this is because the earthenware was in fact warm from prior use, not truly cold.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rava qualifies Shmuel's rule that 'salted food is like boiling food.' When does this rule NOT apply?
A. When the food is meat rather than fish
B. When the salting was done before the food was cooked
C. When the food is still edible despite being salted
D. When the forbidden item is liquid rather than solid
Correct: C — Rava teaches that salting only equals boiling when the food is salted to the point of being inedible — i.e., no one would eat it due to the salt. If the food remains edible despite the salt, it does not carry the stringency of boiling water.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rav Ḥinnana bar Rava of Pashronya ruled a raw bird that fell into kutah is permitted. What changed if the bird had been roasted instead?
A. It would still be permitted since kutah is not dairy
B. It would require the outer peel to be removed before eating
C. It would be fully forbidden with no remedy
D. It would require waiting 24 hours before being permitted
Correct: B — Roasting softens the meat, making it much more susceptible to absorbing surrounding flavors. Therefore, if the bird had been roasted, it would have absorbed the milk flavor from the kutah, and removal of the outer peel would be required to render it permitted.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the dispute between Rav and Levi about aroma (reiḥa), what specific scenario does Rav use to demonstrate that aroma forbids food?
A. Cold meat falling into cold forbidden gravy in a pot
B. Fatty kosher meat roasted in the same oven as lean non-kosher meat
C. Bread placed next to non-kosher meat on the same table
D. Kosher fish stored in a barrel that previously held non-kosher fish
Correct: B — Rav holds that fatty kosher meat roasted together with lean non-kosher meat is forbidden, even without contact. The fatty kosher meat absorbs the non-kosher aroma wafting through the oven. This is because fat is more susceptible to absorbing aromas than lean meat.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The Mishna lists five communal offerings that override ritual impurity. Which offering is explicitly EXCLUDED, and why?
A. The Omer — because it is brought by an individual kohen, not the community
B. The Festival peace-offering of 15 Nisan — because each individual brings it personally, not as a communal act
C. The showbread — because it does not require a fixed time and can be replaced
D. The New Moon goats — because they are sin-offerings and impurity cannot override sin-offerings
Correct: B — The Festival peace-offering of 15 Nisan resembles communal offerings in being time-bound and associated with public celebration. However, the Gemara excludes it because each individual brings it as a personal obligation — it is not a true communal offering brought by the public as a whole, and therefore does not override ritual impurity.
