🗓️ When the 14th of Nisan Falls on Shabbat

Before Shabbat: Remove all leaven from one's possession — both teruma and non-sacred food
↓
Keep only enough for two Shabbat meals: Friday night dinner and Shabbat morning
↓
After the morning meal: Nullify remaining leaven in the heart — one cannot burn chametz on Shabbat
↓
Practical precedent: Rabbi Eliezer bar Tzadok reports that Zonin, Rabban Gamliel's appointee in Yavne, distributed exactly enough food for two Shabbat meals to guests, demonstrating this halakha in action
↓
Logic: Physical burning is forbidden on Shabbat, so pre-Shabbat removal combined with mental nullification (bitul) together fulfill the Torah obligation

💍 The Betrothal Feast — Mitzva or Not?

Rabbi Yehuda
I heard explicitly: the first betrothal feast itself is a mitzva — but the feast of sivlonot (gift-giving) is not. A traveler need not return for that second optional feast.
Rabbi Yosei
Even the second betrothal feast carries mitzva status — the mishna's ruling that a traveler may return applies to both the first and the second feast.
Rav Ḥisda
The dispute between Rabbi Yehuda and Rabbi Yosei concerns only the second betrothal feast. Both agree: the first betrothal feast is unquestionably a mitzva.
Rabbi Shimon
A Torah scholar may not derive benefit from any feast that is not a mitzva — doing so degrades him and harms his descendants' reputation.
Practical Ruling A Torah scholar who feasts excessively everywhere destroys his house, widows his wife, and orphans his children — and his descendants are remembered with disgrace (Abaye: called 'son of the oven-heater'; Rava: 'son of the wine-guzzler')

📚 Marriage Partners — Torah Scholar vs. Ignoramus (Am HaAretz)

Marry Daughter of Torah Scholar

  • Sell everything to achieve this match — highest priority
  • Like grapes of a vine attached to grapes — both partners flourish
  • If husband dies or is exiled, children are still raised in Torah environment
  • Rav Idi bar Avin married a kohen's daughter → sons Rav Sheshet and R. Yehoshua became great halakhic decisors
  • Rabbi Yochanan: one who clings to Aaron's descendants merits wealth — Torah and priesthood combined bring blessing

Marrying Into Ignoramus Family

  • Like grapes of a vine attached to thorns — harmful, unproductive union
  • Marrying daughter to an ignoramus: like binding her before a lion (Rabbi Meir)
  • Rabbi Yitzchak: one who benefits from optional feasts will ultimately be exiled
  • Rabbi Elazar: prohibited to travel with an ignoramus — danger of harm on the road
  • Six restrictions apply to ignoramuses: testimony, guardianship, secrets, lost objects, and appointment as witnesses are all forbidden
