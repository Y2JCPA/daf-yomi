Pesachim 49 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
When the 14th of Nisan falls on Shabbat, what must one do with leaven remaining after the Shabbat morning meal?
A. Burn it before Shabbat ends
B. Nullify it in his heart
C. Give it to a non-Jew immediately
D. Leave it and burn it after Shabbat
Correct: B — Since burning chametz is forbidden on Shabbat, the remaining leaven after the morning meal is handled through mental nullification (bitul) — declaring it ownerless in one's heart.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
A traveler on Erev Pesach is going to save Jews from a flooding river and remembers chametz at home. What is the halakha?
A. Return immediately — chametz removal is a Torah obligation
B. Nullify in his heart even if he could physically return
C. Send a messenger back to handle the chametz
D. The chametz is automatically nullified in emergency situations
Correct: B — The mishna rules that for life-saving missions, a traveler should NOT return even if he could — he nullifies the chametz in his heart and continues his mission.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rav Ḥisda's resolution of the contradiction between the mishna and the baraita regarding the betrothal feast?
A. The mishna is a later ruling that overrides the baraita
B. The dispute between Rabbi Yehuda and Rabbi Yosei concerns only the second betrothal feast; all agree the first is a mitzva
C. The betrothal feast is never considered a mitzva for purposes of returning
D. Only a Torah scholar's betrothal feast has mitzva status
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda clarifies that the mishna and baraita address different situations: the mishna's permissive ruling applies to the first feast (mitzva by all accounts), while the baraita's strict ruling applies to the second feast (disputed between Rabbi Yehuda and Rabbi Yosei).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Abaye's resolution of the apparent contradiction between Rabbi Meir and Rabbi Yehuda's positions on egg-bulk vs. olive-bulk across different contexts is:
A. One source must be textually emended to reverse the opinions
B. No reversal needed: zimmun is debated via verse interpretation while chametz/meat uses logical reasoning — each dispute is independent
C. Rabbi Yehuda changed his opinion between the two rulings
D. The two disputes involve completely different biblical commandments with no relation
Correct: B — Abaye holds no emendation is necessary: in zimmun, the dispute hinges on how to read a biblical verse; in chametz and consecrated meat, the dispute is about logical reasoning. Each sage applies their logic consistently within each context.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What metaphor does the Gemara use for a Torah scholar who marries the daughter of a Torah scholar?
A. A spring flowing into a great sea
B. Grapes of a vine attached to grapes of a vine
C. A cedar of Lebanon joining the great forest
D. A lamp kindled from another lamp
Correct: B — The Gemara describes this ideal match as 'grapes of a vine attached to grapes of a vine' — both flourish together. By contrast, a Torah scholar marrying into an ignoramus family is like grapes attached to thorns — harmful and unproductive.
