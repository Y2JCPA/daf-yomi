📜 Pesach Mitzrayim vs. Pesach Dorot — Side by Side

Pesach Mitzrayim

  • Lamb set aside from 10th of Nisan
  • Blood applied on lintel and doorposts
  • Must be eaten in haste (בְּחִפָּזוֹן)
  • Chametz prohibited for one night only
  • Doorposts served as three altars for portions
  • No permanent altar required

Pesach Dorot

  • No requirement to take from 10th
  • No doorpost blood application
  • No haste requirement
  • Chametz prohibited all seven days
  • 4-day examination derived from Exodus 13:5
  • Uncircumcised and apostates excluded

📅 Deriving the Four-Day Examination Requirement

Egypt Passover: 'Take it from the tenth day of this month' (Exodus 12:3) — lamb selected 4 days before the 14th
↓
Question: Does this 4-day rule apply to Paschal lambs of later generations? Or is it Egypt-only via 'this month'?
↓
Ben Bag Bag on the daily offering: The תָּמִיד requires 4-day examination — derived from verbal analogy (גְּזֵרָה שָׁוָה) on 'You shall safeguard it' (שָׁמַרְתֶּם)
↓
Later generations' Paschal lamb: Exodus 13:5 — 'When the Lord brings you into the land… you shall keep this service' — explicitly extends the law
↓
Conclusion: Later-generation Paschal lambs DO require 4-day examination — not from the Egypt verse, but from the verse about entering the Land

⚖️ Rava vs. Abaye: Timing of Substitution

Rava
Even when the original is found after the slaughter, the substitute is offered only if the substitution was made before Passover. Substitution performed after Passover — the substitute is NOT offered as a peace-offering.
Abaye
Objection from a baraita: 'If he brings a lamb' (Lev. 3:7) — the word 'if' (אִם) expands to include the substitute of a Paschal lamb after Passover, which must be brought as a peace-offering. This contradicts you!
Rava
That verse is needed for something else entirely — to teach that the fat tail (אַלְיָה) must be offered on the altar for a Paschal lamb. This requirement is not obvious from ordinary peace-offering laws.
Abaye
Then what does 'If he brings a lamb' teach? It includes a lamb consecrated as Paschal that became too old (past its first year) — must now be offered as a peace-offering. My source for the substitute stands elsewhere.
Key Principle The verse in Leviticus 3:7 serves multiple purposes: fat tail offering, aged Paschal lamb inclusion — but Rava holds that post-Passover substitution never generates an offering obligation.

🩸 Rabbi Zeira's Famous Question: Doorposts as Altars

מְזוּזָה וּשְׁתֵּי מְזוּזוֹת — The lintel (מַשְׁקוֹף) and two doorposts (מְזוּזוֹת) served as three altars in Egypt
Rabbi Zeira Asks The Torah does not describe an altar in Egypt for the first Passover — so where were the אֵמוּרִים (sacrificial fat portions) burned? They could not simply be discarded. Rav Yosef taught: The doorway structure served as three altars. The lintel stood in place of one altar; each of the two doorposts stood in place of an altar — three altars total. The portions were burned on these in lieu of a formal mizbeach.
