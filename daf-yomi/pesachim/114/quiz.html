Pesachim 114 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Beit Hillel, why does the blessing over wine precede the blessing over the day in kiddush?
A. Wine is inherently holier than any festival
B. The wine causes kiddush to be recited, making it the primary element
C. One should always bless tangible objects before abstract concepts
D. Beit Shammai originally held this position first
Correct: B — Beit Hillel reasons that without wine (or bread), kiddush cannot be recited at all — the wine is the instrument that enables kiddush, and therefore it receives the first blessing.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the primary purpose of the unusual first dipping of vegetables before the Passover meal?
A. To fulfill the mitzva of maror early in the seder
B. To demonstrate the leader's wealth and abundance
C. To arouse children's curiosity so they will ask questions
D. To taste food before the lengthy Hallel recitation
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that the odd pre-meal dipping is designed to prompt children to notice something unusual and ask questions — triggering the central narrative retelling of the Exodus.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What two cooked foods did Rav Huna prescribe for the seder, and how do we know Rava accepted this ruling?
A. Fish and egg — Rava refused to eat anything else
B. Meat and more meat — Rava bought two lambs each year
C. Beets and rice — Rava would specifically seek these out on Passover night
D. Onions and lentils — Rava cited this ruling publicly
Correct: C — Rav Huna ruled the two cooked foods are beets (silka) and rice. The Gemara notes that Rava would actively seek out beets and rice for his seder, demonstrating his deference to Rav Huna's ruling.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Reish Lakish attempt to derive from the mishna's first dipping, and how does the Gemara challenge it?
A. He proves kiddush must precede the meal; challenged by the Tosefta's ruling
B. He proves mitzvot require intent; challenged by suggesting the two dippings merely arouse children's curiosity
C. He proves rice is chametz; challenged by Rav Ashi's incidental ruling
D. He proves Beit Hillel is wrong; challenged by the Divine Voice
Correct: B — Reish Lakish argues the first dipping doesn't fulfill maror because there was no intent — proving mitzvot require kavanah. The Gemara counters: perhaps the reason is simply that two dippings are needed to prompt children's questions, with no implication about intent.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, what halakhic ruling can be learned incidentally from Rav Huna's statement about rice?
A. Rice must be checked for chametz before Pesach
B. No one rules like Rabbi Yoḥanan ben Nuri, who considered rice a grain-type chametz
C. Rava disagreed with Rav Huna on the seder foods
D. Cooked rice is not subject to the laws of biur chametz
Correct: B — Since Rav Huna permits rice as one of the seder's two cooked foods on Passover night, Rav Ashi deduces that no authority rules like Rabbi Yoḥanan ben Nuri, who held that rice is grain-like and would therefore be chametz on Pesach.
