🍷 Kiddush: Which Blessing Comes First?

Beit Shammai
Recite the blessing over the sanctification of the day first, then bless the wine — the holy day is the reason kiddush is recited at all.
Beit Hillel
Recite the blessing over the wine first, then the day — the wine causes kiddush to be recited, so wine is the primary element.
Beit Hillel
Alternatively: the wine is already present before kiddush begins, while the holiness of the day is already ongoing — what enables the act takes precedence.
Gemara
The Tosefta explicitly rules like Beit Hillel. If a Divine Voice already proclaimed the halakha always follows Beit Hillel, why restate it? Either it predates the Voice, or the author follows Rabbi Yehoshua who says we disregard a Divine Voice in halakha.
Why cite it at all? If the Divine Voice settled all Beit Shammai/Hillel disputes in favor of Hillel, the Tosefta's explicit ruling seems redundant. The Gemara resolves this with two alternative explanations about the relationship between rabbinic authority and heavenly proclamations.

🧅 Ancient Wisdom: Live Within Your Means

Rabbi Yehuda son of Rabbi Elai (via R. Yoḥanan)

  • 🧅 Eat an onion (batzal) and sit in the shade (batzel)
  • Do not eat expensive geese and chickens and fall into debt to pay for them
  • Simple food + comfort > luxury food + financial ruin

Ulla's Proverb from Eretz Yisrael

  • 🥩 Eat a fat tail (alita)? → Hide in the attic (aliyata) from creditors who think you're wealthy
  • 🥬 Eat vegetables (kara)? → Sit openly at the door (kara) with nothing to fear
  • Wordplay: the food you eat determines whether you hide or stand proud

🤔 Do Mitzvot Require Intent (Kavanah)?

Reish Lakish
Proof from the mishna: the first dipping of ḥazeret does not fulfill the mitzva of maror — proving that without intent, no obligation is discharged. Therefore mitzvot require kavanah.
Gemara (Rejection)
Not conclusive! Perhaps mitzvot don't need intent — the two dippings exist simply to arouse children's curiosity, not because the first one failed due to lack of kavanah.
Baraita
One who ate maror from demai (doubtfully tithed produce) fulfilled his obligation. But one who ate it without intent did not fulfill it.
Rabbi Yosei (Baraita)
Even though ḥazeret was already dipped once, it is a mitzva to bring ḥazeret and ḥaroset again for maror — implying the first dipping did not fulfill the obligation.
Conclusion Whether mitzvot require intent is an unresolved dispute among tanna'im. The mishna cannot definitively prove either position.

🍷 The First Cup and the Start of the Seder

קידוש היום — מקדש על הכוס של יין
Mishna — Pesachim 114a The attendants poured the first cup for the seder leader.

Beit Shammai: One recites the blessing over the sanctification of the day first, then blesses the wine.
Beit Hillel: One recites the blessing over the wine first, then the day.

Beit Hillel's logic: wine is the instrument that makes kiddush possible — without wine or bread, there is no kiddush. What enables the act receives the first blessing.
