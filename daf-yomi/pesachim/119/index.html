🎭 What is Afikoman? Three Opinions

Rav
Afikoman means: a member of a group that ate the Paschal lamb may not leave that group to join another group for further eating
Shmuel
Afikoman means: do not eat dessert after the Paschal lamb meal — like mushrooms (אוּרְדִּילָאֵי) for me, or chicks for Rav
R. Ḥanina bar Sheila & R. Yoḥanan
Afikoman means: do not bring in entertainments and diversions (אַפִּיקוֹמָן = Greek: after-dinner revelry) after the Seder meal
Shmuel's Ruling — Today Even without the Paschal lamb, one may not eat dessert after matza. The mishna's afikoman prohibition applies equally — and even more so — after matza. (The mishna uses a 'needless to say' style.)

🏺 Joseph's Three Hidden Treasures (Rabbi Ḥama bar Ḥanina)

Revealed to Korah

  • Immense wealth → arrogance → destruction
  • "Riches kept by owner to his hurt" (Eccl 5:12)
  • 300 mules needed just to carry the keys
  • Korah's wealth is the paradigm of dangerous pride

Revealed to Antoninos

  • Antoninos ben Asveiros, king of Rome
  • Granted to a righteous gentile ruler
  • Symbol of Torah's influence on the nations
  • Partial, external revelation of the treasure

Hidden for the Righteous

  • גָּנוּז לַצַּדִּיקִים לֶעָתִיד לָבוֹא
  • Not yet revealed to any person
  • Eschatological reward — messianic era
  • The ultimate destination of Joseph's wealth

📜 Concealing Secrets of the Ancient of Days

לִמְכַסֶּה עַתִּיק — זֶה הַמְּכַסֶּה דְּבָרִים שֶׁכִּסָּה עַתִּיק יוֹמִין
Who is Worthy of 'Stately Clothing'? (Isaiah 23:18) Rabbi Elazar interprets 'limekhaseh atik' as describing one who conceals (מְכַסֶּה) the matters that the Ancient of Days (עַתִּיק יוֹמִין) — God — Himself concealed. These are the hidden secrets of Torah. Such a person is also described as one who recognizes a colleague's place in the yeshiva or greets him there — marks of consistent, dedicated Torah presence.

The Gemara also notes: when God is 'conquered' (i.e., when Israel prevails in prayer), He rejoices — unlike a human king who is saddened by defeat. This is reflected in the psalm heading 'Lamenatzeaḥ' (To the conductor / To the One who is conquered).
