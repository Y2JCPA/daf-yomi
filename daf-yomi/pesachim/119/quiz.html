Pesachim 119 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Elazar, who is worthy of 'stately clothing' (limekhaseh atik)?
A. One who wears fine garments on Shabbat and Yom Tov
B. One who conceals matters that God Himself concealed — secrets of Torah
C. One who recites hallel with full kavvanah
D. One who distributes charity from hidden sources
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar interprets 'limekhaseh atik' as one who conceals (mekhaseh) the things that the Ancient of Days (atik yomin) concealed — the hidden secrets of Torah.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rav say is the meaning of 'afikoman'?
A. One may not eat dessert after the Paschal lamb
B. One may not leave his group to join another after eating the Paschal lamb
C. One must sing songs of hallel until midnight
D. One must drink a fourth cup of wine after eating
Correct: B — Rav holds that afikoman prohibits leaving one's group (חֶבוּרָה) after the Paschal lamb to join another group for additional eating.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Moses decline to recite the hallel blessing at the future eschatological Seder?
A. Because he is not of royal lineage
B. Because the wicked Esau came from him
C. Because he did not merit to enter Eretz Yisrael, in life or death
D. Because he never recited hallel during his lifetime in the desert
Correct: C — Moses says: 'I will not recite the blessing, as I did not merit to enter Eretz Yisrael, neither in my life nor in my death.' He then defers to Joshua, who also declines.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ḥama bar Ḥanina, to whom were two of Joseph's three hidden treasures revealed?
A. Pharaoh and King Solomon
B. Korah and Antoninos ben Asveiros, king of Rome
C. Nebuchadnezzar and Cyrus the Persian
D. Rehoboam and Shishak king of Egypt
Correct: B — Rabbi Ḥama bar Ḥanina states that one treasure was revealed to Korah (who grew arrogant), one to Antoninos ben Asveiros (king of Rome), and one remains hidden for the righteous in the future.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Shmuel rule regarding afikoman today, without the Paschal lamb?
A. The afikoman prohibition no longer applies without the Paschal lamb
B. One may eat dessert but may not leave one's group
C. One may not conclude after matza with afikoman — dessert is forbidden after matza too
D. One must eat a special afikoman of matza at the very end
Correct: C — Shmuel rules that even today, without the Paschal lamb, one may not eat dessert after matza. The Gemara explains the mishna uses a 'needless to say' style — matza is covered even more than the Paschal lamb.
