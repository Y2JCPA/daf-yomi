Pesachim 80 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
In the scenario where exactly half the community is pure and half impure, what does Rav propose?
A. Split the community — pure half brings in purity, impure half in impurity
B. Render one pure person impure with a creeping animal to create a majority
C. Send one pure person to a distant place so the remaining group is majority impure
D. Postpone the entire community to the Second Pesach
Correct: B — Rav says to render one of the pure people impure with a creeping animal (not a corpse), making the impure side the majority — then all bring the Paschal lamb together in a state of impurity.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject rendering someone impure with a corpse (rather than a creeping animal) to resolve the half-half split?
A. Corpse impurity cannot be contracted intentionally
B. Corpse impurity lasts seven days, preventing him from bringing his Festival peace-offering
C. Corpse impurity disqualifies a person from the Paschal lamb entirely
D. The Torah forbids deliberately contracting corpse impurity for this purpose
Correct: B — Corpse impurity lasts seven days, meaning the person would still be impure during the intermediate days of Passover and unable to bring his chagigah (Festival peace-offering) — an unacceptable consequence.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the fundamental dispute between Rav Huna and Rav Adda bar Ahava regarding a Paschal lamb brought in impurity?
A. Whether the tzitz can appease for intentional impurity
B. Whether the Second Pesach applies to communities or only individuals
C. Whether communal impurity is merely 'overridden' (dechuyah) or fully 'permitted' (hutrah)
D. Whether zavim count toward the majority for triggering the impurity override
Correct: C — Rav Huna holds impurity is merely 'overridden' (דחויה) — it remains an impure act, so no Second Pesach redress follows. Rav Adda holds it is fully 'permitted' (הותרה) — treated as if pure, enabling Second Pesach.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Mishna, when does the tzitz (frontplate) appease God for an impure Paschal lamb?
A. Only when the impurity was contracted completely unintentionally
B. Only for impurity of the deep that was unknown to everyone
C. When the blood was already sprinkled before the impurity became known
D. Only when the High Priest himself performs the sprinkling
Correct: C — The Mishna states the tzitz appeases when the blood was already sprinkled and only afterward did it become known the offering was impure — the Gemara clarifies this covers both witting and unwitting impurity.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What makes 'impurity of the deep' (טומאת התהום) uniquely lenient, and to what type of impurity does it apply?
A. It applies to all forms of impurity and grants full atonement without the tzitz
B. It applies only to corpse-impurity and refers to impurity unknown to any person in the world
C. It applies to zavim who were impure below the threshold of awareness
D. It applies to creeping animal impurity contracted in private with no witnesses
Correct: B — Tum'at ha'tehom is impurity from a source completely unknown to any person — not merely unknown to the individual. Rabbi Ḥiyya specifies it applies only to corpse-impurity, not to creeping animals or ziva discharge.
