QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, what does 'cleaning the intestines' of the Paschal lamb involve?
A. Turning the intestines inside-out to remove excrement
B. Puncturing the intestines with a knife to release excrement
C. Washing the intestines thoroughly with water
D. Removing the intestines from the animal entirely
Correct: B — Rav Huna says it means puncturing the intestines with a knife, allowing the excrement to exit. Rav Ḥiyya bar Rav disagrees, saying it means turning them inside-out — a dispute with implications for the nature of the prohibited labor being overridden.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rabbi Yehoshua's position on how to spend Yom Tov?
A. Spend the entire day in Torah study only
B. Spend the entire day eating and drinking only
C. Split the day — half for God (study), half for yourself (enjoyment)
D. Do whatever you wish; there is no specific obligation
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehoshua holds the day must be divided — half devoted to God through Torah study and half to personal enjoyment. Both components are obligatory, not merely optional choices.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How did Rav Yosef justify his special Shavuot celebration with a choice calf?
A. Because his teacher Rav Ḥisda commanded it
B. Because Shavuot is when the Paschal offering is brought
C. Because without the Torah given on this day, he would be an unremarkable person
D. Because the mitzva of rejoicing on Shavuot is explicitly written in the Torah
Correct: C — Rav Yosef said: 'If not for this day — on which the Torah was given — how many Yosefs are there in the marketplace?' The Torah elevated him, making it a day of profound personal gratitude and obligatory celebration.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve Ulla's contradiction between 'He will destroy death forever' (Is. 25:8) and 'the child shall die a hundred years old' (Is. 65:20)?
A. The first verse refers to the World to Come; the second to the Messianic Era
B. The first verse applies to Israel; the second applies to non-Jews
C. The second verse is purely metaphorical, not literal
D. The two verses describe different types of death: physical vs. spiritual
Correct: B — The resolution distinguishes the subjects: the promise of eternal life ('destroy death forever') applies to Israel, while the verse about dying at a hundred years applies to non-Jews, who will still face death even in the future era.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rava derives proof of resurrection from Deuteronomy 32:39. What is the logic?
A. The verse mentions both death and birth, proving both happen in the world
B. The same verse mentions 'I put to death' and 'I make live,' proving the same person who died will be revived
C. The verse commands belief in resurrection as a fundamental principle
D. The word 'heal' in the verse is a metaphor for resurrection of the nation
Correct: B — Rava argues: since the verse pairs 'I put to death' with 'I make live' and 'I wound' with 'I heal' in parallel, the same individual who was killed must be the one revived — one does not wound one person and heal another. This is a direct proof of personal resurrection.
