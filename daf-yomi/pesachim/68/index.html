🍖 Yom Tov: "For You" or "For God"?

Rabbi Eliezer
A person has nothing but to choose on a Festival: either eat and drink or sit and learn all day. There is no specific mitzva to do both.
Rabbi Yehoshua
Split the day — half for God (Torah study) and half for yourself (eating and drinking). Both dimensions are obligatory.
Rabbi Elazar
All agree regarding Shavuot (Atzeret) that it must be both 'for you' and 'for God' — since the Torah was given on it, rejoicing is an absolute obligation.
The Shared Verse "It shall be an assembly for the Lord your God; you shall do no work" (Deuteronomy 16:8) — 'for God' and 'for you' appear in tension, and each amora resolves it differently.

🏕️ Three Camps and Degrees of Impurity

Who must leave which camp?
The Wilderness model: three concentric zones of sanctity
↓
🔴 Metzora (Leper)
Most severe — sent outside all three camps
"He shall dwell alone, outside the camp"
🟠 Zav / Niddah
Sent from Camp of Levites and Divine Presence
Excluded from two inner camps

Sent only from Camp of Divine Presence
One who had relations with a niddah is equated here

🐑 What Does "Cleaning the Intestines" Mean?

Rav Huna

  • Puncture the intestines with a knife
  • Allows the excrement to exit through the hole
  • Involves a cutting-type action
  • Still overrides Shabbat for the Paschal lamb

Rav Ḥiyya bar Rav

  • Turn the intestines inside-out (invert them)
  • Removes the excrement by reversal, not cutting
  • Derived from Isaiah 5:17: מֵחִים — 'fat ones / entrails'
  • This verse also alludes to the future resurrection

✨ The Righteous Will Resurrect the Dead

Rav Ḥananel said Rav said: In the future, the righteous will resurrect the dead — derived from Isaiah 5:17: "Then shall the lambs feed [וְרָעוּ] as in their pasture"
↓
Elijah alluded to by "the fat ones [מֵחִים]" — connected to Elijah who came from Gilead (near the fattened lands)
↓
Elisha alluded to by "Bashan" — derived from I Chronicles 5:12 and I Kings 19:16 (Abel Meḥolah near Bashan)
↓
Rabbi Shmuel bar Naḥmani said Rabbi Yonatan said: Zechariah 8:4 — "Old men and women shall dwell in the streets of Jerusalem" — proves the righteous will resurrect the dead
↓
Rava's proof from Torah: Deuteronomy 32:39 — "I put to death and I make live; I wound and I heal" — the same person killed is the same person revived

📜 Shavuot: Celebrating the Gift of Torah

שָׁבוּעוֹת — יוֹם מַתַּן תּוֹרָה
Rav Yosef's Declaration On Shavuot, Rav Yosef would say: "Prepare me a choice third-born calf."

He explained: "If not for this day — on which the Torah was given — how many Yosefs would there be in the marketplace?" The Torah elevated him above anonymity.

Mar bar Ravina fasted every day of the year except three: Shavuot, Purim, and Erev Yom Kippur. On Shavuot he ate because it is the day the Torah was given — joy is obligatory.

Rav Sheshet, every thirty days reviewing his learning, would say: "Rejoice, my soul — for you, I have toiled; for you, I have studied."
