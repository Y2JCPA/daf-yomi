QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why may wine from untithed produce (tevel) not be used as a libation offering on the altar?
A. Tevel wine is ritually impure and would defile the Temple vessels
B. The Torah requires offerings to come only from what is permitted to Israel to eat
C. Tevel wine has a sour taste unfit for the altar
D. Libations must come exclusively from first-year produce
Correct: B — The Gemara derives from 'the well-watered pastures of Israel' that altar offerings may only come from what is permitted to Jews. Since tevel is forbidden to eat, it is disqualified as a libation.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the core disagreement between Rav Ḥisda and Rabba about the 'Since' (הואיל) principle?
A. Whether untithed produce can be tithed on Shabbat
B. Whether a basket or an oven combines loaves for the purpose of ḥalla
C. Whether potential permissibility of a forbidden item creates present legal obligations
D. Whether impure dough on Passover must be burned or may be eaten
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda holds 'Since' the item could become permitted, we treat it as permitted now. Rabba holds the prohibition stands fully in force until it is actually lifted — potential does not equal present status.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Eliezer, when is ḥalla separated from dough on Passover, and what combines separate loaves into one unit?
A. Before baking; the oven combines the loaves
B. After baking; the basket combines the loaves
C. Before baking; the basket combines the loaves
D. After baking; the oven combines the loaves
Correct: B — Rabbi Eliezer rules ḥalla is separated after baking, and the basket into which the loaves are placed combines them into a single unit for ḥalla purposes — specifically Babylonian loaves that physically touch each other.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What practical rule does Rabbi Akiva give if matzah dough begins to rise during Passover baking?
A. Immediately discard the dough and begin fresh
B. Move the dough closer to the fire to bake it faster
C. Spread cold water from hand-washing on the dough to halt the leavening
D. Divide the dough into smaller pieces and bake all at once
Correct: C — Rabbi Akiva teaches that if dough begins to rise, the baker should spread cold water (from her hand-washing water) on it to slow the leavening process. He also rules that no fixed quantity applies — women, wood, and ovens all differ.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Meir (as recorded in the baraita), what distinguishes siur from the stage of cracking in leavened dough?
A. Siur has locust-antenna cracks; cracking is when the surface pales like a frightened face
B. Siur is when the surface pales like a frightened face; cracking is when locust-antenna cracks appear
C. Siur and cracking are identical in Rabbi Meir's view — both incur karet
D. Siur means the dough is fully leavened inside; cracking refers to surface appearance only
Correct: B — Rabbi Meir defines siur as when the surface pales (like a frightened person's face), and cracking as when locust-antenna cracks appear — the reverse of Rabbi Yehuda's definitions. Rava explains Meir: even one surface crack signals that the interior is filled with cracks.
