🫓 Pesachim 48 — Dough, Ḥalla, and Prohibited Offerings

Wine Libations: May not come from tevel (untithed produce), orla (first-year fruit), or wood of an asheira — only what is permitted to Israel may be offered

⚖️ The 'Since' Principle — Rav Ḥisda vs. Rabba

Rav Ḥisda
We say הואיל ('Since'): since this dough could be rendered permitted, we treat it as already permitted — therefore ḥalla may be separated even before the prohibition is actually lifted.
Rabba
We do not say 'Since': the prohibition remains fully in force until actually rectified. Potential permissibility is irrelevant — the dough is presently forbidden.
Rami bar Ḥama
This dispute mirrors Rabbi Eliezer vs. Rabbi Yehoshua on when ḥalla is separated from impure dough on Passover — making it a Tannaitic-level disagreement.
Rav Pappa
Reject: Rabbi Eliezer may apply 'Since' only because each loaf was individually obligated in ḥalla from the moment it entered the oven — a unique circumstance, not a general principle.
Rav Sheisha b. Rav Idi
Also reject: Rabbi Yehoshua may deny 'Since' only because one loaf that is definitely tevel remains in the oven — a specific case, not a rejection of 'Since' in general.
Core Question Does potential permissibility (הואיל) alter present legal obligations — or must a prohibition be actually lifted before the law changes?

🫓 Ḥalla Separation on Passover — Rabbi Eliezer vs. Rabbi Yehoshua

Rabbi Eliezer

  • Separate ḥalla after baking
  • The basket combines multiple loaves into one unit
  • Applies specifically to Babylonian loaves that 'bite' (physically touch) one another
  • Says 'Since' — potential permissibility counts now
  • Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi rules like Rabbi Eliezer
  • Rav Yosef: women of his household followed this view, baking small batches (kefiza)

Rabbi Yehoshua

  • Separate ḥalla before baking
  • The oven combines the loaves into one unit
  • Basket only combines if loaves are truly touching/biting
  • Does not say 'Since' — prohibition stands until lifted
  • Rabbi Yitzḥak rules like Rabbi Yehoshua
  • A flat board without a rim (levizbezin): unresolved — does it combine like a basket?

👩‍🍳 Three Women Kneading Dough on Passover — Mishna

Rabban Gamliel: Three women may knead simultaneously and bake in one oven sequentially — no concern that dough will leaven while waiting
↓
The Rabbis: Three women work in relay — one kneads while another shapes while a third bakes — no dough sits idle at any stage
↓
Rabbi Akiva: No fixed rule! Women differ in speed, wood differs in heat, ovens differ in temperature — case-by-case judgment required
↓
Rabbi Akiva's emergency principle: If dough begins to rise → immediately spread cold water (from hand-washing) on it to halt the leavening process
↓
Baraita detail: First woman finishes kneading → she shapes while next kneads → she bakes while next shapes — a continuous three-person rotation at all times
↓
Rabbi Akiva (to Rabban Gamliel): 'May our master teach us — if dough begins to rise, what should she do?' — even Rabban Gamliel's system required a practical safeguard
