⚖️ Punishing Desecration of Yom Tov Sheni

Rav Yosef
Rav Natan bar Asya relied on his calendar knowledge and traveled from my study hall to Pumbedita on Shavuot's second day. I ordered the court officer to flog him.
Abaye
Let the Master excommunicate him instead! Both Rav and Shmuel rule that one who desecrates Yom Tov Sheni in the Diaspora is subject to excommunication — not flogging.
Rav Yosef
For me, flogging is equivalent to excommunication. Both serve as a severe public deterrent — the form of punishment matters less than the communal warning it conveys.
Why two days? In the Diaspora, two festival days are observed due to calendar uncertainty. Even one who knows the calendar must conform to communal practice, lest deviation cause dispute (lo titgodedu).

🔄 Rabbis vs. Rabbi Yehuda: Which Region Governs Biur?

Rabbis

  • One follows the rules of their home (origin) region
  • If crop ceased at home → must perform biur even while visiting a region where it hasn't ceased
  • Traveler's obligation is fixed at point of departure
  • If crop has NOT ceased at home, no biur required even if destination crop has ceased

Rabbi Yehuda

  • One follows the rules of their current destination
  • If crop has not ceased at destination → no biur needed even if it already ceased at home
  • Returning home reactivates home region's rules — must then remove produce
  • Stringent in both directions: destination governs, not memory of origin

וְהָיְתָה שַׁבַּת הָאָרֶץ לָכֶם — בָּאָרֶץ וּבְאַרְצְכֶם
📜 Scriptural Derivation: Biur Inside and Outside Eretz Yisrael The Torah states: "And the Sabbatical produce of the land shall be for you" (Lev. 25:7). The Gemara reads this as teaching two halakhot: (1) from "the land" — biur applies within Eretz Yisrael generally; (2) from "your land" — biur is tied to one's specific home region within the land. Rabbi Shimon ben Elazar: exported Shemitah produce must be returned to Eretz Yisrael for biur. The Sages: biur must be performed wherever the produce is located, even in the Diaspora.
