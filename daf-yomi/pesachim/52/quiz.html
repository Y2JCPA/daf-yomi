QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is labor prohibited on Yom Tov Sheni in the Diaspora even in an uninhabited wilderness area?
A. Because the Torah explicitly extends festival prohibitions to all locations worldwide
B. To avoid the appearance of deviation that causes communal dispute (lo titgodedu)
C. Because Diaspora Jews have no reliable way to calculate the calendar independently
D. Because the Sanhedrin decreed a blanket prohibition on all labor for Diaspora Jews
Correct: B — The Gemara explains the prohibition is rabbinic — to prevent deviation that causes dispute. Even in uninhabited areas, the concern applies since the prohibition cannot be selectively enforced.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What punishment did Abaye argue was appropriate for Rav Natan bar Asya, who traveled on Shavuot's second day?
A. Flogging administered by the court officer
B. A monetary fine payable to the study hall
C. Excommunication, citing the rulings of Rav and Shmuel
D. Forced public fasting for thirty days
Correct: C — Abaye cited a ruling of both Rav and Shmuel that one who desecrates Yom Tov Sheni in the Diaspora should be excommunicated. Rav Yosef had ordered flogging, which he equated to excommunication.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Rabbis, what must a person do who travels with Shemitah produce from a place where the crop has already ceased to a place where it has not yet ceased?
A. Follow the destination's rules — no biur required since the crop hasn't ceased there yet
B. Perform biur immediately, since the crop already ceased in their home region
C. Store the produce in a sealed container until returning home
D. The produce automatically loses its Shemitah status upon crossing regional lines
Correct: B — The Rabbis rule that biur obligation follows the home region. Since the crop already ceased there, the person must perform biur even though it hasn't yet ceased in the destination region.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Into how many regions is Eretz Yisrael divided for the purposes of Shemitah biur?
A. Two — northern and southern territories
B. Four — corresponding to the four cardinal directions
C. Three — Judea, Galilee, and Transjordan
D. Twelve — one per tribe's ancestral territory
Correct: C — The mishna in Shevi'it divides Eretz Yisrael into three regions: Judea, Galilee, and Transjordan (Ever HaYarden). Each region has independent cessation timing, so biur in one does not trigger biur in another.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What halakha does the Gemara derive regarding Shemitah produce that has been exported from Eretz Yisrael to the Diaspora?
A. It loses its Shemitah sanctity upon leaving Eretz Yisrael and may be used freely
B. It must be returned physically to Eretz Yisrael and destroyed there
C. Biur must be performed wherever the produce is located, even in the Diaspora
D. It may be stored indefinitely in the Diaspora as long as it is not sold for profit
Correct: C — The Gemara (and the practical ruling endorsed by Rav Safra citing Rav Huna) is that biur must be performed wherever the produce is located — even outside Eretz Yisrael. The verse supports this by yielding two separate halakhot about 'the land' and 'your land.'
