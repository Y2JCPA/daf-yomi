🌾 Mishna: Grain Species and Tithing Requirements

Five grains qualify for matza: wheat (חיטה), barley (שעורה), spelt (כוסמין), rye (שיפון), oats (שיבולת שועל) — derived from "You shall watch over the matzot" (שמרתם את המצות)
↓
Rice and millet are excluded — they do not become halachically leavened (חמץ גמור). They ferment (סרחון) but do not leaven. Derived exegetically by Reish Lakish and the schools of Rabbi Yishmael and Rabbi Eliezer ben Yaakov.
↓
Can fulfill with: demai (doubtful tithe), first tithe after teruma of tithe is separated, properly redeemed second tithe, properly redeemed consecrated grain, ḥalla, and teruma (priests only)
↓
Cannot fulfill with: tevel (untithed produce), first tithe whose teruma was not taken, unredeemed second tithe, or consecrated grain redeemed improperly (e.g., exchanged for land)
↓
Key verse (Deuteronomy 16:3): "You shall eat no leavened bread with it; seven days you shall eat with it matza." The juxtaposition of ḥametz and matza is the basis for comparing their laws throughout this daf.

🍚 Is Rice a Full-Fledged Grain (מין דגן)?

Rabbi Yochanan ben Nuri
Rice IS a full-fledged grain (מין דגן). One is liable to karet for eating leavened rice on Pesach, one must burn leavened rice before Pesach, and one can fulfill the matza obligation with rice. It even leavens quickly — quicker than wheat!
Majority / Tanna Kamma
Rice is NOT one of the five grains. The verse (שמרתם את המצות) excludes it. Rice does not truly leaven — it only decomposes (סרחון). Therefore no karet, no obligation to destroy it, and it cannot fulfill matza.
Gemara's Clarification
'Close to becoming leavened' means rice genuinely leavens — this SUPPORTS Rabbi Yochanan ben Nuri. But the Gemara concludes: the mishna is explicitly not in accordance with his view. The halacha follows the majority.
📌 Halachic Conclusion Rice and millet are excluded from the five grains. They cannot be used for matza and leavened rice does not carry the karet penalty. The mishna rejects Rabbi Yochanan ben Nuri's position.

🍯 Dough Kneaded with Fruit Juice — Is It Ḥametz?

Reish Lakish
One is NOT liable for karet for eating dough kneaded with wine, oil, or honey — because fruit juices (משקה פרי) do not cause true leavening. Such dough is not full-fledged ḥametz.
Rav Pappa
Contradiction! The verse juxtaposes ḥametz and matza (Deut. 16:3). Since matza kneaded with fruit juice DOES fulfill the matza obligation, by analogy leavened dough kneaded with fruit juice SHOULD carry karet!
Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua
Further objection: If one dissolved leavened bread in water and swallowed it — he is still liable for karet! The form of leavening doesn't determine the prohibition; the identity of the food does.
Rav Idi bar Avin
Waking up and clarifying Reish Lakish: The reason is that fruit juice is considered 'fruit' (פרי), and fruit juice simply does not cause dough to leaven. Therefore it is categorically not ḥametz — no karet applies.
⚖️ Core Dispute Does ḥametz status depend on the substance (true leavening agent) or the formal identity of the food? Reish Lakish: substance (fruit juice ≠ leavening). Rav Pappa: formal analogy to matza governs.
