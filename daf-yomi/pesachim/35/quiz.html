Pesachim 35 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Tosefta quoted in the Gemara, which of the following is classified as a 'type of barley' (מין שעורה)?
A. Spelt (כוסמין — kusmin)
B. Rye (שיפון — shifon)
C. Emmer wheat (כוסמת)
D. Keramit (wild wheat)
Correct: B — The Tosefta teaches: spelt (kusmin) is a type of wheat, while oats (shibbolet shu'al) and rye (shifon) are types of barley. Spelt is the wheat sub-type, not barley.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the Gemara's ultimate conclusion regarding Rabbi Yochanan ben Nuri's view that rice is a full-fledged grain?
A. The mishna does NOT follow his view — rice is excluded from the five grains
B. The halacha follows Rabbi Yochanan ben Nuri for karet but not for matza
C. His opinion is accepted only regarding the obligation to destroy leavened rice before Pesach
D. Rice is valid for matza only when mixed with at least half wheat flour
Correct: A — The Gemara explicitly states: 'The mishna is not in accordance with Rabbi Yochanan ben Nuri.' Rice is not one of the five grains and cannot be used for matza; nor does leavened rice carry karet.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rav Idi bar Avin explains Reish Lakish's position that dough kneaded with wine, oil, or honey is not liable for karet. What is the core reason?
A. The verse only mentions grain mixed with water
B. The Sages issued a special leniency for costly ingredients
C. Fruit juice (משקה פרי) does not cause dough to become truly leavened
D. These liquids are permitted on Pesach and therefore cannot become ḥametz
Correct: C — Rav Idi bar Avin, waking up mid-discussion, clarifies: fruit juice is classified as 'fruit' (פרי), and fruit juice simply does not leaven dough. Without true leavening, the dough is not full-fledged ḥametz, so there is no karet liability.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The mishna permits fulfilling the matza obligation with first tithe even in a case where no teruma gedola was separated. Under what specific circumstance does Rabbi Abbahu (citing Reish Lakish) say this is permitted?
A. When the priest himself waived his right to teruma gedola in writing
B. When the grain was already ground into flour before separation
C. When the grain was brought indoors before the Levite took the tithe
D. When the Levite took the tithe while the grain was still on the stalks (קמה)
Correct: D — Rabbi Abbahu citing Reish Lakish: if the Levite preceded the priest while the grain was still on its stalks (before the obligation of teruma gedola was triggered by threshing), the first tithe is exempt from teruma gedola — and therefore fully permitted.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The mishna states one cannot fulfill the matza obligation with consecrated grain that was 'not redeemed.' The Gemara explains this means it was redeemed improperly. What is the example given?
A. It was redeemed by a non-priest in the Temple courtyard
B. It was redeemed by exchanging it for land rather than money
C. It was redeemed at less than its assessed value
D. It was redeemed on Shabbat and therefore the redemption is void
Correct: B — The Gemara explains: the Torah requires monetary redemption of consecrated property (כסף). Exchanging consecrated grain for land (not money) is an improper redemption — the grain retains its consecrated status, remains forbidden, and cannot fulfill the matza obligation.
