Pesachim 29 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov's initial position, why is one NOT flogged for eating a gentile's chametz after Pesach?
A. Because chametz of a gentile is never subject to biblical prohibition
B. Because he derives the eating prohibition from the seeing prohibition, and seeing a gentile's chametz is permitted
C. Because Rabbi Yehuda explicitly exempts gentile chametz from all penalties in a baraita
D. Because chametz after Pesach has no monetary value and thus cannot carry a lash
Correct: B — Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov held that Rabbi Yehuda derives the eating prohibition from lo yera'eh (the seeing prohibition). Since one may see chametz belonging to a gentile without violating any law, one is also not punished for eating it — the derivation links the two prohibitions.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Who identified the anonymous 'Some say' in the baraita about consecrated chametz as Rabbi Neḥunya ben HaKana?
A. Rava
B. Rav Yosef
C. Rabbi Yochanan
D. Rav Ashi
Correct: C — Rabbi Yochanan identified the 'Some say' opinion as belonging to Rabbi Neḥunya ben HaKana, who equated Yom Kippur with Shabbat for liability purposes — an approach that explains why the consecrated chametz on Pesach might not be subject to me'ilah.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav, what is the rule for a minimal amount of chametz mixed with a DIFFERENT type of food during Pesach?
A. Permitted, since different-type mixtures follow the regular 1/60 flavor rule
B. Permitted only if the chametz belongs to a gentile
C. Forbidden — Rav issued a rabbinic decree even for different-type mixtures during Pesach
D. Permitted because Rav holds chametz during Pesach is treated leniently
Correct: C — Rav decreed that during Pesach, chametz is forbidden even in minimal amounts whether mixed with the same type or a different type. He issued this additional decree for different-type mixtures because of the severity of chametz during its time of prohibition.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Shmuel differ from Rav regarding chametz mixed with a different type during Pesach?
A. Shmuel agrees with Rav that it is forbidden
B. Shmuel holds it is permitted — he did not issue Rav's additional decree for different-type mixtures
C. Shmuel requires 1/60 while Rav forbids any amount
D. Shmuel permits it only if the chametz belongs to a gentile
Correct: B — Unlike Rav, Shmuel did not issue a rabbinic decree for different-type chametz mixtures during Pesach. In his view, only same-type mixtures are forbidden during Pesach; after Pesach, chametz is permitted entirely regardless of mixture type.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Rabbi Yochanan's position on chametz that falls into a food mixture during Pesach?
A. Any amount is forbidden, whether same or different type
B. Same type is forbidden in any amount; different type requires 1/60
C. It is forbidden only when it gives flavor (approximately 1/60), whether same or different type
D. It is permitted in all cases since chametz after Pesach is always permitted
Correct: C — Rabbi Yochanan held that chametz during Pesach — whether mixed with the same type of food or a different type — is forbidden only when the chametz gives flavor to the mixture (the standard 1/60 threshold). He did not apply extra stringency beyond ordinary flavor-giving rules.
