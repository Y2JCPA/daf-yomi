💼 Rav's Practical Wisdom: Commerce & Daily Life

Honor your word above all — 'Better to turn over a carcass than to turn over your word.' Breaking a promise is worse than degrading physical labor.
↓
Don't flaunt your status — Skin a carcass in the market and earn wages rather than declaring 'I am a priest' or 'I am a great man' and refusing work.
↓
Sell while demand is hot — 'Sell your merchandise while the dust is still on your feet.' Strike while the market is favorable; don't wait.
↓
Get paid before you deliver — Open your purse to receive payment first, then open your sack to deliver the goods. Secure the transaction.
↓
Safe modest income beats risky large gain — A kav from the ground is better than a kor from the roof. The kav is small but certain; the kor is large but precarious.
↓
Move perishables immediately — If you have dates in your storeroom, run to the brewery to sell them. Rava: keep up to three se'a for personal use; sell the rest quickly.

🌟 Three Praised by God Every Day

שְׁלֹשָׁה הַקָּדוֹשׁ בָּרוּךְ הוּא מַכְרִיז עֲלֵיהֶם בְּכָל יוֹם
Rabbi Yoḥanan The Holy One, Blessed be He, proclaims the goodness of three every day as exceptional and noteworthy:

1. 🧍 A bachelor who lives in a city and does not sin with women — resisting constant temptation without the protection of family structure

2. 💸 A poor person who returns a lost object — overcoming desperate financial need to do what is right

3. 🤫 A wealthy person who tithes secretly without publicizing his generosity

Note: When Rav Safra heard this and smiled, Rava corrected him: 'This doesn't refer to someone like you, Master. It refers to people like Rav Safra of Difti' — ordinary people resisting great temptation, not Torah scholars whose learning already guards them.

⚖️ Is It Permitted — or Even a Mitzva — to Hate a Known Sinner?

Rav
One who sees another committing a sin is permitted to hate him. The verse 'If you see the donkey of he who hates you' (Exodus 23:5) implies the Torah itself acknowledges legitimate hatred — and this must refer to a Jew seen sinning.
Challenge
But the Torah explicitly states: 'You shall not hate your brother in your heart' (Leviticus 19:17)! How can hatred of a fellow Jew ever be sanctioned?
Rav (Resolution)
The prohibition 'do not hate' applies where you have already rebuked the sinner privately and he refused to repent. But one who directly witnesses the sin — before rebuke — may permit himself hatred. The sinner forfeits the full protection of 'brother.'
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
Not only permitted — it is a mitzva to hate such a person! As it is written: 'The fear of God is to hate evil' (Proverbs 8:13). Fearing God demands hating wickedness, even in a person.
Practical Rule A single witness cannot testify against a sinner in court, but may privately hate him. The case of Tuveya and Zigud illustrates the danger: Zigud testified alone against Tuveya and was himself lashed for being a sole witness.

❤️🚫 What God Loves vs. Hates — and Groups That Hate Their Own

God Loves ❤️

  • One who does not get angry easily
  • One who does not become intoxicated easily
  • One who is forgiving toward those who wrong him

God Hates 🚫

  • One who says one thing with his mouth but intends another in his heart (double-tongued)
  • One who has evidence of sin but testifies as a single witness — invalid and harmful
  • One who sees indecency in his neighbor and testifies about it alone

Groups That Hate Their Own Kind 🐕

  • Dogs — hate other dogs
  • Roosters — compete aggressively
  • Persian priests (Zoroastrian clergy)
  • Some add: Prostitutes
  • Some add: Torah scholars in Babylonia
