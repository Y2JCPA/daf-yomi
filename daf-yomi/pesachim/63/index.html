⚖️ Core Dispute: Location of Leaven

Reish Lakish
One is liable only if the leaven belongs to the slaughterer or a group member, AND it is physically present with him in the Temple courtyard. "With" (עִם) means next to.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
He is liable even if the leaven is not in the Temple courtyard. Ownership alone is sufficient — "with" (עִם) means belonging to, not mere physical proximity.
Key Verse לֹא תִשְׁחַט עַל-חָמֵץ דַּם-זִבְחִי — "You shall not offer the blood of My sacrifice with leavened bread" (Exodus 34:25)

🔪 Rabba vs. Abaye: What Is the Case of Dual Intentions?

Rabba
Aḥerim hold that slaughter is significant from beginning to end. The disputed case is where the slaughterer decided in his mind (without speaking) to slaughter for both circumcised and uncircumcised simultaneously — and Rabbi Meir requires mouth and heart to align.
Abaye
No — we deal with one who verbally expressed two intentions within the act: during the cutting of the windpipe he declared for uncircumcised people, and during the esophagus he declared for circumcised people. Each half-cut matters independently.
Rabbi Meir's Principle Piggul (פִּגּוּל) status can be conferred at half of what renders the offering permitted — invalid intent during even half the slaughter disqualifies.

📜 The Mishna — Chametz and the Korban Pesach

הַשּׁוֹחֵט אֶת הַפֶּסַח עַל הֶחָמֵץ עוֹבֵר בְּלֹא תַעֲשֶׂה
Negative Commandment (לאו) One who slaughters the Korban Pesach while leavened bread (חָמֵץ) is in his possession violates a Biblical negative commandment.

Exception: If one slaughters the Paschal lamb for its own purpose during the festival (at an improper time), he is exempt — a disqualified offering is not an offering at all, so there is nothing to prohibit offering alongside chametz.

📍 Does "With" (עִם) Require Physical Co-Location?

Reish Lakish — Proximity Required

  • Chametz must be in the Temple courtyard
  • Must belong to the slaughterer or a group member
  • "עִם" = physically next to the sacrifice
  • Source: thanks-offering bread (לֶחֶם תּוֹדָה) is only sanctified when inside the courtyard — the bread must be "with" the offering literally
  • Outside Beit Pagei (Jerusalem's outer wall) = certainly not liable

Rabbi Yoḥanan — Ownership Sufficient

  • Liable even if chametz is outside the courtyard
  • Ownership of chametz is the determining factor
  • "עִם" = belonging to — associated with the person
  • The thanks-offering bread case is different: sanctification requires presence, but the chametz prohibition is about ownership
  • Teaches two independent lessons (sanctification vs. prohibition)
