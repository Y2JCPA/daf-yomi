🌿 Abaye vs. Isi ben Yehuda: What Triggers Flogging for Kilayim?

Abaye
One is flogged for any form of benefit from kilayim b'kerem — even unusual benefit. The Torah intentionally omitted the word 'eating' to signal that flogging applies even to abnormal benefit.
Isi ben Yehuda
We derive kilayim from neveilah: just as flogging for neveilah requires deriving benefit in the normal manner, so too for kilayim — unusual benefit is exempt.
Abaye
The Torah's silence on 'eating' for kilayim is deliberate — it teaches that unusual benefit is included. Neveilah cannot override this intentional omission.
Gemara (objection)
But if Abaye is right about kilayim, why does Rava say two verses are needed? One verse for the seed, one for the produce — showing two distinct prohibitions for benefit from kilayim b'kerem.
Key Term כִּלְאֵי הַכֶּרֶם Kilayim b'kerem — diverse kinds sown in a vineyard; forbidden and the produce is prohibited for benefit by Torah law.

⚖️ Four Prohibitions — Why Every Analogy Fails

עָרְלָה Orla

  • Fruit of a tree's first three years
  • Never had a permitted time
  • Uniquely stringent from the start
  • Cannot prove flogging-for-benefit from here — it's more severe

חָמֵץ Chametz (Pesach)

  • Leavened bread on Passover
  • Punishable by karet (excision)
  • Exceptionally severe penalty
  • Cannot derive leniencies from a karet prohibition

כִּלְאֵי הַכֶּרֶם Kilayim b'Kerem

  • Diverse kinds in a vineyard
  • Forbidden even before the produce grows fully
  • Debate: unusual benefit = flogging?
  • Two verse-terms indicate two separate prohibitions (Rava)

בָּשָׂר בְּחָלָב Basar b'Chalav

  • Meat cooked in milk
  • Had a permitted time before cooking
  • Less severe than the above
  • Analogies to it are repeatedly rejected

💊 Healing with Prohibited Substances: The General Principle

יֵהָרֵג וְאַל יַעֲבֹר — One should be killed rather than transgress
Rabbi Yochanan's Rule Rabbi Yaakov said in the name of Rabbi Yochanan:

One may heal oneself with any prohibited substance when life is in danger —

except for three:

1. עֲבוֹדָה זָרָה — Idolatry (including wood of an asheira)
2. עֲרָיוֹת — Forbidden sexual relations
3. שְׁפִיכוּת דָּמִים — Bloodshed / murder

For these three: yehareg v'al ya'avor — be killed rather than transgress.

Ravin's version (from Eretz Yisrael) adds this formulation explicitly: all prohibited items are permitted for healing except these three.

How is each exception derived?
↓
Idolatry
"With all your soul" — love God even at the cost of your life (Rabbi Eliezer interpreting Deut. 6:5)
Sexual Immorality
Juxtaposed with murder in Deut. 22: just as murder — be killed rather than act
Murder
Sevara (logic): one life is not preferable to another — you cannot kill someone to save yourself
