📖 Leḥem Oni: The Great Interpretive Dispute

R. Yosei HaGelili
Leḥem oni means bread over which many words are recited — referring to the Haggadah recited over the matza. It must also reflect affliction, excluding any matza made with luxury ingredients like wine, oil, or honey.
Rabbi Akiva
The repetition matzot matzot amplifies — all types of matza are valid for the obligation! The word oni is not spelled with a vav, so we do not compare matza to an onen's (mourner's) bread.
R. Yosei HaGelili
We do not vocalize it as ani (poor man) but as oni — meaning oppression and affliction. The matza itself must embody poverty: simple, unleavened, unremarkable.
Rabbi Akiva
Oni indeed means mourning — but mourners can eat various foods, so all types of matza are included. That said, leḥem oni still excludes boiled matza (ḥalut) and a large cake (ashisha) — those are delicacies, not poor man's food.
Bottom Line R. Yosei HaGelili excludes matza kneaded with wine/oil/honey; Rabbi Akiva permits it but excludes boiled matza and large cakes (ashisha) under leḥem oni.

🌾 Second Tithe vs First Fruits for Matza

✅ Second Tithe (Ma'aser Sheni)

  • Fulfills the matza obligation on Passover
  • Included by the amplification of matzot matzot (Deuteronomy)
  • Has a redemption path: impure second tithe may be redeemed even in Jerusalem
  • Once redeemed, it may be eaten in all your habitations
  • No joy requirement — compatible with leḥem oni's affliction context

❌ First Fruits (Bikkurim)

  • Cannot fulfill the matza obligation
  • Excluded by: 'In all your habitations you shall eat matzot' (Exodus 12:20)
  • First fruits are confined to Jerusalem — not 'all your habitations'
  • No redemption path if they become ritually impure
  • Associated with rejoicing — incompatible with the mourning theme of leḥem oni

🌾 Why Can't Omer Wheat Be Soaked?

Temple meal-offerings: wheat may be soaked in water to loosen husks — priests are diligent and prevent leavening
↓
Shmuel rules: wheat for the omer offering may NOT be soaked, even though it is also a Temple offering
↓
The Gemara's question: If we rely on priestly diligence for kneading, why not for soaking?
↓
Answer: Kneading occurs inside the Temple courtyard — even by non-priests, the location guarantees diligence
↓
Soaking happens outside the Temple — we cannot rely on the diligent environment to prevent leavening there
↓
Omer exception for gathering: The omer requires guarding (shemirah) from harvest, not from soaking — so soaking before harvest is not guarded and is forbidden

🏛️ Beit Shammai & The Ashisha

לֶחֶם עֹנִי — bread of affliction/poverty (Deuteronomy 16:3)
Beit Shammai One may not bake thick bread on Passover — the thick dough may leaven in the center before the outside has a chance to bake. Beit Hillel presumably relies on the baker's vigilance.

Leḥem oni further excludes two special types:
• Ḥalut — matza boiled in hot water after baking, which becomes a soft delicacy
• Ashisha — a large, important cake (Rav Ḥanan bar Abba: made from one-sixth of an eipha of flour)

The word ashisha signals importance: King David distributed ashishot to all the people when he brought the Ark of the Covenant to Jerusalem (II Samuel 6:19) — clearly not a poor man's food.
