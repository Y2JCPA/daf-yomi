QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav, why may a Paschal lamb be offered for a tevul yom but NOT for one impure from a creeping animal (tamei sheretz)?
A. The tevul yom has brought an atonement offering; the tamei sheretz has not
B. The tevul yom's remaining step — nightfall — is passive, while the tamei sheretz must actively go and immerse
C. The tevul yom is considered fully pure under Torah law; tamei sheretz is a rabbinic category
D. The Rabbis issued a special decree permitting tevul yom for an unrelated historical reason
Correct: B — Rav draws a critical distinction: the tevul yom only awaits sunset, which occurs on its own without any action. The tamei sheretz, by contrast, must actively go to a mikveh and immerse — this required action disqualifies him.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why is a mechusar kippurim (one lacking an atonement offering, such as a zav on his eighth day) permitted to have the Paschal lamb offered on his behalf?
A. He is considered fully pure and requires no further action
B. His remaining impurity is only rabbinic, not biblical
C. The court of priests will not leave the Temple until all offerings in the collection boxes are brought for such individuals
D. He may bring his own atonement offering at any point before eating the Paschal lamb that night
Correct: C — The Gemara cites Rav Shemaya's teaching: the court never rises from its seat until all the money in the Temple collection boxes — set aside for the poor's obligatory offerings — has been used. This institutional guarantee means the mechusar kippurim's offering will certainly be brought.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rabbi Oshaya teach about the Mishna in Temura that discusses animals given as payment to a prostitute?
A. It refers to an animal directly consecrated and then given to a prostitute for services
B. It refers to one who registered a prostitute as a partner in his Paschal lamb, giving her a share as payment
C. It follows Rabbi Yosei's opinion requiring formal partnership documents for Paschal lamb shares
D. It discusses only the case of non-sacred animals, not consecrated ones
Correct: B — Rabbi Oshaya establishes that this Mishna specifically concerns one who registered a prostitute as a participant in his Paschal lamb and gave her a share as payment. The Mishna follows Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, who holds that such a share constitutes real monetary value — making the payment subject to the prohibition of etnan zonah.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the Gemara's analysis of the Mishna about a zav who saw two sightings, what does the Gemara ultimately conclude about whether the Mishna refers to a zav who already immersed?
A. The Mishna must refer to a zav who immersed, because the ruling is otherwise obvious
B. The Mishna must refer to a zav who did not yet immerse, based on the structure of the last clause
C. Actually, the Mishna is ambiguous and the Gemara leaves it unresolved
D. The Mishna refers to one who already immersed, proven from the parallel ruling about a zav who saw three sightings on his eighth day
Correct: B — The Gemara ultimately concludes that the first clause (zav with two sightings on Day 7) refers to one who has NOT yet immersed. This is necessary to teach a novel ruling: even in his unimmersed state, a Paschal lamb is offered on his behalf. The last clause about a zav with three sightings on Day 8 is also needed, lest we think the priests might be negligent in bringing his atonement offering.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the baraita, why may the Paschal lamb NOT be slaughtered on behalf of a niddah on her seventh day, even though a tevul yom is permitted?
A. A niddah must count more than seven days — her seventh day is not yet the end of her impurity
B. A niddah is stringently impure and the Rabbis never permitted the Paschal lamb for her
C. A niddah may only immerse at night — so on her seventh day she has not yet immersed and cannot do so during the day, unlike a tevul yom
D. A niddah always requires an atonement offering, placing her in a more stringent category
Correct: C — The verse teaches that a niddah (and yoledes) may not immerse while it is still daytime — they must wait until nightfall. Therefore, on her seventh day she has not yet immersed (and cannot immerse until night), meaning her purification depends on an active step she has not yet taken. This is unlike the tevul yom, who has already immersed and only awaits the passive arrival of nightfall.
