🔄 Abaye's Reanalysis: Could the Baraita Follow Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi?

Abaye
Had Rabbi Oshaya not established that mishna as referring to a prostitute registered for the Paschal lamb, I would not have done so either. I would have explained the baraita as following Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi through a different logic.
Abaye
Money received for selling a share in one's Paschal lamb would not be 'payment for a prostitute' — it would be money used to facilitate the Paschal lamb itself, which Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi permits.
Challenge
The baraita cannot follow Rabbi Yosei — it explicitly prohibits selling shares. And Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi explicitly states that money from registering partners for the Paschal lamb IS considered a valid payment in the baraita about 'if the household be too little.'
Abaye (concedes)
Now that Rabbi Oshaya has established the mishna as referring to a prostitute, learn from it that Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi holds that money received for selling Paschal lamb shares IS considered payment for a prostitute — real monetary exchange.
Takeaway Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi: A share in the Paschal lamb has real monetary value — money received for it = prohibited as 'etnan zonah' (payment for a prostitute)

🩸 Zav: Two Sightings vs. Three Sightings — Paschal Lamb Rules

Zav — Two Sightings

  • Counts 7 clean days with no discharge
  • Immerses on Day 7
  • Slaughter permitted on Day 7
  • Does not require an atonement offering
  • Only waiting for nightfall — passive step
  • Whether or not he has already immersed: ruling stands

Zav — Three Sightings

  • Counts 7 clean days with no discharge
  • Immerses and brings atonement offering on Day 8
  • Slaughter permitted on Day 8
  • Is mechusar kippurim — lacks atonement offering
  • Court guarantees his offering will be brought
  • Might have thought: since he lacks an offering, perhaps we fear the priests will be negligent — teaches us: no concern

Zava — Three Consecutive Days

  • Counts 7 clean days
  • Brings atonement offering on Day 8
  • Slaughter permitted on Day 8
  • Same logic as zav with three sightings
  • Necessary to teach: lest you think she lacks an offering and is therefore excluded
  • Court ensures her birds from collection boxes are offered

📜 Rabbi Oshaya's Teaching: The Prostitute Mishna (Temura)

לֹא תָבִיא אֶתְנַן זוֹנָה וּמְחִיר כֶּלֶב — Deut. 23:19
Core Principle The Mishna in Temura states: Consecrated animals given as payment to a prostitute are disqualified as offerings — because the Torah prohibits bringing etnan zonah (a prostitute's payment) as an offering (Deut. 23:19). Rabbi Oshaya teaches: This Mishna refers specifically to a case where one registered a prostitute as a partner in his Paschal lamb, granting her a share as payment for her services. The Mishna follows Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, who holds that a Paschal lamb share has genuine monetary value — money paid for such a share therefore constitutes a real transaction, making it subject to the prohibition of etnan zonah. The a fortiori argument in the Gemara:
• Non-sacred birds given as payment → disqualified
• Consecrated animals given as payment → disqualified (by verse)
• But the verse is needed only for consecrated animals — without it, we might have said the prohibition does not apply to already-consecrated property
