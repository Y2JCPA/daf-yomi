⚖️ Rava's Principle Under Fire

Rava
An uncertainty cannot override a certainty. The house was certainly searched; a mere safek that the marten returned leaven does not require re-search.
Gemara Challenge
Challenge: What about the ḥaver's storehouse? Produce certainly tithed is seemingly overridden by the uncertainty it became untithed later!
Rava
There it is certainty vs. certainty — by Rabbi Ḥanina Ḥoza'a's rule, a ḥaver never sells untithed produce. No true uncertainty at all.
Gemara Challenge
Challenge: Rabbi Yehuda's maidservant case — a stillborn thrown into a pit, and a polecat is certainly present. Shouldn't that override the certainty of impurity?
Gemara Resolution
There too: certainty vs. certainty. Since a marten and polecat are found there, they certainly dragged it away without delay — no true uncertainty remains.
Core Principle שֶׁאֵין סָפֵק מוֹצִיא מִידֵי וַדַּאי — An uncertainty does not override a certainty

🗓️ 13th vs. 14th of Nisan — Abaye and Rava Clash

Abaye
The mishna (no concern) refers to the 14th of Nisan. The later mishna (hide leaven) refers to the 13th. On the 13th, bread is everywhere — the marten deposits it. On the 14th, bread is scarce — it eats it.
Rava
Is the marten a prophetess?! Does it know that today is the 14th and no one will bake until evening?! This distinction is absurd.
Rava
Rather: the mishna (no concern) speaks of bread found during the search. The other mishna speaks of bread left over after the search — which must be hidden so the marten doesn't scatter it.
Rav Mari
The leftover leaven is hidden as a rabbinic decree: lest one place ten pieces and find only nine — then spend all of Pesach believing chametz remains in the house!
Practical Ruling One who searches should place leftover leaven in a concealed location and carefully count the pieces

⚖️ Challenges to Rava — Certainty vs. Uncertainty Resolved

Challenge 1: Ḥaver's Storehouse

  • A ḥaver died, left a storehouse of produce
  • Produce was certainly tithed — yet treated as uncertain after his death
  • Seems like certainty is overridden by safek!

Resolution 1

  • Rabbi Ḥanina Ḥoza'a: a ḥaver never sells untithed produce
  • Therefore it was certainly tithed — no true safek
  • Alternatively: grain brought in chaff (Rabbi Oshaya) was never obligated — so certainty vs. certainty

Challenge 2: Maidservant's Pit

  • A stillborn was certainly thrown into the pit
  • Uncertain if marten dragged it — yet priest declared impurity resolved
  • Seems like uncertainty overrides certainty of stillborn's presence!

Resolution 2

  • A marten AND a polecat are found there — certainty they dragged it
  • No true safek: they certainly dragged it away immediately
  • Again: certainty vs. certainty — Rava's principle holds

🧺 Two Piles, Two Houses — A Complex Compounded Case

Setup: One pile of matza and one pile of chametz sit before two houses — one already searched, one not yet searched.
↓
Two mice appear: In our presence, one takes from a pile and enters the searched house. The other takes from a pile and enters the unsearched house.
↓
Core problem: We do not know which mouse took the chametz. Did it enter the searched house (requires re-search) or the unsearched house (irrelevant)?
↓
Parallel from Tosefta: Two baskets — one non-sacred, one teruma — before two vessels. Contents mixed into each. Who may eat what?
↓
Proposed principle: כְּדֵי לְהַעֲמִיד — assume everything occurred in a way that preserves permitted status wherever possible.
↓
Gemara's conclusion: The Tosefta's resolution cannot be straightforwardly applied here — the cases are not fully analogous. The two-pile/two-house case remains complex.
