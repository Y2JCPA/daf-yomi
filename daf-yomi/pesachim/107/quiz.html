QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, what is the halakha if someone tasted food before reciting kiddush on Shabbat?
A. They have forfeited the obligation and need not recite kiddush
B. They must fast the remainder of Shabbat as a penalty
C. They may still recite kiddush — tasting does not disqualify the obligation
D. They must wait until the next Shabbat to fulfill kiddush
Correct: C — Rava explicitly rules that one who tasted before kiddush may still recite kiddush. Tasting does not forfeit or sever the obligation.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What did Ameimar do when offered beer for havdala, and what three-fold lesson did the Gemara draw?
A. He accepted the beer; teaching that beer is valid for havdala
B. He refused the beer; teaching (among other things) that havdala in prayer does not exempt one from the cup, and beer is invalid for havdala
C. He poured the beer out; teaching that beer is impure for ritual use
D. He accepted the beer but recited only a shortened havdala
Correct: B — Ameimar refused beer for havdala. From his conduct we learn: (1) reciting havdala in the Amida does not exempt the cup; (2) havdala requires wine; and (3) one may delay havdala if no wine is available.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rav's ruling (as transmitted by Rav Ḥisda) about date beer for kiddush and havdala?
A. Beer is valid for havdala but not kiddush
B. Beer is valid for kiddush but not havdala
C. Just as kiddush cannot be recited over beer, so too havdala cannot be recited over beer
D. Beer is valid for both kiddush and havdala when wine is unavailable
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda corrected listeners' mistaken inference: Rav rules equally for both — neither kiddush nor havdala may be recited over date beer where wine is available.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does the story of King Agrippa prove regarding the Erev Pesach eating prohibition?
A. That even kings must observe the prohibition, proving it begins at midday (greater mincha)
B. That the prohibition begins at the lesser mincha (9.5 hours), not the greater mincha
C. That eating is prohibited only within one hour of nightfall
D. That the prohibition applies only to bread, not to fruit or refreshments
Correct: B — Since Agrippa normally ate at nine hours (just before the lesser mincha), the baraita's novelty is that even he must stop until dark — meaning the prohibition only takes effect at the lesser mincha, motivated by preserving appetite for matza.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Tanna Kamma (first anonymous opinion), how much wine must one drink from the kiddush cup to fulfill the obligation?
A. A reviit (approx. 3 oz — the standard halakhic measure)
B. A majority of the cup
C. A cheekful (melo lugmav)
D. Any amount — even a minimal taste
Correct: D — The Tanna Kamma rules that one fulfills the mitzva of kiddush by tasting any amount of wine. Rabbi Yosei son of Yehuda adds the stricter requirement of a full cheekful.
