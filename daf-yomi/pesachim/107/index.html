🍷 Rava's Three Rulings on Kiddush and Havdala

Ruling 1: One who tasted food before kiddush — may still recite kiddush. The obligation remains.
↓
Ruling 2: One who tasted food before havdala — may still recite havdala. Tasting does not disqualify.
↓
Ruling 3: One who forgot kiddush on Friday night — may recite kiddush any time during the entire Shabbat day.
↓
Ameimar's expanded formulation: even one who ate a full meal may still fulfill kiddush retroactively.
↓
Core principle: tasting or eating does not forfeit or sever the obligation of kiddush or havdala.

🍺 The Beer Debate: Kiddush, Havdala, and Rav's Ruling

Rav Huna
What is the halakha — may one recite kiddush over date beer (shekhar)?
Rav Ḥisda
Rav said: In a place where wine is available, one may NOT recite kiddush over date beer.
Listeners
We infer from this: kiddush — no; but havdala over beer must be permitted!
Rav Ḥisda
No! Rav's full ruling: just as one may not recite kiddush over beer, so one may not recite havdala over beer.
Rava
One who recites kiddush over beer — may his regular drink be beer! (A curse of poverty for degrading kiddush.)
Ruling Wine is required for both kiddush and havdala. Date beer (shekhar) is invalid for either, at least where wine is available. Ameimar's refusal in the story confirms this in practice.

🍇 Wine vs. Date Beer — Ritual Fitness

Wine (יַיִן)

  • ✅ Valid for kiddush
  • ✅ Valid for havdala
  • ✅ Required for blessing over the cup
  • ✅ Whets the appetite — Rava drank wine all Erev Pesach
  • 🍇 Levi's 13-soaking beer: Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi used it only for birkat hamazon — not kiddush

Date Beer (שֵׁכָר)

  • ❌ Invalid for kiddush where wine is available
  • ❌ Invalid for havdala (Rav's ruling)
  • ✅ Valid for shehakol blessing as a normal beverage
  • ⚠️ Rav Yosef took a public vow never to drink beer — negative effects
  • 🚫 Ameimar refused havdala over beer; waited until wine arrived

👑 King Agrippa and the Erev Pesach Prohibition

Mishna: On Erev Pesach one may not eat adjacent to mincha time — but which mincha?
↓
Greater Mincha (מִנְחָה גְּדוֹלָה)
Prohibition from midday+30 min onward
Reason: lest one be drawn into eating and miss the Korban Pesach
Lesser Mincha (מִנְחָה קְטַנָּה)
Prohibition from 9.5 hours onward
Reason: preserve appetite for matza at night
↓
Proof: King Agrippa ate daily at 9 hours — yet on Erev Pesach he may not eat until dark
↓
If the ban started at midday, what is novel about Agrippa? Nothing! The novelty only works if the ban begins at the lesser mincha (9.5 hours) — just when Agrippa would normally eat.
