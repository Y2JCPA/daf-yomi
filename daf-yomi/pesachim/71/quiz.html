5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Ulla, can Festival peace-offerings (Shalmei Chagiga) slaughtered on the 14th of Nisan fulfill the mitzva of simcha on the Festival?
A. Yes — they fulfill simcha on the Festival itself
B. No — they must be slaughtered on the Festival day
C. Only if slaughtered after midday on the 14th
D. Only on Sukkot, not Pesach
Correct: A — Ulla holds that peace-offerings slaughtered on the 14th (erev Pesach) do fulfill the mitzva of rejoicing on the Festival itself, since they were designated for Festival joy.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How long may a Festival peace-offering (Shalmei Chagiga) be eaten, according to the Gemara's conclusion?
A. One day and one night only
B. Three days and two nights
C. Two days and two nights
D. Two days and one night
Correct: D — The Gemara derives from Deuteronomy 16:4 that a Festival peace-offering may be eaten for two days and one night — it may not remain until the second morning.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the ruling when one slaughters a Paschal lamb on Shabbat with intention for a different (non-Paschal) purpose?
A. Valid offering; intent does not affect Shabbat permissibility
B. Disqualified offering; person is exempt since he intended a mitzva
C. Disqualified offering; person is liable for a sin-offering
D. Valid offering; person receives lashes but no sin-offering
Correct: C — The Mishna rules the offering is disqualified and the person is liable for a sin-offering, since he performed prohibited labor without the mitzva justification (the Paschal purpose was removed by changing intent).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Rabbi Eliezer use to counter Rabbi Yehoshua's exemption argument in the kal v'chomer debate?
A. The guilt-offering (asham) brought on Shabbat
B. Communal offerings (tamid, musaf) permitted on Shabbat
C. The Paschal offering itself as its own proof
D. The burnt-offering (olah) of an individual
Correct: B — Rabbi Eliezer points to communal offerings (tamid, musaf of Shabbat/Yom Tov), which are permitted on Shabbat yet incur liability when their purpose is changed — undermining Rabbi Yehoshua's principle.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the baraita exclude the first night of Pesach from the mitzva of simcha (rejoicing)?
A. Because simcha applies only to daytime hours
B. Because the Paschal meal itself constitutes the simcha
C. Because on the first night one has nothing set aside for rejoicing — no peace-offering was designated
D. Because the first night falls before the Festival officially begins
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that the first night of Pesach is excluded from simcha because the person has no peace-offering yet designated for Festival rejoicing — any offering was slaughtered before the Festival, not truly for it.
