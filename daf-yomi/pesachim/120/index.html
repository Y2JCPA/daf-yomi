📜 Two Great Disputes: Matza's Status & the Midnight Deadline

Rava
Matza today is a Torah obligation — an independent verse applies even without the Temple. Maror, however, is only rabbinic, since its verse links it to the Paschal lamb.
R. Elazar b. Azarya
The Paschal lamb may be eaten only until midnight — derived via verbal analogy (gezerah shavah): 'that night' by the Paschal lamb equals 'that night' by the plague of the firstborns, which ended at midnight.
Rava
Consequence: if one eats matza after midnight, according to R. Elazar ben Azarya, he has NOT fulfilled his obligation — matza is bound by the same deadline as the Paschal lamb.
R. Akiva
The Paschal lamb is eaten until dawn — 'you shall eat it in haste (בְּחִפָּזוֹן)' refers to the haste of the Egyptians at dawn. The midnight limit is not derived.
Closing of Seder Moed This final daf of Pesachim establishes that matza remains a Torah mitzva today, the afikoman rule ensures matza's taste lingers, and a disputed midnight deadline governs when the Paschal lamb — and matza — must be eaten.

🫓 The Afikoman Rule

אֵין מַפְטִירִין אַחַר הַפֶּסַח אֲפִיקוֹמָן
Halakha Matza must be the very last food eaten at the seder.

One may not conclude after matza with other foods — and certainly may not eat anything after the afikoman.

Why? The taste of matza must linger. The Tosefta clarifies: needless to say one fulfills the obligation eating matza first (with appetite); the novelty is that eating matza after other foods also counts — but matza must always come last.

Abaye observed Rabba dozing while finishing his final matza and asked, 'Is the Master sleeping?' Rabba replied: 'I am merely dozing — and we learned that one who dozes may continue.'

📜 Matza vs. Maror Today — Rava's Ruling

🫓 Matza

  • Torah obligation — applies even today
  • Source: 'In the evening you shall eat matzot' (Exodus 12:18)
  • Independent verse — not contingent on Paschal lamb
  • Baraita confirms: night 1 remains obligatory always
  • Eating after midnight (per R. Elazar b. Azarya) fails to fulfill it

🌿 Maror

  • Rabbinic obligation only — not Torah law today
  • Source verse: 'With matzot and bitter herbs they shall eat it' (Numbers 9:11)
  • The word 'it' (the Paschal lamb) links maror to the lamb
  • No Temple = no Paschal lamb = no Torah-level maror
  • Rabbis preserved the practice as a remembrance

🕛 Until When May the Paschal Lamb Be Eaten?

R. Elazar b. Azarya
Until midnight only. Gezerah shavah: 'on that night' appears both by the Paschal lamb and by the plague of firstborns — just as the plague ended at midnight, so too eating the Paschal lamb ends at midnight.
R. Akiva
Until dawn. 'You shall eat it in haste (בְּחִפָּזוֹן)' — this refers to the Egyptians' haste at dawn. Hence the lamb may be eaten the entire night until daybreak.
R. Elazar b. Azarya
'You shall let nothing remain until morning' — morning begins after midnight. Therefore, one must finish eating before midnight.
R. Akiva
'Morning' in that verse means the second morning — the day after the Festival. The word 'that' in 'that night' merely excludes other nights entirely, not midnight specifically.
Rava's Practical Ruling According to R. Elazar ben Azarya, eating matza after midnight fails to fulfill the obligation — since matza and the Paschal lamb share the same timing framework.
