QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, what is the halakhic status of eating matza today, after the Temple's destruction?
A. Optional — praiseworthy but not required
B. Rabbinic obligation, like maror
C. Torah obligation, derived from an independent verse
D. Prohibited without the Paschal lamb
Correct: C — Rava holds that matza today is a Torah-level mitzva, derived from 'In the evening you shall eat matzot' (Exodus 12:18) — an independent verse not contingent on the Paschal lamb.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rava rule that maror is only a rabbinic obligation today?
A. Maror was always a custom, never a Torah law
B. The verse links maror to the Paschal lamb ('they shall eat it') — no lamb, no Torah obligation
C. Maror requires kavana (intent) which cannot be verified today
D. The Sages decreed maror optional after the destruction to ease mourning
Correct: B — The verse 'with matzot and bitter herbs they shall eat it' (Numbers 9:11) — the word 'it' refers to the Paschal lamb. Maror is thus subordinate to the lamb, and without a Temple offering, the Torah-level obligation lapses.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, what distinguishes 'dozing' (nimnum) from fully 'falling asleep' at the seder?
A. Duration: dozing is under 30 minutes, sleep is longer
B. Position: dozing is upright, sleep is lying down
C. Responsiveness: dozing — responds when spoken to; asleep — does not respond
D. Awareness: dozing — eyes open; asleep — eyes closed
Correct: C — Rav Ashi defines nimnum (dozing) as a semi-conscious state: asleep but able to respond when spoken to. Full sleep means one cannot respond. Only dozers may resume eating the Paschal lamb.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rabbi Elazar ben Azarya derive the midnight deadline for eating the Paschal lamb?
A. From 'you shall eat it in haste' — haste implies completion before midnight
B. Via gezerah shavah: 'that night' by the Paschal lamb equals 'that night' by the plague of firstborns, which ended at midnight
C. From a tradition received from Moses at Sinai
D. From 'you shall let nothing remain until morning' — morning means midnight
Correct: B — R. Elazar ben Azarya uses a verbal analogy (gezerah shavah): the phrase 'on that night' appears both regarding eating the Paschal lamb and the plague of the firstborns. Just as the plague ended at midnight, eating the Paschal lamb ends at midnight.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the practical consequence Rava draws by combining his ruling on matza with R. Elazar ben Azarya's position?
A. Matza must be eaten twice — once before and once after midnight
B. One who eats matza after midnight, per R. Elazar ben Azarya, has not fulfilled his Torah obligation
C. The afikoman must be finished precisely at midnight
D. Maror eaten after midnight is likewise invalid even rabbinically
Correct: B — Rava rules that since matza is a Torah mitzva juxtaposed with the Paschal lamb for timing, according to R. Elazar ben Azarya's midnight cutoff, matza consumed after midnight does not fulfill the obligation — a ruling one might have wrongly dismissed as obvious.
