🥬 Maror, Matza & Seder Obligations

Rav Huna
One blessing at karpas suffices for the species. Later at maror, recite only al achilat maror — no new borei pri ha'adama needed.
Rav Ḥisda
Recite TWO blessings over the maror: borei pri ha'adama AND al achilat maror. Would you fill your belly with lettuce and then say another blessing over it?
Hillel
Wrap matza and maror together — 'they shall eat it with matza and bitter herbs' means consuming them simultaneously!
Rabbis
Each is eaten separately. Mitzvot do not nullify each other — Torah-level and Rabbinic obligations cannot merge into one act.
Halakha Since the ruling follows neither Hillel nor the Rabbis decisively today: eat matza alone, maror alone, then wrap together (korekh) — without a new blessing.

🌿 How Many Blessings Over Lettuce?

Rav Huna
At karpas, bless borei pri ha'adama on the lettuce. Later at maror, recite only al achilat maror. The earlier blessing covered the same species.
Rav Ḥisda
At maror, recite BOTH blessings: borei pri ha'adama AND al achilat maror. After eating karpas and filling your belly, you cannot rely on that first blessing for a new, separate obligation.
Ruling Halakha follows Rav Ḥisda. In Syria they acted per Rav Huna, but Rav Sheshet son of Rav Yehoshua acted per Rav Ḥisda — and that is the accepted practice.

🥪 The Hillel Sandwich — Korekh

Dispute: Must matza and maror be eaten together (Hillel) or separately (Rabbis)?
↓
Hillel's View
Wrap matza + maror together and eat — 'al matzot u'merorim yochluhu' means simultaneous consumption. Mitzvot do not nullify each other.
Rabbis' View
Eat each separately. Torah obligation (matza) + Rabbinic obligation (maror today) cannot combine — one would be nullified.
↓
Halakha: Perform all three acts.
① Eat matza alone with blessing
② Eat maror alone with blessing
③ Wrap together (korekh) — no new blessing — zecher l'Mikdash k'Hillel

😮 Swallowing Without Chewing — Rava's Ruling

Matza 🫓

  • Swallowed whole — obligation FULFILLED
  • Consumption itself is the required act
  • Tasting is not essential to matza's purpose
  • Food entered the body; that suffices

Maror 🌿

  • Swallowed whole — obligation NOT FULFILLED
  • Must actively taste the bitterness
  • Purpose of maror: relive the bitter experience of slavery
  • Without chewing, no bitterness is perceived — no obligation met

🤲 Why Two Washings? — The Seder Logic

Rabbi Elazar / Rav Oshaya: Any food dipped in liquid before eating requires ritual handwashing (netilat yadayim)
↓
First washing — Urchatz: Before karpas, the vegetable is dipped in salt water → handwashing required
↓
Rav Pappa's caution: Do not leave maror soaking in ḥaroset too long — the spice's sweetness nullifies the required bitterness of the maror
↓
Second washing — Rachtzah: Before matza and maror, since maror is dipped in ḥaroset → another handwashing required
↓
Why not rely on the first washing? Since a new blessing is recited and activity intervenes between the two dippings, a fresh handwashing is needed

🍞 Leḥem Oni — Bread Over Which We Answer

לֶחֶם עֹנִי — לֶחֶם שֶׁעוֹנִין עָלָיו דְּבָרִים הַרְבֵּה
לֶחֶם עֹנִי Shmuel: Leḥem oni (Deut. 16:3) means 'bread over which one answers [onim] many matters' — the Haggadah is recited over the matza.

The table is removed before the Haggadah so that children notice the unusual behavior and ask: Why is this night different? — triggering the retelling of the Exodus.

School of Rabbi Yannai: Abaye was sitting before Rabbah; seeing the table removed, he jumped up and began to ask — showing how this prompt works in practice.
