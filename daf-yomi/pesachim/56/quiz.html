QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why do we recite 'Baruch Shem Kevod Malkhuto' silently during Shema?
A. Moses commanded it be recited silently in the Torah itself
B. It is a private petition not meant for communal declaration
C. Moses did not include it in the Torah, yet Jacob said it — so it is recited silently, like a pearl hidden between two passages
D. The Men of the Great Assembly added it and kept it secret from gentiles
Correct: C — The Gemara explains: Moses did not write it in the Torah, yet Jacob said it on his deathbed. Unable to omit or fully publicize it, the Rabbis established it be said silently — except on Yom Kippur when it is recited aloud.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Which of Hezekiah's acts did the Sages NOT concede to him?
A. Hiding the Book of Remedies so people would pray
B. Crushing the copper serpent Nehushtan to stop idol worship
C. Sealing the waters of the upper Gihon spring
D. Dragging his father Ahaz's bones on a rope
Correct: C — Sealing the Gihon, cutting the Temple doors, and intercalating Nisan within Nisan were the three acts the Sages condemned. Hiding the Book of Remedies, crushing Nehushtan, and the treatment of Ahaz were all approved.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What did Jacob fear when the Shekhinah departed before he could reveal the End of Days?
A. That his sons would fight over the Land of Canaan
B. That one of his sons harbored unbelief in his heart, as Ishmael and Esau had
C. That the prophecy was too frightening to share with them
D. That Esau's descendants were listening outside the tent
Correct: B — Jacob feared one of his sons was unworthy — just as Abraham produced Ishmael and Isaac produced Esau. His sons' unanimous recitation of Shema reassured him, and he responded with Baruch Shem.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Ulla, why are fallen dates on Shabbat not automatically muktzeh?
A. Dates are always pre-designated because they are so commonly eaten
B. The residents of Jericho received a special dispensation from the Sages
C. An item fit for a person does not become prepared for dogs — it remains designated for human use
D. Items that fall on Shabbat carry their pre-Shabbat permitted status forward
Correct: C — Ulla's principle: human-grade food is never 'downgraded' to animal food in terms of mental designation. The fallen dates remain designated for human use and therefore do not acquire muktzeh status.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did the Sages not reprimand Jericho for designating pe'ah from vegetable fields?
A. Vegetables are explicitly required to have pe'ah by Torah law
B. The Sages issued a special ruling permitting the Jericho custom
C. Pe'ah is not obligatory from vegetables even rabbinically — the practice was pious but not required
D. Jericho had a special agricultural charter exempting them from standard pe'ah rules
Correct: C — Vegetables are exempt from pe'ah obligations even by rabbinic decree (they fail the storage criterion). The Jericho residents went beyond the law voluntarily — a praiseworthy act the Sages saw no reason to rebuke.
