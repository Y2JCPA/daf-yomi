🙏 Jericho, Hezekiah, and Jacob's Hidden Blessing

שְׁמַע יִשְׂרָאֵל ה' אֱלֹהֵינוּ ה' אֶחָד
Central Theme Daf 56 surveys non-standard religious practices in pairs of six: Jericho's six customs and Hezekiah's six royal acts. The daf's crown jewel is the origin of בָּרוּךְ שֵׁם כְּבוֹד מַלְכוּתוֹ לְעוֹלָם וָעֶד — tracing how Jacob's dying response to his sons became the silent verse we say after the first line of Shema. The daf closes with debates over muktzeh (fallen fruit) and pe'ah from vegetables.

👑 Hezekiah's Six Actions

✓ Sages Conceded

  • Hid the Book of Remedies — so people would pray rather than rely on medicine
  • Crushed the copper serpent Nehushtan — to stop idolatrous worship of it
  • Dragged his father Ahaz's bones on a rope — denying honor to an idolater

✗ Sages Did Not Concede

  • Cut off the Temple doors and sent them to the King of Assyria — demeaned the Temple
  • Sealed the waters of the upper Gihon spring — disrupted the water supply
  • Intercalated Nisan within Nisan — added a second month of Nisan inappropriately

🙏 The Origin of Baruch Shem Kevod Malkhuto

Jacob gathers his sons at his deathbed
"Gather around and I will tell you what will occur in the end of days" (Gen. 49:1)
↓
The Shekhinah departed — Jacob feared one son was unworthy, like Ishmael (from Abraham) or Esau (from Isaac)
↓
His sons declared: שְׁמַע יִשְׂרָאֵל ה' אֱלֹהֵינוּ ה' אֶחָד
"Just as there is only One in your heart, so in ours"
↓
Jacob responded with joy: בָּרוּךְ שֵׁם כְּבוֹד מַלְכוּתוֹ לְעוֹלָם וָעֶד
↓
Recite it aloud?
Moses did not write it in the Torah
Omit it entirely?
But Jacob our patriarch did say it!
Resolution
Recite it silently — like a pearl hidden between two great passages
(Aloud only on Yom Kippur)

🍂 Fallen Fruit on Shabbat — Are They Muktzeh?

Ulla (citing Resh Lakish)
The Jericho dispute concerns dates that fell from the tree onto lower palm branches on Shabbat. The Sages hold these are muktzeh — the poor had designated only attached fruit in mind before Shabbat. The residents of Jericho disagreed.
Rava
But these dates are set-aside (muktzeh)! They were not prepared as food before Shabbat since they fell on Shabbat itself. Any item prohibited during twilight remains prohibited the entire day, even if circumstances later change.
Ulla
An item that is fit for a person does NOT become prepared for dogs — it stays exclusively for human use. But something fit only for dogs IS prepared for ravens. Fallen dates remain fit for humans and are therefore not deprioritized to mere animal food.
Ravin (alternate version)
Actually, Resh Lakish's dispute is specifically about dates on low branches — the Sages say they are designated for ravens (not humans), while the residents of Jericho say they are designated for human use. The disagreement is which category they fall into.
Halakhic Principle Muktzeh status on Shabbat hinges on mental designation before Shabbat — and whether an item is considered 'human food' or 'animal food' affects that designation

🌿 Pe'ah in Vegetable Fields — Why the Dispute?

Mishna criteria for Pe'ah obligation: The produce must be (1) food, (2) guarded, (3) grown from the soil, (4) harvested all at once, and (5) brought in for storage
↓
Vegetables pass most criteria — but the question is whether they are normally 'brought in for storage'
↓
Rav Yehuda citing Rav: The case involves turnip heads — the dispute is whether storing by means of another substance (e.g., packing in earth) qualifies as 'brought in for storage'
↓
Tosefta: Initially pe'ah was designated from turnips and cabbages. Rabbi Yosei adds: even from leeks (kaflot)
↓
Conclusion: Vegetables are exempt from pe'ah even by rabbinic law — Jericho's practice was supererogatory piety, not required, so the Sages simply did not reprimand them
