📜 The Three-Step Juxtaposition Chain

Step 1 — Removal ↔ Prohibition of Eating: The same verse links them: "Seven days leaven shall not be found in your houses, as anyone who eats leavened bread, that soul shall be cut off" (Exodus 12:19). When eating chametz becomes forbidden, removal must occur.
↓
Step 2 — Prohibition of Eating ↔ Eating Matza: "You shall not eat anything leavened; in all your dwellings you shall eat matzot" (Exodus 13:3). Both appear in the same verse — their timelines are bound together.
↓
Step 3 — Eating Matza begins at night: The obligation to eat matza begins on the night of the 15th. The prohibition chain anchors removal to this same onset point.
↓
Result: Removal of chametz is linked by two juxtapositions to the night of the Festival. The Gemara then narrows exactly which moment triggers the obligation using the word ach.

⏰ When Exactly Must Chametz Be Removed?

Gemara Q
Perhaps the verse "Yet on the first day you shall remove leaven" includes the night of the 14th — must chametz be removed the night before the 14th?
Gemara A
Incorrect. The word ach (yet) is a divisive term — it excludes the night of the 14th from the obligation.
Gemara Q
Then perhaps chametz must be removed from the very morning of the 14th — immediately at dawn?
Gemara A
Also incorrect — ach divides once more, excluding the morning as well.
Rava
The correct time is midday of the 14th. The verse states: "Do not slaughter My offering over leavened bread" — the Paschal lamb is slaughtered at midday, so chametz must already be gone by then.
Final Ruling Chametz must be completely removed by midday on the 14th of Nisan, the time of Paschal lamb slaughter.

📖 The Word Harishon (First) — Three Festival Contexts

Passover — 14th as 'First'

  • "On harishon, the fourteenth day of the month" (Exodus 12:18)
  • School of R. Yishmael: The 14th precedes the 7-day period and is therefore called 'first'
  • Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak: The verse itself confirms this — it says 'fourteenth' alongside harishon
  • Implication: biur chametz obligation applies on the 14th

Sukkot — The Definite Article

  • "You shall take on harishon the fruit of a beautiful tree" (Leviticus 23:40)
  • The definite article heh excludes the intermediate festival days (ḥol ha-moed)
  • "On the eighth day a solemn rest" — the vav conjunction would include middle days
  • The heh and vav cancel each other; a separate verse was still needed for each

School of Rabbi Yishmael — Reward

  • Israel was rewarded for three uses of harishon in their history:
  • 1. Eradicating Esau: "The harishon came forth red" (Genesis 25:25)
  • 2. Building the Temple: "From the first day" (Ezra 3:6)
  • 3. Future redemption: "A harbinger to Zion will I give" (Isaiah 41:27)

🏠 Rava's Practical Ruling: Gentile Soldiers' Chametz

לֹא יִמָּצֵא בְּבָתֵּיכֶם (שמות י״ב:י״ט)
Applied Halakha — Meḥoza Rava instructed the residents of Meḥoza: Remove the chametz belonging to the gentile soldiers from your homes before Passover.

Why? The gentile soldiers forced the Jewish residents to prepare bread for them. In doing so, the Jews accepted monetary responsibility — if the chametz were destroyed, they would owe payment. This makes the chametz legally theirs.

Principle: Even a token legal connection between a Jew and chametz is sufficient to trigger the prohibition — "it shall not be found" applies wherever any ownership or liability exists.

This ruling reflects the broader rule: an object that creates monetary liability is treated as one's property for halakhic purposes.
