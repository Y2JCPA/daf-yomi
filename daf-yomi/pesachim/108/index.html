🤔 Why Did Rav Sheshet Fast on Erev Pesach?

Suggestion
Perhaps Rav Sheshet fasted because: (1) one may not eat near the time of the mitzvah, AND (2) he held like Ben Beteira that the korban Pesach could be slaughtered in the morning — making the entire 14th of Nisan 'adjacent to the mitzvah.'
Refutation
No — Rav Sheshet was simply istenis (physically delicate). If he ate anything in the morning, the food would weigh on him all day and ruin his appetite for the seder. His fast was personal, not a halakhic ruling.
Conclusion Rav Sheshet's fast was a personal health matter (istenis), not evidence that he ruled stringently about Erev Pesach restrictions or the timing of the korban.

👥 Who Must Recline at the Seder?

Required to Recline

  • A son with his father — required
  • An important woman (ishah chashuva) — required
  • A student with his teacher — per baraita, everyone must recline
  • A waiter who eats an olive-bulk of matza — must recline while eating

Exempt or Disputed

  • An ordinary wife with her husband — not required (husband's authority)
  • A student before Rav Yosef — Rav Yosef ruled students need not recline before him
  • Abaye's resolution: reclining on each other's knees is valid when space is short

🛌 Valid Positions for Seder Reclining (הסיבה)

❌ Lying flat on one's back — not classified as reclining; does not fulfill the obligation
↓
❌ Reclining to the right — not the manner of free people; also poses a swallowing/choking hazard
↓
✅ Reclining to the left — the valid position; frees the right hand for eating and drinking
↓
✅ Reclining on another's knees — valid when space is insufficient, as Abaye demonstrated in Rabba's home

🍵 Requirements for the Four Cups

Obligation: Four Cups of Wine at the Seder
↓
Quantity (Shmuel)
Each cup = revi'it (quarter-log) undiluted — enough to dilute a significant cup
Dilution (Rava)
Undiluted: fulfills four cups but NOT derech cherut (the manner of freedom)
Appearance (R. Yehuda)
Must have taste and color of wine, citing Proverbs 23:31: "Look not upon wine when it is red"
↓
All are obligated: men, women, and children. Women: "af hen hayu b'oto ha-nes" — they too were part of the Exodus miracle
