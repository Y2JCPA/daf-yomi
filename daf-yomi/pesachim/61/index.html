✅ Valid vs. ❌ Invalid Slaughter Intentions

❌ Disqualified — Offering Invalid

  • Slaughtered for the sick (unable to eat a k'zayit)
  • Slaughtered for the elderly (unable to eat a k'zayit)
  • Slaughtered for unregistered persons
  • Slaughtered for uncircumcised males
  • Slaughtered for the ritually impure
  • Slaughtered before midday
  • Slaughtered before the daily afternoon tamid

✅ Valid — Offering Acceptable

  • Slaughtered for registered, circumcised, ritually pure Jews
  • Slaughtered with proper intent at the proper time
  • Slaughtered in the Temple courtyard (azarah)
  • Slaughtered after the afternoon tamid offering
  • Group is mostly valid members (mixed group — debated by Rabba/Rav Ḥisda)
  • Slaughtered in the owner's name for its designated purpose

✂️ Does One Uncircumcised Person Disqualify the Entire Group?

Rabba
The verse says 'and ALL uncircumcised males' (Exodus 12:48) — only if the entire group is uncircumcised is the offering disqualified at slaughter. A single uncircumcised person among fit members does not disqualify. During sprinkling, even an all-uncircumcised group does not disqualify since no intent is counted at sprinkling.
Rav Ḥisda
On the contrary — at slaughter, only an all-uncircumcised group disqualifies. But at sprinkling, even part of the group being uncircumcised disqualifies — because piggul status is established at the moment of sprinkling, making intent more potent there.
Rav Ashi
Objects to both: From where do you derive that kol means ALL the group? Perhaps 'kol arelim' simply means any uncircumcised person — even one member would disqualify! The whole interpretive framework is challenged.
The Verse (Exodus 12:48) וְכָל־עָרֵל לֹא־יֹאכַל בּוֹ — 'And no uncircumcised male shall eat of it'

📝 Source for the Registration Requirement (Takhosu)

Exodus 12:4: 'And if the household be too little for a lamb, he and his neighbor next to his house shall take one according to the number of souls' — the word תָּכֹסּוּ (takhosu)
↓
What does takhosu mean? Two opinions:
↓
Tanna Kamma
Hebrew root: 'to count/enumerate' — each person must be counted and registered before the lamb is slaughtered
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
Aramaic (Sursi): like 'kos li et ha-seh' — slaughter for me this lamb. Registration must precede the slaughter act itself
↓
Ruling: Slaughtering the Pesach for those who have not registered in advance renders the offering pasul (invalid)

🔄 Rava: A Pesach with Change of Owner = Ownerless

כְּמִי שֶׁאֵין לוֹ בְּעָלִים — 'like one that has no owner'
Rava's Ruling Rava rejects Rav Pappa's earlier distinction between change-of-owner and change-of-sanctity. A Paschal lamb slaughtered on the rest of the days of the year (not Erev Pesach) with a change of owner is treated as if it has no owner at all — like an ownerless animal — and is therefore disqualified. The Pesach offering is uniquely tied to its registered owner; severing that bond on any day renders it ownerless and invalid.
