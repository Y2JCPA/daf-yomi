QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, what is the halakhic status of a Paschal lamb slaughtered on an ordinary day of the year with a change of owner?
A. It is valid, since change of owner only applies on Erev Pesach
B. It is treated like an offering with no owner and is therefore disqualified
C. It requires a new act of registration by the new owner to become valid
D. It automatically becomes a shelamim (peace offering)
Correct: B — Rava rules that a Pesach slaughtered on the rest of the year with a change of owner is כְּמִי שֶׁאֵין לוֹ בְּעָלִים — like one without an owner — and is therefore disqualified. He rejects Rav Pappa's earlier distinction.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi say about the word תָּכֹסּוּ (takhosu) in Exodus 12:4?
A. It is Hebrew meaning 'to count,' requiring a formal census before Pesach
B. It is Aramaic (Sursi) meaning 'slaughter,' teaching that registration must precede the slaughter
C. It is a Greek loanword meaning 'to gather together as a community'
D. It refers only to the Temple treasurer's financial registration process
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi says 'takhosu' is Aramaic (Sursi), like telling someone 'kos li et ha-seh' (slaughter this lamb for me), teaching that one must be registered before the slaughter takes place — not at the time of eating.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabba's interpretation, if a group contains BOTH circumcised and uncircumcised males, what is the status of the Paschal lamb slaughtered for them?
A. The offering is disqualified because any uncircumcised person taints the entire group
B. The offering is valid — only if ALL members are uncircumcised is it disqualified at slaughter
C. The circumcised members must bring a separate Pesach offering on their own
D. The offering is valid at slaughter but disqualified at the sprinkling of the blood
Correct: B — Rabba derives from 'and ALL uncircumcised males' that only a group where ALL members are uncircumcised disqualifies the offering at slaughter. A mixed group with some circumcised members is not disqualified at slaughter.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The Mishna states the Paschal lamb is disqualified if slaughtered before midday. What is the primary Biblical source cited?
A. Deuteronomy 16:6 — 'at evening, at the going down of the sun'
B. Leviticus 23:5 — 'in the evening is the Lord's Pesach'
C. Exodus 12:6 — 'the whole assembly shall slaughter it in the afternoon (bein ha-arbayim)'
D. Numbers 9:3 — 'in its appointed time you shall observe it'
Correct: C — The Mishna cites Exodus 12:6 — 'the whole assembly of the congregation of Israel shall slaughter it in the afternoon (bein ha-arbayim)' — as requiring slaughter in the afternoon, disqualifying anything done before midday.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the core of Rav Ashi's objection to the interpretations of both Rabba and Rav Ḥisda?
A. He argues the verse about circumcision applies only to non-Israelite slaves, not to Israelite men
B. He says the word kol in 'kol arelim' might mean 'any' uncircumcised person, not 'all members of the group'
C. He holds that circumcision status is entirely irrelevant to group disqualification of the Pesach
D. He insists the verse applies only to the eating, not to the slaughter or sprinkling
Correct: B — Rav Ashi objects that 'kol arelim' (all uncircumcised) might simply mean 'any uncircumcised person' rather than requiring that ALL members of the group be uncircumcised — this undermines the entire shared interpretive framework of both Rabba and Rav Ḥisda.
