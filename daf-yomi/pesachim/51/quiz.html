Pesachim 51 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, when is a traveler from Eretz Yisrael to Babylonia required to adopt Babylonian stringencies?
A. Always, regardless of his intent
B. Only when traveling for business purposes
C. Only when he does not intend to return home
D. Never — his home customs always follow him
Correct: C — Rav Ashi rules that adopting local stringencies is required only when one's intent is not to return. Since Rabba bar bar Ḥana intended to return to Eretz Yisrael, he could maintain Eretz Yisrael leniencies while in Babylonia.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was the reason the city of Kabul decreed that two brothers may not bathe together?
A. It resembled bathing with one's father, which is explicitly forbidden
B. It resembled bathing with one's sister's husband, which is prohibited
C. It was considered immodest by their community's standards
D. A general rabbinic enactment prohibited family bathing in public
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the Kabul decree was based on the concern that brothers bathing together resembles bathing with a sister's husband — which the baraita prohibits — and the community acted to prevent confusion.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rabbi Shimon's position on cabbage after-growths during the Sabbatical Year?
A. Prohibited, like all other after-growths
B. Permitted, because cabbage has no seeds and grows from the stump differently
C. Permitted only if less than an egg's volume is consumed
D. Prohibited in public markets but permitted for private consumption
Correct: B — Rabbi Shimon holds that cabbage after-growths are permitted because cabbage is botanically distinct — it grows from the stump without seeds — and therefore the rabbinic decree protecting against other after-growths was never intended to apply to it.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Abaye, to which clause of the Passover-eve mishna does the 'do not deviate due to conflict' principle apply?
A. Only to one traveling from a lenient place to a strict place
B. Only to one traveling from a strict place to a lenient place
C. To both clauses of the mishna equally
D. To neither — the reason is concern for the traveler, not conflict
Correct: A — Abaye holds that 'mipnei hamachloket' explains only the first clause: one arriving at a strict community must adopt the local stringency to avoid conflict. Rava disagrees, arguing both clauses are explained by the conflict principle.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why was sitting on gentile merchandise stools specifically prohibited in Akko, when it was permitted elsewhere on Shabbat?
A. The stools were used for idolatrous purposes in Akko
B. It appeared as though one was engaged in buying and selling on Shabbat
C. Sitting very low was considered undignified for Torah scholars
D. A specific enactment applied to all port cities near gentile markets
Correct: B — The Gemara explicitly states that sitting on gentile stools appears like commercial activity (buying and selling), which is prohibited on Shabbat. The community of Akko developed a local custom to avoid even this appearance of commerce.
