Pesachim 70 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, what is the primary proof that the Festival peace-offering of the 14th is NOT a Torah obligation?
A. It is a private offering brought by individuals, not the community
B. It does not override Shabbat and is not brought when each person receives a sufficient portion
C. The Torah never explicitly mentions this offering by name
D. It was only instituted in the Second Temple period by rabbinic decree
Correct: B — The Gemara deduces its non-obligatory status from two facts: it does not override Shabbat (obligatory communal offerings do), and it is absent when portions are already sufficient — showing it is discretionary.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Ben Teima derives that the Festival peace-offering of the 14th may not be left until morning from which verse?
A. "You shall not leave any of it until morning" (Exodus 12:10) — the basic Paschal lamb prohibition
B. "Neither shall the offering of the feast of the Passover be left to the morning" (Exodus 34:25)
C. "And you shall celebrate it as a Festival for the Lord, seven days" (Leviticus 23:41)
D. "And you shall slaughter the Paschal offering from the flock and from the herd" (Deuteronomy 16:2)
Correct: B — Rav taught his son Ḥiyya that Exodus 34:25 — 'the offering of the feast of the Passover' — specifically includes the Festival peace-offering brought with the Paschal lamb, extending the overnight prohibition to both.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
The Gemara attempts to prove from the Passover Seder night question ('on this night only roasted') that the Chagigah is eaten roasted. Why is this proof rejected?
A. Because that Haggadah question dates only from post-Talmudic times
B. Because the question is about distinguishing this night generally, not proving the Chagigah's specific preparation
C. Because the Chagigah was never actually eaten on the Seder night
D. Because 'roasted' in the Haggadah refers only to the Paschal lamb shank bone, not any actual sacrifice
Correct: B — The Haggadah question serves to highlight what makes this night unique — it does not constitute legal proof about the Chagigah's required mode of preparation. A baraita ultimately resolves the question instead.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How do the Rabbis interpret 'from the flock and from the herd' (Deuteronomy 16:2) regarding the Paschal offering?
A. The Paschal lamb itself may come from either cattle or flocks
B. "From the herd" refers to burnt-offerings brought during the Passover holiday
C. "From the herd" teaches that the accompanying Festival peace-offering may be brought from the herd
D. "From the herd" indicates that the Chagigah is obligatory and overrides Shabbat
Correct: C — The Rabbis hold that the Paschal lamb itself comes only from the flock (sheep/goats). The phrase 'from the herd' in Deuteronomy 16:2 teaches that the voluntary Chagigah accompanying it may be brought from cattle — but this Chagigah does not override Shabbat.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ile'a citing Rabbi Yehuda ben Safra, why does the Festival peace-offering NOT override Shabbat?
A. Because the Torah explicitly excludes peace-offerings from Shabbat overrides
B. Because it is a private offering and only communal offerings override Shabbat
C. Because the verse states it is celebrated 'seven days,' meaning it need not be brought on any specific day including Shabbat
D. Because the Festival peace-offering was only instituted rabbinically and rabbinic laws never override Shabbat
Correct: C — Rabbi Ile'a derives from the verse about Sukkot's Festival — 'you shall celebrate it as a Festival for the Lord, seven days' — that the peace-offering of rejoicing can be fulfilled across seven days. There is therefore no urgency compelling it to override Shabbat on any particular day.
