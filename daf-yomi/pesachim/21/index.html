📜 Deriving Benefit from Chametz: Two Approaches

Hizkiya
The verse 'Leavened bread shall not be eaten' (Exodus 13:3) uses the passive — meaning it shall not be caused to be eaten. One who feeds chametz to another violates a prohibition. From this passive construction we derive that all benefit from chametz on Passover is forbidden.
Rabbi Abbahu
Wherever the Torah states 'it shall not be eaten,' 'you shall not eat' (singular), or 'you shall not eat' (plural) — it simultaneously prohibits both eating and deriving benefit. This is a universal principle throughout the Torah, not a chametz-specific derivation.
The Core Difference Hizkiya's ruling is local: the passive voice of Exodus 13:3 targets chametz specifically. Rabbi Abbahu's ruling is global: any 'shall not eat' formulation across the Torah carries a built-in benefit prohibition — a far broader principle.

🍷 Teruma Wine: Upper Press Flows into Lower Press

Rabbi Eliezer
One must actively intervene to save the teruma wine — push it back or collect it — to prevent it from descending into the ritually impure non-sacred wine below and becoming impure by contact.
Rabbi Yehoshua
One need not intervene. Let the teruma wine descend and become impure on its own. There is no obligation to actively save teruma from impurity in this case.
Rabbi Yosei b. Ḥanina's Qualification This dispute applies only when the non-sacred wine below is less than 100× the teruma. If there is already 100 se'a of non-sacred impure wine present, all agree: let the teruma descend on its own — do not actively render it impure with your hand.

⏰ Rabbi Meir vs. Rabban Gamliel: Chametz Prohibition Times

Rabbi Meir

  • Eating and benefit from chametz become forbidden simultaneously
  • No intermediate phase between 'permitted' and 'fully forbidden'
  • Under his view, feeding to animals would be prohibited at the same moment eating is
  • The Mishna's phrasing 'as long as permitted to eat' is imprecise or redundant

Rabban Gamliel

  • 4th hour: non-sacred chametz fully permitted — even eating
  • 5th hour: eating forbidden, but feeding to animals and selling still permitted
  • 6th hour: all forms of benefit forbidden
  • Mishna's phrasing 'as long as permitted to eat' precisely marks the end of the first phase

🔥 Hizkiya: Benefit from Chametz Prohibited by Torah Law

לֹא יֵאָכֵל חָמֵץ — שמות י״ג:ג
Key Verse — Exodus 13:3 Hizkiya derives the prohibition of benefit from the passive construction of the verse. לֹא יֵאָכֵל חָמֵץ — 'leavened bread shall not be eaten' — means it shall not be caused to be eaten. One who actively feeds chametz to another violates a Biblical prohibition. This shows that all causing-of-consumption (i.e., all benefit) is forbidden — not only direct eating.
