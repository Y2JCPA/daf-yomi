QUESTION 1 OF 5
When do Rabbi Eliezer and Rabbi Yehoshua agree regarding teruma wine flowing into a lower press?
A. When less than one se'a of teruma has fallen
B. When teruma wine falls into 100 se'a of ritually impure non-sacred wine
C. When the lower press contains only pure non-sacred wine
D. When the flow occurs on Shabbat
Correct: B — Rabbi Yosei b. Ḥanina teaches: when teruma falls into 100× the non-sacred impure wine, all agree — let it descend on its own. Do not actively render it impure with your hand.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara conclude the Mishna follows Rabban Gamliel rather than Rabbi Meir?
A. Rabbi Meir holds chametz may be eaten for the entire 14th of Nisan
B. Under Rabbi Meir, eating and benefit are forbidden simultaneously, so the Mishna's distinction would be meaningless
C. Rabban Gamliel is cited more frequently in Tractate Pesachim
D. Rabbi Meir disputes the validity of feeding chametz to animals
Correct: B — Under Rabbi Meir there is no intermediate phase — eating and benefit become forbidden at the same moment. The Mishna's language 'as long as permitted to eat' implies a phase where eating is forbidden but benefit is still permitted — which only fits Rabban Gamliel.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
From where does Hizkiya derive that benefit from chametz is Biblically prohibited?
A. From 'you shall burn the chametz' — burning implies no prior benefit
B. From 'leavened bread shall not be eaten' (passive) — meaning it shall not be caused to be eaten
C. From a kal v'chomer: if eating is forbidden, benefit certainly is
D. From a rabbinic decree protecting the Biblical prohibition of eating
Correct: B — Hizkiya derives from the passive construction 'לֹא יֵאָכֵל' that chametz shall not be caused to be eaten by others. This implies that causing any consumption — i.e., all benefit — is Biblically prohibited.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rabbi Abbahu's universal principle regarding 'shall not be eaten' in the Torah?
A. Such phrases prohibit Kohanim from eating but permit laypeople to benefit
B. Both eating and deriving benefit are prohibited wherever this formulation appears
C. Such phrases only forbid direct consumption; indirect benefit remains permitted
D. Such phrases apply only to Passover and Yom Kippur prohibitions
Correct: B — Rabbi Abbahu holds that wherever the Torah uses 'it shall not be eaten,' 'you (singular) shall not eat,' or 'you (plural) shall not eat,' it simultaneously forbids both eating and deriving benefit.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda, how must an unslaughtered animal carcass be transferred to a resident alien versus a gentile?
A. To a resident alien — by selling; to a gentile — by giving
B. To both — by either giving or selling, interchangeably
C. To a resident alien — by giving only; to a gentile — by selling only
D. Neither may receive it; it must be buried or burned
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehuda reads Deuteronomy 14:21 literally: 'give' applies to the resident alien (give, not sell) and 'sell' applies to the gentile (sell, not give). The word 'or' separates two distinct methods for two distinct recipients.
