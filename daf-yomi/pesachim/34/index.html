🧠 What Is the Nature of Hesech HaDa'at?

Rabbi Yochanan
Hesech ha-da'at is a disqualification due to concern about ritual impurity — we suspect the item became impure while attention was diverted.
Reish Lakish
Hesech ha-da'at is an inherent disqualification — the item is disqualified in and of itself, entirely apart from any impurity concern.
Rabbi Yochanan
If Elijah comes and declares it pure, we would listen — it retained teruma status throughout and can be restored to full use.
Reish Lakish
Even if Elijah declares it pure, we would not listen — an inherent disqualification cannot be reversed by any later declaration.
Practical Difference Under R' Yochanan the item retains teruma-status and can potentially be restored; under Reish Lakish it loses that status permanently

⚖️ Two Readings of 'Prohibited' in the Mishna

Rabba's Reading

  • Prohibited = forbidden to non-priests
  • A priest may still eat impure teruma
  • Impure teruma retains its sacred teruma status
  • Applies also to growths-of-growths whose seed does not disintegrate

Rav Sheshet's Reading

  • Prohibited = forbidden even to priests
  • The teruma has been fully disqualified
  • Hesech ha-da'at strips its sacred status
  • Neither priest nor non-priest may eat it

🍇 Can Impure Grapes Be Pressed Into Pure Wine?

Rabbi Yochanan
If one trod upon impure grapes, the wine that flows out is ritually pure — pressing functions like planting, which purifies the produce.
Rav Dimi (citing R' Abbahu)
This ruling of Rabbi Yochanan supports the principle that bringing a liquid into contact with a ritual bath can similarly purify impure liquid.
Abaye
Did Rav Oshaya rule that mikveh contact purifies even a large quantity at once, or only a small amount?
Rav Yosef
Grapes of teruma are not a proof — their consecration through speech is like consecration in a sacred vessel, making teruma teruma stricter than ordinary produce.
Resolution Either one treads less than an egg-bulk (k'beitza) at a time — or the distinction is between ordinary produce vs. teruma, which has heightened stringency

פרה אדומה — וְלָקְחוּ לַטָּמֵא מֵעֲפַר שְׂרֵפַת הַחַטָּאת
Rava's Teaching — Stringency for Consecrated Items Rava proves that the Sages created higher standards for consecrated items. The verse regarding the red heifer states: 'For the impure they shall take the ashes of the burning of the sin-offering' — this shows that even impure purification water retains special properties. Sacred items warrant greater stringency than ordinary teruma or non-sacred produce.
