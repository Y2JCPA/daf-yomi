QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda citing Rabbi Yoḥanan, what is the status of the word 'halleluya'?
A. Two words: hallelu (command) and ya (divine name), only ya is sacred
B. One compound word, and both parts are considered sacred
C. One word, but only the 'ya' portion carries holiness
D. Its status was left as an unresolved dilemma (teiku)
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda taught that halleluya is a single compound word in which both components are sacred — comparable to 'kesya' (Exodus 17:16) and 'Yedidya' (II Samuel 12:25). It was Rav who held the opposing view that halleluya is two separate words.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What condition did Elisha require before prophecy could rest upon him, as cited in the discussion about the Divine Presence?
A. Three days of fasting and intensive prayer
B. Immersion in a mikveh at dawn, then solitude in the wilderness
C. A musician to play for him, bringing him to a state of joyful calm
D. The presence of ten scholars to study Torah with him
Correct: C — The Gemara cites II Kings 3:15 — Elisha called for a musician, and when the minstrel played, 'the hand of the Lord came upon him.' This proves that the Divine Presence rests through simcha shel mitzvah, not from sadness or any of the other disqualifying conditions.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Meir, who composed all the psalms in the book of Psalms?
A. Moses composed the first ten psalms; David composed the remaining ones
B. The Prophets collectively composed them over many generations
C. King David composed all of them, based on a creative re-reading of Psalms 72:20
D. Adam, Abraham, Moses, and David each contributed designated sections
Correct: C — Rabbi Meir argues that the verse 'The prayers of David, son of Yishai, are ended (kalu)' should be read as 'kol elu' — all these — meaning the entire book of Psalms was authored by David, who later transmitted individual praises to the community.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What was the central point of disagreement between Rava and the Elders of Pumbedita regarding Yom Tov?
A. Whether kiddush on Yom Tov must be recited while standing
B. Whether to conclude with 'Who sanctifies Israel and the seasons' or distinguish prayer from kiddush
C. Whether Hallel should be recited before or after kiddush on Yom Tov
D. Whether wine is required at all for weekday festival kiddush
Correct: B — The Elders of Pumbedita held that Yom Tov prayer and kiddush both conclude 'Who sanctifies Israel and the seasons.' Rava argued one should distinguish: public prayer says 'Who sanctifies Israel' (honoring the community), while private Shabbat kiddush says 'Who sanctifies Shabbat.' Rava ultimately accepted the Elders' practice.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Over which cup of wine at the Seder is Birkat HaMazon (Grace After Meals) recited, according to the Mishna?
A. The first cup, recited at the very start of the Seder
B. The second cup, after recounting the Exodus story
C. The third cup, poured specifically for this purpose after the meal
D. The fourth cup, together with the completion of Hallel
Correct: C — The Mishna states explicitly: the leader of the Seder is poured the third cup, over which Birkat HaMazon is recited. The fourth cup is then poured for completing Hallel and reciting the blessing of the song (birkat hashir).
