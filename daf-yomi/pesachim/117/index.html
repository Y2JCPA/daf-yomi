🔤 Halleluya: One Sacred Compound or Two Separate Words?

Rav Ḥisda (R. Yoḥanan)
Halleluya is a single compound word — both hallelu and ya are sacred. Like kesya (Exodus 17:16) and Yedidya (II Samuel 12:25), the whole is one inseparable holy name.
Rav
Halleluya is two words. I saw a Psalms scroll in Rabbi Ḥiyya's study hall written as hallelu ya — separated. Hallelu is a mundane command; ya is the divine name.
R. Yehoshua ben Levi
Halleluya means halleluhu b'hillulim rabim — praise Him with many praises. The ya is not a separate divine name but an intensifier. This one word encompasses a multitude of praises.
Teiku — Unresolved The status of מֶרְחַבְיָה (Merḥavya) — is it one word or two? — remained an open question that the Gemara could not resolve.

📜 Who First Recited Hallel (Psalms 113–118)?

Moses & Israel

  • At the splitting of the Reed Sea (R. Eliezer; R. Yosei's son Elazar)
  • Said: 'Not to us, O God, not to us…' (Ps. 115:1)
  • The Divine Spirit responded: 'For My sake, for My sake, will I do it'

Deborah & Barak

  • When Sisera rose against Israel (R. Elazar HaModa'i)
  • Sang praise after the miraculous military victory
  • Divine Spirit echoed the same response as at the sea

King David

  • R. Meir: All Psalms composed by David alone
  • 'Prayers of David are ended (kalu)' — read: kol elu, all these
  • Individual praises later transmitted to the community

The Prophets

  • The Rabbis: No single founding event
  • Prophets instituted it for every occasion of salvation
  • Recited whenever trouble, may it not come, threatens Israel

✨ When Does the Divine Presence (Shekhinah) Rest Upon a Person?

Under what conditions can Divine Presence rest upon a person?
↓
❌ NOT from these
Laziness • Sadness • Frivolous laughter • Idle chatter • Vain pursuits
✅ Only from this
שִׂמְחָה שֶׁל מִצְוָה
Joy connected to the performance of a mitzvah
↓
Proof: Elisha called for a musician — "When the minstrel played, the hand of the Lord came upon him" (II Kings 3:15)
↓
Rav Yehuda / Rav
Introduce a matter of halakha with joy — the ruling will be clearer
Rav Naḥman
Be joyful before sleep — joy ensures a good dream
↓
Reconciliation: A rebbe teaches with joy; a student sits before his teacher with trembling lips, full of reverent awe. Both are right — context determines the mood.

📍 Does Halleluya End a Chapter or Begin the Next One?

Rav Ḥisda
Halleluya at the end of a verse marks the conclusion of that psalm. It is a closing doxology — 'praise God' as a final word of the section just completed.
Rabba bar Rav Huna
Halleluya marks the start of the next chapter — it is an opening declaration calling the congregation to praise before the next psalm begins.
Rav Ḥanin bar Rava
All agree: After Ps. 145:21 — 'let all flesh bless His holy name forever' — the halleluya that follows is unanimously the opening of Psalm 146.
Beit Shammai
Seder Hallel ends at 'A joyful mother of children, halleluya' — that halleluya closes the first section. Beit Hillel: Continue through 'When Israel came forth from Egypt.'
Practical Implication This dispute underlies the Beit Shammai / Beit Hillel disagreement about where to pause in Hallel at the Seder — the placement of halleluya determines whether the chapter has ended.

🍷 The Third and Fourth Cups at the Passover Seder (Mishna)

Third Cup poured for the leader of the Seder after the meal
↓
בִּרְכַּת הַמָּזוֹן — Birkat HaMazon (Grace After Meals) recited over the third cup
↓
Fourth Cup poured — final cup of the Four Cups
↓
Second half of Hallel completed — Psalms 115–118 recited over the fourth cup
↓
בִּרְכַּת הַשִּׁיר — Blessing of the Song recited to conclude Hallel over the fourth cup
↓
Gemara debate: Does Birkat HaMazon always require a cup of wine? Rava: No — the Sages specially instituted a cup for this Seder night. It is an honor, not a universal obligation.
