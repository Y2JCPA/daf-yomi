🎶 What Concludes the Seder's Song?

Rav Yehuda
The blessing of the song is "They shall praise You, Lord our God" — this alone concludes the hallel at the Seder.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
One also recites "The breath of all living" (Nishmat Kol Ḥai) — a fuller conclusion encompassing all creation's praise of God.
Rav Yehuda
The Great Hallel spans from "Give thanks" (Ps. 136:1) until "The rivers of Babylon" (Ps. 137:1).
Rabbi Yoḥanan
The Great Hallel begins from "A song of ascents" (Ps. 134:1) until "The rivers of Babylon" — a broader starting point.
Why Called 'Great' Hallel? Rabbi Yoḥanan: Because Psalm 136 describes God sitting in the heights of the universe and dispensing food to every creature — the most universal and fundamental praise possible.

🍞 Three Comparisons: The Miraculous Difficulty of Providing Food

Food vs. Childbirth

  • Childbirth curse: "In pain you shall bear children" (Gen. 3:16) — one verse
  • Food curse: "Thorns and thistles… toil you shall eat it" (Gen. 3:17–19) — two verses
  • Rabbi Yoḥanan: Providing food is twice as difficult as childbirth
  • Adam wept when told he'd eat the herb of the field like an animal
  • God consoled him: 'You shall eat bread' — but tears still flowed

Food vs. Redemption

  • Redemption: "The angel who redeemed me from all evil" (Gen. 48:16) — a mere angel
  • Food: "God who has fed me all my life" (Gen. 48:15) — God Himself
  • Rabbi Yoḥanan: Providing food is more difficult than redemption
  • Redemption can be delegated to an angel; food requires direct divine action

Food vs. Splitting the Sea

  • Splitting the Sea: the supreme miracle of the Exodus
  • Psalm 136: verse about food immediately follows verse about splitting the Sea
  • Rav Sheizvi citing R. Elazar ben Azarya: Providing food is as difficult as splitting the Sea
  • Juxtaposition in Scripture signals equal miraculous status

🔥 The Fiery Furnace: A Drama in Verses

Nebuchadnezzar throws Hananiah, Mishael, and Azariah into the fiery furnace
↓
Yurkamo (angel of hail) offers: "Let me cool the furnace and save them"
↓
God refuses — He will perform the miracle Himself to sanctify His name
↓
Gabriel offers: "Let me cool it from outside and burn those who threw them in — a miracle within a miracle"
↓
God says: Descend — Gabriel praises God as he descends
↓
Hananiah recited: "Not to us, God, not to us" (Ps. 115:1)
↓
Mishael recited: "But to Your name give glory" (Ps. 115:1)
↓
Azariah recited: "For Your mercy and for Your truth" (Ps. 115:1)
↓
The nations (or Gabriel) responded: "Praise the Lord, all you nations… for His mercy is great toward us" (Ps. 117:1–2)

👑 The Nations' Gifts to the Messiah — Who Is Accepted?

All nations gather before the Messiah
Psalm 68: 'Out of Your temple at Jerusalem, kings shall bring presents to You'
↓
🇪🇬 Egypt
Brings a gift — Accepted!
Despite enslaving Israel, their gift is received
🌍 Kush
Infers: If Egypt, surely we! — Accepted!
Kush never enslaved Israel; draws an a fortiori argument
🏛️ Rome (Edom)
Argues: We are their brothers! — Rejected!
'Rebuke the wild beast of the reeds'
↓
Why is Rome rejected? God says: They do not come with a gift — they come to destroy Jerusalem's Temple
↓
Rome = the "beast of the reeds" — the pig whose deeds are written with a single quill (one-dimensional wickedness, speaks of others only one way)

🌊 The Sea Demands a Guarantor — A Midrashic Exchange

God
Spew out the dead Egyptians onto dry land so Israel can see their enemies have truly fallen.
The Sea
Is there a servant whose master gives him a gift and then takes it from him? You gave me the Egyptians — why must I return them?
God
I will give you one and a half times their number — the army of Sisera will be washed into you by the Kishon River.
The Sea
Can a servant issue a claim against his master for a promised gift? Who will be my guarantor?
God
The Kishon River will be My guarantor. When Sisera's army fell, the Kishon swept them into the sea — the promise was fulfilled exactly.
Rabbi Natan's Teaching The fish in the sea recited 'And the truth of the Lord endures forever' (Ps. 117:2) — because even the fish witnessed God keeping His word to the sea, proving His absolute faithfulness.
