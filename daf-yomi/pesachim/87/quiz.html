Pesachim 87 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the baraita, where does a woman eat her Paschal lamb in the FIRST year after her marriage?
A. From her husband's lamb
B. From her father's lamb
C. She may freely choose either
D. She does not participate that year
Correct: B — The baraita states that on the first Festival following her marriage, a woman eats from her father's Paschal lamb. From the second year onward she eats from her husband's — unless she eagerly rushes back to her father's house.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was Hosea's sin when God told him Israel had sinned?
A. He performed idolatry himself
B. He refused to prophesy to the people
C. He suggested God exchange Israel for another nation
D. He married without God's permission
Correct: C — When God said Israel sinned, Hosea should have defended them as the children of the patriarchs. Instead he said: 'Exchange them for another nation.' This failure of advocacy and love was his grave sin.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, what does 'I am a wall' (Song of Songs 8:10) refer to?
A. The walls of Jerusalem
B. The Jewish people's firmness in exile
C. The Torah
D. The Temple's foundation
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan interprets 'I am a wall' as the Torah, which Israel stood firm as a wall to accept. 'My breasts are like towers' refers to Torah scholars who disseminate Torah and fortify Israel.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why was Jeroboam ben Joash (king of Israel) privileged to be mentioned alongside righteous kings of Judea in Hosea's opening verse?
A. He rebuilt the Temple in Jerusalem
B. He repented fully before his death
C. He supported Torah scholars financially
D. He did not accept slander about the prophet Amos
Correct: D — Rabbi Yoḥanan teaches that when Amaziah accused Amos of conspiring against Jeroboam, the king did not act against the prophet. His refusal to accept lashon hara about a servant of God earned him this honor.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Elazar, what was the primary purpose of God exiling Israel among the nations?
A. So that converts would join them
B. To punish them for the sin of idolatry
C. To protect them from Roman persecution
D. So they would appreciate the Land of Israel upon return
Correct: A — Rabbi Elazar derives from 'I will sow her to Me in the land' (Hosea 2:25) that exile is like sowing — a farmer sows more seed than he planted to reap a greater yield. God scattered Israel among the nations to attract converts and expand His people.
