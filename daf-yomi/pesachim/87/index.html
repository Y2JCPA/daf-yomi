🐑 Mishna: Who Eats From Which Paschal Lamb?

Married Woman

  • Both husband and father slaughtered on her behalf
  • → Eats from husband's lamb (default)
  • First year after marriage: eats from father's lamb (baraita)
  • Exception: if she eagerly rushes to father's house — eats from his even after first year

Orphan with Multiple Guardians

  • Each guardian slaughtered a lamb on the orphan's behalf
  • → He eats in whichever place he wishes
  • No single household obligation applies
  • Freedom of choice granted — no primary home established

Jointly Owned Slave

  • Two masters each slaughtered on his behalf
  • → He eats in whichever place he wishes
  • Parallel to orphan case
  • Split ownership prevents any single primary allegiance

🔥 The Divine Dialogue — God and Hosea

God
Your sons, the Jewish people, have sinned against Me.
Hosea
Master of the Universe — exchange them for another nation!
God
Go, take a prostitute as your wife and father children of uncertain lineage. Then I will command you: send her away.
Hosea
[Years pass — Gomer bears sons and a daughter] I cannot send her away. She is my wife. These are my children!
God
You, whose children may not even be yours, could not send her away. Yet you asked Me to abandon Israel — whom I know are Mine, sons of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob?!
Hosea
Forgive me. I request compassion upon Israel — nullify the harsh decree against them!
Core Teaching God used Hosea's own inability to abandon his family to teach him that no prophet may ask God to abandon Israel. Love — even imperfect love — cannot be extinguished.

🌍 Why Was Israel Exiled? — Five Amoraic Views

Rabbi Elazar: God exiled Israel among the nations so that converts would join them. Source: "I will sow her to Me in the land" (Hosea 2:25). A farmer sows more seed than he planted in order to reap a greater yield — so too God scattered Israel to gather a greater people.
↓
Rabbi Yoḥanan derives the same from Hosea 2:25: "I will say to them that were not My people: You are My people." Even those initially 'not My people' can become My people through joining Israel in exile.
↓
Rabbi Oshaya: Exile was a charitable deed — God scattered Israel so no single empire could destroy them all. "The righteous acts of His rulers [pirzono]" = He scattered them [pizran] among the nations for their own protection.
↓
Rabbi Yoḥanan (alternate): God sent Israel specifically to Babylonia — their mother's house, birthplace of the patriarchs (Aram-Naharaim). Like a man angry at his wife who nevertheless sends her to her mother's home, not a stranger's.
↓
Rabbi Ḥiyya: God knew Israel could not endure the harsh decrees of the Romans. He therefore sent them to Babylonia where they could survive, sustain Torah life, and eventually return.

👑 Jeroboam's Merit — Not Accepting Slander

לֹא אוֹסִיף עוֹד אֲרַחֵם אֶת בֵּית יִשְׂרָאֵל (הושע א:ו)
Rabbi Yoḥanan Why was Jeroboam ben Joash — king of Israel and not fully righteous — privileged to be listed alongside the righteous kings of Judea (Uzziah, Jotham, Ahaz, Hezekiah) in the opening verse of Hosea? Because he did not accept slander (lashon hara) about the prophet Amos. When Amaziah the priest of Beth-El sent word to Jeroboam: "Amos has conspired against you in the midst of the house of Israel" (Amos 7:10) — Jeroboam did not act against Amos. He refused to believe slander about a servant of God. Companion teaching — Rabbi Elazar: Even at the time of God's anger, He remembers compassion. "For I will no more have compassion upon the house of Israel" (Hosea 1:6) — even withholding mercy from Israel, He extended it to Judah.
