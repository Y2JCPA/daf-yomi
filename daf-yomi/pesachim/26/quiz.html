QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Rava reject Abaye's proof from Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai sitting in the shade of the Sanctuary?
A. Because Rabban Yoḥanan had special rabbinic dispensation to sit there
B. Because the Sanctuary was built for its interior, so its exterior walls carry no prohibition
C. Because shade is intangible and therefore never subject to any prohibition
D. Because sitting in shade is only a rabbinic prohibition, not a biblical one
Correct: B — Rava distinguishes: the Sanctuary was constructed for the use of its interior space. The exterior walls were never designated for benefit, so deriving benefit from them involves no prohibition — making Abaye's proof inapplicable to the general debate.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, why are sound and sight not subject to me'ila?
A. Because the Torah explicitly exempts them by name in the laws of me'ila
B. Because they are prohibited only rabbinically, not biblically
C. Because they have no physical substance
D. Because they cannot be appraised monetarily for Temple accounting purposes
Correct: C — Rav Pappa rules that me'ila requires a tangible, physical object — sound and sight are intangible and therefore cannot constitute misuse of consecrated property, regardless of intent.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rav Pappa rule regarding the smell of the incense after its mitzva has been performed?
A. It remains subject to me'ila because it originated from a consecrated item
B. It is no longer subject to me'ila because the mitzva has already been fulfilled
C. It is subject to me'ila only if smelled intentionally inside the Temple
D. The Gemara leaves this question without resolution
Correct: B — Rav Pappa rules that once the mitzva of burning the incense is performed, the object has completed its sacred function and me'ila no longer applies — parallel to the ruling on the removal of altar ashes.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rav Pappa derive the rule for when the red heifer is disqualified by work?
A. From a gezeira shava comparing it to the laws of Shabbat labor
B. From the dual reading of the verse — written 'avad' (active) but read 'ubbad' (passive) — meaning both apply
C. From the ruling that any animal contact with another animal disqualifies the heifer
D. From the general principle that animals used for sacred rites must be entirely untouched
Correct: B — The Torah writes the active form (he worked it) but the traditional reading is passive (it was worked). Rav Pappa derives that both apply simultaneously: the heifer is disqualified only when worked in the same manner an owner typically works a draft animal.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the dispute between Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi and the Sages about bread baked in an oven heated with orla?
A. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi permits the bread; the Sages prohibit it
B. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi prohibits the bread; the Sages permit it
C. Both agree the bread is prohibited but dispute whether the oven must be shattered
D. Both agree the bread is permitted but dispute what must be done with the oven afterward
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi holds the bread is prohibited because benefit from the orla improved the baking. The Sages permit it, ruling that orla provided only heat — an indirect benefit that does not render the bread itself forbidden.
