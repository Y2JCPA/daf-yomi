⚖️ Does Unintended Benefit Violate a Prohibition?

Abaye
One who derives benefit without intent is exempt — lack of intent means the act is not considered a violation at all
Rava
R. Yehuda holds that lack of intent only helps as a leniency — it cannot create a stringency. Unintended benefit can still constitute a violation
Abaye
Proof: Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai sat in the shade of the Sanctuary and taught — this shows unintended benefit is permitted
Rava
The Sanctuary is different! It was built for its interior — the exterior walls carry no prohibition of benefit whatsoever
Status The debate remains unresolved; multiple proofs are attempted from the red heifer, lost objects, and kil'ayim garments — each is deflected by a unique local distinction.

🎵 Sound, Sight & Smell — Are They Subject to Me'ila?

Sound of Musical Instruments

  • ❌ Not subject to me'ila
  • Reason: Sound has no physical substance
  • Source: Bar Kappara via R. Yehoshua ben Levi
  • Rav Pappa confirms: intangibility exempts from me'ila

Sight of Temple Service

  • ❌ Not subject to me'ila
  • Reason: Sight has no physical substance
  • Rav Pappa: only tangible objects can be 'misused'
  • Even if one intends to look — no violation

Smell of Incense

  • ✅ Before mitzva: me'ila applies to the spice
  • ❌ After mitzva performed: me'ila no longer applies
  • Rav Pappa: once the commandment is fulfilled, the object loses its consecrated status for me'ila purposes
  • Parallel: removal of altar ashes — same logic

🐄 Red Heifer: Work Performed Without Intent — Does It Disqualify?

Gemara
Verse: "Which has not been used for work" (Deut. 21:3). If the heifer threshed grain against the owner's will, is it disqualified for use as the red heifer?
Rav Pappa
The Torah writes avad (active: 'he worked') but tradition reads ubbad (passive: 'it was worked') — both are intended. Disqualified only when worked as an owner typically works it
Ruling
Bird lands on heifer → Valid ✅ (not human-style work). Male mounts it → Invalid ❌ (comparable to intentional use). The manner of work, not mere contact, determines disqualification
Textual Key The dual reading (written vs. read) generates a single rule: the heifer is unfit only when worked in the same manner that an owner works a draft animal — not by incidental, non-human-style contact.

🔥 Bread Baked in an Oven Heated with Orla or Chametz

Oven is lit with prohibited fuel: peels of orla fruit, or straw from kil'ayim (prohibited mixture)
↓
The oven becomes heated and strengthened by the prohibited material
↓
Bread is baked in the now-heated oven
↓
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi: Bread is prohibited — benefit from the orla/chametz directly improved the baking process
↓
Sages: Bread is permitted — the prohibited item provided heat only; no prohibited substance entered the bread itself
↓
New oven (absorbs the material) → must be shattered before use. Old oven (already conditioned) → may be cooled and then used normally
