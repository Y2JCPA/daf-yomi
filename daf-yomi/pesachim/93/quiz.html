QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, what is the status of Pesach Sheni?
A. A redress that only applies to those who were obligated on the first Pesach
B. An independent Festival with its own obligation for anyone who missed the first
C. A voluntary offering that carries no karet penalty
D. A repair mechanism that nullifies intentional sin from the first Pesach
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi holds Pesach Sheni is an independent Festival — therefore even a convert who converted after the first Pesach is obligated to bring it, since he need not have been obligated on the first.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
If someone intentionally skipped the first Pesach but unwittingly forgot the second Pesach, what does Rabbi Ḥananya ben Akavya rule?
A. Karet, because the intentional sin on the first Pesach stands
B. Exempt, because Pesach Sheni would have repaired the first, but he was unwitting
C. Karet, because the second Pesach is an independent obligation
D. Lashes, as a lesser penalty than karet
Correct: B — Rabbi Ḥananya holds Pesach Sheni 'repairs' the first. Had he brought it, his sin would be nullified. Since he was only unwitting on the second — not intentionally defiant — he is exempt from karet.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Ulla calculate that Modi'im (15 mil) is the distant journey threshold?
A. 15 mil is the distance one can walk in a full day
B. An average person walks 40 mil per day; half-day = 20 mil; subtract 5 mil for dawn-to-sunrise = 15 mil
C. The Torah explicitly states 15 mil as the threshold in Numbers
D. 15 mil is the distance Lot walked from Sodom to Zoar
Correct: B — Ulla derives: 10 parsa/day = 40 mil; from sunrise to midday = 20 mil; but one must subtract the 5 mil walkable before sunrise (dawn to sunrise), leaving 15 mil as the usable pre-slaughter distance.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
From which biblical episode does the Gemara prove that five mil can be walked from dawn until sunrise?
A. Elijah fleeing from Jezebel to Be'er Sheva
B. The Israelites marching out of Egypt at dawn
C. Lot fleeing Sodom to the city of Zoar
D. Abraham's early morning journey to the Akeidah
Correct: C — Genesis 19:23 states the sun rose when Lot arrived in Zoar. Since Lot left at dawn and Sodom-to-Zoar was five mil, the Gemara concludes the period from dawn to sunrise corresponds to five mil of walking.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the contradiction between two baraitot — one including women in Pesach Sheni, one excluding them?
A. The first baraita is a later ruling that overrides the second
B. The first baraita follows Rabbi Yosei (women may observe); the second follows a different opinion
C. Women were only included when they were ritually impure, not on a journey
D. Both baraitot are rejected and the halacha follows neither
Correct: B — The Gemara explains: the baraita including women follows Rabbi Yosei, who holds one may slaughter the Paschal lamb on behalf of women on Pesach Sheni; the baraita excluding women reflects a dissenting tannaitic view.
