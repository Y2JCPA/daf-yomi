⚖️ Three Opinions: What Is Pesach Sheni?

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi

  • Pesach Sheni is an independent Festival
  • Anyone who missed first Pesach for any reason is obligated
  • A convert who converted mid-month: obligated
  • Intentional on first + unwitting on second = karet
  • Unwitting on first + intentional on second = karet

Rabbi Natan

  • Pesach Sheni is a redress for the first
  • Only those obligated on first Pesach may observe second
  • A convert who converted mid-month: exempt
  • Intentional on first + unwitting on second = karet
  • Unwitting on first + intentional on second = exempt

Rabbi Ḥananya ben Akavya

  • Pesach Sheni repairs the failure of the first
  • Bringing the second nullifies the sin of missing the first
  • A convert who converted mid-month: exempt
  • Intentional on first + unwitting on second = exempt
  • Unwitting on first + intentional on second = exempt

👥 Who Observes Pesach Sheni?

Obligated — All Agree

  • Zavim and Zavot (those with bodily discharges)
  • Male and female lepers (metzora)
  • Menstruating women (niddah)
  • Men who had relations with a niddah
  • Those on a distant journey (derech reḥokah)
  • Anyone else who missed the first Pesach

Disputed — Women

  • Baraita 1: Includes women → follows Rabbi Yosei
  • Baraita 2: Excludes women → only males listed
  • Rabbi Yosei: may slaughter Pesach Sheni on behalf of women
  • Gemara resolves: two baraitot reflect this tannaitic dispute

🗺️ Defining Distant Journey (דֶּרֶךְ רְחוֹקָה)

What counts as a distant journey?
The dot (•) above the ה in reḥokah (Numbers 9:10) signals an inexact, expansive meaning — a specific rabbinic threshold must be set
↓
Ulla
Any distance from which one cannot reach the Temple by the earliest time of slaughter (afternoon of 14 Nisan)
Rav Yehuda
Any distance from which one cannot reach Jerusalem during the time of eating (night of 15 Nisan)
↓
Practical Standard: Modi'im
15 mil from Jerusalem
Ulla's derivation: 10 parsa/day = 40 mil; half-day = 20 mil; minus 5 mil (dawn-to-sunrise) = 15 mil threshold

🤝 Rabba Challenges Ulla and Rav Yehuda

Ulla
A distant journey means any distance from which one cannot reach the Temple by the earliest slaughter time on the 14th of Nisan.
Rabba
Difficult! A ritually impure person standing at the Temple entrance is also exempt from the first Pesach — yet he is clearly not on a 'distant journey.' How can you define distant journey purely by arrival at slaughter time?
Ulla
Not difficult! The concept of distant journey applies only to one who is ritually pure. Only a pure person 15 mil away qualifies. The impure person has a separate, independent exemption — the cases do not conflict.
Rabba
And according to Rav Yehuda — also difficult! An impure person at the Temple entrance cannot eat the Pesach at night either, yet his exemption is treated differently from a distant journey exemption.
Resolution Rav Yehuda likewise responds: distant journey applies only to the ritually pure. The impure person's exemption is categorically distinct, derived from a separate verse.

🌅 From Where Do We Know Dawn to Sunrise = Five Mil?

וְהַשֶּׁמֶשׁ יָצָא עַל הָאָרֶץ וְלוֹט בָּא צֹעָרָה (בראשית י״ט:כ״ג)
📖 Proof Text — Genesis 19:23 Lot fled Sodom at dawn. The verse states: "The sun rose upon the earth when Lot came to Zoar." The distance from Sodom to Zoar was five mil. Since Lot traveled from dawn until sunrise and covered exactly five mil, the Gemara derives that the time from amud hashaḥar (dawn) to netz haḥamah (sunrise) corresponds to five mil of walking.

This is used to calculate: 10 parsa/day = 40 mil; divide equally = 20 mil per half-day; subtract 5 mil for the dawn period → 15 mil is the Modi'im threshold.
