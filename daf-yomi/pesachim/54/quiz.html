Pesachim 54 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara's final resolution, when was earthly fire actually created?
A. On Shabbat eve at twilight alongside the other ten creations
B. At the conclusion of Shabbat, when Adam struck two stones together
C. On the second day of the week, together with Gehenna's fire
D. Before the world was created, like the Torah and Throne of Glory
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes: God conceived earthly fire on Shabbat eve, but it was actually created at the conclusion of Shabbat when Adam struck two flint stones. The baraita listing ten twilight creations referred only to Gehenna's void.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rabbi Yosei teaches that the fire of Gehenna was created on which day?
A. Before the world was created
B. On Shabbat eve at twilight
C. At the conclusion of Shabbat
D. On the second day of the week (Monday)
Correct: D — Rabbi Yosei teaches that the fire of Gehenna was created on the second day of the week (Monday) and will never be extinguished, citing the verse about looking upon corpses whose fire is not quenched (Isaiah 66:24).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which of the following was NOT listed among the ten phenomena created at twilight on Shabbat eve?
A. Miriam's well
B. The grave of Moses
C. The staff of Aaron
D. The garment of Adam
Correct: C — The Gemara's list includes Miriam's well, manna, rainbow, writing, writing instrument, tablets, Moses's grave, the cave, Adam's garment, and the mule. Aaron's staff is not mentioned in this passage.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rava rule regarding pregnant and nursing women on the Ninth of Av?
A. They are fully exempt from fasting
B. They fast but may break the fast before nightfall
C. They fast and complete the full fast, as on Yom Kippur
D. They observe only the daytime hours of the fast
Correct: C — Rava rules that pregnant and nursing women must fast and complete the entire fast on the Ninth of Av, just as they do on Yom Kippur. The Ninth of Av's stringency in this regard matches that of Yom Kippur.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the key distinction between the Ninth of Av and Yom Kippur regarding prayer, according to the Gemara?
A. Yom Kippur has Musaf; Tisha B'Av does not
B. Tisha B'Av has five prayers; Yom Kippur has four
C. Yom Kippur has five prayers (including Neilah); Tisha B'Av has only four
D. Both have five prayers but differ only in the Musaf text
Correct: C — Yom Kippur has five prayer services (Shacharit, Musaf, Mincha, Neilah, Maariv), while the Ninth of Av has only four — it lacks the Neilah closing prayer that marks Yom Kippur's conclusion.
