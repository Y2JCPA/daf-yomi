🕯️ Havdalah: When to Bless Over Fire?

Baraita
One recites the blessing over fire only at the conclusion of Shabbat — NOT at the end of Yom Kippur. Shabbat is the time of fire's original creation.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
One recites the blessing over fire at the conclusion of Yom Kippur as well!
Gemara (resolution)
No contradiction. Rabbi Yoḥanan speaks of fire that rested on Yom Kippur (no prohibition in its kindling). The baraita refers to newly-kindled fire.
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
I distribute the blessings — reciting fire and spices each separately when the opportunity arises throughout Havdalah.
Rabbi Ḥiyya
I collect all the blessings and recite them together in a single unified Havdalah framework.
Further Contradiction Resolved Fire from wood and stones — one baraita permits the blessing, another forbids it. If one witnessed the fire from its kindling: bless it. If discovered already burning: do not bless it.

🔥 Two Fires — Their Origins

Our Earthly Fire

  • Conceived in God's mind on Shabbat eve
  • Actually created at the conclusion of Shabbat
  • Adam struck two flint stones together
  • Basis for the Havdalah blessing over fire
  • Source of all fire we use today

Fire of Gehenna

  • Void of Gehenna: created before the world
  • Fire itself: created on the second day (Monday)
  • Rabbi Yosei: 'will never be extinguished'
  • Twilight baraita = only the void, not its fire
  • Will consume the wicked forever

🗓️ Ninth of Av vs. Yom Kippur: How Similar?

Shmuel
The only communal fast in Babylonia observed with ALL the stringencies of a communal fast is the Ninth of Av — implying it stands on par with Yom Kippur.
Baraita
The sole difference between Tisha B'Av and Yom Kippur: on Yom Kippur, its twilight period is prohibited; on Tisha B'Av, its twilight period is permitted.
Rava
Pregnant and nursing women must fast and complete the fast on Tisha B'Av — just as on Yom Kippur. Twilight on Tisha B'Av is ALSO prohibited to eat or drink.
Rav Pappa
Tisha B'Av is NOT like an ordinary communal fast — it is STRICTER. Even the most severe communal fast allows eating before dawn; Tisha B'Av does not.
Rabbi Elazar
It is forbidden to dip even one's finger in water on Tisha B'Av — just as on Yom Kippur.
Key Distinction Tisha B'Av has only 4 prayers (Shacharit, Musaf, Mincha, Maariv) vs. 5 on Yom Kippur (which adds Neilah). Labor is regionally permitted on Tisha B'Av, unlike Yom Kippur.
