🌾 Soaking Wheat on Passover — The Evolving Debate

May one soak wheat during Passover?
↓
Rabba (initially)
A pious person avoids soaking entirely
Rav Naḥman
Must soak — otherwise flour goes moldy and matza cannot be made
Rava (early)
Permitted — the baraita only prohibits barley, not wheat
↓
Rava's final ruling: It is a mitzva to soak — derived from "guard the matzot" (Exodus 12:17)

🔥 Roasting Stalks — Abaye vs Rava

Abaye
One may not roast two stalks together — liquid may leave one and be absorbed by the other, causing leavening.
Rava
If that is your concern, one should not even roast one stalk alone — liquid at one end could flow and be absorbed at the other end!
Abaye
(Retracts) I was wrong. Something absorbing liquid does not become leavened unless it is fully soaked in water.
Gemara
Support: Abaye himself taught about a soaking jug — partial absorption in transit does not render grain leavened.
Principle Mere absorption of moisture does not cause leavening — full immersion in water is required.

One soaks barley on Passover to remove chaff
↓
Did the grain split?
Yes — visibly split
Prohibited
Leavening has begun
No — intact
Permitted
Swelling but intact

🛡️ Guarding the Matzot — Rava's Final Ruling

The Verse

  • "Guard the matzot" (Exodus 12:17)
  • Heb: ושמרתם את המצות
  • Read as: guard from leavening at the critical moment
  • Debate: does guarding begin at kneading or at harvest?

Rava's Mitzva Ruling

  • Soaking grain is a mitzva, not merely permitted
  • Soaking = active guarding against leavening
  • Rava told field workers: cut grain for the sake of the mitzva
  • Guarding ideally starts at harvest

Mar Son of Ravina

  • His mother placed wheat in a trough from harvest
  • Guarded each grain from the moment of cutting
  • Model of exemplary stringency
  • Demonstrates the ideal fulfillment of shmirat matzot

⛵ The Sunken Wheat — Rava's Evolving Rulings

A boat carrying wheat capsized in the Ḥishta River shortly before Passover — the waterlogged grain now risks leavening
↓
Rava's first ruling: Sell the recovered grain to gentiles before Passover
↓
Objection (Rabba bar Levai): Like a garment with hidden kilayim — selling to a gentile is forbidden if the item may be resold to an unsuspecting Jew
↓
Rava reconsidered: Sell the wheat one kav at a time to different Jews — in small quantities it will be consumed before Passover, eliminating the leavening risk
↓
This solution avoids both the leavening prohibition and the concern of lifnei iver — enabling another's transgression
