Pesachim 39 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Ravina tell Rav Aḥa bar Rava that ḥazeret is the most preferred bitter herb?
A. It is explicitly described as the most bitter in a baraita
B. It is listed first in the mishna, and whatever is listed first is most preferred
C. It is the only species found in all three tannaic lists
D. It is the easiest to obtain in Babylonia
Correct: B — Ravina's argument is simple: whatever the mishna enumerates first is considered most preferred. Ḥazeret heads the list, therefore it is the ideal choice — and its name (ḥassa) also carries the theological meaning of God's mercy (ḥas) toward Israel.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda, to what does the mishna's ruling of 'fresh or dried' apply?
A. To both the leaves and stems equally
B. Only to the leaves of bitter herbs
C. Only to the stems of bitter herbs
D. Only to pickled bitter herbs submerged in vinegar
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda teaches that 'fresh or dried' refers specifically to the stems. Dried leaves lose the taste of bitter herbs and therefore cannot fulfill the obligation — only fresh leaves remain valid.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the baraita's general principle for determining whether a plant may be used as maror?
A. It must be listed explicitly in the Torah by name
B. It must be as intensely bitter as the bile of the kufya fish
C. It must have the taste of bitter herbs and be fit for human consumption
D. It must cause the eyes to water when consumed
Correct: C — The baraita states: 'Anything that has the taste of bitter herbs, one can fulfill his obligation with it; but anything that does not have the taste of bitter herbs, one cannot.' Additionally, it must be fit for human consumption — like matza — which excludes poisonous plants such as harzipu.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rav's ruling about planting multiple bitter herb species together, and what is his reasoning?
A. Forbidden — all bitter herbs are treated as seeds under kilayim law
B. Permitted — all bitter herb species may be planted in one bed, because they are vegetables, not seeds
C. Permitted — but only two species may share a single bed
D. Forbidden — each species requires a full beit rova of separation
Correct: B — Rav rules that all the maror vegetables may be planted in one garden bed. His reasoning: the mishna, Bar Kappara, and the school of Shmuel all use the word 'vegetables' — and vegetable kilayim is governed by less stringent rules than seed kilayim.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the second mishna (39b), what is the correct way to prepare bran for chickens on Passover, and why?
A. Soak it briefly in cold water — less than eighteen minutes prevents leavening
B. Feed it dry without any liquid whatsoever
C. Pour boiling water onto it — scalding briefly prevents leavening, unlike soaking
D. Mix it with salt water, which inhibits the fermentation process
Correct: C — The mishna prohibits soaking coarse bran in water, which could cause it to leaven. The permitted alternative is pouring boiling water onto the bran — the rapid heat treatment denatures the fermenting potential before leavening can occur.
