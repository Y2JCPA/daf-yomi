🔥 May Pure and Impure Leaven Be Burned Together?

Rabbi Meir
Pure teruma leaven and impure teruma leaven may be burned together. I derive this from the testimony of Rabbi Ḥanina the deputy High Priest — or from Rabbi Yehoshua's ruling about the broken barrel in the winepress.
Rabbi Yosei
They may not be burned together. Your proof texts do not apply here — each involves unique circumstances that cannot extend to this case. Burning impure with pure leaven is forbidden.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
The dispute only applies in the sixth hour — when leaven is forbidden rabbinically. By the seventh hour, when Torah law prohibits it, all agree the items must be burned separately.
The Gemara's Quest The Gemara traces exactly which statement Rabbi Meir relies upon, tests whether Rabbi Yosei successfully deflects each proof, and ultimately roots the debate in a deeper dispute: whether liquids transmit ritual impurity by Torah or rabbinic law.

🍷 The Broken Barrel — Rabbi Meir's Proposed Proof Text

A barrel of teruma wine breaks in the upper press — wine threatens to flow down and render the non-sacred grapes impure
↓
Rabbi Eliezer
One must rescue a quarter-log in purity before impurity sets in — but may not actively cause the wine to become impure
Rabbi Yehoshua
One may actively render the remaining wine impure — even though some is still pure — to prevent loss of non-sacred food below
↓
Rabbi Meir's inference: If Rabbi Yehoshua permits actively causing impurity to pure teruma to prevent a loss, surely pure and impure leaven may be burned together
↓
Gemara's difficulty: If so, why does Rabbi Meir say 'from their statements'? Rabbi Eliezer concedes to Rabbi Yehoshua here — Rabbi Eliezer is not really a source at all!

⚔️ Rabbi Yosei Dismantles Each Proposed Source

Rabbi Yosei
If your source is Rabbi Yehoshua (broken barrel) — that case involves potential loss of non-sacred food, a unique factor absent when burning leaven. The cases are not comparable!
Rabbi Yosei
If your source is Rabbi Akiva's testimony about disqualified teruma oil — that involves only rabbinic impurity. You cannot extend it to full Torah-level impurity!
Gemara
But why does Rabbi Yosei respond from Rabbi Ḥanina's statement if Rabbi Meir's source is Rabbi Yehoshua? This proves Rabbi Meir's true source must be Rabbi Ḥanina the deputy High Priest!
Rav Yirmeya
In the mishna too there is loss — of wood! One requires additional wood to burn the impure teruma in a separate fire. So Rabbi Yehoshua's logic should apply equally.
Final Resolution Rabbi Meir's primary source is the testimony of Rabbi Ḥanina the deputy High Priest, who reported that in Jerusalem, meat impure at one degree was burned together with meat impure at another degree.

⚖️ Degrees of Ritual Impurity — What Is the Mishna Discussing?

אב הטומאה — Primary Source (Torah)

  • First-degree ritual impurity
  • Derives directly from Torah law
  • E.g., contact with a corpse or creeping animal
  • Can transmit impurity to people and vessels
  • All agree: do NOT burn together with items of different status

וולד הטומאה — Secondary (Torah)

  • Second-degree impurity by Torah law
  • Derives from contact with a primary source
  • Reish Lakish / Rav Naḥman: mishna discusses these two
  • This is the core machloket: burn together?
  • Rabbi Meir: YES. Rabbi Yosei: NO.

וולד הטומאה — Secondary (Rabbinic)

  • Second-degree impurity by rabbinic law only
  • Rabbi Yoḥanan: mishna discusses Torah primary + rabbinic secondary
  • Even Rabbi Yosei agrees these may be burned together
  • Piggul and notar: rabbinically impure — all agree burn together
  • Dispute only applies to full Torah-level impurity objects

💧 The Deep Root: Do Liquids Transmit Torah-Level Impurity?

טומאת משקין לטמא אחרים — מדרבנן (רבי מאיר) | מדאורייתא (רבי יוסי)
עדות רבי חנינא סגן הכהנים — Temple Testimony & the Liquids Dispute Rabbi Ḥanina the deputy High Priest testified: In Jerusalem, meat that became impure via a secondary source was burned together with meat impure via a primary source.

How did the meat become impure? Through liquids that touched a creeping animal — those liquids then rendered the meat a secondary source of impurity.

Rabbi Meir: Liquids transmit impurity only by rabbinic law → meat is only rabbinically impure → may burn together with Torah-impure meat ✓

Rabbi Yosei: Liquids transmit impurity by Torah law → meat is Torah-impure at second degree → cannot burn together with first-degree Torah-impure meat ✗

This dispute about liquids (mashkin) is the hidden engine driving the entire machloket about leaven.
