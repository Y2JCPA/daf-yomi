Pesachim 15 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, during which hour do Rabbi Meir and Rabbi Yosei dispute whether pure and impure leaven may be burned together?
A. The fifth hour, when leaven is still fully permitted
B. The sixth hour, when leaven is prohibited by rabbinic decree
C. The seventh hour, when leaven is prohibited by Torah law
D. The fourth hour, when one first separates leaven from non-leaven
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan teaches that the dispute between Rabbi Meir and Rabbi Yosei applies specifically to the sixth hour — when leaven carries a rabbinic prohibition. By the seventh hour, when Torah law prohibits leaven, all authorities agree the items must be burned separately.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rabbi Meir's ultimate proof source for permitting pure and impure leaven to be burned together?
A. Rabbi Eliezer's ruling on rescuing teruma wine from a broken barrel
B. Rabbi Akiva's testimony about kindling disqualified teruma oil in an impure lamp
C. The testimony of Rabbi Ḥanina the deputy High Priest about Temple burning practice
D. A ruling by Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi permitting the burning of piggul with notar
Correct: C — The Gemara concludes that Rabbi Meir's true source is Rabbi Ḥanina the deputy High Priest, who testified that in Jerusalem, meat impure via a secondary source was burned together with meat impure via a primary source — demonstrating that items of different impurity levels may be burned together.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the foundational dispute between Rabbi Meir and Rabbi Yosei regarding liquids (משקין) that became impure through a creeping animal?
A. Whether such liquids can become ritually impure at all under Torah law
B. Whether liquids transmit ritual impurity to food by Torah law or only by rabbinic law
C. Whether impure liquids must be discarded immediately or may be stored temporarily
D. Whether the impurity of liquids applies only inside Jerusalem or throughout Israel
Correct: B — Rabbi Meir holds that liquids transmit impurity to food only rabbinically, so meat rendered impure through such liquids is only rabbinically impure — permitting it to be burned with Torah-impure items. Rabbi Yosei holds liquids transmit impurity by Torah law, so the meat is Torah-impure and may not be burned together with Torah-impure items of a different degree.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the broken barrel case, what does Rabbi Yehoshua permit that Rabbi Eliezer forbids?
A. Rescuing a quarter-log of teruma wine before it becomes impure
B. Actively rendering the remaining pure teruma wine impure to prevent loss of non-sacred produce
C. Declaring the teruma wine ownerless to avoid liability for its impurity
D. Selling the potentially impure wine to a non-Jew before Shabbat
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehoshua permits actively causing the remaining pure teruma wine to become impure in order to prevent the non-sacred grapes below from being ruined. Rabbi Eliezer permits only rescuing a quarter-log in purity — he does not allow actively causing impurity even to prevent a loss.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say piggul and notar (forbidden sacrificial flesh) support Rabbi Yoḥanan's view — but then reject this proof?
A. Support: they are burned together. Rejection: piggul and notar are Torah-impure, so this proves Torah-level items can be combined
B. Support: they are burned together. Rejection: piggul and notar are only rabbinically impure, so this does not prove Torah-level items can be combined
C. Support: they are burned separately. Rejection: they are not really leaven and the comparison fails
D. Support: Rabbi Yosei permits burning them together. Rejection: Rabbi Meir disputes this ruling
Correct: B — The Gemara initially suggests that the practice of burning piggul and notar together supports Rabbi Yoḥanan's claim that even Rabbi Yosei permits burning together Torah-prohibited items. The Gemara rejects this: piggul and notar carry only rabbinic impurity, so Rabbi Yosei naturally permits combining them — this says nothing about items that carry full Torah-level impurity.
