Pesachim 97 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel's principle, what is the status of a Paschal lamb that falls into the same category as a sin-offering that would be put in isolation to die?
A. It is left to graze until it becomes blemished
B. It is sacrificed as a peace-offering
C. It is destroyed immediately without benefit
D. It must be redeemed with money
Correct: B — Shmuel states that a Paschal lamb in the 'sin-offering that would be isolated to die' category is sacrificed as a peace-offering — it retains its sanctity and is offered, just not as a Paschal lamb.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was Rav Yosef's main objection to Shmuel's principle?
A. An overaged sin-offering is left to graze — not die — contradicting the principle
B. Shmuel conflated Paschal lambs with burnt-offerings
C. The principle ignores the requirement for a male animal
D. Shmuel's ruling contradicts an explicit verse in the Torah
Correct: A — Rav Yosef pointed out that an overaged sin-offering becomes unfit but is merely left to graze — not to die. This undermines the equation Shmuel drew between such sin-offerings and the Paschal lamb's fate.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When is a lost Paschal lamb that is found before midday on 14 Nisan NOT sacrificed as a peace-offering?
A. When it was found after the replacement was slaughtered
B. When the replacement had not yet been slaughtered
C. When the owner had separated two replacements
D. When the original animal had a blemish
Correct: B — If the original Paschal lamb is found before midday AND before the replacement was slaughtered, it is left to die — the replacement can still serve as the Paschal offering, so the original has no valid ritual role.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, what happens to a lost sin-offering?
A. It is left to graze and eventually sold for its value
B. It is sacrificed as a burnt-offering
C. It is isolated and left to die
D. It is redeemed and the money used for a new offering
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi holds that every lost sin-offering is placed in isolation and left to die. He makes no exceptions for types of lost sin-offerings, unlike his position on deferred offerings (which graze).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Mishna at the end of Daf 97, what happens to a female animal designated as a Paschal lamb?
A. It is sacrificed immediately as a peace-offering
B. It is left to graze until it becomes blemished, then sold for a peace-offering
C. It is burned as an invalid offering
D. It must be returned to its owner without any further use
Correct: B — Since the animal has Paschal sanctity but cannot be offered as such (wrong sex), it is left to graze. Once it acquires a blemish, it is sold and the proceeds are used to purchase a valid peace-offering.
