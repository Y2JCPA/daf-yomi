⚔️ Shmuel vs. Rav Yosef — Scope of the Principle

Shmuel
Any consecrated animal that becomes unfit — such that a sin-offering in its condition would be put in isolation to die — the Paschal lamb among these is sacrificed as a peace-offering.
Rav Yosef
Is this an absolute principle? What about an overaged sin-offering? It is unfit for its purpose yet is merely left to graze — not die! Your equation breaks down here!
Shmuel (clarified)
My principle applies only to Paschal lambs that were lost — not to those that were deferred due to unfitness. The two situations are fundamentally distinct.
Core Issue Does a Paschal lamb in a 'sin-offering that would die' category always become a peace-offering, or are there exceptions for deferred vs. lost cases?

🏛️ Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi vs. Rabbi Shimon — Fate of Lapsed Offerings

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi

  • Every lost sin-offering → isolated to die
  • A deferred sin-offering (e.g., overaged) → left to graze
  • Exception (R' Oshaya): two offerings separated as guarantee — second may graze
  • Shmuel aligns with this view for lost Paschal lambs

Rabbi Shimon

  • Any sin-offering deferred for any reason → isolated to die
  • Does not concede that a deferred sin-offering ever grazes
  • Shmuel cannot align with Rabbi Shimon — Shmuel refers to animals that graze
  • Rabbi Shimon's stricter standard collapses the lost/deferred distinction

📜 Mishna — Invalid Animals Designated as Paschal Lambs

הַמַּפְרִישׁ נְקֵבָה לְפִסְחוֹ, אוֹ זָכָר בֶּן שְׁתֵּי שָׁנִים — יִרְעֶה עַד שֶׁיִּסְתָּאֵב וְיִמָּכֵר
מִשְׁנָה If one separates a female animal for his Paschal offering, or a male in its second year — both are unfit for a Paschal lamb (which must be a first-year male). Such an animal is left to graze until it acquires a blemish, at which point it is sold and the proceeds used for a peace-offering.

Paschal offering requirements: זָכָר (male) · בֶּן שָׁנָה (first year) · תָּמִים (unblemished). Any deviation disqualifies it from Pesach use entirely.
