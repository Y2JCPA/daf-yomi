⚖️ Teruma Repayment: Measure or Monetary Value?

Rav Yosef
Repay by measure — return the same quantity of produce as was consumed, regardless of price changes
Abaye
Repay by monetary value — return the worth of the teruma at the time it was consumed
The Critical Case Teruma worth 1 zuz when eaten, but 4 zuz at repayment — does the non-priest pay the original 1 zuz or today's 4 zuz?

🌾 Dried Figs & Dates: Rav Yosef's Proof

Non-priest eats teruma dried figs (cheap, undesirable commodity)
↓
He pays the priest with dates (worth more)
↓
Baraita: "A blessing rest upon him!" — why the praise?
Rav Yosef
Repayment is by measure — dates are larger than figs, so he overpaid in quantity → deserves a blessing
Abaye's counter
Repayment is by value — blessing is because dried figs are undesirable to buy; he spared the priest from dealing in a poor commodity

🔥 Chametz Teruma on Passover: Three Tannaitic Positions

Rabbi Akiva

  • Impure teruma all year: liable to pay
  • Chametz teruma on Passover: exempt
  • Reason: chametz is forbidden for any benefit — the priest receives nothing of value

Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov

  • Agrees with Rabbi Akiva's conclusion
  • Impure teruma ≠ chametz teruma on Passover
  • When priest cannot benefit at all, no payment obligation arises

Rabbi Yoḥanan ben Nuri

  • Chametz teruma on Passover: liable to pay
  • Treats it like impure teruma — obligation persists
  • Does not hold that deriving benefit from chametz is fully forbidden

📅 Rabbi Neḥunya ben HaKana's Ruling

קִם לֵיהּ בִּדְרַבָּה מִינֵּיהּ — the greater punishment absorbs the lesser
Yom Kippur = Shabbat for Monetary Liability Rabbi Neḥunya holds that one who violates Yom Kippur is exempt from monetary payments, just as one who violates Shabbat is exempt. The principle: when a person incurs a death penalty or karet, the monetary liability is subsumed — he cannot be punished twice. This exempts the Yom Kippur violator from paying for teruma he consumed on that day.

🏛️ Abba Shaul vs. First Tanna: One Condition or Two?

First Tanna
One condition: ate an olive-bulk of teruma → liable to pay principal and fifth, regardless of its monetary worth
Abba Shaul
Two conditions required: (1) at least an olive-bulk in quantity, AND (2) worth at least one peruta. Both must be met — otherwise exempt
Abba Shaul's Source — Leviticus 22:14 "And he shall give to the priest the sacred item" — the word 'give' implies a minimum legally significant amount: one peruta. Giving less than a peruta is not halakhically considered giving at all.
