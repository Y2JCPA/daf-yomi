QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Rav Yosef reject Rav Huna's proof that matza can be a handbreadth thick?
A. The shewbread was only half a handbreadth thick, not a full one
B. Shewbread was made by expert priests using dry summer wood — conditions ordinary households cannot replicate
C. The Talmud never permits any thick bread on Passover under any circumstances
D. A handbreadth thickness is only permitted for Shabbat challah, not Passover matza
Correct: B — Rav Yosef argues that the Temple shewbread's conditions were exceptional: diligent priests and dry summer wood prevented leavening despite the thickness. Ordinary household Passover baking cannot assume these same conditions.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the halakhic status of figured matza (matza shaped in decorative forms)?
A. Completely and unconditionally forbidden on Passover
B. Fully permitted and even encouraged for children to increase joy
C. Forbidden ab initio due to delay risk, but fulfills the obligation after the fact
D. Permitted only according to Beit Shammai, not Beit Hillel
Correct: C — The Sages prohibited baking figured matza l'khatchila because shaping the dough causes delay that risks leavening. However, b'di'avad — after the fact — it fulfills the Passover matza obligation.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rav Yehuda citing Shmuel define 'half-baked matza' that is valid for Passover?
A. Matza that is exactly 50% of the full required thickness
B. Matza baked for half the time it would normally take in the oven
C. Matza sufficiently baked so that when broken, no strands of dough emerge from its sides
D. Matza that has been salted but not yet fully dried
Correct: C — Rav Yehuda said in the name of Shmuel: half-baked matza means any matza baked sufficiently so that when it is broken, no threads or strands of dough emerge. This is the minimum standard for the Passover obligation.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What Biblical verse does Rava use to explain Rabbi Yehuda's position that only oven-baked dough is classified as 'bread' for ḥalla purposes?
A. The verse about shewbread preparation in Leviticus 24
B. "Ten women shall bake your bread in one oven" (Leviticus 26:26)
C. The law of firstfruits offerings in Deuteronomy 26
D. The verse about the thanks-offering loaves in Leviticus 7
Correct: B — Rava explains Rabbi Yehuda's view from Leviticus 26:26: 'ten women shall bake your bread in one oven' — the word 'oven' teaches that legally significant 'bread' requiring ḥalla separation must be baked in an oven.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the unusual feature of the Beit Shammai / Beit Hillel dispute regarding me'isa and ḥalita?
A. Both schools agree that both types are entirely exempt from ḥalla
B. Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel reverse their typical strict/lenient positions between the two cases
C. The dispute applies only on Passover, not to ḥalla separation year-round
D. They agree on the law but disagree on the definitions of the terms
Correct: B — Beit Shammai (usually strict) exempts me'isa but obligates ḥalita, while Beit Hillel (usually lenient) obligates me'isa but exempts ḥalita. Their rulings flip between the two cases, which the Gemara notes as requiring explanation.
