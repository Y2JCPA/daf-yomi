QUESTION 1 OF 5
The shewbread (lechem hapanim) may be eaten no sooner than how many days after it was baked?
A. Seven days
B. Eight days
C. Nine days
D. Eleven days
Correct: C — Nine days is the minimum: baked on Friday (erev Shabbat) and eaten the following Shabbat. The maximum is eleven days, when a two-day Festival precedes Shabbat.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda, when does baking on a Festival override the rabbinic prohibition against Festival labor?
A. For any Shabbat falling within the same month
B. For the Shabbat immediately following the Festival (proximate Shabbat only)
C. Whenever there are insufficient loaves from the previous week
D. Only on Yom Kippur when the Temple service requires it
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda rules that the override applies only for the proximate (immediately following) Shabbat. A Shabbat separated from the Festival by even one weekday cannot benefit from this dispensation.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
From which verse does the Gemara derive that baking the two Shavuot loaves on a Festival is permitted even though they may not be eaten until the following day?
A. Leviticus 23:17 — 'you shall bring two loaves of fine flour'
B. Deuteronomy 16:3 — 'bread of affliction'
C. Exodus 12:16 — 'save that which every man must eat, that only may be done for you'
D. Ezekiel 45:15 — 'one lamb out of two hundred from the pastures of Israel'
Correct: C — Exodus 12:16 permits Festival baking for Temple offerings consumed later. The phrase 'for you' (לָכֶם) additionally indicates the dispensation is for human benefit — Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel uses this to limit its scope.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When Abaye challenges Rabba's list of five prohibitions violated by cooking sciatic nerve in milk on a Festival, what does Rabba ultimately add in place of 'kindling a fire'?
A. Eating on Yom Kippur (a fast day)
B. Violating the Sabbatical year (Shemitah)
C. Using wood that was set aside (muktzeh) from Festival use
D. Mixing species of draft animals
Correct: C — Rabba removes 'kindling fire' (transferring fire is generally permitted on Yom Tov) and substitutes the prohibition of benefiting from muktzeh wood. This is derived from 'prepare' (הכינו) in Exodus 16:5, implying items not designated for Yom Tov use are Torahtically set aside.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The case of eight Torah prohibitions from a single plowed furrow requires which combination of factors?
A. Shabbat, Yom Kippur, diverse seeds, and two ordinary Israelites plowing together
B. Shemitah, Yom Tov, kilayim, and a kohen-nazirite plowing over a burial site
C. A nazirite plowing on Passover with an ox and donkey yoked together
D. Shemitah, kilayim, and a kohen plowing his own field on a Festival
Correct: B — The case requires: Sabbatical year (Shemitah) + Festival (Yom Tov) + diverse seeds/vineyard (kilayim) + mixed plow animals + a kohen (two impurity prohibitions) who is also a nazirite (two impurity prohibitions) plowing a burial site — combining temporal, agricultural, and personal-status violations.
