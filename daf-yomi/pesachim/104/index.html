⚖️ How to Conclude Havdala

Rav
Conclude with: "Who sanctifies Israel" — the blessing focuses on Israel's distinct sanctity
Shmuel
Conclude with: "Who distinguishes between sacred and profane" — emphasizing the act of separation itself
R. Yehoshua ben Ḥananya
Use the combined formula: "Who sanctifies Israel and distinguishes between sacred and profane" — God will lengthen his days and years!
Halakha The halakha follows Shmuel: conclude with מַבְדִּיל בֵּין קֹדֶשׁ לְחוֹל — "Who distinguishes between sacred and profane"

🔢 Range of Havdala Distinctions

Minimum: 3

  • Between sacred and profane
  • Between light and darkness
  • Between Israel and the nations

Maximum: 7

  • Sacred and profane
  • Light and darkness
  • Israel and the nations
  • Seventh day vs. six workdays
  • Priests vs. Levites
  • Levites vs. Israelites
  • Pure and impure

Rabbi Elazar / Rabbi Oshaya

  • Do not decrease below three
  • Do not increase above seven
  • "Son of sacred ones" (Kohen) — says only one
  • People customarily say three

📜 Torah-Based vs. Extended Distinctions

R. Yehoshua ben Levi
Recite only distinctions found explicitly in the Torah — do not invent or add new distinctions beyond what Scripture states
Baraita (objection)
Lists an elaborate havdala order including distinctions like pure vs. impure and sea vs. dry land — seemingly beyond Torah text
Gemara (resolution)
"Between the seventh day and the six workdays" is not a standalone distinction — it merely mirrors the blessing's conclusion per the rule that blessings end similarly to how they begin
Key Point Priests/Levites/Israelites counts as TWO distinctions: (1) Levites from Israelites and (2) Priests from Levites — each grounded in a separate Torah verse

🙏 Blessing Structure: The Baruch Principle

כָּל הַבְּרָכוֹת כֻּלָּן פּוֹתֵחַ בְּ"בָּרוּךְ" וְחוֹתֵם בְּ"בָּרוּךְ"
Structural Rule Most blessings both begin and end with Baruch. The Gemara identifies key exceptions:

• Blessings over mitzvot — begin with Baruch, do not end with Baruch
• Blessings over fruit/food — begin with Baruch, do not end with Baruch
• Blessings linked to a preceding blessing (סמוכה לחברתה) — end with Baruch, do not begin with Baruch

This structural framework explains why Ulla's havdala formula is challenged: if it lacks an opening Baruch, is it properly structured as a standalone blessing?
