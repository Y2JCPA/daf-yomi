🏕️ Who Is Sent Out from the Camp?

Verse (Num. 5:2)
Send out from the camp any leper, any zav, and anyone impure by reason of a corpse.
Question
Why list all three? Could we not derive the inclusion of lepers and corpse-impurity from a kal v'chomer?
Rabbi Shimon
From a single verse we derive three camps: lepers sent from all three; zavim from two; those impure by a corpse from one only.
Gemara
The inclusive words 'and any zav' and 'anyone impure' extend to a ba'al keri (seminal emission) and those impure from a creeping animal.
Core Principle Each impurity type has a distinct level of severity, determining from which camp(s) it is excluded — and whether the bearer is deferred to Pesach Sheni or excluded entirely.

🐑 Paschal Lamb in Impurity: Who Is Deferred?

Paschal lamb offered in a state of communal impurity (14 Nisan)
↓
Majority / Entire Community
Bring Pesach on 14 Nisan in impurity — not deferred to Pesach Sheni
Individual / Minority
Deferred to Pesach Sheni (14 Iyyar) — but only if corpse-impure
↓
Abaye's conclusion: 'By reason of a corpse' — only corpse-impure individuals are deferred. Zavim and lepers are excluded entirely and cannot bring the Paschal lamb even when the community brings it in impurity.

🗣️ Abaye Challenges the Verse — Step by Step

Abaye (Challenge 1)
If the verse need only mention corpse-impurity, why also mention lepers? I could derive their exclusion myself with a kal v'chomer!
Counter-question
Does this mean a zav may also participate when Pesach is brought in impurity? But the mishna explicitly says zavim and zavot do not bring it!
Abaye (Resolution)
The phrase 'by reason of a corpse' teaches: it is only corpse-impurity that is deferred to Pesach Sheni — not zavim or lepers, who are barred altogether.
Gemara (adds)
'A man or men' (Num. 9:10) — individuals are deferred, but the entire community is not deferred; they bring Pesach in impurity on 14 Nisan.
Key Distinction Corpse-impurity → deferred to Pesach Sheni (individual only). Zav / Leper → excluded entirely from the Paschal lamb, even during public impurity.

🏕️ Three Camps — Who Is Excluded from Where?

Leper (מְצֹרָע)

  • Sent outside ALL three camps
  • Camp of Divine Presence ✗
  • Camp of Levites ✗
  • Camp of Israel ✗
  • Must let hair grow, rend garments
  • Prohibited from marital relations
  • Must dwell alone — even from other impure people

Zav / Zavah (זָב / זָבָה)

  • Sent outside TWO camps
  • Camp of Divine Presence ✗
  • Camp of Levites ✗
  • Camp of Israel ✓
  • Impurity issues from the body
  • Ba'al keri included via 'and any zav'
  • Cannot bring Paschal lamb even in public impurity

Corpse-Impure (טְמֵא מֵת)

  • Sent outside ONE camp
  • Camp of Divine Presence ✗
  • Camp of Levites ✓
  • Camp of Israel ✓
  • A corpse itself may enter the Levite camp
  • Individual deferred to Pesach Sheni
  • Community brings Pesach in impurity normally

🧍 Rav Ḥisda: The Leper Dwells Alone

בָּדָד יֵשֵׁב — 'He shall dwell alone' (ויקרא יג:מו)
Rav Ḥisda's Ruling 'He shall dwell in isolation' (Lev. 13:46) — the leper must dwell alone, without even the company of other impure people (e.g., zavim or zavot).

Reasoning: Unlike zavim, who were excluded for the same class of impurity, the leper occupies a uniquely severe status — isolated even from the otherwise-excluded.

Tannaitic dispute: One baraita supports Rav Ḥisda (leper dwells alone); another holds that zavim may dwell with the leper outside the camp. The Gemara resolves this as a genuine dispute between tanna'im.
