🕯️ Why Does 'Oto' Exclude Lamps from Early Kindling?

Baraita
The Paschal lamb is sacrificed after the afternoon Tamid — derived from the verse: the Tamid is described only as 'in the afternoon,' while the Paschal lamb is described as both 'in the evening' and 'in the afternoon.' The one with a later time designation comes later.
Questioner
If so, by the same logic shouldn't the afternoon incense and lamp-lighting also precede the Paschal lamb — since they too are not described as 'in the evening'?
Answer
The Torah writes אוֹתוֹ ('it') about the lamps — emphasizing that the lamps alone are kindled last. This word explicitly excludes early kindling.
Follow-up
But then what does 'in the afternoon he shall burn it' add? If 'oto' already fixes the order, why state the time at all?
Resolution
That phrase does not set order — it teaches that the afternoon incense is performed contemporaneously with the lamp-lighting. The two rites are juxtaposed in timing, not sequenced by this verse.
Key Word אוֹתוֹ (oto) — 'it' — the lamps are kindled last among all Temple rites

🔧 Mechussar Kippurim — Can Atonement Be Completed After the Tamid?

First Tanna
Only one lacking atonement on Passover eve may sacrifice after the afternoon Tamid — the positive mitzva of eating the Paschal lamb overrides the normal prohibition.
R. Yishmael b. R. Yoḥanan
Even on other days of the year, one who lacks atonement may sacrifice after the afternoon Tamid — he immerses and eats sacrificial food that evening.
Gemara
But the guilt-offering requires a sheep! How can completion (mitzva of priestly eating) be fulfilled if a sheep is sacrificed so late?
Ravina (Rav Ḥisda)
We speak of a poor leper whose sin-offering is a bird. Bird sin-offerings are entirely consumed on the altar — no priestly meat-eating is required, so completion is immediate.
Rav Pappa
Even with an animal sin-offering: place the fats on top of the altar overnight, and priests eat the meat before morning. This satisfies mitzva of completion — since it cannot be done otherwise, we treat the fats as if they became impure.
Rava
The leper's burnt-offering is categorically different. The verse says 'the priest shall offer the burnt-offering' — implying it was already offered. The leper achieves atonement even if the standard order (sin-offering before burnt-offering) is reversed.
Core Issue Can post-Tamid offerings achieve full kappara before nightfall on Passover eve?

🌙 Contradictions About Fat Remaining Overnight — Resolved

Rav Kahana raises: Two verses contradict each other about fat remaining overnight
↓
Exodus 23:18
Fat of My offering may not remain overnight until morning — implies fat must be fully burned before dawn
Leviticus 6:2
The burnt-offering may remain on the altar all night until morning — implies overnight presence is permitted
↓
Rav Kahana resolves: Exodus 23:18 speaks of fats that were left before the morning Tamid (must be burned by then); Leviticus 6:2 speaks of fats left after the morning Tamid (may remain all night) — two different periods
↓
Rav Safra raises a parallel contradiction about the Paschal lamb's fat before Rav — Rav invokes Rav Abba bar Ḥiyya's answer before Rabbi Abbahu
↓
Rav's Answer
Exodus 23:18 is written exclusively for 14 Nisan falling on Shabbat — Paschal fats are deferred to Sunday; the verse warns they must not survive to Sunday morning
Challenge
Why limit the verse to that rare case? Rav: the verse's novel concern (Shabbat deferral) drives its language; the general rule is preserved by Leviticus 6

🎯 Mishna: Intent (כַּוָּנָה) for the Paschal Lamb

Valid — לִשְׁמָהּ

  • Slaughtered explicitly for the sake of the Paschal lamb
  • Correct intent maintained through all four rites
  • Owners fulfil their Passover obligation
  • May eat the lamb at the Seder that night

Invalid — שֶׁלֹּא לִשְׁמָהּ

  • Slaughtered with intent as a peace-offering or burnt-offering
  • Wrong intent at slaughter invalidates the sacrifice
  • Owners do NOT fulfil their obligation
  • Must bring a make-up offering on Pesach Sheni

Mixed — לִשְׁמָהּ וְשֶׁלֹּא לִשְׁמָהּ

  • Priest declares: 'for Paschal lamb AND peace-offering'
  • Rav Pappa's dilemma: one rite with dual intent, or separate rites?
  • If one rite → follows Rabbi Yosei (first expression binding)
  • If two rites → may follow Rabbi Meir's framework
