Pesachim 84 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What penalty does one incur for breaking a bone of a ritually PURE Paschal lamb?
A. Karet (spiritual excision)
B. 40 lashes
C. No penalty — only a rabbinic prohibition
D. A monetary fine to the Temple
Correct: B — The mishna rules explicitly: one who breaks a bone of a ritually pure Paschal lamb receives 40 lashes for violating the Torah prohibition 'you shall not break a bone in it.'
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava, how does the principle of edible parts transfer from an adult ox to a young kid?
A. Parts edible raw in an ox may be eaten raw in a kid
B. Parts edible by cooking in an ox may be eaten by roasting in a kid
C. Any part of an ox may be eaten in a kid by any means
D. Only parts the kohen approves transfer between animals
Correct: B — Rava explains that the mishna equates the cooking method in an ox with roasting in a kid — ends of ribs and cartilage, edible by cooking in an ox, are therefore edible by roasting in the Paschal lamb.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was Rabbi Yoḥanan's FINAL ruling on registering for sinews that will eventually harden?
A. One may register for them since they are currently soft
B. One may NOT register for them
C. It depends on whether they harden before or after Passover
D. Only the owner, not guests, may register for them
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan initially permitted registering for such sinews but later retracted his opinion, as reported by Rabbi Abbahu. The final ruling aligns with Reish Lakish: one may NOT register.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does one who LEAVES OVER part of a pure Paschal lamb NOT receive lashes, even though breaking a bone does incur lashes?
A. Leaving over is permitted if there were too many registered members
B. It violates a prohibition with no action (לאו שאין בו מעשה)
C. The Torah only punishes leaving over an impure offering
D. One receives lashes only if the leftover is more than an olive-bulk
Correct: B — Rabbi Ya'akov explains that leaving over meat is a passive transgression — the violation consists of failing to eat, not performing an act. Torah lashes apply only to active violations.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Ravina, why is breaking a bone exempt when there is no olive-bulk of meat AT THE BREAKING SITE specifically?
A. The olive-bulk must exist somewhere on the entire animal
B. We require the minimum edible measure at the precise location of the break
C. Without an olive-bulk the bone is considered already disqualified
D. Ravina only exempts if the entire limb lacks an olive-bulk
Correct: B — Ravina rules that the minimum edible measure (olive-bulk) must be present at the exact site where the bone is broken. Even if olive-bulk exists elsewhere on the limb, the prohibition does not apply at a spot that lacks it.
