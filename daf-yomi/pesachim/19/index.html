⚖️ The Great Impurity Debate: How Far Does It Spread?

Rabbi Yosei
Non-sacred (ḥullin) objects can assume third-degree impurity status — I derive this via an a fortiori argument from teruma.
Rabbi Akiva
I do not hold that view. However, I accept that teruma may reach the 4th degree, and kodashim the 4th degree — but there is NO 5th degree for consecrated items.
Rav Kahana
There must be at least one Tanna who teaches 4th degree for teruma and 5th degree for kodashim — otherwise why state their laws separately?
Rav Ashi
After analysis of the baraita, Rabbi Akiva himself confirms: yes to 4th degree for kodashim, no to a 5th degree. His testimony is decisive.
Bottom Line Rabbi Akiva caps impurity: Kodashim = 4th degree ✓ | 5th degree ✗

📊 Impurity Degrees by Category of Food/Object

Ḥullin (Non-Sacred)

  • Standard ruling: up to 2nd degree
  • Rabbi Yosei: up to 3rd degree
  • Source: Lev. 7:19 — 'any impure thing shall not be eaten'
  • Lowest stringency in the chain

Teruma (Priestly Portion)

  • Standard ruling: up to 3rd degree
  • Debate: possibly extends to 4th degree
  • More stringent than non-sacred food
  • Serves as a fortiori basis for impurity reasoning

Kodashim (Consecrated Items)

  • Rabbi Akiva: up to 4th degree ✓
  • 5th degree explicitly rejected ✗
  • Applies to: fine flour, incense, frankincense, coals
  • Joining in one vessel: impure by rabbinic law

🔪 Anatomy of the Temple Needle Case

Needle found in Temple meat
A metal utensil that became impure from a corpse — it is an av ha-tuma (primary impurity)
↓
Knife cuts the meat that contains the needle
Does the knife become ritually impure?
↓
Did knife touch the meat?
Meat from a living animal has no impurity — it cannot transmit impurity to the knife
Did knife touch the needle?
Needle was inside the meat — no direct contact with the knife
↓
✅ Knife is pure!
Not because of a Temple exemption — the knife simply never touched anything impure

⚡ Uncertain Impurity: Public vs. Private Domain

The Core Rule: When there is doubt (safek) about ritual impurity, the domain in which the doubt arose determines the outcome.
↓
Private Domain (reshut ha-yaḥid): Uncertainty → ruled IMPURE
Precedent: the Sotah (Numbers 5) — doubt in private = impure
↓
Public Domain (reshut ha-rabbim): Uncertainty → ruled PURE
Precedent: nine frogs / one sheretz — doubt in public = pure
↓
Rav Ashi's Ruling: The Temple courtyard is classified as a public domain for these purposes — uncertainty there = pure.
↓
⚠️ The 'Knowledge' Exception: In private domain, a person can be questioned (they have daat). A needle has no knowledge and cannot be asked — yet its doubt in public domain still = pure.
↓
Conclusion: Lost needle recovered in the Temple courtyard → pure, because the Temple courtyard is a public domain for safek tuma rulings.
