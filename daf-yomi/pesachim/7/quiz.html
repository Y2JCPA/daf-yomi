QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why can one not nullify chametz during the sixth hour while burning it?
A. Chametz must be burned before any declaration is made
B. The Sages' prohibition makes chametz legally inaccessible for nullification at that point
C. Bitul chametz is only valid at night, not during the day
D. One must own chametz worth a perutah for bitul to be effective
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that since the Sages prohibited all benefit from chametz in the sixth hour, the chametz loses the legal standing needed for bitul. One cannot nullify something one can no longer legally 'own' or transact with.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rav's ruling about betrothal with sixth-hour chametz prove about rabbinic law?
A. That chametz has no monetary value whatsoever in the sixth hour
B. That one may use chametz for mitzvot even after it is forbidden
C. That the Sages were stringent — rabbinically-forbidden chametz retains legal existence even while its use is blocked
D. That betrothal is always invalid when performed with forbidden items
Correct: C — Rav's teaching demonstrates that the Sages were stringent about rabbinically-forbidden chametz: the woman's betrothal is valid (chametz has enough legal status), yet one cannot freely dispose of it — this paradox is exactly what blocks bitul.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov, what is the situation described in the study hall baraita?
A. A student whose chametz has already leavened and must run home immediately
B. A student who can nullify the dough mentally because it has not yet become chametz
C. A teacher who forgot to search his house the previous night
D. A student who may skip class to burn chametz on Erev Pesach
Correct: B — Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov clarifies that the baraita describes kneaded dough not yet leavened. The student can nullify it mentally from the study hall. The baraita's phrasing 'sitting in the study hall' is precise — it implies the dough is still in a nullifiable state.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Rav Pappi and the opposing view on the blessing for bedikat chametz?
A. Whether a blessing is required at all before searching for chametz
B. Whether the blessing should be recited by the homeowner or a designated agent
C. Whether to say עַל בִּיעוּר חָמֵץ (concerning elimination) or לְבַעֵר חָמֵץ (to eliminate)
D. Whether children are obligated to recite the blessing before searching
Correct: C — Rav Pappi holds 'al biur chametz' — the 'al' prefix indicates an act already in progress (searching initiates the elimination process). The alternative view prefers 'l'vaer chametz,' the standard forward-looking formula recited immediately before performing a mitzva.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
By what method does the Gemara derive that chametz must be searched for specifically by lamplight?
A. A direct verse in the Torah explicitly states 'search with a lamp on the fourteenth'
B. A rabbinic enactment based on the danger of open flames during the night search
C. A three-step chain: gezerah shava linking chametz-finding to Zephaniah, then juxtaposition to lamps, then a derivation of singular lamp from the Menorah verse
D. An analogy to the priestly service, which requires lamplight in the Temple
Correct: C — The Gemara chains three derivations: (1) the root מצא links Exodus 12:19 to Zephaniah 1:12 by gezerah shava; (2) Zephaniah places 'searching' adjacent to 'lamps,' creating a juxtaposition; (3) the plural 'lamps' is reduced to a singular 'lamp' via the Menorah verse — establishing that only a נר (lamp) is valid.
