🕯️ Bedikat Chametz: Law, Logic, and Lamplight

Why not nullify chametz while burning in the sixth hour?
↓
Rabbinic law already prohibits benefit from chametz in the sixth hour — it lacks the legal standing required for nullification
↓
Proof from Rav
One who betroths a woman with 6th-hour chametz — betrothal is valid, showing rabbinic ownership exists but benefit is blocked
Study Hall Case
A student can nullify dough mentally from afar — but only if it has not yet become chametz
↓
Search must be performed before chametz becomes forbidden — and specifically by the light of a lamp (נר), derived via gezerah shava from Exodus and Zephaniah

🚫 Can Bitul Chametz Happen in the Sixth Hour?

Gemara (Question)
Let him simply render the leaven null and void during the sixth hour while burning it — why must burning precede bitul?
Gemara (Answer)
Since the Sages already forbade all benefit from chametz, it is legally as if it does not exist — one cannot nullify what one no longer legally 'owns.'
Rav (via Rav Giddel)
Proof of stringency: if a man betroths a woman with sixth-hour chametz and she accepts it, the betrothal is valid — the Sages treated it as legally real yet prohibited.
Gemara (Conclusion)
This proves the Sages were stringent even about rabbinically-forbidden chametz: legal existence persists (betrothal works), but benefit is blocked — so nullification, which requires 'owning' the item, cannot function.
Core Tension Chametz in the 6th hour occupies a paradox: it has enough legal reality to effect betrothal, yet cannot be freely disposed of — making bitul impossible at that stage.

📚 The Student in the Study Hall — Mental Bitul

A student sitting before his teacher remembers: dough is at home! He fears it will leaven before he can warn his household.
↓
✅ Dough not yet chametz
He may nullify it mentally in his heart from the study hall — bitul works even at a distance, as long as the item remains nullifiable
❌ Dough has already leavened
Once it has become chametz, bitul is impossible — he must physically return and burn it
↓
Linguistic proof: The baraita says 'sitting in the study hall' — this implies the dough has not yet risen. Otherwise going home would be mandatory.

🙏 The Blessing Formula Debate: Al Biur vs. L'vaer

Rav Pappi — עַל בִּיעוּר חָמֵץ

  • Formula: 'Concerning the elimination of leaven'
  • The word al (על) refers to an act already in progress
  • Searching is the beginning of biur — the elimination mitzva has begun
  • Parallel: blessing before circumcision says 'concerning circumcision' (על המילה) — referring to an act performed on another
  • Bedikat chametz sets the stage for biur, so 'al' is appropriate

Alternative — לְבַעֵר חָמֵץ

  • Formula: 'To eliminate leaven'
  • The lamed (ל) prefix indicates the act about to be performed
  • Standard blessing structure: recited immediately before the mitzva
  • Parallel: blessing over shechita says 'to slaughter' (לשחוט) — future-facing
  • Blessings should describe the mitzva one is about to do

🕯️ Why a Lamp? The Three-Step Derivation

שִׁבְעַת יָמִים שְׂאֹר לֹא יִמָּצֵא בְּבָתֵּיכֶם (שמות יב:יט) ⟶ וּפָקַדְתִּי עַל יְרוּשָׁלַיִם בַּנֵּרוֹת (צפניה א:יב)
Gezerah Shava + Juxtaposition Chain Step 1 — Finding → Finding (gezerah shava):
Chametz must not be found in your houses (Exod. 12:19) links to Zephaniah via the root מצא

Step 2 — Searching linked to Lamps (juxtaposition):
'At that time I will search Jerusalem with lamps' (Zeph. 1:12) — searching is adjacent to lamps

Step 3 — Lamps → Lamp (singular, Menorah):
Plural נרות derived from singular נר — just as the Menorah's main light is one lamp, so too the bedika uses a single lamp

Conclusion: Not sunlight, moonlight, or torchlight — only a lamp (נר) is valid for the search
