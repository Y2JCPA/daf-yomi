Pesachim 38 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Meir, why can one NOT fulfill the matza obligation with second-tithe matza?
A. It lacks sufficient guarding with intent for the commandment
B. Second-tithe produce is considered consecrated property
C. It may only be consumed in Jerusalem
D. It is exempt from the challah obligation and therefore invalid
Correct: B — Rabbi Meir holds that second-tithe produce has the status of consecrated property, and consecrated property cannot be used to discharge personal religious obligations such as eating matza on Passover.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rabba's reason why thanks-offering loaves cannot fulfill the matza obligation?
A. They contain oil, making them 'enriched matza' (matza ashira)
B. They may only be eaten in Jerusalem, not in any habitation
C. They were not guarded with the specific intent of fulfilling the matza commandment
D. They were prepared by kohanim, not by the individual Jew
Correct: C — Rabba derives from 'guard the matzot' (Exodus 12:17) that matza must be guarded specifically for the purpose of the commandment. Thanks-offering loaves are guarded for a sacrificial purpose, not for matza.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rav Yosef's reason why thanks-offering loaves cannot fulfill the matza obligation?
A. They are matza ashira — enriched matza containing oil
B. They are restricted to Jerusalem and not permitted in any habitation
C. They were not baked under rabbinic supervision for Passover
D. The merchant who baked them had commercial rather than sacred intent
Correct: B — Rav Yosef derives from 'seven days you shall eat matzot' (Exodus 12:15) that valid matza must be permitted in any habitation. Thanks-offering loaves carry the restriction of being eaten only in Jerusalem.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What practical difference emerges between Rabba and Rav Yosef regarding thanks-offering loaves?
A. Whether second-tithe matza can be used on the first night vs. subsequent nights
B. Whether a nazirite may use his own wafers vs. buying them
C. Whether loaves baked for sale in the market can fulfill the matza obligation
D. Whether the loaves must be baked before or after nightfall on the eve of Passover
Correct: C — Loaves baked for the market were not guarded for the matza commandment (Rabba: invalid), but they are permitted in any habitation since the merchant may change his mind and eat them anywhere (Rav Yosef: valid).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What remarkable claim did Rabbi Eliezer make about the distinction between loaves prepared for oneself versus for the market?
A. He said it was a new rabbinic enactment from the time of Ezra
B. He said it was disputed by the early Tannaim and never resolved
C. He swore by the covenant that it was a halakha transmitted to Moses at Sinai
D. He said it could be derived logically from the phrase 'poor man's bread'
Correct: C — Rabbi Eliezer declared 'by the covenant' — swearing that the distinction between loaves baked for oneself and those baked for the market is a halakha l'Moshe miSinai, a tradition passed down from Moses at Mount Sinai.
