🕯️ Why Search for Chametz at Night?

Why is bedikat chametz performed at night — not the morning?
↓
Proposed: Morning
The vigilant perform mitzvot early — why not search at dawn?
Accepted: Evening
People are home + candlelight reaches crevices best
↓
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak: Evening = people home, lamps lit, searching is effective
↓
Abaye adds: A Torah scholar must not begin a scheduled study session on the eve of the 14th — lest he become absorbed and forget to search

🏠 Who Must Search the Rented House?

Questioner
The lessor should search — the leavened bread belongs to him, and he is the one who will transgress if it remains!
Counter
No — the lessee must search, just as a resident must affix a mezuza, not the owner of the building.
Rav Mesharshiya
Affixing a mezuza is the obligation of the resident — an uninhabited house has no mezuza obligation at all!
Gemara
This comparison holds: just as mezuza follows residence, the obligation to search for chametz falls on whoever dwells in the house.
Conclusion The lessee bears the obligation to search — residence, not ownership, creates the duty

👨‍👩‍👦 Three Halakhot Derived from Rabbi Ḥiyya

Context: Rav arrived in Eretz Yisrael and Rabbi Ḥiyya — his maternal and paternal uncle — was in mourning. He instructed his attendant:
↓
"Remove my shoes" → 1️⃣ A mourner is forbidden to wear shoes (אסור לנעול סנדל)
↓
"Carry my garments after me" → 2️⃣ A mourner's garments are carried by an attendant following behind
↓
"To the bathhouse" → 3️⃣ Details of mourner's conduct in the bathhouse are derived from this phrase
↓
Note: Rav's father Ayevu and mother Imma were both step-siblings of Rabbi Ḥiyya — making Rav nephew on both sides

⏰ Chametz on the 14th — Rabbi Meir vs. Rabbi Yehuda

Rabbi Meir

  • May eat chametz during the first 5 hours
  • Must burn chametz at the start of the 6th hour
  • Two periods: permitted eating, then immediate burning

Rabbi Yehuda

  • May eat chametz during the first 4 hours
  • 5th hour: suspended — neither eat nor burn (תולה)
  • Must burn chametz at the start of the 6th hour
  • Three periods: permitted, suspended, then burning

📜 Abaye: The Seven Days and the Night of the 15th

Verse 1 (Ex. 12:19): "Seven days, leaven shall not be found in your houses"
↓
Does the seven-day prohibition begin with the night of the 15th or from the 14th?
↓
Possibility A
Verse adds night of 15th — elimination begins then
Possibility B
Two verses compared — elimination begins from the 14th
↓
Abaye: Two verses are written and compared — chametz must be eliminated before Passover night; the 14th daytime is when burning occurs
