Pesachim 4 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak, why is bedikat chametz performed at night rather than the morning?
A. Because Scripture explicitly commands a nighttime search
B. Because people are home and candlelight is ideal for searching crevices
C. Because it is forbidden to handle chametz during the day on the 14th
D. Because the vigilant perform mitzvot early and nighttime is considered earliest
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak explains that evening is ideal because people are home and lamps are lit, enabling thorough searching of crevices — not because of the principle of performing mitzvot early.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
When a house is rented on the 14th of Nisan, who is obligated to search for chametz?
A. The lessor (owner), since the chametz legally belongs to him
B. Both lessor and lessee must search together
C. Neither — a rented house on the 14th is exempt
D. The lessee (renter), since the obligation follows residency
Correct: D — Drawing from Rav Mesharshiya's teaching that mezuza is the obligation of the resident, the Gemara concludes that the lessee — as the resident — must search for chametz.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What three halakhot are derived from Rabbi Ḥiyya's instructions to his attendant?
A. Laws of Yom Tov, mourning, and the attendant's duties
B. A mourner must fast, not greet others, and not wear shoes
C. A mourner may not wear shoes, his garments are carried by another, and conduct in the bathhouse
D. Laws of Shabbat restrictions extended to mourners
Correct: C — From "remove my shoes" — mourner may not wear shoes; from "carry my garments after me" — attendant carries them behind; from "to the bathhouse" — details about a mourner's bathhouse conduct.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda, what is the status of chametz during the 5th hour of the 14th of Nisan?
A. It may still be eaten freely
B. It must be burned immediately at the start of the 5th hour
C. It is suspended — one may neither eat it nor is he yet obligated to burn it
D. It must be sold to a non-Jew before the 5th hour ends
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehuda creates a three-part system: permitted (hours 1–4), suspended/תולה (hour 5), and then burning at the start of the 6th hour. The 5th hour is a liminal period of neither eating nor burning.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Abaye rules that a Torah scholar should not begin a regularly scheduled Torah study session on the eve of the 14th of Nisan. Why?
A. Study is prohibited on Erev Pesach by rabbinic decree
B. He must dedicate the entire evening to preparing the Seder
C. He might become absorbed in study and forget to perform bedikat chametz
D. The Haggadah must be reviewed before nightfall
Correct: C — Abaye warns that a scholar engrossed in learning may lose track of time and forget to search for chametz — therefore he should not start his regular study block at that critical time.
