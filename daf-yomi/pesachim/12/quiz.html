Pesachim 12 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, by how much does a person err about time, per the opinion of Rabbi Meir?
A. Half an hour
B. Exactly one full hour
C. Two hours less a bit
D. Three hours less a bit
Correct: C — Rava says per Rabbi Meir a person errs by two hours less a bit — meaning the incident could have occurred up to nearly two hours earlier or later than stated, requiring a corresponding window of protection for conspiring-witness refutation.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does a witness who cannot answer an interrogation (חקירה) have the entire testimony voided?
A. The Torah explicitly requires all seven answers to be complete
B. Without precise time and place, a second pair of witnesses cannot render them conspiring false witnesses
C. The Sages suspected any incomplete witness of lying
D. Examinations and interrogations carry identical legal consequences
Correct: B — Testimony is void when it cannot be rendered as false, conspiring testimony (עדים זוממים). If the time and place are unknown, a second pair cannot testify that the first pair was elsewhere at that exact moment — making refutation impossible and the testimony inadmissible.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rava's final explanation for why Rabbi Yehuda prohibits chametz eating an hour earlier than Rabbi Meir?
A. Common people are less vigilant than witnesses and err more about time
B. Rabbi Yehuda holds that only burning is a valid method of chametz removal
C. It is a rabbinic decree for cloudy days when the sun's position cannot be seen
D. Rabbi Yehuda follows a personal stringency passed down from his teachers
Correct: C — After Ravina challenged the 'burning only' explanation from a baraita, Rava revised his position: the earlier deadline is a rabbinic decree for יום המעונן (a cloudy day), when one cannot observe the sun and might mistake the time of the 6th hour.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the corrected baraita (as amended by Rav Pappa), which hour is the standard mealtime for all people?
A. The 3rd hour
B. The 4th hour
C. The 5th hour
D. The 6th hour
Correct: B — Rav Pappa reverses the baraita's original ordering of the 4th, 5th, and 6th hours: the 4th hour is mealtime for all people, the 5th for workers, and the 6th for Torah scholars. From the 7th hour onward, eating yields no benefit.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If one witness testifies that an incident occurred before sunrise and another says it occurred at sunrise, what is the halakhic status of their combined testimony?
A. Valid — the difference is negligible and people commonly confuse pre-dawn with dawn
B. Void — since people do not err between night and day, the contradiction cannot be reconciled
C. Disputed — Abaye and Rava disagree specifically about this case
D. Valid — this falls under the category of examinations, which do not void testimony
Correct: B — Rav Shimi bar Ashi rules the testimony is void. Unlike hour-to-hour discrepancies (where error is plausible), people do not confuse night with day. Since one says before sunrise (night) and one says during sunrise (day), the contradiction is irreconcilable and the testimony fails.
