⏰ How Much Can a Witness Err About Time?

Abaye
Per Rabbi Meir: a person does not err about time at all. Per Rabbi Yehuda: a person errs by only half an hour.
Rava
Per Rabbi Meir: a person errs by two hours less a bit. Per Rabbi Yehuda: a person errs by three hours less a bit.
Rava (rejecting Abaye)
If the precise hour is only an examination (not an interrogation), witnesses are not even required to know it exactly — so how could one claim they never err? The margin must be much larger.
Stakes The margin of error determines how wide a window must be cleared before a second pair of witnesses can render the first pair conspiring false witnesses (עדים זוממים).

⚖️ Interrogations (חקירות) vs. Examinations (בדיקות)

חקירות — Interrogations

  • Seven core questions: Jubilee cycle, year, month, day, hour, place
  • If one witness says I don't know — testimony is void
  • Reason: without precise time and place, a second pair cannot testify the first pair was elsewhere at that exact moment
  • Such testimony cannot be rendered conspiring — so it is inadmissible entirely

בדיקות — Examinations

  • Additional clarifying questions beyond the seven
  • If one witness cannot answer a detail — testimony remains valid
  • Reason: even without that detail, the core facts still allow refutation by a second pair
  • The testimony can still be rendered false without the missing detail

🍞 Why Does Rabbi Yehuda Prohibit Chametz an Hour Earlier?

Abaye
Testimony is entrusted to the vigilant — a witness scrutinizes carefully, so errs little. But chametz prohibition is entrusted to all people, who err more. Therefore an extra hour of protection is added.
Rava (first attempt)
Rabbi Yehuda conforms to his standard ruling: chametz can only be removed by burning. One must begin earlier to have sufficient time to burn it properly.
Ravina
Objection from a baraita: Rabbi Yehuda himself permits other methods of removal when done at the proper time — so burning cannot be the reason for the earlier deadline!
Rava (revised)
Revised answer: It is a rabbinic decree due to a cloudy day (יום המעונן). When the sky is overcast, one cannot observe the sun's position — so the Sages added a buffer hour.
Rava's Final Position The earlier chametz deadline is not about human error rates but about meteorological uncertainty — cloud cover renders the sun-based clock unreliable, requiring a rabbinic safety margin.

🍽️ Meal Times During the Day (Baraita + Rav Pappa's Correction)

1st Hour: Eating time for Ludim — a nation described as ravenous cannibals, who are hungry from the night
↓
2nd Hour: Eating time for robbers — those who work illicitly through the night
↓
3rd Hour: Eating time for heirs of wealthy families — those who sleep late and rise leisurely
↓
4th Hour: Eating time for all people — the normal, universally accepted mealtime (Rav Pappa corrects the baraita's original listing)
↓
5th Hour: Eating time for workers — who rise early and eat a delayed meal
↓
6th Hour: Eating time for Torah scholars — who study through the morning and eat late
↓
7th Hour and beyond: Like throwing a stone into a leather bottle — no nutritional benefit, does not contribute to health

☁️ Rava's Cloudy Day Decree — Logic Flow

Torah law: Chametz permitted until end of 6th hour (solar midday) on 14 Nisan
↓
Problem: on a cloudy day (יום המעונן), the sun's position is invisible — one cannot determine when the 6th hour ends
↓
Rabbi Meir
Add one buffer hour — prohibit chametz from start of 6th hour. Eat only through end of 5th.
Rabbi Yehuda
Add two buffer hours — prohibit chametz from start of 5th hour. Eat only through end of 4th.
↓
Result: Even if one miscalculates by an hour due to cloud cover, chametz is safely burned well before the Torah-level prohibition begins at the 7th hour
