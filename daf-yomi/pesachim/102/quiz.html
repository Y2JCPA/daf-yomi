QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda's baraita, friends who left their meal to go to the synagogue and returned do NOT need a new blessing when they were eating:
A. Wine and fruit, which have no fixed after-blessing in their place
B. Foods that require a blessing after them in their place, such as bread
C. Only if fewer than three people left the meal
D. Only if they returned within thirty minutes
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda holds that foods requiring a blessing 'after them in their place' (like bread, which triggers Birkat HaMazon) do not require a new blessing when the diners return — the original blessing retains its coverage. The implication is that non-bench-blessing foods would require a new blessing.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rav Ḥisda's specific ruling about friends who were drinking wine, uprooted themselves, and then returned?
A. A new blessing is always required after any movement, regardless of where they sit
B. No new blessing is ever needed for wine once the cup has been blessed
C. No new blessing if they return to their original place; new blessing if they return to a new place
D. A new blessing is required only if they left the house entirely
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda rules based on the location of return: original place = no new blessing (the original context is intact); new place = new blessing required (the original meal context is severed).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Yosei require two cups when Shabbat is sanctified during a meal?
A. One cup of wine is not large enough to hold the blessings for both mitzvot
B. Mitzvot must not be bundled together — Grace after Meals and Kiddush each need their own cup
C. Kiddush must always be recited before Grace after Meals
D. A second cup is required because the first cup became tamei during Shabbat
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak explains: מִצְווֹת חֲבִילוֹת חֲבִילוֹת — mitzvot must not be performed in bundles. Birkat HaMazon and Kiddush are two entirely different matters and each deserves its own dedicated cup.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When a Festival (Yom Tov) immediately follows Shabbat, what does the YaKNeHaZ acronym prescribe?
A. Yayin, Kiddush, Ner, Havdala, Zman — blessings in that specific order over one cup
B. Havdala is recited first, then wine, then Kiddush — Shabbat's end takes priority
C. Kiddush alone is recited; Havdala is omitted since the next day is also holy
D. Sheheḥiyanu replaces Havdala when Yom Tov begins at Shabbat's end
Correct: A — YaKNeHaZ (יקנה"ז) stands for: Yayin (wine), Kiddush (sanctify Yom Tov), Ner (flame), Havdala (distinguish Shabbat from Yom Tov), Zman (Sheheḥiyanu). Havdala and Kiddush can share one cup since both mark sanctified days — unlike Grace after Meals and Kiddush.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The Gemara explains why Havdala and Kiddush can share one cup (unlike Grace after Meals and Kiddush). What is the reason?
A. Havdala is a rabbinic enactment and therefore less stringent than Kiddush
B. Havdala and Kiddush are 'one matter' — both mark the sanctity of sacred days; Grace after Meals and Kiddush are two entirely different matters
C. The cup used for Havdala becomes enhanced by the Kiddush blessing
D. Combining them is a leniency permitted only on the first night of Pesach
Correct: B — The Gemara explicitly states: Havdala and Kiddush are 'one matter' (חדא מילתא) — both function to mark and draw attention to the sanctity of special days. By contrast, Birkat HaMazon (concluding a meal) and Kiddush (sanctifying a day) are two entirely different matters and must have separate cups.
