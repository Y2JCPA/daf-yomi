5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Reish Lakish, one who passes between two palm trees is in danger — but only under what condition?
A. When the palm trees are taller than ten handbreadths
B. When no public domain crosses between the two trees
C. When he is carrying borrowed water
D. When it is nighttime and he is alone
Correct: B — The Gemara qualifies: danger from passing between two palm trees applies only if a public domain does not cross between them. Demons are not permitted to harm people in a public domain.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What remedy does Rav Pappa prescribe when a dangerous object has passed between two travelers?
A. Pour water behind him and spit three times
B. Recite a verse beginning AND ending with the word 'God'
C. Walk backward seven steps while saying Shema
D. Touch the nearest tree and recite a blessing over Torah
Correct: B — Rav Pappa says to begin with a verse starting with the divine name God (אֵל) and conclude with a verse ending with God — bookending the recitation with divine names for spiritual protection.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What makes the ruḥei demon near caper-bushes practically distinct, and why does it matter?
A. It is transparent, so one must listen for its sound to detect it
B. It is blind — it has no eyes — so a person can escape by fleeing
C. It only attacks during daylight, so sheltering inside protects you
D. It can only be repelled by Torah study, not amulets
Correct: B — The ruḥei demon has no eyes. The practical halakhic difference is with regard to fleeing — since it cannot see, running away is an effective defense, as demonstrated by the story of the Torah scholar who escaped it.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Abaye switch Rav Pappa from his right side to his left when he saw the ketev meriri approaching?
A. The ketev always attacks from the left, so the right side is inherently safe
B. Rav Pappa was younger, so his merit offered less protection on the right
C. He placed Rav Pappa's Torah scholar merit on the left to block the demon approaching from that side
D. Three people walking are only protected when the most senior is positioned on the outside
Correct: C — The ketev meriri was coming from Abaye's left. Abaye moved Rav Pappa — a Torah scholar whose merit provides protection — to stand between him and the demon on the left side.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What are the two administering angels mentioned at the end of the daf, and what are their respective domains?
A. Nakid (food/cleanliness) and Naval (poverty)
B. Samael (death) and Raphael (healing)
C. Af (anger) and Ḥema (wrath)
D. Azriel (plenty) and Anavah (humility)
Correct: A — Nakid (from naki, 'clean') is the angel over food — he requires cleanliness and will not remain in a dirty place. Naval is the angel over poverty. Hanging bread in the house drives away Nakid and summons Naval.
