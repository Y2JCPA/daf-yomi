🌴 Reish Lakish's Four Dangerous Situations — and Their Limits

Reish Lakish
Four matters invite an evil spirit, and one who performs them is liable for his own life: (1) relieving oneself between a palm tree and a wall, (2) passing between two palm trees, (3) drinking water borrowed from a minor, (4) passing over spilled water.
Gemara (qualification)
Each danger has limits: (1) only if less than four cubits between them; (2) only if no public domain crosses between the trees; (3) only if it occurred in a field, not a city; (4) only if no dirt was sprinkled on the water and no one spat in it.
Gemara (exception)
However — where there is actual concern for witchcraft, meaning someone intends to harm you, even all these limiting conditions do not help. Intent overrides the safeguards.
⚠️ Key Principle Supernatural dangers are halakhically bounded by precise conditions. But deliberate, targeted witchcraft nullifies all ordinary protections.

🛡️ Objects Passing Between Two Travelers — Danger and Remedy

Three objects must not pass between two people on the road:
🐕 Dog  |  🌴 Palm tree  |  👩 Woman
Some say: also a pig
↓
One of them did pass between you — what now?
↓
Remedy Option 1
Recite a verse beginning with God (אֵל) and conclude with a verse ending with God
e.g., Exodus 15: 'God is a man of war… God is His name'
Remedy Option 2
Recite a verse beginning with lo (לא, 'no') and conclude with a verse ending with lo
Numbers 23:19: 'No man is God that He should lie'
↓
The traveler is protected from harm caused by the passage between them

👁️ Demons by Location — Three Types with Practical Differences

Ruḥei

  • 📍 Location: near caper-bushes
  • 👁️ Characteristic: has NO eyes — completely blind
  • ⚖️ Practical law: you can flee from it — it cannot follow
  • 📖 Story: a Torah scholar fled; the blind demon could not pursue him

Shida

  • 📍 Location: near sorb (hawthorn) trees
  • 🌳 A city sorb tree hosts no fewer than 60 sheidei
  • ⚖️ Practical law: an amulet must specify 'sixty sheidei' to be effective
  • 📖 Story: a Sage wrote a generic amulet for a ruler — only partially worked because he did not know it was a 60-sheidei tree

Rishfei

  • 📍 Location: on rooftops
  • ⚖️ Practical law: amulets must name the correct demon type
  • 🔑 Different spirits require different formulaic remedies
  • ⚠️ Misidentifying the demon type renders an amulet ineffective

☀️ Ketev Meriri — The Seasonal Demon of Tammuz Two forms:
• Morning Ketev (Ketev Yashud Tzahorayim): rolls like a ball, covered in scales and hair, one eye in the center of its chest
• Afternoon Ketev: different form, active after midday

Active season: 1 Tammuz → 16 Tammuz (certain danger); after 16 Tammuz (uncertain)
Found in: the shadow of a sea squill plant (חצב)

Story — Abaye Protects His Companions:
Abaye walked with Rav Pappa on his right and Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua on his left. Seeing a ketev meriri approaching from the left, he switched Rav Pappa to his left side — placing the scholar's protective merit between the demon and himself.
Three Torah scholars walking together are not vulnerable.
