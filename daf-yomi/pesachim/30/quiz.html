Pesachim 30 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What did Rav rule regarding earthenware pots used to cook chametz during Passover?
A. They must be broken
B. They should be filled with boiling water to purge them
C. They should be retained until after Passover and then used
D. They should be sold to a gentile before Passover ends
Correct: A — Rav ruled that earthenware pots used for chametz during Passover must be broken, because the absorbed chametz flavor cannot be expelled through any purging method.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Shmuel refuse to publicly announce his leniency that pots used for chametz need not be broken?
A. He was not sufficiently certain of his own ruling
B. To prevent pot merchants from justifying inflated post-Passover prices
C. Rav had formally prohibited him from teaching it publicly
D. The leniency only applied in Babylonia and not universally
Correct: B — Shmuel withheld his lenient ruling strategically — he threatened pot merchants that he would publicize it if they did not reduce their post-Passover prices, using the ruling as leverage rather than broadcasting it.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the key halakhic distinction that allows an oven to be purged but not a regular earthenware pot?
A. Ovens are always made of metal; pots are made of earthenware
B. An oven is kindled from the inside, so the fire directly consumes the absorbed flavor; a pot heated from outside does not
C. Ovens are used only for bread while pots hold liquid chametz, which is less severe
D. Pots were typically owned by gentiles and therefore less subject to chametz laws
Correct: B — The Gemara rules that when an oven is kindled from the inside, the fire directly burns away the absorbed chametz flavor in the walls. A pot heated from outside retains its absorbed flavor and cannot be purged this way.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What evidence did Ameimar cite to demonstrate that glazed earthenware vessels absorb liquid?
A. The vessels change color noticeably after prolonged use
B. Glazed vessels crack along seams after repeated heating
C. He observed liquid seeping through and appearing on the outside of the vessel
D. He found chametz residue remaining inside after thorough soaking
Correct: C — Ameimar stated that he observed liquid being expelled (demidayeti) from the outside of the vessel, proving the glaze does not form a perfect barrier and the earthenware underneath still absorbs — rendering the vessel prohibited for Passover.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Per the Mishna, what is the status of chametz after Passover that a gentile held as collateral from a Jew throughout the festival?
A. Forbidden — it was still technically associated with Jewish ownership over Passover
B. Permitted to derive benefit from
C. Permitted for consumption only, but not for commercial sale
D. Subject to the same rabbinic penalty as chametz a Jew actively kept over Passover
Correct: B — The Mishna rules the chametz is permitted because it was in the gentile's possession as collateral; the Torah prohibition of bal yera'eh — a Jew may not own chametz over Passover — never applied to chametz already transferred to gentile control.
