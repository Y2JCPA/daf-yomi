📜 What Does 'Begin with Disgrace' Mean?

Rav
Begin: "At first our forefathers were idol worshippers" (Joshua 24:2) — spiritual disgrace, then conclude with the glory of monotheism and Torah.
Shmuel
Begin: "We were slaves to Pharaoh in Egypt" (Deut. 6:21) — physical disgrace, then conclude with the glory of redemption and freedom.
Ruling in Practice Both are accepted: the Haggadah includes Avadim Hayinu (Shmuel) and the passage about Teraḥ as an idol worshipper (Rav). Both forms of disgrace introduce the narrative.

🎵 How Much Hallel Before the Meal?

Beit Shammai

  • Recite until end of Psalm 113
  • "Who makes the barren woman dwell in her house as a joyful mother" (113:9)
  • Halleluyah — stop here before the meal
  • Focuses on praise for past redemption

Beit Hillel

  • Recite until end of Psalm 114
  • "Who turned the rock into a pool of water" (114:8)
  • Includes the splitting of the sea and trembling of Sinai
  • Broader sweep of the Exodus miracles

בַּעֲבוּר זֶה — "because of this" (Exodus 13:8)
Rav Aḥa bar Yaakov — The Blind Person and the Haggadah Ruling: A blind person is exempt from reciting the Haggadah, since the verse states "because of this which the Lord did for me" — this implies pointing at matza and maror physically present before you.

Challenge (Mareimar): Rav Yosef was blind yet recited the Haggadah himself! Who recited it in his house? He did.

Resolution: Rav Yosef held matza is a rabbinic obligation today (after the Temple). The Sages modeled their enactments on Torah law — so he recited it in the rabbinic mode, even without full Torah-level pointing.
