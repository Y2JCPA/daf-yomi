🩸 Stuffed Raw Meat: Permitted or Forbidden?

Rabba
Stuffing unsalted raw meat inside a roasting animal is permitted. As the meat absorbs blood inward, so it emits it outward — the blood drains freely during roasting.
Abaye
Stuffing unsalted meat is forbidden — even with the opening facing downward. Regular stuffing is sealed on all sides; there is no path for the blood to drain.
Why the Paschal Lamb Is Different The mishna permits placing legs and entrails inside the lamb because the spit passes through the open mouth — the cavity is not sealed. This does not prove a general rule for closed stuffing.

🐑 Roasting the Paschal Lamb — The Mishna's Method

Bring a spit of pomegranate wood (shappud — שַׁפּוּד)
↓
Thrust the spit through the mouth of the lamb until it reaches the anus
↓
Place the legs and entrails inside the body cavity of the lamb
↓
Roast everything together — whole animal with organs inside, over the fire
↓
Torah requires: "roasted over fire" — meat must be cooked by the flame, not by the spit's conducted heat

🌳 Why Pomegranate Wood? Eliminating the Alternatives

What material should the spit be made from?
↓
Metal spit?
❌ Once part is hot, all is hot — meat cooks from the spit's heat, not from fire
Oak / Carob / Sycamore?
❌ These have knots requiring cutting to straighten — the knife causes the wood to emit water/sap
Pomegranate?
✅ Its knots are smooth — no cutting needed, no sap emitted; alternatively: no sap at all
↓
Pomegranate wood is the only valid option — dry, smooth, non-conducting

🥐 Dough-Wrapped Roasted Meat — Does Absorbed Blood Forbid It?

Rav
Ravin wrapped a young dove in dough and roasted it. Rav said: "If the dough tastes good, give me some and I will eat." — Rav permits even the breading that absorbed blood during roasting.
Rava
At the Exilarch's house, a young goose was breaded and served. Rava said: "If the breading is clear as a new white coin, I will eat it" — implying: reddened breading would be forbidden.
Final Halakha Fine flour breading: permitted whether or not it turned red from blood.
White flour breading: permitted only if clear as a white coin — if red, forbidden.

🍶 Vinegar Soaking — Keeping Blood Fixed in the Meat

חומץ שהשרה בו בשר פעם אחת
Practice of Ameimar People soaked raw meat in vinegar (חֹמֶץ) to fix the blood in place — preventing it from separating and rendering nearby meat forbidden.

Mar bar Ameimar reported: "My father Ameimar would never reuse vinegar in which he had already soaked meat — once used, it can no longer keep blood fixed in place."

📋 Pesachim 74 — Summary Map

Paschal Lamb Roasting: Pomegranate wood spit required — metal conducts heat (forbidden), other woods emit sap (forbidden), pomegranate is smooth and dry
↓
Core Question: When meat is enclosed during roasting, does blood drain freely or stay trapped?
↓
Opening exposed (Paschal lamb mouth, spit-hung meat, veins cut downward):
Blood drains → Permitted
Sealed on all sides (regular stuffing, whole testicles, uncut veins):
Blood trapped → Forbidden
↓
Universal remedy: Cut + salt any meat → permitted even for pot-cooking. Vinegar fixes blood in place to prevent secondary prohibition.
