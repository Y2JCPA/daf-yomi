⚖️ Half and Half: A Public Divided

Rav
Half = Majority. When the community is split evenly, each group holds majority status. Both pure and impure sacrifice on the first Pesaḥ — the pure normally, the impure invoking the communal tumah dispensation.
Rav Kahana (v.1)
Half ≠ Majority. The impure cannot invoke the communal dispensation. Pure perform on the first Pesaḥ; impure are deferred to the second Pesaḥ like any minority.
Rav Kahana (v.2)
Half ≠ Majority. Pure perform on the first Pesaḥ. Impure perform on neither first nor second — they cannot invoke communal dispensation, and they are not a simple minority either.
The Core Question When exactly half the community is impure, do they constitute a 'majority' sufficient to trigger the dispensation to sacrifice in communal impurity?

⚖️ Rav vs. Rav Kahana: The Half-and-Half Dispute

Rav
Half and half is like the majority. Since neither side is a minority, both groups sacrifice on the first Pesaḥ. The impure do so through the communal dispensation — not deferred to a second Pesaḥ.
Rav Kahana (v.1)
Half and half is NOT like the majority. The impure cannot invoke the communal dispensation. The pure sacrifice on the first Pesaḥ; the impure are deferred to the second Pesaḥ.
Rav Kahana (v.2)
Half is neither majority nor minority. Pure sacrifice on the first Pesaḥ. Impure sacrifice on neither first nor second — they fall between categories entirely.
📜 Mishna's Anchor Majority impure → all do first Pesaḥ in tumah. Minority impure → pure do first, impure do second. The dispute: where does 'half and half' fall?

🏛️ Communal Impurity Scenarios at a Glance

Majority Impure

  • All perform on the first Pesaḥ
  • Service conducted in communal tumah
  • Even the pure join the impure
  • Communal dispensation (dechuyah) fully invoked

Minority Impure

  • Pure perform on the first Pesaḥ normally
  • Impure deferred to second Pesaḥ (Pesach Sheni)
  • No communal dispensation applies
  • Impurity treated as individual matter

Half and Half (Disputed)

  • Rav: both halves sacrifice on first Pesaḥ
  • Rav Kahana v.1: pure on first, impure on second
  • Rav Kahana v.2: pure on first, impure on neither
  • Women's optional status can tip the balance

🔪 Rav Ḥisda and Rava: What Kind of Impurity Matters?

וְהַבָּשָׂר אֲשֶׁר יִגַּע בְּכָל טָמֵא לֹא יֵאָכֵל — ויקרא ז:יט
Two Approaches to the Communal Dispensation Rav Ḥisda: The dispensation applies only when the slaughtering knife became impure through corpse-impurity (tumat met) — a severe 7-day impurity that renders the slaughterer's body impure. But if the knife was rendered impure by a sheretz (creeping animal), it only renders the meat impure, not the person. That lesser impurity does not invoke communal dispensation.

Rava disagrees: Any impurity in the service is sufficient. His proof: wherever we suspend 'flesh touching impurity shall not be eaten,' we simultaneously suspend 'only the pure shall eat.' The two halakhot are coupled — if one falls, both fall.
