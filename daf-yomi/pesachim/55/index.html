⚔️ Completing Labor on the Fourteenth — Rabbi Meir vs. Rabbis

Rabbi Meir
Any labor begun before the 14th of Nisan may be completed on the 14th, even if it is only a small belt or hairnet — provided it was begun prior to the fourteenth.
Rabbis
Only three crafts — tailors, barbers, and launders — may perform labor until midday on Passover eve. All others must stop.
Gemara (Dilemma)
Does Rabbi Meir permit completing only labor for the purpose of the Festival, or also labor that is not for the Festival at all?
Baraita (Resolution)
'With regard to any labor that is for the purpose of the Festival, one may complete it on the fourteenth.' This limits Rabbi Meir's leniency: only Festival-purpose labor may be completed.
Final Ruling Rabbi Meir permits completing prior-begun, Festival-purpose labor until midday. Initiating new labor is forbidden even for a minor item. The Rabbis limit permitted labor to three necessary crafts only.

🌍 Judea vs. Galilee — Erev Pesach Labor Customs

Judea — More Lenient

  • Labor permitted until midday on Erev Pesach
  • Three crafts (tailors, barbers, launders) explicitly permitted
  • Rabbi Yehuda: weeding a field on the 13th — permitted to replant the stalk
  • Night of the 14th: labor permitted (Beit Hillel's view)
  • Mishna reflects Rabbi Yehuda's regional ruling

Galilee — More Stringent

  • No labor whatsoever on Erev Pesach — full day restriction
  • Stricter custom prevails throughout the region
  • Rabbi Yehuda: night of the 14th also prohibited in Galilee
  • Cannot benefit from 'part of day = whole day' principle twice
  • First mishna (custom only) reflects Rabbi Meir's position

🐣 Laws of the Brooding Hen on the Fourteenth of Nisan

Permitted: One may place eggs under a hen to brood on the 14th of Nisan
↓
If the hen flees its nest: One may restore it to its brooding place on the 14th
↓
Rav Huna: Restoring is permitted only within three days of the hen fleeing — the body heat has not yet fully dissipated, so restoring is practically effective
↓
Rabbi Ami: Even after three days, one may restore the hen — we are concerned about major financial loss (the eggs will be ruined)
↓
Core dispute: Rav Huna focuses on practical efficacy (will the hen still warm the eggs?); Rabbi Ami focuses on preventing major loss (even if less effective, try)
↓
Dung under animals: May be swept to the sides of a courtyard on the 14th; in a stable it may be removed entirely to the garbage dump

🏺 Vessels to Craftsmen & Jericho's Six Customs

שִׁשָּׁה דְבָרִים עָשׂוּ אַנְשֵׁי יְרִיחוֹ — Six things did the people of Jericho do (Mishna, Pesachim 55b)
Rava's Test Question (Rav Pappa Reports) Apparent contradiction: The mishna permits taking vessels to a craftsman and bringing others from his house on Erev Pesach. But a baraita says one may bring vessels from a craftsman but not take new ones to him on Chol HaMoed!

Resolution: Bringing from a craftsman = the 14th of Nisan (permitted freely). Taking to a craftsman = Chol HaMoed (restricted). On Chol HaMoed: a completed item (e.g., jug from potter, cup from glassmaker) may be taken home, but wool from a dyer or items requiring major new work may not.

Jericho's Six Practices: The mishna concludes by introducing six customs unique to Jericho — three were approved by the Sages (רְצוּ חֲכָמִים) and three were not (לֹא רָצוּ חֲכָמִים). This sets up the major discussion opening the next daf.
