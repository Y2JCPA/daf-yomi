5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Rabbis (as opposed to Isi ben Yehuda), what does the phrase "They shall perform it" (Numbers 9:12) primarily teach about Pesach Sheni?
A. That only mitzvot performed on the body of the offering apply
B. That the Paschal lamb is not slaughtered on behalf of groups containing impure members
C. That Hallel must be recited during the meal
D. That chametz must be removed before Pesach Sheni begins
Correct: B — The Rabbis use "They shall perform it" to teach that the Pesach Sheni offering is not slaughtered on behalf of a group that includes ritually impure individuals mixed with pure ones. (Isi uses this phrase instead to limit the verse to body-related mitzvot.)
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the key halakhic difference regarding Hallel between the first and second Pesach?
A. Hallel is recited during eating on both Pesachim
B. Hallel is not recited at all on Pesach Sheni — not even during preparation
C. Hallel is recited during preparation on both, but only during eating on the first Pesach
D. Hallel is recited during eating on both, but only during preparation on the first Pesach
Correct: C — The mishna teaches that both Pesachim require Hallel during preparation (due to the joy of performing the mitzva). However, only the first Pesach requires Hallel during eating — on Pesach Sheni, Hallel is not recited at the meal.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When the Paschal lamb is sacrificed in communal impurity (majority of Israel ritually impure), which individuals are explicitly FORBIDDEN from eating it?
A. Those impure from contact with a corpse (tum'at met)
B. Zavim, zavot, menstruating women, and women after childbirth
C. Priests who contracted impurity that same day
D. Any Israelite who did not immerse before the offering
Correct: B — The mishna states explicitly that zavim, zavot, niddot, and yotzet leida may not eat the Paschal lamb even under communal impurity — because the dispensation is for corpse-impurity only, and zav-level impurity remains excluded.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Isi ben Yehuda, what does the phrase "nor break a bone in it" (Numbers 9:12) teach beyond the plain prohibition?
A. That the prohibition against breaking bones applies only on Shabbat
B. That the prohibition applies even to bones that have no marrow
C. That bones may be broken before the offering is slaughtered
D. That this prohibition applies only to the second Pesach, not the first
Correct: B — Isi ben Yehuda, freed from using this phrase for another derivation, applies it to teach that the prohibition of breaking bones includes bones without marrow — not only marrow-containing bones.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Rava's answer to Rav Yosef's question about corpse-impure people who push into the Sanctuary itself during communal impurity?
A. They are fully permitted to enter since the majority is impure
B. They are automatically liable for karet regardless of communal status
C. The phrase 'from the camp' teaches the rule applies even to part of the camp — the Sanctuary is a different domain not covered by the dispensation
D. Rabbi Eliezer exempts them entirely by analogy to the courtyard ruling
Correct: C — Rava derives from "from the camp" (Numbers 5:2) that the dispensation applies specifically to the camp (even partially) — indicating that the Sanctuary, being a distinct zone, is not covered by the communal impurity dispensation for corpse-impure individuals.
