📖 Core Theme Daf 6 explores the boundaries of chametz ownership — when leaven in your possession is truly 'yours' for halakhic purposes. Through cases of gentile ownership, royal confiscation, distant travelers, and Torah chronology, the Gemara defines the scope of 'lo yimatzei' (it shall not be found).

🐄 Arnona: Does the Royal Tax Exempt the Firstborn?

Questioners
If a Jew's animal is born into a herd subject to royal tax (arnona), is he still obligated in the mitzva of the firstborn?
Rava
He is exempt — even if he could have dismissed the tax collector with money. The animal belongs effectively to the king.
Questioners
But what about dough subject to the royal portion — is challah still required?
Rava
Challah IS still required from such dough. An animal is public — everyone sees it was confiscated. But dough is private — people will suspect he ate without separating challah.
Key Distinction Publicity (פרסום) determines the ruling. A confiscated animal is publicly known — no suspicion arises. Dough is private — the obligation to separate challah remains lest others suspect wrongdoing.

🏠 Gentile's Leaven in a Jewish Home

A gentile enters a Jewish courtyard carrying his leaven on Passover
↓
Gentile merely passes through
Jew need not remove the leaven — it was never deposited with him and is not in his possession
Gentile deposits leaven with the Jew
Jew must remove it — he accepted responsibility and it is considered in his possession
Jew rents a room to the gentile for the leaven
Jew need not remove it — the rented space legally belongs to the gentile for this purpose

🗓️ Thirty-Day Rule for Travelers Departing Before Passover

More than 30 days before Passover: One departs on a sea voyage or joins a caravan → no obligation to remove chametz before leaving
↓
Within 30 days of Passover: One departs → must check and remove the chametz from his house before departing
↓
Rava's extension: Even when departing before 30 days — if he intends to return close to Passover, he must still remove chametz, lest he arrive home and find it just before the Festival
↓
Rava's broader rule: One who sells his house within 30 days must also remove chametz — the 30-day obligation applies even when vacating the premises
↓
Grain storage exception: Even beyond 30 days, if he plans to clear stored grain before Passover, he must check for chametz hidden among it

📜 Three Opinions on the Source for 30-Day Pre-Passover Teaching

Tanna Kamma

  • One must ask and teach Passover halakhot 30 days before the Festival
  • Source: Moses warned Israel about the Second Pesach while standing at the First Pesach
  • First Pesach = 14 Nisan; Second Pesach = 14 Iyar — exactly 30 days apart

Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel

  • Teaching begins only 2 weeks (14 days) before Passover
  • Source: Moses spoke on 1 Nisan — only 14 days before Passover
  • Rejects the 30-day proof: Moses simply completed all Passover topics together when discussing them

Rabba bar Shimi / Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak

  • Numbers 9:1 records the Passover offering in the 1st month of Year 2
  • Numbers 1:1 records the census in the 2nd month — yet appears first in the text
  • Proof: The Torah is not bound by chronological order (אין מוקדם ומאוחר בתורה)
  • The Paschal passage's earlier occurrence implies it was taught well before its date

🔥 Why Must Chametz Be Nullified (Bitul) After the Search?

Initial Answer
Because of crumbs he may have missed in his search — those overlooked crumbs could still be in his possession.
Rejection
Crumbs are insignificant — they are already considered ownerless (hefker) and no one cares about them.
Counter
Those crumbs are still protected by his home, which he cares about — so they are not truly hefker and remain significant.
Rava
The real reason for bitul: lest he find a fine cake (geluska) of great value and hesitate to burn it. Nullification removes his ownership mentally, eliminating the temptation entirely.
Objection
But if he finds the geluska after Passover begins, he can no longer nullify it! Rabbi Elazar: one cannot nullify chametz once Passover has started.
Resolution Therefore, nullification (בִּטּוּל) must be performed before Passover begins — ideally at the time of the search — to preemptively release ownership of any undiscovered valuable leaven.
