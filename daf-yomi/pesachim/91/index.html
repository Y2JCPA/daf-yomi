⚖️ May the Paschal Lamb Be Slaughtered for an Individual?

Rabbi Yehuda
We do not slaughter the Paschal lamb on behalf of an individual — only for a group. The verse "in any one of your gates" (Deut. 16:5) teaches this exclusion.
Rabbi Yosei
We may slaughter for an individual. I use "in any one" to teach a different law: one may not offer the Paschal lamb on improvised altars even when such altars are permitted.
Rabbi Shimon
The verse uses the word "man" — a man may bring alone, but a woman may not. She must always join a group on the first Pesach.
Shared Source, Three Readings All three derive from "You may not sacrifice the Paschal lamb in any one of your gates" (Deut. 16:5) — but each applies it to a different restriction: individuals, altars, or gender.

🏛️ Prisoner's Prison: Does Ownership Matter?

Rabbi Yoḥanan (via Rabba bar Huna)
The mishna's ruling — that a prisoner needs a group — applies only to a gentile prison, where release is genuinely uncertain. In a Jewish prison, presumably he can be released.
Rav Ḥisda
Even in a Jewish prison, we slaughter only with a group. Even if the king could release him, we cannot assume he will — so uncertainty remains.
Both Agree A prisoner is never slaughtered for alone — only when included in a group that can definitely eat, given the real chance he cannot be released in time.

👩 Women & the Paschal Lamb: Three Tannaitic Opinions

Rabbi Yehuda

  • First Pesach: woman may participate even alone
  • Second Pesach: only in a group (ancillary role)
  • Source: "souls" includes women on first; "that man shall be cut off" limits second to men only

Rabbi Yosei

  • First Pesach: woman participates — even alone
  • Second Pesach: she may also participate in a group
  • Source: "souls" includes women on both; "that man" only bars a woman from bringing alone on second

Rabbi Shimon

  • First Pesach: woman joins only as part of a group — never alone
  • Second Pesach: women are entirely excluded
  • Source: "man" in Exodus teaches men alone may bring on first; "that man shall be cut off" fully excludes women from second

😢 The Onen (Acute Mourner) on Passover Night

Day of death of an immediate relative: The onen is forbidden by Torah law from eating any sacrificial food — including the Paschal lamb
↓
This Torah-level prohibition applies only on the day of death itself; from the next day onward, eating sacrificial food is permitted again
↓
If the onen was registered in a Paschal lamb group and the lamb was slaughtered while he was still fit to eat — he is formally included
↓
Even if he became an onen after slaughter and cannot eat the lamb that night — the registration covers him
↓
Result: He is exempt from the second Pesach — since the disqualification arose only after slaughter
