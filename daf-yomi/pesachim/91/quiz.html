Pesachim 91 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda, may one slaughter the Paschal lamb on behalf of a single individual?
A. Yes — an individual may always bring his own Paschal lamb
B. No — a group is required, derived from "in any one of your gates"
C. Only if the individual is a Torah scholar
D. Only on the second Pesach, not the first
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda interprets "in any one of your gates" (Deut. 16:5) as excluding a lone individual — the Paschal lamb must be slaughtered for a group, not one person alone.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabba bar Huna citing Rabbi Yoḥanan, to which type of prison does the mishna's ruling about prisoners apply?
A. Any prison, whether Jewish or gentile
B. Only a prison with iron locks
C. Only a prison belonging to gentiles
D. Only a prison run by Jewish authorities
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan taught that the ruling — requiring a group — applies specifically to a gentile prison. A Jewish prisoner under Jewish authority is presumably releasable, though Rav Ḥisda extends it to Jewish prisons too.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Yaakov (citing Rabbi Yoḥanan) say we do not make a Paschal lamb group composed entirely of converts?
A. Converts are not halachically obligated in the Paschal lamb
B. They might be overly scrupulous and unnecessarily disqualify the offering
C. Converts must always bring a separate Paschal lamb
D. A group must include at least one kohen
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan warns that converts tend to be excessively meticulous with mitzvot. A group of only converts might scrutinize the Paschal lamb too zealously and cause unnecessary disqualification.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Shimon, what is a woman's status regarding the second Pesach (Pesach Sheni)?
A. Fully obligated — identical to a man
B. Obligated only if she was ritually impure on the first Pesach
C. May participate as part of a group but not alone
D. Excluded entirely — even from participating in a group
Correct: D — Rabbi Shimon derives from "that man shall be cut off" (Num. 9:13) that only men bear the second Pesach obligation. Women are entirely excluded — they cannot even join a group on the second Pesach.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If a person was registered in a Paschal lamb group, the lamb was slaughtered while he was fit, but he became an onen (acute mourner) afterward and could not eat — what is his legal status?
A. He must bring the second Pesach to compensate for not eating
B. He is exempt from the second Pesach
C. He must bring a guilt offering
D. He is liable for karet (spiritual excision)
Correct: B — Since the person was registered and fit when the lamb was slaughtered, the post-slaughter disqualification does not obligate him in the second Pesach — registration at the moment of slaughter is the determining factor.
