📖 What Is the Source for Tamid Timing?

Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi
The verse says 'bein ha'arbayim' — between the evenings (Numbers 28:4). The first 'evening' begins when the sun starts declining from midday; the second is nightfall. The midpoint is 10.5 hours — and the tamid is offered at 9.5 hours, one full hour before actual nightfall.
Rava
Rejected! If that derivation were correct, the tamid could never be brought before 9.5 hours — but on Erev Pesach it is brought at 8.5 hours! Rather: the source is 'in the turning of the day' (Deuteronomy 16:6) — once the sun turns westward after midday, the time is valid.
Conclusion Rava's source (בְּפָנוֹת הַיּוֹם) is accepted. The tamid is tied to the sun's westward turning, not a mathematical midpoint of בֵּין הָעַרְבַּיִם.

🕯️ Tamid Timing Across Different Days

Regular Weekday

  • Slaughtered: 8.5 hours after sunrise
  • Sacrificed: 9.5 hours after sunrise
  • Many voluntary vow offerings (נְדָרִים) and free-will offerings (נְדָבוֹת) fill the earlier slots
  • Source: 'The fats of the peace-offerings upon it' — all offerings precede the afternoon tamid

Erev Pesach (Weekday)

  • Slaughtered: 7.5 hours after sunrise
  • Sacrificed: 8.5 hours after sunrise
  • No vow or free-will offerings brought on Pesach eve
  • Tamid advanced to allow time for the vast number of Paschal lamb slaughterings

Erev Pesach on Shabbat

  • Slaughtered: 6.5 hours after sunrise
  • Sacrificed: 7.5 hours after sunrise
  • Must also accommodate: Shabbat musaf offerings and two frankincense bowls (בָּזִיכִין)
  • The maximum advancement of the afternoon tamid in halacha

📜 Rabbi Yishmael vs Rabbi Akiva: The Shabbat Tamid

Rabbi Yishmael
Like its arrangement on a regular weekday, so is its arrangement on Shabbat — the tamid is slaughtered at 8.5 hours. We issue a rabbinic decree: even on Shabbat, when no free-will offerings are brought, we do not advance the tamid, lest people conclude it may be advanced on weekdays when they are brought.
Rabbi Akiva
Like its arrangement on an ordinary Erev Pesach (weekday), so is its arrangement on an ordinary Shabbat — the tamid is slaughtered at 7.5 hours. On Shabbat, no free-will offerings are brought at all, so no such decree is necessary. Why delay unnecessarily?
Root Disagreement Do we issue a decree (גְּזֵרָה) on Shabbat to match weekday practice, even though the reason (free-will offerings) doesn't apply? Rabbi Yishmael: yes. Rabbi Akiva: no.

🔥 The Fixed Order of Temple Service on Erev Pesach

Morning Tamid (תָּמִיד שֶׁל שַׁחַר) — no sacrifice may precede it. Source: 'And the priest shall burn wood upon it every morning' (Leviticus 6:5). The morning burnt-offering anchors all that follows.
↓
All other daytime offerings — vow offerings, free-will offerings, communal sacrifices. Source: 'The fats of the peace-offerings upon it' (upon the morning tamid) — meaning all others come after it.
↓
Afternoon Tamid (תָּמִיד שֶׁל בֵּין הָעַרְבַּיִם) — advanced to 7.5 hours on Erev Pesach. Nothing may be offered after it under normal rules. It 'closes' the altar for regular sacrifices.
↓
Korban Pesach (קָרְבַּן פֶּסַח) — the Paschal lamb is slaughtered after the afternoon tamid and before the afternoon incense. Thousands of lambs require this extended window of time.
↓
Afternoon Incense (קְטֹרֶת שֶׁל בֵּין הָעַרְבַּיִם) — burned on the golden altar inside the Sanctuary after the Paschal lambs are complete.
↓
Lighting the Menorah (הַדְלָקַת הַנֵּרוֹת) — the final act of the daily Temple service. Source: 'Aaron shall set it up from evening to morning before the LORD' (Leviticus 24:3).
