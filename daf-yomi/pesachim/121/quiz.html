Pesachim 121 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Yishmael hold that one blessing for the Paschal lamb exempts the Festival offering?
A. The Festival offering is subordinate to the Paschal lamb in sanctity
B. Sprinkling is included within the general category of pouring — both form one mitzvah
C. The Festival offering requires no blessing at all according to Rabbi Yishmael
D. Rabbi Yishmael holds that only one offering is actually brought on Pesach night
Correct: B — Rabbi Yishmael holds that sprinkling is subsumed under the broader category of pouring, making them a single unified mitzvah — therefore one blessing at the Paschal lamb covers both offerings.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Rav Huna and Rav Ḥisda about meat?
A. Whether sacrificial meat may be eaten outside Jerusalem's walls
B. Whether an olive-bulk or egg-bulk of meat is the minimum to contract ritual impurity
C. Whether piggul meat can ever be redeemed through a monetary substitute
D. Whether a priest may consume meat from a blemished offering
Correct: B — Rav Huna says an olive-bulk (k'zayit) is sufficient to convey ritual impurity; Rav Ḥisda requires the larger egg-bulk (k'beitza).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is piggul, and why did the Sages make a decree related to it?
A. Meat left over past its time; Sages feared waste of sacred items
B. An offering slaughtered outside the Temple; Sages required extra witnesses
C. An offering invalidated by the priest's improper intent regarding time; Sages feared priests might exploit this from enmity
D. An offering from a blemished animal; Sages prohibited accepting it to protect donors
Correct: C — Piggul occurs when a priest intends during the service to eat the offering outside its permitted time. The Sages feared that a priest with enmity toward an owner would deliberately cause this invalidation, and instituted a protective decree.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
At the pidyon haben attended by Rabbi Simlai, who was the subject of the sheheḥiyanu dilemma?
A. The mother, who endured the labor and birth of the firstborn
B. The father (who fulfills the mitzvah) versus the priest (who receives five sela)
C. The firstborn son himself, who is being redeemed
D. The assembled congregation who witnesses the redemption ceremony
Correct: B — The question was whether the father — who performs the mitzvah of redeeming his son — or the priest — who financially benefits by receiving five sela — should recite the sheheḥiyanu blessing.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Akiva, why must a separate blessing be recited for the Festival offering even after blessing the Paschal lamb?
A. The Festival offering is brought on a different day and thus unrelated
B. Rabbi Akiva holds that all blessings on Pesach must be recited individually
C. Pouring and sprinkling are two entirely distinct mitzvot — neither exempts the other
D. The Paschal lamb blessing is only valid before nightfall, while the Festival offering is by day
Correct: C — Rabbi Akiva holds that sprinkling and pouring are two independent mitzvot with no overlap. Since each is distinct, each offering requires its own blessing — the Paschal lamb blessing cannot exempt the Festival offering.
