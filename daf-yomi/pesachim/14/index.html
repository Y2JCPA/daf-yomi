⚗️ The Chain of Ritual Impurity

Av HaTumah
Primary source: corpse, creeping animal, zav
↓
Rishon LeTumah
First degree — person or vessel touched av
↓
Sheni LeTumah
Second degree — food touched a rishon
↓
Chullin
Stops here — food cannot transmit further by Torah law
Teruma
May become shelishi (3rd degree) — disqualified (pasul)
Kodashim
May become revi'i (4th degree) — disqualified

🐄 The Two Cows: A National Clock

Two cows plow on the Mount of Olives (Har HaZeitim) on Erev Pesach throughout the morning
↓
While both cows are plowing, the entire nation continues eating chametz normally
↓
When the first cow is removed, people know to place their chametz in suspension — time is running out
↓
When the second cow is removed, all chametz must be burned immediately — no more eating
↓
This public visual system served as a national chametz-removal clock for all of Israel

🍖 Does Food Transmit Impurity to Other Food?

Baraita
Food does NOT transmit impurity to other food. The verse 'And if water is poured on seed' (Lev. 11:38) teaches: only water and liquids transmit to food — not food to food.
Abaye
This rule applies only to chullin (non-sacred food). For teruma and kodashim, food DOES transmit impurity to other food — by rabbinic decree.
Rav Adda bar Ahava (Rava)
The rule applies to chullin AND teruma — neither can transmit food-to-food. But for kodashim, food DOES transmit, by rabbinic decree.
Ravina (Rava)
The Torah states this categorically — no exceptions. Food never transmits impurity to food: not for chullin, not for teruma, not even for kodashim.
Gemara (defense of Ravina)
The mishna's case involves liquids present alongside the meat. The second piece of meat contacts the liquid — not the first piece of meat directly. Liquids DO transmit. No contradiction.
The Key Verse "And if water is poured on seed" (Leviticus 11:38) — only liquid (mashkeh) transmits impurity to food; dry food does not transmit to food

🫙 Metal vs. Earthenware Lamp — Why Does It Matter?

Metal Lamp (Rav Yehuda's Reading)

  • Metal vessels can contract corpse-impurity (tum'at met) through direct contact
  • A metal lamp touched by a corpse becomes an av hatumah itself
  • Oil inside such a lamp becomes rishon leTumah (1st degree)
  • Rabbi Akiva's case: tevul yom oil (3rd degree) poured into this 1st-degree lamp
  • The chiddush: liquids can transmit impurity — proven by this chain
  • Only a metal lamp enables this specific demonstration of liquid impurity

Earthenware Lamp (Why Not?)

  • Earthenware contracts impurity only from its inner airspace, not from outside contact
  • An earthenware lamp cannot become an av hatumah through corpse contact
  • No distinction between corpse-impurity and creeping-animal impurity for earthenware
  • The precise halakhic reasoning about liquid transmission would be impossible to derive
  • Rav Yehuda: the metal lamp is essential — earthenware would yield no novel law

🔥 How Rabbi Meir Derived His Ruling

Rabbi Ḥanina: Priests burned impure teruma together with ritually impure meat — no objection
↓
Rabbi Akiva: Added: priests lit tevul yom oil in an impure lamp — adding impurity to something already destined for destruction
↓
Core principle derived: When items are being destroyed, one need not avoid rendering them further impure
↓
Rabbi Eliezer & Rabbi Yehoshua
Agree: pure teruma burned alone; impure teruma burned alone — they disagree only about leavened teruma
Rabbi Meir's Inference
Since destruction removes concern — pure teruma MAY be burned together with impure teruma on Erev Pesach
The Dispute
Rabbi Eliezer & Yehoshua debate whether chametz teruma may be burned with non-chametz teruma
