📜 Hillel and the Sons of Beteira: Gezerah Shavah

Sons of Beteira
We have forgotten whether the korban Pesach overrides Shabbat. Is there anyone here who knows the halakha?
Hillel
The word 'במועדו' (its appointed time) is written regarding the Paschal offering, and 'במועדו' is also written regarding the daily tamid offering.
Sons of Beteira
From where do you know this? What authorizes the comparison?
Hillel
Just as 'its appointed time' by the tamid overrides Shabbat, so 'its appointed time' by the Pesach overrides Shabbat. Moreover, this is a kal vachomer — and I received the gezerah shavah as a tradition from Shemaya and Avtalyon!
Key Term גְּזֵרָה שָׁוָה — verbal analogy: identical words in two verses teach that the same law applies to both contexts

🔪 The Forgotten Knife: Problem and Solution

The Problem: A person forgot to bring his slaughtering knife before Shabbat. He cannot carry it through a public domain on Shabbat to the Temple.
↓
Hillel Asked: What is the law? He replied: 'I have heard this halakha but I have forgotten it. Leave it to the people of Israel — if they are not prophets, they are the sons of prophets.'
↓
The Lamb-owners' solution: One whose Pesach offering was a lamb stuck the knife into the animal's wool before Shabbat, letting the animal carry it.
↓
The Goat-owners' solution: One whose Pesach offering was a goat (no wool) stuck the knife between its horns before Shabbat began.
↓
Hillel's reaction: Seeing this, he recalled his forgotten tradition and declared: 'This is exactly the halakha I received from Shemaya and Avtalyon!' The people had independently arrived at the correct ruling.

😤 Two Middot That Drive Away Wisdom

Haughtiness — גַּסּוּת הָרוּחַ

  • Source: Rav (cited by Rav Yehuda), derived from Hillel's story
  • Torah scholar → his wisdom departs from him
  • Prophet → his prophecy departs from him
  • Scholar example: Hillel rebuked the sons of Beteira and immediately forgot the halakha of the knife
  • Prophet example: Miriam spoke with arrogance against Moses and was smitten with leprosy

Anger — כַּעַס

  • Source: Reish Lakish (Rabbi Shimon ben Lakish)
  • Torah scholar → his wisdom departs from him
  • Prophet → his prophecy departs from him
  • Scholar example: Moses became angry and ruled incorrectly regarding impure vessels in the wilderness
  • Prophet example: Elisha grew angry at the king of Israel — his prophecy left until a musician played and the spirit returned

👑 Anger Costs Even Heaven-Appointed Greatness

כָּל הַכּוֹעֵס, אֲפִילּוּ גְּדוּלָּה תְּלוּיָה לוֹ מִן הַשָּׁמַיִם — מוֹרִידִין אוֹתוֹ מִגְּדוּלָּתוֹ
Rabbi Mani bar Patish Whoever becomes angry — even if greatness has been designated for him from Heaven — he is brought down from his greatness. This is derived from Eliab, David's oldest brother: the prophet Samuel looked at Eliab's stature and thought 'Surely God's anointed stands before me' — indicating kingship had been apportioned to Eliab from Heaven. Yet when Eliab became angry at his young brother David, that designated greatness departed from him, and the kingship passed to David instead.
