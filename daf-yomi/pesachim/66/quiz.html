QUESTION 1 OF 5
What verbal analogy (gezerah shavah) did Hillel use to prove the Paschal lamb overrides Shabbat?
A. The word 'korban' appears in both the Pesach verse and the tamid verse
B. The phrase 'במועדו' (its appointed time) appears in both the Pesach and daily offering verses
C. The word 'slaughter' (shechitah) links Pesach to the Yom Kippur service
D. The phrase 'children of Israel' appears in both the Pesach and Shabbat passages
Correct: B — Hillel derived a gezerah shavah from the identical word 'במועדו' (its appointed time) in both the Pesach verse and the tamid verse, concluding that just as the daily offering overrides Shabbat, so does the Paschal lamb.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Hillel forget the halakha about transporting a knife on Shabbat after being appointed Nasi?
A. He was elderly and his memory was naturally failing
B. He had never actually learned this halakha from his teachers
C. He became haughty and rebuked the sons of Beteira, causing his wisdom to depart
D. He was distracted by the large crowd gathered to hear him
Correct: C — Rav taught: anyone who acts haughtily — if a Torah scholar, his wisdom departs. After being appointed Nasi, Hillel began rebuking the sons of Beteira arrogantly, and as a consequence he forgot the halakha of the knife.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How did the people solve the problem of transporting the slaughtering knife to the Temple on Shabbat?
A. A non-Jew carried it through the public domain as a rabbinic leniency
B. It was placed in a vessel and carried with a shinui (in an unusual manner)
C. Lamb-owners stuck the knife in the wool; goat-owners placed it between the animal's horns
D. Hillel ruled the knife could be carried normally since it was needed for a mitzvah
Correct: C — The people intuitively figured out the halakha: a lamb's thick wool could hold the knife, while a goat (without wool) had the knife placed between its horns — allowing the animal itself to transport it without the owner violating Shabbat.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Reish Lakish, from which prophet do we learn that anger causes prophecy to depart?
A. Miriam, who was struck with leprosy after speaking against Moses in anger
B. Elisha, who became angry at the king of Israel and temporarily lost his prophetic ability
C. Jonah, who fled from his prophetic mission after becoming angry at God
D. Isaiah, who rebuked the people harshly and could no longer receive prophecy
Correct: B — Elisha told the king of Israel 'Were it not for Jehoshaphat, I would not look at you' in anger — after which he could not prophesy until a musician played for him and the divine spirit returned.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara prove that the Paschal lamb is offered even when the majority of Israel is ritually impure?
A. A kal vachomer: if it overrides Shabbat, it certainly overrides impurity
B. An explicit verse states 'offer the Pesach even in impurity'
C. From the verse about individuals being sent out and deferred to Pesach Sheni — implying the community is never deferred
D. From Hillel's tradition received from Shemaya and Avtalyon
Correct: C — Reish Lakish derived this from the verse commanding individuals to leave the camp — the fact that only individuals are deferred to Pesach Sheni implies that when the community as a whole is impure, they are not deferred and instead offer the Pesach in a state of impurity.
