Pesachim 62 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda, why might an uncircumcised person achieve atonement through the Paschal lamb?
A. The verse 'for him and not for his fellow' explicitly includes him
B. Since he is obligated to bring the offering, he is deemed eligible for its atonement
C. Circumcision status is irrelevant once the blood has been sprinkled
D. An uncircumcised person is treated like a minor who is exempt from all commandments
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda uses the 'since' (miggo) argument: since the uncircumcised person is obligated to bring the Paschal lamb, he is considered eligible for atonement through it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the key difference between offering the Paschal lamb 'for its own purpose THEN a different purpose' versus 'for a different purpose THEN its own purpose'?
A. There is no halakhic difference — both equally disqualify the offering
B. The first disqualifies the offering itself; the second disqualifies the person who performed the rite
C. The first is only a rabbinic violation; the second is a Torah violation
D. The first applies only at the time of slaughter; the second applies only at sprinkling
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan teaches: 'own + different' places the disqualification in the offering (it is entirely invalid); 'different + own' places the disqualification in the offerer (who transgressed), while the offering itself may retain validity.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Regarding meat impurity in the Paschal lamb, what is the ruling if the fats become impure but the meat remains pure?
A. The blood may not be sprinkled — fats are part of the offering and their impurity is decisive
B. The entire offering must be burned immediately without sprinkling
C. The blood may be sprinkled, since the meat is pure and fit for eating
D. The offering is deferred automatically to Pesach Sheni
Correct: C — The Gemara rules: if the fats are impure but the meat is pure and fit for eating, the priest may sprinkle the blood. However, if the meat is impure, blood may not be sprinkled even if the fats are pure.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Yoḥanan refuse to teach Rabbi Simlai the Book of Genealogies in three months?
A. Because the Book had already been hidden and was no longer accessible to any Sage
B. Because Rabbi Simlai had not yet been ordained as a recognized Tanna
C. Because Berurya, despite extraordinary ability, could not complete it in three years
D. Because the Book was restricted only to descendants of the Davidic line
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan cited Berurya, wife of Rabbi Meir and daughter of Rabbi Ḥananya ben Teradyon, who studied 300 halakhot from 300 teachers each day and still could not complete the Book in three years — making three months impossible.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What did Rami bar Rav Yuda say occurred after the Book of Genealogies was hidden away?
A. Disputes between the academies of Sura and Pumbedita multiplied without resolution
B. The strength of the Sages was weakened and the light of their eyes was dimmed
C. The calculation of the calendar became uncertain and festivals were delayed
D. The genealogical purity of the priesthood could no longer be verified
Correct: B — Rami bar Rav Yuda in the name of Rav states: 'From the day the Book of Genealogies was hidden, the strength of the Sages was weakened and the light of their eyes was dimmed' — its loss was a spiritual and intellectual catastrophe.
