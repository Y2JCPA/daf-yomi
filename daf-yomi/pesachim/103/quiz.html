QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the accepted mnemonic order of blessings when Yom Tov falls immediately after Shabbat?
A. יה"נ"ק — Wine, Havdala, Candle, Kiddush (Rabba's order)
B. קנ"יה — Kiddush, Candle, Wine, Havdala (Levi's order)
C. יקנה"ז — Wine, Kiddush, Candle, Havdala, Shehecheyanu
D. קי"נ"ה — Kiddush, Wine, Candle, Havdala (the Rabbis' order)
Correct: C — The accepted halakhic practice is יקנה"ז (YaKNeHaZ): Yayin (wine), Kiddush, Ner (candle), Havdala, Zman (Shehecheyanu). Rav's opinion that kiddush precedes havdala was accepted, and the blessing of time (Shehecheyanu) comes last.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda, what was the actual scope of the dispute between Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel regarding havdala?
A. Whether Grace After Meals is recited before or after havdala
B. Whether wine is required for havdala at all
C. Only the internal order of light and spices blessings within havdala
D. Whether havdala requires a special candle or any flame suffices
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehuda clarifies that Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel agreed that Grace After Meals comes first and that its cup is also used for havdala. Their only disagreement was whether to bless the light (Ner) or the spices (Besamim) first within havdala.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Rava recite a blessing over wine both at the start and end of a meal, according to his own explanation?
A. He held that every individual cup always requires its own blessing regardless of intent
B. He was following a stringent custom of the Exilarch's court
C. Uncertainty about whether more wine would arrive broke his original intent, requiring a new blessing
D. The halakha requires two wine blessings — one to open and one to close a Shabbat meal
Correct: C — Rava explained that because his attendant's actions were unpredictable, he was genuinely uncertain each time whether additional wine would be brought. This uncertainty interrupted his original intent (da'at), making each newly-arrived cup potentially the last and requiring a fresh blessing.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What are the permitted minimum and maximum number of distinctions in the havdala text, according to Rabbi Elazar citing Rabbi Oshaya?
A. Minimum 1, maximum 4
B. Minimum 2, maximum 6
C. Minimum 3, maximum 7
D. Minimum 4, maximum 10
Correct: C — Rabbi Elazar in the name of Rabbi Oshaya rules that one who decreases the distinctions may not go below three, and one who increases may not exceed seven. Rava's standard practice included four distinctions, which became the normative Ashkenazic text.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What halakhic point did Rav Ashi make using Rav's own ruling about festivals occurring after Shabbat?
A. That Rav held a torch is superior to a candle for havdala
B. That Rav required a blessing on every cup of wine regardless of circumstances
C. That Rav's kiddush-havdala practice proved the initial blessing carries forward, contradicting his students
D. That Rav agreed with Beit Shammai that light precedes spices in havdala
Correct: C — Rav Ashi demonstrated from Rav's YaKNeHaZ ruling that Rav himself recited kiddush and then continued drinking without a new blessing — proving that the initial blessing carries forward. This refuted the students of Rav who claimed each cup needs its own blessing.
