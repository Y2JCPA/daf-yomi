🕯️ YaKNeHaZ: The Disputed Order of Blessings

Rabba
יה"נ"ק — Yayin (wine), Havdala, Ner (candle), Kiddush
Levi
קנ"יה — Kiddush, Ner (candle), Yayin (wine), Havdala
Rabbis
קי"נ"ה — Kiddush, Yayin (wine), Ner (candle), Havdala
Accepted Practice
יקנה"ז — Yayin, Kiddush, Ner, Havdala, Zman (Shehecheyanu) — Rav's view prevails
Mnemonic יקנה"ז (YaKNeHaZ) — rendered in Ashkenazic tradition as 'Jag den Has' (hunt the hare), famously illustrated in medieval illuminated Haggadot with a hunting scene

🏠 Rav Ya'akov bar Abba Visits Rava's House

Rav Ya'akov bar Abba
Why did you recite a blessing over wine at the start of the meal and again at the end? One blessing should cover all the wine consumed during the meal!
Rava
This practice is fine in the house of the Exilarch — there it is uncertain whether the host will bring more wine or not. Each new cup may require its own blessing.
Rav Ya'akov bar Abba
But we are in your own home — surely you know what your attendant will bring! Why apply the Exilarch's logic here?
Rava
My attendant too is unpredictable. I genuinely do not know what he will bring next, so the uncertainty about future wine breaks my original intent — requiring a new blessing each time.
Halakhic Principle Uncertainty about future wine interrupts da'at (intent), treating each cup as potentially final and requiring its own blessing over the fruit of the vine

⚖️ Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel: Order Within Havdala

Beit Shammai

  • 🕯️ Light (Ner) first, then spices (Besamim)
  • Fire was created on Saturday night — bless it first
  • Light is more significant than fragrance
  • Per Rabbi Meir's account of the dispute

Beit Hillel

  • 🌿 Spices (Besamim) first, then light
  • Spices comfort the soul as Shabbat departs
  • Fragrance repairs the loss of the extra Shabbat soul
  • Per Rabbi Meir's account — halakha follows Hillel

Rabbi Yehuda's Clarification

  • 📜 Both schools agree: Grace After Meals precedes havdala
  • Both agree the havdala cup serves Grace After Meals
  • Dispute is only about Ner vs. Besamim ordering
  • Contradicts Rabbi Meir's broader version of the dispute

📜 Minimum and Maximum Distinctions in Havdala

המבדיל בין קדש לחול בין אור לחושך בין ישראל לעמים בין יום השביעי לששת ימי המעשה
Rabbi Elazar citing Rabbi Oshaya Minimum: One who decreases the distinctions in havdala may not go below three.

Maximum: One who increases may not exceed seven.

Rava's standard four-distinction text:
• בין קדש לחול — Between sacred and profane
• בין אור לחושך — Between light and darkness
• בין ישראל לעמים — Between Israel and the nations
• בין יום השביעי לששת ימי המעשה — Between the seventh day and the six days of work

Rav Ya'akov questioned whether the Israel/nations distinction was necessary since it appeared in kiddush — Rava defended reciting all four as his established practice.
