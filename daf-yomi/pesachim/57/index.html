⚖️ Corrupt vs. Righteous in the Temple

The Corrupt Priestly Houses

  • House of Baitos — ruled by clubs (violence)
  • House of Ḥanin — ruled by whispering (informing to authorities)
  • House of Katros — ruled by pens (writing slanders)
  • House of Yishmael ben Piakhi — ruled by fists

Those Praised in the Temple

  • Yishmael ben Piakhi — student of Pinehas, praised by the people as righteous
  • Pinehas ben Elazar — the righteous priest
  • Bohayan's son — designated corner produce (pe'ah) for the poor
  • Bohayan himself — protected the poor from accidentally eating untithed produce

"Woe is me due to the High Priests of the house of Baitos — woe due to their clubs.
Woe due to the house of Ḥanin — woe due to their whispers.
Woe due to the house of Katros — woe due to their pens.
Woe due to the house of Yishmael ben Piakhi — woe due to their fists.

For they are High Priests, their sons are treasurers, their sons-in-law are officers, and their servants beat the people with staves."

🥩 From Stolen Hides to Golden Sanctuary Tablets

Original practice: Hides of kodshei kodashim (most sacred offerings) were placed in the vestibule for equal distribution among all priestly watches
↓
Abuse: Prominent priests took all hides by force each Friday. Lesser priests dared not challenge them — no equitable distribution remained
↓
Righteous protest: The animal owners began burning the hides rather than surrender them unjustly to bullies. The Sages explicitly approved this act
↓
Collective resolution: The proceeds from redeeming/selling the hides were pooled into a fund for the Temple
↓
Lasting result: The entire Sanctuary was plated with golden tablets — one cubit × one cubit, as thick as a golden dinar — purchased from injustice transformed

🤚 Yissakhar of Kfar Barkai — The Ignorant Priest

The King
Which animal is most distinguished — which should be brought as the daily offering?
Yissakhar of Kfar Barkai
[Waves contemptuously with silk-wrapped hand] If goat is better, then let goat be brought as the daily offering!
Rav Ashi
He did not learn the Mishnah! It is explicit: Rabbi Shimon teaches that lambs take precedence over goats in every context — this is basic halakha!
The King
Since he contradicts received law and shows contempt for sacred service — cut off his hand!
The Double Offense Yissakhar sinned twice: (1) he wrapped his hands in silk, showing disdain for direct contact with sacrificial animals, and (2) he rejected explicit Mishnaic law through arrogant gesture. A High Priest's ignorance is not merely personal — it corrupts the entire Temple service.

🍽️ Yoḥanan ben Narbbai — Gluttony as Temple Service?

His Household's Daily Consumption

  • 300 calves slaughtered daily
  • 300 jugs of wine consumed
  • 40 se'a of doves — for dessert
  • Scale: a se'a ≈ 7–8 liters; 40 se'a of doves is staggering

The Halakhic Paradox

  • Notar (leftover consecrated meat) must be burned — a serious violation
  • Throughout Yoḥanan's tenure, there was never notar in the Temple
  • His gluttony inadvertently prevented a common Temple transgression
  • The Sages note this without clear praise — the excess is remarkable, not admirable
