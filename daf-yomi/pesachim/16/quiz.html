Pesachim 16 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, what is the status of Temple liquids declared pure by Yosei ben Yo'ezer?
A. They are completely pure and cannot become impure at all
B. They can become impure but cannot transmit impurity to other items
C. They transmit impurity just like any other liquid outside the Temple
D. Their status depends on whether they are inside or outside the Temple courtyard
Correct: B — Shmuel holds that Temple liquids can themselves become impure, but do not transmit that impurity further. Rav disagrees, holding they cannot become impure at all.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What distinction does Rabbi Zeira draw regarding blood rendering produce susceptible to impurity?
A. Only animal blood renders produce susceptible, never human blood
B. Only blood from sacred animals renders produce susceptible
C. Blood that flows freely renders produce susceptible; blood received in a vessel for altar-sprinkling does not
D. All blood renders produce susceptible without exception
Correct: C — Rabbi Zeira teaches that blood poured out like water renders produce susceptible, but blood collected in a vessel for altar-sprinkling does not. He also notes that blood squeezed after the initial spurt never renders susceptible, even from non-sacred animals.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara require two separate verses to teach that water renders food susceptible to impurity?
A. One verse covers hot water and the other covers cold water
B. Each verse covers a different type of food — solid vs. semi-liquid
C. One verse covers detached water and the other attached water; neither can be derived from the other alone
D. The two verses establish susceptibility for two different Jewish holidays
Correct: C — From detached water alone one might exclude stationary spring water (too significant to render susceptible); from attached water alone one might exclude detached water (too insignificant once separated). Both verses are necessary.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
For which sin does the High Priest's tzitz (frontplate) NOT atone?
A. Blood of an offering that became ritually impure
B. Meat of an offering that became ritually impure
C. Piggul — an offering invalidated by improper intent regarding time
D. Fat of an offering that became ritually impure
Correct: C — The Gemara derives that the tzitz does not atone for piggul because the Torah already addresses it explicitly elsewhere (Leviticus 7:18). The tzitz atones specifically for the sin of impurity in offerings.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What resolution does Rav Sheisha son of Rav Idi offer regarding the impurity status of liquids?
A. Temple liquids are in a unique category with absolutely no impurity whatsoever
B. Liquids function like fourth-degree impurity in consecrated items — they become impure but do not transmit it
C. The dispute depends on whether the liquid is a Torah-mandated libation or an incidental liquid
D. Liquids inside the Temple are pure while those outside are subject to regular impurity rules
Correct: B — Rav Sheisha resolves the debate by comparing liquids to revi'i (fourth-degree impurity) in consecrated items — a status universally agreed upon where the item contracts impurity but cannot transmit it further.
