Pesachim 85 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the baraita cited on Pesachim 85a, which bones of the Paschal lamb fall under the prohibition 'you shall not break a bone'?
A. Only bones that contain marrow inside
B. Only bones with an olive-bulk of meat on the outside
C. Both bones with marrow and bones without marrow
D. Only the main leg bone, not smaller bones
Correct: C — The baraita explicitly extends the prohibition: 'This includes both a bone in which there is marrow and a bone in which there is no marrow' — the ban is broad regardless of content.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Abaye say one may not simply peel meat from a bone when part of a limb exits its permitted boundary?
A. Because touching the exited meat creates impurity that spreads
B. Because we are concerned the entire bone will crack
C. Because a priest must be present to authorize such separation
D. Because peeling meat off bone is considered a form of cooking
Correct: B — Abaye ruled: 'One may not do this, due to a concern that the entire bone will crack' — cracking a section of bone that still has meat on it would violate the bone-breaking prohibition.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was the core dispute between Rav Huna and Rav Ḥisda regarding the rabbinic impurity of piggul and notar?
A. Whether the decree was ordained by Torah authority or rabbinic authority
B. The minimum amount required — Rav Huna holds olive-bulk, Rav Ḥisda holds egg-bulk
C. Whether the decree applies inside the Temple or also in Jerusalem
D. Whether liquids are included in the decree alongside solids
Correct: B — One Sage holds the minimum is an olive-bulk (כזית) and the other holds it is an egg-bulk (כביצה), each flowing from a different rationale for why the decree was enacted in the first place.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ami, when exactly does one become liable for moving Paschal lamb meat from one group's location to another?
A. The moment he lifts the meat with intent to move it
B. When he crosses the threshold of his own group's room
C. Only when he places the meat down in the second group's location
D. When two witnesses observe him in transit between the locations
Correct: C — Rabbi Ami: 'One who carries out the meat... is not liable until he places the meat in the location of the second group' — the act is legally complete only upon placement, like Shabbat domain liability.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Shmuel bar Rav Yitzḥak say the insides of Jerusalem's gates were not sanctified?
A. Because they were constructed after the original sanctification ceremony
B. Because lepers sheltered inside them from sun and rain and needed a permitted place
C. Because merchants stored goods there and commercial use precluded sanctity
D. Because Ezra declared gates as neutral diplomatic zones between domains
Correct: B — Rabbi Shmuel bar Rav Yitzḥak explained: 'Because lepers protect themselves by sitting under them' — the Sages deliberately left gate interiors unsanctified so lepers, barred from the city, would still have shelter.
