Pesachim 64 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yitzchak, what is the minimum number of people required in each of the three groups slaughtering the Paschal lamb?
A. Ten
B. Twenty
C. Thirty
D. One hundred
Correct: C — Rabbi Yitzchak derives from the three terms — Assembly, Congregation, and Israel — each indicating a group of no fewer than thirty people, requiring at least 30 per group (90 total minimum).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the practical legal difference between Abaye's and Rava's readings of the mishna about the Temple courtyard doors?
A. Whether the doors were made of wood or metal
B. Whether we rely on a miracle of expansion or count a fixed number of people
C. Whether the doors were closed before or after the blood-sprinkling
D. Whether Levites or priests were responsible for the doors
Correct: B — Abaye holds the doors closed by themselves miraculously (we rely on a miracle); Rava holds priests deliberately closed them to a fixed number (no reliance on miracle).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did the bowls used by the priests in the Temple not have flat bases?
A. To make them easier to pass from hand to hand in the rows
B. To distinguish sacred Temple vessels from ordinary household bowls
C. So priests could not set them down, which would cause the blood to coagulate and become invalid
D. Because flat-based vessels were considered susceptible to ritual impurity
Correct: C — The bowls had no flat bases so priests could not set them down. If blood sat still it would coagulate, rendering it invalid for sprinkling on the altar.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How did King Agrippa conduct his census of the Passover pilgrims in Jerusalem?
A. He stationed guards at the city gates to count every person entering
B. He asked each family to register with the Temple administration before Passover
C. He had the High Priest remove one kidney from each Paschal lamb as a tally
D. He counted the number of firstborn males brought to the Temple
Correct: C — Agrippa instructed the High Priest to set aside one kidney per Paschal lamb. The total of 600,000 pairs implied over 1.2 million lambs — twice the population at the Exodus.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Shimon, for which sacrifice is one liable if one has leaven in one's possession on the 14th of Nisan?
A. Any sacrifice offered anywhere on that day
B. Only the Paschal lamb — not other offerings brought on that day
C. Only the daily afternoon offering (tamid shel bein ha-arbayim)
D. All offerings during the seven days of Passover
Correct: B — Rabbi Shimon holds that liability for leaven applies only to the Paschal lamb on the 14th of Nisan. Other offerings brought that day are exempt — the Torah's deliberate separation of the verse teaches that only the sacrifice most connected to the leaven prohibition carries liability.
