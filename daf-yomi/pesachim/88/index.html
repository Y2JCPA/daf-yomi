🐑 Pesachim 88 — Consent, Memory, and Messianic Redemption

Aggadah: Ulla visits Pumbedita, praises dates — then suffers indigestion and retracts; Rabbi Elazar expounds Isaiah 2:3: the Temple's final name will be Jacob's designation — Beit Elohim

🏺 Ulla's Date Incident in Pumbedita

מלא תירינא דסמא דמותא עבדי בבלאי — ומיכלי ביה ועסקי באורייתא
Aggadic Interlude Ulla visited Pumbedita and was brought a large basket (tirina) of dates. He exclaimed: "How many baskets could be purchased for a single zuz?!" — meaning dates were laughably cheap. That night, the dates gave him severe indigestion. He immediately reversed course and praised the Babylonians: "A basketful of lethal poison — yet they engage in Torah study on them!" The story illuminates how Babylonian Jews sustained intense Torah learning despite difficult physical conditions.

⛰️ The Three Patriarchs Name the Temple

Abraham

  • Called it: "The Mountain" (הַר)
  • Source: Genesis 22:14 — "On the mountain of the Lord it will be seen"
  • Implies distance — a lofty, remote destination
  • Not the final designation

Isaac

  • Called it: "The Field" (שָׂדֶה)
  • Source: Genesis 24:63 — "Isaac went out to meditate in the field"
  • Implies openness — pastoral, accessible
  • Not the final designation

Jacob — Final Name

  • Called it: "The House of God" (בֵּית אֱלֹהִים)
  • Source: Genesis 28:17 — "This is none other than the House of God"
  • Isaiah 2:3: "Come, let us go up to the House of the God of Jacob"
  • This is the messianic designation — the Temple's eternal name

⚖️ What Counts as Consent? Resolving the Baraita's Contradiction

Baraita — First Clause
A master may slaughter for adult children and Hebrew slaves only with their consent — implying explicit agreement is needed.
Baraita — Conclusion
"Except for the wife" — implying adult children and slaves cannot opt out even if they slaughtered their own lamb. This seems to contradict the first clause!
Gemara
The difficulty is stark: if they need consent, why can't they protest by slaughtering their own? And if they CAN protest, why does "except for the wife" single her out?
Rava
"Consent" in the first clause means silent non-protest — not explicit verbal agreement. And when they slaughter their own lamb, you have no greater protest than this act itself! That is why the wife is singled out — she alone can protest even after the master slaughtered.
Halakhic Principle Silent acquiescence = valid consent. Active independent slaughter = active protest. The wife uniquely retains the right to protest by invoking her financial independence from her husband.

👑 Slave Forgot Master's Instructions — Step by Step

Master tells slave: "Go slaughter a Paschal lamb" (specifies kid or lamb)
↓
Does the slave remember which animal was specified?
✅ Remembers
Slaughter the specified animal. Master eats from it. Obligation fulfilled.
❌ Forgot
Slaughter both a kid and a lamb. Stipulate: "Whichever my master specified shall be the Paschal offering; the other shall be a peace offering."
↓
Does the master remember which animal he originally specified?
✅ Master Remembers
Master eats from the animal he specified.
Abaye: Slave goes to master's regular shepherd for a loan of one animal — shepherd is presumed willing to help the master.
❌ Master Also Forgot
Neither animal may be used. Both the lamb and the kid are sent to the place of burning. No Paschal obligation is fulfilled.
