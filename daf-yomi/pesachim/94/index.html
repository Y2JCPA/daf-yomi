🌅 Two Great Disputes of Pesachim 94

Halacha
Who is on a distant journey? Modi'im sets the boundary — if you cannot reach Jerusalem by Pesach time, you are exempt from the first Pesach and observe the second.
Astronomy
How large is the world and how does the sun travel? Jewish sages and the nations' sages dispute both the size of creation and the mechanics of the cosmos.
Rava
The world is 6,000 parasangs across. A person walks 10 parasangs per day; from earth to the firmament is a 500-year walk — approximately 1.8 million parasangs.
Nations' Sages
The sun does not travel above the firmament at night. It circles around behind the west, north, and east — always below the sky. The Gemara concedes they may be correct.
Unifying Thread Halachic distance (Modi'im) and cosmic distance (the world's breadth) are both measured in walking time — the daf weaves law and astronomy through the single unit of human travel.

🏙️ The Modi'im Test: Are You on a Distant Journey?

On erev Pesach at dawn: Where are you standing?
↓
Beyond Modi'im (15+ mil from Jerusalem)
Cannot arrive in time by any means — exempt from first Pesach; observes second Pesach
Closer than Modi'im
Expected to arrive — obligated; failure to come incurs karet
↓
Edge case: Can reach on horses or mules but not by walking?
↓
Still liable — ability to arrive (by any means) obligates; only total inability exempts
↓
Second edge case: Closer than Modi'im but blocked by camels and carriages carrying family?
↓
Still liable — personal impediment does not create a halachic distant journey; the geographic fact is what counts

🚶 How Far Can a Person Walk in One Day?

Rabbi Yoḥanan (via Rabba bar bar Ḥana)

  • Total: 10 parasangs = 40 mil per day
  • Dawn → Sunrise: 5 mil
  • Sunrise → Sunset: 30 mil
  • Sunset → Nightfall: 5 mil
  • Rava uses this to calculate world's total size as 6,000 parasangs

Rabbi Yehuda's Baraita

  • Person walks 10 mil per day (not 40)
  • Firmament thickness = 1/10 of sun's daily path
  • Creates tension with Rabbi Yoḥanan's view
  • Gemara reconciles: different measurement frames
  • Supports a smaller cosmological scale

Halachic Implication

  • Modi'im is 15 mil from Jerusalem
  • A person can walk 15 mil from dawn to the zman
  • 15 mil = exact halachic threshold for Pesach exemption
  • Walking pace is the measuring rod of Jewish law
  • Mode of transport (horse, etc.) does not define the legal category

☀️ Sun's Path at Night: A Cosmic Dispute

Jewish Sages
The celestial sphere is stationary; the constellations revolve within it. During the day the sun is below the firmament and visible; at night it travels above the firmament — warming the earth from above, which is why underground springs are warm in winter.
Nations' Sages
The constellations are fixed; the sphere revolves around them. At night the sun does not ascend above the firmament — it circles behind through the west, north, and east. Springs are warm in winter because the sun passes near underground sources.
Rabbi Natan
In summer the sun is high — the world is hot and springs are cold (sun is far from ground's depths). In winter the sun is low — springs run warm from proximity to the subterranean heat of the sun's underground passage.
Gemara's Verdict
The nations' sages appear correct (nir'in divrei chakhmei umot haolam) — springs are warm in winter and cold in summer, which fits their model. A rare talmudic acknowledgment of non-Jewish wisdom prevailing.
Surprising Conclusion The Gemara explicitly concedes that the nations' sages may be right about the sun's night path — one of the Talmud's most remarkable admissions of external scientific insight.

🌞 The Sun's Four Seasonal Paths

Nisan, Iyyar, Sivan (Spring): Sun travels over the mountains — melts winter snows; world is temperate, neither too hot nor too cold; waters flow down to fertilize the earth
↓
Tammuz, Av, Elul (Summer): Sun travels high through the open sky — world is very hot; deep springs and wells run cold because the sun's heat does not penetrate to underground sources
↓
Tishrei, Ḥeshvan, Kislev (Autumn): Sun travels over the seas — evaporates sea water that rises as vapor and falls as the autumn and winter rains; world begins to cool
↓
Tevet, Shevat, Adar (Winter): Sun travels low through the wilderness — world is cold; underground springs run warm because the sun passes close to their subterranean source
