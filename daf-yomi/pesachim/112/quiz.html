QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the remedy for someone who is thirsty on a Friday night and remembers Psalm 29?
A. Recite the seven 'voices of God' (Psalm 29) over the water, then drink
B. Recite the demon-formula (Lul, Shafan, Anigron) then drink
C. Wait until morning to drink
D. Cup their hands and drink quickly from a river
Correct: A — The Gemara prescribes reciting the seven 'voices' (קוֹל ה׳) from Psalm 29 over the water as protection. The demon-formula is the fallback for those who don't remember the verse.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was the meaning of Rabbi Akiva's proverb: 'If you wish to hang yourself, hang on a tall tree'?
A. Escape from enemies by climbing trees
B. If you want others to accept your teaching, cite a great authority to back it up
C. Build your home on high ground for safety
D. Seek help from the most powerful people available
Correct: B — This proverb means: just as a tall tree makes hanging more effective, attributing one's teaching to a great sage lends it authority. Rabbi Akiva taught this to Rabbi Shimon ben Yoḥai while imprisoned.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
The mnemonic מ-ק-ש (mem-kuf-shin) refers to three instructions from Rabbi Yishmael son of Rabbi Yosei. What do the letters represent?
A. Mishna (study), Kiddush (Shabbat), Shmirat (observance)
B. Mum (blemish), Mekaḥ (purchase/business), Ishto (his wife)
C. Makom (place), Kesef (money), Shalom (peace)
D. Middot (character), Keva (fixed practice), Shir (song)
Correct: B — The mnemonic stands for: מוּם (blemish — don't marry a woman whose brothers died), מֶקַח (business — be careful in transactions), and אִשְׁתּוֹ (his wife — wait an extra night after immersion).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi warn his sons not to live in Shekhantziv?
A. The water there is dangerous to drink at night
B. The city has no Torah scholars or synagogues
C. The people there are mockers who will draw others into mockery
D. It is too close to the Roman border and is dangerous
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi specifically warned that Shekhantziv in Babylonia is populated by mockers, and that their behavior is contagious — they will pull others into their negative and scornful ways.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How did Abaye ultimately restrict Agrat bat Maḥalat?
A. He banished her entirely from the world
B. He restricted her to Tuesday nights only, removing Friday nights
C. He decreed she may not pass through inhabited areas — only open roads
D. He imprisoned her the way Rabbi Ḥanina had tried to before
Correct: C — Abaye decreed she could not travel through any inhabited area at all. When she appealed that her 180,000 demons needed some domain, he permitted only uninhabited, open roads — a stricter restriction than Rabbi Ḥanina's earlier decree.
