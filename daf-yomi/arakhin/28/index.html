🕎 Mishna: What Is Indispensable in Sacred Objects?

Candelabrum: 7 branches integral to each other; 7 lamps integral to each other; goblets, knobs, and flowers each indispensable
↓
Table: Legs, frame (sovev), border, and tubes — each integral; absence of any invalidates the rest
↓
Altar: Base, surrounding circuit, and four horns — each indispensable to fulfillment
↓
Tefillin: Four Torah passages — absence of any one prevents fulfillment with the others; in the head-tefillin, each passage must be on its own scroll
↓
Mezuza: Two passages (Shema + V'haya im shamoa) — each is indispensable; must be written on a single scroll

🩸 Two Baraitot on Sprinkling Not L'Shma — Reconciled

Rav Yosef
No difficulty: the first baraita (sprinkling valid) follows Rabbi Eliezer, who juxtaposes the leper's guilt-offering to the nazirite's guilt — making the leper's oil governed by blood-rules. The second (invalid) follows the Rabbis, who reject that juxtaposition.
Gemara
But according to Rabbi Eliezer — can a halakha derived via hekesh (juxtaposition) itself teach another halakha via juxtaposition? A general principle holds: in consecrated matters, a derived halakha cannot itself be the source of a further derivation!
Rava
Both baraitot follow the Rabbis. Where it says 'valid' — the offering is permitted for consumption despite improper intent. Where it says 'invalid' — the mitzva of sprinkling itself was not fulfilled. Two different standards; no contradiction.
Key Distinction Valid for eating ≠ Valid as fulfillment of the mitzva. Rava resolves both baraitot by distinguishing between the offering's permissibility and the mitzva's proper execution.

⚗️ Candelabrum Materials: Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi vs. Rabbi Yosei b. Rabbi Yehuda

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi — Kelal U'Frat

  • Method: General → Specific → General (kelal u'frat u'khlal)
  • 'You shall make a Candelabrum' = general
  • 'Of pure gold' = specific (a type of metal)
  • 'Of beaten work' = general again
  • Result: Only items similar to the specific — i.e., metals — are valid
  • Wood is unfit; other metals are fit

Rabbi Yosei b. Rabbi Yehuda — Ribui U'Mi'ut

  • Method: Amplification → Restriction → Amplification (ribui u'mi'ut u'ribui)
  • 'You shall make a Candelabrum' = amplification (any material)
  • 'Of pure gold' = restriction
  • 'Of beaten work' = amplification again
  • Result: Any material is technically valid — even wood
  • Hasmonean wooden Candelabrum was technically valid (though not ideal)

📏 The Candelabrum's Structure — 18 Handbreadths (Shmuel's Tradition)

Bottom — 3 handbreadths: The base and the flower upon the base
↓
2 handbreadths: Bare shaft above the base
↓
1 handbreadth knob: First knob — two branches emerge, one to each side, rising to the top of the Candelabrum
↓
1 handbreadth bare + 1 handbreadth knob: Second knob — two more branches emerge similarly
↓
1 handbreadth bare + 1 handbreadth knob: Third knob — two more branches emerge to full height
↓
1 handbreadth bare: Central shaft continues above the third knob
↓
Top — 1 handbreadth: Goblet, knob, and flower at the apex of the central branch. Total = 18 handbreadths

🎺 Trumpets vs. Other Temple Vessels — A Special Rule

All vessels Moses fashioned were fit for his generation and for all future generations
↓
🕎 Candelabrum & Other Vessels
Fit forever — no special limitation in the verse
🎺 Trumpets
Exception: Fit for Moses's generation only — unfit for future generations
↓
Why unfit for future generations?
↓
"Make for yourself" (לְךָ) — The word 'for you' designates the trumpets specifically for Moses, not for subsequent generations. Other readings of לְךָ (from your own property; a divine expression of affection) are considered but the designation-reading is accepted.

📋 Arakhin 28 — Summary Map

Mishna: Which sacred objects have mutually indispensable components?
↓
Candelabrum: 7 branches, 7 lamps, 22 goblets, 11 knobs, 9 flowers — each indispensable
Tefillin/Mezuza: Each Torah passage is indispensable; head-tefillin passages require separate scrolls
↓
Gemara: What material may the Candelabrum be made from?
↓
All agree: Gold from a single block = ideal. Fragments of gold = unfit.
Dispute: R. Yehuda HaNasi — only metals valid (kelal u'frat). R. Yosei b. R. Yehuda — even wood valid (ribui u'mi'ut).
↓
Dimensions: 18 handbreadths total; 3 pairs of branches at 3 knobs; goblets like Alexandrian cups; knobs like Cherethite apples; flowers like those of Corinth
