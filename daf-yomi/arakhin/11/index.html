🪨 Why Three Examples? Each Teaches a Unique Law

Stone

  • Not fit for sacrifice at all
  • Teaches: non-sacrificial foreign objects diminish the handful's measure
  • Could not be derived from salt or frankincense alone

Salt

  • Salted separately — not mixed with the full offering initially
  • Teaches: items not initially combined with the whole meal offering diminish the handful
  • Could not be derived from stone (which is never sacrificial)

Frankincense

  • Sacred and fit for the altar — a genuine sacrificial substance
  • Teaches: even holy, sacrificial items that belong outside the handful diminish its measure
  • Could not be derived from salt or stone

✋ Abaye and Rava: The Technique of Handful Removal

Abaye
How do the priests properly remove the handful from a meal offering?
Rava
They remove it as people normally remove handfuls — by folding all of their fingers over the palm of the hand.
Abaye
Objection! The little finger has no role in a normal handful. And each finger has a specific Temple function — the middle finger measures a cubit, the forefinger sprinkles blood, the thumb anoints a metzora!
Gemara
The little finger is used only for leveling. The priest scoops with a full hand so the handful is not lacking, then wipes away excess flour using the thumb from above and the little finger from below — yielding an exact measure.
Rav Zutra / Rav
Specifically: bend the middle three fingers until their tips reach the palm. That is how a kometz is removed — neither by fingertips alone nor with an overflowing scoop.
Source Verse "And he shall take up from there his handful" (Leviticus 2:2) — 'his handful' means in the normal human manner of scooping.

🤔 Rav Pappa's Dilemmas: Edge Cases in Kometz Validity

וְקָמַץ מִשָּׁם מְלֹא קֻמְצוֹ — Leviticus 2:2
Unresolved Questions (Teiku) Obvious ruling: "His handful" (Leviticus 2:2 / 16:12) means the normal human manner — fingertips angled to the side, not straight down.

Dilemma 1: If the priest took the handful from the sides — passing his palm back and forth so flour collected via the side — is it valid?

Dilemma 2: If he took it from top to bottom instead of the standard inside-out motion — valid?

Dilemma 3: If the priest stuck the handful onto the side wall of the second vessel rather than placing it inside — is it sanctified?

Mar bar Rav Ashi adds: If the vessel was overturned and the handful stuck to the underside (in an indentation) — what is the law? Do we require placement inside the vessel?
