Arakhin 11 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Mishna need three separate examples — stone, salt, and frankincense — to teach that the handful must be complete?
A. The three represent different categories of Temple vessels
B. Each case teaches a unique principle that cannot be derived from the others alone
C. The Mishna lists them in order of increasing sanctity
D. They correspond to three distinct stages of the meal offering service
Correct: B — Each is necessary: a stone teaches that non-sacrificial objects diminish the handful; salt teaches that items not initially mixed with the full offering diminish it; frankincense teaches that even sacred items belonging outside the handful diminish its measure.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Zutra bar Toviyya citing Rav, how does the priest physically remove the kometz?
A. Using only the three middle fingers, folded straight down into the flour
B. Scooping upward from the base of the vessel with all five fingers
C. Bending the three middle fingers to the palm, with thumb pressing from above and little finger leveling from below
D. Folding all five fingers over the palm in one sweeping motion
Correct: C — The priest bends the middle three fingers until their tips reach the palm, creating the handful. The thumb presses from above and the little finger from below to level it — ensuring an exact, non-overflowing measure.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does the Mishna imply about a meal offering whose frankincense was increased beyond the required amount?
A. It is disqualified, just as a decrease disqualifies
B. It is valid — the Mishna only states that a decrease disqualifies
C. It depends on whether the priest added it intentionally
D. The extra frankincense must be removed before burning
Correct: B — The Gemara explicitly infers: since the Mishna says 'if he decreased its frankincense it is unfit,' the implication is that if he increased it, the offering remains valid.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yitzḥak bar Yosef citing Rabbi Yoḥanan, do the three tannaim's dispute about frankincense deficiency apply to frankincense that comes by itself (not accompanying a meal offering)?
A. Yes — their dispute applies equally to all types of frankincense
B. No — for independent frankincense, all agree that any decrease whatsoever disqualifies
C. No — for independent frankincense, all agree it can never become lacking
D. The Gemara leaves this question unresolved
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan teaches that the three-way dispute (Meir, Yehuda, Shimon) only applies to frankincense accompanying a meal offering. For frankincense that comes by itself, all three tannaim agree that any decrease disqualifies it.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rami bar Ḥama rules: if an extra handful of frankincense designated for the meal offering was lost — when does the timing of the loss matter?
A. Timing never matters; any loss of frankincense disqualifies the offering
B. If lost before the handful of the meal offering was removed, the extra frankincense was not yet fixed, so it does not disqualify; if lost after removal, it was already fixed and the loss does not disqualify
C. If lost before burning it disqualifies; if lost after burning begins it does not
D. The loss only matters if it occurs inside the Temple courtyard
Correct: B — Rami bar Ḥama teaches that sanctification/fixation of the frankincense occurs at the moment the handful is removed. Lost before removal: not yet fixed, and the offering lacks its proper frankincense. Lost after removal: already fixed at that moment, so the subsequent loss does not disqualify.
