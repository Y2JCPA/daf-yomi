5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yochanan, what happens if oil is added to the handful of a sinner's meal offering?
A. The offering remains fit — oil does not affect the handful
B. The offering is unfit
C. The oil must be removed before the handful is burned
D. The owner must bring a second offering to compensate
Correct: B — Rabbi Yochanan rules the offering is unfit. The sinner's meal offering is explicitly forbidden from having oil (Leviticus 5:11), and adding oil to the handful renders it invalid.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the heart of Rava's dilemma regarding the handful whose oil was squeezed onto the wood first?
A. Whether a handful can be burned without oil
B. Whether the handful must be placed directly on coals, not wood
C. Whether substances merely contiguous to altar items are considered part of those items
D. Whether oil may be reapplied to a handful that lost it
Correct: C — Rava's dilemma is whether substances that are adjacent (contiguous) to items being burned on the altar are halakhically considered part of those items — with implications for whether the oil counts as ascending together with the handful.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Hisda, which substance do we examine to determine if nullification occurs?
A. The potentially nullified substance — the smaller quantity
B. The potentially nullifying substance — the larger quantity
C. Whichever substance has greater ritual holiness
D. The substance that was present in the vessel first
Correct: B — Rav Hisda holds that we follow the potentially nullifying substance (the larger quantity). If the larger quantity can attain the status of the smaller, they are considered the same type and nullification does not occur.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Hanina, is meat of a slaughtered animal nullified when mixed into a larger quantity of animal carcass?
A. No — slaughtered meat is always dominant over carcass meat
B. Yes — slaughtered meat cannot become carcass on its own, so it is nullified
C. No — carcass meat can become slaughtered, so the two are identical
D. It depends on the ratio of the mixture
Correct: B — Rabbi Hanina follows the potentially nullified substance (the slaughtered meat). Since slaughtered meat cannot on its own become carcass meat, the two are not the same type, and the slaughtered meat minority IS nullified by the carcass majority.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the source of Rabbi Zeira's ruling that a handful and a remainder never nullify each other?
A. A rabbinic decree enacted by the Men of the Great Assembly
B. The principle that items of different status cannot nullify each other
C. A gezerah shavah — the word 'burning' appears for both the handful and the Passover remainder
D. The ruling of Rav Hisda that we always follow the larger quantity
Correct: C — Rabbi Zeira derives a gezerah shavah (verbal analogy) from the word 'burning' shared by the handful and the Passover remainder. Just as two handfuls cannot nullify each other, so too handfuls and remainders can never nullify each other — this is an independent Torah edict.
