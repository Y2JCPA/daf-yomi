QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Kahana, when does a vessel join (מצרף) its contents together for purposes of ritual impurity?
A. Whenever the contents are physically touching one another
B. Only when the vessel is required for the item's sanctification
C. Only when the contents are the same type of food
D. Whenever the vessel has a fixed, sanctified volume
Correct: B — Rav Kahana teaches that a vessel joins its contents only when the item requires a vessel for its sanctification — such as meal offering flour, which must be placed in a sanctified vessel. Items not requiring a vessel for sanctification are not joined by it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rava attempt to prove that a handful may be removed from one half-tenth on behalf of the other?
A. From a mishna about two separate meal offerings placed side by side on the altar
B. From a baraita prohibiting bringing a tenth of an ephah in two separate vessels
C. From the ruling of the sons of Rabbi Ḥiyya about intermixed meal offerings
D. From the case of the lost and replaced half-tenth
Correct: B — Rava cites a baraita that forbids bringing a single tenth of an ephah in two vessels. He infers the inverse: in one vessel, the two portions are joined, and a handful from one portion may validly serve the other.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the dispute between Rava and Abaye regarding the three half-tenths (aleph, bet, gimel)?
A. Rava says all three are always joined; Abaye says none are joined after the replacement
B. Rava says only designated partners are joined; Abaye says all three are joined as 'residents of one cabin'
C. Rava says impurity spreads to all three; Abaye says only the found half is exempt
D. Rava says the handful may be taken from any one; Abaye says only from the replacement
Correct: B — Rava holds that joining is specific: the replacement (gimel) and its designated partner (bet) are joined, but the found original (aleph) is extraneous. Abaye says all three were always meant to be part of one offering — they are 'residents of one cabin' — so any one becoming impure pulls in the other two.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What objection does Rav Pappa raise to Abaye's ruling about priestly consumption after handful removal?
A. A priest may not eat from a meal offering of uncertain ownership
B. One-sixth (danka) of the handful was not sacrificed to permit this specific half-tenth's remainder
C. The handful must be taken from the geometric center of the vessel
D. A meal offering divided into three half-tenths is automatically disqualified by rabbinic decree
Correct: B — Rav Pappa objects that one-sixth (danka) of the removed handful was designated to permit an extraneous half-tenth rather than the one whose remainder the priest seeks to eat. That portion of the handful was therefore not properly sacrificed to permit this remainder, disqualifying it.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the core dispute between Rava and Abaye regarding 'impurity saturation'?
A. Whether a vessel full of impure liquid transmits impurity to dry food inside it
B. Whether a second, sequentially applied lesser impurity can take effect on an item already impure with a lesser impurity
C. Whether food that became impure outside the Temple can receive additional impurity inside the Temple
D. Whether impurity transmitted by a zav is stronger than corpse impurity and can override it
Correct: B — The debate concerns whether a second lesser impurity applied after the first can take effect — or whether the item is 'saturated' and immune. They argue from the case of a zav's sheet: did contact impurity and treading impurity arise simultaneously (Rava) or sequentially (Abaye's proof), and does that distinction matter?
