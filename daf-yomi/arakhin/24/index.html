🏺 Rav Kahana vs. Sons of Rabbi Ḥiyya: Does a Vessel Connect Its Contents?

Sons of R. Ḥiyya
The mishna says a vessel connects (מצרף) its contents. Doesn't this mean all items inside are joined — whether or not they touch?
Rav Kahana
The mishna says it joins — but only when the item requires the vessel for sanctification, like meal offering flour. Items that don't need a vessel for sanctification are NOT joined by it.
Sons of R. Ḥiyya
Then here's a dilemma: if one who immersed that day inserts a finger between two half-tenths in a vessel — touching neither — are the half-tenths rendered impure through the vessel's joining alone?
Rav Kahana
And my question: if two half-tenths are in one vessel but not touching — can one remove a handful from one half-tenth on behalf of the other?
Sons of R. Ḥiyya
We heard an analogous case: two meal offerings in one vessel — if a handful can be taken from each, both are fit. This implies intermixed portions can serve one another.
Core Principle A vessel joins its contents (כלי מצרף) for sanctification and impurity — but only when the vessel itself is integral to the item's sanctity.

✋ When Can a Handful Serve Two Half-Tenths in One Vessel?

Two half-tenths of an ephah placed in one vessel, not touching
↓
Rav Kahana's question: may one remove a handful from one half on behalf of the other?
↓
Rava cites baraita: One may NOT bring a tenth of an ephah in two vessels
↓
Rava's inference: in one vessel, removal of a handful from one portion is valid for the other
↓
Abaye Challenges
Perhaps 'two vessels' means a vessel hollowed into two kefiza-shaped chambers — internally divided counts as two vessels!
Abaye's Alternative
'One vessel like two' = a hen trough divided by a ridge — still one vessel, yet functionally separated
↓
Conclusion: Question unresolved — Rava's proof challenged; we rely on the baraita's logic but its precise case is disputed

🔁 Can Impurity Compound Sequentially? Rava vs. Abaye

Abaye's Challenge

  • Do we really say an item saturated with impurity cannot receive a second lesser impurity?
  • Proof: mishna (Kelim 27:9) — a sheet impure from treading (zav lying on it) then made into a curtain is pure from treading
  • But if a zav later touched the curtain, it IS impure — a second impurity took effect!
  • This proves lesser impurity CAN be added to an already-impure item

Rava's Response

  • Rabbi Yosei's ruling may mean: the zav touched the sheet before treading — not after
  • If so, contact and treading impurity arose simultaneously, not sequentially
  • The question remains: can a second lesser impurity take effect after the first?
  • Final baraita proof: Rabbi Yosei confirms contact adds to treading even after — but only when both are of different types

🎲 The Lost and Found Half-Tenth — Rava's Analysis

Step 1: A tenth of an ephah is split into two halves (½ aleph + ½ bet)
↓
Step 2: ½ aleph is lost → a replacement ½ gimel is separated to stand in for it
↓
Step 3: ½ aleph is later found → now three half-tenths exist together in one vessel
↓
Rava — Impurity logic: If ½ gimel (replacement) becomes impure, it joins ½ bet (its designated partner) into impurity — but ½ aleph (the found half) does NOT join, since it was found after gimel was designated
↓
Abaye disagrees: All three are 'residents of one cabin' — they were all meant to be part of one meal offering, so any one becoming impure pulls in the other two
↓
Rav Pappa objects to Abaye: Even the remainder of the handful-half itself: one-sixth (danka) of the handful was not sacrificed to permit this half's remainder — so how is it eaten?
↓
Rav Yitzḥak objects: One-third of the handful was designated to permit an extraneous half-tenth — it is non-sacred, disqualifying the entire handful
