Arakhin 29 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
When Rav stated the Candelabrum's height was nine handbreadths, what did he actually mean?
A. The total height of the Menorah from base to tip is nine handbreadths
B. The functional lamp area starts nine handbreadths above the ground; the total height is eighteen
C. The decorative base of the Menorah alone measures nine handbreadths
D. The central shaft, excluding branches, is nine handbreadths
Correct: B — Rav clarified to Rav Shimi bar Ḥiyya that the total Menorah height is eighteen handbreadths. He was speaking of the point at which the lamps begin — nine handbreadths up — explaining why steps were still needed to reach them.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What did Moses find the Holy One doing when he ascended to Heaven?
A. Writing the Torah on stone tablets of fire
B. Instructing the angels on Tabernacle construction
C. Tying decorative crowns (taggin) on letters of the Torah
D. Fashioning the heavenly Menorah as a model for Moses
Correct: C — Moses found God tying crowns (ziyyunin/taggin) on the letters of the Torah. God explained that Rabbi Akiva would one day derive heaps of laws from each individual stroke.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What comforted Moses after he sat in Rabbi Akiva's class and could not follow the discussion?
A. Rabbi Akiva recognized Moses and translated the teaching for him
B. God appeared in the study hall and reassured Moses
C. When students asked for a source, Akiva answered: 'It is a law given to Moses at Sinai'
D. Moses suddenly remembered teaching this very law at Sinai
Correct: C — Moses' strength had waned from not understanding anything being taught. When Akiva's students pressed him for a source, Akiva replied 'It is a law given to Moses at Sinai' — hearing his own name invoked as the authority comforted Moses.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why was this world created specifically with the letter heh (ה)?
A. Heh is the numerical value of five, representing the Five Books of Moses
B. Heh is the most frequently occurring letter in the Torah
C. Heh resembles a portico open at the bottom, representing easy exit — and repentance through a narrow gap with Heaven's help
D. Heh begins the word הַבְרָאָה (creation) and was therefore chosen
Correct: C — The letter ה is shaped like a portico with an opening at the bottom — anyone who wishes to sin may easily depart. The narrow suspended left leg represents the difficult path back through repentance, which requires divine assistance (as taught by Reish Lakish).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rava, how many letters require three crowns (ziyyunin), and which group do they form?
A. Five letters — the final forms: ך ם ן ף ץ
B. Seven letters — shin, ayin, tet, nun, zayin, gimmel, tzadi (שעטנזג"ץ)
C. Ten letters — all letters with vertical ascending strokes
D. Three letters — alef, mem, shin, the three mothers of Kabbalistic tradition
Correct: B — Rava teaches that seven letters each require three crowns: ש ע ט נ ז ג ץ — traditionally remembered by the mnemonic שַׁעַטְנֵ"ז גָּ"ץ (sha'atnez gatz). Rav Ashi adds that the precise scribes of Rav's school were even more exacting in their application.
