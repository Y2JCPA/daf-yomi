⏰ When Does Sacrificing the Handful Permit the Remainder?

Rabbi Ḥanina
The remainder is permitted from when the fire first takes hold of the handful — the moment it ignites on the altar.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
The remainder is permitted only from when the majority of the handful has been consumed by the altar fire.
Rav Yehuda
Explaining Rabbi Yoḥanan: "The smoke of the land went up as the smoke of a furnace" — a furnace does not release smoke until most of the fuel is consumed. So too the handful.
Practical Consequence If the handful is placed on the altar precisely at sunset, Rabbi Yoḥanan's view means the majority cannot be consumed before night — the remainder would never become permitted that day.

🥣 Remainder of the Meal Offering — What Invalidates?

Rabbi Eliezer
If the remainder became impure, was burned, or was lost — the meal offering is fit. The handful alone atones, the remainder's fate is irrelevant.
Rabbi Yehoshua
If the remainder became impure — unfit. If burned or lost — the handful may still be sacrificed, because the priests could have eaten it.
Rav
Rabbi Yehoshua's rule applies only when all the remainder became impure. If only part became impure, the offering is fit — the intact portion suffices for priestly consumption.
Key Distinction (Rav) Partial impurity of the remainder ≠ total impurity. Only when every bit of remainder is lost or impure does Rabbi Yehoshua's invalidation apply.

🩸 What Survives the Offering? — Conditions for Blood Sprinkling

Olive-bulk of Meat

  • ✅ Blood IS sprinkled
  • Meat = fit for human eating
  • Classic case — explicit in Torah
  • Baseline for all other rulings

Olive-bulk of Fat (Chelev)

  • ✅ Blood IS sprinkled
  • Fat = fit for altar offering
  • Source: "For a pleasing aroma" (Lev. 17:6)
  • Derived by Rabbi Yoḥanan / Rabbi Yishmael

Lobe of Liver or Two Kidneys

  • ✅ Blood IS sprinkled
  • Obligatory altar portions
  • Same verse: "for a pleasing aroma"
  • Anything offered on altar counts

½ olive meat + ½ olive fat

  • ❌ Blood is NOT sprinkled
  • Half-measures do not combine
  • Meat and fat serve different purposes
  • Neither alone reaches olive-bulk threshold

🤲 Handful Sanctification — The Service Vessel Dispute

Standard procedure: Priest removes handful (kometz) from the meal offering and places it in a kli shareit (service vessel) for sanctification before bringing it to the altar.
↓
The Rabbis: Service vessel sanctification is required throughout — from removal to altar. Without it the handful is unfit. Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak holds all agree on this.
↓
Rabbi Yehuda son of Rabbi Ḥiyya: Rabbi Shimon derives leniency from Lev. 6:10 — "It is most holy, as the sin offering and the guilt offering" — those offerings require no vessel sanctification either.
↓
Rabbi Shimon's view (baraita): Once the handful is sanctified in the vessel at removal, the priest may carry it to the altar in his bare right hand — no vessel needed for transport.
↓
Conclusive refutation of Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak: The baraita ruling that the handful returned to the right hand (not re-placed in a vessel) is valid proves Rabbi Shimon does not require ongoing vessel sanctification.

✌️ Handful Burned in Two Increments — Is It Fit?

Priest burns the handful in two separate acts rather than all at once — is the offering fit?
↓
Mishna rules: It is fit — but what does this mean exactly?
↓
Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi
Fit only if each increment burned is at least half an olive-bulk — a sub-olive burning has no legal significance
Alternative Opinion
Fit even if each increment is less than half an olive-bulk — the combined total burning suffices
↓
Rabbi Zeira's analysis: Dispute turns on whether a sub-olive-bulk handful and a sub-olive-bulk burning carry independent legal significance. Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi says no — therefore the mishna's "two increments" = two halves of exactly one olive-bulk.
