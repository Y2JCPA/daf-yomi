QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Elazar, what is the status of a griddle-cake offering if the High Priest brought only half a tenth of an ephah in the morning?
A. Completely invalid — a full tenth must be brought each time
B. Valid after the fact (b'dieved), since the offering is designed to be sacrificed in halves
C. Valid only if the second half is brought immediately without delay
D. Invalid — the offering must be restarted from the beginning
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar holds that while ideally (l'chatchila) a full tenth must be brought and then divided, if half was brought it remains valid b'dieved, since the griddle-cake is in any case sacrificed in two halves — morning and evening.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rav hold regarding a meal offering whose oil was not placed in the service vessel together with the flour?
A. It is completely invalid — all components must be together for any sanctification
B. It is sanctified, since flour can be sanctified by a service vessel without its oil
C. It is sanctified only if the priest had explicit intention to add oil later
D. It must be returned to the vessel with oil before proceeding
Correct: B — Rav holds that a meal offering can be sanctified by a service vessel even without its oil — analogous to the shewbread, which is sanctified despite containing no oil. Flour and oil may each be sanctified independently.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rabbi Hanina's requirement for a standard meal offering (requiring oil and frankincense) to be sanctified by a service vessel?
A. Only the flour needs to be in the vessel; oil is sanctified when added later
B. Only oil and frankincense must be together; flour can be placed in separately
C. All components — flour, oil, and frankincense — must be placed in the vessel together
D. The vessel itself must be specially anointed for that specific type of offering
Correct: C — Rabbi Hanina holds that for a meal offering requiring oil and frankincense, all components must be in the service vessel together. Neither is sanctified alone: not flour without oil, not oil without frankincense.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The Gemara resolves the baraita's apparent contradiction to Rabbi Elazar's ruling that handful-removal in the Sanctuary is valid. What is the resolution?
A. The verse 'from there' refers exclusively to the Sanctuary, not the courtyard
B. The verse is needed to permit the entire courtyard — not to exclude the more-sacred Sanctuary
C. Rabbi Elazar holds the verse is a mere mnemonic with no practical halakhic force
D. The Sanctuary is treated as equivalent to the courtyard for all sacrificial purposes
Correct: B — The verse 'he shall remove from there his handful' (Lev. 2:2) is needed to establish that the handful may be removed anywhere in the courtyard — not only at one designated spot. It does not come to exclude the Sanctuary, which is of greater sanctity.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara cite Rabbi Yochanan's ruling that peace offerings slaughtered in the Sanctuary are valid?
A. To prove that the Sanctuary is holier than the courtyard for all sacrificial purposes
B. To demonstrate that Rabbi Yochanan does derive halakhot across categories, countering the claim he avoids such derivations
C. To establish that peace offerings may be slaughtered anywhere in the Temple complex
D. To show that a kal v'chomer argument is stronger than a gezerah shavah
Correct: B — The Gemara raises Rabbi Yochanan's ruling on peace offerings to prove that he does apply cross-category halakhic derivations (kal v'chomer). This undermines the suggestion that Rabbi Yochanan avoids deriving one matter from another in the laws of consecrated items — and explains why he can derive the meal offering rule from the griddle-cake.
