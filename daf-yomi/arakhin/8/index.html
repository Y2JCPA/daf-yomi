🫓 Can the High Priest's Griddle-Cake Be Sanctified in Halves?

Rabbi Yochanan
The griddle-cake offering is not sanctified in halves. Since it is brought as a whole and then divided, it must be placed whole in the service vessel to be sanctified.
Rabbi Elazar
Since it is sacrificed in halves — half in the morning, half in the evening — each half is sanctified independently. The verse requiring a whole offering applies only ab initio; after the fact, half is valid.
Gemara
The Mishna (50b) states: the High Priest brings a full tenth of an ephah and then divides it. This implies it must come whole — an objection to Rabbi Elazar!
Rabbi Elazar
The Mishna's requirement is only l'chatchila (ideally). If half was brought, it remains valid b'dieved (after the fact). Rav Giddel confirms this in the name of Rav.
Key Background The griddle-cake offering (חֲבִיתֵי כֹהֵן גָּדוֹל) is unique: it is a full tenth of an ephah brought whole, then divided — half offered in the morning and half in the evening.

⚗️ Can Meal Offerings Be Sanctified Without All Their Components?

Rav
A meal offering can be sanctified by a service vessel even without its oil — just as the shewbread (which has no oil) is sanctified. Oil and frankincense can each also be sanctified independently of one another.
Rabbi Hanina
A meal offering that requires oil and frankincense is sanctified only when all components are placed together in the service vessel. Neither flour, oil, nor frankincense can be sanctified in isolation from the others.
Shmuel
I agree with Rav. Proof from a Mishna: bowls used for blood sanctify dry goods — meaning flour can be sanctified in a vessel used for liquids, separately from oil. The meal offering is considered 'dry' relative to blood.
Proofs for Rav The log of oil of a leper (לֹג שֶׁמֶן שֶׁל מְצוֹרָע) proves oil sanctified alone; the shewbread (לֶחֶם הַפָּנִים) proves flour sanctified without oil.

📏 Half a Tenth of an Ephah Set Aside for a Meal Offering — Three Positions

Rav

  • Half a tenth set aside for any meal offering is not sanctified
  • Even if one intended to later add to reach a full tenth
  • Derives standard meal offering rules from the griddle-cake
  • The griddle-cake requires a whole measure — and so does any meal offering

Rabbi Yochanan

  • For standard meal offerings: half a tenth is sanctified
  • Does not derive standard offerings from the griddle-cake
  • The griddle-cake has a unique requirement to be presented whole
  • Does derive halakhot across categories in other contexts (peace offerings)

Rabbi Elazar

  • For the griddle-cake: each half can be sanctified independently
  • Derives meal-offering rules from other meal offerings only
  • Will not derive from blood offerings to meal offerings
  • L'chatchila: whole; b'dieved: half is valid

🤲 Is Removal of a Handful Inside the Sanctuary Valid?

Priest removes the handful (kemitza) inside the Sanctuary rather than in the Temple courtyard
↓
Rabbi Elazar: Valid — the Sanctuary is more sacred; a kal v'chomer applies: if valid in the less-sacred courtyard, certainly valid in the more-sacred Sanctuary
↓
Objection from Baraita
Verse (Lev. 2:2): 'he shall remove from there his handful' — implies specifically the courtyard, not the Sanctuary
Resolution
The verse is needed to permit the entire courtyard — not just one spot in it. It does not exclude the Sanctuary.
↓
Proof: Burnt offerings and sin offerings are valid when performed anywhere in the courtyard — likewise the handful is valid in any more-sacred location

🕊️ Peace Offerings Slaughtered in the Sanctuary

וּשְׁחָטוֹ פֶּתַח אֹהֶל מוֹעֵד (ויקרא ג:ב)
Rabbi Yochanan's Ruling Peace offerings (shelamim) slaughtered inside the Sanctuary are valid, even though the verse says 'at the entrance of the Tent of Meeting' (Leviticus 3:2). The reasoning is kal v'chomer: if valid in the less-sacred entrance, certainly valid in the most sacred space. The Gemara cites this ruling to prove that Rabbi Yochanan does derive halakhot across categories — undercutting any claim that he avoids cross-category derivations in the laws of consecrated items.
