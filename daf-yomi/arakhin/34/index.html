🏠 Corner Entrance: Is a Mezuza Required?

Ameimar
A corner entrance is obligated in mezuza — the walls forming the corner are its doorposts.
Rav Ashi
But it has no proper doorposts at all — how can there be an obligation?
Ameimar
These (adei) are its doorposts — the ends of the walls constitute the doorpost structure of this entrance.
Rav Pappa
Mar Shmuel affixed a mezuza even to an entrance with only one doorpost — following Rabbi Meir's lenient ruling.
Principle Corner walls and single posts raise the question of what constitutes a valid 'doorpost' for mezuza purposes

📖 Source for Affixing Mezuza on the Right

וּכְתַבְתָּם עַל מְזוּזוֹת בֵּיתֶךָ וּבִשְׁעָרֶיךָ
Baraita — Deuteronomy 6:9 "And you shall write them upon the doorposts of your house [beitekha]" — just as when you enter a house you turn to the right, so too the mezuza is affixed on the right side of the entrance.

Rav Shmuel bar Aḥa (citing Rava bar Ulla) derives it differently: from the chest of Jehoiada the Priest (II Kings 12:10) — placed to the right of the altar entrance.

🏛️ One Doorpost: Tannaitic Dispute

Rabbi Meir — Obligated

  • One doorpost suffices to obligate mezuza
  • Source: 'doorposts' (plural) = ideally two
  • But the verse also has singular usage — includes one
  • Rav Pappa saw Mar Shmuel practice this ruling
  • Even a single post creates the structure of an entrance

Rabbis — Exempt

  • Two doorposts required to obligate mezuza
  • Source: 'doorposts' is consistently plural
  • Without two posts, entrance lacks proper halachic form
  • Single-post entrance → exempt from mezuza
  • Standard construction requires bilateral structure

📋 Ordering the Four Passages in Head Tefillin

Right compartment (right of the reader, left of wearer):
① Kadesh li — Exodus 13:1–10
② V'haya ki yevi'akha — Exodus 13:11–16
↓
Left compartment (left of reader, right of wearer):
③ Shema — Deuteronomy 6:4–9
④ V'haya im shamo'a — Deuteronomy 11:13–21
↓
Abaye resolves contradiction between two baraitot: 'right' means right of the reader (who faces the wearer) — not the wearer's own right
↓
Rav Ḥananel citing Rav: If passages are placed in the wrong order — the tefillin are unfit (פסול). Abaye: this applies even if exchanged unintentionally.
