QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Adda bar Ahava, why does Reish Lakish consider the omer handful removed not for its own sake to be valid?
A. Because improper intent never disqualifies meal offerings
B. Because an offering is not considered 'before its time' if it will be brought on that same day
C. Because the eastern horizon permits the new crop before the handful is removed
D. Because the omer is a communal offering and communal offerings are never disqualified
Correct: B — Rav Adda bar Ahava explains that Reish Lakish holds an offering is not considered 'before its time' if it is to be brought on that same day — so the omer handful is never untimely, and its removal with improper intent is still valid.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rav Pappa identify as Reish Lakish's actual reason that the omer handful is valid?
A. The Sages enacted a special leniency for the omer
B. The omer remainder can always be eaten without a replacement offering
C. The illumination of the eastern horizon permits the new crop, so the omer is never considered before its time
D. A communal offering overrides individual disqualification rules
Correct: C — Rav Pappa argues Reish Lakish's true position is that עמוד השחר (the illumination of the eastern horizon) itself permits the new crop — making the omer handful never 'before its time,' not merely a same-day rule.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If a priest places oil on the leper's thumb before placing blood, what must be done and why?
A. The entire leper purification process must restart from the beginning
B. Nothing — the oil placement is valid regardless of order
C. The oil placement must be performed again because the Torah writes 'shall be' (יִהְיֶה), requiring exact sequence
D. The priest must bring a sin offering for performing the rite out of order
Correct: C — Rav Pappa explains that leper halakhot use the expression יִהְיֶה ('shall be'), indicating the prescribed order — blood then oil — must be maintained. If violated, the oil must be placed again after the blood.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the Gemara's conclusion regarding the attempt to derive that tereifa is disqualified as an offering via a fortiori inference?
A. The a fortiori inference succeeds and no verse is needed
B. The a fortiori inference fails because tereifa is only rabbinically forbidden
C. The a fortiori inference cannot be completed — each proposed proof is refuted, requiring a Torah verse
D. The a fortiori inference works but only for burnt offerings, not other sacrifices
Correct: C — Every attempt to prove the a fortiori inference fails: Rav's omer proof, Reish Lakish's incense proof, Mar b. Ravina's Shabbat proof, and Rav Adda bar Abba's diverse kinds proof are all successfully refuted. A Torah verse is therefore necessary.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What objection does Rav Adda son of Rav Yitzḥak raise from the baraita distinguishing birds from meal offerings?
A. Meal offerings cannot be waved before the omer is brought
B. If an omer handful removed not for its own sake is valid, then meal offerings would also have a case where their general prohibition was permitted — contradicting the baraita
C. Birds require placement in a service vessel just like meal offerings
D. The zav's bird offering must be brought after the omer, not before
Correct: B — The baraita states birds (unlike meal offerings) have a case where their general prohibition was 'permitted' through sanctification. But if the omer handful is valid despite improper intent, meal offerings would similarly have such a case — undermining the baraita's distinction between the two categories.
