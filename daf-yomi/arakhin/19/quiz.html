Arakhin 19 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye, what is the fundamental dispute between Rabbi Shimon and the Rabbis in the baraita about 'with his finger'?
A. Whether a non-priest may collect the blood of a burnt offering
B. Whether a verse is interpreted based on what precedes and what succeeds it simultaneously
C. Whether the right hand or left hand is required for the placement of blood
D. Whether the pouring of oil must precede the placement of frankincense
Correct: B — Abaye resolves that the dispute is whether a single verse governs both the halakha that comes before it and the halakha that comes after it in the text — Rabbi Shimon disagrees with the Rabbis on this interpretive principle.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rav say about sacrificial rites described with the terms 'law' (תורה) or 'statute' (חוק)?
A. Both terms must appear together for the rite to be indispensable
B. These terms indicate the rite is optional and may be omitted without consequence
C. Wherever either term appears, failure to perform that rite invalidates the offering
D. The terms apply only to offerings brought during the Temple's dedication
Correct: C — Rav holds that wherever the Torah writes 'law' or 'statute' regarding a sacrificial rite, omitting or invalidly performing that rite disqualifies the offering. Either term alone suffices — they need not appear together.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Shmuel reject Rav's proof from Aaron's service on the eighth day of the Tabernacle's consecration?
A. Because Aaron used a utensil rather than his hand on that occasion
B. Because the verse refers to a different type of offering entirely
C. Because one cannot derive an eternal halakha from a temporary, once-occurring situation
D. Because Shmuel holds that repetition of a rite always proves it is dispensable
Correct: C — Shmuel argues the eighth-day consecration was a unique, temporary event. Deriving a permanent halakha for all generations from a one-time situational command is invalid — only widespread repetition (like the twelve princes, repeated 12 times) can do so.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The Gemara raises the case of the shewbread to challenge what position?
A. That 'statute' written about eating applies only to the eating rite, not the broader offering
B. That Rabbi Shimon's interpretive principle is inconsistent with the verse about blood placement
C. That Rav's principle about repeated rites applies to the handful (kometz)
D. That non-priests may bring the meal offering to the altar corner
Correct: A — The Gemara challenges the claim that 'statute' written about eating refers only to the eating rite. The shewbread also has 'statute' written about its eating — yet its rites are indispensable. The Gemara resolves: when 'statute' refers to eating, it covers the entire matter of the offering.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Eliezer, where is the meal offering brought, and why does this satisfy both phrases in the verse?
A. The northern corner — as that is where the altar fire burned most intensely
B. The eastern corner — facing Jerusalem and 'before the Lord'
C. The southwestern corner — satisfying 'before the Lord' (west) and 'in front of the altar' (south) simultaneously
D. The center of the altar base — equidistant from all sides
Correct: C — Rabbi Eliezer rules that both phrases must be fulfilled at once: 'before the Lord' points westward (toward the Holy of Holies) and 'in front of the altar' points southward. Only the southwestern corner satisfies both. Rav Ashi adds that Rabbi Eliezer held the altar stood in the northern part of the courtyard.
