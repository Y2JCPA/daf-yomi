Arakhin 18 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara's final resolution, what did Rabbi Elazar ben Shammua confirm for Yosef the Babylonian that restored 'what he had lost'?
A. That the Rabbis deem the offering fit and Rabbi Eliezer deems it unfit
B. That all agree the offering is unfit when one intends to leave blood until the next day
C. That the offering is fit regardless of the priest's intention
D. That Rabbi Yehuda's original ruling was mistaken
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that Rabbi Yehuda had taught Yosef that all agree the offering is unfit when one intends to leave blood overnight. Rabbi Elazar confirmed this same ruling, restoring Yosef's confidence in his teacher's tradition.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Elazar ben Shammua's eyes stream with tears in the story of Yosef the Babylonian?
A. He was distressed at having taught incorrect halakha for years
B. He was overwhelmed by Yosef's extraordinary love of Torah and persistent questioning
C. He was frustrated that Yosef kept repeating the same question
D. He was moved by discovering that Rabbi Yehuda had been correct all along
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar wept with joy upon realizing the depth of Yosef's devotion, exclaiming: 'Happy are you, Torah scholars, for whom matters of Torah are exceedingly dear!'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If a priest broke the loaves of a meal offering into pieces larger than required (pittim merubbot), what is the halakhic ruling?
A. The meal offering is completely invalid and must be repeated
B. The priest must break the pieces again into the proper size
C. The meal offering is fit
D. The handful must be removed again from the rebroken pieces
Correct: C — The Mishna explicitly rules that even breaking into overly large pieces does not invalidate the meal offering — it is fit. The Gemara notes this required explicit teaching lest one think large pieces are worse than no breaking at all.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rabbi Zeira's principle about mixing oil into a meal offering?
A. Any amount of flour must be mixed with oil or the offering is invalid
B. For any measure of flour suitable for mixing, failure to actually mix does not invalidate
C. Only a Kohen Gadol may perform the mixing for validity
D. Mixing must precede pouring or both acts are invalid
Correct: B — Rabbi Zeira teaches that for any measure of flour that is intrinsically suitable for mixing with oil, failure to perform the mixing does not invalidate the meal offering — mixing is a mitzva but not an indispensable requirement.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How do the Rabbis derive that pouring oil onto a meal offering is valid even when performed by a non-priest?
A. From a halakha l'Moshe miSinai (oral tradition from Sinai)
B. From the verse which places the pouring of oil before the command to 'bring it to Aaron's sons'
C. From the principle that all preparatory acts may be performed by non-priests
D. From the fact that oil pouring is not listed among the avodot of the Temple service
Correct: B — The Rabbis read the verse 'He shall pour oil upon it… and bring it to Aaron's sons' as indicating that pouring occurs before priestly involvement — therefore it is not a strictly priestly act and a non-priest's performance does not invalidate.
