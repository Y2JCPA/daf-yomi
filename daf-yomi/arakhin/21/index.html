🧂 Which Offerings Require Salt?

Leviticus 2:13
"Every meal offering of yours you shall season with salt"
↓
❌ Excluded by Scripture
Wine libations — not placed on fire
Blood — sprinkled on altar corners
Wood — not susceptible to impurity
Incense — sacrificed on inner altar
✅ Requires Salting
All meal offerings, burnt offerings, and other items placed directly on the altar fire
↓
Why derive blood's exclusion explicitly?
Lest one think even a tiny sprinkle of salt is required — salting blood extracts it and creates liability for consuming blood

🔍 R. Yishmael's Derivation of Salt Exemptions

Baraita (R. Yishmael b. R. Yoḥanan ben Beroka):
The verse "with all your offerings you shall offer salt" is interpreted to exclude four items
↓
Wood
Not susceptible to ritual impurity → excluded from salting obligation
Wine & Blood
Not placed on the altar fire — they are sprinkled on the corner of the altar
Incense
Sacrificed entirely on the inner altar — a separate domain
↓
Tension: Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's view on wood creates difficulty — the Gemara resolves by attributing the baraita to R. Yishmael's distinct derivation method

🩸 Drinking Cooked Blood — Is There a Prohibition?

Ze'eiri
Blood that one cooked — one does NOT transgress a prohibition by drinking it
Abaye
Objection from a baraita: blood that curdled in the sun — one IS liable for eating it!
Rava
No difficulty: Ze'eiri speaks of blood curdled by fire (altar-disqualified); the baraita speaks of blood curdled by sun (still altar-valid)
Abaye
But even sun-curdled blood is disqualified from the altar — shouldn't it lose its prohibition status, like Rabbi Mani's inquiry suggests?
Abaye
Perhaps: Ze'eiri speaks of inner sin offering blood; the baraita speaks of external sin offering blood — only external blood retains full liability
Final Ruling (Rava) Blood of external sin offerings: eating congealed form = liable (Rav Ḥisda). Blood of inner sin offerings: also liable (Rava), since corresponding blood is fit for the external altar

🏺 External vs. Inner Sin Offering Blood

External Sin Offering Blood

  • Sprinkled on the external altar in the Temple courtyard
  • Congealed blood: eating it = liable (Rav Ḥisda)
  • "The priest shall take of the blood" — prohibition fully applies
  • Interposes during ritual immersion (Rav Giddel / Ze'eiri)
  • Primary case the baraita addresses

Inner Sin Offering Blood

  • Sprinkled on the inner altar or between the curtains
  • Congealed blood: also liable (Rava's extension)
  • Reason: corresponding blood is valid for the external altar
  • Rav Pappa: therefore also interposes in immersion
  • Ze'eiri's leniency (no prohibition) applies only here when curdled by fire

📋 Proper Salting of an Offering — Step by Step

Apply salt to both sides of the meat (Abaye's practice, mirroring Temple salting)
↓
Let it rest until the blood drains out completely
↓
Rinse / wash the meat after salting before cooking
↓
"You shall season" (תִּמְלַח): Not a mere pinch — but also not infused throughout like wisdom fills a person (Rav Ashi)
↓
"You shall not omit salt" (לֹא תַשְׁבִּית): Use the "never-resting" salt — the ongoing supply stored in the Chamber of Salt (לשכת המלח)
↓
Limit: Do not over-salt like mixing straw (תֶּבֶן) into clay — moderate, purposeful application only (Rabba bar Ulla)
