Arakhin 21 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R. Yishmael b. R. Yoḥanan ben Beroka, why is wood excluded from the salting requirement?
A. It is not offered directly on the altar fire
B. It is not susceptible to ritual impurity
C. The verse explicitly excludes it by name
D. It has no food value and thus cannot be salted
Correct: B — R. Yishmael derives that wood is excluded because it is not susceptible to ritual impurity — a key criterion in his interpretive framework for identifying excluded items.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rava distinguishes Ze'eiri's leniency (no prohibition for drinking cooked blood) from the baraita's strict ruling. What is the key difference?
A. Ze'eiri refers to blood of animals; the baraita to blood of birds
B. Ze'eiri refers to blood curdled by fire; the baraita to blood curdled by sun
C. Ze'eiri refers to raw blood; the baraita to cooked blood
D. Ze'eiri permits a small amount; the baraita prohibits any amount
Correct: B — Rava resolves the contradiction: blood curdled by fire is disqualified from the altar and loses its prohibition; blood curdled by sun remains altar-valid and retains its prohibition against consumption.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rava rule that eating congealed blood of inner sin offerings also creates liability?
A. The Torah explicitly mentions inner blood in the prohibition
B. Inner blood was never actually disqualified from the altar
C. Corresponding blood from inner offerings is suitable for the external altar
D. Rabbi Ḥisda's ruling on external blood automatically extends to inner blood
Correct: C — Rava reasons by analogy: since blood corresponding to inner sin offering blood is fit to be placed on the external altar, it retains its blood-prohibition status even in its inner-offering context.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The baraita derives that the Temple — not the individual — must supply salt for offerings. This is derived by analogy to which other Temple provision?
A. Wine libations, which the Temple always supplies
B. Wood for the altar, which applies to all offerings and is publicly funded
C. Frankincense, which accompanies the meal offering in one vessel
D. The shewbread, as per the everlasting covenant of salt
Correct: B — The baraita analogizes salt to wood: both apply to all offerings (not just meal offerings), and just as wood is provided by the Temple from public funds, so too is salt.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rav Giddel citing Ze'eiri rules that blood interposes (ḥatzitzah) during ritual immersion. Under what condition?
A. Only when the blood is still moist and liquid
B. Only when the blood has dried and hardened
C. Whether the blood is moist or dry
D. Only when the blood covers a majority of the body
Correct: C — Ze'eiri rules that blood constitutes an interposition during immersion regardless of state — both moist (liquid) and dry (dried/crusted) blood prevent valid immersion.
