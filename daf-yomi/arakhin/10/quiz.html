QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Torah need BOTH 'upon the blood of the guilt offering' (14:17) AND 'upon the place of the blood' (14:28)?
A. One verse applies to wealthy lepers, the other to poor lepers using different locations on the body
B. Without both, we might think oil may only be placed when blood is present — or only after blood is removed — but not in both situations
C. The first verse governs the right thumb, the second governs the right big toe
D. They teach about two separate offerings brought by the leper
Correct: B — Either verse alone would mislead: 'upon the blood' alone implies oil only when blood is present; 'upon the place of the blood' alone implies oil only once blood is removed. Both are needed to validate oil placement in either scenario.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava, when does mention of 'priesthood' in a verse create a right-hand requirement?
A. Always — anytime a verse assigns a rite to priests, the right hand is mandatory
B. Only when the verse also explicitly names the right hand
C. Only when the rite is indispensable to atonement (precluding atonement if omitted)
D. Only in combination with a mention of the finger
Correct: C — Rava limits the right-hand requirement to rites that preclude atonement. Conveyance of burnt-offering limbs has priesthood mentioned yet does not require the right hand, since the rite is not indispensable to achieving atonement.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What halakha does Rav Sheisha son of Rav Idi derive from the extra mention of the 'left hand' in the poor leper passage?
A. The oil rite may be performed by a non-priest using either hand
B. A priest's right hand is disqualified — only the left hand may be used for this rite
C. The oil must be applied to the leper's left side, not the right
D. A left-handed priest is valid for this rite but not for others
Correct: B — The superfluous mention of the left hand in the poor leper passage comes specifically to teach that a priest may not substitute his right hand — the left is required and the right is disqualified for this rite.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yehuda son of Rabbi Ḥiyya derive that kemitza (removing the handful) requires the right hand?
A. Directly from the verse 'and the priest shall take of the oil with his right finger'
B. From a verbal analogy comparing the meal offering to sin offerings and guilt offerings
C. From Reish Lakish's rule, since the word 'finger' appears in the meal-offering passage
D. From the leper passage, which explicitly states the right hand
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda son of Rabbi Ḥiyya uses a gezerah shavah (verbal analogy) comparing the meal offering to sin and guilt offerings. Just as those require the right hand, so does the removal of the handful from a meal offering.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the key difference between Rava and Rabbi Shimon regarding the right-hand requirement?
A. Rava requires the rite to be performed by a kohen gadol; Rabbi Shimon requires any kohen
B. Rava holds that either 'finger' or 'priesthood' in the verse suffices (for indispensable rites); Rabbi Shimon requires both to appear together
C. Rava derives the rule from leper verses; Rabbi Shimon derives it from the Tabernacle inauguration
D. Rava applies the rule to all rites; Rabbi Shimon limits it to rites involving blood
Correct: B — Rava: either indicator (finger OR priesthood) suffices, provided the rite is indispensable to atonement. Rabbi Shimon: both finger AND priesthood must appear in the same verse — priesthood alone never creates the right-hand requirement.
