QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Rav Ashi argue that a verse — not a kal v'chomer — is required to disqualify a tereifa?
A. The kal v'chomer is refuted from the outset by the case of the blemished animal, which has a priest-animal equation that tereifa lacks
B. Because tereifa is more severe than blemish and cannot be derived from a lenient case
C. Because Rav Sheisha already provided a verse, so no inference is needed
D. Because a kal v'chomer can never disqualify an animal offering
Correct: A — Rav Ashi argues that the kal v'chomer fails immediately: the blemished animal case includes a unique feature (the Torah equates blemished priests with blemished animals) that tereifa does not share, so the inference collapses before it even begins.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the verse 'Whatever passes under the rod' (Lev. 27:32) add to the disqualification of tereifa?
A. It disqualifies a tereifa that was already tereifa before sanctification
B. It disqualifies a tereifa that never grazed in the pastures of Israel
C. It disqualifies a tereifa that became tereifa only after it was sanctified
D. It is the primary source and makes all other verses redundant
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that this verse covers the case of an animal sanctified while still healthy that subsequently became a tereifa — the earlier verses only addressed animals that were already tereifa at or before sanctification.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Mishna on Daf 6, which of the following does NOT disqualify a meal offering's handful-removal?
A. A non-priest removing the handful
B. A ritually impure priest removing the handful
C. A right-handed priest removing the handful
D. A priest who is an acute mourner removing the handful
Correct: C — The Mishna requires the right hand — so a right-handed priest using the right hand is the valid procedure. It is left-hand removal that disqualifies, along with non-priests, impure priests, mourners, and seated priests.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Ben Beteira rule should be done when a priest removes the handful with his left hand?
A. The meal offering is permanently disqualified and must be burned without the handful being offered
B. The handful is returned to the meal offering vessel and the priest re-removes it with his right hand
C. A different priest must perform the entire service from the beginning
D. The handful is discarded and a fresh handful is taken immediately
Correct: B — Ben Beteira rules that the invalid handful is returned to the vessel containing the meal offering, and the priest then removes it again properly with the right hand — rectifying the invalid removal.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Rav Naḥman's resolution of the dispute about whether an unfit person's handful can be returned?
A. An unfit person's removal is never considered a sacrificial rite, so it can always be returned
B. An unfit person's removal is a complete sacrificial rite that permanently disqualifies the offering
C. The removal is a sacrificial rite but is not complete until the handful is placed in a service vessel — so it may be returned before that point
D. Only the placement in the service vessel matters; the removal itself is irrelevant
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman resolves the apparent contradiction by explaining that while an unfit person's removal does count as performing a rite, the rite of the handful is not fully complete until placement in the service vessel. Before that point, the offering has not been fully disqualified and the handful may be returned.
