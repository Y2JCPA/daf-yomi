🌾 Two Versions of Rabbi Ḥanina's Ruling

First Version
Rabbi Ḥanina spoke about a chest (Kelim 18:1): a wooden chest holding 40 se'a is not susceptible to impurity — too large to be a vessel
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
No — Rabbi Ḥanina spoke about wine (Teharot 3:2): impure liquid is always considered first-degree impurity, regardless of quantity
Rabbi Shimon Shezuri adds Regarding the chest: if the legs were one handbreadth high, the enclosed space between the rims and legs is included in measuring toward the 40 se'a threshold

🌾 Untithed Produce (Tevel) Mixed with Tithed

Rabbi Shimon Shezuri: minority of tevel fell into majority of fully tithed produce — what to do?
↓
He asks Rabbi Tarfon for guidance
↓
By Torah Law
Minority tevel is nullified in the majority — no obligation
By Rabbinic Law
Not nullified — tithes must still be separated
↓
Rabbi Tarfon's solution: take produce from a gentile — rabbinicly obligated in tithes but not Toraically — and separate tithes from it on behalf of the mixed tevel
↓
Rav Pappa confirms: halakha follows Rabbi Shimon Shezuri even in this case of tevel

📜 Two Types of Parchment for Torah Scroll Repair

"Old" Parchment

  • Not processed with gall (afatzim)
  • Cannot be sewn regardless of tear size
  • Named 'old' by treatment, not actual age
  • Scroll written on it remains permanently unfit if torn 3+ lines

"New" Parchment

  • Processed and hardened with gall (afatzim)
  • Can be sewn if tear spans only 2 lines
  • Named 'new' by treatment, not actual age
  • Scroll rendered fit again after proper sewing

🔄 How to Prepare and Roll a Mezuza

Write the two Torah passages: Shema (Deut. 6:4–9) and VeHaya (Deut. 11:13–21) — in two-by-two or similar valid format
↓
Final line: write al ha'aretz ('upon the land') alone — the conclusion of the blessing of longevity in the land
↓
Roll from left to right: start rolling from eḥad (last word of first verse) toward Shema (first word) — observed by Rabbi Ḥelbo from Rav Huna
↓
Result: when inserted into the doorpost case, Shema Yisrael faces outward — the declaration of God's unity is visible first
↓
Rabbi Meir's practice (minority): wrote mezuza on dokhsostos (inner hide layer, not outer parchment) in column format — like a Torah scroll
