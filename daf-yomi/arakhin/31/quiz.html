Arakhin 31 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav, if a tear in a Torah scroll parchment extends into three lines, what is the ruling?
A. It may be sewn if the parchment was processed with gall
B. It cannot be sewn and the scroll is unfit
C. It may be sewn only by a certified scribe
D. It depends on whether the tear is vertical or horizontal
Correct: B — Rav rules that a tear extending into three lines cannot be sewn to render the scroll fit. Only a tear spanning two lines or fewer may be repaired by sewing.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does 'old parchment' mean in the context of Torah scroll repair on Daf 31?
A. Parchment that is over fifty years old
B. Parchment that was previously used for a different scroll
C. Parchment not processed with gall (afatzim)
D. Parchment that has yellowed or faded
Correct: C — 'Old' and 'new' do not refer to actual age. 'Old' means parchment not treated with gall — it cannot be sewn. 'New' means processed with gall — it can be sewn if only two lines are torn.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What solution did Rabbi Tarfon offer Rabbi Shimon Shezuri for his tevel mixed with tithed produce?
A. Discard the entire mixture to avoid any violation
B. Separate tithes directly from the mixture
C. Take produce from a gentile and separate tithes from it on behalf of the mixture
D. The mixture is automatically permitted since tevel is a minority
Correct: C — Rabbi Tarfon said to take produce from a gentile (Toraically exempt but rabbinically obligated in tithes) and separate tithes from it on behalf of the mixed tevel — satisfying the rabbinic requirement.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rav Ḥisda rules that the last two words of a mezuza — 'al ha'aretz' — must be written how?
A. As the opening words on the first line
B. Integrated into the middle of the VeHaya paragraph
C. By themselves alone on the final line
D. Written in larger letters than the rest of the text
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda rules that 'al ha'aretz' (upon the land) must appear by themselves on the final line of the mezuza, separated from the preceding word.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In which direction did Rav Huna roll a mezuza, as observed by Rabbi Ḥelbo?
A. From right to left, starting with the word Shema
B. From left to right, starting with eḥad and rolling toward Shema
C. From bottom to top, so the text faces downward
D. It was folded accordion-style rather than rolled
Correct: B — Rabbi Ḥelbo observed Rav Huna rolling the mezuza from eḥad (last word of the first verse) toward Shema — left to right — so that when placed in its case, Shema faces outward.
