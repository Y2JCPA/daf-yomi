⚖️ The Great Nullification Dispute

Rabbis
Items that ascend to the altar do not nullify one another — this is the primary rule. A handful mixed with another offering-type is unfit, but not because of min b'mino universally.
Rabbi Yehuda
Min b'mino — same type in contact with the same type — is never nullified anywhere. A handful mixed with priestly, High Priest, or libation meal offerings is therefore unfit.
Rabbi Yochanan's Bombshell Both the Rabbis and Rabbi Yehuda derive their opposing principles from the very same verse about Yom Kippur blood sprinkling — yet they each emphasize a different facet of it!

🧂 Salt and Wood Must Come from Communal Funds

The Torah grants Israelites the right to use salt when bringing their offerings
↓
This right belongs specifically to those who donate their half-shekel to the Temple chamber
↓
The half-shekel chamber funds supply salt applied to all sacrificial offerings
↓
Wood for the altar must likewise come from communal (public) supplies, not private prior use
↓
R' Elazar ben Shammua: Just as the altar was built exclusively for God, the wood and fire must also be dedicated — never previously used by an ordinary person

🪵 Araunah's Threshing Sleds — What Are Morigim?

מנחות כ"ב — מוֹרִגִּים הַבָּקָר
Challenge to the Wood Requirement The Gemara challenges: Didn't David burn Araunah's threshing sleds (מוֹרִגִּים) as wood on his altar? That wood had been used previously by an ordinary person!

Ulla explains: Morigim means a turbal bed — a sharp threshing instrument.
Rav Yehuda clarifies: It is a device that presses down with teeth and tears apart grain during threshing.

The challenge dissolves: David's offering was an emergency act — not a model for standard Temple procedure requiring communal wood.

🫙 Mishna: Intermingled Kometz — Who Holds What?

Rabbis — Rule of Altar Items

  • Handful mixed with another handful: unfit (both ascend — neither nullifies the other)
  • Handful mixed with priestly meal offering: unfit (both ascend)
  • Handful mixed with High Priest's meal offering: unfit (both ascend)
  • Core principle: anything that ascends to the altar cannot be nullified by a fellow altar-item

Rabbi Yehuda — Rule of Min B'Mino

  • Handful mixed with priestly meal offering: unfit — min b'mino (flour/meal touching flour/meal)
  • Handful mixed with anointed priest's offering: unfit — min b'mino
  • Handful mixed with libation meal offering: unfit — min b'mino
  • Core principle: same type touching same type is NEVER nullified, period

📜 The Yom Kippur Verse — Two Derivations

Rabbis
From the verse about Yom Kippur blood sprinkling (bull + goat): since both ascend to the altar, they cannot nullify each other. The altar-context is the operative factor — min b'mino outside that context can still be nullified.
Rabbi Yehuda
The same verse teaches: since bull's blood and goat's blood are the same type (min b'mino), they never nullify each other. The altar ascent is merely incidental — the same-type rule applies universally.
Gemara's Analytic Resolution Had the verse shown only one feature — either altar-items or same-type — we could not derive the broader rule. Because it shows both simultaneously, each sage uses the dual context to extend his principle beyond the verse's narrow case.

⚖️ Is a Mixture of Meal Offerings Nullified?

A kometz (handful) becomes intermingled with another substance
↓
Same type (min b'mino) — e.g., flour + flour
Rabbi Yehuda: Never nullified
Rabbis: Depends on altar status
Different type (min b'she'eino mino)
Standard majority rule may apply — can be nullified
↓
Does the mixed substance also ascend to the altar?
↓
Yes — both altar-items
Rabbis: Unfit — altar items never nullify each other
Rabbi Yehuda: Same conclusion but via min b'mino logic
No — non-altar mixture
Rabbis: Majority can nullify
Rabbi Yehuda: If same-type, still not nullified
