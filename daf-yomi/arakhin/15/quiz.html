QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, what is the conclusion about the dispute between Rabbi Yehuda and the Rabbis — does it concern the frontplate (Tzitz)?
A. Yes — they dispute whether the Tzitz effects acceptance for impure altar items
B. Yes — they dispute whether the Tzitz effects acceptance for priest-consumed items
C. No — multiple proofs establish their dispute does not concern the Tzitz
D. No — they both agree the Tzitz has no effect whatsoever
Correct: C — Through proofs from Rav Huna b. Rav Natan (frankincense baraita), Rav Ashi (Pesachim mishna), and Ravina (two-loaves mishna), the Gemara concludes the Rabbi Yehuda/Rabbis dispute is NOT about the frontplate.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does a thanks offering render its loaves piggul, but not the other way around?
A. The loaves are more sacred and cannot be made piggul
B. The loaves depend on the offering's blood for their permission — the offering does not depend on the loaves
C. It is a rabbinic decree with no scriptural basis
D. The loaves are brought after the offering, so time-order prevents it
Correct: B — Rav Kahana teaches that the loaves are only permitted by the blood of the offering — they depend on it. Since the offering does not depend on the loaves, intent about the loaves cannot affect the offering.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rav's ruling on Rabbi Elazar's dilemma about combined intent (olive-bulk from offering AND loaves the next day)?
A. Both the thanks offering and the loaves are rendered piggul
B. Neither is rendered piggul since intent is split
C. The loaves are rendered piggul but the thanks offering is not
D. The thanks offering is rendered piggul but the loaves are not
Correct: C — Rav rules the loaves are rendered piggul (since they can be rendered piggul via intent at their own rite), but the thanks offering is not — the asymmetric rule still applies even with combined intent.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rabbi Meir's position on whether libations become piggul?
A. Libations can never become piggul because they are always brought independently
B. Libations become piggul only if the kohen had explicit intent about the libations themselves
C. Once designated, libations are fixed to the offering at slaughter and become piggul through the offering's piggul intent
D. Libations become piggul only at the moment they are poured on the altar
Correct: C — Rava explains that Rabbi Meir holds designated libations are fixed (קבועים) to the specific offering at the moment of slaughter — therefore piggul intent during slaughter renders them piggul as well.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Regarding the Shavuot lambs and two loaves, the Gemara says both cases (thanks offering + loaves AND lambs + two loaves) must be taught. Why?
A. Because the rules are different in each case and cannot be derived from one another
B. Because if only the thanks offering case were taught, one might think the loaves can render the offering piggul in the Shavuot case
C. Because the Shavuot case involves a stricter standard of piggul than the thanks offering
D. Because the loaves of a thanks offering and the two loaves of Shavuot have different levels of sanctity
Correct: B — The Gemara explains: had only the thanks offering case been taught, one might have thought the asymmetry is unique to it and that for Shavuot the loaves CAN render the lambs piggul. Both cases are necessary to establish the universal rule.
