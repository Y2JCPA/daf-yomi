🤔 Service Vessels: Is Specific Intent Required?

Questioner
When the priest returns the handful to the service vessel, it should become re-sanctified and disqualified — it is now inside a service vessel!
R' Yoḥanan
Service vessels sanctify items only when placed there with specific intent to be sanctified. Since the priest does not intend for sanctification on return, none occurs.
Reish Lakish
But your ruling implies vessels sanctify only with intent — what about vessels that sanctify without intent? I raised this very dilemma before you!
Rav Amram
Even if vessels sanctify without intent, one can return the handful to a heaped bowl (levisa) — the handful sits on top of the mound, not within the vessel's interior, avoiding sanctification entirely.
⚠️ Further Objection Once the handful was removed, a furrow was formed in the meal offering — so returning the handful places it within the vessel space, not atop a mound. The levisa solution only applies before any handful was removed.

📚 Avimi & Rav Ḥisda — The Reversed Teacher-Student Relationship

Avimi bar Pappi was a great Tanna; Rav Ḥisda originally studied under him
↓
Avimi forgot tractate Menaḥot — the tractate was 'uprooted' from him — and came before Rav Ḥisda to recall it
↓
Rav Naḥman encountered Avimi returning from Rav Ḥisda's study hall and asked: "How does one properly remove a handful?"
↓
Avimi gestured to a raised vessel: Remove it from here — implying the vessel must be lifted off the ground
↓
Rav Naḥman: Then you require three priests — one to raise the vessel, one to sanctify the offering, one to remove the handful!
↓
Avimi: "And let it require three priests!" — the Temple service can and does involve multiple officiants
↓
Rav Naḥman objects from a mishna (12a): All rites of the meal offering are listed there, yet raising the vessel is never mentioned — proving no such requirement exists

⬇️ Rava's Two Rulings on Grounded Vessels

Removing a Handful

  • ✅ Valid from a vessel resting on the ground
  • Proof: frankincense bowls removed from the shulchan (Table of Shewbread)
  • The Table's surface functions at ground level for this removal
  • No requirement to lift the vessel — removal is valid as-is

Sanctifying a Handful

  • ❓ Unresolved dilemma (Rava himself leaves it open)
  • If derived from meal offering: perhaps valid on the ground
  • If derived from blood collection: may require the vessel to be raised
  • The two paradigms point in different directions — תיקו

🩸 Blood Sprinkling — Verse Derivations (Lev. 4:6)

Verse TermLaw DerivedReasoning
"And the priest shall dip"Must dip finger — wiping alone is invalidActive dipping required, not passive contact
"In the blood" (בְּדַם)Full measure must be initially present in the vesselThe blood context implies sufficiency at the outset
"In the blood" (second application)Valid even if priest must wipe finger — if full measure was there initiallyPrevents invalidating the sprinkling retroactively
"Of the blood" (מִדַּם)Enough blood must remain for dipping after each sprinklingSupply cannot be exhausted mid-service
"Blood of the matter" (הַחַטָּאת)Excludes remainder on finger from prior sprinkling — must return to bowlEach sprinkling draws fresh blood; residue on finger is excluded

🩸 Blood Collection: Full Measure & the Two-Vessel Problem

Priest collects blood of a sin offering (חַטָּאת) for altar sprinkling
↓
Full measure in ONE vessel
✅ Valid — blood is sanctified and ready for sprinkling
Split between TWO vessels
❌ Invalid — blood is not sanctified in halves
Source: Rav Taḥlifa bar Avimi from verse structure
↓
R' Zerika citing R' Elazar: Blood of sin offering not sanctified unless a full measure was initially collected in a single vessel
↓
Between sprinklings
Priest wipes finger on edge of the bowl
(keforei = bowls; root kafar = to wipe — Ezra 1:10)
After all sprinklings
Priest wipes hand on the body of the heifer
(Num. 19:5 — heifer burned with her blood)
