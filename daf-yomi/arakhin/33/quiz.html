5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, where is the ideal placement of the mezuza on the doorpost?
A. One handbreadth from the ground
B. Exactly in the middle of the doorpost
C. At the beginning of the upper third of the doorpost
D. Adjacent to the crossbeam at the very top
Correct: C — Shmuel says it is a mitzva to place the mezuza at the beginning of the upper third. Rav Huna bar Rav Natan later clarifies this means the mezuza should not be distanced more than a third from the top crossbeam.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rabbi Ḥanina's teaching about the difference between God and a human king regarding protection?
A. A human king protects his people from outside; God protects from inside
B. A human king is guarded by servants inside; God needs no guards at all
C. A human king sits inside guarded by servants outside; God's people are inside and He guards them from outside
D. A human king's protection is temporary; God's protection is eternal
Correct: C — Rabbi Ḥanina teaches that unlike a human king guarded by servants outside his palace, Israel dwells inside and the Holy One guards them from outside — symbolized by the mezuza at the entrance.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rav rules that a mezuza affixed horizontally like a bolt is unfit. How is Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's reportedly horizontal mezuza explained?
A. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi disagreed with Rav's ruling
B. His doorpost was so wide that diagonal placement was simultaneously horizontal and vertical
C. His entrance was a gate, not a house, so different rules applied
D. The report was incorrect — his mezuza was actually vertical
Correct: B — The Gemara resolves that Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's doorpost was exceptionally wide, making a diagonal affixing count as both horizontal and vertical at once — a unique exception that does not contradict Rav's ruling.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Rav Ḥisda rule that a portico is exempt from mezuza even if it has doorposts?
A. A portico is considered part of the public domain
B. Its doorposts exist only to support the ceiling, not to frame a real entrance
C. A portico is too small to qualify as a dwelling
D. Porticos are temporary structures and all temporary structures are exempt
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda teaches that even a portico with doorposts is exempt because those posts are built to strengthen the ceiling, not to serve as an entrance — thus lacking the halakhic form of a doorway.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is a 'bei harziki' and what is its mezuza requirement?
A. A study hall — exempt from mezuza entirely
B. A small storage room requiring one mezuza
C. A gatehouse opening to a courtyard with houses also opening onto it — requires two mezuzot
D. A gatehouse with a garden entrance — exempt on the garden side
Correct: C — Rav Pappa the Elder in the name of Rav explains that a bei harziki is a gatehouse opening to a courtyard, with houses also opening onto that courtyard. Since it serves dual access — from outside and to the houses — two mezuzot are required.
