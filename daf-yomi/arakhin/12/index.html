📉 Does a Lacking Remainder Become Piggul?

Rav Huna
Even Rabbi Akiva agrees: a remainder that became lacking in measure CANNOT become piggul. The deficiency is intrinsic to the remainder itself — not an external factor.
Rava
On the contrary! Even Rabbi Eliezer agrees: a lacking remainder CAN become piggul. The lack simply means less is there — it does not disqualify the remainder intrinsically.
Rava
Proof from Rabbi Ḥiyya's mishna: he omits 'or an olive-bulk' for the remainder — implying the remainder already stood at exactly one olive-bulk (i.e., was lacking).
Abaye
The mishna follows Rabbi Elazar, who holds one is liable only for burning the full handful — so 'olive-bulk of the handful' is simply inapplicable. Nothing about a lacking remainder!
Rava's Final Ruling Rava concedes the proof from Rabbi Ḥiyya fails but maintains his ruling based on a baraita about shewbread: a lacking remainder can become piggul and is removed from the prohibition of misuse of consecrated property.

📉 Piggul and the Lacking Remainder

Handful removed → some remainder is lost → measure falls below a full olive-bulk
↓
Does burning the handful with piggul intent affect this lacking remainder?
↓
Rav Huna
No piggul. The remainder is intrinsically deficient. Its disqualification comes from within — unlike meat that left the Temple (external factor).
Rava
Piggul applies. The lack merely reduces quantity, not status. The handful's burning is still the permitting act for whatever remainder exists.
↓
Same dispute applies: does burning the handful remove the lacking remainder from מְעִילָה (misuse of consecrated property)?

📜 Abaye vs. Rava: Interpreting Rabbi Ḥiyya's Mishna

Rava
Rabbi Ḥiyya omits 'or an olive-bulk of the remainder' — why? Because in his case the remainder already measured only one olive-bulk! This proves lacking remainders can become piggul.
Abaye
Rabbi Ḥiyya's mishna follows Rabbi Elazar, who holds liability requires burning the complete handful — not just an olive-bulk. So that phrase is simply inapplicable.
Rava
If Rabbi Elazar is the reason, why doesn't the mishna read 'to burn its handful and its frankincense outside the Temple'?
Abaye
The mishna addresses the sinner's meal offering (מִנְחַת חוֹטֵא) — which has no frankincense! Only the handful permits it, so frankincense is irrelevant.
Gemara's Question Did the tanna go to all that trouble (ve'ikhpal) just to teach a halakha applicable only to the sinner's meal offering? — Yes, he did. And when Rav Dimi came from Eretz Yisrael he similarly derived narrow rulings from single cases.

⚖️ Half Olive-Bulk: Eating vs. Burning

The Mishna Rules

  • Intent to eat ½ olive-bulk of remainder outside area/time
  • Plus intent to burn ½ olive-bulk outside area/time
  • → Offering is fit (כָּשֵׁר)
  • Reason: eating and burning do not join together

Rabbi Eliezer's View

  • Human eating and altar burning can combine
  • ½ olive-bulk of each = full olive-bulk of improper intent
  • → Offering becomes piggul
  • Cross-category intents are treated as unified

Abaye's Resolution

  • Mishna can follow even the Rabbis
  • Don't infer: half-eating + half-eating would combine
  • The mishna teaches only the eating vs. burning case
  • Rabbis: same-category halves do combine (olive-bulk met)
