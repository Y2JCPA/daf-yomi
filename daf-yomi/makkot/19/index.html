From First Fruits to Second Tithe: A Chain of Derivations

🌾 Bikkurim (First Fruits): When does liability begin for a non-priest who eats them?
↓
R' Eliezer: Fruits inside the Temple = consecrated; outside = non-sacred
↓
⚖️ Rav Sheshet: Placement alongside altar invalidates; recitation does not
↓
🏛️ R' Yishmael derives: Second tithe cannot be eaten in Jerusalem without the Temple
↓
🔗 Proof: Torah juxtaposes second tithe with firstborn (Deut. 14:23)
Core Issue The Gemara traces how the ritual requirements of bikkurim illuminate the status of second tithe — linking placement, recitation, and Temple-dependence through a chain of halakhic reasoning.

First Fruits: Inside vs. Outside the Temple

Outside the Temple

  • Status: Non-sacred (chullin)
  • Non-priest who eats: Not liable
  • Per R' Eliezer's baraita
  • Even if some fruits are inside

Inside the Temple

  • Status: Consecrated (hekdesh)
  • Non-priest who eats: Death at hand of Heaven
  • Per R' Eliezer's baraita
  • Liability begins upon entry
Key Principle The physical location of bikkurim — inside or outside the Temple — determines their halakhic status entirely.

Does Recitation Invalidate First Fruits?

Rav Sheshet
Lack of placement (hanacha) alongside the altar invalidates bikkurim.
Rav Sheshet
But lack of recitation (mikra) of the Torah verses does not invalidate them.
Rav Ashi
Even if recitation doesn't invalidate, surely there's still a mitzva to recite? Why didn't the tanna mention it?
Rav Ashi
Because a convert brings bikkurim but cannot recite 'the land the Lord swore to our fathers' — so recitation isn't universal.
Resolution Recitation was omitted from the baraita's refutation not because it lacks importance, but because the case of the ger (convert) shows it isn't universally required.

R' Yishmael: Can Second Tithe Be Eaten Without the Temple?

❓ Must one bring ma'aser sheni to Jerusalem and eat it after the Destruction?
↓
Attempted proof: Firstborn requires Jerusalem → only with Temple. Second tithe also requires Jerusalem → same rule?
↓
Refutation
Firstborn is different — it requires blood & portions on the altar
Counter-proof
Bikkurim prove the point — no blood, yet Temple-dependent
↓
Refuted again: Bikkurim require placement alongside the altar — second tithe does not
↓
✅ Final proof: Hekkesh — Deut. 14:23 juxtaposes tithe with firstborn → Temple required
Conclusion R' Yishmael proves through scriptural juxtaposition (hekkesh) that second-tithe produce may not be eaten in Jerusalem when the Temple is not standing.

Three Offerings Compared: Temple Requirements

FeatureFirstborn (Bekhor)First Fruits (Bikkurim)Second Tithe (Ma'aser Sheni)
Brought to Jerusalem✅ Yes✅ Yes✅ Yes
Blood on altar✅ Required❌ Not required❌ Not required
Placement at altar❌ N/A✅ Required (invalidates)❌ Not required
Recitation of verses❌ No✅ Mitzva (doesn't invalidate)❌ No
Requires Temple standing✅ Yes✅ Yes✅ Yes (via hekkesh)
Aspect involving altar✅ Yes✅ Yes❌ No
Why Hekkesh Is Needed A common-factor derivation from firstborn and bikkurim fails because both have 'an aspect involving the altar' — which second tithe lacks. Therefore the Torah's juxtaposition is necessary.

Why Does the Mishna Repeat the Law of Second Tithe?

Gemara
The mishna on 13a already taught lashes for eating unredeemed second tithe. Why repeat it here?
R' Yosei bar Ḥanina
They are different cases!
R' Yosei bar Ḥanina
Earlier mishna (13a): Impure person eats impure second tithe inside Jerusalem.
R' Yosei bar Ḥanina
This mishna: Pure person eats pure second tithe outside the wall of Jerusalem.
R' Shimon
Source for impurity prohibition: 'I have not put any of it away when impure' (Deut. 26:14) — whether I am impure or the produce is impure.
Two Prohibitions Eating second tithe incurs lashes in two distinct scenarios: (1) impure consumption inside Jerusalem, and (2) pure consumption outside the walls.
