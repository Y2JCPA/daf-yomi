Makkot 21 — Multiple Lashes, Multiple Prohibitions

חורש תלם אחד — חייב שמונה מלקויות
OVERVIEW This daf explores how a single act can generate multiple sets of lashes: from bodily mourning rites (incision, laceration) to shaving, tattooing, and the famous case of one furrow violating eight prohibitions. The Gemara carefully defines the instruments and actions that trigger liability.
3 / 9

Incision over the Dead: Hand or Utensil?

Shmuel
One who cuts his flesh with a utensil (mesaret) in mourning over the dead is liable.
Baraita
An incision (serita) is by hand (fingernails); a laceration is with a utensil. They are one transgression — both over the dead.
Gemara
Shmuel follows R' Yosei, who holds incision and laceration are synonymous in every sense — no distinction between hand and utensil.
R' Yochanan
The baraita on idolatry must be emended: by hand = exempt; with a utensil = liable — as the priests of Baal used swords and lances (I Kings 18:28).
KEY POINT R' Yosei derives from "for the soul" (Lev. 19:28) that one is liable for each soul over whom he cut — generating multiple lashes.

Shaving the Beard: How the Razor Requirement Is Derived

"The edge of their beard they may not shave"
(Lev. 21:5)
↓
Does this include scissors?
Second verse limits
"You may not mar" (Lev. 19:27) — only shaving that destroys from the root. Scissors don't destroy from root → exempt.
What about tweezers/plane?
They destroy from root, but the word "shave" excludes them — tweezers ≠ shaving → exempt.
↓
Only a razor both shaves and mars → liable
R' ELIEZER DISAGREES He derives via gezeira shava but holds tweezers and plane also constitute shaving — the dispute is definitional, not about the derivation method.

Tattoo: Both Elements Required

Dye Only (No Carving)

  • Applied ink/kohl to skin surface
  • Did NOT carve recesses in skin
  • ❌ NOT LIABLE

Carving Only (No Dye)

  • Carved recesses in skin
  • Did NOT insert dye
  • ❌ NOT LIABLE

Dye + Carving

  • Carved recesses AND inserted dye
  • Ink, kohl (keḥol), or any marking substance
  • ✅ LIABLE for lashes
R' SHIMON BEN YEHUDA In the name of R' Shimon: liable only if he writes a name — as bar Kappara teaches, specifically the name of idolatry (derived from "I am the Lord" in Lev. 19:28).

Covering Seeds of Kilayim: Source for the Law

R' Yannai
The Sages counted opinions and concluded: one who covers seeds of diverse kinds with dirt is flogged for sowing kilayim.
R' Yochanan
This is already in our mishna! The plower is liable for kilayim — he must be covering seeds with dirt as he plows. This is the proof!
R' Yannai
True — but had I not lifted the earthenware shard for you, would you have found the gem beneath it?
Reish Lakish
If a great man hadn't praised this, I'd say: the mishna may follow R' Akiva, who holds even maintaining kilayim (without sowing) is punishable!
R' AKIVA'S SOURCE "Kilayim your field you shall not sow" (Lev. 19:19) — read as: do not allow kilayim to remain in your field. Even passive maintenance violates the prohibition.
