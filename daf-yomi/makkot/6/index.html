⚖️ Rabbi Yosei vs. Rabbis: Does a Disqualified Witness Void All Testimony?

Rabbis
If one of three witnesses is disqualified, the entire testimony is voided — even in monetary cases. The group is one unit.
Rabbi Yosei
This stringency applies only in capital cases, which are judged more strictly. In monetary cases, the remaining valid witnesses can still testify.
Rav Yehuda (Shmuel)
The halakha follows Rabbi Yosei — disqualification of one only voids testimony in capital cases.
Rav Naḥman
The halakha follows the Rabbis — a disqualified witness voids the entire testimony even in monetary cases.
The Debate Does 'כולן נדחין' (all are rejected) apply universally, or did the Torah's stringency for capital law create an exception to the default rule?

⚖️ Capital Law vs. Monetary Law: Witness Standards

Capital Law (דיני נפשות)

  • Disqualified witness voids ALL testimony (Rabbis & R. Yosei agree)
  • Witnesses must actively forewarn the transgressor beforehand
  • Disjointed testimony from separate windows is invalid
  • Two witnesses must be connected — see each other or a shared forewarner
  • Sanhedrin cannot hear testimony through an interpreter

Monetary Law (דיני ממונות)

  • Per Rabbi Yosei: valid witnesses in the group can still testify
  • Forewarning requirement is less strict
  • Disjointed testimony may be valid (Rav Naḥman from Deut. 17:6)
  • More lenient thresholds for combining witness accounts
  • Torah's special capital-law stringency does not apply

🔗 How Must 100 Witnesses Testify? — Rava and Ravina

Rava
100 witnesses equal 2 witnesses — but only when they all testify simultaneously (בבת אחת). If they are disqualified as conspiring witnesses (עדים זוממין), all 100 are punished.
Ravina
'Simultaneously' does not mean in literal unison. Each subsequent witness must begin within תוך כדי דיבור — the time to say a short greeting — after the previous finishes.
Rava
This creates an uninterrupted sequential chain. All testimonies are linked and treated as one unified act of testimony, not separate statements.
Practical Result The 100-as-2 rule applies to eidim zomemim (conspiring witnesses), lashes, and other halakhot — the group is legally indivisible when testifying in unbroken sequence.

📜 The Ḥaver and Forewarning — Rabbi Yosei bar Yehuda

חבר אינו צריך התראה, שלא ניתנה התראה אלא להבחין בין שוגג למזיד
Key Ruling Rabbi Yosei bar Yehuda rules: A ḥaver (Torah scholar scrupulous in observance) does not require forewarning (התראה) before execution. Forewarning exists only to distinguish שוגג (accidental) from מזיד (intentional). A ḥaver fully knows the law — his violation is presumed intentional without any warning. The mishna's attribution to 'Rabbi Yosei' is actually Rabbi Yosei bar Yehuda, clarified by Abaye from a baraita.
