Makkot 23 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is the flogging strap made specifically from ox and donkey hide?
A. Because ox and donkey are the strongest animal hides available
B. Because these animals recognize their master, so they punish one who doesn't recognize God
C. Because the Torah explicitly commands using ox and donkey leather
D. Because these hides produce the least pain while still fulfilling the mitzvah
Correct: B — A Galilean taught before Rav Ḥisda: Isaiah 1:3 says the ox knows its owner and the donkey its master's trough — let animals that recognize their Master punish one who does not.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rava solve the problem of needing the strap to reach each person's abdomen?
A. The court keeps straps of different lengths
B. The attendant holds the strap at a different position on the handle
C. The strap has adjustable loops that can be tied or loosened
D. The person's position is adjusted instead of the strap
Correct: C — Rava rejected Abaye's suggestion that each person gets a custom strap (too many straps in court!) and said the strap has loops that are tied to shorten or loosened to lengthen.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Ḥananya ben Gamliel, what is the spiritual consequence of receiving lashes?
A. The sinner must still bring a korban to achieve atonement
B. Lashes exempt the sinner from karet (divine excision)
C. Lashes only reduce the severity of karet but don't eliminate it
D. Lashes have no effect on heavenly punishment
Correct: B — R' Ḥananya ben Gamliel holds that once a sinner receives court-administered lashes, he is exempted from the punishment of karet. Rav rules the halakha follows this opinion.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which of the following is NOT one of the three matters the earthly court implemented that Heaven confirmed?
A. Reading the Megillah on Purim
B. Greeting another person with God's Name
C. Observing a second day of Yom Tov in the diaspora
D. Bringing the first tithe to the Temple treasury
Correct: C — R' Yehoshua ben Levi lists three: reading the Megillah, greeting with God's Name (as Boaz did), and bringing first tithe to the Temple. A second day of Yom Tov is not among them.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Rava support R' Yehuda's view that even a strong but unlearned person may serve as the flogging attendant?
A. Because physical strength is needed to deliver proper lashes with one hand
B. Because the Torah's warning 'he shall not exceed' implies the attendant might not know the law independently
C. Because learned people would refuse to serve in such a role
D. Because R' Yehuda was Rava's teacher and he always deferred to him
Correct: B — Rava reasons: if only exceedingly knowledgeable people were appointed, the Torah's warning not to add lashes would be unnecessary — a scholar would know this already. The warning implies even the unlearned may serve.
