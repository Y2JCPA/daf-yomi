Rav Sheshet's Four Derivations from Juxtaposed Verses

Rav Sheshet / R' Elazar ben Azarya
Strap from ox hide: 'Forty he shall flog him' is next to 'You shall not muzzle an ox' — the strap comes from an ox.
Rav Sheshet / R' Elazar ben Azarya
Yevama and boils: 'Do not muzzle an ox' is next to 'When brothers dwell together' — she is not forced into levirate marriage with a boil-afflicted yavam.
Rav Sheshet / R' Elazar ben Azarya
Festivals and idolatry: 'Molten gods you shall not make' is next to 'The festival of Passover you shall observe' — contempt for festivals is like idol worship.
Rav Sheshet / R' Elazar ben Azarya
Lashon hara and dogs: 'To the dog you shall cast it' is next to 'You shall not accept a false report' — speakers and acceptors of slander deserve to be cast to dogs.
Hermeneutic Principle Semuchin — laws placed next to each other in the Torah teach about one another. Rav Sheshet uses this principle four times in rapid succession.

Abaye vs. Rava: Fitting the Strap to the Person

Abaye

  • The strap must reach the top of his abdomen
  • Therefore we craft a separate strap for each person's size
  • Individual straps tailored to each body

Rava

  • That would require numerous straps in court!
  • Instead: the strap has adjustable loops
  • Tie loops to shorten, loosen to lengthen
  • One strap fits all — practical solution
Halakhic Pragmatism Rava's approach prevails: the court needs a practical system, not a strap warehouse. Adjustable loops solve the requirement elegantly.

Who Serves as the Flogging Attendant?

Sages (Tanna Kamma)
Appoint attendants who are lacking in strength but exceedingly knowledgeable in Torah — knowledge prevents excess.
R' Yehuda
Even those lacking in knowledge but exceedingly strong may be appointed.
Rava
R' Yehuda is correct — the Torah warns 'he shall not exceed,' which implies the attendant may not be knowledgeable. Why warn the wise?
Sages (response)
One implores only one who is already implored — even the wise need reminders.
Rava's Logic If only scholars served as attendants, the Torah's warning not to add lashes would be superfluous. The warning implies even non-scholars may serve.
