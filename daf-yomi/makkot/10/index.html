Location: Intermediate-sized towns — not too small (cannot support the exile) nor too large (enemies may lurk within)
↓
Necessities: Water, markets, and basic commerce so the exiled murderer can truly sustain life
↓
Prohibited commerce: No selling of weapons or hunting tools — lest the blood redeemer acquire means to kill the exile
↓
Signage: "Refuge" (מקלט) posted at every crossroads so the fleeing murderer can find his way without confusion
↓
Escort: Two Torah scholars accompany the murderer to dissuade the blood redeemer from acting
↓
Torah companions: A student's teacher is exiled with him; a teacher's entire school is exiled with him — so Torah life continues

📚 Is a Torah Teacher Exiled — And Does Torah Protect Him?

Rabbi Yochanan (Ruling 1)
If a teacher of Torah is exiled to a city of refuge, his entire school is exiled with him — so his students do not lose their Torah education.
Gemara Challenge
But Rabbi Yochanan himself says: 'The words of Torah provide refuge!' — so why would a Torah scholar need to flee to a city of refuge at all?
Resolution
No contradiction: Torah protects from the blood redeemer only while one is actively engaged in study. A moment of distraction removes that protection — so the scholar still needs the city.
Alternative Resolution
Or: Torah's 'refuge' means protection from the Angel of Death — it extends one's life. But it does not exempt a scholar from the legal penalty of exile for manslaughter.
Key Verse "And he shall flee to one of these cities and live" (Deut. 4:42) — arrange conditions in the city so that the exile can truly live there

🏘️ What Makes a Valid City of Refuge?

✅ Required

  • Intermediate population size
  • Access to water (river or spring)
  • Active marketplace
  • Torah scholars and teachers
  • Elders present in the city
  • Ability to support exiled residents

❌ Disqualifying (Rabbi Elazar)

  • Majority of residents are unintentional murderers
  • No elders in the city
  • Weapons or hunting tool vendors
  • Too small to sustain exile population
  • No framework for legal proceedings

☀️ Moses and the Three Transjordan Cities

אָז יַבְדִּיל מֹשֶׁה שָׁלֹשׁ עָרִים בְּעֵבֶר הַיַּרְדֵּן מִזְרְחָה שָׁמֶשׁ — Deut. 4:41
Rabbi Simlai — Why Moses Established Cities Before They Could Function Moses established three cities of refuge in Transjordan even though they could not yet function — the three Canaanite cities had not yet been designated. Why did he act prematurely? Because each mitzvah available to him, Moses performed immediately. He could not complete the system, but he refused to delay even one piece of it. Rabbi Simlai: The verse says Moses separated cities "mizreḥa shamesh" — to the sunrise. God said: "Shine the sun [hazraḥ hashemesh] for them" — by his eagerness, Moses brought light and dawn to the Transjordanian exiles who would one day be saved. Rav Ashi adds: Anyone who loves to study Torah in abundance — with many colleagues — will succeed. Ravina adds: Anyone who loves to teach Torah before the multitudes will gain increase, for teaching clarifies and deepens understanding.
