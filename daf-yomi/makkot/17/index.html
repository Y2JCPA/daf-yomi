Why Don't Amei Ha'aretz Separate Ma'aser Ani?

Rav Yosef
R' Eliezer and the Rabbis disagree whether poor man's tithe renders produce tevel at all
Abaye
If that were the dispute, they should argue about a case of certainty, not uncertainty!
Abaye
Rather, all agree unseparated ma'aser ani makes produce tevel. The dispute is whether amei ha'aretz are suspected of not separating it
R' Eliezer
It's only money — no sanctity involved. They do separate it, just don't give it to the poor
Rabbis
Since it involves exertion, they don't even bother to separate it
Abaye's Logic If the dispute were about the fundamental status of ma'aser ani, we'd expect them to debate a case of certainty, not the uncertain case of demai.

Minimum Amount of Tevel for Lashes

R' Shimon — Any Amount

  • Even one kernel of wheat triggers lashes
  • Argues: a kernel is 'in the form of its creation' like an ant
  • According to R' Yirmeya / Reish Lakish: even any amount of flour
  • Baraita supports: any amount forbidden; olive-bulk only for sin-offering

Rabbis — Olive-Bulk (Kezayit)

  • Liable only if one eats a kezayit
  • An ant is different — it's a living creature with a soul
  • A kernel of wheat is not inherently significant
  • Both Rav Beivai and R' Yirmeya cite Reish Lakish, but disagree on flour
The Ant Argument R' Shimon: 'You concede that eating an ant of any size incurs lashes — a kernel of wheat is also in its created form!' Rabbis: 'An entity with a soul is significant; a kernel is not.'

Principle Lav she-nitak la'aseh — a prohibition that is 'attached' to a positive command does not carry lashes, because the Torah provides a corrective action.

Shiluach HaKen: Lav She-Nitak La'Aseh

R' Yehuda
One who takes the mother bird with fledglings is flogged and does not send the mother
Rabbis
He sends the mother and is not flogged — the positive command cancels the lashes
Rabbis
This is the principle: any prohibition that entails a command to arise and perform a mitzvah — he is not liable to lashes
לאו שניתק לעשה R' Yehuda treats the taking as an irreversible violation. The Rabbis hold that since the Torah commands 'send the mother,' the positive mitzvah repairs the prohibition, removing liability for lashes.
