↓

HP died after verdict
Murderer returns home upon that HP's death

🗣️ Why Did Joshua Use Harsh Language?

Rabbi Yehuda
Joshua used harsh language because the tribes delayed in establishing the cities of refuge — failing to act with urgency in fulfilling the mitzvah
Rabbis
Harsh language was used because the cities still lacked Torah scholars to guide and teach the exiles who fled there
Gemara
Proof that dibbur always implies harsh speech: 'The man spoke harshly to us' (Genesis 42:30) — said of Joseph's treatment of his brothers in Egypt
Verse "Speak to the children of Israel… the cities of refuge of which I spoke to you by means of Moses" (Joshua 20:1–2) — the repetition of dibbur signals rebuke and urgency

📜 Who Wrote the Final Eight Verses of the Torah?

Rabbi Yehuda

  • The last 8 verses describe Moses's own death
  • Moses could not have written about his own death
  • Joshua wrote those 8 verses after Moses died
  • Proof: 'Joshua wrote these matters in the scroll of the Torah of God' (Joshua 24:26)

Rabbi Neḥemya

  • Moses wrote the entire Torah — including the account of his own death
  • He wrote those verses bedim'ah — with tears
  • The Torah is entirely complete as given through Moses
  • 'Joshua wrote' refers to the cities of refuge appended to the existing scroll

👩 Mothers of High Priests

אִמּוֹתֵיהֶן שֶׁל כֹּהֲנִים גְּדוֹלִים הָיוּ מְסַפְּקוֹת לָהֶן מָזוֹן וּכְסוּת
Mishna — A Touching Detail The mothers of High Priests would provide food and clothing to those exiled in the cities of refuge — so that the exiles would not pray for the death of their sons. The more comfortable the exile's life, the less he would wish for the HP's death to secure his own return home. As they say in Eretz Yisrael: Shechem married a woman and Mavgai circumcised himself — one person's act can cause hardship for another.

⚰️ Judah's Bones: The Power of Conditional Ostracism

Judah declared upon himself: 'I will be a sinner to my father forever' (Genesis 44:32) — a conditional self-ostracism if he failed to return Benjamin safely
↓
For 40 years in the wilderness, Judah's bones rattled in his coffin — the self-declared ostracism remained in effect even after his death
↓
Moses prayed: שְׁמַע ה' קוֹל יְהוּדָה — 'Hear O Lord the voice of Judah' — entreating for Judah's bones to be reunited and his limbs to stop rattling
↓
His limbs entered their place (leshafa) — but the Heavenly academy still barred him from entry
↓
Moses continued: 'And bring him to his people' — Judah finally entered the heavenly academy. Lesson: conditional ostracism requires formal nullification even when the condition was already fulfilled

🔮 The Curse of a Sage Always Comes True

Rav teaches: A Sage's curse takes effect even if it is baseless — based on a mistaken premise
↓
Ahithophel's reasoning: If God's Name is erased to make peace between husband and wife (sota), how much more so should one act to preserve peace among people — he inferred David's curse was unjust
↓
Baseless curse (Rav)
David's curse on Ahithophel came true despite being founded on a mistaken inference
Conditional curse (Rabbi Abbahu)
Eli's curse on his priestly line was stated conditionally — yet still came to fruition
↓
Even so, Ahithophel despaired and hanged himself — the Sage's earlier curse on him had already sealed his fate
