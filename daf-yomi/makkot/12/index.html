🩸 Blood Redeemer Who Kills Outside the Boundary

Rabbi Yosei HaGelili
If the blood redeemer kills the unintentional murderer outside the Shabbat boundary, he is executed. The verse says 'and the blood redeemer murders the murderer' — the word 'murders' signals this is unlawful killing when outside the zone.
Rabbi Akiva
The blood redeemer who kills outside the boundary is exempt. The verse continues: 'he has no blood' — no blood-guilt applies to the blood redeemer even when the murderer has left the city.
Rav (via Mar Zutra bar Toviyya)
A blood redeemer who kills outside the boundary is exempt from execution — and Rav rules this in accordance with Rabbi Eliezer's baraita: the murderer is only fully protected once he stands before the congregation for judgment.
Abaye's Conclusion It stands to reason that the halakha follows the view that the blood redeemer is liable for killing an unintentional murderer who emerged unwittingly from the city, since the Torah speaks in the language of people — distinguishing deliberate exit from accidental.

👨‍👦 Can a Son Be a Blood Redeemer for His Father?

Baraita 1 — Son May Act

  • A father who killed his son
  • The surviving son becomes blood redeemer
  • May kill his father as blood redeemer
  • Mitzva / permission applies normally

Baraita 2 — Son May Not Act

  • A father who killed his son
  • The surviving son does not become blood redeemer
  • Cannot kill his own father
  • Honor of father (כיבוד אב) takes precedence

Gemara's Resolution

  • Baraita 1 = the grandson (son of the murdered victim)
  • Baraita 2 = the son of the killer himself
  • A son cannot avenge against his own father
  • A grandson may avenge his parent — no contradiction

🏙️ Murderer Who Kills Again While in Exile

Unintentional murderer exiled to a city of refuge
↓
He kills again unintentionally within that city
↓
He is exiled from his neighborhood to another neighborhood within the same city
↓
A Levite who is a permanent resident of a Levite city and kills unintentionally:
↓
Exiled from his city to another Levite city in a different district (מחוז)
↓
If exiled to a city in his own district — he is still admitted, but the exile is valid
↓
The city of refuge functions for Levites just as for any Israelite — the Torah provided all six cities as refuge
