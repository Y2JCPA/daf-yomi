Makkot 5 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, why can't lashes be divided among conspiring witnesses?
A. A gezera shava from the word 'wicked' forbids any division
B. Lashes are inherently individual and cannot be shared
C. The verse 'as he conspired to do to his brother' requires the punishment to exactly mirror the intended harm
D. The court cannot calculate fractional lashes
Correct: C — Rava's reasoning is that 'as he conspired to do to his brother' demands the punishment mirror exactly what they intended to inflict. Partial lashes would fail to fulfill the verse.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What must the second set of witnesses say to render the first set conspiring witnesses?
A. That the accused is actually innocent of the alleged crime
B. That the first set's testimony contradicts established facts
C. That the first set was physically with them elsewhere at the time they claimed to witness the event
D. That the first set has a history of giving false testimony in court
Correct: C — Conspiring witnesses (זוממין) are created only when the second set impeaches the witnesses themselves — 'you were with us in another place at that time' — not by merely contradicting the content of their testimony.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the Sadducees' position on when conspiring witnesses are executed?
A. Immediately upon being found to have given false testimony
B. After the verdict is concluded but before the accused is harmed
C. Only after the accused has already been executed based on their false testimony
D. Never — false witnesses only pay monetary compensation
Correct: C — The Sadducees read 'a life for a life' literally: conspirators are killed only if the accused was already executed. The Rabbis refute this from 'as he conspired to do to his brother' — implying the brother is still alive.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What fatal error did Rabbi Yehuda ben Tabbai make, according to Shimon ben Shataḥ?
A. He executed conspiring witnesses before the verdict against the accused was concluded
B. He executed one conspiring witness when only one of the pair was found guilty
C. He divided lashes improperly among two conspiring witnesses
D. He relied on a kal v'chomer inference to impose capital punishment
Correct: B — The rule is that BOTH witnesses in a set must be proven conspiring before either can be executed. Rabbi Yehuda ben Tabbai killed one witness when his partner was not simultaneously found guilty — a tragic violation of this rule.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rabbi Akiva teach about why the Torah mentions a third witness in Deuteronomy 17:6?
A. To be lenient — a third witness is not executed along with the other two
B. To require three witnesses for capital cases instead of two
C. To be stringent — a third conspiring witness is executed just like the other two
D. To indicate that three witnesses create a stronger presumption of truth
Correct: C — Rabbi Akiva says the third witness is mentioned specifically to be stringent: if he joins conspirators and is found guilty along with them, he is executed just as they are. Joining transgressors brings the same punishment.
