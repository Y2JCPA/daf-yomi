Makkot 13 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, what is the actual subject of dispute between Rabbi Yehuda and Rabbi Meir regarding rent in cities of refuge?
A. Whether murderers pay rent in the 6 designated cities of refuge
B. Whether murderers must leave their quarters at the Jubilee year
C. Whether murderers pay rent in the 42 Levite cities (not the 6 refuge cities)
D. Whether the Levites may charge market-rate or only nominal rent
Correct: C — Rava corrects Rav Kahana: since 'for you' means for all needs, the 6 cities of refuge are free of charge. The dispute is only about the 42 Levite cities — Rabbi Yehuda says pay rent, Rabbi Meir says free.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Akiva rule that those liable to karet DO receive lashes, even though those liable to death do NOT?
A. Karet is a more severe punishment than death, so lashes are added as extra measure
B. Karet can be removed through repentance, so lashes ensure punishment if the person repents
C. The Torah explicitly exempts those liable to death but has no such exemption for karet
D. Rabbi Akiva holds all prohibitions in the Torah carry lashes regardless of other punishments
Correct: B — Rabbi Akiva reasons: if the karet-liable person repents, Heaven forgives the karet, leaving them with no punishment at all. Therefore lashes must be imposed by the court. Death cannot be similarly removed, so lashes are absorbed by death.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What verse does Rabbi Yishmael cite as the source that those liable to the death penalty are also flogged?
A. "According to his wickedness" (Deuteronomy 25:2)
B. "The murderer shall return to his ancestral land" (Numbers 35:28)
C. "If you will not observe to perform all the matters of this Torah" (Deuteronomy 28:58)
D. "And they shall be excised before the eyes of the children of their people" (Leviticus 20:17)
Correct: C — Rabbi Yishmael derives from 'all the matters of this Torah' that every prohibition — including those carrying the death penalty — is also subject to lashes. Rabbi Akiva uses 'according to his wickedness' to limit punishment to one per transgression.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rava rules that if witnesses forewarned the transgressor specifically about the death penalty, what is the result?
A. He receives both death and lashes — Rabbi Yishmael and Rabbi Akiva still dispute this case
B. He receives only death, not lashes — all authorities agree
C. He receives only lashes, since actual execution requires a full Sanhedrin
D. He is exempt from both, as the forewarning demonstrates premeditation
Correct: B — Rava rules: once the transgressor was specifically forewarned about death, everyone agrees he receives only death and not lashes. The dispute between Rabbi Yishmael and Rabbi Akiva concerns only cases without specific forewarning about death.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rabbi Abbahu's gezeirah shavah links karet to lashes. Which terms does he compare?
A. 'Malkot' (lashes) in Deuteronomy 25 and 'karet' (excision) in Leviticus 20
B. 'Le'einekha' (before your eyes) in the lashes verse and 'le'einei' (before the eyes of) in the karet verse
C. 'According to his wickedness' and 'the souls who do them shall be excised'
D. 'He shall return' in the exile verse and 'before your eyes' in the lashes verse
Correct: B — Rabbi Abbahu draws a gezeirah shavah between 'le'einekha' in Deuteronomy 25:3 (lashes passage) and 'le'einei' in Leviticus 20:17 (karet passage). The shared lamed prefix and similar form link the two subjects, explicitly including karet-liable persons among those who are flogged.
