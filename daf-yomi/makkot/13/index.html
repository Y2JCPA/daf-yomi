⚖️ Does Death or Karet Exempt From Lashes?

Rabbi Yishmael
Those liable to execution ARE also flogged — 'all the matters of this Torah' covers all prohibitions, and human court lashes vs. heavenly death are not two punishments for one transgression.
Rabbi Akiva
Those liable to death are NOT flogged — 'according to his wickedness' means one punishment per transgression. But those liable to karet ARE flogged, since repentance removes karet, leaving them without any punishment unless lashes are given.
Rabbi Yitzchak
Like those liable to death, those liable to karet are also NOT flogged — karet violations were singled out from the general prohibition, and being singled out exempts them from lashes entirely.
Rava's Bottom Line If witnesses forewarned the transgressor specifically about the death penalty, all agree he is NOT flogged. The dispute between Rabbi Yishmael and Rabbi Akiva applies only when forewarning was general.

🏠 Rent in Cities of Refuge: Rav Kahana vs. Rava

Rav Kahana
The dispute between Rabbi Yehuda (pay rent) and Rabbi Meir (no rent) concerns the 6 designated cities of refuge in the Torah and Joshua.
Rava
The term 'for you' certainly means for all your needs — so the 6 cities of refuge are free of charge. The dispute is only about the 42 Levite cities: Rabbi Yehuda says pay rent; Rabbi Meir says no rent.
Exile Returns to Former Role A baraita derives from 'to the estate of his fathers he shall return' — just as a Hebrew slave freed at Jubilee returns to his former public office, so too an exile returning from a city of refuge resumes his previous position.

📋 New Mishna — אֵלּוּ הֵן הַלּוֹקִין

אֵלּוּ הֵן הַלּוֹקִין — These are those who are flogged (Mishna, Makkot 3:1)
These Are Those Who Are Flogged High Priest who marries a widow or divorcée — two sets of lashes if she was both (violates two separate prohibitions)

Ritual impurity violations: eating sacrificial food while impure; entering the Temple while impure

Prohibited foods: forbidden fat (חֵלֶב), blood, notar (leftover sacrifice), pigul (invalid intent), sacrificial food outside proper status

Seasonal prohibitions: chametz on Pesach; eating on Yom Kippur; laboring on Yom Kippur

Tithes: untithed produce; first-tithe with teruma not separated; unredeemed second-tithe or sacred food

Rabbi Shimon: any amount of untithed produce incurs liability; Sages: an olive-bulk

⚖️ Three Opinions: Karet, Death, and Lashes

Rabbi Yishmael

  • Death penalty → lashes ALSO administered
  • Karet → lashes ALSO administered
  • Source: 'All the matters of this Torah' — all prohibitions trigger lashes
  • Death (heavenly/court) and lashes (court) are distinct — not double punishment
  • Exception: if specifically forewarned about death → no lashes

Rabbi Akiva

  • Death penalty → lashes NOT administered
  • Karet → lashes ARE administered
  • Source: 'According to his wickedness' = one punishment per transgression
  • Karet exception: repentance removes karet, so lashes ensure some punishment remains
  • Basis: prohibition for karet exists as mandate for lashes, not just karet

Rabbi Yitzchak

  • Death penalty → lashes NOT administered
  • Karet → lashes NOT administered
  • Source: karet cases were singled out from the general prohibition
  • Being singled out for karet exempts entirely from lashes
  • Same logic applies to death: death absorbs lashes completely

🔤 Gezeirah Shavah: Le'einei — Verbal Analogy for Karet + Lashes

Rabbi Abbahu: Torah explicitly links karet to lashes via verbal analogy (gezeirah shavah)
↓
Lashes verse (Deut. 25:3): 'He shall be flogged le'einekha' — before your eyes (lamed prefix)
↓
Karet verse (Lev. 20:17): 'They shall be excised le'einei' — before the eyes of their people (lamed prefix)
↓
✓ Valid Analogy
le'einei ~ le'einekha: same lamed prefix — karet is explicitly linked to lashes; this gezeirah shavah stands
✗ Rejected Analogy
me'einei ≠ le'einekha: mem vs. lamed prefix — the Gemara rejects deriving from dissimilar forms; however, school of Rabbi Yishmael permits analogies from variant prefixes (leprosy precedent)
↓
Rav Shmuel bar Rav Yitzchak (from Rabbi Abbahu): The real reason karet + lashes coexist — 'according to his wickedness' limits the verse to one whose prohibition is mandating lashes; karet prohibitions independently mandate lashes

⚠️ Rava's Rule: Forewarning Determines the Punishment

Transgressor about to commit a violation carrying both the death penalty and lashes
↓
Witnesses issue forewarning (התראה) — the nature of this warning is critical
↓
Forewarned specifically about death: ALL authorities agree — receives death only, NOT lashes. Even Rabbi Yishmael concedes once death is triggered, lashes are set aside.
↓
Forewarned about lashes only (or vaguely): Rabbi Yishmael — receives lashes; Rabbi Akiva — receives death (not lashes). This is their core dispute.
↓
Principle behind Rabbi Akiva: 'According to his wickedness' (Deut. 25:2) — the court may impose only one punishment per transgression; the more severe penalty (death) absorbs the lesser (lashes)
