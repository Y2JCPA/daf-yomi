Makkot 22: The Count of Lashes

כמה מלקין אותו — ארבעים חסר אחת
DAF OVERVIEW This daf refines the list of simultaneous prohibitions from the previous discussion, then transitions to the core laws of malkot (lashes): why the Torah prescribes 39 lashes rather than 40, how the court assesses the offender's capacity, and when one assessment can cover multiple transgressions. Rava's famous declaration about honoring the Sages appears here.

Refining the List of Festival Prohibitions

Rava
Remove kindling fire from the list — he's already liable for cooking the gid ha-nasheh (sciatic nerve) on the Festival.
Gemara
But R' Ḥiyya's baraita says: two sets for eating, three for cooking. If we add eating an unslaughtered carcass, that makes three for eating — contradicting the baraita!
Rava (revised)
Remove kindling and instead insert the prohibition of using wood of an ashera (idolatrous tree): 'Nothing of the dedicated item shall cleave to your hand' (Deut. 13:18).
Rav Aḥa b. Rava
If it's ashera wood, he should also violate 'You shall not bring an abomination into your house' (Deut. 7:26)!
Resolution
We're dealing with wood consecrated for the Temple (hekdesh), whose prohibition derives from 'You shall not do so to the Lord your God' (Deut. 12:4).
KEY POINT The Gemara meticulously ensures the count of prohibitions matches R' Ḥiyya's baraita — two for eating, three for cooking.

R' Zeira vs. R' Mani: Can an Oath Add a Prohibition?

R' Zeira
Add the case where one swears: 'I will not plow on a Festival' — then he also violates his oath!
R' Mani
That oath doesn't take effect — he's already under oath from Sinai not to plow on a Festival. An oath cannot add to an existing oath (ein shevuah ḥalah al shevuah).
R' Zeira
What if he swears: 'I won't plow on weekdays or Festivals'? Since it takes effect for weekdays, it extends to Festivals too (kolel)!
R' Mani
The Tanna doesn't teach matters subject to dissolution by a Sage — and oaths can be dissolved.
R' Zeira
But the mishna includes hekdesh (consecrated items), which can be dissolved!
R' Mani
The Tanna refers to a bechor (firstborn animal) — consecrated from the womb, not by vow, so it cannot be dissolved.
PRINCIPLE A prohibition that can be dissolved by a Torah scholar is excluded from the mishna's enumeration of simultaneous violations.

Rava's Famous Declaration

ארבעים יכנו — מניין הסמוך לארבעים
CLASSIC PASSAGE כמה טפשאי שאר אינשי — 'How foolish are the rest of the people who stand before a Torah scroll but do not stand before a great Sage!' For in the Torah scroll it says forty, yet the Sages came and subtracted one, establishing thirty-nine. If the Sages have the authority to reinterpret the Torah itself, how much more so do they deserve honor!

39 vs. 40 Lashes: Rabbis vs. R' Yehuda

Rabbis (Majority)

  • Torah says: 'by number, forty' (במספר ארבעים)
  • Reading: 'a number approaching forty' = 39
  • Lashes divided into 3 equal groups: 13 front, 13 right shoulder, 13 left shoulder
  • Zechariah 13:6 verse refers to schoolchildren struck by a teacher

R' Yehuda

  • He receives a full 40 lashes
  • The extra lash is administered between his shoulders
  • Proof: 'What are these wounds between your arms?' (Zechariah 13:6)
  • This verse refers to the additional lash between the shoulders
HALAKHA The law follows the Rabbis: the offender receives thirty-nine lashes, not forty.
