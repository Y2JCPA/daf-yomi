Makkot 16 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, why is one flogged for taking the mother bird without sending her away?
A. Because it is a case of safek התראה (uncertain forewarning)
B. Because Rabbi Yehuda rules one is flogged for lav she'ein bo ma'aseh
C. Because Rabbi Yosei HaGelili rules one IS flogged for lav she'ein bo ma'aseh
D. Because the verse explicitly mandates lashes for this violation
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan relies on Rabbi Yosei HaGelili's ruling that a prohibition without an action (lav she'ein bo ma'aseh) is nonetheless punishable by lashes — an exception to the usual rule.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How many sets of lashes does one receive for eating a wasp, and why more than for an ant?
A. Five — same as an ant, since both are land creatures
B. Six — the wasp adds the prohibition of winged creeping creatures
C. Four — wasps are covered by fewer prohibitions than ants
D. Three — only the general שֶׁקֶץ prohibitions apply
Correct: B — A wasp receives six sets of lashes: all five that apply to an ant, plus one additional set for the prohibition against winged creeping creatures (פּוֹרֵחַ), since wasps have wings.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rav rule regarding eating produce from which teruma and first tithe were separated but poor man's tithe was not?
A. It is completely permitted since the main tithes were taken
B. One is flogged, as the produce remains partially untithed
C. One pays a fine but is not flogged
D. It is a rabbinic prohibition only, with no biblical lashes
Correct: B — Rav rules that even if teruma and first tithe were separated, if poor man's tithe (maaser ani) remains, the produce is still considered טֶבֶל and one is flogged for eating it, based on Rabbi Yosei's baraita.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Reish Lakish, what is the basis for flogging one who takes the mother bird?
A. It is a lav she'ein bo ma'aseh per Rabbi Yosei HaGelili
B. The verse positions the positive mitzva after the prohibition, implying lashes when the mitzva is unfulfilled
C. It is derived from the laws of pe'a by analogy
D. Rabbi Yehuda rules all prohibitions carry lashes regardless
Correct: B — Reish Lakish learns from the Paschal offering verse (cited in Rabbi Yehuda's name): when Torah places a mitzva after a prohibition, this teaches that failing to fulfill the mitzva results in lashes.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What act does Rav Aḥai say violates 'you shall not make your souls detestable'?
A. Eating forbidden fat (חֵלֶב) knowingly
B. Delaying relieving oneself when the bodily need arises
C. Drinking wine that was left uncovered
D. Eating on Yom Kippur
Correct: B — Rav Aḥai rules that suppressing the need to relieve oneself violates the prohibition of 'וְלֹא תְשַׁקְּצוּ אֶת נַפְשֹׁתֵיכֶם' — you shall not make your souls detestable — extending this concept beyond forbidden foods.
