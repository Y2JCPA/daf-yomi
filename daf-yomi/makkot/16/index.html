⚖️ Core Dispute: Rabbi Yoḥanan vs. Reish Lakish

Rabbi Yoḥanan
One who takes the mother bird and does NOT send her away is flogged — the mitzva of sending (שִׁלּוּחַ) was NOT fulfilled.
Reish Lakish
He is flogged — because the verse positioned the mitzva after the prohibition, implying lashes apply when the mitzva remains unfulfilled.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Grounds my ruling in Rabbi Yosei HaGelili: a lav she'ein bo ma'aseh (לָאו שֶׁאֵין בּוֹ מַעֲשֶׂה) IS punishable with lashes — contrary to the general rule.
Reish Lakish
Grounds my ruling in Rabbi Yehuda: 'safek התראה' (uncertain forewarning) counts as valid forewarning for lashes.
Shared Conclusion Both agree: one IS flogged for taking the mother bird without sending her — but for different legal reasons.

🌾 Eating Untithed Produce — Rav's Ruling

Standard untithed produce (טֶבֶל): All tithes missing → definitely flogged
↓
Rav's ruling: Even produce from which teruma and first tithe were separated, but poor man's tithe (maaser ani) remains — still flogged
↓
Source: Rabbi Yosei in a baraita — liability extends beyond fully untithed produce to any stage where a tithe obligation remains
↓
Proof text: 'לֹא תוּכַל לֶאֱכֹל בִּשְׁעָרֶיךָ' — 'you may not eat within your gates the tithe of your grain' (Deut. 12:17)
↓
Rav Yosef: This ruling is disputed among tanna'im — Rabbi Eliezer disagrees regarding demai (doubtfully tithed produce)

🤢 Unusual Prohibitions: Soul-Detestable Acts

וְלֹא תְשַׁקְּצוּ אֶת נַפְשֹׁתֵיכֶם (ויקרא כ:כה)
Rav Aḥai & Rav Beivai bar Abaye Rav Aḥai: One who delays relieving himself when the need arises violates 'וְלֹא תְשַׁקְּצוּ אֶת נַפְשֹׁתֵיכֶם' — you shall not make your souls detestable (Lev. 20:25).

Rav Beivai bar Abaye: One who drinks from the horn used for bloodletting also violates this prohibition — the act is repugnant and self-degrading.

The Gemara expands the concept of שֶׁקֶץ beyond eating forbidden foods to any act that renders one's soul detestable.
