Makkot 20 — Bodily Prohibitions & Their Measures

קרחה • הקפת הראש • השחתת הזקן • שריטה
OVERVIEW Makkot 20 transitions from the complex case of multiple lashes for untithed produce to the Mishna's list of bodily prohibitions: creating a bald spot (korcha), rounding the head's edges (hakafat ha'rosh), marring the beard, and cutting incisions for the dead. The Gemara defines the minimum measures for each violation and explores who is liable — the one who shaves or the one being shaved.

The Mishna's Four Bodily Prohibitions

ProhibitionActionLiability
Korcha (קרחה)Creating a bald spot on the head in mourningLashes for each bald spot
Hakafat HaRosh (הקפת הראש)Rounding the edges of the head (temples)Lashes
Hashchatat Zakan (השחתת הזקן)Marring the five edges of the beardLashes for each edge
Serita (שריטה)Cutting an incision in mourning for the deadLashes for each incision per dead person
MISHNA RULE Five incisions over one dead person or one incision over five dead people — liable for each and every one separately.

Minimum Size of a Bald Spot (Korcha)

Rav Huna
It must be of sufficient size so that bald skin is visible on his head.
R' Yochanan / R' Elazar b. R' Shimon
The size of a Cilician bean (kigeris).
Rav Yehuda bar Chaviva
Three Tannaim disagree: kigeris, visible bald skin, or the measure of two hairs.
Another Version
One opinion replaces 'two hairs' with the size of a lentil.
MNEMONIC From Nega'im 6:5 — baheret (snow-white mark) = Cilician bean; raw flesh within it = lentil. These measures parallel the bald-spot dispute.

Removing Hair: Shabbat vs. Korcha vs. Grooming

Shabbat

  • Liable for removing a 'scissorsful' (מלא פי הזוג)
  • Rav Yehuda: Two hairs
  • R' Eliezer: Even one hair

Korcha (Bald Spot)

  • Disputed: kigeris / visible / two hairs / lentil
  • The Gemara links: 'And likewise for korcha — two hairs'
  • Same measure as Shabbat according to one view

Grooming (Lo Yilbash)

  • Removing white hairs from black — even one hair
  • Prohibited for men even on weekdays
  • Violates 'a man shall not don a woman's garment'
RABBIS CONCEDE Even the Rabbis who require two hairs for Shabbat agree that removing one targeted white hair from black is a complete, liable action — because his intent was for that specific hair.

Rounding the Head: Is the One Being Shaved Liable?

Baraita
Both the one who rounds and the one whose head is rounded are flogged.
Rav Chisda
Is one who eats dates from a sieve (passive recipient) really flogged? He performed no action!
Rav Chisda (explains)
The Tanna must be R' Yehuda, who holds one is flogged even for a lav she'ein bo ma'aseh (prohibition without action).
Rava
No — it refers to one who rounds his own head. He's liable both as the rounder and the rounded. Everyone agrees — he performed an action.
Rav Ashi
Or: the one being rounded assists by tilting his head — that counts as an action, and everyone agrees he is liable.
THREE ANSWERS Rav Chisda: follows R' Yehuda only. Rava: self-rounding = action. Rav Ashi: assisting = action. Each avoids the problem of lashes without an act.
