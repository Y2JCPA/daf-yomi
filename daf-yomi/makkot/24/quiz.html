Makkot 24 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
How does Rabbi Simlai arrive at exactly 613 mitzvot?
A. Gematria of 'Torah' (611) + 2 commandments heard directly from God
B. Gematria of 'Moshe' (345) + 268 from the prophets
C. 365 negative + 248 positive, counted by the Sanhedrin
D. An explicit count listed in Deuteronomy
Correct: A — Rabbi Simlai teaches that 'Torah' in gematria equals 611 — those were transmitted by Moses. Two more ('I am the Lord' and 'You shall have no other gods') were heard directly from God, totaling 613.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was Rav Safra's demonstration of 'speaks truth in his heart'?
A. He confessed to a sin he committed privately
B. He honored his unspoken intent to accept a buyer's first price
C. He told a king an uncomfortable truth about his kingdom
D. He refused to make a vow he could not keep
Correct: B — While reciting Shema, Rav Safra could not respond to a buyer. The buyer raised the price, but Rav Safra sold at the original offer — honoring the price he had silently accepted in his heart.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How did the Sages refute Rabban Gamliel's fear that one must fulfill ALL eleven attributes?
A. They cited a verse showing God forgives those who try
B. They argued from Leviticus 18:24 — if 'all these' means even one defiles, then 'these' means even one merit suffices
C. They showed that David himself only fulfilled seven of the eleven
D. They proved that the Psalm was meant as poetry, not law
Correct: B — The Sages used a kal va-chomer from Leviticus 18:24: just as 'all these' regarding impurity means even one transgression defiles, so too 'these' regarding merit means even one righteous quality earns reward.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Akiva laugh when he saw the fox emerging from the Holy of Holies?
A. He believed the fox was a good omen sent by God
B. He was in shock and reacted involuntarily
C. The fulfilled prophecy of destruction guaranteed the prophecy of restoration would also be fulfilled
D. He recalled a teaching that foxes represent the Messiah's approach
Correct: C — Isaiah 8:2 links Uriah (who prophesied destruction) with Zechariah (who prophesied restoration). Since Uriah's devastating prophecy was fulfilled — foxes in the Holy of Holies — Zechariah's comforting prophecy of elders in Jerusalem is equally guaranteed.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the connection between the two halves of this daf — distilling mitzvot and the destruction narrative?
A. Both involve the number 613
B. Both teach that God values quality over quantity — core principles matter, and even destruction serves a higher purpose
C. Both feature Rabbi Akiva as the main speaker
D. Both discuss the laws of the Temple service
Correct: B — The daf's unity lies in finding essential meaning: the prophets distill 613 mitzvot to core principles showing what truly matters, and Rabbi Akiva distills the destruction to its essential meaning — a guarantee of redemption. Both teach that beneath complexity lies a profound, hopeful core.
