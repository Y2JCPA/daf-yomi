Must One Fulfill All Eleven?

Rabban Gamliel
He who performs these shall never be moved. — Only one who does all of them is secure!
Rabban Gamliel
😢 He would weep upon reaching this verse.
The Sages
It says 'these' — not 'all these.' Even performing one earns a place in the World-to-Come.
The Sages
Proof: Leviticus 18:24 says 'Do not impurify with all these' — yet even one transgression defiles. The same logic applies here in reverse.
Principle A single righteous quality can secure one's place — the Torah does not demand perfection in all areas simultaneously.

Isaiah's Six Principles (Isaiah 33:15)

Actions

  • Walks righteously — like Abraham (Gen 18:19)
  • Despises gain of oppressions — like R' Yishmael ben Elisha
  • Shakes hands from holding bribes

Speech & Senses

  • Speaks uprightly — does not shame others in public
  • Stops ears from hearing blood(shed)
  • Shuts eyes from looking upon evil
Pattern Isaiah distills David's 11 into 6, grouping them into righteous conduct and guarding one's speech and senses.

Rabbi Akiva and the Fox: Linked Prophecies

The Sages ascend to Jerusalem after the destruction of the Temple
↓
At the Temple Mount, a fox emerges from the Holy of Holies
↓
The Sages
😢 Weep — 'Where a non-priest who approaches shall die, now foxes walk!'
Rabbi Akiva
😄 Laughs — 'This is exactly why I rejoice!'
↓
Uriah prophesied destruction: 'Zion shall be plowed as a field' (Micah 3:12)
Zechariah prophesied restoration: 'Elderly men and women shall sit in Jerusalem's streets' (Zech 8:4)
↓
Scripture links these prophecies (Isaiah 8:2) — fulfillment of destruction guarantees fulfillment of restoration
Famous Conclusion "Akiva, you have comforted us; Akiva, you have comforted us." — The Sages acknowledged that destruction itself is proof that redemption will come.
