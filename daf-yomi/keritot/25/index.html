👑 When Does the Tzitz (Frontplate) Effect Acceptance?

Offering rendered ritually impure (טָמֵא) — priest sacrifices it anyway
↓
Does the tzitz (ציץ) worn by the High Priest effect acceptance (meratzeh)?
↓
✅ Accepted
Impurity of the offering itself — general prohibition permitted 'before the Lord' for communal offerings
❌ Not Accepted
Leaving the courtyard, left-hand service, blemished animal — fail the 'before the Lord' criterion or explicit verse excludes them
↓
Source: "That they may be accepted before the Lord" (Exodus 28:38) — only sins whose general prohibition can be permitted before God in the Temple

📜 Mishna: The Handful Became Impure

נִטְמָא הַקֹּמֶץ וְהִקְטִירוֹ — הַצִּיץ מְרַצֶּה, וְהַשְּׁיָרִים נֶאֱכָלִין
Mishna — Menachot 25a If the handful (קֹמֶץ) became ritually impure and the priest sacrificed it anyway, the tzitz worn by the High Priest effects acceptance — and the remainder (shirayim) is eaten by the priests.

But if the handful left its designated area, or was offered by a non-priest, or offered while sitting, or with the left hand — the tzitz does not effect acceptance for these disqualifications.

⚖️ What Does the Tzitz Atone For? — The Disputes

Rabbi Zeira
Why not say the tzitz atones for offerings that left the courtyard? That prohibition too is sometimes permitted — the Red Heifer is performed entirely outside the Temple!
Abaye
The verse says 'accepted before the Lord' — only a sin whose general prohibition is permitted before the Lord, inside the Temple. The Red Heifer exception is outside — not before the Lord. ✗
Rabbi Ile'a
Why not say the tzitz atones for left-hand service? That too is sometimes permitted — on Yom Kippur during the Ketoret service inside the Holy of Holies!
Abaye
'Aaron shall bear the sin of the sacred items [הַקֳּדָשִׁים]' — the sin must be deferred within the offering itself, not merely a flaw in the mode of service. Left-hand service is excluded. ✗
Rav Sima
Why not say the tzitz atones for a blemished animal sacrificed? Its prohibition is sometimes waived — a firstborn with a blemish may be slaughtered for ordinary use!
Rav Ashi
The Torah explicitly states: 'It shall not be accepted' (Lev. 22:20, 22:25). An explicit verse of non-acceptance overrides the tzitz entirely. ✗
Abaye's Core Principle The tzitz covers only impurity of offerings — a sin that is sometimes permitted 'before the Lord,' i.e., communal offerings on Shabbat or Pesach may be brought even in impurity.

🔍 Four Cases — Included or Excluded from Tzitz Atonement

✅ Impurity of the Offering

  • General prohibition permitted 'before the Lord'
  • Communal offerings override impurity (Pesach, Tamid)
  • Sin resides in the offering itself
  • Abaye: meets all criteria
  • Tzitz EFFECTS ACCEPTANCE ✓

❌ Leaving the Courtyard

  • Red Heifer exception = outside the Temple
  • Fails 'before the Lord' criterion
  • Abaye's objection upheld
  • Tzitz DOES NOT effect acceptance ✗

❌ Left-Hand Service

  • Yom Kippur exception exists inside Temple
  • But: flaw is in mode of service, not the offering
  • Fails 'sin of the sacred items' criterion
  • Tzitz DOES NOT effect acceptance ✗

❌ Blemished Animal

  • Explicit Torah verse: 'It shall not be accepted'
  • Rav Ashi: explicit exclusion overrides tzitz
  • Rav Sima's proposed exception rejected
  • Tzitz DOES NOT effect acceptance ✗

📜 Resolving the Contradiction — Amoraim Weigh In

Rav Yosef
The two baraitot follow different Tannaim: one follows Rabbi Yosei (Sages do not penalize intentional impurity), the other follows Rabbi Eliezer (Sages do penalize). No reversal needed.
Gemara
But can Rabbi Yosei truly hold the tzitz effects acceptance even for intentional impurity of sacrificial portions eaten by priests? That requires further proof — did he say this about consecrated items, not just teruma?
Rav Ḥisda
Reverse the attribution: Rabbi Eliezer says tzitz effects acceptance for impure portions (he extends from teruma leniency to consecrated items); Rabbi Yosei says it does not.
Rav Sheila
No reversal needed! The two baraitot address different variables: one discusses how impurity was contracted (unwitting/intentional); the other discusses how the blood was sprinkled (unwitting/intentional). Both can follow the same authority.
Ravina
Concur with Rav Sheila's structure but with opposite assignment: sprinkling — whether unwitting or intentional, offering IS accepted. Contracting impurity intentionally — offering is NOT accepted.
Key Distinction Two independent variables: (1) how impurity was contracted vs. (2) how the blood was sprinkled. Mixing these up caused the apparent contradiction between the baraitot.
