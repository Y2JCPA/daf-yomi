🫗 Two Verses on Leper Oil Placement

Wealthy Leper — Leviticus 14:17

  • Phrase: "Upon the blood" of the guilt offering
  • Oil placed while blood is still present on thumb and toe
  • Alone it would exclude placement once blood is wiped

Poor Leper — Leviticus 14:28

  • Phrase: "Upon the place of the blood"
  • Oil placed after blood has been wiped away
  • Alone it would exclude placement while blood remains
3 / 9

⚖️ Why Are Both Leper Verses Necessary?

Gemara
Had the Torah written only "upon the blood" — I would say: oil may be placed only while blood still sits on the thumb and toe. Once wiped, the spot is disqualified.
Gemara
And had the Torah written only "upon the place of the blood" — I would say: oil may be placed only after the blood is wiped. While blood remains, no oil.
Resolution
Both verses are essential! Together they teach: oil placement is valid whether the blood of the guilt offering is still present or has already been wiped away.
Principle Each verse covers a case the other would exclude. Neither is superfluous — both are needed to encompass all valid scenarios.

✋ Rava's Rule: When Is the Right Hand Required?

Rabba bar bar Ḥana cites Rabbi Shimon ben Lakish:
Wherever the Torah mentions "finger" or "priesthood" in performing a rite, only the right hand may be used.
↓
One might think: both finger and priesthood must appear together in the same verse to mandate the right hand.
↓
Rava clarifies: Either one alone suffices — if a verse mentions finger or priesthood, the left hand is disqualified.
↓
Critical limit (Abaye's challenge resolved): The rule applies only to rites that are indispensable to atonement (מְעַכֵּב כַּפָּרָה). Auxiliary acts — like conveying burnt offering limbs to the altar ramp — are exempt even if priesthood is written.

🤝 Rava vs. Rabbi Shimon: One Condition or Two?

Rava
If a verse mentions either finger or priesthood in an atonement-essential rite — right hand only. One condition suffices.
Rabbi Shimon
No — both finger and priesthood must appear together in the verse. Priesthood alone does not disqualify the left hand.
Gemara
Proof for Rabbi Shimon: blood collection in a service vessel — only priesthood is written (Lev. 1:5), yet if performed with the left hand it is disqualified. Contradiction?
Rabbi Shimon
Blood collection is different — sprinkling blood mentions only priesthood and disqualifies the left hand, because the verse about sprinkling is where the rule is anchored, not collection alone.
Dispute Summary Rava: finger OR priesthood = right hand (for essential rites). Rabbi Shimon: BOTH together required. Debate hinges on blood collection and sprinkling.
