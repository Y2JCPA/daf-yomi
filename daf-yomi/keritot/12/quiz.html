QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, why can the burning of the handful NOT render a lacking remainder as piggul?
A. Because the lacking remainder was never consecrated to begin with
B. Because a lacking remainder is intrinsically disqualified — unlike external disqualifications such as leaving the Temple courtyard
C. Because piggul can only take effect if the High Priest performs the burning
D. Because a lacking remainder automatically has the status of notar (leftover)
Correct: B — Rav Huna distinguishes between external disqualifications (e.g., meat that left the Temple) — where burning the handful can still be effective — and intrinsic disqualifications like a lacking remainder, which is deficient by its very nature. Therefore burning cannot render it piggul.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was Rava's proof from Rabbi Ḥiyya's mishna that a lacking remainder can become piggul?
A. Rabbi Ḥiyya explicitly rules that piggul applies to any remainder regardless of size
B. Rabbi Ḥiyya omits the phrase 'an olive-bulk of the remainder,' implying the mishna discusses a remainder already lacking — yet piggul still applies
C. Rabbi Ḥiyya's mishna contradicts Rav Huna's position in a direct ruling
D. Rabbi Ḥiyya taught a separate baraita explicitly permitting piggul for lacking remainders
Correct: B — Rava reasoned that since Rabbi Ḥiyya omits 'an olive-bulk' when describing the remainder, his mishna must be discussing a case where the remainder is already lacking — yet the piggul rules still apply, proving that a lacking remainder can become piggul.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How did Abaye successfully refute Rava's proof from Rabbi Ḥiyya's mishna?
A. He showed Rabbi Ḥiyya's mishna was a minority opinion not accepted as law
B. He proved Rabbi Ḥiyya's mishna follows Rabbi Elazar, who requires the full handful for liability — so the omission of 'olive-bulk' for the handful (not the remainder) is the true reason for the unusual phrasing
C. He demonstrated that Rabbi Ḥiyya explicitly excluded lacking remainders in a later statement
D. He showed the mishna was discussing a different tractate's rules entirely
Correct: B — Abaye argued that Rabbi Ḥiyya's mishna follows Rabbi Elazar, who holds one is not liable for burning less than the full handful outside the Temple. Therefore, 'olive-bulk' was omitted because it does not apply to the handful (one cannot burn a mere olive-bulk), and the omission for the remainder follows automatically — it has nothing to do with a lacking remainder.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why is Rabbi Ḥiyya's mishna specifically relevant to the meal offering of a sinner?
A. Because the sinner's meal offering is always offering in a lacking state
B. Because the sinner's meal offering contains no frankincense, making the handful the sole permitting factor — so the mishna cannot add 'or burn an olive-bulk of its frankincense'
C. Because the sinner's meal offering requires a special piggul formula different from other offerings
D. Because the sinner's meal offering is the only one where eating and burning intents can combine
Correct: B — The meal offering of a sinner (מנחת חוטא) contains no frankincense. Since only the handful serves as the permitting factor, Rabbi Ḥiyya could not include the standard phrase about burning frankincense outside the Temple. The Gemara concludes the tanna formulated his mishna specifically for this case.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the final mishna on this daf, if one intends to eat half an olive-bulk of the remainder and to burn half an olive-bulk of it outside its area, what is the ruling?
A. The offering is piggul, because the two halves of an olive-bulk combine across any types of intent
B. The offering is disqualified (pasul) but not piggul, since the halves combine but weakly
C. The offering is fit (kasher), because eating intent and burning intent are different actions and do not combine
D. The offering is piggul only if the intent was expressed in the correct order
Correct: C — The mishna rules the offering is fit (כשר) because eating and burning are fundamentally different acts. Only two intents of the same type — eating+eating or burning+burning — combine to constitute an olive-bulk of improper intent. Mixed eating-and-burning intents cannot join together.
