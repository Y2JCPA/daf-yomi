⚖️ Central Dispute: Does a Lacking Remainder Become Piggul?

Rav Huna
The burning of the handful is NOT effective for a lacking remainder. When meat leaves the Temple, that is an external disqualification — but a lacking remainder is disqualified by its own intrinsic nature. Therefore piggul cannot take effect.
Rava
The burning of the handful IS effective even for a lacking remainder. Proof: Rabbi Ḥiyya's mishna never mentions 'an olive-bulk of the remainder' because it discusses a case where the remainder is already lacking — yet piggul still applies!
Abaye
Rava's proof fails. Rabbi Ḥiyya follows Rabbi Elazar, who holds one is not liable for burning less than the full handful outside the Temple — that is why 'olive-bulk' is omitted. It has nothing to do with a lacking remainder.
Rava Concedes the Proof Rava withdraws his proof from Rabbi Ḥiyya's mishna after Abaye's refutation, but retains his substantive position on other grounds.

🔄 The Four Rites That Can Render an Offering Piggul

1. Removing the Handful (קְמִיצָה) — Taking the handful from the meal offering; if done with improper intent, piggul process begins
↓
2. Placing in a Vessel (נְתִינָה בַּכְּלִי) — Transferring the handful into the sacred service vessel at the Temple
↓
3. Conveying to the Altar (הֲלָכָה) — Carrying the vessel containing the handful toward the altar
↓
4. Burning on the Altar (הַקְטָרָה) — Burning the handful on the altar; this is the critical permitting act
↓
Improper intent during any rite: to consume the remainder or burn the handful outside its designated area OR after its designated time → renders the offering piggul → liability for karet

🗣️ Rav Huna vs. Rava: The Lacking Remainder Dispute

Rav Huna
Even per Rabbi Akiva — who says sprinkling blood is effective for meat that left the Temple — that is because leaving is an external factor. But a lacking remainder is disqualified by itself. Burning the handful cannot help it.
Rava
Even per Rabbi Eliezer — who says sprinkling is NOT effective for meat that left — he only says so because leaving the Temple is a new disqualification. But lacking measure does not newly disqualify; the remainder was already fit before, so burning the handful IS effective.
Rava (proof)
Rabbi Ḥiyya's mishna omits 'olive-bulk' for the remainder — implying the remainder is already lacking — yet the mishna still treats piggul as applicable. This proves a lacking remainder can become piggul!
Abaye
That proof fails. Rabbi Ḥiyya follows Rabbi Elazar who requires the full handful for liability. He omitted 'olive-bulk' for the handful, not because of a lacking remainder — therefore the omission for the remainder follows automatically.
Resolution The Gemara establishes that Rabbi Ḥiyya's mishna concerns specifically the meal offering of a sinner (no frankincense), which is why the tanna formulated it in this unusual manner.

📋 Meal Offerings with Special Piggul Rules

Standard Meal Offering

  • Contains handful + frankincense (לְבוֹנָה)
  • Both serve as permitting factors for the remainder
  • Improper intent during burning of either renders piggul
  • Can state: 'burn an olive-bulk of its frankincense outside'
  • Full piggul rules apply — karet liability for eating

Meal Offering of a Sinner (מִנְחַת חוֹטֵא)

  • Contains handful ONLY — no frankincense
  • Only the handful serves as the permitting factor
  • Cannot say 'burn olive-bulk of frankincense' — there is none
  • Key case: explains why Rabbi Ḥiyya's mishna is formulated unusually
  • Abaye: the tanna went to this trouble specifically for this case

Meal Offering of Jealousy / Sota (מִנְחַת קְנָאוֹת)

  • If handful removed not for its sake → disqualified from the start
  • Even if burned with intent to eat next day — no karet
  • Not considered piggul because preliminary act was improper
  • Grouped with sinner's offering in the mishna
  • Permitting factor not sacrificed in accordance with its mitzva
