🧂 Salt and Wood: Communal Temple Supplies

מנחות כב — מלח ועצים מן הציבור
Halacha The salt applied to offerings and the wood burned on the altar both derive from communal Temple funds — funded by the Israelite half-shekel contributions. Private salt or privately-owned wood may not be used for the altar service. Rabbi Elazar ben Shammua adds a further principle: just as the altar itself was never put to mundane use — being built solely for God — so too the wood for the altar must be new and never previously used by an ordinary person.

🫙 Intermingled Handfuls: Fit or Unfit?

Rabbis
If a handful (kometz) from one meal offering intermingles with another handful, or with the meal offering of priests or the High Priest — the mixture is FIT. Both are destined for the altar; min b'mino (same type) does not nullify either component. We apply the majority principle.
Rabbi Yehuda
If a handful intermingles with the High Priest's offering or with libation meal offerings — the mixture is UNFIT. The High Priest's offering is entirely burned (kalil), but the handful is only partially offered. The two are fundamentally incompatible purposes.
Rabbis
Min b'mino means neither nullifies the other. The offerings are compatible enough — both ascend to the altar. The mixture retains fitness because the majority governs.
Rabbi Yehuda
The problem is not nullification — it is structural incompatibility. You cannot offer the mixture as a handful (partial burning) nor as a kalil (total burning). The mixture has no valid mode of offering.
Core Dispute Does intermingling two altar-bound substances preserve fitness (Rabbis) or create disqualifying incompatibility of purpose (Rabbi Yehuda)?

⚖️ Same Verse, Opposite Conclusions — Rabbi Yoḥanan's Insight

Rabbis

  • Source: Yom Kippur verse — bull blood + goat blood mixed before sprinkling
  • Lesson: Items that ascend to the altar (min b'mino) do NOT nullify each other
  • Scope: This rule applies specifically to mixtures of altar-bound substances
  • Application: Two handfuls intermingled = neither nullifies the other → fit

Rabbi Yehuda

  • Same source: Yom Kippur bull + goat blood verse
  • Lesson: Any same-type substance (min b'mino) is NEVER nullified — broadly
  • Scope: This rule extends beyond altar context to all same-type mixtures
  • Application: Min b'mino = no bitul ever, but mixture may still be unfit for other reasons
