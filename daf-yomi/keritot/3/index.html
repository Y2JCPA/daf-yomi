⚖️ Does Improper Intent Invalidate a Sacrifice?

Rabba
When improper intent is recognizably false — evident from the animal's appearance or the rite performed — the offering is disqualified. When it is not recognizable, the owner fulfills their obligation.
Gemara
But a goat sin offering slaughtered as a guilt offering — a goat has black hair, a guilt offering is a ram with white wool. Clearly recognizable! Should it not effect acceptance?
Rabba
A calf or bull slaughtered as a Paschal or guilt offering is utterly unlike either — such large animals are never offered as Paschal or guilt offerings. The difference is recognizable. Disqualified.
Gemara
Rather, the age-difference between a first-year and second-year animal is not obvious to observers — a large first-year animal can resemble a small second-year one. That distinction is not on people's minds.
Core Tension When does improper sacrificial intent become 'recognizably false' enough to disqualify an offering — vs. being overlooked so the owner still fulfills their obligation?

🕊️ The Bird Offering Challenge to Rabba

Gemara
A bird sin offering whose blood was sprinkled below the red line for the sake of a bird burnt offering should effect acceptance — sprinkling below the line proves it is being treated as a sin offering!
Rabba
People might say: Perhaps it is a sin offering whose blood had already been sprinkled — and the squeezing above the line was merely incidental. The false intent is not recognizable.
Gemara
But Rabbi Shimon says the bird offering does effect acceptance! Yet Rabbi Shimon never directly discussed bird offerings — his leniency was specifically articulated for meal offerings.
Rabbi Shimon
Meal offerings are not similar to slaughtered offerings — the mode of preparation of a meal offering proves its identity. For slaughtered offerings, the name of the offering is what matters: 'And this' indicates a specific law.
Key Distinction Rabbi Shimon's leniency stems from the unique self-identifying nature of meal offering preparation — not a general rule for all sacrifices

⚖️ Three Amoraic Resolutions of Rabbi Shimon's Contradiction

Rabba

  • Meal offering valid when intent is not recognizably false
  • The preparation of a meal offering proves its identity
  • Slaughtered offerings: even unrecognizable false intent is disqualifying
  • Rule: nikar (visible) false intent → disqualified in all cases

Rava

  • Meal offering valid via verse: "Zot torat hamincha"
  • One unified law applies to all meal offerings
  • Slaughtered offerings: verse "And slaughter it" demands specific intent
  • Sin offering for fat → for blood offering: invalid per Rabbis; valid per R. Shimon

Rav Ashi

  • Meal offering valid because vessel-type intent is inconsequential
  • Pan vs. deep-pan is a formal distinction, not a substantive one
  • Slaughtered offerings: even one slaughter method for all, the name matters
  • Intent shelamim is categorically different — the animal itself is called shelamim

📜 The Verse: Zot Torat HaMincha

ויקרא ו:ז — וְזֹאת תּוֹרַת הַמִּנְחָה | ויקרא ד:לג — וּשְׁחַט אֹתָהּ לְחַטָּאת
וְזֹאת תּוֹרַת הַמִּנְחָה "And this is the law of the meal offering" (Leviticus 6:7) — Rava derives that there is one unified law for all meal offerings. Therefore if a priest removes a handful from a pan meal offering intending it for a deep-pan meal offering, the owner still fulfills their obligation. The single 'law' bridges across different meal offering types.

By contrast, for slaughtered offerings: "And slaughter it for a sin offering" (Leviticus 4:33) — the word otah ('it') indicates that the specific offering must be slaughtered as that offering, demanding proper intent. Rav Aḥa son of Rava: this is the basis for disqualifying all such sin offerings.

📋 Keritot 3 — Summary Map

Core Question: Does improper intent during a sacrifice disqualify it?
↓
Rabba:
Depends on whether intent is recognizably false (nikar) from animal appearance or rite
Rava:
Meal offerings unified by "Zot torat hamincha"; slaughtered offerings need specific intent per "slaughter it"
Rav Ashi:
Vessel-type intent is inconsequential; intent about the offering's name/identity is what disqualifies
↓
Shared Conclusion: Meal offering with improper intent for another meal offering → valid. Slaughtered offering with improper intent for a different category → invalid.
