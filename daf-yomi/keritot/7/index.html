🏺 Do Service Vessels Sanctify Without Intent?

Rabbi Yoḥanan
Service vessels sanctify items placed in them only with specific intent — therefore returning the handful to the vessel does not disqualify it, since the priest has no intent to sanctify it again.
Reish Lakish
But I raised a dilemma before you: what if service vessels sanctify even without intent? Your answer implies they do NOT — but is that really settled?
Rav Amram
Even assuming vessels sanctify without intent, one can return the handful to a heaped bowl (levisa) — the handful rests atop the heap, outside the vessel walls.
Gemara
But once the handful was removed, a furrow was left — returning it places it back inside the vessel, which would re-sanctify and disqualify it!
Core Problem Returning the handful to the meal offering risks re-sanctifying it via the service vessel, which would disqualify the entire offering.

✋ How to Return the Handful Without Disqualifying It

Scenario: Priest removes the handful but needs to return it to the meal offering. Risk — the service vessel re-sanctifies the handful, disqualifying it.
↓
Solution 1 — Heaped Bowl (Levisa): Return the handful atop a heaped meal offering so it rests above the vessel rim, not inside it — vessel cannot sanctify it.
↓
Objection to Solution 1: If the offering was already heaped, how could the handful have been removed initially? And removal leaves a furrow — returning to that spot is returning inside the vessel.
↓
Solution 2 — Second Vessel: Remove the handful into a different vessel, then return it to the original vessel containing the meal offering.
↓
Result: The vessel now holding the handful is not the vessel receiving it — no re-sanctification occurs and the offering remains valid.

👨‍🏫 Avimi & Rav Ḥisda: When the Teacher Becomes the Student

Rav Ḥisda
I absorbed many blows (kulfei) from Avimi — he mocked me whenever I questioned his halakhic rulings on tractate Menaḥot.
Gemara
But the tradition says Avimi studied in Rav Ḥisda's study hall — was Avimi not the student? How could he be mocking the master?
Resolution
Avimi was originally Rav Ḥisda's teacher — but tractate Menaḥot was uprooted from him (he forgot it). He came to his own former student to help recall his learning.
Rav Naḥman
I met Avimi returning from Rav Ḥisda's hall. I asked: how does one remove a handful? He pointed to a vessel resting on the ground and scooped — no elevation required.
Reversal of Roles Avimi, the original teacher, forgot the tractate and returned to his own student (Rav Ḥisda) to relearn it — yet his demonstrations still shaped the halakha.

📐 Using a Vessel Resting on the Ground

Priest performs Temple service using a vessel resting on the ground
(not lifted or held aloft)
↓
Removing the Handful
Rava rules: Valid.
Derived from the frankincense bowls removed from the Table of the shewbread — the Table itself rests on the ground.
Sanctifying the Meal Offering
Rava raises a dilemma.
Do we derive from the meal offering (ground = OK) or from blood collection (must be held up)?
↓
Rav Naḥman's challenge to Avimi: If the vessel must be elevated for sanctification, you require three priests — one to lift, one to sanctify, one to remove the handful.
↓
Avimi: And let it require even three priests — the Temple service demands precision, not convenience.

🩸 Blood Sanctification: Full Measure vs. Divided

Full Measure in One Vessel — Valid

  • Priest collects a complete shiur of blood in a single vessel
  • Blood is properly sanctified for altar sprinkling
  • Derived from: 'And the priest shall dip' — adequate blood must be present for dipping
  • Rabbi Elazar (citing Rav Taḥlifa): verse teaches blood is not sanctified unless a full measure is present from the outset
  • Same principle applies to water of purification

Divided Between Two Vessels — Invalid

  • A full handful divided into two vessels: not sanctified
  • Rava: Derived from the halakha of blood collection in two vessels
  • Water of purification also invalid if divided — parallel ruling
  • Dilemma raised: is the water-of-purification ruling due to a specific verse or a general principle?
  • Resolution: The verse 'in the blood' confirms a full measure must be present at the moment of dipping
