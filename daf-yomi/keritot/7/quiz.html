Keritot 7 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, when do service vessels sanctify items placed in them?
A. Always, automatically, regardless of intent
B. Only when items are placed there with specific intent to be sanctified
C. Only when the vessel is elevated off the ground
D. Only during the removal of the handful, not during its return
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan teaches that service vessels sanctify items only when placed there with specific intent — therefore a priest who returns the handful without sanctifying intent does not disqualify the offering.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is a 'levisa' and why was it proposed as a solution for returning the handful?
A. A stone vessel that never sanctifies its contents
B. A sealed container that prevents contact between handful and vessel
C. A heaped bowl where the handful rests atop the heap, not inside the vessel
D. A second service vessel used exclusively for returning handfuls
Correct: C — Rav Amram proposed returning the handful to a 'levisa' (heaped bowl), where it rests above the vessel rim rather than within it — thus the vessel cannot sanctify the handful sitting on the heap.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Avimi come to study in Rav Ḥisda's study hall, even though Rav Ḥisda had been his student?
A. Rav Ḥisda was recognized as the greater authority in all areas of halakha
B. Avimi forgot tractate Menaḥot and came to his own former student to recall it
C. Avimi was testing whether Rav Ḥisda had mastered the material
D. The academy required sages to periodically audit junior scholars
Correct: B — Although Avimi was Rav Ḥisda's original teacher, tractate Menaḥot was 'uprooted' from him — he forgot it. He therefore came to his own former student Rav Ḥisda to help recover that learning.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rava's ruling about removing a handful from a vessel resting on the ground?
A. Invalid — the vessel must be held aloft by a priest
B. Valid — derived from the frankincense bowls removed from the Table of the shewbread
C. Valid only if the ground is the Temple courtyard floor
D. A matter of unresolved dispute between Rav Sheshet and Rava
Correct: B — Rava rules this is clearly valid, deriving the rule from the frankincense bowls removed from the Table of the shewbread — since the Table itself rests on the ground, removal from a grounded vessel is inherently valid.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When does blood that sprays onto a garment during sprinkling require laundering?
A. Always, whenever blood makes any contact with the garment
B. Only if the sprinkling was performed by the High Priest
C. Only if the blood sprayed before the sprinkling left the priest's hand
D. Only if the blood sprayed after the priest completed all of the sprinklings
Correct: C — The Gemara concludes that laundering is required only when blood sprays onto the garment before the sprinkling has left the priest's hand. Once the blood has been properly discharged (left his hand), it no longer carries the laundering obligation.
