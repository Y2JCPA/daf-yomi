⚖️ Can Half Sanctify? The Core Debate of Daf 8

Rabbi Yoḥanan
The High Priest's griddle-cake offering is not sanctified in halves. Like all meal offerings, it must be brought as a whole unit.
Rabbi Elazar
Since this offering is sacrificed in halves — half in the morning, half in the evening — it is sanctified as halves from the outset.
Mishna (50b)
Objection: The mishna states the griddle-cake did not come in halves — the High Priest brings a full tenth of an ephah, then divides it!
Rabbi Elazar
That requirement is only l'chatchila (ab initio). After the fact, if half a tenth was brought in the morning, the offering is valid.
Core Issue Does an offering that is ultimately divided into halves become sanctified as halves, or must it always begin as a whole unit? And does this rule extend to all meal offerings?

🔍 Can a Standard Meal Offering Be Sanctified with Half a Measure?

One sets aside half a tenth of an ephah intending to add more to reach a full tenth
↓
Rav
Not sanctified — derives from the griddle-cake: a half measure can never be sanctified for any meal offering
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Sanctified — the half is consecrated; one may add to complete the measure. Griddle-cake is a unique case.
↓
Tension: Rabbi Yoḥanan elsewhere derives laws across consecrated items (e.g., peace offerings in the Sanctuary). If he does analogize, why doesn't he apply the griddle-cake rule universally?
↓
Resolution: Rabbi Yoḥanan does derive across offering types — but the griddle-cake is exceptional because it is designed to be sacrificed in halves, unlike other meal offerings

🏺 Must All Components Be Together for Sanctification?

Rav
A meal offering is sanctified by a service vessel even without its oil — just as shewbread (לחם הפנים) is sanctified without oil.
Rabbi Ḥanina
A meal offering requiring oil is not sanctified without it. All components — flour, oil, frankincense — must enter the vessel together.
Rav
Oil and frankincense are each sanctified independently — oil like the leper's log (לוג השמן), frankincense like the bowls placed upon the shewbread.
Rabbi Ḥanina
Those are exceptional cases. For a standard meal offering, neither component is sanctified separately. The tenth-ephah vessel exists only for measuring; the log vessel only for the leper.
Shmuel's Support for Rav Shmuel derives from Zevaḥim 88a: bowls (liquid vessels) sanctify dry goods — proving flour can be sanctified even without oil present in the same vessel.

✋ Removing the Handful (קומץ) — Location Rules

Standard rule: The handful is removed in the Temple courtyard (עזרה) — derived from Leviticus 2:2: 'he shall bring it to Aaron's sons the priests; and he shall remove from there his handful'
↓
Rabbi Elazar's ruling: If removed inside the Sanctuary (היכל), the meal offering is still valid — the Sanctuary is more sacred, not less sacred, than the courtyard
↓
Baraita challenge: The verse's language about the left-hand disqualification implies that only the designated location is valid — location is a strict condition
↓
Resolution (Rabbi Ya'akov / some say Rabbi Yirmeya): The verse is needed to permit the entire courtyard — one might think only proximity to the altar suffices. The verse extends validity to the whole azarah
↓
Analogies rejected: Cannot derive from burnt offering (more sacred — entirely consumed), sin offering (stricter atonement laws), or guilt offering (blood-atonement type) — each has a distinguishing feature
