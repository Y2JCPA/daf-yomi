🫙 Oil Added to the Sinner's Meal Offering Handful

Rabbi Yoḥanan
If oil was added to the handful of a sinner's meal offering (which requires no oil), the offering is unfit — the prohibition was violated and cannot be undone.
Reish Lakish
The offering is fit — the handful simply should not be burned. The verse 'he shall put no oil' creates a prohibition, but does not retroactively invalidate the offering.
The Verse (Leviticus 5:11) "He shall put no oil upon it, neither shall he put any frankincense on it" — Reish Lakish: this teaches the act is forbidden, not that violation makes it unfit after the fact.

🥣 Mishna: Intermingled Meal Offerings

Two meal offerings (handfuls not yet removed) become intermingled with each other
↓
Separable
Priest can remove a proper handful from each separately → Both offerings are fit
Inseparable
Priest cannot remove each handful separately → Both offerings are unfit
↓
Handful intermingled with a remainder (its own or another's) → Should not be burned; but if burned, the owner's obligation is satisfied

⚖️ Rav Ḥisda vs Rabbi Ḥanina — Nullification Principles

Rav Ḥisda
We follow the potentially nullifying substance (the majority). If the majority could become like the minority, they are the same type — and the minority is not nullified.
Slaughtered meat can become carcass → carcass minority is not nullified in slaughtered majority
Rabbi Ḥanina
We follow the potentially nullified substance (the minority). If the minority could become like the majority, they are the same type — and it is not nullified.
Carcass can be re-slaughtered → carcass minority is not nullified in slaughtered majority
Practical Difference Carcass (minority) in slaughtered meat (majority): Rav Ḥisda says carcass IS nullified (majority cannot become carcass); Rabbi Ḥanina says it is NOT (minority carcass could become slaughtered).

🔥 Rava's Dilemma — Are Contiguous Substances Counted?

Rabbi Yoḥanan

  • Substances contiguous to items that ascend the altar are considered part of those items
  • A limb with less than olive-bulk of meat + bone completing the measure → liable for offering outside
  • Rava's dilemma: oil squeezed on wood before handful placed may still ascend with the handful

Reish Lakish

  • Contiguous substances are not counted as part of what ascends the altar
  • A bone that can be separated from the meat does not contribute to the measure → exempt
  • If the bone separated, nothing from the bone category would ascend on its own

Rav Ashi's Resolution

  • Rava's dilemma is independent — it can be raised even according to Rabbi Yoḥanan
  • The bone+meat dispute addressed a different scenario (inseparable attachment)
  • Oil squeezed separately onto the wood is a distinct, unresolved question

📜 Rabbi Zeira's Verbal Analogy (Gezerah Shavah)

The word burning (הַקְטָרָה) appears in the Torah regarding the handful removed from a meal offering
↓
The same word burning appears regarding the remainder of a meal offering
↓
Analogy derived: Just as when two handfuls intermingle, one does not nullify the other and both are burned — so too two remainders intermingled, neither nullifies the other
↓
Extension: A handful intermingled with a remainder — the Torah edict teaches nullification simply does not apply in either direction
↓
Result: These cases are governed by a Torah edict, standing independent of the Rabbis vs Rabbi Yehuda dispute over min b'mino
