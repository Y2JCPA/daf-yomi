⚖️ What Makes a Sacrificial Rite Indispensable?

Rav
Any rite where the Torah writes both "law" (תורה) and "statute" (חוקה) is indispensable — failure to perform it invalidates the offering.
Shmuel
Any rite that is repeated in the Torah text is indispensable — the repetition itself signals the rite cannot be omitted.
Gemara
Challenge to Rav: Nazirite, thanks offering, leper, Yom Kippur — all have only one of the two terms, yet their rites are indispensable!
Rav (clarified)
Even where only "law" or "statute" alone appears, it suffices — provided the context indicates indispensability. Both terms together are not always required.
Core Tension The Torah's specific language — paired terms, singular terms, or textual repetition — signals which rites are essential. Three amoraim offer competing keys to reading those signals.

🔗 Rabbi Shimon's Vav Principle — Abaye's Explanation

Abaye
Rabbi Shimon and the Rabbis disagree about whether a verse is interpreted as referring to both the matter preceding it and the matter succeeding it.
Rabbis: "with his finger" applies to both blood collection (preceding) and blood placement (succeeding).
Rabbi Shimon
The word "hand" is not stated regarding collection of blood — only regarding placement. Therefore my reasoning derives only from the vav (ו) in "And he shall bring it to Aaron's sons, the priests" (Leviticus 2:2).
Gemara
But if Rabbi Shimon uses vav to add context, why not derive from "And Aaron's sons, the priests, shall present the blood" that slaughter must also be performed only by priests?
Gemara (answer)
There it is different — slaughter precedes hand-laying in that verse, and hand-laying is owner-only. We cannot derive a priest-only slaughter requirement from an owner-only rite.
Hebrew Source וַיַּקְרֵב אֶת הַמִּנְחָה — "And he presented the meal offering" (Leviticus 9:17). The leading vav is Rabbi Shimon's linchpin.

⚖️ Rav's Law & Statute Principle — With Exceptions

Does the verse use both "law" (תורה) and "statute" (חוקה) in connection with this rite?
↓
✅ Both terms appear
Failure to perform the rite invalidates the offering
Core case: meal offering rites
⚠️ Only one term
Does the single term appear in a context indicating indispensability?
(Nazirite, Toda, Leper, Yom Kippur)
↓
Special case: If "statute" refers to eating (not rites), does it apply to the whole offering?
Shewbread: yes — "statute" on eating = entire matter.
Meal offering: no — only the repeated items (groats & oil) are indispensable.

📋 The Mnemonic & Its Challenges — נ-ת-צ; י-ק-מ-ל

Mnemonic: נ-ת-צ; י-ק-מ-ל — Nun, Tav, Tzadi; Yod, Kuf, Mem, Lamed
A memory aid for the series of challenges to Rav's principle
↓
נ — Nazirite (נזיר): Only "law" written (Numbers 6:21) — yet the mishna (27a) teaches the four species of the nazirite offering are indispensable.
↓
ת — Thanks Offering (תודה): Only "law" written (Leviticus 7:11) — yet the mishna teaches the four loaves of the toda are indispensable.
↓
צ — Leper (מצורע): Only "law" written (Leviticus 14:2) — yet the mishna teaches the four species of the leper's purification are indispensable.
↓
י — Yom Kippur: Only "statute" written (Leviticus 16:29) — yet the mishna teaches the two goats are indispensable to each other.
↓
ק-מ-ל — General Offerings: "Law" is written for many offerings (e.g., burnt offering, Leviticus 6:2) yet failure to perform all their rites does not invalidate them. Rav: those are different contexts entirely.

🔄 Rav vs Shmuel: Are Repeated Verses Decisive?

Rav

  • Every meal-offering rite that the Torah repeats is indispensable
  • "His handful" (Leviticus 2:2) requires use of the hand, not a utensil
  • Reinforced by the eighth day of Tabernacle consecration: "and he took a handful" (Leviticus 9:17)
  • Using a utensil for the handful invalidates the offering

Shmuel

  • One cannot derive permanent halakha from a temporary, one-time event
  • The eighth day of consecration was unrepeatable — not a valid source
  • Exception: offering of the twelve princes is repeated twelve times — sufficient to derive permanent halakha
  • The hand-only rule must be sourced differently than Rav claims
