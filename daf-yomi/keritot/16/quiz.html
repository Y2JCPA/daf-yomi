Keritot 16 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav, in which specific scenario do Rabbi Meir and the Rabbis disagree about whether a meal offering is piggul?
A. When both the handful and frankincense are burned with piggul intent
B. When the handful is burned in silence and the frankincense with piggul intent
C. When the offering is a sinner's meal offering with no frankincense
D. When neither the handful nor the frankincense is burned with piggul intent
Correct: B — Rav limits the dispute to the case where the frankincense (but not the handful) was burned with piggul intent. If the handful was burned first with piggul intent, all agree the offering is piggul, since the rites were performed כדרכן.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the ruling if one of two Shavuot lambs is slaughtered with intent to eat from the other lamb the next day?
A. Both lambs are piggul
B. Both lambs are valid offerings
C. The slaughtered lamb is piggul; the other remains valid
D. The two loaves become forbidden
Correct: B — The mishna rules that if one intends to eat from the other lamb (not the one being slaughtered), both are valid — since piggul intent must concern the very item being offered, not a different one.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rabbi Ḥanina resolve the apparent contradiction between Rav's view and the baraita that mentions 'handful with intent, frankincense in silence'?
A. He reinterprets 'silence' to mean partial intent
B. He says the baraita refers to two priests — one burned the handful with intent, another burned the frankincense in silence
C. He rules the baraita is a minority opinion of Rabbi Meir alone
D. He limits the baraita to the case of a sota's meal offering
Correct: B — Rabbi Ḥanina interprets the baraita as a case of two intentions with two priests — the first burned the handful with piggul intent, and a second priest burned the frankincense in silence. Neither act was a complete permitting act on its own.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rabbi Yochanan's ruling regarding piggul intent during the conveying (הולכה) of the handful?
A. It has no effect, since conveying is not itself a permitting factor
B. It is subject to the Rabbi Meir/Rabbis dispute about partial factors
C. It renders the offering piggul, just as intent during the removal of the handful does
D. It invalidates the offering but does not create piggul with karet liability
Correct: C — Rabbi Yochanan holds that the halakha of conveying equals that of removing (קמיצה): just as piggul intent during removal alone renders the meal offering piggul (since it is the sole permitting factor in some offerings), so too does intent during conveying.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the sesame-seed piggul case, what reasoning supports the view that 'all agree it is piggul'?
A. A sesame-seed equals an olive-bulk for purposes of piggul liability
B. The Rabbis concede to Rabbi Meir whenever both factors are performed with intent
C. Since the normal manner of eating and burning this offering is in small portions, the act is performed כדרכן
D. The priest performed the rite with explicit verbal declaration of intent
Correct: C — The Gemara explains: כך היא אכילתו וכך היא הקטרתו — the normal manner of consuming and burning this particular offering is in small (sesame-seed) amounts. Therefore the act is performed in its proper manner and piggul applies.
