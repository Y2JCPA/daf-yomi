🔥 The Core Dispute: Partial vs. Complete Permitting Factors

Rabbi Meir
Piggul intent during any part of the permitting factors renders the offering piggul — even if he only had intent during the handful but not the frankincense.
Rabbis
Piggul intent must span all permitting factors. Intent during only the handful (without the frankincense) does not render the meal offering piggul.
Exception — Rabbis Concede For a sinner's meal offering and a sota's jealousy offering — which have no frankincense — the Rabbis agree with Rabbi Meir that intent during the handful alone renders the offering piggul.

🤫 Rav vs. Shmuel: When Does the Dispute Apply?

Rav
The Rabbi Meir/Rabbis dispute applies only when one permitting factor was performed in silence and the other with piggul intent — e.g., handful placed in silence, then frankincense with piggul intent. If the handful was placed first with piggul intent, all agree it is piggul, since the rites were performed כדרכן.
Shmuel
The dispute applies even when both permitting factors are done with piggul intent. Rabbi Meir and the Rabbis disagree even in that case — Rabbi Meir says piggul intent during any single factor suffices, regardless of what the other factor was.
Baraita Challenge (Rav Aḥa bar Rav Huna) A baraita describes a case where the handful was placed with piggul intent and the frankincense in silence — yet the Rabbis still disagree with Rabbi Meir. Rabbi Ḥanina resolves this: the baraita refers to two priests, each performing one rite, so neither act was complete on its own.

🫙 Conveying the Handful: Two Opinions

Reish Lakish

  • Conveying (הולכה) the handful is not a permitting factor
  • It is merely part of the process — frankincense is also required
  • Piggul intent during conveying does not render the offering piggul
  • Subject to the Rabbi Meir/Rabbis dispute about partial permitting factors

Rabbi Yochanan

  • Conveying the handful does render the offering piggul if done with piggul intent
  • Halakha of conveying = halakha of removing (קמיצה): both suffice alone
  • Any non-permitting rite performed with one permitting factor transmits piggul
  • Basis: the handful is a sole permitting factor in certain meal offerings — its conveying inherits that status

🌱 Sesame-Seed Piggul: The Minimal Act Edge Case

Setup: A priest repeatedly burns a sesame-seed amount of the handful and frankincense, each time intending to eat a sesame-seed amount of the remainder the next day
↓
Problem: A sesame-seed is less than an olive-bulk (כזית) — the minimum measure required for piggul liability and karet
↓
Dispute: Three opinions — (1) all agree it is piggul; (2) all agree it is not; (3) it is subject to the Rabbi Meir/Rabbis dispute
↓
Cannot map to known dispute: Rabbi Meir may require full-measure intent; the Rabbis may permit sub-minimal acts in certain cases — each tannaitic position has its own logic here
↓
Resolution — 'all agree piggul': כך היא אכילתו וכך היא הקטרתו — the normal manner of eating and burning this offering IS in small portions, so the act is performed properly and piggul applies
