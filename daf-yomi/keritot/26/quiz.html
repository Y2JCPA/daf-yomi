Keritot 26 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav's interpretation of Rabbi Yehoshua, when does the impurity of the remainder render the meal offering unfit?
A. When any portion of the remainder becomes impure
B. When all of the remainder becomes impure
C. When the majority of the remainder becomes impure
D. When the impurity occurs before the handful is removed
Correct: B — Rav clarifies that Rabbi Yehoshua only rules the meal offering unfit if ALL of the remainder became impure. If only part of the remainder was affected, the rest is still sacrificed and the offering is fit.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the ruling when ½ olive-bulk of meat and ½ olive-bulk of fat are all that remain of the offering?
A. The priest sprinkles the blood, since the combined quantity equals an olive-bulk
B. The priest sprinkles the blood only if the fat is the primary remainder
C. The priest does not sprinkle the blood, since the two half-measures do not combine
D. The priest sprinkles half the blood as a compromise
Correct: C — The baraita explicitly teaches that ½ olive-bulk of meat and ½ olive-bulk of fat do not join together. Since neither constitutes a full olive-bulk on its own, the priest does not sprinkle the blood.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rabbi Shimon's ruling if the priest transferred the handful from his right hand to his left hand?
A. The offering is permanently unfit and cannot be corrected
B. The priest should return the handful to his right hand and continue
C. The handful must be returned to the meal offering and re-removed
D. The handful must now be placed in a service vessel to correct the error
Correct: B — According to Rabbi Shimon, if the handful was transferred to the left hand it should be returned to the right hand and the service continues validly. The Rabbis, by contrast, hold that the left-hand placement renders it permanently unfit.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, from when does the sacrifice of the handful permit the remainder for priestly consumption?
A. From when the priest places the handful on the altar
B. From when the fire first ignites and takes hold of the handful
C. From when the majority of the handful has been consumed by the fire
D. From when the priest completes the sprinkling of the blood
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan holds that the remainder is permitted only once the majority of the handful is consumed by the altar fire — derived by analogy from a furnace, which does not produce smoke until the majority of its contents are burned.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rabbi Yehuda son of Rabbi Ḥiyya explains that Rabbi Shimon's source for not requiring service vessel sanctification is a verse equating the meal offering to which other offerings?
A. The burnt offering and the peace offering
B. The sin offering and the guilt offering
C. The first-fruits offering and the thanksgiving offering
D. The Passover offering and the additional festival offering
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda b. Rabbi Ḥiyya derives Rabbi Shimon's position from Leviticus 6:10: "It is most holy, as the sin offering and as the guilt offering." Just as those offerings are not subject to a vessel requirement at the altar stage, neither is the handful.
