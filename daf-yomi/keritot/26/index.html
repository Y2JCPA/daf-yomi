🫙 Must the Handful Be Sanctified in a Service Vessel?

Rabbis
The handful requires sanctification in a service vessel (כְּלִי שָׁרֵת) before being brought to the altar. Without this step, the handful is unfit.
Rabbi Shimon
The handful does not require a service vessel — once removed, it may be brought up and burned even if carried by hand or placed in his lap.
Rabbi Yehuda b. Rabbi Ḥiyya
Rabbi Shimon derives this from "It is most holy, as the sin offering and as the guilt offering" (Lev. 6:10) — just as those require a vessel only at removal, so too the handful.
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
All concede the handful requires vessel-sanctification! The baraitot cited against me refer only to after the vessel-stage — not to eliminating it entirely.
Conclusive Refutation The Gemara concludes: a baraita explicitly teaches that Rabbi Elazar and Rabbi Shimon do NOT require service vessel sanctification — a decisive refutation of Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak's position.

⚖️ Olive-Bulk Threshold for Blood Sprinkling (Rabbi Yehoshua)

Olive-Bulk of Meat

  • One olive-bulk of meat remains
  • → Priest sprinkles the blood ✅
  • Baseline case; offering is accepted

Olive-Bulk of Fat

  • One olive-bulk of fat remains
  • → Priest sprinkles the blood ✅
  • Source: "For a pleasing aroma to the Lord" (Lev. 17:6)

Half + Half (Split)

  • ½ olive-bulk meat + ½ olive-bulk fat
  • → Priest does NOT sprinkle ❌
  • The two halves do not combine to an olive-bulk

Lobe of Liver / Kidneys

  • Only lobe of liver or kidneys remain
  • → Priest sprinkles the blood ✅
  • Derived from same verse: anything you offer to God

⏰ When Does the Handful's Sacrifice Permit the Remainder?

Rabbi Ḥanina
The remainder becomes permitted from when the fire takes hold of the handful — the moment it ignites on the altar.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
The remainder becomes permitted only when the majority of the handful has been consumed by the altar fire.
Rav Yehuda
Explaining Rabbi Yoḥanan: "The smoke went up as the smoke of a furnace" (Gen. 19:28) — a furnace does not produce smoke until the majority of its contents are consumed.
Ravin bar Rav Adda
Challenge from a baraita: items sacrificed during the day may still be burned after sunset — but if the fire starts at sunset, how can majority-consumption precede it?
Resolution Rabbi Elazar: the baraita refers to items already arranged on the altar (fire already burning) — no conflict with Rabbi Yoḥanan's majority-consumption requirement.

🫙 Proper Procedure for Removing and Sacrificing the Handful

Step 1 — Removal: Priest removes the handful (קֹמֶץ) from the meal offering using his right hand
↓
Step 2 — Vessel Sanctification (Rabbis): Handful is placed into a service vessel (כְּלִי שָׁרֵת) — this is required by the Rabbis; without it, the handful is unfit
↓
Step 2 — Rabbi Shimon's View: No service vessel needed after removal; priest may carry it by hand, in his lap, or even with his foot to the altar
↓
If transferred to the left hand: Rabbis — permanently unfit, the left-hand holding cannot be corrected. Rabbi Shimon — return it to the right hand and proceed
↓
Step 3 — Altar: Priest brings handful to the altar; if improper intent (outside time/place) is formed at this stage, offering is invalid but no karet liability
↓
Step 4 — Burning: Handful is burned; once the majority is consumed (Rabbi Yoḥanan) or fire takes hold (Rabbi Ḥanina), the remainder is permitted for priestly consumption
