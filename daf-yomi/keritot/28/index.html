⚗️ Two Hermeneutical Methods: Can Non-Gold Metals Be Used?

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
I apply klal u'prat (generalization and detail): 'Make a Candelabrum' is the generalization; 'of pure gold' is the detail. A generalization followed by a detail restricts the law to the detail only — solely gold is valid!
Rabbi Yosei b. R. Yehuda
I apply ribui u'miut (amplification and restriction): 'Make a Candelabrum' amplifies; 'pure gold' restricts; 'beaten work' amplifies again. Two amplifications around one restriction — include everything similar to the restriction. Gold is a metal, so all metals are valid!
Rav Yosef
A baraita resolves this: Temple service vessels fashioned from wood — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi deems them unfit, Rabbi Yosei b. Rabbi Yehuda deems them fit. Their dispute is whether non-metal materials (wood) qualify at all.
Rav Pappa b. Rav Ḥanin
But another baraita teaches: if one has no gold, one may use silver; if no silver, tin or lead — and it is fit (b'diavad). This implies even Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi permits other metals when gold is unavailable!
Practical Ruling If no gold is available: silver → tin → lead, each progressively acceptable b'diavad. Wood is forbidden even ex post facto according to Rabbi Yosei b. Rabbi Yehuda, who criticized the Hasmonean kings for using it.

🩸 Rava's Resolution: Two Baraitot on Sprinkling Lo Lishmah

Problem: Two baraitot contradict each other on sprinkling not for its own sake (לא לשמה)
↓
Baraita 1
Sprinkling not for its own sake → Invalid
Baraita 2
Sprinkling of leper's oil / inner blood not for its own sake → Valid
↓
Rav Yosef's attempt: Baraita 1 = Rabbi Eliezer (juxtaposes leper's guilt with nazir's guilt via hekkesh); Baraita 2 = the Rabbis
↓
Objection: Can a law derived via hekkesh itself teach another law via hekkesh? Regarding consecrated matters — no!
↓
Rava's resolution: Both baraitot follow the Rabbis — they address different aspects
↓
'Invalid' (Baraita 1)
The sprinkling does not permit eating the offering — not fully effective
'Valid' (Baraita 2)
The sprinkling act itself was performed — not retroactively null and void

📏 Shmuel's Description: The Menorah's 18 Handbreadths

3 handbreadths: The base (ירך) and the opening flower upon it
↓
2 handbreadths: Bare shaft rising above the base
↓
1 handbreadth: First knob — two branches emerge, one to each side, rising to the full height of the Menorah
↓
2 handbreadths: Bare shaft above the first knob
↓
1 handbreadth: Second knob — two more branches emerge to either side
↓
2 handbreadths: Bare shaft above the second knob
↓
1 handbreadth: Third knob — the final two branches emerge, completing all six side branches
↓
2 handbreadths: Bare shaft above the third knob
↓
1 handbreadth: A knob with a goblet, knob, and flower atop the central shaft
↓
3 handbreadths: The seven lamps at the top — goblets like Alexandrian cups, knobs like Cherethite apples, flowers like Cretan lilies

🎺 Moses's Vessels: Eternal vs. Generation-Specific

All Other Vessels Moses Made

  • Fit for Moses's generation
  • Fit for all future generations
  • Examples: Ark, Menorah, Altar, Laver
  • לְךָ ('for you') in Ark verse = from your own property, or: I desired your personal involvement
  • No verse restricts them to Moses alone — their sanctity is perpetual

The Trumpets Moses Fashioned

  • Fit for Moses's generation only
  • Unfit for all future generations
  • Verse: עֲשֵׂה לְךָ שְׁתֵּי חֲצוֹצְרֹת — 'Make for yourself two trumpets' (Num. 10:2)
  • לְךָ = for you specifically, Moses — not your successors
  • Their consecration was personally tied to Moses, unlike all other sacred vessels
