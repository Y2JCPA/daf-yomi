🤔 Is Salt Indispensable? Rav vs. The Challenge

Rav
Only rites explicitly repeated in the Torah verses are indispensable. Salt is not truly repeated — therefore if salt is omitted, the meal offering remains fit.
Challenger
But salt IS stated in a verse: 'And every meal offering of yours you shall season with salt' — is that not a repetition making it indispensable?
Rav (Resolution 1)
That verse functions as the 'detail' (prat) in a klal-prat-klal derivation — it defines the scope of salting, not a standalone requirement. Its purpose is hermeneutical, not an independent obligation.
Rav Yosef
Alternatively: Rav may hold that the word 'covenant' (brit) regarding salt is itself equivalent to a verse repetition, making salt technically indispensable through that language alone.
Mishna Connection Rav aligns with the tanna of the mishna who rules the meal offering is fit even without salt — because salt is not listed among the indispensable rites.

🔄 Step-by-Step: The Klal-Prat-Klal Derivation

Klal (General): 'And every offering [korban] you shall season with salt'
Could include everything burned on the altar — even wood and blood
↓
Prat (Detail): 'And every meal offering [mincha] of yours you shall season with salt'
Narrows focus: the meal offering is the defining case
↓
Klal (General again): 'You shall sacrifice salt with all your offerings'
Expands again — but now limited by similarity to the detail
↓
Qualifying Factor
Other items come as a requirement for it (e.g., wood needed for burning) — like the meal offering handful
Disqualified
Blood: not brought in same vessel as libations; no other items come as requirement for it

🔥 Complete Map: What Requires Salting?

✅ Salting Required

  • Handful of meal offering (primary case from the verse)
  • Frankincense accompanying a meal offering (same vessel)
  • Frankincense brought as a standalone offering
  • Limbs of a burnt offering
  • Meal offerings of priests (no handful removed — derived from 'you shall sacrifice salt with all your offerings')
  • Meal offering of the anointed High Priest (brought daily)

❌ Excluded from Salting

  • Blood — lacks qualifying characteristics of the meal offering
  • Wood — excluded by 'and every meal offering of yours' (even though it can be donated voluntarily)
  • Remainder of meal offering eaten by priests — verse limits salting to what is burned

📖 Three Key Verses

  • Klal: 'And every offering [korban] you shall season with salt' (Lev. 2:13)
  • Prat: 'And every meal offering of yours you shall season with salt' (Lev. 2:13)
  • Klal: 'You shall sacrifice salt with all your offerings' (Lev. 2:13)
  • Prohibition: 'Neither shall you suffer the salt of the covenant of your God to be lacking' (Lev. 2:13)

🪵 Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi: Voluntary Wood Offerings

וְנֶפֶשׁ כִּי תַקְרִיב קָרְבַּן מִנְחָה — Leviticus 2:1
Novel Ruling From the superfluous word korban in "when one brings a korban mincha" (Lev. 2:1), Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi derives that one may voluntarily donate wood as an offering for the altar — the word "korban" by itself (before mincha) indicates wood alone can be brought.

Rava: Such donated wood requires removal of a handful, like a meal offering.
Rav Pappa: Disputes this — wood brought alone does not have a handful removed.

Crucially: wood is excluded from the salting requirement by the phrase "every meal offering of yours" — wood is not a meal offering and therefore does not require salting.
