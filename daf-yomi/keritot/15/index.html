🍞 Piggul Asymmetry: Offerings and Their Accompaniments

קרבן מפגל את הלחם, אבל הלחם אינו מפגל את הקרבן
Core Principle A main offering can render its accompaniments piggul (invalidated by improper intent to consume outside permitted time or place), but the accompaniments cannot render the main offering piggul. This asymmetry flows from the concept of matir: only the agent that permits something can transmit piggul to it — never the reverse.

🏅 Three Proofs: The Tzitz Is Not the Dispute

Rav Huna b. Rav Natan
Can the dispute between Rabbi Yehuda and the Rabbis truly be about the frontplate (tzitz)? Even Rabbi Yehuda concedes the tzitz effects acceptance for items that ascend the altar when impure — a baraita about the frankincense bowls proves this!
Rav Ashi
Further proof from the Paschal offering (Pesaḥim 80a): when the entire community is impure, the tzitz effects acceptance — and even Rabbi Yehuda agrees in that case. The tzitz cannot be their dispute.
Ravina
Yet another proof from the two loaves of Shavuot: if the dispute were about the tzitz, Rabbi Yehuda should have ruled 'both are burned.' But he does not — disproving the tzitz theory entirely.
Conclusion Three independent Amoraim (Rav Huna b. Rav Natan, Rav Ashi, Ravina) each bring proof that the dispute between Rabbi Yehuda and the Rabbis does not concern the frontplate.

🍞 Mishna: How Piggul Flows Between Offering and Loaves

Thanks Offering (Todah) is slaughtered
Permitted: day of slaughter + following night only
↓
What was the priest's intent?
↓
Intent to consume the offering beyond its time
Offering is piggul ✓
Loaves are piggul ✓
The offering's blood is the matir — its piggul flows to the loaves
Intent to consume only the loaves beyond their time
Loaves are NOT piggul ✗
Offering is NOT piggul ✗
Loaves are not the matir for the offering — intent cannot flow upward

🐑 Two Parallel Cases of Asymmetric Piggul

Thanks Offering + Loaves

  • Offering → renders loaves piggul ✓
  • Loaves → do NOT render offering piggul ✗
  • Reason: offering's blood is the matir for its 40 loaves
  • Consumed: day of slaughter + that night only
  • Both cases are needed — neither alone suffices to establish the general rule

Shavuot Lambs + Two Loaves

  • Lambs → render two loaves piggul ✓
  • Two loaves → do NOT render lambs piggul ✗
  • Reason: lambs' blood is the matir permitting the two loaves
  • Consumed: day of slaughter + that night only
  • Unique: two loaves are normally valid for only this one occasion

🍶 Rabbi Meir on Libations and Piggul

Animal offering is slaughtered — blood rite performed
↓
Libations are consecrated in a service vessel (כלי שרת) — the critical moment according to Rabbi Meir
↓
Rabbi Meir: from vessel consecration onward, the libations are fixed to this offering — piggul intent during slaughter renders them piggul
↓
Rabbis object: one can bring libations up to 10 days after the offering — so the blood does not truly 'permit' the libations immediately
↓
Rava clarifies Rabbi Meir: once consecrated in the vessel, those specific libations are irrevocably fixed to that offering — they cannot be transferred. Therefore they are subject to the offering's piggul.
↓
Same law for the leper's log of oil (לוג שמן של מצורע): the guilt offering's blood is its matir — if the guilt offering is piggul, the log of oil is piggul as well
