⚖️ Three Disputes: Rabbi Yoḥanan vs. Reish Lakish on Meal Offerings

Rabbi Yoḥanan
Disqualified. Mixing oil outside the courtyard is invalid — even post-handful rites performed in a service vessel require the Temple courtyard.
Reish Lakish
Valid. After handful removal, pouring and mixing are only a mitzva of the priesthood — location inside the courtyard is not required.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
If offering became lacking before handful removal, owner may fill it from home — removal has not yet fully established it.
Reish Lakish
Must bring an entirely new full measure — a lacking offering cannot simply be refilled.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
If remainder became lacking after handful removal, burn the handful — any remaining portion suffices.
Reish Lakish
Handful is disqualified. The remainder must be fully intact at time of burning — a lacking remainder invalidates.
Core Principle at Stake All three disputes hinge on whether post-handful rites of a meal offering are fully Temple-service obligations (Rabbi Yoḥanan) or relaxed priestly mitzvos (Reish Lakish).

🫗 Mixing Oil Outside the Courtyard

Rabbi Yoḥanan
Disqualified. Granted, the priesthood is not strictly required — yet a meal offering's rites in a service vessel must be performed inside the Temple courtyard regardless.
Reish Lakish
Valid. The verse (Lev. 2:1) teaches that from the removal of the handful onward, pouring and mixing are exclusively a mitzva of the priesthood — no location requirement remains.
Verse at Issue "And he shall pour oil upon it and put frankincense thereon" (Lev. 2:1) — Reish Lakish: this verse strips the location requirement once the handful is removed.

📜 Two Baraitot on Lacking Offerings — Rav Malkiyu's Challenge

Baraita 1 — Before Handful Removal

  • Verse: "And he shall remove… of its fine flour" (Lev. 2:2)
  • Implies: if offering is lacking, the handful may not be removed
  • Teaching: cannot top off a lacking offering and proceed
  • Supports Reish Lakish over Rabbi Yoḥanan
  • Gemara resolves: verse teaches how to refill, not a disqualification

Baraita 2 — After Handful Removal

  • Verse: "That which is left shall be Aaron's and his sons'" (Lev. 2:3)
  • Priests receive remainder only if it was intact at burning
  • Implies: a lacking remainder disqualifies the handful's burning
  • Also supports Reish Lakish
  • Gemara resolves: refers to 'remainder of the remainder' (Ze'eiri)

✋ Deriving Left-Hand Disqualification from the Leper Passage

Mishna (9a): If the priest removed the handful with his left hand, the meal offering is unfit — but from where is this derived?
↓
Rabbi Zeira: Derived from Leviticus 14 (purification of a leper), which specifies "left hand" four separate times.
↓
Two mentions are intrinsic to the leper's ritual: the priest pours the oil onto his own left palm — those are required.
↓
Two extra mentions are apparently redundant — they form a double restriction (restriction after restriction).
↓
Rule: A restriction after a restriction serves to amplify, not narrow — it amplifies to teach the general principle.
↓
Conclusion: The right hand is universally required for Temple service; using the left hand disqualifies any meal offering.
