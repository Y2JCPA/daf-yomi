QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Reish Lakish, what is the status of a meal offering whose oil was mixed outside the Temple courtyard?
A. Disqualified, because service vessel rites must occur inside the courtyard
B. Valid, because after handful removal the remaining rites are only a mitzva of the priesthood
C. Valid only if a kohen gadol performed the mixing
D. Disqualified unless explicitly permitted by the supervising priest
Correct: B — Reish Lakish derives from Lev. 2:1 that once the handful is removed, pouring and mixing become a priestly mitzva only — the location requirement falls away, so mixing outside is valid.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
If a meal offering became lacking before the removal of the handful, what does Rabbi Yoḥanan rule?
A. The offering is entirely disqualified and cannot be used
B. A completely new full measure must be brought
C. The owner may fill the missing portion from home
D. The priest may remove the handful from the lesser amount as-is
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan holds that because the handful has not yet been removed, the offering has not been fully established — the owner may still supplement it from home to complete the measure.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the conclusion of Rav Malkiyu's argument from the two baraitot about lacking meal offerings?
A. Both verses address communal offerings and are irrelevant to individuals
B. One verse addresses lacking before removal, one after — both refute Rabbi Yoḥanan on both disputes
C. The two verses are redundant and one should be interpreted as a mnemonic only
D. Both verses support Rabbi Yoḥanan's lenient position
Correct: B — Rav Malkiyu argues the two verses must address distinct scenarios (before and after removal of the handful), and in both cases the verse implies disqualification — supporting Reish Lakish and challenging Rabbi Yoḥanan.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rabbi Zeira derive that removing the handful with the left hand disqualifies the meal offering?
A. From an explicit prohibition in Leviticus 2 on using the left hand
B. From a Mosaic oral tradition transmitted in the Mishnah
C. From four mentions of 'left hand' in the leper passage — two intrinsic, two forming a double restriction that amplifies to require the right hand generally
D. By analogy to the guilt offering of a nazir which explicitly requires the right hand
Correct: C — Rabbi Zeira uses Lev. 14 (the leper's purification): four mentions of שְׂמֹאל (left hand) appear. Two are required for the leper ritual; the two extra create 'restriction after restriction,' which by hermeneutical rule amplifies — teaching that right hand is required universally.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why, according to the Gemara, did Reish Lakish fail to defend himself against Rabbi Yoḥanan's shewbread objection?
A. Because Reish Lakish conceded the baraita refuted him
B. Because the shewbread is not technically a meal offering
C. Because he should have distinguished communal offerings (where impurity is permitted) from individual offerings — but he did not make this argument
D. Because Reish Lakish had no opinion on shewbread
Correct: C — The Gemara (as noted by Rav Pappa) observes that Reish Lakish could have neutralized the shewbread baraita by pointing out that communal offerings operate under different rules (the community may override ritual impurity), making the baraita inapplicable to individual meal offerings. His silence on this remains unexplained.
