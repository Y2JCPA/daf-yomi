🌾 The Four Rites of the Meal Offering (Mincha)

1. Kemitza (Removal) — Priest removes a handful (kometz) from the meal offering
↓
2. Placement — Handful placed into a kli sharet (sacred service vessel)
↓
3. Conveyance (Holakha) — Handful carried to the altar
↓
4. Burning (Haktara) — Handful burned on the altar; this is the climactic rite
↓
Result: The shiyarei mincha (remainder) becomes permitted to the priests for consumption

🧑‍⚖️ Resolving Rabbi Shimon's Apparent Contradiction

Rabbi Shimon (A)
A voluntary meal offering sacrificed shelo lishma SATISFIES the owner's obligation. (1st statement in baraita)
Rabbi Shimon (B)
A voluntary meal offering is like a guilt offering: valid, but does NOT effect acceptance (does not fulfill obligation). (2nd statement)
Rabba
Statement A = shinui kodesh (sacrificed for a different type of offering). Statement B = shinui shem (sacrificed for a different person's offering of the same type).
Rav Ashi
Reverses Rabba: Statement A = shinui shem. Statement B = shinui kodesh. The mishna follows THIS reading — it discusses shinui kodesh, hence no fulfillment.
Rava
Both refer to shinui shem: Statement A = priest's designation; Statement B = owner's designation. But the mishna cannot fit this reading.
Conclusion Rav Ashi's resolution aligns the mishna with Rabbi Shimon. Rabba's and Rava's resolutions each create difficulties explaining the mishna.

🔄 Why Shelo Lishma Affects Meal Offerings Differently Than Slaughtered Offerings

Meal Offerings (Menachot)

  • Different types have visually distinct preparations
  • Baked in oven, pan-fried, deep-fried — each unique
  • Intent 'for sake of X' is plausible, not obviously false
  • Therefore: shelo lishma can disqualify or remove fulfillment
  • Torah derives rule by comparing mincha to sin offering & guilt offering

Slaughtered Offerings (Zevachim)

  • All offerings share ONE manner of slaughter
  • All share ONE manner of blood collection
  • All share ONE manner of blood sprinkling
  • No visible difference between offering types
  • Therefore: shelo lishma does NOT disqualify slaughtered offerings

📜 The Verse That Proves the Vow Survives

מוֹצָא שְׂפָתֶיךָ תִּשְׁמֹר וְעָשִׂיתָ — דברים כג:כד
Devarim 23:24 "That which has gone out of your lips you shall observe and do; according to what you have vowed as a gift offering to the Lord your God, that which you have promised with your mouth."

The Gemara derives: If even a gift offering — which is entirely voluntary — cannot be deviated from, how much more so must the original vow remain binding after a shelo lishma sacrifice. The offering is valid; the obligation is not discharged.
