🩸 Does Intent to Leave Blood Overnight Invalidate?

Rabbi Yehuda
All agree — Rabbis and Rabbi Eliezer alike — that if the priest intended to leave the blood until the next day, the offering is unfit. Just as the act itself renders it unfit, so too the intent.
Gemara Objection
But the baraita shows Yosef the Babylonian repeatedly insisted the offering is fit! If all agreed it was unfit, what did he mean when he said 'you returned to me what I had lost'?
Rabbi Elazar ben Shammua
I had momentarily wavered — thinking perhaps the offering is unfit. Yosef pressed me three times until I held firm: the offering is fit. He rejoiced that I returned to the correct ruling.
Gemara Resolution
Rabbi Yehuda taught all-agree-unfit. Rabbi Elazar temporarily adopted this view, then corrected himself to fit (the Rabbis' view). Yosef's joy was that the true halakha was restored.
Bottom Line Rabbis: intent to leave blood overnight → offering is fit. Rabbi Eliezer: unfit. Rabbi Yehuda claims all agree it is unfit — but the story of Yosef complicates this claim.

📜 Yosef the Babylonian — Torah's Price

אַשְׁרֵיכֶם תַּלְמִידֵי חֲכָמִים שֶׁדִּבְרֵי תוֹרָה חֲבִיבִין עֲלֵיכֶם בְּיוֹתֵר
Aggadic Story Yosef the Babylonian asked Rabbi Elazar ben Shammua the same question three times:
"If the priest intended to leave the blood overnight — is the offering fit?"

• Afternoon: Rabbi Elazar said: Fit.
• Evening: Rabbi Elazar said: Fit.
• Morning: Rabbi Elazar, now doubting himself, said: "It seems my halakhot were inaccurate — perhaps it is unfit."

Yosef replied: "No! The offering IS fit, as you always said, my teacher."

Upon hearing this, Rabbi Elazar's eyes streamed with tears and he declared:
"Happy are you, Torah scholars, for whom matters of Torah are exceedingly dear!"

🎁 Rav Naḥman vs. Rava — Non-Priest Pouring Oil

Rav Naḥman's Distinction

  • Mishna (non-priest pours → fit) refers to an Israelite's meal offering
  • Baraita (non-priest pours → priest loses priestly gift) refers to a priest's meal offering
  • Priest's meal offering: entirely burned on altar — pouring is more central to its rite
  • No contradiction — two different subjects

Rava's Rebuttal

  • The priest's obligation to pour oil is derived from the Israelite's meal offering law
  • If non-priest pouring is valid for the source case (Israelite), it must be valid for the derived case (priest) too
  • Alternative proposal: Mishna = offerings where handful is removed; Baraita = offerings where it is not
  • But Rava challenges this too: same derivation logic applies
