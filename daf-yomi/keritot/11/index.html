🤲 How the Kometz Is Performed

Priest extends hand into the flour of the meal offering
↓
Bends middle three fingers until fingertips reach the palm
↓
Flour fills the space between fingers and palm — exactly one handful
↓
Thumb levels from above; little finger wipes away overflow
↓
Handful is placed into the keli shareit (service vessel) for sanctification
↓
If the handful is lacking or oversized, the meal offering is unfit

🤲 Abaye & Rava: The Little Finger Problem

Abaye
How do priests properly remove the handful? What technique do they actually use?
Rava
They remove it as people normally remove handfuls — folding all fingers over the palm of the hand.
Abaye
Objection! Each finger has a distinct sacred role. The middle finger measures the cubit, the forefinger sprinkles blood, the thumb... the little finger cannot be part of the kometz!
Gemara
The little finger serves for leveling only: the priest first removes a full handful with all fingers (so it is not lacking), then wipes away overflow with the little finger (so it is not oversized).
Resolution The little finger participates in the initial removal to ensure fullness, then levels by wiping away excess — both roles are harmonized.

🧂 Oil & Frankincense: Fit or Unfit?

⬆️ Too Much Oil

  • Added two log instead of one log
  • Non-sacred oil mixed in: disqualifies
  • Oil from another meal offering: disqualifies
  • Rabbi Eliezer uses 'it is not necessary to say' style
  • Even the owner's own extra oil invalidates

⬇️ Too Little Oil / Frankincense

  • Less than the prescribed measure of oil: unfit
  • Sinner's meal offering has no oil — can't decrease what isn't there
  • Frankincense decreased below one pinch: unfit (Meir/Yehuda)
  • Any decrease in frankincense: unfit (Rabbi Shimon)
  • Separated frankincense lost before burning: unfit

⬆️ Too Much Frankincense

  • Increased frankincense: the offering is fit
  • Mishna only disqualifies for decrease, not increase
  • Frankincense alone (not with meal offering) — separate rules
  • Frankincense in shewbread bowls: burned entirely regardless
  • Rami bar Hama: extra separated frankincense doesn't sanctify alone

🔥 The Hardest Rite in the Temple

קְמִיצָה — זוֹ קָשָׁה שֶׁבְּכָל הָעֲבוֹדוֹת
Most Difficult Sacrificial Act The removal of the kometz (handful) is called the hardest rite in the Temple service. The priest must simultaneously: • Not be lacking — fold all fingers to the palm for fullness
• Not overflow — thumb and little finger level the top
• Use the palm, not fingertips — fingertips alone is invalid
• Not take from the sides — Rav Pappa: ab initio must follow normal human manner Even if taken from the sides or atypically, Rav Pappa leaves the halakha unresolved (teiku). The stakes of this rite are uniquely high — pinching a bird's nape and scooping incense on Yom Kippur are also hard, but the kometz stands apart.
