QUESTION 1 OF 5
From which verse does the Gemara derive that a mourner is prohibited from wearing tefillin?
A. "His head shall be" (Leviticus 13:45)
B. "Bind your headwear [pe'er] upon yourself" (Ezekiel 24:17)
C. "And you shall impart them to your children" (Deuteronomy 4:9)
D. "Neither rend your clothes" (Leviticus 10:6)
Correct: B — God commanded Ezekiel alone to 'bind his pe'er (headwear/tefillin)' while in mourning — implying all other mourners are prohibited. The Sages identify pe'er with tefillin based on other verses.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the halakhic status of Torah study for a Metzora (leper)?
A. Completely prohibited, like an Avel
B. Permitted only in private, not in public study halls
C. Permitted — Zavim, lepers, and those impure from niddah may read Torah, Prophets, and study Talmud
D. Permitted only after immersion in a mikveh
Correct: C — The Gemara explicitly permits Zavim, lepers, and those who had relations with a niddah to read Torah, Prophets, Writings, and to study Midrash, Talmud, halakhot, and aggadah — their impurity does not suspend Torah engagement.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Abaye repeatedly challenge Rav Yosef's proofs from the wilderness generation regarding the Menudeh?
A. The wilderness generation was not formally ostracized by a rabbinic court
B. Rav Yosef himself stated that Heavenly ostracism is MORE serious than court-imposed ostracism
C. The wilderness proof is a narrative text, not a legal ruling
D. The wilderness generation received special dispensations from Moses at Sinai
Correct: B — Abaye notes Rav Yosef's own prior teaching: ostracism by Heaven is stricter than by earthly court. Therefore, Israel's permitted behavior under Heavenly ostracism in the wilderness cannot prove leniency for court-imposed ostracism.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What distinction does the Gemara draw between a Menudeh (ostracized) and a Muḥram (fully excommunicated) regarding Torah?
A. Both may teach and learn freely
B. Both are completely prohibited from any Torah study
C. Menudeh may teach and be taught; Muḥram may not teach or be taught, but may study alone
D. Menudeh may only study alone; Muḥram may teach but not be taught
Correct: C — The baraita distinguishes: the Menudeh may teach others and be taught; the Muḥram (more severe excommunication) may neither teach nor be taught, but may study alone — lest he forget Torah altogether.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rabbi Akiva derive that a Metzora is prohibited from tefillin, and how does Rav Pappa challenge this?
A. Rabbi Akiva derives it from Lev. 13:45; Rav Pappa says the verse means laundering, not tefillin
B. Rabbi Akiva uses a gezerah shavah from 'his head shall be'; Rav Pappa says the verse may refer only to a cap or scarf, not tefillin
C. Rabbi Akiva derives it from Ezekiel 24:17; Rav Pappa says Ezekiel's words apply only to mourners
D. Rabbi Akiva uses kal va-chomer from the Avel; Rav Pappa says a leper's impurity is different in kind
Correct: B — Rabbi Akiva uses a verbal analogy (gezerah shavah) — the phrase 'his head shall be' links leper to mourner, prohibiting tefillin. Rav Pappa counters that the verse may only prohibit ordinary headcoverings (kumta/sudara), not tefillin specifically.
