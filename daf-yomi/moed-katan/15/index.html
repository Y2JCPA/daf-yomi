🔥 Abaye vs. Rav Yosef: Heavenly vs. Earthly Ostracism

Rav Yosef
Come and hear! All the years Israel was in the wilderness they were ostracized by Heaven — yet they engaged in marital relations. Proof: one who is ostracized by a court may also have marital relations!
Abaye
But YOU yourself said that Heavenly ostracism is MORE serious than ostracism by an earthly court! How can you bring a proof from a lenient case to a stricter one?
Rav Yosef
I am uncertain whether Heavenly ostracism is truly stricter — perhaps it is stricter only for sins between man and God, but regarding interpersonal matters they are equal.
Abaye
Your own uncertainty undermines the proof! You cannot resolve our question using a case whose severity you yourself are unsure about.
Pattern Throughout Daf 15 This exchange repeats across multiple topics (marital relations, offerings). Abaye consistently blocks Rav Yosef's wilderness-generation proofs by invoking Rav Yosef's own teaching — a rare intra-Amora self-contradiction resolved only by Rav Yosef's stated uncertainty.

📜 Mourner's Prohibitions Derived from Ezekiel 24:17

The Verse (Ezekiel 24:17): God commands Ezekiel alone, while in mourning: "Sigh in silence; make no mourning for the dead, bind your headwear [pe'er] upon you, put your shoes on your feet, cover not your upper lip, and eat not the bread of men."
↓
"Bind your headwear [pe'er]" — Ezekiel was uniquely commanded to wear his tefillin (pe'er = tefillin). All other mourners are therefore prohibited from donning tefillin.
↓
"Put your shoes on your feet" — Ezekiel alone may wear shoes. All other mourners are prohibited from wearing shoes.
↓
"Cover not your upper lip" — Ezekiel alone may speak and greet. All other mourners must wrap their heads and are prohibited from offering greetings.
↓
"Sigh in silence" — Ezekiel was commanded to be silent. All other mourners are prohibited from Torah study and ordinary conversation.
↓
"Neither rend your clothes" (Lev. 10:6 to Aaron's sons) — They alone were exempt from rending. All other mourners are therefore obligated to rend their garments (kal va-chomer inference).

📖 Torah Study: The Crucial Exception

זָבִים וּמְצֹרָעִים וּבַעֲלֵי קְרָיִין מֻתָּרִין לִקְרוֹת בַּתּוֹרָה וּבַנְּבִיאִים וּבַכְּתוּבִים
Key Halakhic Distinction Avel (Mourner): Prohibited from Torah study — derived from "Sigh in silence" (Ezekiel 24:17). Acute grief uniquely severs Torah engagement.

Menudeh (Ostracized): May teach others and be taught. However, one who is muḥram (fully excommunicated) may neither teach nor be taught — though he may study alone, lest he forget Torah. He may also run a small store to earn a living.

Metzora (Leper): Zavim, lepers, and those who had relations with a niddah — despite their severe ritual impurity — are explicitly permitted to read Torah, Prophets, and Writings, and to study Midrash, Talmud, halakhot, and aggadah.

Principle: Ritual impurity and social isolation do not sever Torah connection; only the acute grief of mourning does.
