QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye, which specific word in the verse 'but on the day it appears in him' teaches that leprosy examination may sometimes be delayed?
A. The word 'day' (יוֹם) — examination is only by day
B. The word 'but' (אַךְ) — a limiting term implying sometimes yes, sometimes no
C. The word 'appears' (הֵרָאוֹת) — only when visible to the priest
D. The word 'him' (בּוֹ) — applies to the person, not the house
Correct: B — Abaye derives the permission to delay from the limiting word 'but' (אַךְ), which implies: sometimes you examine, sometimes you do not. He then uses 'on the day' separately to teach that examination is performed during the day, not at night.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Abaye resolve the apparent contradiction between Rabbi Meir (gathering bones is permitted on Chol HaMoed) and the baraita (one mourns when gathering bones)?
A. Rabbi Meir only refers to bones of children, not parents
B. The baraita discusses reburial in a new cemetery, not ancestral graves
C. It is permitted not because the act itself is joyous, but because the joy of the Festival prevails over the sadness
D. Rabbi Meir's ruling applies only after 12 months have passed since burial
Correct: C — Abaye explains that Rabbi Meir does not claim gathering bones is inherently joyous — the baraita correctly notes one mourns. Rather, the overriding joy of the Festival is powerful enough to allow the activity despite its sadness.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Yehuda, what is the structural difference between a 'nefesh' (crypt) and a 'kever' (grave)?
A. A nefesh is for Torah scholars; a kever is for ordinary people
B. A nefesh is constructed by digging into the ground; a kever is constructed by building above ground
C. A nefesh is permanent; a kever is temporary
D. A nefesh holds multiple bodies; a kever holds only one
Correct: B — Rav Yehuda teaches: crypts (nefesh) are made by digging, while graves (kever) are made by building. A baraita corroborates this distinction. Both are forbidden to construct anew on Chol HaMoed for those still alive.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav (as cited by Rav Daniel bar Ketina), what is the reason marriage is forbidden on the intermediate days of a Festival?
A. Wedding preparations constitute forbidden labor (melachah) during the Festival
B. One may not mix two separate joys — the joy of marriage with the joy of the Festival
C. A groom will neglect the mitzva of rejoicing specifically on the Festival
D. The Sages enacted this as a precaution following a specific historical incident
Correct: B — Rav derives from 'you shall rejoice in your Festival' — 'your Festival' implying that joy, not another. One may not combine the independent joy of a wedding with the joy of Yom Tov. Ulla and Rabbi Yitzḥak Nappaḥa offer different reasons (labor; neglect of Festival mitzva).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What reason does Shmuel give for the prohibition on arousing lamentation within 30 days before a pilgrimage Festival?
A. Thirty days is the standard mourning period and must be completed before celebrating
B. The deceased is not yet forgotten from the heart within 30 days, so renewed grief will persist into and disrupt Festival joy
C. Professional eulogizers will drain the mourner's finances needed for the Festival pilgrimage
D. The Sages enacted 30 days as a fence around the prohibition of eulogizing on Yom Tov itself
Correct: B — Shmuel explains: within 30 days of a death, the pain is still fresh and not yet forgotten. If one renews lamentation (e.g., by hiring eulogizers) within 30 days of the Festival, that rekindled grief will still be felt on the Festival, undermining the Torah's requirement to rejoice.
