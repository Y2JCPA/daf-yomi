Moed Katan 27 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Eliezer, when do mourners begin overturning their beds?
A. When the tomb is sealed with the grave cover
B. When the corpse is taken out of the opening of the house
C. After the first meal in the house of mourning
D. At the start of the seven-day mourning period
Correct: B — Rabbi Eliezer: overturning begins when the corpse exits the house. Rabbi Yehoshua disagrees, requiring the sealing of the tomb. Both were applied in the incident of Rabban Gamliel.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel rule regarding a dargash during mourning?
A. Overturn it like a regular bed
B. Remove it from the house entirely
C. Loosen its loops so it falls on its own
D. Cover it with a cloth and leave it standing
Correct: C — RSbG rules that for a dargash, one loosens the loops holding the pillows, causing it to fall on its own. R. Ya'akov bar Aḥa rules that the halakha follows RSbG.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did the Sages enact that all deceased be carried out on a plain bier?
A. Because dargashes contracted ritual impurity
B. To speed up the burial process on Yom Tov
C. Due to the honor of the poor, who were embarrassed by the contrast
D. Because wealthy families requested simpler funeral customs
Correct: C — The wealthy were carried on a dargash while the poor used plain biers. The poor were embarrassed by the disparity. The Sages instituted plain biers for everyone out of respect for the poor.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
From where does Rav Yehuda (citing Rav) derive that a mourner may not eat his own bread on the first day?
A. From the fasting laws of Yom Kippur
B. From God's instruction to Ezekiel: 'nor eat the bread of men'
C. From the practice in Rabban Gamliel's household
D. From the verse 'Weep not for the dead' (Jeremiah 22:10)
Correct: B — God told Ezekiel during mourning 'nor eat the bread of men' — implying that normally others must provide the mourner's bread. The mourner may not eat his own on the first day.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rabbi Levi say a mourner should imagine during the first three days of mourning?
A. That a sword lies between his thighs — he too may be facing death imminently
B. That the deceased watches over him and is at peace
C. That he stands before the Divine Throne in judgment
D. That the sword has passed before him and the danger is over
Correct: A — Rabbi Levi: Days 1–3 — sword between the thighs (acute dread). Days 4–7 — sword in the corner. After 7 — sword passes before him. Grief diminishes in structured stages.
