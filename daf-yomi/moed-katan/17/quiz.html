5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
If a student ostracizes someone to protect his own dignity, what is the legal status of that ban?
A. Completely invalid — only a teacher or court may impose ostracism
B. Valid — one ostracized by the student is also considered ostracized with regard to the teacher
C. Valid only if ratified by the Nasi within thirty days
D. Valid only for seven days, after which it expires automatically
Correct: B — The Gemara rules that a student's ban imposed to protect his own dignity is fully valid and carries the same force as if the teacher himself had pronounced it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rav say is the meaning of the word 'shamta' (excommunication)?
A. Shama ta — he heard and was astonished
B. Sham ta — he placed it there as a warning
C. Sham mita — there is death
D. Shama eta — he heard a divine sign
Correct: C — Rav derives shamta from sham mita — 'there is death' — indicating excommunication's potentially lethal severity. Shmuel disagrees and derives it from shama ta ('heard and was astonished').
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why are a nazirite and leper permitted to cut their hair on Chol HaMoed even if they had opportunity before the Festival?
A. Because their hair is ritually impure and poses a communal health concern
B. So that they will not delay bringing their required offerings, which depend on the haircut
C. Because the Nasi of the Sanhedrin granted them a permanent dispensation
D. Because hair-cutting is never restricted on Chol HaMoed for anyone
Correct: B — The baraita explains this special leniency: since both the nazirite and leper must bring offerings immediately upon completing their haircut, they cannot be made to wait — delaying the haircut delays the offerings.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Ulla, how does the halakha rule on cutting nails during mourning versus on Chol HaMoed?
A. Prohibited in both mourning and Chol HaMoed
B. Permitted in both mourning and Chol HaMoed
C. Prohibited during mourning (following R. Yehuda); permitted on Chol HaMoed (following R. Yosei)
D. Permitted during mourning (following R. Yosei); prohibited on Chol HaMoed (following R. Yehuda)
Correct: C — Ulla rules that Rabbi Yehuda's stricter view governs mourning (nails prohibited like haircuts), while Rabbi Yosei's lenient view governs Chol HaMoed (nails are a different category and are permitted).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What was Mar Zutra the Pious's distinctive practice when he needed to ostracize a Torah scholar?
A. He would send a written warning to the scholar three days before imposing the ban
B. He would first excommunicate himself as a mark of respect, then ostracize the scholar, then release his own self-imposed ban
C. He would petition the Nasi for permission before imposing any ostracism on a scholar
D. He would impose the ban anonymously so as not to publicly shame the Torah scholar
Correct: B — Mar Zutra the Pious, out of deep respect for Torah scholarship, would first ostracize himself, then impose the necessary ban on the scholar, and then release his own self-imposed excommunication — honoring the scholar's dignity even while disciplining him.
