📜 What to Do with a Scholar of Bad Repute?

Rav Yehuda
A Torah scholar has gained a bad reputation. If I excommunicate him, the Sages need him — he is a great authority. If I do not, God's name will be profaned. What should be done?
Rabbi Yoḥanan (via Rabba bar bar Ḥana)
"For the priest's lips should keep knowledge" — if a teacher resembles an angel of the Lord of Hosts, seek Torah from him. If not, do not seek Torah from him.
Rav Yehuda
On his deathbed, he rejoiced: 'I am happy that I did not flatter even the greatest of men.' He refused to revoke the ban even when the scholar came before him.
Rabbi Shmuel bar Naḥmani
Even the maidservant in Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's house pronounced a ban, and the Sages did not release it for three years. Shall we release a ban imposed by the great Rav Yehuda?
Outcome The Sages refused to release the ban. A wasp stung the scholar and he died. Yet he was buried among the pious — for when overwhelmed by his inclination, he acted discreetly in accordance with Rabbi Ilai's teaching.

🔔 The Etymology and Power of Shamta

חֵרֶם = ח׳ (8) + ר׳ (200) + מ׳ (40) = 248 — "And the city shall be a curse [ḥerem]" (Joshua 6:17)
What Does שַׁמְתָּא Mean? Rav: Sham mita — "there is death" — alluding to the lethal severity of excommunication.

Shmuel: Shama ta — "he heard and was astonished" — the excommunicated person is shocked into silence.

Reish Lakish: Just as ostracism enters all 248 organs of the body when first pronounced, so too when released, it must leave from all 248 organs.

Proof: חֵרֶם (ḥerem) by gimatriyya = 248, matching the count of organs in the human body.

✂️ Haircuts on Chol HaMoed — Who May and Who May Not

✅ Always Permitted

  • Nazirite — whose term of naziriteship ended on Chol HaMoed
  • Leper — undergoing purification on Chol HaMoed
  • Both permitted even if they had time before the Festival — so they will not delay bringing their required offerings

⚠️ Conditionally Permitted

  • Mourner — permitted if his 7th day of mourning fell on Shabbat eve of the Festival (per Abba Shaul: part of the day counts as the whole)
  • Priest — permitted if he completed his watch during the Festival week itself, not before it

❌ Prohibited

  • Anyone who had opportunity to cut hair before the Festival and failed to do so
  • Mourner whose 7th day fell well before Shabbat — per the Rabbis, who reject Abba Shaul's leniency
  • Cutting with scissors rather than a razor, even when the haircut itself is permitted (Rav Ḥisda)

⚖️ Ostracizing Torah Scholars — Rules and Reverence

Reish Lakish: A Torah scholar who sins is not ostracized publicly — as it is stated: "The prophet also shall fall with you in the night" (Hosea 4:5) — his disgrace should be as hidden as the night.
↓
Enacted at Usha: If the president of the court sinned, he is not ostracized — to avoid desecration of God's name. Instead, he is told privately: "You have made yourself weighty" — a private rebuke suffices.
↓
Mar Zutra the Pious: When a Torah scholar became liable to ostracism, Mar Zutra would first excommunicate himself as a mark of respect for the scholar's Torah dignity — then ostracize the scholar — then release his own self-imposed ban.
↓
Rav Giddel in name of Rav: A Torah scholar may impose ostracism upon himself, and he may similarly release himself from self-imposed ostracism — an act of autonomous rabbinic authority.
↓
Rav Pappa: "I am praiseworthy — I have never ostracized a Torah scholar." The Gemara asks: but we recall that he did act against a scholar once! Answer: that man had not yet been ordained — he was not yet a full Torah scholar at the time.
