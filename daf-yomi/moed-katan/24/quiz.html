QUESTION 1 OF 5
What three rites does Shmuel's mnemonic פ-ח-ז indicate are OBLIGATORY for a mourner on Shabbat?
A. Removing shoes, fasting, and covering the head
B. Uncovering the head, reversing the torn garment to the back, and standing the bed upright
C. Rending garments, avoiding bathing, and overturning the bed
D. Wearing sackcloth, refraining from Torah study, and removing shoes
Correct: B — Shmuel's mnemonic פ-ח-ז stands for Periat rosh (uncover the head), reversing the torn garment, and standing the bed upright — all obligatory on Shabbat even though public mourning is suspended.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Shmuel, a mourner who neglects periat rosh (not letting hair grow wild) and fails to rend his garments is subject to what consequence?
A. A monetary fine and public rebuke
B. Thirty-nine lashes
C. Death penalty at the hand of Heaven
D. Invalidation of his mourning period — must restart
Correct: C — Shmuel rules these two rites are so fundamental that neglecting them incurs death at the hand of Heaven, reflecting the gravity of outward mourning expression.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yehuda rule regarding the burial of a 30-day-old infant?
A. Carried in one's bosom by a single person
B. Carried on a bier like an adult, with full public mourning
C. Placed in a coffin carried by two people — not on a single shoulder
D. Buried privately with no coffin and no condolence line
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehuda specifies the infant is placed in a deluskema (coffin) carried in the arms of two people — not on one person's shoulder, indicating a greater level of honor than a newborn but less than an adult.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara rule that keriah for a deceased Sage is valid even if not performed at the exact moment of death?
A. Sages are exempt from the sha'at chimu requirement entirely
B. A Sage's students constitute a communal moment of grief
C. Their teachings are constantly quoted, so every mention renews the grief as if it were the moment of death
D. The ruling only applies to students who learned directly from the Sage
Correct: C — The Gemara reasons that since a Sage's Torah teachings are always being repeated and discussed, each time they are cited the grief is freshly renewed — making any keriah for them timely.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If a relative dies one day before Shavuot, how many days does that one day count toward the shloshim (thirty-day) mourning period?
A. One day only
B. Seven days, since Shavuot is like a full week
C. Fourteen days — one day plus Shavuot together count as fourteen
D. Thirty days — the entire obligation is considered fulfilled
Correct: C — Rabbi Anani bar Sason and Rabbi Yitzḥak Nappaḥa both taught that one day of mourning before Shavuot combined with Shavuot itself counts as fourteen days toward the thirty-day mourning period.
