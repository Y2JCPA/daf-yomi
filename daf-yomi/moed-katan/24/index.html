🕍 Shabbat Mourning: The פ-ח-ז Mnemonic (Shmuel)

Mourner on Shabbat — public mourning is suspended
↓
OBLIGATORY — פ-ח-ז
פ Periat rosh — uncover the head
ח Reverse torn garment to back
ז Stand the bed upright
OPTIONAL
Removing shoes — not required, since not everyone wears shoes at all times and it is not an obvious sign of mourning
↓
Rabbi Ya'akov (R. Yoḥanan): Uncover head ONLY if mourner has no shoes — to avoid appearing too obviously in mourning

🛐 Is There Any Mourning on Shabbat?

Rabbi Yoḥanan
Is there some mourning observed on Shabbat, or is there no mourning on Shabbat at all?
Shmuel
There is NO mourning on Shabbat at all.
Shmuel
Mnemonic פ-ח-ז: these three rites ARE obligatory — uncovering the head, reversing the torn garment, and standing the bed upright.
Rabbi Ya'akov (R. Yoḥanan)
Removing the head covering is obligatory on Shabbat ONLY when the mourner has no shoes — so as not to appear too obviously in mourning.
Gemara
Why is wearing shoes optional? Shmuel holds: any rending not done at the moment of most intense grief is not proper keriah — and the same logic extends to shoe removal.
Principle Public mourning is suspended on Shabbat, but private rites (head covering, bed position, torn garment orientation) remain — with nuance depending on what other signs are visible.

👶 Infant Burial Practices by Age

Under 30 Days

  • Carried in one's bosom (not a coffin)
  • No communal condolence line
  • No eulogy
  • Buried in a side area (beit hashelachim)
  • One person may carry; Abba Shaul: even one man and two women

30 Days Old

  • Carried in a coffin (deluskema)
  • R. Yehuda: in arms of two people — not on the shoulder
  • People do stand in a condolence line
  • Eulogy not given
  • R. Yehuda rules in name of R. Yishmael — Rav rules this is the halakha

12 Months Old

  • Carried on a bier like an adult
  • R. Akiva: applies if developed like a 2-year-old
  • Public grief (matzhivin) observed
  • R. Elazar ben Azarya: if known before birth — eulogized
  • R. Meir (R. Yishmael): poor children eulogized from age 3; wealthy from age 5

👕 Keriah (Rending Garments): When Is It Valid?

Keriah is performed
↓
Was it done at the moment of most intense grief — sha'at chimu?
YES — Valid
Proper keriah. Must be kept visible (before the mourner) for all 7 days. For father/mother: never fully mended.
NO — Invalid
Not considered proper keriah according to Shmuel. Same rule applies to wrapping of head — must be at moment of grief.
↓
Exception — Sages: Their teachings are constantly quoted, so every mention renews the grief. Any keriah for a Sage is therefore valid.

⚠️ Mourner and Marital Relations — Severity

אָבֵל שֶׁלֹּא פָּרַע וְלֹא פָּרַם — חַיָּב מִיתָה בִּידֵי שָׁמַיִם
Issur Chamur — Grave Prohibition Shmuel: A mourner who did not perform periat rosh (let hair grow wild) and did not rend his garments is liable to receive the death penalty at the hand of Heaven.

Rafram bar Pappa (from Evel Rabbati): A mourner is explicitly prohibited from marital relations during the mourning period. An incident is recorded of one who violated this — and died.

Note: The Gemara corrects an earlier version of Shmuel's statement that attributed the death penalty to marital relations — the actual ruling concerns neglecting periat rosh and keriah.
