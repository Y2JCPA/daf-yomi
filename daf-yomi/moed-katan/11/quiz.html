QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Rav permit Ḥiyya bar Ashi to weave simple traps (oharei) on Chol HaMoed, while complex nets (izlei) remained prohibited?
A. Simple traps were needed to catch food for the Festival itself
B. Simple traps are layman's work (מלאכת הדיוט), while complex nets require craftsman's skill
C. Rav held that all net-weaving is permitted during Chol HaMoed
D. Simple traps were made from natural materials rather than manufactured goods
Correct: B — The Gemara states the reason explicitly: simple traps are 'the work of a layman' (מלאכת הדיוט) and therefore permitted on Chol HaMoed, while complex nets require skilled craftsmanship and are prohibited.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How did Rava justify permitting the salting of the vast quantity of fish from the Bedita River on Chol HaMoed?
A. The fish would spoil — it was a case of financial loss (דבר האבד)
B. Freshly salted fish can be made edible immediately by pressing, so they qualify as Festival food
C. Rava held that preserving any food is always permitted on Chol HaMoed
D. The fish were ownerless property and therefore exempt from Chol HaMoed restrictions
Correct: B — Rava argued that recently salted fish can be eaten right away by pressing out the salt and brine — demonstrated by Shmuel's case. Since they are immediately edible, they count as food for the Festival, satisfying the mishna's condition.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rav Ashi resolve the contradiction between two mishnayot — one permitting and one appearing to prohibit repairs to door fixtures on Chol HaMoed?
A. One mishna refers to quiet carpentry; the other to loud smithing
B. One mishna follows Rabbi Yehuda's opinion; the other follows Rabbi Yosei's lenient opinion
C. The permitting mishna is about temporary fixes; the other about permanent repairs
D. One mishna applies to a private home; the other to a public building
Correct: B — Rav Ashi attributes the mishnayot to different tannaitic authorities: the mishna that prohibits follows Rabbi Yehuda's strict view; the mishna that permits follows Rabbi Yosei's lenient view, as derived from Rabbi Yitzḥak bar Avdimi.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel in the baraita, when may the mourner himself perform the olive-pressing?
A. Never — others must always perform the work on his behalf
B. Only during the last day of the seven-day mourning period
C. When no other skilled worker is available who can perform the task properly
D. When the olives are in a partnership with a non-mourner who requests it
Correct: C — Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel rules that if the mourner is the only person skilled enough to press the olives or plug the barrels properly, he himself may do the work — preventing financial loss overrides the normal mourning prohibition.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What principle does Mar son of Rav Aḥa son of Rava demonstrate when he refuses to allow his ox to work even after being told it is technically permitted?
A. That all mourning prohibitions override monetary considerations without exception
B. An important person must hold himself to a higher standard than the strict halakha requires, so people do not take the law lightly
C. That partnership property can never be used during one partner's mourning period
D. That work done publicly is always prohibited for a mourner even if private work is permitted
Correct: B — The Gemara explains: 'An important person is different (אדם חשוב שאני).' Even when something is technically permitted, a person of stature must be more rigorous so that onlookers do not draw incorrect lenient conclusions about the halakha.
