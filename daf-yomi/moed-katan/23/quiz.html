Moed Katan 23 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the baraita, what restriction applies to a mourner in his second week of mourning at synagogue?
A. He may not enter the synagogue building at all
B. He may not sit in his usual assigned seat
C. He must sit on the floor throughout the service
D. He may not recite Kaddish aloud
Correct: B — In the second week, the mourner may go out but may not sit in his usual synagogue place — a visible sign of reduced social reintegration. Rabbi Yehuda considers this the real novelty of the baraita.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Under what specific circumstance is a mourner permitted to remarry immediately, within the 30-day mourning period?
A. If the first Festival falls within the 30 days
B. If the local rabbi grants special dispensation
C. If he has no children and needs to fulfill the mitzva of procreation
D. If the deceased had also violated the mourning rules
Correct: C — If he has no children, he may marry immediately so as not to neglect the mitzva of פרו ורבו. The Gemara illustrates the limits of this — Yosef the Priest was rebuked for hinting at it at his wife's graveside.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, what is the practical halakhic difference between the two opinions on mourning on Shabbat?
A. Whether the mourner wears leather shoes on Shabbat
B. Whether the mourner attends public prayer on Shabbat
C. Whether marital relations are permitted on Shabbat during mourning
D. Whether the mourner eats meat on Shabbat
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan taught that the dispute turns specifically on marital relations during Shabbat of the mourning period — the opinion holding private mourning rites apply would prohibit them; the opposing view permits them.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the behavior of one whose deceased relative lies before him (met lifanav) differ on Shabbat vs. a weekday?
A. On Shabbat he still abstains from meat and wine
B. On Shabbat he reclines, eats meat and wine, and recites Zimmun as normal
C. On Shabbat he must eat in the same room as the deceased
D. On Shabbat he fasts entirely out of respect for the dead
Correct: B — On Shabbat he fully resumes normal meal behavior — reclining, meat, wine, blessings, and Zimmun — because Shabbat suspends the outward expression of mourning rites.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How did Abaye and Rava differ in their practice regarding clothing during their mourning periods?
A. Abaye wore black; Rava wore sackcloth
B. Abaye wore ironed white linen; Rava wore old garments
C. Abaye wore an old white garment; Rava wore a new red Roman cloak
D. Both followed the same ruling and wore unironed garments
Correct: C — Abaye wore an old white garment, following Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi. Rava wore a new red Roman cloak, following a lenient view that colored garments do not project the pressed, festive appearance the prohibition targets.
