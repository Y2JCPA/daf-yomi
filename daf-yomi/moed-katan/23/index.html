💍 Marriage After Bereavement — Rules and Exceptions

Standard Rule

  • Prohibited to marry during entire 30-day mourning period
  • If wife died: must wait until three Festivals have passed
  • Rabbi Yehuda: wait only until the first Festival passes

Exception — No Children

  • May marry immediately to fulfill פְּרוּ וּרְבוּ
  • Has not yet fulfilled the mitzva of procreation
  • ⚠️ Still must act with discretion — Yosef the Priest rebuked for hinting at remarriage at the graveside

Prohibited Activity in House of Mourning

  • May not speak about halakha or aggada — brings joy
  • Rabbi Ḥananya ben Gamliel did speak aggada there — criticized
  • Seven people read Torah, then each prays individually

🕯️ Is There Mourning on Shabbat?

First Tanna
There IS some mourning on Shabbat — private rites observed at home go unnoticed, and Shabbat 'counts as one of the days' implies active mourning status
Other Opinion
There is NO mourning on Shabbat at all — Shabbat merely counts toward the 7-day number but no actual rites are practiced, not even privately
Gemara
'Shabbat counts' (מוֹנֶה) — if mourning applies privately on Shabbat, why does the mishna need to say Shabbat 'does not interrupt'? That would be obvious!
Gemara (Reply)
The mishna teaches both: (1) Shabbat counts toward the 7 days, AND (2) it does not interrupt — both facts needed; one alone would be ambiguous
Rabbi Yoḥanan
The practical difference between the two views: whether marital relations are permitted on Shabbat during the mourning period
Tannaitic Parallel (Met Lifanav) The dispute may map onto the baraita about one whose deceased is laid out before him — on Shabbat does he resume normal conjugal life or not?

⚰️ Met Lifanav — Behavior When Deceased Lies Before Him

On a Weekday — eats in a different room, away from the deceased
↓
Does not recline at his meal — reclining signals festivity
↓
Eats no meat and drinks no wine
↓
Does not lead Grace After Meals (Birkat HaMazon) to exempt others
↓
Does not recite Zimmun — the formal invitation to bentch together
↓
On Shabbat — reclines, eats meat, drinks wine, recites blessings and Zimmun as usual
↓
Reason: public mourning is suspended on Shabbat; private grief continues but Shabbat's honor overrides outward rites

👔 Ironed Garments During Mourning — Opinions and Practice

General Prohibition

  • Prohibited throughout entire 30-day period
  • Applies to both new garments AND old garments taken from the press
  • Ironed garments give a festive, new appearance

Abaye's Practice

  • Went out in an old white garment
  • Followed Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's ruling
  • White garments are most conspicuous when pressed — old white avoids appearance

Rava's Practice

  • Went out in a new red Roman cloak
  • Followed a different lenient opinion
  • Colored garments do not give a pressed/festive appearance — thus permitted
