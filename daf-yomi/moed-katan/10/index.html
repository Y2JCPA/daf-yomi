🧵 Sewing on Chol HaMoed: Who May Do What?

הֶדְיוֹט — The Layman

  • May sew in his usual manner
  • Not a skilled tailor
  • Stitches are ordinary, non-expert
  • Permitted without restriction
  • Mishna: sews as he normally would

אוּמָן — The Craftsman

  • May only form temporary stitches (מַכְלִיב)
  • Skips stitches — does not complete the seam
  • Expert-level labor is restricted
  • Finished professional work is forbidden
  • Rabbi Yoḥanan's definition of makhliv

🛏️ Bed Cords on Chol HaMoed: A Tangled Dispute

Rav Dimi
Interweaving (קוֹלֵעַ) means tying cords lengthwise AND widthwise. Tightening (מֵהַדֵּק) means tying only lengthwise, not widthwise.
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
This cannot be right! The Mishna in Kelim shows Rabbi Meir holds a bed becomes susceptible to impurity when cords are tied — meaning full weaving is standard construction, so 'tightening' cannot mean partial weaving.
Ravin
Revised tradition: Everyone agrees interweaving = tying lengthwise and widthwise. The dispute is only whether one may braid entirely new cords. Rabbi Meir permits; Rabbi Yosei prohibits.
Gemara Conclusion
Even tightening loose cords may be prohibited — since one can sleep on clothing laid in the depression, the repair is not a genuine Festival necessity.
Baraita — Rav Taḥlifa bar Shaul Both Rabbi Meir and Rabbi Yosei agree: one may NOT braid cords anew on the intermediate days of a Festival.

💰 Commerce on Chol HaMoed — Rule, Exception, and Precedent

Rava's General Rule: All commerce is prohibited on the intermediate days of a Festival — buying and selling are labors incompatible with the sanctity of Chol HaMoed.
↓
Exception — Rabbi Yosei bar Avin: If leaving a matter unattended will result in significant financial loss (דָּבָר הָאָבֵד), it is permitted to act.
↓
Case 1 — Ravina's Merchandise: A ship carrying Ravina's goods arrived during Chol HaMoed. Rav Ashi permitted him to unload the cargo — leaving it on the boat risked loss of the merchandise.
↓
Case 2 — Ravina's Debt: Ravina's debtors in Shanvata gathered specifically for the Festival market. Rav Ashi ruled: since they may not be reachable afterward, collecting now prevents significant loss.
↓
Baraita Confirmation: One may attend even an idol-worshipper's fair on Chol HaMoed to prevent loss — the significant-loss principle is broadly applicable.
