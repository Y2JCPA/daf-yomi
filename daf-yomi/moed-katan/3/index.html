⚖️ Is Plowing During Shemita Floggable?

Rabbi Elazar
One who plows during Shemita is flogged. The verse 'a sabbath of solemn rest for the land' (Lev. 25:4) is a general prohibition — it covers all agricultural labors including plowing.
Rabbi Yochanan
One who plows is NOT flogged. The Torah gave explicit prohibition only to sowing, pruning, reaping, and grape-picking. Plowing lacks an explicit verse and cannot carry lashes.
Rabbi Elazar
The singled-out details (sowing, pruning) merely teach their individual flogging status — the general verse still prohibits all other labors at Torah level, plowing included.
Rabbi Yochanan
Since pruning is already a subcategory of sowing, and grape-picking of reaping, why enumerate them? To show these four alone are floggable — not plowing or other extensions.
Unresolved The Gemara finds the debate cannot be pinned solely to the principle of Rabbi Ile'a (whether a detail singled from a general prohibition applies only to that detail). Both positions can be argued either way.

🌿 Rav Ukva bar Chama: Two Types of Light Hoeing

Hoeing to Strengthen Trees

  • Goal: reinforce root system and promote growth
  • Functionally equivalent to sowing — encourages plant vitality
  • Prohibited by Torah law during Shemita
  • Liable to flogging
  • Example: hoeing under olive trees to bolster them

Hoeing to Seal Cracks

  • Goal: seal soil fissures and prevent moisture loss
  • Protective function, not growth-promoting
  • Prohibited by Rabbinic decree only
  • Not liable to flogging
  • The Exodus verse (23:11) is only an asmachta for this

📅 The Pre-Shemita Timeline: A Legal History

Halacha l'Moshe miSinai (original): Prohibition on plowing an orchard begins 30 days before Rosh Hashana of the Shemita year
↓
Beit Shammai & Beit Hillel extended the fence: orchard prohibition from Passover (or Shavuot); grain field restriction until the ground dries and people cease working it
↓
Dispute about the end date for grain fields — Beit Shammai: until Passover; anonymous Tanna: until Shavuot. Rabbi Shimon objects: without a fixed time, each person sets their own standard!
↓
Rabban Gamliel and his court voted and formally nullified the extended periods of Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel
↓
Challenge (Rabbi Zeira / Reish Lakish): How could Rabban Gamliel nullify a decree of Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel, who were greater authorities?
↓
Rabbi Abbahu's answer: Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel stipulated at the time of their decree that any future court wishing to nullify it may do so
↓
Further challenge: But this is Halacha l'Moshe miSinai — Beit Shammai didn't create it! Answer (Rabbi Yitzchak): Beit Shammai extended it; Rabban Gamliel merely restored the original 30-day Sinaitic limit

🕍 Rav Dimi's Tradition: What Is the 'Addition'?

Rav Dimi (from Eretz Yisrael)
Tradition received: one might have thought you are flogged for the 'addition' (תוספת) to the Shemita labors — but a teaching exempts from lashes. What exactly is this addition?
Rabbi Elazar
The addition is plowing itself. It has no explicit prohibition in the Torah. Why enumerate pruning and grape-picking unless to show that only those four are floggable — meaning plowing is exempt?
Rabbi Yochanan
The addition is the extra days before Rosh Hashana that the Sages appended. One is not flogged for plowing during the added pre-Shemita period — but plowing during Shemita proper is a Torah violation.
Key Consequence For Rabbi Yochanan, plowing during the Shemita year itself is a Torah prohibition (general verse). For Rabbi Elazar, plowing is never floggable — the explicit enumeration proves only the four named labors carry lashes.
