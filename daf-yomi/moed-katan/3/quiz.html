QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara's conclusion, which four Shemita labors are Torah-level prohibitions subject to flogging?
A. Sowing, pruning, reaping, and grape-picking
B. Plowing, sowing, pruning, and watering
C. Sowing, watering, fertilizing, and reaping
D. Pruning, fumigating, grape-picking, and plowing
Correct: A — Only sowing (זריעה), pruning (זמירה), reaping (קצירה), and grape-picking (בצירה) are explicitly singled out in Leviticus 25:4. All other labors are rabbinically prohibited; their scriptural citations are asmachta, not bona fide Torah sources.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Ukva bar Chama, what distinguishes the two types of light hoeing with respect to Shemita?
A. One uses metal tools; the other wooden tools — Torah law distinguishes them
B. Hoeing to strengthen roots is Torah-prohibited; hoeing to seal cracks is only rabbinic
C. Hoeing before Shemita is Torah-prohibited; hoeing during Shemita is rabbinic
D. One applies to olive trees and one to grapevines, carrying different legal status
Correct: B — Rav Ukva bar Chama teaches that hoeing intended to strengthen a tree's roots resembles sowing and is prohibited by Torah law during Shemita. Hoeing to seal moisture-draining cracks is a rabbinic prohibition only — not floggable.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yochanan interpret the 'addition' (תוספת) for which one is not flogged, as reported by Rav Dimi?
A. Plowing, which lacks an explicit Torah verse
B. The labor of fumigating trees with sulfur
C. The extra days before Rosh Hashana of the Shemita year added by the Sages
D. Grape-picking when performed in an orchard rather than a vineyard
Correct: C — Rabbi Yochanan holds that the 'addition' refers to the extra pre-Shemita days decreed by the Sages beyond the original Sinaitic 30-day limit. Plowing during Shemita proper is, for him, a genuine Torah prohibition covered by the general verse.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What was the original Halacha l'Moshe miSinai regarding pre-Shemita agricultural restrictions, before Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel extended them?
A. No pre-Shemita restrictions existed at Sinai
B. The prohibition began 30 days before Rosh Hashana of the Shemita year
C. The prohibition covered the entire sixth year of the Shemita cycle
D. The prohibition began from Passover of the sixth year
Correct: B — Rabbi Yitzchak teaches that the Sinaitic tradition restricted plowing only from 30 days before Rosh Hashana of the Shemita year. Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel later extended this fence considerably; Rabban Gamliel's court restored the original 30-day limit.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why were sowing and pruning explicitly singled out in Leviticus 25:4 if they were already included in the general prohibition 'a sabbath of solemn rest for the land'?
A. To teach that these two labors alone are prohibited during Shemita and all others are permitted
B. To teach that one is flogged specifically for these four, while subcategories derived from them are only rabbinically prohibited
C. To distinguish between grain-field labor and vineyard labor for tithing purposes
D. To indicate that these labors require a Sanhedrin of 23 judges for prosecution
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that sowing and pruning (and by analogy reaping and grape-picking) were singled out from the general prohibition to teach that flogging applies to those four specifically. Other agricultural labors are derived by extension and carry only a rabbinic prohibition — no flogging.
