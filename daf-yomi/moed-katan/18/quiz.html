QUESTION 1 OF 5
What three halakhot did the Gemara derive from Rabbi Yoḥanan cutting his nails in the study hall?
A. Scissors are permitted; foot nails may be cut; nails need not be buried
B. Nails may be cut on chol hamoed; in the study hall; and clippings may be disposed of there
C. Mourners may cut nails; foot nails are permitted; scissors are allowed
D. Nails must be burned; cut only with scissors; cutting is a mitzva on chol hamoed
Correct: B — The Gemara says 'learn three halakhot from this': (1) nail cutting is permitted on chol hamoed, (2) doing so in the study hall is permitted, (3) disposing of the clippings there is permitted — despite the baraita's warning about thrown nails.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the baraita, what is the ranking for disposing of nail clippings?
A. Burning = righteous; burying = pious; throwing away = acceptable
B. Burying = righteous; burning = even more pious; throwing away = wicked
C. All three methods are equally valid for a righteous person
D. Throwing = righteous; burning = pious; burying = wicked
Correct: B — The baraita ranks: burying clippings = righteous (safe), burning = pious (destroyed completely), throwing away = wicked because they can cause a pregnant woman to miscarry if she steps over them.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Shmuel rule that one may betroth a woman on chol hamoed, given that matches are Divinely decreed?
A. Because betrothal is a mitzva that overrides Festival restrictions
B. Because the woman's consent may change before the Festival ends
C. Because a rival may pray to God to alter the Heavenly decree in his favor
D. Because the mishna explicitly permits betrothal on the intermediate days
Correct: C — Shmuel's concern was not that a rival would literally betroth her first (her match is Divinely set), but that a rival might beseech God in prayer and cause a change in the Divine decree — prayer has the power to alter Heavenly decisions.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why are linen garments permitted to launder on chol hamoed even when other garments are not?
A. Linen is required for Shabbat and Yom Tov obligations
B. Linen garments are too expensive to risk soiling irreparably
C. Linen is easily soiled and cannot be prepared in sufficient quantity before the Festival
D. Linen dries faster than other fabrics, so laundering is less burdensome
Correct: C — Rav Yitzḥak bar Ya'akov bar Giyorei in the name of Rabbi Yoḥanan rules that linen garments may be laundered on chol hamoed because they soil easily — one cannot be expected to prepare enough clean linen in advance to last the entire Festival.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
From which three sections of Tanakh does Rav (citing Rabbi Reuven ben Itzterobili) derive that a person's marriage partner is Divinely decreed?
A. Torah (Exodus), Prophets (Isaiah), Writings (Psalms)
B. Torah (Genesis), Prophets (I Samuel), Writings (Proverbs)
C. Torah (Deuteronomy), Prophets (Jeremiah), Writings (Job)
D. Torah (Leviticus), Prophets (Ezekiel), Writings (Song of Songs)
Correct: B — The three proofs: (1) Torah — Abraham's servant's prayer in Genesis 24 shows the match was 'designated' by God; (2) Prophets — Merav's reassignment in I Samuel; (3) Writings — Proverbs 19:14 states explicitly 'a prudent wife is from the Lord.'
