✂️ Nails, Mourning & the Covenant of the Lips

Shmuel
My brother Pineḥas had long nails during mourning — I rebuked him. A mourner MAY cut his nails by hand! (Pineḥas then quoted Eccl. 10:5 and the words came true on me — the covenant of the lips.)
Rav Ḥiyya bar Ashi (Rav)
Even cutting by hand: using nail scissors (genustera) is PROHIBITED. An alternate method must be used — biting or using another finger.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
I cut my nails in the study hall and threw the clippings there. Learn THREE halakhot: cutting nails on chol hamoed is permitted; doing so in the study hall is permitted; disposing of clippings there is permitted.
Baraita (challenge)
Three rulings on nail clippings: Bury = righteous. Burn = pious. Throw away = wicked — thrown nails may cause a pregnant woman to miscarry!
Resolution A woman is rarely in the study hall, so Rabbi Yoḥanan was not concerned. If nails are swept up and discarded, no permanent harm results — they are no longer in one place.

👕 Who May Launder on Chol HaMoed?

Permitted — Mishna Cases

  • One returning from overseas travel
  • One released from a house of captivity
  • One who immersed from impurity
  • One who has only ONE shirt

Linen Garments — Always Permitted

  • Linen soils easily → permitted freely (Rav Yitzḥak b. Ya'akov)
  • Barber capes & body-drying towels: permitted
  • Bar Hedya: saw the practice at the Sea of Tiberias
  • Abaye: mishna covers all materials; linen has extra leniency

Prohibited — General Rule

  • Regular garments when one has two changes
  • Laundering without genuine, pressing need
  • Actions done against the will of the Sages

💍 Betrothal on Chol HaMoed — Shmuel's Ruling

May one betroth a woman on chol hamoed?
↓
Shmuel: Yes — lest another man betroth her first!
↓
Challenge
But Shmuel taught: every day a Heavenly Voice announces 'the daughter of so-and-so is destined for so-and-so'! Her match is already decreed — no one can steal her.
Resolution
Concern is that a rival will pray to God to change the decree. Prayer can alter Divine decisions — the rival does not betroth her, but asks Heaven to redirect the match.
↓
Rava's ruling: Do not pray to take someone else's designated wife. If she is decreed for you, she will come. If not, you cause yourself needless pain and act improperly.

💑 Divine Matchmaking — Three Proofs & the Rumor Rule

From the Torah (Genesis 24:14): Abraham's servant prayed: 'Let the maiden to whom I say… she is the one You have designated for Your servant Isaac' — matchmaking is explicitly from God.
↓
From the Prophets (I Samuel 18:19): Saul gave his daughter Merav to Adriel instead of David — this disrupted but did not permanently override a Divinely intended match.
↓
From the Writings (Proverbs 19:14): 'Houses and wealth are an inheritance from fathers, but a prudent wife is from the Lord' — explicit Divine providence in marriage.
↓
Rabbi Reuven ben Itzterobili: A man is only suspected of wrongdoing he has actually committed. A persistent rumor (עמד הקול) that does not stop must be taken seriously — unless spread by an enemy.
↓
Rumor thresholds: Stopped rumor = possibly baseless. Resumed rumor = persistent. Persistent rumor = credible. Exception: if the accused has enemies, the rumor may be malicious and is discounted.
