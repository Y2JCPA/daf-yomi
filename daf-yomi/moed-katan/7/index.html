⚖️ May a Priest Examine Leprous Symptoms on Chol HaMoed?

Rabbi Meir
A priest may examine leprous symptoms on Chol HaMoed — but only to be lenient (declare pure), never to be stringent and declare impure.
Rabbi Yosei
A priest may not examine leprous symptoms on Chol HaMoed at all — neither to declare pure nor impure. Festival joy must not be disturbed either way.
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
Rabbi Meir is correct for a quarantined leper (second week) — declaring him pure only helps. Rabbi Yosei is correct for a confirmed leper — declaring him pure sends him back to his wife, disrupting Festival joy.
Root Tension Protecting Festival joy (simchat yom tov) competes with halakhic process — does examination risk grief from impurity ruling, or disruption from premature freedom?

🐭 Trapping Moles & Mice on Chol HaMoed

Orchard

  • Trap in the usual manner
  • Trees suffer severe, irreversible damage from pests
  • Qualifies as davar ha'aved (significant preventable loss)
  • Standard traps and snares fully permitted
  • R. Shimon b. Elazar: even a grain field fully at risk is treated like an orchard

Field of Grain

  • Trap only in an unusual manner
  • Grain is considered less immediately vulnerable
  • No irreversible loss that cannot wait
  • Modified methods required (e.g., altered trap placement)
  • Base rule: change method to mark it as a Festival concession

🧱 Sealing a Wall Breach on Chol HaMoed

Permitted: Seal a breach in the wall of a garden on Chol HaMoed to protect crops
↓
Method (Rav Yosef): Use palm branches (hutza) and bay tree branches (dafna) — improvised, not standard masonry
↓
Baraita: Fill breach with stones, but do not plaster with clay — appearance must look casual, not professional construction
↓
Rav Ḥisda: This leniency applies only to a garden wall — a courtyard wall has no agricultural urgency and may not be sealed
↓
Exception — Leaning Wall: A wall tilting toward the public domain may be fully demolished and rebuilt in usual manner — danger to life overrides
↓
Rav Ashi's Proof: The mishna states 'in the Sabbatical Year one may even build normally' — this only makes sense for a garden wall; courtyard walls have no Sabbatical Year restriction to contrast with

⚖️ Leprosy Examination: Rava's Clarification of the Dispute

Rava
Unexamined pure person: All agree — the priest does not examine on Chol HaMoed. Examination can only worsen his status. No upside.
Rava
Confirmed impure leper: All agree — the priest does not reexamine. If declared pure, he returns to his wife and disrupts Festival joy (simcha).
Rava
The actual dispute: A leper in his second week of quarantine. Rabbi Meir: priest may examine at his discretion. Rabbi Yosei: still forbidden on Chol HaMoed.
Baraita (7b)
One view: company of the public is preferable — keep confirmed leper isolated. Other view: company of his wife is preferable — do not prematurely free him during the Festival.
Underlying Tension Two goods compete: (1) restoring an isolated leper to normal society; (2) protecting the domestic joy of Yom Tov. Which matters more shapes the halakha.

🤵 Bridegroom Who Develops Leprous Symptoms

Leprous symptoms appear on a bridegroom — when must the priest examine him?
↓
During wedding feast
Grant the full 7 days of the wedding feast before any examination — protecting joy of bride and groom
After wedding feast
Examine immediately — no further delay is warranted once the feast concludes
↓
Source (Gemara): 'Then the priest shall command that they empty the house before the priest goes in' (Lev. 14:36) — Torah itself delays examination to prevent financial loss of contents
↓
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's simpler proof: 'He shall be brought to Aaron the priest' — voluntary presentation implies the Torah allows flexible timing for legitimate personal reasons
