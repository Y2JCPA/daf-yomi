⚖️ Mourning: Parents vs. Other Relatives

💙 Other Relatives

  • Rend one handbreadth only
  • Rend outer garment only
  • May rend on the inside
  • Tack roughly after 7 days; join after 30 days
  • Cut hair after 30 days
  • Quick burial is praiseworthy

💛 Father or Mother

  • Rend until heart is revealed
  • Must rend all garments worn
  • Must rend on the outside (visibly)
  • Tack roughly after 30 days; join only when rebuked by colleagues
  • Cut hair only when colleagues rebuke his unkempt appearance
  • Quick burial is condemnable

⏳ Does an Arriving Mourner Count With the Household?

Question
The principal household mourner went to the cemetery and hasn't returned. Another mourner arrives at the house of mourning. Does he count his days with the household or on his own?
R. Yoḥanan
Even if the principal mourner went to the cemetery, the arriving mourner counts together with the remaining household members.
Contradiction
But another ruling of R. Yoḥanan says the mourner counts on his own! How can both be correct?
Resolution (Gemara)
Principal returns within 3 days: counts with the household.
Principal away more than 3 days: arriving mourner counts independently from the start.
Rava
Those of you in Meḥoza who do not follow the coffin to burial — begin counting mourning from when you turn your faces from the city gates to return home.
Practical Rule The moment of turning away from the burial direction is the trigger for beginning the mourning count when not present at interment.

✂️ Kriah: The Laws of Rending the Garment

💙 Other Relatives

  • Rend one handbreadth
  • Only the outermost garment
  • May rend on the inside (inner garment, hidden from view)
  • May rend with a utensil
  • May rend at the hem if desired — or not
  • Tack roughly after 7 days, join after 30 days

💛 Father or Mother

  • Rend until heart is revealed
  • All garments must be rent
  • Must rend on the outside (outermost, visible)
  • Must rend by hand only
  • R. Yehuda: must rip at the hem or it is a frivolous, meaningless rent
  • Tack after 30 days; join only when colleagues rebuke him

👕 Removing the Garment From the Shoulder

Who has died?
🔵 A Sage
Remove garment from the right shoulder
🟣 Av Beit Din
Remove garment from the left shoulder
👑 The Nasi
Remove garment from both shoulders
👨‍👩‍👧 Father or Mother
Must always bare one shoulder — it is obligatory, not optional
↓
Incident: When Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel (father of R. Yehuda HaNasi) died, a leading authority wished to bare his shoulder. Another leader present refrained — so the first refrained as well, lest it appear the second was mourning a parent.
↓
Dispute: Was the second leader R. Yehuda HaNasi himself (who could not bare his shoulder as it was his own father's death — requiring not just shoulder but full mourning)? Or was it Rabbi Ya'akov bar Aḥa?

💈 Mourning Timeline: Hair, Business, and Joyous Gatherings

During shiva (days 1–7): No haircuts, no joyous gatherings — applies to all mourners for all deceased relatives
↓
After 30 days — other relatives: May cut hair; may reduce business voluntarily but not required to do so
↓
Joyous gatherings (Rabba bar bar Ḥana): May attend turn-hosting meals after 30 days; but if the mourner is first to host in his group — must wait until 30 days have elapsed
↓
After 30 days — parents: Must still reduce business and cannot cut hair. Hair may only be cut when colleagues rebuke him for his unkempt appearance — no fixed deadline
↓
R. Abbahu on 'revealing the heart': The Torah says וַיִּקְרַע יַעֲקֹב שִׂמְלֹתָיו — Jacob rent his garments to the point of exposing his chest in grief for Joseph
