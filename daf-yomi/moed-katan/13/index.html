⚖️ Do Rabbinic Penalties Extend to the Violator's Heirs?

Father commits a violation subject to Rabbinic penalty — then dies
↓
Torah prohibition
Slitting a firstborn's ear
Son IS penalized — Torah-level severity demands continuity
Rabbinic prohibition
Performing labor on Chol HaMoed
Rabbi Zeira: like Shevi'it thorns — son is NOT penalized
↓
Abaye's principle: Rendering impure another's items — Sages did not penalize the son.
Reason: damage with no monetary restitution is not imposed retroactively on heirs.

💰 May One Pay Worker Wages on Chol HaMoed?

Rava
Is it permitted to provide wages to a poor worker who has nothing to eat on the intermediate days of the Festival?
Rav Naḥman
We learn from the mishna: purchases are permitted for a seller who has nothing to eat — so too, wages may be paid to a worker who has nothing to eat.
Abaye
Objection from Chol HaMoed: promissory notes are written only if the lender doesn't trust the borrower — this implies normal commerce is restricted even for the needy!
Rav Sheshet
Objection from erev Pesach: only three crafts are permitted until midday — if wage logic applied broadly, all labor would be permitted on erev Pesach too!
Rav Ashi
No contradiction at all — Chol HaMoed and erev Pesach are completely distinct halakhic domains. One cannot derive rulings for one from the other.
Halakhic Ruling Chol HaMoed and erev Pesach operate under separate legal frameworks — cross-derivation between them is invalid.

🛒 Purchases & Commerce on Chol HaMoed

❌ Prohibited

  • Buying houses, slaves, or cattle for personal use
  • Acquiring property for non-Festival business
  • Moving possessions from house to house
  • Retrieving utensils from craftsman (if not Festival-use)
  • Selling produce publicly in the marketplace (usual manner)

✅ Permitted

  • Any purchase needed for the sake of the Festival
  • Buying from a seller who has nothing to eat
  • Moving items to one's own courtyard (if necessary)
  • Bringing utensils that will be used on the Festival itself
  • Selling produce, clothing, and utensils in private

🏺 Retrieving Utensils from a Craftsman on Chol HaMoed

One wishes to collect finished utensils from a craftsman's house on Chol HaMoed
↓
Will be used on the Festival
✅ Permitted
e.g., a jug from the jug-maker, a cup from the glass-blower
Not needed for the Festival
❌ Prohibited
But items causing anxiety if left (lest they be lost) may be collected
↓
Resolution of contradictory mishnayot:
Mishna permitting = erev Pesach (14 Nisan) or craftsman's house in same courtyard.
Mishna prohibiting = Chol HaMoed proper or craftsman in a different domain.

✂️ Who May Shave & Launder on Chol HaMoed?

General rule: Shaving and laundering are prohibited on Chol HaMoed — one was obligated to prepare before the Festival began.
↓
✅ One who returns from overseas — could not prepare before the Festival due to travel; permitted to shave and launder.
↓
✅ One released from a house of captivity (בֵּית הַשְּׁבוּיִים) — was held against his will; permitted to shave and launder.
↓
✅ One released from prison — imprisonment prevented prior preparation; permitted to shave and launder on Chol HaMoed.
↓
✅ One released from excommunication (נִידּוּי) — was previously forbidden to shave; now that the Sage has released him, he is permitted.
↓
Unifying principle: All permitted cases share an unavoidable prior inability — they were halakhically or physically prevented from preparing before the Festival.
