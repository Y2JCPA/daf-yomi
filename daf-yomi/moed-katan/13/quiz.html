QUESTION 1 OF 5
Rabbi Zeira proves from the Shevi'it mishna that Rabbinic penalties do not extend to heirs. What is his proof?
A. A field plowed in Shevi'it cannot be sown even in the eighth year — showing penalties persist
B. A field whose thorns were removed in Shevi'it may be sown in the eighth year — showing no retroactive penalty
C. Shevi'it violations are punished by lashes, not financial penalties
D. The Shevi'it mishna teaches that heirs must repay all debts of the deceased
Correct: B — Just as removing thorns in Shevi'it (a Rabbinic act, not full labor) does not penalize the field retroactively, so too Rabbinic penalties — like those for Chol HaMoed labor — are not imposed on the violator's son.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
On what grounds does the mishna permit purchasing goods from a seller on Chol HaMoed?
A. The buyer needs the item urgently for Festival use
B. The item was contracted and paid for before the Festival
C. The seller has nothing to eat and needs the income
D. The purchase is conducted in private, not in the marketplace
Correct: C — The mishna's leniency for purchases on Chol HaMoed is based on the seller's basic needs — a seller who has nothing to eat may sell even on Chol HaMoed. The leniency is humanitarian, not commercial.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rav Ashi resolve the contradiction between Chol HaMoed labor rules and erev Pesach labor rules?
A. He rules that worker wages are prohibited on both Chol HaMoed and erev Pesach
B. He says erev Pesach is stricter than Chol HaMoed in all respects
C. He rules that the two contexts are entirely distinct — one cannot derive rulings for one from the other
D. He permits all labor on erev Pesach based on the Chol HaMoed worker-wages leniency
Correct: C — Rav Ashi dismisses Rav Sheshet's objection: Chol HaMoed and erev Pesach are independent halakhic domains with different foundations. Attempting to cross-derive between them is methodologically invalid.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Abaye, what does the term 'targis' (תַּרְגִּיס) refer to?
A. Emmer wheat soaked in water before husking
B. One grain of wheat broken into two pieces
C. One kernel of grain broken into three pieces
D. One grain broken into four fine pieces
Correct: C — Abaye's sequence: ḥilka = one kernel broken into two; targis = one kernel broken into three; tisanei = one grain broken into four. Rav Dimi offers an alternative definition based on emmer wheat that was soaked and husked.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which of the following is explicitly listed in the mishna as permitted to shave and launder on Chol HaMoed?
A. A Torah scholar who was too busy studying before the Festival
B. A merchant who forgot to shave before Yom Tov began
C. One who was released from a house of captivity during Chol HaMoed
D. An elderly person who was too weak to launder before the Festival
Correct: C — The mishna explicitly permits shaving and laundering on Chol HaMoed for those with unavoidable prior inability: one returning from overseas, one released from captivity, and one released from prison — all cases where preparation before the Festival was impossible.
