🪨 Contradiction: One Stone vs. Two Stones

Rav Yehuda
One marked stone → ground beneath it is ritually impure. Two stones with plowed ground between them → area between is pure.
Baraita
One stone → beneath it is impure. Two stones → both are impure and the space between them is also impure! This contradicts Rav Yehuda — how?
Rav Pappa
No contradiction. In the baraita: lime was poured on top of the stones and spread to both sides — so each stone marks a separate grave, and the space between is pure. When lime is on the sides, the two stones bracket a single impure zone → space between is impure.
Key Principle The placement of the lime marker — on top (spreading outward) vs. on the sides — determines whether two stones indicate one shared zone of impurity or two separate graves.

🏛️ Rabbi Asi: How Many Borders Are Marked?

One border marked → only that border is impure; entire rest of the field is pure
↓
Two borders marked → only those two borders are impure; remainder is pure
↓
Three borders marked → all three are impure; unmarked fourth border remains pure
↓
All four borders marked → the entire field is ritually impure
↓
Reasoning: 'We do not distance the marker from the actual site of impurity' — so as not to unnecessarily restrict access to Eretz Yisrael (loss of usable land)

🌿 When Do Inspectors Go Out for Kilayim?

Our Mishna
Court inspectors go out on Chol HaMoed (intermediate Festival days) to uproot shoots of diverse kinds that sprouted during the rainy season.
Mishna Shekalim
On the 15th of Adar, they go out to clear roads and uproot diverse kinds. Why do we need Chol HaMoed too?
Rabbi Elazar
Adar = the early crop; Chol HaMoed = the late crop. They are two separate rounds targeting different growth stages.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Chol HaMoed applies when the blossom was not yet recognizable in Adar — it was impossible to identify the kilayim earlier. Also: workers are available and wages are paid from the Temple treasury chamber funds.
Rav Zevid / Rav Mesharshiyya We learn from this that wages paid to workers who uproot diverse kinds are disbursed from the Temple treasury collection chamber funds — a public obligation.

💧 Watering Fields on Chol HaMoed

Moist Field (Metunenet)

  • Field was moist before the Festival
  • Permitted to water the entire field
  • Even if it dried up in the interim: Rav Yehuda permits watering
  • R. Eliezer ben Ya'akov: permitted to draw water via channels between trees

Dry Field

  • Field was dry before the Festival
  • First Tanna: forbidden to water on Chol HaMoed
  • Rabbis: permit watering even a dry field
  • Ravina derives: sprinkling a garden (tarbitza) is also permitted on Chol HaMoed

Grain Field (Sprinkling)

  • Sprinkling permitted during Shemita (Sabbatical Year)
  • Baraita A: forbidden on Chol HaMoed
  • Baraita B: permitted on Chol HaMoed
  • Rav Huna resolves: depends on whether crop ripens before Yom Tov ends
