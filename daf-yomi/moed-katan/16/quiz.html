Moed Katan 16 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
With how many shofarot did Barak ostracize Meroz for failing to join the battle?
A. 40 shofarot
B. 200 shofarot
C. 400 shofarot
D. 1,000 shofarot
Correct: C — Ulla said: Barak ostracized Meroz with the blowing of four hundred shofarot due to his failure to come to battle after being summoned.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda, how many warnings must a court issue before ostracizing someone who ignores a monetary judgment?
A. One warning on Monday
B. Two warnings on Monday and Thursday
C. Three warnings: Monday, Thursday, and the following Monday
D. Seven warnings over three weeks
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda said: Before excommunicating a person, the court warns him three times — on Monday, Thursday, and the following Monday. This applies specifically to monetary cases.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How long does admonition last in Babylonia, according to the Gemara's conclusion?
A. 7 days
B. 30 days
C. 3 days
D. 1 day
Correct: D — The Gemara derives from the incident of Shmuel and Mar Ukva that admonition in Babylonia lasts only one day, since the Babylonian authorities lack the ordained status of Eretz Yisrael.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the verse in Psalms 7:1 call Saul a 'Cushite' when his name is Saul?
A. Saul was born in the land of Cush
B. Saul had dark skin like a Cushite
C. Just as a Cushite is distinguished by his skin, Saul was uniquely distinguished by his deeds
D. Saul ruled over the Cushite nation
Correct: C — The Gemara explains: just as a Cushite is distinguished by his dark skin, so too Saul was distinguished among all people by his exceptional deeds. 'Cushite' is a title of distinction, not a proper name.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the Gemara's interpretation of the epithet 'Taḥkemoni' applied to David's warrior Josheb-Basshebeth?
A. David was the wisest [ḥakham] of all the kings
B. God told David: since you humbled yourself, be like Me [tehe kamoni] — your decrees can annul Mine
C. David commanded three thousand [sheloshet alafim] soldiers
D. David sat at the head of the great Sanhedrin
Correct: B — Rav interpreted taḥkemoni as 'tehe kamoni' — God said to David: since you humbled yourself and sat on the ground, I will make you like Me; just as I issue decrees, your righteous decrees will be able to annul even My own decrees.
