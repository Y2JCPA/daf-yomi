'Curse Meroz' (Judges 5:23) — ostracized for refusing to come to battle after being summoned

🌍 Ostracism & Admonition: Eretz Yisrael vs. Babylonia

Eretz Yisrael

  • Ostracism minimum: 30 days
  • Admonition: 30 days
  • Nasi's admonition: even longer
  • Ordained Rabbis carry greater legal weight
  • Their admonition = our ostracism in force
  • Evidence: Rabbi Ḥiyya served 30 days under Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi

Babylonia

  • Ostracism minimum: 30 days
  • Admonition: 1 day only
  • Evidence: Shmuel & Mar Ukva — one day sufficed
  • Rav Ḥisda: our ostracism = their admonition
  • Less ordained authority than Eretz Yisrael
  • Babylonian courts operate under reduced jurisdiction

🔓 Can Three Others Release an Ostracism Decree?

Abaye
Three of us ostracized the butcher who insulted Rav Tuvi bar Mattana. Now that he has made peace, can we simply release him — or must the same three who decreed it be the ones to lift it?
Rav Idi bar Avin
Shmuel taught: A shofar blast at the time of ostracism makes it binding, and a shofar blast at the time of release removes it. The same three must release.
Ameimar
The halakha is: if three people ostracized someone, three others may indeed come and nullify the decree — they need not be the original three.
Rav Ashi
But a baraita contradicts this! Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel says: if a student ostracized for his own dignity, the Nasi may release him — implying only one with greater authority can release.
Baraita Resolution One ostracized by his city is ostracized elsewhere. One ostracized by another city is NOT ostracized in his own city. One ostracized by the Nasi — all must treat him as ostracized.

📚 The Decree: Torah Must Stay in the Study Hall

מַה יָּרֵךְ בְּסֵתֶר — אַף דִּבְרֵי תוֹרָה בְּסֵתֶר (שִׁיר הַשִּׁירִים ז:ב)
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's Ruling Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi decreed: Torah may not be taught in the marketplace — only in the study hall. Source: 'Your rounded thighs are like jewels, the work of an artist's hands' (Song of Songs 7:2). Just as thighs are kept hidden, so Torah — 'the work of the hands of an Artist' (God) — must remain hidden in the study hall. Violation: Rabbi Ḥiyya taught his nephews Rav and Rabba bar bar Ḥana in the marketplace. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi placed him under admonition for 30 days. Rava's resolution: One who occupies himself with Torah study privately — his Torah will proclaim his praise publicly (Proverbs 1:20). The verse 'I have not spoken in secret' (Isaiah 48:16) refers only to the days of the kalla gatherings — not to ordinary teaching.
