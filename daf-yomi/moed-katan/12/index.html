🏗️ Gentile Contractors: Three Amoraim Tighten the Rule

Shmuel
Within the Shabbat limit — gentile contractors may NOT work for a Jew on Shabbat or Yom Tov. Outside the limit — it is permitted.
Rav Pappa
Even outside the limit: if another city is nearby, it is prohibited — passersby will assume the Jew ordered the work, causing a chillul Hashem.
Rav Mesharshiyya
Even with no nearby city: only on Shabbatot and Yom Tov is it permitted — Jews don't normally go to remote sites then. On Chol HaMoed weekdays, when Jews do travel there, it is prohibited.
Gemara (re: Mar Zutra)
Rav Safra and Rav Huna bar Ḥinnana refused to enter a mansion built for Mar Zutra outside the limit — an important person's actions draw public attention, requiring extra stringency.
Key Pattern Each amora adds a layer: Shmuel (location) → Rav Pappa (proximity) → Rav Mesharshiyya (day type) → Gemara (prominence of person)

🍺 Sealing Containers on Chol HaMoed — Three Views on the Nature of the Law

Shmuel
One may seal a jug (small vessel) with pitch on Chol HaMoed, but NOT a barrel — sealing a large barrel is too labor-intensive and labor-like.
Rav Dimi of Neharde'a
One may seal a barrel with pitch, but NOT a kupa (jar) — sealing a jar is a more final, significant act of completion and therefore prohibited.
Rav Ḥama bar Gurya (Rav)
The halakhot of Chol HaMoed resemble the halakhot of Samaritans — not fully defined. Each case must be decided individually. Rav Dami bar Levai: they are like neither Shabbat nor a weekday.
Abaye
We have a tradition: Chol HaMoed halakhot ARE like Shabbat — some acts exempt by Torah are still Rabbinically prohibited; some are permitted ab initio.
Three Frameworks Shmuel & Rav Dimi: case-by-case; Rav Dami: entirely sui generis; Abaye: structured like Shabbat with two tiers (Rabbinic prohibition vs. full permission)

🛖 Bringing Produce Home & Rescuing Flax — Rules and Cases

Mishna: One may bring fruit in from the field on Chol HaMoed if concerned about thieves — protecting property from loss is permitted.
↓
Baraita condition: Must bring it in discreetly — not openly and publicly. The appearance of performing full-scale labor is prohibited.
↓
Rav Yosef's case: He moved large wooden beams during the day. Abaye objected — isn't daytime public? Rav Yosef replied: for heavy beams, daytime is discreet — fewer people help carry them by night, making nighttime more conspicuous.
↓
Mishna: One may draw flax out of a soaking pool on Chol HaMoed to prevent it from being ruined — provided he did not plan from the outset to leave this work for the Festival.
↓
Intent is decisive: Emergency rescue of property already in jeopardy = permitted. Deliberate postponement of work to Chol HaMoed = prohibited. The Festival is not a time to catch up on planned labor.
