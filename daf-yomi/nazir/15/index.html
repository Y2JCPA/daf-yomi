↓

Refutation of R' Yosei b. R' Chanina

וטמא ראש נזרו
Baraita A nazirite whose term is completed (but hasn't shaved) is forbidden to shave, drink wine, and contract impurity.

If he violates any of these — he receives forty lashes (malkot).

This conclusively refutes R' Yosei b. R' Chanina, who held he is only liable for impurity (based on "v'timei rosh nizro" — Numbers 6:9).

Does the 70th Day Count for Both Terms?

Rav
The 70th day counts as both the last day of the first 70 days and the first day of the son's naziriteship (miktzat hayom k'kulo).
Gemara
But the Mishna says 'up to 70 he has not lost anything.' If the 70th day counts double, he actually gains a day!
Rav (defense)
The Mishna uses 'not lost' as a contrast to the next clause where he does lose — it's stylistic, not precise.
Gemara
But the next clause says 'after 70 it negates.' If one day counts double, then day 71 should still leave 30 days for hair growth!
Rav (defense)
'After' means after-after — day 72, when even with double-counting there aren't 30 days left.
Gemara
Then why didn't the Mishna say 'up to 71'? Conclusion: The Tanna of the Mishna disagrees with Rav.
Outcome The Gemara concludes the Mishna's anonymous Tanna does not accept Rav's principle that one day counts for two terms.

Seeking Support for Rav's Principle

Abba Shaul — Mourning

  • Buried dead 8 days before a Festival
  • The 7th day of mourning counts as both end of shiv'ah and start of shloshim
  • Festival then voids the 30-day period
  • May cut hair even after the Festival
  • ⚠️ Rejected: Maybe only applies to rabbinic mourning, not Torah-level naziriteship

Rabbi Yosei — Shomeret Yom

  • Woman observing a clean day had Paschal offering slaughtered for her
  • Later saw blood — retroactively impure
  • Cannot eat the offering, but exempt from second Pesach
  • Possibly because part of the day = whole day
  • ⚠️ Rejected: Maybe she's pure until discharge, not retroactive at all
Pattern The Gemara attempts two sources for Rav — both are rejected as having alternative explanations.

Rabbi Yosei's Shomeret Yom Case

Woman has one day of non-menstrual discharge (zavah ketanah)
↓
On day 2 (her clean day), she immerses in a mikveh
↓
They slaughter & sprinkle the Paschal offering for her
↓
Later that day she sees blood again
Eating the Offering
❌ May not eat the Paschal lamb — she is tamei
Second Pesach
✅ Exempt — the offering was validly brought when she was pure
Dispute Is she exempt because part of the day = whole day (supporting Rav)? Or because impurity starts only from the moment of discharge onward?
