QUESTION 1 OF 5
What does the verse "he defiles his consecrated head" (Numbers 6:9) come to teach?
A. Only a pure nazirite who became impure must shave and bring birds
B. All nazirites who become impure must shave regardless of when they vowed
C. A nazirite who vowed in a cemetery must shave twice
D. Shaving applies only to the leper, not the nazirite
Correct: A — The verse limits the obligation of impurity-shaving and bird offerings to a nazirite who was pure and subsequently became impure, exempting one who vowed while already in a cemetery.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Yehuda HaNasi, when does naziriteship of purity begin after purification from impurity?
A. On the seventh day of purification
B. On the eighth day of purification
C. Immediately upon leaving the cemetery
D. Only after bringing all offerings including the guilt-offering
Correct: B — R' Yehuda HaNasi interprets 'he shall sanctify his head on that day' (Numbers 6:11) as referring to the eighth day — the day designated for bringing offerings.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rav Ḥisda attribute the Karetot mishna (one offering for multiple impurities) to R' Yosei son of R' Yehuda?
A. Because R' Yosei holds purity begins on Day 7, allowing impurity on Day 7 to be a distinct event while offerings aren't yet due
B. Because R' Yosei holds that offerings are never required for multiple impurities
C. Because R' Yehuda HaNasi doesn't believe in multiple impurities at all
D. Because R' Yosei holds that bird offerings are optional
Correct: A — Since R' Yosei holds purity begins on Day 7, becoming impure again on Day 7 constitutes a separate event — but since offerings aren't brought until Day 8, only one set is needed. Under R' Yehuda HaNasi, Day 7 impurity is still the original period.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the practical difference between the Rabbis and R' Yishmael son of R' Yoḥanan ben Beroka regarding an impure nazirite?
A. The Rabbis require shaving; R' Yishmael does not
B. The Rabbis say purity begins after the sin-offering; R' Yishmael requires both sin- and guilt-offerings
C. The Rabbis say one set of offerings always suffices; R' Yishmael requires multiple sets
D. The Rabbis follow R' Yehuda HaNasi; R' Yishmael follows R' Yosei
Correct: B — According to the Rabbis, once the sin-offering is brought the nazirite begins counting purity — even without the guilt-offering. R' Yishmael holds both the sin-offering and guilt-offering must be brought before purity naziriteship can begin.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Rav Adda bar Ahava say nothing can be inferred about whether Rav Ḥisda considers night 'premature'?
A. Because the Gemara never discusses the concept of night
B. Because whether night is premature or not, R' Yehuda HaNasi's view still cannot explain the Karetot mishna
C. Because Rav Ḥisda explicitly stated his view on night elsewhere
D. Because the concept of premature only applies to daytime offerings
Correct: B — Rav Adda shows that offerings and naziriteship are interdependent: if night is premature, naziriteship doesn't start until morning; if not, impurity at night triggers a new obligation. Either way, you can't create the Karetot scenario under R' Yehuda HaNasi.
