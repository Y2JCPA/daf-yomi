Nazir Daf 47: Impurity, Offerings & Met Mitzva

A nazirite's offering blood was sprinkled on the altar
↓
The nazirite becomes tamei (impure) before bringing remaining offerings
R' Eliezer
Must bring all offerings again — impurity negates them all
Rabbis
Bring only the remaining offerings and be purified
↓
Separate question: High Priest & nazirite find a met mitzva — who becomes impure?
R' Eliezer
High Priest becomes impure (he doesn't bring an offering for tum'ah)
Rabbis
Nazirite becomes impure (his kedusha is temporary, not permanent)
Key Principle Two major disputes on this daf: whether impurity negates sprinkled offerings, and who takes priority when a met mitzva must be buried.

Impurity After Blood Was Sprinkled

Mishna
A nazirite's offering blood was sprinkled, then he became tamei before bringing remaining offerings. What happens?
R' Eliezer
His impurity negates the entire tally — he remains a nazirite and must start over.
Rabbis
Let him bring the rest of his offerings and be purified. The sprinkled blood still counts.
Rav
Clarification: R' Eliezer means impurity negates all his offerings — he must re-bring them. Not that he re-counts his naziriteship.
Gemara
This is logical — the Rabbis say 'bring the rest of her offerings,' showing the dispute is only about offerings, not the nazirite term itself.
Resolution Both sides agree the nazirite term is complete. They disagree only on whether the already-sprinkled offering must be repeated.

The Case of Miriam of Tarmod

מעשה במרים התרמודית שנזרקה עליה אחת מן הדמים ובאו והודיעוה על בתה שהיא מסוכנת
Historical Precedent Miriam of Tarmod (מרים התרמודית) was a nazirite whose offering blood had been sprinkled. She was told her daughter was mortally ill. She went and found her daughter dead, contracting tum'at met.

The Rabbis ruled: Let her bring the rest of her offerings and be purified — supporting the Rabbis' position against R' Eliezer.

Met Mitzva: Who Becomes Impure?

R' Eliezer

  • The High Priest should become impure
  • Reason: He does NOT bring an offering for his impurity
  • The nazirite should stay pure — he must bring a korban for tum'ah
  • Prioritizes avoiding financial/ritual burden

Rabbis

  • The nazirite should become impure
  • Reason: His kedusha (sanctity) is TEMPORARY
  • The High Priest should stay pure — his kedusha is PERMANENT
  • Prioritizes preserving permanent sanctity
  • Even a common priest stays pure over a nazirite
Core Logic R' Eliezer focuses on the cost of impurity (offerings). The Rabbis focus on the permanence of sanctity.

War-Anointed Priest vs. Deputy: Resolving the Contradiction

Baraita 1: Deputy stays pure, war-anointed becomes impure
↓
Baraita 2: War-anointed priest takes precedence over deputy!
↓
Mar Zutra resolves: These address different contexts
Saving Life / Ransom
War-anointed takes precedence — the public depends on him in wartime
Ritual Impurity
Deputy stays pure — he must be ready to substitute for the High Priest in the Temple
R' Chanina b. Antigonus Why did the Sages institute a deputy? So that if a disqualification befalls the High Priest, the deputy enters and serves in his stead.
