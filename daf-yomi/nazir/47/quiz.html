5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav's clarification, what does R' Eliezer mean when he says impurity 'negates the entire tally'?
A. The nazirite must re-count his entire nazirite term
B. The nazirite must re-bring all his offerings, including the one already sprinkled
C. The nazirite loses his nazirite status permanently
D. Only seven days of the nazirite term are negated
Correct: B — Rav explains that R' Eliezer does not require re-counting the nazirite term, but rather that all offerings must be brought again, including the one whose blood was already sprinkled.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why do the Rabbis say the nazirite — not the High Priest — should become impure for a met mitzva?
A. Because the nazirite is less important than the High Priest
B. Because the nazirite's sanctity is temporary while the High Priest's is permanent
C. Because the nazirite does not bring an offering for impurity
D. Because the nazirite has already completed most of his term
Correct: B — The Rabbis prioritize preserving permanent sanctity. Since a nazirite's kedusha ends when his term is complete while a High Priest's kedusha is lifelong, the nazirite should become impure.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the dispute between a priest anointed for war and the deputy High Priest regarding met mitzva, who stays pure and why?
A. The war-anointed priest, because the public depends on him
B. The deputy High Priest, because he may need to substitute for the High Priest in the Temple
C. The war-anointed priest, because he was anointed with oil
D. Neither — they both become impure equally
Correct: B — Mar Zutra explains that for ritual impurity, the deputy stays pure because he must be available to serve in the Temple if the High Priest is disqualified. The war-anointed priest takes precedence only for saving life or ransom.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What happened with Miriam of Tarmod, and what did the Rabbis rule?
A. She completed her nazirite offerings and then became impure; the Rabbis said she must restart
B. Her offering blood was sprinkled, she became impure from her daughter's death; the Rabbis said bring the remaining offerings
C. She refused to bring offerings; the Rabbis exempted her
D. She became impure before any blood was sprinkled; the Rabbis said she must re-count
Correct: B — Miriam's offering blood had been sprinkled when she learned her daughter was ill. She found her daughter dead and became impure. The Rabbis ruled she need only bring the rest of her offerings.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara derive that a High Priest must become impure for a met mitzva from Leviticus 21:11?
A. The verse explicitly states the High Priest must bury unattended corpses
B. The phrase 'for his father' implies he does NOT become impure for his father, but DOES for a met mitzva
C. A gezeira shava links the High Priest's obligations to a nazirite's
D. The word 'any' in 'any dead bodies' includes met mitzva
Correct: B — The phrase 'for his father' is seemingly superfluous (since 'any dead bodies' already covers relatives). It teaches: for his father he may not become impure — implying that for a met mitzva (which has no one else to tend to it), he must.
