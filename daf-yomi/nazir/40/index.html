Nazir Daf 40 — The Razor and the Shave

Numbers 6:5: "A ta'ar (razor) shall not come upon his head"
↓
Two halakhot derived from one verse:
"Shall not come"
Prohibits removing hair by any means
"Razor"
Extra prohibition for using a razor specifically (two violations)
↓
After his term ends → shaving must be with a razor
Core Theme This daf establishes the razor requirement for three categories of ritual shaving and explores how the leper's obligation is derived through the prohibition against destroying the beard.

Why Must a Nazirite Shave with a Razor?

First Tanna
We cannot learn the nazirite's razor requirement from the leper — one does not derive a stringency in a lenient case from a more stringent one.
First Tanna
Therefore the verse must explicitly state that the nazirite's final shaving requires a razor.
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
The word 'razor' is not needed for that! The verse says: "A razor shall not come upon his head until the completion of the days…"
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
This implies: after completion, the shaving must be performed only with a razor.
Gemara
But if 'razor' means only a razor — the first part says 'a razor shall not come upon his head' and yet he is prohibited from all means of hair removal!
Gemara
Resolution: 'Shall not come' = prohibited by any means. 'Razor' = an additional violation for using a razor — two prohibitions for one act.
Key Insight The single verse Numbers 6:5 yields both the prohibition during naziriteship (any means) AND the requirement for the final shaving (razor only).

Three Who Must Shave — and Their Sources

Nazirite

  • Source: Numbers 6:5 — "A razor shall not come upon his head"
  • Implies: when he does shave, it must be with a razor
  • If two hairs remain uncut → shaving is invalid

Leper (Metzora)

  • Source: Leviticus 14:8–9 — "Shave off all his hair"
  • Razor requirement derived indirectly (debated)
  • Offering includes a poverty level — makes derivation harder

Levites

  • Source: Numbers 8:7 — "Let them cause a razor to pass over all their flesh"
  • Razor explicitly stated in the verse
  • Required waving (tenufah) — unique stringency
Mishna Rule All three: if they shaved without a razor or left two hairs uncut, they have done nothing — the mitzva is unfulfilled.

Where Does the Leper's Razor Requirement Come From?

Can we derive it from Levites?
Both require shaving → both need a razor?
Objection
Levites have a unique stringency: waving (tenufah)
Add Nazirite?
Nazirite also requires a razor — derive from both?
↓
Objection (Rava of Barnish): Both nazirite and Levite offerings are fixed (no poverty level), but a leper's offering has a poverty level — the cases are not comparable!
↓
Cannot derive leper's razor from analogy alone
Resolution The Rabbis derive it from the beard-destruction prohibition; R' Eliezer, who disagrees about that prohibition, must rely on the failed analogy.

Rabbis vs. R' Eliezer — Destroying the Beard

Rabbis
Destroying the beard is prohibited only with a razor — the intersection of 'shaving' and 'destruction' defines the forbidden tool.
R' Eliezer
Even tweezers or a plane (rehitni) violate the beard prohibition — it's not limited to a razor.
Rava
The baraita (that takes for granted the leper uses a razor) follows the Rabbis, who derive it from the beard prohibition.
Rava
The passage trying to derive it by analogy follows R' Eliezer, who cannot use the beard prohibition as a source.
Rava bar Mesharshiyya's Question The baraita first assumes the leper must use a razor, then tries to prove it by analogy and fails. Rava resolves this by attributing the two passages to different Tannaim.
