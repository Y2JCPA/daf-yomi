Nazir 40 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda, how much hair must a nazirite shave mid-term to negate days of his naziriteship?
A. One hair
B. Two hairs
C. Most of his head
D. All of his hair
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda distinguishes three thresholds: flogging for one hair, invalidation for leaving two hairs, but negation of naziriteship requires shaving most of the head in the manner of a razor.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why can't the leper's obligation to use a razor be derived from the Levites by analogy?
A. Levites don't actually require a razor
B. Levites have a unique stringency of waving (tenufah)
C. The leper's shaving is on different body parts
D. The Torah explicitly states the leper needs no razor
Correct: B — The Gemara objects that Levites have a unique stringency — they require waving (tenufah) — which makes their case non-comparable to the leper.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How do the Rabbis derive that a leper must shave with a razor?
A. From a kal va-ḥomer from the nazirite
B. From the Levites by analogy
C. From the prohibition against destroying the beard
D. From an explicit verse about lepers
Correct: C — The Rabbis connect the extra mention of 'his beard' in the leper's shaving to the prohibition against destroying the beard, which applies only to a razor (the intersection of 'shaving' and 'destruction').
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does R' Eliezer hold about the prohibition of destroying one's beard that affects this derivation?
A. He holds the prohibition doesn't exist
B. He holds even tweezers or a plane violate it
C. He holds it applies only to priests
D. He holds it applies only during naziriteship
Correct: B — R' Eliezer holds that even tweezers or a plane (rehitni) violate the beard prohibition, so the prohibition is not specific to a razor. Therefore he cannot use it to prove the leper must use a razor specifically.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
A nazirite who shaves during his term using a razor violates how many prohibitions?
A. One — removing his hair
B. Two — removing hair and using a razor specifically
C. Three — removing hair, using a razor, and negating his term
D. None — the prohibition is only on others shaving him
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that 'shall not come upon his head' teaches the general prohibition against removing hair, while 'razor' adds a second prohibition specifically for using a razor — totaling two prohibitions.
