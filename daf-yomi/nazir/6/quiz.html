Nazir 6 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Bar Padda, how long is an unspecified term of naziriteship?
A. 30 days
B. 29 days
C. 28 days
D. 31 days
Correct: B — Bar Padda holds that an unspecified naziriteship lasts 29 days, based on the mishna's teaching that one shaves for the first term on the 30th day (meaning day 29 was the last day of observance).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What principle does Rav Mattana use to explain how the 30th day can count for both consecutive terms of naziriteship?
A. Ein me'arvin simcha b'simcha
B. Miktzat hayom k'kulo (part of the day is like the whole day)
C. Kal va'chomer from Shabbat
D. Bereirah (retroactive clarification)
Correct: B — Rav Mattana applies miktzat hayom k'kulo — part of the day counts as a full day — so the beginning of day 30 completes the first term and the rest begins the second term.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Reish Lakish, why does Rabbi Eliezer say that impurity on day 100 of a 100-day naziriteship negates only 30 days?
A. Because miktzat hayom applies and the naziriteship is already complete
B. Because the Torah says to give the day-of-completion nazirite the law of an unspecified nazirite (30 days)
C. Because 30 days is the maximum a nazirite can ever negate
D. Because Rabbi Eliezer follows Bar Padda's opinion of 29 days rounded up
Correct: B — Reish Lakish derives from Numbers 6:13 that if one becomes impure on the completion day, the Torah prescribes the law of an unspecified nazirite — requiring him to recount 30 days, not the entire term.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the suggestion that 'days that require completion' refers to a week?
A. Because a week has no connection to naziriteship
B. Because a week is never lacking — it is always exactly 7 days
C. Because the Torah explicitly says 'month'
D. Because hair cannot grow in 7 days
Correct: B — A week is always 7 days and never needs 'completion.' A month, however, can be 29 or 30 days, so the 30th day genuinely 'completes' what was lacking.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rav Mattana reconcile the positions of Rabbi Yoshiya and Rabbi Yonatan?
A. He says they disagree on the length of naziriteship — 30 vs 29 days
B. He says they agree naziriteship is 30 days but disagree on whether 'until' (ad) is inclusive or exclusive
C. He says Rabbi Yonatan's opinion is rejected
D. He says they disagree on whether miktzat hayom applies
Correct: B — Rav Mattana argues both tannaim agree on 30 days. Their dispute is whether 'until' means 'up to but not including' (R' Yoshiya) or 'up to and including' (R' Yonatan), leading them to derive the halakha from different phrases.
