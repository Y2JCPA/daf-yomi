Nazir 37 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Rabbis, what principle does the term 'soaked' (משרה) in Numbers 6:3 teach?
A. Permitted food combines with forbidden food (heter mitzaref l'issur)
B. The legal status of the flavor of forbidden food is like that of its substance (ta'am k'ikkar)
C. A nazirite who merely smells wine is liable
D. Grape derivatives are forbidden only when consumed as food
Correct: B — The Rabbis derive from 'soaked' that if a nazirite soaked grapes in water and the water tastes like wine, he is liable — establishing ta'am k'ikkar. R' Akiva, by contrast, uses 'soaked' for heter mitzaref l'issur.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rav Dimi dismiss Abaye's objection from teruma?
A. Teruma no longer exists after the destruction of the Temple
B. Teruma in the present era applies only by rabbinic law, not Torah law
C. The laws of teruma were never meant to apply to mixtures
D. Teruma is not a true prohibition but merely a sanctity status
Correct: B — Rav Dimi explains that since the exile, teruma applies only miderabanan (by rabbinic law), which is why the lenient ruling about mixtures applies — it cannot serve as a Torah-level objection.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
From where does R' Akiva derive the principle of ta'am k'ikkar (flavor = substance)?
A. The term 'soaked' regarding a nazirite
B. The purging of Midianite vessels (Numbers 31:23)
C. The sin-offering verse 'anything that touches its flesh'
D. The prohibition of kilayim in a vineyard
Correct: B — R' Akiva derives ta'am k'ikkar from the vessels of gentiles — the Torah required purging the Midianite vessels of their absorbed non-kosher flavor, proving that flavor has the legal status of substance.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why do the Rabbis reject deriving ta'am k'ikkar from the vessels of gentiles?
A. The verse about Midianite vessels is not about food law
B. Vessels of gentiles is a novelty — even tainted flavor (noten ta'am lifgam) is forbidden there
C. The purging requirement was a one-time wartime command
D. Absorbed flavor in vessels is too minimal to be legally significant
Correct: B — The Rabbis hold that vessels of gentiles is a chiddush (novelty) because normally noten ta'am lifgam (flavor that taints) is permitted, yet here it is forbidden. You cannot derive general principles from a novelty.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does R' Yochanan limit heter mitzaref l'issur to only the case of a nazirite?
A. The verse 'soaked' explicitly limits it to nazirite law
B. Nazirite and sin-offering are 'two verses that come as one' (shnei ketuvim), which do not teach beyond their cases
C. Other prohibitions are more severe than naziriteship
D. Heter mitzaref is a rabbinic principle that only applies to vows
Correct: B — Both the nazirite ('soaked') and sin-offering ('anything that touches its flesh') teach that permitted combines with forbidden. Since two verses teaching the same principle cannot extend it to other cases (shnei ketuvim ha'ba'im k'echad ein melamdim), the principle stays limited.
