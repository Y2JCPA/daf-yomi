What Does 'Soaked' (משרה) Teach?

Verse: "Nor shall he drink anything soaked in grapes"
(Numbers 6:3)
↓
R' Yochanan (via R' Akiva)
Permitted food combines with forbidden food
(היתר מצטרף לאיסור)
But only for a nazirite
Rabbis (Baraita)
Flavor of forbidden food = its substance
(טעם כעיקר)
Derived to all Torah prohibitions
Core Dispute Abaye challenges Rav Dimi: perhaps 'soaked' teaches ta'am k'ikkar (flavor = substance) rather than heter mitzaref l'issur (permitted combines with forbidden)

Abaye Challenges Rav Dimi on Teruma

Abaye
If heter mitzaref l'issur — when non-sacred produce is the majority, the mixture should be permitted. That works.
But if olive-bulk in time of half-loaf — even with majority non-sacred, there's still an olive-bulk of teruma eaten in that time!
Rav Dimi
Set aside teruma in the present — it applies only by rabbinic law (מדרבנן) since the exile. That's the basis for the lenient ruling.
Abaye
Fine. But from where do you derive that 'soaked' teaches heter mitzaref? Perhaps it teaches ta'am k'ikkar — that the flavor of forbidden food has the legal status of the substance itself!
Key Shift After Rav Dimi resolved Abaye's earlier objections, Abaye pivoted: maybe the verse teaches an entirely different principle

The Baraita: Ta'am K'Ikkar from 'Soaked'

משרה — להביא שטעם כעיקר
טעם כעיקר The term 'soaked' (משרה) establishes: if a nazirite soaked grapes in water and the water tastes like wine, he is liable — the water has the legal status of wine.

From here we derive for all Torah prohibitions: the legal status of the flavor of forbidden food is like that of its substance.

Kilayim (כלאי הכרם)✅ Permanent✅ Benefit forbidden❌ Cannot be dissolved✅ Kal vachomer — all 3 stricter

Logic If the Torah applies ta'am k'ikkar to a nazirite — the most lenient case — how much more so for stricter prohibitions!

R' Akiva vs. Rabbis: Source for Ta'am K'Ikkar

Rabbis

  • Derive ta'am k'ikkar from 'soaked' (Numbers 6:3)
  • Nazirite grapes soaked in water → water tastes like wine → liable
  • Extended to all Torah prohibitions via kal vachomer
  • Vessels of gentiles (גיעולי גויים) is a novelty — can't derive from it

R' Akiva

  • Uses 'soaked' for heter mitzaref l'issur instead
  • Derives ta'am k'ikkar from vessels of gentiles (Numbers 31:23)
  • Midianite vessels absorbed non-kosher flavor → must be purged
  • Purging required only for pot used that day (not novelty)
Vessels of Gentiles "Every thing that passes through fire, you shall make it pass through fire" (Numbers 31:23) — even mere absorbed taste renders vessels forbidden

Why Vessels of Gentiles Is (or Isn't) a Novelty

Normally: Noten ta'am lifgam (flavor that taints food) is permitted
↓
Are vessels of gentiles an exception to this rule?
↓
Rabbis
Yes — even tainted flavor is forbidden in gentile vessels
→ This is a novelty (חידוש)
→ Cannot derive general principles from a novelty
Rav Huna b. Chiyya (for R' Akiva)
Torah only forbade a pot used that day
→ Flavor is still fresh, not tainted
→ Not a novelty → Can derive from it
Rabbis' Response Even a pot used that day slightly taints the absorbed food — so it remains a novelty, and we cannot derive ta'am k'ikkar from it
