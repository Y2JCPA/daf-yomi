Nazir 3: Decoding Ambiguous Vow Language

A person makes an ambiguous statement that may relate to naziriteship
↓
Does context clarify intent?
(holding hair, nazirite passing by)
Yes — Context Clarifies
Statement is interpreted as a nazirite vow
No — Remains Ambiguous
Statement may refer to Torah study, charity, or other meanings
Key Principle Shmuel repeatedly establishes that ambiguous language becomes a nazirite vow only when physical context (holding hair or a nazirite passing) clarifies intent.

Is the Nazirite a Sinner?

R' Elazar HaKappar
The nazirite is called a sinner (ḥoṭe) — he denied himself wine.
Gemara
This applies only to a ritually impure nazirite (nazir tamei) who must void his days and start over.
Gemara
Since he now faces a longer term than intended, he may come to transgress his vow.
Resolution
A ritually pure nazirite (nazir tahor) is not called a sinner — even according to R' Elazar HaKappar.
Source "The first days will be void, because his consecration was defiled" (Numbers 6:12)

The Gezera Shava: Pera (Long Locks)

"Leshale'aḥ" could mean sending away (removing hair)
↓
Verbal analogy links two verses using the word pera (long locks)
↓
Nazirite (Num. 6:5)
"He shall let his locks (pera) grow long"
= growing hair
Priest (Ezek. 44:20)
"They shall not let their locks (pera) grow long (lo yeshaleḥu)"
↓
Shilu'aḥ = letting hair grow, not removing it
Alternate Answer Rav Yosef: Even "sends water" (Job 5:10) means growth — watering produces growth.

"An Obligation of Birds" — R' Meir vs the Sages

R' Meir
One who says "an obligation of birds is upon me" is a nazirite.
Sages
He is not a nazirite — this is too indirect.
Reish Lakish
R' Meir's reason: birds are juxtaposed to hair in Daniel 4:30 — "hair grown like eagles' feathers."
R' Yochanan
No — everyone agrees one does not associate via juxtaposition. R' Meir is concerned he meant birds of an impure nazirite (Num. 6:10).
Key Question Does a person associate the object of his vow with something merely juxtaposed to it in a verse (סמוכים)?

Reish Lakish vs R' Yochanan: When Is It a Nazirite Vow?

Reish Lakish

  • A person DOES associate via juxtaposition (סמוכים)
  • Birds + hair are juxtaposed in Daniel 4:30
  • Even if NO nazirite is passing, if he says 'birds juxtaposed to hair' → nazirite vow
  • Context of a nazirite passing is NOT required

R' Yochanan

  • A person does NOT associate via juxtaposition
  • Concern: he meant birds of an IMPURE nazirite's offering
  • A pure nazirite MUST be passing before him
  • Without a nazirite passing → NOT a nazirite vow
Practical Difference If he explicitly said "birds juxtaposed to hair" but no nazirite was passing: Reish Lakish says nazirite; R' Yochanan says not.
