Nazir 28 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the baraita, why can't a son use his father's separated animal for his own sin-offering?
A. The verse says 'his offering' — he must use his own, not his father's
B. The father's animal is automatically disqualified upon the father's death
C. Animals cannot be transferred between people under any circumstances
D. The son must bring a more expensive offering than his father's
Correct: A — The verse 'his offering' (Lev. 4:32) teaches that one fulfills his obligation only with his own offering, not with an animal separated by his father.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rav Nachman hold that a blemished animal separated by one's father has the status of unallocated funds?
A. Because blemished animals are considered ownerless property
B. Because a blemished animal is only fit for its monetary value, which is essentially money
C. Because the Torah explicitly equates blemished animals with coins
D. Because blemished animals can be eaten by anyone
Correct: B — The Gemara explains: 'What is a blemished animal fit for? Its value — and this value is essentially money.' Therefore it has the same status as unallocated funds.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does R' Akiva say the husband cannot nullify after slaughter even before blood sprinkling?
A. Because the wife has already completed her nazirite term
B. Because slaughter is an irreversible act of dedication to God
C. Because nullification would cause loss of consecrated property (hefsed hekdesh)
D. Because the husband implicitly consented by allowing her to bring the animal
Correct: C — R' Akiva's concern is that if the husband nullifies after slaughter, the slaughtered animal (especially a sin-offering) would go to waste, causing a prohibited loss of consecrated Temple property.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why can't R' Zeira's solution (sprinkle blood 'not for its sake') work in R' Akiva's case?
A. Because all nazirite offerings must be sprinkled for their sake
B. Because the case involves a sin-offering slaughtered first, and a sin-offering sprinkled not for its sake is invalid
C. Because the wife has not yet completed her nazirite term
D. Because sprinkling not for its sake is forbidden on weekdays
Correct: B — The Sages explain that R' Akiva's case involves a sin-offering slaughtered first. Unlike a burnt-offering or peace-offering, a sin-offering whose blood is sprinkled not for its sake is completely invalid.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the source for the rule that only a father (not a mother) can declare his minor son a nazirite?
A. A verse in Bamidbar (Numbers) chapter 6
B. A rabbinic enactment from the Men of the Great Assembly
C. A halakha l'Moshe miSinai — oral law transmitted to Moses at Sinai
D. A kal va-chomer from the father's right to betroth his daughter
Correct: C — R' Yoḥanan states that this rule is a halakha l'Moshe miSinai — a tradition transmitted orally to Moses at Sinai, not derived from any verse.
