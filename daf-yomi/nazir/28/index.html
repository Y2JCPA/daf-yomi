Nazir 28: Offerings, Nullification & Father's Vow

Core Question: When can separated offerings be redirected or a vow nullified?
↓
Father→Son
Son cannot use father's allocated animal; can use unallocated money
Husband→Wife
Cannot nullify after blood sprinkled (or slaughter per R' Akiva)
Father→Minor Son
Father can impose naziriteship; son/relatives can protest to cancel
Key Verse "His offering… for his sin" (Lev. 4:28, 32, 35) — offerings must match both the person and the specific transgression.

Allocated vs. Unallocated: Animals & Money

Unblemished Animal

  • Status: ALLOCATED (מיוחדים)
  • Son CANNOT use father's animal
  • Cannot lose consecrated status
  • Cannot be redesignated for different sin
  • If misused — no me'ilah violation applies to the animal itself

Blemished Animal

  • Status: UNALLOCATED (like money)
  • Son CAN use for his own offering
  • Fit only for its monetary value
  • Rav Nachman: equivalent to unallocated funds
  • Gemara: 'What is it fit for? Its value — which is money'

Money (Coins)

  • Unallocated money: son CAN use
  • CAN lose consecrated status (me'ilah applies)
  • But CANNOT redesignate for different sin
  • Verse: "For his sin" — must match specific transgression
  • Allocated money: son CANNOT use
Rava's Challenge Rava questioned Rav Nachman: The baraita says a son can't use his father's animal — doesn't that include blemished ones? Gemara answers: only unblemished animals are 'allocated.'

When Can a Husband Nullify His Wife's Nazirite Vow?

Tanna Kamma
Once blood is sprinkled (זריקה) on her behalf, the husband cannot nullify her vow.
R' Akiva
Even before sprinkling — once any animal is slaughtered (שחיטה) he cannot nullify!
Gemara
Tanna Kamma's logic: after sprinkling she may drink wine immediately — she is no longer 'downcast,' so the vow doesn't affect the husband.
Gemara
R' Akiva's logic: nullifying after slaughter causes loss of consecrated property (הפסד הקדשים) — the offering would go to waste.
Key Distinction Tanna Kamma focuses on her status (is she still restricted?), while R' Akiva focuses on Temple property (would nullification waste a sacrifice?).

R' Zeira's Challenge: Why Not Sprinkle 'Not For Its Sake'?

R' Zeira asks: If husband nullifies after slaughter, why is the offering wasted?
↓
Sprinkle the blood not for the sake of nazirite offerings → meat can still be eaten!
↓
Proof: Shavuot peace-offering slaughtered at wrong time — blood sprinkled shelo lishmah and meat is eaten
↓
Sages respond: R' Akiva's case is where the sin-offering (חטאת) was slaughtered first
↓
A sin-offering sprinkled not for its sake is invalid — the animal IS wasted
Source The sin-offering can be brought first because a nazirite may change the order of offerings (Mishna 45a): shaving after any of the three sacrifices fulfills the obligation.

The Shaving Dispute: R' Meir vs. Tanna Kamma

Tanna Kamma
At shaving of impurity — husband can nullify. At shaving of purity — he cannot.
R' Meir
He can nullify even at the purity shaving, because he can say: "I don't want a shaven wife!" (אי אפשי באשה מגולחת)
Tanna Kamma
She can wear a wig (פאה נכרית) — so her shaven head doesn't affect him!
R' Meir
A wig is dirty/unpleasant (מאוסה) — he is not amenable to this solution!
Principle A husband can only nullify vows that cause his wife to be 'downcast' (עינוי נפש) or that affect the relationship between them (דברים שבינו לבינה).
