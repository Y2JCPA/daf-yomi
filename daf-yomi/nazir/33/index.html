Nazir 33: Correcting the Conditional Nazirite Vow

A person sees someone approaching and makes a conditional nazirite vow: 'If that is so-and-so, I shall be a nazirite'
↓
Abaye says: He also said: 'Even if it is not so-and-so, I shall be a nazirite'
↓
It IS that person
Nazirite — first statement fulfilled
It is NOT that person
Still a nazirite — last statement fulfilled
Key Insight Abaye reinterprets the mishna: 'whose statement was not fulfilled' means the first conditional statement wasn't fulfilled, but the person is still a nazirite because the unconditional fallback statement takes effect.

Abaye's Correction of the Mishna

Mishna
If a person said 'I will be a nazirite if that is so-and-so,' and it turns out not to be that person — 'whose statement was not fulfilled' — he is still a nazirite.
Gemara
How can his statement be 'not fulfilled' yet he is still a nazirite? The vow was conditional on identity!
Abaye
One must correct (לְתַקֵּן) his understanding: the person also said 'Even if it is not so-and-so, I shall be a nazirite.'
Abaye
'Whose statement was not fulfilled' means his first statement wasn't fulfilled — it wasn't that person. But he is a nazirite because his last statement was fulfilled.
Textual Method Abaye employs תַּקֵּן — emending or correcting one's reading of the mishna to resolve an internal contradiction.

The Compound Vow Structure

Person sees someone approaching from a distance
↓
Statement 1 (Conditional):
'If that is so-and-so, I shall be a nazirite'
↓
Statement 2 (Unconditional Fallback):
'Even if it is NOT so-and-so, I shall be a nazirite'
↓
Identity confirmed
Nazirite via Statement 1
Identity disproved
Nazirite via Statement 2
Person turns back (unknown)
Not a nazirite — see next slide
Note The compound vow ensures nazirite status regardless of identity — unless the person turns back and identity is never resolved.

Turning Back vs. Being Identified

Person Turns Back (חָזַר)

  • Identity remains unknown
  • No nazirite vow takes effect
  • None of them is a nazirite
  • Doubt prevents obligation

Person Is Identified (בָּא לְפָנֵינוּ)

  • Identity is confirmed or disproved
  • The conditional vow can be evaluated
  • The one who vowed IS a nazirite
  • The vow takes definitive effect
Inference The Gemara derives from the mishna's emphasis on 'turned back' that if the person were identified, the conditional vow would indeed take effect.

The Role of Tosafot on This Daf

אין כאן גמרא — כולו תוספות
Historical Note In the classic Vilna Shas layout, this amud (33a) is entirely filled with the commentary of Tosafot (תּוֹסָפוֹת), with no Gemara text appearing on the page. This reflects the Rishonim's extensive engagement with the conditional nazirite vow and its halakhic implications — a topic that generated significant discussion among the Baalei HaTosafot.
