Nazir 33 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye, why is the person in the mishna a nazirite even though 'his statement was not fulfilled'?
A. Because conditional vows are always binding
B. Because he also said 'even if it is not so-and-so, I shall be a nazirite'
C. Because the Sages imposed nazirite status as a penalty
D. Because uncertainty about identity always triggers nazirite status
Correct: B — Abaye explains that the person made a compound vow with an unconditional fallback. His 'first statement' (the conditional one) was not fulfilled, but his 'last statement' (the unconditional one) was.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What happens when the person walking toward them turns back without being identified?
A. All of them become nazirites as a stringency
B. Only the one who made the vow becomes a nazirite
C. None of them is a nazirite
D. They must observe nazirite status until the person is found
Correct: C — The mishna teaches that if the person turns back and identity remains unknown, not one of them is a nazirite, because the condition of the vow cannot be resolved.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What inference does the Gemara draw from the mishna's emphasis on 'he turned back'?
A. Turning back is itself a violation of nazirite law
B. If the person had been identified, the conditional vow would take effect
C. Turning back automatically cancels all vows
D. The mishna only applies to someone who physically turns around
Correct: B — The Gemara infers that the reason no one is a nazirite is specifically because the person turned back. If the person had come before them and been identified, the one who vowed conditionally would indeed be a nazirite.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Abaye mean by saying one must 'correct' (תַּקֵּן) his understanding of the mishna?
A. The text of the mishna contains a scribal error that must be emended
B. The mishna must be reread to include an additional statement by the vower
C. The mishna applies only to a corrected, proper nazirite vow
D. One must perform tikkun (repair) by observing nazirite practices
Correct: B — Abaye's 'correction' means adjusting one's reading of the mishna to understand that the vower also made a second, unconditional statement — 'even if it is not so-and-so' — which resolves the apparent contradiction.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is notable about the layout of Nazir 33a in the classic Vilna edition of the Talmud?
A. It contains the longest single Gemara passage in Seder Nashim
B. It is entirely filled with Tosafot commentary, with no Gemara text
C. It features a unique diagram illustrating nazirite vow conditions
D. It is left blank as a sign of mourning for the Temple
Correct: B — In the classic Vilna Shas layout, Nazir 33a has no Gemara text on the page — the entire amud is occupied by the commentary of Tosafot.
