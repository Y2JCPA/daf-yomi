QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does R' Tarfon hold that none of the six people in the mishna are nazirites?
A. Naziriteship requires hafla'a — explicit, clear pronouncement of the vow
B. Conditional vows are never valid for naziriteship
C. The koy is definitely a domesticated animal, so half the vows fail
D. Naziriteship cannot be triggered by an animal's classification
Correct: A — R' Tarfon derives from 'yafli' (Numbers 6:2) that naziriteship requires explicitness (hafla'a). Since the vowers don't know the koy's true nature at the time of vowing, the vows lack the required clarity.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rav Sheshet explain '9 sets of naziriteship in one person' when only 6 conditions relate to the koy directly?
A. The person accepts naziriteship nine times on nine different days
B. One person says 'I accept all their naziriteship upon me' — taking on all nine terms
C. Three of the conditions are counted double due to uncertainty
D. The koy actually has nine possible classifications
Correct: B — Rav Sheshet explains that after nine people make the respective vows, one individual says he accepts all their obligations, thereby undertaking nine separate terms of naziriteship.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the practical difference between R' Elazar and the Rabbis regarding nazirite vine prohibitions?
A. R' Elazar forbids wine; the Rabbis permit it
B. R' Elazar forbids grape leaves and tendrils; the Rabbis do not
C. R' Elazar permits grape seeds; the Rabbis forbid them
D. R' Elazar requires a larger quantity for liability than the Rabbis
Correct: B — R' Elazar uses amplification-restriction to include all vine products (excluding only branches), while the Rabbis use generalization-detail to limit the prohibition to fruit and fruit waste, excluding leaves and tendrils.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Yosei, what mnemonic helps remember which grape part is the 'zag'?
A. A zag looks like the letter zayin
B. Like a zog (bell) — the outer shell is the zog, so zag = the outer skin
C. Zag sounds like 'sak' (sack), referring to the seed's shell
D. The word zag appears first in the verse, so it refers to the first thing you see — the skin
Correct: B — R' Yosei's mnemonic: a zog (bell) has an outer shell and inner clapper. The outer part (skin) is the zag, and the inner part (seeds) is the inbal/ḥartzan. This is opposite to R' Yehuda's identification.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why do the Rabbis include unripe grapes in the nazirite prohibition despite using the restrictive generalization-detail method?
A. Unripe grapes are explicitly mentioned in the verse
B. If unripe grapes were excluded, the generalization would add nothing — grapes, raisins, wine, and vinegar are already stated explicitly
C. R' Akiva's ruling about bread soaked in wine necessitates including unripe grapes
D. The Rabbis follow R' Elazar specifically regarding unripe grapes
Correct: B — The Gemara reasons that wine, vinegar, grapes, and raisins are already explicit in the text. If the generalization excluded unripe grapes, it would teach nothing new — so unripe grapes must be what the generalization comes to include.
