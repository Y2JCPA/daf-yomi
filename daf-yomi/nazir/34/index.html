Nazir 34 — The Koy, Vine Products & Exegetical Methods

כי יפלא לנדר נדר נזיר — Numbers 6:2
CORE THEME This daf resolves uncertain naziriteship through the koy (ambiguous animal), then pivots to the three core prohibitions of a nazirite — exploring which vine products are forbidden through a clash of hermeneutical methods between R' Elazar and the Rabbis.
3 / 9

Uncertain Naziriteship: The Heap of Grain

R' Shimon
If someone vows naziriteship conditional on a heap containing 100 kor, and the heap is stolen — he is a nazirite. Since it might have contained 100 kor, we treat the uncertainty stringently.
R' Yehuda
He is permitted. We are lenient in cases of uncertain naziriteship (safek nezirut le-hakel). If the condition cannot be verified, naziriteship does not take effect.
R' Tarfon
None of them are nazirites at all! Naziriteship requires explicitness (hafla'a) — as the Torah says 'yafli' (Numbers 6:2). An uncertain vow lacks the required clarity.
KEY PRINCIPLE The Gemara concludes the mishna follows R' Yehuda — lenient on uncertain naziriteship, yet the koy case is stringent because every possibility triggers at least one vow.

The Koy: 6 Conditional Vows + 3 Meta-Vows = 9 Nazirites

A koy (uncertain animal) is seen — its classification is disputed
↓
Vows 1-2
"If it's a ḥayah" / "If it's not a ḥayah"
Vows 3-4
"If it's a behemah" / "If it's not a behemah"
Vows 5-6
"If it's both" / "If it's neither"
↓
Three more people vow based on the group: "if one of you is a nazirite" / "if none of you" / "if all of you"
↓
✅ All nine are nazirites — no matter the koy's nature, each vow's condition is satisfied
RAV SHESHET One person can accept nine terms of naziriteship by saying: 'I accept all their naziriteship upon me' — each term observed separately.

The Three Nazirite Prohibitions

☠️ Corpse Impurity

  • Cannot contract tumat met
  • Applies to all forms of corpse contact
  • Violation requires restart of count

✂️ Shaving

  • Cannot shave or cut hair
  • Hair grows for duration of term
  • Shaved at completion as offering

🍇 Vine Products

  • Wine, vinegar, grapes, raisins
  • Seeds (ḥartzannim) and skins (zaggim)
  • All combine to an olive-bulk for lashes
R' AKIVA Even bread soaked in wine — if together they total an olive-bulk, the nazirite is liable for lashes.

Leaves & Tendrils of the Vine: R' Elazar vs. Rabbis

R' Elazar
Even leaves and tendrils of the vine are forbidden to a nazirite. I derive this through amplifications and restrictions (ribbui u-mi'ut) — 'anything made of the grapevine' amplifies to include all vine products, restricting only branches.
Rabbis
We use generalizations and details (klal u-frat). The detail is 'wine and strong drink,' the generalization is 'anything of the vine,' and the re-detail is 'pits to skins.' Only items similar to fruit or fruit waste are forbidden — not leaves or tendrils.
HERMENEUTICAL CLASH Both sides agree on the same verses (Numbers 6:3-4) — the dispute is purely about which exegetical method to apply to them.
