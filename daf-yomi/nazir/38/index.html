Combining Permitted & Forbidden Substances

R' Yochanan
A permitted substance combines with a forbidden one for a nazirite — even foods (e.g., bread + grapes to form an olive-bulk)
R' Elazar
Combination applies only to liquids — a quarter-log of permitted liquid combines with wine. But solids do not combine.
Source
Both derive from the word "mishrat" (soaked) — Numbers 6:3 — that soaking/mixing triggers liability
R' Shimon
"Anything made of the grapevine" teaches that one is not a nazirite unless he vows from all the prohibitions — not about combination at all
Practical Difference If a nazirite eats bread soaked in wine: R' Yochanan holds liable if combined = olive-bulk; R' Elazar requires a full quarter-log of liquid

Measuring Nazirite Liability: Food vs. Drink

First Tanna

  • Food liability = olive-bulk (ke-zayit)
  • Drink liability = quarter-log
  • Standard Torah measures apply
  • Does NOT equate eating and drinking measures

Rabbi Akiva

  • ALL nazirite prohibitions = olive-bulk
  • Derives from: "nor eat fresh grapes or dried"
  • Just as eating = olive-bulk, so too drinking
  • Drinking wine measured by olive-bulk, not quarter-log
R' Akiva's Logic The verse links all grape prohibitions to "eating" — so the olive-bulk measure governs everything, even liquids

Multiple Lashes for Grape Products

Abaye
Ate a grape seed → 2 sets of lashes: (1) specific prohibition on seeds, (2) general "anything from the grapevine"
Abaye
Ate a seed + skin → 3 sets: seed, skin, and the general prohibition
Rava
Only 1 set for a seed or skin. One is never flogged for a general prohibition (lav she'bichlalut).
R' Eliezer
Ate grapes, raisins, seeds, skins, and drank juice → 5 sets of lashes (not 6!)
Rav Pappa
Challenges Abaye: if the general prohibition adds lashes, R' Eliezer should say 6, not 5!
Resolution Rav Pappa concludes R' Eliezer stated "he is flogged" without specifying a precise number — so no proof against Abaye
