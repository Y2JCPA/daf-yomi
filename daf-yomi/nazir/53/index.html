Bone Quantities & Nazirite Shaving Obligations

Core Question: What quantity of bones triggers nazirite shaving?
↓
Half-Kav
All agree: imparts tum'ah in a tent (ohel)
Nazirite must shave
Quarter-Kav
Disputed — Early Elders disagree
Later court: only invalidates terumah & kodashim, not nazirite shaving
One Bone (Shammai)
Shammai: one bone from spine/skull imparts tum'ah
Rabbis disagree
Key Principle The later court's ruling — distinguishing nazirite obligations from terumah invalidation — came from prophetic tradition (Haggai, Zechariah, Malachi), not as a compromise.

Rava's Change of Mind on Spine Bones

Rava (initially)
A whole spine and skull that lack a quarter-kav of bones still impart tum'ah — implying a quarter-kav from a spine does impart impurity
Gemara
But then Rava heard the tanna's teaching and understood R' Akiva's dispute concerns spine/skull from two corpses
Rava (revised)
Changed his position based on this new understanding of the baraita (52a)
Note Rava's intellectual honesty — he revised his view upon hearing a more precise reading of R' Akiva's dispute.

Shammai vs. The Rabbis on Bone Impurity

Shammai

  • Even ONE bone from spine or skull imparts tum'ah
  • Extremely stringent position
  • Cannot infer Rabbis' view from his

Rabbis (possible views)

  • Option 1: Quarter-kav from spine imparts tum'ah
  • Option 2: Only half-kav from spine imparts tum'ah
  • Gemara: Perhaps they only disagree about ONE bone, but concede on quarter-kav
Conclusion Since Shammai is known for extreme stringency, neither direction of inference from his view is reliable.

The 'Third Opinion' Principle

אין הכרעת שלישית הכרעה
Legal Principle A compromise ruling (da'at shelishit) that introduces factors not found in either original opinion is not considered authoritative. The Gemara challenges: How could the later court distinguish between Pesach and other cases? Answer: Their ruling was not a compromise at all — it was an independent tradition from the last prophets.

Mishna's Exclusions: 'For These' — What Does It Exclude?

Mishna says 'for these' a nazirite shaves
↓
First Clause Excludes
Barley-grain-bulk bone:
Touching/carrying → shaves ✓
Overlying (ohel) → does NOT shave ✗
Latter Clause Excludes
Overhanging stone (sekhukhit):
Though it imparts ohel tum'ah, nazirite does NOT shave for it
Note Half-kav of 'flour-like' ground bones — no single barley-grain-bulk piece — still imparts ohel tum'ah and requires nazirite shaving.
