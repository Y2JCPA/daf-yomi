Nazir 53 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Rava change his view about a quarter-kav of bones from a spine?
A. He heard the tanna's teaching clarifying R' Akiva's dispute was about spine/skull from two corpses
B. He was overruled by a vote in the academy
C. He found a contradictory baraita about spine bones
D. Abaye convinced him through logical argument
Correct: A — After hearing the tanna, Rava understood that R' Akiva's dispute in the baraita (52a) concerned a spine and skull from two corpses, not a quarter-kav from a single spine.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why can't we infer the Rabbis' opinion from Shammai's ruling that one bone from the spine imparts tum'ah?
A. Shammai's opinion was retracted
B. Shammai is known for extreme stringency, so no inference is reliable
C. The Rabbis explicitly agreed with Shammai
D. Shammai was discussing a different type of bone
Correct: B — Since Shammai is exceptionally stringent, neither a close inference (quarter-kav) nor a distant one (half-kav) can reliably be drawn about the Rabbis' position.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was the source of the later court's ruling that a quarter-kav only invalidates terumah and kodashim?
A. A compromise between the two groups of Early Elders
B. A new Sanhedrin vote
C. Prophetic tradition from Haggai, Zechariah, and Malachi
D. A ruling of R' Akiva
Correct: C — R' Yaakov bar Idi explained that this was not a compromise (which would violate the 'third opinion' principle) but an independent tradition from the last prophets.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the dispute about a severed limb without sufficient flesh, what is Reish Lakish's key argument?
A. The mishna explicitly says a nazirite shaves for it
B. It can be derived from a kal va'chomer
C. Since the next mishna does NOT list it among items for which one does NOT shave, it must require shaving
D. A baraita from R' Akiva requires it
Correct: C — Reish Lakish argues from omission: since the latter mishna (54a) doesn't include 'limb without flesh' in its list of items for which a nazirite does NOT shave, it must require shaving.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Gemara's reading of Numbers 19:16, what does 'one who is slain' (חלל) refer to?
A. A corpse killed in battle
B. A limb severed from a living person with enough flesh to heal
C. A quarter-kav of bones
D. A sealed grave
Correct: B — The Gemara expounds 'one who is slain' (chalal) as referring to a limb 'slain' (severed) from a living person that has sufficient flesh that it could heal — a source of ohel impurity.
