Core Question: What Does 'His Head' Teach?

Torah says a leper must shave:
"He shall shave all his hair; his head" (Lev. 14:9)
↓
Tanna 1
Overrides pe'ot
Rounding the entire head is called rounding — verse permits leper to shave despite prohibition
Tanna 2
Overrides nazir
Rounding the entire head is not called rounding — verse is needed for a nazir leper
KEY DISPUTE Is shaving the entire head considered 'rounding' (הקפת כל הראש)?

What Does 'His Head' Come to Teach?

Tanna 1
"His head" overrides the prohibition of rounding pe'ot — because rounding the entire head is called rounding (הקפת כל הראש שמה הקפה)
Tanna 2
"His head" overrides the nazir prohibitions — both 'no razor' and 'let the locks grow' — because rounding the whole head is not called rounding
Rava
Perhaps everyone agrees it's not rounding. The verse addresses someone who rounded only the corners first and then shaved the rest — he's exempt since it could all be done at once
Gemara
But Reish Lakish taught: if you can fulfill both the positive and negative mitzva, do so! A leper shouldn't violate a prohibition unnecessarily
Gemara (conclusion)
Rather: everyone agrees rounding the entire head is called rounding (הקפת כל הראש שמה הקפה)
REVERSAL Rava's attempt to reconcile both tannaim is rejected — the Gemara concludes rounding the entire head IS called rounding

Two Sources for Aseh Docheh Lo Ta'aseh

From Leper (מצורע)

  • "His head" — positive mitzva to shave overrides prohibition of rounding pe'ot
  • Teaches: aseh docheh lo ta'aseh
  • Derives principle from a case involving the whole body

From Tzitzit (ציצית)

  • "You shall make fringes" next to "do not wear sha'atnez"
  • Teaches: positive mitzva of tzitzit overrides prohibition of kilayim
  • But Rava says this verse teaches something ELSE — wool/linen fringes exempt all garments
DISPUTE Each tanna uses a different verse as the primary source for the principle that a positive mitzva overrides a prohibition

Rava's Teaching on Tzitzit Materials

Torah says fringes must match the corner material
AND be of wool and linen
↓
Wool/Linen Fringes
Exempt garments of any material — their own type or not
Other Material Fringes
Exempt only garments of their own type
RAVA According to this reading, the tzitzit/sha'atnez juxtaposition teaches about which fringes exempt which garments — not the override principle

Defining Prohibited Shaving

איזהו גילוח שיש בו השחתה? הוי אומר זה תער
HALACHIC DEFINITION Which shaving involves 'destruction' (השחתה)?

• "Neither shall they shave" (Lev. 21:5) — indicates shaving
• "You shall not destroy" (Lev. 19:27) — indicates destruction from roots
• Together: only a razor (תער) combines both shaving AND destruction

✦ Scissors alone — not destruction ✦ Tweezers/plane — not shaving
✦ Only a razor = גילוח שיש בו השחתה
