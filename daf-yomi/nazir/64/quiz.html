Nazir 64 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yitzḥak bar Avudimi, how does the first tanna derive that uncertain floating impurity leaves one pure?
A. From contradictory verses about creeping animals — 'any place it swarms' vs. 'upon the earth'
B. From the verse about springs and cisterns being pure
C. From a gezeirah shavah linking corpse impurity to creeping animal impurity
D. From the principle that all doubts in Torah law are resolved leniently
Correct: A — R' Yitzḥak bar Avudimi reconciles two verses: one implies impurity everywhere, the other limits it to 'upon the earth.' The resolution: definite contact = impure, uncertain/floating contact = pure.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In Rami bar Chama's chain of dilemmas, why might a corpse atop a floating creeping animal be treated differently than a single floating item?
A. Because the corpse is heavier and would sink
B. Because they have different durations of impurity (7-day vs. 1-day), making them like separate items
C. Because a corpse cannot float by Torah law
D. Because creeping animals do not transmit impurity in water
Correct: B — The Gemara suggests that because corpse impurity lasts 7 days and creeping animal impurity lasts 1 day, they may be considered separate items — making the corpse 'in a vessel' rather than one floating unit.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rava concede that the person bringing the Paschal offering is pure when walking over a grave in the depths on day 7?
A. Because the Paschal offering overrides all impurity
B. Because he has no remaining bodily action to perform, giving him presumptive purity
C. Because grave in the depths never applies to the Paschal offering
D. Because the seventh day is considered equivalent to the eighth day
Correct: B — Unlike the nazirite who still needs to shave (an active step), the Paschal offering person has no remaining action on his body. Since sunset happens passively, he is considered to already have presumptive purity.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does the principle 'the sun sets by itself' (חמה ממילא קאתיא) establish?
A. That purification rituals must be completed before sunset
B. That the passage of time until sunset is not considered a deficiency preventing purity
C. That sunset creates a new day of impurity
D. That immersion is only valid after sunset
Correct: B — Rava establishes that needing to wait for sunset is not a 'deficiency' in one's purity status, because sunset happens automatically without any human action. This principle is later adopted by Abaye as well.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Tosefta, which of the following is NOT an exception to the rule that thrown impurity is treated as floating (and therefore pure in uncertain cases)?
A. An olive-bulk from a corpse
B. An item that overlies a corpse when thrown
C. A creeping animal carcass the size of a lentil-bulk
D. A zav or zavah who transmit impurity above and below
Correct: C — The Tosefta lists three exceptions: an olive-bulk of corpse, items overlying impurity, and zav/zavah. A standard creeping animal carcass follows the general rule — if thrown, it is treated as floating impurity and the person remains pure.
