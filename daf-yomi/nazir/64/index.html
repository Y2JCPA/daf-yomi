Nazir 64: Floating Impurity & Grave in the Depths

Core Question: When is uncertain contact with tumah considered 'floating' and therefore pure?
↓
First Tanna
All uncertain floating impurity → pure
(derived from creeping animal verses)
R' Shimon
Floating in ground water → pure
Floating in vessels → impure
↓
Rami bar Chama: Series of dilemmas about layered impurity — corpse in floating vessel, corpse on creeping animal, etc.
↓
Amud B: Rav Hamnuna — nazirite/Paschal offering person at grave in depths on day 7 → pure
↓
Principle: 'The sun sets by itself' — lacking sunset is not a deficiency that blocks purity
Key Concept Uncertain contact with impurity that is in motion ('floating') is treated leniently — the person remains pure — but the definition of 'floating' generates extensive debate.

Biblical Sources for Floating Impurity

R' Yitzḥak bar Avudimi
Two verses about creeping animals: 'any swarming thing that swarms' implies impurity everywhere...
R' Yitzḥak bar Avudimi
But 'that swarm upon the earth' limits it to on the ground. Resolution: definite contact = impure; uncertain floating contact = pure.
Ulla (for R' Shimon)
'A fountain or cistern... shall be pure' — creeping animal in ground water doesn't impart impurity.
Ulla
But 'he who touches their carcass shall be impure' — so floating in vessels of water = impure, in ground water = pure.
First Tanna vs. R' Shimon The first tanna derives a blanket rule that all uncertain floating impurity is pure. R' Shimon distinguishes between ground water (pure) and water in vessels (impure).

Rami bar Chama's Chain of Dilemmas

Dilemma 1: Corpse in a floating vessel — do we follow the vessel (floating → pure) or the corpse resting inside (at rest → impure)?
↓
Dilemma 2: Corpse atop a floating creeping animal — are they one unit (both float → pure) or separate (corpse 7-day vs. sheretz 1-day → like vessel → impure)?
↓
Dilemma 3: Creeping animal atop floating animal carcass — both 1-day impurity, but different shiurim (olive-bulk vs. lentil-bulk). One unit or two?
↓
Dilemma 4: Creeping animal atop another creeping animal — same impurity, same measure. Surely one unit?
↓
Dilemma 5: Creeping animal atop dissolved carcass — is dissolved meat like liquid (floating) or still food (solid surface)?
↓
Dilemma 6: Creeping animal atop semen — is ejaculated semen liquid or viscous like food?
↓
Dilemma 7: Creeping animal atop waters of purification (red heifer ashes) — unresolved
Unresolved Rami bar Chama builds each dilemma on a possible resolution of the prior one, creating a chain of uncertainty. The Gemara leaves these dilemmas without definitive answers (teiku).

Grave in the Depths: Nazirite & Paschal Offering

Rav Hamnuna
A nazirite or one bringing the Paschal offering who walked over an unknown grave (kever ha-tehom) on their 7th day of purification — they are pure.
Rava
Objection: The mishna says one whose status is presumptively impure remains impure! On day 7 before sunset, their status is still impure!
Rav Hamnuna
I concede regarding a nazirite who hasn't yet shaved — he lacks an action and retains impure status.
Rava
And I concede regarding the Paschal offering person — he lacks no action on his body, so he has presumptive purity on day 7.
Abaye
But he still lacks sunset! He's a tevul yom!
Rava
The sun sets by itself — it's not considered a deficiency requiring action.
Resolution Rav Hamnuna and Rava reach agreement: the nazirite (lacking shaving) follows presumptive impurity, but the Paschal offering person (lacking only sunset) is pure, because the sun sets by itself.

Nazirite vs. Paschal Offering Person at Kever HaTehom

Nazirite on Day 7

  • Still needs to shave for impurity
  • Shaving = active bodily action
  • Presumptive status remains impure
  • Grave in the depths → impure
  • Must restart count

Paschal Offering Person on Day 7

  • No bodily action remaining
  • Only lacks sunset
  • Sun sets by itself — not a deficiency
  • Presumptive status → pure
  • Grave in the depths → pure
  • Offering remains valid
Principle Tumaat kever ha-tehom (impurity from an unknown grave) is a leniency — it does not retroactively invalidate completed purification acts, but it does apply when the person's status is still presumptively impure.
