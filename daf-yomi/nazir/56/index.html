Nazirite Impurity: What Counts Toward the Tally?

Does a nazirite's time in a state of impurity or leprosy count toward his term?
↓
Rav Ḥisda
Days of confirmed leprosy do count toward naziriteship
Rav Ashi / Baraita
Days of confirmed leprosy do NOT count — proven from 4-year naziriteship
↓
Rav Ḥisda's ruling is rejected
↓
Final Halakha: Days of confirmed leprosy and counting days don't count; days of zav/zava impurity and quarantine days do count
Key Outcome The Gemara conclusively rejects Rav Ḥisda: a confirmed leper's days do not count toward naziriteship

Timeline for a 30-Day Uncertain Nazirite-Leper

📅 Day 30 — First shaving: for uncertain confirmed leprosy or uncertain pure naziriteship
↓
📅 Day 60 — Second shaving: completes leper purification if he was a leper; may now eat sacrificial food
↓
📅 Day 90 — Third shaving: in case he was impure, this shaving is for his impurity
↓
📅 Day 120 — Fourth shaving: naziriteship in purity complete; may drink wine and become impure from a corpse
Principle Due to multiple uncertainties (leper? impure? pure nazirite?), each possibility must be resolved sequentially

Which Days Count Toward Naziriteship?

DO NOT Count

  • Days of confirmed leprosy (requires shaving + offering)
  • Days of counting for leper purification (also requires shaving)
  • Days of corpse impurity (negate previous days)

DO Count

  • Days of zav/zava impurity
  • Days of leper's quarantine (no shaving or offering required)
  • Regular clean days of naziriteship
Distinguishing Factor States requiring shaving and an offering before resuming naziriteship do not count; those that don't require shaving do count

Impurity from a Corpse and Temple Entry

R' Eliezer (in name of R' Yehoshua)
Any corpse impurity for which a nazirite must shave — one is liable for entering the Temple with it
R' Eliezer
Any corpse impurity for which a nazirite does not shave — one is not liable for Temple entry
R' Meir
This lesser corpse impurity should not be more lenient than impurity from a creeping animal (sheretz)!
R' Meir
The Torah explicitly states that sheretz impurity prohibits Temple entry — so surely corpse impurity should too
Core Dispute Does nazirite shaving status determine Temple-entry liability, or is any corpse impurity sufficient?

The Chain of Tradition (Shalshelet HaKabbalah)

R' Yehoshua ben Ḥananya
Original source of the teaching
↓
R' Yehoshua bar Memel
Middle link — omitted in the Mishna
↓
R' Eliezer
Final transmitter — cited in the Mishna
↓
Principle: In a chain of three scholars, cite the first and last, omit the middle
Parallel Naḥum the Scribe similarly cited a chain from Prophets → Pairs → R' Meyasha → Father, following the same pattern
