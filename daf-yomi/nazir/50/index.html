Nazir 50: Impurity of Corpses, Fluids & Streams

When must a nazirite shave for corpse impurity?
↓
R' Yochanan
Miscarried fetus — limbs not joined by sinews
Rava
Majority of bone structure of a tiny corpse (< ¼ kav)
↓
What about liquefied flesh (mohal)?
↓
If it congeals → impure flesh
If it doesn't congeal → maybe phlegm/spittle → pure
Key Theme This daf explores the boundaries of corpse impurity — what counts as a 'corpse,' what counts as 'flesh,' and how physical transformation affects ritual status.

Shaving for Unusual Corpse Cases

R' Yosei
A nazirite must shave even for a corpse without an olive-bulk of flesh
Gemara
But if he shaves for a limb without an olive-bulk, surely a whole corpse is a kal va-chomer!
R' Yochanan
It refers to a miscarried fetus (נפל) whose limbs aren't yet joined by sinews — the whole body imparts impurity though individual limbs do not
Rava
It refers to the majority of bones of a very small corpse — less than a quarter-kav total, but still the majority of the body's structure
Context The mishna's ruling about corpse impurity needed explanation because it seemed redundant with the rule about limbs.

Animal Carcass Fluid — Does It Impart Impurity?

Impurity Until Inedible for Humans

  • Severe impurity (contact/carrying) requires fitness for human consumption
  • Liquefied meat is NOT fit for humans
  • Therefore: fluid from animal carcass is PURE
  • Dilemma resolved easily

Impurity Until Inedible for Dogs

  • Severe impurity lasts as long as a dog would eat it
  • Liquefied meat may still be edible for a dog
  • Therefore: the dilemma about fluid remains open
  • Tosefta about fat melted in sun brought as proof but refuted
Abaye's Dilemma Is the impurity of fluid (mohal) a special tradition limited to human corpses, or a general principle applying to animal carcasses too?

Stream Connection (ניצוק) — What Connects?

SubstanceConnects Upper & Lower Vessel?Reason
Regular liquidsNo — stream is purePoured liquid does not link vessels
Zifim honey (דבש זיפים)Yes — connectsHighly viscous; acts as soft solid food
Batter (צוק)Yes — connectsViscous mixture; returns backward when pouring stops
Bean stew (Beit Shammai)Yes — connectsFlow returns backward (חוזר לאחוריו)
Bean stew (Rabbis)NoNot viscous enough to return backward
Mishna Makhshirin 5:9 The general rule: poured streams don't connect. The exceptions are thick, viscous substances that 'return backward' when pouring stops.

Is There a 'Stream' for Melted Food?

Rami bar Chama
If one pours melted food into an impure vessel — does it become impure? Is there a 'stream' (ניצוק) for food?
Rava
From a Tosefta: whole fat of a corpse (olive-bulk) that was melted remains impure — proving food retains unity when melted!
R' Zeira
No proof — that case is where fire rose under the pan and fat congealed at the mouth of the vessel. It was all in one place, no stream at all!
Ravina
From the mishna: Beit Shammai say bean stew connects — implying the Rabbis accept viscous food streams like honey!
Rav Ashi
Not comparable — honey connects because of viscosity, bean stew because of backward flow. Different criteria entirely.
Unresolved The dilemma about whether melted food creates a connecting stream remains without definitive resolution.
