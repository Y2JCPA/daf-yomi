R' Shimon ben Gamliel's case is also special: male/female distinctions inherently allocate the animals.
Rav Hamnuna
Objection to Rav Naḥman: A baraita distinguishes animals from money — doesn't that include blemished animals?

No — the baraita means unblemished animals. A blemished animal is consecrated only for its sale value = money.

Allocated Animals vs. Unallocated Funds

Allocated (מפורשים)

  • Unblemished animals separated for specific offerings
  • Birds designated by owner or priest (per verse)
  • Animals with inherent male/female designation
  • Sin-offering animal of deceased → must die
  • Burnt-offering animal → sacrificed as burnt-offering

Unallocated Funds (סתומים)

  • Money separated without specifying which offering
  • Blemished animals (value = their sale price)
  • Silver bars or other non-specific items
  • If owner dies → go to communal gift offerings (נדבה)
  • Treated as currency, not as offerings themselves
Rav Naḥman's Position A blemished animal is consecrated only for its sale value — it functions like money, not like an allocated offering.
