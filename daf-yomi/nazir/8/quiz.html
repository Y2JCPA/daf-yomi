QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Tanna Kamma, what happens when someone says 'I am a nazirite like the hair of my head'?
A. He is a nazirite for one 30-day term
B. He is a nazirite forever, shaving every 30 days
C. He is a nazirite forever and never shaves
D. He must count the hairs on his head to determine the length
Correct: B — The Tanna Kamma holds that each hair represents a separate 30-day term of naziriteship. Since the hairs are effectively uncountable, he is a nazirite forever but shaves at the end of each 30-day term.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does R' Yochanan say that even R' Yehuda (who is lenient on uncertain naziriteship) agrees the basket vow is stringent?
A. Because R' Yehuda always defers to R' Shimon in cases of naziriteship
B. Because the basket case involves certain naziriteship with uncertain duration, unlike the pile-of-grain case where naziriteship may never begin
C. Because mustard seeds are the standard measure for baskets
D. Because R' Yehuda holds like Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi in all cases
Correct: B — R' Yochanan distinguishes: in the pile-of-grain case, it's uncertain whether naziriteship begins at all (so R' Yehuda is lenient). In the basket case, naziriteship certainly takes effect — the only question is duration — so even R' Yehuda agrees one cannot remove the established naziriteship.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the practical difference between a 'permanent nazirite' (nezir olam) and one who vows naziriteship for 1,000 years?
A. The permanent nazirite can drink wine; the 1,000-year nazirite cannot
B. The permanent nazirite trims his hair yearly; the 1,000-year nazirite may never cut his hair
C. The 1,000-year nazirite can bring offerings; the permanent nazirite cannot
D. There is no practical difference between them
Correct: B — A permanent nazirite (like Absalom) trims his hair on a yearly basis. But one who accepts a fixed number of years — even 1,000 — is a regular nazirite who can never cut his hair until the term ends, which effectively means never.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the apparent contradiction that R' Yehuda both agrees and disagrees with Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi?
A. R' Yehuda only agreed with Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi when teaching in the academy
B. R' Yehuda agrees that singular language means one long term, but disagrees about 'like the number of' — he considers that as multiple terms
C. The two R' Yehuda references are actually different sages
D. R' Yehuda changed his mind over time
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that R' Yehuda agrees with Rabbi in one area (singular 'I am a nazirite like X' = one long term) but disagrees in another ('like the NUMBER of X' = many terms, while Rabbi still requires the explicit plural 'naziriteships').
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If someone says 'I am a nazirite and one and more and again,' how many terms of naziriteship has he accepted?
A. 3 terms
B. 4 terms
C. 6 terms
D. 8 terms
Correct: B — The baraita teaches: 'nazirite' = 1, 'and one' = 2, 'and more' = 3, 'and again' = 4. The Gemara clarifies that 'and again' adds just one more term, not a doubling of all previous terms (which would have yielded 6).
