Nazir 31 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, what do Beit Hillel say about a case where one consecrated a black bull but a white bull emerged first?
A. It is consecrated
B. It is not consecrated
C. The black bull is consecrated instead
D. A new vow must be made
Correct: B — Beit Hillel hold that erroneous consecration (hekdesh b'ta'ut) does not take effect — since the white bull was not what the person intended to consecrate, it remains non-sacred.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
From what halakha do Beit Shammai derive that initial consecration takes effect even in error?
A. Nedarim (vows)
B. Temurah (substitution)
C. Ma'aser (tithes)
D. Terumah (priestly gifts)
Correct: B — Beit Shammai derive from temurah: just as substitution of a non-consecrated animal for a consecrated one takes effect even in error, so too initial consecration should take effect in error.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rav Pappa reinterpret Beit Shammai's ruling about the black bull?
A. The man intended to consecrate any bull that emerged
B. The man meant 'the first of my black bulls to emerge,' not the first bull overall
C. The man changed his mind after seeing the white bull
D. The consecration transfers automatically from black to white
Correct: B — Rav Pappa explains the man had multiple black bulls and meant to consecrate whichever black bull emerged first — regardless of whether white bulls preceded it. This avoids genuine erroneous consecration.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say the mishna's barrel-of-wine example was taught in the Galilee?
A. Because only Galilean courts ruled on such matters
B. Because wine was more valuable than oil there, fitting the principle of consecrating sparingly
C. Because the Galilee had special consecration laws
D. Because Beit Shammai's academy was in the Galilee
Correct: B — In the Galilee, olive trees were plentiful making oil cheaper than wine. So when wine was intended but oil came up, the oil was the lesser item — consistent with the principle that people consecrate sparingly.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What challenge does the nazirite-group mishna (about people vowing based on a passerby's identity) pose to Rav Pappa's interpretation?
A. It shows Beit Hillel actually agree with Beit Shammai
B. It proves that some contradictory vows must be erroneous, yet Beit Shammai still say all are nazirites
C. It demonstrates that nazirite vows cannot be dissolved
D. It proves that only monetary consecration can be erroneous
Correct: B — In the nazirite-group case, contradictory conditional vows mean some must be wrong — yet Beit Shammai say all are nazirites. This shows Beit Shammai do validate erroneous consecration in general, contradicting Rav Pappa's claim that even Beit Shammai reject it.
