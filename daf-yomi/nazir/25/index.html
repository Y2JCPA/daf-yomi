What Happens to a Deceased Nazirite's Surplus Funds?

Gemara
The nazirite's unallocated funds include money for a sin-offering. How can it all go to gift burnt-offerings?
R' Yochanan
It is an accepted halakha (tradition from Sinai) regarding a nazirite that unallocated funds go to gift offerings — even though sin-offering money is mixed in.
Reish Lakish
The verse states: 'Whether for any of their vows or gift offerings' (Lev 22:18) — the Torah teaches that surplus of a vow becomes a gift offering.
Gemara
According to Reish Lakish, why limit this to unallocated funds? Even allocated money should become gift offerings!
Rava
The Tanna of R' Yishmael's school already ruled: the word 'Only' (Deut 12:26) excludes sin-offering offspring and guilt-offering substitutes from being sacrificed.
KEY ISSUE When a nazirite dies, what is the source for using unallocated surplus funds as gift burnt-offerings, given that sin-offering money is mixed in?

Source for Unallocated Funds → Gift Offerings

Gemara
Sin-offering money is mingled with the unallocated funds. How can it all be used for gift burnt-offerings?
R' Yochanan
It is an accepted halakha (הלכה) — a tradition specific to a nazirite that unallocated funds go to gift offerings.
Reish Lakish
The verse 'for any of their vows or gift offerings' (Lev 22:18) teaches: surplus of a vow → gift offering.
Gemara
For R' Yochanan, the halakha limits it to unallocated funds. But for Reish Lakish, even allocated funds should qualify!
PRACTICAL DIFFERENCE R' Yochanan's tradition-based source naturally limits the rule to unallocated funds. Reish Lakish's verse-based derivation requires additional reasoning to exclude allocated funds.

R' Yishmael vs. R' Akiva — Excluding Guilt-Offering Substitutes

R' Yishmael

  • Derives from the word "Only" (אַךְ) in Deut 12:26
  • "Only" excludes offspring of sin-offerings and substitutes of guilt-offerings
  • These may NOT be sacrificed on the altar
  • Uses a single verse to exclude both cases

R' Akiva

  • For guilt-offering: the verse "It is a guilt-offering" (Lev 5:19) is sufficient
  • "It" = it alone, not its substitute
  • No need for "Only" to exclude guilt-offering substitutes
  • "Only" is needed only for sin-offering offspring
AGREEMENT Both agree that offspring of sin-offerings and substitutes of guilt-offerings cannot be sacrificed — they differ on the scriptural source.

Why Can't We Let Offspring of Peace-Offerings Die?

💭 Perhaps offspring of peace-offerings, like sin-offerings, should be brought to the Temple and left to die?
↓
📖 The verse states: 'You shall sacrifice your burnt-offerings, the flesh and the blood' (Deut 12:27)
↓
🐑 Treat a burnt-offering's substitute like a burnt-offering — sacrificed entirely
↓
🍖 Treat a peace-offering's offspring like a peace-offering — innards on altar, flesh eaten
↓
✅ Conclusion: Offspring of peace-offerings are sacrificed, not left to die
LOGIC Only the offspring of a sin-offering is left to die. Without the verse, we might have extended this stringency to peace-offerings as well.
