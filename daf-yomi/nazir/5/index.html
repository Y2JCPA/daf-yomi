How Long Is 'Yamim'? And How Long Is Naziriteship?

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
Yamim in Absalom's case means 12 months, derived via gezerah shavah from houses of walled cities (Leviticus 25:29).
Rabbi Nehorai
Absalom cut his hair every 30 days — the same interval as kohanim, because that's when hair gains noticeable weight.
Rabbi Yosei
He cut his hair every Shabbat eve — as was the custom of sons of kings.
Rav Mattana
An unspecified naziriteship is 30 days, derived from the numerical value of the word yihyeh.
Bar Padda
The principal days of naziriteship are 29 days — the 30th day is only for shaving and offerings.
Core Tension The daf explores how the ambiguous term yamim is resolved through verbal analogies, and whether naziriteship is fundamentally 29 or 30 days.

How Often Did Absalom Cut His Hair?

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
Once every 12 months — derived from gezerah shavah of yamim from walled city houses (Lev. 25:29-30).
Rabbi Nehorai
Once every 30 days — just like kohanim. After 30 days, hair has noticeable weight, and the verse says he cut it because it was heavy (II Sam. 14:26).
Rabbi Yosei
Every Shabbat eve — as was the practice of sons of kings.
Gemara
If Absalom cut his hair weekly like all king's sons, what made him different as a nazirite?
Key Point All three opinions agree Absalom was a nazirite — the debate is how long his nazirite terms lasted, which determines the frequency of his haircuts.

Can You Make a Gezerah Shavah from Similar (Not Identical) Words?

Can we derive yamim from yamim yamima (Jephthah's daughter)?
↓
Challenge
The school of R' Yishmael derived veshav from uva (different words!) regarding leprosy of houses — so surely yamim and yamima should work!
Answer
That applies only when no identical term exists. Here, identical uses of yamim exist (walled cities) — so we derive from the identical term instead.
↓
Alternative answer: Yamim yamima is ambiguous — Jephthah's daughter may have been visited at irregular intervals (e.g., 4 months, then 2 months, then 4, then 2).
Rule When identical terms are available for a gezerah shavah, we prefer them over merely similar terms.

Rav Mattana vs. Bar Padda: Duration of Unspecified Naziriteship

Rav Mattana — 30 Days

  • Derived from gematria of yihyeh (יהיה = 30)
  • Nazirite shaves on day 31 ab initio
  • If shaved on day 30 — fulfilled, via miktzat hayom k'kulo (part of day = whole day)
  • Two naziriteships: shave on days 31 and 61

Bar Padda — 29 Days

  • Derived from multiple verses using the word nazir
  • Principal naziriteship is 29 days
  • Day 30 = shaving/offerings day (prohibitions still apply)
  • Mishna says 30 days because the 30th day is included functionally
  • Ab initio shaves on day 31: Sages equated unspecified vows with explicit '30 days' vows
Practical Difference Both agree a nazirite shaves on day 31 l'chatchila. The dispute: if he shaves on day 30, does miktzat hayom rescue him (Rav Mattana) or was naziriteship actually complete already (Bar Padda)?
