QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi derive 'yamim' from walled city houses rather than from 'two years of yamim' (Genesis 41:1)?
A. Because yamim without a qualifier is derived from yamim without a qualifier — not from yamim paired with 'years'
B. Because the Genesis verse is about dreams, not hair
C. Because two years is too long for any naziriteship
D. Because the walled cities verse is older and therefore takes priority
Correct: A — The Gemara's principle is that we derive yamim (standing alone) from another yamim (standing alone), not from yamim that appears with a qualifying term like 'years' or 'months.'
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Nehorai, why does Absalom cut his hair every 30 days specifically?
A. Because that is the gematria of the word 'nazir'
B. Because after 30 days hair gains noticeable weight, like the interval for kohanim
C. Because 30 days is the standard naziriteship
D. Because the Torah says 'a month of yamim' equals 30 days
Correct: B — Rabbi Nehorai's reasoning is based on the verse stating Absalom cut his hair 'because it was heavy' — and kohanim demonstrate that 30 days is when hair gains noticeable weight.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Bar Padda explain the mishna's statement that unspecified naziriteship is 30 days, if he holds it is really 29?
A. He says the mishna is an error
B. He says the 30th day is included because nazirite prohibitions still apply while shaving and bringing offerings
C. He says the mishna rounds up for simplicity
D. He says the 30th day is for purification only
Correct: B — Bar Padda explains that while the principal naziriteship is 29 days, the mishna counts 30 because on the 30th day the nazirite shaves and brings offerings while nazirite prohibitions remain in effect.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the practical significance of 'miktzat hayom k'kulo' for Rav Mattana's position?
A. It means a nazirite can drink wine on the last day
B. It allows a nazirite who shaved on day 30 to have fulfilled his obligation b'dieved
C. It means the nazirite vow takes effect from the middle of the first day
D. It allows combining two naziriteships into one
Correct: B — For Rav Mattana, naziriteship is 30 full days — but 'part of the day is like the whole day' means that once day 30 begins, it is as if completed, so shaving on day 30 fulfills the obligation after the fact.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why can't the gezerah shavah be drawn from 'yamim yamima' (Jephthah's daughter) according to the first answer?
A. Because Jephthah's daughter was not a nazirite
B. Because yamim yamima is a different form — and when identical terms exist, we derive from identical terms, not merely similar ones
C. Because the verse about Jephthah's daughter is in Nevi'im, not Torah
D. Because yamim yamima refers to mourning, which is unrelated to hair
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes: when identical terms are available for a gezerah shavah (yamim = yamim), we use them rather than deriving from a merely similar term (yamim ≈ yamima).
