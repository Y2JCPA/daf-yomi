Who Can Become a Nazirite?

Torah states: "Speak to the children of Israel… when a man or woman shall utter a vow" (Numbers 6:2)
↓
"Children of Israel"
Excludes gentiles from naziriteship
"Man or woman"
Includes women — and by extension slaves
Key Principle The Gemara must explain why the word "man" includes gentiles for valuations but not for naziriteship — several Amoraim offer different derivations.

Leper's Shaving vs. Nazirite's Shaving

Leper (Metzora)

  • Shaves before immersion in water
  • Shaves before blood is sprinkled
  • Purpose: purification from tzaraat

Impure Nazirite

  • Shaves after immersion in water
  • Shaves after blood is sprinkled
  • Purpose: completing naziriteship
R' Ḥiyya Because the timing and prerequisites differ, a leper's shaving does not count as a nazirite's shaving.

The Four Shavings: Mitzva or Hair Removal?

Rami bar Ḥama
Are all four shavings of a nazirite-leper performed as a mitzva, or are some merely to remove impure hair so new hair can grow?
Gemara
What practical difference (nafka minah) does this make?
Gemara
If it's a mitzva — he must use a razor. If it's for hair removal — even a depilatory (sam) would suffice.
Rava
The Tosefta states he shaves with four acts. If some were just for removal, three would suffice. Conclude: all four are for the mitzva.
Resolution All four shavings are mitzvah-based, requiring an actual razor — a depilatory cream is insufficient.

Why Must the Torah Separately Include Slaves?

Principle: Any mitzva a woman is obligated in, a slave is also obligated in
↓
Problem: Regarding vows, the verse says "to bind his soul" — only one whose soul is in his own possession
↓
Without extra verse
A slave — whose soul is not in his possession — might be excluded
With "say to them"
Torah specifically includes slaves despite their subordinate status
Rava The general principle equating slaves to women would fail here because a slave is under his master's jurisdiction. The extra verse is needed.

Why Doesn't "Man" Include Gentiles for Naziriteship?

Gemara
For valuations, "children of Israel" excludes gentiles from vowing, but "man" includes them as subjects of valuation. Why not split naziriteship similarly?
First Answer
The nazirite verse mentions impurity for "his father" — a gentile has no father in halakhic terms (no recognized paternal lineage).
Gemara
But R' Yoḥanan says a gentile inherits his father! So he does have a halakhic father in some sense.
Second Answer
A gentile cannot become tamei (ritually impure). Since naziriteship includes impurity laws, it cannot apply to one outside the tumah framework.
Rava
The real reason: for valuations, "man" can include gentiles in one aspect (being valuated). For naziriteship, there is no partial inclusion possible — so "children of Israel" fully excludes gentiles.
Key Distinction Valuations have separable components (vowing vs. being valuated), allowing partial gentile inclusion. Naziriteship does not split this way.
