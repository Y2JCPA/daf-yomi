QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does a leper's shaving not count for a nazirite's shaving?
A. The leper uses a different type of razor
B. The timing differs: the leper shaves before immersion, the nazirite after
C. A leper's shaving is only on the head, not the whole body
D. The leper's shaving is performed by a kohen, not himself
Correct: B — R' Ḥiyya teaches that the leper shaves before immersion and before blood sprinkling, while the nazirite shaves after both — the different timing means one cannot count for the other.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the practical difference (nafka minah) of whether the four shavings are for a mitzva or for hair removal?
A. Whether the shaving must be done by a kohen
B. Whether a depilatory (chemical hair remover) can be used instead of a razor
C. Whether the shaving must take place in the Temple courtyard
D. Whether the hair must be burned under the pot of the shelamim
Correct: B — If the shaving is a mitzva, it must be done specifically with a razor. If it's merely to remove impure hair, even a depilatory cream would suffice.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Torah need a special verse to include slaves in naziriteship, if the general principle already equates slaves to women for mitzvot?
A. Slaves are not considered Jews for purposes of naziriteship
B. The vow verse requires one 'whose soul is in his own possession,' which excludes slaves
C. Slaves cannot bring animal offerings required of nazirites
D. A master's authority automatically nullifies any vow a slave makes
Correct: B — Rava explains that 'to bind his soul with a bond' implies only one who controls his own person. Since a slave is under his master's jurisdiction, a special verse is needed to include him.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why can't the word 'man' (ish) in the naziriteship verse include gentiles, as it does for valuations?
A. Because 'man' in Numbers always excludes gentiles
B. Because for valuations 'man' can include gentiles in one separable aspect (being valuated), but naziriteship has no such partial inclusion
C. Because gentiles are explicitly mentioned as excluded in a later verse
D. Because 'man' in the naziriteship verse is modified by 'children of Israel' making it redundant
Correct: B — Rava explains that valuations have two separable components (vowing and being the subject), so 'man' can include gentiles in one. Naziriteship cannot be split similarly, so 'children of Israel' fully excludes gentiles.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara prove that gentiles cannot become ritually impure (tamei)?
A. From a verse stating that only kohanim can become impure
B. From the principle that impurity and purification are interdependent — one who cannot be purified cannot become impure
C. From the fact that gentiles are not obligated in the red heifer ritual
D. From a tradition that Moshe received at Sinai excluding gentiles from tumah
Correct: B — The verse 'the pure shall sprinkle upon the impure' shows that impurity and purification go together. Since gentiles cannot attain purity through the waters of purification, they cannot become impure either.
