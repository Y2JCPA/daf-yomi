When Does Corpse Dust Impart Impurity?

A corpse decays into a full ladle of dust (מלא תרווד רקב)
↓
Was it buried in conditions that preserve pure dust?
✅ Naked in marble/stone
Dust is solely from the corpse → Impure
❌ Clothed/wood/brick
Dust is mixed with foreign particles → Pure
Key Principle Corpse dust imparts impurity only when it comes exclusively from the corpse itself, with no admixture of foreign materials.
3 / 9

Burial Conditions for Impure Dust

Dust IS Impure

  • Buried naked (no clothing)
  • In a marble coffin (ארון של שיש)
  • Or on a stone floor
  • All dust must come solely from the corpse

Dust is NOT Impure

  • Buried in clothing
  • In a wooden coffin
  • Or on a brick floor
  • Dust includes particles from non-body sources
Source Tosefta Oholot 2:3 — The principle: only dust that came entirely from the corpse imparts impurity.

What Must the Dust Contain?

Ulla
Impure dust must come from flesh, sinews, and bones together — not from any one alone.
Rava
A baraita says dust from flesh alone is pure — implying dust from bone alone is impure! This contradicts you!
Ulla
Read it: dust from flesh is pure unless there is bone in the flesh.
Rava
But there's no mention of sinews at all!
Ulla
It is impossible for there to be flesh and bones without sinews. They are necessarily included.
Resolution Ulla's requirement stands: flesh + bones + sinews must all be present, but sinews are automatically included wherever flesh and bones exist together.

Two Corpses and the Mixture Problem

R' Yoḥanan (via Rav Shmuel bar Abba): Two corpses buried together form a mixture (גלגלין) — their dust is not impure
↓
Rav Natan objects: A baraita says dust from two corpses is impure!
↓
How does Rava reconcile?
Baraita's Case
Each corpse buried separately, decayed separately, each yielded a full ladle → Impure
R' Yoḥanan's Case
Corpses buried together, decayed together → dust is a mixture → Pure
Principle Dust from each corpse must have independently reached a full ladle. If they decayed together, the dust cannot be attributed to either corpse alone.

Abaye's Tradition: Ground Corpse

כתשוהו — אין לו תורת רקב
Halakha Abaye transmits a tradition: A corpse that was ground into small pieces (כתשוהו) has no halakha of dust.

Dilemma: If it was ground after death and then decayed, what is the law?
• Perhaps: flesh + sinews + bones are present → impure
• Perhaps: it must decay from its initial natural state → pure

This dilemma stands unresolved (תיקו).
