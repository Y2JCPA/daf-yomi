Nazir 23 — Intent, Sin & Atonement

גדולה עבירה לשמה ממצוה שלא לשמה
Core Theme This daf explores the moral weight of intention. Through the nullified nazirite vow, R' Akiva's parables of pork and lamb, and dramatic biblical examples — Tamar, Yael, Lot's daughters, and Balak — the Gemara teaches that intent transforms the nature of an act. A sin committed for Heaven's sake can surpass a mitzvah done for the wrong reasons.

R' Akiva Weeps: The Weight of Sinful Intent

R' Akiva
When I reach this verse, I weep. If one intended to eat pork but picked up lamb — no actual sin — yet she needs forgiveness...
R' Akiva
Then one who intended pork and actually ate pork — kal va'chomer, how much more does he need atonement!
Isi ben Yehuda
My case: two pieces before him — one forbidden fat, one permitted. He ate one not knowing which. Even here: "he shall bear his iniquity."
Isi ben Yehuda
If this person requires atonement, then one who deliberately picks up the forbidden piece — all the more so!
Principle Even unexecuted sinful intent carries moral weight requiring atonement and forgiveness.

Same Act, Different Intentions

Tamar

  • Engaged in relations with Judah (Genesis 38)
  • Intention: for the sake of a mitzvah — to bear children
  • Result: Kings of David's house descended from her
  • Prophets also descended from her line

Zimri

  • Engaged in relations with a Midianite woman (Numbers 25)
  • Intention: for the sake of transgression
  • Result: 24,000 of Israel fell in a plague
  • Caused catastrophic punishment for the nation
Ulla's Teaching Externally identical acts — yet intention determined whether kings or plague followed.

Transgression for Heaven's Sake vs. Mitzvah Shelo Lishmah

R' Nachman bar Yitzchak
Greater is a transgression for its own sake (lishmah) than a mitzvah not for its own sake (shelo lishmah)!
Rav Yehuda / Rav
But Rav said: One should always engage in Torah even shelo lishmah, because from shelo lishmah comes lishmah!
Gemara
Correction: A transgression lishmah is equivalent to — not greater than — a mitzvah shelo lishmah.
R' Yochanan
Proof from Yael: "Blessed above women in the tent" — she is compared to Sarah, Rebecca, Rachel, and Leah. Her act with Sisera was for Heaven's sake.
Key Principle גדולה עבירה לשמה כמצוה שלא לשמה — A transgression for the sake of Heaven is equivalent to a mitzvah performed not for its own sake.
