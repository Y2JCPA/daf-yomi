Nazir 23 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, if a husband nullifies his wife's nazirite vow without her knowledge and she drinks wine, what happens?
A. She incurs 40 lashes
B. She does not incur lashes
C. She must bring a sin-offering
D. Her naziriteship is extended
Correct: B — Since the husband nullified the vow, she is no longer a nazirite and therefore does not incur lashes — even though she believed she was sinning.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did R' Akiva weep when he reached the verse 'Her husband has nullified them, and the Lord will forgive her'?
A. He felt pity for the woman's suffering
B. He derived a kal va'chomer: if one who intended to sin but didn't still needs forgiveness, how much more so an actual sinner
C. He believed the husband acted wrongly
D. He disagreed with the law of nullification
Correct: B — R' Akiva's tears came from the powerful kal va'chomer: if mere sinful intent (like reaching for pork but grabbing lamb) requires atonement, then actually sinning requires far greater atonement.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the Gemara's corrected version of R' Nachman bar Yitzchak's statement about transgression lishmah?
A. A transgression lishmah is greater than a mitzvah lishmah
B. A transgression lishmah is equivalent to a mitzvah shelo lishmah
C. A transgression lishmah requires no atonement
D. A transgression lishmah is always forbidden
Correct: B — The Gemara corrects the statement from 'greater than' to 'equivalent to' (כ not מ) — a transgression for Heaven's sake is equivalent to a mitzvah not for its own sake, based on the proof from Yael.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara prove that Balak's insincere sacrifices still had merit?
A. Balak later repented and converted
B. Ruth descended from Balak, and from her came King David
C. The sacrifices were accepted by God at the time
D. Balaam praised Balak for his offerings
Correct: B — Despite Balak's intent to curse Israel, his 42 offerings earned him the merit that Ruth descended from him — and from Ruth came King David, proving that even shelo lishmah has reward.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara need ALL THREE cases (nullified vow, one uncertain piece, two pieces) to teach about atonement for unintentional sin?
A. They correspond to three different offerings
B. Each case has a unique element that cannot be derived from the others
C. They represent three different historical periods
D. Each was taught by a different Tanna and cannot be combined
Correct: B — The nullified vow teaches about sinful intent alone; the uncertain piece teaches about unintentional sin without intent; the two-piece case (Isi ben Yehuda) teaches that even with an established prohibition present, forgiveness still suffices. None can be derived from the others.
