Why Does the Torah Mention Both Parents?

Gemara
According to Rabbi Akiva, the prohibition against a nazirite becoming impure is derived from 'for his brother' (Numbers 6:7). So why does the Torah also say 'for his father, or for his mother'?
Answer
Both are necessary! If only 'father' were written — paternity is merely a ḥazaka (presumption). You might think a nazirite may become impure for his mother, whose identity is certain.
Answer
And if only 'mother' were written — since lineage follows the father (mishpaḥat av), you might think he may become impure for his father.
Conclusion
Therefore both are stated to teach that a nazirite may not become impure for either parent.
Principle A verse is never superfluous — each word excludes a potential leniency we might have derived by logic alone.

Rabbi Akiva: Deriving 'Dead Bodies' (Lev. 21:11)

'Neither shall he go in to any dead bodies'
(Leviticus 21:11, regarding a High Priest)
↓
'In to any'
Excludes contracting impurity for distant people (non-relatives)
'Dead'
Excludes impurity for relatives
'Bodies' (plural)
Includes a quarter-log of blood from two corpses that combines for impurity in a tent
Note The plural 'bodies' (nefashot) teaches that blood from two different corpses can combine to form the minimum quantity for ritual impurity.

Three Modes of Contracting Corpse Impurity

Contact (Maga)

  • Physically touching the source of impurity
  • Applies to ALL listed sources
  • Including a barley-grain-bulk bone

Carrying (Masa)

  • Lifting or bearing the impurity source
  • Applies to ALL listed sources
  • Including a barley-grain-bulk bone

Tent (Ohel)

  • Being under the same roof as the impurity
  • Or forming a tent over it / it forming a tent over you
  • Does NOT apply to a barley-grain-bulk bone
Exception A bone the size of a barley-grain (se'ora) imparts impurity only through contact and carrying — NOT through tent (ohel).

↓
💧 Sprinkled again on day 7
↓

↓
❌ All previously counted days are negated
↓
🐑 Brings purification offerings
↓

Key Rule Corpse impurity completely resets the nazirite's count — unlike other forms of impurity. He starts over from day one.
