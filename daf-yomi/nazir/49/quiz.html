Nazir 49 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why does the Torah need to mention 'for his father' regarding a nazirite's impurity prohibition?
A. Because a father's death is more tragic than a mother's
B. Because without it, since lineage follows the father, one might permit impurity for him
C. Because the father is the head of the household
D. Because the prohibition only applies to fathers by default
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that since 'one's family ancestry follows the father' (mishpaḥat av), without the verse one might have thought a nazirite should become impure to bury his father due to the special importance of paternal lineage.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the plural word 'bodies' (nefashot) in Leviticus 21:11 come to teach?
A. That a High Priest may not enter any cemetery
B. That a quarter-log of blood from two different corpses combines for tent impurity
C. That impurity from multiple corpses is more severe
D. That a nazirite must shave separately for each corpse
Correct: B — The plural 'bodies' teaches that blood from two corpses can combine to reach the quarter-log minimum needed to impart impurity in a tent (ohel).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which source of impurity does NOT require the nazirite to shave for ohel (tent) impurity?
A. An olive-bulk of a corpse
B. A half-kav of bones
C. A barley-grain-bulk bone
D. A skull
Correct: C — The Mishna specifies that a bone the size of a barley-grain imparts impurity only through contact and carrying, but NOT through tent (ohel). All other listed sources impart impurity through all three modes.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Yehuda grow angry when Sumakhos cited Rabbi Meir's version of the Mishna?
A. Because Sumakhos entered without permission and cited a version with a superfluous clause ('for a corpse') that was unnecessary given the kal va-ḥomer from an olive-bulk
B. Because Rabbi Meir's version contained a factual error
C. Because Sumakhos was not a qualified student
D. Because the halakha had already been decided differently
Correct: A — Rabbi Yehuda objected that mentioning 'a corpse' is unnecessary — if a nazirite must shave for an olive-bulk, all the more so for an entire corpse. He saw this as an example of Rabbi Meir's students being 'vexatious.'
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What happens to the days a nazirite already counted toward his term if he contracts corpse impurity?
A. They are preserved and he continues counting after purification
B. He loses only the last seven days
C. All previously counted days are negated and he restarts from the beginning
D. He adds penalty days equal to the days already counted
Correct: C — The Mishna states explicitly that corpse impurity negates all prior counted days. After purification and bringing offerings, the nazirite begins counting his entire term of naziriteship again from the start.
