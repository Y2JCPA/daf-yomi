QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara conclude that R' Yehuda follows Beit Hillel regarding Queen Helene?
A. Because he says she was a nazirite for 14 years, not 14 years and 30 days
B. Because he explicitly states he follows Beit Hillel
C. Because Beit Shammai's view was already rejected by the Sanhedrin
D. Because Queen Helene herself followed Beit Hillel
Correct: A — If R' Yehuda followed Beit Shammai, the total would include an extra 30 days (the unspecified naziriteship). Since he says exactly 14 years, he must follow Beit Hillel's view that she observed 7 additional years without becoming impure.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Beit Hillel in the mishna, why is testimony of '2 terms' vs. '5 terms' not a contradiction?
A. Because the more recent testimony overrides the earlier one
B. Because two terms are included within five terms
C. Because witnesses about naziriteship are never fully credible
D. Because the person can choose which testimony to follow
Correct: B — Beit Hillel's reasoning is that since 2 is contained within 5, both sets of witnesses agree on at least 2 terms. The unanimous portion of the testimony is accepted.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In a chain of 'And I' nazirite vows, what happens if the first person's vow is dissolved?
A. Only the first person is released
B. All subsequent vows are dissolved
C. The second person becomes the new foundation
D. Each person must individually seek dissolution
Correct: B — Since each subsequent vow depends on the one before it, dissolving the foundational first vow causes the entire chain to collapse — all are dissolved.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why can't a husband nullify his wife's nazirite vow when he said 'And I' after her declaration?
A. Because her vow was made first and takes priority
B. Because nullifying hers would effectively nullify his own vow
C. Because 'And I' creates an irrevocable bond
D. Because a husband can never nullify nazirite vows
Correct: B — Since his vow depends on hers, nullifying her vow would dissolve his as well. A man cannot use the mechanism of nullifying his wife's vow to effectively nullify his own nazirite vow.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is R' Yehuda Nesia's objection to Reish Lakish's 'toch kedei dibbur' time limit?
A. The time is too short for anyone to respond
B. A student whose rabbi passes by must greet him first, losing the window
C. The greeting formula is too long to serve as a measure
D. Students should not be making nazirite vows at all
Correct: B — R' Yehuda Nesia notes the irony: the time window is defined by greeting one's rabbi, but if the rabbi actually walks by at that moment, the student is obligated to greet him — thereby missing the chance to say 'And I.'
