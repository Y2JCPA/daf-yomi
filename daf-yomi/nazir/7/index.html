Months Compose Years, Not Days

מנין שאין מונין ימים לשנים
FOUNDATION The Sages of Caesarea teach: We derive from "Of the months of the year" (Exodus 12:2) that months are counted toward years, but individual days are not. This is why only a month can be described as "completed" by a particular day — not a year.

'Until the End of the World' = 30 Days?

Mishna
If one says "I am a nazirite for one long term," or "one short term," or even "from now until the end of the world" — he is a nazirite for 30 days.
Gemara
But he said 'until the end of the world'! Why only 30 days?
Gemara
He means: the difficulty of naziriteship feels as long as from now until the end of the world. He accepted only one standard term.
INSIGHT Hyperbolic language about duration expresses emotional weight, not actual timeframe — so the vow defaults to the standard 30 days.

Fixed vs. Infinite Quantities

Fixed Amount

  • "From here until place X" — countable days
  • Defines the length of ONE term
  • Result: single naziriteship of that duration
  • Minimum always 30 days

Infinite Amount

  • "Like dust / hair / sand of the sea"
  • Defines the number of separate terms
  • Result: nazirite forever, shaves every 30 days
  • Each grain/hair = one 30-day term
RABBA Hairs are physically separated from each other, so each one represents a distinct term. Days, by contrast, are continuous — 'evening and morning' (Genesis 1:5) defines the calendar day but time itself is unbroken.

Mishna: 30 Days and One Hour

DeclarationDurationReasoning
"Nazirite for 30 days and 1 hour"31 daysNo naziriteship for hours — rounds up to next full day
"Nazirite for 31 days" (Rav)31 days (one term)Combined number = single term of that length
"Nazirite for 30 days and 1 day" (Rav)Two full terms (60 days)Separated phrasing implies two distinct acceptances
RULE There is no naziriteship for hours — only complete days. A partial day triggers a full additional day.
