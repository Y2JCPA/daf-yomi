Nazir 22 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Elazar HaKappar, why does the nazirite bring a sin-offering?
A. For becoming ritually impure during naziriteship
B. For depriving himself of wine by taking the vow
C. For failing to complete the full nazirite term
D. For violating the terms of the vow
Correct: B — R' Elazar HaKappar derives from Numbers 6:11 that the nazirite sinned 'by reason of the soul' — by depriving himself of wine. The sin-offering atones for the act of taking the vow itself.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
If Woman B says 'And I' after hearing Woman A's nazirite vow, and A's husband nullifies A's vow, what happens to B?
A. Both women are permitted
B. Woman B remains prohibited as a nazirite
C. Woman B must ask a sage to dissolve her vow
D. Woman B brings a sin-offering but is otherwise free
Correct: B — The Tanna Kamma of the baraita rules that with 'And I,' the second woman's vow is independent enough to survive the nullification of the first. Only R' Shimon's case ('I am like you') results in both being freed.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why can't a husband who says 'And I' to his wife's nazirite vow simply nullify her vow?
A. Because men cannot nullify nazirite vows at all
B. Because saying 'And I' is considered an implicit upholding of her vow
C. Because his vow was made first
D. Because the Torah requires two witnesses to nullify
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that by saying 'And I,' the husband implicitly tells his wife 'It is upheld for you' (kiyum). He must first have a sage dissolve his upholding before he can nullify.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In Rami bar Ḥama's dilemma about peace-offering flesh, what is the significance of 'fundamental state' vs. 'eventual permitted state'?
A. Whether the offering was valid or invalid
B. Whether one associates with the object as originally forbidden or as later permitted
C. Whether the peace-offering was brought in the Temple or outside
D. Whether the prohibition is biblical or rabbinic
Correct: B — The dilemma asks: when someone associates a vow with another object, do they link to its original prohibited state (before blood-sprinkling) or its final permitted state (after sprinkling)? This parallels whether the second woman's vow references the original active vow or its later nullified state.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When a husband says 'I am a nazirite and you' and his wife does NOT say Amen, what is the result according to the baraita?
A. Only she is bound by the vow
B. Only he is bound by the vow
C. Both are bound by the vow
D. Both are permitted — neither is bound
Correct: D — The baraita teaches that if she does not say Amen, both are permitted, because he made his vow conditional on her acceptance. Since she did not accept, his vow is also canceled.
