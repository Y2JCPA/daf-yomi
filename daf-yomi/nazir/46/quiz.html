Nazir 46 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Rabbis, when may a nazirite drink wine?
A. After all offerings, shaving, and waving are completed
B. After sacrificing a single offering, even before shaving
C. Only after the priest performs the waving ritual
D. After shaving but before bringing offerings
Correct: B — The Rabbis derive from a gezeirah shavah on the word 'after' that a nazirite may drink wine after a single action — sacrificing one offering — even without shaving.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rav's novel teaching (chiddush) about waving?
A. Waving is never required for any offering
B. Waving is required only for peace-offerings
C. Even though waving is peripheral in other offerings, it IS indispensable for a nazirite's completion
D. Waving can be performed by the nazirite himself instead of the priest
Correct: C — Rav teaches that although waving is not indispensable for atonement in other offerings, for the nazirite's release from his prohibitions it is essential (per Rabbi Eliezer).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Pedat, what principle do Beit Shammai and Rabbi Eliezer share?
A. A nazirite may substitute one ritual for another
B. If a Torah-mandated ritual cannot be performed precisely as specified, the person has no remedy
C. Waving always overrides shaving in importance
D. A bald person is exempt from all nazirite obligations
Correct: B — Rabbi Pedat says both Beit Shammai and Rabbi Eliezer hold that if the ritual cannot be performed in the precise manner the Torah delineates, the person cannot fulfill the obligation and has no remedy.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If a nazirite shaved based on a sin-offering that was sacrificed not for its own sake (shelo lishmah), what happens?
A. His shaving is valid but the offering must be repeated
B. His shaving is invalid and his other offerings do not count
C. He is exempt from further offerings
D. Only the sin-offering is invalid; other offerings still count
Correct: B — The Mishna teaches that if he shaved based on a sin-offering found to be sacrificed not for its own sake, both his shaving is invalid and his other offerings do not count toward fulfillment.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the second version of the discussion (Rabbi Avina's reading), what does Beit Hillel say about a bald nazirite?
A. He is exempt from the razor requirement entirely
B. He must pass a razor, and if he cannot actually shave, he has no remedy
C. He can substitute another ritual for shaving
D. He must grow his hair before completing naziriteship
Correct: B — In Rabbi Avina's version, Beit Hillel hold that the bald nazirite must shave, and if he fails to do so, he has no remedy and can never drink wine — the opposite of Ravina's reading.
