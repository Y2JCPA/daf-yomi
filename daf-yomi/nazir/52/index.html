Nazir 52: Spine, Skull & the Measure of Impurity

Does a nazirite shave for a spine and skull together, or for either one?
↓
Multiple Proofs Attempted
Rava's baraita, box of bones incident, Rabbi Akiva's six items
Result
Each proof is rejected — the dilemma remains unresolved
THEME This daf wrestles with whether conjunctive vav (ו) means 'and' or 'or' — a question with major practical consequences for nazirite impurity law.

Lentil-Bulk: The Measure for Sheretz Impurity

Verse: "Whoever touches them" (Lev. 11:31) — implies all of them
↓
Verse: "any of them" (Lev. 11:32) — implies even part
↓
How to reconcile?
↓
A part equal to the whole — the size of a lentil-bulk (כעדשה)
↓
Because a skink (חומט) begins formation at lentil size
APPLICATION Rav Yehuda of Diskarta uses this to argue an ant missing a limb should not be considered whole — but Rav Shemaya refutes: a living creature with a soul is different. The dilemma remains unresolved.

Spine AND Skull, or Spine OR Skull?

The Dilemma
The mishna says a nazirite shaves for שדרה וגולגולת (spine ve skull). Does ו mean 'and' (both required) or 'or' (either suffices)?
Rava
A baraita teaches: a spine whose majority of ribs were removed is pure. This implies if ribs are intact, the spine alone is impure — so it's 'or'!
Gemara
No — the baraita only teaches that removing ribs purifies the spine. It says nothing about the spine being impure on its own without a skull.
Conclusion
Proof rejected. The dilemma stands.
KEY POINT A spine in a grave remains impure even if broken — the grave joins bones together (צירוף).

The Box of Bones at the Synagogue of Blacksmiths

תודוס הרופא
HISTORICAL INCIDENT A box of bones was brought to the synagogue of the tarsiyyim (blacksmiths). It was placed in the open air to avoid tent-impurity. Todos the doctor and other physicians examined it and declared: "There is no complete spine from a single corpse here."

The Gemara attempts to derive from this that either a spine or skull suffices — but the proof is rejected: the doctors meant there were not even parts of a single spine or skull that could combine.

Rabbi Akiva's Six Items Deemed Impure

ItemWhat Makes It Controversial
Limb from a corpse — from two corpsesParts from different bodies combining
Limb from a living person — from two peopleSame issue with living persons
Half-kav of bones — from two corpsesCombining bones across bodies
Quarter-log of blood — from two corpsesR' Akiva never retracted this one
Barley-grain bone — divided in twoOnly R' Yochanan b. Nuri disagrees
Spine & skull — from two corpsesCombining across bodies
SIX OR SEVEN? If spine and skull are separate items, the list has seven. The Gemara offers four explanations for which item is excluded — remembered by the mnemonic: יחיד, שהוא, מגלח, רביעית.

Four Explanations: Which Item Is Excluded from the Six?

Barley-Grain Bone

  • Only one Sage (R' Yochanan b. Nuri) disagrees
  • The list counts cases where many Sages disagree

Limb from Living

  • Simply not counted in the tally
  • Only limb from corpse is listed

Barley-Grain (Alt.)

  • Does not impart impurity in a tent (ohel)
  • List only includes tent-impurity cases

Quarter-Log Blood

  • R' Akiva never retracted this opinion
  • List only includes his retractions
NOTE R' Yehuda HaNasi told bar Kappara: Do not include quarter-log of blood in R' Akiva's retractions — the verse supports him.
