Naziriteship in the Cemetery: Does the Vow Bite?

R' Yochanan
A vow of naziriteship taken in a cemetery takes effect, and the nazirite is flogged for remaining impure.
Reish Lakish
The vow takes effect, but the nazirite is not flogged for impurity contracted while in the cemetery.
Mar bar Rav Ashi
Both agree the vow takes effect. The only dispute is whether he receives lashes (malkot) for the impurity.
Gemara
A baraita conclusively refutes Reish Lakish: one who vowed while impure and then drank wine or touched a corpse incurs forty lashes.
Resolution The Gemara concludes that R' Yochanan is correct: a nazirite who vowed in a cemetery is liable for lashes if he violates nazirite prohibitions.

Mar bar Rav Ashi Reframes the Dispute

Mar bar Rav Ashi
Everyone agrees the vow takes effect even in the cemetery. The real dispute is about flogging.
R' Yochanan
Since the vow takes effect, he is flogged for becoming impure — the nazirite prohibitions apply immediately.
Reish Lakish
He is not flogged for impurity — the vow takes effect for prohibitions like wine and shaving, but not for corpse impurity while still in the cemetery.
R' Yochanan
The mishna says he doesn't bring offerings of impurity — implying he is flogged! This refutes Reish Lakish.
Key Shift The dispute is not about whether the vow takes effect — both agree it does. It is only about whether flogging applies.

Impure Vower vs. Pure Nazirite Who Became Impure

Impure Person Who Vowed

  • Took vow while already in cemetery
  • 7th day of purification COUNTS toward tally
  • Starts naziriteship count upon becoming pure
  • Must shave (disputed by Rav Ashi)
  • Is flogged for violations (per R' Yochanan)

Pure Nazirite Who Became Impure

  • Began naziriteship in purity, then defiled it
  • 7th day of purification does NOT count
  • Starts counting from the next day (8th day)
  • Must shave before restarting
  • Is flogged for impurity violations
Baraita The only stated difference between them is whether the 7th day of purification counts toward the tally. With regard to flogging, both are equal.

Rava's Dilemma: Delayed Exit from Cemetery

Nazirite enters cemetery in a chest/box/cabinet
(does not contract impurity)
↓
Takes a vow of naziriteship while inside the container
↓
Someone removes the lid — he becomes impure
↓
He is warned to leave the cemetery
↓
Must his exit be delayed (שהייה) to incur lashes?
Temple Rule Applies
Just as in the Temple — lashes only if he delays his exit beyond the minimum time
Cemetery Is Different
Outside the Temple, he is liable immediately without needing a delayed exit
Teiku The dilemma remains unresolved (תיקו). The Gemara does not determine whether the delay requirement applies outside the Temple.

Rav Ashi's Dilemma: Must the Impure Vower Shave?

🧔 A pure nazirite who became impure must shave before restarting
↓
❓ But what about one who vowed while ALREADY impure?
↓
📜 Mishna proof attempt: 'He does not bring offerings' — implying he DOES shave?
↓
🚫 Rejected: Maybe it means he doesn't shave EITHER, and that's WHY no offerings
↓
📜 Baraita proof attempt: Only one difference stated — so shaving must be equal?
↓
🚫 Rejected: The equality is about flogging, not shaving
↓
📜 Leprosy baraita mentions 'whose hair is fit for shaving' in the cemetery — implying shaving IS required
Analysis The Gemara attempts multiple proofs but each is rejected. The leprosy baraita provides the strongest indication that shaving is required.
