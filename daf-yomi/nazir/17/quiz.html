QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Mar bar Rav Ashi, what is the actual dispute between R' Yochanan and Reish Lakish?
A. Whether the nazirite vow takes effect in a cemetery
B. Whether the nazirite is flogged for impurity
C. Whether the 7th day counts toward the tally
D. Whether the nazirite must bring offerings of impurity
Correct: B — Mar bar Rav Ashi reframes the dispute: both agree the vow takes effect. The disagreement is solely about whether the nazirite receives lashes (malkot) for remaining impure.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the ONE stated difference between an impure person who vowed naziriteship and a pure nazirite who became impure?
A. Only the pure nazirite must bring offerings of impurity
B. Only the impure vower is flogged
C. The 7th day of purification counts for the impure vower but not for the pure nazirite who became impure
D. The impure vower does not need to shave
Correct: C — The baraita explicitly states that the only difference is that for an impure person who vowed, the 7th day of purification counts toward his tally, while for a pure nazirite who became impure, it does not.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How is Reish Lakish conclusively refuted?
A. From the mishna stating days in the cemetery don't count
B. From a baraita comparing impure and pure nazirites
C. From a Tosefta stating an impure nazirite who drank wine or became impure incurs 40 lashes
D. From a kal vachomer argument about leprosy
Correct: C — Tosefta 2:14 explicitly states that an impure person who took a nazirite vow incurs 40 lashes for drinking wine, shaving, or becoming impure from a corpse — directly contradicting Reish Lakish's view that he is not flogged.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In Rava's dilemma about delayed exit, how does the nazirite enter the cemetery without becoming impure?
A. He enters blindfolded and unaware
B. He enters in a chest, box, or cabinet that doesn't contract impurity
C. He enters before sunrise when impurity doesn't apply
D. He enters with a protective amulet
Correct: B — Rava's scenario involves a nazirite carried into the cemetery inside a large wooden container (chest, box, or cabinet) that does not contract ritual impurity, thus shielding him. Someone then removes the lid, exposing him to impurity.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara use the case of a nazirite in a cemetery to prove that days of confirmed leprosy don't count toward the naziriteship tally?
A. Because leprosy and cemetery impurity have identical laws
B. Because the cemetery nazirite's hair is fit for shaving yet his days don't count — so leprosy days, where the hair is NOT fit for counting, certainly don't count (kal vachomer)
C. Because both require bringing two birds as offerings
D. Because both require a seven-day purification process
Correct: B — The kal vachomer argument reasons: if cemetery days don't count even though the hair IS fit for shaving, then leprosy days — where the hair is NOT fit for the nazirite's required shaving — should certainly not count toward the tally.
