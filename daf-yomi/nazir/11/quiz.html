Nazir 11 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why is an intoxicated person who says 'I am a nazirite and will refrain from it' NOT considered a nazirite?
A. Because intoxicated people cannot make valid legal declarations
B. Because the statement is interpreted as a konam — forbidding only that specific cup
C. Because the Sages automatically dissolve vows made while drunk
D. Because one must explicitly name all nazirite prohibitions to become a nazirite
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that an intoxicated person's statement is interpreted as meaning 'this cup is forbidden to me like an offering (konam)' — only that cup is forbidden, not all wine, so they are not a nazirite.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the intoxicated person use nazirite language instead of simply declaring the cup konam?
A. Because konam vows are not valid for wine
B. Because he fears they will bring him another cup if he only bans this one
C. Because the Torah requires nazirite language for all wine prohibitions
D. Because konam declarations require witnesses
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the person reasons: 'If I specify only this cup, they will bring me another and aggravate me. Better to use definitive language so they understand I want no more wine.'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the principle Ravina invokes regarding one who says 'I am a nazirite on condition I may drink wine'?
A. A vow that contradicts itself is automatically void
B. Conditions attached to vows must be stated twice to take effect
C. Anyone who stipulates counter to what is written in the Torah — his stipulation is void
D. Nazirite conditions are valid only if approved by a halakhic authority
Correct: C — Ravina applies the principle: כל המתנה על מה שכתוב בתורה תנאו בטל — one who stipulates against Torah law has a void stipulation, but the underlying naziriteship remains in force.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How do the Rabbis and R' Shimon differ on dissolving nazirite prohibitions?
A. Rabbis say dissolution is impossible; R' Shimon allows it for any single prohibition
B. Rabbis say dissolving one prohibition dissolves all; R' Shimon requires dissolving each one separately
C. Both agree all prohibitions must be dissolved together
D. Rabbis require a halakhic authority; R' Shimon allows self-dissolution
Correct: B — The Rabbis hold that since accepting one prohibition binds all, dissolving one releases all. R' Shimon holds that since all must be accepted, all must be dissolved — dissolving just one is insufficient.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When two people each vow to be nazirites and to 'shave a nazirite,' what is the clever (perspicacious) arrangement?
A. They each shave themselves and split the cost of offerings
B. They shave each other, so each fulfills the other's 'shave a nazirite' obligation at no extra cost
C. They find a third nazirite and split the cost of his offerings
D. They request dissolution of the shaving obligation from a halakhic authority
Correct: B — If each pays for the other's nazirite offerings (shaves the other), both fulfill their obligation to 'shave a nazirite' without incurring any additional expense beyond their own naziriteship.
