Nazirite Declarations: Intent, Conditions & Dissolution

↓
Is the person intoxicated?
Not intoxicated
Full nazirite — all prohibitions apply
Intoxicated
Not a nazirite — treated as a konam (offering-vow) on that cup only
Key Theme This daf explores how intent, conditions, and knowledge affect the validity of nazirite vows — and whether partial acceptance or partial dissolution is possible.

Offered Wine: Sober vs. Intoxicated

Someone pours a cup of wine for a person
↓
Person says: 'I am hereby a nazirite and will refrain from it'
↓
Is the person intoxicated?
Sober
✅ Full nazirite — all prohibitions in effect
Intoxicated
❌ Not a nazirite — statement means: 'This cup is konam (forbidden like an offering) to me'
↓
Why not say konam explicitly? Because he thinks: 'If I ban only this cup, they'll bring me another!'
Incident An intoxicated woman said these exact words. The Sages ruled she intended only to forbid that single cup, not to become a nazirite.

Conditional Naziriteship: Stipulating Against the Torah

Mishna
If one says: 'I am a nazirite on condition that I may drink wine and become impure from corpses' — he is a full nazirite, prohibited from everything.
Ravina
This is because he stipulates counter to the Torah (matneh al mah shekatuv baTorah). His stipulation is void but his naziriteship stands.
R' Yehoshua b. Levi
No — 'on condition' really means 'apart from'. He's accepting partial naziriteship from the start, not stipulating against full naziriteship.
Ravina
A Tosefta supports my reading: one who stipulates against Torah — his stipulation is void. He remains a full nazirite.
Key Principle כל המתנה על מה שכתוב בתורה תנאו בטל — Anyone who stipulates counter to what is written in the Torah, his stipulation is void.

Dissolution of Naziriteship: One Part vs. All

Rabbis (Chachamim)

  • Accepting ONE prohibition → bound by ALL
  • Dissolving ONE prohibition → ALL dissolved
  • Symmetry: acceptance and dissolution work the same way
  • Follow Shmuel: vows impeded by circumstances are dissolved automatically

R' Shimon

  • Must accept ALL prohibitions for naziriteship to take effect
  • Must dissolve ALL prohibitions — dissolving one is insufficient
  • Follows Rav Asi: must request dissolution from a halakhic authority
  • Remains a nazirite until formal dissolution of every prohibition
Background Four types of dissolved vows (Nedarim 20b): vows of exhortation, exaggeration (havai), unintentional vows, and vows impeded by circumstances beyond one's control.

Perspicacious Nazirites: Shaving Each Other

📢 Person A says: 'I am a nazirite AND it is incumbent upon me to shave (pay for) a nazirite'
↓
🙋 Person B hears and says: 'And I too — a nazirite, and I will shave a nazirite'
↓
🤔 Both are now nazirites AND each owes the cost of another nazirite's offerings
↓
💡 If perspicacious: they shave EACH OTHER — A pays for B's offerings, B pays for A's
↓
💰 Result: their 'shaving a nazirite' vow costs them nothing extra!
↓
😞 If not perspicacious: each must find and pay for a separate nazirite's offerings
Clever Solution By shaving each other, both fulfill their obligation to pay for another nazirite's offerings without any additional expense — a mutually beneficial arrangement.
