QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does R' Yochanan say all women are forbidden when an agent betroths an unspecified woman, but birds from other nests are permitted?
A. Women are more valuable than birds
B. Women are kavu'a (fixed in place) creating 50/50 doubt; birds move and follow the majority
C. There is a special Torah decree about women but not birds
D. The prohibition on women is only rabbinic while birds follow Torah law
Correct: B — R' Yochanan distinguishes between kavu'a (fixed) items, where doubt is 50/50, and parish (moving) items, where we follow the majority. Women return to their homes (fixed); birds roam freely (not fixed).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava, when does R' Yochanan concede that the man who sent the agent MAY marry a specific woman?
A. When the agent confirms he didn't betroth her relative
B. When the woman has no unmarried daughter, granddaughter, mother, grandmother, or sister
C. When the woman lives in a different city than the agent
D. When 30 days have passed since the agency appointment
Correct: B — Rava says R' Yochanan concedes when there are no available (unmarried) relatives who could have been betrothed by the agent. If relatives were married at the time of appointment, they are excluded because the agent can only act on matters present before him.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the dispute about 'shaving half a nazirite,' what does Rava say all agree upon?
A. The vow is always invalid
B. 'Half the offerings of a nazirite' = pays half; 'offerings of half a nazirite' = pays all
C. One always pays for a full nazirite
D. The vow needs to be released by a sage
Correct: B — Rava clarifies: if someone explicitly says 'half the offerings' — he pays half. If he says 'offerings of half a nazirite' — since there's no such thing as half a naziriteship, he pays all. The dispute is only about the ambiguous phrasing in the Mishna.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If someone vows 'I am a nazirite when I have a child (וָלָד)' and a tumtum is born, what is the halacha?
A. He is not a nazirite because the child's status is uncertain
B. He must ask a sage to release the vow
C. He is a nazirite, because 'child' includes any offspring
D. He observes naziriteship out of doubt
Correct: C — The Mishna distinguishes between 'son' (בן), which means specifically a male, and 'child' (ולד), which includes any offspring — daughter, tumtum, or androginos. Since he said 'child,' he is a nazirite.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why can't the Gemara prove from R' Yoshiya's ruling about a steward nullifying vows that an agent CAN act on future matters?
A. R' Yoshiya's ruling was overturned
B. R' Yoshiya may follow R' Eliezer, who holds the husband himself can nullify future vows — so the agent does only what the principal can do now
C. Vow nullification is different from betrothal by Torah decree
D. The steward is not considered a true agent
Correct: B — The Gemara suggests R' Yoshiya follows R' Eliezer, who says a husband CAN nullify future vows. So the steward-agent is only doing what the husband himself could do — not extending agency beyond present capability.
