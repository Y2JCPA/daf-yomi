Nazir 12 — Fixed Doubt, Agency Limits & Half a Nazirite

כל קבוע כמחצה על מחצה דמי
CORE THEME This daf explores when halachic doubt creates blanket prohibitions through the kavu'a (fixed location) principle, the limits of appointing an agent, and disputes about partial nazirite obligations.
3 / 9

Can Birds Be Forbidden Like Women?

R' Yochanan
If one tells an agent 'Go betroth a woman for me' without specifying which, he is forbidden to all women in the world — any could be a relative of the betrothed woman.
Reish Lakish
Objection from Kinnim: If a consecrated bird flew away, other people's birds are still fit for offerings. Why don't we say every bird in the world might be the consecrated one?
R' Yochanan
A woman is kavu'a — fixed in her home. Anything fixed is treated as 50/50 doubt. But a bird moves and is not fixed, so we follow the majority (most birds are unconsecrated).
Reish Lakish
But the agent could have found the woman in the marketplace — she was moving too!
R' Yochanan
A woman returns to her fixed home. A bird does not return to a fixed place. Therefore, the woman remains kavu'a.
KEY PRINCIPLE Kavu'a (כל קבוע): An item fixed in place creates 50/50 doubt. A moving item follows the majority.

Kavu'a vs. Parish — When Does Majority Apply?

An item of doubtful status is encountered
↓
Is the item fixed in place (קבוע)?
YES — Kavu'a
Treated as 50/50 doubt
e.g., a woman in her home → forbidden
NO — Parish (separated)
Follow the majority
e.g., a bird that flew away → permitted
APPLICATION This is why all women are forbidden (each is fixed at home) but birds from other nests are permitted (the lost bird moves freely among the majority).

Can an Agent Act on Future Possibilities?

Gemara
Challenge to Rava from our Mishna: The first person says 'I will shave a nazirite' — but the second person hasn't become a nazirite yet! This shows one can commit to future matters.
Gemara
He means: 'If I find someone' who becomes a nazirite, I will shave him. This is anticipating a future event — shouldn't agency work the same way?
Sages
No. A person appoints an agent only for what he himself can do now. He cannot betroth a married woman, so his agent cannot either — even if she later becomes divorced.
Gemara
Challenge from R' Yoshiya: A steward can nullify a wife's future vows — proving agency extends to future matters!
Gemara
R' Yoshiya follows R' Eliezer, who says the husband himself can nullify future vows. So the agent does only what the principal can do now.
RESOLUTION Why not nullify before traveling? He might forget, get angry, or be preoccupied — so he appoints an agent as a precaution.

Mishna: Shaving Half a Nazirite

Rabbi Meir

  • Each person shaves a whole nazirite
  • Once he says 'it is incumbent upon me' — full obligation kicks in
  • 'Half a nazirite' cannot retract the initial commitment
  • There is no such entity as half a naziriteship

The Rabbis

  • Each person shaves half a nazirite
  • They may combine to pay for one nazirite's offerings
  • The statement is a 'vow with its own opening' (נדר ופתחו עמו)
  • 'Half' clarifies his original intent — only partial obligation
RAVA'S CLARIFICATION All agree: 'Half the offerings' = pays half. 'Offerings of half a nazirite' = pays all (no half-naziriteship exists). They dispute only the ambiguous wording of the Mishna.
