Entering the Land of the Nations in an Enclosure

R' Yehuda HaNasi
One who enters the land of the nations in a chest, box, or cabinet is ritually impure
R' Yosei b'R' Yehuda
He is ritually pure — the enclosure protects him
Gemara
Multiple explanations are proposed: Is the decree about air or earth? Is a moving tent considered a tent? Or is it about the commonality of the practice?
Rav Ḥisda
A leper-nazirite who shaves for leprosy: only a short naziriteship requires recounting — a lengthy one continues
Core Issue The Gemara cycles through multiple frameworks to explain a single tannaitic dispute, revealing deep uncertainty about the basis of impurity in gentile lands

The Tannaitic Dispute: Chest in Foreign Lands

R' Yehuda HaNasi
One who enters ארץ העמים (land of the nations) in a chest, box, or cabinet is טמא (impure)
R' Yosei b'R' Yehuda
He is טהור (pure) — the enclosure shields him from impurity
Gemara
Can we say this reflects a deeper dispute about whether the decree concerns the air or the earth of foreign lands?
Background The Sages decreed that the land of the nations conveys ritual impurity. The question is: what exactly transmits the impurity — the air above it or the soil itself?

Attempt #1: Air vs. Earth Dispute

What is the source of impurity in the land of the nations?
R' Yehuda HaNasi
Decree is on the air — a chest cannot block it → Impure
R' Yosei b'R' Yehuda
Decree is on the earth — chest prevents overlying → Pure
↓
❌ Rejected — Gemara says both may agree the decree is on the earth
Note This initial explanation is rejected because the dispute may hinge on a different issue entirely

Attempt #2: Moving Tent (אהל מיטלטל)

R' Yosei b'R' Yehuda

  • Decree is on the earth
  • A moving tent IS called a tent
  • The chest shields from overlying impurity
  • Result: PURE

R' Yehuda HaNasi

  • Decree is on the earth
  • A moving tent is NOT called a tent
  • No valid separation from impurity
  • Result: IMPURE
Refuted! A baraita shows R' Yosei b'R' Yehuda holds a box thrown over a corpse does NOT protect — proving he agrees a moving tent is not a tent. This explanation fails.
