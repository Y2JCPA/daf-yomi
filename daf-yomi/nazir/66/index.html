Nazir 66 — Final Daf: Zav Impurity & Concluding Aggadah

כל בנייך למודי ה׳ — אל תקרי בנייך אלא בונייך
OVERVIEW The Gemara analyzes the impurity of a zav's semen — does it transmit tum'ah by carrying (masa) like ziva itself? Multiple tannaitic opinions are explored, along with a dispute about its duration. The tractate concludes with aggadic teachings about the primacy of blessing vs. amen and Torah scholars as builders of peace.
3 / 9

Does a Zav's Semen Impart Impurity by Carrying?

Gemara
The mishna says a zav's semen is impure. For what halakha? Even a pure man's semen imparts impurity by contact!
R' Eliezer
A zav's semen does NOT impart impurity by carrying (masa).
R' Yehoshua
It DOES impart impurity by carrying — because it inevitably contains droplets of ziva.
Gemara
Even R' Yehoshua only says this due to the ziva droplets, not the semen itself!
KEY QUESTION The mishna must be teaching something beyond mere contact-impurity — the novelty concerns carrying-impurity (tum'at masa).

Rav Adda bar Ahava: Attribution of Ziva to Semen

A zav experiences a seminal emission
↓
Within 24 hours, he sees a ziva-like discharge
↓
Do we attribute the discharge to weakness from the emission?
Normal Man
YES — discharge is attributed to semen; not counted as ziva
Confirmed Zav
NO — one does not attribute the ziva to the semen; it counts as another ziva discharge
RAV ADDA BAR AHAVA The mishna's novelty: for a confirmed zav, a discharge after seminal emission is still counted as ziva, not excused as weakness.
5 / 9

Rav Pappa vs. Rava: The Convert Who Becomes a Zav

Rav Pappa
A confirmed zav's later discharges aren't attributed to semen because he is already weakened from prior ziva.
Rava
But a convert who just converted can become a zav immediately — even with no prior ziva weakness!
Rav Pappa
There is no greater illness than the acceptance of mitzvot! The burden of mitzvot itself is like an illness that explains the discharge.
INSIGHT Kabbalat ha-mitzvot is so weighty that it is considered an illness sufficient to explain a subsequent discharge — a remarkable statement about the gravity of accepting Torah.

Duration of Carrying-Impurity for a Zav's Semen

OpinionDurationSource Verse
Tanna KammaFull 24-hour period after ziva"When evening comes on" — implies two evenings (Deut. 23:12)
R' YoseiOnly until end of that day"By reason of that which chances him by night" — only until nightfall (Deut. 23:11)
SHMUEL'S CONTRADICTION The dispute hinges on two verses in Deuteronomy 23 that Shmuel identified as contradictory — each tanna resolves the contradiction differently.

Blessing vs. Answering Amen — Who Is Greater?

Reciting the Blessing

  • Rav Huna & Rav Naḥman urge: Seize the cup — recite the blessing!
  • Baraita: Both are included in 'stand up and bless'
  • But one hurries to reward the one who recites first

Answering Amen

  • R' Yosei: The one who answers amen is greater
  • R' Nehorai confirms: By Heavens, it is so!
  • Parable: Assistants start the war, but the mighty prevail — amen is the mighty
TANNAITIC DISPUTE The Gemara concludes this is a dispute between tanna'im — no single resolution.
