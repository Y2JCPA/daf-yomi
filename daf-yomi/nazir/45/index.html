Nazir Daf 45: From Camp Boundaries to Completion

Abaye: Three camps correspond to areas of Jerusalem — who may enter each?
↓
Even a corpse may enter the Levite camp (Gate of Nicanor) — Joseph's bones prove this
↓
But one lacking atonement (mechusar kippurim) may not enter
↓
Mishna: After completing naziriteship — shave and bring 3 offerings
↓
Throw hair under the pot of the peace-offering and burn it
Key Theme This daf traces the nazirite's path from impurity restrictions through the completion ritual — shaving, burning hair, and the final offering that permits wine.

The Three Camps and Their Boundaries

Wilderness Camps

  • Camp of Israel — outermost
  • Camp of the Levites — middle
  • Camp of the Divine Presence — innermost

Jerusalem Equivalents

  • Jerusalem — Israelite camp
  • Temple Mount — Levite camp
  • Temple itself — Divine Presence

Who May Not Enter?

  • Corpse-impure → may enter Levite camp
  • Even a corpse itself may enter (Joseph's bones)
  • Lacking atonement → barred from Levite camp AND Divine Presence
Abaye's Proof Moses took Joseph's bones with him — into the Levite camp. So corpse-impurity alone doesn't bar entry; it's lacking atonement that does.

Why Can't the Impure Enter Before Day 8?

A zav who immersed — can he enter the Gate of Nicanor?
↓
Initial Suggestion
He is still impure and cannot enter the Levite camp
Abaye's Answer
He can enter in terms of impurity, but is barred because he lacks atonement
↓
Verse: "He shall come before the Lord to the entrance of the Tent of Meeting" (Lev. 15:14)
↓
Just as mechusar kippurim may not enter the camp of the Divine Presence, so too the Levite camp
Source "He shall be impure" — includes one who immersed that day. "His impurity is yet upon him" — includes one lacking atonement (Numbers 19:13).

When Does the Nazirite Shave?

R' Yehuda
The nazirite shaves after slaughtering the peace-offering (shelamim). The verse says "at the entrance to the Tent of Meeting" — this alludes to the peace-offering.
R' Elazar
He shaves after the sin-offering (ḥatat), because the sin-offering always comes first among offerings.
Both
If he shaved after any one of the three offerings, he has fulfilled his obligation bedi'avad (after the fact).
R' Shimon b. Gamliel
If he brought three animals without specifying which is for which: female sheep → sin-offering; male lamb → burnt-offering; ram → peace-offering.
Three Offerings The nazirite brings: (1) Ḥatat — female sheep, first year; (2) Olah — male sheep, first year; (3) Shelamim — ram (male sheep, over one year).

Female Nazirite: Where Does She Shave?

R' Shimon Shezuri
A female nazirite should not shave in the Temple courtyard — the young priests (pirḥei kehuna) may become aroused when she uncovers her hair.
A Sage
But a sota has her hair uncovered before God, and we don't worry about the priests being aroused by her!
R' Shimon Shezuri
The nazirite woman paints her eyes (koḥelet) and applies blush (fokeset) — she is beautified. The sota is deliberately made to look disheveled and wretched.
Distinction A nazirite woman appears beautified; a sota is intentionally humiliated. The concern about arousal applies only where the woman looks attractive.
