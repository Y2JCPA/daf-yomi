QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Abaye say a zav who immersed cannot enter the Gate of Nicanor before day 8?
A. Because he is still impure from the zav emission
B. Because he lacks atonement — he hasn't yet brought his offerings
C. Because the Gate of Nicanor is in the camp of the Divine Presence
D. Because the Torah prohibits all who immersed that day from entering any camp
Correct: B — Abaye explains that in terms of impurity, the immersed zav may enter the Levite camp. The prohibition is because he is mechusar kippurim — lacking atonement, as he hasn't yet sacrificed his offerings.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How do we know that even a corpse may be brought into the Levite camp?
A. From the verse about the Red Heifer being burned outside the camp
B. From Moses taking Joseph's bones 'with him' — within the Levite camp
C. From the law that corpse-impurity only lasts seven days
D. From the verse permitting the nazirite to shave at the Gate of Nicanor
Correct: B — The baraita derives from 'And Moses took the bones of Joseph with him' (Exodus 13:19) that 'with him' means within Moses's boundary — the Levite camp — proving even a corpse may enter there.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Yehuda, after which offering does the nazirite shave?
A. The sin-offering (ḥatat)
B. The burnt-offering (olah)
C. The peace-offering (shelamim)
D. Any of the three — there is no preference
Correct: C — R' Yehuda holds the nazirite shaves after the peace-offering. The verse says he shaves 'at the entrance to the Tent of Meeting,' which alludes to the peace-offering, as another verse uses the same phrase specifically for peace-offerings.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does R' Shimon Shezuri distinguish between a female nazirite and a sota regarding uncovering hair?
A. A nazirite is beautified with cosmetics; a sota is deliberately made to look wretched
B. A nazirite shaves completely; a sota only loosens her hair
C. A nazirite is in the courtyard; a sota is inside the Sanctuary
D. A nazirite is voluntary; a sota is being punished
Correct: A — R' Shimon Shezuri explains that a female nazirite paints her eyes (koḥelet) and applies blush (fokeset), making her attractive, so young priests might be aroused. A sota is made to look disheveled, so there is no such concern.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What two laws does the Gemara derive from the phrase 'the sacrifice of the peace-offering' (Numbers 6:18)?
A. That the hair must be shaved before the offering, and that a woman may not shave in the courtyard
B. That gravy (rotev) must be poured on the hair, and that the sin-offering/guilt-offering pots also suffice
C. That the peace-offering must come last, and that the hair must be completely burned
D. That the offering must be cooked in a pot, and that the nazirite must wave the foreleg
Correct: B — Rava derives two things: (1) 'sacrifice of' teaches that part of the sacrifice — the gravy — must be placed on the hair; (2) 'sacrifice' broadens the law to include the sin-offering and guilt-offering pots, not just the peace-offering.
