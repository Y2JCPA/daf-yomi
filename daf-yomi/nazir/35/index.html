How Do We Read 'From Pits to Grape Skin'?

Verse: "From pits to grape skin" (Numbers 6:4)
↓
Rabbis (Frat-Klal-Frat)
Serves as a detail in the hermeneutical structure — limits the generalization to items similar to the detail
R' Elazar ben Azarya
Teaches that a nazirite is liable only if he eats two grape seeds (ḥartzanim) and one grape skin (zag)
↓
Gemara reconciles: The verse serves both purposes — the placement after the generalization teaches frat-klal-frat, and the singular/plural forms teach R' Elazar ben Azarya's halakha
Key Question The sugya explores the foundations of two major hermeneutical systems and how they produce different results for nazirite prohibitions

How Does R' Elazar ben Azarya Read the Verse?

Gemara
According to R' Elazar ben Azarya, "from pits to grape skin" teaches liability only for two seeds and a skin. So how does he derive frat-klal-frat?
Answer 1
He holds like R' Elazar, who uses ribui u'mi'ut (restriction and amplification) — he doesn't need frat-klal-frat at all
Answer 2
He agrees with the Rabbis that it is frat-klal-frat. The phrase's placement after the generalization teaches the method, and the singular/plural forms teach his halakha
Textual Proof If the Torah only meant to teach R' Elazar ben Azarya's rule, it would have placed 'pits and skin' alongside the other details of wine and vinegar — not after the generalization

Singular vs. Plural — R' Elazar ben Azarya's Proof

TermFormImplication
חרצנים (Ḥartzanim)Plural — pitsAt least two grape seeds
זג (Zag)Singular — skinOnly one grape skin
Expected patternBoth plural or both singularWould indicate no special halakha
Actual textPlural + SingularTeaches minimum measure: 2 seeds + 1 skin
Key Derivation The mismatch between plural 'ḥartzanim' and singular 'zag' is not accidental — it establishes the minimum quantity for nazirite liability

Where Does R' Elazar Learn Frat-Klal-Frat?

R' Abbahu's Source

  • From the bailees verse (Exodus 22:9)
  • Detail: 'donkey, ox, sheep'
  • Generalization: 'or any animal'
  • Detail: 'to guard'
  • → Only items similar to detail: things that can be guarded

Rava's Source

  • From the offerings verse (Leviticus 1:10)
  • Detail: 'if his offering is from'
  • Generalization: 'the flock'
  • Detail: 'sheep' and 'goats'
  • → Only items similar to detail: animals that copulated with a person may not be offered
Why Not Leviticus 1:2? Rav Yehuda of Diskarta challenged Rava: derive it from 'from animals, from the herd or flock'! Rava responded: 'animals' there includes undomesticated animals in the generalization, so the method cannot be properly demonstrated

Three Hermeneutical Methods Compared

Generalization → Detail
The generalization is added to the detail — everything is included
↓
Generalization → Detail → Generalization
Only items similar to the detail in one aspect are included
↓
Detail → Generalization → Detail
Only items similar to the detail in two aspects are included (more restrictive)
↓
Restriction & Amplification
Includes more than klal-frat but less than frat-klal — e.g., tendrils yes, leaves no
Critical Distinction With two generalizations (klal-frat-klal), similarity in ONE aspect suffices. With two details (frat-klal-frat), similarity in TWO aspects is required — the extra detail creates a more restrictive filter
