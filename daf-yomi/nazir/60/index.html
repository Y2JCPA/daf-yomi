Year-Long Nazir with Uncertain Status: The Four-Shaving Process

Year-long nazirite who might be a metzora (leper) and might be tamei (impure)
↓
1st shaving after 1 year — brings leper's birds + bird sin-offering + animal burnt-offering
↓
2nd shaving after 2 years — bird sin-offering + animal burnt-offering → may eat kodashim
↓
3rd shaving after 3 years — in case first two were for leprosy, now counts naziriteship
↓
4th shaving after 4 years — offering of purity with stipulation → may drink wine & touch dead
Key Principle When status is uncertain, every possible obligation must be covered through repeated shavings and conditional offerings

Short vs. Long Naziriteship with Uncertain Status

Short Nazir (30 Days)

  • Eats sacrificial food after 60 days
  • Drinks wine after 120 days
  • Four shavings over ~120 days
  • Each shaving period: 30 days

Year-Long Nazir

  • Eats sacrificial food after 2 years
  • Drinks wine after 4 years
  • Four shavings over 4 years
  • Each shaving period: 1 year
Why the Difference? Each uncertain shaving must wait the full naziriteship period — 30 days for a standard nazir, one year for a year-long nazir

First Shaving: Covering All Possibilities

After 1st shaving — what is this person's true status?
Definite Leper, Not Impure
Birds = leprosy obligation
Bird sin-offering → burial (unneeded)
Burnt-offering = gift
Not Leper, Impure from Dead
Birds = no transgression (outside Temple)
Bird sin-offering = impurity obligation
Burnt-offering = gift
Neither Leper nor Impure
Birds = no transgression
Bird sin-offering → burial
Burnt-offering = nazir obligation
Why No Transgression? The leper's bird ritual is performed outside the Temple, so unnecessary performance does not violate the prohibition against bringing non-sacred animals into the courtyard

Four Cases: Certain & Uncertain Combinations

📋 Case 1: Uncertain impurity + Definite leper → eats kodashim after 8 days, wine after 67 days
↓
📋 Case 2: Uncertain leprosy + Definite impurity → eats kodashim after 37 days, wine after 74 days
↓
📋 Case 3: Definite impurity + Definite leprosy → eats kodashim after 8 days, wine after 44 days
↓
⚡ When both statuses are certain, the timeline is shortest — no extra shavings needed for uncertainty
Counterintuitive Insight Having definite impurity and leprosy actually results in a shorter waiting period than uncertain cases, because there is no need for extra shavings to cover doubt

R' Shimon ben Yoḥai: Can One Shaving Count for Both?

Students of R' Shimon b. Yoḥai
Can a pure nazirite who is also a leper shave once and have it count for both?
R' Shimon ben Yoḥai
No! A nazir shaves to remove hair, but a leper shaves to grow hair (for the second shaving). The purposes are opposite.
Students
Fine — but let it count for the leper's second shaving (after days of counting), which is also to remove hair!
R' Shimon ben Yoḥai
A leper shaves before sprinkling of blood; a nazir shaves after sprinkling. They differ in timing.
Students
Then let it count for leprosy and impure naziriteship — both are before sprinkling!
R' Shimon ben Yoḥai
Still no! An impure nazir shaves after immersion; a leper shaves before immersion. Every combination has a distinguishing difference.
Final Distinction Each type of shaving differs in at least one dimension: purpose (remove vs. grow), timing relative to sprinkling, or timing relative to immersion
