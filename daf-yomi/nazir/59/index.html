Is Male Hair Removal a Torah Violation?

R' Yochanan
A man who removes armpit or pubic hair is flogged — it violates lo yilbash gever simlat isha (Deut. 22:5).
Gemara
Objection: A baraita teaches hair removal is only a rabbinic prohibition, not Torah law!
Gemara
R' Yochanan follows a different tanna who holds it is a Torah violation of lo yilbash.
First Tanna
The verse doesn't teach about hair removal — it prohibits a man wearing women's clothes to sit among women, and vice versa. The word to'evah (abomination) implies deception, not mere grooming.
KEY DISPUTE The tannaitic debate hinges on whether lo yilbash covers grooming practices or only cross-dressing for deceptive mingling.

Two Readings of 'Lo Yilbash' (Deut. 22:5)

First Tanna (Rabbinic Hair Removal)

  • Verse prohibits cross-dressing to infiltrate the opposite gender's spaces
  • Man dresses as woman → sits among women
  • Woman dresses as man → sits among men
  • Hair removal is only rabbinic
  • The word to'evah implies deceptive intent

R' Eliezer ben Yaakov (Broad Reading)

  • Man may not adorn with women's cosmetics/ornaments
  • Woman may not go out with weapons to war
  • Covers both appearance and function
  • Hair removal = Torah violation (R' Yochanan follows this)
  • Gender-specific items define the prohibition
R' YOCHANAN'S VIEW R' Yochanan rules that male hair removal is a Torah-level violation, following R' Eliezer ben Yaakov's broader reading of the verse.

R' Yehoshua's Solution: When One Nazirite Dies

Two nazirites — uncertain which is impure. One dies before offerings.
↓
Surviving nazirite finds a volunteer in the marketplace to become a nazirite
↓
He tells the volunteer: 'If I was impure — you are a nazirite now; if I was pure — you are a nazirite in 30 days'
↓
Both count 30 days, bring impurity + purity offerings
↓
They assign offerings conditionally: 'If I'm impure, impurity offering is mine…'
↓
Count another 30 days, bring a second purity offering to cover remaining possibility
BEN ZOMA'S OBJECTION Who will agree to become a nazirite for a stranger? There must be a simpler way!

Ben Zoma vs. R' Yehoshua: Offerings in Stages

Ben Zoma
No need for a partner! After 30 days, bring a bird sin-offering and animal burnt-offering. Assign conditionally: if impure, sin-offering is obligatory; if pure, burnt-offering is obligatory.
Ben Zoma
Count another 30 days, bring a second purity offering. Assign whichever burnt-offering wasn't used as a gift offering.
R' Yehoshua
Your solution means the nazirite brings his offerings in halves — the burnt-offering 30 days before the rest!
Shmuel
R' Yehoshua only said this to sharpen the students' minds (leḥadded). He didn't truly object.
Rav Naḥman
What will R' Yehoshua do with the intestines of the animals — let them spoil for 30 days? His own method has the same problem!
HALAKHA The Rabbis agreed with Ben Zoma. A nazirite may bring offerings in stages when uncertainty requires it.
