Nazir 44 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, which nazirite prohibition is NEVER exempted under any circumstances?
A. Ritual impurity from a corpse
B. Shaving the hair
C. Products of the vine
D. All three are equally absolute
Correct: C — The Mishna teaches that wine/vine products have no exceptions, while impurity is exempted for a met mitzva and shaving is exempted for a metzora's purification.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara need a specific verse to permit a nazirite to become impure for a met mitzva?
A. Because a kal va'chomer from wine would forbid it — wine is never exempted, so impurity shouldn't be either
B. Because the Torah explicitly forbids it in all cases
C. Because Rabbi Akiva objected to the exemption
D. Because a met mitzva is only a rabbinic obligation
Correct: A — The Gemara presents a kal va'chomer: if wine (which doesn't negate naziriteship) is never exempted, then impurity (which does negate it) surely shouldn't be exempted. A verse is needed to override this logical argument.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the core disagreement between R' Akiva and R' Tarfon regarding an impure nazirite who shaves on day 8?
A. Whether shaving is required at all
B. Whether offerings can be brought the same day as shaving or must wait until the next day
C. Whether the nazirite must restart his entire term
D. Whether sprinkling is needed on both day 3 and day 7
Correct: B — R' Akiva says the nazirite can bring offerings on day 8 (same day as shaving) because his purification depends on days, not shaving. R' Tarfon compares it to a metzora who must wait until the next day.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rava reconcile Hillel the Amora's baraita (offerings on day 9) with R' Akiva's ruling (offerings on day 8)?
A. Hillel follows R' Tarfon exclusively
B. The baraita refers to a nazirite who did NOT immerse on day 7, so he must wait an extra day
C. R' Akiva later retracted his position
D. The baraita is about a metzora, not a nazirite
Correct: B — Rava explains that R' Akiva's case assumes immersion on day 7 (so the nazirite is pure on day 8), while Hillel's baraita concerns one who didn't immerse until day 8, requiring him to wait for sunset — pushing offerings to day 9.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did the Elders rule that Rabbi Yitzḥak the priest could NOT become impure to rebury his father after three years?
A. Because reburial is forbidden by Torah law
B. Because the body was certainly no longer whole after three years, and 'for his father' implies wholeness
C. Because Rabbi Yitzḥak was a nazirite in addition to being a priest
D. Because Ginzak was outside the Land of Israel
Correct: B — The verse 'for his father' is interpreted to mean a priest may become impure only when the father's body is whole (shalem). After three years, the body was certainly decomposed, so the exemption no longer applied.
