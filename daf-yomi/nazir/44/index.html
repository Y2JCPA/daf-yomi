The Three Nazirite Prohibitions: A Web of Stringencies

Impurity (Tum'ah)

  • Negates ALL days of naziriteship
  • Requires bringing an offering
  • BUT exempted for met mitzva

Shaving (Tiglachat)

  • Negates only 30 days
  • No offering required
  • Exempted for metzora purification

Wine / Vine Products

  • Does NOT negate naziriteship
  • No offering required
  • NEVER exempted — no exceptions
Key Insight Each prohibition is both stricter AND more lenient than the others in different respects — no single prohibition is universally the most severe.

Priest's Impurity for Incomplete Remains

לאביו — כשהוא שלם ולא כשהוא חסר
Background A baraita teaches that a priest becomes impure for close relatives even to bury a spine, skull, or most of the skeleton. But Rav holds a priest may not become impure for a relative whose body is not whole.

An incident: Rabbi Yitzḥak the priest's father died in Ginzak. After three years, Rabbi Yehoshua ben Elisha and four Elders ruled he could not become impure — the body was certainly no longer whole.

Kal Va'chomer Chain: Why Can't We Derive Laws Between Prohibitions?

Challenge 1: Wine is never exempted → Shouldn't impurity also never be exempted?
↓
Verse: "For his father or mother… he shall not become impure" — implies: for them no, but for met mitzva, yes
↓
Challenge 2: Impurity is exempted → Shouldn't wine also be exempted?
↓
Verse: "He shall abstain from wine and strong drink" — obligatory wine is like optional wine (no exceptions)
↓
Challenge 3: Wine is never exempted → Shouldn't it negate naziriteship too?
↓
Verse: "For his naziriteship was rendered impure" — only impurity negates, not wine
Method Each kal va'chomer argument is blocked by a specific verse — demonstrating that the unique status of each prohibition is Biblically mandated, not logically derivable.

R' Akiva vs R' Tarfon: When Can an Impure Nazirite Bring Offerings?

Mishna
An impure nazirite is sprinkled on days 3 and 7, shaves on day 7, and brings offerings on day 8.
R' Akiva
If he shaved on day 8 instead, he brings offerings that same day (day 8). His purification depends on his days — he immersed on day 7, so he's already pure.
R' Tarfon
What's the difference between this and a metzora (leper)? A metzora who shaves on day 8 brings offerings on day 9, not the same day!
R' Akiva
The cases differ: a nazirite's purification depends on days (immersion on 7th). A metzora's purification depends on shaving — he must immerse again and wait for sunset.
Key Distinction Nazirite purification = counting days (immersion on day 7 suffices). Metzora purification = shaving triggers a new immersion requirement, pushing offerings to the next day.

Rava's Resolution: Did R' Tarfon Accept R' Akiva's View?

Hillel the Amora taught: If a nazirite shaved on day 8, he brings offerings on day 9
↓
This seems to follow R' Tarfon, not R' Akiva — did R' Tarfon reject R' Akiva?
↓
Immersed on Day 7
Already pure on day 8 → Can bring offerings same day as shaving (R' Akiva's case in Mishna)
Did NOT Immerse on Day 7
Immerses on day 8 → Must wait for sunset → Offerings on day 9 (Hillel's baraita)
Rava's Answer R' Tarfon may have accepted R' Akiva's view. The baraita of Hillel refers to a nazirite who failed to immerse on day 7 — so he must wait an extra day, which even R' Akiva would agree to.
