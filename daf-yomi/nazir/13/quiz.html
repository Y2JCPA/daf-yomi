Nazir 13 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yehuda, if a man vows naziriteship 'when I have a son' and his wife miscarries, what is the ruling?
A. He is not a nazirite — uncertain naziriteship is ruled leniently
B. He is a nazirite — we assume the fetus was viable
C. He must observe a voluntary naziriteship as a precaution
D. He must wait to see if the wife gives birth again before deciding
Correct: A — R' Yehuda holds that in cases of uncertain naziriteship, we rule leniently (l'kula). Since the viability of the miscarried fetus is uncertain, he is not a nazirite.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What practical difference does R' Abba's unresolved question (teyku) about offerings after a miscarriage address?
A. Whether the man must observe naziriteship at all
B. Whether the animals may be sheared or used for labor
C. Whether the offerings must be brought to the Temple
D. Whether the wife's next child triggers the vow
Correct: B — The practical question is about shearing and labor: if the offerings were already consecrated from the initial separation, they cannot be sheared or used for work. If not yet consecrated, they may be used.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If a man says 'I am a nazirite when I have a son' and a daughter is born, what is the ruling?
A. He is a nazirite — any child fulfills the vow
B. He is not a nazirite — he specified a son (ben)
C. He must observe a voluntary naziriteship
D. It depends on whether the daughter is his firstborn
Correct: B — The mishna teaches that 'son' (ben) is taken literally. A daughter, tumtum, or hermaphrodite does not fulfill the condition. The Gemara rejects the idea that 'ben' means 'being built up' by any child.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the mishna's Case 2 — 'I am a nazirite when I have a son, and I am also a nazirite now' — what happens when a son is born mid-term?
A. He completes his current naziriteship first, then counts the son's term
B. He pauses his current term, counts the son's naziriteship, then resumes his own
C. Both naziriteships are fulfilled simultaneously
D. He must restart both naziriteships from the beginning
Correct: B — Because the son's naziriteship was vowed first, it takes priority when triggered. He pauses his personal naziriteship, completes the son's term, then resumes and completes his own.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When someone hears a nazirite vow and responds 'And I' (ve-ani), what is the unresolved question?
A. Whether he must bring the same offerings as the first person
B. Whether his statement means he'll be a nazirite when the first person's son is born, or when his own son is born
C. Whether he can retract the statement within 30 days
D. Whether his naziriteship is 30 days or the same length as the first person's
Correct: B — The Gemara cannot determine whether 'And I' means solidarity with the first person's vow (nazirite when YOUR son is born) or an independent vow about his own future child. This remains teyku.
