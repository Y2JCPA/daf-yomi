Nazirite Vows: Miscarriage, Uncertainty & Overlapping Terms

Man vows: "I am a nazirite when I have a son"
↓
Wife miscarries — was the fetus viable?
R' Yehuda: Lenient
Not a nazirite — doubt resolved leniently
R' Shimon: Stringent
Must say: "If viable → obligatory; if not → voluntary"
↓
Wife later gives birth to a live son — now certainly a nazirite
Core Tension This daf explores how uncertainty affects nazirite vows — from miscarriage doubts, to ambiguous bystander declarations, to overlapping terms of naziriteship.

Miscarriage: Is He a Nazirite?

Tanna Kamma
If his wife miscarried, he is not a nazirite — she did not give birth to a live child.
R' Shimon
Since the fetus might have been viable, he should hedge: "If viable — obligatory nazirite; if not — voluntary nazirite."
Gemara
The Tanna Kamma follows R' Yehuda: in cases of uncertain naziriteship, we rule leniently (l'kula).
R' Shimon
And if she later gives birth again, he must accept an additional naziriteship to cover both possibilities.
Key Principle R' Yehuda: Uncertain naziriteship → lenient. R' Shimon: Uncertain naziriteship → stringent. This dispute parallels the heap of wheat case.

R' Abba's Question: Offerings After Miscarriage

Man vows naziriteship "when I have a son"
↓
Wife miscarries → he separates offerings (per R' Yehuda: not required, but he did)
↓
Wife gives birth to a live son → he is now certainly a nazirite
↓
Are the previously separated offerings already consecrated?
If Consecrated
Prohibited to shear their wool or use them for labor
If Not Consecrated
Permitted to use them — must re-consecrate for the naziriteship
Teyku The Gemara finds no resolution — the dilemma stands (teyku). The practical difference: may one shear or work the animals in the interim?

"When I Have a Son" — What Counts as a Son?

"When I have a child (valad)"✅ Is a naziriteAny child fulfills the vow, including tumtum & hermaphrodite
Potential objectionMaybe ben means 'built up' by any child?Mishna rejects this — ben means son literally
Potential objectionMaybe valad means only a 'significant' child?Mishna rejects this — any child counts
Hermeneutic Point The Gemara justifies seemingly obvious rulings: each case prevents a mistaken interpretation of the vow language.

Ben Reḥumi's Inquiry: What Does "And I" Mean?

Interpretation A: About Himself

  • "And I" = I will be a nazirite when I have a son
  • He echoes the form of the vow, not the trigger
  • Applies to his own future child

Interpretation B: About the First Person

  • "And I" = I too will be a nazirite when your son is born
  • He is expressing solidarity and love
  • Especially if said face-to-face — embarrassment factor
Teyku The Gemara explores variations: "And it is incumbent upon me" vs. "And I" vs. saying it about a third party. All remain unresolved.

Overlapping Nazirite Terms: Order of Counting

📋 Case 1: "I am a nazirite now, and also when I have a son"
↓
✅ Son born mid-term → complete his own naziriteship first, then count son's term
↓
📋 Case 2: "I am a nazirite when I have a son, and I am also a nazirite now"
↓
⏸️ Son born mid-term → pause his own naziriteship, count son's term, then resume
↓
🔑 Reason: The son's naziriteship in Case 2 was vowed first, so it has priority when triggered
Mishna Rule The order of the vows determines which naziriteship takes priority when a son is born during an active term.
