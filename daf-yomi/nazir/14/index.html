Nazir 14: Overlapping Vows, Impurity & Negation

Opening Inquiry: Can the first 20 days of naziriteship count toward a longer vow when interrupted by a second vow?
↓
Tosefta resolves: 20 days first vow → 30 days second vow → 80 days to complete first vow
↓
Core Dispute: If one becomes tamei during the son's naziriteship term, what happens to the earlier counted days?
R' Yochanan
Negates all days — it is one long naziriteship
Reish Lakish
Only negates the son's term — each naziriteship is discrete
Key Theme When multiple naziriteship obligations overlap, how do impurity and interruption affect the total count?

Impurity During Son's Naziriteship Term

Mishna Context
One vows naziriteship for a son's birth plus a standard term. He pauses the standard term when the son is born and begins the son's term.
R' Yochanan
If he becomes tamei during the son's term, it negates everything — including the days counted for his own term.
R' Yochanan
Reason: It is one long naziriteship (nezirut achat arukkah). Impurity resets the entire count.
Reish Lakish
It does not negate the days of his own naziriteship — only those of the son's term are lost.
Reish Lakish
Reason: His own naziriteship is discrete (nezirut bifnei atzmah) and the son's is discrete. Two separate obligations.
Core Dispute Are the paused personal naziriteship and the son's naziriteship one continuous period or two discrete ones?

Parallel Case: Leprosy + Corpse Impurity

R' Yochanan

  • A nazir contracts leprosy, then touches a corpse during leprosy
  • Corpse impurity NEGATES all prior naziriteship days
  • Reason: He is still in a period of naziriteship even as a leper
  • Leprosy pauses but does not end naziriteship

Reish Lakish

  • Same case: nazir contracts leprosy, then touches a corpse
  • Corpse impurity does NOT negate prior naziriteship days
  • Reason: Leprosy is discrete, naziriteship is discrete
  • After purification, he completes only the remaining days
Why Both Cases? The Gemara explains both cases are necessary: from the son's case alone, one might think R' Yochanan only rules strictly when both periods are naziriteship; from the leprosy case alone, one might think Reish Lakish only rules leniently when the periods are fundamentally different.

Impurity During the Hair Growth Period

Setup
A nazir contracted leprosy, was purified, and shaved. He then waits 30 days for hair growth before shaving for naziriteship. He becomes tamei during this period.
Rav
It does not negate his prior naziriteship days.
Gemara
Even R' Yochanan would agree here — the hair growth period is merely an addition, not part of the naziriteship term itself.
Shmuel
It does negate his earlier counted days.
Gemara
Even Reish Lakish would agree here — the hair growth completes one naziriteship, unlike the son's case which involves two distinct terms.
Twist Rav and Shmuel each align with the opposite side of the R' Yochanan/Reish Lakish dispute, because the hair growth period has a unique status.
