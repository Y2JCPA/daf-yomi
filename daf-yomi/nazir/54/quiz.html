Nazir 54 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the baraita's interpretation of Numbers 19:18, what does 'or the slain' (חלל) refer to?
A. A person killed in battle
B. A limb severed from a living person without enough flesh to heal
C. A limb severed from a corpse
D. A grave that predates the Torah
Correct: B — The baraita interprets 'the slain' (חלל) as a limb severed from a living person that does not contain enough flesh for it to heal, distinguishing it from 'the dead' which refers to a limb from a corpse.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does R' Yochanan counter Reish Lakish's proof that a nazirite shaves for a boneless limb from a corpse?
A. He argues the verse is not authoritative
B. He says the limb does contain a barley-grain bone, and the verse's novelty teaches about carrying, not contact
C. He agrees but says it applies only to a kohen
D. He says the verse refers only to Shabbat restrictions
Correct: B — R' Yochanan argues the limb does contain a barley-grain bone. Since contact impurity for a bone is already stated, the verse about the limb teaches the new law that carrying it also imparts impurity — eliminating Reish Lakish's proof.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
The Gemara concludes that the mishna's phrase 'until he is purified' follows whose opinion?
A. R' Eliezer — counting resumes after sunset on day 7
B. The Rabbis — counting resumes only after bringing offerings on day 8
C. Reish Lakish — counting resumes immediately
D. R' Akiva — counting resumes after immersion
Correct: B — The Gemara resolves the dilemma by noting that the next mishna uses different language for one who needs no offering. This implies the first clause means 'until he brings offerings' — following the Rabbis who require offerings before restarting.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject the proof that land of the nations was decreed impure due to its earth (not air)?
A. Because the verse explicitly mentions air
B. Because the sprinkling rule in the mishna may refer to other listed items like grave cover and blood, not to land of the nations
C. Because Reish Lakish ruled it is due to air
D. Because land outside Israel has no sanctity at all
Correct: B — The Gemara shows that the sprinkling clause doesn't apply to every item in the mishna — vessels touching a corpse, also listed, only require waiting until evening, not sprinkling. So the sprinkling may refer to other items, not proving earth-based impurity for land of the nations.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What happens to a nazirite's previously counted days when he becomes impure from a beit haperas?
A. All previous days are negated and he must restart
B. Only the last 7 days are negated
C. The days are NOT negated — he resumes counting the remainder after purification
D. He must add 30 additional days as a penalty
Correct: C — Beit haperas is among the 'lesser' impurities listed in the mishna of Nazir 54. For these sources, the nazirite does not shave, does not negate previous days, and does not bring an offering of impurity — he simply completes the remaining days after purification.
