Listed in This Mishna

Sealed Grave: Impurity Breaks Through

טומאה בוקעת ועולה בוקעת ויורדת
Principle Ritual impurity in a sealed grave (קבר סתום) breaks through and ascends, breaks through and descends — the grave imparts impurity like the corpse itself.

This means even without an opening, the tumah radiates both upward through the earth and downward beneath the grave.

Does a Nazirite Shave for a Limb from a Corpse?

Reish Lakish
The verse lists a limb from a corpse separately from a bone. If it already has a barley-grain bone, it's redundant! So it must refer to a limb without such a bone — and the Torah still includes it. A nazirite shaves for it.
R' Yochanan
Actually, it does contain a barley-grain bone. The verse's novelty is about carrying (masa), not contact. Since contact is already taught for a bone, the limb verse teaches that carrying it also imparts impurity.
Reish Lakish
My reading proves a nazirite shaves even for a limb without a full-sized bone — the Torah explicitly includes it as a source of impurity!
R' Yochanan
No — the verse is not needed for contact, so apply it to carrying. No proof for your position on nazirite shaving.
Stakes This dispute determines whether a nazirite must restart his entire term for contact with a boneless limb from a corpse

When Does a Nazirite Resume Counting After Impurity?

Nazirite becomes impure from a listed source and undergoes seven days of purification
↓
What does the Mishna mean by "until he is purified"?
R' Eliezer
Means: until sunset of day 7
Counting resumes on day 8, even before bringing offerings
Rabbis
Means: until he brings his offerings on day 8
Counting resumes only after sacrifice
↓
Gemara resolves: The next mishna says one without an offering counts immediately after 7 days → the first clause means after offerings = Rabbis' view
Resolution The Mishna follows the Rabbis: naziriteship of purity does not commence until the eighth day after bringing offerings

Impurities: Shaving Required vs. Not Required

Must Shave (Previous Mishna)

  • Corpse (met)
  • Olive-bulk of a corpse
  • Olive-bulk of netzel
  • Skull, spine, majority of bones
  • Full spoonful of grave dust
  • Sealed grave

No Shaving (This Mishna)

  • Hanging branches (s'khakhot)
  • Projecting stones (pera'ot)
  • Beit haperas (plowed grave area)
  • Land of the nations
  • Grave cover & grave walls
  • ¼ log blood / ¼ kav bones
  • Vessels touching a corpse
Consequence For the second category: no negation of previous days, no offering required — the nazirite simply completes the remaining days after purification
