Nazir 30: Using a Father's Nazirite Offerings

Core Question When a nazirite father dies leaving unallocated money for his offerings, can his son use that money to complete his own naziriteship? The Mishna rules yes for a son but not a daughter — and the Gemara explores why, revealing a halakha l'Moshe miSinai that operates independently of inheritance law.

Mishna: Who Can Shave with Father's Nazirite Money?

Son ✓

  • A son may shave (bring closing offerings) using money his nazirite father set aside
  • This applies even though the money was unallocated among the three offerings
  • Based on a halakha l'Moshe miSinai

Daughter ✗

  • A daughter may not use her father's nazirite money
  • Even if she is the sole heir and inherits his entire estate
  • The rule is not based on inheritance — it is a special transmitted tradition
R' Yochanan This distinction is a halakha l'Moshe miSinai — a tradition transmitted to Moses at Sinai regarding nazirites, independent of general inheritance law.

R' Yosei vs. the Rabbis: When Can the Son Use the Money?

R' Yosei
If the father died and then the son vowed naziriteship, the son can use the money.
R' Yosei
But if both were nazirites during the father's lifetime and the father died, the money goes to communal gift offerings (nedavah).
R' Eliezer, R' Meir & R' Yehuda
The exact opposite: if both were nazirites during the father's lifetime, that is when the son can use the money.
R' Eliezer, R' Meir & R' Yehuda
If the son vowed only after the father's death, the money goes to nedavah.
Total Disagreement The Rabbis disagree with R' Yosei on both clauses — wherever he permits, they forbid, and vice versa.
