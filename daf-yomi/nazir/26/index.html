Allocated vs. Unallocated Nazirite Funds

A nazirite separates money for offerings (sin, burnt, peace)
↓
Did the nazirite specify which money is for which offering?
Yes — Allocated
Sin-offering money → Dead Sea
Burnt-offering money → burnt-offering
Peace-offering money → peace-offering
No — Unallocated
All money goes to communal gift burnt-offerings (nedavah)
Core Principle The fate of a deceased nazirite's offering funds depends entirely on whether the money was allocated (mefaresh) or unallocated (stam).

Nazirite Funds vs. Separate Obligations

Nazirite / Nests (Same Obligation)

  • All offerings stem from one obligation (naziriteship)
  • If funds are unallocated → communal gift offerings (nedavah)
  • Owner who became wealthy may redesignate funds
  • Can supplement money to upgrade from birds to animals

Sin-Offering + Vow (Separate Obligations)

  • Sin-offering and burnt-offering are independent obligations
  • Ambiguous designation: 'These are for my obligatory offering'
  • Cannot bring either a sin-offering or burnt-offering with them
  • If owner dies → money cast into the Dead Sea
Key Distinction Unallocated funds go to nedavah only when all offerings share a single obligation. When obligations are separate, ambiguous funds are destroyed.

How Broadly Does 'Allocated' Extend?

Rav Ashi (Version 1)
Even saying 'These are for my sin-offering and for my burnt-offering and for my peace-offering' in one statement counts as allocated — not just separate designations.
Rav Ashi (Version 2)
Even saying 'These are for my obligation' (b'chovi) — the broadest possible language — is considered allocated!
Rava
If the sin-offering money fell out and separated from the rest, the remaining money is now considered allocated — half for burnt-offering, half for peace-offering.
Practical Effect Rav Ashi and Rava both expand what counts as allocated, making it harder for funds to qualify as fully unallocated (and thus go to nedavah).

Animals vs. Money: What Counts as Allocated?

A nazirite designates items for offerings and then dies
↓
What form are the designated items?
💰 Mixed Coins
Unallocated
→ All go to nedavah
🐑 Unblemished Animals
Allocated (Rav)
Sin-offering → left to die
Burnt-offering → sacrificed
Peace-offering → eaten in one day, no bread
🐑 Blemished Animals
Unallocated (Rav Naḥman)
Will be sold → treated like money
Rav Huna says Rav Animals are inherently allocated because the type of animal reveals its purpose: female sheep = sin-offering, male sheep = burnt-offering, ram = peace-offering.

The Allocation Spectrum: Money → Bars → Beams → Animals

💰 Mixed coins — Always unallocated (all agree)
↓
🪙 Silver bars (naskha) — Rav Naḥman: allocated; Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak: unallocated
↓
🪵 Piles of beams (sevar) — All agree: allocated (three distinct piles = three distinct offerings)
↓
🐑 Unblemished animals — All agree: allocated (animal type reveals its offering)
↓
🐑 Blemished animals — Rav Naḥman: unallocated (must be sold → becomes money)
Underlying Logic The more distinct and identifiable the separated items are, the more likely they are deemed allocated. Fungible items (coins) blur together; discrete items (animals, beam-piles) do not.
