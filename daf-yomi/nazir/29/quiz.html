Nazir 29 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Reish Lakish, why can a father impose naziriteship on his son?
A. It is a halakha transmitted to Moses from Sinai
B. To educate him in mitzvot (chinuch)
C. Because the son implicitly consents
D. Because the mother delegates her authority to the father
Correct: B — Reish Lakish holds the father's power derives from the rabbinic obligation to educate his son in mitzvot, unlike R' Yochanan who considers it a halakha l'Moshe mi'Sinai.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Reish Lakish explain that a father cannot impose naziriteship on his DAUGHTER?
A. A halakha from Sinai specifies sons only
B. A father has no chinuch obligation toward his daughter
C. Daughters cannot become nazirites at all
D. The mother is responsible for the daughter's education
Correct: B — Reish Lakish holds that a father is obligated to educate his son but not his daughter, and therefore his chinuch-based power to impose naziriteship applies only to sons.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Reish Lakish resolve the problem that a minor nazirite must shave his entire head (rounding the head)?
A. Chinuch is a Torah obligation that overrides it
B. The prohibition of rounding applies only to adults
C. Rounding the ENTIRE head is only a rabbinic prohibition, so rabbinic chinuch overrides it
D. A nazirite is exempt from the rounding prohibition
Correct: C — The Torah prohibits shaving only the corners of the head. Rounding the entire head is rabbinic, so the rabbinic obligation of chinuch can override it.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Yosei b'R' Yehuda, until when can a father impose naziriteship on his son?
A. Until the son develops two pubic hairs
B. Until the son reaches the age of vows
C. Until the son turns 13
D. Until the son marries
Correct: B — R' Yosei b'R' Yehuda holds the father's power ends when the son reaches the age of vows (approximately a year before maturity), when the child demonstrates understanding of the nature of vows.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What was remarkable about young Rabbi Chanina's response to Rabban Gamliel?
A. He recited the entire tractate of Nazir from memory
B. He said whether he was a minor or adult, he would be a nazirite either way
C. He proved that his father's vow was invalid
D. He demonstrated that chinuch overrides halakha l'Moshe mi'Sinai
Correct: B — Rabbi Chanina cleverly told Rabban Gamliel not to bother examining him: if he was a minor, the father's vow applied; if an adult, his own vow applied. Either way, he was a nazirite. Rabban Gamliel predicted he would become a halakhic authority.
