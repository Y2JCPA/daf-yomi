Nazir 39 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Rav Pappa alter the wording of the baraita when challenging Abaye?
A. He thought Abaye's view was his own opinion and could be refuted by a baraita
B. He wanted to test if Abaye had memorized the baraita correctly
C. He was trying to reconcile two contradictory baraitot
D. He misremembered the original wording of the baraita
Correct: A — Rav Pappa assumed Abaye's ruling was a personal opinion rather than a received tradition (mesorah). He modified the baraita to create an explicit contradiction, expecting Abaye to retract.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What practical halakhic difference depends on whether hair grows from the bottom or top?
A. Whether a nazirite can use scissors instead of a razor
B. Whether remaining hair after partial shaving preserves or negates naziriteship days
C. Whether grape skins or grape seeds are more forbidden
D. Whether a nazirite must shave completely at the end of his term
Correct: B — If hair grows from the bottom, remaining hair after partial shaving is new growth — the sanctified hair is gone, and days are negated. If from the top, the original hair partially remains and the naziriteship can continue.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which proof that hair grows from the bottom does the Gemara accept without rejection?
A. Live lice are found at the root of hair
B. Gentile braided hair (belorit) loosens underneath
C. Dyed sheep wool and old men's dyed beards show undyed roots
D. Dead lice are found at the ends of hair
Correct: C — The proofs from lice and braided hair are all rejected. The conclusive proof comes from dyed sheep wool and old men's dyed beards, where new undyed growth appears at the roots.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Eliezer, why does a nazirite shaved after completing his term but before offerings negate only seven days?
A. Because the Torah explicitly states seven days for this case
B. Because he derives shaving of purity from shaving of impurity — both require only seven days
C. Because seven days is the minimum naziriteship period
D. Because the offerings can only be brought after seven days of purification
Correct: B — R' Eliezer derives the law of purity shaving from impurity shaving: just as becoming impure after completing a term requires waiting only 7 days, the same applies to being shaved — and hair regrows sufficiently in 7 days.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the baraita, why does the Torah specifically mention 'razor' if ALL methods of hair removal are forbidden to a nazirite?
A. To establish that the razor carries the most severe punishment
B. To teach that the concluding shaving of purity must be performed with a razor
C. To distinguish the nazirite's prohibition from the leper's requirements
D. To indicate that only a razor causes full-length hair removal
Correct: B — Since the verse 'He shall be holy' already forbids all hair removal methods, the specific mention of 'razor' teaches that the nazirite's final shaving ritual (shaving of purity) must be performed specifically with a razor.
