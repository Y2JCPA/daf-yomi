Central Dilemma: Where Does Hair Grow From?

A nazirite is shaved by bandits but enough hair remains to bend to its root
↓
Grows from Bottom
The remaining hair is new growth — the sanctified hair was removed → must add days
Grows from Top
The original sanctified hair still partially exists → may complete naziriteship
↓
Conclusion: Hair grows from the bottom — proven from dyed wool, old men's beards, and other evidence
📖 Key Sugya The Gemara brings five proofs to determine hair growth direction, a question with real halakhic consequences for the nazirite.

Rav Pappa Explains His Challenge to Abaye

Rav Pappa
I raised my objection assuming Abaye's ruling that a nazirite is flogged for 'anything made of the grapevine' was merely his own opinion.
Rav Pappa
I changed the wording of the baraita to test if Abaye would retract when faced with a contradiction.
Rav Pappa
But I did not know this halakha is a tradition in Abaye's hand (mesorah), and therefore he would never retract.
💡 Insight A received tradition (mesorah) holds more weight than a personal opinion — Rav Pappa would not have challenged Abaye had he known it was transmitted tradition.

Five Proofs: Does Hair Grow from Bottom or Top?

Proof 1: Live lice are found at the root of hair
↓
❌ Rejected: Live lice crawl down toward the scalp for nourishment
↓
Proof 2: Dead lice are found at the end of hair
↓
❌ Rejected: Dead lice have no strength and are carried upward by growth
↓
Proof 3: Gentile braided hair (belorit) becomes loose underneath
↓
❌ Rejected: Loosening could be from lying on it (crumpling)
↓
Proof 4: Dyed sheep wool becomes loose underneath ✅
↓
Proof 5: Old men who dye beards — roots turn white first ✅
✅ Conclusion Hair grows from the bottom. New undyed hair pushes up from the root, proving the direction of growth.

Nazirite Shaved by Bandits — When Hair Remains

During Naziriteship

  • Hair grows from the bottom → remaining hair is new growth
  • The sanctified hair has been removed
  • Must count additional days for proper regrowth
  • 30 days negated and must restart

After Completion (R' Eliezer)

  • If shaved after completing term but before offerings
  • R' Eliezer: impurity after completion negates only 7 days
  • Same applies to shaving: negates only 7 days
  • Hair grows enough in 7 days to bend end to root
⚖️ R' Eliezer's Derivation He derives the shaving of purity from the shaving of impurity: just as impurity after completion requires only 7 days, so too shaving after completion negates only 7 days.

Liability for Non-Razor Hair Removal

R' Yoshiya
The Torah says 'a razor shall not come upon his head' — from this I derive only a razor is forbidden.
R' Yoshiya
But the verse also says 'He shall be holy, he shall let the locks grow long' — this teaches that tearing, uprooting, or pulling out any amount is also forbidden.
R' Yonatan
From 'razor' I derive only a razor. One who tears out, uproots, or pulls out hair is exempt.
Gemara
But it says 'He shall be holy'! R' Yonatan answers: that verse adds a positive mitzva violation to the razor prohibition — not a separate liability for other methods.
📜 Mishna A nazirite who shaved with scissors, a razor, or pulled out any amount is liable — the Mishna follows R' Yoshiya's expansive reading.
