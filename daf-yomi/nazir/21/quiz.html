Nazir 21 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the maximum time window for associating oneself with another's nazirite vow by saying 'And I'?
A. The time to recite a short blessing
B. The time for a student to greet his rabbi
C. The time to walk four cubits
D. Until the end of the same day
Correct: B — The Gemara defines the 'time required for speaking a short phrase' (toch kedei dibbur) as the time it takes a student to inquire after the welfare of his rabbi — 'Shalom alecha rabbi.'
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Mishna list 'And I' exactly twice (three people total)?
A. Because only two additional people may ever associate with a vow
B. Because the dissolution rule requires a first, middle, and last person
C. Because Reish Lakish limits it to three
D. Because the greeting-time window only allows two more people
Correct: B — The Gemara explains: the Mishna needs three people so the latter clause can distinguish between dissolving the first person's vow (all dissolved) and the last person's (only last dissolved), implying a middle person exists.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If one says 'My hand is a nazirite,' what is the halakhic result?
A. He is a full nazirite
B. He is a nazirite only regarding his hand
C. He has not said anything of consequence — he is not a nazirite
D. He must consult a Sage to determine the vow's validity
Correct: C — The hand is not an organ upon which life depends (davar she-haneshama teluya bo). Only vows made upon vital organs (head, liver) create naziriteship.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the practical difference between 'uproot' and 'sever' regarding a husband nullifying his wife's nazirite vow?
A. Whether the husband also becomes a nazirite
B. Whether another woman who associated with the wife's vow remains bound
C. Whether the wife must bring a guilt-offering
D. Whether the nullification requires a Sage's approval
Correct: B — If the husband uproots, it's as if the wife never vowed — so another woman who said 'And I' to her vow is also freed. If he merely severs, the original vow was valid when made, and the other woman remains bound.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why can't the husband nullify his wife's nazirite vow after he said 'And I'?
A. Because his vow takes priority over hers
B. Because a Sage must dissolve it instead
C. Because saying 'And I' is treated as upholding her vow, and once upheld, he cannot nullify
D. Because both vows merged into a single joint obligation
Correct: C — The Gemara explains (per one reading) that by saying 'And I,' the husband effectively upheld (kiyyem) his wife's vow. Once a husband has upheld a vow, he can no longer nullify it — unless he first has the upholding dissolved by a Sage.
