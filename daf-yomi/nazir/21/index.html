Nazir 21: Chains of Association & Uprooting vs. Severing Vows

Mishna
If one says 'I am a nazirite' and another says 'And I', and a third says 'And I' — they are all nazirites.
Gemara
Does this support Reish Lakish — that only those within the 'greeting time' window can associate?
Gemara
No proof — the Mishna uses two examples only because it needs a first, middle, and last for the dissolution rule.
Dilemma
Does each person associate with the one before him (chain forever) or with the first person (time-limited)?
Amud B
New dilemma: When a husband nullifies his wife's vow, does he uproot it entirely or merely sever it going forward?
Core Tension Two parallel dilemmas define the daf: how vow-association chains work, and whether nullification is retroactive or prospective.

Associate with First or with Preceding?

Gemara
Possibility 1: Each person associates with the one immediately before him. Result: the chain can continue indefinitely, as long as each speaks in time.
Gemara
Possibility 2: They all associate with the first person's vow. Result: once the greeting-time window from Person A's vow passes, no more can join.
Attempted Proof
Mishna says if the first is dissolved, all are dissolved — implying all depend on the first?
Refutation
Even if each links to the prior, dissolving the first cascades through the chain. No proof either way.
Unresolved The Gemara brings multiple proofs from the Mishna but refutes each one. The dilemma remains unresolved (teiku-style).

Dissolution Rules for Chained Nazirite Vows

Whose Vow Is Dissolved?Effect on Others
First person's vow dissolvedAll are dissolved — every vow depends on the first
Last person's vow dissolvedOnly the last is dissolved; all others remain bound
Middle person's vow dissolvedDepends on unresolved dilemma — do later vowers link to him or to the first?
Why Three People? The Mishna lists 'And I' twice — not because a fourth person cannot join, but because the dissolution rule requires a first, middle, and last.

Naziriteship on Body Parts

NOT a Nazirite

  • 'My hand is a nazirite'
  • 'My foot is a nazirite'
  • Rule: limbs upon which life does not depend
  • Hair is also non-vital — yet see Rav Yehuda's reading

IS a Nazirite

  • 'My head is a nazirite'
  • 'My liver is a nazirite'
  • Rule: entity upon which life depends (דבר שהנשמה תלויה בו)
  • Rav Yehuda: 'My hair' means 'let my mouth abstain from wine, my hair from cutting'
Principle Accepting naziriteship upon a vital organ binds the whole person; upon a non-vital limb, it has no effect.

Does a Husband Uproot or Sever His Wife's Vow?

Gemara
Uproot (עוקר): The vow is erased retroactively — as if she never vowed at all.
Gemara
Sever (מיגז גייז): The vow was valid until nullified — he only cuts it off going forward.
Proof Attempt
Mishna: If wife vows nazirite and husband says 'And I,' he cannot nullify. If uproot — nullifying hers destroys his too!
Refutation
Maybe he can't nullify because saying 'And I' counts as upholding her vow (קיום), and once upheld, he cannot nullify.
Practical Difference If another woman associated with the wife's vow: uproot = her vow also dissolves; sever = her vow stands, since it was valid when made.
