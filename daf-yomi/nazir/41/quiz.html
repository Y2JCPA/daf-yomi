Nazir 41 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Reish Lakish, when a positive mitzva clashes with a prohibition, what should one do first?
A. The positive mitzva always overrides immediately
B. Try to fulfill both; override only if impossible
C. The prohibition always takes precedence
D. One must ask a beit din to decide
Correct: B — Reish Lakish's principle: if you can fulfill both the mitzva and the prohibition, do so. Only if it's impossible does the positive mitzva override.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the fact that the Torah permits a metzora to use a razor prove that tweezers are NOT valid?
A. The Torah explicitly says tweezers are forbidden
B. If tweezers worked, the razor would be prohibited per Reish Lakish's principle
C. Tweezers were not available in Temple times
D. Tweezers cannot fully remove hair
Correct: B — If the metzora could fulfill his obligation with tweezers (no prohibition violated), then per Reish Lakish, the razor — which violates 'destroying the beard' — would be forbidden. The Torah's permission of the razor proves it's the only valid method.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does the term 'his head' teach according to the Rabbis?
A. A metzora must use a razor
B. A nazir who is a metzora must shave
C. The metzora's shaving overrides the prohibition of rounding the corners of the head
D. Shaving the head is always permitted
Correct: C — The Rabbis use 'his head' to teach that the metzora's shaving mitzva overrides the prohibition of hakafat ha-rosh (rounding the corners of the head), and that full-head shaving counts as 'rounding.'
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why are BOTH 'his head' and 'his beard' necessary in Leviticus 14:9?
A. They refer to two different shaving rituals on different days
B. One is for men and one is for women
C. Each overrides a different prohibition (rounding the head vs. destroying the beard)
D. One applies to kohanim and one to non-kohanim
Correct: C — 'His head' overrides the prohibition of rounding the head and teaches that full-head shaving is considered rounding. 'His beard' overrides the prohibition against destroying the beard (especially for kohanim) and proves the use of a razor.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
From where does R' Eliezer derive the general principle that a positive mitzva overrides a prohibition?
A. From the Pesach offering overriding Shabbat
B. From circumcision overriding Shabbat
C. From tzitzit — wool fringes on a linen garment override sha'atnez
D. From the metzora's shaving itself
Correct: C — R' Eliezer derives the principle from tzitzit: the Torah commands placing wool-dyed strings on linen garments despite the sha'atnez prohibition, demonstrating that a positive mitzva can override a negative prohibition.
