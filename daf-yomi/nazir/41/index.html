Must a Metzora Shave with a Razor?

Rabbis
A metzora (leper) must shave with a razor specifically — derived from the word 'his beard' (Lev. 14:9), since destroying the beard implies a razor.
R' Eliezer
The requirement for a razor is derived from 'his head' — the Torah must override the nazir's prohibition, proving the metzora must use a razor.
Gemara
Both 'his head' and 'his beard' are necessary: one teaches about rounding the head, the other about destroying the beard.
Core Dispute All agree a metzora must use a razor — the debate is which verse teaches this and what each extra term comes to teach.

Reish Lakish's Foundational Principle

כל מקום שאתה מוצא עשה ולא תעשה — אם אתה יכול לקיים את שניהם מוטב
Key Rule Any place where a positive mitzva (עשה) and a prohibition (לא תעשה) clash:

If you can fulfill both — do so.
If not — the positive mitzva overrides the prohibition.

This principle drives the entire sugya: if a metzora could shave with tweezers, he must not use a razor (which violates a prohibition). Since the Torah permits a razor, it must be the only valid method.

The Razor Proof (Rabbis' View)

Can a metzora shave with tweezers or a plane?
Yes — tweezers work
Then per Reish Lakish, he may NOT use a razor (no need to violate the prohibition)
No — only razor works
The Torah permits a razor because it's the only valid method
↓
Since the Torah needed to teach that a razor is permitted, the metzora must use a razor exclusively
Logic If tweezers were valid, the Torah wouldn't need to permit a razor — it would be automatically prohibited by Reish Lakish's principle.

R' Eliezer vs. Rabbis: What Does 'His Head' Teach?

R' Eliezer
'His head' (Lev. 14:9) teaches that a nazir-metzora must shave with a razor — proving the metzora's obligation requires a razor specifically.
Gemara challenges
Maybe 'his head' just teaches the nazir-metzora is permitted to use a razor, not that it's required?
R' Eliezer responds
If tweezers were valid, per Reish Lakish the razor would be forbidden. The verse must mean the razor is required.
Rabbis
We use 'his head' to teach that shaving a metzora overrides the prohibition of rounding the corners of the head (הקפת הראש).
Distinction R' Eliezer uses 'his head' for the razor requirement; the Rabbis use it for the rounding prohibition override.

Why Both 'His Head' AND 'His Beard' Are Needed

VerseWithout It, You'd Think...So It Teaches...
'His beard' (Lev. 14:9)A leper-kohen may not shave off his beard (Lev. 21:5 prohibits kohanim)Metzora's shaving overrides the kohen's beard prohibition
'His head' (Lev. 14:9)Shaving the entire head isn't 'rounding' — only the sides are (Lev. 19:27)Full-head shaving is considered rounding, and the metzora mitzva overrides it
Key Insight Neither term is redundant — each addresses a different prohibition that the metzora's shaving must override.

Three Prohibitions Overridden by the Metzora's Shaving

Destroying the Beard

  • Lev. 19:27 — 'You shall not destroy the corners of your beard'
  • Also Lev. 21:5 for kohanim
  • Overridden by 'his beard' in Lev. 14:9
  • Proves metzora uses a razor

Rounding the Head

  • Lev. 19:27 — 'You shall not round the corners of your heads'
  • Debate: does full-head shaving count as 'rounding'?
  • Overridden by 'his head' in Lev. 14:9
  • 'His head' proves full shaving IS rounding

Nazir's Hair

  • Num. 6:5 — 'No razor shall come upon his head'
  • A nazir-metzora must still shave
  • Positive mitzva of purification overrides
  • Source of R' Eliezer's razor proof
Principle The metzora's positive mitzva to shave overrides all three of these prohibitions — עשה דוחה לא תעשה.
