Ketubot 106 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Elijah the Prophet stop visiting Rav Anan?
A. Rav Anan accepted a bribe from a litigant
B. Rav Anan inadvertently caused a miscarriage of justice by showing honor to one party
C. Rav Anan refused to study the Seder deEliyahu
D. Rav Anan insulted a Torah scholar in court
Correct: B — Rav Anan gave priority to a man he believed was a scholar's relative, which intimidated the other litigant and caused him to lose. This indirect perversion of justice caused Elijah to depart.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How many scholars remained in the yeshiva of Rav Huna after others departed?
A. 1,200
B. 400
C. 800
D. 2,200
Correct: C — 800 scholars remained in Rav Huna's academy. He used 13 speakers to relay his lectures, and the dust from departing students was visible from Eretz Yisrael.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Rav Naḥman argue that curtain-weavers should be paid from Temple maintenance funds rather than the chamber collection?
A. Because weaving is manual labor, not sacred service
B. Because the curtains served in place of the solid building construction
C. Because the chamber collection had already been depleted
D. Because curtain-weaving was considered a form of building repair
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman reasoned that since the curtains (parokhet) served in place of the solid wall separating the Sanctuary from the Holy of Holies, they were part of the building itself — and thus should be funded from maintenance, not offering funds.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the contradiction between II Chronicles 24:14 (vessels made from maintenance funds) and II Kings 12:14 (no vessels made from such funds)?
A. One verse refers to gold vessels and the other to silver
B. The verses refer to different historical periods with different rules
C. When there were leftover maintenance funds they could be used for vessels; when there were none, all went to maintenance
D. One verse is authoritative and the other is merely advisory
Correct: C — The Gemara resolves the contradiction by distinguishing between cases: when maintenance funds had a surplus, the extra could fund vessels (Chronicles); when there was no surplus, all funds went to maintenance (Kings). R' Abbahu adds that the court makes an initial mental stipulation allowing this.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Akiva, what should be purchased with leftover funds from the chamber collection of shekels?
A. Service vessels for the Temple
B. Golden plates to coat the Holy of Holies
C. Animals for kayitz ha-mizbe'aḥ (repletion of the altar)
D. Incense for the golden altar
Correct: C — R' Akiva holds that leftover chamber funds should purchase animals for kayitz ha-mizbe'aḥ — burnt-offerings sacrificed when the altar would otherwise be idle — since the funds were originally collected for offerings. He assigns leftover libation funds to service vessels instead.
