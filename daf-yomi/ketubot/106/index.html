Rav Anan's Unintended Perversion of Justice

A man brings a gift to Rav Anan, claiming to be a relative of his
↓
Rav Anan declines the gift but gives the man priority in court to honor a Torah scholar's family
↓
The opposing litigant sees the honor shown and becomes intimidated
↓
Result
The litigant's mouth became closed — he lost his ability to argue and lost the case
Consequence
Elijah the Prophet departs from Rav Anan and only returns after fasting and prayer — but now appears in frightening forms
Seder deEliyahu The teachings Elijah gave before the incident became Seder deEliyahu Rabba (the Major Order); those taught afterward, while Rav Anan sat in a box, became Seder deEliyahu Zuta (the Minor Order).

Rav Yosef Refuses to Pray During Famine

The Sages
Let the Master pray for mercy concerning this divine anger and famine!
Rav Yosef
If Elisha, who supported 2,200 scholars from his own pocket, did not pray for mercy during famine — should I pray for mercy?
The Gemara
How do we derive 2,200? From "How should I set this before 100 men?" (II Kings 4:43) — each of 20 loaves served 100, plus 2 extra portions.
Humility Rav Yosef reasoned kal va-ḥomer: if a prophet who personally sustained thousands of scholars did not intervene in divine decrees, how much more so should he refrain.

Who Receives Wages from the Temple Treasury?

From the Chamber Collection (Offerings)

  • 🔍 Inspectors of blemishes — examined sacrificial animals (R' Yitzḥak b. Redifa / R' Ami)
  • 🔪 Teachers of shechita — taught priests the laws of slaughter (Shmuel)
  • ✋ Teachers of kemitza — taught priests handful-removal (Rav)
  • 📜 Torah scroll proofreaders in Jerusalem (R' Yoḥanan)
  • 🧵 Curtain weavers — according to the initial teaching

From Temple Maintenance Funds (Rav Naḥman's View)

  • 🧵 Curtain weavers — since curtains serve in place of the building itself
  • The parokhet separating the Sanctuary from the Holy of Holies is structural
  • Therefore paid from building maintenance funds, not offering funds
Objection A baraita groups curtain weavers with the House of Garmu (shewbread) and House of Avtinas (incense) — all paid from the chamber collection, challenging Rav Naḥman.

Service Vessels: Chamber or Maintenance Funds?

Rav Huna
Vessels can come from maintenance funds! As it says: "Of which were made vessels for the House of the Lord" (II Chron. 24:14)
Rav
Whoever taught you Writings didn't teach you Prophets! — "There were not made for the House of the Lord cups of silver…" (II Kings 12:14)
The Gemara
The verses contradict each other!
Resolution
No contradiction — when there are leftover maintenance funds, they may be used for vessels. When there is no surplus, all funds go to maintenance.
R' Abbahu
The court makes a mental stipulation (tenai) from the start: if the money isn't needed for maintenance, it may be redirected to vessels.
Key Principle R' Yishmael's school teaches: Service vessels come from the chamber collection, as the verse says "the rest of the money" — referring to money that has a remainder, i.e., the chamber funds.
