She'era & Kesuta: Clothing or Intimacy?

R' Eliezer ben Yaakov
She'era = according to her flesh (age), give her kesuta (clothing). Don't give a young girl's garments to an elderly woman!
R' Eliezer ben Yaakov
Onata = according to the season, give her clothing. No heavy clothes in summer, no worn-out garments in winter.
Rav Yosef
She'era = closeness of flesh during relations. He may not behave like the Persians, who have relations in their clothes.
Rav Huna
One who says: 'I will only have relations while we are both clothed' — he must divorce her and pay her ketubah.
Practical Halacha Rav Yosef's baraita supports Rav Huna: the Torah mandates physical closeness during marital intimacy.

Funeral Obligations: First Tanna vs. Rabbi Yehuda

First Tanna

  • Flutes and lamenting women are NOT obligatory
  • 'She ascends with him' applies only while alive
  • After death — no obligation to match husband's family status
  • If it's not her family's custom, he need not provide it

Rabbi Yehuda

  • Even the poorest must provide 2 flutes + 1 lamenting woman
  • 'She ascends with him' applies even after death
  • Must bury her according to HIS family's dignity
  • Halacha follows R' Yehuda (per Rav Ḥisda / Mar Ukva)
Ruling Rav Ḥisda said that Mar Ukva said: The halacha follows Rabbi Yehuda — a wife ascends to her husband's status even in death.

Insane Husband vs. Overseas Husband: Court Authority

Insane HusbandHusband Overseas
Feed wife?✅ Yes✅ Yes
Feed sons & daughters?✅ Yes❌ No
Give 'something else'?✅ Yes❌ No
ReasoningLeft involuntarily — assumed he'd want to provideLeft voluntarily — if he didn't provide, he chose not to
What is 'Something Else'? Rav Ḥisda: Wife's ornaments  |  Rav Yosef: Charity donations. One who denies ornaments certainly denies charity; one who denies charity may still grant ornaments — he wouldn't want his wife demeaned.

Transfer of Authority: Father → Husband

👰 Bride is under father's authority
↓
How is she delivered to the groom?
Father accompanies
Father goes with husband's messengers, OR father's messengers go with husband's messengers → Still under father's authority
Full delivery
Father delivers to husband's messengers without accompanying, OR father's messengers deliver to husband's messengers → Under husband's authority
↓
But for which matters does delivery (mesira) take effect?
Key Principle The Mishna uses the word 'Always' (לעולם) to exclude the earlier ruling that brides eat teruma once the designated wedding time arrives — she remains under her father until actual chuppah or delivery.

Amoraic Dispute: What Does Mesira (Delivery) Accomplish?

Rav
Delivery transfers authority for all matters EXCEPT teruma. A Kohen's bride cannot eat teruma until the chuppah.
Rav Asi
Delivery transfers authority for all matters INCLUDING teruma.
Shmuel
Delivery is effective only for inheritance — the husband inherits her if she dies after delivery.
Reish Lakish
Also effective for her ketubah — if he dies, she collects as a widow from marriage (200 zuz), not from betrothal (100 zuz).
R' Yoḥanan & R' Ḥanina
Delivery is effective for everything, including teruma.
Refutation! A Tosefta conclusively refutes all who permit teruma after delivery: A bride may not eat teruma until she actually enters the chuppah.
