When Death Meets Wedding: The Procedure

💀 A close relative dies right before the wedding
↓
🏠 Move the corpse into a private room
↓
💍 Bride & groom are ushered to the chuppah and married
↓
🤝 Groom fulfills initial mitzva of intercourse, then withdraws
↓
⚰️ The corpse is buried
↓
🎉 Seven days of wedding feast observed first
↓
😢 Then seven days of mourning
↓
🛏️ Throughout both periods: groom sleeps among men, bride among women
KEY CONDITION This procedure applies only when the father of the groom or mother of the bride dies — the ones responsible for wedding preparations. If the opposite parent dies, the corpse is buried first, mourning observed, and only then do they marry.
3 / 9

Which Parent Died? Different Rules Apply

Father of Groom / Mother of Bride

  • These are the ones who handle wedding preparations
  • Wedding proceeds immediately
  • Corpse moved to a room
  • Marriage → mitzva intercourse → burial
  • 7 days feast THEN 7 days mourning

Mother of Groom / Father of Bride

  • Others can step in for preparations
  • Wedding is postponed
  • Corpse is buried immediately
  • 7 days of mourning first
  • Only afterward does the couple marry
LOGIC The distinction depends on who is irreplaceably responsible for the wedding arrangements — their death means the completed preparations would go to waste.

The 'Water on the Meat' Dispute

Rav Ḥisda
The wedding proceeds immediately only if water was already placed on the meat — otherwise it can be sold and there's no significant loss.
Rava
In a city, even if water was placed on the meat, it can still be sold — there are plenty of buyers. Postpone for mourning.
Rav Pappa
In a village, even without water on the meat, it cannot be sold — no buyers exist for such a quantity. Proceed with the wedding.
Gemara
If Rava's rule governs cities and Rav Pappa's governs villages, where does Rav Ḥisda's condition ever apply?
Rav Ashi
It applies in a place like Mata Meḥasya — too small to be a city, too large to be a village!
PRINCIPLE The core concern is financial loss (hefsed merubeh). Whether to proceed depends on whether the prepared food can be sold to recoup costs.

Can You Sell the Wedding Meat?

A death occurs before the wedding.
Meat has been prepared.
↓
Where is the wedding taking place?
City (Rava)
Meat can be sold regardless.
Postpone wedding for mourning.
Mid-size (Rav Ḥisda)
Was water placed on the meat?
Yes → Proceed with wedding
No → Postpone for mourning
Village (Rav Pappa)
Meat cannot be sold regardless.
Proceed with wedding.
MATA MEḤASYA Rav Ashi's hometown — the only locale where Rav Ḥisda's 'water on the meat' distinction is practically relevant.

His Mourning vs. Her Mourning

Gemara
A baraita says when his in-laws die, he mourns with her. Does 'with her' mean together in bed?
Answer
No — 'with him/her' means in the same house, as Rav told his son Ḥiyya: observe mourning before her, but not when she's absent.
Rav Ashi
You can't compare this mourning to regular mourning! Regular mourning is treated strictly and won't be taken lightly.
Rav Ashi
But post-wedding mourning — since the Sages already made leniencies (feast before mourning), people might take it lightly. So extra safeguards are needed: no seclusion.
DISTINCTION When she is mourning, he need not sleep apart — she would resist any improper advance. When he is mourning (or both), they must sleep separately as an extra safeguard.
