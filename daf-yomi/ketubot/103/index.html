The Millstone Rental Dispute

Owner
I no longer need your grinding services — pay me money for the millstone rental instead.
Renter
I agreed to grind, not to pay money. I'll keep grinding for you as agreed.
Ravina
Like two supporters of a girl — when circumstances change, switch to monetary payment.
Rav Avira
Not comparable! The girl has one stomach. But you can grind and sell, grind and store — use your new millstone!
Gemara
Exception: if the renter has other grinding work available, we force him to pay money — otherwise it's middas Sedom (conduct of Sodom).
PRINCIPLE Changed circumstances may not override contract terms — unless insisting on the original terms is purely obstructive (middas Sedom).

Widow's Right to Housing

Widow after husband's death — where does she live?
↓
She says: I stay in husband's house
Heirs must sustain her there
She keeps full lifestyle: slaves, utensils, pillows, linens
She says: I stay in father's house
Heirs can refuse
"Live with us = sustenance. Not with us = no sustenance."
↓
Exception: She is young and heirs are young — improper to live together
↓
They sustain her in her father's house
SOURCE Ketubah text: "You will reside in my house and be sustained from my property all the days you live in my house as a widow."

Orphans Selling Property: Two Cases

Selling Widow's Quarters

  • Rav Naḥman: Sale is INVALID
  • Property mortgaged from husband's lifetime
  • Lien existed while husband was alive
  • Widow has prior claim — buyers lose

Selling Daughters' Support Property

  • R' Yoḥanan: Sale is VALID
  • Property NOT mortgaged from father's lifetime
  • Lien only begins after father's death
  • Orphans acted improperly but sale stands
DISTINCTION The key difference: when did the lien attach? A lien from the husband's lifetime makes the widow's claim stronger than the daughters' post-death support claim.

Why Gamliel Over Shimon?

Rebbi
Shimon is a Sage. Gamliel should be the Nasi.
Levi
Need this even be said? Gamliel is the firstborn — he inherits automatically!
R' Shimon b"R
"It is necessary — for you and for your limp!" (sharp retort to Levi)
Gemara
Jehoram inherited because he equaled his fathers. Gamliel did not equal Rebbi in wisdom.
Gemara
But Gamliel did fill his father's place in fear of sin — and that qualifies him for the Nesiut.
INSIGHT Leadership requires yiras shamayim (fear of Heaven) — Torah greatness alone is not sufficient for communal authority.
