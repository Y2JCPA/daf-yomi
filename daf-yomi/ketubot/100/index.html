Who Is the Agent Like?

Rava / Rav Naḥman
An agent is like judges — he acts for others' benefit, not his own
Rav Shmuel bar Bisna / Rav Naḥman
An agent is like a widow — he is a single individual, not a panel
Gemara
The halakha: An agent is like a widow
Key Ruling An agent who errs in selling property has the same law as a widow — the sale may be void if the error exceeds one-sixth of value.

Agent: Like Judges or Like a Widow?

Like Judges (Rava)

  • Agent acts for others' benefit, not his own
  • Judges assess property for third parties
  • Excludes widow who sells for her own benefit

Like a Widow (Rav Shmuel bar Bisna)

  • Agent is a single individual
  • Widow also acts alone
  • Excludes the court, which is many people
Halakha The law follows Rav Shmuel bar Bisna — an agent is like a widow. If he errs, the sale can be voided.

Standard (1/50)

Agent can say: "I estimated you to be miserly"

All valid — the range is reasonable estimation, not error

Rav Naḥman: Rabbis vs. 'Power of the Court'

Rav Naḥman
Halakha follows the Rabbis: if judges err by 1/6, the sale is void
Gemara
But Rav Naḥman also said orphans cannot protest a court division — 'What advantage is there to the power of the court?'
Rav Naḥman
No contradiction! The mishna deals with cases where judges erred — sale is void
Rav Naḥman
The orphans' case is where the court did not err — so the division stands
Resolution When the court errs → sale void (Rabbis). When the court acts correctly → orphans cannot protest (power of the court). Protest only about location, not valuation.

When Are Court Sale Announcements Required?

📢 Standard rule: Court must announce sale for 30 days (orphans) or 60 days (hekdesh)
↓
⚠️ Without announcement → Ameimar: It's as if the court erred in a Mishna matter — sale reversed
↓
👤 Exception: Slaves — no announcement, lest they hear and escape
↓
📋 Exception: Movable property & contracts — no announcement, lest they be stolen
↓
💰 Exception: Pressing needs — head tax, children's sustenance, burial — no time to wait
↓
🏘️ Exception: Neharde'a — no announcements made, because buyers were stigmatized as exploiters
Rav Naḥman's Explanation In Neharde'a, buyers of announced property were called 'people who consume publicly announced property' — decent people refused to buy, so announcements were abolished.
