Ketubot 3 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why are the Sages able to permit a married woman to remarry even when, by Torah law, the get may be invalid?
A. Because Torah law always defers to rabbinic enactments
B. Because anyone who betroths does so contingent upon the agreement of the Sages, who can annul the betrothal retroactively
C. Because the Torah explicitly grants rabbis the power to dissolve marriages
D. Because conditional divorces are always valid regardless of whether conditions are met
Correct: B — The principle of 'kol hamekadesh ada'ata d'rabbanan mekadesh' means that every betrothal is implicitly contingent on rabbinic approval, giving the Sages power to retroactively annul it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
When Ravina challenges Rav Ashi about betrothal through intercourse, how does Rav Ashi respond?
A. Betrothal through intercourse cannot be annulled
B. The Sages rendered the intercourse as licentious intercourse (be'ilat zenut), retroactively removing its legal effect
C. The woman must receive a new get from a beit din
D. Intercourse-based betrothal is invalid from the start
Correct: B — Rav Ashi explains that the Sages can render the betrothal-intercourse as mere be'ilat zenut, stripping it of its legal significance as an act of betrothal.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Shmuel's ruling about the ferry case NOT definitively prove that ones has no legal standing in gittin?
A. Because Shmuel's rulings are never authoritative
B. Because the ferry being on the other side is common and foreseeable — he should have stipulated that exception
C. Because the man actually arrived on time
D. Because the get was never properly written
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes between common, foreseeable ones (like a ferry being unavailable) and uncommon ones. The man should have anticipated this possibility and stipulated accordingly.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why didn't the Sages completely abolish the Wednesday wedding ordinance when the prefect's decree was issued?
A. Because the decree only affected certain cities
B. Because persecution decrees are likely to be abrogated, and we don't permanently abolish rabbinic ordinances for temporary threats
C. Because the prefect's decree was not considered dangerous
D. Because Wednesday weddings were a Torah requirement
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that a shemada (persecution decree) is likely temporary, so rather than abolish the ordinance permanently, the Sages issued a lenient temporary ruling allowing Tuesday weddings.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the case where wedding preparations are complete and a parent of the bride or groom dies, what is the procedure?
A. The wedding is postponed until after the mourning period
B. The wedding is cancelled entirely
C. The corpse is moved to a room, and the couple is married before burial triggers mourning
D. The couple mourns for one day and then marries
Correct: C — When all preparations are complete (bread baked, animal slaughtered, wine diluted) and a parent dies, the deceased is moved to a room and the wedding proceeds immediately, before burial begins the mourning period.
