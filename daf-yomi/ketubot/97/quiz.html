Ketubot 97 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the accepted halakha (Ameimar), how often does a widow sell her late husband's property for sustenance?
A. Every 30 days
B. Every 6 months
C. Every 12 months
D. Whenever she needs money
Correct: B — Ameimar rules like Rav Yehuda that she sells every 6 months, with the buyer providing funds every 30 days. Rav Huna's opinion of every 12 months is rejected.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why can't a widow sell all her husband's property for sustenance and then seize back what she sold for her ketubah?
A. The orphans forbid it
B. She implicitly guarantees her own actions as seller
C. The court must approve ketubah collection
D. Buyers have stronger lien rights than her ketubah
Correct: B — Even though the general property guarantee (aḥarayut) rests on the orphans, the widow implicitly guarantees that she herself will not seize back what she sold. She must leave property equal to her ketubah.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the Neharde'a drought case, why were the mansion sales voided?
A. Rav Naḥman had compassion on the sellers
B. The wheat ship was already in the river bays at the time of sale — the error existed at the time of transaction
C. Selling due to financial pressure is always reversible
D. The buyers colluded to suppress the price
Correct: B — The Gemara clarifies that the sale was a mekach ta'ut (sale in error) because the wheat ship had already arrived before the sales took place — the emergency that motivated the sales didn't actually exist.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the practical difference (nafka minah) between Ulla and R' Yoḥanan regarding why a widow can sell out of court?
A. Whether a widow from betrothal can sell
B. Whether a divorcée can sell out of court
C. Whether orphans must approve the sale
D. Whether the sale requires witnesses
Correct: B — According to Ulla (desirability), a divorcée should also sell out of court since she too needs to be desirable for remarriage. According to R' Yoḥanan (husband's concern for dignity), a man doesn't care about his ex-wife's disgrace, so a divorcée must sell in court.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Shimon (followed by the Mishna), when does a widow lose her right to sustenance from the estate?
A. Only when she sells her entire ketubah
B. When she sells or mortgages even part of her ketubah
C. When she remarries
D. When the orphans reach adulthood
Correct: B — R' Shimon holds that selling even half the ketubah causes loss of sustenance rights. The Mishna follows his view: once she sells, mortgages, or gifts any part of her ketubah, she may sell the remainder only in court.
