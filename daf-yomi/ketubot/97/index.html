Widow's Sale of Estate Property — Key Questions

Widow needs sustenance from late husband's estate
↓
How often may she sell property?
Rav Huna
Every 12 months
Rav Yehuda / Halakha
Every 6 months
↓
Buyer pays her every 30 days in both opinions
↓
She must leave property equal to her ketubah — she cannot seize back what she sold
KEY RULE Ameimar rules like Rav Yehuda: she sells every 6 months, buyer provides monthly support. She cannot later seize sold property for her ketubah.

How Often Does a Widow Sell for Sustenance?

Rav Huna
She sells her late husband's property once every 12 months, and the buyer provides her money every 30 days.
Rav Yehuda
She sells once every 6 months, and the buyer provides her money every 30 days.
Gemara
A baraita supports Rav Huna — every 12 months. Another baraita supports Rav Yehuda — every 6 months.
Ameimar
The halakha is she sells every 6 months, buyer pays every 30 days — like Rav Yehuda.
Rav Ashi
What about Rav Huna's opinion?
Ameimar
"Lo shemia li" — I do not hold in accordance with it.
HALAKHA The widow sells every 6 months; the buyer disburses funds to her monthly for sustenance.

Can a Widow Seize Sold Property for Her Ketubah?

Widow sells husband's property for sustenance
↓
Rav Yosef: Property guarantee (aḥarayut) rests on the orphans, not on the widow
↓
Can she now seize back the sold property for her ketubah?
Possible Argument FOR
Guarantee is on orphans — she never personally guaranteed
Buyers' Counter-Argument
You implicitly guaranteed your own actions — you can't seize what you sold!
↓
Rav Sheshet's proof: Baraita says she must leave property equal to ketubah value — she cannot seize back sold land
CONCLUSION The widow must set aside property equal to her ketubah before selling the rest. She cannot sell everything and then reclaim property from buyers.

Sale Conducted in Error — Two Test Cases

CaseCircumstancesRulingReasoning
Man sells land to Rav PappaSold to buy oxen; ended up not needing themLand returnedRav Pappa acted beyond the letter of the law (lifnim mishurat hadin)
Neharde'a droughtEveryone sold mansions (appadna) to buy wheat; wheat ship then arrivedRav Naḥman: Mansions returnedShip was already in the bays at time of sale — error existed at time of transaction
KEY DISTINCTION A sale is voided only if the error existed at the time of sale. If circumstances changed afterward, the sale stands.

Why Can a Widow Sell Property Without Going to Court?

Ulla
Due to desirability (ḥen) — the Sages enacted leniencies for women so that men will want to marry them.
R' Yoḥanan
Because a man does not want his wife disgraced by being involved in court proceedings.
Gemara
What is the practical difference (nafka minah) between these views?
Gemara
A divorcée: Ulla says she too needs desirability. R' Yoḥanan says the husband doesn't care if his ex-wife is disgraced.
NAFKA MINAH According to Ulla, a divorcée should also sell out of court (desirability). According to R' Yoḥanan, a divorcée must sell in court since the ex-husband doesn't care about her dignity.

Selling Ketubah Rights — R' Meir vs. R' Shimon

R' Meir

  • Loses sustenance only if she sold all of her ketubah
  • Or mortgaged all of her ketubah
  • Or designated all of it as repayment to another
  • Part of the money = like the whole money

R' Shimon

  • Loses sustenance even if she sold only half
  • Once she gives up any part, she forfeits sustenance
  • Remainder can only be sold in court
  • Part of the money ≠ the whole money
MISHNA The anonymous Mishna follows R' Shimon: if she sold, mortgaged, or gifted part of her ketubah, she sells the remainder only in court.
