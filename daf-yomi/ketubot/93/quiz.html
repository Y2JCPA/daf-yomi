QUESTION 1 OF 5
When the estate is 200 dinars, why does the wife with a 100-dinar ketubah receive 50 instead of 33⅓?
A. The wife with the 200-dinar ketubah concedes her claim to the first 100 vis-à-vis the 100-dinar wife
B. The court grants her extra as compensation for having the smallest ketubah
C. She receives interest on her original claim
D. The other wives voluntarily gift her the difference
Correct: A — Shmuel explains that the 200-dinar wife writes a document removing herself from litigation with the 100-dinar wife regarding the first 100 dinars, leaving only two claimants (100 and 300) to split it equally — hence 50 each.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Shmuel, what is the nature of the concession (siluk) that a higher-claiming wife makes?
A. She permanently surrenders all rights to that tranche
B. She waives her claim only against a specific claimant, not absolutely
C. She transfers her share to the orphans
D. She reduces her total ketubah amount
Correct: B — The concession is personal — the 200-dinar wife removes herself from dealings with the 100-dinar wife only. Against the 300-dinar wife, she still maintains her claim to the first 100 dinars.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the dispute between Rabba and Rav Hamnuna regarding an ox bought for plowing but slaughtered?
A. Rabba says divide equally; Rav Hamnuna says proportionally
B. Rabba says divide proportionally to investment; Rav Hamnuna says divide equally
C. Both agree to divide equally
D. Both agree to divide proportionally
Correct: B — Rabba holds that when an ox bought for plowing is slaughtered, profits follow monetary investment (proportional). Rav Hamnuna holds profits are still divided equally regardless of how the ox was used.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the Mishna of four wives, who does Ben Nanas say must also take an oath?
A. The first wife
B. All four wives to the orphans
C. The fourth (last) wife
D. The husband's creditors
Correct: C — The Tanna Kamma says the fourth wife collects without an oath since no one comes after her. Ben Nanas disagrees — since she collects from property that would go to orphans, she too must swear.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rav Naḥman (citing Rabba bar Avuh) reconcile the Mishna's proportional partnership division with Shmuel's equal-split rule?
A. The Mishna overrules Shmuel
B. The Mishna's 'profit and loss' refers only to changes in coin value, not actual business earnings
C. Shmuel later retracted his opinion
D. The Mishna applies only to three or more partners
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman explains that 'profit' means receiving newer, more valuable coins and 'loss' means receiving defective ones — this is currency exchange, not business profit. Actual business profits would still be divided equally per Shmuel.
