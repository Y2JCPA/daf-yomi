Ketubot 60 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara's conclusion, what is Shmuel's true position on when a baby recognizes its mother?
A. 30 days
B. 50 days
C. There is no fixed age — check each child
D. 3 months
Correct: C — Rami bar Yeḥezkel clarified that Shmuel never set a fixed time. His brother Yehuda misquoted him. Shmuel's actual position: 'Whenever he recognizes her' — test each child individually.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does a blind baby recognize its mother, according to Rav Ashi?
A. Through her voice alone
B. Through smell and the taste of her milk
C. Through the warmth of her body
D. A blind baby cannot recognize its mother
Correct: B — Rav Ashi explains that a blind baby recognizes his mother through smell and through the taste of her milk, not through sight.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the key distinction between human milk that is permitted and milk that is 'like nursing from a non-kosher animal'?
A. The age of the mother
B. Whether it was expressed/detached vs. nursed directly
C. Whether the child is Jewish
D. Whether it was heated first
Correct: B — Human milk is fundamentally kosher. When detached from the body, it is fully permitted. But for anyone other than a small child, nursing directly from a woman is rabbinically prohibited — 'like nursing from a non-kosher animal.'
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Abaye run three parasangs after his tenant farmer?
A. To collect rent money
B. To correct his erroneous ruling about betrothing a nursing woman
C. To deliver a message from Rav Yosef
D. To tell him the halakha follows R' Yehuda
Correct: B — Abaye had permitted the tenant to betroth a nursing woman after 15 months, but then learned from Rav Yosef that Rav and Shmuel require waiting 24 full months even for betrothal. He ran to retract his ruling but couldn't catch up.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Rav Naḥman permit members of the Exilarch's household to remarry after arranging a wet nurse, but not ordinary people?
A. The Exilarch had special Torah authority
B. Their employees would not renege out of fear of consequences
C. They were wealthy enough to pay for extra nurses
D. The Exilarch's children didn't need nursing
Correct: B — The Exilarch's household was powerful enough that their employees would not dare renege on a commitment. With ordinary people, there was a real danger the wet nurse might quit, leaving the child without care after the mother remarried.
