When Does a Baby Recognize Its Mother?

Rav
A baby recognizes his mother at three months.
Shmuel
At thirty days.
R' Yoḥanan
At fifty days.
Rav Shimi bar Abaye
The halakha follows R' Yoḥanan — fifty days.
Rami bar Yeḥezkel
Don't accept my brother Yehuda's version! Shmuel actually said: Whenever he recognizes her — no fixed age. Check each child!
RESOLUTION Shmuel's true position: there is no fixed age — each baby must be tested individually to see if he recognizes his mother.

Shmuel's Test: The Divorcée Who Refused to Nurse

👩 A divorcée came before Shmuel — she refused to nurse her son
↓
🧪 Shmuel told Rav Dimi bar Yosef: Go and check if the child recognizes her
↓
👩‍👩‍👧 He placed her in a row of women
↓
👶 He carried the baby past each woman in the row
↓
😊 When the baby reached his mother, he looked at her face with joy
↓
😔 She averted her eyes — she didn't want to look at him
↓
⚖️ Verdict: "Lift up your eyes — take your son!" He recognizes you.
💡 BLIND BABIES? Rav Ashi: A blind baby recognizes his mother through smell and the taste of her milk.

How Long May a Child Continue to Nurse?

R' Eliezer

  • Maximum: 24 months
  • After 24 months — like nursing from a non-kosher animal (שרץ)
  • Strict cutoff regardless of circumstances

R' Yehoshua

  • May nurse even 4 or 5 years
  • But if weaned after 24 months and resumed — forbidden
  • "Even if he can carry his package on his shoulder" — same measure as 4–5 years
KEY DISTINCTION R' Yehoshua allows continued nursing past 24 months only if the child was never weaned. Once weaned after 24 months, resuming is forbidden.

Human Milk & Blood: Attached vs. Detached

Is human milk permitted?
↓
Detached from body
✅ Permitted
Fundamentally kosher
(derived from Lev. 11:4 — 'it' is impure, not human milk)
Nursing directly
❌ Forbidden for adults
Rabbinically prohibited
Like nursing from non-kosher animal
↓
What about human blood?
The opposite rule applies!
↓
Detached (e.g. on bread)
❌ Forbidden
Scrape off the blood, then eat the bread
Not detached (between teeth)
✅ Permitted
May suck and swallow without concern
📖 SOURCE Leviticus 11:4 — The word "הוא" (it) teaches: it alone is impure — but human milk and blood are not inherently non-kosher.
