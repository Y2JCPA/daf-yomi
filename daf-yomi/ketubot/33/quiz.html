Ketubot 33 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara need to find a case where the verse about lashes for conspiring witnesses is fulfilled?
A. Because every Torah verse must have at least one practical application
B. Because conspiring witnesses are always flogged
C. Because the verse was disputed by the Sadducees
D. Because Beit Shammai required it
Correct: A — The Gemara insists that if conspiring witnesses always pay instead of being flogged, the verse 'if the wicked man deserves to be flogged' would be rendered moot. Every verse must have a case where it applies.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava, what does the Gemara say Hananiah, Mishael, and Azariah would have done if flogged instead of thrown into the furnace?
A. They would have fled Babylon
B. They would have worshipped the graven image
C. They would have denied their faith publicly
D. They would have died from the flogging
Correct: B — Rav's dramatic statement: had they been flogged rather than cast into the fiery furnace, they would have worshipped the idol — suggesting lashes can be more severe than death.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Abaye's ultimate argument for why conspiring witnesses do not require forewarning?
A. Forewarning is only required for Shabbat violations
B. The Torah explicitly exempts witnesses from forewarning
C. It would be unjust to require forewarning of them when they sought to harm the defendant without forewarning him
D. Beit Din is not authorized to forewarn witnesses
Correct: C — Abaye argues: if they tried to have the defendant punished without forewarning him (since their testimony was false), requiring forewarning of them would contradict 'ka'asher zamam' — doing to them as they conspired to do.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara establish that one forewarned for a capital offense is considered forewarned for lashes?
A. From a gezeirah shavah between two verses
B. From the case of quarreling men who injured a pregnant woman, where forewarning for death exists yet Torah prescribes payment
C. From an explicit mishnah in Makkot
D. From a kal va'chomer from Shabbat violations
Correct: B — The case in Exodus 21:22 involves men forewarned for potential capital liability, yet the Torah prescribes monetary payment — implying the forewarning also covers the lesser liability of lashes, which is then superseded by payment.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Reish Lakish, whose opinion does our mishna follow, and what is that opinion?
A. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi — one is executed and does not pay
B. Rabbi Meir — one is both flogged AND pays
C. Rabbi Eliezer — conspiring witnesses cannot be forewarned
D. Ulla — the verbal analogy from 'ki innah' exempts from lashes
Correct: B — Reish Lakish resolves the contradiction between the mishna (pay a fine for raping one's sister) and Makkot (liable for lashes) by attributing our mishna to Rabbi Meir, who holds 'loken u'meshalem' — one is flogged AND pays.
