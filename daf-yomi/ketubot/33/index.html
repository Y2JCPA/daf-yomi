When Lashes Meet Payment: Which Penalty Prevails?

Gemara
If one who injures another pays and is not lashed, you've rendered the prohibition of excessive lashes moot!
Gemara
The verse about lashes can be fulfilled in a case where the blow caused no damage worth a perutah — so there is no payment and lashes apply.
R' Eliezer
Conspiring witnesses pay money and are not flogged because they cannot be properly forewarned (hasra'ah).
Reish Lakish
Our mishna follows Rabbi Meir, who holds: one is flogged AND pays.
Core Tension The daf explores whether monetary liability and corporal punishment can coexist, examining multiple sources and opinions.

Preserving Verses: How Lashes Apply Without Payment

Problem: If one who injures always pays instead of being lashed, the verse about lashes becomes moot!
↓
Conspiring Witnesses
Fulfilled when they testify a kohen is son of a divorcée/ḥalutzah — no payment, so they are lashed
Injuring Another
Fulfilled when the blow causes no damage worth a perutah — no payment, so he is lashed
Principle Even if the general rule is 'pays and is not lashed,' each verse about lashes must have at least one case where it applies.

The Verbal Analogy: 'Ki Innah' (Because He Tormented Her)

R' Yochanan
The verse 'ki innah' (Deut. 22:29) is not available for a verbal analogy — I need it for Abaye's derivation.
Abaye
The 50-dinar fine is 'because he tormented her' — implying there is additional compensation for humiliation (boshet) and degradation (pegam).
Ulla
I use 'ki innah' for the verbal analogy. I derive humiliation and degradation from Rava's teaching instead.
Rava
The superfluous phrase 'who lay with her' teaches: the 50 shekel is for the pleasure of lying — implying additional compensation for boshet and pegam.
Key Dispute Both opinions agree that a rapist pays humiliation and degradation beyond the fine — they disagree only on the scriptural source.

Can Conspiring Witnesses Be Forewarned?

🔔 Before testimony? — They can claim 'we forgot the warning'
↓
⏱️ During testimony? — They'll simply leave and not testify
↓
✅ After testimony? — Too late; what's done is done
↓
💡 Abaye's objection: Warn them within tokh kedei dibbur (the brief interval after testimony)
↓
👋 Rav Aḥa's objection: Warn before, then gesture during testimony as a reminder
↓
🎯 Abaye's conclusion: If they tried to kill without forewarning the defendant, can we really require forewarning of them?
R' Eliezer's Position Conspiring witnesses pay money and are not flogged because hasra'ah (forewarning) is practically impossible for them.

Two Sources for 'Pays and Is Not Flogged' for Injury

Rav Sheisha b. Rav Idi

  • Source: Men quarreling who hurt a pregnant woman (Exodus 21:22)
  • The quarrel involves potential death (capital liability)
  • He was forewarned for the severe matter (death)
  • Forewarning for severe = forewarning for lesser (lashes)
  • Yet Torah says: pay damages — not lashes

Rav Ya'akov (in name of Rava)

  • Source: One who struck another with stone or fist (Exodus 21:19)
  • Aggressor is imprisoned while victim recovers
  • If victim dies — aggressor is executed
  • If victim lives — aggressor pays lost wages and healing
  • Despite forewarning for death → he pays, not lashed
Note Both derivations rely on the same logic: one forewarned for a capital offense is also forewarned for lashes, yet the Torah prescribes payment.

Rav Ashi's Double Objection

Rav Ashi
First: Who says forewarning for a severe matter counts as forewarning for a lesser matter? Perhaps it doesn't.
Rav Ashi
Second: Who says death is more severe than lashes? Perhaps lashes are worse!
Rav
Had they flogged Hananiah, Mishael, and Azariah instead of the fiery furnace, they would have worshipped the idol. Lashes > death!
Rav Samma b. Rav Asi
There's a difference! Limited lashes (40 by Torah) are less severe. Unlimited lashes (to induce compliance) are more severe.
Dramatic Claim Rav's statement about Hananiah, Mishael, and Azariah is one of the most striking aggadic claims on the daf — physical suffering can break the will more than the threat of death.
