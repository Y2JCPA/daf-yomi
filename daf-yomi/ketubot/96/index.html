Ketubot 96: The Widow's Rights & Obligations

Widow sustained by heirs
What rights and duties apply?
↓
Her Rights
Found items belong to her (Shmuel)
Can seize movable property for sustenance
Must claim within 2–3 years
Her Duties
All wifely tasks except intimate ones:
filling cup, making bed, washing face/hands/feet
Key Theme The Gemara carefully calibrates which marital obligations transfer to heirs and which remain personal to the husband-wife bond.

Does a Widow Keep Found Articles?

Shmuel
Any lost article (metzia) found by a widow — she acquires for herself.
Gemara (Challenge)
If the mishna means the widow is sustained by heirs, shouldn't heirs be like the husband and own her finds?
Gemara (Answer)
The husband owns finds to prevent enmity (eiva) — he'd suspect the money's source. But heirs? Let them have enmity! The Sages weren't concerned about that.
Principle The rationale behind a husband's rights (preventing domestic strife) does not extend to heirs, so the widow keeps what she finds.

Tasks a Widow Performs vs. Does Not Perform for Heirs

Performs for Heirs ✅

  • Spinning, weaving, laundering
  • Cooking, baking, nursing
  • All standard household tasks

Does NOT Perform ❌

  • Filling his cup (pouring wine)
  • Making his bed
  • Washing his face, hands, and feet
  • These are expressions of affection (chibbah) — reserved for husband only
R' Yosei bar Ḥanina The boundary line is intimacy: functional duties transfer to heirs, but acts of personal affection do not.

Student Serving Teacher — Limits & Exceptions

AuthorityRuling
R' Yehoshua ben LeviA student performs all tasks a Canaanite slave does for his master — except untying his shoe
RavaThis restriction applies only where people don't know the student (he'd be mistaken for a slave)
Rav AshiEven where unknown — if the student wears tefillin, he won't be mistaken for a slave
R' Ḥiyya bar Abba / R' YoḥananPreventing a student from serving you withholds kindness from him (Job 6:14)
Rav Naḥman bar YitzḥakIt even removes fear of Heaven from the student
Insight Service to a teacher is itself a form of Torah learning — blocking it harms the student spiritually.

Widow Seizing Movable Property

Widow seizes movable property
from the estate
↓
For what purpose
did she seize it?
For Sustenance
She keeps it
(R' Elazar, confirmed by baraita & incident of R' Shabbtai's daughter-in-law)
For Ketubah Payment
Ravina: We remove it
Mar bar Rav Ashi: She keeps it — both sustenance & ketubah are supposed to come from real estate only
Dispute Ravina distinguishes sustenance from ketubah payment; Mar bar Rav Ashi says both have the same rule — if seizure works for one, it works for the other.

Forfeiting Sustenance by Delay

📋 R' Yoḥanan in the name of R' Yosei ben Zimra: A widow who waits and does not demand sustenance forfeits her right
↓
👩‍🦰 Poor woman — forfeits after 2 years (she clearly must have forgone the right)
↓
💎 Rich woman — forfeits after 3 years (she could support herself, so delay is less telling)
↓
🗣️ Unabashed woman — forfeits after 2 years (she wouldn't hesitate to ask if she wanted it)
↓
🤫 Modest woman — forfeits after 3 years (her shame may explain the delay)
Key Factor The time period depends on whether the delay is better explained by forgoing the right or by personal circumstances.
