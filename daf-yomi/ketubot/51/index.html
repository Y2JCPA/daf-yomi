Ketubot 51: Ketubah Defaults & Court-Imposed Obligations

Core Question: What obligations exist even without being written in the ketubah?
↓
The court establishes default stipulations (tenai beit din) that apply automatically
↓
Financial
Virgin: 200 dinars
Widow: 100 dinars
All property as surety
Captivity
Husband must redeem wife
Cannot divorce to avoid obligation
Illness
Husband must heal wife
But may divorce and pay ketubah
Key Principle Certain marital obligations are so fundamental that the court enforces them regardless of whether the husband wrote them in the ketubah.

Supporting an Orphan Girl from Her Brother's Estate

Rava
Increase the orphan boy's allocation so there is enough for the orphan girl as well.
The Sages
But Master, you ruled that sustenance cannot be collected from movable property — and that's all there is!
Rava
If the boy needed a maidservant, we'd buy him one from his estate. Kal va-chomer — his own sister can serve him and be supported!
Rava
Two factors justify it: she is his sister, and she will serve him — it benefits both orphans.
Insight Rava finds a creative halakhic path: framing the girl's sustenance as a benefit to the boy, allowing expenditure even from movable property.

Collecting from Estate Property: R' Yehuda HaNasi vs. R' Shimon ben Elazar

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi

  • Wife's sustenance collected from both land and movable property
  • Daughters' sustenance collected from both types
  • More expansive collection rights

R' Shimon ben Elazar

  • Daughters' sustenance only from land (property with guarantee)
  • Movable property: divided equally among same-gender heirs
  • If daughters seize movable property, it's taken and given to sons
Halakha Despite the usual rule that halakha follows R' Yehuda HaNasi over a colleague, here the halakha follows R' Shimon ben Elazar, as confirmed by Rava.

Default Ketubah Stipulations (Tenai Beit Din)

📜 Virgin collects 200 dinars, widow 100 dinars — even without a written ketubah
↓
🏠 All property serves as surety for the ketubah — even if not written
↓
🔓 Husband must redeem captive wife and restore her — cannot divorce to avoid it
↓
🏥 Husband must pay medical expenses — but may divorce and pay ketubah instead
↓
⚖️ R' Meir: reducing below the minimum makes relations licentious (be'ilat zenut)
Captivity vs. Illness The husband cannot escape the redemption obligation by divorcing, but he can escape the healing obligation by divorcing and paying the ketubah.

Property Guarantee (Acharayut) in Documents

Question: If a document omits the property guarantee clause, does the lien still apply?
↓
Rabbi Meir
Omission is intentional
No guarantee = no lien on sold property
Collect only from free assets
The Rabbis
Omission is a scribal error
Guarantee exists regardless
Can collect from purchasers
↓
Does this distinction apply equally to ketubot and other documents?
↓
A baraita shows R' Meir treats ketubah the same as other documents — no special leniency
Resolution The Gemara offers two ways to reconcile: (1) The Mishna follows R' Yehuda, and the case of 54b involves explicit waiver; or (2) The Mishna follows R' Meir, and 'obligated' means only from free assets.
