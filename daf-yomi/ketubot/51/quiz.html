Ketubot 51 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Rava permit using the orphan boy's movable property to support the orphan girl?
A. Because the halakha follows Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi who allows collecting from movable property
B. Because she is his sister AND she will serve him, making it beneficial to both
C. Because the court always prioritizes daughters over sons
D. Because Abaye ruled that attached dates count as land
Correct: B — Rava argues with a kal va-chomer: if the court would buy the boy a maidservant from his estate, certainly his own sister can be supported — she both serves him and benefits from the arrangement.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the halakha follow R' Shimon ben Elazar over R' Yehuda HaNasi on this daf, despite the usual rule favoring R' Yehuda HaNasi?
A. Because R' Shimon ben Elazar was his teacher
B. Because Rava explicitly ruled that sustenance is collected only from land, not movable property
C. Because the Mishna explicitly sides with R' Shimon ben Elazar
D. Because R' Yehuda HaNasi later retracted his opinion
Correct: B — Rava's independent ruling — that collection for ketubah, sustenance, and livelihood is only from land — aligns with R' Shimon ben Elazar, overriding the usual presumption in favor of R' Yehuda HaNasi.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the key difference between the husband's obligation to redeem his captive wife versus healing her illness?
A. Redemption applies only to a priest's wife
B. He can divorce and pay the ketubah to avoid healing, but cannot do so to avoid redemption
C. Healing is only required if specified in the ketubah
D. Redemption is required only for the first marriage
Correct: B — The Mishna teaches that the husband cannot escape the captive-redemption obligation by divorcing, but he may divorce and pay the ketubah to avoid paying medical expenses.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Shmuel's father, when would a definitely raped married woman be permitted to return to her husband?
A. Whenever there are witnesses that she was raped
B. Only if she is married to a priest
C. Only when witnesses confirm she screamed continuously from beginning to end
D. Whenever she claims it was entirely against her will
Correct: C — Shmuel's father fears that any rape may end with willing participation. The only way to be certain it remained entirely forced is if witnesses testify she screamed continuously throughout.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the dispute about omitting a property guarantee from a document, what does Rabbi Meir hold?
A. The omission is a scribal error and the guarantee still applies
B. The omission is intentional, so the creditor can only collect from the debtor's free assets
C. The omission applies only to ketubot but not to other documents
D. Found documents with guarantees should be returned to the lender
Correct: B — Rabbi Meir holds that omitting the property guarantee is intentional — therefore the creditor has no lien on sold property and may collect only from free (unsold) assets. The Rabbis disagree and treat the omission as a scribal error.
