Presumptive Virgin Found Non-Virgin: Ketubah Rights

Rabba
If one married a woman with the presumptive status of a virgin and she was found non-virgin — her ketubah is 100 dinars.
Rav Ashi
Generally she gets no ketubah at all — it's a mistaken transaction (mekaḥ ta'ut).
Rav Ashi
But here is different: the first husband brought her into his home. The second husband should have considered she may no longer be a virgin.
Gemara
But there are witnesses she didn't have intercourse with the first husband! Perhaps she committed adultery after betrothal to the second?
Rav Sherevya
The case is where he betrothed her and had intercourse immediately — no opportunity for adultery.
Key Distinction Two versions of this debate exist — one based on the baraita (with explicit witnesses), one on the mishna (entering the ḥuppah). The baraita version is the stronger proof.

Wedding Customs: Judea vs. Galilee

Judea

  • Groom and bride were secluded together before the wedding canopy (ḥuppah)
  • Two shushvinin (groomsmen) appointed — one for him, one for her
  • Groomsmen examined groom and bride at entry to ḥuppah
  • Groomsmen slept in the same house to verify the sheet immediately
  • Groom who eats at father-in-law's house cannot claim virginity loss

Galilee

  • No pre-wedding seclusion of bride and groom
  • No groomsmen appointed for examination
  • No overnight supervision of the couple
  • Groom retains right to make virginity claims
  • Less elaborate verification customs
Abaye's Insight Even within Judea there were different customs in different places — the mishna says 'one who eats,' implying not all grooms in Judea ate at the father-in-law's house.

The Shushvinin System in Judea

👫 Two shushvinin (groomsmen) appointed — one for the groom, one for the bride
↓
🔍 They examine the couple at entry to the wedding canopy
↓
🏠 Groomsmen sleep in the same house as the couple on the wedding night
↓
🩸 They verify the sheet immediately after consummation
↓
🛡️ Prevents groom from obscuring blood to falsely claim bride was not a virgin
↓
⚠️ Anyone who did not follow this custom cannot make a virginity claim
Purpose The shushvinin system protected both parties from deception — ensuring the groom couldn't hide evidence of virginity, and the bride couldn't fabricate it.

She Says Raped After Betrothal; He Says Before

Mishna
She says: After you betrothed me I was raped — your field was inundated (sadekha nishtefa), it is your misfortune.
Husband
No — you were raped before I betrothed you. My transaction was a mistaken transaction.
R' Gamliel & R' Eliezer
She is deemed credible — her certain claim prevails.
R' Yehoshua
We don't rely on her word alone. She has a presumption of pre-betrothal intercourse until she brings proof.
Halakha Shmuel rules the halakha follows Rabban Gamliel — even in this first dispute where the money is in the husband's possession, her certain claim (bari) prevails.
