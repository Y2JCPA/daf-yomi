Does a Change in Body Change the Punishment?

Gemara
A na'arah me'orasah sinned and then became a bageret (grown woman). The Mishnah says: she is sentenced to strangulation.
Gemara
Contradiction! A baraita about a motzi shem ra (defamer) says: even if she became a bageret and married, she is still stoned!
Rava
A defamer's case is different — it's a chiddush (novel halakha). Normally a married woman gets strangulation, yet here the Torah says stoning. So the defamer case follows the time of sin.
Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua
Perhaps the chiddush of motzi shem ra only applies when her body hasn't changed (still a na'arah). If she became a bageret, even the defamer case would be strangulation!
Rava's Principle The defamer case is inherently novel — a married woman executed by stoning — so you cannot extrapolate from it to other cases.

Tannaitic Dispute: Sin vs. Awareness (Horayot 10a)

Tanna Kamma (First Opinion)

  • A Kohen Gadol or King who sinned before appointment
  • Became aware after appointment
  • They are like commoners — bring a female sheep/goat
  • Status at time of sin determines the offering

Rabbi Shimon

  • If sin became known before appointment → liable as commoner
  • If sin became known after appointment → entirely exempt
  • Change in status nullifies original obligation
  • Analogous: na'arah who becomes bageret → strangulation, not stoning
Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak Whether bodily change affects the punishment is a tannaitic dispute — R' Shimon holds the change in status retroactively alters liability.

R' Yochanan vs. R' Chanina: 'The Young Woman' (הנערה)

R' Yochanan
Teach that she is sentenced to stoning, not strangulation — even if she became a bageret.
Gemara
But the Torah says 'na'arah' — and she is now a grown woman!
R' Ile'a
The verse says 'ha-na'arah' (THE young woman) — referring to the na'arah she already was at the time of her sin.
R' Chanina
If so, the defaming husband should also be flogged and pay 100 sela — since she was a na'arah at the time of sin!
R' Ile'a
May the Merciful One save us from this opinion! Her act caused her punishment (she was a na'arah then). His lips caused his punishment (she was a bageret then).
R' Chanina
May the Merciful One save us from your opinion!
Resolution (R' Yitzchak bar Avin) Her sin is judged by when she acted (she was a na'arah). His sin is judged by when he spoke (she was already a bageret). Different moments determine different liabilities.

Deriving the Place of Execution: Gezerah Shavah

Idol worshipper: "To your gates" (Deut. 17:5)
↓
Which gates? Gezerah shavah with "your gates" (Deut. 17:2) — the gate where he worshipped
↓
"Your gates" — not gates of gentiles; and the gate where he sinned
↓
Na'arah: "petach" (entrance) → linked to Tabernacle "petach" → linked to "sha'ar" (gate) → linked to idolatry "she'arekha"
↓
Na'arah without father's house → stoned at city gate, derived via R' Abbahu's chain of gezerah shavah
R' Abbahu A triple verbal analogy links 'entrance' (na'arah) → 'entrance of the gate' (Tabernacle) → 'gate' → 'your gates' (idolatry) to derive the default place of stoning.
