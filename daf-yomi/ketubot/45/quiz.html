Ketubot 45 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Where is a betrothed na'arah who committed adultery in her father's house stoned?
A. At the city gate
B. At the entrance to her father's house
C. At the entrance to the court
D. At the place where she sinned
Correct: B — The Mishnah states she is stoned at the entrance to her father's house, as if to say: 'See what you have raised.' This sends a message to the family.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava, why can't we derive from the defamer (motzi shem ra) case that a changed body doesn't affect the punishment?
A. Because the defamer case is a novel halakha (chiddush) with unique rules
B. Because the defamer case involves a married woman
C. Because Rava holds strangulation always applies
D. Because the Torah explicitly addresses this case separately
Correct: A — Rava explains that the defamer case is a chiddush — normally a married woman would receive strangulation, yet the Torah prescribes stoning. Being novel, it cannot serve as a model for other cases.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does R' Yitzchak bar Avin say the defaming husband does NOT pay the 100 sela fine even though his wife was a na'arah at the time of the alleged sin?
A. Because the fine only applies to virgins
B. Because his punishment is determined by when he spoke — and she was then a bageret
C. Because a defamer never pays a fine
D. Because the court cannot verify her prior status
Correct: B — Her punishment follows her act (she was a na'arah when she sinned → stoning). His punishment follows his speech (she was a bageret when he defamed her → no fine). Different triggering moments determine different liabilities.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rabbi Shimon's position in the Horayot mishna regarding a king who sinned before his appointment?
A. He brings a male goat like a king
B. He brings a female sheep like a commoner
C. He is entirely exempt if he learned of his sin after appointment
D. He brings both offerings to cover both statuses
Correct: C — R' Shimon holds that if the sin became known after appointment, the change in status nullifies the original obligation entirely — he is exempt. This parallels the view that a na'arah who becomes a bageret would have her punishment changed.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does R' Abbahu derive that a na'arah without a father's house is stoned at the city gate?
A. From a direct verse about the na'arah
B. From a kal va'chomer from idolatry
C. Through a chain of gezerah shavah: 'entrance' (na'arah) → 'entrance/gate' (Tabernacle) → 'your gates' (idolatry)
D. From the general principle that all executions occur at the city gate
Correct: C — R' Abbahu links 'petach' (entrance) from the na'arah verse to 'petach' in the Tabernacle verse that also mentions 'sha'ar' (gate), and then links 'gate' to 'your gates' in the idolatry verse — creating a triple verbal analogy.
