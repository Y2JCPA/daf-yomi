Ketubot 8 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Levi recite only five wedding blessings while Rav Asi recited six?
A. They disagreed about whether man was created in one act or two
B. They agreed on one act of creation but disagreed whether to follow God's initial thought or actual deed
C. Levi forgot one of the blessings
D. The sixth blessing had not yet been composed in Levi's time
Correct: B — The Gemara rejects the idea that they disagree about one vs. two acts of creation. Instead, all agree there was one act, but Levi follows the action (one creation = fewer blessings) while Rav Asi follows the initial thought (planned two = more blessings).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
After the first day of the wedding celebration, what determines whether all seven blessings are recited during the remaining six days?
A. Whether the groom declares it a hillula
B. Whether new faces (panim chadashot) are present
C. Whether a minyan of ten is available
D. Whether the couple has consummated the marriage
Correct: B — Rav Ashi's practice: on subsequent days, all seven blessings are recited only if there are new faces present. Without new faces, only the zimmun addition and one blessing (Asher Bara) are said.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the quorum requirement differ between wedding blessings and mourner's blessings?
A. Wedding blessings require 10 men; mourner's blessings require only 3
B. Both require 10 men, and both include the principals in the count
C. Both require 10 men, but grooms are included while mourners are excluded from the count
D. Wedding blessings require 7 men; mourner's blessings require 10
Correct: C — R' Yitzchak in the name of R' Yochanan teaches that both require a quorum of 10, but grooms count toward the 10 while mourners do not — because the mourner's blessings are recited on their behalf.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What lesson does Abaye derive from the mourner's consolation formula recited by Yehuda bar Naḥmani?
A. One should always mention future suffering to prepare the mourner
B. One should say 'many have drunk' (past tense) but not 'many will drink' (future) — don't open your mouth to Satan
C. One should never comfort a mourner on the first day
D. The consolation should focus exclusively on the merits of the deceased
Correct: B — Abaye corrects the formula based on the principle of al yiftach adam piv la'satan — a person should never open his mouth to Satan by speculating about future disasters, even in a comforting context.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Rav Pappa begin reciting 'In Whose dwelling is joy' from the time of betrothal rather than from when beer preparation began?
A. He held that betrothal is halakhically equivalent to marriage
B. The wedding preparations had already been completed, so only the designated time remained
C. He wanted to establish a new precedent for earlier celebration
D. The barley for beer was unavailable that year
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that Rav Pappa's case was unique: the wedding preparations had already been completed, so there was nothing left but to wait for the appointed time. Therefore, the celebration effectively began at betrothal.
