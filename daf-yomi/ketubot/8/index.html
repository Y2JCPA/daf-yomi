Ketubot 8: Wedding Blessings & Their Boundaries

Sheva Brachot
The Mishnah lists the seven wedding blessings recited under the chuppah
↓
How many blessings?
Dispute: Levi said 5, Rav Asi said 6
One Act of Creation
Man & woman created as one entity → only one 'Creator of mankind' blessing needed
Two Acts of Creation
Man & woman created separately → two distinct blessings for mankind's creation
↓
Resolution: All agree one act — dispute is whether we follow God's initial thought (two) or actual deed (one)
↓
Duration rules
Day 1: all 7 blessings · Days 2–7: depends on new faces · Days 7–30: 'In Whose dwelling is joy' · Up to 12 months if stated for hillula
Key Theme This daf explores both the joyous structure of wedding blessings and the parallel structure of mourner's blessings — linking celebration and consolation through the same halakhic framework of quorum and duration.

How Many Blessings? One Creation or Two?

Levi
Recited five blessings at the wedding of R' Shimon, son of Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
Rav Asi
Recited six blessings at the wedding of Mar, son of Rav Ashi
Gemara
Do they disagree whether man was created in one act (two-sided being) or two acts (separate creations)?
Gemara
No — all agree it was one act. The dispute: do we follow God's initial thought (to create two) or the actual action (created as one)?
Rav Yehuda
Resolves the contradiction: 'God created man in His image' (one) vs. 'Male and female He created them' (two) — thought was two, deed was one
Proof Text Genesis 1:27 indicates one creation; Genesis 5:2 indicates two. The resolution: God's initial plan was two beings, but the actual creation was a single dual-sided entity.

When Are Wedding Blessings Recited After the Wedding?

Day 1 of celebration
All 7 blessings are recited
↓
Are new faces (panim chadashot) present?
Yes — New Faces
Recite all seven blessings again
No — Same Guests
Only she'hasimcha bim'ono in zimmun + Asher bara after bentching
↓
After 7 days through day 30:
Recite she'hasimcha bim'ono (In Whose dwelling is joy) automatically
↓
After 30 days up to 12 months:
Only if groom explicitly states the meal is for the hillula (wedding celebration)
Rav Ashi's Practice Rav Ashi attended a wedding at Rav Kahana's house: Day 1 — all 7 blessings. Subsequent days — all 7 only if new faces; otherwise just the zimmun addition and one blessing.

Groom vs. Mourner: Parallel Quorum Rules

Wedding Blessings

  • Require a quorum of 10 men
  • The grooms are included in the count of 10
  • Recited for 7 days of celebration
  • New faces trigger full repetition of blessings

Mourner's Blessings

  • Require a quorum of 10 men
  • The mourners are NOT included in the count of 10
  • Recited in the square (rechavah) adjacent to the cemetery
  • New faces during shiva trigger repetition of blessings
R' Yitzchak / R' Yochanan The mourner is excluded from the quorum because the blessings are recited for the mourner — unlike the groom, who actively participates in the joy being blessed.
