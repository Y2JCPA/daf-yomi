Ketubot 62 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does R' Yoḥanan require a husband to spend TWO months at home for every one month studying, rather than equal time?
A. Because Torah study is less important than work
B. Because Torah study brings no monetary profit, so a wife won't tolerate equal absence
C. Because the Torah explicitly commands a 1:2 ratio
D. Because R' Yoḥanan was lenient on all marital obligations
Correct: B — R' Yoḥanan distinguishes Torah study from David's army service: soldiers earned profit (making the wife more willing to wait), but Torah study brings no monetary gain, so she requires more time at home.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What happened when the gentile reminded the Jewish man of the Temple's destruction to slow him down?
A. The Jew stopped walking entirely
B. The Jew sighed but kept walking faster than the gentile
C. The Jew collapsed from grief
D. The Jew refused to acknowledge the destruction
Correct: B — The Jew sighed but still walked faster. He explained that groaning only breaks the body for NEW sorrows — old, familiar grief no longer has the same physical effect. 'One accustomed to bereavement does not panic.'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Abaye, why can't a donkey driver become a camel driver without his wife's permission?
A. Because the Rabbis forbade changing professions
B. Because a woman prefers modest means with her husband present over wealth with abstinence
C. Because camel driving is a more dangerous profession
D. Because the Torah prohibits earning more than necessary
Correct: B — Abaye teaches the famous principle: 'A woman prefers a kav with conjugal relations to ten kav with abstinence.' Emotional closeness and a husband's presence outweigh material wealth.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What directly caused the death of Rav Reḥumi?
A. He was punished for studying on Yom Kippur
B. His wife cursed him for not returning
C. The roof collapsed under him at the moment his wife shed a tear of distress
D. He fell ill from exhaustion after years of study
Correct: C — Rav Reḥumi was sitting on the roof when his wife, expecting him on Erev Yom Kippur, shed a tear of distress at his absence. At that exact moment, the roof collapsed and he died — teaching the severity of causing a spouse anguish, even inadvertently.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How did Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi comfort his son who was ashamed for wanting to marry immediately rather than studying first?
A. He said Torah study could wait because marriage is more important
B. He compared his son's eagerness to God advancing the building of the Mishkan out of love
C. He said the bride's beauty was a sign from Heaven
D. He quoted a verse that marriage should always precede study
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi said: 'You have your Maker's perception!' God originally planned the Temple for after entering Eretz Yisrael, but out of closeness and love, He advanced the Mishkan to the desert — just as his son advanced marriage out of love.
