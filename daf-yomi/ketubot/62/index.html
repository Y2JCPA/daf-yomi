Ketubot 62: Balancing Torah Study & Marriage

Core Tension This daf explores the delicate balance between a husband's devotion to Torah study and his conjugal obligations to his wife. Through debates between Rav and R' Yoḥanan, dramatic stories of Rav Reḥumi and the son of Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, the Gemara demonstrates that neglecting a spouse carries severe consequences — while Torah study is paramount, a wife's emotional needs are sacred.

How Long May a Husband Leave to Study Torah?

Rav
A husband may spend one month in the study hall and one month at home — like King David's army rotating month by month (I Chronicles 27:1).
R' Yoḥanan
He may spend one month studying and two months at home — like the Temple workers: one month in Lebanon, two at home (I Kings 5:28).
Gemara
Why doesn't Rav use R' Yoḥanan's proof? Because Temple work could be done by others, but Torah study cannot — so he gets equal time away.
Gemara
Why doesn't R' Yoḥanan use Rav's proof? David's soldiers earned profit, so the wife would tolerate equal absence. Torah study has no monetary profit, so she won't wait as long.
Key Distinction The nature of the work — whether others can do it and whether it brings profit — determines how long a wife is expected to wait.

Does Groaning Break Half or All of a Person's Body?

Rav — Half the Body

  • Source: "Groan... with the breaking of your loins" (Ezekiel 21:11)
  • Loins = halfway point → groaning breaks half the body
  • The verse about total collapse refers specifically to the destruction of the Temple, which is uniquely devastating

R' Yoḥanan — Entire Body

  • Source: "Every heart shall melt, all hands slack, every spirit faint" (Ezekiel 21:12)
  • The verse describes total bodily collapse
  • "Breaking of loins" means that's where the sigh begins, not where it ends
Practical Story A gentile reminded a Jew of the Temple's destruction to slow him down. The Jew sighed but kept walking fast — because old, familiar grief no longer breaks the body. "One accustomed to bereavement does not panic."

ScholarDefinitionReasoning
RavaTorah students who review lectures at a local study hall dailyThey return home every evening
Abaye (rejects Rava)Wealthy men like R' Shmuel bar Sheilat — self-sufficient, no worriesTorah scholars' wives "deprive their eyes of sleep" — they are NOT home nightly

Abaye's Objection Psalms 127:2 says scholars' wives "eat the bread of toil" — indicating scholars work intensely and aren't home each evening. They can't be the 'men of leisure.'

Can a Donkey Driver Become a Camel Driver?

A married donkey driver (ḥammar) wants to become a camel driver (gammal) for more money
↓
This would change conjugal interval from weekly to monthly
↓
More Money
10 kav with abstinence
Her Preference
1 kav with closeness
↓
Abaye's ruling: He may NOT change professions without her permission — a woman prefers modest means with her husband present
Principle A woman prefers a kav (small measure) with conjugal relations over ten kav with abstinence. Emotional closeness outweighs material wealth.

The Tragedy of Rav Reḥumi

📚 Rav Reḥumi studied before Rava in Meḥoza, coming home every Erev Yom Kippur
↓
📖 One year he was engrossed in his studies and did not leave the study hall
↓
🏠 His wife waited expectantly: "Now he is coming, now he is coming..."
↓
😢 He did not come. She was distressed and a tear fell from her eye
↓
⚠️ At that exact moment, the roof collapsed under Rav Reḥumi and he died
Moral Even inadvertent neglect of a spouse's emotional expectations carries severe consequences. The Sages relied on the lenient opinion allowing 2-3 years away — but the results were sometimes fatal.
