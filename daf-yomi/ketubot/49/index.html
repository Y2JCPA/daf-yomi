Ketubot 49: A Father's Duty to Feed His Children

אין האב חייב במזונות בתו
CORE QUESTION Is a father obligated to sustain his children, or is it merely a mitzva? The Mishna rules he is not obligated to feed his daughter — but the Gemara explores whether this means there is still a mitzva, and how the courts handle fathers who refuse.
3 / 9

Woman Leaving Father's Domain

Father delivers daughter to husband's messengers
↓
She is widowed or divorced en route to the chuppah
↓
Maybe Father's Domain?
She returned home — perhaps father can nullify vows again?
Rava's Answer
School of R' Yishmael teaches: once she left father's domain, even briefly, he cannot nullify vows (Numbers 30:10)
PRINCIPLE Once a woman leaves her father's authority — even momentarily — she retains her independent status, even if she physically returns home.

The Mishna: No Obligation to Sustain Daughters

R' Elazar ben Azarya
Sons inherit the ketubah only after the father's death. Daughters are sustained from his estate only after his death. During his lifetime — no obligation.
Gemara
The Mishna says he's not obligated for his daughter — implying he is obligated for his son? And for his daughter there's at least a mitzva?
Gemara
Whose opinion does this Mishna follow? It doesn't match R' Meir, R' Yehuda, or R' Yochanan ben Beroka cleanly!
KEY PHRASE אין האב חייב במזונות בתו — A father is not obligated to provide his daughter's sustenance.

Usha Enactment: Sustain Minor Children

The Enactment

  • R' Ile'a in the name of Reish Lakish: In Usha, the Sages instituted that a man should sustain his minor sons and daughters
  • Dilemma raised: Is this the binding halakha in practice?

The Practice

  • Courts did NOT force fathers to pay
  • Instead, Amoraim used public shaming and moral pressure
  • Conclusion: Not a binding court-enforceable obligation
EXCEPTION A wealthy father can be coerced — just as Rava compelled Rav Natan bar Ami to give 400 dinars to charity.

How Amoraim Shamed Refusing Fathers

📋 Father refuses to sustain his minor children
↓
🦊 Rav Yehuda: 'The jackal bears young and casts them on the townspeople?!'
↓
🪨 Rav Ḥisda: 'Stand him on a mortar in public — let him announce: The raven feeds its young, but this man does not!'
↓
🤲 Rava: 'Is it satisfactory that your children are sustained by charity?'
↓
💰 But if wealthy — the court coerces him, based on the obligation of tzedakah
NOTE The Gemara asks: do ravens actually feed their young? Answer: black ravens do; white ravens do not (Psalms 147:9 refers to white ones).
