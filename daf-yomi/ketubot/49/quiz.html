Ketubot 49 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, why is a father not obligated to sustain his daughters during his lifetime?
A. Because daughters can work to support themselves
B. Because the obligation to sustain daughters from the estate applies only after the father's death, paralleling when sons inherit the ketubah
C. Because the Torah explicitly exempts fathers from this duty
D. Because daughters are sustained by their future husbands
Correct: B — R' Elazar ben Azarya equates the sons' inheritance of the ketubah (only after death) with daughters' sustenance (only after death) — both apply post-mortem, not during the father's lifetime.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the school of R' Yishmael derive from Numbers 30:10 about a woman widowed en route to the chuppah?
A. She must return to her father's house and his authority resumes
B. Her husband's family must support her
C. Once she left her father's domain, even briefly, he can no longer nullify her vows
D. She may nullify her own vows only with a beit din
Correct: C — The verse about a widow/divorcée's vows teaches that once a woman leaves her father's domain — even momentarily — she has permanently left his authority regarding vow nullification.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How did Rav Ḥisda respond when a father refused to sustain his children?
A. He forced the father to pay through court order
B. He excommunicated the father
C. He had the father stand on an overturned mortar in public and shame himself
D. He arranged for the community to adopt the children
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda used public shaming — making the father stand on an overturned mortar and publicly declare that even a raven feeds its young, but he does not. This was moral pressure, not legal coercion.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Under what circumstance can a court actually coerce a father to sustain his children?
A. When the children are under age three
B. When the father is wealthy
C. When the mother has died
D. When the children are studying Torah
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes: a non-wealthy father receives only moral pressure, but a wealthy father can be coerced — just as Rava compelled the wealthy Rav Natan bar Ami to give 400 dinars to charity.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What objection was raised against the Usha enactment that a man who gives all his property to his sons is still sustained from it?
A. It contradicts the principle that gifts are irrevocable
B. It is unnecessary because a greater novelty already exists — a widow is sustained even from property inherited by her daughter's husband
C. It was never actually enacted at Usha
D. It applies only to sons, not daughters
Correct: B — R' Zeira objected that this ruling is obvious: we already know the greater novelty that a widow is sustained from her husband's estate even after it passes through the daughter to the daughter's husband — so certainly a man himself can be sustained from what he gave his sons.
