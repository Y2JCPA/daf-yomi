Penalties for Direct Business Between Divorced Priest & Ex-Wife

Background
A kohen borrowed from his wife's nichsei melog property, then divorced her. She must be repaid through an intermediary — not directly.
Rav Sheshet
If they violated this and did business directly, we do not attend to their case in court.
Rav Pappa
We excommunicate (menadin) them for the violation.
Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua
We also flog them with lashes.
Escalating Penalties Three Amoraim offer progressively stricter consequences — from ignoring, to excommunication, to corporal punishment — showing how seriously the Sages took preventing renewed intimacy.

Divorced from Marriage vs. Betrothal

Divorced from Marriage

  • Already accustomed to each other
  • Must use an intermediary for repayment
  • Direct contact risks transgression
  • Full separation required

Divorced from Betrothal

  • Never shared intimacy
  • She may be repaid directly
  • No concern of renewed familiarity
  • Source: Rav Nahman citing Evel Rabbati
Rava's Case Even a couple divorced from betrothal needed an intermediary when Rava (or Rav Adda bar Mattana) observed that they were already accustomed to each other — the rule follows the reality, not just the technical status.

Teruma Distribution: Slave or Priest?

Gemara
If a minor testifies someone shared teruma at the threshing floor — perhaps he was a slave of a priest, not a priest himself?
R' Yehuda
One distributes teruma to a slave only if his master is present. So someone receiving alone must be a kohen.
R' Yosei
A slave can receive teruma alone — he just says: 'If I'm a priest, for my sake; if a slave, for my master's sake.'
Key Difference
In R' Yehuda's place, eating teruma elevates to priestly status. In R' Yosei's place, it does not.
R' Elazar b. R' Yosei He once testified about teruma distribution he witnessed in R' Yosei's locale — and the court in R' Yehuda's locale nearly elevated a slave to priesthood based on his testimony. God protects the righteous from causing harm.
