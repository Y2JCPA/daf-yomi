Ketubot 77: Blemishes, Forced Divorce & Divine Reward

Husband develops blemishes
Can the court force divorce?
Minor Blemishes
Rabbis: Court does not force divorce
Major Blemishes
R' Shimon b. Gamliel: Court does force divorce
↓
Special Category: Boils, polyp, foul occupations
Court always forces divorce
↓
R' Yehoshua ben Levi: Studied Torah among ra'atan patients
"Torah bestows grace and protects"
Key Theme The daf balances a spouse's right to dignity with compassion toward the afflicted — culminating in R' Yehoshua ben Levi's radical empathy.

When Did the Blemishes Arise?

Rav Yehuda
The Mishna speaks of blemishes that developed after marriage — court does not force divorce.
Rav Yehuda
And kal va-chomer: if he had them before, she knew and accepted them!
Ḥiyya bar Rav
No — the Mishna refers to blemishes he had before marriage. She saw and accepted them.
Ḥiyya bar Rav
But if they developed after marriage — she never agreed to this! He must divorce.
Practical Difference According to Ḥiyya bar Rav, post-marriage blemishes are worse — she married under different conditions. Rav Yehuda says the opposite: post-marriage, she already committed.

Major vs. Minor Blemishes

Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel

  • Minor blemishes: court does NOT force divorce
  • Major blemishes: court DOES force divorce
  • Examples: eye blinded, hand cut off, leg broken
  • Even if she initially accepted — she can say: 'I thought I could handle it, but I cannot'

The Rabbis (Chachamim)

  • No distinction between major and minor
  • Court does not force divorce for any blemish
  • She accepted the situation at marriage
  • Rava / Rav Nachman: Halakha follows the Rabbis
Halakhic Ruling R' Yochanan rules like Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel. The Gemara reconciles this with his general principle that halakha always follows RSBG except in three cases.

The Sidon Incident A tanner died childless; his brother (also a tanner) needed to do yibum. The Sages ruled: she can say 'I accepted your brother but not you' — he must do chalitza.

Ra'atan: Cause, Symptoms & Cure

🩸 Cause: Bloodletting followed immediately by relations without eating
↓
👁️ Symptoms: Eyes water, nose runs, drool from mouth, flies settle on patient
↓
🧪 Cure (Abaye): Cook pila, ladanum, nut shell, hide shavings, artemisia, date-palm calyx
↓
🏠 Bring patient into sealed marble house (or 7½-brick-thick walls)
↓
💧 Pour 300 cups of mixture on his head until skull softens
↓
🔬 Open skull, place myrtle leaves under each foot of the insect, remove with tweezers
↓
🔥 Burn the insect — if not burned, it returns to the patient
Medical Context Rav Pappa qualifies: the danger only applies if one ate nothing between bloodletting and relations. If he tasted something, there is no problem.

The Sages & Ra'atan: Fear vs. Faith

How did the Sages relate to ra'atan patients?
Avoidance
R' Yochanan: Beware the flies!
R' Zeira: Wouldn't sit downwind
R' Elazar: Wouldn't enter their tent
R' Ami & R' Asi: Wouldn't eat eggs from their alley
Compassion
R' Yehoshua ben Levi:
Attached himself to ra'atan patients and studied Torah with them
"If Torah bestows grace, surely it protects!" (Proverbs 5:19)
Measure for Measure R' Yehoshua ben Levi's extraordinary compassion is directly rewarded — he merits entering paradise alive, a distinction shared by very few in the Talmud.
