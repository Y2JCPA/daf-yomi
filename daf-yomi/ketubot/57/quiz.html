Ketubot 57 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Rav Yosef initially tell the couple with the lost ketubah that they could remain together?
A. He held the halakha follows the Rabbis, who allow 2-3 years without a written ketubah
B. He believed a verbal declaration replaces a written ketubah
C. He thought the witnesses could testify to the original amount
D. He ruled that a lost ketubah automatically renews itself
Correct: A — Rav Yosef cited Shmuel's statement that R' Meir's strict view (no ketubah = licentious) is only R' Meir's, while the Rabbis allow living without one for 2-3 years since she relies on eventual collection. Abaye then corrected him that the halakha follows R' Meir in his decrees.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the Gemara's harmonization of Rav Dimi and Ravin, when do R' Yehuda and R' Yosei agree that a bride CANNOT verbally waive her ketubah rights?
A. Before the wedding ceremony
B. During the wedding ceremony
C. After consummation of the marriage
D. At the time of betrothal
Correct: C — Both Rav Dimi's and Ravin's traditions, as harmonized through R' Abbahu's testimony from R' Yoḥanan, agree that after consummation all opinions hold the wife cannot waive her rights verbally — she must write a receipt.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara derive that a virgin gets 12 months to prepare for her wedding?
A. From a direct statement in Deuteronomy about marriage preparation
B. From the word 'yamim' (days) in Rebecca's story, meaning a year, plus 'or ten' meaning ten months
C. From a tradition of Moshe at Sinai (halakha l'Moshe mi'Sinai)
D. From the 12 tribes of Israel as a symbolic number
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda derives from Genesis 24:55 — 'Let the damsel abide with us for days (yamim) or ten.' Since 'yamim' unmodified means a year (as in Leviticus 25:29), and 'ten' means months, the total preparation time equals twelve months.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say a father may delay his minor daughter's wedding until she reaches majority?
A. Because the Torah requires the father's consent for all marriages
B. Because he fears she doesn't fully understand marriage and may later rebel and return as a burden
C. Because he wants to collect a higher bride price when she is older
D. Because a minor cannot legally consent to any contract
Correct: B — The Gemara explains the father's concern: she may agree now without fully understanding, then later realize it was a mistake, rebel against her husband, and come back to be a burden on her father. He prefers she wait until she has full awareness.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What methodological principle does Rav Pappa teach about resolving conflicting amoraic traditions?
A. Always follow the later authority (hilkheta k'batrai)
B. It is preferable to say Amora'im disagree about reasoning (sevara) rather than about transmitted traditions
C. The Babylonian tradition always overrides the Eretz Yisrael tradition
D. When two traditions conflict, both are rejected in favor of the Mishna
Correct: B — Rav Pappa teaches that it is more plausible for a dispute to be about logical reasoning than about the correct transmission of a halakhic tradition. Therefore, when possible, we should attribute disagreements to differences in analysis rather than errors in oral transmission.
