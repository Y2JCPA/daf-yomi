The Lost Ketubah: Must It Be Replaced Immediately?

Rav Yosef
Rav Yehuda said in Shmuel's name: The rule that reducing the ketubah makes the marriage be'ilat zenut (licentious) is only R' Meir's view.
Rav Yosef
The Rabbis say: Since she relies on eventually collecting, a man may keep his wife 2–3 years without a written ketubah.
Abaye
But Rav Naḥman said Shmuel said: The halakha follows R' Meir in all his gezeirot (decrees)! This ruling about the ketubah is a decree!
Rav Yosef
If so — go write her a new ketubah.
Practical Halakha Since the halakha follows R' Meir's decrees, a lost ketubah must be replaced — the couple cannot simply rely on the oral obligation.

Verbal Waiver of Ketubah: Beginning vs. End

R' Yehuda

  • A bride can verbally stipulate to reduce her ketubah
  • Verbal confirmation is sufficient
  • Applies at certain stages of the marriage process

R' Yosei

  • A bride cannot waive rights by verbal statement alone
  • She must write a formal receipt
  • Greater protection of the wife's financial interests
Core Dispute R' Yehuda and R' Yosei disagree about whether a woman can verbally waive her ketubah rights — the key question is at which stage of the marriage process this dispute applies.

Harmonizing Rav Dimi & Ravin's Traditions

Rav Dimi's Version
R' Yehoshua ben Levi: Dispute applies only to the beginning (wedding ceremony).
After consummation — all agree she cannot waive verbally.
↓
Ravin's Version
R' Yehoshua ben Levi: Dispute applies only to the end (after ceremony).
At the beginning — all agree she can waive verbally.
↓
R' Abbahu resolves:
R' Yoḥanan told me personally — we don't actually disagree!
↓
Both agree: dispute is about post-ceremony / pre-consummation.
Before ceremony: she can waive. After consummation: she cannot.
R' Abbahu's Key Rav Dimi's 'beginning' = start of ceremony. Ravin's 'end' = end of ceremony. Both refer to the same transitional period — the dispute of the Tanna'im applies only between the ceremony and consummation.

Rav Pappa's Methodological Principle

ניחא ליה לאוקמי פלוגתא בסברא מלאוקמי פלוגתא בשמועה
Legal Methodology When interpreting conflicting amoraic traditions, it is preferable to say two Amora'im disagree about their own reasoning rather than to say they disagree about what another Amora said.

A dispute about logic (sevara) is more plausible than a dispute about the accurate transmission of a tradition.

However, since R' Abbahu testified that R' Yoḥanan personally told him otherwise, Rav Pappa defers to this direct testimony.
