Ketubot 20 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman, when two pairs of witnesses contradict each other about the validity of a document, what happens?
A. The second pair is believed because they are more recent
B. The first pair is believed because they signed the document
C. Both testimonies are neutralized and money stays with its current owner
D. The court must investigate which pair is more credible
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman's principle: establish two against two — the testimonies cancel out, and the money remains in the possession of its owner (as in the bar Shatya case).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the bar Shatya case, when does the property NOT remain with bar Shatya even though the witnesses neutralize each other?
A. When bar Shatya is currently insane
B. When bar Shatya purchased (not inherited) the property himself
C. When the buyer has witnesses that the sale was valid
D. When more than 60 years have passed since the sale
Correct: B — If bar Shatya bought the property himself (rather than inheriting it), he may have purchased it while insane — so he has no presumptive ownership, and the buyer keeps it.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why can't a litigant's own documents be used to authenticate signatures by comparison?
A. Documents in one's own possession are presumed invalid
B. The litigant may have practiced copying the signatures and forged them
C. Self-serving documents are never admissible
D. Only court-validated documents can be used for comparison
Correct: B — Rav Shimi bar Ashi explains: if the documents were in the litigant's possession, he could have learned to copy the signatures. But from another's possession, he can't reproduce them accurately from memory alone.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the halakha regarding a litigant reminding a witness of testimony the witness forgot?
A. The witness may never testify after being reminded
B. The witness may always testify after being reminded by anyone
C. Generally the witness may not testify, but a Torah scholar may
D. The witness may testify only if two other people also remind him
Correct: C — The halakha follows Mar son of Rav Ashi that a litigant may not remind a witness — except for a Torah scholar, who would not testify unless he truly remembered independently.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject Rav Ḥisda's derivation that testimony is only remembered for 60 years (based on R' Meir's rule about mounds)?
A. Because R' Meir's opinion is not authoritative
B. Because mounds involve no obligation to remember, while testimony does — so testimony may be remembered longer
C. Because 60 years is only a rabbinic estimate, not a Torah law
D. Because written testimony extends the time limit indefinitely
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes: no one was responsible for remembering the mound's status, so it's forgotten after 60 years. But testimony carries an obligation (responsibility was imposed), so it may be remembered even longer.
