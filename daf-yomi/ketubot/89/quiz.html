Ketubot 89 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the mishna, what happens when a woman produces a ketubah but no get, and each party claims the other's document was lost?
A. She collects the full ketubah amount
B. She collects only the main sum
C. She cannot collect at all
D. The court splits the amount
Correct: C — The mishna rules that when she has a ketubah but no get, and he claims his receipt was lost just as her get was lost, these debts cannot be collected. The standoff means she cannot prove her claim.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rav's final ruling about a woman who produces only a get?
A. She collects nothing without a ketubah
B. She collects the main sum (ikar ketubah) only
C. She collects the full ketubah including tosefet
D. She must produce witnesses to collect
Correct: B — Rav retracted his earlier position and ruled that in all places, a woman who produces only a get collects the main sum (200 for a virgin, 100 for a non-virgin). If she later produces a ketubah, she collects only the additional sum (tosefet).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel allow women to collect ketubot without a get?
A. He believed receipts were sufficient safeguard
B. During the time of danger, people destroyed documents to avoid persecution
C. He held that ketubot are never written
D. He disagreed with the concept of requiring proof
Correct: B — When the ruling authorities banned mitzvah observance, people destroyed gittin and prosbol documents immediately after signing to avoid being caught. Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel therefore assumed documents were written but could not be preserved.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara prove that receipts must sometimes be written when a debt is repaid?
A. From the case of a creditor with a prosbol
B. From Rav's ruling about the main sum
C. From the case of a widow from betrothal collecting with death witnesses
D. From Shmuel's two-case distinction
Correct: C — A widow from betrothal doesn't live with her husband, so there's no way to know if he divorced her. Without receipts, she could collect multiple times using death witnesses in different courts. This proves receipts are necessary where no other safeguard exists.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Shmuel (as explained by Rav Anan), who bears the burden of proof in a place where ketubot are customarily NOT written?
A. The wife must prove the ketubah was never paid
B. The husband must prove he wrote a ketubah
C. The court investigates independently
D. Neither party — collection is automatic
Correct: B — In a place where ketubot are not written, if the husband claims he wrote one (contradicting local custom), the burden is on him to prove it. If he cannot, she collects based on the rabbinic enactment without needing to produce a document.
