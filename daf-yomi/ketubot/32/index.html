Lashes or Payment: Which Takes Priority?

Gemara
Our Mishna says one pays a fine for relations with his sister. But in Makkot, the Mishna says one receives lashes. How can both be true?
Ulla
No contradiction! The Mishna here discusses a na'arah (fine applies), while Makkot discusses a bogeret (no fine, so lashes apply).
R' Yochanan
Both discuss a na'arah! With hasra'ah (forewarning) → lashes. Without forewarning → pays the fine.
Gemara
So Ulla holds: money wins over lashes. R' Yochanan holds: lashes win when forewarned.
Core Dispute When both lashes and payment apply, which takes priority? Ulla says payment; R' Yochanan says lashes (with forewarning).

Ulla's Resolution: Narrowing the Sister Case

Problem: One Mishna says pay a fine for relations with sister; another says lashes
↓
Mishna here = sister who is a na'arah (young woman) → fine applies, no lashes
↓
But a bogeret still owes boshet/pegam (humiliation/degradation)?
↓
Case: sister is a shotah (imbecile) — no humiliation payment
↓
But there is still payment for pain?
↓
Case: she was seduced (mefutah), not raped — no pain payment
Key Insight Ulla narrows the Makkot case to a seduced imbecile bogeret sister — eliminating all monetary obligations so that lashes alone apply.

Ulla vs. R' Yochanan: When Both Lashes and Payment Apply

Ulla

  • Payment always takes priority over lashes
  • Derives principle from chovel + edim zomemim
  • Also uses gezeira shava: tachat/tachat
  • Two Mishnayot refer to different cases (na'arah vs. bogeret)
  • Lashes only when no monetary liability exists

R' Yochanan

  • Lashes take priority when forewarned (hasra'ah)
  • Derives from: 'For one wickedness' — not two punishments
  • The verse specifies lashes as the chosen punishment
  • Both Mishnayot refer to a na'arah
  • Without forewarning → pays the fine instead
Bottom Line Ulla: Money wins. R' Yochanan: Lashes win (with hasra'ah). Both agree you cannot receive both punishments.

Can We Learn from Chovel (One Who Injures)?

Gemara
Where does Ulla learn that money takes priority? From chovel b'chaveiro — one who injures pays money, not lashes.
Challenger
Chovel is uniquely stringent — he pays five types of indemnity (nezek, tza'ar, ripui, shevet, boshet)!
Challenger
And chovel has a lenient aspect: injury is permitted in court (administering lashes)!
Gemara
Then learn from edim zomemim (conspiring witnesses) — they pay money, not lashes.
Challenger
Edim zomemim are uniquely stringent — no forewarning required! And lenient — they performed no action, only speech!
Gemara
Then learn from both together — the common denominator (tzad ha-shaveh): money over lashes.
Tzad Ha-Shaveh Neither paradigm case alone suffices. But their common element — liability for both money and lashes, with money taking priority — establishes the general principle.

R' Yochanan's Derivation: 'For One Wickedness'

כְּדֵי רִשְׁעָתוֹ — עַל רִשְׁעָה אַחַת אַתָּה מְחַיְּבוֹ, וְאִי אַתָּה מְחַיְּבוֹ עַל שְׁתֵּי רְשָׁעוֹת
Key Verse "The judge shall cause him to lie down and be beaten… according to the measure of his wickedness" (Deut. 25:2) — For one act of wickedness you punish him, not for two. The next verse specifies lashes ("Forty shall he strike him"), teaching that when both punishments apply and the offender was forewarned, lashes take precedence over payment.

📋 Ketubot 32 — Summary Map

Contradiction: Mishna says pay fine for sister; Makkot says lashes for sister
↓
Ulla: Different cases — na'arah (fine) vs. bogeret/shotah/mefutah (lashes). Money wins over lashes. Source: gezeira shava tachat/tachat
R' Yochanan: Both = na'arah. With hasra'ah → lashes; without → fine. Lashes win when forewarned. Source: k'dei rish'ato
↓
Both agree: You cannot be punished twice for one transgression (אינו לוקה ומשלם)
↓
Exception (R' Ile'a): Torah explicitly amplified chovel & edim zomemim for payment — these are special cases where money takes priority even per R' Yochanan
