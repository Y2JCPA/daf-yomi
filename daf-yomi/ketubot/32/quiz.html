Ketubot 32 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Ulla, why does the Mishna in Makkot rule that one who has relations with his sister receives lashes (not a fine)?
A. Because the case involves a bogeret/shotah/mefutah — eliminating all monetary obligations
B. Because the offender was forewarned by witnesses
C. Because lashes always take priority over payment
D. Because the Torah explicitly states lashes for this sin
Correct: A — Ulla resolves the contradiction by saying the Makkot case involves a seduced imbecile bogeret sister — no fine (bogeret), no humiliation (imbecile), no pain (seduced) — leaving only lashes.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is R' Yochanan's source that lashes take priority over payment when forewarned?
A. The gezeira shava of tachat/tachat
B. The verse 'according to the measure of his wickedness' — one punishment, not two, and the next verse specifies lashes
C. The common denominator of chovel and edim zomemim
D. The principle that lashes are always more severe than money
Correct: B — R' Yochanan derives from 'k'dei rish'ato' (Deut. 25:2) that only one punishment is given, and the following verse ('Forty shall he strike him') specifies that punishment is lashes.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why can't the principle of money over lashes be derived from chovel (one who injures) alone?
A. Because chovel involves only one type of payment
B. Because chovel is both uniquely stringent (5 indemnities) and uniquely lenient (permitted in court)
C. Because chovel never involves lashes
D. Because the Torah does not mention lashes for chovel
Correct: B — Chovel cannot serve as a paradigm because it has a unique stringency (five types of indemnity) and a unique leniency (injury is permitted when administered by the court as lashes).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Ile'a, how does the Torah indicate that conspiring witnesses pay money rather than receive lashes?
A. The verse says 'you shall not bear false witness' which implies monetary penalty
B. The verse 'hand for hand' indicates something given hand-to-hand — i.e., money
C. The verse says conspiring witnesses must repay double
D. The Oral Torah tradition from Sinai specifies payment
Correct: B — R' Ile'a explains that since 'you shall do unto him as he conspired' already covers the punishment, the additional phrase 'hand for hand' teaches that the priority punishment involves giving hand-to-hand — meaning money.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If R' Yochanan holds that lashes take priority, how does he explain that one who injures another pays money even when forewarned?
A. He concedes that Ulla is correct in the case of injury
B. He says the Torah explicitly amplified chovel for payment, making it a special exception
C. He says injury never truly involves lashes
D. He holds that one who injures does receive both lashes and payment
Correct: B — R' Yochanan agrees chovel pays money even when forewarned, but explains this is because the Torah explicitly amplified the case of chovel for payment (R' Ile'a's teaching), making it a specific exception to the general rule.
