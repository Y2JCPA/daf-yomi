The Surprising Defense of Charity Swindlers

R' Ḥanina in name of R' Elazar
Come and appreciate the swindlers who ask for charity they don't need!
Gemara
Why would we appreciate dishonest people?
R' Elazar
Were it not for them, we would sin daily by failing to support the truly poor. Their presence gives us partial excuse.
R' Yehoshua ben Korḥa
Averting eyes from charity is like idol worship — both use the word beliya'al (Deut. 15:9 & 13:14).
Baraita
One who fakes blindness, illness, or lameness to collect charity — will not leave this world before truly suffering that ailment.
Gezera Shava The word בליעל (beliya'al) links the sin of ignoring the poor (Deut. 15:9) to idol worship (Deut. 13:14).

How to Calculate the Dowry

R' Yehuda

  • Second daughter gets same as first
  • Father's prior giving sets the standard
  • Precedent-based approach

The Rabbis (Shmuel's reading)

  • Appraise current property value
  • Circumstances change — rich become poor, poor become rich
  • Give one-tenth of current estate
  • Do NOT speculate what father would have given
Shmuel vs. Baraita Shmuel says: evaluate based on the father's circumstances. The Baraita disagrees: appraise the property directly, don't speculate about the father's wishes.

Matured Orphan: Does She Lose Her Dowry?

R' Yehuda HaNasi
Daughters who matured or married lose sustenance (מזונות) but not their dowry support (פרנסה).
R' Shimon b. Elazar
They lose even their dowry support once they mature. They must rush to marry before maturity!
Rav Naḥman / Rav Huna
The halakha follows R' Yehuda HaNasi — orphans collect dowries even after maturing.
Rava (objecting)
But the Mishna implies she collects only because she married as a minor — not as an adult!
Resolution
R' Yehuda HaNasi distinguishes: if she protests, she collects. If she's silent, she waives.
Halakha An orphan daughter who matures can still collect her dowry — provided she actively protests (מחאה) her reduced share.
