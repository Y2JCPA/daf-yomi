Ketubot 68 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Elazar, why should we 'appreciate' charity swindlers?
A. They expose weaknesses in the charity system
B. Without them, we would sin daily for neglecting the truly poor
C. They motivate real poor people to work harder
D. They teach us to be more discerning with our money
Correct: B — R' Elazar argues that because swindlers take our charity money, we have a partial excuse for not fully meeting the needs of the truly poor — without them, our failure would be a daily sin (Deut. 15:9).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What verbal link (gezera shava) connects neglecting charity to idol worship?
A. The word 'sin' (ḥet) in both verses
B. The word 'base' (beliya'al) in Deut. 15:9 and Deut. 13:14
C. The word 'heart' (levav) in both contexts
D. The word 'brother' (aḥ) in both passages
Correct: B — R' Yehoshua ben Korḥa links 'beliya'al' in the charity verse (Deut. 15:9) to 'beliya'al' in the idolatry verse (Deut. 13:14), showing that ignoring the poor is as grave as idol worship.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava son of Rabba, which possessions must a charity recipient sell?
A. Gold cups and silver plates
B. His bed and table
C. Decorative items like a silver comb
D. His house and all furnishings
Correct: C — Rava son of Rabba rules that necessities — even luxurious ones like gold cups or fine beds — need not be sold. Only decorative novelties like a silver table-comb must be sold.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Under what condition can a mature orphan still collect her full dowry, according to the Gemara's resolution of R' Yehuda HaNasi's statements?
A. Only if she was married before age 12
B. Only if her brothers voluntarily offer it
C. If she actively protests (מחאה) her reduced share
D. Only if the father left a will specifying it
Correct: C — The Gemara resolves the contradiction in R' Yehuda HaNasi's rulings: a mature orphan who protests retains her dowry rights; one who remains silent implicitly waives them.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Mattana, what happens when all daughters come to marry at the same time?
A. Only the eldest receives one-tenth
B. They each take one-tenth successively from remaining property
C. They each take one-tenth but then redistribute so all shares are equal
D. The court divides the entire estate equally among them
Correct: C — Normally each daughter takes one-tenth of the remaining estate successively (earlier daughters get more). When all marry at once, the shares are recalculated and redistributed equally.
