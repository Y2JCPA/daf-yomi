Ketubot 43: Father's Rights Over His Daughter

Father's rights over daughter: earnings, fines, ketubah
↓
When does the father lose these rights?
Betrothal only
Father retains rights to ketubah & earnings
Full Marriage (Nisuin)
Father loses authority — ketubah is hers
Key Theme This daf explores the limits of a father's authority over his daughter — through earnings, court claims, and marriage contracts.

What Does the Father Claim in Court?

Rav Pappa
What exactly is the dispute between R' Shimon and the Rabbis?
R' Shimon
A person doesn't leave aside a fixed amount (the fine/קנס) to claim something unfixed (humiliation/בושת). So the claim is really about the fine.
Rabbis
A person doesn't leave aside a claim the defendant can't escape by admission (humiliation) to claim something he can escape (fine). The claim is about humiliation & degradation.
Rav Pappa
This matters for false oaths — if the claim is a fine, a false denial doesn't create an offering obligation, since מודה בקנס פטור.
Principle מודה בקנס פטור — One who admits to a fine is exempt from paying it. This shapes what the father is understood to be claiming.

Orphan Daughter Sustained by Brothers: Whose Earnings?

Father dies. Brothers sustain sister from father's estate per ketubah stipulation
↓
Do her earnings (מעשה ידיה) belong to the brothers?
Rav Sheshet: Yes
Brothers stand in place of father. Just as a widow's earnings go to orphans, so too here.
Rav: No
Her earnings are hers. A father doesn't bequeath rights over his daughter to his sons. (Lev. 25:46)
↓
✅ Halakha follows Rav — her earnings belong to her
Halakha The Gemara concludes: The halakha is in accordance with Rav — a daughter sustained by her brothers keeps her own earnings.

Widow vs. Daughter: What Does the Father Prefer?

Degradation (בזיון)

  • Father prefers his widow NOT be degraded
  • If someone must beg, he'd rather it be his daughter
  • Widow takes priority over daughter for sustenance from a small estate

Comfort (נחת רוח)

  • Father prefers his daughter live in comfort
  • He allows her to keep extra earnings for comfort
  • This is why she may retain her earnings even though the widow cannot
Resolution The Gemara distinguishes: a father prioritizes his widow's dignity but his daughter's comfort — different concerns, different rulings.

Mishna: Who Gets the Ketubah?

ScenarioKetubah Belongs To
Father betrothed her → divorced → betrothed again → widowedFather (she never left his authority)
Father married her off → divorced → married again → widowedHer (even first ketubah)
R' Yehuda: Married off → divorced → married again → widowedFirst ketubah = Father's; Second = Hers
R' Yehuda concedes: Betrothed as minor → matured → then marriedHers (she's now an adult)
Rabbis to R' Yehuda Once the father married her off (נישואין) even the first time, he no longer has authority over her.

Why Does R' Yehuda Give the First Ketubah to the Father?

Rabba & Rav Yosef
Initial answer: Since the father's right to the ketubah began at betrothal (אירוסין), he keeps it even after marriage.
Rava
Objection! R' Yehuda concedes that if she matured before marriage, the father loses the ketubah. If rights began at betrothal, why would maturity matter?
Rabba & Rav Yosef
Corrected answer: The ketubah is written right before marriage. If she's still under his authority at that time, he gets the money.
Rava
Now it works — if she matured before marriage, the ketubah was written when she was independent, so the father gets nothing.
Key Shift The determining moment is when the ketubah is written (at marriage), not when betrothal occurred.
