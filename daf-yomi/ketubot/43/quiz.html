QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, why does R' Shimon hold that a father's court claim for his raped daughter is essentially about the fine (knas)?
A. Because a person doesn't leave aside a fixed amount to claim an unfixed amount
B. Because the fine is larger than humiliation payments
C. Because only fines can be collected in court
D. Because humiliation payments require witnesses
Correct: A — R' Shimon's reasoning is that a person doesn't leave aside something fixed (the fine) to claim something that requires assessment (humiliation/degradation).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the halakha regarding earnings of an orphan daughter sustained by her brothers from their father's estate?
A. Her earnings belong to the brothers since they sustain her
B. Her earnings belong to her
C. Half goes to the brothers, half to her
D. Her earnings go to charity
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that the halakha follows Rav: her earnings belong to her. A father does not bequeath his rights over his daughter to his sons (based on Leviticus 25:46).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara distinguish between a father's concern for his widow versus his daughter?
A. He cares equally for both
B. He prefers the widow in all respects
C. He prefers the widow regarding degradation, but the daughter regarding comfort
D. He prefers the daughter regarding degradation, but the widow regarding comfort
Correct: C — The Gemara distinguishes: regarding degradation (begging), the father prefers his widow not suffer it; regarding comfort (extra earnings), he prefers his daughter enjoy it.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Mishna, when does the father lose his rights to his daughter's ketubah?
A. When she is betrothed (eirusin)
B. When she is married off (nisuin)
C. When she reaches the age of majority
D. When the husband writes the ketubah
Correct: B — The Mishna teaches that once the father married her off (nisuin), even the first time, the father no longer has authority over her, and the ketubah belongs to her.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, when does the lien for the basic ketubah amount (100/200 dinars) attach to the husband's property?
A. From the time of marriage (nisuin) only
B. From the time of betrothal (eirusin)
C. From the time the ketubah document is written
D. From the time of divorce or death
Correct: B — Rav Huna distinguishes: the basic ketubah amount creates a lien from betrothal, while additional amounts (tosefet) create a lien only from marriage. Rav Asi disagrees and says both attach only at marriage.
