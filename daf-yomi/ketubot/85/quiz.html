Ketubot 85 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Rava reject the seizure of the boat by Rav Pappa and Rav Huna bar Rav Yehoshua?
A. The boat was seized after the debtor's death, when heirs had already inherited
B. The boat was not in the public domain
C. Neither sage had a valid debt claim
D. They seized it simultaneously, so neither could claim sole ownership
Correct: A — Rava cited Rav Naḥman's principle that seizure of a debtor's property is valid only during the debtor's lifetime. Since the boat was seized after death, the heirs had already taken possession.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why were the Bei Ḥozai creditors believed when they claimed the payment was for 'side debts'?
A. They had witnesses supporting their claim
B. They had a miggo — they could have denied receiving any payment at all
C. The agent admitted he didn't specify which debt he was paying
D. The original debtor confirmed it was for side debts
Correct: B — Rabbi Abbahu applied the principle of miggo: since the creditors could have made the stronger claim that no payment was ever made, their lesser claim — that it went to a different, undocumented debt — is believed.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Rava accept his wife's testimony about the oath-suspect but reject Rav Pappa's testimony about the paid document?
A. His wife was a qualified witness while Rav Pappa was not
B. He had personal certainty (yada'na bah) about his wife's truthfulness but not about Rav Pappa's
C. His wife had a second witness to corroborate her claim
D. Rav Pappa had a conflict of interest in the case
Correct: B — Rava distinguished between personal certainty of someone's reliability and formal testimony. He knew with complete certainty that his wife was always truthful, but could not say the same about Rav Pappa, and one witness alone is insufficient without that certainty.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the deposited property cases (pearls, goblet, silk), what limitation did the Gemara place on using distinguishing marks (simanim)?
A. Simanim are only valid for items worth more than a certain amount
B. Simanim work only if the claimant does NOT regularly visit the guardian's house
C. Simanim must be corroborated by at least two witnesses
D. Simanim are effective only if the guardian explicitly acknowledged the deposit
Correct: B — The Gemara warned that if the claimant regularly enters and exits the guardian's house, they could have seen another person's deposit and learned its distinguishing features, making simanim unreliable.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Gemara's conclusion, when a man says on his deathbed 'give my property to Toviya' and both a neighbor-Toviya and a relative-Toviya come forward, who takes precedence?
A. The relative, because inheritance follows bloodline
B. The neighbor, based on 'a close neighbor is better than a distant brother'
C. They split the property equally
D. It is left to the judges' discretion
Correct: B — The Gemara resolves this dilemma by citing Proverbs 27:10 — 'A close neighbor is better than a distant brother' — giving precedence to the neighbor over the relative.
