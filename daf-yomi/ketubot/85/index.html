Ketubot 85: Creditors, Agents & Deposited Property

תפיסה לאחר מיתה לאו כלום היא
CORE THEME This daf explores the limits of property seizure by creditors, the liability of agents who fail their mission, and how courts determine ownership of deposited items after the guardian dies. A recurring principle: seizing property after the debtor's death is ineffective (tefisah l'achar mitah lav tefisah).

The Battle of the Boat: Two Sages Seize a Ship

Rav Pappa
I steered the boat with an oar — I acquired all of it!
Rav Huna b"R Yehoshua
I pulled it with a rope — I acquired all of it!
Rav Pineḥas bar Ami
But Rav and Shmuel say seizure only works from items in a pile in the public domain!
Both Sages
We seized it from the middle of the river — that has the status of public domain!
Rava
You white geese (kakei ḥivvarei)! Rav Naḥman says seizure only works during the debtor's lifetime — this was after death!
RULING Seizure of a debtor's property is valid only while the debtor is alive. After death, the heirs have already inherited — tefisah l'achar mitah lav tefisah.

The Failed Agent: Avimi's Payment to Bei Ḥozai

Avimi sends agent Ḥama to pay debt to Bei Ḥozai creditors
↓
Ḥama pays the money and asks: Return the debt document
↓
Bei Ḥozai: That payment was for side debts — we keep the document!
↓
Does Ḥama have witnesses that he paid?
No witnesses
R' Abbahu: They have miggo — since they could deny receiving anything, they can also claim it was for side debts
Agent's liability?
Gemara concludes: Agent must pay — 'I sent you for my benefit, not my detriment'
MIGGO Since the creditors could have denied receiving any payment at all, their lesser claim — that it went to a different debt — is believed.

One Witness vs. Trusted Insider

Rav Ḥisda's Daughter

  • Told Rava a litigant is suspect of false oaths
  • Rava reversed the oath obligation to the other party
  • Rava: 'I know with certainty she is always truthful'
  • Her testimony was accepted despite being disqualified as a formal witness

Rav Pappa

  • Told Rava a document records a paid debt
  • Rava asked: Is there a second witness? No.
  • Rava: 'One witness is nothing' — I don't know him with the same certainty
  • His testimony was not accepted as sole witness
PRINCIPLE Rava distinguished between personal certainty of someone's reliability (yada'na bah) and formal witness requirements. A judge may rely on personal knowledge of trustworthiness — but only with complete certainty.

'Give My Property to Toviya' — Which Toviya?

📣 Deathbed declaration: 'My property goes to Toviya'
↓
👤 A man named Toviya arrives → R' Yoḥanan: He receives the property
↓
🎓 If Rav Toviya claims it → Rejected, unless he's familiar with the deceased
↓
🏘️ Two Toviyas: neighbor vs. scholar → Scholar wins
↓
👨‍👩‍👦 Relative vs. scholar → Scholar wins
↓
🏘️ Neighbor vs. relative → Neighbor wins — 'A close neighbor is better than a distant brother' (Proverbs 27:10)
↓
⚖️ Same category (both neighbors, both relatives) → Judge's discretion
SOURCE "טוב שכן קרוב מאח רחוק" — A close neighbor is better than a distant brother (Mishlei 27:10).
