QUESTION 1 OF 5
In the mishna's case of a 400-dinar ketubah, why is only the LAST sale void?
A. Because she had already collected her full ketubah amount and was now selling orphans' property at a loss
B. Because the last buyer paid with invalid currency
C. Because three sales is the maximum allowed by the Rabbis
D. Because the last buyer was a relative of the deceased husband
Correct: A — The first three sales were from her own entitlement at correct prices. The last sale involved a one-dinar overage from the orphans' property sold at a reduced price — the loss falls on the orphans, making it void.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the Gemara, when an employer tells his agent to sell a kor and the agent sells only half a kor, what is the agent's defense?
A. He claims the buyer only wanted half a kor
B. He argues he preserved the employer's option to keep the remaining property if the money isn't needed
C. He says he couldn't find a buyer for the full amount
D. He claims the employer's instructions were ambiguous
Correct: B — The agent argues: 'I did what is good for you — you can still decide whether you truly need more money. If I had sold everything and you didn't need it, you couldn't reverse the sale.'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the me'ila case, why is the EMPLOYER guilty of misusing consecrated property?
A. Because the employer knew the dinar was consecrated
B. Because the agent bought the robe as instructed (worth 6 sela for 3 sela), fulfilling the agency
C. Because the employer benefited from both the robe and the cloak
D. Because the employer should have checked the dinar before giving it
Correct: B — The Gemara explains the robe was actually worth 6 sela but purchased at a bargain for 3. The agent fulfilled the employer's exact wishes regarding the robe, so the employer bears guilt through the valid agency for that portion.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rav Naḥman's position on an employer who says 'sell to one person'?
A. The agent may sell only to exactly one person
B. The agent may sell to one or two but not more
C. The agent may sell to any number of people, but not if he errs in the price
D. The instruction is too vague to be binding
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman rules that 'to one' means 'even to a hundred' — the number is not a limitation. However, this latitude does NOT extend to pricing errors; an agent who sells for less than market value has voided the agency.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara prove that an agent's error is treated more strictly than an owner's error?
A. From a case of a widow who sold property at a loss
B. From the laws of teruma — an agent may separate only 1/40 to 1/60, while an owner's separation is valid even at 1/20
C. From the laws of me'ila where only the agent is punished
D. From a case where Rav Naḥman invalidated an agent's land sale
Correct: B — The Gemara cites Terumot 4:4: an agent separating teruma must stay within 1/40 to 1/60, but if the homeowner himself accidentally separates 1/20, it is valid teruma. This demonstrates the owner has broader latitude than an agent acting on his behalf.
