Ketubot 75 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak, how many people make an oath irrevocable?
A. Two
B. Three
C. Ten
D. Seven
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak derives from the laws of zava: 'days' = 2 (plural minimum), 'many days' = 3. Rabbi Yitzḥak says ten, based on the word 'assembly.'
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Meir forbid remarrying a woman whose vow required investigation by an authority?
A. Because the vow cannot actually be dissolved
B. Because a man is willing for his wife to be degraded in court, so he might claim he wouldn't have divorced her had he known
C. Because the woman might make new vows
D. Because the halakhic authority might refuse to dissolve it
Correct: B — R' Meir holds a man is willing for his wife to be degraded in court. He might later say: 'Had I known the vow could be dissolved, I would not have divorced her' — casting doubt on the get's validity.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does the saying 'Tav l'meitav tan du' (טב למיתב טן דו) mean, and who said it?
A. Abaye — a woman prefers wealth over marriage
B. Reish Lakish — it is better to dwell as two than to dwell alone as a widow
C. Rava — a man should investigate before betrothal
D. Rav Pappa — a married woman sits among noblewomen
Correct: B — Reish Lakish reports that women say: 'It is better to dwell together as two (tan du) than to dwell alone as if a widow.' This principle explains why women cannot easily claim mistaken betrothal.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rava resolve the apparent contradiction between the two clauses of the mishna about blemishes?
A. The mishna follows two different tannaim and cannot be reconciled
B. The presumption is: 'discovered here, created here' — wherever the blemish is found is where it presumably originated
C. Both clauses follow Rabban Gamliel's lenient view
D. The husband always bears the burden of proof regardless of location
Correct: B — Rava's principle: if blemishes are discovered in the father's house, they presumably arose there (before betrothal); if in the husband's house, they presumably arose there (after betrothal). This creates a consistent rule for both clauses.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the meaning of the metaphor 'A person does not drink from a cup unless he first examines it'?
A. Women should inspect their husbands before marriage
B. A halakhic authority must investigate before dissolving a vow
C. A man does not betroth a woman without first investigating her, so he presumably knew of pre-existing blemishes
D. The court must examine witnesses before accepting testimony
Correct: C — Rava uses this metaphor to explain that if blemishes existed at the time of betrothal, the husband presumably saw and accepted them. This limits his ability to later claim the betrothal was a mistake.
