Ketubot 10 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, why is the groom deemed credible when he claims his bride was not a virgin?
A. Because the Torah grants husbands authority over such matters
B. Because a person would not invest in a wedding feast only to destroy it with a false claim
C. Because witnesses testify to the marriage consummation
D. Because the bride cannot prove her own virginity
Correct: B — Rava's reasoning is that the groom's significant investment in wedding preparations demonstrates his sincere intent to marry — if he still claims she wasn't a virgin, he must be telling the truth.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why was the bachelor who claimed his bride was not a virgin flogged with palm branches by Rav Naḥman?
A. Because he insulted his bride publicly
B. Because he should have brought witnesses
C. Because as a bachelor, he could only know the difference through experience with prostitutes
D. Because he waited too long to bring his claim
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman reasoned that a never-married man has no legitimate basis for comparison, so his ability to make such a claim implies immoral experience.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the practical difference if the ketubah is considered rabbinic rather than Torah law?
A. A rabbinic ketubah cannot be collected after the husband's death
B. A rabbinic ketubah can only be collected from the husband's lowest quality land
C. A rabbinic ketubah is only 100 dinars regardless of the bride's status
D. A rabbinic ketubah requires witnesses to be valid
Correct: B — The Gemara states that since the ketubah is a rabbinic ordinance (according to the first opinion), she may collect only from the husband's land of the most inferior quality (ziburit).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How did Rabban Gamliel bar Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi verify the bride's virginity using the wine barrel test?
A. He checked whether the bride could taste wine through her skin
B. He tested whether wine scent diffused through the mouth when seated on a barrel — it did not for virgins
C. He measured how quickly wine evaporated in the bride's presence
D. He observed whether the bride could balance on the barrel opening
Correct: B — The test involved seating women on a wine barrel opening. For a non-virgin, the wine scent diffused from her mouth; for a virgin, it did not. The bride passed the test — no scent diffused.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's case during a famine, why was no blood found initially?
A. The bride was from the Dorketi family
B. The groom diverted his approach as in Rabban Gamliel's parable
C. The bride and groom were malnourished — their bodies lacked sufficient blood due to drought
D. The examination cloths had been laundered improperly
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi recognized that famine caused the absence of blood. After feeding, bathing, and nourishing them, the groom found blood upon a second attempt. He cited Lamentations 4:8: 'Their skin is shriveled upon their bones.'
