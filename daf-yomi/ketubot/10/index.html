Ketubah Framework: Amounts, Credibility & Origins

Shmuel in name of R' Shimon ben Elazar:
Sages instituted the ketubah
↓
Virgin
200 dinars
Widow
100 dinars (maneh)
↓
Key question: Is the groom believed if he claims she wasn't a virgin?
↓
Rava: Yes — a person doesn't prepare a wedding feast just to lose it
Core Principle The ketubah protects the wife financially, but the groom's claim is taken seriously because of the investment he made in the marriage.

Ketubah Amounts: The Rabbinic Institution

Virgin (Betulah)

  • Ketubah: 200 dinars
  • Subject to virginity claim by groom
  • Higher amount reflects expected status
  • Includes virgin widows/divorcées from betrothal

Widow (Almanah)

  • Ketubah: 100 dinars (maneh)
  • No virginity claim applicable
  • Name almanah derives from maneh
  • Lower amount — already married once
Mishna A virgin who became a widow, divorcée, or ḥalutza from betrothal still receives 200 dinars and is subject to a virginity claim.

Is the Groom Believed? Rava's Reasoning

Gemara
If the groom is believed that she's not a virgin, what did the Sages accomplish by setting the ketubah at 200?
Rava
A person does not exert himself to prepare a wedding feast and then cause it to be lost. If he still claims she's not a virgin — he must be telling the truth.
Rav Aḥai
A bachelor (never married) is not believed — he lacks experience to know the difference.
Rav Naḥman
A bachelor who makes such a claim? Flog him with palm branches! He must have gained experience with prostitutes (mevarakhta).
Key Distinction Only a previously married man is deemed credible regarding a virginity claim. A bachelor making such a claim is punished, not believed.

Rabban Gamliel's Parable: The Door in the Dark

Groom claims: "I found an unobstructed orifice"
↓
Rabban Gamliel's parable:
A man walking in darkness reaches a door…
↓
Diverts approach
Finds door open
(Appears unobstructed but isn't proof)
Doesn't divert
Finds door locked
(Encounters the obstruction)
↓
Conclusion: Perhaps the groom diverted his approach — the absence of obstruction proves nothing
Two Versions Version 1: He accidentally diverted. Version 2: He intentionally diverted and displaced the obstruction. Either way, the claim is not conclusive.

The Wine Barrel Test — R' Gamliel bar R' Yehuda HaNasi

👰 Groom says: No blood found. Bride says: I am still a virgin.
↓
👩‍👩‍👧 Rabban Gamliel requests two maidservants — one virgin, one not
↓
🍷 Each is seated on the opening of a wine barrel
↓
❌ Non-virgin: wine scent diffuses from her mouth
↓
✅ Virgin: wine scent does NOT diffuse from her mouth
↓
👰 The bride is tested — scent does NOT diffuse. Verdict: she is a virgin!
↓
📝 He first verified the method worked before subjecting a Jewish woman to it — to avoid unnecessary indignity
Sensitivity Rabban Gamliel knew the method from tradition but tested it on volunteers first — it is improper to demean Jewish women by subjecting them to an unverified test.
