Who Receives the Fine (Kenas)?

Central Question: Which women are excluded from receiving the rape/seduction fine?
↓
Death Penalty Relations
No fine — severity exempts payment
Karet Relations
Dispute: Shimon HaTimni vs. R' Shimon b. Menasya
Regular Prohibitions
Fine applies per Shimon HaTimni; no fine per R' Shimon b. Menasya
↓
Additional exclusions: me'unah (refusal), ailonit, bad reputation, captive/convert over age 3
KEY PRINCIPLE The severity of the prohibition determines whether the rapist pays a fine — the more severe the sin, the less likely monetary payment applies.

Redefining 'Arayot' and 'Shniyot' for Fines

Shimon HaTimni

  • Arayot = death penalty relations
  • Shniyot = karet relations
  • Regular prohibitions → fine applies
  • Key: no fine only where kiddushin is ineffective

R' Shimon ben Menasya

  • Arayot = death penalty AND karet
  • Shniyot = regular prohibitions (lavin)
  • Even regular prohibitions → no fine
  • Broader exclusion despite kiddushin being effective
CONTEXT The baraita's terms 'forbidden relatives' and 'secondary relatives' are redefined specifically for the laws of kenas — they do not carry their usual meanings here.

Does a Minor Girl Receive the Fine?

Rabbis
A minor girl (ketanah) does receive the fine for rape.
R' Meir
A minor girl does not receive the fine — only a na'arah (young woman) does.
Gemara
The baraita's clause about me'unah (refusal) implies minors normally get a fine → this follows the Rabbis.
Gemara
But the clause about ailonit getting no fine implies minors don't get one → this follows R' Meir!
Gemara
The baraita has a mixed authorship problem — first clause follows Rabbis, latter clause follows R' Meir.
RESOLUTION ATTEMPT Rafram suggests the entire baraita follows R' Meir: 'me'unah' means one who is fit to refuse (i.e., a minor) — she has no fine. But the Gemara finds this forced: why not just say 'minor'?

The Ailonit Contradiction

Baraita #1: An ailonit has no fine for rape or seduction
↓
⚡ Contradiction!
↓
Baraita #2: A deaf-mute, imbecile, and ailonit do have a fine
↓
Resolution
Baraita #1 = R' Meir (minor has no fine; ailonit skips na'arah stage)
Resolution
Baraita #2 = Rabbis (minor has a fine; ailonit included)
KEY LOGIC Per R' Meir, the ailonit goes directly from minor status to bogeret (adult) at age 20, never passing through the na'arah stage — and only a na'arah receives the fine.

R' ZEIRA'S REASONING All girls fall and may break their hymen, but sighted girls notice the blood and show their mother. A blind girl cannot — so her husband cannot later claim she wasn't a virgin.

The Tainted Document Analogy

שטרא מרעא — tainted document
RAV PAPPA'S PRINCIPLE Just as a woman with a bad reputation (shem ra) from her youth loses her claim to a fine, a tainted document (shtar) whose authenticity is compromised cannot be used to collect a debt.

The Gemara clarifies: mere rumor is insufficient (per Rava). Rather, two witnesses testified she propositioned them — parallel to two people saying the debtor asked them to forge a document.

Even if others wouldn't cooperate with forgery, since he actively sought it, we suspect he wrote it himself.
