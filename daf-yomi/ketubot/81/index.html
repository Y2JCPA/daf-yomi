The Yavam's Obligations: Burial, Ketubah & Property

Mishna
A widow's burial falls on the heirs of her ketubah, not her husband's general heirs
Abaye
This refers to a yevama — the yavam inherits the ketubah and therefore must bury her
Rava
The yavam can claim: I inherit my brother, not his wife — I have no obligation to bury her
Abaye
They come upon him from two sides: either accept burial duties or pay her ketubah
Rava
A ketubah cannot be collected during the husband's lifetime — the yavam stands in his place
Core Issue Can the yavam avoid both burying the yevama and paying her ketubah? The Gemara explores whether the ketubah's language traps him either way.

Abaye's 'Two Sides' Argument

Abaye
The yavam is trapped: If you inherit your brother, you inherit his duties — bury his wife
Abaye
If you refuse to bury her, you reject his obligations — then pay her ketubah
Rava
I inherit my brother. As for the ketubah — it says 'when you marry another' — she hasn't married another!
Rava
A ketubah cannot be collected during the husband's lifetime, and I stand in his place
Rav Ashi
The yavam himself counts as 'another man' — she is considered ready to marry another
Resolution Rav Ashi resolves the problem: since the yevama can marry the yavam, she satisfies the ketubah clause 'when you marry another'.

Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel on Sotah's Ketubah

Beit Shammai

  • Sotah whose husband died takes her ketubah
  • She does not drink the bitter waters
  • An uncertain claim (maybe unfaithful) supersedes the heirs' certain claim
  • Implies: a document ready to be collected is considered collected

Beit Hillel

  • She cannot drink — no husband to bring her
  • Since she doesn't drink, she cannot collect the ketubah
  • Uncertainty about her faithfulness blocks collection
  • The heirs' certain claim prevails
Key Proof Beit Shammai treat a ketubah ready to be collected as already collected — this undermines Rava's claim that the yevama's ketubah is uncollectable.

Rav Yosef vs. Abaye: Is the Yavam's Sale Valid?

Rav Yosef
The Sages said a yavam may not sell — therefore if he sold, the sale is void
Abaye
Not so! Beit Hillel say a betrothed woman may not sell, yet if she sold — the sale is valid!
R' Ḥanina bar Pappi
The halakha is in accordance with Rav Yosef
Abaye
Has R' Ḥanina hung jewelry on it? A ruling without reasoning adds nothing!
Rav Minyumi
The halakha follows Abaye — but if Rav Yosef has a better reason, send it to me
Pumbedita Case A man promised his brother half the inheritance to allow yibbum, then reneged. Rav Yosef voided the promise; Abaye argued it should stand.
