Ketubot 81 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye, what does the mishna about a widow's burial teach about a yevama?
A. The yavam must bury her because he inherits the ketubah
B. The orphans must bury her as a form of respect
C. She requires no burial because she has no husband
D. The beit din is responsible for her burial
Correct: A — Abaye identifies the widow with 'two sets of heirs' as a yevama — the yavam inherits the ketubah and therefore bears the burial obligation.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Abaye's 'two sides' (from two directions) argument against the yavam?
A. Either the yavam buries her as heir, or he pays the ketubah — he cannot escape both
B. The yavam must choose between yibbum and ḥalitza
C. Two different courts must rule on the case
D. The yavam owes both the ketubah and a separate burial fee
Correct: A — Abaye argues the yavam is trapped: if he claims inheritance rights, he accepts duties (burial). If he rejects duties, he must release the ketubah. He cannot avoid both.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara cite Beit Shammai's ruling about a sotah whose husband died?
A. To prove that a document ready to be collected is considered already collected
B. To show that sotah waters can be drunk without a husband present
C. To demonstrate that Beit Shammai always favor the woman
D. To establish that burial obligations override ketubah claims
Correct: A — Beit Shammai allow the sotah to collect her ketubah despite uncertainty, proving they hold a document ready for collection is considered collected — undermining Rava's argument.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Sumakhos advise the yavam to divorce and remarry rather than simply designate funds for the ketubah?
A. Designation is halakhically prohibited
B. To avoid enmity — she would think he wants to leave her
C. The ketubah amount is unknown until divorce
D. A yavam cannot designate property for any purpose
Correct: B — Setting aside ketubah funds would signal to the wife that he plans to divorce her, causing enmity (איבה). The divorce-remarriage strategy avoids this perception.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the Pumbedita case, why did Rav Yosef rule the yavam's promise to share property was void?
A. Because all the deceased brother's property is mortgaged for the yevama's ketubah and cannot be sold or given away
B. Because verbal promises are never binding in halakha
C. Because the other brother had no inheritance rights
D. Because the yavam had not yet performed yibbum
Correct: A — Rav Yosef held that since the Sages prohibited the yavam from selling the property (all mortgaged for the ketubah), his promise to share it is equivalent to an invalid sale.
