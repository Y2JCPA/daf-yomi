Ketubot 86 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, if a creditor sells a promissory note and then forgives the debtor, what happens?
A. The forgiveness is void since the note was sold
B. The debt is forgiven, and the purchaser loses out
C. The purchaser automatically becomes the new creditor
D. The debtor must pay both the purchaser and creditor
Correct: B — Shmuel rules that the debt obligation is essentially non-transferable — the creditor (or even his heir) can forgive the debt even after selling the note, and the forgiveness is valid.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
When a debtor owes both a creditor and his wife's ketubah, and has only one plot of land, who receives it?
A. The wife, because ketubah has priority
B. They split it equally
C. The creditor, to encourage future lending
D. Whoever filed their claim first
Correct: C — The Gemara rules the creditor receives priority. The rationale: women desire marriage more than the ketubah payment, and giving preference to the creditor encourages people to lend money.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Rav Naḥman regret advising the daughter to forgive her mother's ketubah?
A. The advice was halakhically incorrect
B. An important person must avoid appearing to favor family
C. The purchasers successfully challenged the forgiveness
D. The daughter refused to follow his advice
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman initially relied on 'do not hide from your own flesh' but concluded that a person of stature must be extra careful to avoid any impression of favoritism in judgment.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the dispute regarding R' Eliezer's opinion about a husband administering an oath to his wife?
A. Whether the oath applies to movable or immovable property
B. Whether it is an oath ab initio or only by extension (gilgul) of an existing oath
C. Whether the wife can refuse the oath
D. Whether the oath requires witnesses
Correct: B — The Gemara asks whether R' Eliezer means the husband can impose an oath from the start (ab initio) or only extend an existing storekeeper oath to household matters. The baraita proves he means ab initio.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Gemara, how can a court compel a debtor who refuses to repay his loan?
A. By seizing his property without his consent
B. By imprisoning him until he agrees
C. By striking him as with any positive mitzva, even until his soul departs
D. By excommunicating him from the community
Correct: C — Repaying a debt is a positive mitzva. The baraita teaches that for positive mitzvot — like sukkah or lulav — the court strikes the person until he complies, even to the point of death. The same applies to debt repayment.
