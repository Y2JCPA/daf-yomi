How Much Teruma for a Betrothed Woman?

R' Tarfon
He may give her everything from teruma — a kohen's daughter knows how to handle it properly.
R' Akiva
He must give half non-sacred food and half teruma — she needs practical food she can use even when ritually impure.
Abaye
Their dispute is only about a bat kohen betrothed to a kohen. For an Israelite woman betrothed to a kohen — all agree she gets half and half, since she's unfamiliar with teruma laws.
Abaye
Also, this dispute is only about a betrothed woman. For a married woman — all agree: half and half. It's not fitting to make a wife go to the trouble of selling teruma.
Practical Point Abaye narrows R' Tarfon's leniency to a very specific case: a priest's daughter who is only betrothed (not yet married) to a priest.

Teruma Proportions — Four Opinions

SageTeruma : Non-Sacred RatioReasoning
R' Akiva1:1 (half and half)Basic need for usable food when impure

R' YehudaAll teruma (sufficient to sell & buy non-sacred)Amount must cover purchase of non-sacred food from sale proceeds
Rabban Shimon ben GamlielDouble the non-sacred amount in terumaExtra teruma so she can sell at lower prices and find buyers easily
R' Yehuda vs. Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel The difference is effort: R' Yehuda requires her to search for buyers; Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel gives extra teruma so she doesn't have to work as hard to sell it.

Why Can't a Yevama Eat Teruma?

קנין כספו — Kinyan kaspo
Key Verse The Torah states: "The acquisition of his money" (Leviticus 22:11) may eat teruma. But a yevama is his brother's acquisition, not his own. The yavam does not complete the marriage until yibbum (consummation), so she cannot partake of teruma through him.

Can a Husband Consecrate His Wife's Earnings?

Husband says: "Your earnings are hekdesh"
↓
Does this apply to her basic earnings (required production)?
↓
❌ Consecration is ineffective
She works and sustains herself from her earnings
↓
What about the surplus — earnings beyond her required amount?
↓
R' Meir
✅ Surplus is consecrated
He can consecrate what hasn't yet come into being
R' Yochanan HaSandlar
❌ Surplus is non-sacred
He has no claim to the surplus
Why R' Meir? Reish Lakish explains: R' Meir's reason is not that one can consecrate something not yet in the world, but rather that since a husband can compel her to work, it's as if he said "Your hands are consecrated to the One Who made them" — consecrating something that already exists.

Can a Wife Opt Out? Sustenance vs. Earnings

Rav Huna / Rav
A woman may say to her husband: "I will not be sustained by you, and I will not work for you." The husband's obligation to sustain her is the primary enactment; her earnings were given to him only to prevent animosity.
Objection
But a baraita says: They instituted sustenance in exchange for earnings — implying earnings came first!
Rav Huna / Rav
Emend the text: They instituted earnings in exchange for sustenance. Sustenance is primary; she can waive both.
Reish Lakish
He can compel her to work — implying she cannot say "I won't be sustained and I won't work." This disputes Rav Huna.
Core Dispute Rav Huna says the wife controls the arrangement and may opt out. Reish Lakish implies the husband can compel her labor — she cannot unilaterally exit.
