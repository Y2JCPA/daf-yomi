Ketubot 37 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
How does Rav Sheshet resolve the contradiction in Rabbi Yehuda's rulings about captive women?
A. Rabbi Yehuda changed his mind over time
B. The strict ruling applies only when witnesses saw the captive have relations
C. The lenient ruling applies only to a woman captured before age three
D. Rabbi Yehuda distinguishes between Jewish and non-Jewish captors
Correct: B — Rav Sheshet explains that Rabbi Yehuda requires a 3-month waiting period for a captive woman only in a case where witnesses actually saw her engage in intercourse during captivity.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rabba abandon the 'contraceptive resorbent' (mokh) explanation for Rabbi Yosei's leniency?
A. The Sages prohibited use of mokh
B. It doesn't explain cases without advance knowledge, like a maidservant freed by tooth extraction
C. It only works for married women, not converts
D. Rabbi Yosei explicitly rejected that reasoning
Correct: B — A maidservant freed unexpectedly (e.g., when her master knocks out her tooth) had no advance warning and would not have used a resorbent. Since R' Yosei is lenient even for her, the resorbent theory fails.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the principle of 'Kim Lei Bid'rabba Minei'?
A. A person can choose which punishment to receive
B. When liable for two punishments, only the more severe one is administered
C. The court always imposes both punishments for a single act
D. Monetary payment can substitute for any corporal punishment
Correct: B — Kim lei bid'rabba minei teaches that when a single act incurs two punishments (e.g., death and payment), the offender receives only the more severe one — the death penalty exempts him from payment.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Torah need separate verses to teach that neither an intentional killer nor an unwitting killer can pay ransom?
A. One applies to Jews and one to non-Jews
B. Each verse prevents a mistaken inference that would allow ransom in the other case
C. One is about monetary payment and the other about animal sacrifice
D. The verses address different time periods in Jewish history
Correct: B — Without the verse about intentional killers, one might say only unwitting killers can't pay (since they aren't losing their life). Without the verse about unwitting killers, one might say only intentional killers can't pay (since their sin is more severe). Both are needed.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why is a murderer NOT beheaded with a cleaver like the eglah arufah, even though his execution method is derived from it?
A. Cleavers were too expensive for the court to maintain
B. The verse 'Love your neighbor as yourself' requires choosing an agreeable death
C. The eglah arufah was a special temporary law not meant as a model
D. Rabbi Yosei disagrees and holds a cleaver should be used
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman in the name of Rabba bar Avuh derives from 'V'ahavta l'rei'akha kamokha' (Leviticus 19:18) the principle of 'b'ror lo mitah yafah' — choose an agreeable death. Using a cleaver at the nape would be undignified.
