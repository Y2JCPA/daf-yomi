Does Rabbi Yehuda Trust the Captive Woman?

Rav Pappa bar Shmuel
R' Yehuda says a convert must wait 3 months — he's concerned about prior relations. Doesn't this apply to a captive too?
Rav Yosef
A convert doesn't protect herself before conversion. A captive is conscious of her sanctity and resists — they're different!
Rav Pappa bar Shmuel
But a baraita groups captive women with converts and maidservants — all must wait 3 months per R' Yehuda!
Rav Yosef
(Silent — unable to respond)
Rav Sheshet
R' Yehuda's strict ruling refers to a captive whom witnesses saw engage in intercourse.
RESOLUTION Rav Sheshet resolves the contradiction: R' Yehuda requires a 3-month wait for a captive only when there is testimony that she had relations.

Rabbi Yehuda vs. Rabbi Yosei — Three-Month Wait

Rabbi Yehuda — Must Wait

  • Convert must wait 3 months before marrying
  • Captive (with witnesses) must wait 3 months
  • Freed maidservant must wait 3 months
  • Raped/seduced woman must wait 3 months
  • Concern: she may be pregnant from prior relations

Rabbi Yosei — May Marry Immediately

  • Convert may be betrothed and marry at once
  • Captive may marry immediately
  • Freed maidservant may marry immediately
  • Raped/seduced woman may marry immediately
  • Rationale: women take steps to prevent pregnancy
SCOPE This dispute applies across four categories of women — converts, captives, freed maidservants, and victims of assault.

Rabba's Two Explanations of Rabbi Yosei

Why does R' Yosei permit immediate marriage?
She might be pregnant!
↓
First answer: She uses a contraceptive resorbent (mokh) before relations
↓
Problem: What about a maidservant freed unexpectedly (tooth/eye extracted)?
She had no advance warning!
↓
Revised answer: She turns over after relations to prevent pregnancy — no advance planning needed
↓
R' Yehuda responds: We are concerned she may not have turned properly — pregnancy still possible
KEY SHIFT Rabba abandons the resorbent theory because it requires advance knowledge, and adopts the 'turning over' theory which works even for unplanned situations.

Kim Lei Bid'rabba Minei — Dual Punishment Exemptions

Verse SourceTeachingWhy Both Are Needed
Exodus 21:22 — "And yet no harm follow"Death penalty exempts from monetary paymentWithout this, one might say: body + money are different categories — impose both
Deut. 25:2 — "Measure of his iniquity"Lashes exempt from monetary paymentWithout this, one might say: only death (more severe) exempts, not lashes
(Derived principle)Death exempts from lashesWithout this, one might say: both are bodily — treat lashes as extended death punishment
PRINCIPLE Kim lei bid'rabba minei — when two punishments apply, only the more severe is administered. The Torah teaches this separately for each combination.

"No Ransom" Verses — Why Multiple Are Needed

📖 Numbers 35:31 — No ransom for an intentional murderer; he must be executed
↓
📖 Numbers 35:32 — No ransom for an unwitting killer; he must go to exile
↓
❓ Why both? If only intentional — maybe unwitting killer can pay (lesser sin)
↓
❓ Why both? If only unwitting — maybe intentional killer can pay (he'll die anyway)
↓
✅ Both verses needed: neither murderer can buy his way out of punishment
LEGAL LOGIC The Gemara demonstrates that each verse prevents a specific mistaken inference, making both indispensable.
