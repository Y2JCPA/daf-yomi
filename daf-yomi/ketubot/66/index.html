Who Owns a Wife's Found Objects?

First Tanna
Lost objects found by a wife belong to her husband, to prevent enmity between them.
R' Akiva
They belong to her. If surplus from her own handiwork is hers, kal va-chomer — found objects are certainly hers!
Ravin (R' Yochanan)
Surplus without extraordinary effort — all agree it's the husband's. They only disagree about surplus through extraordinary effort.
Rav Pappa
A found object is comparable to surplus through extraordinary effort — it's not a regular occurrence. So it follows R' Akiva's dispute.
Unresolved What if she performed 2, 3, or 4 tasks simultaneously? Is the extra earnings like surplus through effort? — Teiku

Humiliation Compensation: Does the Husband Get a Share?

Someone humiliates a married woman
↓
Does the husband receive part of the compensation?
First Tanna
Humiliation payments belong entirely to her
R' Yehuda b. Beteira
Husband receives a portion — her shame is his shame
↓
Rav Ashi's Distinction:
A wife is considered gufo (his own self) — unlike other relatives, her humiliation is his humiliation
Contrast Spitting on someone's garment → exempt. But humiliating someone's wife → husband suffers real dishonor, because she is like his own person.

Dowry Pledge: Does It Transfer to the Yavam?

Original Son-in-Law

  • Father pledged a specific dowry sum
  • Pledge was made to this individual
  • Even if he was an ignoramus — the commitment was personal

Yavam (Levir Brother)

  • Father can refuse: 'To your brother I wanted to give, not to you'
  • Even if the yavam is a Torah scholar
  • The obligation was to a specific person, not transferable
Principle A dowry pledge is a personal commitment to a specific son-in-law — it does not automatically transfer through yibum.

Ketubah Math: Cash vs. Goods

Cash (ma'ot)1,000 dinars1,500 dinars+50%
Cash (small)4 dinars (1 sela)6 dinars+50%
Appraised GoodsValued at 100 dinarsRecords 100 dinarsFace value
Actual Goods ValueWorth ~125 dinarsRecords 100 dinars−20% from appraisal
Why the Difference? Cash generates profit for the husband, so he adds 50%. Goods are appraised higher than real value, so he records 1/5 less to reflect the true worth.
