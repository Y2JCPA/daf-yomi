Ketubot 109 — Admon's Rulings & Property Disputes

DAF OVERVIEW This daf continues the series of Admon's rulings, exploring broken dowry promises, a claimant who signed the opposing deed, and the famous case of a lost path to one's field. The Gemara clarifies when Rabban Gamliel's endorsement of Admon establishes the halakha, and Rava explains the logic behind the Rabbis' position on property access disputes.
3 / 9

Broken Dowry Promise: Who Suffers?

Rabbis (Tanna Kamma)
If a father promised a dowry and didn't pay, the betrothed woman sits until her head turns white. The groom cannot be forced to marry without the dowry.
Admon
She can say: 'Had I made the promise myself, fine. But my father promised — what can I do?' Either marry me or divorce me!
Rabban Gamliel
I see as correct the statement of Admon. The woman should not be trapped indefinitely by her father's broken promise.
R' Yosei b'R' Yehuda
They never disagreed about a father's broken promise! The dispute is when she herself promised the dowry and her father didn't help — then Admon says she can claim reliance on her father.
HALAKHA Wherever Rabban Gamliel endorses Admon, the halakha follows Admon.

Minor vs. Adult: Who Gets Compelled?

A woman promised a dowry but cannot pay
↓
Adult Woman
Dispute between Admon and Rabbis applies — depends on who made the promise
Minor Girl (Rava)
Court compels the groom to either marry her or give a get — a minor's promises have no legal force
KEY POINT A minor cannot be held to a financial promise she made, so the groom bears the consequence.

Claimant Signed the Opposing Deed

Rabbis
If you signed as a witness on the bill of sale transferring the field to the buyer, you have forfeited your claim to that field.
Admon
His signature doesn't disprove his claim! He may have thought: 'The second buyer is easier to deal with than the first' — better to buy it back from the new owner.
Abaye
If he established the field as a boundary marker in someone else's document — he loses his claim. But if he did so in his own purchase document, he can say: 'I had no choice or the seller wouldn't sell me the other field.'
PRACTICAL RULE Establishing a field as a marker for another person's document = conceding ownership. For your own document = preserving your claim.

The Clever Steward: Defending Orphans' Property

📜 A man referenced a field as a boundary marker (conceding ownership), then contested it, then died
↓
👶 His orphans' steward comes before Abaye, who rules against the orphans
↓
💡 Steward argues: 'The father meant only ONE FURROW, not the whole field!'
↓
⚖️ Abaye accepts — per R' Yoḥanan, this claim is credible. 'Give one furrow.'
↓
🌴 The furrow has palm trees! Steward pivots: 'He sold it but BOUGHT IT BACK!'
↓
✅ Abaye accepts again — praises the steward: 'Appoint stewards like this one!'
ABAYE'S PRAISE One who wants to appoint a steward (apotropos) should appoint someone like this, who knows how to advocate for orphans.
