Ketubot 109 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yosei b'R' Yehuda, what is the actual dispute between Admon and the Rabbis regarding the dowry?
A. When the father promised and went bankrupt
B. When she herself promised the dowry expecting her father to pay
C. When the groom demanded more than was promised
D. When the father changed his mind about the match
Correct: B — R' Yosei b'R' Yehuda says all agree about a father's broken promise. The dispute is when she herself promised, relying on her father to provide the funds.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the case of a minor girl whose dowry was not paid, what does Rava say the court does?
A. Compels the father to pay the dowry
B. Compels the groom to marry her or give a get
C. Nullifies the betrothal automatically
D. Assigns the dowry from communal funds
Correct: B — Rava explains that since a minor's promises have no legal force, the court compels the groom — either marry her or release her with a bill of divorce.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Gemara's final interpretation of R' Zeira, when does the halakha follow Admon?
A. In all seven of his rulings
B. Only in the three cases where Rabban Gamliel endorsed him
C. In none of his rulings
D. Only when the ruling also appears in a baraita
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that halakha follows Admon specifically where Rabban Gamliel said 'I see as correct the statement of Admon,' but not where Rabban Gamliel remained silent.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In Rava's framework about the lost path, when do ALL opinions agree the field owner can be deflected?
A. When one person owns all four surrounding fields from one seller
B. When four different people own the fields, each from a different seller
C. When the path owner has no witnesses
D. When the surrounding fields were inherited, not purchased
Correct: B — When four separate owners each acquired from different sellers, each can truthfully say 'your path didn't go through my land' — all agree the claimant cannot force any of them.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Abaye praise the orphans' steward (apotropos)?
A. He found the original deed proving ownership
B. He successfully raised multiple creative legal arguments to protect the orphans' property
C. He paid for the field out of his own pocket
D. He convinced the other party to withdraw the claim voluntarily
Correct: B — The steward cleverly argued first that only one furrow was conceded, then that the father had repurchased the field — both accepted by Abaye based on R' Yoḥanan's principles. Abaye praised this as model advocacy for orphans.
