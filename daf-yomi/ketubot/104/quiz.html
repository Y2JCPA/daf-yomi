Ketubot 104 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Rebbi's maidservant throw a jug from the roof?
A. To signal bar Kappara that Rebbi had died
B. To momentarily silence the Sages' prayers so Rebbi's soul could depart
C. To call the Sages to come upstairs to Rebbi's room
D. To shatter it as a sign of mourning
Correct: B — The Sages' constant prayers were preventing Rebbi from dying despite his great suffering. The sudden noise of the jug breaking caused a momentary silence in their prayers, allowing his soul to depart.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the Rabbis (Chachamim), why can a widow in her husband's house collect her ketubah forever?
A. Because the heirs are obligated to support her indefinitely
B. Because she is embarrassed to demand payment from heirs who are caring for her
C. Because living in the husband's house proves she never waived her rights
D. Because the ketubah document remains valid as long as she stays
Correct: B — The Rabbis reason that in her husband's house, the heirs are caring for her, which makes her embarrassed to sue them. Her silence is not a waiver but a product of social pressure.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the halakhic status of a widow who holds her ketubah document in her possession?
A. She may collect only within 25 years regardless of where she lives
B. The dispute between R' Meir and the Rabbis applies to her case
C. All agree she may collect her ketubah forever
D. She may collect only the principal, not the tosefet
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman bar Ya'akov rules that when she has her ketubah document in hand, all agree — both R' Meir and the Rabbis — that she may collect forever. The dispute only applies when she lacks the document.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the dispute about the tosefet (additional sum), what does R' Yochanan hold?
A. The tosefet can always be collected even after 25 years
B. The tosefet is waived along with the principal because it has the same legal status
C. The tosefet is a separate obligation unrelated to the ketubah
D. The tosefet is collected first, before the principal
Correct: B — R' Yochanan (via R' Abbahu) holds that the tosefet has the same status as the principal ketubah sum (as R' Yannai taught). Therefore, if she waived her rights to the principal through 25 years of silence, the tosefet is also waived.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Rabba bar Sheila rule that Rav Ḥiyya Arikha's sister-in-law could still collect her ketubah after 25 years in her father's house?
A. Because she had her ketubah document in her possession
B. Because Rav Ḥiyya carried her food on his shoulders, creating the same embarrassment dynamic as living in the husband's house
C. Because she had formally protested within the 25-year period
D. Because the halakhah always follows R' Meir for a widow in her father's house
Correct: B — Rabba bar Sheila reasoned that Rav Ḥiyya's extraordinary personal care (delivering food on his own shoulders) created the same embarrassment that normally protects a widow in her husband's house. Her silence was not a waiver but a product of his devoted treatment.
