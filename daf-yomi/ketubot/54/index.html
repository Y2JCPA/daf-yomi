Three Unresolved Dilemmas (Teku)

Rava's Dilemma: Daughter of a woman who violated a prohibition — mother lost her ketubah
Penalize Daughter Too?
Sustenance flows from mother's ketubah → no ketubah, no sustenance
Don't Penalize?
Daughter didn't violate anything — why should she suffer?
↓
Teku — Unresolved

Same result for daughter of betrothed woman and daughter of raped woman (per Rabbis)
Key Principle All three dilemmas ask: Can a daughter's sustenance rights exist independently of her mother's ketubah status?

Daughter of a Raped Woman

Rav Pappa
A man raped a woman, married her, had a daughter, and died. Does the daughter receive sustenance from his estate?
R' Yosei b"R Yehuda
No dilemma here — a raped woman has a ketubah of 100 dinars, so the daughter receives sustenance
Rabbis
The fine (kenas) replaces the ketubah — she has no separate ketubah
Rav Pappa
But the reason for ketubah — so he won't easily divorce her — doesn't apply here, since he can never divorce her! Perhaps the stipulations still apply?
Gemara
Teku — the dilemma stands unresolved
Devarim 22:29 "He may not send her away all his days" — a rapist can never divorce his victim, removing the primary purpose of the ketubah

Widow's Right to Residence & Sustenance

Rav Yosef

  • "In my house" — not in my hovel (bikati)
  • If no room in husband's house, heirs need not house her
  • But she STILL receives sustenance from the estate

Mar bar Rav Ashi

  • She does NOT even receive sustenance
  • Sustenance is tied to living in the husband's house
  • If she resides elsewhere, she gets nothing
  • ⚠️ Halakha is NOT like Mar bar Rav Ashi
Halakha The Gemara explicitly rejects Mar bar Rav Ashi — a widow retains sustenance even if she cannot live in the husband's house

When Does a Widow Lose Sustenance?

AuthorityShe Loses Sustenance When...
ShmuelShe agrees to a marriage proposal
Rav Anan (clarifying Shmuel)She refuses suitors because they are unsuitable for her (showing willingness to remarry in principle)
Rav Anan (exception)She refuses saying "I still feel connected to my late husband" → she keeps sustenance
Rav ḤisdaShe engages in licentious sexual relations
Rav YosefShe paints her eyes and dyes her hair (to attract men)
Kal Va'chomer According to Rav Ḥisda: if relations forfeit sustenance, then cosmetic adornment certainly does. But according to Rav Yosef: only adornment forfeits, while relations alone do not — she may have acted impulsively without intent to remarry.

Designating Land for a Widow's Sustenance

R' Yochanan
Tell your father to set aside land for her sustenance — if she accepts, she relinquishes claim to the rest of the estate
Reish Lakish
All the more so — he has increased her sources of sustenance! She gets estate support AND the land
R' Yochanan
What can I do? A man equal to me disagrees with me
R' Abbahu
R' Yochanan explained: it depends on his language. 'For her sustenance' = adds a source. 'As her sustenance' = replaces other sources
Resolution The key distinction: "for" (le-) sustenance adds to existing support, while "as" (be-)" sustenance replaces it entirely
