Ketubot 105 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the contradiction between the Mishna listing 2 judges and the Baraita listing 3?
A. The third judge served only temporarily
B. The Tanna of the Mishna included only those important to him
C. The Baraita is from a later period when a third was added
D. One of the three was actually a scribe, not a judge
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the Tanna taught in the Mishna only those judges pertinent to the topic at hand — 'those important to him he teaches, those not important he does not teach.'
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was Karna's practice, and why was it permitted?
A. He judged for free — compensation was forbidden
B. He took an istera from each party as compensation for lost wages
C. He took payment only from the winning party after the verdict
D. He accepted gifts of food instead of money to avoid the bribery prohibition
Correct: B — Karna took a small coin from both parties — not as a bribe or salary for judging, but as compensation for lost wages (שְׂכַר בַּטָּלָה), which is the only permitted form of judicial compensation.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rava's etymological explanation of the word שׁוֹחַד (bribe)?
A. שֶׁהוּא חַד — 'he is one' with the litigant
B. שׁוֹ חָד — 'his view is sharp' toward one side
C. שֶׁחָדַל — 'he ceases' to see truth
D. שֶׁחָדָשׁ — 'he renews' his loyalty to the giver
Correct: A — Rava reads שׁוֹחַד as שֶׁהוּא חַד — once a judge accepts a bribe, he becomes 'one' with the litigant, and since a person does not find fault in himself, he cannot judge impartially.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Yishmael, son of Rabbi Yosei, disqualify himself even though the fruits legally belonged to him?
A. Because accepting early delivery created a sense of gratitude that biased him
B. Because sharecroppers are automatically disqualified as litigants
C. Because the fruits were of unusually high quality that week
D. Because he had already promised to judge a different case that day
Correct: A — Even though the fruits were legally his (from his own sharecropper), the early delivery in connection with a court case created bias. R' Yishmael even caught himself mentally advocating for the sharecropper afterward, proving how deeply even potential gifts distort judgment.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Gemara, under what condition is a judge who borrows items from others disqualified?
A. If he borrows items worth more than a perutah
B. If he borrows but has no items to lend in return, creating one-sided dependency
C. If he borrows from anyone who has ever appeared in his court
D. If he borrows more than three times from the same person
Correct: B — Rabba bar Rav Sheila taught that a judge who only borrows without having items to lend back is disqualified — the one-sided dependency is equivalent to receiving a salary from the community. But reciprocal borrowing is fine.
