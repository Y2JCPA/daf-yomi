Ketubot 15 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say that R' Yannai cannot literally mean the woman was raped 'in the wagons'?
A. Because wagons are impure places
B. Because it was a crowded marketplace — such an act would not go unnoticed
C. Because wagons are exempt from mezuzah
D. Because there were no wagons in Tzippori
Correct: B — The Gemara objects that a rape could not have occurred in the crowded marketplace area where wagons conduct business. Rather, R' Yannai means 'at the time' the wagon convoys pass through.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the case of nine kosher stores and one non-kosher store, if a person bought from one store but forgot which, what is the ruling?
A. The meat is kosher — follow the majority
B. The meat is prohibited — kavu'a makes it 50/50
C. The meat requires salting and is then kosher
D. It depends on whether the city gates are locked
Correct: B — Since the person went to a fixed location (kavu'a), R' Zeira's principle applies: the uncertainty is treated as equally balanced (50/50), and the meat is prohibited despite the 9:1 majority.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav, what is the status of an abandoned baby found in a majority-Jewish city?
A. Fully Jewish for all purposes
B. Jewish for sustaining him, but NOT for lineage
C. Jewish for pikuaḥ nefesh only
D. Gentile until proven otherwise
Correct: B — Rav distinguishes: the baby is deemed Jewish for purposes of sustaining him (food, care), but for lineage matters (e.g., marriage to a Jewish woman), a single majority is insufficient and he would require conversion.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Shmuel hold regarding saving a life (pikuaḥ nefesh) and following the majority?
A. Always follow the majority, even for saving life
B. One does NOT follow the majority in matters of saving a life — always save
C. Follow the majority only if it is a double majority
D. Pikuaḥ nefesh only applies to known Jews
Correct: B — Shmuel rules: Ein holkhin b'pikuaḥ nefesh aḥar ha-rov — even in a majority-gentile city, one desecrates Shabbat to save the abandoned baby because we don't follow the majority when life is at stake.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Reish Lakish, in a city that is half Jewish and half gentile, what is the practical halakhic implication of deeming the abandoned baby Jewish?
A. He must be circumcised immediately
B. He is obligated in all mitzvot
C. For damages — if his ox gored a Jew's ox, he pays as a Jew and the claimant must prove otherwise
D. He may marry a Jewish woman without conversion
Correct: C — Reish Lakish applies this to damages: the baby pays half-damage as a Jew. For the claimant to demand full damages (as if he were a gentile), the claimant bears the burden of proof — ha-motzi me-ḥaveiro alav ha-re'aya.
