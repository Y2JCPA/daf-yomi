When Do We Follow the Majority (Rov)?

A woman was raped — is the child of flawed or unflawed lineage?
↓
R' Yannai: She is fit to marry a Kohen if it occurred at the time of the wagons (keronot) in Tzippori
↓
R' Ami: This requires a contingent of unflawed men passing through — two majorities needed
↓
Two Majorities Present
City majority + contingent majority both unflawed → Fit for Kehuna
Single Majority Only
One majority alone is insufficient for lineage matters
Key Principle The Sages established a higher standard for lineage (yiḥus) — requiring two concurrent majorities, unlike other areas of halakha where a single majority suffices.

The Incident at Tzippori: What Does R' Yannai Mean?

R' Yannai
If she was forced to engage in intercourse at the time of the wagons (keronot), she is fit to marry a Kohen.
Gemara
Does it enter your mind she was raped in the wagons? In the crowded marketplace?!
Gemara
Rather — at the time the convoys pass through, when the majority of passersby are of unflawed lineage.
R' Ami
This ruling applies only when there is a contingent of men of unflawed lineage passing there — creating a double majority.
Clarification The ruling is not about the physical wagons, but about the time period when traveling contingents of known lineage pass through Tzippori's marketplace.

Kavu'a vs. Parish: Fixed vs. Separated

Is the item/person in its fixed place or has it separated from the group?
↓
Parish (Separated)
Kol d'parish me-rubba parish
Whatever separates from a group is presumed from the majority
Kavu'a (Fixed)
R' Zeira: Uncertainty about a fixed item is treated as equally balanced (50/50) — we do NOT follow the majority
↓
This distinction explains why city residents (fixed) are treated differently from passing travelers (separated)
R' Zeira's Rule Kol kavu'a k'meḥetza al meḥetza dami — The legal status of uncertainty regarding any item fixed in its place is that of an uncertainty that is equally balanced.

Abandoned Baby: Rav vs. Shmuel

Rav

  • Majority Jewish city → baby deemed Jewish only for sustaining him (feeding, care)
  • For lineage (e.g., marrying a Jewish woman) — not deemed Jewish based on majority alone
  • Requires conversion for lineage purposes
  • Consistent: single majority insufficient for lineage

Shmuel

  • Majority Jewish → deemed Jewish for saving his life on Shabbat (pikuaḥ nefesh)
  • Even in a gentile majority city — still desecrate Shabbat to save him
  • One does not follow the majority in matters involving saving a life
  • Any doubt about endangering a Jewish life → save him
Rav Yosef / Shmuel Ein holkhin b'pikuaḥ nefesh aḥar ha-rov — One does not follow the majority in matters of saving a life. Even the slightest chance of a Jewish life at risk overrides Shabbat.

Practical Rulings for the Abandoned Baby (Rav Pappa & Reish Lakish)

🏘️ Majority Gentile city → Baby deemed gentile: may be fed non-kosher food (neveilot)
↓
✡️ Majority Jewish city → Baby deemed Jewish: obligated to return his lost property
↓
⚖️ Half and half → Baby deemed Jewish for damages (Reish Lakish)
↓
🐂 His ox gored ours → He pays half-damage (as a Jew); for the other half, burden of proof is on the claimant
↓
📜 Principle: In uncertain status, the claimant must bring proof — ha-motzi me-ḥaveiro alav ha-re'aya
Reish Lakish In a 50/50 city, the baby has presumptive Jewish status for damages. The one seeking additional payment must prove the baby is not Jewish.
