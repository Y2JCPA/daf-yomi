Ketubot 61 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
If a wife wants to nurse but the husband wants a wet nurse, whose preference is followed?
A. The husband's — he decides household matters
B. The wife's — she suffers the physical discomfort
C. A beit din decides based on the child's welfare
D. They follow the wife's family custom
Correct: B — The Gemara rules we follow the wife's wishes because she is the one who suffers from breast engorgement if she does not nurse.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the principle 'she ascends with him but does not descend' mean in context?
A. She must follow all his family customs
B. She gains her husband's higher status but keeps her own if his is lower
C. She must live wherever he lives
D. She inherits his debts along with his assets
Correct: B — A wife rises to her husband's socioeconomic level if it is higher, but if his status is lower than hers, she retains her own standard — she never descends.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does bringing 3 maidservants still not fully exempt the wife from all tasks?
A. Because she must always spin wool by Torah law
B. Because 3 servants cannot cover Shabbat duties
C. Because a larger household attracts more guests and wayfarers
D. Because the husband needs personal attention from her specifically
Correct: C — The husband argues that as the household grows, more guests come — so 3 servants still can't cover everything. At 4, they assist one another and form a self-sufficient team.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which tasks must a wife still perform even if she has four or more maidservants?
A. Cooking and nursing
B. Spinning wool and grinding wheat
C. Pouring his cup, making his bed, and washing his face, hands, and feet
D. Greeting guests and serving wayfarers
Correct: C — Rav Huna teaches that pouring his cup, making his bed, and washing his face, hands, and feet are acts of affection — not household labor — and cannot be delegated to servants.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the practical difference between R' Eliezer ('idleness leads to licentiousness') and Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel ('idleness leads to idiocy')?
A. R' Eliezer requires divorce; RSBG does not
B. If she plays games or with pets, RSBG is satisfied but R' Eliezer is not
C. R' Eliezer applies only to wealthy women; RSBG applies to all
D. There is no practical difference — they agree completely
Correct: B — Playing with dogs or board games prevents idiocy (satisfying RSBG) but may still lead to licentiousness (failing R' Eliezer's standard). R' Eliezer requires productive work like spinning wool.
