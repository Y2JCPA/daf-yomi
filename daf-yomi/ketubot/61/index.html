Ketubot 61: A Wife's Duties, Status & Dignity

Nursing Dispute
Who decides — husband or wife?
↓
She wants to nurse, he doesn't
We follow her — she suffers the engorgement
He wants her to nurse, she doesn't
Depends on family custom — she ascends to his status
↓
Household Tasks
Exemptions based on maidservants brought into the marriage
↓
Even fully exempt → she still performs acts of affection
Core Principle Marriage balances practical obligations with dignity and affection — a wife ascends with her husband but never descends.
3 / 9

Who Decides About Nursing?

Rav Huna bar Ḥinnana
If she wants to nurse and he doesn't — we follow her, because she suffers the engorgement of her breasts.
Rav Huna bar Ḥinnana
But if he wants her to nurse and she doesn't — what if her family doesn't nurse but his family does?
The Students
A wife ascends with her husband's status but does not descend. If his family doesn't nurse, she is exempt.
Rav Huna
The source: "She is a man's wife" (בְּעוּלַת) — be'ula hints at aliya (ascent), not descent.
R' Elazar
My source: "Mother of all living" (Gen. 3:20) — given for living, not for suffering.
Key Principle A wife rises to her husband's socioeconomic status but never descends below her own family's standard.

Why Can't She Claim Full Exemption With Fewer Servants?

Wife: "I brought a woman in my place — she does everything!"
↓
Husband: "She works for me AND herself — but who works for you?"
↓
Wife brings 2nd: "One for you, one for me!"
↓
Husband: "Who serves the guests and wayfarers?"
↓
Wife brings 3rd: "One for guests too!"
↓
Husband: "More household members = more guests!"
↓
With 4 maidservants — they assist one another and complete all tasks
Logic Each additional servant covers more needs, but the household also grows — only at four do they form a self-sufficient team.

Acts of Affection: Cannot Be Delegated

Even With 4+ Servants

  • ✅ Pouring his cup
  • ✅ Making his bed
  • ✅ Washing his face, hands, and feet
  • These are gestures of love, not labor

Niddah Restriction

  • ❌ Must NOT pour his cup
  • ❌ Must NOT make his bed
  • ❌ Must NOT wash his face, hands, and feet
  • Precisely because they are intimate acts
Rav Huna's Ruling All tasks a wife does for her husband, a menstruating woman may also do — except these three acts of affection.

Idleness: Licentiousness or Idiocy?

R' Eliezer
Even with 100 maidservants, he can compel her to spin wool — idleness leads to licentiousness (zimmah).
Rabban Shimon b. Gamliel
One who vows his wife from all work must divorce her — idleness leads to idiocy (shiamum).
Gemara
What's the practical difference? If she plays with small dogs or board games — she avoids idiocy but may still fall into licentiousness.
Rav Malkiyyu / Rav Adda bar Ahava
The halakha follows R' Eliezer.
R' Yehuda's Addition A husband cannot compel his wife to spin flax — it causes the mouth to smell foul and lips to stiffen. This applies specifically to Roman flax.
