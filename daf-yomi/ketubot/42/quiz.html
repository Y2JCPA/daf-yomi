Ketubot 42 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say the Mishna's teaching about payments belonging to the father is not obvious?
A. Because the Torah never mentions the father's rights
B. Because the Mishna needs to address what happens when the father dies after trial but before collection
C. Because the daughter always collects directly
D. Because the payments are considered gifts, not obligations
Correct: B — The Gemara explains the Mishna is necessary to teach the case where she stood for judgment before the father died — addressing the dispute between R' Shimon and the Rabbis about inheritance.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Abaye's dilemma to Rabba about a post-trial fine?
A. Whether the daughter or father collects
B. Whether a false oath denying a post-trial fine triggers an oath offering
C. Whether the court can reverse its verdict
D. Whether the seducer must also pay for pain
Correct: B — Abaye asks: According to R' Shimon, if someone denies under oath a fine that was already imposed by court, is it now considered mamon (triggering oath offering liability) or still a knas?
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Abaye prove that the baraita's latter clause (R' Shimon's exemption) refers to a post-trial case?
A. From a separate Mishna about inheritance
B. From the fact that the first clause mentions double payment, which is only relevant post-trial
C. From a verse in Deuteronomy about giving
D. From R' Shimon's own explicit statement
Correct: B — The first clause mentions double payment — since everyone agrees one who admits to a knas is exempt, double payment only makes sense post-trial. Since the first clause is post-trial, the latter clause must be as well.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What was Rav Yosef's resolution to the 22-year-old question?
A. Fines can never be inherited under any circumstances
B. The verse 'shall give the father' means the father's right arises only at the time of actual giving — so if he dies first, sons don't inherit
C. R' Shimon and the Rabbis actually agree on inheritance
D. The daughter forfeits all rights once the father stands trial
Correct: B — Rav Yosef derived from 'v'natan... la'avi ha'na'arah' that the father is entitled to rape/seduction payments only from the moment of giving. If he dies before receiving payment, sons cannot inherit — the daughter collects instead.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabba's final position, what is the practical difference between post-trial fines and the specific case of rape/seduction payments?
A. Post-trial fines are generally inheritable, but rape/seduction payments are not — due to the verse's 'from time of giving' language
B. There is no difference; all fines work the same way
C. Rape/seduction payments are inheritable but other fines are not
D. Both are subject to oath offering liability
Correct: A — Rabba maintains that post-trial fines generally become mamon and are inheritable. But rape/seduction fines are a special exception — the Torah conditions the father's right on actual receipt ('v'natan'), so they cannot be inherited if he dies before collection.
