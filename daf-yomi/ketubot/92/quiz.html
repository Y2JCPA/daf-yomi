Ketubot 92 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, when Shimon sells a field back to Reuven with a guarantee and Reuven's OWN creditor repossesses it, what happens?
A. Shimon must compensate Reuven
B. Shimon does NOT need to compensate Reuven
C. They split the loss equally
D. The creditor must return the field
Correct: B — Rava holds that a guarantee does not cover repossession by the buyer's own creditors — only creditors of prior owners.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why can Reuven's orphans refuse to compensate Shimon for repossession while still demanding he pay his debt?
A. Orphans are exempt from all their father's obligations
B. The debt Shimon owes is considered movable property, which is not liened to creditors of orphans
C. Shimon waived his guarantee rights at the time of sale
D. The guarantee expired when Reuven died
Correct: B — The money Shimon owes is classified as movable property (metaltelin), and movable property of orphans is not subject to a creditor's lien.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rava's 'clever' strategy for Shimon when dealing with Reuven's orphans?
A. Refuse to pay until the orphans compensate him first
B. Pay the orphans with real estate so he can then seize it back for his guarantee claim
C. Appeal to a higher court for arbitration
D. Sell the promissory note to a third party
Correct: B — If Shimon pays with land, the orphans now hold inherited real estate — which CAN be seized by their father's creditor (i.e., Shimon's guarantee claim).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When can Levi deflect a creditor who wants to repossess his field in Rabba's chain-of-sales case?
A. Only if Levi bought the field directly from Reuven
B. If Levi bought superior or inferior quality land, or if Shimon retained equivalent intermediate-quality land
C. Only if Levi paid more than market value
D. Levi can never deflect — the creditor always has priority
Correct: B — Since creditors collect from intermediate-quality land, Levi can deflect if his land is not intermediate quality or if Shimon still holds equivalent intermediate land.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Abaye, can a seller who sold a field WITHOUT a guarantee intervene to stop a creditor's repossession?
A. No — without a guarantee the seller has no legal standing
B. Yes — but only if the buyer requests it
C. According to one version, yes — because the seller doesn't want the buyer to have a grievance against him
D. Only if the seller is also a creditor of the buyer
Correct: C — The second version (yesh omrim) of Abaye's ruling allows even a seller without a guarantee to intervene, arguing he doesn't want the buyer to bear a grievance against him.
