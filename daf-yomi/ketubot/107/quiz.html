5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Shmuel agree that the court does not touch the husband's estate for the first three months?
A. Because a person does not leave his house empty — he surely left provisions
B. Because the wife has not yet proven her claim in court
C. Because the husband might return within three months
D. Because the ketubah obligation only begins after three months
Correct: A — Shmuel states that even Rav agrees on this point: 'a person does not leave his house empty' (אין אדם מניח ביתו ריקם), so for the first three months, the wife presumably has provisions.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the practical difference between Rav Zevid's 'bundle of money' theory and Rav Pappa's 'earnings waiver' theory?
A. They differ regarding an adult wife whose earnings are insufficient, and a minor wife whose earnings suffice
B. They differ only regarding whether the wife must take an oath
C. One applies to a first wife and the other to a second wife
D. They differ regarding whether the court can sell the husband's land
Correct: A — An adult whose earnings are insufficient: Rav Zevid says no support needed (he left money), Rav Pappa says court must supplement. A minor whose earnings suffice: Rav Zevid says court must help (no money left with a child), Rav Pappa says no support (she can use earnings).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Shmuel consistently respond to the multiple baraitot that seem to show the court apportioning sustenance?
A. He argues those baraitot are rejected and not authoritative
B. He reinterprets each one as referring to a case where they heard the husband died
C. He claims those baraitot follow Rav's opinion, not the halakha
D. He distinguishes between court-initiated and wife-initiated requests
Correct: B — Shmuel's consistent strategy is to reinterpret every challenging source as referring to a case where the husband was rumored dead — a scenario where even Shmuel agrees the court provides sustenance.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why can't Shmuel use his usual defenses (bundle of money / earnings waiver) in the case of the yevama?
A. Because the yevama is always a minor
B. Because the yavam is not emotionally close enough to leave her money, and she doesn't yet owe him her earnings
C. Because the yavam explicitly accepted responsibility in court
D. Because the deceased husband's estate covers her needs automatically
Correct: B — The yavam wouldn't leave her a bundle of money because he's not that close to her, and he can't waive her earnings because she has no earnings obligation to him yet — making the yevama case structurally different from a regular wife.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the final practical conclusion of the Rav-Shmuel dispute on this daf?
A. The halakha follows Rav in all cases
B. The halakha follows Shmuel in all cases
C. No definitive resolution is reached — historical authorities ruled both ways
D. A compromise position is adopted: court apportions only after six months
Correct: C — The Gemara asks 'What is the conclusion?' and presents conflicting reports of how R' Yehuda HaNasi and R' Yishmael ruled in practice, with two different versions (Rav Dimi vs. Ravin) — leaving the dispute unresolved on this daf.
