5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Mishna need to teach the case of a captive woman claiming purity, according to one opinion?
A. To introduce the rule that witnesses arriving after marriage don't force her to leave
B. To teach that captivity is more severe than divorce
C. To establish that monetary matters are different from ritual matters
D. To show that a single witness is sufficient for captivity cases
Correct: A — According to one opinion, the captive woman case is taught so the tanna can add the ruling that if witnesses come after she already married, she need not leave her husband.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Abaye resolve R' Yehuda's apparent contradiction between the priests case and the donkey drivers case?
A. He reverses the attribution in one mishna
B. He says demai is inherently lenient since most amei ha'aretz tithe, so R' Yehuda is lenient only there
C. He says the donkey drivers case involves a different R' Yehuda
D. He says both cases actually involve the same level of concern
Correct: B — Abaye explains that R' Yehuda is lenient about donkey drivers specifically because demai is a rabbinic stringency based on a remote concern — most amei ha'aretz do tithe. His usual position is to suspect collusion.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In R' Ḥama bar Ukva's resolution about the potter's vessels, what determines whether the pots are pure or impure?
A. Whether the potter washed his hands before making them
B. Whether the tools of the potter's trade are in his hand, indicating intent to sell
C. Whether the pots are glazed or unglazed
D. Whether the potter descended to the river or stayed with his pots
Correct: B — R' Ḥama bar Ukva distinguishes based on whether the potter holds his tools of trade: if yes, he intends to sell, and buyers will handle all the pots; if no, strangers won't touch them.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the alternative resolution ('if you wish, say'), what is the real disagreement between R' Yehuda and the Rabbis about two men claiming priesthood?
A. Whether women can also testify about priestly status
B. Whether one elevates from teruma consumption to full priestly lineage
C. Whether collusion between the two men is likely
D. Whether three witnesses are needed for any court testimony
Correct: B — The alternative resolution reframes the dispute entirely: R' Yehuda holds one elevates from teruma to lineage (requiring stricter proof even for teruma), while the Rabbis hold they are separate matters.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why might the Priestly Benediction be a weaker basis for establishing priestly lineage than teruma consumption?
A. The Priestly Benediction is only rabbinic while teruma is biblical
B. A non-priest reciting the benediction violates only a positive commandment, while a non-priest eating teruma faces death at Heaven's hand
C. The Priestly Benediction can be recited by Levites as well
D. Teruma requires two witnesses while the benediction requires three
Correct: B — A non-priest eating teruma risks death at Heaven's hand — a powerful deterrent ensuring only real priests would do so. A non-priest reciting the Priestly Benediction only violates a positive commandment, a lesser deterrent, making it less reliable proof of priesthood.
