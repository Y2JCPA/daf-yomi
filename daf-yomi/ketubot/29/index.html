Ketubot 29 — The Kenas for Forbidden Relations

CORE QUESTION The Mishna lists women of flawed lineage (mamzeret, netina, kutit) and women in forbidden relationships (sister, aunt, niddah) for whom a rapist pays the fifty-sela fine. The Gemara asks: how do we know the fine applies to women the rapist cannot legally marry? Multiple derivations are offered from the repeated terms na'arah and betulah in the Torah.

Na'arah vs. Minor: Who Gets the Fine?

R' Meir
A minor (before two hairs) can be sold by her father but has no fine. A na'arah (after two hairs) has a fine but cannot be sold.
R' Meir's Principle
Where there is sale, there is no fine. Where there is a fine, there is no sale.
The Rabbis
A minor from age three years and one day until she matures — there is a fine for her.
Gemara
The Rabbis hold: there is a fine even where there is sale. Both can apply simultaneously.
RULING Rav Yehuda said in Rav's name: The Mishna's language (na'arah — not minor) follows R' Meir.

Deriving the Fine for Forbidden Women

Reish Lakish

  • Derives from three mentions of na'arah (young woman) in Deut. 22:28-29
  • "na'arah" + "na'arah" + "ha-na'arah" (the definite article = extra mention)
  • One for the basic law, one to include prohibitions (lavin), one for karet

Rav Pappa

  • Derives from three mentions of betulah (virgin) in Exod. 22:15-16
  • "betulah" + "betulot" + "ha-betulot"
  • One for the basic law, one to include prohibitions (lavin), one for karet
RESOLUTION They don't actually disagree — there are six verses total across both passages. Each uses his three for specific derivations, and together they cover all the needed teachings.

'To Him She Shall Be a Wife' — Who Qualifies?

Shimon HaTimni
"Tihye" (she shall be) means: a woman for whom there is havaya (valid betrothal). If betrothal cannot take effect, no fine.
R' Shimon b. Menasya
"She shall be a wife" means: a woman suitable for him to sustain — one he is not obligated to divorce.
R' Zeira
The practical difference: a mamzeret. Betrothal does take effect for her, but she is not suitable to sustain because he must divorce her.
Gemara
According to R' Akiva (betrothal doesn't work for lavin), the difference is a widow married to a Kohen Gadol.
MISHNA vs. BARAITA Our Mishna rejects these restrictive opinions — it rules that the fine applies even to mamzeret and other forbidden women.
