Are Self-Imposed Written Obligations Binding?

R' Yochanan
If one writes "I owe you 100 dinars", even without receiving anything, he is obligated to pay.
Reish Lakish
A written obligation without an underlying transaction is not binding — it is a mere asmakhta.
Gemara
The Mishna's case may involve שטרי פסיקתא — documents recording pre-marriage stipulations, which are binding per Rav Giddel.
Rava
This dispute parallels the Tannaitic debate between R' Yishmael and Ben Nannas regarding a guarantor who signs after the contract.
Core Issue The daf explores when verbal commitments and self-imposed written obligations create legally enforceable duties — especially in the context of marriage negotiations.

Rav Giddel's Rule: Marriage Negotiations Are Binding

👨‍👦 Father of groom is asked: "How much do you give your son?" — answers a specific amount
↓
👨‍👧 Father of bride is asked: "How much do you give your daughter?" — answers a specific amount
↓
💍 The son and daughter arise and perform betrothal (קידושין)
↓
✅ All verbal obligations are acquired and binding — no additional act of acquisition (קנין) needed
↓
📜 These are דברים הנקנים באמירה — things acquired through words alone
Key Principle The betrothal itself serves as the mechanism that makes the parents' verbal financial commitments legally binding.

Guarantor After Signatures: R' Yishmael vs. Ben Nannas

R' Yishmael
A guarantor who writes his guarantee after the contract signatures — the creditor may collect from his unsold property (בני חורין).
Ben Nannas
The creditor cannot collect at all — not from unsold property, nor from liened property.
Ben Nannas
Parable: If someone strangling a debtor in the marketplace is told by a bystander "Leave him, I'll pay" — the bystander is exempt. The lender didn't rely on him.
Rava
R' Yochanan follows R' Yishmael (self-imposed obligations bind). Reish Lakish follows Ben Nannas (they do not).
Gemara
Per Ben Nannas, everyone agrees writing "I owe you" without a transaction is not binding. The dispute is only within R' Yishmael's view.
Resolution Reish Lakish holds that even R' Yishmael only validates a guarantor's obligation because it relates to an existing Torah-law debt — not a self-created obligation from nothing.

Pidyon HaBen: Writing "I Owe You 5 Sela"

Father writes to a Kohen:
"I owe you 5 sela"
↓
Is the father obligated to pay?
Yes — pays the money
He must pay 5 sela
(Torah obligation exists independently of the document)
But...
His son is still NOT redeemed
even after payment
↓
Why not redeemed? Ulla's decree:
Rabbinically invalidated lest people think
one can redeem with documents (שטרות) instead of actual money (כסף)
Distinction The document doesn't create the obligation — the Torah does. The document merely selects which Kohen receives payment.

Rava's Limitation: When Do Verbal Agreements Bind?

Father of a Na'arah (Young Woman)

  • Betrothal money belongs to the father
  • Ketubah rights belong to the father
  • Father derives direct monetary benefit
  • ✅ Verbal commitment is binding

Father of a Bogeret (Grown Woman)

  • Betrothal money belongs to her
  • Ketubah rights belong to her
  • Father has no direct monetary benefit
  • ❌ Rava initially: commitment not binding

Rava's Reversal

  • By God! Rav meant even a bogeret
  • Father of groom also has no monetary benefit
  • The marriage itself is the benefit
  • ✅ Both fathers' commitments are binding
Conclusion The mutual benefit of the marriage union itself — not direct monetary gain — is sufficient consideration to make verbal commitments binding.

Can Verbal Marriage Stipulations Be Written Down?

Ravina
Are verbal marriage stipulations allowed to be written in a proper contract afterward?
Rav Ashi
No, they may not be written down afterward.
Ravina
But the Mishna says the perspicacious ones would write their stipulations! ("הפקחין היו כותבין")
Rav Ashi
"Write" means "say" — as in the Mishna (83a): "One who writes to his wife" means "one who says to his wife," per R' Ḥiyya.
Gemara
Challenge from Bava Batra 167b: "One writes betrothal documents with consent of both" — implying writing is permitted!
Gemara
Rejected: That refers to actual betrothal documents (שטרי קידושין), not documents of stipulation — per Rav Pappa and Rav Sherevya.
Practical Impact Per Rav Ashi, verbal marriage agreements are binding but cannot be formalized into enforceable written contracts — they remain oral obligations only.
