Can Money Be Called 'Flogging'?

Baraita
The defamer is flogged only if he had intercourse with her.
Rav Pappa
This 'flogging' refers to the monetary fine — he pays only if he consummated the marriage.
Gemara
Does anyone call monetary payment 'flogging'?
Rav Pappa
Yes! R' Yosei b. R' Yehuda says one who vows half his erekh is 'flogged' — meaning he must pay his full valuation.
Gemara
Why the full amount? It's a rabbinic decree — lest people confuse 'half my valuation' with 'valuation of half my body' (a vital limb = full valuation).
KEY INSIGHT The term 'malkot' (flogging) can metaphorically refer to financial penalties, broadening how we read punishment language in the Torah.

Gezera Shava Chain: Source for Defamer's Flogging

Defamer: 'And they shall chastise him' (Deut. 22:18)
↓
Rebellious Son: 'They chastise him' (Deut. 21:18) — same word links these
↓
Rebellious Son: Contains word 'ben' (son) in same verse
↓
Flogging: 'If the wicked man deserve (bin) to be flogged' (Deut. 25:2)
METHOD A double gezera shava: 'chastise'→'chastise' links defamer to rebellious son, then 'ben'→'bin' links to the flogging verse.

Source of the Prohibition Against Defamation

R' Elazar

  • Source: 'You shall not go as a talebearer' (Lev. 19:16)
  • This verse warns against spreading slander
  • R' Natan's verse? Needed for R' Pinchas ben Yair's teaching
  • R' Pinchas: 'Keep from every evil thing' = avoid impure thoughts by day lest they cause nocturnal emissions

R' Natan

  • Source: 'Keep yourself from every evil thing (davar)' (Deut. 23:10)
  • Reads davar ra as dibbur ra — evil speech
  • R' Elazar's verse? Reads 'rakhil' as 'rakh la'
  • Meaning: a court must not be soft to one litigant and harsh to the other
PATTERN Each Sage uses one verse for the defamer's prohibition and repurposes the other's verse for an entirely different halakha.

Who Gets the Betrothal Money?

Rav Yehuda
'She shall go out for nothing, without money' (Ex. 21:11) — no money for this master (the buyer), but there is money for another master — her father.
Gemara
Maybe 'another master' means herself — she keeps the betrothal money?
Rav Yehuda
No — the father accepts the betrothal: 'I gave my daughter to this man' (Deut. 22:16). If he accepts it, he takes the money.
Gemara
That works for a minor. But a na'arah (young woman) can act for herself — shouldn't she keep the money?
Rav Yehuda
'Being in her youth, in her father's house' (Num. 30:17) — all gains during youth belong to her father.
OBJECTION The Gemara counters: that verse is about vow nullification, not money. We cannot derive monetary matters from ritual matters (ein danin mamon me-issurin).
