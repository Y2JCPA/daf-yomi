The Land of Israel: Longing, Oaths & Future Glory

Rav Yehuda
It is prohibited to ascend from Babylonia to Eretz Yisrael — the exile is a divine decree based on Jeremiah 27:22.
Rabbi Zeira
That verse refers to the Temple vessels, not the people! Individuals may ascend to Eretz Yisrael.
R' Yosei b. R' Ḥanina
Three Oaths from Shir HaShirim: Jews don't ascend as a wall, don't rebel against the nations, and nations don't oppress Jews excessively.
Rabbi Elazar
Whoever lives in Eretz Yisrael dwells without sin. Whoever is buried there is as if buried beneath the altar.
Core Tension Does the divine decree of exile prohibit all return, or only collective, forced return — leaving room for individual ascent?

Rav Yehuda vs. Rabbi Zeira: Leaving Babylonia

Rav Yehuda
"They shall be taken to Babylonia and there they shall remain" (Jeremiah 27:22) — we are bound by divine decree to stay in Babylonia.
Rabbi Zeira
That verse refers to the Temple service vessels, not the Jewish people! The prior verse (27:21) explicitly says "concerning the vessels."
Rav Yehuda
Then I derive it from Shir HaShirim: "I adjure you… that you not awaken or stir up love until it please" (2:7) — no act of redemption before its time!
Rabbi Zeira
That oath means Jews should not ascend as a wall — en masse. Individuals may immigrate as they wish.
Key Distinction Rabbi Zeira distinguishes between mass collective return (prohibited) and individual aliyah (permitted).

The Six Oaths of Shir HaShirim

SourceOath
R' Yosei b. R' Ḥanina #1Jews shall not ascend to Eretz Yisrael as a wall (en masse)
R' Yosei b. R' Ḥanina #2Jews shall not rebel against the nations of the world
R' Yosei b. R' Ḥanina #3Nations shall not subjugate Jews excessively
Rabbi Levi #4Those who know shall not reveal the End of Days
Rabbi Levi #5They shall not distance the End by saying it is far off
Rabbi Levi #6They shall not reveal the secret of Israel to the nations
Three + Three R' Yosei b. R' Ḥanina derives three oaths from three verses in Shir HaShirim (2:7, 3:5, 8:4). Rabbi Levi expands to six using the doubled phrase "that you not awaken or stir up."

Living vs. Being Buried in Eretz Yisrael

Living in the Land

  • R' Elazar: One who resides there dwells without transgression
  • Source: "The people that dwell there shall be forgiven their iniquity" (Isaiah 33:24)
  • Rava to Rav Ashi: This specifically applies to those who suffer from sickness

Buried in the Land

  • Rav Anan: Being buried there is like being buried beneath the altar
  • Derives from wordplay: adama (earth/altar) and admato (His land) in Deut. 32:43
  • Ulla died outside the Land — R' Elazar mourned: absorption in the soil while alive is superior to burial after death
Ulla's Tragedy Even though Ulla's coffin was brought to Eretz Yisrael, R' Elazar taught: "One absorbed by the soil while alive is not similar to one absorbed only after death."

Who Merits Resurrection? (Teḥiyat HaMetim)

R' Elazar: "The dead live not" (Isaiah 26:14) — the uneducated (amei ha'aretz) will not be resurrected
↓
R' Yoḥanan objects: God is not pleased you say this about ordinary Jews!
R' Yoḥanan's View
The verse speaks of those who succumb to idol worship — only they lose resurrection
R' Elazar's Proof
"Your dew is the dew of light" (Isaiah 26:19) — whoever uses the light of Torah will be revived; whoever does not, will not
↓
R' Elazar offers a remedy: "You who cleave to Hashem are alive every one of you" (Deut. 4:4)
Resolution Even the uneducated merit life in the World-to-Come by supporting Torah scholars — marrying daughters to them, investing their money, or benefiting them with property.

How to 'Cleave to Hashem' (Devekut)

❓ "Cleave to Hashem" (Deut. 30:20) — but Hashem is a consuming fire! (Deut. 4:24)
↓
💍 Marry your daughter to a Torah scholar
↓
💰 Conduct business (perakmatya) on behalf of Torah scholars
↓
🤝 Utilize wealth to benefit Torah scholars with your property
↓
✨ Scripture credits such a person as though cleaving to the Divine Presence itself
Practical Takeaway Physical closeness to God is impossible — but supporting those who learn Torah is considered equivalent to direct attachment to the Shekhinah.
