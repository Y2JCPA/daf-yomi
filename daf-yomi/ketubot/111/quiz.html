Ketubot 111 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Zeira, what does Jeremiah 27:22 ('they shall remain in Babylonia') refer to?
A. The Jewish people in exile
B. The Temple service vessels
C. The prophets of Israel
D. The kohanim and levi'im
Correct: B — Rabbi Zeira interprets the verse as referring to the Temple vessels, based on the preceding verse (27:21) which explicitly mentions 'the vessels that remain in the house of the Lord.' This allowed him to argue that individuals could still ascend to Eretz Yisrael.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the oath 'not to ascend as a wall' mean according to the Gemara?
A. Not to build the walls of Jerusalem
B. Not to ascend to Eretz Yisrael en masse as a collective
C. Not to fortify Babylonian cities
D. Not to rebel militarily against any nation
Correct: B — Rabbi Zeira interprets 'as a wall' (ke-ḥomah) to mean a mass, collective immigration. This is distinct from individual aliyah, which Rabbi Zeira permits. The image of a 'wall' conveys a unified, forceful national movement.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Elazar initially say the uneducated (amei ha'aretz) won't be resurrected, and how does Rabbi Yoḥanan respond?
A. R' Elazar derives it from Isaiah 26:14; R' Yoḥanan says it refers only to idol worshippers
B. R' Elazar derives it from Deuteronomy; R' Yoḥanan says all Jews are resurrected
C. R' Elazar cites a baraita; R' Yoḥanan says it was retracted
D. R' Elazar derives it from Psalms; R' Yoḥanan says it refers to wicked kings
Correct: A — R' Elazar derives from 'The dead live not' (Isaiah 26:14) that those spiritually 'dead' — the uneducated — won't be resurrected. R' Yoḥanan objects, saying God is displeased with this reading, and the verse actually refers to those who succumb to idol worship.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara define 'cleaving to Hashem' in practical terms?
A. Through constant prayer and fasting
B. By marrying one's daughter to a Torah scholar, conducting business for scholars, or benefiting them with one's property
C. Through living in Eretz Yisrael and performing all mitzvot
D. By studying Torah for at least eight hours daily
Correct: B — Since Hashem is described as a 'consuming fire' and physical closeness is impossible, the Gemara redefines devekut practically: marrying daughters to scholars, investing scholars' money, and supporting them materially. This is credited as if one cleaves to the Divine Presence.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What kal va'ḥomer (a fortiori reasoning) does R' Ḥiyya bar Yosef use to prove the righteous will be resurrected in their clothes?
A. If Adam was created clothed, the righteous will surely rise clothed
B. If wheat — buried as a naked seed — emerges with layers of covering, the righteous buried clothed will certainly rise dressed
C. If the kohanim served in special garments, the righteous will rise in garments
D. If Elijah ascended in his cloak, the righteous will descend in theirs
Correct: B — R' Ḥiyya bar Yosef reasons from wheat: a bare seed is planted 'naked' yet sprouts with straw, chaff, and other coverings. If nature provides clothing for a seed, all the more so will the righteous — who are buried fully clothed — emerge properly dressed at the resurrection.
