Rejecting the Baraita: Two Stringencies Combined

Rav Yosef
This baraita supports my position — movable property can be seized for a ketubah debt through a chain of obligations.
Gemara
Perhaps the baraita follows R' Natan: a creditor can appropriate funds from a debtor's debtor (Numbers 5:7 — 'he shall give it to him against whom he has been guilty').
Gemara
But we find no single Tanna who holds both R' Meir's stringency (movable property mortgaged for ketubah) and R' Natan's (chain appropriation).
Rava
Now I understand what Abaye meant when he said: 'This is not a mishna' — the baraita is non-authoritative because it combines two opinions no one holds together.
Principle A teaching that can only be explained by combining two independent stringencies — held by no single authority — is rejected as non-authoritative.

The Yavam of Mata Meḥasya: Dividing the Inheritance

A man's yevama falls to him in Mata Meḥasya. His brother tries to disqualify her via a get.
↓
Yavam offers: 'I'll divide the property with you'
↓
Brother fears a Pumbedita swindle — promises made and retracted
↓
Yavam: 'Divide it for yourself from now'
↓
Mar bar Rav Ashi
Invalid — the yavam doesn't yet own the property (hasn't performed yibum), so he cannot transfer what isn't his
Cow Precedent
R' Yochanan: pulling a cow with 30-day delay works — but only because the seller already owns the cow
Key Distinction Delayed acquisition works only when the transferor currently owns the item. A yavam who hasn't yet performed yibum cannot divide property he doesn't possess.

Delayed Acquisition: 'From Now' vs. Unspecified

Rav Dimi: R' Yochanan

  • Pull a cow, acquire after 30 days
  • Even if cow is in a distant meadow at day 30
  • The present act of pulling IS effective for later
  • ✅ Acquisition succeeds

Ravin: R' Yochanan

  • Pull a cow, acquire after 30 days
  • Acquisition does NOT take effect
  • Seems to contradict Rav Dimi's version!
  • ❌ Acquisition fails

Resolution

  • Rav Dimi's case: seller said 'acquire from now'
  • Retroactive acquisition after 30 days
  • Ravin's case: no 'from now' stipulation
  • Without present activation, future acquisition fails
קנה מעכשיו The phrase 'from now' (מעכשיו) is the key to delayed acquisitions. It activates the kinyan in the present, with completion deferred.

Ulla & Reish Lakish: Can the Yavam Divide the Property?

ScenarioRulingReasoning
Yibum first, then divided property❌ He has done nothingThe division is void — property belongs to the yevama's ketubah lien
Divided property then yibum❌ He has done nothingCannot transfer what he doesn't yet own
Rav Sheshet's objectionIf yibum-then-divide fails, why even ask about divide-then-yibum?Two separate incidents brought before Ulla independently
Reish Lakish's rule❌ Either order — ineffectiveWhether yibum first or division first, it has no legal effect

הלכה The Gemara concludes: regardless of the sequence, the yavam's attempt to divide inherited property with his brother is legally void.

Why the Ketubah Falls on the First Husband's Property

דברים כה:ה — וּלְקָחָהּ לוֹ לְאִשָּׁה — 'Once he has taken her, she is like his wife in all respects'
מן השמים זיכו לו — From Heaven They Acquired Her for Him The yavam did not choose his wife — Heaven assigned her to him through the mitzvah of levirate marriage. Therefore he is not obligated to write a new ketubah from his own property. He relies on his deceased brother's marriage contract.

However, if the first husband left no property at all, the yevama still receives a ketubah from the yavam — so that she will not be demeaned in his eyes (שלא תהא קלה בעיניו להוציאה), ensuring he cannot easily divorce her.
