Ketubot 82 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is the baraita cited by Rav Yosef rejected as non-authoritative?
A. It contradicts the Mishna directly
B. No single Tanna holds both R' Meir's and R' Natan's stringencies together
C. It was transmitted incorrectly from Eretz Yisrael
D. Abaye found a contradictory baraita in another tractate
Correct: B — The Gemara rejects the baraita because it can only be explained by combining R' Meir's view (movable property mortgaged for ketubah) with R' Natan's view (chain appropriation) — but no single Tanna holds both stringencies.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Mar bar Rav Ashi, why can't the yavam divide the deceased brother's property before performing yibum?
A. The brother's wife must consent first
B. Beit Din must supervise all property transfers
C. He doesn't yet own the property, so he cannot transfer it
D. Property division requires a written document
Correct: C — Mar bar Rav Ashi distinguishes the yavam's case from the cow case: the cow's seller already owns the item, but the yavam hasn't yet performed yibum and therefore doesn't own the property he's trying to divide.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the contradiction between Rav Dimi's and Ravin's versions of R' Yochanan's ruling on delayed acquisition?
A. One version applies to movable property, the other to land
B. Rav Dimi's case includes 'acquire from now' (מעכשיו); Ravin's does not
C. R' Yochanan changed his mind between the two rulings
D. One applies to sales, the other to gifts
Correct: B — When the seller says 'acquire from now,' the kinyan activates in the present and takes effect retroactively after 30 days (Rav Dimi). Without 'from now,' the acquisition cannot take effect later (Ravin).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the yevama's ketubah obligation fall on the first husband's property rather than the yavam's?
A. The yavam is too poor to pay
B. The Torah explicitly states this rule
C. From Heaven they acquired her for him — he didn't choose her, so he relies on his brother's ketubah
D. The first husband's family must bear responsibility for his widow
Correct: C — Since the yavam married her through a Torah obligation rather than by personal choice ('from Heaven they acquired a wife for him'), he is not required to write a new ketubah. He relies on the deceased brother's existing marriage contract.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What was the key innovation of Shimon ben Shatach's ordinance regarding the ketubah?
A. He raised the ketubah amount from 100 to 200 dinars
B. He required the ketubah to be deposited with Beit Din
C. He eliminated the cash requirement and made all the husband's property serve as a lien
D. He required two witnesses for every ketubah
Correct: C — Previous systems required cash set aside (causing men to delay marriage) or deposited sums (making divorce too easy). Shimon ben Shatach's reform created a property lien — enabling marriage without ready cash while making divorce financially consequential.
