What Was Established in Exchange for What?

Rav & Shmuel
They established something common for something common: sustenance (common) in exchange for earnings (common), and the silver ma'a in exchange for the surplus.
Rav Adda bar Ahava
They established something fixed for something fixed: the silver ma'a (fixed amount) in exchange for her earnings (fixed quota), and sustenance in exchange for the surplus.
Gemara
A baraita states: 'They established sustenance in exchange for her earnings' — this contradicts Rav Adda!
Rav Adda bar Ahava
Emend it: 'sustenance in exchange for the surplus of her earnings.'
Gemara
But the mishna on 64b says: 'If he does not give her a silver ma'a, her earnings belong to her' — supporting Rav Adda!
Rav & Shmuel
Emend that too: 'the surplus of her earnings belongs to her.'
Key Principle Both sides agree the husband provides sustenance and a silver ma'a — the dispute is which obligation corresponds to which right.

Resolving Shmuel's Contradiction on Konamot

Gemara
Shmuel rules like R' Yochanan the Cobbler (can't consecrate future items), but also rules like R' Yochanan ben Nuri that a wife's konam on her earnings takes effect after divorce!
Rav Yosef
Konamot are different (קונמות שאני). A person can prohibit another's produce to himself — konam has unique power over items not in one's domain.
Abaye
That analogy fails! In those cases at least one aspect is in the person's domain. But here the earnings don't exist yet and belong to another!
Rav Ashi
Konamot are a form of inherent sanctity (קדושת הגוף). As Rava taught: consecration, ḥametz on Pesach, and freeing a slave can all abrogate a lien. So konam overrides the husband's rights — but only after divorce.
Resolution The Sages reinforced the husband's lien so the konam doesn't take effect during marriage — but it activates upon divorce because konam has the inherent power to abrogate liens.

Scaling Down Duties: The Maidservant Ladder

👩 No maidservants — wife performs all seven tasks
↓
👩‍👧 1 maidservant — no grinding, baking, or washing
↓
👩‍👧‍👦 2 maidservants — also no cooking or nursing
↓
👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 3 maidservants — also no bed-making or spinning
↓
👑 4 maidservants — she sits on a katedra (chair) like a queen
Note The maidservants she 'brings' refers to servants she brought into the marriage as part of her dowry — not ones the husband acquired.
