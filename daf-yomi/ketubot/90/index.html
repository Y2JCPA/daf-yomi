Ketubot 90: Validity of Ketubot & Priority of Collection

Overview This daf addresses when marriage contracts written under unusual circumstances — by a minor or before conversion — remain valid, and whether they include the tosefet (additional sum). It then turns to the complex rules of priority among wives collecting from an estate and the institution of ketubot b'nin dichrin — the right of sons to inherit their mother's ketubah value.

Minor's / Convert's Ketubah: Is It Valid?

A minor writes a ketubah, or a convert had a ketubah before conversion
↓
He continues to live with her after coming of age / converting
↓
The ketubah is valid — on this condition he maintained her as his wife
↓
Rav Huna
She collects only the main sum (maneh/matayim) — the tosefet is not binding
Rav Yehuda
She collects even the tosefet (additional sum)
Halacha Rav Yehuda is conclusively refuted by a baraita: without a new tosefet after maturity/conversion, only the base amount is collected.

Does the Tosefet Apply to a Minor's Ketubah?

Rav Huna
The ketubah is valid only for the main sum (100 or 200 dinars). The tosefet written by a minor has no legal force.
Rav Yehuda
She collects even the tosefet — 'her ketubah is valid' means the entire document.
Gemara
A baraita states: if they introduced a new tosefet after maturity, she takes it. If not, she takes only the base sum.
Gemara
This is a conclusive refutation (תיובתא) of Rav Yehuda. 'Her ketubah is valid' refers only to the Sages' base ordinance.
Key Term Tosefet Ketubah (תוספת כתובה) — any sum the husband adds beyond the rabbinically mandated minimum of 100/200 dinars.

Ketubot B'nin Dichrin: Three Conclusions?

Initial Reading of Mishna

  • If one wife died in his lifetime and one after — sons of first wife still collect ketubot b'nin dichrin
  • One ketubah becomes surplus for the other (no need for extra dinar)
  • Ketubot b'nin dichrin cannot be collected from liened (sold) property

Rav Ashi's Objection

  • Perhaps when one wife died in his lifetime, no one collects ketubot b'nin dichrin at all
  • 'Precede' may only mean: second wife's heirs collect the debt first, then all sons share equally
  • Perhaps one ketubah does NOT become surplus — the mishna simply didn't mention the extra dinar
Context Ketubot B'nin Dichrin (כתובת בנין דכרין) — a stipulation that sons inherit their mother's ketubah value, ensuring a father-in-law's dowry stays in his family line.

Ben Nanas vs. Rabbi Akiva: One Wife Predeceases Husband

Ben Nanas
Sons of the first wife (who died in husband's lifetime) tell sons of second wife: 'You are children of a creditor — collect your mother's ketubah and leave!' We inherit the rest via ketubot b'nin dichrin.
Rabbi Akiva
When the husband died, the inheritance already passed to sons of the second wife. The Sages did not institute ketubot b'nin dichrin when one wife was still alive at death.
Gemara
Ben Nanas holds: even if one wife died in his lifetime, her sons collect ketubot b'nin dichrin.
Gemara
Rabbi Akiva holds: after second wife's debt is paid, all sons share equally — standard biblical inheritance applies.
Dispute The core question: Does ketubot b'nin dichrin apply when one wife survived the husband, or only when both wives predeceased him?
