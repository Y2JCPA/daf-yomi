Ketubot 90 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the conclusion of the Gemara, what does a wife collect from a ketubah written by her husband when he was a minor?
A. The full ketubah including tosefet
B. Only the base sum (maneh or matayim)
C. Nothing — a minor's document is void
D. Half the tosefet plus the base sum
Correct: B — Rav Huna's view is upheld: the ketubah is valid only for the Sages' base ordinance (100 or 200 dinars). Rav Yehuda, who said she collects even the tosefet, was conclusively refuted by a baraita.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the second wife's ketubah take priority over the heirs of the first wife (who predeceased the husband)?
A. Because the second ketubah is dated later
B. Because the second wife's ketubah is a debt, while the first wife's heirs claim through an inheritance stipulation
C. Because the Sages favored the surviving wife
D. Because the first wife's ketubah was automatically voided at her death
Correct: B — A living wife's ketubah is a direct debt (חוב) owed by the estate, which must be paid before inheritance stipulations like ketubot b'nin dichrin are applied.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the purpose of requiring that the estate exceed the total ketubot by at least one dinar before applying ketubot b'nin dichrin?
A. To ensure the widow receives a minimum payment
B. To cover court fees
C. To technically fulfill the biblical law of equal inheritance among sons
D. To prevent fraud in estate claims
Correct: C — The extra dinar ensures that biblical inheritance (equal shares among all sons) is technically fulfilled, since at least one dinar is distributed equally. Only then can the rabbinic institution of ketubot b'nin dichrin redistribute the rest.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In Rabbi Akiva's view, when the first wife dies in the husband's lifetime and the second wife survives him, what happens to the estate?
A. Sons of the first wife collect ketubot b'nin dichrin first
B. The second wife's sons inherit everything
C. After the second wife's ketubah debt is paid, all sons share equally
D. The estate is divided based on the dates of the ketubot
Correct: C — Rabbi Akiva holds that ketubot b'nin dichrin does not apply when one wife was alive at the husband's death. After the second wife's ketubah is paid as a debt, the remaining estate is divided equally among all sons via standard biblical inheritance.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What can we infer from the mishna's use of the word 'precedes' (קודמת) regarding two wives collecting their ketubot?
A. The second wife has no claim at all
B. If the second wife seizes property first, it may not be expropriated from her
C. The first wife must wait for the second wife to collect
D. Both wives collect equal shares simultaneously
Correct: B — The Gemara notes that the mishna says the first wife 'precedes' rather than saying the second wife has no right at all. This implies that if the second wife seized property first, it might not be taken from her — her rights are not completely nullified.
