Ketubot 110 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Rabbis, why didn't the lender declare his intent when selling the field to the borrower?
A. Because declarations are not legally binding
B. Because 'your friend has a friend' — word would reach the borrower and he'd refuse the sale
C. Because the lender forgot about the outstanding debt
D. Because declarations are only valid in the presence of a court
Correct: B — The Rabbis explain that if the lender publicized his intention to use the field as collateral, word would spread ('your friend has a friend, and your friend's friend has a friend') and the borrower would refuse the transaction entirely.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rav Sheshet mean by 'shifting donkey packs' (matrata)?
A. That debts should be collected using donkeys as transport
B. That mutual collection of equal debts is a futile exercise — each should keep what they have
C. That land assessments should be done by traveling assessors
D. That promissory notes should be physically exchanged between parties
Correct: B — Rav Sheshet argues that when two people owe each other the same amount, going through the process of each collecting from the other is pointless — like moving donkey packs from one side to the other for no net gain.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why can a husband NOT compel his wife to move from a town to a city?
A. Because towns have better agricultural land
B. Because city dwelling is difficult — as shown by those who 'willingly offered to dwell in Jerusalem' being blessed (Nehemiah 11:2)
C. Because the ketubah specifies the town of residence
D. Because a beit din must approve all relocations
Correct: B — R' Yosei bar Ḥanina derives from Nehemiah 11:2 that city life is difficult — the fact that people who volunteered to live in Jerusalem were blessed shows that urban dwelling was considered a hardship.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Gemara, what does 'all may force ascent to Eretz Yisrael' come to INCLUDE?
A. Non-Jewish servants only
B. Children under bar/bat mitzva age
C. Slaves — and even moving from a pleasant residence abroad to a noxious one in Eretz Yisrael
D. Elderly parents who cannot travel easily
Correct: C — The inclusive language 'all' comes to include slaves (who must ascend with their master's household) and the case of moving from a pleasant residence abroad to a noxious one in Eretz Yisrael — the mitzva of settling the Land overrides comfort.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What happens to a Canaanite slave who flees from abroad to Eretz Yisrael?
A. He is returned to his master immediately
B. He is freed automatically upon entering the Land
C. His master must sell him in Eretz Yisrael — he may not be taken back abroad
D. He must serve in the Beit HaMikdash
Correct: C — The Gemara rules that the master must sell the slave within Eretz Yisrael and may not take him back abroad, because the mitzva of settling Eretz Yisrael applies even to Canaanite slaves.
