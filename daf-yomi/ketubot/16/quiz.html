QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the principle of 'peh she'asar hu hapeh she'hitir'?
A. A person who makes a vow can also annul it
B. The mouth that created the legal problem is the mouth whose resolution we accept
C. A witness who testifies against someone must also testify in their favor
D. A rabbi who prohibits something must also provide the permissible alternative
Correct: B — The principle means: since this person's own admission created the legal claim against him (e.g., admitting the field belonged to someone's father), his further claim (that he purchased it) is believed — because without his initial statement, no one would have known.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rabban Gamliel concede that the bride needs hinnuma witnesses when the groom's claim is certain?
A. Because Rabban Gamliel only trusts women in cases involving Kohanim
B. Because his rule only applies when the groom's claim is uncertain — here both parties are certain
C. Because the bride has a financial incentive to lie about her status
D. Because Rabban Gamliel defers to Rabbi Yehoshua in all cases involving the ketubah amount
Correct: B — Rabban Gamliel's principle that 'she is believed' applies only when her claim is certain and his is uncertain. When the groom is also certain (he insists he married a widow), even Rabban Gamliel requires evidence.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Ravina, why can witnesses testifying about a hinnuma be valid even though most virgins' weddings generate publicity?
A. Because witnesses always override statistical majorities
B. Because the lack of publicity is only a rabbinic concern, not biblical
C. Because it's a 'rov' (majority), not a 'chazakah' (presumption) — so exceptions are possible
D. Because the witnesses are testifying about a different wedding entirely
Correct: C — Ravina distinguishes: if it were a chazakah (absolute presumption), contradictory witnesses would be automatically suspect. But since it's only a rov (majority), some virgin marriages lack publicity — so witnesses adding evidence of hinnuma are meaningful.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the practical difference between Rabbi Abbahu and Rav Pappa regarding collecting a ketubah via witnesses?
A. R' Abbahu requires two witnesses; Rav Pappa requires three
B. R' Abbahu says write a receipt to prevent double collection; Rav Pappa says it only applies where no ketubah document exists
C. R' Abbahu says she collects 200; Rav Pappa says she collects 100
D. R' Abbahu allows collection in any court; Rav Pappa restricts it to her husband's city
Correct: B — R' Abbahu's solution is universal: write a receipt so the husband can prove she already collected. Rav Pappa limits the Mishna to places where no ketubah document is written, eliminating the double-collection risk entirely.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does R' Yehoshua reject miggo in the case of the pregnant woman who claims the father is a Kohen?
A. Because she waited too long to make her claim
B. Because her pregnancy is visible — she cannot make the better claim that she never had intercourse
C. Because a Kohen's testimony is required to verify paternity
D. Because miggo only applies to monetary cases, not to personal status
Correct: B — The Gemara explains: 'her belly is between her teeth' — her pregnancy is conspicuous. She has no miggo because she cannot plausibly deny having had intercourse at all, so there is no 'better claim' she passed up.
