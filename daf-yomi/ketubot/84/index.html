Can One Stipulate Against Torah Law?

Gemara
Does Rav hold that stipulating counter to Torah law is valid?
Challenge
But Rav said regarding fraud: the seller must reimburse — one cannot abrogate 'You shall not wrong one another' (Lev. 25:17)!
Rav
The halakha follows Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel — if she died, he inherits — but not because of his reasoning.
Gemara
What does this mean? Multiple attempts to explain…
Resolution
RSBG holds husband's inheritance is Torah law. Rav holds it's rabbinic — but the Sages reinforced their laws with Torah-level severity.
Principle עשו חיזוק לדבריהם כשל תורה — The Sages reinforced their pronouncements with the severity of Torah law.

Is Husband's Inheritance Torah or Rabbinic Law?

Bekhorot 52b Challenge:
R' Yochanan ben Beroka says husband must return inherited land in Jubilee Year
If Torah law
Inheritance is never returned at Jubilee!
If Rabbinic law
Why does he get any compensation from her family?
↓
Rav's answer: The case involves a family graveyard (beit ha-kevarot)
↓
Due to פגם משפחה (family flaw), Sages ruled: take compensation, return the graveyard
↓
Resolution: Rav was explaining R' Yochanan ben Beroka's reasoning — not stating his own view
Key Concept פגם משפחה — Harm to family dignity if relatives must be buried in strangers' plots overrides normal property rules.

Mishna: Who Gets the Deceased's Deposits?

R' Tarfon

  • Give deposits/loans to the weakest party
  • Prioritizes mercy and need
  • The most vulnerable claimant receives first
  • Whoever seizes first — acquires

R' Akiva

  • אין מרחמין בדין — No mercy in judgment
  • Money goes to the heirs
  • All others (wife, creditor) require an oath to collect
  • Halakha follows R' Akiva
Halakha The law follows R' Akiva: deposits go to heirs because all other claimants need to swear an oath before collecting, and one cannot swear to collect from orphans' property.

When Is Seizure of a Debtor's Assets Effective?

ConditionRav & ShmuelR' Yochanan & Reish Lakish
Seized while debtor alive✅ Effective✅ Effective
Seized after debtor died❌ Belongs to heirs❌ Belongs to heirs
Produce in public domain (reshut ha-rabim)✅ First to grab acquires✅ First to grab acquires
Produce in an alley (simta)❌ Heirs acquire automatically✅ First to grab still acquires
According to R' TarfonWeakest party takes itWeakest party takes it
According to R' AkivaHeirs take — no mercy in lawHeirs take — no mercy in law
Why the Alley Matters A simta (alley) is a valid location for acquisition — so items there are automatically acquired by heirs. The public domain is not, so items there are ownerless and available to any claimant.

R' Yochanan vs. Reish Lakish: Reversing the Judges

Background
Judges ruled like R' Tarfon — giving money to the weakest party.
Reish Lakish
I reverse their ruling! The money goes to the heirs per R' Akiva.
R' Yochanan
You treated this like a Torah-level error! That's too strict.
Gemara
Three possible explanations of their dispute:
Option 1
R' Yochanan: Halakha follows R' Akiva vs. colleague only, not vs. teacher. R' Tarfon was his teacher.
Option 2
Both agree re: colleague only — they disagree whether R' Tarfon was Akiva's teacher or colleague.
Option 3
R' Yochanan: The rule favoring R' Akiva is only an inclination, not a definitive halakha — so we don't reverse the judges.
Practical Case R' Yochanan's own relatives seized an orphan's cow. He approved it per R' Tarfon, but when Reish Lakish reversed it, R' Yochanan conceded: 'One whose stature equals mine disagrees — what can I do?'
