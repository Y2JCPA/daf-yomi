Why Not Marry on Sunday Night?

Rav Zeira
A virgin cannot marry at the conclusion of Shabbat because one might slaughter a young fowl on Shabbat for the wedding feast.
Abaye
If so, Yom Kippur on Monday should also be moved — one might slaughter on Shabbat for the Yom Kippur eve meal!
Gemara
Preparing a meal for oneself (Yom Kippur eve) — one is not preoccupied and won't forget it's Shabbat.
Gemara
Preparing a meal for others (wedding feast) — one is preoccupied and may violate Shabbat.
Gemara
Alternatively: For Yom Kippur eve there is time the next day to slaughter. For a wedding feast at night, there is no interval.
KEY DISTINCTION Preoccupation (tarud) and lack of available time together explain why the decree applies only to weddings, not to Yom Kippur eve.

Virgin vs. Widow: Wedding & Intercourse Timing

Virgin (Betulah)

  • Married on Wednesday
  • Intercourse on Thursday (Day 5)
  • Aligned with the blessing to the fish: "Be fruitful and multiply"
  • Bar Kappara's teaching based on Creation

Widow (Almanah)

  • Married on Thursday
  • Intercourse on Friday (Day 6)
  • Aligned with the blessing to man on the sixth day of Creation
  • Also ensures three days of rejoicing: Thu, Fri, Shabbat
BAR KAPPARA The blessings of Creation determine the ideal timing — the fish were blessed on Day 5, mankind on Day 6.

The Sages' Assiduousness for a Widow's Welfare

📅 Thursday: The widow is married — celebration begins
↓
💑 Friday: Intercourse takes place — intimacy and connection
↓
🕯️ Shabbat: The couple rejoices together — a full day of rest
↓
⚠️ Why not Thursday night? The groom would go to work Friday morning and leave her alone
↓
💡 Result: Three consecutive days of togetherness ensured
PRACTICAL DIFFERENCES For an idle person (no job) or when a Festival falls on Friday, the assiduousness reason doesn't apply — but the blessing reason still does.

The Fingers and the Ears

מפני מה אצבעותיו של אדם דומות ליתדות
AGGADAH R' Elazar: Why are fingers pointed like pegs? So that if one hears an inappropriate matter, one can place them in the ears to block it.

School of R' Yishmael: Why is the ear hard but the earlobe soft? So one can fold it over to seal the ear from improper speech.

Each finger has a sacred function:
• Pinky — measuring a zeret (span) for the priestly breastplate
• Ring finger — taking a fistful (kemitzah) of the meal-offering
• Middle finger — measuring an amah (cubit)
• Index finger — sprinkling sacrificial blood
• Thumb — purification ritual of the metzora (leper)
