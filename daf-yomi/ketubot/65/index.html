Ketubot 65 — Wine, Clothing & Marital Obligations

אין פוסקין יין לאשה
CORE THEME This daf explores the boundaries of a husband's obligations to his wife — from wine and clothing allotments to Shabbat meals and intimacy — calibrated to her prior lifestyle and his means. Dramatic narratives involving Ḥoma (Abaye's widow) and Rava's wife illustrate the real-world stakes of these rulings.

Are Women Allotted Wine?

Initial Position
Wines are not allotted to a wife as part of her sustenance.
Objection
But Hosea 2:7 mentions 'my drink' (shikkuyai) — implying women receive wine!
Resolution
Shikkuyai doesn't mean wine — it refers to things a woman desires (mishtokeket): jewelry and ornaments.
R' Yehuda of Neviraya
Proof from I Samuel 1:9 — 'He had drunk' (masculine), not 'she had drunk.' Hannah ate but did not drink wine.
Gemara Challenge
By that logic, 'she had eaten' (feminine) should mean only she ate, not him!
Resolution
The verse changed its language mid-sentence — that deliberate switch teaches he drank, but she did not.
KEY PRINCIPLE The default rule: wine is not part of a wife's mandatory sustenance allotment.

Wine Allotment: Accustomed vs. Not

Is the woman accustomed to drinking wine?
YES — Accustomed
With husband: 2 cups
Without husband: 1 cup
NO — Not Accustomed
With husband: 1 cup
Without husband: No wine at all
↓
Alternative view (Abaye): Even if accustomed, wine may be given only for tzikei (meat pudding), not for drinking.
SHMUEL via RAV ḤINNANA Wine allocation depends on both the woman's prior habits and her husband's presence.

Effects of Wine on Women

CupsEffect
1 cupGood for a woman — no concern
2 cupsA disgrace — beginning of drunkenness
3 cupsShe becomes lustful and verbally requests relations — unseemly

Clothing & Leftover Garments

Husband's Obligation

  • 50 dinars of clothing per year
  • Abaye: These are simple dinars (⅛ of Tyrian)
  • Proof: A poor man couldn't afford 50 full dinars
  • New clothes in winter, not summer
  • Worn clothes in summer, not winter
  • Shoes 3× per year (on Festivals)

Leftover Worn Clothes

  • Leftover sustenance → belongs to husband
  • Leftover clothes → belongs to wife
  • Raḥava: She wears them during niddah so she doesn't become repulsive
  • Abaye: A widow's leftover clothes go to the heirs
  • Reason: No need to preserve attractiveness for a dead husband
ABAYE'S LOGIC Worn clothes serve to protect the marital relationship — once the husband dies, that rationale disappears.
