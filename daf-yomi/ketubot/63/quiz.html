Ketubot 63 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
How many years total did Rabbi Akiva spend studying away from home?
A. 12 years
B. 18 years
C. 24 years
D. 6 years
Correct: C — Rabbi Akiva studied 12 years, then overheard his wife saying she wished he'd study another 12, so he returned for a total of 24 years.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the Gemara's conclusion, what does EVERYONE agree constitutes rebellion (moredet)?
A. Refusing household tasks
B. Refusing marital relations
C. Refusing to cook
D. Leaving the home
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that rebellion against marital relations is universally agreed to be rebellion. The dispute between Rav Huna and R' Yosei b. R' Ḥanina is only about whether refusal of tasks ALSO qualifies.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What did the later enactment (חכמים חזרו ונמנו) change about the moredet procedure?
A. Eliminated all penalties
B. Replaced weekly deductions with public announcements over 4 Shabbatot
C. Doubled the weekly deduction amount
D. Required the husband to divorce immediately
Correct: B — Instead of gradual weekly deductions, the later enactment instituted public announcements in synagogues and study halls for 4 consecutive Shabbatot, after which the wife loses her entire ketubah if she persists.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Ameimar distinguish between 'I want him but refuse' and 'he disgusts me' (ma'is alai)?
A. Only the first case requires a get
B. The first is rebellion warranting penalties; the second means she cannot be compelled to stay
C. The second case requires a longer waiting period
D. There is no practical difference — both are treated the same
Correct: B — Ameimar holds that moredet penalties apply when she wants to remain married but refuses relations to cause anguish. But if she genuinely finds him repulsive (ma'is alai), she should not be compelled to remain with someone who disgusts her.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What was Rav Gamda's objection to the ruling about a moredet's worn clothes?
A. He argued the clothes belonged to her father
B. He said Rava had raised this question and never resolved it, so they shouldn't rule definitively
C. He claimed worn clothes have no monetary value
D. He said only a Sanhedrin could decide this issue
Correct: B — Rav Gamda challenged that Rava himself raised a dilemma about whether a moredet loses her worn clothes and never resolved it — suggesting the other Sages were ruling based on favoritism toward Rav Zevid rather than established halakha.
