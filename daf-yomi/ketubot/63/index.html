Ketubot 63: Devotion, Rebellion & Marital Obligations

מורדת
DAF OVERVIEW Ketubot 63 transitions from powerful aggadic narratives — Rabbi Akiva's wife's extraordinary sacrifice and Ben Kalba Savua's reconciliation — into the halakhic framework of the moredet (rebellious wife). The Mishna establishes weekly financial penalties, while the Gemara debates the nature of rebellion and evolves the procedure from gradual deductions to public announcements.

Two Father-Son Torah Conflicts

Rabbi Akiva's Daughter & Ben Azzai

  • Daughter followed her mother's path
  • Betrothed Ben Azzai (a simple man)
  • Sent him off to study Torah
  • Folk saying: 'The ewe follows the ewe'

Rav Yosef b. Rava & Rav Yosef

  • Agreed to study 6 years at the study hall
  • After 3 years, wanted to visit wife on Erev Yom Kippur
  • Father came with a weapon to confront him
  • Neither father nor son ate the cessation meal
CONTRAST The Daf juxtaposes women who encourage prolonged Torah study with a father who demands it — showing different motivations behind devotion to learning.

What Constitutes 'Rebellion'?

Rav Huna
She rebels against marital relations (תשמיש).
R' Yosei b. R' Ḥanina
She rebels against performing household tasks (מלאכה).
Gemara
The Mishna says a husband can also rebel — can a husband rebel against tasks? He doesn't perform tasks!
R' Yosei b. R' Ḥanina
Yes — a husband who says 'I will not sustain or support her' is rebelling against his obligations.
Gemara
But Rav says such a husband must immediately divorce and pay the ketubah!
Answer
The weekly fine applies while the court consults with him to see if he'll retract.
RESOLUTION The Gemara concludes: rebellion against marital relations is universally agreed to be rebellion. The dispute is only about whether refusal of tasks also qualifies.

Later Enactment: The Moredet Procedure

Woman declares rebellion against her husband
↓
Court sends messengers before announcements
↓
Public announcements in synagogues & study halls for 4 consecutive Shabbatot
↓
She retracts
Keeps her ketubah
She persists
Loses entire ketubah — even if worth 10,000 dinars
↓
Court sends messengers after announcements (Rami bar Ḥama)
RAVA vs. RAV NAḤMAN Rav Naḥman bar Rav Ḥisda: Halakha follows this later enactment (immediate total loss after announcements).
Rava: This is absurd (burkha)! Halakha follows gradual weekly deductions while consulting her.
