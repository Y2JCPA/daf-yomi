Credibility, Presumption & the Burden of Proof

Rabban Gamliel
A woman is credible about her own status — whether about her virginity or the lineage of a man she was with.
R' Yehoshua
We do not rely on her word alone. She retains a negative presumption until she brings proof.
R' Yochanan
In the mukat etz case, the dispute is 200 vs 100 dinars — following R' Meir's view.
R' Elazar
The dispute is 100 vs nothing — following the Rabbis' view that mukat etz always gets 100.
Core Tension When a woman's claim conflicts with presumption, does she have inherent credibility (Rabban Gamliel) or must she overcome the status quo with proof (R' Yehoshua)?

Mukat Etz: Who Is Credible?

Bride
My hymen was ruptured by wood (mukat etz), not by intercourse.
Groom
No — you were trampled by a man (derusat ish).
Rabban Gamliel & R' Eliezer
She is deemed credible — she certainly knows what happened to her.
R' Yehoshua
We do not conduct our lives based on her mouth. She has the presumptive status of derusat ish until she brings proof.
Mishna This is the second dispute in the series where Rabban Gamliel trusts the woman's claim and R' Yehoshua demands proof.

What Are the Financial Stakes?

R' Yochanan (follows R' Meir)

  • She claims: mukat etz → still a virgin → 200 dinars
  • He claims: intercourse → non-virgin → 100 dinars
  • R' Meir: mukat etz who married unknowingly still gets 200
  • Dispute range: 200 vs 100

R' Elazar (follows the Rabbis)

  • She claims: mukat etz → not full deception → 100 dinars
  • He claims: intercourse → mistaken transaction → nothing
  • Rabbis: mukat etz receives 100 whether known or unknown
  • Dispute range: 100 vs 0
Key Difference R' Yochanan holds a non-virgin still gets 100 dinars, so under R' Elazar's reading both claims would yield the same 100 — making the dispute meaningless.

Seclusion in a Ruin: Is She Credible?

Mishna
A woman was seen entering seclusion or a ruin with a man. She says: He is a priest, my father's brother's son.
Rabban Gamliel & R' Eliezer
She is deemed credible to marry a priest.
R' Yehoshua
She has the status of one who had relations with a Gibeonite or mamzer until she brings proof.
Ze'eiri
'Speaking' means seclusion — two cases needed: seclusion (lesser concern) and ruin (greater concern).
Rav Asi
'Speaking' means intercourse — one case: seclusion within a ruin. Or: ruin in city vs ruin in field.
Dispute Ze'eiri and Rav Asi disagree about whether 'speaking' (נשאת ונתנה) refers to mere seclusion or actual intercourse.
