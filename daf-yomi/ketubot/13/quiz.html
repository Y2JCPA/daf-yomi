Ketubot 13 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
In the mukat etz mishna, what does R' Yehoshua hold about the woman's claim?
A. She is credible because she knows what happened
B. She retains the presumption of derusat ish until she brings proof
C. She is automatically believed if she swears an oath
D. She is believed only if her father testifies on her behalf
Correct: B — R' Yehoshua consistently holds that the woman's own testimony is insufficient to overcome a negative presumption — she must bring independent proof.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Yochanan, what is the financial dispute in the mukat etz case?
A. 100 dinars vs nothing
B. 200 dinars vs nothing
C. 200 dinars vs 100 dinars
D. 400 dinars vs 200 dinars
Correct: C — R' Yochanan follows R' Meir, who holds that a mukat etz has the status of a virgin for ketubah purposes (200 dinars). The groom claims she had intercourse and is entitled to only 100.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does R' Yochanan reject R' Elazar's explanation of the mishna?
A. Because R' Elazar follows a minority opinion
B. Because under R' Elazar's reading, both claims yield the same amount (100 dinars), leaving no dispute
C. Because R' Elazar's view contradicts Rabban Gamliel
D. Because R' Elazar does not account for the groom's claim
Correct: B — R' Yochanan holds that a non-virgin still receives 100 dinars. Under R' Elazar's reading (Rabbis' view), both mukat etz and intercourse yield 100 — so there would be no meaningful financial dispute.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does R' Yehoshua mean by 'her belly is between her teeth'?
A. She is ill and cannot testify
B. She is pregnant, so she cannot deny having had intercourse
C. She is hiding evidence of her claim
D. She swallowed something forbidden
Correct: B — R' Yehoshua argues that the woman's pregnancy is visible, so she cannot claim she never had intercourse — just as a captive woman has witnesses to her captivity, this woman's body testifies against a claim of innocence.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the halakhic status of the child when a woman's testimony about the father's identity is not accepted?
A. Mamzer
B. Kohen
C. Shetuki — father unknown, treated with mamzer stringencies
D. The child follows the mother's status entirely
Correct: C — Even Rabban Gamliel agrees that while a woman may be believed about her own fitness, she is NOT believed regarding the identity of her child's father. The child is classified as a shetuki (שתוקי) — identity of father unknown.
