Ketubot 25 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say 'Great is chazakah' is novel regarding priests returning from Babylon?
A. Because they were elevated from rabbinic teruma to Torah-level teruma based on presumptive status
B. Because they had no documents proving their lineage
C. Because the Sanhedrin had been dissolved
D. Because DNA testing was not available
Correct: A — The novelty is that priests who ate only rabbinic teruma in Babylon were permitted to eat Torah-level teruma in Eretz Yisrael based on their existing chazakah — an elevation in the level of obligation.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In R' Ami's incident, what convinced him that the man was truly a priest and not just a prominent scholar?
A. The man showed his priestly genealogical documents
B. A Levite read the Torah after him, confirming the first reading was priestly
C. Two witnesses testified they saw him receive teruma
D. He performed Birkat Kohanim in the synagogue
Correct: B — The witness confirmed that a Levite read after the man. Since a Levite only reads second when a priest reads first, this proved the man was called as a priest, not merely as a great man.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Reish Lakish glare at R' Elazar?
A. R' Elazar disagreed with him publicly
B. R' Elazar had quoted R' Yochanan's ruling without citing the source
C. R' Elazar sided with the Sadducees
D. R' Elazar refused to accept his authority
Correct: B — When R' Yochanan later said the exact same words R' Elazar had used — 'If there's no threshing floor, does priesthood cease?' — Reish Lakish realized R' Elazar had been quoting R' Yochanan without attribution. Had he cited the source, Reish Lakish would have accepted it immediately.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Yehuda HaNasi, why is a father believed to say his son is a priest for teruma but NOT for marriage?
A. Marriage requires two witnesses, teruma requires only one
B. Feeding his son teruma is within his purview (b'yado), but marrying a woman to him is not
C. Teruma is only rabbinic while marriage is Torah-level
D. The father might be biased about marriage prospects
Correct: B — R' Yehuda HaNasi's principle is that a person is believed regarding matters within their own power (b'yado). A father can directly feed his son teruma, but cannot unilaterally contract a marriage for his son — so his credibility differs accordingly.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the geographic location matter for establishing priestly chazakah?
A. Different locations had different Torah scrolls
B. The presence of permanent courts and Torah-level obligations varied by location
C. Priests were forbidden from traveling between regions
D. Each region had its own High Priest
Correct: B — In Eretz Yisrael, where Torah-level obligations applied and permanent courts investigated lineage, chazakah was stronger. In Syria and Babylonia, where some obligations were only rabbinic and court oversight varied, the standards for establishing priestly status differed.
