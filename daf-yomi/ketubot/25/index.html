How Is Priestly Status Established?

Core Question: What actions create a chazakah (presumptive status) of priesthood?
↓
For Teruma
Lifting hands, challah, priestly gifts
For Lineage
Disputed — does chazakah elevate to full priestly lineage?
↓
Multiple baraitot examined — each time the Gemara asks if chazakah establishes lineage, the answer deflects to a lesser status
↓
Practical rulings: Torah reading order + witness testimony can establish priestly status
Key Concept The Gemara systematically tests whether presumptive status (chazakah) for priesthood — established through actions like Birkat Kohanim or receiving teruma — is strong enough to establish full priestly lineage (yichus).

What Does 'Great Is Chazakah' Mean?

Gemara
The priests maintained their practice from Babylon to Eretz Yisrael. What's novel about saying chazakah is great?
Answer 1
In Babylon they ate rabbinic teruma. Now in Eretz Yisrael they eat Torah-level teruma based on that same chazakah — an elevation in status!
Answer 2
Even in Eretz Yisrael they only eat rabbinic teruma. The novelty: we don't decree against rabbinic teruma out of concern for Torah-level teruma.
Gemara
But the verse in Ezra implies they did eat Torah-level teruma — only kodesh hakodashim (offerings) were forbidden!
Reinterpretation
Read the verse differently: they ate neither kodesh (= teruma) nor kodashim (= offerings). Both were forbidden.
Ezra 2:63 The verse "that they should not eat of kodesh hakodashim" is reinterpreted as two separate prohibitions: kodesh (teruma) AND kodashim (sacrificial portions).

Torah vs. Rabbinic: Teruma and Challah

After the return from Babylon, not all Jews ascended to Eretz Yisrael
↓
Key Dispute: Are teruma and challah obligations Torah-level or rabbinic today?
↓
View A (First Baraita)
Challah = Rabbinic
Teruma = Torah law
We elevate from challah → teruma
View B (Second Baraita)
Teruma = Rabbinic
Challah = Torah law
We elevate from teruma → challah
↓
Each baraita is deflected: chazakah elevates only to the next level, not to full lineage
Rav Huna b. R' Yehoshua His opinion — that challah today is only a rabbinic obligation — is used to explain why the first baraita teaches elevation from challah to teruma, not to lineage.

Establishing Priesthood via Torah Reading: Three Incidents

R' Ami

  • A witness says: 'He is a priest'
  • R' Ami asks: Did he read first because he's a priest or a great man?
  • Answer: A Levite read after him — proving he was called as a priest
  • ✅ R' Ami elevated him to priesthood

R' Yehoshua b. Levi

  • A witness says: 'He is a Levite'
  • R' Yehoshua asks: Did he read second as a Levite or a great man?
  • Answer: A priest read before him — confirming Levite status
  • ✅ R' Yehoshua elevated him to Levite status

Reish Lakish

  • A witness says: 'He read first — he's a priest'
  • Reish Lakish demands: Did he receive teruma at the threshing floor?
  • R' Elazar objects: If there's no threshing floor, does priesthood cease?
  • ⚡ R' Yochanan later says the same — Reish Lakish glares at R' Elazar
The Glare Reish Lakish looked harshly at R' Elazar because he realized R' Elazar had quoted R' Yochanan's ruling without attribution. Had he cited the source, Reish Lakish would have accepted it immediately.

Reish Lakish vs. R' Yochanan: What Proves Priesthood?

Reish Lakish
Only receiving teruma at the threshing floor establishes priestly status. Torah reading is insufficient!
R' Elazar
And if there is no threshing floor there, does the priesthood cease to exist?
R' Yochanan
(Later, same ruling:) And if there is no threshing floor there, does the priesthood cease to exist?
Reish Lakish
(Turns to glare at R' Elazar:) You heard this from bar Nappaḥa and didn't tell me it was his ruling? I would have accepted it!
Bar Nappaḥa 'Son of a blacksmith' — an epithet for R' Yochanan. Reish Lakish's deference to his teacher shows the weight of received tradition over independent reasoning.
