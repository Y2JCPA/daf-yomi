May One Consummate Marriage on Shabbat?

Rav
The halakha follows Rabbi Yehuda: unintentional acts are prohibited on Shabbat
Shmuel
The halakha follows Rabbi Shimon: unintentional acts are permitted on Shabbat
Gemara
Each locale attributes the lenient ruling to its own authority — these are lenient with regard to themselves
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
Your mnemonic: each community claims the permissive view for their local authority
Core Question Does consummating a marriage on Shabbat violate the melakha of wounding (ḥovel) or squeezing blood (mefarek)?

Two Views on the Blood of Virginity

Version 1: Blood Is Pooled

  • Blood is already collected behind the hymen
  • Breaking the membrane releases stored blood
  • He is destructive vis-à-vis the opening
  • Even R' Yehuda permits — destructive act

Version 2: Blood Flows from Vessels

  • Blood flows from blood vessels attached to the body
  • Rupturing causes an active wound
  • He is destructive in causing the wound
  • R' Yehuda concedes — destructive wounding is permitted
Key Principle Under both versions, Rav can permit intercourse on Shabbat because the act is destructive (מקלקל), not constructive — and even R' Yehuda permits destructive unintentional acts.

The Barrel Stopper Challenge to Rav

Gemara
Rav said inserting a cloth stopper (nazyata) tightly into a barrel is forbidden on Yom Tov because liquid will be squeezed out
Gemara
This proves Rav forbids even unintentional squeezing — so how can he permit intercourse?
Answer
The barrel stopper is a psik reisha — liquid will inevitably be squeezed. Even R' Shimon forbids this!
Answer
Intercourse is different: the wound is destructive (מקלקל), which R' Yehuda permits
Resolution Rav follows R' Yehuda but still permits consummation because a destructive wound is not the type of melakha R' Yehuda prohibits.

Groom's Exemption from Shema — What Does It Prove?

Rav Yosef
A groom is exempt from Shema even on Shabbat night if he hasn't consummated — proof he may consummate on Shabbat!
Abaye
No — he's exempt because he's preoccupied that he hasn't yet fulfilled the mitzva, not because he may do it on Shabbat
Rava
If preoccupation exempts from Shema, would one whose ship sank also be exempt? A mourner must still recite Shema!
Rava
The groom is exempt because he is preoccupied with a future mitzva — not past distress. This is a tannaitic dispute
Distinction Preoccupation with a past event (mourning, shipwreck) does not exempt from Shema. Only preoccupation with a future mitzva still to be performed exempts.

📋 Ketubot 6 — Summary Map

Core Issue: Consummation causes a wound — is this a Shabbat violation?
↓
R' Yehuda: Unintentional acts are forbidden
But destructive acts (מקלקל) are permitted → consummation allowed
R' Shimon: Unintentional acts are permitted
Unless psik reisha — but experts can avoid it → consummation allowed
↓
Result: Under both views, consummation on Shabbat can be permitted — Rav and Shmuel each find a path to leniency
