QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Rav permit intercourse with a virgin on Shabbat despite holding like R' Yehuda that unintentional acts are forbidden?
A. Because the wound is destructive (mekalkel), and R' Yehuda permits destructive unintentional acts
B. Because he holds it is a mitzva that overrides Shabbat
C. Because the bleeding is only a rabbinic prohibition
D. Because he disagrees with R' Yehuda in this specific case
Correct: A — The Gemara explains that under both versions of how the blood works, the act is destructive (mekalkel) — either destructive vis-à-vis the opening or in causing the wound — and even R' Yehuda concedes that destructive unintentional acts are permitted.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the principle of 'psik reisha' (cut off its head and will it not die)?
A. An act is forbidden if its forbidden consequence is certain to occur, even without intent
B. One must always intend the consequences of one's actions on Shabbat
C. Destructive acts are always permitted on Shabbat
D. Only actions that cause visible bleeding are forbidden on Shabbat
Correct: A — Abaye and Rava both agree that even R' Shimon, who permits unintentional acts, concedes that when the forbidden result is an inevitable consequence of the action (psik reisha), it is forbidden.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak, why did each locale attribute the lenient ruling on Shabbat consummation to its own authority?
A. Each community wanted to follow the lenient opinion and so attributed it to their binding local authority
B. There was a scribal error in the transmission of the rulings
C. Rav and Shmuel changed their minds over time
D. The students were confused about who said what
Correct: A — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak's mnemonic is 'these are lenient with regard to themselves' — each locale attributed the lenient ruling to the sage whose authority was binding locally, so they could follow the permissive view.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Rava reject the idea that the groom is exempt from Shema due to general preoccupation?
A. Because a mourner, who is clearly preoccupied, is still obligated in Shema — so general preoccupation doesn't create an exemption
B. Because the groom is happy, not preoccupied
C. Because Shema can never be exempted for any reason
D. Because only Torah study creates an exemption from Shema
Correct: A — Rava argues that if general preoccupation exempted from Shema, someone whose ship sank would also be exempt. Since a mourner must still recite Shema, the groom's exemption must be specifically because he is preoccupied with a future mitzva, not mere distress.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rabba explain that R' Shimon can permit consummation on Shabbat despite the principle of psik reisha?
A. Some men are expert in diverting and can avoid rupturing the hymen, so bleeding is not inevitable
B. R' Shimon holds that psik reisha is always permitted
C. The bleeding from consummation is too minimal to be considered a melakha
D. R' Shimon considers consummation a mitzva that overrides Shabbat
Correct: A — Rabba explains that unlike Babylonians, some men are experts in diverting during intercourse and can avoid rupturing the hymen. Since the bleeding is not an inevitable consequence for them, it is not a psik reisha, and R' Shimon permits unintentional acts.
