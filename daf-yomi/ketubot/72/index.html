Why Can't He Forbid the House of Mourning?

Gemara
Forbidding a house of feasting locks the door on her joy. But what about a house of mourning?
Tanna
In the future she too will die, and no one will eulogize her — because she never eulogized others.
R' Meir
As Kohelet says: 'Better to go to a house of mourning' — the living will take it to heart. Reciprocity in death rites is essential.
R' Meir
He who eulogizes — will be eulogized. He who buries — will be buried. He who escorts — will be escorted.
Principle Preventing a wife from comforting mourners isolates her from the community and guarantees she will be neglected in death.

Dat Moshe vs. Dat Yehudit

דת משה — Precepts of Moses

  • Feeds him untithed food (טבל)
  • Has relations while a niddah
  • Does not separate challah
  • Vows and does not fulfill

דת יהודית — Jewish Custom

  • Goes out with uncovered hair
  • Spins wool in public marketplace
  • Speaks / flirts with every man
  • Abba Shaul: curses his parents before him
  • R' Tarfon: a 'loud woman'
Result Both categories cause her to be divorced without payment of her ketubah, but Dat Moshe violations are halakhic prohibitions while Dat Yehudit violations are breaches of accepted communal norms.

Head Covering: How Much Is Required?

Woman covers head with a basket (קלתא)
↓
By Torah Law (Dat Moshe)
✅ Basket is sufficient — no violation
By Custom (Dat Yehudit)
❌ Basket is not enough — requires a substantial covering
↓
R' Zeira asks: Where is this woman?
↓
In the Marketplace
Basket alone = violation of Dat Yehudit
In Her Own Courtyard
If we require full covering here — no daughter of Avraham could remain married!
Alleyway / Between Courtyards (Abaye)
✅ This is the setting R' Yochanan meant — semi-public spaces
R' Yochanan via R' Asi A basket covering suffices by Torah law but not by Jewish custom. Abaye resolves: R' Yochanan refers to transitional spaces — alleyways between courtyards.

Defining the 'Loud Woman' (קולנית)

R' Tarfon
A loud woman (קולנית) also violates Dat Yehudit — when she speaks in her house and neighbors hear her voice.
Shmuel
She raises her voice about matters relating to intercourse — quarreling loudly enough for neighbors to overhear.
Baraita
She screams during relations in one courtyard and is heard in the next courtyard.
Gemara
If the baraita refers to physical pain, it should be listed with blemishes, not conduct violations. Therefore Shmuel's explanation stands — it's immodest behavior.
Resolution A 'loud woman' is defined by immodest vocal conduct regarding intimate matters, not a physical condition — which is why it appears in the Dat Yehudit list rather than the blemishes list.
