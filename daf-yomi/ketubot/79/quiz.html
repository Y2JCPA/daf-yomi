Ketubot 79 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Rav Naḥman tear the document in the case of the mother who gave property to her daughter?
A. It was a shtar mabrachat — a document of evasion, not a genuine gift
B. The document had a technical scribal error
C. The daughter was a minor and could not legally receive property
D. The husband had already acquired the property before the transfer
Correct: A — Rav Naḥman determined it was a shtar mabrachat — the mother transferred property only to keep it from her husband, not as a wholehearted gift. Shmuel had ruled that such documents should be torn.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Zeira, when is a document of evasion clearly void?
A. When the document is not witnessed properly
B. When it transfers ALL of the woman's property
C. When it transfers only part of the property
D. When the recipient is a family member
Correct: B — Rabbi Zeira distinguishes: a person does not genuinely give away ALL their property, so such a document is obviously an evasion and void. A document for only part of the property may be a genuine gift.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the double payment for a stolen usufruct animal go to the wife rather than the husband?
A. Because the animal was stolen from her field
B. Because the husband forfeited his rights through negligence
C. Because the double payment is 'produce of produce,' which the Sages did not grant to the husband
D. Because the animal dies and the husband loses all rights
Correct: C — The Sages instituted that the husband consumes produce (פירות), but NOT produce of produce (פירי פירות). The double payment is a gain resulting from the theft of the offspring — making it produce of produce, which belongs to the wife.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Rabbis, why does the child of a usufruct maidservant belong to the wife while an animal's offspring belongs to the husband?
A. Maidservants have higher monetary value than animals
B. If the maidservant dies, the wife loses everything — but an animal leaves its hide
C. Children of maidservants are considered family assets
D. The Torah explicitly assigns maidservant children to the wife
Correct: B — The Rabbis are concerned that if the mother dies, the wife loses the principal entirely. With an animal, the hide remains even after death, so the principal isn't fully lost. With a maidservant, nothing remains, so the child is given to the wife as protection.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the practical difference between Rabbi Shimon and the Rabbis regarding produce attached to the ground?
A. Rabbi Shimon requires the husband to buy land with the produce
B. At the time of divorce, the Rabbis say attached produce is his; Rabbi Shimon says it is hers
C. Rabbi Shimon does not allow the husband to eat any produce
D. The Rabbis say attached produce must be evaluated; Rabbi Shimon disagrees
Correct: B — Rava explains that the key difference is produce attached at the time of departure from the marriage. The Rabbis hold it belongs to the husband, while Rabbi Shimon — applying his principle that superior rights at entry mean inferior rights at exit — holds it belongs to the wife.
