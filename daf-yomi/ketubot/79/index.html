Attached Produce

Usufruct Animals

Core Principle The husband consumes produce (פירות) from his wife's property, but the principal (קרן) remains hers — the challenge is defining which is which.

The Torn Document: Shtar Mabrachat

Rav Anan
Look at Naḥman the farmer — he tears people's documents!
Mar Ukva
Tell me — what actually happened?
Mar Ukva
A shtar mabrachat (document of evasion)? Shmuel said: If one comes to my hand, I tear it.
Rava
But she gave it to her daughter — a mother gives wholeheartedly to a daughter!
Rav Naḥman
Even so — where her interests clash with her daughter's, her own interests come first.
Key Concept Shtar Mabrachat (שטר מברחת) — a document transferring property solely to evade the husband's rights, not as a genuine gift. Such documents are void.

How to Distance Property from a Husband

Wife wants to protect property from husband's control
↓
R' Shimon b. Gamliel
Write a document giving property to another person who agrees not to acquire it
Rabbis
Recipient could deceive her and keep the property!
↓
Solution: Write "from today, while I still wish to give it" — she can revoke at any time
↓
R' Zeira's distinction: Document covering all property = obvious evasion (void). Only part = may be valid.
Abaye Property given away to evade is treated like property unknown to the husband (נכסים שאינן ידועין) — per R' Shimon, her sale is valid.

Inherited Produce: Attached vs. Detached

Rabbi Meir

  • Evaluate land with produce vs. without
  • The surplus value belongs to the wife
  • Buy land with the surplus
  • Husband eats produce of that land

The Rabbis

  • Attached produce = his (husband's right to פירות)
  • Detached produce = hers (like money)
  • Buy land with detached produce
  • Husband eats produce of that land

Rabbi Shimon

  • Superior at entry → inferior at exit
  • Attached: his at marriage, hers at divorce
  • Detached: hers at marriage, his at divorce
  • Balances rights across the marriage
Rava The practical difference between R' Shimon and the Rabbis: produce attached at the time of divorce — Rabbis say his, R' Shimon says hers.

Usufruct Offspring: Who Owns What?

CategoryRabbisḤananya
Offspring of usufruct animalBelongs to husbandBelongs to husband
Child of usufruct maidservantBelongs to wifeBelongs to husband
Reason for maidservant rulingConcern: if mother dies, wife loses everythingSame as animal — offspring is produce
Why animal is different (Rabbis)Animal's hide remains — principal not fully lostNo distinction needed
Double payment for stolen offspringGoes to wife — produce of produceGoes to wife — produce of produce
Halakha—Shmuel: Halakha follows Ḥananya
Key Distinction The Sages gave the husband rights to produce (פירות) but NOT to produce of produce (פירי פירות) — so double payment for theft goes to the wife.

Rav Naḥman: Consuming the Principal

🐐 Wife brings a goat for its milk — husband drinks milk until goat dies
↓
🐑 Wife brings a sheep for its shearings — husband takes wool until sheep dies
↓
🐔 Wife brings a hen for its eggs — husband takes eggs until hen dies
↓
🌴 Wife brings a palm tree for its dates — husband eats dates until tree dies
↓
🧥 Wife brings a cloak as usufruct property — husband wears it until it's worn out
Rabbis vs. R' Meir Salt mines and sulfur quarries: R' Meir says extractions are principal (finite resource). Rabbis say extractions are produce, the location is principal — Rav Naḥman follows the Rabbis.
