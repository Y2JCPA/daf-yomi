The One-Fifth Charity Limit

R' Ile'a: Usha ordinance — don't give more than one-fifth (חומש) to charity
↓
Source: "עשר אעשרנו" (Genesis 28:22) — double tithe = two-tenths = one-fifth
↓
Objection
Second tenth is of the remainder — not exactly 1/5!
Rav Ashi's Answer
Verse says אעשרנו (not אעשר) — indicating the second tenth equals the first
STORY R' Yeshevav (or R' Akiva) stopped his friend from giving more than one-fifth — lest he become destitute and need others' help.

Stuffing Like an Ox vs. Treating Gently

Rav
From age 6, accept a child and stuff him like an ox — force-feed him Torah!
Usha Ordinance
Treat a son gently until 12. Only then harass him to study.
Resolution 1
Both are true: teach intensively from 6, but don't harass him for refusing until 12.
Resolution 2
Age 6 = Bible (force-feed). Age 12 = Mishnah (harass if needed).
RAV KETINA Starting school before 6: the child will be physically weaker but academically ahead. Both views are correct — it depends on the child's health.

Daughters' Sustenance: Land vs. Movable Property

Original Halakha

  • Daughters sustained only from land (קרקע)
  • Parallel to sons inheriting only land
  • Rav Hamnuna's teaching (re: ketubot bnin dikhrin)

Upper Chamber Enactment

  • Daughters sustained from movable property too
  • Enacted in the עלייה due to persecution
  • Rav Yitzchak bar Yosef reports this ordinance
DISPUTE Was Rav's sustaining orphan girls with wheat about livelihood (dowry per father's status — Shmuel's view) or actual food sustenance (per the upper chamber enactment)?

Amoraim Who Were Stopped from Ruling

🏛️ Neharde'a judges rule: sustain daughters from movable property
↓
🏛️ Rav Ḥana bar Bizna in Pumbedita does the same
↓
⚠️ Rav Naḥman threatens: Reverse your rulings or I'll seize your houses!
↓
🤔 R' Ami & R' Asi consider ruling — R' Ya'akov bar Idi stops them: R' Yoḥanan and Reish Lakish didn't rule on this!
↓
💔 R' Elazar acts out of pity — R' Shimon ben Elyakim warns: students will mistakenly establish this as halakha!
PATTERN Multiple Amoraim wanted to require sustaining daughters from movable property, but each was deterred — showing deep hesitancy to change established practice.
