Two Documents — Does the Second Nullify the First?

Rav Nachman
Two documents on the same matter produced one after the other — the second nullifies the first.
Rav Pappa
Rav Nachman concedes: if the second document added even a palm tree 🌴, it shows it was written as an addition, not a replacement.
Gemara
This resolves Rav Huna's ruling on two marriage contracts — since the second has a larger sum, it's clearly an addition, so both remain valid.
Principle Any new content in the second document signals supplementation, not cancellation.

Sale vs. Gift — Do Two Documents Cancel Each Other?

First: Sale → Second: Gift

  • Second does NOT negate first
  • Gift was written to improve recipient's rights
  • Related to halakha of bordering neighbor

First: Gift → Second: Sale

  • Second does NOT negate first
  • Sale protects against creditor seizure
  • Allows recipient to recover purchase price

Both Same Type

  • Second DOES nullify first
  • Applies to two sales or two gifts
  • Dispute: WHY does it nullify?
Key Insight Mixed document types (sale + gift) serve complementary purposes and coexist; identical types raise suspicion about the first.

Marriage Contract Lien — Final Halakha

📋 R' Elazar b. R' Shimon: Base sum (100/200 dinars) — lien from betrothal
↓
📋 R' Elazar b. R' Shimon: Additional sum (tosefet) — lien from marriage
↓
👥 The Rabbis: Both base and additional — lien from marriage only
↓
⚖️ HALAKHA: Follows the Rabbis — all liens from time of marriage
Practical Impact Property sold by the husband between betrothal and marriage cannot be seized by the wife to collect her ketubah.
