Ketubot 44 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, when does a second document NOT nullify the first?
A. When the second is written by different witnesses
B. When the second document adds new content (e.g., a palm tree)
C. When the first document was signed by three witnesses
D. When more than 30 days have passed between them
Correct: B — Rav Pappa qualifies Rav Nachman's rule: if the second document adds even a palm tree, it shows the intent was to supplement, not replace the first document.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the final halakha regarding when the lien on a husband's property for the ketubah takes effect?
A. From the time of betrothal for all sums
B. Base sum from betrothal, additional from marriage
C. From the time of marriage for all sums
D. From when the ketubah document is physically written
Correct: C — The halakha follows the Rabbis who hold that the lien for both the base sum and the additional sum takes effect from the time of marriage, not betrothal.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava, why must the defamer of a Jewish-born orphan be obligated to pay the fine?
A. Because the Torah explicitly states orphans are included
B. Because the fine goes to the orphan herself instead of her father
C. Because if orphans were exempt, the special exclusion of converts would be unnecessary
D. Because Beit Din stands in place of the father
Correct: C — Rava reasons: since converts (who are all like orphans) needed a special verse to be excluded, Jewish-born orphans must be included — otherwise the convert exclusion would be redundant.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the practical difference between Rafram and Rav Aḥa regarding why the second document nullifies the first?
A. Whether the seller can retract the sale
B. Whether witnesses on the first document are under suspicion, produce compensation, and tax liability
C. Whether the buyer can resell the property
D. Whether the second document requires new witnesses
Correct: B — Three practical differences: (1) reliability of the first document's witnesses, (2) who pays for produce consumed between the two dates, and (3) who bears the land tax for the interim period.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara derive that a girl whose conception was before her mother's conversion but whose birth was after is subject to stoning for adultery?
A. From the phrase "in Israel" in Deuteronomy 22:21
B. From the seemingly superfluous phrase "that she die" in Deuteronomy 22:21
C. From a kal va-chomer from a full convert
D. From an oral tradition (halakha l'Moshe mi-Sinai)
Correct: B — Reish Lakish derives from the superfluous phrase "that she die" (Deut. 22:21) that it includes a girl whose conception was not in sanctity but whose birth was — for the death penalty, but not for the monetary fine.
