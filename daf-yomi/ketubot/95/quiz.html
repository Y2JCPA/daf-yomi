Ketubot 95 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
In the Mishna's 'ḥalila' cycle, why can the purchaser repossess the field from the first wife?
A. Because his purchase deed predates her ketubah
B. Because she wrote a waiver relinquishing her rights vis-à-vis the purchaser
C. Because the second wife transferred her rights to him
D. Because the husband authorized the repossession
Correct: B — The first wife explicitly wrote to the purchaser that she has no legal dealings with him. When she later repossesses from the second wife, the purchaser can take it back from her based on her own waiver.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Meir, when a wife signs the bill of sale for a SECOND purchaser but not the first, what happens?
A. She can collect her ketubah from either purchaser
B. She loses her ketubah claim entirely
C. She can collect only from the first purchaser
D. The second sale is automatically nullified
Correct: B — R' Meir holds that by signing the second sale, she lost her ketubah claim. The first purchaser says: 'I left you a field to collect from (the second purchaser's field), and you brought this loss upon yourself by signing away that field too.'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara rule that if unsold property becomes 'blighted,' a creditor may collect from sold (liened) property?
A. Because the Torah explicitly permits it
B. Because the purchasers assumed the risk — they knew property can deteriorate
C. Because blighted property has no legal status as 'property'
D. Because the debtor intentionally ruined the property
Correct: B — The Gemara reasons that purchasers 'brought this loss upon themselves' — they knew (e.g., that orchards age) and should have anticipated the creditor might need to collect from their purchases.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Abaye, if property is bequeathed to an UNMARRIED woman ('to you, and after you to X') and she marries, why does the second beneficiary receive nothing?
A. Because the husband's ketubah obligation takes precedence
B. Because the husband is considered a 'purchaser' and per RSBG, the second beneficiary gets only what's left
C. Because marriage automatically cancels all prior bequests
D. Because the second beneficiary never performed a kinyan
Correct: B — Abaye follows Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel: the second beneficiary receives only what the first beneficiary left behind. Since the husband acquires his wife's property (as a 'purchaser'), nothing remains for the second beneficiary.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the cycle of repossession RESOLVE (property stays with the purchaser) in the case of a married woman who was bequeathed property, unlike the Mishna's endless ḥalila?
A. Because the married woman's sale is invalid
B. Because in the Mishna all parties have financial losses at stake, but here only the purchaser paid money — the others received gifts
C. Because Abaye ruled that cycles are forbidden
D. Because the husband waived his rights as part of the marriage contract
Correct: B — In the Mishna, all parties stand to lose money (purchase price, ketubah), so no one will concede. Here, only the purchaser actually paid — the husband and second beneficiary received the property as gifts — so the cycle resolves in the purchaser's favor.
