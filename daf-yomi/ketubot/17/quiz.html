Ketubot 17 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Beit Hillel say to always call the bride 'fair and attractive' even if she isn't?
A. Because the Torah requires exaggeration at weddings
B. Because after someone acquires something, you should praise it in their eyes — not cause anguish
C. Because the groom might return her if she isn't praised
D. Because it is a prophetic statement about her future beauty
Correct: B — Beit Hillel uses the analogy of praising a purchase someone already made — you don't criticize it after the fact. The principle is empathy: a person's disposition should always be considerate of others.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What happened when Rav Shmuel bar Rav Yitzchak died, vindicating his enthusiastic dancing?
A. A rainbow appeared over his grave
B. A pillar of fire separated him from everyone else
C. Three myrtle branches sprouted from his burial site
D. A heavenly voice proclaimed his righteousness
Correct: B — A pillar of fire demarcated between him and everyone else — a sign reserved for only one or two people in a generation. R' Zeira, who had initially criticized him, acknowledged that his enthusiastic mitzvah fulfillment earned him this reward.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why was King Agrippa praised for yielding to a bride's procession, if a king cannot waive his honor?
A. He was not a legitimate king so the rule didn't apply
B. It was at a crossroads, so his yielding was not publicly obvious
C. The Sages gave him special permission beforehand
D. The bride was from a royal family
Correct: B — The Gemara resolves the contradiction by explaining it happened at a crossroads — since his yielding wasn't obvious to onlookers, royal honor was preserved while he still performed a gracious act.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, why can't one establish chazakah (presumptive ownership) on a minor's inherited property?
A. Because minors cannot own property under Torah law
B. Because the minor is unaware of his father's holdings, so his silence proves nothing
C. Because the court must appoint a guardian first
D. Because inheritance doesn't transfer until the minor reaches majority
Correct: B — Rav Huna explains that a minor doesn't know what property his father owned, so the fact that he didn't challenge someone's possession is meaningless — it cannot count toward the three years needed for chazakah.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the practical halachic difference between the two definitions of 'hinnuma'?
A. Ze'eiri says it's a myrtle canopy; R' Yochanan says it's a face veil — but both serve as evidence of virgin status for the 200-dinar ketubah
B. One applies only in Eretz Yisrael and the other only in Babylonia
C. One requires witnesses and the other does not
D. Ze'eiri's definition gives 200 dinars but R' Yochanan's gives only 100
Correct: A — Both Ze'eiri (myrtle canopy) and R' Yochanan (face veil under which the bride dozes) agree on the function — the hinnuma is a wedding custom that serves as evidence of virgin status, entitling the bride to a 200-dinar ketubah.
