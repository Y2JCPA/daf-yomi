Conditional Betrothal & the Presumption Against Licentious Relations

Gemara
Why does Rav require a get when a man betroths conditionally but marries without restating the condition?
Rav
Because אין אדם עושה בעילתו בעילת זנות — a person does not intend licentious relations. He knows the condition might void the betrothal, so he intends the consummation as unconditional betrothal.
Shmuel
He does not intend new betrothal through intercourse — he relies on the original (conditional) betrothal. If the condition fails, no get is needed.
Gemara
But they already dispute this principle regarding a minor who didn't refuse (mi'un)! Why state both cases?
Gemara
Both are necessary: the conditional case has a tnai, the minor case does not — neither ruling could be derived from the other.
Core Principle אין אדם עושה בעילתו בעילת זנות — A person does not intentionally engage in licentious sexual intercourse. This presumption drives Rav's requirement of a get even when the original condition was never fulfilled.

Rav's Logic: Why a Get Is Required

Man betroths woman with a condition
(e.g., no vows upon her)
↓
He then marries her without restating the condition
↓
Condition turns out to be unfulfilled
↓
Rav
He intended consummation as new unconditional betrothal (אין אדם עושה בעילתו בעילת זנות).
→ Get required, but no ketubah payment
Shmuel
He relied on the original betrothal and did not intend new betrothal.
→ No get required
Key Nuance Even Rav agrees the man did not waive his condition entirely — so she gets no ketubah payment. He simply intended a valid marriage act.

Parallel Dispute: Minor Who Didn't Refuse (Mi'un)

Rav

  • Minor married off by mother/brother
  • She did NOT exercise her right of refusal (mi'un)
  • She became an adult and married a second man
  • First husband presumably had intercourse after she became adult
  • He intended it as valid betrothal (אין אדם עושה בעילתו בעילת זנות)
  • First marriage is binding — NO get needed from second man

Shmuel

  • Same scenario — minor who didn't refuse
  • First husband's intercourse was NOT intended as new betrothal
  • He intended to continue the existing relationship from her minority
  • That original marriage was inherently deficient
  • She is NOT his wife by Torah law
  • Second marriage is binding — she DOES need a get from the second man
Why Both Cases? The minor case has no explicit condition — you might think Rav only rules strictly there. The conditional betrothal case has a violated tnai — you might think Shmuel only rules leniently there. Both are needed.

The Mishna as Proof Against Shmuel

Gemara
The Mishna says: If he married her without specification and vows were found — no ketubah, but she needs a get. Isn't this the case of conditional betrothal + unconditional marriage?
Shmuel
No — the Mishna refers to where he betrothed without specification AND married without specification. There was never an explicit condition on the betrothal itself.
Gemara
But then, if conditional betrothal + unconditional marriage truly needs no get, why didn't the Mishna teach that more novel case?
Gemara
Answer: The Mishna does teach both — read it as two clauses: (1) conditional betrothal + unconditional marriage = not betrothed; (2) unconditional betrothal + unconditional marriage = needs a get but no ketubah.
Reread of the Mishna Shmuel reinterprets the Mishna as containing two separate rulings, avoiding the proof against his position.

Why No Ketubah but Still a Get? Three Explanations

AmoraExplanationGet Status
RabbaGet is required mi'derabanan (by rabbinic decree). By Torah law the betrothal is truly invalid since it was a mistaken transaction.Rabbinic requirement
Rav ḤisdaSame as Rabba — get is mi'derabanan. Since he didn't explicitly stipulate, the Sages require a get as a protective measure.Rabbinic requirement
RavaThe Tanna is uncertain about the betrothal's validity. For money (ketubah) — be lenient (burden of proof on claimant). For prohibitions (adultery) — be stringent (require a get).Stringency due to doubt
Practical Difference For Rabba/Rav Ḥisda, she is fundamentally unmarried — the get is a rabbinic safeguard. For Rava, genuine uncertainty means treating her as possibly married for issur purposes.

Rabba's Distinction: One Woman vs. Two Women

What is the scope of the Rav–Shmuel dispute?
↓
Rabba (Initial Version)
Dispute is only about two women:
He married Woman A conditionally, then married Woman B without specification.
Does the condition for A prove he's particular about B too?

But for one woman (conditional betrothal → unconditional marriage) — all agree no get needed.
Abaye's Objection
The Mishna discusses one woman and we raised a proof against Shmuel — so clearly they dispute one-woman cases too!
↓
Revised version of Rabba:
The dispute is about one woman similar to two women — he betrothed her conditionally, divorced her, then remarried her without specification.
↓
But for a simple one-woman case (condition → marriage → discovery), all agree no get needed.
The Twist Rabba narrows the dispute to cases where the second marriage act is separated from the condition — either by involving a different woman or by an intervening divorce.
