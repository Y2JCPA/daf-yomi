Ketubot 7 — From Shabbat Rulings to Wedding Blessings

Can a groom have first relations with his virgin bride on Shabbat?
↓
Stringent View
R' Yoḥanan in Tzaidan:
It is prohibited — causes a wound
Lenient View
Rav Ami, Rav Zevid:
It is permitted on Shabbat
↓
Final Halakha: Permitted on Shabbat — no concern for wound, opening, or bleeding
↓
Gemara transitions to Birkat Chatanim — wedding blessings and their requirements
DAF OVERVIEW This daf resolves the Shabbat intercourse debate, then establishes the sources and formulas for the wedding and betrothal blessings.

Is First Intercourse Permitted on Shabbat?

Rav Ami
Permitted on Shabbat. Have her seize movable property as a deposit for the ketubah.
Sages
But her ketubah is not yet written! Relations without a ketubah are an act of promiscuity!
Rav Zevid
Permitted on Shabbat — and some say Rav Zevid himself did so.
R' Yoḥanan
Issued a ruling in Tzaidan: It is prohibited on Shabbat.
Gemara
Final Halakha: It is permitted. No concern for wound, opening, or bleeding.
KEY RULING Despite multiple opinions, the Gemara concludes that first intercourse with a virgin is permitted on Shabbat.

Abscess Analogy — Why Is This Different?

Opening an abscess on Shabbat is permitted — the pus is pooled and fully detached from vessels
↓
Can we compare this to intercourse with a virgin?
Abscess
Pus is completely removed from vessels — nothing enduring is created
Hymen
Blood is not fully detached from vessels — removing it from its place is an aspect of melacha
DISTINCTION The Gemara rejects the abscess comparison because hymenal blood remains connected to blood vessels, unlike pooled pus.

Festival vs. Shabbat — The Rav Pappi / Rav Pappa Dispute

Rav Pappi (in Rava's name)

  • Rav Yehuda permitted on a Festival
  • The same is true on Shabbat — also permitted
  • The Festival ruling was just what happened to occur

Rav Pappa (in Rava's name)

  • On a Festival — permitted (wounding for food prep allowed, extended to all)
  • On Shabbat — prohibited
  • Uses the 'since it was permitted' (הואיל) logic for Festivals only
CONTEXT Both cite Rava but reach opposite conclusions about Shabbat. The final halakha follows the lenient view.

The 'Equal for Every Person' (שווה לכל נפש) Test

ItemUniversal Need?Permitted on Festival?
🔥 Incense (מוגמר)No — only for the delicate❌ Prohibited
💑 IntercourseYes — universal practice✅ Permitted
🦌 Deer meatYes — universal need (even if rare)✅ Permitted to slaughter
🍞 Food preparationYes — equal for all✅ Permitted
PRINCIPLE Exodus 12:16 — 'That which every person must eat, that alone may be done.' The standard is universal need, not universal availability (Rav Ashi).

Source for Ten Men at a Wedding

Rav Naḥman
Source is from Boaz: "He took ten men of the Elders" (Ruth 4:2).
R' Abbahu
Source is from Psalms 68:27: "In assemblies (מקהלות) bless God… from the source of Israel" — a kahal of ten blesses at conjugal matters.
Rav Naḥman
That Psalms verse? R' Meir uses it — even fetuses in the womb sang at the Red Sea: 'from the source of Israel.'
R' Abbahu
If it meant fetuses, the verse should say 'from the belly of Israel.' 'Source' clearly refers to conjugal matters.
R' Abbahu
And Boaz's ten Elders? That was to teach: Ammonite — not Ammonitess; Moabite — not Moabitess.
HALAKHA Birkat Chatanim requires a minyan of ten. The dispute is about the scriptural source, not the requirement itself.
