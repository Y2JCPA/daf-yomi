Ketubot 52 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, when is a husband obligated to redeem his captive wife?
A. Only when the captivity itself causes her to be forbidden to him
B. Whenever she has a valid ketubah
C. Only if her ransom is less than the ketubah amount
D. In all cases, even if she was previously forbidden
Correct: A — Rava distinguishes between prohibitions: if the captivity causes the prohibition (e.g., a priest's wife), he must redeem her. If another prohibition already existed, he is exempt.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why was Sumakhos concerned about the case where the vow comes AFTER captivity?
A. Because the vow might not be valid after captivity
B. Because the husband could use trickery — vowing after capture to avoid paying ransom
C. Because captivity automatically annuls vows
D. Because R' Eliezer would then agree with R' Yehoshua
Correct: B — If a post-captivity vow could exempt the husband from redemption, he could simply vow after his wife is captured to escape his obligation — paying only the ketubah instead of the full ransom.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel, what TWO limits apply to a husband's ransom obligation?
A. He pays no more than the captive's standard value AND no more than the ketubah amount
B. He pays no more than 10× the ketubah AND only on the first capture
C. He pays no more than he can afford AND no more than half his property
D. He pays no more than the market rate AND only for Jewish captives
Correct: A — RSBG combines two leniencies: the general rule against overpaying for captives AND a cap at the ketubah amount specifically for husbands.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did R' Yochanan regret advising his relatives to fix a lump sum with the doctor for the widow's treatment?
A. The doctor refused the arrangement
B. It appeared he was acting as a biased legal advisor favoring his own family
C. The treatment cost more than expected
D. The widow objected to the arrangement
Correct: B — R' Yochanan realized that as an important person, advising his relatives to convert unfixed costs into fixed ones (deducted from the ketubah rather than paid by the heirs) made him look like he was unfairly favoring his family at the widow's expense.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What was the purpose of enacting 'Ketubot Bnin Dikhrin' — that sons inherit their mother's ketubah?
A. To ensure sons receive equal shares of inheritance
B. To encourage fathers to give generous dowries, knowing grandchildren will inherit them
C. To prevent widows from losing their property rights
D. To discourage polygamy by tying inheritance to each wife's ketubah
Correct: B — R' Shimon ben Yochai explains: a father will give a larger dowry if he knows that even should his daughter die, his grandchildren (her sons) will inherit it — rather than it being absorbed into the general estate of the son-in-law.
