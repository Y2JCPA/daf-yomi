Ketubot 39 — Rapist vs. Seducer: Payments & Obligations

Rapist (אונס)

  • Pays four things: humiliation, degradation, fine, and pain
  • Pays immediately
  • Must marry her — even if lame, blind, or afflicted with boils
  • Cannot divorce her (by Torah law)

Seducer (מפתה)

  • Pays three things: humiliation, degradation, and fine
  • No payment for pain — she consented
  • Pays only when he opts not to marry her
  • May choose not to marry her
MISHNA The rapist 'drinks from his vessel' — he must marry the woman he violated and may never divorce her, unless she is unfit or committed adultery.

Can a Minor Conceive?

Rava
My dilemma about a rape victim dying assumes she can conceive before becoming a grown woman (bogeret).
Rav Beivai
Three women may use a mokh (contraceptive): a minor (lest she conceive and die), a pregnant woman, and a nursing woman.
R' Meir
A minor who may use contraceptive is 11 years + 1 day to 12 years + 1 day. Outside that range — normal relations.
Rabbis
In all these cases she may have normal relations — 'שׁוֹמֵר פְּתָאיִם ה׳' — God preserves the simple (Psalms 116:6).
Gemara
So a minor cannot conceive. And Shmuel says there are exactly six months between na'arah and bogeret — too short for pregnancy and birth.
RESOLUTION Rava's dilemma is reframed: not about inheritance, but whether there is achievement of grown-woman status in the grave — causing the father's right to lapse.

Does Death Effect Bogeret Status?

A na'arah (young woman) is raped, then dies before becoming a bogeret
↓
Does the passage of time in the grave produce bogeret status?
Yes — Bogeret in Grave
Father's right to the fine lapses. No claimant exists → rapist does not pay.
No — No Bogeret in Grave
Father's right did not lapse. The fine passes to his heirs.
TEIKU Mar bar Rav Ashi rephrased: Does death effect bogeret status? The dilemma stands unresolved.

Raped Then Betrothed — Who Gets the Fine?

Rava
If he raped a na'arah and she was later betrothed to someone else — what happens to the fine?
Abaye
The verse says 'who was not betrothed' (Deut. 22:28) — referring to the time of the rape, not the time of payment. Fine goes to the father.
Rava
But a baraita says if she later married, the fine goes to her — even though the verse doesn't say 'not married'!
Abaye
Marriage, like bogeret, removes her from her father's authority. But betrothal does not — her father and husband jointly annul her vows (Nedarim 66b).
KEY DISTINCTION Betrothal ≠ full removal from father's authority. Therefore the halakha of marriage cannot be derived for betrothal.

What Is the 'Pain' Payment?

❌ R' Shimon ben Yehuda first suggests: pain of future pregnancy — but she'd have pain with her husband too!
↓
💡 Response: Forced intercourse is not comparable to willing intercourse
↓
🦵 Rav Naḥman / Rabba bar Avuh: Pain of spreading her legs — as in Ezekiel 16:25
↓
❓ But a seduced woman also spreads her legs! Answer: She consented — like saying 'tear my silk and be exempt'
↓
🗣️ 'Clever women' say: a willing woman has negligible pain — like hot water on a bald head (Abaye's foster mother)
↓
🔪 Rava's wife (Rav Ḥisda's daughter): Like the stab of a bloodletting knife
↓
🍞 Rav Pappa's wife: Like hard bread on the gums — uncomfortable but bearable
CONCLUSION First intercourse causes some pain, but when willing, it is minor and waived by consent. The rapist must pay because the victim did not consent to that pain.
