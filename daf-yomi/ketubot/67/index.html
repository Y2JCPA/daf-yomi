Ketubot 67: The Measure of Charity

דֵּי מַחְסֹרוֹ אֲשֶׁר יֶחְסַר לוֹ
CORE THEME This daf explores the obligation and calibration of tzedakah — from Nakdimon ben Guryon's failure to give proportionally, to the principle of "dei machsoro" (sufficient for his deficiency): charity must restore each person to their dignity, not merely keep them alive. The daf also addresses how gold is valued in the ketubah and how to preserve the dignity of those too proud to accept help.

Was Nakdimon ben Guryon's Charity Enough?

Question
Nakdimon spread fine fabrics in the streets for the poor to collect — surely he gave abundant charity!
Answer 1
He did it for his own honor — to show the expense was trivial to him.
Answer 2
Even if altruistic, he didn't give proportionally to his enormous wealth.
Proverb
"According to the camel is the burden" — the stronger the camel, the heavier the load it must carry.
R' Elazar b. R' Tzadok
I saw his daughter gathering barley from between horse hooves in Akko — the family's fortune was lost.
LESSON Wealth brings proportional obligation. Nakdimon's generosity, though real, fell short of what his means demanded — and his descendants bore the consequence.

Gold in the Ketubah: How Is It Appraised?

Type of GoldStatusHow Recorded
Gold pieces / fragmentsLike utensilsAppraised at actual value — no increase or decrease
Gold dinars (where exchanged)Like cash / silver coinsValue is increased by one-third in the ketubah
Gold dinars (where NOT exchanged)DisputedRabban Shimon ben Gamliel: appraised at actual worth
R' YOCHANAN'S RULE Gold brought as dowry is appraised at its actual value — neither inflated nor reduced — because it retains its worth like gold utensils.

Caring for Orphans: Order of Priority

Orphan boy and orphan girl both come to the charity fund to be married off
↓
The orphan girl is married off first — because a woman's humiliation from being unmarried is greater
↓
For the orphan boy: rent a house → furnish with bed & utensils → then marry him a wife
↓
Source: "Sufficient for his deficiency in that which is deficient for him" (Deut. 15:8) — lo = a wife
DERASHA "Sufficient for his deficiency" = house. "Which is deficient" = bed and table. "For him" (lo) = a wife, as in Genesis 2:18: "I will make him a helpmate."

Dei Machsoro — Matching the Person's Former Standard

📜 "Sufficient for his deficiency" — you must support him, but need not make him wealthy
↓
🐴 "Which is deficient for him" — includes even a horse to ride and a servant to run before him, if that was his status
↓
🏃 Hillel the Elder once ran before a poor nobleman for 3 mil when no servant could be found
↓
🥩 People of Upper Galilee slaughtered an entire animal daily for one poor man of noble descent in a small village
↓
⚠️ R' Nehemiah's visitor who ate only fatty meat and aged wine died after eating lentils — he was too accustomed to rich food
↓
🐔 Rava's visitor claimed he eats from the Merciful One's funds, not the community's — and Providence sent Rava a fattened hen to prove it
PRINCIPLE Charity is not one-size-fits-all. Each person's deficiency is measured against their own former standard of living — even luxuries can be necessities for someone unaccustomed to deprivation.

Rava and the Man Who Ate Fattened Hen

Rava
You dine on fattened hen and aged wine? Are you not concerned for burdening the community?
The Pauper
Do I eat from their funds? I eat from the support of the Merciful One!
Proof Text
"You give them their food in its time" (Ps. 145:15) — God gives each person his own appropriate sustenance.
Providence
Rava's sister arrives after 13 years — bringing exactly a fattened hen and aged wine.
Rava
"Na'aneti" — I have been answered. Arise and eat.
INSIGHT The community is merely God's agent. When a person's sustenance arrives through unexpected channels, it confirms that Providence calibrates each person's needs individually.
