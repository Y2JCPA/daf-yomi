Can the Mitzva to Marry Override a Prohibition?

Rav Kahana
Let the positive mitzva — "And to him she shall be as a wife" (Deut. 22:29) — override the prohibition against marrying a woman forbidden to him!
Rav Zevid of Neharde'a
An asei overrides a lo ta'aseh only when the mitzva cannot be fulfilled without violating the prohibition — like circumcision on leprosy.
Rav Zevid of Neharde'a
But here — if she says 'I don't want him', there's no mitzva at all! Since the mitzva is conditional, it cannot override.
Principle A positive mitzva overrides a prohibition only when the mitzva is unconditional and unavoidable — not when a third party can negate it.

R' Elazar: Orphan Betrothed & Divorced

Rapist

  • Obligated to pay the fine (knas)
  • She is still a virgin na'arah
  • Fine is paid to HER (no father's authority)
  • Halakha follows R' Elazar (per Rav)

Seducer

  • Exempt from payment
  • She is independent (no father)
  • By consenting she forgoes the fine
  • R' Elazar follows R' Akiva his teacher
Key Insight A betrothed-and-divorced young woman is like an orphan: even if her father is alive, the fine goes to her, not to him. Rav called R' Elazar 'the happiest of the Sages' because the halakha so often followed his rulings.

What Does the 50-Sela Fine Cover?

R' Zeira (attempt 1)
A king's daughter and a commoner's daughter can't both get 50 sela — so the fine must be separate from humiliation!
Abaye
That's no proof — the ox that kills a slave pays 30 sela whether the slave pierces pearls or does needlecraft!
R' Zeira (attempt 2)
If two men assault her — vaginal then anal — would the second also pay 50 for a tainted victim?
Abaye
Same problem — an ox goring a healthy vs. sick slave both pay 30!
Abaye (his own proof)
The verse says "50 shekels… because he tormented her" — the 50 is for the tormenting. Humiliation and degradation are additional.
Rava
The verse says "the man who lay with her shall give 50" — the 50 is for the act of lying. The rest is extra.
Resolution The 50-sela fine covers only the act itself. Humiliation (boshet) and degradation (pegam) are assessed and paid separately, varying by circumstance.

How Is Degradation (Pegam) Assessed?

View her as a maidservant
sold in the marketplace
↓
Before
How much would a master pay for a virgin maidservant
to marry to his valued slave?
After
How much would he pay for a non-virgin maidservant
to marry to his valued slave?
↓
Degradation = Difference in price
(Shmuel's father)
Why Marriage? For mere service, virginity makes no difference. The price gap matters only when the master wants to reward a favored slave with a virgin bride — that emotional premium reflects the degradation.

R' Meir vs. Rabbis This mishna follows R' Meir. The Rabbis disagree: a minor IS entitled to a fine even where there is sale — sale and fine can coexist.
