The Perspicacious Husband's Strategy

One witness saw wife receive partial ketubah payment
↓
Husband pays full ketubah before both witnesses
↓
First payment is now recharacterized as a loan (הלוואה)
↓
One witness testifies she received money → Torah-level oath (שבועה דאורייתא)
RAV ASHI'S REFINEMENT He must tell the witnesses: 'This payment is for the one ketubah I wrote' — otherwise she can claim she had two separate ketubot.

Can Witnesses Be Joined Across Events?

Initial Suggestion
Join the first witness (who saw earlier payment) with the last witness (who sees current payment) to create testimony of two witnesses.
Rav Sheisha b. Rav Idi
Objection: Their testimonies refer to different events — you cannot combine them!
Rav Sheisha's Fix
Pay the full ketubah in the presence of both witnesses simultaneously.
Rav Ashi
Objection: She can claim: 'I had two ketubot!' — so explicitly state it's for the one ketubah.
PRINCIPLE Testimony must relate to the same event, and the husband must foreclose the wife's counter-claims to make the strategy work.

KEY RULE (Rava) כל האומר לא לוויתי כאומר לא פרעתי — Anyone who says 'I did not borrow' is like one who says 'I did not repay.' Since the document proves he borrowed, his denial undermines any repayment claim.

Collecting Debts in the Debtor's Absence

R' Yitzḥak (Antioch)
Only a wife may collect her ketubah in her husband's absence (with oath) — because the Sages wanted men to find favor in women's eyes (שימצא חן).
R' Yitzḥak
A creditor may NOT collect in the borrower's absence — perhaps he was already paid.
Rava / Rav Naḥman
Even a creditor may collect with oath in the borrower's absence.
Rava / Rav Naḥman
Otherwise, debtors will flee overseas and you lock the door before borrowers (נועל דלת בפני לווין) — no one will lend!
POLICY Both opinions balance the risk of wrongful collection against the need to keep credit markets functioning.

What Does Rabbi Shimon's Rule Refer To?

R' Yirmeya's Reading

  • Refers to collecting in husband's absence
  • R' Shimon = Ḥanan: oath only at conclusion (ketubah claim)
  • Rabbis = Sons of High Priests: oath also at beginning (sustenance)

Rav Sheshet's Reading

  • Refers to wife who was exempted from oath by husband
  • R' Shimon = Abba Shaul: father-appointed steward — no oath unless claiming ketubah
  • Rabbis = Rabbis in Gittin: father-appointed steward must take oath

Abaye's Reading

  • Refers to husband who wrote exemption from all oaths
  • R' Shimon = Abba Shaul b. Imma Miriam: exemption doesn't help for orphan's property
  • Rabbis: full exemption means she collects without oath
OBJECTION PATTERN Each interpretation is challenged on textual or logical grounds, leading the Gemara to propose successive alternatives.
