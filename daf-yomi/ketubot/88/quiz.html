Ketubot 88 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the 'perspicacious husband's' goal in the Gemara's scenario?
A. To avoid paying the ketubah entirely
B. To obligate his wife in a Torah-level oath
C. To invalidate the ketubah document
D. To disqualify the single witness
Correct: B — The husband's strategy converts a ketubah payment into a loan claim supported by one witness, which triggers a Torah-level oath obligation — stronger than a rabbinic oath.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rav Ashi require the husband to declare 'this is for the one ketubah I wrote'?
A. To fulfill a requirement of the marriage ceremony
B. Because witnesses must know the purpose of the payment
C. To prevent the wife from claiming she had two separate ketubot
D. Because oral declaration is required for all financial transactions
Correct: C — Without this declaration, the wife could claim she had two ketubot and collected them at different times, undermining the husband's claim that the first payment was a loan.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava, what is the legal significance of orphans saying 'Our father said he did not borrow'?
A. The creditor cannot collect at all
B. The creditor can collect only with an oath
C. The creditor can collect even without an oath
D. The case goes to binding arbitration
Correct: C — Rava's principle: 'Anyone who says I did not borrow is like one who says I did not repay.' Since the loan document proves he did borrow, his denial negates any repayment defense, so the creditor collects without an oath.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Rava/Rav Naḥman allow a creditor to collect in the borrower's absence?
A. Because the Torah commands repayment of all debts
B. Because the court stands in for the absent borrower
C. To prevent borrowers from fleeing overseas to avoid repayment, which would deter lending
D. Because a creditor's document is self-authenticating
Correct: C — The concern is 'locking the door before borrowers' (נועל דלת בפני לווין) — if debtors can escape collection by moving away, no one will be willing to lend money.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Abaye's textual objection to Rav Sheshet's interpretation of Rabbi Shimon?
A. Rabbi Shimon never discusses stewards
B. The mishna uses 'whenever she claims' — implying stringency, but Rav Sheshet makes R' Shimon the lenient view
C. Rav Sheshet confuses two different mishnayot
D. The phrase 'heirs administer an oath' contradicts Rav Sheshet's reading
Correct: B — Abaye argues that 'whenever she claims her ketubah, the heirs CAN administer an oath' sounds like R' Shimon is adding an oath requirement (stringent), not removing one (lenient) — which contradicts Rav Sheshet's reading.
