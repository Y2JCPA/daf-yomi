Ketubot 112 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
How large was the peach brought to R' Ḥelbo and his colleagues?
A. As large as a person's head
B. As large as a stewpot of Kefar Hino (5 se'a capacity)
C. As large as a watermelon (3 se'a capacity)
D. As large as a barrel (10 se'a capacity)
Correct: B — The peach was compared to an ilpas (stewpot) of Kefar Hino, which had a capacity of 5 se'a. They divided it into thirds — eating one third, declaring one third ownerless, and feeding one third to animals.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say 'nivneta' (Numbers 13:22) means Hebron was more fertile than Zoan, not literally built before it?
A. Because Hebron was destroyed and rebuilt
B. Because archaeological evidence contradicts a literal reading
C. Because a father wouldn't build for his younger son (Canaan) before his older son (Mizraim)
D. Because the word can only mean 'developed' in Biblical Hebrew
Correct: C — Genesis 10:6 lists Mizraim (Egypt) before Canaan, making Egypt the older son. A father would not build for his younger son first, so 'nivneta' must mean Hebron was seven times more developed/fertile than Zoan.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What happened to the grape clusters in Gavla after R' Yehoshua ben Levi's visit?
A. They grew even larger the next year
B. They disappeared entirely
C. They shrank from calf-sized to goat-sized
D. They turned sour and became unusable
Correct: C — R' Yehoshua ben Levi cursed the land's bounty since it benefited gentile rulers. When R' Ḥiyya visited the next year, the clusters had shrunk from calf-size to goat-size. The locals told him: 'Don't do to us what your colleague did!'
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why is Eretz Yisrael compared to a deer (tzvi) according to Rav Ḥisda?
A. Because it is swift to produce fruit
B. Because it is beautiful like a deer
C. Because just as a deer's skin cannot contain its meat once skinned, the land expands and contracts
D. Because deer were abundant in the land
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda explains that just as a deer's hide shrinks and cannot contain its meat once removed, Eretz Yisrael expands to hold all of Israel when they dwell there and contracts when they are exiled.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rav teach about barren trees in the messianic era?
A. They will be uprooted to make room for fruit trees
B. All barren trees will bear fruit, as derived from Joel 2:22
C. Only fig and vine trees will become fruitful
D. Barren trees will serve as building material for the Temple
Correct: B — Rav derives from Joel 2:22 ('the tree bears its fruit, the fig tree and the vine yield their strength') that ALL trees — not just fig and vine — will produce fruit in the messianic era. The verse mentions 'the tree' generally before specifying fig and vine.
