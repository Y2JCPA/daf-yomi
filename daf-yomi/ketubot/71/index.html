When Must a Husband Divorce Over His Vow?

Rav
If he vows without specifying an end point, he must divorce her immediately and pay her ketubah.
Shmuel
Even an unspecified vow — he should not divorce immediately, as perhaps he will find a petaḥ (opening) to dissolve it.
Gemara
But they already disputed this regarding vows on marital relations (Daf 61b)! Why repeat it here regarding sustenance?
Gemara
Both cases are needed: each side might concede in one scenario but not the other — a trustee can compensate for sustenance but not for intimacy.
KEY PRINCIPLE The Gemara establishes that Rav and Shmuel's dispute must be stated for both vows on sustenance and vows on relations, because neither case is derivable from the other.

Rav vs. Shmuel: Unspecified Vows

Rav
The 30-day grace period with a trustee applies only when the husband specifies a time limit. An unspecified vow → immediate divorce + ketubah.
Shmuel
Even an unspecified vow does not require immediate divorce — perhaps he will discover a petaḥ (halakhic opening) to dissolve the vow.
Gemara
Why state this dispute twice — once for vows on relations (61b) and again for vows on sustenance?
Gemara
Each is necessary: with sustenance, Rav might concede since a trustee can help; with relations, Shmuel might concede since no trustee can substitute.
LEGAL LOGIC Neither ruling is derivable from the other — each party might have conceded in the alternate scenario, so both must be stated explicitly.

When She Vows & He Ratifies (R' Meir's View)

Wife vows to prohibit herself from tasting a type of produce
↓
Husband hears the vow — does he nullify it?
He nullifies
Vow dissolved.
No divorce needed.
He ratifies / stays silent
He is 'putting his finger between her teeth' — he caused the vow to persist.
Must divorce + pay ketubah.
R' MEIR'S PRINCIPLE When a husband ratifies his wife's vow, he bears responsibility — he must pay her ketubah upon divorce because he could have prevented the situation.

Nullifying Vows 'Between Him & Her'

Rav Huna

  • Husband CAN nullify vows on matters between them
  • Removal of pubic hair counts as 'between him and her'
  • These vows relate to intimacy and he may override them

Rav Adda bar Ahava

  • Husband CANNOT nullify such vows
  • Analogy: 'A fox does not die from the dirt of its own hole'
  • He is familiar with her body — no real harm to him
  • Pubic hair does not truly interfere with relations
RESOLUTION Even for Rav Adda, the case works if she vowed: 'Pleasure of intercourse with you is forbidden to me if I adorn myself' — creating a contingency that qualifies as a matter between them.

Rav Kahana's Distinction on Vow Language

הֲנָאַת תַּשְׁמִישֵׁךְ עָלַי אָסוּר
CRITICAL DISTINCTION 'The pleasure of intercourse with me is forbidden to you' — He may compel her to fulfill marital duties, since she cannot unilaterally render herself forbidden.

'The pleasure of intercourse with you is forbidden to me' — This vow is valid but he may nullify it. One cannot feed a person something forbidden to them (ein ma'akhilin lo davar ha-asur lo).
