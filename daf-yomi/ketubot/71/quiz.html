Ketubot 71 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara need Rav and Shmuel's dispute stated for BOTH vows on sustenance and vows on relations?
A. Because sustenance involves a trustee and relations do not — each side might concede in the other case
B. Because the Torah lists them as separate prohibitions
C. Because Rav changed his mind between the two statements
D. Because Shmuel only applies his rule to sustenance vows
Correct: A — A trustee can substitute for sustenance but not for marital relations. In each case, one might think that the opposing view would concede, so both must be stated explicitly.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Kahana, when a wife says 'The pleasure of intercourse with YOU is forbidden to ME,' what is the husband's recourse?
A. He may compel her to have relations despite the vow
B. He may nullify the vow since it relates to matters between them
C. He must immediately divorce her
D. He must wait 30 days before acting
Correct: B — Rav Kahana distinguishes: 'with me forbidden to you' — he can compel her (she can't unilaterally forbid herself). But 'with you forbidden to me' — the vow is valid and he must nullify it, because one cannot feed a person something forbidden to them.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why is immediate divorce required when SHE vows and HE remains silent, but not when HE vows?
A. His silence indicates he agrees with the vow and wants the marriage to end
B. When he vows, she hopes he'll calm down; when he's silent about her vow, she concludes he despises her
C. The Torah only allows nullification within the first day
D. Her vow is automatically more severe than his
Correct: B — The Gemara explains the psychological distinction: when he vows, she thinks he's angry and will dissolve it. But when she vows and he says nothing, she interprets his silence as contempt — making the marriage untenable.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Rav Adda bar Ahava mean by 'a fox does not die in the dirt of its own hole'?
A. A husband is familiar with his wife's body, so pubic hair does not truly harm him
B. A woman should not be forced to leave her home
C. Vows made at home are less binding than those made in court
D. A fox is clever enough to escape any trap
Correct: A — Rav Adda bar Ahava argues that since the husband is accustomed to his wife's body, the presence of pubic hair does not constitute real interference with relations — just as a fox is not harmed by the familiar dirt of its own den.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Mishna, how long may a husband maintain his wife if he vows she cannot visit her father in a DIFFERENT city?
A. Up to 30 days
B. Up to one pilgrim Festival (until the next regel)
C. Up to two months
D. Up to 12 months
Correct: B — When the father lives in a different city, the wife normally visits during the pilgrim Festival. She can endure missing one Festival, but if the vow extends through three Festivals, divorce is required.
