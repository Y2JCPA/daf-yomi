Ketubot 94 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the First Tanna, why does the 4th wife NOT need to take an oath before collecting?
A. Because whatever she collects can be taken from her to pay earlier wives
B. Because she is the last in line and has the weakest claim
C. Because oaths are never required for marriage contracts
D. Because the estate always has enough property for all wives
Correct: A — The First Tanna holds that what a later creditor collected, he has NOT fully collected — it can be repossessed. Since the 4th wife's collection is reversible, no oath is needed to protect the earlier wives.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rav Nachman's alternative explanation for why Ben Nanas requires the 4th wife to swear?
A. Concern that she may not be legally entitled to any payment
B. Concern that she will deplete the field knowing it may be repossessed
C. Concern that the orphans cannot defend themselves
D. Concern that the field was stolen property
Correct: B — Rav Nachman (citing Rabba bar Avuh) says everyone agrees property can be taken back. The dispute is about whether we fear the 4th wife will exploit the land short-term, knowing she may lose it — the oath prevents this reckless behavior.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If one of two partners loses a court case while the other partner is in town but didn't attend, what is the ruling?
A. The absent partner can demand a retrial
B. The absent partner is bound by the verdict — he chose not to come
C. The absent partner has 30 days to appeal
D. The verdict is automatically void without both partners present
Correct: B — If the absent partner is in town and fails to appear, he is deemed to have accepted the representation of the partner who did appear. Only if he was out of town can he claim 'I would have argued better.'
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Rav Nachman reject Rav Sheshet's ruling in the case of Rami bar Ḥama vs. Mar Ukva?
A. Because Mar Ukva's deed was witnessed by more people
B. Because the mother explicitly revoked the first deed
C. Because hours are not recorded on legal documents, and Rav Sheshet was not an official judge
D. Because evening deeds take precedence over morning deeds
Correct: C — Rav Nachman gave two reasons: (1) since hours aren't recorded on documents, the time of writing is irrelevant, and (2) Rav Nachman held official judicial authority while Rav Sheshet did not, and Rav Sheshet's original reasoning was flawed.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the fundamental disagreement between Rav and Shmuel regarding two deeds issued on the same day?
A. Whether the deeds are valid at all
B. Whether to divide the property or leave it to judicial discretion
C. Whether the seller can retract both sales
D. Whether witnesses must testify about which deed came first
Correct: B — Rav holds that when ownership is uncertain, the recipients divide equally. Shmuel holds the matter should be resolved by shuda d'dayyanei — the judge uses discretion to determine the likely intent of the grantor.
