QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Abaye challenge Rav Yosef's reliance on Rabban Gamliel for the betrothed woman?
A. Because Rabban Gamliel's ruling only applies to unmarried women
B. Because Shmuel requires majority of nearby men to be fit, and for a betrothed woman most are unfit
C. Because the betrothed man denied paternity
D. Because Abaye holds the halakha never follows Rabban Gamliel
Correct: B — Shmuel qualified his ruling: follow Rabban Gamliel only when the majority of men in her proximity are of fit lineage. Since she is betrothed, any man other than her betrothed would produce a mamzer — making the majority 'unfit.'
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rav Yosef resolve the contradiction in Shmuel's ruling (follow Rabban Gamliel, but don't act on it)?
A. Shmuel retracted his ruling
B. The requirement for majority fit men is l'chatchila; accepting her claim regardless is b'dieved
C. The ruling only applies in Eretz Yisrael
D. Shmuel was speaking about a different case entirely
Correct: B — Rav Yosef distinguishes: l'chatchila (ideally), one should verify the majority are fit. B'dieved (after the fact), especially when the child would be branded a mamzer, the woman's claim is accepted.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava, why is Rabban Gamliel consistent in believing the woman who had relations but NOT the widow of the issa family?
A. The widow is suspected of lying
B. The woman's claim about relations is certain; the widow's claim about her husband's lineage is uncertain
C. Rabban Gamliel changed his mind over time
D. The issa family case involves a Torah prohibition while relations do not
Correct: B — Rava explains that Rabban Gamliel weighs the strength of the claim: a certain, direct claim ('I know who the father is') is powerful enough to overcome even single doubt, but an uncertain claim about a husband's family lineage is too weak even with compound doubt.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the baraita distinguish between uncertain chalal status and uncertain mamzer status regarding the widow of an issa family?
A. Chalal is a less severe defect than mamzer
B. Mamzerim are publicly identified and don't assimilate; chalalin are not identified and may assimilate into families
C. Chalal status can only be determined by a Beit Din
D. The Torah explicitly permits marriage to a chalal's widow
Correct: B — The baraita explains that Jews identify mamzerim among them (they don't blend in), but chalalin are not identified and can assimilate into families unnoticed. This distinction in social visibility drives the different rulings.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the three-way dispute about silence to lineage allegations, what is R' Meir's unique position?
A. Silence always indicates guilt regardless of the allegation
B. Silence never indicates guilt — only witnesses matter
C. Silence to chalal allegations is mere indifference (not admission), since chalal only bars priestly marriage
D. Silence to mamzer allegations is indifference because mamzer status generates publicity
Correct: C — R' Meir holds that silence to mamzer/Gibeonite/slave allegations renders one unfit, but silence to chalal allegations does not — because being a chalal only restricts marriage to Kohanim, so the person may simply be indifferent to a relatively minor stigma.
