Credibility, Certainty & Silence in Lineage Claims

Rav Yosef
A betrothed woman who says 'the child is from my betrothed' is believed — he admits paternity, and Shmuel rules like Rabban Gamliel
Abaye
But Shmuel requires majority of nearby men to be fit — and here she is betrothed, so any other man produces a mamzer!
Rav Yosef
That requirement is l'chatchila. Since the child's status as mamzer is at stake, this is b'dieved — and she is believed
Rava
Rabban Gamliel and R' Yehoshua are consistent — the key is certain vs. uncertain claims and single vs. compound uncertainties
Core Theme The Gemara builds a framework distinguishing certain claims from uncertain ones, and single uncertainties from compound ones, to harmonize seemingly contradictory rulings about a woman's credibility regarding lineage.

Can We Rely on the Betrothed Woman's Claim?

Rav Yosef
She says the child is from her betrothed, and he admits it. Plus, Shmuel rules halakha follows Rabban Gamliel — she'd be believed even without his admission.
Abaye
But Shmuel told Rav Yehuda: Follow Rabban Gamliel, but don't act on it unless majority of nearby men are fit (rov kesheirin etzlah).
Abaye
She is betrothed — any man besides her betrothed makes the child a mamzer. The majority are unfit!
Rav Yosef
The 'majority must be fit' requirement is l'chatchila. Shmuel's general ruling is b'dieved. Here, where the child would be a mamzer, it's b'dieved — so she is believed.
Key Distinction L'chatchila (ideally): Require majority of fit men nearby. B'dieved (after the fact): Accept her claim regardless, to avoid branding a child a mamzer.

Rava Resolves: Rabban Gamliel vs. R' Yehoshua

Rabban Gamliel

  • A certain claim ('I know who the father is') is so powerful →
  • Even with one uncertainty (single doubt), she is believed
  • An uncertain claim (widow of issa family) is so weak →
  • Even with a compound uncertainty (sfek sfeka), she is not believed

Rabbi Yehoshua

  • A single uncertainty is so powerful →
  • Even with a certain claim, she is not believed
  • A compound uncertainty (sfek sfeka) is so weak →
  • Even with an uncertain claim, she is believed (deemed fit)
Elegant Symmetry Rabban Gamliel weights the strength of the claim; R' Yehoshua weights the degree of factual uncertainty. Each is internally consistent.

Widow of Issa Family — Types of Questionable Lineage

Type of UncertaintyStatus of WidowReason
Uncertain MamzerUnfit to marry KohenJews identify mamzerim — no assimilation concern, so uncertainty = real doubt
Uncertain Gibeonite (Nasin)Unfit to marry KohenSimilarly identifiable — uncertainty is a genuine problem
Uncertain Slaves of KingsUnfit to marry KohenKnown category — same logic applies
Uncertain ChalalFit to marry KohenJews do NOT identify chalalin — they assimilate, so we are lenient
Why the Difference? Mamzerim are publicly known and tracked; chalalin blend into the community unnoticed. Since chalal status is harder to verify, the Sages were lenient about the widow's fitness.

Silence to Allegations — Does It Confirm Unfitness?

First Tanna
If someone is called mamzer, Gibeonite, slave, or chalal and remains silent — he is unfit. Silence = admission.
R' Meir
Silence to mamzer/Gibeonite/slave allegations = unfit. But silence to chalal does NOT make one unfit — it's mere indifference, since chalal only bars marriage to Kohanim.
R' Shimon b. Elazar
R' Meir's leniency is actually about silence to mamzer allegations — since mamzer status generates publicity, silence shows indifference, not admission.
R' Shimon b. Elazar
But silence to chalal allegations? That does render one unfit — he accepts it because at least he's not expelled from the congregation.
R' Yochanan's Key The entire baraita dispute revolves around interpreting silence: Is it admission, indifference, or strategic acceptance? Each Tanna reads the psychology differently.
