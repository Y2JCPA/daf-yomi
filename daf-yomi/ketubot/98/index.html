Widow Collecting Her Ketubah from the Estate

Widow seeks to collect her ketubah from orphans' estate
↓
Does she sell property in court or outside court?
In Court
Standard procedure with court oversight
Outside Court
Must take an oath but no public announcement required
↓
Did she sell property worth more than her ketubah?
No — within value
Sale is valid; ketubah collected
Yes — exceeds ketubah
Sale is void entirely
Halakha A widow selling estate property outside court must take an oath but does not need to make a public announcement.

Seizing Partial Payment & Demanding Sustenance

Case
A woman seized a silver cup as partial payment of her ketubah and also demanded sustenance from the orphans.
R' Shimon
Once she has received part of the money, it is as if she received all of it — she should lose her sustenance rights.
Rava
Give her sustenance! No one follows R' Shimon's view that part of the money is like the whole sum.
Ruling Partial collection of the ketubah does not terminate the widow's right to sustenance from the estate.

Selling Property Outside Court — Oath & Announcement

Widow sells orphans' property outside of court
↓
Does she need a public announcement (hakrazah)?
No
R' Zeira / Rav Naḥman: If she assessed it herself, her act is void — but selling to others without announcement is valid
Self-Assessment
"Who assessed this for you?" — Without proper assessment, acquisition never occurred
↓
Does she need to take an oath?
Yes
She must swear she has not taken more than she deserves
Halakha
Oath required, announcement not required
Key Distinction Self-assessment is always invalid ('Who assessed this for you?'), but selling to others without public announcement is effective.

Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel vs. Tanna Kamma on Void Sales

Tanna Kamma

  • Any excess beyond the ketubah voids the sale
  • Even one extra dinar invalidates everything
  • Returning the extra amount does not help

Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel

  • Sale is valid unless the error is extreme
  • Void only if remaining land ≥ beit 9 kav (field)
  • Or ≥ beit ½ kav for a garden (R' Akiva: ¼ kav)
  • For smaller errors — she returns the excess
Dispute The dispute centers on whether any excess voids the sale, or only excess large enough that the orphans have a meaningful claim to the remaining land.

Who Keeps the Agent's Profit?

R' Yehuda
If the seller added an extra item beyond what the agent was sent to buy, the profit belongs to the agent.
R' Yosei (1st view)
The owner and agent split the profit.
R' Yosei (2nd view)
Everything belongs to the owner of the money.
Rami bar Ḥama
Fixed price item → extra is a gift, unclear for whom → split.
Non-fixed price → part of the deal → belongs to owner.
Rav Pappa
Halakha: Fixed price → split. Non-fixed price → all to the owner.
Application This principle explains the mishna: when a widow sells property for more than its value, the profit belongs to the estate owner (the heirs), not to her — she has merely collected her ketubah.
