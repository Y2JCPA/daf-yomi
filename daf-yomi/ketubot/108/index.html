Ketubot 108 — Vow Benefits, Inheritance, & Partial Admissions

DAF OVERVIEW This daf transitions from the laws of nedarim (vows) — specifically how one may repay another's debt without violating a vow prohibition — into Admon's seven dissenting rulings. The first involves a son's inheritance rights in a small estate, and the second concerns whether admitting to owing pitchers constitutes partial admission to a claim for jugs of oil.
3 / 9

Repaying a Debt for One Under a Vow

Mishna (Nedarim)
If Reuven may not derive benefit from Shimon, Shimon may still contribute his shekel, repay his debt, and return his lost object on his behalf.
Gemara
Shekel — he's just doing a mitzvah (communal offerings apply to all). Lost object — also a mitzvah. But repaying his debt? That's real monetary benefit!
Rav Oshaya
This follows Ḥanan, who says when someone supports another's wife, 'he has lost his money.' Here too — the payer loses his money; the debtor had no real obligation to repay.
Rava
Even the Rabbis agree — when the borrower took a loan with no fixed repayment date, repaying it benefits the lender, not the borrower.
KEY DIFFERENCE Rav Oshaya limits the mishna to Ḥanan's view; Rava broadens it to work even according to the Rabbis.
4 / 9

Why Didn't Rav Oshaya Accept Rava's Explanation?

Loan has no fixed repayment date — borrower need not repay yet
↓
Does the borrower receive any benefit from early repayment?
Rava says NO
No monetary benefit — the borrower didn't have to pay yet
Rav Oshaya says YES
The borrower still has shame (busha) from the outstanding debt — removing that shame is a benefit
INSIGHT Rav Oshaya considers psychological relief from shame as a form of hana'ah (benefit), so even a no-deadline loan doesn't solve the problem.

Admon's First Case: Sons vs. Daughters in a Small Estate

Rabbis' View

  • Small estate → daughters receive sustenance
  • Sons get nothing and must beg at doors
  • Daughters' maintenance takes priority
  • Based on ketubah conditions

Admon's View

  • "Because I am male, will I lose out?"
  • Sons should not be worse off than daughters
  • Rabban Gamliel supports Admon
  • Males have greater inheritance rights by Torah law
ABAYE vs. RAVA Abaye: Admon means — I study Torah, shouldn't I benefit? Rava: Torah study is irrelevant to inheritance. Admon means — by Torah law males inherit, so they shouldn't lose out entirely.

Wheat & Barley: Does Partial Admission Apply Across Types?

Rav Naḥman / Shmuel
If one claims wheat and barley and the other admits to barley only, he must take an oath — it's a partial admission.
R' Ḥiyya bar Abba
If one claims wheat and barley and the other admits to one type, he is exempt from an oath — the admission is not of the same type as the claim.
Rav Shimi bar Ashi
Jugs and oil are like a pomegranate in its peel — inherently connected. Wheat and barley are separate items entirely.
Ravina
Objection! A pomegranate can't survive without its peel, but oil can be stored without pitchers. The analogy doesn't hold!
RESOLUTION Ravina reframes the case: the defendant said the oil claim never happened but admitted to 5 of 10 pitchers — making it a standard partial admission dispute.
