Ketubot 30 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is a High Priest's non-virgin different from an Egyptian convert regarding mamzerut according to Rabbi Yeshevav?
A. The High Priest's prohibition is a positive mitzva not equal for all
B. The High Priest's prohibition is a negative prohibition, not a positive mitzva
C. Betrothal does not take effect with a non-virgin
D. A non-virgin is not suitable to sustain as a wife
Correct: A — The Gemara explains that the High Priest's obligation to marry a virgin is a 'mitzvat aseh she'einah shavah bakol' — a positive mitzva that applies only to the High Priest, not all Jews. This leniency means even R' Yeshevav agrees the child is not a mamzer.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the practical difference (nafka minah) between Abaye's and Rava's derivations of R' Neḥunya ben HaKana's principle?
A. Whether one who violates Shabbat pays for damages
B. Whether a non-priest who ate teruma must pay the priest
C. Whether one who eats chametz on Pesach is exempt from payment
D. Whether one who worships Molech must pay damages
Correct: B — A non-priest eating teruma is liable to death at the hand of Heaven but NOT karet. Abaye (all Heavenly death exempts) would exempt him from payment; Rava (only karet exempts) would require him to pay.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Yosef/R' Ḥiyya, what is the Divine equivalent of the court-imposed death penalty of burning (sereifah)?
A. Drowning in a river
B. Being trampled by a beast
C. Falling into fire or being bitten by a snake
D. Being killed by bandits
Correct: C — The baraita teaches that one liable to burning either falls into a conflagration or is bitten by a snake (which creates a burning sensation), paralleling the court-imposed penalty of sereifah.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Rav Ḥisda say that EVERYONE agrees a rapist of a menstruating woman (niddah) pays the fine?
A. Because niddah is only a rabbinic prohibition
B. Because betrothal takes effect with a niddah AND she is suitable to sustain as a wife
C. Because the prohibition is temporary and will be lifted
D. Because the fine for niddah is explicitly stated in the Torah
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda explains that a niddah satisfies BOTH criteria in the debate: betrothal does take effect with her, and she is suitable to sustain as a wife (since niddah is a temporary state). Therefore, all opinions agree the fine applies.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rav Ashi resolve the problem of simultaneous liability for a non-priest eating teruma?
A. Another person forced the teruma into his throat
B. The teruma was liquid so it was ruined immediately
C. The non-priest ate his own teruma that he inherited from a priest
D. The non-priest did not know it was teruma
Correct: C — Rav Ashi's solution: the non-priest inherited or purchased teruma from a priest. Since it is his own property, there is no theft and no payment obligation at all — only the death penalty for a non-priest eating teruma. This avoids the timing problem entirely.
