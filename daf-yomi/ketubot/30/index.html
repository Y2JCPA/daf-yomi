Ketubot 30: Death Penalties, Divine Punishment & Payment Exemptions

Mamzerut: When does violating a positive mitzva produce a mamzer?
↓
Distinction: mitzvot equal for all vs. not equal for all (e.g., High Priest's non-virgin)
↓
R' Neḥunya ben HaKana: Yom Kippur = Shabbat for kim leih bid'rabba minei
↓
Abaye
Gezerah shavah: 'harm' — death at hand of Heaven = death at hand of man
Rava
Juxtaposition: karet with Molech — only karet exempts, not all Heavenly death
Core Theme This daf explores when severe spiritual liability (karet or death at Heaven's hand) exempts one from monetary payment — and the precise derivation determines the scope of that exemption.

Mamzerut from Positive Mitzvot

Gemara
If R' Yeshevav states his own view: anyone without possibility of permitted relations → child is mamzer, even from violations of positive mitzvot (Egyptian/Edomite converts)
Gemara
Then what is the difference between Shimon HaTimni and R' Shimon ben Menasya?
Gemara
The difference: a non-virgin raped by a High Priest
Gemara
But that too violates a positive mitzva — 'a virgin of his own people shall he take' (Lev. 21:14)!
Gemara
It is different: a positive mitzva not equal for all (mitzvat aseh she'einah shavah bakol) — applies only to the High Priest, not all Jews
Key Distinction A positive mitzva that applies only to specific people (like the High Priest's obligation to marry a virgin) has a more lenient status — its violation does not produce mamzerut even according to the broadest view.

Rav Ḥisda's Ruling: Forced Relations with a Niddah

אונס נדה — משלם קנס
Unanimous Agreement Rav Ḥisda said: Everyone agrees that one who has forced intercourse with a menstruating woman (niddah) pays the fine.

• According to the view that betrothal is the criterion — betrothal does take effect with a niddah
• According to the view that suitability to sustain is the criterion — she is suitable to sustain as a wife (after purification)

R' Neḥunya ben HaKana: Yom Kippur = Shabbat

Shabbat (agreed by all)

  • Intentional violation → death penalty (stoning)
  • Kim leih bid'rabba minei applies
  • Exempt from monetary payments for damages caused while violating Shabbat

Yom Kippur (R' Neḥunya)

  • Intentional violation → karet (divine excision)
  • R' Neḥunya: treated like court-imposed death
  • Exempt from monetary payments for damages caused while violating Yom Kippur
Mishna Context The mishna's ruling that one who has relations with his sister pays a fine comes to exclude R' Neḥunya ben HaKana's view, since such relations carry karet.

The Four Death Penalties After the Temple's Destruction

Court PenaltyDivine Equivalent
Stoning (Sekilah)Falls from roof or trampled by beast
Burning (Sereifah)Falls into fire or bitten by snake
Decapitation (Hereg)Handed to monarchy or killed by bandits
Strangulation (Ḥenek)Drowns in river or dies of diphtheria (serunki)
Rav Yosef / R' Ḥiyya Though the Sanhedrin was abolished, the punishment of the four death penalties was not abolished — they continue through Divine Providence in equivalent forms.

Abaye vs. Rava: Source for R' Neḥunya's Rule

Abaye: Gezerah shavah on the word 'harm' (ason)
↓
'Harm' at hands of man (Exodus 21:23) ↔ 'Harm' at hand of Heaven (Genesis 42:38)
↓
Result: All death at hand of Heaven exempts from payment
↓
Rava: Juxtaposition in Leviticus 20:4–5 — Molech/karet passage
↓
'My karet is like your death penalty'
↓
Result: Only karet exempts from payment, not mere death at hand of Heaven
Practical Difference A non-priest who ate teruma is liable to death at the hand of Heaven but NOT karet. According to Abaye — exempt from payment. According to Rava — liable to pay.
