Ketubot 70 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why does the court give the deceased father's sons more than the shekel he specified?
A. Because R' Meir's principle of fulfilling the dead's wishes doesn't apply to child support
B. Because the father certainly preferred his children be properly sustained — his limit was to encourage thrift
C. Because the court always overrides instructions of the deceased regarding minors
D. Because the father's vow was automatically annulled at death
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that while R' Meir says it's a mitzva to fulfill the dead's wishes, here the father obviously wanted his children provided for — he only set a limit to encourage frugal habits.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does R' Yehuda give a priest more time before requiring divorce when the husband vows against his wife?
A. Priests have greater sanctity and their vows are harder to annul
B. A priest cannot remarry his own divorcée, making divorce irreversible
C. Priests are wealthier and can sustain a trustee arrangement longer
D. The Torah gives priests special exemptions from vow-related divorces
Correct: B — Since a priest is prohibited from marrying a divorcée — including his own ex-wife — Beit Din gives him extra time to find a way to annul the vow before forcing an irreversible divorce.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the problem that a husband's vow should not override his prior ketubah obligation to sustain his wife?
A. The vow automatically nullifies the ketubah
B. He must explicitly tell her to support herself from her own earnings, and a trustee covers the shortfall
C. The ketubah obligation is only rabbinic so a Torah-level vow overrides it
D. The wife's consent to the marriage waives her sustenance rights
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes he must actually invoke his right to redirect her to her own earnings. Since those earnings may not cover everything, a trustee is appointed for the balance.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the significance of the 30-day threshold in the mishna?
A. After 30 days the vow becomes permanent and cannot be annulled
B. 30 days is when the wife's earnings run out
C. After 30 days people hear about the arrangement and it becomes demeaning for her
D. 30 days is the standard waiting period for all divorce proceedings
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that for up to 30 days, receiving sustenance through an intermediary goes unnoticed. After that, it becomes public knowledge and is demeaning to the wife.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, when does the wife need a trustee (parnas) if the husband redirects her to her own earnings?
A. When she refuses to work at all
B. When her earnings cover basic needs but not small extras she was accustomed to in her father's house
C. When her earnings exceed her needs but the husband still owes her ketubah payments
D. When she has no marketable skills and cannot earn anything
Correct: B — Rav Ashi explains the trustee is needed when her earnings cover 'large things' (basics) but not 'small things' (extras) she was accustomed to in her father's house and can no longer forgo now that he has vowed.
