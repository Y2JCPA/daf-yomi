Fulfilling the Instructions of the Dead

Rav Ḥisda / Mar Ukva
Whether the father says 'Give a shekel' or 'Don't give more than a shekel,' the court gives the sons enough for all their needs.
Gemara Challenge
But R' Meir says it is a mitzva to fulfill the statements of the dead — how can we ignore the father's limit?
Resolution
R' Meir's principle applies to other matters. Here, the father certainly prefers his children be properly provided for — his limit was only to encourage thrift.
Principle We look past the literal words of the deceased to discern their true intent — a father always wants his children sustained.

Rafram: Minors' Transactions & the Steward (Apotropos)

Do minors' sales and acquisitions have legal effect?
↓
Is there a steward (apotropos) overseeing their affairs?
No Steward
Movable property: transactions valid
Real estate: transactions void
With Steward
All transactions void — even movable property
Source Rafram derives this from our mishna: 'Any action of a minor girl is nothing' — even generally, without a specific steward, a minor cannot transact in real estate.

R' Yosei: Vow Against Adornment — Poor vs. Wealthy

Poor Women

  • Must divorce only if he set no time limit on the vow
  • A time-limited vow is tolerable since she is not accustomed to elaborate adornment

Wealthy Women

  • If prohibited from adornment for 30 days, he must divorce
  • Wealthy women are accustomed to adorning themselves more elaborately
  • Going without is a greater deprivation for them
R' Yosei's Logic The hardship of a vow is measured relative to the woman's accustomed lifestyle, not by an absolute standard.

The Core Problem: How Can His Vow Override the Ketubah?

Gemara
He is obligated by ketubah to sustain her. A vow cannot override a prior obligation — just as her vow not to work for him doesn't take effect!
Initial Answer
Since he can say 'spend your earnings to sustain yourself,' his vow is as if he said that.
Challenge (Rav Huna / Rav)
But a wife can say 'I won't be sustained and won't work' — so by that logic, her vow should also take effect!
Revised Answer
He must explicitly tell her: 'Spend your earnings to sustain yourself.' The vow works only when he actually redirects her sustenance.
Key Distinction A mere theoretical ability ('he could say...') does not activate a vow. The husband must actually invoke his right to redirect her earnings.
