Ketubot 11 — Conversion of Minors & Status Presumptions

Mishna: Convert, captive, or freed maidservant under 3 years + 1 day → ketubah = 200 dinars (virgin status)
↓
Can a minor be converted by the court?
↓
Rav Huna
Yes — immerse with court consent; conversion is a privilege for a minor
Challenge
Perhaps licentiousness is preferable for a gentile, making conversion a disadvantage?
↓
Rav Huna answers: That concern applies only to an adult who has tasted prohibition. A minor has not — conversion is a privilege.
↓
Rav Yosef: Minor converts may protest at majority and annul conversion
↓
Once she reaches majority even one moment without protest → can no longer protest
Core Principle The court may act in a minor's interest (זכין לאדם שלא בפניו), and conversion is deemed beneficial for a child who has not yet experienced forbidden conduct.

Can a Minor Be Converted by the Court?

Rav Huna
A minor convert is immersed in a mikveh על דעת בית דין — with the consent of the court.
Gemara
What is Rav Huna teaching? We already know זכין לאדם שלא בפניו — one may act in a person's interest without their knowledge!
Rav Huna
You might think a gentile prefers licentiousness, so conversion is against his interests — like a Canaanite slave.
Rav Huna
That only applies to an adult who has tasted prohibition. A minor has not — for him, conversion is a privilege (זכות).
Gemara
Our Mishna supports this — it discusses converts under age 3, implying their conversion is valid!
Gemara
Rejected: The Mishna may refer to children who converted with their father — they are content with his decision.
Distinction An adult slave/gentile may prefer the freedom of fewer obligations. A minor has no such experience, so conversion is presumed beneficial.

Rav Yosef: Right of Protest at Majority

Rav Yosef: Minor converts, upon reaching majority, can protest and annul their conversion
↓
Abaye objects: If she can annul, why does she receive a ketubah of 200 dinars? She might revert to gentile status!
↓
She receives the ketubah only after reaching majority without protesting
↓
But even at majority — might she still protest later?
↓
Once she reaches majority even one moment without protesting → she can never protest again
Resolution The window to protest is instantaneous: the very first moment of majority. Silence = permanent acceptance of conversion.

Abaye vs. Rava: Why Different Objections?

Abaye's Objection

  • Objects from ketubah (200 dinars)
  • Cannot use Rava's source (fine for rape)
  • Reason: Fine exists so the sinner won't profit — it would be imposed regardless of conversion status
  • Ketubah is a better proof because it's a benefit for her

Rava's Objection

  • Objects from the fine (for rape of a convert)
  • Cannot use Abaye's source (ketubah)
  • Reason: Ketubah exists so she won't be trivial in his eyes — it protects the marriage, not just her
  • Fine is a better proof because it's a pure monetary benefit to her
Both Agree Both Abaye and Rava accept Rav Yosef's resolution: she collects only after reaching majority without protest, and silence for even one moment = permanent acceptance.

Amud B: Minor Boy's Intercourse — Rav vs. Shmuel

Rav
A minor boy who has intercourse with an adult woman renders her like mukat etz (hymen ruptured by wood) — it is not full intercourse.
Shmuel
A woman does not become mukat etz through flesh. Intercourse by a minor is still intercourse by flesh, not wood.
Rava (interpreting Mishna)
Adult man + girl under 3 = like poking a finger in an eye — a tear falls, another replaces it. The hymen regenerates.
Rava
Minor boy + adult woman = renders her as mukat etz. The dispute between R' Meir and the Rabbis is about actual mukat etz only.
Rava's Reading The Mishna lists three cases: (1) adult + girl under 3 → no effect; (2) minor boy + woman → mukat etz; (3) actual mukat etz → disputed between R' Meir (200) and Rabbis (100).
