Ketubot 11 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, why is the court authorized to convert a minor?
A. Because a minor has no legal standing to refuse
B. Because conversion is a privilege for one who hasn't tasted sin
C. Because the father's consent is sufficient
D. Because the Torah explicitly commands converting minors
Correct: B — Rav Huna teaches that unlike an adult who has experienced forbidden pleasures, a minor has not tasted sin, so conversion is a genuine privilege (זכות) — and one may act in a person's interest without their consent.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Yosef, what happens when a minor convert reaches majority?
A. The conversion automatically becomes permanent
B. They must undergo a second immersion to confirm
C. They have the ability to protest and annul the conversion
D. The court must re-evaluate whether conversion is still beneficial
Correct: C — Rav Yosef holds that minor converts may protest upon reaching majority. However, the Gemara clarifies that even one moment of silence at majority permanently forecloses the right to protest.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Abaye not use the case of the fine (for rape) to challenge Rav Yosef, as Rava does?
A. Because the fine is too small to be relevant
B. Because the fine exists so the sinner won't profit — it would be imposed regardless
C. Because Abaye doesn't believe in the fine at all
D. Because rape is not discussed in connection with converts
Correct: B — The fine's purpose is to penalize the rapist (שלא יהא חוטא נשכר). Since it exists to punish the sinner rather than to benefit the woman, its imposition doesn't prove anything about whether the woman's conversion is settled.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the dispute about mukat etz, what does R' Meir compare her to and why?
A. A non-virgin — because an action was performed on her
B. A grown woman (bogeret) — because no person caused the rupture
C. A captive woman — because her status is uncertain
D. A minor — because the rupture occurred before maturity
Correct: B — R' Meir likens mukat etz to a bogeret (mature woman) whose hymen loosened naturally — in both cases, no person performed an act of intercourse, so she retains virgin status and a 200-dinar ketubah.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rava's interpretation, what is the practical difference between R' Meir and the Rabbis when the husband was NOT aware his wife was mukat etz?
A. R' Meir gives 200 dinars; Rabbis give 100 dinars
B. Both agree she receives 200 dinars
C. R' Meir gives 200 dinars; Rabbis say she receives nothing (mistaken transaction)
D. Both agree she receives nothing
Correct: C — Rava holds that R' Meir always gives 200 dinars regardless of the husband's awareness, while the Rabbis say: if he knew — 100 dinars; if he didn't know — nothing at all, because the marriage was based on a false assumption (מקח טעות).
