Ketubot 78 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
When a woman inherits property BEFORE betrothal and then sells it, what do Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel hold?
A. Both agree she may sell it
B. Beit Shammai permit, Beit Hillel forbid
C. Both agree she may not sell it
D. Beit Hillel permit, Beit Shammai forbid
Correct: A — When property was inherited before betrothal, it came during a time of her full rights — both schools agree she may sell freely.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the School of R' Yannai say Beit Hillel holds the sale is valid after the fact for property inherited after betrothal?
A. Because the husband explicitly waived his rights
B. Because there is uncertainty (safek) whether the husband or wife has rights during betrothal
C. Because betrothal is not a real acquisition
D. Because movable property cannot be tracked
Correct: B — R' Yannai's school explains that during betrothal, it is uncertain who has property rights — so l'chatchilah she cannot sell, but b'dieved the sale stands.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was Rabban Gamliel's argument against restricting the sale of 'old property' inherited before marriage?
A. The Torah explicitly permits it
B. We are already uncomfortable restricting new property — don't add the burden of old property
C. A husband has no rights to any property
D. Betrothal is not legally binding
Correct: B — Rabban Gamliel's rhetorical argument: we are already 'ashamed' about restricting new property (inherited after marriage), so certainly we should not extend that restriction to old property she owned beforehand.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara distinguish the Mishna's ruling from the Usha ordinance?
A. The Mishna applies only to betrothed women; Usha applies to married women
B. The Mishna deals with produce value during her lifetime; Usha allows repossessing the land itself, even after her death
C. The Mishna is Beit Shammai; Usha is Beit Hillel
D. There is no distinction — they are the same ruling
Correct: B — The Mishna's case is about collecting produce value while she is alive. The Usha ordinance goes further — the husband repossesses the land itself, even after her death, as her heir.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Yosei b. R' Ḥanina, what does R' Shimon mean by 'property known to the husband'?
A. Property listed in the ketubah
B. Property the husband personally inspected
C. Land, which cannot be concealed
D. Any property worth more than a certain amount
Correct: C — R' Yosei b. R' Ḥanina explains that 'known property' refers to land (קרקע), which by nature cannot be hidden, while 'unknown property' refers to movable goods that can be concealed.
