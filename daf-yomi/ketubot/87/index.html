What Oath Did the Husband Exempt?

Rav Yehuda (Rav)
The exemption refers to a woman who became a steward (apotropa) during her husband's lifetime — she managed his property and is exempt from an oath about it
Rav Naḥman (Rabba b. Avuh)
It refers to a woman who claims partial payment of her ketubah — she took some money early and is exempt from swearing she took no more
Rav Mordekhai
The stewardship view is problematic — how could she anticipate being appointed steward and request the exemption in advance?
Rav Ashi
We apply Rav Yehuda's teaching to the later clause of the Mishnah — about oath exemption from heirs after the husband's death, not before marriage
Resolution Rav Ashi resolves the difficulty by applying the stewardship ruling to the case of a widow dealing with her husband's heirs, not the pre-marriage exemption

"Not an Oath" vs. "Clear from an Oath"

Rabba (R' Ḥiyya)

  • "Not a vow / not an oath" → He cannot, but heirs can administer
  • "Clear from a vow / oath" → Neither he nor heirs can administer
  • Meaning: 'You are clear from any oath, no matter who seeks it'

Rav Yosef (R' Ḥiyya)

  • "Not a vow / not an oath" → He cannot, but heirs can (same)
  • "Clear from a vow / oath" → Either he or heirs CAN administer
  • Meaning: 'Clear yourself of suspicion — take an oath!'
Opposite Readings! Rabba and Rav Yosef both quote R' Ḥiyya but reach opposite conclusions about the phrase 'clear from an oath'

Is Vitiating a Ketubah a Torah Oath?

Rami bar Ḥama
The oath of a woman who vitiates her ketubah is a Torah oath — it's a classic partial admission (modeh b'miktzat): husband claims 200, she admits to 100
Rava
Two refutations:
1) Torah oaths are taken to avoid paying — here she takes an oath and collects
2) No Torah oath applies to liens on land, and a ketubah involves land
Rava
Rather, this oath is Rabbinic — the payer remembers precisely, the payee does not. The Sages imposed the oath so she will be precise in her accounting
Key Distinction Torah oath: one swears and does not pay · Rabbinic oath here: she swears and collects
