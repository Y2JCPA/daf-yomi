Ketubot 87 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Rav Ashi say Rav Yehuda's ruling about stewardship applies to the later clause of the Mishnah?
A. Because a wife could anticipate becoming a steward before marriage
B. Because the later clause discusses a widow dealing with heirs, where stewardship is foreseeable
C. Because the earlier clause was already explained by Rav Naḥman
D. Because stewardship only begins after the husband dies
Correct: B — Rav Ashi resolves Rav Mordekhai's objection: in the later clause about heirs, it makes sense the husband exempted her from oath about stewardship during his lifetime, as that situation was foreseeable.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava, why is the oath of a woman who vitiates her ketubah NOT a Torah oath?
A. Because women are exempt from Torah oaths
B. Because the ketubah is only a Rabbinic institution
C. Because Torah oaths are for those who swear and don't pay, and Torah oaths don't apply to land liens
D. Because she is the plaintiff, not the defendant
Correct: C — Rava gives two reasons: (1) Torah oaths are taken by those who swear and are exempted from payment, but she swears and collects; (2) Torah oaths don't apply to land liens, and a ketubah involves a lien on land.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the practical difference between Rabba's and Rav Yosef's interpretations of 'clear from an oath'?
A. Rabba says it means total exemption; Rav Yosef says it means she must take the oath
B. Rabba applies it only to vows; Rav Yosef applies it to oaths
C. They disagree only about whether the husband himself can administer the oath
D. There is no practical difference — both agree heirs are bound
Correct: A — Rabba reads 'clear from an oath' as a total exemption — no one can administer the oath. Rav Yosef reads it as an instruction to clear herself BY taking an oath, so both husband and heirs can require it.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Rav Mattana hold that heirs cannot administer an oath for the period between the husband's death and burial?
A. Because the husband's exemption extends past death
B. Because she has no legal standing during that period
C. Because urgent matters like burial don't require precise accounting, similar to selling orphans' property without announcement
D. Because the heirs have not yet inherited at that point
Correct: C — Rav Mattana compares it to the Neharde'a principle: for urgent matters like head tax, children's sustenance, and burial, the court doesn't demand precise accounting — so she can't be held to an oath about funeral expenses.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
A woman says her ketubah is worth 100 dinars, not the 1,000 written in the document. What is the halakha?
A. She must take an oath like one who vitiates her ketubah
B. She collects 100 without an oath, but there is a problem — the document states a false amount
C. She collects nothing because she invalidated the document
D. She collects the full 1,000 since the document is binding
Correct: B — The baraita rules she collects without an oath. However, the Gemara raises a difficulty: since she admits the document records a false amount, the ketubah is like a broken shard — how can she collect with it?
