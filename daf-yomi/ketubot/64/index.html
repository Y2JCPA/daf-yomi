↓
Did the moredet seize her possessions?

We do not take it away from her
She did not seize
We do not give it to her
↓
Court delays her get for 12 months — no sustenance during this period
Principle When the halacha is unresolved, the rule of seizure (תפיסה) determines the outcome — possession creates presumptive right.

Iggeret Mered: Betrothed vs. Yevama

Shmuel
The court writes an iggeret mered (letter of rebellion) for a betrothed woman who rebels.
Shmuel
But the court does not write one for a yevama (widow awaiting yavam).
Baraita
Objection! The baraita says it applies equally — betrothed, married, menstruating, ill, or yevama!
Gemara
The baraita refers to where he asked to marry and she refuses. Shmuel refers to where she asked and he refuses.
Resolution R' Tachalifa bar Avimi in Shmuel's name: If he asks and she rebels — the court responds. If she asks and he refuses — the court does not add to her ketubah.

Chalitza vs. Yibum: When Does the Court Act?

Yavam Requests Chalitza

  • She refuses chalitza → court WRITES iggeret mered against her
  • His valid claim: 'She is attached to me — no one else will marry me'
  • R' Yochanan: Court responds to his request

Yavam Requests Yibum

  • She refuses yibum → court does NOT write iggeret mered
  • Court tells him: 'Go marry another woman'
  • Shmuel: Court does not compel her in this case
Key Distinction With chalitza refusal, the yavam is truly stuck — potential wives fear the unresolved levirate bond. With yibum refusal, he can simply marry someone else.

Why Is a Rebellious Wife's Penalty Greater?

R' Chiyya bar Yosef
Why is the moredet's fine greater than that of the rebellious husband?
Shmuel
Go learn from the marketplace — who hires whose services? A man suffers more from lack of intimacy.
Shmuel
Additionally, his desire is visible externally — he suffers both pain and shame. Hers is internal and not obvious.
Practical Halacha The asymmetric penalties reflect the Gemara's assessment of differential suffering — the greater the deprivation experienced, the greater the penalty for causing it.
