Ketubot 64 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
When the halacha about a moredet's worn clothes was left unresolved, what rule did the Gemara apply?
A. She keeps everything automatically
B. If she seized it we don't take it; if not, we don't give it
C. The husband keeps all possessions
D. The court divides them equally
Correct: B — When a halachic dilemma is unresolved, the rule of seizure (tafasta) applies — the possessor has the advantage, but the court won't proactively transfer property.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Shmuel, why does the court NOT write an iggeret mered for a yevama who refuses yibum?
A. Because yibum is only a mitzvah d'rabbanan
B. Because the yavam can marry another woman instead
C. Because the yevama has no ketubah
D. Because the yavam must perform chalitza first
Correct: B — The Gemara initially suggests telling the yavam to marry someone else, but ultimately distinguishes: refusing chalitza traps him (no one will marry him), while refusing yibum does not.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does the phrase 'a staff in my hand and a hoe for burial' refer to?
A. A wife's claim for agricultural tools
B. A wife's desire for children to support her in old age and bury her
C. A husband's obligation to provide work implements
D. A metaphor for the get document
Correct: B — This poignant phrase expresses a woman's claim that she wants children who will care for her in old age (the 'staff') and attend to her burial (the 'hoe') — a valid basis for the court to compel her husband.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Mishna, how much wheat must a husband provide his wife weekly when feeding her through a trustee?
A. 1 kav
B. 2 kav
C. 4 kav
D. Half a kav
Correct: B — The Mishna states 2 kav of wheat (or 4 kav of barley as a cheaper alternative). The Gemara then struggles to match this with known opinions about meal sizes.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why is the penalty for a rebellious wife (moredet) greater than for a rebellious husband?
A. Because the wife has a Torah obligation to serve her husband
B. Because a man suffers more from lack of intimacy and his desire is externally visible
C. Because the ketubah specifically penalizes wives more
D. Because Beit Din has more authority over women
Correct: B — Shmuel explains with a marketplace analogy: the man suffers more from deprivation (as evidenced by market dynamics), and additionally his desire is externally noticeable, causing both pain and shame.
