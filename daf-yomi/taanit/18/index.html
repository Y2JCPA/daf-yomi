⚖️ How Far Do Megillat Ta'anit Restrictions Extend?

Rabbi Meir
The prohibition applies only on the listed day itself. The day before and day after are not restricted — and Megillat Ta'anit was annulled once the Temple was destroyed.
Rabbi Yosei
The prohibition extends to the day before and the day after each listed date. Megillat Ta'anit remains fully in force today.
Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel
The doubled language — "on them, on them" — in the phrases 'not to eulogize on them' and 'not to fast on them' teaches that restrictions apply on the listed days only, not before or after.
Final Halakha Rav rules like Rabbi Yosei. Shmuel initially ruled like Rabbi Meir, then revised to Rabbi Yosei after Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel's lenient view emerged. Rabbi Yoḥanan also rules like Rabbi Yosei.

📅 Three Positions on Megillat Ta'anit Restrictions

Rabbi Meir

  • Restriction: listed day only
  • Day before: permitted
  • Day after: permitted
  • Rationale: Megillat Ta'anit annulled post-Temple
  • Supported by: Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel's 'on them, on them'

Rabbi Yosei

  • Restriction: day before + listed day + day after
  • Day before: prohibited (preparation for joy)
  • Day after: prohibited (continuation of joy)
  • Rationale: Megillat Ta'anit still in force
  • Followed by: Rav, Shmuel (revised), Rabbi Yoḥanan

Practical Consequence

  • 1 Nisan listed → 29 Adar & 2 Nisan restricted
  • 8 Nisan–end of Passover listed → 7 Nisan restricted
  • 28 Adar listed → 27 Adar & 1 Nisan restricted
  • Each day stands independently if prior day canceled
  • Explains why 1 Nisan is listed separately in Megillat Ta'anit

🏛️ Amoraic Dispute: Who Rules Like Whom?

Rav
Halakha follows Rabbi Yosei. Fasting and eulogizing are prohibited on the day before and day after every date in Megillat Ta'anit.
Shmuel (initially)
Halakha follows Rabbi Meir — the most lenient opinion. Restrictions apply only on the listed day itself.
Shmuel (revised)
Upon learning that Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel also holds a lenient view, Rabbi Meir is no longer uniquely most lenient. Shmuel revised: Halakha follows Rabbi Yosei.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Halakha follows Rabbi Yosei. (Transmitted via Bali ← Rabbi Ḥiyya bar Abba ← Rabbi Yoḥanan.) Though Rabbi Yoḥanan normally follows unattributed mishna, the relevant mishna is re-interpreted to apply to a different case.
Key Insight Shmuel changed his ruling not because of new facts, but because the halakhic landscape shifted — once Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel's lenient view was known, Rabbi Meir was no longer uniquely most lenient, removing Shmuel's original justification.

🗡️ Origin of Nicanor's Day — 13 Adar

Nicanor, a Greek general (iparkhei), would wave his hand mockingly toward Jerusalem every day, declaring: "When will this city fall into my hands so I can trample it?"
↓
The Hasmoneans marched out to battle and, with God's help, defeated him decisively in combat.
↓
They cut off his thumbs and big toes — the very hands that waved and the feet that marched arrogantly — and hung them opposite Jerusalem.
↓
Nicanor himself said: "With this mouth that spoke arrogantly and these hands that waved mockingly — let them be shamed."
↓
This victory on 13 Adar was declared a day of celebration: Nicanor's Day. This is why 12 Adar (the day before) is also restricted — and why Trajan's Day on 12 Adar being annulled becomes halakhically significant.

👑 The Martyrdom of Luleyanus and Pappas

יב באדר — יום טריינוס
12 Adar — Trajan's Day (Later Annulled) Trajan sought to execute the leaders Luleyanus and his brother Pappas in Laodicea.

Trajan challenged them: "If you are truly from the nation of Hananiah, Mishael, and Azariah — let your God come and save you as He saved them from Nebuchadnezzar!"

Luleyanus and Pappas replied: "Hananiah, Mishael, and Azariah were wholly righteous, worthy of a miracle. Nebuchadnezzar was a true king — worthy of a miracle being performed through him. But you are a mere commoner, not worthy of such a miracle. And we — we have sinned and have been condemned to destruction by the Omnipresent."

Trajan killed them immediately. Before they even moved from the execution site, two Roman officials (diyoflei) arrived from Rome — with orders to remove Trajan from power — and split his skull with their axes.

The day of their execution, 12 Adar, was initially declared a day of mourning (Trajan's Day). It was later annulled, which is why fasting on 12 Adar is discussed in the context of Nicanor's Day.

🌱 New Mishna: When to Decree Severe Communal Fasts

Has the first seasonal rainfall fallen?
↓
❌ No rain at all
Follow the standard graduated sequence: 3 fasts → 3 more fasts → 7 severe fasts (as in Chapter One)
⚠️ Rain fell but crops were harmed
Skip straight to severe fasts immediately — no gradual buildup
↓
What counts as "crops harmed"?
↓
🌿 Vegetation changed appearance due to disease
Severe fasts immediately
🌳 Enough for trees but not vegetation (or vice versa)
Severe fasts immediately
💧 Cisterns, ditches, or caves not filled
Severe fasts immediately
