QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the final halakha on Daf 18, whose opinion do Rav, Shmuel, and Rabbi Yoḥanan all follow regarding Megillat Ta'anit?
A. Rabbi Meir — restriction applies only on the listed day itself
B. Rabbi Yosei — restriction extends to the day before and the day after
C. Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel — the doubled 'on them, on them' limits to the day itself
D. Rav Ashi — each day stands independently regardless of adjacent days
Correct: B — All three amoraim rule like Rabbi Yosei: the prohibitions on fasting and eulogizing extend to the day before and the day after each date listed in Megillat Ta'anit.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Shmuel change his ruling from Rabbi Meir to Rabbi Yosei?
A. He received a new oral tradition from his teacher Rav
B. He realized Rabbi Meir's position contradicted an unattributed mishna
C. Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel also held a lenient view, so Rabbi Meir was no longer uniquely most lenient
D. He discovered that the practice in all Babylonian communities followed Rabbi Yosei
Correct: C — Shmuel initially ruled like Rabbi Meir because he was most lenient. Once he learned that Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel also held a lenient view, Rabbi Meir was no longer the uniquely most lenient opinion — removing Shmuel's justification for following him.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was the origin of Nicanor's Day on 13 Adar?
A. The defeat of Trajan's army by Jewish rebels in Laodicea
B. The day the decree against Torah study was rescinded by the Greeks
C. The Hasmoneans defeated the Greek general Nicanor, who had daily waved his hand mockingly at Jerusalem
D. The restoration of the daily Temple offering (tamid) by the Maccabees
Correct: C — Nicanor, a Greek general (iparkhei), daily waved his hand mockingly toward Jerusalem. The Hasmoneans defeated him, cut off his thumbs and big toes, and hung them opposite Jerusalem. This victory on 13 Adar became a commemorative day.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When Trajan challenged Luleyanus and Pappas to produce a miracle, what was the core of their response?
A. They prayed aloud for God to strike Trajan with a plague immediately
B. Hananiah, Mishael & Azariah were wholly righteous; Trajan was a mere commoner unworthy of a miracle, and they themselves had sinned
C. They quoted the verse 'His blood be upon us and our children' to invoke divine protection
D. They argued that miracles only occur in the Land of Israel, not in Laodicea
Correct: B — Luleyanus and Pappas said: Hananiah, Mishael, and Azariah were wholly righteous and Nebuchadnezzar was a true king worthy of a miracle. Trajan was a mere commoner — not worthy of a miracle through him — and they themselves had sins deserving punishment.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the new Mishna introduced at the end of Daf 18, when does the community skip the gradual three-stage fast sequence and begin severe fasts immediately?
A. When no rain has fallen at all by the first of Kislev
B. When rain fell but vegetation changed appearance due to disease, or cisterns were not filled
C. When a fast day falls on Rosh Chodesh, Hanukkah, or Purim
D. When the community is struck by a plague or epidemic
Correct: B — The graduated sequence applies only when the first rainfall has not come. If rain fell but crops were damaged by disease, if rain was enough for trees but not vegetation (or vice versa), or if cisterns and ditches were not filled — the community begins severe fasts immediately without the gradual buildup.
