QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Shmuel bar Naḥmani, why was Jephthah's vow worse in outcome than those of Eliezer or Saul?
A. His vow was explicitly forbidden by Torah law
B. He made the vow after the battle, not before
C. Pride prevented him from consulting Phineas, so the vow could not be annulled
D. He did not trust God to send a worthy person from his house
Correct: C — Phineas said Jephthah should come to him; Jephthah said Phineas should come to him. Neither yielded. The vow was never annulled — Phineas lost the divine spirit and Jephthah died limb by limb. The tragedy was caused by mutual pride, not the vow itself.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
When the Congregation of Israel asked God to be 'as the rain,' what was God's response?
A. God accepted the comparison as a sign of Israel's devotion
B. God said rain is sometimes undesired, and offered the sun as a superior analogy
C. God rebuked Israel for comparing Him to a natural phenomenon
D. God promised to send abundant rain as a sign of His presence
Correct: B — God explained that rain, while beneficial, is sometimes undesired — e.g., during harvest. The Divine Presence is always desired. God responded that He would be like the sun (Isaiah 30:26) — universally and consistently beneficial.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rav Ḥisda resolve the contradiction between the baraita (Rabbi Yehuda: until Passover has passed) and the mishna (mention stops at the first Festival day)?
A. The baraita applies only in Babylonia; the mishna applies in Eretz Yisrael
B. The baraita refers to the request for rain; the mishna refers only to the mention
C. Two tanna'im recorded different opinions in Rabbi Yehuda's name
D. The baraita is a later ruling that supersedes the mishna
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda distinguishes: the baraita discusses the request for rain (which continues until the end of Passover), while the mishna discusses the mention of rain (which stops at the first Festival day). Although Ulla challenged this distinction, it remains the primary attempted resolution.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Rav rule that in the Diaspora one begins the mention of rain on the second day of Shemini Atzeret, not the first?
A. Because Diaspora Jews are not obligated to mention rain at all on Sukkot
B. To avoid treating the first day as merely the seventh day of Sukkot (putting the lulav down), which would defile its sanctity as an independent holiday
C. Because the second day aligns with the calendar of Eretz Yisrael
D. Because the first day is entirely devoted to the Musaf prayer
Correct: B — Shmuel challenged Rav: if you treat the first day of the Eighth Day as sanctified (a holiday), you cannot also treat it as the day for putting the lulav down (seventh day of Sukkot). Therefore begin the mention of rain on the second day, once the first day's full sanctity has passed.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rava say about a Torah scholar who grows angry, and what verse supports this?
A. He has sinned and must bring a guilt offering; source: Leviticus 5
B. He is presumed to be under spiritual attack; source: Psalms 91
C. It is his Torah study that angers him — like fire it cannot be suppressed; source: Jeremiah 23:29
D. He should be removed from his position until he repents; source: Proverbs 16
Correct: C — Rava teaches that a Torah scholar's anger is driven by his intense engagement with Torah, which burns within him like fire. The verse 'Is not My word like fire?' (Jeremiah 23:29) demonstrates that Torah's intensity naturally erupts — so we give the scholar the benefit of the doubt.
