Taanit 19 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Akiva, what is the obligation of communities surrounding a plague-stricken city?
A. Fast and cry out, identical to the city itself
B. Fast alongside the city but do not cry out
C. Cry out but do not fast
D. Neither fast nor cry out — only pray privately
Correct: C — Rabbi Akiva disagrees with the Tanna Kamma: surrounding areas cry out (blow the shofar) but do not fast. The afflicted city itself both fasts and cries out.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
If rain falls before sunrise on a community fast day for lack of rain, what is the ruling?
A. They must still complete the fast until nightfall
B. They fast only until midday as a compromise
C. They need not complete the fast
D. They schedule a makeup fast the following week
Correct: C — The Mishna rules: rain before sunrise → need not complete the fast. Rain after sunrise → must complete it. In Lod, rain before midday led Rabbi Tarfon to declare the rest of the day a festival.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What did Shimon ben Shetaḥ threaten he would have done to Ḥoni HaMa'agel, were it not for his unique relationship with God?
A. Praised him before the entire Sanhedrin
B. Declared him a prophet of Israel
C. Decreed an additional fast in his name
D. Issued a decree of ostracism (נידוי) against him
Correct: D — Shimon ben Shetaḥ said he would have ostracized Ḥoni for his audacious ultimatum to God — but could not, because Ḥoni was beloved by God like a son who pesters his father and always gets what he wants.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the baraita, until when do they sound the alarm over trees that have not received enough rain?
A. Until Sukkot, at the beginning of the rainy season
B. Until Purim, midway through the year
C. Near Passover, after which rain no longer benefits trees
D. Until Shavuot, when grain harvest concludes
Correct: C — They cry out for trees only until near Passover — rain that falls after that point will no longer benefit trees. For cisterns and caves they continue to sound the alarm even beyond Passover.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, in which situation do they cry out immediately without waiting for formal drought thresholds to be met?
A. When a se'a of grain costs a sela but supply is available
B. When produce must be imported from another province
C. When forty days pass between one rainfall and the next
D. When money is scarce and produce is also expensive simultaneously
Correct: D — Rabbi Yoḥanan rules: when money is expensive (scarce) and produce is also expensive, the combination of poverty and shortage is an immediate emergency — no formal drought threshold is needed before crying out.
