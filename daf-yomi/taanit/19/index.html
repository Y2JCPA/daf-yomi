⭕ Ḥoni HaMa'agel: The Circle-Drawer

חוני המעגל — עגל עוגה ועמד בתוכה ואמר: רבונו של עולם, בניך שמו פניהם עלי
Central Story of Daf 19 In one of the Talmud's most dramatic episodes, Ḥoni HaMa'agel drew a circle on the ground, stood inside it, and swore to God he would not move until proper rain fell. Drizzle came — he rejected it. A downpour came — he rejected it. Finally, steady, gentle rain fell and filled the land. Shimon ben Shetaḥ rebuked him: 'Were you not Ḥoni, I would have had you ostracized — but you nag God like a son who nags his father, and He does your bidding.'

🏙️ Surrounding Areas: Fast or Cry Out?

Tanna Kamma
The afflicted city fasts and cries out. Surrounding communities fast in solidarity — but do not cry out by blowing the shofar.
Rabbi Akiva
Surrounding communities cry out — but do not fast. Crying out is the primary communal obligation; fasting belongs to the city directly afflicted.
Plague Threshold A plague of pestilence is defined as: a city of 500 fighting men loses 3 people in 3 consecutive days — not all at once, not spread over time.

🌾 Drought, Famine, and When to Cry Out

Drought (בצורת)

  • A se'a of grain costs a sela and is scarce (Rav Yehuda)
  • A se'a for a sela but available — prices are high but supply exists (Rabbi Ḥanina)
  • Money is plentiful; produce is expensive → cry out

Famine (רעב)

  • Produce must be imported from province to province
  • Local supply has broken down entirely
  • More severe crisis than drought pricing alone

Cry Out Immediately

  • Money is scarce and produce is also expensive (Rabbi Yoḥanan)
  • Double crisis: poverty + shortage = no delay
  • Normal drought thresholds do not apply — act at once

⭕ Ḥoni HaMa'agel — Step by Step

Step 1: Ḥoni draws a circle (עוגה) on the ground and stands inside it — a visual declaration that he will not leave until God responds
↓
Step 2: He swears: 'Master of the Universe — I will not move from here until You have mercy on Your children and send rain!'
↓
Step 3: Light drizzle falls — Ḥoni says: 'Not for this did I ask — but for rain that fills cisterns, pits, and caves!'
↓
Step 4: A violent downpour falls — Ḥoni says: 'Not this either — I asked for rain of goodwill, blessing, and generosity!'
↓
Step 5: Steady, normal rain falls and continues until Jerusalem floods — people flee to the Temple Mount and beg him to stop
↓
Step 6: Shimon ben Shetaḥ sends word: 'Were you not Ḥoni, I would ostracize you — but you are like a son who pesters his father, and his father grants his every wish'
