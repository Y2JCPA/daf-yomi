Taanit 6 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
The word yoreh is interpreted positively as deriving from which Hebrew root?
A. Yeriya — shooting/destruction
B. Moreh — teaching and instructing
C. Rova — penetrating the earth
D. Mal — circumcising
Correct: B — The positive reading derives yoreh from moreh (to teach): the rain instructs people to plaster roofs and bring in produce. Yoel 2:23 uses the word moreh with 'in His kindness,' confirming that yoreh is a blessing, not destructive.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yosei (whose opinion is the halakha), what is the LATEST possible date for the first rainfall?
A. 17 Marḥeshvan
B. 23 Marḥeshvan
C. Rosh Chodesh Kislev
D. 7 Kislev
Correct: C — Rabbi Yosei provides three dates: earliest is 17 Marḥeshvan, intermediate is 23 Marḥeshvan, and the latest is Rosh Chodesh Kislev (1 Kislev). Rav Ḥisda rules the halakha follows Rabbi Yosei.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rav Zevid says the second rainfall matters for which halacha involving olives?
A. Tithes must be separated from olives after the second rain
B. Owners who have not harvested must allow the poor to collect gleanings and pe'a
C. Olives may first be pressed into oil after the second rain
D. Olives become muktze on Shabbat once the second rain falls
Correct: B — After the second rain falls on olive trees, owners who still have not collected their olives lose exclusive rights — the poor may now take gleanings, forgotten sheaves, and pe'a from those trees, as taught by Rav Zevid from a mishna.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How deep must the first rain (yoreh) penetrate the ground to qualify halachically, per Rabbi Abbahu?
A. Three fingerbreadths
B. Half a handbreadth
C. One handbreadth
D. One cubit
Correct: C — Rabbi Abbahu rules that for rain to count as the first rainfall, it must saturate the earth to a depth of one handbreadth. The second rain's standard is different — it must be enough to seal the cracks in the ground.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Abbahu, at what moment may one begin reciting a blessing over rain?
A. As soon as the first drops fall
B. After one full hour of rainfall
C. When puddles form and the water below splashes upward to meet the falling rain
D. Only after Shabbat is over (Havdalah)
Correct: C — Rabbi Abbahu uses the metaphor of a groom going out to meet the bride: once puddles form and water splashes upward from below to meet the descending rain, the condition is met and one recites the blessing. This represents the rain and earth 'greeting' each other.
