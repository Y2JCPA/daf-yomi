📅 When Does the First Rain Fall?

Rabbi Meir
The first rain (yoreh) falls in Marḥeshvan and the last rain (malkosh) in Nisan.
The Rabbis
The time of the first rain is in Kislev, not Marḥeshvan.
Rabbi Yosei
Earliest: 17 Marḥeshvan. Intermediate: 23 Marḥeshvan. Latest: Rosh Chodesh Kislev. Torah scholars resume wearing sandals after the latest date.
Rav Ḥisda
The halakha follows Rabbi Yosei. Ameimar taught: on the 3rd of Marḥeshvan one begins requesting rain; Rabban Gamliel says on the 7th.
Who are 'the Rabbis'? Rav Ḥisda identifies the anonymous 'Rabbis' who say the first rain is in Kislev as Rabbi Yosei — whose opinion the halakha follows, with his three-tiered date range.

🌱 Yoreh — Blessing or Destruction?

Positive Interpretation
Yoreh derives from moreh (instructs): the rain teaches people to plaster their roofs, bring in produce from fields, and prepare their homes for winter.
Negative Interpretation
Yoreh derives from yeriya (shooting): the rain shoots down destructively, knocking fruit from trees, washing away seeds, and uprooting them — a damaging force.
Resolution — Yoel 2:23 'He has given you the moreh in His kindness' — the word moreh proves yoreh is the rain of blessing and divine generosity, not destruction. The negative reading is rejected.

⚖️ The Four Halachot of the Second Rainfall

1. Vow Duration (Gemara, Amud B:1)
One who vows 'until the rains' — the vow lasts from the first through the second rainfall. This window is the halachic 'time of rains.'
↓
2. Gleanings & Pe'a for the Poor (Rav Zevid)
After the second rain falls on olive trees, owners who have not harvested must open their fields — the poor may now collect gleanings, forgotten sheaves, and pe'a from those trees.
↓
3. Walking on Private Field Paths (Rav Pappa)
One may use paths through private fields without liability — until the second rainfall. Once the second rain falls and the ground becomes saturated, using these paths causes damage and is prohibited.
↓
4. Removing Shevi'it Produce (Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak)
Sabbatical Year produce must be removed from one's home once the same species is no longer found in the fields. The second rainfall determines when fields are depleted of that produce.

📏 Rainfall Sufficiency: First Rain vs Second Rain

First Rain (Yoreh)

  • Must penetrate the earth to a depth of one handbreadth
  • Enough to moisten the soil for germination
  • This is the minimum for yoreh to count halachically
  • Based on Rabbi Abbahu: revia means 'that which penetrates [rove'a] the earth to bear fruit'

Second Rain

  • Must be sufficient to seal cracks in the ground
  • Like a cloak closing shut — closes the surface fissures of the soil
  • Deeper, more sustained soaking than first rain
  • Triggers all four halachot: vows, gleanings, paths, shevi'it

🙏 Blessing Over Rain — Conditions and Formula

Rain begins to fall
↓
Insufficient — No Blessing Yet
Water has not splashed up from the ground — condition not met
Sufficient — Bless Now
Puddles form: water below splashes up to meet rain from above — like a groom going out to meet the bride
↓
Recite the blessing over rain
↓
Rav (via Rav Yehuda)
'We thank You, O Lord our God, for each and every drop that You have made fall for us'
Rabbi Yoḥanan's Conclusion
'If our mouth were as full of song as the sea... we still could not thank You sufficiently'
