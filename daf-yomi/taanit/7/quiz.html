5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Rabbi Abbahu says the day of rain is greater than the resurrection of the dead. What is his reason?
A. Rain occurs more frequently and is therefore more important
B. Rain benefits both the righteous and the wicked, while resurrection benefits only the righteous
C. Rain is mentioned in the Torah more times than resurrection
D. Rain sustains life on earth while resurrection only restores past life
Correct: B — Rabbi Abbahu's reasoning is inclusive universality: rain's blessing extends to all humanity — righteous and wicked alike — whereas resurrection is reserved for the righteous only.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rav Yehuda use Deuteronomy 32:2 to prove that the day of rain equals the day the Torah was given?
A. The verse was spoken by Moses on the same day he received the Torah
B. The word lekaḥ in the verse means Torah, and the same verse describes it 'dropping as the rain'
C. Rain fell on Mount Sinai the day the Torah was given
D. The numerical value of 'rain' equals the numerical value of 'Torah'
Correct: B — Rav Yehuda applies a gezera shava: lekaḥ in Deut. 32:2 (describing rain-like doctrine) equals lekaḥ in Prov. 4:2 ('I give you good lekaḥ — my Torah'), equating rain with Torah-giving.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rabba bar bar Ḥana compares Torah to fire. What specific lesson about study does he draw from this comparison?
A. Torah purifies the soul the way fire purifies metal
B. Torah study should be intense and passionate like a flame
C. Just as fire needs multiple sticks of wood to ignite, Torah thrives in group study — not when studied alone
D. Torah will consume you if you approach it without proper preparation
Correct: C — From Jer. 23:29 ('Is not My word like fire'), Rabba bar bar Ḥana teaches that just as a single stick cannot sustain fire, a lone scholar cannot sustain Torah — communal study is essential.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Gemara, which sin does Rabbi Shimon ben Pazi identify as causing rain to be withheld?
A. Robbery of the poor
B. Neglect of prayer
C. Nullification of tithes
D. Slander and evil speech
Correct: D — Rabbi Shimon ben Pazi derives this from Prov. 25:23: 'A backbiting tongue brings an angry countenance' — the tongue that slanders causes God's 'countenance' to withdraw the rain.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Ḥanina bar Idi say Torah is specifically compared to water (not wine or fire)?
A. Water is transparent — Torah must be studied openly and publicly
B. Just as water flows from high places to low places, Torah rests only upon the humble
C. Water is free to all — Torah must be taught without payment
D. Water cleanses impurity — Torah atones for sin
Correct: B — Rabbi Ḥanina bar Idi uses water's natural downward flow as a metaphor for humility: Torah, like water, cannot rest on someone spiritually 'elevated' by arrogance — only on those who make themselves low.
