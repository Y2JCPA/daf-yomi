📐 Forty-Day Calculation: Proving the Ninth of Av

29 Sivan — Moses dispatches the twelve spies (baraita)
↓
Tammuz: full 30-day month (Abaye — from Lam 1:15: "He called an appointed time against me")
↓
30 days (Tammuz) + 9 days (Av) + 1 departure day = exactly 40 days → return on 9 Av
↓
Israel weeps that night — Rabba/R. Yoḥanan: that night was the eve of Ninth of Av

"You wept for nothing — I set this night as weeping for generations"

🔄 Month of Av vs. Month of Adar — Fortune's Mirror

☹️ Month of Av

  • Decrease acts of rejoicing (Rav)
  • Israel's fortune is bad (מַזָּל רַע)
  • Avoid litigation with gentiles (Rav Pappa)
  • Week of 9 Av: no haircuts, no laundering
  • Spies' weeping decreed; Temples destroyed; Beitar fell; Jerusalem plowed

😊 Month of Adar

  • Increase acts of rejoicing (Rav)
  • Israel's fortune is good (מַזָּל טוֹב)
  • Make yourself available for litigation (Rav Pappa)
  • Purim joy reaches its height
  • Mirror image of Av — same teaching, opposite direction

🧺 Laundering After Tisha B'Av: How Long Does the Prohibition Last?

Rav Naḥman
The prohibition applies to laundering and wearing freshly laundered clothes before the Ninth of Av. On the Ninth of Av itself, one may wear already-laundered garments.
Rav Hamnuna
Objection from the Mishna! On Thursday of that week, these actions are permitted for Shabbat's honor — implying the rest of the week they are prohibited, not just before 9 Av.
Rav
Laundering is prohibited only before the Ninth of Av. Once the fast concludes, laundering is entirely permitted.
Shmuel
Even after the Ninth of Av, laundering remains prohibited for the rest of that week. The fine laundering (gihutz) practiced in Babylonia equals plain laundering in Eretz Yisrael — it is included in the ban.
Resolution The Thursday permission in the Mishna refers specifically to washing garments and setting them aside until Shabbat. The baraita confirms: it is prohibited to launder before 9 Av even intending to wear the clothes afterward.

Midrashic Tradition — Final Moments of the First Temple When the Temple was destroyed, young priests gathered with the Temple keys in their hands. They ascended to the roof of the Sanctuary and cried out: "Master of the Universe! Since we were not worthy to be Your faithful treasurers, let Your keys be returned to You!"

They threw the keys heavenward — and a hand emerged from heaven and received them. The priests then leaped into the flames below. The prophet Isaiah lamented over them: "Your slain are not slain with the sword, nor dead in battle" (Isaiah 22:2) — meaning they chose the flames of sanctification over the sword of the enemy.
