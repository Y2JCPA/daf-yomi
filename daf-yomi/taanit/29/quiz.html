Taanit 29 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the baraita, on what date did Moses send the spies to scout Eretz Yisrael?
A. 1st of Tammuz
B. 29th of Sivan
C. 15th of Av
D. 1st of Av
Correct: B — The baraita states: 'On the twenty-ninth of Sivan, Moses sent the spies.' With Tammuz a full 30-day month, forty days later lands precisely on the Ninth of Av.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What did God say when Israel wept on the night of the Ninth of Av after hearing the spies' fearful report?
A. You have sinned and shall wander forty years in the wilderness
B. You wept for nothing — I will establish this as a weeping for all generations
C. Return to Egypt, for the land is forfeit to you
D. Fast and repent and I will rescind the decree
Correct: B — Rabba said in the name of Rabbi Yoḥanan: God declared, 'You wept for nothing — I will establish for you a weeping for generations,' permanently fixing 9 Av as a day of mourning in Jewish history.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What did the young priests do with the Temple keys when the First Temple was destroyed?
A. Buried them beneath the Temple Mount for future restoration
B. Surrendered them to the Babylonian general
C. Threw them heavenward; a hand emerged from heaven and received them
D. Melted them down so enemies could not profane them
Correct: C — The Sages taught: the young priests ascended the Temple roof, cried that they were unworthy custodians, and threw the keys skyward. A hand emerged from heaven and received the keys, after which the priests leaped into the flames below.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What practical ruling did Rav Pappa derive from the opposing fortunes of Av and Adar?
A. Jews should fast extra days in Av and feast extra days in Adar
B. A Jew should avoid litigation with gentiles in Av and seek it in Adar
C. Travel is forbidden in Av but required in Adar for pilgrimage
D. Torah study is doubled in Av to counteract the evil fortune
Correct: B — Rav Pappa: since Israel's fortune is bad in Av and good in Adar, a Jew with a legal dispute against a gentile should avoid court in Av and make himself available for court in Adar.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How did Shmuel's ruling about laundering after Tisha B'Av differ from Rav's?
A. Shmuel was more lenient — he permitted laundering even before 9 Av with intention
B. Shmuel agreed with Rav — both permitted laundering once the fast ended
C. Shmuel was stricter — the prohibition extended through the rest of the week even after the fast
D. Shmuel restricted laundering only on 9 Av itself, not the surrounding week
Correct: C — Rav permitted laundering once the fast of Tisha B'Av ended. Shmuel was stricter, ruling that the prohibition continues for the remainder of that week even after the fast concludes.
