🌧️ Nakdimon's Miracle: Rain, Debt, and Divine Intervention

Nakdimon borrows 12 cisterns of water from a Roman official for Festival pilgrims
↓
Deadline arrives — no rain has fallen. Sun sets; official demands repayment.
↓
Nakdimon wraps in tallit and prays: "I acted for Your honor alone"
↓
First miracle
Rain falls — cisterns are filled and overflow
Official objects
Sun already set — the day is legally over; debt stands
↓
Nakdimon prays again: "Let it be known You have beloved ones in Your world"
↓
Clouds scatter, sun shines — day is extended. Debt cancelled. Merit confirmed.

⚖️ Nakdimon vs. The Roman Official

Roman Official
The sun has set — the deadline has passed! You owe me the full value of 12 cisterns of water. A miracle happened, yes, but the day is legally over.
Nakdimon
Master of the Universe! It is revealed and known before You that I did not act for my own honor, nor for my father's house — but for Your honor, so there would be water for the Festival pilgrims!
Roman Official
I know God performed a miracle for you — but I will only cancel the debt if the sun was still up when the water flowed. The books say otherwise.
Nakdimon
(Second prayer) Let it be known that You have beloved ones in Your world — cause the sun to shine again!
☀️ Divine Response The clouds immediately scattered and the sun shone. The day was extended. The debt was cancelled. The Sages record this as one of three times the sun broke through at an irregular hour.

🌿 Reed vs. Cedar: Which Is the Real Blessing?

🌾 The Reed — Ahijah's 'Curse'

  • Ahijah the Shilonite cursed Israel: "God will smite Israel as a reed is shaken in the water" (I Kings 14:15)
  • Rav (cited by Rav Yehuda): This is actually a blessing
  • ✓ A reed grows in a place of water — sustained environment
  • ✓ Its roots replenish even after being cut
  • ✓ It bends when the wind blows — does not uproot
  • ✓ Even if all stalks are cut, new shoots grow back
  • Israel survives because it bends without breaking

🌲 The Cedar — Balaam's 'Blessing'

  • Balaam blessed Israel: "As cedars beside the waters" (Numbers 24:6)
  • The Gemara: This is actually a curse
  • ✗ Cedar does not grow near water — fragile environment
  • ✗ Its roots do not replenish after cutting
  • ✗ When the wind blows, the entire tree uproots
  • ✗ Once felled, it cannot regenerate
  • Rigid pride leads to total, irrecoverable collapse

😳 Rabbi Elazar's Lesson in Humility

Rabbi Elazar, son of Rabbi Shimon, returns from his rabbi's house in Migdal Gedor, riding on a donkey, feeling very joyful and proud of his Torah learning
↓
He encounters an exceedingly ugly man on the road who greets him respectfully: "Greetings to you, my rabbi"
↓
Rabbi Elazar does not return the greeting. Instead he says: "Worthless [reika] person! How ugly you are! Are all the people of your city as ugly as you?"
↓
The man replies calmly: "I do not know — but go tell the Craftsman Who made me: How ugly is this vessel You have made!" — redirecting the insult to God as Creator
↓
Rabbi Elazar immediately realizes his terrible sin. He dismounts from the donkey, prostrates himself before the man, and begs forgiveness
↓
The man refuses until they reach the city. The people greet Rabbi Elazar with great honor. The man asks: "Who are you calling 'rabbi'? May there not be many like him in Israel!"
↓
After the crowd explains, the man forgives — on condition that Rabbi Elazar never again become accustomed to such behavior
↓
Rabbi Elazar enters the study hall and teaches directly from this experience: "A person should always be soft like a reed, and not stiff like a cedar"

כָּל דִּצְרִיךְ לֵיתֵי וְלֵיכוֹל — Whoever needs, let him come and eat (Rav Huna's declaration at every meal)
🏠 The Righteous Deeds of Rav Huna Open Table (every meal): When Rav Huna ate bread, he opened his doors and declared: "Whoever needs, let him come in and eat." Rava said: I can fulfill all his customs — except this one, which is too difficult in Babylonia.

Every Erev Shabbat: He sent a messenger to buy all remaining vegetables from market gardeners — then threw them in the river. Why? So vendors would not keep leftover stock and mix old produce with fresh to deceive future buyers.

New Medicine: He would fill a jug with medicine and hang it at his doorpost declaring: "All who need, let them come and take." Some say this referred to a different remedy.

Rav Adda bar Ahava's merits: I never walked 4 cubits without Torah or tefillin; never thought Torah thoughts in filthy alleyways; never slept in the study hall; never rejoiced at a colleague's stumbling; never called anyone by a nickname.
