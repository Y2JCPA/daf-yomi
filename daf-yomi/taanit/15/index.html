Remove the Ark from its locked room to the main city square
↓
Place burnt ashes upon the ark as a symbol of communal mourning
↓
Place ashes on the head of the Nasi, then the deputy Nasi
↓
Each person places ashes upon their own head
↓
Elder delivers words of reproof — citing Nineveh: God saw their deeds, not their fasting
↓

↓

⚖️ Priestly Watch (Mishmar) vs. Patrilineal Family (Beit Av) — Fasting Rules

Tanna Kamma (Mishna)

  • Fasts 1–3: Mishmar fasts, does NOT complete
  • Fasts 1–3: Beit Av does NOT fast at all
  • Fasts 4–6: Both fast but do NOT complete
  • Fasts 7–13: Both fast AND complete
  • Mishmar: may drink wine at night, not day
  • Beit Av: may NOT drink wine day or night

Rabbinic Opinion

  • Fasts 1–3: NEITHER group fasts at all
  • Fasts 4–6: Mishmar fasts, does NOT complete
  • Fasts 4–6: Beit Av does NOT fast at all
  • Fasts 7–13: Both fast AND complete
  • Rationale: Priests must be able
  • to perform Temple service properly

🏺 Why Is the Nasi First to Receive Ashes?

Question
For matters involving a curse or mourning, we normally begin with the least important person. Why then does the Nasi — the most important — receive ashes placed on his head first?
Gemara
Placing ashes on another person is an act of honor, not self-mourning. When you perform an act of honor, you begin with the greatest — this follows the 'matters of greatness' principle.
Greatness Order
Precedent: Moses → Aaron → Elazar → Itamar (Lev. 10:6). When honoring, begin with the most distinguished.
Curse Order
Contrast: When receiving rebuke or punishment, the process reverses — lesser persons are addressed first, greatest last.
Resolution The ashes are placed on the Nasi first because being chosen to receive ashes from the community's hands is itself a leadership honor — and honors always begin with the greatest.

🗓️ Restrictions on Declaring Fast Days

אֵין גּוֹזְרִין תַּעֲנִית עַל הַצִּבּוּר בַּתְּחִלָּה בַּחֲמִישִׁי
When May a Fast NOT Be Decreed? May not begin a fast series on Thursday — doing so causes Erev Shabbat price spikes as everyone rushes to buy food for after the fast and for Shabbat.

May not decree fasts on:
• New Moons (Rosh Chodesh)
• Hanukkah
• Purim

Megillat Ta'anit — days listed therein:
• Days with no eulogizing: also no eulogizing the day before, permitted the day after
• Days with no fasting: permitted to fast both before and after

Rabbi Yosei dissents: even fasting the day before a no-fast day is prohibited

Mid-series conflict: If a fast day falls on Hanukkah or Purim mid-series, the community does not interrupt the series — they complete and resume.
