QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Reish Lakish, what does going out to the public square on a fast day symbolize?
A. Public shame and humiliation before the community
B. Exile — as if to say: may our exile atone for us
C. A place where the elder delivers words of reproof
D. Proximity to the cemetery to invoke merit of ancestors
Correct: B — Reish Lakish says the move to the square symbolizes exile: 'We have been exiled; may our exile atone for us.' Rabbi Ḥiyya bar Abba, by contrast, sees it as an act of public mourning and shame.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why are ashes placed on the Torah ark during a communal fast?
A. To recall the ashes of Isaac at the Akeidah
B. Because God shares in Israel's suffering — 'I am with him in distress'
C. To symbolize that the community is like ashes before God
D. To mourn the destruction of the Temple
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that ashes are placed on the Ark to show that God Himself participates in Israel's distress, as Psalm 91:15 states: 'I am with him in distress.' This is distinct from the ashes on individuals' heads.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What did the king of Nineveh decree regarding stolen goods, according to Shmuel's interpretation?
A. Stolen goods must be donated to the Temple treasury
B. Stolen goods must be publicly confessed before the community
C. Even stolen beams built into a palace must be returned — tear down the whole building
D. Stolen goods could be repaid with double their monetary value
Correct: C — Shmuel explains that 'the violence in their hands' required complete restitution. Even if stolen materials were already incorporated into a building, the entire structure had to be demolished to return them to their rightful owners.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
To what does Rav Adda bar Ahava compare a person who confesses sins but does not truly repent?
A. A person who prays without proper intention (kavvanah)
B. A broken vessel that cannot hold water
C. One who holds a dead creeping animal while immersing in a mikveh
D. A tree that blossoms in spring but bears no fruit
Correct: C — Rav Adda bar Ahava says such a person is like one holding a dead sheretz (creeping creature, a source of impurity) while immersing. The immersion is worthless until the impure object is released first — just as confession is meaningless without abandoning the sin.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the key difference between fast day prayer practice in the Temple and in outlying areas?
A. The Temple used 18 blessings; outlying areas used 24
B. In outlying areas the congregation answers Amen; in the Temple they respond 'Blessed be the name of His glorious kingdom'
C. The Temple omitted shofar blasts; outlying areas included them
D. Outlying areas omitted the Remembrances passages; the Temple recited them
Correct: B — The Gemara establishes that amen was not said in the Temple (derived from Nehemiah 9:5). Instead of amen, the Temple congregation responded with 'Blessed be the name of His glorious kingdom forever and ever' (ברוך שם כבוד מלכותו לעולם ועד) after each blessing.
