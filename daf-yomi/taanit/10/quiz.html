Taanit 10 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Eliezer, what is the ultimate source of rainwater?
A. The upper waters suspended in heaven by God's word
B. The overflow of the Garden of Eden's river
C. Ocean water that is sweetened in the clouds
D. Condensation from the firmament above the earth
Correct: C — Rabbi Eliezer derives from the word חֹשֶׁכַת (ḥeshkhat) in Ps. 18:12 — comparing it with חַשְׁרַת from another verse, swapping a kaf for a reish — that ocean water rises, is sweetened in the clouds, and descends as fresh rain.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
When does the halakha require one in Eretz Yisrael to begin inserting 'give dew and rain' in the Amida?
A. 3rd of Marḥeshvan, per the Tanna Kamma
B. 7th of Marḥeshvan, per Rabban Gamliel
C. 60 days after Tekufat Tishrei
D. The day immediately after Sukkot ends
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar rules that the halakha follows Rabban Gamliel — the 7th of Marḥeshvan — allowing the last Jerusalem pilgrim enough time to reach the Euphrates River before rain begins falling.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When does Ḥananya say one begins requesting rain in the Diaspora?
A. 7th of Marḥeshvan, identical to Eretz Yisrael
B. 3rd of Marḥeshvan
C. The first day of Kislev
D. 60 days after Tekufat Tishrei
Correct: D — Ḥananya rules the Diaspora waits 60 days after Tekufat Tishrei (~Dec. 4–5) because Babylonia has abundant water sources and does not need rain as urgently as Eretz Yisrael does.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, who are the 'individuals' (יְחִידִים) who fast starting on the 17th of Marḥeshvan?
A. Members of the Sanhedrin
B. Kohanim and Levi'im serving in the Temple
C. Torah scholars, held to a higher standard than ordinary people
D. Communal leaders and appointed judges only
Correct: C — Rav Huna explains that these 'individuals' are Torah scholars, who are held to a higher personal standard and therefore begin fasting even before the community is required to do so.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What did Rabbi Elazar interpret as the meaning of Joseph's warning 'See that you not fall out by the way' (Gen. 45:24)?
A. Do not travel on dangerous roads without companions
B. Do not engage in new halachic analysis while traveling, lest you stumble
C. Do not eat more than famine rations on a journey
D. Do not take long strides that diminish your eyesight
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar interprets this as a warning against novel halachic debate (חידוש) while traveling, which may cause one to stumble into danger. Reviewing familiar material by rote (שינון) remains permitted and even encouraged.
