Taanit 8 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, what is the cause when a student's studies feel as difficult as iron?
A. The student has not mastered the Mishnah
B. The teacher does not show the student a friendly countenance
C. The student does not review his material enough times
D. The student prays without sincerity
Correct: B — Rava attributes the difficulty specifically to the teacher's harshness. The earlier opinion (Tanna Kamma) blames lack of Mishnah fluency — the Gemara presents both as separate teachings with separate remedies.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How many times did Reish Lakish review his studies before going before Rabbi Yoḥanan, and what does this number correspond to?
A. Seven times — the seven days of creation
B. Thirteen times — the thirteen divine attributes of mercy
C. Forty times — the forty days Torah was given on Sinai
D. Three times — the three daily prayer services
Correct: C — Reish Lakish reviewed forty times, parallel to the forty days Moses spent on Mount Sinai receiving the Torah. This became a model for thorough preparation before learning from one's teacher.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ami, what is the condition for rain to fall?
A. That the Sanhedrin declares a public fast
B. That Torah scholars study without interruption
C. That faithful, trustworthy people (ba'alei emunah) exist in the generation
D. That a pious individual prays on behalf of the community
Correct: C — Rabbi Ami derives from Psalms 85:12 — "Truth (emet/faithfulness) springs from the earth" — that when people below are faithful and trustworthy, "righteousness looks down from Heaven," meaning rain falls.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does the Gemara teach about praying for two troubles at the same time (e.g., both famine and plague)?
A. It is praiseworthy to address all troubles at once to show comprehensive trust in God
B. It is improper — one should pray for only one trouble at a time
C. One may pray for two troubles only if a Torah scholar leads the prayer
D. It is permitted on fast days but not on regular days
Correct: B — From Ezra 8:23 — "we fasted and beseeched our God for THIS" — the singular word 'this' teaches that one prays for only one trouble at a time. In the story, the Sages chose to pray about plague (not famine), since people can survive famine more easily than plague.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yitzḥak, what is rain falling on Shabbat eve a sign of, and why?
A. A great blessing — the rain waters crops before the day of rest
B. The ingathering of the exiles is imminent
C. A curse — because it disrupts Shabbat preparations
D. Divine mercy for the poor who cannot afford food
Correct: C — Rabbi Yitzḥak says rain on Shabbat eve is 'nothing other than a sign of a curse' because it prevents people from properly preparing for Shabbat (shopping, cooking, bathing). He contrasts this: sun on Shabbat is 'charity for the poor,' who can enjoy the outdoors without suffering from cold.
