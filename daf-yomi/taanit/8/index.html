📚 Why Are a Student's Studies Hard as Iron?

Tanna Kamma
Studies feel like iron because the student is not fluent in Mishnah. Without that foundation, all Gemara study becomes impossible — one must first master the base.
Rava
No — the cause is that the teacher does not show a friendly countenance. Harshness shuts down the student's mind. Warmth is a prerequisite for Torah transmission.
Tanna Kamma
Remedy: Increase sitting and studying. As Reish Lakish reviewed 40 times (= 40 days of Sinai) before appearing before Rabbi Yoḥanan. Rav Adda bar Mattana did likewise.
Rava
Remedy: Send many colleagues to intercede with the teacher. "Then must he increase his strength" — ḥayalim means both strength and soldiers/colleagues (Ecclesiastes 10:10).
📖 Shared Proof-Text Both opinions derive their remedy from the same verse: "Then must he increase his strength" (Ecclesiastes 10:10) — but they read ḥayalim differently.

Community does not pray together as one unit
↓
🐍 Like a snake that bites before being charmed — the charmer (prayer-leader) gains nothing (Ecclesiastes 10:11)
↓
Step 1: Go to one who knows how to whisper prayers (laḥash) in the proper manner — "Its noise tells concerning it" (Job 36:33)
↓
Still unanswered
Step 2: Go to the most pious person of the generation — he will multiply prayers on your behalf (Job 36:32)
Answered but prideful
⚠️ Brings anger upon the world — "The cattle (mikne) also (af) concerning the rising storm" — mikne = acquisition, af = anger
↓
Rabbi Ami: Prayer is only heard when you place your soul in your palm — full, sincere submission. "Let us lift up our heart with our hands" (Lamentations 3:41)

🤰 Rain Parallels Childbirth — Three Matched Expressions (Reish Lakish)

עֲצִירָה — Closing Up

  • Woman: "For the Lord has fast closed up all the wombs" (Genesis 20:18) — regarding Abimelech's household
  • Rain: "And He will close up the heavens" (Deuteronomy 11:17) — divine drought as consequence of sin

לֵדָה — Giving Birth

  • Woman: The birth language used for Rachel (Genesis) — opening of the womb
  • Rain: "With the pool of God that is full of water" (Psalms 65:10) — the kuba (vault) in the sky 'gives birth' to rain; the vault fills and releases

זְכִירָה — Remembering

  • Woman: "And the Lord remembered Sarah" (Genesis 21:1) — divine attention restores fertility
  • Rain: "You have remembered the earth and watered it" (Psalms 65:10) — divine remembering brings rain

⚖️ Faithful People, Exact Judgment & Blessings

אֶמֶת מֵאֶרֶץ תִּצְמָח וְצֶדֶק מִשָּׁמַיִם נִשְׁקָף (תהלים פה:יב)
Three Teachings on Divine Precision Rabbi Yoḥanan: Whoever is exact with himself on earth is judged exactly in Heaven — in order to perfect him further.

Rabbi Ami: Rain falls only for faithful people (אמונה). When truth springs from earth (faithful people act with integrity), righteousness/rain looks down from Heaven.

📖 The Marten and the Pit Story: A young man rescued a girl from a pit; they vowed to marry, calling the pit and a marten as witnesses. He forgot and married another. Their children died — one bitten by a marten, one fallen into a pit. The girl kept her vow and was ultimately vindicated. God rewards the faithful.

Rabbi Yitzḥak: A blessing is found only in objects hidden from the eye — not in things measured before all. One who enters to measure grain recites: "May You send blessing upon the work of our hands."
