⚔️ The Burial of Beitar's Slain

הַטּוֹב וְהַמֵּטִיב
Rav Mattana — Double Salvation on 15 Av Rav Mattana taught two linked teachings about the 15th of Av:

1. The slain of Beitar were finally permitted burial by Rome — years after the catastrophic battle (see Gittin 57a). The bodies had been miraculously preserved from decay.

2. On that very same day, the Romans granted permission for the burial. In gratitude, the Sages instituted the fourth blessing of Birkat HaMazon: HaTov v'HaMeitiv — 'Who is good and does good' — Tov because the bodies did not decay, u'Meitiv because burial was finally granted.

🪵 The Cessation of Tree-Chopping: A Seasonal Turning Point

Until 15 Av: Trees were actively felled and chopped to supply the altar's wood arrangement (ma'arachah) in the Temple
↓
15 Av — The Turning Point: Rabbi Eliezer the Great taught that from this date, the sun's intensity weakens. Wood cut in diminishing heat retains moisture, breeds worms, and becomes unfit for the altar
↓
Rav Menashya: Called it Yom Tevar Magal — the woodcutters broke their scythes in celebration, for the trees would no longer be felled
↓
From 15 Av onward: Those who wished to add merit would study Torah through the lengthening nights — the long nights become an opportunity for spiritual growth
↓
Admonition: One who does NOT increase Torah study from this date — yikaber! May he be buried! (A sharp rabbinic warning not to waste the gift of longer nights)

💬 Three Types of Women Speaking to Suitors in the Vineyard

Beautiful Women

  • Opened with an appeal to their physical appearance
  • Yet tempered it: 'For grace is false and beauty is vain'
  • Acknowledged that beauty is transient — a woman who fears God shall be praised
  • Used their looks as an opening, then redirected to deeper values

Distinguished Lineage

  • 'Lift your eyes and see what you choose for a wife'
  • 'Do not set your eyes on beauty alone'
  • Set your eyes on family — yichus (lineage) matters
  • 'A woman who fears God — she shall be praised' (Proverbs 31:30)

Plain-Looking Women

  • 'Take your acquisition for the sake of Heaven'
  • Do not seek a wife for external appearance
  • 'Adorn us with gold jewelry' — invest in us and we will flourish
  • A spiritually-framed, practical appeal emphasizing inner worth

🌿 The Dancing in the Vineyard: Halakhic Details

The Baraita
The daughters of Jerusalem went out in borrowed white clothes so that no woman would be embarrassed by not owning fine garments. Even the daughter of a king borrowed from the daughter of a High Priest — all were equal before potential suitors.
Rabbi Elazar
Even garments that were folded and placed in a box (kufsa) require immersion before being borrowed and worn — indicating that the concern for ritual impurity was taken seriously even for stored, unused clothing.
Tanna (Baraita)
One who did not yet have a wife would turn toward the vineyards to find one. This was an institutionalized matchmaking occasion — the dancing in vineyards served a practical social function for the entire community.
Why White Garments? White garments ensured social equality: the wealthy could not display finery, the poor would not be shamed. All women appeared equal before prospective suitors. It also reflects themes of purity associated with Yom Kippur, the other day of this celebration.

🕺 The Future Dance of the Righteous — End of Taanit

Ulla of Bira'a said in the name of Rabbi Elazar:
'In the future, the Holy One will arrange a dance (machol) of the righteous'
↓
God will be seated among them in Gan Eden — at the center of the circular dance
↓
Each righteous person will point to God with their finger and declare:
↓
The Declaration (Isaiah 25:9)
"This is God, we hoped for Him and He saved us — this is the Lord for Whom we waited; let us rejoice and be glad in His salvation"
Why Tractate Taanit Ends Here
A tractate of fasting and sorrow concludes with the ultimate joy — Israel dancing with God at the End of Days. Mourning is transformed into celebration.
