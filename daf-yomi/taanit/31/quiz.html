QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Mattana, what additional reason makes the 15th of Av historically significant?
A. The day the desert generation stopped dying
B. The day the slain of Beitar were finally brought to burial
C. The day Hoshea removed the border guards blocking pilgrimage
D. The day the tribes of Israel were permitted to intermarry freely
Correct: B — Rav Mattana taught that the slain of Beitar, denied burial by Rome for years after the battle, were finally brought to burial on 15 Av. In gratitude, the Sages instituted the fourth blessing of Birkat HaMazon: HaTov v'HaMeitiv.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Rabba and Rav Yosef say tree-cutting for the altar stopped on the 15th of Av?
A. There were no more suitable trees in the forest near Jerusalem
B. A rabbinic decree banned all labor during the second half of Av
C. After this date the sun weakens and cut wood becomes moist, breeds worms, and is unfit for the altar
D. The Kohanim declared the wood supply sufficient for the remainder of the year
Correct: C — Rabbi Eliezer the Great taught that from 15 Av onward the sun's intensity diminishes. Wood cut in reduced heat retains moisture and would breed worms, rendering it unfit for burning on the altar — so cutting ceased.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What name did Rav Menashya give to the 15th of Av, and what does it mean?
A. Yom HaBikurim — the day of first fruits brought to the Temple
B. Yom Tevar Magal — the Day of the Breaking of the Scythe, celebrating that no more trees would be felled
C. Yom HaKippurim Katan — a minor day of atonement for unintentional sins
D. Yom HaMachol — the Day of Dancing, named for the vineyard celebrations
Correct: B — Rav Menashya called it 'Yom Tevar Magal' (Day of the Breaking of the Scythe) because from that day forward no more trees were cut down — woodcutters broke their scythes, making it a celebration for the trees themselves.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What did the women of distinguished lineage say to potential suitors in the vineyard?
A. 'Set your eyes on beauty — for a beautiful woman brings honor to her husband'
B. 'Take your acquisition for the sake of Heaven and adorn us with gold jewelry'
C. 'Do not set your eyes on beauty — set your eyes on family, for a woman who fears God shall be praised'
D. 'Young man, come learn Torah first and then choose a wife from among us'
Correct: C — Women of distinguished lineage emphasized family background and yirat shamayim (fear of Heaven) over physical beauty, quoting Proverbs 31:30: 'Grace is false and beauty is vain — a woman who fears God, she shall be praised.'
QUESTION 5 OF 5
With what vision does Tractate Taanit conclude, according to Ulla of Bira'a citing Rabbi Elazar?
A. A description of the final fast day that will precede the arrival of the Messiah
B. A ruling that all fasts will be permanently abolished in the messianic era
C. God will arrange a circular dance of the righteous, sitting among them in Gan Eden as each points to Him saying 'This is God, we hoped for Him'
D. The daughters of Israel will dance again on the Temple Mount when the Temple is rebuilt
Correct: C — Ulla of Bira'a (citing Rabbi Elazar) says: In the future God will arrange a machol of the righteous in Gan Eden. He will sit at the center, and each righteous person will point to God declaring 'This is God, we hoped for Him' (Isaiah 25:9) — ending the tractate on ultimate joy.
