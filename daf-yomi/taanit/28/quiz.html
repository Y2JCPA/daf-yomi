QUESTION 1 OF 5
How did the descendants of Salmai of Netophat smuggle wood past the Greek guards?
A. They bribed the guards with silver and gold
B. They hid wood inside ladders [sulamot] carried on their shoulders
C. They floated wood down the river at night and retrieved it inside the city
D. They disguised wood as bundles of straw on donkeys
Correct: B — They hid pieces of wood inside ladders (sulamot) and carried them on their shoulders. Guards assumed they were ordinary travelers needing ladders; once past the checkpoint, they disassembled the ladders and brought the wood to the Temple altar.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why was there no non-priestly watch at all on the first of Tevet?
A. It fell on Shabbat, which automatically cancels all watches
B. The Kohen Gadol performed the full service alone that day
C. It combined three watch-cancelling factors: hallel, additional offering, and wood offering
D. The Sanhedrin issued a special one-time dispensation for that date
Correct: C — Three independent factors converged: hallel cancels the morning watch, the additional offering cancels the afternoon watch, and the wood offering also cancels the afternoon watch. Together they eliminated both watches entirely, leaving no ma'amad at all.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava, why does the Mishna say Jerusalem's walls were breached on the 17th of Tammuz when Jeremiah 39:2 says the 9th?
A. Jeremiah made a scribal error that was later corrected
B. Both dates refer to the same event — the discrepancy is a regional calendar difference
C. Jeremiah 39:2 describes the First Temple; the Mishna's 17th of Tammuz describes the Second Temple
D. The Mishna refers to the First Temple; the verse refers to the Second Temple
Correct: C — Rava resolved the contradiction: Jeremiah 39:2 (9th of Tammuz) records the breaching during the First Temple's destruction, while the Mishna (17th of Tammuz) records the Second Temple's destruction — two different events on two different dates.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
On how many days per year does an individual complete the full Hallel, according to Rabbi Yoḥanan?
A. Twelve days
B. Fifteen days
C. Eighteen days
D. Twenty-one days
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan in the name of Rabbi Shimon ben Yehotzadak: 18 days — 8 days of Sukkot (including Shemini Atzeret), 8 days of Hanukkah, the first day of Pesaḥ, and Shavuot. In the Diaspora, add the first night of Pesaḥ = 18 days + 1 night.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why is the family of Pahath Moab ben Yehuda called 'Moab'?
A. They served as diplomatic envoys to the kingdom of Moab
B. They lived in exile in the land of Moab for several generations
C. Their ancestor Ruth was a Moabite, and she is part of their lineage
D. They adopted Moabite agricultural and building practices
Correct: C — The Talmud identifies the descendants of Pahath Moab as descendants of David ben Yehuda. David was called 'Moab' because his ancestor Ruth was a Moabite — the same Ruth who converted and became the great-grandmother of King David, tying this family's identity to her origins.
