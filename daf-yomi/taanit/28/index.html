🪵 Taanit 28: Wood, Watches & Calamities

Wood Offering Families

  • 15 Av: Zattu ben Yehuda + priests, Levites, unknowns
  • 20 Av: Pahath Moab = descendants of David (via Ruth)
  • 20 Elul: Adin ben Yehuda = descendants of David
  • 1 Tevet: Parosh returns a second time
  • Heroism under Greek persecution earned these dates

Ma'amad Watch Rules

  • Hallel → cancels the morning watch
  • Additional offering → cancels the afternoon watch
  • Wood offering → also cancels the afternoon watch
  • 1 Tevet: all three → no watch whatsoever
  • Rabbi Yosei: additional offering day always has a watch

17th of Tammuz — Five Tragedies

  • Tablets broken by Moses (counted from 7 Sivan)
  • Daily tamid offering nullified — by tradition
  • Jerusalem walls breached (Second Temple)
  • Apostemos burned a Torah scroll — by tradition
  • Manasseh placed an idol in the Sanctuary

🏛️ Heroic Deeds Under Greek Persecution

The Greek Decree: The evil kingdom of Greece forbade bringing wood for the altar and first fruits to Jerusalem; guards [prozovin] were stationed along the roads
↓
First Fruits Smuggled: Sin-fearing individuals placed first fruits in baskets, covered them with dried figs, and carried them with a pestle on their shoulders — guards assumed they were bringing figs and let them pass
↓
Wood Smuggled (Descendants of Salmai of Netophat): They hid pieces of wood inside ladders [sulamot] carried on their shoulders — guards assumed they were ordinary travelers; once past, they disassembled the ladders and brought the wood to the Temple
↓
Legacy: These families were permanently designated on specific annual dates to donate wood for the altar arrangement, honoring their courage
↓
Scriptural reward: "The memory of the righteous shall be for a blessing" (Proverbs 10:7) — contrasted with Jeroboam ben Nevat who caused Israel to sin and is forever cursed

📿 The Ma'amad Watch Controversy

Mar Kashisha
What is different about hallel — it overrides the morning watch (its own); yet the additional offering does NOT override its own morning watch, only the afternoon watch? Why the asymmetry?
Rav Ashi
On the contrary — since the additional offering already overrides the afternoon watch (which is not even its own!), should it not certainly override its own morning watch? The additional offering DOES cancel the morning watch!
Mar Kashisha
I meant the reverse: the additional offering cancels the morning watch but NOT the afternoon watch. However, hallel — merely a rabbinic institution — does NOT cancel the morning watch at all.
Rav Ashi
Rabbi Yosei supports your view: on any day with an additional offering, there IS a non-priestly watch — meaning the watch persists despite the offering, not that it is cancelled.
Final Resolution Rabbi Yosei: additional offering → watch exists (it does NOT cancel). Wood offering → cancels the afternoon watch. 1 Tevet has hallel + additional offering + wood offering → no watch at all, because each factor independently cancels one service watch.

🕎 Eighteen Days of Complete Hallel

שְׁמוֹנָה עָשָׂר יוֹם בַּשָּׁנָה יָחִיד גּוֹמֵר בָּהֶן אֶת הַהַלֵּל
Rabbi Yoḥanan in the name of Rabbi Shimon ben Yehotzadak On 18 days per year, the individual completes the full Hallel:

• 8 days of Sukkot (including Shemini Atzeret)
• 8 days of Hanukkah
• 1 day — first day of Pesaḥ
• 1 day — Shavuot

In the Diaspora: 18 days + 1 night (first night of Pesaḥ)

Rav in Babylonia: Saw people reciting hallel on Rosh Ḥodesh (New Moon); thought to stop them since this was not obligatory — then realized it was their fathers' established custom. He let them continue.
Conclusion: New Moon hallel is a minhag (custom), not an obligation — recited with an abbreviated, interrupted form

💔 Five Calamities of the Seventeenth of Tammuz

17th of Tammuz — Five National Tragedies
Each requiring a Scriptural or traditional source
↓
1. Tablets Broken
Moses descended on the 17th (counted: 7 Sivan + 40 days ascent). Saw the Golden Calf, shattered the tablets. Source: Exodus 32:19 + calendar calculation
2. Daily Offering Nullified
The tamid offering ceased during the siege. Source: established by tradition (מָסוֹרֶת)
3. City Walls Breached
Jer. 39:2 says 9th Tammuz (First Temple). Mishna's 17th Tammuz = Second Temple (Rava's resolution). Two destructions, two dates
↓
4. Torah Scroll Burned
Apostemos publicly burned a Torah scroll in the streets. Source: tradition (מָסוֹרֶת)
5. Idol in the Sanctuary
Manasseh placed an idol in the Sanctuary. Derived from Daniel 12:11: 'from the time the daily offering was nullified and the abomination was placed.' Same day as the nullified tamid
