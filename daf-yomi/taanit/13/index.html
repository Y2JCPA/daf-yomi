🚿 Cold Water for Mourners: The Central Dispute

Rav Ḥisda
Anything prohibited due to mourning is forbidden in both hot and cold water. The prohibition on bathing is about the pleasurable act itself, not merely the temperature.
Rav Idi bar Avin
The mishna says 'they lock the bathhouses' — this implies only hot water bathing is prohibited. If cold water were also forbidden, one could still bathe in a river; why lock bathhouses?
Rava (Version 1)
A mourner may bathe in cold water all seven days of mourning — just as eating meat and wine is permitted despite the pleasure derived, so too cold water bathing.
Rava (Version 2)
A mourner is prohibited from bathing in cold water all seven days of mourning — any form of bathing constitutes forbidden pleasure during shiva.
Daf's Core Tension Does 'bathing' as a mourning prohibition target the hot-water bathhouse experience, or all washing for pleasure? The Gemara brings baraita after baraita — each rebutted — leaving the question alive. The daf then pivots to a parallel structure: how the Aneinu prayer is inserted and how many blessings communal vs. individual fasts require.

🌊 Proofs & Rebuttals — Hot Water Only or Cold Too?

Rav Idi bar Avin
Proof from mishna: 'They lock the bathhouses' — the verb implies hot-water facilities only. Cold-water bathing in a stream remains untouched.
Abaye
If cold water were also prohibited, the mishna should have said so explicitly. Its silence is a leniency.
Rav Sheisha (son of Rav Idi)
Difficulty for my father: The mishna separately lists 'bathing' as a prohibition — if bathhouse closure covers everything, why list bathing again? The extra mention must add a cold-water prohibition.
Gemara (baraita)
From the Tisha B'Av baraita: All obligated in immersions immerse normally — implying those not obligated may not even rinse in cold water. Supports Rav Ḥisda.
Gemara (rebuttal)
The baraita concerns hot water throughout. 'Similarly' (כיוצא בו) means the same type of water — not an extension to cold water.
📜 Key Baraita Incident Rabbi Yosei son of Rabbi Ḥanina's sons died in succession — he bathed in cold water during mourning. Does this prove cold water is permitted? The Gemara says no: it was a case of successive mourning periods, which carry special leniencies.

⚖️ Rav Ḥisda vs. Rava — Mourner's Bathing Rules Compared

Rav Ḥisda

  • Cold water prohibited — bathing is bathing
  • Same stringency as Tisha B'Av restrictions
  • Successive mourner leniencies: razor (not scissors) for hair
  • Successive mourner: water (not detergent) for laundry
  • Successive mourner: tool (not teeth/hand) for nails
  • Grown woman exception carved out separately

Rava — Version 1 (Lenient)

  • Cold water permitted all 7 days of shiva
  • Analogy: meat and wine are permitted despite pleasure
  • Cold water does not rise to level of forbidden 'bathing'
  • Grown woman may not render herself unattractive — implies bathing allowed
  • Rabbi Yosei's incident: supports cold-water leniency

Rava — Version 2 (Stringent)

  • Cold water prohibited all 7 days of shiva
  • Any bathing is a forbidden pleasure
  • Grown woman exception: special case, not the norm
  • Rabbi Yosei's incident: successive mourning only
  • Full stringency identical to Tisha B'Av

🙏 The Aneinu Prayer — Where Does It Belong?

Fast Day Amida
Where is ענינו Aneinu (Answer Us, O Lord) inserted?
↓
Rav Yehuda's Instruction
Insert Aneinu as its own independent 19th blessing, placed between Geulah (Redemption) and Refuah (Healing)
Rav Yitzḥak's Objection
An individual may not establish a new blessing for himself beyond the fixed Amida. This would be halachically improper.
↓
Rav Yitzḥak's Ruling: The individual mentions his fast within the blessing of Shomea Tefillah (Who Listens to Prayer) — no new blessing added
↓
Individual
Inserts Aneinu within Shomea Tefillah → total: 18 blessings
Prayer Leader
Recites Aneinu as a separate 19th blessing → total: 19 blessings (communal fasts)

🔁 Successive Mourning Periods — The Special Leniency Track

Trigger: Two (or more) mourning periods arrive immediately one after the other — e.g., a parent and sibling die within a week
↓
Hair: May trim with a razor in a modified way, but not in the normal manner with scissors — departure from standard practice is required
↓
Laundry: May wash garment in water, but not with detergent (natron/lye) — minimal cleaning permitted
↓
Nails: May cut nails with a nail-cutting tool, but not bite them off or pull them by hand — tool use is the middle path
↓
Bathing: The incident of Rabbi Yosei son of Rabbi Ḥanina is reinterpreted here — his cold-water bathing during mourning was permitted because of successive periods, not as a general rule
↓
Grown woman: Even in normal mourning, she may not render herself unattractive (for marriage prospects). In successive mourning, she may dye and paint — Rav Ḥisda infers laundry is similarly permitted
