🌊 Where Does Rain Originate?

Rabbi Eliezer
The entire world drinks from ocean water (אוֹקְיָינוֹס). Evaporated ocean water rises, sweetens, and falls as rain — proof: 'a mist went up from the earth' (Gen. 2:6).
Rabbi Yehoshua
The world drinks from the upper waters above the firmament — proof: 'it drinks water from the rain of heaven' (Deut. 11:11).
Rabbi Eliezer
But if rain comes from above, why does Deut. 11:11 say 'it drinks water'? Because the earth draws it up like a person drinking — through underground channels from the ocean.
Rabbi Yehoshua
Clouds ascend, fill with upper waters, become perforated like a sieve, and sprinkle the earth. Between each drop is only a hairbreadth — yet each drop falls into its own furrow.
Proof-text Showdown 'Who waters the mountains from His upper chambers' (Ps. 104:13) supports Rabbi Yehoshua. Rabbi Eliezer counters: the verse merely describes clouds that have risen to the heavens before descending.

💰 The Tithing Wordplay

עַשֵּׂר בִּשְׁבִיל שֶׁתִּתְעַשֵּׁר — Deut. 14:22 / Mal. 3:10
עַשֵּׂר תְּעַשֵּׂר → עַשֵּׁר / תִּתְעַשֵּׁר Tithe [asser] so that you will become wealthy [titasher] — Deut. 14:22 read homiletically. Give one-tenth of your produce, and God will grant you abundance so great that your lips will be worn out saying 'enough' — the meaning of עַד בְּלִי דַּי (Mal. 3:10). This is the one domain in which testing God is explicitly permitted, overriding the general prohibition of Deut. 6:16.

👦 Rabbi Yohanan & the Child Prodigy

Child (son of Reish Lakish)
My verse: 'A tithe shall you tithe.' Rabbi, what is the meaning of the double expression?
Rabbi Yohanan
Tithe so that you become wealthy! And you may even test God in this — as Malachi says: 'Test Me, please, in this, says the Lord of Hosts.'
Child
Is it permitted to test the Holy One? It is written: 'You shall not test the Lord your God' (Deut. 6:16)!
Rabbi Yohanan (citing R. Hoshaya)
That prohibition applies everywhere except tithing — where Malachi explicitly grants permission.
Child
Is anything in the Writings not hinted at in the Torah? 'The foolishness of man perverts his way, and his heart rages against God' (Prov. 19:3) — where is this in the Torah?
Rabbi Yohanan
(sat and marveled) I cannot think of any hint in the Torah…
Child
Is it not written: Joseph told his brothers of his dream, they hated him more — then he told his father, who rebuked him — yet his father 'kept the matter in mind.' Man's own foolishness ruins his path, then he rages at God!
Aftermath Rabbi Yohanan stared at the boy with admiration. His mother quickly pulled him away: 'Come, lest he do to you what he did to your father' — a veiled reference to the death of Reish Lakish.

🌧️ Can Rain Fall for a Single Individual?

Rabbi Yohanan: Rain falls even for the sake of a single individual, not only for the community
↓
Objection from baraita: The manna came for the merit of Moses — implying great sustenance only for leaders who represent all Israel!
↓
Resolution: Moses is different — he was shekul ke-neged kol Yisrael (שְׁקוּל כְּנֶגֶד כָּל יִשְׂרָאֵל), equivalent to the entire Jewish people
↓
Reish Lakish's Proof (Zech. 10:1)
'Ask of the Lord rain… for a man' — even one person's request is answered. Then: 'for a man' lest you think only for the righteous — even for the wicked!
Practical Example: Rav Daniel bar Ketina
He inspected his garden daily, saying 'this bed needs water, this does not' — and rain would fall precisely on each bed that needed it

🌊 Two Opinions on the Source of Rain

Rabbi Eliezer — Ocean Water

  • Source: ocean (אוֹקְיָינוֹס) water evaporates and falls as rain
  • Proof: 'a mist went up from the earth' (Gen. 2:6) — earth = ocean
  • Ocean water rises, becomes sweet, returns as rain
  • Ps. 104:13 'waters mountains from upper chambers' = clouds that rose to the heavens
  • Ps. 33:7 'gathers sea waters as a heap' — ocean stored for this purpose

Rabbi Yehoshua — Upper Waters

  • Source: upper waters above the firmament
  • Proof: 'it drinks from the rain of heaven' (Deut. 11:11)
  • Clouds ascend, fill from above, perforated like a sieve
  • Each drop falls into its own furrow — miraculous precision
  • Ps. 104:13 directly proves rain descends from God's upper chambers
  • Ps. 33:7 — storerooms filled by merit of 'those who fear Him'
