QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Meir, until when do we request rain in prayer?
A. Until Passover has passed
B. Until the month of Nisan has ended
C. Until 21 days after Rosh HaShana
D. Until Shavuot
Correct: B — Rabbi Meir derives from Joel 2:23 that the 'last rain' falls during Nisan itself — so we request rain until the entire month of Nisan is complete. Rabbi Yehuda disagrees, holding that Passover marks the cutoff.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the miraculous Nisan harvest story, on what date did the last rains (geshem malkosh) fall?
A. 1st of Nisan
B. 16th of Nisan
C. 5th of Nisan
D. 10th of Nisan
Correct: C — The desperate people sowed on the 2nd, 3rd, and 4th of Nisan. God sent the last rain on the 5th of Nisan. The crops grew so quickly that the Omer could be offered on 16 Nisan — right on schedule.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rabbi Yoḥanan mean when he says 'Jacob our patriarch did not die'?
A. Jacob was physically immortal and never experienced death
B. Jacob's soul never entered Gehinnom
C. Just as his seed lives on, so does he — the righteous are connected to life in a way that transcends physical death
D. Jacob was secretly taken to the Garden of Eden while still alive
Correct: C — The verse never explicitly says Jacob 'died' — only that he was embalmed and mourned. Rabbi Yoḥanan derives that Jacob's life is tied to his descendants: just as they live, he lives. The righteous are never truly cut off.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did God cause premature old age to spring upon Samuel?
A. As punishment for his sons' corrupt judgeship
B. Because he anointed Saul against God's wishes
C. To conceal that his lifespan was shortened so David's reign could begin without causing public scandal
D. Because he mourned too long for Saul after God rejected him
Correct: C — Samuel petitioned God to keep Saul alive. If Samuel died young right before Saul, people would accuse Samuel of praying his student to death. God sprung old age on him so his early death at 52 would appear natural, clearing the way for David.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What blessing did Rabbi Yitzḥak give Rav Naḥman at their parting?
A. He recited the full Priestly Blessing (Birkat Kohanim)
B. He blessed him with wealth and honor
C. He told a parable: like a perfect tree that already has sweet fruit and pleasant shade — may your children be like you
D. He declined, saying only God may bless
Correct: C — Rabbi Yitzḥak compared Rav Naḥman to a wayfarer's fruit tree — already perfect in every way. Since he could add nothing (sweet fruit, pleasant shade, water were already present), he blessed that Rav Naḥman's children should inherit his virtues.
