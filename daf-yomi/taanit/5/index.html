🌧️ Until When Do We Request Rain?

Rabbi Yehuda
We request rain until Passover has passed — by then the season of grain crops is complete.
Rabbi Meir
We request rain until the month of Nisan has ended — as Joel 2:23 says: 'He causes the first rain and the last rain to fall in the first month' (i.e., Nisan).
Key Verse (Joel 2:23) "And He causes to come down for you the rain, the first rain and the last rain in the first month" — Rabbi Meir: 'first month' = Nisan; Rabbi Yehuda: the verse only means crops ripen, not that rain falls in Nisan

🌧️ Once You Begin Mentioning Rain — Do You Ever Stop?

Rava
Once one has started mentioning rain (in Shemoneh Esrei), he no longer stops — he continues consistently until summer.
Rav Sheshet
Agreed: Once one has started mentioning rain, he no longer stops — the mention continues straight through to the end of the rainy season.
Rav (revised position)
I retract my earlier view: Count 21 days from Rosh HaShana (paralleling the 10 days to Yom Kippur), then begin. Once begun — do not stop.
Halakhic Principle All agree: once rain mention begins, it continues without interruption through the summer months.

🌱 The Miracle of Nisan — Sowing in Tears, Reaping in Joy

Severe famine: Each person had only 1 kav wheat or 2 kav barley remaining — barely enough to eat, certainly not enough to sow
↓
The prophet commands: 'Go out and sow!' — an act requiring enormous faith
↓
People sow on the 2nd, 3rd, and 4th of Nisan — weeping, risking starvation
↓
5th of Nisan: The geshem malkosh (last seasonal rain) falls miraculously
↓
Crops grew so rapidly — stalk one span tall, grain three spans — that the Omer offering could be brought on 16 Nisan in its proper time!

⚰️ Jacob Our Patriarch Did Not Die

יַעֲקֹב אָבִינוּ לֹא מֵת — כְּשֵׁם שֶׁזַּרְעוֹ בַּחַיִּים, אַף הוּא בַּחַיִּים
Rabbi Yoḥanan's Teaching (via Rabbi Yitzḥak) Our patriarch Jacob did not die. The verse never explicitly says he died — only that he was embalmed and mourned. Just as his seed is alive, so is he alive. The righteous are connected to the divine in a way that transcends physical death — their lives continue through Torah, progeny, and sacred presence.

🔥💧 Idol Worshippers Who Never Change — Worse Than Israel

Kittites (Islands)

  • Worship 🔥 fire
  • Know that water extinguishes fire
  • Have not exchanged their god
  • Remain loyal to fire despite its weakness vs. water
  • Their constancy shames Israel's faithlessness

Kedar (Arabia)

  • Worship 💧 water
  • Know that fire can evaporate water
  • Have not exchanged their god
  • Remain loyal to water despite its weakness vs. fire
  • Even nations with absurd contradictions stay loyal — Israel did not

👴 Why God Sprang Old Age Upon Samuel Prematurely

God declared: 'I regret making Saul king' (I Samuel 15:11) — Saul must be replaced by David
↓
Samuel petitioned God: Do not let Saul die — his grief over Saul would be unbearable
↓
God's dilemma: Cannot kill Saul (Samuel objects) — but David's reign must begin. Cannot let Samuel die young (people will blame him for praying against his student)
↓
God's solution: 'I will spring old age upon Samuel' — his shortened lifespan will appear natural, not punitive
↓
Samuel died at only 52 years old, yet appeared elderly. Lesson: One person's time is set aside before another's — David's era required Samuel's to end
↓
The tamarisk tree at Gibeah symbolizes: Saul was sovereign over Nob, Gibeon, and the priestly cities — a kingdom whose time had passed
