QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥama bar Gurya (citing Rav), what was the sequence of establishing the priestly watches?
A. Moses: 16, Samuel: 20, David: 24
B. Moses: 8, Samuel: 16, David: 24
C. Moses: 8, David and Samuel together: 24
D. Moses: 24, exile reduced them to 4
Correct: B — Rav Ḥama bar Gurya said that Rav said: Moses instituted 8 watches (4 from Elazar, 4 from Itamar), Samuel expanded to 16, and David established the final 24. This is a three-stage development — the other baraitot disagree.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Which four priestly watches returned from the Babylonian exile?
A. Jehoiarib, Jedaiah, Harim, and Pashhur
B. Elazar, Itamar, Jedaiah, and Immer
C. Jedaiah, Harim, Pashhur, and Immer
D. Harim, Pashhur, Immer, and Bilgah
Correct: C — The Gemara (27b) states that only four watches returned: Jedaiah, Harim, Pashhur, and Immer. The prophets among them re-established all 24 watches by lot drawn from a kalfei (receptacle).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was God's response to Abraham's concern about Israel's atonement when the Temple is not standing?
A. Prayer and repentance will fully substitute for all offerings
B. Fasting on Yom Kippur will atone for the nation's sins
C. Reading the scriptural passages of the offerings is credited as if they were actually sacrificed
D. The Sanhedrin's rulings would serve as a substitute for the Temple service
Correct: C — God told Abraham: 'I have already enacted for them the order of offerings. When they read the portion about them, I account it as if they sacrificed them before Me and I forgive all their sins.' This is the basis for the non-priestly watch reading Bereishit.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did the non-priestly watch not fast on Sunday, according to Rabbi Yoḥanan?
A. Sunday was designated for Torah reading, not fasting
B. The transition from Shabbat rest made fasting medically dangerous
C. Christians observe Sunday as their day of rest, and fasting would appear to honor it
D. Sunday corresponded to the day Adam was created, making it festive
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan said: Due to the Christians — if Jews fasted on Sunday, Christians would claim that even the Jews ascribe significance to their special day. Reish Lakish adds a different reason: the extra soul (neshama yeteira) from Shabbat departs, leaving one weak.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rav propose dividing the 5-verse Sunday Genesis reading (Gen 1:1–5) between two readers?
A. Reader 1 reads verses 1–2; Reader 2 reads verses 3–5
B. Reader 1 reads verses 1–3; Reader 2 repeats verse 3 and reads verses 4–5
C. Reader 1 splits verse 3 in half; each reader gets 2.5 verses
D. One reader reads all 5 verses, then the second reader repeats them
Correct: B — Rav said: The first reader reads verses 1–3, and the second reader repeats verse 3 (the last verse of the first reader) then reads verses 4–5. This ensures each reader reads at least 3 verses. Shmuel disputes this, preferring to split verse 3 rather than repeat it.
