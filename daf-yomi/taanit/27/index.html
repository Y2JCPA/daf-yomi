🏛️ How the 24 Priestly Watches Were Established

Moses instituted either 8 or 16 priestly watches — tannaim dispute this
↓
Samuel expanded the watches (according to Rav Ḥama bar Gurya: Moses→8, Samuel→16)
↓
David established the final 24 watches, 12 from Elazar and 12 from Itamar
↓
Babylonian exile: only 4 watches returned; prophets re-established 24 by lot
↓
Stipulation: Even if Jehoiarib returns, Jedaiah retains its position as first watch

🏛️ Three Tannaitic Opinions on How Many Watches Moses Instituted

Rav Ḥama bar Gurya (citing Rav)

  • Moses: 8 watches
  • 4 from Elazar, 4 from Itamar
  • Samuel expanded → 16
  • David expanded → 24
  • Three-stage development

Baraita — Version 1

  • Moses: 8 watches
  • 4 from Elazar, 4 from Itamar
  • David and Samuel together
  • jointly established → 24
  • Two-stage development

Baraita — Version 2

  • Moses: 16 watches
  • 8 from Elazar, 8 from Itamar
  • Elazar's descendants increased
  • Divided Elazar's 8 → added 8 more
  • One-stage expansion → 24

🤝 Abraham's Covenant: What Atones When the Temple Is Gone?

Abraham asks God: When the Temple is not standing, what will become of Israel?
↓

📖 Dividing the Genesis Reading Between Two Readers (27b)

Rav
Reader 1 reads verses 1–3; Reader 2 repeats verse 3 then continues with verses 4–5. Repetition of a verse is permitted.
Shmuel
Split the middle verse (v.3) between the two readers — each reads a distinct half. No verse is repeated.
Gemara — Objection to Shmuel
Can one split a single verse? Rabbi Ḥanina the Great forbade reciting even half of a verse that Moses did not divide himself!
Gemara — Resolution
That prohibition applies to public Torah reading. The non-priestly watch reads privately — splitting a verse in private recitation is permitted.
Gemara — Why Shmuel rejects Rav
Repetition of verses is a rabbinic prohibition (lo pelug) applied even here. Therefore Shmuel prefers splitting over repeating.
Bottom Line Both opinions divide 5 verses between 2 readers without either reading fewer than 2 verses. The dispute is: repeat a verse (Rav) vs. split a verse (Shmuel).

🕍 Stipulation of the Returning Exiles Only four priestly watches returned from Babylon: Jedaiah, Harim, Pashhur, and Immer. The prophets among them wrote all 24 watch names on slips of paper, mixed them in a kalfei (receptacle), and drew lots — redistributing the four returning families across all 24 positions.

They also stipulated: Even if Jehoiarib (originally first among the 24 watches) later ascends to Eretz Yisrael, Jedaiah will not be demoted. Instead, Jehoiarib becomes subordinate to Jedaiah — second in line.
