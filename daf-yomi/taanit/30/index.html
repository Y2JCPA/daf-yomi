⚖️ When Do Av Restrictions Begin?

Opinion A
Restrictions begin from Rosh Chodesh Av — the New Moon is likened to a Festival, derived from the phrase "her Festival."
Opinion B
Restrictions begin from the week in which Tisha B'Av falls — derived from the phrase referring to the month itself.
Rava
Halakha follows Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel: restrictions apply only during the week of Tisha B'Av. AND halakha follows Rabbi Meir: restrictions end immediately after the fast, not at week's end.
Practical Ruling Restrictions apply during the week of Tisha B'Av and lift the moment the fast concludes — both rulings operate as leniencies.

🍽️ The Concluding Meal — Eve of Tisha B'Av

Before midday (6 hours): Normal eating is permitted — pre-fast restrictions have not yet begun
↓
From 6 hours onward: The concluding meal (סעודה המפסקת) before the fast begins
↓
No two cooked dishes in one meal — e.g., not meat and a vegetable dish together
↓
No meat, no wine — statement of Rabbi Meir. Rabbis say: one must reduce and adjust consumption
↓
Exception: Heavily salted meat (מליח) and unfermented press-wine (יין מגיתו) are permitted — they are not enjoyable foods
↓
Rabbi Yehuda bar Ilai's custom: Stale bread with salt, a jug of water only, sitting between oven and stove — "resembling one whose dead lies unburied before him"

😢 Tisha B'Av — Mourning Practices (Like an Avel)

❌ Prohibited

  • Eating and drinking
  • Smearing oil on the body
  • Wearing leather shoes
  • Conjugal relations
  • Bathing
  • Torah study (gives joy — Ps. 19:9)
  • Exchanging greetings
  • Work (where local custom prohibits it)

✔️ Permitted (with conditions)

  • Reading unfamiliar Torah portions — difficult, no pleasure
  • Studying unfamiliar Talmud tractates
  • Reading Job, Lamentations, mournful parts of Jeremiah (Rabbi Yehuda's minimum)
  • Children's study — teachers may instruct
  • Work in communities where custom permits it

📚 Torah Study on Tisha B'Av — How Restricted?

Tanna Kamma
One may read from a part of the Bible he is unaccustomed to reading — the difficulty removes the pleasure. Similarly, one may study from an unfamiliar tractate.
Rabbi Yehuda
Even unfamiliar Torah is prohibited — any Torah study inherently brings joy. Only Iyov (Job), Eichah (Lamentations), and the mournful sections of Yirmiyahu (Jeremiah) are permitted.
Core Tension "פִּקּוּדֵי ה׳ מְשַׂמְּחֵי לֵב" — The Torah gladdens the heart (Ps. 19:9). On Tisha B'Av, gladness is forbidden. Does difficulty override Torah's inherent joy? Tanna Kamma says yes; Rabbi Yehuda says no.

🛏️ Overturning the Bed on Tisha B'Av

Must one overturn the bed on Tisha B'Av?
↓
Rabbi Yehuda
Yes — required
Tisha B'Av is like full mourning; a mourner overturns all beds in the house
Rabbis
Not required
Too difficult — pregnant and nursing women cannot sleep on the floor
↓
But both sides concede to each other:
↓
Rabbi Yehuda concedes
One who cannot sleep on the floor (ill, pregnant, nursing) is exempt from overturning
Rabbis concede
One who can sleep on the floor should overturn the bed
↓
Practical difference (baraita): Rabbi Yehuda requires overturning all beds in the house, not only one's own bed — same as a mourner's obligation
