5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava's dual ruling, when do the restrictions of the week of Tisha B'Av end?
A. At the end of the week on Shabbat
B. Immediately after the fast concludes
C. At midday on the 10th of Av
D. At the following Rosh Chodesh
Correct: B — Rava rules that halakha follows Rabbi Meir: restrictions end immediately after the fast — not at the end of the week. Both the Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel ruling and the Rabbi Meir ruling operate as leniencies.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
On the eve of Tisha B'Av, from what time do the meal restrictions (no two cooked dishes, no meat, no wine) take effect?
A. From sundown the night before
B. From the beginning of the day (dawn)
C. From six hours — midday — onward
D. From three hours before sunset
Correct: C — Rav Yehuda rules that the restriction against two cooked dishes applies only from six hours (midday) onward, when the concluding meal begins. Before midday, one may eat normally.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which texts does Rabbi Yehuda permit studying on Tisha B'Av, when he prohibits all other Torah?
A. Any prophetic book containing rebuke (tochacha)
B. Job, Lamentations, and the mournful sections of Jeremiah
C. Only halachic codes, since they do not gladden the heart
D. Unfamiliar Talmud tractates only
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda prohibits even unfamiliar Torah because all Torah inherently gladdens the heart. He permits only Iyov, Eichah, and the sorrowful portions of Yirmiyahu due to their mournful content.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Shmuel (as cited by Rav Yehuda), why is the fifteenth of Av a day of great joy?
A. The deaths of the wilderness generation finally ceased on that day
B. King Hoshea removed the roadblocks that prevented pilgrimage to Jerusalem
C. The tribes were permitted to intermarry with one another
D. The tribe of Benjamin was readmitted to the congregation
Correct: C — Shmuel's reason: Tu B'Av was the day inter-tribal marriage became permitted. Daughters who inherited ancestral land had been required to marry within their tribe; the verse 'let them marry whom they think best' was expounded to mean this restriction had ended.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What was the specific custom of Rabbi Yehuda bar Ilai on the eve of Tisha B'Av?
A. He fasted from midday without eating any concluding meal at all
B. He ate stale bread with salt, drank only water, and sat between the oven and stove
C. He reclined in mourner's position surrounded by overturned vessels
D. He ate a full final meal including meat and wine, then fasted the entire following day
Correct: B — As described by Rav: Rabbi Yehuda bar Ilai would eat stale dry bread with salt and drink only a jug of water, sitting between the oven and the stove — the least dignified spot in the house — resembling one whose deceased relative lies unburied before him.
