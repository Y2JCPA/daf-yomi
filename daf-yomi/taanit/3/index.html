💧 Water Libation: A Three-Way Dispute

Rabbi Yehoshua
Water libation is performed all 7 days of Sukkot. One begins mentioning rain when the lulav is set down — the last day of Sukkot.
Rabbi Yehuda ben Beteira
Water libation begins only on the 2nd day. The Torah hints at water on day 2 via the doubled term hassekh nesekh (Numbers 28:7) — one for wine, one for water.
Rabbi Natan
The term for libation appears twice in the verse — one refers to wine, one to water — indicating the water libation is performed on that day of Sukkot.
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
Resolution: The 'ben Beteira' in the baraita is Rabbi Yehoshua ben Beteira. Rabbi Yehuda sometimes calls him by name, sometimes by surname — eliminating all contradictions!
Talmudic Method An apparent contradiction between two baraitot is resolved by distinguishing the referent — 'ben Beteira' in one source does not equal 'ben Beteira' in another source.

🌬️ Dew & Wind vs. Rain — Amida Prayer Rules

Dew & Wind

  • Never withheld by God as punishment
  • Dew: Elijah's oath against dew — yet dew continued during the drought
  • Wind: 'I spread you as the four winds of heaven' (Zechariah 2:10) — always present
  • Mention in Amida is optional in all seasons
  • Summer: mentioned wind → no need to repeat the blessing
  • Rainy season: omitted wind → no need to return and repeat

Rain

  • Can be withheld as divine punishment (Deut. 11:17)
  • Mandatory mention in the rainy season (second blessing of Amida)
  • Summer: mentioned rain → must return and repeat the blessing
  • Rainy season: omitted rain → must return and repeat the blessing
  • Source: 'And He will close up the heavens' — rain specifically can be stopped
  • Atypical wind (destructive) can also be withheld — baraita distinction

🧩 Gemara's Back-and-Forth: Identifying the Mishna's Author

Gemara
The mishna (Sukka 42b) says water libation is all 7 days. If by Rabbi Yehoshua — contradiction! He says start at the end of Sukkot. If by Rabbi Yehuda ben Beteira — contradiction! He says start on day 2, giving only 6 days!
First Answer
The mishna follows Rabbi Yehuda ben Beteira, who agrees with Rabbi Yehuda (Sukka 48b): on day 1, only one flask was poured — so 'all 7 days' means 6 days of proper libation.
Gemara Retracts
Actually, the mishna follows Rabbi Yehoshua, based on a principle from Rabbi Ami citing Rabbi Yoḥanan: the law of ten saplings teaches 'all seven days' by analogy.
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
The 'ben Beteira' whom Rabbi Yehuda quotes in the baraita is Rabbi Yehoshua ben Beteira — not Rabbi Yehuda ben Beteira. Rabbi Yehuda uses both the full name and surname interchangeably.
Key Principle Rabbi Yehoshua ben Beteira holds: the prayer leader on Shemini Atzeret (concluding Festival day) mentions rain in Musaf but not Shacharit — a compromise position.

🙏 Rabbi Ḥanina's Four Rules for Wind & Rain in Prayer

Rule 1 — Summer, mentioned wind: Do not require him to return and repeat. Wind blows in summer as well — the mention is never wrong.
↓
Rule 2 — Summer, mentioned rain: Require him to return and repeat. Mentioning rain in summer is seasonally inappropriate and constitutes an error.
↓
Rule 3 — Rainy season, omitted wind: Do not require him to return. Wind blows regardless of season — its omission is inconsequential.
↓
Rule 4 — Rainy season, omitted rain: Require him to return and repeat. Rain mention is obligatory in its season; omitting it is a significant liturgical error.
↓
Core Principle: Only rain is season-sensitive in the Amida. Dew and wind — being perpetually present — are always optional mentions, never required and never wrong.
