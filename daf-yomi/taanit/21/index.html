⚖️ Torah or Commerce? The Choice of Ilfa and Rabbi Yoḥanan

Both
We are impoverished from Torah study. Let us go into commerce and fulfill: 'There shall be no needy among you.'
Angel 1
Let us knock this wall down upon them and kill them — they abandon eternal life for temporal sustenance!
Angel 2
Leave them — one of them is destined to fulfill a great need for others in the future.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
I heard the angels. I will return and instead fulfill: 'The poor shall never cease out of the land.' I choose Torah.
Ilfa
I continue in commerce. But my colleagues later taunted: 'Had you stayed and studied, would you not have been appointed head of the academy?'
Outcome Rabbi Yoḥanan became the preeminent Amora of Eretz Yisrael. Ilfa suspended himself from a ship's mast to prove he still remembered his learning — but lost the appointment.

🌍 Naḥum of Gam Zu — Faith That Transforms Reality

גַּם זוּ לְטוֹבָה — תענית כ"א ע"א
גַּם זוּ לְטוֹבָה Naḥum was blind, armless, legless, and covered in boils — yet he said gam zu letova ('this too is for the good') for everything that befell him. His name literally encoded his philosophy. The Gift to the Emperor: Jews sent a chest of jewels to Rome. Thieves at an inn replaced the jewels with earth. Naḥum said: 'This too is for the good.' At the palace, the emperor was furious — but Elijah appeared disguised as a Roman official and said: 'Perhaps this is from the earth of Abraham, whose dust turned to swords.' Rome tested the earth against an unconquerable province — and won. The chest was returned filled with treasure. His Suffering: He brought it on himself — he once delayed giving food to a starving poor man who then died. He declared: may what failed to show compassion be afflicted.

🌐 Three Rabbis — Fasting for Distant and Unusual Plagues

Rav Yehuda

  • Locusts arrived but not yet destroying
  • Still decreed a fast immediately
  • 'Have they brought provisions? They will eat here!'
  • Also: pestilence among pigs → decreed a fast
  • Reason: pig anatomy resembles humans internally

Shmuel

  • Plague reported in distant Bei Ḥozai
  • Decreed a fast in Babylonia
  • 'There is no crossing here to stop the pestilence'
  • No natural barrier = plague can travel
  • Distance is not protection

Rav Naḥman

  • Plague in Eretz Yisrael
  • Decreed a fast in Babylonia
  • 'If the lady of the house suffers, the maidservant suffers more'
  • Eretz Yisrael = lady; Diaspora = maidservant
  • The inferior suffers when the superior does

🩸 Abba the Bloodletter — The Hidden Tzaddik

Heavenly Greetings: Abba received greetings from the yeshiva on High every day; Abaye every Shabbat eve; Rava only once a year — reflecting extraordinary hidden piety
↓
Separated men from women during bloodletting — to maintain complete tzniut (modesty)
↓
Had a special garment with a slot so women could insert their arm without being seen at all
↓
Never charged a Torah scholar — and after treatment, gave the scholar money to buy nourishing food for recovery
↓
Maintained a separate room for patients who could not pay, so the poor would not be embarrassed before those who could
↓
Lesson: Private acts of chesed performed with sensitivity to human dignity may surpass public Torah greatness in Heaven's eyes
