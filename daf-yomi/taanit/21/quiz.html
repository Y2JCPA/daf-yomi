QUESTION 1 OF 5
What characteristic phrase gave Naḥum his name 'Gam Zu'?
A. Shema Yisrael — God will save us
B. Gam zu letova — This too is for the good
C. Ein adam meisim atzmo rasha — One does not incriminate himself
D. Kol Yisrael areivim zeh bazeh — All Israel are responsible for one another
Correct: B — Naḥum of Gam Zu would respond to every misfortune — including his own severe physical suffering — with 'גַּם זוּ לְטוֹבָה' (gam zu letova), meaning 'this too is for the good,' which became his name.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the halakhic threshold for declaring a plague of pestilence in a city of 500 infantrymen?
A. Five deaths in any seven-day period
B. Three deaths on three consecutive days, one per day
C. Nine deaths in a single week
D. Ten percent of the population within a month
Correct: B — The Mishnah defines a plague as three deaths removed from a city of 500 infantrymen over three consecutive days — exactly one death per day. Nine deaths in a single day or spread over four days does not qualify.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Yoḥanan abandon the journey to commerce while Ilfa continued?
A. Rabbi Yoḥanan was appointed head of the academy immediately upon leaving
B. He overheard angels discussing knocking a wall down; one said to spare them because one would fulfill a communal need
C. He received a letter from his teacher summoning him back
D. Ilfa convinced him that commerce was more spiritually valuable
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan overheard one angel say to kill them for abandoning Torah; the other said to spare them because one would fulfill a great need. Fearing this referred to him, he chose to return to Torah and leave the protection of Providence to Ilfa.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What was Rav Naḥman's reason for decreeing a fast in Babylonia when plague struck Eretz Yisrael?
A. The Exilarch commanded solidarity fasts for all Jewish communities
B. Babylonia traded heavily with Eretz Yisrael and was at risk
C. If the lady of the house suffers, the maidservant suffers all the more
D. Eretz Yisrael and Babylonia share the same climate patterns
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman used the metaphor: Eretz Yisrael is the 'lady of the house' and Babylonia the 'maidservant.' If the lady is afflicted, the maidservant will certainly be afflicted too — meaning the Holy Land's suffering portends Diaspora suffering.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How often did Abba the Bloodletter receive heavenly greetings, and how did this compare to Abaye and Rava?
A. Weekly; Abaye monthly; Rava yearly
B. Daily; Abaye every Shabbat eve; Rava once a year
C. Yearly; Abaye weekly; Rava daily
D. Every Shabbat; Abaye every festival; Rava every Rosh Hashanah
Correct: B — Abba the Bloodletter received greetings from the heavenly yeshiva every single day. Abaye received them every Shabbat eve. Rava — the greatest public Torah authority among them — received them only once a year, showing that hidden private chesed can surpass public greatness.
