📋 How the Ma'amadot System Worked

The Problem: The daily Tamid offering requires a representative Israelite witness — but the entire nation cannot travel to Jerusalem every day.
↓
The Solution: Samuel and David divided the priests into 24 priestly watches (משמרות), each serving one week in the Temple.
↓
Priests and Levites of that week's watch ascended to Jerusalem to perform the Temple service in person.
↓
Israelites of that watch split: some ascended to Jerusalem as witnesses; others gathered in their local towns to fast and pray.
↓
Local Israelites fasted Monday–Thursday and read Torah portions from Genesis 1 — the account of Creation — standing in spiritual solidarity with the Temple service.

🤲 When May Priests Recite Birkat Kohanim? Three Opinions

Rabbi Meir
All four services — Shacharit, Musaf, Mincha, and Ne'ila — include the full Priestly Benediction with raised hands on fast days. My reasoning: each of these is a distinct, proper prayer.
Rabbi Yehuda
Only Shacharit and Musaf permit raising the hands. Mincha and Ne'ila have only a verbal blessing — no hand-raising. Reason: drunkenness is possible by afternoon on fast days (people may eat before Mincha).
Rabbi Yosei
Mincha is prohibited every day (the decree against drunkenness applies, since Mincha is recited daily). But Ne'ila is permitted — it is not a daily prayer, so no general decree was issued for it.
Rav Naḥman bar Avuh
The Mishna follows Rabbi Meir. However, the established halakha and the accepted custom both follow Rabbi Yosei. And indeed — this is how we rule today.
⚖️ Final Ruling (הֲלָכָה) The halakha follows Rabbi Yosei: Ne'ila includes Birkat Kohanim with raised hands; Mincha on ordinary days does not.

💔 Five Calamities Each: 17 Tammuz vs. 9 Av

שִׁבְעָה עָשָׂר בְּתַמּוּז — 17th of Tammuz

  • 1. Moses shattered the Tablets of the Law upon seeing the Golden Calf
  • 2. The daily Tamid offering was suspended by the Roman authorities
  • 3. The walls of Jerusalem were breached by the enemy
  • 4. Apostemos the general publicly burned a Torah scroll
  • 5. An idol was erected and placed inside the Temple sanctuary

תִּשְׁעָה בְּאָב — Ninth of Av

  • 1. Decreed that the desert generation would die without entering the Land
  • 2. First Temple destroyed by Nebuchadnezzar (Babylonia)
  • 3. Second Temple destroyed by Titus (Rome)
  • 4. Beitar, the last Jewish stronghold, was captured and destroyed
  • 5. Jerusalem was plowed under by Turnus Rufus — fulfilling 'Zion shall be plowed as a field'

💃 The Most Joyous Days in the Jewish Year

לֹא הָיוּ יָמִים טוֹבִים לְיִשְׂרָאֵל כַּחֲמִשָּׁה עָשָׂר בְּאָב וּכְיוֹם הַכִּפּוּרִים
🌟 Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel — Closing Words of Ta'anit There were no days as joyous for Israel as the Fifteenth of Av (Tu B'Av) and Yom Kippur. The daughters of Jerusalem would go out in borrowed white garments — borrowed so that no one would be ashamed — and dance in the vineyards.

They would call out: "Young man, please lift your eyes and see what you choose for yourself. Do not look at beauty — look for a good family!"

The verse (Song of Songs 3:11): "Go forth, daughters of Zion, and gaze upon King Solomon on the day of his wedding and the day of gladness of his heart" — 'day of his wedding' refers to the Giving of the Torah at Sinai; 'day of the gladness of his heart' refers to the building of the Temple.
