Taanit 11 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, what title is given to a person who voluntarily fasts?
A. Sacred (kadosh)
B. Pious (chasid)
C. A sinner (choteh)
D. Righteous (tzaddik)
Correct: C — Shmuel derives from the nazirite's sin-offering that one who afflicts himself unnecessarily is called a sinner — just as the nazirite required atonement for abstaining from wine, so too the faster has sinned against his own body.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What did Rav Yehuda say in Rav's name would happen to one who restrains himself from eating during a famine year?
A. He earns extra reward in the World-to-Come
B. He will be saved from an unusual death
C. He is obligated to also abstain from conjugal relations
D. He is considered as if he brought a burnt-offering
Correct: B — Rav Yehuda citing Rav teaches from Job 5:20 — 'In famine He will redeem you from death' — that one who treats the famine as real famine (rationing his own food) will be saved from an unusual, premature death.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When a person separates from communal suffering and stays home in peace, what happens at his door according to the baraita?
A. His neighbors are commanded to rebuke him
B. The Heavenly Court opens his book of deeds
C. Two ministering angels place their hands on his head and say he will not see the community's consolation
D. A heavenly voice (bat kol) proclaims his shame publicly
Correct: C — The baraita teaches that the two angels who accompany every person come and place their hands on his head and declare that this person who separated from the community shall not witness the consolation that the community will ultimately experience.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Abaye explains that Rav Huna permits an individual to fast even after eating the previous night. What is the reason?
A. Rav Huna holds that fasting by hours is never valid anyway
B. The night hours were never included in his original acceptance of the fast
C. Eating before nightfall does not technically break a fast
D. Individual fasts in Babylonia follow a more lenient standard than communal fasts
Correct: B — Abaye clarifies: Rav Huna does hold that fasting for hours is meaningful and valid. But here, since the individual only accepted the fast upon himself for daytime hours — he never accepted the nighttime hours — eating at night cannot retroactively invalidate his daytime fast.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rabbi Elazar seems to contradict himself: he calls the faster 'sacred' but also warns not to afflict oneself. How does the Gemara resolve this?
A. The first statement applies to Tisha B'Av; the second to voluntary fasts
B. The first statement is rejected as a later interpolation
C. The phrase 'sacred' refers only to the nazirite's hair growing, not a model for fasting
D. Rabbi Elazar only permits fasting in Babylonia, not in the Land of Israel
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that when the verse calls the nazirite 'sacred,' it refers specifically to the sanctity of the growing locks of hair — the nazirite's hair genuinely has a special status. This does not mean fasting itself is sacred, thereby resolving Rabbi Elazar's apparent contradiction.
