QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Yosei bar Avin leave the study hall of Rabbi Yosei from Yokrat?
A. He was expelled for questioning Rabbi Yosei's rulings
B. He was disturbed by Rabbi Yosei's harsh treatment of his own son and daughter
C. He wished to study under a more lenient halachic authority
D. Rabbi Yosei from Yokrat moved to a different city
Correct: B — Rav Ashi asked Rabbi Yosei bar Avin why he left, and the Gemara answers with two incidents — Rabbi Yosei's extreme piety harmed both his son (for praying for miraculous food) and his daughter (for being beautiful), driving away his own student.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What miraculous event occurred after Elazar of Birta donated all his money to orphans' weddings?
A. A spring of water appeared in his courtyard
B. His lost money was returned doubled by a stranger
C. His granary filled with so much wheat the door could barely open
D. The orphans repaid him with a field of grain
Correct: C — Elazar's daughter went to open the granary and found it overflowing with wheat — so much that it pressed against the door. His extreme charity to orphans was rewarded with miraculous abundance in his own home.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How did Rabba explain that Rav Yehuda brought rain with one shoe while he could not despite far more Torah study?
A. Rav Yehuda lived in the Land of Israel where prayers carry greater weight
B. Rav Yehuda fasted more days consecutively than Rabba
C. Quantity of Torah learning cannot substitute for the personal merit of earlier generations
D. Rabba mispronounced key words in the prayer for rain
Correct: C — Rabba openly acknowledged the paradox: his generation learns all six Mishnaic orders in thirteen yeshivot, yet Rav Yehuda only needed to remove one shoe. He concluded it was personal Divine favor, not volume of study — and his tears of humility brought the rain.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What did Rabbi Ḥanina ben Dosa pray that caused rain to stop while he was traveling?
A. 'Blessed is He who withholds rain from the unworthy'
B. 'The entire world is comfortable from this rain, but Ḥanina is suffering' — and the rain stopped
C. 'May this rain fall only on the fields and not on the roads'
D. 'Grant us fair weather for our journey as You granted our ancestors'
Correct: B — Rabbi Ḥanina prayed that the whole world needed rain and was benefiting, but he personally was suffering from it while traveling — and God stopped the rain for him, demonstrating his extraordinary personal relationship with the Divine.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What did Rav Yosef conclude by comparing the High Priest's Yom Kippur prayer to Rabbi Ḥanina ben Dosa's prayer?
A. The High Priest's prayer was superior due to the sanctity of Yom Kippur
B. Both prayers were equally effective but for different purposes
C. Rabbi Ḥanina's personal prayer so surpassed the High Priest's that the comparison was rhetorical
D. The High Priest prayed for all Israel, making his prayer objectively more powerful
Correct: C — Rav Yosef asked rhetorically: 'What effect does the High Priest's prayer have against that of Rabbi Ḥanina ben Dosa?' — meaning Ḥanina's personal tzaddik status so eclipsed institutional office that a comparison was almost absurd. The individual righteous person outweighs formal religious hierarchy.
