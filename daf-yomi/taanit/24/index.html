🏡 Rabbi Yosei of Yokrat — Son & Daughter

Rabbi Yosei hires day laborers; his son prays for food since workers are starving and a fig tree miraculously produces fruit
↓
Rabbi Yosei rebukes both his son and daughter for drawing Divine gifts before their appointed time
↓
🌿 The Son
Told the fig tree: 'Return your fruit — you caused my son to lose his World-to-Come by acting prematurely'
💎 The Daughter
Her beauty caused a man to bore a hole in the hedge to gaze at her. Rabbi Yosei said: 'Return to dust before you cause others to sin'
↓
Rabbi Yosei bar Avin was so disturbed by these incidents that he left Rabbi Yosei's study hall entirely

💰 Elazar of Birta — Charity Without Limit

The Problem

  • Charity collectors hid whenever they saw Elazar
  • He gave away everything he possessed
  • They feared he would leave himself destitute
  • He chased them down demanding to participate

The Act

  • He swore by the Temple service that the orphans were like his own children
  • Donated all his money for an orphan boy and girl's wedding
  • His daughter reported he threw everything into the granary
  • The granary door could barely open — wheat pressed against it

The Miracle & Lesson

  • Granary miraculously overflowed with wheat
  • What is given to the community returns as personal blessing
  • צדקה (charity) to orphans brings Divine abundance into one's own home
  • Treating communal causes as personal family obligations earns heavenly reward

📉 Rabba's Lament — Why Won't Rain Come for Us?

People
When Rav Yehuda decreed a fast, rain would come immediately. Why does rain not come for you, Rabba?
Rabba
What can I do? If the difference is Torah study — we learn all six orders of Mishna while they only learned Nezikin!
Rabba
We learn tractate Uktzin in thirteen yeshivot. Yet when Rav Yehuda removed just one shoe as a sign of distress, rain would come immediately!
Narrator
Rabba wept over the contrast — and rain came. Humility and honest self-examination, not quantity of learning, is what moves Heaven.
⚖️ Key Contrast Rav Yehuda: one shoe removed → instant rain. Later Sages: all six Mishnaic orders studied → rain delayed. Merit of character exceeds volume of scholarship.

🌂 Rabbi Ḥanina ben Dosa — Master of Rain

כָּל הָעוֹלָם כֻּלּוֹ אֵינוֹ נִזּוֹן אֶלָּא בִּשְׁבִיל חֲנִינָא בְּנִי — The entire world is sustained only by the merit of My son Ḥanina
The Tzaddik Who Controlled the Skies Scene 1 — Rain Stops: While traveling, rain fell. Ḥanina prayed: "Master of the Universe — the entire world is comfortable but Ḥanina is suffering!" The rain stopped immediately.

Scene 2 — Rain Resumes: He arrived home soaked and prayed: "The whole world is suffering but Ḥanina is comfortable!" Rain resumed at once.

Rav Yosef's Verdict: "What effect does the High Priest's Yom Kippur prayer have against that of Rabbi Ḥanina ben Dosa?" — The individual tzaddik's personal relationship with God surpasses even the most sacred institutional prayer.

Daily Voice from Horeb: "The entire world is sustained only by the merit of My son Ḥanina."
