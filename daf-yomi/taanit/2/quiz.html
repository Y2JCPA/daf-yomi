QUESTION 1 OF 5
In which blessing of the Amida is the mention of rain (Mashiv HaRuach u'Morid HaGeshem) inserted?
A. The first blessing — Avot (Patriarchs)
B. The second blessing — Gevurot (Resurrection of the Dead)
C. The ninth blessing — Birkat HaShanim (For the Years)
D. The seventeenth blessing — Avodah (Temple Service)
Correct: B — Rain is mentioned in Gevurot, the second blessing, because rain is comparable in might to resurrection of the dead — both are acts of divine power that revive what was lifeless.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Yehoshua object to mentioning rain during the festival of Sukkot?
A. Because the obligation to mention rain had not yet been established by Moshe
B. Because rain during Sukkot is a sign of a curse — it forces Jews to leave their sukkot
C. Because mentioning rain is only permitted after the New Moon of Cheshvan
D. Because one may only praise God for rain after experiencing it
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehoshua's core argument: rain during Sukkot interrupts the mitzva of dwelling in the sukkah, making it a sign of divine displeasure (a curse), not something one should praise God for.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which three 'keys' does Rabbi Yoḥanan say God keeps directly in His own hand, never delegating to an intermediary?
A. Rain, Torah, and Redemption from exile
B. Rain, Childbirth, and Resurrection of the Dead
C. Rain, Livelihood, and Healing the sick
D. Childbirth, Resurrection of the Dead, and Repentance
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan derives each key from a verse: rain from Deut. 28:12, childbirth from Gen. 30:22 (Rachel), and resurrection from Ezek. 37:13. Eretz Yisrael adds livelihood as a fourth.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yehuda ben Beteira derive that one should begin mentioning rain on the second day of Sukkot?
A. From a tradition (kabbalah) that the water libation began on day two at Sinai
B. From letters hidden in the libation verses of days 2, 6, and 7 that spell מַיִם (water)
C. From the fact that the lulav is waved in six directions starting on day two
D. From a ruling of the Sanhedrin that day one is for wine and day two for water
Correct: B — The Gemara shows that extra letters in the libation verses — Mem (day 2), Yod (day 6), final Mem (day 7) — spell מַיִם (water), the first allusion appearing on day 2, which is when Rabbi Yehuda ben Beteira says rain mention begins.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What baraita does the Gemara cite to prove that rain must be mentioned in the Amida prayer specifically?
A. "You shall love the Lord your God" — love is expressed only through prayer
B. "To serve Him with all your heart" = prayer; the next verse immediately states "I will give rain"
C. "These are the appointed times of the Lord" — rain is an appointed divine act requiring liturgical acknowledgment
D. "And you shall bind them as a sign upon your hand" — the hand of God opens for rain
Correct: B — A baraita interprets 'to serve Him with all your heart' (Deut. 11:13) as referring to the Amida prayer (service of the heart), and the very next verse promises 'I will give rain' — directly linking rain to the Amida.
