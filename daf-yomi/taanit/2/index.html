⚖️ The Great Sukkot Rain Debate

Rabbi Eliezer
Begin mentioning rain from the first day of Sukkot. One may praise God's might at any time — I said to mention it, not to request it!
Rabbi Yehoshua
Rain during Sukkot is a sign of a curse — it forces us from our sukkot! Why mention something inauspicious during the festival?
Rabbi Eliezer
Praise of God is never inappropriate. The four species come to appease for water — they symbolize the rains of the coming year!
Rabbi Yehoshua
One should only praise God for rain in its proper season. Rain in Nisan is cursed (Mishna 12b) — rain is not always a blessing!
Halacha The law follows Rabbi Yehoshua via Rabbi Yehuda: one begins mentioning rain only at Shemini Atzeret, when the rainy season truly begins

🤔 Where Does the Tanna Stand? The Gemara's Opening Question

Mishna opens: "From when does one mention the might of rains?" — implying the obligation to mention rain is already established
↓
The Gemara asks: where was this obligation established? Why didn't the tanna teach it in Berakhot at the beginning of Zera'im?
↓
Source 1 — Berakhot
Mention rain (Mashiv HaRuach) in the 2nd blessing of Amida — Gevurot, alongside resurrection of the dead
Source 2 — Rosh HaShana
"On Sukkot, all creatures are judged for water" — connects rain to Sukkot season and raises the question of when
↓
Taanit interrupts from Rosh HaShana to answer: from when does this obligation begin each year?

📅 Four Opinions: When Does One Begin Mentioning Rain?

Rabbi Eliezer

  • Day 1 of Sukkot (first day)
  • Derived from the mitzva of lulav
  • As lulav is taken, one begins mention
  • Lulav = appeasement for water
  • Praise of God's might is always fitting

Rabbi Yehuda ben Beteira

  • Day 2 of Sukkot
  • First Torah allusion to water libation appears on day 2
  • Mem-Yod-Mem (מים) hidden in libation verses
  • The plural וּנְסָכֶיהָ hints at a second libation — water

Rabbi Akiva

  • Day 6 of Sukkot
  • Verse: וּנְסָכֶיהָ (Numbers 29:31)
  • Extra letter alludes to two libations
  • Holds like Rabbi Yehuda ben Beteira re: water libation

Rabbi Yehuda (R' Yehoshua) — Halacha

  • Shemini Atzeret (8th/last day)
  • Evening prayer leader: begins mentioning
  • Morning prayer leader: begins requesting
  • Rain now is a blessing, not a curse

🔑 Three Keys in God's Hand

שְׁלֹשָׁה מַפְתְּחוֹת בְּיַד הַקָּדוֹשׁ בָּרוּךְ הוּא שֶׁלֹּא נִמְסְרוּ בְּיַד שָׁלִיחַ
Rabbi Yoḥanan — Aggadah Three keys were never transmitted to an intermediary — they remain directly in the hand of the Holy One, Blessed be He:

🌧️ Rain — "The Lord will open for you His good treasure, the heavens, to give the rain" (Deut. 28:12)

👶 Childbirth — "God remembered Rachel… and He opened her womb" (Gen. 30:22)

⚰️ Resurrection of the Dead — "You shall know that I am the Lord when I have opened your graves" (Ezek. 37:13)

In Eretz Yisrael, a 4th key is added: Livelihood — "You open Your hand and satisfy every living thing" (Ps. 145:16). Rabbi Yoḥanan excluded it since livelihood is subsumed under rain.

🙏 Why Is Rain Mentioned in Gevurot (the Second Blessing)?

Question: Why is the mention of rain placed specifically in the second blessing of the Amida — Gevurot?
↓
Step 1 — Rain = Gevurah (might): Rabbi Yoḥanan: rain falls with mighty acts of God. Job 37:5 — "God does great things beyond comprehension [לְאֵין חֵקֶר]"
↓
Step 2 — Verbal Analogy (Gezerah Shavah): The word חֵקֶר (comprehension) in Job appears also in Isaiah 40:28 — "His understanding is beyond comprehension [אֵין חֵקֶר]" — said of God's creation of the world. This links rain's might to creation itself.
↓
Step 3 — Mention in Prayer specifically: Baraita on Deut. 11:13 — "to serve Him with all your heart" = the Amida prayer. The very next verse: "I will give the rain of your land" — rain belongs in prayer!
↓
Step 4 — Alongside resurrection: Rain is comparable in power to resurrection — it revives the world as resurrection revives the dead. Therefore it belongs in the blessing of resurrection (Gevurot).
↓
Conclusion: Recite מַשִּׁיב הָרוּחַ וּמוֹרִיד הַגֶּשֶׁם in Gevurot — God's blessing of mighty acts and resurrection of the dead
