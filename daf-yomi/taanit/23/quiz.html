QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Shimon ben Shetaḥ say he would have decreed ostracism on Ḥoni but ultimately did not?
A. Because Ḥoni was a Nasi and exempt from rabbinic discipline
B. Because Ḥoni, like a child nagging a father, gets results from God that cannot be condemned as merely disobedient
C. Because the Sanhedrin already praised Ḥoni with verses from Job
D. Because the rain Ḥoni brought saved lives, justifying any means
Correct: B — Shimon compared Ḥoni to a son who nags his father and the father cannot refuse — the relationship itself exempts him. One cannot punish intimacy, even when it looks like impropriety.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was Ḥoni's third and final request for rain, after rejecting the first two?
A. Rain to fill the cisterns, ditches, and caves
B. Rain that falls only at night so people can go out by day
C. Rain of benevolence, blessing, and generosity
D. Rain like the days of Shimon ben Shetaḥ when wheat grew as big as kidneys
Correct: C — After the first drizzle (too little) and the second furious rain (destructive, barrel-mouth drops), Ḥoni asked for rain of benevolence, blessing, and generosity — and this third request was granted.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did the clouds form on Abba Ḥilkiyya's wife's side of the roof before his own?
A. She prayed more fervently than he did
B. She was on the windward side of the roof
C. She gives bread directly to the poor; he gives money — her direct giving is more immediately meritorious
D. Women's prayers are answered before men's according to the Talmud
Correct: C — Abba Ḥilkiyya explained that she distributes bread directly (immediate relief for the hungry), while he gives money that must first be used to buy food. Direct giving generates faster merit.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What verse troubled Ḥoni all his life, and how did his seventy-year sleep help him understand it?
A. Psalm 126: 'When the Lord brought back those who returned to Zion, we were like dreamers' — he now understood how restoration can feel like waking from a dream
B. Job 22:28: 'You shall decree a matter' — he wondered why his prayers always worked
C. Proverbs 3:34: 'He gives grace to the humble' — he lived it through his circle prayer
D. Isaiah 55:10: 'As rain comes down from heaven' — he needed to experience real drought
Correct: A — Ḥoni puzzled: how could return from exile feel like a dream? After sleeping 70 years and awakening to a transformed world he no longer belonged to, he understood — exile and return span generations so vast the past feels dreamlike.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What was unique about Ḥanan HaNeḥba's prayer strategy compared to his grandfather Ḥoni?
A. He drew a circle and refused to leave, just like Ḥoni
B. He used the innocence of schoolchildren who could not distinguish between earthly and heavenly 'Abba' as the vehicle for prayer
C. He climbed to the roof and prayed silently like Abba Ḥilkiyya
D. He offered a thanks-offering at the Temple and prayed simultaneously
Correct: B — Ḥanan HaNeḥba, 'the hidden one,' operated through proxy — children who cried 'Abba! Abba!' not knowing if they addressed God or Ḥanan. He prayed that God act for those too innocent to know the difference. This is the opposite of Ḥoni's bold first-person demands.
