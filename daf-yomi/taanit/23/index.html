🌧️ Three Generations of Rain-Makers

Ḥoni HaMe'aggel

  • Drew a circle; refused to leave until rain fell
  • Demanded three rounds of rain — drizzle, flood, blessing
  • Rebuked by Shimon ben Shetaḥ but exempt from ostracism
  • Slept 70 years; died of alienation upon awakening

Abba Ḥilkiyya

  • Grandson of Ḥoni; silent, unassuming laborer
  • Would not greet Sages mid-work day
  • Prayed on rooftop with wife; her clouds formed first
  • Wife's direct bread-giving outpaced his money-giving

Ḥanan HaNeḥba

  • Son of Ḥoni's daughter; הַנֵּחְבָּא = 'the hidden one'
  • Children grabbed his hem crying 'Abba! Abba!'
  • Prayed through the innocence of those who cannot tell Abba from Abba
  • Humility and hiddenness — opposite of Ḥoni's bold demands

⭕ Ḥoni's Three Demands for Rain

Most of Adar passed — no rain
People beg Ḥoni HaMe'aggel to pray
↓
Ḥoni draws a circle, stands inside
"I will not move until You have mercy upon Your children"
↓
1st — Light drizzle
Rain begins but barely falls — "Not what I asked for"
2nd — Violent drops
Each drop like a barrel-mouth — destructive, not beneficial
3rd — Blessed rain
Rain of benevolence, blessing, and generosity ✓

⚖️ Shimon ben Shetaḥ Rebukes — and Defends — Ḥoni

Shimon ben Shetaḥ
If you were not Ḥoni, I would decree ostracism upon you! You drew a circle and made demands of God like an impudent child!
Shimon ben Shetaḥ
Were these years like the years of Elijah — when the keys of rain were in his hands — would you not bear responsibility for deaths caused by withheld rain?
Shimon ben Shetaḥ
But what can I do to you? You nag God and He does your bidding — like a son who nags his father and his father cannot refuse him.
Chamber of Hewn Stone
About you the verse says (Job 22:28): 'You shall decree a matter, and it shall be established for you.' You decree from below; the Holy One fulfills from above.
The Core Tension Ḥoni's prayer was technically improper — yet spiritually effective. The Sages could neither punish his intimacy with God nor fully endorse it. His relationship with God defied legal categories.

😴 Ḥoni and the Carob Tree — Seventy Years Asleep

Ḥoni sees a man planting a carob tree. He asks: "How long until it bears fruit?" The man answers: Seventy years.
↓
"Do you expect to live seventy more years to eat from it?" — Man: "I found carobs because my ancestors planted for me; I plant for my children."
↓
Ḥoni sits, eats bread, and falls asleep. A cliff forms around him. He sleeps for seventy years.
↓
He awakens. A man gathers carobs from the tree — it is the planter's grandson. The original planter has been dead for decades.
↓
Ḥoni returns home. Family does not believe he is Ḥoni; they say his son died but his grandson lives. He goes to the study hall — no one recognizes him.
↓
Scholars quote rulings "in the name of Ḥoni HaMe'aggel" but dismiss the man before them. He cries: "Either companionship or death!" He prays to die and does.

🏠 Abba Ḥilkiyya's Behaviors — Question and Answer

Puzzling Behavior the Sages ObservedAbba Ḥilkiyya's Explanation
Did not return the Sages' greeting at allWas a hired day-laborer; could not interrupt his employer's paid time
Carried wood on one shoulder, cloak on the other — never togetherThe cloak was borrowed; must return it undamaged. His hoe was his own.
Barefoot the whole way, except put on shoes in waterOn land he can see and avoid hazards; underwater he cannot see the riverbed
His wife came out dressed in her finest to meet himSo that walking through the city he would not set his eyes upon another woman
Did not invite the Sages to share his mealNot enough bread — he did not want to gain credit for hospitality he could not truly give
Rain-clouds formed first on the wife's side of the roofShe gives bread directly to the poor (immediate relief); he gives money (one step removed) — her merit ascended faster

👶 Ḥanan HaNeḥba — The Hidden One

אַבָּא אַבָּא הַב לַן מִיטְרָא — "Abba, Abba, give us rain!"
Son of Ḥoni's Daughter When the world needed rain, Sages sent schoolchildren to Ḥanan HaNeḥba. They grabbed the hem of his garment and cried:

"Abba! Abba! Give us rain!"

He prayed to God: "Master of the Universe — do this for the sake of those who cannot distinguish between the Abba who gives rain and the Abba who does not."

The children's inability to tell heaven from earth becomes the prayer itself. His nickname הַנֵּחְבָּא — 'the hidden one' — captures his method: invisible piety, operating through others' innocence rather than his own bold demands.
