Stage 1: 3 individual fasts — partial day, eating permitted before nightfall, lenient restrictions
↓
No rain answered → court decrees the next stage upon the community
↓
Stage 2: 3 communal fasts — eat and drink only while still day (before nightfall); bathing, anointing, shoes, and marital relations forbidden
↓

🌅 Does the Sun Need to Set for a Valid Fast?

Rav Ḥisda
Any fast upon which the sun does not set — where one eats in the middle of the day — is not called a fast at all.
Objection: Priestly Watch
The kohanim and Levites of the weekly priestly watch would fast during their service day yet eat in the evening — and their fast counts!
Rav Ḥisda (Response)
The priestly watch fast is a unique communal institution with special rules — it cannot override the general principle for individual fasts.
Objection: Rabbi Yoḥanan
Rabbi Yoḥanan would say: 'I will be in a fast until I come to my house' — a partial-day fast with no sunset! Yet it was considered valid.
Resolution
Rabbi Yoḥanan explicitly accepted a fast of hours only. Rav Ḥisda's rule targets regular fasts where one intends a full day but eats midday — that is no fast.
Core Rule (Rav Ḥisda) A standard fast must last until sunset to be called a fast. A partial fast is only valid if one explicitly accepted a fast of limited hours from the outset.

🤝 When Must Kabbalah (Acceptance) Occur?

Rav

  • Accept the fast during minḥa gedolah (early afternoon) of the preceding day
  • From midday onward — at the minḥa prayer
  • Verbal declaration during tefillat minḥa is required
  • Missing this window invalidates the fast's halachic status

Shmuel

  • Accept during minḥa ketana (late afternoon) of the preceding day
  • Must be 'while it is still day' — the final afternoon prayer
  • Megillat Ta'anit says: 'he will be bound' — binds himself at prayer
  • Fast not accepted beforehand is not called a fast

What If One Forgot?

  • Accidentally fasted without prior acceptance?
  • Rabba bar Sheila: he is like a bell — fasts but earns no mitzvah reward
  • Dozed off at minḥa time: Gemara implies he may still accept upon waking
  • The kabbalah is the formal act that transforms abstaining into a fast

😴 Can One Eat the Night Before a Communal Fast?

Night before communal fast — may one still eat and drink?
↓
Has NOT slept at all
May eat and drink until dawn
(Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi)
Finished meal, stood up
May still eat more — completing a meal does not close the door to further eating
(Rava's baraita)
Dozed off (נִים וְלֹא נִים)
Cannot eat further
Rav Ashi: asleep but not fully asleep — answers when called, cannot reason, remembers when reminded
↓
Practical rule: Dozing is the breaking point — once one nods off, eating for that night effectively ends and the fast has begun

🔥 Fasting for a Bad Dream — and Borrowing a Fast Rabba bar Meḥasseya said in the name of Rav Ḥama bar Gurya in the name of Rav:
A fast is effective to neutralize a bad dream like fire is effective for burning chaff.

Rav Ḥisda adds: The fast must be on the same day as the dream — and this applies even on Shabbat, when fasting is normally forbidden.

Borrowing a Fast (Rav): A person may 'borrow' a fast — if unable to fast on the intended day, he may annul it and repay on another day. Because an individual fast is like a vow, and vows may be rescheduled with proper intent. Shmuel's reaction: 'This is obvious!'
