QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda, what is required for an ordinary fast to be considered valid?
A. One must eat only bread and water throughout the day
B. The fast must be decreed by the rabbinic court
C. The sun must set — the fast must extend until evening
D. One must pray the special fast-day Amidah three times
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda rules that any fast upon which the sun does not set — where one eats in the middle of the day — is not called a fast at all. A full-day fast lasting until evening is required.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rav Ashi defines 'dozing' (נִים וְלֹא נִים) as which state?
A. Sleeping for less than thirty minutes in a bed
B. Asleep but not fully asleep — answers when called but cannot give a coherent reason
C. A deep sleep from which one cannot be awakened by calling
D. Closing one's eyes during prayer without losing consciousness
Correct: B — Rav Ashi defines dozing as an in-between state: one answers if called, cannot give a coherent reason, but remembers events when reminded. This state ends one's permitted eating before a communal fast.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Shmuel, when must one formally accept (kabbalah) a fast upon himself?
A. At midnight the night before the fast
B. At the morning prayer on the day of the fast itself
C. At minḥa (afternoon prayer) of the day preceding the fast
D. At any point before one sits down to eat on the fast day
Correct: C — Shmuel requires accepting the fast 'while it is still day' — at the minḥa prayer of the day before the fast. A fast not accepted in advance is not halakhically considered a fast, though one who fasts anyway is compared to a bell (ringing without reward).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara (12b) permit work (melacha) on communal fast days even while prohibiting bathing and anointing?
A. Work is permitted because it is economically essential for the community
B. Work was excluded from fast restrictions by a later rabbinic amendment
C. Work causes suffering rather than pleasure, so it does not contradict the fast's purpose
D. Work is only prohibited on the seven severe fasts, not the first three
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that bathing, anointing, shoes, and marital relations are forbidden because they provide pleasure. Work causes suffering — it is therefore not only permitted but actually reinforces the fast's spirit of affliction.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rav teach (via Rabba bar Meḥasseya) about fasting after a bad dream?
A. One should not fast after a bad dream as it demonstrates lack of trust in God
B. Fasting for a bad dream is only permitted on weekdays, never on Shabbat
C. A fast neutralizes a bad dream like fire burns chaff — even fasting on Shabbat is permitted for this purpose
D. One must have the fast decreed by a rabbi before it is effective against a dream
Correct: C — Rav teaches that a fast is as effective against a bad dream as fire against chaff. Rav Ḥisda adds that this applies even on Shabbat — the urgency of neutralizing a distressing dream overrides the normal prohibition against Shabbat fasting.
