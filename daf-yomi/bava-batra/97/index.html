🫗 Tamad Batches by Original Wine Category

Teruma Pomace

  • 1st batch: treated as teruma — forbidden to non-kohen
  • 2nd batch: also retains teruma status
  • 3rd batch: permitted — teruma status spent

Second-Tithe Pomace

  • 1st batch: treated as second tithe
  • Must be consumed in Jerusalem only
  • 2nd batch: permitted outside Jerusalem

Consecrated (Hekdesh) Pomace

  • Always forbidden — even after many steepings
  • Apparent contradiction with second-tithe rule
  • Resolved: refers to Temple consecration proper

🕯️ What Does Rav's Kiddush Ruling Exclude?

Rav (via Rav Zutra bar Toviyya)
Kiddush may only be recited over wine fit to be poured as a libation upon the altar. What does this come to exclude?
Gemara — Suggestion 1
Exclude fresh grape juice (not yet fermented)? — Rejected! Rava says: one may squeeze a cluster directly and recite Kiddush over it immediately.
Gemara — Suggestion 2
Exclude wine from the mouth of the barrel (scum) or the bottom (pomace sediment)? — Rejected! Rabbi Ḥiyya permits both for libation.
Gemara — Suggestion 3
Exclude black, borek, sweet (heiliston), cellar, or raisin wine? — Rejected! A baraita already disqualifies all of these from libations independently.
Gemara — Final Answer
Rav's ruling excludes wine with a foul odor. Alternatively: wine left uncovered — even passing it through a sieve cannot restore its fitness.
Core Principle Kiddush wine must meet altar-libation standards — dignified, unadulterated, and safe to drink.

🏺 Tamad & Susceptibility to Ritual Impurity

Question: Does tamad render foods susceptible to ritual impurity (טומאה)?
↓
Only seven liquids can render foods susceptible: water, dew, wine, oil, blood, milk, bee-honey
↓
If tamad = wine → renders food susceptible as wine (clearly)
↓
If tamad = water → renders food susceptible only if intended for use (not like unused rainwater)
↓
3rd-batch tamad is treated as water — but it was intentionally placed in the pomace, so it counts as intended water
↓
R. Yoḥanan (citing R. Shimon ben Yehotzadak): Tamad's wine/water threshold is uniform across all halakhic domains — prohibition, susceptibility, and blessing

📦 Sharon Region & Seller Liability for Soured Wine

מקח טעות — עסקה שנעשתה בטעות יסודית
Mishna — Commercial Wine Law Sharon region custom: When purchasing wine there, the buyer accepts up to 10 inferior-quality jugs per 100 purchased as local commercial practice (the clay is thin and jars crack or spoil).

General rule: If a seller's wine sours after the sale, the seller bears no financial responsibility — natural souring is a buyer's risk.

Exception — מקח טעות (mistaken transaction): If it is known that this seller's wine always sours, the sale is voidable as a transaction based on false premises.
