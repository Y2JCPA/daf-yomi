🔄 The Proof-Rejection Cycle of Daf 18

Question: Is distancing only required because of a neighbor's wall or structure?
↓
Proof Attempt
Mishna mentions wall → wall is the necessary condition for the rule
Rejection
Wall teaches the type of damage caused, not that a wall is required
↓
Repeated for: salt/lime, seeds/plow, mill, oven, bakery, tree, cistern, flax/vegetables…
↓
Conclusion: Distancing is required even without a neighbor's wall or structure

📋 Items & Their Required Distances

3 handbreadths: Salt, lime, rocks — corrode and erode a wall's foundation
↓
3 handbreadths: Seeds, the plow, urine — moisture seeps and weakens masonry
↓
3 handbreadths from lower stone (= 4 from upper stone): Mill — vibration damages structures nearby
↓
3 handbreadths from base (= 4 from upper rim): Oven — radiant heat cracks and damages walls
↓
4 cubits: Tree from neighbor's field — roots impede the plow during agricultural work
↓
25 cubits: Tree from a cistern — deep roots penetrate and destroy water storage

🔍 The Gemara's Repeated Proof Attempts

Gemara
Come and hear! The mishna says salt must be kept 'from the wall' — this implies a wall is the reason for the distancing rule!
Rejection
No — even without a wall, one may not place salt close to the boundary. The wall teaches us that these substances damage walls specifically.
Gemara
Then come and hear from the mishna about a bakery beneath a wine storeroom — surely that shows context matters!
Rejection
A residence is different — one may generally use a domicile however one wishes unless directly causing damage to another.
Gemara
Come and hear from the tree mishna — 4 cubits because of 'the work of the vineyard,' implying no vineyard, no requirement!
Rejection
Read the full mishna: if there is a fence, each plants close to his own side — this shows distancing is required regardless of the other's field.
Pattern Every proof is undermined by the full text of the same mishna, forcing the Gemara to concede distancing is universally required.

⚖️ Rabbi Yosei vs. The Rabbis: Who Must Move?

The Rabbis

  • The mazik (damage-causer) must distance himself
  • Even a buyer who acted in good faith must remove damaging items
  • Bees do not damage mustard — seed hidden, leaf regrows
  • Proactive duty: prevent harm before it occurs

Rabbi Yosei

  • The nizak (damaged party) must protect himself
  • If cistern came after tree, cistern owner bears responsibility
  • Bees do damage mustard — beekeeper is the mazik
  • "Just as you say I must distance, you must distance from me!"

🌳 Tree & Cistern: Resolving the Contradiction

Rule: Tree must be distanced 25 cubits from a cistern
↓
Cistern came first
Tree owner must cut down the tree — he is the mazik
Tree came first
Tree owner need not cut it down — cistern owner assumed the risk
↓
Problem: If distancing is always required, how could a tree ever lawfully precede a cistern?
↓
Rav Pappa's Answer: Refers to a buyer who purchased part of a neighbor's land — the seller's tree already existed before the buyer dug his cistern
