QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Bava ben Buta advise Herod to rebuild the Temple, given the Gemara's concern that Daniel was punished for advising Nebuchadnezzar?
A. Bava ben Buta was unaware of the precedent from Daniel
B. A slave like Herod is obligated in mitzvot, and/or the Temple requires royal support to be built
C. Herod had already been forgiven by a rabbinic court before approaching Bava ben Buta
D. The prohibition only applies to giving financial advice, not religious counsel
Correct: B — The Gemara offers two justifications: (1) Herod as a slave is obligated in mitzvot and Bava ben Buta was facilitating his repentance; (2) the Temple uniquely requires government involvement, making the advice necessary and meritorious.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabba, what did Herod use to build the Temple, and what did the Sages say when he wanted to cover it in gold?
A. Cedar and cypress; the Sages praised his plan to add gold
B. Red granite; the Sages said gold would make it look like a palace, not a Temple
C. White and green marble; the Sages said to leave it — it already resembles the waves of the sea
D. Chiseled Jerusalem limestone; the Sages ordered him to add a gold facade
Correct: C — Rabba says Herod built with alternating white and green marble (umarmara). When he wanted to add gold, the Sages told him to leave it — its marble appearance already resembles the beautiful waves of the sea.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, how does the builder of a stone partition prove that it belongs to him?
A. By directing the tips of the stones outward toward the neighbor
B. By bending the top edge of the wall inward toward himself
C. By smearing clay on the outside of the wall up to a cubit at the top
D. By building the partition two handbreadths thicker on his side
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan says: smear clay on the outside (neighbor's side) of the wall, up to a cubit at the top. This marks the wall as yours since only you could have applied clay from the outside.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Abaye say is the only reliable way to prove ownership of a palm branch partition?
A. Direct the branch tips toward the neighbor's property
B. Construct a small stone marker at each end of the partition
C. Have two witnesses testify at the time of construction
D. Obtain a written document from the neighbor stating he has no claim to the partition
Correct: D — Abaye rules that for a palm branch partition, no physical marking is sufficient — the only remedy is a written document from the neighbor waiving his claim to the partition and the land it stands on.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the dispute between Rav Huna and Ḥiyya bar Rav regarding how much the owner of the surrounded field pays under Rabbi Yosei's ruling?
A. Rav Huna says B pays for only the last wall built; Ḥiyya bar Rav says B pays for all four walls
B. Rav Huna says B pays based on actual construction costs; Ḥiyya bar Rav says B pays based on the value of a cheap reed partition
C. Rav Huna says B pays immediately upon enclosure; Ḥiyya bar Rav says B pays only if he uses the enclosure
D. Rav Huna says the halakha follows Rabbi Yosei; Ḥiyya bar Rav says the halakha follows the first Tanna
Correct: B — Rav Huna holds that B reimburses A based on what A actually spent. Ḥiyya bar Rav holds that B pays only the equivalent value of an inexpensive reed partition, regardless of the actual (possibly more expensive) materials A used.
