🗣️ Herod Tests Bava ben Buta in Disguise

Herod (disguised)
See, Master — what this evil slave Herod is doing to the Sages of Israel!
Bava ben Buta
What can I do? The verse says 'do not curse a leader of your people' — one may not speak against the king.
Herod (disguised)
But this one does not do the deeds of your people — the verse applies only to a fit Jew who acts as a member of your people!
Bava ben Buta
I am still afraid of him. (Herod reveals his identity.) 'I am he.' Had I known the Sages were so cautious, I would not have killed them.
Herod's Question Now — what is that man's remedy? What can I do to atone for killing the Sages?

🏛️ The Temple's Marble Splendor

כָּל מִי שֶׁלֹּא רָאָה בִּנְיַן הוֹרְדוֹס לֹא רָאָה בִּנְיָן נָאֶה מֵעוֹלָם
Talmudic Teaching "One who has not seen Herod's building has never seen a beautiful building in his life."

Rabba: He built with white and green marble (umarmara) — stones alternately projecting and receding, so that plaster would adhere properly.

Herod wished to overlay it entirely with gold. The Sages told him: Leave it as it is — it is more beautiful this way, for it resembles the waves of the sea.

⚖️ Was Bava ben Buta Right to Advise Herod?

The Challenge

  • Rav Yehuda citing Rav: Daniel was punished for advising Nebuchadnezzar to give charity to redeem his sins
  • Giving counsel to a wicked ruler enables them — Daniel was punished for this
  • Why did Bava ben Buta counsel Herod, who had slaughtered the Sages?

Answer 1: A Slave Is Different

  • Herod, as a slave, is obligated in mitzvot like any Jew
  • Bava ben Buta was helping him fulfill his obligation to repent
  • Unlike Nebuchadnezzar (a foreign king), Herod had standing to perform mitzvot

Answer 2: The Temple Is Different

  • The Temple cannot be built without government support
  • Advising Herod was not enabling wickedness — it was necessary for a communal/sacred goal
  • The spiritual benefit of rebuilding overrides the general concern

🌾 Surrounding a Field on Three Sides — Mishna & Gemara

Owner A builds partitions on 3 sides of Owner B's field — B pays nothing
↓
A then builds a partition on the 4th side, fully enclosing B's field
↓
First Tanna
B still pays nothing — no prior obligation was created; A acted unilaterally on all sides
Rabbi Yosei (halakha)
B must now pay his proportional share for all 4 partitions — the completed enclosure creates mutual benefit
↓
Rav Huna
B pays based on actual cost A spent constructing the partitions
Ḥiyya bar Rav
B pays only the value of a cheap reed partition, regardless of what A actually spent
