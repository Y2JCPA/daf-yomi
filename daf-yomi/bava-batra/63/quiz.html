QUESTION 1 OF 5
What does Sumakhos rule is the minimum 'portion' when one says 'give so-and-so a portion of my cistern for his water needs'?
A. One quarter of the cistern's volume
B. One full kav of water
C. Not less than one quarter log
D. Whatever the recipient needs daily
Correct: C — Sumakhos rules that a 'portion' of a cistern must be at least one quarter log — a specific minimum measure. The term 'portion' alone is ambiguous, so a threshold is established.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
A Levite sells his field with the condition: 'tithes are mine as long as this field is in your possession.' The buyer sells the field, then later buys it back. Does the Levite still receive tithes?
A. Yes — the original condition always follows the field
B. No — the field left the buyer's possession, ending the condition
C. Only if the second purchase was within a year
D. Yes, but only for one tithe cycle after the repurchase
Correct: B — The stipulation 'as long as the field is in your possession' terminates when the field leaves the buyer. When he reacquires it, it is treated as a new acquisition — the Levite's claim has lapsed.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What principle does the Gemara invoke to challenge the Levite's tithe stipulation?
A. A Levite may not impose conditions on a field sale
B. Tithes belong to the kehunah, not individual Levites
C. One cannot transfer ownership of something not yet in the world (davar shelo ba l'olam)
D. An oral condition cannot override a written bill of sale
Correct: C — Davar shelo ba l'olam: a person cannot transfer ownership of an object that doesn't yet exist. The tithes don't exist at the time of the stipulation, raising the question of whether the condition is legally effective.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Zevid, what is the actual novelty in Reish Lakish's ruling about the reserved upper story (deyota)?
A. That the seller — not the buyer — owns the upper story
B. That the upper story is transferred by 'depth and height' language
C. That if the upper story collapses, the seller may rebuild it and demand access for supports
D. That the buyer owns the upper story once the seller dies
Correct: C — It is obvious that the seller retains what he explicitly withheld. Rav Zevid explains that Reish Lakish's novel teaching is the right to rebuild a collapsed upper story and to maintain necessary structural supports in the lower story.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Per the mishna cited in 63b, if a seller writes 'I sell you the depth and height (עומקא ורומא) of the house,' are the pit and cistern included in the sale?
A. Yes — 'depth' explicitly includes underground structures
B. Yes, but only the cistern; the pit remains excluded
C. No — pit and cistern are excluded even with this language
D. Only if the buyer is an Israelite, not a Levite
Correct: C — The mishna (64a) is explicit: even when 'depth and height' is written in the bill of sale, the pit and cistern are not included. These structures must be enumerated separately for ownership to transfer.
