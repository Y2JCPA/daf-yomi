Bava Batra 56 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, how does the definition of a domain for Shabbat relate to gittin?
A. They are completely independent — different laws apply
B. The Shabbat domain is always larger than the gittin domain
C. They are identical — a domain defined for one applies to the other
D. Only Rabbinic domains overlap; Biblical domains differ
Correct: C — Rava explicitly states that a domain for Shabbat is like a domain for bills of divorce — the same definitions govern both areas of law.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the significance of the 'sea squill' (חצב) plant according to Rav Yehuda in the name of Rav?
A. It was used to mark graves in ancient Israel
B. Joshua used it to establish the boundaries of Eretz Yisrael for the tribes
C. It indicates soil quality suitable for agriculture
D. It marks areas where converts' property automatically transfers to the Temple
Correct: B — Rav says the sea squill is 'the growth by which Joshua established the boundaries of Eretz Yisrael for the Jews' — making it a halachic boundary marker.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Shmuel say defines the legal boundaries of Eretz Yisrael?
A. Only the land conquered by Joshua in his seven-year campaign
B. Any area that God showed to Moses before his death
C. The borders listed in the Covenant between the Pieces (brit bein habetarim)
D. The land settled by the twelve tribes after the conquest
Correct: B — Shmuel teaches: any area that God showed Moses before his death (Deuteronomy 34) is considered Eretz Yisrael — this excludes the Kenite, Kenizzite, and Kadmonite lands promised to Abraham.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the mishna's ruling about three brothers testifying conflict with Rabbi Akiva's position?
A. Rabbi Akiva holds that brothers cannot testify at all, even about separate years
B. Rabbi Akiva requires witnesses to testify about the same specific act, not just the same legal conclusion
C. Rabbi Akiva holds 'a complete matter, not half a matter' — brothers sharing a familial bias give only half-testimony
D. Rabbi Akiva rules that conspiring witnesses are never penalized when testifying about land
Correct: C — Rabbi Akiva interprets 'a complete matter, not half a matter' to disqualify combined testimony where each witness's credibility is incomplete due to family bias — the mishna follows the Rabbis who accept such testimony.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Yehuda, if one witness says a possessor consumed wheat from a field and another says he consumed barley, what is the legal status of this testimony?
A. Invalid — they must testify about the exact same crop
B. Sufficient for establishing presumptive ownership (chazakah)
C. Valid only if a third witness corroborates one of the two
D. Valid only for half the years of the required three-year period
Correct: B — Rav Yehuda rules this is sufficient for chazakah. Both witnesses confirm possession and use of the field — the difference in crop type is irrelevant since both testify to the same legal fact (working the land).
