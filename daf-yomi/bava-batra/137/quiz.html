QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel, if the first recipient of an 'after you' gift sells the property, what is the outcome?
A. The sale is invalid and the second designee receives the full property
B. The sale is valid and the second designee receives nothing
C. The sale is valid but the second designee still receives half
D. The first recipient must compensate the second designee monetarily
Correct: B — Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel holds that the first recipient has full ownership and may sell. The second designee only receives what remains at the first recipient's death — if the property was sold, nothing remains for him. Rabbi Yoḥanan rules this is the final halakha.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Abaye calls someone an 'עריום רשע' (cunning wicked person) for doing what?
A. Advising the second designee to contest the gift in beit din
B. Advising the first recipient to sell under Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel's ruling
C. Giving property as a deathbed gift to avoid paying debts
D. Refusing to return a conditional gift (al menat lehachzir)
Correct: B — Even though the law (per Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel) permits the first recipient to sell, advising someone to do so specifically to defeat the second designee's expectation is morally condemned. The legal permission does not mean the action is praiseworthy.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel concede that the second designee acquires when the first recipient gives the property as a deathbed gift?
A. Because all deathbed gifts are inherently invalid
B. Because a deathbed gift only takes effect at death, the same moment 'after you' triggers — they are simultaneous
C. Because the original giver intended to protect the second designee in all cases
D. Because Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel agrees with Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi in this specific case
Correct: B — Abaye explains: a matanat shechiv mera takes legal effect only at the moment of the first recipient's death. But 'after you' also triggers at that exact same moment. Since both fire simultaneously, the deathbed gift cannot transfer ownership before the 'after you' clause activates — so the second designee acquires.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rava rules on an etrog given 'on condition you return it.' The recipient takes it on Day 1 of Sukkot and then returns it. Has he fulfilled his obligation?
A. No — the condition makes it a loan, never a true gift
B. No — one must own the etrog unconditionally at the moment of use
C. Yes — he returned it as required, completing the condition; the acquisition was valid at use
D. Only if the original owner explicitly waives the return condition afterward
Correct: C — Rava rules: if he returned it, he fulfilled his obligation. An 'al menat lehachzir' gift confers genuine (if conditional) ownership during use. Since he fulfilled the return condition, his ownership was retroactively complete at the moment he took the etrog on Sukkot.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rav Yehuda in the name of Shmuel rules on someone who receives a property deed but loudly protests 'I do not want it.' What is the halakha?
A. He does not acquire — explicit refusal always blocks unwanted acquisition
B. He acquires the property even while standing and shouting in protest
C. The transfer is suspended for 30 days pending acceptance
D. The property reverts to the original owner's closest heir
Correct: B — Shmuel applies the principle of זכין לאדם שלא בפניו — a benefit may be conferred on a person without (or even against) their expressed consent. Receiving property is a benefit, so acquisition occurs. Rabbi Abba bar Memel clarifies that this does not conflict with other rulings — the cases are factually distinguishable.
