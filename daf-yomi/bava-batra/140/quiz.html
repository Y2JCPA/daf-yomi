Bava Batra 140 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, what happens to the remainder of the estate after the court appropriates the daughters' sustenance?
A. It is divided equally between sons and daughters
B. It is given to the sons
C. It is held in trust until the daughters marry
D. It is returned to the court for distribution
Correct: B — Rava rules that the court appropriates daughters' sustenance until they reach majority, and the remainder of the estate is given to the sons who inherited it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the ruling of Rabbi Asi (citing Rabbi Yoḥanan) regarding orphans who pre-emptively sold land from a small estate before the court could act?
A. The sale is void and daughters may reclaim the land
B. The sale is valid and daughters cannot reclaim the land
C. The sale is valid only if a court approved it
D. The orphans must compensate daughters with the land's value in cash
Correct: B — Rabbi Asi in the name of Rabbi Yoḥanan rules that if orphans sold estate land before the court appropriated it for daughters' sustenance, the sale is valid and daughters cannot reclaim it.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How did Rabbi Abbahu (transmitting from Rabbi Abba citing Rabbi Asi) resolve whether the widow's sustenance reduces the estate?
A. It never reduces the estate since it might end upon remarriage
B. It reduces the estate because the Sages gave her the status of a creditor
C. It reduces the estate only if she has already begun collecting
D. Only half of her expected sustenance is counted against the estate
Correct: B — Rabbi Abbahu ruled that the Sages established the widow's status like that of a creditor — therefore her sustenance obligation does reduce the estate when calculating the large/small threshold.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When there is a large estate with both sons and a tumtum, what do the sons do with the tumtum according to the mishna?
A. They grant the tumtum an equal inheritance share
B. They direct the tumtum to join the daughters for sustenance
C. They exclude the tumtum from the estate entirely
D. They petition the court to determine the tumtum's sex
Correct: B — In a large estate the sons direct the tumtum to the females, meaning he receives sustenance like a daughter rather than an inheritance share. Rava clarifies he does receive that sustenance.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If a father stipulated 'if a male is born, he receives 100 dinars; if a female, she receives 200 dinars,' and both a male and a female were born, what is the ruling?
A. Each child receives 150 dinars (the average of the two amounts)
B. The male receives 100 and the female receives 200 dinars
C. Only the firstborn receives the stated amount
D. Neither receives anything since both conditions were triggered simultaneously
Correct: B — The mishna rules that each child receives exactly what was stipulated for their sex: the male receives 100 dinars and the female receives 200 dinars, since both conditions were independently fulfilled.
