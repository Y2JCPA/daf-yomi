❓ Does the Widow's Sustenance Reduce the Estate?

Rabbi Yirmeya
Does the widow's sustenance reduce the estate when evaluating the large/small threshold for sons vs. daughters?
Rabbi Yirmeya
If she remarries she loses sustenance — treat her as if she never had a claim, so it does not reduce?
Rabbi Yirmeya
If she dies she also loses it — perhaps it simply does not reduce the estate value?
Rabbi Abbahu
Come back tomorrow… (considers overnight)
Rabbi Abbahu
Rabbi Abba citing Rabbi Asi: The Sages gave the widow the status of a creditor — her sustenance does reduce the estate!
Resolution — מזונות האלמנה Widow is treated like a creditor (בעלת חוב): her sustenance obligation reduces the estate for purposes of the large/small threshold calculation

⚖️ Three Estate Obligations — Do They Reduce the Estate?

Widow's Sustenance

  • Entitled while widowed and alive
  • Ends upon remarriage or death
  • Ruled: reduces estate — like a creditor
  • מזונות האלמנה
  • Status: creditor (בעלת חוב)

Creditor's Debt

  • Fixed obligation of estate
  • Does not end upon any life event
  • Clearly reduces estate value
  • חוב הבעל
  • Baseline: always counts

Stepdaughter's Sustenance

  • Pledged by deceased to wife's prior daughter
  • Status debated by Rabbi Yirmeya
  • Does it function like widow or like creditor?
  • מזונות בת אשתו
  • Raised as part of dilemma chain

🔀 Tumtum (טומטום) — Navigating Both Worlds

Tumtum = person of indeterminate sex; halakhic status as male or female is unclear
↓
Large Estate: Sons direct the tumtum to the females — receives only sustenance, not an inheritance share like a son
↓
Small Estate: Females direct the tumtum to the males — he joins the sons who receive nothing (daughters take all)
↓
Rava: Directed to females in large estate → he does receive sustenance. The clause granting nothing follows Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel
↓
Baraita reconciliation: 'Inherits as a son' = small estate scenario (directed to sons); 'Sustained as a daughter' = large estate scenario (directed to females with sustenance)
