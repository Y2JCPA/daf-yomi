📜 Blank Lines Between Document Text and Signatures

Rav Amram
We cannot derive halakhic details from the last line of a document — the holder might insert forged text in the blank space between the text and the witnesses' signatures.
Rav Naḥman
From where do you derive this rule? What is your source?
Rav Amram
From a baraita: if two blank lines are left between the text and the signatures, the document is invalid. One blank line — valid. One line cannot contain a complete forged clause; two lines can.
Ḥizkiyya
If the gap was filled with signatures of relatives, the document is valid — just as invalid sukka roofing material placed in a gap smaller than three handbreadths does not disqualify the sukka.
Core Principle Blank lines between document text and signatures create a forgery risk. Two blank lines invalidate a document; one line is acceptable. Filling the gap with disqualified signatures (e.g., relatives) validates the document by removing the empty space.

📜 The Problem: Why Blank Lines Invalidate a Document

שטר שהרחיק עדיו שתי שורות מן הכתב — פסול
Rav Amram's Explanation A document holder might exploit blank space between the body of text and the witnesses' signatures by inserting forged clauses — adding conditions or obligations that were never agreed upon. Therefore, the Sages ruled: we cannot learn from the last line of a document, and leaving excessive blank space renders the document pasul (invalid). The witnesses' signatures must be close to the text to prevent tampering.

⚖️ One Blank Line vs. Two Blank Lines

One Blank Line — Valid

  • Document is VALID (כשר)
  • A single line cannot contain a complete forged clause
  • Forgery risk is minimal
  • Witnesses' signatures are sufficiently close to the text
  • Inferred from the baraita's contrast with two lines

Two Blank Lines — Invalid

  • Document is INVALID (פסול)
  • Two lines provide enough room to add a meaningful clause
  • Forgery risk is significant
  • Cannot be admitted as evidence in court
  • Explicitly stated in the baraita (Tosefta 11:1)

❓ The Unresolved Dilemma: One and a Half Blank Lines

The Gemara
A dilemma was raised: if one and a half blank lines are left between the text and the witnesses' signatures, what is the halakha — valid or invalid?
Attempted Proof
From the first clause of the baraita: 'two lines — invalid.' Perhaps anything more than one full line is also invalid, including one and a half?
Deflection
The last clause states 'one line — valid.' This implies only exactly one line is explicitly valid — but one and a half lines is neither one nor two. The proof is deflected; the dilemma stands.
Second Proof Attempt
A different baraita is cited to resolve the question — but the Gemara's analysis leaves this matter unresolved (תיקו).
Unresolved — תיקו The halakha for a gap of exactly one and a half blank lines between document text and signatures remains undetermined by the Talmud.

✍️ Ḥizkiyya: Filling the Gap with Relatives' Signatures

Document has a gap of two blank lines
Between the text and the witnesses' signatures
↓
Gap left blank
Document is INVALID
Two blank lines — risk of forgery
Gap filled with relatives' signatures
Document is VALID
Ḥizkiyya: disqualified signatures fill the space — no room to forge
↓
Key Insight: The relatives' signatures are invalid for testimony, but their physical presence on the document eliminates the blank space and thus removes the forgery risk entirely.
