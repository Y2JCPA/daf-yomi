Bava Batra 162 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Amram, why can we not derive halakhic details from the last line of a document's text?
A. The last line is traditionally written in Aramaic, making it legally ambiguous
B. The holder of the document might insert forged text in the blank space between the text and the witnesses' signatures
C. Witnesses are only required to read the first half of a document before signing
D. The last line must be left for the court's seal, not legal content
Correct: B — Rav Amram's concern is that blank space between the last line and the signatures could be exploited to add fraudulent clauses, so we distrust any content near the document's end.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the baraita cited by Rav Amram, what is the halakha if exactly two blank lines are left between the document text and the witnesses' signatures?
A. The document is valid but requires ratification by a court of three
B. The document is valid only if witnessed by five or more signatories
C. The document is invalid
D. The document is valid as long as the content is not disputed
Correct: C — Two blank lines provide sufficient space to forge a complete clause, so the Sages invalidated such a document entirely — as stated explicitly in the baraita.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was the Gemara's conclusion regarding a gap of one and a half blank lines between the text and the signatures?
A. It follows the ruling for two lines and the document is invalid
B. It follows the ruling for one line and the document is valid
C. Rav Naḥman issued a definitive ruling that it is invalid
D. The dilemma was not resolved
Correct: D — The Gemara attempted multiple proofs from the baraita but was unable to resolve the question definitively. The dilemma remains as תיקו (unresolved).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Ḥizkiyya rules that if the two-line gap between the text and signatures is filled with the signatures of relatives, the document is:
A. Invalid, because relatives' signatures demonstrate intentional manipulation
B. Valid, because the filled space can no longer be used for forgery
C. Invalid, because relatives are absolutely disqualified and cannot appear on any legal document
D. Valid only if a majority of the signatures on the document are from qualified witnesses
Correct: B — Ḥizkiyya holds that although relatives cannot testify, their signatures physically fill the blank space, eliminating the forgery risk and thereby validating the document.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
To what halakha does Ḥizkiyya compare his ruling about filling a document's gap with relatives' signatures?
A. A mezuza placed on a doorpost with a gap in the parchment
B. Tithes separated from produce grown in a seventh-year field
C. Invalid sukka roofing material (skhakh) placed in a gap of less than three handbreadths
D. A Torah scroll written with impermanent ink that is later overwritten
Correct: C — Ḥizkiyya draws an analogy to sukka law: just as invalid skhakh can close a gap under three handbreadths without disqualifying the sukka, invalid signatures can close a document gap without invalidating the document.
