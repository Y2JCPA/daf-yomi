🐂 Goring Ox: Mistaken Transaction?

Rav
This is a mistaken transaction (mekach ta'ut). We follow the majority: most people buy oxen for plowing. Since this ox habitually gores and cannot plow, the buyer was misled — the seller must return the money.
Shmuel
This is not a mistaken transaction. The ox still has slaughter value. Since the buyer received something of worth, the sale stands — he cannot void it simply because the ox is unfit for one specific use.
Core Question When a purchased item is usable for one purpose but not another — and the buyer's specific intent is unknown — do we follow majority practice (Rav) or assess residual utility (Shmuel)?

⚖️ The Logic Behind Each Opinion

Rav
Follow the majority (rov): most people buy oxen for plowing. Therefore we presume this buyer intended plowing. The ox cannot fulfill that purpose — it is a mistaken transaction and the seller must refund.
Shmuel
Rov governs prohibitions, not monetary disputes over utility. Here the ox retains slaughter value — the buyer received something of real worth. The sale is valid and stands as-is.
💡 The Deeper Dispute Does rov (majority) determine presumed intent in monetary cases? Rav says yes — majority practice defines what the buyer must have wanted. Shmuel limits rov's role and focuses on objective residual value instead.

👰 Woman (Ketubot 15b)

  • Widow/divorcée claims 200-dinar virgin ketuba
  • Majority of women marry as virgins → supports her claim
  • Yet: requires witnesses, majority alone insufficient
  • Ravina resolves: virgin marriages generate public notice (kol)
  • No kol here → majority inference undermined → needs witnesses

🧑 Slave

  • Slave sold → found to be a thief or gambler (kuvyustus)
  • Baraita: sale stands despite the character defect
  • Challenges Rav: majority of slaves are not thieves, yet no refund?
  • Challenges Shmuel: slave retains value — consistent with his view
  • First clause of baraita analyzed separately

🌾 Produce

  • Mishna: seller of produce not liable if seeds don't sprout
  • Even flaxseeds (occasionally eaten) covered by this rule
  • Buyer assumes risk for agricultural unpredictability
  • Seller need not guarantee germination unless explicitly warranted

💍 Why Majority Fails for the Virgin Ketuba

רוב נשואות בתולות נישאות, ורוב בתולות יש להן קול
Ravina's Resolution Two facts are simultaneously true: (1) the majority of women marry as virgins, and (2) the majority of virgin marriages generate public notice (kol).

When a woman's marriage lacked that expected publicity, the absence of kol undermines the majority inference — we cannot assume she was a virgin based on rov alone. She therefore requires witnesses to collect the 200-dinar virgin ketuba.

This shows that majority reasoning can be neutralized when a correlated expected indicator is absent.
