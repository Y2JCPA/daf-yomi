5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What happens to a sealed entrance whose doorposts were broken, regarding its four-cubit zone?
A. It retains four cubits because an entrance once existed there
B. The entrance is completely negated and loses its four-cubit zone
C. It gains additional cubits as compensation for the loss
D. The court rules case-by-case depending on surrounding land
Correct: B — Breaking the doorposts and sealing the entrance completely negates the entrance — unlike merely sealing it (which can be reopened). Therefore it loses its four-cubit zone entirely.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rav Yosef's standard for field division in Babylonia refers to a day's plowing in which season?
A. Planting season (winter), when the earth is already soft from prior plowing
B. Harvest season, when the field is at peak productivity
C. Plowing season, when the soil is hardest and unturned
D. Either season — the Gemara leaves the question unresolved
Correct: C — The Gemara resolves that it means the plowing season (harder soil). Even though one plows again before sowing, that second plowing is of already-turned earth and doesn't change the standard.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Avdimi from Haifa and Rabbi Yoḥanan, to whom was prophecy given after the Temple's destruction?
A. Kings, rulers, and the heads of the great academies
B. The Kohanim who continued service in Jerusalem
C. Sages, imbeciles, and children
D. Only the direct disciples of the last prophets
Correct: C — Rabbi Avdimi says prophecy was given to Sages; Rabbi Yoḥanan adds it was also given to imbeciles and children, illustrated by incidents involving Mar bar Rav Ashi and the daughter of Rav Ḥisda.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
From which verse does Ameimar prove that a Sage is greater than a prophet?
A. "The spirit of God spoke through me" (II Samuel 23:2)
B. "A prophet has a heart of wisdom" (Psalms 90:12)
C. "Prophecy shall not cease from Israel" (Amos 2:11)
D. "Write the vision and make it plain" (Habakkuk 2:2)
Correct: B — Ameimar cites Psalms 90:12: "a prophet has a heart of wisdom." Since the prophet is compared to the wise man, the Sage must be the greater — for in comparisons, the lesser is compared to the greater.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When does Rabba say a court compels a brother to grant another's request for the boundary-adjacent plot during estate division?
A. Always — a brother's adjacent-land request must always be honored
B. Never — brothers may always refuse citing the plot's relative value
C. When the plots adjoin two separate water channels and one brother already owns adjacent land
D. Only when the plot is worth less than the average share of the estate
Correct: C — Rabba rules that when two plots adjoin two separate water channels and one brother already owns a field next to one parcel, the court compels granting his request — refusing in this configuration constitutes conduct characteristic of Sodom.
