🛤️ Can Residents Block Their Alleyways?

Rav (principle)
One is PROHIBITED from ruining a path the public has established as a thoroughfare — even if residents own the land.
Rav Anan (Shmuel)
If alleyway residents wish to erect doors, those using the public thoroughfare can prevent them from doing so.
Rabbi Zeira (Rav Naḥman)
The restriction applies only within four cubits of the public thoroughfare. Beyond that, residents may erect doors freely.
Some Sages
This entire dispute concerns only the four-cubit zone adjacent to the thoroughfare — consistent with Rav Naḥman's ruling.
Key Rule Public paths cannot be obstructed; a protected four-cubit buffer zone shields thoroughfares from being blocked by adjacent alleyway owners.

🌾 Minimum Field Size for Division

Eretz Yisrael (Mishna)

  • Minimum: nine kav of seed per party
  • Rabbi Yehuda: half a kav minimum for gardens
  • Based on standard local farming plot
  • Below minimum → court will NOT force division

Babylonia (Rav Yosef)

  • Minimum: area of a day's plowing per party
  • Refers to plowing season (hard, unturned soil)
  • Even if plowed twice (pre-sowing), still one day's work
  • Adapted to Babylonian agricultural conditions

🔮 Prophecy After the Temple's Destruction

מיום שחרב בית המקדש ניטלה נבואה מן הנביאים וניתנה לחכמים ולשוטים ולתינוקות
Rabbi Avdimi / Rabbi Yoḥanan From the day the Temple was destroyed, prophecy was taken from the prophets and given to three groups:

1. Sages — arrive at truths matching ancient tradition independently (Ameimar: Sage is greater than prophet)
2. Imbeciles (שוטים) — Mar bar Rav Ashi accidentally prophesied who would win the exilarch appointment
3. Children — the daughter of Rav Ḥisda, as a child on her father's lap, pointed to Rava as her future husband

⚖️ The Sodom Doctrine in Inheritance

Father dies leaving an estate to multiple sons
↓
One son requests his portion along his own boundary — to consolidate adjacent fields he already owns
↓
Brothers refuse, even though granting the request costs them nothing meaningful
↓
Rav Yosef objects: Brothers may argue the requested field is particularly valuable — a legitimate defense
↓
Rabba rules: When two plots adjoin two separate water channels and one brother already owns adjacent land, court compels granting his request
↓
Refusing a reasonable request that costs you nothing = conduct characteristic of Sodom — courts will intervene
