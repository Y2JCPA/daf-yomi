📢 When Is a Protest (מחאה) Valid?

↓
Secrecy imposed
Owner or witnesses suppress the protest from reaching possessor
Open protest
Witnesses are free to spread word of the protest
↓
❌ Invalid
Protest has no legal effect — Rav Zevid
✅ Valid
Even if not made in possessor's presence — Rav Naḥman / Rava
↓

⚠️ Rav Zevid: Three Cases of Silenced Protests

Case 1
Owner protests and adds: 'Do not tell the possessor about this'
Rav Zevid
Invalid protest — he is ensuring word will never reach the possessor, which defeats the entire purpose
Case 2
Witnesses themselves declare: 'We will not tell the possessor'
Rav Zevid
Invalid — owner must go find other witnesses who will actually spread the word
Case 3
Owner instructs witnesses: 'A word [שוטא] should not leave your mouths'
Rav Zevid
Invalid — same logic; protest suppressed by the owner's own instruction is no protest at all
Core Principle A מחאה exists to alert the possessor — or at least create a public record — that the owner has not abandoned his claim. Any condition of secrecy nullifies it entirely.

👤 Can a Protest Be Lodged Outside the Possessor's Presence?

Rav Naḥman: A protest lodged not in the possessor's presence is a valid protest
↓
Rava raises an objection from the Mishnah: R. Yehuda says חזקה applies only when the possessor could have defended himself — implying the protest must reach him
↓
Initial reading
Rava disagrees with Rav Naḥman — protest must be in-person?
Resolution
Rava himself states the same rule as Rav Naḥman — the objection was dialectical, not a real disagreement
↓
Both Rava and Rav Naḥman agree: protest is valid even without the possessor present, so long as word can plausibly reach him

🔢 How Many Witnesses Are Required for a Valid Protest?

2 Witnesses

  • Rava quoting Rav Naḥman: A protest requires 2 witnesses
  • A protest is a legal act of testimony — the standard two-witness rule applies
  • The 3-person threshold is about lashon hara, not legal validity
  • Minority view: concern for spread of information is secondary to testimonial form

3 Witnesses

  • Rabbi Yoḥanan (via R. Ḥiyya bar Abba): 3 people required
  • Rooted in Rabba bar Rav Huna: anything said before 3 is no longer private — it will spread
  • Purpose: guarantees the protest becomes public knowledge and reaches the possessor
  • Majority view cited in Gemara's initial suggestion

🔄 How Often Must an Owner Protest?

Background: If a possessor holds land uncontested for 3 years, he establishes חזקה (presumptive ownership). The owner must protest to interrupt this.
↓
Bar Kappara / Reish Lakish: The owner must protest at the end of each and every 3-year period — one protest is not a permanent solution.
↓
Rabbi Yoḥanan is surprised by this view — he holds that a single valid protest should permanently prevent the possessor from ever claiming חזקה.
↓
Rava rules: The owner must protest at the end of each 3-year cycle so the possessor never completes a full uncontested period.
↓
Bar Kappara adds: If the owner protested, returned and protested again, and again — if the later protests were before the same witnesses, they remain valid and effective.
