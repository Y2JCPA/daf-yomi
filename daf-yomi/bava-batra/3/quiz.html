Bava Batra 3 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What are the two interpretations of 'meḥitza' that the Gemara debates on this daf?
A. A wall of stone versus a wall of brick
B. A physical partition (dividing wall) versus a division of ownership (allocated share)
C. A permanent fence versus a temporary barrier
D. An inner wall versus an outer boundary
Correct: B — The Gemara debates whether meḥitza refers to a physical partition separating the shared space, or a division of the courtyard into separately owned shares. The two readings carry different implications for whether one party can compel the other to build.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rav Ashi explain our knowledge of what 'gevil' and 'kefisin' refer to?
A. From a Biblical verse describing construction of the Tabernacle
B. By logical deduction from their relative wall thicknesses
C. The Sages received these definitions as an oral tradition
D. From a baraita quoting a ruling of the early courts
Correct: C — When Rabba son of Rava (or Rav Aḥa son of Rav Avya) challenges Rav Ashi to prove the definitions from internal evidence, Rav Ashi responds that these are simply received traditions — the Sages transmitted the definitions without deriving them.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did the Second Temple use two curtains instead of a stone amah teraksin wall to divide the Kodesh HaKodashim?
A. Chiseled stone was forbidden inside the Temple by rabbinic decree
B. The craftsmen lacked the skill to build such a wall
C. The Second Temple was taller, making a floor-to-ceiling stone wall structurally impractical
D. Ezra the Scribe ruled that curtains are holier than stone partitions
Correct: C — Abaye explains that tradition requires the partition to span from floor to ceiling. Since the Second Temple was taller than the First (Haggai 2:9 — 'greater glory'), a stone wall of that height could not stand. Two curtains with a gap between them were used instead.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, which of the following IS sufficient to permit demolishing an old synagogue before the new one is built?
A. The full cost of construction has been collected and is set aside
B. All building materials (bricks, beams, boards) are prepared and on-site
C. The community has voted unanimously to proceed
D. Visible structural cracks threaten the safety of those inside
Correct: D — Rav Ashi rules that having money ready and even having all materials on-site is still insufficient — the new synagogue must be physically built first. The only recognized exception is when visible cracks in the old building pose a danger to life.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What atonement did Bava ben Buta recommend to Herod for having killed the Sages of Israel?
A. Observe a forty-day fast and distribute charity to the poor
B. Appoint new Sages to reconstitute the Sanhedrin
C. Rebuild and beautify the Temple — 'attend to the light of the world'
D. Free all Jewish slaves throughout his kingdom
Correct: C — Bava ben Buta used a poetic parallel: 'You extinguished the light of the world [the Sages, called a light]. Go and tend to the light of the world [the Temple, also called a light].' This led Herod to undertake the famous renovation of the Second Temple.
