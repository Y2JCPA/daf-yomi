QUESTION 1 OF 5
The mother of Rami bar Ḥama gave two deeds to two different sons. Who was awarded the property and why?
A. Rami bar Ḥama — his deed came first and was never explicitly revoked
B. Rav Ukva bar Ḥama — a shechiv mera may retract and redirect a gift to another person
C. Neither son — giving the same property twice rendered both deeds void
D. Rami bar Ḥama — she died before the second deed could take legal effect
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman ruled in favor of Rav Ukva bar Ḥama. A shechiv mera retains authority over her property until death, including the power to revoke a prior gift and reassign it to a different recipient.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the final halakha regarding a deathbed gift that includes only part of the donor's property?
A. Valid without any kinyan — same rule as a full deathbed gift
B. Requires kinyan; without it, heirs inherit even if the donor dies
C. Requires two witnesses but no formal kinyan
D. Always invalid — a shechiv mera may only give all property or none
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that a partial deathbed gift requires a kinyan (act of acquisition). Without kinyan, it fails and the heirs inherit — even though the donor subsequently died.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How did Rav Naḥman know that the sister of Rav Dimi bar Yosef's final transfer was a deathbed gift and not a regular gift?
A. She had previously transferred the orchard many times when sick
B. Witnesses testified she said: 'Woe, that woman is dying!'
C. She sent the transfer via messenger, which is exclusive to deathbed gifts
D. Rav Dimi reserved part of the orchard for her use, showing it was conditional
Correct: B — The witnesses told Rav Naḥman that when she transferred the property she declared 'Woe, that woman is dying!' — an expression of mortal fear that legally established it as a matnat shechiv mera.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rava rebuked the Sages saying 'Do not hang empty pitchers upon Rav Naḥman.' What error was he correcting?
A. They falsely claimed Rav Naḥman said deathbed gifts to family are always invalid
B. They attributed to Rav Naḥman the view that a partial deathbed gift needs no kinyan — but Rav Naḥman actually required kinyan
C. They said Rav Naḥman ruled that written deeds override all verbal gifts
D. They claimed Rav Naḥman held that all deathbed gifts require two witnesses
Correct: B — The Sages reported in the name of Mar Zutra in the name of Rav Naḥman that a partial deathbed gift needs no kinyan. Rava corrected this: Rav Naḥman requires kinyan for a partial deathbed gift. Attributing the opposite position to him was 'hanging empty pitchers' — falsely crediting him with an incorrect ruling.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rav Sheshet challenged Rav Naḥman: 'Did Shmuel say he can retract to give to another?' What was the basis of this challenge?
A. Shmuel explicitly prohibited reassigning a deathbed gift to a second person
B. Shmuel only permitted retraction to retain the property for oneself — retraction to benefit a third party was a separate, unaddressed case
C. Shmuel ruled that any deed, once written, is irrevocable
D. Shmuel said a deathbed gift becomes final the moment the donor speaks it aloud
Correct: B — Rav Sheshet argued that Shmuel's ruling covered only the case of retaining property for oneself. Whether Shmuel also permitted retraction in order to give to a different person was a distinct legal question that Shmuel had not addressed — and could not simply be inferred.
