Bava Batra 58 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did a Divine Voice prevent Rabbi Bena'a from viewing Adam's full form in the cave?
A. Rabbi Bena'a was ritually impure and unworthy to enter
B. Adam's form is God's own image — gazing upon it directly was forbidden
C. The cave of Adam had not yet been marked for purity
D. Eliezer the servant refused him entry
Correct: B — The Divine Voice said: 'You gazed upon the likeness of My image (Abraham). Do not gaze upon My image itself (Adam).' Adam embodies the original Divine image — a higher level than any descendant, even Abraham.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the chain of beauty described in the Gemara, what is said about Sarah's beauty relative to all other people?
A. Sarah's beauty was equal to Rachel's but less than Eve's
B. All people compared to Sarah are like a monkey compared to a human
C. Sarah was the most beautiful woman but less beautiful than Adam
D. Sarah's beauty is compared to the brightness of the sun
Correct: B — The Gemara states: 'All people compared to Sarah are like a monkey compared to a human.' Sarah's extraordinary beauty traces back through Eve to Adam, who bore the original Divine image.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How did Rabbi Bena'a identify the legitimate son among the ten sons of the wife who confessed infidelity?
A. He examined their physical resemblance to the deceased father
B. He divided the estate equally among all ten and watched who complained
C. He told all sons to strike the father's grave — the one son who refused out of respect was named the heir
D. He summoned the mother and compelled her to confess
Correct: C — Rabbi Bena'a instructed all sons to go strike the father's grave. All complied except one, who refused out of filial respect. Rabbi Bena'a told that son: 'You are the legitimate son — the estate belongs to you.'
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the Halakhic distinction between a Tyrian ladder and an Egyptian ladder regarding chazakah?
A. A Tyrian ladder is used only at night; an Egyptian ladder is used during the day
B. A Tyrian ladder is large and permanently fixed, establishing chazakah; an Egyptian ladder is small and portable, establishing none
C. Both types establish chazakah after three years of use
D. An Egyptian ladder establishes chazakah for placement only, not use
Correct: B — Tyrian ladders (and windows) are large and fixed in place — their permanence establishes an acquired privilege (chazakah). Egyptian versions are small and portable, meaning they can be removed at will and thus no chazakah is established for their use.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rabbi Bena'a corrected a gate inscription that read: 'Any judge summoned to judgment is not a judge.' What was his correction?
A. A judge is disqualified if he refuses to appear before the court
B. A judge is disqualified only if money is lawfully taken from him in judgment — not merely for being summoned
C. All judges who appear in civil court are automatically disqualified in criminal cases
D. A judge from another city cannot be summoned at all
Correct: B — Rabbi Bena'a reasoned: if merely being summoned disqualified a judge, any litigant could neutralize a judge by summoning him falsely. Only a judge who is found liable and has money taken from him in judgment is disqualified.
