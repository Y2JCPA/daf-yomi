🕯️ Rabbi Bena'a at the Cave of Machpela

Rabbi Bena'a
Tell Abraham that Bena'a stands at the entrance — let him assume an appropriate position.
Eliezer (Abraham's servant)
Enter — there is no evil inclination in this place. You may proceed freely.
Rabbi Bena'a
I saw Abraham — his likeness is the image of Adam. I now approach Adam's cave to mark it as well.
Divine Voice (בת קול)
You gazed upon the likeness of My image (Abraham). Do NOT gaze upon My image itself (Adam)!
Rabbi Bena'a
I saw only his two heels — and they were like two suns in their brightness.
Principle Abraham resembles Adam; all human beauty traces back to the Divine image of Adam the first man.

⚖️ Rabbi Bena'a's Four Wise Rulings

Case 1 — The Barrel Riddle: A dying man left a barrel of earth, barrel of bones, and barrel of wool to three sons. Rabbi Bena'a decoded: land (earth), cattle (bones), garments (wool) — each son receives his inheritance.
↓
Case 2 — The Illegitimate Sons: A wife confessed only one of her ten sons was the husband's. Rabbi Bena'a said: All sons go strike the father's grave. The one son who refused out of respect was named the legitimate heir.
↓
Case 3 — The Water Skin Riddle: Rabbi Bena'a's wife told guards: "I had one servant — they cut off his head, skinned him, filled him with water, and gave friends to drink." Rabbi Bena'a decoded: she was describing a zarnuka (water skin).
↓
Case 4 — The Gate Inscription: "Any judge summoned to judgment is not a judge." Rabbi Bena'a corrected: only a judge from whom money is lawfully taken is disqualified — not one merely summoned and later exonerated.
↓
Insight: Rabbi Bena'a's method combined logical decoding of riddles with behavioral tests — observing how people act, not just what they say.

📜 Written at the Gate (Be'abbula) Inscription 1: "At the head of all death am I — blood. At the head of all life am I — wine." Rabbi Bena'a's analysis: For someone who needs bloodletting but has no blood to spare, even wine that normally gives life could hasten death — context determines whether something is life-giving or deadly. Inscription 2 (Kapotekiyya): Anpak, Anbag, Antal — all names for the same unit of measurement, the quarter-log (רביעית) of the Torah.

🪟 Egyptian vs. Tyrian: When Does Chazakah Apply?

Egyptian — No Chazakah for Use

  • Egyptian ladder — small, portable, easily moved
  • Egyptian window — small, does not require large opening
  • Spout — no chazakah for its length/size
  • Owner of field below may demand the spout be shortened
  • The temporary nature means no acquired rights

Tyrian — Chazakah Established

  • Tyrian ladder — large, fixed permanently in place
  • Tyrian window — large, requires significant structural opening
  • Spout placement — chazakah for its location (not its size)
  • Once established, owner below cannot force removal
  • Permanence and visibility create acquired privilege
