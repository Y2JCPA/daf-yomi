Bava Batra 103 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ukva bar Ḥama, what is the specific reason deep crevices are not measured with the rest of the field?
A. They are legally considered a separate domain under rabbinic enactment
B. They are filled with water and unfit for sowing, so they cannot be consecrated under the Torah's field-valuation system
C. Their depth makes them physically unmeasurable with standard tools
D. They were excluded by tradition from Sinai without a stated reason
Correct: B — Rav Ukva bar Ḥama explains that the mishna discusses water-filled crevices. Since Lev. 27:16 ties field consecration to seed capacity, land unfit for sowing falls outside the valuation system entirely.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the baraita derive from the extra word 'field' in Leviticus 27:19?
A. That only a full beit kor can be consecrated — smaller areas are excluded
B. That even very small areas fit for sowing (down to a half-tarkav) can be consecrated
C. That a field must be appraised by three judges before consecration
D. That consecration applies only to fields owned for at least one year
Correct: B — The baraita uses the redundant 'field' to extend the consecration laws to any area fit for sowing, no matter how small — including a letekh, se'a, tarkav, or even a half-tarkav.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the new Mishna (103b), if a seller says 'I sell you a beit kor measured precisely with a rope' and gives slightly less than a beit kor, what is the halakha?
A. The buyer has no claim if the deficit is less than one-sixth
B. The seller must make up the deficit no matter how small the shortage
C. The sale is void and the full price must be returned
D. The buyer may deduct only if the shortage exceeds a half-se'a
Correct: B — The phrase 'measured precisely with a rope' (b'chevel) signals exact measurement. Any deviation — no matter how small — requires correction. This waives the standard one-sixth tolerance that applies to approximate language.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rabbi Yitzḥak's ruling about rocks smaller than ten handbreadths that are included in a field's measurement?
A. They pass automatically to the buyer as part of the field with no separate accounting
B. They must be sold separately, with the seller pricing them at the rate of the best land in the field
C. They are assessed at half the normal field rate since they impede plowing
D. The buyer may demand their removal before the sale is finalized
Correct: B — Rabbi Yitzḥak rules that although small rocks are measured with the field, they must be bought separately. The seller charges for them at the highest-quality land rate in the field.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which of the following dilemmas raised in Daf 103 is explicitly left unresolved (תיקו)?
A. Whether Rav Ukva bar Ḥama or Ravina is correct about crevice exclusion
B. Whether rocks arranged in a ring throughout the greater part of a field are measured with it
C. Whether the beit kor standard applies to the Temple treasury's assessment
D. Whether a seller who used 'approximately' language must refund any amount
Correct: B — Rabbi Yirmeya's dilemma about rocks arranged in a ring (also: a row, angle, or other geometric pattern) scattered throughout the greater part of a field is left unresolved — as are the related dilemmas of Rav Pappa and Rav Ashi.
