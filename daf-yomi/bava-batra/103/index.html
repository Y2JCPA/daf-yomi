💧 Why Are Deep Crevices Excluded?

Gemara
Why not measure deep crevices with the field? At minimum, they should be consecrated separately and require their own redemption!
Rav Ukva bar Ḥama
The mishna in Arakhin deals with crevices filled with water. They are unfit for sowing — and Lev. 27:16 anchors field consecration to seed capacity. No sowing = no consecration.
Gemara
If unfitness for sowing is the reason, then even crevices LESS than ten handbreadths should also be excluded from the field!
Ravina
The mishna treats crevices like rocks — just as rocks are unfit for sowing, so too these crevices. The ten-handbreadth line applies only in the context of land sales, not consecration.
📜 Key Verse "Then your valuation shall be according to the seed required for it" (Lev. 27:16) — only land fit for sowing participates in the Torah's fixed field-valuation system.

📜 Baraita: Consecrating Any Size of Field

Base verse: "And if he that sanctified the field will redeem it" (Lev. 27:19) — why repeat the word field if all prior verses already discuss a field?
↓
Standard case covered: The plain meaning applies to consecrating a standard beit kor (an area requiring one kor of seed, approx. 50×50 amot)
↓
Extra word extends the law: The redundant 'field' teaches that even smaller areas — a letekh, half-letekh, se'a, tarkav, or half-tarkav — can be consecrated
↓
Governing principle: Any area fit for sowing, regardless of size, can be consecrated and must be redeemed at the Torah's fixed valuation rate
↓
Rav Ukva's limit: Areas unfit for sowing — water-filled crevices, barren rocks — are excluded from this valuation system entirely and cannot be formally consecrated
