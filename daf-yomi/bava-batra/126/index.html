👑 Rav Pappa vs Rav Pappi: Did the Firstborn Waive His Double Portion?

Rav Pappa
A firstborn who took an equal portion waived his double portion only for that specific field — because a firstborn has no right to his double portion before the division of the property.
Rav Pappi
He has waived his double portion for all the property — because a firstborn does have a right to his double portion before division, so taking an equal share shows he relinquished his claim entirely.
Practical Consequence If the firstborn sells his share before division — Rav Pappa: sale of double portion is void; Rav Pappi: sale of double portion is valid, but sale of brother's share is always void.

🍇 Firstborn Protests Enhancement of Inherited Property

Brothers inherit grapes on a vine / olives on trees from their father
↓
Firstborn protests the harvesting
Retains his double portion of the enhanced value — Rav Asi
Firstborn does not protest
Loses double portion of enhancement — he consented to joint labor
↓
❓ What if brothers tread the grapes into wine — does the firstborn get double of the wine?
↓
Rav Ukva bar Ḥama: Ruling refers to treading — brothers acquire via labor transformation. Firstborn gets double of pre-tread value only, not the enhanced wine.

⚖️ Rav Pappa vs Rav Pappi: Does the Firstborn Have Rights Before Division?

Rav Pappa

  • Firstborn has NO right to double portion before division
  • Taking equal share: waiver only affects that divided field
  • Sale of double portion before division = void
  • Supported by ruling from Eretz Yisrael

Rav Pappi

  • Firstborn DOES have right to double portion before division
  • Taking equal share: waiver affects ALL remaining property
  • Sale of double portion before division = valid
  • Sale of brother's share = void regardless

📜 Stipulations That Violate Torah Inheritance Law

האומר: פלוני בני בכורי לא יטול פי שניים — לא אמר כלום
MISHNA "So-and-so my firstborn son will not take a double portion" — he has said nothing; stipulation is void.

"So-and-so my son will not inherit among his brothers" — he has said nothing.

Exception — Deathbed Gift: If one apportions his estate as a gift, increasing one son's share, reducing another's, or equalizing the firstborn's portion — this is valid, as it is framed as a gift, not an inheritance modification.

🧬 Tumtum: Indeterminate Sex and Firstborn Rights

Tumtum: A person born with covered/indeterminate sexual organs — sex unknown at birth
↓
Later: skin perforates, genitals exposed — confirmed to be male
↓
❓ Is he the 'firstborn' entitled to a double portion of his father's estate?
↓
Rabbi Ami rules: NO double portion
Verse says "the firstborn of his vigor" (Deut. 21:17) — requires definitively male from the moment of birth, not merely confirmed later
↓
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak adds: Tumtum found male is also not judged as a stubborn and rebellious son — verse says "yihyeh (will be) a son", meaning son-status must be certain from the outset
