🌳 Buying Trees: Land Acquisition & Bikkurim

Buys 2 Trees

  • Rabbis: ❌ Does NOT acquire the ground
  • Rabbi Meir: ✅ DOES acquire the ground
  • Brings bikkurim (first fruits)
  • Does NOT recite the bikkurim passage
  • Uncertainty: does he truly own land?

Buys 3 Trees

  • ✅ Acquires the ground (all agree)
  • Brings bikkurim AND recites the passage
  • Field owner may trim branches if they grow
  • Growth from stumps belongs to buyer
  • A defined portion of field was acquired

🌾 Shmuel's Ruling: Even Marketplace Buyers?

Rav Yehuda citing Shmuel
Rabbi Meir would obligate even one who buys fruit from the marketplace to bring bikkurim — not only one who grew fruit on his own tree!
Gemara (challenge)
But the verse says 'which you shall bring in from YOUR land' (Deut. 26:2) — implying the fruit must come from land you own!
Gemara (answer)
That verse excludes stolen fruit from bikkurim. A separate verse ('choicest first fruits of YOUR land,' Exod. 23:19) excludes fruit grown on gentile land in Eretz Yisrael.
Rabba (objection)
A baraita contradicts Shmuel! It teaches: one who buys ONE tree brings bikkurim without recitation — this is Rabbi Meir. He requires land ownership after all!
Conclusion Shmuel's sweeping ruling is challenged by a baraita showing Rabbi Meir still distinguished between cases — the dispute remains complex.

📜 Resolving Complications of Bikkurim Without Recitation

Problem 1: Perhaps these are not true bikkurim — bringing non-sacred fruit to the Temple courtyard is prohibited!
↓
Solution: The case is where he consecrates them before entering the courtyard.
↓
Problem 2: How can a priest eat them? Consecrated items have restrictions on who may partake!
↓
Solution: He gives teruma gedola to a priest per Rabbi Elazar ben Azarya: teruma goes to priests, tithes to Levites — the priest eats it as either bikkurim or teruma.
↓
Problem 3: Is recitation not indispensable? Rabbi Zeira: 'anything suitable for mixing — failure to mix does not invalidate.' Does omitting recitation invalidate the bikkurim?
↓
Resolution: The case involves fruits rendered exempt from recitation — e.g., sent via an agent, or harvested at night (per Rabbi Yosei bar Ḥanina).

🌿 Identifying the Four Trees

קסיטה — אילן דקורלין | ארונים — אילן דאריי | ארמונים — דולבי | אלמוגים — קסיטא
Opening Passage — Tree Names The daf opens resolving the identity of the four tree-types from the prior discussion: • Kasita = coral trees • Aronim = laurel trees (arei) • Armonim = plane trees (dulevei) • Almugim = coral trees (kasita) Two traditions disagree on which term maps to which tree — particularly regarding 'almugim' and 'kasita.'
