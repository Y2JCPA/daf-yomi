Bava Batra 81 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna (and the Rabbis), how many trees must one buy in another's field to acquire the ground?
A. One tree
B. Two trees
C. Three trees
D. Five trees
Correct: C — The Mishna rules that buying three trees acquires the ground. Buying two trees does not acquire the ground according to the Rabbis — though Rabbi Meir disagrees.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Shmuel say in Rabbi Meir's name regarding bikkurim obligations?
A. Only landowners are ever obligated in bikkurim
B. Even one who buys fruit from the marketplace is obligated to bring bikkurim
C. Bikkurim applies only to the seven species grown on one's own land
D. The recitation passage is always required when bringing bikkurim
Correct: B — Rav Yehuda citing Shmuel states that Rabbi Meir would obligate even a marketplace fruit buyer to bring bikkurim — showing that in Rabbi Meir's view, land ownership is not the core requirement.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rabba's explanation for why Rabbi Meir requires bikkurim (without recitation) for a buyer of one tree?
A. Rabbi Meir holds one tree definitively acquires the land
B. Rabbi Meir is uncertain (safek) whether buying one tree acquires the land
C. Rabbi Meir holds the recitation was never biblically required
D. Rabbi Meir exempts single-tree buyers from bikkurim entirely
Correct: B — Rabba explains that Rabbi Meir is uncertain whether purchasing one tree acquires the land. He therefore requires bikkurim (in case it does) but not the recitation (in case it doesn't) — this is the safek principle.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why must bikkurim brought under doubtful status be consecrated before entering the Temple courtyard?
A. To fulfill the rabbinic requirement for all bikkurim
B. To prevent the prohibition of bringing non-sacred fruit into the courtyard
C. Because consecration replaces the need for recitation
D. To allow the owner to redeem them if he changes his mind
Correct: B — The Gemara raises the concern that if these fruits are not definite bikkurim, bringing them unconsecrated into the Temple courtyard would violate the prohibition against bringing non-sacred (chullin) items into the sacred space.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Elazar ben Azarya's baraita, to whom is teruma gedola given, and to whom is first tithe given?
A. Both teruma and first tithe are given to priests
B. Teruma gedola to a priest; first tithe to a Levite
C. Teruma gedola to a Levite; first tithe to a priest
D. Both are given to the Temple treasury
Correct: B — The baraita of Rabbi Elazar ben Azarya states: teruma gedola is given only to a priest, and first tithe is given only to a Levite. This allows the priest to consume the separated teruma gedola whether its status is bikkurim or teruma.
