⚖️ Resolving the Bar Sisin Contradiction

Questioner
Rava rules FOR the buyer in the bar Sisin case, but AGAINST a similar claim elsewhere — there is a contradiction between Rava and Rava!
Gemara
No difficulty. In the bar Sisin case, the seller was ESTABLISHED as having that land in his possession — the parcel was literally called 'of bar Sisin.'
Questioner
And the contradiction between Rav Naḥman and Rav Naḥman?
Gemara
Also resolved. The seller said 'ALL the property I own of bar Sisin' — by using the word 'all,' he implicitly confirmed his ownership, which lends additional credibility to the claim.
📌 Rule Prior established possession (חזקה) strengthens a seller's credibility. Without it, similar claims may fail.

🔀 Two Rulings, Two Contexts — Reconciled

Case A — Bar Sisin (Credible)

  • Land named 'of bar Sisin' = established in seller's possession
  • Seller's claim supported by known prior ownership
  • Saying 'all my property of bar Sisin' self-confirms ownership
  • Ruling: Sale valid — buyer retains the land
  • Key: Prior chazakah + broad sale language

Case B — No Prior Establishment (Weak)

  • Land not established as seller's beforehand
  • No chazakah supporting the seller's underlying claim
  • Seller cannot self-certify ownership by assertion alone
  • Ruling: Claim is insufficient to override dispute
  • Key: Without establishment, credibility fails

The Reconciling Principle

  • Same Amora (Rava or Rav Naḥman) ruled differently in different cases
  • The cases differ in one critical factor: prior establishment
  • Gemara never abandons a ruling — it distinguishes the facts
  • חזקה is always contextual: what was already known?
  • This method is standard Talmudic harmonization

🏠 'What Do You Want With My Land?' — Third-Party Purchase

Original Owner
What do you want with this land of mine?
Possessor
I purchased it from so-and-so, who told me he purchased it from you. And I worked and profited from it for the full chazakah period.
Original Owner
Don't you concede that this land was formerly mine, and that you did NOT purchase it from me? Go away — I am not legally answerable to you!
Rava
The claimant stated the halakha to the possessor. This is a legitimate claim — I accept it. The possessor must pursue his title against the third-party seller.
⚖️ Rava's Ruling An owner who never sold directly to the possessor has no legal obligation to litigate with him. The possessor's quarrel is with whoever sold to him.

🤝 Did Advising to Buy = Legal Concession? The Consultation Case

Claim: Possessor says 'I bought from so-and-so and used the land for chazakah period'
↓
New argument: 'I have witnesses that I consulted you — and YOU told me to go purchase the land!'
↓
Owner's response: 'I advised you only to protect your financial interests — not to concede that the seller had authority to convey my land'
↓
Gemara asks: Is Rava's ruling like Admon (Ketubot 109a)? Admon: one who signs a bill of sale is estopped from contesting the field!
↓
Resolution: Even per the Rabbis — signing a deed is an action creating estoppel. Verbal advice is mere speech. Different cases entirely!
