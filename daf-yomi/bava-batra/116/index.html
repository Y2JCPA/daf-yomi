📜 Who Has 'No Exchange'? — Psalms 55:20

R. Yochanan
"Those that have no exchange" refers to one who does not leave behind a student — spiritual continuity is the irreplaceable legacy.
R. Yehoshua ben Levi
"Those that have no exchange" refers to one who does not leave behind a son — biological continuity is the gravest absence.
Proof (left)
R. Yochanan lost ten sons yet carried the bone of his tenth son to console mourners — showing loss of sons is consolable; loss of students is not.
Proof (right)
R. Yehoshua ben Levi only attended mourning houses for those who died without sons — "Weep for one who goes away" — consistent with sonless death as the gravest loss.
Gemara Resolution Each rabbi's behavior reveals his position. R. Yochanan's consolation via his son's bone proves he holds 'no student' is irreplaceable. R. Yehoshua ben Levi's mourning practice proves he holds 'no son' is the gravest 'no exchange.'

👨‍👧 Daughter of a Son — Inheritance Logic

Can a daughter of a son inherit alongside the brothers of her deceased father?
↓
General Rule
Her right is enhanced: she steps into her father's place and inherits from grandfather even though her father predeceased him
Benjamin Exception
After the Gibeah crisis (only 600 men survived), elders stipulated: daughter of a son shall NOT inherit with her father's brothers — to preserve tribal lands
↓
Principle: Anyone who precedes another in inheritance — his descendants precede the other as well

🔥 Dying Without a Son

כָּל מִי שֶׁאֵינוֹ מַנִּיחַ בֵּן לְיָרְשׁוֹ — הַקָּדוֹשׁ בָּרוּךְ הוּא מָלֵא עֶבְרָה עָלָיו
R. Yochanan in the name of R. Shimon ben Yochai Concerning anyone who does not leave behind a son to inherit from him, the Holy One, Blessed be He, is filled with wrath (evra) toward him — as it is stated regarding the daughters of Zelophehad (Numbers 27:8): the word evra links the inheritance law to divine displeasure at sonlessness.

📚 Two Positions — 'No Exchange' (Psalms 55:20)

R. Yochanan: No Student

  • "No exchange" = leaves no student
  • Carried bone of his 10th son to console mourners
  • Even catastrophic loss of sons has a consolation formula
  • Torah continuity (students) is the truly irreplaceable legacy
  • Implies: R. Yehoshua ben Levi holds 'no son' = no exchange

R. Yehoshua ben Levi: No Son

  • "No exchange" = leaves no son
  • Only attended mourning for those who died sonless
  • Cites: "Weep ye not for the dead… weep for the one who goes away"
  • Going away without sons = the ultimate irreplaceable loss
  • Implies: R. Yochanan holds 'no student' = no exchange

🩺 R. Pinchas ben Hama's Three Homilies (Mnemonic: Hadad · Poverty · Sage)

1. Hadad — The Mark of a Great Son: "David slept with his fathers" — the word sleeping is used only when one leaves a son as great as himself (Solomon). Joab, who left no such son, merely died. When Hadad heard David slept, he knew David's legacy was secured.
↓
2. Poverty — Harder than Fifty Plagues: Poverty in one's household is harder than fifty plagues, as derived from Job 19:21: "Have pity upon me, have pity upon me, O ye my friends, for the hand of God has touched me" — the doubled plea signals a uniquely crushing suffering beyond illness or persecution.
↓
3. Sage — Pray for the Sick: One who has a sick person in his home should go to a sage, who will ask for mercy on the sick person's behalf — as it is written: "The wrath of a king is as messengers of death, but a wise man will pacify it" (Proverbs 16:14). The sage intercedes like one who pacifies a wrathful king.
