5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the tradition (mesorah) cited by Rabbi Levi or Rabbi Yoḥanan about the Ark of the Covenant?
A. The Ark was carried on the shoulders of Levites at all times, never set down
B. The space occupied by the Ark and cherubs is not included in the Temple's measurements
C. The Ark disappeared before the First Temple was destroyed
D. The Ark's gold cover weighed exactly one talent
Correct: B — The tradition states: אין ארון מן המידה — the Ark (and cherubs) occupied miraculous space not counted in the Temple's physical measurements, supporting the idea of supernatural non-occupation of space.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why are TWO locks required on the cistern opening — one for each party?
A. To ensure no water is stolen when the cistern owner is away
B. Biblical law mandates double locks on all shared property
C. To prevent yichud — the cistern owner being secluded with the homeowner's wife
D. To prevent damage to the cistern from overuse
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan explains: Both locks prevent yichud (forbidden seclusion). Without locks, the cistern owner could freely enter the house when the homeowner is absent and be alone with his wife.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the aggadic teaching, when did the cherubs' faces turn AWAY from each other toward the House?
A. When the High Priest entered the Holy of Holies on Yom Kippur
B. When Israel did NOT fulfill God's will
C. When the Temple was first consecrated by Solomon
D. When the Ark was brought in from Kiryat Ye'arim
Correct: B — The cherubs mirrored Israel's spiritual state: facing each other = Israel in God's favor; facing the House (away from each other) = Israel not fulfilling His will, symbolizing divine displeasure.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When Rav Pappa objected to the rule permitting a channel owner to repair banks with earth from the field, what was his argument?
A. The channel owner should buy earth from a separate source
B. The field owner never sold the right to use his field's soil
C. The flowing water in the channel carried away the channel owner's own earth, so he has no right to take the field owner's earth
D. Repairing banks is the responsibility of both owners equally
Correct: C — Rav Pappa argued: 'Your water carried away your earth — you cannot claim my earth as a replacement.' The Gemara resolves this by limiting repair to earth taken from within the channel bed itself.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why, according to Rav Zevid citing Rava, is a field owner prohibited from reclaiming the original public thoroughfare after making an exchange?
A. Torah law permanently transfers any path given to the public
B. Rabbinic decree prevents it, lest owners give circuitous routes that lengthen public travel
C. The court retains ownership of all public thoroughfares
D. Once exchanged, the land becomes ownerless (hefker)
Correct: B — Rav Zevid citing Rava: A rabbinic decree prevents reclaiming the original path. Otherwise, owners would have incentive to give the public a roundabout alternative, lengthening their journey while reclaiming prime land.
