🐝 Shmuel vs. Rabbi Yoḥanan: The Meaning of Mesares

Shmuel
After the buyer takes three swarms, the seller renders the bees impotent — he applies an herb to the queen bee so she can no longer produce new swarms, ending the buyer's entitlement.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Mesares derives from seirus, meaning skipping. The buyer takes every other swarm — the 1st, 3rd, and 5th — while the 2nd, 4th, and 6th remain with the seller. No physical intervention is required.
Shmuel
But the term mesares connotes active neutralization — like sirus (castration). Why use such language if it merely means alternating swarms?
Rabbi Yoḥanan
The root is distinct. Beseirus means 'in a skipping fashion.' This reading preserves the hive's health while fairly defining the buyer's three-swarm portion.
Mishna One who buys the produce of a beehive takes three swarms, and then the seller does mesares.

🍯 Beehive Legal Status: Rabbi Eliezer vs. the Rabbis

Rabbi Eliezer

  • Beehive = like land
  • Purchased via deed, as real estate
  • A prosbol may be written based on it
  • Not susceptible to ritual impurity
  • Removing honey on Shabbat = liable
  • Proof: Jonathan dipped in ya'arat hadevash — ya'ar (forest) implies land status

The Rabbis

  • Beehive = movable property
  • Sold like chattels, no deed required
  • A prosbol may not be written based on it
  • Susceptible to ritual impurity
  • Removing honey on Shabbat = exempt
  • Honey in the hive subject to food/liquid impurity rules

🌲 The Righteous: Like Cedar and Like Palm

צַדִּיק כַּתָּמָר יִפְרָח כְּאֶרֶז בַּלְּבָנוֹן יִשְׂגֶּה (תהילים צב:יג)
Aggadah Rabbi Ḥiyya bar Lulyani explains why Psalms 92:13 mentions both trees:

• If only cedar: One might think the righteous bear no fruit — i.e., no reward in this world, just as the cedar produces no edible fruit

• If only palm: One might think the righteous leave no lasting legacy after death — just as a palm's trunk does not regrow once felled

• Together: The righteous do bear fruit (reward in this world, like palm) and their legacy regrows and endures (like the cedar). Both worlds are theirs.
