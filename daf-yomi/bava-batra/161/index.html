⚖️ Rami bar Ḥama vs. Rav Ḥisda: Tied Document Signatures

Rami bar Ḥama
According to Rav Yirmeya bar Abba, witnesses sign on the back, exactly opposite the writing. But doesn't blank space on the outside allow fraudulent additions?
Rav Ḥisda
Witnesses don't sign top-to-bottom — they sign bottom to top in a perpendicular column. This leaves no blank space for additions.
Rami bar Ḥama
But what if something harmful appears in the final line? The holder could excise it while the signature above remains — 'Ya'akov, witness' still stands!
Rav Ḥisda
The signature is split: 'Reuven son of' sits opposite the last line, and 'Ya'akov, witness' continues upward. Cutting the last line destroys part of the signature itself.
Mar Zutra's Resolution Skip all this — any tied document whose witnesses do not end on a single line is simply invalid. No manipulation possible.

📌 Halakha: When Erasures Invalidate Documents An erasure invalidates a document only if it appears where the confirmation formula "Everything is confirmed and established" should appear — and the erasure is not explicitly listed at the end. Rav Yitzḥak bar Yosef citing Rabbi Yoḥanan: the scribe must write at the end "And this is their verification", enumerating all erasures.

🔄 How Witnesses Sign a Tied Document

Step 1: Document text is written on the front (inside face)
↓
Step 2: Witnesses sign on the back, exactly opposite the written text
↓
Step 3: Signatures run bottom to top — perpendicular to the lines of text, not parallel
↓
Step 4: Each signature spans two positions: e.g., "Reuven son of" opposite the last line + "Ya'akov, witness" opposite the second-to-last line
↓
Step 5 (Mar Zutra): All witness signatures must end on a single line — otherwise the document is entirely invalid

🐟 Can a Name or Mark Substitute for a Signature?

Rami bar Ḥama
Perhaps the witness signed using his father's name out of respect — making 'Ya'akov' a valid signature even after excision of the last line!
Gemara
A person does not discard his own name and sign solely with his father's name. This is simply not done.
Rami bar Ḥama
Perhaps he made the name a mere distinguishing mark — like Rav who drew a fish, or Rabbi Ḥanina who drew a palm branch!
Mar Zutra
Why do you need all this elaborate reasoning? Any tied document whose witnesses don't end on a single line is invalid. One rule eliminates every scenario.
Historical Note Rav signed with a fish (דג — dag), Rabbi Ḥanina with a palm branch. Distinctive symbols served as valid witness marks in Talmudic practice.

📋 Valid vs. Invalid: Document Rules

✅ Valid Document

  • Erasures explicitly listed before confirmation formula
  • Ends with: 'Everything is confirmed and established'
  • Tied doc: all witness signatures end on a single line
  • Signatures run bottom to top, perpendicular
  • No blank space left outside the signature column
  • Witness identity verifiable (name or known mark)

❌ Invalid Document

  • Erasure in location of confirmation formula — undeclared
  • Erasures not listed at end of document
  • Tied doc: witnesses' lines do not converge on single line
  • Blank space outside signatures — open to fraud
  • Signature unverifiable or easily forged after excision
  • Word 'witness' written in manipulable position
