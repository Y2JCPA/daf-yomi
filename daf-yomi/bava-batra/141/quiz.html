Bava Batra 141 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda, what is the significance of a woman giving birth to a daughter first?
A. It is a bad omen and the father should mourn
B. It is a good sign that sons will follow
C. It obligates the father to give her 200 dinars by law
D. It means the mother will not conceive again
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda teaches that a first-born daughter is a good sign (סִימָן יָפֶה) for sons to follow — Shmuel applies this to explain why the mishna's father offers more for a daughter: he celebrates her as a harbinger of future sons.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rav Huna rule that a gift made to a fetus does not take effect?
A. Because the father's wording was legally ambiguous
B. Because a fetus has absolutely no legal standing in any context
C. Because a gift requires a formal act of acquisition (kinyan), which a fetus cannot perform
D. Because only the Beit Din can authorize gifts to unborn children
Correct: C — The Gemara's core distinction: a gift (מַתָּנָה) requires a formal kinyan by the recipient. A fetus has no capacity to perform kinyan. Inheritance is categorically different because it vests automatically without any act.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why is Rabbi Meir's opinion ultimately rejected as a basis for a fetus receiving a gift?
A. Rabbi Meir actually forbids all transfers of future assets
B. Rabbi Meir's rule allows transfer to existing persons — he never addressed transferring to a fetus
C. Rabbi Meir's opinion was overruled by the later Amoraim
D. Rabbi Meir only addressed inheritance, not gifts
Correct: B — Rabbi Meir permits transferring ownership of something 'not yet in the world' — but the Gemara clarifies this means to an existing recipient. He never ruled on transferring to a fetus, which is a different and more extreme case.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the fundamental distinction between inheritance and a gift regarding a fetus's ability to acquire?
A. Inheritance requires a court document while gifts are informal
B. Gifts can only be given to daughters; inheritance goes only to sons
C. Inheritance vests automatically without kinyan; gifts require kinyan which a fetus cannot perform
D. There is no distinction — the Gemara treats them identically
Correct: C — The Gemara explicitly states: 'Inheritance is different since it comes by itself' — no formal act of acquisition is needed. Therefore a fetus can inherit but cannot receive a gift, which requires kinyan.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Gemara's resolution, why does the mishna's father offer MORE dinars for a daughter than for a son?
A. Because daughters are legally preferred over sons in all monetary matters
B. For inheritance a son is preferred (carries the name), but for monetary provision a daughter needs more support and a dowry
C. Because the father was following a minority opinion that daughters inherit double
D. Because Rabbi Yehuda ruled all daughters receive 200 dinars at birth
Correct: B — The Gemara resolves the apparent contradiction: for inheritance, a son is preferred because he carries the father's name and tribal heritage. But for monetary gifts and provision, a daughter requires more — she needs a dowry and ongoing support — so the father offers 200 dinars for her birth.
