Bava Batra 136 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, what language must a healthy person write when gifting property to his sons while retaining benefit until death?
A. I give this property after my death
B. From today and after my death
C. I hereby transfer all rights to my sons now
D. This property belongs to my sons from this day forward
Correct: B — The Mishna requires the specific phrase 'from today and after my death' (Tanna Kamma's view), establishing that the grant begins now but full possession passes at death. Rabbi Yosei disputes that any such language is required.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Whose opinion does Rav rule the halakha follows regarding whether 'from today' language is necessary in a gift document?
A. The first Tanna of the Mishna
B. Rav Naḥman
C. Rabbi Yosei
D. Rabba bar Avuh
Correct: C — Rav Huna asked Rav Naḥman to pose the question to the ill Rabba bar Avuh, who reported that Rav rules the halakha follows Rabbi Yosei — a proper deed suffices without the 'from today' clause.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Huna son of Rav Yehoshua, when a kinyan (act of acquisition) is performed alongside a gift deed, what is the effect on the 'from today' requirement?
A. The kinyan makes the requirement stricter — both kinyan and phrase are needed
B. The ordering of deed language determines whether the phrase is required
C. A kinyan always eliminates the need to write 'from today,' regardless of wording order
D. A kinyan is only effective for movable property
Correct: C — Rav Huna son of Rav Yehoshua rejected Rav Pappi's distinction based on word order and ruled that any kinyan — regardless of whether the deed says 'transferred then acquired' or 'acquired then transferred' — eliminates the 'from today' requirement.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Rabbi Yoḥanan rule if a son sells property (gifted 'from today and after my death') during his father's lifetime?
A. The sale is fully valid and the buyer takes immediate possession
B. The sale is valid but the buyer must wait until the father's death
C. The purchaser did not acquire the property at all
D. The father may choose to ratify or void the son's sale
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan holds that ownership of usufruct is like ownership of the item itself — since the father owns the produce rights, the son effectively owns nothing transferable, and the purchaser acquires nothing.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The dispute between Rabbi Yoḥanan and Reish Lakish about the son's sale during the father's lifetime is rooted in which fundamental disagreement?
A. Whether a minor can inherit property during a parent's lifetime
B. Whether ownership of usufruct (peiros) equals ownership of the item (guf) itself
C. Whether a deed requires two witnesses to be legally valid
D. Whether a conditional gift can be revoked by the donor
Correct: B — The entire dispute turns on whether peiros (produce/usufruct rights) constitutes full ownership of the corpus. Rabbi Yoḥanan says yes — blocking the son's sale; Reish Lakish says no — permitting it. This principle generates parallel disputes throughout the Talmud, including first-fruits obligations and sequential inheritance.
