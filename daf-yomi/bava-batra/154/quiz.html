QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, how must recipients prove that a shechiv mera's gift is valid and irrevocable?
A. By ratifying the deed before a court of three judges
B. By bringing witnesses who testify the giver was healthy
C. By having the giver's heirs sign a release
D. By presenting the deed within thirty days of the gift
Correct: B — Rav Huna holds that recipients prove validity by bringing witnesses who testify the giver was healthy at the time of the gift. Ratifying the deed alone is insufficient under his view.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the mnemonic מנ"יח (Manniaḥ) indicate about the tannaitic dispute?
A. The four categories of bailees discussed in Bava Metzia
B. That Rabbi Meir's position aligns with Rabbi Natan's, and the Rabbis' position aligns with Rabbi Ya'akov's
C. The sequence of tractates in Seder Nezikin
D. The four sages who signed the disputed deed in the baraita
Correct: B — מנ"יח = Meir, Natan, Ya'akov, Ḥakhamim. The mnemonic signals that the same underlying dispute — whether a giver can impair his deed — appears in multiple tannaitic contexts with different speaker names.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the Bnei Brak incident, why does Reish Lakish conclude that the buyers (not the family) held presumptive possession?
A. Because the buyers produced a ratified deed
B. Because Rabbi Akiva told the buyers to bring witnesses and they could not
C. Because Rabbi Akiva told the family they could not disgrace the deceased by exhuming his body
D. Because the family had no written documentation of their claim
Correct: C — Reish Lakish argues: Rabbi Akiva saying 'you may not disgrace him' (by exhuming the body) makes sense only if the family was the contesting party challenging the buyers' possession — proving the buyers held ḥazaka.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is a שטר פסים (shtar passim)?
A. A promissory note secured by real property
B. A deed ratified by witnesses in court
C. A document written only to make the holder appear wealthy, not reflecting a real transaction
D. A deed issued before payment is received, based on trust
Correct: C — A shtar passim (document of appeasement) is written solely to give the holder the appearance of wealth — no actual transaction occurred. It cannot be used to establish legal ownership of property.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the content of Rav Yosef's resolution (citing Rav Yehuda citing Shmuel) to the contradictions between the mishna and the baraita of bar Kappara?
A. The baraita of bar Kappara is a forgery and should be disregarded
B. The mishna and baraita discuss different types of deeds — gifted vs. sold property
C. The opinions of Rabbi Meir and the Rabbis should be reversed in the mishna
D. Both sources agree but use different terminology for the same rule
Correct: C — Rav Yosef says the tannaitic attributions must be reversed: what the mishna assigns to the Rabbis belongs to Rabbi Meir and vice versa. This resolves the apparent contradictions between the mishna and the baraita of bar Kappara.
