Bava Batra 165 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav, which transgression does EVERYONE succumb to?
A. Robbery (גזל)
B. Sexual immorality (עריות)
C. Hints of malicious speech (אבק לשון הרע)
D. False oaths (שבועת שוא)
Correct: C — Rav teaches: the majority sin with robbery, a minority with sexual matters, but EVERYONE succumbs to avak lashon hara — even hints or traces of malicious speech that people utter without realizing.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the common principle Abaye derives from Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel, Rabbi Shimon, and Rabbi Elazar?
A. An agent who deviates from instructions must compensate the principal
B. When one instructs an agent, he merely indicates his position — not an absolute insistence on details
C. An agent must follow instructions exactly or the transaction is void
D. Regional custom always overrides personal instructions to an agent
Correct: B — Abaye shows from all three authorities that instructing an agent expresses a general preference (גילוי דעת בעלמא) rather than an absolute requirement — so deviations that benefit the principal are valid.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Ameimar validated a document with one written witness signature and one oral witness. What was Abaye's ruling on such a document?
A. Valid — the two forms of testimony combine sufficiently
B. Valid only if the oral witness also signs afterward
C. Not valid — written and oral testimony cannot combine
D. Valid only for loans above 100 dinars
Correct: C — Abaye ruled that combining a written witness signature with oral testimony does NOT validate the document — the mishna explicitly teaches this, and Rav Ashi cited Abaye's ruling against Ameimar.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why was Rabbi Yirmeya readmitted to the study hall after having been expelled (as mentioned at the end of this section)?
A. He publicly apologized to the Sages for his earlier behavior
B. Rav Ashi personally vouched for his scholarship
C. His wise responsum to the dilemma sent from Eretz Yisrael demonstrated his greatness
D. He brought a baraita that vindicated his original position
Correct: C — The Gemara explicitly states it was because of his fitting answer — to whichever version of the dilemma is adopted — that the Sages brought Rabbi Yirmeya back into the study hall.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
A document states: 'One hundred dinars, which are twenty sela.' What amount may the holder claim, and why?
A. 100 dinars — the higher numerical figure prevails
B. 25 sela — the correct halakhic equivalent of 100 dinars
C. 20 sela — the lesser of the two stated amounts
D. Nothing — an internally contradictory document is entirely void
Correct: C — The mishna rules that when a document is internally contradictory (100 dinars actually equals 25 sela, not 20), the holder collects only the lesser stated amount — here, 20 sela — since ambiguity resolves in the debtor's favor.
