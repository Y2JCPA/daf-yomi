💍 Does the Widow Have Standing in the Orphans' Estate?

Gemara
What is the deceased's wife doing with the orphans' property? She is merely a creditor for her ketubah — why should her enhancements go to the middle at all?
Answer
Since she delays collecting her ketubah and remains in the household, she is treated like any other heir. Her enhancements to the estate go to the middle.
New Objection
Couldn't we say: since it is not a woman's way to toil and enhance property, even if she did, she intended it only for herself — not for the shared estate?
Resolution
The mishna must therefore explicitly teach: she enhanced it for the middle. Her toil is credited to the shared estate just as any heir's labor would be.
Ruling A widow who remains and enhances the estate — profits go to the middle, shared with the orphans.

What exactly did the father designate?
↓

Son acquires the house

Son acquires upper story
NOT the lower house beneath it

📯 Brothers as Partners: Forced Service vs. Voluntary Trade

Forced Imperial Service

  • One brother summoned on behalf of all
  • Costs and benefits → to the middle
  • True even if he was chosen because he is sharper or smarter
  • He serves on account of the family's collective obligation
  • Result: shared burden, shared benefit

Voluntary Trade or Study

  • Brother engages in trade on his own initiative
  • Profits → his alone; losses → his alone
  • Went to study Torah or learn a trade independently
  • Brothers may say: 'If here, we'd support you; absent, we need not'
  • Rav Huna: blessing of the house is in its abundance

אמר מר זוטרא: שלשה דברים נאמרו הלכה ואין להם טעם
Mar Zutra's Three Unexplained Enactments Mar Zutra taught that the Sages enacted three halakhot whose legal mechanism is unclear, yet their ruling stands:

1. 🏠 A father who designates a house for his son's wedding — the son acquires it as a gift.
2. 🎁 Groomsmen gifts that return to the yavam when a brother dies childless.
3. [A third matter — the Gemara develops each in turn.]

These are termed halakha without explanation of their process — authoritative by enactment, not derivation.
