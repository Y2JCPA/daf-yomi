🗣️ Rav's Dilemma: Acquiring a Gift

Rav
I raise a dilemma: for a gift, must the giver say 'Go, take possession (חזק וקנה)' for the recipient to acquire it — just as a seller must when the sale is not done in his presence?
Shmuel
What dilemma is raised to Abba (Rav)? Surely the answer is obvious — a gift follows the same rule as a sale and requires verbal authorization!
Rav
Not so fast. In a sale, the seller must authorize because he is transferring ownership he retains. In a gift, the giver's intent to give is already clear — perhaps no verbal declaration is needed.
Gemara
The dilemma is left unresolved (teiku). The question of gift acquisition by chazakah without verbal declaration remains open in halacha.
Context A sale not performed in the seller's presence requires the seller to say 'Go take possession' (חזק וקנה). Rav questions whether this verbal authorization is equally required when someone receives a gift.

🧱 Fence Chazakah: The 'A Bit' Standard

One builds or breaches a fence 'a bit'
Measure = 10 tefachim (Shmuel)
↓
Building fence — invalid case
Before: Could not climb over
After: Still cannot climb over
→ Nothing changed. No chazakah.
Building fence — valid case
Before: Could climb over
After: Cannot climb over
→ Meaningful change. Valid chazakah!
↓
Breaching fence — invalid case
Before: Could already enter
After: Still can enter
→ Nothing changed. No chazakah.
Breaching fence — valid case
Before: Could not enter
After: Can now enter
→ Meaningful change. Valid chazakah!
↓
Principle: Only an act that changes the field's accessibility or condition constitutes valid chazakah

🪨 Placing and Removing a Stone — Rav Asi citing Rabbi Yochanan

Placing a Stone

  • Valid: Placed stone in fence → stopped floodwater from entering the field
  • Invalid: Placed stone with no functional effect on the field
  • Analogy: like chasing someone's animal — only counts if it accomplishes something
  • The act must improve or protect the property's condition

Removing a Stone

  • Valid: Removed stone from fence → released trapped floodwater from the field
  • Invalid: Removed stone that had no effect on the field's drainage
  • Key question: did the act serve the field's needs?
  • Rule: 'Placed a stone that helps' or 'removed a stone that helps' = valid chazakah

🏛️ Acquiring a Convert's (גר) Ownerless Property

Background: When a convert (גר) dies without Jewish heirs, his property is ownerless (hefker). The first person to perform a valid chazakah acquires it entirely.
↓
Building Palaces (פלטרין) — Rav Nachman/Rabba bar Avuh: One builds large palaces on the land. If a second person arrives and locks the door first, that second person acquires the entire property — locking = chazakah.
↓
Plastering or Tiling — Rav Dimi bar Yosef citing Rabbi Elazar: Finds already-built palaces on the land and applies even one coat of plaster or lays one tile → acquires the entire property.
↓
Spreading Mattresses — Rav Sheshet from a baraita: One who spreads mattresses on the convert's property acquires it. This is derived from the law of slave acquisition — just as spreading mattresses for a master demonstrates service and control.
↓
Rabbi Shimon's clarification: The first tanna holds that a slave is acquired even by lifting the master's garment; Rabbi Shimon requires actually lifting the master himself. Rav Yirmeya Bira'a citing Rav Yehuda continues the discussion.
