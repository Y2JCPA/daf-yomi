Bava Batra 104 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman, what is the built-in tolerance ratio that a buyer need not return?
A. One full kav per se'a
B. One quarter-kav per se'a (= 7.5 kav per kor)
C. Half a kav per kor
D. One se'a per kor
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman rules that 7.5 kav per kor (= ¼ kav per se'a) is a built-in tolerance. Only if surplus remains after subtracting this allowance — AND reaches 9 kav — must the buyer return it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the surplus threshold at which a buyer of a field must return the land itself (not merely pay money)?
A. Seven and a half kav
B. Half a kav
C. One se'a
D. Nine kav
Correct: D — The mishna teaches: if the surplus in a field equals the sowing area of nine kav, the buyer must return the land to the seller. Below that, he pays money instead.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was the common thread in all three of Rava's objections to Rav Naḥman?
A. That gardens always take precedence over fields in land disputes
B. That the seller's intention controls the threshold, not the field size
C. That the 9-kav and ½-kav thresholds are absolute, not proportional to the field sold
D. That money payment is always preferable to returning land
Correct: C — Rava argued three times that Rav Naḥman's proportional calculation is wrong — the mishna's thresholds (9 kav for fields, ½ kav for gardens, ¼ kav per Rabbi Akiva) are fixed absolute amounts with no reference to the total size of the field sold.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When may a seller insist on return of land rather than accept a money payment for the surplus?
A. Whenever the surplus exceeds 7.5 kav
B. When the surplus abuts the seller's adjacent field — even if the surplus is minimal
C. Only when the surplus equals exactly nine kav
D. Only when the land's value has increased since the time of sale
Correct: B — A baraita teaches that if the surplus is adjacent to another field belonging to the seller, the seller can demand that even a minimal surplus be returned as land — compelling the buyer to return a contiguous parcel rather than paying money.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If a seller says 'I am selling you a field measured precisely with a rope, more or less,' what is the legal outcome?
A. The precise measurement controls; any deviation entitles the buyer to cancel
B. The sale is entirely void due to contradictory conditions
C. The 'more or less' clause nullifies the precise measurement; buyer accepts whatever is delivered
D. The seller may demand return of any surplus at any time
Correct: C — The new mishna at the end of the daf rules that when two contradictory stipulations are stated together — 'precisely with a rope' and 'more or less' — the 'more or less' clause prevails and nullifies the precision requirement. The buyer accepts the land as-is.
