⚖️ Rava vs. Rav Naḥman: Is the 9-Kav Threshold Absolute or Proportional?

Rav Naḥman
Calculate 7.5 kav per kor (= ¼ kav per se'a). Only if there is STILL a surplus of 9 kav AFTER this built-in allowance must the buyer return the land.
Rava
Objection 1 — Field: The mishna says a 9-kav surplus triggers return even if the field sold was smaller than one kor! No minimum field size is stated.
Rava
Objection 2 — Garden: The mishna says a ½-kav surplus triggers return regardless of garden size. Your proportional ratio would never apply to a tiny garden.
Rava
Objection 3 — Rabbi Akiva: His ¼-kav threshold for gardens also states no proportional calculation. All three clauses use fixed, absolute amounts.
Teku — Unresolved Rava's three objections stand unrefuted. The 9-kav (field) and ½-kav (garden) thresholds are absolute fixed amounts, not ratios proportional to field size.

🌾 Field vs. Garden: Surplus Return Thresholds

🌾 Field

  • Surplus threshold: 9 kav (sowing area)
  • Below 9 kav → buyer pays money
  • At/above 9 kav → buyer returns land
  • Tolerance: ¼ kav per se'a (7.5 kav/kor)
  • Entire surplus returned, not just excess

🏡 Garden (Rabbis)

  • Surplus threshold: ½ kav (sowing area)
  • Gardens more valuable → lower threshold
  • Below ½ kav → buyer pays money
  • At/above ½ kav → buyer returns land
  • Rava: absolute amount, not proportional

🏡 Garden (Rabbi Akiva)

  • Surplus threshold: ¼ kav (sowing area)
  • Even stricter than the Sages' ruling
  • Gardens are especially precious — smallest patch matters
  • Also an absolute fixed amount
  • Rava's 3rd objection targets this clause

🏘️ Adjacent Field: Must Seller Accept Money?

Baraita
If the surplus abuts the seller's own adjacent field, even a minimal surplus triggers return of land — the seller cannot be forced to accept money payment.
Rav Ashi
Dilemma 1: What if there is a pit between the surplus and the adjacent field? Does it constitute an interposition that breaks the adjacency?
Rav Ashi
Dilemma 2: What about a fence between them? A fence may or may not create legal separation — does the seller still get land returned?
Rav Ashi
Dilemma 3: What about a furrow between the two plots? These questions remain unresolved — teiku.
Rationale The adjacent-field rule protects the seller from having to 'chase around' a buyer for scattered parcels. When fields are contiguous, land return is especially fair — but physical barriers may alter this logic.

📜 New Mishna: When Stipulations Contradict Each Other

מדה בחבל יתר וחסר — יתר וחסר מבטל את המדה
Ruling If a seller says: 'I am selling you a field measured precisely with a rope, more or less' — two contradictory conditions are stated. The Sages rule: the words 'more or less' nullify and override the precise measurement clause. The buyer accepts whatever land he receives, and has no claim for adjustment in either direction.
