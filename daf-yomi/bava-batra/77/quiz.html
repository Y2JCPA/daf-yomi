Bava Batra 77 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
When Rav Ashi asked Ameimar whether his ruling that documents are acquired by transfer was based on tradition or logic, what did Ameimar say?
A. It is based on a tradition received from his teachers
B. It is based on his own logical reasoning
C. It is based on a Biblical verse (d'oraita)
D. He declined to answer until he could consult further
Correct: B — Ameimar said it was based on logical reasoning — which meant Rav Ashi was entitled to challenge and refute the logic if he could.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, if a seller says 'Take possession of this field for so-and-so on condition that you write him a document,' and the document has NOT yet been delivered, what can the seller retract?
A. He can retract the document only, but not the field
B. He cannot retract anything — the deal is final
C. He can retract both the document and the field
D. He can retract the field only, but not the document
Correct: C — In the conditional document case, before delivery, the seller can retract both the document and the field — because the document is essential to the transaction, not merely incidental.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How did Rav Pappa transfer ownership of his 12,000 dinars in Bei Ḥozai to his agent Rav Shmuel bar Aḥa?
A. By writing a formal document (shtar) of transfer
B. By means of the threshold of his own house
C. By verbal declaration before a beit din
D. By sending a messenger with symbolic coins
Correct: B — Rav Pappa used his house threshold as a tangible local object to effect kinyan for the distant money — illustrating that money in a distant location can be transferred via a physically present object.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Mishna, if one sells a yoke (tzemed), which of the following is correct?
A. The oxen are always included in the sale
B. The oxen are included only if the price is high enough
C. The Rabbis say oxen are not included; Rabbi Yehuda disagrees based on price
D. The sale includes whatever the buyer claims was discussed orally
Correct: C — The Rabbis hold that selling a yoke does not include the oxen. Rabbi Yehuda holds that the sum of money paid can prove the oxen were intended to be included (הדמים מודיעים).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The Gemara explains that the dispute between Rabbi Yehuda and the Rabbis about tzimda arises specifically in what situation?
A. Where tzimda unambiguously means 'yoke' in all local dialects
B. Where the buyer and seller are from different towns
C. Where some people call a yoke tzimda and others call oxen tzimda
D. Where no witnesses were present at the time of the sale
Correct: C — The dispute arises because tzimda is linguistically ambiguous — used by some for the yoke and by others for the oxen. Rabbi Yehuda holds the price paid resolves this ambiguity; the Rabbis disagree.
