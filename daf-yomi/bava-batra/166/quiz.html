Bava Batra 166 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, what does the plural form 'dinarei' (דִּינָרֵי) indicate in a legal document?
A. Silver dinars
B. Golden dinars
C. Any denomination of dinar
D. Half-dinars (perutot)
Correct: B — Rav Ashi emended the baraita to clarify that 'dinarei' specifically refers to golden dinars (higher denomination), while 'dinarin' refers to silver dinars. This linguistic distinction is proven from the Karetot mishna about bird prices in Jerusalem.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did the price of bird-nests in Jerusalem skyrocket to golden dinarei (Karetot 8a)?
A. A Temple decree required golden vessels for all bird offerings
B. A drought destroyed the bird population near Jerusalem
C. Uncertain women each needed multiple bird-offerings for childbirth and zava, creating massive demand
D. Roman taxation on animal sales drove up costs
Correct: C — Women uncertain about multiple births or irregular discharges needed to bring bird-offerings for each uncertainty, creating enormous demand. Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel ruled one pair covers all doubtful obligations — and the price immediately collapsed to ¼ of a silver dinar.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When a document states 200 dinars above and 100 dinars below, how much does the debtor owe?
A. 200 dinars — the larger written amount
B. 100 dinars — the bottom text controls
C. 150 dinars — the average of both figures
D. The document is invalid due to the contradiction
Correct: B — The mishna rules that when amounts conflict between what is written above and below, we always follow the bottom amount. This applies regardless of whether the bottom figure is higher or lower than the top.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why can a name written below NOT be corrected from the name written above when two letters are missing?
A. Two missing letters automatically render the document a forgery
B. Rabbinic decree prohibits corrections of more than one letter as a general fence
C. A completely different 3-letter name might share some letters with the longer name above, creating unresolvable ambiguity
D. Two missing letters means the name below is illegible and cannot be read at all
Correct: C — The Gemara explains: we worry that the name below is actually a distinct 3-letter name, which happens to share letters with the longer name above. With two letters missing we cannot confidently identify which name was intended, so correction is prohibited.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the core of Rav Pappa's unresolved dilemma about 'sefel' and 'kefel'?
A. Whether a cup (sefel) can serve as collateral for a double-payment debt (kefel)
B. When 'kefel' (larger obligation) is above and 'sefel' (smaller) is below — does the bottom's lower amount control, or is it a correctable scribal error?
C. Whether samekh and kuf are considered legally identical letters in all documents
D. Whether Rav Pappa's ruling about obvious cases applies equally to non-obvious ones
Correct: B — Rav Pappa is certain that sefel-above / kefel-below follows the bottom (kefel). His dilemma is the reverse: kefel-above / sefel-below. The letters are visually similar enough to suggest scribal error, yet the bottom-controls rule would favor the smaller obligation (sefel). This remains unresolved.
