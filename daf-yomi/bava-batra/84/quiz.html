Bava Batra 84 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
An item worth 6 dinars is sold for 5 dinars. The price then drops to 3 dinars. Who was exploited, and who may renege?
A. The buyer was exploited; the buyer may renege
B. The seller was exploited; the seller may renege
C. Both were exploited; either may renege
D. Neither may renege since the market simply changed
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda: The seller received less than the item's worth (5 instead of 6), so the seller was exploited. Only the exploited party — the seller — has the right to rescind the sale.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the sun appear red in the morning, according to the Gemara's resolution?
A. It reflects light off the earth's red soil
B. It passes over the roses of the Garden of Eden
C. It passes over the entrance to Gehinnom
D. The sun is inherently red; it only looks white during an eclipse
Correct: B — The Gemara explains: in the morning the sun reddens as it passes over the roses of the Garden of Eden; in the evening it reddens passing over the entrance to Gehinnom. At midday the sun is truly white.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Regarding buying and selling (as opposed to teruma), how do the Rabbis classify wine and vinegar?
A. One type — both derive from grapes
B. Two distinct types — they serve different purposes and preferences
C. One type only if the transaction occurred in the same market
D. The Rabbis made no ruling on commercial classifications
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes: for teruma the Rabbis say wine and vinegar are one type, but for commercial transactions everyone agrees they are two types, since some people prefer wine and others prefer vinegar.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Abaye and Rava, which act effects acquisition in an alleyway (מבוי)?
A. Passing or carrying the object through the alleyway
B. Measuring the object in the alleyway
C. Pulling (meshicha) the object in the alleyway
D. Verbal declaration by both buyer and seller
Correct: C — Abaye and Rava: passing effects acquisition in the public domain and a courtyard belonging to neither party; pulling (meshicha) effects acquisition in an alleyway or a courtyard belonging to both parties.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If a buyer measures produce into his own basket but does not pull the produce, has he acquired it?
A. No — measuring can never substitute for pulling or carrying
B. Yes — measuring into the buyer's own vessel effects acquisition because the vessel acts as the buyer's domain
C. Only if the measurement occurred in the public domain
D. Only if the seller verbally confirmed the transfer
Correct: B — Rabbi Zeira and Rabbi Asi discuss this: measuring into the buyer's own basket or vessel effects acquisition because the vessel acts like the buyer's courtyard, acquiring on his behalf even without a separate act of pulling.
