5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Giddel citing Rav, in which specific case does Rabbi Eliezer's ruling apply — that the public may keep the thoroughfare they choose?
A. In all cases where the owner voluntarily offered an alternative path
B. Only when the original path's location was lost or forgotten
C. Only for paths through vineyards
D. Only when the alternative path is wider than the original
Correct: B — Rav Giddel explains that Rabbi Eliezer's ruling applies only when the original thoroughfare was lost — its location was forgotten — making the alternative path the one the public validly acquired.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the standard width of the road leading to a city of refuge (ir miklat)?
A. 8 cubits
B. 16 cubits
C. 24 cubits
D. 32 cubits
Correct: D — Rav Huna derives from a verse that the road to a city of refuge must be 32 cubits wide — double the standard public thoroughfare of 16 cubits, to ensure fleeing manslayers can reach safety.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
From which verse does Rabbi Elazar derive that walking the length and breadth of land constitutes acquisition?
A. Leviticus 25:23 — 'The land shall not be sold permanently'
B. Deuteronomy 19:3 — 'You shall prepare the roads to the cities of refuge'
C. Genesis 13:17 — 'Arise, walk through the land, its length and its breadth; for I will give it to you'
D. Numbers 35:2 — 'Give the Levites cities to dwell in'
Correct: C — Rabbi Elazar derives from God's instruction to Abraham in Genesis 13:17 — to walk the land in order to receive it — that walking constitutes a form of kinyan (acquisition) for paths.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In how many ways did the Amoraim say Rav Avya erred when performing ma'amad u'moshav for his deceased wife?
A. One error — he performed it for a single individual
B. Two errors — both identified by Rav Yosef
C. Three errors — Rav Yosef named two, Abaye added a third
D. Four errors — Rav Yosef, Abaye, and Rava each contributed one
Correct: C — Rav Yosef identified two errors (ceremony requires a group, not one person; plus another condition), and Abaye added a third: the ceremony must take place in the cemetery, not in the city.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In what one case do the Rabbis agree with Rabbi Eliezer that walking alone acquires a path?
A. Any public thoroughfare used continuously for thirty days
B. A path through a vineyard, since it exists solely for the purpose of walking
C. A path adjacent to a city of refuge
D. Any path that is at least four cubits wide
Correct: B — Rabbi Yosei son of Rabbi Ḥanina: The Rabbis concede to Rabbi Eliezer for paths through vineyards — since such a path is made exclusively for walking, the act of walking is itself the means of acquisition.
