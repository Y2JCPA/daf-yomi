🏺 Can a Loan Be Given as a Gift?

Mishna Rule
A shekhiv mera who says 'give my loan to so-and-so' — the recipient acquires the loan as a gift, even though a healthy person cannot ordinarily gift a loan.
Rav Aḥa b. Rav Ika
A loan without a promissory note can also be gifted by a healthy person! Citing Rav Huna citing Rav: saying 'you owe me nothing' is effective because there is no tangible document to acquire.
Rav Huna citing Rav
One who tells another 'you owe me nothing' — if there is no promissory note, the statement itself effects the waiver. Words of oral cancellation work where there is no written instrument.
Key Distinction Loan WITH a promissory note → only shekhiv mera can gift it (no kinyan otherwise). Loan WITHOUT a note → even a healthy person can verbally waive it.

🌴 Palm Tree to One, Fruit to Another

Donor gives:
Palm tree → Person A
Fruit of tree → Person B
↓
❓ Did donor reserve a place for the fruit on the tree, giving Person B a tangible object to acquire?
↓
Place WAS reserved
Person B acquires the fruit as a tangible, attached object — valid gift
Place NOT reserved
Problem: can you give something not yet detached from donor's domain?
↓
Rav Naḥman: Even if the gift to B is unclear, if the donor gave the tree to A and retained the fruit for himself — he clearly reserved the fruit's place. By analogy, the same reservation logic grants the fruit to B.

🏠 Palm Tree & House: Parallel Reservation Cases

🌴 Palm Tree Case

  • Tree body → Person A
  • Fruit → reserved for Person B (or self)
  • Question: Is the fruit's 'place' reserved?
  • Rav Naḥman: Retaining fruit = reserving its place on tree
  • Fruit can be validly gifted as tangible attached object

🏠 House / Upper Story Case

  • Lower story sold to Person A
  • Upper story retained or sold to Person B
  • Question: Do projections (zizim) go with upper story?
  • Rav Naḥman: Retaining upper story = reserving its place + projections
  • Rabbi Shimon ben Lakish's ruling applies symmetrically

⚖️ Shared Principle

  • Retaining a component = reserving its physical place
  • The retained portion carries its appurtenances
  • Rabbi Abba explicitly linked these two cases
  • Applies whether retaining for self or giving to a third party
  • Both cases resolved by Rav Naḥman's parallel reasoning

↩️ Is Partial Retraction a Full Retraction?

Setup: Shekhiv mera gives all property to Recipient 1 (Gift 1), then gives part of property to Recipient 2 (Gift 2)
↓
If he dies: Recipient 1 gets all (Gift 1 valid at death). Recipient 2 also gets their portion — gifts coexist.
↓
If he recovers: Gift 1 (all property, nothing reserved) is invalid. Gift 2 (partial only) is valid — he reserved something for himself.
↓
Rav Yeimar's proof to Rav Ashi: If partial retraction were NOT a full retraction, Gift 1 should survive even after Gift 2. But it doesn't — proving that giving away part = retracting the whole first gift.
↓
Final Halakha: A partial retraction IS a retraction of the entire gift. This applies whether the giver dies or recovers — the conclusion holds in both scenarios.
