QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Rav Safra say the court may not write two deeds of sale for the same field?
A. Two deeds would confuse the court record and cause clerical errors
B. A dishonest purchaser could use the deeds to repossess the same field twice from different people
C. Duplicate deeds automatically void each other under rabbinic law
D. The seller could use both deeds to collect the purchase price twice
Correct: B — Rav Safra explains that with two deeds, a purchaser whose land was repossessed by the seller's creditor could then use one deed to take the field from a third party, and later the second deed to take it again — a double fraud.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rafram hold about a bill of sale that omits the guarantee (אחריות)?
A. The sale is void without a guarantee clause
B. The omission is intentional and the buyer accepted risk knowingly
C. The omission is a scribal error — buyers always expect a guarantee
D. The seller must compensate the buyer double for omitting it
Correct: C — Rafram holds that omitting the guarantee is presumed to be a scribal error, since every buyer expects his purchase to be guaranteed. Rav Ashi disagrees and says there is no guarantee when it is absent.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel, what is the legal effect of a gift recipient returning the deed to the giver?
A. No effect — the gift was completed when the deed was first handed over
B. The gift is canceled and the land returns to the original owner
C. The recipient must pay rent for any time the land was used
D. The court must issue a new deed to finalize the return
Correct: B — Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel holds that the deed embodies the gift (kinyan shtar). Just as the gift was conveyed by handing over the deed, returning the deed un-conveys it — the gift is canceled.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why is writing a receipt for the land seller rejected as a solution to the replacement-deed fraud problem?
A. Receipts are only legally valid for monetary loans, not real estate
B. The seller might destroy the receipt before the fraud is discovered
C. Some purchasers buy land without a guarantee and know they have no recourse against the seller, so they won't expose the fraud
D. The Rabbis ruled that court-written receipts have no legal standing
Correct: C — A purchaser who bought land without a guarantee accepted that he cannot recover from the seller if he loses the land. Knowing this, he has little incentive to expose the double collection, rendering the receipt ineffective as a safeguard.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Dimi, what is the core issue underlying the dispute between Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel and the Rabbis about returning a gift deed?
A. Whether the recipient's intent to return the gift is legally sufficient
B. Whether kinyan shtar — acquisition through the physical transfer of the document — is legally valid
C. Whether gifts require witnesses to be binding
D. Whether a court can compel a donor to honor a returned gift
Correct: B — Rav Dimi reports from Eretz Yisrael that the dispute between Rabban Shimon and the Rabbis is specifically about kinyan shtar: Rabban Shimon holds that letters are acquired by transferring the document, while the Rabbis hold they are not.
