5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman and Rabbi Yoḥanan, if someone says 'I give this field to the child my wife is carrying,' what is the result?
A. The fetus acquires the field immediately
B. The fetus does not acquire the field — a fetus cannot acquire by gift
C. The mother acquires the field on the fetus's behalf
D. The gift is valid only if witnessed by ten people
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman and Rabbi Yoḥanan rule clearly: a fetus cannot acquire property through voluntary transfer. The gift is simply ineffective.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, how can a gift to a fetus be made legally effective?
A. By giving it to the mother as trustee
B. By waiting until the child is one day old
C. By wording it 'the transfer shall take effect when she gives birth'
D. By registering the gift before a court
Correct: C — Rav Huna explains: if the transfer is conditioned on the birth ('when she gives birth'), the fetus acquires retroactively at that moment, since the condition is now fulfilled.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Abaye reject Rav Sheshet's proof from the convert's estate baraita?
A. Because the baraita discusses a convert, not an Israelite
B. Because the people who took the property were acting illegally
C. Because inheritance (ירושה) is automatic and differs from voluntary acquisition
D. Because the fetus in the baraita was already born when they took possession
Correct: C — Abaye argues that inheritance passes automatically by force of law — even to a fetus. This tells us nothing about whether a fetus can acquire property through a deliberate gift or transfer.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the Sura tradition regarding a son born after his father's death and the firstborn's double portion?
A. The posthumous son reduces the firstborn's double portion like any other son
B. The posthumous son does not reduce the firstborn's double portion
C. The posthumous son receives a double portion himself
D. The firstborn forfeits his double portion entirely
Correct: B — In Sura, Mar son of Rav Yosef citing Rava teaches: 'And they shall divide' implies all sons must be present at division — a son born after death was not present, so he doesn't count against the firstborn's share.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When a man said 'My property is given to the sons I will have from you' and then told his older son 'you too shall take as much as one of them,' how is the older son's share determined?
A. He receives twice the share of one of the new sons
B. He receives only his standard inheritance share with no addition
C. He receives a share equal to one of the sons born from the second wife
D. He receives nothing, since the original gift excluded him
Correct: C — Rabbi Yirmeya's opinion prevails: the father's words 'take as much as one of them' mean the older son gets a share equal to one of the new sons — not a windfall above and beyond.
