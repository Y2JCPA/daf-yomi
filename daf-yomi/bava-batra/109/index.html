⚖️ Who Inherits First? Resolving the Order

Question: Why does the father precede the brothers in inheritance — and does he also precede the daughter?
↓
Father vs. Brothers
Father precedes brothers — derived from she'ero or ve'ha'avarta
Father vs. Daughter
Father does NOT precede daughter — son = daughter for levirate, so same for inheritance
↓
Moral coda: Jonathan linked to Manasseh for wickedness; Pinehas linked to Yitro for righteousness — cleave to the good

📜 Does the Father Precede the Daughter? A Talmudic Exchange

Gemara
The verse places the father above the brothers — but why not above the daughter too? Perhaps 'his kinsman' means the father inherits before everyone!
Gemara
Since levirate marriage is not performed whether the deceased had a son OR a daughter, son and daughter are treated equally. Therefore the father cannot precede the daughter in inheritance either.
Gemara
Then perhaps 'his kinsman' teaches only that the father precedes his OWN brothers — not the deceased's brothers?
Gemara
No verse is needed for that. The father's brothers only inherit BY VIRTUE of the father — so if the father is alive, he obviously precedes his own brothers. The verse must address the deceased's brothers.
Ruling The father precedes the deceased's brothers, but does NOT precede the daughter — son and daughter are equivalent in this halakha.

📖 Two Derivations for the Father's Priority in Inheritance

From 'She'ero' — His Kinsman (Numbers 27:11)

  • Verse: 'give to his kinsman of his family'
  • Rava: 'of his family' = paternal family only
  • Mother's family is NOT halakhically 'his family'
  • 'She'ero' therefore refers to the father
  • R' Yishmael son of R' Yosei uses this derivation

From 'Ve'ha'avarta' — And You Shall Pass (Numbers 27:8)

  • Verse: 'you shall pass his inheritance to his daughter'
  • Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi: 'pass' signals upward transfer
  • The word teaches the father can receive inheritance
  • Also used as a general rule across all inheritance verses
  • The other sage uses 've'ha'avarta' for a different teaching

🗿 Who Was Jonathan ben Gershom? The Mystery Name

Judges 18:30: 'Jonathan son of Gershom son of Manasseh' served as priest for Micah's idol
↓
Problem: Gershom was the son of Moses — why does the verse say Manasseh?
↓
His actual name
Rava bar Rav Ḥanan: His name was Levi; his father was from the tribe of Judah (hence 'family of Judah')
Why 'Manasseh'?
He acted like Manasseh the wicked king — notorious idol worshipper — so Scripture linked him to Manasseh
↓
R' Yoḥanan (R' Shimon ben Yoḥai): Corruption is linked to one who is corrupt — even without family ties, the wicked are associated with wickedness

👨‍👩‍👧 Cleave to Good People — The Power of Lineage

לְעוֹלָם יִדְבַּק אָדָם בְּטוֹבִים — A person should always cleave to good people (Rabbi Elazar, Bava Batra 109b)
📣 Rabbi Elazar's Teaching Rabbi Elazar teaches: A person should always cleave to good people — specifically, one should marry a woman from a righteous family, for this is beneficial for one's offspring.

The proof: Moses married the daughter of Yitro. Although the tribes mocked Pinehas as 'the son of one who fattened calves for idols,' the very same Pinehas became one of Israel's greatest heroes — because good lineage on one side shapes the character of descendants.
