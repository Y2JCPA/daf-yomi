QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the father NOT precede the daughter in the order of inheritance?
A. Because daughters are explicitly mentioned before fathers in Numbers 27
B. Because son and daughter are equivalent for levirate marriage, so they must be equivalent for inheritance
C. Because the Gemara derives from 've'ha'avarta' that daughters come before fathers
D. Because a daughter's husband inherits on her behalf, giving her priority
Correct: B — Levirate marriage is not performed whether the deceased had a son OR a daughter — they are equivalent. By analogy, they are treated equally in inheritance, so the father does not precede the daughter.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rava derive from the phrase 'his kinsman of his family' (Numbers 27:11)?
A. That the mother's family inherits before the paternal family
B. That 'she'ero' refers specifically to the paternal family — i.e., the father
C. That brothers always precede the father in inheritance
D. That maternal uncles have full inheritance rights equal to paternal uncles
Correct: B — Rava teaches that 'kinsman of his family' means the paternal family specifically. The mother's family is not halakhically called 'his family,' so 'she'ero' points to the father.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why is Jonathan ben Gershom called 'son of Manasseh' in Judges 18:30, according to Rava bar Rav Ḥanan?
A. His biological father was from the tribe of Manasseh
B. He served in the Temple built during Manasseh's reign
C. He acted like Manasseh the wicked king who worshipped idols, so Scripture linked him to Manasseh
D. His mother was from the royal house of Manasseh
Correct: C — Although Jonathan was the grandson of Moses (son of Gershom son of Moses), because he engaged in idol worship like Manasseh the wicked king, the verse associates him with Manasseh — corruption is linked to the corrupt.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What practical teaching does Rabbi Elazar derive from the story of Moses and Yitro?
A. One should study Torah only with scholars of pure lineage
B. One should always marry into a righteous family, as good lineage benefits one's descendants
C. One should choose business partners from families known for honesty
D. One should live in a community of learned people to absorb their Torah
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar says: 'A person should always cleave to good people' — meaning marry into a righteous family. Proof: Moses married Yitro's daughter, and from that union descended Pinehas, a great hero of Israel.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara reconcile calling Pinehas's ancestor 'Putiel' with descent from both Yitro and Joseph?
A. Putiel was Yitro's brother, so the connection to Yitro is through a collateral line
B. Pinehas descended from Yitro through one parent and from Joseph through the other — both called 'Putiel' via wordplay
C. The Gemara concludes Pinehas descended only from Yitro, not Joseph
D. Putiel is a title of honor unrelated to biological ancestry
Correct: B — The Gemara uses wordplay: 'Putiel' comes from pitem (Yitro who fattened calves for idols) AND from pitpeit (Joseph who suppressed his passion). Pinehas had both lineages — hence both are called Putiel.
