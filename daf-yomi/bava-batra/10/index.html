⚔️ Turnus Rufus vs. Rabbi Akiva on Poverty & Charity

Turnus Rufus
If your God loves the poor, why doesn't He support them Himself? And furthermore — your charity condemns you! A king who imprisons his son would punish a servant who feeds him. God imprisoned Israel in poverty; you defy Him by giving charity!
Rabbi Akiva
God commands us to act as His agents to merit us. And your parable fails — a king angry at his son commands others not to feed him. But if a commoner defies the decree, is the son not grateful? God wants us to care for the poor.
Turnus Rufus
You are called both 'sons' and 'slaves.' When you fulfill God's will — sons. When you do not — slaves. And since you are currently in exile, you are slaves. A slave may not defy his master by feeding another slave who was cut off.
Rabbi Akiva
The scripture says: 'Is it not to share your bread with the hungry?' — your bread, your own. The obligation of charity comes directly from God. We are His agents of compassion, and giving charity exalts us before Him.
🔑 Core Principle Poverty exists so that Israel may merit reward through charity — we are God's agents, not defying Him but fulfilling His will.

📅 Rosh HaShana Decree & Charity's Power

Rosh HaShana
God decrees all of a person's income and losses for the year
↓
Rabbi Yehuda bar R. Shalom:
If a person is destined to lose money, let it go to charity — not to tax collectors
↓
With Charity
Rabban Yochanan's nephews: destined to lose 700 dinars → gave to charity → Rome took only 17 more
Without Charity
The full decree of loss falls to tax collectors, thieves, or misfortune — no mitzvah earned

🏛️ Levels of Charity — The Secret Chamber

Lishkat Chasha'im — Secret Chamber

  • Giver does not know to whom he gives
  • Receiver does not know from whom he takes
  • Highest level — total anonymity preserved
  • Located in the Holy Temple in Jerusalem
  • Protects the dignity of the poor

Public Charity (Lower Level)

  • Rabbi Elazar: give a coin before praying
  • 'I will behold Your face through charity'
  • Still meritorious but lacks full anonymity
  • One who says charity to merit children — still valid?
  • Debate: is conditional charity full tzedakah?

Refusing Charity (Prohibited)

  • R. Yehoshua ben Korcha: turning eyes from poor = idol worship
  • Derived from verbal analogy — 'base thought' (Deut 15) / 'base' (idol worship)
  • Rav Pappa's near-fall: feared he deserved death like one who abandons the poor
  • Riches cannot save on day of wrath — only charity can

👑 Rabbi Abbahu: The Power of Charity — Royal Wisdom

צְדָקָה תַּצִּיל מִמָּוֶת — Charity delivers from death (Proverbs 10:2)
🌟 Solomon's Teachings Question to Solomon: How far does the power of charity extend?

Answer: 'He has distributed freely, he has given to the poor, his righteousness endures forever' (Ps 112:9) — the merit of charity is eternal.

Question to Solomon: Who is destined for the World-to-Come?

Answer: Those before whom God's elders are honored — those who acquire Torah and good deeds together. Yosef (from the afterlife) confirmed: 'Happy is one who arrives with his studies in hand' — and those executed by the government stand at the highest levels.

⚖️ Why Does God Allow the Poor to Suffer? — The Full Argument

The Challenge (Rabbi Meir's baraita): An opponent asks — 'If God loves the poor, why not support them Himself?'
↓
Rabbi Meir's Answer: God commands charity so we may be saved from Gehenna — we are His agents of compassion, earning merit through the act
↓
Turnus Rufus escalates: It is your charity that condemns you — like a servant who feeds the king's imprisoned son against orders
↓
Rabbi Akiva responds: A father who imprisons his son still wants the son to be helped by others — God commands charity in His Torah; we fulfill His will, not defy it
↓
Deeper truth (R. Elazar, R. Yehuda): Charity makes peace between Israel and God; it advances the redemption — 'uphold justice and do charity, for My salvation is near' (Isaiah 56:1)
↓
Practical consequence: Ifera Hurmiz (King Shapur's mother) sent 400 dinars — Rava accepted for peace; R. Ami refused, saying it should go to gentile poor, not Jewish poor (dispute about gentile charity)
