QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Abaye rule that one should not write a signature at the very end of a blank piece of parchment?
A. A signature at the edge has no legal validity in court
B. An unscrupulous person may write text above the signature, creating a fraudulent document
C. The ink at the edge dries unevenly, making the signature hard to verify
D. It is a sign of disrespect to the court receiving the document
Correct: B — Abaye's concern is purely practical: blank space above a valid signature can be filled in by a forger, making the entire fabricated document appear legitimate.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the Mishna, for which of the following may a scribe write a document at the request of ONE party alone?
A. A promissory note requested by the creditor
B. A bill of sale requested by the purchaser
C. A get (bill of divorce) requested by the husband
D. An arbitration agreement requested by one litigant
Correct: C — The husband may request a get alone because the document cannot be misused against him — he is the one who gives it. By contrast, a creditor requesting a promissory note could harm the debtor, so the debtor must be present.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How did Abaye correct the colleagues' misreading of Rav's requirement that 'the scribe must recognize the parties'?
A. Rav meant the scribe need only know the man's name, not the woman's
B. Rav meant the scribe need only know the woman's name, not the man's
C. Rav meant the scribe must know both the man's name and the woman's name
D. Rav meant the scribe must have two witnesses verify the identities
Correct: C — Abaye clarified that Rav's ruling requires knowledge of both names. Knowing only one name leaves a loophole — a document could be misused by finding another person with the same single known name.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Huna bar Ḥinnana, when is a newcomer's claimed name trusted without further investigation?
A. Immediately, as soon as he states his name to the scribe
B. Once two witnesses from his hometown testify to his name
C. After his name has been established in the city for thirty days
D. Only after the local rabbinical court formally recognizes him
Correct: C — Rav Huna bar Ḥinnana rules that thirty days of residency establishes a presumption of genuine identity — the concern that the name is false is no longer entertained after that threshold.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the forgery case involving 'the portions of Reuven and Shimon, brothers [aḥei],' what was the nature of the alleged manipulation?
A. The names Reuven and Shimon were erased and replaced with other names
B. The word aḥei (brothers) could be read as the proper name Aḥai, adding a third supposed seller
C. The amount of land being sold was changed by adding a letter
D. The date on the document was erased and rewritten
Correct: B — Since the sellers had a brother literally named Aḥai, and Hebrew writing without vowel points makes aḥei (brothers) and Aḥai (the name) look identical, the document was ambiguous — and potentially altered to implicate Aḥai as having sold his portion too.
