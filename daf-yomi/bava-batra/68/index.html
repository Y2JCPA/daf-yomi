🏙️ What Is Sold with a City?

✅ Included in City Sale

  • Houses
  • Pits, ditches, and caves
  • Bathhouses
  • Dovecotes
  • Olive presses
  • Beit hashelaḥin (city gardens)
  • Santar — per Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel

❌ Not Included in City Sale

  • Movable property / livestock
  • Canaanite slaves (move independently)
  • Animal enclosures facing outward
  • Remnants and outer surroundings
  • Detached produce and equipment

⛓️ Is a Canaanite Slave Like Land or Movable Property?

Rav Aḥa b. Rav Avya
Learn from the mishna that a Canaanite slave has the legal status of movable property — since the slave is not sold with the city, he cannot be treated like land.
Rav Ashi
Not a proof. There is a difference between movable property that moves by itself (slaves) and inanimate movables. Slaves are excluded from city sales because they walk about on their own — not because of their legal classification.
Conclusion Rav Ashi: Even if slaves were legally like land, they would still not be sold with the city because they move independently. The mishna proves nothing about their legal category vis-à-vis land vs. movables.

📜 What Does 'Santar' Mean?

Mishna: Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel says — one who sells a city has sold with it the santar. What is santar?
↓
Babylonian interpretation
Bar maḥavanita
The land registrar — a city official who maintains property records
Eretz Yisrael interpretation
Surrounding fields
Agricultural land encircling the city walls — sold along with the city
↓
Tension: If the first tanna already includes city gardens (beit hashelaḥin), what does Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel add? This drives the santar debate.

💧 What Is 'Beit HaShelaḥin'?

❌ Initial Assumption — Rejected

  • Shelaḥin = irrigated fields outside the city
  • Would support: santar means surrounding fields
  • Rabban Shimon would add what first tanna excluded
  • But this creates a contradiction with other sources

✅ Correct Interpretation

  • Shelaḥin = garden plots within the city
  • Source: 'Your shoots (shelaḥayikh) are a paradise of pomegranates' (Shir HaShirim 4:13)
  • Shelaḥ = shoot / branch → cultivated city gardens
  • Resolves the tension with Rabban Shimon's addition

🐄 Animal Enclosures: A Contradiction Resolved

בבא בתרא סח — פתחן לפנים נמכרין / פתחן לחוץ אין נמכרין
Baraita Contradiction Baraita 1: One who sells a city has not sold the enclosures for animals, birds, and fish.

Baraita 2: If the city has 'daughters' (nearby villages), they are sold with it.

Resolution: The two baraitot address different configurations — enclosures whose openings face inward (toward the city) are considered part of the city and sold with it; enclosures whose openings face outward are not part of the city and are excluded.
