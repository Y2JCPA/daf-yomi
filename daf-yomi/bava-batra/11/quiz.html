Bava Batra 11 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
King Munbaz said his ancestors stored 'below' while he stores 'above.' What does his storing 'above' mean?
A. He built a fortified upper-floor vault for his gold
B. He gave charity, accumulating merit in the World to Come
C. He invested in profitable trade caravans
D. He donated directly to the Temple treasury
Correct: B — Munbaz meant that charity stores reward in Heaven — 'above' — where it cannot be stolen or lost, unlike earthly treasure stored 'below' in vaults.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan (cited by Rabbi Asi), what is the relationship between the mishna's 4 cubits and the 4 cubits in front of each entrance?
A. They are the same 4 cubits — no additional area is needed
B. The entrance cubits are deducted from each owner's 4-cubit share
C. The mishna's 4 cubits are in addition to the 4 entrance cubits
D. Entrance cubits only apply in fields, not courtyards
Correct: C — R. Yoḥanan teaches that the 4×4 minimum in the mishna is separate from and on top of the 4 cubits automatically reserved in front of each house entrance.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does a portico (akhsadra) that opens onto a courtyard NOT receive the adjoining 4 cubits?
A. Porticos are communal and cannot receive private allocations
B. The owner can unload items inside the portico itself
C. A portico has no door and therefore has no entrance rights
D. Rav Naḥman ruled porticos are treated as open fields
Correct: B — Rav Huna explains that the 4 cubits exist to allow unloading in front of the entrance. Since the portico owner can unload within the covered portico itself, no additional courtyard space is needed.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Under what condition does the mishna permit division of a courtyard even if it falls below the normal minimum?
A. When a rabbinical court orders the division
B. When the courtyard is worth more than 200 zuz
C. When both owners mutually agree to divide it
D. When one owner has resided there for 12 months
Correct: C — The mishna distinguishes: a court cannot compel division below minimum at one owner's request, but when both owners consent, they may freely divide even below the threshold.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If a courtyard resident had an entrance to an alleyway that was previously sealed and now wishes to reopen it, can the other alleyway residents stop him?
A. Yes — any new opening requires unanimous consent
B. No — he is restoring a prior established right
C. Only if a majority of residents vote to block it
D. Yes, but he may pay compensation to proceed
Correct: B — The baraita rules that reopening a formerly existing entrance is considered restoring a prior right, not creating a new imposition — so other residents cannot prevent it.
