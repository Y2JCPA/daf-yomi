Bava Batra 145 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What claim allows a betrothed woman to keep the betrothal money when her betrothed dies?
A. The betrothal was conditional on his survival
B. Give me my husband and I will rejoice with him
C. Custom determines all such cases
D. The money was paid as a penalty, not a gift
Correct: B — Shmuel rules that a woman can claim 'Give me my husband and I will rejoice with him' — since it is not her fault the marriage did not occur, she need not return the betrothal money.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Which of the following is NOT one of the five listed properties of gifts of groomsmen?
A. They are collectible in court
B. They are subject to the prohibition of interest
C. The Sabbatical Year does not abrogate them
D. The firstborn does not take a double portion from them
Correct: B — The baraita explicitly states that gifts of groomsmen are NOT subject to the prohibition of interest — they are exempt, since the original giving was done in a spirit of friendship, not commerce.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yosei, how much must the betrothed wife of a priest return after she was raped?
A. The full betrothal amount — she is still obligated
B. Nothing — she is the victim and bears no fault
C. Half the betrothal money, due to uncertainty
D. One third, adjusted by local custom
Correct: C — Because it is uncertain whether she must return the money (she can't claim 'give me my husband' but was wronged), Rabbi Yosei rules she returns half. If betrothed with 20 sela (80 dinars), she returns 10 sela (40 dinars).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Zeira citing Rav, 'All the days of the poor are terrible' (Proverbs 15:15) refers to:
A. An empathetic person always troubled by others' suffering
B. One who studies mishnah and is burdened by unresolved rulings
C. One who has a wicked wife
D. One who studies gemara and is always in doubt
Correct: B — Rabbi Zeira says in the name of Rav: 'The poor' refers to one who studies mishnah (troubled by rulings without reasons), while 'the feast' refers to one who studies gemara and finds joy in reasoning through problems. Rava says the exact opposite.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If someone served as groomsman at his friend's public wedding, and the friend wants him to reciprocate at a private wedding, what can he claim?
A. He must serve regardless — any reciprocation fulfills the debt
B. He may serve privately but with a larger gift to compensate
C. He served publicly and is therefore owed public reciprocation in return
D. He is only obligated if he personally attended the feast
Correct: C — The baraita rules that reciprocation must mirror the original act in kind and scale. Since he served publicly, he can insist: 'I served you at a public wedding — you must serve me publicly too,' not at a private gathering.
