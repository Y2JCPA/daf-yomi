Bava Batra 86 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak, what does the baraita mean when it says the seller 'unloaded' the merchandise?
A. He placed it into his own storage vessels
B. He emptied it directly onto the ground
C. He handed each item individually to the buyer
D. He transported it to the buyer's home
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak clarifies that the baraita refers to emptying merchandise onto the ground — not into seller's vessels. Only then does the buyer truly acquire it, since the seller's vessel cannot effect kinyan for the buyer.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rav and Shmuel's principle about a person's vessel teach?
A. A vessel only acquires items when it is physically in the buyer's courtyard
B. A vessel acquires items only when the buyer holds it himself
C. A person's vessel effects acquisition for him in any place it is situated
D. A vessel can only be used for kinyan in a public domain
Correct: C — Rav and Shmuel teach that a person's vessel effects acquisition anywhere it stands — even in the seller's courtyard or the public domain. The phrase 'in any place' is the key teaching of this statement.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the baraita about stealing a purse on Shabbat by dragging it out, what is the thief's legal status?
A. Liable for both theft and Shabbat violation
B. Liable for theft only, exempt from Shabbat
C. Liable for Shabbat only, exempt from theft
D. Exempt from both penalties
Correct: D — When dragging, the act that completes the theft and the act that violates Shabbat are one and the same (simultaneous and intertwined), so the principle that one who incurs two punishments receives the greater one exempts him — but he receives neither here.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the mishna in Kiddushin cited by Ravina, how are large domesticated animals (behema gassa) acquired?
A. By lifting the animal
B. By handing over payment
C. By passing the animal's leash to the buyer
D. By driving the animal into the buyer's field
Correct: C — The mishna in Kiddushin (25b) states that large animals are acquired through mesira — passing the leash to the buyer — because they are too large and heavy to lift. Small animals are acquired through hagbaha (lifting).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When a seller says 'I am selling you one kor for thirty sela' and measuring begins with the seller's vessel, how does the buyer acquire the grain?
A. He acquires the entire kor at once when measuring begins
B. He acquires each measure one by one as the vessel is filled
C. He acquires nothing until all thirty sela are paid
D. The sale requires a written bill before any acquisition
Correct: B — The baraita teaches that when the measuring vessel belongs to one of the parties, the buyer acquires one by one as each measure is filled and transferred — partial acquisition proceeds incrementally throughout the measuring process.
