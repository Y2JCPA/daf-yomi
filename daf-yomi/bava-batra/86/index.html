🏠 Does Unloading Complete the Acquisition?

Huna b. Mar Zutra
The baraita says 'he unloaded them' — unloading alone completes acquisition! Whether price was fixed before or after measuring makes no difference once goods are unloaded.
Ravina
Not so. Unloading into the seller's own vessels doesn't complete kinyan — the goods remain in the seller's domain. Only emptying onto the ground or into the buyer's vessel effects acquisition.
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
Ravina is correct. The baraita describes emptying merchandise onto the ground. A seller's vessel cannot effect acquisition for the buyer — ownership has not truly transferred.
Ruling A seller's vessel standing in the seller's domain does NOT effect acquisition for the buyer — the goods must be genuinely transferred to the buyer's sphere.

🫙 Rav & Shmuel: A Vessel Acquires Anywhere

כליו של אדם קונים לו בכל מקום שהן
Rav and Shmuel "A person's vessel effects acquisition for him of any item placed inside it, in any place that it is situated."

The phrase 'in any place' (בכל מקום שהן) is the key addition — even if the buyer's vessel sits in the public domain or the seller's courtyard, placing merchandise inside it completes acquisition. The buyer need not physically lift or carry anything.

👛 The Purse on Shabbat — Abaye's Teaching & the Objection

Abaye teaches: Items typically lifted are acquired only by hagbaha (lifting), not by meshicha (pulling).
↓
Rav Adda bar Mattana objects from a baraita about one who steals a purse on Shabbat.
↓
If he lifted the purse: liable for theft only (hagbaha completes kinyan; lifting preceded Shabbat labor act, so only one penalty applies).
↓
If he dragged the purse out of the private domain: exempt from both — the prohibition of Shabbat labor and theft are fully intertwined.
↓
Rav Adda's point: A purse is clearly liftable, yet the baraita implies dragging (pulling) can also effect kinyan — directly challenging Abaye's rule!

🐄 Acquisition of Animals — Kiddushin 25b

Large Animals (בהמה גסה)

  • Acquired by passing the leash (מסירה)
  • Too large and heavy to lift
  • Meshicha / mesira is the operative kinyan
  • Examples: ox, cow, horse, camel
  • Statement of Rabbi Meir in the mishna

Small Animals (בהמה דקה)

  • Acquired by lifting (הגבהה)
  • Light enough to be picked up fully
  • Meshicha (pulling) alone is insufficient
  • Examples: sheep, goat
  • Ravina cites this as proof for the meshicha debate
