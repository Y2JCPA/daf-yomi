QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why can townspeople NOT prevent traveling perfume sellers from entering their market?
A. Perfume sellers pay a royal tax that exempts them from local restrictions
B. Women need perfume, making it a public benefit that overrides merchant protections
C. Perfume is not classified as a craft under rabbinic law
D. The Exilarch issued a blanket exemption for all traveling merchants
Correct: B — The Gemara (22a) explains that traveling perfume sellers cannot be barred because women depend on them — it is a matter of public need (צורך הרבים) that overrides the townspeople's right to protect their market.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What difficult question did Rav Adda bar Abba pose to test Rav Dimi's scholarship?
A. Whether a Torah scroll written by a gentile is valid for synagogue use
B. Whether figs that began to rot still require the separation of tithes
C. Whether an elephant that swallowed a wicker basket and excreted it intact is still susceptible to ritual impurity
D. Whether a vessel submerged in the Euphrates River is considered immersed in a valid mikveh
Correct: C — Rav Adda asked: if an elephant swallowed a wicker basket and excreted it intact with its waste, does it retain susceptibility to tumah (ritual impurity) or is it considered destroyed? Rav Dimi's failure to answer this question led Rav Adda to withhold market exclusivity.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How many sages each claimed their curse or grievance caused Rav Adda bar Abba's death?
A. Two — Rav Yosef and Rav Dimi
B. Three — Rav Yosef, Rav Dimi, and Abaye
C. Four — Rav Yosef, Rav Dimi, Abaye, and Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
D. Five — including Rava himself
Correct: C — Four sages claimed responsibility: Rav Yosef (cursed him for disrespecting Rav Dimi), Rav Dimi (Rav Adda caused his financial ruin), Abaye (Rav Adda publicly humiliated him), and Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak (Rav Adda disrupted the kalla lecture by delaying students).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Mishna (22a), how far must one distance a new wall from a neighbor's existing wall?
A. Two cubits
B. Three cubits
C. Four cubits
D. Six cubits
Correct: C — The Mishna requires four cubits (approximately 2 meters) between a newly constructed wall and a neighbor's existing wall, to ensure space for walking and working the soil between the two structures.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Mishna (22b) require four cubits between a ladder and a neighbor's dovecote?
A. To prevent the ladder's shadow from disturbing the nesting birds
B. So that a mongoose cannot climb the ladder and leap onto the dovecote to devour the birds
C. To ensure the neighbor can access the dovecote entrance without obstruction
D. Because the ladder's vibrations could dislodge eggs from the nesting boxes
Correct: B — The Mishna explicitly states the reason: the four-cubit gap prevents a mongoose (נמייה) from using the ladder as a stepping stone to reach the neighbor's dovecote and kill or consume the birds inside.
