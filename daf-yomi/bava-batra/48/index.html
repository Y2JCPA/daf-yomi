🔥 Challenging Rav Huna's Ruling

Rav Huna
If one was coerced and thereby sold something, his sale is valid — even under duress he exercised choice in the moment of selling.
Rav Yehuda
Objection from Gittin: a compelled get is valid only if a Jewish court ordered it, not a gentile court. This shows coercion matters for the validity of transactions!
Rav Mesharshiyya
By Torah law even a gentile-compelled get is valid. The Rabbis nullified it only as a safeguard — not because coercion itself destroys legal will.
Rav Hamnuna
Objection from Sicarius! If you buy from a Sicarius then from the original owner, the second purchase is void — proving the coerced first owner's resignation has no legal effect.
Rav
That holds only when the prior owner said 'go acquire what you stole from me' — resignation language, not a real sale. Where he genuinely sold afterward, it is valid.
The Core Question Does coercion negate גְּמִירַת דַּעַת (full intent to transact), or does the seller retain enough residual will for a valid sale?

📋 Rava's Three-Tier Framework for Coerced Sales

Tier 1 — Specified vs. Unspecified Field
If the coercer demanded an unspecified field, the seller retained choice over which field — this residual choice implies genuine decision-making. Sale is valid.
↓
Tier 2 — Did the Seller Count the Money?
Even for a specified field: if the seller counted the money he received, that deliberate act signals mental acceptance of the transaction. Sale is valid.
↓
Tier 3 — Did the Seller Have an Escape Route?
Even without counting: if an escape was available and the seller chose not to flee, he is deemed to have chosen to sell. Sale is valid.
↓
Rava's Final Ruling: The halakha is that the sale is valid in all these cases — even in the case of a specified field. This is inferred from comparing coerced betrothal (specified person) to the specified-field rule.
↓
Mar bar Rav Ashi Dissents on Betrothal: A woman forced to accept betrothal — that betrothal is never valid. The man acted wrongly, so the Sages declared his money ownerless, retroactively cancelling the betrothal's monetary basis.

💍 Coerced Betrothal: Two Amoraic Views

Rava — Follows Coerced Sale Rules

  • Coerced betrothal is analogous to a coerced sale
  • Woman = 'specified field' scenario
  • Apply Rava's three-tier test for validity
  • Seller/woman's passive acceptance can constitute will
  • Default: betrothal valid unless all three conditions fail

Mar bar Rav Ashi — Categorically Invalid

  • Coerced betrothal is NEVER valid — unique rule
  • Man acted improperly → Sages acted improperly with him
  • Sages declared his money ownerless (הֶפְקֵר)
  • No valid money transfer → no betrothal can take effect
  • Ravina's challenge: What if betrothed with a document or act? No money to expropriate!

📜 The Puzzle of Dual Contradictory Documents

תָּוִי תְּלָאֵיהּ לְפָפִי אַקִּינָּרָא וְזַבְּנֵיהּ לְאַרְעֵיהּ
Real Case — Tavi & Pafi Tavi suspended Pafi on a kinara (torture rack) and compelled him to sell his field. Rabba bar bar Ḥana then signed BOTH documents: (1) Pafi's preemptive declaration nullifying the sale, and (2) the bill of sale (ashkarta) itself. Challenge by Rav Huna bar Ḥinena: Whichever way you look at it, there's a problem. If the preemptive declaration is valid → the sale never went through → why sign a bill of sale? If the bill of sale is valid → the sale went through → what purpose does the declaration serve? Resolution: Rabba bar bar Ḥana signed both as a protective strategy for Pafi — ensuring that under any legal outcome, Pafi's rights would be documented. A precautionary dual-signing, not a logical contradiction.
