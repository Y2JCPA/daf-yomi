⚖️ Where Does Exactly One-Sixth Fall?

Rav Huna
A difference of exactly one-sixth is treated like less than one-sixth — the sale is valid and the seller returns the proportional monetary difference to the buyer.
Rav Yehuda
A difference of exactly one-sixth is treated like more than one-sixth — the discrepancy is large enough to render the sale void and cancelable.
The Mishna's Framework When a field is sold 'within its demarcations,' a discrepancy less than one-sixth is settled with money; more than one-sixth voids the sale. The core dispute: exactly one-sixth — monetary adjustment or cancellation?

⚖️ Rav Huna vs. Rav Yehuda: The Exact One-Sixth

Rav Huna
The mishna's phrase 'less than one-sixth' includes exactly one-sixth. The sale stands; seller refunds proportionally. כיון דאמר 'פחות' — בכלל פחות הוא
Rav Yehuda
'Less than one-sixth' excludes exactly one-sixth. A full sixth is like more than a sixth — the buyer may void the sale entirely.
Baraita Challenge
Baraita: If the seller gave a field one-sixth more than stated, the surplus belongs to the buyer. One-sixth-more is treated like less-than-one-sixth in the other direction!
Rav Huna's Reply
Even on your reasoning, there is still a difficulty — the baraita says the sale is final. The comparison is inexact; the two cases are not truly parallel.
הלכה — Practical Rule Deficiency under 1/6: seller refunds. Deficiency over 1/6: buyer voids. Surplus in buyer's favor: buyer keeps it. Exactly one-sixth: machlokes.

🌾 Rav Pappa's Field: Twenty Griv or Fifteen?

כי האי גוונא — שדה טובה כשדה של עשרים גריוי
Case Study — Rav Pappa & Abaye Rav Pappa purchased a field. The seller specified the borders, then added: 'It is an area of twenty griv' — meaning fit for planting twenty se'a of grain.

After purchase, Rav Pappa measured and found the field fit for only fifteen se'a — a deficit of five out of twenty, or one-quarter, far exceeding one-sixth.

Abaye's first ruling: Since the seller specified demarcations, the sale is governed by its borders — the stated measurement is secondary.

Rav Pappa objects: But the seller explicitly said 'twenty griv' — that must be a guarantee!

Abaye's answer: The seller meant 'a field as productive as one fit for twenty' — a quality description, not a precise measurement warranty.

🏠 Brothers Dividing an Estate: Two Scenarios

Rabbi Yosei: Division by Lottery

  • Brothers divide estate into shares of equal value
  • Draw lots (גורל) to determine who receives which portion
  • Once lots are drawn: division is final and binding
  • Compared by Rabbi Yosei to the tribal division of Eretz Yisrael
  • Gemara challenges: Eretz Yisrael also used Urim v'Tumim!
  • Resolution: the lot itself provides sufficient finality

Unknown Third Brother Arrives

  • Two brothers divided father's estate equally between them
  • Later: a third brother surfaces — he had been overseas
  • Rav: Division is void — must be entirely redone among three
  • Shmuel: Division stands; each brother cedes a third of his share
  • Rava questions Rav: is this like overturning a mistaken court verdict?
  • Rav Naḥman distinguishes: three known partners vs. two unaware brothers

👥 Rav vs. Shmuel: The Returning Third Brother

Rav
The original two-way division is void entirely. It was made under a fundamental error — both brothers thought they were the only heirs. The estate must be redistributed from scratch among all three.
Shmuel
The division stands. Each brother simply gives the third his proportional share from their respective portions. No need to undo everything.
Rav Pappa to Abaye
Does Shmuel hold that even a mistaken division is valid? The brothers had no idea a third heir existed — how can the division stand?
Abaye
Shmuel distinguishes: the brothers may have known implicitly that unknown heirs could emerge. Their division carried an implied condition of adjustment — so it was not fundamentally flawed.
Rav Naḥman's Key Distinction Three known partners dividing property enter with full awareness. Two brothers who didn't know of a third are in a different position — hence Rav may rule the division void while Shmuel sees it as adjustable.
