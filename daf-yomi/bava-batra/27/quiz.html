Bava Batra 27 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara's accepted resolution, how is Ulla's 16-cubit root measurement calculated?
A. In a square — 16 cubits in each of the four cardinal directions
B. In a circle surrounding the tree trunk
C. Only toward the neighboring property, not in all directions
D. Along the diagonal of the beit se'a plot
Correct: B — The Gemara resolves the mathematical problem by measuring in a circle, which equals ¾ of the square area (768 sq. cubits), fitting within the beit se'a allotment of 833⅓ sq. cubits per tree.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
If one buys two trees in another person's field (Mishna Bikkurim 1:6), what is the halakha regarding first fruits?
A. He brings bikkurim and recites the declaratory verses
B. He brings bikkurim but does not recite the verses
C. He neither brings bikkurim nor recites
D. He recites the verses but does not bring the fruit
Correct: B — Two trees do not confer land ownership. Without land, he brings bikkurim (the trees are his) but cannot recite the verses that declare 'the land which You gave me,' since the land is not his.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the contradiction between Rav Dimi's and Ravin's reports from Eretz Yisrael?
A. They disagree about whether Ulla's ruling is accepted in Eretz Yisrael
B. They report opposite rulings from Rabbi Yohanan about bikkurim for a tree within 16 cubits of the border
C. They dispute whether tithes apply to trees straddling the border
D. They disagree about Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel's opinion on retroactive designation
Correct: B — Rav Dimi reports Rabbi Yohanan ruled one does NOT bring bikkurim or recite (produce grew in borrowed space), while Ravin reports Rabbi Yohanan ruled one DOES bring and recite (obligation fell on this tree). The contradiction is left unresolved.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Shimon require ALL branches overhanging the public domain to be cut along the plumb line?
A. To allow the widest wagons to pass freely
B. Because the branches' shade ruins crops grown in the public domain
C. Due to ritual impurity — a bone might land on the branches, creating a tent of corpse impurity over passersby
D. To prevent children from climbing dangerously low branches
Correct: C — Rabbi Shimon's concern is tum'at ohel: if a bone lands on overhanging branches, they create a 'tent' that conveys corpse impurity to anyone walking beneath. The Gemara adds that a scarecrow would not address this concern since it cannot prevent an actual bone.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yohanan explain that even the Rabbis (who normally prohibit digging under public domain) can agree with the mishna's camel-height rule for tree branches?
A. The Rabbis hold that trees are the owner's full responsibility regardless of public benefit
B. Unlike underground covers which rot over time, a tree only grows larger — so cutting to current camel height is always sufficient, with no future deterioration to worry about
C. The Rabbis follow Rabbi Eliezer's principle that future damage is never assessed
D. The Rabbis make an exception for living trees because they can be trimmed repeatedly
Correct: B — Rabbi Yohanan draws a key distinction: underground tunnel covers will eventually weaken (creating certain future danger, so Rabbis prohibit digging), but a tree only grows larger and can always be re-trimmed. There is no worsening hazard, so current cutting suffices even for the Rabbis.
