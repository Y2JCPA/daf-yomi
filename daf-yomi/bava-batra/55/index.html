🏛️ Does Kingdom Law Override Jewish Property Rights?

Shmuel
One who hoes ownerless property acquires only the place struck by the hoe — not the entire field.
Rav Naḥman
In this matter I follow Shmuel: dina d'malkhuta dina. The kingdom's land grants and seizures are legally binding on us.
Rav Huna bar Avin
If a Jew buys a field from a gentile and a second Jew seizes it, the field is not returned — the kingdom's prior claim stands.
Rabbi Avin
Agreed. And Ukvan bar Neḥemya the Exilarch confirmed: Shmuel's three rulings — including Persian land law — all derive from this same principle.
Core Principle דינא דמלכותא דינא — The law of the kingdom is the law. Royal land grants, tax seizures, and Persian sharecropping terms carry full halakhic weight.

📜 Three Rulings Transmitted by the Exilarch

אָמַר רַבָּה: שְׁלֹשָׁה דְּבָרִים אָמַר לִי עוּקְבָן בַּר נְחֶמְיָה רֵאשׁ גָּלוּתָא מִשְּׁמֵיהּ דִּשְׁמוּאֵל
Ukvan bar Neḥemya → Shmuel 1. Dina d'malkhuta dina — The law of the kingdom is the law in all property matters.

2. Persian sharecropping (arisuta) lasts up to 40 years. A sharecropper who cultivates land for 40 years gains a presumption of ownership under Persian law — and halakha respects this.

3. Land seized for the land tax — the crown may legally reassign it. The new holder's title is valid. But: land seized for the head tax is NOT legally transferred, since that tax is a personal obligation.

🌿 Sea Squill as a Barrier: Three Halakhic Contexts

Pe'a (Corner Offering)

  • A boundary or sea squill divides one field into two
  • Each side requires its own separate pe'a
  • Rav Asi & Rabbi Yoḥanan: sea squill serves as barrier ✓
  • Mishna (Pe'a 2:1): public road, private road, and trail also divide

Ritual Impurity (Tumah)

  • Valley in rainy season: uncertain entry into impure area
  • Rabbi Eliezer: doubtful entry = ritually pure
  • Boundary divides: impurity in one section doesn't cross
  • Ravin: sea squill serves as tumah barrier ✓

Shabbat (Carrying)

  • Ravin: squill does NOT serve as Shabbat barrier
  • Rava disagrees — it DOES divide domains for carrying
  • Proof: baraita on carrying half a dried fig in two acts
  • Rava: field boundary separates domains ✓

🔄 The Dried Fig Case — Shabbat Carrying Across a Boundary

Act 1: A person carries half a dried fig (below the prohibited measure) from a private domain to the public domain and sets it down.
↓
Act 2: Later, carries the other half of the same fig and places it alongside the first.
↓
The Question: Do the two halves combine to create the full prohibited measure (shiur) that triggers Shabbat liability?
↓
Role of the boundary: If a field boundary (or sea squill) separates the two placement points, Rava rules they are distinct domains — the halves do not combine.
↓
Rava's ruling: The sea squill IS a valid Shabbat barrier — proving it divides fields for carrying law, not just pe'a and tumah.
↓
Rabbi Yosei: If both carryings happened within one lapse of awareness (he'elem echad), the acts combine and liability follows regardless.
