Bava Batra 55 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, what does a person acquire when hoeing ownerless property?
A. The entire field, as labor demonstrates intent to acquire
B. Only the specific spot struck by the hoe
C. Nothing — ownerless land requires a formal kinyan
D. The field after 40 days of continuous work
Correct: B — Shmuel rules that hoeing grants acquisition only of the exact place struck — not the whole field. Rav Naḥman invokes this ruling in the context of dina d'malkhuta.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What are the three rulings Ukvan bar Neḥemya transmitted from Shmuel?
A. Dina d'malkhuta; Persian sharecropping lasts 40 years; land seized for land tax is valid
B. Dina d'malkhuta; Persian sharecropping lasts 7 years; head-tax seizure is valid
C. Firstborn inheritance; sea squill boundaries; Persian tax law
D. Hoeing ownerless land; head tax rules; pe'a obligations
Correct: A — The three rulings: (1) dina d'malkhuta dina, (2) Persian arisuta (sharecropping) creates ownership presumption after 40 years, (3) land seized for the land tax is legally reassigned — but not land seized for the head tax.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why can land be seized for non-payment of the land tax but NOT the head tax?
A. The head tax amount is too small to justify seizure
B. The head tax is a personal obligation on the individual, not a charge on the land
C. Persian law only recognized land taxes, not head taxes
D. The rabbis created a special exemption for head tax debts
Correct: B — The land tax (karga) is levied against the property itself — non-payment means the land reverts to the king, who may reassign it. The head tax (gulgolta) is a personal debt of the individual and cannot be satisfied by seizing the debtor's land.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
For which halakhic domains does Rava rule that a sea squill serves as a dividing barrier?
A. Pe'a only
B. Pe'a and ritual impurity, but not Shabbat
C. Pe'a, ritual impurity, and Shabbat carrying
D. Shabbat carrying only — the others are rabbinic decrees, not biblical
Correct: C — Rav Asi and Rabbi Yoḥanan establish the sea squill as a barrier for pe'a and ritual impurity. Rava extends this to Shabbat carrying as well, citing a baraita about carrying a dried fig in two acts as proof.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the dried fig case, what causes the two half-figs to combine and create Shabbat liability?
A. If the same person carries both halves using the same hand
B. If both halves end up touching each other in the public domain
C. If both acts of carrying occur within one lapse of awareness (he'elem echad)
D. If the total distance carried exceeds four cubits
Correct: C — Rabbi Yosei rules: if both carryings — each below the prohibited measure — occur within a single lapse of awareness (he'elem echad), they combine into one act and the person is liable, even though each individual carrying was under the shiur.
