🏛️ What Is a Gallery (Yatzia)?

Babylonian Sages
A gallery is an apta — an attic above the main structure of the house.
Rav Yosef
No — it is a bidka ḥalila, a windowed structure attached to the side of the main building.
Rav Yosef (teaching)
Such a structure has three biblical names: yatzia (gallery), tzela (side chamber), and ta (cell) — as written in Ezekiel and Kings.
Biblical Source "The bottommost gallery (hayyatzia) was five cubits broad" — 1 Kings 6:6, describing the Temple's side chambers

📐 Does Size Matter? Mar Zutra vs. Ravina

Mar Zutra
A gallery is only excluded from the sale if it measures at least four by four cubits. A smaller annex is insignificant and goes with the house.
Ravina
But according to you — the mishna says excavations (pits, cisterns) are excluded from a house sale, and they are certainly not four by four! How do you explain that?
Mar Zutra
Different cases: excavations have their own discrete use (storing water) separate from the house. A gallery shares the same living-space function, so below four by four it merges with the house.
Halakhic Rule When two items serve distinct functions, size is irrelevant for exclusion. When they share the same function, a minimum significant size (4×4 cubits) is required.

📜 Rav Naḥman: Boundaries Don't Expand the Sale

Selling a Residence in a Bira

  • Seller delineates external boundaries of entire building
  • Buyer receives only the one residence sold
  • Delineation is just for location — not transfer of all units
  • Other residences in the bira remain with seller

Selling a Field in a Large Expanse

  • Seller delineates external boundaries of entire expanse
  • Buyer receives only the one field sold
  • Local usage: some call an expanse a 'field' — ruling needed
  • Other fields in the expanse remain with seller

📋 Rules for Bills of Sale (Shtar Mekhira)

Seller must write: 'I have not withheld anything for myself' — per Rav Mari b. daughter of Shmuel in name of Abaye (follows Rav Naḥman's view)
↓
If seller says 'I sell you land of the house of Ḥiyya' and two plots bear that name — Sages deliberate whether both are included
↓
If seller says 'I sell you fields' (plural, unspecified) — minimum to justify plural is two fields; buyer receives only two
↓
General principle: Ambiguous language in sales is construed against the seller's transfer — he retains what he did not clearly convey
↓
Delineating external boundaries in a bill of sale is for identification purposes, not to expand what is transferred beyond what was named
