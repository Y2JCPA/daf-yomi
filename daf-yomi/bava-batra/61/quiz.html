QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Yosef, what is a 'gallery' (yatzia)?
A. An attic (apta) above the house
B. A windowed structure (bidka ḥalila) attached to the main building
C. A storage room beneath the house
D. A courtyard attached to the house
Correct: B — Rav Yosef taught that a gallery is a bidka ḥalila — a windowed annex attached to the side of the main structure. The Babylonian sages interpreted it as an attic (apta), but Rav Yosef disagreed.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Mar Zutra, under what condition is a gallery excluded from the sale of a house?
A. Only if it has its own entrance from the street
B. Only if it is on a different floor than the house
C. Only if it is at least four by four cubits in area
D. Always, regardless of size
Correct: C — Mar Zutra ruled that a gallery is only excluded from the sale if it is at least four by four cubits. Below that size, it is too insignificant to be treated as a separate unit and is considered part of the house.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How did Mar Zutra distinguish a gallery from excavations (pits/cisterns) when Ravina challenged him?
A. Excavations are below ground, galleries are above
B. Galleries are named in the Torah, excavations are not
C. Excavations have a discrete use (water storage) separate from the house's living function
D. Excavations always belong to the buyer regardless of size
Correct: C — Mar Zutra argued that excavations serve a distinct purpose (water storage) entirely separate from the living function of the house, so they are naturally excluded regardless of size. A gallery shares the same living-space function, so minimum size matters.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rav Naḥman's ruling about selling a residence within a larger building (bira)?
A. Delineating the bira's external boundaries transfers all residences within it
B. Even if the seller delineates the bira's boundaries, only the one residence sold is transferred
C. The buyer receives half the building by default
D. The sale is void unless each room is listed separately
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman (citing Rabba bar Avuh) ruled that delineating the external boundaries of the larger building serves only to identify the location — it does not transfer all the residences within. The buyer receives only the one residence specifically sold.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If a seller says 'I sell you fields' without specifying how many, how many fields must he give the buyer?
A. All fields he owns
B. One field, since 'fields' can refer to a single plot
C. Two fields, since that is the minimum to justify the plural
D. Three fields, since courts require a minimum of three
Correct: C — The Gemara concludes that when a seller uses the plural 'fields' without specifying a number, he must give the buyer at least two fields — the minimum required to justify use of the plural form.
