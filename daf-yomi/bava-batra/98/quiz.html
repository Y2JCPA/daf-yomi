QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yosei son of Rabbi Ḥanina, in which specific circumstance may a buyer void a wine sale due to souring?
A. When the wine was labeled as 'spiced' but was not
B. When the wine soured in the seller's own jugs before delivery
C. When the wine soured after the festival of Shavuot
D. When the buyer stored it incorrectly
Correct: B — R. Yosei b. R. Ḥanina holds that souring in the seller's jugs proves the wine was already defective at the time of sale, giving the buyer grounds to void it as a mistaken transaction (מקח טעות).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rav Ḥiyya bar Yosef say is the true cause of wine turning sour?
A. Improper sealing of the barrel
B. The seller's negligence in preparation
C. The new owner's bad fortune (מזל)
D. Exposure to heat during transport
Correct: C — Rav Ḥiyya bar Yosef derives from Habakkuk 2:5 that wine is 'treacherous' — once ownership transfers, souring is attributed to the new owner's personal misfortune, not any fault of the seller.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In Rava's case of a vintner who sends wine with a middleman to Vol Shefat, who bears the loss if the wine turns to vinegar BEFORE reaching Vol Shefat?
A. The middleman, since he accepted responsibility for the wine
B. They split the loss equally as partners
C. The vintner, since the loss fell outside the middleman's accepted risk
D. The buyer at the destination market
Correct: C — The middleman only accepted liability for wine souring after reaching Vol Shefat. If it turns before, the vintner bears the loss — the middleman's acceptance of risk did not cover this earlier scenario.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What are the standard dimensions of a banquet hall (טרקלין / teraklin) as taught in the Mishna?
A. 8 × 10 cubits
B. 12 × 12 cubits
C. 6 × 8 cubits
D. 10 × 10 cubits
Correct: D — The Mishna gives the teraklin (banquet hall) as ten by ten cubits — a square hall. The Gemara explains the word teraklin means 'pavilion of roses,' indicating a grand reception space.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Mishna and Gemara, what is the standard formula for the height of residential structures, and what is its source?
A. Height equals the full length; modeled on Noah's Ark
B. Height equals half the length plus half the width; modeled on the Sanctuary
C. Height equals the width of the structure; derived from logic alone
D. Height equals twice the width; based on Roman architectural norms
Correct: B — The standard height is ½ length + ½ width. Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel states this is proven from the proportions of the Sanctuary (Tabernacle), which was 10 cubits high and 10 cubits wide.
