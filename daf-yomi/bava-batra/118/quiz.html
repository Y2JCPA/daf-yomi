Bava Batra 118 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye, why did the daughters of Zelophehad protest — even according to those who say Eretz Yisrael was divided among those who *entered* the land?
A. They were excluded from the census entirely
B. Their protest concerned the return of portions from the entering generation back to the leaving-Egypt generation
C. Their father had been a member of Korah's assembly
D. They objected to the allocation of the Negev specifically
Correct: B — Abaye explains that the daughters' complaint was about the transfer mechanism — portions reverting from those who entered back to the generation that left Egypt — not simply about receiving a share.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was the Gemara's reason for rejecting Abaye's inference that 'only' Zelophehad's daughters and Joseph's descendants lacked portions?
A. The Torah always records all land disputes
B. The Torah only records protests that were effective and yielded a ruling worth noting
C. Abaye misquoted the verse
D. The number of tribes proves many others also protested
Correct: B — The Gemara notes that the Torah would not bother recording an ineffective protest. Joseph's descendants' case is included not because they received new land, but to teach good advice Joshua gave them.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the homiletical proof that Joseph's descendants are immune from the evil eye?
A. Joseph himself never sinned with his eyes
B. The word אלי עין in Genesis 49:22 is interpreted as 'above the eye' — beyond its reach
C. Jacob explicitly blessed them with invisibility
D. They are compared to fire which the eye cannot harm
Correct: B — Rabbi Abbahu interprets 'alei ayin' (עלי עין) in Jacob's blessing of Joseph as meaning Joseph's seed is above the dominion of the evil eye.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Baraita 2, which three groups forfeited their portions of Eretz Yisrael, all of which Joshua and Caleb inherited?
A. The spies, the protesters, and the mixed multitude
B. The spies, the assembly of Korah, and the protesters
C. Korah's assembly, the golden calf worshippers, and the spies
D. The protesters, Zelophehad, and the scouts
Correct: B — Baraita 2 states that Joshua and Caleb inherited the portions of the spies, the protesters (מתאוננים), and the assembly of Korah — one sage juxtaposes the protesters to the spies, another does not.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov, how many separate portions did the daughters of Zelophehad receive, and what is the source for the extra portion?
A. Two portions; from the double expression 'naton titten' (נתון תתן)
B. Three portions; 'naton titten' indicates an additional portion from their father's brother
C. Four portions; each daughter received her own share
D. Five portions; one per daughter
Correct: B — Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov derives from the double expression נתון תתן (naton titten) that they also received a portion of their father's brother — giving them three total portions as noted in the Mishna.
