⚔️ Two Opinions: How Was Eretz Yisrael Divided?

Opinion A
Eretz Yisrael was divided among those who left Egypt (יוצאי מצרים). Each tribe received land based on its census at the Exodus.
Opinion B
Eretz Yisrael was divided among those who entered the land (באי הארץ). Each tribe received land based on its census upon entry.
Rav Pappa
Challenge to Opinion B: Why did the daughters of Zelophehad cry out? If divided among those who entered, they should still receive their father's portion!
Abaye
Answer: Their protest was about returning portions from the generation that entered back to the generation that left Egypt — a double inheritance issue.
Rav Pappa
Challenge: Why did Joseph's descendants protest an inadequate portion if land was divided by those who entered?
Gemara
Answer: They protested on behalf of their many children who were not entitled to a separate portion of land.
📌 Key Principle The debate shapes every detail about Zelophehad's daughters, Joseph's tribe, and the spies — one opinion elegantly explains each case; the other must work harder.

🌿 Joseph's Descendants and the Evil Eye

Joseph's descendants protest they need more land
↓
Joshua tells them: "You are a great people — go to the forest."
↓
Their Reply
"We are descendants of Joseph, upon whom the evil eye has no dominion" (Genesis 49:22 — alei ayin)
Proof from Jacob
Jacob blessed Ephraim and Manasseh: "Let them multiply like fish" — fish are hidden from the evil eye beneath the water
↓
Conclusion: Joseph's seed is immune to the evil eye — a spiritual basis for their claim to abundant land

🕵️ The Spies' Portions — Two Baraitot in Tension

Baraita 1

  • Joshua and Caleb inherited the portions of the twelve spies
  • The spies forfeited their share by bringing back an evil report
  • Caleb's portion: Hebron (confirmed in Joshua)
  • Joshua's portion: Timnath-serah

Baraita 2

  • Joshua and Caleb inherited portions of the spies, the protesters, AND the assembly of Korah
  • These three groups all forfeited their land
  • One sage juxtaposes protesters to spies
  • Other sage does not juxtapose them — separate inheritances

📜 Daughters of Zelophehad — The Baraita's Testimony

במדבר כז:ג — אָבִינוּ מֵת בַּמִּדְבָּר... וְלֹא הָיָה בְּתוֹךְ הָעֵדָה הַנּוֹעָדִים עַל ה'
בנות צלפחד "Our father died in the wilderness, and he was not among the assembly of them that gathered themselves together against the Lord in the company of Korah" (Numbers 27:3).

The daughters made a point of distinguishing their father from Korah's assembly — because membership in Korah's company would have caused the loss of his portion. By clarifying this, they preserved their inheritance rights.

This verse also serves as proof that the assembly of Korah forfeited their portions, since the daughters specifically disclaim that connection.

🧩 Abaye's Inference — and the Gemara's Rejection

Abaye
We can learn from the fact that only the daughters of Zelophehad and Joseph's descendants are recorded as protesting — there was no other individual who lacked a portion. If many had gone without, the Torah would have recorded more protests!
Gemara (Rejection)
This inference is rejected: The Torah only records a protest when it was effective — when the complaint yielded a result worth recording. Many others may have protested but received no new ruling, so their cases are not mentioned.
Gemara (Positive use)
Joseph's descendants' protest is recorded despite receiving no new land — because the Torah uses it to teach good advice: Joshua directed them to the forest of the Perizzites and Rephaim, where they could expand.
