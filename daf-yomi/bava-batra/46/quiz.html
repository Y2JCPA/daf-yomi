Bava Batra 46 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥiyya son of Rav Naḥman, when is a customer permitted to use utensils he mistakenly received from a craftsman's shop?
A. Only if he paid for the utensils in advance
B. Only if the craftsman himself handed them to the customer
C. Even if the craftsman's wife or children gave them
D. Only if two witnesses confirm the mix-up
Correct: B — The reasoning is that the owner may have asked the craftsman to sell them — this logic only applies when the craftsman directly handled the transaction, not when his family members did.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the 'Pumbedita swindler' case, why does Rava rule in favor of the craftsman over the owner who visually identifies his cloak?
A. Craftsmen always receive the presumption of ownership
B. The owner must have witnesses who previously saw the cloak in the craftsman's possession
C. Visual identification alone is sufficient for reclaiming property
D. The craftsman's oath outweighs the owner's claim
Correct: B — The baraita requires that the owner AND witnesses previously saw his specific cloak in the craftsman's possession — mere visual similarity at the time of dispute is insufficient.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman, under what circumstances can a sharecropper establish a presumption of ownership (chazaka)?
A. After working the land for three consecutive years
B. When he divides the land among sub-sharecroppers as administrator
C. When he installs other sharecroppers in his place without the owner's objection
D. When he pays taxes on the land directly to the government
Correct: C — Installing unknown third-party sharecroppers — and the owner staying silent — suggests real ownership, because an owner would normally protest strangers working his land.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does the 'mem' (מ) in the AMALEK mnemonic stand for, and when can this person testify?
A. מַלְכוּת (government); a tax collector can always testify
B. מַלְוֶה (creditor); can testify only if the debtor has other assets to cover the debt
C. מִצְוָה (commandment); testimony motivated by a mitzvah is always valid
D. מַשְׁכּוֹן (collateral); the holder of collateral may freely testify
Correct: B — Mem = Malve (creditor). A creditor is biased if the disputed land is the debtor's only asset — he wants the debtor to win so the debt can be collected. If other assets exist, the bias disappears.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Yoḥanan rule that a sharecropper who divides the field among sub-sharecroppers CANNOT establish chazaka?
A. Because dividing land is prohibited without a formal document
B. Because the owner would have been notified of any division
C. Because the owner could claim he merely appointed the sharecropper as an administrator
D. Because sub-sharecroppers automatically acquire rights in the land
Correct: C — An administrator (apotropos) might also divide land among workers — so this act is ambiguous and does not prove ownership intent. Chazaka requires unambiguously owner-like behavior.
