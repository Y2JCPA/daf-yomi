⚖️ Does the Ketubot Mishna Follow R. Yoḥanan ben Beroka?

Rav Mesharshiyya
Come hear a resolution! The Ketubot mishna — which lets sons inherit an extra portion — can only work according to R. Yoḥanan ben Beroka, who permits reallocating inheritance. Your dilemma is resolved: his ruling applies even to a healthy person.
Rav Pappa
That proves nothing! Court stipulations (תנאי בי"ד) are different — they operate outside normal property-transfer rules. Even R. Meir, who allows transferring something not yet in the world, only applies to a real person receiving it, not an unborn entity.
Abaye
The mishna uses 'יִירְשׁוּ' — they will INHERIT. That biblical language signals R. Yoḥanan ben Beroka's position, not a mere court stipulation!
Abaye
…Actually I retract that. Even the Rabbis use 'inherit' here — because both clauses (sons' inheritance / daughters' sustenance) form one joint enactment written together. It is a single bequest by one court.
Rav Naḥumi
But how do you know both clauses came from ONE court? Perhaps two separate courts enacted them, and you cannot derive one from the other!
Gemara Resolution The two clauses must be from one court — for R. Elazar ben Azarya already derived the halakhot of one from the other. Two independent courts would not permit cross-derivation.

🔄 Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's Retraction

מִפְּנֵי שֶׁהָיָה בִי קַלּוּת רֹאשׁ — due to the immaturity within me
A Model of Scholarly Humility Rabbi Natan challenged Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi: 'Your Mishna follows R. Yoḥanan ben Beroka!' Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi initially pushed back, distinguishing 'they will inherit' from 'they will take.' But later he fully retracted, saying: 'That response came from the immaturity I had in me — I was insolent before Rabbi Natan the Babylonian.' The Talmud preserves this retraction as a model: even the redactor of the Mishnah was willing to publicly admit error before a peer.

🏠 Deathbed Property Document: Steward or True Gift?

Steward Only — Not a Full Gift

  • All property → current wife on deathbed
  • All property → adult son (distributes to brothers)
  • All property → minor son in his crib
  • All property → daughter (before sons exist)
  • All property → wife (before husband's brothers)
  • Logic: presumed intent is management, not disinheritance

True Gift — Full Acquisition

  • All property → betrothed wife (not yet married)
  • All property → divorced wife
  • Half property + another person (other person gets half as gift)
  • Logic: no natural inheritance expectation → must be a gift

👥 Shmuel's Rule: Natural Heir or Outsider?

Man on deathbed writes:
'All my property to X'
↓
X = Natural/Expected Heir
(wife, son, daughter, sibling)
X is appointed steward (אפוטרופא) — the estate passes to rightful heirs; X manages it
X = Non-Heir
(betrothed wife, divorced wife)
X receives a true gift — no family inheritance relationship → face-value reading applies
↓
Ravina in the name of Rava: Ambiguous cases — daughter before sons, wife before brothers-in-law — all default to steward, not gift
