Bava Batra 65 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, when brothers divide their father's estate, what is the rule regarding rights of way?
A. Each brother retains a right of way through the other's property as before
B. They do not have rights of way against each other after the division
C. Rights of way must be negotiated in writing at the time of division
D. The eldest brother retains all rights of way by default
Correct: B — Shmuel rules that brothers who divide an estate do not retain rights of way against each other — consistent with his principle that a seller (or divider) acts generously and does not withhold paths.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why was it necessary to state the Rav-Shmuel dispute in BOTH the brothers' case AND the house-sale case?
A. Because the monetary amounts involved differ between the cases
B. Because each case has a unique rationale that cannot be derived from the other
C. Because Rav ruled differently in each case
D. Because Shmuel reversed his opinion between the two rulings
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that in the brothers' case one might expect sharing to continue, while in a sale one might expect even greater generosity. Each case has its own logic; neither can be derived from the other.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which of the following is NOT included in a house sale by default, according to the mishna and baraita?
A. The door
B. The door lock
C. The key
D. The door bolt
Correct: C — The key is specifically excluded because it is a portable, personal item. The door, bolt, and lock are all fixed to the structure and are sold with the house.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When the seller explicitly says 'I am selling you it and everything in it,' what happens to the pit and cistern?
A. They are sold along with everything else under the inclusive phrase
B. They remain excluded even with this inclusive phrase
C. Only the cistern is excluded; the pit is sold
D. The buyer must negotiate separately for each one
Correct: B — The baraita teaches that even the phrase 'it and everything in it' does not include the pit or cistern — they are considered separate property and the seller must always reserve access to them.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Eliezer, what is the halakhic status of a beehive attached to the ground with clay?
A. A movable vessel, susceptible to ritual impurity like any container
B. Ownerless property that anyone may take
C. Land — documents may be written on it and it is not susceptible to vessel impurity
D. Hefker during the Sabbatical year only
Correct: C — Rabbi Eliezer rules that a beehive attached to the ground with clay has the status of land: it can be the subject of a legal document (like real estate) and does not contract ritual impurity as a vessel would.
