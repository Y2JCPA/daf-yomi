Bava Batra 9 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why do the Sages say charity collectors do NOT need to submit to financial audits?
A. Their collections are too small to be worth auditing
B. They are trusted public servants, just as Moses and Aaron were not audited for the wilderness donations
C. Civil courts have no jurisdiction over charitable funds
D. They work anonymously and cannot be identified
Correct: B — The Sages derived this from Moses and Aaron who handled communal funds without being audited. Charity collectors carry the same presumption of trustworthiness — though Rabbi Elazar adds that individuals should still count their own money before handing it over.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, which type of request should charity collectors give WITHOUT first examining the person's level of poverty?
A. Requests for clothing
B. Requests for both food and clothing
C. Requests for food
D. Requests for lodging
Correct: C — Rav Huna says to not examine those who ask for food — the hungry person is suffering immediately and urgently. However, those who ask for clothing should be examined first. Rav Yehuda holds the opposite position.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yitzḥak, how many blessings does a person receive for consoling a poor person with words of comfort — and why is this MORE than for giving a coin?
A. 6 blessings — same as giving a coin, since intent matters equally
B. 3 blessings — less, because no material help was given
C. 11 blessings — because 'drawing out your soul' (Isaiah 58:10) is a greater act of self-giving than parting with money
D. 11 blessings — but only if money was also given alongside the words
Correct: C — Giving a peruta earns 6 blessings (Isaiah 58:7). Consoling with words earns 11 blessings (Isaiah 58:10), because 'drawing out your soul to the hungry' represents deeper personal investment — giving of oneself, not just one's possessions.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What minimum yearly charity does Rav Asi say a person should never fall below, and from where does he derive it?
A. One-half shekel per year — from the Temple half-shekel tax
B. One-tenth of income — from the tithe laws in Deuteronomy
C. One-third of a shekel per year — from Nehemiah 10:33
D. One peruta per week — from the charity fund (kuppah) rules
Correct: C — Rav Asi derives the minimum from Nehemiah 10:33, where the returnees pledged: 'we established mitzvot upon ourselves, to charge ourselves yearly with the third part of a shekel for the service of the House of our God.'
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rabbi Elazar teaches a surprising hierarchy: what is GREATER than personally performing a meritorious charitable act?
A. Giving charity anonymously so the recipient does not know the donor
B. Causing or enabling others to perform charitable acts
C. Giving to Torah scholars rather than the general poor
D. Establishing a permanent charity fund for future generations
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar teaches: 'One who causes others to perform a meritorious act is greater than one who performs it himself.' The facilitator or organizer of charity multiplies the mitzvah through others, making their merit even greater than a single act of personal giving.
