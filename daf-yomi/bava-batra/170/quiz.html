QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does Abaye reject Rav Dimi's explanation that Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel holds letters are not acquired by transfer?
A. Because Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel explicitly stated his own reason in a Mishna
B. Because it contradicts Rabba's earlier explanation of the very same baraita
C. Because the Gemara in Bava Kamma rules against Rav Dimi on this question
D. Because Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel actually agrees letters are acquired by transfer
Correct: B — Abaye argues the baraita (168b) can only be read Rabba's way, so if Rav Dimi's interpretation of Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel were correct, it would contradict Rabba — an untenable result.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Meir, what is the critical element that legally effects a document transaction?
A. The presence of witnesses at the physical moment of delivery
B. Verbal declaration by the buyer in front of the court
C. The witnesses who sign the document
D. Registration of the document with a rabbinic court
Correct: C — Rabbi Meir holds that 'the signatory witnesses on the document effect the transaction' — their signatures constitute the legal act, not the delivery.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rabbi Yosei say should happen when a debtor repays part of his debt?
A. The court tears up the original note and writes a new one for the balance
B. The creditor tears up the original and writes a smaller note
C. The creditor keeps the original note and gives the debtor a receipt for the partial payment
D. The debtor holds the original note in escrow until full repayment
Correct: C — Rabbi Yosei holds the creditor may keep the original promissory note while giving the debtor a receipt (shover) for the amount paid — the original document remains intact.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What halakha does Rav rule, as cited by Rav Huna, regarding partial debt repayment?
A. Follow Rabbi Yehuda: the creditor writes a new promissory note
B. Follow Rabbi Yosei: issue a receipt for partial payment
C. Neither opinion: the court tears up the old note and writes a new one for the balance
D. The debt must be repaid in full before any document changes are permitted
Correct: C — Rav rules the halakha follows neither Rabbi Yehuda nor Rabbi Yosei — instead, the court itself supervises the process: tearing up the original note and writing a new one for the remaining balance.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the case involving Rav Yitzḥak bar Yosef and Rabbi Abba, what is the final ruling of Rabbi Yitzḥak Nappaḥa?
A. Rabbi Abba automatically loses for claiming repayment without witnesses who corroborate
B. Rabbi Abba must verify his claim of repayment if he is able to do so, but is not automatically penalized
C. The promissory note is void because Rabbi Abba admitted he wrote it
D. The debt must be divided equally since both parties have claims
Correct: B — Rabbi Yitzḥak Nappaḥa clarifies: Rabbi Abba is not automatically penalized — he is only required to bring verification if he can. The ruling does not impose loss, only an obligation to verify.
