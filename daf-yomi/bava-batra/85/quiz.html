QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, what is the real difference between Rav/Shmuel and Rabbi Yoḥanan/Reish Lakish on vessel acquisition?
A. They fundamentally disagree — one holds vessels never acquire, the other holds they always do
B. Rav/Shmuel spoke of a vessel in the actual public domain; R. Yoḥanan spoke of a vessel in an alleyway — no real dispute
C. Rav/Shmuel hold only seller's vessels acquire; R. Yoḥanan holds only buyer's vessels acquire
D. They disagree about whether lifting (הגבהה) is required in addition to vessel acquisition
Correct: B — Rav Pappa harmonizes the opinions: Rav & Shmuel address a vessel placed in the true public domain (no acquisition), while Rabbi Yoḥanan & Reish Lakish address a vessel in an alleyway where one has permission to be (acquisition valid). The apparent contradiction dissolves.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
When a seller measures grain for a buyer using the buyer's vessel in a neutral courtyard (belonging to neither party), when does the buyer acquire the grain?
A. Only after he lifts the entire measured amount
B. Only when the seller verbally declares the sale complete
C. Each kernel acquired as it falls into the buyer's vessel
D. Only when he removes it from the courtyard entirely
Correct: C — The baraita teaches that when the buyer's own vessel is used for measuring in a neutral location, kinyan keli takes effect immediately — the buyer acquires each item one by one as it enters his vessel, since he has permission to keep his vessel there.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rav Naḥman reject Rav Huna's attempt to resolve Rav Sheshet's dilemma from the Gittin mishna?
A. The Gittin mishna discusses divorce law, which has entirely different acquisition rules
B. That mishna has been 'struck with 100 hammer blows' — it is so disputed it cannot serve as a reliable proof
C. Rav Naḥman holds women cannot effect kinyan through vessels under any circumstances
D. The Gittin case only applies in Babylonia, not in the Land of Israel
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman uses the vivid expression 'struck with 100 hammer blows' (אוקי בה מאה אוקי) to indicate the mishna has been subjected to so many challenges and conflicting interpretations (by Shmuel, Reish Lakish, and R. Yoḥanan) that it cannot be used as a proof-text.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, why does a wife acquire a get placed in her basket even while standing in her husband's domain?
A. A wife always has property rights equal to her husband in Jewish law
B. The basket is halachically treated as an extension of her hand (יד האשה)
C. The place of her lap belongs to her — the space she occupies is considered her personal domain
D. Her husband's domain is treated as shared property whenever she is present
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan's principle is 'מקום חיקה קנוי לה' — the place of her lap belongs to her. The physical space she personally occupies is considered her domain, so her basket in that space acquires on her behalf, even within her husband's property.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the final baraita cited by the Gemara, how does a buyer complete acquisition of merchandise located in the seller's domain?
A. By placing his vessels anywhere within the seller's domain
B. By paying the full purchase price, which transfers ownership automatically
C. By lifting (הגבהה) the item or removing it from the seller's domain
D. By having a third-party witness declare the transaction binding
Correct: C — The baraita is explicit: if merchandise is in the seller's domain, the buyer does NOT acquire through vessels alone — he must either lift the item (הגבהה) or physically remove it from the seller's domain. A buyer's vessel situated in the seller's space is legally ineffective for acquisition.
