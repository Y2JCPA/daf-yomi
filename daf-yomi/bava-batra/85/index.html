🏺 Do Vessels Acquire in the Public Domain?

Rav & Shmuel
A person's vessels effect acquisition anywhere — except the public domain (רשות הרבים). In the public domain, no kinyan through vessels.
R. Yoḥanan & Reish Lakish
Even in the public domain, one's vessels effect acquisition — location does not restrict the power of כלי. A vessel acquires wherever the owner has permission to be.
Rav Pappa Reconciles No true dispute! Rav & Shmuel spoke of a vessel placed in the actual public domain; R. Yoḥanan & Reish Lakish spoke of a vessel in a semi-private alleyway (מבוי). Each addresses a different case.

🗣️ Vessel Acquisition: The Core Dispute and Its Resolution

Rav & Shmuel
Vessels acquire everywhere except the public domain. In רשות הרבים, a vessel cannot acquire on the owner's behalf.
R. Yoḥanan & Reish Lakish
Even the public domain — vessels still effect acquisition. No location limitation on kinyan keli.
R. Abbahu citing R. Yoḥanan
Vessels acquire wherever the owner has permission to keep them (רשות לעמוד). This is the precise formulation of R. Yoḥanan's view.
Rav Pappa
These amoraim do not truly disagree. Rav & Shmuel: vessel in actual public domain. R. Yoḥanan: vessel in an alleyway — a semi-private space where one has permission.
Key Principle Acquisition requires both a vessel and a permitted location. The two opinions define the boundary differently, not contradict each other.

⚖️ Four Seller Acquisition Cases (ארבעה מוכרין)

Seller Measuring → Buyer's Vessel

  • Seller measures goods for buyer
  • Vessel belongs to the buyer
  • Each item acquired as it enters vessel
  • Location: neutral or buyer's domain

Seller Measuring → Seller's Vessel

  • Seller pours into his own vessel
  • Buyer does NOT yet acquire
  • Must wait for further act (lifting/removal)
  • Seller retains control of the goods

Goods in Seller's Domain

  • Merchandise located at seller's property
  • Buyer's vessel there is insufficient
  • Must lift (הגבהה) OR remove from domain
  • Baraita: explicit rule, no dispute

Pulling Laden Workers/Animals

  • Buyer pulls donkey drivers or laborers
  • Workers carry goods buyer wishes to purchase
  • Pulling (משיכה) effects acquisition
  • Rava: valid kinyan even in transit

🔨 The 'Hundred Strikes' — Why the Gittin Mishna Cannot Prove Rav Huna's Point

Rav Sheshet asks Rav Huna: If buyer's vessels are in seller's domain, does the buyer acquire once goods are placed in them?
↓
Rav Huna answers: You learned the answer in the Gittin mishna — a woman acquires her get placed in her basket, even while standing in her husband's domain.
↓
Rav Naḥman objects: That mishna has been struck with 100 hammer blows (אוקי בה מאה אוקי) — it is too disputed to serve as proof.
↓
Dispute 1 — Shmuel: Only valid if her basket was hanging from her body. Must be physically on or in her hand.
↓
Dispute 2 — Reish Lakish: Sufficient if merely tied to her, even without body contact — attachment creates the connection.
↓
Dispute 3 — Rabbi Yoḥanan: The place of her lap belongs to her (מקום חיקה קנוי לה) — she acquires the space she occupies in her husband's home.
↓
Final Resolution (Baraita): Buyer's vessels in seller's domain do NOT acquire. Must lift or remove. The Gittin case is a narrow exception, not a general rule.
