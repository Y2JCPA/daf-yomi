🫏 What's Included When You Sell a Donkey?

Rabbis
Selling a donkey does not include its vessels (sack, saddlebag, kumni). The donkey is bought primarily for riding — accessories are separate.
Naḥum the Mede
Selling a donkey does include its vessels. The donkey is used primarily for transport of goods — its equipment is integral to its function.
Ulla's Clarification The dispute specifically concerns the sack, saddlebag, and kumni (women's saddle). Items like the riding saddle and harness are included by all opinions.

📦 Donkey Sale: Included vs. Not Included

✅ Sold With the Donkey

  • Saddle (אוכף)
  • Saddlecloth (מרדעת)
  • Harness (רסן)
  • Saddle band (חגור)
  • Per Naḥum the Mede: sack, saddlebag & kumni also included

❌ Not Sold With the Donkey

  • Sack (שק)
  • Saddlebag (דיסקיה)
  • Kumni — women's saddle (per Rav Pappa bar Shmuel)
  • Per Rabbis: donkey is a riding animal; cargo accessories are separate

⚖️ Rabbi Yehuda: When Are Vessels Sold?

Vessels are on the donkey — buyer approaches seller
↓
"Sell me this donkey of yours"
The word זו (this) points to the donkey as he sees it with vessels — vessels are included
"Is the donkey yours for sale?"
General inquiry about the animal only — vessels are not included
↓
Rava explains: "This" signals intent to purchase what he sees, vessels included; a general question targets only the animal itself

🐄 Why Is a Foal Sold But Not a Calf?

Question
The seller says: "I am selling you a nursing donkey" or "a nursing cow." Both statements mention nursing — why different outcomes for the young?
Rav Pappa
For the cow: the buyer needs it for milk — the suckling calf actively diminishes the milk supply, so the calf is not included.

For the donkey: the buyer needs it for work — the foal keeps the mother calm and able to labor, so the foal is included.
Principle 'Nursing' signals the young animal's relevance — whether it helps or hinders the buyer's purpose determines if it transfers with the sale.

📖 Homily: Hamoshlim — Masters of the Self

עַל כֵּן יֹאמְרוּ הַמֹּשְׁלִים בֹּאוּ חֶשְׁבּוֹן — במדבר כ״א:כ״ז
Rabbi Yoḥanan (Numbers 21:27–30) "Therefore they that speak in parables [hamoshlim] say: Come to Heshbon!"

• המושלים = those who rule over their evil inclination
• "Come to Heshbon" = Come and make a ḥeshbon (accounting) — weigh your mitzvot against your sins
• "Let it be built and established" = You will be built in this world and established in the World-to-Come
• "City of Sihon" = Make yourself like the young donkey (סיח) of the wilderness — untamed by desire — and the wicked shall have no dominion over you
• "Flame from the city" = A flame emerges from the city of the righteous, who are called trees (שִׂיחִין)
