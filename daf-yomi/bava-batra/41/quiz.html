QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, what must accompany three years of possession in order to establish chazakah?
A. Witnesses who observed every year of possession
B. A valid claim explaining how ownership was acquired
C. Payment of land taxes to the local authority
D. Written approval from a rabbinic court
Correct: B — The Mishna's principle is חזקה שאין עמה טענה אינה חזקה — possession without a claim cannot establish the presumption of ownership. The possessor must articulate a coherent explanation (e.g., 'you sold it to me') when challenged.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Rav Naḥman rule that Rav Anan's neighbor helping build the wall was NOT a valid waiver of land rights?
A. The neighbor was legally a minor at the time
B. A formal written document is always required for land waivers
C. It was an erroneous waiver — neither party knew the wall was misplaced
D. The neighbor had already filed a prior claim in court
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman explained that since Rav Anan himself would not have built the wall in that spot had he known the true boundary, the neighbor's assistance was equally based on a mistaken assumption. An erroneous (unknowing) act carries no legal force as a waiver.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In Rav Kahana's case, whose opinion holds that contradicting witnesses (one says 2 rows, one says 3) can be combined for the minimum agreed amount?
A. Rabbi Shimon ben Elazar, following Beit Shammai
B. Rav Yehuda, in his ruling against Rav Kahana
C. Rabbi Yoḥanan, in a tradition from Eretz Yisrael
D. Rava, based on inference from the Mishna
Correct: A — Rabbi Shimon ben Elazar holds (in line with Beit Shammai) that contradicting witnesses are valid for the amount on which they agree. However, Rav Kahana successfully argued — confirmed by a letter from Eretz Yisrael — that the halakha follows Beit Hillel, who invalidate such combined testimony.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the Kashta loft case, what was Rabbi Ḥiyya's ruling for the man who lived there four years?
A. He must vacate immediately; four years of residence does not constitute chazakah
B. He must produce two witnesses confirming he paid for the house
C. He must present the bill of sale or forfeit all rights to the property
D. He should hold possession — his claim of purchase and resale is plausible
Correct: D — Rabbi Ḥiyya ruled 'chazak' — hold possession. Since it is common for someone to purchase and resell a house quickly, the man's four years in the loft during that transitional period constitutes valid possession backed by a coherent (if hard to document) claim.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav, what is required when three successive buyers combine their years of possession to establish chazakah?
A. Each buyer must have personally resided on the land during their period
B. Each transfer must be documented with a bill of sale
C. The combined period must exceed five years, not just three
D. A rabbinic court must ratify each sale at the time of transfer
Correct: B — Rav holds that a bill of sale generates the public notice (פרסומא) needed to alert the original owner to contest the transaction. Witnesses alone — without a written bill — lack this publicity, and without it, the buyers' combined years cannot establish chazakah.
