📜 The Mishna's Core Rule on Chazakah

כל חזקה שאין עמה טענה — אינה חזקה (בבא בתרא מא.)
חזקה שאין עמה טענה Any possession not accompanied by a claim explaining how the possessor became the owner cannot establish the presumption of ownership. If the prior owner asks "What are you doing in my land?" and the possessor says only "I was using it" — even three full years of possession grant nothing. But if he says "You sold it to me" or "Your father gave it to me as a gift" — that is a valid claim and chazakah is established.

🌊 Rav Anan's Flooded Boundary Wall

Neighbor
A torrent swept away our boundary wall. You rebuilt it — but placed it too far inside my land! Return the wall to the true boundary!
Rav Anan
Why should I return it? I have already used this strip of land — I have established chazakah on it!
Rav Naḥman
Whose opinion are you relying on? The Rabbis require a valid claim alongside possession — and you have no valid claim for this extra strip. Return it.
Rav Anan
But my neighbor waived his rights — he came and helped me build the wall right there! That is a voluntary relinquishment.
Rav Naḥman
That was an erroneous waiver. You yourself would not have built there had you known the true boundary — and your neighbor helped assuming the placement was correct. An unknowing act of assistance carries no legal force.
Ruling Rav Naḥman: The wall must be returned to the true boundary. Neither chazakah nor a valid waiver was established.

👥 Conflicting Witnesses: The Rav Kahana Dispute

Witness 1

  • Rav Kahana entered 2 rows into neighbor's field
  • Confirms encroachment occurred
  • Disagrees only on the extent

Witness 2

  • Rav Kahana entered 3 rows into neighbor's field
  • Confirms encroachment occurred
  • Disagrees only on the extent

R' Shimon b. Elazar (Beit Shammai)

  • Contradicting witnesses ARE valid
  • The minimum overlap (2 rows) is proven
  • Testimony stands for the agreed amount

Beit Hillel (Halakha accepted)

  • Contradicting witnesses are NOT combined
  • Letter from Eretz Yisrael confirms this ruling
  • Rav Kahana's encroachment: unproven — testimony dismissed

🏠 The Kashta Loft — Four Years of Residence

Man resides in a loft in Kashta for 4 years.
The prior owner returns: "What are you doing in my house?"
↓
Possessor's claim: "I purchased this house from you, then immediately sold it back — but I lived in it for four years during that period."
↓
Rav's question to Rabbi Ḥiyya: Isn't it common for someone to buy a house and sell it in the same night? The seller may have had no prolonged visible tenure at all.
↓
Rabbi Ḥiyya rules: "Chazak — hold possession!" The claim of purchase-and-resale is plausible; four years in the loft constitutes valid possession with a coherent explanation.
↓
Rava supports Rabbi Ḥiyya: The Mishna proves that inherited land needs no claim — similarly, some possession scenarios don't require standard publicity.
↓
Gemara rejects the proof: Inheritance is categorically different. But Rabbi Ḥiyya's ruling stands independently — the claim itself is sufficient.
