Bava Batra 138 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, when do ALL authorities agree that a gift recipient did NOT acquire the property?
A. When the transfer was done through a third party
B. When he shouts in protest immediately upon receiving the deed
C. When he was silent and later shouted after receiving the slaves
D. When he was silent throughout the entire transfer
Correct: B — Rava rules that when the recipient shouts in protest at the very outset — immediately upon being given the gift — all authorities agree he did not acquire it. The dispute only concerns one who was silent and later protested.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
If a dying man says 'Give 200 dinars to my wife, as is appropriate for her,' what does she receive?
A. Only the 200 dinars; the phrase is redundant
B. Only her ketubbah, since that is what is 'appropriate' for a wife
C. Both the 200 dinars and her ketubbah payment, with the advantage of choosing
D. The 200 dinars only if it exceeds the ketubbah amount
Correct: C — The phrase 'as is appropriate for her' is interpreted as adding her ketubbah entitlement on top of the gift. She has the advantage: if she wants she takes the 200 dinars, and if she wants she takes payment of her ketubbah.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What legal effect does using the phrase 'after him' in a deathbed bequest have?
A. It doubles the gift amount for each subsequent recipient
B. It establishes a priority order, allowing creditors to seize from later-named recipients first
C. It cancels the gift to anyone named after the first person
D. It requires each gift to be approved by a rabbinic court
Correct: B — When 'after him' language is used, a sequence priority is created. A creditor can collect from later-named recipients first, and those recipients may in turn recover from whomever was named before them.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Whose halakhic principle explains why the creditor receives both the 200 dinars AND his debt when the dying man says 'as is appropriate for him'?
A. Shmuel, who rules based on initial silence and intent
B. Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel, who follows ultimate demonstrated intent
C. Rabbi Akiva, who expounds superfluous language as adding meaning
D. Rabbi Meir, who requires witness verification of claims
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman cites Rav Huna explaining this follows Rabbi Akiva's principle that superfluous language must be adding something. Since 'as is appropriate for him' was unnecessary, it comes to add the right to collect the underlying debt as well.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the Mishna at the end of the daf, when a father writes his property to his son but retains the produce rights, what happens to produce the father detached before his death?
A. It belongs solely to the son who received the property deed
B. It is inherited by all the heirs equally
C. It is used first to pay the father's outstanding debts
D. It reverts automatically to the son named in the deed
Correct: B — The Mishna distinguishes: produce the father detached during his lifetime is inherited by all the heirs equally (since he had rights to it as reserved produce). Only produce still attached to the ground at the time of his death goes to the son who received the property.
