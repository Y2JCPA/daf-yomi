Bava Batra 15 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the baraita, who completed the book of Samuel after Samuel's death?
A. Joshua and Eleazar
B. Gad the seer and Nathan the prophet
C. Ezra and Nehemiah
D. David and Solomon
Correct: B — The Gemara (15a) states that Gad the seer and Nathan the prophet completed the book of Samuel after Samuel's death.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the dispute between Rabbi Yehuda and Rabbi Shimon regarding the last 8 verses of the Torah?
A. Whether they are part of the Torah at all
B. Whether Joshua or Moses wrote them
C. Whether they can be read on Shabbat
D. Whether Ezra or Joshua wrote them
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda says Joshua wrote the last 8 verses (describing Moses' death). Rabbi Shimon says Moses wrote them 'with tears,' since the Torah cannot be missing a single letter.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rabbi Shmuel bar Naḥmani use to prove that Job was a real historical person?
A. The detailed account of his suffering
B. The fact that his name and hometown are both specified
C. The large number of children and livestock he had
D. The fact that God speaks to him directly
Correct: B — Rabbi Shmuel bar Naḥmani argues that a parable (like Nathan's) gives no name or location. Job's story specifies both his name ('Job') and his place ('land of Utz'), indicating a real person.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does the Gemara mean when it says Job's livestock 'breached the order of the world' (paretzu)?
A. They multiplied faster than normal
B. They entered others' fields and paid for the damage, unlike normal livestock
C. They were immune to disease and theft
D. They crossed the boundary of Eretz Yisrael
Correct: B — Rabbi Yosei bar Ḥanina explains: normally livestock damage fields and the owner pays — but Job's animals would enter fields and then Job would compensate beyond what was required, 'breaching' normal custom through generosity.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, how does the praise of Job in God's words compare to the praise of Abraham?
A. They are equal in every way
B. Abraham is praised more regarding fear of God
C. Job is praised more regarding fear of God than Abraham
D. Abraham is praised more for his deeds but Job for his patience
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan (15b) states that what God says about Job regarding his fear of God surpasses what is said about Abraham — God uses greater language of praise for Job's God-fearing nature.
