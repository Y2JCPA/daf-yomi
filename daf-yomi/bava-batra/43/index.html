⚖️ Can a Partner Testify About Joint Property?

Questioner
Why can a partner testify about jointly owned property? Partners are financially interested — they are biased witnesses!
Gemara
Answer 1: He wrote to his partner: 'I have no legal dealings with this field' — relinquishing his share removes the bias.
Gemara
Answer 2: He performed a formal act of acquisition (kinyan) transferring his share — legally effective removal of interest.
Questioner
But a baraita shows that relinquishing ownership in communal property (Torah scroll) is NOT fully effective to remove interest!
Gemara
Torah scroll is different: residents remain obligated to hear Torah reading from it — they always retain a benefit, regardless of ownership.
Core Principle A witness is disqualified when they have a financial stake in the outcome. The question is: what acts are legally sufficient to sever that stake?

🔓 Three Ways to Qualify a Partner as a Witness

Problem: Partner wants to testify about jointly owned field
↓
Method 1
Written Waiver
He writes: 'I have no dealings with this field' — relinquishes all claims in writing
Method 2
Formal Kinyan
He transfers his share via act of acquisition — legally binding transfer of ownership
Method 3
Assumes Responsibility
He guarantees to compensate the other party if the field is seized — takes on liability instead
↓
Result: Bias removed — partner may now testify

📜 Communal Property: Can Residents Testify?

Regular Property

  • Relinquishing ownership via kinyan IS effective
  • Former owner loses financial interest
  • Testimony becomes valid once transfer complete
  • Standard rules of disqualification apply

Torah Scroll

  • Relinquishing ownership is NOT fully effective
  • Residents remain OBLIGATED to hear Torah reading
  • They always benefit from the scroll's existence
  • Cannot testify about gifts designated for Torah scrolls

Gifts to the Poor

  • City judges disqualified from adjudicating distribution
  • City residents disqualified as witnesses
  • Support of poor is incumbent on ALL residents
  • Each resident has a financial stake in communal charity

💸 Shmuel: Seller's Guarantee & Testimony

Shmuel
One who sells a field WITHOUT a guarantee of compensation cannot testify on behalf of the buyer — even without a guarantee, he may still have a reputational or moral interest in the sale standing.
Gemara
What if he sells WITH a guarantee? Then he definitely cannot testify — if the buyer loses the field, the seller must compensate him. Clear financial interest!
Questioner
But the baraita says a seller CAN testify for the buyer. How does this square with Shmuel?
Rav Sheshet
The baraita addresses the case of Reuven who ROBBED Shimon's field and sold it to Levi. Shimon (original owner) testifies — he has no guarantee obligation to Levi at all.
Ravin bar Shmuel citing Shmuel מוכר שדה לחבירו שלא באחריות — אינו יכול להעיד עליה לפני הלוקח / A seller without guarantee still cannot testify for the buyer

💼 Partners as Paid Bailees — The Paradox

Mishnah rule: Partners become שומרי שכר (paid bailees) for each other's share of joint property
↓
Question raised: This should be a case of שאלה בבעלים — 'borrowing with the owner present' — which normally EXEMPTS from all liability!
↓
Reasoning: Each partner safeguards the other's property, so they are effectively employed by the other — this should exempt, not impose paid bailee liability
↓
Resolution: The partnership creates mutual obligations simultaneously — neither partner's employment of the other is 'at the time of borrowing' in the classic sense
↓
Practical result: If a partner fails to safeguard joint property through negligence, they owe compensation as a paid bailee — loss or damage without neglect is exempt
