📏 Burial Chamber Layout: Tanna Kamma vs Rabbi Shimon

Tanna Kamma

  • 4 cubits long × 6 cubits wide per chamber
  • 8 niches per chamber
  • 2 chambers per courtyard (opposite sides)
  • Niche width: 6 handbreadths

Rabbi Shimon

  • Chamber interior: 6 cubits wide × 8 cubits long
  • 13 niches per chamber
  • 4+4 along long walls, 3 along back, 2 at entrance
  • 4 chambers per courtyard (one per side)

🏗️ Building a Catacomb: Step by Step

Step 1: Dig the entrance courtyard — 6×6 cubits, accommodating bier length and pallbearers on either side
↓
Step 2: Open burial chambers off the courtyard walls — 2 or 4 depending on the authority followed
↓
Step 3: Carve niches (כּוּכִין, kokhim) into chamber walls — 4 cubits deep, 6 handbreadths wide
↓
Step 4: Assess bedrock quality — hard rock supports tighter, more numerous niches
↓
Step 5: Place 2 niches at chamber entrance — their direction is the subject of Talmudic debate

🚪 Which Direction Do the Entrance Niches Face?

Rabbi Yosei b. R. Ḥanina
The two entrance niches run vertically — like a door bolt (בְּרִיחַ), parallel to the entrance wall.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Burial in a vertical niche is 'burial of donkeys' — a disgrace! The niches cannot run vertically.
Anonymous Resolution
Per Rabbi Yoḥanan: the two niches extend diagonally from the corners of the chamber entrance — not vertically, not straight in.
Gemara Challenge
But then three niches converge at each back corner — won't they cut through each other and collapse the cave wall?
Core Tension Kavod ha-met (dignity of the deceased) requires horizontal burial, but diagonal niches risk structural overlap at cave corners

📐 Solving the Corner Overlap Problem

Problem: Three niches converge at each back corner — overlap threatens structural integrity
↓
Rav Ashi ✓
Sink the diagonal niches lower in the wall — they pass beneath adjacent niches without intersecting
Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua ✗
Make all niches diagonal — but the Gemara flags this as an error
Rav Sheisha b. Rav Idi ✓
Refers to non-viable newborns — their smaller niches don't overlap at corners
↓
Why Rav Huna's view fails:
Diagonal of a 1-cubit square = 1⅖ cubits (≈√2)
All-diagonal layout exceeds available cave dimensions
