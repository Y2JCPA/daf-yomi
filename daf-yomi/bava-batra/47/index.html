⚖️ Who Can Establish Chazakah?

Person claims chazakah (חזקה) — presumption of ownership after 3 years
↓
❌ Cannot Establish
Craftsman — works on others' property by permission
Sharecropper — possesses land as worker
Robber — seized by force, no valid title
✅ Can Establish
Son of craftsman who separated from father's trade
Son of sharecropper who separated
Son of robber — if prior owner admitted ownership

🔨 Craftsman vs. Son: Chazakah Rights

Craftsman / Sharecropper / Robber

  • ❌ Cannot establish chazakah
  • Possession is explained by work/force relationship
  • No presumption of ownership arises from use
  • Three years of possession does NOT help them
  • Rabbi Yoḥanan's ruling — Amud A

Son of Craftsman / Son of Sharecropper

  • ✅ Can establish chazakah
  • Son separated from father's trade/role
  • No permission relationship exists for the son
  • Three years of possession = ownership presumed
  • Even if baraita silent, son is not forgiven like father

📜 Sikarikon Purchase: Rav vs. Shmuel

Rav
The purchase from a Sikarikon buyer is void only when the prior owner told the buyer: 'Go acquire it from the Sikarikon, and I will sell to you afterward.' Only then is the subsequent sale void. Otherwise — valid.
Shmuel
He does not acquire the field even with a document, until the original owner writes a proper bill of sale. Merely purchasing from a Sikarikon is insufficient — the true owner's deed is required.
Rav Huna
I rule in accordance with Shmuel: A robber does not retain the field even if he brings proof by document or witnesses — the field is returned to the original owner along with his payment.
Halacha Rav Huna follows Shmuel: document alone is insufficient; a proper bill of sale from the original owner is required for full acquisition

😰 Duress Sale — Rav Huna's Ruling

Scenario: A person is suspended from a tree (or similarly coerced) and sells an item under that duress
↓
Rav Huna's Ruling: The sale is valid — קנין חל
↓
Principle — גמר ומקני: 'He resolved and transferred ownership' — even under pressure, the seller mentally consented to rid himself of the item in exchange for release
↓
Inner Consent: The duress does not negate the will; the seller wanted to complete the deal to escape the situation
↓
Limit: If the coercion comes from the buyer himself unlawfully, the sale may be void — the seller's consent is then wholly involuntary
