Bava Batra 47 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, which person CAN establish a chazakah?
A. A craftsman who has worked on the property for three years
B. A sharecropper currently farming the land
C. The son of a craftsman who separated from his father's trade
D. A robber who has held the property openly for three years
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan rules that craftsmen and sharecroppers cannot establish chazakah because their possession is explained by their work relationship. But a son who separated from that role has no such relationship — his three years of possession does establish chazakah.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Under what condition does Rava say even the grandson of a robber CANNOT establish chazakah?
A. When he has held the property for fewer than three years
B. When he comes to court claiming his father purchased the field
C. When the original owner has died
D. When there are fewer than two witnesses to his possession
Correct: B — Rava teaches that even the son of the son of a robber cannot establish chazakah when he claims the field because his father purchased it — this merely perpetuates the robbery narrative. Only an admission by the prior owner before witnesses can override the taint.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rav Huna's ruling when a robber brings a document proving his acquisition of a field?
A. The document permanently establishes his ownership
B. The document is valid only if witnessed by two qualified witnesses
C. The robber does not retain the field even with proof — it returns to the original owner
D. The robber keeps the field but must pay the original owner double
Correct: C — Rav Huna (ruling per Shmuel) holds that a robber cannot retain possession of the field even when he brings proof via document or witnesses. The field is returned to the original owner, who pays the robber for his investment.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
A man is suspended from a tree and coerced into selling his field. What is Rav Huna's ruling?
A. The sale is void — physical coercion entirely negates consent
B. The sale is valid — he mentally resolved to transfer ownership to escape the duress
C. The sale requires rabbinical court ratification to take effect
D. The sale is void unless confirmed in writing afterward
Correct: B — Rav Huna rules the duress sale valid based on גמר ומקני — 'he resolved and transferred.' The seller's desire to escape the situation constitutes mental consent to the transaction, making it legally binding.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the key dispute between Rav and Shmuel regarding purchasing land from a Sikarikon (violent extorter)?
A. Rav says always void; Shmuel says always valid with any document
B. Rav says void only if the original owner explicitly promised to sell later; Shmuel requires a proper deed from the original owner
C. Rav requires two witnesses; Shmuel requires three
D. Rav and Shmuel agree — both require the buyer to return the field
Correct: B — Rav holds the subsequent purchase from the original owner is void only when the owner explicitly told the Sikarikon-buyer to acquire it first and then he would sell — implying a coerced promise. Shmuel requires the original owner to write a proper bill of sale for the acquisition to be complete, regardless of other circumstances. Rav Huna rules like Shmuel.
