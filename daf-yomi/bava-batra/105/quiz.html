Bava Batra 105 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Ben Nanas, when a contract contains contradictory expressions, which one governs?
A. The earlier expression, as it reflects original intent
B. The later (final) expression, as it represents the ultimate statement
C. Both expressions are void and the contract is cancelled
D. The disputed amount is divided equally between the parties
Correct: B — Ben Nanas rules that one should 'attend to the final expression' — the last words spoken represent the party's definitive intent and nullify any earlier contradictory statement in the same document.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the intercalated month case, how did Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel and Rabbi Yosei rule regarding the extra month's rent?
A. The tenant owes full rent for the extra month
B. The tenant owes nothing extra for the intercalated month
C. The landlord and tenant divide the extra month's payment
D. The rental contract is declared void for the leap year
Correct: C — Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel and Rabbi Yosei ruled that the landlord and tenant divide the payment for the intercalated month — embodying the Sages' principle that genuine contractual ambiguity results in splitting the disputed amount.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When a seller says 'I sell a kor for 30 sela, each se'a for 1 sela,' when does the buyer acquire ownership of the grain?
A. Only when the entire kor has been measured and fully paid
B. Immediately upon verbal agreement, before any measuring
C. Each se'a is acquired individually as it is measured out
D. Ownership transfers at the close of the market day
Correct: C — By specifying a per-unit price ('each se'a for 1 sela'), the seller signals an intent to transact incrementally. The buyer therefore acquires each se'a the moment it is measured out, so the seller cannot renege on already-measured portions.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
An istera silver coin is worth how many copper ma'a?
A. 72 copper ma'a
B. 80 copper ma'a
C. 100 copper ma'a
D. 96 copper ma'a
Correct: D — An istera is a silver coin equal to 96 copper ma'a. The ambiguity in this daf arises when a seller says 'for an istera, 100 ma'a' — giving two different figures. Rav Huna rules we assume he meant one istera, i.e., 96 ma'a.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara insist it was necessary for Rav to state BOTH the istera ruling AND the intercalated month ruling separately?
A. Because they appear in different tractates of Talmud
B. Because Shmuel agreed with one ruling but not the other
C. Because neither ruling can be inferred from the other alone
D. Because Ben Nanas only addressed the intercalated month case
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that the two rulings address different scenarios with different variables. If only the istera ruling were known, one could not derive the intercalated month ruling, and vice versa. Each was independently necessary.
