Bava Batra 121 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Sheshet, what did Ben Azzai mean when he said 'dissolution of vows was not stated' in the Festival portion?
A. Vows cannot be dissolved on festival days
B. Dissolution of vows by three laymen is not Torah-mandated — it is a rabbinic institution
C. Only the Sanhedrin of 71 may dissolve vows
D. The Torah entirely prohibits dissolving vows
Correct: B — Rav Sheshet explains that while the Torah discusses vows in detail, the institution of three ordinary laymen dissolving vows is rabbinic — the Torah's own provision requires a single expert sage ('heads of the tribes').
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Dimi bar Yosef in Rav Naḥman's name, what happened on the 15th of Av to make it so joyous?
A. King Hoshea removed Jeroboam's roadblocks to Jerusalem
B. The tribe of Benjamin was re-admitted to the congregation
C. The condemned generation of the wilderness stopped dying and God resumed speaking with Moses
D. The slain of Beitar received burial
Correct: C — Rav Dimi bar Yosef in Rav Naḥman's name: on the 15th of Av the last of the decreed generation died. The verse juxtaposes 'when all the men of war were consumed' with 'that the Lord spoke to me' — showing God only resumed speech after the decree was complete.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why was Ahijah the Shilonite able to have known Amram, despite the decree that the entire wilderness generation would die before entering Eretz Yisrael?
A. Ahijah was under 20 years old at the time of the sin of the spies
B. Ahijah was from the tribe of Levi, which was exempt from the death decree
C. Amram died before the sin of the spies so the decree did not apply
D. Ahijah was over 60 at the time and therefore exempt
Correct: B — The decree stated 'all those who were numbered' — the tribe of Levi was never counted in the general census. Therefore Levites like Ahijah were exempt from the decree and lived to enter Eretz Yisrael.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov, which groups were NOT subject to the wilderness death decree?
A. Those under 13 and over 70 at the time of the sin
B. The firstborns of each family
C. Those under 20 and those over 60 at the time of the sin of the spies
D. Only those who personally refused to accept the spies' report
Correct: C — Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov: the decree covered those 'from twenty years old and upward' — understood as up to sixty. Those under 20 were not legally accountable adults; those over 60 were past the age range of those formally counted.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What blessing was instituted specifically in connection with the burial of the slain of Beitar?
A. Shehecheyanu — 'Who has kept us alive to this time'
B. HaTov veHameitiv — 'Who is good and does good'
C. Go'el Yisrael — 'Redeemer of Israel'
D. Birkat Kohanim — the Priestly Blessing
Correct: B — When the slain of Beitar were finally permitted burial (their bodies had been miraculously preserved), the Sages instituted HaTov veHameitiv: 'Who is good' — because the bodies did not decay; 'and does good' — because they were finally granted burial.
