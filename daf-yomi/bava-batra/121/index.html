🏛️ Dissolution of Vows — Who Has Authority?

Ben Azzai
The Torah portion of Festivals mentions the Festivals — but dissolution of vows is not stated there, implying it is a distinct institution.
Rabbi Yosei bar Natan
But the Torah explicitly writes about dissolving vows! How can we say it is 'not stated'?
Rav Sheshet
Ben Azzai means: dissolution of vows by three laymen (hedyotot) is not explicitly stated in the Torah — it is a rabbinic institution derived by the Sages.
Questioner
But the verse says 'heads of the tribes' in the vow portion — doesn't this require tribal leaders, not mere laymen?
Rav Ḥisda (R. Yoḥanan)
'Heads of the tribes' teaches that even a single expert sage (yachid mumḥeh) can dissolve a vow alone — the emphasis is on expertise, not plurality.
Key Principle Three laymen may dissolve vows by rabbinic authority; a single expert sage may do so alone — both derive from 'heads of the tribes.'

💀 Who Was Exempt from the Wilderness Death Decree?

Sin of the Spies — Numbers 14
Decree: the entire generation shall die in the wilderness over 40 years
↓
Who was counted in the decree?
Numbers 14:29: 'all those who were numbered among you, according to your whole number, from twenty years old and upward'
↓
✅ Exempt: Tribe of Levi
Levi was never counted in the general census — 'your whole number' excludes them. Ahijah and Amram survived as Levites.
✅ Exempt: Under 20 or Over 60
Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov: decree covered only ages 20–60. Those under 20 were not accountable adults; those over 60 were beyond the census age.
✅ Exempt: Yair & Machir (Manasseh)
Born in Jacob's era, died after entering Eretz Yisrael. Yair alone was equivalent to most of the Sanhedrin in merit.
↓
15th of Av: Last of the decreed generation died — God resumed direct speech with Moses (Deuteronomy 2:16–17)

מכאן ואילך דמוסיף — יוסיף; דלא מוסיף — יסוף. מאי יסוף? אמר רב יוסף: תיסף אמיה (בבא בתרא קכ"א ע"ב)
🕯️ Tu B'Av — Torah Study and the Lengthening Nights From the 15th of Av onward, the nights begin to lengthen. The Gemara teaches: one who adds (מוסיף) to his nightly Torah study — will add (יוסיף) years to his life. One who does not add — יסוף: his mother shall bury him.

The wordplay: יוסיף (he will add) vs. יִסָּף (he will be gathered/die) — the same root, opposite destinies, hinging on one's commitment to Torah as darkness increases.
