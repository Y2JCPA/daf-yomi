🍷 Wine or Vinegar? The Central Disputes of Daf 96

Rabbi Yoḥanan
The first three days after inspection are definitely wine. After that, it is uncertain — because souring starts at the top of the barrel, where the inspector tasted.
R. Yehoshua b. Levi
The last three days before discovery are definitely vinegar. Before that, it is uncertain — because souring starts at the bottom, invisible to the inspector.
Sages of the South
Both are true: first three days = definitely wine; last three days = definitely vinegar; middle period = uncertain.
Rav Yosef
This baraita can be used as proof for the correct blessing to recite over wine that has the odor of vinegar but still tastes like wine.
Core Question A barrel is inspected and later found to contain vinegar. During the intermediate period, was it wine or vinegar — for purposes of teruma, blessings, and sales?

⚖️ Rav vs. Shmuel: Seller Liability for Soured Wine

Rav
If wine sours within the first three days after the sale, the seller bears responsibility — we assume it had already begun to sour at time of sale and the defect was pre-existing.
Shmuel
Even if wine sours shortly after purchase, the seller is not liable — wine is agitated when carried on the buyer's shoulders, and this disturbance causes it to sour quickly.
Rav Yosef (Ruling)
In an actual case: ruled like Rav for beer that spoiled; ruled like Shmuel for wine that soured. The halakha follows Shmuel for wine.
Final Halakha The halakha follows Shmuel — the seller of wine is not responsible for souring after the sale, as transport agitation is a known cause.

🙏 Blessings Over Various Beverages

Borei Pri HaGafen (Wine Blessing)

  • Standard wine — unambiguous
  • Tamad: 3 jugs water → 4 jugs output (wine absorbed into pomace leaches out)
  • Vinegar-scent but wine-taste (per Sages of South / Rav Yosef)
  • Must have actual wine character or wine-derived volume gain

She'hakol (Generic Blessing)

  • Date beer (שכר תמרים)
  • Barley beer (שכר שעורים)
  • Tamad: 3 jugs water → only 3 jugs output (no net gain — 'it is nothing')
  • Beverages without wine character or grape origin with volume gain

🍇 Rava's Ruling: When Is Tamad Considered Wine?

Step 1 — Define Tamad (תמד): A beverage made by soaking grape pomace (pressed grape skins/seeds) in water after wine production.
↓
Step 2 — Case A (Clear wine status): Pour 3 jugs of water over pomace → resulting liquid = 4 jugs. The extra jug came from wine absorbed in the pomace. This is definitely wine — all agree.
↓
Step 3 — Case B (Definitely not wine): Pour 3 jugs of water → resulting liquid = 3 jugs. No net gain from the pomace. 'It is nothing' — definitely not wine — all agree.
↓
Step 4 — Disputed case: Pour 3 jugs → resulting liquid = 2.5 jugs. The pomace absorbed 0.5 jugs net, but contributed 1 jug of pure wine. Tanna'im disagree here.
↓
Step 5 — Aḥerim's reasoning: Even in the disputed case, 1 jug of pure wine replaced the absorbed water. Since pure wine is present, the beverage should recieve the wine blessing — borei pri hagafen.
