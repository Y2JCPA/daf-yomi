Bava Batra 96 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, where does the wine-souring process begin in a barrel, and what is the legal consequence?
A. At the bottom — so the last 3 days are definitely vinegar
B. At the top — so the first 3 days after inspection are definitely wine
C. Uniformly throughout — so the entire barrel changes at once
D. At the spout — so only the drawn wine is affected
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan holds that souring starts at the top of the barrel. Since the inspector tasted the top wine and it was fine, the first three days after inspection are considered definitely wine.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the apparent contradiction in the Sages of the South's position (first 3 days = wine; last 3 days = vinegar)?
A. They hold that smell determines status, not taste
B. They hold that taste determines wine status — vinegar smell alone during wine-taste period is irrelevant
C. They hold that both smell and taste must indicate vinegar simultaneously
D. They hold that a rabbinical decree resolves all intermediate cases as wine
Correct: B — The Sages of the South hold that as long as wine tastes like wine, it is wine. The certainty of vinegar in the last 3 days is due to the souring process, not merely the smell.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Shmuel's reasoning for exempting a wine seller when the wine sours after the sale?
A. The buyer should have inspected the wine before purchase
B. The seller cannot control temperature during delivery
C. Wine is agitated when carried on the buyer's shoulders, causing it to sour
D. The sale is complete and all risk transfers immediately to the buyer
Correct: C — Shmuel explains that the physical agitation of carrying wine on one's shoulders disturbs the wine and can cause it to sour quickly after purchase — the seller is not responsible for this.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rava, when is tamad (grape pomace soaked in water) unambiguously considered wine by all opinions?
A. When it has the color and smell of wine
B. When 3 jugs of water poured over pomace yield 3 jugs of beverage
C. When 3 jugs of water poured over pomace yield 4 jugs of beverage
D. When the pomace came from red grapes specifically
Correct: C — Rava rules that if pouring 3 jugs of water yields 4 jugs of beverage, the extra jug clearly came from wine absorbed in the pomace — all opinions agree this is wine and requires borei pri hagafen.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How do Rav Yosef's practical rulings in actual cases reflect the debate between Rav and Shmuel?
A. He ruled like Rav in all cases involving fermented beverages
B. He ruled like Shmuel for beer and like Rav for wine
C. He ruled like Rav for beer and like Shmuel for wine — and the halakha follows Shmuel
D. He split the loss 50/50 between buyer and seller in all cases
Correct: C — Rav Yosef ruled like Rav (seller liable) for beer that spoiled, but like Shmuel (seller exempt) for wine that soured. The final halakha follows Shmuel for wine sales.
