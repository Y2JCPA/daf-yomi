5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman (Version 1), if someone acquires the right to place thin beams on a neighbor's wall, what additional right does this grant?
A. The right to build a permanent structure on the wall
B. The right to place thick beams on the wall
C. The right to install a drainpipe through the wall
D. No additional right — thin beams are the maximum permitted
Correct: B — Version 1 of Rav Naḥman rules that acquiring the lesser use-right (thin beams) grants the greater one (thick beams), and thick beams grant the right to a full roof covering.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Ravina rules that a beam resting on a neighbor's wall for how long does NOT yet create an acquired right?
A. Three days
B. Seven days
C. Thirty days
D. Three years
Correct: C — Ravina rules that up to thirty days, the neighbor can claim he allowed use only as a temporary courtesy and no chazakah is yet established.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Abaye, when two houses face each other across a public domain, how should rooftop privacy fences be built?
A. One neighbor builds the full fence; the other pays half the cost
B. Each builds a fence on half his own roof, positioned so the fences are NOT directly opposite each other
C. Each builds a fence on his entire roof
D. The municipality is responsible for constructing the barrier
Correct: B — Abaye rules each owner builds a fence on half his own roof, but the fences must be offset so they are not directly aligned — eliminating the line of sight between the two rooftops.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the dispute about two courtyards at different heights, whose opinion is supported by the baraita?
A. Rav Huna — each owner builds only from his own level upward
B. Rav Ḥisda — the lower owner builds up to the upper level; the upper owner completes the rest
C. A third opinion not mentioned by the amoraim
D. The baraita is inconclusive and supports neither opinion
Correct: B — The baraita explicitly states the lower courtyard owner builds up to the height of the upper courtyard, and the upper courtyard owner then completes the wall above that point — supporting Rav Ḥisda.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman citing Shmuel, what must a person whose roof is adjacent to his neighbor's courtyard build?
A. A fence ten handbreadths high between his roof and the neighbor's roof
B. A fence four cubits high on his roof overlooking the neighbor's courtyard
C. A fence of any height — the exact measurement is not specified
D. Nothing — the neighbor accepted the overlooking situation by building there
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman citing Shmuel requires a four-cubit fence on the roof that overlooks the neighbor's courtyard, preventing the roof-owner from seeing down into the courtyard.
