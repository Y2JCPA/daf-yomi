📜 Ratifying a Document: Brothers as Co-Signatories

Ravina
This case of brother co-signatories is like the Mishna's ruling about three brothers each testifying about a separate year alongside an unrelated witness — just as that is valid, so too here!
Rav Ashi
The cases are NOT comparable! There, three-quarters of the property is upheld NOT based solely on brothers' mouths — each brother has an independent co-witness. Here, the entire document rests on the brothers alone.
Rav Ashi
There, each pair functions independently. Here, the surviving brothers cannot form a valid ratification panel at all — they are disqualified as a group.
Ruling When two brothers co-signed a document and one dies, ratification is impossible — the remaining brothers are an invalid panel, and their combined testimony cannot authenticate the document.

🏠 Acts That Do vs. Don't Establish Chazakah

❌ Insufficient for Chazakah

  • Standing an animal in another's courtyard
  • Placing items or objects in the yard
  • Letting manure dry in the courtyard
  • Temporary, minor, or tolerated use
  • Acts an owner would not specifically protest

✅ Sufficient for Chazakah

  • Constructing a partition (mechitza)
  • Building a stable for animals
  • Structural alterations to the property
  • Permanent improvements signaling ownership
  • Acts an owner would specifically object to

👕 Partners, Laundry, Modesty & the Torah Scholar's Garment

הֹלֵךְ צְדָקוֹת וְדֹבֵר מֵישָׁרִים — ישעיהו לג:טו
Rabbi Yochanan in the name of Rabbi Bena'a Partners' Laundry Rule: Partners may prevent each other from using a shared courtyard for virtually any purpose — except washing laundry. It is not the way of Jewish women to be degraded by laundering in more exposed locations; the shared courtyard must remain available to them.

Verse (Isaiah 33:15): 'He that walks righteously… that shuts his eyes from seeing evil' — this refers to one who averts his gaze from women doing laundry in public, choosing to walk away rather than expose himself to seeing them.

Torah Scholar's Garment: Must be long enough that no flesh is visible beneath it. The clasp/ring: positioned on the outside if he has no attendant (so he can fasten it himself); on the inside if he has an attendant — or adjusted based on whether it is daytime or nighttime.
