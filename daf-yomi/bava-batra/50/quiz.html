QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Ameimar, what happens if a husband unilaterally sells his wife's usufruct property and then dies?
A. The sale is fully valid since he held usufruct rights during his lifetime
B. The wife may come and reclaim the property from the buyer
C. The buyer retains the property but must compensate the wife
D. The sale is voidable only if the wife objects within 30 days of his death
Correct: B — Ameimar rules that since the husband held only usufruct (not title), his unilateral sale was invalid. After his death, the wife can reclaim the property directly from the buyer.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Which Tanna holds that NEITHER master in the slave sale scenario is included in the 'a day or two days' exemption?
A. Rabbi Meir
B. Rabbi Yehuda
C. Rabbi Yosei
D. Rabbi Elazar
Correct: D — Rabbi Elazar holds that neither master qualifies: the first lacks 'his money' (title) and the second lacks authority (possession). Since neither fulfills both conditions of Exodus 21:21, neither is exempt — both face court-imposed death.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rava say Rav means when he states that a married woman must 'protest' regarding her property?
A. She must protest any stranger who uses her property for three years
B. She must protest her husband when he digs pits, ditches, and caves in her field
C. She must file an annual declaration in court preserving her ownership
D. She must protest any sale of her property within 30 days of learning of it
Correct: B — Rava explains that Rav is referring to protest against the husband himself — specifically when he performs damaging acts (pits, ditches, caves) that exceed his permitted usufruct rights. Silence in such cases could forfeit her claim.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Rabbi Elazar's principle require for a valid sale of property?
A. The seller must have possessed the property for at least three years
B. The seller must hold both title to the item and the right to enjoy its profits
C. Both parties must transact before a rabbinic court with written documentation
D. The sale must be ratified by the legal owner within thirty days
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar holds that a valid sale requires the seller to possess both the item itself (title/ownership) and the right to enjoy its profits (usufruct). Holding only one of the two renders any sale invalid.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rav Naḥman rule in the name of Rabba bar Avuh about damage and chazakah?
A. Damaging use of land establishes chazakah faster — in one year instead of three
B. There is no presumptive ownership established through damage
C. Only intentional damage witnessed by two people can block chazakah
D. Damage voids any chazakah already established by prior peaceful use
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman in the name of Rabba bar Avuh states that one cannot establish chazakah (presumptive ownership) through damaging use of another's property — damage simply does not count toward the three-year period.
