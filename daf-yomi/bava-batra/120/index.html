⚖️ Daughters of Zelophehad: Marriage Freedom or Tribal Restriction?

Shmuel
'Let them marry whom they think best' (Num. 36:6) — the daughters of Zelophehad were permitted to marry members of any tribe in Israel.
Rabba
Objection from a baraita! 'Every daughter who possesses an inheritance shall be wife to one of the family of her father's tribe' — this is a mandatory restriction!
Baraita Resolution
The phrase 'this is the matter' (זֶה הַדָּבָר) signals a ruling limited to that generation only — daughters of later generations were not bound to marry within their tribe.
Rabba Zuti
Then 'this is the matter' regarding offerings slaughtered outside the Tabernacle should also be generation-limited — yet that prohibition is eternal! The analogy is problematic.
Central Tension Numbers 36:6 says 'marry whom they think best' — yet 36:8 mandates tribal marriage. The phrase 'Zeh HaDavar' resolves the scope: a ruling for that generation only, unlike the eternal prohibition on outside slaughter.

👵 Jochebed: 130-Year-Old 'Daughter' of Levi

Jacob descends to Egypt with 70 souls (Gen. 46) — Jochebed born at that very moment, 'between the walls' at the border
↓
Jews in Egypt: 210 years total — Jochebed's birth coincides precisely with the arrival, establishing her as the oldest of that generation
↓
Moses was 80 years old at the time of the Exodus — subtracting: 210 − 80 = Jochebed was 130 when Moses was born
↓
Yet called 'daughter of Levi' (Exodus 2:1) — the word bat (daughter) signals miraculous rejuvenation: her youthful appearance was restored
↓
Amram 'went and took' her — new language of betrothal implies a remarriage; Amram had separated from Jochebed and returned on Miriam's persuasion

🏛️ Daughters of Zelophehad: Which Generation Was Bound?

Rav Yehuda / Shmuel
'Marry whom they think best' — complete freedom. The daughters could wed any Israelite man from any tribe without restriction.
Rabba (objection)
But the baraita says: the mitzva of tribal marriage was specifically for the daughters themselves — they initiated the inheritance request, so they must keep it in the tribe!
Baraita (resolution)
'This is the matter' — a phrase that limits the ruling to that generation only. Later daughters inheriting land were never commanded to marry within their tribe.
Rabba Zuti to Rav Ashi
If so, 'this is the matter' by outside-slaughter (Lev. 17) should also limit that prohibition to one generation — yet we hold it as eternal law!
Answer The context of each 'Zeh HaDavar' determines scope. Outside slaughter = addressed to all Israel for all time. Tribal inheritance = addressed in the context of dividing the Land once, for that generation.

🔗 The 'Zeh HaDavar' Verbal Analogy Chain

זֶה הַדָּבָר — 'This is the matter'
An unusual phrase appearing in three Torah passages — signals a gezerah shavah verbal analogy
↓
Inheritance (Num. 36)
Daughters must marry within tribe — but only that generation; limited by context of Land division
Vows (Num. 30)
Moses spoke to heads of tribes — linked to outside offerings, giving rabbinic sages authority to dissolve vows
Outside Offerings (Lev. 17)
Slaughtering outside Tabernacle incurs karet — permanent, applies to all Israel including Aaron's sons
↓
Analogy: Vows ↔ Outside Offerings
Just as offerings apply to Aaron, his sons, and all Israel — so too vow dissolution applies to all. Just as offerings involve tribal heads — so too vow release.
↓
Practical ruling: A halakhic authority (sage) can dissolve vows; tribal heads have special relevance to both domains
