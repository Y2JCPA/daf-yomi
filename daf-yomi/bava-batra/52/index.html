🔑 When Does Chazaka (Presumptive Ownership) Take Effect?

One is found in possession of property
↓
Possession WITH a Claim
Prior owner protests — possessor must counter with a claim. Requires three years of use plus a credible claim.
Possession WITHOUT a Claim
Prior owner concedes ownership — e.g., gift, divided inheritance, windfall. No years required.
↓
Special rule (Rav Hoshaya): Acts done in the seller's presence — locking door, fencing, breaching fence — establish chazaka immediately

💼 Bailee Receives a Deposit from a Minor

Cannot return the deposit to the minor — a minor cannot properly safeguard property
↓
Must make a segulla (safe investment) on the minor's behalf
↓
Rav Ḥisda: Purchase a Torah scroll for the minor — spiritually and practically valuable
↓
Rabba bar Rav Huna: Purchase date palms — their lumber serves ongoing material needs (e.g., sukka walls)
↓
If the minor dies before reclaiming the deposit, return the investment to his heirs

🪦 The Rings of Rabba bar bar Ḥana's Wife

אִם נֶאֱמֶנֶת עָלֶיךָ — עֲשֵׂה כְּדִבְרֶיהָ
Case Study — Deathbed Declaration As she was dying, the wife of Rabba bar bar Ḥana declared: "These rings in my possession belong to Marta and her daughter's sons." Rabba bar bar Ḥana asked Rav what to do. Rav's ruling: If you assess it is likely the rings were deposited with her, act as she explained and return them. If not credible, form a charitable explanation of her explanation — find a reasonable account of how the rings were legitimately hers.

📜 Brother Managing Family Commerce — Who Bears the Burden of Proof?

Rav
The brother managing commerce must bring proof that items documented under his name truly belong to him — the burden falls on him, not the other heirs.
Shmuel
Even orphan-heirs must bring proof — we do not simply award them property without evidence merely because their father died.
Rava
I removed scissors and a book of aggada from orphans without requiring proof from the claimant — items typically lent or rented are presumed not owned by the holder.
Rav Huna bar Avin
One holding items typically lent or rented who claims 'I purchased them' is not believed — the very nature of such items undermines his presumption of ownership.
Resolution (Rav Naḥman) A baraita confirms: the brother managing commerce must bring proof. The same rule applies to a widow managing her late husband's estate — documents under her name do not suffice.

👨‍👩‍👦 Who Cannot Establish Chazaka on Whom?

Father ↔ Son

  • A father cannot establish chazaka on his son's property
  • A son cannot establish chazaka on his father's property
  • Reason: each freely uses the other's property — silence implies permission, not concession of ownership

Brothers

  • Brothers cannot establish chazaka on each other's property
  • Rava: Even if they separated (lived apart), the rule does not change
  • Rav Yirmeya of Difti: Rav Pappi ruled this way in practice — separation is legally irrelevant

Widow in Husband's Estate

  • A widow managing commerce in her husband's home must also bring proof
  • Bills of sale circulating under her name do not establish ownership
  • Same standard as the managing brother — bring proof or property reverts to heirs

🚪 Acts That Establish Chazaka Immediately (Rav Hoshaya's Baraita)

Buyer performs a physical act on the purchased property
↓
Done in Seller's Presence
Locked the door
Fenced the property
Breached the fence — even slightly
NOT in Seller's Presence
Act alone is insufficient — buyer still needs a full three-year chazaka with a valid claim
↓
Act performed in seller's presence = immediate, full chazaka — no waiting period required
