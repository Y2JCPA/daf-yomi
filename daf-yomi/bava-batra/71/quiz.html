Bava Batra 71 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the mishna, which of the following is NOT included when one sells a field, even if he says 'and everything in it'?
A. The trees growing in the field
B. The cistern, winepress, and dovecote
C. The boundary stones of the field
D. The topsoil and earth
Correct: B — The mishna explicitly excludes the cistern (בור), winepress (גת), and dovecote (שובך) from a field sale — even when the seller uses the inclusive phrase 'and everything in it.'
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Akiva, when must a seller purchase a path through the buyer's land to reach his retained cistern?
A. Always — any time a seller retains items in a sold field
B. Only when the cistern is completely underground
C. When the bill of sale did NOT explicitly exclude the retained items
D. Only when the buyer demands it before a rabbinic court
Correct: C — Rabbi Akiva rules that if the seller retained a cistern but the sale document did not say 'apart from these items,' he must purchase a path. An explicit exclusion in the bill of sale implies retained access rights.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the key reason the mishna treats a gift differently from a sale regarding which items are transferred?
A. Gifts are governed by entirely different biblical laws than sales
B. A gift giver acts with a generous eye (בעין יפה) and is presumed to give everything
C. A gift legally cannot exclude items under rabbinic enactment
D. The recipient of a gift has stronger property claims than a buyer
Correct: B — Rav Ashi derives from the mishna that הנותן בעין יפה נותן — one who gives a gift gives generously. Unlike a seller who bargains for value, a giver is presumed to give all contents without reservation.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rav Huna's novel ruling about a seller who retains trees within a field he has sold?
A. The seller forfeits the trees once the field is sold to another
B. The seller retains the trees but the land beneath them belongs to the buyer
C. The seller retains both the trees AND the surrounding land to sustain the trees
D. The buyer must pay the seller an annual fee for use of the land beneath the trees
Correct: C — Rav Huna rules that when a seller retains trees, he also retains the surrounding land — otherwise the buyer could demand the seller uproot the trees, since they draw nutrients and water from the buyer's soil.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is Rabbi Shimon's position on consecrating a field, and how does it differ from the Rabbis?
A. Rabbi Shimon says consecration includes everything; the Rabbis say certain items are excluded
B. Rabbi Shimon says the grafted carob and sycamore trunk are NOT consecrated along with the field; the Rabbis say they ARE
C. Rabbi Shimon says no trees are ever consecrated; the Rabbis say all trees are consecrated
D. Rabbi Shimon and the Rabbis agree on consecration but disagree about sales
Correct: B — The Rabbis hold that consecrating a field consecrates everything including the grafted carob and sycamore trunk. Rabbi Shimon says items ordinarily excluded from a sale are also not consecrated — EXCEPT for the grafted carob and sycamore trunk, which ARE consecrated.
