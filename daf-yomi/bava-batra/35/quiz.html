Bava Batra 35 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
When two parties dispute property and neither has proof, what does Shmuel rule?
A. Divide the property equally between them
B. The current possessor automatically wins
C. The judges decide based on shudda — their own discretion
D. Draw lots to determine the winner
Correct: C — Shmuel rules shudda d'dayaynei — the case is resolved by judicial discretion. Rav disagrees and holds they divide the property equally.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is 'derara' and why does it matter in the exchange case?
A. A Babylonian legal document proving prior ownership
B. Each party's definite financial stake — both once owned the animal, giving each a legitimate claim
C. A ruling that the offspring always belongs to the buyer
D. A synonym for shudda in Aramaic
Correct: B — Derara means definite financial involvement or claim. In the exchange case, both buyer and seller actually owned the cow/maidservant at some point, so both have a concrete claim to the offspring — warranting equal division.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, what is the correct ruling when an outsider seizes disputed property after a 'stronger prevails' court ruling?
A. His seizure stands — the court's ruling licensed it
B. He pays a fine but keeps the property
C. The property is taken back from him — he is a robber
D. He must share it equally with the original disputants
Correct: C — Rav Ashi holds the outsider IS called a robber and the property is returned. 'Not called a robber' only means he cannot benefit from takkanat hashavim (the special leniency for penitent robbers), not that his seizure stands.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How can an original landowner prevent a possessor from establishing chazakah through three years of use?
A. File a formal lawsuit in court within the first year
B. Physically re-enter the field at least once during the three years
C. Lodge a public protest (machaa) against the possessor's occupation
D. Produce two witnesses to the original sale
Correct: C — Rabbi Abba and the subsequent discussion establish that a public protest (machaa) breaks the chain of chazakah. The Sura mortgage custom confirms this: protest preserves the owner's rights even during another party's permitted occupation.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav (cited by Rav Yehuda), how is a Jew treated who claims land he received from a gentile?
A. He must produce a written deed — no other form of proof is accepted in Jewish courts
B. He is treated like a gentile and may rely on the same acquisition methods a gentile uses
C. He automatically wins because Jewish law favors Jewish claimants over gentile land customs
D. His claim is invalid until ratified by a full rabbinical court session
Correct: B — Rav rules that the Jew steps into the gentile's legal shoes. Just as a gentile can establish ownership through chazakah-type possession without a deed, the Jew who received the land from him may rely on the same mode of acquisition.
