Bava Batra 149 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
When a dying man says 'יזכה פלוני בנכסי' (so-and-so shall benefit from my property), what does the Gemara conclude?
A. The recipient acquires all the property as a complete gift
B. The recipient gets only partial use of the property
C. The recipient must pay fair value for the property
D. The Gemara leaves the question unresolved (תיקו)
Correct: D — The Gemara raises the dilemma — does 'benefit' mean full ownership or mere use? — but never resolves it. It remains a תיקו.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why could Rav Mari NOT acquire Issur's dinars through inheritance?
A. Rav Mari was a minor and legally incompetent at the time
B. Issur had already donated the money to the Temple treasury
C. Rav Mari was conceived before Issur converted, so he is not Issur's legal heir
D. The dinars were in a foreign country beyond Jewish legal jurisdiction
Correct: C — Rava explains: since Rav Mari was conceived before Issur converted to Judaism, he does not have the status of a Jewish heir and cannot inherit from his father.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Ika bar Rav Ami, how could Rav Mari successfully acquire the dinars?
A. By traveling to the banker and performing meshicha in person
B. By having Issur execute a document of admission that the money belongs to Rav Mari
C. By waiting until Issur's death and claiming as a universal heir
D. By performing kinyan sudar with a third-party agent at the bank
Correct: B — Rav Ika proposes that Issur issue a שטר הודאה — an admission document — declaring the dinars already belong to Rav Mari. This transfers ownership without requiring physical possession.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Zeira praise the rulings of Rav Yehuda and Rav Yirmeya as 'meticulous'?
A. Both sages agree that only land — never movables — can satisfy the reservation requirement
B. Each opinion, though different, follows an internally consistent and elegant logical structure
C. Both opinions perfectly match the plain reading of the mishna
D. Both sages derived their rulings directly from a single biblical verse
Correct: B — Rabbi Zeira admires that each sage's ruling is logically coherent on its own terms. Even where they disagree, each position flows from a consistent underlying rationale.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Abaye counter Rav Yosef's claim that the mishna requires 'land' (קרקע) specifically?
A. He shows from a baraita that the mishna's author holds movables are always sufficient
B. He argues that Rav Yirmeya's view was only a minority opinion and was rejected
C. He cites Pe'a 3:8 where 'land' refers to a Canaanite slave, proving the term is not always literal
D. He demonstrates that Rav Yosef misread the mishna due to a scribal error
Correct: C — Abaye points to Pe'a 3:8, where writing 'all his property' to a Canaanite slave frees that slave — showing 'land/property' in halachic texts can include non-literal items like a person, undercutting Rav Yosef's strict reading.
