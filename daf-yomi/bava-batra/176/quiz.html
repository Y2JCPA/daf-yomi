Bava Batra 176 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa's final ruling, can a creditor with an oral loan (no document) collect from purchasers of the debtor's property?
A. Yes, always — oral loans have full lien status
B. No — only from unsold property of the debtor
C. Yes, but only from the debtor's heirs
D. Only if the loan was made before a court
Correct: B — Rav Pappa rules that an oral loan creditor CAN collect from the debtor's heirs, but CANNOT collect from purchasers of the debtor's property — only from unsold property.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the 'strangled debtor' scenario, why does ben Nannas argue the guarantee is NOT binding?
A. The guarantee lacked witnesses
B. The guarantor was a minor
C. The guarantor spoke only to spare the debtor embarrassment, not as a real obligation
D. The creditor acted unlawfully by strangling the debtor
Correct: C — Ben Nannas holds that the guarantee was given under social pressure to end the embarrassing scene — not as a genuine financial commitment — and therefore creates no binding obligation.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the final halakha for a guarantor who accepts responsibility AT THE TIME the loan money is given?
A. He requires an act of acquisition (kinyan) to be obligated
B. He does NOT require a kinyan — obligation is fully binding
C. He can only be held to unsold property regardless
D. His guarantee is invalid without signed witnesses
Correct: B — The Gemara (176b) concludes: a guarantor who accepts at the time of the giving of money does NOT require a kinyan — the obligation is fully binding with lien on all property.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara ultimately resolve the apparent contradiction between Rav and Rabbi Yoḥanan on guarantors?
A. Rav retracted his first opinion in a later teaching
B. The first statement of Rav applied to a different type of loan
C. The presentation of their dispute was inaccurate — they actually agree
D. Rabbi Yoḥanan only disagreed about guarantees given under duress
Correct: C — The Gemara says the framing was inaccurate: 'Say instead — and so Rabbi Yoḥanan says as Rav did.' They are in agreement that even a guarantee written before signing grants only unsold-property collection.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
A guarantor accepts responsibility for a loan AFTER the money has already been given, with no kinyan performed. What are the creditor's rights?
A. Full collection including sold property
B. Collection from unsold property only
C. No collection — guarantee is invalid without kinyan
D. Collection from heirs of the guarantor only
Correct: B — Without a kinyan, a guarantor who accepts after money was given has not created a lien on previously sold property. The creditor can collect only from the guarantor's currently unsold property.
