⚔️ Rav's Two Opinions — Guarantor Timing

Rav (1st opinion)
If the guarantee is written BEFORE the signing of the promissory note, the creditor CAN collect from property the guarantor has already sold to others — full lien applies.
Mishna (undisputed)
If the guarantee emerged AFTER the signing of the note, the creditor can collect ONLY from the guarantor's unsold property.
Rav (2nd opinion)
Even if the guarantee is written BEFORE the signing, the creditor can still collect ONLY from unsold property. Both cases are the same.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
In BOTH cases — before or after signing — only unsold property. Even if the document explicitly says 'and so-and-so is a guarantor' (with the conjunction 'and').
Gemara's Resolution The apparent dispute between Rav and Rabbi Yoḥanan was inaccurately framed. 'Say instead: And so Rabbi Yoḥanan says as Rav did' — they ultimately agree.

📜 Debt Instruments and Collection Rights

Oral Loan (No Document)

  • ✓ Collect from debtor's heirs
  • ✗ Cannot collect from purchasers of debtor's property
  • Source: Rav Pappa — final halakha

Handwritten Doc (No Witnesses)

  • ✓ Collect from unsold property
  • ✗ Cannot collect from liened/sold property
  • Parallel: a get handed before witnesses is valid even without signatures (R. Elazar)

Guarantor After Signing

  • ✓ Collect from unsold property only
  • ✗ Cannot collect from sold property
  • Mishna ruling — undisputed by all opinions

Guarantor With Kinyan

  • ✓ Fully obligated — can collect from sold property
  • Act of acquisition (קנין) substitutes for proper timing
  • Ruling: Rav Yehuda citing Shmuel

📌 Guarantor Obligation: The Definitive Rule

ערב שקיבל עליו בשעת מתן מעות — אינו צריך קנין; לאחר מתן מעות — צריך קנין
Final Halakha — 176b A guarantor who accepts responsibility at the time the loan money is given does NOT require an act of acquisition (קנין) — the obligation is fully binding and the creditor may collect even from property the guarantor has since sold. BUT if the guarantor accepts responsibility after the money was given, he requires a kinyan to create a binding lien on sold property.
