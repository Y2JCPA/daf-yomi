Bava Batra 130 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the mishna, if a man says 'my daughter shall inherit' when he has a son, what is the ruling of the first tanna?
A. The daughter inherits as he stated
B. He has said nothing — the bequeathal is void
C. The estate is split equally between son and daughter
D. The bequeathal is valid only if confirmed in writing
Correct: B — The first tanna rules he has 'said nothing' because this stipulates against Torah inheritance law, which takes precedence.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rabbi Yishmael (son of Rabbi Yoḥanan ben Beroka) say is the actual scope of the dispute?
A. They dispute even bequeathing to a daughter over a son
B. They only dispute when redistributing within the same class — one son among other sons
C. They agree that all bequeathal by fathers is valid
D. They dispute only regarding a firstborn's double portion
Correct: B — Rabbi Yishmael clarifies that the dispute is only when redistributing within the same heir-class. Bequeathing to a daughter when sons exist is void according to everyone.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
From where does Rava derive that a father may choose among his sons for inheritance?
A. "Honor your father and your mother" (Exodus 20:12)
B. "You shall not move your neighbor's boundary" (Deuteronomy 19:14)
C. "Then it shall be, in the day that he causes his sons to inherit" (Deuteronomy 21:16)
D. "The inheritance shall pass to his daughter" (Numbers 27:8)
Correct: C — Rava derives from Deuteronomy 21:16 that the Torah grants the father permission to bequeath to whichever of his sons he wishes.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does the baraita teach about the tereifot principle?
A. All tereifot may be derived from each other by analogy
B. One may not compare one type of fatal wound to another — each stands independently
C. Only a Sage who personally witnessed a tereifah may rule on similar cases
D. Tereifot categories are derived by kal v'chomer from the most severe wound
Correct: B — The baraita states: 'With regard to tereifot, one does not say this wound is similar to that wound.' Each wound-category has independent halakhot and cannot be extended by analogy.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What did Rava instruct Rav Pappa and Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua to do if they found a refutation of his ruling?
A. Publicize the refutation immediately so all students could evaluate it
B. Discard the ruling and follow the refutation without delay
C. Do not tear up the ruling until first discussing it with him in person
D. Apply the ruling anyway unless the refutation is confirmed by three Sages
Correct: C — Rava said: do not tear up my ruling until you come before me. If I have a valid explanation I will give it, and if not, I will retract — collaborative engagement, not unilateral rejection.
