💍 Examine Her Brothers Before Marrying

וַיִּקַּח אַהֲרֹן אֶת-אֱלִישֶׁבַע בַּת-עַמִּינָדָב אֲחוֹת נַחְשׁוֹן (שמות ו:כג)
Rava — Practical Wisdom One who marries a woman should first examine her brothers — for children often resemble their maternal uncles in character and lineage.

Proof: Aaron married Elisheva, described as sister of Nahshon. Since Nahshon was a prince of his tribe, the Torah hints: Aaron's children would likewise become princes. The brother's qualities foreshadow the children's potential.

🗣️ Equal Inheritance for Sons and Daughters?

Abaye
If there is no son, who inherits — the city ruler? Surely the daughter inherits! But do a son and daughter share equally?
Rav Pappa
Perhaps when both a son and daughter exist, they should split the estate equally — neither alone should inherit all of it.
Abaye
If they inherit equally, then 'if a man has no son' is obvious! Of course the daughter alone gets everything when there is no son!
Rav Pappa
The verse IS needed — it teaches that when a son DOES exist, he inherits everything and the daughter inherits nothing alongside him.
Resolution 'If a man dies and has no son, you shall transfer his inheritance to his daughter' — the verse teaches that only the absence of a son triggers the daughter's inheritance.

🗿 Jonathan ben Gershom: Idol Priest to Temple Treasurer

Family tradition received: 'Always hire yourself out to avodah zarah rather than become dependent on charity from others'
↓
Jonathan's application: When the tribe of Dan offered him a paid role as priest for Micah's idol, he accepted — believing his tradition literally permitted idol worship for pay
↓
The Gemara corrects this: The tradition did NOT mean actual idol worship! Avodah zarah here means strange/unfamiliar labor — any degrading work is preferable to receiving charity
↓
Jonathan's real flaw: He was excessively devoted to money, which led him to misapply the tradition and rationalize serving an idol for financial gain
↓
King David's wise response: Recognizing Jonathan's attachment to wealth, David appointed him director of the Temple treasuries — channeling the trait constructively (Shebuel = 'he returned to God')

📚 Three Amoraic Derivations: Son Precedes Daughter

Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov

  • Source: Daughters of Zelophehad (Num 27:3)
  • They said: 'Our father had no son'
  • Implies: a son would have excluded them
  • Objection: Perhaps this was only the daughters' assumption, not actual law?
  • Answer: God affirmed their claim, validating the premise

Ravina

  • Source: hakkarov elav (Num 27:11)
  • 'The closest relative inherits'
  • Son is closer than daughter: he carries the father's name
  • Son stands in father's place for ancestral field redemption
  • Objection raised and addressed: daughter also has closeness bonds

Third Derivation

  • Source: Slave inheritance (Lev 25:46)
  • 'You may make them inheritance for livneikhem'
  • livneikhem = sons specifically, not daughters
  • Objection: In blessing contexts, beneikhem means all children broadly
  • Answer: Blessings use broad meaning; legal halakha uses narrow sense
