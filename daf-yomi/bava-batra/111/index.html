⚖️ Who Inherits from a Mother?

First Tanna
A son precedes a daughter even regarding the mother's estate — derived from 'every daughter who possesses an inheritance from the tribes' (Num 36:8). The plural implies a son inherits first.
R. Zekharya ben HaKatzav
Son and daughter are equal regarding the mother's property. The a fortiori reasoning (kal v'chomer) gives the son no advantage over the daughter when it comes to inheriting from the mother.
Rav Naḥman
We rule against R. Zekharya ben HaKatzav. Despite Rav Nittai and Rav Huna bar Ḥiyya attempting to rule in his favor, the halakha follows the first tanna — the son inherits first.
Core Dispute Does the a fortiori inference from 'daughter inherits mother' elevate son and daughter to equal footing, or does a separate verse restore the son's priority?

📐 Deriving Maternal Inheritance: The Kal V'Chomer

Numbers 36:8: 'Every daughter who possesses an inheritance from the tribes...'
Explicit: daughter inherits from her mother
↓
Question: From where do we derive that a son also inherits from his mother?
↓
A Fortiori (Kal V'Chomer)
A daughter's power is diminished with regard to other inheritances (son precedes), yet she inherits from her mother → a son, whose power is stronger, certainly inherits from his mother
Dayo Limitation
The dayo principle: the conclusion cannot exceed its source. Son's right from mother = daughter's right from mother → they are equal (R. Zekharya)

🗣️ Sages React to R. Zekharya ben HaKatzav

Rav Nittai
I considered ruling in accordance with R. Zekharya ben HaKatzav — son and daughter are equal in the mother's estate.
Shmuel
Do not act on that opinion! The halakha does not follow R. Zekharya.
Rav Huna bar Ḥiyya
Rav Huna [my teacher] says in the name of Shmuel that we rule like R. Zekharya — I also considered ruling this way.
Rav Naḥman
What is this?! We rule like Rabbi Yannai — the son precedes the daughter even in the mother's property, based on the verse 'from the tribes of the children of Israel.'
Outcome Halakha follows the first tanna / Rabbi Yannai: son precedes daughter even in inheriting from the mother.

👶 Firstborn Double Portion — Whose 'First' Counts?

Father's Firstborn

  • Deut 21:17: 'First fruits of his strength' (ono)
  • Double portion applies to firstborn of the father
  • Even if the child is not the mother's firstborn
  • Rava: 'The right of the firstborn is his' — masculine, referring to the father

Mother's Firstborn — Excluded

  • A child who is first for the mother but not the father does NOT receive double portion
  • E.g., bachelor marries widow — her existing children don't count for him
  • The double portion tracks the father's lineage exclusively
  • Confirmed by the possessive 'his strength' (ono vs. on)

Non-Viable Birth (Nefel)

  • 'First fruits of his strength' — even after non-viable births
  • A child born after nefelim (premature non-viable newborns) is still considered the firstborn
  • The possessive 'his' (ono) teaches this additional ruling
  • Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak's derivation

💍 Deriving: Husband Inherits Wife (But Not Vice Versa)

Baraita source: Numbers 27 — 'And if he has no daughter… and if he has no brothers, give his inheritance to his kinsman [she'ero]' (Num 27:9–11)
↓
Abaye's reading: 'She'ero' (his kinsman) can be read as 'his flesh' — referring to a wife, since husband and wife are 'one flesh' (Genesis 2:24). Thus a husband inherits his wife.
↓
Rava objects: 'Does a sharp knife cut verses?' You cannot rearrange and omit letters from the text. Abaye's method is forced.
↓
Rava's alternative: The verse says 'v'yarash otah' — 'and he shall inherit her' (feminine object). This teaches the husband inherits the wife's property.
↓
R. Yishmael's source: Numbers 36:8-9 — daughters who inherit must marry within their tribe 'so that no inheritance transfers from tribe to tribe.' If a husband did NOT inherit his wife, there would be no risk of transfer. Therefore he must inherit her.
↓
Proof from Prophets and Writings: Pinehas inherited land from his mother's family (Joshua 24:33); Yair inherited cities through his mother (I Chronicles 2:22) — confirming cross-tribal inheritance through marriage.
