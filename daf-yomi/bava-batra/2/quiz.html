5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
If partners in a shared courtyard build a partition from uncut stone (gavil), how much of his own land does each partner contribute?
A. 1 tefach
B. 2 tefachim
C. 3 tefachim
D. 4 tefachim
Correct: C — Uncut stone (gavil) walls are 6 tefachim thick. Since the wall sits on the property line, each partner contributes 3 tefachim from their respective portions.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
The Gemara argues that meḥitza cannot mean 'division.' What is the key linguistic proof?
A. The word meḥitza appears in a baraita about vineyards
B. If it meant division, the Tanna would have written 'who wished to divide,' not 'to make a meḥitza'
C. Numbers 31:43 uses a different Hebrew root
D. The Talmud Yerushalmi explicitly defines meḥitza as wall
Correct: B — The Gemara argues that had the Tanna intended 'division,' the natural expression would be לַחֲלֹק (to divide). Using 'make a meḥitza' specifically implies constructing a physical structure.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why can one garden co-owner compel the other to build a partition even where there is no local custom, unlike a courtyard?
A. Gardens are more valuable and require greater protection
B. Rav rules it is forbidden to stand in another person's field and observe the harvest
C. Garden walls are less expensive and thus a minor burden
D. The Torah explicitly mandates privacy in agricultural settings
Correct: B — Rabbi Abba citing Rav Huna citing Rav: It is forbidden to stand in another's field at harvest time to observe the yield. This heightened privacy concern distinguishes gardens from courtyards.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the Gemara's decisive proof that hezek re'iya (damage by sight) is legally actionable?
A. The Mishna (5a) requiring partners to rebuild a fallen wall
B. The Mishna (7b) about building a gatehouse for the courtyard
C. Rav Naḥman citing Shmuel: a roof adjoining a neighbor's courtyard requires a 4-cubit parapet
D. The Mishna (22a) requiring distance from a neighbor's windows
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman in Shmuel's name rules that one whose roof overlooks a neighbor's courtyard must build a parapet 4 cubits high — purely to prevent visual intrusion, proving hezek re'iya is recognized damage.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If one partner builds a partition unilaterally in a place where there is no local custom to do so, where must he build it?
A. On the property line, billing the neighbor later
B. Entirely within his own land, with a boundary mark on the outer face
C. He may not build at all without the neighbor's agreement
D. On the neighbor's land, since the neighbor benefits from privacy
Correct: B — Where no custom exists and only one partner wishes to build, he must withdraw into his own field entirely and place a boundary mark (סִימָן) on the outer face to document his sole ownership of the structure.
