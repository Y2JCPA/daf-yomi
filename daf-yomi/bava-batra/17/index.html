🌊 Two Worlds on One Daf

בבא בתרא יז
Daf 17 — Bava Batra The daf opens with aggadic heights: the Patriarchs transcended the evil inclination, six tzaddikim died by divine kiss, and four souls perished only from the primordial snake's legacy. It then descends to halakhic earth: the laws of pit-digging, distancing requirements from a neighbor's wall, and the pivotal Abaye–Rava dispute about excavating near a boundary where no pit yet exists.

🏆 Three Ascending Levels of Spiritual Protection

Evil Inclination (3)

  • Abraham — 'with everything' (Gen. 24:1)
  • Isaac — 'from everything' (Gen. 27:33)
  • Jacob — 'everything' (Gen. 33:11)
  • Total blessing = mastery over the yetzer hara
  • No foothold for the evil inclination

Angel of Death (6)

  • All three Patriarchs (above)
  • Moses — 'by the mouth of the Lord' (Deut. 34:5)
  • Aaron — same phrase implied
  • Miriam — verbal analogy on word 'there' (Num. 20:1)
  • All died by divine kiss (מיתת נשיקה)

Worm & Maggot (7)

  • All six from above, plus:
  • Benjamin son of Jacob
  • Never sinned in his life
  • Body remained fully incorruptible
  • Perfect purity left no opening for decay

💋 How Do We Know Miriam Died by Divine Kiss?

וַתָּמָת שָׁם מִרְיָם — גזירה שווה מ'שם' דמשה רבינו
Rabbi Elazar's Verbal Analogy — גזירה שווה Miriam's death is not written with the phrase 'by the mouth of the Lord' — yet the Sages ruled she died by divine kiss through a verbal analogy (גזירה שווה):

The word שָׁם ('there') appears in Miriam's death: 'And Miriam died there' (Num. 20:1), and also in Moses' death (Deut. 34:5).

Just as Moses died by the mouth of the Lord, so too Miriam's soul was taken gently — the Angel of Death never touched her.

⛏️ Mishna: Distancing Rules for Excavations and Hazards

Pit near a pit: Must distance 3 handbreadths from the neighbor's wall (not merely from the lip of the pit)
↓
Ditch, cave, water channel, launderer's pond: Same 3-handbreadth minimum applies to all excavations
↓
Solid residue, manure, salt, lime, rocks: Distance 3 handbreadths AND must plaster the surface to prevent seepage
↓
Seeds, plow furrows, urine: Must be distanced sufficiently so moisture does not damage the neighbor's wall foundations
↓
Mill: 3 handbreadths from the lower millstone = effectively 4 handbreadths from the upper stone above it
↓
Oven: 3 handbreadths from side wall; if in upper story, 4 handbreadths clearance to the floor below

⚖️ May One Dig Near a Boundary Where No Pit Yet Exists?

Abaye
One MAY dig close to the boundary. The neighbor's field is not currently designated for pits — no existing pit is being damaged. He cannot compel me to stop.
Rava
One may NOT dig close to the boundary. Even without an existing pit, we prevent foreseeable future damage. The neighbor may later dig a pit, and proximity will harm it.
Abaye
The Mishna's 3-handbreadth rule applies only when BOTH neighbors dig simultaneously — that's why it reads 'wall of his pit,' meaning both are digging at the same time.
Rava
Even Rabbi Yosei — who says each owner may do as he wishes on his land — agrees one cannot actively impair a neighbor's future pit. Proactive protection is the rule.
The Core Legal Question Is property damage law reactive (protect only existing harm) or proactive (prevent foreseeable future harm)? Rava holds the latter.
