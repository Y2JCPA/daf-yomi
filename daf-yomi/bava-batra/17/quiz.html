Bava Batra 17 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Over how many people did the Angel of Death have no sway, and who were the additions beyond the three Patriarchs?
A. 3 people — only the Patriarchs
B. 6 people — adding Moses, Aaron, and Miriam
C. 7 people — adding Benjamin son of Jacob
D. 4 people — adding Moses alone
Correct: B — The Gemara lists 6: Abraham, Isaac, Jacob, Moses, Aaron, and Miriam — all died by divine kiss (מיתת נשיקה), without the Angel of Death's dominion.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How did Rabbi Elazar prove that Miriam died by divine kiss, given that the phrase 'by the mouth of the Lord' does not appear at her death?
A. An explicit oral tradition from Moses
B. A verbal analogy (גזירה שווה) on the word 'there' (שם)
C. The Torah states it explicitly in Deuteronomy
D. Miriam is not counted among those who died by divine kiss
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar used a verbal analogy: the word 'there' (שם) appears in both Miriam's death (Num. 20:1) and Moses' death, linking hers to the same mode of dying 'by the mouth of the Lord.'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which four individuals died only because of the primordial snake's counsel, and not due to any personal sin?
A. Abraham, Isaac, Jacob, Moses
B. Benjamin, Amram, Jesse, Kilaab
C. Aaron, Miriam, Samuel, David
D. Adam, Noah, Shem, Eber
Correct: B — The Gemara identifies Benjamin son of Jacob, Amram (Moses' father), Jesse (David's father), and Kilaab (David's son) as dying solely because all humanity became mortal through Adam's sin.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The Mishna says one must distance a pit from a neighbor's wall. Abaye re-reads 'the wall' as referring to what specifically?
A. The outer perimeter wall of the neighbor's courtyard
B. The wall of the neighbor's house
C. The wall of the neighbor's own pit
D. Any shared wall between properties
Correct: C — Abaye clarifies that the Mishna means one must distance 3 handbreadths from 'the wall of his [neighbor's] pit' — i.e., the structural wall of the neighbor's existing pit, not a property boundary wall.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the Abaye–Rava dispute about digging near a boundary where no pit yet exists, what is Rava's halakhic ruling?
A. One may dig freely since no current damage exists
B. One may dig if he plasters the walls of his excavation
C. One may not dig, as we protect against foreseeable future damage
D. One may dig only with the neighbor's explicit consent
Correct: C — Rava rules that one may NOT dig close to the boundary even when the neighbor has no pit yet, because halakha requires proactive prevention of foreseeable future damage — not merely reacting to existing harm.
