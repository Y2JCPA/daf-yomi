QUESTION 1 OF 5
How did Elazar reconcile the two verses — 'by lot' (Num. 26:55) and 'by the pronouncement' (Num. 26:56) — in dividing the land?
A. He used the lottery for large tribes and the Urim VeTummim for small tribes
B. He first prophetically announced each tribe-region pairing, then drew lots that confirmed his announcement exactly
C. He alternated between methods on different days of the division
D. He used the Urim VeTummim only when two tribes disputed the same region
Correct: B — Elazar, filled with the Divine Spirit through the Urim VeTummim, first declared which tribe would receive which region. He then drew from both receptacles and the lots matched his prophecy — thus both methods (lot and pronouncement) operated simultaneously, reconciling the two verses.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda, who receives the 13th tribal portion in the messianic division of Eretz Yisrael?
A. The tribe of Levi, which received no portion in Joshua's time
B. Converts who join Israel in the messianic era
C. The king (Moshiach/David's line), as indicated in Ezekiel 45:7
D. The portion is divided equally among all 12 tribes as a bonus
Correct: C — Rav Ḥisda says the 13th portion is for the king, deriving this from Ezekiel 45:7: 'The prince shall have a portion on one side and on the other side of the holy portion and of the city's portion.' This extra portion acknowledges the king's special status.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rabbi Eliezer and Rabbi Yehoshua disagree about tribal compensation for unequal land. What are their respective positions?
A. R. Eliezer: land by boundary adjustment; R. Yehoshua: money payment
B. R. Eliezer: money payment; R. Yehoshua: land by boundary adjustment
C. Both say money, but disagree on who initiates the payment
D. R. Eliezer: agricultural produce; R. Yehoshua: livestock exchange
Correct: B — Rabbi Eliezer holds that tribes compensated with money — those receiving inferior land were paid by those receiving superior land. Rabbi Yehoshua holds they compensated with land itself by adjusting the boundaries between tribal territories to equalize quality.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The Gemara explains why Joshua's burial site has two different names ('Timnath Serah' and 'Timnath Heres'). What is the explanation?
A. They are two different locations where different parts of Joshua were buried
B. One name was used by Israelites, the other by Canaanites living nearby
C. Heres means 'sun' — commemorating the miracle where the sun stood still; a sun image was carved on his grave, giving rise to both names
D. The name changed after the Philistines conquered and renamed the city
Correct: C — The Gemara explains both names refer to the same place. 'Heres' (חֶרֶס) means sun, honoring the miracle Joshua performed when the sun stood still. A sun image was carved on his tomb to commemorate this. 'Timnath Serah' is a rearrangement of 'Timnath Heres' — same letters, same place.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Mar bar Rav Ashi, what does the Mishna's opening clause — 'both the son and the daughter are included in the laws of inheritance' — actually teach?
A. Sons and daughters always inherit in exactly equal shares with no distinction whatsoever
B. Both son and daughter can inherit the mother's property as part of their father's estate
C. A father may legally designate either a son or a daughter as sole heir
D. Sons and daughters are equally eligible to inherit from both the mother and the father
Correct: D — Mar bar Rav Ashi explains the Mishna teaches that sons and daughters stand equally — both are eligible to inherit from the mother's property AND from the father's property. This interpretation is preferred because it avoids redundancy with other mishnayot and creates a meaningful contrast with the following clause about the firstborn's special double portion.
