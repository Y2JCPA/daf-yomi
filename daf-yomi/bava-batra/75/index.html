🌅 Visions of the World to Come — and Back to Halacha

לֶעָתִיד לָבוֹא — In the future to come
Aggadah meets Halakha Daf 75 is dominated by sweeping eschatological aggadah: the Leviathan hunted by Gabriel, its flesh served as a feast for the righteous and its skin fashioned into sukkot graded by merit, Jerusalem's pinnacles of gem-stone disputed by angels, its gates enormous and its walls radiating glory, the city lifted three parasangs skyward, and canopies of seven layers for every tzaddik. The daf then pivots sharply back to the mishna's practical world, examining how a ship changes legal ownership.

🐋 The Leviathan — From Sea Monster to Sacred Feast

Gabriel hunts the Leviathan — but only with God's direct assistance could he succeed (Job 40:25: 'Can you draw out leviathan with a fish hook?')
↓
When hungry: Leviathan breathes fire, boiling all the waters of the deep — 'He makes the deep boil like a pot' (Job 41:23)
↓
When thirsty: Leviathan digs great furrows in the sea — 'He makes a path to shine after him' (Job 41:24). Rav Aḥa bar Yaakov: the sea never returns to its prior state after this.
↓
Future feast: God will prepare a banquet from its flesh for the righteous — 'The ḥabbarim will make a feast [yikhru] of him' (Job 40:30). Kera = feast; ḥabbarim = scholars
↓
Remainder of the flesh sold in the markets of Jerusalem — 'They will part him among the kena'anim' (merchants, Job 40:30)

⛺ Leviathan's Skin — Distributed According to Merit

God prepares a sukkah for the righteous from the skin of the Leviathan
'Can you fill his skin with sukkot [barbed irons]?' (Job 40:31)
↓
Fully deserving
A full Sukkah — complete covering of Leviathan skin
Moderately deserving
A necklace — 'necklaces about your neck' (Proverbs 1:9)
Minimally deserving
An amulet — a small piece of the skin worn as adornment
↓
Remaining Leviathan skin: spread on the walls of Jerusalem — its glory radiates from one end of the world to the other (Isaiah 60:3)

💎 What is 'Kadkhod'? — The Pinnacles and Gates of Future Jerusalem

Gabriel
The word kadkhod (Isaiah 54:12) — describing Jerusalem's pinnacles — refers to sardius stone
Michael
No — kadkhod refers to jasper stone; that is the material for the pinnacles
God
God declares: 'Let it be as both of your opinions' — the pinnacles will be fashioned from both sardius and jasper
R. Yoḥanan's Student
I doubted Rabbi Yoḥanan's teaching that gates would be carved from stones 30 by 30 cubits… until my ship passed and I saw ministering angels sawing exactly such stones!
Rebuke to the Student 'Had you not seen it, you would not have believed it. You mock the words of the Sages!' — The student's face then filled with wounds.

📐 Future Stature of the Righteous — Tannaitic Dispute

Rabbi Meir — 200 Cubits

  • Derives from komemiyyut (Leviticus 26:13): two heights (כ + ממ = 200 cubits)
  • Corresponds to the original height of Adam the first man
  • Adam spanned from earth to sky; the righteous will be similarly restored
  • Rabbi Ḥama bar Ḥanina: God fashioned ten canopies for Adam in Eden

Rabbi Yehuda — 100 Cubits

  • Corresponding to the height of the Sanctuary and its walls
  • Based on Psalms 144:12: 'daughters as corner-pillars carved after the fashion of the Sanctuary'
  • The righteous will stand tall as the pillars of the Temple
  • More bounded but still a miraculous restoration of stature

⛵ Acquiring a Ship — Rav vs. Shmuel

Rav
A ship is acquired once the buyer has pulled it and moved it even a minimal distance — any movement suffices for kinyan meshikha
Shmuel
A ship is acquired only when the buyer has pulled it the full length of the ship — a minimal nudge is insufficient
First Tanna (Baraita)
An animal is acquired when it lifts two legs — one foreleg and one hind leg — even before the others move
Shmuel (distinction)
I can agree with the first Tanna on animals — once two legs lift, the other legs are ready to follow. A ship has no such inherent readiness; full displacement is needed.
Rabbi Natan (Baraita) A ship is acquired by pulling (meshikha). Letters/documents inside the ship are acquired together with the ship itself.
