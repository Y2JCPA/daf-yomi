QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the unique legal feature of a matnat shechiv mera (deathbed gift)?
A. It requires two extra witnesses beyond a normal gift
B. It is valid without a formal act of acquisition (kinyan)
C. It can transfer only land, not movable property
D. It must be written in a document signed by a court
Correct: B — The essential rule of matnat shechiv mera is that verbal instruction alone suffices — no formal kinyan is required, unlike gifts made by a healthy person which require an act of acquisition.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Which sage derives the law of deathbed gifts from the verse about Hezekiah's illness?
A. Rav Zeira
B. Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
C. Rav Menashya bar Yirmeya
D. Rami bar Yeḥezkel
Correct: C — Rav Menashya bar Yirmeya derives the law from Isaiah 38:1, where Hezekiah on his deathbed was told by the prophet 'Instruct your household' (צַו לְבֵיתֶךָ) — treating this verbal deathbed instruction as a legally binding transfer.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava citing Rav Naḥman, what is the precise legal status of a deathbed gift?
A. Full Torah law, derived directly from the law of daughters inheriting
B. A mere custom with no binding legal force
C. Rabbinic law, but enforced with Torah-equivalent strength
D. Valid only within the land of Israel
Correct: C — Rava says in Rav Naḥman's name that deathbed gifts are valid only by rabbinic enactment — instituted to ease the dying person's mind. However, the Rabbis gave their enactment Torah-level force: even an heir cannot override a debt already transferred by deathbed gift.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
One of Ahithophel's three deathbed instructions to his sons was especially ironic given his life choices. Which instruction was it?
A. Do not lend money at interest
B. Do not sell your ancestral land
C. Do not rebel against the kingship of the House of David
D. Do not take more than one wife
Correct: C — Ahithophel — who had himself supported Absalom's rebellion against King David — died instructing his sons never to rebel against the Davidic dynasty. The Gemara preserves this as a model of deathbed wisdom, and his final instruction is the source Rami bar Yeḥezkel uses for deathbed gift law.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If a person on his deathbed says 'So-and-so shall reside in this house,' what is the halakhic ruling?
A. Fully valid — deathbed instructions cover all types of benefit
B. Valid only if the beneficiary is a direct family member
C. Invalid — intangible rights of use cannot be given as a deathbed gift
D. Valid for the duration of the giver's illness only
Correct: C — Rava rules in Rav Naḥman's name that granting intangible rights — the right to reside in a house or to eat a palm's fruit — is invalid even as a deathbed gift. Only property a healthy person could transfer via kinyan qualifies; intangible use-rights (דָּבָר שֶׁאֵין בּוֹ מַמָּשׁ) do not.
