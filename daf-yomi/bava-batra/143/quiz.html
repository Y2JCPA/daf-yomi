5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Hamnuna, what happens when someone says 'You and a donkey shall acquire my property'?
A. The person acquires half the property
B. The person acquires nothing — the entire gift is void
C. The donkey's owner acquires the property
D. The gift reverts to the giver's heirs
Correct: B — Rav Hamnuna holds that since part of the transfer is directed at a donkey (which cannot acquire property), the entire transaction is void — the ineligible recipient taints the whole.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Yosei rule that teruma separated from an entire cucumber (including the bitter inner part) is valid?
A. The bitter inner part is not considered part of the cucumber for teruma purposes
B. By Torah law, even inferior produce separated as teruma is valid teruma
C. One must separate teruma only from the outer skin of vegetables
D. Cucumbers are exempt from teruma by rabbinic decree
Correct: B — Rabbi Ile'a derives from the verse that teruma separated from inferior produce is Toraically valid. The bitter part counts as part of the teruma even though rabbinically one should re-separate from superior produce.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When a man says 'My property is given to you (his wife) and your sons,' how does Rav Yosef rule on the wife's share?
A. She receives nothing — only blood relatives may inherit
B. She receives one-third, the sons dividing the rest equally
C. She receives half the property
D. She shares equally with however many sons there are
Correct: C — Rav Yosef rules the wife acquires half, drawing on the shewbread precedent where Aaron was named separately to receive a larger portion. The halakha follows Rav Yosef in this matter.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Abaye's proof from Tanakh that one son can be referred to using the plural 'sons' (בָּנַי)?
A. Reuben's sons are listed as a plural in Numbers
B. 'The sons of Dan: Hushim' — Dan had one son yet the verse says 'sons'
C. Jacob calls all twelve tribes 'my sons' even when addressing one
D. The word 'sons' in Hebrew always implies at least two by grammar
Correct: B — Abaye cites Genesis 46:23: 'The sons of Dan: Hushim' — Dan had only one son Hushim, yet the text uses the plural form 'sons,' proving a single son can be called by the plural.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Mishna, when adult sons enhance shared inherited property WITHOUT declaring upfront they won't support the minors, where does the profit go?
A. The adult sons keep all profit as compensation for their labor
B. The profit is distributed only to the minor sons
C. The profit goes to the middle — shared equally among all heirs including minors
D. The profit is held in trust until the minors come of age
Correct: C — Unless the adult sons explicitly declared from the outset that they would not support the minors from common funds, enhancement profit goes to the middle — shared by all heirs. Their labor benefited the common estate and all heirs share in it.
