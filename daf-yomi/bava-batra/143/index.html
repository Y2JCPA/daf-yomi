🫏 The Donkey Acquisition Principle

"Acquire as a donkey does"
said to a person
↓
Person acquires nothing — a donkey cannot acquire, so the instruction is meaningless
↓
"You and a donkey shall both acquire"
said to a person
↓
Rav Hamnuna
Acquires nothing — part goes to what cannot acquire, so the entire transfer is void

🫏 Rav Avya's Objection to Rav Hamnuna

Rav Hamnuna
If one says 'You and a donkey shall acquire my property,' he said nothing — since the donkey cannot acquire, the entire gift is void.
Rav Avya
Objection from Kiddushin 50b! If one betroths two sisters simultaneously, the sisters are not betrothed — but the unrelated women ARE betrothed. Yet this is analogous to 'you and a donkey,' and yet here the valid part does take effect!
Rav Ashi
We already resolved that mishna — it speaks of betrothing the women sequentially, not simultaneously. The sisters were betrothed first (forbidden), voiding the attempt; the unrelated women come after. It is not truly analogous.
Rav Mordekhai
This is why I saw Rav Huna bar Avya in a dream — because you were about to raise this very objection to me!
Resolution The Kiddushin mishna involves sequential betrothal, not simultaneous — the sisters-case is distinguishable from the donkey principle, and Rav Hamnuna's ruling stands.

🥒 Bitter Cucumber & Teruma Validity

אין במלפפון מר אלא תוכו — Nothing bitter in the cucumber except its inner part
Rabbi Yosei + Rabbi Ile'a Rabbi Yosei: Nothing in the cucumber is bitter except its inner part. If one separates an entire cucumber as teruma, does the bitter part (unfit for teruma) void the whole separation — like 'you and a donkey'?

Answer: No. By Torah law, even inferior produce separated as teruma is valid teruma. The bitter inner part counts. Rabbinically one must re-separate from superior produce, but the original separation stands.

Rabbi Ile'a's source: The verse on teruma teaches that one who separates from inferior for superior has given valid teruma.

🎁 'My Property to You (Wife) and Your Sons' — How to Divide?

Rav Yosef's Ruling ✓

  • Wife acquires half the property
  • Sons together share the remaining half
  • Precedent: shewbread — Aaron named separately to receive more than other priests
  • Shmuel in Neharde'a and Rabbi Ami in Tiberias similarly gave named individual half
  • ⚖️ Halakha follows Rav Yosef in this matter

Abaye's Challenge

  • Shewbread analogy is flawed — Aaron was already eligible; he was named to take more
  • The wife may be named only to clarify she receives something, not to guarantee half
  • Perhaps she shares equally with the sons rather than taking a guaranteed half

Tax Case Counter-Example

  • Prominent citizens sometimes joined ordinary residents in a tax assessment
  • This did not mean prominent citizens bore half — they were included for special reasons
  • Gemara rejects: tax writs were originally on councilmen alone; prominent citizens joined secondarily — not comparable

👨‍👧‍👦 Mishna: Adult & Minor Heirs — Enhancement of Property

Situation: Father dies leaving both adult sons and minor sons with a shared undivided estate
↓
Adult sons invest labor or funds to enhance the shared property (e.g., improve a field or business)
↓
Default rule: All enhancement profit goes to the middle — shared equally among all heirs, including minors who contributed nothing
↓
Exception: If from the outset the adult sons declared 'We will not care for the minor sons from common funds,' they keep all enhancement profits for themselves
↓
Rav Ḥaviva citing Rava: The default rule applies only when enhancement was funded from the common estate. If enhanced solely through personal labor unconnected to estate funds, different rules apply.
