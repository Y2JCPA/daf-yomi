👴 Can the Grandson Repossess His Grandfather's Land?

Tanna
If a son sold his father's property during the father's lifetime, and then the son died, the son's son repossesses from the buyers. This is a difficult monetary halakha!
Gemara
What is the difficulty? The grandson can say: I come to repossess on the basis of my grandfather's right — I inherit directly from him, skipping my father!
Gemara
Proof: 'In the place of his fathers shall be his sons' (Ps. 45:17) — the grandson stands in the father's stead in the inheritance chain.
Conclusion
The verse is merely a blessing, not a legal ruling. The difficulty stands: the grandfather never protested the sale himself, so how does the grandson gain standing to repossess?
Core Principle A son who sells property he has not yet received has sold nothing. The grandfather's ownership passes intact to the grandson when the son predeceases.

📜 Disqualified Witness — Two Scenarios

A witness signs a document, then is later disqualified
↓
Became a Robber
Defense available: Court may have already 'presumed' the signature valid before disqualification — others can testify it is genuine
Became Son-in-Law
Defense blocked: Rav Yosef b. Minyumi citing Rav Naḥman — presumption of signature only applies to monetary disqualification, not familial relatedness
↓
Gemara rejects son-in-law as 'truly difficult': perhaps it is a King's edict (divine decree) that a relative is disqualified, unrelated to epistemic grounds
↓
All witness-based candidates ultimately rejected — the grandson case remains the sole genuine difficulty

☠️ Does a Son Inherit From His Mother While In the Grave?

Hypothetical: YES

  • Son dies first, then mother dies shortly after
  • Son 'inherits' mother's estate momentarily while in the grave
  • That inheritance then passes to his paternal brothers
  • Result: mother's wealth diverted from her family to the son's paternal line
  • Would create complex cross-tribal inheritance chains

Ruling: NO (Abaye)

  • The word וְעָבְרָה ('transfer') in Numbers 36 governs inheritance logic
  • Just as the Torah blocks inter-tribal transfer of land, so too inheritance cannot pass through a dead intermediary
  • Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel both agree: house-collapse case — son's heirs and mother's heirs divide the estate
  • No posthumous inheritance pathway is created

⚖️ Rava's Principle: Burden of Proof and Admission

General Rule (המוציא מחבירו עליו הראיה): The one seeking to extract property from another bears the burden of proof
↓
The party currently in possession (מוחזק) is presumed rightful owner until proven otherwise
↓
Exception — Party's Own Words: When a party's language constitutes an admission against interest, the burden shifts
↓
Bar Sisin case: 'All I own of bar Sisin' implies the seller acknowledged that estate — his own words placed burden on himself
↓
House case: Occupant's admission that he 'purchased' the house obligates him to prove the purchase before the presumption of possession protects him
