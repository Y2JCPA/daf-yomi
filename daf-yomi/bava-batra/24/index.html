⚖️ The Great Majority vs. Proximity Debate

Abaye
Proof from blood in the corridor: we follow the majority (uterus) over proximity (nearby cervical wound). This shows we always follow rov!
Rava
That case has both majority AND frequency. No one disputes following majority when frequency is also present — your proof proves nothing about majority alone!
Rava
From Rabbi Ḥiyya's ruling on corridor blood, derive three things: (1) majority beats proximity, (2) this rule is d'oraita, (3) Rabbi Zeira's ruling on the lone woman applies.
Core Principle Majority + Frequency together → everyone follows majority (undisputed). Majority alone → Rava says follow majority; others may dispute. Proximity alone → generally follow proximity.

🍷 Barrel of Wine in the River — Rav vs. Shmuel

Rav
Barrel found floating opposite a town with a Jewish majority → wine is permitted. We follow the majority of residents of the nearest town.
Shmuel
Wine is forbidden. The river currents in that region flow from Dekira, a predominantly gentile settlement — proximity to the actual source overrides the nearby town's majority.
Gemara
Both agree with Rabbi Ḥanina (follow majority in principle). Their dispute: does the barrel's upriver source (Dekira) override the majority of Jews in the nearest downstream town?
Practical Ruling Ravina later permitted a barrel hidden in a vineyard with orla grapes — reasoning that thieves would not hide stolen goods in the very vineyard from which they stole.

🌳 Tree Distancing Laws from the City

Standard Tree

  • Distance: 25 cubits from the city
  • Reason (Ulla): preserves the beauty of the city
  • Branches obscure city walls unattractively
  • Cannot derive from open-area rules (machloket R. Elazar / Rabbis)

Carob or Sycamore

  • Distance: 50 cubits from the city
  • Reason: exceptionally large, wide branches
  • Far greater visual and physical obstruction
  • Double the standard tree requirement

Barren Tree (Abba Shaul)

  • Distance: 50 cubits (Abba Shaul's opinion)
  • Barren trees yield no fruit — pure detriment
  • Standard Sages: still only 25 cubits
  • Abba Shaul: fruitlessness warrants stricter rule

🏙️ City vs. Tree: Who Came First?

Tree preceded city: Owner must cut it down; city pays full compensation — the city caused the harm by expanding toward the tree
↓
City preceded tree: Owner must cut it down; city pays nothing — the owner planted unlawfully within the protected zone
↓
Uncertain who came first: Owner must cut it down; city pays nothing — burden of proof falls on the claimant (המוציא מחבירו עליו הראיה)
↓
Why different from cistern case (Daf 25b)? With a cistern, if tree preceded it the tree owner wins — so in doubt, the tree stays. Here, city always wins in doubt because damage to public is distinct.
↓
Rav Kahana's aphorism: A pot belonging to partners is neither hot nor cold — public property has no single protector, so the tree owner cannot demand compensation from the city collectively.
