⚖️ Dovecotes, Damage & Disputed Principles

Amud A
Bloodletters sit under palm trees → crows eat blood → damage dates. No acquired privilege for damaging activities. Dovecote must be 50 cubits from city and owner must have 50 cubits of land in each direction.
Amud B
Found dove chick within 50 cubits → belongs to dovecote owner. Proximity overrides majority (Rabbi Ḥanina). Rabbi Yirmeya asks about a chick straddling the boundary — and gets expelled from the beit midrash.
Core Principle אין חזקה לנזקין — There is no acquired privilege of use in cases of damage

🩸 Bloodletters Under the Palm Trees

Rav Yosef
Bloodletters — remove these crows from here! You sit beneath my palms, crows eat your blood, then fly up and damage my dates!
Abaye
But they have established an acquired privilege (חזקה) to use that particular spot — they have been working there for a long time without objection!
Rav Yosef
Doesn't Rav Naḥman say in the name of Rabba bar Avuh: אין חזקה לנזקין — There is no acquired privilege of use in cases of damage!
Ruling One can never acquire the right to damage another's property — no matter how many years the damaging activity has gone unchallenged

🕊️ Dovecote Distance Requirements

Where May a Dovecote (שובך) Be Established?
↓
From the City
50 cubits minimum distance — prevents doves from eating seeds and grain in town
Within One's Own Property
50 cubits in each direction owned by the dovecote builder — cannot encroach on neighbors' land
↓
Reason (Abaye): Doves' stomachs fill after 50 cubits — they stop eating at that distance, ending the damage risk

⚖️ Abaye vs. Rava: Resolving the 50-Cubit Contradiction

Abaye

  • Contradiction: If doves only travel 50 cubits, why must traps be 30 ris from settled areas?
  • Doves can fly great distances — hence traps must be 30 ris away to avoid catching others' birds
  • But as they eat along the way, their stomachs fill after 50 cubits
  • The two laws address different concerns: flying range (traps) vs. eating range (damage to crops)

Rava

  • The 30-ris trap prohibition refers to a settled area of dovecotes — many dovecotes clustered together
  • Combined populations of many dovecotes fly far together, so traps must be kept 30 ris away
  • A single dovecote's doves reliably reach only 50 cubits
  • The two laws address different situations: single dovecote vs. a cluster of many

ועל דא אפקוהו לרבי ירמיה מבי מדרשא
🦵 The Question That Got Him Expelled Rabbi Yirmeya asked: What if one leg of the chick was within 50 cubits of the dovecote, and the other leg was beyond 50 cubits — what is the halakha? The Sages found this question so far-fetched and disruptive to reasoned legal discussion that they removed him from the study hall (בי מדרשא). His question exposes the tension inherent in all legal line-drawing: any precise boundary will generate absurd edge cases. The Talmud acknowledges this — but won't let the edge case unravel the rule.
