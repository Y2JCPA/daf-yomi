Bava Batra 23 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Rav Yosef tells bloodletters to move away from his palm trees. What principle does he invoke to override their claimed right to stay?
A. A landowner may always expel workers from his property without cause
B. אין חזקה לנזקין — there is no acquired privilege of use in cases of damage
C. The bloodletters never formally acquired a right to use the spot
D. Damage to food-producing trees overrides all other considerations
Correct: B — Rav Yosef cites Rav Naḥman's ruling (in the name of Rabba bar Avuh): אין חזקה לנזקין — no length of prior undisturbed use can grant the right to continue causing damage.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Abaye explain why traps must be 30 ris from settled areas yet dovecotes need only be 50 cubits from the city?
A. The trap rule applies only to wild birds, not domesticated doves
B. Doves fly far but eat as they go, filling their stomachs within 50 cubits — beyond that they fly but don't damage crops
C. The 30-ris rule is a stringency added by later authorities and is not reconcilable
D. Traps catch birds from multiple cities, while dovecotes only affect one city
Correct: B — Abaye distinguishes flying range from eating range: doves can fly 30 ris, but their stomachs fill after 50 cubits of eating, so the actual damage to crops ends at 50 cubits.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ḥanina, when a found item is near a known source of that item, which principle takes precedence?
A. Majority (רוב) always overrides proximity
B. The item is considered ownerless until claimed in court
C. Proximity (קרוב) overrides majority
D. Both principles apply equally and the item must be split
Correct: C — Rabbi Ḥanina establishes that proximity overrides majority — even if the majority of similar items in the area belong to others, the item is attributed to the nearby source.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
A dove chick is found exactly equidistant between two dovecotes. What does the Mishna rule?
A. It goes to whichever owner arrives first to claim it
B. It is given to the finder, since neither owner has a clear claim
C. It is split between the owners of both dovecotes
D. It goes to the owner of the larger dovecote
Correct: C — When the chick is equidistant, neither dovecote has a stronger proximity claim, so it is divided between the two dovecote owners.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What consequence did Rabbi Yirmeya face for asking about a dove chick with one leg inside and one leg outside the 50-cubit boundary?
A. His question was praised and recorded as a model of careful legal thinking
B. He was fined for wasting the court's time
C. He was removed from the study hall (בי מדרשא)
D. He was required to bring a korban for causing confusion
Correct: C — The Gemara records that it was for this far-fetched question that Rabbi Yirmeya was expelled from the beit midrash — his scenario was seen as undermining legal reasoning rather than refining it.
