Bava Batra 49 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Mar bar Rav Ashi, what is the key difference between a shtar amana claim and a modaa claim made by witnesses after signing a document?
A. Both claims are credible and can nullify the document
B. Neither claim is credible — oral testimony never defeats a written document
C. A shtar amana claim is not credible, but a modaa claim is credible
D. A modaa claim is not credible, but a shtar amana claim is credible
Correct: C — Mar bar Rav Ashi agrees that a shtar amana claim is not credible (oral cannot undo written), but holds a modaa is a formal legal instrument declared in advance — not mere speech — and therefore credible.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why can a husband NOT establish a chazaka on his wife's field by working and profiting from it for three years?
A. Three years is insufficient for chazaka on marital property
B. His use is presumed to stem from his legal marital right to profits (usufruct), not from purchase
C. Women's fields are exempt from the laws of chazaka entirely
D. He must first formally declare his intent to acquire the field
Correct: B — A husband has a legal right to profit from his wife's property by virtue of marriage (usufruct). His use is therefore legally accounted for and cannot constitute evidence of purchase.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
The school of Rabbi Yannai rules that a husband's declaration 'I have no dealings with your property' is only legally effective if written at what time?
A. At any point during the marriage, as long as it is written
B. During the first year of marriage, before habits are established
C. After the wife inherits property, not before
D. During betrothal (erusin), before the marriage and before his rights vest
Correct: D — Rabbi Yannai's school rules the declaration only works if made during betrothal — at that point he has no existing rights, so he is creating a valid condition. After marriage, his rights have already vested and cannot be surrendered unilaterally.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What ruling does Rav (transmitted by Rav Huna) give regarding a woman and her marital maintenance?
A. A woman can never waive her right to maintenance under any circumstances
B. A woman may tell her husband she will not accept maintenance and will not give him her earnings
C. A husband may unilaterally cancel maintenance obligations at will
D. Maintenance waivers require approval from a rabbinical court to be valid
Correct: B — Rav rules a woman may opt out of the default arrangement — forgoing maintenance in exchange for retaining her own earnings. This is valid because she is waiving a future right, not a vested one.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When a woman sells her property and later claims she only did so to please her husband, what is the legal concern raised by the Gemara (drawing on Gittin 55b)?
A. Women are presumed incapable of making valid property sales
B. The sale may have lacked genuine consent due to marital pressure, allowing her to retroactively void it
C. A husband must always co-sign his wife's property sales for them to be valid
D. Sales made for non-financial reasons automatically violate chazaka rules
Correct: B — The Gemara recognizes that a woman selling property merely to appease her husband may not have acted with genuine free will. Such sales can be challenged on the grounds that marital social pressure undermined true consent.
