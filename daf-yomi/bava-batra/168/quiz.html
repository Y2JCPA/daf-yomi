QUESTION 1 OF 5
Who pays the scribe's wages for writing a bill of sale (שטר מכר)?
A. The seller, who initiates the transaction
B. The court, which oversees all property transfers
C. The purchaser, who benefits from holding the deed
D. Both parties split the cost equally
Correct: C — The purchaser pays the scribe for a bill of sale, since the buyer benefits from holding proof of ownership. This follows the general principle: whoever benefits from a document pays the scribe's wages.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Abaye recommend a Torah scholar do when going to betroth a woman?
A. Bring two Torah scholars as reliable witnesses
B. Arrange the betrothal through an official matchmaker
C. Demand a written guarantee from the bride's family
D. Bring an ignoramus (am ha'aretz) to identify her
Correct: D — Abaye advises bringing an am ha'aretz who is worldly enough to recognize the woman and detect a substitution fraud. Torah scholars may be naive in practical matters, so a worldly companion provides necessary protection.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the final halakhic ruling regarding asmakhta (conditional acquisition)?
A. Asmakhta is binding, following Rabbi Yosei
B. Asmakhta is binding only when a trustee holds the document
C. The dispute between Rabbi Yosei and Rabbi Yehuda remains unresolved
D. Asmakhta is NOT binding — conditional acquisition lacks full intent
Correct: D — The Gemara concludes that the halakha does not follow Rabbi Yosei. An asmakhta is not binding because valid acquisition requires full, unconditional intent, which is absent in a conditional penalty arrangement.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Abaye instruct his scribe to write on paper, erase it, and then write the actual document on top?
A. Erased parchment accepts ink more cleanly
B. To make the document look older and more authoritative
C. To circumvent the rule against writing two deeds for one field
D. The court required all documents to be written on pre-erased surfaces
Correct: C — Rav Safra rules one may not write two deeds for the same field. By writing the document over an erased surface, Abaye's solution technically produced a replacement document rather than a duplicate, working around this prohibition for landowners whose deeds were stolen by Arab robbers.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the required text of a ratification document for an erased promissory note?
A. Three judges write: 'We sat as three, witnesses appeared before us and testified to this note's contents and signatories'
B. Two witnesses sign confirming they personally saw the original debt transaction
C. The debtor and creditor jointly execute a new acknowledgment before a rabbi
D. The original witnesses rewrite the entire promissory note from memory
Correct: A — A court of three judges writes a formal ratification stating they convened, witnesses appeared and testified about the note's contents, date, and original signatories. This court document serves as a valid replacement for the damaged original.
