5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye, what is Rav's requirement before witnesses may sign a document written over an erasure?
A. A court of three judges must be present to supervise the signing
B. The parchment must have been erased in the witnesses' presence, before new text was written
C. The witnesses must record the exact date on which the erasure occurred
D. Two independent pairs of witnesses are required — one for the erasure and one for the new document
Correct: B — Abaye explains that Rav requires the witnesses to have personally seen the blank parchment after the old writing was erased and before the new text was written — they serve as guarantors that no prior forgery occurred.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Yirmeya reject the Gemara's suggestion to verify documents by retaining them an extra day to compare old and new erasures?
A. Ink fades too rapidly to allow comparison after even one day
B. Courts lack jurisdiction to hold private documents overnight
C. We are concerned for an erring court — intricate procedures increase the risk of judicial mistake
D. Both erasures become visually identical after aging one day, making comparison pointless
Correct: C — Rabbi Yirmeya's reasoning is that overly elaborate verification methods — even clever ones — introduce so many procedural steps that courts are more likely to err than if simpler rules were applied consistently.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's practical objection to Rabbi Ḥanina ben Gamliel's ruling about tied documents?
A. Tied documents with internal signatures cannot legally be converted into ordinary documents
B. A creditor could open a tied document after the debtor repaid early and falsely claim payment is still owed, exploiting the different date conventions
C. Tied documents are valid only in Eretz Yisrael but not in Babylonian courts
D. The witnesses' signatures inside a tied document cannot be verified by examining judges
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi warns that if a debtor repays early and loses his receipt, a dishonest creditor could open the tied document later, point to its different date convention, and fraudulently claim the repayment date had not yet arrived.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
A document bears the date: 'In the year of Archon so-and-so' with no year of reign specified. How does the Gemara resolve this?
A. The document is invalid because its date is fatally ambiguous
B. Investigate when this Archon first began his reign and assume the document was written in year one
C. Assume the document is dated to the last recorded year of the Archon's reign
D. The word 'Archon' is reread as the Aramaic 'arikh,' meaning his reign had already been long
Correct: B — Rabbi Ḥanina rules that we investigate the start of the Archon's reign and assume the document dates to year one. The Gemara further distinguishes: if he was deposed and reinstated, he would be called 'Archon Digon,' marking the second reign.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which three sins does Rav say no person escapes committing every single day?
A. Lying, stealing, and taking God's name in vain
B. Idolatrous thoughts, sexual immorality, and bloodshed
C. Sinful thoughts, deliberation (distraction) in prayer, and malicious speech
D. Forbidden foods, Shabbat desecration, and dishonest business dealings
Correct: C — Rav lists הרהור עבירה (sinful thoughts), עיון תפילה (distraction or self-interest in prayer), and לשון הרע (malicious speech) as daily subtle transgressions that no person fully avoids — illustrated by Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's unintended criticism of the document's scribe.
