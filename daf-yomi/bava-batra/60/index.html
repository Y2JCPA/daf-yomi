🚪 Source for Privacy Laws — And How to Apply Them

Gemara
From where is it derived that one may not open a door opposite another door or a window opposite a window in a shared courtyard?
Rabbi Yoḥanan
From the verse: 'Balaam lifted his eyes and saw Israel dwelling tribe by tribe' — he saw that their tent openings did not face each other, and he blessed them for it.
Rami bar Ḥama
'He may not enlarge a small entrance' means: if it was 4 cubits he cannot make it 8, because two people could then stand there without blocking each other, greatly increasing oversight into the courtyard.
Resolution
No — 'small' means less than 4 cubits. He may not make it a standard 4-cubit opening at all. The concern is any increase in the ability to observe one's partner's space.
🔑 Key Verse במדבר כד:ב — 'וַיִּשָּׂא בִלְעָם אֶת עֵינָיו וַיַּרְא אֶת יִשְׂרָאֵל שֹׁכֵן לִשְׁבָטָיו' — Balaam praised Israel's privacy-conscious arrangement of tents facing away from each other.

🌳 Rabbi Yannai's Tree — A Lesson in Judicial Integrity

Situation: Rabbi Yannai had a tree leaning into the public domain — as did a certain man
↓
The public demanded the man cut his tree; he came to Rabbi Yannai for support
↓
Rabbi Yannai said: 'Go now, come back tomorrow' — deliberately delaying
↓
That night: Rabbi Yannai secretly sent someone to cut down his OWN tree
↓
Next day: Yannai told the man: 'Go check — if mine is cut down, go cut yours down too'
↓
Principle: A judge must remove his own violation before ruling on another's. Integrity demands self-correction first — physician, heal thyself.

🏛️ Temple Mourning: Restrictions and Permitted Adjustments

❌ What Is Prohibited

  • Plastering a house with smooth, lustrous plaster
  • Tiling floors or painting decorative images
  • Eating meat and drinking wine (by right — though not formally decreed)
  • Mourning so excessively that daily life becomes impossible

✅ Permitted with a Small Reminder

  • Plastering — if sand or straw is mixed in to dull its shine
  • Full festive meal — but leave out one small dish
  • Woman's full cosmetic routine — but leave one small area bare
  • Wedding celebrations — place ashes on the bridegroom's head

📐 Size of the Unplastered Patch

  • One cubit by one cubit (אמה על אמה)
  • Positioned opposite the entrance — most visible spot
  • Symbolizes ongoing mourning for the Destruction
  • Even in a beautiful home, the memory remains

⚖️ Rabbi Yehoshua vs. the Abstainers

Abstainers
Since the Temple is destroyed, we should decree upon ourselves not to eat meat, not to drink wine, not to marry — let us mourn completely!
Rabbi Yehoshua
Then we should not eat bread either — meal-offerings have ceased. And not drink water — the water libations are gone. And not eat produce — first-fruits are no more!
Abstainers
You are correct… one cannot survive without bread and water. Those prohibitions are impossible.
Rabbi Yehoshua
My children: not to mourn at all is impossible — the decree was issued. But to mourn excessively is also impossible — we do not impose decrees the community cannot bear. Mourn a little, in every domain of life.
⚖️ Talmudic Principle הַנַּח לָהֶם לְיִשְׂרָאֵל — 'Leave the Jews alone' — better they sin unwittingly than wittingly. Do not impose impossible decrees.
