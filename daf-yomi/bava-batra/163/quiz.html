Bava Batra 163 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
How does Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak prove that the two invalidating lines are measured with their interlinear spaces (not bare lines alone)?
A. He cites an explicit ruling from Rabbi Shabbtai
B. A bare line without its space cannot be written upon — such a measurement would be meaningless
C. The baraita uses the word 'space' directly
D. He reasons from the size of a lamed and final khaf
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak argues: what purpose is a bare line without its space? You cannot write in it. Therefore the baraita must mean lines measured together with their interlinear spaces.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Ulla (transmitted by Rav Ḥiyya bar Ami), what defines the required interlinear space?
A. Enough to write the letter dalet
B. Enough to write the name Barukh ben Levi on one line
C. Enough for a lamed ascending above the upper line and a final khaf descending below the lower
D. Enough for two standard letters side by side
Correct: C — Ulla holds the space must accommodate a lamed (tall ascender) from the upper line and a final khaf (long descender) from the lower line — the maximum vertical extension of Hebrew script.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does inking in the blank space FAIL to fix the problem when the gap is between the document text and the witnesses' signatures?
A. Ink dissolves over time, leaving the gap vulnerable again
B. The court refuses to ratify documents with inked gaps
C. People will say the witnesses signed only on the inking, implying the text above was added afterward
D. Inking is only valid if done by the original scribe
Correct: C — The concern is that observers will say the witnesses signed only on the ink marks — raising suspicion that the document text above was written after the witnesses signed, i.e., forged.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the key difference between Rav and Rabbi Yoḥanan regarding a ONE-line gap between witnesses' signatures and the court's ratification?
A. Both hold it is valid; they disagree only about remedy
B. Rav holds it is invalid; Rabbi Yoḥanan holds it is valid
C. Rav holds it is valid; Rabbi Yoḥanan holds it is invalid
D. Both hold it is invalid for different reasons
Correct: C — Rav considers a one-line gap between witnesses and court ratification valid. Rabbi Yoḥanan disagrees — he worries someone could excise the original document text and write a new, short document that fits above the signatures on that one blank line.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When a court ratifies a document whose text AND witnesses' signatures are both written over an erasure, what is the basis of the ratification according to Rav?
A. The court accepts it on the parties' word alone
B. The court relies solely on recognizing the witnesses' signatures
C. The court relies on both recognizing the witnesses' signatures and the consistent handwriting of the scribe throughout
D. The court requires the witnesses to reaffirm their signatures in person
Correct: C — Rav holds the court ratifies by verifying both the witnesses' signatures and the scribe's consistent handwriting throughout the document — comprehensive authentication that guards against post-signing tampering even on an erasure.
