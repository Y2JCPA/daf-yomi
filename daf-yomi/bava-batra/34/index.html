🪙 The Snatched Metal: Rabbi Abba vs. the Rabbis

Rabbis before Abaye
How should the judges rule here? There are no two witnesses who saw the metal snatched — so we cannot simply order payment. But we also cannot ignore what one witness saw.
Rabbi Abba
The snatcher is liable to take an oath but is unable to take that oath because one witness contradicts him. The rule is: חייב שבועה ואינו יכול לישבע — חייב לשלם. He must pay.
Abaye
Are these two cases truly comparable? In Rabbi Abba's case, the witness actively undermines the snatcher. If a second contrary witness appeared, the snatcher would go free — proof the first witness is working against him. That is what triggers the oath-displacement rule.
Key Insight The witness must be positioned against the possessor — not merely supplementing another party's claim — for Rabbi Abba's oath-displacement rule to apply.

🤝 The Oath-Liability Principle

חייב שבועה ואינו יכול לישבע — חייב לשלם
Rabbi Abba's Rule חייב שבועה ואינו יכול לישבע — חייב לשלם

One who is obligated to swear in order to defend their position, but is prevented from swearing due to witness testimony against them, is instead obligated to pay.

Normally one witness creates an oath requirement on the opposing party. But when the very person who would swear is the subject of that testimony, the oath is disqualified — leaving them with no valid defense and full liability.

🌾 Abaye's Distinction: Two Types of One-Witness Cases

Rabbi Abba's Case (Metal)

  • One witness testifies against the possessor
  • Witness undermines the snatcher's position
  • A second contrary witness would free the snatcher
  • Witness stands in opposition to claimant
  • Result: oath displaced → liable to pay

The Land/Produce Case

  • One witness testifies to two years of land use
  • Witness supports the claimant's position
  • A second contrary witness would harm the claimant
  • Witness stands in alignment with claimant
  • Result: different legal dynamic — Rabbi Abba's rule does not apply
