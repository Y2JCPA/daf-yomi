5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Abba, what is the ruling for someone who is liable to take an oath but cannot take it?
A. They are fully exempt from liability
B. They are liable to pay instead of swearing
C. The case is dismissed for insufficient evidence
D. They must bring two witnesses to replace the oath
Correct: B — Rabbi Abba rules: חייב שבועה ואינו יכול לישבע — חייב לשלם. The inability to fulfill the oath obligation converts it into a monetary liability.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the crucial distinction Abaye draws between Rabbi Abba's metal case and the land/produce case?
A. Rabbi Abba's case involves movable property while the other involves land
B. In Rabbi Abba's case the witness undermines the possessor; in the other the witness supports the claimant
C. Rabbi Abba's case involves two witnesses while the other has only one
D. The land case involves a greater monetary amount requiring stricter proof
Correct: B — Abaye's key point: in Rabbi Abba's case the single witness actively works against the snatcher (a second contrary witness would free him), whereas in the land case the witness supports the claimant — making them legally incomparable.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the boat dispute on Amud B, what did the court do when one party made a formal request?
A. Divided the boat's value equally between both claimants
B. Awarded the boat to the first claimant to appear in court
C. Seized the boat while the requesting party searched for witnesses
D. Ordered both parties to take simultaneous oaths
Correct: C — The court seized the boat to preserve the status quo and prevent self-help while the requesting party attempted to find witnesses to substantiate their ownership claim.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does כל דאלים גבר mean in the context of these property disputes?
A. The court divides the contested property equally
B. The ruling is deferred until a later rabbinic court convenes
C. Whoever is the more forceful or stronger party takes possession
D. The property reverts to whoever held it most recently
Correct: C — כל דאלים גבר — literally 'whoever is stronger prevails' — is the court's reluctant ruling when it has no legal basis to prefer one ownership claim over another and no evidence can break the deadlock.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the ancestral property dispute on Amud B, why did the court apply כל דאלים גבר?
A. Both parties refused to appear before the court
B. Neither party could produce any evidence; both claimed identical ancestral inheritance
C. The property had already been seized by one party before the case began
D. One party had a witness who was disqualified on procedural grounds
Correct: B — Both parties made symmetric claims — 'it belonged to my ancestors and I inherited it' — with no witnesses, documents, or other evidence to differentiate their claims, leaving the court with no halakhic tool except כל דאלים גבר.
