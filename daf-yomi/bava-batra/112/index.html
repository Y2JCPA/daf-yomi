⚖️ Does a Husband Inherit His Wife's Land?

Baraita
Yair had land because he married a woman who inherited from her father — she died and he inherited from her. This proves a husband inherits from his wife.
Rav Pappa
How do you know? Perhaps a husband does not inherit, and the verses only teach about tribal transfer through a son, not a husband!
Abaye
If Pinehas purchased the land, it would return in the Jubilee — and this righteous man would have been buried in land that wasn't his! That's impossible.
Rav Pappa
Then perhaps Pinehas received it as a dedicated field (שדה חרם) in his capacity as a priest — still no proof the husband inherits!
Abaye
Even if only the son inherits, the inheritance still leaves the mother's tribe — so what force does the tribal prohibition have? The husband must inherit for the prohibition to make sense.
📌 Core Dispute The fundamental question: Do biblical prooftexts (Yair, Pinehas) establish that a husband inherits his wife's estate, or can each case be explained away by other means?

🌿 Pinehas: Purchase, Priest, or Inheritance?

Abaye
Pinehas inherited the land through his father Elazar (who inherited from his wife) — confirming husband inherits from wife.
Rav Pappa
Objection 1: Maybe Elazar purchased the land? The verse needn't prove inheritance at all.
Abaye
Impossible — purchased land returns in the Jubilee. Elazar (a righteous man) would have been buried in land that wasn't truly his!
Rav Pappa
Objection 2: Then perhaps as a Kohen, Pinehas received it as a dedicated field (שדה חרם) — still no proof of inheritance.
📜 Source Joshua 24:33 — Pinehas was buried in the hill of his father Elazar in Ephraim

🔄 Two Logic Paths: 'Already Transferred'

כבר עבר — 'Already Transferred' (Applies)

  • Once the mother married outside her tribe, her tribal land already transferred
  • The Torah is no longer concerned with re-transferring it again
  • Therefore: no problem if husband (from another tribe) inherits
  • Supports Abaye's reading of the verses

כבר עבר — 'Already Transferred' (Doesn't Apply)

  • Even if previously transferred, each new transfer still matters
  • Rav Pappa: verse still needed to warn against further tribal movement
  • Rav Yeimar: creates difficulty — why is marriage to same-tribe required?
  • The debate shapes how we read Numbers 36

👨‍👩‍👧 Rav Ashi's Dual-Tribe Solution

Problem posed by Rav Yeimar: If a woman inherits land from both parents (different tribes), any marriage will transfer the inheritance away from one tribe.
↓
Requirement: "She shall be a wife to one of the family of the tribe of her father" (Numbers 36:6) — but how do we satisfy both tribal loyalties?
↓
Rav Ashi's Answer: Marry her to a man whose father is from her father's tribe AND whose mother is from her mother's tribe.
↓
Result: The land retains its exact tribal character on both sides — the inheritance does not transfer away from either tribe.
↓
The Gemara's challenge: If so, the verse becomes extremely restrictive — limiting eligible husbands to a very narrow pool!
