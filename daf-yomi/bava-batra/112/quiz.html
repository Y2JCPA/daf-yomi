Bava Batra 112 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, how did Yair ben Seguv come to own land in Gilead?
A. He purchased it from members of the tribe of Manasseh
B. He inherited it from his father Seguv
C. He married a woman who inherited land from her father, and he inherited from her after she died
D. He received it as a dedicated field in his capacity as a leader
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that Seguv had no land — Yair's land came from his wife, proving that a husband inherits from his wife.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Abaye reject Rav Pappa's suggestion that Elazar purchased the land where Pinehas buried him?
A. A priest is forbidden to purchase land outside his ancestral territory
B. Purchased land returns to its original owner in the Jubilee Year, so Elazar would be buried in land that wasn't truly his
C. There is no record in Scripture of Elazar having sufficient funds to purchase land
D. The tribe of Ephraim would not have sold land to a member of the tribe of Levi
Correct: B — Abaye argues that purchased land reverts in the Jubilee — it would be unseemly for the righteous Elazar to be permanently buried in land he didn't truly own.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the 'already transferred' (כבר עבר) argument in this sugya?
A. Once a wife dies, her estate immediately transfers to her husband by Rabbinic decree
B. Once the mother married outside her tribe, the land already changed tribal hands, so Torah is no longer concerned with further transfers
C. A dedicated field transferred to a priest can never revert to the original owner
D. Land purchased before the Jubilee cycle is exempt from reversion
Correct: B — The argument is that the maternal tribal connection was already severed when the mother married outside her tribe, so subsequent transfers raise no new prohibition.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What creative solution does Rav Ashi propose for a woman who inherits land from both of her parents (from different tribes)?
A. She must donate the land to the Temple treasury to avoid tribal complications
B. She must sell the land before marrying so that tribal land laws no longer apply
C. She should marry a man whose father is from her father's tribe and whose mother is from her mother's tribe
D. She may only marry a Kohen, who is exempt from tribal land restrictions
Correct: C — Rav Ashi's elegant solution ensures the inheritance retains its tribal character on both sides by finding a husband who mirrors both tribal lines of the woman.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Yeimar's challenge to Abaye, what difficulty arises if we do NOT accept the 'already transferred' logic?
A. The Torah's prohibition against inter-tribal marriage would apply in all generations, making marriage nearly impossible
B. Even when a woman marries within her father's tribe, the inheritance is still uprooted from her mother's tribe — making the same-tribe requirement meaningless
C. Pinehas could not have owned the land at all, contradicting the explicit biblical verse
D. The husband's inheritance would be valid but the tribal prohibition would still be violated
Correct: B — Rav Yeimar points out that if every tribal transfer counts, then even marrying within her father's tribe doesn't help — her mother's tribe still loses the land.
