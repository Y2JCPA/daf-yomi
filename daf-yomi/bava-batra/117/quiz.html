Bava Batra 117 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
The mishna's ruling that Zelophehad and Hepher each receive portions in Eretz Yisrael follows which Tannaic opinion?
A. Rabbi Yonatan — land divided among those who entered
B. Rabbi Yoshiya — land divided among those who left Egypt
C. Rabbi Shimon ben Elazar — land divided among both groups
D. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi — land divided by the parable principle
Correct: B — The mishna follows R. Yoshiya, because under his view the dead who left Egypt still hold inheritable portions — allowing Zelophehad (who died in the wilderness) to transmit a share to his daughters.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Shimon ben Elazar, what happened to the portions of the ten spies who brought a bad report?
A. Their portions were divided equally among all twelve tribes
B. Their portions were given to the Levites as compensation
C. Joshua and Caleb each inherited the spies' forfeited portions
D. The portions reverted to the wilderness generation as a whole
Correct: C — R. Shimon ben Elazar teaches that Joshua and Caleb, the two faithful spies, received all the forfeited portions of the ten disloyal spies as a reward for their faithfulness.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the purpose of Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's parable about two priest-brothers at the threshing floor?
A. To explain why Levites received no land inheritance
B. To reconcile how a person could simultaneously hold a share with the exiters and with the enterers
C. To demonstrate that older brothers always receive larger priestly gifts
D. To show that priestly gifts must be divided by household, not by individual
Correct: B — Rebbi's parable illustrates that a single person can receive portions through two different frameworks at once — just as a son both receives as an individual and his father receives through him as a member of his brother's household.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What mnemonic does the Gemara provide for Rav Pappa's series of questions to Abaye about dividing Eretz Yisrael?
A. יוֹסֵף צְלָפְחָד מְנַשֶּׁה חוֹלֵק יִתֵּר יִמְנֶה
B. יִתֵּר צְלָפְחָד יוֹסֵף חוֹלֵק מְנַשֶּׁה יִמְנֶה
C. מְנַשֶּׁה יוֹסֵף צְלָפְחָד יִמְנֶה חוֹלֵק יִתֵּר
D. חוֹלֵק יִתֵּר יִמְנֶה יוֹסֵף מְנַשֶּׁה צְלָפְחָד
Correct: B — The Gemara explicitly states the mnemonic: 'To the more; Zelophehad; and Joseph; contested; Manasseh; will count' — serving as a memory device for the six questions Rav Pappa poses to Abaye.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yonatan, which verse supports dividing Eretz Yisrael among those who entered the land?
A. 'According to the names of the tribes of their fathers they shall inherit' (Num. 26:55)
B. 'Unto these the land shall be divided for an inheritance' (Num. 26:53)
C. 'Every man to his own inheritance' (Num. 36:8)
D. 'The land shall not be sold in perpetuity' (Lev. 25:23)
Correct: B — R. Yonatan derives his view from Numbers 26:53 — 'unto these (אֵלֶּה) the land shall be divided' — the word 'these' pointing to the living generation who actually entered and conquered the land.
