🗺️ How Was Eretz Yisrael Divided?

R. Yoshiya
Divided among those who left Egypt — including the dead, whose portions passed to their heirs (Numbers 26:55: 'according to the names of the tribes of their fathers')
R. Yonatan
Divided among those who entered the Land — the living inheritors at conquest (Numbers 26:53: 'unto these the land shall be divided')
R. Shimon b. Elazar
Divided among both groups — each person received a share with the exiters AND a share with the enterers, fulfilling both verses simultaneously
R. Yehuda HaNasi
Parable: Two priest-brothers, one with one son and one with two sons — all go to the threshing floor together; when dividing priestly gifts, each takes a portion both as a brother and as a son
Mishna's Premise The mishna (Zelophehad & Hepher each receive portions) follows R. Yoshiya — the dead who left Egypt retain inheritable shares

🏺 Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's Parable

מָשָׁל לִשְׁנֵי אַחִים כֹּהֲנִים שֶׁהָיוּ בְּעִיר אַחַת
Mashal — רַבִּי יְהוּדָה הַנָּשִׂיא Two priest-brothers live in the same town. One has one son, the other has two sons. All three sons go together to the threshing floor to collect priestly gifts.

The gifts are divided three ways (one per son). But when a son returns home, his father receives half — meaning the son with one brother gets ½ of ⅓, while the sons with two brothers each get ½ of ⅓ as well, but their father's share is split between them.

Nimshal: Similarly in Eretz Yisrael — each tribe member receives as an individual enterer AND transmits a share back through his exodus-generation ancestor.

⚖️ Spies, Protesters & Korah's Assembly — Their Portions

Joshua & Caleb

  • Received their OWN portions
  • Also inherited the portions of the OTHER 10 spies
  • Reward for faithfulness during the spy mission
  • Basis: 'none of the men who came up from Egypt shall see the land… except Caleb and Joshua'

The Protesters (מַלְעִיזִים)

  • Those who spoke against the Land
  • Lost their own portions
  • Portions redistributed to Joshua & Caleb
  • Died in the wilderness — no inheritance

Assembly of Korah (עֲדַת קֹרַח)

  • Also forfeited their portions
  • Swallowed by the earth
  • Their shares absorbed into general redistribution
  • Exception: sons of Korah did not die — their line continues

📜 Inheritance Chain: Zelophehad's Case (Amud B Context)

Zelophehad — from tribe of Manasseh, died in the wilderness without sons
↓
His father Hepher was among those who left Egypt → Hepher holds a portion
↓
Zelophehad himself also qualifies as an exiter → he too holds a portion (per R. Yoshiya)
↓
Zelophehad's five daughters (Mahlah, Noah, Hoglah, Milcah, Tirzah) petition Moses to inherit
↓
God rules: daughters inherit when there is no son → they receive Zelophehad's full portion
↓
Result: Mishna states both Hepher AND Zelophehad receive independent portions — confirming R. Yoshiya's view that exiters hold heritable shares
