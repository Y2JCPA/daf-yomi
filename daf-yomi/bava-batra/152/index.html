✍️ Matnat Shkhiv Mera With a Written Kinyan

Rav
He mounted two steeds: the recipient benefits from both a healthy person's gift (irrevocable) and a deathbed gift (takes effect without formal delivery). Valid from both angles.
Shmuel
I do not know what to rule. Perhaps the kinyan signals the giver intended transfer only via deed — requiring delivery. Or it was performed merely due to fear of death and is a standard deathbed gift.
Case A shkhiv mera (deathbed person) gave a gift and had an act of acquisition (קנין) explicitly recorded in the deed. Does this change the gift's legal status?

🐎 Rav's 'Two Steeds' — Both Frameworks Apply

Rav
The kinyan renders it irrevocable like a healthy person's gift — even if the giver recovers, he cannot retract it.
AND simultaneously:
It retains deathbed gift rules — takes effect without formal delivery of the deed.
Shmuel
The kinyan may mean he resolved to transfer it only through a deed — requiring actual delivery. If so, it is not a standard deathbed gift but a deed-dependent transfer. I am uncertain which applies.
Idiom — הרכיבו על שני סוסים 'He caused him to mount two steeds' — Rav granted the recipient the combined benefits of both legal categories at once.

🔄 Resolving the Internal Contradictions

Rav contradicts himself
Statement 1: deathbed gift + kinyan = irrevocable
Statement 2: deathbed gift takes effect only at death
↓
Statement 1 (irrevocable)
Kinyan was performed and delivered before witnesses — gift is complete immediately
Statement 2 (at death)
Kinyan recorded in deed but not yet delivered — normal deathbed gift rules apply
↓
Shmuel's contradiction resolved similarly: kinyan for the recipient's benefit vs. kinyan merely incidental to the document

🤝 What Counts as a Kinyan That 'Enhances' the Recipient?

Rav Ḥisda's Ruling

  • A kinyan enhances the recipient only when the deed contains reinforcing language
  • Example: 'מהיום ולעולם' — 'from today and forever'
  • Such language proves the giver's intent to make the transfer immediately irrevocable
  • Without such language the kinyan is merely incidental and does not elevate the gift

Rav Naḥman's Gesture to Rava

  • Rav Naḥman (sitting above Rava) gestured silently indicating agreement
  • He signaled that Rav Yehuda's ruling applies only where the giver was clearly enhancing the recipient's legal position
  • Rava later decoded the gesture to Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
  • Not every kinyan in a deathbed deed achieves this — intent and language are decisive

📜 The Double-Gift Problem

Step 1: Shkhiv mera writes deed with kinyan transferring property to Recipient A
↓
Step 2: The same giver then writes a second deed transferring the same property to Recipient B
↓
Rav: Recipient A acquires — the first kinyan rendered the gift irrevocable like a healthy person's gift; the giver had no power to retract it
↓
Shmuel: Recipient B acquires — a deathbed gift remains revocable; writing the second deed constitutes valid retraction in favor of B
↓
Why cite both disputes? Each case adds a novel rule: one tests kinyan + single deed; the other tests kinyan + retraction via second deed. Neither is redundant.
