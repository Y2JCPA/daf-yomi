🌬️ Flying Chaff: Whose Act Is It?

Ravina
The chaff flies because of the wind — it is not the person's direct missile. Bar Maryon's household is not liable for harm caused at a distance by wind-carried chaff.
Mar bar Rav Ashi
How is this different from one who winnows on Shabbat by throwing grain into the wind? The wind assisting is still counted as the person's act — so too the flax-beater is liable!
Ravina
Winnowing is designed to use the wind — that is its very purpose. Flax-beating aims to soften flax; flying chaff is unintended, more like sparks from a hammer that require proximity for liability.
Gemara
But even sparks from a hammer cause liability! So how does Ravina avoid liability for the chaff? The tension remains unresolved in this sugya.
Background Bar Maryon's household beat flax (rakta = chaff) injuring neighbors. The debate turns on whether wind-carried harm is 'one's arrow' or an independent natural force.

📜 Mishna: The Four Cubits Rule

לא יטע אדם אילן סמוך לשדה חבירו אלא אם כן הרחיק ממנו ארבע אמות
משנה — בבא בתרא כ״ו Basic Rule: One may not plant a tree near a neighbor's field without distancing it four cubits — whether grapevines or any other tree.

With a fence between them: Each party may plant right up to their side of the fence.

Baraita clarifies the reason: The four cubits are not for shade protection — they are for the work of the vineyard, so the owner can bring oxen and a plow without entering the neighbor's land.

Apparent contradiction: A second baraita states only three cubits for a tree. Resolution: four cubits applies specifically to a vineyard; three cubits is the rule for an individual tree.

🐦 Rava bar Rav Ḥanan's Palm Trees

Setup: Rava bar Rav Ḥanan's palms sit on the boundary of Rav Yosef's vineyard. Birds roost on palms → descend → damage the vineyard.
↓
Rav Yosef demands: Cut down your palms!
↓
Rava's Objection
The Mishna permits any tree with 4 cubits — my palms comply! Why must I cut them?
Rav Yosef's Reply
4 cubits governs tree-to-tree spacing, not bird-damage liability. Birds are a separate issue.
↓
Rava invokes Bal Tashchit: Rav ruled — a palm yielding even 1 kav may not be cut down.
"You shall not destroy the trees" — Devarim 20:19
↓
Outcome: Rava refuses to cut. The palms stand. Rav Yosef must absorb the bird damage.

⚖️ Ulla's Sixteen-Cubit Rule

Within 16 Cubits of Boundary

  • Tree draws nourishment from neighbor's soil
  • Owner deemed a גַּזְלָן — robber
  • Fruit may NOT be brought as בִּכּוּרִים (first fruits) — mitzva fulfilled through a sin
  • Roots treated as part of the tree, not the land
  • Source: derived from Shevi'it 1:5–6 (saplings in a beit se'a; 16² cubits per tree)

Beyond 16 Cubits from Boundary

  • Roots extending there belong to the land they enter
  • Tree owner has no claim over those distant roots
  • Neighbor who digs a cistern acquires the wood from cut roots
  • No robber status — tree is legitimately planted
  • First fruits (bikkurim) may be brought from such a tree
