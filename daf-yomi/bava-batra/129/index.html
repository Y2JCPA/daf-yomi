📡 Two Traditions: What Did Rabbi Yochanan Actually Rule?

Rav Dimi
Rabbi Yochanan says the bequest takes effect only when one person and one field are involved — both 'giving' and 'inheriting' must refer to the same person and same field.
Ravin
Rabbi Yochanan says it works even for two people and two different fields — 'Field A is given to Reuven' and 'Shimon will inherit Field B' both take effect.
Abaye
Ravin, you resolved the Rabbi Elazar contradiction, but you created a new one! You and Rav Dimi both cite Rabbi Yochanan yet completely contradict each other — this is difficult!
Ravin
We are both amora'im with different received traditions. Each of us heard a different version from Rabbi Yochanan — our dispute is one of variant mesorah, not error.
Key Principle When two amoraim cite contradictory rulings from the same authority, they may each have a legitimate but different tradition — neither necessarily wrong.

⚖️ The Four Combinations of Gift + Inheritance Language

1 Person + 1 Field

  • 'This field is given to Reuven and he will inherit it'
  • ✓ All agree: fully valid
  • Both terms refer to same person and same field
  • The baseline case — undisputed

1 Person + 2 Fields

  • 'Field A given to Reuven; Reuven will inherit Field B'
  • ⚠️ Disputed: Rav Dimi (invalid)
  • Rabbi Elazar and Ravin: valid — same person links both
  • One recipient, two different properties

2 People + 1 Field

  • 'Field given to Reuven; Shimon will inherit the same field'
  • ⚠️ Disputed similarly
  • Same field links both — Rabbi Elazar says valid
  • One property, two different recipients

2 People + 2 Fields

  • 'Field A given to Reuven; Shimon will inherit Field B'
  • Most complex — Rav Hamnuna (invalid) vs. Rav Sheshet (valid)
  • Rav Sheshet upheld by baraita proof
  • Conclusion: valid per Rav Sheshet

🏛️ Rav Sheshet's Proof from the Baraita

Baraita case: Father on deathbed tells steward — 'Give my children a shekel per week, and others will inherit their portion'
↓
This is structurally two people + two fields: children receive a gift (weekly shekel), while 'others' inherit the remaining estate
↓
Rav Hamnuna's read
The mishna's combined language is necessary for validity — without it, 'others' acquire nothing
Rav Sheshet's read
The baraita proves both people acquire property — vindicating two-field / two-person validity!
↓
Rav Sheshet's own resolution: 'Others' = heirs fit to inherit per Rabbi Yochanan ben Beroka — the baraita is a special case, but the principle still holds

🗣️ Toch Kedei Dibbur — The Continuous Speech Rule

What is תוך כדי דיבור? The brief time needed to utter a short greeting — shalom alecha rebbe ('peace unto you, my teacher')
↓
If a person completes, retracts, or modifies a statement within this window, the later words are legally treated as simultaneous with the first
↓
Application in our sugya: Reish Lakish requires 'giving' to be stated explicitly for both recipients. If the entire bequest is spoken toch kedei dibbur, the single 'giving' phrase covers both gifts — resolving his requirement
↓
This resolves the baraita contradiction: When both bequests were stated within one continuous speech unit, Reish Lakish's condition is automatically satisfied
↓
Critical Exception: Toch kedei dibbur does not apply to idol worship — one who accepts an idol within that time span is fully liable, with no retraction possible
