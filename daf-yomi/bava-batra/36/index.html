🗣️ Can a Jew Relay a Gentile's Testimony About a Sale?

Gemara
The Mishna says a gentile's claim that he purchased a field is credible. But if a Jew says 'the gentile told me he bought it' — is that credible?
Gemara (Question)
Is there any case where a gentile's own statement is NOT credible, but a Jew repeating it in his name IS credible? That would be strange!
Rava
The real case: A Jew says to the prior owner: 'A gentile purchased your field in my presence, and then sold it to me.' This is credible — because if he wanted to lie, he could have simply said: 'I purchased it from you directly.'
Principle Migu — a claim is credible when the claimant could have made a stronger (unchallenged) claim but chose a weaker one instead.

🚫 Acts That Do NOT Establish Chazakah

Rav Yehuda

  • Holding only from the fence (built against wild donkeys) outward — not the whole field
  • Consuming orla produce (years 1–3 of a new planting) — owner may not protest forbidden fruit
  • Owning land by fence perimeter only — inner field unclaimed

Rav Yosef

  • Consuming unripe fodder (stalks with no ripe grain) — not real use of the land
  • Exception: if the land is in the city, consuming fodder IS sufficient — urban land's fodder has market value

Rav Naḥman

  • Produce from fissured (cracked/dry) land — owners don't bother protesting poor-quality produce
  • Exilarch's household cannot establish chazakah in others' land (people fear to protest them), nor can others establish chazakah in their land

🌱 Does Plowing Establish Chazakah?

Rav Beivai → Rav Naḥman
What is the reason of the one who says plowing does NOT establish chazakah?
Rav Naḥman
Plowing prepares the field but produces no visible benefit — the owner might not notice or might not bother to protest mere preparation.
Rav Ashi
I asked all the great men of the generation and they said: Plowing IS sufficient to establish chazakah.
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
But Rav and Shmuel in Babylonia, and Rabbi Yishmael and Rabbi Akiva in Eretz Yisrael all say: Plowing does NOT establish chazakah. Can you dismiss four pillars of tradition?
Resolution The baraita records both opinions. The Gemara identifies Rabbi Yishmael and Rabbi Akiva via the Mishna; Rav from a teaching about the case of a plower; Shmuel from Rav Yehuda's report that the 18-month rule is specifically R. Yishmael's and R. Akiva's — implying Shmuel requires full produce use.

🍇 Rabbi Yishmael vs. Rabbi Akiva: 18 Months or 3 Full Years?

Mishna baseline: A non-irrigated grain field (שדה לבן) requires 3 full years of use to establish chazakah.
↓
Rabbi Yishmael: For a grain field — 3 months in year 1 + 12 months in year 2 + 3 months in year 3 = 18 months total suffices, because harvest season spans only part of each year.
↓
Rabbi Akiva: Even a single month (the harvest month) in year 1 and year 3, plus the full middle year = 14 months total suffices.
↓
Gemara's challenge: If even one day of harvest in year 1 and year 3 counts, why does Rabbi Akiva require a full month at all? Even one day should suffice!
↓
Resolution: Everyone agrees plowing alone is insufficient. The dispute concerns how much of the harvest season must be used. For orchards (שדה האילן), Rabbi Yishmael requires only one harvest day per year — the orchard's season is concentrated.
↓
Practical outcome: Amud B opens: For a white field (grain), 18 months per R. Yishmael; for an orchard, one harvest event per year across 3 years suffices.
