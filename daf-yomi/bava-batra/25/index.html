🌅 Where Does the Divine Presence Rest?

Rabbi Abbahu
The Divine Presence is in the west — the word oriyya (west) means avir Yah, 'the air of God.'
Rabbi Yehoshua b. Levi
The Shekhinah is on the western wall. One should always pray facing west — the Divine Presence never departs from there.
Rabbi Oshaya
The Divine Presence is everywhere: 'You alone have made heaven… You preserve them all alive' (Neh. 9:6) — God encompasses all creation.
Rav Sheshet
Divine Presence is in every direction — I told my servant: face me any direction except east, not that I require facing west specifically.
Practical Ruling Tannery may NOT be placed on the west side — out of respect for the Divine Presence. All other sides are permitted with a 50-cubit buffer from the city.

🏭 Tannery Placement: East vs. West

East/North/South — Permitted

  • May establish tannery on these sides
  • Must distance 50 cubits from city
  • Both Mishna and Rabbi Akiva agree
  • Wind direction less problematic here

West Side — Totally Forbidden

  • Cannot establish tannery at all
  • West wind is most frequent — blows toward city
  • Divine Presence rests in the west
  • Foul odors disrespectful to the Shekhinah

Other Distancing Rules (Mishna)

  • Animal carcasses: 50 cubits from city
  • Graves: 50 cubits from city
  • Tannery: 50 cubits, east side only
  • Flax water: distanced from vegetables

🌳 Tree vs. Cistern: Who Must Yield?

Mishna
A tree must be distanced 25 cubits from a cistern. Carob and sycamore (deep roots): 50 cubits. Applies whether above or below grade.
Anonymous Tanna
If the cistern preceded the tree, the tree owner must cut it down and compensate the cistern owner for any damage done.
Rabbi Yosei
Even if the cistern preceded the tree, the tree owner need not cut it down — each one digs in his own property and plants in his own property.
Shmuel (via Rav Yehuda)
The halakha follows Rabbi Yosei — property rights protect lawful use; neither party forces the other to undo improvements on their own land.
Halakhic Ruling Halakha follows Rabbi Yosei: planting in one's own land is protected even if roots later extend toward a neighbor's cistern — mutual property rights prevail.

🏛️ Post-Temple: Cosmic Changes in Rainfall

Beit HaMikdash Destroyed
Rafram bar Pappa / Rav Ḥisda: two permanent spiritual consequences
↓
Southern Wind
No longer brings rain
"He decrees on the right [south] and there is hunger" (Job 36:31)
Good Storehouse
Rain no longer descends from the good storehouse
"The Lord will open His good storehouse" — only for those who do His will (Deut. 28:12)
↓
Result: Both right (south) and left (north) consumed by hunger
"And consumes on the left" — all directions of abundance withdrawn

🧭 Wisdom & Wealth: Which Direction to Face?

Rabbi Yitzḥak's ruling: One who wishes to become wise should face south when praying
↓
One who wishes to become wealthy should face north when praying
↓
Mnemonic from the Temple: The Menorah (symbolizing wisdom/Torah light) stood in the south; the Shulchan/Table (symbolizing abundance) stood in the north
↓
Problem raised: Rabbi Yehoshua b. Levi says the Divine Presence is in the west — how can one pray facing south or north instead?
↓
Resolution (Rabbi Ḥanina to Rav Ashi): Do not face due south — turn slightly to face southwest, honoring both the wisdom/wealth direction and the western Divine Presence simultaneously
