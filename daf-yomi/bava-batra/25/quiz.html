Bava Batra 25 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara's conclusion, why does Rabbi Akiva prohibit placing a tannery on the west side specifically?
A. The west wind blows most frequently and carries odors toward the city
B. The west side is always the most densely populated area
C. The Divine Presence rests in the west, making foul odors disrespectful
D. Western soil causes hides to spoil faster
Correct: C — Rava's analysis concludes Rabbi Akiva's prohibition is not about wind frequency (Rav Ḥanan bar Abba taught all four winds blow daily) but because the Divine Presence rests in the west, and placing a foul-smelling tannery there would be disrespectful.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rabbi Yitzḥak say about facing north when praying?
A. One who faces north will become wise like Torah scholars
B. One who faces north will become wealthy
C. One should never face north since the world's north side is unenclosed
D. Facing north is associated with the Divine Presence
Correct: B — Rabbi Yitzḥak says one who wishes to become wealthy should face north — the Temple's Table (symbolizing abundance/parnasah) stood in the north. The Menorah (symbolizing wisdom/Torah) stood in the south.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What distance must a carob or sycamore tree be from a cistern, and why is it different from other trees?
A. 25 cubits — same as all other trees
B. 50 cubits — their roots extend much farther underground
C. 100 cubits — they are especially large trees
D. 10 cubits — they are planted closer to homes
Correct: B — The Mishna requires 50 cubits for carob and sycamore trees specifically because their roots extend much farther underground than ordinary trees, posing greater risk of penetrating and damaging a cistern's walls.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rafram bar Pappa citing Rav Ḥisda teaches that since the Temple's destruction, which wind no longer brings rain?
A. The north wind
B. The east wind
C. The south wind
D. The west wind
Correct: C — Rafram bar Pappa in the name of Rav Ḥisda: from the day the Temple was destroyed, the southern wind has not brought rain — derived from 'He decrees on the right [south] and there is hunger' (Job 36:31), indicating the south no longer brings blessing.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the tension between facing south for wisdom and Rabbi Yehoshua b. Levi's teaching that the Divine Presence is in the west?
A. One should face east as a compromise between south and west
B. The wisdom teaching only applies outside of prayer contexts
C. One should face slightly southwest to honor both directions
D. One must choose one value and accept the trade-off
Correct: C — Rabbi Ḥanina advised Rav Ashi not to face due south but to turn slightly — facing southwest — thus orienting toward both the wisdom direction (south, via the Menorah) and the direction of the Divine Presence (west, per Rabbi Yehoshua b. Levi).
