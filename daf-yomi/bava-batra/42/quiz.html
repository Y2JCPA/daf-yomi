QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara's resolution, why can a buyer collect liened property through witnesses alone — unlike a borrower who needs a promissory note?
A. Because a sale is a public transaction and buyers had constructive notice of prior liens
B. Because Rav holds that witnesses are always superior to written documents
C. Because sales are private and only witnesses can prove their occurrence
D. Because a promissory note cannot be used in property disputes
Correct: A — The Gemara distinguishes loans (discreet, so buyers have no notice) from sales (public, so buyers could have investigated). Since a sale is publicly known, witnesses alone suffice for the seller to collect from liened property.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the Mishna on 42b, which of the following people CANNOT establish chazakah regardless of how long they possess the property?
A. A person who purchased land openly from its owner
B. A craftsman holding an owner's item for repair
C. An heir who inherited land from his father
D. A person who worked land for three consecutive years with no claim against him
Correct: B — The Mishna lists craftsmen, partners, sharecroppers, and stewards as unable to establish chazakah because their possession is understood to be by the owner's permission — not adverse possession.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rav Pappa resolve the contradiction between the baraita (which seems to allow combining years) and the rule that a buyer's transaction must generate publicity?
A. He rules that any three-year possession combines regardless of publicity
B. He explains that the baraita refers to a son who sold fields without specifying which ones — lacking publicity, the buyer cannot combine his years with the father's
C. He holds that the baraita only applies when the original owner died intestate
D. He limits the rule to cases where the father's possession was also combined from multiple parties
Correct: B — Rav Pappa clarifies: the baraita deals with a son who sold 'without specification' — since no particular field was identified, the sale lacked publicity. Without publicity, a buyer cannot piggyback on the father's years to complete chazakah.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Rav Naḥman bar Avuh say was the primary teaching in Shmuel's statement about partners?
A. That partners can never testify on behalf of each other in any context
B. That a partner who improves joint property collects his share of the enhancement when the produce reaches shoulder-height
C. That partners must formally divide property before any chazakah can begin
D. That a partner's years of possession count double due to the shared ownership interest
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman clarifies that Shmuel's main intent was about compensation for improvement: a partner who proactively works to improve joint property is entitled to collect his enhancement share once the produce 'reaches shoulders' and is ready for harvest.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Ravina's resolution of the contradiction in Shmuel's rulings about partners and chazakah, when does a partner establish chazakah?
A. Never — a partner can never establish chazakah under any circumstances
B. Only when the partner has a written agreement specifying his exclusive portion
C. When he enters the entire field and it is large enough to be subject to chazakah requirements on its own
D. Only after ten years of uncontested possession, double the normal requirement
Correct: C — Ravina resolves that both rulings discuss entering the entire field: if the field is large enough to independently satisfy chazakah requirements, entering the whole establishes chazakah; if too small (where entering the whole suggests shared use), it does not.
