Son of the deceased (or son's descendants)
↓
Daughter of the deceased (or daughter's descendants)
↓
Father of the deceased
↓
Paternal line
Brothers → Paternal sisters → Father's father…
Maternal line
Maternal uncles — inherit but do not bequeath back
↓
Principle: Any heir's descendants replace him entirely and precede the next category. A father precedes all his own descendants.

🗣️ Does the Mishna Follow Rabbi Zekharya ben HaKatzav?

Rabbi Yoḥanan
I raise an objection from the mishna: a woman inherits from her husband and son, but they do not inherit from her — this supports my view against Rabbi Yehuda ben Shimon.
Questioner
Why not resolve the difficulty by saying the mishna follows Rabbi Zekharya ben HaKatzav, who does not derive son-precedes-daughter from the tribal verse?
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Impossible! The mishna states that only sons of a sister inherit — but Rabbi Zekharya holds son and daughter are equal in inheriting from their mother. That is a direct contradiction!
Gemara
Confirmed: the mishna privileges sons of a sister over daughters of a sister, which contradicts Rabbi Zekharya ben HaKatzav's view of maternal equality.
Conclusion The mishna cannot be attributed to Rabbi Zekharya ben HaKatzav. The tanna of the mishna uses the word 'tribes' (מַטּוֹת) but treats women differently due to the verse about daughters who 'possess' an inheritance.

📜 Baraita: Deriving the Full Inheritance Order from Numbers 27

Numbers 27:8 — "If a man dies and has no son, pass his inheritance to his daughter"
→ Son precedes daughter; daughter precedes all except son's descendants
↓
Numbers 27:9 — "If he has no daughter, give his inheritance to his brothers"
→ Daughter precedes brothers
↓
Numbers 27:10 — "If he has no brothers, give it to his father's brothers"
→ Brothers precede father's brothers
↓
Numbers 27:11 — "If his father has no brothers, give it to his nearest kinsman"
→ Continue through all relatives of that tribe
↓
No offspring at all? Genealogy is traced successively all the way back to Reuben son of Jacob

📖 Why a Woman Does Not Bequeath to Her Son

כָּל בַּת יֹרֶשֶׁת נַחֲלָה מִמַּטּוֹת בְּנֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל (במדבר לו:ח)
Key Verse Derivation The Gemara asks: if the mishna derives laws from the word "tribes" (מַטּוֹת), shouldn't a woman also be able to transmit inheritance to her son — since the tribal verse applies equally? The Gemara answers: the verse states "every daughter who possesses [יֹרֶשֶׁת] an inheritance from the tribes of Israel" (Numbers 36:8). The word yoreshet implies she is the receiver of tribal inheritance, not its transmitter. A woman inherits into a tribe from the outside but does not pass tribal inheritance onward — therefore she inherits from her son but her son does not inherit from her via the tribal chain.

⚔️ Rabbinic View vs. Sadducee View: Daughter and Son's Daughter

Rabbinic (Pharisee) View

  • A son's daughter (granddaughter) entirely replaces the son and takes precedence over a daughter
  • A daughter cannot inherit alongside a son's daughter — they do not share
  • Rav (cited by Rav Huna): Anyone who rules otherwise, even a prince of Israel, should not be listened to
  • Basis: The principle that each heir's descendants step into his place and precede the next category entirely

Sadducee View

  • A daughter should inherit together with a son's daughter (granddaughter)
  • Argument: If the son's daughter (more distant) inherits, the daughter (closer) should inherit alongside her — kal va-chomer
  • Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai called their scholars 'imbeciles' and demanded a source
  • Refuted by the Anah/Zibeon genealogical proof — legal categories cannot be conflated by surface similarity

🌳 Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai Silences the Sadducees

Sadducees issue a ruling: A daughter may inherit alongside a son's daughter — based on a kal va-chomer (if the more distant granddaughter inherits, surely the daughter does too).
↓
Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai joins the discussion and challenges them: "Imbeciles! From where do you derive this ruling?" No scholar could answer him.
↓
One old Sadducee finally murmured the kal va-chomer: "If the son's daughter, who does not stand in the son's place for levirate marriage, inherits — how much more so the daughter herself!"
↓
Rabban Yoḥanan's refutation via genealogy: He cited Genesis 36:20 (Anah as son of Seir) and 36:24 (Anah as son of Zibeon) — Anah appears as both son and brother of Zibeon, proving Zibeon committed incest with his own mother.
↓
The lesson: Apparent equivalence (same name, similar relationship) does not mean legal equivalence. A son's daughter entirely replaces the son; a daughter occupies a wholly different legal slot — they do not co-inherit.
↓
Sadducee's partial concession: He admits a son's son precedes a daughter. But he continues to argue a daughter should inherit alongside a son's daughter — a point the Gemara will continue to address on the next daf.
