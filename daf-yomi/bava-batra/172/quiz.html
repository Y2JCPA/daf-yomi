5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava bar Rav Sheila, if a scribe doesn't know the exact date of an acquisition, what should he write?
A. An estimated date close to the actual event
B. The date of the current writing
C. No date at all
D. The date the buyer takes possession
Correct: B — Rava bar Rav Sheila instructed scribes to write the current date if the actual acquisition date is unknown, so that the document should not appear as a falsehood.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rava say a court cannot split a 100-dinar promissory note into two 50-dinar notes?
A. It would create a forgery
B. It benefits neither party
C. It harms the balance of protections the Sages designed for both creditor and debtor
D. The court lacks jurisdiction over financial documents
Correct: C — The single large note benefits the creditor (more coercion power) AND the debtor (partial payment vitiates it, requiring the creditor to swear an oath to collect the rest). Splitting would undermine these dual protections.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the Mishna's case of two brothers inheriting a bathhouse their father built for personal use, what can the rich brother tell the poor brother?
A. You must sell me your share
B. Go buy servants to bathe in it — I won't convert it to commercial use
C. We must divide the profits equally
D. The bathhouse must be sold to a third party
Correct: B — When the father built the facility for personal use (not profit), the poor brother cannot force commercial conversion. The rich brother can say: 'Buy servants and use the bathhouse yourself.'
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Abaye's distinction regarding concern for 'fallen' promissory notes?
A. We are never concerned about fallen notes
B. We are always concerned about fallen notes
C. We are not concerned between specific known people, but are concerned among the general public
D. We are concerned only when the amounts exceed 100 dinars
Correct: C — Abaye distinguishes: the mishna's case of two specific people named Yosef ben Shimon is too unlikely to worry about, but a note falling from one random person and being found by another in the general public is a real concern.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What fraud scenario does Rav Ashi describe to explain why a 100-dinar note cannot be reduced to 50?
A. The creditor forges the debtor's signature on a new note
B. The debtor may have paid all 100, received a receipt, and the creditor could claim the receipt was for a different loan
C. The scribe may have written the wrong amount
D. The debtor might claim he never borrowed the money
Correct: B — Rav Ashi's concern: the debtor paid all 100, the creditor said 'I lost the note' and gave a receipt. If a new 50-dinar note is issued, the creditor presents it and claims the receipt was for a completely different loan — leaving the debtor unable to defend himself.
