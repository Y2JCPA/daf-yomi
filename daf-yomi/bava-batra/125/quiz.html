QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does a firstborn NOT receive a double portion of debts collected by the orphans after the father's death?
A. The Torah explicitly exempts monetary debts from double-portion rules
B. The father never actually held that specific money — it is Ra'ui, not Muchzak
C. Debts are always split equally among all heirs regardless of birth order
D. Only real estate can ever be subject to a firstborn's double portion
Correct: B — The core principle is Ra'ui vs. Muchzak. The father never possessed the specific money collected after his death, so it is 'property due' (Ra'ui), and the firstborn receives no double portion of Ra'ui.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the great-grandmother case, what did the dying man specify about his property after the grandmother's death?
A. It should be sold and divided equally among his grandchildren
B. It goes to his grandmother's heirs, whoever they may be
C. It goes to his own heirs — not to the grandmother's heirs
D. It reverts to the estate and is distributed by a rabbinic court
Correct: C — The man said: 'All my property is given to my grandmother; after she dies, it goes to MY heirs' — explicitly excluding the grandmother's heirs and limiting the gift to revert to his own bloodline.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was Rav Huna's position regarding the son-in-law's claim to the inheritance?
A. The son-in-law has no claim whatsoever because he is not a blood relative
B. 'My heirs' includes heirs of heirs, so the son-in-law (as heir to the daughter) inherits
C. The son-in-law must share equally with the grandchildren in a three-way split
D. The son-in-law inherits only if there are no surviving grandchildren
Correct: B — Rav Huna held that 'my heirs' extends to heirs of heirs. Since the daughter was the man's heir and the husband is the daughter's heir, the husband qualifies under the bequest.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The ruling from Eretz Yisrael agreed with Rav Anan's conclusion but rejected his reasoning. What was the actual correct reasoning?
A. The phrase 'my heirs' is inherently ambiguous and must be interpreted strictly against claimants
B. The grandmother had already sold the property before dying, so nothing remained
C. The property is Ra'ui — since the daughter never possessed it, her husband cannot inherit it as Muchzak
D. A husband only inherits when a valid ketubah was written before the wife's illness
Correct: C — The ruling from Eretz Yisrael: the husband does not inherit — not because of how 'my heirs' is parsed, but because this was Ra'ui property the daughter never possessed. Husbands do not inherit Ra'ui as they do Muchzak.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa's final ruling, which statement is correct?
A. A husband inherits both Muchzak and Ra'ui property from his wife equally
B. A firstborn receives a double portion of Ra'ui but not of Muchzak — the opposite of what was thought
C. Neither a husband nor a firstborn has enhanced inheritance rights over Ra'ui property
D. Only the firstborn is limited regarding Ra'ui; a husband always inherits his wife's estate in full
Correct: C — Rav Pappa rules: a husband does not take Ra'ui as he does Muchzak, AND a firstborn does not receive a double portion of Ra'ui as he does Muchzak. Both enhanced rights are restricted to Muchzak — property actually possessed.
