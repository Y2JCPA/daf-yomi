🗣️ Bitzit or Dugit? Same Boat, Two Names

Rabbi Natan
One who sells a ship has also sold its bitzit — the small boat used as a lifeboat or for fishing, attached to the main vessel.
Sumakhos
The small boat is called a dugit, not bitzit. This term appears in Scripture (I Samuel 13:21) and is the standard usage in Eretz Yisrael.
Rava
They are speaking of the same object. Rabbi Natan was a Babylonian — in Babylonia people say botziata (small boats of Miashan). Sumakhos was from Eretz Yisrael and used dugit. No real dispute exists.
Principle Regional dialects can create the appearance of halakhic disagreement where none exists. Rava resolves the tension by identifying the geographic linguistic difference.

🌊 Rabba's Seafarers' Tales

The Ship-Sinking Wave: A wave appears with a ray of white fire at its crest. Sailors strike it with clubs inscribed: "I am that I am, Yah, the Lord of Hosts — Amen Amen Selah." This divine inscription calms the deadly wave.
↓
Two Waves Converse: One wave shouts to another: "Did you leave anything undestroyed that I may come and finish?" The other replies: "Go see the power of your Master — not even a single reed may the sea cross." God's decree at the shoreline holds firm.
↓
The Height of a Wave: Between wave crests: 300 parasangs. Height of a wave: 300 parasangs. A wave once lifted a ship to the sky — sailors saw the tiny stars of the Orion constellation up close before crashing back down.
↓
Hurmin Son of Lilith: A supernatural being Rabba witnessed running along the pinnacles of Meḥoza's city walls — a horseman riding below could not keep pace. He was then saddled two mules on two bridges of the Rognag river, leaping between them, pouring wine from cup to cup without spilling a single drop.

🦕 Rabba bar bar Chana's Legendary Creatures

🦌 Giant Antelope (Urzila)

  • A day-old antelope = size of Mount Tabor
  • Mount Tabor = 4 parasangs
  • Neck length = 3 parasangs
  • Head-resting area = 1.5 parasangs
  • Its dung blocked the Jordan River entirely

🐸 Giant Frog (Akrokta)

  • Frog = size of Hagronya fortress
  • Hagronya fort = 60 houses
  • A snake came and swallowed the frog
  • A raven came and swallowed the snake
  • Raven sat in a tree — picture the tree's scale!

🐟 Fish Mistaken for Land

  • Sand settled on a giant fish's back
  • Grass grew atop it; looked like dry land
  • Sailors baked and cooked on it
  • Fish grew hot — flipped over
  • Ship barely escaped the churning water

🦅 Sky-High Bird

  • Bird stood with water at its ankles
  • Its head reached into the sky
  • Sailors assumed water was shallow
  • Heavenly voice warned: Do not descend!
  • A carpenter's axe fell 7 years ago — still falling

🏜️ The Arab Guide and the Dead of the Wilderness

אֲמַר לִי: תָּא אַחֲוֵי לָךְ מֵתֵי מִדְבָּר. אֲזַלִי וַחֲזִיתִינְהוּ, וְדָמוּ כִּמְאַן דִּמְבַסְּמֵי
Aggada The Fat Geese: In the desert, Rabba saw enormous geese so fat their wings drooped to the ground, with streams of oil flowing beneath them. He asked: "Shall we have a portion of you in the World-to-Come?" One raised its wing and one raised its leg — a sign of assent.

The Arab Navigator: An Arab companion would take dust from the ground, smell it, and say: "This is the road to such-and-such a place, and that is the road to such-and-such a place." He knew every route by scent alone.

The Dead of the Wilderness: The Arab offered: "Come, I will show you the dead of the wilderness" — those who perished after the Exodus. Rabba went and saw them. They had the appearance of one who is intoxicated, lying on their backs — a haunting vision of Israel's unfinished journey.
