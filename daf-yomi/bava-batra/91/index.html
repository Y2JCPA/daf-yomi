🌾 Economics, Exile & Ancestral Memory

בבא בתרא צ"א
Daf Overview Daf 91 weaves three major threads: (1) rabbinic price controls protecting basic commodities in Eretz Yisrael, (2) the prohibition on leaving the Land during famine — illustrated by Elimelech's tragic fate as a leader who abandoned his people — and (3) a treasury of aggadic traditions recording hidden names of biblical figures' mothers and ancestors. The daf closes with a utopian vision of overflowing abundance.

🥚 Double Profit on Eggs: What Does It Mean?

First Opinion (Rav or Shmuel)
One may not charge double the price paid for eggs — the markup itself is capped at 100% of purchase cost.
Second Opinion (Rav or Shmuel)
One may not profit twice from the same batch — the same lot cannot pass through two middlemen each taking profit.
Mari bar Mari
This is a dispute between Rav and Shmuel. Both protect consumers from price gouging on basic foodstuffs — they differ on the mechanism of the prohibition.
Background Rule The Sages prohibited profiteering on basic sustenance items (wines, oils, flours) in Eretz Yisrael. Exception: Rabbi Elazar ben Azarya sold wine commercially, following Rabbi Yehuda ben Beteira who permits wine export from the Land.

🚪 When May One Leave Eretz Yisrael?

Default rule: Leaving Eretz Yisrael to settle abroad is prohibited
↓
Tanna Kamma
Permitted when 2 se'a of grain = 1 sela (double normal price) — severe but not absolute scarcity
Rabbi Shimon
Only permitted when one cannot find food at any price — mere expense does not justify leaving the Land
↓
Elimelech left Bethlehem while food was available (merely expensive) → he and his sons Mahlon and Chilion all died in exile
↓
Rabbi Shimon ben Yoḥai: They were leaders of their generation — departure of prominent figures demoralizes the people, compounding the sin

⚖️ Ibzan = Boaz: Greatness and Downfall

Ibzan's Greatness

  • Judge of Israel — identified by Rav as Boaz himself
  • Had 60 children: 30 sons + 30 daughters
  • Made two feasts per wedding — one at the groom's father's house, one at the father-in-law's house
  • 120 total feasts hosted — a mark of extraordinary generosity

His Tragic Downfall

  • Did not invite Manoah (future father of Samson) to any of the 120 feasts
  • All 60 children died during his own lifetime
  • Folk saying: "Why have 60 who die before you? Better one child who outlives you"
  • Lesson: Humiliating a Torah scholar — even by omission — cannot be offset by other good deeds

🍯 The Blessing of Abundant Produce (Leviticus 25–26)

Leviticus 25:22 — "You shall eat of the produce of the old store" while the new crop is still growing in the field
↓
Rav Naḥman: The blessing is that old produce will still be available when new produce arrives — Israel never faces a gap between harvests
↓
Rav Sheshet: The blessing is qualitative — old produce remains tasty and unspoiled, not merely that you are forced to eat it because the new hasn't come yet
↓
Leviticus 26:10 — "You shall eat old store long kept" — older produce will be superior to newer produce of the same type
↓
Ultimate Vision: Storehouses overflowing with old grain AND threshing floors full of new grain — Israel will struggle to make room for the new abundance
