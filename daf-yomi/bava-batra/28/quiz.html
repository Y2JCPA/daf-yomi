Bava Batra 28 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yishmael, how many total months establish chazakah for a non-irrigated grain field?
A. 12 months
B. 14 months
C. 18 months
D. 36 months
Correct: C — Rabbi Yishmael requires 3 + 12 + 3 = 18 months: partial possession at the beginning and end of the outer years, plus a full middle year of use.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rabbi Yoḥanan's source (transmitted from Usha) for the 3-year chazakah rule?
A. Jeremiah's purchase of a field during the siege
B. The forewarned ox (shor muad) — three gorings on three occasions
C. The Jubilee year cycle of land redemption
D. The verse commanding return of a lost object
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan derives it from the shor muad: just as three gorings on three separate days establish the ox as forewarned, three years of possession establish ownership of land.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does possession of land without any ownership claim (ta'anah) fail to establish chazakah, even after three years?
A. Scripture explicitly requires a written deed for all land transfers
B. Without a claim the possessor cannot produce witnesses
C. The original owner could say the possessor was merely a sharecropper or renter with no real title
D. Rabbinic law requires both possession and a public announcement
Correct: C — The Gemara explains: if the possessor has no claim, the original owner can confront them and demand they leave — the possessor's silence suggests they know they are there only as a hired worker, not as an owner.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Abaye challenge Rav Yosef's proof from the verse in Jeremiah 32?
A. Jeremiah's purchase was made under duress and cannot serve as a legal model
B. The verse applies only during wartime sieges in Eretz Yisrael
C. Jeremiah may simply be offering good practical advice (eitzah tovah), not establishing a law of chazakah
D. The verse speaks of a gift to a relative, not an ordinary sale
Correct: C — Abaye argues that Jeremiah 32:44 ('subscribe the deeds and seal them') might just be sound counsel to use documents — not proof that the presumption of ownership is derived from that passage.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why doesn't harvesting a caper bush three times in three consecutive days establish chazakah?
A. Caper bushes are not legally classified as agricultural produce
B. Chazakah requires the owner to have been absent for three years, not three days
C. The three profitable uses must occur in distinct periods — not in rapid back-to-back succession
D. Only grain harvests, not fruit, count toward establishing chazakah
Correct: C — Parallel to the forewarned ox, whose three gorings must occur on three separate occasions rather than successively, the three profitable uses of land must be spread across meaningfully distinct time periods.
