Bava Batra 113 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara's resolution, what is the primary mechanism of tribal inheritance transfer addressed in Numbers 36:9?
A. Transfer by means of the son inheriting his mother's land
B. Transfer by means of the husband marrying outside the tribe
C. Transfer by means of a court-ordered redistribution
D. Transfer by means of a daughter inheriting and marrying out
Correct: B — Both baraitot ultimately agree that Numbers 36:9 primarily addresses transfer by means of the husband — a woman marrying outside her tribe causes her inherited land to pass to her husband's different tribe.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rava's explanation for why 'shall cleave' in the tribal context proves that marriage causes tribal transfer?
A. The land itself cleaves to whoever physically dwells on it
B. Tribes cleave to one another through the bond of marriage between their members
C. A husband must cleave to his wife's tribe and adopt her identity
D. The heirs cleave to the inheritance and cannot release it
Correct: B — Rava explains: 'The tribes of the children of Israel shall cleave' — tribes as entities can only bond with one another through marriage. This proves marriage is the mechanism of tribal transfer.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What halakhic principle does the story of Pinehas inheriting land (Joshua 24:33) prove?
A. A Levite may inherit tribal land through his mother
B. A husband who inherits from his wife does not take the place of her father's heirs
C. Land once transferred by marriage can never revert to the original tribe
D. A son always inherits in place of his deceased father's creditors
Correct: B — Elazar (a Levite) inherited his wife's Ephraimite land; Pinehas inherited from Elazar. This proves a husband is an additional heir — he does not replace or displace his wife's family in the inheritance chain.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the baraita cited by Rav Sheshet, which relatives inherit from a maternal uncle?
A. Sons of sisters only, not daughters of sisters
B. Daughters of sisters only, not sons of sisters
C. Both sons of sisters and daughters of sisters
D. Neither — only paternal relatives inherit
Correct: C — The baraita extends the Mishna's ruling: both sons of sisters (nephews) AND daughters of sisters (nieces) inherit from their maternal uncle, though sons take priority over daughters per standard inheritance rules.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
From the word 'day' in Deuteronomy 21:16, what rule about inheritance does Rabba bar Ḥanina derive?
A. A will must be written before noon on the day of death
B. Inheritance matters may only be adjudicated during daylight hours, not at night
C. The firstborn receives his double portion on the first day after death
D. An oral will is only valid if declared on a weekday
Correct: B — Rabba bar Ḥanina derives from 'on the day that he causes his sons to inherit' that all inheritance adjudication — including oral wills made before three visitors to a sick person — is valid only during the daytime.
