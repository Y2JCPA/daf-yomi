Bava Batra 127 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Ameimar, how does a tumtum (found to be male) affect the firstborn's double portion?
A. He receives his own double portion as the firstborn
B. He reduces the firstborn's portion like any other brother
C. He does not reduce the firstborn's additional portion at all
D. He receives half a portion in cases of doubt
Correct: C — Ameimar rules that a tumtum found to be male does not factor into the calculation of the firstborn's double portion — his share is simply ignored in that calculation.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rav Sheizevi rule that a tumtum is NOT circumcised on the eighth day when it falls on Shabbat?
A. Because circumcision for a tumtum is always performed later
B. Because the verse 'gives birth to a male' excludes a tumtum, who was not definitively recognized as male at birth
C. Because Shabbat always takes precedence over all circumcisions
D. Because tumtum circumcision requires a rabbinic court ruling first
Correct: B — Rav Sheizevi derives from 'if a woman gives birth to a male' that only a definite male at birth qualifies; a tumtum was not recognized as male at birth, so the Shabbat override does not apply.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What did Rava ultimately correct regarding two wives who gave birth in hiding?
A. Each uncertain firstborn receives a full double portion
B. Neither son receives a double portion if they were never previously recognized as firstborn
C. Both sons split the double portion equally between them
D. The court assigns firstborn status based on physical examination
Correct: B — Via Ravin's letter citing Rabbi Yannai, Rava retracted: partial double portions apply only if the sons were once recognized and later lost track of. If never recognized at all, neither receives any double portion.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the key dispute between Rabbi Yehuda and the Rabbis regarding a father's credibility about his firstborn?
A. Whether a father's testimony requires corroboration by two witnesses
B. Whether a father can override an existing court presumption about the firstborn
C. Whether the father must declare in writing or orally
D. Whether the declaration must be made before the father's death
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda holds a father can override even an established presumption; the Rabbis hold his credibility is limited to cases where no prior presumption exists about who is the firstborn.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
At the tax house, if someone first says 'He is my son' and then says 'He is my slave,' which statement is credible?
A. The first statement — 'He is my son'
B. Both statements carry equal evidentiary weight
C. Neither statement is credible since they contradict each other
D. The latter statement — 'He is my slave'
Correct: D — At the tax house the latter statement is credible because we assume the first declaration ('my son') was made solely to evade taxes, not as a genuine halakhic claim — so the reversal stands.
