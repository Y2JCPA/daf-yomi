📐 The Fourth Boundary Problem

Seller writes bill of sale with three boundaries but omits the fourth
↓
Rav
Buyer acquires the field except one furrow along the missing boundary side
Rav Asi
Seller withheld the furrow AND also retains the entire fourth side — entire sale is void
↓
Rava rules: Follow Rav — buyer gets all except the one furrow (or, per alternate version, gets all including furrow)
↓
Row of trees OR ≥9 kav area
Furrow is legally significant — seller retains it
No row of trees AND <9 kav
Furrow is insignificant — buyer acquires it too

🏠 Selling 'My Property'

מוכר לך נכסיי — אפילו בתים ועבדים במשמע
Ruling If a seller says "I am selling you my property" without further specification, this includes even his houses and Canaanite slaves. The sweeping language conveys the full estate.

🔺 The Triangular Plot (Rosh Tor)

Rav Kahana & Rav Asi
When one boundary line is drawn longer than the opposite side, shouldn't the buyer also acquire the triangular corner plot (rosh tor) formed by the diagonal connecting the two unequal ends?
Rav
[Silence — no response given.] The question is left unresolved.
Rav (concedes)
Where the boundary is defined by named neighbors (Reuven & Shimon on one side, Levi & Yehuda on the other), and the seller delineates those names accurately, the named owners' fields define the limits precisely.
Key Term Rosh Tor (ראש תור) — literally 'dove's head,' the triangular gap created when two opposite boundary lines are of unequal length.

🌳 When Does the Withheld Furrow Matter?

Furrow IS Legally Significant

  • A row of trees stands on the furrow, OR
  • The strip is large enough to sow nine kav of seed
  • → Seller retains this strip
  • → It is a meaningful portion of land

Furrow Is NOT Significant

  • No row of trees on the strip, AND
  • Area is less than nine kav
  • → Seller's 'retention' is legally void
  • → Buyer acquires the furrow as well

🤝 Partnership Land & The Halving Boundary

Rabba
If a co-owner says 'I sell you the half I have in this land,' he sells his entire share. But if he says 'I sell you half of my half,' he sells only a quarter of the whole field.
Abaye
When Rabba fell silent to my challenge, I thought he conceded. But later I saw bills of sale issued from Rabba's own court using language I had questioned — so he did not retract.
Rabba
If the bill of sale says the fourth boundary is 'the land through which the field is halved,' the seller has sold exactly half the field — the language itself demarcates the midpoint.
Abaye
What is the difference between writing that and the ordinary missing-boundary case you ruled on differently?
Rabba
[Silence.] The Sages inferred Abaye understood Rabba's silence as acceptance of the question's force.
Takeaway Precise boundary language ('halved by this line') can itself constitute a demarcation, making the field division self-defining.
