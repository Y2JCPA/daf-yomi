Bava Batra 8 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak rebuked Rav Naḥman bar Rav Ḥisda for taxing scholars, citing three sections of Tanakh. Which verse from Ketuvim (Writings) did he use?
A. Psalms 1:2 — 'His Torah he meditates day and night'
B. Ezra 7:24 — 'It shall not be lawful to impose tribute, impost or toll upon them'
C. Daniel 12:3 — 'They who are wise shall shine like the brightness of the firmament'
D. Proverbs 3:9 — 'Honor God from your wealth'
Correct: B — Ezra 7:24 exempts Temple priests and Levites from taxation; the Gemara applies this to Torah scholars as well, since they serve as Israel's spiritual guardians.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the critical difference between the Kuppah and the Tamḥui regarding who may receive from them?
A. Kuppah serves poor of the world; Tamḥui serves poor of the city
B. Kuppah serves poor of the city; Tamḥui serves poor of the world
C. Both serve only residents of the city
D. Kuppah serves travelers; Tamḥui serves permanent residents
Correct: B — The Kuppah distributes money weekly to the local city poor. The Tamḥui distributes food daily to any poor person — including travelers from anywhere in the world.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How long must one reside in a city before being obligated to contribute to the Kuppah (charity fund)?
A. 30 days
B. 3 months
C. 6 months
D. 12 months
Correct: B — The baraita lays out a graduated schedule: 30 days → tamḥui; 3 months → kuppah; 6 months → clothing fund; 9 months → burial fund; 12 months → full city member.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why was Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi initially distressed after feeding the visitor during the drought, and why was he later consoled?
A. He gave too much food; he was consoled when he saw others had enough
B. He thought he fed an ignoramus; he was consoled to learn it was his student Yonatan ben Amram who refused to benefit from Torah honor
C. He violated the Shabbat; he was consoled by a ruling that it was permitted
D. He was criticized by the Sages; they later retracted their criticism
Correct: B — Yonatan ben Amram had disguised his identity and asked to be fed 'like a dog or crow.' Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi feared he had violated his own policy, but learned the man was his student — who never accepted honor from the Torah.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan's teaching cited in the Gemara, which form of suffering is the most severe?
A. Death by sword
B. Plague
C. Famine
D. Exile
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan teaches: sword is worse than natural death (because one suffers before dying); famine is worse than the sword (because it causes even greater prolonged suffering). This hierarchy explains why redeeming captives — saving them from sword and potential famine — is a supreme mitzva.
