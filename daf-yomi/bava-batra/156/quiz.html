Bava Batra 156 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why do the Sages void a minor's sale even when he received MORE than market value for the property?
A. A minor can never comprehend monetary value regardless of the price
B. Buyers may rattle dinars to tempt him, so the Sages applied a blanket rule
C. A minor's sale is only void when the price is below market value
D. Parental consent is required for any transaction involving a minor
Correct: B — The Sages recognized that children are inherently attracted to money. A blanket rule of invalidity prevents exploitation — if some sales were valid, buyers would devise ways to tempt minors with coins to secure deals at any price.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Until what age may a person NOT sell property inherited from his father, according to Rav Naḥman?
A. 13 years old
B. 16 years old
C. 18 years old
D. 20 years old
Correct: D — Rav Naḥman rules that selling inherited property requires being 20 years old. Moreover, the 20th year itself counts as 'before the time' — the right to sell only becomes effective after fully completing that year.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How did the man from Meron successfully transfer his movable property to various recipients?
A. He gave a verbal gift before witnesses in the Temple courtyard
B. He wrote a detailed deed listing every animal and barrel individually
C. He acquired a small plot of rocky land and bundled the movables with shares of it
D. He appointed a court-appointed agent to distribute property after his death
Correct: C — Movables cannot be transferred by deed or verbal gift alone. He purchased rocky land near Jerusalem and assigned portions to each recipient with movables attached — since movables can be acquired alongside land, the transfers were legally valid.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Eliezer, what is the rule for a shkhiv mera who wishes to give gifts on Shabbat?
A. He must wait until after Shabbat and execute a written deed
B. His verbal statement stands — the Sages enacted this lest distress worsen his condition
C. He may give gifts only through a court-appointed guardian on Shabbat
D. His gifts are void on Shabbat regardless of circumstances
Correct: B — Rabbi Eliezer rules that a deathbed person's verbal statement on Shabbat is valid. Since he cannot write, the Sages enacted verbal declaration as sufficient — the motivation is compassion, preventing the distress of an unfulfilled final wish.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does the halakha (following Mar Zutra) rule about someone who is not yet fit to sell inherited land?
A. He is also disqualified from inheriting land until he reaches the required age
B. He must have a guardian appointed for all financial transactions
C. He is also not fit to serve as a witness in land transactions
D. He may receive land as a gift even if he lacks capacity to sell it
Correct: C — Mar Zutra's ruling — accepted as halakha — links selling capacity to testimonial capacity: one who lacks the legal standing to sell land also lacks the standing to testify about land transactions. The two disabilities travel together.
