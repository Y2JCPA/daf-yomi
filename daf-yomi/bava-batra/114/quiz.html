5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabba, until when may a party renege on a transaction?
A. Until the object of sale physically changes hands
B. As long as both parties remain seated at the same location
C. Until they arise from their seats
D. Until a third-party witness has observed the deal
Correct: B — Rabba holds that while the parties remain seated at the location of the transaction, either may still withdraw. Remaining seated signals the transaction is still in process; leaving (or arising, per Rav Yosef) seals it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What act does Rav Yosef identify as the moment a transaction becomes irrevocable?
A. When the buyer physically lifts the item (hagbahah)
B. When a kinyan sudar (handkerchief exchange) is performed
C. When the parties arise from their seats
D. When the price is verbally agreed upon
Correct: C — Rav Yosef holds that the act of rising from one's seat is the decisive moment — once the parties stand up, neither can renege. This is supported by his reading of Rav Yehuda's sick-room ruling.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why are the three men who witnessed the sick person's will at night disqualified from judging any dispute about it the next day?
A. Because nighttime testimony is invalid in Jewish law
B. Because a minimum of five judges is required for inheritance cases
C. Because they are considered a 'court that saw' — their prior knowledge disqualifies them from impartial judgment
D. Because witnesses and judges must always be entirely different people in all cases
Correct: C — They witnessed the sick person's intentions firsthand, making them a beit din she-ra'u (a court that already saw the matter). This prior knowledge creates bias and disqualifies them from adjudicating disputes about the will.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the mishna need to state BOTH that a woman bequeaths to her son AND to her husband?
A. Because the laws for sons and husbands come from different Torah verses
B. To teach that both cases share the same limitation: neither inherits property that comes to her estate after her death
C. Because daughters are excluded while sons are included, requiring explicit statement
D. To establish that husbands and sons have equal rights to her estate
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that stating both cases teaches their structural parallel: just as a husband does not inherit property arriving posthumously to her estate, the same limitation applies to the son. One case alone would not have revealed this shared rule.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan citing Rabbi Yehuda ben Rabbi Shimon, under what condition does a mother inherit from her son?
A. Always — a mother has a primary Torah claim on her son's estate
B. Only when the son left no written will naming another heir
C. Only when the father is not alive, since the father's claim takes precedence
D. Only when the son died childless and without brothers
Correct: C — By Torah law the father's right to inherit from his son precedes the mother's. A mother can only inherit from her son in the absence of the father — the father's claim is primary and supersedes hers.
