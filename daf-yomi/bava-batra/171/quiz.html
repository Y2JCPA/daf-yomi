Bava Batra 171 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda, what happens when a debtor repays half of a 1,000-dinar debt?
A. A receipt is written for 500 dinars while the original note is kept intact
B. The original note is torn and a new note is written for 500 dinars, dated from the original date
C. The original note is postdated to reflect only the remaining balance
D. Nothing is written; the debtor's oral acknowledgment suffices
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda holds that the original promissory note is torn and a new note is written for the remaining balance, backdated to the original date to preserve the creditor's lien on previously owned property.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rav Hamnuna rule that postdated bills of sale are invalid, even though postdated promissory notes are permitted?
A. Bills of sale are governed by stricter biblical law than loans
B. A postdated bill of sale could allow a creditor to seize land from a buyer who acquired it after the real (but before the written) sale date
C. Courts have no way to verify the actual date of a property sale
D. A postdated bill of sale enables double collection of the purchase price
Correct: B — If a deed is backdated, the field appears to have been in the seller's possession during the intervening period, allowing the seller's creditors to seize it from the innocent buyer. No equivalent harm arises from postdated promissory notes (per Rabbi Yehuda's system).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which case proved that a receipt is written even when the entire debt — not just a portion — is repaid?
A. Rav Yehuda's ruling on the deed of gift with no guarantee clause
B. Rav Hamnuna's invalidation of postdated bills of sale
C. Rav Yitzchak bar Yosef's debt to Rabbi Abba, adjudicated by Rabbi Ḥanina bar Pappi
D. Rav Safra's instruction to his court regarding the 'paid' annotation
Correct: C — When Rav Yitzchak bar Yosef came to collect from Rabbi Abba, Rabbi Ḥanina bar Pappi presided and ruled that a receipt should be written even for full repayment — directly contradicting Rav Huna b. Rav Yehoshua's limitation.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What practical solution did Rav Safra propose to prevent double collection of a debt after a receipt is written?
A. Require two additional witnesses to countersign the receipt
B. Insist the receipt be written only by a rabbinic court, never by private witnesses
C. Have the court write 'this note has been paid' directly on the original promissory note
D. Destroy both the original note and the receipt simultaneously in front of witnesses
Correct: C — Rav Safra instructed his court to write 'paid' (פרוע) directly on the promissory note itself, rendering the original document permanently unenforceable and eliminating any risk of double collection.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Pedat, on what point do Rabbi Yehuda and Rabbi Yosei actually agree regarding promissory notes dated on Shabbat or Yom Kippur?
A. Both agree such notes are valid if a rabbinic court retroactively approves them
B. Both agree such notes are self-evidently postdated and are therefore invalid
C. Both agree such notes are presumed antedated rather than postdated
D. Both agree such notes are valid because the lender acted in good faith
Correct: B — Rabbi Pedat explains that all agree a note visibly dated on Shabbat or Yom Kippur is obviously postdated and thus invalid. Their actual dispute concerns postdated notes whose false dating is not immediately apparent from the document itself.
