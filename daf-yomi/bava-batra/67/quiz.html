Bava Batra 67 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Sages, when one sells a 'courtyard,' which of the following IS included in the sale?
A. Movable property stored in the courtyard
B. Outer houses that open directly into the courtyard
C. Stores that open only to the public domain
D. The seller's personal belongings
Correct: B — The Sages hold that outer houses accessible from the courtyard, plus pits, ditches, and caves, are all sold with the courtyard. Movable property is explicitly excluded by the mishna.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rabbi Eliezer's position on the sale of a courtyard?
A. All houses and outbuildings are included in the sale
B. Only the outer houses are included, not inner ones
C. Only the airspace of the courtyard is sold — no houses at all
D. Stores opening to the public domain are included but not inner houses
Correct: C — Rabbi Eliezer takes the most restrictive view: selling a 'courtyard' conveys only its airspace. None of the houses or structures are included, since they would need to be mentioned explicitly.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rabba cites Rav Naḥman's ruling about a sand field and a pond sold together. What is the ruling?
A. Possessing the sand field does NOT acquire the pond since they have different uses
B. Possessing the sand field DOES acquire the pond, as they were sold together as a unit
C. Each property requires a separate act of acquisition regardless of how they were sold
D. The ruling depends on whether the two properties are geographically adjacent
Correct: B — Rabba's final version of Rav Naḥman's ruling is that taking possession of the sand field acquires the pond as well, since they were sold together. The Gemara initially questioned this but ultimately accepts it.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the sale of an olive press, which element does Rabbi Eliezer say IS sold (unlike the Sages)?
A. The galgal (wheel), because it is the primary moving part
B. The avirim (movable boards), because they are stored on-site
C. The korah (heavy wooden beam), because it defines the olive press
D. The yam (lentil basin), which both agree is sold
Correct: C — Rabbi Eliezer holds that the korah (heavy beam) is sold because the press is called 'beit habad' on account of this beam — it is the most essential, defining element. The Sages classify it as movable and exclude it.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the mishna, when does a seller of a bathhouse transfer the movable furnishings (boards, basins, curtains) to the buyer?
A. Always — movable items in a bathhouse are always included in the sale
B. Never — movable items can never be transferred in a real estate sale
C. Only when the seller explicitly says 'it and all that is within it'
D. Only when the buyer takes physical possession before the seller removes the items
Correct: C — The mishna states that the boards, basins, and curtains (habilaniyot) are NOT sold with the bathhouse by default. They transfer only when the seller includes them explicitly with a phrase like 'it and all that is within it.'
