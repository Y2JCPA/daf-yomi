Why Is the Anointed Priest Excluded from the Provisional Guilt-Offering?

Gemara
The baraita says all agree the anointed priest doesn't bring a provisional guilt-offering (asham talui). From where?
R' Yehuda HaNasi
Leviticus 5:18 says 'for his unwitting act that he performed unwittingly' — this excludes one whose sin-offerings are not all for unwitting acts alone.
R' Yehuda HaNasi
The anointed priest requires he'elem davar (absence of awareness leading to erroneous ruling) — except for idol worship, where a simple unwitting act suffices.
Rabbanan
We agree on the verse, but the anointed priest requires he'elem davar for all transgressions — including idol worship. So he is fully excluded.
Gemara
But the verse only says 'his unwitting act' — not 'all his unwitting acts'?
Gemara
The seemingly redundant phrase 'that he performed unwittingly' teaches: liability exists only when all one's sin-offerings are for unwitting acts.
Core Dispute R' Yehuda HaNasi: The priest's idol worship offering is for a simple unwitting act (like an individual). Rabbanan: Even idol worship requires he'elem davar for the priest.

Who Is Liable and How Is It Derived?

The Court (Sanhedrin)

  • Gezera shava: 'that' (Lev 4:14 ↔ Lev 18:18)
  • Only for karet + fixed sin-offering offenses
  • Brings a bull as communal sin-offering

Anointed Priest (Kohen Mashiach)

  • Derived from: 'so as to bring guilt upon the people' (Lev 4:3)
  • Status equated to the public's transgression
  • Same karet + sin-offering threshold applies

The King (Melekh)

  • Verbal analogy: 'commandments' (Lev 4:22 ↔ Lev 4:13)
  • King's 'commandments' = public's 'commandments'
  • Same karet + sin-offering threshold applies
Principle All three — court, anointed priest, and king — share the same threshold of liability, each derived through a distinct textual link back to the communal paradigm.

Can We Derive Verbal Analogies from Different Conjugations?

Sages
But 've'ashem' (and he shall be guilty) is also written regarding the sliding-scale offering (Lev 5:5)! So the analogy should apply there too!
Gemara
We derive 've'ashemu' (they are guilty) from 've'ashem' (he is guilty) — identical conjugation. But not from 'ye'sham' (he shall be guilty) — a different form.
Sages
But the school of R' Yishmael derived a gezera shava between 'veshav' (return) and 'uva' (come) for leprosy — completely different words!
Rav Pappa
The real derivation uses two phrases together: 'and he is guilty' + 'commandments of the Lord' — this combination appears for the provisional guilt-offering and the communal offering, but not for the sliding-scale offering.
Rav Shimi bar Ashi
But we could also link 'and he is guilty and bearing of iniquity' (Lev 5:1) to the same phrase in Lev 5:17!
Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak
The correct derivation requires a different combination of terms to avoid these objections.
Takeaway Rav Pappa resolves the challenge by requiring two matching phrases — 've'ashem' AND 'mitzvot Hashem' — to establish the verbal analogy, excluding the sliding-scale offering which lacks the second phrase.
