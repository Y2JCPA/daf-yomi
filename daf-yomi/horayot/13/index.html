Female Goat vs. Ewe — Which Takes Precedence?

Tanna 1
The female goat takes precedence — it has increased applicability, since it is also brought for individual idol worship
Tanna 2
The ewe takes precedence — it has more sacrificial portions (its tail is also offered on the altar)
Abaye
This is a dispute between Tanna'im — each values a different criterion of superiority
Insight The dispute reflects a deeper question: Is broader applicability or greater sanctity the better measure of an offering's importance?

Man vs. Woman: When Each Takes Precedence

Man Precedes Woman

  • Rescue from life-threatening danger
  • Returning a lost item
  • Release from captivity when both face degradation

Woman Precedes Man

  • Providing clothing — her humiliation is greater
  • Release from captivity — concern for rape
  • Unless both face degradation, then man first
Key Factor The Mishna balances dignity, vulnerability, and immediate risk in determining who takes precedence

Five Things That Cause Forgetting Torah — and Five That Restore It

❌ Causes of Forgetting:
• Eating food nibbled by a mouse or cat
• Eating an animal's heart
• Habitual olive consumption
• Drinking wash-water remnants
• Washing feet one atop the other
↓
Can the damage be reversed?
↓
✅ Restores Forgotten Torah:
• Bread baked on coals
• Hard-boiled egg without salt
• Olive oil (not olives!)
• Wine and smelling spices
• Water from kneading dough
R' Yochanan Olives cause one to forget 70 years of Torah, but olive oil restores 70 years of Torah study
