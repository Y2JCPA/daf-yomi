Follow the time of awareness — status at the time the sin becomes known
Rabbis

KEY THEME This daf explores how changes in a person's halakhic status — from commoner to king, or Nasi to commoner — affect their obligation in sin-offerings, and whether the Nasi of Eretz Yisrael qualifies as a 'king.'

Precedence in Mitzvot: Lot's Daughters

לעולם יקדים אדם לדבר מצוה
MORAL PRINCIPLE R' Ḥiyya bar Avin in the name of R' Yehoshua ben Korḥa: A person should always be first to perform a mitzvah.

The elder daughter of Lot, who acted first, merited that Ruth the Moabite (ancestor of King David) preceded Naamah the Ammonite (wife of Solomon) by four generations of royalty.

Status Change Mid-Transgression

A commoner eats half an olive-bulk of forbidden fat (ḥelev)
↓
He is appointed king
↓
He eats the second half olive-bulk of ḥelev
↓
Do the two halves combine?
They Combine
He brings a ewe or female goat (commoner's offering)
They Don't Combine
King's status is different — the halves cannot join; he is exempt entirely
RAVA'S RULING The verse 'from among the common people' teaches that a king's status is distinct — the half olive-bulk eaten as a commoner does NOT combine with the half eaten as king (Rav Zevid in the name of Rava).

Does Kingship Interpose Even After Removal?

Rava
If a commoner ate half an olive-bulk, was appointed king, then removed, then ate the second half — do they combine?
Rava
Perhaps since both halves were eaten as a commoner, they combine?
Rava
Or perhaps once his status changed, the two halves can never combine?
Gemara
Attempt to resolve from Ulla/R' Yoḥanan: An apostate who designated an offering, then apostatized, then repented — he remains disqualified from that offering.
Gemara
Rejected: An apostate is ineligible for any offering; a king is eligible — just for a different type.
UNRESOLVED The dilemma of whether kingship interposes between two halves of a transgression even after the king is removed remains unresolved.

Is the Nasi of Eretz Yisrael a 'King'?

R' Yehuda HaNasi
If I sin unwittingly, do I bring a male goat like a king, or a ewe/female goat like a commoner?
R' Ḥiyya
Your rival, the Exilarch in Babylonia, is as great as you — so you are not akin to a king.
R' Yehuda HaNasi
But kings of Israel and kings of the House of David both brought king's offerings — even though they had counterparts!
R' Ḥiyya
Those kings were not subject to each other's authority. Here, we in Eretz Yisrael are subject to the Exilarch's authority.
SCEPTER vs. STAFF The Exilarch holds a scepter (שבט) — authority backed by the government. The Nasi holds a staff (מחוקק) — teaching Torah publicly but without governmental enforcement power (Genesis 49:10).

Types of High Priest (Mishna)

Anointed with Oil

  • Anointed with the sacred anointing oil (שמן המשחה)
  • Brings a bull for transgressing any mitzvah based on his erroneous ruling
  • Full status of High Priest

Consecrated by Garments

  • Served after the anointing oil was sequestered (end of First Temple)
  • Does NOT bring the bull for transgression of mitzvot
  • Otherwise has full High Priest status
MISHNA The only practical difference between an oil-anointed High Priest and a garment-consecrated one is the bull offering for unwitting transgression of mitzvot.
