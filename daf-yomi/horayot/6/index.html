How Do You Get 12 Sin-Offerings?

R' Yehuda
Each of the 12 tribes that sinned brings its own goat. Or even if only 7 sinned — the minority is drawn after the majority.
R' Shimon
If 11 tribes sinned, they bring 11 goats, plus one goat from the court (בית דין) — totaling 12.
Gemara
But according to R' Meir, only the court brings an offering (one bull). How do you reach 12?
Gemara
They sinned 12 separate times — each time requiring a new bull from the court.
CONTEXT Ezra 8:35 records that the returnees from Babylonia brought 12 goats as sin-offerings — the Gemara explains how each opinion accounts for this number.

Sin-Offering Whose Owner Died: Individual vs. Congregation

Rav Pappa: The rule that a sin-offering whose owner died must be left to die applies only to individuals
↓
Why? Because אין מיתה בציבור — there is no 'death' for a congregation
↓
What is the source for this principle?
❌ Psalms 45:17
"Instead of your fathers shall be your sons" — but this would apply to individuals too!
❌ New Moon Goat
Paid from treasury though some contributors died — but maybe no one died
✅ Egla Arufa
Deut. 21:8 — "whom You redeemed" atones even for the Exodus generation
OBJECTION The Gemara challenges: the egla arufa works because living residents are present. Were any from Zedekiah's era still alive? Yes — Ezra 3:12 records elders who saw the First Temple weeping.

Which Groups Count as a 'Congregation' (קהל)?

Counts as Congregation

  • Tribes with ancestral land (נחלה)
  • Reuben, Simeon, Judah, etc.
  • Ephraim & Manasseh — counted as two (Gen. 48:5)
  • Source: "I will make of you a congregation of peoples" (Gen. 48:4)

Does NOT Count

  • Tribe of Levi — no ancestral land
  • Priests (Kohanim) — part of Levi
  • Rav Aḥa b. R' Yaakov: only tribes with inheritance = congregation
  • Without Levi: 11 tribes → Ephraim + Manasseh restore the count to 12
RAVA'S CHALLENGE Gen. 48:6 says Ephraim and Manasseh are called by their brethren's name "in their inheritance" — perhaps only for inheritance, not other matters? The Gemara resolves: banners and princes were for honorary purposes only.

Ephraim & Manasseh: Two Tribes or One?

Abaye
Genesis 48:5 — "Ephraim and Manasseh shall be like Reuben and Simeon to me" — they are two full tribes.
Rava
But Gen. 48:6 says "in their inheritance" — they are separate only for land, not other matters!
Gemara
But they had separate banners (דגלים) in the wilderness — Manasseh, Ephraim, Benjamin under one banner!
Gemara
That was to honor the banners — needing three tribes per banner. Same for separate princes (נשיאים).
Baraita
Moses set 12 days of Tabernacle dedication to honor each prince — Ephraim and Manasseh were split for honor, not legal status.
RESOLUTION The separate treatment of Ephraim and Manasseh in banners and princes was ceremonial. Their status as two tribes for congregational purposes derives specifically from their inheritance of separate ancestral lands.
