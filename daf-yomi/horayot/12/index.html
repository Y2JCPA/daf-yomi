How Kings vs. Priests Are Anointed

Kings 👑

  • Oil smeared in the shape of a crown (כמין נזר) around the head
  • Anointed only upon a spring (מעיין) — an omen for enduring kingship
  • Source: Solomon anointed at Gihon (I Kings 1:33–34)

Priests ✋

  • Oil smeared in shape of Greek letter chi (כמין כי)
  • Rav Menashya bar Gadda: Like the Greek equivalent of the Hebrew letter כ (kaf)
  • Dispute: Pour on head first, or anoint between the eyes first?
Key Distinction The crown shape for kings symbolizes sovereignty; the chi/kaf shape for priests reflects their unique consecration for Temple service.

Order of Priestly Anointing: Pour or Smear First?

Tanna 1
Pouring first — as it says: 'He poured the anointing oil on Aaron's head and anointed him' (Lev. 8:12). The verse lists pouring before anointing.
Tanna 2
Anointing (smearing) first — because the service vessels were anointed but oil was never poured on them (Num. 7:1). Anointing is the primary act.
Tanna 1
But the verse explicitly says 'he poured' and then 'anointed'!
Tanna 2
Reread the verse: Why did he pour? Because he had already anointed. The pouring follows the anointing — anointing is the עיקר (primary component).
Takeaway The dispute turns on whether the verse in Leviticus describes chronological order or explains causation.

Aaron's Miraculous Oil Drops

כשמן הטוב על הראש יורד על הזקן זקן אהרן (תהלים קלג:ב)
Aggadic Passage Two drops of anointing oil, shaped like pearls, hung from Aaron's beard. When he spoke and his beard moved, the drops miraculously rose to settle at the roots so they wouldn't fall.

Moses worried: 'Perhaps I misused consecrated oil — extra drops remain!'
🔊 Bat Kol: 'Like the dew of Hermon' (Ps. 133:3) — just as dew is not consecrated, there is no me'ila.

Aaron worried: 'Perhaps I misused it, since the oil is on my beard and I benefit!'
🔊 Bat Kol: 'How good and pleasant for brothers to dwell together' (Ps. 133:1) — just as Moses did not commit me'ila, neither did you.
