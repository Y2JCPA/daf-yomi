Horayot 2: Who Bears Responsibility for a Court's Error?

Court issues erroneous ruling permitting a Torah violation
↓
Did the individual rely on the court's ruling?
Yes — relied on court
Exempt from offering
Associates action with court
No — knew they erred
Liable to bring offering

Key Principle One who associates his action with himself is liable; one who associates his action with the court is exempt.

↓
An individual transgresses based on this ruling
↓

No — relied on court
✅ Exempt
Whether judges acted with him, before him, or didn't act at all
Yes — judge/qualified student
❌ Liable
He did not truly associate his action with the court
Mishna The three scenarios (acted together, after, or alone) all yield the same result — what matters is whether the individual genuinely relied on the court.

What Constitutes a Complete Ruling? (Version 1)

Shmuel
The court is liable only when they say: "It is permitted for you" (muttar lakhem).
Rav Dimi of Neharde'a
They must say: "It is permitted for you to perform this action" (muttar lakhem la'asot).
Rav Dimi's Reasoning
Saying only 'it is permitted' might be a theoretical opinion, not an actionable ruling.
Abaye
Proof for Rav Dimi: A rebellious elder is exempt if he merely teaches, but liable only if he instructs others to act.
R' Abba
Proof: The court told a woman she may marry, but she is liable if she was promiscuous — they only permitted a specific act.
Ravina
Proof from our Mishna: "issued a ruling to violate" implies instruction to perform a specific action.
Note The Gemara presents a second version where Shmuel and Rav Dimi's positions are reversed, and the same proofs are used to challenge Rav Dimi.

Why Does the Mishna Say 'Unwittingly'?

Gemara asks
If he acted on the court's ruling, it's obviously unwitting — why state it explicitly?
Rava
It includes a case where the court permitted forbidden fat (ḥelev), and the fat got mixed up with permitted fat.
Rava (cont.)
He ate the ḥelev thinking it was permitted fat — not relying on the court's ruling at all. Still exempt.
Rava (alt. version)
Alternatively: 'Unwittingly' means he forgot the court's ruling entirely and just thought the act was permitted on his own — and he's liable.
Key Distinction 'On the basis of the court's ruling' = exempt. Acting on one's own mistaken assumption = liable, even if a court ruling exists.

Who Is 'Qualified to Issue Halakhic Rulings'?

Judge Who Knew

  • A member of the court who dissented
  • Both learned (gamir) and analytical (savir)
  • Knew the ruling was wrong but was outvoted
  • If he transgresses anyway — liable

Qualified Student

  • Example: Shimon ben Azzai or Shimon ben Zoma
  • Outstanding scholars who were not ordained
  • Per Rava: even one who is learned but not analytical
  • If he transgresses anyway — liable
Abaye's Challenge A scholar of Ben Azzai's caliber would not truly err — he'd be an intentional sinner! Answer: He erred about the mitzva to heed the Sages, believing he must follow them even when certain they're wrong.
