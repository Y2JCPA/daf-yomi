Horayot 14 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did R' Yehuda HaNasi initially refuse to mention Rabbi Meir by name?
A. Rabbi Meir's teachings were considered unreliable
B. Rabbi Meir had sought to undermine R' Yehuda HaNasi's family honor
C. It was forbidden to quote teachers who had passed away
D. Rabbi Meir had been excommunicated by the Sanhedrin
Correct: B — R' Yehuda HaNasi explained that these were people who sought to abolish the honor of his son and his father's house, which is why he avoided naming them.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What argument convinced R' Yehuda HaNasi to finally credit Rabbi Meir?
A. A heavenly voice commanded it
B. The Sages voted to require proper attribution
C. His son Rabban Shimon argued that their actions were not effective
D. Rabbi Meir's students threatened to leave the yeshiva
Correct: C — Rabban Shimon argued that the concern about mentioning enemies applies only when their actions were effective. Since these Sages' efforts to undermine the family were unsuccessful, their names could be mentioned.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Eretz Yisrael rule that 'Sinai' takes precedence over 'Oker Harim'?
A. Analysis without sources leads to heresy
B. Sinai was older and therefore had seniority
C. Everyone requires the 'owner of the wheat' — the expert in sources
D. Sharp analysis is only useful in court, not in the yeshiva
Correct: C — The Gemara explains the ruling with the principle that 'everyone requires the owner of the wheat' (marei chitaya) — meaning that broad, authoritative knowledge of sources is the foundation upon which all scholarship depends.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How did Rav Yosef demonstrate his deference to Rabba during Rabba's 22-year tenure?
A. He never attended Rabba's lectures
B. He never issued any halachic rulings
C. He never even called a bloodletter to his home
D. He left Babylonia for Eretz Yisrael
Correct: C — Rav Yosef would not even summon a bloodletter to his own home — he went to the bloodletter himself. This showed he refused to assume even the smallest air of authority that might rival Rabba's position.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How was Abaye selected as head of his study group?
A. He was the eldest member of the group
B. He was the only one whose statement was not refuted
C. Rava nominated him after a vote
D. He received a letter of appointment from Eretz Yisrael
Correct: B — The group agreed that whoever could state a teaching that was not refuted would become head. Everyone else's statements were refuted, but Abaye's remained standing, and Rabba recognized him by calling him 'Naḥmani' and inviting him to lecture.
