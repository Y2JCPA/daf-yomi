Horayot 9 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is the king excluded from the sliding-scale offering?
A. Because the Torah explicitly lists him as exempt
B. Because he cannot come to a state of poverty
C. Because he brings a bull instead
D. Because his sins are judged by the Sanhedrin
Correct: B — The sliding-scale offering presupposes that the person can experience poverty ('if his means suffice not'). The king, who has only God above him and is greater than the nation, cannot become poor.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Akiva, why is the anointed priest exempt from ALL tiers of the sliding-scale offering?
A. Because his daily meal-offering replaces all other obligations
B. Because 'from one of these' links all tiers — exempt from one means exempt from all
C. Because the High Priest only sins through erroneous rulings
D. Because his brethren bring offerings on his behalf
Correct: B — Rava explains R' Akiva's reasoning: Since the anointed priest cannot bring the tenth-ephah meal-offering (extreme poverty tier), the verse 'from one of these' teaches that inability to bring one tier exempts from all tiers.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What happens if the High Priest does not have personal wealth?
A. He is disqualified from serving
B. He brings a poor person's offering
C. His brethren provide him with wealth
D. The Temple treasury covers his expenses
Correct: C — The Gemara derives from 'the priest that is greatest among his brethren' — make him great from the property of his brethren, who provide him with enough to render him wealthy.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does R' Shimon exempt the anointed priest specifically from tum'at Mikdash liability?
A. Because the High Priest can never become impure
B. Because his Yom Kippur offering (bull) differs from the congregation's (goat)
C. Because tum'at Mikdash requires witnesses
D. Because the High Priest has automatic atonement through daily service
Correct: B — Ḥizkiyya explains that Numbers 19:20 ('excised from the congregation') applies to one whose offering equals the congregation's. The High Priest brings a bull on Yom Kippur while the congregation's atonement comes through a goat — his offering is different.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Yoḥanan, what is the scope of R' Eliezer's ruling that the king brings a goat?
A. It applies to all transgressions in the Torah
B. It applies only to defiling the Temple or its sacrificial foods
C. It applies to all cases where individuals bring a sliding-scale offering
D. It applies only when the king sins intentionally
Correct: B — R' Yoḥanan limits R' Eliezer's ruling to tum'at Mikdash, since karet is stated there as in other fixed sin-offering cases. Rav Pappa supports this by noting that R' Eliezer never says the anointed priest brings a bull for oath-related cases.
