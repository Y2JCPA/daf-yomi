When Is a Court Liable for an Erroneous Ruling?

The Sanhedrin issues an erroneous ruling (hora'ah)
↓
Did they abolish the entire essence of the mitzva?
Yes — Entire Mitzva
Court is exempt — this is not a valid ruling at all
No — Only a Detail
Court is liable to bring a communal sin-offering
KEY PRINCIPLE "Ve'ne'elam davar" — liability exists only when a matter (detail) is hidden, not the entire mitzva.

Twilight Analogy for Sumakhos's Opinion

R' Yosei bar Avin
Sumakhos's case is like one who brings an atonement offering during twilight (bein hashmashot) — it can never be resolved whether it was still day or already night.
Gemara
Since this uncertainty can never be clarified, it is not a typical safek and one does not bring an asham talui (provisional guilt-offering).
R' Yosei bar Avin
Even according to the view that the congregation brings the offering — where the reversal is publicized — if he asks about twilight, no one can answer him.
INSIGHT An asham talui applies only to resolvable doubts. Twilight — an inherently unresolvable uncertainty — does not trigger this obligation.

Ben Azzai vs. Rabbi Akiva: The Overseas Traveler

Ben Azzai's View

  • One who set out to travel but is still in his city
  • He is still physically present — no different from one sitting at home
  • He is liable to bring a sin-offering
  • Proximity to information = responsibility

Rabbi Akiva's View

  • One who set out to travel but is still in his city
  • He is preoccupied with his journey — mentally already gone
  • He is exempt from bringing an offering
  • Mental state and intent matter, not just location
RAVA'S CLARIFICATION The dispute is specifically about one who has set out on the way but hasn't yet left his city — not one already overseas.

Rav Yosef's Dilemma: Plowing on Shabbat

Court rules: No prohibition of plowing on Shabbat
↓
Is this abolishing a detail of Shabbat or the entire essence?
Partial Nullification
Judges accept all other melakhot — plowing is just one category among 39
Total Nullification
Plowing is an av melakha (principal category) — abolishing it entirely may equal abolishing the essence
↓
Gemara attempts resolution from the mishna's examples but rejects each proof — the mishna cases can be read as partial nullifications within each category
UNRESOLVED Does abolishing an entire av melakha (principal labor category) count as abolishing the essence of the mitzva? The Gemara leaves Rav Yosef's dilemma open through multiple attempted proofs.

Rabbi Zeira's Dilemma: Shabbat During Shemitah

R' Zeira
If a court ruled that Shabbat does not apply during the Sabbatical Year — based on misreading "In plowing and in harvest you shall rest" (Exodus 34:21) — what is the law?
Gemara
Since Shabbat is observed during the other six years of the cycle, perhaps this is merely partial nullification?
Gemara
Or perhaps abolishing all of Shabbat for an entire year equals abolishing the entire essence?
Ravina
A baraita about a false prophet teaches: nullifying part of a mitzva while fulfilling the rest is partial nullification. Shabbat in Shemitah is the same — partial nullification.
EXCEPTION For idol worship, even partial nullification ("worship today, revoke tomorrow") is treated as total — all agree the prophet is liable.
