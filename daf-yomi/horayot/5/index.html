Who Brings What When Tribes Sin?

Mishna
The Gemara analyzes who brings offerings when tribes sin based on an erroneous court ruling — and whether the court, the tribes, or both must bring sacrifices.
R' Meir
Only the court brings the bull — the tribes themselves are exempt.
R' Yehuda
Each sinning tribe brings a bull, plus non-sinning tribes also bring one bull each.
R' Shimon
Each sinning tribe brings a bull and the court brings one additional bull.
Gemara
A key dilemma: If one tribe sins based on the High Court's ruling, are the remaining tribes liable? The Gemara resolves that according to R' Yehuda — yes, they are.
Core Theme This daf explores the intersection of communal responsibility and judicial error — who bears the burden of atonement when a court's mistake leads tribes astray?

The Sin Must Be 'Known'

וְנוֹדְעָה הַחַטָּאת אֲשֶׁר חָטְאוּ עָלֶיהָ
Baraita Principle From "And the sin that they sinned became known" (Lev. 4:14) — the court must know which specific erroneous ruling caused the sin. If the judges know they erred but forgot which ruling was wrong, they are not liable to bring an offering.

Rav Yehuda said in the name of Rav: This view is not in accordance with Rabbi Eliezer, who holds one is liable even when uncertain which prohibition was violated.

Three Tannaitic Views: 7 Tribes Sin

R' Meir

  • The COURT brings ONE bull
  • The tribes are EXEMPT
  • "Congregation" = court only
  • R' Shimon ben Elazar adds: 6 tribes = majority by population also triggers this

R' Yehuda

  • Each sinning tribe brings a bull (7 bulls)
  • Non-sinning tribes ALSO bring bulls
  • Even innocent tribes atone for sinners
  • "Congregation" = each tribe is its own congregation

R' Shimon

  • 7 sinning tribes bring 7 bulls
  • Court brings 1 additional bull = 8 total
  • For idol worship: 8 bulls AND 8 goats
  • "Congregation" = court + tribes together
Key Dispute The debate hinges on what "congregation" (עֵדָה) means in Leviticus 4:14 — the court alone, the tribes alone, or both together.

Abaye vs. Rava: Source for Court + Congregation

Question
R' Shimon and R' Meir both hold that the ruling depends on the court and the action depends on the congregation. What is their source?
Abaye
From Numbers 15:24 — "If it was performed unwittingly, hidden from the eyes of the congregation" — the action was generated by the court's ruling.
Rava
From Numbers 15:26 — "For all the people it was performed unwittingly" — the unwitting act is shared: court through ruling, people through action.
Gemara
Both verses are needed: One teaches a majority must sin; the other teaches the court need not act — only rule erroneously.
Note These verses are from the idol worship passage, but the Gemara extends them to all transgressions via a gezeirah shavah on "from the eyes of the congregation."
