🌙 The Moon Argues Before the Holy One

Moon
Master of the Universe! אי אפשר לשני מלכים שישתמשו בכתר אחד — Is it possible for two kings to share one crown? Both of us were created equal — this cannot stand!
God
לְכִי וּמַעֲטִי אֶת עַצְמֵךְ — Go and diminish yourself. The greater light shall rule the day; the lesser light shall rule the night.
Moon
Because I said something proper and logical, I should be punished?! What shall I do now — rule by day alongside the sun?
God
Rule the night. Israel will count months and years by you. The righteous will be named after you. And Israel shall bring atonement for Me on Rosh Chodesh.
Divine Atonement The Rosh Chodesh musaf goat is called חַטָּאת לַה׳ — God says 'bring atonement for Me' because He diminished the moon. Unique in the Torah: a korban offered as if God Himself needs kapparah.

🐐 God's Atonement — The Rosh Chodesh Offering

אָמַר הַקָּדוֹשׁ בָּרוּךְ הוּא הָבִיאוּ כַּפָּרָה עָלַי עַל שֶׁמִּעַטְתִּי אֶת הַיָּרֵחַ
חַטָּאת לַה׳ After diminishing the moon, God said to Israel: 'Bring atonement for Me, for I diminished the moon.' This is why the Rosh Chodesh additional offering is uniquely described in the Torah as חַטָּאת לַה׳ — a sin-offering directed at God Himself. No other korban in the Torah is phrased this way. The monthly new moon ceremony thus carries a cosmic weight: it is simultaneously Israel's renewal and a divine acknowledgment of an ancient injustice.

↓

↓
God decrees: "Go and diminish yourself" — the moon becomes the lesser luminary
↓
Moon protests: "Because I said something proper I am punished?!" — the Talmud acknowledges the moon's claim had merit
↓

↓
