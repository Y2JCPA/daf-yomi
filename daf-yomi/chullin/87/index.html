📖 Overview

וְשָׁפַךְ אֶת-דָּמוֹ וְכִסָּהוּ בֶּעָפָר — ויקרא י״ז:י״ג
📜 Core Mitzvah of This Daf Chullin 87 is devoted to Kisui Ha-Dam (כיסוי הדם) — the Torah command (Vayikra 17:13) to cover the blood of a slaughtered chayah (wild animal) or of (bird) with earth. The daf probes the edge cases: Does inadvertent covering by wind count? Can one cover with one's foot accidentally? What materials are valid? And when multiple animals are slaughtered in sequence, does one covering and one blessing suffice for all?

🩸 The Mitzvah: Source, Scope, and Obligation

חיה ועוף — חייבים בכיסוי; בהמה — פטורה
Torah Foundation The Torah commands: "He shall pour out its blood and cover it with earth" (Lev. 17:13). This applies exclusively to chayot (wild animals: deer, gazelle, etc.) and ofot (birds) — not to domesticated animals (behemot). The blood must be covered both below (a bed of earth placed before slaughter) and above (earth placed on top after). The obligation rests primarily on the shochet (slaughterer). If the shochet neglects it, others may perform it — but the shochet forfeits the mitzvah and may be fined by the beit din.

🌬️ Inadvertent Covering: Does Intent Matter?

Rav Huna
If the wind blew and covered the blood with dust before the shochet acted — the obligation is discharged b'dieved. He need not cover again.
Rav Chisda
Inadvertent covering by wind does not fulfill the obligation. The verse says 'he shall cover' — implying a conscious human act (מעשה כיסוי). He must cover again.
Gemara
A parallel case: if the shochet covered the blood with his foot while walking — unintentionally — did he fulfill the mitzvah? Same debate applies. Either way, no blessing is ever recited over an inadvertent act.
Conclusion
Even if inadvertent coverage is accepted b'dieved, the blessing (על כיסוי הדם) requires a fully intentional, commanded act. No intent = no blessing.
Key Rule The blessing for kisui ha-dam is recited only when the shochet deliberately covers with valid material. Accidental coverage — by wind or foot — never permits a blessing.

🪣 Valid vs. Invalid Covering Materials

✅ Valid — עפר (Earth-Like)

  • Fine earth / dust (עפר הארץ)
  • Sand
  • Ground spices (fine powder)
  • Sawdust
  • Lime powder (סיד)
  • Fine ash from permitted sources
  • Crumbled soft stone (powder form)

❌ Invalid — Not Called 'Earth'

  • Water — not a granular solid
  • Gold dust — not called 'earth' (afar)
  • Coarse pebbles — do not 'cover' the blood
  • Leaves or vegetation
  • Mud / wet earth — cannot be spread properly
  • Any substance not derived from the ground
  • Material mixed with blood (impure)
