Chullin 132 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Which three body parts must be given as matanot kehuna from a slaughtered animal?
A. Head, lungs, and liver
B. Foreleg, cheeks, and maw
C. Heart, kidneys, and tail fat
D. Tongue, hide, and right thigh
Correct: B — Devarim 18:3 specifies zero'a (foreleg/shoulder), lechayim (cheeks), and keiva (maw/stomach) — these three specific anatomical parts constitute the matanot kehuna.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In a Kohen–Israelite partnership that slaughters an animal together, what does the Gemara conclude regarding matanot?
A. The Kohen's presence exempts the entire animal
B. Only the Kohen owes matanot to another Kohen
C. The Israelite's proportional share remains obligated
D. The animal must be resold before slaughter
Correct: C — Rava rules like Rav Yehuda: the Kohen's co-ownership exempts only his own portion, while the Israelite partner must still give matanot from his proportional share of the animal.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which type of animal is exempt from the matanot kehuna requirement according to the Gemara?
A. An animal slaughtered for a private wedding feast
B. An animal in a Kohen–Israelite partnership
C. An animal slaughtered for the royal table
D. An animal slaughtered in a city with no Kohen present
Correct: C — Animals slaughtered for the royal table (the king's feast) are exempt — since there is no single private owner in the conventional sense, the standard obligation does not attach.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What restrictions govern a Kohen's use of the matanot he receives?
A. He must eat them in a state of ritual purity
B. He may only eat them within the Temple precincts
C. He must consume them before the following dawn
D. No special restrictions — he may eat, sell, or use them freely
Correct: D — Unlike teruma, matanot kehuna carry no inherent kedusha. Once the Kohen receives the zero'a, lechayim, and keiva, he may do with them as he wishes — eat, sell, or even feed to animals.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If an Israelite sells the three gift portions to a butcher before any Kohen arrives to claim them, what is the halachic concern raised by the Amoraim?
A. None — he owned the animal and may sell all parts freely
B. He violates only a rabbinic enactment with no monetary consequence
C. Some authorities consider this gezel (theft) from the Kohen
D. He must bring a korban for intentional transgression
Correct: C — The Gemara debates whether selling the matanot portions before a Kohen claims them constitutes gezel — since the Torah effectively designated those parts for Kohanim from the moment of slaughter, pre-emptive sale may deprive them of what is rightfully theirs.
