Chullin 35 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded

Question 1 of 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi (via Rabbi Yonatan), if someone eats teruma that has third-degree impurity, which of the following is TRUE?
Correct. By eating third-degree impure teruma, the person becomes a second-degree source of impurity. Second-degree impurity disqualifies teruma — so they may not eat other teruma. However, mere contact is permitted since the chain doesn't escalate further through touch in this scenario.
Question 2 of 5
What does the principle "the purity of teruma is impurity of sacrificial food" mean?
The principle means that purity is relative. What counts as "pure enough" for teruma is still considered a form of impurity when measured against the stricter standards of sacrificial food. Each level's ceiling is the next level's floor.
Question 3 of 5
Why does the Gemara say both Ulla's statement AND Rabbi Yonatan's statement were each necessary (tzrikhuta)?
This is a classic tzrikhuta (necessity proof). Without Ulla, we'd not extend the rule to non-sacred food prepared at higher standards. Without R' Yonatan, we'd miss the eating-vs-touching distinction. Each is independently necessary to avoid incorrect inferences from the other.
Question 4 of 5
What is the source in Chagiga that proves the "purity of teruma = impurity of kodesh" principle?
The Mishna in Chagiga 18b illustrates a cascade: an am ha'aretz's garment is midras-impure for a Perush; a Perush's garment is impure for a Kohen eating teruma; a Kohen's garment is impure for someone eating sacrificial food. Each step demonstrates that one level's "pure" is the next level's "impure."
Question 5 of 5
Rava challenges the proof from the Chagiga garment ruling. What is his concern?
Rava's concern is that midras impurity (impurity from an am ha'aretz "treading" on something) is a specific Rabbinic stringency — not the standard Biblical impurity chain. Using it as proof for general principles about impurity cascades may be illegitimate. The Gemara engages seriously with this challenge.
