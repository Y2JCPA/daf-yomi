🏛️ The Three-Tier Impurity Hierarchy

Foundation: Not all food is equally sensitive to ritual impurity. The Torah creates three tiers — and what counts as "pure" at one level is considered "impure" at the next.
Food TypeDisqualified bySensitivity
Regular (Chullin)1st-degree impurity onlyLowest
Teruma2nd-degree impurityMedium
Sacrificial (Kodesh)Even 3rd-degree impurityHighest
Key insight: This isn't just a ladder of strictness — each level's "ceiling of purity" is the next level's "floor of impurity." That principle drives the entire daf.

🍽️ Eating Impure Teruma — What Happens to You?

Rabbi Yonatan quotes Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi: If you eat teruma that has 3rd-degree impurity, you absorb a level of impurity yourself. What are the consequences?
You eat teruma with 3rd-degree impurity
↓
You become a 2nd-degree source of impurity
↓
❌ May NOT eat other teruma
Your 2nd-degree status would disqualify it
✅ May TOUCH other teruma
Mere contact doesn't cascade further at this level

📐 Tzrikhuta: Why Both Statements Were Needed

The Gemara asks: Ulla already taught a similar rule. Why did Rabbi Yonatan need to add his own statement? Each fills a gap the other leaves open.
Ulla
Non-sacred food prepared at sacrificial standards absorbs the impurity cascade rules of that level — even 3rd-degree disqualifies it.
R'YN
If you eat 3rd-degree impure teruma, you may not eat other teruma — but you MAY touch it. The eating/touching distinction matters!
Without Ulla: we'd miss the rule for non-sacred food at higher preparation standards
↓
Without R' Yonatan: we'd miss the eating vs. touching distinction
↓
Both statements are independently necessary — classic tzrikhuta

⚖️ "Purity of Teruma = Impurity of Sacrificial Food"

Rav Yitzchak bar Shmuel bar Marta: Non-sacred food prepared at sacrificial purity standards, if it becomes 3rd-degree impure, renders sacrificial food impure on contact. Why? Because teruma-level purity is itself impurity relative to sacrificial food.
👕 Am ha'aretz garment → impure for a Perush (midras)
↓
👕 Perush's garment → impure for a Kohen eating teruma
↓
👕 Kohen's garment → impure for one eating sacrificial food
↓
Each level's "ceiling" is the next level's "floor" — proved from Chagiga 18b

⚔️ Rava's Challenge: The Midras Problem

Proof
The Chagiga garment ruling proves "purity of teruma = impurity of kodesh." The cascade is clear: each level's clean is the next level's dirty.
Rava
Wait — that ruling involves midras impurity! Midras is a special Rabbinic stringency for am ha'aretz garments. You can't use a Rabbinic decree to prove a general principle about Biblical impurity chains!
The tension: Midras impurity (caused by an am ha'aretz "treading" on something) is uniquely strict — it was specifically decreed to create social separation between scholars and non-scholars. Using it as proof for standard impurity-cascade principles may be illegitimate. The Gemara grapples with this seriously but upholds the core principle.
