Chullin 35

חולין לה
Impurity cascades: eating impure teruma, the "purity of teruma is impurity of sacrificial food" principle, and when non-sacred food changes status

🔄 Recap: The Impurity Hierarchy

Foundation

From the previous daf, three categories with increasing sensitivity to impurity:

Regular (Chullin)

Only first-degree (rishon) impurity disqualifies it

Teruma

Second-degree (sheni) impurity disqualifies it

Sacrificial (Kodesh)

Even third-degree (shlishi) impurity disqualifies it

This hierarchy shapes today's entire discussion.

🍽️ The "Eating Impure Teruma" Problem

New Rule

Rabbi Yonatan quotes Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi: If someone eats teruma that already has third-degree impurity, what happens to them?

  • ✅ Forbidden to eat other teruma — they become a second-degree impurity source, which would disqualify the next teruma
  • ✅ Permitted to merely touch other teruma — mere contact doesn't trigger a new disqualification at this level
"For one who partakes of actual teruma that is impure with third-degree impurity, it is prohibited to partake of other teruma, but it is permitted to come into contact with it."

🔁 Why Both Statements Were Necessary

Ulla vs. R' Yonatan

The Gemara notes that both Ulla's statement (from Daf 34) and Rabbi Yonatan's statement were independently needed:

Without Ulla

We might think R' Yonatan's rule applies only to teruma, not non-sacred food prepared at higher standards

Without R' Yonatan

We might think Ulla's rule applies to all purity-level eating scenarios, missing specific rules about eating vs. touching

Each statement fills a gap the other leaves open. This is a classic Talmudic tzrikhuta (necessity proof).

⚖️ Key Principle: "Purity of Teruma = Impurity of Sacrificial Food"

Major Concept

Rav Yitzchak bar Shmuel bar Marta introduces a ruling: non-sacred food prepared at sacrificial purity standards, if it becomes third-degree impure, renders sacrificial food impure when they come in contact.

Why? Because the purity standard of teruma is itself considered a form of impurity relative to sacrificial food — it doesn't meet the higher bar.

"Leave teruma alone — its state of purity is impurity vis-à-vis sacrificial food."

In other words: what counts as "pure" at one level is "impure" at a higher level.

🏘️ Proof from the Am Ha'aretz Garment Ruling

Source

The Gemara cites a Mishna in Chagiga (18b): the garments of an am ha'aretz (non-scholarly person) carry midras impurity for a Perush (strict observer), but that same garment — at the purity level of a Perush — is impure for a Kohen eating teruma. And at that level, it is impure for those eating sacrificial food.

Each tier's "purity" is the next tier's "impurity." The chain is real and practical.

🎯 Rava's Challenge: The Treading Impurity Problem

Objection

Rava pushes back: can we really derive the "purity of teruma = impurity of kodesh" principle from the garment ruling? That ruling involves midras (treading) impurity — a Rabbinic stringency — not standard Biblical impurity chains.

The Gemara works through this carefully, distinguishing between types of impurity sources and which derivations are valid. The core principle is upheld, but its proof is refined.

📌 Bottom Line

Summary
  • Eating third-degree impure teruma makes you a second-degree source → you may not eat other teruma but may touch it
  • The "purity of teruma is impurity of sacrificial food" principle is real — purity is always relative to the level you're protecting
  • Both Ulla's and R' Yonatan's statements are independently necessary (tzrikhuta)
  • Non-sacred food prepared at sacrificial standards absorbs the cascading impurity rules of that higher level
