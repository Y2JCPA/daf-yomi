🐄 New Topic: The Moribund Animal

The Case: An animal is gravely ill or injured and near death. Someone slaughters it properly. Is the slaughter valid? Can we eat the meat? Or is such an animal already effectively a neveilah — halachically dead even while still breathing?
❓ Moribund animal: halachically "alive" enough for slaughter to work?
↓
Concern: It's effectively a neveilah — slaughter on a "nearly-dead" animal does nothing
Counter: It IS still alive — the Torah only forbids eating an unslaughtered carcass, implying slaughter always works on a living animal

📜 Three Failed Proofs — Then the Real One

The Gemara tries several derivations and rejects each one before finding the real source:
Attempt 1: Infer from "you shall not eat neveilah" → dying animal is permitted by slaughter
↓
❌ Rejected: maybe a dying animal IS a neveilah, already included in the prohibition
↓
Attempt 2: Infer from treifah prohibition → dying animal is separate
↓
❌ Rejected: maybe a dying animal IS a treifah — same problem
↓
Attempt 3: Combine both inferences
↓
❌ Rejected: two uncertain categories can't jointly prove a third
↓
✅ Real source: Leviticus 7:24 — "fat of neveilah AND fat of treifah" distinguishes them as separate categories, implying a dying animal is separate from both and permissible by slaughter

🦵 The Mishna: Three Opinions on Signs of Life

The Mishna teaches: Slaughter of a moribund animal is only valid if the animal demonstrates it was alive at the time. But what counts as proof?

Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel

  • 🦵 Most demanding
  • Must convulse with both foreleg AND hind leg
  • Two-limb proof of coordinated life

The Rabbis

  • 🦵 Moderate standard
  • Convulsion of foreleg OR hind leg
  • Any single-limb coordinated movement

Rabbi Eliezer

  • 🩸 Different type of proof
  • Blood spurting from the neck
  • Arterial pressure proves life

💀 Life Sign vs. Death Spasm — Small Animals

Critical distinction: Not every movement after slaughter counts. Some movements are caused by death itself — muscle relaxation masquerading as life.
MovementRulingReason
Extends bent foreleg AND restores it✅ Valid life signActive muscle contraction required
Extends bent foreleg but does NOT restore❌ Death spasmPassive muscle release — not active life
Bends extended foreleg AND restores✅ Valid life signTwo-directional purposeful movement
Head falls to side only❌ Not sufficientGravity-driven, not muscular
The rule: A genuine sign of life requires purposeful, bidirectional movement — extension AND restoration, or bending AND straightening. A single direction only = death spasm.

🌙 Rabbi Shimon's Night Slaughter Case

Someone slaughters an animal at night — cannot observe post-slaughter convulsions in the dark
↓
The next morning: the walls are splattered with blood
↓
Does this retroactively validate the slaughter as evidence the animal was alive?
↓
✅ Rabbi Shimon: Valid. Blood reaching the walls requires powerful arterial pressure — only possible in a living animal with a beating heart
Significance: This case shows that signs of life need not be directly observed at the moment of slaughter — retroactive physical evidence is accepted. The force of blood spurting is itself a reliable indicator.
