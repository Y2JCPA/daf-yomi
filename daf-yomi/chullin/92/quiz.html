Chullin 92 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Ulla, how many mitzvot did the non-Jewish nations accept upon themselves?
A. Seven (the Noahide laws)
B. Thirty
C. Ten
D. Forty-eight
Correct: B — Ulla teaches that the nations accepted 30 mitzvot upon themselves — a number beyond the seven Noahide laws — though they only actively observe three of them in practice.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Which of the following is NOT one of the three laws that non-Jews actually observe according to Ulla?
A. They do not write marriage contracts for male unions
B. They do not sell human flesh by weight in the market
C. They honor the Torah of Israel
D. They do not worship idols on the Sabbath
Correct: D — Ulla's three kept laws are: no ketubbah for males, no selling human flesh in markets, and showing reverence for the Torah. Sabbath observance is not among them.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the primary Torah source for the prohibition of gid hanasheh?
A. Leviticus 11 — laws of kosher species
B. Genesis 32 — Jacob wrestling the angel who struck his thigh
C. Deuteronomy 14 — dietary restrictions
D. Exodus 23 — prohibition of mixing meat and milk
Correct: B — The gid hanasheh prohibition derives from Genesis 32:33: 'Therefore Israel does not eat the gid hanasheh to this day' — commemorating the angel's strike on Jacob's hip socket.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Regarding the fat surrounding the gid hanasheh: according to Rava, what is the status of fat located ABOVE the hip joint?
A. Permitted, since only the gid itself is forbidden
B. Forbidden, because it has substance and imparts taste
C. Permitted only after double salting
D. Depends on the species of the animal
Correct: B — Rava rules that fat above the hip joint has real substance and taste, making it integral to the gid itself and therefore forbidden. Fat below the joint is hardened tasteless matter and is permitted.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the prohibition of gid hanasheh generally NOT apply to fowl?
A. The Torah explicitly exempts fowl in a separate verse
B. Fowl do not have a true hip socket (kaf yerech) where Jacob was struck
C. The Sages never enacted a rabbinic fence around fowl
D. Fowl are only rabbinically forbidden as meat, so the gid law is inapplicable
Correct: B — The anatomical structure of fowl lacks the true hip socket (kaf yerech) that is central to the prohibition. Since the biblical verse references the specific joint struck in Jacob, and fowl lack this structure, the biblical prohibition does not apply to them.
