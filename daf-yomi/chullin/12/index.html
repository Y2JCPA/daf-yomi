👁️ Watching the Slaughterer

Rav Nachman's rule: If you see someone slaughter an animal from beginning to end — you may eat the meat. But if you arrive in the middle and didn't see the beginning — you may not eat it without verifying his expertise.
You see a slaughterer at work. Do you know if he's an expert?
↓
Watched beginning to end ✅
→ May eat
Arrived mid-slaughter ❌
→ Cannot verify → forbidden
↓
Exception: if you already know he's an expert (his reputation is established)

🥩 Found a Slaughtered Chicken

RN
You send an agent to slaughter a chicken. You return and find the chicken already slaughtered. May you eat it?
Q
Same question: you find a slaughtered chicken in the marketplace. May you eat it?
RN
Yes! We rely on rov — the majority of those who slaughter are experts in the halachot of shechitah.
Q
But that disproves your earlier rule about needing to watch from beginning to end!
RN
Different case: that applies when you know the slaughterer is NOT an expert. Here, the person's expertise is simply unknown — so rov applies.

RN = Rav Nachman · Q = Questioner

🤝 The Agent Dilemma

RD
You send your agent to slaughter. He went and found the chicken already dead! If you sent him, he didn't slaughter it — but someone else did. May you eat it?
RN
Whichever way you look at it, there's a problem! Either the agent acted (then why is it already dead?) or a stranger slaughtered it (then who certified it was done properly?).
RN
Actually: presumption that an agent performs his mission — plus rov that most slaughterers are expert — together permit eating it.
Rav Nachman's witty answer: "Eat a kor of salt over it" — think about it more carefully! Once you analyze it, the combination of chazkat shliach (agent does his mission) and rov permits it.

🌀 Is "Majority of Slaughterers are Experts" a Tannaim Dispute?

View A — R' Chanina b. R' Yosi HaGelili

  • 📊 Majority of those associated with slaughter ARE experts
  • ✅ Found anonymous meat → assume permitted
  • 💡 Rov applies: most slaughterers know the halachot

View B — R' Yehuda (possibly)

  • ⚠️ Cannot assume an unknown slaughterer is expert
  • ❌ More cautious about anonymous slaughter
  • 📏 Requires more active verification
Resolution: The Gemara concludes this is NOT actually a dispute — both agree on the principle. The disagreement was about a different underlying issue.

🔪 A Thrown Knife — Can It Slaughter?

A person throws a knife to embed in the wall. As it flies, it passes through an animal's neck.
↓
Is this valid shechitah? There was no conscious back-and-forth motion!
↓
Normal shechitah requires deliberate forward-and-back motion on the simanim
A baraita (R' Natan's view): if the knife went and returned during flight — it's valid!
↓
Rava: this is R' Natan's view — who holds that inadvertent slaughter can be valid
