🏺 The Unique Rule of Earthenware

Earthenware Vessels

  • 🌬️ Become impure from airspace (inside)
  • ❌ Do NOT become impure from outside contact
  • 📜 Source: "into which any of them falls" (Vayikra 11:33)
  • 🏺 Even full of mustard seeds — all impure!

All Other Vessels

  • 👆 Become impure from outside contact
  • ❌ Do NOT become impure from airspace alone
  • 📜 Different derivation: contact-based impurity
  • 🔄 Opposite rule from earthenware!
The word "tokho" (into it): The Torah's choice of "into which" teaches that earthenware vessels have a unique impurity pathway — through their hollow interior airspace. An impure item need only enter the airspace (not touch the walls) to render the entire vessel and its contents impure.

🔄 Why Not Learn One From the Other?

Can we derive via kal va'chomer that earthenware also becomes impure from outside contact?
↓
Attempted inference: If other vessels (which DON'T become impure from airspace) become impure from contact, then earthenware (which DOES become impure from airspace) should certainly become impure from contact!
↓
Blocked! Torah says "every open vessel that has no sealed cover upon it is impure" — impurity depends on the MOUTH of the vessel, not its exterior
And the reverse? Can we derive that other vessels become impure from airspace via kal va'chomer from earthenware? Also blocked — the Torah specifies "tokho" (into it) — its inside, and not the insides of other vessels.

🦴 Bone Vessels — A Third Category?

RI
R' Yishmael b. R' Yochanan ben Beroka: Bone vessels ARE susceptible to impurity. Source: the Torah lists animal skins alongside wood, meaning materials from animals (including bones) can form vessels subject to impurity.
Gm
The practical difference between the opinions in the Gemara: bone vessels from horns. Are expensive horn vessels susceptible? If we follow the derivation from animal materials — yes. Otherwise — debate.

🔨 When Is a Vessel "Complete"?

A vessel is susceptible to impurity only when it is complete — fit for its intended use
↓
If it still needs smoothing, planing, setting gems, adding a base/rim/handle → NOT yet susceptible
↓
Once it can serve its function — even if aesthetic finishing remains — it IS susceptible
Wooden vs. metal vs. earthenware: The Gemara notes the rules differ. For wood: if it can hold something, it's a vessel. For earthenware: completion standards are stricter (firing in kiln). For metal: depends on the finishing process. Each material follows its own threshold.

🌰 Bitter vs. Sweet Almonds — Opposite Tithing

Mishna: The stage at which bitter almonds are obligated in tithes is the stage at which sweet almonds are EXEMPT — and vice versa!
StageBitter AlmondsSweet Almonds
Small / unripe✅ Edible (eaten as snack) → obligated❌ Not edible yet → exempt
Large / ripe❌ Too bitter to eat → exempt✅ Edible and sweet → obligated
🎓 Principle: Tithing obligation tracks edibility. Since bitter and sweet almonds have opposite edibility curves, their tithing obligations are mirror images. Small bitter almonds are eaten as a snack (obligated); large bitter almonds are too bitter (exempt). The reverse for sweet.
