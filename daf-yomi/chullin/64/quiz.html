Chullin 64 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Which physical characteristic marks a kosher egg according to the Gemara's signs?
A. Both ends are round and symmetrical
B. One end is pointed and one end is round
C. Both ends are pointed
D. The shell is thicker on one side
Correct: B — The Gemara teaches that a kosher egg has one pointed end and one round end — this shape reflects the egg's origin from a kosher species of bird.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Where must the yolk (חלמון) be located for an egg to have the kosher sign?
A. Surrounding the white on the outside
B. Inverted, with white at the center
C. Inside, fully surrounded by the white (חלבון)
D. It can be anywhere; only shape matters
Correct: C — A kosher egg has the yolk inside and the white surrounding it. If inverted — yolk outside, white inside — it is a sign of a non-kosher bird's egg.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the status of a fully-shelled egg found inside a properly slaughtered kosher bird?
A. Forbidden, since it was inside a carcass
B. Permitted — it is a separate entity from the mother
C. Requires salting like meat before it may be eaten
D. Forbidden unless the bird was inspected for treifot first
Correct: B — Fully-shelled eggs found inside a slaughtered kosher bird are permitted, because they are considered separate entities and are not subject to the same laws as the bird's meat.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the normative ruling in the Gemara, what is the status of eggs laid by a treifah bird?
A. Forbidden by Torah law — treifah extends to its products
B. Forbidden by Rabbinic decree out of caution
C. Permitted — the egg is a separate entity; treifah doesn't transfer
D. Permitted only if laid before the treifah defect occurred
Correct: C — The halacha follows Shmuel's position: eggs of a treifah bird are permitted, since the egg is a distinct entity and the treifah prohibition does not automatically extend to it.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If a blood spot is found on the yolk of an egg, what is the correct halachic ruling?
A. Remove the blood spot; the rest of the egg is permitted
B. Only the yolk must be discarded; the white may be eaten
C. The entire egg is forbidden
D. Boil the egg first, then remove the blood and eat it
Correct: C — Blood found on the yolk renders the entire egg forbidden, as it indicates the egg was fertilized and embryonic development has begun. Blood on the white is treated more leniently.
