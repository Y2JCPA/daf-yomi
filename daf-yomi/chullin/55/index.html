📜 Context: The Eighteen Treifot The Mishna (54a) enumerates 18 conditions — treifot — that render an animal fatally compromised and forbidden for consumption. Daf 55 drills into the lung (re'ah), the most complex organ in this halacha, examining exactly which defects disqualify it. The Sages considered this list halacha l'Moshe miSinai — an unbroken oral tradition from Sinai, not derived from Scripture alone.

⚖️ Missing vs. Perforated Lung Lobe

Hisar — Missing Lobe

  • A major lobe (אוּלָא) is entirely absent
  • Animal is treifah — no debate
  • Present from birth or removed: equally disqualifying
  • Minor subsidiary lobes: subject to amoraic dispute

Nikev — Perforated Lobe

  • A hole exists through the lung tissue
  • Outer membrane seals the hole → kosher
  • Open hole through which air escapes → treifah
  • Test: submerge inflated lung in water; bubbles = treif

💧 The Lung Bedika (Inspection)

After shechita, carefully remove the lung with trachea intact
↓
Seal the trachea opening and inflate the lung with air
↓
Submerge the fully inflated lung in a basin of water
↓
Gently press on the lobe in question with steady pressure
↓
Observe: bubbles rising to the surface = air escaping through a hole
↓
No bubbles detected → membrane is intact → KOSHER
↓
Bubbles present → perforation confirmed → TREIFAH

🗣️ Debate: The Shrunken Lung (צִימֵק)

Rav Huna
A lung that has shrunken and dried out (tzimek) is treifah. Such a lung cannot sustain the animal — it will die within twelve months regardless of external appearance.
Rav Chisda
I disagree with a blanket ruling. If you press on the shrunken lung and fluid still seeps out, the lung retains functional moisture. The animal is kosher — the lung is not truly dead.
Rav Huna
On that specific point I will concede. When moisture seeps out, the lung functions. But if pressing produces nothing at all — completely desiccated — even you agree it is treifah.
Rav Chisda
Correct. The operative distinction is moisture. Fluid present = kosher. Completely dry = treifah. This becomes the practical ruling for inspection.
Practical Ruling Press the shrunken lung: any seeping moisture → kosher. Completely dry with no moisture → treifah.
