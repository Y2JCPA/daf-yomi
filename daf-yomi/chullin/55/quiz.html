QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the Gemara's prescribed method for testing whether a perforation in a lung lobe makes the animal treifah?
A. Cut open the lung and visually locate the hole
B. Inflate the lung and submerge it in water, watching for rising bubbles
C. Weigh the lung against a standard healthy lung
D. Place the lung in saline solution and measure leakage
Correct: B — The Gemara describes inflating the lung via the trachea and submerging it in water. Air bubbling out through a hole confirms a treifah-level perforation. No bubbles means the membrane seals it and the animal is kosher.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
If a major lobe (aula) of the lung is entirely missing, what is the animal's halachic status?
A. Kosher, since the remaining lobes compensate for its function
B. Requires a case-by-case ruling by a qualified posek
C. Treifah — a missing major lobe is unconditionally disqualifying
D. Kosher only if the animal showed no symptoms before slaughter
Correct: C — A missing major lobe is one of the clear-cut treifot with no room for leniency. The animal is treifah regardless of whether the other lobes appear healthy.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the debate about a shrunken lung (tzimek), what does Rav Chisda identify as the decisive factor for a kosher ruling?
A. The animal behaved normally in the days before slaughter
B. The outer membrane of the lung remains visually intact
C. Moisture or fluid still seeps out when the lung is pressed
D. More than half of the lung tissue retains its normal volume
Correct: C — Rav Chisda holds that if pressing the shrunken lung produces any moisture, the lung is still functionally alive and the animal is kosher. Only when the lung is completely dry — producing nothing — is it treifah.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does an animal attacked by a predator (doreset) require special halachic examination even after valid shechita?
A. The animal's blood may be mixed with impure substances from the predator
B. Claws and talons can penetrate and perforate vital internal organs invisibly
C. The stress of the attack invalidates the shechita retroactively
D. The animal may have consumed non-kosher material during the struggle
Correct: B — Predators' claws and talons can pierce through to the lung, kidneys, or intestines without visible external wounds. Such penetrations create treifah conditions, so the organs must be examined even when the shechita was performed correctly.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does the term 'halacha l'Moshe miSinai' mean in the context of the eighteen treifot?
A. The list is explicitly written in the Torah text of Parshat Re'eh
B. The list was derived by the Talmudic Sages through logical analysis of verses
C. The list is an oral tradition from Moses at Sinai, authoritative without a scriptural proof-text
D. The list may be expanded by any generation's Sanhedrin as new conditions arise
Correct: C — Halacha l'Moshe miSinai designates oral laws transmitted from Moses without a direct biblical source. The eighteen treifot — their specific number and details — are binding as tradition, not derived from textual exegesis.
