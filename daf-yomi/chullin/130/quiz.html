5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Which three body parts must be given to the kohen as matanot from a slaughtered domestic animal?
A. Liver, heart, and lungs
B. Foreleg (zeroa), cheeks (lechayayim), and maw (keiva)
C. Head, tongue, and tail
D. Right thigh, kidney, and fat-covering
Correct: B — Devarim 18:3 specifies zeroa (foreleg), lechayayim (cheeks), and keiva (maw/abomasum) as the three priestly gifts from every slaughtered domestic animal.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Do the laws of matanot kehuna apply to wild animals (chayot) such as deer?
A. Yes, all slaughtered animals require matanot
B. Only the zeroa applies to wild animals
C. No, wild animals (chaya) are completely exempt
D. Only outside the Land of Israel are they exempt
Correct: C — The Mishnah explicitly limits matanot to domestic behema (cattle, sheep, goats). Wild animals (chaya) are entirely exempt from all three gifts.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, where does the zeroa gift begin on the animal's leg?
A. From the hoof upward
B. From the knee joint only
C. From the breast/shoulder joint (בֵּית הַנַּחַת) downward
D. Only the single upper arm bone
Correct: C — Rav Huna rules the zeroa extends from the breast joint (beit hanachat) downward — a generous foreleg portion given raw to the kohen.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Do matanot kehuna obligations apply outside the Land of Israel?
A. No — they are restricted to the Land of Israel only
B. Only if a kohen is permanently residing in that country
C. Yes — the Mishnah explicitly states they apply both in and outside the Land
D. Only in Babylonia, by rabbinic extension
Correct: C — The Mishnah clearly states matanot apply both in the Land of Israel and outside it — this is a universal Torah obligation wherever Jews slaughter domestic animals.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Whose conduct does the Gemara cite as a negative model for how a kohen should NOT take his priestly gifts?
A. Aaron's sons Nadav and Avihu
B. Korach and his supporters
C. Chofni and Pinchas, sons of Eli the High Priest
D. The Levites who served alongside the kohanim
Correct: C — The Gemara invokes Chofni and Pinchas (I Shmuel 2), who forcibly seized meat before the fat was offered — condemned as a 'very great sin before God.' A kohen must receive matanot honorably, not by force.
