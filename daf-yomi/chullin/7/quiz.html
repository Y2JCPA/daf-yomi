Chullin 7 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded

Question 1 of 5
Why did Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi exempt Beit She'an from terumot and tithes?
Rebbi held that the kedusha from Yehoshua's conquest was temporary (קְדוּשָּׁה רִאשׁוֹנָה קִדְּשָׁה לִשְׁעָתָהּ וְלֹא קִדְּשָׁה לֶעָתִיד לָבֹא). Cities not reconquered by the olei Bavel lost their sanctity, and some were intentionally left that way to support the poor during Shemittah.
Question 2 of 5
What remarkable thing happened when R' Pinchas ben Ya'ir reached the Ginai River?
The river initially argued that it was fulfilling God's will by flowing. R' Pinchas threatened to decree it dry forever. The river split three times: for him, for a man carrying Pesach wheat, and for an Arab traveler. Rav Yosef declared him as great as Moshe at the Yam Suf!
Question 3 of 5
Why did R' Pinchas ben Ya'ir's donkey refuse to eat the barley at the inn?
The hosts tried sifting, cleaning, and hand-picking the barley — nothing worked. Only after tithes were separated did the donkey eat. This illustrates the principle: "God does not bring mishap even through the animals of the righteous."
Question 4 of 5
Why did R' Pinchas ben Ya'ir refuse to dine at Rebbi's house?
R' Pinchas initially agreed to dine and wasn't opposed to benefiting from Jews. But when he returned and saw white mules (considered extremely dangerous), he declared: "The Angel of Death is in this house!" Rebbi offered to sell or abandon them, but R' Pinchas found problems with each solution — a mountain rose between them.
Question 5 of 5
What broader theological principle does R' Chanina derive from "Ein od milvado" (Devarim 4:35)?
R' Chanina interprets "There is none besides Him" expansively: even sorcery (kishuf) has no real power against God's will. This is paired with his other teaching that a person doesn't injure even a finger on earth unless it was decreed in Heaven — complete Divine providence over every detail.
