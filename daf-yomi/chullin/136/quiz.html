Chullin 136 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the minimum number of sheep one must own to be obligated in the mitzvah of Reishit HaGez?
A. One sheep
B. Three sheep
C. Five sheep
D. Ten sheep
Correct: C — The Mishnah (Chullin 135) establishes that at least five sheep are required to be obligated in Reishit HaGez — fewer than five exempts the owner entirely.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Beit Hillel (and accepted halacha), how much wool must be given to the kohen for Reishit HaGez?
A. 60 maneh by Judean measure
B. One-sixtieth of the total shearing
C. Enough to make a small garment
D. 5 sela by weight
Correct: C — Beit Hillel uses a functional standard: the wool given to the kohen must be sufficient to weave a small garment (kiton). This ensures the gift is practically usable, not merely symbolic.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was Beit Shammai's minimum amount for Reishit HaGez?
A. Enough to make a small garment
B. One-tenth of the total shearing
C. 60 maneh by Judean measure (= 5 maneh Galilean)
D. No minimum — any first wool suffices
Correct: C — Beit Shammai required a large fixed quantity: 60 maneh by Judean reckoning, equivalent to 5 maneh by the Galilean standard — a substantially heavier weight than Beit Hillel's functional measure.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What distinguishes Reishit HaGez from the priestly gifts of zeroa, lechayayim, and keivah?
A. Reishit HaGez applies only in Eretz Yisrael
B. Reishit HaGez comes from wool, not from a slaughtered animal
C. Reishit HaGez requires Temple-era observance only
D. Reishit HaGez has no minimum amount required
Correct: B — Zeroa, lechayayim, and keivah are taken from the meat and organs of a slaughtered animal. Reishit HaGez is unique in coming from the renewable wool of the flock — not from slaughter.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rabbi Dosa ben Harkinas offered an intermediate view on the Reishit HaGez shiur. What was it?
A. Enough wool for a full adult robe
B. Any amount greater than a kezayit
C. 5 sela by weight
D. One-fifth of the total shearing
Correct: C — Rabbi Dosa ben Harkinas took a middle position between Beit Shammai's large fixed measure and Beit Hillel's qualitative garment standard, requiring a specific weight of 5 sela.
