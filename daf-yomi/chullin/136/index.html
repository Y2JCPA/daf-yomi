🐑 Reishit HaGez: The First Shearing

רֵאשִׁית גֵּז צֹאנְךָ תִּתֶּן לּוֹ — The first of the shearing of your flock you shall give to him (דברים י״ח:ד׳)
Torah Obligation Chullin 136 deepens the laws of Reishit HaGez — the first shearing of one's flock that must be given to the kohen (Devarim 18:4). The Gemara debates the minimum flock size that triggers the obligation and how much wool must be given. Beit Hillel's functional standard — enough to make a small garment — emerges as the accepted ruling.
3 / 9

📖 The Mitzvah and Its Source

רֵאשִׁית גֵּז צֹאנְךָ תִּתֶּן לּוֹ (דברים י״ח:ד׳)
Torah Source The Torah commands giving the first shearing of one's flock to the kohen. Unlike other priestly gifts (zeroa, lechayayim, keivah) which come from slaughtered animals, Reishit HaGez comes from wool — the renewable produce of the flock. The Mishnah establishes a minimum of five sheep to be obligated, and the Gemara asks: how much wool must actually be given?

⚖️ How Much Wool Must Be Given to the Kohen?

Beit Shammai
The minimum is 60 maneh by Judean measure — equivalent to 5 maneh by Galilean reckoning. A fixed, substantial quantitative standard is required.
Beit Hillel
The minimum is enough wool to make a small garment (kiton) for the kohen — a functional standard: sufficient to produce a usable item, not a fixed weight.
Rabbi Dosa ben Harkinas
The minimum is 5 sela by weight — an intermediate position between Beit Shammai's large fixed measure and Beit Hillel's qualitative approach.
Halacha The law follows Beit Hillel: give enough wool to weave a small garment for the kohen. The gift must be meaningful and usable, not merely a token amount.

📊 Reishit HaGez Compared to Other Priestly Gifts

Reishit HaGez

  • Source: Devarim 18:4
  • From: wool of the flock
  • Trigger: owning 5+ sheep
  • Amount: enough for a small garment
  • Applies inside and outside Israel

Zeroa, Lechayayim, Keivah

  • Source: Devarim 18:3
  • From: slaughtered non-sacred cattle
  • Trigger: any single animal
  • Specific organs: foreleg, jaw, maw
  • No minimum flock required

Bechor Behemah

  • Source: Devarim 15:19
  • From: firstborn of the flock
  • Trigger: birth of firstborn male
  • Animal given alive to kohen
  • Kohen must be blemish-free for eating
