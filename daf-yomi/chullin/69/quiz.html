Chullin 69 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What does the principle ירך אמו (the fetus is like the mother's thigh) mean legally?
A. The fetus must be slaughtered separately after the mother dies
B. The fetus is considered part of the mother's body and is permitted by her shechita
C. The mother's shechita covers only developed fetuses near full term
D. The fetus is forbidden until examined by a rabbinic authority
Correct: B — ירך אמו means the fetus is treated as an integral part of the mother's body — like her own thigh. Therefore, her valid shechita automatically permits the fetus without any additional act of slaughter.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R. Meir, what is the status of a fetus limb that protruded outside the womb and remained there at the time of shechita?
A. Permitted, since the fetus is always covered by the mother's shechita
B. Forbidden as ever min hachai — treated as a limb severed from a living animal
C. Permitted only if the limb is less than a kezayit in size
D. Requires a separate blessing but is permitted to eat
Correct: B — R. Meir holds that the womb is the legal boundary of the mother's domain. A limb outside this boundary is not covered by her shechita and acquires the forbidden status of ever min hachai — a limb from a living creature.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If a limb of the fetus protruded from the womb but retracted back inside before the mother was slaughtered, what is the ruling?
A. The limb is forbidden because it once exited the womb
B. The limb is permitted — all authorities agree that retraction restores coverage
C. This case is disputed between R. Meir and R. Shimon
D. The entire fetus is suspect and requires rabbinic supervision
Correct: B — All authorities agree that retraction before shechita fully restores the limb's covered status. Since it re-entered the womb before slaughter occurred, it is once again part of the mother's body-domain and is permitted via her shechita.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
On what grounds does R. Shimon permit even a limb that protruded and stayed outside the womb at shechita?
A. He holds the law of ever min hachai does not apply to animals at all
B. He holds that physical attachment of the fetus body to the mother covers all its parts regardless of limb location
C. He holds that only the fetus's head determines coverage, not the limbs
D. He requires a separate shechita for the limb but considers it a rabbinic matter
Correct: B — R. Shimon's view is that the fetus remains one physically attached organism. Since the fetus body itself is inside and connected to the mother, all of its parts — including protruding limbs — are still subsumed under her body and covered by her shechita.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In all opinions on Chullin 69, which part of the fetus is ALWAYS permitted through the mother's shechita in the case of a protruding limb?
A. Only the protruding limb itself
B. The entire fetus including the protruding limb
C. The body of the fetus (excluding the protruding limb per R. Meir)
D. Nothing — if any limb protrudes the entire fetus is suspect
Correct: C — Even according to R. Meir who forbids the protruding limb, the body of the fetus that remained inside is fully permitted through the mother's shechita via ירך אמו. The dispute is only about the status of the specific limb that exited the womb.
