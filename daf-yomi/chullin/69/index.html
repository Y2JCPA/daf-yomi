🐄 Slide 1: The Foundational Principle — ירך אמו

עוּבָּר יֶרֶךְ אִמּוֹ — The fetus is [like] the thigh of its mother (Chullin 69a)
עוּבָּר יֶרֶךְ אִמּוֹ הוּא A fetus found inside a slaughtered animal is permitted for consumption without any additional act of shechita. The Gemara derives this from the principle that the fetus is considered like the thigh of its mother — an integral, inseparable part of her body. When valid shechita is performed on the mother, it automatically extends to cover everything within her body, including the fetus, regardless of its stage of development.

⚖️ Slide 2: The Dispute — Limb Outside the Womb at Shechita

R. Meir
If a limb of the fetus protruded out of the womb before shechita and remained there, it is forbidden. The womb is the boundary of the mother's body-domain. A limb outside that boundary is no longer covered by her shechita — it is treated as ever min hachai, a limb severed from a living creature, and is biblically prohibited.
R. Shimon
The fetus is still physically attached to the mother as a whole unit. The protruding limb has not separated — only its location changed. Since the fetus body itself remains inside, all of its parts — including the external limb — are still covered by the mother's shechita. Location within or outside the womb is not legally determinative.
Core Disagreement Is the womb a legal boundary that severs coverage, or is physical attachment of the fetus body the sole criterion for ירך אמו status?

🔄 Slide 3: Three Cases of the Fetus Limb Compared

Limb Never Protruded

  • Fetus entirely inside womb at shechita
  • No dispute — universally permitted
  • Shechita of mother covers completely
  • Zero ever min hachai concern

Limb Protruded Then Retracted

  • Limb exited, then re-entered before slaughter
  • Permitted — all Tannaim agree
  • Retraction restores legal status as part of mother
  • The prior exit creates no lasting consequence

Limb Protruded and Stayed Out

  • Limb outside womb at moment of shechita
  • R. Meir: limb forbidden as ever min hachai
  • R. Shimon: limb permitted via mother's shechita
  • Fetus body itself is permitted in both opinions

🔪 Slide 5: The Legal Logic Step by Step

Step 1 — Base Rule: A fetus inside a slaughtered mother is permitted by her shechita. It requires no independent slaughter — it is part of her body (ירך אמו).
↓
Step 2 — The Principle's Basis: The fetus has no independent halakhic status while inside the mother. It is legally subsumed into her body, just as any internal organ would be.
↓
Step 3 — The Complication: A limb of the fetus extends outside the womb before shechita. Does it now exist in 'two worlds' — inside (fetus body) and outside (limb)?
↓
Step 4 — R. Meir's Logic: The womb defines the mother's domain. Exit = separation from coverage. The limb is now like ever min hachai — from a living animal's perspective, it was severed.
↓
Step 5 — R. Shimon's Logic: Physical attachment is the only criterion. The fetus is still one organism connected to the mother. No part of an attached fetus can acquire independent 'living limb' status.
