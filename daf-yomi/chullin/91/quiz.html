Chullin 91 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did the angel urgently demand that Jacob release him as dawn approached?
A. His supernatural power expired at sunrise and he feared being trapped
B. He needed to return to sing praises before the Divine Throne at sunrise
C. He was embarrassed to be seen losing to a human in daylight
D. He needed to report the outcome of the struggle to the heavenly court
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that angels of daytime service must begin their shirah (celestial praise) precisely at sunrise. The angel had a divine appointment it could not miss — hence the frantic plea despite being unable to defeat Jacob.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Chananel, how are angelic singing duties structured?
A. All angels sing together in one unified choir throughout the entire day
B. Angels sing only at night when the world is quiet and receptive
C. Daytime angels sing by day and nighttime angels sing by night — each cohort has its own appointed time
D. Only the highest-ranking seraphim sing; other angels merely listen
Correct: C — R' Chananel holds there are two distinct angelic cohorts with their own designated times of service. This directly explains the angel's urgency — as a daytime-service angel, it had to begin its shift at sunrise and could not linger past dawn.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
The verse 'al kaf hayarech' uses the singular form. How does the Gemara rule regarding the gid hanasheh prohibition?
A. Only the right thigh's gid is forbidden, as Jacob's right hip was struck
B. Only the left thigh's gid is forbidden, based on a tradition about the injury
C. Both thighs are forbidden — the singular form is a general expression covering both sides
D. Which thigh depends on a dispute: Beit Shammai says right, Beit Hillel says left
Correct: C — The Gemara derives from 'al kein lo yochlu bnei Yisrael' that the prohibition is sweeping and covers both thighs. Each animal has two forbidden gidin — one from each thigh. The singular 'kaf' does not restrict the prohibition to one side.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Does the prohibition of gid hanasheh apply to an animal that was not properly slaughtered (neveilah)?
A. No — the gid prohibition only applies when the meat itself is otherwise permitted
B. Yes — gid hanasheh is an independent prohibition that applies regardless of slaughter status
C. Only if the animal is a kosher species that happened to die without shechitah
D. It applies only rabbinically to neveilah, but not biblically
Correct: B — The gid hanasheh prohibition is intrinsically bound to the gid itself, not to the kosher status of the meat. Even though neveilah meat is already forbidden for other reasons, there is an additional independent issur on the gid — as Abaye teaches on this daf.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If gid hanasheh was accidentally cooked together with permitted meat, what is the halachic ruling regarding the pot?
A. The entire pot is forbidden since gid hanasheh is a biblical prohibition
B. The pot is forbidden only if the gid constituted more than 1/60th of the volume
C. The pot remains permitted because the gid does not impart significant flavor (ta'am) to other meat
D. It depends on whether the gid was whole or cut — whole gidin impart more flavor
Correct: C — The Gemara on Chullin 91 discusses whether the gid has ta'am (flavor) and concludes it does not impart significant flavor to other meat. Therefore, meat accidentally cooked with gid hanasheh is not rendered forbidden — the pot remains permitted.
