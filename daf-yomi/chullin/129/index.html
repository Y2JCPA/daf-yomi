📜 The Core Rule: Kazayit of Flesh from a Met

כְּזַיִת מִן הַמֵּת — מְטַמֵּא בְּמַגָּע וּבְמַשָּׂא וּבְאֹהֶל
Torah Law Flesh from a human corpse (מֵת) conveys tumah when it reaches the minimum measure of a kazayit (כְּזַיִת — olive's bulk). Below this measure, the flesh does not convey tumah through contact, carrying, or ohel. The derivation comes from the verses in Bamidbar 19 governing tumah of the dead. This kazayit applies to flesh alone — bone and sinew are evaluated under separate rules.

⚔️ Does Gid (Sinew) Join Flesh Toward the Kazayit?

Rav
The gid haNasheh and sinews in general do NOT join with flesh to complete the kazayit. Sinew is a distinct halachic category from 'basar' — it carries its own prohibition (gid haNasheh) but does not contribute to the flesh measure for tumah.
Shmuel
Sinew DOES join with flesh to complete the minimum measure. Since sinew is physically attached to and integrated with the flesh of a limb, it is considered part of the limb-unit for purposes of tumah calculation.
Gemara
The practical nafka mina: if one has less than a kazayit of pure flesh but the flesh and sinew together equal a kazayit — according to Rav, no tumah of basar hamet; according to Shmuel, tumah does apply to this combination.
Ruling In matters of tumah, we apply the stricter position — one who contacts such a flesh-plus-sinew combination reaching kazayit should treat it as tumah-conveying.

⚖️ Ever Min HaChai vs. Basar HaMet

Ever Min HaChai

  • Limb severed from a living person or animal
  • Conveys tumah if it has flesh sufficient to heal (ראוי להתרפאות)
  • Evaluated as a unified limb: bone + flesh + sinew together
  • Source: Devarim 14:21 — neveilah prohibition
  • The living donor is not rendered impure by losing the limb

Basar HaMet

  • Flesh from a deceased human (corpse)
  • Minimum measure: kazayit of flesh alone
  • Bone does not combine with flesh for this shiur
  • Source: Bamidbar 19 — laws of tumah hamet
  • Conveys tumah via contact, carrying (masa), and ohel (tent/roof)

🔍 Step-by-Step: Evaluating a Severed Limb for Tumah

Step 1 — Identify the source: Is the limb/flesh from a living being (ever min hachai) or a deceased person (basar hamet)? The evaluation method differs entirely.
↓
Step 2 — For basar hamet: Measure the flesh component alone (excluding bone and, per Rav, sinew). Compare this to a kazayit (~27–30 cc by volume).
↓
Step 3 — For ever min hachai: Assess whether the severed limb retains enough flesh that it could theoretically heal (ראוי להתרפאות). If yes, the entire limb unit conveys tumah regardless of exact weight.
↓
Step 4 — Check tziruf (joining): If multiple smaller pieces exist, determine whether they can be combined. Pieces from the same corpse in the same location may join to reach the kazayit threshold.
↓
Step 5 — Apply the tumah ruling: If the threshold is met, the flesh conveys tumah to people and utensils through contact (מַגָּע), carrying (מַשָּׂא), and ohel (אֹהֶל — being under the same roof/covering).
