📜 Ulla's Eight: Treifot Given at Sinai

Ulla's landmark teaching: All treifot — however many specific cases there are — fall into exactly 8 master categories given to Moses at Sinai. Every case in the Mishna, baraitot, and Amoraic discussions is a sub-case of one of these 8.

🔢 Chiyya bar Rava: 8 in "Perforated" Alone

Chiyya bar Rava adds: Just within the single category of "perforated" (nekuvah), there are 8 different organs whose perforation renders the animal treifah. The 8 Sinai categories are not simple — each branches into many specifics.

8 Perforated-Organ Treifot

  • 1. Gullet (esophagus)
  • 2. Brain membrane
  • 3. Heart (to its chamber)
  • 4. Lung
  • 5. Abomasum (4th stomach)
  • 6. Intestine
  • 7. Inner rumen
  • 8. Reticulum/omasum (to outside)

What About Gallbladder?

  • Gallbladder perforation seems like it should be #9
  • Chiyya bar Rava says: NOT a treifah
  • Job 16:13: "He pours out my gall upon the ground" — yet Job lived!
  • Rabbi Yochanan rules: the halacha DOES render it treifah
  • ⚖️ Dispute remains

📖 Job's Gallbladder — A Torah Proof Text

R'Yosei
A perforated gallbladder IS a treifah. The gallbladder is essential — perforation means certain death.
Friends
What did his colleagues respond? Job said: "He pours out my gall upon the ground" (Job 16:13) — and Job was still alive! This proves a perforated gallbladder is NOT necessarily fatal.
R'YH
Rabbi Yochanan rules: the halacha IS in accordance with R' Yosei — a perforated gallbladder renders the animal treifah, despite Job's case.
The Job argument is remarkable: The Gemara uses a verse about a human being's suffering as evidence about animal physiology. Job's survival with a "poured out" gallbladder is cited as empirical proof that gallbladder perforation is survivable — and therefore not automatically treifah. Rabbi Yochanan overrules this biological argument with a halachic ruling.

🫒 Liver Treifah: How Much Must Remain?

Mishna
If the liver was removed and nothing remained of it — the animal is a treifah.
R'YH
Rabbi Yochanan says: the halacha follows the opinion that if one olive-bulk (כזית) of liver remains, the animal is kosher.
Q
But doesn't Rabbi Yochanan say the halacha always follows an unattributed Mishna — and the Mishna says "nothing remained"? That implies even a small piece remaining makes it kosher!
Ans
The Mishna's phrase "nothing remained" is deliberately ambiguous — it can be read as requiring a meaningful amount (olive-bulk) to remain. Both interpretations are compatible.

🩺 Special Cases: Gallbladder Sealed by Liver

Rabbi Yochanan (via Rabbi Yitzchak son of Rabbi Yosef): Several nuanced rulings about borderline organ conditions:
Gallbladder is perforated — but the liver is adjacent and seals the hole. Treifah?
↓
✅ Kosher — the liver's proximity seals the perforation. The animal can survive.
↓
Bird's gizzard is perforated — but the inner lining is intact. Treifah?
↓
✅ Kosher — the intact inner lining prevents internal leakage. The gizzard functions normally.
↓
Inner lining is perforated but gizzard flesh is intact. Treifah?
↓
⚠️ Unresolved dilemma — left as a teku
