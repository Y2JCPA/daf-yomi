Chullin 43 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded

Question 1 of 5
According to Ulla, how many master categories of treifot were given to Moses at Sinai?
Ulla teaches that exactly 8 master categories of treifot were given to Moses at Sinai: perforated (nekuvah), missing (chaserah), removed (akurah), torn (keruah), entirely removed (netulah), clawed (derusah), fell (nefulah), and broken (shevirah). All 18 specific treifot in the Mishna — and all other cases — are sub-categories within these 8 master types.
Question 2 of 5
Which of the following organs does Chiyya bar Rava EXCLUDE from the "perforated = treifah" category?
Chiyya bar Rava lists 8 organs whose perforation renders an animal treifah, but he excludes the gallbladder — giving him exactly 8, not 9. He holds that a perforated gallbladder does NOT render the animal treifah, bringing Job 16:13 as support ("He pours out my gall upon the ground" — yet Job lived). Rabbi Yochanan disagrees and rules the gallbladder IS treifah.
Question 3 of 5
How does the Gemara use the verse "He pours out my gall upon the ground" (Job 16:13) in the gallbladder dispute?
The colleagues of R' Yosei ben R' Yehuda cite Job 16:13 as real-world evidence: Job describes God "pouring out his gall" — understood as a perforated/emptied gallbladder — yet the verse implies Job was still alive to describe it. If gallbladder perforation were fatal, Job couldn't be speaking. This biological argument supports the lenient position that gallbladder perforation is NOT treifah.
Question 4 of 5
If a gallbladder is perforated but the adjacent liver tissue seals the hole, what is the ruling?
Rabbi Yochanan rules it kosher. The fact that the perforation is sealed by the adjacent liver means the gallbladder is effectively intact in function — the bile cannot leak out. This demonstrates a key principle: treifah is not merely about the technical existence of a hole, but about whether the organ can still function. A sealed perforation = functional organ = not treifah.
Question 5 of 5
What is the ruling regarding a bird's gizzard that is perforated — if the inner lining remains intact?
Rabbi Yochanan rules it kosher. The gizzard has two linings — an outer muscular wall and an inner protective lining. If the outer wall is perforated but the inner lining is intact, the gizzard still functions normally. No contents can escape; the inner lining provides full protection. The perforation is in the outer layer only and does not compromise the gizzard's ability to digest food.
