⚖️ Rav vs. Shmuel — Do Pieces of Neveilah Combine?

Rav
Two partial pieces of neveilah flesh combine (tziruf) to constitute a kezayit. The Torah says 'neveilah' without restricting it to one continuous piece.
Shmuel
Pieces do not combine. Each piece must itself be a kezayit. The Torah's shiur requires a single complete unit — fragments cannot be aggregated.
Rav
We find that tum'at ochlin allows combining pieces to reach k'beitza — the same logic applies to neveilah reaching kezayit.
Shmuel
Tum'at ochlin is different — it is a rabbinic category with looser aggregation rules. Torah-level neveilah tumah demands a stricter standard.
Halacha The halacha follows Rav: partial pieces of neveilah flesh may combine (tziruf) to form a kezayit for the purpose of tum'at neveilah.

🔁 Two Categories of Tumah from Neveilah

Tum'at Neveilah

  • Minimum: k'zayit (olive's bulk)
  • Source: Torah — וְהַנֹּשֵׂא אֶת נִבְלָתָהּ
  • Acquired by carrying or contact
  • Applies to flesh and fat of carcass
  • Bones, sinew, hide: excluded

Tum'at Ochlin

  • Minimum: k'beitza (egg's bulk)
  • Source: Rabbinic / derived
  • Requires prior hechsher (liquid contact)
  • Neveilah can qualify as ochel
  • Intent (machshava) can establish ochel status

🍖 When Is Neveilah Considered 'Ochel' (Food)?

כָּל שֶׁהוּא רָאוּי לַאֲכִילָה — חָשִׁיב אוֹכֶל לְעִנְיַן טוּמְאָה
Core Principle The Gemara debates whether neveilah retains the legal status of ochel for tum'at ochlin. Abaye holds that since neveilah flesh is still fit for consumption (it may be sold to a non-Jew), it remains categorized as ochel and can become susceptible to food-impurity. Rava teaches that human intention (machshava) is the decisive factor — if one designates the meat as food, it acquires ochel status regardless of its neveilah classification.

🔎 Determining Neveilah Tumah — Step by Step

Step 1 — Identify the material: Is it flesh or fat of neveilah? Bone, sinew, and hide are excluded.
↓
Step 2 — Measure the quantity: Does a single piece reach a kezayit (olive's bulk)?
↓
Step 3 — If below kezayit: Can partial pieces combine? Per Rav (halacha): yes, tziruf applies.
↓
Step 4 — Tumah is conveyed to anyone who carries or contacts the kezayit of neveilah flesh.
↓
Step 5 — Secondary check: Does the amount reach k'beitza? If yes, tum'at ochlin may also apply (requires prior hechsher).
