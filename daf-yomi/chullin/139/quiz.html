QUESTION 1 OF 5
What word in Genesis 3:11 is used as a hint to Haman in the Torah?
A. הֲמִן הָעֵץ — 'HaMin ha-etz' (did you eat from the tree?)
B. וְהָמָן — 'VeHaman' (and Haman)
C. הֲמוֹן עַם — 'Hamon am' (a multitude of people)
D. מֵהָמָן — 'MeHaman' (from Haman)
Correct: A — Chullin 139b: God's question to Adam — 'HaMin ha-etz?' — contains the letters ה-מ-ן spelling Haman, linking his evil to the primordial sin in the Garden of Eden.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Which verse in Devarim hints at Esther's name?
A. הַסְתֵּר אַסְתִּיר פָּנַי (Deut. 31:18) — 'I will surely hide My face'
B. וְאָנֹכִי נֹתֵן (Deut. 11:13) — 'And I hereby give'
C. לֹא תַסְתִּיר (Deut. 22:1) — 'You shall not hide'
D. כִּי אֶסְתֹּר (Deut. 6:18) — 'When I hide'
Correct: A — The double root ס-ת-ר in 'haster astir' (הַסְתֵּר אַסְתִּיר) is identical to the root of Esther's name, symbolizing God's hidden presence during the Persian exile when His name is absent from the Megillah.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara find Mordechai's name in the Torah?
A. 'Mor deror' (Exod. 30:23) rendered by Targum as 'mira dakia' = Persian Marduka
B. 'Mordeh ba-kol' (rebel against all) in Deut. 21
C. 'Mardut' (discipline) in Lev. 26:28
D. 'Moreh derekh' (guide the path) in Gen. 24
Correct: A — Exodus 30:23 lists מָר דְּרוֹר (pure myrrh) among the holy anointing oil spices. Targum Onkelos renders it 'mira dakia,' the Aramaic form of the Persian royal name Marduka — which is Mordechai.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Tanna Kamma, which situation is EXEMPT from shiluach ha-ken?
A. A domesticated hen nesting inside her owner's courtyard
B. A wild dove found on eggs in a field
C. A kosher bird sitting on eggs in a tree in the forest
D. A wild bird sitting on chicks in the desert wilderness
Correct: A — Shiluach ha-ken requires a bird found in a wild, ownerless setting. A domesticated bird nesting within its owner's domain is not considered a 'free nest in the field' and is therefore exempt from the mitzvah.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What phrase in Exodus 2:2 hints at Moshe, and what is its deeper connection?
A. כִּי טוֹב הוּא — 'Ki tov hu' — echoes the light of Creation (Gen. 1:4 'ki tov ha-or')
B. וְהִיא טוֹבָה — 'She was good' — his mother's virtue
C. מֹשֶׁה טוֹב — 'Moshe is good' — his name means 'drawn out'
D. כִּי טוֹב הַמַּיִם — 'The water was good' — refers to the Nile
Correct: A — Exodus 2:2 says his mother saw 'ki tov hu' (that he was good). The Gemara links this to God's declaration about the primordial light — 'ki tov ha-or' (Gen. 1:4) — hinting that Moshe carried the light of Creation within him from birth.
