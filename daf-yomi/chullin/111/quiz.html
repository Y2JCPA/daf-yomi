QUESTION 1 OF 5
Mar Ukva calls himself 'vinegar the son of wine' compared to his father. What does this describe about their respective meat-dairy practices?
A. His father waited 6 hours; Mar Ukva only waited 3 hours
B. His father waited until the equivalent meal the next day; Mar Ukva waited only until the next meal
C. His father never ate meat and dairy in the same week; Mar Ukva ate them daily
D. His father required full hand-washing; Mar Ukva only rinsed his mouth
Correct: B — Mar Ukva's father would not eat dairy until the same mealtime the following day (~24 hours), while Mar Ukva himself waited only until the next meal. His self-deprecating metaphor (vinegar vs. fine wine) acknowledges the lesser stringency while still establishing the halachic minimum.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
On what basis does the Talmud prohibit eating fish and meat together?
A. It violates the Torah prohibition of basar b'chalav
B. It is a rabbinic fence around the laws of forbidden mixtures
C. It creates a physical health danger (sakana)
D. It is prohibited only when cooked together, not when eaten separately
Correct: C — The prohibition against combining fish and meat is not a kashrut category violation — it is rooted in concern for health danger (sakana). This is why it applies even to cold preparations and requires its own separate precautions from basar b'chalav rules.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If a knife was used to cut cold fish and you now want to cut meat with it, what is required?
A. Full kashering in boiling water (hagalah)
B. Rinsing or cleaning the knife to remove fish residue
C. Burying the knife in soil for purification
D. Nothing — knives used with cold food do not require any cleaning
Correct: B — For cold use, rinsing the knife is sufficient to prevent fish residue from transferring to the meat. Full kashering would be required if the knife was used with hot food, since heat causes deeper absorption of flavor into the blade.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the halachic ruling derived from Mar Ukva's statement, how long must one wait after eating meat before eating dairy?
A. 30 minutes
B. 3 hours
C. 6 hours
D. 12 hours
Correct: C — The Rambam and Shulchan Aruch (YD 89:1) derive a 6-hour wait from Mar Ukva's 'meal to meal' standard, since meals were typically spaced 6 hours apart. Some Ashkenazic communities ruled 1 or 3 hours based on alternate readings of this sugya.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is required before eating meat after dairy — as opposed to eating dairy after meat?
A. The same 6-hour wait required after meat
B. A 1-hour wait plus washing hands thoroughly
C. Only rinsing the mouth and checking or washing hands — no time wait
D. Eating a full piece of bread and waiting 20 minutes
Correct: C — The Talmud teaches a crucial asymmetry: dairy does not linger in the body the way meat does. Therefore, after dairy, one need only rinse the mouth and check/wash the hands before eating meat — no waiting period is required. The time wait applies exclusively from meat to dairy.
