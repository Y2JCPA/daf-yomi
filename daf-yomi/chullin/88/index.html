🤝 Who Must Cover? Shochet vs. Shaliach

Rav Huna
The obligation of kisui is upon the one who slaughtered. He may appoint a shaliach — shlucho shel adam kemoto — and the agent's covering fulfills his obligation.
Opposing View
The verse says ve'shafach… ve'cissahu — the same person who pours must cover. Pouring and covering are one act; an uninstructed third party cannot substitute for the shochet.
Rav Huna
Even so: if a bystander covered without being appointed, the obligation is fulfilled b'dieved. But the shochet himself did not perform the mitzvah and may not recite the blessing retroactively.
Practical Ruling Ideally the shochet covers and blesses. If another covered it, fulfilled after the fact — no blessing is recited.

🐟 Why Fish Blood Needs No Covering

The verse (Leviticus 17:13) obligates covering only the blood of chaya (wild animal) and עוף (bird)
↓
Fish (dagim) are not included in either biblical category of chaya or bird
↓
The Gemara adds a second reason: dam dagim is not visually recognizable as blood from afar — it does not look like blood when spilled on the ground
↓
Therefore: dam dagim einam tzarichin kisui — fish blood does not require covering, even if slaughtered intentionally
↓
Exception: if fish blood is mixed with chaya blood and the chaya blood remains visible, the mixture triggers the obligation

✡️ Abraham's Oath and Israel's Eternal Reward

אָמַר לוֹ הַקָּב״ה: אַתָּה אָמַרְתָּ מִחוּט וְעַד שְׂרוֹךְ נַעַל — חַיֶּיךָ שֶׁאֲנִי מְשַׁלֵּם לְבָנֶיךָ חוּט שֶׁל תְּכֵלֶת וּרְצוּעוֹת שֶׁל תְּפִלִּין
Aggadah — Chullin 88b Abraham declared to the King of Sodom: "Im michut ve'ad sroch na'al lo ekach" — 'I will take nothing from a thread to a sandal strap' (Genesis 14:23). The Holy One said: You swore by My Name not to take a thread or a strap — by your life, I will reward your children: the thread of techelet wound in tzitzit, and the leather straps of tefillin. Abraham's act of selfless restraint became the source of Israel's most visible physical mitzvot.

🦌 Chaya vs. Behema: Kisui HaDam

Chaya (Wild Animal)

  • Examples: deer, gazelle, wild ox (צבי, איל, תאו)
  • Blood must be covered after kosher slaughter
  • Blessing recited before covering: עַל כִּסּוּי הַדָּם
  • Must use earth or earth-like material (afar)
  • Failure to cover violates a positive commandment

Behema (Domesticated Animal)

  • Examples: cow, sheep, goat (בקר, צאן)
  • Blood does not require covering
  • No blessing is recited at all
  • Verse explicitly limits obligation to chaya and bird
  • Standard shechita still required for permissibility
