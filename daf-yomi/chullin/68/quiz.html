QUESTION 1 OF 5
If a laboring animal's fetus extends its foreleg BEFORE shechita, what is the status of that limb?
A. Permitted — shechita covers the entire fetus
B. Forbidden as ever min hachai
C. Requires separate shechita to become permitted
D. Permitted only if the limb is returned inside first
Correct: B — A limb that protrudes from the live fetus before shechita acquires the prohibition of ever min hachai (אבר מן החי) — a limb from a living creature. The mother's subsequent shechita cannot retroactively remove this prohibition from the limb, though the rest of the fetus is permitted.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is a 'ben pekuah' (בן פקועה)?
A. An animal that resisted slaughter and had to be forced
B. A live fetus found inside a properly slaughtered mother
C. A firstborn animal redeemed by a Kohen
D. An animal born prematurely before 9 months
Correct: B — Ben pekuah is a fetus found alive inside an animal after valid shechita. Since its mother was slaughtered, the fetus is treated as if it too was slaughtered (ירך אמו — 'the thigh of its mother') and does not require its own ritual slaughter.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the dispute between Rabbi Yochanan and Reish Lakish on Chullin 68?
A. Whether ben pekuah requires its own shechita
B. Whether a human fetus follows the same rules as an animal fetus
C. Whether returning an emerged limb before shechita removes the ever min hachai prohibition
D. Whether the mother's shechita requires kavvanah (intent)
Correct: C — Rabbi Yochanan holds that pushing the limb back inside before shechita resolves the problem — the limb was never truly detached. Reish Lakish disagrees: once the prohibition of ever min hachai attached upon emergence, returning the limb cannot undo it.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If a fetus is entirely inside the mother at the moment of shechita, what is its status?
A. Forbidden — it requires its own shechita
B. Permitted — shechita covers all contents of the animal
C. Permitted only if it is already fully developed
D. Depends on whether it is alive or dead at the time
Correct: B — The halachic principle is that shechita covers whatever is fully inside the animal at the moment of slaughter. The fetus is treated as ירך אמו ('the thigh of its mother'), an organic part of her body, and is fully permitted by her shechita.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the fetus's limb that emerged AFTER shechita have no ever min hachai problem?
A. Because the limb emerged after the animal was already dead
B. Because a fetus's limbs have a different legal status than an adult animal's
C. Because the prohibition of ever min hachai only applies to humans
D. Because the shechita must have preceded the emergence to be effective
Correct: A — Once shechita is completed, the animal is halachically dead. A limb that extends after this point is not emerging from a living creature. Therefore ever min hachai — which requires a living animal — cannot apply, and the entire fetus including the limb is permitted.
