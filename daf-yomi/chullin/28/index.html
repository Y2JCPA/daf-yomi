🐦 Bird Shechitah — Torah or Rabbinic?

RY
Rabbi (R' Yehuda HaNasi): Bird shechitah is a Torah obligation. Source: "as I have commanded you" (Devarim 12:21) — God commanded Moshe the laws of shechitah at Sinai, including for birds. This is the majority view.
RK
R' Elazar HaKappar: Bird shechitah is only Rabbinic. The Torah never explicitly requires it for birds. The Sages enacted it to maintain consistency.
Blood covering as proof: The Torah commands covering the blood of birds after shechitah. If shechitah for birds were merely Rabbinic, why would the Torah specifically mention covering blood afterward? This supports the majority view that it's Torah-mandated.

🔪 Which Siman for a Bird?

RN
Rav Nachman: For a bird, one may cut EITHER the windpipe or the gullet — either one is sufficient. The choice is the slaughterer's.
Gm
The Gemara derives this from the Mishna's language: "one siman" — without specifying which. This implies either one is valid for a bird.

🦆 The Duck with the Questionable Windpipe

A duck was brought whose windpipe might have been severed BEFORE shechitah (by injury)
↓
Problem: If the windpipe was already severed, cutting it during shechitah would mean nothing was actually slaughtered — resulting in neveilah!
↓
Suggestion: Slaughter it and then examine the windpipe afterward?
↓
Rejected! The slaughterer might cut precisely at the severed spot, making it impossible to tell if the windpipe was already cut before
↓
Solution (Rav Yosef b. Rava): Examine the windpipe externally first (you can see a prior severance from outside), then cut the windpipe and render it kosher

🩸 R' Yehuda — Must Cut the Veins

RY
R' Yehuda (in the Mishna): Shechitah is not valid until one also cuts the jugular veins. The blood must flow from the veins — not just the simanim.
RH
Rav Chisda (explaining R' Yehuda): This requirement applies only to birds, because when people eat birds, they typically want the blood drained properly. For animals, the regular shechitah suffices.
Is this about blood or about shechitah? The Gemara questions: if R' Yehuda's concern is blood drainage, the veins should be cut AFTER shechitah (for draining). But he says "until he cuts the veins" — implying it's part of the shechitah itself. Resolution: "cuts" means "punctures at the moment of slaughter" — the veins are nicked during the slaughter process so blood flows immediately.

🎓 Key Principle: The duck case is a masterclass in practical halachic problem-solving.
