⚔️ Does Chatzi Sheretz (Half a Creeping Creature) Convey Tumah?

Rav
A half-sheretz that totals a k'adasha is tamei. The Torah says 'the sheretz' — it does not require the complete body, only the requisite volume of sheretz-matter.
Shmuel
A half-sheretz is tahor. The Torah's tumah is tied to the whole creature as a named entity — splitting it removes that designation, even if volume is met.
Rabbi Yochanan
I agree with Rav's ruling in practice. The shiur k'adasha functions independently of wholeness — it is a volume standard, not a form standard.
Reish Lakish
We must ask: was the piece cut while the creature was alive or after death? The circumstance of cutting changes the analysis entirely.
📌 Core Question Is the k'adasha shiur a measure of 'sheretz-ness' (permitting any portion) or a size-threshold requiring the creature's identity to remain intact?

💧 Tzir HaSheretz — Brine/Liquid of Creeping Creatures

View A: Tzir IS Tamei

  • The tzir (צִיר) of a sheretz flows from its body and retains sheretz-identity
  • Just as tzir of a fish can be significant for kashrut, so too for tumah
  • The liquid is considered an extension of the creature's substance
  • Source: analogy to dam (blood) — blood of sheratzim is tamei

View B: Tzir is NOT Tamei

  • Tzir is a pirsha b'alma — mere secretion with no tumah force
  • The Torah specifies 'the sheretz' — only solid body-matter qualifies
  • Liquid cannot achieve a k'adasha shiur in the normal sense
  • Tzir of neveilah is also debated similarly — same logic applies here

Practical Outcome

  • Majority ruling: tzir of sheretz does NOT convey tumah
  • Dam (blood) of sheretz IS tamei — distinguished from tzir
  • The distinction: blood is categorized as part of the body; tzir is waste
  • Applies to: handling sheretz liquid, vessels that contain it

🔗 Ever min HaChai from Non-Kosher Animals

אֵבֶר מִן הַחַי מִן הַבְּהֵמָה הַטְּמֵאָה — טָמֵא טֻמְאַת נְבֵלוֹת
⚠️ Key Distinction A limb separated from a living non-kosher animal (ever min hachai) presents a dual-tumah problem: (1) it may convey neveilah-tumah and (2) it may convey sheretz-type tumah if the animal is among the sheratzim. The Gemara asks: if a sheretz is still alive and a limb falls off, is that limb tamei as a sheretz-portion or as an ever min hachai? These are governed by different rules and different shiurim.

⚖️ How to Determine if a Sheretz Portion is Tamei — Step by Step

Step 1: Identify the creature. Is it one of the eight sheratzim listed in Vayikra 11:29–30? If not, no sheretz-tumah applies (though other tumah categories may).
↓
Step 2: Check if the creature is dead. Sheretz tumah applies to the carcass. A living sheretz does not convey tumah through contact in the same way.
↓
Step 3: Measure the portion. Is the piece at least a k'adasha (lentil-size, approximately 100 mg)? If below this shiur, it is tahor regardless of source.
↓
Step 4: Assess integrity. Is this a whole small sheretz, or a piece of a larger one? Apply the machloket of Rav vs. Shmuel regarding chatzi sheretz.
↓
Step 5: Check for tzir or dam. If only liquid is present, apply the ruling that tzir is tahor but dam (blood) of sheretz retains tumah status.
↓
Step 6: Determine tumah transmission. Sheretz conveys tumah by maga (touch) and masa (carrying) — but NOT by ohel (tent/overhang), unlike a human corpse.
