QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why were the Kutim (Samaritans) ultimately declared non-Jews whose shechitah is invalid?
A. They refused to pay the Temple tax
B. It became clear they worshipped idols on Mount Gerizim and rejected Rabbinic authority
C. They ate pork publicly
D. They intermarried with Canaanite nations
Correct: B. The Kutim partially adopted Judaism but maintained idol worship on Mount Gerizim and rejected Rabbinic tradition (Oral Torah). When the Sages confirmed this, they declared the Kutim as non-Jews — making their shechitah, wine, and other religious acts invalid.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
A knife was checked and found smooth BEFORE shechitah. After shechitah, a nick is found. What is the status of the meat?
A. Kosher — the pre-check proved the knife was fine
B. Treif — we presume the nick was there during the cut (Chazakah)
C. Doubtful — requires a Rabbi's ruling
D. Kosher if the cut looks clean
Correct: B. The nick found after shechitah creates a presumption (Chazakah) that it was present during the cut. Since a nicked knife may cause tearing (Ikkur) instead of cutting, the meat is considered treif. The post-check finding overrides the pre-check.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What are the three surfaces of a shechitah knife that must be checked ("three on three")?
A. The handle, the blade, and the tip
B. The cutting edge, one flat side, and the other flat side
C. Top, middle, and bottom of the blade
D. The front, back, and heel of the blade
Correct: B. The three surfaces are: (1) the cutting edge itself, (2) one flat side of the blade, and (3) the other flat side. Each is checked with both the fingernail (to catch tiny nicks) and the fingertip (to detect broader imperfections). This "three on three" check ensures thoroughness.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Is a non-Jew's shechitah ever valid — even with a perfect knife, perfect cut, and Jewish supervision?
A. Yes — if supervised by a competent Jew
B. Yes — if the non-Jew is a Noahide who believes in God
C. No — a non-Jew's shechitah is NEVER valid, regardless of circumstances
D. Only for birds, not mammals
Correct: C. A non-Jew's shechitah is categorically invalid. The Gemara gives multiple reasons: lack of proper intent for God, concern for idolatrous dedication, and the Torah's address to Israel specifically ("you shall slaughter"). No amount of supervision or technical perfection can overcome this.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does the principle "Rov Beheimot Kesheirot" mean?
A. Most meat in the market is kosher
B. Most animals are presumed healthy (kosher) — we don't suspect every animal is treif without cause
C. The majority of an animal's body must be intact for it to be kosher
D. Most Rabbis agree on the rules of kashrut
Correct: B. "Rov Beheimot Kesheirot" means most animals are presumed healthy and kosher. We don't need to suspect every animal has a treifah (fatal defect) unless there's reason to check — such as an animal attack, convulsions, or abnormal behavior. After shechitah, a standard lung check confirms the presumption.
