⚔️ The Kuti (Samaritan) — A Complex Status

Who are the Kutim? The Samaritans — non-Jews resettled in the Land of Israel by Assyria (II Kings 17), who partially adopted Judaism but maintained their own practices and rejected Rabbinic authority.
RV
Initial ruling: A Kuti's shechitah is valid — "HaKol Shochatin" includes them. They accepted the Written Torah, so they know the basics of shechitah.
LT
Later ruling: The Kutim were ultimately declared as non-Jews by the Rabbis (R' Ami & R' Assi). From that point: their shechitah = non-Jew's shechitah = invalid.
Historical shift: Initially the Sages debated whether Kutim were "geirei arayot" (converts from fear of lions) or "geirei emet" (sincere converts). When it became clear they worshipped idols on Mount Gerizim, the Sages ruled them as non-Jews.

🔪 Examining the Knife — Before & After

BEFORE shechitah:
Check the knife for nicks/flaws ✅
Run fingernail + fingertip along both sides + edge
"Three on three" — 3 surfaces × both directions
↓
PERFORM the shechitah
Cut the simanim (trachea + esophagus)
Continuous back-and-forth motion
↓
AFTER shechitah:
Check the knife AGAIN for nicks! ⚠️
If a nick is found → what happened?
↓
Nick found after? The meat is treif!
We presume the nick existed DURING the cut (Chazakah)
The nick may have caused tearing instead of cutting = Ikkur

📜 Chazakah — The Status Quo Presumption

One of the most important halachic principles, applied extensively in Chullin:

What Is Chazakah?

  • 📖 "Presumption of status quo"
  • ⚖️ An object/person remains in their last known state
  • 🔪 Knife was smooth before → presumed smooth during shechitah
  • 🐄 Animal was alive → presumed alive until proven dead
  • 💡 We don't assume change without evidence

When Chazakah Breaks

  • 🔪 Nick found AFTER = chazakah says "nick was there during"
  • ❌ This OVERRIDES the pre-check — the nick presumably appeared during the cut
  • ⚠️ Multiple chazakot can conflict!
  • 📖 Knife checked clean BEFORE vs. nick found AFTER
  • 💡 The Gemara resolves: post-check finding wins

👤 Non-Jew's Shechitah — Always Invalid

Why is a non-Jew's shechitah always invalid — even with a perfect knife, perfect cut, and supervision?
↓
Three explanations in the Gemara:
↓
1. No intent for God
Even a righteous Gentile doesn't slaughter "in the name of Heaven" as required
2. Concern for idolatry
We suspect the non-Jew may dedicate the slaughter to their god
3. Torah exclusion
"You shall slaughter... and eat" — addressed to Israel, not the nations

🔍 The Knife Check — "Three on Three"

The slaughterer checks the knife by running their fingernail AND fingertip along three surfaces:
1️⃣
The Edge
The cutting blade itself — must be razor-smooth
2️⃣
Side A
One flat side of the blade — checking for burrs
3️⃣
Side B
Other flat side — even tiny imperfections matter
Why fingernail AND fingertip? The fingernail catches tiny nicks that the soft fingertip would miss. The fingertip detects broader imperfections the nail might skip over. Together, they create a thorough inspection system.
