Chullin 121 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the minimum measure (shiur) of neveilah flesh required to convey biblical ritual impurity (tum'ah)?
A. A kezayit (olive-size)
B. A kebeitzah (egg-size)
C. A revi'it (quarter-log)
D. Any amount — no minimum required
Correct: A — A kezayit (כַּזַּיִת) of neveilah flesh is the biblical minimum to convey tum'ah through touch (maga) or carrying (massa).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the ruling accepted in the gemara, does marrow inside an intact, uncracked bone combine with flesh to complete the kezayit for neveilah tum'ah?
A. Yes — marrow is always considered flesh
B. No — enclosed marrow does not combine with flesh
C. Yes, but only for birds, not land animals
D. Only if the bone belongs to the spine
Correct: B — Marrow inside an intact bone is not directly accessible and therefore does not combine with flesh for the kezayit. This follows Shmuel's position and is the accepted halacha.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the halachic status of animal hide (עוֹר) regarding neveilah tum'ah, when it appears alone without flesh?
A. It conveys full biblical neveilah tum'ah
B. It conveys tum'ah only when combined with sinews
C. It generally does not convey neveilah tum'ah on its own
D. It conveys tum'ah only if thicker than a kezayit
Correct: C — Animal hide is not flesh and does not independently convey neveilah tum'ah. Only flesh (and exposed marrow by some views) is the vehicle for this biblical impurity.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The halachic concept of 'tziruf' (צֵירוּף) as applied to neveilah tum'ah means:
A. Salting the meat to remove absorbed blood
B. Combining separate pieces of neveilah to reach the minimum kezayit
C. Wrapping the carcass in cloth to contain impurity
D. Separating the marrow from the bones before handling
Correct: B — Tziruf means combining — pieces of neveilah flesh that individually fall short but collectively equal a kezayit may be combined, so that touching any one piece conveys tum'ah.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the dispute between Rav and Shmuel on Chullin 121, what is the specific substance whose status is debated?
A. Blood of the neveilah
B. Marrow (מוֹחַ) inside a bone
C. The animal's hide
D. Pieces of flesh in two separate vessels
Correct: B — Rav and Shmuel dispute whether marrow (מוֹחַ) inside a bone counts toward the kezayit for neveilah tum'ah. The accepted ruling distinguishes between intact bones (no) and cracked/open bones (yes).
