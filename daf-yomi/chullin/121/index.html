🦴 Neveilah Tum'ah: The Kezayit Standard

נְבֵלָה — כַּזַּיִת מִבְּשָׂרָהּ מְטַמֵּא בְּמַגָּע וּבְמַשָּׂא
Core Question Chullin 121 examines which parts of a neveilah (animal carcass) count toward the minimum olive-sized (kezayit) measure required to convey ritual impurity. The gemara debates whether bones, marrow, hide, and sinews combine with flesh to reach the shiur — and under what conditions each substance is included.

🔥 Does Marrow Inside a Bone Count Toward the Kezayit?

Rav
The marrow (מוֹחַ) inside the bone is considered like the flesh itself. It combines with the surrounding flesh to complete the kezayit measure for neveilah tum'ah.
Shmuel
The marrow does not combine with the flesh. Only the visible, accessible flesh counts. Marrow enclosed in an intact bone is not directly accessible and is therefore excluded.
Rav Nachman
For practical rulings: when the bone is cracked and the marrow exposed, even Shmuel would agree it combines. The dispute concerns only intact, uncracked bones.
Halacha The accepted ruling: marrow inside an intact bone does not combine with flesh to form a kezayit. Once the bone is cracked and marrow exposed, it is treated as flesh and does combine.

📊 What Counts Toward the Neveilah Kezayit?

✅ Counts Toward Kezayit

  • Flesh (בָּשָׂר) of the neveilah
  • Exposed marrow from a cracked bone
  • Fatty tissue attached to the meat
  • Multiple pieces combined via tziruf (if touching)

❌ Does Not Count

  • Marrow in an intact, uncracked bone
  • Hide and skin (עוֹר) of the animal
  • Sinews and tendons (גִּידִים)
  • Hooves, horns, and feathers
  • Bones themselves (without marrow)

⚠️ Disputed or Conditional

  • Marrow in a partially cracked bone
  • Skin adjacent to a kezayit of flesh (some hold it joins)
  • Pieces in same vessel but not touching — tziruf disputed

🧮 Applying the Rule of Tziruf — Step by Step

Step 1: Identify all pieces of neveilah flesh present — check if any are in contact with each other or in a shared vessel
↓
Step 2: Measure each piece individually. Does any single piece already equal a kezayit on its own?
↓
Step 3: If not, check for tziruf: pieces touching directly combine. Pieces in the same vessel but not touching — this is disputed by the Rishonim.
↓
Step 4: If the combined total equals a kezayit, touching any one piece conveys tum'ah to the person — even if they only touched a sub-kezayit fragment.
↓
Step 5: Carrying (massa) the combined kezayit also conveys tum'ah, even without skin-to-skin contact with the flesh.
