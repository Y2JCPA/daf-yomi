🔥 Cooking Birds with Milk — D'Oraita or D'Rabbanan?

Beit Shammai
Bird meat cooked with milk is forbidden from the Torah — the verse לא תבשל גדי בחלב אמו applies to any flesh that could have a mother's milk relationship.
Beit Hillel
Birds have no mother's milk, so the biblical verse cannot apply. The prohibition is only rabbinic — one may even serve bird meat on the same table as cheese.
Rabbi Akiva
Fish and locusts are entirely permitted with milk — neither has a womb-and-milk relationship. No verse touches them even rabbinically.
Halacha L'Maaseh We rule like Beit Hillel: bird + milk is d'rabbanan. The Sages nonetheless forbade it publicly lest observers confuse it with the d'oraita prohibition of domestic-animal meat with milk.

🐄 Which Meat Triggers Which Level of Prohibition?

D'Oraita — Torah Level

  • Domestic animals (בהמה טהורה)
  • Cooked in milk of same or related species
  • Forbidden to cook, eat, AND benefit from
  • Source: לא תבשל גדי בחלב אמו (three times in Torah)

D'Rabbanan — Rabbinic Level

  • Birds (עוף) — no actual mother's milk
  • Wild animals (חיה) — majority view
  • Non-kosher animal meat with milk
  • Forbidden to cook and eat; benefit is permitted

Fully Permitted

  • Fish (דגים) with any dairy
  • Locusts (חגבים) with dairy
  • Pareve cooked in clean fleishig vessel
  • No prohibition — biblical or rabbinic

🍶 Was Flavor Absorbed? — The Pot Question

Dairy ingredient placed in a fleishig pot
↓
Is the pot בן יומו — used for meat within the last 24 hours?
Yes — Ben Yomo
Pot imparts actual flavor (נותן טעם לשבח). The dairy mixture is forbidden as basar b'chalav.
No — Eino Ben Yomo
Absorbed flavor is degraded (נותן טעם לפגם). The mixture is permitted b'dieved.

⚖️ Abaye & Rava — Accidental Mixtures and Bitul

Abaye
A drop of milk that falls accidentally into a pot of meat: if there is sixty parts meat to one part milk, the milk's flavor is imperceptible and the mixture is permitted (בטל בשישים).
Rava
Agreed — but if the milk fell while the pot was actively on the fire, active cooking intensifies absorption. One must ensure 60:1 is calculated after full boiling, not before.
Rav Ashi
Both agree: intentional mixing is never permitted regardless of ratio. Bitul applies only to accidents — someone who deliberately cooks meat in milk cannot rely on שישים.
Practical Rule Bitul b'shishim (1:60 nullification) is the halachic standard for accidental meat-milk contamination. Intent to mix forfeits all leniencies.

🦌 Status of Wild Animals (חיה) in Meat-Milk Laws

Biblical verse analyzed: 'לא תבשל גדי בחלב אמו' — the word גדי (kid) refers to domestic animals (בהמה), specifically goats, sheep, and cattle.
↓
Question posed: Does חיה (wild animal — deer, gazelle, etc.) fall under this prohibition? It has mother's milk and is eaten, but is not a בהמה.
↓
First position: Since the Torah lists only בהמה explicitly, wild animals are excluded from the d'oraita prohibition entirely.
↓
Second position: Rabbinic authorities extended the prohibition to חיה as a גזירה (decree), parallel to the rabbinic prohibition on birds — the same logic applies.
↓
Conclusion accepted: חיה meat with milk is forbidden rabbinically. No benefit prohibition applies; nullification ratios still govern accidental mixtures.
