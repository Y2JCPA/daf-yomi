⚖️ Is a Fully Formed Fetus Covered by the Mother's Slaughter?

Chachamim
Once a fetus is fully formed — its hair has emerged (שיצאו עליו שערות) — it is a separate creature. The mother's shechita does NOT cover it; it requires its own slaughter.
Rabbi Meir
The fetus is always ירך אמו — the thigh of its mother — at every stage of development. The mother's shechita always renders the fetus permitted, no separate slaughter needed.
🔑 Core Question At what point — if ever — does a fetus in the womb become legally independent of its mother for purposes of ritual slaughter?

📜 The Mishnah: Finding a Fetus Inside a Slaughtered Animal

השוחט את הבהמה ומצא בה עובר — אם יצאו עליו שערות, טעון שחיטה; ואם לאו, אינו טעון שחיטה
Mishnah — Chullin 74 If one slaughters an animal and finds a fetus inside her womb:

• If the fetus was fully formed (שיצאו עליו שערות — its hair had emerged), it requires its own shechita and is subject to the law of ever min hachai.

• If it was not yet fully formed, it is permitted together with its mother — it is treated as part of her body and slaughtered along with her.

⚖️ The Fundamental Dispute: Yerech Imo vs. Independent Entity

Chachamim
Hair growth signals a threshold crossing — the fetus has become a fully realized animal. It is no longer merely 'part of' the mother; it stands as its own creature requiring independent halachic treatment.
Rabbi Meir
From conception until birth, the fetus is ירך אמו — like a limb of the mother. No external sign changes this. The mother's shechita covers it completely at every stage.
📌 Halacha l'Maaseh The halacha follows Chachamim: a fully formed fetus (hair grown) requires its own shechita. A fetus without hair is permitted with the mother.

🦴 Ever Min HaChai and the Fetus

Limb of Fetus While Mother Is Alive

  • Fetus has living status independent of its mother for this prohibition
  • A limb severed from the living fetus is forbidden as ever min hachai
  • This applies even though the fetus is entirely enclosed within the mother
  • The fetus's own vitality triggers the prohibition independently

After the Mother's Valid Shechita

  • Not fully formed fetus: ever min hachai concern dissolves — permitted entirely
  • Fully formed fetus (Chachamim): still requires own shechita; limbs remain problematic
  • Limbs that protruded before slaughter and were severed remain forbidden
  • Shechita of fetus, when required, removes the ever min hachai status
