Chullin 74 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Chachamim, what sign indicates that a fetus found in a slaughtered animal requires its own shechita?
A. It has opened its eyes and is breathing
B. Its hair has emerged (שיצאו עליו שערות)
C. It weighs more than a kezayit
D. It has separated from the placenta
Correct: B — Chachamim hold that the emergence of the fetus's hair (שיצאו עליו שערות) is the threshold marker for being 'fully formed,' at which point it is a separate creature requiring its own shechita.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rabbi Meir mean when he says the fetus is 'ירך אמו' (yerech imo)?
A. The fetus must be buried alongside its mother
B. The fetus is legally treated as a limb or part of the mother at all stages
C. The fetus inherits the mother's firstborn status
D. The fetus is only permitted if the mother was a firstborn animal
Correct: B — 'Yerech imo' means 'the thigh of its mother' — Rabbi Meir holds the fetus is always legally an extension of the mother's body, so her shechita covers it completely regardless of how developed it is.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
The prohibition of 'ever min hachai' in relation to the fetus means:
A. One may not slaughter a pregnant animal on a Festival
B. A limb severed from a living fetus is forbidden even though the fetus is inside the mother
C. The fetus is forbidden if the mother was not slaughtered on the correct day
D. One may not mix the fetus meat with milk
Correct: B — Ever min hachai (a limb from a living animal) applies to the fetus independently. Even enclosed within the mother, the fetus is considered alive, and a limb severed from it carries the ever min hachai prohibition.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If a fetus is found inside a slaughtered animal and has NOT yet grown its hair, what is the halacha?
A. It is forbidden as nevelah since it was never directly slaughtered
B. It is permitted — it is considered part of its mother and covered by her shechita
C. It requires a separate beracha before eating
D. It may only be eaten by the one who performed the slaughter
Correct: B — A fetus that has not yet grown hair is not fully formed and is treated as part of the mother (ירך אמו). All opinions agree it is permitted when the mother is validly slaughtered — no separate shechita is required.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If a fully formed fetus is found in a slaughtered animal and is NOT given its own shechita, according to the halacha (Chachamim), what is its status?
A. It is permitted since the mother's slaughter partially covers it
B. It is rabbinically forbidden but biblically permitted
C. It is forbidden as nevelah — an unslaughtered carcass
D. It is permitted only if consumed within the same day
Correct: C — According to Chachamim (who rule the halacha), a fully formed fetus is an independent creature. Without its own valid shechita, it is considered nevelah — a forbidden carcass — even though its mother was properly slaughtered.
