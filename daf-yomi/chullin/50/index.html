⚖️ Does a Clean-Peeling Sirkha Permit the Lung?

Rav Huna
If a sirkha between two lobes peels away cleanly — separating like a membrane without tearing the underlying tissue — we assume the lung was never perforated. The animal is kosher.
Rav Chisda
Even a clean peel cannot reassure us. The adhesion may have formed over a prior perforation that has since sealed. We must suspect hidden damage — the animal should be ruled treifah.
Rava
The halakha follows Rav Huna: if the sirkha peels without tearing, the lung is presumed intact. This peel test is the operative ruling for Ashkenazic practice.
Legacy of This Dispute Rav Huna's ruling (peel test) underlies Ashkenazic practice (Rema). Rav Chisda's stringency underlies the Sephardic chalak requirement — no sirkhot permitted at all, regardless of how they peel.

✨ Chalak vs. Standard: Two Traditions from One Debate

Chalak (Smooth) — Beit Yosef / Sephardic

  • Lung must be entirely smooth — no sirkhot at all
  • Any adhesion = treifah, peel test irrelevant
  • Based on the stricter reading: sirkha may hide perforation
  • Higher monetary cost; stricter butcher oversight required
  • Standard among Sephardic, Yemenite, and many Mizrachi communities

Non-Chalak — Rema / Ashkenazic

  • Sirkhot permitted if peel test passes cleanly
  • Follows Rav Huna as codified by Rava
  • Lobe-to-lobe: kosher if peels without tearing
  • Lung-to-chest-wall adhesion: treifah regardless
  • Standard Ashkenazic practice; widely available

💀 The Dried-Out Lobe

נִתְיַבְּשָׁה הָרֵאָה — כְּנִקְּבָה דָּמְיָא וּטְרֵפָה
Gemara Ruling If a lobe of the lung has dried out and shriveled — and when rubbed, it crumbles rather than remaining elastic — the animal is treifah. The Gemara teaches: a dried lobe is halakhically equivalent to a missing lobe. Since a missing lung lobe is among the eighteen treifot, the animal cannot have survived and is forbidden.

Rav Pappa provides the practical test: rub the dried area between your fingers. If it disintegrates, it is treifah. If it remains firm (just discolored), it may still be kosher and requires further examination.

🔍 The Full Lung Inspection Protocol

Step 1 — Remove the lung intact: After shechitah, extract the lung carefully, preserving all lobes and the surrounding membrane.
→
Step 2 — Visual scan: Look for discoloration, shriveling, visible lesions, or areas of abnormal texture on each lobe.
→
Step 3 — Sirkha check: Run fingers over every lobe surface and between lobes. Note any tissue bridging lobes or connecting to the chest wall.
→
Step 4 — Peel test (Ashkenazic): Gently attempt to separate any adhesion. If it lifts cleanly without tearing underlying tissue → kosher. If it tears → treifah.
→
Step 5 — Inflation in water: Inflate the lung while submerged. Air bubbles escaping = perforation = treifah. No bubbles = lung membrane intact.
→
Step 6 — Chalak standard (Sephardic): If any sirkha was found at all — stop. The lung is treifah regardless of the peel result or inflation test.
