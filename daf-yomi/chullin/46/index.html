🦴 Spinal Cord, Liver & Lung — Boundaries & Tests

The Gemara continues its precision work: How far down does the spinal cord extend for treifah purposes? Where exactly must liver tissue remain? And when a lung makes a sound — how do you test whether it's actually perforated? Daf 46 introduces the famous "tepid water test" and debates the exact anatomy of the spinal cord's lower boundary.

🦴 Spinal Cord: Where Does It End?

Shmuel (via Rav Yehuda): The spinal cord extends until the point between the branches that split off behind the thighs
↓
Three successive points where nerves branch from the spine behind the pelvis
↓
Before 1st branch
Cut here = definitely treifah
Between 1st & 3rd
⚠️ Unknown — Shmuel says "I do not know"
After 3rd branch
Cut here = kosher
The fat kid vs. the skinny kid: Rav Dimi asked R' Yehuda to show him the branching points on a real animal. A fat kid: too much fat, can't see. A lean kid: bones stick out too far, can't see. R' Yehuda gave up showing and taught the rule verbally instead.

🐦 Bird Spinal Cord: How Far Down?

RY
R' Yannai: The spinal cord of a bird extends until below the level of the wings.
RL
Reish Lakish: Only until the point between the wings — not below them.
Ulla
I was standing before R' Shimon ben Pazi when they brought him an injured bird. He checked until the point between the wings. Then a messenger from the Nasi called him away...
?
Did he leave because he'd finished checking (supporting Reish Lakish)? Or did he just leave mid-inspection out of respect for the Nasi? We don't know!

🫒 Liver: Where Must the Olive-Bulk Remain?

R' Zeira

  • 📍 The olive-bulk must remain at the gallbladder attachment point
  • Told this to Rabba & Rav Yosef while they were fleeing an army!
  • "Refugees, hear this halakha..."

Rav Adda bar Ahava

  • 📍 The olive-bulk must remain where the liver "lives" — connected under the right kidney
  • The place of the liver's vital blood supply

Rav Pappa (Final Ruling)

  • 📍 To satisfy BOTH: need an olive-bulk at BOTH locations
  • One at the gallbladder AND one under the right kidney
  • Otherwise = treifah
Unresolved: What if the olive-bulk is scattered in small pieces? Or long and thin like a strip? Or flat? All remain teku.

🫁 Lung Membranes: Which Perforation Matters?

ScenarioResultReason
Outer membrane perforated, inner intact Kosher Inner membrane protects — like Rabba's "red date" lung
Inner membrane perforated, outer intact Kosher (halakha) Outer membrane seals it — dispute, but halakha follows lenient view
Both membranes perforated Treifah No protection remains — air escapes
Outer membrane entirely removed ("red date") Kosher Rabba: if inner membrane is intact, the lung still functions
Rav Nachman's mnemonic: "The red robe in which the lung rests" — the inner membrane is red, the outer is white. The "red robe" (inner) is the one that matters most.

💧 The Tepid Water Test

Lung makes a sound when inflated — is it perforated?
↓
If you know WHERE the sound comes from
Place a feather, saliva, or straw on that spot and inflate
↓
Saliva bubbles → Treifah
No bubbles → Kosher
If you DON'T know where
Submerge lung in tepid water and inflate
↓
Water bubbles → Treifah
No bubbles → Kosher
(Sound was just air between membranes)
Why tepid? Hot water contracts the lung (closes the hole). Cold water hardens it (may crack it). Only tepid water gives an accurate test.

🗺️ Daf 46 Summary Map

🦴 Spinal cord ends at three branching points behind pelvis. Before 1st = treifah. After 3rd = kosher. Between = unknown. Birds: until/between the wings (disputed)
↓
🫒 Liver: olive-bulk must remain at both the gallbladder connection AND the right kidney connection (Rav Pappa). Scattered/thin/flat pieces = unresolved
↓
🫁 Lung membranes: either membrane alone can protect the other. Both perforated = treifah. Outer removed entirely ("red date") = still kosher if inner is intact
↓
💧 Tepid water test: submerge lung, inflate, check for bubbles. Not hot (contracts), not cold (cracks). This is the definitive diagnostic tool
🎓 Key Principle: The Talmud is doing empirical medicine — the tepid water test is a genuine diagnostic procedure that was used by shochtim for centuries. The rabbis weren't guessing about lung perforations; they developed a reliable, replicable test. Halacha and applied science converge here.
