🐔 Is Poultry + Milk Biblical or Rabbinic?

R' Akiva
The verse says 'gedi' — a young domesticated mammal. Poultry is NOT called 'gedi' and is NOT included in the biblical prohibition at all.
Gemara
If poultry is biblically permitted with milk, why do the Sages forbid it entirely?
Chachamim
The Rabbis issued a decree (גְּזֵרָה) — poultry with milk is RABBINICALLY forbidden, lest one come to confuse it with the biblical prohibition and permit actual meat.
Gemara
Then why not also rabbinically forbid fish and locusts with milk by the same logic?
Chachamim
Fish and locusts bear no resemblance to meat. No bystander would confuse eating fish with milk for the forbidden mixture. The decree was not extended to them.
Bottom Line Poultry + milk: Rabbinically forbidden. Fish and locusts + milk: Completely permitted.

🧀 Hard Cheese Before Meat

לֹא תְבַשֵּׁל גְּדִי בַּחֲלֵב אִמּוֹ — שמות כג:יט
Special Stringency The Gemara teaches that one who eats hard, aged cheese (גְּבִינָה קָשָׁה) must take extra care before eating meat — cleansing the mouth and checking between the teeth — because aged cheese clings and lingers in the mouth like meat does after meat. Soft fresh cheese requires only rinsing the mouth. This asymmetry flows from the same logic as waiting after meat: physical residue creates a real mixture concern.

⚖️ Biblical vs. Rabbinic Basar B'Chalav

Biblical Prohibition (ד'אורייתא)

  • Applies to domesticated animals: cattle, sheep, goats
  • Three repetitions of verse → three separate prohibitions
  • Forbids cooking, eating, AND deriving any benefit
  • Violation subject to lashes (מַלְקוֹת)
  • Applies to the meat cooked in milk of any animal of that species

Rabbinic Prohibition (ד'רבנן)

  • Applies to poultry — visually resembles forbidden meat+milk
  • Enacted as a protective fence around Torah law
  • Some hold only eating is forbidden, benefit may be permitted
  • No biblical lashes — rabbinic penalty only
  • Does NOT extend to fish, locusts, or their eggs

🔍 Determining Basar B'Chalav Status — Step by Step

Step 1: Is the food flesh of a domesticated mammal (בְּהֵמָה) — cow, sheep, or goat?
↓
If Yes → Full biblical prohibition applies: forbidden to cook, eat, or derive benefit.
↓
If No — Is it poultry? The Rabbis decreed it forbidden to eat with milk as a precautionary fence (סְיָג לַתּוֹרָה).
↓
If No — Is it fish or locusts? No prohibition applies. May be eaten with milk freely.
↓
Special case — Hard aged cheese: Extra cleansing required before eating meat due to physical residue in mouth.
