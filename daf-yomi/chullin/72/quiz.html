Chullin 72 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is a 'ben pekuah'?
A. An animal born prematurely in the field
B. A fetus found alive in the womb of a properly slaughtered animal
C. The firstborn of a kosher animal
D. An animal whose mother died before it was weaned
Correct: B — Ben pekuah refers to a fetus found alive inside the womb of an animal that was properly slaughtered (שחיטה). Such a fetus does not require its own shechita and is permitted to eat.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the principle of 'guf echad' mean in the context of Chullin 72?
A. The fetus and mother have separate identities for all laws
B. The fetus is considered one body with the mother, limiting its independent legal status
C. A limb separated from the fetus has no tumah
D. Only the firstborn fetus is considered part of the mother
Correct: B — Guf echad (one body) means that in certain legal contexts — especially tumah — the fetus inside the womb is not treated as an independent entity but as part of the mother's body.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava, what role does the fetus play regarding tumah even if it is 'guf echad' with the mother?
A. It can become an av ha-tumah (primary source of impurity) independently
B. It has no role — it is completely invisible to tumah law
C. It functions as a 'yad' (handle), transmitting the mother's tumah upon contact
D. It purifies whoever touches it because of the mother's shechita
Correct: C — Rava holds that even within the guf echad framework, the fetus serves as a 'yad' (appendage or handle) — meaning touching the fetus is halakhically equivalent to touching the mother and can convey her tumah.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If a mother animal was a treifah (slaughtered but halakhically forbidden), what is the status of a live fetus found inside her?
A. The fetus is fully permitted — it is ben pekuah
B. The fetus requires its own shechita to be eaten
C. The fetus is forbidden to eat but is not a neveilah source of tumah
D. The fetus is tahor because it was never a living animal
Correct: C — A fetus inside a treifah animal is itself forbidden to eat (since the mother's treifah status affects it), but because shechita was performed on the mother, the fetus does not carry neveilah tumah — it is not a neveilah even though it is forbidden.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the key practical halakhic difference between Abaye's and Rava's positions on the fetus's tumah status?
A. Abaye says the fetus is tamei; Rava says it is tahor
B. Abaye says one who touches the fetus does not become tamei; Rava says contact with the fetus does convey the mother's tumah
C. Abaye permits eating the fetus; Rava forbids it
D. Abaye requires shechita for the fetus; Rava does not
Correct: B — The practical dispute: Abaye says the fetus is so fully 'inside' the mother that touching it yields no tumah independently. Rava says the fetus is a yad — touching it is like touching the mother, and therefore does convey tumah to the one who touches it.
