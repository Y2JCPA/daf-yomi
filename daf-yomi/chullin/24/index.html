🐄 Red Heifer — "Statute" Blocks Inference?

The Torah calls the Red Heifer a "chukat" (statute) — a law beyond rational understanding
↓
Does writing "statute" mean we cannot learn new laws from it via kal va'chomer (a fortiori inference)?
↓
Yes — blocked
The word "statute" limits derivations: this is a fixed, unique law
Not necessarily
Yom Kippur also says "statute" — yet we derive laws from it!
Resolution: The Gemara concludes that "statute" alone does not block kal va'chomer. There must be a specific verse to block the inference. Each case has its own limiting factor.

🪓 Eglah Arufah — Why Not Slaughter?

Eglah Arufah (Broken Neck)

  • 🪓 Neck is broken (arifah), not slaughtered
  • 📍 Performed in a rough valley (nachal eitan)
  • 🚫 Cannot be made fit through shechitah
  • 📜 Torah specifies this unique method

Red Heifer (Parah Adumah)

  • 🔪 Slaughtered (shechitah), not broken neck
  • 📍 Performed outside the camp
  • 🔥 Completely burned
  • 📜 Torah says "and he shall slaughter it"
The Gemara asks: Why not derive by kal va'chomer that the eglah arufah could be rendered fit through slaughter too? Answer: the Torah specifically restricts the broken-neck heifer to arifah — closing this logical loophole.

🎵 Levite Age Requirements

The Torah (Bamidbar 8:24-25) sets Levite service age: 25 to begin, 50 to retire
↓
But another verse (Bamidbar 4:3) says age 30! How to reconcile?
↓
Resolution: Age 25 = begin learning. Age 30 = begin actual service. Five years of apprenticeship!
"The trumpeters and singers were as one" (Divrei HaYamim II 5:13) — the Levites must sing in perfect unison. Those who cannot maintain the proper pitch are unfit for the singing service. This verse teaches the standard of excellence required.

👴 When Is a Kohen "Too Old"?

RH
R' Chanina: A Kohen is fit until his hands and feet begin to tremble — the physical signs of aging that prevent proper Temple service.
Gm
They said about R' Yishmael b. R' Yose: Even in his old age, he could stand on one leg and remove his shoe. When he could no longer do this, he declared himself unfit.

🧍 Physical Tests for Fitness

R' Chanina's test for "young" vs "old": Can the person stand on one leg and remove or put on a shoe? If yes — still considered young and fit for service. If not — aging has set in.
Full beard = community leader eligible: Once a man's beard is fully grown, he may serve as a communal emissary, lead public prayer, and perform the Priestly Blessing (birkat kohanim). The beard signals maturity and community standing.
Seminal emission immersion: One who immersed but did not urinate first — when he later urinates, he becomes ritually impure again. This teaches that incomplete purification processes can undo themselves.
