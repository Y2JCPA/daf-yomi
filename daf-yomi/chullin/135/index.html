⚖️ Reishit HaGez: What Triggers the Obligation?

Rabbi Dosa b. Harkinas
Five sheep are required — and each must have at least 60 maneh of wool. Only then is the owner obligated to give the first shearing to the Kohen.
Sages
Any five sheep create the obligation regardless of individual wool yield. Ownership of the flock is the criterion, not the quantity each sheep produces.
Gemara
The practical dispute concerns low-yield flocks: R. Dosa exempts lightly-shorn sheep; the Sages obligate as long as there are five animals.
Biblical Source רֵאשִׁית גֵּז צֹאנְךָ תִּתֶּן לּוֹ — 'The first of the shearing of your flock shall you give to him [the Kohen]' (Devarim 18:4)

🐑 Rabbi Dosa b. Harkinas vs. Sages: Minimum Requirements

Rabbi Dosa b. Harkinas

  • 5 sheep required
  • Each sheep must produce ≥ 60 maneh of wool
  • Combined minimum: 300 maneh total
  • Enough wool to produce a full garment
  • Stricter standard — smaller flocks may be exempt
  • Low-yield sheep do not count toward the obligation

Sages (Accepted Halacha)

  • 5 sheep required
  • No minimum wool quantity per sheep
  • Any shearing of 5 sheep triggers obligation
  • Give enough for a small garment to the Kohen
  • More inclusive — most flocks are obligated
  • Wool quantity is irrelevant to the threshold

🧥 How to Fulfill Reishit HaGez Step by Step

Step 1 — Own at least 5 sheep
This is the minimum number agreed upon by all opinions to trigger the mitzva
↓
Step 2 — Shear the flock
The obligation arises at the time of shearing; it does not apply to wool that falls off naturally
↓
Step 3 — Separate the first portion
Must set aside a portion before using any wool for personal purposes
↓
Step 4 — Determine the minimum amount
Enough to make a small garment: at least the weight of 5 sela of Judean wool or 10 sela of Galilean wool (Galilean wool was lighter)
↓
Step 5 — Give to any Kohen of your choosing
Unlike some priestly gifts, reishit hagez may be given to any Kohen; it carries no inherent kedusha once received

🌍 Does Reishit HaGez Apply Outside the Land of Israel?

Reish Lakish
Reishit hagez does NOT apply outside Israel. It belongs to the category of matanot (priestly gifts) tied to the sanctity of the Land, similar in spirit to bikkurim.
Rabbi Yochanan
Reishit hagez DOES apply outside Israel. The verse says 'your flock' with no geographic qualifier, and unlike bikkurim it requires no Temple or Land-specific act.
Halacha (Gemara conclusion)
The accepted ruling is that reishit hagez applies universally — both in Israel and in the Diaspora — distinguishing it clearly from bikkurim.
Key Distinction from Bikkurim Bikkurim requires bringing first fruits to the Temple in Jerusalem — impossible outside Israel. Reishit hagez involves no Temple service; it is simply a gift to a Kohen, making Diaspora observance fully possible.
