Chullin 11 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded

Question 1 of 5
What is the difference between a "quantifiable" and "non-quantifiable" majority (rov)?
A quantifiable majority (rov haminuy) is physically countable — like 9 kosher shops out of 10. A non-quantifiable majority is a general statistical truth — like "most people are not treifot" or "most children are legitimate" — which cannot be physically counted. Daf 11 establishes that we follow both kinds of rov.
Question 2 of 5
How does the case of capital punishment for murder prove the principle of rov?
We execute the murderer even though theoretically the victim might have been a treifah — killing whom would not be a capital offense. We don't examine the corpse (it would mutilate it). Instead we rely on rov: most people are not treifot. This proves we follow a non-quantifiable majority even in capital cases.
Question 3 of 5
What is the challenge with following rov for "one who strikes his father"?
The capital punishment for striking one's father assumes the man is indeed the legal father. But since we can't verify paternity with certainty, we rely on rov: most children born during a marriage are the husband's children (not from adultery). This non-quantifiable majority justifies the death penalty even in cases of uncertain paternity.
Question 4 of 5
Why can't we examine an animal's internal organs before slaughter to avoid relying on rov?
You simply cannot check an animal's lungs, liver, and other organs for treifah conditions while the animal is still alive — the examination itself requires the animal to be dead. Therefore the Torah must be relying on rov (most animals are not treifot) when it permits slaughter without pre-examination. This is Rav Ashi's elegant proof from the act of slaughter itself.
Question 5 of 5
What key insight does Rav Ashi's proof from "slaughter itself" add to the rov discussion?
Rav Ashi's insight is that the Torah's basic permission — "slaughter from the cattle and flock" — with no required pre-examination, proves the Torah trusts rov. Without rov, every slaughter would be forbidden since we can't confirm the animal is not treifah. The Torah's silence on pre-examination is itself the proof that rov is a biblical principle.
