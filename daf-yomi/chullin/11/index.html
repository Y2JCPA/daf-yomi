📊 What Is Rov?

The principle: When there is a doubt about the status of something, follow the majority. If 9 out of 10 butcher shops sell kosher meat and you find an anonymous piece of meat, it is presumed kosher — because the majority of shops are kosher.

Quantifiable Majority

  • 📐 The majority is visible and countable before you
  • 🛒 Example: 9 kosher shops out of 10 in the marketplace
  • ✅ All agree: follow the majority

Non-Quantifiable Majority

  • 📐 Majority exists but cannot be counted or verified
  • 👶 Example: most children born to married women are legitimate
  • ⚖️ Do we follow this majority too? This is the main question!

🔍 Proof #1 — The Burnt Offering Head

The Torah says: sever the head of the bird burnt offering but do not divide it
↓
How can we sever it without checking whether the animal is a treifah?
↓
Answer: We rely on the majority — most animals are not treifot!
↓
This proves: follow a non-quantifiable majority (we can't count "most animals")
R' Elazar's derivation: The Torah permits certain Temple procedures without requiring examination of the animal's internal organs — implicit reliance on the majority status of kosher animals.

⚖️ Proof #2 — Capital Punishment

RK
We execute a murderer based on witness testimony. But what if the murdered person was already a treifah (about to die anyway)? Killing a treifah is not a capital offense!
Ans
We rely on the majority: most people are not treifot. We assume the victim was healthy — and execute accordingly.
Q
Why not examine the corpse to check?
Rv
Examining the corpse would mutilate it — forbidden! And even if we could, we couldn't be sure. So we rely on rov.

RK = Rav Kahana · Ans = Answer · Q = Question · Rv = Ravina

🗡️ Proof #3 — Striking One's Parent

The scenario: A person strikes his mother or father — capital offense. But what if he was conceived through adultery and the "father" is not actually his biological father? The Torah still commands capital punishment for striking him!
How can we apply the death penalty for striking one's "father" when paternity is uncertain?
↓
We follow the majority: most children are born to their legal father (not through adultery)
↓
This is a non-quantifiable majority — proves we follow even unverifiable rov in life-and-death cases

🐑 Proof #4 — The Scapegoat on Yom Kippur

The scapegoat (Sa'ir lazazel): Sent off a cliff on Yom Kippur while alive — no shechitah. But what if the goat chosen was a treifah? We'd be sending an invalid animal!
How can the Torah permit this without checking if the goat is treifah?
↓
We rely on rov — most goats are not treifot
↓
Even if it were a treifah: the scapegoat doesn't get slaughtered — so no forbidden meat to eat anyway. But the atonement might be invalid. Hence we apply rov.

🔪 Proof #5 — From Slaughter Itself!

Rav Ashi's elegant proof: The Torah says "slaughter from the cattle and the flock" — without requiring examination beforehand. Yet the animal might be a treifah internally!
RA
The very act of slaughter proves rov! We slaughter without checking every animal's lungs first — because we trust the majority are kosher.
Q
But maybe R' Meir disagrees — he holds we follow the minority in certain cases!
RA
Even R' Meir agrees here — there is no practical alternative. You cannot examine every internal organ before slaughter. The Torah must be relying on rov.

RA = Rav Ashi · Q = Question
