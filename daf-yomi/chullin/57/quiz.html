Chullin 57 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is a 'sirkha' (סִירְכָא) as discussed in Chullin 57?
A. A type of knife used for shechita
B. An adhesion found on the surface of the lung
C. A perforation in the gallbladder
D. A missing lobe of the liver
Correct: B — A sirkha is a fibrous adhesion connecting a lung lobe to the chest wall or to another lobe. Its halachic significance depends on whether the underlying membrane is intact — determined by peeling it away.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav, if a sirkha peels away from the lung cleanly without tearing, what is the ruling?
A. Treifah — any adhesion automatically disqualifies the animal
B. Kosher — the intact membrane beneath shows no perforation
C. Requires rabbinic inspection before any ruling can be made
D. Treifah only if the sirkha is found on the upper lobe
Correct: B — Rav rules that a sirkha peeling cleanly indicates the underlying membrane is whole — there is no perforation beneath, and the animal is kosher. Tearing, however, suggests a perforation and renders it treifah.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does the 'nifuach' (inflation) test detect?
A. Whether the lung is the correct size for the animal
B. Adhesions between lung lobes not visible to the naked eye
C. Perforations in the lung that allow air to escape as bubbles
D. The presence of disease in the liver or gallbladder
Correct: C — The lung is inflated while submerged in water. If air bubbles emerge from the lung tissue, it indicates a perforation (nikev) — the animal is treifah. No bubbles means the lung is intact.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the underlying principle (cited in Chullin 57b) that defines all 18 treifot?
A. Any organ with a visible blemish renders the animal forbidden
B. Any condition from which the animal could not survive 12 months
C. Any defect present at the time of birth, not acquired later
D. Any injury caused by a predator rather than natural disease
Correct: B — The principle of 'yechol lichyot' — survivability for 12 months — underlies all treifot. An animal with such a condition is legally treated as already non-viable, regardless of when it was slaughtered.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If an animal's gallbladder (marah) is perforated, what is its halachic status?
A. Kosher — the gallbladder is not considered a vital organ
B. Treifah — a perforated gallbladder is one of the 18 classic treifot
C. Depends entirely on the size and location of the perforation
D. Kosher for birds but treifah for four-legged animals
Correct: B — A perforated gallbladder renders the animal treifah. It is explicitly listed among the 18 categories of treifot established by the Sages, regardless of perforation size.
