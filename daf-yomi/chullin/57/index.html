⚖️ Rav vs. Shmuel: The Lung's Two Membranes

Rav
If the outer membrane of the lung is perforated but the inner membrane — the one directly lining the lung tissue — remains whole, the animal is kosher. The inner membrane is the true protective barrier.
Shmuel
Any perforation of either membrane renders the animal treifah. Both layers function as a single protective system; a breach in either compromises the lung's integrity.
Rav
The outer layer is secondary — like a garment over the body. What matters halachically is the inner layer touching the lung substance itself.
Shmuel
We have no basis to distinguish between the layers. The Torah prohibition applies to any state of perforation that compromises the organ as a whole.
Practical Ruling Later authorities rule stringently: any perforation penetrating to the inner membrane renders the animal treifah.

🔍 Types of Sirkhot and Their Halachic Status

Sirkha: Lobe to Chest Wall

  • Adhesion attaching lung lobe to the ribcage
  • Most common type found in examination
  • Rav: if it peels without tearing → kosher
  • If it tears upon removal → treifah
  • Suggests possible perforation beneath adhesion

Sirkha: Between Two Lobes

  • Adhesion connecting two adjacent lung lobes
  • Generally treated more leniently
  • Natural proximity of lobes reduces concern
  • Must still be examined by peeling test
  • Clean separation → kosher

Sirkha: On the Unkula (Upper Lobe)

  • Upper lobe is the most sensitive lung region
  • Adhesions here treated most stringently
  • Some authorities require extra inspection
  • Local minhag (custom) plays significant role
  • Moreh hora'ah (decisor) often consulted

🔬 The Five Steps of Lung Examination

Step 1 — Removal: After shechita, carefully extract the lung from the chest cavity, keeping the lobes intact and undamaged for examination.
↓
Step 2 — Visual Survey: Inspect all lobes for discoloration, abnormal texture, visible adhesions (sirkhot), or areas of hardening that may indicate disease.
↓
Step 3 — Sirkha Peel Test: Locate any adhesion and attempt to peel it away gently. A clean separation indicates an intact membrane beneath (kosher); tearing indicates a perforation (treifah).
↓
Step 4 — Nifuach (Inflation Test): Submerge the lung in water and inflate via the trachea. Emerging air bubbles reveal a perforation — the animal is treifah.
↓
Step 5 — Doubtful Cases: Any ambiguous finding must be brought to a qualified moreh hora'ah. The default ruling in cases of genuine doubt is stringency.

📜 The Foundation: 18 Treifot and Yechol Lichyot

כָּל שֶׁאֵין כָּמוֹהָ חַיָּה — anything like which [an animal] cannot live (Chullin 57b)
Core Principle — Chullin 57b The Sages enumerated 18 categories of treifot — conditions rendering an animal forbidden regardless of kosher slaughter. The underlying principle: any injury or defect from which the animal could not survive 12 months makes it legally equivalent to a carcass. The lung receives the most attention in practice because lung disease is the most common cause of treifah rulings. The Rishonim debate whether new categories beyond the 18 can be added; Rambam says no, Ramban allows it.
