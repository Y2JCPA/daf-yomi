Chullin 134 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Which three gifts must be given to a kohen when slaughtering a domestic animal as chulin?
A. Liver, heart, and right kidney
B. Foreleg (zero'a), cheeks (lechayayim), and maw (keivah)
C. Hindquarters, tongue, and stomach lining
D. Head, hooves, and the hide
Correct: B — Devarim 18:3 specifies the three matanot kehunah: zero'a (right foreleg), lechayayim (jawbones), and keivah (maw) — these are given from every domestic ox, sheep, or goat slaughtered for non-sacred use.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Huna, if a kohen takes the matanot by force because the owner refused to give them, what is the halacha?
A. He must return them — taking by force is forbidden even for one's own property
B. He acquires them legally but is labeled a rasha (wicked person)
C. He is penalized monetarily and must pay double
D. He loses his priestly status for acting without dignity
Correct: B — The kohen possesses an absolute Torah right to the gifts; the seizure is legally valid and he need not return them. However, acting aggressively earns him condemnation as a rasha among his fellow kohanim.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why are wild animals (chayot) exempt from the priestly gifts?
A. Their fat is prohibited and the keivah cannot be separated
B. The verse in Devarim 18:3 limits the obligation to ox and sheep (domestic livestock)
C. Chayot are considered ownerless and thus kohanim already own their share
D. The Rabbis exempted them to encourage hunting and food availability
Correct: B — The Torah states 'from those who slaughter an ox or sheep' — the Gemara derives that this restricts the obligation to domesticated cattle and small livestock, excluding wild animals, fowl, and sacred offerings.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the correct ruling regarding the fat inside the keivah before it is given to the kohen?
A. The owner may remove the fat first since the Torah only mentions the stomach itself
B. The fat is hefker (ownerless) and neither party has a claim to it
C. The kohen receives the keivah as-is with the fat intact — the owner may not drain it first
D. The fat must be salted and removed before transfer to preserve kashrut
Correct: C — The fat deposits within the keivah are considered an integral part of the gift. If the owner drains the fat before handing over the keivah, he has not fulfilled his obligation and owes the kohen compensation for the fat's value.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the fundamental theological reason the Torah mandated these priestly gifts?
A. To teach Israelites generosity and prevent attachment to material possessions
B. Because kohanim performed labor inspecting slaughterhouses for ritual fitness
C. Because kohanim received no tribal land inheritance and required alternative sustenance
D. To fund the repair and upkeep of the Jerusalem Temple
Correct: C — The Torah explicitly states 'for God chose him from all your tribes' — the kohen's exclusive dedication to divine service meant he received no land. The matanot kehunah, along with terumah and tithes, form the covenantal support system for the priestly class.
