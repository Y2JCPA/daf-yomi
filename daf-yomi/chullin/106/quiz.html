Chullin 106 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Chisda on Chullin 106, what is the halakhic problem with washing hands before eating fruit?
A. It wastes water needed for bread washing later
B. It constitutes yuhara — arrogant, presumptuous piety
C. It invalidates the subsequent washing before bread
D. Fruit is too acidic and ruins the ritual washing water
Correct: B — Rav Chisda ruled that washing before fruit is an act of yuhara (יוּהֲרָא) — false religious pretension implying one is on a higher spiritual level than the Sages required. The decree never applied to fruit.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Rav Papa declare mayim acharonim a 'chova' (obligation) rather than merely a pious custom?
A. To honor Solomon, who instituted all meal-time washing rituals
B. Because unwashed hands would halakhically invalidate Birkat Hamazon
C. Melach Sedomit — a caustic Sodomite salt — remains on fingers and can blind the eyes
D. Mayim rishonim alone is insufficient to achieve the required level of purity
Correct: C — Melach Sedomit (מֶלַח סְדוֹמִית) is an extremely fine, caustic salt that can cling invisibly to fingers after eating. If rubbed into the eyes, it causes blindness. This physical danger obligates post-meal handwashing.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the minimum amount of water required for a valid netilat yadayim before bread?
A. A full log (rova log) to ensure coverage
B. A revi'it — one quarter of a log
C. Any amount that visibly wets the entire hand
D. Half a revi'it, since only one hand is washed at a time
Correct: B — The Sages fixed a revi'it (רְבִיעִית) — a quarter-log — as the minimum quantity for valid handwashing. This is the baseline; some authorities require a revi'it per hand.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the indispensable requirement regarding the vessel (kli) for netilat yadayim?
A. It must be made of stone or earthenware, never metal
B. It must be able to hold at least one full log of water
C. The water must be poured from a kli by human action — not from an automatic pipe
D. The vessel must be inspected for cracks before each use
Correct: C — Netilat yadayim requires that water be poured from a kli (כְּלִי — a vessel) through human action. Water flowing automatically from a pipe or faucet without human direction does not fulfill the obligation according to this principle.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
To which specific food does the primary rabbinic decree of netilat yadayim apply, according to Chullin 106?
A. Any food consumed as part of a formal sit-down meal
B. Specifically bread (pat) — baked grain products
C. All grain-based foods including cooked porridge and pasta
D. Any food dipped in liquid or touched by salt water
Correct: B — The rabbinic decree of netilat yadayim was instituted specifically for eating bread (פַּת). Other foods — notably fruit — are explicitly excluded, and the Gemara stresses that one should not expand the decree beyond its original scope.
