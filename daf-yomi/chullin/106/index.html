⚖️ Must One Wash Before Eating Fruit?

Rav Chisda
One who washes hands before eating fruit is guilty of yuhara — arrogant piety. The decree was never extended to fruit, and to act as if it were implies false superiority.
Rav Nachman
The obligation of netilat yadayim applies strictly to bread (pat). Fruit falls entirely outside the scope of the rabbinic decree — washing for it is simply unnecessary.
Halakhic Conclusion Washing before fruit is not merely optional — it may be actively discouraged as presumptuous. The Sages restricted their decree to bread, and one should not expand it.

🫙 Steps of Valid Netilat Yadayim

Use a כְּלִי (kli) — a vessel. Water must be poured by human action, not flowing automatically from a pipe.
↓
Pour at least a רְבִיעִית (revi'it) — approximately 86–150 ml — of water over the hand.
↓
Water must cover the entire hand up to the wrist; the wrist joint is the boundary for bread-level washing.
↓
The water must flow from human initiative — an automatic stream does not satisfy the requirement.
↓
Dry the hands before eating — residual moisture from an invalidated washing can compromise the act.
↓
Recite: אֲשֶׁר קִדְּשָׁנוּ בְּמִצְוֹתָיו וְצִוָּנוּ עַל נְטִילַת יָדַיִם

💧 Mayim Rishonim vs. Mayim Acharonim

מַיִם רִאשׁוֹנִים — First Waters

  • Washing before the meal begins
  • Required before eating bread (pat)
  • Instituted due to concern for tumah and purity
  • Minimum: one revi'it of water from a vessel
  • Halakhic status: rabbinic decree
  • Blessing is recited after washing

מַיִם אַחֲרוֹנִים — Last Waters

  • Washing after the meal ends
  • Required before reciting Birkat Hamazon
  • Instituted to prevent danger from melach Sedomit
  • Even a minimal amount of water suffices
  • Halakhic status: chova — obligatory
  • No blessing is recited

🧂 The Danger of Sodomite Salt

מַיִם אַחֲרוֹנִים חוֹבָה — מִפְּנֵי מֶלַח סְדוֹמִית שֶׁמְּסַמֵּא אֶת הָעֵינַיִם
Why Mayim Acharonim is Obligatory מֶלַח סְדוֹמִית (Melach Sedomit) — Sodomite salt — is an extremely fine, caustic salt that clings invisibly to the fingers after eating. If one then rubs their eyes without washing, it can cause blindness.

Rav Papa therefore declares: מַיִם אַחֲרוֹנִים חוֹבָה — post-meal handwashing is a true obligation, not merely pious practice. The physical danger elevates it above all other categories of optional washing.
