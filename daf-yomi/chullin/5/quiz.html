QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is it forbidden to slaughter an animal into a pit (bor)?
A. The blood becomes impure when it touches the ground
B. It resembles pagan worship — idolaters collected blood in pits as offerings to underground spirits
C. The pit might collapse on the animal
D. It wastes the blood that should be drained on level ground
Correct: B. The prohibition is based on Marit Ayin (appearance). Pagan worshippers would slaughter animals into pits as offerings to subterranean deities. Even though the Jew has no such intent, the act LOOKS like pagan worship. Solution: slaughter near the pit and let blood flow in afterward.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
The prohibition of "Oto V'Et B'no" (Lev 22:28) — slaughtering an animal and its young on the same day — applies to which types of slaughter?
A. Only sacred (Kodashim) slaughter in the Temple
B. Only secular (Chullin) slaughter
C. Both sacred and secular slaughter
D. Only when both animals are being offered as Korbanot
Correct: C. Although the verse appears in the context of Kodashim (Leviticus 22), the Gemara derives that the prohibition extends to Chullin (secular) slaughter as well. Whether slaughtering for the Temple or for dinner, you may not slaughter a mother and her child on the same day.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why is slaughtering Chullin (secular meat) into a vessel (keli) forbidden l'chatchila?
A. The vessel might contaminate the blood
B. It resembles the sacred Temple practice of catching blood in a Keli Sharet for Kodashim offerings
C. Vessels are too expensive to use for this purpose
D. The blood must flow freely for the meat to be kosher
Correct: B. Collecting blood in a vessel is part of the Temple service (Kabbalat HaDam). When done for secular slaughter, it looks like the person is performing a sacred offering outside the Temple — which is a serious prohibition. B'dieved (if done), the meat IS kosher since the shechitah act itself was valid.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Chachamim, does "Oto V'Et B'no" apply to the FATHER animal as well as the mother?
A. Yes — both parents are included
B. No — only the MOTHER and her child, since maternity is always certain but paternity is not
C. Only for cattle, not sheep or goats
D. Only if both are being slaughtered for meat
Correct: B. The Chachamim hold that "Oto V'Et B'no" applies only to the mother and her child. The reason: we can always identify the mother (the animal that gave birth), but we cannot always be certain of the father. R' Meir disagrees and includes both parents.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Name one key difference between shechitah for Kodashim (sacred) and Chullin (secular).
A. Kodashim requires a specific location (Temple courtyard); Chullin can be anywhere
B. Kodashim uses a different type of knife
C. Chullin requires a Kohen; Kodashim does not
D. Both are identical in all requirements
Correct: A. Kodashim must be slaughtered in the Temple courtyard (Azarah), with blood caught in a sacred vessel, often on the north side. Chullin can be slaughtered anywhere in the world by any competent Jewish person. The knife and the five requirements of shechitah are the same for both.
