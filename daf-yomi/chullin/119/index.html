⚖️ K'beitzah vs. K'zayit: The Core Minimum-Measure Dispute

R' Meir
The minimum measure for any food (ochel) to receive tum'ah from a sheretz is כביצה (k'beitzah) — the volume of a hen's egg. This applies uniformly to all food items.
R' Yehudah
While k'beitzah governs general tum'at ochlin, nevelah (an improperly slaughtered animal) conveys tum'ah at the smaller shiur of כזית (k'zayit) — the volume of an olive.
📌 Halacha l'Moshe miSinai The k'beitzah standard is not derived from any biblical verse — it is an oral tradition transmitted to Moses at Sinai (הלכה למשה מסיני), the bedrock of all food-impurity measurements.

🗣️ Tziruf — Can Two Sub-Minimum Portions Combine?

Rav
Two pieces of food, each below k'beitzah, combine (מצטרפין) to reach the shiur — the operative requirement is that they are touching each other. Contact creates halachic union.
Shmuel
Physical touching is insufficient. Tziruf requires the pieces to share a common vessel. Two half-portions in separate containers do not combine, even if adjacent.
Rava
Even within one vessel, liquid (mashkeh) and solid food do not combine with each other — they belong to distinct halachic categories and each must independently meet its own shiur.
💡 Practical Difference Two half-k'beitzah pieces of meat on a single plate — touching, same vessel: all agree they combine. Two halves in separate bowls but touching: Rav says tamei, Shmuel says tahor.

📊 Shiurim: Minimum Measures Across Tum'ah Categories

כביצה — K'beitzah

  • Volume: one hen's egg
  • Governs: tum'at ochlin (food impurity)
  • Source: Halacha l'Moshe miSinai — no verse
  • When food touches a sheretz or other source
  • Applies to cooked meat, bread, produce, etc.

כזית — K'zayit

  • Volume: one olive (~half of k'beitzah)
  • Governs: tum'at nevelah (carcass impurity)
  • Nevelah = died without valid shechita
  • Conveys tum'ah through contact and carrying
  • Also the eating-prohibition threshold

כעדשה — K'adasha

  • Volume: one lentil — smallest tum'ah shiur
  • Governs: tum'at sheretz (the 8 creeping creatures)
  • Even minute contact with sheretz causes tum'ah
  • Specific to the eight sheratzim named in Torah
  • Does not apply to food tum'ah shiurim

🐑 Beit HaShechita — Does the Slaughter Site Join the Shiur?

בֵּית הַשְּׁחִיטָה — כֵּיצַד מִצְטָרֵף לְכַזַּיִת לְטַמֵּא בְּנְבֵלָה?
🔪 Special Case The beit hashechita (בֵּית הַשְּׁחִיטָה) — the neck region prepared for ritual slaughter — raises a pointed tum'ah question:

When an animal dies as a nevelah, does the meat of the beit hashechita count toward the k'zayit minimum required for the carcass to convey tum'ah?

The Gemara's deeper question: Does designation (ייחוד) for slaughter affect this region's status before the act occurs? Or is it identical to all other meat until shechita actually takes place?

This debate mirrors broader halachic arguments about whether thought and intent can transform an item's legal status in advance.
