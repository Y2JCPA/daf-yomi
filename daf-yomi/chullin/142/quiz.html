QUESTION 1 OF 5
What two mitzvot does the boy fulfill simultaneously in the famous aggadah on this daf?
A. Shiluach haken and Shabbat observance
B. Kibbud av and shiluach haken
C. Tzitzit and mezuzah
D. Kibbud av and returning lost objects
Correct: B — The boy was commanded by his father to climb the tree and retrieve eggs (fulfilling kibbud av), and he sent away the mother bird first (fulfilling shiluach haken). Both mitzvot explicitly promise long life, making his immediate death the theological crisis at the center of this daf.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Who is 'Acher,' and what conclusion does he reach after witnessing the boy's death?
A. Rav Ashi — that mitzvot guarantee physical protection
B. Elisha ben Avuyah — 'there is no justice and no Judge'
C. Reish Lakish — that we must accept suffering with love
D. R' Akiva — that suffering itself is the reward
Correct: B — 'Acher' ('the Other One') is the nickname given to Elisha ben Avuyah, a great Tanna who became a heretic. Witnessing a boy fulfill two mitzvot promising long life and then die immediately led him to declare 'ein din v'ein dayan' — there is no divine justice — and he abandoned Torah entirely.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Yaakov, what does 'לְמַעַן יִיטַב לָךְ' (that it may be good for you) refer to?
A. Long physical life and prosperity in This World
B. Protection from enemies and illness
C. The World to Come, which is entirely good (עולם שכולו טוב)
D. The mitzvah of Torah study specifically
Correct: C — R' Yaakov interprets 'yitav lach' as referring to 'olam she'kulo tov' — the World to Come that is entirely good. Similarly, 'ha'arachta yamim' refers to 'olam she'kulo aroch' — the World to Come that is entirely long, i.e., eternal. Both promises are exclusively about Olam HaBa.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is R' Yaakov's fundamental ruling regarding reward for mitzvot in This World?
A. Reward is guaranteed but may be delayed for years
B. Only Torah study yields reward in This World
C. There is no reward for mitzvot in This World at all
D. Mitzvot protect from sin but not from physical harm
Correct: C — R' Yaakov establishes the principle: אֵין שְׂכַר מִצְוָה בְּהַאי עָלְמָא — there is no reward for mitzvot in This World. All Torah promises of reward (long life, prosperity) refer exclusively to the World to Come. This World cannot contain the full weight of that reward.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If a mother bird is hovering just above her nest but not physically touching the eggs, what is the law of shiluach haken?
A. One is exempt — she is not 'sitting' on the eggs
B. One must still send her away — hovering counts as 'sitting'
C. One may take the eggs but must leave the mother alive nearby
D. The mitzvah only applies to nests found on the ground
Correct: B — The Mishna teaches that even if the mother bird is hovering above the nest without touching the eggs, the mitzvah of shiluach haken still applies. The Torah's word 'יֹשֶׁבֶת' (sitting) is interpreted broadly to include hovering — she is considered 'sitting' over the nest in a functional sense.
