💔 The Daf That Broke a Sage

לְמַעַן יִיטַב לָךְ — זֶה עוֹלָם שֶׁכֻּלּוֹ טוֹב; וְהַאֲרַכְתָּ יָמִים — זֶה עוֹלָם שֶׁכֻּלּוֹ אָרֹךְ
Central Crisis A father commands his son to climb a tree, shoo away the mother bird, and take the eggs — fulfilling both kibbud av (honoring his father) and shiluach haken (sending the mother bird), the two mitzvot that most explicitly promise long life. The boy performs both mitzvot perfectly — and then falls and dies. Elisha ben Avuyah (Acher) witnesses this and declares: אֵין דִּין וְאֵין דַּיָּן — 'There is no justice and no Judge!' The Gemara's answer reshapes how we understand every Torah promise of reward.

🔗 Two Mitzvot — The Same Promise of Long Life

כִּבּוּד אָב וָאֵם

  • Honor your father and your mother
  • שמות כ:יב — Exodus 20:12
  • Promise: לְמַעַן יַאֲרִיכוּן יָמֶיךָ

  • Send away the mother bird before taking eggs/chicks
  • דברים כב:ז — Devarim 22:7
  • Promise: לְמַעַן יִיטַב לָךְ וְהַאֲרַכְתָּ יָמִים
  • 'That it may be good for you and long life'
  • Seemingly the 'lightest' of all mitzvot

⚖️ The Great Argument — Where Is the Reward?

Acher
The Torah guarantees long life for shiluach haken and for kibbud av — the lightest and weightiest of mitzvot. This boy fulfilled both simultaneously and died on the spot. Therefore: there is no Judge, there is no justice, and the Torah's promises are false!
R' Yaakov
You misread the verse entirely. אֵין שְׂכַר מִצְוָה בְּהַאי עָלְמָא — there is no reward for mitzvot in This World at all. 'לְמַעַן יִיטַב לָךְ' refers to the world that is entirely good; 'וְהַאֲרַכְתָּ יָמִים' refers to the world that is entirely long. Both mean Olam HaBa.
Acher
But I also saw a man fall from a ladder while performing a mitzvah — no sin interrupted his act! If mitzvot don't even provide protection during their performance, what value do they have in This World at all?
R' Yaakov
Protection from bodily harm (שְׁמִירָה) is also not guaranteed in This World during mitzvah performance. The verse 'וְנִשְׁמַרְתֶּם מְאֹד לְנַפְשֹׁתֵיכֶם' addresses only situations where danger is foreseeable and avoidable — not accidents. The reward remains fully intact in Olam HaBa.
Core Ruling אֵין שְׂכַר מִצְוָה בְּהַאי עָלְמָא — There is no reward for mitzvot in This World

🌍 The Olam HaBa Resolution — Step by Step

The Problem: Torah promises 'long life' for shiluach haken (Devarim 22:7) and kibbud av (Shemot 20:12). We observe tzaddikim die young after performing these mitzvot.
↓
R' Yaakov's Axiom: אֵין שְׂכַר מִצְוָה בְּהַאי עָלְמָא — There is no reward for mitzvot in This World. This World cannot contain the full weight of spiritual reward.
↓
Reinterpreting 'לְמַעַן יִיטַב לָךְ': 'That it may be GOOD for you' = עוֹלָם שֶׁכֻּלּוֹ טוֹב — the World to Come, which is entirely and perfectly good.
↓
Reinterpreting 'וְהַאֲרַכְתָּ יָמִים': 'And your days shall be LONG' = עוֹלָם שֶׁכֻּלּוֹ אָרֹךְ — the World to Come, which is entirely long (i.e., eternal).
↓
The Corridor Metaphor (Avot 4:16): This World is only a פְּרוֹזְדוֹר (corridor) leading to Olam HaBa. Mitzvot are eternal investments — not This-World transactions.
↓
Acher's Fatal Error: He expected This-World payment for mitzvot. When it didn't come, his faith collapsed. The correct framework — that reward is deferred to Olam HaBa — would have prevented his apostasy.
