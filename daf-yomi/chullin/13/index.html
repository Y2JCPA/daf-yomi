👶 Does a Minor Have Halachic Intent?

RY
R' Yochanan raises a dilemma: does a minor (katan) have halachically effective thought (machshava)? Or is a minor's mind considered legally empty?
RA
Why phrase it about "thought"? Just ask whether a minor's physical actions have halachic significance — that's the real question!
RY
No — we already know actions matter: the mishna says if minors hollow out a nut to use as a vessel, it becomes susceptible to tumah. Actions clearly count. The question is specifically about thought alone.
The distinction: A minor's action can create halachic status (like making a vessel). But can a minor's thought alone — without an accompanying action — affect halachic status? This dilemma remains unresolved.

🧠 Action vs. Pure Thought

Action + Thought (Minor)

  • ✅ Has halachic effect
  • 🌾 Turning produce over on a roof: shows intent that rain should fall on it → renders produce susceptible to tumah
  • 📐 Even R' Yochanan agrees action+intent by a minor counts

Pure Thought (Minor)

  • ❓ Unresolved dilemma
  • 🤔 Can thinking alone (no action) affect halachic status?
  • 📐 Example: intending an animal be a sacrifice without any physical act
Key nuance: R' Yochanan's dilemma is specifically about thought that accompanies action — is a minor's thought effective when action alone would not suffice? (e.g., slaughter requires intent; action without intent is invalid)

📜 The New Mishna — Gentile Slaughter

MISHNA: Slaughter performed by a gentile renders the animal a neveilah — an unslaughtered carcass. And it imparts ritual impurity through carrying (tuma b'masa).
Why is gentile slaughter invalid? Can't they cut the simanim properly?
↓
The Gemara: gentile slaughter is not invalid due to technical error — a gentile can physically cut the simanim
↓
Rather: it's a gezeirat hakatuv — a Scriptural decree that gentile slaughter is categorically invalid, regardless of technique
↓
The concern: a gentile might intend the slaughter as an act of idol worship

🌍 Do Gentiles Really Worship Idols?

Q
The Mishna invalidates gentile slaughter because they might intend worship for their gods. But most gentiles today (in the Talmudic era) aren't actual idol worshippers — they just follow family tradition!
RY
Rav Yosef bar Minyumi says in the name of Rav Nachman: There are no real heretics among the nations. Gentile idol worship is more cultural than sincere religious belief.
Gm
Yet the Torah still forbids accepting offerings from them (most) — so halachically, we treat their slaughter as if potentially dedicated to idols.
The Gemara's resolution: Even if most gentiles don't sincerely worship idols, the Sages maintained the rule — partly because "they follow their ancestral custom" and partly as a rabbinic fence to prevent mingling with idol-worship culture.

💀 Neveilah and Tumah Through Carrying

Gentile slaughter → neveilah (as if the animal died without any slaughter)
↓
Is it obvious that neveilah is tamei? Why does the Mishna need to state it?
↓
Rava (version 1): The Mishna teaches that gentile-slaughtered neveilah is tamei even though it was slaughtered — you might have thought slaughter removes some tumah
Rava (version 2): Teaches it imparts tumah be'masa (carrying) — same category as an actual carcass, not a lesser status
R' Yehuda ben Beteira: Even idolatrous offerings impart tumah in a tent — derived from a special verse. The Mishna teaches that gentile-slaughter neveilah at minimum imparts tumah through carrying.

📖 Torah Source for Gentile Slaughter

וְקָרָא לְךָ וְאָכַלְתָּ מִזִּבְחוֹ

"And he called you and you ate of his sacrifice" (Devarim 12:30) — worshipping through slaughter

The concern: A gentile slaughtering might be calling on his god at that moment — even if it looks like regular food preparation. The Torah is strict: anything slaughtered by a gentile is treated as if it might be an idolatrous sacrifice.
Exception noted elsewhere: If a gentile slaughters in front of Jews who supervise the entire process and confirm it's not intended for idolatry — this is a different discussion for later dapim.
