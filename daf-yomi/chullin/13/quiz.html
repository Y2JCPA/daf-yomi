Chullin 13 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded

Question 1 of 5
What exactly is R' Yochanan's unresolved dilemma about a minor's intent?
R' Yochanan's dilemma focuses on thought that accompanies action — specifically when action alone would be insufficient (shechitah requires intent). We already know a minor's actions can matter (hollowing a nut = vessel). The question is: does a minor's accompanying thought add the required intentionality that would make the act halachically valid?
Question 2 of 5
According to the Gemara, why is gentile slaughter invalid as neveilah?
The Gemara is clear: the invalidity is not technical. A gentile can physically cut the simanim properly. The disqualification is categorical — a gezeirat hakatuv (Scriptural decree). The concern is that the gentile might intend the slaughter as an act of idol worship, even if it appears to be ordinary food preparation.
Question 3 of 5
How does Rav Nachman address the concern that most gentiles don't really worship idols sincerely?
Rav Nachman acknowledges that gentile idol worship is largely cultural ("they follow ancestral custom") rather than sincere religious devotion. Nevertheless, the prohibition on gentile slaughter remains. The Sages maintained it as a fence against mingling with idol-worship culture and because we cannot verify intent at the moment of slaughter.
Question 4 of 5
What tumah does neveilah (from gentile slaughter) impart, and why does the Mishna need to specify it?
Neveilah imparts tumah through carrying (be'masa). The Mishna specifies this because you might have thought: since the animal was actually cut (the physical act of slaughter occurred), perhaps it has a lesser tumah status than an animal that simply dropped dead. The Mishna teaches no — it is full neveilah with complete tumah through carrying.
Question 5 of 5
What is the essential difference between a minor's action and pure thought in the context of halacha?
The Gemara establishes from the mishna about minors hollowing nuts that a minor's physical actions can create halachic status (the nut becomes a vessel susceptible to tumah). The unresolved question is about pure thought — can a minor's mental intention alone (without action) affect a halachic status? R' Yochanan leaves this as a teiku (unresolved).
