✂️ Is There a Minimum Shiur for Reishit HaGez?

Tanna Kamma
There is a minimum amount — the wool must reach a meaningful quantity before the obligation of giving reishit hagez to the Kohen is triggered.
Rabbi Dosa
There is no minimum — even the smallest shearing creates an obligation. The word reishit implies priority, not quantity.
Rav Nachman
A gift with no shiur is impractical. The Kohen must receive something of substance — we rule that a minimum amount is required.
📜 The Torah Source Devarim 18:4 — 'Reishit gez tzonecha titen lo' — the first of your flock's shearing you shall give to him (the Kohen). The entire dispute hinges on whether 'reishit' demands a quantifiable minimum.

✂️ What Is Reishit HaGez?

רֵאשִׁית גֵּז צֹאנְךָ תִּתֶּן לּוֹ — דברים יח:ד
🔑 The Mitzvah The Torah commands giving the first of the sheep's shearing to the Kohen as one of the 24 priestly gifts. Unlike terumah and maaser, this obligation applies both inside and outside the Land of Israel. The Gemara on 138 probes the exact parameters: how many animals, how much wool, and whether any minimum is required at all.

⚖️ Two Views on the Shiur

Fixed Minimum Required

  • Need at least 5 sheep to be obligated
  • Wool must reach a substantive weight
  • A trivial amount does not honor the Kohen
  • Practical: prevents constant tiny gifts
  • Halacha ultimately follows this view

No Minimum — Any Amount

  • Even one sheep's shearing triggers obligation
  • 'Reishit' means first in order, not in size
  • Broadens the mitzvah's reach
  • Supported by linguistic reading of verse
  • Position of Rabbi Dosa ben Harkinas

🕊️ Introduction to Shiluach HaKen

You encounter a bird's nest
on the road, in a tree, or on the ground — with eggs or chicks inside
↓
Mother bird is sitting on nest
Must send away the mother before taking the eggs or young (Devarim 22:6–7)
Mother bird is absent
No obligation — may take immediately without sending away
↓
After sending away the mother: permitted to take.
Torah reward: אֲרִיכַת יָמִים — length of days

📋 How to Fulfill Reishit HaGez

Step 1: Shear a flock of at least five sheep during the shearing season
↓
Step 2: Verify the total yield of wool meets the minimum quantity required by the accepted halachic opinion
↓
Step 3: Designate a portion as reishit hagez — the first-shearing gift — and set it apart
↓
Step 4: Present the entire gift to one Kohen — it may not be split among multiple Kohanim according to most opinions
↓
Step 5: The Kohen may use the wool for any permitted purpose; no special purity or consumption rules apply
