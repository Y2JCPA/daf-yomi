🩸 The Cascade of Kisui HaDam Obligation

Trigger: Shechita performed on a bird (of) or wild animal (chayah) — blood flows onto the ground
↓
Primary duty: The shochet must cover the blood immediately with earth — above and below
↓
Transfer: If the shochet delays, the obligation cascades to anyone who witnesses the uncovered blood
↓
Competition: 'Kol HaKodem Zachah' — the first person to cover earns the mitzvah and recites the blessing
↓
Completion: Blessing recited before covering: al kisui dam b'afar — 'on the covering of blood with earth'
3 / 9

⚔️ Shochet vs. Bystander — Who Covers?

Shochet
I performed the shechita — the primary mitzvah of kisui hadam rests with me. I am the one who caused the blood to flow, so I must be the one to cover it.
Bystander
The shochet walked away without covering! Once I see the uncovered blood, the obligation falls on me too. 'Kol HaKodem Zachah' — I will cover it and recite the blessing!
Rav Huna
A shochet who neglects to cover may be penalized — he loses the first-mover advantage and the credit transfers. He should not have left without fulfilling his primary obligation.
The Ruling The shochet has primary priority. Failure to cover immediately transfers both the obligation and the merit to any observer who acts first.

🔄 When Does the Kisui Obligation Apply?

Shechita performed on a bird or wild animal (chayah v'of)
↓
Does blood flow visibly onto the ground?
Yes — blood visible
Kisui hadam obligatory — must cover with earth above and below
No visible blood
No obligation — mitzvah requires actual blood present on the ground surface
↓
Did the shochet cover before departing?
Yes
Mitzvah complete — shochet recites blessing and covers with earth
No
Kol HaKodem Zachah — any observer now steps in; blessing and covering transfer to first actor

🩸 Dam HaMefuzar vs. Dam She'Nitkaseh Me'Eilav

Scattered Blood (Dam HaMefuzar)

  • Blood that splattered over a wide area during slaughter
  • Obligation of kisui hadam still fully applies
  • Must cover the primary concentration at minimum
  • Debate: must every distant drop be covered?
  • One blessing suffices for all the blood from one shechita

Naturally Covered Blood

  • Blood that fell directly into existing loose earth or dust
  • Already 'covered from below' by circumstance
  • Still requires covering from above to complete the mitzvah
  • Rav: no blessing — mitzvah partially fulfilled already
  • Shmuel: blessing still recited — deliberate act required

🙏 The Blessing of Kisui HaDam

עַל כִּסּוּי דָם בֶּעָפָר — חולין פו
ברכה על כיסוי הדם Before covering, one recites: בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה ה׳... אֲשֶׁר קִדְּשָׁנוּ בְּמִצְוֹתָיו וְצִוָּנוּ עַל כִּסּוּי דָם בֶּעָפָר — 'Who commanded us concerning the covering of blood with earth.'

The blessing is recited before the act, as with all mitzvot. If one covered without a blessing (b'di'avad), the mitzvah is still valid — the covering itself is what matters. The shochet who forfeited first-mover status may not recite the blessing if another has already stepped forward.
