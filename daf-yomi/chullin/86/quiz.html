Chullin 86 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Who bears the primary obligation to perform kisui hadam after shechita?
A. The owner of the animal
B. Any adult Jewish male who witnesses it
C. The shochet who performed the slaughter
D. A kohen, if present
Correct: C — The shochet bears the primary obligation, since he caused the blood to flow. Only if he fails to cover does the duty transfer to others who witness the uncovered blood.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does 'Kol HaKodem Zachah' mean in the context of kisui hadam?
A. The shochet always has priority regardless of delay
B. Whoever covers the blood first earns the mitzvah
C. The first drop of blood must be covered before the rest
D. A kohen takes precedence over a Yisrael in covering
Correct: B — 'Kol HaKodem Zachah' — whoever acts first acquires the mitzvah. Once the shochet has not covered, the merit becomes available to any bystander who steps forward first.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the dispute between Rav and Shmuel regarding blood that fell naturally onto existing dust?
A. Whether the blood needs to be moved before covering
B. Whether a blessing is still recited when the blood was already naturally covered from below
C. Whether such blood is tamei and requires special treatment
D. Whether wild animals require kisui hadam at all
Correct: B — When blood fell into existing loose earth and is already partially covered, Rav holds no blessing is recited (the mitzvah condition is partially met), while Shmuel holds a deliberate act of covering always requires a blessing.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If the shochet covered the blood without reciting the blessing beforehand (b'di'avad), what is the halacha?
A. He must uncover and recover while reciting the blessing
B. He recites a blessing after the fact (bracha l'acharecha)
C. The mitzvah is valid — the covering itself is what is required
D. He is penalized and another person must reperform the covering
Correct: C — B'di'avad, if the blood was covered without a preceding blessing, the mitzvah is still fulfilled. The blessing should ideally precede the act, but its absence does not invalidate the covering.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which animals require kisui hadam after shechita?
A. All animals including cattle and sheep
B. Only birds (of)
C. Birds and wild animals (chayah v'of) only
D. Domesticated animals (beheimah) only
Correct: C — Kisui hadam applies only to birds and wild animals (chayah v'of), not to domesticated animals (beheimah) like cattle, sheep, or goats. This is derived from Vayikra 17:13 — 'any man who hunts game, beast or bird.'
