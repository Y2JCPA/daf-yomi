אַךְ בָּשָׂר בְּנַפְשׁוֹ דָמוֹ לֹא תֹאכֵלוּ — בראשית ט:ד
📜 The Prohibition of Ever Min HaChai The Torah forbids eating אֵבֶר מִן הַחַי — a limb torn from a living animal. This is one of the Seven Noahide Laws, rooted in Genesis 9:4. The prohibition applies to both Jews and non-Jews, though its details differ. For Jews, violation requires eating a kazayit of flesh from an intact limb (with bone and sinew). The animal must be alive at the moment of severance — this is the meaning of "basar b'nafsho" (flesh with its life-force).

⚖️ Is Basar Min HaChai (Flesh Alone) a Separate Prohibition?

Rami b. Chama
The verse says 'basar b'nafsho' — the word basar (flesh) stands independently! This teaches that flesh alone from a living animal is Biblically forbidden, even without a complete limb with bone and sinew.
Rava
The verse does not create two separate prohibitions. 'Basar b'nafsho' means flesh that still contains the nefesh — i.e., the animal must be alive. The prohibition is still rooted in the concept of the intact ever.
Rami b. Chama
If so, why does the verse specifically mention 'basar'? The Torah would not use extra language without teaching a novel law — flesh alone must be independently prohibited!
Rava
The term 'basar' contextualizes what kind of flesh is forbidden — flesh that is imbued with life (nefesh). It is a qualification of the limb prohibition, not a separate one.
Halachic Outcome The Gemara accepts that basar min hachai constitutes a real Biblical prohibition — a kazayit of flesh from a living animal incurs liability even without a structurally complete limb.

🌍 Ever Min HaChai: Noahides vs. Israel

Bnei Noach (Noahides)

  • Source: Genesis 9:4 — given to Noah
  • Applies to all animals, kosher or not
  • No defined minimum shiur
  • Violation potentially capital under Noahide law
  • Even partial or small amount may be forbidden

Israel (Jews)

  • Prohibition reinforced and detailed at Sinai
  • Applies to kosher and non-kosher animals alike
  • Requires kazayit of flesh for lashes
  • Ideally: intact limb with bone, flesh, sinew
  • Slaughter permits still-attached limbs — not already-severed ones

🔄 Determining Ever Min HaChai Liability — Step by Step

Step 1: Was the animal alive when the piece was severed? (Nefesh requirement — must be a living animal at time of severance)
↓
Step 2: Does the piece contain flesh (basar)? Bone or sinew alone is insufficient for the prohibition.
↓
Step 3: Is there a kazayit (olive's worth) of forbidden flesh? Below this shiur, one is exempt from lashes.
↓
Step 4: Is it a full ever (limb with bone + flesh + sinew), or basar alone? Both can be forbidden, but the analysis differs.
↓
Step 5: Was the animal subsequently slaughtered? If yes → already-severed limbs remain forbidden; limbs still attached become permitted.
