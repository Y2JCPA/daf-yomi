Chullin 109 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the famous principle stated in Chullin 109b about Torah prohibitions?
A. Every prohibition requires a monetary penalty as atonement
B. Everything the Torah forbade, it permitted an equivalent
C. All prohibitions were given as a fence around permitted acts
D. Rabbinic law must reinforce every Biblical prohibition
Correct: B — Chullin 109b states: כָּל דַּאֲסַר לַן רַחֲמָנָא שְׁרָא לַן כְּוָותֵיהּ — 'Everything the Merciful One forbade us, He permitted us something equivalent.' This is proven through four famous examples of forbidden-permitted pairs.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Which food serves as the permitted parallel to forbidden blood (dam) in Chullin 109b?
A. Red wine
B. Pomegranate seeds
C. Liver
D. Beet juice
Correct: C — The Gemara cites liver as the permitted parallel to blood. Although liver releases blood and resembles it, once properly broiled (which expels the blood), liver is fully permitted — making it the Torah's permitted equivalent to forbidden dam.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why is the shibuta fish significant in Chullin 109b?
A. It is the largest kosher fish and thus a symbol of Divine generosity
B. Its brain tastes like pork, making it the permitted parallel to chazir
C. It was offered on the altar alongside the korban Pesach
D. Its scales are used to determine kosher status for all fish
Correct: B — The Gemara teaches that the shibuta fish's brain tastes like pork (chazir). Since the shibuta is a kosher fish (having fins and scales), its brain is fully permitted — and serves as Hashem's permitted parallel to the forbidden taste of chazir.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Shmuel's ruling (accepted as halachah), the gid hanashe is forbidden in:
A. The right thigh only, following the narrative in Bereishit
B. The left thigh only, as that is where the angel actually touched
C. Both the right and left thighs
D. Neither thigh of a domesticated animal — only wild animals
Correct: C — Shmuel rules that the gid hanashe is forbidden in both thighs since the Torah verse specifying the prohibition does not limit it to one side. The halachah follows Shmuel's stringent position.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is 'shumet' and what role does it play in Chullin 109b?
A. The sciatic nerve itself — the primary subject of the chapter
B. A rabbinic prohibition on all fats as a fence around chelev
C. The permitted tail fat or external muscle fat — the analog to forbidden chelev
D. An Aramaic term for forbidden internal fat identical to chelev
Correct: C — Shumet refers to the permitted tail fat of a sheep (and similar external/muscle fats), as opposed to chelev — the forbidden fat that covers internal organs. The Gemara cites chelev/shumet as one of the four classic forbidden-permitted pairs demonstrating Divine balance.
