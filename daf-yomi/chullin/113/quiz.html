Chullin 113 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Beit Hillel, what is permitted regarding fowl and cheese that Beit Shammai forbids?
A. Placing them on the same table at all
B. Eating them at the same meal
C. Cooking fowl in a milk-based sauce
D. Selling fowl next to dairy products in a shop
Correct: B — Beit Shammai permits placing them on the table together but forbids eating together. Beit Hillel permits even eating them at the same meal, since the prohibition is only rabbinic and therefore more lenient.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How many times does the Torah write 'Do not cook a kid in its mother's milk,' and what does each teach?
A. Once — the prohibition applies only to cooking
B. Twice — cooking and eating are both forbidden
C. Three times — cooking, eating, and deriving benefit are each forbidden
D. Four times — cooking, eating, benefit, and selling are each forbidden
Correct: C — The verse appears three times (Exodus 23:19, 34:26; Deuteronomy 14:21). The Sages derive three independent prohibitions: cooking meat in milk, eating the cooked mixture, and deriving any benefit from it.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Mar Ukva describe his own waiting practice compared to his father's?
A. Mar Ukva waits a full day; his father waited only one meal
B. Mar Ukva waits until the next meal; his father waited a full day
C. Both wait exactly six hours between meat and dairy
D. Mar Ukva is stricter; his father was lenient like Beit Hillel
Correct: B — Mar Ukva calls himself 'vinegar, son of wine' — he only waits until the next meal before eating dairy, while his pious father would wait a full day after eating meat before permitting himself milk.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why are fish and locusts entirely exempt from the basar b'chalav prohibition?
A. They are not slaughtered with shechitah and thus outside the meat-milk system
B. They are not mammals and have no 'mother's milk' relationship
C. The Rabbis created a separate explicit decree exempting them
D. They are too small for the biblical measure to apply
Correct: B — The Torah specifies 'its mother's milk' — fish and locusts do not nurse their young and have no mother-milk relationship. They therefore fall entirely outside the Torah's prohibition of cooking meat in milk.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the halachic status of fowl cooked in milk, according to the Gemara on this daf?
A. A full Torah prohibition identical in force to beef cooked in milk
B. A rabbinic decree (gezeirah) due to resemblance to prohibited meat-milk mixtures
C. Completely permitted since fowl does not produce milk
D. Forbidden only when cooked together but permitted to eat separately afterward
Correct: B — The Gemara establishes that fowl and milk is a rabbinic prohibition — 'kid in its mother's milk' in the Torah applies only to nursing mammals. The Sages issued a decree (gezeirah) forbidding fowl with milk due to visual appearance (mar'it ayin).
