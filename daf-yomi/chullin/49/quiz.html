Chullin 49 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded

Question 1 of 5
Why is a needle found protruding only from the inner wall of the reticulum ruled kosher — even if its eye faces outward — unlike a needle in the liver?
The liver has no food or liquid to exert pressure, so the orientation of the needle's eye reveals its direction of travel (eye out = came from gullet). But the reticulum is always full of food and liquid. That pressure can push the blunt eye of a needle through the wall from the inside. So even if the eye faces outward, we can say: the needle was inside the reticulum and got pushed through the wall — not that it entered from the gullet. The needle protruding from one side only = kosher.
Question 2 of 5
What is the dispute between Rav and Rav Sheshet about fat sealing perforations?
Rav holds that only permitted (kosher) fat forms a valid seal on a perforated organ — because it is firmly attached and reliable. Forbidden fat does not seal. Rav Sheshet disagrees: both permitted and forbidden fat can seal a perforation — fat is fat, and if it physically covers the hole, it seals it. Rava tried to rely on both Rav Sheshet's opinion AND the principle of "Torah spares Jewish money" to rule leniently, but Rav Pappa objected that you can't use that principle to override a Torah-level dispute.
Question 3 of 5
Ravin (in the name of R' Yochanan) corrected a misattribution between R' Yishmael and R' Akiva regarding abomasum fat. What was the correction?
The first baraita attributes the lenient view (abomasum fat permitted) to R' Yishmael and the strict view to R' Akiva. But a second baraita — which uses the more careful "just as" format with textual proofs — contradicts this. Ravin, in the name of R' Yochanan, rules: reverse the first baraita. The second baraita with its textual reasoning is authoritative. Therefore: R' Akiva permits abomasum fat, and R' Yishmael forbids it.
Question 4 of 5
Regarding the "chimtza" and "bar chimtza" fats on the abomasum — Rava forgot which one seals perforations. What was the final ruling, and what mnemonic was provided?
Rav Huna bar Chinnana and Rav Huna son of Rav Nachman clarified: the bar chimtza (the "son" fat) seals abomasum perforations, while the chimtza (the "father" fat) does not. Rav Tavot provided the mnemonic: "The power of the son is greater than the power of the father." This helped distinguish the two fats when Rava could no longer remember which was which from his teaching with Rav Nachman.
Question 5 of 5
What does the exposed honey debate reveal about the principle "the Torah spares the money of Israel"?
Rava tried to use "the Torah spares the money of Israel" to rule that exposed honey (potentially contaminated by snake venom) is permitted. Rav Pappa challenged him on the fat/perforation case: you can't invoke this principle to settle a Torah-law dispute. Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak challenged him on the honey: this involves mortal danger, not just money. The principle is real — it can tip the scales in a legal dispute — but it has clear limits: it cannot override life-threatening risks, and it doesn't resolve fundamental disagreements between Tannaim.
