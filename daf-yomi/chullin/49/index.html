🫙 Fat, Foreign Objects & the Money of Israel

Daf 49 finishes the needle cases and pivots to fat. How does fat — permitted or forbidden — affect whether an organ perforation renders an animal treifah? The Gemara also uncovers a dispute about abomasum fat that's been misattributed for generations, and closes with a colorful side discussion: does the halachic principle "the Torah spares the money of Israel" allow rulings that Amoraim would otherwise call forbidden?

🪡 Needle in the Reticulum Wall

Needle found embedded in the wall of the reticulum (second stomach)
↓
Protrudes from one side only (inner face)
✅ Kosher — needle entered from inside, no wall breach
Protrudes from both sides
❌ Treifah — penetrated all the way through the wall
↓
But wait — even if the eye faces outward, why don't we say it came from the gullet like with the liver?
↓
The reticulum always has food and liquid inside. The pressure of food/liquid can push the eye of the needle through the wall — so even eye-outward doesn't mean it came from the gullet. Therefore: inner protrusion only = kosher.

🥩 Does Fat Seal a Perforation?

Rav: Kosher Fat ✅

  • Permitted fat adjacent to a perforated organ seals the perforation
  • Result: animal is kosher despite the hole
  • Logic: permitted fat is firmly attached and forms a reliable seal

Rav: Forbidden Fat ❌

  • Forbidden fat does NOT seal a perforation
  • Result: animal remains treifah

Rav Sheshet: Both ✅

  • Both kosher AND forbidden fat seal perforations
  • Fat is fat — if it covers the hole, it seals it
Rava in practice: When a case with forbidden fat sealing a perforation came before him, he ruled leniently citing (1) Rav Sheshet's opinion AND (2) "the Torah spares the money of Israel." Rav Pappa pushed back: you can't use "Torah spares money" to resolve a Torah-level dispute!

🥩 Abomasum Fat: R' Yishmael vs. R' Akiva

RY
R' Yishmael (first baraita): The word "all" in Leviticus 3:3 includes the fat on the small intestines. The fat on the abomasum is permitted for consumption.
RA
R' Akiva (first baraita): The word "all" includes the fat on the abomasum. That fat is forbidden.
2nd
A second baraita reverses the positions! It uses the "just as" format and gives detailed textual reasoning — making it more reliable.
Ravin
Ravin (from R' Yochanan): Flip the first baraita. The second one is correct — it gives the textual proof. So it's actually R' Akiva who permits the abomasum fat and R' Yishmael who forbids it.
The priests' custom: Priests who received abomasum meat as a priestly gift ate its fat alongside — in accordance with what was originally attributed to R' Yishmael (now reattributed to R' Akiva).

💡 Chimtza & Bar Chimtza: Which Fat Seals?

Two types of fat on the abomasum:
Chimtza and Bar Chimtza ("son of chimtza")
↓
Rava heard from Rav Nachman: one seals perforations of the abomasum, one doesn't. But Rava forgot which is which!
↓
Rav Huna bar Chinnana & Rav Huna son of Rav Nachman
Bar chimtza SEALS ✅
Chimtza does NOT ❌
Rav Tavot's mnemonic
"The power of the son is greater than the power of the father" — the bar (son) has the greater sealing power
Note on "hat-shaped" fat: Rav Nachman also rules that permitted fat shaped like a hat (loose, rounded) does NOT seal perforations — even if it's technically permitted fat. The texture and attachment matter, not just the kashrut status.

🍯 "The Torah Spares Jewish Money"

Rava
A jug of honey was left uncovered. May we suspect snake venom contaminated it? The Mishna only prohibits three liquids (wine, water, milk) from exposure. Honey is permitted. And furthermore — the Torah spares the money of Israel!
RN
Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak: But R' Shimon says even honey is prohibited if exposed! This is a matter of mortal danger. You can't use "Torah spares money" when lives are at stake!
Rava
At least concede to me about fish brine — R' Pappa and R' Huna would throw exposed liquids INTO fish brine to neutralize venom. Proof that brine isn't prohibited!
RN
Concede to me that exposed honey IS prohibited — R' Shimon ben Elazar holds like R' Shimon about honey!
The principle in context: "The Torah spares the money of Israel" is a real halachic consideration — but the Gemara here shows its limits. It can tip the scales in a legal dispute, but it cannot override health and safety concerns.

🪡 Reticulum needle: one side only = kosher (food pressure explains eye position). Both sides = treifah. Different logic from liver needle (no food pressure in liver)
↓
🥩 Fat as seal: Rav — only permitted fat seals. Rav Sheshet — both types seal. Hat-shaped fat = never seals regardless of type
↓
🥩 Abomasum fat: a textual mix-up between R' Yishmael and R' Akiva corrected by Ravin (from R' Yochanan). The "just as" formatted baraita is more reliable
↓
💡 Chimtza vs. bar chimtza: the "son" (bar chimtza) seals; the "father" (chimtza) does not. Mnemonic: the son's power exceeds the father's
↓
🍯 "Torah spares money" is real — but has limits. Can tip a legal dispute, cannot override danger to life
🎓 Key Principle: The Gemara shows that halachic rules aren't just about categories — context matters. The same needle is kosher in the reticulum and treifah in the liver, because the environments are different. The same fat seals a perforation if firmly attached, but not if shaped like a hat. Halacha requires anatomical and contextual precision.
