📜 The Mishnah

הוֹצִיא הָעֻבָּר אֶת יָדוֹ וְהֶחְזִירָהּ — אָסוּר מִשּׁוּם אֵבֶר מִן הַחַי
MISHNA — CHULLIN 76 If an animal was giving birth with difficulty and the fetus thrust forth its foreleg and retracted it, and the mother was then slaughtered — the foreleg is forbidden because of ever min hachai (אֵבֶר מִן הַחַי), a limb from a living animal. The retraction does not undo the prohibition — it applied the moment the limb left the mother's body.

🗣️ Gid HaNasheh of the Protruding Hindleg

Rav
If the hindleg of the fetus protrudes, the gid hanasheh within it is also forbidden. The limb was outside the mother — it acquired ever min hachai status — and everything within that limb, including the sciatic nerve, is subsumed in the prohibition.
Shmuel
The gid hanasheh prohibition is independent of ever min hachai. One cannot automatically apply both prohibitions to the same act of protrusion without a specific derivation. Each issur stands on its own.
Rav
Precisely because the gid hanasheh is a separate Torah prohibition, the hindleg now carries two independent counts of liability — ever min hachai and gid hanasheh simultaneously.
Shmuel
Agreed that both apply — the question is whether one who eats it receives one set of lashes or two. I hold: two distinct prohibitions mean two sets of malkot.
HALACHA The hindleg carries both prohibitions. One who eats the gid hanasheh of such a limb violates two independent Torah prohibitions.

🦵 Foreleg vs. Hindleg — Halakhic Comparison

Foreleg (יָד)

  • Explicitly mentioned in the Mishnah
  • Forbidden as ever min hachai upon protrusion
  • No gid hanasheh present in foreleg
  • One prohibition applies
  • Shechita of mother does not rectify it

Hindleg (רֶגֶל)

  • Derived from Mishnah by extension
  • Forbidden as ever min hachai upon protrusion
  • Gid hanasheh runs through the hindleg
  • Two prohibitions apply simultaneously
  • Eating it may incur two sets of malkot

🔄 Step-by-Step: The Protruding Hindleg

Stage 1: Animal enters difficult labor — fetus cannot emerge normally
↓
Stage 2: Fetus thrusts hindleg outside the mother's body
↓
Stage 3: At the moment of full protrusion — ever min hachai takes hold AND gid hanasheh prohibition applies to that exposed nerve
↓
Stage 4: Limb is pushed or pulled back inside the mother
↓
Stage 5: The mother is slaughtered with valid shechita
↓
Stage 6: The previously-protruded hindleg is forbidden to eat — neither retraction nor shechita removed the prohibition
↓
Stage 7: The gid hanasheh within that leg is ALSO separately forbidden — two issurim, potentially two sets of lashes
