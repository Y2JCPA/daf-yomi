Chullin 38 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded

Question 1 of 5
Which movement is a sign of LIFE (not a death spasm), according to Shmuel?
Bending an extended foreleg requires active muscle contraction — only possible while alive. A bent limb straightening, on the other hand, is a passive death spasm: as muscles relax at death, a bent limb naturally releases to a straight position. So bending an extended limb = life; straightening a bent limb = death spasm.
Question 2 of 5
When does lowing (an animal making sound) count as a valid life sign during slaughter?
The Gemara reconciles the dispute: Shmuel counts lowing as a convulsion, but Rabbi Meir (via Rabbi Yosei) excludes it when the throat is already cut. The resolution: lowing counts only when the neck is still intact — then it proves active muscular contraction. Once severed, the sound is just air being forced out mechanically as the animal dies.
Question 3 of 5
What is the position of Rav Chisda and Rava regarding WHEN a convulsion must occur to be valid?
Both Rav Chisda and Rava hold that only a convulsion at the CONCLUSION of slaughter is a valid life sign. Their reasoning: the Mishna treats an extension at conclusion as insufficient (if not restored), implying conclusion is the relevant time frame. Movement earlier in the process might be residual activity unconnected to true life at the critical moment.
Question 4 of 5
Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak takes the most lenient position on timing. What does he hold?
Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak is the most lenient: even a convulsion at the very beginning of slaughter suffices as a life sign. He draws support from Rabbi Shimon's case of blood spurting to the walls — if beginning-of-slaughter spurting proves life, so should beginning-of-slaughter convulsions. The Gemara challenges this equivalence but doesn't fully refute it.
Question 5 of 5
What does the Gemara mean by a "death-engendered" movement?
A "death-engendered" movement is one caused by the dying process itself, not by active muscular effort. For example, a bent limb straightening as muscles lose tension is death-engendered — it looks like movement but is actually just the body releasing. These do NOT count as signs of life. Only active, contra-natural movements (like bending an extended limb) prove genuine life.
