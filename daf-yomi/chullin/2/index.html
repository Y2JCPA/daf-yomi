🔪 The Opening Mishna — "HaKol Shochatin"

הַכֹּל שׁוֹחֲטִין, וּשְׁחִיטָתָן כְּשֵׁרָה
"Everyone may slaughter, and their slaughter is valid."
Q
Why does the Mishna need to say "everyone"? Why not just list who CAN slaughter?
A
The word "HaKol" (everyone) comes to INCLUDE cases you might think are excluded. Specifically: even those who are not experts, even women, even slaves — their slaughter is valid.
Q
Then who IS excluded? If "everyone" can slaughter...
A
The Mishna continues with important exceptions: a deaf-mute (Cheresh), an incompetent person (Shoteh), and a minor (Katan) — they should NOT slaughter. And if they did, with supervision, it may still be valid...

👤 Who Can & Cannot Slaughter?

CAN Slaughter ✅

  • 👨 Any adult Jewish man — even if not a scholar
  • 👩 Women — fully qualified
  • 🧑‍🦯 A blind person — if someone guides the knife
  • 👷 Slaves — their slaughter is valid
  • 🌙 At night — valid (with a torch for visibility)
  • 🚢 On a ship or rooftop — valid

Should NOT Slaughter ❌

  • 🔇 Cheresh — deaf-mute (lacks awareness)
  • 🤯 Shoteh — mentally incompetent
  • 👶 Katan — minor (lacks responsibility)
  • ⚠️ BUT: if they DID slaughter with OTHERS watching → may be valid b'dieved
  • 🚫 A non-Jew — slaughter is ALWAYS invalid
  • 🐒 Not a person — obviously invalid

🤔 The Gemara's First Question: Why "HaKol"?

The Gemara asks: "HaKol" comes to include WHOM?
Who would you have excluded that the Mishna needs to include?
↓
Rav's answer
Includes a Kuti (Samaritan/Cuthean)
You'd think their slaughter is forbidden
because they might not intend for proper shechitah
→ But "HaKol" includes them ✅
R' Kahana's answer
Includes a Yisrael Mumar
(apostate Jew)
You'd think an apostate's shechitah is invalid
→ "HaKol" includes them too ✅
(If they eat non-kosher for appetite, not belief)

⚖️ The Yisrael Mumar — Two Types

An apostate Jew who violates Torah law — does their shechitah remain valid?

Mumar L'Teiavon (For Appetite)

  • 🍖 Eats non-kosher because of craving/convenience
  • 💡 Knows it's forbidden but yields to desire
  • 📖 Does NOT reject the Torah's authority
  • ✅ Their shechitah IS valid
  • 🔪 They know HOW to slaughter properly
  • 📌 "HaKol" includes them

Mumar L'Hachis (To Spite)

  • 😤 Eats non-kosher to defy God
  • 💡 Deliberately rejects the Torah's authority
  • 📖 Has ideologically abandoned the mitzvot
  • ❌ Their shechitah is NOT valid
  • 🚫 Cannot be trusted for any religious act
  • 📌 Equivalent to a non-Jew for this purpose

🌙 Shechitah at Night — Valid!

Can you slaughter in the dark? The Mishna says the shechitah of a blind person is valid — implying that seeing is NOT required for valid slaughter.
Shechitah at night → Valid! ✅
↓
But practically:
The Sages recommend using a torch 🔦
Another person should watch to ensure proper cuts
Without visibility, hard to check the knife and the cut
↓
Key distinction:
The ACT of shechitah doesn't require daylight (unlike some Temple services).
But VERIFICATION of the cut needs light — either before or after.

🔪 Five Requirements of Valid Shechitah

The Gemara begins referencing these — expanded in later dapim:
#RequirementHebrewMeaning
1No pausingשְׁהִיָּהThe cut must be continuous — no stopping mid-cut
2No pressingדְּרָסָהMust draw the knife back and forth, not press down like a chopper
3No hidingחֲלָדָהKnife must not be covered/hidden during the cut
4No deviationהַגְרָמָהMust cut in the correct place on the throat
5No tearingעִקּוּרMust not tear the simanim (trachea/esophagus) instead of cutting
Mnemonic: שד"ח הע"ג — These five invalidations are the backbone of all shechitah law. Violating any one makes the animal a neveilah (carcass), not kosher meat.

🎓 Opening Principle: Shechitah is remarkably accessible. The Torah doesn't restrict it to priests, scholars, or specialists. Almost anyone with a proper knife and the knowledge of the five requirements can render an animal kosher. The barriers to kosher slaughter are about competence and intent — not status.
