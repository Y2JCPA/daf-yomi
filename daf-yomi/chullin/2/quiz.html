Chullin 2 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded
QUESTION 1 OF 5
The first Mishna of Chullin begins "HaKol Shochatin" — "Everyone may slaughter." According to the Gemara, who does the word "HaKol" come to INCLUDE?
A. Non-Jews
B. Kutim (Samaritans) and apostate Jews who sin for appetite
C. Children under bar/bat mitzvah
D. Animals trained to use a knife
Correct: B. According to Rav, "HaKol" includes a Kuti (Samaritan). According to R' Kahana, it includes a Yisrael Mumar (apostate Jew) who sins for appetite (l'teiavon). Both are cases where you might think the person's shechitah is invalid — the Mishna teaches that it IS valid.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the difference between a "Mumar L'Teiavon" and a "Mumar L'Hachis"?
A. L'Teiavon sins from craving but doesn't reject the Torah; L'Hachis sins to deliberately defy God
B. L'Teiavon is a non-Jew; L'Hachis is a Jew
C. L'Teiavon sins publicly; L'Hachis sins privately
D. They are the same — both are invalid for shechitah
Correct: A. A Mumar L'Teiavon eats non-kosher food out of desire or convenience — they still acknowledge the Torah is right, they just can't resist. Their shechitah is valid. A Mumar L'Hachis eats non-kosher specifically to spite God and reject Torah authority — their shechitah is invalid, comparable to a non-Jew's.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Is shechitah performed at night valid?
A. No — shechitah requires daylight like Temple service
B. Yes — the act of shechitah itself is valid at night, though a torch is recommended for verification
C. Only on Shabbat
D. Only if performed by a Kohen
Correct: B. The Mishna implies that shechitah at night is valid — it even says a blind person's shechitah is valid. Practically, the Sages recommend using a torch so the slaughterer (or a supervisor) can verify the cut was done correctly. The act itself doesn't require daylight.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which three categories of people should NOT slaughter (l'chatchila)?
A. Women, slaves, and children
B. Non-Jews, blind people, and the elderly
C. Cheresh (deaf-mute), Shoteh (incompetent), and Katan (minor)
D. Converts, Levites, and Kohanim
Correct: C. The Mishna specifically excludes Cheresh (deaf-mute), Shoteh (mentally incompetent), and Katan (minor) from slaughtering l'chatchila. The concern is that they lack the awareness, stability, or responsibility to perform the act correctly. However, if they DID slaughter with others watching, the shechitah may be valid b'dieved.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which of these is NOT one of the five invalidations of shechitah?
A. Shehiyah (pausing mid-cut)
B. Derasah (pressing down instead of drawing the knife)
C. Kelilah (burning the entire animal)
D. Chaladah (hiding/covering the knife during the cut)
Correct: C — Kelilah is NOT one of them. Kelilah means "entirely burned" and relates to the Olah offering, not shechitah. The five invalidations are: Shehiyah (pausing), Derasah (pressing), Chaladah (hiding), Hagramah (deviating from the correct spot), and Ikkur (tearing instead of cutting).
