📏 Three Skills of a Talmid Chacham

✍️

Writing

Ability to write Torah texts and documents

🔪

Slaughter

Know the halachot and practice of shechitah

🩹

Circumcision

Ability to perform a bris milah

Shmuel's critical rule: Any slaughterer who doesn't know the halachot of shechitah — it is forbidden to eat from his slaughter. The five potential disqualifications: shehiyah (pausing), derasah (pressing), chaladah (hiding the blade), hagramah (slipping off position), and ikkur (tearing).

🔍 Must You Examine After Slaughter?

Sh
The slaughterer must examine the simanim (windpipe and gullet) after completing slaughter to confirm they were properly cut.
RY
The knife must be checked before slaughter too — within the interval of "the time it takes to examine" the blade.
Q
What if he didn't examine the simanim after slaughter?
RE
R' Elazar ben Antigonus in the name of R' Elazar b'R' Yannai: The meat is invalid — once thrown on the trash heap, we can't verify.
BR
A braita disagrees: Even if thrown on a heap of trash, one may examine the simanim retroactively — still valid.

Sh = Shmuel · RY = Rav Yosef/Abaye · RE = R' Elazar ben Antigonus · BR = Braita

⚖️ Chazakah — Presumptive Status

A living animal has a chazakah of issur (presumed forbidden) — because it hasn't been slaughtered yet
↓
Slaughter occurs — but did it happen properly?
↓
Confirmed proper slaughter ✅
Chazakah broken → permitted
Uncertain ❓
Chazakah stands → forbidden
🎓 Rav Huna's principle: "An animal during its lifetime exists with the presumptive status of prohibition until it becomes known to you in what manner it was slaughtered." In any doubt — the animal remains forbidden.
בְּהֵמָה בְּחַיֶּיהָ בְּחֶזְקַת אִיסּוּר עוֹמֶדֶת עַד שֶׁיִּוָּדַע לְךָ בַּמֶּה נִשְׁחֲטָה

🐺 What If a Wolf Took the Innards?

ScenarioCan We Eat the Animal?Reasoning
Wolf completely took the innards away❌ ForbiddenCannot verify the animal wasn't a treifah
Wolf punctured the innards and left them❓ DilemmaDid the wolf puncture at a pre-existing hole, or make a new one?
Innards intact, no interference✅ PermittedCan be examined normally
The deeper question: This dilemma connects to the broader issue of combining two uncertainties — a pre-existing perforation (which would make it treifah) plus the wolf's interference. The Gemara uses this to explore when we trust the status quo vs. when we require verification.

🐍 Danger vs. Prohibition

⚠️ Danger (Sakana)

  • 🐍 Exposed water — a snake might have poisoned it
  • 🐭 Figs found nibbled — might be venomous
  • 📏 Rule: Always stringent in cases of doubt
  • ❌ Cannot rely on majority or chazakah to be lenient

📜 Prohibition (Issur)

  • 🔪 Uncertain slaughter — was it valid?
  • 🐄 Possible treifah — internal organs doubtful
  • 📏 Rule: Follow majority/chazakah
  • ✅ Can sometimes be lenient based on probability
Rav Huna: "Are you comparing danger to prohibition? Danger is different — we are more stringent!" You can rely on a majority to permit doubtful meat, but you cannot rely on it when life is at stake.

🔪 The Notched Knife Dilemma

You slaughter an animal. Afterward, you discover the knife has a notch 😰
↓
When did the notch appear — before or after slaughter?
↓
Rav Huna: Forbidden — even if you cut bones afterward. The chazakah of issur stands.
Rav Chisda: Valid — we assume the notch came from the bones, not during the slaughter of the simanim.
↓
Rav Yosef once invalidated 13 animals based on Rav Huna's stringent view!
