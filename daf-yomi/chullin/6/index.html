🌿 Tevel — Untithed Produce

What is Tevel? Produce grown in Israel from which tithes (Ma'aser) have NOT yet been separated. It is forbidden to eat Tevel — it's as if the Kohen's/Levi's portion hasn't been "released" yet.
Does produce grown by a Kuti (Samaritan) require tithing?
↓
Initial view
✅ Kutim DO tithe
They accepted the written Torah
→ Their produce does NOT need re-tithing by Jews
After Kuti reclassification
❌ Now treated as non-Jews
Non-Jews' produce needs tithing before Jews eat it
→ Buy from Kuti = must tithe
Parallel to shechitah: Just as the Kutim's legal reclassification from quasi-Jews to non-Jews affected their shechitah (daf 4), it equally affects their produce. The Sages' ruling was comprehensive — it changed the halachic status of everything they did.

🏘️ Mixed Cities — A Real-World Challenge

Q
What if a city has BOTH Jews and Kutim — and you buy produce from the market without knowing who grew it?
A
Follow the majority! If most residents are Jewish → assume the produce was grown by a Jew → no tithing needed. If majority are Kuti → assume Kuti produce → must tithe.
Q
What about 50/50?
A
When there's a tie, we're strict — must tithe, but WITHOUT reciting the blessing (since it may not actually be required). This is the principle of "safek" (doubt) under religious law.

🐄 The Two Simanim — Trachea and Esophagus

For valid shechitah of an animal, the slaughterer must cut through the simanim — the identifying signs:

Veshet — Esophagus 🍽️

  • 📍 The food pipe — soft, flexible
  • 🔴 Has two layers (inner + outer)
  • ⚖️ Both layers must be cut for valid shechitah
  • 🔪 If only one layer → invalid (Shehiyah or Ikkur)
  • 💡 Carries food down to the stomach
  • 🐦 For BIRDS: cutting either siman alone can suffice (dispute)

Kaneh — Trachea 🫁

  • 📍 The windpipe — hard, ringed cartilage
  • 🔴 Single-layered tube
  • ⚖️ Must be cut through the majority
  • 🔪 "Majority" = more than half the circumference
  • 💡 Carries air to the lungs
  • 🐦 For BIRDS: one siman may suffice

🔢 How Much Must Be Cut? — The Majority Rule

How much of each siman must the knife cut through?
↓
For Animals (Beheimah)
Must cut BOTH simanim
(Veshet + Kaneh)
Each must be cut through its majority
✅ Both = Kosher
For Birds (Of)
Cut ONE siman = valid (per most opinions)
Cut BOTH = definitely valid
R' Yehuda: needs both
Chachamim: one suffices

"Rov" of the siman:
Cut the majority of the trachea = valid even without cutting the entire width.
Minimum: cut more than half the circumference of the Kaneh.

🧠 Deep Dive: Why These Specific Signs?

Why did the Torah choose the trachea and esophagus as the identifying signs (simanim) of valid slaughter? The Gemara doesn't give an explicit reason, but several make sense:
R1
Medical logic: Cutting through both the trachea and esophagus in the neck causes rapid unconsciousness and death from blood loss. It's the most humane way to kill an animal quickly.
R2
Identification logic: The two simanim are easy to identify even by someone without medical training. A sharp knife through the throat that hits both tubes = valid. Simple, consistent, teachable.
R3
Blood drainage: Cutting through the major blood vessels in the neck drains the blood efficiently. The Torah commands that blood must not be eaten — the simanim check ensures maximum drainage.
