QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is "Tevel"?
A. Produce that has been tithed and is ready to eat
B. Produce from which tithes have NOT yet been separated — forbidden to eat
C. Produce grown outside the Land of Israel
D. Produce that was planted during Shemittah
Correct: B. Tevel is produce grown in the Land of Israel from which the required tithes (Terumah, Ma'aser Rishon, Ma'aser Sheni, etc.) have not yet been separated. Eating Tevel is a serious prohibition — the Kohen's and Levi's portions haven't been "released" yet.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In a city with both Jewish and Kuti residents, how do you determine whether market produce needs tithing?
A. Always tithe, regardless of demographics
B. Never tithe — city produce is always exempt
C. Follow the majority (Rov): if most residents are Jewish, no tithing needed; if most are Kuti, must tithe
D. Ask the seller directly and trust their answer
Correct: C. The principle of Rov (majority) applies. If the majority of residents are Jewish, we presume the produce was grown by Jews and is already tithed. If the majority are Kutim (now classified as non-Jews), we must tithe. In a 50/50 situation, we tithe but WITHOUT a blessing (safek/doubt).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
For valid shechitah of an ANIMAL (beheimah), how many simanim must be cut?
A. One — either the trachea or esophagus
B. Both — the trachea (Kaneh) AND esophagus (Veshet)
C. Three — trachea, esophagus, and jugular vein
D. It depends on the species of animal
Correct: B. For animals (beheimot), BOTH simanim must be cut: the Kaneh (trachea/windpipe) and the Veshet (esophagus/food pipe). Each must be cut through its majority. For birds, there is a dispute — the Chachamim say one siman suffices, while R' Yehuda requires both.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does "majority of the Kaneh (trachea)" mean in the context of valid shechitah?
A. Most of the length of the trachea must be cut through
B. More than half the CIRCUMFERENCE of the trachea must be cut through at the point of slaughter
C. At least 75% of the trachea must be severed
D. The entire trachea must be cut, but "majority" means most of the time
Correct: B. "Majority of the Kaneh" means more than half the circumference of the trachea ring must be cut through at the point where the knife passes. You don't need to cut the ENTIRE tube — just more than 50% of the circle. This is a precise measurement with real practical implications.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The first six dapim of Chullin establish five foundational halachic frameworks. Which of the following is NOT one of them?
A. Rov (follow the majority)
B. Chazakah (presumption of status quo)
C. Gezeirah Shavah (verbal analogy between two Torah passages)
D. Marit Ayin (forbidden because of appearance)
Correct: C. The five frameworks established in Chullin 1–6 are: (1) Rov — follow the majority, (2) Chazakah — status quo presumption, (3) Marit Ayin — appearance-based prohibitions, (4) Kavvanah — intent is required, and (5) L'chatchila vs. B'dieved — ideal vs. post-facto. Gezeirah Shavah is a Torah interpretation tool, not one of the frameworks developed in these dapim.
