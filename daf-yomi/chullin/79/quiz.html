Chullin 79 Quiz

QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the Talmudic term for the hybrid animal of uncertain species classification discussed in Chullin 79?
A. Pered (mule)
B. Koi (כּוֹי)
C. Behema gamur
D. Ben Pekuah
Correct: B — The koi (כּוֹי) is described as an animal whose status the Sages could not determine — neither definitively behema (domesticated) nor chaya (wild). It appears throughout tractate Bikkurim and Chullin as the paradigm case of halachic uncertainty in species classification.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the Gemara in Chullin 79, does the prohibition of oto v'et beno apply to the koi, and does a transgressor receive lashes?
A. Applies biblically; lashes are given
B. Does not apply at all; no lashes
C. Applies rabbinically; no lashes for safek
D. Applies only if the koi's mother was a goat
Correct: C — The Gemara rules that oto v'et beno applies rabbinically to the koi out of doubt — we treat it stringently as if it might be a behema. However, since it is only a safek (doubt), violators do not receive malkot (lashes), as corporal punishment requires a definitive biblical violation.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
The Torah verse for kilayim b'vehema states 'לֹא תַרְבִּיעַ בְּהֶמְתְּךָ כִּלְאַיִם'. What does the keyword 'kilayim' indicate?
A. Slaughtering on the same day
B. Mixing two different kinds/species
C. Covering the blood of a wild animal
D. Offering a firstborn animal
Correct: B — Kilayim (כִּלְאַיִם) literally means 'two kinds' — it refers to the prohibition of mixing different species, whether in plant grafting, fabric mixing (shatnez), or animal crossbreeding. In the context of animals, it forbids causing two distinct species to mate.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When covering the blood (kisuy ha-dam) of a koi, what is the halachic ruling?
A. No covering required — it is definitely behema
B. Cover with a blessing — it is definitely chaya
C. Cover without reciting a blessing — due to safek
D. Cover only if slaughtered on a weekday
Correct: C — Since the koi may or may not be a chaya, we cover its blood to fulfill the possible mitzvah — but we do not recite a blessing, because if the koi is actually a behema, reciting the blessing would be a bracha l'vatala (blessing in vain). Safek demands action but not the blessing.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What general principle does Chullin 79 establish regarding the application of lashes (malkot) to doubtful cases?
A. Lashes apply whenever a prohibition is violated, certain or not
B. Lashes apply only for safek when the Sanhedrin rules so
C. Lashes require certainty — a doubtful violation cannot incur malkot
D. Lashes never apply to animal-related prohibitions
Correct: C — A foundational rule in halachic jurisprudence is that malkot (biblical lashes) can only be administered when the violation is certain and clear. The daf applies this principle to show that even though we rule stringently on the koi regarding oto v'et beno and kilayim, no lashes are given because the case remains in doubt.
