🫁 The Lung: Lobes, Colors & Strange Conditions

Daf 47 is the lung encyclopedia. Rava maps out the five lobes. The Gemara asks: what if one is missing? What if there's an extra? Then it shifts to an extraordinary color chart — can a lung be green and still be kosher? Black? Red? R' Natan's real-world circumcision cases provide the evidence. Finally: what happens when a lung literally liquefies?

🫁 Rava: Five Lobes of the Lung

Right Side: 3 Lobes

  • When hanging by legs, face toward you
  • Three lobes visible on YOUR right
  • This is the standard, healthy configuration

Left Side: 2 Lobes

  • Two lobes visible on YOUR left
  • Total: 5 lobes
  • Missing, extra, or switched = treifah
ConditionRuling
Missing a lobeTreifah
Extra lobe, in line with othersKosher (Mareimar overrules Rava)
Extra lobe, between the two sidesTreifah (unless "rose lobe" on inner face)
Lobes switched (2 right, 3 left)Treifah
Extra lobe on back of lungTreifah (even tiny as myrtle leaf)

🌹 The "Rose Lobe" Exception

RA
Rav Ashi saw an extra lobe between the two sides and was about to rule it treifah...
RH
Rav Huna Mar bar Avya: Wait! All those animals grazing in the fields have this extra lobe. Butchers call it the "little rose lobe" (vardah). It's normal anatomy!
Gem
The Gemara clarifies: this only applies when the extra lobe is on the inner face of the lung, facing the heart. If it's on the back — even as small as a myrtle leaf — treifah.
Real-world anatomy saves the day: A theoretical ruling nearly declared thousands of animals treifah — until a butcher's practical knowledge corrected the halachic analysis. The "rose lobe" is a normal anatomical variant, not a defect.

👶 R' Natan's Circumcision Stories

RN
I once went to the cities overseas. A woman came to me — she circumcised her first son, he died. Her second son, he died. She brought her third son to me.
RN
I saw that he was red — too much blood. I said: "Wait until his blood is absorbed." She waited, circumcised him, and he survived. They called him Natan the Babylonian.
RN
On another occasion in Cappadocia — same situation, two sons died. I saw the third was green — too little blood. I said: "Wait until his blood enters him." She waited, and he survived too.
From pediatrics to kashrut: Both the red and green babies recovered — proving that red and green are recoverable conditions, not signs of decay. The Gemara applies this to lungs: a red or green lung can heal, so the animal is kosher. R' Natan's real-world medical cases become the proof text for animal anatomy.

💧 Liquefied Lung: When a Lung Melts

Lung has liquefied — can be poured out like water from a jug. Treifah?
↓
Ulla (R' Yochanan): Kosher! A deficiency on the inside of an organ is NOT considered a deficiency. Only membrane/wall damage counts.
↓
Rava's condition: Only kosher if the bronchi still exist — just the flesh dissolved. If bronchi dissolved too = treifah.
↓
How to check: Pour the liquefied lung into a clear glazed vessel. White streaks = liquefied bronchi = treifah. No white streaks = bronchi intact = kosher.
↓
Rav Nachman: Partially atrophied lung with intact membrane = kosher, even if the empty space holds a quarter-log of fluid.

🗺️ Daf 47 Summary Map

🫁 Five lobes: 3 right, 2 left. Missing/switched = treifah. Extra in line = kosher (Mareimar). "Rose lobe" on inner face = normal anatomy
↓
🎨 Color chart: Dark = kosher. Black/ink = treifah (decayed). Green (leek) & red = kosher (R' Natan). Flesh-like or yellow shades = treifah
↓
👶 R' Natan's circumcisions: real-world pediatric cases prove red and green are recoverable conditions — applied to lungs as halachic evidence
↓
💧 Liquefied lung: kosher if bronchi survive. Check by pouring into clear vessel — white streaks = bronchi dissolved = treifah. Atrophied but membrane intact = kosher
🎓 Key Principle: The distinction between internal and external deficiencies drives this entire daf. A lung can literally dissolve on the inside and remain kosher — as long as its structural framework (membranes, bronchi) is intact. The treifah system cares about the organ's ability to function, not its cosmetic appearance.
