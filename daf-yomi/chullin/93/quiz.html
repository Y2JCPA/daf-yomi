Chullin 93 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the primary physical characteristic that distinguishes chelev (forbidden fat) from shuman (permitted fat)?
A. Its proximity to the animal's heart
B. Whether it is covered by a distinct separating membrane (כרום)
C. Its white color compared to yellow shuman
D. Whether the fat is solid at room temperature
Correct: B — Chelev is fat covered by a distinct peelable membrane (krum). Fat that disperses through flesh without such a membrane is shuman and is permitted. This anatomical distinction drives all the specific rulings on Chullin 93.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
When Rav and Shmuel dispute the status of fat from the beit hakosot (rumen), which decisional principle governs?
A. We always follow Shmuel since he lived in Babylonia
B. In ritual prohibitions (איסורי) we follow Rav — so the fat is chelev
C. The Mishnah's ruling overrides both Rav and Shmuel
D. We follow whoever rules more stringently in kashrut matters
Correct: B — The principle הלכה כרב באיסורי — in matters of ritual prohibition, the law follows Rav. Since chelev status is a kashrut (ritual) question, Rav's ruling that the fat is chelev (forbidden) prevails over Shmuel's permissive view.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What biblical episode is the source for the prohibition of gid hanasheh (sciatic nerve)?
A. Abraham's covenant with God at Beit El
B. Jacob wrestling with the angel at Peniel; the angel struck his hip socket
C. Moses receiving the Torah at Sinai, which included all oral laws
D. Joseph's brothers eating a meal after selling him into slavery
Correct: B — Genesis 32:33 states that after the angel struck Jacob's כַּף יָרֵךְ (hip socket) at Peniel, 'therefore the Children of Israel shall not eat the gid hanasheh which is on the socket of the thigh.'
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why might birds be exempt from the prohibition of gid hanasheh according to the Talmud's analysis?
A. Birds are classified as pareve and have no meat-related prohibitions
B. The Torah says 'behema' (animal) explicitly, excluding birds entirely
C. Birds lack the rounded hip socket (כַּף יָרֵךְ) that is central to the prohibition's source
D. Birds were not present when Jacob wrestled with the angel
Correct: C — The prohibition is rooted in the injury to Jacob's כַּף יָרֵךְ — a rounded ball-and-socket hip joint. Birds lack this anatomy; their leg structure differs fundamentally. Without the anatomical basis for the prohibition, birds are likely exempt biblically.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The fat of the sheep's tail (אליה) is forbidden — but on what basis, separate from regular chelev?
A. It is forbidden as chelev like other fat under a membrane
B. It is listed separately in Leviticus 3:9 as something offered on the altar, making it forbidden to eat
C. It was forbidden by rabbinic decree to prevent confusion with chelev
D. The Talmud rules it has the same status as blood and is forbidden for that reason
Correct: B — The fat tail (אליה) of broad-tailed sheep is explicitly listed in Leviticus 3:9 as an offering brought to the altar — 'the entire fat tail.' Since it is designated for the altar, it is forbidden for human consumption, though this is a distinct category from standard chelev.
