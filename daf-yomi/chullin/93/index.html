⚖️ Chelev vs. Shuman: Two Types of Animal Fat

🚫 Chelev — Forbidden Fat

  • Fat covering the kidneys (כליות)
  • Fat on the loins (כסלים)
  • Fat beneath a distinct separating membrane (כרום)
  • Fat of the sheep tail (אליה) — forbidden separately
  • Carries malkot (lashes) if eaten intentionally (Lev. 7:23)

✅ Shuman — Permitted Fat

  • Fat dispersed through intestinal flesh
  • Fat of the heart
  • Fat on stomach lining without membrane separation
  • Fat of wild animals (חיה) — always shuman
  • Fat of birds — never in chelev category

🤝 Rav vs. Shmuel: Fat of the Beit HaKosot (Rumen)

Rav
The inner fat of the beit hakosot (כרס, the rumen/first stomach) is chelev — it is covered by a krum (distinct membrane) that separates it, making it forbidden like other membrane-covered fats.
Shmuel
The fat of the beit hakosot is shuman and permitted. The membrane there is not sufficiently distinct and firm to classify the fat beneath as chelev — it blends with the stomach tissue.
Rav Nachman
The principle is: הלכה כרב באיסורי — in ritual prohibitions we follow Rav. Since this is a kashrut question, we rule like Rav: the fat is chelev and forbidden.
📜 Decisional Rule Rav's rulings govern ritual prohibitions (איסורי); Shmuel's rulings govern monetary law (ממונות). Fat status = ritual prohibition → follow Rav.

🦴 Gid HaNasheh — The Forbidden Sciatic Nerve

עַל כֵּן לֹא יֹאכְלוּ בְנֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל אֶת גִּיד הַנָּשֶׁה אֲשֶׁר עַל כַּף הַיָּרֵךְ — בראשית לב:לג
📖 Biblical Source The sciatic nerve (גִּיד הַנָּשֶׁה) is forbidden based on Jacob's wrestling match at Peniel (Genesis 32). When the angel struck Jacob's hip socket (כַּף יָרֵךְ), the verse states: 'Therefore the Children of Israel shall not eat the gid hanasheh.' The prohibition covers both the inner and outer sinews running through the thigh socket. It applies to domestic animals on both sides of the animal. The requirement of a כַּף יָרֵךְ (rounded hip socket) becomes the critical anatomical criterion for determining which animals are included.

🐦 Are Birds Subject to Gid HaNasheh?

Stringent View
Birds are included in gid hanasheh. The verse says 'bnei Yisrael shall not eat' — a broad prohibition covering all meat. Bird thighs have sinews, and the prohibition should apply.
Lenient View
Birds are exempt. The prohibition derives from the strike to Jacob's כַּף יָרֵךְ — a rounded ball-and-socket hip joint. Birds lack this anatomy entirely; their leg structure is fundamentally different.
Practical Ruling
Biblically, birds are likely exempt from gid hanasheh. However, many communities maintain a custom (מנהג) of stringency. In practice, the hindquarters of large animals require nikur (deveining); bird practice varies by community and custom.
🔑 Anatomical Key The כַּף יָרֵךְ (hip socket/acetabulum) is present in domestic quadrupeds but absent in birds and fish — this anatomical distinction drives the entire legal analysis.
