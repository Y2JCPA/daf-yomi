📜 The Biblical Source of Shiluach HaKan

שַׁלֵּחַ תְּשַׁלַּח אֶת-הָאֵם וְאֶת-הַבָּנִים תִּקַּח-לָךְ לְמַעַן יִיטַב לָךְ וְהַאֲרַכְתָּ יָמִים (דברים כב:ז)
Devarim 22:6–7 "If a bird's nest happens to be before you on the road, in any tree or on the ground, with chicks or eggs, and the mother is sitting upon the chicks or upon the eggs — you shall surely send away the mother, and the young you may take for yourself, so that it will be good for you and you will prolong your days."

⚖️ Is a Treifah Mother Bird Subject to Shiluach HaKan?

Rav Huna
A treifah bird sitting on a nest is exempt from shiluach hakan. The Torah says 'send away the mother,' implying a viable, life-sustaining mother — a treifah cannot sustain life and falls outside the verse's scope.
Rabbi Yochanan
Even a treifah bird is obligated in shiluach hakan. She is still the halakhic 'mother' of these eggs and chicks; the Torah's language does not exclude her merely because she is unfit for consumption.
Rava
The treifah cannot produce viable offspring capable of perpetuating life. Since the mitzvah is rooted in the compassion shown toward a living nesting relationship, her fundamental unfitness should render her patur.
Practical Halacha The accepted ruling is that a treifah mother bird is exempt from shiluach hakan, as the mitzvah presupposes a viable and living nesting relationship between mother and offspring.

🔗 Two Mitzvot — One Identical Reward

Shiluach HaKan

  • Send away the mother before taking eggs or chicks
  • Seemingly a 'light' (קלה) commandment
  • Reward: 'so that it will be good for you'
  • Reward: 'and you will prolong your days'
  • Applies in an incidental encounter — no active seeking required

Kibbud Av Va'em

  • Honor your father and your mother
  • Considered among the 'weightiest' commandments
  • Reward: 'so that your days will be lengthened'
  • Reward: 'and that it will be good for you'
  • Ongoing daily obligation — not incidental

🐣 Fulfilling Shiluach HaKan: Step by Step

Step 1 — Identify the nest: You encounter a bird's nest by chance (בדרך) with a mother sitting on eggs or chicks in the wild or a qualifying location
↓
Step 2 — Verify the species: Confirm the bird belongs to a kosher species (e.g., dove, pigeon, turtle-dove) — non-kosher birds are entirely exempt
↓
Step 3 — Check physical contact: Confirm the mother is מרחפת — hovering and touching the eggs or chicks. If she is perched nearby but not touching, some authorities hold one is exempt
↓
Step 4 — Send away the mother (שלח תשלח): Cause the mother to leave before taking the eggs or chicks. Do not take both the mother and young simultaneously — this violates the prohibition
↓
Step 5 — Take the young: You may now take the eggs or chicks. The Torah promises: לְמַעַן יִיטַב לָךְ וְהַאֲרַכְתָּ יָמִים — that it will be good for you and you will have long life
