🫁 The "Basket" Metaphor — Reish Lakish

Reish Lakish's teaching: Once the windpipe is cut during shechitah, the lung is considered "as though it was placed in a basket." Meaning: whatever happens to the lung AFTER that moment — like a perforation — does not make the animal a neveilah (unslaughtered carcass). The shechitah process has begun and the animal is no longer "un-slaughtered."

🦴 Siman + Siman — Joint Removal of Neveilah-Tumah?

Gm
Dilemma: Does cutting the FIRST siman (alone) join together with the second to remove neveilah-tumah from the foreleg? Or must both simanim be fully cut before the foreleg loses its neveilah-tumah status?
Gm
The Gemara clarifies: this dilemma is ONLY about tumah. For eating purposes, everyone agrees: you need both simanim cut (majority of each) before you may eat. The tumah question is separate.
Why separate standards? Tumah laws operate on a technical "when does the animal stop being a potential neveilah" principle. Eating laws require the animal to be fully, validly slaughtered. These are different questions with potentially different thresholds.

🥩 Eating vs. Tumah — Two Different Thresholds

🥩 For EATING:
Need majority of BOTH simanim properly cut. First siman alone doesn't permit eating.
↓
🦠 For TUMAH (neveilah impurity):
Does the first siman's cutting start to "remove" the neveilah-tumah potential? Open question.
↓
The Gemara leaves this as an unresolved dilemma (teiku) — a reminder that not all halachic questions have clean answers!
