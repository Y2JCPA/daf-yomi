חולין עג.
Core Principle שְׁחִיטַת הָאֵם מְטַהֶרֶת אֶת עֻבָּרָהּ — The slaughter of the mother purifies her fetus. A fetus found inside a properly slaughtered animal does not require its own shechita and is not considered neveilah. The fetus is legally regarded as part of the mother's body (יֶרֶךְ אִמּוֹ — the thigh of its mother), and thus one act of shechita covers both.

⚖️ Limb Protruding Before Shechita — Covered or Not?

Rav Huna
If a limb of the fetus extended outside the womb before the mother was slaughtered, that limb is like neveilah. It has effectively separated from the mother's domain during the critical moment — shechita cannot retroactively recapture it.
Rav Chisda
Even a protruding limb is covered by the mother's shechita. Since the fetus remained alive and attached within the mother at the moment of slaughter, the shechita reaches the entire organism — including the extended limb.
Halachic Ruling The stricter position prevails: a limb that protruded outside the womb before shechita is not covered and is forbidden as a limb severed from a living animal (אֵבָר מִן הַחַי) or as neveilah.

🔄 Fetus (עוּבָּר) vs. Yotzei Dofen (יוֹצֵא דֹּפֶן)

עוּבָּר — Fetus In Womb

  • Located entirely within the mother's womb
  • Mother's valid shechita covers it fully
  • No independent shechita required
  • Not considered neveilah or tereifah
  • May be eaten after the mother is slaughtered

יוֹצֵא דֹּפֶן — Cesarean Born

  • Removed surgically through the mother's side
  • Did not pass through the birth canal
  • Mother's shechita does NOT apply to it
  • Requires its own valid shechita
  • Cannot serve as a firstborn offering (bechor)
  • Subject to special disputes re: other mitzvot

📋 Deriving Shechitat HaEm: The Logic Chain

Biblical Hook: Vayikra 17:13 — "v'et dama yishpoch" (and its blood shall be poured). The singular possessive implies one blood-pouring covers both the mother's life-blood and the fetus's.
↓
Principle of Yerekh Imo: The fetus is halachically 'the thigh of its mother' — an extension of her body, not an independent entity. What exempts the mother exempts the fetus.
↓
Boundary Condition: The exemption holds only while the fetus is entirely within the mother. Any part that exits the womb has symbolically 'departed' — it is no longer yerekh imo.
↓
Yotzei Dofen Paradox: Such an animal is still considered 'not yet born' for some laws (e.g., bechor) — yet the mother's shechita still does not exempt it, since the surgical removal severs the halachic connection.
↓
Practical Outcome: Find a fetus in a properly slaughtered animal → permitted to eat. Find it in a neveilah → both are forbidden. Protruding limb → the limb alone is forbidden.
