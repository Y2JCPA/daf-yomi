🥛 Is Benefit (Hana'ah) from Basar B'Chalav Forbidden?

Rav
Basar b'chalav is forbidden for hana'ah (benefit) by Torah law — one may not sell it, feed it to dogs, or derive any use from the mixture. The word bishul implies total prohibition.
Shmuel
Only eating (achila) is forbidden d'oraita — one may sell the cooked mixture to non-Jews or use it for non-food purposes, since the verse focuses on consumption.
Accepted Halacha The law follows Rav: meat and milk cooked together is forbidden for all benefit (issur hana'ah d'oraita). This is derived from the triple repetition of לֹא תְבַשֵּׁל גְּדִי בַּחֲלֵב אִמּוֹ.

🐾 Which Species Are Included in Basar B'Chalav?

D'Oraita (Torah Level)

  • Domesticated animals — beheima (ox, sheep, goat)
  • Wild animals — chaya (deer, gazelle)
  • Applies regardless of whether animal is kosher
  • Forbidden to cook, eat, and derive benefit

D'Rabbanan or Excluded

  • Poultry (ofot) — rabbinic fence only, not d'oraita
  • Fish (dagim) — fully excluded, no prohibition
  • Locusts (chagavim) — fully excluded
  • Human milk — leniency applies for medical/infant use

לֹא תְבַשֵּׁל גְּדִי בַּחֲלֵב אִמּוֹ — שמות כג:יט, שמות לד:כו, דברים יד:כא
📜 Scriptural Source — Triple Repetition The Torah writes לֹא תְבַשֵּׁל גְּדִי בַּחֲלֵב אִמּוֹ three times (Shemot 23:19, 34:26; Devarim 14:21). The Gemara derives: one prohibition for bishul (cooking), one for achila (eating), and one for hana'ah (benefit). Each repetition adds a new dimension of the prohibition.

🍽️ Step-by-Step: Ruling on a Meat-Milk Situation

Step 1 — Identify the species: Is this beheima, chaya, of (poultry), dag (fish), or chagav (locust)?
↓
Step 2 — Determine if bishul occurred: Was there actual cooking together? Cold mixing does not trigger d'oraita.
↓
Step 3 — Check for davar hamaamid: Is a coagulating agent (rennet, enzymes) present? If so, no nullification is possible.
↓
Step 4 — Apply bitul calculation: Is there 60 times the volume of the intruding ingredient? Taste test may also be used.
↓
Step 5 — Rule on hana'ah: If beheima cooked with milk, the mixture is forbidden even for benefit — cannot be sold, fed, or used commercially.
