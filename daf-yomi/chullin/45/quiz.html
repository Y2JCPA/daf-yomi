Chullin 45 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded

Question 1 of 5
According to Rav (via R' Chelbo), if the windpipe has tiny sieve-like perforations, what must they collectively equal to render the animal treifah?
Rav distinguishes two types of perforations: significant holes (missing flesh) join to the size of an issar, but tiny sieve-like holes that don't remove material join to a majority of the circumference. The logic: sieve holes aren't "missing" — they're like a cut, so the threshold is the same as for a cut windpipe (majority).
Question 2 of 5
What area of the neck is valid for slaughter, according to Rava's explanation of R' Chiyya bar Yosef?
Rava clarifies that the valid area for slaughter is the length of neck an animal naturally extends when grazing — not forced further. If the slaughterer forces the animal to stretch its neck beyond its natural range, that extra area is not valid for slaughter. The question of what happens if the animal forces itself to stretch remains unresolved (teku).
Question 3 of 5
According to Rav and Shmuel, which membrane of the brain, if perforated, renders the animal treifah?
Rav and Shmuel both agree the brain has two membranes — a thick outer one near the skull and a thin inner one near the brain. One version of their ruling says perforation of the outer membrane alone = treifah. Another version says the inner membrane must also be perforated. The mnemonic given is: "The bag in which the brain rests" — pointing to the inner membrane as the critical one.
Question 4 of 5
In the dispute about the aorta (the chief artery from the heart), what is the practical difference between Rav and Shmuel?
Rav holds that any perforation of the aorta renders the animal treifah, while Shmuel requires a majority perforation. Shmuel's reaction to hearing Rav's ruling was famously sharp: "If Abba said that, he knows nothing at all about treifot!" The aorta is the artery covered in fat exiting the heart along the sides of the lung.
Question 5 of 5
What do the two "bean-like protrusions" at the base of the skull determine?
R' Yehoshua ben Levi teaches that two bean-shaped protrusions sit at the opening of the skull. From the beans inward = brain territory (any perforation of its membrane = treifah). From the beans outward = spinal cord territory (majority must be cut to be treifah). The beans themselves are likely treated as brain. This matters because the two regions have very different damage thresholds.
