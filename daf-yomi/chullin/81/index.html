⚖️ Direction of the Oto V'et Beno Prohibition

Tanna Kamma
The prohibition applies in both directions — whether one slaughters the dam first and then the offspring, or the offspring first and then the dam on the same day, a violation occurs.
Rabbi Yehuda
The verse says אֹתוֹ וְאֶת בְּנוֹ — 'it and its son.' The prohibition is one-directional: only when the dam is slaughtered first, then the offspring. If the offspring is slaughtered first, no prohibition is violated.
Gemara
The practical nafka mina: if Reuven already slaughtered the offspring, may another person now slaughter the dam that same day? Per R' Yehuda — permitted; per Tanna Kamma — still forbidden.
Halacha
The halacha follows the Tanna Kamma: oto v'et beno is violated regardless of which — dam or offspring — was slaughtered first. The second slaughterer receives malkot.
Core Verse וְשׁוֹר אוֹ שֶׂה אֹתוֹ וְאֶת בְּנוֹ לֹא תִשְׁחֲטוּ בְּיוֹם אֶחָד — 'Whether it be a cow or ewe, do not slaughter it and its offspring on the same day' (Vayikra 22:28)

אֹתוֹ וְאֶת בְּנוֹ לֹא תִשְׁחֲטוּ בְּיוֹם אֶחָד (ויקרא כב:כח)
📖 The Prohibition: Oto V'et Beno — Core Rules The Torah forbids slaughtering a dam (mother) and her offspring on the same calendar day. This applies to domesticated kosher animals — ox, cow, sheep, and goat (behema tehora). The prohibition is violated by the second slaughterer, who receives malkot (lashes). The day runs from nightfall to nightfall (like all halachic days).

🧬 Kilayim Offspring — Does Oto V'et Beno Apply?

Question
A domesticated goat (behema) and a wild deer (chaya) produced hybrid offspring (kilayim). If the mother-goat and this hybrid are slaughtered the same day, is oto v'et beno violated?
First Opinion
The prohibition does apply. The maternal line determines status. Since the mother is a behema, the maternal-offspring bond is established — the prohibition follows the mother's category.
Second Opinion
The prohibition does not apply to kilayim offspring. The verse addresses 'it and its son' — implying both must be of the same species. A kilayim hybrid is not truly the 'ben' (son) of a pure behema for this prohibition.
Underlying Dispute Does the maternal bond alone define the oto v'et beno relationship, or must there be species homogeneity between dam and offspring?

🐾 Which Animals Are Covered by Oto V'et Beno?

✅ Fully Applies — Behema

  • 🐄 Cattle — ox and cow
  • 🐑 Sheep (and rams)
  • 🐐 Goats
  • Male and female offspring alike
  • Both directions: dam→offspring or offspring→dam

⚠️ Disputed — Hybrid/Edge Cases

  • 🧬 Kilayim offspring (hybrid animals)
  • Tumtum and androginos animals
  • Animal of unknown parentage
  • Behema raised as chaya or vice versa

❌ Does Not Apply

  • 🦌 Wild animals (chaya) — not included in verse
  • 🕊️ Birds (of) — separate halachic category
  • 🐟 Fish — not subject to shechita prohibition
  • Non-kosher species entirely
