Chullin 82 Quiz

QUESTION 1 OF 5
If Reuven slaughters a mother cow and then Shimon (who had no prior involvement) slaughters the calf the same day, who receives lashes?
A. Reuven, because he created the prohibited situation
B. Shimon, because he performed the second forbidden slaughter
C. Both Reuven and Shimon equally
D. Neither, since different people were involved
Correct: B — Only the second slaughterer receives lashes, regardless of who performed the first slaughter. The prohibition of oto v'et bno targets the second act of slaughter on that day.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
A non-Jew slaughters the mother animal, rendering it neveilah. May a Jew then slaughter the offspring on the same day without violating oto v'et bno?
A. No — the prohibition is triggered by any killing of the first animal
B. Yes — since the non-Jew's slaughter is not valid shechita, no prohibition was triggered
C. Only if the Jew did not know the non-Jew had slaughtered the mother
D. It depends on whether the neveilah was sold in the market
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that a non-Jew's slaughter is not halachically valid shechita and therefore does not trigger the oto v'et bno prohibition. A Jew may slaughter the offspring without concern.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the halachic status of the second animal slaughtered in violation of oto v'et bno?
A. The animal is treif and forbidden to eat
B. The animal is kosher, but the slaughterer receives lashes
C. The animal is only permitted after the fact (b'dieved) but not sold
D. The animal is forbidden as if it were neveilah
Correct: B — Despite the violation, both animals remain kosher and may be eaten. The punishment is lashes for the transgressor, but the shechita itself is valid. This is a case where a prohibition does not invalidate the act.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the principle of 'chazakat hashuk' (market presumption) in the context of oto v'et bno?
A. Any animal bought in a market must be checked for oto v'et bno concerns
B. Animals sold in a market are presumed not to have had a related animal slaughtered that day
C. Market animals are exempt from oto v'et bno entirely
D. The buyer assumes liability and must ask the seller in all cases
Correct: B — The Gemara establishes that in a large market, most animals have not had a related animal slaughtered that day. Therefore, a buyer may rely on this presumption and does not need to investigate unless given specific information to the contrary.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Regarding oto v'et bno on Yom Tov: if someone slaughtered the mother animal on Erev Yom Tov, what is the ruling about slaughtering the offspring on Yom Tov?
A. It is permitted because Yom Tov shechita overrides the oto v'et bno restriction
B. It is forbidden, since oto v'et bno applies even when the second slaughter falls on Yom Tov
C. It is permitted if one forgot about the first slaughter
D. It depends on whether the slaughter is for the Yom Tov meal or other purposes
Correct: B — The oto v'et bno prohibition is not overridden by the general permission to slaughter on Yom Tov for food. Even though shechita on Yom Tov is normally permitted, it remains forbidden to slaughter the related animal when the first was already slaughtered on Erev Yom Tov.
