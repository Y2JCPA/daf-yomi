QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Chisda, what is the ruling on a smooth, intact sirkha that adheres to the chest wall (dofan)?
A. Permitted, because the smoothness proves there was no perforation
B. Treif, because adhesion to the chest wall always indicates a prior breach
C. Permitted only if the inflation (nifuach) test shows no bubbles
D. Requires consulting two qualified poskim before rendering a ruling
Correct: B — Rav Chisda holds that any adhesion to the chest wall (dofan) is treif, regardless of smoothness. The very existence of the adhesion proves the lung was perforated at some point, even if it healed smoothly over time.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What halakhic test is performed to detect hidden perforations in a lung?
A. Pressing each lobe firmly to feel for softness or spongy areas
B. Soaking the lung in salted water and checking for discoloration
C. Inflating (nifuach) the lung while submerged in water and watching for bubbles
D. Weighing the lung and comparing it to a standard healthy lung weight
Correct: C — The nifuach involves sealing the trachea, submerging the lung in water, and inflating it through the bronchus. Emerging air bubbles indicate a perforation, rendering the animal treif. No bubbles means no breach.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Regarding the shidra (spinal cord), what precisely triggers the treifah ruling?
A. Any fracture of one or more vertebrae in the spinal column
B. The spinal cord (chut hashidra) being severed for the majority of its length
C. The animal showing signs of paralysis before slaughter
D. Three or more vertebrae being dislocated simultaneously
Correct: B — The Mishnah specifies that the treifah requires two conditions: the shidra (vertebral column) broken AND the chut hashidra (spinal cord) severed in its majority. The cord itself is the key criterion, not merely external vertebral damage.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rav Huna's leniency regarding sirkhot applies specifically to which type of adhesion?
A. Any adhesion anywhere in the chest cavity, provided it is smooth
B. Only adhesions between lobes of the same lung that are smooth and intact
C. Adhesions between the lung and the diaphragm when less than a tefach wide
D. Adhesions to the chest wall formed within 30 days of the animal's birth
Correct: B — Rav Huna's leniency is limited to lobe-to-lobe adhesions within the lung that are smooth (chalak) and intact, indicating no underlying perforation. This position became the basis for Ashkenazic practice regarding internal sirkhot.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the ruling if an entire lobe of the lung is missing (chisaron)?
A. Permitted, provided the remaining lobes are healthy and inflate properly
B. Treif — a missing lobe is treated as equivalent to a perforation
C. Depends on which specific lobe is missing; only the main lobe triggers treifah
D. Requires the nifuach test on remaining lobes before a ruling can be issued
Correct: B — A missing lobe (chisaron) renders the animal treif regardless of the condition of the remaining lobes. The absence of a lobe represents a fundamental defect in the lung's structure, classified as a treifah by the Gemara on Chullin 54.
