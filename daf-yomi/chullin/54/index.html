⚖️ Rav Huna vs. Rav Chisda: Smooth Adhesion to Chest Wall

Rav Huna
A sirkha that is smooth (chalak) and intact raises no concern — we do not presume an underlying perforation sealed over. Even an adhesion to the chest wall, if perfectly smooth, is permitted.
Rav Chisda
I disagree. An adhesion to the dofan (chest wall) is treif regardless of smoothness. Only an adhesion running lobe-to-lobe within the lung itself can benefit from the leniency of smoothness.
Rav Huna
The test of smoothness (chalak) is precisely to rule out perforation. If the surface is smooth, there is no sign of damage — what basis do you have for declaring it treif?
Rav Chisda
The very fact that the lung adhered to the chest wall at all is proof that it was punctured at some point and then healed. Smoothness cannot undo the logic of the presumption.
Legacy of This Dispute This machloket became the basis for the Ashkenaz/Sephardic split: the Rema follows Rav Huna's leniency for smooth adhesions; the Shulchan Aruch follows Rav Chisda's strictness regarding the chest wall.

🫁 Sirkhot — Permitted, Treif, and Disputed

✅ Mutar (Permitted)

  • Smooth (chalak) lobe-to-lobe adhesion within the same lung
  • Intact adhesion showing no fraying or tearing
  • Small accessory lobe adhered to main lobe — considered natural
  • No bubbles found on nifuach (inflation) test

❌ Treif (Forbidden)

  • Any adhesion to the chest wall (dofan) per Rav Chisda
  • Frayed, ruptured, or non-smooth adhesion anywhere
  • Lung missing an entire lobe (chisaron)
  • Perforation confirmed by inflation test (nifuach)

⚠️ Safek (Disputed)

  • Smooth adhesion to dofan — permitted by Rav Huna, treif by Rav Chisda
  • Adhesion to the diaphragm — Rishonim debate
  • Partial lobe adhesion in unusual anatomical location — requires expert

🦴 The Shidra — Spinal Cord as Treifah

נִשְׁבְּרָה שִׁדְרָתוֹ וְנִפְסַק חוּט הַשִּׁדְרָה — טְרֵפָה
Mishnah Chullin 3:1 If the shidra (spinal column) is broken and the chut hashidra (spinal cord) is severed for the majority of its length, the animal is treif. The Gemara clarifies: it is the cord itself — not merely vertebral fracture — that is determinative. Even if the vertebrae appear intact externally, severance of the cord's majority renders the animal a treifah that cannot survive.

🔬 The Nifuach — Step-by-Step Lung Inspection

Step 1 — Visual survey: Count all lung lobes. A missing lobe (chisaron) immediately renders the animal treif without further examination.
↓
Step 2 — Locate sirkhot: Feel for adhesions between lobes or between the lung and surrounding tissue (chest wall, diaphragm, pericardium).
↓
Step 3 — Classify the sirkha: Lobe-to-lobe → apply smoothness test. External (to dofan) → treif per Rav Chisda; permitted per Rav Huna if smooth.
↓
Step 4 — Inflate underwater (nifuach): Seal the trachea, submerge the lung in water, and inflate via the bronchus. Emerging bubbles reveal a perforation → treif.
↓
Step 5 — Render the ruling: No bubbles + smooth internal adhesion only = permitted. Any doubt, external adhesion, or bubbles = treif. When in doubt, consult a posek.
