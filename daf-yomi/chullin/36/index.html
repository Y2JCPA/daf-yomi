💧 The Seven Liquids — Background

Leviticus 11:34,38: Food only becomes susceptible to ritual impurity after it contacts one of seven specific liquids. Before that contact, impurity cannot "take hold" of the food.

The Seven Liquids

  • 💧 Water
  • 🌿 Dew
  • 🫒 Oil
  • 🍷 Wine
  • 🥛 Milk
  • 🩸 Blood
  • 🍯 Honey

Today's Question

  • Blood is on the list ✅
  • But does ALL blood qualify?
  • What about blood that spurts out in an arterial surge?
  • What about blood that flows during slaughter specifically?
  • ⚠️ Not all blood is the same
3 / 8

🚫 Arterial Surge Blood — Excluded

School of Rabbi Yishmael: The verse says "drinks the blood of carcasses." Only blood that flows naturally from a carcass is included. Blood ejected in a sudden arterial surge (kishush) is categorically excluded.
🩸 Blood that flows freely from a carcass
↓
✅ Renders food susceptible to impurity
⬇
💥 Blood ejected in arterial surge (kishush)
↓
❌ Does NOT render food susceptible — it's a different phenomenon

⚖️ Three-Way Dispute: Blood on a Teruma Gourd

Case: Someone slaughters an animal and blood splashes onto a gourd of teruma. Does the slaughter blood render the gourd susceptible to impurity?
Rabbi
✅ The gourd IS rendered susceptible. Slaughter blood counts as one of the seven liquids — it flows during a legitimate act, so it qualifies.
R'CH
❓ Abeyance. I cannot rule either way. The status of slaughter blood is genuinely uncertain — I leave it suspended.
RbL
❌ The gourd is NOT rendered susceptible. Slaughter only "happens" at its conclusion — blood during the act is just wound blood, not slaughter blood.
5 / 8

🔑 The Root Dispute: When Does Slaughter "Happen"?

Rav Pappa's analysis: The three positions reduce to one key question — at what moment does slaughter take legal effect?
Rabbi
Slaughter takes effect throughout the act — all the blood that flows is "slaughter blood"
Reish Lakish
Slaughter only defined at its conclusion — earlier blood is "wound blood," not slaughter blood
↓
Rabbi Chiyya: Also holds conclusion-only — but uncertain whether conclusion-blood qualifies as "slaughter blood" or "wound blood." Permanent uncertainty.
Rav Pappa's refinement: When blood is present continuously from start to end, even Rabbi Chiyya sees it as a dispute. When blood is wiped off mid-slaughter, Rabbi Chiyya holds it in abeyance because it might be wound blood throughout.
6 / 8

♾️ Rav Ashi: An Eternal Teku

Interp
Rabbi Chiyya's "abeyance" is just a placeholder — eventually the law will be clarified and we'll know if slaughter blood renders food susceptible or not.
R'Ashi
"One places the matter in abeyance — forever." Rabbi Chiyya identified this as a genuine teku — an unresolvable halakhic uncertainty that remains suspended indefinitely.
What is a teku? The word teku (תֵּיקוּ) marks questions the Talmud cannot resolve. Some Sages say it means "let it stand." Some say it's an acronym: Tishbi yetaretz kushyot u'ba'ayot — Elijah the Tishbite will resolve difficulties and questions (when he comes to herald the Messianic era). Either way: this case stays permanently open.
