Chullin 8 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded

Question 1 of 5
Why is slaughter with a white-hot knife considered valid?
Shmuel's ruling explains that the cutting of the simanim occurs before the burning effect takes hold. Since shechitah requires cutting (not burning), the slaughter is completed validly by the cutting action, even though burning follows immediately after.
Question 2 of 5
According to Rav Nachman, why is it permitted to slaughter with an idol-worship knife but forbidden to cut meat with it?
The key distinction is about "benefit" (hana'ah). Slaughtering an animal doesn't derive benefit from the idol-worship knife — it's just a cutting tool doing a necessary action. But cutting meat into portions improves the meat, which constitutes benefiting from the forbidden object. Exception: if the animal is dying, even slaughter is forbidden because saving the meat is itself a benefit.
Question 3 of 5
What is the disagreement between Rav and R' Yochanan regarding slaughtering with a gentile's knife?
Rav requires peeling a layer of flesh from the cut area because he holds the slaughter area is hot, causing the forbidden residue on the knife to be absorbed. R' Yochanan says rinsing suffices, implying the area is cold and residue stays on the surface. Both agree the slaughter itself is valid.
Question 4 of 5
Why does Rav say a slaughterer needs three separate knives?
The three knives ensure strict separation: one for slaughter, one for kosher meat, one for forbidden fats (chelev). Using a knife that touched chelev to then cut meat would transfer forbidden residue. Even using one knife for fats first and then meat is problematic — the residue contaminates the meat. Similarly, two separate water vessels prevent cross-contamination during rinsing.
Question 5 of 5
What broader principle about kashrut practice emerges from the discussions on daf 8?
Daf 8 demonstrates that kashrut is not just about the act of shechitah — it's a comprehensive system. Every knife, every vessel, and every surface that contacts the meat must be monitored. Residue from forbidden fats, idol worship, or treifah animals can contaminate through tools, requiring separate equipment and specific purging procedures.
