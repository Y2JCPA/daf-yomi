5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the minimum number of sheep required to become obligated in reishit hagez?
A. One sheep
B. Three sheep
C. Five sheep
D. Ten sheep
Correct: C — The Mishnah (Chullin 11:1) establishes that only a person who owns at least 5 sheep is obligated in reishit hagez. Fewer sheep constitute too small a flock to impose the burden of the gift.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Beit Hillel, what is the minimum amount of wool that satisfies the reishit hagez obligation?
A. 1 sela weight
B. 5 sela weight
C. 60 sela weight
D. Any amount at all
Correct: B — Beit Hillel rules that 5 sela weight of wool is the minimum required. This lighter standard is accepted as halakha. Beit Shammai required the much larger amount of 60 sela.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
The Gemara derives the geographic scope of reishit hagez (whether it applies in the diaspora) by comparing it to which other mitzvah?
A. Terumah and maaser
B. Bikurim (first fruits)
C. Matanot — the slaughter gifts (foreleg, cheeks, stomach)
D. Challah separation from dough
Correct: C — The Torah in Devarim 18:3–4 juxtaposes matanot (slaughter gifts) and reishit hagez. The Gemara uses this textual proximity (hekesh) to argue that since matanot applies in the diaspora, reishit hagez may as well.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is a key halakhic difference between reishit hagez and matanot (the priestly slaughter gifts)?
A. Matanot requires 5 animals; reishit hagez has no minimum
B. Reishit hagez requires at least 5 sheep; matanot has no minimum animal count
C. Reishit hagez must be given in the Temple; matanot can be given anywhere
D. Matanot applies only to sheep; reishit hagez applies to all livestock
Correct: B — Unlike matanot — which applies every time a person slaughters even a single animal — reishit hagez imposes a minimum ownership threshold of 5 sheep. Below that number, the owner is completely exempt.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which biblical verse is the direct source (kra) for the mitzvah of reishit hagez?
A. Shemot 23:19 — 'The first of your firstfruits you shall bring'
B. Vayikra 19:9 — 'When you reap the harvest of your land'
C. Devarim 18:4 — 'The first shearing of your flock shall you give to him'
D. Bamidbar 18:12 — 'All the best of the oil and wine'
Correct: C — Devarim 18:4 is the explicit Torah source: 'רֵאשִׁית גֵּז צֹאנְךָ תִּתֶּן לּוֹ' — the first shearing of your flock shall you give to the kohen. This verse appears immediately after the verse establishing the priestly slaughter gifts.
