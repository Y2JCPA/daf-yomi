📜 The Biblical Source and Its Scope

ויקרא כב:כח — אֹתוֹ וְאֶת-בְּנוֹ לֹא תִשְׁחֲטוּ בְּיוֹם אֶחָד
Vayikra 22:28 The Torah forbids slaughtering an ox or a sheep together with its young on the same day. The Gemara on Daf 80 probes the precise limits of this verse: Does it cover chayot (wild animals) as well as behemot (domestic animals)? Does it apply when the sex of the animal is unknown — a tumtum or androginos? And what level of certainty about the relationship is required before the prohibition is triggered? Each boundary determines both the issur and whether malkot (lashes) follow a violation.

⚧️ Tumtum and Androginos: Are They Subject to Oto V'et Bno?

Abaye
A tumtum IS subject to oto v'et bno. The Torah says "oto" and "bno", but these words describe the relationship, not the sex. Wherever a genuine parent-offspring bond exists, the prohibition applies — the animal's unresolved gender does not eliminate that biological reality.
Rava
The verse uses explicit masculine language throughout. A tumtum's sex is entirely unresolved — it may not be male at all. Since malkot requires that the violator acted against a definite Torah prohibition, the uncertainty in gender creates a safek that bars lashes entirely.
Abaye
Agreed on malkot — no lashes in the safek. But l'chatchila one must still refrain. The precautionary stringency stands even when the Torah-level issur is uncertain. We do not permit the act just because lashes cannot follow.
Rava
And an androginos — having both male and female organs — is its own category of doubt. It is neither definitively male nor definitively female. It receives the same treatment: no malkot, but rabbinic caution applies.
Halacha l'ma'aseh Tumtum and androginos: no malkot, but the act is rabbinically forbidden as a safek violation of oto v'et bno.

❓ Three Categories of Safek in Oto V'et Bno

Seller's Uncertainty

  • Sold a cow AND its calf on the same day
  • Uncertain which sale occurred first
  • Seller cannot slaughter the remaining animal that day
  • The safek binds the one who HAS knowledge of the relationship
  • Buyer who is unaware faces no prohibition

Unknown Parentage

  • Animal's lineage is entirely unknown
  • Cannot identify whether two animals are related
  • No prohibition applies in a pure safek of identity
  • But if circumstantial evidence suggests relationship — be stringent
  • The doubt must be genuine, not manufactured to evade the law

Two Buyers, Same Day

  • Two people buy one animal each from a known pair
  • Both slaughter on the same day
  • The SECOND slaughter violates oto v'et bno
  • If simultaneous — neither receives malkot (cannot determine sequence)
  • The issur was still violated even without malkot

🔍 Step-by-Step: Ruling a Safek Oto V'et Bno Case

Step 1 — Identify the Relationship: Is there a known or suspected parent-offspring bond between the two animals? If none can be established at all, no prohibition applies.
↓
Step 2 — Assess Knowledge: Who is aware of the relationship — the seller, the buyer, or both? The prohibition falls on the person who knows the connection exists.
↓
Step 3 — Check for Certainty: Is the relationship a vadai (certainty) or a safek (doubt)? Certainty → full Torah prohibition. Doubt → rabbinic stringency only.
↓
Step 4 — Gender Certainty: Is the animal definitively male or female? If tumtum or androginos, no malkot can be given regardless of other factors.
↓
Step 5 — Apply the Dual Rule: Rule l'chumra (stringently) for the prohibition itself in all safek cases — do not slaughter. But rule l'kula for malkot — lashes require absolute certainty at every level.
