⏳ Mar Ukva vs. His Father: How Long to Wait?

Mar Ukva's Father
When I eat meat, I do not eat cheese until the following day — a full day's separation between meat and dairy at all times.
Mar Ukva
I am like vinegar compared to wine beside my father. I avoid dairy only at the same meal — at the next meal I may eat dairy freely.
Mar Ukva's Humility Mar Ukva openly acknowledges his father's greater stringency, yet his practice — waiting meal-to-meal — became the accepted halachic baseline for separating meat and dairy.

🥩 Two Traditions: Meat-to-Dairy Waiting

Mar Ukva's Father

  • Waited a full 24 hours after eating meat
  • Would not eat dairy until the next day
  • An extraordinary personal stringency
  • His son called him 'wine' — the gold standard of piety

Mar Ukva

  • Did not eat dairy at the same meal as meat
  • At the next meal, dairy was permitted
  • Called himself 'vinegar' by comparison
  • Represents the accepted halachic minimum practice

🐓 Does the Torah Forbid Poultry with Milk?

Question: Is poultry with milk forbidden by Torah law or only rabbinically?
↓
Torah Law
Poultry is excluded from the Torah prohibition — 'do not cook a kid in its mother's milk' requires an actual mother–offspring relationship, which poultry lacks
Rabbinic Decree
The Rabbis prohibited poultry with milk as a protective fence — it resembles meat and consumption could lead to confusion with actual meat
↓
Rav Chisda: Poultry with milk is forbidden rabbinically — one who eats it transgresses a rabbinic prohibition

💧 Mayim Acharonim — Chova!

מַיִם אַחֲרוֹנִים חוֹבָה מִפְּנֵי מֶלַח סְדוֹמִית שֶׁמְּסַמֶּמֶת אֶת הָעֵינַיִם
מַיִם אַחֲרוֹנִים חוֹבָה Post-meal handwashing is not a custom but an obligation (חוֹבָה). The Gemara provides a concrete danger: Melach Sedomit (מֶלַח סְדוֹמִית) is physically hazardous — visually indistinguishable from regular salt, it can blind a person if rubbed into the eyes after handling. Salt accumulates on hands throughout the meal, making washing after eating essential for safety.

🧂 Melach Sedomit — Why It Makes Mayim Acharonim Obligatory

AspectDetail
What is it?A salt from the Sodom region, finely ground and nearly invisible to the eye
The dangerCan permanently blind a person if residue on hands is rubbed into the eyes
Why at meal's end?Salt is used throughout the meal; hands accumulate residue without awareness
The halachic rulingChazal declared Mayim Acharonim 'חוֹבָה' (obligatory) to prevent this injury
ScopeApplies after any meal where salt was handled; not merely a pious practice

🤲 From Meat Meal to Dairy: The Proper Sequence

Eat meat at a meal
↓
Clear the table (קִנּוּחַ) and rinse the mouth (הַדָּחָה) — required before any subsequent dairy
↓
Wash hands (מַיִם אֶמְצָעִיִּים) — intermediate handwashing between meat and dairy courses or meals
↓
Wait between meals — do not eat dairy at the same meal; at minimum wait until the next meal (Mar Ukva's standard)
↓
Eat dairy — now permitted; the next-meal standard is the accepted halachic baseline
↓
Perform Mayim Acharonim after the dairy meal — obligatory, due to the danger of Sodomite salt on the hands
