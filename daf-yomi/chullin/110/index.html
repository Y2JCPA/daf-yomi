נ"ט בר נ"ט דהיתירא — שרי
📖 Core Concept of the Daf Nat bar nat (טעם בר טעם — "flavor of a flavor") is the principle that a food absorbing flavor indirectly, through a neutral intermediary, receives only a second-degree taste. When the original source was itself permitted, this attenuated flavor does not render the food forbidden from being combined with the opposing category (meat or dairy). The Gemara applies this across several practical cases on this daf.

🍞 May Bread Baked in a Meat Oven Be Eaten with Cheese?

Questioner
Meat was roasting in an oven and bread was baked alongside it simultaneously. The bread absorbed the steam and vapor of the roasting meat. May this bread now be eaten with cheese?
Stringent Position
The bread absorbed genuine meat flavor — the vapor rose directly into the bread. This is no different from cooking with meat. The bread should be treated as meaty and forbidden with dairy.
Mar bar Rav Ashi
Vapor is already an indirect medium. The meat first gave off vapor (first degree), and the vapor then entered the bread (second degree). This is nat bar nat — and since the meat itself was permitted, this second-degree absorption does not prohibit the bread.
Gemara's Ruling The bread may be eaten with dairy. Vapor-based absorption constitutes nat bar nat — an indirect transfer through a neutral intermediary — and is therefore permitted.

🥛 Bird with Milk: Biblical vs. Rabbinic Prohibition

Biblical Prohibition (De'oraita)

  • Applies only to domesticated animals (behemot)
  • Derived from: "Do not cook a kid in its mother's milk"
  • Requires potential mother-child nursing relationship
  • Three Biblical mentions: cooking, eating, deriving benefit
  • Stringent — no nat bar nat leniency at Biblical level

Rabbinic Prohibition (Derabanan)

  • Applies to birds (עוף) with milk — no nursing relationship
  • Decreed as a protective fence (seyag) around Torah law
  • Nat bar nat of poultry flavor is more readily permitted
  • Poultry/dairy mixture may not be cooked but some leniences apply
  • Some Rishonim debate exact scope and circumstances of decree

⚖️ Halachic Decision Tree: Is This Absorbed Flavor Prohibited?

Step 1: Identify the source — is the flavor coming from meat, dairy, or something pareve?
↓
Step 2: Count the degrees of separation between the original source and the food in question.
↓
Step 3: First-degree direct contact → the food takes on the identity of the source (meat or dairy) and is forbidden with the opposite.
↓
Step 4: Second-degree indirect absorption (nat bar nat) from a permitted intermediary → apply leniency; the food may be combined with the opposite category.
↓
Step 5: Apply meluach k'mevushal rule — if salted for 24 hours, treat all resulting contact as equivalent to direct cooking. No nat bar nat leniency.
↓
Step 6: Assess whether the original item was itself forbidden or permitted — nat bar nat of a forbidden source (nat bar nat de'issura) is more stringent.
