QUESTION 1 OF 5
What does 'nat bar nat dehetera' permit on Chullin 110?
A. Eating meat and dairy together when both are fully cooked
B. Combining a food that absorbed flavor indirectly through a permitted intermediary with the opposing category
C. Using a meat pot for dairy if it was washed thoroughly
D. Salting meat and fish together when both are fresh
Correct: B — Nat bar nat dehetera — 'flavor of a flavor from a permitted source' — allows a food that absorbed flavor indirectly (through a neutral intermediary) to still be combined with the opposing category (meat or dairy), since the resulting taste is too attenuated to create a prohibition.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why is poultry cooked with milk only a Rabbinic rather than a Biblical prohibition?
A. Birds are not considered food under Torah law
B. The Rabbis of the Talmud never actually finalized this decree
C. The Biblical prohibition requires a possible mother-child nursing relationship, which birds do not have with milk
D. Poultry fat is classified differently from animal fat in all cases
Correct: C — The Torah's prohibition of basar b'chalav is derived from 'do not cook a kid in its mother's milk,' implying a mother-child nursing relationship. Since birds do not nurse young with milk, there is no Biblical prohibition. The Rabbis extended the ban to poultry with milk as a protective fence (seyag).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Gemara, may bread baked in an oven where meat was simultaneously roasting be eaten with cheese?
A. No — the bread absorbed direct meat flavor and is permanently fleishig
B. Yes — bread cannot absorb flavor under any circumstances
C. Yes — vapor absorption is nat bar nat, an indirect transfer, so the bread may be eaten with dairy
D. Only if the meat had not yet reached the temperature of yad soledes bo
Correct: C — The Gemara rules the bread is permitted with dairy. Vapor is an indirect medium — the meat first produced vapor (one step), and the vapor entered the bread (second step). This second-degree absorption from a permitted source is nat bar nat dehetera and does not make the bread meaty.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the rule of 'meluach k'mevushal' and how does it apply to meat and milk?
A. Roasted meat is always treated more leniently than boiled meat
B. Meat that has been salted for 24 hours is treated halachically as if it were cooked, making contact during salting as serious as cooking together
C. Salting neutralizes all absorbed flavors and renders meat pareve
D. Salted meat may always be combined with dairy because salt draws out the meat flavor
Correct: B — Meluach k'mevushal means 'salted is like cooked.' Prolonged salting (particularly 24 hours) extracts and transmits flavor with the intensity of heat-based cooking. Therefore, meat salted alongside other foods for 24 hours creates the same halachic issues as if they were cooked together — overriding potential nat bar nat leniencies.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Fish placed on a plate that had meat residue — what is the ruling when that fish is later served with dairy?
A. Forbidden — the plate transferred meat flavor directly to the fish in one step
B. Permitted — this is nat bar nat: meat to plate (first degree), plate to fish (second degree), through a permitted intermediary
C. Forbidden only if the plate was warm at the time
D. Permitted only if the fish was itself salted to draw out residual absorbed flavor
Correct: B — This is a classic nat bar nat case on Chullin 110. The meat drippings transferred to the plate (first degree), and the plate transferred to the fish (second degree). Since the intermediary (the plate) is a neutral, permitted vessel and the transfer is indirect, this qualifies as nat bar nat dehetera — and the fish may be served with dairy.
