🐄 Must a Fallen Animal Be Examined?

Rav
A nefulah that fell from 10 tefachim onto a hard surface requires examination of all its organs — we suspect internal damage to the lung, liver, and spinal cord even if the animal walks.
Shmuel
Standard shechita examination suffices. Unless there is a visible wound or the animal cannot walk, we do not require additional bedikah beyond the normal procedure.
R. Yochanan
Even a lesser fall onto hard stone demands we inspect the lung for perforation — the external appearance of health cannot rule out internal damage from impact.
Practical Ruling A nefulah falling 10+ tefachim onto a hard surface requires full bedikah. If the organs cannot be examined, the animal is forbidden.

🦴 Spinal Injuries and Their Treifah Status

Vertebra Cracked (Cord Intact)

  • Spinal column cracked but still continuous
  • חוט השדרה — spinal cord uninjured
  • Animal is kosher after valid shechita
  • No additional spine-specific bedikah required

Spinal Cord Severed

  • חוט השדרה itself is cut through
  • Animal cannot survive — classic treifah
  • Treif regardless of other organ status
  • One of the 18 enumerated treifot (Chullin 42a)

Majority of Ribs Broken

  • רוב צלעות — majority on one side broken
  • Indicates severe blunt trauma to chest
  • Raises concern for lung perforation
  • Combined with bedikah findings determines ruling

⚖️ Rov Tzlaot — Counting Ribs and the Spine

Rav Huna
We count majority from the spine side — if most ribs on one side are broken at the vertebral attachment, the structural integrity of the thorax is destroyed and the animal is treif.
Rav Chisda
We evaluate from the breastbone side. A 'majority' means the majority of the full rib length is compromised. The lung requires the complete rib arc for protection.
Rava
Rib count is only a proxy for organ damage. The ultimate question is always whether the lung could have been perforated — we must still examine the lung directly after slaughter.
Counting Note There are 11 full ribs on each side. Majority = 6 or more. The most stringent position counts breakage at the spinal attachment point.

🩸 The Principle of Hidden Damage

חולין נ"א — נפולה הצריכה בדיקה פנימית
Core Halachic Insight — חולין נ"א The entire sugya of nefulah rests on a fundamental principle: internal damage may not be visible externally. An animal may walk normally and appear completely healthy yet have sustained a fatal internal injury from the fall.

This is why the Torah's concern for treifah requires examining what we cannot see. The halacha distinguishes nefulah from a simple wound precisely because the trauma was real but its effects are hidden.

This connects to the broader rule: אין אנו בודקין מבחוץ לבד — external appearance alone cannot certify an animal's kashrut status when a known traumatic event has occurred.
