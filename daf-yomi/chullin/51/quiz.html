Chullin 51 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
From what minimum height must an animal fall (onto a hard surface) to require a bedikah for nefulah concerns?
A. 3 tefachim
B. 10 tefachim
C. 4 amot
D. Any height triggers a bedikah
Correct: B — The Gemara in Chullin 51 establishes 10 tefachim as the threshold height for a fall onto a hard surface to trigger the nefulah concern requiring examination of internal organs.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the primary organ examined after a nefulah fall, and why?
A. The heart — it can rupture from impact
B. The lung — it is the most vulnerable to perforation from rib fractures and impact
C. The liver — it controls blood and must be checked
D. The stomach — it may burst from internal pressure
Correct: B — The lung (ריאה) is the primary focus of nefulah examination because it is enclosed by the ribs and most susceptible to perforation from broken ribs or direct impact, and a perforated lung is a classic treifah.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If the חוט השדרה (spinal cord) of an animal is found to be severed, the animal is:
A. Kosher if the shechita itself was performed correctly
B. Treif regardless of the condition of other organs
C. Requires examination of the lungs before a ruling is issued
D. Permitted if a majority of other organs are found intact
Correct: B — Severing of the spinal cord (chut hashidra) is one of the 18 classic treifot enumerated in Chullin 42a. It renders the animal treif absolutely, independent of any other organ's condition.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does 'rov tzlaot' mean and why is it halachically significant?
A. A majority of the animal's fat — relevant to chelev prohibitions
B. A majority of ribs broken on one side — indicates severe chest trauma threatening the lungs
C. The larger portion of the animal — determines how treifah invalidation spreads
D. Most of the spinal vertebrae — a separate category of shidra treifah
Correct: B — Rov tzlaot (majority of ribs broken on one side) indicates severe blunt trauma to the thoracic cavity, raising concern that the lung — protected by those ribs — may have been perforated, potentially rendering the animal treif.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If a nefulah is fully examined after slaughter and all internal organs are found completely intact, what is the halachic ruling?
A. Still forbidden — the act of falling itself disqualifies the animal
B. Permitted — the bedikah resolves the concern
C. Forbidden until inspected by a qualified posek in person
D. Permitted only if it also walked normally immediately after the fall
Correct: B — The bedikah (examination) exists precisely to resolve the nefulah concern. If examination confirms that all organs are intact and undamaged, the concern is fully cleared and the animal is permitted — the fall alone, without resulting organ damage, does not render it treif.
