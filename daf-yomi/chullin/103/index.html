🐔 Fowl + Milk: Torah or Rabbinic?

Beit Shammai
Fowl may be placed on the table alongside cheese — but may not be eaten together with milk. We treat it stringently.
Beit Hillel
Fowl may be placed on the table with cheese and eaten together — the Torah prohibition does not apply to fowl, only to mammals with mother's milk.
Rabbi Yose
This is one of the cases where Beit Shammai ruled leniently and Beit Hillel ruled strictly — a remarkable reversal of their usual positions!
Why the debate? The verse says 'do not cook a גְּדִי (kid) in its mother's milk' — a kid is a mammal. Fowl produces no mother's milk, so the Torah prohibition may not apply. The Sages nonetheless decreed against it.

🦴 Ever Min HaChai

אֵבֶר מִן הַחַי — שִׁיעוּרוֹ בְּכַזַּיִת
Torah Prohibition — Seven Noahide + Israel It is forbidden to eat a limb severed from a living animal. The minimum shiur rendering one liable is a כזית (ke'zayit) — an olive's bulk of flesh and bone combined. This applies to all animals, including non-kosher ones, and is both a Noahide law and a Torah obligation for Israel.

📜 Three Prohibitions from One Repeated Verse

The verse: "לֹא תְבַשֵּׁל גְּדִי בַּחֲלֵב אִמּוֹ" — written three times in the Torah
Exodus 23:19 · Exodus 34:26 · Deuteronomy 14:21
↓
First repetition → Cooking (בישול): Forbidden to cook meat and milk together
↓
Second repetition → Eating (אכילה): Forbidden to eat meat and milk together
↓
Third repetition → Benefit (הנאה): Forbidden to derive any benefit from meat-and-milk mixture
↓
Result: Three independent Torah prohibitions, each with its own violation — one who only cooks (without eating) still transgresses

🥛 Which Animals Are Subject to Basar B'Chalav?

Torah Prohibition

  • Domesticated mammals — בהמה טהורה
  • Ox, sheep, goat: classic cases
  • Requires offspring + mother who produces milk
  • All three prohibitions (cooking, eating, benefit) apply

Rabbinic Decree Only

  • Fowl (עוף) — per Beit Hillel and final halacha
  • No mother's milk biologically
  • Sages decreed due to maarit ayin (appearance of wrongdoing)
  • May not eat chicken with milk, but less severe than Torah violation

Fully Exempt (Rabbi Akiva)

  • Fish (דגים) — not 'basar' in this context
  • Locusts (חגבים) — no mother-offspring milk relationship
  • Permitted even with actual milk by Torah law
  • No rabbinic decree according to Rabbi Akiva's ruling

🍽️ The Table Dispute — Fowl and Cheese Together

Beit Shammai
Fowl may be placed on the table alongside cheese. However, it may not be eaten with milk — we maintain a stringency against it.
Beit Hillel
Fowl may be placed on the table with cheese and also eaten together — since there is no Torah-level basar b'chalav for fowl.
Gemara
The halacha follows Beit Hillel that fowl+milk is rabbinic — yet the Sages' decree stands. The leniency vs. stringency here is unusual: Beit Shammai is relatively lenient!
Practical Halacha Eating fowl with milk is rabbinically forbidden. Placing them on the same table together (without eating together) was historically debated but many Rishonim are lenient.
