🏜️ Could Jews Eat Meat in the Wilderness?

R' Yishmael

  • 🏜️ In the wilderness: "meat of desire" (regular meat) was forbidden
  • 🐄 Could only eat meat from sacrifices brought to the Mishkan
  • 🏠 Upon entering Eretz Yisrael: "meat of desire" became permitted
  • 📜 "If the place is too far from you... you shall slaughter" — distance permits meat

R' Akiva

  • 🏜️ In the wilderness: "meat of desire" was permitted
  • ⚔️ Permitted via nechira (stabbing) — no shechitah needed
  • 🏠 Upon entering Eretz Yisrael: stabbing was forbidden
  • 📜 "If the place is too far" — taught the method (shechitah), not the permission

⚔️ Nechira (Stabbing) vs. Shechitah (Slaughter)

Nechira: Killing by stabbing — a quick knife thrust into the neck. No back-and-forth cutting motion.
↓
Shechitah: Cutting with a back-and-forth motion across the simanim (windpipe and esophagus). Requires specific technique.
↓
According to R' Akiva: in the wilderness, nechira was enough. After Sinai/entering the land, shechitah became mandatory.
↓
Today: nechira is completely invalid — the animal is neveilah
Why does this matter now? Even though nechira is long forbidden, the dispute explains the Torah's language. R' Akiva's view means the Torah changed the method — from stabbing to shechitah. R' Yishmael's view means the Torah changed the access — from sacrificial-only to personal-use meat.

🔪 Three Types of Notched Knives

Rava identifies three categories of knife defects:

1. "Catches" (Ogeret)

  • 🔪 The notch catches on your fingernail when you slide across it
  • ❌ May NOT slaughter with it
  • ❌ If slaughtered — INVALID

2. "Entangles" (Masrekes)

  • 🔪 The notch tangles fibers but doesn't fully catch
  • ❌ May NOT slaughter with it
  • ⚠️ If slaughtered — debated

3. "Smooth-ish" (Sasa)

  • 🔪 Like an awn of grain — slightly rough but no actual notch
  • ✅ May slaughter with it
  • ✅ Valid

🧪 Knife Examination — A Torah Obligation

RH
Rav Chisda: From where do we know that examining the knife is a Torah obligation? From the verse: "And you shall slaughter with this" — the word "this" implies: examine the instrument first!
Gm
This teaches that David HaMelech handed Shmuel a specific knife and said "slaughter with THIS" — meaning: inspect and use this particular verified instrument.
Practical implication: Before every shechitah, the knife must be examined for nicks and defects. This isn't just good practice — it's a Torah-level obligation. A slaughterer who fails to present the knife to a scholar is subject to penalty.

🔍 Multiple Notches = A Saw

A knife with several notches is like a saw
↓
A saw TEARS rather than CUTS — invalid for shechitah
↓
What about a knife with only ONE notch?
↓
If the notch has a sharp edge on only one side — that leading edge tears on the way in
↓
Invalid — the first edge rips the tissue before the blade can cut
The Torah's standard: Shechitah must be clean cutting — a smooth, continuous back-and-forth motion. Anything that tears, rips, or catches the tissue renders the slaughter invalid because it causes suffering and doesn't produce a clean cut.
