5 questions · Self-graded

Question 1 of 5
Where on a bird's neck is melikah (pinching) performed, and how does it differ from shechitah?
Melikah is performed by a Kohen from the nape (back of the neck) using his thumbnail — it cuts the simanim from the back. Shechitah is performed from the front of the throat using any sharp detached instrument. The two methods operate on exactly opposite sides of the bird's neck — and what qualifies for one disqualifies for the other.
Question 2 of 5
What is the halachic consequence if a Kohen performs melikah using a knife instead of his thumbnail?
Using a knife for melikah invalidates it. The Torah specifies that melikah is done with the Kohen's own thumbnail — not any instrument. A knife cutting the simanim from the back of the neck would constitute shechitah performed from the wrong direction, which is also invalid for bird offerings. Neither melikah nor valid shechitah is achieved.
Question 3 of 5
What is the dispute about whether bird shechitah is Biblical or Rabbinic?
If bird shechitah is Biblical, then violating the laws of shechitah for birds (e.g., ripping the simanim) is a Biblical violation. If shechitah for birds is only Rabbinic, then violations are only Rabbinic. Rav Ashi points out this asymmetry: the level of the requirement determines the severity of violations, affecting how courts treat different cases.
Question 4 of 5
What does Ze'eiri rule about an animal whose neck bone is broken and most surrounding flesh is cut?
Ze'eiri rules it is a treifah. An animal that has both its neck bone broken AND most of the surrounding flesh severed cannot survive. Since treifot are animals that were already fatally wounded before slaughter, shechitah (no matter how perfect) cannot render them kosher. The prior fatal injury preempts the shechitah.
Question 5 of 5
What is the principle R' Yirmeya states about the relationship between what is valid for shechitah vs. melikah?
R' Yirmeya's principle is that shechitah and melikah are symmetrically exclusive. The location valid for shechitah (front/throat) is invalid for melikah, and the location valid for melikah (nape/back) is invalid for shechitah. The instruments too: what works for one (knife vs. thumbnail) disqualifies the other. They are designed as complementary opposites.
