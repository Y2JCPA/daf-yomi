⭐ THE Mishna — Simanim Count

הַשּׁוֹחֵט אֶחָד בָּעוֹף וּשְׁנַיִם בִּבְהֵמָה שְׁחִיטָתוֹ כְּשֵׁרָה

"One who slaughters one siman in a bird and two simanim in an animal — his shechitah is valid."
This is the foundational rule: A bird requires cutting the majority of ONE siman (either windpipe or gullet). An animal requires cutting the majority of BOTH simanim. And the majority of one siman plus one in a bird is also valid. This Mishna codifies what was debated in earlier dapim.

📍 Where Does "Slaughter From the Neck" Come From?

The Gemara notes: The Torah never explicitly says "slaughter from the neck." Three different derivations are offered — a mnemonic: Kaf, Mem, Shin (Rav Kahana, Rav Yeimar, School of R' Yishmael).
RK
Rav Kahana: "And he shall slaughter" (v'shachat) — the root sh-ch-t relates to shach (bending/lowering). Where does an animal bend? The NECK. So shechitah is from the place that bends.
Gm
Challenge: Why not say "from the tail" — that also bends! Answer: We need a place where the animal bends DOWN — the neck slopes downward. The tail bends outward, not downward.

📜 Three Derivations for "From the Neck"

SourceDerivationLogic
Rav KahanaV'shachat → shach (bends)The neck is where the animal bends/slopes
Rav Yeimar / School of R' Yishmael"The pieces, the head, and the fat" (Vayikra 1:8)The head is listed separately from the body → it was already severed during shechitah → slaughter is at the neck
Baraita"This is the law of the animal and of the bird" (Vayikra 11:46)Juxtaposition teaches that animal and bird share the same slaughter location
Why three? Each derivation has a vulnerability. Rav Kahana's could mean the tail. The head-separation argument could be challenged. The juxtaposition could teach other things. Together, they reinforce each other — a principle often seen in the Gemara: multiple proofs strengthen a law.

🐦↔️🐄 The Torah's Juxtaposition

"This is the law of the animal and of the bird" (Vayikra 11:46)
↓
What does the juxtaposition teach? Two directions:
↓
Animal → Bird:
Just as an animal requires shechitah, so does a bird require shechitah (from the neck)
Bird → Animal:
Just as a bird's fitness is from the front of the neck, so is an animal's shechitah from the FRONT of the neck
R' Eliezer's version: Just as a bird is pinched from the NAPE (back of neck), we might think animal shechitah is also from the back. The Gemara rejects this: "opposite the nape" — the front of the neck — is the correct location.

🔪 Why Not Rend or Stab?

Q
If we only know shechitah is from the neck — maybe one could just rend (tear) from the ear downward until the blood spills? Why a back-and-forth cutting motion?
Gm
The answer: the five disqualifying factors (shehiyah, derasah, chaladah, hagramah, ikkur — taught in Chapter 1) define shechitah as a precise, controlled act. Rending or stabbing would violate these rules. Shechitah is not just WHERE you cut but HOW.

🩸 Blood Covering — A Proof for Birds

The Torah requires covering the blood of birds and wild animals after shechitah
↓
Does this prove that bird shechitah is a Torah obligation (not just Rabbinic)?
↓
Yes! If bird shechitah were optional (Rabbinic), why would covering blood apply? The Torah's commandment to cover blood after shechitah implies shechitah itself is required by Torah law.
