🤝 Does Hide Join Flesh for the Shiur of Tumah?

Rav
The hide of a neveilah joins (מצטרפת) with the flesh to constitute a k'zayit. The Torah calls the entire carcass neveilah — hide and flesh are one halachic unit.
Shmuel
The hide does not join with the flesh. Hide is a distinct entity — once separated, it must reach a k'zayit on its own to convey tumah as food.
Rav
The hide was always part of the living animal — it emerged from the same source of life. Death does not sever their halachic connection.
Shmuel
The verse speaks of flesh that imparts tumah. Hide, being structurally separate and commonly used for leather, has its own category — it cannot borrow significance from the flesh.
Nafka Mina (Practical Difference) If neveilah flesh is less than k'zayit — does attaching a piece of untanned hide complete the required measure to convey tumah to what is touched?

👤 Human Skin vs. Animal Hide — Contrasting Laws

עור האדם — Human Skin

  • Susceptible to midras tumah (tumah through treading/sitting)
  • Instituted by Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai as a rabbinic decree
  • Purpose: prevent disrespect — if exempt, skin might be used for goods
  • Does not become tamei as food — humans are not eaten
  • Special category: rabbinic, not biblical

עור הבהמה — Animal Hide

  • Susceptible to tumah as food if k'zayit (when untanned neveilah)
  • Susceptible to midras if used as mat or seat
  • Biblical law governs most categories
  • Tanning removes food-tumah status entirely
  • Debate (Rav/Shmuel) on joining with flesh for shiur

📏 Step-by-Step: Determining if Neveilah Hide Conveys Tumah

Step 1 — Identify species: Is this a land animal? Fish hide is automatically exempt from all categories of tumah.
↓
Step 2 — Check manner of death: Did it die as a neveilah (not properly slaughtered)? If properly slaughtered, hide does not carry food-tumah.
↓
Step 3 — Check for tanning: Has the hide been processed (tanned)? If yes, it has lost its food-tumah status — treat as leather/vessel.
↓
Step 4 — Measure the hide: Does the untanned hide constitute a k'zayit on its own? If yes, it conveys tumah independently.
↓
Step 5 — Apply the Rav/Shmuel debate: If under k'zayit, Rav allows combining with adjacent flesh; Shmuel requires the hide to stand alone.
↓
Step 6 — Ruling: If k'zayit is met (directly or via tziruf per Rav) — the hide conveys tumah and the one who touches it must immerse in a mikveh.

🐟 Why Is Fish Hide Completely Exempt?

עור הדג — טהור, שאין עורו חשוב כעור בהמה וחיה
Gemara's Reasoning Fish hide lacks chashivuta — halachic significance. The Torah's tumah laws for neveilot target land animals whose hides are routinely used for leather, clothing, and vessels. Fish, being structurally distinct creatures of a separate domain, have hides that are neither commonly worked nor valued in the same way. Without that significance, there is no basis for tumah to attach.

Corollary: This also explains why fish do not require shechita — the entire framework of slaughter, neveilah, and its associated tumah simply does not apply to them.
