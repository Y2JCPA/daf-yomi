QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is fish hide exempt from tumah according to the Gemara?
A. Fish are not kosher and therefore have no halachic status
B. Fish hide lacks chashivuta — the halachic significance attributed to land animal hides
C. The Torah explicitly excludes fish in the verse about neveilah
D. Fish hides are always tanned before use, removing tumah status
Correct: B — Fish hide lacks chashivuta (significance) — it is not commonly used or valued like land animal hide, so the tumah framework for neveilot simply does not apply to it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the practical difference (nafka mina) between Rav and Shmuel on hide joining with flesh?
A. Whether tanning removes tumah from a neveilah hide
B. Whether a hide smaller than k'zayit can combine with adjacent flesh to reach the minimum for tumah
C. Whether human skin requires immersion after contact with neveilah
D. Whether fish scales are classified as a type of hide
Correct: B — If flesh is under k'zayit, Rav holds that hide joins to complete the shiur; Shmuel holds hide must independently reach k'zayit. Their debate only matters when neither piece alone is sufficient.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What effect does tanning have on the tumah status of a neveilah hide?
A. It increases stringency — processed leather is a greater tumah source
B. It has no effect — tumah status is set at death and cannot change
C. It removes food-tumah status — the hide becomes leather and exits the food category
D. It only removes tumah if the animal was not a neveilah to begin with
Correct: C — Tanning transforms hide into leather — a vessel category. It thereby removes the food-tumah status that otherwise attaches to untanned neveilah hide.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Who instituted the rabbinic decree making human skin susceptible to midras tumah, and why?
A. Hillel, to extend the biblical laws of purity to all organic matter
B. Rabbi Akiva, based on a novel reading of the tumah verses
C. Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai, to prevent disrespect to the dead
D. Rav Yehuda, following a Babylonian tradition on human remains
Correct: C — Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai decreed that human skin is susceptible to midras tumah. The concern was: if exempt, people might use human skin for leather goods, which would be deeply disrespectful to the deceased.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which combination correctly describes when a neveilah hide conveys tumah as food?
A. Any hide from a neveilah, regardless of processing or size
B. Untanned neveilah hide that constitutes at least a k'zayit (with debate about joining flesh)
C. Tanned neveilah hide larger than a k'zayit used as a sitting surface
D. Any neveilah hide from a non-kosher species slaughtered improperly
Correct: B — Three conditions must be met: (1) the animal must be a neveilah, (2) the hide must be untanned — tanning removes food-tumah, and (3) it must reach the k'zayit minimum, with Rav and Shmuel debating whether it can join with flesh.
