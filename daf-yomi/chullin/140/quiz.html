Chullin 140 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the halakhic ruling on Chullin 140b, if a mother bird is hovering over her nest with wings spread but not physically touching the eggs, what is the law?
A. She is exempt from shiluach haken since she is not 'sitting' (רֹבֶצֶת)
B. Shiluach haken applies — hovering with spread wings is considered 'sitting' per Chachamim
C. One sends her away but recites no blessing
D. The case is left as a teku (unresolved question)
Correct: B — The halakha follows Chachamim over Rabbi Yehuda: a mother bird spreading her wings over the nest is considered רֹבֶצֶת and the full obligation of shiluach haken applies.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why is shiluach haken NOT required when a domestic chicken is found sitting on eggs in a courtyard?
A. Chickens lay unfertilized eggs, which are always exempt
B. Domestic birds are not encountered 'by chance' (דרך מציאה) as the Torah implies
C. The chicken is considered treifa by rabbinic decree
D. The eggs of domestic birds are not considered 'eggs' under Torah law
Correct: B — The Torah says 'if a nest happens before you (כִּי יִקָּרֵא)' — implying a chance encounter with a wild bird. Domestic birds, kept in one's domain, do not meet this criterion.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If a wild dove is found sitting on eggs that belong to a chicken (a different species), what is the ruling regarding shiluach haken?
A. Shiluach applies because a kosher wild bird is present
B. Shiluach does not apply because the mother and eggs must be the same species
C. Shiluach applies but without reciting a blessing
D. One must send away the dove and the eggs are forbidden to take
Correct: B — The mitzvah requires a match between mother and offspring. When the sitting bird is not the species that produced the eggs, the natural mother-child relationship is absent and shiluach haken does not apply.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The Gemara records that one who prays 'just as Your mercy extends to the bird's nest, so extend it to us' is silenced. Why?
A. Because this prayer contains God's name used incorrectly
B. Because the commandments must be treated as divine decrees, not expressions of God's attribute of mercy
C. Because shiluach haken is not a compassion-based commandment at all
D. Because only a Cohen may invoke mercy in the Temple service
Correct: B — The Gemara rejects treating the mitzvot as purely expressions of divine mercy, lest it imply God's qualities are limited. Mitzvot are royal decrees (גזרות) to be fulfilled regardless of our reasoning about them.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the Torah's stated reward for performing the mitzvah of shiluach haken?
A. Wealth and prosperity in this world
B. Atonement for sins committed unintentionally
C. Length of days (אֲרִיכוּת יָמִים) and good fortune
D. The merit to bring a korban in the Beit HaMikdash
Correct: C — Devarim 22:7 states: 'לְמַעַן יִיטַב לָךְ וְהַאֲרַכְתָּ יָמִים' — 'that it may be well with you and you may lengthen your days.' This makes shiluach haken one of only two mitzvot with an explicit reward of long life (the other being honoring one's parents).
