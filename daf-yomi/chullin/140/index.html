🐦 The Core Dispute: Which Eggs Require Shiluach HaKen?

Beit Shammai
Even unfertilized eggs (שאינם מוזרות) obligate שילוח הקן — the mother's presence over them is sufficient.
Beit Hillel
Only fertilized eggs (מוזרות) — eggs capable of producing life — trigger the obligation of שילוח הקן.
Rav Huna
Even spoiled eggs (זקוקין) that will never hatch require the mother to be sent away, since the Torah says 'eggs' without qualification (דְּבָרִים).
Rava
The Torah's intent is for viable potential life. Eggs that cannot produce offspring have no sacred status requiring shiluach — we follow the purpose of the commandment.
📜 Torah Source כִּי יִקָּרֵא קַן צִפּוֹר לְפָנֶיךָ... וְהָאֵם רֹבֶצֶת עַל הָאֶפְרֹחִים אוֹ עַל הַבֵּיצִים — 'If a bird's nest happens before you… and the mother is sitting upon the chicks or upon the eggs' (Devarim 22:6)

🕊️ Hovering vs. Sitting — Does It Count?

Rabbi Yehuda
The Torah says רֹבֶצֶת — 'sitting.' If the mother's wings merely hover (פורשת כנפיה) over the eggs without touching them, she is NOT considered 'sitting' and you are not obligated to send her away.
Chachamim
Hovering with wings spread over the nest IS considered רֹבֶצֶת. A bird in that posture is protecting and incubating — the spirit of the verse applies. You MUST send her away.
Abaye
Support for Rabbi Yehuda: the Torah specified 'sitting' precisely to exclude hovering. The verse's word choice is deliberate — physical contact with the nest is required.
Rava
The halakha follows Chachamim. A bird spreading wings over her nest fulfills the essence of רֹבֶצֶת, and the mitzvah of shiluach applies. The wing-spread is the mother's embrace.
⚖️ Practical Ruling Halakha follows Chachamim: even a hovering mother bird requires shiluach haken before taking her eggs or chicks.

🥚 Types of Eggs and Shiluach HaKen Status

✅ Obligates Shiluach

  • Fertilized eggs (מוזרות) — viable, capable of hatching
  • Eggs with chick embryo visible inside
  • Eggs the mother was incubating (regardless of stage)
  • Live chicks still in the nest under the mother
  • Chicks that cannot yet fly (fledglings)

❌ Does Not Obligate Shiluach

  • Unfertilized eggs (per Beit Hillel's ruling)
  • Completely rotten/spoiled eggs with no life potential
  • Eggs of a non-kosher species
  • Eggs the mother was NOT sitting on (not her nest)
  • Domestic poultry (chickens, geese in the house)

⚠️ Disputed Cases

  • Eggs with blood spots — purity debate affects ruling
  • Mixed batch: some valid, some not
  • Treifa bird's eggs — mother cannot be sent away with blessing
  • Eggs of one species with mother of another species
  • Eggs found alone with no mother present at the time

💡 The Deep Reason Behind Shiluach HaKen

הָאֵם רֹבֶצֶת עַל הָאֶפְרֹחִים אוֹ עַל הַבֵּיצִים — לֹא תִקַּח הָאֵם עַל הַבָּנִים (דברים כב:ו)
🌿 Philosophical Foundation The Rambam (Moreh Nevuchim 3:48) explains: the Torah seeks to instill compassion (מידת הרחמים) — we must not take the mother together with her young, lest we cause her anguish. This parallels the prohibition of slaughtering a cow and her calf on the same day (Vayikra 22:28).

The Ramban disagrees: the commandment has no reason we can fully grasp — it is a chok, a divine decree, and performing it earns merit regardless of any rationale.

The Gemara here notes: one who says 'just as Your mercy extends to the bird's nest, so extend Your mercy to us' — is silenced, because the commandments must not be treated as expressions of God's mercy alone, but as royal decrees we accept unconditionally.
