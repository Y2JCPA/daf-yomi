🐍 Exposed Liquids — The Venom Rule

The rule: Three liquids are forbidden if left uncovered: water, wine, and milk. A snake may have deposited venom in them. How long must they remain exposed? Long enough for a snake to emerge from nearby, drink, and return to its hiding place.

Left Uncovered, Found Exposed

  • 🐍 Assume a snake may have drunk from it
  • ❌ Forbidden — even in doubt
  • ⚠️ Danger overrides all leniency rules

Left Covered, Found Covered

  • ✅ Permitted — the cover prevented access
  • 📏 Even if theoretically a snake could have entered
  • 🔒 The covering creates a chazakah of safety

🛁 The Immersion Analogy

Rv
Rava's proof for Rav Huna: If someone immerses in a mikveh, then finds an interposition (chatzitzah) — the immersion is invalid! Same logic: notch found = slaughter invalid.
Gm
But that's different! The impure person had a chazakah of impurity, and we "establish him on his chazakah." Here, the knife had no prior chazakah of being notched.
Rv
True — but the animal had a chazakah of prohibition. The animal is what matters, not the knife!
Gm
Counterpoint: The slaughter itself happened — the "flaw" in the knife is external. We can't compare a failed immersion to a completed slaughter.

Rv = Rava · Gm = Gemara's response

🏠 The Leprous House — Closing the Door

1️⃣

Priest Enters

A Kohen enters a house to inspect a leprous mark on the wall

2️⃣

Priest Exits

"The priest shall go out of the house to the door" — he exits and stands at the doorway

3️⃣

Locks the Door

He locks the house for 7 days of quarantine — everything inside becomes tamei

The chazakah question: When the priest exits and turns away, the leprous mark is behind him — out of sight. We rely on the chazakah that the mark is still there (as it was when he saw it). This proves chazakah works to maintain a status even without ongoing visual confirmation.
Rav Acha bar Yaakov challenges: Maybe the priest exits backward, keeping his eyes on the mark! Response: "Exiting backward is not called exiting" — and the verse specifically says he exits to the door and then locks it.

📖 The Source Verse

וְיָצָא הַכֹּהֵן מִן הַבַּיִת אֶל פֶּתַח הַבָּיִת וְהִסְגִּיר אֶת הַבַּיִת שִׁבְעַת יָמִים

"And the priest shall go out of the house to the door of the house, and lock the house for seven days" (Vayikra 14:38)

R' Shmuel bar Nachmani in R' Yonatan's name: The verse teaches that between exiting and locking — the priest turns away from the mark. Yet we declare everything in the house tamei. This proves we rely on chazakah: the mark's status is presumed unchanged from when the priest last saw it.
Application to shechitah: Just as we rely on chazakah for the leprous mark (it's still there even when unseen), so we apply chazakah to the knife and the animal's status during slaughter.
