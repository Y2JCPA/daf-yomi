Chullin 66 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Akiva, when must a locust's wings cover most of its body to render it kosher?
A. At the time of purchase in the market
B. They must currently cover most of the body
C. They will eventually cover most of the body as the locust matures
D. At least one wing must cover half the body at all times
Correct: C — Rabbi Akiva rules leniently, permitting immature locusts whose wings will eventually cover most of the body — even if they do not yet do so at the time of inspection.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
The Gemara teaches that any fish with scales also has fins. What is the halachic consequence?
A. Fins are irrelevant and never need to be checked
B. Checking for scales alone is sufficient to confirm both required kosher signs
C. Both fins and scales must always be independently verified
D. A fish with scales but no fins is still kosher
Correct: B — Since scales logically guarantee fins, verifying the presence of scales is a complete test. There is no fish with scales that lacks fins, so scales serve as the single reliable indicator of kosher status.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
The Shibuta fish has scales in the sea but loses them upon removal. What is its kosher status?
A. Forbidden, because no scales are visible at market
B. Permitted, because it possessed scales at a natural stage of its life
C. Forbidden, because scales must be present when the fish is eaten
D. Debated with no final ruling
Correct: B — The Gemara rules the Shibuta is permitted. The Torah's requirement of scales refers to the nature of the species — if the species naturally has scales at any life stage or habitat, the fish is kosher even when scales are no longer present.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which of the following is NOT among the four required signs of a kosher locust?
A. Four walking legs (אַרְבַּע רַגְלַיִם)
B. Two jumping legs (כְּרָעַיִם)
C. Four wings (אַרְבַּע כְּנָפַיִם)
D. A striped or patterned body
Correct: D — The four signs are: four walking legs, two jumping hind legs (כְּרָעַיִם), four wings, and wings that cover most of the body. Body coloration or pattern is not among the required signs.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
A fish has fins but no scales — what is its status, and why?
A. Kosher, because fins alone are sufficient
B. Kosher, because we check fins first by Torah law
C. Forbidden, because scales are required and their absence means fins do not suffice
D. Kosher only if the fish is known to shed its scales
Correct: C — Fins without scales do not confer kosher status. The rule 'scales imply fins' does not work in reverse — there are fish with fins but no scales (like eels), and these are explicitly forbidden. Both signs must be present (or have been present) in the species.
