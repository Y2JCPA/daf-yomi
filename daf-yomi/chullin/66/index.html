⚖️ Dispute: Must Wings Cover the Body Now or Eventually?

R' Eliezer
A locust is kosher only if its wings currently cover most of its body — along with four legs, four wings, and two jumping legs (כְּרָעַיִם).
R' Akiva
A locust is kosher if its wings will eventually cover most of its body as it matures — permitting young locusts whose wings are not yet fully grown.
Practical Significance Rabbi Akiva's lenient ruling permits immature locusts that have not yet developed full wing coverage. Rabbi Eliezer requires the wing coverage to be present and verifiable at the moment of inspection. Both agree all four signs must ultimately be present.

🦗 The Four Signs of a Kosher Locust

Signs 1–2: Legs

  • אַרְבַּע רַגְלַיִם — four walking legs
  • Standard locomotion legs
  • Shared broadly among insects
  • Must number exactly four

Sign 3: Jumping Legs

  • כְּרָעַיִם — two elongated hind legs
  • Built for leaping (not walking)
  • Key distinguishing marker
  • Absent in flies and beetles

Sign 4: Wings

  • אַרְבַּע כְּנָפַיִם — four wings
  • Must cover most of the body
  • Dispute: now (Eliezer) vs. eventually (Akiva)
  • Both pairs must be present

🐟 How to Verify a Kosher Fish

Step 1 — Check for Scales (קַשְׂקֶשֶׂת): Scales that can be peeled off by hand or with a tool without tearing the skin.
↓
Step 2 — Check for Fins (סְנַפִּיר): Any fin projecting from the body. In practice, scales alone suffice — see the key rule below.
↓
Key Rule: כָּל שֶׁיֶּשׁ לוֹ קַשְׂקֶשֶׂת יֶשׁ לוֹ סְנַפִּיר — every fish with scales also has fins. Scales are the reliable single indicator.
↓
Step 3 — Verify Lifetime Possession: If the fish had scales at any point in its life (e.g., in the sea), it remains kosher even if those scales are now absent.
↓
Conclusion: Inspect for scales. Their presence (past or present) confirms kosher status for the entire species.

🌊 The Shibuta: Scales Lost After Leaving Water

Question
The Shibuta fish has scales while swimming in the sea, but loses them upon being caught and removed from water. When you purchase it at market, no scales are visible. Is it kosher?
Gemara's Answer
It is permitted. The Torah's requirement of scales speaks to the nature of the species, not the current state of each individual fish. Having had scales at any stage of its natural life suffices.
Ruling A fish species that naturally possesses scales at some stage of its life — even if those scales are shed in a later stage or upon removal from its habitat — retains full kosher status. Knowledge of the species matters, not just the specimen's current appearance.

💡 Scales Guarantee Fins

כָּל שֶׁיֶּשׁ לוֹ קַשְׂקֶשֶׂת יֶשׁ לוֹ סְנַפִּיר, אֲבָל יֵשׁ שֶׁיֶּשׁ לוֹ סְנַפִּיר וְאֵין לוֹ קַשְׂקֶשֶׂת — חֻלִּין סו
Talmudic Principle — Chullin 66b The Gemara records a biological-legal axiom: כָּל שֶׁיֶּשׁ לוֹ קַשְׂקֶשֶׂת יֶשׁ לוֹ סְנַפִּיר — any fish that has scales necessarily has fins. The converse is not true: there are fish with fins but no scales (e.g., the eel), and those are forbidden. Therefore, checking for scales alone is a complete and sufficient test of a fish's kosher status, since scales logically entail the presence of fins.
