🐄 Ben Pekuah: Status & Slaughter Debate

Tanna Kamma
A ben pekuah does NOT require its own shechita. It is rendered permitted through the mother's slaughter — the fetus is like the thigh of the mother (ubar yerech imo).
Rabbi Meir
The ben pekuah DOES require its own shechita once it is eaten — it emerged as a living, breathing independent creature after birth.
Rabbi Yochanan
Even the offspring of a ben pekuah are likewise exempt from shechita, for the exemption passes through generations.
Reish Lakish
Once a ben pekuah grows to adulthood and breeds, we must reconsider — its offspring may be entirely standard animals requiring shechita.
Core Principle עוּבָּר יֶרֶךְ אִמּוֹ — The fetus is legally treated as the thigh of its mother; its permissibility is acquired through the mother's slaughter

בֶּן פְּקוּעָה — הַנִּמְצָא חַי בִּמְעֵי שְׁחוּטָה, וְאֵינוֹ צָרִיךְ שְׁחִיטָה לְעַצְמוֹ
📖 What Is Ben Pekuah? A ben pekuah (בֶּן פְּקוּעָה) is a living fetus found inside an animal's womb after the mother has been properly slaughtered. The word pekuah derives from splitting open. Because it was never independently born, it never had an independent prohibition — its heter (permissibility) was subsumed in the mother's slaughter. Chullin 71 explores the reach of this exemption: Does it extend to offspring of a ben pekuah? Does it apply after the animal matures? And what tumah does a dead fetus transmit?

⚖️ Does Ben Pekuah Need Shechita?

Living fetus found inside a properly slaughtered mother
↓
Tanna Kamma
No shechita needed — mother's slaughter covers it via ubar yerech imo
Rabbi Meir
Shechita required — it is now an independent living being
↓
Halacha: Ben pekuah is permitted WITHOUT its own shechita
Majority opinion prevails; principle of ubar yerech imo applied

🔗 Ubar Yerech Imo — When It Helps vs. Hurts

Leniency (Kula)

  • Ben pekuah permitted via mother's shechita
  • No independent obligation of kisuy hadam (covering blood)
  • Offspring of ben pekuah may also be exempt
  • No firstborn (bechor) sanctity attaches independently

Stringency (Chumra)

  • Fetus inside a nevela is itself nevela — forbidden
  • Dead fetus transmits tumah through the mother's body
  • Fetus of a treifah animal is itself treifah
  • Cannot redeem fetus separately from mother's status

🌀 Tumah of a Dead Fetus — How It Spreads

Step 1: Fetus dies in utero — becomes a source of tumas meis (corpse impurity)
↓
Step 2: The mother's womb/body acts as an ohel (enclosure) — the tumah is sealed within
↓
Step 3: Items and people outside the mother remain tahor — tumah has not yet exited the ohel
↓
Step 4: Once any limb of the fetus begins to exit, tumah breaks out — whoever touches the mother or exiting fetus becomes tamei
↓
Step 5 (Dispute): If only the fetus's hand exits first — does tumah exit with it? The Gemara debates whether a yad (limb) carries the tumah outward
