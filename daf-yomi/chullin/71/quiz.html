QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is a 'ben pekuah'?
A. A firstborn animal consecrated to the Temple
B. A living fetus found inside an animal after proper slaughter
C. An animal slaughtered on the same day as its offspring
D. An animal born prematurely in the field
Correct: B — Ben pekuah (בֶּן פְּקוּעָה) refers to a live fetus found inside an animal whose mother was properly slaughtered. 'Pekuah' relates to 'splitting open' the carcass.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does 'ubar yerech imo' literally mean, and what is its legal consequence?
A. The fetus is older than its mother — it must be slaughtered first
B. The fetus is like the thigh of its mother — it is permitted through her slaughter
C. The fetus must be returned to the earth — it cannot be eaten
D. The fetus is independent — it requires its own ritual slaughter
Correct: B — Ubar yerech imo (עוּבָּר יֶרֶךְ אִמּוֹ) means the fetus is treated legally as a limb of the mother. Therefore, the mother's shechita renders the fetus permitted, just as it permits any other part of her body.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Gemara, what role does the mother's body play when a fetus dies in utero?
A. It transmits impurity to all those in the same room immediately
B. It acts as an ohel, containing the tumah within until the fetus begins to exit
C. It becomes tahor itself, since the fetus never lived
D. It requires burial along with the fetus immediately
Correct: B — The mother's body functions as an ohel (enclosure/tent), trapping the tumas meis inside. Tumah does not escape to the outside world until part of the fetus crosses the threshold and exits.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the status of a fetus found inside an animal that was a nevela (improperly slaughtered)?
A. The fetus is permitted — it had no part in the mother's improper slaughter
B. The fetus is also considered nevela and is forbidden
C. The fetus needs only a light salting (melicha) to become permitted
D. The fetus is permitted if it was fully formed at the time of death
Correct: B — Since ubar yerech imo means the fetus is part of the mother, it inherits the mother's forbidden status. Just as the ben pekuah of a properly slaughtered animal is permitted, the fetus of a nevela is itself nevela.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rabbi Meir disagrees with the Tanna Kamma regarding ben pekuah. What is his position?
A. Ben pekuah is forbidden entirely and may never be eaten
B. Ben pekuah requires its own shechita before it may be eaten
C. Ben pekuah must be buried but not eaten
D. Ben pekuah's offspring require shechita but not the animal itself
Correct: B — Rabbi Meir holds that since the ben pekuah is now a living, independent creature, it cannot be eaten without its own shechita. The halacha, however, follows the Tanna Kamma who does not require shechita.
