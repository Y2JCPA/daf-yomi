⚖️ Abaye vs. Rava: Sircha to the Chest Wall

Abaye
If a sircha runs from the lung to the chest wall, we remove it and blow air into the lung. If the lung holds air — the membrane was never perforated — kasher!
Rava
The sircha itself is evidence that the lung was punctured at that spot. A wound that formed the adhesion was a real perforation — treifa regardless of the current air test.
Abaye
But wounds can heal! If the membrane subsequently sealed itself, the animal is kasher now. We judge its present state, not its past condition.
Rava
Lung perforations do not heal. Unlike skin, the lung membrane cannot regenerate. The sircha is merely covering an existing hole — the animal was and remains treifa.
Halacha The halacha follows Rava: a sircha connecting the lung to the chest wall raises a serious treifa concern; the blowing test is performed but a permanent defect cannot be cured by a membrane cover.

💧 Bua D'Maya — The Fluid Blister

נִקְּבָה הָרֵאָה — אִם נִסְתַּם הַנֶּקֶב בְּקְרוּם, כְּשֵׁרָה; וְאִם לָאו, טְרֵפָה
Key Case The Gemara discusses a bua d'maya (בועא דמיא) — a water-filled blister appearing on a lobe of the lung. The question: does the outer membrane of the blister count as sealing an underlying perforation?

If the blister's membrane is intact → it seals the defect → Kasher
If the blister's membrane is perforated → nothing seals the hole → Treifa

This case illustrates the broader principle: nikuv she'nitatem b'krum — a perforation covered by a membrane may be kasher if the membrane is whole.

🔬 Kasher Sircha vs. Treifa Sircha

Kasher Sircha

  • Between two lobes of the same lung (internal)
  • Thin, filament-like strand that rubs away
  • Lung holds air fully when inflated
  • No underlying hole found when sircha removed
  • Rav Pappa: dissolves easily = mere inflammation, not perforation

Treifa Sircha

  • Runs from lung to chest wall, diaphragm, or rib
  • Thick, cord-like, cannot be separated cleanly
  • Air escapes at adhesion site when lung is blown
  • Hole visible beneath where sircha was attached
  • Rava: lung membrane never heals — this is a permanent nikuv

💨 The Nifrach (Blowing) Test — Step by Step

Step 1 — Shechita Complete: After valid ritual slaughter, the lungs are carefully removed from the chest cavity without tearing.
↓
Step 2 — Visual Inspection: Examine all five lobes for discoloration, adhesions (sirchin), blisters, or visible perforations.
↓
Step 3 — Remove the Sircha: If an adhesion is found connecting lung to chest wall, carefully detach it and note the site.
↓
Step 4 — Inflate the Lung: Seal the trachea and blow air in (or submerge in water and blow) — watch for bubbles at the former adhesion site.
↓
Step 5 — Rule: Bubbles or escaping air → perforation confirmed → TREIFA. Lung holds air completely → membrane intact → KASHER.
