QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, why does a sircha connecting the lung to the chest wall render the animal treifa even if the lung currently holds air during the blowing test?
A. Because the chest wall is an inherently forbidden organ that contaminates anything touching it
B. Because Rava rules strictly in all doubtful cases as a precautionary measure
C. Because lung perforations do not heal — the sircha proves an existing permanent nikuv
D. Because the blowing test is only valid for internal lobe-to-lobe adhesions
Correct: C — Rava's reasoning is that the lung membrane, unlike skin or other tissues, cannot regenerate. Therefore the sircha is not covering a healed wound but an active perforation — the animal is treifa.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is a 'bua d'maya' (בועא דמיא) and why does it matter for the kashrut of an animal?
A. A water-filled blister on the lung lobe whose membrane status determines whether a perforation is sealed or exposed
B. A type of parasitic cyst in the liver that always renders an animal treifa
C. A frothy accumulation in the trachea indicating a lung infection
D. A water bubble in the intestine that can indicate a perforation of the digestive tract
Correct: A — Bua d'maya is a fluid-filled blister on the lung. If its outer membrane is intact, it seals the underlying defect and the animal is kasher. If the membrane is torn, the defect is exposed and the animal is treifa.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which type of sircha is generally considered kasher without requiring further testing?
A. A sircha running from the lung lobe to the diaphragm
B. A sircha connecting the lung to the inner rib cage
C. A thick cord-like adhesion on the outer surface of the lung
D. A thin adhesion between two lobes of the same lung (internal)
Correct: D — An internal adhesion between lobes within the same lung does not indicate an external perforation of the lung wall and is generally ruled kasher by the Gemara.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the nifrach (blowing) test, what result indicates that the animal is KASHER?
A. Bubbles appear at the former sircha site when the lung is submerged in water
B. The lung expands asymmetrically, suggesting only one side is affected
C. The lung holds air completely with no leakage at any point
D. The trachea collapses when air is blown in, sealing the lung
Correct: C — When the sealed lung is inflated and holds air without any leakage — especially at the former adhesion site — this demonstrates the membrane is intact and the animal is kasher.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Abaye and Rava disagree about sircha cases. What is the core issue dividing them?
A. Whether sirchin that appear during the winter months are treated more strictly than summer ones
B. Whether the lung or the liver is more susceptible to treifa conditions
C. Whether lung membranes can heal after a perforation, making a current clean test sufficient
D. Whether the blowing test was instituted by Biblical or Rabbinic authority
Correct: C — Abaye holds that wounds can heal — if the lung passes the air test, it is kasher now regardless of past damage. Rava disagrees: lung tissue cannot heal, so a sircha to the chest wall proves a permanent, unremedied perforation.
