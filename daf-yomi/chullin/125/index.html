🥚 When Does Food Become Susceptible to Impurity?

Can this food become tameh (ritually impure)?
↓
Prerequisite: Hechsher
Food must first contact one of the seven liquids (water, oil, wine, blood, milk, dew, honey)
Prerequisite: Minimum Shiur
Food must reach k'beitza (egg-size) — below this amount it cannot receive impurity
↓
Both conditions met? Food is susceptible — contact with a source of impurity renders it tameh

📜 The K'beitza Principle The minimum measure for food (ochlin) to receive or transmit ritual impurity is a k'beitza — the volume of a whole egg. This is derived from the egg itself as the paradigm of a complete food unit. Smaller amounts, even if food, are not halachically significant for tumah purposes.

🗣️ Do Yolk and White Combine for the K'beitza?

Tanna Kamma
The yolk and the white of an egg combine with one another to constitute the k'beitza measure. They are both part of the same egg-food and thus join together.
R' Yehuda
They do not combine. The yolk and white are distinct food substances — each must independently reach the shiur. Partial amounts of distinct types do not join.
Stakes of the Dispute If an egg's yolk alone is less than k'beitza and the white alone is less — can they together render the food susceptible to impurity? R' Yehuda says no; Tanna Kamma says yes.

⚖️ Ever Min HaChai vs. Ever Min HaMet

Ever Min HaChai

  • Limb severed from a living animal or person
  • Biblically forbidden (one of the 7 Noahide laws)
  • Conveys tumah similar to neveilah
  • No minimum flesh required if bone is present
  • Applies even if the animal later recovers

Ever Min HaMet

  • Limb severed from a dead animal or human
  • Human limb conveys tum'at ohel (tent impurity)
  • Requires k'zayit of flesh or k'se'orah of bone
  • Animal limb follows laws of neveilah
  • Stricter shiurim apply before tumah is conveyed

🔄 The Four Steps to Food Impurity

Step 1 — Detachment: Food must be detached from the ground. Produce still attached (m'chubor l'karka) cannot become tameh.
↓
Step 2 — Hechsher: Food must have contact with one of the seven liquids (מַשְׁקֶה), rendering it 'prepared' to receive impurity. The owner must have been pleased by this wetting.
↓
Step 3 — Minimum Shiur: Food must be at least k'beitza. Partial amounts that may combine (tziruf) are subject to dispute between Tanna Kamma and R' Yehuda.
↓
Step 4 — Contact with Tumah: Food must be touched by an av ha-tumah or a rishon l'tumah. The resulting grade of impurity depends on the source.
↓
Result: The food becomes a rishon or sheni l'tumah and may defile other foods it contacts — but only if all four conditions are met.
