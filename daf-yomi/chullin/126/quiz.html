QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the minimum measure of neveilah flesh required to convey biblical tum'ah?
A. A k'beitza (egg-size)
B. A k'zayit (olive-size)
C. Any detectable amount
D. A revi'it (quarter-log)
Correct: B — The Torah requires a k'zayit (olive-size bulk) of neveilah flesh to convey ritual impurity through contact or carrying. This is the standard biblical measure.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What exactly is the dispute between Rav and Shmuel regarding ohr habakar (the hide of a domesticated animal)?
A. Whether eating the hide is forbidden
B. Whether the hide conveys tum'ah entirely on its own
C. Whether the hide joins with flesh to complete the k'zayit for neveilah tum'ah
D. Whether the hide requires burial like a neveilah carcass
Correct: C — Rav holds the hide joins with the flesh to complete the k'zayit measure for tum'ah. Shmuel holds it does not join — only actual flesh counts toward the required measure.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the halakhic status of a 'chatzi shiur' — less than a k'zayit — of neveilah?
A. Completely permitted with no restriction
B. Forbidden rabbinically; does not convey biblical tum'ah
C. Same as a full k'zayit in all respects
D. Only prohibited if consumed, not if touched
Correct: C — A chatzi shiur does not convey biblical tum'ah, but the Rabbis prohibited handling it. Many authorities also hold that eating chatzi shiur is itself a biblical prohibition.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which pieces of neveilah may combine (tziruf) to form a k'zayit for tum'ah purposes?
A. Pieces from any two different animals, if touching
B. Pieces from the same animal that are touching one another
C. Any pieces found in the same house
D. Only pieces in the same sealed vessel
Correct: B — Only pieces from the same animal that are in direct contact (nogen) may combine to form a k'zayit. Pieces from two different neveilot cannot combine with each other.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Shmuel, which parts of a neveilah do NOT convey tum'ah on their own?
A. Internal organs
B. A full k'zayit of flesh
C. The hide, bones, and sinews when separated from the flesh
D. Blood absorbed into the flesh
Correct: C — Shmuel holds that the hide does not join with flesh and does not convey tum'ah alone. Similarly, bones and sinews alone lack the flesh component required for neveilah tum'ah.
