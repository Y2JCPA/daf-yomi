🏹 Arrow Shechitah — Shooting a Flying Bird

The scenario: R' Yona bar Tachlifa would shoot arrows at birds in flight, and the arrow would slit the bird's throat — cutting the simanim while the bird was airborne!
How do we know the arrow cut from the FRONT of the neck (as shechitah requires)?
↓
Proof: The feathers at the front of the neck were unraveled by the arrow — a clear indication the cut was from the proper direction
↓
But what about covering the blood? It falls mid-air!
↓
Solution: R' Yona would designate the earth of the entire valley beforehand. That earth serves as the lower covering, and the bird lands on it.

🔪 Decapitation During Slaughter — Knife Length

Mishna: If one was slaughtering normally and accidentally decapitated the animal in one motion — if the knife is long enough (extends beyond the neck), the shechitah is valid.
RZ
R' Zeira's dilemma: Does the knife need to extend beyond the neck by the full width of the neck PLUS additional space? Or just beyond the neck itself?
Gm
The Gemara works through the precise measurement: the knife must be long enough that the entire slaughter could have been completed BEFORE the decapitation occurred. The length proves the cut happened before the head came off.

🌊 Immersion in Waves

Valid Immersion

  • 🌊 In a wave that has DETACHED from the sea
  • 💧 The water has landed — it's on the ground
  • 📜 Contains 40 se'ah of water
  • ✅ This is like a natural mikvah

Invalid Immersion

  • 🌊 In the ARCS of waves (water still in the air)
  • 💨 Water is mid-arc — not yet settled
  • 🚫 Not considered "gathered water"
  • ❌ Cannot immerse in airborne water
Why does this appear in Chullin? The Gemara digresses to discuss immersion in the context of purity laws that relate to handling slaughtered meat. The principle: water must be "gathered" (settled on the ground) to serve as a valid mikvah — even if it came from the sea.

💧 Intent for Mikvah — Person vs. Vessel

A person who sits and waits for a wave to detach and land — does this show "intent" for purification?
↓
Vessels require intent for purification (the owner must intend to immerse them)
↓
Does a person also require specific intent to be purified?
↓
Answer: Just as a person requires intent for purification, so too vessels require intent. The wave scenario shows that passive waiting (without active immersion) raises questions about whether sufficient intent exists.

📏 How Long Must the Knife Be?

ScenarioKnife Length Requirement
Normal shechitahLong enough to draw back and forth across the simanim
Shechitah + accidental decapitationMust extend beyond the neck — proving shechitah was complete BEFORE decapitation
Arrow shechitahArrow's cutting edge must be sharp enough to slit (not tear) the simanim
The logic: If the knife is too short and the head comes off, we can't prove the simanim were cut through shechitah (smooth drawing) rather than through the force of decapitation (pressing/tearing). A sufficiently long knife proves the simanim were properly cut before the head separated.
