🫁 The 18 Treifot — The Master List

The Mishna opens a brand new chapter with the most comprehensive list of treifot (fatal conditions) in the Torah. An animal with any of these conditions cannot survive and is forbidden to eat.
CategoryCondition
GulletPerforated (hole through the wall)
WindpipeCut (majority of its width)
Brain membranePerforated
HeartPerforated to its chamber
SpineSevered with most of spinal cord cut
LiverRemoved — nothing remained
LungPerforated or missing (R' Shimon: through to bronchi)
Abomasum / Gallbladder / Small intestinesPerforated

📖 Biblical Source for Treifah

Q
Where is the allusion to treifah in the Torah? The verse "you shall not eat any flesh that is torn of animals (treifah) in the field" (Exodus 22:30) is explicit — why does Reish Lakish ask for an "allusion"?
RbL
The Exodus verse only covers animals actually mauled in the field. Where is the allusion to the broad category of treifot — internal conditions, perforations, etc.? For that, I need a different verse.
Leviticus 11:2: "These are the living things which you may eat"
↓
✅ "Eat a living thing" — you may eat only what can LIVE. An animal that cannot survive is excluded.
↓
This is the source for all treifot: the animal must be capable of living — if a wound makes survival impossible, it is forbidden.

🌱 Can a Treifah Survive? The Big Dispute

The fundamental question: Is a treifah forbidden because it cannot survive? Or is it forbidden by category (as defined by Sinai), regardless of whether it actually dies?

View 1: Treifah Cannot Live

  • 📖 Derived from "eat living things"
  • 🐄 A treifah will die — that's why it's forbidden
  • ✅ If an animal somehow survives despite a treifah wound, it would arguably become permitted
  • ⚠️ The prohibition is consequence-based

View 2: Treifah Can Live

  • 📜 The category is defined by Sinai — not by survival
  • 🐄 Even if the animal survives, it remains forbidden
  • ✅ Source: the word "these" in Leviticus 11:2 — only THESE specific living things may be eaten
  • ⚠️ The prohibition is category-based

🔢 Counting to 18: Why the Number Matters

The Gemara asks: "But aren't there more?" — The Mishna and various baraitot list many conditions. How do we arrive at exactly 18 treifot?
A mnemonic is given: Beit, Samekh, Gimmel, Reish — plus 7 additional halakhot
↓
Some conditions seem to be separate but are actually sub-cases of the same category

Example: Severed hind legs and removed hide — are these separate treifot or extensions of existing categories?
