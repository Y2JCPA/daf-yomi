Chullin 59 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Travelers once camped on what they thought was a hill. What did they actually discover?
A. A giant turtle that had been sleeping for decades
B. A re'em calf so enormous it resembled a mountain — it rose and walked away by dawn
C. The behemoth described in the book of Job
D. An island formed by whale-sized fish
Correct: B — The gemara uses this story to illustrate the re'em's legendary size. Despite being just a calf, it was large enough that travelers mistook it for a geographical feature
QUESTION 2 OF 5
The shibuta fish tastes remarkably like pork. What is its kosher status and why?
A. Forbidden — if it resembles a forbidden food in taste, it takes on that status
B. Questionable — a rabbi must be consulted each time
C. Fully kosher — it has fins and scales, and signs are the sole determining factor
D. Permitted only after the fact (bediavad) if accidentally eaten
Correct: C — The shibuta is the gemara's prime example that simanim (signs) are absolute. Taste, appearance, and resemblance to forbidden foods are irrelevant — fins and scales make it unambiguously kosher
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which statement best describes the rule about fish scales and fins?
A. A fish with fins but no scales is kosher
B. Scales alone are sufficient — fins are a rabbinic addition
C. Any fish with scales necessarily also has fins, so scales are a reliable practical indicator
D. Both signs must be examined at the time of eating, not at capture
Correct: C — The gemara establishes that kol she-yesh lo kaskeset yesh lo snapir — every fish with scales also has fins. While both are technically required, scales alone are a complete practical indicator
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How many distinct physical signs must a locust possess to be considered kosher?
A. Two — four wings and wing covers
B. Three — legs, wings, and jumping appendages
C. Four — four walking legs, two jumping legs (kra'ayim), four wings, and wings covering most of the body
D. One — community tradition (masorah) is the only requirement
Correct: C — The Torah requires four signs for a kosher locust: four walking legs, two jointed jumping legs above the feet, four wings, and wings that cover the majority of the body
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the unique halachic status of the 'koi' and why?
A. It is fully kosher — rabbis determined it has all required signs
B. It is forbidden — as a hybrid it cannot be kosher
C. It is a beryah bifnei atzma (creature unto itself) — its classification as domestic or wild animal is genuinely unresolved
D. It is treated identically to a domestic animal for all purposes
Correct: C — The koi is one of the Talmud's most famous uncertain cases. The Sages could not definitively determine whether it is a domesticated animal, wild animal, or neither — so it carries unique stringencies from all categories
