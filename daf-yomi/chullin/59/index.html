🦏 The Re'em: How Large Was This Creature?

Gemara
A story is told: travelers encountered what appeared to be a hill. They camped on it, cooked food, and rested through the night — only to discover at dawn that it was a re'em calf that rose and walked away!
Rav Yehuda
The re'em could not fit on Noah's ark — its sheer size made it impossible. It was tied alongside and swam through the flood waters
Rabba bar bar Chana
I once saw a re'em the moment it was born — it was the size of Mount Tabor. Its neck alone stretched the length of a three-day journey
Abaye
These descriptions follow the pattern of aggadic hyperbole — conveying the awesome power of God's creation rather than literal measurement
Key Point Despite its legendary size, the re'em has split hooves and chews cud — making it kosher by Torah law. Signs determine status, not size or rarity

🦗 The Four Signs of a Kosher Locust

Signs 1 & 2: Legs

  • 🦿 Arba raglayim — Four walking legs
  • 🦵 Kra'ayim — Two jointed jumping legs above the feet (totaling six appendages)
  • The jumping legs distinguish locusts from other insects with similar wings

Signs 3 & 4: Wings

  • 🪶 Arba kenafayim — Four wings
  • 🛡️ Kanfav chofin et rubo — Wings cover the majority of the body
  • Wing covers (elytra) are the distinguishing protective outer layer

Practical Ruling

  • All four signs → Kosher even if species is unknown
  • R. Eliezer b. R. Tzadok: tradition (masorah) relied upon in his town
  • Dispute: do we need both signs AND masorah, or is masorah alone sufficient?
  • Most Rishonim require both signs and community tradition

⚖️ The Shibuta Principle: Signs Trump Taste

כָּל שֶׁיֶּשׁ לוֹ קַשְׂקֶשֶׂת יֵשׁ לוֹ סְנַפִּיר — Chullin 59a | אֵין לְךָ דָּג שֶׁיֵּשׁ לוֹ קַשְׂקֶשֶׂת שֶׁאֵין לוֹ סְנַפִּיר
Core Teaching The gemara highlights the shibuta fish as proof that simanim are objective and absolute. This fish reportedly tastes exactly like pork (chazir) — the paradigmatic forbidden food. Yet because it possesses fins and scales, it is completely and unambiguously kosher. This principle cuts in both directions: no matter how "kosher" something looks or tastes, without the required signs it is forbidden. And no matter how "non-kosher" something seems, with the proper signs it is permitted. The Torah's wisdom is in creating a system that cannot be gamed by appearance or palatability.
