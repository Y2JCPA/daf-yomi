⏸️ Shehiyah — How Long a Pause Invalidates?

MISHNA: If the knife fell mid-slaughter and the slaughterer lifted it and completed the shechitah, or if his garments fell and he paused to lift them — if the interval was as long as it takes to slaughter another animal — the shechitah is INVALID (shehiyah).
Rv
Rav: The interval is measured against slaughtering another animal of the SAME TYPE. Bird paused? Measured against time to slaughter another bird. Animal paused? Against another animal.
SH
Shmuel: Always measured against a large animal (the longest type) — this is the maximum permitted interval for any shechitah.

🐄 Red Heifer + Gourd Cut Together

Someone slaughters a Red Heifer and simultaneously cuts a gourd in the same action
↓
Rava: The Red Heifer is DISQUALIFIED. The Torah requires the Red Heifer's slaughter to be performed exclusively — no other work may be done simultaneously.
↓
What if another ANIMAL was slaughtered alongside the Red Heifer?
↓
This depends on R' Natan's view: does slaughtering a non-sacred animal simultaneously count as "other work"? Debate among the Sages.
Why is a gourd worse? Everyone agrees cutting a gourd is "other work" that disqualifies the Red Heifer. But slaughtering another animal might qualify as "the same type of activity" (both are shechitah), which is less clearly a disqualification.

You're mid-shechitah and the knife falls from your hand
↓
You pick it up and resume cutting
↓
Was the interval too long?
↓
Measure: could you have slaughtered a whole other animal in the time it took to resume?

Shorter → Valid

  • Shechitah continues

Longer → Invalid

  • Shehiyah occurred

💨 Cut Gullet Then Severed Windpipe (or Vice Versa)

The case: One cut the gullet properly (shechitah), then severed the windpipe NOT through shechitah (just ripped or broke it). Or vice versa.
Rv
Rava: If the gullet was properly cut first, and then the windpipe was severed (not through shechitah) — this is a problem. But which problem? Treifah (the windpipe was already damaged before the second siman was properly cut)?
RA
Rav Acha bar Huna: Objects — perhaps the windpipe was already severed when the gullet was first cut, making the animal a treifah from the start. The ORDER matters!
Rava resolves: The cases are different. If one "cut then severed" — shechitah happened first, then damage. If one "severed then cut" — damage came first, possibly making it treifah before shechitah could help.

📊 Shehiyah Measurement Summary

OpinionInterval Measured AgainstPractical Difference
RavSame type of animalBird interval is shorter than animal interval
ShmuelAlways a large animalOne universal maximum for all types
Practical impact: Under Rav's view, a brief pause during bird shechitah might be shehiyah (since birds are faster to slaughter), while the same pause during animal shechitah would be fine. Under Shmuel, that same pause is fine for both — since the measuring stick is always the longer animal timeline.

🗺️ Daf 32 Summary Map

⏸️ Shehiyah: interval = time to slaughter another animal. Rav: same type. Shmuel: always large animal.
↓
🐄 Red Heifer + gourd = disqualified. + another animal = debate.
↓
🔪 Knife fell: measure the interval. Shorter than another shechitah = valid. Longer = shehiyah.
↓
💨 Order matters: cut gullet then severed windpipe ≠ severed windpipe then cut gullet
🎓 Key Principle: Shechitah must be a continuous, uninterrupted act. The Sages quantified "interruption" with remarkable precision — not an arbitrary time limit, but a functional one: how long it takes to perform an entire shechitah. If you could have slaughtered a whole animal in the pause, you've broken the continuity beyond repair.
