Chullin 32 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded

Question 1 of 5
According to Rav, how is the shehiyah (delay) interval measured?
Rav holds the interval is measured against slaughtering another animal of the same type. This means the permissible pause for a bird is shorter (birds are faster to slaughter) than for a large animal. The measuring stick adapts to the context of what is being slaughtered.
Question 2 of 5
If someone slaughters a Red Heifer and simultaneously cuts a gourd, what happens?
Rava rules: the Red Heifer is disqualified. The Torah requires the Red Heifer's slaughter to be performed exclusively — "and he shall slaughter it" implies dedicated action. Cutting a gourd simultaneously constitutes "other work" that breaks this exclusivity. Everyone agrees on this — the debate is only about whether slaughtering another animal simultaneously also disqualifies.
Question 3 of 5
A slaughterer's knife falls mid-shechitah. He picks it up and resumes. When is this valid?
The Mishna is explicit: if the knife fell and the slaughterer picked it up, the shechitah is valid ONLY if the interval was shorter than the time to slaughter another animal. This is the definition of shehiyah — an excessive pause during the shechitah process invalidates it. The continuity of the act must be maintained.
Question 4 of 5
Why does the ORDER of cutting the gullet and severing the windpipe matter?
If the windpipe was severed (torn, not properly cut) first, the animal becomes a treifah at that moment — before the gullet was properly slaughtered. Shechitah on a treifah cannot make the meat kosher. But if the gullet was properly cut first (valid shechitah of one siman), and then the windpipe was damaged afterward, the sequence of events changes the halachic analysis entirely.
Question 5 of 5
What is the practical difference between Rav and Shmuel's views on shehiyah?
Rav measures against the same type: a brief pause during bird shechitah (measured against the short time to slaughter another bird) might exceed the limit, while the same pause during animal shechitah (measured against the longer time to slaughter another animal) would be fine. Shmuel uses a universal standard (large animal), so both the bird and animal have the same generous time limit.
