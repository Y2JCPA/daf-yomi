Chullin 26 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded

Question 1 of 5
What is "temed" and what determines its halachic status?
Temed is water poured over grape husks or lees (wine sediment). Its halachic status depends on fermentation: if it ferments, it demonstrates that enough wine character was extracted to transform it, and it's treated as wine for tithes and second-tithe purchasing. If it doesn't ferment, it remains water.
Question 2 of 5
What is R' Yochanan ben Nuri's principle regarding mixed substances?
R' Yochanan ben Nuri's principle is beautifully simple: appearance determines halachic status. He applies this broadly — to mikvah water mixed with milk (if it looks like water, it's valid), to temed (if it looks and acts like wine, it IS wine). The observable reality determines the law.
Question 3 of 5
Why does mikvah contact purification work for unfermented temed but NOT for fermented temed?
Before fermentation, the temed is still "water mixed with grape residue" — the water component is intermingled throughout and can be reached by the mikvah's purifying contact. After fermentation, the mixture has chemically transformed into a new unified substance. The intermingling that allowed purification no longer exists.
Question 4 of 5
According to R' Meir in the Mishna, what is the relationship between a father's right to sell his daughter and the fine for rape/seduction?
R' Meir holds a clean, categorical system: during the minor years (under 12), the father has the right to sell but there is no fine. During the na'arah period (12-12½), the fine applies but sale does not. These are mutually exclusive windows. The Rabbis disagree and allow overlap during the minor years.
Question 5 of 5
If someone purchases unfermented temed with second-tithe money and it later ferments, what happens?
Rav Nachman (citing Rabba bar Avuh) teaches that if temed purchased with second-tithe money later ferments, the purchase retroactively acquires the sanctity of second-tithe. The fermentation reveals that the substance always had wine character — it just hadn't manifested yet. This is a form of retroactive clarification.
