🍷 What Is Temed?

Temed is water poured over grape husks or lees (wine residue). The question: is it wine or water? The answer determines tithing, second-tithe purchasing, and purity laws.
Pour water over grape residue → wait
↓
Did it ferment?
↓

✅ Fermented

  • Treated as wine
  • Subject to tithes
  • Can buy with tithe money

❌ Not Fermented

  • Treated as water
  • Not subject to tithes
  • Cannot buy with tithe money

💰 Second-Tithe Money & Temed

RN
Rav Nachman (citing Rabba bar Avuh): If you buy unfermented temed with second-tithe money, and it later ferments — the purchase retroactively acquires sanctity. It was wine all along, it just hadn't shown itself yet.
Rv
Rava: The Mishna follows R' Yochanan ben Nuri, who holds: "Everything follows the appearance." If it looks like wine, treat it as wine — regardless of its components.
R' Yochanan ben Nuri's principle: Appearance determines halachic status. Applied to mikvah: if 3 log of water mixed with milk looks like water, it's valid. If it looks like milk, it's not. Applied to temed: if it looks and acts like wine, it IS wine halachically.

💧 Mikvah Contact Purification

Temed that is ritually impure can be purified by "contact" — immersing the vessel in a mikvah
↓
Does this work if the water was impure from the outset?
↓
Rav Geviha asks Rav Ashi: What's different between water that was pure and became impure vs. water impure from the start?
↓
Answer: The water and residue are intermingled — so contact with the mikvah reaches the water component throughout, regardless of when impurity began
Key rule: Once temed ferments, contact purification no longer works. Fermentation transforms the mixture from "water mixed in grape residue" into a unified substance that cannot be reached by mikvah contact through mere intermingling.

👧 Hebrew Maidservant — Sale vs. Fine

Mishna: When there is "sale" of a daughter (she is a minor), there is no fine (the 50 sela paid if she is raped/seduced). When there is a fine (she is a na'arah), there is no sale. The two statuses are mutually exclusive.
AgeStatusSale?Fine?
Under 12Minor (ketanah)✅ Father can sell❌ No fine
12 – 12½Na'arah❌ Cannot sell✅ Fine applies
Over 12½Bogeret (adult)❌ Cannot sell❌ No fine
Rav Yehuda says in Rav's name: This is R' Meir's view. The Rabbis disagree: they hold a fine CAN apply even during the period of sale (when she's a minor). The debate hinges on the interpretation of "na'arah" in the fine verses.

⚖️ R' Meir vs. Rabbis on Sale/Fine Overlap

RM
R' Meir: Sale and fine are mutually exclusive. The Torah designates specific ages for each right. There is never overlap. This is clean and categorical.
Rb
Rabbis: A minor who is raped/seduced DOES generate a fine paid to the father. The sale and fine categories can overlap during the minor years — the father holds both rights simultaneously.
