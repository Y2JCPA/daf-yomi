Chullin 84 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
To which animals does the mitzvah of kisui hadam apply according to the Torah?
A. All animals slaughtered for kosher consumption
B. Domestic animals only — cows, sheep, and goats
C. Birds and wild animals (chayot) only
D. Only animals slaughtered in the Temple courtyard
Correct: C — Kisui hadam applies specifically to birds (עוף) and wild animals (חיה) such as deer and gazelle. Domestic animals (בהמה) like cows and sheep are explicitly exempt, as the verse singles out these categories.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Who bears the primary obligation to perform kisui hadam?
A. The owner of the animal who purchased it
B. The shochet (ritual slaughterer) who performed shechita
C. The first bystander to notice the uncovered blood
D. A designated kohein present at the slaughter
Correct: B — The shochet bears the primary obligation. The Mishnah teaches that if the shochet does not cover the blood, bystanders may fulfill the mitzvah — but the shochet has transgressed by failing to act immediately.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which of the following materials is INVALID for kisui hadam?
A. Fine sand collected from a riverbed
B. Sawdust from a carpenter's workshop
C. Gold dust, even though it is powdery
D. Ground spices such as cumin or pepper
Correct: C — Gold dust is invalid despite being powdery, because the Torah specifies עָפָר — earth-derived, granular material. Gold is precious metal, not earth, and does not meet the requirement of something that can be sown or grown in.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When is the blessing for kisui hadam recited relative to the act?
A. After the covering is complete, as a thanksgiving
B. Before covering the blood, following the standard rule for mitzvot
C. Only by the shochet — bystanders recite no blessing
D. No blessing is recited; kisui hadam is a biblical silent act
Correct: B — Like all mitzvot, the blessing is recited before performing the act — עובר לעשייתן. Any person who covers, whether shochet or bystander, recites the full blessing before placing the covering material.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Talmud's teaching about eating meat on Chullin 84, what is the appropriate conduct for a person of modest means?
A. Never eat any meat — all funds should go to Torah study
B. Eat as much meat as possible on Shabbat to fulfill oneg
C. Eat poultry more freely but reserve beef for Shabbat and Yom Tov
D. Eat meat daily since it strengthens one's body for mitzvot
Correct: C — The Talmud teaches proportionality: a person of modest means may eat poultry more freely while reserving beef for Shabbat and festivals. The poor should be particularly careful. All classes, however, should strive for meat on Shabbat as part of oneg Shabbat.
