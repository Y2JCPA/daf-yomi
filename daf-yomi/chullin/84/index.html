🩸 Kisui HaDam: Obligated vs. Exempt

Obligated in Kisui

  • Birds (עוף) — all kosher species
  • Wild animals / Chayot (חיה)
  • e.g. deer, gazelle, wild goat
  • Shochet bears primary duty
  • Bystanders may step in if shochet delays

Exempt from Kisui

  • Domestic animals / Behemot (בהמה)
  • e.g. cow, sheep, goat
  • Fish (no shechita required)
  • Hybrid/doubtful animals — subject to dispute
  • Blood of non-kosher species (no valid shechita)

ויקרא יז:יג — וְשָׁפַךְ אֶת דָּמוֹ וְכִסָּהוּ בֶּעָפָר
📜 The Torah Source — Kisui HaDam When one slaughters a bird or wild animal, the Torah commands: cover the blood with earth. The verse states: "וְשָׁפַךְ אֶת דָּמוֹ וְכִסָּהוּ בֶּעָפָר" — 'He shall pour out its blood and cover it with dust' (Vayikra 17:13). The Sages learn from the juxtaposition of שְׁחִיטָה (slaughter) and כִּסּוּי (covering) that both must be performed by the same person — or at minimum, with the same intent. This applies to all kosher birds and chayot but never to domestic animals such as cows and sheep.

🗣️ Who Is Obligated to Perform the Covering?

Mishnah
If the slaughterer does not cover the blood, it is a mitzvah for all those standing by to cover it. Whoever covers first recites the blessing.
Gemara
The primary obligation falls on the shochet. If he fails to cover immediately, he has transgressed — yet the mitzvah remains available to any bystander who steps in.
Rav Huna
There must be no undue delay between shechita and covering. The blood should be covered immediately after it flows — hesitation diminishes the mitzvah.
Rava
Even a bystander who covers recites: "אֲשֶׁר קִדְּשָׁנוּ בְּמִצְוֹתָיו וְצִוָּנוּ עַל כִּסּוּי הַדָּם". The full blessing is recited regardless of who performs the act.
Halachic Ruling The shochet bears primary responsibility. Delay is a transgression. Any bystander may fulfill the mitzvah and recites the blessing in full.

🌱 Valid vs. Invalid Materials for Kisui

What material may be used for כִּסּוּי הַדָּם?
The Torah says עָפָר — earth/dust
↓
✅ Valid
Fine earth, sand, sawdust, ground spices, ground brick — anything granular that grows from or is derived from the ground
❌ Invalid
Gold dust (too precious, not 'earth'), water/liquids, whole salt, lime that has been dissolved — does not fulfill עָפָר
↓
Key Principle: The material must resemble עָפָר — granular, earth-like, capable of being sown or grown in. Must be placed both beneath and above the blood.

📋 Step-by-Step: Fulfilling Kisui HaDam Correctly

Step 1 — Valid Shechita: Slaughter a kosher bird or wild animal (חַיָּה) with valid shechita. If the shechita is invalid, there is no obligation of kisui.
↓
Step 2 — Position the Blood: Ensure blood flows onto a surface where it can be covered. The blood must be accessible — blood absorbed into the ground already cannot be covered.
↓
Step 3 — Recite the Blessing BEFORE Covering: "בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה ה'...אֲשֶׁר קִדְּשָׁנוּ בְּמִצְוֹתָיו וְצִוָּנוּ עַל כִּסּוּי הַדָּם"
↓
Step 4 — Cover the Blood: Place valid earth or granular material beneath and above the blood. The blood must be completely concealed — partial coverage is insufficient.
↓
Step 5 — Verify: The blood must be entirely hidden from view. If any blood remains visible, the mitzvah is not yet fulfilled and one must add more covering material.
