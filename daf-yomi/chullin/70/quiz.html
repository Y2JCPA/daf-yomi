QUESTION 1 OF 5
What does the halachic principle עובר ירך אמו literally mean and what does it determine?
A. The fetus is a separate legal entity requiring independent slaughter
B. The fetus is considered the mother's limb, so her shechita covers it
C. The mother is obligated to protect the fetus from harm
D. A firstborn fetus has greater sanctity than subsequent offspring
Correct: B — עובר ירך אמו means 'the fetus is the thigh (limb) of the mother.' It determines that the fetus has no independent halachic status — it is subsumed within its mother's body, so her valid shechita renders it permitted for consumption.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
A pregnant cow is slaughtered with a proper shechita. A live healthy calf is found inside. What is the calf's halachic status?
A. Forbidden — it is a living animal that was never slaughtered
B. Permitted only if a second shechita is performed on it immediately
C. Permitted as a ben peku'ah — covered by the mother's shechita
D. Permitted only after checking it for treifot independently
Correct: C — The calf is a ben peku'ah and is fully permitted by virtue of the mother's shechita. Since עובר ירך אמו applies, no independent slaughter is required. The Gemara on Chullin 70 discusses the parameters of this ruling.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the universally agreed-upon threshold at which a fetus loses its 'ירך אמו' status and becomes an independent halachic entity?
A. When the fetus begins to move inside the womb
B. When any single limb crosses outside the womb
C. When the fetus's head emerges from the womb
D. When the umbilical cord is visible
Correct: C — The Gemara establishes that emergence of the head is the critical threshold — paralleling human birth law. Once the head exits, all agree the fetus has crossed into independent existence and the mother's shechita no longer covers it.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is unique about the offspring of a ben peku'ah with respect to בְּכוֹר (firstborn) laws?
A. The offspring has enhanced bechor sanctity as the child of a holy animal
B. The offspring is exempt from bechor sanctity — ben peku'ah exists outside normal categories
C. The offspring must be given to the kohen immediately at birth
D. The offspring's bechor status depends on whether it too was found inside a slaughtered animal
Correct: B — A ben peku'ah exists in an anomalous halachic category. When it later gives birth, its offspring are NOT subject to the standard bechor (firstborn sanctity) laws. The Gemara derives that since the ben peku'ah itself bypassed normal birth and slaughter parameters, its progeny similarly do not enter the standard sanctity framework.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Abaye and Rava dispute the case of a fetal limb that emerged and then retracted before the mother's shechita. What is the core of their disagreement?
A. Abaye holds the limb must be inspected; Rava holds it may be eaten immediately
B. Abaye rules by current position (back inside = covered); Rava holds emergence itself partially severs the ubar's status
C. Both agree the fetus is forbidden; they dispute only the mother's continued permissibility
D. Abaye requires a new shechita; Rava says the mother's shechita retroactively covers all limbs
Correct: B — Their debate reflects two theories of halachic status: Abaye reasons spatially — if it retracted, it is back inside, so yerech imo is restored. Rava reasons that the act of emergence itself registers a change in ontological status that retraction cannot fully undo. Practically, the stringent view (Rava's) is often followed.
