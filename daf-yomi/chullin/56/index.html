🦴 How Much Spinal Cord Must Remain Intact?

Rav Huna
If the majority of the spinal cord (חוּט הַשִּׁדְרָה) is severed — even if the vertebral column itself is intact — the animal is treif, because it cannot survive such damage.
Rav Chisda
We follow the principle of majority: once the greater part of the cord is gone, the animal's ability to sustain life is fatally compromised. The cord is the operative element, not the bone.
Rav Pappa
What if the surrounding membrane (קְרוּם) covering the spinal cord remains intact even though the cord within is severed? Does the protective membrane preserve the animal's kosher status?
Halacha The spinal cord itself is determinative. If the majority is severed or crushed — regardless of whether the vertebral column or its membrane is intact — the animal is treif.

🔬 The Water Test for Lung Adhesions (Sirachot)

Step 1: Carefully remove the lung after shechita, preserving all adhesions (סִירָכוֹת) in their original position relative to the lobes.
↓
Step 2: Inflate the lung by blowing air through the trachea until fully expanded.
↓
Step 3: Submerge the inflated lung in a basin of lukewarm water — not hot, which could obscure results.
↓
Step 4: Observe carefully. If bubbles emerge from beneath an adhesion, a perforation (נִיקּוּב) is indicated beneath it — the animal is treif.
↓
Step 5: If no bubbles and the adhesion survives firm rubbing (חִיכּוּךְ) without dissolving, the lung is kosher — the adhesion is healthy scar tissue.

🫁 Kosher vs. Treif Lung Adhesions

✅ Kosher Adhesion

  • Connects one lobe to an adjacent lobe
  • Survives rubbing (חִיכּוּךְ) without dissolving
  • No bubbles emerge in the water inflation test
  • Adhesion runs lobe-to-chest-wall at a permissible location
  • Indicates healed tissue, not an active perforation

❌ Treif Adhesion

  • Dissolves or crumbles under rubbing — indicates underlying hole
  • Bubbles visible in water test — active perforation beneath
  • Adhesion connects lung to pericardium (heart sac)
  • Lung lobe fails to inflate — major structural breach
  • Adhesion in a location where the lung itself is perforated

⏱️ Ein Treifah Chayah — A Treifah Does Not Live

אֵין טְרֵיפָה חַיָּה — A treifah cannot survive (principle applied throughout Chullin 57–58)
⏱️ The Twelve-Month Survival Principle A core axiom of hilchot treifot: by definition, a treifah is an animal with an injury from which it cannot survive twelve months. This principle (some hold it is halacha l'Moshe miSinai) functions as a theoretical litmus test — if a type of injury theoretically allows survival for a full year, it cannot qualify as a treifah. The Gemara on Daf 56 applies this reasoning to borderline spinal and organ damage cases: have animals with such injuries been observed to live a full year? If yes, the injury may not constitute a treifah.
