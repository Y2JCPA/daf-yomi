📏 The Thyroid Cartilage Boundary

The halacha: If one slaughters from the incline of the thyroid cartilage and below (toward the body) — the shechitah is valid
↓
What about from the incline and ABOVE (toward the jaw)?
↓
Invalid — this is above the "place of shechitah"
↓
The "incline" itself is the boundary line — exactly at the slope of the thyroid cartilage
"Neither Chillek nor Billek": When challenged about his sources, Rav Nachman famously declared: "I don't know the reasons of this one or that one — I only know the halacha: from the incline and below is valid!" A model of practical ruling over theoretical debate.

🔄 The "Cutting Thirds" Problem

Scenario: A slaughterer cuts one-third of the windpipe properly, then the knife diverts out of the valid zone, then cuts the remaining portion. Is the slaughter valid?
Cut ⅓ properly → diverted → cut ⅓ diverted → returned → cut ⅓ properly
↓
Do the two valid thirds combine?
↓
Rav Nachman: Valid — the two properly-cut thirds combine to form a majority
Possible objection: The diverted section broke the continuity of the cut

The rule: Majority of each siman (windpipe/esophagus) must be cut in the valid zone. Properly-cut portions from different passes can combine — the cut doesn't need to be continuous, as long as the total proper cutting exceeds half.

🧵 Thread-Breadth — The Minimum

Rb
The Rabbis say: If one slaughtered within the large upper ring and left a "thread-breadth" of ring over the full circumference — the shechitah is valid.
RH
R' Chanina ben Antigonus: The thread-breadth requirement ensures the ring isn't completely severed from the windpipe — maintaining structural connection.
Why thread-breadth matters: If the entire ring is cut through without leaving any connection, the windpipe may be considered "severed" before the shechitah is complete — potentially creating a treifah (fatally injured animal) before the slaughter is finished. The thread-breadth proves the animal was still viable at the time of shechitah.

⚖️ Rav Nachman at Sura

S
When Rav Nachman visited Sura, they asked him: If one cut one-third properly, then diverted and cut one-third, then returned and cut one-third properly — what is the law?
RN
Valid. The two properly-cut thirds combine.
S
But R' Elazar bar Minyumi said: the knife must go "up and down" within the place of shechitah — doesn't diversion break this?
RN
"Up and down" means back-and-forth cutting motion — not that the knife must stay in one position. As long as the total proper cutting is a majority, the cut is valid.

📖 The Chain of Learning

A beautiful scene in the beit midrash: R' Abba sat behind Rav Kahana, and Rav Kahana sat before Rav Yehuda. Each asked progressively harder questions about cutting-thirds and diversion, building on the previous answer.
Rav Kahana asks: ⅓ proper → ⅓ diverted → ⅓ proper? Valid.
↓
Then: ⅓ diverted → ⅓ proper → ⅓ diverted? Invalid.
↓
Then: ⅓ diverted → ⅓ diverted → ⅓ proper? Invalid — only one-third valid.
↓
Pattern: total proper cutting must be majority of the siman's diameter
