Chullin 19 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded

Question 1 of 5
If one slaughters starting from the incline of the thyroid cartilage going downward — is the shechitah valid?
The halacha (as ruled by Rav Nachman and confirmed by the Gemara) is that slaughtering from the incline of the thyroid cartilage downward is valid. Above the thyroid is outside the valid zone. The "incline" itself is the boundary — and everything from there downward toward the body is valid territory for shechitah.
Question 2 of 5
What is the meaning of Rav Nachman's famous "neither Chillek nor Billek" statement?
"Chillek and Billek" are fictional placeholder names (like "so-and-so"). Rav Nachman was saying: I don't know the reasons behind this one's view or that one's view — but I know the practical ruling. It's a model of a posek (halachic decisor) who rules based on received tradition rather than needing to reconstruct the underlying reasoning.
Question 3 of 5
One cuts ⅓ of the windpipe properly, then the knife diverts, then cuts the final ⅓ properly. Is the shechitah valid?
Rav Nachman rules this valid. The two properly-cut thirds (⅓ + ⅓ = ⅔) combine to form a majority of the siman. The shechitah does not need to be a single uninterrupted stroke — partial cuts in the valid zone can accumulate. The key measure is whether the majority of each siman was cut within the proper location.
Question 4 of 5
Why does the halacha require a "thread-breadth" of ring to remain connected during slaughter?
The thread-breadth requirement ensures structural connection during the slaughter. If the entire ring were completely severed before the simanim were fully cut, the animal might already be considered fatally wounded (a treifah) before the shechitah was complete — invalidating it. The remaining thread-breadth of connection proves the animal was still viable at the crucial moment.
Question 5 of 5
What does the phrase "up and down" (ailu v'ailu) mean in the context of diversion rulings?
Rav Nachman clarifies that "up and down" refers to the sawing back-and-forth motion of the knife during cutting — this is what valid shechitah requires. It does NOT mean the knife must remain in one fixed spot within the trachea. The valid-zone requirement is about location of the cut, while "up and down" is about the method of cutting.
