QUESTION 1 OF 5
What physical condition of the spinal cord renders an animal a tereifah according to the Gemara on Daf 77?
A. Any visible bruising or discoloration of the cord
B. The majority of the cord's circumference is severed at one point
C. The spine bone cracks even slightly near the cord
D. The cord shifts position within the vertebral column
Correct: B — The Gemara rules that tereifah status depends on rov — the majority of the spinal cord's cross-section being severed at a single point, indicating the animal cannot survive.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the meaning and legal implication of 'ubar yerech imo'?
A. A fetus is an independent entity requiring its own ritual slaughter
B. The fetus is considered like a limb of its mother, so her shechita covers it
C. A fetus born naturally is always treated as a first-born animal
D. The fetus's chelev is always forbidden regardless of the mother's status
Correct: B — 'Ubar yerech imo' — the fetus is the thigh of its mother — means the fetus has no independent legal standing for slaughter; the mother's valid shechita renders it permitted.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If a mother animal's spine is broken but her spinal cord is completely intact, what is the halachic ruling?
A. Tereifah — a broken spine always renders the animal forbidden
B. Kosher — the intact spinal cord preserves the animal's status
C. Requires three days of observation before a ruling can be issued
D. Depends on whether the fracture was caused by a predator or accident
Correct: B — The tereifah status is determined by the spinal cord (chut hashidra), not the vertebral bone. An intact cord means the animal is kosher even if the spine itself is fractured.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
A shochet slaughters a mother animal and finds a living, fully-formed fetus inside. Based on the principle discussed on Daf 77, what is the fetus's status?
A. Forbidden — the fetus requires its own shechita
B. Permitted — the mother's shechita covers the fetus under ubar yerech imo
C. Permitted only if the fetus is immediately killed after discovery
D. Requires inspection for tereifah defects before it may be eaten
Correct: B — Because the fetus is legally part of the mother (ubar yerech imo), the mother's valid shechita extends to and covers the fetus — no separate slaughter is required.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is a 'golem' in the context of Chullin 77, and how is it treated halachically?
A. A stillborn fetus — it has the same laws as neveilah
B. An undeveloped or unformed fetus — it follows the mother's status entirely
C. A fetus older than eight months — it requires separate shechita
D. A fetus that survived after the mother was found to be a tereifah
Correct: B — A golem is an undeveloped, incompletely formed fetus. Since it has no independent form or viability, it is entirely subsumed under ubar yerech imo and follows the mother's legal status in every respect.
