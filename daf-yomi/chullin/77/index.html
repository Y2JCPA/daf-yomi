🦴 Spine Damage and the Status of the Fetus

Rav Huna
If the shidra (spine) is broken and the chut hashidra (spinal cord) is severed — even if most of the cord survives in one section — the animal is a tereifah. The rupture itself is the determining factor.
Rava
The decisive test is rov: if the majority of the spinal cord's circumference is severed at any one point, the animal cannot survive and is therefore a tereifah.
Dual Theme of Daf 77 The daf addresses two interlocking questions: what degree of spinal damage creates a tereifah, and whether a fetus (ubar) is legally independent of its mother or merely a part of her body — with sweeping consequences for slaughter, kashrut, and sanctity.

🦴 Mishnah: The Broken Spine

נִשְׁבְּרָה הַשִּׁדְרָה וְנִפְסַק חוּט הַשִּׁדְרָה — טְרֵפָה
Mishnah — Core Tereifah An animal whose shidra (spinal column) is broken and whose chut hashidra (spinal cord) is severed is a tereifah. The Gemara probes: must both conditions coexist? What if only the cord is severed? What counts as 'severed'? The answer hinges on the principle of rov — majority of the cord's width at a single cross-section.

👶 Ubar Yerech Imo: Fetus as Limb of the Mother

Tanna Kamma
Ubar yerech imo — the fetus is considered like a limb (yerech) of its mother. Shechita performed on the mother covers the fetus entirely; no separate slaughter is needed. The fetus's tereifah status also follows the mother's.
Rabbi Meir
While the fetus generally follows the mother, certain laws treat it independently — particularly bechor (first-born) status and, in some opinions, the prohibition of chelev (forbidden fat) in a fully-formed fetus.
אוּבָר יֶרֶךְ אִמּוֹ This Talmudic principle — 'the fetus is the thigh of its mother' — determines kashrut, sacrificial law, inheritance, and the laws of first-born animals across multiple tractates.

🐄 Fetus: Dependent vs. Independent — Legal Consequences

Ubar Yerech Imo (Part of Mother)

  • Mother's shechita renders fetus permitted
  • Fetus tereifah status follows mother
  • No independent dam (blood) prohibition
  • Golem (undeveloped) fully follows mother
  • Fetus cannot independently establish bechor

Fetus as Independent Entity

  • Would require own shechita to be permitted
  • Tereifah assessed independently
  • Chelev of formed fetus has own status
  • Can count toward first-born for the father
  • Dam hadam (blood of blood) analyzed separately
