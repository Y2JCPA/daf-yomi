🍗 Is Poultry-Milk Prohibition Biblical or Rabbinic?

R' Akiva
Poultry has no mother that produces milk — birds hatch from eggs. The verse says 'in its mother's milk,' implying a nursing mother. Therefore poultry with milk cannot be a biblical prohibition!
Chachamim
Even so, the Rabbis decreed that one may not cook or eat poultry with milk — a protective fence (gezeirah) lest one confuse it with actual meat-milk which is a Torah prohibition.
R' Akiva
I fully agree it is rabbinically forbidden. But the distinction matters greatly: poultry with milk = rabbinic; domesticated animal meat with milk = Torah prohibition with lashes.
Gemara
This distinction is legally critical: biblical violation incurs malkot (lashes); the rabbinic prohibition does not — though both are binding and forbidden.
Key Distinction Biblical: beef/lamb/goat + milk → lashes. Rabbinic: poultry + milk → no lashes, still forbidden

🦌 Are Wild Animals (Chayyot) Included in Basar B'Chalav?

Does the prohibition apply to a wild animal (chayya)?
e.g., deer, gazelle, wild ox
↓
The verse uses "gedi" (kid) — pointing to domesticated animals. The word "imo" (its mother) implies a mother-offspring nursing relationship
↓
Included?
🦌 Some argue: A chayya also nurses its young with milk — perhaps the verse should extend to wild animals
Excluded?
🐄 Halacha rules: Only the three domesticated species (shor, keves, ez) are biblically included
↓
✅ Conclusion: Wild animals are not included in the biblical prohibition — only the three domesticated species. Rabbinically, wild animal meat with milk is also prohibited.

⚖️ What Is and Isn't Included in Basar B'Chalav

🔴 Torah Prohibition

  • Beef (שור) with any milk
  • Lamb / mutton (כֶּבֶשׂ) with milk
  • Goat (עֵז) with milk
  • All three acts: cooking, eating, benefit
  • Lashes (malkot) for violation

🟡 Rabbinic Prohibition

  • Poultry (עוֹף) with milk
  • Wild animal (חַיָּה) meat with milk
  • No lashes, but fully forbidden
  • Enacted as protective fence (גְּזֵרָה)
  • Some permit serving at same table (machloket)

🟢 Fully Permitted

  • Fish (דָּגִים) — not basar in this sense
  • Locusts (חֲגָבִים) — permitted freely
  • No restriction on serving with milk
  • Biblical and rabbinic sources agree
  • (Later minhag adds stringency for fish+meat)

🐐 Unpacking 'Gedi B'Chalav Imo' — Key Terms

גְּדִי (Gedi) — literally 'kid' (young goat); halacha extends this to any young of the three domesticated species (shor, keves, ez)
↓
בַּחֲלֵב (B'Chalav) — 'in milk' — requires actual mammal milk; non-dairy substitutes are not subject to the biblical prohibition
↓
אִמּוֹ (Imo) — 'its mother' — implies a nursing mother-offspring relationship; this is the key reason poultry is excluded from the biblical verse
↓
Three-fold repetition — Each of the three occurrences is independently necessary; were it written only once, we would know only one prohibition. Each repetition adds a new distinct issur
↓
Triple liability: One who cooks and eats a meat-milk mixture for personal benefit violates all three prohibitions simultaneously and may incur three separate sets of lashes
