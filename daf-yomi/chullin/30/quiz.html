Question 1 of 5
Can two people slaughter one offering together according to the Rabbis?
The Rabbis hold that two people MAY slaughter one offering together — if both hold the knife and cut, the shechitah is valid. R' Elazar b. R' Shimon disagrees, concerned that two people may inadvertently cause each other to press (derasah) instead of drawing the knife smoothly.
Question 2 of 5
What is the concern R' Elazar b. R' Shimon has about two people slaughtering together?
R' Elazar b. R' Shimon's concern is practical: when two people hold the same knife, they may inadvertently push against each other's motion, causing pressing (derasah) instead of the required smooth drawing motion. Derasah is one of the five factors that invalidate shechitah. The risk of accidental disqualification is what concerns him.
Question 3 of 5
What is a "kekulmos" (reed-point) cut, and is it valid?
A "kekulmos" cut refers to a single diagonal stroke — like the pointed tip of a reed — where the blade crosses the gullet at one height and the windpipe at a different height. R' Avin clarifies that this is ONE continuous cut at an angle, not two separate cuts. Since it's a single uninterrupted motion, both simanim are validly cut.
Question 4 of 5
If someone inserts a knife between the two simanim and cuts outward, what is the halachic problem?
This is a classic case of chaladah (concealment). The knife is hidden between the two simanim, cutting from a concealed position. Chaladah requires that the blade be concealed under something — here, between the two simanim. Proper shechitah requires an exposed blade that cuts in a visible, controlled manner.
Question 5 of 5
A bull was slaughtered with cuts at 2-3 different places on its simanim. Is it kosher?
Rav Yitzchak bar Shmuel bar Marta demonstrated his confidence in the validity by taking meat from such a bull. The principle: multiple cut marks on a siman don't automatically invalidate the shechitah. As long as the cumulative cutting achieved majority of both simanim, and no shehiyah (excessive delay), derasah, chaladah, hagramah, or ikkur occurred, the shechitah is valid.
