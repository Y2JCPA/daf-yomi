👥 Two People Slaughtering One Offering

RE
R' Elazar b. R' Shimon: Two people may NOT slaughter one offering. Each offering requires a single, unified act of slaughter by one person.
Rb
The Rabbis: Two people MAY slaughter one offering together. If both hold the knife and cut, the shechitah is valid.
Why would R' Elazar b. R' Shimon disqualify? His concern: two people may inadvertently cause each other to press (derasah) — one pushing against the other instead of drawing the knife smoothly. This would invalidate the shechitah through one of the five disqualifying factors.

✂️ Diagonal Slaughter — Like a Reed's Point

The case: One cut the gullet low on the neck and the windpipe high on the neck (or vice versa). Is this valid?
Are these two separate cuts (invalid — too far apart)?
↓
R' Avin resolves: This refers to a DIAGONAL cut — like the point of a reed (kekulmos) — where a single angled stroke crosses both simanim at different heights. ONE continuous cut, not two separate ones. Valid!
Visualize it: Imagine a single diagonal knife stroke across the neck. The blade crosses the gullet at a lower point and the windpipe at a higher point (or vice versa). It's still one continuous motion, just angled. The key: both simanim are cut in a single stroke without interruption.

🐂 Bull Slaughtered in Multiple Places

Gm
A bull was brought that had been slaughtered with cuts in 2-3 different places on its simanim (neck). Is it kosher?
RI
Rav Yitzchak bar Shmuel bar Marta: He entered the butcher shop and confidently took a cut of meat from the hip — indicating he considered the shechitah valid.
The principle: As long as the majority of both simanim were ultimately cut (even if the cuts happened at slightly different spots on the siman), and none of the five disqualifying factors occurred, the shechitah is valid. Multiple cut marks don't necessarily mean multiple separate acts.

🔪 Concealed Knife Between Simanim (Chaladah)

Rav Yehuda citing Rav: If one concealed (inserted) the knife BETWEEN the two simanim and cut outward, severing one siman from inside...
↓
Is this chaladah (concealment), which is one of the five disqualifying factors?
↓
Yes — this is chaladah. The knife is hidden between the simanim, cutting from a concealed position. This is exactly what the disqualifying factor of chaladah prohibits.
Reminder — the 5 disqualifying factors: Shehiyah (delay), Derasah (pressing), Chaladah (concealment), Hagramah (wrong location), Ikkur (tearing). Inserting the knife between simanim and cutting outward is a classic case of chaladah — the blade is hidden, not exposed.

↓
🐏 Paschal offering intent: the "when does shechitah happen" debate from Daf 29 applies here
↓
✂️ Diagonal cut (like a reed point) = valid — one continuous stroke at an angle across both simanim
↓
🔪 Knife concealed between simanim = chaladah (disqualified)
🎓 Key Principle: Shechitah is about control, visibility, and continuity. A diagonal cut is fine because it's one continuous exposed motion. A concealed knife is not fine because the blade is hidden. The Torah's shechitah system values transparency — the slaughterer must see what he's cutting, and the cut must be one clean, controlled act.
