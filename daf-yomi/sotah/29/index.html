Daf 29: Degrees of Defilement & Uncertain Impurity

"Venitma'a" (Numbers 5:29) — How many prohibitions does the sota verse teach?
↓
R' Akiva (4 derivations)
Expounds the vav: Husband, Paramour, Priesthood, Teruma
R' Yishmael (3 derivations)
Does not expound the vav: Husband, Paramour, Teruma; Priesthood via kal vachomer
↓
The Gemara then derives uncertain impurity rules from both the sota and Leviticus 7:19
↓
Discussion of degrees of tumah: How far does impurity chain for teruma and kodshim?
Core Theme This daf bridges the laws of sota's defilement to the broader framework of uncertain ritual impurity and the chain-reaction of tumah through multiple degrees.

How Many Prohibitions from 'Venitma'a'?

R' Akiva
The prefix vav in venitma'a is expounded — yielding four derivations from the verse.
R' Yishmael
The vav is not expounded. Only three derivations come from the verse.
R' Akiva
The four: forbidden to husband, to paramour, to priesthood, and to teruma.
R' Yishmael
Three: forbidden to husband, paramour, and teruma. The priesthood prohibition is derived via kal vachomer.
Key Dispute R' Akiva and R' Yishmael agree on the prohibitions but disagree on whether each requires its own verse or can be derived logically.

Uncertain Impurity: Rav Giddel's Derivation from Leviticus 7:19

Leviticus 7:19: "The flesh that touches any impure thing shall not be eaten"
↓
Implication: Only certainly impure meat is forbidden — uncertain impurity = permitted?
↓
But the verse continues: "Every one that is pure may eat" — only certainly pure people may eat?
↓
Resolution: The verse distinguishes two types of uncertain impurity
↓
Yesh Da'at Lisha'el
Entity with awareness to be asked (e.g., a person) — uncertain impurity = impure
Ein Da'at Lisha'el
Entity without awareness (e.g., food) — uncertain impurity = pure
Rav Giddel / Rav Both derivations are needed: from the verse (to distinguish awareness) and from sota (to limit uncertain impurity to the private domain).
6 / 9

Why We Need Both Derivations for Uncertain Impurity

From Leviticus 7:19

  • Teaches: yesh da'at = impure, ein da'at = pure
  • Without sota: would apply in BOTH private and public domains
  • Needed for: the awareness distinction

From Sota (Numbers 5:29)

  • Teaches: uncertain impurity is impure only in private domain
  • Without Lev. 7:19: would require BOTH parties to have awareness
  • Needed for: the domain distinction (private vs. public)
Complementary Sources Each derivation corrects a flaw we'd have if we relied on only one source. Together they establish the full framework of uncertain tumah.

Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai's A Fortiori Inference

🔹 One who immersed that day (tevul yom) — does NOT render non-sacred food impure, but DISQUALIFIES teruma
↓
🔹 An earthenware vessel — does NOT render non-sacred food impure, but DISQUALIFIES teruma
↓
⚖️ Common denominator: both permit non-sacred food but disqualify teruma on contact
↓
🍞 A loaf with second-degree impurity disqualifies even non-sacred food — which is MORE stringent
↓
✅ Kal vachomer: it should certainly disqualify teruma on contact (third-degree for teruma)
Future Challenge RYBZ predicted a future generation would refute this by arguing that both sources share a unique stringency — but he held the inference valid since their stringencies differ from each other.
