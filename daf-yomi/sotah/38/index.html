Temple vs. Country: Birkat Kohanim

🏛️ In the Temple

  • God's name pronounced as the Tetragrammaton
  • Hands raised above the head
  • Exception: High Priest does not raise above the tzitz (frontplate)
  • R' Yehuda disagrees: Even the High Priest raises above it (Lev. 9:22)

🏘️ In the Country

  • God's name replaced with Adonai (substitute name)
  • Hands raised only to shoulder height
  • No distinction between High Priest and regular priests
  • Same requirements of standing, Hebrew, and lifted hands apply
Why the Difference? The Tetragrammaton is inscribed on the High Priest's frontplate (tzitz). Outside the Temple, the ineffable Name is not pronounced at all, so the issue doesn't arise.

Can Lifted Hands Be Derived from the Tabernacle Dedication?

Baraita
Gezera shava: "Bless" (Num. 6:23) ↔ Aaron "lifted his hands and blessed them" (Lev. 9:22). Therefore: lifted hands required.
R' Yonatan
If we learn from the Tabernacle dedication, we should also require: only the High Priest, on Rosh Chodesh, and during communal offerings!
R' Natan
No need for gezera shava at all! Deut. 18:5 says "him and his sons forever" — sons are juxtaposed with Aaron, and "forever" means it's not limited to special occasions.
R' Natan
Furthermore, Deut. 10:8 juxtaposes blessing with Temple service: "to minister to Him and to bless in His name."
Resolution R' Natan resolves R' Yonatan's objection by using a direct derivation rather than a gezera shava, avoiding the problematic analogy to the Tabernacle dedication entirely.

R' Yehoshua ben Levi: The Priest's Obligation to Bless

🙌 God desires the Priestly Benediction — "I will bless them" (Num. 6:27)
↓
✨ A priest who blesses is blessed from Heaven — "I will bless those who bless you" (Gen. 12:3)
↓
⚠️ A priest who does not ascend violates 3 positive mitzvot: "So you shall bless," "Say to them," "Put My name"
↓
🚶 He must begin ascending during Birkat Avodah (blessing of Temple service in the Amida)
↓
🔎 Rav adds: A priest who never ascends — suspect he may be a disqualified Kohen (son of a divorcée or ḥalutza)
Reconciliation Rav and R' Yehoshua ben Levi don't disagree: R"YBL speaks of one who sometimes ascends (he's sinning), Rav speaks of one who never ascends (he may be disqualified).

When Can a Priest Still Ascend to Bless?

Did the Kohen begin moving toward the platform during Birkat Avodah?
Yes
He may ascend even after Birkat Avodah concludes
No
He may no longer ascend for this blessing
↓
R' Ami & R' Asi would begin walking during Birkat Avodah but arrive at the platform after it concluded — this was sufficient
↓
Even a prayer leader who is a Kohen: if he shifts slightly from his place during Birkat Avodah, he may recite the benediction
Source R' Oshaya: The prohibition applies only if the Kohen did not begin walking — even a slight movement counts as beginning.
