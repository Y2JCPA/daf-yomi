Sotah 38 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
How does the Gemara derive that the Priestly Benediction must be recited in Hebrew?
A. A gezera shava linking "bless" to the blessings on Mount Gerizim
B. A direct command in Numbers 6:23 specifying Hebrew
C. An oral tradition from Moses at Sinai
D. A kal va'chomer from the Temple service
Correct: A — The Gemara uses a gezera shava: 'bless' in the Priestly Benediction is linked to 'bless' at Mount Gerizim (Deut. 27:12), where the blessings were recited in Hebrew. R' Yehuda derives it differently from the word 'כֹּה' (Thus).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does R' Yonatan object to deriving 'lifted hands' from Aaron's blessing at the Tabernacle dedication?
A. Aaron was not really a Kohen at that point
B. The verse doesn't mention hands explicitly
C. It would also require the blessing only by the High Priest, on Rosh Chodesh, during communal offerings
D. The Tabernacle dedication was a one-time event with no lasting authority
Correct: C — R' Yonatan argues that if we derive from the Tabernacle dedication, we'd have to import all its conditions: only the High Priest, on Rosh Chodesh, and during communal service — which we know is not the halakha.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Yehoshua ben Levi, how many positive mitzvot does a priest violate by not ascending to bless?
A. One
B. Two
C. Three
D. Five
Correct: C — R' Yehoshua ben Levi says a non-ascending priest violates three positive mitzvot: 'So you shall bless,' 'And you shall say to them' (Num. 6:23), and 'So shall they put My name' (Num. 6:27).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the latest point at which a Kohen can begin moving toward the platform and still recite the Priestly Benediction?
A. Before the Amida begins
B. During Birkat Avodah (the blessing of Temple service)
C. During the recitation of Modim
D. Any time before the prayer leader says 'Kohanim'
Correct: B — R' Oshaya teaches that if a Kohen began moving toward the platform during Birkat Avodah, he may still ascend even after that blessing concludes. If he did not begin moving during Birkat Avodah, he may no longer ascend.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does R' Yehoshua ben Levi connect the Eglah Arufah (broken-necked heifer) to miserly people?
A. Because miserly people are more likely to commit murder
B. Because the elders must declare they did not send the victim away without food or escort — implying failure of hospitality caused the death
C. Because the heifer is an expensive offering meant to punish stinginess
D. Because the victim was likely a poor person ignored by the wealthy
Correct: B — The elders declare 'Our hands did not shed this blood' — meaning they did not dismiss the victim without food or accompaniment. The Eglah Arufah addresses a failure of generosity and communal responsibility, which is the domain of the miserly.
