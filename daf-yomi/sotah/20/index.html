When Can the Sota Retract Her Decision?

R' Akiva (Baraita 1)
The erasure of the scroll prevents compulsion — she can retract until the Name is erased.
R' Akiva (Baraita 2)
The sacrifice of the handful (קְמִיצָה) prevents compulsion — she can retract until it is offered.
Gemara
These are two tanna'im who disagree about R' Akiva's opinion — not an actual contradiction.
Dilemma
If she first says "I will not drink" while healthy, then retracts and says "I will drink" — is her refusal treated as a confession of defilement?
Gemara
The dilemma stands unresolved — תֵּיקוּ.
Unresolved Does saying "I won't drink" constitute an implicit confession of guilt, or could it simply reflect fear?

R' Meir's Iron Sulfate Ink (קַנְקַנְתּוֹם)

R' Meir
When I studied before R' Akiva, I added iron sulfate to my ink for writing Torah scrolls, and he said nothing in protest.
R' Yishmael
My son, what is your vocation? Be careful — your work is the work of Heaven! One omitted or added letter could destroy the world.
R' Meir
I am an expert — and I even add iron sulfate so a fly cannot erase a דָּלֶת into a רֵישׁ by sitting on it!
R' Yishmael
May iron sulfate be added? The Torah says "he shall blot them out" — we need ink that can be erased for the sota!
Contradiction
Wait — another baraita says R' Meir studied with R' Yishmael first, then R' Akiva prohibited the iron sulfate!
Scribal Care R' Meir's concern: a fly landing on the crown of a ד (dalet) could erase it into a ר (reish), potentially changing a word's meaning catastrophically.

Teaching Daughters Torah: Ben Azzai vs. R' Eliezer

Ben Azzai — Obligated

  • A person is obligated to teach his daughter Torah
  • Reason: If she drinks and doesn't die immediately
  • She should know that merit delayed her punishment
  • Without knowledge, she might think the water doesn't work
  • Understanding prevents false conclusions

R' Eliezer — Forbidden

  • Teaching a daughter Torah is like teaching her תִּפְלוּת (promiscuity)
  • Torah knowledge could be misused
  • She might accumulate merit deliberately to circumvent the sota process
  • This would undermine the entire deterrent effect
Context This debate directly follows the Mishna's teaching that merit can delay punishment for 1, 2, or even 3 years — raising the question of whether women should know this.
