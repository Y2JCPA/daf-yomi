5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say there is no contradiction between R' Akiva's two statements about when a sota can retract?
A. Two tanna'im disagree about R' Akiva's opinion
B. R' Akiva changed his mind over time
C. One statement refers to a willing sota and the other to an unwilling one
D. The two baraitot address different stages of the ritual
Correct: A — The Gemara resolves the contradiction by explaining that two different tanna'im transmitted different versions of R' Akiva's opinion — it's a dispute about what R' Akiva actually held.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did R' Meir add iron sulfate (kankantom) to his ink?
A. To make the letters more beautiful
B. To prevent a fly from erasing a dalet into a reish
C. To fulfill a special mitzvah of permanent writing
D. Because R' Yishmael instructed him to do so
Correct: B — R' Meir explained that the iron sulfate prevented accidental erasure — specifically, a fly sitting on the protrusion of a dalet could erase it into a reish, potentially altering the meaning of the Torah text.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Ben Azzai's reason for obligating a father to teach his daughter Torah?
A. So she can study Talmud independently
B. So she knows that merit can delay the sota water's punishment
C. So she can serve as a witness in court
D. So she can write her own Torah scroll
Correct: B — Ben Azzai says a woman should know Torah so that if she drinks the bitter water and doesn't die immediately, she understands that her merit delayed the punishment — rather than concluding the water doesn't work.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Gemara, what is the practical difference between R' Yehuda's and R' Ya'akov's versions of R' Meir's ruling on iron sulfate?
A. Whether iron sulfate may be used in mezuzot
B. Whether the sota passage may be erased from a Torah scroll for use
C. Whether a scribe must be ordained
D. Whether iron sulfate affects the kashrut of tefillin
Correct: B — R' Yirmeya explains: According to R' Yehuda (no iron sulfate in the Torah's sota passage), you may erase that passage from a Torah scroll for sota use. According to R' Ya'akov (only the dedicated sota scroll is restricted), you may not erase from a Torah scroll.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rav Ḥisda attributes the Mishna's position to R' Akiva. Why can't it follow R' Shimon alone?
A. R' Shimon holds the meal-offering comes after drinking
B. R' Shimon says merit does NOT delay the water's punishment, but the Mishna says it does
C. R' Shimon doesn't believe the water works at all
D. R' Shimon holds the woman is never forced to drink
Correct: B — The Mishna states both that the water affects her while drinking (matching R' Shimon's ritual order) AND that merit delays punishment (which R' Shimon denies). Rav Ḥisda resolves this by attributing it to R' Akiva, who agrees with R' Shimon on order but with the Rabbis on merit.
