Why Was the Tithe Declaration Canceled?

Levites did not ascend from Babylonia with Ezra
↓
Ezra penalized them: first tithe given to Kohanim instead
↓
Owner can no longer say "I have done all You commanded me" (Deut. 26:14)
↓
Can he at least declare the other tithes?
Reish Lakish
No — if you can't declare first tithe, you can't declare any tithe
Abaye explains
The verse begins with the Levite's tithe (Deut. 26:13) — skip the start, lose the rest
Key Principle The vidui ma'aser is an all-or-nothing declaration — partial compliance means no declaration at all.

Yochanan the High Priest's Demai Ordinance

CategoryRuling for Buyer of Produce from Am Ha'aretz
Teruma GedolaAlready separated by most people — not required
First TitheMust separate; give terumat ma'aser to a Kohen
Second TitheMust separate; eat it in Jerusalem
First Tithe (to Levite)Not required — burden of proof on claimant (ha-motzi me-chavero alav ha-re'ayah)
Poor Man's TitheNot required — burden of proof on claimant
Context Yochanan sent investigators across Eretz Yisrael and found people separating teruma gedola but neglecting first and second tithes. He warned them: eating untithed produce carries death at the hand of Heaven.

The Awakeners (Me'orerin): "Awake, O Lord!"

Rachava
Each day the Levites on the duchan would recite: "Awake, why do you sleep, O Lord?" (Psalms 44:24)
Yochanan the High Priest
Does the Omnipresent sleep?! It is stated: "He that keeps Israel neither slumbers nor sleeps" (Psalms 121:4)!
Gemara
The verse is appropriate only when Israel suffers while the nations are at ease — not as a daily recitation
Yochanan the High Priest
Daily recitation would be misinterpreted as if God literally sleeps — therefore I cancel it
Insight The concern was theological: routine recitation of anthropomorphic language could lead to misconceptions about God's nature.

The Strikers (Nokfin): Two Explanations

Shmuel's Explanation

  • Workers scratched the calf between its horns
  • Blood fell into its eyes so it couldn't see
  • Purpose: prevent the animal from resisting slaughter
  • Problem: appeared to be inflicting a blemish

Baraita's Explanation

  • Workers beat the calf with sticks to stun it
  • Resembled idolatrous practices before their gods
  • Problem: beatings could perforate the brain membrane
  • Making the animal a tereifa — 'feeding carcasses to the altar'
Solution Yochanan installed rings in the ground to secure the animals, eliminating the need for scratching or beating before slaughter.
