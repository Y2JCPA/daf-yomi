Sotah 16 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Isi ben Menaḥem, why is there no need for a verse to prove the dust must come from the Sanctuary?
A. A kal va-chomer from the impurity laws of the Sanctuary proves it
B. It is an established oral tradition from Sinai
C. The word 'Tabernacle' inherently includes all future locations
D. The dust requirement is only rabbinic, not biblical
Correct: A — Isi ben Menaḥem argues: if even entering the Sanctuary while impure (a lenient matter) applies to all locations, then surely the sota ritual (a stringent matter carrying strangulation) also applies everywhere — a fortiori.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara conclude that ashes may NOT substitute for dust in the sota water?
A. Beit Shammai's view is accepted as authoritative in this case
B. Ashes will not decompose into dust, unlike vegetable matter
C. The verse explicitly prohibits ashes
D. R' Akiva ruled against it
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes via Rav Huna bar Ashi in the name of Rav: decomposed vegetable matter is acceptable because it will become dust, but ashes will never become dust — making them unsuitable.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What distinguishes R' Yishmael's hermeneutic method from R' Akiva's regarding the leper's shaving?
A. R' Yishmael uses gezeirah shavah; R' Akiva uses binyan av
B. R' Yishmael uses klal u'frat u'khlal (only items similar to the detail); R' Akiva uses ribbui u'mi'ut u'ribbui (includes everything)
C. R' Yishmael requires rabbinic authority; R' Akiva derives directly from the verse
D. R' Yishmael is more expansive; R' Akiva is more restrictive
Correct: B — R' Yishmael's generalization-detail-generalization limits the law to items similar to the detail (visible collected hair), while R' Akiva's amplification-restriction-amplification includes virtually everything (excluding only nose hairs).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does R' Yishmael require the blood of the leper's bird to be visible in the water?
A. Because the Torah says 'dip in them' — implying both must be seen
B. Because if the verse only meant to mix them, it would have said 'dip in them' rather than listing 'in the blood' and 'in the water' separately
C. Because the quarter-log measurement requires visibility
D. Because it is derived from the sota dust requirement by analogy
Correct: B — R' Yishmael argues that the verse's explicit listing of 'in the blood of the slain bird and in the running water' (rather than simply 'in them') is superfluous unless it teaches that the blood must be recognizable in the water.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does R' Zeira respond to R' Yirmeya's question about a bird whose blood might efface or be effaced by the water?
A. He says it is a valid question requiring further study
B. He says in such cases the ritual is invalid
C. He rebukes him — the Sages calibrated the law to a sparrow, which never presents this problem
D. He says one should simply add more water or more blood as needed
Correct: C — R' Zeira tells R' Yirmeya not to go beyond the bounds of practical halakha — the Sages specifically measured the ratio with a sparrow (tzipor dror), which is never so large or so small as to create this problem.
