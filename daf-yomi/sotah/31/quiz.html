QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Tanḥum, how did the fetuses see God at the splitting of the sea?
A. An angel opened a window in the womb
B. Their mothers' stomachs became like luminous crystal (aspaklarya)
C. They heard the sounds and prophetically 'saw' without sight
D. God temporarily removed them from the womb and returned them
Correct: B — R' Tanḥum teaches that the mothers' stomachs were transformed into transparent crystal (aspaklarya), allowing the fetuses to see through and declare 'This is my God.'
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does R' Meir prove that Job served God out of love?
A. From the verse 'Though He slay me, I will trust in Him'
B. By comparing Job's 'God-fearing' to Abraham's 'God-fearing,' and Abraham is known to have loved God
C. From Job's willingness to sacrifice his children
D. By interpreting the word 'lo' as lamed-vav meaning 'to Him'
Correct: B — R' Meir uses a gezeirah shavah: both Job and Abraham are called 'God-fearing,' and since Abraham is explicitly described as loving God (Isaiah 41:8), Job's fear must also have been motivated by love.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Shimon ben Elazar, how long do the merits of one who serves God out of love endure compared to one who serves out of fear?
A. Love: 1,000 generations; Fear: 500 generations
B. Love: 2,000 generations; Fear: 1,000 generations
C. Love: eternal; Fear: 1,000 generations
D. Love: 10,000 generations; Fear: 1,000 generations
Correct: B — Exodus 20:5 uses 'thousands' (plural, minimum 2,000) for those who love God, while Deuteronomy 7:9 uses 'a thousand' (singular, 1,000) for those who keep commandments out of fear.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Torah require only ONE witness for defilement but TWO witnesses for seclusion?
A. Because defilement is more serious and requires stricter proof
B. Because the word 'bah' in Numbers 5:13 limits the one-witness rule to defilement only, excluding warning and seclusion
C. Because seclusion is a public act while defilement is private
D. Because the husband himself counts as one witness for defilement
Correct: B — The Gemara derives from the word 'bah' (בה) in Numbers 5:13 that the one-witness exception applies only to the matter of defilement ('with regard to it'), but not to warning and not to seclusion, which require the standard two witnesses.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the case of contradictory testimony about a sota's defilement — one witness says 'defiled' and two say 'not defiled' — what is the ruling?
A. She does not drink and is forbidden to her husband
B. She drinks the bitter water due to unresolved uncertainty
C. She is immediately divorced
D. The case is dismissed and she returns to her husband
Correct: B — Even when two witnesses say she was NOT defiled and only one says she WAS, she still drinks the bitter water. The two-against-one majority only works the other way: if two say 'defiled' and one says 'not defiled,' she does NOT drink and must be divorced.
