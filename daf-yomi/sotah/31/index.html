Sotah 31 — Love, Fear & the Weight of Testimony

גדול העושה מאהבה מן העושה מיראה
Core Theme This daf explores two major themes: the spiritual superiority of serving God out of love over fear, demonstrated through Job and Abraham, and the evidentiary requirements for the different stages of the sota process — warning, seclusion, and defilement each require different levels of testimony.

Did Job Serve God Out of Love?

R' Yehoshua ben Hyrcanus
Job served God only out of love, as it says: 'Though He will slay me, still I will trust in Him' (Job 13:15)
Gemara
But the word לו is ambiguous! Is it לא (lamed-alef = 'I will NOT trust') or לו (lamed-vav = 'I trust in HIM')?
Gemara
Proof that לא doesn't always mean 'not': In Isaiah 63:9 — 'In all their affliction לא afflicted' — yet the verse continues that God DID save them!
R' Meir
We learn it differently: Job is called 'God-fearing' (Job 1:1), and Abraham is called 'God-fearing' (Gen 22:12). Just as Abraham feared God out of love, so too Job.
Key Proof Abraham's love is established from Isaiah 41:8: 'The offspring of Abraham who loved Me'

Serving God: Love vs. Fear

Out of Love (מאהבה)

  • Merit endures for 2,000 generations
  • Source: 'Showing mercy unto thousands of generations of those who love Me' (Exodus 20:5)
  • Rava's student dreamed: 'All who take refuge in You rejoice…those who love Your name exult' (Ps 5:12)
  • The phrase 'thousands' (plural) = at least 2,000

Out of Fear (מיראה)

  • Merit endures for 1,000 generations
  • Source: 'Keeps covenant…for a thousand generations' (Deut 7:9)
  • Rava's student dreamed: 'How abundant is Your goodness…for those who fear You' (Ps 31:20)
  • Adjacent to 'keep His commandments' — i.e., observance without mention of love
R' Shimon ben Elazar Greater is the one who performs mitzvot out of love than out of fear — love yields double the generational merit

Fetuses Singing at the Sea

נעשה להן מעי אמן כאספקלריא המאירה וראו
Aggadic Teaching Even fetuses in the womb sang praise at the splitting of the sea. R' Tanḥum explains: their mothers' stomachs became transparent like luminous crystal (aspaklarya), allowing the babies to see and declare: 'This is my God and I will glorify Him' (Exodus 15:2).

Witness Requirements in the Sota Process

StageWitnesses NeededSource
Warning (קינוי)Two witnessesDerived from 'bah' — one witness suffices for defilement, but not for warning
Seclusion (סתירה)Two witnessesDerived from 'bah' + verbal analogy of 'davar' (Deut 24:1 ↔ Deut 19:15)
Defilement (טומאה)One witness suffices'There was no witness (עד) against her' (Num 5:13) — singular = only one, not two
Principle The more severe the consequence, the fewer witnesses are needed — defilement creates an irrevocable prohibition yet requires only one witness

Sota: When Contradictory Witnesses Testify About Defilement

Witnesses testify about whether the woman was defiled
↓
1 says defiled, 1 says not
She drinks the bitter water (uncertainty)
1 says defiled, 2 say not
She drinks the bitter water (uncertainty remains)
2 say defiled, 1 says not
She does NOT drink — husband must divorce her
R' Eliezer's Rule Even if the husband heard of seclusion from a 'flying bird' (any unverified source), he must divorce her — but must still pay the ketubah since there is no proof
