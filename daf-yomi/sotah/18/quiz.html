Sotah 18 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is it invalid to write one letter of the sota scroll, erase it, then write the next letter and erase it?
A. The ink must dry before erasure
B. The entire passage must be written completely before being erased all at once
C. Each letter requires its own cup of water
D. The priest cannot write and erase at the same time
Correct: B — The Torah requires 'all this law' — the entire passage must be written in full and then erased together, not letter by letter.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In Rava's chain of dilemmas about two scrolls for two women, what is the final question?
A. Can two women drink from the same cup?
B. If the mixed water is re-divided into two cups, is there retroactive clarification (בררה)?
C. Can one priest write both scrolls simultaneously?
D. Must the erasure water be from a specific spring?
Correct: B — The chain escalates: one cup → two cups mixed → re-divided. The final question is whether בררה (retroactive clarification) can establish that each woman drank 'her own' water. All remain תיקו.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, what is the correct formula for an oath with a curse?
A. I adjure you that if you were defiled, curses will come upon you
B. I adjure you that you were not defiled — and separately, curses upon the guilty
C. I adjure you that you were not defiled, and that if you were defiled, all these curses will come upon you
D. I curse you if you were defiled, and I place you under oath
Correct: C — Rav Ashi requires both elements integrated: the oath of innocence ('you were not defiled') AND the conditional curse ('if you were, curses come') in a single statement.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why do the Sages of Eretz Yisrael reject Rav Hamnuna's ruling about a shomeret yavam?
A. They hold adultery by a shomeret yavam does not forbid her to the yavam
B. They hold the yavam has no standing to bring a sota claim
C. They hold the sota ritual doesn't apply before yibum
D. They hold the shomeret yavam is already divorced
Correct: A — The Sages of Eretz Yisrael rule that a shomeret yavam who commits adultery does NOT become forbidden to the yavam — contrary to Rav Hamnuna. The Mishna's position follows Rabbi Akiva's stricter view.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the Baraita clarifying Rabbi Meir, what happens if a sota who drank the water later becomes defiled?
A. She must drink the water again for the new offense
B. The water she already drank activates and destabilizes her at that future time
C. The original oath is automatically void
D. Her husband must bring her back to the Temple for a new ceremony
Correct: B — The Baraita clarifies that the water doesn't judge future actions now — rather, it lies dormant and activates later if she becomes defiled, destabilizing her at that future point.
