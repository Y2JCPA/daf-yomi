Why Did Pinehas Lead the War Against Midian?

Tanna
It was not for nothing that Pinehas specifically went to war — he came to exact judgment for his ancestor Joseph, whom the Midianites sold into Egypt.
Gemara
But wasn't Pinehas from the family of Yitro (Putiel), who fattened calves for idol worship?
Resolution
His wife came from two 'daughters of Putiel' — one line from Joseph (who pitpet/mocked his desire), one from Yitro (who pittem/fattened calves).
Gemara
The language is precise: 'from the daughters of Putiel' — plural, implying descent from two men called Putiel.
Wordplay Putiel = pit·tem (fattened for idols — Yitro) + pit·pet (overcame desire — Joseph). Both meanings embedded in one name.

Three Military Exemptions: House, Vineyard, Wife

ExemptionVerseIncludes
🏠 Built a HouseDeut. 20:5Built, purchased, inherited, or received as gift — even a storehouse, barn, shed, or warehouse
🍇 Planted a VineyardDeut. 20:6Planted, layered, grafted, purchased, inherited, or received as gift — even 5 fruit trees of different species
💍 Betrothed a WifeDeut. 20:7Betrothed a virgin, a widow, or even a yevama waiting for levirate marriage
Common Thread In each case the Torah says 'lest he die and another man' complete his milestone — the exemption protects against the anguish of unfulfilled beginnings.

Who Returns vs. Who Does NOT Return

✅ Returns Home

  • Built a storehouse, barn, shed, or warehouse
  • Planted 5+ fruit trees (even mixed species)
  • Betrothed a virgin or widow
  • Yevama waiting for levirate marriage
  • Purchased, inherited, or received as gift

❌ Does NOT Return

  • Built only a gateway, veranda, or balcony
  • Planted only 4 fruit trees or 5+ non-fruit trees
  • Remarried his divorced wife
  • Betrothed a woman forbidden to him (e.g. widow to Kohen Gadol, mamzeret to Israelite)
  • R' Yehuda: Rebuilt house exactly as before
  • R' Eliezer: Built brick house in the Sharon (unstable)
R' Eliezer's View Brick houses in the Sharon region were known to collapse periodically and need rebuilding — so building one is not a true new beginning.

Key Distinction Rav Ḥisda: The mishna's grafting exemption refers to permitted grafting. R' Eliezer b. Ya'akov excludes forbidden grafting because the verse says 'and has not used the fruit thereof' — specifically lawful fruit.
