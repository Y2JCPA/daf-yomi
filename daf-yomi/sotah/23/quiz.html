Sotah 23 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What happens to the meal-offering of an Israelite woman married to a kohen?
A. It is eaten by the kohanim
B. It is burned entirely
C. The handful is sacrificed and the remainder scattered
D. It is returned to the woman
Correct: C — Because both she (non-kohen) and her husband (kohen) share the offering, it can neither be fully eaten nor fully burned. The kometz is sacrificed and the remainder is scattered on the ash heap.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why can a kohen not become disqualified (mechulal) through forbidden relations?
A. Because kedusha is permanent by rabbinic decree
B. Because the verse says 'his offspring' are disqualified, not he himself
C. Because only women can become chalalot
D. Because the Sanhedrin cannot revoke priestly status
Correct: B — Leviticus 21:15 states 'He shall not disqualify his offspring' — the Torah specifies that his children become chalalim, but the kohen himself retains his sanctity.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does R' Eliezer permit burning the remainder of the offering on the altar?
A. He holds the prohibition of lo taktiru doesn't apply to mincha offerings
B. He allows burning it as firewood rather than as a sacrificial offering
C. He says the wife's share transforms it into a regular offering
D. He permits it based on a special enactment for sota cases
Correct: B — R' Eliezer distinguishes between burning 'for a pleasing aroma' (forbidden) and burning 'as firewood' (permitted). The prohibition only applies when one burns it as an offering.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the source for the halakha that a man can vow his minor son to be a nazir but a woman cannot?
A. A verse in Bamidbar about nazirite vows
B. A gezeira shava linking father to son
C. A halakha l'Moshe mi'Sinai — oral tradition from Sinai
D. A rabbinic enactment from the Sanhedrin
Correct: C — R' Yochanan states that both this halakha and the rule about shaving with a father's nazirite offerings are halakha l'Moshe mi'Sinai — traditions transmitted orally from Sinai with no biblical derivation.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why is a man stoned without clothing but a woman is not?
A. The Rabbis enacted this for modesty based on sevara alone
B. Leviticus 24:14 says 'stone him' — 'him' excludes his garment; 'him' also implies: not her without her garment
C. Deuteronomy explicitly states women must be covered
D. It is a dispute and the halakha follows the lenient opinion
Correct: B — The word 'oto' (him) in 'let the congregation stone him' teaches two things: (1) 'him' without his garment, and (2) specifically 'him' — but a woman is stoned with her garment.
