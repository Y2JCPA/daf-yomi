Sotah 32 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, which of these must be recited specifically in Hebrew?
A. The Amida prayer
B. The Shema
C. The ḥalitza recitation
D. Grace after Meals
Correct: C — The Mishna lists ḥalitza among the recitations that must be in Hebrew, derived from 'she shall speak and say' linked to the Levites' 'speak and say' via gezera shava. Shema, Amida, and Grace after Meals may all be recited in any language.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Abaye reject Rav Zevid's attempt to require Hebrew for the declaration of tithes?
A. The tithes verse uses 'speak and say' which means any language
B. One derives gezera shava from 'saying' alone to 'saying' alone — not from 'speaking and saying'
C. The declaration of tithes is only rabbinic, not biblical
D. Rav Zevid's gezera shava was not received from his teacher
Correct: B — Abaye's principle is that a gezera shava links matching verbal forms: 'saying' alone (tithes) is linked to 'saying' alone (sota = any language), not to 'speaking and saying' (Levites = Hebrew).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why must the priest explain to the sota the circumstances under which the bitter water will NOT affect her?
A. To give her a chance to confess
B. To frighten her into admitting guilt
C. To prevent people from casting aspersions on the bitter water if it doesn't work
D. Because the Torah requires the priest to review all the laws of sota
Correct: C — If a woman who sinned unwittingly or under coercion drinks and is unaffected, people might doubt the water's power. By explaining these exemptions beforehand, the sanctity and reputation of the bitter water is preserved.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
The Gemara corrects R' Shimon ben Yoḥai's teaching about speaking loudly. What is the corrected version?
A. One should say both praise and discredit in a loud voice
B. One should publicize one's pain loudly so the community prays for him — not one's sins
C. One should always whisper, whether praise or discredit
D. One should speak loudly only when reciting Torah portions
Correct: B — The Gemara resolves the contradiction with R' Shimon b. Yoḥai's own teaching about whispering prayer: the 'loud voice' applies to publicizing suffering (to invite communal prayer), not to publicizing sin (which should be whispered to preserve dignity).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara answer the objection that sin-offerings (female) and burnt-offerings (male) are visibly distinguishable?
A. Both offerings could use either male or female animals
B. The animals were covered so no one could see them
C. Only the priest performing the service can tell — the general public cannot
D. The distinction between male and female animals was abolished
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that while the priest up close can distinguish the offerings, the general public observing from a distance cannot tell whether someone is bringing a sin-offering or burnt-offering, thus preserving the sinner's dignity.
