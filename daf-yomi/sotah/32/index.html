The Great Language Divide: Hebrew vs. Any Language

Any Language 🌍

  • Sota warning & oath
  • Declaration of tithes
  • Shema
  • Amida prayer
  • Grace after Meals
  • Oath of testimony
  • Oath on a deposit

Hebrew Only 🕎

  • First fruits (Bikkurim)
  • Ḥalitza recitation
  • Blessings & curses (Gerizim/Ebal)
  • Priestly Benediction
  • High Priest's Yom Kippur blessing
  • King's Torah reading (Hakhel)
  • Eglah Arufa portion
  • Anointed priest's war address
Key Principle The Torah uses "speaking and saying" (v'anu v'amru) to indicate Hebrew is required via gezera shava from the Levites at Gerizim/Ebal. Where only "saying" appears, any language suffices.

How Do We Derive the Language for Tithes?

Rav Zevid
Why not derive the declaration of tithes from "the Levites shall speak and say" (Deut. 27:14)? That would require Hebrew!
Abaye
We derive a gezera shava from "saying" alone to another instance of "saying" alone — not from a verse that uses "speaking and saying."
Rav Zevid
What is the significance of that distinction?
Abaye
R' Shimon ben Yoḥai teaches: "Saying" alone = soft voice (one's own praise, like tithes). "Speaking and saying" = loud voice (one's pain/discredit, like Bikkurim recounting hardships).
Principle The verbal form matters: אמירה (saying) alone vs. דיבור ואמירה (speaking and saying) are treated as distinct categories for gezera shava derivations.

The Ceremony at Mount Gerizim & Mount Ebal

🏔️ Israel crosses the Jordan and arrives at the two mountains near Shechem
↓
👥 Six tribes ascend Gerizim, six tribes ascend Ebal
↓
📦 Priests, Levites, and the Ark stand in the valley between
↓
✨ Levites face Gerizim: "Blessed be the man who does not make a graven image" — Both sides answer Amen
↓
⚡ Levites face Ebal: "Cursed be the man who makes a graven image" — Both sides answer Amen
↓
🔄 Continue through all blessings and curses in this pattern
Location The Mishna identifies these mountains as being in Samaria, near Shechem, proven by linking "oaks of Moreh" (Deut. 11:30) to Abraham's journey to Shechem (Gen. 12:6).

What the Priest Explains to the Sota

Priest addresses the sota in any language she understands
↓
Why she drinks
Because of the warning and subsequent seclusion
From what vessel
A mekeida — a simple clay vessel
What defiled her
Matters of levity and immaturity
How she was defiled
Unwitting vs. intentional, coerced vs. willing
↓
Purpose: Not to cast aspersions on the bitter water — if she sinned unwittingly or under coercion, the water won't affect her
Why Explain? If the woman drinks and nothing happens, people might doubt the water's power. By explaining that coercion or accident exempts her, the sanctity of the bitter water is preserved.

Loud Voice or Whisper? Dignity vs. Publicizing Pain

R' Shimon b. Yoḥai
Say your praise softly (derived from tithes — just "say"). Say your discredit loudly (derived from Bikkurim — "speak and say").
Gemara
But R' Shimon b. Yoḥai himself says the Amida is whispered so as not to embarrass sinners! The sin-offering and burnt-offering are slaughtered in the same place for this reason!
Gemara (corrected)
Correction: Don't say one should state discredit loudly. Rather, one should publicize one's pain loudly — so that the community will pray for mercy on his behalf.
Proof
A leper must cry "Impure! Impure!" (Lev. 13:45) — announcing his pain so the public prays for him.
Distinction Sin → whisper (protect dignity). Suffering → announce (invite communal prayer). The Bikkurim recitation recounts national suffering, not personal sin.
