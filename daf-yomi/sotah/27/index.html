Sotah 27 — Mutual Standards & Parallel Consequences

DAF OVERVIEW This daf explores symmetry in the sota process: the water tests both the woman and her paramour; disqualifications that apply to the wife also apply to the husband; and the court can step in to warn when the husband cannot. The daf concludes with a series of teachings from the famous day R' Elazar ben Azarya was appointed Nasi.

Marry a Suspected Woman or Her Daughter?

First Opinion
Better to marry the duma (suspected woman) herself — she comes from unflawed seed
R' Yoḥanan
Better to marry her daughter — the daughter has a ḥezkat kashrut (presumption of virtue), but the duma does not
Rava
The baraita saying one 'may marry' a duma is imprecise — it means: if he already married her, she is permitted. It does not endorse it ab initio
Rav Taḥalifa
Halakha follows R' Yoḥanan: most intercourse is attributed to the husband (rov be'ilot min ha'ba'al), so her daughter's lineage is unflawed
HALAKHA The law follows R' Yoḥanan — marry the daughter, not the suspected woman herself, since children are presumed to be from the husband.

Rav Amram's Dilemma: The Extremely Promiscuous Woman

A married woman is extremely promiscuous — can we still attribute most intercourse to her husband?
↓
Conception near menstruation
Husband doesn't know the timing → cannot watch her → children's lineage is uncertain
Conception near immersion
Husband knows the timing → perhaps he watches her? Or perhaps she is too promiscuous to be watched?
↓
⚠️ TEIKU — The dilemma remains unresolved
KEY CONCEPT Even when the general rule favors the husband, extreme circumstances can cast doubt on the children's lineage.

Disqualifications Apply to Both Husband & Wife

Wife Cannot Drink If She Is…

  • 🚫 Blind — 'hidden from the eyes' requires sight
  • 🚫 Lame — 'shall stand the woman' requires standing
  • 🚫 Without hands — offering must be 'placed in her hands'
  • 🚫 Mute — 'the woman shall say Amen' requires speech

Husband Cannot Administer If He Is…

  • 🚫 Blind — same verse applies symmetrically (Rav Sheshet)
  • 🚫 Lame — same verse applies symmetrically (Rav Ashi)
  • 🚫 Without hands — same verse applies symmetrically (Rav Ashi)
  • 🚫 Mute — same verse applies symmetrically (Mar bar Rav Ashi)
SOURCE Numbers 5:29 — 'This is the law of jealousy' compares the man to the woman and the woman to the man.

The Sota Water Tests Both Parties

🌊 The water 'enters into her' is stated TWICE (Num 5:24 & 5:27)
↓
🔍 Double mention teaches: water evaluates BOTH her and the paramour
↓
🚫 She becomes forbidden to her HUSBAND after defilement
↓
🚫 The extra vav in 've-nitma'a' (5:29) teaches she is ALSO forbidden to the PARAMOUR
↓
📖 R' Akiva, Zekharya ben HaKatzav, and Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi each derive this from different sources
MISHNA Just as the water evaluates her, it evaluates him. Just as she is forbidden to her husband, she is forbidden to her paramour.
