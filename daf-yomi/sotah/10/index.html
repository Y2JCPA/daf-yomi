Sotah 10: Samson's Rise & Fall / Tamar's Righteousness

CORE THEME This daf examines Samson's extraordinary qualities and downfall — how divine-like gifts become the instruments of punishment when misused — then pivots to Tamar's self-sacrifice, teaching that it is better to be cast into a furnace than to publicly humiliate another person.
3 / 9

Why Did Samson Choose Foxes?

Gemara
Samson caught 300 foxes and tied torches between their tails to burn Philistine fields. Why specifically foxes?
R' Ḥiyya bar Abba
The fox walks backwards when trying to escape — let the animal that goes in reverse punish the Philistines who reneged on Abimelech's oath to Abraham.
R' Shimon the Pious
Samson's shoulders were 60 cubits wide, matching the gates of Gaza that he carried to Hebron (Judges 16:3).
Rav
Samson judged Israel for 22 years without ever asking anyone to even move a stick for him — total selflessness.
PRINCIPLE Samson's choice of foxes was not arbitrary — it was midda k'neged midda (measure for measure) against those who went back on their word.

R' Yoḥanan's Teachings on Samson

R' Yoḥanan: Samson judged Israel as God does — 'Dan shall judge his people as One' (Gen. 49:16)
↓
Samson's name (Shimshon) is akin to God's name — 'The Lord God is a sun (shemesh) and shield' (Ps. 84:12)
↓
Not Literally God's Name
His name is not sacred and may be erased — it is merely akin to God's name
The Parallel
As God protects the whole world, Samson protected all Israel in his generation
ALSO R' Yoḥanan: Balaam was lame in one leg (shefi). Samson was lame in both — from shefifon (Gen. 49:17), a doubled shefi.

Tamar's Modesty — Why Judah Didn't Recognize Her

The Question

  • Judah 'thought her a prostitute for she covered her face' (Gen. 38:15)
  • But prostitutes typically uncover their faces!
  • How does covering her face make her seem like a prostitute?

R' Elazar's Answer

  • Tamar always covered her face in her father-in-law Judah's home out of modesty
  • So when she sat uncovered at the crossroads, Judah didn't recognize her
  • The verse means: because she had been modest, he never saw her face

R' Shmuel bar Naḥmani

  • A daughter-in-law who is modest in her father-in-law's home merits kings and prophets
  • From Tamar came King David and Isaiah the prophet
  • Amoz (Isaiah's father) and Amaziah (king of Judah) were brothers — both from David's line
TRADITION R' Levi: We have a tradition — Amoz and Amaziah were brothers. Isaiah was therefore royalty, and both lines trace back to Tamar.

Tamar's Signs & the Angelic Struggle

📜 Tamar is brought to trial — she sends Judah's signet, cords, and staff as proof
↓
😈 The angel Samael distances the signs — trying to prevent Judah's admission and David's lineage
↓
😇 The angel Gabriel brings the signs back — ensuring the truth is revealed
↓
🕊️ 'Yonat eilem reḥokim' (Ps. 56:1) — 'the mute dove whose signs were distanced' alludes to Tamar
↓
🔥 Tamar refuses to name Judah directly — 'By the man whose these are, am I with child'
↓
✅ Judah acknowledges: 'She is more righteous than I' (Gen. 38:26)
CRITICAL TEACHING Better to throw oneself into a fiery furnace than to publicly humiliate another person — derived from Tamar, who risked being burned rather than shame Judah.
