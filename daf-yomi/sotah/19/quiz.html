Sotah 19 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to ALL three opinions in the baraita, in which scenario does the sotah definitely drink and repeat?
A. One husband, one paramour
B. One husband, two paramours
C. Two husbands, two paramours
D. Two husbands, one paramour
Correct: C — All three Tannaim agree that with two different husbands and two different paramours, the woman drinks and repeats. This is the case included by 'the law of jealousy' even according to the most restrictive view (the Rabbis).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara derive that the WOMAN (not just the priest) must participate in waving the sotah's meal-offering?
A. From the word 'this' in Numbers 5:29
B. From a gezeira shava of 'hand' linking the sotah to the peace-offering
C. From an explicit verse in Numbers 5:25
D. From a kal va-chomer from the meal-offering of a nazir
Correct: B — R' Yoshiya derives it through a verbal analogy (gezeira shava): 'hand' in the sotah passage (Num. 5:25) is linked to 'hand' in the peace-offering (Lev. 7:30). Just as the owner waves the peace-offering, so the sotah waves her meal-offering — with the priest placing his hand beneath hers.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rav Ashi say the requirement of 'scroll erasure before drinking' comes to teach?
A. That the scroll must be written in a specific ink
B. That if ink impression is still visible on the parchment, she may not yet drink
C. That the scroll must be erased into wine, not water
D. That a new scroll must be written for each drinking
Correct: B — Rav Ashi explains that the halakha about scroll erasure is needed for a case where the scroll was erased but the impression of the ink is still discernible — the woman may not drink until the writing is totally dissolved.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Akiva (as reinterpreted by the Gemara), when can the sotah NO LONGER retract her decision to drink?
A. Once the oath is administered
B. Once the scroll is written
C. Once the handful of the meal-offering is sacrificed
D. Once the water touches her lips
Correct: C — The Gemara reinterprets R' Akiva: before the handful is sacrificed, she can retract (and her refusal counts as an admission of guilt). But once the handful is sacrificed on the altar, she cannot retract — and if she refuses out of fear, she is forced to drink.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say that a woman who refuses to drink AFTER the handful is sacrificed is forced to drink, even though refusal normally indicates guilt?
A. Because the priest has already invested effort in the ritual
B. Because the meal-offering would be wasted
C. Because her refusal at that point may stem from fear rather than guilt
D. Because the Torah explicitly commands forced drinking in all cases
Correct: C — The Gemara distinguishes: after the handful is sacrificed, the woman's refusal may be due to fear of the ordeal rather than an admission of guilt. Since she might actually be innocent and merely frightened, she is compelled to drink so the water can properly evaluate her.
