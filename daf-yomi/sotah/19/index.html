Sotah 19: The Ritual Order & When She Drinks Again

🔁 Gemara clarifies four combinations of husband/paramour for repeat drinking
↓
🌾 Meal-offering: placed in service vessel → woman's hand → priest waves with her
↓
⚖️ Rabbis vs. R' Shimon: Does she drink before or after the meal-offering is sacrificed?
↓
📜 R' Shimon: Three things must precede drinking — handful, scroll erasure, and oath
↓
💪 Once scroll is erased, she can be forced to drink — but R' Akiva qualifies this
KEY THEME The Gemara dissects three nearly identical verses about making the sotah drink, deriving from each a distinct halakha about the ritual's sequence and enforceability.

The Sotah Meal-Offering Procedure (Mishna)

🧺 Meal-offering taken from Egyptian wicker basket
↓
🏺 Placed into a service vessel (כלי שרת)
↓
✋ Placed on the woman's hand; priest places his hand beneath hers
↓
🌊 Priest and woman wave the offering together (תנופה)
↓
📐 Brought near to the southwest corner of the altar
↓
🔥 Priest removes a handful (קמיצה) and burns it on the altar
↓
🍞 Remainder eaten by the priests
GEZEIRA SHAVA R' Yoshiya derives that the owner (the woman) must also wave from a verbal analogy: "hand" in the sotah passage (Num. 5:25) is linked to "hand" in the peace-offering (Lev. 7:30) — just as the owner waves the shelamim, so the sotah waves her mincha.

Drink First or Sacrifice First?

Rabbis (Tanna Kamma)
The priest forces her to drink first, and afterward sacrifices the meal-offering.
R' Shimon
The priest sacrifices the meal-offering first, and afterward forces her to drink — as it says: "and burn it upon the altar, and afterward he shall make the woman drink" (Num. 5:26).
Rabbis (Tanna Kamma)
The verse in 5:24 — "And he shall make the woman drink" — comes before the meal-offering passage, teaching the proper order: drink first.
R' Shimon
That first verse teaches only that if he did make her drink first, it is valid after the fact (בדיעבד).
Rabbis (Tanna Kamma)
The Torah does not begin a discussion with a rule that applies only after the fact!
AGREEMENT R' Shimon concedes: if the priest did make her drink first and only then sacrificed the meal-offering, it is still valid (כשר).

Three Verses About Drinking — Three Halakhot

Three times the Torah says 'make her drink':
① Num. 5:24 · ② Num. 5:26 · ③ Num. 5:27
↓
Rabbis' Reading
Verse ①: Teaches proper order — drink before meal-offering
Verse ②: Scroll ink must be completely erased before drinking
Verse ③: If scroll already erased and she refuses — she is forced to drink
R' Shimon's Reading
Verse ②: Teaches proper order — meal-offering before drinking
Verse ①: If she drank first — still valid (בדיעבד)
Verse ③: If scroll already erased and she refuses — she is forced to drink
NOTE Both opinions agree on verse ③: once the scroll is erased into the water, the woman can be compelled to drink against her will.

R' Shimon's Three Prerequisites Before Drinking

1. Handful Sacrificed (קמיצה)

  • R' Shimon holds: sacrifice meal-offering FIRST
  • Consistent with his ruling in the Mishna
  • Derived from 'and afterward he shall make the woman drink' (5:26)

2. Scroll Fully Erased (מחיקה)

  • Obvious: what would she drink without the erased ink?
  • Rav Ashi: needed for where ink impression is still VISIBLE
  • Must be totally dissolved — no discernible writing remains

3. Oath Accepted (שבועה)

  • She must accept the oath upon herself before drinking
  • Rava: scroll written BEFORE oath is completely INVALID
  • The oath is a prerequisite even for WRITING the scroll
RAVA'S PRINCIPLE A scroll of a sotah written before she accepted the oath is entirely invalid — the scribe 'did nothing.'
