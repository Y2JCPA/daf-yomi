The Kohen Mashuach Milchama: Address to the Troops

📜 The priest approaches the people and speaks in Hebrew (lashon hakodesh)
↓
⚔️ 'Against your enemies' — not your brothers; enemies show no mercy
↓
💛 'Let not your heart faint' — against the neighing of horses
↓
😨 'Fear not' — against the clashing of shields (terisin) and boots (calgassin)
↓
📢 'Nor be alarmed' — against the sound of trumpets
↓
🔊 'Do not be terrified' — against the sound of battle shouts
↓
🙏 'For Hashem goes with you' — referring to the Aron HaKodesh in the camp
Derivation How do we know the address is in Hebrew? A gezeirah shavah: 'And speak' (Deut. 20:2) is linked to 'Moses spoke' (Exodus 19:19) — just as Sinai was in the sacred tongue, so too the war address

Goliath vs. David: Two Champions

Goliath — Flesh and Blood

  • Stood with brazenness (gilui panim) before God
  • His own mouth foretold his defeat three times
  • Came morning & evening to disrupt Shema
  • Appeared for 40 days — opposing the 40 days of Torah
  • Championed by mortal strength alone

David — The Name of God

  • Called ben ish — God felled Goliath through 'the son of a man'
  • Responded: 'I come in the name of Hashem Tzeva'ot'
  • Championed by the Omnipresent
  • Goliath fell by the sword, and the Philistines fell with him
  • Fulfilled the priest's promise: God fights for Israel
R' Yochanan / R' Meir In three statements, Goliath's own mouth ensnared him — he unwittingly prophesied his own defeat

School of R' Sheila

R' Yochanan
Bein — Born from among many fathers; son of 100 pappi and one nanai
Linguistic Analysis Each Amora derives meaning from the root ב-נ-ה (build) or בין (between) — revealing Goliath's nature through his name
