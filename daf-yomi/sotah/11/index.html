Sotah 11: Egypt's Enslavement & Israel's Redemption

בשכר נשים צדקניות שהיו באותו הדור נגאלו ישראל ממצרים
Core Theme The Gemara traces the arc from divine justice through Egypt's enslavement to miraculous redemption — showing that God's measure of reward always exceeds His measure of punishment, and that the righteous women of that generation merited Israel's deliverance.

Miriam's Reward: Same Measure, Greater Portion?

Mishnah
Miriam waited one hour for baby Moses — so Israel waited seven days for her. The same is so with reward for good deeds.
Gemara
But these aren't comparable! She waited one hour, they waited seven days!
Abaye
The Mishnah should read: With reward it is not so — reward may be greater than the deed.
Rava
No — the Mishnah is correct. The manner (מידה) is the same, but the measure of good is always greater than the measure of punishment.
Principle מידה טובה מרובה ממידת פורענות — The measure of reward always exceeds the measure of punishment.

"A New King Over Egypt" — Was Pharaoh Actually New?

Rav
He was literally a new king — because the verse says "new" (חדש).
Shmuel
It was the same king with new decrees — because it does not say the old king died and a new one reigned.
Gemara
According to Shmuel, "who knew not Joseph" means he acted as if he didn't know Joseph — though he certainly did.
Key Insight Pharaoh said "lo" (לו) — singular — not "lahem" (plural). R' Ḥama b. R' Ḥanina: He was scheming against the Savior of Israel, i.e., God Himself.

What Does "Befarekh" (בפרך) Mean?

R' Elazar: בפה רך — Soft Mouth

  • Egyptians enticed Jews into labor
  • Gradual subjugation — started gently
  • By the time they realized, freedom was lost
  • Applies to the first verse (Ex 1:13)

R' Shmuel b. Naḥmani: בפריכה — Crushing

  • Backbreaking physical labor
  • Mortar, brick, and field work
  • Men given women's tasks and vice versa
  • Applies to the second verse (Ex 1:14)
R' Yonatan They exchanged men's and women's work — forcing everyone to do labor to which they were unaccustomed.

The Righteous Women: How They Redeemed Israel

🏺 Women went to draw water — God sent small fish into their pitchers
↓
🔥 They prepared two pots: one of hot water to bathe husbands, one of fish to feed them
↓
🌾 They went out to the fields to care for their exhausted husbands
↓
🍎 When pregnant, they gave birth in the field under apple trees (Song of Songs 8:5)
↓
👼 An angel cleaned the newborns like a midwife; two stones provided oil and honey
↓
🌱 Egyptians plowed over hidden babies — they sprouted like grass (Ezekiel 16:7)
↓
🐑 Children came home in flocks and later recognized God first at the Sea: "This is my God!"
Rav Avira בשכר נשים צדקניות שהיו באותו הדור נגאלו ישראל ממצרים — In the merit of the righteous women, Israel was redeemed from Egypt.
