Sotah 40: Congregational Responses & Humility

DAF OVERVIEW This daf explores what the congregation says during the Priestly Benediction and the Modim prayer, featuring a rich debate among Amoraim. It then presents three unforgettable stories of Rabbi Abbahu's humility, before transitioning to the Yom Kippur Torah reading ceremony of the High Priest — including the chain of honor in passing the scroll and the question of where the reading takes place.

When Does the Congregation Recite Psalms 128 During Birkat Kohanim?

Rav Yosef
The congregation recites the verses of Psalms 128 between each blessing of the Priestly Benediction.
Rav Sheshet
They are said during the mention of God's name in each of the three blessings.
One opinion
The congregation recites one verse at a time, corresponding to each verse the Kohanim recite.
Other opinion
For every single verse the Kohanim recite, the congregation says all three verses.
R' Ḥiyya bar Abba
Anyone who recites these verses outside the Temple is simply mistaken.
R' Aḥa bar Ḥanina
One must recite them even outside the Temple — is there a servant being blessed whose face doesn't brighten?
RESOLUTION Rabbi Abbahu initially recited these verses, but stopped when he saw that Rabbi Abba of Akko did not say them.

Three Stories of Rabbi Abbahu's Humility

📖 The Interpreter's Wife boasts that her husband doesn't need Rabbi Abbahu. Rabbi Abbahu's wife reports this to him.
↓
🤷 Rabbi Abbahu responds: "What difference does it make? Through me and through him, the One above will be exalted."
↓
🏛️ The Sages decide to appoint Rabbi Abbahu as Rosh Yeshiva.
↓
💰 He declines, saying Rabbi Abba of Akko is greater — really to help him escape his creditors.
↓
📢 Rabbi Abbahu teaches Aggada; R' Ḥiyya bar Abba teaches Halakha. Everyone flocks to Aggada.
↓
💎 Rabbi Abbahu consoles R' Ḥiyya: "You sell precious stones; I sell small items (sidkit). Who do customers flock to?"
INSIGHT Rabbi Abbahu consistently elevated others above himself — deflecting honor, yielding positions, and comforting a colleague whose audience he had drawn away.

Modim d'Rabbanan — What Does the Congregation Say?

SageFormulation
RavWe give thanks to You… for the merit of giving thanks to You
ShmuelGod of all living flesh, for the merit of giving thanks to You
Rabbi SimaiOur Creator, Who created everything in the beginning, for giving thanks
Sages of Neharde'aBlessings and praises to Your great name, for You have given us life and sustained us
Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akovGive us life, show favor, collect us and gather our exiles to Your courtyards
Rav PappaCombine them all together and recite everything
HALAKHA Rav Pappa's ruling to combine all formulations is the basis for the Modim d'Rabbanan prayer we recite today.

Temple vs. Outlying Areas: Responding to Blessings

🏛️ In the Temple

  • God's full Name (Shem HaMeforash) is pronounced
  • People respond: "Barukh Shem Kevod Malkhuto"
  • One does NOT answer Amen
  • Source: Nehemiah 9:5 — "Stand up and bless the Lord"
  • No pause between blessings

🏘️ Outside the Temple

  • God's Name is replaced with a kinui (substitute)
  • People respond: Amen
  • Each blessing is a separate unit
  • Congregation may recite Psalms verses (disputed)
  • Pauses between blessings for Amen
SOURCE "Blessed be Your glorious name, which is exalted above all blessing and praise" (Nehemiah 9:5) — for each blessing, give Him praise.

The Yom Kippur Torah Reading: Chain of Honor

📜 Synagogue attendant (ḥazzan) takes the Torah scroll
↓
Gives it to the Rosh HaKnesset (head of the synagogue)
↓
Gives it to the Segan (deputy High Priest)
↓
Gives it to the Kohen Gadol (High Priest)
↓
Does this prove honor may be shown to a student before his teacher?
Suggested
Yes — others are honored even in the High Priest's presence
Abaye
No — the entire chain is itself for the honor of the High Priest
KEY PRINCIPLE The ascending chain of dignitaries demonstrates the Kohen Gadol's supreme status — it is honor, not a challenge to his authority.
