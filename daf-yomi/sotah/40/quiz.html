Sotah 40 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, what should one do with the different formulations of Modim d'Rabbanan?
A. Choose the version of one's teacher
B. Recite only Rav's version
C. Combine all formulations together
D. Alternate between them on different days
Correct: C — Rav Pappa ruled that since each Sage added a different element, they should all be combined into one prayer — the basis of our Modim d'Rabbanan today.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Abbahu compare himself to a seller of small items (sidkit) and Rabbi Ḥiyya bar Abba to a seller of precious stones?
A. To insult Rabbi Ḥiyya's teaching style
B. To explain why crowds preferred Aggada over Halakha — accessibility, not importance
C. To argue that Halakha is less valuable
D. To convince Rabbi Ḥiyya to switch to teaching Aggada
Correct: B — Rabbi Abbahu humbly suggested that his Aggada was like cheap goods that attract crowds, while Rabbi Ḥiyya's Halakha was like precious stones — more valuable but attracting fewer buyers.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does one not answer 'Amen' in the Temple?
A. It was considered disrespectful to the Kohen Gadol
B. The verse in Nehemiah 9:5 prescribes 'Blessed be Your glorious name' instead
C. Amen was only instituted after the Temple's destruction
D. The acoustics of the Temple made Amen inaudible
Correct: B — The Gemara derives from Nehemiah 9:5 that in the Temple, one responds with 'Barukh Shem Kevod Malkhuto' rather than Amen.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What did Abaye say about the chain of people passing the Torah scroll to the High Priest?
A. It proves one may honor a student before his teacher
B. The entire process is itself for the honor of the High Priest
C. It was unnecessary and merely ceremonial
D. Each person in the chain must read a verse before passing it
Correct: B — Abaye rejected the proof that one may honor a student before his teacher, arguing that the ascending chain of dignitaries was entirely designed to demonstrate the High Priest's supreme honor.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How did Rabbi Abbahu respond when his wife told him the interpreter's wife claimed her husband didn't need him?
A. He fired the interpreter immediately
B. He said 'Through me and through him the One above will be exalted'
C. He challenged the interpreter to a public debate
D. He reported the matter to the Rosh Yeshiva
Correct: B — Rabbi Abbahu's humble response was that it doesn't matter who gets the credit — what matters is that God is exalted through both of their teaching.
