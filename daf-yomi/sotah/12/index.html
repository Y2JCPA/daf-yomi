Caleb's Appellations — Character Revealed Through Names

Names from I Chronicles 2:18

  • Jesher (ישר) — He set himself straight (yisher), refusing to join the spies' counsel
  • Shobab (שובב) — He broke (sibbev) his evil inclination by rebelling against the spies
  • Ardon (ארדון) — He ruled (rada) over his evil inclination

Names from I Chronicles 4:5

  • Ashhur (אשחור) — His face became blackened (husheḥaru) from fasting to resist the spies
  • Father of — He became like a father to Miriam through his care
  • Tekoa (תקוע) — He attached (taka) his heart to his Father in Heaven
🔑 Key Idea The verse says "these were her sons (vaneha)" — read as boneha, her builders. Caleb's marriage to Miriam built her up, restoring her health and beauty.

Pharaoh's Three Escalating Decrees

📋 Decree 1: Commands midwives Shifrah & Puah to kill Jewish boys on the birthstool
↓
🌊 Decree 2: Orders all his people — "Every Jewish son born, cast into the Nile"
↓
⚠️ Decree 3: Extends the decree even to Egyptian infant boys
R' Yosei b. R' Ḥanina "Every son that is born" — even his own nation. Pharaoh's astrologers saw that Israel's savior would be stricken through water, so he cast the net wider and wider.

Miriam Rebukes Her Father Amram

Amram
Pharaoh has decreed that every son be cast into the Nile. We are laboring for nothing. I will divorce my wife.
Narrator
All of Israel followed Amram's example and divorced their wives.
Miriam
Father, your decree is harsher than Pharaoh's! He decreed only on the males — you decree on males and females!
Miriam
Pharaoh decrees only in this world. You decree in this world and the World-to-Come — the unborn cannot enter it!
Amram
He accepted her argument, remarried Yocheved, and all of Israel remarried their wives.
💡 Turning Point A young girl's moral clarity reversed a communal decision and made the birth of Moses possible.

What Does "Amatah" Mean? — Pharaoh's Daughter at the Nile

R' Yehuda
Amatah (אמתה) means her arm — as the word literally denotes her forearm. It miraculously extended many cubits to reach the basket.
R' Neḥemya
Amatah means her maidservant (ama) — because the verse didn't use the more common word yadah (her hand).
Gemara
But didn't Gabriel strike down all the maidservants who protested?
R' Neḥemya
Gabriel left her one maidservant — it is not proper for a princess to stand alone.
R' Yoḥanan Pharaoh's daughter came to the Nile to cleanse herself from her father's idols — she was immersing as part of conversion, as Isaiah 4:4 uses "washing" for spiritual purification.
