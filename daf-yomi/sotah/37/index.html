Sotah 37: Faith at the Sea, Covenants on the Mountains

R' Meir
The tribe of Benjamin descended into the sea first — as it says: 'There is Benjamin, the youngest, ruling them'
R' Yehuda
No — each tribe refused to go first. Then Nahshon ben Amminadab of Judah jumped in, and his whole tribe followed
R' Meir
Because Benjamin went first, the Temple was built in Benjamin's territory — 'He rests between his shoulders'
R' Yehuda
Because Judah went first, Judah merited kingship — 'Judah became His sanctuary… the sea saw it and fled'
Core Theme This daf explores who acted with faith at the Red Sea, how blessings and curses were proclaimed at Gerizim and Ebal, and the staggering number of covenants binding every Jew to every mitzva.

Who Entered the Sea First?

R' Meir
Benjamin went first — 'There is Benjamin the youngest, rodem' — read as red yam (descending into the sea)
R' Meir
The princes of Judah stoned them for going out of order — 'rigmatam' (their stoning)
R' Yehuda
Every tribe said: 'I'm not going first!' Then Nahshon ben Amminadab jumped in with all of Judah
R' Yehuda
Nahshon prayed as he sank: 'Save me, God, for the waters are come unto the soul… I am sunk in deep mire' (Psalms 69)
HaKadosh Baruch Hu
To Moshe: 'My beloved ones are drowning and you prolong your prayer?!' — Lift your rod and stretch out your hand!
Reward R' Meir: Benjamin hosted the Beit HaMikdash. R' Yehuda: Judah merited kingship over Israel.

Where Did the Levites Stand at Gerizim & Ebal?

R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov

  • The elders of the priesthood and Levites stood below between the mountains with the Ark
  • The rest of the Levites stood above on Mount Gerizim

R' Yoshiya

  • Levites fit to serve in the Temple stood below
  • Those too young or old to serve stood above on the mountain

Rebbi (R' Yehuda HaNasi)

  • Both Levites and Israelites stood below
  • They faced Gerizim for blessing, then faced Ebal for curse
  • 'Al' means adjacent to, not literally 'on' the mountain
Proof Rebbi proves 'al' means 'adjacent to' from the curtain before the Ark — it screened the Ark but was not placed on top of it (Exodus 40:3).

Structure of Each Mitzva's Covenants

Each mitzva includes 4 aspects:
Learn · Teach · Keep · Perform
↓
General blessing/curse + Particular blessing/curse = 8 per mitzva
↓
8 blessings + 8 curses = 16 covenants per mitzva at each location
↓
Tanna Kamma
3 locations:
Sinai · Gerizim/Ebal · Plains of Moab
= 48 covenants per mitzva
R' Shimon
3 locations:
Sinai · Tent of Meeting · Plains of Moab
= 48 covenants per mitzva
Key Dispute R' Shimon (following R' Akiva) excludes Gerizim/Ebal because only some mitzvot were mentioned there, and substitutes the Tent of Meeting.

Rules for Proclaiming Blessings & Curses

1️⃣ Blessing precedes each curse — one blessing, then one curse, alternating
↓
2️⃣ Both blessings and curses are recited by the Levites
↓
3️⃣ Both are proclaimed in a loud voice
↓
4️⃣ Both are proclaimed in Lashon HaKodesh (Hebrew)
↓
5️⃣ Both are stated in general and in detail
↓
6️⃣ After each, both groups on both mountains respond: Amen
Source The juxtaposition of 'blessing' and 'curse' in Deuteronomy 11:29 teaches that every rule applying to the curse applies equally to the blessing.
