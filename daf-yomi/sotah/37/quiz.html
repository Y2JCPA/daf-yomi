Sotah 37 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yehuda, who jumped into the Red Sea first?
A. The tribe of Benjamin
B. Nahshon ben Amminadab of Judah
C. Moshe Rabbeinu
D. The tribe of Reuben
Correct: B — R' Yehuda says each tribe refused to go first, until Nahshon ben Amminadab of Judah jumped in with his entire tribe. R' Meir is the one who says Benjamin went first.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What reward did the tribe of Judah receive for entering the sea first (according to R' Yehuda)?
A. The Temple was built in their territory
B. They received the priesthood
C. They merited to govern Israel (kingship)
D. They were given the largest portion of land
Correct: C — Because Judah went first into the sea, they merited kingship over Israel, as Psalms 114:2-3 states: 'Judah became His sanctuary… the sea saw it and fled.' The Temple being in their territory is the reward R' Meir gives to Benjamin.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rebbi (R' Yehuda HaNasi) interpret the word 'al' (על) in 'These shall stand on Mount Gerizim'?
A. Literally on top of the mountain
B. Adjacent to the mountain
C. Facing the mountain from across the valley
D. Surrounding the mountain
Correct: B — Rebbi argues that 'al' means 'adjacent to' — everyone stood below between the mountains. He proves this from the curtain before the Ark, where 'al' clearly means 'near' not 'on top of.'
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the practical difference between R' Shimon ben Yehuda and Rebbi regarding the number of covenants?
A. R' Shimon counts 3 locations, Rebbi counts only 2
B. Rebbi adds a layer of guarantor-for-the-guarantor, squaring the multiplier
C. Rebbi includes the Tent of Meeting but R' Shimon does not
D. There is no practical difference — they agree on the final number
Correct: B — Both agree on 48 × 603,550. But Rebbi adds the concept of arev for the arev — each Jew guarantees not only every other Jew's observance but also their responsibility as guarantors, multiplying by 603,550 again.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How were the blessings and curses proclaimed at Mount Gerizim and Mount Ebal?
A. All blessings first, then all curses
B. One blessing then one curse, alternating
C. Curses first to inspire repentance, then blessings
D. Blessings on day one, curses on day two
Correct: B — The singular form 'blessing' and 'curse' in Deuteronomy 11:29 teaches that one blessing preceded each corresponding curse in alternating fashion — not all blessings before all curses.
