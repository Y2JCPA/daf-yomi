Sotah 39 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava bar Rav Huna, what is prohibited once a Torah scroll is opened?
A. Sitting down
B. Conversation — even about halakha
C. Eating or drinking
D. Walking around the synagogue
Correct: B — Rava bar Rav Huna teaches that once a Torah scroll is opened, all conversation is forbidden — even discussing matters of halakha — as derived from the verse describing how the people stood in silence.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What must a kohen do before lifting his hands for Birkat Kohanim, according to R' Yehoshua ben Levi?
A. Fast for the day
B. Recite Shema
C. Wash his hands
D. Remove his shoes
Correct: C — R' Yehoshua ben Levi derives from Psalms 134:2 ('Lift up your hands in sanctity') that a kohen must sanctify — i.e., wash — his hands before the Priestly Benediction.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why must the caller wait until the congregation finishes 'Amen' to the thanksgiving blessing before announcing 'Kohanim'?
A. To give the kohanim time to reach the platform
B. So that everyone will hear the voices of the kohanim clearly
C. Because it is disrespectful to interrupt a blessing
D. To allow time for hand washing
Correct: B — The Gemara explicitly states the reason: to ensure that everyone will hear the voices of the priests. The sequential protocol prevents overlapping sacred speech.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which of the following was NOT one of R' Elazar ben Shammua's practices for longevity?
A. Never using a synagogue as a shortcut
B. Never stepping over people in the study hall
C. Always fasting on Mondays and Thursdays
D. Always reciting a blessing before Birkat Kohanim
Correct: C — R' Elazar ben Shammua listed three practices: not using a synagogue as a shortcut, not striding over people's heads, and always blessing before Birkat Kohanim. Fasting on Mondays and Thursdays was not mentioned.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the congregation recite Psalms 134 during Musaf instead of repeating Psalms 103 from Shacharit?
A. Psalms 134 is holier than Psalms 103
B. It is not appropriate to repeat the same verses of praise they already recited
C. Psalms 134 specifically mentions Shabbat
D. The kohanim requested different verses for each service
Correct: B — The Gemara asks what the congregation says during the second Birkat Kohanim at Musaf, noting that it is not appropriate to repeat the same verses of praise recited during Shacharit, so different Psalms are used.
