Silence When the Torah Is Opened

Rava bar Rav Huna
Once a Torah scroll is opened, it is prohibited to converse — even about a matter of halakha.
Rava bar Rav Huna
Proof: "When he opened it, all the people stood up" (Nehemiah 8:5) — standing means silence.
R' Zeira / Rav Ḥisda
The proof is from: "The ears of all the people were attentive to the book of the law" (Nehemiah 8:3) — they listened to no other voice.
Shared Ruling Both agree: when the Torah scroll is open, all conversation ceases — even halakhic discussion. They differ only on the scriptural source.

Priests Must Wash Before Blessing

שְׂאוּ יְדֵכֶם קֹדֶשׁ וּבָרְכוּ אֶת ה׳
R' Yehoshua ben Levi Any kohen who did not first wash his hands may not lift them for Birkat Kohanim.

Source: "Se'u yedekhem bakodesh u'varekhu et Hashem" — "Lift up your hands in sanctity and bless the Lord" (Psalms 134:2).

One must sanctify (wash) the hands before the benediction.

R' Elazar ben Shammua's Three Keys to Longevity

🚫 No Shortcut Through Shul

  • Never used a synagogue as a kappendarya (shortcut)
  • Treated the sacred space with full respect

🚶 No Striding Over Heads

  • Never stepped over people sitting in the study hall
  • Avoided appearing scornful of fellow scholars

🤲 Blessing Before Birkat Kohanim

  • Never lifted hands for the Priestly Benediction without first reciting a berakha
  • Always preceded sacred duty with proper preparation
Lesson Longevity comes from consistent reverence — toward sacred spaces, fellow Jews, and the mitzvot themselves.

The Kohen's Three Prayers for Birkat Kohanim

🚶 Walking to the platform: "May it be Your will… that this blessing shall contain no stumbling block or iniquity"
↓
🤲 Before reciting: "…Who has sanctified us with the sanctity of Aaron and commanded us to bless His people Israel with love"
↓
🔄 Turning away after: "Master of the Universe, we have performed what You decreed upon us — do unto us as You have promised"
↓
📖 Citing: "So shall they put My name upon the children of Israel, and I will bless them" (Numbers 6:27)
↓
📖 And: "Look forth from Your holy habitation… and bless Your people Israel" (Deut. 26:15)
R' Zeira / Rav Ḥisda The kohen's role is framed by humility: asking for Divine assistance before, during, and after the blessing.

Precise Sequence of Birkat Kohanim

Congregation finishes Amen to Modim (Thanksgiving blessing)
↓
Caller announces: "Kohanim!"
↓
Caller's voice finishes completely
↓
Kohanim recite first blessing word
↓
Blessing concludes from Kohanim's mouths
↓
Congregation responds "Amen"
↓
Amen concludes → Kohanim begin next blessing
↓
After final Amen: Kohanim may not turn away until Ḥazzan begins Sim Shalom
Key Principle No voice may begin until the previous voice has completely finished — ensuring every sacred word is heard distinctly by all.
