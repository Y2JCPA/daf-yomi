Sotah 47 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why was a forest needed for the miracle of the bears attacking the children who mocked Elisha?
A. To hide the bears from Elisha
B. Bears are frightened to attack in open areas
C. The forest was needed to trap the children
D. The children were playing in a forest already
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that bears are frightened in open areas and only attack in their natural habitat — a forest. So the forest was created to enable the bears to carry out the attack.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Ḥanina, why were specifically 42 children harmed in the Elisha incident?
A. There were 42 children present that day
B. It corresponded to the 42 journeys in the wilderness
C. It corresponded to Balak's 42 offerings
D. Elisha invoked the 42-letter Name of God
Correct: C — R' Ḥanina connects the 42 children to the 42 offerings Balak brought when trying to curse Israel through Balaam. Balak's intent was to harm Israel, so that curse eventually manifested.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What principle does the Gemara derive from Elisha's treatment of Gehazi?
A. A teacher must never rebuke a student
B. The left hand should push away and the right hand draw near
C. A student who sins should be permanently excommunicated
D. Rebuke must always be done publicly
Correct: B — The Baraita teaches: 'Always let the left hand push away and the right hand draw near' — even rebuke must leave room for the person to return. Elisha erred by pushing Gehazi away with both hands.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the eglah arufah laws, what happens if even one witness knows the identity of the killer?
A. The witness must bring the killer to trial before the ritual
B. The heifer's neck is still broken but the witness testifies separately
C. They do not break the heifer's neck at all
D. The witness replaces the elders in the ritual
Correct: C — The verse states 'It is not known who has smitten him' — if even one person anywhere in the world knows the killer's identity, the eglah arufah ritual is not performed.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does R' Neḥemya resolve the difficulty in the Mishna about contradicting witnesses in eglah arufah?
A. The Mishna deals with disqualified witnesses, and we follow the majority opinion among them
B. The Mishna is discussing a case where all witnesses are valid
C. He rejects the Mishna entirely
D. He rules that contradicting testimony always leads to breaking the neck
Correct: A — R' Neḥemya explains the Mishna deals with disqualified witnesses (e.g., women). The novel ruling is that among disqualified witnesses, we follow the majority — two women override one woman, just as two men override one man.
