Sotah 30 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
How many Tanna'im does Rav cite as holding that second-degree impurity cannot create third-degree in non-sacred food?
A. Three
B. Five
C. Seven
D. Two
Correct: B — Rav lists five: R' Meir, R' Yosei, R' Yehoshua, R' Elazar, and R' Eliezer — and provides proof from each one's rulings.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Akiva, how did the Jewish people sing the Song of the Sea?
A. Like a minor leading Hallel — repeating every word
B. Like an adult leading Hallel — responding only with the refrain
C. Like a cantor — all singing together in unison
D. Each person sang their own individual song
Correct: B — R' Akiva says they sang like an adult leading Hallel: Moshe said each line and the people responded with the chapter heading 'I will sing unto the Lord.'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the practical difference between R' Akiva and R' Eliezer b. R' Yosei HaGelili regarding Shabbat boundaries (teḥumin)?
A. They disagree about the distance — 1,000 vs. 2,000 cubits
B. R' Akiva holds it's Torah law; R' Eliezer b. R' Yosei holds it's rabbinic
C. They disagree about whether it applies to Levite cities
D. R' Akiva says there are no Shabbat boundaries at all
Correct: B — R' Akiva derives the 2,000-cubit boundary from Torah verses (making it d'oraita), while R' Eliezer b. R' Yosei HaGelili uses those verses for other purposes, leaving the boundary as rabbinic law.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the ḥalla dispute, what is Rav Mari bar Rav Kahana's key contribution?
A. He proves R' Eliezer wrong
B. He shows the dispute can be explained without disagreeing about impurity transfer to non-sacred food
C. He introduces a sixth Tanna who agrees with Rav's principle
D. He argues the dispute is about teruma, not ḥalla
Correct: B — Rav Mari offers alternative readings where both sides agree that second-degree can't create third in non-sacred food — they may instead disagree about the status of untithed dough or about causing impurity to food in Eretz Yisrael.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say R' Yehoshua's case of 'one who eats food with third-degree impurity' must refer to non-sacred food prepared at teruma-level purity?
A. Because teruma itself cannot become impure
B. Because ordinary non-sacred food cannot contract third-degree impurity
C. Because one may not eat impure teruma or sacrificial food, and ordinary non-sacred food doesn't reach third-degree
D. Because R' Yehoshua only discusses teruma
Correct: C — Eating impure teruma or sacrificial food is prohibited, so third-degree food must be non-sacred. But ordinary non-sacred food can't reach third-degree — so it must be non-sacred food that was voluntarily prepared at teruma-level purity.
