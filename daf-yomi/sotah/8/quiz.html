Sotah 8 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say the sota is brought up and down repeatedly in the Temple courtyard?
A. To allow multiple witnesses to identify her
B. To fatigue her mentally so she will confess
C. To fulfill a Torah requirement of walking before the Lord
D. To give the bitter water time to take effect
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that the repeated movement is designed to wear down her mental state and encourage confession.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the practical difference between R' Shimon and R' Yehuda regarding two sotot drinking simultaneously?
A. R' Shimon requires two priests; R' Yehuda requires one
B. If one woman is trembling, R' Shimon permits it; R' Yehuda still forbids it
C. R' Shimon forbids it on Shabbat; R' Yehuda permits it
D. R' Shimon requires them to stand apart; R' Yehuda does not
Correct: B — Since R' Shimon's reason is emboldening, a trembling woman shows no emboldening, so he would permit it. R' Yehuda treats it as a Torah edict ("ota" — her alone) regardless of her emotional state.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the contradiction in the Mishna about who may watch the sota?
A. Only kohanim may watch
B. Rava says 'anyone may watch' means all are permitted, while 'all women' means women are obligated
C. The Mishna is discussing two different time periods
D. Men watch from behind a partition
Correct: B — Rava resolves the apparent contradiction: the first clause permits all people (men and women), while the second clause teaches that women have an obligation to watch, based on Ezekiel 23:48.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Gemara's conclusion, why is an Egyptian rope specifically NOT indispensable to the sota rite?
A. Because the verse doesn't mention a rope at all
B. Because its primary function is just to hold her clothes up, so any tie suffices
C. Because Egyptian rope was too expensive
D. Because the rope represents her sin and is therefore optional
Correct: B — Rav Huna cites a baraita showing that the rope's purpose is practical — to prevent her clothes from falling off — not primarily measure-for-measure. Therefore any means of tying suffices.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the debate about uncovering a woman at stoning, what do both the Rabbis and R' Yehuda agree upon?
A. That stoning must be done publicly
B. That the principle of 'select a compassionate death' applies
C. That degradation is always worse than pain
D. That men should not witness the execution
Correct: B — Both sides accept Rav Naḥman's principle derived from 'Love your neighbor as yourself' — select a compassionate death. They disagree only about whether compassion means minimizing degradation (Rabbis) or minimizing physical pain (R' Yehuda).
