QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Avin in the name of R' Ile'a, what indicates that a verse is expressing a prohibition?
A. The verse mentions a punishment
B. The verse uses 'beware,' 'lest,' or 'not'
C. The verse is in Deuteronomy
D. The verse mentions God's name
Correct: B — R' Avin says that wherever Scripture uses 'beware' (הִשָּׁמֶר), 'lest' (פֶּן), or 'not' (אַל), it is a prohibition. This is how Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak derives the prohibition of arrogance from Deuteronomy 8.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did God choose Mount Sinai for giving the Torah, according to the Gemara?
A. It was the tallest mountain in the region
B. It was located nearest to Egypt
C. It was the lowest and most humble mountain
D. It had no vegetation, symbolizing purity
Correct: C — The Gemara says God passed over all tall mountains and hills and rested His Shekhinah on lowly Mount Sinai, demonstrating that God values humility. This also proves that God descends to the humble rather than raising the humble up to Him.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Yehoshua ben Levi, what is the spiritual status of a person with a humble spirit?
A. He is like one who brought a burnt-offering
B. He is like one who brought a meal-offering
C. He is credited as if he sacrificed all the offerings
D. He is exempt from bringing sacrifices
Correct: C — R' Yehoshua ben Levi derives from Psalms 51:19 — 'The sacrifices (plural) of God are a broken spirit' — that a humble person is credited as if he brought ALL the sacrificial offerings, not just one type.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Abaye's clarification, what combination triggers the sotah prohibition?
A. Warning about speaking + she spoke with the man
B. Warning about speaking + she secluded herself
C. Warning about seclusion + she spoke with the man
D. Warning about seclusion + she secluded herself
Correct: D — Abaye clarifies the mishna: the warning (kinui) must specifically concern seclusion, and the wife must then actually seclude herself (stirah) with that man for sufficient time. Warning about speaking, or seclusion after a speaking-only warning, has no legal effect.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the sotah require chalitza even though yibum is forbidden to her?
A. Because chalitza is always required before remarriage
B. Because the levirate bond must be formally dissolved, just as a forbidden wife still needs a get
C. Because the Rabbis added chalitza as an extra precaution
D. Because she might actually be innocent
Correct: B — Rav Yosef explains by analogy: even when a woman is forbidden to her husband, she still needs a get to dissolve the marriage. Similarly, even though yibum is prohibited for the sotah, she still needs chalitza to formally release the levirate bond with the yavam.
