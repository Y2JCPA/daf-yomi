Key Insight The Mishna says "one who warns" (descriptive) — not "one should warn" (prescriptive). This implies that issuing a warning is permitted only after the fact, but one should not do so ideally.

Witnesses for Warning & Drinking

R' Eliezer
The husband warns her before two witnesses for the warning to be effective.
R' Eliezer
But he gives her the bitter water based on one witness to the seclusion — or even his own testimony.
R' Yehoshua
He both issues a warning based on two witnesses…
R' Yehoshua
…and gives the bitter water based on two witnesses to the seclusion.
Core Dispute R' Eliezer requires witnesses only for the warning (kinui), not the seclusion (setira). R' Yehoshua requires two witnesses for both stages.

Why Does Sotah Follow Nazir?

📖 In the Torah, the Sotah passage (Numbers 5) comes before the Nazir passage (Numbers 6)
↓
🔗 R' Yehuda HaNasi: The portions are adjacent to teach that one who sees a Sotah in her disgrace should renounce wine
↓
💍 But the Tanna had just taught Ketubot, which includes "One who vows" (vows between spouses)
↓
📜 Vows led naturally to Nedarim (laws of vows)
↓
🍷 Nedarim led to Nazir (nazirite vow is a type of neder)
↓
⚠️ And then Sotah follows Nazir — per R' Yehuda HaNasi's principle of adjacency
Takeaway The Mishna's tractate order follows a chain: Ketubot → Nedarim → Nazir → Sotah, combining topical logic with the Torah's own arrangement.

Divine Matchmaking: Two Views

Reish Lakish

  • Matches are made according to one's actions
  • Source: "The rod of wickedness shall not rest upon the lot of the righteous" (Psalms 125:3)
  • A wicked wife is due to one's own evil conduct
  • This refers to second marriages

Rav Yehuda / Rav

  • Matches are predestined before birth
  • 40 days before the embryo forms, a Bat Kol declares: "The daughter of so-and-so is for so-and-so"
  • Houses and fields are also predestined
  • This refers to first marriages
Resolution There is no contradiction: First marriages are predestined (Bat Kol). Second marriages are matched according to one's merit and actions.

The 'Ed' (Witness) Paradigm

PrincipleSource & Application
"A witness [ed] shall not rise up against a man" (Deut. 19:15)The singular yakum already implies one — so why say "one"?
Paradigm establishedWherever Torah says ed (witness) without a number, it means two witnesses
Unless Torah specifies "one"Then and only then does it mean a single witness
Applied to Sotah (Num. 5:13)"There is no ed against her" = there are not two witnesses
"And she was not taken"She was not raped — she acted willingly, and one witness saw it
Key Rule For a Sotah: one witness to the seclusion/intercourse is sufficient to render her forbidden, because the Torah specifically indicates fewer than two witnesses apply here.
