Sotah 2 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Eliezer, how many witnesses are required for the seclusion (setira) to trigger the Sotah process?
A. Two witnesses
B. One witness or the husband himself
C. Three witnesses
D. No witnesses needed — the husband's suspicion suffices
Correct: B — R' Eliezer holds that the warning (kinui) requires two witnesses, but for the seclusion (setira), even one witness — or even the husband's own testimony — is sufficient.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why is Tractate Sotah placed after Tractate Nazir in the Mishna?
A. Because Sotah is alphabetically after Nazir
B. Because R' Yehuda HaNasi taught that seeing a Sotah in disgrace should lead one to renounce wine, linking the Torah portions
C. Because Nazir is a shorter tractate
D. Because both deal with the Temple service
Correct: B — R' Yehuda HaNasi noted that the Torah places the Nazir portion adjacent to Sotah to teach that witnessing a Sotah's disgrace should inspire one to become a Nazir and abstain from wine. The Mishna follows this adjacency, though in reverse order due to the chain from Ketubot → Nedarim → Nazir → Sotah.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
The Gemara resolves the contradiction between predestined matches (Bat Kol) and merit-based matches by distinguishing between:
A. Rich and poor families
B. First marriages and second marriages
C. Marriages in Israel and outside Israel
D. Marriages of scholars and common people
Correct: B — First marriages are predestined — announced by a Bat Kol 40 days before the embryo is formed. Second marriages are according to one's actions and merit, and are described as being as difficult as the splitting of the Red Sea.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does the Torah's paradigm of 'ed' (witness) establish?
A. That one witness is always sufficient
B. That wherever the Torah says 'ed' without specifying a number, it means two witnesses
C. That women cannot serve as witnesses
D. That witnesses must be related to the parties
Correct: B — From Deuteronomy 19:15, which says 'one witness shall not rise up,' the Gemara derives that the word 'ed' alone (without 'one') always means two witnesses. This paradigm is then applied to the Sotah verse to show that 'there is no ed against her' means there are not two witnesses — but there is one.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
After the husband's warning, if the wife merely speaks with the named man but does NOT seclude herself, what is her status?
A. She is forbidden to her husband
B. She must drink the bitter water immediately
C. She remains permitted to her husband and to teruma
D. She must bring a guilt offering
Correct: C — The Mishna is clear: if she merely spoke with the man, she is still permitted to her husband and (if a Kohen's wife) still permitted to eat teruma. It is only seclusion for sufficient time that triggers the prohibition.
