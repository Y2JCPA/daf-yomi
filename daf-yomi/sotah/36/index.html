Did the Hornet (צִרְעָה) Cross the Jordan?

Baraita
The hornet (tzira) did not cross the Jordan with them.
Gemara
But the verse says: "I will send the hornet before you" (Exodus 23:28)!
Reish Lakish
It stood on the bank of the Jordan and threw its venom — blinding their eyes from above and castrating them from below.
Reish Lakish
As it says: "I destroyed their fruit from above and their roots from beneath" (Amos 2:9).
Rav Pappa
There were two hornets: one of Moshe (east of Jordan) and one of Yehoshua (west). Moshe's didn't cross — Yehoshua's did.
Resolution Both opinions reconcile the verse with the baraita — either the hornet acted from a distance, or only one of two hornets stayed behind.

Key Point Both opinions agree there were 50 letters total across both stones, and the Gemara refutes Rav Kahana's claim that the ephod division matched the Mt. Gerizim/Ebal division — תיובתא (conclusive refutation).

The 50th Letter — How to Reach 50?

R' Yitzchak

  • Added a ה to יוסף → יהוסף
  • Proof: "He appointed it in Yehosef" (Psalms 81:6)
  • The extra letter ה comes from God's name

Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak

  • Objects: Names must be "according to their birth" — he wasn't called Yehosef at birth
  • Instead: בנימין is written with a second י (as in Genesis 35:18)
  • "His father called him Binyamin" — with full spelling at birth
Significance The requirement of "according to their birth" (כתולדותם) means the names used by Yaakov, not Moshe — Reuben not Reubenites, Shimon not Simeonites.

Evil Eye & the Descendants of Joseph

Tribe of Yosef complains to Yehoshua:
"We are a great people" (Joshua 17:14)
↓
Yehoshua: "Go hide in the forests"
so the evil eye won't affect you
↓
Yosef's tribe responds:
The evil eye has no dominion over us!
↓
R' Abbahu's Source
"Yosef is a fruitful vine by a fountain" (Gen 49:22)
Read as: olei ayin — those who rise above the eye
R' Yosi b. R' Chanina's Source
"Let them grow [veyidgu] like fish" (Gen 48:16)
Just as fish are covered by water, the evil eye cannot reach Yosef's seed
Lesson The tribe of Yosef's large numbers were a sign of blessing, not vulnerability — their ancestor's merit shielded them from jealousy.
