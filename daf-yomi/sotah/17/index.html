What Does the Priest Write on the Sota Scroll?

Mishna
The priest writes specific verses from Numbers 5:19–22 on the sota scroll. But which portions exactly?
R' Meir (Tanna Kamma)
Write the blessings (5:19), the curses (5:20–22), but skip the commands and 'Amen, amen'
R' Yosei
Write the entire passage without interruption — don't skip anything
R' Yehuda
Write only the curses (5:21–22) — nothing else
Key Verse Their dispute hinges on interpreting "ha'eleh et ha'alot" — each word either amplifies or restricts what gets written (Numbers 5:23)

The Hermeneutics Behind the Dispute

Gemara
All three derive their views from one verse: "The priest shall write ha'eleh et ha'alot in a scroll" (Numbers 5:23)
R' Meir
"Alot" = actual curses
"Ha-" on ha'alot = amplifies → include curses inferred from blessings
"Eleh" = restricts → exclude Deuteronomy curses
"Ha-" on ha'eleh = restricts → exclude commands & amens
R' Yosei
I agree with R' Meir — but the word "et" amplifies to include commands and acceptances too
R' Yehuda
Every term in the verse is exclusionary:
"Alot" = only actual curses
"Ha'alot" = exclude blessings
"Eleh" = exclude Deuteronomy
"Ha'eleh" = exclude commands & amens
Principle A heh (definite article) near an amplifier amplifies further; near a restrictor, it restricts further

How Does the Heh Work Both Ways?

The prefix heh (ה) appears twice in the verse
↓
הָאָלוֹת (ha'alot)
"Alot" is an amplifier (curses = broad category)
→ Heh extends the amplification
→ Include curses from blessings
הָאֵלֶּה (ha'eleh)
"Eleh" is a restrictor ("these" = limiting)
→ Heh extends the restriction
→ Exclude commands & amens
Rule Heh near an amplifier amplifies; heh near a restrictor restricts — context determines direction

R' Tanchum: The Hidden Curse in the Blessing

הִנָּקִי → חִנָּקִי
Linguistic Insight R' Meir holds you cannot infer a positive from a negative. So how are the blessings useful?

R' Tanchum explains: The word הִנָּקִי (hinnaki — "you shall be free") can be read as חִנָּקִי (chinnaki — "you shall choke").

Read with the next verse: "You shall choke… if you have gone astray."

The blessings themselves contain a dimension of curse — so they are written as actual curses, not merely implied ones.

R' Akiva: The Divine Presence in Marriage

Merit — שכינה

  • אִישׁ (ish) contains the letter yod (י)
  • אִשָּׁה (isha) contains the letter heh (ה)
  • Together: י-ה = God's Name
  • The Divine Presence rests between them

No Merit — אֵשׁ

  • Remove the yod from אִישׁ → אֵשׁ (fire)
  • Remove the heh from אִשָּׁה → אֵשׁ (fire)
  • Without God's letters, only fire remains
  • Rava: the woman's fire is stronger — her אש letters are adjacent
Rava's Observation In אִשָּׁה the א and ש are adjacent (fire is immediate); in אִישׁ the י separates them (fire is delayed)
