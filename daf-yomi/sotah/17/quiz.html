Sotah 17 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Meir (the first tanna), which sections does the priest write on the sota scroll?
A. Only the actual curses (5:21–22)
B. The curses plus the blessings, but not the commands or 'Amen'
C. The entire Torah passage without omissions
D. Only the oath formula and the woman's acceptance
Correct: B — R' Meir holds that 'ha'alot' includes curses from blessings, but 'ha'eleh' excludes commands and acceptances. R' Yehuda writes only curses; R' Yosei writes everything.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the definite article 'heh' amplify in one word but restrict in another?
A. It always amplifies — R' Yehuda simply disagrees
B. It depends on whether the root word is itself an amplifier or restrictor
C. The heh on the first word always amplifies and the second always restricts
D. It depends on the position of the word in the verse
Correct: B — The Gemara's principle: a heh near an amplifier (alot) extends the amplification; a heh near a restrictor (eleh — 'these') extends the restriction.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does R' Tanchum resolve the problem of R' Meir writing blessings if he doesn't infer positives from negatives?
A. The blessings are written but not dissolved in the water
B. The word 'hinnaki' (you shall be free) can be read as 'chinnaki' (you shall choke) — making it a curse
C. R' Meir actually does accept inferring positives from negatives in this case
D. The blessings are included only for context, not as operative text
Correct: B — R' Tanchum reinterprets הִנָּקִי as חִנָּקִי ('you shall choke'), so the blessing text itself contains a curse dimension when read with the following verse.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In R' Akiva's teaching, why does Rava say the fire consuming the woman is stronger?
A. Because the woman drinks the bitter water
B. Because the letters alef and shin are adjacent in 'isha' but separated by yod in 'ish'
C. Because the woman's oath is more severe
D. Because the letter heh has a higher numerical value than yod
Correct: B — When God's letters (yod/heh) depart, 'isha' becomes 'esh' with adjacent א-ש (immediate fire), while in 'ish' the yod separates the א from ש (delayed fire).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why must the sota scroll be written with soot-based ink rather than iron sulfate (kankantom)?
A. Iron sulfate is too expensive for a scroll that will be destroyed
B. The verse says 'blot them out' — the ink must be erasable in water
C. Iron sulfate would make the water poisonous
D. Soot ink is what was traditionally used for all sacred scrolls
Correct: B — Numbers 5:23 states 'he shall blot them out into the water of bitterness,' requiring ink that can be fully dissolved — iron sulfate and gum leave permanent marks.
