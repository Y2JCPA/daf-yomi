Sotah 44 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Beit Hillel, when is a courtyard adjacent to a grave considered pure with only 4 handbreadths × 4 handbreadths?
A. When the entrance is from the side
B. When the entrance is from above
C. When the grave is covered
D. When a kohen is not present
Correct: A — The Gemara corrects the initial text: Beit Hillel's leniency of 4×4 handbreadths applies when the entrance is from the side, since one can slip in and out without overlying the grave. If the entrance is from above, all agree 4×4 cubits is required.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why doesn't a man who betrothed a woman forbidden to him return home from war under the 'fearful and fainthearted' exemption?
A. Because forbidden betrothals are not recognized at all
B. Because Rabba says one is not liable until actual intercourse, so mere betrothal is not yet a sin
C. Because only kohanim are subject to marriage restrictions
D. Because the war exemption only applies to first marriages
Correct: B — Rabba explains that a man is never liable for a forbidden marriage until he engages in intercourse. Since betrothal alone doesn't constitute the sin, he has no guilt to trigger the 'fearful' exemption — even according to R' Yosei HaGelili.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the practical difference between R' Yosei HaGelili and R' Yosei regarding who returns from war due to fear of sin?
A. R' Yosei HaGelili requires capital sins; R' Yosei requires any sin
B. R' Yosei HaGelili includes rabbinic sins; R' Yosei requires Torah-level sins
C. R' Yosei HaGelili exempts only kohanim; R' Yosei exempts all
D. There is no practical difference between them
Correct: B — The Gemara identifies the practical difference: R' Yosei HaGelili holds that even violating a rabbinic prohibition (like speaking between tefillin) sends one home, while R' Yosei requires a Torah-level transgression.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rava, what type of war is the subject of the dispute between R' Yehuda and the Rabbis?
A. Wars of conquest like Joshua's
B. Wars of territorial expansion like David's
C. Preventative wars to weaken enemies before they attack
D. Wars to defend against an active invasion
Correct: C — Rava clarifies that all agree Joshua's conquest wars were obligatory and David's expansion wars were elective. The dispute is about preventative wars — R' Yehuda calls them a mitzvah, while the Rabbis call them elective.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How many members of the Sanhedrin go out to the site of an unsolved murder according to R' Yehuda?
A. Three
B. Four
C. Five
D. Seven
Correct: C — R' Yehuda derives five from the verse: 'your Elders' (plural = 2) + 'your judges' (plural = 2) = 4, but since a court cannot have an even number (they need a majority ruling), one more is added for a total of five.
