Courtyard Adjacent to a Grave

Beit Shammai
The courtyard must be 4 cubits × 4 cubits to constitute its own space and remain pure.
Beit Hillel
A space of 4 handbreadths × 4 handbreadths is sufficient.
Gemara
Wait — kelapei layya? Which direction does the distinction go? If the entrance is from the side, one can slip away without becoming impure!
Gemara (Corrected)
Beit Hillel's leniency applies when the entrance is from the side. If from above, all agree 4 cubits × 4 cubits is required.
Gemara
This only applies where partitions are delineated. A corpse in the open certainly affects four cubits around it.
Principle The four-cubit impurity zone around a corpse is a rabbinic safeguard to protect kohanim and others from inadvertent tum'ah.

R' Yosei HaGelili & the Fearful Soldier

"Fearful and fainthearted"
(Deut. 20:8)
↓
R' Akiva
Literally afraid of war — trembles at swords and shields
R' Yosei HaGelili
Afraid because of sins he has committed
↓
Challenge: If sin-based, shouldn't one with a forbidden betrothal also return?
↓
Rabba resolves: No liability for forbidden marriage until bi'ah (intercourse) — mere betrothal is not yet a sin
R' Yosei vs. R' Yosei HaGelili R' Yosei HaGelili: even rabbinic sins warrant return. R' Yosei: only Torah-level sins (e.g., kohen who married a divorcée).

Elective vs. Obligatory Wars

Obligatory War (Milḥemet Mitzvah)

  • Joshua's wars of conquest — agreed by all
  • Everyone goes: even groom from his room, bride from her canopy
  • No exemptions apply
  • R' Yehuda: Preventative wars are also in this category

Elective War (Milḥemet Reshut)

  • David's wars of territorial expansion — agreed by all
  • Standard exemptions apply (house, vineyard, wife)
  • Fearful/fainthearted return home
  • Rabbis: Preventative wars are in this category
Rava's Framework The dispute between R' Yehuda and the Rabbis is specifically about preventative wars — waged to weaken enemies before they attack. One calls it a mitzvah, the other calls it elective.
