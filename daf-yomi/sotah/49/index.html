R' Ile'a bar Yeverekhya: Torah Scholars' Obligations

R' Ile'a bar Yeverekhya
Without David's prayer, all Israel would be engaged in debased occupations — as it says: "Place for them mastery, O Lord" (Psalms 9:21)
R' Ile'a bar Yeverekhya
Without Habakkuk's prayer, two Torah scholars would have to share a single cloak — read bekerev shanim as bikrov shenayim
R' Ile'a bar Yeverekhya
Two scholars walking without Torah words between them deserve to be burned in fire — as with Elijah's chariot of fire (II Kings 2:11)
R' Ile'a bar Yeverekhya
Two scholars in the same city who are not pleasant to each other in halakha — one dies and one is exiled (Deut. 4:42)
PRINCIPLE Torah scholars bear special responsibility — in how they walk, talk, and relate to one another.

What Sustains the World?

במה העולם מתקיים? בקדושא דסידרא ובאמן יהא שמיה רבא דאגדתא
RAVA'S TEACHING Each day is more cursed than the last since the Temple's destruction (Deut. 28:67) — in the evening they pine for the previous morning because things keep worsening.

So why does the world endure?
Through the Kedusha (קדושה) recited in the order of prayers, and through answering Yehei Shmei Rabba after the study of aggada.

As Job 10:22 implies: where there are orders of prayer, light emerges from darkness.

Greek Language vs. Greek Wisdom

Greek Language (לשון יוונית)

  • The spoken Greek tongue
  • R' Yehuda HaNasi: Preferred over Aramaic in Eretz Yisrael
  • Rav Yosef: In Babylonia, speak Hebrew or Persian instead of Aramaic
  • ✅ NOT prohibited by the Sages

Greek Wisdom (חכמה יוונית)

  • A coded system of communication using Greek allusions
  • Used by an Elder to communicate with besiegers during the Hasmonean civil war
  • Led to the pig-on-the-wall incident that shook Eretz Yisrael 400 parasangs
  • ❌ PROHIBITED by the Sages
KEY DISTINCTION The Gemara resolves the contradiction: Greek language is separate from Greek wisdom. Only the coded wisdom system was banned.
