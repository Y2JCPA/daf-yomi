↓

↓

↓

KEY VERSE Numbers 5:15 — "Then shall the man bring his wife to the priest" — By Torah law, the husband alone brings her; the Sages added the two scholars.

Why Two Torah Scholars?

Gemara's Initial Assumption
Two scholars + husband = three men. This supports Rav's ruling: on the road, a woman needs three men (not just two) to avoid improper seclusion.
Gemara's Refutation
No — the two scholars are needed as witnesses in case the husband has relations with her on the way. One scholar would suffice for seclusion laws.
Gemara's Second Attempt
The Mishna says specifically Torah scholars — not just anyone. This supports Rav: seclusion is only permitted with men of fit morals.
Gemara's Refutation
No — Torah scholars are required because they know how to properly warn the husband not to have relations with her. Not about moral fitness.
INCIDENT The Gemara records: Ten men once carried a woman on a bier pretending she was dead, and assaulted her — proving that numbers alone don't guarantee safety.

R' Yehuda vs. The Rabbis: Is the Husband Trusted?

R' Yehuda

  • Husband is trusted — no scholars needed
  • Kal vachomer from niddah:
  • Niddah carries penalty of karet (excision) yet husband is trusted alone with her
  • Sota carries only a regular prohibition — all the more so he should be trusted!
  • Also derives from verse: "The man shall bring his wife" — the man alone

The Rabbis

  • Husband is NOT trusted — two scholars required
  • The kal vachomer actually proves the opposite:
  • Niddah = karet → the severity itself deters him
  • Sota = mere prohibition → not stringent enough to deter
  • Additional concern: niddah will become permitted again; sota may never be permitted
KEY DISTINCTION The Rabbis add: A niddah will eventually become permitted again after immersion. A sota may never be permitted to her husband — weakening his self-restraint.

Before the Sanhedrin: Threatening the Sota

Sota is brought before the Sanhedrin of 71
(derived from verbal analogy: tora/tora)
↓
Has the scroll been erased yet?
Before Erasure
They threaten her not to drink if guilty
Goal: prevent erasing God's name for nothing
After Erasure
They encourage her to drink if innocent
"The water is like dry poison on healthy flesh — it cannot harm the pure"
SOURCE Numbers 5:30 — "The priest shall execute upon her all this tora" ↔ Deuteronomy 17:11 — "According to the tora they shall teach you" — linking sota to the Sanhedrin.

Rendering the Sota Unattractive

👗 Remove any fine garments she is wearing
↓
💍 Remove jewelry: necklaces (katliyot), nose rings, finger rings
↓
🪢 Priest ties an Egyptian rope (palm fiber) above her breasts
↓
👁️ Anyone may come watch — EXCEPT her slaves and maidservants
↓
👩‍👩‍👧 All women ARE permitted to watch
WHY EXCLUDE SLAVES? Her slaves embolden her — seeing one's servants reinforces feelings of status and pride, which may cause her to falsely maintain her innocence. Source for women watching: Ezekiel 23:48 — "That all women may be taught not to do after your lewdness."
