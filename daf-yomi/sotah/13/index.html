↓
Brothers respond: 'You sold your rights to Jacob'
↓
Esau demands: 'Bring me the bill of sale!'
↓
Naphtali Sent
Naphtali, swift as a doe (Genesis 49:21), runs to Egypt to retrieve the document — imrei sefer, words of the book
Hushim Acts
Hushim ben Dan, hard of hearing, asks why there's a delay. Upon learning, he says: 'Shall our grandfather lie in degradation?' He strikes Esau with a club — Esau's eyes fall out onto Jacob's legs
Psalms 58:11 Jacob opened his eyes and smiled — 'The righteous shall rejoice when he sees the vengeance.' Rebecca's prophecy was also fulfilled: 'Why should I be bereaved of you both in one day?' — their burials occurred on the same day.

Why Was Joseph Buried Specifically in Shechem?

R' Ḥama b. R' Ḥanina
His brothers kidnapped him from Shechem (Genesis 37), so to Shechem we return his lost body.
Questioner
But who actually brought Joseph's bones? One verse credits Moses (Exodus 13:19), another credits the Children of Israel (Joshua 24:32)!
R' Ḥama b. R' Ḥanina
Anyone who begins a task but doesn't complete it, and another completes it — Scripture credits the one who completed it as though they did it all.
Joseph's Descendants
We deliberately left the task to others: it is more honor for Joseph to be buried by the many than the few, and by a great man like Moses than by lesser ones like us.
Principle The one who completes a mitzvah receives primary credit — but this also teaches the danger of leaving a mitzvah unfinished.

The Danger of an Incomplete Mitzvah

Judah — Started but Didn't Finish

  • Initiated saving Joseph from the pit
  • Suggested selling him instead of killing him
  • Did NOT complete the rescue — left Joseph enslaved
  • Was demoted (vayyered) from his greatness
  • Lost his wife and two sons (Er and Onan)

Joseph — Called 'Bones' in His Lifetime

  • Did not protest when brothers called Jacob 'your servant'
  • Failed to defend his father's honor
  • Called 'bones' (atzamot) even while alive
  • Predeceased his brothers
  • R' Yehuda/Rav: Acting authoritatively shortens life
R' Elazar One who begins a mitzvah and does not complete it when capable — moridin oto, he is demoted from his position of greatness.
