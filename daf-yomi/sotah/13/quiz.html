Sotah 13 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Amram strike Miriam on the head after Moses was placed in the river?
A. She had disobeyed by watching from the reeds
B. Her prophecy about a savior seemed to have failed
C. She had revealed the prophecy to Pharaoh's daughter
D. She refused to help place Moses in the basket
Correct: B — When Moses was placed in the river, Amram challenged Miriam: 'Where is your prophecy now?' — her earlier prophecy that her mother would bear a son who would save Israel seemed unfulfilled.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How many crowns were hung on Jacob's casket, and who placed them there?
A. 12 crowns, placed by the 12 tribes
B. 36 crowns, placed by the children of Esau, Ishmael, and Keturah
C. 7 crowns, placed by the elders of Egypt
D. 36 crowns, placed by Pharaoh's servants
Correct: B — A Sage taught that 36 crowns were hung on Jacob's casket. The descendants of Esau, Ishmael, and Keturah — who initially came to wage war — saw Joseph's crown on the casket and placed their own crowns on it as well.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why was Hushim ben Dan the one to act against Esau at the Cave of Machpelah?
A. He was the strongest warrior among Jacob's grandsons
B. He was hard of hearing and didn't understand the negotiation — he saw only the disgrace of delay
C. He was specifically appointed by Jacob to guard the burial
D. He had a personal grudge against Esau from an earlier conflict
Correct: B — Hushim was hard of hearing (כבד אוזניים). When he finally understood the situation, he couldn't fathom why Jacob should lie in degradation while waiting for a bill of sale, so he acted immediately.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Ḥama b. R' Ḥanina, what principle explains why the Children of Israel — not Moses — are credited with burying Joseph?
A. Moses forfeited his credit by sinning at the rock
B. One who completes a mitzvah receives credit as if they performed the whole act
C. A leader must defer honor to the community
D. The Torah always credits the majority over the individual
Correct: B — R' Ḥama teaches: Anyone who begins a task but doesn't complete it, and another completes it — Scripture credits the one who completed it as if they performed the entire act.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What consequence does the Gemara derive for Judah from his failure to complete the rescue of Joseph?
A. He lost his share in the World to Come
B. He was demoted from his greatness and lost his wife and two sons
C. He was excluded from the burial procession of Jacob
D. His tribe was denied the kingship for three generations
Correct: B — R' Elazar teaches that Judah was demoted (vayyered — 'went down') from his position among the brothers. R' Shmuel bar Naḥmani adds that one who starts a mitzvah without completing it will bury his wife and children, as happened to Judah.
