Who Goes Out to Measure? The Eglah Arufah Delegation

R' Yehuda
Five elders of the Great Sanhedrin go out to measure the distance from the corpse to the nearest city.
R' Shimon
Only three elders are needed — the plural 'elders' implies two, plus one more for an odd-numbered court.
R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov
The entire Sanhedrin goes out, plus the king and High Priest — 'your judges' refers to them.
Rav Yosef
Proof: the rebellious elder found the entire Sanhedrin in Beit Pagei — they left for a mitzvah like the eglah arufah measurement.
Abaye
No proof — perhaps they left to expand the city of Jerusalem or the Temple courtyards, which also requires seventy-one.
Resolution A baraita supports Rav Yosef: it lists the eglah arufah measurement alongside expanding Jerusalem as reasons the Sanhedrin would leave the Hewn Chamber — confirming R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov requires the full Sanhedrin.

How Many Judges for the Measurement?

R' Yehuda
Five elders go out — 'your elders' (2) + 'and your judges' (2) + 1 for an odd court = 5.
R' Shimon
Three suffice — 'your elders' (2) + 1 for an odd court. The vav in 'and your judges' teaches nothing extra.
Gemara
But if every plural adds two, then 'and they shall go out' and 'and they shall measure' should add four more!
Gemara
Those phrases are needed for other teachings: 'they shall go out' = they themselves, not agents; 'they shall measure' = measuring is a mitzvah even when the nearest city is obvious.
Key Principle Each word in the Torah serves a distinct purpose — a plural form that might add judges can instead teach an independent halakha.

R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov's Unique Reading

R' Yehuda / R' Shimon

  • "Your Elders" = members of the Sanhedrin
  • "Your Judges" = additional senior elders
  • Only 3 or 5 elders required
  • King & High Priest NOT required

R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov

  • "Your Elders" = the entire Sanhedrin
  • "Your Judges" = King & High Priest
  • King called 'judge': "A king by justice establishes the land" (Proverbs 29:4)
  • High Priest called 'judge': "You shall come to the priests...and to the judge" (Deut. 17:8–9)
Note Our Mishna does not follow R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov — it does not require the king or High Priest.

The Beit Pagei Proof (Rav Yosef vs. Abaye)

A rebellious elder finds the Sanhedrin in Beit Pagei, not the Hewn Chamber
↓
He rebels against their ruling — is he liable?
↓
No — 'the place causes' liability; it must be the Hewn Chamber (Deut. 17:8)
↓
But why was the entire Sanhedrin in Beit Pagei?
↓
Rav Yosef
They went out for the eglah arufah measurement — proving R' Eliezer ben Ya'akov requires the full Sanhedrin
Abaye
Perhaps they went to expand Jerusalem or Temple courtyards — that also requires 71 judges
Resolution A baraita explicitly lists both the eglah arufah measurement and city expansion as reasons — supporting Rav Yosef.

Concealed Sheaf Parallel — Same Type Concealment

🌾 A man holds a sheaf to bring to the city and places it on top of another sheaf
↓
😶 He forgets both sheaves — are they 'forgotten sheaves' owed to the poor?
↓
📜 Rabbis: Lower sheaf IS a forgotten sheaf; upper is not (it's 'floating')
↓
📜 R' Shimon ben Yehuda in name of R' Shimon: Neither is forgotten — lower is concealed, upper is floating
↓
🔑 Core dispute: Is a substance in contact with the same type of substance considered 'concealed'?
↓
📏 This parallels the eglah arufah question: a corpse concealed among stones vs. a corpse buried under another corpse
Connection R' Yehuda's view that 'in the field' excludes concealed items applies both to forgotten sheaves (pe'ah) and to eglah arufah — the same exegetical principle spans two areas of halakha.
