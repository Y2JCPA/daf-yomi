QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does a blemish NOT disqualify the eglah arufah, even though it disqualifies the red heifer?
A. The word 'bah' in the red heifer verse limits blemish-disqualification to the red heifer alone
B. The eglah arufah is too young for blemishes to matter
C. Blemishes only apply to animals offered on the altar
D. A gezeira shava excludes blemishes from the eglah arufah
Correct: A — The verse about the red heifer says 'wherein (bah) has no blemish' — the word 'bah' teaches that blemish disqualification applies only to IT (the red heifer), not to the eglah arufah.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Yehuda citing Rav, what is the difference between placing a bundle of sacks on a red heifer vs. an eglah arufah?
A. Both are immediately disqualified
B. The red heifer is immediately disqualified; the eglah arufah only when it actually pulls the burden
C. Neither is disqualified unless it pulls a yoke
D. The eglah arufah is immediately disqualified but the red heifer is not
Correct: B — The red heifer is disqualified by any labor, even passive weight-bearing. The eglah arufah is disqualified only when it pulls and moves the burden, as the verse specifies 'that has not pulled a yoke.'
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the dispute between R' Yoshiya and R' Yonatan regarding 'which may be neither worked nor sown'?
A. Whether the valley must be rocky or flat
B. Whether the prohibition applies to the past (never previously worked) or only the future
C. Whether sowing includes planting trees
D. Whether the elders or the priests choose the valley
Correct: B — Rava clarifies: both agree the land is prohibited for future use. Their dispute is whether the valley must ALSO have been unworked in the past. R' Yoshiya says 'which' indicates past tense; R' Yonatan says 'which' is a term of amplification.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why is combing flax permitted in the eglah arufah valley even though sowing is prohibited?
A. Flax is not a crop mentioned in the Torah
B. Combing flax is not labor performed on the land itself
C. The prohibition applies only to planting, not processing
D. Flax was used for sacred purposes and is therefore exempt
Correct: B — The verse uses sowing as the paradigm: just as sowing is labor performed ON the land itself, only such labor is prohibited. Combing flax and cutting stones are not done on the land itself, so they are permitted.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What reward did the man who showed the entrance to Bethel receive, according to the Gemara?
A. He was appointed king over a tribe of Israel
B. He received a portion of the Land of Israel
C. He built the city of Luz, which could not be conquered or entered by the angel of death
D. He and his descendants were converted and joined the Jewish people
Correct: C — The man founded Luz — a city where techelet is dyed, which Sennacherib could not exile, Nebuchadnezzar could not destroy, and the angel of death has no permission to enter. This demonstrates R' Meir's teaching that the reward for accompaniment is without measure.
