QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Elazar b'R' Shimon, why must the Jordan's waters have risen over 300 mil high?
A. Because water moves faster than people — if only 12 mil high, it would have collapsed and drowned Israel before they crossed
B. Because the Jordan riverbed was 300 mil wide
C. Because the Ark of the Covenant required that height to pass through
D. Because 300 mil was the distance from the Jordan to the sea
Correct: A — R' Elazar argued that since water is faster than people, a 12-mil wall of water would have flowed back and drowned Israel before they finished crossing. The water needed to pile up much higher (300+ mil) to allow enough time.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How did the Gemara calculate that the spies' cluster of grapes weighed 480 se'a?
A. Each spy carried 48 se'a on two trips
B. Based on the Jordan stones (~40 se'a each), a person can carry 120 se'a when loaded by others, and 8 spies carried the cluster on poles
C. The Torah explicitly states 480 se'a
D. Rahab reported the weight to Joshua's messengers
Correct: B — Each Jordan stone weighed ~40 se'a (self-lifted). Since a shoulder-load is ⅓ of the max loaded weight, each person could carry 120 se'a. 'Between two' means two poles (4 carriers per R' Yehuda, 8 per R' Yitzchak), yielding a total of 480 se'a.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did Caleb go to pray at the graves of the forefathers in Hebron, but Joshua did not?
A. Joshua was not allowed to enter Hebron
B. Joshua had already been protected by Moses' prayer when Moses changed his name from Hoshea to Yehoshua
C. Joshua was busy leading the other spies
D. Caleb was from the tribe of Judah, which had a connection to Hebron
Correct: B — Moses had already prayed for Joshua by changing his name to Yehoshua (י-ה יושיעך — 'God will save you from the counsel of the spies'). Caleb, lacking this protection, needed to seek his own spiritual help at the graves of the Avot.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Reish Lakish derive from the phrase 'שלח לך' (send for yourself) regarding the spies?
A. God commanded Moses to personally accompany the spies
B. Sending the spies was Moses' decision, not a divine command — God would not have chosen something bad for Himself
C. Moses should send only spies from his own tribe
D. The spies should go alone without military escort
Correct: B — Reish Lakish reads 'lekha' (for yourself) as 'at your own discretion.' Since God knew the spies would fail, He would never have commanded it. Moses later confirmed this: 'It was good in my eyes' — in my eyes, but not in God's eyes.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does the Gemara conclude about 'Hebron was built seven years before Zoan of Egypt'?
A. Hebron was literally constructed seven years earlier
B. Hebron had seven times more inhabitants than Zoan
C. Hebron was seven times more fruitful than Zoan, despite being the stoniest land in Israel
D. The walls of Hebron were seven times taller than those of Zoan
Correct: C — It cannot mean literally 'built' first, because Canaan was Ham's youngest son — no one builds for the younger before the older. Rather, 'built' (מבונה) means 'fruitful' — Hebron was 7× more productive than Zoan, remarkable since Hebron was the rockiest land in Israel.
