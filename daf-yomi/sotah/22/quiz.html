Sotah 22 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov, one who learned Torah and Mishna but didn't serve scholars is compared to what?
A. A Samaritan (Kuti)
B. A sorcerer (magosh)
C. A beast
D. An am ha'aretz
Correct: B — Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov says this person is like a sorcerer who uses knowledge to mislead. Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak supports this: 'The sorcerer chants and doesn't know what he says; the tanna teaches and doesn't know what he says.'
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Abaye and Rava object to including 'righteous due to love' and 'righteous due to fear' in the list of pseudo-righteous?
A. Because love and fear are equal motivations
B. Because Rav said performing mitzvot even not for their own sake leads to performing them for their own sake
C. Because the Torah commands both love and fear of God
D. Because these categories overlap with the others on the list
Correct: B — Rav taught that one should always engage in Torah and mitzvot even shelo lishmah (not for their own sake), because through this one comes to perform them lishmah (for their own sake). So these motivations shouldn't be condemned.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was wrong with the 'maiden who prays constantly' mentioned as eroding the world?
A. She prayed too loudly in the synagogue
B. She was like Yoḥani bat Retivi — pretending piety while practicing sorcery
C. She distracted others from their prayers
D. She refused to marry in order to keep praying
Correct: B — The Gemara clarifies this refers to someone like Yoḥani bat Retivi, who pretended to be saintly but actually engaged in sorcery. A genuinely pious maiden — like the one R' Yoḥanan overheard praying that men not stumble because of her — is praiseworthy.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to King Yannai's deathbed warning, who should his wife fear most?
A. The Pharisees (perushin)
B. The Sadducees
C. The hypocrites who appear like Pharisees but act like Zimri
D. The Romans
Correct: C — Yannai warned: Don't fear the Pharisees or the Sadducees. Fear the hypocrites — those whose actions are like the wicked Zimri yet they request reward like the righteous Pinehas.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is R' Shimon's concern about saying merit delays the sota water's punishment?
A. It contradicts a verse in the Torah
B. It weakens the water's deterrent power and defames innocent women who survived
C. It implies God's justice is imperfect
D. It encourages women to accumulate merit as insurance for sinning
Correct: B — R' Shimon argues that if merit delays punishment, guilty women won't fear the water (weakening its power), and innocent women who survived will be suspected — people will say 'she's guilty but her merit saved her,' defaming the innocent.
