Sotah 41 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye's final answer, when may one skip sections during Torah reading?
A. Only when the passages are physically close together in the scroll
B. When both passages pertain to a single topic
C. Never — one may only skip in the Prophets
D. Whenever the congregation agrees to allow it
Correct: B — Abaye's revised explanation is that skipping in the Torah is permitted when both sections deal with a single topic (inyan echad), such as the two Yom Kippur portions read by the Kohen Gadol.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why doesn't the Kohen Gadol use a second Torah scroll pre-furled to Numbers 29:7?
A. It is forbidden to have two Torah scrolls in the Temple
B. The second scroll would require a separate minyan
C. People might think there was a flaw in the first scroll (Rav Huna b. Yehuda) / it requires an unnecessary blessing (Reish Lakish)
D. Only the king may authorize a second scroll
Correct: C — Two reasons are given: Rav Huna bar Yehuda says people will suspect a flaw in the first scroll, and Reish Lakish says it would require an unnecessary additional blessing.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why was using three Torah scrolls on Rosh Chodesh Tevet (Shabbat during Chanukah) NOT a problem of suspected flaws?
A. The scrolls were all brand new
B. Three different people each read from their own scroll
C. The rabbis made a special decree permitting it
D. It only happened in the Temple where different rules applied
Correct: B — When three different individuals each read from a separate scroll, people assume each person merits his own scroll. The concern only arises when one person reads from two scrolls.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Natan, what happened when the people told Agrippa 'You are our brother'?
A. Agrippa was officially converted and accepted as Jewish
B. The people were sentenced to destruction for flattering him
C. God forgave Agrippa's foreign ancestry
D. The Sages revoked the law about appointing a foreigner
Correct: B — Rabbi Natan taught that at that moment the Jewish people were sentenced to destruction because they flattered Agrippa rather than upholding the Torah's prohibition against appointing a foreigner as king.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rabbi Levi interpret Jacob's statement 'I have seen your face as one sees the face of angels'?
A. Jacob was genuinely flattering Esau to gain his favor
B. Jacob was making a prophetic statement about Esau's future repentance
C. Jacob was strategically intimidating Esau by hinting he had encountered angels
D. Jacob was comparing Esau's beauty to that of the angels
Correct: C — Rabbi Levi disagrees with Reish Lakish and says Jacob was not flattering Esau. Rather, like a guest who mentions the king to scare a murderous host, Jacob hinted that he had seen angels to instill fear in Esau and prevent him from causing harm.
