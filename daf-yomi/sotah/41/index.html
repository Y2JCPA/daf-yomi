Skipping Sections in Torah Reading

Mishna (Megilla)
One may skip in the Prophets but not in the Torah.
Contradiction
But our Mishna says the Kohen Gadol skips from Acharei Mot to 'But on the tenth'!
Abaye (Attempt 1)
The prohibition is only when the skip is so long the translator finishes before you reach the next section. Here the passages are close together.
Gemara
A baraita implies one may never skip in the Torah!
Abaye (Revised)
One may skip in the Torah when both passages deal with a single topic (inyan echad). In the Prophets, even between two different topics.
RULE In all cases — Torah or Prophets — one may only skip if the furling is completed before the translator finishes his translation.

Skipping Sections: Torah vs. Prophets

Torah

  • May skip only within a single topic
  • Furling must finish before translator concludes
  • Example: Acharei Mot → 'But on the tenth' (both about Yom Kippur)

Prophets (Nevi'im)

  • May skip even between two different topics
  • Same furling/translator time constraint applies
  • May NOT skip between different books of Prophets
  • Exception: Within the Twelve Minor Prophets one may skip — but only forward, never backward
KEY DISTINCTION The Torah demands thematic unity when skipping; the Prophets allow greater flexibility — but always within time limits of the scroll's furling.

Why Does the Kohen Gadol Read by Heart?

The Kohen Gadol must also read 'And on the tenth' (Numbers 29:7) — a passage far from the previous reading
↓
Why not just furl the scroll to that section?
Rav Sheshet
One may not furl a Torah scroll in public — out of respect for the congregation (kevod hatzibbur)
Next Question
Then bring a second scroll already furled to that section!
↓
Rav Huna bar Yehuda
People might think there was a flaw (pesul) in the first scroll
Reish Lakish
He would need to recite an unnecessary blessing (berakha she'eina tzerikha)
RESOLUTION When one person reads from two scrolls, people suspect a flaw. When three different people each read from their own scroll (e.g., Rosh Chodesh Tevet on Shabbat during Chanukah), there is no such concern.

The Hakhel Assembly: Passing the Torah Scroll

📜 Synagogue attendant (chazan haknesset) takes the Torah scroll
↓
🏛️ Gives it to the head of the synagogue (rosh haknesset)
↓
👤 Passed to the deputy (segan)
↓
⚖️ Passed to the Kohen Gadol
↓
👑 Kohen Gadol gives it to the King
↓
📖 The King stands, receives the scroll, and reads while sitting
↓
🌟 King Agrippa stood and read while standing — the Sages praised him
ABAYE This chain of dignitaries is not proof that a student may be honored before a teacher — the entire process exists to magnify the honor of the king.

The Tragedy of Flattering Agrippa

אתה אחינו, אתה אחינו
TURNING POINT When Agrippa reached "You may not appoint a foreigner over you" (Deut. 17:15), tears flowed from his eyes — for he was a descendant of Herod and not fully Jewish.

The entire nation called out: "You are our brother! You are our brother!"

Rabbi Natan taught: At that moment, the Jewish people were sentenced to destruction for flattering Agrippa.
