Sotah 14 — Walking in God's Ways

אחרי ה׳ אלהיכם תלכו (דברים י״ג:ה׳)
CORE THEME This daf transitions from the laws of sotah to one of the Talmud's most famous ethical teachings: imitatio Dei — we must emulate God's acts of loving-kindness (gemilut chasadim). The Torah itself is bookended by divine kindness: clothing Adam and Ḥavah at its beginning, and burying Moses at its end. The daf then returns to the sotah procedure, examining the meal-offering and its unique vessels.

Imitatio Dei — Following God's Attributes

God's Act

  • 👗 Clothed the naked — made garments for Adam & Ḥavah (Gen. 3:21)
  • 🏥 Visited the sick — appeared to Avraham after his circumcision (Gen. 18:1)
  • 💐 Consoled mourners — blessed Yitzḥak after Avraham's death (Gen. 25:11)
  • ⚰️ Buried the dead — buried Moses in the valley of Moab (Deut. 34:6)

Our Obligation

  • 👗 Clothe the naked
  • 🏥 Visit the sick (bikur cholim)
  • 💐 Console mourners (nihum aveilim)
  • ⚰️ Bury the dead (halvayat ha-met)
KEY QUESTION How can we 'walk after God' when He is a 'devouring fire' (Deut. 4:24)? Answer: We follow His attributes, not His literal presence.

Garments of Skin — What Were They?

Rav
The garments were made of something that comes from skin — i.e., wool or similar animal-derived material
Shmuel
The garments were something from which skin benefits — i.e., linen or smooth fabric pleasant against the body
R' SAMLAI'S TEACHING The Torah begins with kindness (clothing Adam, Gen. 3:21) and ends with kindness (burying Moses, Deut. 34:6). Gemilut chasadim is the Torah's framework.

R' Samlai Expounds Isaiah 53:12 on Moses

Verse PhraseApplication to Moses
"I will divide him a portion among the great"Moses receives reward like the Patriarchs — Avraham, Yitzḥak, and Yaakov
"He bared his soul unto death"Offered his life for Israel: "Blot me out of Your book" (Ex. 32:32)
"Numbered with the transgressors"Counted among those who died in the desert without entering Eretz Yisrael
"He bore the sin of many"Atoned for the sin of the Golden Calf
"Made intercession for the transgressors"Prayed for sinners to do teshuvah — pegia = prayer
WHY MOSES WANTED ERETZ YISRAEL Not for its produce or goodness — but to fulfill the mitzvot that can only be performed there. God replied: I credit you as if you performed them.
