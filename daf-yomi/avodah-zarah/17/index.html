Avodah Zarah 17: Boundaries of Holiness

הרחק מעליה דרכך ואל תקרב אל פתח ביתה — Remove your way far from her
CORE THEME This daf explores the mortal dangers of proximity — to heresy, to forbidden intimacy, and to hostile authorities. R' Eliezer learns that even deriving pleasure from a heretical teaching brings heavenly punishment. The Gemara then contrasts two arrested Sages: one who combined Torah with charity is saved through miracles, while one who studied Torah alone faces execution.

R' Eliezer's Fateful Encounter

Ya'akov of Kefar Sekhanya
Is it permitted to use payment to a prostitute to build a bathroom for the Kohen Gadol in the Temple? (Deut. 23:19)
R' Eliezer
I said nothing in response...
Ya'akov of Kefar Sekhanya
Jesus the Nazarene taught: It is permitted — 'From filth they came, to a place of filth let them go' (Micah 1:7)
R' Eliezer
I derived pleasure from this teaching — and for this I was arrested for heresy!
R' Akiva
This is why you were punished: you violated 'Remove your way far from her' (Proverbs 5:8)
LESSON Even passive enjoyment of heretical teaching — without adopting it — constitutes a dangerous breach of spiritual boundaries.

Two Readings of Proverbs 5:8

First Interpretation

  • "Remove your way far from her" = Heresy (מינות)
  • "Do not come near her door" = The Ruling Authority (רשות)
  • Each danger gets its own clause

Alternative Interpretation

  • "Remove your way far from her" = Heresy AND the Ruling Authority
  • "Do not come near her door" = A Prostitute (זונה)
  • Rav Ḥisda: Must keep 4 cubits distance
KEY DEBATE Both readings agree: heresy, government power, and sexual immorality all require preemptive distancing, not just avoidance of the act itself.

Can One Return from Heresy?

"None that go to her return" (Proverbs 2:19)
↓
If they don't return, why add 'they do not attain the paths of life'?
↓
Generally
Those drawn to heresy do not return at all
Even If They Return
The pain of regret shortens their life
↓
Challenge: A woman confessed heresy + incest to Rav Ḥisda — yet did not die!
↓
Answer: She never truly repented — had she done so properly, the verse would have applied
PRINCIPLE True repentance from heresy is possible but exacts a devastating spiritual toll — incomplete repentance, paradoxically, may allow one to survive.

R' Elazar ben Perata & R' Ḥanina ben Teradyon — Arrested

R' Elazar ben Perata
Fortunate are you — arrested on one charge only (teaching Torah). I face five charges!
R' Ḥanina ben Teradyon
Fortunate are you — you will be saved. I will not. You engaged in Torah AND charity; I in Torah alone.
Rav Huna
One who engages in Torah alone is like one who has no God (II Chron. 15:3)
Gemara
But R' Ḥanina supervised the charity purse! — He was trusted as supervisor, but didn't give enough of his own wealth.
MUSSAR Torah study without commensurate acts of charity leaves a person spiritually vulnerable — even a great sage.
