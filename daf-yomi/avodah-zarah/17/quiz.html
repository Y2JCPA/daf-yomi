5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why was R' Eliezer arrested for heresy?
A. He taught Torah publicly in defiance of Roman law
B. He derived pleasure from a teaching transmitted in the name of Jesus the Nazarene
C. He visited a house of idol worship in the marketplace of Tzippori
D. He refused to pay taxes to the Roman authorities
Correct: B — R' Eliezer encountered Ya'akov of Kefar Sekhanya who shared a teaching in the name of Jesus the Nazarene about using 'payment to a prostitute' for a Temple bathroom. R' Eliezer derived pleasure from this clever interpretation, and this enjoyment of heretical teaching brought heavenly punishment.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why was R' Ḥanina ben Teradyon told he would not be saved despite being arrested on only one charge?
A. His single charge of teaching Torah publicly was more severe than R' Elazar's five charges
B. He had angered the Roman authorities more than R' Elazar had
C. He engaged in Torah study but did not sufficiently engage in acts of charity
D. He refused to recant his Torah teachings before the Romans
Correct: C — R' Ḥanina ben Teradyon himself explained that R' Elazar ben Perata would be saved because he engaged in both Torah study and charity, while R' Ḥanina engaged in Torah study alone. Rav Huna teaches that one who studies Torah alone is like one without God.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the Gemara's resolution regarding the woman who confessed heresy to Rav Ḥisda but did not die?
A. The verse about heretics dying applies only to men, not women
B. Rav Ḥisda's merit protected her from the punishment
C. She never truly and fully repented, so the consequence of repentance did not apply
D. Her other sins were so great that heresy was not her primary transgression
Correct: C — The Gemara states that Proverbs 2:19 teaches that those who return from heresy do not attain the paths of life. The woman who confessed to Rav Ḥisda survived specifically because she did not repent properly — had she truly repented, the verse's consequence would have applied.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What internal contradiction does the Gemara identify in Ulla's behavior?
A. He taught about distancing from heresy but visited heretical gatherings
B. He ruled that mere intimacy with forbidden women is prohibited, yet he kissed his own sisters
C. He said one must distance from prostitutes but lived near a brothel
D. He taught Torah publicly but refused to engage in charitable acts
Correct: B — Ulla ruled that intimacy with any woman forbidden to a man is prohibited, invoking the principle 'Go around, nazirite — do not approach the vineyard.' Yet his own practice of kissing his sisters contradicted this strict ruling, following instead the lenient view of R' Pedat.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How did R' Elazar ben Perata identify the warp and woof coils when tested by the Romans?
A. He had secretly studied weaving before his arrest
B. A female hornet sat on the warp (which receives) and a male on the woof (which is threaded through)
C. An angel whispered the answer to him during the trial
D. He guessed correctly and the Romans accepted his answer without verification
Correct: B — A miracle occurred: a female hornet sat on the warp coil and a male hornet on the woof coil. R' Elazar understood the sign — the warp is fixed in the loom and receives the woof thread, just as the female receives the male. This miracle was attributed to his merit of combining Torah with charity.
