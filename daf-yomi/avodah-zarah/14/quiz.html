QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why can't itzterubalin be identified as torenita?
A. Torenita has no root, so it wouldn't be subject to Sabbatical Year laws
B. Torenita is not found in Eretz Yisrael
C. Torenita is already listed separately in the mishna
D. Torenita is a type of animal, not a plant
Correct: A — Since itzterubalin is listed as subject to Sabbatical Year laws, and only plants with roots are subject to those laws, it cannot be torenita, which has no root. Rav Safra identifies it instead as the fruit of the cedar tree.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Abaye, why is selling a large bundle of frankincense permitted even though the buyer might resell it to an idolater?
A. The prohibition of lifnei iver applies only to direct causation, not secondary stumbling blocks
B. Large quantities of frankincense cannot be used for idol worship
C. The seller can stipulate that it not be used for idolatry
D. Frankincense sold in bulk loses its sanctity
Correct: A — Abaye teaches that the Torah prohibition of 'placing a stumbling block before the blind' (Lev. 19:14) applies only to being the direct cause. One is not required to prevent a secondary chain of events (lifnei d'lifnei).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Under what circumstance does R' Yehuda permit selling a white rooster to a gentile?
A. When the gentile specifically asks for a white rooster by name
B. When the gentile selects it along with other roosters (mixed purchase)
C. When the rooster is damaged and unfit for sacrifice
D. When the seller does not know the gentile's religion
Correct: B — R' Yehuda permits selling a white rooster when the gentile says 'sell me this one and that one' — selecting multiple roosters including a white one. This mixed selection indicates the purchase is not specifically for idolatrous sacrifice.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Rav Ḥisda's difficulty about the 'good palm tree' reveal?
A. R' Meir's prohibition refers to the detached fruit, not the tree itself
B. Good palm trees are never sold to anyone
C. Bad palm trees are more valuable than good ones
D. Palm trees cannot be used for idol worship
Correct: A — Rav Ḥisda noted a contradiction: if only 'good' palms are prohibited, bad ones should be permitted — but another mishna prohibits selling anything attached to the ground. Avimei resolves this by explaining 'good palm tree' means the detached fruit, not the tree itself.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the mishna need to teach that it's prohibited to sell an item when the gentile explicitly states it's for idol worship?
A. Because we might think he's bluffing — exaggerating his devotion to idolatry to persuade the seller
B. Because some forms of idol worship are actually permitted
C. Because the prohibition only applies to kohanim
D. Because verbal statements have no legal standing in commerce
Correct: A — The Gemara explains that we might have thought the gentile is so attached to idolatry that he assumes everyone else is too, and merely says 'for idol worship' as a persuasion tactic. The mishna teaches we take his words at face value and prohibit the sale.
