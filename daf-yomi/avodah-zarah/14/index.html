When May One Sell Items to Gentiles?

Gentile requests an item that could be used for idolatry
↓
Without Specification
Generally permitted — no proof of idolatrous intent
With Specification
Prohibited — indicates intent for idol worship
↓
Special cases: white roosters, frankincense, and certain fruits have stricter rules
Key Theme The Gemara distinguishes between direct facilitation of idolatry and indirect, uncertain enablement — balancing commerce with the prohibition of placing a stumbling block before the blind.

What Is Itzterubalin?

Initial View
Itzterubalin is the plant called torenita.
Objection
But itzterubalin is subject to Sabbatical Year (שמיטה) laws! Torenita has no root — only rooted plants are subject to שמיטה.
Rav Safra
Itzterubalin is actually the fruit of the cedar tree.
Ravin / R' Elazar
Confirmed from Eretz Yisrael: itzterubalin = fruit of the cedar tree.
Principle Only plants with roots are subject to Sabbatical Year laws — this rule helps identify unknown species.

The Large Bundle (300 Dinars) Exception

Items normally prohibited to sell to gentiles (frankincense, etc.)
↓
Is it a large bundle (≥ 300 dinars)?
Yes — Large Bundle
Permitted: clearly for resale, not personal idol worship
No — Small Amount
Prohibited: may be used directly for idolatry
↓
Abaye: But what if the buyer resells to another gentile who worships idols?
↓
We are only commanded not to place a stumbling block directly before the blind — not to prevent secondary stumbling blocks (לפני דלפני)
לפני עור Abaye limits the Torah prohibition of lifnei iver (Lev. 19:14) to direct causation only — one is not liable for a chain of events.

Selling a White Rooster — When Is It Permitted?

ScenarioRuling
Gentile says: "Who has a rooster?" (no color specified)✅ Permitted to sell white rooster (R' Zeira)
Gentile says: "Who has a white rooster?"❌ Prohibited — indicates idolatrous intent
Gentile says: "Sell me this and that one" (mixed selection)✅ Permitted even if one is white (R' Yehuda)
Gentile preparing a feast for his son or has a sick person✅ Permitted — clearly not for idolatry
Gentile asks for a damaged white rooster❓ Unresolved (תיקו) — perhaps artifice?
Gentile asked for white but accepted a black/red one❓ Unresolved (תיקו) — perhaps still artifice
R' Yehuda Selling a white rooster is only prohibited when the gentile specifically singles it out — mixed purchases show non-idolatrous intent.

What Does 'With Specification' Mean?

Initial Reading
Without specification = asks for wheat (permitted). With specification = asks for white wheat (prohibited). This implies a white rooster is prohibited even without specification!
Gemara's Rebuttal
No — without specification = asks for white wheat (permitted). With specification = says he needs it for idol worship (prohibited).
Objection
But isn't it obvious that explicitly requesting items for idolatry is prohibited?
Gemara
You might think he's bluffing — he's so attached to idolatry he assumes everyone is, and says it just to persuade the seller. The mishna teaches: take him at his word.
Insight Even if a gentile might be exaggerating his devotion to idolatry, we do not assume he is lying about his intended use.

Rav Ashi's Unresolved Dilemmas (תיקו)

Dilemma 1: Damaged Rooster

  • Gentile asks: "Who has a damaged white rooster?"
  • Perhaps: he doesn't need it for idolatry (gentiles don't sacrifice defective animals) → Permitted
  • Perhaps: he's using artifice — hoping to receive an undamaged one → Prohibited

Dilemma 2: Accepted Other Colors

  • Gentile asked for white, but accepted a black or red rooster
  • Perhaps: this proves he doesn't need white specifically → Permitted
  • Perhaps: still employing artifice to eventually get a white one → Prohibited
תיקו Both dilemmas remain unresolved — the Gemara cannot determine whether the gentile's behavior proves innocent intent or is mere subterfuge.
