Glazed Earthenware: Wine vs. Leavened Bread

Mareimar
Glazed earthenware permanently absorbs leavened bread (chametz), but vessels that contained gentile wine are permitted.
Gemara
But shouldn't rabbinic prohibitions follow Torah-law standards? Wine for libation should be treated like chametz!
Gemara (Answer)
Leavened bread is used hot — deep absorption.
Wine is used cold — lesser absorption. That's the distinction.
Background
The Torah attests that earthenware never fully expels absorbed substances — but the degree of absorption depends on temperature.
KEY PRINCIPLE Hot substances penetrate vessel walls more deeply than cold ones. This is why leavened bread (cooked hot) is treated more strictly than wine (consumed cold) in glazed earthenware.

The 12-Month Rule — Items That Become Permitted

↓

↓
🏺 Gentile jugs (Rav Chaviva b. Rava): permitted after 12 months
↓
🐪 Arab canteens (Rav Chaviva): permitted after 12 months
↓
🫒 Aramean grape pits (Rav Acha b. Rav Ika): permitted after 12 months
↓
⬛ Brown & black jugs (Rav Acha b. Rava): permitted after 12 months
R' YOCHANAN'S RULE When grape residues are prohibited (within 12 months), benefit is also forbidden. When they are permitted (after 12 months), even consumption is allowed — no middle ground.

Fish Stew (Muryas): When Is It Permitted?

Is the fish stew prepared by a professional expert?
No
Prohibited — may contain wine
Yes
How many times has the fish been used?
↓
1st or 2nd time
✅ Permitted
Fish oil is still plentiful, no need for wine
3rd time onward
❌ Prohibited
Oil runs low — wine may be added
STORY R' Abba placed guards over a fish-stew shipment at Akko. Rava challenged him: who guarded it before? R' Abba replied: where it was made, fish stew costs 1 luma but wine costs 4 — no one would waste expensive wine on cheap stew!

Cheese of Beit Unyaki: Why Prohibited?

Reish Lakish's Reason

  • Most calves of Beit Unyaki are slaughtered for idol worship
  • Cheese is curdled using stomach contents (keiva) of these calves
  • Therefore benefit is prohibited

Why 'Most' Matters

  • R' Meir is concerned even for a minority
  • But a minority of a minority — even R' Meir permits
  • If only a minority of calves + minority of all animals = minority of minority → no concern
  • Therefore we must say most calves are slaughtered for idolatry
LOGIC The Gemara's precision: saying 'most calves' ensures this is a simple minority concern (calves among all animals), not a minority-of-minority which even R' Meir would disregard.
