Who Prohibited Gentile Oil — And Could It Be Reversed?

Shmuel
The oil of gentiles is forbidden because of secretion of prohibited vessels — the oil absorbs forbidden flavors from non-kosher cookware.
Rav
The prohibition on gentile oil was decreed by Daniel himself, as derived from the verse: "Daniel purposed in his heart that he would not defile himself" (Daniel 1:8).
Shmuel
If Daniel decreed it, how could Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi and his court vote to permit it? A court cannot override another court unless greater in wisdom and number!
Rav
Was it Rabbi Simlai of Lod who reported this? Residents of Lod disparage the Sages' decrees — his testimony is unreliable!
Shmuel
Shall I send for him? — At which Rav became embarrassed.
Resolution The prohibition on oil never spread among the majority of the Jewish people, so Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi could revoke it — since the Sages issue a decree on the community only if most of the community can abide by it.

Source of the Oil Prohibition: Rav vs. Shmuel

Shmuel
The oil is forbidden because of secretion of prohibited vessels (בלועי כלים) — the oil absorbs non-kosher flavors from gentile cookware.
Rav
Daniel decreed a prohibition on gentile oil. The word מִשְׁתָּיו (mishtav) is plural — referring to two types of drinking: wine and oil.
Shmuel
Daniel placed the prohibition upon his heart — for himself only. He did not instruct all Jews to follow.
Rav
Daniel placed it upon his heart and instructed all Jews to adhere to it as well.
Key Verse "Daniel purposed in his heart that he would not defile himself with the king's food, nor with the wine which he drank" (Daniel 1:8)

How Could Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi Permit the Oil?

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi and his court voted to permit gentile oil
↓
A court cannot void another court's ruling unless greater in wisdom and number (Eduyyot 1:5)
↓
Shmuel's View
The prohibition was based on absorbed flavors. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi held: noten ta'am lifgam — a flavor that impairs the mixture is permitted.
Rav's View
Daniel decreed it, then Beit Shammai/Hillel extended it. But the prohibition never spread among the majority — so it could be revoked.
Principle R' Shmuel bar Abba in the name of R' Yochanan: The Sages inspected and found the oil prohibition had not spread among the majority of the Jewish people.

Daniel's Decree vs. The Eighteen Matters

Daniel's Decree

  • Daniel personally refrained from gentile oil
  • Rav: He decreed for all Jews; Shmuel: Only for himself
  • According to Rav: Initially applied only in the city
  • Derived from the plural word mishtav in Daniel 1:8

The 18 Matters

  • Decreed by students of Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel
  • Included: bread, oil, wine, and daughters of gentiles
  • Extended oil prohibition even to the field
  • Virtually irrevocable — even Elijah's court could not void them
Balei in the name of Rav The prohibitions on gentiles' bread, oil, wine, and daughters are all from the eighteen matters decreed in one day by the students of Shammai and Hillel.

The Chain of Preventive Decrees (Amud B)

🍞 Bread of gentiles was prohibited
↓
🫒 Oil of gentiles was prohibited
↓
🍷 Wine of gentiles was prohibited — bread & oil lead to wine-drinking together
↓
💍 Daughters — wine-drinking leads to familiarity and intermarriage
↓
🛕 Idolatry — intermarriage leads to idol worship
Geneiva in the name of Rav Bread, oil, wine, and daughters were all decreed due to concern for idol worship — each prohibition serves as a fence for the next.

Layers of Prohibition on Relations with Gentile Women

SourceScope of Prohibition
Torah Law (Deut. 7:3)Intermarriage with the seven Canaanite nations only
R' Shimon ben YochaiTorah prohibition includes all nations (from "he will turn away your son")
Court of ShemLicentious intercourse (harlotry) prohibited — as seen with Tamar and Yehuda
Halakha l'Moshe MiSinaiZealots may attack one who has public relations with a gentile woman (as Pinchas did)
Court of the HasmoneansPrivate intercourse — liability for four prohibitions: נשג"א (Nidda, Shifcha, Goya, Eshet Ish)
Students of Shammai/HillelEven seclusion (yichud) with a gentile woman is prohibited
Key Insight Each successive court added another layer of prohibition — from marriage, to public relations, to private relations, to seclusion — building ever-stronger fences against assimilation.
