Avodah Zarah 36 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, why was the oil of gentiles originally prohibited?
A. Daniel decreed against it
B. It absorbed forbidden substances from non-kosher vessels
C. It was part of the eighteen matters
D. It was used in idol worship
Correct: B — Shmuel held that the prohibition was due to 'secretion of prohibited vessels' — the oil absorbs non-kosher flavors from gentile cookware. He then argued that Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi permitted it because noten ta'am lifgam (a flavor that impairs) is permitted.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the problem of Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi overriding the prohibition on oil, given that a court normally cannot void an earlier court's decree?
A. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's court was greater in wisdom and number
B. Daniel's decree was never actually binding
C. The prohibition never spread among the majority of Israel
D. The prohibition was only a custom, not a formal decree
Correct: C — Rav Mesharshiyya explains that the eighteen matters cannot be voided because they spread among the majority. But the oil prohibition did NOT spread among the majority, so it could be revoked — as the Sages only institute a decree the community can abide by.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Geneiva in the name of Rav, what is the ultimate concern behind the prohibitions on gentiles' bread, oil, wine, and daughters?
A. Ritual impurity of gentile food
B. Health and kashrut concerns
C. Idol worship (avodah zarah)
D. Economic competition with Jewish merchants
Correct: C — Geneiva explains that all these prohibitions form a chain of protective fences: bread and oil lead to wine-drinking together, wine leads to familiarity and intermarriage with their daughters, and intermarriage leads to idol worship.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What did the students of Beit Shammai and Beit Hillel add to the Hasmonean court's decree regarding relations with gentile women?
A. They prohibited public intercourse
B. They prohibited private intercourse
C. They prohibited seclusion (yichud) with gentile women
D. They prohibited marriage with all nations, not just Canaanites
Correct: C — The Hasmonean court had already prohibited private intercourse. The students of Shammai and Hillel added a further fence — prohibiting even being secluded (yichud) with a gentile woman, addressing the situation that could lead to the transgression.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did Rav dismiss Shmuel's proof from Rabbi Simlai that Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi permitted the oil?
A. Rabbi Simlai was not ordained
B. Rabbi Simlai was from Lod, whose residents disparage the Sages' decrees
C. Rabbi Simlai lived after the relevant period
D. Rabbi Simlai misquoted the ruling
Correct: B — Rav attempted to reject the proof by saying Rabbi Simlai was from Lod, and the residents of Lod are known for disparaging rabbinic decrees — making his testimony unreliable. Shmuel challenged this by offering to send for Rabbi Simlai, which embarrassed Rav.
