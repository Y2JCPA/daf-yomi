The Nations, the Torah & Divine Justice

Nations of the World
Master of the Universe — give us the Torah and we will fulfill it!
HaKadosh Baruch Hu
Fools! One who toils on Erev Shabbat eats on Shabbat. You had your chance with the seven Noahide mitzvot and failed.
Nations of the World
Did Israel really keep the Torah? Where is the proof?
HaKadosh Baruch Hu
Let witnesses come from among you — Nimrod, Laban, Potiphar's wife, Nebuchadnezzar, Darius — and testify!
Core Theme Daf 3 dramatizes God's response to the nations who protest they never received the Torah — exposing that they failed even the mitzvot they were given, while gentile witnesses confirm Israel's faithfulness.

Commanded vs. Not Commanded

ויקרא י״ח:ה׳
R' Ḥanina's Principle גדול המצווה ועושה יותר ממי שאינו מצווה ועושה

Greater is one who is commanded and performs a mitzva than one who is not commanded and performs it.

Even a gentile who studies Torah is like a Kohen Gadol (R' Meir, from Leviticus 18:5 — "which if a person do" — not Priests, Levites, or Israelites, but person). Yet the reward of the commanded is still greater.

Who Can Testify for Israel?

HaKadosh Baruch Hu
I will testify that Israel fulfilled the entire Torah.
Nations
Can a father testify about his son? "Israel is My son, My firstborn" (Exodus 4:22)!
HaKadosh Baruch Hu
Heaven and earth will testify for them.
Nations
They are interested parties! Without Torah, creation returns to chaos (R' Shimon ben Lakish).
HaKadosh Baruch Hu
Then let witnesses come from among you — your own people will testify for Israel.
Key Verse "All the nations are gathered together… let them bring their witnesses, that they may be justified" (Isaiah 43:9)

The Sukka Test

Nations demand: "Give us the Torah afresh!"
↓
God: "One who toils on Erev Shabbat eats on Shabbat — but here is an easy mitzva: Sukka"
↓
Each one builds a sukka on his rooftop
↓
God extends the heat of Tammuz — the sun blazes
↓
Expected
Leave the sukka (even Jews are exempt when suffering — Rava)
What They Do
They kick the sukka on the way out
R' Yehoshua b. Levi "Today is to do them" (Deut. 7:11) — this world is for performing mitzvot; the World to Come is for receiving reward. The test illustrates attitude, not obligation.
