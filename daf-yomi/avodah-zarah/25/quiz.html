Avodah Zarah 25 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Ḥiyya bar Abba in the name of R' Yoḥanan, what is the 'Book of Yashar'?
A. The Book of Judges
B. The Book of Genesis
C. The Book of Deuteronomy
D. A lost prophetic scroll
Correct: B — The 'Book of Yashar' is Genesis — called so because it is the book of Avraham, Yitzḥak, and Yaakov, who were called 'yesharim' (upright/righteous ones).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the practical difference between R' Yirmeya and Rav Idi bar Avin regarding women secluding with gentiles?
A. Whether armed gentiles are more dangerous
B. A woman who is politically distinguished but unattractive
C. Whether the gentile's wife is present
D. The number of gentiles present
Correct: B — R' Yirmeya says a distinguished woman is safe from both murder and assault. Rav Idi says all women face assault risk. So a politically distinguished but unattractive woman would be permitted by R' Yirmeya but forbidden by Rav Idi.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to R' Shmuel bar Naḥmani, how many total hours elapsed before the sun set for Joshua?
A. 24 hours
B. 36 hours
C. 48 hours
D. 12 hours
Correct: C — R' Shmuel bar Naḥmani holds 48 hours: the sun traveled 6, stood 12, traveled 6, then stood 24 more — reading two separate suspensions from the verse.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did R' Akiva's students tell the bandits they were going to Akko instead of Keziv?
A. Akko was a safer city
B. To misdirect the bandits about their destination, following Yaakov's example with Esav
C. They were actually going to Akko first
D. R' Akiva lived in Akko
Correct: B — The Gemara teaches to extend one's stated destination beyond the real one, just as Yaakov told Esav he was going to Seir but went to Sukkot. This causes potential attackers to delay, allowing safe arrival.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara reconcile 'no day like it before or after' (Joshua 10:14) with the claim that the sun also stood for Moses?
A. It claims the verse is not literal
B. Moses' miracle was at night, not day
C. Either fewer hours for Moses, or Joshua's miracle uniquely included hailstones
D. The verse refers only to the hailstones, not the sun
Correct: C — The Gemara offers two answers: (1) the sun stood fewer hours for Moses than for Joshua, or (2) Joshua's miracle was unique because it included hailstones raining down on the enemy, which did not occur for Moses.
