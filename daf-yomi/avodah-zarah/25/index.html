Avodah Zarah 25 — Overview

ספר הישר — The Book of the Upright
TWO WORLDS This daf bridges Aggadic exploration of the sun standing still for Joshua — debating its duration and parallels to Moses — with Halakhic rulings on seclusion with gentiles and practical survival strategies when traveling.

What Is the Book of Yashar?

Gemara
Joshua 10:13 references "the Book of Yashar" — what is this book?
R' Ḥiyya bar Abba / R' Yoḥanan
It is Sefer Bereishit (Genesis) — the book of Avraham, Yitzḥak, and Yaakov, who are called yesharim (righteous/upright)
Gemara
Where is the sun standing still alluded to in Genesis?
R' Ḥiyya bar Abba / R' Yoḥanan
"His seed shall become a multitude of nations" (Gen 48:19) — referring to Ephraim, Joshua's ancestor. This was fulfilled when the sun stopped and all nations witnessed it.
KEY CONCEPT ספר הישר = Genesis, named for the patriarchs who were called ישרים (upright ones)

The Sun Stood Still for Three People

Joshua

  • Explicit verse: Joshua 10:13
  • Accompanied by hailstones from heaven
  • "No day like it before or after" (Josh 10:14)

Moses

  • Derived via gezera shava: "I will begin" / "delivered"
  • R' Shmuel: from the verse itself — nations trembled
  • Fewer hours OR no hailstones — hence Joshua's day was unique

Nakdimon b. Guryon

  • Known by tradition (not from a verse)
  • A later, post-biblical miracle
RESOLUTION Joshua's day was still unique — either Moses had fewer hours of suspension or Joshua's miracle included hailstones that Moses' did not

Seclusion with Gentiles — The Halakhic Analysis

May a Jewish woman seclude herself with three gentile men?
↓
Problem: Even among Jews steeped in immorality, seclusion is forbidden even with ten men (Shmuel)
↓
Answer: The mishna refers to a case where the gentile's wife is with him
↓
Gentile's Wife
Does not guard him from sinning → still forbidden
Jew's Wife
Does guard him from sinning → permitted
DISTINCTION A Jewish man's wife is assumed to prevent him from sinning; a gentile man's wife is not assumed to have the same deterrent effect

Why Can't a Woman Seclude with Gentiles?

Gemara
The mishna says: concern for illicit relations. But shouldn't we also derive a concern for bloodshed?
R' Yirmeya
We deal with a distinguished woman — gentiles fear her status and won't kill her. But illicit relations remain a concern.
Rav Idi bar Avin
"A woman carries her weapons upon her" — being female itself protects her from murder, as they would assault rather than kill.
Gemara
Practical difference: a woman distinguished politically but unattractive. R' Yirmeya permits seclusion; Rav Idi forbids it.
BARAITA A baraita supports Rav Idi: even though a woman's protection accompanies her, she may not seclude herself with gentiles due to suspicion of forbidden relations
