Traveling To & From Idol Fairs

Gentile

  • Going to a fair — permitted to do business
  • Returning from a fair — permitted to do business
  • Reasoning: assume he sold clothing or wine, not idols

Jew (at a Gentile Fair)

  • Going to a fair — permitted to do business
  • Returning from a fair — prohibited
  • Reasoning: if he only had wine/clothing, he would have sold them locally — going to the fair implies he sold idols

Apostate Jew (Festival)

  • Going to a festival — prohibited
  • Returning from a festival — prohibited
  • Per Rav Ashi: will certainly attend and not change his mind
Reish Lakish Gentiles returning in groups are prohibited — they may intend to return to the festival together

Who Pours Wine Into Pitched Containers?

Baraita
If a gentile pitched and lined the container and added wine while a Jew stands over him — it is permitted
Gemara
But the gentile is placing wine! How does a Jew standing there help?
Rav Pappa
The Jew pours the wine. Another Jew stands guard in case the gentile tries to libate while the first Jew is preoccupied with pouring
Rav Zevid
No — the gentile pours. But pouring wine into a pitched container is like splashing water on clay — he has no intent to libate
Practical Outcome Rav Pappi derives from Rav Zevid: wine a gentile pours into a Jew's salt receptacle should also be permitted — but Rav Ashi disagrees, since salt preserves the wine's flavor

Three-Day Water Purification for Wineskins

🍷 Wineskins seized by gentile and filled with wine
↓
💧 Fill the skins with water
↓
⏱️ Wait 24 hours, then pour out the water
↓
💧 Refill with fresh water (per Rava — must change each day)
↓
🔄 Repeat for a total of 3 days
↓
✅ Wineskins are now permitted for use
R' Shimon ben Lakish This applies to both Jewish-owned skins seized by gentiles and gentile-owned skins — Ravin brought this ruling from Eretz Yisrael. Rav Ashi adds: no difference between wineskins and jugs.
