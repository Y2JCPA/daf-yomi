Avodah Zarah 43 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda, the figure of Sar Apis is forbidden only under what condition?
A. When it has scales between its joints
B. When it is holding a dry measure and measuring
C. When it is made of gold or silver
D. When it is found on a vessel near a temple
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda specifies that the Sar Apis figure (symbolizing Joseph who distributed food) is forbidden only when depicted holding a dry measure and measuring with it — representing his role feeding the world during famine.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why couldn't the minor gentile boy revoke the idolatrous status of the dragon ring?
A. Minors are forbidden from touching idols
B. Only Jewish courts can revoke idol status
C. One must be aware of the nature of idol worship to revoke it
D. The ring belonged to a different gentile
Correct: C — The Gemara derives from R' Elazar HaKappar's actions that only one who is aware of the nature of idol worship and its accessories can revoke an idol's status — a minor lacks this awareness.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the owner's despair (יאוש) over a lost idol NOT automatically revoke its idolatrous status?
A. Because despair only applies to monetary value, not religious status
B. Because the owner assumes whoever finds it will eventually worship it or sell it to someone who will
C. Because the Torah requires a formal ceremony of revocation
D. Because only a Jewish court can declare an idol nullified
Correct: B — Abaye explains that while the owner despairs of recovering the object, he does NOT despair of its idolatrous status — he assumes a gentile finder will worship it, or a Jewish finder will sell it to a gentile who will worship it.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the concern with a ring whose seal has a PROTRUDING human figure?
A. It may be used to create idolatrous coins
B. Wearing it arouses suspicion that one possesses an idol
C. The figure may come to life according to mystical tradition
D. It violates the prohibition of fashioning images
Correct: B — A protruding figure on a ring worn on one's finger creates the appearance of possessing an object of idol worship. Therefore it is forbidden to wear but permitted to seal with (since sealing creates a sunken — not protruding — impression).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara explain why Shmuel's father and Levi prayed in a synagogue containing a statue of the king?
A. The statue had been formally revoked by a gentile
B. A public institution is not suspected of idolatrous intent — the statue is assumed to be ornamental
C. The statue was sunken into the wall, not protruding
D. They prayed there only under duress from the authorities
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes between individuals and public institutions: a public space is not suspected of idolatrous intent, so a decorative statue in a synagogue does not create forbidden suspicion. This is contrasted with Rav Yehuda's individual case.
