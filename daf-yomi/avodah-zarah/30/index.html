Wine Categories & Their Halakhic Status

Wine in a Gentile's Possession
↓
Cooked Wine
Not subject to libation prohibition or exposure laws
Diluted Wine
Rava rules: Subject to both exposure and libation prohibition
Regular Wine
Subject to all standard prohibitions of yayin nesekh
Key Principle The more wine is processed (cooked, diluted), the more its halakhic status changes — but Rava's final ruling treats diluted wine strictly.

Aluntit vs. Anomlin: Ancient Beverages

Anomlin

  • Wine + honey + pepper
  • May be prepared on Shabbat
  • A standard mixed drink

Aluntit

  • Aged wine + clear water + balsam
  • Drunk after bathing in a bathhouse
  • May NOT be prepared on Shabbat (remedy)
  • If made by a Jew before entering gentile possession — permitted
Baraita Cooked wine and aluntit of gentiles are prohibited, but pre-made aluntit by a Jew is permitted.

Is Cooked Wine Subject to Exposure (Gilui)?

Rabba & Rav Yosef
Diluted wine is not subject to gilui (exposure); cooked wine is not subject to libation prohibition.
Gemara
But is cooked wine subject to the laws of exposure?
R' Ya'akov bar Idi
I testify: cooked wine is not subject to exposure.
Reish Lakish (via R' Ḥiyya)
Cooked wine is not subject to exposure.
R' Yannai bar Yishmael
"Upon me and upon my neck" — you can certainly rely on this ruling!
Incident Shmuel sat with the gentile scholar Ablet. When cooked wine was served, Ablet pulled his hand back — but Shmuel told him it was unnecessary, as cooked wine has no libation prohibition.

The Snake & Diluted Wine: A Chain of Stories

Does a snake drink diluted wine?
↓
Rabba bar Rav Huna's ship: Attendant added water to wine → snake turned away
↓
Conclusion: Snake won't risk its life for diluted wine, but will drink it if unguarded
↓
R' Yannai at Bei Akhborei: Snake poured water through cloth into container, diluted wine itself, then drank the overflow!
↓
Sages
A snake drinks wine it diluted itself — not wine others diluted
Rav Ashi / Rav Mesharshiyya
Don't rely on such reasoning when danger to life is at stake!
Final Halakha Rava rules: Diluted wine IS subject to both gilui (exposure) and the libation prohibition.

Foods & Beverages: Exposure (Gilui) Status

🫗 Fermenting wine — NOT subject to gilui (for first 3 days)
↓
🥬 Cress dishes without vinegar — Diaspora custom to prohibit
↓
🥬 Cress dishes WITH vinegar — permitted (vinegar repels snakes)
↓
🫕 Babylonian kutaḥ — NOT subject, but Diaspora stringent; if indentations found → prohibited
↓
💧 Dripping water — NOT subject while being collected (continuous drips)
↓
🍇 Mouth of a fig — NOT subject (R' Eliezer: 'The Lord preserves the simple')
Rav Menashei If kutaḥ has indentations, we suspect they are fang marks from a snake — and the food is prohibited.
