Avodah Zarah 2 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Mishna prohibit transactions with gentiles before their festivals?
A. Because the transactions might involve forbidden items
B. Because bringing them joy leads them to thank their idols on the festival
C. Because Jews might accidentally participate in the festival
D. Because the money might be used to fund idolatrous ceremonies
Correct: B — The Mishna's concern is that favorable transactions bring joy to the gentile, who would then give thanks to his object of idol worship on the festival day.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does R' Yehuda permit collecting debts from gentiles before their festivals?
A. Because debt collection is a mitzvah that overrides the prohibition
B. Because the amount is usually too small to cause joy
C. Because being forced to repay causes the gentile distress, not joy
D. Because the gentile already owes the money anyway
Correct: C — R' Yehuda argues that repaying a debt is distressing, not joyful. The Rabbis counter that the subsequent relief from being debt-free ultimately makes the gentile happy.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why does the Roman Empire enter for judgment before all other nations?
A. Because Rome was the first empire chronologically
B. Because Rome destroyed the Temple and must answer for it first
C. Because Rome is the most important empire, and the more important enters first
D. Because Rome volunteered to go first
Correct: C — The Gemara derives from Rav Chisda that the more important party enters for judgment first. Rome is identified as the most important empire based on Daniel 7:23 — 'it shall devour the whole earth.'
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does God refute Rome's claim that they built marketplaces for Israel's benefit?
A. God says the marketplaces sold non-kosher food
B. God says the marketplaces were established to place prostitutes in them
C. God says Israel never actually used those marketplaces
D. God says the marketplaces were built after the Temple was destroyed
Correct: B — God exposes Rome's true motivation: marketplaces were for prostitutes, bathhouses for personal enjoyment, and the silver and gold belong to God — their claims of benefiting Israel were self-serving lies.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did the Persian Empire believe it would succeed where Rome failed in the divine judgment?
A. Because Persia had more scholars who studied Torah
B. Because Persia never persecuted the Jewish people
C. Because Persia had built the Second Temple under King Cyrus
D. Because Persia's military achievements were greater than Rome's
Correct: C — Persia believed its case was stronger because, unlike Rome which destroyed the Second Temple, Persia had actually built it — the Second Temple was constructed under the auspices of Cyrus, king of Persia.
