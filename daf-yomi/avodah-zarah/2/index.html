R' Yehuda vs. The Rabbis: Collecting Debts

R' Yehuda
One may collect debts from gentiles before their festivals — being forced to repay causes them distress, not joy.
Rabbis
Even though he is distressed now when repaying, he is happy afterward — relieved of the debt burden.
R' Yehuda
The immediate experience is what matters — at the moment of repayment, it is painful.
Rabbis
The subsequent relief and gratitude is what concerns us — he will thank his idol on the festival for being debt-free.
Halakha The Rabbis prevail: even debt collection is prohibited, because the net effect is joy and thanksgiving to idolatry.

Rav vs. Shmuel: How to Spell 'Eideihen' (Their Festivals)

With Alef (א) — Calamity

  • Reads as eideihen from the root eid (calamity)
  • Source: "The day of their calamity (eidam) is at hand" (Deut. 32:35)
  • Festivals of idolaters foreshadow their future downfall
  • A term of calamity is preferable — it captures the ultimate consequence

With Ayin (ע) — Witnesses

  • Reads as eideihen from the root eid (witness/testimony)
  • Source: "Their own witnesses see not, nor know" (Isaiah 44:9)
  • Idols themselves testify against their worshippers
  • Testimony is preferable — it reveals the cause of the calamity
Resolution Both readings are valid. The one who reads ayin argues: What causes the calamity? The testimony against them — so testimony is the more fundamental term.

The Future Judgment of the Nations (R' Chanina bar Pappa / R' Simlai)

📜 God brings a Torah scroll, places it in His lap, and announces: 'Whoever studied it — come take your reward'
↓
🌍 All nations gather together, intermingled — God tells them to enter separately, nation by nation
↓
🏛️ The Roman Empire enters first, as the most important (Daniel 7:23 — R' Yochanan)
↓
🐻 The Persian Empire enters second (Daniel 7:5 — compared to a bear by Rav Yosef)
↓
🌐 Each remaining nation enters in turn, each believing their claims will succeed
↓
⚖️ All are asked: 'Who among you can declare this?' — 'this' = the Torah (Deut. 4:44)
Source Verse "All the nations are gathered together, and let the peoples be assembled; who among them can declare this?" (Isaiah 43:9)

Rome Before the Divine Court

Rome
Master of the Universe! We established marketplaces, built bathhouses, and increased silver and gold — all for the sake of Israel, so they could study Torah!
God
Fools! Everything you did was for your own needs. Marketplaces — to place prostitutes. Bathhouses — for your own enjoyment.
God
As for the silver and gold — it is Mine: "Mine is the silver and Mine the gold, says the Lord of Hosts" (Haggai 2:8).
God
Is there anyone among you who studied this Torah? (Isaiah 43:9) — and 'this' refers only to Torah (Deut. 4:44).
Rome
...leaves disappointed.
Rav Chisda's Principle Why does the most important nation enter first? A king enters for judgment before the community — either for dignity, or so he is judged before God's anger intensifies (I Kings 8:59).
