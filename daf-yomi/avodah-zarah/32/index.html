Aramean Beer Vinegar & Storeroom Exception

Mishna
Vinegar made of Aramean beer is prohibited — they mix in yeast from yayin nesekh (libation wine).
Rav Ashi
But vinegar from a storeroom is permitted — if another substance were mixed in, it would spoil over time.
Reasoning
The storeroom vinegar's quality proves no foreign substance was added — adulteration would cause spoilage.
Principle Observable physical evidence (spoilage) can indicate whether a prohibited substance was added.

The Story of Hadrianic Earthenware

🌱 Emperor Hadrian tilled virgin soil never before cultivated
↓
🍇 Planted grapevines that yielded wine of the highest quality
↓
🏺 Stored the wine in white jugs that absorbed the wine deeply
↓
💔 Broke the jugs into shards for portable use
↓
💧 Soaked the shards in water anywhere they stopped — and drank the flavored water
↓
🏅 R' Yehoshua ben Levi: Our first-rate wine equals only their third soaking!
Why Prohibited These shards still contain absorbed gentile wine — benefiting from them in any way raises the question of deriving benefit from yayin nesekh.

Can You Use Prohibited Shards to Support a Bed?

Hadrianic earthenware shards
Absorbed with gentile wine
↓
Can one use them to support bed legs?
(No wine benefit — just structural support)
↓
One Opinion
Permitted — no actual wine benefit is derived from the shards
Other Opinion
Prohibited — one desires the continued existence of a forbidden item
↓
⚖️ Halakha: Follows the opinion that prohibits this use
Legal Concept Rotzeh b'kiyumo — desiring a prohibited item's continued existence for another purpose. The strict ruling: even indirect reliance on a prohibited object is forbidden.

Wineskins vs. Jugs of Gentiles

Gentile Wineskins

  • Prohibited to make donkey blankets
  • Decree: one might sew a broken wineskin onto his own
  • Absorbed wine would mix with Jew's wine
  • Even indirect benefit forbidden

Gentile Jugs

  • Permitted to sell
  • No substantive prohibited entity visible
  • Wine not recognizable in the vessel
  • Indirect benefit allowed
Rava's Distinction Wineskins are banned by rabbinic decree — one might repair his own wineskin with the gentile's, causing absorbed wine to mix. Jugs lack a recognizable prohibited substance.

Hides Torn Opposite the Heart

FeatureStatus
Torn opposite heart + aperture shape + trace of blood❌ Prohibited (idolatrous sacrifice)
Torn opposite heart but no blood trace✅ Permitted
No blood + gentile salted the hide❌ Prohibited (salt may have removed blood)
Circular tear (R' Shimon ben Gamliel)❌ Prohibited — sign of ritual extraction
Elongated tear (R' Shimon ben Gamliel)✅ Permitted — not a ritual sign
Halakha Shmuel rules like Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel: only a circular tear indicates idolatrous sacrifice. Abaye insists on understanding the reasoning, not just memorizing the ruling — "Is it simply learn the lesson; let it be like a song?"
