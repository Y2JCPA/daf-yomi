Avodah Zarah 32 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is vinegar from a storeroom permitted according to Rav Ashi?
A. Because storerooms are under Jewish supervision
B. Because any foreign substance mixed in would cause spoilage over time
C. Because Aramean beer vinegar is a different category
D. Because storeroom vinegar is too dilute to contain wine
Correct: B — Rav Ashi reasons that if wine yeast had been added to storeroom vinegar, the vinegar would have spoiled — its preservation proves no prohibited substance was mixed in.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What was special about Hadrianic earthenware that made it so valuable?
A. It was made from rare clay found only in Rome
B. White jugs absorbed high-quality wine from Hadrian's virgin-soil vineyards, flavoring water even after multiple soakings
C. Hadrian personally blessed each vessel
D. The earthenware contained medicinal herbs along with wine
Correct: B — Hadrian cultivated virgin soil producing exceptional wine, stored in white jugs that deeply absorbed it. R' Yehoshua ben Levi said Jewish first-rate wine equaled only the third soaking of these shards.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the halakha regarding using Hadrianic earthenware shards to support bed legs?
A. Permitted — no wine benefit is derived
B. Permitted — the shards are considered nullified
C. Prohibited — one desires the continued existence of a forbidden item
D. Prohibited — the wine might drip onto the floor
Correct: C — The concept of 'rotzeh b'kiyumo' — desiring a prohibited item's continued existence for another purpose — applies. The halakha follows the stringent opinion that prohibits even this indirect use.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel, when is a hide with a tear opposite the heart permitted?
A. When there is no blood trace at all
B. When the tear is elongated rather than circular
C. When the hide has been salted by the gentile
D. When the tear is on the left side only
Correct: B — Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel distinguishes: a circular tear indicates ritual heart extraction for idolatry, but an elongated tear does not bear this sign — so it is permitted. The halakha follows his view.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Shmuel permit doing business with a Jew on his way to an idolatrous festival but prohibit it upon his return?
A. The Jew might repent before arriving, but after returning he has already sinned
B. Jewish law only applies after the sin is committed
C. Business on the way would fund the trip, but after returning it would not
D. It is a gezeirah from the time of Hadrian
Correct: A — Shmuel gives the Jew the benefit of the doubt — perhaps he will retract and not attend. But upon return, the transgression is already done, and engaging in business would effectively support the completed sin.
