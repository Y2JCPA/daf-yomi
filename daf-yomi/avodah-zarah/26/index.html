Avodah Zarah 26: Trust, Caregiving & Boundaries

מילדת — midwife · מינקת — wet nurse · מומר — apostate
CORE THEME This daf explores the limits of trust between Jewish and gentile communities in life-and-death caregiving — midwifery, nursing, and circumcision — and the treatment of those who sever themselves from the Jewish community: heretics, informers, and apostates.

Gentile Midwife for a Jewish Woman

Rabbi Meir
A gentile midwife may never deliver a Jewish child — even with observers present.
The Rabbis
She may deliver when other women stand over her watching her actions.
Rabbi Meir
She can press the infant's temple (צֶדַע) and kill him — an act invisible to bystanders!
The Rabbis
The story of the boastful midwife? She was just bragging to intimidate her rival — בדברים בעלמא.
KEY STORY A gentile midwife boasted: 'May evils befall you equal to the Jewish infants I've killed — extracting blood like foam of a river (אופיא דנהרא).' The Rabbis dismiss this as empty intimidation.

TALMUDIC METHOD This is a classic tzrikhuta — showing why two seemingly redundant rulings are each independently necessary.

The Pit: Who Goes Down, Who Comes Up?

Gentiles (General)

  • One may NOT lower them into a pit
  • One need NOT raise them from a pit
  • Passive non-assistance only

Heretics, Informers & Apostates

  • One LOWERS them into a pit
  • One does NOT raise them out
  • Active measures permitted
  • May scrape away a ledge (pretext: protecting from animals)
  • May cover pit with stone (pretext: passing animals over it)
  • May remove a ladder (pretext: getting son off roof)
SOURCES Rav Yosef bar Ḥama / Rav Sheshet (ledge), Rabba & Rav Yosef (stone), Ravina (ladder) — each provides a different scenario with a permissible pretext.

Who Is an Apostate (מומר) vs. a Heretic (מין)?

Rav Aḥa
Sins due to appetite (תיאבון) = apostate. Sins to express insolence (להכעיס) = heretic.
Ravina
Even sinning to express insolence = apostate. A heretic is specifically an idol worshipper.
Objection
A baraita says: one who eats a flea or mosquito is an apostate — but that's clearly not appetite-driven!
Resolution
He desires to experience the taste of forbidden food — so it is still classified as appetite (תיאבון).
R' YOḤANAN TO R' ABBAHU R' Yoḥanan challenges: Deuteronomy 22:3 includes the apostate in the obligation to return lost items — so how can you also lower him into a pit? Resolution: the lost-item verse refers to an apostate of appetite; the pit refers to one of insolence.
