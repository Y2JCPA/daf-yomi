How Do We Kasher Utensils Absorbed with Forbidden Flavor?

Rav Amram
The mishna says spits and grills need libun (white-hot heating). But for sacrificial meat, the mishna in Zevachim says hagalah (purging in hot water) suffices!
Rav Sheshet
Sacrificial meat was permitted when absorbed and later became forbidden. Gentile vessels absorbed inherently forbidden food — a stricter standard applies.
Rava
When sacrificial meat emits its flavor, it emits forbidden flavor regardless! The distinction doesn't hold.
Abaye
The two mishnayot supplement each other — 'its counterpart reveals about it' — both libun and hagalah are required in both cases.
Rava
That principle only works when one source has all the info. Here each source has only part. Rather, daily use itself constitutes purging for sacrificial vessels.
Core Issue The Gemara resolves why sacrificial vessels need only hagalah while gentile vessels need the stricter libun — daily Temple use automatically purges absorbed flavors before they become forbidden.

Noten Ta'am Lifgam: Flavor to the Detriment

Gentile's pot used for cooking that same day
↓
Does it impart flavor to the detriment (lifgam)?
Same Day
Flavor is not to the detriment — food is forbidden by Torah law
Next Day+
Flavor is to the detriment — but still forbidden rabbinically (decree lest one use same-day pot)
Opposing View The other Tanna holds that even flavor to the detriment is Torah-forbidden — so even a same-day pot's flavor is detrimental yet still prohibited.

Rav Amram's Contradiction: Libun vs. Hagalah

Rav Amram
Our mishna: Spits and grills from gentiles require libun (heating white-hot) 🔥
Rav Amram
Zevachim 97a: Spits and grills used for sacrificial meat need only hagalah (purging in hot water) 💧
Rav Sheshet
Amram, my son! Sacrificial meat was permitted when absorbed. Gentile food was forbidden when absorbed — hence the stricter requirement.
Rava
But when the sacrificial flavor exits the vessel, it exits as forbidden notar! What difference does it make that it was once permitted?
Key Dispute Rav Sheshet distinguishes by the status at absorption; Rava argues the status at emission is what matters.
5 / 9

Three Approaches to the Contradiction

Rav Sheshet

  • Absorbed permitted → hagalah suffices
  • Absorbed forbidden → libun required
  • Status at absorption determines kashering method

Abaye

  • Both mishnayot are incomplete
  • 'Its counterpart reveals about it'
  • Both libun AND hagalah required in both cases

Rava

  • Daily Temple use = automatic purging
  • Each day's cooking purges yesterday's flavor
  • No special libun needed for sacrificial vessels
Rava Rejects Abaye The 'counterpart reveals' principle requires one source to have all the information — here neither source is complete, so Abaye's approach fails.

Daily Purging: Peace-Offering vs. Sin-Offering

Offering TypeEating PeriodPurging Challenge
Shelamim (Peace)Two days + nightNext day's cooking purges yesterday's flavor before it becomes notar ✅
Chatat (Sin)One day + nightBecomes notar by morning — next day's cooking expels forbidden flavor ⚠️
Solution for ChatatSame dayCook a shelamim in same pot same day → both expire simultaneously the next morning
Rav Nachman / Rabba bar Avuh Each and every day constitutes purging for the previous day — this is why sacrificial vessels never need special libun.

Kashering the Knife (Amud B)

🔪 Polish the knife — the mishna's basic requirement
↓
🌍 Thrust 10 times into the ground (Rav Ukva bar Chama)
↓
🪨 Must be untilled (hard) earth (Rav Huna son of Rav Yehoshua)
↓
✨ Only for a good knife without notches — entire surface must contact ground (Rav Kahana)
↓
❄️ This suffices only for cold food (Rav Huna son of Rav Yehoshua)
Principle As the knife absorbs (through direct contact with food), so it expels — scraping against hard earth removes surface-absorbed flavor.
