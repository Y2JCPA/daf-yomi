QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say a gentile's pot used the PREVIOUS day is only rabbinically forbidden?
A. Because the flavor is to the detriment (noten ta'am lifgam) after a day
B. Because the pot was already kashered by time
C. Because gentile cooking is only rabbinically forbidden
D. Because the food inside was kosher
Correct: A — After a day, the absorbed flavor imparts taste to the detriment of the food (lifgam), which is not Torah-forbidden. The rabbinic decree exists lest one use a same-day pot.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rava resolve why sacrificial vessels don't need libun (white-hot heating)?
A. Sacrificial meat is less fatty so doesn't penetrate as deeply
B. Each day's cooking automatically purges the previous day's absorbed flavor
C. The Kohanim performed libun anyway as extra stringency
D. Sacrificial vessels were made of special material that doesn't absorb
Correct: B — Rava cites Rav Nachman in the name of Rabba bar Avuh: each day's use constitutes purging for the previous day's flavor, so absorbed sacrificial meat never stays long enough to require libun.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the solution for a sin-offering (chatat) whose meat becomes notar by the next morning?
A. Perform libun on the vessel that same evening
B. Cook a peace-offering in the same pot the same day so both expire simultaneously
C. Only use disposable vessels for sin-offerings
D. Wait two days before reusing the vessel
Correct: B — By cooking a peace-offering (shelamim) in the same pot on the same day, the sin-offering flavor is expelled and both offerings' time limits end simultaneously the next morning.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Huna son of Rav Yehoshua, thrusting a knife 10 times into the ground suffices for what purpose?
A. Using the knife for any food preparation
B. Eating hot food with the knife
C. Eating cold food with the knife
D. Permanently kashering the knife
Correct: C — Rav Huna son of Rav Yehoshua specifies that thrusting the knife into hard, untilled earth 10 times is sufficient only for the purpose of eating cold food — hot food would require more thorough kashering.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did King Shapur thrust the knife into the ground before cutting etrog for Mar Yehuda but NOT for Bati bar Tuvi?
A. Bati bar Tuvi had already eaten non-kosher food
B. Mar Yehuda was a more important guest
C. Shapur was certain Mar Yehuda was meticulous about halakha but uncertain about Bati
D. Bati bar Tuvi told Shapur he didn't need it
Correct: C — King Shapur explained that he was certain Mar Yehuda was meticulous about halakha, but not certain about Bati bar Tuvi — evidenced by Bati having accepted a woman sent by Persian custom the previous night.
