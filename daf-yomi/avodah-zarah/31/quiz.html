QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is wine deposited with a gentile permitted for benefit but not for drinking (according to the Rabbis)?
A. A designated corner prevents exchange, but a single seal doesn't prevent tampering
B. The gentile might have used the wine for libations but not exchanged it
C. Benefit from wine never requires a seal, only drinking does
D. The wine loses its sanctity when stored with a gentile
Correct: A — The designated corner prevents the gentile from exchanging the wine (permitting benefit), but according to the Rabbis, a single seal is insufficient to prevent the gentile from touching or manipulating the wine, so drinking remains prohibited without a double seal.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava, which scenario does NOT qualify as a 'seal within a seal'?
A. A basin smeared with clay and stamped over a barrel
B. A wineskin in a sack with the stopper facing upward
C. A basket fastened over a barrel
D. A wineskin with neck bent inward in a tied and sealed sack
Correct: B — When the stopper faces upward, the sack alone does not conceal or protect the seal — the stopper is accessible. The stopper must face downward so the sack serves as a genuine outer seal.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did the Sages initially prohibit ALL wine from Samaritan cities near idolatrous villages, but later permit sealed barrels?
A. They discovered Samaritans had stopped worshipping idols
B. They realized Samaritans are only lax about gentile contact with open barrels, not sealed ones
C. Sealed barrels cannot absorb impurity from idols
D. The idolatrous villages had been destroyed
Correct: B — The Sages revised their reasoning: Samaritans are not particular about gentile contact only with open barrels. With sealed barrels, they maintain security because allowing tampering publicly would cost them Jewish business.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Rav permit Aramean beer generally but say his son Ḥiyya should not drink it?
A. Ḥiyya held a stricter halakhic opinion about gentile beverages
B. The hops impair snake venom enough for healthy people, but Ḥiyya's weak constitution makes even impaired venom dangerous
C. Ḥiyya was a nazir and prohibited from all fermented drinks
D. Rav was concerned about intermarriage specifically for his son
Correct: B — Rav's concern was exposure to venom. The bitterness of hops impairs the venom enough for most people, but someone with a weak constitution like Ḥiyya could still be harmed by the weakened venom.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the significance of R' Yoḥanan quoting 'Where the tree falls, there it shall be' about R' Tanḥum?
A. He was criticizing R' Tanḥum for never leaving Parod
B. He was praising R' Tanḥum as bar Kappara's disciple who perpetuates his teacher's wisdom in the place where bar Kappara lived
C. He was mourning that bar Kappara's teachings had fallen and been lost
D. He was suggesting that R' Tanḥum's rulings were as immovable as a fallen tree
Correct: B — R' Yoḥanan reinterpreted the verse: 'there its fruits shall be' — meaning a Sage's disciples (fruits) continue his Torah in the very place he lived. R' Tanḥum, bar Kappara's student, preserved his master's teaching in Parod.
