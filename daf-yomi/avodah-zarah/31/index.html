Wine Deposited with a Gentile

Mishnah
Wine deposited with a gentile: drinking is prohibited, but benefit is permitted.
Gemara
But Bekhorot teaches: produce deposited with a gentile has the status of gentile produce — shouldn't wine be entirely prohibited?
Gemara
Benefit is permitted when the gentile designated a corner (יִחֵד לוֹ קֶרֶן זָוִית) — locked storage with Jew-only access.
R' Tanḥum
Bar Kappara taught: even drinking deposited wine is permitted!
R' Yoḥanan
"Where the tree falls, there it shall be" — bar Kappara's fruits (disciples) perpetuate his wisdom through R' Tanḥum.
Resolution R' Zeira resolves: Bar Kappara's leniency follows R' Eliezer; the Mishnah's stricter view follows the Rabbis.

R' Eliezer vs. the Rabbis: Sealed Wine in a Gentile's Property

R' Eliezer — Lenient

  • House in gentile's courtyard filled with wine
  • Key or seal in Jew's possession → Permitted
  • One seal is sufficient security
  • R' Yoḥanan rules like R' Eliezer
  • Halakha follows R' Eliezer (per Rav/Ze'eiri)

Rabbis — Strict

  • Same scenario with key/seal
  • Still prohibited — not enough security
  • Wine requires a seal within a seal (חותם בתוך חותם)
  • R' Elazar rules like the Rabbis
  • Concern: gentile may tamper despite single seal
Halakha Rav Ḥisda transmits: The halakha follows R' Eliezer — one seal suffices for wine stored with a gentile.

What Counts as a Seal Within a Seal? (Rava)

ScenarioStatus
Basin over barrel mouth, smeared with clay + stamped✅ Seal within a seal
Basin over barrel mouth, not smeared/stamped❌ Not double-sealed
Basket over barrel, fastened to it✅ Seal within a seal
Basket over barrel, not fastened❌ Not double-sealed
Wineskin in sack, stopper facing down✅ Seal within a seal
Wineskin in sack, stopper facing up❌ Not double-sealed
Wineskin neck bent inward + sack tied & sealed✅ Seal within a seal
Principle A חותם בתוך חותם requires two independent layers of closure — the inner seal must be concealed or secured by the outer one.

Why Did the Sages Prohibit Gentile Beer?

Rami b. Ḥama / R' Yitzḥak
Due to concern about befriending gentiles while drinking together, which might lead to intermarriage (חתנות).
Rav Naḥman
Due to the concern of exposure (גִּלּוּי) — snake venom in the water used to brew beer.
Gemara
But we also expose our vats and barrels! What's different about gentile beer?
Gemara
The concern is where water is allowed to settle (exposed and still for longer periods).
Gemara
Then aged beer should be permitted — R' Yehuda HaNasi taught poison prevents aging!
Gemara
The Sages decreed on aged beer due to new beer — a blanket protective decree.
Two Reasons The dual rationale — social proximity and venom exposure — leads to a broad decree covering all gentile beer.
