When Does Grape Juice Become 'Wine'?

🍇 Grapes are trodden in the upper winepress
↓
Juice flows down through a pipe toward the vat
↓
Does the juice enter the collection vat (בור)?
Not yet
Still 'juice' — gentile touch does not create yayin nesekh
Yes
Now has status of wine — gentile touch renders it forbidden
Key Principle The Mishnah rules that grape juice becomes halakhically 'wine' — subject to the prohibition of yayin nesekh — only once it descends into the collection vat.

Working with Grapes: What's Permitted?

Forbidden

  • Harvesting grapes with a Jew who makes wine in impurity (causes ritual impurity)
  • Treading grapes with such a Jew (actively assisting in rendering impure)

Permitted

  • Harvesting grapes into the winepress with a gentile
  • Reason: One may impart impurity to non-sacred produce (ḥullin) in Eretz Yisrael
Note The distinction turns on whether the produce is sacred (terumah) or non-sacred (ḥullin). Non-sacred produce may be rendered impure.

When Must One Separate Tithes from Wine?

Rabbis
One must tithe from the time the wine descends into the collection vat (בור).
R' Akiva
One must tithe only from misheyikpe — when solid residue floats on top of the wine.
Gemara
What does R' Akiva mean by 'floating'? Seeds in the vat, or yeast in the barrel?
Gemara
Proof from the Mishnah: One may still drink from the upper press even after floating → it means seeds floating in the vat.
Rava
Our Mishnah on yayin nesekh follows the Rabbis; the tithes Mishnah follows R' Akiva. No contradiction.
Resolution Rava harmonizes the two Mishnahs by assigning them to different Tannaitic opinions about when wine production is 'complete.'

Rav Zevid's Version (School of R' Oshaya)

OpinionTithes Obligated From
RabbisWine descends into vat AND seeds float on top
R' AkivaWine is siphoned from vat into barrels
Three Tannaim? The Gemara asks: Does the yayin nesekh Mishnah (descent into vat alone) represent a third view? Answer: No — yayin nesekh is stricter; the Sages imposed the prohibition even before seeds float.

Rav Huna: The Wicker Basket (Gargutni)

A gentile touches wine in the collection vat → wine in vat is forbidden
↓
Was the wicker basket returned to the upper winepress?
No
Wine remaining in the winepress is permitted
Yes
Forbidden wine in the basket contaminates the wine in the winepress
Nitzok (Stream) The Gemara asks: Doesn't the stream from the basket connect it to the vat? Rejected — Rav Huna refers to a case where the wine level in the vat rose and reached the basket directly (like R' Ḥiyya's funnel case).

The Child Prodigy: Treading Grapes with a Gentile

Questioners
May one tread grapes in the winepress together with a gentile?
Child (age 6)
Yes — this is the ruling of our Mishnah.
Questioners
But the gentile touches the wine with his hands — making it yayin nesekh! How can the Jew benefit?
Child
We tie his hands so he cannot pour a libation.
Questioners
But he touches it with his feet!
Child
Rendering wine a libation with one's foot is not considered a libation.
Halakhic Principle Foot contact does not constitute an act of libation — only intentional hand contact renders wine yayin nesekh.
