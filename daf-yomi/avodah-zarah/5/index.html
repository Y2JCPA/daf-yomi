Avodah Zarah 5 — Repentance, Mortality & Festival Preparation

CORE THEME The Gemara explores the universality of teshuvah (repentance) through David and the Golden Calf, then asks a radical question: Would we exist if Israel hadn't sinned? Reish Lakish argues Israel would have become angel-like. The daf concludes with the practical halakhah of the mishna — why three days of restriction before gentile festivals, tied to the time needed to check animals for blemishes.

Individual vs. Community Repentance

Individual — King David

  • If an individual sins → 'Go to David and learn'
  • One might think: only accepted because sin is not publicized
  • David 'raised the yoke (ullah) of repentance' (II Samuel 23:1)
  • Teaches: even great individuals can return

Community — Golden Calf

  • If the community sins → 'Go to Israel at the Golden Calf'
  • One might think: only accepted because prayers are numerous
  • Public sin doesn't prevent teshuvah
  • Teaches: even collective shame can be overcome
KEY PRINCIPLE Both cases are necessary — neither individual privacy nor communal prayer is the reason teshuvah works. It is inherently available to all.

Mitzvot & Transgressions in the World to Come

R' Shmuel bar Naḥmani / R' Yonatan:
A person's deeds accompany them
↓
Performs a Mitzvah
It walks before him in the World to Come
'Your righteousness shall go before you' (Isaiah 58:8)
Commits a Transgression
It shrouds and leads him on the Day of Judgment
'They go up into the waste and are lost' (Job 6:18)
↓
R' Elazar adds: A transgression is tied to a person like a dog — it never leaves.
Joseph refused Potiphar's wife: 'to lie by her' in this world → lest he be 'with her' in the next.
TAKEAWAY Our actions — good and bad — become our eternal companions.

Would We Exist Without the Golden Calf?

Reish Lakish
Be grateful to our ancestors who sinned — had they not, we would not have come into the world. After Sinai they were like angels (Psalms 82:6), and would not have had children.
Gemara
But Noah was told 'Be fruitful and multiply'!
Reish Lakish
That was only until Sinai. At the revelation they became angel-like.
Gemara
But God said 'Return to your tents' — meaning resume marital relations!
Reish Lakish
That refers to conjugal enjoyment, not procreation.
Gemara
But 'with their children forever' implies future generations! And R' Yosei says Mashiaḥ requires all destined souls to be born!
Resolution
We would have been born — but it would be as if we hadn't come into the world, being insignificant next to the immortal Sinai generation.
RESOLUTION Future generations would still exist, but would be of no importance compared to the deathless generation of Sinai.

Understanding One's Teacher

אין אדם עומד על דעת רבו עד ארבעים שנה
RABBA'S INSIGHT Moses called Israel ingrates, children of ingrates — they complained about the manna; Adam complained about Ḥavah. Yet Moses himself waited 40 years before rebuking them (Deuteronomy 29:3-4).

Rabba concludes: A student does not fully grasp his teacher's reasoning until forty years have passed.

Torah, Kindness & the Evil Inclination

📖 R' Yoḥanan / R' Bana'a: 'Happy are you that sow beside all waters' (Isaiah 32:20)
↓
🌱 'Sowing' = acts of charity (tzedakah) — from Hosea 10:12
↓
💧 'Waters' = Torah — from Isaiah 55:1: 'Come for water'
↓
🐂 'Ox and donkey' = subjugate yourself to Torah like an ox to a yoke, a donkey to a burden
↓
✨ Result: When Israel engages in Torah & kindness, their yetzer hara is given over to THEM — not the reverse
SCHOOL OF ELIYAHU A person should always make himself subjugated to Torah — like an ox to its yoke and a donkey to its load.
