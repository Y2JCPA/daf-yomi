Avodah Zarah 5 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara need to teach repentance for BOTH an individual and a community?
A. Because individual repentance involves different rituals than communal repentance
B. Because each case alone could be misunderstood — private sin seems easier, and communal prayer seems more powerful
C. Because the Torah mentions both David and the Golden Calf in the same verse
D. Because communal repentance requires a minyan while individual does not
Correct: B — Without both examples, one might think an individual can only repent because his sin is private, or that a community succeeds only because of numerous prayers. Both cases are needed to show teshuvah is inherently available.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Elazar, what is a transgression compared to?
A. A chain around the neck
B. A shadow that follows
C. A dog that never leaves
D. A wound that never heals
Correct: C — R' Elazar says a transgression is 'tied to a person like a dog' — it follows them and never departs. He derives this from the verse about Joseph and Potiphar's wife.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve Reish Lakish's claim that without the Golden Calf we wouldn't exist?
A. It rejects Reish Lakish's position entirely
B. It says we would have been born but would be of no importance next to the immortal Sinai generation
C. It says only the tribe of Levi would have survived
D. It says God would have created a new humanity
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes we would still have been born (future souls must come into the world for Mashiaḥ), but we'd be insignificant — 'as though we had not come into the world' — overshadowed by the deathless Sinai generation.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why do gentiles need only 3 days to prepare a sacrificial animal, while Jews need 30?
A. Gentile festivals are less important
B. Gentiles only disqualify animals missing a limb, while Jews disqualify for even minor blemishes
C. Gentiles use smaller animals that are easier to inspect
D. The Torah gives gentiles a shorter preparation period explicitly
Correct: B — Jewish law disqualifies animals for even minor blemishes (like a corneal spot), requiring extensive inspection. Gentile sacrificial law (Bnei Noaḥ) only disqualifies an animal missing an entire limb, which is easy to spot in 3 days.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rabba derive from Moses waiting 40 years before rebuking Israel?
A. A prophet must wait until old age to deliver rebuke
B. Israel wandered 40 years as punishment for the Golden Calf
C. A student does not fully understand his teacher's reasoning until 40 years
D. The Torah was not complete until 40 years after Sinai
Correct: C — Rabba says: 'A person does not understand the opinion of his teacher until after forty years' — derived from the fact that Moses waited 40 years before telling Israel 'God has not given you a heart to know' (Deuteronomy 29:3).
