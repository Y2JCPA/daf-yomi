When Does Gentile Contact Prohibit Wine?

Shmuel
I delayed my ruling — seeking a tanna to either prohibit benefit (like R' Natan) or permit drinking (like R' Shimon)
Rav
A gentile baby one day old who touches wine renders it yayin nesekh — but only prohibited for drinking, not benefit
Rav Shimi bar Ḥiyya
A baraita distinguishes adults from minors — only those who know idol worship render wine prohibited!
Rav Naḥman / Shmuel
Even after circumcision and immersion, purchased slaves render wine prohibited until idolatry leaves their mouths
Core Issue The Gemara navigates multiple variables: intent, age, conversion status, and upbringing to determine when gentile contact with wine creates a prohibition.

The Incident at Biram: Accidental Contact

Narrator
A gentile climbing a palm tree accidentally touched wine with the tip of a branch while descending
Rav
The wine may be sold to gentiles — it is permitted for benefit
Rav Kahana & Rav Asi
But Master, you said a gentile baby one day old renders wine yayin nesekh without intent!
Rav
I said prohibited for drinking — did I say prohibited for benefit? Selling is permitted!
Distinction Rav distinguishes between isur shetiyah (prohibition of drinking) and isur hana'ah (prohibition of benefit) — even when wine is rendered nesekh, benefit may still be allowed.

Purchased Slaves vs. Sons of Maidservants

Purchased Slaves

  • Acquired from gentiles
  • Circumcised but not yet immersed
  • Adults render wine as nesekh
  • Rav Naḥman/Shmuel: Even AFTER immersion, wine is prohibited until idolatry leaves their mouths

Sons of Maidservants

  • Raised in a Jewish home
  • Circumcised but not yet immersed
  • Only adults render wine prohibited
  • Minors who don't know idolatry do NOT render wine prohibited
  • Once immersed — do not render wine prohibited
Dispute The baraita challenges Rav Naḥman/Shmuel by teaching that once immersed, slaves no longer render wine prohibited — upbringing and environment matter.
