QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did the Rabbis reject Moses' grinding of the Golden Calf as proof that grinding alone suffices?
A. Moses ground it to test the Israelites like a sota, not to destroy an idol
B. The Golden Calf was made of gold and couldn't truly be ground up
C. Moses acted on a special Divine command that doesn't apply generally
D. The calf dust was thrown into a river that doesn't grow vegetation
Correct: A — The Rabbis point to Exodus 32:20 which says Moses made Israel drink the water — he ground the calf to test the people like sota women, identifying the guilty, not as the standard method of idol destruction.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why couldn't the Jewish people render the brazen serpent a forbidden idol, according to the Rabbis?
A. It was made of bronze, which cannot become an idol
B. It belonged to Moses, and a person cannot render forbidden another's property
C. It was originally made for healing, so it had inherent sanctity
D. Hezekiah had already nullified its idol status before the people worshipped it
Correct: B — God told Moses 'Make yourself a serpent' — meaning from his own property. Since the serpent belonged to Moses, the people could not give it forbidden idol status because one cannot prohibit what belongs to someone else.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was Rabban Gamliel's halakhic basis for saying Aphrodite's statue was not a forbidden idol?
A. Greek gods have no real power and thus cannot be considered idols
B. The statue was too small to qualify as an object of worship
C. It was not treated as a deity — people urinated before it and it stood on a sewage pipe
D. Aphrodite was a goddess of love, not a forbidden form of worship
Correct: C — Rabban Gamliel invoked the verse 'their gods' — only a statue treated as a deity is forbidden. Since people urinated before it and it stood on a sewage pipe, it was treated with disrespect, indicating it lacked true idolatrous status.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rav Ḥama bar Yosef defend Rabban Gamliel against the challenge from the idol Peor?
A. Peor was not a real idol but merely a folk custom
B. Peor's standard worship involved defecation, so such behavior didn't negate its status — but Aphrodite's worship didn't involve such acts
C. Peor was located outdoors, not in a bathhouse
D. The comparison to Peor is invalid because Peor predated the Torah
Correct: B — Rav Ḥama distinguishes: Peor was worshipped through defecation, so doing so before it didn't show disrespect. But Aphrodite was not worshipped that way, so people urinating before it genuinely indicated a lack of reverence and negated its idol status.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why was Rabban Gamliel unable to answer Proclus's question while still inside the bathhouse?
A. He was naked and it is improper to speak while unclothed
B. It is prohibited to discuss Torah matters — even in a secular language — in a bathhouse
C. He needed time to formulate his three-part response
D. He did not want to dignify the question in front of other bathers
Correct: B — Abaye teaches that it is prohibited to say sacred (Torah-related) statements even in a secular language in a bathhouse or bathroom. Since Proclus's question required a Torah-based response, Rabban Gamliel had to wait until he left.
