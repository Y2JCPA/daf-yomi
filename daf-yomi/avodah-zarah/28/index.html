Internal vs. External Injuries

Internal Injury

  • From the lips inward (R' Ami)
  • May desecrate Shabbat for treatment
  • May NOT be treated by gentiles (too dangerous)
  • Includes: burning fever (R' Yochanan)

External Injury

  • From the lips outward
  • Standard Shabbat restrictions apply
  • Rabbinic healing prohibitions in effect
  • Exception: back of hand/foot = internal (Rav)
Special Cases Rav Zutra bar Toviyya: Any injury requiring medical evaluation to determine if fatal — one may desecrate Shabbat for its treatment.

Are Gum/Teeth Afflictions Internal or External?

R' Eliezer
Afflictions in the gums (kakhei) or teeth — what is the halakha?
R' Eliezer
They are in firm parts of the body → like external?
Or inside the mouth → like internal?
Abaye
Proof from Mishnah Shabbat 111a: One who is merely 'concerned' about tooth pain may not sip vinegar. But if it hurts greatly — it's permitted!
Gemara
Rejected: Perhaps the Tanna calls even great pain mere 'concern.'
Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak
Tzafdina is different — it begins in the mouth but spreads to the intestines. No general proof for gums.
Outcome The dilemma of R' Eliezer remains unresolved (teiku). We cannot definitively classify gum afflictions as internal or external.

R' Yochanan and the Gentile Matron — The Tzafdina Incident

🤒 R' Yochanan contracts tzafdina — a disease of the teeth and gums
↓
👩‍⚕️ He visits a well-known gentile healer who prepares medicine on Thursday and Friday
↓
❓ R' Yochanan asks: What about Shabbat? She says he won't need it
↓
🤫 She demands an oath of secrecy before revealing the remedy
↓
✋ R' Yochanan swears: 'To the God of the Jews, I will not reveal it'
↓
📢 The next day he teaches the remedy publicly!
↓
💡 His oath meant: 'I won't reveal it to God' — but I will reveal it to His people
But What About Chillul Hashem? R' Yochanan explained the loophole to the matron immediately after she disclosed the remedy, so there was no desecration of God's name.

Tzafdina: Symptoms, Causes & Remedies

CategoryDetails
SymptomsPlace item between teeth → blood flows from gum rows
CauseVery cold wheat foods, very hot barley foods, remains of fried fish (kasa deharsena)
Remedy #1 (R' Acha b. Rava)Water with steeped leaven + olive oil + salt
Remedy #2 (Mar bar R' Ashi)Goose fat smeared with a goose feather
Remedy #3 (Abaye's Arab)Olive seeds (< ⅓ ripe) burned on a new hoe, applied to gum line
Abaye's Testimony "I prepared all of these medicines and was not cured until a certain Arab told me: Take olive seeds less than one-third ripe, burn them on a new hoe, and stick them along the gums."

Can an Important Person Seek Gentile Medical Care?

Rule: Any injury for which Shabbat is desecrated → may NOT be treated by gentiles
↓
But R' Yochanan was treated by a gentile for tzafdina!
↓
An important person is different — gentiles would not dare harm him
↓
Challenge: R' Abbahu was important, yet Ya'akov the heretic poisoned his leg!
↓
R' Yochanan's Case
His healer was an expert physician who wouldn't risk her reputation
R' Abbahu's Case
Heretics (minim) are different — they act with self-destructive hatred
Distinction An expert gentile physician would not harm an important patient, but heretics are driven by ideology and will risk their own reputation to cause harm.
