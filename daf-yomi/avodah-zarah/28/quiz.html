Avodah Zarah 28 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ami, from what point on the body is a wound considered an 'internal injury'?
A. From the neck inward
B. From the lips inward
C. From the ribcage inward
D. From the navel inward
Correct: B — Rabbi Ami defined 'internal injury' as from the lips inward — meaning any wound or affliction inside the oral cavity and deeper is treated with the severity of an internal injury regarding Shabbat laws.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How did R' Yochanan justify publicly revealing the remedy despite his oath to the gentile matron?
A. He argued the oath was made under duress and therefore void
B. His oath 'To the God of the Jews I will not reveal it' meant he would reveal it to God's people
C. He claimed the remedy was ineffective so the oath didn't matter
D. He had another rabbi annul the oath through hatarat nedarim
Correct: B — R' Yochanan's carefully worded oath — 'To the God of the Jews, I will not reveal it' — was interpreted as: I will not reveal it to God, but I WILL reveal it to His people, Israel. He disclosed this interpretation to her immediately.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why was R' Yochanan permitted to seek treatment from a gentile healer despite the rule against it for life-threatening conditions?
A. Tzafdina was not actually life-threatening
B. The treatment was only on weekdays, not Shabbat
C. He was an important person and she was an expert physician who wouldn't risk her reputation
D. He received special permission from the Sanhedrin
Correct: C — The Gemara gives two reasons: (1) An important person like R' Yochanan would not be harmed by gentiles, and (2) his healer was an expert physician whose professional reputation prevented her from causing harm.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Rav Yehuda tell Rav Shmuel bar Yehuda that treating his eye on Shabbat was permitted for everyone else but prohibited for him?
A. Rav Shmuel bar Yehuda's condition was not severe enough
B. Because Rav Shmuel bar Yehuda had publicly disparaged the lenient ruling
C. Rav Shmuel bar Yehuda was not an important enough person
D. The medicine required was different from standard eye treatment
Correct: B — Rav Shmuel bar Yehuda had publicly declared that following Rav Yehuda's ruling was a desecration of Shabbat. Because he showed disdain for the ruling, Rav Yehuda held him to his own stricter standard — a form of measure-for-measure accountability.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What distinguishes the heretic Ya'akov (who poisoned R' Abbahu) from the gentile matron (who healed R' Yochanan)?
A. Ya'akov was not a trained physician while the matron was
B. Heretics act from ideological hatred and will harm even important people, unlike ordinary gentile physicians
C. Ya'akov used forbidden magic while the matron used natural remedies
D. The matron was Jewish and Ya'akov was not
Correct: B — The Gemara distinguishes heretics (minim) from ordinary gentiles: heretics harbor deep ideological animosity and will risk their own standing to cause harm, while a professional gentile physician would not jeopardize her reputation by harming a prominent patient.
