Avodah Zarah 13 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the core disagreement between Reish Lakish and R' Yochanan on this daf?
A. Whether deriving benefit from idol worship is prohibited
B. Whether causing (indirect) benefit to idol worship is prohibited
C. Whether one may attend a pagan fair at all
D. Whether the wreath of roses constitutes actual idol worship
Correct: B — Both agree deriving benefit is prohibited. Their dispute is whether merely causing benefit — such as paying taxes that indirectly support idolatry — is also forbidden. R' Yochanan says yes (kal va'chomer); Reish Lakish says no.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In R' Natan's wreath scenario, why is the Jew in an impossible situation?
A. He must choose between wearing an idol and paying a fine
B. Wearing the wreath = deriving benefit; refusing = causing benefit (via full tax)
C. The wreath itself is an idol that must be destroyed
D. He is forced to worship the idol publicly
Correct: B — If he wears the wreath, he derives benefit (tax reduction from idolatry). If he refuses, he pays the full tax — which causes benefit to idol worship. Either way, he is entangled with idolatry.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why must a consecrated animal from a pagan fair be starved rather than have its hooves cut?
A. Starving is faster and more humane
B. Cutting hooves would degrade sanctified property or appear as inflicting a blemish
C. The Torah explicitly commands starvation for consecrated animals
D. Cutting hooves would make the meat kosher and someone might eat it
Correct: B — Abaye says cutting hooves degrades hekdesh. Rava says it appears as inflicting a mum (blemish) on a sacrificial animal. Slaughtering is also rejected because someone might accidentally eat it and misuse consecrated property.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Yosei, when may a Kohen leave Eretz Yisrael to study Torah?
A. Only if there is no Torah scholar at all in Eretz Yisrael
B. Never — a Kohen must remain in Eretz Yisrael at all times
C. Even if suitable teachers exist in Eretz Yisrael, since one may not merit to learn from everyone
D. Only with the permission of the Sanhedrin
Correct: C — R' Yosei permits leaving even when teachers are available in Eretz Yisrael, because 'a person does not merit to learn from everyone' — a specific teacher abroad may be more suitable. R' Yochanan rules halakha like R' Yosei.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why were R' Yaakov and R' Yirmeya actually permitted to buy items at the pagan fair?
A. They purchased only food items, which are always permitted
B. They both bought from homeowners (private individuals), not from taxed merchants
C. The fair had been deconsecrated by a Roman decree
D. They purchased the items before the official fair began
Correct: B — The prohibition applies only to buying from merchants, whose sales are taxed to support idolatry. Both Sages bought from homeowners (private sellers) whose sales were not taxed, though each mistakenly thought the other had bought from a merchant.
