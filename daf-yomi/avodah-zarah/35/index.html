Why Is the Cheese of Gentiles Prohibited?

R' Yehoshua ben Levi
It is due to concern for puncturing — a snake may have deposited venom in the cheese
R' Yirmeya
If so, dry cheese and aged cheese should be permitted! Snake venom prevents drying and aging
R' Ḥanina
It is because the cheese inevitably contains particles of non-kosher milk
Shmuel
It is because gentiles curdle cheese with the skin of the stomach of a neveilah (unslaughtered carcass)
Rav Malkiyya
They smooth its surface with pig fat
Rav Ḥisda
They curdle it with vinegar from gentile wine, from which benefit is prohibited
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
They curdle it with sap subject to the prohibition of orlah (first three years' fruit)
KEY QUESTION The Gemara presents at least six different reasons for the prohibition — reflecting the policy of concealing the true reason for a new decree.

Rabbi Yehoshua vs. Rabbi Yishmael: Cheese & Idol Worship

R' Yehoshua
Cheese of gentiles is prohibited because they curdle it with the stomach contents of calves used for idol worship
R' Yishmael
If so, why didn't the Sages prohibit deriving benefit from the cheese entirely?
R' Yehoshua
Diverts the conversation to Song of Songs: "For your love is better than wine"
Gemara
Why not answer that the cheese has no substantive prohibited entity (ein bo mammash)?
Sages
Because rennet curdles the cheese — it is essential to the cheese's formation, so it is considered substantive
PRINCIPLE A prohibited substance that is essential to an item's formation is considered a substantive prohibited entity, even if not independently discernible.

Six Reasons for Prohibiting Gentile Cheese

Eating Concern

  • R' Yehoshua ben Levi: Snake venom (puncturing)
  • R' Ḥanina: Particles of non-kosher milk
  • Shmuel: Curdled with neveilah stomach skin

Benefit Concern

  • Rav Malkiyya: Surface smoothed with pig fat
  • Rav Ḥisda: Curdled with gentile wine vinegar
  • Rav Naḥman b. Yitzḥak: Curdled with orlah sap
DIFFICULTY Rav Ḥisda's and Rav Naḥman's reasons imply benefit should be prohibited — but the Mishna permits benefit. The Gemara leaves this unresolved (kashya).

Why Did R' Yehoshua Refuse to Answer?

הַשְׁקֵנִי מִנְּשִׁיקוֹת פִּיהוּ – שִׂפְתוֹתֶיךָ דּוֹחֲקוֹת זוֹ לָזוֹ
ULLA'S PRINCIPLE When the Sages in Eretz Yisrael issued a new decree, they would not reveal the reason behind it for 12 months — lest someone who disagrees come to treat the decree with contempt.

R' Yehoshua's deflection to Song of Songs was deliberate: "Press your lips together and do not be hasty to retort."
