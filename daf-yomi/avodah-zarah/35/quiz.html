Avodah Zarah 35 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Gemara conclude that rennet in cheese IS considered a 'substantive prohibited entity,' unlike wine in fish stew?
A. Because rennet is essential to the cheese's formation (it curdles the milk)
B. Because rennet is visible in the final product
C. Because rennet comes from idol worship specifically
D. Because the Torah explicitly prohibits rennet
Correct: A — The Gemara distinguishes: wine in fish stew is incidental and not discernible, but rennet is essential to forming the cheese, so it is treated as a substantive prohibited entity even though it is not independently visible.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Ulla, why did Rabbi Yehoshua refuse to explain the real reason for prohibiting gentile cheese?
A. He did not know the actual reason
B. The policy was to conceal reasons for new decrees for 12 months to prevent contempt
C. He was embarrassed by Rabbi Yishmael's challenge
D. The reason involved a secret tradition that could not be shared publicly
Correct: B — Ulla explains that in Eretz Yisrael, the Sages would not reveal the reason behind a new decree for 12 months, lest someone who disagrees treat it with contempt.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yirmeya challenge the explanation that gentile cheese is prohibited due to snake venom?
A. He argues that cheese is too salty for snakes to approach
B. He notes that dry and aged cheese should be permitted, since venom prevents drying and aging
C. He says there are no snakes in Babylonia
D. He argues that cooking destroys snake venom
Correct: B — Rabbi Yirmeya cites Rabbi Ḥanina's principle: snake venom does not allow substances to dry or age. Therefore, dry cheese and aged cheese should be safe — yet they too are prohibited, undermining the venom explanation.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What difficulty does the Gemara raise against Rav Ḥisda's and Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak's reasons for the cheese prohibition?
A. Their reasons would only apply in Eretz Yisrael, not Babylonia
B. Their reasons imply benefit from the cheese should also be prohibited, contradicting the Mishna
C. Wine vinegar and orlah sap are actually permitted
D. Their reasons conflict with the principle of bitul (nullification)
Correct: B — Both gentile wine vinegar and orlah are items from which benefit is prohibited. If cheese contains these, benefit from the cheese should be forbidden — but the Mishna permits benefit. The Gemara leaves this difficulty unresolved (kashya).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say milk from a gentile cannot simply be tested by curdling it to check for non-kosher milk?
A. Because curdling is itself a prohibited process
B. Because non-kosher milk also curdles
C. Because the concern applies when one wants to use the milk as liquid in kutaḥ, not as cheese
D. Because the gentile may have already removed the non-kosher portion
Correct: C — Curdling works as a test only if one intends to make cheese (non-kosher milk won't curdle). But if one wants to use the milk as liquid in a dish like kutaḥ, curdling is not applicable, and the concern about non-kosher milk admixture remains.
