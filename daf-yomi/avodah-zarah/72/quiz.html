QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Abaye, why doesn't the first buyer acquire the land in the conditional sale case?
A. The kinyan was performed improperly
B. No price was fixed between them
C. The seller explicitly retracted
D. A verbal commitment is not binding
Correct: B — Abaye holds that even with a valid kinyan, an acquisition does not take effect until a price has been fixed. This is supported by the Mishna about selling wine to a gentile and the baraita about produce on donkeys.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the case where the seller promised to sell for 100 dinars but sold to another for 120, why does Rav Ya'akov of Nehar Pekod rule against the first buyer?
A. The kinyan was invalid without witnesses
B. The first buyer failed to pay in time
C. The additional 20 dinars compelled the seller — he wasn't acting in bad faith
D. A verbal price commitment is never binding
Correct: C — Rav Ya'akov argues that the 20-dinar premium shows the seller had a legitimate financial reason to sell to the second buyer, not merely that he was reneging on his promise. The halakha follows this view.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does R' Ḥiyya explain why the wine remnant in the funnel is forbidden?
A. The stream (nitzok) from the funnel connected it to the gentile's jug
B. The gentile touched the funnel directly
C. The wine in the gentile's jug rose up and physically reached the funnel
D. The funnel absorbed forbidden wine from a previous use
Correct: C — R' Ḥiyya explains the Mishna as referring to a case where wine in the gentile's jug actually rose and reached the funnel — making direct contact rather than relying on the stream as a connection. This avoids resolving the nitzok question.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does Rava forbid all the wine in the barrel when the gentile touched the siphon tube?
A. Because nitzok (a stream) is a halakhic connection
B. Because the gentile's touch rendered the tube a forbidden vessel
C. Because all the wine in the barrel is drawn toward the tubes, as if the gentile touched it all
D. Because siphoning is a rabbinically prohibited method of transferring wine
Correct: C — Rava explicitly rejects the nitzok explanation. Instead, he reasons that the suction of the siphon draws all the wine in the barrel toward the tubes, so it is as though the gentile touched all the wine directly.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Under what condition does Mar Zutra son of Rav Naḥman permit a Jew and gentile to drink simultaneously from a kenishkanin?
A. Only if a blessing is recited first
B. Only if the Jew stops drinking before the gentile
C. Only in the house of the Exilarch
D. Only if the tubes are made of metal rather than reed
Correct: B — Mar Zutra permits simultaneous drinking only if the Jew stops first. If the gentile stops first, wine from the gentile's mouth flows back into the vessel and renders all the wine forbidden.
