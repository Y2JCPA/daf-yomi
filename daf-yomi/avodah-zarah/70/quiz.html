Avodah Zarah 70 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
A gentile is locked in a room with wine barrels, but there's a crack in the door. What is Rava's ruling?
A. All wine is forbidden
B. Barrels visible through the crack are permitted; others are forbidden
C. All wine is permitted because the door was locked
D. Barrels visible through the crack are forbidden; others are permitted
Correct: B — Rava rules that barrels the gentile knew could be seen through the crack are permitted — he'd be afraid to tamper with those. Barrels out of sight are forbidden since he could act unobserved.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Rava permit wine when a Jew leaves for Shabbat from a ship, even though the gentile knows the Jew won't return until after Shabbat?
A. The Jew appointed a guardian before leaving
B. Gentiles believe Jews don't truly keep Shabbat and would return for their property
C. Shabbat is too short for the gentile to make a libation
D. The ship's other passengers serve as witnesses
Correct: B — Rava cites Issur the Convert: gentiles assume Jews don't really observe Shabbat and would violate it for monetary gain. So the gentile fears the Jew might come back at any moment.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
When thieves opened many barrels of wine in Pumbedita, why did Rava permit the wine?
A. The barrels were sealed with a special mark
B. Most thieves in Pumbedita are Jewish, so we follow the majority
C. Thieves don't have intent to make libations
D. The wine had already been boiled (mevushal)
Correct: B — Rava's reasoning is based on the principle of rov (majority) — since most thieves in Pumbedita are Jewish, we follow the majority and the wine is not rendered forbidden.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
A Jewish shopkeeper gave her wine shop key to a gentile woman. Why is the wine permitted?
A. The gentile woman converted before entering
B. Transferring a key only grants responsibility for guarding, not authorization to enter
C. The wine was already sealed
D. A gentile woman cannot render wine forbidden
Correct: B — R' Yitzchak citing R' Elazar explains that handing over a key means the recipient guards the key — it does not authorize entry. Abaye supports this from a parallel mishna about an am ha'aretz.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the dispute about a courtyard divided by a low partition (meseifas), what is Rav's position?
A. Both purity and wine libation laws are lenient with a partition
B. An am ha'aretz neighbor makes pure items impure, but a gentile neighbor does NOT render wine as yayin nesekh
C. A gentile neighbor renders wine forbidden, but an am ha'aretz does not affect purity
D. Both purity and wine libation laws are strict with a low partition
Correct: B — Rav holds that purity laws (tahara) are MORE stringent than wine libation laws. So an am ha'aretz behind a low partition renders items impure, but a gentile behind the same partition does not render wine as yayin nesekh.
