Avodah Zarah 7 — Overview

כל המשנה — ידו על התחתונה
CORE THEMES This daf weaves together halakhic methodology — when an unattributed Mishna settles a dispute, whether the Mishna is sequential across tractates, and rules for consulting multiple sages — with practical law on business with gentiles before their festivals and the repeated rejection of Naḥum the Mede's lenient rulings.

The Dyer Who Deviated: Who Keeps the Wool?

R' Meir
The dyer who deviated is like a robber — he acquires the changed item and pays the owner the original value of the wool.
R' Yehuda
The dyer does not acquire the wool. The owner pays whichever is less: the dyer's expenses or the value of the enhancement.
Rav Yosef
Rav Huna's ruling that the halakha follows R' Yehuda was unnecessary — an unattributed Mishna in Bava Metzia already establishes this!
Rav Huna
It was necessary — the Mishna may not be sequential across different tractates, so the unattributed Mishna might have come first.
KEY PRINCIPLE כל המשנה — ידו על התחתונה — Whoever deviates from the agreed terms is at a disadvantage (Bava Metzia 76a).

Is the Mishna Sequential Across Tractates?

A dispute appears in one tractate and an unattributed Mishna (stam) on the same topic appears in another
↓
Does the stam Mishna appear after the dispute? If so, halakha follows the stam.
↓
Rav Huna
Across different tractates, we cannot assume the Mishna is sequential — explicit ruling needed
Rav Yosef
All of Seder Nezikin (Bava Kamma, Bava Metzia, Bava Batra) is one tractate — order is sequential
ALTERNATE ANSWER Rav Yosef also suggests: the stam Mishna in BM 76a is taught amid other decided halakhot, making it clearly authoritative regardless of order.

Rules for Asking a Sage a Halakhic Question

🔎 You ask a Sage and he rules the item is impure or forbidden
↓
🚫 You may NOT ask a different Sage hoping for a lenient ruling
↓
👥 If two Sages are sitting together and disagree — follow the one greater in wisdom and number
↓
⚖️ If they are equal: for a Torah law — follow the stringent opinion (R' Yehoshua ben Korḥa)
↓
📋 For a rabbinic law — follow the lenient opinion (R' Yehoshua ben Korḥa)
HALAKHA Rabba bar bar Ḥana says R' Yoḥanan says: The halakha is in accordance with R' Yehoshua ben Korḥa — both on Shabbat speech and on consulting sages.

Business Before Gentile Festivals

R' Yishmael

  • Prohibited 3 days before festivals
  • Prohibited 3 days after festivals
  • For Christians (Sunday festival) — effectively always prohibited (Shmuel)

The Rabbis

  • Prohibited before their festivals
  • Permitted after their festivals
  • Multiple explanations of how they differ from the first tanna
4 DIFFERENCES FROM FIRST TANNA The Gemara offers four explanations: (1) Whether the festival day counts in the three; (2) Whether post-facto profit is permitted; (3) Whether Shmuel's Diaspora leniency applies; (4) Whether Naḥum the Mede's one-day rule applies.
