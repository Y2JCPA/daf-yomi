Rabba's Location Distinction: Cities vs. Villages

City Statues

  • Dispute between R' Meir and Rabbis applies here
  • Likely made for ornamental purposes
  • Rabbis: permitted unless symbols present
  • R' Meir: still forbidden

Village Statues

  • All agree: forbidden
  • Certainly made for idol worship
  • No ornamental purpose in villages
  • No dispute between Tannaim
Key Point The Gemara corrects an initial version of Rabba's statement — village statues cannot plausibly be ornamental, so the dispute must apply to cities.

Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel: ANY Object in Hand

Rabbis (Mishna)
Only a staff, bird, or orb make a statue forbidden — these are specific symbols of dominion.
R' Shimon b. Gamliel
Even any item whatsoever in a statue's hand renders it forbidden — even a stone or twig!
Rav Ashi
What if the idol holds excrement? Does it mean the world is inferior like excrement to the idol?
Rav Ashi
Or perhaps it means the idol itself is inferior like excrement to the world?
Gemara
Teiku — The question remains unresolved.
Teiku Rav Ashi's dilemma about excrement highlights the ambiguity: does any object in an idol's hand always glorify the idol, or can it degrade it?

Idols That Broke by Themselves

R' Yochanan
Forbidden. The owner never explicitly revoked its status as an idol — it is still worshipped.
Reish Lakish
Permitted. The owner presumably said: 'If it can't save itself, how can it save me?' — status is revoked.
R' Yochanan
Proof from Dagon: it broke before the Ark, yet the Philistines continued to honor the threshold!
Reish Lakish
That proves my point! They abandoned Dagon and worshipped the threshold instead — the spirit moved to the threshold.
R' Yochanan
The Mishna says fragments of statues are permitted — implying fragments of idols are forbidden!
Reish Lakish
No — the implication is that whole statues are forbidden, per R' Meir. It says nothing about idol fragments.
The Dagon Argument I Samuel 5:4-5 — Dagon fell and broke before the Ark. R' Yochanan sees continued worship; Reish Lakish sees transferred worship to the threshold.
