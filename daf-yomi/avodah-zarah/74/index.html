Mishna: Certain items are forbidden and render any mixture forbidden in any amount
↓
Gemara asks: What criterion unifies this list?
↓

↓

All wine in cistern forbidden

What Criterion Unifies This List?

Gemara
If the criterion is davar she-b'minyan (counted/significant items), the Mishna should also list significant cuts of neveilah!
Gemara
If the criterion is issur hana'ah (forbidden from benefit), it should also list ḥametz on Pesach!
R' Ḥiyya bar Abba
The tanna has two criteria: an item must be both counted AND forbidden from benefit.
Gemara
Then list perekh nuts and Badan pomegranates of orlah — they meet both criteria!
R' Ḥiyya bar Abba
Those are already taught in Tractate Orlah 3:7 — no need to repeat them here.
DUAL TEST An item must satisfy both conditions — (1) individually significant and (2) forbidden from benefit — to forbid any mixture it enters.

Libation Wine Falls into a Cistern

🍷 Yayin nesekh falls into a wine cistern
↓
Is any amount of the cistern's wine now forbidden?
↓
Tanna Kamma
Yes — all wine in the cistern is forbidden from benefit
Rabban Shimon b. Gamliel
Sell all the wine to a gentile; only deduct the value of the forbidden wine
PRACTICAL IMPACT RSBG's leniency saves enormous financial loss — one drop of libation wine could otherwise destroy an entire cistern's worth of wine.

When Does RSBG's Leniency Apply? — Rav Naḥman's Ruling

Actual Libation Wine (Yayin Nesekh)

  • Wine mixed with wine → Entire mixture FORBIDDEN
  • Barrel mixed with barrels → May sell per RSBG (deducting value)

Stam Yeinam (Gentile Wine, Uncertain Status)

  • Even wine mixed with wine → May sell per RSBG
  • More lenient because prohibition is only rabbinic suspicion
  • Rav, Shmuel, R' Yoḥanan, R' Ḥanina all agree RSBG applies to wine-with-wine
RAV NAḤMAN The practical distinction: definite libation wine mixed directly is treated more strictly than wine of uncertain idolatrous use.

Purifying a Stone Winepress Lined with Pitch

🍇 Gentile lines stone winepress with pitch (no grape-treading)
↓
🧹 Owner cleanses (mehadḥo) the winepress — sufficient for stone
↓
🪵 If wood: R' Yehuda HaNasi says cleanse; Rabbis say peel off all pitch
↓
🏺 If earthenware: even peeling the pitch is insufficient — vessel is forbidden
↓
⚠️ Rava: If gentile also trod grapes in it, cleansing alone is NOT enough
PRINCIPLE Material matters: stone absorbs least, wood absorbs more, and earthenware absorbs permanently. Grape-treading adds a layer of absorption beyond pitch.
