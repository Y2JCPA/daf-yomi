Avodah Zarah 74 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Ḥiyya bar Abba, what TWO criteria must an item meet to forbid any mixture it enters?
A. Counted (significant) AND forbidden from benefit
B. Counted AND forbidden by Torah law
C. Forbidden from benefit AND forbidden to eat
D. Significant AND belonging to idolatry
Correct: A — R' Ḥiyya bar Abba explains the tanna uses a dual test: the item must be both a 'davar she-b'minyan' (individually significant/counted) AND an item from which deriving benefit is prohibited (issur hana'ah).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why doesn't the Mishna list perekh nuts and Badan pomegranates of orlah, which meet both criteria?
A. They are only rabbinically forbidden
B. They are already listed in Tractate Orlah 3:7
C. They can be nullified in a majority
D. They are not considered individually significant
Correct: B — The Gemara answers that these cases are already taught in Tractate Orlah (3:7), so there is no need to repeat them in our Mishna.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman's practical ruling, what happens when actual yayin nesekh (libation wine) becomes mixed with permitted wine?
A. It may be sold to a gentile per RSBG
B. The entire mixture is forbidden
C. It is permitted if the majority is kosher wine
D. It must be poured out but the vessels are permitted
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman rules that for actual yayin nesekh, when wine mixes directly with wine the entire mixture is forbidden. Only when barrels are intermingled (not the wine itself) does RSBG's leniency to sell apply.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Rav ultimately require peeling rather than mere cleansing for the winepress he inspected?
A. It was made of earthenware
B. The gentile had trodden grapes in it
C. He discovered a crack underneath filled with absorbed wine
D. R' Ḥiyya instructed him to be strict
Correct: C — While the surface was smooth and appeared suitable for cleansing, Rav discovered a hidden crack beneath his feet that was full of wine. This absorbed wine could not be removed by cleansing alone, requiring peeling instead.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does R' Yehuda HaNasi concede that earthenware jugs belonging to gentiles are forbidden even though he permits cleansing for ladles and funnels?
A. Earthenware absorbs more deeply than other materials
B. Jugs store wine for extended periods, while ladles and funnels hold wine only temporarily
C. Jugs are larger and hold more wine
D. The Torah explicitly forbids earthenware jugs
Correct: B — The Gemara explains the distinction: jugs contain wine for storage purposes (extended contact), while ladles and funnels hold wine only temporarily during use. Storage allows deeper absorption that cleansing cannot address.
