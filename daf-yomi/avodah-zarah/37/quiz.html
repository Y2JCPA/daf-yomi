5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why was R' Yehuda Nesia concerned about also permitting gentile bread?
A. He would be called the head of a 'permissive court'
B. Gentile bread was explicitly prohibited by the Torah
C. R' Simlai would object publicly
D. It would require a full Sanhedrin vote
Correct: A — R' Yehuda Nesia worried that permitting too many things would earn his court the label of a 'permissive court,' as happened to Yosei ben Yo'ezer who was called 'Yosef the Permissive.'
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to R' Yehuda Nesia's court, why is the conditional get valid even though the husband died before the 12 months?
A. Death automatically finalizes any pending divorce
B. The date on the document proves when it takes effect — retroactively
C. The wife's acceptance overrides the condition
D. A beit din can nullify conditions after death
Correct: B — The court followed R' Yosei's principle that the date written on a document proves when it takes effect, meaning the divorce is considered retroactively initiated from the date of issuance.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the fundamental dispute between Rav and Shmuel regarding Temple slaughterhouse liquids?
A. Whether the liquids are considered blood or water
B. Whether liquid impurity is entirely rabbinic (Rav) or the impurity itself is Torah law (Shmuel)
C. Whether Temple liquids require libation
D. Whether the slaughterhouse is considered inside or outside the Temple courtyard
Correct: B — Rav holds that all liquid impurity is rabbinic and was never decreed for Temple liquids. Shmuel holds the impurity of liquids themselves is Torah law, but their ability to transmit impurity to other items is rabbinic and was not applied to Temple liquids.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does the Gemara explain that Yosei ben Yo'ezer's testimony about corpse-touching was actually lenient?
A. He ruled that priests may touch corpses
B. He removed the rabbinic stringency on secondary contact without concurrent touching
C. He ruled corpse impurity lasts only one day
D. He permitted Kohanim to enter cemeteries
Correct: B — The Sages had decreed 7-day impurity even for one who touches a corpse-toucher without concurrent contact. Yosei ben Yo'ezer restored the Torah law that without concurrent contact, the impurity is only until evening.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Abaye, what is the difference between saying a get takes effect 'when the sun emerges' versus 'on the condition that the sun emerges'?
A. The first is valid; the second is not
B. The first takes effect at sunrise; the second takes effect retroactively from the time of giving
C. Both mean the same thing but use different formulations
D. The first requires witnesses; the second does not
Correct: B — Abaye distinguishes: 'when' sets a future time for the get to take effect, while 'on the condition that' makes it retroactive per Rav Huna's principle. If the husband dies before sunrise, only the conditional formulation produces a valid get.
