Avodah Zarah 22 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What makes a Jewish-gentile Shabbat labor partnership permitted?
A. The gentile voluntarily chooses to work on Shabbat
B. They stipulated the arrangement from the outset of the partnership
C. The Jew does not directly benefit from Shabbat labor
D. A rabbi supervises the profit distribution
Correct: B — The baraita teaches that if they stipulated from the beginning that the gentile takes Shabbat profits and the Jew takes weekday profits, it is permitted. Without prior stipulation, retroactive calculation is prohibited because it implies the gentile worked on the Jew's behalf on Shabbat.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does R' Shimon ben Elazar distinguish between renting a field to a gentile versus a Samaritan?
A. Samaritans are not considered trustworthy witnesses
B. A Samaritan claims greater learning and refuses to comply with restrictions
C. Gentiles are more careful about agricultural laws
D. Samaritans are obligated in fewer mitzvot than gentiles
Correct: B — A gentile will comply when told not to work on certain days, but a Samaritan (Cuti) refuses, claiming 'I am more learned than you and know it is permitted.' This creates both a lifnei iver problem and a marit ayin issue since the field bears the Jewish owner's name.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If gentiles are suspected of bestiality, why may one purchase animals from them for korbanot?
A. The Temple priest inspects the animal for signs of abuse
B. Gentiles protect their own animals because bestiality damages them
C. Only certain species of animals are at risk
D. The animal is purified through the offering process
Correct: B — Rav Sheila bar Avina in the name of Rav teaches that gentiles protect their own animals because bestiality causes barrenness in females and weakens male animals. Self-interest serves as a deterrent when it's their own property.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why is a Jew's animal more at risk with a gentile shepherd than another gentile's animal?
A. Jewish animals are physically weaker
B. Gentile shepherds resent working for Jews
C. Gentiles fear detection by fellow gentiles but not by Jews
D. Jewish law imposes stricter purity standards
Correct: C — Rabba explains with the adage: 'A sinner knows his fellow sinner' — gentiles are aware of each other's behavior and thus deter one another. But they don't fear Jews discovering their actions, so a Jewish-owned animal under gentile care lacks this deterrent.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Yochanan, what ended the 'moral contamination' (zuhama) for the Jewish people?
A. The Exodus from Egypt
B. Circumcision (brit milah)
C. Standing at Mount Sinai and receiving the Torah
D. The construction of the Mishkan (Tabernacle)
Correct: C — R' Yochanan teaches that the snake's contamination of Eve was transmitted to all humanity, but for those who stood at Sinai, this contamination ended (paska zuhamatam). This theological principle underlies the different behavioral presumptions applied to Jews versus gentiles.
