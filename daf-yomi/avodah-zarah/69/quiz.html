QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the final halakhic ratio for nullifying a mouse that fell into vinegar or beer?
A. 50 times its volume
B. 60 times its volume
C. 101 times its volume
D. It cannot be nullified
Correct: B — Despite various proposals (50×, 60×, 101×), the Gemara concludes that both vinegar and beer require 60× the mouse's volume — the standard ratio for all Torah prohibitions (bitul b'shishim).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the Mishna, when is wine permitted if a gentile is transporting it with a Jew?
A. Only if the Jew watches continuously
B. If the wine is under presumption of being supervised
C. Only if the barrels are sealed with clay
D. Only during daytime transport
Correct: B — The Mishna states that if the wine is 'under the presumption of being supervised' (be-ḥezkat ha-mishtamer), it is permitted — even without constant direct observation.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the key difference between the Rabbis and Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel regarding wine tampering?
A. Whether gentile wine is ever permitted
B. The time measure: boring a hole vs. removing the entire stopper
C. Whether sealed barrels are permitted
D. Whether the wine requires 60× nullification
Correct: B — The Rabbis measure time to bore a hole and seal with plaster; R. Shimon b. Gamliel requires the longer time to remove the whole stopper, fashion a new one, and let it dry — because a patched hole is detectable.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say we cannot leave sealed wine with gentiles nowadays, despite ruling like R. Shimon b. Gamliel?
A. Because gentiles are not trustworthy at all
B. Because of the bunghole — the gentile can widen it to drink
C. Because R. Eliezer's ruling was overturned
D. Because sealed barrels are no longer manufactured
Correct: B — Even though the halakha follows R. Shimon b. Gamliel (not concerned about boring holes) and R. Eliezer (seals aren't easily forged), the bunghole used for smelling wine can be widened slightly by a gentile to drink from it.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
If a Jew is eating with a gentile and says 'mix and drink,' what happens to wine on the side table?
A. It remains permitted since it's out of reach
B. It becomes forbidden because the invitation extends access
C. It must be nullified at 60×
D. Only sealed containers are affected
Correct: B — Normally wine on the side table (dulebaki) is permitted since the gentile wouldn't handle it. But saying 'mix and drink' gives the gentile permission to access all available wine, making even the side table wine forbidden.
