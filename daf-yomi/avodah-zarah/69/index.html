Mouse in Vinegar: What's the Ratio?

Rav Hillel
Rav Kahana deemed the vinegar forbidden when a mouse fell in — it must enhance flavor.
Rav Ashi
No proof! That mouse was dismembered — the concern was consuming a piece of it, not the taste.
Ravina
Nullify at 101× volume — it shouldn't be stricter than teruma!
Rav Taḥlifa bar Giza
Perhaps the mouse is like spice of teruma — its flavor is too strong to nullify at 101×!
Rav Aḥai
Vinegar is sharp enough to nullify the mouse at only 50×.
Rav Shmuel b. Rav Ika
Beer needs the full 60× to nullify the mouse.
HALAKHA Both vinegar and beer require 60× the mouse's volume for nullification — and this is the rule for all Torah prohibitions.

Rabbis vs. Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel: Tampering Standard

Rabbis (Tanna Kamma)

  • Time to bore a hole (sheyishtom)
  • Seal it with lime plaster
  • Wait for plaster to dry
  • Shorter time threshold
  • Concerned even about subtle tampering

Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel

  • Time to remove the entire stopper
  • Fashion a new stopper
  • Wait for new stopper to dry
  • Longer time threshold
  • Sealing of a hole is noticeable from above and below
RAVA'S RULING Halakha follows R. Shimon ben Gamliel — supported by the unattributed mishna about sealed barrels at the table.

Why the Mishna Lists Three Scenarios

📦 Transporting barrels — Gentile fears owner might come and see him
↓
🚢 Wagon or ship — Maybe gentile can sail far off? No — owner might take a different road or stand on the riverbank
↓
🏪 Left in shop — Maybe gentile can lock the door? No — same fear of being caught applies
↓
⚖️ Conclusion: All three cases teach the same rule — forbidden only if the Jew explicitly said he's going far
PRINCIPLE Each case might have led to a kal va-ḥomer (a fortiori) — the Mishna teaches all three to show the rule is uniform.
