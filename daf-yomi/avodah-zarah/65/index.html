Key principle: Noten ta'am lifgam — if the forbidden item's flavor harms the mixture, it is permitted
Core Theme This daf examines when benefit from yayin nesekh is permitted — through wages, rental fees, and food contamination — culminating in the principle that detrimental flavor does not render food forbidden.

Giving a Gift to Avidarna on His Festival

Rav Yehuda
I sent a gift to Avidarna on his festival day because I know he does not worship idols.
Rav Yosef
But a ger toshav must accept before three ḥaverim not to worship idols! Avidarna never did this.
Rav Yehuda
That baraita about three ḥaverim applies to the mitzvah to sustain him, not to giving gifts.
Rav Yosef
R' Yoḥanan says a ger toshav who doesn't circumcise within 12 months is considered a heretic!
Rav Yehuda
That refers only to one who accepted circumcision upon himself and then reneged. Avidarna never committed to circumcision.
Key Point Rav Yehuda distinguishes between a formal ger toshav and a gentile personally known not to worship idols — the latter may receive a gift on his festival.

Rava & Bar Sheshakh: The World-to-Come

עינא דבעי למחזי בישותא — לפקע
Narrative Rava visited bar Sheshakh on his festival and found him bathing in rose water with attendants before him. Bar Sheshakh asked: "Do you have anything this fine in the World-to-Come?"

Rava replied: "Ours is better — we will have no fear of the government."

Bar Sheshakh scoffed — "What fear do I have?" — and at that moment a royal officer arrived summoning him before the king. As he departed he said: "May any eye that wishes evil upon you burst!" Rava answered Amen — and bar Sheshakh's own eye burst.

Wages for Transporting Yayin Nesekh

A Jew is hired by a gentile for general labor
↓
Employer says: "Transport this barrel of yayin nesekh"
↓
Per-barrel pay
Each barrel = one peruta
➜ Wage is PERMITTED
(yayin nesekh barrel wage is excluded)
Lump-sum pay
100 barrels = 100 perutot
➜ Entire wage is FORBIDDEN
(single task includes yayin nesekh)
Rava's Distinction Per-barrel payment makes each barrel a separate task — only the forbidden barrel's peruta is lost. A lump sum makes all barrels one task, and any yayin nesekh contaminates the entire wage.

Renting a Donkey: Permitted vs. Forbidden Fees

Rental FORBIDDEN

  • Donkey rented specifically to carry yayin nesekh
  • The rental was designated for transporting the forbidden wine
  • Entire rental fee is forbidden

Rental PERMITTED

  • Donkey rented for the gentile to ride
  • Gentile incidentally places his wine jug on it
  • Placing a jug is a rider's right — but rental wasn't for the wine
  • Fee remains permitted
Principle The rental fee follows the designated purpose of the rental. If the donkey was rented for riding, incidental placement of yayin nesekh does not render the fee forbidden.

Yayin Nesekh Falls on Produce: What's the Din?

Produce TypeConditionHalakha
Whole grapesWine splashed on intact grapesRinse them — PERMITTED
Cracked grapesWine absorbed into broken skinFORBIDDEN (wine absorbed)
Figs / DatesWine imparts positive flavorFORBIDDEN
Dried figs (Boethus)Wine flavor is detrimentalPERMITTED
WheatWine in slits (like cracked grapes)FORBIDDEN to eat — sell as bread to gentiles
Boethus ben Zunen A barrel of yayin nesekh broke onto his dried figs on a ship. The Sages permitted the figs because wine flavor on dried figs is noten ta'am lifgam — it worsens the taste.
