Nullification of Forbidden Wine: Core Framework

Libation wine falls into a mixture
↓
What type of substance did it mix with?
Same type (wine into wine)
Min b'mino — Forbidden with any amount
Different type (wine into water)
Min b'she'eino mino — Forbidden only if it imparts flavor (noten ta'am)
MISHNA PRINCIPLE Same-type substances: forbidden with any amount. Different-type substances: forbidden only when the forbidden item imparts detectable flavor.

KEY DISTINCTION The Gemara will push back: does the Mishna refer to forbidden falling into permitted, or permitted falling into forbidden? This question drives the entire sugya.

Bitul B'Bitul: Gradual Nullification

Rav Dimi (reporting R' Yoḥanan)
One who pours libation wine from a barrel into a wine cistern — even all day long — the forbidden wine is nullified little by little (בטל בבטל) and the wine is permitted.
Gemara (Objection)
But the Mishna says libation wine renders other wine forbidden with any amount! This contradicts you!
Rav Dimi
The Mishna refers to permitted falling into forbidden — the majority is forbidden. My case is the reverse: forbidden falling into a large permitted quantity.
Rav Yitzḥak bar Yosef (refining R' Yoḥanan)
This applies only to pouring from a small canteen (whose stream is insignificant). From a barrel, the stream is significant and it does not apply.
PRACTICAL DIFFERENCE The size of the vessel matters: a small canteen's trickle is nullified drop by drop, but a barrel's heavy flow enters as a significant quantity that cannot be nullified gradually.

Ravin's Case: Wine Cistern + Pitcher of Water

A wine cistern contains permitted wine
↓
Libation wine and a pitcher of water both fall in
↓
Did the water fall in first?
Yes — water fell first
Treat permitted wine as nonexistent (רואין). Water nullifies the libation wine → PERMITTED
No — libation wine fell first
Libation wine found its own type (the permitted wine) and was 'awakened' — all the wine is now forbidden, water cannot nullify → FORBIDDEN
RAV SHMUEL BAR YEHUDA The order matters: if water enters first, it creates a buffer. If libation wine contacts permitted wine first, min b'mino takes effect before the water can help.

Two Versions of the Water-Nullification Rule

Taught on Ravin's Statement

  • Adds a qualification to Ravin's ruling
  • Water must fall in FIRST
  • If water did not fall first → forbidden (min b'mino activated)
  • Order of ingredients is critical

Taught on the Mishna

  • Qualifies the Mishna's rule directly
  • Water does NOT need to fall in first
  • In any event, water nullifies the libation wine
  • Ro'in (treating same-type as nonexistent) applies regardless of order
NAFKA MINA The practical difference: must the water fall in before the libation wine, or does the water nullify the forbidden wine regardless of sequence?

Ḥizkiyya vs. R' Yoḥanan: The Core Dispute

Ḥizkiyya
If the forbidden wine fell in last — the mixture is forbidden. The libation wine finds its own type (permitted wine) and renders it all forbidden; water cannot then nullify it.
Ḥizkiyya
But if the permitted wine fell in last (after water already nullified the libation wine) — it is permitted, since the libation wine was already nullified.
R' Yoḥanan
Even if the forbidden wine fell in last, the mixture is permitted. We apply ro'in: treat the permitted wine as nonexistent, and the water nullifies the libation wine.
R' Yirmeya to R' Zeira
Does this parallel the dispute between R' Eliezer (follow the last ingredient) and the Rabbis (look at the forbidden substance alone)?
Gemara
No — per Abaye, R' Eliezer's rule doesn't map cleanly. The real dispute is whether we apply ro'in (treating same-type as nonexistent) or not.
CORE DISPUTE Ḥizkiyya does NOT hold of ro'in — he considers all the wine together. R' Yoḥanan DOES hold of ro'in — the permitted same-type substance is disregarded so the different-type substance (water) can nullify the forbidden item.
