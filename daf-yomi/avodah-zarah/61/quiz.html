Avodah Zarah 61 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, why is wine permitted in a city with both Jews and gentiles?
A. Because Jews have inspected the wine
B. Because the gentile fears being seen by Jews
C. Because the wine is sealed with a Jewish seal
D. Because the gentile signed a receipt
Correct: B — The Mishna explains that in a mixed city, the gentile refrains from touching the wine lest Jews see him doing so — the fear of observation is the key deterrent.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say a watchman who comes at SET times is actually detrimental?
A. Because he might forget to come
B. Because the gentile knows when to expect him and acts freely otherwise
C. Because it creates a false sense of security for the Jew
D. Because it violates Shabbat if the schedule falls on Shabbat
Correct: B — The Gemara corrects the Baraita: if the watchman comes at predictable times, the gentile simply waits until after the visit to tamper with the wine. Unpredictable visits are required.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the difference between a gentile who writes a receipt and one who holds a lien on the wine?
A. The receipt makes the wine more expensive; the lien makes it cheaper
B. The receipt transfers ownership psychologically; the lien makes the gentile feel entitled to touch it
C. The receipt requires witnesses; the lien does not
D. There is no practical difference — both result in permitted wine
Correct: B — A receipt signals that the wine belongs to the Jew, deterring the gentile from touching it. A lien gives the gentile a sense of entitlement over the wine, removing his compunctions about touching it.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the confirmed reading of R' Shimon ben Elazar's statement, what does 'the domain of gentiles is all one' mean?
A. All gentile homes are legally equivalent to public property
B. Wine in any gentile's domain is prohibited because gentiles may be in collusion
C. Wine in another gentile's domain is safer than the owner's domain
D. Gentiles share a single domain for purposes of eruv
Correct: B — The Baraita confirms Rav Naḥman's reading: R' Shimon ben Elazar is stringent — he holds that even placing wine in a different gentile's domain doesn't help, because the bailee may collude with the owner ('due to the swindlers').
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Yoḥanan, when a Jew who rendered wine permitted has another Jew living in the SAME courtyard, what is required?
A. A key and a seal
B. A constant watchman
C. Nothing — the wine is permitted even without a key or seal
D. A written receipt from the gentile
Correct: C — R' Yoḥanan told the tanna to teach that when a Jew lives in the same courtyard, even without a key or seal the wine is permitted — the Jewish neighbor's proximity is sufficient deterrence.
