What Counts as a 'Public Thoroughfare'?

Rav Yosef's Equivalents

  • 🪟 A Jew's window open to that area
  • 🗑️ A trash heap — many people frequent it
  • 🌴 A palm tree — someone might climb it and see

Dispute: Cut Palm Tree

  • ❌ One says prohibited — no fruit, no reason to climb
  • ✅ One says permitted — someone may climb to search for a lost animal
  • Disputants: Rav Aḥa and Ravina
Principle Any vantage point from which a Jew might observe the gentile functions as a deterrent — even a palm tree.

Receipt vs. Lien: Financial Arrangements

Mishna
If the gentile writes a receipt — 'I received money from you' — even before actual payment, the wine is permitted.
Reasoning
The wine is considered the Jew's property. The gentile doesn't dare touch it.
Mishna
If the Jew wants to remove the wine but the gentile won't allow it until paid — the wine is prohibited.
Incident
This occurred in Beit She'an — the Sages deemed the wine prohibited.
Reasoning
The gentile believes he has a lien on the wine, so he has no compunction about touching it.
Distinction A written receipt transfers psychological ownership to the Jew. A lien keeps the gentile feeling entitled to the wine — and therefore willing to touch it.

R' Shimon ben Elazar: Lenient or Stringent?

R' Shimon b. Elazar
The domain of gentiles is all one (reshut ha-nokhrim aḥat hi).
Rav Yehuda / Ze'eiri
Lenient: Wine in another gentile's domain is permitted — gentiles are not in collusion. Only the owner's domain is problematic.
Rav Naḥman / Ze'eiri
Stringent: Wine in any gentile's domain is prohibited — the bailee may be in collusion with the owner.
Baraita
Supports Rav Naḥman: R' Shimon b. Elazar said 'all one' due to the swindlers — confirming the stringent reading.
Resolution The Baraita confirms Rav Naḥman's reading: R' Shimon ben Elazar is stringent — all gentile domains are suspect because of potential collusion between 'swindlers.'
