Avodah Zarah 45 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, why are mountains worshipped as idols still permitted for use?
A. Because they were created by God, not by humans
B. Because they are too large to destroy
C. Because the Torah only prohibits small idols
D. Because mountains cannot be moved
Correct: A — The fundamental principle is that natural objects not made by human hands cannot become forbidden through idol worship. The Torah says 'their gods upon the mountains' — not the mountains themselves.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the practical difference between the First Tanna and R' Yosei HaGelili according to Rav Sheshet?
A. Whether mountain coatings are forbidden
B. Whether a tree planted and subsequently worshipped is forbidden
C. Whether hills have the same status as mountains
D. Whether R' Akiva's interpretation is authoritative
Correct: B — Rav Sheshet rejects Reish Lakish's explanation about coatings and says the real dispute is about trees planted before they were worshipped — the First Tanna permits them, R' Yosei HaGelili forbids them.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does R' Yosei son of R' Yehuda derive that a tree planted and later worshipped is forbidden?
A. From the phrase 'under every leafy tree' (Deut. 12:2)
B. From an oral tradition at Sinai
C. From 'hew down their asherim' (Deut. 7:5), using 'if it were not stated' logic
D. From the general prohibition against idol worship
Correct: C — Using the hermeneutical method of 'if it were not stated,' he shows that since Deut. 12:3 already covers trees planted for worship ('burn their asherim'), Deut. 7:5's 'hew down their asherim' must refer to trees planted then worshipped.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to R' Akiva, what does 'upon the high mountains, upon the hills, and under every leafy tree' teach?
A. That only worship in these locations is forbidden
B. That mountains, hills, and trees are all forbidden as idols
C. These are indicators of where idol worship is commonly found
D. That idol worship outdoors is worse than indoors
Correct: C — R' Akiva interprets the verse not as defining legal categories of idols, but as practical guidance: wherever you find these landscape features, expect to find idolatrous shrines nearby.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Yehoshua ben Levi, what is the correct order of priorities upon entering Eretz Yisrael?
A. Conquer first, then destroy all idols, then fell trees
B. Fell idolatrous trees first, then conquer, then eradicate remaining idols
C. Destroy altars first, then conquer, then fell trees
D. Conquer first, then fell trees, then destroy altars
Correct: B — R' Yehoshua ben Levi derives from the order of Deut. 7:5 that felling asherim precedes conquest of the land, and conquest precedes the full eradication of all idolatrous objects.
