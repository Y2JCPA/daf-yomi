CombinationStatus

Fasting after small fish⚠️ Dangerous
Cress then bloodletting⚠️ Dangerous
Fever + bloodletting☠️ Life-threatening

⚕️ Six Healing Foods vs. Ten Harmful Foods

6 That Heal Permanently

  • 🥬 Cabbage
  • 🟣 Beets
  • 🌼 Dried chamomile water
  • 🐄 Stomach contents of animal
  • 🐄 Womb of animal
  • 🫀 Lobe of the liver
  • (Some say: small fish)

10 That Worsen Illness

  • 🥩 Ox meat, fat, roasted meat
  • 🐔 Bird meat, roasted egg
  • 🌿 Cress
  • 💈 Shaving & bathing
  • 🧀 Cheese & liver
  • (Some say: nuts, cucumbers)
  • קִישׁוּאִין — harsh as swords!
SCHOOL OF R' YISHMAEL Why are cucumbers called קִישׁוּאִין? Because they are as קָשִׁין (harsh) to the body as swords.

💈 Haircuts by Gentiles: The Mirror Rule

Baraita
A Jew having his hair cut by a gentile should observe the barber's actions in a mirror.
Gemara
In public — why need a mirror? The gentile won't harm him publicly. In private — what good is watching?
Gemara (answer)
It's in private, but having a mirror makes the Jew appear important — the gentile will hesitate to attack.
Rav Ḥana bar Bizna
Was getting a haircut in a side street of Neharde'a. The barber said: "Ḥana, Ḥana — your throat appeals to the razor!"
Rav Ḥana bar Bizna
"I deserve this — I violated R' Meir's ruling!"
Gemara
He also violated the Rabbis' ruling — even they only permitted haircuts in a public domain, and a side street is not public!
R' MEIR vs. RABBIS R' Meir: Forbidden anywhere. Rabbis: Forbidden only in private; permitted in public (with a mirror for extra safety).

🚫 Mishna: Prohibited Gentile Items (Issur Hana'ah)

🍷 Wine of gentiles — and vinegar that was originally wine
↓
🏺 Hadrianic earthenware — absorbs wine
↓
🐄 Hides with a tear opposite the heart (Rabban Shimon b. Gamliel: only if circular, not elongated)
↓
🥩 Meat exiting a house of idol worship is prohibited; meat entering is permitted (R' Akiva)
↓
🍶 Wineskins & jugs — R' Meir: benefit prohibited; Rabbis: benefit permitted
↓
🍇 Grape residues — R' Meir: always prohibited; Rabbis: only moist residues prohibited
↓
🧀 Fish stew & cheese of Beit Unyaki — R' Meir: benefit prohibited; Rabbis: benefit permitted
PATTERN R' Meir consistently rules that benefit is prohibited (issur hana'ah). The Rabbis consistently limit the prohibition to consumption only.
