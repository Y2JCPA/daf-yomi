👑 Emperor sends second troop with orders: 'Don't speak to him!'

Burning at a King's Death: Idolatrous or Not?

R' Meir
Both deaths with and without public burning are festivals — gentiles worship idols on any royal death
Rabbis
Only a death with burning is important enough to be a festival with idol worship
Gemara
Does R' Meir think burning is not idolatrous? And do the Rabbis think it is?
Baraita
We burn items at kings' deaths — this is not the ways of the Amorites, it is a Jewish custom (Jeremiah 34:5)
Resolution
All agree burning itself is not idolatrous — it honors the king. They disagree on whether only important deaths trigger idol worship
HALACHA Burning possessions at a king's funeral is a legitimate Jewish practice of honor — not an imitation of idolatrous customs ('darkei ha-Emori').

Antoninus & Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi: The Two 'Proud Ones'

The Drasha

  • Genesis 25:23 — 'Two goyim are in your womb'
  • Rav Yehuda / Rav: Read as geyim — 'proud ones'
  • Refers to Antoninus and Rebbi, descendants of Esau and Jacob

Their Shared Wealth

  • Tables never lacked lettuce, cucumbers, or radish
  • Available in summer and rainy season alike
  • Radish breaks up food; lettuce stirs it; cucumbers expand intestines

Cucumbers: Helpful or Harmful?

  • School of R' Yishmael: Kishuin = kashim — harmful as swords!
  • Resolution: Large cucumbers are harmful
  • Small cucumbers are beneficial — these were on their tables
NOTE The close relationship between Antoninus (Roman emperor) and Rebbi (Nasi of the Sanhedrin) is a recurring Talmudic theme representing the ideal of respectful coexistence.

Rome's 70-Year Festival: Esau over Jacob

סך כירי דיעקב פלסתר
AGGADAH Once every 70 years, Romans staged a spectacle: a whole man rode a lame man (Esau over Jacob), dressed in Adam's garments, wearing the scalp of R' Yishmael, draped in gold. They announced: 'The calculation of Jacob is fraudulent — the brother of our master is a forger!'

Rav Ashi's observation: Their own words betrayed them — by saying 'the brother of our master, a forger,' the grammar implies our master himself is the forger. Woe to Esau when Jacob arises.
