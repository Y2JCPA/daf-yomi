Why Is God Jealous of Powerless Idols?

Agrippas the General
Your Torah calls God a jealous God regarding idols. But jealousy only makes sense between equals — a wise man of a wise man, a rich man of a rich man. Why would God be jealous of nothing?
Rabban Gamliel
Parable: A man takes a second wife. If the second wife is more distinguished, the first wife feels no jealousy. But if the second wife is less distinguished — then she is jealous!
Rabban Gamliel
The very worthlessness of the idol is what provokes God's anger — how could you abandon Me for that?
Insight God's jealousy is not about the idol's power — it is about the insult of the betrayal. The lesser the rival, the greater the offense.

The Idol That Brings Rain

Rava bar Rav Yitzḥak
There is an idol in our town. When rain is needed, the idol appears in a dream and says: "Slaughter a man for me and I will bring rain." They do so — and rain comes!
Rav Yehuda
Good thing you asked me while I'm alive! Rav teaches: "Which the Lord your God has allotted to all nations" (Deut. 4:19) — God allows the nations to be misled (sheheḥelikan) to expel them from the world.
Reish Lakish
"If it concerns the scornful, He scorns them" (Prov. 3:34) — one who comes to sin is given an opening. One who comes to be purified is given assistance.
Principle God preserves free will — even at the cost of allowing apparent "evidence" for idol worship. The rain is not from the idol but from Heaven's allowing the wicked to deepen their error.

Mishna: When Does Wine Become Yayin Nesekh?

Before Descent into Vat

  • Gentile treads grapes in winepress
  • He picks up grapes and places them on the pile
  • Juice has not yet entered the collection vat
  • ✅ Permitted — not yet considered "wine"

After Descent into Vat

  • Wine flows down into the bor (collection vat)
  • Now has the halakhic status of wine
  • Gentile's touch renders it yayin nesekh
  • ❌ Prohibited — what is in the vat is forbidden
Halakha The critical transition point: grape juice becomes "wine" susceptible to libation prohibition only upon descending into the collection vat.

Rav Huna's Ruling vs. The Mishna

PositionWhen Wine Becomes SusceptibleBasis
Mishna (initial version)When it descends into the collection vatOne may tread grapes with a gentile in the press
Rav HunaOnce it begins to flow toward the exitStricter — even flowing juice in the press is susceptible
Mishna (revised version)Agrees with Rav Huna — once it flowsOne may not tread grapes with a gentile in the press
Resolution Rav Huna reconciles by distinguishing between the initial version (lenient, before flow) and the ultimate revised version (strict, from flow). The later ruling follows Rav Huna.
