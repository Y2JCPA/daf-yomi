Avodah Zarah 54 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rava, when is one required to sacrifice one's life rather than worship an idol under duress?
A. Only when the idol is made of gold
B. When worshipping in public
C. When worshipping in private
D. Only during a time of religious persecution
Correct: B — Rava resolves the conflicting verses: 'He shall live by them' exempts duress in private, but 'You shall not profane My holy name' requires self-sacrifice in public, as public idol worship constitutes chilul Hashem.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the key distinction between pouring a libation ON the horns vs. BETWEEN the horns of another's animal?
A. On the horns requires intent; between does not
B. On the horns treats the animal as a platform (permitted); between the horns worships the animal itself (forbidden)
C. Both are forbidden but carry different penalties
D. Between the horns is permitted; on the horns is forbidden
Correct: B — Rav Adda bar Ahava explains that pouring ON the horns uses the animal as a mere altar/platform, while pouring BETWEEN the horns constitutes worshipping the animal itself — a sacrificial rite that renders it forbidden.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara prove from the vessels of Ahaz that a sacrificial rite can forbid another's property?
A. Ahaz destroyed the vessels, so they needed replacing
B. Ahaz's sacrificial rites on Temple vessels (not his property) required them to be interred and replaced
C. Ahaz purchased the vessels, making them his property
D. The vessels were only temporarily forbidden
Correct: B — The Temple vessels belonged to the Temple, not to Ahaz. Yet because he performed idolatrous sacrificial rites upon them, they had to be interred ('prepared') and new ones sanctified in their place — proving that a sacrificial rite forbids even another's property.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the chain of Sabbatical Year exchanges, what happens to the ORIGINAL produce when one purchases meat with it?
A. It loses its sanctity entirely
B. It transfers sanctity to the meat and becomes non-sacred
C. Both the produce and the meat retain Sabbatical Year sanctity
D. Only the meat has sanctity; the produce is consumed
Correct: C — Unlike consecrated property, Sabbatical Year produce is never desacralized — 'It shall be sacred for you' (Lev. 25:12). When one purchases meat with it, BOTH the original produce and the meat have kedushat shevi'it.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the opinion that forbids only the direct exchange (but not the exchange of the exchange) reject learning from idol worship and Sabbatical Year together?
A. Because those are different categories of prohibition entirely
B. Because two verses that come as one (shnei ketuvim ha-ba'im k'echad) do not teach other cases
C. Because Sabbatical Year is only rabbinic
D. Because idol worship only applies to gentiles
Correct: B — This Tanna holds that idol worship and Sabbatical Year are 'two verses that come as one' — both independently teach that an exchange is forbidden. Since they both teach the same principle, the rule cannot be extended to other cases like orlah or kilei hakerem.
