5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does R' Elazar permit selling an animal to a gentile even where seclusion is prohibited?
A. Because the sale is only temporary
B. Because a gentile spares his own animal from sterility
C. Because the Torah only prohibits seclusion, not selling
D. Because the animal will be slaughtered immediately
Correct: B — R' Elazar reasons that once the animal belongs to the gentile, he will protect it from bestiality because he doesn't want it to become sterile. This concern only applies to others' animals, not his own.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the 'testing' concern raised by Rami son of Rav Yeiva?
A. The gentile tests the animal's fitness by working it on Shabbat
B. The Jewish seller commands the animal near sunset on Erev Shabbat to demonstrate it
C. The buyer tests the animal for hidden blemishes during the week
D. A rabbi must test the animal before approving the sale
Correct: B — The concern is that the seller, wanting to demonstrate the animal's fitness, tells the buyer to 'test it' close to Shabbat. The animal walks at the Jewish owner's voice, making him liable for driving his laden animal on Shabbat.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara prove that leasing does NOT confer ownership?
A. From a verse in Deuteronomy about property rights
B. From a logical argument by Rav Huna about temporary possession
C. From a Mishna showing a priest who leases a cow cannot feed it teruma
D. From the principle that money alone does not create acquisition
Correct: C — If leasing conferred ownership, a priest who leased an Israelite's cow could feed it teruma (priestly food) since it would be 'his' cow. The Mishna in Terumot explicitly prohibits this — proving the lessee does not acquire the item.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, what determines whether a sale is permitted when the item could be used for a forbidden purpose?
A. Whether the seller is commanded to let the item rest
B. Whether the buyer is Jewish or gentile
C. Whether a plausible innocent motive can be assigned to the buyer
D. Whether the item is livestock or an inanimate object
Correct: C — Rav Ashi rejects Rabba's distinction about being 'commanded to rest.' Instead, the rule is: if an innocent motive is plausible, assign it — even if you must rest the item. If no innocent motive exists (like selling a plow in Shevi'it), it's forbidden regardless.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Abaye object to Rabba selling a donkey to a Jew suspected of reselling to gentiles?
A. Because the suspected Jew is treated like a gentile for all purposes
B. Because a baraita shows selling to Samaritans is prohibited where they are suspected of reselling to gentiles
C. Because Abaye holds one may never sell livestock to any Jew
D. Because the donkey was too valuable to risk
Correct: B — Abaye cites a baraita about Samaritans: since they are not suspected of bestiality, the only reason to prohibit selling them livestock is because they might resell to gentiles. This proves that selling to a suspected reseller is itself prohibited.
