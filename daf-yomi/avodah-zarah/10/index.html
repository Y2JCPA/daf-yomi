Dating the Promissory Note: Greek Rule or Exodus?

Students of Rabba
This note is dated 6 years ahead — it's a postdated note. We must hold it until the date arrives.
Rav Naḥman
No — this was written by an exacting scribe (lavlar medakdek) who counts the 6 years the Greeks ruled only in Elam before spreading worldwide.
Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov
How do you know we count from Greek rule? Maybe we count from the Exodus, dropping the thousands digit — making this note postdated!
Rav Naḥman
In the exile, we count only according to the Greek kings. This is established practice.
Rav Aḥa bar Ya'akov
(Initially skeptical, but later investigated and confirmed:) Indeed, a baraita confirms — in exile we count only by Greek kings.
Resolution Ravina proves from the Mishnah: since we count document years from Tishrei (not Nisan), we must be using the Greek system, not the Exodus.

Ravina's Proof: Which Calendar System?

Mishnah (Rosh HaShanah 2a):
Two New Years for dating documents
1 Nisan
New Year for kings of Israel
1 Tishrei
New Year for kings of the nations
↓
We currently date documents from Tishrei
↓
If we used the Exodus, we'd count from Nisan (when the Exodus occurred)
↓
∴ We count by Greek kings — Rav Naḥman is correct!
Practical Rule A promissory note dated 6 years ahead is assumed to be from an exacting scribe who counts Greek rule in Elam — it is NOT postdated.

Antoninus & Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi: Coded Vegetable Messages

🌱 Antoninus sends gargira (rocket plant) — hinting his daughter Gira acted promiscuously (gar)
↓
🌿 Rabbi sends kusbarta (coriander) — meaning: kos (kill) your barta (daughter)
↓
🧅 Antoninus sends karti (leeks) — I will be karet (cut off) if I do so!
↓
🥬 Rabbi sends ḥasa (lettuce) — have ḥas (mercy) on her
Why Speak in Code? Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi feared Roman officials might overhear, as Ecclesiastes 10:20 warns: 'A bird of the air shall carry the voice.'

Antoninus's Devotion to Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi

Secret Visits

  • Built an underground tunnel between their houses
  • Killed one servant at each entrance to maintain secrecy
  • Demanded no one else be present during visits
  • Even objected to R' Ḥanina bar Ḥama's presence

Acts of Service

  • Sent crushed gold hidden in wheat sacks
  • Fed and gave drink to Rabbi daily
  • Bent down as a step for Rabbi to climb into bed
  • Said: 'Oh that I were a mattress under you in Olam HaBa!'
R' Ḥanina's Miracle When Antoninus found R' Ḥanina present, R' Ḥanina discovered the killed servant and prayed — reviving him from the dead rather than deliver bad news to the emperor.
