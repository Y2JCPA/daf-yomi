Avodah Zarah 59 — Overview

סחור סחור, אמרינן לנזירא — Go around, go around, we say to the nazirite
DAF SNAPSHOT This daf explores the boundaries of gentile interaction with Jewish food and wine. Beginning with worshipped water (public vs. private), it moves to R' Chiyya bar Abba's visit to Gavla where R' Yochanan issues three strict rulings on children, wine, and cooked food. The daf then addresses gentiles transporting grapes, an etrog in a wine barrel, and the right to collect damages when a gentile deliberately renders wine prohibited.

Worshipped Water: When Is It Prohibited?

A gentile bows to water — is it prohibited?
Public Water
Permitted
R' Yochanan: Water belonging to the public is not rendered prohibited
Private Water
Depends on attachment to ground and method of detachment
↓
Connected to Ground
Permitted
Worshipping something attached to ground does not prohibit it
Wave Detached It
Detached without human involvement — disputed (R' Yochanan vs. sons of R' Chiyya)
Hand Struck Water
Prohibited
Detached by human action + private ownership
KEY PRINCIPLE The Gemara narrows the case: R' Yochanan's ruling about public water is necessary specifically where a person struck the water, detaching it through human involvement.

The Gavla Incident: Three Problematic Practices

R' Chiyya bar Abba
I visited Gavla and saw three things: Jewish women pregnant from gentiles who were circumcised but not yet immersed; wine diluted by gentiles that Jews drank; and lupines cooked by gentiles that Jews ate.
R' Chiyya bar Abba
I did not say anything to correct them.
R' Yochanan
Go declare: (1) Their children are mamzerim. (2) Their wine is prohibited as yayin nesekh. (3) Their lupines are forbidden due to bishul akum.
R' Yochanan
They are not well-versed in Torah — any leniency would be misapplied too broadly.
CONTEXT R' Yochanan issued especially strict rulings because the people of Gavla lacked Torah scholarship and would extend leniencies beyond their proper scope.

Why the Children Are Mamzerim

👤 Gentile man undergoes circumcision as part of conversion
↓
🚫 He has not yet immersed in a mikveh
↓
⚖️ R' Yochanan's rule: One is not a convert until circumcised AND immersed
↓
👤 Without immersion, the father is still legally a gentile
↓
📜 R' Yochanan via Rabba bar bar Chana: A gentile who has relations with a Jewish woman — the child is a mamzer
↓
👶 Therefore the children of Gavla are declared mamzerim
HALAKHA Both circumcision AND immersion are required to complete conversion. Without both, the person remains a gentile for all halakhic purposes.

Bishul Akum: Two Versions of Rav's Standard

Version 1: Raw Edibility

  • Rav Shmuel bar Rav Yitzchak says Rav says:
  • Anything eaten raw is exempt from bishul akum
  • Lupines are NOT eaten raw (too bitter)
  • Under this version: lupines ARE subject to bishul akum
  • Problem: This contradicts R' Yochanan's implication that for Torah scholars lupines would be permitted

Version 2: King's Table

  • Alternate version of Rav Shmuel bar Rav Yitzchak:
  • Anything NOT served on a king's table is exempt
  • Lupines lack the importance to appear on a king's table
  • Therefore: lupines are inherently permitted
  • R' Yochanan follows this version — prohibition in Gavla was only due to their lack of Torah knowledge
R' YOCHANAN'S VIEW R' Yochanan holds like Version 2 (king's table standard). Lupines are normally permitted; the Gavla ban was a special stringency for people not well-versed in Torah.

Gentile Bringing Grapes to the Winepress

Questioners
May a gentile bring grapes to the winepress?
Rav Kahana
Prohibited ab initio — 'Go around, go around, we say to the nazirite; do not come near the vineyard!'
Rav Yeimar
But a baraita teaches: A gentile who brought grapes in baskets and barrels — even if juice sprays onto the grapes — they are permitted!
Rav Kahana
That baraita discusses after the fact (b'dieved). I am saying one should not permit it ab initio (l'chatchila).
DISTINCTION L'chatchila (ab initio): Don't let a gentile bring grapes. B'dieved (after the fact): If he already did, the grapes and wine are permitted.
