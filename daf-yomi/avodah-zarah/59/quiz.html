Avodah Zarah 59 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Yochanan, why is water belonging to the public not prohibited when worshipped by a gentile?
A. Because water can never be prohibited
B. Because public ownership prevents prohibition even when detached by human action
C. Because water is always considered attached to the ground
D. Because only wine can become prohibited through worship
Correct: B — R' Yochanan teaches that public water is not rendered prohibited. The Gemara clarifies this is relevant even when the water was detached by human action (striking it with a hand), which would otherwise prohibit privately-owned water.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did R' Yochanan declare the children of Gavla to be mamzerim?
A. Because the mothers had not immersed in a mikveh
B. Because the fathers were circumcised but not immersed, making them still legally gentile
C. Because the marriages were not performed according to halakha
D. Because the fathers had not yet accepted the mitzvot
Correct: B — R' Yochanan holds that conversion requires both circumcision AND immersion. Since the fathers had only been circumcised but not immersed, they were still legally gentiles. A gentile who has relations with a Jewish woman produces a mamzer according to R' Yochanan.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the difference between the two versions of Rav Shmuel bar Rav Yitzchak's statement regarding bishul akum?
A. Version 1 exempts all vegetables; Version 2 exempts only grains
B. Version 1 exempts food eaten raw; Version 2 exempts food not served at a king's table
C. Version 1 applies only to Jews; Version 2 applies to gentiles
D. Version 1 is more lenient; Version 2 is more strict
Correct: B — Version 1 exempts foods that can be eaten raw (which would NOT exempt bitter lupines). Version 2 exempts foods not important enough for a king's table (which DOES exempt lupines). R' Yochanan follows Version 2, which is why lupines are generally permitted but were banned in Gavla due to the residents' lack of Torah knowledge.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rav Kahana reconcile his prohibition against a gentile bringing grapes to the winepress with the baraita that permits the grapes?
A. He says the baraita is discussing a different type of grape
B. He distinguishes between ab initio prohibition and after-the-fact permission
C. He rejects the baraita as unreliable
D. He limits the baraita to cases where no juice sprayed
Correct: B — Rav Kahana distinguishes between l'chatchila (ab initio) and b'dieved (after the fact). His prohibition is about not letting a gentile bring grapes in the first place, while the baraita permits the grapes after a gentile has already brought them.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does Rav Ashi rule that a Jew may collect monetary compensation from a gentile who deliberately poured the Jew's wine as a libation?
A. Because the wine is still usable after purification
B. Because it is treated as if the gentile destroyed the wine, requiring payment for damages
C. Because the gentile violated a contract
D. Because the wine can be sold to other gentiles
Correct: B — Rav Ashi holds the wine is prohibited and one may not derive benefit from it. Nevertheless, the gentile's act is treated as destruction of property (like burning it), and the gentile must pay damages. He derives this from R' Yehuda ben Bava and R' Yehuda ben Beteira's reasoning that the gentile cannot render the wine prohibited against the Jew's will.
