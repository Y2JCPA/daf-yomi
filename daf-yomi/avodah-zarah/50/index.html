How Many Stones Are Prohibited Near Markulis?

R' Yishmael
Idol worshippers sometimes build a small Markulis pile beside a large one. Therefore 3 stones (resembling a pile) are prohibited, but 2 stones are permitted.
Rabbis
They do not build small piles beside large ones. Any stones visible together with the pile are prohibited — whether 2 or 3 — as they may have fallen from it.
Rava
The baraita that seems to show them disagreeing about stones that fell should be emended: it means stones found adjacent to the pile, not confirmed fallen.
Rava
R' Yishmael permits 2 stones only when they are in two adjacent areas separated by an elevation — evidence they did not fall from the pile.
Resolution R' Yishmael's leniency for 2 stones applies only when terrain separates them from the pile, making it evident they didn't fall from it

What Qualifies as a Markulis Pile?

מרקוליס
Structure A proper Markulis pile consists of one stone on one side, one on the other, and one on top. Stones merely adjacent to each other do not form a main pile.

However, stones added to an existing pile (offerings to Mercury) need not follow this arrangement — any stone tossed toward the pile counts.

Why Some Rabbis Permitted and Others Prohibited the Roads

Stringent View — Withdraw

  • Rav Giddel citing Rav: offerings to idols can never be nullified
  • Compared to a corpse (met) — always prohibited for benefit
  • Psalm 106:28: "They ate offerings to the dead"
  • Just as a corpse is never nullified, neither are idol offerings

Lenient View — Permitted

  • The "never nullified" rule applies only to offerings parallel to Temple sacrifices
  • Stones are not brought as offerings in the Temple
  • Therefore stones lack the status of true idolatrous offerings
  • A gentile's repurposing effectively nullifies them
Baraita A gentile who paves roads with Markulis stones — permitted (he nullified them). A Jew who does so — prohibited (a Jew cannot nullify idol worship).
