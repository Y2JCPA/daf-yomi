5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to R' Eliezer, when is a prostitute's payment permitted for sacrifice?
A. When she sacrificed it before the intercourse took place
B. When she consecrated it verbally before intercourse
C. When the payment was given after intercourse
D. When the payment was an unspecified animal
Correct: A — R' Eliezer specifically says the baraita refers to a case where she sacrificed the payment in the Temple first, before the intercourse. The Gemara's dilemma about whether mere consecration suffices remains unresolved (teyku).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rav Chisda resolve the contradiction between the two baraitot about payment given after intercourse?
A. One refers to a Jewish prostitute, the other to a gentile
B. One is where he specified 'this lamb' (forbidden), the other where he said 'a lamb' (permitted)
C. One is where she consecrated it, the other where she sacrificed it
D. One refers to payment within 30 days, the other to payment after 30 days
Correct: B — Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak in the name of Rav Chisda distinguishes: 'this lamb' (specific designation) makes it forbidden even years later, while 'a lamb' (unspecified) means whatever he later gives is not deemed prohibited payment.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say the principle 'declaration to the Most High equals transfer to an ordinary person' might make consecration equivalent to sacrifice?
A. Because verbal consecration transfers the animal to Temple ownership, removing it from being 'payment'
B. Because the Temple always acquires by speech rather than physical acts
C. Because a prostitute cannot perform physical acquisition
D. Because the animal ceases to exist once consecrated
Correct: A — The principle (אמירתו לגבוה כמסירתו להדיוט) suggests that verbal consecration effects a legal transfer to the Temple, which could mean the animal is no longer 'extant' as payment at the time of intercourse — but the Gemara leaves this unresolved.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rava, why were the Sages of R' Yannai's school permitted to borrow Sabbatical-Year produce and repay afterward?
A. Because Sabbatical produce has no monetary value
B. Because they repaid within the Sabbatical year itself
C. Because they did not designate specific coins as repayment for the produce
D. Because the poor people voluntarily gave them the produce
Correct: C — Rava rules that although an abstract debt is incurred, the prohibition against commerce in Sabbatical produce only applies when specific coins are designated as payment. Since R' Yannai's scholars repaid with unspecified money, it was permitted.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
In the case of an employer and laborers, what scenario does Rav Pappa say the baraita's prohibition refers to?
A. The employer verbally authorized the purchase of any food
B. The employer paid the storekeeper a dinar in advance for the food
C. The laborers used their own money and were reimbursed
D. The storekeeper refused to give credit to the employer
Correct: B — Rav Pappa explains that the baraita's prohibition applies because the employer first paid the storekeeper a dinar for the food — this constitutes designating specific money for the purchase, making the employer liable if the laborers bought forbidden produce.
