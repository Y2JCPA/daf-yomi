Renting & Selling Property to Gentiles: A Regional Framework

Rabbi Meir
In Eretz Yisrael: rent houses, but not fields. In Syria: sell houses, rent fields. Outside: sell houses and rent fields.
Rabbi Yosei
In Eretz Yisrael: rent houses only. In Syria: sell houses and rent fields. Outside: sell both houses and fields.
Shmuel
The halakha follows Rabbi Yosei — it is permitted to sell houses outside Eretz Yisrael.
Core Principle The strictness of property transactions with gentiles depends on proximity to Eretz Yisrael and whether the property is a house or field — fields carry two concerns (tithes + settlement), houses carry fewer.

Why Are Fields Stricter Than Houses?

Why can't we rent fields in Eretz Yisrael?
↓
Problem #1
Aids gentiles in encamping in the land (yishuv ha'aretz)
Problem #2
Releases land from obligation of tithes (ma'aser)
↓
Houses also aid encamping — but what about mezuzah?
↓
Rav Mesharshiyya: Mezuzah is the resident's obligation, not the house's. No mitzvah is removed from the house itself.
Bottom Line Fields have two inherent problems (tithes + settlement), while houses have only one — so the Sages applied stricter decrees to fields.

Kibush Yachid: Does David's Conquest Count?

Rabbi Meir
Kibush yachid shemei kibush — the conquest of an individual IS called a conquest. Syria has the sanctity of Eretz Yisrael.
Rabbi Yosei
Kibush yachid lav shemei kibush — the conquest of an individual is NOT a full conquest. Syria has only rabbinic-level restrictions.
Gemara
This explains why R' Meir is stricter about Syria — for him it's like Eretz Yisrael itself, and for fields with two problems, the Sages issued preventive decrees.
Gemara
For R' Yosei, Syria's restrictions are only rabbinic decrees, so we don't pile decree upon decree (gezeira l'gezeira) for houses.
Halachic Impact This dispute about King David's conquest of Syria determines the entire framework of how strictly we treat Syrian property transactions.

Decree Upon Decree — When Do We Stack?

We DO Stack Decrees

  • Fields in Syria — may not rent
  • Fields outside Eretz Yisrael — may not sell (R' Meir)
  • Reason: Fields have TWO problems, warranting extra caution

We Do NOT Stack Decrees

  • Houses in Syria — renting permitted
  • Houses outside Eretz Yisrael — selling permitted
  • Reason: Houses have only ONE problem — not severe enough for stacked decrees
Principle The Sages only issued a decree to prevent violation of another decree (gezeira l'gezeira) when the matter involved two separate concerns.

Renting a House for Residence

לֹא תָבִיא תוֹעֵבָה אֶל בֵּיתֶךָ
Even Where Renting Is Permitted Even where the Sages permitted renting a house to a gentile, one may not rent it as a residence, because the gentile will bring objects of idol worship into it. As it states: "You shall not bring an abomination into your house" (Devarim 7:26) — and a rented property is still considered the Jew's house.
