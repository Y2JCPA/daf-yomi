Avodah Zarah 21 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why are the restrictions on renting FIELDS stricter than those on renting HOUSES?
A. Fields are more valuable property
B. Fields involve two concerns: tithes and aiding settlement, while houses have fewer
C. Fields are always in Eretz Yisrael
D. Gentiles are more likely to use fields for idol worship
Correct: B — The Gemara explains that renting a field releases it from the obligation of tithes AND aids gentile settlement — two problems. Houses lack the tithe concern because mezuzah is an obligation on the resident, not the house.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the practical difference between Rabbi Meir and Rabbi Yosei regarding Syria?
A. Rabbi Meir permits selling fields in Syria; Rabbi Yosei forbids it
B. Rabbi Meir considers Syria as having Eretz Yisrael-level sanctity; Rabbi Yosei does not
C. Rabbi Yosei forbids all transactions in Syria
D. They agree on Syria but disagree about outside Eretz Yisrael
Correct: B — Rabbi Meir holds kibush yachid shemei kibush — David's individual conquest of Syria gives it the sanctity of Eretz Yisrael. Rabbi Yosei disagrees, treating Syria's restrictions as purely rabbinic.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why is renting a bathhouse to a gentile forbidden even where renting a house is permitted?
A. Bathhouses are more valuable than houses
B. A bathhouse is called by the owner's name and people will think the Jew operates it on Shabbat
C. Gentiles will use the bathhouse for idol worship
D. The Torah explicitly forbids bathhouse rental
Correct: B — The bathhouse is known by the Jewish owner's name, and since people don't typically assume sharecropping arrangements for bathhouses, onlookers will think the Jew is having work done on Shabbat.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why does the Gemara say renting a field to a gentile is permitted regarding Shabbat concerns, but renting a bathhouse is not?
A. Fields don't require Shabbat labor
B. Everyone knows field workers are sharecroppers working for themselves, but bathhouse workers are assumed to be hired by the owner
C. Bathhouses are in the city where people can see them
D. Fields are exempt from Shabbat prohibitions
Correct: B — People commonly know that a gentile working a field is a sharecropper (aris) working for his own tenancy. But sharecropping arrangements for bathhouses are unusual, so observers assume the gentile is a hired worker acting on behalf of the Jewish owner.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to R' Shimon ben Elazar, why may one NOT rent a field to a Samaritan, even though renting to a gentile is permitted?
A. Samaritans don't pay fair rent
B. Samaritans will perform work during Chol HaMoed, and it's called by the owner's name
C. Samaritans will plant forbidden crops
D. Samaritans don't observe the laws of tithes
Correct: B — Samaritans observe Shabbat but do not observe the restrictions of Chol HaMoed. Since the field is called by the Jewish owner's name, people will think the Jew authorized festival labor. Gentiles are permitted because they're assumed to be sharecroppers working for themselves.
