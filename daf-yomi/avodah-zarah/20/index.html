Three Meanings of 'Lo Teḥonnem'

"לא תחנם"
"You shall not show them mercy"
(Devarim 7:2)
↓
No Encampment
Do not let them acquire land in Eretz Yisrael
(חניה)
No Favor
Do not praise them or give them חן
No Free Gifts
Do not give them an undeserved gift
(חינם)
KEY CONCEPT The Gemara derives all three prohibitions from the precise spelling of תחנם — without a vav or yud — allowing multiple readings from one word.

Can You Give a Free Gift to a Gentile?

R' Meir
The verse says 'give it… or sell it' — this means neveilah may be given or sold to both a ger toshav and a gentile.
R' Yehuda
The verse means exactly what it says: give to a ger toshav, sell to a gentile. Free gifts to gentiles are prohibited.
Gemara
R' Meir's reading seems straightforward. Why does R' Yehuda disagree?
R' Yehuda
If R' Meir is correct, why does the verse say 'or' (או) between giving and selling? This word teaches they are separate — each method applies only to its stated recipient.
R' Meir
The word 'or' teaches that giving to a ger toshav takes precedence over selling to a gentile.
VERSE "You may give it to the ger toshav… or you may sell it to a foreigner" (Devarim 14:21)

Rav's Prohibition on Praising Beauty

אסור לאדם שיאמר כמה נאה גויה זו
HALAKHA Rav says: It is prohibited for a person to say "How beautiful is this gentile woman!"

This is derived from "Lo teḥonnem" — do not give them חן (favor/praise).

But Rabban Shimon ben Gamliel saw a beautiful gentile woman and exclaimed: "How great are Your works, O Lord!" (Tehillim 104:24)

The Gemara resolves: He was thanking God for creating beautiful creatures — not praising her. One who sees outstanding creatures recites: "Blessed be He Who has created such in His world."

The Angel of Death: Poison or Slaughter?

Baraita
The Angel of Death stands above the sick person with his sword drawn, a drop of poison hanging from the blade. The person trembles, opens his mouth, and the drop falls in — from this he dies and putrefies.
Shmuel's Father
The Angel of Death told me: "Were I not concerned for human dignity, I would uncover the incision like ritual slaughter." This implies he slaughters with a sword!
Gemara
No contradiction — the drop of poison itself cuts the trachea and esophagus, effectively slaughtering the person.
R' Ḥanina bar Kahana
This explains putrefaction: the poison enters through the mouth. To prevent it, turn the deceased face down immediately.
VIVID IMAGE The Angel of Death is described as entirely covered in eyes — seeing from every direction. When the dying person sees him, he trembles and opens his mouth.
