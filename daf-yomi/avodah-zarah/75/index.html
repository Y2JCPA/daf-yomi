Cleansing Stages for Winepresses: Sura Version

AuthorityDamp PressDry Press
Rav (Sura)2 stages: Ashes → Water3 stages: Water → Ashes → Water
Shmuel (Sura)3 stages: Ashes → Water → Ashes4 stages: Water → Ashes → Water → Ashes
Rav (Pumbedita)3 stages: Ashes → Water → Ashes4 stages: Water → Ashes → Water → Ashes
Shmuel (Pumbedita)4 stages: Ashes → Water → Ashes → Water5 stages: Water → Ashes → Water → Ashes → Water
Resolution The Pumbedita versions don't truly disagree — Shmuel counts the final water rinse (to remove ashes), while Rav does not count it.

Purifying Equipment by Material

Rinse Only

  • Hair straining bags (not absorbent)
  • Baskets plaited from palm strips
  • Winepress planks & troughs

Ashes & Water

  • Wool straining bags
  • Pressing baskets of palm leaves or hemp (bitzbutz)
  • Baskets plaited from tzavta

Leave Dormant 12 Months

  • Flax straining bags (highly absorbent)
  • Pressing baskets of bulrush or reeds
  • Flax-plaited baskets — also undo knots
Principle The more absorbent the material, the more intensive the purification process required.

Am Ha'aretz Touches Grape Clusters in the Winepress

One Opinion
Only the cluster he touched and its surroundings are impure. The rest of the winepress is pure.
Other Opinion
The entire winepress is impure — the wine connects everything as one unit.
Gemara
But in a mishna, liquid flowing in an oil press connects everything for impurity!
Gemara
In the oil press nothing interrupts the flow. Here, the grape clusters interrupt the wine's continuity.
Conclusion
The Sages taught R' Yirmeya in accordance with: cluster and surroundings impure, rest of winepress pure.
Key Distinction Grape clusters break the continuity of the wine, preventing impurity from spreading through the entire press — unlike an oil press with uninterrupted flowing liquid.

Mishna: Kashering Gentile Cooking Utensils

Purchase a cooking utensil from a gentile
↓
How was the utensil used?
Cold use only
Immerse in mikveh (40 se'ah)
Boiling liquids (pots)
Purge with boiling water + immerse
Direct fire (grills, spits)
Heat white-hot (libun) + immerse
↓
Knife: Polish it and it is pure
Rule Ke-vol'o kach polto — the method of kashering matches the method of absorption. All utensils also require immersion.

Biblical Source for Immersion in 40 Se'ah

Rava
The verse says 'you shall pass it through fire and it shall be pure' (Bamidbar 31:23) — this adds another purification: immersion.
Bar Kappara
'With the water of nidda' — not sprinkling water, but water fit for a nidda to immerse: a mikveh of 40 se'ah.
Gemara
Why do we need both verses?
Gemara
'And it shall be pure' alone → maybe any amount of water suffices. 'Water of nidda' alone → maybe sunset is required too. Together: 40 se'ah, effective immediately.
Halacha Both verses are necessary: one establishes the 40 se'ah requirement, the other teaches that purification takes effect immediately — no need to wait for sunset.
