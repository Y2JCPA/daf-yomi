School of R' Yannai: Sinking & Grafting

School of R' Yannai
The rule about removing regrowth applies even when one sank a vine shoot or grafted onto the tree — a more radical change.
Gemara (objection)
But the Mishna says even lopping and trimming — lesser acts — make regrowth forbidden! Why mention sinking/grafting?
Gemara (resolution)
R' Yannai's school speaks about revocation: even with sinking/grafting, which resembles replanting, removing regrowth still permits the tree.
Gemara
You might have thought sinking/grafting = planting for idolatry, making the entire tree forbidden. R' Yannai teaches: no — just remove regrowth.
INSIGHT Even a major modification like grafting for idolatry doesn't turn the tree into one 'initially planted' for idolatry.

Shmuel vs. Rabbis: Bowing to a Tree

Shmuel (per R' Yosei b. Yehuda)

  • Bowing to a tree makes subsequent growth forbidden
  • Follows R' Yosei bar Yehuda: planted-then-worshipped = forbidden
  • Source: "Burn their asherim with fire" (Deut. 12:3) — entire tree

The Rabbis

  • Only lopping/trimming makes regrowth forbidden
  • Mere bowing does NOT forbid additional growth
  • Source: "Hew down their asherim" (Deut. 7:5) — only hewn part forbidden
RAV ASHI'S CHALLENGE Perhaps both agree growth is forbidden — their dispute is only about the trunk. The Gemara rejects this because then no one would teach the Mishna's middle case.

Sitting in the shade of an ashera
↓
Primary Shade (within tree's height)
Forbidden ab initio
But if one sat: remains pure
Secondary Shade (beyond tree's height)
Also forbidden ab initio
If one sat: remains pure
↓
Passing beneath an ashera
renders one impure
↓
Why? Impossible that no remnant of idolatrous offering remains beneath it
(R' Yehuda ben Beteira: offerings = corpse-level ohel impurity)
R' YOCHANAN Even the 'shade of its shade' — the secondary, weaker shadow — is forbidden. This extends the prohibition beyond the obvious.

Ashera Over Public Property

Mishna
If the ashera's branches extend over public property ('robbing the public') and one passed beneath — he remains pure.
R' Yitzḥak b. Elazar / Ḥizkiyya
One may even pass ab initio — it is fully permitted.
R' Yoḥanan
Only after the fact is one pure, but it remains forbidden ab initio.
Gemara (reconciliation)
No dispute: no other route → permitted ab initio. Another route exists → forbidden ab initio, but if one passed, pure.
Rav Sheshet
Even when permitted (no other route), I run past — an important person (adam ḥashuv) must avoid misleading onlookers (marit ayin).
PRACTICAL RULE When there is no alternative path, one may pass under the ashera, but a prominent person should move through quickly to avoid giving the wrong impression.
