Avodah Zarah 48 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, what is the status of a tree that was initially planted for the purpose of idol worship?
A. Only the branches are forbidden
B. The entire tree is forbidden — no benefit allowed
C. Only the regrowth is forbidden
D. The tree is permitted once the idol is removed
Correct: B — A tree planted from the start for idol worship is the most severe category — it is entirely forbidden and one may not derive any benefit from it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What did the School of R' Yannai clarify about sinking a vine shoot or grafting onto a tree for idolatry?
A. It makes the entire tree forbidden like one planted for idolatry
B. It is not considered a significant enough act to forbid anything
C. Even so, removing the regrowth permits the tree — it is not like initial planting
D. It only applies to grapevines, not other trees
Correct: C — R' Yannai's school taught about revocation: even though sinking/grafting resembles replanting, it does not render the entire tree forbidden — removing regrowth suffices.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why is one who passes beneath an ashera rendered ritually impure?
A. The shade itself transmits impurity
B. It is impossible that no remnant of an idolatrous offering remains underneath
C. The roots of the tree are forbidden
D. Passing beneath is considered an act of worship
Correct: B — R' Yehuda ben Beteira teaches that idolatrous offerings transmit tent-impurity (like a corpse), and it is assumed remnants of offerings remain beneath the tree.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why did Rav Sheshet ask his attendant to run past the ashera, even though there was no other route?
A. He believed it was forbidden even when there was no other route
B. He wanted to avoid the impurity of the shade
C. An important person should avoid giving the impression that walking under an ashera is permitted when there IS another route
D. His attendant was worried about the idolatrous offerings
Correct: C — Although it was fully permitted since there was no other way, Rav Sheshet — as a prominent figure — wanted to run past to prevent onlookers from incorrectly concluding it is always permitted to walk under an ashera.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does R' Yosei forbid planting vegetables under an ashera even during the rainy season?
A. The shade still benefits the vegetables in winter
B. The falling foliage serves as fertilizer for the plants
C. Rain washes forbidden substances into the soil
D. The roots of the ashera entangle with the vegetable roots
Correct: B — R' Yosei holds that fallen foliage (neviyya) from the ashera fertilizes the vegetables. Since both the permitted soil and the forbidden foliage contribute to growth (zeh va-zeh gorem), R' Yosei rules the result is forbidden.
