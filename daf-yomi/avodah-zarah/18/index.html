↓

↓

↓
✨ 'The parchment burns but the letters fly to heaven'
↓

Why Was Rabbi Ḥanina ben Teradyon Punished?

R' Ḥanina pronounced the Shem HaMeforash (Ineffable Name) with all its letters
↓
But Abba Shaul says one who does this has no share in Olam HaBa!
↓
Answer: He did it to teach himself — 'You shall not learn to do' but you may learn to understand (Deut. 18:9)
↓
So why was he still punished?
↓
Because he pronounced it in public (בפרהסיא) instead of privately
Principle Learning forbidden matters for the sake of understanding is permitted — but one must still exercise discretion about where and how that knowledge is expressed.

Punishments of R' Ḥanina's Family — Measure for Measure

His wifeExecution by decapitationDid not protest his public pronunciation
His daughterSent to a Roman brothelTook vain pleasure in the compliments of Roman nobles about her walk
Key Rule Anyone who has the ability to protest sinful conduct and does not protest is punished for that person's sin. Even subtle vanity — sins 'trampled by one's heel' (Ps. 49:6) — encircle a person on the Day of Judgment.

R' Yosei ben Kisma Warns R' Ḥanina ben Teradyon

R' Yosei ben Kisma
Ḥanina, my brother — don't you see that Rome rules by Heaven's decree? They destroyed the Temple, burned the Sanctuary, killed the pious — and still stand!
R' Ḥanina ben Teradyon
Heaven will have mercy and protect me.
R' Yosei ben Kisma
I say reasonable matters and you respond with faith alone? I fear they will burn you and your Torah scroll by fire!
R' Ḥanina ben Teradyon
My teacher — am I destined for Olam HaBa?
R' Yosei ben Kisma
Did any special incident occur to you as a sign?
R' Ḥanina ben Teradyon
I confused my personal Purim money with charity funds and distributed it all to the poor at my own expense.
R' Yosei ben Kisma
If so — may my portion be of your portion, and my lot be of your lot.
Turning Point Days later R' Yosei ben Kisma died and Roman dignitaries attended his funeral. On their return they found R' Ḥanina still teaching Torah publicly — and sentenced him.

The Martyrdom: 'The Letters Fly to Heaven'

📜 Romans wrapped R' Ḥanina in his Torah scroll and encircled him with branches
↓
🔥 They set fire to the scroll and placed wet wool on his heart to slow his death
↓
💬 His daughter cried: 'Father, must I see you like this?'
↓
📖 He replied: 'He who seeks retribution for the Torah scroll will also seek retribution for me'
↓
✨ Students asked: 'What do you see?' — 'The parchment burns but the letters fly to heaven'
↓
🚫 Students urged him to open his mouth to die faster — he refused: 'Let He who gave me my soul take it'
↓
⚔️ The executioner offered to increase the flame and remove the wool — R' Ḥanina promised him Olam HaBa
↓
🕊️ The executioner did so, and R' Ḥanina's soul departed — then the executioner leapt into the fire himself
Heavenly Voice A Bat Kol declared: Rabbi Ḥanina ben Teradyon and his executioner are both destined for life in the World-to-Come. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi wept and said: Some acquire their share in one moment.

Rabbi Meir's Daring Rescue Mission

R' Ḥanina's daughter was placed in a Roman brothel
↓
Rabbi Meir went to rescue her, disguised himself, and tested whether she was still chaste
↓
He bribed the guard — the guard worried: 'What if my money runs out?'
↓
R' Meir said: 'Say: God of Meir, answer me!' and you will be saved
↓
R' Meir proved this by throwing a clod of earth at carnivorous dogs — the phrase saved him
↓
The guard released her; when caught, the guard said the phrase and could not be hanged
↓
Romans engraved R' Meir's face at Rome's entrance — he fled to Babylonia
Two Traditions How did R' Meir evade capture? One opinion: he fooled pursuers by appearing to eat non-kosher food. Another: Elijah appeared as a prostitute and embraced him, so Romans said 'That can't be Rabbi Meir.'
