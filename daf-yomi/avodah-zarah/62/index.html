When Are Wages from Forbidden Items Prohibited?

A Jew is hired by a gentile to do work
Working directly with yayin nesekh
Wage is FORBIDDEN
Rabbinic penalty (R' Yochanan)
Other work, incidental wine transport
Wage is PERMITTED
Even if he says 'move this barrel for me'
Key Principle The Sages imposed a special penalty (קנס) on wages connected to libation wine — stricter than the general rule for forbidden-benefit items.

The Mishna's Cases: Hiring a Jewish Laborer

Wage FORBIDDEN

  • Hired specifically to work with yayin nesekh (libation wine)
  • Direct involvement in producing or handling the forbidden wine
  • Similarly: renting a donkey specifically to carry yayin nesekh

Wage PERMITTED

  • Hired for other work, then asked to move a barrel of yayin nesekh
  • The wine transport is incidental to the main job
  • Similarly: renting a donkey to ride — even if gentile places his wine jug on it
Mishna The critical distinction is whether the primary hiring was for work with the forbidden wine, or whether the wine involvement was incidental.

Why Is the Wage Forbidden?

Gemara
Is it because benefit from yayin nesekh is forbidden, so the wage is also forbidden?
Challenge
Orlah and kilei hakerem are also forbidden for benefit — yet money from selling them is valid for kiddushin! (Kiddushin 56b)
Gemara
Perhaps because yayin nesekh transfers its idol-worship status to the money?
Challenge
Shevi'it produce also transfers sanctity — yet a laborer's wage is permitted when the work isn't tied to purchase!
R' Abbahu / R' Yochanan
This is a rabbinic penalty (קנס) imposed on those who work with yayin nesekh and on donkey drivers transporting Shevi'it produce.
Resolution R' Yochanan explains the forbidden wage is not a natural legal consequence of the item's status — it is a special penalty the Sages imposed to discourage involvement with forbidden items.

Donkey Drivers & Shevi'it: Abaye vs. Rava

AmoraInterpretation of 'Wage is Shevi'it'Reasoning
AbayeWe pay them with Shevi'it produce — given as a gift, not paymentLike the mishna in Ma'aser Sheni: what may not be given as payment may be given as a gift
RavaTheir monetary wage becomes sacred with Shevi'it sanctityDonkey drivers' wages are large, so the Sages penalized them — unlike a regular laborer whose wage is small
Rava's Distinction A regular laborer (small wage) → not penalized.
A donkey driver (large wage) → penalized with Shevi'it sanctity.
Yayin nesekh → always penalized, even for a laborer, due to extra stringency.

Stam Yeinam (Nondescript Gentile Wine): Is the Wage Forbidden?

A Jew rents out his ship to transport stam yeinam
↓
Gentiles pay him with wheat
↓
He asks Rav Ḥisda: What is the status of this wheat?
↓
Burn it and bury it in a graveyard!
Ruling Rav Ḥisda rules that wages from stam yeinam are forbidden — just like yayin nesekh itself. The wheat must be burned (to prevent eating) and buried (to prevent using ashes as fertilizer).
7 / 9

Why Burn AND Bury? Preventing All Benefit

❓ Why not just scatter the wheat?
↓
🚫 Someone might find scattered kernels and eat them
↓
❓ Why not burn and scatter the ashes?
↓
🌱 Someone might use the ashes to fertilize fields
↓
❓ Why not just bury it unburned?
↓
🤔 Unlike court execution items (buried in a known place), someone might think it was stolen goods and dig it up
↓
✅ Solution: Burn THEN bury in a graveyard — no one will use graveyard soil
Principle The double measure (burning + burial in a graveyard) ensures no possible avenue of benefit remains open.
