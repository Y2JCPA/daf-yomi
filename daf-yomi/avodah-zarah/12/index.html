What Concern Did the Sages Dismiss Regarding the Pots in Tyre?

R' Ḥanina
A Jew and gentile may place two pots on one stove — the Sages were not concerned. Similarly, business in the bazaar of Gaza near its festival is permitted.
Abaye
The Sages were not concerned about neveilah — we don't fear the gentile will throw non-kosher meat into the Jew's pot. Likewise, we don't fear idol-worship money will reach Jewish hands.
Rava
The leniency is only for a rabbinic concern — the gentile won't throw meat (no benefit, fear of being seen). The parallel: we don't fear the Jew does business on the gentile's festival day (rabbinic).
Rabba bar Ulla
The Sages weren't concerned about food splattering (tzinnora) between pots — unlikely and nullified. Likewise, business before the festival (outside in time and place) is permitted.
KEY ISSUE Three Amoraim identify three different concerns the Sages dismissed — each with a different parallel to the bazaar of Gaza.

Three Explanations of the Two Pots Leniency

Abaye
No concern the gentile will throw neveilah into the Jew's pot → No concern idol-worship coins will reach the Jew (Torah-level leniency)
Rava
No concern the gentile will cook the Jew's food (bishul akum — rabbinic) → No concern about doing business on the festival day (also rabbinic)
Rabba bar Ulla
No concern about splattering (tzinnora) — unlikely + nullified → Business before the festival is similarly remote and insignificant
DISTINCTION Rava holds the Sages would NOT be lenient if the concern were a Torah violation — only rabbinic prohibitions allow such leniency.

Dangers of Improper Drinking

🚰 Don't drink directly from rivers/ponds with your mouth or one hand — danger of swallowing a leech (nima)
↓
🩺 R' Ḥanina: One who swallows a leech — it is pikuaḥ nefesh; may heat water on Shabbat
↓
🫗 Rav Huna b. R' Yehoshua: Meanwhile, swallow vinegar as first aid
↓
🐝 Rav Idi bar Avin: One who swallows a hornet will not survive — give sharp vinegar to extend life for final instructions
↓
🌙 Don't drink water at night — danger of the shavrirei spirit
↓
🗣️ Remedy: Wake another person and tell them 'I thirst,' or recite the incantation against the shavrirei
PRINCIPLE The Sages prioritized physical safety alongside spiritual concerns — life-threatening danger overrides Shabbat prohibitions.

Adorned vs. Non-Adorned Shops (Beit She'an)

Adorned Shops

  • Decorated for the sake of idol worship
  • Roses & myrtle → benefit from their smell (Reish Lakish)
  • These serve as offerings to idols
  • PROHIBITED to buy from them

Non-Adorned Shops

  • Not decorated for idol worship
  • Adorned with fruit → permitted (Reish Lakish)
  • No prohibited benefit derived
  • PERMITTED to buy from them
SOURCE Reish Lakish derives from Devarim 13:18 — 'Nothing dedicated shall cleave to your hand' — it is prohibited to derive benefit from items dedicated to idol worship.
