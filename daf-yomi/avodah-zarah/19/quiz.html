Avodah Zarah 19 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara, why is Abraham identified as the 'happy man' of Psalm 1:1?
A. He separated from the wicked of his generation at three levels: the Dispersion, Sodom, and the Philistines
B. He was the first to study Torah day and night
C. He built the Beit HaMikdash
D. He defeated the four kings in battle
Correct: A — The Gemara matches each clause — 'did not walk,' 'did not stand,' 'did not sit' — to Abraham's separation from the generation of the Dispersion, Sodom, and the Philistines respectively.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi derive from 'His delight is in the Torah of the Lord'?
A. One must study all areas of Torah equally
B. A person can learn Torah only from a subject his heart desires
C. Torah study must be done joyfully with song
D. One should study Torah only with a study partner
Correct: B — Rebbi teaches that 'his delight' means a person should follow his own desire in choosing what to study. He demonstrated this when Levi took permission to leave and study Proverbs instead of Psalms.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava, what does the shift from 'Torah of the Lord' to 'his Torah' in Psalm 1:2 teach?
A. That one may change the words of Torah
B. That through study, Torah is called by the student's name — he acquires ownership of it
C. That Torah belongs only to great scholars
D. That one should write his own Torah scroll
Correct: B — Rava teaches that initially Torah is attributed to God ('Torah of the Lord'), but through diligent study it becomes identified with the student ('in his Torah he meditates').
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the danger of a scholar who IS qualified to rule but refuses to issue decisions?
A. He will forget his learning
B. He prevents the public from learning Torah properly — 'a mighty host are all her slain'
C. He violates a positive commandment
D. He will lose his share in the World to Come
Correct: B — The Gemara interprets 'a mighty host are all her slain' as referring to a qualified scholar who withholds rulings, thereby depriving the community of proper Torah guidance.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When R' Alexandri proclaimed 'Who desires life?', what was his ultimate teaching?
A. That one should pray three times daily
B. That guarding one's tongue is sufficient for a good life
C. That avoiding evil is not enough — one must actively do good, meaning engage in Torah
D. That one should give charity generously
Correct: C — After citing 'Keep your tongue from evil,' R' Alexandri emphasizes 'Depart from evil AND do good' — teaching that passivity is insufficient. 'Good' is identified with Torah (Proverbs 4:2), requiring active engagement.
