↓
Did Not Walk

Did Not Stand
🧍 In the way of sinners — People of Sodom, 'wicked and sinners exceedingly'
Did Not Sit
🪑 In the seat of scorners — Philistines who mocked Shimshon for sport

"Happy is the Man" — What Does 'Man' Emphasize?

Gemara
Is it 'happy is the man' but not the woman? Why specify man?
Rav Amram / Rav
Happy is one who repents while still a man — while he is young and strong, before old age
R' Yehoshua b. Levi
Happy is one who overpowers his yetzer hara like a man — with strength and vigor
R' Elazar
"Delights in His mitzvot" — in the mitzvot themselves, not in their reward
Antigonus of Sokho
Do not serve the Master on condition of receiving reward. Serve without conditions! (Avot 1:3)
Key Idea True devotion means loving the mitzvot themselves — not performing them as a transaction for reward.

Rava's Four Teachings on Torah Study

Torah Ownership

  • Initially: 'Torah of the Lord' — called by God's name
  • Ultimately: 'His Torah' — called by the student's name
  • Through study, one acquires the Torah as his own

Breadth Before Depth

  • First: 'His delight is in Torah' — study broadly (ligeris)
  • Then: 'In his Torah he meditates' — analyze deeply
  • Study and review even if you forget or don't fully understand

Growth in Status

  • Initially: 'Upon the highest places' — no secure seat
  • Ultimately: 'On a seat in the high places' — a seat of honor
  • Initially private, ultimately teaching publicly

Study Leading to Action

  • 'Brings forth fruit in its season' — act on what you learn
  • If you act: 'His leaf will not wither'
  • If not: 'Like chaff the wind drives away'
Soul & Study "My soul breaks (garesa) for longing" — the soul is satisfied even with breaking apart material on a basic level, even without grinding it into fine analysis.

Follow Your Heart in Torah Study

אֵין אָדָם לוֹמֵד תּוֹרָה אֶלָּא מִמָּקוֹם שֶׁלִּבּוֹ חָפֵץ
Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi "A person can learn Torah only from a place that his heart desires."

When Levi and R' Shimon sat before Rebbi and finished their book, Levi wanted Proverbs and R' Shimon wanted Psalms. Rebbi chose Psalms. When they reached "His delight is in the Torah of the Lord," Rebbi taught this principle. Levi said: "My teacher — you have given me permission to leave!" He rose to go study Proverbs, the book his heart desired.

Dividing Study Time & When to Issue Rulings

TopicTeaching
Dividing Study
(R' Tanchum b. Chanilai)		Divide each day into thirds:
⅓ Mikra (Bible) · ⅓ Mishna · ⅓ Talmud
Premature Rulings
(Rav Huna / Rav)		A student not ready who issues decisions is like a miscarriage — 'she has cast down many wounded'
Withholding Rulings
(Rav Huna / Rav)		A scholar who is ready but refuses to rule — 'a mighty host are all her slain' — prevents the masses from learning
Minimum AgeA scholar is too immature to rule until age 40
Exception (Rava)If one's knowledge equals the leading scholar of his city, he may rule even before 40
Balance Both premature ruling and withholding rulings cause harm. The scholar must find the right time to teach.
