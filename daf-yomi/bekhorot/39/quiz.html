Bekhorot 39 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabba, the uppermost knot of tzitzit is required by:
A. Rabbinic decree only
B. Torah law
C. Custom (minhag) alone
D. Neither — it is entirely optional
Correct: B — Rabba proves the uppermost knot is Torah law: if it were only rabbinic, merely inserting the strings through the corner hole would satisfy the Torah requirement. Since a knot is obligatory, it must originate from the Torah itself.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
If a tzitzit string is severed at its very base — where it attaches to the garment — the tzitzit are:
A. Fit, since some string still extends
B. Fit only if the three remaining strings are extra-long
C. Unfit
D. Fit by Torah law but unfit rabbinically
Correct: C — Rav rules that a string severed at its base renders the tzitzit unfit. The baraita's phrase 'remainder of severed strings is fit at any length' applies only to strings cut mid-length — not those cut at the root.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What are the minimum and maximum permitted sets of windings (ḥulyot) for tzitzit?
A. 3 minimum, 7 maximum
B. 5 minimum, 10 maximum
C. 7 minimum, 13 maximum
D. 7 minimum, 39 maximum
Correct: C — The minimum is 7 sets, corresponding to the 7 firmaments, and the maximum is 13 sets, corresponding to the 7 firmaments plus the 5 additional higher heavens. Going below 7 or above 13 is invalid.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rav and Rabba bar bar Ḥana debate a cloak with tzitzit that are entirely wound. Rav's position is:
A. The tzitzit are valid and beautiful
B. The tzitzit are not beautiful because loose strings are required
C. The cloak itself disqualifies the tzitzit
D. All-wound tzitzit are only rabbinically invalid, not by Torah law
Correct: B — Rav holds that loose strings hanging beyond the windings are mandatory. He interprets 'twisted cords' (Deut. 22:12) as teaching the number of strings required — not as permitting all-wound fringes. Without loose strings, the tzitzit are not valid.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman (relying on a tanna of Rabbi Yishmael's school), garments made of silk are:
A. Fully obligated in tzitzit by Torah law like wool
B. Obligated in tzitzit by rabbinic law only
C. Entirely exempt from the tzitzit obligation
D. Obligated only when dyed with sky-blue tekhelet
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman holds that silk garments are entirely exempt from tzitzit, following the tanna of Rabbi Yishmael who derives from the Torah that only wool and linen qualify as 'garments' subject to the tzitzit obligation. Silk strings can only exempt garments of their own type.
