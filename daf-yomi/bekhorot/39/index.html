🧥 The All-Wound Sky-Blue Cloak

Rav
The cloak is beautiful — but the tzitzit are not beautiful. Loose strings hanging beyond the windings are required; this all-wound fringe is invalid.
Rabba bar bar Ḥana
The cloak is beautiful — and the tzitzit are beautiful too! The Torah uses both 'twisted cords' (Deut. 22:12) and 'fringes' (Num. 15:38), and having all windings is perfectly valid.
Root of Dispute Rabba bar bar Ḥana: The two verses together teach both elements are required yet either alone suffices. Rav: 'Twisted cords' (Deut. 22:12) merely specifies the number of strings needed — loose strings beyond windings remain mandatory.

🔵 The Uppermost Knot: Torah or Rabbinic?

קֶשֶׁר הָעֶלְיוֹן דְּאוֹרַיְיתָא — מנחות לט
Rabba's Proof Rabba teaches: The uppermost knot of tzitzit is required by Torah law.

Proof: If it were only rabbinic, then by Torah law it would suffice to merely insert the strings into the hole without tying any knot. Since a knot is required, it must be mandated by the Torah itself — and not merely by rabbinic enactment.

✂️ Severed Strings: When Are Tzitzit Unfit?

A tzitzit string is severed
↓
Severed at its base
❌ Unfit
Rav (cited by Rabba bar Rav Adda): Even a tiny remainder does not save it when cut at the attachment point
Severed in the middle (part remains)
✅ Fit
Baraita: 'Remainder of severed strings is fit at any length' — applies when some length persists beyond the cut
↓
Rava's objection resolved: the baraita teaches one law — only mid-string cuts are covered; base cuts are categorically unfit

🧵 Which Fabric Strings Exempt Which Garments?

Wool Strings

  • ✅ Exempt wool garments
  • ✅ Exempt linen garments
  • ✅ Exempt all other fabric garments
  • Sky-blue (tekhelet) must be wool — wool holds the highest status

Linen Strings

  • ✅ Exempt linen garments
  • ✅ Exempt wool garments (Raḥava/Rav Yehuda)
  • ✅ Wool+linen combined exempt any garment
  • Shatnez exception: normally forbidden, but Torah juxtaposes tzitzit with shatnez (Deut. 22:11–12)

Silk (Shira'in) Strings

  • ✅ Exempt silk garments of same type only
  • ❌ Do NOT exempt wool or linen garments
  • Rav Naḥman: silk garments are entirely exempt from tzitzit
  • Tanna d'vei R. Yishmael: only wool/linen are Torah-level 'garments'
