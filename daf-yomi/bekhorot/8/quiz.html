QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Elazar, what does it mean halachically when the Shabbat time to detach the shewbread 'arrives' even if the bread has not been physically removed?
A. The bread is considered broken and the frankincense cannot be burned
B. It is treated as if the bread was already detached — the frankincense may be burned
C. The priests must immediately remove the bread to avoid a violation
D. The frankincense is burned only if the bread is also intact
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar rules that the arrival of the designated time for detachment is legally equivalent to actual detachment. Once that moment passes, the frankincense is fully designated and may be burned even if the bread subsequently broke.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rav holds that a standard meal offering is NOT sanctified in halves. What does Rabbi Yochanan hold about half a tenth of an ephah set aside for a standard meal offering?
A. It is not sanctified under any circumstances
B. It is sanctified, even if the person intended to add more later
C. It is sanctified only if oil is added first
D. It depends on whether the Temple vessels were used
Correct: B — Rabbi Yochanan holds that half a tenth IS sanctified for a standard meal offering — he is more lenient than Rav on this point. This creates a tension: Rabbi Yochanan's general rule (from the griddle-cake) that meal offerings are not sanctified in halves applies differently to the griddle-cake than to ordinary meal offerings.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ḥanina, for what purpose was the vessel that measures one log of oil anointed as a Temple service vessel?
A. To sanctify the oil of standard meal offerings independently
B. To measure oil for wine libations
C. To sanctify the oil of the leper's offering — the one case oil is brought alone
D. To measure frankincense for the shewbread
Correct: C — Since Rabbi Ḥanina rules that oil cannot be sanctified independently for standard meal offerings, the log-measuring vessel must have been anointed for the leper's log of oil (Lev. 14:10) — the unique offering where oil alone is brought without flour, making it the one context where a vessel sanctifies oil by itself.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the Gemara's final resolution for why the verse (Lev. 2:2) specifying handful removal location was necessary?
A. To exclude removal performed inside the Sanctuary
B. To disqualify removal done with the left hand anywhere
C. To permit removal anywhere in the courtyard, not just near the altar
D. To require the priest to remove the handful while facing the entrance
Correct: C — One might have thought removal is valid only in the area near the altar, since Leviticus 2:8 implies the offering is brought to the altar. The verse teaches that removal of the handful is valid anywhere throughout the entire Temple courtyard.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When the Gemara proves that Rav derives halakhot by analogy, which three cases does it cite as evidence?
A. Blood offerings, wine libations, and incense
B. Shewbread (no oil), leper's log of oil (oil alone), and meal offering of a sinner (no frankincense)
C. Burnt offerings, sin offerings, and guilt offerings
D. Griddle-cake, omer offering, and two loaves of Shavuot
Correct: B — Rav's three derivations demonstrate cross-category reasoning: (1) meal offering sanctified without oil — from shewbread; (2) oil sanctified alone — from the leper's log; (3) meal offering sanctified without frankincense — from the sinner's meal offering. This proves Rav does derive by analogy, making his stance on the griddle-cake halachically significant and puzzling.
