🍞 Central Dispute: High Priest's Griddle-Cake (Chavitin)

Rabbi Yochanan
The griddle-cake offering (chavitin) is not sanctified in halves. A whole tenth of an ephah must be brought before it is divided into two halves.
Rabbi Elazar
Since the griddle-cake is sacrificed in halves — half in the morning, half in the evening — it can be sanctified in halves from the outset.
Gemara (Objection)
The Mishna (50b) states: the High Priest brings a full tenth and then divides it — implying halves alone are insufficient!
Rabbi Elazar (Reply)
The Mishna prescribes a whole tenth only l'chatchila (ab initio). B'dieved (after the fact), if half a tenth was brought in the morning it is valid.
Key Question Does Rabbi Yochanan's rule — meal offerings are not sanctified in halves — derive from the griddle-cake? Or is the griddle-cake itself an exception because it is designed to be split? And where does Rav fit between them?

🧂 When Is the Frankincense Burned? (Shewbread Scenario)

Shewbread sits on the Table; frankincense in bowls alongside it on Shabbat
↓
Bread broke before time to detach arrived
Frankincense is NOT burned — analogous to a meal offering that became lacking before removal of the handful: designation was never finalized
Bread broke after time to detach arrived
Frankincense IS burned — Rabbi Elazar: arrival of the time for detachment legally equals actual detachment
↓
Why the difference? Once detachment-time arrives, the frankincense is fully designated — parallel to the remainder of a meal offering after the handful is already removed and burned.

🏺 Are Meal Offering Components Sanctified Independently?

Rav
A meal offering is sanctified by a service vessel even without its oil. Proof: the shewbread has no oil yet is fully sanctified. Oil and frankincense are likewise each sanctified independently of the other.
Rabbi Ḥanina
Neither oil nor frankincense is sanctified alone. Any meal offering requiring both is sanctified by a service vessel only when flour, oil, and frankincense are placed in the vessel all together.
Shmuel
Agrees with Rav. Proof from Zevachim 88a: vessels for liquids sanctify only liquids, not dry goods — yet a blood-bowl can sanctify a dry meal offering, showing flour can be sanctified independently.
Rav Aḥa of Difti
Challenges Shmuel: a meal offering mixed with oil is itself a liquid! How does the verse prove dry goods are sanctified separately?
Resolution: relative to blood, a meal offering counts as a dry item.
Implication for Rabbi Ḥanina The vessel measuring a tenth (עשרון) was anointed to sanctify items; the log-measuring vessel was anointed specifically for the leper's oil offering — the only case where oil is brought alone and sanctified by itself.

📊 Positions on Sanctification in Halves

Rabbi Yochanan

  • Griddle-cake: NOT sanctified in halves
  • Derives rule to all meal offerings from the griddle-cake
  • If half a tenth is set aside, it IS sanctified (standard offerings)
  • Griddle-cake is the paradigm — its rule radiates outward

Rabbi Elazar

  • Griddle-cake: IS sanctified in halves (b'dieved)
  • Reason: it is sacrificed in halves → can be sanctified in halves
  • Derives meal offering from meal offering (e.g., shewbread)
  • Does NOT derive from blood — different halachic category

Rav

  • Standard meal offering: NOT sanctified in halves
  • Even if intent was to add more later (stricter than R. Yochanan)
  • Position on griddle-cake itself: unresolved — cannot consistently follow either authority
  • Does derive halakhot by analogy (proven from shewbread, leper's oil, sinner's offering)

🔄 Proving Rav Derives by Analogy — The Logical Chain

Question: Does Rav derive halakhot for consecrated items from analogous cases? This determines whether his ruling about halves is borrowed from the griddle-cake.
↓
Proof 1 — Flour without oil: Rav says a meal offering is sanctified by a vessel even without its oil. This is derived from shewbread, which has no oil yet is fully sanctified.
↓
Proof 2 — Oil alone: Rav says oil is sanctified independently, derived from the leper's log of oil, which is brought and sanctified without any flour or frankincense.
↓
Proof 3 — Without frankincense: A meal offering is sanctified without frankincense, derived from the meal offering of a sinner (מנחת חוטא), which contains neither oil nor frankincense.
↓
Conclusion: Rav clearly does derive by analogy. Therefore his ruling that standard meal offerings are NOT sanctified in halves must reflect his view of the griddle-cake — yet he cannot consistently align with either Rabbi Yochanan or Rabbi Elazar on all points.
↓
Unresolved: The Gemara leaves Rav's position on the griddle-cake itself as undecided (תיקו). His positions create an internal tension with both authorities.
