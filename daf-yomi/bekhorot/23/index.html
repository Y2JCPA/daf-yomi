⚖️ Two Central Disputes of Menachot 23

R' Yoḥanan
Oil added to the sinner's handful renders it unfit (pasul). The verse 'he shall put no oil upon it' applies to the handful stage as well.
Reish Lakish
The handful is fit. The verse's prohibition governs preparation of the offering — not what happens to the handful after removal.
Rav Ḥisda
In nullification, we follow the larger quantity (nullifying substance). If the majority can become like the minority, they are the same type and neither nullifies the other.
Rabbi Ḥanina
We follow the smaller quantity (nullified substance). If the minority can become like the majority, they are the same type and the minority is not nullified.
📖 Key Verse לֹא יָשִׂים עָלֶיהָ שֶׁמֶן — 'He shall put no oil upon it, neither shall he put any frankincense on it' (Leviticus 5:11) — governs the sinner's meal offering

🫗 Slide 1: Oil Added to the Sinner's Handful

R' Yoḥanan
Unfit. The verse absolutely forbids oil on the sinner's offering — adding oil to the handful after removal disqualifies it entirely.
Reish Lakish
Fit. The verse teaches only that the offering is not initially invalidated by oil. What befalls the handful afterward is a separate matter.
R' Yoḥanan
Objection from baraita: A dry meal offering intermingled with an oil-mixed offering — Rabbi Yehuda rules it unfit. Proof that oil contaminates!
Reish Lakish
Reinterpretation: That baraita discusses the bull/ram libation offering intermingled with the lamb libation offering — an entirely different case.
R' Yoḥanan
Second objection: Another baraita explicitly addresses bull/ram vs. lamb libation offerings as a separate halakha — so your reinterpretation is untenable.
Unresolved The Gemara leaves this dispute without final resolution — a teiku-style conclusion

🔥 Slide 2: Rava's Dilemma — Oil Squeezed First

The Case: Priest squeezes oil from handful onto altar wood first, then places the handful on the wood. What is the halakha?
↓
Core Question: Are substances contiguous to items on the altar considered legally part of those altar items?
↓
✅ Yes — Contiguous = Part of Item
Oil on the wood is still 'part of' the handful → handful ascends with its oil intact → valid offering
❌ No — Contiguous ≠ Part of Item
Oil was separated before handful ascended → handful offered without its oil → possibly invalid
↓
Related Dispute (R' Yoḥanan vs Reish Lakish): Does a bone contiguous to meat combine with it to complete an olive-bulk for liability when offered outside the Temple? R' Yoḥanan: Yes. Reish Lakish: No.
↓
Rav Ashi: Rava's dilemma stands regardless — the oil/handful scenario may differ from the bone/meat scenario even within each opinion. The dilemma remains unresolved.

⚖️ Slide 3: Rav Ḥisda vs Rabbi Ḥanina on Nullification

Rav Ḥisda

  • Follow the nullifying substance (the majority)
  • If the majority can become like the minority → same type → no nullification
  • Carcass meat in slaughtered majority → nullified ✓
    (slaughtered cannot become carcass)
  • Slaughtered meat in carcass majority → not nullified ✗
    (carcass can become slaughtered via future slaughter)
  • Supported by: mishna about intermingled handful and non-removed offering

Rabbi Ḥanina

  • Follow the nullified substance (the minority)
  • If the minority can become like the majority → same type → no nullification
  • Carcass meat in slaughtered majority → nullified ✓
    (carcass can potentially be slaughtered = same type)
  • Slaughtered meat in carcass majority → not nullified ✗
    (slaughtered cannot revert to carcass status)
  • Supported by: mishna about two non-removed offerings intermingled

📜 Slide 5: Rabbi Zeira's Gezerah Shavah (Verbal Analogy)

וְהִקְטִיר הַכֹּהֵן אֶת הַקֹּמֶץ — 'The priest shall burn the handful' (Lev. 2:2)
Torah Edict — Remainders Never Nullify Handfuls The Torah uses the word burning (הקטרה) for both the handful (kometz) and the remainder (shiyarei). Analogy: Just as when two handfuls are intermingled, neither nullifies the other and all agree both are burned on the altar — so too, when remainders are mixed with handfuls, the remainder does not nullify the handful, regardless of quantity. Practical ruling: Any mixture of handfuls and remainders is governed by this Torah-level edict — nullification simply does not apply. This resolves both the mishna about intermingled handfuls and the seemingly contradictory rulings about b'diavad validity. This principle is independent of the Rav Ḥisda / Rabbi Ḥanina debate — it operates on a separate Torah track.
