QUESTION 1 OF 5
By Torah law, who is liable for me'ila (misuse of consecrated property) regarding the red heifer?
A. One who derives benefit from its ashes after burning
B. One who derives benefit from the animal itself
C. One who sprinkles its water on an uncontaminated person
D. No one — red heifer is entirely exempt from me'ila
Correct: B — Rav Ashi clarifies: Torah me'ila liability attaches only to the animal itself. The prohibition on ashes was a temporary rabbinic decree, later revoked when people avoided sprinkling out of uncertainty about impurity.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why was the rabbinic decree forbidding benefit from red heifer ashes eventually revoked?
A. Kohanim refused to prepare the heifer under the stricter rules
B. The Sanhedrin ruled it contradicted a Torah-level ruling
C. People were refraining from sprinkling in cases of doubtful impurity
D. The ashes lost their sanctity after being mixed with water
Correct: C — The decree had an unintended consequence: people avoided sprinkling uncertain individuals, leaving them in potential impurity. To encourage proper use of the purification ritual, the decree was lifted.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Abba Yosei ben Dostai, how many handfuls of frankincense does the High Priest bring daily with his griddle-cake offering?
A. Half a handful total, divided between morning and afternoon
B. One complete handful, divided between morning and afternoon
C. Two handfuls — one complete handful per offering
D. Three handfuls — matching the three log of oil
Correct: C — Abba Yosei reasons: since there is no source in the Torah for sacrificing half a handful, the High Priest must bring a complete handful for each of the two daily chavitin offerings — one in the morning and one in the afternoon.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
When the High Priest dies and no replacement is yet appointed, what happens to his daily griddle-cake offering?
A. It is suspended entirely until a new High Priest is appointed
B. Half a tenth is brought once daily by the deputy High Priest
C. A complete tenth is brought twice daily — morning and afternoon
D. The offering is divided among all kohanim and not brought on the altar
Correct: C — A baraita cited by Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak explicitly resolves the dilemma: if the High Priest died without a replacement, a complete tenth of an ephah is brought twice each day, maintaining the full twice-daily schedule.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda in the new Mishna, what is the proper method for leavening the leavened meal offerings?
A. Separate leaven from within the same dough batch being used for the offering
B. Bring fresh yeast from the market and dissolve it into the flour
C. Bring aged leaven from a separate, older dough and add it to the measuring vessel first, then fill with flour
D. Leave the dough uncovered overnight and allow it to leaven naturally
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehuda holds that taking leaven from the same dough (Rabbi Meir's method) is not optimal. Instead, one should bring aged leaven from a different dough — aged leaven is a more effective leavening agent — place it in the vessel first, then add the flour to fill it.
