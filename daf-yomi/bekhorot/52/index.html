🔥 Me'ila on Red Heifer Ashes

Baraita
One who derives benefit from the ashes of the red heifer is not liable for me'ila — this is a Torah-level ruling.
Rav Ashi
Actually this is Torah law, but two rabbinic ordinances were enacted: (1) benefit from the animal itself renders one liable; (2) a decree was added to forbid benefit from its ashes as well.
Rav Ashi
Once they saw that people were refraining from sprinkling in cases of uncertain impurity due to this decree, it was revoked. Liability for ashes remains rabbinic only.
Gemara
Therefore the baraita is correct: by Torah law no me'ila for ashes — the ashes decree was a temporary rabbinic safeguard, later lifted for practical reasons.
Key Principle A rabbinic decree may be revoked when it causes people to avoid fulfilling a mitzva. The red heifer's ashes are Torah-exempt from me'ila; only the animal itself triggers Torah liability.

⚖️ Communal Sin Bull — Rabbi Shimon vs. Rabbi Yehuda

Rabbi Shimon

  • Concerned about negligence (פשיעה) in handling offerings
  • Heirs may act carelessly if consequences are deferred
  • Applies this concern broadly across different offering categories
  • His position appears as the first opinion in one baraita and the second in another

Rabbi Yehuda

  • Less concerned about negligence in the communal bull case
  • Distinguishes between categories where negligence is realistic
  • His position also appears reversed across the two baraitot
  • Gemara conclusion: unresolved — two contradictory baraitot remain

Rav Ashi's Attempted Resolution

  • When Rabbi Shimon expressed concern about negligence, that was only in a specific context
  • The negligence concern does not apply uniformly across all cases
  • Rabba Zuti cites a baraita about the daily sacrifice to try to resolve the dispute
  • Ultimately: the Gemara leaves the question teku (unresolved)

🍞 Griddle-Cake Offering When High Priest Dies

High Priest Dies
No replacement yet appointed (אין כהן גדול אחר תחתיו)
↓
Is the daily chavitin (griddle-cake, מנחת חביתין) still sacrificed twice daily?
↓
Rava's Proof (Tamid 31b)
9 priests carry limbs of daily offering; griddle-cake is among them → implies always brought regardless of HP status
Rabbi Yirmeya Rejects It
"Those foolish Babylonians!" — Tanna speaks only of the typical case, not hypotheticals like HP's death
↓
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak cites a Baraita:
Explicit ruling — if HP died without replacement, a complete tenth is brought twice daily (morning + afternoon)
↓
Rava responds to Rabbi Yirmeya's critique: The verse says "it shall be perpetually offered" (תמיד) — implying the offering continues even without a High Priest

🥖 New Mishna — Matza Rule & Leavened Exceptions

General Rule (מנחות כולן באות מצה): All meal offerings must be brought as matza (unleavened bread)
↓
Exception 1 — Thanks Offering (קרבן תודה): Ten of the forty loaves that accompany the thanks offering are leavened (חמץ)
↓
Exception 2 — Two Loaves (שתי הלחם): The Two Loaves brought on Shavuot (Pentecost) are explicitly required to be leavened
↓
Rabbi Meir on leavening method: One separates bodeh — takes leaven from within the same dough batch to leaven the offering
↓
Rabbi Yehuda disagrees: That is not the optimal method. Bring aged leaven from a separate dough, place it in the measuring vessel first, then fill with flour — aged leaven is more effective
↓
Gemara opens: Rabbi Perida asks Rabbi Ami — from where is the matza requirement for all meal offerings actually derived? Rabbi Ami is puzzled... (to be continued on 52b+)
