🐚 The Ḥilazon — Source of Tekhelet

גּוּפוֹ דּוֹמֶה לַיָּם, וּבְרִיָּתוֹ דּוֹמָה לְדָג, עוֹלֶה אַחַת לְשִׁבְעִים שָׁנָה
Characteristics (Baraita) The ḥilazon (חִלָּזוֹן) is the creature whose blood yields the precious sky-blue tekhelet thread for tzitzit:

• Its body resembles the sea
• Its form resembles a fish
• It emerges only once in seventy years
• With its blood one dyes the sky-blue thread
• Its rarity makes tekhelet extremely precious and expensive

🧵 The Man Saved by His Tzitzit — Reward in This World

A man diligent about tzitzit heard of a famous prostitute overseas charging 400 gold coins
↓
He sent the payment, traveled overseas, and arrived at her door; she admitted him
↓
She arranged seven beds for him — six of silver, one of gold — and sat naked on the highest bed
↓
As he climbed up to face her, his four tzitzit fringes slapped him in the face — he fell to the ground in awe
↓
She came down beside him; he explained: 'There is a mitzva called tzitzit — it is written that I am the Lord your God, who brought you out… to be your God'
↓
Deeply moved, she divided all her wealth: one-third to the government, one-third to the poor, one-third kept with the beds of gold and silver
↓
She came to Rabbi Ḥiyya's study hall, converted, and the same beds were arranged for her and the man in a permitted fashion

📿 Tefillin — Are Arm and Head Independent Obligations?

Mishna
Absence of phylacteries of the arm does not prevent one from wearing the head phylactery, and vice versa. Each fulfills its own mitzva independently.
Rav Ḥisda (initial)
This ruling applies only when one has both types. But if a person lacks one type, he should not wear either one alone — the two must accompany each other.
Students
Rav Ḥisda, do you still maintain that position? (It seems unduly strict — why forgo the one mitzva you can perform?)
Rav Ḥisda (retracted)
No — I retract my ruling. Should someone who cannot fulfill two mitzvot also fail to perform the one he possesses? He should certainly wear whichever phylactery he has!
Final Ruling Each phylactery is a fully independent mitzva. One who has only the arm or only the head phylactery should wear it and fulfill that mitzva.

🍷 Meal Offering vs. Libations — Which Comes First?

The Rabbis

  • Animal offering is sacrificed first
  • Then libations (wine) are poured immediately after
  • Then the meal offering is brought last
  • Source: 'an animal offering, and libations' (Numbers 29:18) — libations adjoin the animal
  • They interpret 'burnt offering, and meal offering' as independent, not sequential

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi

  • Animal offering is sacrificed first
  • Then the meal offering is burned on the altar
  • Then the libations (wine) are poured last
  • Source: 'a burnt offering, and a meal offering' — meal offering precedes libations
  • Meal offering accompanies the fire; libation is poured separately afterward
