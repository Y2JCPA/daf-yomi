📍 Where Exactly on the Doorpost?

Shmuel
Place the mezuza at the beginning of the upper third of the doorpost — it is a mitzva to position it there, and it must not be more than one handbreadth from the crossbeam.
Rav Huna
Raise the mezuza one handbreadth from the ground, or distance it one handbreadth from the crossbeam. The entire space between those two points is valid for placement.
Rav Huna bar Rav Natan
Shmuel follows Rabbi Yosei. "Beginning of the upper third" means the maximum distance from the top — do not go more than one handbreadth below the crossbeam. Both Shmuel and Rav Huna agree on the lower limit.
Resolution Shmuel sets the upper limit (no more than one handbreadth from crossbeam); Rav Huna permits the entire range above one handbreadth from the ground. They do not truly dispute — Shmuel specifies the ideal position within the valid range.

✅ Fit vs. ❌ Unfit Mezuza Placements

✅ Fit

  • Affixed vertically on the doorpost
  • Placed on the right side as one enters
  • Positioned within the upper two-thirds of doorpost height
  • In the handbreadth adjacent to the public domain (Rava)
  • Written on a single sheet
  • Carved into a reed tube on a reed-frame entrance

❌ Unfit

  • Written on two separate sheets
  • Affixed horizontally like a bolt (Rav)
  • Dug one handbreadth deep into the doorpost (Rav Yosef son of Rava)
  • Placed on the left side of the entrance
  • Positioned more than one handbreadth below the top crossbeam (per Shmuel)
  • Placed at a broken entrance lacking proper doorpost form (Rava)

👑 Rabbi Ḥanina's King Analogy

בא וראה שלא כמידת בשר ודם מידת הקדוש ברוך הוא
מידת בשר ודם vs. מידת הקב״ה Rabbi Ḥanina teaches: "Come and see that the attribute of flesh and blood is not like the attribute of the Holy One, Blessed be He."

A human king sits inside his palace while his servants guard him from outside.

But with the Holy One, Israel dwells inside their homes — and He stands guard outside, as symbolized by the mezuza upon the doorpost. The servant-king dynamic is reversed: God protects His people.

🔑 The Exilarch's House — A Practical Mezuza Lesson

The Exilarch built a new house and asked Rav Naḥman: "Affix mezuzot for me."
↓
Rav Naḥman replied: "First erect the doors" — I must see the hinge placement before I can determine which side is the right side for the mezuza.
↓
Principle established: The right side is always determined by the direction of entry into the room — the hinge-side is considered the right.
↓
Rava adds a further refinement: Place the mezuza in the handbreadth closest to the public domain — so one encounters its protection immediately upon entering.
↓
The deeper teaching (Rabbi Ḥanina): Every entry and exit, Israel passes the mezuza — a sign that God guards them from outside, unlike any earthly king.
