🍞 Menachot 48 — Loaves, Sheep, and Sacred Dilemmas

כבשי עצרת — The Shavuot sheep (Leviticus 23:19)
Core Questions When four loaves accompany two Shavuot sheep, which two become consecrated with inherent sanctity? Can extra loaves be redeemed inside the Temple courtyard? May the court ever instruct a priest to sin for a greater gain? And are Shavuot sheep that were slaughtered not for their own sake valid or disqualified? This daf wrestles with intentionality, consecration, and the limits of permissible sacrifice.
3 / 9

🏛️ Where Are the Extra Shavuot Loaves Redeemed?

Rav Ḥisda
The baraita IS in accordance with Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi — and one redeems the loaves inside the courtyard. This does not violate the prohibition of bringing non-sacred items into the Temple.
Ravina
But a baraita teaches explicitly: he may redeem them only outside the courtyard! This directly contradicts your claim that R. Yehuda HaNasi permits redemption inside!
Rav Ashi
That baraita follows Rabbi Elazar b. Rabbi Shimon — not Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi. According to R. Yehuda HaNasi, taking the loaves outside the courtyard would disqualify them — so redemption must happen inside.
The Underlying Dispute Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi: slaughter of the sheep consecrates two loaves with inherent sanctity. Rabbi Elazar b. R. Shimon: slaughter does NOT grant inherent sanctity — all loaves remain in instrumental sanctity only.

⚠️ 'Arise and Sin In Order to Gain' — Is This Ever Permitted?

Rabbi Ḥanina Tirata
If four sheep were slaughtered with two loaves, the priest should sprinkle the blood of ALL four not for their own sake — this preserves the fit status of the extra two sheep.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Does the court say: 'Arise and sin in order that you may gain'?! Is it proper to instruct invalid sprinkling so that extra sheep remain fit?
Rabbi Ḥanina Tirata
Yes — we do say 'arise and sin' when the sin and the gain concern the same type of offering. Here: sheep for sheep.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Even granting that — you cannot say 'sin on Shabbat to gain on a weekday!' The sin and gain must exist in the same time domain.
Rabbi Ḥanina Tirata
Agreed: we say 'arise and sin on Shabbat to gain on that same Shabbat' — but NOT to gain on a later weekday.
Resulting Rule 'Arise and sin to gain' is permitted only when (1) the sin and gain concern the same type of offering, AND (2) the sin and the gain occur in the same time domain.

🐑 Shavuot Sheep Slaughtered Not For Their Own Sake — Three Views

Rav Yitzḥak

  • Derives Shavuot sheep from sin offering (juxtaposed in Leviticus 23:19)
  • Result: Slaughtering not for their own sake → DISQUALIFIED
  • Just as a sin offering slaughtered not for its own sake is disqualified

Levi / Rav Naḥman

  • Derives Shavuot sheep from voluntary peace offering
  • Result: Slaughtering not for their own sake → VALID
  • Valid as offerings, but do NOT fulfill the owner's obligation to bring them
  • Analogy: nazirite's other peace offerings slaughtered improperly are valid but non-obligatory

Rav Shimi bar Ashi

  • Offers a meta-principle for derivation
  • Derive an item prepared in an invalid manner from another item prepared in an invalid manner
  • Do NOT derive from items that are normally valid
  • This limits the scope of Levi's analogy
