Bekhorot 59 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Which meal offering requires frankincense but NOT oil?
A. The omer meal offering
B. The shewbread
C. The meal offering of priests
D. The two loaves of Shavuot
Correct: B — The shewbread is placed on the Table inside the Sanctuary and requires frankincense (in two gold bowls alongside the loaves) but no oil. The omer requires both oil and frankincense.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why is the meal offering of priests included in the oil requirement rather than the shewbread, according to the baraita's final answer?
A. Because the meal of priests is more important than the shewbread
B. Because 'Anyone' (Lev. 2:1) refers to individual offerings, and the meal of priests is individual while shewbread is communal
C. Because the meal of priests is eaten by priests, just like the omer
D. Because the shewbread is placed inside the Sanctuary, not on the outer altar
Correct: B — The decisive verse is 'Anyone' (Lev. 2:1), which refers to individual meal offerings. The meal of priests is brought by an individual (the High Priest), while the shewbread is a communal offering — so 'Anyone' includes the former and excludes the latter.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If oil is placed on the meal offering of a sinner, what is the halakha?
A. The offering is permanently disqualified and cannot be remedied
B. The oil must be removed and the offering remains valid
C. The offering is valid but the one who added oil is flogged
D. The priest removes the handful and the oil is disregarded
Correct: A — Adding oil permanently disqualifies the sinner's meal offering. Unlike frankincense — which can be gathered and removed — oil cannot be extracted from the flour. The Torah's prohibition implies disqualification with no remedy.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rav Ashi, why does improper intent (piggul) take effect on a sinner's meal offering that has intact frankincense on it?
A. Because frankincense is considered part of the meal offering for piggul purposes
B. Because any offering called 'a sin offering' is always susceptible to piggul
C. Because the frankincense can be gathered and removed, the offering is not rejected, and therefore remains susceptible to improper intent
D. Because piggul applies to all meal offerings regardless of their condition
Correct: C — Rav Ashi's principle: 'Any matter within one's power to remedy is not deemed rejected.' Since intact frankincense CAN be removed, the offering is not halachically rejected — it retains its status as an offering and therefore remains susceptible to disqualifying improper intent.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Which of the following is a point of similarity between the two loaves (Shavuot) and the omer, used to argue that the two loaves should require oil and frankincense?
A. Both are entirely burned on the altar
B. Both are consecrated in a service vessel
C. Both are eaten by priests and both are subject to the law of piggul
D. Both come as matza (unleavened bread)
Correct: C — The Gemara argues that the two loaves resemble the omer because both are eaten by priests and both are subject to piggul (improper sacrificial intent). However, the 'Anyone' verse ultimately prevails: it includes the meal of priests (individual) and not the two loaves (communal).
