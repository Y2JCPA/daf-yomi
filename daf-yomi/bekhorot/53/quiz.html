QUESTION 1 OF 5
Rabbi Ami initially cites which verse to prove that meal offerings must be matza and that this is indispensable?
A. Leviticus 2:5 — 'It shall be of matza'
B. Leviticus 6:7 — 'The sons of Aaron shall sacrifice it before the Lord'
C. Leviticus 6:10 — 'It shall not be baked as leavened bread'
D. Deuteronomy 16:3 — 'You shall eat no leavened bread with it'
Correct: C — Rabbi Ami cites Lev. 6:10 ('It shall not be baked as leavened bread'), but this verse faces objections from Rav Ḥisda, Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak, and Ravina. The final proof comes from Lev. 2:5.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to the Gemara's resolution, how many halakhot does the word 'tehiye' (it shall be) in Leviticus 2:5 teach?
A. One: that matza is indispensable
B. Two: that matza is indispensable AND that dough must be kneaded with lukewarm water
C. Three: indispensability, lukewarm water, and that boiling is forbidden
D. One: that dough must be kneaded with cold water to prevent early leavening
Correct: B — The Gemara reads tehiye as haḥaye — 'preserve it as matza.' Had there been only one teaching, the verse would have written matza without tehiye. The extra word yields two halakhot: indispensability and lukewarm-water kneading.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In Rabbi Ezra's yadid homily, the phrase 'in the portion of yadid' refers to which tribe?
A. Judah, since David and Solomon were of that tribe
B. Levi, since the priests served in the Temple
C. Benjamin, in whose territory the Temple was built
D. Ephraim, because of Joseph's special love from Jacob
Correct: C — Moses blessed Benjamin: 'The beloved [yedid] of the Lord shall dwell safely by Him' (Deut. 33:12). The Temple was built in Benjamin's territorial portion, making Benjamin the yadid of this phrase.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What did the divine voice say to comfort Abraham after all his arguments defending Israel were refuted?
A. That the exile would last only seventy years due to the merit of circumcision
B. That Israel, like a leafy olive tree, would never be lost in this world or the next
C. That the Temple would be rebuilt in the days of Ezra and Nehemiah
D. That the merit of Isaac's binding would protect the people from total destruction
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi interprets 'The Lord called your name a leafy olive tree' (Jer. 11:16): just as olive leaves never fall in summer or winter, Israel will never cease to exist in this world or the World to Come.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda, why is stiff leavening dough (from elsewhere) preferable to soft leavening dough when preparing a meal offering?
A. Stiff dough mixes more evenly and produces a better texture in the offering
B. When stiff dough is measured with the fine flour, the tenth of an ephah is complete — stiff dough has small volume relative to its flour content
C. Soft dough is considered full chametz and automatically invalidates the offering
D. Stiff dough leavens more slowly, giving the priest more time to prepare
Correct: B — Rav Ḥisda explains that stiff dough was made with little water and is dense — small in volume. When added to the fine flour and measured together, the required tenth of an ephah is met. Soft dough (more water, larger volume) risks displacing fine flour, leaving the measurement short.
