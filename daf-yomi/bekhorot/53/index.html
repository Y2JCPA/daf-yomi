📜 The Matza Requirement: Seeking an Airtight Proof

Starting point: All agree meal offerings must be matza (unleavened). But from where do we derive this is indispensable (me'akev)?
↓
Rabbi Ami cites Lev. 6:10: 'It shall not be baked as leavened bread.' He reads this as a complete prohibition rendering the offering invalid.
↓
Three objections: Rav Ḥisda (siur/slight leavening may be allowed), Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak (boiling may be allowed), Ravina (perhaps only a prohibition, not me'akev).
↓
Resolution — Lev. 2:5: 'It shall be [tehiye] of matza' — read as 'preserve [haḥaye] it as matza' — proves matza is indispensable.
↓
Bonus derivation: The same word tehiye also teaches that meal offering dough must be kneaded with lukewarm water so it bakes well.

⚖️ Four Challenges to Rabbi Ami's Proof (Lev. 6:10)

Rabbi Ami
'It shall not be baked as leavened bread' (Lev. 6:10) — this proves that a meal offering must be matza, and the requirement is indispensable.
Rav Ḥisda
Objection: Perhaps only full chametz is forbidden, but siur (slightly leavened dough) is permitted — chametz and siur are distinct categories!
Rav Ḥisda (rebuttal)
Even if siur is your reading, Rabbi Meir holds siur IS chametz-like on Pesaḥ — so 'not baked as leavened' would still forbid siur. The objection fails.
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
Objection: Perhaps boiling a meal offering is permitted — 'not baked as leavened' only prohibits baked-leavened, not boiled offerings.
Ravina
Objection: Perhaps the verse establishes only a prohibition (lav) for bringing leavened, but does not render the offering retroactively invalid (me'akev).
Gemara's Conclusion All three objections stand. Lev. 6:10 alone cannot prove indispensability. A new source is needed.

🔑 Deriving the Matza Requirement from 'Tehiye'

Question: From where do we derive that all meal offerings must be matza — and that this requirement is indispensable?
↓
Leviticus 2:5: 'It shall be [tehiye] of matza' — read as haḥaye: preserve the matza status
↓
Halakha 1
Matza is indispensable
A leavened meal offering is invalid — derived from 'preserve it as matza'
Halakha 2
Knead with lukewarm water
Preserve the matza state by enabling good baking — not cold water, not boiling water
↓
One word (tehiye) yields two distinct halakhot — the verse would have been superfluous if it taught only one

💛 Rabbi Ezra's Yadid (Beloved) Homily — Five Meanings

yadid #1 — The Builder

  • = King Solomon
  • God named him Yedidya
  • II Samuel 12:25
  • "Let yadid come..."

yadid #2 — His Ancestor

  • = Abraham
  • "What has My beloved [lididi]..." (Jer. 11:15)
  • Beloved of God
  • "...son of yadid..."

yadid #3 — The Structure

  • = The Temple
  • "I love the habitation of Your house" (Ps. 26:8)
  • God's beloved dwelling
  • "...build yadid..."

yadid #4 — The Territory

  • = Tribe of Benjamin
  • "The beloved of the Lord shall dwell safely" (Deut. 33:12)
  • Temple built in Benjamin's portion
  • "...in the portion of yadid..."

yedidim — The Beneficiaries

  • = Israel
  • The people achieve atonement through the Temple
  • "...let yedidim atone through it"
  • Purpose of the whole structure
