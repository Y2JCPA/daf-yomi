📿 Tefillin: Donning, Duration & Boundaries

1. Don arm tefillin (שֶׁל יָד) first — recite blessing over it
↓
2. Do not speak between arm and head tefillin — speech is a hefsek (interruption)
↓
3. Don head tefillin (שֶׁל רֹאשׁ) — one blessing if silent, two blessings if spoke
↓
4. Wear through the day and touch them regularly — until sunset / traffic ceases
↓
5. Remove head tefillin first, then arm — reverse of donning order

📢 Speaking Between Tefillin: How Many Blessings?

Rav Ḥisda
If one spoke between donning the arm tefillin and the head tefillin, he must recite a new blessing when donning the head tefillin. Speech creates an interruption.
Rav Ḥiyya b. Rav Huna
Rabbi Yoḥanan sent a ruling: one always recites a blessing over the head tefillin — implying even without speech, a separate blessing is recited.
Abaye & Rava
Resolution: If he did NOT speak — one blessing covers both tefillin. If he DID speak — two blessings: one for the arm, one for the head.
⚖️ Halakha Speech between arm and head tefillin is a hefsek requiring a second blessing for the head tefillin. Silence = one blessing; speech = two blessings.

🔄 The Proper Order of Donning and Removing Tefillin

DONNING — Arm First: "And it shall be a sign on your arm… and frontlets between your eyes" — arm precedes head in Torah verse
↓
Don arm tefillin (שֶׁל יָד), recite blessing, then don head tefillin (שֶׁל רֹאשׁ)
↓
REMOVING — Head First: So that arm tefillin remains on longest, maximizing the mitzva
↓
Source: Rabba citing Rav Huna — derived from verse sequence logic; the arm tefillin should never be present without the head tefillin
↓
Baraita confirms: One who speaks between donning arm and head tefillin has sinned — this sin can exempt him from military service (cf. Deut. 20)

🌅 Until When Does One Wear Tefillin? Three Views

Until Traffic Ceases

  • Rabbi Ya'akov: until people stop walking in the marketplace
  • Understood as just before nightfall / sunset
  • Rav Naḥman rules the halakha follows Rabbi Ya'akov
  • If removed for bathroom — no new blessing needed upon return

Until Sunset

  • Implied by Rav Ḥisda and Rabba bar Rav Huna who prayed the evening prayer with tefillin
  • This view disagrees with Rav Naḥman's ruling
  • Reflects opinion that tefillin apply into twilight zone

Until Sleep

  • The Rabbis: until the time of sleep (latest opinion)
  • Rabbis concede to R. Ya'akov on bathroom exception
  • Most expansive interpretation of wearing time

🌙 Night, Shabbat, and the Tefillin Mitzva

Does the mitzva of tefillin apply at night?
↓
YES — Night is a time for tefillin
Then Shabbat should also require tefillin!
(Rabba bar Rav Huna prayed evening prayer with tefillin)
NO — Night is NOT a time
Twilight uncertainty: neither don nor remove tefillin
(Rabba bar Rav Huna: safest approach at bein hashmashot)
↓
Shabbat is always exempt from tefillin
Source A: "miyamim yamimah" — days but not nights; days but not Shabbat
Source B (Rabbi Akiva): Tefillin are a "sign" — Shabbat itself is a sign, so tefillin are superfluous
