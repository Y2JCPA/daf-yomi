Bekhorot 31 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav (cited by Rav Ze'eira and Rav Ḥananel), when may a torn Torah scroll be sewn to render it fit?
A. When the tear extends into one line
B. When the tear extends into two lines
C. When the tear extends into three lines
D. Whenever the parchment is new, regardless of tear length
Correct: B — Rav rules that a tear extending into two lines may be sewn to restore fitness, but a tear into three lines cannot be repaired by sewing.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the Gemara mean when it labels parchment as 'old' vs. 'new' in the context of Torah scroll repair?
A. Age of the scroll — over 10 years old vs. newly written
B. Era of production — pre-Temple vs. post-Temple parchment
C. Processing method — ungalled is 'old'; gall-processed is 'new'
D. Animal source — ox hide is 'old'; deer hide is 'new'
Correct: C — The Gemara clarifies that 'old' and 'new' refer not to age but to processing: parchment not treated with gall is 'old' (cannot be sewn); parchment treated with gall is 'new' (may be sewn).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What did Rav Ḥisda rule regarding the final words of a mezuza?
A. The final line must repeat the opening verse of Shema
B. The final line must be left blank as a decorative border
C. The word 'eḥad' must always appear alone on the last line
D. The words 'al ha'aretz' must be written by themselves on the final line
Correct: D — Rav Ḥisda rules that the last two words of a mezuza — 'al ha'aretz' — must be written alone on the final line, separate from the preceding word.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How did Rabbi Tarfon resolve Rabbi Shimon Shezuri's problem of untithed produce mixed with a tithed majority?
A. The entire mixture must be destroyed to avoid a Torah violation
B. Separate tithes directly from the mixture, as the minority is nullified
C. Use proxy produce (e.g., from a gentile) from which to separate tithes on behalf of the mixture
D. The mixture is fully permitted since the untithed produce is automatically nullified by rabbinic decree
Correct: C — Rabbi Tarfon advised using gentile produce as a proxy: separate tithes from it on behalf of the mixed tevel. By Torah law the tevel is nullified, but rabbinic law requires this indirect remedy.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ḥelbo's testimony, how did Rav Huna roll a written mezuza?
A. From shema toward eḥad (right to left), so eḥad faces outward
B. From eḥad toward shema (left to right), so shema appears first when unrolled
C. Folded lengthwise with the text facing inward for protection
D. Rolled from bottom to top so the final words are visible on the outside
Correct: B — Rabbi Ḥelbo saw Rav Huna roll the mezuza from eḥad toward shema — left to right — so that when unrolled, the opening word Shema appears first, matching the reading order of the text.
