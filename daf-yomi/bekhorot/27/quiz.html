Bekhorot 27 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
What is the Gemara's source for the rule that the minority of the handful prevents the majority from rendering the meal offering permitted?
A. The word 'his handful' (קֻמְצוֹ) appears twice in the Torah
B. The handful is juxtaposed with frankincense in a single verse
C. The term חֻקָּה (statute) appears in the context of the handful
D. A verbal analogy (גְּזֵרָה שָׁוָה) from the hyssop bundle
Correct: A — The Gemara derives indispensability from the double use of 'his handful' — once for the voluntary meal offering and once for the sinner's meal offering — teaching that the handful is an indivisible unit where each part is necessary.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda, why must the four lulav species be physically bound together?
A. The term חֻקָּה appears with regard to the lulav commandment
B. A verbal analogy (גְּזֵרָה שָׁוָה) links לְקִיחָה of lulav to לְקִיחָה of hyssop at Pesach
C. The four species are explicitly juxtaposed in a single verse as a single unit
D. The lulav is compared to the nazirite's bundle of offerings
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda applies a גְּזֵרָה שָׁוָה: the same word לְקִיחָה (taking) used for the lulav also appears for the hyssop bundle used at Pesach. Since the hyssop must be taken as an אֲגֻדָּה (bundle), so too the four species must be bound together.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve the apparent contradiction between two baraitot about red heifer sprinklings performed not precisely toward the Tent of Meeting entrance?
A. One baraita follows Rabbi Yehuda, the other follows the Rabbis
B. One refers to sprinklings slightly off-direction; the other to sprinklings in a completely wrong direction
C. One baraita discusses the red heifer; the other discusses Yom Kippur inner sprinklings
D. One baraita is rejected as a lone opinion not accepted as halakha
Correct: B — The Gemara resolves the contradiction by distinguishing degree of deviation: sprinklings that are slightly off from the entrance remain valid (one baraita), while sprinklings performed in a completely wrong direction are invalid (the other baraita).
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to the Rabbis (disagreeing with Rabbi Yehuda), what are the consequences of an unauthorized person entering the Sanctuary (Heikhal) vs. the Holy of Holies?
A. Both are punishable by death at the hand of Heaven
B. Entering the Sanctuary = lashes; entering the Holy of Holies = death at Heaven's hand
C. Entering the Sanctuary = no punishment; entering the Holy of Holies = lashes
D. Both areas are subject only to lashes, not death at Heaven's hand
Correct: B — The Rabbis read Leviticus 16:2 as prohibiting both: 'the holy place' (Sanctuary/Heikhal) and 'within the Curtain' (Holy of Holies). Unauthorized entry into the Sanctuary carries lashes; unauthorized entry into the Holy of Holies carries death at the hand of Heaven.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rav Ḥanan bar Rava limits the mishna's rule about the four lulav species being indispensable to each other. When does this limitation apply?
A. Only when the lulav species are not bound together into a bundle
B. Only when one does not possess all four species — if one has all four, the rule of indispensability may not apply
C. Only on the first day of Sukkot; on subsequent days any three species suffice
D. Only in the Temple; in the diaspora each species can be taken independently
Correct: B — Rav Ḥanan bar Rava teaches that the mishna's ruling — that each of the four species prevents fulfillment of the mitzva with the others — applies specifically when one lacks some species. When one has all four, the question of indispensability (and binding) becomes subject to the tannaitic dispute between Rabbi Yehuda and the Rabbis.
