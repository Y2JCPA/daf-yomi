🌊 Rav vs. the Gemara: All vs. Part of Remainder Becoming Impure

Rav
Rabbi Yehoshua's ruling applies only when all of the remainder (שְׁיָרַיִם) became impure. If only part of the remainder became impure, the meal offering is still fit — and we sacrifice the remaining portion.
Gemara
Does Rav then hold that if only part was lost or burned, the offering is unfit? What is the relevant difference between impurity and loss/burning?
Rav (resolved)
In all cases — impurity, loss, or burning of part — the offering remains fit. What remains is still significant, and we bring up that portion. Partial impurity does not differ from partial loss.
Rava
Rabbi Yehoshua teaches: for any offering, if an olive-bulk (כְּזַיִת) of meat fit to eat or an olive-bulk of fat fit to offer remains, we sprinkle the blood. This is the minimum threshold.
Core Principle Partial impurity or partial loss of the remainder does not invalidate the meal offering — the surviving portion retains its significance and is sacrificed.

🏺 Handful Sanctification: Must It Be Placed in a Service Vessel?

Rabbis + Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak

  • The handful must be sanctified in a service vessel (כְּלִי שָׁרֵת) before burning on the altar
  • Handful removed without a service vessel → offering is unfit
  • If transferred to the left hand, it is rendered permanently unfit — cannot be corrected by returning to the right
  • Rav Naḥman holds this is unanimous — but the Gemara conclusively refutes him from two baraitot

Rabbi Shimon (and Rabbi Elazar)

  • The handful is fit even without sanctification in a service vessel
  • Lev. 6:10: 'most holy, as the sin offering' (קֹדֶשׁ קָדָשִׁים כַּחַטָּאת) — comparison teaches leniency on vessel requirement
  • Once sanctified in a vessel, it may be burned even if subsequently held in the left hand or a garment
  • Rabbi Yannai: no restrictions on manner of bringing up after initial removal from the vessel

🔥 Handful Burned in Two Increments — Is It Valid?

Mishna: If the priest burned the handful (קֹמֶץ) in two increments, the offering is fit.
↓
Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi: This mishna refers only where each increment was at least an olive-bulk (כְּזַיִת) — e.g., two half-portions each equal to an olive-bulk.
↓
Opposing view: Even if each increment was less than an olive-bulk, the offering is fit so long as both burnings together constitute the full handful.
↓
Rabbi Zeira's analysis: The dispute turns on two questions: (1) Does a handful smaller than two olive-bulks have legal significance? (2) Does burning less than an olive-bulk on the altar count halakhically?
↓
Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi's reasoning: Less than an olive-bulk on the altar has no significance (אֵין בּוֹ מַמָּשׁ) — so each of the two burnings must individually meet the olive-bulk minimum.
↓
Practical rule: Two valid burnings of a handful = fit. Each burning must constitute a halakhically meaningful act, i.e., at least an olive-bulk consumed.

⏱️ When Does Burning the Handful Permit the Priests to Eat the Remainder?

Rabbi Ḥanina
The remainder (שְׁיָרַיִם) is permitted for priestly consumption from the moment the fire takes hold (מִשֶּׁתֹּאחֵז הָאֵשׁ) — i.e., when the handful ignites on the altar.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
The remainder is not permitted until the majority (רֻבּוֹ) of the handful has been consumed by the fire. Mere ignition is insufficient.
Rav Yehuda (for Rabbi Yoḥanan)
Proof from Gen. 19:28: 'The smoke of the land went up as the smoke of a furnace' — a furnace produces smoke only when the majority of its fuel is consumed. So too, the handful must be mostly burned.
Ravin bar Rav Adda
A baraita asks: from when may items usually sacrificed during the day (like the handful, incense, priest's meal offering) be burned after sunset? This question itself implies something about timing of majority consumption.
Practical Difference Rabbi Ḥanina: priests may eat remainder the moment the handful ignites. Rabbi Yoḥanan: must wait until majority is consumed by fire. A handful set alight at sunset — can majority be consumed before nightfall? (Ravin bar Rav Adda's challenge)
