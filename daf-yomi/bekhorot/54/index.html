⚖️ The Central Dispute: How Do We Measure Changing Foods?

Rav / R. Ḥiyya / R. Yoḥanan
Measure food as it currently is — its present, post-cooking or post-mixing volume is the halakhic baseline. What you see now is what counts.
Shmuel / Reish Lakish / R. Shimon
Measure food as it was initially — its original volume before transformation is the legal identity. The item's starting state never loses its legal force.
Why It Matters This single dispute radiates outward: it governs when meal offerings are measured, whether shrunken meat is still ritually impure, and whether dried figs can cover teruma obligations for fresh ones.

🍎 Does Apple Juice Properly Leaven Meal Offerings?

R. Ḥanina ben Gamliel
Permitted and valid. Apple juice (מי תפוחים) does cause proper leavening — one may leaven meal offerings with it, and such leavening is halakhically significant.
Rabbanan
Problematic. Apple juice does not leaven properly. Meal offerings leavened this way are considered defective — the leavening is inadequate for offering purposes.
Mishna Link (Terumot 10:2) Apple of teruma is mashed into non-sacred dough; the juice leavens the dough — the dough is forbidden. The Gemara asks: whose opinion does this follow? It fits R. Ḥanina ben Gamliel, who holds apple juice truly leavens. If it did not truly leaven, why would the teruma-juice render the dough forbidden?

📏 When Must Flour for a Meal Offering Be Measured?

Flour is designated for a meal offering
Must be precisely one-tenth of an ephah (עשרון)
↓
Rabba & Rav Yosef
Measure the flour before adding water — while still dry. 'Dry' (חרבה) in the verse defines the baseline state.
Rabbi Ila
You must measure each and every meal offering — implying measurement in its current, mixed state after water is added.
↓
Rav Yitzḥak bar Avdimi: The word חרבה ('dry') in Scripture teaches that meal offerings are defined and measured in their pre-water, dry state — not after mixing

🔄 The Two Camps: Current vs. Initial Measurement

Measure As They Are Now

  • Rav, Rabbi Ḥiyya, Rabbi Yoḥanan
  • Cooked meat → measure its shrunken size
  • Mixed dough → measure the mixed volume
  • Dried figs → measure the dried (smaller) volume
  • Straightforward: the item before you is what the law addresses

Measure As They Were Initially

  • Shmuel, R. Shimon bar R. Yehuda HaNasi, Reish Lakish
  • Cooked meat → measure its pre-cooking size
  • Mixed dough → measure the pre-water flour
  • Fresh figs later dried → measured at their fresh volume
  • Principled: legal identity is fixed at origin, not altered by physical change

🥩 Shrunken Meat: From Torah Law to Rabbinic Stringency

Calf meat initially has requisite volume (כביצה) for contracting ritual impurity — and it does become impure
↓
The meat is cooked and shrinks below the minimum volume threshold
↓
By Torah law (per Shmuel/Reish Lakish): shrunken meat is no longer susceptible to new impurity — current volume is below minimum
↓
Rabbinic decree: The Sages nevertheless declare it impure — lest people assume shrunken food always escapes impurity laws
↓
Same logic extends to piggul and notar: the Gemara clarifies the baraita means only the ritual impurity these impart is governed rabbinically — not the prohibitions themselves on shrunken portions
