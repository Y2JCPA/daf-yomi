⚖️ The Great Shavuot Dependency Debate

Rabbi Akiva
Failure to bring the two loaves prevents sacrifice of the two sheep — but failure to sacrifice the two sheep does not prevent bringing the loaves. The loaves are primary.
R. Shimon ben Nannas
The opposite! Failure to sacrifice the two sheep prevents the bringing of the loaves — but failure to bring the loaves does not prevent sacrifice of the sheep. The sheep are primary.
Rabbi Shimon
I agree with Rabbi Shimon ben Nannas, but for a different reason: the sheep permit themselves through blood sprinkling — they are valid for sacrifice independent of any loaves.
Halakha The ruling follows Rabbi Shimon ben Nannas: the sheep may always be sacrificed (they are self-permitting), but the two loaves depend on the presence of the two sheep.

🌑 New Moon Offerings: Ezekiel's 'Fewer Animals' Rule

Torah (Numbers 28:11) requires: 2 young bulls + 7 lambs for the New Moon offering
↓
Ezekiel 46:6 states only 1 bull and 6 lambs — does this contradict the Torah?
↓
🐂 Bulls
If only 1 bull is available, bring 1. Each bull is independent — missing one does not prevent the other.
🐑 Lambs
If fewer than 7 lambs are available, bring as many as possible — even 1. Ezekiel's '6' signals every lamb is independently valid.
↓
Principle: Ezekiel's reduced numbers teach permissibility of partial fulfillment — they do not prescribe a new standard, they reveal flexibility in the original law.

📜 Ḥanina ben Ḥizkiyya: The Man Who Saved Ezekiel

זָכוּר אוֹתוֹ הָאִישׁ לַטּוֹב וַחֲנַנְיָה בֶּן חִזְקִיָּה שְׁמוֹ
Historical Note The Sages sought to suppress [לִגְנֹז] the book of Ezekiel because its passages seemed to contradict the Torah. Ḥanina ben Ḥizkiyya ascended to his attic carrying 300 barrels of oil for his lamp, and sat until he had resolved every last difficult verse.

Three categories of Ezekiel's hard verses were discussed on this daf:
• New Moon offering quantities (Ezek. 46:6)
• The first-month purification bull (Ezek. 45:18)
• The seventh-day atonement (Ezek. 45:20)

"That man is remembered for good — were it not for him, the book of Ezekiel would have been hidden away." — Rav Yehuda citing Rav

🌾 Shavuot Offerings: Numbers vs. Leviticus

Numbers 28:27 — Mussaf (Additional Offering)

  • 2 young bulls
  • 1 ram
  • 7 male lambs
  • 1 goat (sin offering)
  • Sacrificed in the wilderness — before loaves were possible
  • Independent of the two loaves entirely
  • These prove loaves and some offerings are separable

Leviticus 23:18–20 — Two Loaves Offering

  • 1 bull, 2 rams, 7 lambs (burnt offerings)
  • 1 goat (sin offering)
  • 2 lambs — the peace offering (subject of the debate!)
  • Loaves and lambs wave-offered together
  • Not sacrificed in the wilderness
  • The 2 lambs are 'waved with the bread of first fruits'

🔗 Verbal Analogy Wars: Who Derives What from 'Yihyu'?

Rabbi Akiva
I derive from tihyena (Lev. 23:17 — non-identical term, 'they shall be' applied to loaves) that the loaves are the essential element. Therefore missing loaves prevents the sheep. I prefer a free (uncontested) analogy even if the words differ slightly.
R. Shimon ben Nannas
I derive from the identical term yihyu (Lev. 23:20 — 'they shall be holy' applied to sheep). Since my analogy uses identical words, it overrides Rabbi Akiva's non-identical derivation. Therefore missing sheep prevents the loaves.
Gemara
Rule established: Where identical terms exist for a gezerah shavah, we always prefer the identical match — unless the non-identical analogy is transmitted as a free (uncontested) tradition, in which case Rabbi Akiva prefers freedom over precision.
Gezerah Shavah Principle An identical-word verbal analogy overrides a non-identical one — unless the non-identical analogy is 'free' (uncontested by any teacher), which Rabbi Akiva holds is a stronger credential than mere lexical identity.
