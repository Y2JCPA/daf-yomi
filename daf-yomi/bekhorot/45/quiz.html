Bekhorot 45 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Akiva, what happens if the two loaves are unavailable on Shavuot?
A. The two peace-offering sheep are sacrificed anyway — they are independent
B. The two peace-offering sheep cannot be sacrificed — the loaves block them
C. All Shavuot offerings are postponed until loaves are available
D. Only the mussaf offerings from Numbers are sacrificed instead
Correct: B — Rabbi Akiva holds that the loaves are primary: their absence prevents the accompanying two sheep from being sacrificed. He derives this from a 'free' verbal analogy on tihyena (Lev. 23:17).
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Ezekiel 46:6 prescribe only 1 bull and 6 lambs for the New Moon when the Torah requires 2 bulls and 7 lambs?
A. Ezekiel was recording a diminished post-exile standard due to limited resources
B. The verse teaches that each animal is independently valid — bringing fewer is permissible
C. Ezekiel's text is corrupted and was later corrected by the Sages
D. Ezekiel describes a private individual's offering, not the communal one
Correct: B — The baraita explains that Ezekiel's reduced numbers are didactic — each animal is independently valid, so if the full complement is unavailable, one brings as many as possible, down to a single animal.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Who preserved the book of Ezekiel from being suppressed, and how?
A. Rav Ashi, by writing a comprehensive commentary in Babylon
B. Rabbi Yoḥanan, by ruling that Ezekiel's prophecies were all conditional
C. Ḥanina ben Ḥizkiyya, by sitting in his attic until he resolved every difficult verse
D. Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, by including Ezekiel in the final canon of scripture
Correct: C — Rav (cited by Rav Yehuda) praised Ḥanina ben Ḥizkiyya, who brought 300 barrels of oil for his lamp and stayed in his attic until he had reconciled all of Ezekiel's passages with the Torah.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What verbal analogy (gezerah shavah) does Rabbi Shimon ben Nannas use to prove the sheep are the primary element?
A. He links 'tihyena' (Lev. 23:17) referring to loaves with a similar term in Numbers
B. He links the identical word 'yihyu' — 'they shall be holy' — applied to the sheep in Lev. 23:20
C. He links 'wave offering' appearing in both the loaves and sheep passages
D. He links 'kodesh' (holy) from the sheep verse with 'kodesh' in the Shabbat passage
Correct: B — Rabbi Shimon ben Nannas uses the identical word yihyu in Leviticus 23:20 ('they shall be holy to the Lord for the priest') referring to the sheep, establishing the sheep as the primary element through an identical-word verbal analogy.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Shimon (bar Yochai), why may the two peace-offering sheep be sacrificed even without the two loaves?
A. Because a Sanhedrin ruling exempts them when loaves are scarce
B. Because the verse in Numbers explicitly permits the sheep without loaves
C. Because the sheep permit themselves — blood sprinkling is an independent permitting act
D. Because the sheep are more costly and thus given halakhic priority over baked goods
Correct: C — Rabbi Shimon reasons that the sprinkling of the sheep's blood constitutes an independent permitting act — the sheep 'permit themselves.' This means they can always be sacrificed alone, regardless of whether the loaves are present.
