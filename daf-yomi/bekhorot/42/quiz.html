QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the combined rulings of Rav Pappa and Rabbi Ya'akov, where must the tzitzit hole be located on the garment corner?
A. Within 1 fingerbreadth only — closer ensures maximum attachment
B. Between 1 full thumb-joint and 3 fingerbreadths from the edge
C. Exactly at the midpoint of the garment's corner fold
D. No closer than 4 fingerbreadths from the edge
Correct: B — Rav Pappa (41b) requires the hole to be within 3 fingerbreadths of the edge so the strings remain 'on the corner.' Rabbi Ya'akov citing Rabbi Yoḥanan requires at least 1 full thumb-joint distance so the fabric doesn't tear. Both apply simultaneously, creating a valid zone.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What critical halakha does Shmuel add to Rav's ruling about valid tzitzit threads?
A. Threads must be double-looped before being tied into knots
B. Only threads dyed with natural plant dyes are acceptable
C. The spinning of the threads must be done for the sake of the mitzva (לשמה)
D. Threads must extend at least 4 handbreadths beyond the corner
Correct: C — Shmuel taught Rav Yehuda that even unseparated wool swatches not spun for the sake of the mitzva are invalid. Just as tefillin parchment must be written לשמה, so too tzitzit strings must be spun לשמה. This is an additional requirement beyond Rav's ruling.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What happens to the test wad of wool used to check the tekhelet dye (according to the Tanna Kamma)?
A. It may be reused as tzitzit if the color matches correctly
B. It is given to a Torah scholar as a gift
C. It is burned, and the remaining dye in the pot becomes unfit
D. It is valid for tzitzit but not for sale to others
Correct: C — The Tanna Kamma holds that dye used for testing is unfit for actual tzitzit (derived from 'for you' — dyeing must be for the mitzva purpose, not testing). The test wad is burned so it cannot be mistaken for valid tekhelet. R' Yoḥanan ben Dahavai permits a second application of the dye.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is the basis of the dispute between Rav Adda bar Ahava and the opposing opinion regarding reciting a blessing when attaching tzitzit?
A. Whether the blessing formula should be in Hebrew or Aramaic
B. Whether tzitzit is a garment-obligation (bless at wearing) or a personal obligation (bless at attaching)
C. Whether one must have intent (kavanah) to fulfill the mitzva while reciting the blessing
D. Whether women are obligated in tzitzit and therefore whether the blessing is universally required
Correct: B — Rav Adda bar Ahava holds tzitzit is a chovat habegged (garment obligation) so the blessing 'lehit'atef betzitzit' is said at wearing. The other view treats it as a personal obligation with the blessing 'la'asot tzitzit' said at the time of attaching. Rav Naḥman challenged Rav Adda based on hearing him chant the latter blessing, but Rav Adda clarified he was merely humming, not formally blessing.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does the Gemara resolve Rav Ḥisda's principle when challenged by the case of circumcision (where a Jew blesses despite gentile seeming to perform it validly)?
A. Rav Ḥisda retracts his principle entirely in light of the circumcision case
B. The blessing on circumcision is Rabbinic, not tied to the mitzva's validity, so it is an exception
C. Rav himself holds circumcision by a gentile is invalid, so there is no actual contradiction
D. Circumcision is different because it creates a permanent physical sign, unlike other mitzvot
Correct: C — The resolution is that Rav Ḥisda's contradiction only works if Rav validates circumcision by a gentile. But Rav himself holds it is invalid — derived from 'himmol yimmol' (only one who is circumcised may circumcise) and Rabbi Yoḥanan's reading that the doubled verb excludes uncircumcised gentiles. With no contradiction in Rav's own views, Rav Ḥisda's principle remains intact.
