🧵 Do Tekhelet and White Strings Prevent Each Other?

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
The absence of tekhelet DOES prevent fulfillment with white strings alone, and vice versa — the two are mutually indispensable. 'The fringe of the corner' demands both.
The Rabbis
The absence of tekhelet does NOT prevent fulfillment with white, and the absence of white does NOT prevent fulfillment with tekhelet — 'that you may look upon it' means each fulfills an independent mitzva.
Mishna ruling (follows the Rabbis) The absence of sky-blue strings does not prevent fulfillment with white strings, and the absence of white strings does not prevent fulfillment with sky-blue strings.

🔵 Tekhelet vs. White — Two Interpretive Camps

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
Mutual prevention: 'The fringe of the corner' — the string must match the garment's material. Neither white alone nor tekhelet alone can fulfill the mitzva. Both are required together.
The Rabbis
Independent obligations: 'That you may look upon it' — each string individually fulfills a mitzva. White alone is valid, tekhelet alone is valid, even without the other present.
Verse in dispute — Numbers 15:38 "וְנָתְנוּ עַל צִיצִת הַכָּנָף פְּתִיל תְּכֵלֶת" — And they shall put on the fringe of the corner a sky-blue thread

🪢 Proper Order of Inserting Strings into the Garment

Preferred order (l'chatchila): Insert white strings first, then tekhelet — this is the mitzva performed in its optimal manner
↓
Post-facto (b'dieved): If tekhelet was inserted before white, the mitzva is still fulfilled — but a mitzva was 'omitted,' meaning the preferred method was not followed (Rav, cited by Rav Yehuda)
↓
Rami bar Ḥama — special case: A garment made entirely of sky-blue wool requires tekhelet strings inserted first, since 'the fringe of the corner' matches the garment's own color
↓
Rava rejects Rami bar Ḥama: Garment color does not determine insertion order. The ruling actually addresses severed strings — even severed sky-blue strings suffice when white strings are absent
↓
Bottom line: The mishna's statement that tekhelet absence does not prevent white fulfillment is necessary to teach that even severed tekhelet strings leave the white strings valid

✂️ Severed Strings — Positions on Minimum Valid Length

Sons of Rabbi Ḥiyya

  • Severed white or tekhelet strings are still ritually fit
  • Parallel: severed hyssop branches remain valid for red heifer sprinkling
  • Validity of each segment stands independently after severance

Shmuel's Ruling

  • Severed strings must still be long enough to tie a slipknot (קֶשֶׁר עַנְיָבָה)
  • Debate: must all strings together reach knot length, or each string individually?
  • Rav Ashi adds: what if strings are thick and cannot be knotted at that length?

Rav Aḥa son of Rava

  • Response to Rav Ashi's dilemma on thick strings
  • If strings are long enough to be folded and recognizable as tzitzit, they are valid
  • The functional recognizability as fringe — not knottability alone — is the criterion
