Bekhorot 38 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the mishna, what is the law if a garment has only white strings and no tekhelet?
A. The garment is entirely invalid and the mitzva cannot be fulfilled at all
B. The mitzva of tzitzit is fulfilled with the white strings alone
C. One must wait until tekhelet becomes available before wearing the garment
D. A rabbinical penalty applies for wearing the garment without tekhelet
Correct: B — The mishna rules that the absence of tekhelet does not prevent fulfillment of the mitzva with white strings — following the Rabbis who hold each string type fulfills an independent obligation.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi derive that tekhelet and white strings are mutually indispensable?
A. From the commandment to tie the strings 'together' as a single unit
B. From the phrase 'the fringe of the corner,' implying the string must match the garment's material
C. From a direct tradition passed down from Moses at Sinai (halakha l'Moshe miSinai)
D. From the prohibition against wearing shatnez (mixed fibers)
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi reads 'the fringe of the corner' as requiring the string to match the corner — the same material as the garment — making both white and tekhelet strings necessary simultaneously.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rav (cited by Rav Yehuda) mean when he says that inserting tekhelet before white 'omits a mitzva'?
A. The entire tzitzit is invalid and must be redone from the beginning
B. The mitzva is fulfilled but not in the optimal manner, since white should have been inserted first
C. A Torah prohibition against improper ordering has been violated
D. One must recite an additional blessing to compensate for the error in ordering
Correct: B — Rav explains that inserting tekhelet before white is valid post-facto (b'dieved), but 'omitting a mitzva' means failing to perform it in the preferred manner — not an outright invalidation of the tzitzit.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Shmuel, what is the minimum length required for severed tzitzit strings to remain ritually valid?
A. At least four finger-breadths remaining after severance
B. Long enough to reach the ground when the garment is worn
C. Long enough to tie a slipknot (kesher anivah)
D. At least half the original length of the string before it was severed
Correct: C — Shmuel rules that severed strings must retain enough length to tie a slipknot. This is the minimum measure for them to maintain their ritual validity as tzitzit.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rava derive from the teaching of the sons of Rabbi Ḥiyya that severed strings are valid?
A. That any material may be used for tzitzit strings, not only wool or linen
B. That a knot must be tied after each and every set of windings, not only one knot at the very end
C. That tekhelet and white strings are fully interchangeable in all halakhic respects
D. That one may fulfill the mitzva with a single string folded in half rather than multiple strings
Correct: B — Rava reasons: since severed strings between knots remain valid, each winding section must be secured by its own knot. If only one final knot existed, a severance mid-winding would leave that section with no valid knot at all.
