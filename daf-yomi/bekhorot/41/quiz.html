QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why did Rabba bar Huna rebuke Rava bar Rav Naḥman for attaching tzitzit to the corners of a folded cloak?
A. The fold corners are not the true corners of the garment required by Torah
B. A folded cloak is entirely exempt from the mitzva of tzitzit
C. He had used the wrong color of strings on the fold corners
D. He had attached too few strings per corner at the fold
Correct: A — Rabba bar Huna told him: 'This is not the corner of the garment that the Merciful One writes about in the Torah.' Tzitzit must be at the actual structural corners of the garment, not at a fold that merely creates temporary corners.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What did Rabba bar Huna tell Rava bar Rav Naḥman after he threw off the folded cloak — and what does this reveal about the nature of the tzitzit obligation?
A. You should have kept it, since tzitzit on the fold is valid after the fact
B. Tzitzit is an obligation on the garment — go properly affix tzitzit to the unfolded cloak at its true corners
C. You are now exempt since you no longer own a four-cornered garment
D. You must consult a posek before removing tzitzit from any garment
Correct: B — Rabba bar Huna clarified that tzitzit is an obligation incumbent upon the garment itself — therefore, go affix proper tzitzit to the cloak at its real corners rather than abandoning it.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What was the angel's response when Rav Ketina argued that one should not be punished for failing to perform a positive mitzva?
A. The angel agreed, but warned that linen cloaks were actually obligated
B. At a time of divine anger, even failure to fulfill a positive mitzva incurs punishment
C. The angel retracted and said only Torah prohibitions are enforced above
D. The angel said the punishment applied only to scholars who knew the law
Correct: B — The angel responded: 'At a time of divine anger (עֵת רָצוֹן), we punish even for the failure to fulfill a positive commandment.' The Gemara adds that the deeper point is that Rav Ketina was using legal ploys to avoid the mitzva altogether.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
On which three specific halakhic rulings did Rabba deviate from Rav and follow Shmuel's lenient position?
A. Tekhelet color, cloak size requirements, and burial shrouds
B. Untying tzitzit for transfer, lighting one Hanukkah lamp from another, and dragging items on Shabbat
C. Folded cloaks, linen garments, and all-tekhelet garments
D. Number of strings per corner, minimum string length, and distance from edge
Correct: B — Abaye recorded that Rabba followed Rav in all matters except three: (1) one may untie tzitzit and transfer them, (2) one may light a Hanukkah lamp from another, and (3) one may drag furniture on Shabbat (following Rabbi Shimon that an unintended furrow is permitted).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Shmuel (as cited by Rav Tovi bar Kisna), do garments stored in a box require tzitzit?
A. No — tzitzit is only obligatory when actively wearing the garment
B. Yes — tzitzit is an obligation on the garment itself, regardless of whether it is worn
C. Only if the garments are made of wool and have four distinct corners
D. Only if the owner regularly wears four-cornered garments in public
Correct: B — Shmuel rules that stored garments in a box require tzitzit because the obligation pertains to the garment, not the person. Shmuel concedes only one exception: an old man's burial shroud during his lifetime (since the dead do not 'wear' garments), though tzitzit are affixed at death to avoid 'mocking the poor.'
