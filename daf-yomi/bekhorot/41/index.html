🧵 Core Dispute: Is Tzitzit an Obligation on the Man or the Garment?

Rabba bar Huna
Tzitzit is an obligation incumbent upon the garment — any four-cornered cloak you own must have fringes, even stored ones not being worn.
Rav Ketina's Angel
Ketina! You wear a linen cloak in summer and a rounded felt cloak in winter — both exempt from tzitzit. Are you seeking ploys (תַּצְדְּקִי) to exempt yourself from the mitzva?
Rav Ketina
Do you punish us even for failing to fulfill a positive mitzva? Tzitzit only applies when one actually wears a four-cornered garment!
Angel
At a time of divine anger (עֵת רָצוֹן), we punish even for failure to fulfill a positive commandment — especially when the exemption is deliberately sought.
Resolution Both views agree: one who deliberately chooses exempt garments to evade the mitzva of tzitzit is blameworthy. The mitzva should not be circumvented through legal technicality.

📐 Folded Cloak — Where Must Tzitzit Be Placed?

A very long cloak is folded in half and worn as a double-layered garment
↓
Tanna Kamma
Tzitzit required at corners of the fold — it functions as the garment's effective edge
Rabbi Shimon
Fold corners are exempt — true corners are at the original garment ends, not the fold
↓
Story: Rava bar Rav Naḥman wore a folded cloak with strings at fold corners. It unfolded — leaving tzitzit in the middle of the garment.
↓
Rabba bar Huna rebuked him: 'This is not the corner the Merciful One writes about in the Torah!' — Rava immediately discarded the cloak and took a different one.

🔄 Rav vs. Shmuel — Three Parallel Leniency Disputes

Rav
One may not untie tzitzit strings from one garment in order to affix them to another garment.
Shmuel
One may untie tzitzit from one garment and transfer them to another — the strings retain their holiness.
Rav
One may not light a Hanukkah lamp from another Hanukkah lamp — each flame has sanctity.
Shmuel
One may light one Hanukkah lamp from another. Similarly, one may drag furniture on Shabbat despite possibly making a furrow — davar she'eino mitkaven (unintended act) is permitted per Rabbi Shimon.
Abaye
Our master Rabba followed Rav in all things — except these three, where he ruled like Shmuel: tzitzit transfer, Hanukkah lighting, and Shabbat dragging.
Pattern These three disputes all concern whether an action that might show disregard for a holy object or Shabbat is nevertheless permitted — Shmuel is consistently lenient.

✂️ Beit Shammai vs. Beit Hillel — Tzitzit String Specifications

Beit Shammai

  • 4 strings inserted per corner hole (= 8 strings hanging)
  • Strings hang 4 fingerbreadths beyond the knots and windings
  • Stricter position on minimum string count

Beit Hillel

  • 3 strings inserted per corner hole (= 6 strings hanging)
  • Strings hang 3 fingerbreadths beyond the knots — equal to ¼ of a handbreadth
  • More lenient; the ¼ handbreadth standard provides the minimum

Amoraic Rulings

  • Rav Huna: 4 strings within 4 fingerbreadths of edge; hang 4 fingerbreadths
  • Rav Pappa: 4 strings within 3 fingerbreadths of edge; hang 4 fingerbreadths
  • Baraita challenge: 'Strings' implies no minimum measure — resolved: no minimum for length, but structural requirements remain

🕊️ Pious Men's Practice & the Ideal of Tzitzit Observance

Early pious men (חֲסִידִים הָרִאשׁוֹנִים) attached tzitzit strings while the garment was still being woven — not waiting until it was complete
↓
Once three fingerbreadths of the garment were woven, they immediately affixed the white and sky-blue (tekhelet) strings — demonstrating that the garment-obligation begins at the garment's formation
↓
This practice supports Rabba bar Huna's view: tzitzit is an obligation on the garment itself, arising as soon as the garment exists in obligated form
↓
Contrast Rav Ketina: he wore garments specifically shaped to be exempt (linen cloak, rounded felt). The angel's rebuke shows the ideal is to embrace the mitzva, not avoid it through technicality
↓
Torn corner halakha (Raḥava citing R. Yehuda): If torn beyond 3 fingerbreadths from the edge — sew and re-attach. If torn within 3 fingerbreadths — do not sew (the corner is lost); a patch from elsewhere is never valid for new tzitzit placement

📋 Bekhorot 41 — Summary Map

Does the garment have four corners?
↓
Yes → Tzitzit required at the true corners (not fold corners). 4 strings per corner, hang 4 fingerbreadths beyond knots.
No → Technically exempt — but deliberately choosing exempt garments is condemned by the angel as seeking 'ploys' against the mitzva.
↓
Obligation is on the garment: stored cloaks still require tzitzit (Shmuel); pious men attached strings while still weaving. Burial shrouds receive tzitzit at death to avoid mocking the deceased.
↓
Transferring strings: Rav prohibits; Shmuel permits. Rabba followed Shmuel here — as in lighting Hanukkah lamps from each other and dragging on Shabbat (davar she'eino mitkaven).
