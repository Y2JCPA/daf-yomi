QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yitzḥak, what is the status of the requirement that tefillin straps be black?
A. A rabbinic enactment instituted after the destruction of the Temple
B. A halakha l'Moshe miSinai — transmitted directly from Sinai
C. A minhag (custom) that varies by community
D. A requirement that applies only to the head tefillin, not the arm
Correct: B — Rabbi Yitzḥak states that the requirement for black straps is a halakha l'Moshe miSinai. The Gemara resolves the baraita's apparent contradiction by distinguishing between the outer (must be black) and inner (more flexible) side of the strap.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava, when two of the walls between the compartments of tefillin shel rosh have torn, when are the tefillin still fit?
A. When the outer hide remains fully intact regardless of which walls tore
B. When the two torn walls are adjacent (side by side) to each other
C. When the two torn walls are non-adjacent (not aligned next to each other)
D. When at least two of the original four walls remain completely untorn
Correct: C — Rava rules that two torn walls render tefillin unfit only when they are adjacent — creating a joined breach. If the torn walls are non-adjacent, the compartments retain sufficient structural integrity and the tefillin remain fit.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rabbi Eliezer the Great, to what does the verse 'All nations shall see that the Name of God is called upon you' (Deuteronomy 28:10) refer?
A. The tzitzit fringes worn on all four corners of a garment
B. The mezuzah affixed to the doorpost of every Jewish home
C. The tefillin shel rosh (phylacteries of the head)
D. The priestly vestments worn during Temple service
Correct: C — Rabbi Eliezer the Great teaches in a baraita that this verse refers to the tefillin shel rosh — worn visibly on the head — whose sight causes the nations to stand in awe and fear of Israel.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What does Rav Pappa clarify about the halakha l'Moshe miSinai that tefillin must be square?
A. Squareness applies only to the outer seams, not the internal structure
B. The tefillin must be square along both their seams and their diagonals
C. A slight rounding at the corners is permitted for comfort
D. Squareness is required for the head tefillin only, not the arm tefillin
Correct: B — Rav Pappa specifies that 'square' means perfectly square in two ways: along the seams (sides) AND along the diagonals — a rigorous geometric standard ensuring true squareness.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rami bar Ḥama citing Reish Lakish, what is the minimum required length for tefillin straps?
A. Until the navel of the person wearing them
B. Until the heart/chest of the person wearing them
C. Until the index finger of the hand wearing the tefillin
D. Until the middle finger of the hand wearing the tefillin
Correct: C — Rami bar Ḥama transmits in the name of Reish Lakish that the minimum strap length is until the index finger — derived from the inference that strap 'remnants' left after cutting may be unfit, implying straps have a required minimum measure.
