Bekhorot 11 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Mishna cite three separate cases (stone, salt, frankincense) that disqualify the handful, rather than just one?
A. Each case carries a unique legal rationale that cannot be derived from the others
B. The Mishna is repetitive purely for rhetorical emphasis
C. To enumerate every possible item ever found in the Temple
D. Because three witnesses are required to establish any Temple law
Correct: A — A stone is unfit for sacrifice; salt was not fixed with the whole offering (salted separately); frankincense is a valid Temple item but belongs to the remainder, not the handful. Each reason is logically distinct.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Zutra bar Toviyya citing Rav, how does the priest physically form the handful?
A. He uses a flat open hand like a shovel and scoops upward
B. He bends his middle three fingers until their tips reach over the palm of his hand
C. He curls only his fingertips and takes the minimum that fits
D. He uses a designated Temple measuring vessel, not his bare hand
Correct: B — The priest bends his middle three fingers until their tips touch his palm, forming a natural cup. This is the normal human way of taking a handful — neither overflowing nor meager.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the role of the little finger (pinky) in the handful removal, and why does this matter for Abaye's objection?
A. It initiates the scoop from the base of the vessel
B. It has no role whatsoever in the kometz procedure
C. It levels the handful from below, wiping away excess flour
D. It seals the handful to prevent spillage during the transfer
Correct: C — The little finger levels the handful from below while the thumb levels from above, ensuring an exact measure. This resolves Abaye's objection: all five fingers serve a Temple function, and none is excluded from the handful removal.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rabbi Yitzchak bar Yosef says in the name of Rabbi Yochanan that all three tannaim agree on one matter. Which type of frankincense does NOT involve their dispute?
A. Frankincense accompanying a standard meal offering
B. Frankincense that comes by itself (not with a meal offering) — all agree it disqualifies if any is missing
C. Frankincense burned by the High Priest on Yom Kippur
D. Frankincense mixed directly into the flour
Correct: B — Rabbi Yochanan teaches that while the three tannaim dispute frankincense that accompanies a meal offering, they all agree that frankincense brought on its own (without a meal offering) disqualifies if any amount is lacking — the verse 'all' is uncontested there.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Eliezer, what scenario illustrates that increased oil disqualifies a meal offering?
A. Adding even a single extra drop of oil beyond the log measure
B. Separating two log of oil for a meal offering that requires only one log
C. Using anointing oil instead of standard olive oil
D. Adding oil after the handful has already been removed and transferred
Correct: B — Rabbi Eliezer uses the rhetorical style of 'it is not necessary to say' — even doubling the oil (two log instead of one) disqualifies, and certainly mixing in non-sacred or foreign oil does too. The mishna's focus on 'his own oil' teaches the lesser case.
