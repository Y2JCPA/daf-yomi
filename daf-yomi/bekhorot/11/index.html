🤲 The Kometz: Removing the Handful

Priest inserts hand into meal offering flour in the Temple vessel
↓
Bends middle three fingers until their tips reach over his palm — forming a natural cup
↓
Levels overflow: thumb wipes from above, little finger wipes from below
↓

↓
Handful is burned on the altar — this act permits the remainder for priestly consumption

✋ How Is the Handful Properly Removed?

Abaye
How do priests properly remove the handful? Walk me through the technique.
Rava
They remove it as people normally do — folding all fingers over the palm of the hand.
Abaye
Objection! Every finger has a distinct Temple role: the middle finger measures the cubit, the forefinger sprinkles blood, the thumb presses the kometz. What of the little finger?
Gemara
The little finger is used only for leveling — the priest first fills a full hand so the measure is not lacking, then wipes away excess: thumb above, little finger below.
Ruling Each finger of the priest's hand has a designated Temple function. The handful is scooped by bending the middle three fingers to the palm and leveled with the thumb and little finger.

📖 Deriving the Handful from Scripture

וְקָמַץ מִשָּׁם מְלֹא קֻמְצוֹ — Vayikra 2:2
בְּקֻמְצוֹ — 'With His Handful' (Vayikra 2:2) Two verses create a tension resolved by the baraita:

"And he shall remove from there his handful" — one might think the handful should be overflowing with flour.

"With his handful" — one might think only fingertip-pressure suffices, i.e., the priest merely folds fingers inward and takes the minimum.

Resolution: Neither extreme. The priest bends his middle three fingers until their tips reach his palm — the natural, normal way a person takes a handful. Not overflowing, not deficient.

⚖️ Why Three Cases? Each One Is Necessary

🪨 Stone

  • Not fit for sacrifice at all
  • Might have thought: only unfit items diminish measure
  • Cannot derive the salt or frankincense cases from this

🧂 Salt

  • Fit for sacrifice — but salted on the handful alone, not the whole offering
  • Teaches: items not initially fixed with the whole offering diminish measure
  • Cannot derive this reasoning from stone alone

🌿 Frankincense

  • Belongs to the meal offering — but to the remainder, not the handful
  • Teaches: even a proper Temple ingredient, if misplaced, voids the handful
  • Only this case teaches items from the offering itself that still disqualify

🤔 Rav Pappa's Unresolved Dilemmas

Starting Point: It is obvious that the handful must be removed in the normal manner — fingers angled to side, filling the palm
↓
But what if the priest deviated from the standard method?
↓
Took from the sides?
Back of hand swept across flour; flour collected via palm's side — not standard scooping motion
Stuck handful to vessel wall?
Instead of dropping handful into second vessel, pressed it onto the inner wall — not placed at the bottom
Placed on underside of overturned vessel?
Mar bar Rav Ashi: overturned the vessel and pressed handful into an indentation on its exterior underside
↓
All three dilemmas left unresolved (תֵּיקוּ) — the Gemara offers no definitive ruling
