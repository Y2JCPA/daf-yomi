🔗 When Are the Loaves and Sheep Irrevocably Bound?

Rabbi Yoḥanan
Slaughter (שְׁחִיטָה) of the sheep establishes the bond between the two loaves and two sheep of Shavuot.
Ulla (dilemma)
The Sages of Eretz Yisrael ask: Does waving (תְּנוּפָה) also establish a bond — or only slaughter, as Rabbi Yoḥanan says?
Gemara
From the thanks offering we see slaughter sanctifies loaves. But it is different — the Torah calls it a peace offering, so it follows peace offering rules.
Rabbi Yirmeya
If waving establishes a bond: losing loaves after waving = sheep are lost too. If it does not: losing loaves raises new dilemmas about replacement loaves.
Core Question At what ritual moment — waving (תְּנוּפָה) or slaughter (שְׁחִיטָה) — are the two Shavuot loaves and two sheep bound together such that the loss of one invalidates the other?

❓ Rabbi Yirmeya: What Happens When Loaves Are Lost After Waving?

Loaves and sheep waved together
תְּנוּפָה performed on Shavuot
↓
If waving DOES establish bond
Loaves lost after waving:
Sheep are also lost — must bring entirely new loaves AND new sheep
If waving does NOT establish bond
Loaves lost after waving:
Bring new replacement loaves — but do they require waving?
↓
Sub-dilemma (waving does not bond):
Sheep were already waved — do new loaves wave alone, or with sheep again?
↓
Rabbi Yirmeya resolves: Primary waving obligation is with the sheep. Since sheep were already waved, new replacement loaves do not require re-waving.

🐑 Abaye and Rava: Why Do the Two Sheep Sanctify the Loaves?

Abaye
Why do the two sheep as peace offerings sanctify the Shavuot loaves upon slaughter — but the musaf (additional) offerings of Shavuot do not?
Rava (1st answer)
Because the sheep and loaves are waved together. The musaf offerings are not waved with the loaves — hence no bond.
Rava (2nd answer)
The two sheep are comparable to a thanks offering — just as a thanks offering (called a peace offering) has loaves sanctified by slaughter, so too the two Shavuot sheep.
Gemara (challenge)
The thanks offering has no other animal offerings alongside it. Shavuot has many musaf offerings — so that analogy breaks down.
Rava (final answer)
The two sheep are comparable to the nazirite's ram — a peace offering accompanied by loaves. The baraita explicitly derives that the ram's slaughter sanctifies the nazirite's loaves. So too the two Shavuot sheep.
Derived Source The nazirite brings a ram as a peace offering along with loaves. A baraita derives that slaughter of this ram sanctifies its loaves — the Shavuot two-sheep offering is analogous.

⚖️ Two Loaves Brought Without Sheep: Three Amoraic Views

Rabba

  • Torah law: loaves are brought to be eaten
  • Rabbinic decree: may NOT be eaten
  • Reason: lest next year sheep are available and people think loaves may always be eaten without sheep
  • Result: loaves left overnight → disqualified by לִינָה → burned

Rav Yosef

  • Torah law: loaves are brought to be burned (not eaten)
  • Yet we do not burn them immediately
  • Reason: rabbinic decree lest sheep become available later that day
  • Also: lest people err in future years (same concern as Rabba)

Rava

  • Torah law: loaves come to be eaten
  • Rabbinic decree prevents eating them
  • Left overnight → disqualified by לִינָה → burned
  • Source: 'You shall bring out of your dwellings two wave-loaves' — implies they come to be eaten, not burned

📜 Rabba's Proof from Mishna Shekalim Priests claimed: The omer and two loaves are communal offerings brought with public funds — since priests share in the communal funds, their personal meal offerings must be entirely burned (Lev 6:16), so they should not eat the two loaves. Rabban Yoḥanan ben Zakkai rejected this: priests who do not contribute the half-shekel are sinners, yet they voluntarily waive their share so the loaves can be eaten by all.

Rabba's inference: The priestly claim presupposes the loaves would in principle be eaten — proving they are brought to be eaten, but only rabbinic decree prevents it when brought alone.
