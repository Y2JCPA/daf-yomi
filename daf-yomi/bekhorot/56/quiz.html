Bekhorot 56 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Gemara's final conclusion, what does the word 'it' (אותו) in the verse about the king's sin offering teach?
A. Both the animal and the slaughterer must stand in the north
B. The animal must be positioned in the north, but the slaughterer need not stand there
C. The Paschal offering is entirely excluded from Temple service laws
D. Bird offerings must also be slaughtered in the north
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes that 'it' restricts the northward requirement to the animal's position — the goat must be in the north, but the one performing the slaughter does not need to stand there. This distinction is necessary even after Rabbi Aḥiyya's teaching about the burnt offering.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does the Gemara reject deriving that the Paschal offering requires northward slaughter from a logical inference based on the burnt offering?
A. The burnt offering requires laying of hands and the Paschal offering does not
B. The burnt offering is entirely consumed on the altar, making it uniquely stringent — one cannot derive from it
C. The burnt offering is only an individual sacrifice, while the Paschal offering is communal
D. The burnt offering is brought for unintentional sin, the Paschal offering is not
Correct: B — The inference from the burnt offering fails because the burnt offering is entirely burned on the altar (כליל), a special stringency not shared by the Paschal offering. A uniquely stringent case cannot serve as a basis for derivation.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Pappa, how many sets of lashes does one receive for baking a meal offering that became leaven?
A. One set, since baking is a single act
B. Three sets, one for each of kneading, shaping, and baking
C. Two sets, one for the act of baking and one for the resulting leavened state
D. No lashes, because baking is only a rabbinic prohibition regarding meal offerings
Correct: C — Rav Pappa says one who bakes a leavened meal offering receives two sets of lashes. The baraita's ruling of one set applies when the same person both shaped and baked the dough — the shaping already incurred one set, so baking adds a second.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rabbi Yehuda's position regarding a firstborn animal whose blood circulation is constricted and whose life depends on blood-letting?
A. One may let its blood only if a non-Israelite performs the procedure
B. One may let its blood only at a site that will not cause a permanent blemish
C. One may not let its blood under any circumstances, even if the animal will die
D. One may slaughter it immediately before it dies naturally
Correct: C — Rabbi Yehuda takes the most stringent position: even if the firstborn would die without the blood-letting, it is forbidden to inflict any blemish on a consecrated animal — the prohibition is absolute with no life-saving exception.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
On what halakha do all Sages agree, according to Rabbi Yoḥanan, despite their disagreement about causing blemishes on consecrated animals?
A. One may never let blood from a firstborn animal under any circumstances
B. Castrating a consecrated animal after it has already been castrated still incurs full liability
C. A blemished animal that was consecrated must be redeemed immediately
D. Communal sin offerings do not require laying of hands under any opinion
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan states all Sages agree: one who castrates an already-castrated consecrated animal is still fully liable, derived from 'you shall not do this in your land' (Lev. 22:24). The act is prohibited regardless of the animal's prior condition.
