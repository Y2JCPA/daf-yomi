⚖️ What Materials Are Valid for the Menorah?

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi
I interpret via generalizations and details (klal u'frat): 'Candelabrum of' = generalization; 'pure gold' = detail. Only what resembles the detail — a metal — is permitted. Wood is unfit.
Rabbi Yosei ben Rabbi Yehuda
I interpret via amplifications and restrictions (ribui u'miut): 'Candelabrum of' = amplification; 'pure gold' = restriction. Any type of metal — or even wood — is valid. The Hasmonean kings proved this in practice.
Rav Yosef
A baraita confirms Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi: 'If one has no gold, one may fashion the Menorah from silver.' This shows metals substitute for gold — but wood is excluded by the detail.
Rav Pappa b. Rav Ḥanin
On the contrary — remove your baraita in light of mine! My baraita lists tin, lead, and iron as valid, supporting Rabbi Yosei's broader reading.
Resolution Rav Yosef: multiple baraitot corroborate that metals are valid but wood is not (Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi). Rabbi Yosei b. Rabbi Yehuda: even wood, as the Hasmoneans used — though Rabbi Yosei himself disapproves of the Hasmonean practice.

🔩 Menorah Materials: Valid, Disputed, and Invalid

Valid (All Agree)

  • Gold — ideal; must be a single beaten block
  • Silver — permitted when gold is unavailable
  • Tin — valid per baraita
  • Lead — valid per baraita
  • Iron — valid per baraita
  • All types of metal: included by 'will be made' (Exodus 25:31)

Disputed

  • Wood: R. Yehuda HaNasi — unfit (only metals qualify under klal u'frat)
  • Wood: R. Yosei b. R. Yehuda — fit (ribui u'miut allows anything)
  • Hasmonean kings used iron spits plated with tin — then switched to wood
  • R. Yosei himself disapproves of the Hasmonean wooden Menorah

Invalid (All Agree)

  • Fragments (gerutaot) of gold — must be one piece
  • Assembled pieces — Menorah must be 'beaten work,' a single unit
  • Earthenware — clearly excluded
  • Any incomplete Menorah (missing branches, lamps, ornaments)

📏 Menorah Dimensions: 18 Handbreadths

גֹּבַהּ הַמְּנוֹרָה שְׁמוֹנָה עָשָׂר טְפָחִים (שמואל בשם זָקֵן)
Shmuel in the name of a certain Elder Total height: 18 handbreadths.

• 3 HB — Base and flower upon the base
• 2 HB — Bare shaft
• 1 HB — Knob (1st branching point; two branches extend outward and rise to full height)
• 2 HB — Bare shaft
• 1 HB — Knob (2nd branching point)
• 2 HB — Bare shaft
• 1 HB — Knob (3rd branching point)
• 2 HB — Bare shaft
• 1 HB — Three goblets, knob, and flower at the top of the central shaft

Goblets resembled Alexandrian cups (long and narrow). Knobs resembled Cretan apples. Flowers resembled Cretan lilies.

🎺 Why Were Moses's Trumpets Unfit for Future Generations?

The verse: 'Make for you two silver trumpets; of beaten work you shall make them' (Numbers 10:2)
↓
Initial suggestion: 'for you' (לְךָ) means fit for your generation but not for future ones — but this reasoning would equally disqualify the Ark (Exodus 25:10 also says 'for you').
↓
The Ark's 'for you' is resolved differently: either Moses paid from his own property, or God said 'I desire that the Ark be made through you personally.' Neither applies to trumpets.
↓
Conclusion: The trumpets were uniquely tied to Moses's personal commission. All other vessels he fashioned — the Ark, Menorah, Table, Altars — were valid for all generations.
↓
Practical rule derived: One may not build a house in the form of the Sanctuary, a portico like the Entrance Hall, a courtyard like the Temple courtyard, or a table like the Table — nor a candelabrum like the Menorah (even in a different size or material).
