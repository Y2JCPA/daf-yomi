📜 Mezuza & Tefillin: Placement, Doorposts, and Structure

Mezuza Laws
Which entrances require me? Must there be two doorposts or will one suffice? And on which side must I be placed?
Tefillin Laws
How many compartments must the head tefillin contain? How are my four passages arranged — and what if they are placed out of order?
Daf Overview Menachot 34 bridges two major topics: the nuanced laws of mezuza placement — covering gatehouse exemptions, corner entrances, single doorposts, and the right-side requirement — and the structural laws of tefillin, including the derivation of four compartments from totafot and the essential ordering of the four passages.

🚪 Corner Entrance & The Single Doorpost

Ameimar
An entrance located at the corner of a house — one is obligated to affix a mezuza there.
Rav Ashi
But it has no doorposts at all! How can there be an obligation without doorposts?
Ameimar
Adei — these walls are its doorposts. The surrounding structure serves as the doorframe.
Rav Pappa
I visited Mar Shmuel's house and saw a mezuza on a single left-side doorpost. He ruled like Rabbi Meir — one doorpost suffices to obligate mezuza.
Tannaitic Dispute Rabbi Meir: One doorpost obligates mezuza — derived from the verse which implies even the minimum. Sages: Two doorposts required. The word 'mezuzot' (plural) demands two.

➡️ Two Proofs: Mezuza Belongs on the Right

Baraita — Beitekha / Biatekha

  • "Upon the doorposts of your house" (Deut. 6:9)
  • Beitekha read as biatekha — your entry
  • As you enter: mezuza is on your right hand
  • Right side = the side of entry

Rav Shmuel bar Aḥa (Rava bar Ulla)

  • "Jehoiada the priest set the chest beside the altar on the right side" (II Kings 12:10)
  • The right is the position of honor
  • Mezuza belongs on the honored, right side
  • Derived from sacred Temple practice

Practical Ruling

  • Mezuza must be on the right as one enters
  • If only one doorpost exists (R. Meir): affix to whichever post exists
  • If both exist: right side is mandatory
  • Writing must precede placement (from 'u-khetavtam')

📿 Totafot: Deriving Four Compartments in Head Tefillin

טט בכתפי שתים, פת באפריקי שתים — הרי כאן ארבע
טוטפות Three appearances of totafot: twice spelled deficiently (טטפת = 2+2) and once fully (טוטפות = implied 4). The three instances together demand four compartments.

Rabbi Akiva's linguistic proof: Tot in the language of Katfei = 2; Pat in the language of Afriki = 2. Thus totafot = 4.

The Baraita rules: Ideally — 4 passages on 4 separate hides, one per compartment. If written on one hide and slit between passages — valid, provided passages are in correct sequence and outer hide is whole.

Arm tefillin: One compartment, one hide. If written on 4 hides placed in one compartment — also valid.
