QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why is the entrance from the gatehouse to the garden exempt from mezuza, according to the Gemara?
A. Gardens are non-residential and never require mezuza under any circumstance
B. The gatehouse was built to provide access to the garden, not as a home entry
C. Abaye and Rava disagreed with Rabba and did not require mezuza anywhere in the gatehouse
D. Only the main house entrance is ever obligated; all secondary entrances are exempt
Correct: B — The Gemara rules that because the gatehouse was built for the purpose of reaching the garden, that entrance is not treated as a primary home entry and is therefore exempt. Abaye and Rava practiced accordingly.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What did Ameimar mean when he told Rav Ashi: 'These (adei) are its doorposts'?
A. He pointed to decorative pillars installed at the corner entrance
B. The surrounding walls of the building themselves serve as the doorposts
C. A corner entrance is always exempt, so no doorposts are necessary
D. He conceded to Rav Ashi that the entrance was exempt due to missing doorposts
Correct: B — Ameimar responded to Rav Ashi's objection that a corner entrance has no doorposts by ruling that the walls of the surrounding structure themselves function as its doorposts, thereby obligating a mezuza.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the primary biblical source the baraita uses to derive that mezuza must be on the right side of the entrance?
A. 'And strike the lintel and the two doorposts' (Exodus 12:22)
B. 'Beitekha' read as 'biatekha' (your entry) — right side as one enters
C. 'Jehoiada set the chest beside the altar on the right side' (II Kings 12:10)
D. A verbal analogy (gezera shava) between 'writing' of mezuza and 'writing' of sotah
Correct: B — The baraita derives from 'beitekha' (your house), reinterpreted as 'biatekha' (your entry): as one enters the house, the mezuza must be on the right. Rav Shmuel bar Aḥa offers a separate proof from the Kings verse.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rabbi Akiva independently derive that head tefillin require four compartments from the word totafot?
A. Totafot appears three times; each appearance implies two, totaling six — but one is excluded
B. 'Tot' = 2 in the Katfei language; 'Pat' = 2 in the Afriki language; together totafot = 4
C. Four compartments parallel the four letters of the Divine Name
D. A gezera shava links totafot to the four expressions of redemption in Egypt
Correct: B — Rabbi Akiva derives four compartments through linguistic analysis: 'tot' means two in the Katfei language and 'pat' means two in the Afriki language, so the compound word totafot = 2+2 = four compartments.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the law if the four passages are placed in the wrong order in the head tefillin?
A. The tefillin are valid post-facto (bediavad), though one should correct them if possible
B. Only a reversal of all four makes them unfit; swapping two is permitted
C. The tefillin are unfit (pasul), as ruled by Rav Ḥananel in the name of Rav
D. The tefillin are unfit only if the first and last passages are exchanged
Correct: C — Rav Ḥananel rules in the name of Rav that if one exchanged the passages and placed them out of their required order, the tefillin are unfit (pasul). The correct order runs: Kadesh, Ki Yeviacha, Shema, V'haya Im Shamo'a.
