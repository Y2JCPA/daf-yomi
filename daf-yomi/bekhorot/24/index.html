🗣️ Rav Kahana vs. Sons of Rabbi Ḥiyya: What Does 'Joins' Mean?

Rav Kahana
The mishna says the vessel joins (מצרף) its contents — not merely connects them. This applies even when the contents are not touching each other!
Sons of R. Ḥiyya
If one who immersed that day inserted his finger between the two half-tenths without touching either one — are both half-tenths rendered impure through that airspace?
Rav Kahana
The vessel joins only items that require a vessel for sanctification. Items that do not require a vessel are not joined by it for this purpose.
Sons of R. Ḥiyya
Can one remove a handful from this half-tenth on behalf of that half-tenth, even though they do not touch? We have no explicit ruling — but consider this similar case…
Key Distinction Joining power (כלי מצרף) applies only to items that require the vessel for their sanctification — this is what makes meal offering half-tenths a special case.

⚖️ Baraita: One Vessel vs. Two Vessels — Handful Validity

❌ One Tenth in Two Vessels

  • May NOT remove handful from one vessel on behalf of the other
  • The meal offering is invalid
  • Physical separation of vessel = separation of the offering
  • Like Abaye's case: vessel with internal ridge = two vessels

✅ Two Tenths in One Vessel

  • MAY remove handful from one tenth on behalf of the other
  • The meal offering is valid
  • Unity of vessel = unity of offering
  • Even if clumps are arranged like teeth of a comb (Rava)

🔥 Can Impurity Be Saturated? — Abaye Challenges Rava

Rava
A piece of meal offering already impure from a tevel yom's touch — when touched again by another tevel yom — it cannot become doubly impure. It is already saturated with that impurity!
Abaye
But we learned: A sheet impure with zav treading, made into a curtain, becomes pure from that treading-impurity. This suggests impurity categories can stack or shift — not saturate!
Rava
Rabbi Yosei explains the sheet case: the zav touched it after lying on it — so a second category of impurity (contact) applies. That is different — here both are the same lesser impurity.
Abaye
Actually — consider the baraita's last clause: the bottom sheet's impurities take effect simultaneously. But in the meal offering case they are sequential — perhaps that distinction, not saturation, is what matters.
Gemara's Conclusion When both impurities are sequential and of the same lesser grade (tevel yom), the question of saturation remains — the Gemara does not definitively resolve whether a second identical impurity takes effect.

🏡 Rava's Three Half-Tenths — 'Residents of One Cabin'

Setup: One tenth divided into two halves. The first half (א) is lost.
↓
Replacement: A replacement half (ב) is separated to complete the tenth with the first half.
↓
Complication: The original lost half (א) is found. Now three half-tenths sit in one vessel: א (first), ב (replacement), א (found).
↓
Rava on impurity: If ב (replacement) becomes impure → ב and א (first) join and become impure together. The found half does not join. If the found half becomes impure, it becomes impure alone.
↓
Abaye on impurity: If any of the three becomes impure → all three join and become impure. Reason: "They are all residents of one cabin" (בני מלון אחד) — all were at some point associated with the same offering.
↓
Rav Pappa objects to Abaye: When the handful is removed, one-sixth (danka) of it was not designated to permit the remainder of the half from which it was taken — so how can that half's remainder be eaten?
↓
Rav Ashi resolves: The handful's validity depends on the priest's intention at the moment of removal — he intends to permit the remainder of the full tenth, which encompasses all valid components.
