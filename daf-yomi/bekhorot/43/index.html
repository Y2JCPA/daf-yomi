⚖️ Are Women Obligated in Tzitzit?

Rabbis
Women ARE obligated. Tzitzit is a standard positive commandment. The verse 'with which you cover yourself' teaches that any four-cornered garment — regardless of wearer — is obligated. Women fulfill mitzvot like tzitzit.
Rabbi Shimon
Women are EXEMPT. The verse says 'that you may look upon it' — this excludes nighttime garments (no seeing at night). Since tzitzit is thus time-bound (day-only mitzva), women are exempt per the rule that they are free from positive time-bound mitzvot.
Rabbis
'That you may look upon it' excludes the garment of a BLIND person — one who literally cannot see. It does not exclude night garments, and it certainly does not make tzitzit time-bound for everyone else.
Rabbi Shimon
I derive the blind person's exemption from 'with which you cover yourself.' That frees up 'that you may look upon it' to exclude nighttime garments — making tzitzit a daytime, time-bound mitzva from which women are exempt.
Practice: Rav Yehuda Rav Yehuda attached white and tekhelet strings to his wife's garment and recited the blessing each morning — following Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, who held that even obligatory women could fulfill the mitzva.

🛍️ Purchasing a Garment with Tzitzit from the Market

From a Jew

  • Retains presumptive fitness (chazakah)
  • Strings assumed attached for sake of mitzva
  • No further verification required
  • May wear and fulfill mitzva immediately

From a Gentile Merchant

  • Requires verification of intent
  • Merchant assumed dyed for commerce — thus fit
  • Homeowner-gentile: uncertain, may have dyed for beauty
  • Seller must remove strings before sale to gentile — but buyer may re-examine and use

🌊 Why Tekhelet? The Chain of Sacred Resemblance

Tekhelet (sky-blue dye) resembles the color of the sea
↓
The sea resembles the color of the sky
↓
The sky resembles the color of the rainbow
↓
The rainbow resembles the splendor of the Shechina (Divine Presence)
↓
The splendor of the Shechina resembles the Throne of Glory — "And above the firmament… as the appearance of sapphire stone, like the image of a throne" (Ezekiel 1:26)
