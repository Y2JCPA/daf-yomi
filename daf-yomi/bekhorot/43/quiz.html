QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Aḥai's ruling, what is the correct procedure when testing tekhelet?
A. Use only Rav Adda's baking method — it is the more reliable of the two
B. Test with Rav Yitzḥak's soaking method first; if color holds, it's fit; if it fades, proceed to Rav Adda's test
C. Both tests must always be performed regardless of the outcome of the first
D. Either test alone is sufficient — use whichever is more convenient
Correct: B — Rav Aḥai ruled: test first with Rav Yitzḥak b. Rav Yehuda's soaking method. If color does not fade — fit, no further testing needed. If color fades, apply Rav Adda's baking test. If color then intensifies — fit; if it worsens — unfit.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rabbi Shimon's core reasoning for exempting women from the mitzva of tzitzit?
A. Women do not typically wear four-cornered garments
B. Tzitzit is a positive time-bound mitzva because nighttime garments are exempt, derived from 'that you may look upon it'
C. The word 'you shall make' in the Torah refers exclusively to men
D. Women are exempt from all positive commandments by rabbinic decree
Correct: B — Rabbi Shimon derives from 'that you may look upon it' that nighttime garments are exempt from tzitzit (one cannot see at night). This makes tzitzit time-bound (day only), and therefore — like all positive time-bound mitzvot — women are exempt.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why did the Sages prohibit selling a garment with ritual fringes to a gentile?
A. A gentile who wears tzitzit violates the prohibition of non-Jews performing Jewish mitzvot
B. The concern that the gentile will visit a prostitute while wearing the tzitzit, causing her to mistakenly think he is a righteous Jew
C. Tekhelet wool may not leave Jewish possession under any circumstances
D. The strings might be improperly knotted and mislead observers about the law
Correct: B — The Gemara explains: the concern is that the gentile will visit a prostitute while wearing the garment with tzitzit, and she may see the strings and assume he is a Torah scholar, thereby causing confusion or desecration.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How did Rabbi Meir derive that a person must recite one hundred blessings every day?
A. He counted all the blessings in Shacharit, Mincha, and Maariv and arrived at exactly one hundred
B. From the verse 'You shall bless the Lord your God' — the plural 'blessings' implies many
C. He read the word מה (what) in Deuteronomy 10:12 as מאה (one hundred)
D. From a tradition handed down orally from Moses at Sinai
Correct: C — Rabbi Meir interpreted 'And now Israel, what [מה] does the Lord your God require of you' — reading מה as מאה (one hundred) — as a Scriptural hint that one is obligated to recite one hundred blessings each day.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
When David stood naked in the bathhouse and despaired of having no mitzva, what restored his peace of mind?
A. He remembered that he had recited the Shema that morning
B. He recalled that circumcision, engraved in his flesh, was a mitzva he always carried with him
C. An angel reminded him that his intention to serve God counts as a mitzva
D. He looked at the mezuza on the bathhouse doorway
Correct: B — David lamented being stripped of all mitzvot. When he remembered the mitzva of circumcision that was in his flesh, his mind was put at ease — circumcision is a mitzva one carries constantly, even in the bathhouse.
