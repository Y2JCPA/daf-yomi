⚔️ Can Logic Alone Exclude the Tereifa from Sacrifice?

Rav Ashi
A verse is necessary because the a fortiori is refutable from the outset. The tereifa's general prohibition was permitted — bird sin offering pinching renders it fit despite tereifa-like slaughter. Blemished and caesarean animals were never so permitted.
Rav Aḥa Sava
A caesarean-born animal proves the inference survives: the Torah did not equate priest with animal for it, yet it remains unfit. This refutes your a fortiori just as well.
Rav Ashi
A caesarean animal is not sanctified with firstborn status — but a firstborn tereifa IS sanctified. The caesarean is therefore the weaker case and cannot refute the inference.
Rav Aḥa Sava
Then a blemished animal proves it — it does become sanctified as a firstborn, is permitted for consumption, yet prohibited to the Most High. The inference still collapses into circularity.
Rav Aḥa b. Rava
And what 'permitted prohibition' do you claim exists for tereifa? The bird burnt offering pinching does not count — the bird itself must be a tereifa for it to matter, and the sin offering pinching is what actually applies.
Rav Ashi
The common element of blemished and caesarean animals is that their prohibition was never permitted AND their disqualification is noticeable. A tereifa satisfies neither condition — so a verse is indispensable.
Conclusion Every attempt to derive the tereifa's disqualification by a fortiori reasoning is refuted. Three separate verses are ultimately required, each addressing a distinct scenario.

🐑 Three Animals and Their Key Distinctions

Blemished Animal (בעל מום)

  • Torah equated blemished priest with blemished animal
  • Becomes sanctified with firstborn status
  • Prohibition was never permitted for sacrifice
  • Blemish is visible and noticeable
  • Used as one side of the a fortiori argument

Caesarean-Born (יוצא דופן)

  • Torah did NOT equate priest with animal for it
  • NOT sanctified with firstborn status
  • Prohibition was never permitted for sacrifice
  • Weakness: less sanctified than tereifa firstborn
  • Cannot fully support the a fortiori

Tereifa (טריפה)

  • General prohibition WAS permitted (bird sin offering)
  • Firstborn tereifa IS sanctified
  • A fortiori inference collapses — verse required
  • Three different verses each needed
  • Disqualification is not always visible

📜 Why Three Separate Verses Exclude the Tereifa

Verse 1: "Well-watered pastures of Israel"
Excludes a tereifa that did not become injured before sanctification — i.e., it was injured after being dedicated. Without this verse one might think only pre-sanctification injuries disqualify.
↓
Verse 2: "Of the herd" (Leviticus 1:3)
Excludes a tereifa sanctified after injury — the owner dedicated an already-injured animal. Without this verse one might think only post-sanctification injury disqualifies.
↓
Verse 3: "Whatever passes under the rod" (Leviticus 27:32)
Excludes a tereifa in the context of animal tithe — all that pass are holy, except the tereifa. Covers injury occurring during the tithing process itself.
↓
All three are necessary
Each verse is the only source for its specific scenario. Remove any one and a leniency could be inferred for that case — the tereifa remains permitted for sacrifice in that situation.

✋ Ben Beteira vs. Rabbis: Can an Invalid Handful Be Returned?

Rabbis
Once a non-priest or unfit person removes the handful, the meal offering is disqualified. There is no remedy — the act of removal itself constitutes an invalid sacrificial rite.
Ben Beteira
If removed with the left hand: return the handful to the vessel and remove it again with the right hand. The verse 'he shall remove from there his handful' implies the possibility of a corrective removal.
Rav
Ben Beteira's logic extends further — even a non-priest who removed a handful may have it returned to the meal offering. Without this ruling one might limit Ben Beteira only to left-hand cases.
Rav Naḥman
The removal by an unfit person IS a sacrificial rite — but the rite is not complete until the handful is placed in a service vessel. Before placement: the handful may be returned. After placement: it cannot.
The Critical Threshold Placement in a service vessel (קידוש בכלי) completes and finalizes the handful rite. Before that moment, Ben Beteira and Rav permit correction; after it, the disqualification is sealed.
