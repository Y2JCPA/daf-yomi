🗣️ What Did Rav's Ruling Actually Cover?

Rav Ḥananel (citing Rav)
The halakha follows Rabbi Shimon ben Elazar — Rav endorsed the open manner of writing mezuza passages.
Rav Menashya bar Taḥlifa
No — Rav only ruled in accordance with Rabbi Shimon ben Elazar regarding the required margin space above and below the text, not the manner of writing.
Abaye
But Rav holds we follow established customs! The custom is the open manner — so surely he endorsed that too?
Rav Yosef
Rav's 'follow customs' principle applied to ḥalitza — using a sandal even if Elijah later rules otherwise. It is not a general rule for mezuza writing.
Final Ruling — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak L'chatchila it is a mitzva to write in the closed manner; b'di'eved the open manner is permitted.

📖 Closed (סתומה) vs. Open (פתוחה) Manner

✅ Closed Manner (סתומה)

  • Second passage begins on the same line immediately after the first ends
  • Preferred l'chatchila — fulfills the mitzva optimally
  • Endorsed by Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
  • Analogous to how a Torah scroll's sections are divided
  • A worn Torah scroll written this way may be repurposed as a mezuza

⚠️ Open Manner (פתוחה)

  • Second passage begins on a new line after the first
  • Permitted b'di'eved — valid after the fact
  • Rabbi Shimon ben Elazar's preferred style (reinterpreted)
  • Rav's ruling did not authorize this as the ideal
  • A Torah scroll written in this open style may also become a mezuza

⬇️ Can Worn Tefillin Be Repurposed as a Mezuza?

Tefillin parchment became worn — can we reduce sanctity → mezuza?
↓
What material were the tefillin written on?
Written on Klaf (parchment)
✅ Permitted
Klaf is valid for both tefillin and mezuza; repurposing is allowed
Written on Dokhsostos (inner hide)
❌ Unfit
Dokhsostos is invalid for tefillin — those tefillin were never valid to begin with
↓
Additional factor: Tefillin must be written on the flesh side; if written on the hair side they are unfit — and still cannot become a mezuza

🏠 Halakhot of Affixing the Mezuza

Must be affixed directly to the doorpost — Shmuel: a mezuza hung on a stick in the entranceway is unfit, as the verse requires it be 'upon your doorposts'
↓
Placed within the airspace of the entrance, not on the outside — fulfilling the verse "upon your gates" (seemingly obvious, but teaches it must be inside the space)
↓
Exception — House of Munbaz: They hung mezuzot on sticks in inns — not to fulfill the mitzva (travelers in inns are exempt), but only to protect the mezuza parchment
↓
Must be written with intent — if written casually 'in the manner of a missive' to a friend, the mezuza is unfit (Shmuel)
↓
Treating a Torah scroll with reverence: Rav Huna overturned a jug as a platform rather than sit on the same bed as a Torah scroll — Rabba bar bar Ḥana (citing R. Yoḥanan) disagrees and permits sitting on the bed
