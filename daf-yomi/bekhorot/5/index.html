🌾 Omer Removed Not For Its Own Sake

Rav
An omer meal offering from which a handful was removed not for its own sake is disqualified. The handful cannot be burned upon the altar.
Reish Lakish
It is valid — the handful may be burned on the altar. However, another omer must be brought afterward, since the remainder cannot be consumed by the priests until a proper omer is sacrificed.
Rav Adda bar Ahava
Supporting Reish Lakish: An offering is not considered 'before its time' if it will be brought on that same day. The new crop becomes permitted on the same day the omer is offered.
Rav Pappa
Reish Lakish's real reason: The illumination of the eastern horizon permits the new crop — not the omer sacrifice itself. Therefore the handful was fit for the altar from dawn onward.
Practical Consequence If Reish Lakish is correct, the handful from an improper-intent omer is burned on the altar, but the community must then bring an additional valid omer so the priests may eat the remainder.

⏰ The 'Before Its Time' Principle — Step by Step

Problem: Can the handful from a not-for-its-sake omer be burned on the altar when new crop is still forbidden?
↓
Rav Adda bar Ahava: An offering is NOT 'before its time' (לֹא הִגִּיעַ זְמַנוֹ) if it will be brought on that same day — so the handful is valid
↓
Objection from Leper Ritual
If oil is placed on leper's thumb before the blood, the placement must be redone — even though both happen the same day. Doesn't this contradict 'same-day is fine'?
Rav Pappa's Answer
Leper halakhot are different: the verse זֹאת תִּהְיֶה (Lev. 14:2) requires it to remain exactly as prescribed. This is an exception, not a disproof.
↓
Final ruling of Reish Lakish: The eastern horizon's illumination permits new crop — the handful was always fit. The 'same-day' principle is secondary.

🕊️ Birds vs. Meal Offerings — Unique Halakhot

🐦 Birds Only

  • An atonement-seeker (זָב, זָבָה, יוֹלֶדֶת) whose general prohibition was permitted for the Most High
  • Pinching (מְלִיקָה) at the nape of the neck is valid for the altar, even though it renders a non-sacred bird a forbidden carcass
  • No service vessel, waving (תְּנוּפָה), or bringing to the altar corner required
  • Prohibition created by sanctification itself — the bird was permitted before consecration

🌾 Meal Offerings Only

  • Require placement in a service vessel (כְּלִי שָׁרֵת)
  • Require waving (תְּנוּפָה) and bringing to the corner of the altar
  • Require salting before placement on the altar
  • General prohibition (new crop) was permitted when consecrated — the substance itself was always permitted
  • Disqualified by improper intent (לֹא לִשְׁמָהּ) of one fit for Temple service

🩸 Leper Purification — The Required Order

Step 1: Slaughter the leper's guilt offering (אָשָׁם) — this must come first
↓
Step 2: Place blood from the guilt offering on the leper's right thumb and right big toe
↓
Step 3: Place oil on the same spots — right thumb and right big toe
↓
⚠️ If the priest places oil before blood → the oil placement is halakhically void and must be fully redone
↓
Rav Pappa's Principle: Leper halakhot have a unique 'shall be' (תִּהְיֶה) expression — the ritual must remain exactly as originally prescribed, with no deviation in order
↓
Source: "זֹאת תִּהְיֶה תּוֹרַת הַמְּצֹרָע" (Vayikra 14:2) — 'This shall be the law of the leper' → it shall remain as it was at Sinai

🐄 Can Tereifa Be Disqualified by A Fortiori Inference?

Baraita
Could we derive that a tereifa is unfit for the altar via kal va-chomer? A blemished animal — permitted for ordinary consumption — is disqualified. How much more so a tereifa — which is forbidden for ordinary people!
Refutation
Not so! Fat (חֵלֶב) and blood (דָּם) are forbidden to ordinary people yet are offered on the altar. 'Forbidden to ordinary people' does NOT guarantee disqualification from the altar.
Counter
Fat and blood come from a fundamentally permitted animal. Tereifa is itself forbidden — the source animal is prohibited.
Counter-Counter
Bird pinching (מְלִיקָה) proves otherwise: the act of pinching renders the bird entirely forbidden for ordinary consumption, yet a pinched bird offering is valid on the altar!
Final Rejection
Pinching differs — its sanctity itself creates the prohibition. Therefore we need the explicit verse: "מִן הַבָּקָר" — 'from the herd' (Lev. 1:3) — to exclude the tereifa.
Bottom Line Every proposed a fortiori inference to disqualify tereifa can be defeated by a counterexample. An explicit scriptural derivation is therefore required.
