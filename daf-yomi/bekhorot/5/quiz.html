QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Reish Lakish, what is the halakhic status of an omer meal offering from which a handful was removed not for its own sake?
A. Entirely disqualified — nothing from it may be used on the altar
B. The handful is valid and burned on the altar, but another omer must then be brought
C. Valid only if the Kohen Gadol performed the removal
D. The handful must be returned and the removal repeated with proper intent
Correct: B — Reish Lakish rules the handful is valid and burned on the altar. However, since the remainder cannot be consumed by the priests without a proper omer, a second omer must be brought — making this a valid but incomplete sacrifice.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rav Adda bar Ahava's principle for why the handful from a not-for-its-sake omer is not considered 'before its time'?
A. An offering brought in error is retroactively treated as if brought with proper intent
B. An offering is not classified as 'before its time' if it will be brought on that same day
C. The Kohen's intent is irrelevant once the handful has been removed from the vessel
D. The omer's handful is always valid regardless of the priest's intent
Correct: B — Rav Adda bar Ahava teaches that שֶׁלֹּא הִגִּיעַ זְמַנוֹ does not apply when the offering's appointed time arrives on the very same day — so from dawn of the 16th of Nisan, the handful is considered fit.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does Rav Pappa derive from the phrase זֹאת תִּהְיֶה תּוֹרַת הַמְּצֹרָע (Leviticus 14:2)?
A. The leper's guilt offering must be worth at least two silver shekels
B. The leper's purification ritual must remain exactly as prescribed — the order of blood before oil is absolute
C. The leper is exempt from the omer offering in the year of his purification
D. The term 'shall be' teaches that the ritual applies even during the Sabbatical Year
Correct: B — Rav Pappa derives from תִּהְיֶה ('shall be') that the leper's ritual must remain 'as it was' at Sinai — the prescribed order is fixed. If oil is placed before blood, the placement is void and must be redone, unlike other same-day sequences which Rav Adda bar Ahava would permit.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which of the following is cited in the baraita as a halakha that applies to BIRDS but NOT to meal offerings?
A. Requires placement in a service vessel (כְּלִי שָׁרֵת)
B. Requires waving (תְּנוּפָה) before placement on the altar
C. An atonement-seeker (zav/zavah/yoledet) whose general prohibition was permitted for the Most High
D. Must be salted before being placed on the altar fire
Correct: C — The baraita teaches that birds have a unique rule regarding atonement-seekers (those requiring a sin offering before being permitted in the Temple): the general prohibition was permitted for the Most High. This applies to birds (whose atonement involves bird offerings) but not to meal offerings. Service vessels, waving, and salting are meal offering requirements.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why does the Gemara conclude that the disqualification of tereifa from the altar cannot be derived through a kal va-chomer from blemished animals?
A. Because tereifa animals are in a separate biblical category from blemished animals
B. Because the Sages enacted a rabbinic fence forbidding such logical derivations for Temple law
C. Because bird pinching shows that even items made forbidden by their own sacrifice-act can be valid on the altar — every a fortiori argument can be refuted
D. Because the Torah explicitly permits tereifa in the context of certain communal offerings
Correct: C — The Gemara shows that melikah (bird pinching) creates a counterexample: pinching renders the bird entirely forbidden for ordinary consumption, yet the offering is valid on the altar. This defeats the a fortiori logic. Since every proposed inference can be similarly refuted, an explicit verse — מִן הַבָּקָר (Lev. 1:3) — is required to exclude tereifa.
