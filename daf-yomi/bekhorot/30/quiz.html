Bekhorot 30 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav, where may a scribe finish writing one of the five books within a Torah scroll?
A. Only at the very end of the entire scroll
B. Only at the end of a column
C. Anywhere in the column, even the middle
D. Only at the start of a new column
Correct: C — Rav rules that a scribe may finish any of the five books anywhere in the column, even mid-column. Rav Ashi is more stringent, requiring conclusion at the end of the column specifically.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Shimon's final resolution, who wrote the last eight verses of the Torah?
A. Moses wrote them before his death, weeping
B. Ezra restored them from memory after the exile
C. God wrote them directly on the parchment
D. God dictated them and Joshua wrote them after Moses died
Correct: D — Rabbi Shimon explains: up to the death verse, God dictated and Moses wrote (with tears). From the death verse onward, God dictated and Joshua wrote — Moses could not have written about his own death.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the minimum number of columns permitted on a Torah scroll sheet (except the final sheet)?
A. One column
B. Two columns
C. Three columns
D. Four columns
Correct: C — A standard Torah scroll sheet must contain at least three columns. Fewer than three is prohibited. The exception is the final sheet of the scroll, which may contain even one verse or one letter.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
If a scribe omits God's Name and writes the next word, what is the correct correction procedure according to Rabbi Yosei?
A. Discard the entire sheet and begin again
B. Strike through the word and write God's Name above it
C. Scrape off the subsequent word, suspend it above the line, and write God's Name in the cleared space
D. Leave the error and add a marginal note
Correct: C — The procedure is: (1) scrape off the words written after the omission, (2) suspend those words above the line, (3) write God's Name in the cleared space below. Rav Ḥananel rules the halakha follows this approach.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rav compare to 'snatching a mitzva in the marketplace'?
A. Reading from someone else's Torah scroll in synagogue
B. Purchasing a Torah scroll rather than writing one yourself
C. Hiring a scribe to write a mezuzah on your behalf
D. Reciting someone else's blessings aloud
Correct: B — Rav teaches that buying a pre-written Torah scroll is like snatching a mitzva — expedient but spiritually diminished. The ideal fulfillment of the mitzva of writing a Torah scroll is to write it oneself.
