✍️ Who Wrote the Final 8 Verses of the Torah?

Rabbi Yehuda
Moses himself wrote the entire Torah including the account of his own death — he wrote them beDema, with tears. A Torah scroll cannot be missing even a single letter, so Moses must have written it all.
Rabbi Shimon
Is it possible that a Torah scroll was missing even one letter while Moses was alive? Impossible! Yet equally impossible is that Moses wrote 'And Moses died there' while still living. Therefore Joshua wrote the last eight verses.
Rabbi Shimon (resolution)
Rather: God dictated and Moses wrote the whole Torah up to the death verse — with tears (beDema). From that verse onward, God dictated and Joshua wrote and read it aloud. This preserves Torah's completeness.
Gemara
Rav's ruling that the last 8 verses are read by a single reader aligns with Rabbi Yehuda — since according to Rabbi Shimon, those verses are like any other Torah portion and should follow standard laining rules.
⚖️ Rav's Practical Ruling Rav (via Rabbi Yehoshua bar Abba → Rav Giddel): The last 8 verses of the Torah (Deut. 34:5–12) must be read by a single person in the synagogue and may not be divided between two readers.

📜 How and Where to Finish Writing a Torah Scroll

Rav's ruling: A scribe finishing one of the five books may conclude writing anywhere in the column — even mid-column. This is the halakha.
↓
Gemara's clarification: Rav spoke about finishing one of the five individual books within a scroll, not the scroll as a whole — so mid-column is permitted for each book's ending.
↓
The Rabbis: One may finish in the middle of a line or at the end of a line — both are equally valid. No specific position is mandated.
↓
Rav Ashi (stringent): The Torah scroll must be finished specifically at the end of the column — the most structured and dignified conclusion.
↓
Related teaching (Rav): One who purchases a Sefer Torah in the marketplace is like one who snatches a mitzva. The ideal is to write the Torah oneself — buying shortcuts the spiritual act.

✡️ A Scribe Omits God's Name — Correction Procedure

Scribe omits writing God's Name (השם) and inadvertently writes the next word in its place
↓
Step 1: Scrape off (מגרד) the word(s) written after the omission
↓
Step 2: Suspend the scraped words above the line (hang them there)
↓
Step 3: Write God's Name in the cleared space below
↓
✅ Rabbi Yosei / Rav (via Rav Ḥananel)
Halakha: Suspend the Name above the line — this correction is valid
✅ Rabbi Shimon Shezuri
May suspend the entire Name above the line, but not a partial Name
❌ Rabbi Shimon ben Elazar (R. Meir)
May not suspend the Name above the line at all — requires full rewrite

⚖️ Which Ruling Did Rabbi Ḥanina Endorse for Rabbi Shimon Shezuri?

❌ Not: God's Name suspension

  • Rabbi Ḥanina said halakha follows R. Shimon Shezuri
  • But this was already covered by Rav Ḥananel's ruling
  • No need to restate it — so this isn't the referent

❌ Not: Ḥullin 74b (limb of living animal)

  • Ze'eiri already cited R. Ḥanina on that mishna
  • Redundant to state it again
  • Ruled out as the referent

❌ Not: Gittin 65b (kolar/divorce case)

  • Rabbi Yoḥanan already ruled on that mishna
  • Also not the referent
  • Systematically eliminated

✅ Likely: A chest or wine case

  • Rav Pappa: referent is the case of a chest
  • Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak: referent is a case involving wine
  • Gemara's final position — not yet fully resolved at daf's end
