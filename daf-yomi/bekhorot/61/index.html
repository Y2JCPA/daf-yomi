🌾 Who Taught That First Fruits Require Waving?

R. Eliezer b. Ya'akov
First fruits require waving — derived by gezera shava (verbal analogy) on the word 'hand': just as the priest waves the peace offering, so too the priest waves the basket of first fruits.
R. Yehuda
First fruits require waving — derived from the verse 'And you shall set it down before the Lord your God' (Deut. 26:10). Since placement before the altar is already stated earlier, 'setting down' here implies a waving motion.
Rava
The Mishna could have cited R. Yehuda — but preferred R. Eliezer ben Ya'akov because his source verse appears first in the Torah portion dealing with first fruits.
Bottom Line Both agree first fruits require waving. The Mishna attributes the ruling to R. Eliezer ben Ya'akov as an editorial preference, not a legal disagreement.

🙌 How Is Waving (Tenufah) Performed?

Step 1: Place the two loaves on top of the two lambs (for Shavuot two-loaf offering)
↓
Step 2: Priest places both hands beneath both the loaves and the lambs simultaneously
↓
Step 3: Extend the offerings forward to each of the four directions — east, south, west, north — bringing back to center each time
↓
Step 4: Raise upward and lower downward — symbolizing God's sovereignty over all creation
↓
📍 Location: Waving is performed to the east of the altar. The phrase 'before the Lord' in the context of waving refers east — unlike meal offerings where it refers west.

🧩 Waving Obligation: Who Is Included?

Male Jews (Israelites)

  • ✅ Peace offerings require waving
  • ✅ Owner waves the offering personally
  • ✅ Also obligated in placing hands (semicha)
  • Full obligation — all three acts apply

Women

  • ✅ Women's peace offerings require waving
  • ⚠️ Priest performs waving on their behalf
  • ❌ Not obligated in semicha (placing hands)
  • R. Yosei: tenufah applies because priest waves anyway

Converts & Freed Slaves

  • ✅ Waving obligation applies to them
  • Derived: 'children of Israel' extended by verse
  • ❌ Not obligated in semicha
  • Priest performs waving on their behalf

🤲 Rabbi Yosei's Key Distinction: Why Waving Extends Further Than Placing Hands Placing hands (semicha) is performed by the owner alone on a living animal before slaughter — it is an intimate, owner-specific act. Therefore the Torah limits it to male Jews only.

Waving (tenufah) is performed by the priest — even for male Jewish owners. Since the priest performs it regardless of the owner's identity, there is no basis to distinguish between male Jews, women, converts, or freed slaves. All their peace offerings require waving.

The Gemara confirms: 'Can we differentiate between male Jews and others with regard to waving? Since waving is also performed by priests, there is no reason to distinguish.'
