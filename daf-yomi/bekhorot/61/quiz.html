Bekhorot 61 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, which two items associated with a recovered leper's purification require waving?
A. The two birds and the cedar wood
B. The log of oil and the guilt offering
C. The sin offering and the burnt offering
D. The meal offering and the guilt offering
Correct: B — The Mishna teaches that both the log of oil and the guilt offering of a recovered leper require waving, as derived from Leviticus 14:12 — 'And the priest shall take one of the lambs and wave it.'
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How is the waving ritual physically performed according to the Mishna (61a)?
A. Raise and lower only, facing east toward the altar
B. Wave side to side three times while reciting a blessing
C. Extend to four directions bringing back each time, then raise and lower
D. Circle the altar holding the offering, then hand it to the priest
Correct: C — The Mishna describes: place hands beneath the offering, extend toward all four directions (bringing back each time), then raise upward and lower — symbolizing divine sovereignty over all directions and heaven/earth.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Which two offerings require BOTH waving (tenufah) AND bringing near (hagasha)?
A. The two loaves and the Shavuot peace offering lambs
B. The omer meal offering and the sota's meal offering
C. First fruits and the individual peace offering
D. The leper's log of oil and the guilt offering
Correct: B — The Mishna explicitly states that the omer meal offering and the meal offering of the sota (suspected adulteress) require both waving and bringing near — unlike most offerings which require only one of these acts.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is Rabbi Yosei's reasoning that women's peace offerings require waving even though women are exempt from placing hands (semicha)?
A. Women are treated identically to men in all Temple service matters
B. The verse explicitly names women in the waving requirement
C. Waving is performed by the priest regardless — so there is no basis to distinguish between owner types
D. Women's offerings are subject to more stringent requirements than men's
Correct: C — R. Yosei argues: semicha is an owner-exclusive act on a living animal, hence limited to male Jews. But tenufah is performed by the priest even for male owners — so there is no reason to exclude women, converts, or freed slaves from this requirement.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rava explains why the Mishna attributes the waving of first fruits to Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov rather than Rabbi Yehuda. What is his reason?
A. Rabbi Yehuda's opinion is rejected by all later authorities
B. Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov's rulings are always given priority in the Mishna
C. The verse Rabbi Eliezer ben Ya'akov derives from appears earlier in the Torah portion on first fruits
D. Rabbi Yehuda's derivation only appears in a Tosefta, not a Mishnaic source
Correct: C — Rava says the Mishna could have cited either authority, but preferred R. Eliezer ben Ya'akov because the verse he uses as his source ('And you shall wave it') appears earlier in Deuteronomy 26 than the verse R. Yehuda relies upon.
