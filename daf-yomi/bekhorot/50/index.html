🐑 What Happens When Morning Tamid Is Missed?

Mishna
If they did not sacrifice a lamb in the morning, they should sacrifice one in the afternoon.
Gemara
The mishna is incomplete — it means: if the morning was skipped on a new uninitiated altar, the afternoon is also not offered.
Rabbi Shimon
When does this apply? Only when the altar had not yet been initiated. If the priests were prevented or forgot (unintentional), the afternoon Tamid is still brought.
Rava
If priests intentionally failed to sacrifice the morning Tamid, those same priests are barred from the afternoon — but other innocent priests may serve in their place.
Principle Intentional sin punishes the sinning priests personally — it does not permanently ground the altar or punish those who are innocent.

🏛️ Initiating Service on a New Golden Altar

New golden (inner) altar installed
No service has yet been performed on it
↓
Which service inaugurates it?
Opinion A
Morning incense
Derived from: Aaron shall burn incense each morning (Exodus 30:7)
Abaye — Opinion B
Afternoon incense
Candelabrum ashes removed each morning prove lamps were lit the previous evening — so the first act was an afternoon lighting
↓
Both agree: The outer altar and the new Table are both initiated by their respective morning services first

🕯️ Voluntary Incense Offerings — Who May Bring?

Individual

  • ❌ Cannot bring voluntary incense on the inner (golden) altar
  • ❌ Cannot bring voluntary incense on the outer altar either
  • No individual has an obligatory incense offering — so no model for a voluntary one
  • The Nasi's gift at the Tabernacle was a one-time exception, not normative law
  • Verse: 'You shall not offer strange incense thereon' bars all unauthorized incense

Community

  • ✅ Obligated to burn incense on the inner altar daily (fixed communal offering)
  • ❌ Cannot bring extra voluntary incense on the inner altar
  • ❌ Cannot bring voluntary incense on the outer altar either
  • Rav Pappa: the baraita uses lo tzricha style — 'it need not even be said'
  • No halakhic precedent for adding to the fixed communal incense service

👑 High Priest Succession — The Chavitin Dilemma

Each day: The High Priest brings one complete isaron (tenth of an ephah) of fine flour from his own home
↓
Divides it in half: He offers half in the morning service — the other half is set aside for afternoon
↓
Crisis: The High Priest dies after the morning half is offered but before the afternoon
↓
Appointment: A replacement High Priest is immediately appointed to fill the role
↓
Replacement's duty: He brings a new complete isaron from his own house, divides it in half, and sacrifices one half in the afternoon
↓
Result: Two halves are sacrificed (one from each Kohen Gadol) + two halves are discarded — neither half from either Kohen Gadol is ever eaten, since the full offering belongs to fire
