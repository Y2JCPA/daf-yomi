🕎 The Candelabrum: Structure, Materials & Indispensability

Mishna: The seven branches and seven lamps of the Menorah — each prevents fulfillment of the mitzva with the others. Same applies to the four passages in tefillin.
↓
Material Rule: The Candelabrum must be fashioned from a complete block of gold — fragments of gold render it unfit, but other metals are fit.
↓
Great Dispute: Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi (wood unfit) vs. Rabbi Yosei son of Rabbi Yehuda (wood fit) — two different hermeneutical principles applied to the same verse.
↓
Trumpets: Moses's trumpets were fit only for his generation — 'make for you' (Numbers 10:2) limits them to his era.
↓
Shmuel's Description: The Menorah stood exactly 18 handbreadths tall — base, bare sections, branching knobs, and ornamental top all specified precisely.

🌿 Resolving the Contradiction: Sprinkling Not for Its Own Sake

Rav Yosef
No difficulty. The first baraita follows Rabbi Eliezer, who juxtaposes the leper's guilt-offering with his burnt-offering — so sprinkling not for its own sake is valid. The second baraita follows the Rabbis, who do not make this juxtaposition.
Gemara
But there is a principle: in consecrated matters, a halakha derived via juxtaposition does not itself teach another halakha via juxtaposition! Rabbi Eliezer's derivation cannot extend this far.
Rava
Both baraitot follow the Rabbis. Where the first baraita says 'valid' — it means the offering is permitted for consumption. The sprinkling not for its own sake allowed the meat to be eaten, but the mitzva obligation itself was not fulfilled.
Rava's Key Distinction 'Valid' (כשר) = the offering may be consumed — not that the obligatory mitzva is discharged. Both baraitot are entirely consistent with the Rabbinic view.

⚖️ Candelabrum Materials: Two Hermeneutical Approaches

Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi

  • Principle: Generalizations & Details (כלל ופרט)
  • "Make a Candelabrum" = general (כלל)
  • "of pure gold" = detail (פרט)
  • Rule: Only what matches the detail applies
  • Result: Only metal is valid
  • Wood is unfit — Temple vessels from wood: unfit

Rabbi Yosei son of Rabbi Yehuda

  • Principle: Amplifications & Restrictions (ריבוי ומיעוט)
  • "Make a Candelabrum" = amplification (ריבוי — anything)
  • "of pure gold" = restriction (מיעוט — gold specifically)
  • Rule: Everything not the restriction remains included
  • Result: Even wood is fit
  • Cites the Hasmonean kings who used wooden candelabra

🔨 Candelabrum Material: Fit or Unfit?

What material was used to fashion the Candelabrum?
↓
Complete block of gold
✅ Fit — the ideal and required form
Fragments of gold
❌ Unfit — must be beaten from a single whole block (עשת)
Other metals (silver, tin, lead)
✅ Fit — derived from passive 'will be made' (יֵעָשֶׂה) in Exodus 25:31
Wood
⚠️ Disputed
R. Yehuda HaNasi: unfit
R. Yosei b. R. Yehuda: fit

📐 Shmuel's Description of the Menorah — 18 Handbreadths Total

3 handbreadths: The base and the flower that was upon the base
↓
2 handbreadths bare — unadorned central shaft rising above the base
↓
1 handbreadth knob — from which two branches emerge, one to each side, rising up to the full height of the Menorah
↓
1 handbreadth bare + 1 handbreadth knob — second pair of branches emerges here
↓
1 handbreadth bare + 1 handbreadth knob — third pair of branches emerges here (six total branch ends)
↓
3 handbreadths at top of central shaft: Three goblets, a knob, and a flower — completing the central branch
↓
Goblets: like Alexandrian goblets (long and narrow). Knobs: like Cherethite apples. Flowers: like those of Crete. Total: 18 handbreadths.

📋 Temurah 28 — Summary Map

Mishna Foundation: Seven branches + seven lamps of the Menorah — each is indispensable (מעכב את חבירו)
↓
Same principle extends to tefillin passages (4 each) and mezuza passages (2) — absence of any one invalidates the whole
↓
Material Dispute:
Gold block = ideal ✅
Gold fragments = unfit ❌
Other metals = fit ✅
Wood = disputed ⚠️
Trumpets Exception:
Moses's trumpets were fit only for his generation — 'make for you' (Numbers 10:2) restricted them to his era alone
↓
Hasmonean Precedent: Kings used wood → then iron skewers → then silver → finally gold as prosperity grew. Rabbi Yosei b. R. Yehuda cites this approvingly; Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi rejects wood as a valid material.
