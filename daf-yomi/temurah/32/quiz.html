Temurah 32 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak, what is the preferred ab initio format for writing a mezuza's passages?
A. Open (פתוחה) — second passage begins on a new line after the first
B. Closed (סתומה) — second passage follows the first on the same line
C. Either format is equally valid ab initio
D. Each passage must be written on a separate piece of parchment
Correct: B — Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak rules it is a mitzva ab initio to use the closed (סתומה) format, though writing in the open (פתוחה) manner is valid post facto.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Rav Huna overturn a jug before placing a Torah scroll on his bed?
A. To keep the scroll from rolling off the bed
B. To elevate and honor the Torah scroll above the sleeping area
C. To keep the scroll separated from any impurity in the bedding
D. Both B and C — elevation for honor and separation from impurity
Correct: D — Rav Huna's act of placing the Torah on an overturned jug served both to elevate it (showing honor) and to separate it from the space where he sat — a stringency beyond Rabbi Yoḥanan's lenient ruling.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
If a mezuza was hung on a stick placed in the doorway rather than affixed to the doorpost, what is the ruling?
A. Valid, since it is within the entrance space
B. Valid only if it faces the interior of the room
C. Unfit — the verse requires writing 'upon the doorposts of your house'
D. Valid post facto but should be corrected going forward
Correct: C — Rav Yehuda in the name of Shmuel rules such a mezuza is unfit. The verse 'And you shall write them upon the doorposts of your house' requires physical affixing to the post itself, not merely placement nearby.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Which ruling about parchment scoring (שרטוט) is correct according to the Gemara's conclusion?
A. Both phylacteries and mezuzot require scoring; Torah scrolls do not
B. Torah scrolls and mezuzot require scoring; phylacteries do not
C. Only Torah scrolls require scoring; mezuzot and phylacteries do not
D. All three require scoring equally
Correct: B — The Gemara concludes: phylacteries do NOT require scoring, but a mezuza DOES require scoring (ruling lines). Torah scrolls also require scoring. This distinguishes phylacteries from the other two.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why did the household of King Munbaz hang mezuzot on sticks in their inns?
A. To fulfill the mitzva of mezuza even while traveling
B. Because they believed hanging on sticks was a valid method
C. They were exempt as temporary guests but did so as a personal reminder
D. To show that the mitzva applies even in inns one does not own
Correct: C — One who sleeps in an inn is exempt from mezuza, as it is not a permanent residence. The household of King Munbaz hung mezuzot on sticks not to fulfill the mitzva (which would in any case be unfit) but as a pious personal reminder.
