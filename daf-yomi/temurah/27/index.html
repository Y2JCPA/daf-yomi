🔗 The Principle of Mutual Indispensability (מְעַכֵּב)

Core Rule: The absence of even the minority of a required component disqualifies the entire offering
↓
Handful (קֹמֶץ): derived from double mention of "his handful" in Leviticus — full handful always required
↓
Fine flour + oil: mutually indispensable — juxtaposed in the verse "The priest shall take"
↓
Handful + frankincense: mutually indispensable — the pair is mentioned together twice in the Torah
↓
Extended to: Yom Kippur goats, Shavuot sheep, shewbread arrangements, nazirite loaves, four lulav species, and all seven sprinklings

🌿 Must the Four Lulav Species Be Bound Together?

Rabbis
A lulav is fit whether or not it is bound with the myrtle and willow. The four species do NOT need to be held as a single bundle for the mitzva to be fulfilled.
Rabbi Yehuda
If it is not bound, it is unfit. A gezeirah shavah (verbal analogy) links the 'taking' of the lulav to the 'taking' of the hyssop bundle at the Paschal offering — just as there the hyssop must be a bundle, so too the lulav must be bound.
Rabbis
The Paschal text explicitly says 'bundle of hyssop' — but the lulav passage says only 'taking.' No bundle is specified, so the analogy cannot obligate bundling for validity.
Gemara (reconciling baraita)
There IS a mitzva to bind the lulav even for the Rabbis — not for validity, but due to hiddur mitzvah: 'This is my God and I will glorify Him' (Exodus 15:2). We beautify the performance of commandments.
Bottom Line Rabbis: binding is a mitzva (beautification) but not required for validity. Rabbi Yehuda: binding is required for the mitzva to be fulfilled at all.

🏛️ Prohibition of Entering the Holy of Holies: Two Views

The Rabbis

  • Both the Sanctuary (Heichal) AND within the Curtain (Holy of Holies) are governed by the prohibition 'he shall not come at all times'
  • Violating either space carries death at the hand of Heaven
  • The verse mentions both zones because each refers to a distinct prohibited space
  • 'El penei the Ark Cover' establishes a paradigm (school of R. Eliezer b. Yaakov) — not a restriction to direct entry only

Rabbi Yehuda

  • Only entering within the Curtain (the Holy of Holies) triggers death at the hand of Heaven
  • Entering the Sanctuary (Heichal) alone violates a prohibition — punishable only by lashes
  • The term el is used specifically: liability for death applies only when entering directly 'before the Ark Cover'
  • Entry through a roundabout path is excluded from the death penalty by the precise language of the verse

זֶה אֵלִי וְאַנְוֵהוּ — This is my God and I will beautify Him (Shemot 15:2)
✨ Key Concept: Hiddur Mitzvah The Gemara teaches that even the Rabbis — who hold that binding the lulav is not required for validity — agree there is a mitzva to bind the myrtle and willow to the lulav. The source is: "This is my God and I will glorify Him" (Exodus 15:2), meaning we beautify the performance of commandments beyond the strict minimum. This principle of hiddur mitzvah motivates countless enhancements in halakhic practice, from beautiful mezuzot to decorated Torah scrolls. The lulav binding is a paradigm case: not required, but a genuine religious obligation of beautification.
