5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yehuda, what is the ruling if a priest intends during a rite to leave the blood of the offering until the next day?
A. The offering is fit, since intent alone cannot invalidate
B. The offering is unfit, and all authorities agree on this
C. Only Rabbi Eliezer deems it unfit; the Rabbis disagree
D. The offering must be brought again with new blood
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda holds that intent to leave the blood overnight is halakhically equivalent to actually leaving it — and on this, even the Rabbis would concede that the offering is unfit.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why did Rabbi Elazar ben Shammua's eyes stream with tears during the exchange with Yosef the Babylonian?
A. Because Yosef had publicly humiliated him before his students
B. Because the offering had already been sacrificed under his mistaken ruling
C. Because he realized he had been teaching an inaccurate ruling, and Yosef's persistence corrected him
D. Because Yosef refused to accept any ruling about the offering
Correct: C — Rabbi Elazar wept upon recognizing that his repeated ruling ('the offering is fit') had been inaccurate. He was moved by Yosef's love of Torah — asking the same question until the true ruling emerged.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does the Mishna rule if a priest did not pour oil onto the meal offering before presenting it?
A. The meal offering is valid (fit) after the fact
B. The meal offering is entirely invalid and must be redone
C. The handful may not be removed until oil is added
D. The priest is disqualified from future service
Correct: A — The Mishna teaches that failure to pour oil does not invalidate the meal offering. Several preparatory steps — including pouring, mixing, breaking, and salting — may be omitted without disqualifying the offering.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Rav Naḥman resolve the apparent contradiction between the Mishna (non-priest pours oil = fit) and Rabbi Shimon's baraita (pouring is a priestly rite)?
A. Rabbi Shimon's view is rejected as a minority opinion
B. The Mishna applies only to the omer offering, not standard meal offerings
C. Rabbi Shimon refers to the priest's meal offering; the Mishna discusses an Israelite's meal offering
D. The Mishna speaks of the outer altar; Rabbi Shimon speaks of the inner altar
Correct: C — Rav Naḥman distinguishes by context: Rabbi Shimon's baraita addresses a priest's meal offering (which has no handful removal and is entirely burned), while the Mishna discusses an Israelite's meal offering, which follows different rules.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does Rabbi Shimon's baraita say about a priest who denies the validity of the meal offering rites listed therein?
A. He must bring an atonement offering
B. He is disqualified from performing any future Temple service
C. He is flogged for transgressing a rabbinic prohibition
D. He does not receive a share in the priestly gifts
Correct: D — Rabbi Shimon's baraita teaches that any priest who denies the validity of the sacrificial rites entrusted to Aaron's sons — including the meal offering rites — forfeits his portion in the priestly gifts distributed among the priests.
