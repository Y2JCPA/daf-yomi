Temurah 11 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the mishna need to teach all three cases — stone, salt, and frankincense — as items that disqualify the handful?
A. Each case teaches something that cannot be derived from the others
B. To enumerate the most common priestly errors
C. Because the Torah explicitly lists all three items together
D. To show that only these three items can ever diminish the handful
Correct: A — The Gemara demonstrates that each case is indispensable: a stone (unfit for any sacred use) cannot teach about salt (required in offerings), and neither can teach about frankincense (itself sacred). All three are needed.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav Zutra bar Toviyya citing Rav, how does the priest form the kometz?
A. He scoops with an open palm dragged along the side of the vessel
B. He bends his middle three fingers until their tips reach over his palm
C. He uses only his thumb and forefinger in a pinching motion
D. He fills his hand to overflowing and then scrapes off the excess with a knife
Correct: B — Rav Zutra bar Toviyya says that Rav says: The priest bends the middle three fingers until the tips fold over the palm — this is the normal, natural way a person takes a handful.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the specific role of the little finger (pinky) in the kometz procedure?
A. It is the primary scooping finger and does most of the work
B. It is excluded entirely — the kometz uses only four fingers
C. It is used solely for leveling — wiping away overflow from below the handful
D. It steadies the vessel while the other fingers scoop
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that the little finger is used only for leveling (chalifah): after the handful is taken with all fingers, the little finger wipes away any flour overflowing beneath the handful, ensuring it is not overfull.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rabbi Yoḥanan reports a three-way tannaitic dispute. What is the core disagreement between Rabbi Meir, Rabbi Yehuda, and Rabbi Shimon regarding frankincense?
A. Whether frankincense must be added before or after the handful of flour is removed
B. The minimum weight of frankincense that constitutes a valid offering
C. Whether the word 'all' in the verse requires the entire frankincense to be present and burned
D. Whether non-sacred frankincense can be substituted for consecrated frankincense
Correct: C — All three interpret 've'et kol ha-levonah' (and all the frankincense) differently. Rabbi Meir requires the full amount at every stage; Rabbi Yehuda is lenient; Rabbi Shimon is strict. The dispute turns on what 'all' demands.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Rami bar Ḥama rules about extra frankincense that was separated for a meal offering but subsequently lost. What is the decisive factor in his ruling?
A. Whether the lost portion was greater or less than an olive-bulk (kazayit)
B. Whether the loss occurred before or after the kometz of flour was removed
C. Whether the High Priest or an ordinary priest was performing the service
D. Whether the loss occurred inside or outside the Temple courtyard
Correct: B — Rami bar Ḥama rules that if the extra frankincense was lost before the kometz removal, it was never 'fixed' (sanctified together with the meal offering), so the remainder is sufficient. If lost after, it disqualifies the offering.
