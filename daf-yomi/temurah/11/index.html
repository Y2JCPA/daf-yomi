🪨 Why All Three Cases Are Necessary

Stone

  • Not fit for sacrifice at all
  • Clearly diminishes the kometz
  • Teaches the basic rule
  • But cannot teach about salt or frankincense — too dissimilar

Grain of Salt

  • Required in all offerings (Lev. 2:13)
  • But salted onto the handful separately, not the whole offering
  • One might think it belongs in the handful
  • Unique status requires its own explicit ruling

Pinch of Frankincense

  • Sacred — part of the offering itself
  • Placed atop the flour before kometz
  • One might think it belongs inside the handful
  • Must be taught: it too disqualifies by displacement

🤲 The Proper Kometz Technique (Rav Zutra b. Toviyya citing Rav)

Insert: Priest places entire hand flat into the meal offering flour
↓
Grip: Bends the three middle fingers until fingertips fold over the palm — not fingertips only, not full fist
↓
Level top: Thumb presses down from above to prevent overflow
↓
Level bottom: Little finger wipes away any excess from beneath
↓
Result: Kometz shalem — exact measure, neither lacking nor overflowing. This is the hardest meal-offering rite.

🗣️ Abaye & Rava on the Hardest Temple Rite

Abaye
How do priests properly remove the handful? I raise an objection: every finger has a distinct ritual role — the middle finger measures a cubit, the forefinger sprinkles blood. How is the little finger involved at all?
Rava
They remove it as people normally remove a handful — folding fingers over the palm. The little finger is used only for leveling the kometz from below, not for the removal itself.
Gemara
Is kometz truly the hardest sacrificial rite? What about melikah (pinching a bird's nape) or the High Priest scooping incense with both hands on Yom Kippur?
Resolution
Kometz is the hardest rite among meal offering services. Melikah and the Yom Kippur incense scoop (chafina) are separate categories with their own difficulties.
Baraita 'From there his handful' — one might think overflowing; 'with his handful' — one might think fingertips only. The verse teaches: exact measure, using the natural grip.
