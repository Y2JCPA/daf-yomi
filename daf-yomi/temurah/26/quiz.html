Temurah 26 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav, when does Rabbi Yehoshua's ruling (remainder impure = unfit) apply?
A. When any part of the remainder becomes impure
B. When all of the remainder becomes impure
C. When the handful itself becomes impure before burning
D. Only when the remainder is lost, not when it is impure
Correct: B — Rav limits Rabbi Yehoshua's ruling strictly to cases where all of the remainder became impure. If only part became impure, the offering remains fit and the intact portion is sacrificed.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the minimum that must remain for a priest to be permitted to sprinkle the blood of an offering?
A. Half an olive-bulk of meat plus half an olive-bulk of fat (they combine)
B. An olive-bulk of meat OR an olive-bulk of fat (either alone suffices)
C. A full olive-bulk of both meat and fat together
D. Two full olive-bulks of any permitted portion
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehoshua rules that an olive-bulk of meat OR an olive-bulk of fat is each independently sufficient. Half an olive-bulk of each does NOT combine to permit sprinkling.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the textual basis Rabbi Yehuda son of Rabbi Ḥiyya gives for Rabbi Shimon's leniency on service vessel sanctification?
A. 'And Aaron's sons shall sprinkle the blood' (Lev. 1:11)
B. 'It is most holy, as the sin offering and as the guilt offering' (Lev. 6:10)
C. 'For a pleasing aroma to the Lord' (Lev. 17:6)
D. 'And he shall remove his handful' (Lev. 2:2)
Correct: B — Rabbi Yehuda son of Rabbi Ḥiyya explains that Rabbi Shimon derives from Lev. 6:10 — the meal offering is compared to sin and guilt offerings, which have no service vessel restriction once on the altar.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Rabbi Yoḥanan, when does the burning of the handful first permit the remainder for priestly consumption?
A. When the fire first takes hold and ignites
B. When the priest places the handful on the altar
C. When the majority of the handful has been consumed by fire
D. At sunset of the day on which the handful is burned
Correct: C — Rabbi Yoḥanan requires the majority to be consumed, analogized from Genesis 19:28 — 'smoke of a furnace' — since a furnace only produces smoke once most of its fuel is burning.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What is the root of the dispute between Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi and the opposing view on burning the handful in two increments?
A. Whether two priests may share the burning of a single handful
B. Whether a sub-olive-bulk amount has halakhic significance on the altar
C. Whether two burnings on separate days are permitted
D. Whether a second increment requires its own vessel sanctification
Correct: B — Rabbi Zeira identifies the core dispute: Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi holds a sub-olive-bulk has no significance (so each increment must independently be ≥ olive-bulk), while the other view holds the total measure of the handful governs.
