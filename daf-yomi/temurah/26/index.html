⚖️ Handful Fitness: Contested Rules of the Meal Offering

Rav
Rabbi Yehoshua's ruling (remainder impure = unfit) applies only when all of the remainder became impure. If only part became impure, the meal offering is still fit.
Rava
The same logic extends to lost or burned remainder — if only part is lost or burned, what remains is still significant and the offering is fit.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Blood is sprinkled as long as an olive-bulk of fat OR any item offering a 'pleasing aroma' remains — derived from Leviticus 17:6.
Rabbi Shimon
The handful does not require sanctification in a service vessel to be brought to the altar — derived by analogy from 'most holy, as the sin offering and guilt offering' (Lev. 6:10).
Core Tension Throughout the daf: what minimum conditions must be met for a meal offering to remain valid? The status of the remainder, method of vessel sanctification, and manner of burning are all contested.

🩸 Olive-Bulk Threshold for Sprinkling Blood

Sufficient — Sprinkle

  • Olive-bulk of meat remains
  • Olive-bulk of fat remains
  • Lobe of the liver remains
  • Two kidneys remain
  • Any item offering a 'pleasing aroma'

Insufficient — Do Not Sprinkle

  • Half olive-bulk of meat + half olive-bulk of fat
  • They do not combine to form an olive-bulk
  • Nothing of the offering remains
  • Only non-aroma, ineligible portions remain

Scriptural Sources

  • Fat: Lev. 17:6 — explicit verse
  • Pleasing aroma items: Rabbi Yoḥanan / Rabbi Yishmael
  • Meal offering: Rav Pappa — refers to frankincense (blood-relevant)

🏺 Must the Handful Be in a Service Vessel When Burned?

Rabbis
The handful requires sanctification in a service vessel throughout. Placing it in the left hand renders it permanently unfit — returning it to the right hand cannot fix this.
Rabbi Shimon
Once sanctified in a vessel at removal, no further vessel is needed. The priest may carry the handful in his hand to the altar and burn it. Analogy: sin/guilt offerings have no vessel restriction at the altar stage.
Rabbi Elazar
I agree with Rabbi Shimon. The baraita's phrase 'right hand or vessel' is disjunctive — either suffices. After vessel sanctification, hand-carrying to the altar is permitted.
Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak
All concede the handful requires vessel sanctification — the dispute is only about later stages. (This opinion is ultimately refuted by a conclusive baraita.)
Conclusion A definitive baraita demonstrates that Rabbi Shimon and Rabbi Elazar genuinely do not require vessel sanctification beyond the initial removal — a conclusive refutation of Rav Naḥman bar Yitzḥak.

🔥 Burning the Handful in Two Increments

Mishna ruling: If the priest burned the handful in two separate increments, the offering is fit.
↓
Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi: Valid only if each increment was at least one olive-bulk. A handful is two olive-bulks total — each burning of one olive-bulk is independently valid.
↓
Rav Pappa (alternative view): Even if each individual burning was less than an olive-bulk, the total handful (≥ two olive-bulks) is valid — the whole, not each part, is measured.
↓
Underlying dispute (Rabbi Zeira): Does a sub-olive-bulk amount on the altar have halakhic significance? This is the root of the disagreement between the two opinions.
↓
Result: Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi — no, sub-olive has no significance; each burning must be ≥ olive-bulk. Rav Pappa — yes, the total handful's measure governs.
