✋ When Is the Right Hand Mandatory?

Rabba b. b. Ḥana
Anywhere the Torah mentions 'finger' or 'priesthood,' one must use the right hand.
Rava
Either mention alone suffices — finger OR priesthood disqualifies the left hand.
Abaye
But conveyance of burnt offering limbs mentions priesthood — and yet the left hand is permitted!
Rava
The rule applies only to rites that preclude atonement. Conveyance is not such a rite.
Deeper Resolution Rabbi Shimon holds that BOTH finger AND priesthood must appear in the verse to mandate the right hand — priesthood alone is not enough for him.

🩸 Two Verses on the Leper's Oil — Why Both Are Needed

"Upon the blood" — Lev. 14:17

  • Wealthy leper purification (rich offering)
  • Implies oil valid while blood still present on thumb & toe
  • Alone: would exclude oil application once blood is wiped away

"Upon the place of the blood" — Lev. 14:28

  • Poor leper purification (lesser offering)
  • Implies oil valid even after blood has been wiped away
  • Alone: would exclude oil application while blood is still there

🌿 Rava's Verbal Analogies Derived from the Leper Passage

Both leper verses are redundant for leper law itself — what else do they teach?
↓
Rava: these verses generate gezerot shavot (verbal analogies) extending right-side requirements to other laws
↓
"Foot" (Lev. 14:17)
Verbal analogy to chalitzah (yavam release) → right foot required for the ceremony
"Ear" (Lev. 14:17)
Verbal analogy to eved ivri (Hebrew slave) → right ear required for boring ceremony

🌾 Kemitzah — Which Derivation Covers Removal vs. Sanctification?

Question
For what does the verbal analogy from the leper passage come — removal of the handful or its sanctification into a vessel?
Gemara
Both are necessary — one derivation for kemitzah (removal), one for sanctification by placing the handful into a service vessel.
Challenge
But kemitzah is already derived from Rabbi Yehuda son of Rabbi Ḥiyya — he compared the meal offering to sin and guilt offerings!
Gemara
The verbal analogy is still needed for the sinner's meal offering specifically — one might have thought it needs no right hand since it has no frankincense requirement.
Rabbi Yehuda b. Rabbi Ḥiyya The meal offering is equated to sin and guilt offerings — just as those require the right hand, so too does the removal of the handful from every meal offering.

🫗 Oil Application in Leper Purification — Step by Step

Day 8 of purification: Guilt offering (asham) slaughtered; its blood applied to leper's right ear, right thumb, right big toe
↓
Log of oil prepared: Priest waves the log of oil as a wave offering before Hashem
↓
Oil sprinkled: Priest dips right finger and sprinkles seven times before Hashem
↓
Oil on thumb & toe — wealthy leper: Applied upon the blood (while blood still present) — Lev. 14:17
↓
Oil on thumb & toe — poor leper: Applied upon the place of the blood (even if blood removed) — Lev. 14:28
↓
Remaining oil: Priest places it on the leper's head, effecting full atonement and completing purification
