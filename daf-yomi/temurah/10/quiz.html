QUESTION 1 OF 5
Why does the Torah need BOTH 'upon the blood' (Lev. 14:17) AND 'upon the place of the blood' (Lev. 14:28) for the leper's oil rite?
A. They refer to different body parts — thumb and toe respectively
B. Together they teach the oil is placed whether blood is still present or already removed
C. One verse applies to rich lepers and one is a rabbinic extension to poor lepers
D. The second verse corrects an error in the first verse's application
Correct: B — Either verse alone implies the oil is valid only under one condition. Together, they cover both cases: blood still present and blood already wiped away.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rava's clarification, what triggers the requirement to use the right hand in a Temple rite?
A. Both 'finger' and 'priesthood' must appear together in the same verse
B. Only an explicit verse saying 'right hand' suffices
C. Either 'finger' OR 'priesthood' mentioned in the verse — one alone suffices
D. Only the word 'finger' — priesthood language is insufficient on its own
Correct: C — Rava clarifies Rabba bar bar Ḥana's ruling: mentioning either a finger or priesthood in the verse is sufficient to disqualify use of the left hand.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does conveyance of burnt offering limbs to the altar ramp NOT require the right hand, despite the verse mentioning priesthood?
A. The verse specifies the left hand for this particular rite
B. Levites rather than priests perform this conveyance
C. It is not a rite that precludes atonement — the right-hand rule applies only to atonement-essential rites
D. The priest uses both hands simultaneously so right-hand rules do not apply
Correct: C — The Gemara limits the right-hand rule to rites indispensable to atonement. Conveying limbs to the ramp is a permitted but non-essential step, so the left hand is permitted.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What law does Rava derive from the word 'foot' written in the wealthy leper's purification (Lev. 14:17)?
A. The right foot must lead when a priest ascends the altar
B. The leper's right foot must be anointed before his right thumb
C. Chalitzah — the levirate release ceremony — must be performed with the right foot
D. The water libation on Sukkot must be poured with the right foot forward
Correct: C — Rava derives a verbal analogy (gezerah shavah) from 'foot' in the leper passage to 'foot' in the chalitzah passage, teaching that the yavam must remove his sandal with the right foot.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
For which specific case does the Gemara ultimately need the verbal analogy from the leper passage regarding kemitzah?
A. The two loaves brought on Shavuot
B. The daily afternoon meal offering (minchah)
C. The sinner's meal offering — which has no frankincense requirement
D. The meal offering of the Nazirite upon completion of his vow
Correct: C — Since the sinner's meal offering lacks frankincense, one might think its handful also lacks the right-hand requirement. The verbal analogy from the leper passage teaches it still requires the right hand.
