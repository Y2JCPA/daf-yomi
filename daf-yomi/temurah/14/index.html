🔀 Does Piggul Intent Combine Across Loaves & Rites?

Rav Huna
The baraita about the two loaves follows Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi, not Rabbi Yosei — so no inference about Rabbi Yosei can be drawn from it
Rav Ashi
Refutation: A baraita in Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi's name cites Rabbi Yosei — intent to pour remaining blood renders the meat piggul, even though the intent was not about the meat itself
Ravina
Refutation: From the mishna (13a) — intent to burn the handful renders the remainder piggul; the handful itself cannot become piggul, yet the remainder is affected
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Resolution: Rabbi Yosei holds the two loaves are one body. If intent encompasses an olive-bulk from both combined → both piggul. If intent addresses only one → neither is piggul
Core Question Are the two Shavuot loaves one body (intent on one affects the other) or two distinct bodies? And does piggul intent during one sacrificial rite combine with intent during a different rite?

🧩 Rabbi Yosei's View: One Body or Two?

Rav Huna's Reading

  • Rabbi Yosei views right & left thighs as two distinct bodies
  • Piggul intent for one thigh does not render the other piggul
  • The two-loaves baraita follows Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi — not Rabbi Yosei
  • No inference about Rabbi Yosei's view of the loaves can be made
  • Challenged by Rav Ashi and Ravina with counter-baraitot

Rabbi Yoḥanan's Resolution

  • Rabbi Yosei views the two Shavuot loaves as one body
  • Intent on one loaf can render the other loaf piggul
  • If priest intends olive-bulk from both combined → both are piggul
  • If priest's intent concerns only one loaf → neither is piggul
  • The verse treats them as a single unit — they are mixed together

⏰ Do Piggul Intents Combine Across Two Rites?

Priest performs slaughter with intent to eat ½ olive-bulk of meat the next day
↓
Priest performs blood sprinkling with intent to eat another ½ olive-bulk the next day
↓
Some Say: YES — they combine
Both slaughter & sprinkling are permitting factors → the two ½-measure intents join → offering is piggul with kareit liability
Levi's Baraita: NO — they do not
Intents during the four rites (slaughter, collection, carrying, sprinkling) do not combine with each other for piggul — each rite stands alone

🤝 Abaye vs. Rava bar Rav Ḥanan on Rabbinic Decrees

Rava b. Ḥanan
If Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi holds that intent during a full permitting factor + half-measure combines for piggul, why does he rule the two-loaves case is NOT piggul?
Rava b. Ḥanan
Rabbi Yosei decreed disqualification for handful+frankincense; Rabbis decreed for handful+sinner's offering. These are rabbinic decrees modeled on similar cases of actual piggul
Abaye
Those decrees each have a parallel piggul case justifying the decree. But in the two-lambs scenario, there is no case anywhere where intent during one rite re: half a permitting factor truly creates piggul!
Gemara
Abaye's reasoning is confirmed by the mishna's language: the Rabbis use the term disqualified (rather than piggul) precisely because their ruling is a rabbinic decree, not Biblical piggul
Key Principle Rabbinic decrees of disqualification are issued only where an analogous case of genuine (Biblical) piggul liability exists — without such a parallel, no decree is issued

👑 Mishna: One Shavuot Loaf Becomes Impure

Scenario: One of the two Shavuot loaves becomes ritually impure before the blood of the lambs is sprinkled (the act that permits the loaves for consumption)
↓
Rabbi Yehuda: Both loaves go to the place of burning — communal offerings cannot be divided; if one is invalid, both are invalid
↓
Rabbis: The pure loaf is consumed normally; only the impure loaf is burned — we do not disqualify the valid loaf on account of the invalid one
↓
Rabbi Elazar: The dispute applies only when the loaf became impure before blood sprinkling; if it became impure afterward, all agree the pure loaf may be consumed
↓
Rav Pappa — root of dispute: Whether the High Priest's frontplate (ציץ, tzitz) effects atonement for impurity of communal offerings — Rabbis say yes; Rabbi Yehuda says no (or it does not apply here)
