QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rabbi Shimon, what does the vav (וְ) conjunction do in 'And he shall bring it to Aaron's sons, the priests' (Lev 2:2)?
A. It refers to both the preceding act (oil-pouring) and the succeeding act (handful) simultaneously
B. It links the priestly requirement backward to the preceding act of pouring the oil
C. It introduces an entirely new and independent law about the handful
D. It indicates that a non-priest may perform the entire rite in the absence of a kohen
Correct: B — Rabbi Shimon holds the vav adds to the previous matter — the mention of 'priests' links back to the preceding act of pouring the oil, making priestly involvement indispensable for that step, and not merely for the handful that follows.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is Rav's principle about sacrificial rites where both 'law' (תורה) and 'statute' (חוקה) appear?
A. The rite may be performed by a non-priest since it requires two authorities
B. The offering must be repeated if performed incorrectly
C. Failure to perform the rite invalidates the offering
D. The rite applies only to communal, not individual, offerings
Correct: C — Rav teaches that the co-occurrence of both 'law' and 'statute' for a sacrificial rite signals that the rite is indispensable — omitting it invalidates the offering entirely.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
The Gemara's mnemonic נ ת צ י ק מ ל lists challenges to Rav's rule. What does נ (Nun) represent?
A. The Nesi'im (princes) whose offerings are listed twelve times in Numbers
B. The Nazirite offering, where only 'law' is written but rites are still indispensable
C. The Neilah service on Yom Kippur requiring special priestly vestments
D. The libation (nesech) poured on the altar alongside animal offerings
Correct: B — Nun stands for Nazirite (נָזִיר) — the verse uses only 'law,' yet the mishna teaches that the four species used for his offering are indispensable. This challenges Rav's requirement for both terms, and the Gemara resolves it by locating 'statute' in the nearby context.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Where does the Gemara conclude the meal offering handful must be brought on the altar?
A. The northern side, where all most-holy offerings are slaughtered
B. The eastern side, facing the main entrance of the Temple courtyard
C. The southwestern corner, satisfying both 'in front of the altar' and 'before the Lord'
D. The center of the altar top, equidistant from all four sides
Correct: C — Rabbi Eliezer's principle states that when two verses seem to point to different locations, one must find the spot that fulfills both. The southwestern corner satisfies 'in front of the altar' (south face) and 'before the Lord' (west face, oriented toward the Sanctuary).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
On what point do Rav and Shmuel disagree regarding the handful of the meal offering?
A. Whether the handful must be taken by a priest or may be taken by a Levite
B. Whether Aaron's eighth-day service proves that the hand — not a vessel — must be used for all generations
C. Whether the handful may be divided between two priests in cases of doubt
D. Whether frankincense must be placed on the handful before or after it is taken
Correct: B — Rav uses Aaron's eighth-day service ('he filled his hand' — i.e., used his hand not a vessel) as a proof for all generations that using a vessel for the handful is invalid. Shmuel counters that Aaron's service was a one-time event (הוֹרָאַת שָׁעָה) and cannot establish permanent halakha.
