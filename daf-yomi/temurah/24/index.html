🫙 Does a Vessel Join Its Contents?

Sons of R. Ḥiyya
The mishna says a vessel joins its contents — implying in all cases, whether or not the contents are touching each other.
Rav Kahana
The vessel joins only when the item requires a vessel for sanctification — as with two half-tenths of a meal offering.
Sons of R. Ḥiyya
What if one inserts a finger between the two half-tenths without touching either? Do both become impure via the air-gap connection?
Rav Kahana
We also ask: can one remove a handful from one half-tenth on behalf of the other, even though they are not touching in the vessel?
Both Unresolved Both dilemmas — the finger-in-the-air-gap and the kemitza-across-the-gap — are left unresolved (teiku), illustrating the boundaries of the vessel-joining principle.

💧 Can an Already-Impure Item Become Impure Again?

Abaye
We do not say an item saturated with impurity cannot receive more. A sheet made impure by a zav's lying can still receive the more severe impurity of a zav's touch — proof that layered impurity is possible!
Rava
That case involves a more severe impurity overriding a lesser one. Here both impurities are equally minor (two touches of one who immersed that day) — perhaps neither can compound the other.
Abaye
Then consider the baraita: Rabbi Yosei rules the sheet impure even after impurity from lying — showing a second impurity of any kind can follow the first.
Rava
In the baraita the two impurities take effect simultaneously. In our meal offering case they are sequential — a decisive distinction!
Key Distinction Simultaneous dual-impurity is proven possible; sequential dual-impurity (same level) remains unresolved in the meal offering context.

🔀 Three Half-Tenths: Rava vs. Abaye

Rava's Position

  • Original ½ + replacement ½ are paired — vessel joins them
  • Found ½ (the originally lost one) stands apart from that pairing
  • Impurity: If replacement becomes impure → it pulls the original with it; found half unaffected
  • Impurity: If found half becomes impure → only it is impure; original and replacement untouched
  • Kemitza: Handful from found half permits found + original remainders; replacement is not permitted

Abaye's Position

  • All three halves are 'residents of one cabin' — meant for the same tenth
  • Their shared origin and destination link them indissolubly
  • Impurity: Any one of the three becomes impure → both remaining halves join in impurity
  • Kemitza: Handful from any one of them → both remaining halves are forbidden (cannot be eaten)
  • Rav Pappa objects: even the half from which the handful was taken — 1/6 was not sacrificed for it!

🏠 'Residents of One Cabin' — Abaye's Unifying Principle

כולהו בני מנא חד נינהו
Core Principle — Abaye All three half-tenths — the original, the replacement, and the found — are 'residents of one cabin'. Because they were each separated with the intention of completing the same tenth of an issaron, they remain permanently connected. Any impurity striking one pulls all others with it; any kemitza performed on one forecloses the remainder of all others. Shared intent creates shared legal fate.

Rav Ashi's resolution: The handful's permitting power is determined by the priest's intention at the moment of removal — intention focuses the act and defines which remainder is released for eating.
