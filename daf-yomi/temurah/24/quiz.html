QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav Kahana, under what condition does a vessel join its non-touching contents for ritual impurity?
A. Always — any vessel joins whatever is inside it
B. Only when the contents are liquids
C. Only when the item requires a vessel for its sanctification
D. Only when both contents belong to the same owner
Correct: C — Rav Kahana limits the joining principle: a vessel joins its contents only when the item requires that vessel for sanctification (as a meal offering requires a vessel). Items that don't need a vessel for sanctification are not joined by being placed in one.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What unresolved dilemma (teiku) do the sons of Rabbi Ḥiyya raise about the vessel and its contents?
A. Whether two offerings of different owners in one vessel join together
B. Whether inserting a finger in the air-gap between two half-tenths without touching either causes impurity to both
C. Whether the vessel itself becomes impure when its contents do
D. Whether kemitza can be performed while one hand is impure
Correct: B — The sons of Rabbi Ḥiyya asked: if one who immersed that day inserts a finger between the two half-tenths without touching either one, do both halves become impure via the air-space? This remained unresolved.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is Rava's crucial distinction in the debate about whether a second impurity can affect an already-impure meal offering?
A. A more severe impurity can never follow a lesser one on the same object
B. Liquid foods can absorb second impurities but dry foods cannot
C. Two impurities taking effect simultaneously is different from one following sequentially after another
D. The second impurity is only effective if administered by a different person
Correct: C — Rava argues that the proof-cases (like the zav's sheet) involve two impurities taking effect simultaneously. In the meal offering scenario, the second impurity follows after the first — and it is unproven that sequential same-level impurities compound.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
According to Abaye's 'residents of one cabin' principle, what is the result if the kemitza (handful) is removed from any one of the three half-tenths?
A. Only the remainder of that specific half is permitted for priestly consumption
B. The handful is invalid and must be retaken
C. The remainder of the selected half plus one other half may be eaten
D. Neither of the two remaining half-tenths may be eaten
Correct: D — Abaye holds all three halves are permanently linked as 'residents of one cabin.' Kemitza on any one of them does not cleanly permit any remainder, because it is impossible to determine whose consumption has been permitted — so both remaining halves are forbidden.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rav Ashi resolve the objections of Rav Pappa and Rav Yitzḥak bar Mesharshiyya against Abaye's kemitza ruling?
A. He rules that all three halves must be combined before kemitza is performed
B. He rules that the handful is valid only if taken from the center of all three halves
C. The permitting power of the handful depends on the intention of the priest at the moment of removal
D. He rules that three half-tenths require three separate handfuls, one for each
Correct: C — Rav Ashi answers that the permissibility of the remainder is determined by the priest's intention when removing the handful. The priest's focused intent defines which portion the handful is serving to permit, resolving the fraction-of-the-handful problem.
