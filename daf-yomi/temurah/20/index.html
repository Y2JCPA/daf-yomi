⚖️ Is Salt Indispensable for Meal Offerings?

Rav
Only sacrificial rites repeated in multiple verses are indispensable (me'akev). Salt is not literally repeated — therefore a meal offering without salt is still valid.
Rav Yosef
But the Torah calls salt a brit — a covenant! That word is equivalent to the rite being written twice, making salt fully indispensable.
Rav Yosef
Also: a non-priest cannot approach the altar to salt the handful — the scenario of skipping altar-side salting is practically impossible.
Rav
The non-priest concern applies only to altar-side salting; pre-altar salting is distinct. But I accept: the word brit functions as a repetition, so salt is indispensable by that logic.
Key Verse "It is an everlasting covenant of salt" (Numbers 18:19) — brit language elevates salt to indispensable status, per Rav's own concession

🔥 Why Limbs Require Salt but Blood Does Not

🔥 Limbs of Burnt Offering

  • ✓ Burned on the altar
  • ✓ Wood comes as a requirement for burning
  • ✓ Subject to me'ila (misuse of consecrated property)
  • ✓ Forbidden to eat while ritually impure
  • ✓ Requires a service vessel
  • ✓ Has a fixed minimum measure
  • → REQUIRES SALTING

🩸 Blood

  • ✓ Renders the offering permitted (like the handful)
  • ✗ No items come as requirement for blood itself
  • ✗ Not subject to me'ila
  • ✗ Not brought in the same vessel as libations
  • ✗ Not comparable to meal offering in key ways
  • ✗ Lacks the defining characteristics of the 'detail'
  • → EXCLUDED FROM SALTING

🪵 Voluntary Wood Offerings — Baraita (Amud B)

Source Verse: "When one brings a korban mincha" (Lev. 2:1) — the seemingly superfluous word korban teaches that one may voluntarily donate wood for the altar as a personal offering
↓
Minimum Amount: One must donate at least two pieces of wood — enough to produce a flame for burning on the altar
↓
Rava's View: Voluntarily donated wood requires removal of a handful, analogous to a meal offering — that handful is sacrificed separately on the altar
↓
Rav Pappa's Ruling: The designated handful of wood is burned; the remainder follows standard rules governing what is set aside from altar offerings
↓
Connection to Salting: The Gemara ultimately removes wood from items excluded by "every meal offering of yours" — wood's exclusion from salt requirements must rest on a different basis entirely
