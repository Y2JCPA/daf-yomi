Temurah 20 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav, what is the general rule for determining whether a sacrificial rite is indispensable (me'akev)?
A. It must be performed by a kohen at the altar
B. It must be mentioned in repeated verses
C. It must involve a physical act of placement
D. It must have a biblical source in Leviticus
Correct: B — Rav holds that only rites repeated across multiple verses are indispensable. This is why he initially rules that omitting salt does not invalidate the meal offering — salt appears to be stated only once.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
How does Rav ultimately reconcile salt being indispensable despite not being literally repeated in two verses?
A. He quotes a Mishnaic source that explicitly states this
B. The Sages overruled him by majority vote
C. The word 'covenant' (brit) is halachically equivalent to a second verse
D. A non-priest cannot approach the altar, proving it is critical
Correct: C — Rav accepts that the term brit (covenant) applied to salt functions as if the requirement were written twice, conferring indispensable status without a literal repetition in a second verse.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In the klal-prat-klal derivation, what is the defining characteristic of the 'detail' (prat) — the handful — that determines which other items require salting?
A. It is offered by a Levite
B. It renders the remainder of the offering permitted to be eaten
C. It is burned on the altar and other items come as requirements for it
D. It has a minimum measure of one issaron
Correct: C — The Gemara identifies the handful's key characteristic as: (1) burned on the altar, and (2) other items (like wood) come as requirements for it. Items sharing both features — like frankincense and burnt offering limbs — are included.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Why is blood ultimately excluded from the requirement to be salted, even though it renders the offering permitted — just as the handful does?
A. Blood is sprinkled, not burned, so it is never on the altar
B. Blood does not have other items coming as prerequisites for it
C. The verse explicitly excludes blood by name
D. Blood was already covered by a separate rabbinic decree
Correct: B — Although blood does render the offering permitted (like the handful), it lacks the second critical characteristic: other items do not come as requirements specifically for the blood. The Gemara shows that sharing only one feature is insufficient.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What does the baraita derive from the superfluous word 'korban' in the phrase 'korban mincha' (Leviticus 2:1)?
A. All meal offerings must be brought in a sacred vessel
B. One may voluntarily donate wood to be burned on the altar as a personal offering
C. A meal offering brought by a non-priest is still valid
D. The minimum measure for a meal offering is one issaron
Correct: B — The baraita reads the extra word korban as teaching that wood may be donated voluntarily as an independent offering for the altar. Rava adds that such wood even requires removal of a dedicated handful, parallel to the meal offering.
