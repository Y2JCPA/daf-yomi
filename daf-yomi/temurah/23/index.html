⚖️ Two Disputes at the Heart of Menachot 23

Rabbi Yoḥanan
If oil was added to the handful of a sinner's meal offering, it is unfit — the Torah's prohibition disqualifies it entirely.
Reish Lakish
The handful remains fit — the verse (Lev. 5:11) only forbids applying oil to the offering itself, not to the handful once removed.
Rav Ḥisda
Nullification follows the nullifying substance (majority). If the majority can attain the status of the minority, the two are identical and no nullification occurs.
Rabbi Ḥanina
Nullification follows the nullified substance (minority). If the minority can attain the status of the majority, the two are identical and the minority is not nullified.
Core Question When two items of the same type are mixed, does their identity — and therefore resistance to nullification — depend on what the majority can become, or what the minority can become?

🫒 Oil Added to the Sinner's Meal Offering Handful

Rabbi Yoḥanan
Unfit. The Torah says 'he shall put no oil upon it' (Lev. 5:11) — this disqualifies the offering if oil contacts the handful at any stage.
Reish Lakish
Fit. The verse only forbids oil on the meal offering before the handful is removed. Oil added to the handful afterward does not invalidate it.
Rabbi Yoḥanan
Objection from a baraita: a dry meal offering intermingled with an oil-mixed one — Rabbi Yehuda rules the priest may not sacrifice it, implying oil-contact disqualifies.
Reish Lakish
That baraita discusses the minḥat nesakhim of bulls (2 log per tenth) intermingled with that of lambs (1 log per tenth) — a different scenario. The counter-baraita proves this explicitly.
📜 Verse (Lev. 5:11) "He shall put no oil upon it, neither shall he put any frankincense on it" — the unique stringency of the sinner's meal offering

⚖️ Nullification: Rav Ḥisda vs. Rabbi Ḥanina

Rav Ḥisda — Follow the Majority

  • Nullification hinges on whether the majority can become like the minority
  • If yes → identical type → no nullification
  • Carcass into slaughtered (majority slaughtered): slaughtered cannot become carcass → not identical → carcass IS nullified
  • Slaughtered into carcass (majority carcass): carcass can become slaughtered via shechitah → identical → slaughtered is NOT nullified

Rabbi Ḥanina — Follow the Minority

  • Nullification hinges on whether the minority can become like the majority
  • If yes → identical type → no nullification
  • Carcass into slaughtered (minority carcass): carcass can become slaughtered → identical → carcass is NOT nullified
  • Slaughtered into carcass (minority slaughtered): slaughtered cannot become carcass → not identical → slaughtered IS nullified

📜 Rabbi Zeira's Verbal Analogy — A Torah Edict

וְהֵרִים מִמֶּנּוּ בְּקֻמְצוֹ... וְהַנּוֹתֶרֶת מִן הַמִּנְחָה לְאַהֲרֹן וּלְבָנָיו (ויקרא ב:ב–ג)
גְּזֵרָה שָׁוָה: הַקְטָרָה (Burning) The word burning (הַקְטָרָה) appears in the Torah for both the handful (Lev. 2:2) and the remainder (Lev. 2:3). Just as when two handfuls are mixed together neither nullifies the other and both are burned, so too when a handful is mixed with a remainder — neither nullifies the other. This is not a rabbinic safeguard but a Torah-level edict, which explains why the mishna's rulings do not depend on which opinion (Rav Ḥisda or Rabbi Ḥanina) one follows.
