QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Reish Lakish, what happens if oil is added to the handful removed from a sinner's meal offering?
A. The handful is unfit — any oil contact violates Leviticus 5:11
B. The handful is fit — the verse only prohibits oil on the offering before removal
C. The handful is fit — only frankincense contact disqualifies the handful
D. The handful is unfit — but the remainder of the offering still satisfies the obligation
Correct: B — Reish Lakish limits Lev. 5:11 to prohibiting oil on the meal offering itself before the handful is removed. Once the handful is taken, adding oil to it does not disqualify it.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the precise scenario of Rava's dilemma about the handful and oil?
A. The priest removes the handful without proper kavvanah (intent)
B. The priest squeezes the handful's oil onto the wood first, then places the handful on it
C. The priest burns the handful outside the Temple and then brings the oil in
D. The priest mixes the handful with a second handful before burning
Correct: B — Rava asks whether substances that are contiguous to items ascending the altar count as ascending with them — tested by the case where oil is placed on the wood before the handful is laid on top.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rav Ḥisda, what happens when carcass meat (minority) becomes mixed into slaughtered meat (majority)?
A. The carcass meat is nullified — slaughtered meat cannot become carcass
B. The carcass meat is not nullified — carcass can become slaughtered via shechitah
C. The slaughtered meat is nullified — we follow the nullified substance
D. Neither is nullified — they are identical in appearance
Correct: A — Rav Ḥisda follows the majority (nullifying substance). Since slaughtered meat cannot become carcass, the two are not of identical type, and the minority carcass meat IS nullified.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rabbi Zeira's ruling that a handful mixed with a remainder does not nullify the other is classified as:
A. A rabbinic fence to protect the sanctity of the Temple service
B. A Torah-level edict derived from the shared word 'burning' in Leviticus 2
C. A ruling that follows Rabbi Ḥanina's principle about the nullified substance
D. A ruling that follows Rav Ḥisda's principle about the nullifying substance
Correct: B — Rabbi Zeira uses a gezerah shavah (verbal analogy) from the word הַקְטָרָה appearing for both handful and remainder. Just as two handfuls never nullify each other, neither does a handful nullify a remainder — a davar Torah.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
The mishna rules that if a handful was intermingled with an unskimmed meal offering and the priest burned the mixture anyway (b'di'avad), which obligation is satisfied?
A. Neither obligation is satisfied — the entire mixture is disqualified
B. Only the owner of the handful's offering satisfies his obligation
C. The meal offering (whose handful was already taken) satisfies its owner's obligation
D. Both owners satisfy their obligations retroactively
Correct: C — The mishna rules that b'di'avad the meal offering (from which the handful was already separated) satisfies its owner's obligation, even though the mixture should not have been burned initially.
