Temurah 34 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
On which side of an entrance must a mezuza be affixed, and what is the Talmudic source?
A. Left side — derived from the Passover doorposts passage in Exodus
B. Right side — derived from 'beitekha' read as 'bi'atkha' (your entering)
C. Center of the doorpost — no specific side is required
D. Right side — but only when facing east
Correct: B — The baraita reads 'beitekha' (your house) as 'bi'atkha' (your entering), teaching that as one enters, the mezuza must be on the right hand side.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rabbi Akiva, how does the word 'totafot' prove that head tefillin require four compartments?
A. 'Totafot' is written four times in the Torah, each indicating one compartment
B. 'Tot' means two in Katfei and 'pat' means two in Afriki, yielding four total
C. 'Totafot' is a gematria equal to four
D. The word totafot appears in a verse alongside four different commandments
Correct: B — Rabbi Akiva analyzes the foreign-language components of the word: 'tot' = 2 (Katfei) and 'pat' = 2 (Afriki), totaling four compartments.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What is the ruling if the four passages inside head tefillin are placed in the wrong order?
A. They are valid but a blessing should not be recited on them
B. They are valid since all four passages are present
C. They are unfit (pasul) according to Rav Ḥananel in the name of Rav
D. They must be burned and rewritten, but are valid in the interim
Correct: C — Rav Ḥananel in the name of Rav rules that exchanging the passages renders the tefillin unfit (pasul), since order is integral to the mitzva.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Regarding a gatehouse with both a garden entrance and an inner-room entrance, what is the ruling on mezuza?
A. Both entrances require a mezuza
B. Only the garden entrance requires a mezuza
C. Only the inner-room entrance requires a mezuza
D. Neither entrance requires a mezuza — the gatehouse serves the garden, not a dwelling
Correct: D — Abaye and Rava ruled like Rabba and Rav Yosef: since the gatehouse exists to access the garden (not as a dwelling), neither of its entrances is obligated in mezuza.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why can old (already-worn) head tefillin NOT be converted into arm tefillin, even though new ones may be?
A. Old tefillin have different dimensions that do not fit the arm
B. The principle of maʿalin ba-kodesh: we ascend in sanctity but never diminish it
C. Old tefillin passages may be too faded to use for arm tefillin
D. The Tanna explicitly prohibited reuse of worn tefillin
Correct: B — The principle is מַעֲלִין בַּקֹּדֶשׁ וְלֹא מוֹרִידִין — we elevate in sanctity, never diminish. Already-worn head tefillin have higher sanctity and cannot be downgraded.
