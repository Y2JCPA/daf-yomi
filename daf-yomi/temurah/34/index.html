🚪 Corner Entrance — Are Walls Valid Doorposts?

Ameimar
A corner entrance IS obligated in mezuza. The ends of the walls themselves serve as its doorposts — affix the mezuza on the right side as one enters.
Rav Ashi
But it has no proper doorposts! A mezuza requires formal doorposts — a corner has none.
Ameimar
These (adei) ARE its doorposts. The wall-ends flanking the corner function legally as doorposts for purposes of mezuza obligation.
Rav Pappa (incident)
At Mar Shmuel's house: saw an entrance with only ONE doorpost on the left, yet Mar Shmuel had affixed a mezuza there — in accordance with Rabbi Meir, who requires only one doorpost.
Practical Ruling Ameimar: corner walls = valid doorposts. Rav Pappa confirms Rabbi Meir's view in practice: one doorpost suffices for obligation.

➡️ Mezuza on the Right — Two Biblical Derivations

Derivation 1: Baraita on 'Beitekha'

  • Verse: 'upon the doorposts of your house [beitekha]' (Deut. 6:9)
  • Read as bi'atkha — 'your entering'
  • As you enter, mezuza must be on your RIGHT hand
  • Universally accepted Talmudic derivation

Derivation 2: Rav Shmuel bar Aḥa (name of Rava bar Ulla)

  • From: Jehoiada placed the charity chest 'to the right as one enters' (II Kings 12:10)
  • Verbal analogy: 'right' by the chest = 'right' for mezuza
  • Temple practice validates the right-side principle
  • Secondary support confirming the baraita's ruling

📜 One Doorpost — Sufficient or Not?

מְזוּזוֹת בֵּיתֶךָ — וּבִשְׁעָרֶיךָ
Tannaitic Dispute Rabbis: Two doorposts required. The plural 'mezuzot' (doorposts) means at minimum two. A house with only one doorpost is exempt.

Rabbi Meir: Even one doorpost obligates a mezuza. Proof: Exodus 12:22 says 'the two doorposts' — if two were always required, why specify 'two'? Therefore, 'doorposts' alone can mean one.

Rabbi Akiva's support: The word 'two' in the Passover verse is superfluous, teaching that even one doorpost triggers the law — and the same applies to mezuza by analogy.

Baraita on parchment vs. wood: 'Upon the doorposts' means on the doorpost material itself — not written on the stones of the entrance. Verified by verbal analogy with writing of the Sotah, confirming the mezuza must be written first, then affixed.

📋 Arranging the Four Passages in Head Tefillin

Right side (wearer's right, reader's left): Kadesh Li (Exod. 13:1–10) and V'hayah Ki Yevi'acha (Exod. 13:11–16)
↓
Left side (wearer's left, reader's right): Shema (Deut. 6:4–9) and V'hayah Im Shamoa (Deut. 11:13–21)
↓
Abaye resolves apparent contradiction between two baraitot: 'right' in one source means right of the reader (facing the wearer); 'right' in the other means right of the wearer — same physical arrangement, different perspectives
↓
Passages exchanged / wrong order: Rav Ḥananel in name of Rav — tefillin are unfit (pasul). Abaye adds: this applies only when fully exchanged, not just partially
↓
Converting head tefillin to arm tefillin: New (unworn) head tefillin may be converted. Old (already worn) head tefillin may NOT be downgraded — maʿalin ba-kodesh v'lo moridin (we ascend in sanctity, never descend)
