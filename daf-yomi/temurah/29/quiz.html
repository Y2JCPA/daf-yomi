QUESTION 1 OF 5
When Rav said the Menorah's height is nine handbreadths, what did he actually mean?
A. The Menorah was only nine handbreadths tall in total
B. Nine handbreadths from the base to the central knob — the lower half of the total 18-handbreadth height
C. Nine handbreadths was the height of the branches above the shaft
D. Nine handbreadths referred only to the decorative ornaments
Correct: B — Rav clarified to Rav Shimi bar Ḥiyya that he was referring to the lower half of the shaft — from the base to the middle knob. The Menorah's total height was eighteen handbreadths, which is why the three-step stone was needed.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
In the aggadah of Moses ascending on High, why was Moses ultimately comforted after failing to understand Rabbi Akiva's lesson?
A. God explained the entire lesson to Moses privately afterward
B. Moses realized Rabbi Akiva was actually inferior to him in wisdom
C. Rabbi Akiva attributed his teaching to 'a halakha given to Moses at Sinai,' honoring Moses as the source
D. Moses was shown a vision of Rabbi Akiva's tragic death and felt vindicated
Correct: C — When Akiva's students asked his source, he answered 'Halakha l'Moshe miSinai' — a law transmitted from Moses at Sinai. This showed Moses that all of Akiva's teachings traced back to Moses himself, restoring Moses' sense of purpose.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why was this world created specifically with the letter heh (ה)?
A. Heh is the most frequently appearing letter in the Torah
B. Heh equals five, representing the five books of Moses given at creation
C. Heh is open at the bottom like a portico — one may leave freely, but return requires Heaven's help
D. Heh represents God's mercy attribute, which governs this world
Correct: C — The Gemara explains that the letter heh is open at the bottom, resembling a portico open on one side. This world is like that opening — anyone who wishes to leave (to sin) may do so. But return requires Heaven's assistance (per Reish Lakish), which is why one cannot re-enter through the same opening.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What are the three matters the School of Rabbi Yishmael says were too difficult for Moses until God showed them with His finger?
A. The Tabernacle dimensions, the priestly garments, and the altar design
B. The form of the Candelabrum, the exact appearance of the new moon, and the forbidden insects
C. The Ten Commandments' lettering, the Ark's measurements, and the incense formula
D. The Shema text, mezuza placement, and tefillin knot shape
Correct: B — The baraita from the School of Rabbi Yishmael lists: (1) the precise form of the Candelabrum, (2) the exact visual appearance of the new moon used to sanctify the month, and (3) the identifying features of forbidden insects (sheratzim).
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to the baraita, what is the halakha for a Torah scroll with four errors on every column?
A. A scribe must correct it within thirty days
B. It may still be used for Torah reading until corrected
C. It shall be interred — it is too defective to be used or corrected
D. A rabbinic court must rule on each individual column
Correct: C — The baraita rules explicitly: three errors per column — the scroll may be corrected; four errors per column — it shall be interred (buried). However, Abaye adds that if even one column has only three errors, it enables correcting the entire scroll.
