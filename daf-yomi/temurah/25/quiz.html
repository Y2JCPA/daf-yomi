Temurah 25 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to the Mishna, if the handful of a meal offering became impure and the priest sacrificed it, what is the law?
A. The offering is entirely void and must be brought again
B. The tzitz effects acceptance and the remainder is eaten by the priests
C. The offering is accepted but the priests may not eat the remainder
D. The priest is flogged and the offering is burned
Correct: B — The Mishna teaches that the tzitz effects acceptance for an impure handful, and the remainder of the meal offering is eaten by the priests as normal.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Why does Abaye argue the tzitz does NOT atone for left-hand service, even though left-hand service was permitted on Yom Kippur?
A. Left-hand service invalidates the offering entirely with no remedy
B. The verse limits tzitz to sins 'committed with the offering itself,' not procedural defects in service
C. Yom Kippur is unique and cannot create a general leniency
D. Left-hand service is a Torah prohibition too severe for tzitz to override
Correct: B — Abaye derives from 'Aaron shall bear the sin committed with the sacred items' that the tzitz atones for sins involving the offering itself — impurity is a defect of the offering, while left-hand service is merely a procedural violation.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
What does the word 'lahem' (for them) in the verse teach about tzitz acceptance?
A. That tzitz effects acceptance only for priests, not lay Israelites
B. That tzitz effects acceptance only for Jews, not gentiles
C. That tzitz only applies when the majority of the offering is intact
D. That tzitz requires the High Priest to be present in the Temple
Correct: B — The Gemara derives from 'that it may be accepted for them before the Lord' that 'for them' means for the Jewish people — the tzitz does not effect acceptance for offerings brought by non-Jews.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How does Ravina resolve the contradiction between the two baraitot regarding impure blood?
A. He attributes each baraita to a different Tanna (Rabbi Yosei vs. Rabbi Eliezer)
B. He limits one baraita to communal offerings and the other to individual offerings
C. He distinguishes based on how the impurity was contracted: unwitting vs. intentional impurity determines acceptance
D. He rules that both baraitot follow Rabbi Eliezer, who is always stringent
Correct: C — Ravina resolves the contradiction by focusing on the manner in which impurity occurred: whether the blood contracted impurity unwittingly or intentionally determines acceptance — the manner of sprinkling is irrelevant.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
Why is an impure person who eats sacrificial meat that has left the Temple courtyard NOT liable for violating the impurity prohibition?
A. Leaving the courtyard is a more severe disqualification that supersedes impurity
B. The tzitz already effected acceptance for meat that left the courtyard
C. The impurity prohibition applies only to meat that is permitted to pure people — disqualified meat never was
D. The prohibition of eating while impure applies only within the Temple precincts
Correct: C — The Gemara teaches that liability for eating while impure is triggered only when eating what is 'permitted to the pure.' Meat that left the courtyard is already forbidden even to pure people, so the impure person is not liable for the impurity violation specifically.
