🤔 Rav Hoshaya's Dilemma: Why Does Rabbi Shimon Validate?

Option A
Rabbi Shimon requires deliberate mislabeling to disqualify — removing the handful for the sake of another offering is not intentional enough to invalidate
Option B
'This is the law of the meal offering' (Lev. 6:7) establishes one unified law for all meal offerings — no distinction between types, even jealousy vs. standard
Rabba
Distinguishes between removing a handful for another offering of the same vessel vs. a different vessel — but Rav Asi cannot apply this to resolve the dilemma
Rav Ashi
Distinguishes vessel type — but this premise also fails to settle whether Rabbi Shimon's rule rests on intentionality or on the unifying verse
Result
Dilemma remains unresolved — we do not know Rabbi Shimon's exact reasoning, and this affects whether the jealousy offering would be valid if removed not for its sake
The Stakes If Rabbi Shimon's reason is the unifying verse, then even the jealousy offering (which resembles a sin offering) should be valid — against the mishna's ruling

🔗 Gezeirah Shava: How 'Iniquity' Links Jealousy Offering to Sin Offering

Meal Offering of Jealousy
Numbers 5:15 — 'bringing iniquity (avon) to remembrance'
↓
Verbal analogy (gezeirah shava) on word avon (עָוֹן)
↓
Sin Offering
Leviticus 10:17 — 'to bear the iniquity (avon) of the congregation'
↓
Therefore: jealousy offering is disqualified when not sacrificed for its sake — like a sin offering
Why NOT guilt offering?
Guilt offering uses avono (his iniquity) — different grammatical form breaks the analogy
Scope of the Analogy
Torah revealed sin offering halakha cannot spread further — gezeirah shava applies only to the surplus funds rule, not to disqualification logic

📊 The 'It' (הוא) Exclusion — Sin Offering vs. Guilt Offering

חַטָּאת — Sin Offering

  • Verse: 'It (הוא) is a sin offering' — Lev. 4:24
  • 'It' excludes: disqualified when slaughtered not for its sake
  • Derives disqualification for: meal offering of sinner
  • Derives disqualification for: meal offering of jealousy (via gezeirah shava)
  • Halakha cannot be derived from sin offering to extend to other offerings

אָשָׁם — Guilt Offering

  • Verse: 'It (הוא) is a guilt offering' — Lev. 7:5
  • 'It' does NOT disqualify when not for its sake
  • Instead: 'It' teaches Rav Huna's ruling in Rav's name
  • If owner died or transgression was atoned → guilt offering grazes
  • After grazing: valid if slaughtered as a burnt offering

🌾 Why the Omer Is Excluded From the General Rule

Mishna's General Rule: All meal offerings not for their sake are valid but unfulfilled
↓
Limitation 1 — Individual Only: The mishna speaks only of meal offerings brought on behalf of an individual, not the community
↓
Limitation 2 — No Fixed Time: The mishna speaks only of offerings that may be brought on any date (no fixed time)
↓
The Omer Fails Both: It is (1) a communal offering and (2) has a fixed time — the 16th of Nisan
↓
Result: The Omer is disqualified if the handful was removed not for its sake — it falls entirely outside the mishna's leniency
↓
Similarly: The Two Loaves of Shavuot — also communal and time-fixed — would likewise be excluded
