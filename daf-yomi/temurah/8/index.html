🍰 The Great Griddle-Cake Debate

Rabbi Yoḥanan
The High Priest's griddle-cake offering is not sanctified in halves. A whole tenth must be brought and sanctified before dividing — this applies to all meal offerings.
Rabbi Elazar
Since it is sacrificed in halves — half in the morning, half in the evening — it may also be sanctified in halves.
Gemara
Objection from Mishna (50b): The griddle-cake did not come in halves — the High Priest brings a full tenth of an ephah, then divides it.
Rabbi Elazar
That requirement is only ab initio (לכתחילה). After the fact (בדיעבד), if half a tenth was brought in the morning it is valid. Rav Giddel supports this.
Key Tension Rav holds standard meal offerings are never sanctified in halves even with intent to add. Rabbi Yoḥanan derives from the griddle-cake that all meal offerings are not sanctified in halves — yet learns from blood that consecrated items can transfer holiness to others.

🕯️ When Is Frankincense Burned if Shewbread Breaks?

Shewbread loaves break before the frankincense is burned
↓
Before the time to detach arrived
Frankincense is NOT burned
Like a meal offering whose handful was never removed — the handful was not yet clearly designated
After the time to detach arrived
Frankincense IS burned
Once the time came, it is treated as if the shewbread was already detached — the handful is 'designated'
↓
Rabbi Elazar's rule: Arrival of the time to perform an act is halakhically equivalent to performing the act itself

⚗️ Can Meal Offering Components Be Sanctified Separately?

Rav — Components Are Independent

  • Flour without oil — derived from shewbread, which is brought without oil
  • Oil alone — derived from the leper's log of oil (a standalone offering)
  • Frankincense alone — derived from the shewbread frankincense bowls
  • Each component sanctified independently by a service vessel
  • Shmuel agrees: dry goods (flour) can be sanctified by liquid-vessels (bowls)

Rabbi Ḥanina — All Must Be Together

  • Flour, oil, and frankincense must all be together in the vessel at once
  • The tenth-of-ephah vessel was anointed for measuring flour, not sanctifying oil separately
  • The log-of-oil vessel was anointed specifically for the leper's oil — not standard meal offering oil
  • No meal offering component is sanctified apart from its companions
  • Challenge to Rav: how can a vessel sanctify something not yet placed inside it?

🤲 Handful Removal — Valid Inside the Sanctuary?

Rabbi Elazar
If a priest removed the handful inside the Sanctuary (Heikhal), the meal offering is valid. The entire Temple complex bears the name 'Tent of Meeting.'
Rabbi Yirmeya
Objection from baraita: The verse (Lev. 2:2) implies removal must occur outside the Heikhal. The Sanctuary is distinctly separated from the courtyard for this purpose.
Rabbi Ya'akov
The verse was needed to permit removal anywhere in the courtyard — one might have thought removal must occur specifically at the altar entrance. The verse expands, it does not restrict.
Gemara
The derivation proceeds by gezeirah shavah: just as other offerings are valid throughout the courtyard, so too the meal offering's handful may be removed anywhere in the Temple precinct.
Key Verse "And he shall bring it to Aaron's sons the priests; and he shall remove from there his handful" (Leviticus 2:2) — 'from there' implies a specific location that requires derivation to expand

🕊️ Peace Offerings Slaughtered in the Sanctuary — Rabbi Yoḥanan's Ruling

The verse: "And slaughter it at the entrance of the Tent of Meeting" (Lev. 3:2) — the entrance refers to the Temple courtyard
↓
Rabbi Yoḥanan's derivation: "At the entrance" implies even not at the entrance is valid — anywhere in the Tent of Meeting complex, including the Heikhal itself
↓
Objection (baraita, Rabbi Yehuda ben Beteira): If gentiles surround the courtyard and fire inside, making it impure, the community may sacrifice peace offerings on the roof of the Heikhal
↓
Apparent implication: The Heikhal roof is mentioned as an emergency alternative — suggesting the Heikhal itself is normally not a valid slaughter location
↓
Resolution: Rabbi Yoḥanan maintains the Heikhal is valid normally; the emergency case adds a teaching about maintaining sacrifice during impure conditions, not about valid locations

📋 Temurah 8 — Summary Map

Core Question: What are the conditions for valid sanctification of a meal offering?
↓
Measure: Full tenth required (Rav, R. Yoḥanan)
Half-tenth is never sanctified for standard offerings; griddle-cake valid only after the fact (R. Elazar)
Components: Rav — each sanctified independently; Rabbi Ḥanina — all must be together in vessel simultaneously
Location: Handful removal valid throughout courtyard; Heikhal valid (R. Elazar); peace offerings in Heikhal valid (R. Yoḥanan)
↓
Underlying principle: Designation in time = designation in fact (R. Elazar on shewbread); the form of an offering shapes its sanctification rules — offerings sacrificed in halves may be sanctified in halves
