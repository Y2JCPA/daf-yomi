Temurah 8 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
Rabbi Elazar holds that the High Priest's griddle-cake offering may be sanctified in halves. What is his reasoning?
A. He derives from blood that any offering may be divided before sanctification
B. Since the offering is ultimately sacrificed in halves, it may also be sanctified in halves
C. The verse explicitly permits dividing the griddle-cake before placing it in the vessel
D. He holds that any offering brought voluntarily may be divided at any stage
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar reasons that since the griddle-cake is sacrificed in two halves (morning and evening), the form of its sacrifice determines that it may also be sanctified in halves.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
According to Rav, which of the following correctly describes meal offering sanctification?
A. All components must be placed in the service vessel simultaneously
B. Only the flour requires a service vessel; oil and frankincense are sanctified automatically
C. Flour, oil, and frankincense can each be sanctified independently by a service vessel
D. A meal offering is only sanctified once the priest declares his intention aloud
Correct: C — Rav holds each component can be sanctified independently: flour without oil (like shewbread), oil alone (like the leper's log), and frankincense alone (like the shewbread bowls of frankincense).
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Why does Rabbi Elazar rule that the frankincense is burned when the shewbread broke after the 'time to detach' had arrived?
A. Because frankincense is never invalidated by problems with the shewbread
B. Because once the time to detach arrives, it is treated as if the shewbread was already detached
C. Because the kohen had physically begun detaching the shewbread
D. Because the frankincense was placed in the bowls before the shewbread broke
Correct: B — Rabbi Elazar's principle is that the arrival of the designated time is legally equivalent to the act itself — once time came to detach the shewbread, it is treated as detached, and the frankincense is valid for burning.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
Rabbi Yoḥanan rules that peace offerings slaughtered in the Sanctuary (Heikhal) are valid. From where does he derive this?
A. From a principle that any offering valid in the courtyard is valid in the Heikhal
B. From the verse 'at the entrance of the Tent of Meeting' — 'at the entrance' implies even not at the entrance, including the Heikhal
C. From the emergency ruling that peace offerings may be brought on the Heikhal roof
D. From a gezeirah shavah comparing peace offerings to burnt offerings
Correct: B — Rabbi Yoḥanan reads 'at the entrance of the Tent of Meeting' as implying that even locations other than the entrance — including the Heikhal interior — are valid, since the entire structure is called the Tent of Meeting.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
According to Rabbi Ḥanina, why was the vessel that measured a tenth of an ephah anointed as a service vessel?
A. To allow the priest to sanctify flour independently, without oil
B. To sanctify the complete meal offering — flour and oil placed together in the vessel
C. To serve as a backup vessel when the primary kli shareit was unavailable
D. To measure frankincense for the shewbread bowls
Correct: B — Rabbi Ḥanina holds that no component is sanctified separately; the vessel was anointed to sanctify the complete combination of flour and oil together. It cannot be used to sanctify flour independently of oil.
