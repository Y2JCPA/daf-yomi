Temurah 33 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Shmuel, on which side of the doorpost must the mezuza be placed, and how is that side determined?
A. The left side, determined by the direction one exits
B. The right side, determined by the direction one enters the room
C. Whichever side bears the door hinges
D. Either side is acceptable as long as it is visible
Correct: B — Shmuel rules the mezuza must be on the right side of the doorpost, with 'right' determined by the direction from which one enters the room — not by hinge placement or any other factor.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What is the ruling if a mezuza is written on two separate sheets of parchment?
A. Valid if both sheets are placed together on the same doorpost
B. Valid if one sheet has Shema and the other has VeHaya
C. Unfit regardless of how it is placed
D. Valid only if wrapped in a protective tube
Correct: C — Rav Yehuda citing Shmuel rules a mezuza on two sheets is unfit. The supporting baraita confirms: even placing them on two doorposts does not help — it remains unfit.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
In Rabbi Ḥanina's analogy contrasting God and a human king, what is the key difference regarding protection?
A. A human king protects from outside; God protects from inside the palace
B. A human king is protected from outside by servants; God stands at Israel's doorpost protecting those inside
C. Both kings are protected from outside; only the method differs
D. God's servants protect Him, unlike a human king whose servants protect the people
Correct: B — Rabbi Ḥanina teaches that unlike a human king whose servants guard him from outside, God stands at His servants' doorpost (the mezuza) to protect Israel dwelling inside — a remarkable reversal of the protective role.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
How many mezuzot does a bei harziki require, and what makes it unique?
A. One mezuza; it is a portico open on the front
B. Two mezuzot; it is a gatehouse opening to a courtyard onto which houses also open
C. Three mezuzot; it has entrances on three sides
D. None; it is exempt as a non-dwelling space
Correct: B — A bei harziki is a gatehouse that opens to a courtyard from which houses are also entered. Both of its entrances — street-side and courtyard-side — require a mezuza, totaling two.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
How does Rav Huna bar Rav Natan resolve the contradiction between Shmuel's 'upper third' ruling and the opinions of Rabbi Yosei and Rabbi Yehuda?
A. Shmuel follows Rabbi Yehuda and means the mezuza must be exactly at the top
B. Shmuel created a new third opinion between Rabbi Yosei and Rabbi Yehuda
C. Shmuel follows Rabbi Yosei: 'upper third' means do not place the mezuza lower than one-third from the top
D. Shmuel's ruling applies only to unusually tall doorposts
Correct: C — Rav Huna bar Rav Natan resolves that Shmuel follows Rabbi Yosei. 'Beginning of the upper third' sets a maximum distance — the mezuza must not be placed more than one-third of the doorpost's height below the crossbeam. It is a limit, not a pinpoint target.
