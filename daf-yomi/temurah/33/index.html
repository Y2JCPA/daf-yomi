🚪 Mezuza Placement: Core Rules of Daf 33

Is this mezuza valid and properly placed?
↓
Written correctly?
Must be on one sheet only — two sheets renders it unfit (Shmuel)
Orientation?
Must be placed vertically — horizontal like a bolt is unfit (Rav)
Position?
Right side upon entry, in the upper third of the doorpost height, not dug into the post
↓
All conditions met → mezuza is valid and fulfills the mitzva

📏 Mezuza Height: Shmuel vs. Rav Huna

Shmuel
It is a mitzva to place the mezuza at the beginning of the upper third of the doorpost.
Rav Huna
Raise the mezuza one handbreadth from the ground, or distance it one handbreadth from the crossbeam. Anywhere between those points is valid.
Gemara
Rav Huna follows Rabbi Yehuda — but whose opinion does Shmuel follow? His ruling seems to fit neither Rabbi Yehuda nor Rabbi Yosei!
Rav Huna b. Rav Natan
Shmuel does follow Rabbi Yosei. 'Beginning of the upper third' means: do not place the mezuza lower than one-third from the top — it is a maximum distance rule, not a precise location.
Resolution The phrase 'upper third' is a ceiling, not a target. So long as the mezuza is within the top third of the doorpost, it is ideally placed — consistent with Rabbi Yosei's view.

🏛️ Structures: Mezuza Obligation?

✅ Obligated

  • Bei harziki (gatehouse to courtyard) — two mezuzot
  • Gatehouse opening to a small room (ulekitonit)
  • Gatehouse built for dwelling (e.g., Exilarch's)
  • Entrance between house and gatehouse

❌ Exempt

  • Open portico — lacks proper doorposts
  • Broken entrances (pitḥei shima'ei) — no doorway form
  • Entrance from gatehouse to garden (garden itself exempt)
  • Portico whose posts serve only to strengthen the ceiling

⚖️ Disputed

  • Gatehouse opening to both garden and small room — Rabbi Yosei vs. Sages
  • Portico with actual doorposts — Rav Ḥisda holds exempt regardless

👑 The King and His Servant

בא וראה שלא כמידת בשר ודם מידת הקדוש ברוך הוא — מידת בשר ודם מלך יושב מבפנים ועבדיו משמרין אותו מבחוץ
Rabbi Ḥanina — Come and See Attribute of flesh and blood: A king sits inside his palace. His servants stand outside to protect him from without.

Attribute of the Holy One, Blessed be He: His servants (Israel) dwell inside their homes — and He stands at their doorpost, guarding them as they go and come.

The mezuza is not Israel protecting God; it is God standing watch at Israel's door.

🏠 Bei Harziki — Why Two Mezuzot?

What is a bei harziki? (Raḥava citing Rav Yehuda)
↓
A gatehouse that opens to a courtyard, and houses also open onto that same courtyard (Rav Pappa the Elder citing Rav)
↓
Entrance 1: Street → Gatehouse
Serves as a dwelling entry point → requires mezuza
Entrance 2: Gatehouse → Courtyard
Leads to the houses beyond → also requires mezuza
↓
Ruling: Two mezuzot required — one at each entrance of the bei harziki
