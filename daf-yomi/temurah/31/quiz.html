Temurah 31 Quiz

5 questions · Self-graded · Test your learning!
QUESTION 1 OF 5
According to Rav (via Rav Ḥananel), if a Torah scroll tear extends across three lines of text, what is the halakha?
A. It may be sewn if done by a qualified scribe
B. It may be sewn only if the parchment is gall-processed
C. It cannot be sewn — the scroll is unfit
D. It must be evaluated case by case by a rabbinic court
Correct: C — Rav rules that a tear spanning three lines cannot be sewn to restore the scroll's fitness. Only tears spanning two lines or fewer may be repaired by sewing.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
What does the Gemara mean when it calls parchment 'old' versus 'new'?
A. Old means it was written on previously; new means it is blank
B. Old means not processed with gall; new means processed with gall
C. Old means from an aged animal; new means from a young animal
D. Old and new refer to whether the parchment has dried out
Correct: B — The Gemara explicitly clarifies: 'old' and 'new' are not literal age descriptions. 'Old' parchment = not processed with gall; 'new' = processed with gall. The two cannot be joined by sewing.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rav rules that a mezuza written 'two by two' is fit. What does this mean?
A. Two columns side by side, each with identical text
B. Two copies of the mezuza written on one piece of parchment
C. Two words on each line throughout the entire mezuza
D. Two lines repeated alternately for emphasis
Correct: C — 'Two by two' means the mezuza was written with exactly two words on each line. Rav rules this format is valid for a kosher mezuza.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In which direction did Rav Huna roll the mezuza, and why?
A. From shema to eḥad (right to left), so eḥad faces outward when placed
B. From eḥad to shema (left to right), so shema faces outward when placed
C. From the center outward, so the Divine Name is protected inside
D. Vertically from top to bottom, following Torah scroll practice
Correct: B — Rabbi Ḥelbo observed Rav Huna rolling from eḥad toward shema — left to right. This means when unrolled, the word shema (the first word of the mezuza text) faces outward and is seen first.
QUESTION 5 OF 5
What limitation did Rabbi Ḥanina of Sura apply to the principle 'the halakha follows Rabbi Shimon Shezuri'?
A. It applies only in cases involving ritual impurity
B. It applies only when Rabbi Yehuda HaNasi endorsed his view
C. It applies only where he taught in a Mishna, not in a Baraita
D. It applies only in the Land of Israel, not in Babylonia
Correct: C — Rabbi Ḥanina of Sura (reported by Mar Zutra to Rav Ashi) argued that the principle is limited to cases where Rabbi Shimon Shezuri taught his ruling in a Mishna — it does not extend to his statements found only in baraitot.
