Menachot 19 - Quiz

Test your knowledge of the daf

1. According to Chachamim, does Yetzikah (pouring oil on a Minchah) require a Kohen?

A) YES — all Minchah Avodos require a Kohen
B) NO — only from Kemitzah and onward must a Kohen perform the Avodah; Yetzikah beforehand may be done by a non-Kohen
C) Only for voluntary Menachos (Nedavah), not obligatory ones
D) Only if the Minchah has more than one log of oil
Chachamim derive from the verse: 'v'Hevi'ah El Bnei Aharon ha'Kohanim v'Kamatz' — only from the bringing (leading to Kemitzah) and onward must a Kohen perform the Avodah. Yetzikah and Belilah before Kemitzah may be done by a non-Kohen, and the Minchah remains valid.

2. What is R. Shimon's 'Vav Mosif' principle and how does it apply?

A) The vav means the verse only applies to the next Avodah in sequence
B) The vav (connecting 've'Hevi'ah') adds the PREVIOUS act (Yetzikah) to 'Bnei Aharon' — teaching that Yetzikah also requires a Kohen
C) R. Shimon holds all Minchah Avodos require a Kohen based on a general rule
D) The vav excludes all non-Kohanim from Minchah preparation
R. Shimon applies 'Vav Mosif Al Inyan Rishon' — the connecting 'vav' adds the PREVIOUS matter to the current requirement. The 've'Hevi'ah El Bnei Aharon' connects backward to Yetzikah, teaching it also falls under 'Bnei Aharon' (requiring a Kohen).

3. Why is slaughter (Shechitah) valid when performed by a non-Kohen?

A) Shechitah is entirely outside Temple Avodah
B) From 'v'Samach... v'Shachat' — just as Semichah (laying hands) is by the owner (non-Kohen), Shechitah may be done by a non-Kohen; plus a Kal va'Chomer from Zerikah
C) A verse explicitly permits non-Kohanim to slaughter
D) Only the High Priest must do Shechitah
The Gemara derives: 'v'Samach... v'Shachat' juxtaposes laying hands and slaughtering. Just as laying hands is by the owner (non-Kohen), Shechitah may be by a non-Kohen. A Kal va'Chomer: if Zerikah (the primary Kapparah act) doesn't require the owner, certainly Shechitah doesn't. Non-Kohen Shechitah is valid.

4. According to Rav, what Torah signal words make an act Me'akev?

A) 'Mitzva' or 'Aseh' wherever they appear
B) The words 'Torah' or 'Chukah' in context of the act — either word alone suffices
C) Any verse that repeats a commandment twice
D) Only acts stated 'v'Asah' (he shall do) are Me'akev
Rav's rule: wherever the Torah writes 'Torah' (instruction) or 'Chukah' (statute) in connection with an act, that act is Me'akev. After multiple challenges (Nazir has only 'Torah'; Yom Kippur has only 'Chukah'), the conclusion: EITHER word alone is sufficient.

5. Why are the four Metzora species Me'akev even though the verse only says 'Torah' not 'Chukah'?

A) All Metzora Avodos are automatically Me'akev due to the severity of Tzara'as
B) The verse 'Zos Tihyeh Toras ha'Metzora' with the emphatic 'Zos Tihyeh' makes it equivalent to Chukah
C) A special Mesorah independent of the Torah/Chukah rule
D) Four is a sacred number always treated as Me'akev
The Gemara explains: 'Zos Tihyeh Toras ha'Metzora' — the emphatic 'Zos Tihyeh' (this shall be) adds the same force as 'Chukah.' It is as if the verse said 'Chukah.' Therefore all four Metzora species are indispensable to each other.
