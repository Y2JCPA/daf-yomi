Menachot 20 - Quiz

Test your knowledge of the daf

1. According to Rav Yosef's final answer, why is salt Me'akev even though it was not 'repeated' in the Torah?

A) Salt is repeated in 'v'Chol Korban Minchascha ba'Melach Timlach'
B) The word 'Bris' (covenant) written about salt is equivalent to a Torah repetition — making salt Me'akev
C) Salt is Me'akev only Rabbinically
D) The Tanna of the Mishnah holds salt is never Me'akev
Rav Yosef's final answer: since the Torah writes 'Bris' (covenant) about salt — 'Bris Melach Olam' (Numbers 18:19) — it is as if the salt requirement was REPEATED. The covenant language serves as an equivalent of repetition, satisfying Rav's rule. Therefore salt IS Me'akev.

2. What is R. Shimon's Gezerah Shavah for the indispensability of salt?

A) He compares salt to Levonah — both are separate items added to the Minchah
B) 'Bris Melach Olam' (salt) parallels 'Bris Kehunas Olam' (priesthood) — just as offerings cannot be sacrificed without Kohanim, they cannot be sacrificed without salt
C) He derives from Sodom that salt represents permanence
D) He compares salt to the Omer
R. Shimon uses a Gezerah Shavah: both salt (Numbers 18:19 — 'Bris Melach Olam') and the priesthood (Numbers 25:13 — 'Bris Kehunas Olam') use 'Bris Olam.' Just as offerings need Kehunah, they need salt. Salt is as fundamental as the priesthood itself.

3. Why is wood NOT included in the salt requirement?

A) Wood is only Rabbinically required
B) Wood itself does not require other things — it IS the supporting item. Only items requiring other things (like wood) need salt, not the support itself
C) Wood is not considered a Korban
D) Salt cannot adhere to wood physically
The Klal-Prat-Klal teaches: include all Korbanos similar to the Minchah — those 'requiring other things' (wood). Wood itself is the 'other thing' that Korbanos require. Wood doesn't require wood. It is the SUPPORT, not the supported. Therefore wood is excluded.

4. What is Abaye's challenge to interpreting 'lo malach' (didn't salt) as meaning no salt was added?

A) Any omission of a Me'akev act makes a Minchah Pasul
B) By comparison with 'lo yatzak' (didn't pour) — which means a non-Kohen poured — 'lo malach' should similarly mean a non-Kohen salted
C) The Torah explicitly says 'do not let salt be lacking' — total omission is impossible
D) Kemitzah automatically includes salt from the Mizbe'ach surface
Abaye's challenge: the Mishnah's 'lo yatzak' (didn't pour) was explained as a non-Kohen pouring instead of a Kohen (since Yetzikah is Me'akev, omitting it entirely would invalidate). By the same logic, 'lo malach' should mean a non-Kohen salted. Rav Yosef counters: a non-Kohen cannot approach the Mizbe'ach to salt the Kometz — so 'lo malach' must mean no salt was added at all.

5. What is the basis for including Levonah in the salt requirement?

A) Levonah is burned on the Mizbe'ach — same as the Kometz
B) Levonah accompanies the Minchah in the same Kli (vessel) — being in the same vessel includes it in the salt requirement
C) Levonah has its own explicit verse requiring salt
D) Levonah is a Korban Tzibur and all communal offerings need salt
The Klal-Prat-Klal would include Korbanos 'requiring other things' — but Levonah brought independently might not qualify alone. The Gemara adds: Levonah accompanies the Minchah in the SAME KLI ('ba'ah imah b'Kli Echad'). Being placed in the same vessel includes Levonah in the salt requirement.
