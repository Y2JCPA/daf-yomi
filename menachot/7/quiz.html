1. According to R' Yochanan, when do Klei Shares (service vessels) sanctify items placed in them?

A) Always, regardless of intent
B) Only when the item is placed with specific intent (mi'Da'as)
C) Never ‚Äî they only hold items but don't sanctify
D) Only on Shabbat and Yom Tov
R' Yochanan teaches that Klei Shares sanctify items only when placed with intent. This explains why returning a Kometz casually to the Minchah vessel doesn't permanently disqualify it ‚Äî there was no intent for the vessel to sanctify it.

2. What is the "monkey trick" solution for returning the Kometz without sanctifying it?

A) Have a non-Kohen return the Kometz
B) Place the Kometz on the vessel wall and shake the vessel so it falls in by itself
C) Use a different vessel that isn't a Kli Shares
D) Cover the vessel with a cloth first
Place the Kometz on the wall of the vessel, then shake it so the Kometz falls into the furrow on its own. Since no person directly placed it inside, it's as if a monkey returned it ‚Äî no human intent, no sanctification.

3. What proof does Rav Sheshes bring that Kemitzah from a Kli on the ground is valid?

A) A verse in Vayikra explicitly permits it
B) The Mishnah about Lechem ha'Panim counts every Kohen but none lifts the Shulchan off the ground
C) Avimi demonstrated it in front of Rav Nachman
D) Ben Beteira's ruling implies it
The Mishnah (99b) about replacing the Lechem ha'Panim meticulously counts all 8 Kohanim involved, yet never mentions anyone lifting the Shulchan. If lifting were required, it would be counted. Therefore, removing items from a vessel on the ground (like the Shulchan) is valid ‚Äî and so is Kemitzah.

4. If a Kometz is split into two vessels, what is the final Halachah?

A) It is sanctified and valid
B) It is not sanctified ‚Äî we learn from blood that sanctification cannot be done in halves
C) It depends on whether the vessels are on the ground
D) Both Rav Nachman and Rava agree it is sanctified
Rav Nachman says not sanctified. Rava initially says sanctified but then retracts, learning from blood (Kabalah) that sanctification in halves is invalid. Source: Kidush Mei Chattas with less than a Shi'ur in each vessel is also invalid.

5. Why did Avimi travel to his student Rav Chisda's Beis Midrash?

A) Rav Chisda refused to come to him
B) Avimi forgot Masechet Menachot and believed the effort of going would help him retain the learning
C) Rav Chisda had the only copy of the Masechta
D) It was a public lecture that Avimi wanted to attend
Avimi forgot Masechet Menachot (it was "uprooted" from him) and went to his own student Rav Chisda to recover it. He could have summoned Rav Chisda, but believed the exertion and humility of going himself would help the learning stick better.
