Chachamim generally require the ENTIRE Matir. But Minchas Chotei and Minchas Kena'os have NO Levonah â€” the Kometz alone IS the complete Matir. Therefore, even Chachamim agree: intent during Kemitzah alone = full Pigul + Karet.

3. What is Rav's position on when the R. Meir vs. Chachamim machloket applies?

A) They argue even when both Kometz and Levonah had improper intent

C) They argue only in cases involving Minchas Chotei
D) They argue only when two different Kohanim performed the acts
Rav holds: when the Kometz had Chutz li'Zmano intent and the Levonah was silent, ALL agree it is Pigul (each act follows the initial intent). The machloket only applies when the Kometz was in silence and the Levonah with intent. Shmuel disagrees and says the machloket applies in both orderings.

Rav Chanina explains: the Baraisa describes a case where TWO SEPARATE KOHANIM performed the acts. Since the second Kohen is a different person, we cannot apply the rule 'each act follows the initial intent' â€” that only works when the SAME Kohen performs all the Avodos. When different Kohanim are involved, even Rav agrees the machloket applies.

B) No Karet unless ALL required blood applications were done with Chutz li'Zmano intent

Chachamim hold consistently: 'Ain bo Karet ad sheyefagel b'chol ha'Matir' â€” no Karet unless the ENTIRE permitting factor had improper intent. For inner Korbanos with many blood applications, ALL must have Chutz li'Zmano intent for Karet. One improper application = Pasul only.
