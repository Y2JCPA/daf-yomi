The New Mishnah: Pigul in Half the Matir

R. Meir vs. Chachamim: can Pigul result from intent during only ONE of the two Matir components?

R. Meir

  • Mefaglin b'Chatzi Matir — even half the Matir creates Pigul + Karet
  • Intent during Kometz-only OR Levonah-only suffices

Chachamim

  • No Karet unless intent during the ENTIRE Matir
  • Both Kometz AND Levonah must have Chutz li'Zmano intent
  • One-sided intent = Pasul, no Karet
Exception where all agree: Minchas Chotei and Minchas Kena'os have no Levonah — the Kometz alone IS the entire Matir. Intent during Kemitzah alone = Pigul + Karet (all agree).

Rav vs. Shmuel: When Does the Machloket Apply?

RAV
Rav: The machloket (R. Meir vs. Chachamim) only applies when Kometz was in silence and Levonah with intent. If Kometz had intent and Levonah was silent — ALL agree it is Pigul (each subsequent act follows the initial intent).
SHM
Shmuel: They argue EVEN when Kometz had intent and Levonah was silent — the machloket is broader.
RCH
Rav Chanina resolves: A Baraisa supports Shmuel. Answer: that Baraisa describes TWO DIFFERENT KOHANIM performing the Kometz and Levonah — we cannot assume the second followed the first's intent.
