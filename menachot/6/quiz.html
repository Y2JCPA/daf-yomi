Test your knowledge of the daf

1. What ultimately proves that the kal va'chomer for Tereifah requires a verse?

A) Chelev and Dam come from permitted animals
B) Ba'al Mum and Yotzei Dofen have visible (nikar) defects, but a Tereifah's defect may be hidden
C) Melikah is entirely forbidden to ordinary people
D) A Tereifah Kohen can still perform Avodah
The final answer is "Muman Nikar" ‚Äî the common element of Ba'al Mum and Yotzei Dofen is that their defects are visible. A Tereifah's defect (like a punctured intestine) may be completely hidden, so the comparison breaks down and a verse is needed.

2. Why are three verses needed to exclude a Tereifah from being a korban?

A) One for birds, one for animals, one for Menachot
B) One for each of the three categories of Pesul: person, item, place
C) Each covers a different scenario: never fit, became Tereifah then sanctified, sanctified then became Tereifah
D) One is d'Oraisa, one is d'Rabbanan, one is an Asmachta
"Mi'Mashke Yisrael" covers items never permitted (like Orlah). "Kol Asher Ya'avor" covers an animal that became Tereifah before being sanctified. "Min ha'Bakar" covers an animal sanctified first that later became Tereifah.

3. According to Ben Beteira, what do you do if Kemitzah was done with the left hand?

A) The Minchah is permanently disqualified
B) Return the Kometz to the Minchah and redo Kemitzah with the right hand
C) Offer the Kometz as-is since left hand is valid b'di'eved
D) Add oil and Levonah to fix the problem
Ben Beteira says: return the handful to the Minchah and do Kemitzah again with the right hand. Rav extends this to all the Pesulim in the Mishnah (Zar, Onen, etc.), not just left-hand Kemitzah.

4. What makes a Kometz "Chaser" (deficient)?

A) If the Kohen uses his left hand
B) If it includes a pebble, grain of salt, or grain of Levonah ‚Äî or if the fingers were curled instead of extended
C) If it was done while sitting down
D) If a non-Kohen assisted in the scooping
A Kometz is Chaser if foreign objects (pebble, salt grain, Levonah grain) take up space inside it, or if the Kohen curled his fingers instead of extending them flat, resulting in less flour than a proper handful.

5. Why does the Mishnah specifically mention Minchas Chotei alongside "all Menachot"?

A) Because Minchas Chotei is the most common Minchah
B) Because you might think R' Shimon (who says a sinner's korban shouldn't be beautiful) would allow Kemitzah by a Pasul
C) Because Minchas Chotei has extra oil requirements
D) Because it is brought on behalf of the community
R' Shimon holds that Minchas Chotei has no oil or Levonah so the sinner's korban won't be "Mehudar" (beautiful). You might extend that logic and think Kemitzah by a Pasul is also acceptable. The Mishnah teaches that even R' Shimon agrees this disqualifies.
