1. What is R. Meir's principle regarding Pigul and the Shavuot lambs?

A) Only the second lamb slaughtered can create Pigul
B) Mefaglin b'Chatzi Matir ‚Äî even half a permitting factor (one of two lambs) can create Pigul
C) Both lambs must be slaughtered by the same Kohen for Pigul to apply
D) Pigul only applies to the loaves, not the lambs themselves
R. Meir holds "Mefaglin b'Chatzi Matir" ‚Äî even when only ONE of the two lambs was slaughtered with Chutz li'Zmano intent, this creates Pigul with Karet for the loaves. One lamb IS a partial Matir, and that is sufficient for R. Meir to apply the Pigul status.

2. According to Rebbi's Baraisa, if each lamb was slaughtered with intent to eat ¬Ω k'Zayis from a DIFFERENT loaf, what is the result?

A) Pigul ‚Äî the two halves combine to form a full k'Zayis
B) Kosher ‚Äî because the intent was split (¬Ω from one loaf, ¬Ω from the other), the halves do not combine
C) Pasul but no Karet ‚Äî the intent was for two different items
D) Pigul only if both lambs were slaughtered by the same Kohen
Rebbi (citing his ruling in the Baraisa) says: when each lamb was slaughtered with intent for ¬Ω k'Zayis from a DIFFERENT loaf ‚Äî the offering is Kosher. The halves targeting different loaves do not combine. But if each lamb was slaughtered with intent for "a k'Zayis from BOTH loaves," then the intents do combine and create Pigul.

3. Rav Huna interprets the Baraisa's requirement as "Bishneihem" (masculine) rather than "Bishteihem" (feminine). What does this mean?

A) Both loaves must have been waved for Pigul to apply
B) The Pigul intent must occur during the slaughter of BOTH lambs (masculine), not just both loaves (feminine)
C) Both Kohanim must have agreed to the intent
D) The intent must target both animals before both loaves
By reading "Bishneihem" (masculine = both lambs), Rav Huna explains the Baraisa as Chachamim's view: Karet applies only when BOTH lambs were slaughtered with Chutz li'Zmano intent. The Baraisa is not requiring intent for both loaves (that would be feminine "Bishteihem") ‚Äî it requires intent during both lambs' slaughter.

4. How does Rav Ashi use the Shirei Dam proof to challenge the claim that R. Yosi views the thighs as "two separate bodies"?

A) R. Yosi holds that Shirei Dam intent never creates Pigul
B) R. Yosi says intent for Shirei Dam (not even meat) creates Pigul in the meat ‚Äî so surely intent for one thigh (which IS meat) must Mefagel the other thigh, showing thighs are NOT separate bodies
C) Rav Ashi proves R. Yosi views the loaves as completely independent
D) The proof shows that only the Kohen Gadol can create Pigul in inner Korbanos
Rav Ashi reasons: R. Yosi holds that intent to "leave the Shirei Dam until tomorrow" (which is not even an intent regarding the meat!) still creates Pigul in the meat (a fortiori from a Baraisa). Therefore, intent for one thigh (which IS meat) must certainly Mefagel the other thigh. This proves R. Yosi does NOT consider the thighs as two completely separate bodies immune to cross-Pigul.

5. What is Chachamim's position on Pigul for inner Korbanos (e.g., Yom Kippur Par) when only one blood application had Chutz li'Zmano intent?

A) Pigul + Karet ‚Äî each application of blood independently creates full Pigul
B) No Karet unless ALL required blood applications were done with Chutz li'Zmano intent
C) The offering is entirely Kosher ‚Äî inner Korbanos cannot become Pigul at all
D) Karet applies only to the Kohen who performed that specific application
Chachamim hold consistently: "Ain bo Karet ad sheyefagel b'chol ha'Matir" ‚Äî there is no Karet unless one has Chutz li'Zmano intent during the sacrifice of the ENTIRE permitting factor. For inner Korbanos with multiple blood applications, ALL the required applications must be done with Chutz li'Zmano intent. This is parallel to their position on the two Shavuot lambs.
