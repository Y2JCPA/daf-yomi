1. What is the Teyuvta (conclusive refutation) against Rav's position on Omer Lo Lishmah?

A) A Mishnah about the Two Loaves (Shtei ha'Lechem)
B) A Baraisa about the Asham Metzora slaughtered Lo Lishmah ‚Äî it goes on the Mizbe'ach but the Metzora needs another Asham
C) Reish Lakish's statement about He'ir Mizrach
D) Rava's three conditions for Machshavah
The Baraisa about the Asham Metzora proves that a "Ba'ah l'Hattir" korban slaughtered Lo Lishmah is still valid (goes on the Mizbe'ach) ‚Äî it just doesn't render the Metzora fit. This refutes Rav's blanket disqualification.

2. According to Rav Papa, what permits Chadash (new crop) on 16 Nisan according to Reish Lakish?

A) The actual offering of the Omer on the Mizbe'ach
B) The Kemitzah (removal of the handful)
C) The illumination of the eastern horizon (dawn)
D) The waving (Tenufah) of the Omer
Rav Papa explains that Reish Lakish holds "He'ir Mizrach Mattir" ‚Äî the illumination of the eastern horizon (dawn) on 16 Nisan permits Chadash, even before the Omer is actually sacrificed. R' Yochanan and Reish Lakish both hold this even when the Beis HaMikdash stands.

3. What are Rava's three conditions for improper intent (Machshavah) to disqualify a korban?

A) Fit animal, fit time, fit owner
B) Fit Kohen, fit item (korban), fit place (Mizbe'ach)
C) Fit Kohen, fit time, fit intent
D) Fit blood, fit oil, fit flour
Rava requires: (1) a fit Kohen (excluding a Ba'al Mum), (2) a fit item (excluding the Omer which is a "Chiddush" ‚Äî made of barley), and (3) a fit place (excluding a damaged Mizbe'ach). Since the Omer fails condition #2, Lo Lishmah intent cannot disqualify it at all.

4. Why does Rav Papa reject the principle of "Ein Mechusar Zeman l'vo ba'Yom" as the basis for Reish Lakish's ruling?

A) A Baraisa about a Metzora's Chattas slaughtered before the Asham ‚Äî we let it rot rather than wait, proving same-day timing doesn't help
B) The principle only applies to animal offerings, not Menachot
C) Reish Lakish explicitly rejected this principle
D) The Torah says "ba'Yom" excludes Menachot
Rav Papa's own objection (from a Baraisa about the Metzora's Chattas slaughtered before the Asham) shows that even for Shechitah ‚Äî which isn't technically an Avodah and shouldn't require the "Tihyeh" order ‚Äî the same-day principle doesn't help. This leads him to abandon "Mechusar Zeman" and instead propose "He'ir Mizrach Mattir."

5. In the kal va'chomer chain about Tereifah, what is the refutation (pircha) against learning from Chelev and Dam?

A) Chelev and Dam are only forbidden by Rabbinic decree
B) Chelev and Dam come from a generally permitted animal ‚Äî unlike a Tereifah which is entirely forbidden
C) Chelev and Dam are not actually offered on the Mizbe'ach
D) The prohibition of Chelev and Dam only applies inside the Beis HaMikdash
The Gemara rejects Chelev/Dam as a pircha because they "come from something generally permitted" (ba'in min ha'Mutar) ‚Äî the animal itself is kosher for eating. A Tereifah, by contrast, is entirely forbidden. So you can't compare the two.
