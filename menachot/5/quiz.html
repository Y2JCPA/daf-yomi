According to Reish Lakish, if Minchas Ha'Omer was offered Lo Lishmah, the Kometz is burned on the Mizbeach but the remainder may not be eaten. What is the basis for allowing the Kometz to be burned?
A. The Omer is an exception to "mi'Mashke Yisrael"
B. Reish Lakish holds "He'ir Mizrach Mattir" ‚Äî dawn of the 16th of Nisan itself permits Chadash, even before the Omer is offered
C. The Kometz is considered like Hekdesh and doesn't need permission
D. A second Omer retroactively permits the first
Correct: B. Rav Papa concludes that Reish Lakish's real reasoning is "He'ir Mizrach Mattir" ‚Äî the illumination of the eastern horizon on the 16th permits Chadash, even while the Beis HaMikdash is standing. This is also the view of R' Yochanan. Since Chadash is already permitted by dawn, the grain is "mi'Mashke Yisrael" and can be offered.
QUESTION 2 OF 5
Rav Adda bar Ahavah initially proposed "Ein Mechusar Zeman l'Vo ba'Yom" (same-day timing doesn't count as missing its time). What case from the laws of Metzora ultimately defeated this principle?
A. Oil placed on the Behonos before blood ‚Äî must be redone
B. Seven sprinklings done before the blood ‚Äî must be redone
C. Chatas Metzora slaughtered before the Asham ‚Äî the blood must rot and the offering is burned
D. The Log of oil was measured incorrectly
Correct: C. Rav Papa's own objection: if Chatas Metzora was slaughtered before the Asham, we should be able to stir the blood, do the Asham first, then finish the Chatas. But the Beraisa says we can't ‚Äî the Chatas must rot. Since Shechitah isn't an Avodah (so "Tihyeh" doesn't explain it), this disproves "Ein Mechusar Zeman l'Vo ba'Yom."
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava, Lo Lishmah intent has no effect on Minchas Ha'Omer. Which of his three conditions does the Omer fail?
A. Fit person ‚Äî the Kohen is disqualified
B. Fit item ‚Äî the Omer is a "Chiddush" (from barley, not wheat) and isn't a regular fit item for Avodah
C. Fit place ‚Äî the Mizbeach is damaged
D. The Omer fails all three conditions
Correct: B. Rava's second condition: intent only disqualifies "b'Davar ha'Ra'uy la'Avodah" ‚Äî with regard to an item fit for regular service. Minchas Ha'Omer is a "Chiddush" because it comes from barley (all other Menachot are wheat). Since it's inherently unusual, Lo Lishmah intent simply cannot take effect on it.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
In the Kal va'Chomer that a Tereifah shouldn't be offered, the Gemara tries to refute it with "Melikah" (bird offering killed by nape-pinch). Why is Melikah rejected as a counter-proof?
A. Melikah is only done on birds, not animals
B. Melikah is a Rabbinic enactment
C. The Kedushah itself causes the prohibition ‚Äî only upon consecration does pinching make it Neveilah
D. Melikah was already rejected by Rav earlier
Correct: C. "Kedushasa Osarsa" ‚Äî the sanctity itself is what creates the prohibition. A bird only becomes "Neveilah" through Melikah at the moment of its consecrated service. Before that, it was a perfectly normal bird. A Tereifah, by contrast, was always forbidden ‚Äî its prohibition predates and is independent of any Kedushah.

Correct: B. "Mitzvaso b'Chach" ‚Äî in each case, the prohibition itself IS how the mitzvah works: the Omer must be from Chadash (forbidden grain), Ketores must be prepared in its unique way (forbidden for personal use), Korbanos override Shabbat (forbidden labor), and Bigdei Kehunah include Shatnez (forbidden mixture). You can't use them to prove that Tereifah ‚Äî which has NO mitzvah requiring it ‚Äî should also be permitted.
