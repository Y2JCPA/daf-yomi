Why does the Baraisa conclude that the Tzitz atones for Tum'ah but NOT for Piggul or Notar?
A. The Tzitz only covers sins done by Kohanim
B. Piggul is excluded by "Lo Yechashev" and Notar by "Lo Yeratzeh" ‚Äî both are categorically rejected by the Torah. Tum'ah has a permitted counterpart (communal Tum'ah), so the Tzitz can atone for it.
C. The Tzitz can atone for all three equally
D. Notar and Piggul are more severe than Tum'ah
Correct: B. The Baraisa works by elimination. Piggul = "Lo Yechashev" (never reckoned). Notar = "Lo Yeratzeh" (never accepted). Only Tum'ah remains ‚Äî and since Tum'ah is permitted for communal offerings, the Tzitz can effect acceptance for individual Tum'ah cases.

Correct: B. Abaye derives from "l'Ratzon lahem Lifnei Hashem": the Tzitz effects acceptance only for sins committed "before God" ‚Äî i.e., inside the Temple. A Yotzei that left the Azarah is no longer "before God," so the Tzitz cannot help. R' Zeira's challenge (Bamah also has a permitted counterpart) is answered by this "Lifnei Hashem" limitation.
