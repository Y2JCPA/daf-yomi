The Gemara asks: if Kodshei Kodashim were slaughtered in the north l'Shem Kodashim Kalim, shouldn't it be "Ma'aseha Mochichin" (provably wrong intent)? Why doesn't the Gemara accept this?
A. Kodashim Kalim can only be slaughtered in the south
B. Kodashim Kalim may be slaughtered anywhere in the Azarah, including the north ‚Äî so the location proves nothing
C. The Kohen cannot determine his own location in the Azarah
D. R' Shimon doesn't apply his principle to Kodashim
Correct: B. The Mishnah in Zevachim states: "Shechitasan b'chol makom ba'Azarah" ‚Äî Kodashim Kalim can be slaughtered anywhere. So slaughtering in the north doesn't prove it's Kodshei Kodashim.

The Gemara asks: an Olah (male) slaughtered l'Shem Chatas (female) ‚Äî shouldn't the gender difference prove the intent is wrong? What is the Gemara's answer?
A. Male and female animals look identical
B. There is a male Chatas ‚Äî the Se'ir Nasi ‚Äî so the gender doesn't conclusively prove anything
C. The Torah doesn't distinguish between male and female offerings
D. Gender is irrelevant for Kodashim
Correct: B. Since the Se'ir (goat) of a Nasi is a male Chatas, one cannot definitively conclude from the animal's gender that it cannot be a Chatas. Even after narrowing to "Chatas of an individual" (female), the Gemara says people simply don't pay attention to gender ‚Äî "lav a'da'ataihu d'inshi."
QUESTION 3 OF 5
According to Rava's resolution, what verse teaches that all Menachot share one law, allowing a Minchah offered l'Shem a different Minchah to still fulfill the obligation?
A. "V'Shachat Osah l'Chatas" (Vayikra 4:33)
B. "Kodesh Kodashim Hi ka'Chatas" (Vayikra 6:10)
C. "V'Zos Toras HaMinchah" (Vayikra 6:7) ‚Äî one law for all Menachot
D. "Motza Sefatecha Tishmor" (Devarim 23:24)
Correct: C. Rava derives from "V'Zos Toras HaMinchah" that there is "Torah Achas l'chol HaMenuchos" ‚Äî one law for all meal offerings. Therefore, offering one Minchah l'Shem a different Minchah is like offering it Lishmah. But the Torah does NOT write "V'Zos Toras HaMinchah v'Zevach," so Minchah l'Shem Zevach does NOT work.

According to Rav Ashi, what is the critical distinction in R' Shimon's position?
A. Whether the intent was Shinuy Kodesh or Shinuy Ba'alim
B. Whether the intent concerned the vessel (e.g., "l'Shem Marcheshes") or the offering itself (e.g., "l'Shem Minchas Marcheshes")
C. Whether the Minchah was Charevah or Blulah
D. Whether the Kohen or the owner had the wrong intent
Correct: B. Rav Ashi distinguishes: if the Kohen said "l'Shem Marcheshes" (just the vessel name), his intent is about the Kli ‚Äî vessel intent doesn't disqualify, so the offering is valid. But if he said "l'Shem Minchas Marcheshes" (the offering name), his intent concerns the offering itself, which DOES disqualify.
