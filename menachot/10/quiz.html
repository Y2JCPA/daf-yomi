1. Why does the Torah write both "Al Dam ha'Asham" and "Al Makom Dam ha'Asham" for the Metzora's oil?

A) One is for a wealthy Metzora and one for a poor Metzora
B) Together they teach that the oil is placed on the thumb whether the blood is still there or already wiped off
C) One refers to the right hand and one to the left
D) They teach the order of application
"Al Dam ha'Asham" alone would suggest oil only goes if blood is present. "Al Makom Dam" alone would suggest only if blood is wiped off (otherwise it's a Chatzitzah). Together: oil goes either way, and the blood is not an interposition.

2. What three Halachos does Rava derive from the Metzora parshah via Gezerah Shavah?

A) Shechitah, Kabalah, and Zerikah all require the right hand
B) Kemitzah uses the right hand (Yad-Yad), Chalitzah uses the right foot (Regel-Regel), Retziah pierces the right ear (Ozen-Ozen)
C) Three types of Menachot require oil
D) The Kohen Gadol's three daily Avodos
The "extra" mentions of right hand, foot, and ear in the Metzora oil verses are unnecessary for the Metzora itself (since "Al Makom Dam" already tells us the location). Rava uses them as Gezerah Shavah links: Yad‚ÜíKemitzah, Regel‚ÜíChalitzah, Ozen‚ÜíRetziah of an Eved Ivri.

3. According to Rava (Chachamim), what in the Torah mandates using the right hand?

A) Only when both "Etzba" and "Kehunah" appear together
B) Either "Etzba" (finger) OR "Kehunah" (priesthood) alone ‚Äî for matters that are Me'akev Kaparah
C) Whenever "Yad" (hand) is written
D) Only when "Yemanis" (right) is explicitly stated
Rava corrects the initial understanding: either "Etzba" or "Kehunah" alone requires the right hand, but only for things that are Me'akev Kaparah (indispensable for atonement). That's why conveying limbs to the ramp (not Me'akev) can use the left hand despite mentioning "Kehunah."

4. How does R' Shimon differ from the Chachamim on the right-hand requirement?

A) R' Shimon never requires the right hand
B) R' Shimon holds "Etzba" alone requires the right hand, but "Kehunah" alone only requires wearing Bigdei Kehunah ‚Äî not the right hand
C) R' Shimon requires both hands for every Avodah
D) R' Shimon permits left hand for all Avodos
R' Shimon reads "Kehunah" as teaching "b'Kehuno" ‚Äî the Kohen must wear proper garments. It doesn't mandate the right hand. Only "Etzba" (finger) or "Yad" (hand) mandates the right side. This is why R' Shimon permits left-hand Kabalah and Zerikah (which only mention "Kehunah").

5. Why does R' Shimon need Rava's Gezerah Shavah "Yad-Yad" for Minchas Chotei?

A) Minchas Chotei doesn't mention "Kehunah" at all
B) You might think that since R' Shimon says the sinner's korban shouldn't be beautiful, left-hand Kemitzah would be acceptable ‚Äî "Yad-Yad" teaches it must still be the right hand

R' Shimon already learns that regular Kemitzah needs the right hand from "Kodesh Kodoshim ka'Chattas." But for Minchas Chotei specifically, since R' Shimon says the korban shouldn't be "Mehudar" (beautiful), you might extend this to say left-hand Kemitzah is fine. The Gezerah Shavah teaches: no ‚Äî always right hand, even for the sinner's Minchah.
