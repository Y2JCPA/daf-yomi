Menachot 18 - Quiz

Test your knowledge of the daf

1. According to R. Yehudah, if a priest slaughters with intent to leave SOME blood until tomorrow, what is the result?

A) Kosher — only intent to leave ALL blood is problematic
B) Pasul — Rabbinic decree because of the worse case of leaving all blood
C) Pigul + Karet — leaving blood is similar to Chutz li'Zmano intent
D) Machloket: R. Eliezer Pasul, Chachamim Kosher
R. Yehudah holds all Tannaim agree: Pasul. If one ACTUALLY leaves all the blood (without Zerikah), the offering is Pasul. Therefore, even intent to leave SOME blood is Rabbinically decreed Pasul — as a fence against the worse case.

2. Why did Yosef the Babylonian's face light up when R. Elazar mentioned 'R. Eliezer says Pasul'?

A) He disagreed with R. Eliezer and was glad to find someone who ruled Kosher
B) R. Yehudah had taught Yosef it is Pasul but no other source confirmed it — R. Eliezer's opinion confirmed Yosef's tradition was authentic
C) Yosef wanted the offering to be valid
D) He discovered R. Elazar agreed with his strict position
Yosef the Babylonian had been taught by R. Yehudah that the offering is Pasul. But no one else confirmed this. When R. Elazar mentioned 'R. Eliezer says Pasul,' Yosef understood: R. Yehudah (son of R. Elai, student of R. Eliezer) had transmitted R. Eliezer's Pasul opinion. His tradition was verified!

3. What does R. Elazar ben Shammua's emotional reaction to Yosef teach us?

A) That minority opinions should never be taught
B) That confirming a Torah tradition — even a minority view — brings profound joy; R. Elazar wept and quoted 'How I love Your Torah'
C) That Yosef was wrong to seek other scholars' opinions
D) That the Halacha always follows R. Eliezer
R. Elazar ben Shammua wept and said: 'Happy are Torah scholars for whom Torah is so precious!' He quoted Psalms 119:97: 'How I love Your Torah; it is my meditation all the day.' The love of Torah precision and joy at confirming a tradition — even about a single law — is a supreme expression of Torah devotion.

4. Which Avodos does the new Mishnah declare are NOT Me'akev?

A) Kemitzah and Haktarah
B) Yetzikah, Belilah, Petisah, Tenufah, Hagashah, and salting
C) Only Tenufah and Hagashah
D) Only salting and mixing
The Mishnah lists many non-Me'akev Avodos: Yetzikah (pouring oil), Belilah (mixing), Petisah (breaking), Melach (salting), Tenufah (waving), and Hagashah (bringing near). If these were not properly performed, the Minchah remains valid. Only Kemitzah and Haktarah are truly indispensable.

5. Why does the Gemara say 'lo yatzak' must mean a non-Kohen poured, not that no oil was poured at all?

A) Non-Kohanim always perform preparatory Avodos
B) Yetzikah is Me'akev (written in Torah) — not pouring at all would invalidate; the Mishnah ruling it valid must mean a non-Kohen poured instead of a Kohen
C) The Mishnah refers only to Avodos valid regardless of who performs them
D) A non-Kohen's Minchah act equals a Kohen's
Abaye explains: Yetzikah (pouring oil) is repeated in the Torah making it Me'akev. If the Mishnah ruled 'lo yatzak — Kosher,' it cannot mean no oil was poured (that would be invalid). It must mean a non-Kohen poured instead of a Kohen. The same logic applies to other listed acts.
