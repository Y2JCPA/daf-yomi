Intent to Leave Over Blood: Three Views

OpinionIntent to leave SOME blood tomorrowIntent to leave ALL blood
First TannaKosher (all agree)Pasul (all agree)
R. YehudahPasul — Rabbinic decreePasul
R. Elazar's BaraisaMachloket: R. Eliezer=Pasul; Chachamim=KosherPasul (all agree)
R. Yehudah's reasoning: If one ACTUALLY leaves all the blood until tomorrow (without Zerikah), the offering is Pasul. Therefore, even intent to leave SOME blood is Rabbinically decreed Pasul — as a fence against the worse case.

The Inspiring Story of Yosef the Babylonian

RBB
Rebbi relates: "I went to R. Elazar ben Shammua and found Yosef the Babylonian sitting before him — who treasured every teaching..."
YOF
Yosef asked repeatedly: "If one slaughters with intent to leave some blood until tomorrow — what is the law?"
REB
R. Elazar replied "Kosher" — evening, morning, noon, afternoon — until: "Kosher... but R. Eliezer says Pasul."
YOF
Yosef's face lit up! "R. Yehudah taught me Pasul but I could find no one else who said so. You have returned what I lost!"
REB
R. Elazar wept: "Happy are Torah scholars for whom Torah is so dear!" He quoted Psalms 119:97: "How I love Your Torah — it is my meditation all day."

New Mishnah: Non-Me'akev Avodos of the Minchah

NOT Me'akev

  • Yetzikah — pouring oil (if non-Kohen poured)
  • Belilah — mixing with oil
  • Petisah — breaking into pieces
  • Melach — salting (if non-Kohen)
  • Tenufah — waving
  • Hagashah — bringing near the altar

ME'AKEV — Indispensable

  • Kemitzah — removing the handful
  • Haktarah — burning the Kometz
  • These are the core Matir acts
