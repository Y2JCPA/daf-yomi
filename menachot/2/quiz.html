B. Kosher, and fulfills the owner's obligation

QUESTION 2 OF 5
Which two meal offerings are the exceptions ‚Äî where Lo Lishmah makes the offering completely Pasul?
A. Minchas Machavas and Minchas Marcheshes
B. Minchas Chotei and Minchas Kenaot
C. Minchas Nedavah and Minchas Sotah
D. Minchas Kohanim and Minchas Chavitin
Correct: B. Minchas Chotei (the sinner's meal offering) and Minchas Kenaot (the Sotah's jealousy offering) parallel the Chatas ‚Äî just as a Chatas slaughtered Lo Lishmah is Pasul, so too these Menachot.
QUESTION 3 OF 5
Rava teaches that if an Olah was slaughtered Lo Lishmah, it is forbidden to also do Zerikah Lo Lishmah. What is the logical argument?
A. Two Lo Lishmah actions cancel each other out
B. The Torah specifically forbids double Lo Lishmah
C. Just because you deviated once, should you continue deviating in every subsequent step?
D. The second Lo Lishmah would make it Piggul
Correct: C. Rava's sevara (logical argument): Just because one step was done improperly doesn't give license to continue doing all remaining steps improperly. One deviation doesn't justify further deviations.
QUESTION 4 OF 5
What is R' Shimon's principle of "Ma'aseha Mochichin" (its actions prove)?
A. The Kohen's physical actions determine intent, not his words
B. If the offering's physical appearance contradicts the stated Lo Lishmah intent, the intent is disregarded
C. All offerings are validated by the act of Kemitzah alone
D. The owner's intent overrides the Kohen's intent
Correct: B. R' Shimon holds that if you say "l'Shem Marcheshes" but the Minchah is visibly a Machavas (different texture, cooked differently), the physical evidence overrides the verbal intent. The Torah only disqualifies intent that is "not recognizably false" (machshavah d'lo minakra).
