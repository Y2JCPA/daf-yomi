1. Why can't we use the "Tefel/Ikar" logic to permit eating Kodshei Kodoshim in the Heichal without a verse?

A) The Heichal is too small for eating
B) A slave serves in his master's place but does NOT eat there ‚Äî eating in the holier place is presumptively disrespectful
C) The Heichal has no tables for eating
D) Only the Kohen Gadol may enter the Heichal
The Gemara distinguishes service from eating: a servant serves before his master (so performing Avodah in the more sacred Heichal makes sense), but a servant doesn't eat before his master. Therefore, we can't assume eating in the holier place is permitted without an explicit verse.

2. What is the machloket about Belilah (mixing) done outside the Azarah?

A) Both agree it's valid since a Zar can do it
B) R' Yochanan: Pasul (Kli Shares requires inside the Azarah). Reish Lakish: valid (no Kehunah needed = no Azarah needed)
C) Both agree it's Pasul since oil must be sanctified first
D) Only Pasul if done by a non-Kohen
Both agree a Zar can do the mixing (since pouring/mixing are before Kemitzah, which is where Kehunah begins). But R' Yochanan says that since mixing involves a Kli Shares, it must be done inside the Azarah. Reish Lakish disagrees: no Kehunah = no Azarah requirement. A Baraisa supports R' Yochanan.

3. If a Minchah became Chaser (depleted) before Kemitzah, what does R' Yochanan say?

A) The Minchah is permanently disqualified
B) Bring flour from home and refill it ‚Äî Kemitzah is what "establishes" the Minchah
C) Do Kemitzah on whatever remains
D) Start over with a new Minchah
R' Yochanan holds that Kemitzah establishes (Kov'ah) the Minchah. Before Kemitzah, you can still add flour from home to complete the measure. Reish Lakish holds Kidush Kli establishes it (and thus you can't refill), but he's refuted from the Metzora's Log of oil which CAN be refilled after vessel sanctification.

4. According to R' Yehoshua (per R' Yochanan's reading), when are the Shirayim too depleted to burn the Kometz?

A) Whenever any amount is missing
B) Only when the Shirayim are completely gone ‚Äî if some portion remains, the Kometz is burned
C) When less than half remains
D) R' Yehoshua always permits burning the Kometz
R' Yochanan reads R' Yehoshua narrowly: he only disqualifies when the Shirayim are entirely gone (impure, burned, or lost). But if even a portion remains, R' Yehoshua concedes the Kometz may still be burned. This is supported by R' Yehoshua's own Baraisa about a k'Zayis of meat or fat remaining.

5. Why was Reish Lakish silent when R' Yochanan challenged him from the Lechem ha'Panim Baraisa?

A) He agreed with R' Yochanan
B) He couldn't distinguish communal from individual offerings: "Chaser" is like a Ba'al Mum (even the community can't offer it), or they already argued about the Omer (which IS communal)
C) The Baraisa was only d'Rabbanan
D) He was waiting for more sources before responding
Two explanations for his silence: Rav Adda says "Chaser is like a Ba'al Mum" ‚Äî even communal offerings can't be blemished, so the Tzibur distinction doesn't help. Rav Yosef says they also argued about the Omer (a communal offering), so Reish Lakish couldn't escape by saying "Tzibur is different."
